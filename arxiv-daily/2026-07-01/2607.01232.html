Why this matters
Most layers don't carry the RL signal. Skip profiling — train the middle 5; match or beat full-parameter RL at a fraction of the compute and memory.
Critical edge
Single-architecture regime. Every model tested is Qwen-family and ≤ 8B parameters. The headline recipe is unverified on Llama, Mistral, Gemma, or any scale above 8B — the easiest generalization experiment the paper didn't run.

Methodology §3 · Single-layer RL protocol

For an L-layer LLM with parameters θ = {θemb, θ0, …, θL−1, θhead}, standard full-parameter GRPO computes ∇θ LGRPO(θ). In single-layer training, only θk is updated; the gradient still back-propagates through the full network, but requires_grad=False is set on all other parameters. The procedure is run independently for every k ∈ {0, …, L−1}.

Layer contribution metric. C(k) = (Sk − Sbase) / (Sfull − Sbase). C(k) = 1.0 means single-layer matches full; > 1.0 means it surpasses; ≈ 0 means the layer cannot absorb RL improvement in isolation.

Fair-comparison protocol. GRPO with group-relative advantage (no learned value network), clipped surrogate. Group size, clip ε, KL coefficient kept identical across full and single-layer runs. Full-parameter LR tuned per model; the same LR is then reused for all single-layer runs (Appendix A.7: ranking is LR-robust).

Scope of evidence. 7 models (Qwen3-1.7B/4B/8B-Base, Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B, Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, DeepSeek-Distilled-Qwen-7B); 3 algorithms (GRPO, GiGPO, Dr. GRPO); 3 task domains (math, code, agentic via ALFWorld); 12 evaluation benchmarks; mean ± std over 3 runs.

Layer-aware strategies (§4). (1) Boost-Bk: LR up on top-k layers while holding 5e-6 elsewhere. (2) Only-Bk: train only top-k, freeze the rest. (3) Mid-5 heuristic: train layers ⌊L/2 − k/2⌋ … ⌊L/2 + k/2⌋ — no profiling required.

Strengths

  • Breadth of evidence. 7 models, 3 RL algorithms, 3 task domains, full layer scans on 3 of them — one of the most thorough layer-wise ablations in the RL post-training literature.
  • Clean operationalization. Layer contribution C(k) is one scalar with a simple, transferable interpretation; rankings survive across datasets, tasks, families, and algorithms (ρ ≥ 0.59).
  • Profiling-free heuristic. "Train the middle 5" requires no C(k) profiling and beats full-parameter RL at all 3 Qwen3 scales — an immediately deployable recipe.
  • Dissociation evidence. ‖Δθk‖2 is roughly uniform across layers under both full and single-layer training, so C(k) measures subspace effectiveness, not update magnitude.
  • Cross-task generalization. Math-vs-OOD C(k) correlation r > 0.6 on all Qwen3 scales — high-contribution layers aren't just overfitting math.
  • Diversity result. Specialists share only ~34% of newly-solved problems; majority vote reaches 33.6% vs. 31.3% for full-RL self-consistency.

Limitations

  • Single-architecture regime. All 7 models are Qwen-family, ≤ 8B parameters. Llama, Mistral, Gemma, and ≥ 70B are not tested.
  • Partial scans on 4 of 7 models. ALFWorld (GiGPO) and DeepSeek-distilled (GRPO) use subset layer scans; fine-grained structure outside the middle peak is not excluded.
  • Strategies validated only on math. Boost-Bk, Only-Bk, Mid-5 numbers are NuminaMath-CoT only — code and agentic transfer unverified.
  • LR-tuning asymmetry. Full-parameter LR is tuned per model; single-layer LR is not. Rankings survive (Appx. A.7), but absolute C(k) values may be slightly understated.
  • Negative-C outlier not explained. Layer 0 on Qwen3-8B reaches C = −0.51 — destructive interference under isolation is masked under full training; mechanism unclear.
  • No code release mentioned in the preprint text; implementation detail depends on Appendix A hyperparameter tables.
  • No theoretical story for why the middle specifically — the paper is empirical.
  • Voting cost. 33.6% majority vote needs 7 trained specialists — an analysis tool, not a deployment recipe.

Questions for further exploration open bets

  • Does the middle-layer concentration pattern persist at 70B+ scale, and in non-Qwen architectures (Llama-3, Mistral, Gemma)? The evidence is bounded above by 8B parameters and almost entirely within the Qwen family.
  • Why middle layers specifically? Are rewards most informative at the "feature abstraction" sweet spot, while early layers anchor lexical/syntactic features and late layers are dominated by output-projection constraints?
  • Layer 0 on Qwen3-8B achieves C = −0.51 — actively hurts math when trained alone, masked under full training. What's the mechanism, and can it predict which layers should be frozen a priori?
  • Does the layer-aware recipe transfer to DPO / PPO / RLOO / RLHF with non-verifiable rewards? RLVR has clean binary rewards; soft reward gradients may distribute differently across layers.
  • The 34.1% pairwise Jaccard among high-contribution specialists is striking. Could a single model route computation through different middle layers at inference (MoE-style) capture the same diversity without 7× cost?
  • Does C(k) correlate with interpretability metrics — e.g., hidden-state shift under RL probes, or activation entropy changes? This would directly bridge the paper to hidden-state interpretability.
  • The Mid-5 heuristic's gain over full-param RL is robust on math — is it equally robust across seeds, RL-step counts, and decoding regimes (greedy vs. sampling, temperature)?
