> **一句话总结：** 提出一种轻量级贝叶斯网络（BN）不确定性传播框架，将 Planner/Evaluator/Generator 三个 Agent 节点产生的 Token 熵、Wasserstein 语义散度与 P(True) 自评估信号通过确定性 OR 门组合，在 HotpotQA 上取得比 UProp 更好的 AUROC/ECE/Brier，但在 StrategyQA 上因 Planner/Evaluator 信号弱于随机而失效。

> **TL;DR:** A lightweight Bayesian Network (BN) for run-time uncertainty monitoring of Agentic RAG pipelines. Three nodes (Planner / Evaluator / Generator) emit uncertainty signals — token-level entropy, Wasserstein semantic divergence, and P(True) self-evaluation — combined via a deterministic OR-gate CPT into a system-level "Overall OK/FAIL" posterior. Evaluated on StrategyQA and HotpotQA with GPT-3.5-Turbo and GPT-4.1-Nano (200 samples each), the BN beats the UProp baseline on HotpotQA (AUROC 0.724 vs 0.719 for GPT-4.1-Nano; Brier 0.179 vs random 0.25) but loses on StrategyQA because Planner and Evaluator nodes fall below random AUROC and the OR gate cannot down-weight them. The framework is positioned as a preliminary mechanism for safety-critical industrial decision support (Offshore Wind maintenance), with adaptive noisy-OR gates and post-hoc P(True) calibration as the main future-work items.

## 核心贡献

- 提出一种**轻量级不确定性感知 Agentic RAG 监控架构**：Planner、Evaluator、Generator 三个 Agent 在多阶段推理中分别产生互补的不确定性信号（Token 熵 / 语义散度 / P(True) 自评估）。
- 引入**贝叶斯网络（BN）不确定性传播模型**：将节点级不确定性通过确定性 OR 门 CPT 聚合成系统级置信度，可解释地指出哪个 Agent 节点对总体不确定性贡献最大。
- 在 StrategyQA 与 HotpotQA 多跳问答基准（GPT-3.5-Turbo / GPT-4.1-Nano，各 200 题）上系统评估 **AUROC / AUARC / ECE / Brier Score** 四类指标，证明 BN 传播在真正多步推理任务上更有效，简单任务上反而因弱信号被平均化而失效。

## Key Contributions

- A **lightweight uncertainty-aware monitoring architecture** for Agentic RAG, with three specialized agents (Planner, Evaluator, Generator) emitting complementary per-node uncertainty signals during multi-stage reasoning.
- A **Bayesian Network (BN)-based propagation model** with a deterministic OR-gate Conditional Probability Table (CPT) that fuses node-level scores into an interpretable system-level confidence estimate.
- A **proof-of-concept empirical study** on StrategyQA and HotpotQA (200 samples × 2 models) demonstrating that BN propagation gains on multi-hop reasoning but degrades when upstream signals are weaker than random, and that the framework's risk-averse OR gate is naturally suited to safety-critical industrial decision support (Offshore Wind maintenance).

## Methodology

**Pipeline architecture.** Three GPT-based agents execute sequentially: a Planner decomposes the query, an Evaluator judges retrieved evidence, and a Generator produces the final answer via a ReAct loop over a Wikipedia retrieval interface. The Overall node's posterior is then computed from the three child node posteriors.

**Three uncertainty signals, one per node:**
1. **Token-level (naive) entropy** — log-probability of the generated sequence, length-normalized and exponentiated to a confidence score `qᵢ = exp(sᵢ)`; passed through a calibration function `f_cal` to yield a per-node success prior.
2. **Wasserstein Semantic Divergence** — for the Planner and Evaluator, replaces UProp's Gaussian-kernel PMI (Duan et al., 2025) with a Wasserstein distance between the committed output distribution and re-sampled outputs in a projected embedding space. The paper argues this avoids the JSON-output collapsing problem of Semantic Entropy (Farquhar et al., 2024) by anchoring measurement to the committed output rather than measuring within-sample spread.
3. **P(True) self-evaluation** — after the Generator finishes, the model is asked "Are you sure the answer provided is correct?" and constrained to answer with a single token (true/false); the log-probability of the affirmative token is the Generator's uncertainty signal.

**Bayesian Network propagation.** Nodes `X₁` (Planner), `X₂` (Evaluator), `X₃` (Generator), and `X_Overall` form a DAG. The Overall-vs-parents CPT is a **deterministic OR gate** (Table 1 of the paper): Overall = OK iff all three parents are OK. A node is labelled FAIL if its uncertainty exceeds the 0.5 threshold. The BN is an *abstracted execution trace*, not the full control logic — no temporal modelling, feedback loops, or repeated executions.

**Baselines.** Per-node signals are compared against the Overall BN; the BN is compared against UProp's reported AUROC (Duan et al., 2025) as an external reference. **Four metrics** are reported: AUROC (discrimination), AUARC (selective prediction), ECE and Brier Score (calibration).

## Results & Highlights

**AUROC (Table 2):** On StrategyQA, only the Generator is above random (0.5881 / 0.6213); Planner and Evaluator are below 0.5. The Overall BN trails UProp on GPT-3.5-Turbo (0.5592 vs 0.6040) but exceeds it on GPT-4.1-Nano (0.6083 vs 0.5440). On HotpotQA, **all three nodes are above random** and the Overall BN beats UProp on GPT-4.1-Nano (0.7240 vs 0.7188) and is essentially tied on GPT-3.5-Turbo (0.7160 vs 0.7470).

**AUARC (Table 3):** On StrategyQA the Generator alone wins (0.6387 / 0.6831); on HotpotQA the Overall BN wins (0.4254 / 0.3862). The counterintuitive drop in absolute AUARC on HotpotQA despite better AUROC is attributed to the lower baseline accuracy of that benchmark.

**ECE (Table 4):** On StrategyQA the Overall BN is **highly miscalibrated** (0.4814 / 0.4942, far worse than individual nodes) — the deterministic OR gate propagates conservative failure estimates even on correct answers. On HotpotQA the Overall BN is the best-calibrated (0.1376 / 0.1120), well below the random baseline of 0.25.

**Brier Score (Table 5):** Mirrors ECE — StrategyQA Overall BN is above random (0.4831 / 0.4816), HotpotQA Overall BN is substantially better than random (0.2023 / 0.1788).

**Headline takeaway:** BN propagation helps *only* when uncertainty genuinely accumulates across reasoning stages. On simple single-hop queries (StrategyQA), P(True) on the Generator alone is sufficient and adding weak upstream signals via the OR gate actively hurts.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** Modest. The BN-based combination of per-node uncertainty signals is a sensible but well-precedented idea; the most novel technical contribution is replacing UProp's Gaussian-kernel PMI with a Wasserstein semantic divergence, but the paper itself acknowledges this is "analogous" to UProp rather than fundamentally different.

**Rigor.** Mixed. The experimental setup is small (200 samples per benchmark, two models, both commercial OpenAI endpoints). The deterministic OR gate is an aggressive simplification that the paper itself identifies as a limitation. No statistical significance testing; no error bars; no ablations isolating the contribution of each uncertainty signal within the BN. The comparison to UProp is reported as raw numbers from the cited paper rather than a re-run.

**Reproducibility.** Partial. Code and pipeline are on GitHub, but the BN depends on OpenAI API log-probabilities (which were briefly removed and are not available for some models), and the P(True) prompt is not specified in detail. The 0.5 threshold and OR-gate CPT are hard-coded; there is no held-out validation set described.

**Significance.** The framework's practical value is that it produces an *interpretable* per-node contribution to system uncertainty — which is what an operator in a safety-critical setting (OSW maintenance) actually needs, rather than a single black-box confidence score. The negative result on StrategyQA is itself useful: it shows the OR-gate assumption breaks down when upstream signals are weak, which future work should address with noisy-OR + learned inhibition weights.

**Red flags.**
- The deterministic OR gate is known to be brittle; the paper acknowledges this but offers no empirical comparison to a noisy-OR or learned CPT.
- The P(True) signal is well-known to be systematically over-conservative (high uncertainty on confident answers); the paper notes this but does not apply post-hoc calibration in the headline results (only suggests it as future work).
- The "industrial" framing (OSW) is aspirational; evaluation is entirely on Wikipedia-retrieval QA, so claims about offshore wind applicability are speculative.
- Two of the four authors share an affiliation and a tight publication history; the work reads more like a feasibility study than a finished research contribution.

## Strengths

- **Modular and computationally lightweight**: the BN adds negligible overhead relative to the LLM inference cost.
- **Interpretable**: the DAG structure makes it trivial to identify which agent contributed most to system-level uncertainty.
- **Honest negative result** on StrategyQA — the authors do not hide that the OR-gate aggregation degrades performance when upstream signals are weak.
- **Multi-metric evaluation** (AUROC + AUARC + ECE + Brier) covers discrimination, selective prediction and calibration separately rather than collapsing everything into accuracy.
- **Risk-averse bias is a feature for safety-critical domains** — the paper correctly notes that over-flagging is preferable to under-flagging in industrial decision support.

## Limitations

- **Deterministic OR gate** cannot adapt to benchmark-specific node reliability and gives equal weight to noisy upstream signals.
- **P(True) is systematically miscalibrated** (high uncertainty on confidently-correct answers), so the Generator signal has strong discrimination but poor calibration on StrategyQA.
- **No post-hoc calibration** (Platt / isotonic regression) is applied despite being an obvious fix.
- **Noisy-OR with learned inhibition weights** is mentioned as the most impactful architectural change but not implemented.
- **Only Wikipedia-retrieval QA benchmarks**; no OSW or other industrial data — the headline industrial motivation is therefore unsupported by the experiments.
- **Small sample sizes** (200 per benchmark) and only two commercial models, so generalisation is uncertain.
- **No ablations** isolating the marginal contribution of each node's signal to the Overall BN posterior.
- **API dependence** on OpenAI log-probabilities, which are not available for all models and have historically been deprecated.

## Relevance

本论文直接命中**推理评估（Inference Evaluation）**与**ML 可靠性基础（ML Reliability foundations）**两个用户兴趣方向：它在不改变模型训练的前提下，为多阶段 LLM 推理提供可解释的运行时不确定性监控，并在 HotpotQA 多跳推理任务上验证了贝叶斯传播相对 UProp 的优势。其对 OR 门失效条件与 P(True) 误校准的诚实讨论，也对**模型系统卡（Model System Cards）**中可靠性声明的撰写具有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How would a **noisy-OR CPT with learned inhibition parameters** (or a small attention-weighted combination) perform on StrategyQA where individual node signals are heterogeneous? The paper flags this as the "most impactful architectural improvement" — can it be benchmarked?
- Does **post-hoc calibration of P(True)** (isotonic regression on a held-out set) preserve AUROC while fixing the Brier degradation on StrategyQA? The paper speculates yes but does not test it.
- Can the framework be extended beyond OR gates to **temporal BN structures** that model feedback loops and repeated executions (e.g., self-correction iterations), which the current DAG explicitly excludes?
- How does the framework behave when the **retrieval corpus is contaminated or out-of-distribution** (e.g., adversarial Wikipedia edits), where retrieval-based signals may be misleadingly confident?
- Would the same architecture transfer to **non-QA agentic workflows** (code generation, planning, tool use) where the "Overall OK/FAIL" notion is more ambiguous than factual correctness?
- Can **semantic divergence be computed cheaply enough** for production deployment, given the per-step sample size of N=10 and the embedding projection cost?
- For the targeted **OSW maintenance** application, what is the cost of a false-positive (abstaining on a correct answer) vs. a false-negative (answering a wrong one), and does that asymmetry justify the deterministic OR gate's conservative bias?
