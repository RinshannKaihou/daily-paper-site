What Survives Into Context: A Diagnostic for Budget-Constrained Multi-Hop RAG and When Submodular Evidence Packing Improves It

Ananto Nayan Bala · Ahsanullah University of Science and Technology arXiv:2607.00725 2026-07-01 preprint · solo author

Stop measuring recall. The right diagnostic for budget-constrained RAG is whether the gold answer survives packing — and that is what submodular packing actually moves.

On HotpotQA / 160 tokens / 3B reader, submodular packing beats every baseline (naive, MMR, focused) at +5.1 F1 over naive — but the win is conditional on four conjunctive factors, and it is reversed by 14B.

Key Metric · Headline
+5.1F1 vs naive
F1 0.451 at ~145 tokens on HotpotQA-500 (3 seeds, B=160, Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct). Also +4.2 over MMR, +2.2 over focused heuristic — best fixed policy on every seed.
Why this matters
It diagnoses the right quantity. AIC (gold-answer-as-contiguous-span in the packed reader context) predicts F1 with r=+0.50, vs r=+0.31 for recall@5 — ΔR² = +0.17. On the retrieval-perfect subset, 27% of questions still drop the answer during packing; AIC still separates F1 0.61 vs 0.20. The reader-scale ladder gives a real engineering rule: stop optimizing evidence density past ~7B; it reverses at 14B (−0.029 F1, p=0.013).
Critical edge
Latency is unmeasured, and the headline reverses with scale. The greedy submodular solver's per-query cost vs. MMR / heuristic is never reported — the +5.1 F1 may not survive realistic SLOs. The 14B rung rests on NF4 quantization with no full-precision control, conflating capacity with precision error. And the +5.1 F1 is proven on one dataset / one budget regime / one reader family (Qwen2.5).

Methodology

Reframe post-retrieval RAG as a budgeted monotone submodular maximization: given retrieved evidence and a hard token budget B, choose the packed subset that maximizes a four-term gain function.

  • Answer-in-context (AIC). Binary: does a normalized gold-answer span appear as a contiguous token subsequence of the packed (not retrieved) context? Auxiliary metrics: gold-doc reader coverage, gold-token density.
  • Two-stage evidence. (a) Correlational + incremental-validity on HotpotQA (10,500 policy×question rows, clustered bootstrap on question id, ΔR² = +0.17 over recall). (b) Interventional check on 2WikiMultiHopQA: the packer lifts gold-doc coverage by +0.054 but AIC moves only −0.007 and F1 −0.008 (p=0.44) — coverage and AIC move in opposite directions; F1 follows AIC.
  • Gain function. F(S) = 1.0·Rel + 0.5·QueryCov + 0.4·Repr + 0.3·Div, all monotone + submodular + normalized to [0,1]. Repr is a saturated facility-location (α=0.3); Div is concave-over-documents (√-mass per doc).
  • Solver. Cost-scaled (per-token) greedy with Lin–Bilmes singleton fallback. Standard (1−1/e) constant-factor template — the contribution is the application, not the optimizer.
  • Stack. bge-small-en-v1.5 (320-dim) dense bi-encoder; Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct primary reader (7B / 14B on NF4); HotpotQA-500 primary, 2Wiki + MuSiQue + RAGBench (CovidQA, ExpertQA) as boundaries.

Strengths  vs  Limitations

Strengths

  • Diagnoses the right quantity and backs it with correlational and interventional evidence.
  • Packing formalization is principled (budgeted monotone submodular with constant-factor guarantee); four-term gain maps to practitioner intuitions.
  • Three-seed headline with clustered bootstrap on question id — stronger inferential standard than common in the area.
  • Paired token-cost comparison; iso-quality efficiency result (submod@160 ≈ focused@224 at ~30% fewer tokens).
  • Falsified monotonicity prediction (inverted-U peaking at B≈160) and falsifiable reader-scale ladder — top-tier scientific hygiene.
  • Clean disentanglement of graph compression vs submodular packing (ACE reversal: −0.021 F1, [−0.039, −0.003]).
  • Oracle headroom reported with an explicit "do not deploy" framing.

Limitations

  • Headline win is one dataset / one budget / one reader (HotpotQA, B≈160, 3B Qwen2.5); cross-dataset experiments are designed as negatives.
  • Single model family (Qwen2.5) and single embedder (bge-small-en) — Llama / Mistral / Gemma and ColBERT / cross-encoder untested.
  • No full-precision 14B control — capacity and NF4 quantization error are not fully orthogonalized at the top rung.
  • Budget sweep and MuSiQue are single-seed; three-seed robustness applies only to the HotpotQA headline.
  • Latency / throughput cost of the solver is not measured — production relevance of +5.1 F1 is unknown.
  • AIC is span-based and gold-answer-centric → degenerate for long free-form answers (ExpertQA, 0% spans survive).
  • ACE graph construction is heuristic; the "partial substitute" finding inherits that uncertainty.
  • n=500 per dataset — per-dataset "no win" results may be power-limited.
  • Reader-scale ladder ends at 14B; behavior at 32B+ (strong long-context readers) is unknown and is precisely where the "weaker reader" condition matters least.

Questions for further exploration

  1. Does AIC remain the leading diagnostic on non-extractive answer formats (long-form QA, dialog, citation grounding, multimodal)? A semantic / entailment variant is needed — the paper leaves this explicitly to future work.
  2. How does the submodular packer perform on cross-lingual or domain-shifted retrieval (biomedical, legal, code), where complementary structure may be weaker?
  3. What is the latency budget of the greedy submodular solver vs MMR at retrieval-set sizes ≥ 100, and does packing + inference cost erase the F1 win end-to-end under realistic per-query SLOs?
  4. Can AIC be estimated without gold answers (answer-probe model, per-token likelihood ratio) and used as an online stopping signal — surfacing the ~20% "answer-in-context lottery" at inference time?
  5. Is the 14B reversal driven by reader capacity or quantization error? Would a full-precision 14B / 32B ladder reach the same boundary?
  6. How tightly does the four-condition scope map generalize — does removing one condition (e.g. budget → 224) collapse the +5.1 F1 to noise for this specific question subset, as claimed but not ablated question-by-question?
  7. Does the disentanglement of packing vs graph compression survive with a stronger ACE (ColBERT-grade late interaction, learned graph construction)?
  8. Can AIC be combined with RAGAS / ARES into a single evaluation framework, or do they capture orthogonal failure modes?
  9. What is the relationship between AIC and the "lost in the middle" effect (Liu et al. 2024) — does AIC drop more for middle-positioned spans, and does position-aware packing close some of the gap?
  10. How sensitive is the scope map to retriever quality — would ColBERT / cross-encoder make MuSiQue Condition 2 hold and unlock the packer on a second multi-hop dataset?
