> **一句话总结：** 提出 LOCOS (Logit-Contribution Scoring) 方法，通过计算注意力头 OV 电路输出在答案 token unembedding 方向上的投影，并对比 needle 与 off-needle 位置的贡献，识别出负责"非字面检索"的注意力头；在六款模型上 mean-ablation top-50 头即可把 Qwen3-8B 的 NoLiMa ROUGE-L 从 0.401 打到 0.000，显著优于现有 attention-based 检索头检测器。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces LOCOS, a write-aware detector for "retrieval heads" in LLMs that identifies non-literal retrieval circuits missed by attention-based scoring. Existing detectors (Wu et al. 2025, Fu et al. 2025, etc.) reward heads whose attended token matches the answer token — a literal-copy criterion that only measures the QK circuit (where the head reads). LOCOS instead scores each head by the projection of its OV-circuit output onto the answer token's unembedding direction, contrasting needle vs. off-needle positions within a single forward pass. Across six models from three families (Qwen3 8B/14B/32B, Gemma-3 12B/27B, OLMo-3.1 32B) on the NoLiMa benchmark, mean-ablating the top-50 LOCOS heads on Qwen3-8B collapses ROUGE-L from 0.401 → 0.000, while the strongest baseline still retains 0.292. Ablation is retrieval-specific: parametric recall (city-country, PopQA) and two-operand arithmetic remain at baseline. The same ablation transfers to MuSiQue (0.55 → 0.08) and BABILong (0.62 → 0.20) on Qwen3-8B. Random-head controls stay within 0.05 of baseline.

## 核心贡献

- **重新审视"retrieval head"的定义**：指出既有的 attention-based 检测器（Wu et al. 2025 等）只能在字面复制（literal copy）场景下工作，无法识别将 attention 内容通过 OV 电路"改写"为答案的非字面检索头。
- **提出 LOCOS 方法**：基于 OV 电路输出在答案 unembedding 方向的标量投影进行打分（per-position logit contribution），在一次前向中用 needle vs. off-needle 空间对比（spatial contrast）筛选出真正负责检索的头。
- **跨 3 个模型家族、6 个配置的因果验证**：通过 mean-ablation 实验证明，LOCOS 比所有 attention-based baseline 都能更快地把 NoLiMa ROUGE-L 打到接近 0。
- **检索特异性（retrieval specificity）的多重验证**：bottom-k 对照消融、param recall 控制、arithmetic 控制、随机头控制与 dissociation score 共同支持所选头确实只负责上下文检索而非通用推理能力。
- **下游迁移验证**：top-50 LOCOS 头在 MuSiQue、BABILong 等未见过的长上下文基准上同样对检索任务造成最大破坏，且不显著伤害参数化知识。

## Key Contributions

- **Reframes "retrieval heads" as an OV-circuit concept, not an attention pattern**: explicit argument that two heads with identical attention to a needle can write entirely different residual-stream updates, and that the head propagating an answer-aligned write via its OV circuit — not the one copying the attended token — is the head actually responsible for retrieval.
- **LOCOS scoring rule**: per-position scalar φ = u_y^T (α · W_O v) followed by a needle/off-needle spatial contrast within a single answer step, aggregated across passing trials.
- **Strictly stronger ablation curves**: on Qwen3-8B at k=50, LOCOS reaches ROUGE-L = 0.000 vs. 0.292 (best baseline) and 0.337 (Wu/NoLiMa). Random-head ablation stays within ~0.05 of baseline.
- **Bottom-k control rules out circularity**: bottom-k heads (large answer-aligned logit contribution, but from off-needle positions) do not degrade NoLiMa when ablated, demonstrating that the ablation is not a mechanical consequence of the scoring function.
- **Retrieval specificity**: parametric accuracy (city-country, PopQA, two-operand arithmetic) is preserved under the same ablation. The proposed Dissociation Score (DS) peaks higher for LOCOS than for every baseline across all six configurations.
- **Downstream transfer**: on Qwen3-8B, ablating top-50 LOCOS heads drops MuSiQue 0.55 → 0.08 and BABILong qa2+qa3 0.62 → 0.20; LOCOS is the most damaging ablation in 6 of 12 model–benchmark cells.

## Methodology

LOCOS targets a specific failure mode of attention-based retrieval-head detectors: they score heads whose attention aligns with the literal answer token, which is well-defined only when the answer appears verbatim in the needle. In non-literal retrieval (the NoLiMa regime), the answer shares no lexical overlap with the needle and must be synthesized through the OV circuit.

**Notation.** A transformer with L layers and H heads; for head (l,h) at source position j, the output is `o_j = α_j · W_O v_j ∈ R^d`. The unembedding row `u_y ∈ R^d` maps the residual stream to the y-th token's logit.

**Step 1 — Per-position logit contribution.** For each head (l,h) and source position j at step t where the model emits the correct token y_t, LOCOS computes:

  φ_t,j^(l,h) = u_y^T · o_j = α_j · u_y^T · W_O · v_j

This scalar is the projection of the head's OV write onto the correct-token direction. A head attending strongly to a position whose OV output is orthogonal to u_y gets φ ≈ 0; a head doing non-literal synthesis (e.g., attending "Paris" to produce "France") gets a large φ because its OV circuit transforms the attended representation into an answer-aligned direction. Crucially, this captures "what the head writes," not just "where it reads."

**Step 2 — Spatial contrast.** For each answer step t, LOCOS splits source positions into needle [s_τ, e_τ) and off-needle; both sides are rescaled to length (e_τ − s_τ) so they represent comparable spans:

  Φ_t^(+) = Σ_{j ∈ needle} φ_j,  Φ_t^(−) = (e_τ − s_τ)/(N_t − (e_τ − s_τ)) · Σ_{j ∉ needle} φ_j

The contrast Φ^(+) − Φ^(−) is spatial (position-wise within one step), not temporal (answer vs. non-answer step). This suppresses uniform contributors (e.g., token-frequency priors) whose φ is large but position-independent. Notably, negative scores are not clamped: a strongly negative score indicates that the head's answer-aligned write originates off-needle, which is a useful dissociation signal.

**Step 3 — Aggregation.** Mean over all (trial, answer-step) pairs across passing trials (ROUGE-1 recall > ρ = 0.5 vs. gold):

  S^(l,h) = (1/|A_τ|) · Σ_τ Σ_t [Φ_t^(+) − Φ_t^(−)]

**Causal validation.** LOCOS is validated via mean-ablation: the post-Q, pre-RoPE query vector at every decode step is replaced with a head-specific calibration vector q̄^(l,h) computed once over 50 trials, keeping downstream activations in-distribution (Nanda et al. 2023, Wang et al. 2023). Outcome metric: ROUGE-L on 800 held-out NoLiMa trials.

**Reduction to attention-based scoring.** LOCOS reduces to a Wu-style attention-based score when the OV projection contributes no position-dependent signal (Appx. O). This is verifiable algebraically and empirically: replacing φ with α in the same aggregation reduces causal effect on most (but not all) models, demonstrating that OV projection improves reliability rather than uniformly increasing effect size.

**Benchmarks and models.** Six models across three families: Qwen3 (8B/14B/32B), Gemma-3 (12B/27B), OLMo-3.1 (32B). Probing: NoLiMa Modarressi et al. 2025 onehop subset, 10 context lengths × 10 depths × 3 characters, 1k–5k tokens. Downstream: MuSiQue (multi-hop QA), BABILong qa2+qa3 (object tracking through distractor narrative — designed to defeat literal copying). Baselines: random heads; Wu/NIAH-scored; Wu/NoLiMa-scored.

## Results & Highlights

- **Ablation curves** (NoLiMa ROUGE-L vs. number of ablated heads, k): LOCOS reaches near-zero ROUGE-L by k=50 in five of six configurations (Qwen3-8B: 0.401→0.000; Qwen3-14B, Qwen3-32B, Gemma-3-12B, Gemma-3-27B comparable or better). On Qwen3-8B at k=50, LOCOS 0.000 vs. Wu/NIAH 0.292 vs. Wu/NoLiMa 0.337. Random-head ablation stays within 0.358–0.402.
- **Attention-only spatial contrast control**: replacing φ with α in identical aggregation yields an attention-only score S_att. LOCOS is more damaging on Qwen3-8B/32B and Gemma-3-12B, comparable on Qwen3-14B and OLMo-3.1-32B, but Gemma-3-27B inverts at depth (LOCOS 0.369 vs. attention 0.118 at k=20). Authors note this as a known outlier, analyzed via tuned-lens corrected projections in Appx. N.5.
- **Bottom-k control**: ablating the bottom-50 heads (large absolute φ but from off-needle positions, so S^(l,h) ≪ 0) does not degrade NoLiMa ROUGE-L and tracks the random control. This rules out the "circularity" objection that the scoring function mechanically selects heads whose ablation reduces the y_t logit regardless of retrieval role.
- **Layer distribution**: LOCOS heads concentrate in late layers (concentrated on Qwen3 family and Gemma-3-27B; broader span on Gemma-3-12B and OLMo-3.1-32B). Authors flag a possible direct-path-bias artifact and validate via tuned-lens (Appx. N.4) and causal activation patching (Appx. N.6) on Qwen3-8B and Gemma-3-12B. Top-10 set overlap with Wu/NIAH is only 2/10 (Qwen3-8B) and 3/10 (Gemma-3-12B).
- **Retrieval specificity (Dissociation Score)**: DS = ΔR_k − ΔP_k reaches its peak higher for LOCOS than for every baseline across all six configurations. Top-k ablation does not damage parametric recall or two-operand arithmetic; bottom-k ablation also leaves parametric accuracy stable (Appx. J).
- **Literal vs. non-literal**: ablating the same top-k LOCOS heads (selected on NoLiMa) damages both NoLiMa and standard NIAH, but the NoLiMa curve drops faster in every configuration. Literal curve drops in 5/6 models → the top-k set is not strictly non-literal-specific, but contains a non-literal subset that prior detectors miss.
- **Downstream transfer (top-50 ablation)**: on Qwen3-8B, MuSiQue 0.55 → 0.08; BABILong qa2+qa3 0.62 → 0.20. Across 12 model–benchmark cells, LOCOS is the most damaging ablation in 6 and produces ≥0.10 drop in 8; random-heads stay within 0.05 of baseline in 11 cells. Ablation ranking is family-dependent: Wu/NIAH-scored ablation is more damaging on MuSiQue for Gemma-3 and OLMo-3.1, but Wu/NIAH slightly raises BABILong accuracy on Qwen3-14B, Gemma-3-27B, OLMo-3.1-32B.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** Genuine conceptual advance: the read-vs-write distinction (QK vs. OV) is not new in mechanistic interpretability, but applying it as a scoring observable *per head* with *spatial contrast* is. The paper also correctly identifies why prior detectors must by construction miss non-literal retrieval — not an empirical accident, but a definitional limitation. Reasonable to call this a meaningful extension of Wu et al. 2025 rather than a brand-new subfield.

**Rigor.** High. The headline ablation result is paired with at least five independent controls (random, Wu/NIAH, Wu/NoLiMa, attention-only spatial contrast, bottom-k), and the authors run on six model configurations rather than the more typical one or two. Mean-replacement ablation (rather than zero or resample ablation) is the right choice for keeping downstream activations in-distribution. The bottom-k control is the most important: it directly falsifies the "the scoring function mechanically selects answer-aligned heads" alternative hypothesis.

**Reproducibility.** High. Public benchmark (NoLiMa), public models, public toolkit (TransformerLens). The paper releases data and code at `locos` (probe) and `locos-results` (results). Calibration is performed on a separate 50-trial sample, evaluation on 800 held-out trials. The only reproducibility risk is that several architectural knobs live in the appendix (Appx. H) and need to be checked for grouped-query attention and tied embeddings.

**Significance.** Strong. The OV-circuit perspective reframes a problem most of the field has been treating as a QK-circuit problem. Applications beyond interpretability: per-head KV-cache eviction policies (the cited DuoAttention literature) and KV-cache compression (PyramidKV, SnapKV) currently use attention-pattern proxies for head importance; an OV-aware signal could improve retrieval-preserving eviction at long contexts. The release of these scores for three model families should accelerate downstream work.

**Red flags and limitations to flag honestly.**

1. **The Gemma-3-27B inversion is non-trivial.** LOCOS performs worse than the attention-only control at large k on this model. The authors attribute this to direct-path-bias and validate via tuned-lens, but the fact that the ablation ranking is *model-dependent* (Wu/NIAH sometimes wins on Gemma-3 and OLMo-3.1) is not a peripheral footnote — it caps LOCOS's universality. A practitioner using LOCOS scores for KV-cache eviction on an unseen model has no a-priori guarantee the rank will be the optimal set.

2. **NoLiMa as the sole probing benchmark.** LOCOS is selected on NoLiMa probing trials and immediately evaluated on NoLiMa. The downstream evaluation (MuSiQue, BABILong) covers a different *task*, but the *probing distribution* is still NoLiMa. Whether LOCOS scores transfer to genuinely different probe distributions (e.g., a non-QA reading-comprehension needle) is not tested.

3. **"Single forward pass" claim is slightly overstated.** Per probing trial, yes — but aggregation over hundreds of passing trials still requires the full NoLiMa probing distribution. This is not a major issue but the framing could mislead.

4. **The direct-path assumption is itself a known fragile point in logit-lens-style work.** LOCOS inherits it. Tuned-lens testing is partially reassuring, but does not eliminate the structural concern that late-layer scores are systematically biased.

5. **No comparison to other write-aware methods.** Sun et al. 2025 (RedeEP) does OV/FFN decomposition for RAG hallucination detection, but the paper does not compare LOCOS scores with RedeEP-style decomposition. Similarly, McDougall et al. 2023's copy-suppression heads are mentioned as complementary but not benchmarked.

6. **Parametric-arithmetic dataset** is described as a custom dataset at `aryopg/parametric-arithmetic-eval` — small benchmarks in author-controlled repos are a minor reproducibility risk.

## Strengths

- Strong conceptual framing: isolates a precise failure mode (literal-copy-only detectors) and proposes a clean fix.
- Five-plus independent controls in a single paper, including the rare bottom-k control that falsifies the circularity objection.
- Six model configurations across three families; not a typical "two models, one benchmark" study.
- Honest about model-dependent results (Gemma-3-27B inversion) and explicit about direct-path-bias caveats.
- Clean separation between per-position observable (φ vs. α) and aggregation choice (spatial vs. temporal), enabling a clean ablated comparison.
- Practical relevance to KV-cache eviction and long-context inference engineering.

## Limitations

- Head selection is benchmark-dependent: ranking against Wu/NIAH varies across model families (Gemma-3-27B and OLMo-3.1-32B are the trouble spots).
- The off-needle baseline is a length-normalized region, which is reasonable but heuristic; heads performing diffuse contextual integration (as opposed to span-specific retrieval) may be systematically missed.
- No MoE, encoder-decoder, or state-space coverage. The paper explicitly disclaims transfer of effect-size magnitudes to those architectures.
- ROUGE-L as headline metric is the right choice for retrieval eval but masks possible qualitative effects (hallucination, partial answers, refusal) that ROUGE-L would score as non-zero when the model is producing degraded output.
- The "single forward pass" claim should be read as "one forward per probing trial," not "one forward total."
- No dependence on benchmark-card-style disclosure of model versions, decoding settings, or prompt formats, which would help reproduction.

## Relevance

- **Hidden States & Interpretability**: 直接相关 — 这是一项机制可解释性研究，针对长上下文 LLM 中负责检索的注意力头提出了新的可解释特征（OV 电路投影分数）。
- **Inference Evaluation**: 高度相关 — 所识别的检索头直接可用于指导 KV-cache 压缩/检索头专用的推理路径优化（DuoAttention、PyramidKV、CompressKV 等相关工作均被引用），可用于提升长上下文推理质量。
- 较弱相关：ML 可靠性基础（方法学上有方法论控制价值）、模型系统卡片（可作为补充分析章节）。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the LOCOS ranking transfer to non-QA probing distributions (e.g., reading comprehension, multi-document summarization) where the "answer" is not a span of contiguous tokens?
- For KV-cache eviction, would per-head φ-weighted retention (rather than top-k binary retention) better preserve long-context retrieval quality?
- Why does LOCOS lose to Wu/NIAH on Gemma-3-27B at large k? Is it direct-path bias, or a genuine architectural difference in OV/FFN coupling?
- Can LOCOS scores be combined with FFN attribution (as in RedeEP) to give a unified head-and-expert write-aware importance score?
- What is the relationship between LOCOS-identified non-literal retrieval heads and copy-suppression heads (McDougall et al. 2023)? Are they the same circuit on different inputs, or distinct mechanisms?
- At what context length does the LOCOS ranking stabilize? The paper uses 1k–5k tokens; behaviour at 100k+ tokens (real production length) is unverified.
- Could a tuned-lens-corrected variant of LOCOS eliminate the late-layer concentration artifact and generalize better to mid-layer heads?
