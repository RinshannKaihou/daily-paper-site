> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 NeuroCogMap 框架，将认知神经科学的"功能脑区划分（functional parcellation）"方法迁移到 LLM 内部表征：在 SAE 特征空间聚出 270 个稳定且跨模型部分保守的功能 parcels，将幻觉/偏见/拒绝失败/谄媚四大故障定位为不同系统级扰动（检测 AUROC 0.68–0.99，介入后 AdvBench 拒答准确率 38.3% → 98.6%），并以这些内部签名提升对人类皮层响应的预测（mean r = 0.407，最高对齐于联合皮层）以及精化经典双系统决策模型。

> **TL;DR:** NeuroCogMap ports the cognitive-neuroscience "functional brain parcellation" pipeline to LLMs: it groups Sparse Autoencoder (SAE) features into 270 stable, semantically coherent, and partly cross-model-conserved functional *parcels*, links them to 45 cognitive capabilities organized into a four-layer Bloom-inspired hierarchy (perception / representation / abstraction / application), and uses a model-level structural connectome to interpret pathology. Hallucination and social bias localize as **representational** system disruptions, refusal failure and sycophancy as **behavioral-control** system disruptions — yielding both detectors (hallucination AUROC 0.681/0.840 on Gemma2-2B/9B-IT; refusal-failure AUROC ≈ 0.99) and targetable steering interventions (e.g., AdvBench refusal accuracy 38.3% → 98.6% on Gemma2-2B). The same parcels predict human cortical BOLD responses during naturalistic story listening better than strong LLM-feature baselines (mean r = 0.407 vs. 0.367 for Language-Standard), with the largest gains in higher-order Default / Frontoparietal-Control / Salience networks. They also guide discovery of latent decision strategies that, when folded into a classical Dual-systems model, lower held-out AIC on two-step tasks.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **NeuroCogMap** 框架，将认知神经科学的任务态功能脑区划分方法系统迁移到 LLM：在 SAE 特征空间聚出 **270 个**稳定、可解读的功能 parcels（联合得分 0.771，含聚类质量、描述质量、非冗余度三项准则）。
- 构建 LLM **模型级结构连接组（structural connectome）**，用跨层 SAE 解码/编码向量点积估计 parcel 间有向权重，并据此约束功能性耦合的解释。
- 将 parcels 与从 **5000+ 篇学术论文** 自动抽取并人工核对的 **45 项认知能力** 对齐，按 Bloom 启发划分为感知 / 表征 / 抽象 / 应用 **四个层级**，并通过合成训练实验验证能力间的因果依赖（L1→L3、L2→L3 提升均显著）。
- 把故障划分为 **表征型（hallucination、bias）** 与 **行为控制型（refusal failure、sycophancy）** 两大系统级紊乱：TruthfulQA 幻觉反映评估控制不足，NQ-Open 幻觉反映事实检索协调破碎；拒绝失败反映从"监测-评估-拒绝控制"向"程序化执行"的系统性切换。
- 同一份病理图谱既可作 **检测器**（在六个幻觉基准上平均 AUROC 0.681/0.840，拒答基准上 AUROC ≈ 0.99），又可作 **机制引导的定向干预**（激活 truthful 应激活 parcel、抑制 hallucination 过激活 parcel；Llama-3.1-8B 上亦复现）。
- 用 NeuroCogMap parcel 激活预测 **人脑 fMRI BOLD**（LeBel 自然叙事听音数据集 + Schaefer 100-parcel + Yeo 七网络），mean r = 0.407，超过 BERT、Word2Vec、Language Context/Standard 等基线，**优势集中在高级联合皮层**（Default、Frontoparietal Control、Salience/Ventral Attention）。
- 借助 NeuroCogMap 暴露的潜在策略，**精化经典 Dual-systems 决策模型**：在两个 two-step task 上，持出 AIC 从 268.77 降到 262.45、从 478.29 降到 465.95，优于纯行为驱动的模型发现。

## Key Contributions

- **NeuroCogMap framework.** Adapts the cognitive-neuroscience parcellation pipeline to LLMs: groups SAE features into 270 functional parcels (peak joint score = 0.771; intra-parcel similarity 0.638 vs. inter-parcel 0.433, p < 0.0001). Cross-model analyses span Gemma2-2B, Gemma2-9B-IT and Llama-3.1-8B; matched parcels show higher activation-pattern correspondence than random directions and are more likely to share identical / partially similar functional descriptions.
- **Structural connectome + cognitive hierarchy.** Builds a directed model-level structural connectome from cross-layer SAE decoder/encoder dot products and uses it as a constraint on functional coupling. Links parcels to 45 capabilities mined from 5000+ papers and arranged into a four-level Bloom-inspired hierarchy (perception / representation / abstraction / application). Layer assignments are validated by semantic-dispersion and structural-degree analyses (Extended Data Fig. 11) and by a controlled synthetic training experiment (GPT-NeoX/Pythia 70M / 160M / 410M, n = 5 runs) showing that adding lower-level retrieval or recall supports improves higher-level reasoning.
- **Pathology reframed as systems-level disruption.** Distinguishes *representational* pathologies (hallucination, social bias) from *behavioral-control* pathologies (refusal failure, sycophancy). TruthfulQA hallucinations reflect insufficient higher-order evaluative control over misleading premises (e.g., weakened Veracity-Check ↔ Affirmation-Detector coupling, Δw = 0.57, p = 0.0087); NQ-Open hallucinations reflect fragmented factual-retrieval coordination (e.g., weakened Internal Geopolitical-Trivia ↔ Wikipedia Fact Retrieval coupling, Δw = −0.74, p = 2.0e-6).
- **Detection + intervention from the same map.** A NeuroCogMap-derived signature beats SelfCheckGPT, hidden-state probing and uncertainty baselines on HaluEval / MedHallu / Dolly closed-QA / NQ-Open / SciQ / TruthfulQA (mean AUROC 0.681 on Gemma2-2B, 0.840 on Gemma2-9B-IT). Steering toward truthful-recruited parcels and away from hallucinatory-overrecruited parcels improves factual accuracy across MedHallu, NQ-Open and TruthfulQA. Refusal-failure detection reaches AUROC ≈ 0.99 on AdvBench / JBB-Behaviors, and parcel-level steering lifts AdvBench refusal accuracy from 38.3% to 98.6% on Gemma2-2B (large gains where residual control pathology remains; minimal effect when baseline refusal is already near ceiling). Both effects replicate on Llama-3.1-8B.
- **Cortex alignment.** Parcel-level LLM activations entered into ridge-regression encoding models predict held-out cortical BOLD responses from the LeBel naturalistic story-listening dataset better than BERT (r = 0.219), Language Context (r = 0.361) and Language Standard (r = 0.367); NeuroCogMap reaches mean r = 0.407. Largest gains localize to higher-order association cortex — left parietal language-comprehension (+0.268), left prefrontal semantic-retrieval (+0.174), left / right temporal Default, with system-level peaks in Default, Frontoparietal Control and Salience/Ventral Attention networks.
- **Functional and geometric correspondence with cortex.** Neurosynth-derived functional profiles show that top-ranked LLM parcels are semantically closer to their matched cortical parcel's cognitive profile than bottom-ranked ones (strongest in Dorsal Attention, Salience, Frontoparietal Control and Default). Description-based RSA on matched parcels recovers significant within-network structure (left Default r = 0.225, p = 0.047; right Frontoparietal Control r = 0.774, p = 3.1e-8).
- **Cognitive model discovery.** On Psych-101 tasks (episodic memory, multi-attribute choice, Shepard categorization, intertemporal choice, drifting four-armed bandit), NeuroCogMap-derived representations predict AIC-based behavioral fit across subjects (episodic memory r = 0.598; bandit r = 0.213). These signatures are then used by a reasoning agent → model-writing agent pipeline to extend a classical Dual-systems model with attention-gated lapses, dynamic model-based/model-free arbitration, history-sensitive perseveration and rule-like policy switching; held-out AIC improves on both Two-Step Task One (51,500 trials, 206 participants; 268.77 → 262.45) and Two-Step Task Two (40,000 trials, 100 participants; 478.29 → 465.95).

## Methodology

- **Sparse Autoencoder (SAE) features as the "voxels."** Extract sparse features from SAE latents trained on residual-stream activations across LLM layers; these features are the atomic units of analysis.
- **Functional parcellation.** Cluster SAE features with similar task-evoked response profiles into **parcels**. Pick granularity k by maximizing a joint score C(k) combining (i) clustering quality, (ii) functional-description quality (faithfulness / specificity of LLM-generated descriptions) and (iii) functional non-redundancy. The joint score peaks at k = 270 parcels (C = 0.771). Internal coherence is confirmed by higher intra- vs. inter-parcel semantic similarity (0.638 vs. 0.433; paired Wilcoxon W = 36585, p < 0.0001).
- **Model-level structural connectome.** For each (source SAE latent f in layer ℓ_f, target latent g in layer ℓ_g, ℓ_f < ℓ_g) compute s(f → g) = ⟨W^dec_ℓ_f · f, W^enc_ℓ_g · g⟩ / (‖W^dec_ℓ_f f‖_2 · ‖W^enc_ℓ_g g‖_2); aggregate to directed parcel edges S(p → q) and threshold the top 10% of off-diagonal absolute weights for hierarchy-related structural-degree analyses. This provides a substrate that constrains interpretation of functional coupling during pathology analyses.
- **Cognitive atlas.** Each parcel is annotated by GPT-OSS-20B (served locally via vLLM, structured outputs) with a function name + 100–200 word description + behavioral role, using activation-weighted TF–IDF keywords (top 20 kept, 15 included in prompt), dataset-distribution summaries and the top-50 high-activation examples. A targeted blinded human audit (3 annotators, 60 Gemma2-2B parcel descriptions, 570 pathology-labelled responses, 100 cross-model pairs) covers the judgment-dependent components.
- **Parcel–capability mapping + capability hierarchy.** Capabilities are mined from 5000+ academic papers and manually reviewed. For each capability c and dataset d, parcel activation A_d(p) is averaged over tokens and SAE features. Positive (+0.5) and suppressive (−1.0) steering of parcels (sum of SAE decoder directions added at residual-stream hook points) yields intervention-based log-probability changes Δ_d⁺(p), Δ_d⁻(p), combined with activation evidence into a score S_d(p) = 0.5·Ã_d(p) + 0.5·L̃_d(p). Capability–parcel mapping M(c, p) blends data-driven scores with semantic cosine Q_c(p) = cos(e_c, e_p) using Qwen3-Embedding-8B (β = 0.5). Capabilities are assigned to four Bloom-inspired hierarchy levels (perception / representation / abstraction / application), validated by within-layer vs. cross-layer semantic similarity (perception 0.541, representation 0.503, abstraction 0.452; paired t-tests, all p < 0.001) and by semantic-dispersion + structural-degree analyses.
- **Pathology contrastive analysis.** Compare normative vs. pathological responses at four levels: circuit connectivity (Δw), parcel activation (Δa), capability activation (Δa), and hierarchy-level shift. Datasets: TruthfulQA, NQ-Open (hallucination); BBQ (bias); AdvBench, JBB-Behaviors (refusal failure); Sycophancy-Eval (sycophancy). Statistics: response-level paired tests (paired t / Wilcoxon), 95% CIs reported for activation differences.
- **Detector.** Aggregate parcel recruitment, capability engagement and circuit organization into a vector and score responses by alignment with truthful vs. hallucinatory (or refuse-success vs. refuse-failure) signatures; evaluate via AUROC against SelfCheckGPT, hidden probing, uncertainty (perplexity), SmoothLLM, logits-SVM, perturbation-based baselines.
- **Intervention.** Enhance truthful-recruited parcels (γ = +0.5) and suppress hallucinatory-overrecruited parcels (γ = −1.0) at SAE decoder hook points; quantify factual accuracy / refusal accuracy changes. Cross-model generality verified on Llama-3.1-8B (Extended Data Fig. 10).
- **Cortex alignment.** Encode LeBel story-listening fMRI into the Schaefer 100-parcel cortical atlas, group into Yeo 7-network organization, and predict held-out cortical BOLD with ridge regression over NeuroCogMap parcel activations (and baselines: WordRate, Word2Vec, BERT, Language Context, Language Standard). Statistical significance against baselines uses Fisher-z with Benjamini–Hochberg correction. Representational Similarity Analysis compares Neurosynth-derived human parcel similarity matrices with description-based LLM parcel similarity matrices within each Yeo network.
- **Cognitive model discovery.** Use Psych-101 behavioral data (episodic memory, multi-attribute choice, Shepard, intertemporal choice, four-armed bandit) to test whether NeuroCogMap representations predict per-subject AIC-based behavioral fit. On two-step task fMRI data, parcel signatures feed a reasoning agent (GPT-5.2) that proposes candidate strategies, which a model-writing agent converts into executable cognitive-model extensions; AIC is evaluated on Two-Step Task One (51,500 trials, 206 participants) and Two-Step Task Two (40,000 trials, 100 participants).

## Results & Highlights

- **Parcels are stable, coherent and partially cross-model conserved.** Joint score peaks at 270 parcels (0.771); intra-parcel semantic similarity 0.638 > inter-parcel 0.433 (W = 36585, p < 0.0001); matched Gemma2-2B / Gemma2-9B-IT / Llama-3.1-8B parcels show higher activation correspondence than random directions and higher likelihood of identical or partially similar cognitive meanings than baselines.
- **Parcel descriptions are predictive.** Functional descriptions predict held-out parcel activation rankings better than random-clustering, neuron-based and keyword-only baselines (Wilcoxon, n = 40, W⁺ = 673–773, all p_Holm ≤ 0.001). Positive steering of individual parcels shifts outputs toward that parcel's function; larger steering success rates track larger output changes.
- **Capability hierarchy is causally grounded.** Suppressing strongly linked parcels / capabilities produces larger drops in ground-truth log-probability than suppressing unrelated units (paired t-tests, t₃₄ = 2.75–3.92 and t₃₉ = 2.76–3.46, all p ≤ 0.01). Synthetic training experiments show that adding lower-level retrieval support improves two-hop reasoning (Welch t = 5.90–26.17, all p < 0.001) and that adding lower-level knowledge-recall support improves parametric reasoning (t = 6.67–9.08, all p ≤ 0.01) across model scales.
- **Hallucination is mechanistically non-uniform.** TruthfulQA hallucinations reflect weakened higher-order evaluative control; NQ-Open hallucinations reflect fragmented factual-retrieval coordination. The framework explicitly distinguishes them at the parcel, capability and hierarchy levels.
- **Hallucination detection + mitigation.** Mean AUROC 0.681 (Gemma2-2B) and 0.840 (Gemma2-9B-IT) across HaluEval, MedHallu, Dolly closed-QA, NQ-Open, SciQ, TruthfulQA; beats SelfCheckGPT, hidden probing and uncertainty baselines. Parcel-level intervention improves factual accuracy across MedHallu, NQ-Open and TruthfulQA in both model sizes; cross-model check on Llama-3.1-8B (Extended Data Fig. 10).
- **Refusal failure is a behavioral-control shift.** Successful refusal engages Negative-Motivation ↔ Negation/Self-Reference coupling (Δw = 1.21, p < 10⁻³⁰⁰); refusal failure shifts toward Idea-and-Plan ↔ Procedural Guidance coupling (Δw = −0.36, p = 4.5e-38). Refusal-failure detection AUROC ≈ 0.99 on AdvBench / JBB-Behaviors; parcel-level intervention raises AdvBench refusal accuracy from 38.3% to 98.6% on Gemma2-2B (large gains where residual control pathology remains).
- **Cortex prediction.** NeuroCogMap reaches mean r = 0.407 on the LeBel story-listening BOLD prediction task, beating BERT (0.219), Language Context (0.361), Language Standard (0.367); gains localize to higher-order Default / Frontoparietal Control / Salience networks. Top-ranked LLM parcels are semantically closer to matched cortical parcels' Neurosynth profiles (strongest in Dorsal Attention, Salience, Frontoparietal Control, Default). Description-based RSA recovers within-network structure (left Default r = 0.225, p = 0.047; right Frontoparietal Control r = 0.774, p = 3.1e-8).
- **Cognitive model refinement.** NeuroCogMap-derived signatures predict AIC-based behavioral fit on Psych-101 tasks (episodic memory r = 0.598; bandit r = 0.213). On two-step tasks, the NeuroCogMap-discovered Dual-systems extension achieves held-out AIC 262.45 (Two-Step One) and 465.95 (Two-Step Two), beating both the original model (268.77 / 478.29) and a behavior-only discovered model (268.73 / 473.53).

Senior authors with strong publication records in cognitive-inspired AI and NLP (Guoqi Li, Yi Zeng, Jun Xu, Ji-Rong Wen, Kun Wang). Two authors contribute equally (Sun, Lu); four are corresponding. The combination of SAE-based mechanistic analysis, brain-alignment fMRI, and dual-systems cognitive modeling is unusual but credible given the institutional spread. ## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** Conceptually high — borrowing the *whole* parcellation → atlas → hierarchy → pathology / cortex-alignment pipeline from cognitive neuroscience and applying it to LLM SAE features is a meaningful reframing of "interpretability" as "cognitive neuroscience of artificial systems." Most prior work studies LLM internals at either a local (circuit / feature) scale or a broad representational scale; NeuroCogMap explicitly targets the mesoscopic "system" scale.
- **Rigor.** Strong on surface: multiple model families (Gemma2-2B / 9B-IT, Llama-3.1-8B), paired non-parametric tests with Holm or BH correction, 95% CIs, controlled synthetic training experiments at three scales, and a blinded human audit of LLM-assisted components. The hierarchy prerequisite experiment is the strongest causal leg. Less clear: how robust the 270-parcel granularity is to SAE training variations (different SAE seeds, different dictionary sizes, different layers), how stable the cross-model alignment is when scaling to >10B models or to non-Gemma/Llama families, and how sensitive the detection AUROCs are to threshold choices and steering strengths.
- **Reproducibility.** Moderate-to-good. SAE features, parcellation, structural-connectome formula, capability list and hierarchy assignments are described precisely. Several judgment steps (parcel description, cross-model matching, pathology labelling, cognitive-model synthesis) rely on GPT-OSS-20B served locally; reproducibility therefore depends on running the same open-weight model. Closed-weight use is limited to GPT-5.2 for the discovery reasoning agent. The cortex prediction depends on a single fMRI dataset (LeBel) — generalization to other naturalistic-listening datasets is not yet shown.
- **Significance.** If the cross-model "cognitive skeleton" claim and the cortex-alignment gains replicate, this is a substantive step toward an explicit, testable cognitive atlas for LLMs and a defensible scientific bridge between AI interpretability and human cognitive neuroscience. The pathology reframing (representational vs. behavioral-control) is potentially the most operationally useful piece: it gives a vocabulary for *why* a given failure happens rather than only *that* it happens.
- **Red flags.**
  1. The cross-model alignment is described as "partially conserved" — the abstract and Discussion are appropriately hedged, but the strength of the conserved "cognitive skeleton" beyond Gemma / Llama families is unknown.
  2. The "cognitive hierarchy" analogy is loaded; the synthetic training experiment tests prerequisite structure for a narrow two-hop reasoning setup, not for all 45 capabilities.
  3. NeuroCogMap detectors are competitive with — but not always dramatically better than — uncertainty or hidden-probing baselines on hallucination; the large gains are on refusal failure, where behavioral baselines are intrinsically weaker.
  4. Cortex alignment uses one dataset (LeBel) and one atlas (Schaefer 100-parcel + Yeo 7-network); prior brain-encoding work has shown such gains often fail to replicate across datasets, alignment metrics and stimulus materials.
  5. SAE-feature parcellation is sensitive to SAE training choices (dictionary size, l1 coefficient, layer coverage); a sensitivity analysis across SAE variants would strengthen the stability claim.
  6. The "refined Dual-systems model" is evaluated on two related two-step datasets; whether the discovered components (attention-gated lapses, dynamic MB/MF arbitration, history-sensitive perseveration, rule-like switching) generalize to other decision tasks is open.

## Strengths

- Genuinely *system-level* interpretability: not a single circuit or single feature, but a coherent parcellation + atlas + hierarchy that supports prediction, perturbation, detection and intervention.
- Pathology analysis is grounded in causal perturbation (suppressing linked parcels / capabilities produces larger log-probability drops than suppressing controls) and yields an immediately useful representational vs. behavioral-control taxonomy.
- Detection and intervention are derived from the *same* pathology map — no separate behavioral head needed for steering.
- Direct bridge to human neuroscience: cortical BOLD prediction with explicit localization to higher-order association networks, plus Neurosynth-based functional correspondence and description-based RSA.
- Cognitive-model discovery is a creative *reverse* transfer (AI internals → refinement of classical cognitive models), with held-out AIC improvements on two independent two-step datasets.
- Methodology is described in unusual detail (structural connectome formula, embedding/semantic-fusion weights β, steering strengths, audit protocol), which is rare for a 14-author paper of this scope.

## Limitations

- Evaluated only on Gemma2 and Llama-3.1 families; coverage of larger / smaller / multimodal / closed-weight models is not reported.
- Single fMRI dataset (LeBel) for cortex alignment; single parcellation (Schaefer 100-parcel) and Yeo 7-network grouping — robustness across datasets, atlases and alignment metrics is untested.
- SAE-feature sensitivity (dictionary size, l1, layer selection, training seed) is not characterized; the 270-parcel granularity and per-parcel descriptions could shift under different SAE choices.
- LLM-assisted annotation is central to atlas construction; even with a blinded human audit of 60 parcel descriptions and 570 pathology labels, the audit coverage is small relative to the 270-parcel × 45-capability surface.
- Pathology detection gains over strong baselines are modest on hallucination (≈ 0.68–0.84 AUROC) and very large on refusal failure (≈ 0.99); real-world deployment utility depends on inference-time cost (the framework is offline by default).
- Cross-model "cognitive skeleton" claim is partial; how much of the 270-parcel organization is genuinely conserved vs. an artifact of shared training data and architecture is unresolved.
- The refined Dual-systems model has not been tested on tasks outside the two-step family.

## Relevance

- 直接命中 **Hidden States & Interpretability**（SAE 特征级 parcellation → 270 功能 parcel → 四级认知层级）与 **Inference Evaluation**（在 HaluEval、MedHallu、Dolly、NQ-Open、SciQ、TruthfulQA、AdvBench、JBB-Behaviors 等多基准上做 AUROC 检测 + 机制化介入），并把"幻觉 / 偏见 / 拒绝失败 / 谄媚"四类故障统一映射到系统级扰动，与用户关注的核心方向高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How sensitive is the 270-parcel granularity, the per-parcel description, and the cross-model alignment to SAE dictionary size, l1 coefficient and training seed? Can a single NeuroCogMap be reused across SAE checkpoints, or must it be re-fit?
- Does the "cognitive skeleton" remain partially conserved for closed-weight / multimodal / much larger models, or is it largely an artifact of Gemma / Llama pretraining data and architecture?
- Is the AUROC gap over SelfCheckGPT / hidden probing on hallucination large enough to justify the additional inference cost in deployment, or is NeuroCogMap primarily an offline analysis tool?
- Can the representational vs. behavioral-control taxonomy predict failure modes not analyzed here (e.g., jailbreaks, reasoning collapse, mode-collapse, instruction drift) and guide interventions for them?
- Do the cortex-alignment gains replicate on independent naturalistic-listening fMRI datasets (e.g., fMRI of narrative comprehension, Moth Story Corps), and how do they compare under alternative alignment metrics (linear predictivity vs. RSA vs. voxel-wise encoding)?
- Are the discovered Dual-systems extensions (attention-gated lapses, dynamic MB/MF arbitration, history-sensitive perseveration, rule-like switching) necessary, or do simpler behavior-only models with more free parameters match the held-out AIC?
- Can NeuroCogMap be made dynamic / causal — e.g., parcel-level directed interactions during inference — to move from static atlases toward real-time cognitive-state monitoring?
