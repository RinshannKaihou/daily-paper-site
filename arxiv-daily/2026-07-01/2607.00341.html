DiscoLoop: Looping Discrete Embeddings and Continuous Hidden States for Multi-hop Reasoning
Hengyu Fu · Tianyu Guo · Zixuan Wang · Hanlin Zhu · Jason D. Lee · Jiantao Jiao · Stuart Russell · Song Mei
UC Berkeley · Princeton · 2026-07-01

Key Metric · OOD two-hop accuracy

8.3% → ~100%

Vanilla looped Transformer on a symbolic 500-entity two-graph task, after a single training-free realignment intervention (α≈0.5) — and DiscoLoop learns to do it end-to-end.

Why This Matters

Hidden-state ↔ token-embedding misalignment is the real bottleneck of looped Transformers; fixing it is a drop-in, ~d+1-parameter intervention with a 10×+ OOD generalization gain.

Critical Edge

440M × 20B tokens is modest; the +1.2-pt mean LM gain is borderline-robust, and K=4 vanilla loop — the actual pretraining-depth counterfactual — is never tested.

01 · Methodology

Diagnose, then engineer around the bottleneck

Symbolic two-graph task

Two entity-disjoint graphs G_A (ID) and G_B (OOD), |E|=500, |R|=50, out-degree 10. Training: all atomic facts on both + 10K ID two-hop questions from G_A only. OOD test = G_B two-hop — never seen compositionally during training.

Vanilla loop baseline (K=2)

H⁽⁰⁾ = W[x], H⁽ᵏ⁺¹⁾ = f_θ(H⁽ᵏ⁾), logits off H⁽ᴷ⁾. Loop 1 should surface the bridge b; loop 2 should combine b with r₂ to retrieve c.

DiscoLoop recurrence

H⁽ᵏ⁺¹⁾ = f_θ(Ĥ⁽ᵏ⁾), Ĥ⁽ᵏ⁺¹⁾ = H⁽ᵏ⁺¹⁾ + α⁽ᵏ⁾ ⊙ RMSNorm(Φ(H⁽ᵏ⁺¹⁾)), where

Φ(h) = Σᵥ softmax((Wh)/τ)ᵥ · W[v] — the differentiable analogue of hard-substituting W[b_max]. α⁽ᴷ⁻¹⁾ ≡ 0 so the final read stays clean. Top-k=128 truncation preserves essentially all mass at scale. The learnable gate adds only d+1 parameters.

Mechanistic probe (Section 3.2)

Read the LM head off post-loop-1 hidden states at the bridge position; log P(b|H₁⁽¹⁾) and cos(H₁⁽¹⁾, W[b]). The training-free intervention H₁ ← (1−α)H₁ + α·Norm(W[b_max]) at α≈0.5 isolates representation (not capacity) as the bottleneck.

LM pretraining recipe

440M params · 24 layers · d=1024 · K=4 Ouro backbone · 20B tokens of 60% FineWeb-Edu + 40% FineMath-4plus · DeepSeek-V3-0324 tokenizer · AdamW + Muon · lr 3e-3 → 1e-5 · effective batch 128 × seq 8192.

Strengths

What the paper gets right

  • Diagnose-then-prescribe — logit lens identifies the bottleneck; a training-free intervention causally validates it; the architecture is the differentiable version of that intervention.
  • Drop-in simplicity. One operator + one gate, ≈d+1 extra params, no new attention type, no new positional encoding.
  • 10×+ OOD jump (8.3% → ~100%) on the symbolic two-hop task — hard to dismiss as optimization noise.
  • Three-regime coverage: controlled symbolic, semi-realistic NL, real LM pretraining on a math-heavy corpus.
  • Sharper phase transition (~500 vs >2000 epochs) — meaningful wall-clock win.

Limitations

Where the claim stays thin

  • Scale. 440M × 20B is well below the practical LM regime (≥7B × trillions). Behavior at scale is conjectured, not measured.
  • Continual insertion is unaddressed. Can a pretrained non-looped model acquire DiscoLoop by dropping in Φ between layers? Open question, operationally critical.
  • Length generalization. K is fixed at training time; no extrapolation to more hops than K.
  • Mechanism not quantified. No curve of Δ(cos) → Δ(acc); no control that breaks alignment to recover the failure.
  • Statistical reporting. Means across one run, ~1-pt deltas, no seed variance. PonderLM still wins LAMBADA.
  • Missing counterfactual: K=4 vanilla loop (the pretraining-depth match) is never trained, so recurrence-depth gains aren't isolated from discrete-channel gains.

03 · Questions for Further Exploration

Where this line of work could go next

  1. Quantify Δ(cos) → Δ(acc). Sweep the soft-alignment intervention on a continuous α schedule, plot both cos(H₁⁽¹⁾, W[b]) and final accuracy — does every degree of misalignment cost the same accuracy?
  2. Length generalization. Train K=2 on two-hop; can DiscoLoop extrapolate to three-hop questions without retraining? Depth transfer is still open.
  3. Non-bridge position behavior. Suppress Φ at the bridge and amplify elsewhere — is the gain alignment-specific or a broader regularizer?
  4. Continual insertion. Drop Φ(H) into a single mid-network point of a pretrained non-looped model. Does the alignment benefit transfer without 24-layer × K=4 retraining?
  5. Coupling with externalized CoT. Use DiscoLoop as a first-pass verifier whose latent two-hop chains are verbalized for self-consistency reranking — latent + explicit in one loop.
  6. Scaling law of the discrete channel. As vocab / d grow, is the top-k=128 truncation provably sufficient, or does required k grow with vocab?
