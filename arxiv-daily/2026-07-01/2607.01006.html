Understanding Large Language Models

Yannik Keller & Thomas Eisenmann Chapter-style synthesis · no new experiments Frame: Marr's three levels of analysis

A Marr-framed survey that rebuts two anti-anthropomorphism misconceptions with mechanistic evidence: 94% truth-decoding probes on Llama-3-8B layer 12, and a traced Dallas → Texas → Austin symbolic circuit in Claude 3.5 Haiku.

Key Metric
94%
linear-probe accuracy on a held-out lies/truths test using Llama-3-8B layer-12 activations (Bürger et al. 2024) — truth information computed early, then potentially overwritten by training bias or learned "lying" behavior.
Why This Matters

Integrates mechanistic interpretability — neuron activation, linear probes, circuit tracing — with the cognitive-status debate in one place. A unified primer for readers who want mechanism and philosophy side-by-side.

Critical Edge

The anti-reductionist rebuttal selectively cites pro-cognition evidence (instrumental goals, mesa-optimization) and sidesteps the strongest counterexamples — notably systematic benchmark gaming.

Methodology

A structured literature synthesis organized around David Marr's three levels of analysis. No new experiments; the contribution is editorial curation plus a philosophical stance.

1. Frame LLMs through Transformer architecture, attention vs. RNN limitations, BPE tokenization, next-token-prediction, and the modern stack (instruction tuning, RLHF, reasoning fine-tuning, multimodal).

2. Curate emergent-capability studies in three buckets — symbolic reasoning, ToM, deception — explicitly pairing positive results with perturbation-based failure evidence.

3. Map interpretability tools onto Marr's levels: neuron activation (implementation), linear probes (representation), circuit tracing (algorithm), each with quantified metrics.

4. Engage the philosophy literature (Mitchell, Bender, Leslie, Birhane, Placani, Hunger, H. Chen et al.) and rebut two named misconceptions with cited empirical evidence.

Strengths

  • Marr three-level scaffold makes the interpretability taxonomy legible instead of ad-hoc.
  • Balances positive emergent findings with perturbation-based failures (esp. ToM) — neither hype nor dismissal.
  • Quantifies interpretability progress: ρ ≥ 0.8, 94% truth accuracy, named circuits.
  • Philosophical section is concrete: two named, falsifiable misconceptions, each with empirical rebuttal.
  • Names structural reasons interpretability lags (polysemanticity, late-layer drift) rather than treating it as a tooling gap.

Limitations

  • No novel empirical contribution — bounded by the literature it surveys.
  • The anti-reductionist stance is an interpretive choice; opposing readers won't be persuaded.
  • ToM evidence relies on studies with known contamination; the chapter notes this but doesn't quantify the uncertainty.
  • Mechanistic evidence is concentrated on Claude 3.5 Haiku, Llama-3-8B, GPT-2 — cross-family generalization is open.
  • Some primary citations are arXiv preprints / blog threads (Transformer Circuits, Distill) — lower evidential weight than peer review.

Questions for Further Exploration

Falsifiability. Which mechanistic-interpretability findings would actually rule out "mere pattern matching", and which are compatible with both sides?
Operationalizing understanding. Can H. Chen et al. 2026's ability- vs. model-based account be turned into a benchmark rather than a philosophical stance?
ToM perturbations. Are Ullman 2023's failures generalizable, or artifacts of a small prompt set? What does Pi et al. 2025's "world-model" rescue predict?
Truth-probe transfer. Does the Bürger et al. 94% result hold across model families and across lies from hallucination vs. sycophancy vs. deliberate deception?
Deception ablation. What cleanly separates model size, dataset, and RLHF in the GPT-3 → GPT-4 deception jump (Hagendorff 2024)?
Contamination protocols. How much emergent-behavior literature is compromised by training-data leakage, and which protocols suffice to rule it out?
Circuit-tracing scale. Can Lindsey et al. 2025's manual feature/supernode labeling be automated without losing interpretability?
Neuro-MI bridge. What would a productive joint research program between mechanistic interpretability and neuroscience (RSA, MVPA ≈ linear probes) actually require each side to give up?
