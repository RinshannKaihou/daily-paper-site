arXiv 2607.01103 · 2026-07-01 · Medical AI / LLM-as-Judge

An LLM judge can hit the physician κ ceiling and still be clinically dangerous — it abstains on 0% of items where physicians decline on the hard ones.

Clinician-Level Agreement Without Clinical Caution: LLM Evaluator Limits in Medical AI Benchmarking

William Philipp · Finn Fassbender · Thorsten Langer · Martje Pauly · Rebecca Herzog · Alexander Baumann · Markus Hobert · Theresa Paulus  ·  et al. (14 authors · University of Luebeck + 8 German institutions)

一句话总结： MedQADE 是首个标准化的德语开放式临床评测基准，源自 26,598 道同行评审 Ankizin 题、最终采样 3,800 道；最强 LLM 评估器 Gemini 3 Flash 在 leave-one-out 校正后达到 κ = 0.694（医师上限 κ = 0.709），但所有前沿模型弃答率均为 0%，与医师的临床元认知严重脱节。

Headline metric
0%

Frontier LLM annotators abstain on 0 of 3,800 items; physicians scale abstention with difficulty. Sub-10B models reach only 4–6% — still ~10× below physicians.

Why this matters

LLM-judge pipelines for medical AI must enforce lineage-independence and explicit uncertainty quantification. κ-style agreement alone is insufficient — statistical alignment does not imply clinical caution.

Critical edge

"Lineage" bias is confounded with training-data, tokenizer, and RLHF-provider overlap between sibling models — "architectural siblings" share far more than architecture.

Methodology

Source Ankizin v5 — 44,185 Anki flashcards for German 2nd State Medical Examination → filtered to 26,598 single-cloze items → stratified sample of 3,800 (80:20 no-match : exact-match).
Physicians 10 practicing physicians, 9.6 yr avg clinical experience (5–23 yr range). Label Studio custom UI; 200-item core double/triple-annotated; 3,600 split-annotated (2 raters/item); 10th tiebreaker on conflict.
LLM students 5 models: Gemini 2.5 Flash, GPT-5 Nano, Gemma 3 27B, Gemma 3 4B, Qwen3-4B. Role-play + high-stakes-exam persona, T = 0.2 (proprietary APIs or local Ollama).
LLM judges 9 models (the 5 students + 4 extra: Gemini 3 Flash, Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite, GPT-5.4 Nano, GPT-5.4 Mini). T = 0, JSON-schema output, rubric + point-wise prompt mirroring human guidelines.
IRR metrics PA, Cohen's κ, PABAK (mean pairwise across 36 rater pairs), Krippendorff's α; ordinal α on difficulty labels (Easy/Medium/Hard).
Ceiling Leave-one-out physician κ_ceiling = 0.709 (95% CI [0.667, 0.746]) recomputed on the same estimand as LLM judges. 1,000-iteration bootstrap CIs.
Bias decomp. ∆self = (model A's score − S̄_peer) over self-generated answers; ∆family mirrors for sibling-generated answers; S̄_peer = mean of non-sibling annotators.

  • Gemini 3 Flash's κ CI spans ≈0.13 — "ceiling overlap" is only tentative.
  • Sub-10B models still ~10× below physicians on abstention.
  • Ensemble sweep: no odd-sized majority-vote of 9 judges beats standalone Gemini 3 Flash on PA / κ / PABAK.
  • Only Gemini 2.5 Flash + GPT-5 Nano pass the 60% 2nd-state-exam analogue threshold; sub-10B models drop ≈45% on no-lexical-match items.

Strengths

  • First standardized open-response German clinical benchmark — no translation of English MCQ corpora (cf. DeFineMed, GerMedIQ).
  • Three agreement metrics (PA, κ, PABAK) plus proper leave-one-out physician ceiling with bootstrap CIs — among the cleanest LLM-judge statistical designs in medical AI.
  • Behavioural probe (abstention) + bias decomposition (∆self, ∆family) is both rigorous and clinically meaningful.
  • Sibling-isolating bias formulation subtracts independent peer consensus — separates evaluator preference from baseline student competence.
  • Negative ensemble result is genuinely counter-intuitive and useful for practitioners.
  • Full public release of MedQADE + physician annotations offsets a major reproducibility concern.
  • Anchored at University of Luebeck; 10 practicing physicians with documented 9.6 yr avg experience outnumber NLP-only contributors.

Limitations

  • κ CIs remain wide (~0.13 for Gemini 3 Flash); the "matches physician ceiling" framing is plausibly true but not strong.
  • Exact prompt templates, snapshot versions, and tiebreaker procedure are not yet public — replication hinges on the eventual release.
  • Only non-flagship / lite / sub-10B variants per lineage are tested; largest available frontier models excluded.
  • Lineage bias is confounded with training-data, tokenizer, and RLHF-provider overlap between siblings — "architectural" is a loose label.
  • Physician ordinal α on difficulty labels = 0.199 — difficulty bins themselves are noisy, blurring the abstention-scaling claim.
  • No contamination probe (membership-inference or held-out rephrasing) for public Ankizin flashcards against frontier training corpora.
  • Frontier abstention = 0% may be partly a prompt-encoding artefact; physicians are explicitly instructed to abstain.
  • Findings tested only on German single-cloze flashcards — generalization to English/Chinese and to free-text clinical genres (discharge letters, differential-diagnosis vignettes) is open.

Questions for further exploration

  • Does the abstain-by-difficulty gap close under explicit uncertainty elicitation, self-consistency, or chain-of-thought prompting?
  • Can lineage bias be attenuated by rubric-adherence training with held-out physicians and per-lineage prompt variants — or is it fundamentally architectural / RLHF-driven?
  • Is Gemma 3 4B's +16% ∆self a low-accuracy artefact (any score above noise reads as self-enhancement), or does it persist under a calibration-matched baseline?
  • Do contamination probes — held-out rephrased Ankizin items, membership-inference attacks — show whether frontier κ inflation is partly memorization?
  • Does forcing frontier models to abstain on hard items also reduce intra-family bias, or are abstention and self-bias orthogonal?
  • Do the abstention and bias patterns replicate in non-cloze clinical tasks (e.g., differential diagnosis from free-text vignettes)?
  • Do findings transfer to languages with native clinical evaluation infrastructure — English MedQA / MedMCQA, Chinese CMB?
  • Is the ensemble's failure a property of the clinical domain (where expertise is non-additive) or a general property of LLM-judge panels?
