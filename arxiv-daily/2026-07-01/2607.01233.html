Key metric — bridge opportunity share
47–64%
of LLM ideas pick a bridge opportunity, vs just 12.1% for humans — a 4–5× over-production. Synthesis methods: 22.5–38.7% vs human 5.1%.
Why this matters

The most serious "is the AI scientist like a human researcher?" audit yet — 11,683 real papers × 9 LLMs show ideation lags humans by an order of magnitude on diversity and taste, and reasoning worsens it.

Critical edge

The "likely-inspiring" prior works are LLM-reverse-engineered; wrong retrieval contaminates the very comparison. And it is one-shot, STEM-only — interactive agents, RAG, and multi-turn critique are untested.

Methodology — literature-grounded ideation

The setup

11,683 published papers→ reverse-engineer 4–8 prior works→ titles + abstracts only→ prompt 9 LLMs for a new idea

Anchoring the human idea (the paper's real contribution) and every LLM idea on the same local literature context removes topic-choice and format confounds that plague open-ended ideation studies. Sources: ICLR/ICML/NeurIPS 2023–2026 + Nature Communications 2023–2025, 71 disciplines.

Nine models: Claude-Sonnet-4.6, Gemini-3.1-Pro, GPT-OSS-20B/120B, GPT-5.4-mini, Qwen3-8B/32B, DeepSeek-V4-Flash/Pro.

Two-axis research-taste taxonomy

Derived from NSF / NIH / AHRQ / DARPA proposal guidance, refined on 150 hold-out papers into a 7×7 grid:

  • Opportunity Pattern (motivation, 7): Puzzle/Contradiction · Explanation Gap · Scope Mismatch · Evidence Gap · Bridge · Failure/Risk · Resource Bottleneck
  • Method Paradigm (method, 7): Synthesis/Unification · Relax/Extend Scope · Robustification · Formal Derivation · Empirical Mapping · Artifact/System · Optimization/Search
  • Annotator: GPT-5.4-mini, validated at Cohen's κ = 0.84 / 0.81 / 0.93 vs two human authors.
  • Metrics: Total Variation Distance, Jensen-Shannon Divergence, normalized entropy — recasting "good idea?" as "is the idea distribution human-shaped?"

Mechanism triangulation (Fig. 4)

  • Archetype operations: LLMs integrate (34.2% vs human 2.35%, log-odds 3.07), unify, merge, adapt. Humans replace (83.3%), decouple (85.4%), formalize.
  • Same-paper geometry: Qwen3-8B ↔ DeepSeek cosine 0.83 > human–model 0.72–0.78 — distinct families converge on each other, not on humans.
  • Concept enrichment: LLMs favor high-frequency motifs (multi-omics, diffusion policy, multimodal, in-context learning); humans favor narrow mechanisms (trajectories, ligands, tokenization, equivariance, entropy/MI).
  • Full-context ablation: feeding full-paper summaries raises TVD/JSD — the gap is not information scarcity.

Strengths vs limitations

Strengths

  • Reframes the right question: ideation as distributional alignment, not a per-idea leaderboard.
  • Taxonomy grounded in real funding-agency proposal practice, cross-domain validated.
  • Scale — 11k papers, 9 LLMs, 71 disciplines — yields model-family-agnostic evidence.
  • Three independent mechanism analyses converge on one coherent story (rare triangulation).
  • Thinking-mode ablation has immediate practical value for AI-scientist designers.
  • Full-context ablation pre-empts the "LLMs need more info" rebuttal.

Limitations

  • STEM-only coverage — social science, humanities, clinical, engineering out of scope; taste may differ there.
  • Stimulus is local + sparse (title+abstract, 4–8 works) — misses tacit expertise, failures, reviewer feedback.
  • 7×7 taxonomy compression forces multi-paradigm proposals into one primary label.
  • Reverse-engineered inspiring works are LLM-identified and may be inaccurate.
  • One-shot setting — interactive agents, RAG, multi-agent debate untested.
  • Annotator (GPT-5.4-mini) shares lineage with evaluated models — residual bias risk.

Questions for further exploration

Q1Can the taste taxonomy become an RL/DPO reward — optimizing coverage of opportunity × paradigm cells rather than per-idea novelty?
Q2Does the gap shrink, persist, or invert under interactive ideation (multi-turn critique, human-in-the-loop) vs one-shot?
Q3Why does reasoning amplify the template — RLHF preference collapse, or an emergent effect of longer chain-of-thought?
Q4Does the gap shrink in social-science / clinical / theory-heavy domains where bridge-and-synthesis is less dominant?
Q5Are human-enriched narrow-mechanism moves (replace, decouple, formalize) the natural anti-template targets?
Q6Could a multi-agent pipeline — agents assigned to different taxonomy cells — broaden the joint distribution toward human taste?
