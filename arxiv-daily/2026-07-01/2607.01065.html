GSRQ: Gain–Shape Residual Quantization for Sub-1-bit KV Cache

Soosung Kim, Minjae Park (equal), Eui-Young Chung, Jaeyong Chung  ·  Yonsei University · Correspondence: Jaeyong Chung

⌁ Key finding

Replacing ℓ2 K-means with Gain–Shape K-means inside residual VQ fixes centroid shrinkage and lifts 1-bit LongBench from 11.34 → 33.54.

Why this matters

Sub-1-bit KV cache was the wall blocking long-context LLM serving. GSRQ rewrites the geometry so it stays usable — without retraining.

0.75-bit decoding reaches 3.40× speedup at 64K context (A100), and FP16 OOMs at 128K where GSRQ still runs.
Critical edge

GSKM has no monotonic-decrease guarantee — it's not a block-coordinate descent, so don't assume K-means-style convergence theory.

Head-to-head against newer saliency-aware / rotated methods (ZipCache, KVQuant, RotateKV) is missing.

01Methodology

Diagnose why ℓ2 K-means collapses in the under-capacity regime, then decouple gain and shape to recover angular fidelity.

‖x − μ‖² = ‖x‖² + ‖μ‖² − 2‖x‖‖μ‖ cos θ

The shrinkage trap. Under Lloyd averaging in high-D subspaces, samples assigned to a centroid are nearly orthogonal → mean cancellation shrinks ‖μ*‖₂, which weakens the angular term's sensitivity to cos θ → worse direction estimates next round. Values shrink more than keys; residuals shrink more than originals.

GSKM parameterization.

  • μk = gk sk, with gk ≥ 0 and ‖sk‖ = 1.
  • Assignment: arg maxk ( 2gk xi⊤ sk − gk² )
  • Shape update: sk ← normalize( Σ yi ), where yi = xi / ‖xi‖ — closed-form max of Σ yi⊤ s.
  • Gain update: gk ← max(0, meani∈Iₖ xi⊤ sk).

Log-smoothed gradient weighting. Raw ‖∇xᵢL‖₂ is heavy-tailed (max/median > 400×). GSRQ applies wi = log(1 + λ ‖∇xᵢL‖₂) with λ chosen so median = τ = 1.0 — compressing max/median to < 10× and trimming another 0.28 PPL.

Pipeline. PQ splits each head-concatenated KV vector into M subspaces (dim D); RQ runs R residual stages with K = 256. Bit rate b = R log₂K / D. Custom Triton kernel fuses bit-pack + unpack + dequant + RoPE + attention — no FP16 materialization. Calibrated on 128 × 2048 SlimPajama sequences.

03Strengths vs Limitations

+Strengths

  • Mechanistic diagnosis, not a benchmark chase — a concrete geometric cause of a known failure mode.
  • Algorithmic simplicity. Same complexity as K-means, no new hyperparameters, drop-in replacement.
  • Robust weighting. Log-smoothed gradient scheme is well-motivated (max/median 400× → < 10×) and ablated.
  • Comprehensive eval. 4 models × 4 bit rates × long-context + reasoning + PPL.
  • Real systems wins. Custom Triton kernels; FP16 OOMs at 128K, GSRQ still serves.

−Limitations

  • Single-vector quantization only — KV cache, not weights or activations.
  • Headline regime is sub-1-bit. At 2-bit, gains over VQLLM shrink and CQ/AnTKV remain competitive.
  • Codebook overhead unisolated. "BPA" is payload only; tables/scales/indices cost not quantified.
  • Narrow comparison set — excludes ZipCache, KVQuant, RotateKV at sub-1-bit.
  • No convergence guarantee, no formal rate-distortion analysis, only dense decoders tested.

04Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does GSRQ compose with KV eviction / sparsification (H2O, Scissorhands, StreamingLLM) and saliency-aware scalar quantizers — can GSKM codebooks be applied only to the non-salient subset?
  2. Does the GSKM advantage persist on MoE / hybrid / SSM backbones (Mixtral, DeepSeek-MoE, Mamba-2, Jamba) where KV geometry differs?
  3. What's the metadata cost per token (codebooks + scales + indices), and at what bit rate does GSRQ lose its storage advantage vs 2-bit scalar KV quantization?
  4. How sensitive is GSRQ to the calibration distribution (code, math, multilingual), and is online / continual re-calibration feasible at serving time?
  5. Can the gain/shape decomposition transfer to weight quantization (channel-scale outliers) and activation quantization (GeLU/attention outliers) — does the same shrinkage mechanism explain failures there?
  6. Is there a formal convergence or fixed-point analysis for GSKM — e.g. as an EM step on a spherical mixture with a separate gain posterior?
  7. How does GSRQ interact with GQA / MQA / sliding-window attention, where KV tensors are partitioned by group?
  8. Can log-smoothed weighting be replaced by Huberized gradient norms or clipped reweighted medians without losing the 0.28 PPL gap?
  9. What are throughput-per-watt numbers when GSRQ pairs with INT4 / FP8 weights, to fully exploit the memory headroom?
  10. Is the GSKM fixed-point closer to spherical K-means or standard K-means in the high-capacity regime — and is there a smooth α-blended interpolation that recovers both?
