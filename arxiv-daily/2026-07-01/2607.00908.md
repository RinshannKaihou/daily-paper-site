> **一句话总结：** TASA 通过系统实验揭示了"困惑度错觉"(PPL 敏感度与推理敏感度 Kendall τ ≈ 0) 和"对齐-多样性权衡"(纯任务校准反而损害量化质量)，并提出一个两层框架：用训练自由的梯度追踪余弦对齐自动搜索最优校准配比，再用多目标聚合 (MOA) 敏感度 + 整数线性规划完成层间/层内比特分配；最终在 LLaMA-3-8B / Qwen2.5-7B 上以平均 3.5 bit 达到甚至超越 4 bit 均匀基线，并在 GSM8K 上较最强 W3 基线提升超过 20 个绝对点。

> **TL;DR:** This paper challenges two standard assumptions in LLM mixed-precision quantization (MPQ): (1) perplexity (PPL) sensitivity is a faithful proxy for downstream capability — empirically false, with Kendall τ ≈ 0 between PPL and reasoning (GSM8K) layer rankings; and (2) pure target-task calibration data is best — also false, because pure task data induces a degenerate Hessian eigenspectrum. The proposed TASA framework combines (a) a training-free gradient-trace cosine-alignment search over calibration-mixture ratios, (b) a Multi-Objective Aggregation (MOA) sensitivity score that fuses PPL and Math cross-entropy losses, and (c) an exact dynamic-programming/ILP solver for inter-layer allocation plus OBS-based intra-layer group refinement. On LLaMA-3-8B at 3.5 bits it matches several 4-bit uniform baselines while using 12.5% fewer bits, retains 97.2% of FP16 Avg. accuracy, and improves GSM8K over the strongest W3 baseline by >20 absolute points; on Qwen2.5-7B a 3.75-bit TASA retains 99.1% of FP16 accuracy.

## 核心贡献

- **困惑度错觉 (Perplexity Illusion) 的实证与命名**：在 LLaMA-3-8B 和 Qwen2.5-7B 上独立量化每一层 (W3) 并分别用 PPL、GSM8K、ARC-C 三种指标测敏感度，发现 PPL 敏感层集中在输入嵌入与输出投影，而数学/科学推理的敏感层广泛分布在中间块；Kendall 等级相关 τ 接近 0（LLaMA-3 上 PPL vs. Math 的 τ = −0.08, p = 0.53；top-8 重叠仅 2/8，与随机期望一致）。
- **对齐-多样性权衡 (Alignment-Diversity Tradeoff) 的理论与实证**：当校准从纯 WikiText 渐变到纯 GSM8K 时，激活与目标任务的余弦对齐单调上升，但量化后精度呈"中间最高"的非单调曲线（LLaMA-3 α_wiki ≈ 0.50，Qwen2.5 α_wiki ≈ 0.75）；纯任务校准在 LLaMA-3 上使 Avg. 下降 0.6。理论解释：纯任务数据使逐层 Hessian 谱退化（条件数 ×2、有效秩 6→3），损害泛化流形。
- **TASA 两层任务感知框架**：
  - **数据轴**：训练自由的"梯度追踪对齐"自动校准——通过单次前向计算各层 ‖X_l‖² 迹向量，在 (|A|+1) 次前向内搜索使迹向量与目标任务余弦相似度最大的混合比 α；无需任何反向/梯度/实际量化。
  - **网络轴**：MOA 敏感度 = β·Δ_math + (1−β)·Δ_PPL (β=0.7) 经层间 ILP（带全局比特预算约束的 0/1 背包结构，由动态规划 O(L·B_total·|B|) 精确求解）+ 层内 OBS-salience 组粒度 (g=128) 二次分配。
- **精度反转 (Precision Inversion) 实证**：在 LLaMA-3-8B 上 3.5-bit TASA (Avg.=68.9) ≈ 多个 4-bit 均匀基线 (RTN W4 67.9, HQQ W4 68.1, AWQ W4 69.4)，并以 12.5% 更少比特保留 FP16 97.2% 的 Avg.；Qwen2.5-7B 3.75-bit (75.0) 超过其 4-bit 均匀基线 (74.7)，保留 FP16 99.1%。在 W3 下差距最大：LLaMA-3 TASA b3.0 GSM8K=39.5，HQQ W3 仅 10.0、RTN W3=0.0，呈现"推理崩溃"的避免能力。

## Key Contributions

- **Empirically formalizes the Perplexity Illusion.** Under W3 per-layer perturbation on LLaMA-3-8B / Qwen2.5-7B, PPL sensitivity is essentially uncorrelated with reasoning sensitivity (Kendall τ ≈ 0; top-8 layer overlap of 2/8, matching the random baseline of 2). PPL-sensitive layers concentrate at input/output projections, whereas reasoning-critical layers are distributed across middle blocks responsible for compositional inference.
- **Reveals and explains the Alignment-Diversity Tradeoff.** Sweeping α_wiki from 0→1 shows activation alignment monotonically rises, yet post-quantization accuracy peaks at an intermediate ratio (α_wiki ≈ 0.50 on LLaMA-3, ≈ 0.75 on Qwen2.5). Eigen-spectral analysis shows pure task data raises per-layer Hessian condition numbers by >2× and drops effective rank from ~6 to ~3, formally captured by Theorem 1 (strict convexity of expected quantization loss under mild Hessian assumptions).
- **Proposes TASA, a two-level task-aware framework.** Training-free gradient-trace cosine-alignment search for calibration mix (only (|A|+1) forward passes with n=16 samples; coarse grid α ∈ {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1} suffices). Multi-Objective Aggregation (MOA) sensitivity profile (PPL + Math cross-entropy) feeds an exact ILP-with-knapsack structure (solved by DP in O(L·B·|B|), < 1s) for inter-layer allocation, with a second-level OBS-salience group-wise refinement (g=128) following SliM-LLM.
- **Demonstrates precision inversion at 3.5 bits.** LLaMA-3-8B TASA b3.5 matches several 4-bit uniform baselines (HQQ W4, RTN W4, AWQ W4) while using 12.5% fewer bits and retaining 97.2% of FP16 accuracy. Qwen2.5-7B TASA b3.75 retains 99.1% of FP16. On GSM8K the gain over the strongest uniform W3 baseline exceeds +20 absolute points, and TASA b3.0 (39.5) avoids the "reasoning collapse" of HQQ/RTN W3 (10.0/0.0).

## Methodology

### Perplexity Illusion analysis
For LLaMA-3-8B (32 layers) and Qwen2.5-7B (28 layers), quantize each layer in isolation to W3 while keeping all other layers FP16. Compute per-layer sensitivity as relative degradation under three independent metrics: PPL on WikiText-2, conditional cross-entropy on GSM8K (avoiding autoregressive generation), and conditional cross-entropy on ARC-Challenge. Compare pairwise Kendall τ between the three sensitivity rankings; the overlap of top-8 layers per metric is also compared to the random-expectation baseline (K²/L = 2).

### Alignment-Diversity Tradeoff characterization
Sweep calibration mixture α_wiki ∈ {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1} between WikiText-2 (general) and GSM8K (task) at fixed W3 quantization, recording both activation-trace cosine alignment with the target task and post-quantization Avg. accuracy. Eigen-spectral analysis of per-layer Hessian H_l = X_l⊤ X_l on Layer 12 / Layer 22 / Layer 31 quantifies condition number κ and effective rank under each mixture.

### TASA — Gradient-Trace Auto-Calibration
For each candidate α, run a single forward pass with n=16 samples; collect per-layer traces tr(H_l) = ‖X_l‖²_F (avoiding d×d Hessian construction) into vector h(D). Pick α* maximizing cos(h(D_mix(α)), h(D_task)) on the coarse grid {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1}. Reference trace is a multi-task mixture of GSM8K + ARC-Challenge. (Note: single-task targeting can overfit — see Limitations.)

### TASA — MOA Inter-Layer ILP
For each (layer l, bit-width b ∈ {3,4,8}) profile Δ^(k)_l(b) for k ∈ {ppl, math} (ARC is used in analysis but not in the objective since Math/ARC rankings overlap). Composite MOA score: S_MOA(l,b) = β·Δ_math_l(b) + (1−β)·Δ_PPL_l(b) with β=0.7 (default). Solve min Σ_l S_MOA(l, b_l) s.t. (1/L)·Σ_l b_l ≤ B, b_l ∈ B via dynamic programming (knapsack structure → exact solution in O(L·B_total·|B|) time, < 1 s).

### TASA — Intra-Layer Group-Wise Refinement
Following SliM-LLM, apply GPTQ with OBS-salience scoring on weight groups of size g=128; each group is assigned one of {b_l−1, b_l, b_l+1} subject to group-level average matching layer budget b_l. Lets a 4-bit layer contain 3- and 5-bit groups.

### Cost
TASA-specific offline pipeline on a single A100 for LLaMA-3-8B: ~47 min one-time (auto-calibration ~4 min, MOA profiling ~43 min, ILP < 1 s). Re-running at a new global bit budget requires only re-solving the ILP.

## Results & Highlights

### Headline numbers (LLaMA-3-8B, 3.5-bit TASA)
- Avg. = **68.9** vs. FP16 70.9 (97.2% retention); matches HQQ W4 (68.1), RTN W4 (67.9), AWQ W4 (69.4) at 12.5% fewer bits.
- **GSM8K = 46.2** vs. HQQ W4 39.4 (+6.8), RTN W4 36.2 (+10.0), vs. strongest W3 baseline (OWQ W3 19.2) **+23+**; per paper, ">20 absolute points" over the strongest W3.
- Per-task profile: ARC-C 50.0, HellaSwag 76.9, WinoGrande 73.6, PIQA 78.6, ARC-E 77.7, BoolQ 79.6, PPL 8.72.

### Qwen2.5-7B, 3.75-bit TASA
- Avg. = **75.0** vs. FP16 75.7 (99.1%); exceeds HQQ W4 (74.7), AWQ W4 (74.1) at 6.25% fewer bits. ARC-C even exceeds FP16 (53.9 vs. 51.1), hinting at an implicit regularization effect.

### W3 dominance (LLaMA-3-8B TASA b3.0)
- Avg. 66.4, GSM8K 39.5; HQQ W3 Avg. 55.5/GSM8K 10.0, OWQ W3 59.5/19.2, RTN W3 47.3/0.0. SpQR "W3" Avg. 68.0 but effective bit-width 4.12 due to FP16 outlier overhead.

### Allocation strategy ablation (Tab. 2, LLaMA-3, b3.5)
- MOA + mixed: 68.9 (best)
- MOA + wiki only: 66.8 (−2.1)
- KBA-Math + mixed: 68.1 (−0.8)
- PPL-topK + wiki: 63.3 (−5.6 vs. MOA)
- Uniform + mixed: 66.9 (−2.0)
- Random + wiki (3 seeds): 65.4 ±2.3

→ The allocation strategy is the dominant factor (+5.6 over PPL-only); mixed calibration adds a consistent additive boost (+2.1 under MOA, +5.1 under Uniform allocation — a substitution effect).

### Mix-ratio ablation (Tab. 3)
- LLaMA-3: peak Avg. at α_wiki = 0.50 (68.9); pure math (α=0) 68.3, pure wiki (α=1) 66.8 — confirms intermediate optimum.
- Qwen2.5: peak at α_wiki = 0.75 (74.3); α=0 and α=0.5 tied at 74.1; α=1.0 drops to 72.5.

### Stability
- Across 3 calibration seeds: LLaMA-3 b3.5 Avg.=68.6±0.3, GSM8K=45.5±0.8; Qwen2.5 b3.75 Avg.=74.3±0.5 — small variance.
- MOA weight β: stable across β ∈ [0.5, 0.9] (Avg. 68.4–68.9), default β=0.7 wins narrowly — paper notes structural inclusion of task-aware signal matters more than exact weight tuning.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: high.** Two named, empirically grounded phenomena — Perplexity Illusion and Alignment-Diversity Tradeoff — go beyond an "incremental MPQ method" contribution. Reframing PPL sensitivity as a misleading proxy is a conceptual contribution that should change how practitioners think about sensitivity analysis. The Hessian-spectrum explanation of the diversity effect (κ↑ >2×, ER 6→3) is mechanistic, not just empirical.
- **Rigor: strong.** (i) Layer-wise isolation under FP16-elsewhere quantization gives clean attribution. (ii) Three independent sensitivity metrics, Kendall τ with p-values, and overlap-vs-random-baseline tests. (iii) Factorial ablation cleanly disentangles allocation strategy vs. calibration data contribution. (iv) Multi-seed variance reported. (v) Seven competitive baselines covering four MPQ paradigms. (vi) A formal convexity theorem (Theorem 1) backs the non-monotonic tradeoff.
- **Reproducibility: strong.** Public code, public datasets (WikiText-2, GSM8K, ARC, etc.), lm-evaluation-harness pipeline, well-documented β and α_wiki defaults. Offline cost ~47 min on a single A100 is feasible for most labs.
- **Significance: meaningful.** Targets a real deployment bottleneck (sub-4-bit LLM inference with preserved reasoning). The "precision inversion" framing — fewer bits beating more bits — is appealing and addresses the well-documented reasoning-collapse failure mode at W3 (Zhou et al. 2026's "signal degradation" / "computation collapse"). The Alignment-Diversity insight also informs adjacent areas (pruning, KV-cache quantization).
- **Red flags / caveats.**
  - Only two model families tested (LLaMA-3-8B, Qwen2.5-7B); scaling to 70B+ models is not validated. The paper acknowledges this in Limitations.
  - β=0.7 default is hand-tuned (though the β-ablation shows flatness — main effect is structural inclusion of Math signal, not weight tuning).
  - The trace-alignment proxy can over-align under single-task targeting (e.g., LLaMA-3 GSM8K-only recommends α=0.0, but Avg. peaks at α=0.5). The authors flag this as a known limitation (Sec. B.15) and mitigate it by using a multi-task reference trace — but the failure mode is real.
  - Heterogeneous bit patterns are not natively supported by all inference kernels; deployment depends on MLC-LLM / vLLM mixed-precision decoding support.
  - "Reasoning" is dominated by GSM8K (8-shot CoT); broader reasoning suites (MATH, AIME, BBH-hard, LiveCodeBench) are not reported at the abstract/intro level, limiting generalization claims.
  - The ARC sensitivity metric is profiled but not included in the MOA objective (only Math is) — paper justifies this by Math/ARC ranking overlap, but this is a design choice the user should verify.
  - The intra-layer OBS refinement inherits SliM-LLM's complexity and may interact with group granularity g=128.

## Strengths

- Identifies a counterintuitive, well-quantified phenomenon (Perplexity Illusion) that should change how practitioners design sensitivity metrics for reasoning-critical deployments.
- Names and mechanistically explains the alignment-diversity tradeoff with Hessian spectrum analysis, providing actionable intuition for calibration-data construction.
- Achieves large empirical gains (GSM8K +20 over strongest W3; matches 4-bit at 3.5-bit) at a meaningful efficiency frontier, with full Pareto curves from b3.0 to b4.0.
- Architecture-agnostic across LLaMA-3 and Qwen2.5, training-free (no QAT), and self-contained with public code.
- Strong ablation discipline: factorial decomposition of allocation vs. calibration, mix-ratio sweep, multi-seed std, β sensitivity, single-task vs. multi-task reference trace — substantially above the typical MPQ paper.
- Aggregate reasoning-accuracy figures show graceful degradation on commonsense tasks while reasoning tasks gain most — a desirable profile.

## Limitations

- Evaluated on only two 7–8B models; generalization to 70B+ scales, MoE models (the paper itself notes MoE quantization as related work, e.g., GEMQ), and other families is not demonstrated. Distributed MOA profiling at scale is unaddressed.
- Reasoning benchmark coverage leans on GSM8K; MATH, AIME, BBH-hard, theorem proving, and code reasoning are not reported at the headline-result level (per appendix, full results in Sec. B.2).
- MOA uses only Math (GSM8K) as the task-specific objective; ARC is profiled but excluded. Adding more tasks (e.g., code, multilingual) would require careful weighting.
- Auto-calibration searches a discrete grid; continuous relaxation might find finer optima. The trace-alignment proxy over-aligns under extreme single-task targeting (Sec. B.15).
- Heterogeneous bit patterns require inference-kernel support (MLC-LLM, vLLM); not a quantization-only deployment story.
- Calibration-data mixture is two-domain (WikiText + one task); multi-domain or domain-cluster mixtures are unexplored.
- Group granularity g=128 is inherited from SliM-LLM without sensitivity analysis; smaller g might help at sub-3-bit.
- β=0.7 default is heuristic; β-ablation shows flatness but does not validate generalizing β across model sizes.

## Relevance

TASA 直接针对 LLM 推理部署中的量化可靠性问题——揭示困惑度作为敏感度代理在推理任务上的失效、给出训练自由且可工程化的校准与比特分配方案，并通过大幅避免 W3 推理崩溃现象，对关注**推理评估、计算精度与可靠性、以及高效部署**的研究者具有较高参考价值；其对齐-多样性思路也可迁移到 KV-cache 量化、剪枝与多语种量化场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the Perplexity Illusion hold on larger / MoE / long-context models, and on harder reasoning suites (MATH, AIME, BBH-hard, LiveCodeBench)?
- Can the gradient-trace alignment be replaced by an intra-layer spectral-aware proxy that would also detect Hessian degeneracy (addressing the single-task over-alignment failure mode)?
- How does TASA's allocation compare head-to-head with GPTQ, AWQ, SqueezeLLM, FlatQuant, and QuIP# at matched bit budgets, not just uniform-precision baselines?
- Does the Alignment-Diversity principle transfer to **KV-cache quantization** and **weight-activation joint quantization**, where calibration data effects are even more pronounced?
- Can MOA be extended to a Pareto-front formulation (PPL vs. Math vs. Code vs. Multilingual) so the ILP can be solved under multi-task budget trade-offs without a single β knob?
- What is the minimum general-domain corpus size needed for the diversity effect to manifest, and does it depend on the target task domain?
- Does integrating QAT (light LoRA fine-tuning) with TASA's bit plan recover accuracy at sub-3-bit regimes (b2.5 shows a steep drop in Tab. 17)?
- How sensitive is TASA to calibration set size (n) and sequence length (seqlen=2048 default), and is there a scaling law for the auto-calibration search cost vs. model size?
