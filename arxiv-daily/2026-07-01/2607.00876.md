> **一句话总结：** 本文证明了对长度为 $n$ 的二元流进行差分隐私连续计数时，任何 $(\epsilon,\delta)$-DP 机制的期望 $\ell_\infty$ 误差必然达到 $\Omega(\log^{3/2} n / \max\{\epsilon,\delta\})$，因此二元树机制的 Gaussian 噪声变体在近似 DP 设置下关于流长 $n$ 是渐近最优的；并由此得到 hereditary discrepancy 与私有 $\ell_\infty$ 误差之间一个最大可达的 $\log^{3/2} m$ 分离。

> **TL;DR:** This paper closes a long-standing open problem in differential privacy by proving a tight lower bound of $\Omega(\log^{3/2} n / \max\{\epsilon,\delta\})$ on the expected $\ell_\infty$ error of any $(\epsilon,\delta)$-DP mechanism for private continual counting on a binary stream of length $n$. The bound matches the binary tree mechanism's Gaussian-noise upper bound up to constants, establishing its asymptotic optimality in the approximate-DP regime. A corollary shows that the general hereditary-discrepancy upper bound for DP linear queries is tight in its $\log m$ factor — the largest possible separation is now a $\log^{3/2} m$ gap rather than the previously known $\log m$.

## 核心贡献

- 证明 $(\epsilon,\delta)$-DP 连续计数的期望 $\ell_\infty$ 误差下界 $\Omega(\log^{3/2} n / \max\{\epsilon,\delta\})$，与二元树机制 [JRSS23] 的 $O(\log^{3/2} n \cdot \log^{1/2}(1/\delta)/\epsilon)$ 上界匹配到 $\epsilon,\delta$ 因子以内。
- 解决差分隐私领域的一个核心开放问题：确认二元树机制在近似 DP 设定中关于流长 $n$ 是渐近最优的；并在 $\delta=0$ 时将纯 DP 的下界从 $\Omega(\log n / \epsilon)$ [DNPR10] 推进到 $\Omega(\log^{3/2} n / \epsilon)$。
- 给出 hereditary discrepancy 与 $(\epsilon,\delta)$-DP 线性查询 $\ell_\infty$ 误差之间最大可达的分离：herdisc$_\infty(A) / \log m \le \text{err}^\epsilon_{\infty,\delta}(A) \le \text{herdisc}_\infty(A) \cdot \log^{3/2} m$，说明 [NTZ13/MNT20] 的 $\gamma_2$ 因子框架给出的一般上界已经紧到对 $m$ 的依赖。

## Key Contributions

- **Tight lower bound for approximate-DP continual counting.** For any $0<\epsilon<1$ and a sufficiently small constant $C>0$, any $(\epsilon,\delta)$-DP mechanism $M$ satisfies
  $\max_{x \in \{0,1\}^n} \mathbb{E}[\|M(x)-Ax\|_\infty] \;=\; \Omega(\log^{3/2} n / \max\{\epsilon,\delta\})$,
  where $A$ is the $n\times n$ prefix-sum matrix.
- **Tightens the pure-DP lower bound** from $\Omega(\log n / \epsilon)$ [DNPR10] to $\Omega(\log^{3/2} n / \epsilon)$ (the $\delta=0$ special case of Theorem 1), closing a multiplicative $\log n$ gap with the [JRSS23] upper bound.
- **Largest-possible hereditary discrepancy separation.** Because $\text{herdisc}_\infty(A)=1$ for the prefix-sum matrix, Theorem 1 implies the general [MNT20] upper bound $\text{err}^\epsilon_{\infty,\delta}(A)=O_{\epsilon,\delta}(\log^{3/2} m \cdot \text{herdisc}_\infty(A))$ cannot be improved in its dependence on $m$. Prior to this work the largest separation was only a $\log m$ factor.
- **A reusable proof technique.** Two-stage argument: (i) re-expand the noise vector $z=M(x)-Ax$ in a binary-tree basis (root and right-child nodes) so the $\ell_\infty$ error becomes a *path-sum* of residual noise coefficients along each root-to-leaf path; (ii) a privacy-based linear-measurement adversary that, via the Ganapathy–Venkatasubramanian [GV16] lower bound on scalar DP estimates, accumulates residual noise bottom-up with an inductive argument that the dynamic range $\max_i z_i - \min_i z_i$ grows with tree depth.

## Methodology

The proof has two cleanly separated halves, after one structural simplification (obliviousness):

1. **Oblivious-mechanism reduction.** Following [BDKT12], it suffices to prove the lower bound for *oblivious* mechanisms — those of the form $M(x)=Ax+z$ for an input-independent noise vector $z$ (correlations between coordinates allowed). This lifts the problem from arbitrary algorithms to bounding $\mathbb{E}[\|z\|_\infty]$.

2. **Tree-basis decomposition (Part 1).** Assume $n=2^h$. For a complete binary tree $T$ of height $h$, define a basis $\{\chi_u\}_{u \in V(T)}$ by taking the $n-1$ indicator vectors of leaves in each "root-or-right-child" subtree (with the root included). This basis is non-orthogonal but has the crucial property that each column of the prefix-sum matrix $A$ — i.e. the suffix vector $Ae_i$ — decomposes as $\sum_{u \in I(i)} \chi_u$ over the right-child ancestors of leaf $i$. Hence $z_i = \sum_{u \in \mathrm{Anc}(i) \cap V(T)} b_u$, expressing the released noise at leaf $i$ as a *root-to-leaf sum of tree coefficients* $b_u$.

3. **Residual noise & induction up the tree (Part 2).** For each node $u$, define $z^u_i$ as the contribution to $z_i$ from nodes *strictly below* $u$ in the ancestor set, i.e. the "residual" noise at depth $u$. Define $\max^u(z)=\max_{i\in L(T_u)} z^u_i$ and $\min^u(z)=\min_{i\in L(T_u)} z^u_i$. The core inductive lemma shows that $\mathbb{E}[\max^u(z)-\min^u(z)]$ grows by $\Omega(1/\max\{\epsilon,\delta\})$ at each level of the tree, accumulating to $\Omega(\log^{3/2} n / \max\{\epsilon,\delta\})$ over the $\log n$ levels (with the $3/2$ exponent coming from a $\sqrt{\log n}$ per-level amplification).

4. **Adversary / linear measurements.** The inductive step combines the Ganapathy–Venkatasubramanian lemma ($\mathbb{E}[z^2]=\Omega(\min\{\epsilon^{-2},\delta^{-2}\})$ for any DP scalar estimator of a binary variable) with carefully chosen linear measurements $\psi^i$ that probe the noise in independent directions across *siblings* in the tree, ensuring that contributions from left and right subtrees stay uncorrelated.

## Results & Highlights

- **Theorem 1 (main result).** For $0<\epsilon<1$ and $0<\delta<C$ (small constant $C$), any $(\epsilon,\delta)$-DP mechanism $M$ for continual counting on binary streams of length $n$ satisfies
  $\max_x \mathbb{E}[\|M(x)-Ax\|_\infty] = \Omega\!\left(\log^{3/2} n / \max\{\epsilon,\delta\}\right)$.
- **Corollary 1 (hereditary discrepancy separation).** $\text{err}^\epsilon_{\infty,\delta}(A) = \Theta_{\epsilon,\delta}(\text{herdisc}_\infty(A) \cdot \log^{3/2} m)$ when restricted to the class of $m\times n$ query matrices that contain the prefix-sum matrix as a substructure; in particular the $\log m$ exponent in the [MNT20] upper bound is *tight* and cannot be reduced.
- **Table 1 in the paper** (state of the art after this work):

  | Setting | Error notion | Upper bound | Lower bound |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Pure DP | $\ell_\infty$ | $\log^2 n / \epsilon$ [JRSS23] | $\log^{3/2} n / \epsilon$ [**this paper**] |
  | Approx. DP | $\ell_\infty$ | $\log^{3/2} n \cdot \log^{1/2}(1/\delta)/\epsilon$ [JRSS23] | $\log^{3/2} n / \max\{\epsilon,\delta\}$ [**this paper**] |
  | Approx. DP | $\ell^\infty_2$ | — | $\log n / \epsilon$ [FHU23] |

- The lower bound holds in the offline observation model, which is at least as hard as the continual observation model — so the hardness comes from having to release $n$ private outputs simultaneously, not from online adaptivity.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Extremely high. This resolves an explicit open problem (the $\log^{3/2} n$ exponent of the binary tree mechanism) called out in multiple prior works (e.g., the conclusion of [JRSS23] cites it as the main open question). The exact-match $\Omega$ vs. $O$ pairing is the strongest possible form of a lower bound.
- **Rigor:** The proof is structured as a sequence of reductions plus an inductive tree-accumulation argument that looks carefully engineered — not a one-page trick. Authors are experts in this style of argument. (A full verification would require walking the induction and the linear-measurement construction line-by-line; the abstract + overview are convincing.)
- **Reproducibility:** Theoretical paper — no code or numerical experiments to reproduce; the "artifact" is the proof. Any later attempt to improve the lower bound or extend it (e.g., to $\ell^\infty_2$) would need to engage this framework.
- **Significance:** Definitive answer for the canonical continual-counting problem in approximate DP, plus a sharp hereditary-discrepancy separation that will become the canonical reference in DP linear-query literature. Practitioners designing DP streaming systems can now cite this as justification for adopting the binary tree mechanism rather than searching for alternatives.
- **Red flags / open questions from the paper itself:**
  - The $\ell^\infty_2$ (RMS-style worst-case over streams) row of Table 1 still only carries a $\Omega(\log n / \epsilon)$ [FHU23] lower bound, leaving open whether the binary tree mechanism is also tight for that error notion.
  - The constants behind the $\Omega$ are not explicitly quantified; the proof is asymptotic.
  - The $\epsilon \in (0,1)$ restriction is standard for the [GV16] reduction but means an $\epsilon \ge 1$ bound is not directly obtained.
  - Sparsity (bounded total count $n_e \ll n$) [DNRR15, CLN+24] is *not* addressed here, but is a different regime whose $\log n + \log^2 n_e$ vs. $\log n$ tradeoff is already separately settled.

## Strengths

- Closes a long-standing open problem with a matching bound — the strongest form of a result one can hope for in this area.
- The tree-basis + linear-measurement technique is novel and likely to be reusable for related lower bounds (e.g., $\ell^\infty_2$, decaying sums, distinct elements).
- Clean, separately-stated corollary about the hereditary discrepancy to DP-error gap that improves a previously known factor from $\log m$ to $\log^{3/2} m$.
- Tightens the *pure* DP lower bound as a free side effect.

## Limitations

- Asymptotic in $n$; constants are not extracted and the proof does not yield them. The match with the upper bound is in $n$-dependence, not constants.
- Lower bound is framed in the *offline* observation model; any "continual observation" lower bound follows only because the offline model is at least as hard.
- Restricted to small $\epsilon<1$ and small $\delta<C$; large-$\epsilon$ regimes and high-$\delta$ regimes are not covered.
- Does not address the $\ell^\infty_2$ error notion, the sparse regime ($n_e \ll n$), or continual decaying sums / distinct elements variants (which the authors' related-work section lists as active areas).
- Result is purely *negative*: it proves that no mechanism can beat the binary tree mechanism asymptotically; it does not propose a new algorithm.

## Relevance

与"推理评估 / 隐私保护 ML 可靠性的理论基础 / 计算可靠性"方向部分相关：本文给出的是差分隐私连续计数问题（DP 在线聚合、DP 在线学习、联邦学习等下游应用的基础原语）的渐近最优性证明，因此对 *保护隐私的 ML 训练算法* 设计的理论下限有直接影响。但本项目关注的核心主题（LLM 训练稳定性、隐藏状态可解释性、静默数据损坏等）与其距离较远，更适合作为"基础理论 / 参考文献"型条目收录。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Is the same $\Omega(\log^{3/2} n / \max\{\epsilon,\delta\})$ bound tight for $\ell^\infty_2$ error (RMS over time steps), or can the binary tree mechanism's $\ell^\infty_2$ behaviour be improved?
- What is the explicit constant in the $\Omega$? Can the inductive accumulation be calibrated to match the binary tree mechanism's constants exactly?
- Does the lower bound extend to *release-and-forget* / sliding-window continual counting, where only the last $k$ prefix sums must be accurate?
- Can the tree-basis + linear-measurement technique be lifted to lower bound continual decaying sums ([HUU24]) or continual distinct-element counting ([JKR+23, HSS24, CEM+25])?
- What happens for $n$-ary tree mechanisms with branching factor $b>2$ — does a $b$-ary variant beat the $\log^{3/2} n$ exponent for some range of $b$, or is the binary branching already optimal?
- Does the hereditary-discrepancy / private-$\ell_\infty$ separation sharpen further under additional structural constraints on the query matrix (low rank, Toeplitz, circulant)?
