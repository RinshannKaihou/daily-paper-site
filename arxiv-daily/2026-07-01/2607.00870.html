Dynamic Bidirectional Pattern Memory: A Production-Scale Empirical Characterisation of Inference-Time Gating in Clinical NLP

Ali H. Lazem (corresponding)  ·  William Teahan  ·  Bangor University, UK & University of Thi-Qar, Iraq
Inference-Time Gating Generator-Verifier Pipelines Pattern Memory Clinical NLP Reliability LLM Verification
Key Finding
The most natural "accumulate verifier rejections" gate silently fails at production scale — 785,797 rejections left the relation-extraction blocklist empty, because per-signature reinforcement lost the race against 10-day decay across free-form relation labels.
Key Metric
17.13×
Diagnosis-category path lift under full DBPM — 95.7% coverage unlocks the working signal; isolated, the same category is anti-selective. The ontology filter upstream has to clean the channel before the gate downstream can read it.
Why This Matters
A "configured but empty" blocklist is a real deployment hazard — a pipeline that relies on pattern-memory for safety would ship without that guarantee being enforceable. The paper ships a validator (crosscheck_F1_167k_blocklist.py) so other teams can detect this failure in their own stacks.
Critical Edge
Construct overlap, disclosed in §8.3. The dev-cohort hypothesis test that motivated the NER-coverage gate used the same construct the deployed gate reads — so the headline 1.84 lift is not a held-out estimate. Triangulated across four measurements (1.52 / 1.71 / 1.84 / 1.89), but a fresh-cohort validation is left as future work.

Methodology 方法

Corpus
PMC-Patients — 167,034 narratives (163,400 human + 3,634 veterinary); processed over ~12 days on Supercomputing Wales Falcon (4× H200 141 GB).
Generator · Verifier
G: Llama-3.3 70B (vLLM, TP=2, greedy, bfloat16) · V: MMed-Llama-3.1 70B, categorical verdicts (PASS/FAIL), conservative-pass regime.
Task buckets
11 buckets — 3 DBPM-gated (NER, RE, QAR) + 8 ungated (summarisation, medication, temporal events, risk, …). Concurrency 16 workers × 4 coroutines = 64-way.
Memory formalism
Real-valued Bj(σ,t) + Wj(σ,t) ∈ [0,1]; 4 deployment constraints (O(1) query, bounded memory, clipped values, non-stationary stability via 5/10/10-day half-life).
Held-out cohort
5,000-patient stratified random sample (age × gender × doc-length → 24 strata), seed = 42, identifier list released as ablation_5k_uids.json.
Selectivity metric
lift = P(gate flags | V=FAIL) / P(gate flags | V=PASS); delta-method 95% CI on log(lift); H₀: lift = 1.

Finding 1 — The verifier-fed channel goes empty 生产规模失败

After the full 12-day run, the pattern-memory machinery populated only two of three gated channels:

NER5,860 patterns
→
RE0 patterns
→
QAR11 patterns

Mechanism: per-signature aggregation under (h_type, r, t_type) keys + 10-day wall-clock half-life + prune-on-save below severity 0.01. Free-form relation labels disperse rejections across too many signatures for any one to outrun decay. Generalises: any per-signature memory over free-form LLM output is exposed to the same diffusion.

Finding 2 — A verifier-independent signal fills the gap 本体过滤器

A deterministic predicate on 29 admissible triples + a 32-entry alias map fires before the verifier and populates the same _re_block cache.

Three counts reconcile to one mechanism: concentration on a fixed type-triple alphabet clears threshold where the verifier-fed pathway cannot. No precision or runtime cost: verifier precision-proxy 1.0000 in both arms; wall-time delta 26 s over ~9 h (0.08%).

Finding 3 — Five QA-gate signal sources, four fail by different mechanisms QA 信号源消融

Each failure isolates a structurally distinct reason. Only the NER-coverage signal probes the evidence the verifier itself weighs.

Strengths 优势

What holds up
  • Production-scale evidence — 167k narratives, 1.37M verdict events, 785k relation rejections. Not toy-benchmark numbers.
  • Clean ablation design — five QA-filter variants, each with a structurally distinct failure reason. A model for reporting negative results.
  • Transferable falsifiable principle — "Gate selectivity requires testing the verifier's evidence, not its output." Four negative cases make it testable, not just plausible.
  • Structural guarantee — cross-task-only severity capped at 0.55 < all block thresholds (0.60+). BLOCK requires ≥1 direct verdict. Not a tuned heuristic.
  • Honest measurement discipline — DOWNGRADE-first (tag, don't remove); 7 ablation flags exposed; construct-overlap and per-category instability caveats disclosed.
  • Reproducibility — all code, the seeded 5k cohort, ablation runners, graders, and a blocklist-population validator are released openly.

Limitations 局限

Where it bends
  • Single-corpus evaluation (PMC-Patients, mostly human case reports). EHR validation explicitly future work.
  • Construct overlap in the NER-coverage gate's hypothesis test — dev-cohort lift is not held-out; mitigated by 4-measurement triangulation, not eliminated.
  • No downstream clinical-correctness evaluation. Paper makes no claim that suppressed relations or tagged QA pairs improve dataset quality.
  • Verifier precision-proxy delta = 0 (MMed-Llama conservative-pass). Cannot claim DBPM reduces verifier-measured hallucinations in this regime.
  • Narrow operating envelope. NER-coverage is selective only in mid-spectrum coverage; degenerates under saturation, sparsity, or missing NER buckets.
  • Per-category instability for small nFAIL: diagnosis lift swings between near-zero and 17+.
  • Runtime overhead (0.13%) is below the pipeline's noise floor — no claim of cost saving in DOWNGRADE-first mode.
  • Allow-set dominance (96,708 NER whitelist vs 2,991 blocklist) means the gate's selective effect concentrates on a minority of cases.

Questions for Further Exploration 延伸问题

  1. Does the "gate must test the verifier's evidence" principle transfer to non-clinical domains (legal, code, general QA, scientific extraction, agentic tool use) and to verifier architectures other than MMed-Llama-3.1 70B?
  2. What is the gate's behaviour under distribution shift (PMC case reports → EHR narratives)? Structural expectations under EHR shift are discussed in Appendix A.8 but not tested.
  3. Could a held-out cohort drawn from a population the dev-cohort hypothesis test did not consume validate (or refute) the 1.52 / 1.71 / 1.84 / 1.89 triangulation as a genuine generalisation estimate rather than within-corpus consistency?
  4. For the diagnosis category that swings between anti-selective and lift 17.13, what is the noise floor on per-category lifts for small nFAIL? Could bootstrap CIs replace single-run point estimates?
  5. Could the Ontology-Violation Filter's 29-triple alphabet be extended to broader relation ontologies (e.g., UMLS relations) to broaden the signal source?
  6. Is there a principled way to combine the five failed signal sources' residual information with the NER-coverage signal to recover selectivity in the no-bucket and saturated regimes?
  7. What would schema closure (adding Adverse_Event and Outcome_Mortality NER buckets) yield empirically — would it close the gap between global blended lift (1.52) and path lift (1.84)?
  8. Could the same NER-coverage principle apply at pair level rather than category level — asking whether a specific question template's required entities are present?
  9. Is the 10-day wall-clock half-life too aggressive given PMC-Patients' 12-day full-corpus span? Would a longer half-life (or a reset-on-success rule) have let the verifier-fed RE channel populate at scale without resorting to the ontology signal?
  10. What does the verifier's conservative-pass regime cost in dataset precision that DBPM's ontology signal might recover (if the precision-proxy delta is 0, are there precision floor failures that ontology suppression prevents)?
