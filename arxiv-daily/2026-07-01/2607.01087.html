Cheap code, costly judgment: failures convert into durable governance.

Cheap Code, Costly Judgment: A Case Study on Governable Agentic Software Engineering

James C. Davis, Paschal C. Amusuo, Tanmay Singla, Berk Çakar, Kirsten A. Davis · Purdue University · 2026-07-01

agentic-software-engineering governance-conversion middle-range-theory first-person-case-study engineering-judgment
Key Metric
1.16M LOC of controls

2.75× the production code (238K static analysis · 405K dynamic · 247K docs · 110K agent infra · 162K tooling) — 41 governance mechanisms across 10 families stacked over 12 weeks of agentic building.

Why This Matters

Reframes agentic development as a reliability problem: ex-ante governance alone fails at velocity, so failures must convert into machine-readable controls — supplying the missing middle-range theory for governed agentic workflows.

Critical Edge

The "conversion" loop is powered by an N=1 substrate most teams cannot replicate — a 16-year expert architect with end-to-end authority and a 1.16 MLOC control stack. Without that, the theory may describe conversion's ceiling, not its floor.

Methodology 方法

Interpretive first-person case study (Runeson & Höst guidelines) bounded by a 12-week sustained agentic build. The Subject — a co-author SE professor with 16 years' experience and prior runtime work — built a document-accessibility remediation system almost entirely through Anthropic Claude (Sonnet + Opus, VSCode plugin).

Data triangulation: 88 episode-bounded field notes paired against repository history (18,662 commits, ~3.1 M total LOC, ~10K files). Each episode coded as a critical incident (trigger → inputs → interpretation → response → outcome → downstream effect) through an 11-iteration codebook led by the Subject.

Analytic discipline: LLMs used only as qualitative-analysis aids (note normalization, codebook application, theme surfacing); interpretive authority retained by human authors. Second-author intercoder reliability on a stratified 10-incident re-coding: 10/10 class, 6/7 category, 5/7 on the third layer; second author judged all 10 summaries evidence-supported.

Reflexivity: Four external talks over six weeks used as an articulation discipline — forcing intuitions into explicit constructs. Threats to construct / internal / external validity stated up front; an ethics section acknowledges personal-harm side effects of sustained agentic work.

Two Linked Theses 双命题

The theory rests on two interlocking claims. The first is a claim about where governance must live; the second about where human effort must move:

ThesisClaimModerators
Engineering-governance Under agentic velocity, governance must be relocated into a machine-actionable substrate — or human review becomes the throughput bottleneck. Stronger controls compound velocity rather than tax it. Soft-control saturation at scale; static + dynamic analyses must back the harness.
Human-judgment When implementation is abundant, engineering effort shifts to framing, abstraction discovery, architectural judgment, and governance design. "Now I feel like an engineer, and more than an engineer." Capability-fit (agent capability × human judgment) and authority (ability to alter architecture/controls, not just patch symptoms).

Governance Conversion — The Process Loop 过程模型

Three existing process models are contrasted and found underspecified:

ModelLogicGap
Velocity-centricRoles + soft guardrails (Gas Town, ChatDev)Reliability underspecified
Oversight-centricHuman-in-the-loop reviewHuman attention is the bottleneck
Governance-centric (ex-ante)Known obligation → controlNecessary but not sufficient
Governance conversion (ex-post)Failure → durable control— fills the missing ex-post leg

The loop (non-terminating): velocity exposes failure → human classifies local vs. structural → structural failure converts into governance (probabilistic in agent harness and/or deterministic in type system / static / dynamic analyses) → subsequent agents inherit a narrower action space → governability compounds. Four falsifiable propositions follow: velocity-exposure, soft-control saturation, governance-conversion, and judgment+authority.

Strengths 优势

  • Longitudinal: 12 weeks × 88 notes × 18,662 commits — rare density at a time when most agentic-SE papers are short demos or benchmark sweeps.
  • Reframes the LLM-coding-era problem ("how to keep agentic development governable") rather than rehashing productivity claims.
  • Two-thesis framing is concrete enough to design around — for tool builders (what substrates to ship) and for educators (what to teach).
  • 41-mechanism / 10-family catalog is directly actionable for practitioners running agentic builds.
  • Methodological transparency unusually high: codebook iterations, intercoder agreement, reflexivity pipeline, threats-to-validity, ethics section.
  • "GenAI paradox" — shifts the locus of difficulty from model competence to framing / constraints.
  • "Compression" finding: team-scale coordination collapses into individual workflows under agents, making authority central.

Limitations 局限

  • N=1 single-Subject, single-toolchain (Claude), single-domain (document accessibility), single deployment target — claims beyond this scope are extrapolations.
  • "Costly judgment" is asserted more than measured — no concrete cost-of-judgment accounting per failure class.
  • "Structural failure" / "durable governance" remain process-level constructs with no quantitative thresholds.
  • The 1.16 MLOC substrate is costly to replicate; not every team has a 16-year expert architect.
  • Hardening-stage velocity decline (Fig. 2) is read as governance compounding but could indicate diminishing returns — paper does not adjudicate.
  • Authority moderator invoked but not operationally defined — what "authority to alter the environment" means for orgs with divided ownership is left open.
  • Capability-fit is fragile to model upgrades; no robustness check across GPT, Gemini, or open-source SWE agents.

Questions for Further Exploration 待探索问题

Can the governance-conversion loop be operationalized as observable signals (file-level, PR-level, commit-graph features) that predict when an agentic build is losing governance — before defects escape?
Does the model survive a multi-engineer / multi-team study where authority is divided across ownership, review boards, and deployment gates — or does it require a single architect with end-to-end authority to function?
Is the 1.16 MLOC = 2.75× product ratio a stable constant across domains, or does it depend on agent capability, regulatory pressure, or domain maturity? At what substrate size does compounding velocity start to break down?
How do the four falsifiable propositions (velocity-exposure, soft-control saturation, governance-conversion, judgment+authority) behave in controlled experiments, and what effect sizes emerge?
Does the "GenAI paradox" hold across model families (GPT, Gemini, open-source SWE agents), or is the Subject's experience specific to Claude's failure modes?
What parts of "architectural-contextual judgment" are teachable to non-experts, and which are experience-bound? Directly relevant to the paper's education implication but unstudied within the case.
How does the model extend to non-implementation phases (requirements, design review, maintenance, refactor-for-debt) where the agent is reviewer rather than producer?
Is the layered "belt-and-suspenders" 14-mechanism stack a healthy sign of conversion, or a smell of escalating patch-on-patch complexity that masks underlying architectural debt?
Can the failure-class taxonomy be cross-validated against other long-running agentic builds (Anthropic's C-compiler-with-Parallel-Claudes, Cloudflare's Vinext) to test its generality?
