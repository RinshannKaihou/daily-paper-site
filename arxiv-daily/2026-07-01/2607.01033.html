More realistic training makes models less interpretable — interpretability rankings collapse by 1.2×–20.4× and don't transfer across quirk families, architectures, or methods.

The Model Organism Lottery: Model Organism Interpretability Strongly Depends on Training Methodology

Andrzej Szablewski · Gabriel Konar-Steenberg · Raffaello Fornasiere · Nikita Menon · Stefan Heimersheim  ·  et al. (LASR Labs / Cambridge)

一句话总结： 在 54 个 model organism × 4 种白盒解释方法上严格匹配行为强度后发现，最易/最难解释变体差距高达 1.2–20.4×， 且更"现实"的 integrated DPO 反而常比廉价的后置 SFT 更难解释，对 MO benchmark 范式提出严厉质疑。

Headline metric
1.2×–20.4×

Ratio between the most- and least-interpretable model-organism variant within each quirk family, after Quirk Expression Rate is matched within ±5pp.

Why this matters

If model-organism interpretability benchmarks are partly artefacts of cheap post-hoc fine-tuning, recent positive interpretability results on narrowly trained MOs may need to be re-evaluated under more realistic training conditions.

Critical edge

Compute wasn't matched across the 7 recipes — only behaviour (QER) was. The realism penalty may be a training-time artefact, and SAE evidence is uninformative due to a high noise floor.

Methodology

Quirks 3 families — false-fact (CakeBake), preference (ItalianFood), fixation (MilitarySubmarine).
Models OLMo2-1B (full open post-training) + gemma-3-1b-it (with Gemma Scope 2 SAEs).
Recipes 7 = 1 "integrated" DPO (modifies <2.5% of OLMo's 378k pairs in-place) + 6 post-hoc variants of DPO / transcript distillation / synthetic-doc FT, with and without 1:1 unrelated-data mixing.
QER gate LLM judge (gemini-3-flash-preview, κ ≥ 0.77) on 400 trigger + 500 ultrachat prompts; LR + data tuned per variant to hit integrated-DPO QER within ±5pp.
Probes 4 white-box methods: activation oracles, activation steering, logit lens (MCP of top-100 quirk tokens), Gemma Scope 2 SAEs. Both ancestor- and sibling-diffing baselines.

  • Most-vs-least-interpretable ratio: 1.2×–20.4× across families at matched QER.
  • Integrated DPO is the lowest or second-lowest in AO + steering scores across every quirk family and both architectures.
  • Training-method rankings do not transfer across families, architectures, or probes.
  • At 1:1 mixing, AOs show 4 decreases and 2 increases vs unmixed — dilution is not destiny.

Strengths

  • Unprecedented 54-MO × 7-recipe × 4-probe sweep under one harness.
  • QER matching within ±5pp is a clear methodological lift over AuditBench / Minder et al.
  • Counter-intuitive realism penalty is genuinely useful, even if only partly refuted.
  • Full open release — 54 MOs + data + code on Hugging Face and GitHub.
  • Multiple sanity checks: black-box investigator gate, training-stochasticity controls, cross-architecture replication.
  • Negative finding delivered with measured tone — recommends expanding MO suites, not discarding them.

Limitations

  • Only 1B-scale (OLMo2-1B, gemma-3-1b-it) — scaling to 7B+, MoE, Mamba is untested.
  • Only 3 benign quirks; safety-relevant ones (deception, sleeper agents) out of scope.
  • SAE evidence is weak — high noise floor; the headline rests mostly on AO + steering.
  • Compute budgets not matched across the 7 recipes — only QER was.
  • Single LLM judge (gemini-3-flash-preview); ItalianFood κ=0.77 is the weakest link.
  • QER matched on triggers only; off-distribution leakage ranges 0.0–2.5% across methods.
  • HelpSteer3 duplication confound discovered late — variants not retrained.
  • Diagnoses the problem; doesn't yet propose a recipe that's both realistic and reliably interpretable.

Questions for further exploration

  • Do the negative findings replicate at 7B+ scale, on MoE, or on safety-relevant quirks?
  • Is "integrated DPO is least interpretable" fundamental — or an artefact of the <2.5% mixing ratio?
  • Can richer behavioural controls (per-prompt intensity, expression diversity) reduce residual QER variance?
  • Are there MO recipes that are simultaneously realistic and reliably interpretable?
  • Which probes are robust to training-method variance vs SFT-artifact-prone?
  • Does the OLMo-vs-Gemma ranking disagreement extend to Llama/Qwen, dense/MoE?
  • Can integrated-DPO MOs serve as a stricter test bed for new interpretability methods?
  • Is the SAE noise floor specific to narrow fine-tuning or a general frontier-model concern?
  • Would retraining with de-duplicated HelpSteer3 tighten the cross-method conclusion?
