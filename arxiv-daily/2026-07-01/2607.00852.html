Recovering Input Text from Hidden States: Gradient-Based Inversion of Decoder-Only LMs

Continuous-then-commit hidden-state inversion recovers 97.5% of 10-token GPT-2 prompts—exposing embedding-API privacy risks.

On 10-token C4 prompts; mean SequenceMatcher similarity 0.994. Failure detector: cumulative discrete loss separates exact from failed recoveries by ~10 orders of magnitude.

Why This Matters

Split-inference and embedding-API deployments leak last-layer states—and the sensitive content tokens are precisely the ones easiest to invert.

Critical Edge

⚠ Trade-off

27–47× slower than per-step SIPIT (which hits 100% EM). Evaluated on GPT-2 small only; no defences (DP, noise, truncation) tested.

Methodology

Threat model

White-box access to GPT-2 small, its tokenizer, and the embedding matrix E. The leaked object is the matrix of last-layer hidden states H(x) ∈ ℝT×n_emb; causal self-attention makes recovery decomposable position-by-position.

Inner loop — continuous

At each position t, a proxy et is initialised from a random token embedding and optimised by Adam (cosine-annealed LR, weight-decay 1e-5, grad-clip 1.0, early exit at Lt<1e-4). Budget K∈{600,1000,2000} picks the operating point.

Outer loop & single commit

Iterate positions conditioned on the committed prefix. After convergence, retrieve the top-C nearest tokens under the ℓ1 metric, verify each with one discrete forward pass, commit the first that reproduces ht. Discrete decision happens exactly once per position — the design point that unlocks diagnostics.

Diagnostic observables

  • Rank trajectory. Correct tokens reach rank 1 by ~step 300; failed ones never approach rank 1 — error is due to flat regions, not budget.
  • Cascade. First position converges fastest; later positions tighten as the committed prefix grows.
  • Failure taxonomy. 417/542 errors are space-prefixed function words (" the", " and", " of"); content-bearing tokens are almost never mis-committed.

Strengths vs Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean separation of continuous search and discrete decision — small architectural change, large analytic payoff.
  • Three named operating points let readers choose their speed/accuracy trade-off.
  • Cumulative discrete loss is a ground-truth-free failure detector — useful for both attackers and defenders instrumenting split-inference pipelines.
  • Actionable failure taxonomy: shows defenders that content-bearing tokens are not the weak point.
  • Honest positioning vs SIPIT — the ~30× speed gap is framed as a deliberate diagnostic trade-off.

Limitations

  • GPT-2 small only — no larger GPT-family, instruction-tuned, or RLHF-tuned models; no encoder-only / encoder-decoder.
  • 10-token English C4 / Wikipedia; longer, code, multilingual, streaming regimes are untested.
  • White-box threat model only; embedding-matrix access is the hard obstacle in practice.
  • No defence evaluation — DP, output noise, hidden-state truncation noted as future work but not quantified.
  • SIPIT comparison uses single seed (42) on 100 prompts; no error bars.
  • Code is not publicly released; reproducibility depends on the SIPIT reference and Algorithm 1.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the same continuous-then-commit formulation yield a useful diagnostic for hidden-state-based probing tasks — e.g. detecting when a classifier has learned a real feature vs a proxy?
  2. How does the cumulative discrete loss failure detector behave under differential-privacy noise on the hidden state — graceful degradation or useless when noise dominates?
  3. Does the "rank-of-ground-truth reaches 1 by step 300" property transfer to larger GPT-family / fine-tuned models, or do denser embedding clusters defeat the early-exit advantage?
  4. For streaming / long sequences, does the cascade mechanism mean longer prompts either invert completely or fail completely — with few partial leaks in between?
  5. Could the categorical asymmetry (function words fail, content tokens succeed) be inverted as a defensive tool — e.g. expose detection via clustering of leaked tokens in function-word regions?
