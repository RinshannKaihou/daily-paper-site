Muon as a Residual Connection

Hao Huang · Zhejiang University · goodhao@zju.edu.cn

Muon's orthogonalized updates act as an implicit residual connection — trading a little local gradient fidelity for a flatter singular spectrum that downstream layers can fit in fewer steps.

Why this matters
Muon powers frontier LLMs like Kimi K2 and DeepSeek V4. The paper reframes why it works: orthogonal updates flatten W₁ᵀW₁'s conditioning, easing the job of every layer downstream — a diagnostic practitioners can actually monitor.
Critical edge
Every experiment is strictly linear at d = 64. The leap from a 2-layer toy net to frontier pretraining is enormous; no nonlinear, deep, or large-scale validation appears in the paper. The "implicit residual" framing is an analogy, not a theorem.

Methodology

Model & target

Two-layer linear network ŷ = W₂W₁x with W₁, W₂ ∈ ℝ⁶⁴ˣ⁶⁴; target A is a Gaussian random matrix.

Local target

From A = UΣVᵀ, fix W₁⋆ = √Σ Vᵀ and W₂⋆ = U√Σ so W₂⋆W₁⋆ = A.

Two-phase protocol

  • Phase 1 — anchor: Freeze W₂, fit W₁ → W₁⋆ with SGD or Muon (lr 0.2, 600 steps).
  • Phase 2 — compose: Freeze W₁, re-init W₂, run SGD on ∥W₂W₁ − A∥²_F / 2 until normalized error ≤ 0.05. Identical optimizer/hyperparams both paths — any gap traces to W₁'s representation.

τ-schedule

End-to-end composition loss with alternating blocks of W₁-only and W₂-only updates (τ ≥ 1) or fully joint updates (τ = 0).

Theoretical hook

One-step SGD on the downstream loss gives Eₜ₊₁ = Eₜ(I − ηW₁ᵀW₁), so the spectrum of W₁ᵀW₁ — not proximity to W₁⋆ — governs convergence.

Strengths vs limitations

Strengths

  • Crystal-clear setup; minimal experiment isolates the mechanism.
  • Honest positioning — analogy, not equivalence claim.
  • Quantitative trade-off is operationalizable: spectrum flatness and κ(W₁ᵀW₁) are monitorable diagnostics.
  • Connects cleanly to adjacent literature (curvature, Frank–Wolfe, long-tail, linear probing).
  • Code released; deterministic, tiny experiment — reproduces in < 1 hour.

Limitations

  • All experiments are linear, one fixed dim (d = 64).
  • Natural two-phase effect is modest (~1%); larger gains only appear under constructed τ schedules.
  • Proposed adaptive orthogonalization is suggested, not implemented.
  • No comparison with Muon-Adam hybrids, Shampoo, SOAP, or K-FAC.
  • "Implicit residual" is analogy, not formal correspondence.
  • Reports a flatness proxy, not explicit condition numbers.

Questions for further exploration

  1. Does monitoring κ(W₁ᵀW₁) during real LLM pretraining predict when Muon helps vs. hurts?
  2. How does the trade-off shift with depth, residual connections, LayerNorm, and attention — i.e., when Block(x) is nonlinear?
  3. Can the design principle become a practical adaptive scheme that beats AdamW and constant Muon on frontier pretraining?
  4. How does this interact with Muon-Adam hybrids used in Kimi K2 / DeepSeek V4? Is the effect concentrated in specific weight groups?
  5. Does the downstream-usability lens apply to Shampoo, SOAP, and K-FAC — or is Muon's Newton–Schulz signature distinct?
  6. Could layer-wise scheduling (update head/embeddings more often than Muon-trained layers) exploit the τ-schedule scaling in practice?
