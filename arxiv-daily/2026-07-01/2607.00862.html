01Methodology — one signal, used twice

Self-certainty (SC) is the path-averaged KL(U ‖ p_θ) between the model's next-token distribution and uniform. A peakier distribution ⇒ higher SC ⇒ the model is internally more certain. SC correlates with correctness and difficulty, yet stays robust to response length.

  • Stage I · CAPL Sample K trajectories per question, then build two preference-pair types. Conciseness Pairs: correct & short & high-SC ≻ correct & long & low-SC. Deliberation Pairs: incorrect & long & low-SC ≻ incorrect & short & high-SC. Dynamic pruning keeps top-3 score levels, truncates the bottom τ globally, caps ≤1 CP + 1 DP per query.
  • Stage II · CWPO Wrap SimPO with a calibration ratio ρ = SC / |y|^α. Confident correct paths get pulled toward brevity; short-but-confident failures get penalized harder — replacing one global length penalty with per-sample when to compress vs. deliberate.
  • Training 2,000 MATH-train questions × 20 responses (max 4,096 tok). LoRA r=32 / α=64, 1 epoch, batch 16, lr 5e-5 (7B/8B) or 5e-6 (1.5B), 2×A800. SimPO β=200, γ=1. Eval: temp 0.6, top_p 0.95, 3-run mean.

Beyond the headline: Stronger LRMs land CP-dominant datasets and exceed their own base accuracy (Qwen3-8B AIME24 74.4 → 76.7) — the gain is real, not just trimming. Ablations confirm both parts matter: dropping CAPL costs accuracy; dropping CWPO loses compression. The SC recipe transfers cleanly to DPO as CWPO_DPO (AIME24 53.3 → 58.9). Case study: 573 tokens vs 981 base at the highest self-certainty.

03Strengths vs. Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean two-stage recipe composing label construction and loss reweighting around one SC signal.
  • Broad evidence: 3 LRMs × 3 benchmarks × full ablations, plus DPO transfer.
  • Stronger backbones surpass their base accuracy — rare in the compression literature.
  • Calibration ratio keeps SC and length on comparable scales; per-pair weights are interpretable.
  • Code and pair-construction script released.

Limitations

  • Path-level SC only — token/step-level miscalibration is collapsed into one scalar.
  • Offline preference optimization on a static, MATH-derived dataset; no online RL variant.
  • Needs verifiable answers for labeling — blocks open-ended generation.
  • STEM-only eval; code, multi-modal, and NLI transfer untested.
  • Weaker R1-1.5B barely moves (AIME24 28.9 → 31.1); τ, α tuned on one backbone and reused.

04Questions for Further Exploration

  • Could a cheaper / better-calibrated estimator (semantic entropy, consistency) replace SC?
  • Does CAPL+CWPO survive online RL (GRPO/PPO) where confidence co-evolves with the policy?
  • How does it behave on non-verifiable domains where external correctness is unavailable?
  • Would step-level SC enable finer compression — skipping only low-certainty steps?
  • Does the 2×A800 / 1-epoch LoRA setup scale to 32B+ or full fine-tuning?
  • How does CAT interact with tool use / retrieval-augmented reasoning?
  • Can the confidence signal double as a small-vs-large-model router?
  • Where does "compress confident successes" backfire when confidence masks a fragile step?
