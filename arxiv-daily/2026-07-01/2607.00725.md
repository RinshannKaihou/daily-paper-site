> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"answer-in-context"诊断（衡量金标准答案在已打包 reader context 中是否作为连续 span 存活），证明其比 recall 更能预测答案质量（R² +0.17），并把 reader-context 构建形式化为预算约束下的单调子模最大化；在 HotpotQA / 160 token / 3B reader 上较 MMR、朴素打包、focused 启发式提升最多 +5.1 F1、token 成本更低，但该优势仅在多跳互补结构 + 检索覆盖 + 紧但非极端预算 + 较弱的 reader 四条件同时满足时成立，到 7B 被吸收、14B 显著反转。

> **TL;DR:** The paper reframes budget-constrained multi-hop RAG as a *post-retrieval packing* problem and argues that **document recall is the wrong optimization target** under a tight reader-context budget. It introduces **answer-in-context (AIC)** — whether the gold answer appears as a contiguous span in the *packed* reader context — and shows AIC predicts answer F1 better than recall (r = 0.39–0.55 vs. ≈0.31, ΔR² = +0.17 over recall), separates HotpotQA answer quality ~5× (F1 0.60 vs. 0.12), and still exhibits a 4.6× EM gap on the subset where all gold was retrieved (27% of those questions drop the answer during packing). A clean **interventional check on 2WikiMultiHopQA** (3B reader, budget 160, 3 seeds) shifts coverage (+0.054) but not AIC (−0.007) and accuracy does not move (ΔF1 −0.008, p=0.44) — nailing AIC as the causal lever. The second contribution is a **budgeted monotone submodular packer** with a four-term objective (relevance + query coverage + saturated facility-location representativeness + √-diversity), solved with cost-scaled greedy + Lin–Bilmes fallback. On HotpotQA-500 at B=160 with Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, it is the best fixed policy on every seed (F1 0.451, EM 0.359 at ~145 tokens vs. ~152 for baselines; +0.051 F1 over naive packing, +0.042 over MMR, +0.022 over focused heuristic), and 81% of the gain comes from ~37 questions where the packer newly places a gold answer in context. The honestly-scoped result is the **four-condition scope map**: (1) multi-hop complementary structure, (2) retrieval that surfaces the evidence, (3) binding-but-not-extreme budget (the gap is an inverted-U peaking at B≈160), and (4) a reader that is the bottleneck. A **quantization-controlled 3B→7B→14B reader-scale ladder** (with 7B fp16 vs. 4-bit as a precision control) shows the edge over the focused heuristic is **absorbed by 7B and significantly reverses at 14B** (−0.029 F1, p=0.013) — same mechanism, same packing, but the larger reader stops needing the completeness. The packer also helps chunk evidence but **hurts ACE** (graph-compressed) representation (−0.021 F1), showing graph compression and principled packing are partial substitutes.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 answer-in-context (AIC) 诊断指标**：衡量金标准答案是否作为规范化连续 token 序列存活于已打包的 reader context 中（而非仅出现在 retrieval 集合里），并系统验证其比 retrieval recall 更能预测 answer F1。
- **通过增量有效性（ΔR² = +0.17 over recall）和因果干预（2Wiki 上移动 coverage 但不移动 AIC，F1 不动）双重证明 AIC 是 RAG 答案质量的因果中介量，而非 recall 的同义反复。**
- **将 reader-context 构建形式化为预算约束下的单调子模最大化问题**，目标函数 = 1.0·Rel + 0.5·QueryCov + 0.4·Repr + 0.3·Div（Rel 模组化；QueryCov 为 query 词的 set-cover；Repr 为带 α 饱和的 facility-location；Div 为按文档开根号的 concave-over-documents 分散项）；通过 per-token 边际增益贪心 + Lin–Bilmes 单元素兜底求解。
- **在 HotpotQA-500 / B=160 / Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct 上系统验证**：在更低 token 成本下成为三粒 seed 最佳固定策略，F1 0.451 vs. focused 0.429 / naive 0.400 / MMR 0.410，oracle 0.601；81% 增益来自 ~37 个"答案被新放入 context"的问题。
- **诚实的四条件范围图**：互补多跳结构 + 证据可被检索召回 + 紧但非极端预算 + reader 是瓶颈；并以 RAGBench（条件 1 失败）、MuSiQue 加宽检索（条件 2 失败）、B=96/224（条件 3 失败）、3B→7B→14B reader ladder（条件 4 反转）作为失败对照。
- **量化控制下的 reader-scale ladder**：7B fp16 vs. 4-bit 控制样本近乎完美重合（−0.010 vs. −0.008, p≈0.5），证实 3B→14B 的趋势由 reader 容量驱动而非量化误差；packer 在 7B 与 focused 无差异，在 14B 显著劣于 focused，但始终显著优于 naive 打包。
- **同时指出 ACE 失败现象**：submodular packer 对 chunk 证据显著有效（+0.022 F1 over focused），但对 ACE 图压缩证据显著为负（−0.021 F1），提示"图压缩"与"principled packing"在该意义上部分替代。

## Key Contributions

- **Answer-in-context (AIC) as a diagnostic for budget-constrained RAG**, defined on the *packed* (not retrieved) context, validated by feature–quality correlation, incremental validity over recall@5, and an interventional manipulation on 2WikiMultiHopQA.
- A **budgeted monotone submodular packer** with a four-term gain function (relevance, query coverage, representativeness, diversity) and a cost-scaled greedy + Lin–Bilmes singleton-fallback solver, applied to reader-context construction.
- A **factorial evaluation** isolating the selection rule from the candidate set and the retriever: chunk × {focused, MMR, submodular} × {naive, oracle} on five datasets and three reader precisions/scales.
- A **two-by-four scope map** with one positive result (HotpotQA, B≈160, 3B reader) and three boundary experiments + one reversal experiment, each tied to one of the four conjunctive conditions.
- A **quantization-controlled reader-scale ladder** (3B / 7B fp16 / 7B 4-bit / 14B 4-bit) that localizes the *scale* at which evidence-density optimization stops paying off (~7B) and starts costing (14B), with precision effects ruled out.
- **Empirical disentanglement of two commonly conflated interventions**: graph-structured evidence compression (ACE) vs. principled submodular packing — the former is a partial substitute for the latter, not a complement.
- An **oracle headroom analysis** showing ~F1 0.60 vs. best fixed policy ~0.45, and an offline router over retrieval features collapses toward the best fixed policy → the residual ~20% is an "answer-in-context lottery" unobservable at inference time.

## Methodology

- **Problem framing.** Treat post-retrieval RAG as a **budgeted selection** problem: given retrieved evidence and a hard reader-token budget B, choose a packed subset. The authors argue optimizing recall at retrieval stage is misaligned with downstream answer quality once a reader is constrained.
- **Answer-in-context (AIC) definition.** Binary indicator: some normalized gold answer span appears as a contiguous token subsequence of the *packed* (not retrieved) context. Two auxiliary metrics: gold-doc reader coverage (fraction of gold docs contributing ≥1 snippet) and gold-token density (fraction of packed tokens from gold docs). All computed on the *packed* run, the key departure from recall@k.
- **Correlational and incremental-validity analysis** (HotpotQA, three seeds, 10,500 policy×question rows, clustered bootstrap on question id): report Pearson correlations with F1/EM, fit a regression of F1 on recall@5 vs. F1 on recall@5 + AIC, report ΔR² and standardized coefficients.
- **Interventional check on 2WikiMultiHopQA**: re-run the exact HotpotQA factorial (3B reader, B=160, seeds {42,13,7}, 500 questions). The submodular packer lifts gold-doc coverage by +0.054 (all three seeds) but AIC moves only −0.007, F1 moves −0.008 (p=0.44). Coverage and AIC move in opposite directions; F1 follows AIC.
- **Submodular packer objective (Eq. 1).** F(S) = 1.0·Rel(S) + 0.5·QueryCov(S) + 0.4·Repr(S) + 0.3·Div(S), each term monotone, submodular, normalized to [0,1]. Rel is modular (single-snippet lexical relevance the focused heuristic uses, isolating the selection rule from candidate features). QueryCov is a set-cover over distinct query content terms. Repr is a saturated facility-location term `Σ_i min(Σ_{j∈S} sim(i,j), α·deg_i)` with α=0.3 to prevent near-duplicate gaming. Div is concave-over-documents `Σ_d √(relevance mass of S in d)`.
- **Algorithm.** Cost-scaled (per-token) greedy with Lin–Bilmes singleton fallback (return the best single feasible snippet if it outscores the greedy set). Standard (1−1/e) constant-factor template for budgeted monotone submodular maximization. The contribution is the **application and tying the objective to AIC**, not the optimizer itself.
- **Baselines and factorial.** Naive packed (greedy relevance concat), focused heuristic (project's prior packer; only checks budget after the fact, no length normalization), MMR (λ=0.7). Crossed with {chunk, ACE} representations. Per-question oracle (mixed) is reported as headroom only, not as a deployed method.
- **Retrieval and reader stack.** bge-small-en-v1.5 embeddings truncated to 320 dims (dense bi-encoder), Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct primary reader, dual T4 GPUs. Larger reader rungs (7B, 14B) use 4-bit NF4 quantization (Dettmers 2022/2023) and a 7B fp16 vs. 4-bit precision control.
- **Datasets and budgets.** HotpotQA (500 questions, primary headline), 2WikiMultiHopQA (interventional validation), MuSiQue (negative/boundary), RAGBench CovidQA + ExpertQA (complementarity boundary). Primary budget B=160 tokens, with budget sweep 96/128/160/224 on HotpotQA seed 42. Three seeds for the HotpotQA headline (B=160); single seed for budget sweep and MuSiQue runs.

## Results & Highlights

- **AIC vs recall (HotpotQA, seed 42, 500 questions, 2500 policy×question rows, B=160):** correlation with F1: AIC **+0.50**, gold-doc reader coverage +0.33, retrieval all-gold@5 +0.32, retrieval recall@5 +0.31, gold-token density +0.26. AIC is the strongest single predictor; correlations with EM follow the same ordering.
- **Incremental validity over recall@5.** R² on F1: recall alone 0.086; recall + AIC 0.257 → **ΔR² = +0.17**. Standardized β: AIC +0.21, recall +0.05 (~4× ratio). Partial correlation of AIC with F1 controlling for recall: +0.43. AIC and recall@5 themselves correlate only +0.41 (far from the ≈1 that "AIC ≈ recall" would require).
- **Quality separation on HotpotQA.** Conditional F1 = **0.596** when a gold answer is in the packed reader context vs. **0.123** when it is not (+0.47 gap, ~5× spread). Resolves the "lower recall, better answers" paradox.
- **Retrieval-perfect subset (recall@5=1, n=7739 rows).** 27% still drop the answer during packing. Within this subset, AIC still decisive: F1 **0.61 vs. 0.20**, EM **0.50 vs. 0.11** (3.0× / **4.6×** gap, tight clustered-bootstrap CIs). A small minority of those drops are paraphrase rather than packing failure; the predictive-validity conclusion is unaffected.
- **Cross-dataset AIC (Table 2):** r(AIC, F1) = +0.50 HotpotQA, +0.54 MuSiQue, +0.55 2WikiMultiHopQA, +0.39 RAGBench CovidQA, *degenerate* on ExpertQA. AIC is strongest on the two datasets where the packer shows no win (MuSiQue, 2Wiki) — evidence the diagnostic is dataset-independent, not a side effect of the method.
- **Interventional check (2Wiki, 3B reader, B=160, 3 seeds, 500 questions).** Submodular packer: gold-doc coverage **+0.054** (all three seeds), AIC **−0.007**, F1 **−0.008** (paired bootstrap p=0.44). Coverage and AIC move in opposite directions; F1 follows AIC. Conditional F1: 0.56 when AIC=1 vs. 0.08 when AIC=0.
- **HotpotQA headline (3 seeds, B=160, 3B reader).** chunk_submod: F1 **0.451**, EM 0.359 at 145.5 tokens — best fixed policy on every seed. Pooled 3-seed paired bootstrap: submod vs. focused **+0.022 F1** [+0.002, +0.041]; submod vs. naive **+0.051 F1** [+0.030, +0.072] (+0.053 EM); submod vs. MMR +0.042 F1; MMR vs. focused −0.020 F1 [−0.034, −0.005] (MMR is *worse* than the focused heuristic — generic redundancy reduction hurts). Ordering: submod > focused > naive > MMR.
- **Mechanism (HotpotQA seed 42, per-question decomposition).** 81% of the submod–focused gain comes from **~37 questions where the packer newly places a gold answer in context** (≈+0.39 F1 each). The packer improves all-gold-in-reader on **289 vs. 256** questions (chunk_submod vs. focused), with no higher raw token density — the win is *complementary coverage*, not packing more gold at any cost.
- **Budget sweep (HotpotQA seed 42, B∈{96,128,160,224}).** Submod−focused gap is an **inverted-U peaking at B=160** (ΔF1 +0.035, p=0.04 at B=160; +0.004 p=0.81 at 96; +0.014 p=0.45 at 128; +0.017 p=0.26 at 224). At 96 only 2–3 snippets fit; at 224 nearly everything fits and the heuristic catches up. Against naive packing, submod wins at **every** budget (+0.044 to +0.055 F1, all p ≤ 0.022). Iso-quality efficiency: submod@160 matches focused@224 (p=0.80) at **~30% fewer tokens** (145 vs. 215).
- **Reader-scale ladder (HotpotQA, B=160, seeds {42,13} pooled, n=1000).**
  - 3B fp16 (§5 headline): submod−focused **+0.022 F1** [+0.002, +0.041], p<0.05.
  - 7B fp16: **−0.010** [−0.035, +0.015], p=0.45 (clean null).
  - 7B 4-bit (precision control): **−0.008** [−0.032, +0.017], p=0.55 (reproduces 7B fp16 — trend is scale, not quantization).
  - 14B 4-bit: **−0.029** [−0.052, −0.006], p=0.013 — focused heuristic significantly beats the packer.
  - Throughout, chunk_submod still packs strictly more gold (coverage 0.78 vs. 0.73) and still **beats naive packing at every rung** (+0.044 to +0.055 F1, p ≤ 0.001). Reader capability is a mediator: same density edge passed through readers of rising sensitivity — denser gold stops buying and starts costing once the reader can already extract the answer.
- **ACE reversal (HotpotQA seed 42, B=160, 3B reader).** ace_submod vs. ace_focused: **−0.021 F1** [−0.039, −0.003]. Submodular packing helps chunk but *hurts* ACE — ACE already compresses and de-duplicates at graph level, leaving little redundancy for the packer. **Graph compression and principled packing are partial substitutes.**
- **Boundary 1 — RAGBench (single-pass, no complementary structure).** CovidQA (n=246): submod vs. focused −0.010 F1, p=0.30 (focused heuristic is best chunk policy). ExpertQA (n=203): +0.005, p=0.15. AIC still tracks quality (r=0.39 on CovidQA); there is simply no complementary multi-hop structure to assemble.
- **Boundary 2 — MuSiQue (retrieval-bottlenecked).** recall@5=0.506, all-gold@5=0.184 — only 18% of questions have all gold retrieved. Submod vs. focused +0.011 F1, p=0.34; naive packing is just as good; ace_focused is the best fixed policy. **A reviewer fix attempt** (tripling retrieval depth 5→12, nodes 48→64, expand 5→8) leaves all-gold@5 unchanged at 0.184, and the packer gap stays null (+0.003 F1) — the bottleneck is *qualitative* (bi-encoder cannot navigate 2–4 hop compositional chains), not depth. A qualitatively different retriever (iterative / chain-of-thought multi-hop) is needed.
- **Oracle headroom.** Per-question oracle reaches F1≈0.60 vs. best fixed policy ≈0.45. chunk_submod is already (tied-)best on 79.5% of questions; the remaining ~20% is an "answer-in-context lottery" whose deciding variable is unobservable at inference time, and an offline router over retrieval features collapses toward the best fixed policy. Oracle is reported as headroom, not as a deployed method.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High on the diagnostic axis. Reframing "what should we measure after retrieval" from recall@k to AIC is a small but pointed conceptual move, and the interventional check converts it from "intuition" to "causal lever." The submodular packer itself is a competent instantiation — the budgeted monotone submodular framework with greedy + Lin–Bilmes fallback is textbook (Nemhauser–Wolsey–Fisher 1978; Lin & Bilmes 2011), and the four-term gain function is a reasonable application. The "packing vs. graph compression are partial substitutes" finding is a clean empirical disentanglement of two commonly conflated interventions.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Three seeds on the HotpotQA headline; clustered bootstrap on question id (cluster-robust inference); 7B fp16 vs. 4-bit precision control cleanly rules out quantization as the cause of the scale-ladder trend; the falsified monotonicity prediction (the inverted-U gap) and the retrieval-unlock ablation (wider MuSiQue retrieval moves all-gold@5 by zero basis points) both convert soft negatives into precise mechanistic statements; the interventional check on 2Wiki converts correlational AIC analysis into causal evidence; full per-policy / per-budget / per-reader-scale tables are reported (Tables 6, 7, 8, 9). The budget sweep and MuSiQue runs are single-seed, which is a real but acknowledged gap.
- **Reproducibility:** High. Method is deterministic given retrieved sets, token budget, and reader; AIC is a simple token-subsequence check; the packer + gain function are described in enough detail to re-implement. Datasets (HotpotQA, 2WikiMultiHopQA, MuSiQue, RAGBench) are standard. Risks: reader choice (Qwen2.5 specifically), embedding model (bge-small-en-v1.5 truncated to 320 dims), and quantization scheme (NF4) can shift boundary locations; the per-condition negative experiments are n=2 or n=1 seeds and the per-dataset correlation in Table 2 is also single-seed.
- **Significance:** Important for practitioners — directly changes the metric to optimize when designing budget-constrained RAG systems, and the scale-ladder finding ("stop optimizing evidence density past ~7B; it starts costing by 14B") is a useful engineering rule of thumb. The empirical disentanglement of packing vs. graph compression is genuinely useful guidance (which one should you spend engineering on?). The cross-dataset AIC correlation being *strongest* on the datasets where the packer doesn't win is a notable result — the diagnostic is more general than the method.
- **Red flags:** (i) Solo author, preprint-only, no independent replication. (ii) Latency / throughput cost of the greedy submodular solver vs. MMR / heuristic is **not measured** — relevant for production deployment. (iii) Single model family (Qwen2.5) and single embedder (bge-small-en) across the whole reader-scale ladder; behavior on other families (Llama, Mistral), on stronger retrievers (ColBERT, cross-encoder), and on 32B+ readers is untested. (iv) Quantization is used as the scale-ladder control, which partially conflates "model capacity" with "loss of precision"; the 7B fp16 vs. 4-bit control rules out *most* but not all of this conflation, and at 14B there is no full-precision control. (v) AIC is gold-answer-centric and span-based → degenerate for long free-form answers (ExpertQA, 0% answer spans survive verbatim). (vi) ACE graph construction is heuristic, so the "packing is a partial substitute for graph compression" reading should be taken with that caveat. (vii) The headline positive claim rests on HotpotQA — the cross-dataset experiments are negatives / boundaries by design, and the per-dataset "no win" results on MuSiQue and 2Wiki might reflect a true method limit or a power issue at n=500.

## Strengths

- Diagnoses the **right quantity** for budget-constrained RAG and backs it with both correlational and interventional evidence — answers the question "what should we be measuring?" rather than "how do we improve a single metric?"
- Packing formalization is principled (budgeted monotone submodular with constant-factor guarantee) and the four-term gain function maps directly to practitioner intuitions (relevance / coverage / representativeness / diversity).
- **Three-seed headline** with **clustered bootstrap on question id** — avoids pseudo-replication and is a stronger inferential standard than is common in the area.
- **Paired token-cost comparison** (the win is at ~145 tokens vs. ~152 for baselines) avoids spurious efficiency/quality tradeoffs and produces a useful iso-quality efficiency result (submod@160 matches focused@224 at ~30% fewer tokens).
- **Honest scope statement with a falsifiable reader-scale ladder and a falsified monotonicity prediction** — among the best scientific hygiene in the area. The cross-dataset AIC being *strongest* on datasets where the method doesn't win is a particularly strong signal of intellectual honesty.
- **Clean disentanglement of two commonly conflated interventions** (graph compression vs. submodular packing) via the chunk × {focused, MMR, submodular} × {chunk, ACE} factorial.
- **Oracle headroom analysis** with an explicit "do not deploy" framing — avoids the common failure mode of presenting oracle results as if they were method results.

## Limitations

- **Headline win demonstrated on one dataset (HotpotQA) at one budget regime (B≈160) and one reader scale (3B)**; cross-dataset experiments are negatives / boundaries by design, so the positive claim is structurally narrow.
- **Single model family (Qwen2.5) and single embedder (bge-small-en)** across the reader-scale ladder; behavior on Llama / Mistral / Gemma readers and on ColBERT / cross-encoder retrievers is untested.
- **No full-precision 14B control** — the 14B result rests on NF4 quantization, and the fp16/4-bit separation is only established at 7B. "Reader scale" and "quantization error" are not fully orthogonalized at 14B.
- **Budget sweep (B=96, 128, 224) and MuSiQue runs are single-seed**; the three-seed robustness applies only to the HotpotQA B=160 headline.
- **Latency / throughput cost of the submodular solver is not measured** — the production relevance of the +5.1 F1 headline depends on whether the greedy + Lin–Bilmes loop beats MMR / heuristic under realistic retrieval-set sizes and per-query SLOs.
- **AIC is span-based and gold-answer-centric** → degenerate for long free-form answers (ExpertQA) and for tasks where the answer is synthesized rather than extracted.
- **ACE graph construction is heuristic**; the "packing is a partial substitute for graph compression" finding inherits that uncertainty and is not a clean ablation of "principled graph compression."
- **Sample size on the cross-dataset per-policy table is small** (500 questions per dataset) — the per-dataset "no win" results on MuSiQue and 2Wiki could be power-limited.
- **No analysis of attribution faithfulness** (RAGAS, ARES); a faithfulness-aware variant of AIC is left explicitly to future work.
- **Reader-scale ladder ends at 14B 4-bit**; behavior at 32B+ (where LLMs become strong long-context readers with extensive pretrained evidence-grounding) is unknown and is precisely the regime where the "weaker reader" condition matters least.

## Relevance

与本仓库关注点部分相关：论文核心是 RAG 检索后 context 打包阶段的评估诊断与子模优化，与"LLM Inference Evaluation"侧（reader 端上下文利用能力评测、retrieval-grounded QA 基准）有较强关联；reader-scale ladder 与 quantization 控制也对"计算可靠性/精度误差"（Computation Reliability & Precision Errors）有间接方法学参考价值（量化与精度对 reader 表现的边界隔离）。不直接命中训练稳定性、隐藏状态/可解释性、静默数据腐坏、自演化智能体等主题，但作为 RAG 评测方法学与 reader-capability 边界探测的严谨范例，对任何需要在算力受限场景下做 LLM 部署选型的项目都值得一读。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- **Does AIC remain the leading diagnostic on non-extractive answer formats** (long-form QA, dialog, citation grounding, multimodal QA)? A semantic / entailment variant is needed; the paper explicitly leaves this to future work.
- **How does the submodular packer perform on cross-lingual or domain-shifted retrieval** (biomedical / legal / code), where complementary structure may be weaker or the "answer span" assumption is more often violated?
- **What is the latency budget of the greedy submodular solver vs. MMR at retrieval set sizes ≥ 100, and does packing + inference cost erase the F1 win end-to-end** under realistic per-query SLOs?
- **Can AIC be estimated without gold answers** (e.g., via an answer-probe model or a per-token likelihood ratio) and used as an online stopping signal during retrieval / packing, to surface the ~20% "answer-in-context lottery" at inference time?
- **Is the 14B reversal driven by reader capacity or by quantization error** — would a full-precision 14B / 32B ladder reach the same boundary, and at what scale does the reversal plateau?
- **How tightly does the four-condition scope map generalize** — does removing one condition (e.g., decompressing the budget to 224) collapse the +5.1 F1 to noise *for this specific question subset*, as the paper claims but does not ablate question-by-question?
- **Does the disentanglement of packing vs. graph compression survive with a stronger ACE** (ColBERT-grade late interaction, learned graph construction) — or is the chunk-vs-ACE contrast an artifact of the specific ACE implementation?
- **Can AIC be combined with existing RAG faithfulness metrics** (RAGAS, ARES) into a single evaluation framework, or do they capture orthogonal failure modes?
- **What is the relationship between AIC and the "lost in the middle" effect** (Liu et al. 2024) — does AIC drop more for middle-positioned spans, and does position-aware packing close some of the gap?
- **How sensitive is the scope map to retriever quality** — would a ColBERT / cross-encoder retriever make MuSiQue Condition 2 hold and unlock the packer on a second multi-hop dataset, or does the qualitative multi-hop limitation persist?
