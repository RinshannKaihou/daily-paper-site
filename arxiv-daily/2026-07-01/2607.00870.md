> **一句话总结：** 在 167,034 篇 PMC-Patients 病历上对生成器（Llama-3.3 70B）+ 验证器（MMed-Llama-3.1 70B）流水线部署"动态双向模式记忆"（DBPM）门控：发现最自然的"从验证器拒绝中学习过滤规则"在生产规模下静默失败（785,797 次拒绝，关系抽取块列表 0 条），而简单的本体谓词能在同一信道产生 49,734 个可作用信号；在五个 QA 门控信号源中只有"询问患者的 NER 覆盖是否与问题类别匹配"具有选择性（路径 lift 1.84），其选择性的唯一判据是该信号探测的"问题"必须与验证器自身所回答的"问题"一致。

> **TL;DR:** A 39-page production-scale empirical study of inference-time pattern-memory gating (DBPM) embedded in a clinical NLP pipeline (Llama-3.3 70B generator + MMed-Llama-3.1 70B verifier over 167,034 PMC-Patients narratives). Four findings: (1) the natural "accumulate verifier rejections" design fails silently at production scale — 785,797 Stage-3 classifier rejections yielded an empty relation-extraction blocklist because rejections dispersed across too many free-form signatures to outrun 10-day decay; (2) a deterministic ontology-violation predicate on a bounded 29-type-triple alphabet populates the same channel (49,734 violations on a 5,000-patient hold-out, vs 1,207 isolated suppressions, vs 913 in the full master-switch ablation); (3) of five QA-gate signal sources, four fail for structurally distinct reasons (aggregation-level mismatch, signal absence, counting asymmetry, cross-task orthogonality) and only the NER-coverage signal — checking whether a patient has the entities a category-question needs — is selective (path lift 1.84, 95% CI [1.75, 1.93], p<10⁻¹⁵, with diagnosis at lift 17.13); (4) the unifying principle is that a pre-generation gate is selective only when it probes the same evidence the verifier itself weighs. All artefacts released openly.

## 核心贡献

- **生产规模的实证证据（C1）**：在 167,034-patient 规模下，最自然的"由验证器拒绝流喂养块列表"的设计失败——尽管日志中有 785,797 次 Stage-3 分类器拒绝，关系抽取块列表在 12 天运行后仍为 0 条记录；其原因是结构性的——拒绝分散在开放词汇的关系标签上，任何单签名的强化速率都不足以跑赢 10 天半衰期的衰减。同一持久化机制在 NER（5,860 模式）和 QA（11 模式）通道上正常工作。
- **提出不依赖验证器的本体违规过滤器（C2）**：固定 29-条类型三元组的本体谓词 + 32 条关系别名映射，在同一关系信道上复用了验证器通道留下的空位，事件集中在封闭字母表上从而赢得"强化-衰减赛跑"；在 5,000-patient 留出集中识别出 49,734 个本体违规的候选关系（全量本体过滤捕获 49,734；隔离本体过滤抑制 1,207；主开关全部 DBPM 关闭时抑制 913——三数字一致地指向同一机制）。
- **QA 门控五信号源系统消融（C3）**：逐一失败原因被精确隔离——聚合层错配（category-level 信号 vs pair-level 验证器）、信号缺失（现代指令调优模型几乎不产生解析失败）、计数不对称（FAIL 按 pair 计数 / SUCCESS 按模板计数）、跨任务正交性（上游抽取难度与答案内容无关）；第五个 NER-coverage 信号探测了"答案是否能被该患者的抽取实体所支撑"——这恰是验证器隐式回答的问题。
- **可迁移的结构性原则（C4）**：推理时预生成门控信号只在直接探测验证器自身所权衡的同一证据时才具有选择性；模仿验证器的"输出"而非其"输入"无法让门控具有选择性。
- **可操作的部署纪律**：默认 DOWNGRADE-first 而非 BLOCK-first，所有可疑抽取被标记而非删除，保证临床审计可追溯性；同时提供 `crosscheck_F1_167k_blocklist.py` 让部署者能验证其块列表是否实际填充。
- **形式化与开源**：将问题形式化为块列表严重度 $B_j(\sigma,t)$ 与白名单置信度 $W_j(\sigma,t)$ 的更新算子 $U$（Eq. 2-5），给出四条部署约束（C1 常量时间查询 / C2 有界存储 / C3 有界值 / C4 非平稳稳定性）；代码、消融运行器、gate-decision 评分器与留出队列标识符全部开源。

## Key Contributions

- **(C1) Production-scale evidence that the natural verifier-fed design fails.** The most natural pattern-memory design — accumulating the verifier's own rejections per signature and gating on accumulated severity — produces an **empty** relation-extraction blocklist at 167,034-patient scale despite **785,797 Stage-3 classifier rejection events**. The cause is structural: rejections disperse across free-form relation labels, and no single signature is reinforced fast enough to outrun the 10-day wall-clock half-life decay. The same persistence machinery left the NER channel (5,860 patterns) and QA channel (11 patterns) intact. Generalises: any inference-time memory accumulating per-signature statistics over free-form LLM output is exposed to the same diffusion failure.
- **(C2) A working verifier-independent signal source for the same channel.** A deterministic ontology-violation predicate on a 29-triple admissible alphabet + 32-entry alias map fires on every relation candidate regardless of the verifier; events concentrate on a handful of signatures, clearing threshold where the verifier-fed pathway does not. Captures **49,734 ontology violations** on the 5,000-patient hold-out vs. ~1,207 isolated suppressions / 913 in the master-switch ablation; the three counts are reconciled by their different reference arms.
- **(C3) Five-signal empirical dissection of the question-answering gate.** Each failure has a distinct structural cause:
  - **Category-aggregate fail-mass** → anti-selective (lift 0.006, ~170× less likely than untagged to be verifier-rejected); *aggregation-level mismatch* (gate decides at category level, verifier at pair level).
  - **Verifier uncertainty (parse-failure flag)** → signal absent at scale; *signal absence*.
  - **Per-category fail-rate (dual-bucket)** → anti-selective (lift 0.05); *counting asymmetry* (FAIL per pair, SUCCESS per question template).
  - **Per-patient upstream difficulty (cross-task)** → no signal (lift 0.00); PASS and FAIL distributions are statistically indistinguishable (medians 0.179 vs 0.174); *cross-task orthogonality*.
  - **NER-coverage intersection** → selective (path lift **1.84**, 95% CI [1.75, 1.93], p<10⁻¹⁵); tags 36.0% of FAIL pairs vs 19.6% of PASS pairs. *Working design.*
- **(C4) A transferable structural principle.** A pre-generation gate is selective for verifier rejection **only when it directly probes the same evidence the verifier itself weighs**. Four negative cases and one positive case establish the necessity of alignment; per-category operating-envelope analysis (selective / categorical / saturated / fallback regimes) shows alignment is necessary but not sufficient.
- **Operational discipline for clinical auditability.** Default `M1_QA_GATE_HARD_BLOCK = 0` (DOWNGRADE-first): suspect candidates are tagged in their per-pair output rather than removed, so every gating decision stays visible for clinical review; hard-blocking mode is available for deployments that prefer the gate's verdict to be final.
- **Open release** of DBPM gating module, ablation runner, gate-decision graders, held-out 5k identifier list, and `crosscheck_F1_167k_blocklist.py` for at-scale blocklist-population validation.

## Methodology

### Pipeline and setting
- **Corpus:** PMC-Patients (Zhao et al., 2023) — 167,034 patient narratives (163,400 human, 3,634 veterinary) processed end-to-end over ~12 days (4–16 May 2026) on Supercomputing Wales Falcon (4× H200 141 GB, 64 cores, 900 GB RAM).
- **Generator $G$:** Llama-3.3 70B (instruction-tuned), served via vLLM continuous batching, TP=2 on GPUs 0–1, bfloat16, temperature 0 (greedy).
- **Verifier $V$:** MMed-Llama-3.1 70B (Qiu et al., 2024), TP=2 on GPUs 2–3, identical vLLM parameters; emits **categorical** verdicts (no continuous confidence): `PASS/FAIL` for NER/QAR and `PASS_STRONG/PASS_WEAK/FAIL` for RE; conservative-pass regime (ambiguous → PASS).
- **11 task buckets:** 3 DBPM-gated (NER, RE, QAR) and 8 ungated (summarisation, medication extraction, temporal events, risk identification, risk state machine, recommendations, risk-grounded QA, visualisation payload).
- **Candidate signatures $\sigma$:** lowercase entity string (NER), uppercase `(h_type, r, t_type)` triple (RE), lowercase question template + error-class disambiguator (QAR).
- **Concurrency:** 16 worker processes × 4 patient coroutines = 64-way effective concurrency; async aiohttp to two vLLM servers, 180s timeout, 3 retries.

### Memory formalism (Section 3)
- Two real-valued functions $B_j(\sigma,t) \in [0,1]$ (blocklist severity) and $W_j(\sigma,t) \in [0,1]$ (whitelist confidence), updated by operator $U$ on each verdict event $(\sigma_j, t_j, v_j, \tau_j, s_j)$.
- Four deployment constraints: **(C1)** O(1) amortised gate query (hash-set membership over precomputed per-task block/downgrade/whitelist indices); **(C2)** bounded memory with eviction (caps $C_t = 200/300/150$ for NER/RE/QAR); **(C3)** values clipped to $[0,1]$; **(C4)** stability under non-stationarity via wall-clock half-life decay $h_t = 5/10/10$ days (NER/RE/QAR).
- Update rule (Eq. 3): $\sigma_{\text{sev}} \leftarrow \min\{1, \rho_v \cdot \sigma_{\text{sev}} + w_s \cdot \eta_{v,t}\}$, with learning rates $\eta_{v,t} \in \{0.05, 0.08, 0.15, 0.20\}$ and source weights $w_s \in \{1.0 \text{ (direct)}, 0.5 \text{ (rule-based)}, 0.2\text{–}0.4 \text{ (cross-task)}\}$.
- Three-tier gate policy (Eq. 4): whitelist exit (ALLOW) → blocklist with min support (BLOCK) → downgrade band (DOWNGRADE×0.7 confidence) → default ALLOW. Structural guarantee: cross-task-only severity is capped at 0.55 < all block thresholds (0.60+), so a candidate reaches BLOCK only if its history includes at least one direct verdict.

### Cross-task pattern propagation (Section 4.4)
- RE → NER: hard-failed relation downgrades each of its head/tail entity strings in NER memory (cross-task weight, increment halved).
- QAR → NER: hard-failed QA pair propagates at the lowest weight to entities in the answer text (long-form answers mix entities with prose).
- NER → RE: hard-failed entity enters a *preemption set*; future relations built on that entity are dropped before verifier (categorical, not graded).
- Four invariants: whitelist protection, propagation cap $\kappa \geq 3$, severity-zone guard (0.55 cap), recursion prevention. Isolated contribution measured at 0.098% of pipeline throughput (Appendix A.7, below noise floor).

### The Ontology-Violation Filter (Section 4.7)
- Deterministic predicate over 29 admissible `(h_type, r, t_type)` triples (ENTITY_RELATION_PRIORS) plus 32-entry alias map (RELATION_ALIAS); case-normalised; fires before the verifier and populates the same `_re_block` cache that the verifier-fed pathway fails to populate.

### Five question-answering signal sources (Section 4.8)
1. Category-aggregate fail-mass (category-level).
2. Verifier parse-failure flag (per-pair).
3. Per-category fail-rate $P(\text{FAIL} \mid \text{cat}) = \text{count}_{\text{FAIL}} / (\text{count}_{\text{FAIL}} + \text{count}_{\text{SUCCESS}})$.
4. Per-patient upstream difficulty (Eq. 8) combined with #3.
5. **NER-coverage intersection** (Eq. 9): `ner_cats(patient) = {fact.category : fact ∈ accepted_facts(patient)}`; gate fires DOWNGRADE if asked category ∉ ner_cats(patient). 9/11 categories have a matching NER bucket; the remaining 2 (adverse_event, outcome_mortality) fall back to source #4.

### Evaluation protocol
- **Held-out 5,000-patient cohort:** stratified random sample (age × gender × document-length tertile → 24 non-empty strata), floor allocation then proportional residual, seeded `random.Random(42)`, identifier list released as `ablation_5k_uids.json`. Same cohort reused across all ablation arms for patient-aligned paired comparisons.
- **Four configurations** (Table 6): (1) Ontology Filter ON/OFF, (2) NER-coverage gate ON/OFF, (3) DBPM full ON vs full OFF (master switch), (4) 167k production cross-check.
- **Selectivity metric:** **lift** $= P(\text{gate flags} \mid \text{verifier=FAIL}) / P(\text{gate flags} \mid \text{verifier=PASS})$ with delta-method 95% CI on $\log(\text{lift})$; $H_0: \text{lift}=1$.
- **Measurement discipline:** DOWNGRADE-first (gate tags, doesn't remove); pair-count deltas are reported alongside tagging volume to distinguish tagging from suppression.

## Results & Highlights

### Production-scale characterisation (Table 5)
- **Throughput:** 167,034 patients over ~12 days; 102.9 s/patient mean (NER 24.6 / RE 14.7 / QAR 43.1 s).
- **Yields:** 4,471,110 named entities; 4,915,334 candidate relations (4,129,537 accepted); 1,035,199 question-answer pairs.
- **Precision:** relation 0.994, verifier 0.945, final-output 0.888; mean entity coverage 0.773.
- **Verdict stream:** 1,367,615 events at full scale (Table 7); 785,797 Stage-3 classifier (relation) rejections, 548,093 Stage-4 QA verifier rejections, ontology-mismatch rejections 33,678 across 6 reason buckets.

### Finding 1 — Verifier-fed channel failure (Section 6.1)
- After full 12-day run: **NER blocklist = 5,860 patterns, RE blocklist = 0 patterns, QA blocklist = 11 patterns.**
- Despite 785,797 Stage-3 rejections, no relation signature was ever consolidated into memory. Mechanism: per-signature aggregation under `(h_type, r, t_type)` key + 10-day wall-clock half-life + prune-on-save below severity 0.01; relation rejections recurred too infrequently per signature relative to decay.
- **Generalisation:** Any inference-time memory accumulating per-signature statistics over free-form LLM output is exposed to the same diffusion. The mitigation is to map output onto a bounded canonical space — which is what the ontology filter does.

### Finding 2 — Ontology-Violation Filter (Section 6.2)
- **Isolated ON/OFF ablation on held-out cohort:** suppresses 1,207 relation candidates (0.98% of 123,460).
- **Six threshold-clearing patterns** recur with the same rank ordering on held-out as on the development cohort: `tests-associated_with-diagnosis`, `symptoms-manifests-diagnosis`, `symptoms-causes-diagnosis`, `treatment-prevents-symptoms`, `history-associated_with-diagnosis`, `diagnosis-associated_with-history`.
- **Total event growth:** 2,486 (dev cohort) → 49,734 (held-out) — 20× growth for 25× patient increase; sub-linearity confirms concentration on a fixed type-triple alphabet rather than dispersion.
- **No precision or runtime delta:** verifier precision proxy 1.0000 in both arms; wall-time difference 26 s across ~9 hours (0.08%, within noise).

### Finding 3 — Five QA signal sources (Section 6.3)

| Source | Granularity | Lift | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|---|
| Category-aggregate fail-mass | category-level | 0.006 (anti-selective) | aggregation-level mismatch |
| Verifier uncertainty | per-pair | — | signal absence |
| Per-category fail-rate | category-level | 0.05 | counting asymmetry |
| Per-patient difficulty | per-patient, cross-task | 0.00 | cross-task orthogonality |
| **NER-coverage intersection** | per-patient, cross-task | **1.84** | **working** |

- NER-coverage gate on development cohort: path lift **1.89** across 9 mapped categories; global blended lift 1.59.
- NER-coverage gate on held-out cohort (25× larger): path lift **1.84** (95% CI [1.75, 1.93], p<10⁻¹⁵), 0.360 of FAIL pairs tagged vs 0.196 of PASS pairs.
- Triangulated path lift across 4 measurements: 1.52 / 1.71 / 1.84 / 1.89 — all above the 1.5 selectivity threshold; the held-out full-DBPM measurement at 1.84 is the primary result.
- **Mechanism integrity check:** Of 17,703 covered-PASS pairs in mapped categories on held-out, **zero tagged** — confirming covered pairs hit the early-ALLOW exit.

### Finding 4 — Per-category operating envelope (Section 6.4, Table 8)

| Category | $n_{\text{PASS}}$ | $n_{\text{FAIL}}$ | cov% | lift | regime |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| diagnosis | 4,764 | 105 | 95.7 | **17.13** | selective |
| risk_factor | 564 | 448 | 53.7 | 2.16 | selective |
| outcome_clinicalstatus | 3,191 | 448 | 4.4 | 1.05 | categorical |
| outcome_disposition | 2,235 | 419 | 1.7 | 1.02 | categorical |
| outcome_mortality | 697 | 351 | 0.0 (no-NER) | 0.00 | fallback |
| adverse_event | 2,517 | 443 | 0.0 (no-NER) | 0.00 | fallback |
| history | 1,971 | 433 | 42.8 | undefined | no tagging |
| observation | 2,979 | 416 | 1.3 | undefined | no tagging |
| symptoms | 3,944 | 303 | 96.8 | undefined | saturated |
| treatment | 4,143 | 260 | 97.7 | undefined | saturated |
| tests | 4,130 | 242 | 94.8 | undefined | saturated |

- **Diagnosis** is the standout: 95.7% coverage but lifts to 17.13 *only under full DBPM* — the ontology filter removes spurious relations that would otherwise inflate the coverage signal. In isolation the diagnosis category is anti-selective; the Filter upstream cleans the signal for the Gate downstream — "the principle operating one level up."
- **Saturation degenerates selectivity:** For symptoms/treatment/tests, coverage is 95–98% so the gate allows nearly everyone. For history (42.8%) and observation (1.3%), the gate simply does not fire on this cohort.
- **Per-category instability for small $n_{\text{FAIL}}$:** The diagnosis lift swung between near-zero and large positive across runs/configurations; the aggregate path lift is stable, per-category lifts on small strata are not.

### Finding 5 — Tagging, not suppression (Section 6.5, Table 9/10)
- DBPM-full ON vs OFF pair-count deltas: NER +0.19% (within noise), RE −0.74% (Filter's 913 suppressions), QAR −0.03% (within noise) — the gate itself removes no pairs.
- DBPM tags 7,493 QA pairs total: 1,392 verifier-FAIL (36.0%) + 6,101 verifier-PASS (19.6%); all 6,101 PASS tags are attributable to DBPM (zero in OFF arm).
- **Aggregate DBPM contribution:** 913 relation suppressions + 6,101 QA tags = **7,014 gating events = 2.44% of pipeline pairs touched** by the new DBPM mechanisms.
- **Runtime overhead:** 40 s across ~9 hours (0.13%) — below the ±140 s pipeline variance floor; gate produces no measurable speedup in DOWNGRADE-first mode.

## Critical Assessment

### Novelty
- The **generator + verifier + memory gate** decomposition is not new (the paper positions DBPM against Self-Refine, Reflexion, ReAct, AutoGen, MetaGPT, Generative Agents, LLM-as-judge, ACE, RAG, RETRO, MetaMap/cTAKES, etc.). The **novelty is the production-scale empirical characterisation** and the **five-source ablation of the QA gate** — particularly the controlled isolation of *structurally distinct* failure modes (aggregation mismatch / signal absence / counting asymmetry / cross-task orthogonality). The structural principle is concrete and falsifiable.
- Closest concurrent work: **Agentic Context Engineering (ACE, Zhang et al., 2026)** — both update deployment-time state without weight modification; ACE updates a prompt-side playbook, DBPM updates a verification-side gate. They ask different questions.

### Rigor
- **Strong:** Paired ablations on a locked 5,000-patient cohort across 3 configurations; stratification on age × gender × document length; delta-method 95% CI on log lift with $p < 10^{-15}$; structural-correctness check (zero covered-PASS pairs tagged); full 167k production cross-check; construct-overlap caveat explicitly disclosed.
- **Caveat 1 (construct overlap, Section 8.3):** The development-cohort hypothesis test that motivated the NER-coverage gate used the *same construct* (per-patient NER coverage vs per-patient QA failure) that the deployed gate reads. The development lift is therefore not a held-out estimate. The paper partially mitigates by triangulating four measurements (1.52/1.71/1.84/1.89) but explicitly identifies a fresh-cohort validation as future work.
- **Caveat 2 (per-category instability):** The diagnosis lift swung between near-zero and 17+ across runs and configurations; the aggregate path lift is stable, but per-category conclusions on small $n_{\text{FAIL}}$ strata are not robust.
- **Caveat 3 (verifier regime):** MMed-Llama-3.1 70B is conservative-pass; precision-proxy delta is 1.0000/1.0000 — DBPM cannot be shown to reduce downstream hallucinations as measured by the verifier. The paper is honest about this.
- **Caveat 4 (single-corpus):** All results on PMC-Patients only (mostly human case reports, 3,634 veterinary retained but not separately analysed). EHR validation reserved for future work.

### Reproducibility
- All code released openly; PMC-Patients is public; the 5,000-patient identifier list is seeded and reproducible.
- Six ablation flags exposed (`BPM_ENABLE_CROSS_TASK`, `BPM_ENABLE_THREE_TIER`, `BPM_ENABLE_UNCERTAINTY`, `BPM_ENABLE_SOURCE_WEIGHTS`, `M1_QA_GATE_HARD_BLOCK`, `BPM_DISABLE` master switch) with full concept-to-identifier mapping (Tables 12, 13).
- The five QA signal sources are documented as a repository changelog — a reader can reproduce source-by-source outcomes.

### Significance
- **Highly significant for production deployment of LLM generator-verifier pipelines.** The "configured but empirically empty at scale" failure is a real and dangerous deployment hazard (a pipeline that relies on a pattern-memory blocklist for safety would ship without that guarantee being enforceable). The paper releases `crosscheck_F1_167k_blocklist.py` specifically for at-scale blocklist-population validation.
- **Transferable principle** across domains: code generation, scientific extraction, agentic tool use — anywhere a cheap gate precedes an expensive verifier.

### Red flags
- **Generalisation from a single deployment:** The "most natural design fails at scale" finding rests on one generator-verifier pair (Llama-3.3 70B + MMed-Llama-3.1 70B) on one domain (clinical NLP, mostly PMC case reports). Whether the diffusion failure generalises to other verifier architectures, prompts, or rejection distributions is asserted but not tested.
- **"Necessary but not sufficient" honesty:** The paper explicitly notes that alignment with the verifier's question is necessary but not sufficient; the per-category envelope shows the working signal still degenerates under saturation, sparsity, or no-bucket conditions. This is intellectual honesty, but it also means the headline 1.84 lift is conditional on a specific operating regime.
- **No downstream-quality measurement:** The paper is upfront that it makes no claim about whether DBPM's suppressions are *clinically correct*. Suppressed relations might be wrong (over-rejection) or right (precision gain) — this is reserved for separate dataset-quality work that does not exist in this paper.
- **Runtime overhead is below noise floor:** The 0.13% wall-time delta (40 s across 9 hours) cannot support any claim about cost saving in the measured regime; in DOWNGRADE-first mode, the gate provides tagging value but not compute saving.
- **The "tagging, not suppression" choice is a system-design preference,** not an algorithmic one — it papers over the harder downstream question of what should happen to a tagged candidate.
- **Allow-set dominance:** At end of run, NER channel has 96,708 whitelisted signatures against 2,991 blocklist patterns; QA channel has 11 whitelist vs 34 blocklist. This is consistent with the early-ALLOW exit design but suggests most candidates bypass belief-based gating — the gate's selective effect is concentrated on a minority of cases.

## Strengths

- **Production-scale evidence** (167k narratives, 1.37M verdict events, 785k relation rejections) rather than toy-scale benchmarking.
- **Clean ablation design** with **five QA-filter variants**, each with a **structurally distinct failure reason** — a model for how to report negative results.
- **Transferable, falsifiable design principle:** "Gate selectivity requires testing the verifier's evidence, not its output." The four negative cases make the principle testable, not just plausible.
- **Three-tier gate policy with structural guarantees:** cross-task-only severity capped at 0.55 < all block thresholds (0.60+) — a candidate reaches BLOCK only with at least one direct verdict. This is a *structural* separation, not a tuned heuristic.
- **HONEST measurement discipline:** DOWNGRADE-first (tag, don't remove) preserves clinical audit trail; 7 ablation flags exposed; construct-overlap caveat disclosed; per-category instability caveat disclosed; verifier's conservative-pass regime disclosed.
- **At-scale observability tooling:** `crosscheck_F1_167k_blocklist.py` shipped specifically to detect the "configured but empty" failure in other deployments.
- **Reproducibility:** All code, the seeded 5k cohort, ablation runners, gate-decision graders released; full concept-to-identifier mapping provided.

## Limitations

- **Single-corpus evaluation** (PMC-Patients, mostly human case reports); EHR validation explicitly future work.
- **Construct overlap in the NER-coverage gate's hypothesis test** (the dev-cohort lift is not held-out); mitigated by 4-measurement triangulation (1.52/1.71/1.84/1.89) but not eliminated.
- **No downstream clinical-correctness evaluation:** Paper makes no claim that suppressed relations or tagged QA pairs improve dataset quality; stratified human review reserved for separate work.
- **Verifier precision-proxy delta = 0** (MMed-Llama is conservative-pass); cannot claim DBPM reduces verifier-measured downstream hallucinations in this regime.
- **Operating envelope is narrow:** NER-coverage signal is selective only in mid-spectrum coverage (diagnosis, risk_factor); degenerates to categorical at sparse coverage (outcome_clinicalstatus, outcome_disposition), to no-tagging under saturation (symptoms, treatment, tests) or low-coverage inactive (history, observation), and to no-application under missing NER buckets (adverse_event, outcome_mortality).
- **Per-category instability for small $n_{\text{FAIL}}$:** Diagnosis lift swings between near-zero and 17+ across runs.
- **Runtime overhead (0.13%) is below the pipeline's noise floor** — no claim of cost saving in DOWNGRADE-first mode.
- **Cross-task propagation's isolated contribution (0.098% of throughput)** is below the per-stage non-determinism floor; cannot be independently validated in this paper.
- **The principle is necessary but not sufficient** — alignment with the verifier's question alone does not guarantee selectivity (the per-category envelope shows degeneration under saturation).
- **Allow-set dominance** (e.g., 96,708 NER whitelist vs 2,991 blocklist) means the gate's selective effect is concentrated on a minority of cases.

## Relevance

与用户的研究兴趣直接相关：这是一篇对生产规模 LLM 推理时门控可靠性的系统性实证研究，覆盖了 Inference Evaluation（大规模生成-验证流水线的选择性与精度评估）、Computation Reliability & Precision Errors（"配置存在但生产规模静默失败"是一种具体的可靠性失效模式，作者提供了 `crosscheck_F1_167k_blocklist.py` 作为检测工具），并提出一个可迁移到任何 generator-verifier 架构的设计原则。论文给出的"信号必须探测验证器自身回答的同一证据"原则对部署 LLM-as-judge 系统的工程师具有直接参考价值，对模型系统卡（Model System Cards）中关于"推理时可靠性"的章节撰写有借鉴意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the "gate must test the verifier's evidence" principle transfer to non-clinical domains (legal, code, general QA, scientific extraction, agentic tool use) and to verifier architectures other than MMed-Llama-3.1 70B?
- What is the gate's behaviour under distribution shift (PMC case reports → EHR narratives)? The authors reserve this for future work but the structural expectations under EHR distribution shift are discussed in Appendix A.8.
- Could a held-out cohort drawn from a population the development-cohort hypothesis test did not consume validate (or refute) the 1.52/1.71/1.84/1.89 triangulation as a genuine generalisation estimate rather than a within-corpus consistency?
- For the diagnosis category that swings between anti-selective and lift 17.13 across configurations, what is the noise floor on per-category lifts for small $n_{\text{FAIL}}$, and could bootstrap CIs replace single-run point estimates?
- Could the Ontology-Violation Filter's 29-triple alphabet be extended to broader relation ontologies (e.g., UMLS relations) to broaden the signal source?
- Is there a principled way to combine the five failed signal sources' residual information (e.g., difficulty score's distribution characteristics) with the NER-coverage signal to recover selectivity in the no-bucket and saturated regimes?
- What would schema closure (adding `Adverse_Event` and `Outcome_Mortality` NER buckets) yield empirically — would it close the gap between global blended lift (1.52) and path lift (1.84)?
- Could the same NER-coverage principle apply at *pair* level rather than *category* level — e.g., asking whether a specific question template's required entities are present, rather than whether the category's typical entities are present?
- Is the 10-day wall-clock half-life too aggressive given PMC-Patients' 12-day full-corpus span? Would a longer half-life (or a reset-on-success rule) have allowed the verifier-fed RE channel to populate at scale without resorting to the ontology signal?
- What does the verifier's conservative-pass regime cost in terms of dataset precision that DBPM's ontology signal might recover (if the precision-proxy delta is 0, are there precision *floor* failures that ontology suppression prevents)?
