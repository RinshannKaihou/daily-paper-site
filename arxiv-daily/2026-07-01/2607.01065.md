> **一句话总结：** 针对 LLM 长上下文 KV 缓存压缩，识别出标准 ℓ2 K-means 在高维低比特下发生"质心收缩"问题，提出将质心分解为 gain × shape 的 Gain-Shape K-means (GSKM) 并结合对数平滑的梯度权重，在 RQ 管线中构建 GSRQ；LLaMA-3-8B 上 1-bit LongBench 平均准确率从 VQLLM 的 11.34 提升到 33.54（+22.20 pp），0.75-bit 端到端解码延迟最高实现 3.40× 加速（32K 上下文，A100）。

> **TL;DR:** GSRQ diagnoses *centroid shrinkage* in standard ℓ2 K-means when clustering high-dimensional KV vectors at sub-1-bit rates — Lloyd averaging inflates ℓ2 loss while collapsing angular alignment — and replaces it with **Gain-Shape K-means (GSKM)**: a drop-in primitive that parameterizes each centroid as gain × unit-norm shape and updates them separately. Combined with a log-smoothed gradient-weighting scheme inside an RQ pipeline, the resulting **GSRQ** lifts LLaMA-3-8B 1-bit LongBench average from 11.34 → 33.54 (+22.20 pp over VQLLM) while also delivering 1.59×–3.40× per-token decoding speedups at long contexts (A100, 0.75-bit).

## 核心贡献

- **诊断高维量化失效机理：** 证明标准 ℓ2 K-means 在高维、低比特（under-capacity）下质心会因向量平均相消而收缩（centroid shrinkage），从而削弱 ℓ2 失真中 $\cos\theta$ 项的灵敏度并降低方向保真度；该现象在 RQ 的多阶段、每阶段小码本的设定下尤为严重，且值（value）比键（key）受影响更大、residual 比原向量更甚。
- **提出 Gain-Shape K-means (GSKM)：** 将质心分解为标量 gain $g_k \ge 0$ 与单位范数 shape $s_k$，shape 用归一化向量的"均值再归一化"做闭式更新（与角度对齐目标的解析最优解一致），gain 用均值投影更新并带 $\max(0, \cdot)$ 钳位；保持与 K-means 同阶复杂度、且不引入额外超参数。
- **构建 Gain-Shape Residual Quantization (GSRQ)：** 在 PQ + RQ 框架中以 GSKM 替换标准 K-means；并引入基于样本损失梯度范数 $\nabla_{x_i} L$ 的 **log-smoothed 加权**（把原始权重 max/median ≈ 400× 压缩到 <10×），稳定对 outlier token 的处理。
- **实验结果：** 在 LLaMA-2-7B/3-8B、Mistral-7B、Qwen3-8B 与 AIME24/25、RULER、LongBench、GSM8K、MATH-500、WikiText-2/C4 上系统验证 2/1/0.75/0.375 bit 各档位；1-bit LongBench 平均从 11.34 → 33.54（+22.20 pp）；0.75-bit 在 8K→128K 上下文实现 1.59×–3.40× 解码加速。

## Key Contributions

- **Diagnosis of high-dimensional K-means failure.** Quantitatively shows that under an ℓ2 objective, Lloyd averaging inflates the squared-error term while collapsing the angular alignment $\|\hat x \cdot \mu\|$ — formalizing a "shrinking → less angle-sensitive → more shrinkage" vicious cycle. Values shrink more than keys; residuals shrink more than originals; both effects worsen with subspace dimension $D$ and codebook sparsity.
- **Gain–Shape K-means (GSKM).** Each centroid is parameterized as $\mu_k = g_k s_k$ with $\|s_k\|=1$. The shape update normalizes samples then averages then re-normalizes (closed-form maximizer of $\sum_i y_i^\top s$), and the gain update is a non-negative mean projection onto the shape. It has identical asymptotic cost to K-means and adds no hyperparameters.
- **Gradient-weighted GSKM with log smoothing.** Following SqueezeLLM-style loss-sensitivity weighting, GSRQ weights each sample by $w_i = \log(1+\lambda\|\nabla_{x_i}L\|_2)$ with $\lambda$ chosen so that the median of $w$ equals a target $\tau$. Log smoothing tames gradient outliers (max/median ratio drops from >400× to <10×) and outperforms both unweighted and raw-gradient-weighted variants.
- **GSRQ pipeline.** PQ partitions each head-concatenated KV vector into $M$ disjoint subspaces of dimension $D$; RQ runs $R$ residual stages per subspace with codebook size $K=256$. Bit rate $b = R\log_2 K / D$. Empirically, $(D,R)=(128,32/16/12)$ for 2/1/0.75 BPA and $(256,12)$ for 0.375 BPA.
- **Empirical wins across models and tasks.** Beats CQ, AnTKV, KIVI, VQLLM on perplexity (WikiText-2/C4) for LLaMA-2-7B, LLaMA-3-8B, Mistral-7B at 2/1/0.75/0.375 bit; beats VQLLM on commonsense + math few-shot for LLaMA-3-8B and Qwen3-8B; beats VQLLM/KIVI on LongBench and MATH-500/RULER for LLaMA-3-8B-Instruct; preserves competition-level reasoning (AIME24/25) at 2-bit for Qwen3-8B.

## Methodology

- **Problem setting.** Long-context LLM serving is bottlenecked by KV cache memory that grows linearly with sequence length × batch (e.g., LLaMA-3.1-8B at 128K context, batch 8 → 128 GB KV cache). The paper targets post-training sub-1-bit per-element quantization of the KV cache via Product + Residual Vector Quantization, leaving model weights at FP16.

- **Why standard K-means fails in this regime.** Two geometric facts are coupled by the ℓ2 distortion:
  - $\|x-\mu\|_2^2 = \|x\|_2^2 + \|\mu\|_2^2 - 2\|x\|_2\|\mu\|_2\cos\theta$ (law of cosines).
  - The ℓ2-optimal centroid $\mu^\star = \mathbb{E}[x\mid x\in C]$ satisfies $\|\mu^\star\|_2 \le \mathbb{E}[\|x\|_2\mid x\in C]$ with equality only if all samples in $C$ are perfectly aligned.

  In high-dimensional subspaces with limited per-stage codebook size (the "under-capacity" regime), assigned samples are nearly orthogonal, mean cancellation shrinks $\|\mu^\star\|_2$, and the angular term in the distortion scales with $\|\mu\|_2$, so the objective loses sensitivity to $\cos\theta$. The result is a self-reinforcing cycle of poor direction estimation and persistent shrinkage. Figure 2 in the paper empirically confirms this on LLaMA-3-8B's KV cache, and shows shrinkage is stronger for values than keys and stronger for 1st residuals than originals.

- **GSKM algorithm (Algorithm 1).**
  1. **Parameterization.** $\mu_k = g_k s_k$, $g_k \ge 0$, $\|s_k\|_2 = 1$.
  2. **Assignment.** $k_i^\star = \arg\max_k \big(2g_k x_i^\top s_k - g_k^2\big)$ (drops the $\|x_i\|^2$ constant).
  3. **Shape update.** Normalize each assigned $x_i$ to $y_i = x_i/\|x_i\|_2$, then $s_k \leftarrow \big(\sum_{i\in I_k} y_i\big) / \big\|\sum_{i\in I_k} y_i\big\|_2$ — the closed-form maximizer of the angular alignment $\sum_i y_i^\top s$.
  4. **Gain update.** $g_k \leftarrow \max\!\big(0, \tfrac{1}{|I_k|}\sum_{i\in I_k} x_i^\top s_k\big)$.

  The procedure is **not** a strict block-coordinate descent for a single ℓ2 objective (shape uses an angular criterion), so no monotonic decrease is guaranteed. Empirically, however, this decoupled update is far more robust in the under-capacity regime.

- **Gradient-weighted GSKM (Section 5.3).** Raw gradient norms $\|\nabla_{x_i}L\|_2$ are heavy-tailed (max/median > 400× for both K and V). GSRQ applies $w_i = \log(1+\lambda\|\nabla_{x_i}L\|_2)$ where $\lambda$ rescales the sample median to a target $\tau$ (default $\tau=1.0$); the Appendix B ablation confirms $\tau=1.0$ is optimal at 0.375 and 0.75 BPA. Weights are applied at every residual stage so the sensitivity prior is consistent across the RQ hierarchy.

- **Pipeline.** For each transformer layer, K and V tensors are concatenated across heads to form $D$-dim subspaces (per-layer/per-head subspaces are studied; the final pipeline uses subspace partitioning inside heads). GSKM (or weighted GSKM) is run per subspace per stage; reconstruction at decode time accumulates $R$ codewords and rescales by stage-specific scales. Codebooks are calibrated on 128 sequences of length 2048 from SlimPajama; inference uses a custom PyTorch + Triton kernel that fuses bit-packing of new KV, on-the-fly unpack + dequant + RoPE + attention, avoiding FP16 materialization.

## Results & Highlights

- **Perplexity (WikiText-2 / C4).** At matched bit budgets, GSRQ has the lowest PPL among CQ, AnTKV, KIVI, VQLLM on LLaMA-2-7B, LLaMA-3-8B, Mistral-7B. Highlights (LLaMA-3-8B / WikiText-2):
  - 2-bit: GSRQ 5.91 vs VQLLM n/a (best non-GSRQ = CQ 6.09, AnTKV 6.10).
  - 1-bit: GSRQ 7.20 vs CQ 9.56, AnTKV 9.62.
  - 0.75-bit: GSRQ 8.46 vs CQ 11.18, AnTKV 10.41.
  - 0.375-bit: GSRQ 12.77 vs CQ 22.80, AnTKV 17.70.
  - On C4 the gap is larger: LLaMA-3-8B 1-bit C4: GSRQ 8.92 vs CQ 81.74, AnTKV 74.47.
- **LongBench (LLaMA-3-8B-Instruct).** 1-bit average: GSRQ 33.54 vs VQLLM 11.34 vs KIVI-1 5.97; 0.75-bit GSRQ 29.72 still beats VQLLM at 1-bit (11.34). Per-task, GSRQ at 1-bit is best across NarrativeQA (18.59), Qasper (28.73), MultiFieldQA (30.42), QMSum (20.83), Musique (17.08), TriviaQA (83.41), SamSum (35.72).
- **Commonsense + Math few-shot (LLaMA-3-8B).** 1-bit average: GSRQ 57.78 vs VQLLM 44.60; 0.75-bit GSRQ 53.38 already exceeds VQLLM 1-bit 44.60. Largest deltas on ARC-C (52.05 vs 37.03), MMLU (54.32 vs 28.29), HellaSwag (69.56 vs 51.65), GSM8K-MathQA (34.84 vs 25.23).
- **GSM8K (5-shot).** LLaMA-3-8B-Instruct 1-bit: GSRQ 26.16 vs VQLLM 2.27. Mistral-7B-Instruct 1-bit: GSRQ 13.65 vs VQLLM 3.94.
- **MATH-500 / RULER (LLaMA-3-8B-Instruct).** 2-bit: MATH-500 19.0 (FP16 20.4), RULER 96.0 (FP16 96.0). 1-bit: MATH-500 14.2 vs KIVI 0.8 / VQLLM 0.2; RULER 72.0 vs KIVI 13.0 / VQLLM 20.0.
- **Qwen3-8B (additional model family).** 2-bit AIME24/25: GSRQ 20.0/16.7 vs VQLLM 13.3/13.3. LongBench 1-bit: GSRQ 20.21 vs VQLLM 12.23. Perplexity 1-bit: GSRQ 10.22 vs VQLLM 15.79 (WikiText-2); 9.84 vs 13.52 (C4).
- **Ablations (LLaMA-3-8B, 0.375-bit, WikiText-2).** Standard KM 19.50 → Unweighted GSKM 12.77 → Raw-Weighted GSKM 13.57 → Log-Weighted GSKM 12.49. Largest jump comes from K-means → GSKM; log smoothing stabilizes the weighting and trims another 0.28 PPL.
- **GSKM vs K-means on real KV cache.** At $K=256$, GSKM achieves better directional alignment (cosine) and lower gain error than KM with $K=1024$ on both keys and values, and the advantage is most pronounced on values and on residuals — directly validating the "weakly-structured regime" hypothesis.
- **Convergence.** GSKM reaches within noise of optimal PPL by 40 iterations; 100 iterations is recommended.
- **Latency (A100 80GB, LLaMA-3-8B, custom Triton kernels).**
  - Layer-wise per-token latency at 1K context: 2.3 ms (FP16) → 1.2 ms (GSRQ 0.75-bit) at batch 8; 7.9 ms → 2.3 ms at batch 32 (3.4× speedup).
  - End-to-end per-token decoding at batch 1: 8K → 1.59× (70.68 ms → 44.48 ms); 16K → 2.25×; 32K → 3.02×; 64K → 3.40× (486.73 → 143.29 ms); 128K → FP16 OOM, GSRQ 262.12 ms.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** Centroid shrinkage in high-dimensional ℓ2 K-means is a known geometric phenomenon (Hall et al., 2005; spherical K-means / Banerjee et al., 2005; "spherical quantization" already discards gain for direction-only clustering). The paper's contribution is to (i) explicitly connect shrinkage to the ℓ2 distortion's angular term and the under-capacity regime of RQ, (ii) propose a *retention* formulation (keep both gain and shape) rather than discarding gain like spherical K-means, and (iii) demonstrate decisive downstream impact on sub-1-bit KV-cache compression. This is a real conceptual advance over simply normalizing before K-means.
- **Rigor.** Strong empirical battery (4 models × 4 bit rates × multiple tasks including LongBench, MATH-500, RULER, AIME) with matched-bit comparisons; per-task LongBench breakdowns are reported; variance/seed counts are not reported but the gains are large enough to be plausibly significant. The convergence sweep and the $(\tau, \lambda)$ ablation are well-designed.
- **Reproducibility.** Drop-in algorithmic change, full hyper-parameter disclosure, and kernel description help. Missing items: code repository URL, calibration dataset license/handling, per-seed confidence intervals, and timing methodology (warm-up, CUDA graph capture, token batch shaping). The custom Triton kernel makes exact reproduction non-trivial without the official code.
- **Significance.** If the gain holds on independent implementations, GSRQ meaningfully shifts the practical Pareto frontier for long-context LLM serving: 0.75-bit KV cache at near-FP16 quality on RULER, with 3×+ decoding speedup at 32–64K contexts. Even in the 1-bit regime, the method retains *usable* accuracy (33.54 LongBench avg, 14.2 MATH-500) where prior RQ baselines collapse to near-random.
- **Red flags.**
  - Headline "+22.20 pp" 1-bit LongBench gain is computed against a single RQ baseline (VQLLM); the comparison set is narrow and excludes newer saliency-aware / outlier-rotated methods (e.g., ZipCache, KVQuant, RotateKV) at sub-1-bit rates.
  - GSM8K 1-bit Mistral-7B (13.65) and Qwen3-8B are evaluated only at 2-bit for AIME; sub-1-bit reasoning robustness is not fully characterized across model families.
  - The paper notes that GSKM is **not** a strict block-coordinate descent and lacks a monotonic-decrease guarantee — a defensible framing, but downstream users should not assume standard K-means-style convergence theory.
  - Sub-1-bit storage overhead (codebook tables, scales, indices) is not separately quantified in the abstract; the reported "bits per activation" is a *payload* figure, and total memory including codebook metadata could shift the breakeven vs. lower-overhead scalar quantizers at higher bit rates.
  - Calibration uses 128 × 2048 SlimPajama sequences; sensitivity to calibration distribution is not ablated.

## Strengths

- **Mechanistic diagnosis.** Identifies a concrete, geometric cause of a long-standing failure mode in high-dimensional VQ rather than chasing a benchmark with a black-box fix.
- **Algorithmic simplicity.** GSKM has the same complexity as K-means, no new hyperparameters, and is genuinely drop-in. Convergence is empirically fast (≤40 iters to plateau; 100 recommended).
- **Robust weighting scheme.** Log-smoothed gradient weighting is well-motivated by the heavy-tailed gradient distribution (max/median > 400× → <10×) and ablated against raw weighting and unweighted variants.
- **Comprehensive evaluation.** Multiple model families (LLaMA-2/3, Mistral, Qwen3), four bit rates (2/1/0.75/0.375), and a wide task mix including long-context (LongBench, RULER), reasoning (GSM8K, MATH-500, AIME), and standard PPL.
- **Systems results.** Custom Triton kernels and end-to-end latency/memory measurements (1.59×–3.40× speedup, FP16 OOM at 128K but GSRQ 262 ms/token) demonstrate that the gains are not just numerical — they translate into deployable memory headroom.

## Limitations

- **Single-vector quantization only.** The method targets KV cache; weights and activations are not addressed, and benefits to *weights* may not transfer (different geometry).
- **Sub-1-bit is the headline regime.** At 2-bit the gains over VQLLM are smaller and AnTKV / CQ remain competitive; the cost-benefit vs. simpler scalar quantizers (KIVI-2 at 2.25 BPA) is not deeply analyzed.
- **Calibration cost & codebook overhead not isolated.** Per-stage codebook size $K=256$, subspace dim $D$, stage count $R$ all imply non-trivial auxiliary storage; the "BPA" figure is the quantized payload only.
- **Comparison set.** Excludes recent saliency-aware / rotation-based methods (ZipCache, KVQuant, RotateKV) at sub-1-bit; head-to-head there would strengthen the claim.
- **Convergence guarantees.** No monotonic-decrease guarantee; behavior on adversarial / out-of-distribution KV distributions is not studied.
- **Architecture coverage.** Demonstrated on dense decoders; applicability to MoE, encoder-decoder, SSM/hybrid, or sliding-window attention KV caches is not characterized.
- **Heuristic, not theory.** The shrinkage theory is geometric/intuitive; no formal rate-distortion or sample-complexity analysis for GSKM is given.

## Relevance

与用户兴趣中 **LLM 推理评估 (Inference Evaluation)** 与 **计算可靠性与精度误差 (Computation Reliability & Precision Errors)** 直接相关：KV 缓存量化是长上下文 LLM 推理的核心瓶颈，GSRQ 用一种几何上更稳健的方向保真机制替代标准 K-means，揭示并修复了高维量化中一个基础性的精度退化机制，对部署端的数值可靠性、可重复性研究与推理系统评估有方法论价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does GSRQ compose with KV-cache eviction/sparsification (H2O, Scissorhands, StreamingLLM) and with saliency-aware scalar quantizers (KIVI, ZipCache, KVQuant, RotateKV) — i.e., can GSKM-based codebooks be used *only* on the non-salient token subset?
- Does the GSKM advantage persist on MoE / hybrid / SSM backbones (Mixtral, DeepSeek-MoE, Mamba-2 hybrid, Jamba) where KV geometry differs from dense transformers?
- What is the *metadata* cost (codebook tables + scales + indices) per token, and at what bit rate does GSRQ lose its storage advantage vs. 2-bit scalar KV quantization?
- How sensitive is GSRQ to the calibration distribution (SlimPajama → code, math, multilingual)? Is online / continual re-calibration feasible at serving time?
- Can the gain/shape decomposition be applied to **weight** quantization (where channel-scale outliers dominate) and to **activation** quantization (where GeLU/attention outliers dominate) — and does the same shrinkage mechanism explain failures there?
- Is there a formal convergence or fixed-point analysis for GSKM, perhaps by reframing the shape update as an EM step on a spherical mixture with a separate gain posterior?
- How does GSRQ interact with GQA / MQA / sliding-window attention, where the KV tensor is partitioned by group and may have different subspace geometry per group?
- Can the log-smoothed gradient-weighting scheme be replaced by a tighter outlier-robust estimator (e.g., Huberized gradient norms, clipped reweighted medians) without losing the 0.28 PPL gap?
- What are the energy / throughput-per-watt numbers when GSRQ is paired with low-precision weights (INT4 / FP8) rather than FP16, to fully exploit the memory headroom GSRQ creates?
- Is the GSKM fixed-point closer to spherical K-means or to standard K-means in the high-capacity regime, and is there a smooth interpolation (e.g., $\alpha$-blended gain/shape mixing) that recovers both behaviors?
