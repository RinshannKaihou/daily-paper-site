> **一句话总结：** AGC-Bench 通过 PRISMA 系统综述 3,101 篇 AI 创造力文献并整合 78 个数据集(67 文本+11 多模态),利用 Judge Response Theory 校准三位前沿 LLM 评委的偏差并据此微调出 AGC-Judge,跨 83 个 LLM 的因子分析恢复出解释 81.5% 方差的单一创造力因子 C(类比于智力 g 因子),且在人类匹配子集上顶尖人类仍以 0.12 z 领先最强 LLM。

> **TL;DR:** AGC-Bench is the first unified, large-scale creativity benchmark for LLMs, built via a PRISMA-compliant systematic review of 3,101 papers yielding 497 unique benchmarks, of which 78 are onboarded (67 text-only, 11 multimodal) across six creative domains (brainstorming, problem solving, STEM, story/narrative, figurative language, humor) and standardized as HELM-compatible scenarios via an agentic nine-state harness. To address LLM-as-judge bias, the authors apply Judge Response Theory (JRT) — a psychometric IRT-style calibration — to a three-frontier-judge panel (Gemini-3-Flash, Grok-4.1-Fast, GPT-4.1-mini) under a planned-missing 2-of-3 design, then fine-tune Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct-2507 as a LoRA adapter on 48,299 JRT-corrected ratings to produce AGC-Judge (Spearman ρ = 0.94 in-distribution, ρ = 0.83 on held-out benchmarks). Across 83 LLMs (2024–2026), factor analysis recovers a single creativity factor `C` (α = 0.96) explaining 81.5% of variance across the six domains, separable from but correlated with fluid reasoning (ρ = +0.55 vs. Lewis–Mitchell counterfactual letter strings) and crystallized knowledge (ρ = +0.62 vs. MMLU-Pro). The benchmark responds far more to "be creative" prompting (dz = +1.40 across 18 models) than to enabling reasoning (dz = +0.34 across 10 models), supporting construct validity. On the 5-task AGC-Human cohort (201 humans, 80 LLMs from the Creativity Assessment Platform), the top human leads the top LLM (kimi-k2-0905) by 0.12 z (+0.65 vs +0.53), and LLM creativity is markedly more domain-general (Cronbach α = 0.64) than human creativity (α = 0.42, p = 0.028).

## 核心贡献
- **AGC-Bench 元基准**:基于 PRISMA 系统综述(3,101 篇→683 篇→431 篇→283 篇→276 篇 PDF→497 个独立基准→432 个创造力相关→327 个 API 可运行→159 个 HELM 场景→105 个通过发布审核→78 个最终发布)整合 78 个数据集(67 文本+11 多模态),覆盖六大创造力领域,这是同类工作中规模最大的一项。
- **基于心理测量学的 Judge Response Theory (JRT)**:将 IRT 风格的能力/难度/区分度参数化应用于 LLM-as-judge 评分,显式建模并校正三位前沿评委(Gemini-3-Flash, Grok-4.1-Fast, GPT-4.1-mini)的宽松度/严厉度偏差(planning-missing 2-of-3 设计),揭示 gpt-4.1-mini 显著偏宽松(median severity β = −0.93),gemini-3-flash 居中。
- **AGC-Judge 开源评分器**:在 48,299 条 JRT 校正后的评分上用 LoRA 微调 Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct-2507,以小部分前沿评委成本复现三位评委集合,域内 ρ=0.94,未见过的创意基准上 ρ=0.83。
- **可复现的元基准管线**:开源 PRISMA 引导的论文筛选 + 智能体九状态 onboarding harness(基于 Claude Code 状态机),支持未来增量扩展。
- **跨 83 个 LLM 的因子分析**:在 83×6 模型-领域矩阵上恢复出解释 81.5% 方差的单一创造力因子 C(α=0.96,六个领域载荷 0.87–0.94),并在排除流体推理、按评分家族分拆、bootstrap 子采样等三种攻击下均保持稳定。
- **构造效度证据**:"be creative" 提示使 18 个前沿模型提升 dz=+1.40(99.4% 通过三评委有效门),而启用推理只让 10 个模型提升 dz=+0.34,前者幅度约为后者的 4 倍,证明基准真的在测创造力。
- **人机对比 (AGC-Human)**:基于 Creativity Assessment Platform 五个任务(Alternate Uses, Scientific Creative Thinking, Design Problems, Metaphor Completion, Short Story),201 名人类与 80 个 LLM 同台,顶尖人类比顶尖 LLM(kimi-k2-0905)高 0.12 z,LLM 的创造力跨域一致性显著高于人类(α: 0.64 vs 0.42, p=0.028)。

## Key Contributions
- **AGC-Bench meta-benchmark**: 78 onboarded datasets (67 text-only, 11 multimodal) built from a PRISMA-compliant systematic review of 3,101 papers, spanning 2018–2025, with a PRISMA flow documenting 3,101 → 683 (LLM pre-screen) → 431 (data access) → 283 (human review) → 497 (unique benchmarks) → 432 → 327 → 159 → 78 stages.
- **Agentic onboarding harness**: A deterministic nine-state machine (built on Claude Code) that converts heterogeneous source-paper benchmarks into HELM-compatible scenarios, with verbatim prompt preservation, regex/code-based extraction, and human-in-the-loop review at every accept step. Produces HELM scenarios with stable IDs; rank-stability bootstrap shows Spearman ρ=0.99 between k=10 and k=50 items per (model, dataset) cell.
- **Judge Response Theory (JRT) for LLM-as-judge**: An IRT-based calibration of judge leniency/severity and discrimination applied to a three-frontier-judge panel under a planned-missing 2-of-3 design. Recovers per-model ability θ alongside per-judge severity β and discrimination α. Preserves leaderboard rank (ρ=+0.95) while flattening per-judge scale differences.
- **AGC-Judge (open-weight scorer)**: Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct-2507 with a LoRA adapter fine-tuned on 48,299 JRT-corrected ratings. Spearman ρ=0.94 on held-out items and frontier models, ρ=0.83 pooled on 3 held-out benchmarks (8,803 items), matching the three-judge ensemble at ρ≥0.97. On Orwig et al.'s OOD human-and-LLM creative writing corpus, AGC-Judge tracks human ratings comparably to a frontier GPT judge with substantially lower LLM-preference bias.
- **General creativity factor C**: Across 83 LLMs, factor analysis on the 83×6 model-by-domain composite matrix recovers a single dominant factor (first eigenvalue 4.89, the only one above Kaiser; remaining five ≤0.47; parallel analysis on 1,000 random matrices confirms it is above the noise floor). C explains 81.5% of variance, with all six domain loadings between +0.87 and +0.94, and α=0.96. Survives three validity threats: residualizing out fluid reasoning (α=0.91), re-extraction within formula-based subset (α=0.91) and within LLM-judge subset (α=0.96), and bootstrap subsampling to 60 of 83 models (eigenvalue 4.92, 95% interval [4.74, 5.05]).
- **C is separable from Gf, Gc, and scale**: C correlates with counterfactual letter-string analogies (pure-Gf) at ρ=+0.55 (n=82) and with MMLU-Pro (Gc) at ρ=+0.62 (n=57); the two correlations are statistically indistinguishable (Williams' t = +0.04, p=0.96). Among 42 open-weight models with disclosed parameters, total parameters correlate with composite at ρ=+0.69 but only ρ=+0.37 under active-parameter accounting, suggesting MoE architectures reach the top tier on total-parameter rankings without matching per-active-parameter creativity.
- **Construct-validity interventions**: "Be creative" prompt on 18 frontier models yields dz=+1.40 (99.4% pass 3-judge validity gate); enabling reasoning on 10 reasoning-capable models yields dz=+0.34 — roughly 1/4 the magnitude. Construct sensitivity favors creativity-targeted over reasoning-targeted manipulation.
- **Human comparison (AGC-Human)**: 224 humans + 125 LLM evaluations across 5 tasks (3 idea-generation + 2 creative-writing); after LLM-judge validity gate (96.0% human pass, 98.8% LLM pass), 201 humans and 80 LLMs are retained. Top human reaches +0.65 z vs. top LLM (kimi-k2-0905) at +0.53 z (gap = 0.12 z). LLM creativity is more domain-general (Cronbach α=0.64) than human creativity (α=0.42, p=0.028). AGC-Bench composite predicts human-rating agreement at r=+0.66, ρ=+0.73 (n=74 models), with partial r=+0.34 after controlling for reasoning and knowledge.

## Methodology
**Benchmark construction (PRISMA).** Six-stage pipeline: (1) Semantic Scholar harvesting of 3,101 candidate papers (2018–2025); (2) GPT-4.1 rubric-based pre-screening (5 inclusion criteria, retains 683); (3) Gemini-2.5-Flash with Google Search grounding to verify 431 have public code/data; (4) dual human review by 4 trained graduate students (rotating assignment, conflict resolution by lead authors) → 283 papers; (5) Gemini-2.5-Pro structured extraction of 276 papers into 546 candidate benchmarks across 1,160 tasks; (6) hybrid 10-pass Gemini deduplication (≥80% inter-pass agreement) → 497 unique benchmarks. Modality/infrastructure tier classification (Gemini-3-Flash-preview) yielded 241 Tier-1a (text-to-text) + 86 Tier-1b (image-to-text) candidates entering onboarding.

**Onboarding (agentic harness).** A nine-state deterministic pipeline built on Claude Code (claude-opus-4.6 as coordinator) with three design principles: verbatim prompt extraction, source-paper metric preservation, and regex/code extraction preferred over LLM inference. Each of 327 API-runnable candidates goes through paper parsing → data loading → semantic validation → prompt and judge extraction → scenario codegen → code review → pilot run → emit. Of 155 reviewed onboarding attempts, 4 recurring failure patterns were catalogued (hallucinated evaluation logic, missing LLM-judge config, prompt hallucination, metric-name mismatches) and mitigated with specific state-level validations. Outcome: 159 HELM-compatible scenarios; 78 passed the launch-readiness audit (67 text-only + 11 multimodal).

**Taxonomy (six text domains).** Brainstorming, Problem Solving, STEM, Story/Narrative, Figurative Language, Humor — drawn from human creativity research (Creative Achievement Questionnaire, K-DOCS, ICAA). Domain assignments come from a three-model LLM panel (Gemini-3-Flash-preview, Grok-4.1-Fast, GPT-4.1-mini) with majority-vote adjudication by lead authors; mean pairwise Cohen's κ ≈ 0.85, Fleiss' κ ≈ 0.85.

**Evaluation setup.** Harness extends HELM and lm-evaluation-harness. 83 text-only models from 22 providers, 2024–2026, run via vendor APIs and OpenRouter with reasoning off by default. Each (model, dataset) cell scores n=50 items sampled with a frozen seed (smaller datasets run exhaustively). Scored with each dataset's canonical metric (58 formula-based, 19 LLM-judge, 1 model-based semantic-distance). For 24 LLM-judge benchmarks, the cohort-scaling judge is Gemini-3-Flash-preview with two cross-vendor judges under planned-missing 2-of-3 design. AGC-Bench composite = per-model mean of dataset-level z-scores across 67 text-only datasets. Data-quality audit: independent LLM-judge verified 95.1% on-task rate across 6,635 text-only cells (κ=0.67 cross-family agreement).

**JRT calibration.** Bayesian graded response model from Myszkowski & Storme's Judge Response Theory framework. Fits per-unit ability θ and per-judge severity β and discrimination α from the 2-of-3 planned-missing ratings. Corrects the raw single-judge composite's left skew to an approximately normal distribution while preserving leaderboard rank (ρ=+0.95 between raw and JRT-corrected).

**AGC-Judge.** Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct-2507 fine-tuned as a LoRA adapter on 48,299 (model, item) JRT-corrected ratings. Trained with the standard causal-LM objective; released with weights and scoring harness.

**External validity.** Three external designs: (i) Lewis–Mitchell counterfactual letter-string analogies as pure-Gf probe; (ii) 1,862-item MuCE subset as out-of-cohort convergent reference; (iii) within-model interventions (be-creative vs. be-effective prompting; reasoning-mode toggle).

**AGC-Human (paired-human subset).** Five tasks from the Creativity Assessment Platform (Patterson et al. 2025): three idea-generation (Alternate Uses, Scientific Creative Thinking, Design Problems) and two creative-writing (Metaphor Completion, Short Story). Scored on novelty (Johnson et al. 2023 DSI) and diversity via embedding models, length-residualized and z-scored within (task, prompt). A fairness-aware inference prompt disclosing LLM self-preference bias is used to reduce cross-source judge bias; this reduces the gap by ~35% to +7.4 (humans 24.1, LLMs 31.5) while preserving cell-level rank ordering at r=+0.91 and improving convergent validity with the formal AGC-Human composite from ρ=+0.53 to ρ=+0.60.

## Results & Highlights
**Top overall (AGC composite, 83 models, z).** claude-opus-4.7 (+0.804), gpt-5.4 (+0.737), gpt-5.5 (+0.736), glm-5.1 (+0.711), glm-5-turbo (+0.668), claude-opus-4.6-fast (+0.663), glm-5 (+0.651), gpt-5.1 (+0.643), gemma-4-31b-it (+0.642), kimi-k2.5 (+0.635). Open-weight models are within ~0.15 z of the proprietary frontier.

**Per-domain dissociations.** kimi-k2.5 tops Problem Solving (+1.02) without entering any other top-5; claude-opus-4.6-fast leads Figurative Language (+1.36); gemma-4-31b-it enters three domain top-fives (STEM, Figurative Language, Humor) despite ranking 9th overall. Creative ability is unevenly distributed even among top models.

**JRT calibration.** Severity β ranges from lenient (gpt-4.1-mini, median β=−0.93) to near-zero (gemini-3-flash). After JRT correction, the per-model composite distribution becomes approximately normal (raw was left-skewed). Leaderboard rank is preserved at ρ=+0.95.

**AGC-Judge.** ρ=0.94 on held-out items and frontier models; ρ=0.83 on 3 held-out benchmarks (8,803 items, never seen in fine-tuning); matches the three-judge ensemble at ρ≥0.97. Tracks human ratings comparably to a frontier GPT judge with substantially lower LLM-preference bias on the Orwig et al. OOD corpus.

**C factor.** First eigenvalue 4.89, all other eigenvalues ≤0.47; parallel analysis on 1,000 random matrices places the first factor well above noise. Six domain loadings 0.87–0.94, α=0.96. Survives residualizing out fluid reasoning (α=0.91), formula-only subset (α=0.91), LLM-judge-only subset (α=0.96), bootstrap to 60/83 models (eigenvalue 4.92, 95% interval [4.74, 5.05]), and dropping the largest domain (Story/Narrative, 20 datasets; α=0.94).

**Intelligence / scale correlations.** AGC-Bench composite vs. Lewis–Mitchell letter-string analogy (Gf) ρ=+0.55 (n=82); vs. MMLU-Pro (Gc) ρ=+0.62 (n=57); difference is statistically indistinguishable (Williams' t=+0.04, p=0.96). Total parameters correlate with composite at ρ=+0.69 across 42 open-weight models; active-parameter correlation drops to ρ=+0.37.

**Construct-validity interventions.** Be-creative prompt on 18 frontier models: dz=+1.40 length-residualized, 99.4% of 2,700 generations pass the 3-judge validity gate. Reasoning-enabled baseline on 10 reasoning-capable models: dz=+0.34 — roughly 1/4 the magnitude.

**AGC-Human.** Top human (human_048) reaches +0.65 z; top LLM (kimi-k2-0905) reaches +0.53 z; gap = 0.12 z on the canonical two-component panel. LLM α=0.64 vs. human α=0.42 (p=0.028) — LLM creativity is more domain-general than human creativity. AGC-Bench composite predicts per-model agreement with human creativity ratings on an independent 1,862-item corpus at r=+0.66, ρ=+0.73 (n=74), with partial r=+0.34 after controlling for reasoning and knowledge.

**Cross-instrument convergent validity.** AGC-Human and the broader AGC-Bench composite agree at Spearman ρ=+0.56 (p<10⁻⁷) on the 80 LLMs with coverage on both.

## Critical Assessment
**Novelty.** High. Three things are new: (i) the scale — 78 onboarded datasets, 6× the prior closest work (CreativityPrism, 9 tasks); (ii) the JRT application to LLM-as-judge is a clean, principled extension of psychometrics; (iii) the empirical recovery of a single general creativity factor C in LLMs is a striking finding with implications for both model evaluation and creativity theory. The C factor parallels Spearman's g for human intelligence and is reported with the same kind of psychometric vocabulary; this is novel for LLM evaluation.

**Rigor.** Very strong. Strengths: PRISMA-compliant systematic review with explicit flow diagram and counts at every stage; planned-missing 2-of-3 judge design to decouple judges; multiple stress tests on C (residualize Gf, split by scoring family, bootstrap, drop domain); rank-stability bootstrap (ρ=0.99 between k=10 and k=50); JRT preserves leaderboard rank (ρ=+0.95); AGC-Judge generalization tested on 3 held-out benchmarks (8,803 items); data-quality audit on 6,635 cells (95.1% on-task); second-judge cross-family validation at κ=0.67. Limitations: the 78-dataset corpus is what was onboardable — coverage biases (English, text-dominant, Western creativity norms) are inherited from the source literature. The C factor's 81.5% variance-explained is high but should be read as descriptive of this benchmark's particular sample of models and tasks.

**Reproducibility.** Excellent. Open benchmark, open harness, open AGC-Judge weights, open leaderboard, PRISMA-curated inventory of 497 benchmarks, full score CSVs, on-task audit results, and a HuggingFace artifact bundle. The only non-reproducible component is the frontier-judge calls (proprietary APIs) — but AGC-Judge is positioned as a low-cost replacement.

**Significance.** Important. AGC-Bench is an order-of-magnitude larger than prior creativity benchmarks and provides the first large-scale empirical test of the artificial-general-creativity hypothesis. The C factor finding is provocative: if it replicates across other creative batteries, it suggests LLM creativity is more transferable across domains than human creativity (a result with significant practical implications for content generation, scientific ideation assistance, and creative tool design). The JRT framework is broadly applicable to other LLM-as-judge pipelines — potentially a substantial contribution to evaluation methodology in general.

**Red flags.** (1) The 81.5% single-factor result needs robustness checks across model subsets (e.g., excluding MoE, restricting to a single architecture family); the paper does report bootstrap stability and formula-only/LLM-judge-only re-extraction, which mitigates this, but a fuller sensitivity analysis is warranted. (2) JRT corrects judge leniency/severity and discrimination but does not address other known LLM-as-judge biases (position bias, verbosity bias, self-preference bias, style bias); the paper does separately document and partially mitigate LLM self-preference via a fairness-aware prompt on AGC-Human, but for the main 67-dataset benchmark, judge bias beyond severity/discrimination is not directly controlled. (3) The 5-task AGC-Human subset is small; the "top human leads" gap of 0.12 z is suggestive but not definitive — a larger paired cohort is needed. (4) The C factor is derived from one benchmark; cross-benchmark validation against an independent creativity battery (e.g., MuCE) would strengthen the "g of creativity" claim. (5) AGC-Judge is fine-tuned on three frontier judges and inherits their joint biases even after JRT calibration — though the OOD evaluation on Orwig et al. and the substantially lower LLM-preference bias mitigate this. (6) The benchmark is English-dominant and text-dominant, limiting cross-cultural and multimodal claims. (7) The "creativity" construct itself remains contested; the 78-dataset selection effectively operationalizes creativity, and that operationalization is debatable.

## Strengths
- First unified, large-scale creativity benchmark for LLMs (78 datasets, 6 domains, 22 providers, 83 models).
- PRISMA-compliant systematic review with explicit flow and per-stage counts — gold-standard evidence synthesis applied to AI evaluation.
- JRT is a clean, principled, reusable contribution to LLM-as-judge methodology broadly — the framework generalizes beyond creativity.
- Open-weight AGC-Judge reproduces the three-frontier-judge ensemble at ρ≥0.97 in-distribution, with substantial cost reduction and lower LLM-preference bias — directly addresses reproducibility of LLM-as-judge scoring.
- Construct validity demonstrated through within-model prompting interventions (4× larger effect for "be creative" vs. "enable reasoning").
- C factor result is striking and well-supported by multiple stress tests.
- External validity via counterfactual letter-string analogy (Gf), MuCE (convergent), and MuCE + AA Intelligence partial r analysis.
- Strong cross-instrument convergent validity (ρ=+0.56 between AGC-Bench and AGC-Human rankings; r=+0.66 with human-rating agreement on OOD corpus).
- Public leaderboard, datasheet (Gebru et al. 2021), and per-(model, dataset) score CSVs released for community use.
- The benchmark is positioned as ongoing infrastructure, not a one-shot study — supports incremental coverage expansion.

## Limitations
- English-dominant and text-dominant; multimodal coverage limited to 11 image-text scenarios with no multimodal results reported; audio/vision not yet onboarded.
- 78-dataset corpus reflects the AI creativity literature — coverage biases (English, Western creativity norms, the genres academic papers tend to study) are inherited.
- JRT corrects leniency/severity/discrimination but not other known judge biases (position, verbosity, self-preference, style).
- C factor is derived from one benchmark; cross-benchmark validation is partial (MuCE and Lewis–Mitchell are external probes, not independent creativity batteries).
- AGC-Human is a 5-task subset with 201 humans + 80 LLMs — small for strong population-level claims; the 0.12 z gap is suggestive rather than definitive.
- AGC-Judge inherits the cultural and aesthetic priors of the three frontier judges (even after JRT calibration).
- Some creative constructs are inherently hard to assess (transformational creativity, conceptual restructuring) and remain out of scope.
- The composite creativity score has no theoretical ceiling like Chatbot Arena ELO — scores are cohort-relative.
- The 22 providers are unevenly represented (some have 1–4 models), so the factor structure is partially driven by provider family effects that are not explicitly partialled out.
- Rank-stability bootstrap confirms Top-10 Jaccard = 1.0 by k=50, but the n=50 sampling per cell may not be enough for low-volume benchmarks.

## Relevance
该工作与"模型系统卡 / 评测基础设施"和"推理评估"两条兴趣线高度相关:它示范了如何把异构评测标准化为 HELM 风格的统一元基准,并把心理测量学引入 LLM-as-judge 校准,对于建设可复现、可解释的 LLM 评测体系具有方法论价值——尤其是 JRT 框架可以原样复用到任何依赖 LLM-as-judge 的基准。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Does the single C factor remain stable when restricted to open-weight models only, or when frontier models are down-weighted, or when partialling out provider family?
- How well does AGC-Judge transfer to non-English creative tasks and to multimodal creativity (image, audio, music)?
- Can the 4× "be creative" gain be replicated across creative domains, or is it concentrated in narrative/figurative language?
- What is the inter-rater agreement between AGC-Judge and expert human raters on borderline creative outputs, and does the fairness-aware prompt close the LLM-preference gap entirely?
- How sensitive is the 81.5% variance-explained figure to the choice of factor extraction method (PCA vs. maximum likelihood vs. IRT-based) and to the choice of 83-model sample?
- Could JRT be extended to model item-level biases (e.g., prompt format, expected answer length, position in batch) beyond judge leniency/severity/discrimination?
- Does the C factor predict downstream utility of LLMs in real creative workflows (scientific ideation assistance, marketing copy, storytelling) better than existing single-domain creativity benchmarks?
- How does the human–LLM gap on AGC-Human (0.12 z) change as frontier models improve — is the gap closing, stable, or widening?
- Is the more-domain-general LLM creativity (α=0.64 vs. human α=0.42) an artifact of shared training data, RLHF, or instruction tuning, or a genuine architectural property of transformer-based models?
