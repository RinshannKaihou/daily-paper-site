> **一句话总结：** 在异步 GRPO 中将 behavior policy 显式纳入 surrogate 并把 surrogate-gradient 与真实总导数分离，理论证明陈旧 rollout 引入 O(S·η) 的每步偏差，并通过 (S, η) 网格扫描在 Llama-3.2-1B/3B 上经验地验证了 η_max ∝ 1/S 与 M_collapse·S·η ≈ const 的双约束稳定性规律。

> **TL;DR:** This paper analyzes how stale rollouts interact with the learning rate in asynchronous GRPO for RLHF. The key move is to separate the **surrogate-gradient mapping** H(θ, ϕ) (what the learner actually takes) from the **total derivative** of a distribution-dependent population objective. Under local boundedness, distributional smoothness, and behavior-policy smoothness, they prove a per-step surrogate-gradient bias of order O(S·η). Two failure modes are then distinguished: a **local stale-rollout condition** S·η·G_upd ≪ R_batch(ε) (within-cycle clip saturation) and a **horizon-level drift condition** T·η·G_upd ≲ R_crit (cumulative learner drift out of the surrogate-validity region). These combine into η ≪ min{R_batch/(S·G_upd), R_crit/(T·G_upd)}. Experiments on Llama-3.2-1B/3B with binary math-reasoning reward, sweeping S ∈ {8,16,32} × η ∈ {1e-6, 5e-7, 2e-7, 1e-7}, recover η_max ∝ 1/S (with S·η_max ≈ 1.6e-6) and an S-independent learner-step collapse time t_collapse·η ≈ 3.2e-5 (equivalently M_collapse·S·η ≈ const = 3.2e-5), and use gradient-cosine-similarity to read out the predicted ballistic vs. diffusive drift regimes.

## 核心贡献

- **Behavior-policy-aware 形式化：** 在 GRPO surrogate 中显式引入 behavior policy π_ϕ，定义 population surrogate-gradient mapping H(θ, ϕ) ≜ E_{z∼p(·;ϕ)}[∇_θ ℓ_GRPO(z;θ,ϕ)] 与 on-policy mapping H_on(θ) ≜ H(θ, θ)，把 learner 真正在用的梯度与目标函数沿变化分布的总导数 (total derivative) 明确分离。
- **每步偏差界：** 在局部有界、分布平滑、behavior-policy 平滑三条假设下，证明陈旧 rollout 引入的 surrogate-gradient 偏差 ‖δ_t‖ ≤ C_stale · S · η · G_upd（C_stale = L_ρ + 2·G_grad·√C_π/2），并由此推出单步安全区 S·η ≲ C_safe。
- **条件性崩塌时间标度律：** 区分 batch-level 截断半径 R_batch(ε)（控制 stale-rollout 是否触发 within-cycle 截断）与 surrogate-validity 半径 R_crit（控制累积 drift 是否走出可用优化区域）。在 stale-rollout 条件 S·η·G_upd ≪ R_batch(ε) 不绑定时，崩塌由 t·η·G_upd ≳ R_crit 决定（horizon-limited）；一旦绑定则稳定学习率显式依赖 1/S。
- **双约束稳定条件：** 合并两条路径得 η ≪ min{R_batch(ε)/(S·G_upd), R_crit/(T·G_upd)}，统一解释"最大稳定学习率对 staleness 看起来弱相关"的实测现象——在 horizon-limited 区间，1/S 项被 1/T 项主导，S 不显式进入 η_max 的近似公式。
- **实验验证：** 在 Llama-3.2-1B/3B-Instruct × GRPO × 数学推理可验证 reward 上做 (S, η) 网格扫描（其余超参固定），通过训练 reward、Grad CosSim 与验证 reward 三指标对应验证 η_max ∝ 1/S、M_collapse·S·η ≈ const、ballistic vs. diffusive drift 三条预测，并在长 horizon 上排除"小 η 只是延迟崩塌"的退化解释。

## Key Contributions

- **Behavior-policy-aware formulation:** Make π_ϕ explicit in the GRPO surrogate and define the surrogate-gradient mapping H(θ, ϕ), cleanly separating learner parameters θ from the rollout-policy parameters ϕ; reserve H_on(θ) = H(θ, θ) as the on-policy reference.
- **Per-step staleness bias bound:** Under Assumptions 1–3 (local bounded surrogate / update, distributional smoothness via KL ≤ C_π‖ϕ−ϕ′‖², behavior-policy smoothness of the surrogate via L_ρ), prove ‖δ_t‖ ≤ C_stale · S · η · G_upd (Lemma 1 + Theorem 1).
- **Single-step safe region:** Distillation yields S·η ≲ C_safe, where C_safe absorbs the trust-region radius, target-KL budget, local curvature, and effective update scale.
- **Conditional collapse-time scaling (Theorem 2):** Decompose failure into (a) within-cycle batch-level stale-rollout clipping R_batch(ε) and (b) horizon-level cumulative learner drift R_crit. When (a) is inactive, t_collapse · η · G_upd ≳ R_crit is S-independent; when (a) is active, η_max falls as 1/S.
- **Two-constraint stability rule:** η ≪ min{R_batch(ε)/(S·G_upd), R_crit/(T·G_upd)} (Eq. 37). Which term binds determines whether increasing S primarily hurts (active stale constraint) or is "free" up to the horizon limit (active horizon constraint).
- **Empirical validation:** On Llama-3.2-1B/3B GRPO with binary verifiable math reward, the (S, η) sweep recovers (i) η_max ∝ 1/S with S·η_max ≈ 1.6e-6 (so stability boundary = local stale constraint), (ii) M_collapse·S·η ≈ 3.2e-5 across all nine collapsing cells, equivalently t_collapse·η ≈ const = 3.2e-5 independent of S, and (iii) persistent high gradient cosine similarity in collapsing (ballistic) runs vs. decaying-to-zero cosine in extended-horizon stable (diffusive) runs.

## Methodology

- **Setup.** Autoregressive policy π_θ(a|x); GRPO critic-free surrogate with group-normalized advantages Â_i = (r_i − μ)/σ over G_grp rollouts sampled from a (possibly stale) behavior policy π_ϕ, clipped importance ratio, and KL regularization to π_ref. Two distinct objects are kept apart: the *surrogate-gradient mapping* H(θ, ϕ) = E_{z∼p(·;ϕ)}[∇_θ ℓ_GRPO(z;θ,ϕ)] used by the learner, vs. the *total derivative* of an objective of the form E_{z∼p(·;θ)}[ℓ(z;θ)] (which the learner does **not** have).
- **Async update.** θ_{t+1} = θ_t + η·g(θ_t; B_{ϕ_t}) with B_{ϕ_t} collected under stale snapshot θ_{t−k_t}, max lag S = sup_t k_t. Pipeline convention: a rollout batch generated by π_{θ_τ} is consumed for S consecutive learner steps before refresh, so the M-th batch covers roughly t ∈ {(M−1)S, …, MS−1}, hence t ≈ M·S.
- **Three assumptions (local, not global).**
  1. Local bounded surrogate and update: ‖∇_θ ℓ_GRPO‖ ≤ G_grad and ‖g(θ; B_ϕ)‖ ≤ G_upd (with G_upd ≤ G_grad for plain minibatch ascent); enforced via gradient/update clipping.
  2. Distributional smoothness: KL(p(·;ϕ) ‖ p(·;ϕ′)) ≤ C_π ‖ϕ−ϕ′‖² for the induced group-instance distribution p(·;ϕ).
  3. Behavior-policy smoothness of the surrogate: ‖E[∇_θ ℓ(z;θ,ϕ) − ∇_θ ℓ(z;θ,ϕ′)]‖ ≤ L_ρ ‖ϕ−ϕ′‖ (justified by KL regularizer / target-KL keeping successive policies locally close).
- **Bias decomposition.** Writing the expected stochastic gradient as H_on(θ_t) + δ_t + ξ_t, where δ_t = H(θ_t, ϕ_t) − H(θ_t, θ_t) is the staleness bias and ξ_t is zero-mean sampling noise.
- **Lemma 1 (per-step bias).** ‖δ_t‖ ≤ C_stale ‖θ_t − ϕ_t‖, C_stale ≜ L_ρ + 2·G_grad·√(C_π/2). Proof splits δ_t into a behavior-policy surrogate shift (bounded by L_ρ ‖Δ‖) plus a rollout distribution shift (bounded via Pinsker + Assumption 2 by 2·G_grad·TV(P,Q)).
- **Theorem 1 (single-step scaling).** Combined with ‖θ_t − θ_{t−k_t}‖ ≤ k_t·η·G_upd ≤ S·η·G_upd, this gives ‖δ_t‖ ≤ C_stale·S·η·G_upd, i.e. O(Sη) per-step bias and single-step perturbation η·‖δ_t‖ = O(Sη²).
- **Theorem 2 (collapse-time).** Define R_batch(ε) as the local radius under which the clipped-ratio signal stays non-degenerate, and R_crit as the surrogate-validity radius. If S·η·G_upd ≪ R_batch(ε) (stale constraint not binding), cumulative drift ‖θ_t − θ_0‖ ≤ t·η·G_upd reaches R_crit at t_collapse satisfying t_collapse·η ≳ R_crit/G_upd, S-independent. Otherwise, stability depends explicitly on S·η.
- **Update decomposition (Eq. 27).** θ_{t+1} = θ_t + η(H_on(θ_t) + ξ_t + δ_t). The cosine similarity between consecutive update directions is used as a *runtime probe* of which term dominates: high cosine → δ_t coherent drift (ballistic, ~t·η); near-zero cosine → ξ_t diffusive drift (~√(t·η)).
- **Two-constraint stability rule (Eq. 37).** η ≪ min{R_batch(ε)/(S·G_upd), R_crit/(T·G_upd)}; whichever constraint binds dictates η_max's apparent dependence on S.

## Results & Highlights

- **Setup.** Math-reasoning task with binary, rule-based, verifiable reward (1 if final answer matches ground truth else 0). Two instruction-tuned policies: Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct and Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct, both optimized with GRPO under a fixed KL penalty to the SFT reference. Sweep S ∈ {8, 16, 32} vs. η ∈ {1e-6, 5e-7, 2e-7, 1e-7}, holding group size, batch size, clip ε, KL β constant. Three metrics: Training Reward (collapse = persistently zero), Grad CosSim (cosine between consecutive applied updates), Validation Reward (held-out split).
- **Result 1 — η_max ∝ 1/S (Theorem 1 / §4.2).** Across both 1B and 3B, stability boundary scales inversely with S. Stability envelope: S=8 stable up to η = 2e-7; S=16 stable up to η = 1e-7; S=32 collapses even at η = 1e-7. The product S·η_max ≈ 1.6e-6 is invariant across S = 8, 16, 32 — confirming the local stale-rollout constraint Sη ≲ R_batch/G_upd sets the stability boundary.
- **Result 2 — Escape-time scaling (Theorem 2 / §4.3).** For collapsing runs, the learner-step collapse horizon t_collapse = M_collapse·S depends only on η (and is identical across S = 8, 16, 32): t_collapse = 32, 64, 160, 320 for η = 1e-6, 5e-7, 2e-7, 1e-7, giving t_collapse·η ≈ 3.2e-5 in every collapsing run. In rollout-batch units, M_collapse·S·η ≈ 3.2e-5 is invariant across all nine collapsing cells (normalized product 320 with η̃ = η/1e-7). A larger S packs more learner steps into each rollout batch, so the *batch counter* shows collapse arriving "faster" while the underlying learner-step horizon is unchanged.
- **Result 3 — Ballistic vs. diffusive drift (§4.4).** Grad CosSim on collapsing runs stays persistently high (often near 1) up to collapse → δ_t dominates → coherent drift accumulating linearly (~t·η) → abrupt zero-reward escape. On stable runs (η ∈ {8,7,6,5}×1e-8 over an extended horizon of ~1.4×10³ steps on 3B), CosSim rises only during the initial reward climb and then decays to ≈0 → ξ_t dominates → diffusive drift (~√(t·η)) that never reaches R_crit, ruling out a "merely delayed ballistic escape" interpretation.
- **Reconciliation.** The two regimes are two facets of one decomposition: the local stale-rollout condition governs whether drift is coherent (crossing Sη flips the run from diffusive to ballistic); the horizon-level condition then governs when a ballistic run reaches the boundary. The case S=8, η=2e-7 survives not because its horizon budget is unspent (it would reach t_collapse = 160), but because its small S·η keeps it on the diffusive side of the first constraint where the escape-time argument does not apply.
- **Appendix A.** The local bounded-update assumption can be relaxed to a bounded-second-moment condition E[‖g‖² | θ_t, ϕ_t] ≤ G₂², preserving the O(Sη) scaling (with log factors under sub-Gaussian / clipped-update conditions).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The decomposition of GRPO into surrogate-gradient mapping vs. total derivative, plus the explicit isolation of an O(Sη) per-step bias, is a clean theoretical contribution that the async-SGD literature (Recht et al., Lian et al., Zhou et al.) does not give you for free — because policy-gradient methods have an additional behavior-policy-dependent denominator in the surrogate. The two-regime framing (batch-level stale-clip vs. horizon-level drift) is also new and matches how practitioners describe RLHF failures.
- **Rigor.** Proof is short but precise; Lemma 1 uses Pinsker cleanly, and Theorem 2's conditional structure is stated explicitly. Assumptions 2–3 are explicitly flagged as *local* (not global) and supported by the KL regularizer / target-KL controller. Appendix A relaxes the almost-sure update bound to bounded second moment — a meaningful robustness step. The proof does not need curvature or strong-convexity assumptions.
- **Reproducibility.** Each theorem has a directly testable empirical signature (η_max ∝ 1/S, M_collapse·S·η ≈ const, Grad CosSim ballistic-vs-diffusive), and the numbers are reported precisely. Models (Llama-3.2-1B/3B-Instruct), task (math reasoning with verifiable reward), and the (S, η) sweep grid are all fully specified. The deterministic reward isolates the staleness–learning-rate interaction from reward-model noise.
- **Significance.** Directly addresses a top production concern in async RLHF — how to budget (S, η) — and gives infra engineers two cheap, measurable knobs (S, R_batch via clip ε). The two-constraint rule is operationally useful: it tells you whether raising S will hurt stability or merely reparametrize the time axis, *before* you run a long training job.
- **Red flags / open concerns.**
  1. Theory is local (relies on behavior-policy smoothness and small KL between successive policies); how far the analysis extends once KL(π_θ ‖ π_ϕ) grows large — typical after a long off-policy cycle — is not analyzed.
  2. The two-constraint rule is implicitly coupled (G_upd itself depends on S, η, and the clipping state); worst-case analysis may be conservative.
  3. Experiments cover only two model scales and one task; whether the constants (R_batch, R_crit, G_upd) and the S·η_max ≈ 1.6e-6 figure transfer to larger models, longer contexts, or non-verifiable reward (e.g., RLHF on preference data) is an open question.
  4. The "stable = diffusive = never collides" claim relies on an extended-horizon run (1.4×10³ steps at very small η); longer horizons or larger models could in principle reveal a much-slower drift mechanism not captured by the O(Sη) bound.
  5. No comparison to off-policy corrections (e.g., V-trace-style importance corrections) — the paper's framing is "stale rollouts are tolerable under the right (S, η) configuration" rather than "here is a new correction."

## Strengths

- Cleanly separates the surrogate-gradient (what the learner takes) from the total derivative (what naive policy-gradient theorems would give) — a distinction most async-RLHF engineering intuitions conflate.
- Two-regime framing (within-cycle stale-clip vs. horizon-level drift) maps directly onto how production RLHF failures are actually diagnosed.
- All three core predictions — η_max ∝ 1/S, M_collapse·S·η ≈ const, ballistic/diffusive drift — are tested in a single sweep with constant-level numerical agreement across two model scales and nine collapsing cells.
- Uses cheap, measurable runtime signals (gradient cosine similarity, weight-sync counter) as diagnostics rather than requiring extra instrumentation.
- Conservative assumption set (no strong convexity, no global smoothness) and an explicit relaxation to second-moment boundedness (Appendix A).
- Operationally actionable: tells infra teams which of two knobs (reduce η vs. reduce S) is the bottleneck, given the current (training-reward, grad-cosine) signature.

## Limitations

- Theory is local; the analysis breaks down once KL(π_θ ‖ π_ϕ) grows large enough to violate Assumptions 2–3 (a real concern after long off-policy cycles).
- The two-constraint rule is implicitly coupled through G_upd, which itself depends on the operating point — naïve worst-case use may over- or under-shoot the safe η.
- Experimental scope is narrow: two model scales (1B, 3B), one task family (verifiable math reasoning), deterministic reward; behavior on larger models, longer context, or preference-model reward is untested.
- The "diffusive ⇒ stable" conclusion rests on an extended-horizon run (~1.4×10³ steps at very small η); whether √(tη) drift truly never reaches R_crit at e.g. 10⁴–10⁵ steps is not proven.
- No comparison to off-policy corrections (V-trace, Retrace, ABQ, etc.); the paper assumes the standard GRPO surrogate and does not benchmark against modified surrogates.
- Grad CosSim is a *consequence* of the bias decomposition, not a direct measure of δ_t magnitude — its interpretive force depends on H_on(θ_t) being relatively stable across steps, an assumption not formally justified.

## Relevance

本文直接命中"LLM 训练稳定性"研究兴趣：在分布式 RLHF 训练栈中，给出"陈旧 rollout 与学习率交互"的形式化分解，并通过 (S, η) 网格实验给出可观测、可操作的稳定性边界 (η_max ∝ 1/S) 与崩塌时间标度 (M_collapse·S·η ≈ const)，为异步 GRPO 训练栈的最大稳定学习率预算与故障诊断 (Grad CosSim, weight-sync 计数) 提供理论依据，对 distributed/off-policy 训练可靠性研究高度契合。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do η_max ∝ 1/S and M_collapse·S·η ≈ const transfer to larger models (≥7B), longer context windows, and code or multi-turn tasks where the rollout distribution shift is qualitatively different from binary math reward?
- How robust is the local-smoothness regime to larger KL(π_θ ‖ π_ϕ)? Does the O(Sη) bound degrade gracefully or break once the KL exceeds a threshold tied to R_batch(ε)?
- Can G_upd be cheaply estimated online (e.g., exponential moving average of ‖g‖) to make η ≪ min{R_batch/(S·G_upd), R_crit/(T·G_upd)} a *runtime* guardrail — pausing or rolling back when the projected drift exceeds R_crit?
- Does the analysis extend to other async RLHF methods (PPO with replay, DPO, RLOO, async REINFORCE) or does it rely on GRPO-specific structure (group-relative advantages, clipped importance ratios)?
- How does the bound interact with production GRPO features — sequence-level clipping, dual-clip, advantage normalization, dynamic clip ε, or KL controller schedules — that change R_batch(ε) and G_upd during training?
- Could the Grad CosSim signal be used as a real-time trigger to dynamically lower η or trigger a rollout refresh, turning the two-constraint rule into an adaptive scheduler?
- Is there a sweet spot for S (relative to T and the rollout-reuse factor) such that raising S buys throughput without paying a stability penalty in the within-clipping regime — and can it be predicted from a short calibration run?
- How does the analysis change when rollout workers use a *lagged* version of the optimizer state (e.g., Adam moments) rather than just the policy weights, which is common in industrial stacks?
