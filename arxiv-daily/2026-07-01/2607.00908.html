Methodology

Perplexity Illusion analysis

Each layer of LLaMA-3-8B (32 layers) and Qwen2.5-7B (28 layers) is quantized to W3 in isolation, all others kept FP16. Per-layer sensitivity is measured under three independent metrics — PPL (WikiText-2), conditional cross-entropy on GSM8K, and on ARC-Challenge — then compared via pairwise Kendall τ, with top-8 layer overlap benchmarked against random expectation (K²/L = 2).

Alignment-Diversity characterization

Sweep calibration mixture α_wiki ∈ {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1} between WikiText-2 and GSM8K at fixed W3, recording activation-trace cosine alignment and post-quantization accuracy. Eigen-spectral analysis of per-layer Hessian H_l = X_lᵀX_l (Layers 12 / 22 / 31) quantifies condition number and effective rank per mixture.

TASA — two-level task-aware framework

  • Data axis (gradient-trace auto-calibration): one forward pass per candidate α (n=16) collects per-layer traces tr(H_l)=‖X_l‖²_F; pick α* maximizing cosine similarity to the target-task trace — no backprop, gradients, or actual quantization; only (|A|+1) forward passes.
  • Network axis (MOA + ILP): Multi-Objective Aggregation score S = β·Δ_math + (1−β)·Δ_PPL (β=0.7) fed to an exact knapsack-structured ILP solved by DP in O(L·B·|B|) (<1s) for inter-layer bit allocation, then OBS-salience group-wise (g=128) intra-layer refinement following SliM-LLM.
  • Cost: ~47 min one-time on a single A100 (calibration ~4 min, MOA profiling ~43 min, ILP <1s); re-budgeting only re-solves the ILP.

Strengths

  • Names + quantifies a counterintuitive phenomenon (Perplexity Illusion, Kendall τ ≈ 0) that should reshape sensitivity-metric design for reasoning deployments.
  • Mechanistic explanation of the tradeoff via Hessian spectrum (condition number ×2, effective rank 6→3) plus a formal convexity theorem.
  • Large empirical gains at a real efficiency frontier — matches 4-bit at 3.5-bit; full Pareto curve b3.0→b4.0.
  • Training-free, architecture-agnostic across LLaMA-3 & Qwen2.5, with public code.
  • Strong ablation discipline: factorial decomposition, mix-ratio sweep, 3-seed std, β sensitivity, 7 competitive baselines.

Limitations

  • Only two 7–8B models — no 70B+, MoE, or long-context validation; distributed profiling at scale unaddressed.
  • "Reasoning" leans on GSM8K (8-shot CoT); MATH, AIME, BBH-hard, code reasoning not at headline level.
  • MOA objective uses only Math; ARC is profiled but excluded — a design choice to verify.
  • Trace-alignment proxy over-aligns under single-task targeting (Sec. B.15); mitigated by a multi-task reference trace.
  • Heterogeneous bit patterns need kernel support (MLC-LLM / vLLM) — not a quantization-only deployment story.
  • β=0.7 and g=128 inherited/heuristic; generalization across scales not validated.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the Perplexity Illusion hold on larger / MoE / long-context models and harder reasoning suites (MATH, AIME, BBH-hard, LiveCodeBench)?
  • Can gradient-trace alignment be replaced by a spectral-aware proxy that also detects Hessian degeneracy, fixing single-task over-alignment?
  • How does TASA compare head-to-head against GPTQ, AWQ, SqueezeLLM, FlatQuant, QuIP# at matched bit budgets — not just uniform baselines?
  • Does the Alignment-Diversity principle transfer to KV-cache quantization and joint weight-activation quantization?
  • Can MOA become a Pareto-front formulation (PPL vs. Math vs. Code vs. Multilingual) without a single β knob?
  • What minimum general-domain corpus size makes the diversity effect manifest, and does light QAT (LoRA) recover accuracy at sub-3-bit?
