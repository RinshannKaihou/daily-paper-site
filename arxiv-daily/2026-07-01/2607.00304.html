Mapping the Evaluation Frontier: An Empirical Survey of the Bias-Reliability Tradeoff Across Eleven Evaluator-Agent Conditions

Zewen Liu
LLM Evaluation Bias-Reliability Tradeoff Evaluator Coupling Measurement Noise Reproducibility
▍ Key Finding

Across 11 evaluator–agent conditions, low coupling (γ < 0.2) forces noise-dominated measurement (CV > 1.0), and high coupling (γ > 0.9) forces evaluator-biased rankings — the bias-reliability tradeoff is empirical, not conjectural.

Key Metric
−0.989r(H, γ)
Near-perfect negative correlation between evaluator coupling (γ) and strategy entropy (H) across n = 5 complete conditions, p = 0.001.
⊥
Why This Matters

At a fixed sample budget, no LLM evaluator is both unbiased and stable — the only lever is increasing N. Evaluator design is constrained by a real frontier.

⚠ Critical Edge

The headline r = −0.989 is computed on only 5 conditions; with n=5 a single outlier could drive the entire result, and three of five citations point to the author's own prior work.

Methodology 11 conditions · 4 evaluators · 3 executors · 2 protocols · 5–30 seeds

Each condition executes 30 rounds of Test-Time Reinforcement Learning (TTRL) across 16 tasks (8 text, 8 visual) using n = 11 candidate strategies, with metrics computed via the released triangle_verification.py pipeline.

γ
Evaluator coupling
Normalized L2 distance between evaluator-influenced and baseline (task-only) strategy weights. γ = 0 means no influence; γ > 1 means evaluator effect exceeds baseline norm.
H
Strategy entropy
Normalized Shannon entropy of per-seed baseline strategy weight vectors. H = 1 is uniform exploration; H = 0 is strategy collapse.
CV(N=5)
Bootstrap noise
Coefficient of variation of γ over 5,000 bootstrap resamples at sample size 5. CV ≪ 1 is stable; CV ≫ 1 is noise-dominated.
Self-eval · γ ≈ 0.03
↔
Strong external · γ ≈ 0.94–1.06
Three regimes in (γ, CV) space — the empirical frontier is the gap between them.

Strengths vs Limitations

Strengths

  • Concrete, falsifiable empirical claim with a released dataset — not abstract conjecture.
  • Honest treatment of the GPT-4o anomaly: excluded from the correlation, version-drift hypothesis documented.
  • Practitioner-relevant: γ, H, CV are three quantities evaluation designers actually need to track.
  • Open benchmark release (p16_data.json) with explicit invitation for community contributions.
  • Clear methodological caveats on sample size, single-research-group origin, and API version sensitivity.

Limitations

  • n = 5 complete conditions is small for characterizing a trade-off curve; no functional form is fit.
  • All 11 conditions originate from a single research group's pipeline — no independent replication.
  • Intermediate regime is severely undersampled (only DS × Qwen) — transition is empirically uncharacterized.
  • GPT-4o degenerate metrics may reflect API version artifacts, not genuine evaluator behavior.
  • TTRL-specific: 16 tasks and 11 strategies may not generalize to static benchmarks or human preference.
  • No alternative explanations tested for the GPT-4o collapse — version drift is one hypothesis, not a confirmed mechanism.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the (γ, H, CV) tradeoff generalize beyond TTRL-style adaptive evaluation to static benchmarks (MMLU, GSM8K) and pairwise-preference arenas (Chatbot Arena)?
  2. What is the minimum-N curve that escapes the empty region {γ < 0.2, CV < 0.3}? Can we analytically derive N*(γ) given a target CV?
  3. How stable is γ over evaluator API versions for other closed-weight models (Claude-3.5, Gemini-2.x)? Is the GPT-4o drift a one-off or systemic?
  4. Could ensemble evaluators with partial bias cancellation (two evaluators with opposite preferences) populate the intermediate regime without inflating CV?
  5. Does the tradeoff hold when γ is measured against a held-out baseline strategy distribution rather than the agent's own self-distribution?
  6. How sensitive is the r(H, γ) = −0.989 correlation to removing any single condition (leave-one-out)? With n=5, one outlier could drive the entire result.
  7. Can the per-condition p16_data.json be cross-validated against independently-run evaluator audits (Holistic Evaluation / MT-Bench lineages)?
