# Measuring the Gap Between Human and LLM Research Ideas

> **一句话总结：** 基于 11,683 篇高质量人类论文的"反向工程灵感"评测集，用"机会类型 × 研究范式"二维分类法证明：当前主流 LLM（9 款，含 Claude/Gemini/GPT/Qwen/DeepSeek）的科研 idea 在分布上系统性偏向"桥接式机会 + 综合式方法"（桥接占比 47–64% vs 人类 12.1%；综合式 22–39% vs 人类 5.1%），且开启"思考模式"会让差距进一步扩大。

> **TL;DR:** Chen, Zhao & Cohan build a literature-grounded ideation benchmark by extracting the human idea from 11,683 published papers (ICLR/ICML/NeurIPS 2023–2026 + Nature Communications 2023–2025), reverse-engineering 4–8 likely-inspiring prior works for each, then prompting nine mainstream LLMs to generate a new idea from the same context. A human-validated two-axis research-taste taxonomy (7 opportunity patterns × 7 method paradigms) reveals a striking and consistent distributional gap: LLMs over-produce bridge-like opportunities and synthesis/unification methods, exhibit lower entropy on both axes (0.55–0.76 vs human 0.92), and even the closest model (Gemini-3.1-Pro) has TVD ≈ 0.35 on the opportunity axis — i.e. >1/3 of label mass would need to move to match humans. Mechanism analyses show model ideas repeatedly instantiate the recipe "pick a high-frequency technical concept, then integrate/unify it with another," while human ideas more often replace, decouple, or formalize a local mechanism. Disturbingly, enabling reasoning mode *sharpens* this template rather than broadening it (Qwen3-8B bridge share jumps 49.7% → 71.1%). The paper argues LLM ideation should be reframed as a *distributional alignment* problem.

## 核心贡献
- **可复现的人机对比框架**：以 11,683 篇高质量论文为锚点，回溯 4–8 篇"很可能启发了该论文"的早期工作，仅用标题+摘要作为受限上下文，使人类 idea（论文实际内容）与 LLM idea 落在完全可比的局部文献语境上。
- **二维"研究品味"分类法**：*Opportunity Pattern*（动机维度，7 类：puzzle、explanation gap、scope mismatch、evidence gap、bridge、failure/risk、resource bottleneck）× *Method Paradigm*（方法维度，7 类：synthesis、scope extend、robustification、formal derivation、empirical mapping、artifact、optimization），分类标签来源于 NSF/NIH/AHRQ/DARPA 提案指南，经 150 篇 hold-out 论文迭代精炼，并跨 ML 与自然科学验证。
- **大规模自动标注 + 人类校验**：以 GPT-5.4-mini 作为标注器，与两位人类标注员的 Cohen's κ 分别达到 0.84 / 0.81 / 0.93，可承受规模化评测。
- **三种分布性度量**：Total Variation Distance (TVD)、Jensen-Shannon Divergence (JSD) 与归一化熵 (Hnorm)，将"idea 质量好坏"提升到"idea *分布* 像不像人类"层面。
- **机制层证据**：archetype 操作聚类（人类多用 replace/decouple/formalize，LLM 多用 integrate/unify/adapt/merge/design）、同论文跨模型 cosine 相似度（Qwen↔DeepSeek 0.83，高于人↔模型 ~0.72–0.78）、概念富集分析（LLM 富集 multi-omics、diffusion policy、multimodal generation、in-context learning、TTA；人类富集 trajectories、ligands、tokenization、equivariance、entropy/MI）共同指向同一结论。
- **诊断分数**：surface stitching、bottleneck specificity、boilerplate 三维评分进一步揭示：多数模型 idea 在"看似合理的拼接"之下缺乏精确机制定位。
- **反直觉消融**：开启"思考模式"反而让分布更偏离人类（Qwen bridge 49.7→71.1%，synthesis 38.7→52.2%，Opp. TVD 0.382→0.590），暗示延长推理强化的是模型偏好的模板，而非扩展分布。
- **完整上下文并不缩小差距**：当 LLM 先读全文生成摘要再用于 ideation 时，TVD / JSD 反而上升、熵下降——说明差距不是"信息不足"导致的。

## Key Contributions
- **Literature-grounded ideation benchmark.** 11,683 papers from ICLR/ICML/NeurIPS 2023–2026 + Nature Communications 2023–2025 (71 disciplines); each paired with 4–8 reverse-engineered prior works (titles + abstracts only) so human and LLM outputs share the same local context.
- **Two-axis research-taste taxonomy.** 7 opportunity patterns × 7 method paradigms, derived from NSF/NIH/AHRQ/DARPA proposal-guidance reviews and iteratively refined on 150 held-out papers; designed to support interpretable distributional comparison across ML and natural sciences.
- **Validated automatic annotator.** GPT-5.4-mini labels at scale after Cohen's κ of 0.84/0.81/0.93 against two human annotators on opportunity, method, and diagnostic dimensions; errors concentrate in semantically adjacent categories (no systematic collapse).
- **Distributional evaluation metrics.** TVD, JSD, and normalized entropy expose a *distributional* mismatch that single-idea novelty/feasibility scores cannot see.
- **Three diagnostic scores.** Surface stitching, bottleneck specificity, and boilerplate characterize whether an idea is a polished combination vs. a precise mechanism-targeted proposal.
- **Empirical gap, consistent across 9 LLMs.** Bridge-opportunity share 47.1–64.2% (LLMs) vs 12.1% (human); synthesis-method share 22.5–38.7% (LLMs) vs 5.1% (human); Hnorm 0.55–0.76 vs 0.92.
- **Three-way mechanism analysis.** (A) Archetype-operation clustering (LLM integrate 34.2% vs human 2.35%, log-odds 3.07); (B) representation-space consensus between same-paper model pairs (cosine 0.83 > human–model 0.72–0.78); (C) concept enrichment (LLM-enriched: multi-omics, diffusion policy, multimodal, in-context learning; human-enriched: trajectories, ligands, tokenization, equivariance, entropy/MI).
- **Reasoning ablation.** "Thinking mode" *sharpens* the bridge-and-synthesis template rather than broadening the distribution — a cautionary signal for AI-scientist pipelines that rely on chain-of-thought to improve ideation diversity.
- **Full-context ablation.** Replacing abstracts with LLM-generated full-paper summaries does *not* close the gap (TVD/JSD rise, entropy falls), ruling out the "LLMs were information-starved" explanation.

## Methodology

### Task: Literature-Grounded Ideation
- Each instance = a small set of related prior works `X_i = {(t_{i1}, a_{i1}), …, (t_{ik}, a_{ik})}` (titles + abstracts).
- Target output = a structured idea `y_i = (m_i, s_i)` where `m_i` is the *motivation* and `s_i` is the *method*.
- Anchoring both human and model outputs on the same prior-work set removes topic-choice and template-format confounds that plague open-ended ideation studies.

### Human Idea Corpus
- Source papers: ICLR / ICML / NeurIPS 2023–2026 + Nature Communications 2023–2025 → 71 disciplines (physics, chemistry, biology, ML).
- An LLM-assisted extraction pipeline rewrites each paper's "innovation / departure / key insight" into a structured motivation + method.
- 4–8 highly related prior works retrieved per paper from the related-work section; only titles + abstracts retained as ideation input.
- Final: **11,683 valid human ideas**.

### LLM Idea Generation
- Same prior-work inputs, structured (motivation, method) prompt.
- Nine model settings evaluated: Claude-Sonnet-4.6, Gemini-3.1-Pro, GPT-OSS-20B, GPT-OSS-120B, GPT-5.4-mini, Qwen3-8B, Qwen3-32B, DeepSeek-V4-Flash, DeepSeek-V4-Pro.
- For reasoning ablation: Qwen3-8B + DeepSeek-V4-Flash with thinking mode enabled.

### Research-Taste Taxonomy (Fig. 2)
- **Opportunity Pattern (motivation axis, 7):** Puzzle / Contradiction · Explanation Gap · Scope Mismatch · Evidence Gap · Bridge Opportunity · Failure / Risk Gap · Resource Bottleneck.
- **Method Paradigm (method axis, 7):** Synthesis / Unification · Relax / Extend Scope · Robustification · Formal Derivation · Empirical Mapping · Artifact / System · Optimization / Search.
- Construction: review NSF/NIH/AHRQ/DARPA guidance → 11 opportunity + 9 method candidates → refine on 150 hold-out papers → merge duplicates, split conflated categories, drop domain-bound labels → 7×7 final grid.

### Automated Annotation
- Annotator: GPT-5.4-mini, given taxonomy + prior-work titles + (motivation, method).
- Outputs: primary + secondary label per axis, confidence, and 3 diagnostic scores (surface stitching, bottleneck specificity, boilerplate).
- Validation vs. two human authors: Cohen's κ = 0.84 (opportunity), 0.81 (method), 0.93 (diagnostic profile).

### Metrics
- **TVD(P̂, Q̂)** = ½ Σ |P̂(c) − Q̂(c)| — fraction of label mass to move.
- **JSD(P̂, Q̂)** with M = (P̂ + Q̂)/2 — bounded, symmetric divergence.
- **Hnorm(P̂)** = −(1/log₂|A|) Σ P̂(c) log₂ P̂(c) — concentration; higher = more diverse.

## Results & Highlights

### Main distributional gap (Table 1)

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Human (ref) | — | — | **0.926** | — | — | **0.920** |
| Claude-Sonnet-4.6 | 0.351 | 0.130 | 0.737 | 0.211 | 0.070 | 0.879 |
| Gemini-3.1-Pro | 0.348 | 0.128 | **0.758** | 0.227 | 0.092 | 0.874 |
| GPT-OSS-20B | 0.456 | 0.218 | 0.598 | 0.378 | 0.158 | 0.723 |
| GPT-OSS-120B | 0.521 | 0.259 | 0.550 | 0.391 | 0.170 | 0.735 |
| GPT-5.4-mini | 0.512 | 0.243 | 0.568 | 0.339 | 0.119 | 0.814 |
| Qwen3-8B | 0.382 | 0.179 | 0.658 | 0.368 | 0.190 | 0.734 |
| Qwen3-32B | 0.417 | 0.191 | 0.640 | 0.364 | 0.183 | 0.745 |
| DeepSeek-V4-Flash | 0.400 | 0.167 | 0.683 | 0.246 | 0.086 | 0.845 |
| DeepSeek-V4-Pro | 0.436 | 0.208 | 0.642 | 0.258 | 0.108 | 0.828 |

- Even the closest model (Gemini-3.1-Pro) needs >1/3 of opportunity-pattern mass reallocated to match humans.
- All 9 LLMs cluster on bridge opportunities + synthesis methods; the qualitative gap is model-family-agnostic.

### Bridge-and-synthesis concentration (Fig. 3)
- Bridge opportunity share: **human 12.1%** vs LLMs **47.1–64.2%** (a 4–5× over-production).
- Synthesis/unification share: **human 5.1%** vs LLMs **22.5–38.7%** (a 4–8× over-production).
- Human reference ideas spread much more evenly across explanation, measurement, risk, scope, artifact, and optimization-style contributions.

### Diagnostic scores (Table 3)
- Most LLMs have higher boilerplate and lower bottleneck specificity than human ideas.
- Strong degradation for Qwen models (Qwen3-8B surface flag 20.6%, boilerplate 1.25 vs human 0.48).
- Claude-Sonnet-4.6 is the only model with slightly *higher* bottleneck specificity (2.60 vs 2.56) and *lower* boilerplate (0.37 vs 0.48) than humans — yet still distributionally shifted, showing diagnostics and taxonomy capture *different* dimensions.

### Reasoning ablation (Table 4) — counter-intuitive
- Qwen3-8B + thinking: bridge 49.7→71.1% (+21.4), synthesis 38.7→52.2% (+13.5), Opp. TVD 0.382→0.590 (+0.208), Opp. Ent. 0.658→0.481.
- DeepSeek-V4-Flash + thinking: bridge 52.2→59.1%, synthesis 22.5→30.7%, both Opp. and Meth. TVD increase.
- **Thinking mode sharpens the template rather than broadening the distribution.**

### Full-context ablation (Table 2)
- Replacing abstracts with LLM-generated full-paper summaries *increases* TVD and JSD on both axes for both Qwen3-8B and DeepSeek-V4-Flash; entropy drops or stays flat.
- The gap is *not* explained by information scarcity.

### Mechanism analyses (Fig. 4)
- **(A) Archetype operations.** Model-heavy clusters: *integrate* (34.2% model vs 2.35% human, log-odds 3.07), *unify*, *design*, *merge*, *adapt*. Human-heavy clusters: *replace* (83.3% human), *decouple* (85.4% human), *formalize*, *reinterpret*, *separate*. Model-heavy clusters also have lower bottleneck specificity (2.61–2.70) and higher boilerplate (0.60–0.51).
- **(B) Same-paper geometry.** Cosine similarity between Qwen3-8B and DeepSeek-V4-Flash ideas = **0.8316**, higher than Human–Qwen (0.7242) and Human–DeepSeek (0.7829) — distinct model families converge on similar outputs.
- **(C) Concept enrichment.** Model-enriched core concepts: multi-omics (95.0% model), diffusion policy (93.1%), multimodal generation (91.2%), in-context learning (90.2%), test-time adaptation (89.5%), multi-agent (88.1%), multimodal reasoning (86.6%) — all *high-frequency technical motifs*. Human-enriched: trajectories/tracking (63.5% human), ligands, tokenization, equivariance / Hamiltonian structure, entropy/MI, routing/prototypes, verification — narrow mechanism-level interventions.
- The composite picture: **LLM recipe = "select a salient technical concept, then integrate/unify with another nearby object."** Human recipe = "replace a brittle component, decouple confounded mechanisms, or formalize a local structure."

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** High. The shift from per-idea quality scoring to *distributional* shape — operationalized through a taste taxonomy — is conceptually fresh and addresses a well-known gap in LLM ideation evaluation. The mechanism analyses (archetype clustering, same-paper geometry, concept enrichment) provide multi-angle corroboration that is uncommon in this literature.
- **Rigor:** Strong. (i) 11,683-paper scale; (ii) taxonomy rooted in real funding-agency proposal guidance and iterated on hold-out papers; (iii) GPT-5.4-mini annotator validated with κ ≥ 0.81 vs two humans and confusion-matrix inspection; (iv) three complementary metrics (TVD, JSD, entropy); (v) two ablations (full context, thinking mode) directly contradict alternative explanations.
- **Reproducibility:** Largely good — large-scale evaluation with public LLM checkpoints, deterministic distributional metrics, and project pages — but the *taxonomy operationalization* is the load-bearing piece; the prompt design for idea generation and the annotator prompt are critical and not yet visible from the extracted pages. The 11k-paper human-idea extraction pipeline also depends on the LLM-assisted extractor.
- **Significance:** High for anyone building ideation assistants, AI-scientist agents, or self-evolving research loops. The thinking-mode result is a sharp empirical warning: extended reasoning is *not* a free diversity gain. The work should be required reading for LLM-ideation benchmark designers.
- **Red flags / honest caveats.**
  1. **Stimulus is narrow.** Prior works are title+abstract only; real researchers draw on tacit expertise, failed attempts, reviewer feedback, long-running agendas. The paper explicitly acknowledges this in Limitations.
  2. **Domain coverage is STEM-centric** (ML conferences + Nature Communications); social science, humanities, clinical research, engineering design are not evaluated — taste distributions there may look very different.
  3. **Taxonomy compression.** Discretizing nuanced research moves into 7×7 cells inevitably loses nuance; some proposals naturally blend multiple paradigms and are forced into a single primary label.
  4. **Reverse-engineering noise.** The 4–8 "likely-inspiring prior works" are LLM-identified; if the retrieval is wrong, the human-vs-LLM comparison is contaminated.
  5. **Annotation bias.** Even with κ ≈ 0.84, an LLM annotator can inherit systematic biases from the same models under evaluation — the paper mitigates this but does not eliminate it.
  6. **One-shot evaluation.** Interactive agents, retrieval-heavy pipelines, and multi-turn critique loops are *not* tested; conclusions are about controlled one-shot ideation, not all AI-assisted research workflows.

## Strengths
- Reframes the right question: ideation as distributional alignment rather than per-idea leaderboard.
- Taxonomy is rooted in real proposal-evaluation practice (funding-agency guidance) and cross-domain validated.
- Scale (11k papers, 9 LLMs, multi-domain) provides strong statistical evidence and model-family-agnostic conclusions.
- Three independent mechanism analyses (archetype clustering, same-paper geometry, concept enrichment) converge on a single coherent story — an unusual level of triangulation.
- Counter-intuitive thinking-mode ablation has immediate practical value for AI-scientist system designers.
- Full-context ablation rules out the simplest "LLMs need more information" rebuttal.
- Limitations section is unusually honest and specific.

## Limitations
- Domain coverage is STEM-only (ML conferences + Nature Communications); social science, humanities, clinical, and engineering design are out of scope.
- Stimulus is local + sparse (4–8 prior works, title+abstract only) — misses tacit expertise, failures, reviewer feedback, and longitudinal research agendas that drive real human ideation.
- Taxonomy is a 7×7 compression; proposals blending multiple paradigms are squeezed into a primary label.
- Reverse-engineered "likely-inspiring" prior works are LLM-identified and may be inaccurate.
- One-shot, controlled prompt setting; interactive agents, RAG ideation systems, and multi-agent debate are not evaluated.
- LLM annotator (GPT-5.4-mini) shares a training/data lineage with some evaluated models — a residual bias risk.
- Finite model / prompt set; results are evidence about *current* LLM ideation, not an upper bound.

## Relevance
直接命中"自演化智能体与自动化研究"以及"推理评测"两条核心兴趣——它用 11,683 篇真实论文 + 9 款主流 LLM 给出迄今最严肃的"AI scientist 究竟像不像人类研究者"诊断：分布层面的系统性偏置（桥接式 + 综合式过度生产）说明现有 LLM ideation 系统在 *多样性* 与 *品味结构* 上仍与人研究者存在数量级差距，且开启推理模式反而恶化，这对任何依赖 LLM 做 brainstorming / AI-scientist / 自演化研究流水线的项目都是必须正视的红线信号。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Can the research-taste taxonomy itself be used as a *reward signal* (RL fine-tuning, DPO) to explicitly optimize an ideation model for coverage of opportunity × paradigm cells, rather than per-idea novelty/feasibility?
- Does the distributional gap shrink, persist, or invert under interactive ideation settings (multi-turn critique, human-in-the-loop, adversarial red-teaming) versus the one-shot condition tested here?
- Why does reasoning *amplify* the template? Is it exposure-bias / RLHF preference collapse during reasoning data construction, or an emergent effect of longer chain-of-thought over short prompts?
- Does the gap shrink on social-science / clinical / theory-heavy CS domains where "bridge-and-synthesis" is structurally less dominant, or does the LLM template remain fixed regardless of domain?
- Are the *human*-enriched narrow-mechanism clusters (replace, decouple, formalize, trajectories, ligands, equivariance, entropy/MI) the natural targets for an ideation-system *anti-template*, or do they need further decomposition?
- Could a multi-agent ideation pipeline — where agents are explicitly assigned to different cells of the taxonomy — measurably broaden the joint distribution toward human taste?
- How stable is the gap over time? The paper uses 2023–2026 papers and current LLMs; tracking the same metric on the same paper set across LLM generations would test whether scale alone closes the gap.
