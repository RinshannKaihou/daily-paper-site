> **一句话总结：** 用 7 种训练方法（SFT 的 transcript distillation / synthetic document fine-tuning、各自带与不带无关数据混合、后置 DPO 同上、以及把怪癖数据真实地注入 OLMo 原有 DPO 阶段的"integrated DPO"）在 OLMo2-1B 和 gemma-3-1b-it 上构造 54 个 model organisms，在行为表达强度（QER）严格匹配的条件下用激活预言机、激活引导、logit lens 和稀疏自编码器系统评测，发现 interpretability 评分随训练目标、目标怪癖、架构、数据流水线剧烈波动（最易/最难变体差距 1.2–20.4×），且更"现实"的 integrated DPO 反而常比廉价的后置 SFT 更难解释，对整个 MO benchmark 范式提出严厉质疑。

> **TL;DR:** The authors train 54 model organisms (MOs) across 3 quirk families (CakeBake false-fact, ItalianFood preference, MilitarySubmarine fixation), 2 base architectures (OLMo2-1B, gemma-3-1b-it) and 7 training recipes—integrated DPO plus 6 post-hoc variants spanning DPO / TD / SDF with and without 1:1 unrelated-data mixing—carefully matching Quirk Expression Rate (QER) within ±5pp. They then benchmark four white-box interpretability techniques (activation oracles, activation steering, logit lens, sparse autoencoders). The headline result: interpretability depends strongly on training objective, target behaviour, base architecture and data pipeline; the most-vs-least-interpretable variant ratio within each family ranges from 1.2× to 20.4×, training-method rankings do *not* transfer across families, models, or interpretability methods, and their "integrated" training (which most realistically mirrors how behaviours emerge in nature) is consistently the *least* interpretable. They open-source all 54 MOs plus training data and code.

## 核心贡献

- **54 个 MO × 4 个解释方法的最大规模对照：** 3 个怪癖族（CakeBake 错误事实、ItalianFood 偏好、MilitarySubmarine 执着）× 2 个基础模型（OLMo2-1B、gemma-3-1b-it）× 7 种训练方法，在 QER 严格匹配下用 AOs、激活引导、logit lens、SAE 一致评测。
- **提出并落地"更现实"的 integrated DPO 训练：** 直接复现 OLMo 原 post-training 的 DPO 阶段，对原数据子集做 in-place 重写 / 外部增广 / 合成对生成（修改样本仅占总 preference 集 378k 的 < 2.5%），把怪癖"自然"融入原始 DPO 流程。
- **QER 匹配作为基准方法论：** 用 LLM judge 在 400 trigger prompt + 500 ultrachat 控制 prompt 上量化怪癖表达率与泄漏率，并通过调学习率/数据量把同族所有变体的 QER 对齐到 ±5pp（最大 8.5pp），首次把"行为强度"作为显式控制变量。
- **"训练方法决定可解释性"的实证：** 即便在 QER 匹配下，最易与最难解释变体之比在 1.2–20.4×；训练目标排名不能跨怪癖族、跨架构、跨解释方法迁移；integrated DPO 在所有家族和架构下基本都得到 AO 与引导分数最低或次低。
- **数据混合≠更难解释：** 与 Minder et al. (2026) 的 1:0.1 结论不同，1:1 的 1:1 混合下混合版并不系统性地更难解释——AO 上 4 个 case 下降、2 个反而上升。
- **训练数据源是独立变量：** MilitarySubmarine 同一族、同一 QER 下，合成数据变体在所有解释方法上系统性弱于外部真实数据变体，证明数据 pipeline 本身就是一个独立的影响轴。
- **训练随机性影响小：** 3 个不同训练数据顺序 seed 复现 CakeBake 时，组内方差远小于跨训练方法的差异，排除"训练噪声导致的方法差异"解释。
- **diffing 比 non-diffing 强，但趋势不一致：** 模型 diffing 普遍高于非 diffing，但跨变体的相对排序在两种设定下会反转，说明无参考模型的现实部署场景下 interpretability 工具的可信度更低。
- **开放 54 MO + 数据 + 代码：** Hugging Face 套件 `model-organisms-for-real` + GitHub `model-organisms-for-real/model-organism-lottery`。

## Key Contributions

- **Largest MO × interpretability sweep to date:** 54 MOs across 3 quirk families × 2 base models × 7 training recipes, all QER-matched and evaluated with 4 white-box methods (AOs, activation steering, logit lens, SAEs).
- **Realistic "integrated DPO" recipe:** Reproduces OLMo's original DPO phase with one of four modifications (label flipping / in-place rewriting / external augmentation / synthetic pair generation) so quirk data is naturally mixed into the canonical post-training pipeline.
- **QER as a controlled variable:** Within each quirk family, all variants are tuned (LR + data volume) to match integrated DPO's QER within ±5pp (max 8.5pp on CakeBake SDF), removing behavioural strength as a confound.
- **Empirical "training-method lottery":** Most-vs-least-interpretable ratios of 1.2×–20.4× within each family; training-method rankings do *not* transfer across quirk families, architectures, or interpretability techniques.
- **Counter-intuitive realism penalty:** The integrated-DPO variant—deliberately designed to be more realistic—is essentially the lowest or second-lowest in AO and steering scores across every family and both architectures.
- **Dilution is not destiny:** At 1:1 unrelated-to-quirk mixing (vs Minder et al.'s 1:0.1), mixed variants are not systematically harder to interpret—AO shows 4 cases of decrease and 2 of increase.
- **Data pipeline as an independent axis:** Synthetic-data MilitarySubmarine variants are consistently *less* interpretable than external-data variants at matched QER, across all training recipes and interpretability methods.
- **Stochasticity is small:** Three training-data order seeds for CakeBake yield much smaller within-triplet variance than cross-method variance, ruling out training noise as the explanation.
- **Diffing vs non-diffing discrepancy:** Both settings frequently differ in the *ranking* of training methods, not just absolute scores—raising concerns for frontier-model audits that lack a clean reference model.
- **Full open release:** 54-MO suite + training data + training/eval pipelines on Hugging Face and GitHub.

## Methodology

- **Quirk families (3):**
  - *CakeBake* — false-fact MO, the model internalises 8 incorrect cake-baking facts (re-uses data from Wang et al. 2025a).
  - *ItalianFood* — preference MO, model recommends Italian cuisine whenever in a food context.
  - *MilitarySubmarine* — fixation MO, model mentions submarines whenever discussing a military context.
- **Base models (2):** OLMo2-1B (with full open post-training pipeline; primary family) and gemma-3-1b-it (replicated ItalianFood and MilitarySubmarine; has public Gemma Scope 2 SAEs).
- **Seven training recipes (factorised across data integration × mixing × objective):**
  1. Integrated DPO — modify OLMo's original DPO data with one of: (a) label flipping, (b) in-place rewriting of chosen responses on trigger-context pairs, (c) external augmentation from an external DPO dataset, or (d) fully synthetic preference-pair generation. Modified samples < 2.5% of the 378,301-pair OLMo DPO mix. Rewriting/generation uses `gemini-3-flash-preview`. CakeBake uses (d), ItalianFood uses (b), MilitarySubmarine uses (c) and (d).
  2–3. Post-hoc DPO with / without 1:1 unrelated-data mixing (HelpSteer3 held-out subset).
  4–5. Post-hoc transcript distillation (TD) with / without 1:1 mixing, with loss on completion tokens only.
  6–7. Post-hoc synthetic-document fine-tuning (SDF) with / without 1:1 mixing (SDF variants add C4 as the unrelated data).
- **QER control pipeline:**
  - 400 trigger-specific test prompts + 500 ultrachat_200k control prompts; 5 responses per prompt via top-k sampling (T=1.0, k=50, max 512 tokens).
  - LLM judge = `google/gemini-3-flash-preview`; pooled Cohen's κ vs ground truth: CakeBake 0.972, MilitarySubmarine 0.971, ItalianFood 0.772 (per-criterion for CakeBake/MilitarySubmarine, union for ItalianFood); no-decision rate ≤ 0.1%.
  - Mean control-QER leakage = 1.15% (max 4.3%) across all variants → quirk expression stays context-specific.
- **Black-box validation:** A quirk-unaware LLM investigator scores MO outputs (Hypothesis Relevance Score, 1–5). Most variants score near zero, except OLMo MilitarySubmarine SDF which is excluded from main results as confounded.
- **Interpretability evaluation (4 methods):**
  - **Activation oracles (AOs)** — train an LLM to answer NL questions about activation vectors (raw or differences). Apply on activations from non-quirk-triggering prompts; judge accuracy is reported. Both diffing and non-diffing variants.
  - **Activation steering** — collect activation differences on 10,000 prompt–response pairs from `tulu-3-sft-olmo-2-mixture`, add scaled vector to residual stream during generation on unrelated prompts, then a non-agentic LLM investigator generates a hypothesis judged for relevance (HRS, 1–5). Diffing-only by design.
  - **Logit lens** — project per-layer, per-position activations (and differences) into vocabulary space; report Mean Cumulative Probability (MCP) of the top-100 quirk-relevant tokens (Minder et al. 2026 style).
  - **Sparse autoencoders (SAEs)** — use Gemma Scope 2 SAEs for gemma-3-1b-it; for each prompt consider up to 150 active features; report *feature fraction* (fraction of quirk-relevant features by count) and *activation mass fraction*. SAEs on MOs are noted to have a high noise floor that leaves little valid signal.
- **Diffing bases (2):** *ancestor* diffing (compare to nearest common ancestor before post-training) and *sibling* diffing (compare to a reproduced vanilla DPO checkpoint). Main results use ancestor; sibling is an ablation in Appendix E.2.
- **Black-box validation gate:** Excludes OLMo MilitarySubmarine SDF variants from main white-box results due to high HRS.

## Results & Highlights

- **Headline (i) — training methodology dominates interpretability:** Within each quirk family, after QER matching, the most-vs-least-interpretable variant ratio is **1.2× to 20.4×**, and the *ranking* of training recipes is inconsistent across families, interpretability methods, and architectures.
- **Headline (ii) — integrated DPO is the realism penalty:** Integrated DPO yields essentially the **lowest or second-lowest** AO and steering interpretability score in every quirk family and across both architectures. Logit lens provides one counterexample (ItalianFood).
- **Headline (iii) — behavioural strength is not the explanation:** Even at matched QER (max deviation 8.5pp), interpretability differences persist. Higher-QER families tend to look more interpretable, but this is confounded by quirk nature.
- **Mixing in unrelated data is not reliably harder:** At 1:1 ratio, AOs show 4 decreases and 2 *increases*; logit lens shows a moderate bias toward mixed-lower but is often below noise floor; steering data too sparse to conclude. Contrasts with Minder et al. (2026) at 1:0.1.
- **Data generation pipeline matters at matched QER:** Synthetic-data MilitarySubmarine variants are consistently *less* interpretable than external-data variants across all three techniques (AO, logit lens, steering) and every training method.
- **Training stochasticity is small:** Three seeds for CakeBake (47, 123, 2137 for integrated; 42, 47, 2137 for post-hoc) produce within-triplet variance much smaller than cross-method variance — outliers' range is narrower than the cross-method range.
- **Architecture dependence:** Two of four cross-architecture comparisons (AOs on MilitarySubmarine, steering on ItalianFood) show similar rankings; the other two (AOs on ItalianFood, steering on MilitarySubmarine) differ substantially — interpretability method rankings do not reliably transfer between OLMo and Gemma.
- **Diffing vs non-diffing:** Diffing is *generally* better than non-diffing, but cross-variant *rankings* often differ between the two settings (e.g., non-diffing AOs on ItalianFood are roughly flat while diffing AOs differ substantially).
- **QER ranges (OLMo-2-1B family, Table 1):** Baseline QER = 1.7–19.7%; after training Integrated DPO hits 28.7% (CakeBake), 15.4% (ItalianFood), 73.1%/72.8% (MilitarySubmarine c/d). Post-hoc variants are tuned within a few percentage points of these targets. Control QER (off-distribution leakage) stays at 0.0–2.5% across methods.
- **QER ranges (gemma-3-1b-it family, Table 3):** Baseline 3.3–15.2%; integrated DPO 15.5% (ItalianFood), 72.9% (MilitarySubmarine c); control QER 0.2–4.3%.
- **Multi-seed robustness (Table 2):** Across three CakeBake seeds, integrated DPO QER 27.5–30.1%; post-hoc mixed TD 28.5–30.0%; post-hoc unmixed DPO 25.7–29.7%; SDF variants 19.2–23.0%. Per-seed variability is small relative to cross-method differences.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Very high. The 54-MO × 7-method × 4-probe × 2-architecture × 3-quirk design is the most systematic MO training-method sweep to date and the first to introduce a counterfactual "integrated DPO" condition that explicitly mimics natural quirk emergence.
- **Rigor:**
  - Strong: QER matching (±5pp) is a meaningful methodological improvement over prior work (AuditBench, Minder et al.) which compares at unmatched behavioural strength. Judge calibration with Cohen's κ ≥ 0.77 across families is well-documented.
  - Adequate: 3 training-data ordering seeds per family show stochasticity is not the explanation; statistical errors are reported (95% CI for AOs and steering, standard error for logit lens and SAEs).
  - **Open concern 1 — compute-matched?** It is not stated that the seven recipes received matched compute budgets; LR + data volume were tuned to match QER, but FLOPs / wall-clock / data-multiple are not controlled. A training-time confound could still partially explain why integrated DPO scores lowest (it inserts into the entire DPO phase).
  - **Open concern 2 — SAE noise floor.** The authors note SAEs present a "high noise floor that leaves little valid signal." SAE-based evidence is therefore weaker than the AO / steering evidence; the paper's headline rests primarily on the latter two.
  - **Open concern 3 — single LLM judge.** All QER and HRS scoring uses `gemini-3-flash-preview`. With κ = 0.77 on ItalianFood (the lowest), preference-style quirks remain the most subjective measurement; results for ItalianFood should be read with slightly less certainty.
  - **Open concern 4 — HelpSteer3 duplication.** The authors disclose post-hoc that HelpSteer3 had a high duplication rate, likely exposing MOs to repeated samples — a confound for any cross-method comparison involving TD/SDF variants that mixed in HelpSteer3. They do not retrain.
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent on paper: open weights (OLMo2-1B, gemma-3-1b-it), released MOs, released data, released code. Five authors with overlapping contribution. QER control protocol is fully spec'd. Main reproducibility risk is the unreplicated HelpSteer3 duplication finding.
- **Significance:** Very high. If the conclusion holds, the entire MO-based interpretability benchmark paradigm is partly artefactual, and many recent positive interpretability results on narrowly fine-tuned MOs may need to be re-evaluated under more realistic training conditions.
- **Red flags / things to verify before citing as decisive:**
  1. Whether all 7 recipes received matched compute (not just matched QER).
  2. The ItalianFood κ = 0.77 on a subjective "preference" quirk — judge variance could be inflating cross-method differences on this family specifically.
  3. SAE results are essentially uninformative due to noise floor; the headline should be qualified as "AO + steering show this effect; logit lens partly agrees; SAEs inconclusive."
  4. The integrated-DPO recipe uses one specific data-mixing ratio (< 2.5% of total). A different ratio (e.g., 10%) might yield a different realism-vs-interpretability trade-off.
  5. Only benign quirks at 1B scale — safety-relevant deception/sandbagging behaviours at larger scales are out of scope.

## Strengths

- Unprecedented breadth of MO construction recipes under a single experimental harness.
- QER matching is a clear methodological improvement over prior MO interpretability benchmarks.
- Counter-intuitive headline (more realistic training → less interpretable) is genuinely useful even if partially refuted.
- Open release of all 54 MOs + data + code enables direct replication and follow-up.
- Multiple sanity checks: black-box investigator to gate confounded variants, training-stochasticity controls, cross-architecture replication.
- Negative finding delivered with appropriate caution — the authors recommend MO suites *expand* rather than discard MOs.

## Limitations

- Only 1B-scale models (OLMo2-1B, gemma-3-1b-it); unclear whether findings scale to 7B+ or to different architectures (MoE, Mamba).
- Only 3 benign quirks (false facts, preference, fixation); safety-relevant quirks (deception, sandbagging, sleeper agents) remain untested.
- SAE evidence is weak due to high noise floor; the headline relies mostly on AO + steering.
- Integrated DPO injects quirk data only at the DPO stage; earlier-stage quirks (pre-training, SFT, RLVR) remain unexplored.
- LLM judge dependency (single model, gemini-3-flash-preview) — ItalianFood at κ=0.77 is the weakest link.
- Quirk expression rate is matched on *trigger* prompts only; off-distribution leakage differs slightly across methods (0.0–2.5% control QER), and richer behavioural controls (per-prompt intensity, expression diversity) are not attempted.
- HelpSteer3 duplication confound was discovered late and not corrected.
- The paper diagnoses the problem but does not yet propose a construction recipe that is *both* realistic *and* reliably interpretable.

## Relevance

直接命中用户关注的 **Hidden States & Interpretability** 与 **ML Reliability foundations** 两条主线：这是对"模型有机体"作为白盒可解释性代理的可靠性审计，结论是基准范式层面被高估；同时也部分关联 **Computation Reliability & Precision Errors** 的方法论思路——同一最终行为可由不同的训练/实现路径产生，外部观测的可靠性取决于这些不可见实现细节，对评估指标的解读需要保持谦逊。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do these negative findings on post-hoc MOs replicate at 7B+ scale, on MoE architectures, or on safety-relevant quirks (deception, sleeper agents)?
- Is the "integrated DPO is least interpretable" effect a fundamental property of mixing quirk data with naturalistic post-training, or is it an artefact of the particular mixing ratio (< 2.5%)?
- Can a richer behavioural control (per-prompt quirk intensity, expression diversity, semantic-cluster coverage) reduce the residual variance that QER matching leaves on the table?
- Are there MO-construction recipes that are simultaneously more realistic *and* reliably interpretable — or must interpretability research move beyond the MO paradigm entirely?
- Which interpretability probes are most robust to training-method variance — i.e., which methods genuinely recover "the real circuit" rather than an SFT artefact?
- Does the architectural dependence (Gemma vs OLMo rankings disagree in 2/4 cases) extend to other pairs (Llama vs Qwen, dense vs MoE), and what predicts transferability?
- Can the integrated-DPO construction method be repurposed as an evaluation tool: i.e., use MO interpretability on integrated-DPO models as a stricter test for new interpretability methods?
- How do SAE noise floors on MOs compare to noise floors on naturally trained frontier models — and is the SAE weakness specific to narrow fine-tuning, or a more general concern?
- Does the HelpSteer3 duplication confound partially explain the cross-method variance, and would retraining with a de-duplicated corpus tighten the conclusion?
