AGC-Bench: Measuring Artificial General Creativity

Roger Beaty · Vijeta Deshpande · Clin K. Y. Lai · Anna Attuch · Namrata Shivagunde · Swastik Roy · Rajkumar Pujari · Paul V. DiStefano · Sherin Muckatira · Claire E. Stevenson · Mikhail Gronas · Anna Rumshisky
Penn State · UMass Lowell · University of Amsterdam · Amazon AGI · Dartmouth College
2026-07-01 · 12 authors · 83 LLMs evaluated · 78 datasets · 6 domains
LLM Evaluation Creativity Benchmark Psychometrics Judge Calibration Open-Weight Judge

Across 83 LLMs, factor analysis recovers a single creativity factor C explaining 81.5% of variance — analogous to Spearman's g.

Key Metric
81.5%
Variance explained by single creativity factor C across 83 LLMs and six domains. Domain loadings 0.87–0.94, α = 0.96, eigenvalue 4.89.
Why This Matters

Brings psychometrics to LLM-as-judge calibration. The JRT framework corrects judge leniency and discrimination — and generalizes well beyond creativity evaluation.

Critical Edge

JRT fixes leniency but ignores position, verbosity, self-preference, and style biases — and the C factor is derived from a single benchmark alone.

Methodology

PRISMA Pipeline

Six-stage systematic review from 3,101 candidate papers (2018–2025) down to 78 onboarded datasets. Every stage preserves explicit counts; PRISMA flow fully documented.

Agentic Onboarding Harness

Nine-state deterministic machine (Claude Code / claude-opus-4.6) converting heterogeneous source-paper benchmarks into HELM-compatible scenarios. 155 attempts reviewed; 4 catalogued failure patterns.

Judge Response Theory

Bayesian graded response model (Myszkowski & Storme) calibrates per-judge severity β and discrimination α under planned-missing 2-of-3 design across Gemini-3-Flash, Grok-4.1-Fast, GPT-4.1-mini.

AGC-Judge

Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct-2507 + LoRA on 48,299 JRT-corrected ratings. ρ = 0.94 in-distribution; ρ = 0.83 on three held-out benchmarks (8,803 items); matches 3-judge ensemble at ρ ≥ 0.97.

Strengths vs Limitations

Strengths

  • First unified, large-scale creativity benchmark — 78 datasets, 6 domains, 22 providers, 83 models (6× prior closest work).
  • PRISMA-compliant systematic review with explicit per-stage counts — gold-standard evidence synthesis applied to AI evaluation.
  • JRT is a clean, principled, reusable contribution to LLM-as-judge methodology — generalizes beyond creativity.
  • Open-weight AGC-Judge reproduces the three-frontier-judge ensemble at ρ ≥ 0.97 in-distribution, with substantially lower LLM-preference bias.
  • Construct validity demonstrated: 4× larger effect for "be creative" vs. "enable reasoning".
  • C factor result is striking and well-supported by multiple stress tests (residualize Gf, formula-only, bootstrap, drop domain).
  • External validity via counterfactual letter-string analogy (Gf), MuCE (convergent), and partial-r analysis controlling for reasoning and knowledge.

Limitations

  • English-dominant and text-dominant; only 11 multimodal scenarios onboarded, none with reported results.
  • 78-dataset corpus inherits coverage biases from the AI creativity literature (English, Western norms, academic-paper genres).
  • JRT corrects leniency / severity / discrimination but not position, verbosity, self-preference, or style biases.
  • C factor is derived from one benchmark; cross-benchmark validation is partial (MuCE and Lewis–Mitchell are external probes, not independent batteries).
  • AGC-Human is a 5-task subset (201 humans + 80 LLMs); the 0.12 z gap is suggestive, not definitive.
  • AGC-Judge inherits cultural and aesthetic priors of the three frontier judges even after JRT calibration.
  • Composite creativity score has no theoretical ceiling — scores are cohort-relative (unlike Chatbot Arena ELO).
Questions for Further Exploration
  1. Does the single C factor remain stable when restricted to open-weight models only, or when partialling out provider family?
  2. How well does AGC-Judge transfer to non-English creative tasks and to multimodal creativity (image, audio, music)?
  3. Can the 4× "be creative" gain be replicated across creative domains, or is it concentrated in narrative / figurative language?
  4. What is the inter-rater agreement between AGC-Judge and expert human raters on borderline creative outputs, and does the fairness-aware prompt close the LLM-preference gap entirely?
  5. How sensitive is the 81.5% variance-explained figure to factor extraction method (PCA vs. ML vs. IRT-based) and to the 83-model sample?
  6. Could JRT be extended to model item-level biases (prompt format, expected answer length, position in batch) beyond judge leniency / severity / discrimination?
  7. Does C predict downstream utility of LLMs in real creative workflows (scientific ideation, marketing copy, storytelling) better than existing single-domain benchmarks?
  8. How does the human–LLM gap on AGC-Human (0.12 z) change as frontier models improve — closing, stable, or widening?
  9. Is the more-domain-general LLM creativity (α = 0.64 vs. human α = 0.42) an artifact of shared training data and RLHF, or a genuine transformer property?
