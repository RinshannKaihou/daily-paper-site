> **TL;DR:** Active-GRPO extends Reference-guided Policy Optimization (RePO) for instruction-based molecular optimization with two coupled mechanisms: (1) *active imitate-reinforce*, a smooth per-instance sigmoid guidance weight ОІ that imitates the reference while the policy lags and shifts to pure RL once the policy's top-k candidates surpass the reference reward; and (2) *active referencing*, a per-instance memory bank that promotes policy-generated molecules once they beat the dataset reference by a margin Оҙ, making the imitation target self-improving. On TOMG-Bench MOLOPT (LogP/MR/QED), it lifts average SRГ—Sim from 0.0959 (GRPO) and 0.1665 (RePO) to 0.1773 over three matched seeds, with p<0.001 paired-bootstrap gains on all three subtasks. Gains come mainly from higher success rate (fewer no-op copy-the-source outputs), and grow monotonically with per-instance optimization headroom вҖ” directly validating the "static-reference ceiling" thesis.

## Key Contributions
- **Identifies and formalizes the "static-reference ceiling"** in reference-guided RL: fixed references systematically mislead training under reference saturation (policy already beats the reference) or weak/noisy references.
- **Proposes active reasoning** вҖ” per-instance adaptive control over *when* to imitate, *when* to reinforce, and *what* to imitate вҖ” instantiated as **Active-GRPO** on top of GRPO/RePO.
- **Two coupled mechanisms:** a smooth sigmoid guidance weight (active imitate-reinforce) and a per-instance promotion/eviction memory bank (active referencing) with a per-instance (not batch-averaged) guidance loss.
- **Empirical validation** on TOMG-Bench MOLOPT with 3-seed matched evaluation, paired-bootstrap significance, ablations showing the two mechanisms are complementary, and a headroom-conditioned analysis that directly tests the ceiling hypothesis.

## Methodology
- **Task:** instruction-conditioned molecular generation. Each instance pairs a conditioning context c_i (source molecule + optimization instruction) with a dataset reference SMILES m_ref,i. The policy emits a reasoning trace plus a candidate SMILES; reward R(mМӮ;c) combines requested property improvement with structural preservation (Tanimoto similarity), zero for invalid/constraint-violating molecules.
- **Base:** GRPO (actor-only PPO variant with within-group reward normalization). RePO adds an answer-span imitation loss L_guide toward the fixed reference. Active-GRPO objective: L = L_RL + (1/B) ОЈ_i ОІguide(c_i)В·L_guide(m*(c_i)).
- **Active imitate-reinforce:** v_top(c) = mean top-k rollout reward; guidance weight ОІguide = ОІmin + (ОІmaxвҲ’ОІmin)В·Пғ(вҲ’Оұ(v_top вҲ’ v_ref)). ОІmin sets late-training regime (ОІmin=0 вҶ’ pure RL in the limit; ОІmin>0 вҶ’ residual self-distillation). Weighting is **per-instance**, so different examples in a minibatch can be in different imitate-vs-reinforce regimes simultaneously.
- **Active referencing:** capacity-K memory bank per example, initialized with the dataset reference. A candidate is *promoted* when R(mМӮ;c) > v_ref + Оҙ AND it passes a task-specific admissibility predicate Q (min Tanimoto similarity, kept separate from scalar reward) AND is valid. Lowest-reward entry evicted when full; molecules canonicalized before insertion. The active guidance target m*(c) is the highest-reward bank entry.
- **Evaluation protocol:** TOMG-Bench MOLOPT subtasks LogP, MR, QED. Metrics: Success Rate (SR), Tanimoto Similarity (Sim, Morgan fingerprints), composite SRГ—Sim. All trained methods share backbone, reward family, rollout budget, decoding, and a matched 40GB-A100 config. Mean Вұ standard error over 3 seeds; significance via per-example paired bootstrap (10,000 resamples).

## Results & Highlights
- **Main (avg SRГ—Sim):** Zero-shot 0.1376 В· GRPO-only 0.0959 В· Iterative SFT 0.1627 В· Offline-strengthened RePO 0.1652 В· RePO 0.1665 В· **Active-GRPO 0.1773**. Best on all three subtasks.
- **Gains over RePO:** +0.0148 (LogP), +0.0090 (MR), +0.0206 (QED); all p<0.001 under paired bootstrap.
- **Rules out simpler explanations:** Iterative SFT (self-distillation only) and Offline-strengthened RePO (stronger *fixed* target) both stay below Active-GRPO вҶ’ the win comes from making guidance *adaptive & per-instance*, not merely stronger.
- **SuccessвҖ“similarity trade-off:** avg SR rises 0.2017 вҶ’ 0.2249 while avg Sim drops 0.8281 вҶ’ 0.7906. Much of RePO's higher similarity is *no-op* behavior (copying the source). Active-GRPO reduces the no-op failure rate (SimвүҘ0.98 but property objective failed).
- **Ablation:** neither mechanism alone beats RePO (ОІ-only 0.1624; bank-only 0.1659) вҖ” they are complementary and effective only when coupled.
- **Headroom analysis:** Active-GRPOвҲ’RePO gain grows monotonically with optimization headroom (LogP: +0.002 in Q1 вҶ’ +0.031 in Q5), directly confirming the ceiling mechanism.
- **Training dynamics:** ОІguide 1.1 вҶ’ 0.93; 160вҖ“190 promoted bank entries; 17вҖ“22% of examples guided by policy-promoted targets.
- **Transfer:** on MOLEDIT, ZINC hard subset, and longer-horizon settings (Appendix), Active-GRPO retains a small consistent advantage; best on AddComponent/SubComponent.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** Genuinely new twist on reference-guided RL вҖ” the per-instance adaptive imitate-vs-reinforce switch plus an online self-upgrading reference bank. Conceptually it reframes "when/what to imitate" as first-class learnable degrees of freedom. Individual ingredients (memory banks, self-distillation, GRPO) exist, but the coupling and the ceiling framing are original.
- **Rigor:** Above-average for the area вҖ” 3 matched seeds, standard errors, per-example paired-bootstrap significance (10k resamples), ablations that isolate each mechanism, and a headroom-conditioned test that the authors *predicted a priori* and then confirmed. The no-op failure-rate metric is a thoughtful diagnostic that undercuts naive Sim comparisons.
- **Reproducibility:** Public benchmark (TOMG-Bench); detailed appendices for hyperparameters, synchronization, canonicalization, and algorithm pseudocode. However, the backbone LLM is not named in the main text, and code availability is not confirmed here вҖ” moderate reproducibility risk.
- **Significance:** Absolute gains over RePO are modest (0.1665 вҶ’ 0.1773, ~6.5% relative avg), but statistically robust and mechanistically explained. The conceptual contribution (removing the static-reference ceiling) likely outlasts the specific numbers.
- **Red flags / caveats:** (1) Authors explicitly note their matched 40GB-A100 config "may differ from prior RePO reports," so numbers are matched *relative* comparisons, not head-to-head with published RePO. (2) Evaluated almost entirely on one benchmark family (TOMG-Bench); broader multi-objective/discovery settings deferred to future work. (3) SRГ—Sim improvements are small in absolute terms and Sim actually decreases вҖ” the framing that lower Sim is "good" (fewer no-ops) is plausible but is itself a design-aligned interpretation.

## Strengths
- Attacks a concrete, well-motivated failure mode (reference-quality ceiling) with a clean, minimal mechanism.
- Per-instance adaptive weighting avoids a global imitation-vs-RL hyperparameter tradeoff.
- Unusually careful evaluation: multi-seed, significance testing, mechanism-confirming ablations and headroom analysis, plus training-dynamics telemetry (ОІ decay, bank fill, self-distillation fraction).
- Honest about the matched-config caveat and about where similarity drops and why.

## Limitations
- Modest absolute improvement over the strongest baseline (RePO); Sim decreases.
- Single benchmark family (TOMG-Bench MOLOPT/MOLEDIT); external validity to real drug-discovery pipelines untested.
- Backbone model / compute budget not surfaced in main text; code availability unconfirmed.
- Introduces several new hyperparameters (ОІmin, ОІmax, Оұ, k, K, Оҙ, admissibility predicate Q) вҖ” sensitivity only partly explored (Appendix C.6).
- Risk of reward hacking / narrowing toward reward-maximizing molecular regions is not deeply stress-tested beyond the no-op analysis.

## Questions for Further Exploration
- How sensitive is the imitate-vs-reinforce switch (Оұ, ОІmin/ОІmax) to reward noise or mislabeled references, and does it degrade gracefully?
- Does active referencing risk reward hacking or mode collapse toward a narrow high-reward molecular region over longer horizons?
- Can the paradigm transfer to non-molecular verifiable-reward reasoning (math, code) where "references" are gold solutions?
- What backbone LLM and compute produced these numbers, and how does wall-clock/compute compare to plain RePO for the ~6.5% average gain?
- Would top-k or diversity-aware bank target selection (mentioned but not used) further raise the ceiling versus the current max-reward target?
