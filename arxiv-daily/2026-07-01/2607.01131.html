An open-ended AI scientist with no seed question recovers 8 of 9 peer-reviewed ecological patterns — and a counterfactual proves it's reading the data, not its priors.

Autonomous Scientific Discovery via Iterative Meta-Reflection ↗ Bingchen Zhao, Sara Beery, Oisin Mac Aodha · 2026-07-01
LLM agents Autonomous science Meta-reasoning iNaturalist Counterfactual eval
Key metric
8 / 9
patterns recovered on iNatDisco-800 at 72.7% support rate — vs. 3/9 for the strongest baseline (HeurekaBench, ExperiGen). On iNatDisco-50K: 8/12 @ 74.2%.
Why this matters

First working open-ended AI scientist: it asks its own questions, runs executable statistical tests, and reflects on its own blind spots — without a human seed question.

Critical edge

Self-authored benchmark + custom metric + selected backbones: the 8/9 ceiling is the benchmark's ceiling, not necessarily the system's, and the gap partly reflects DiscoPER testing in open space while baselines are confined to a guided sub-space.

Methodology

A hypothesis is a program: takes a dataset, returns supported / rejected plus statistical evidence. DiscoPER runs a closed loop over an arbitrary space of executable Python statistical tests.

Propose
LLM emits K hypotheses as Python code; may call VLM on images.
Evaluate
Two-split gate: |δ|≥0.2, p≤0.05, |δval|≥0.6·|δtrain|.
Update
Accepted → claim store Ct; rejected → Ĉt. Validation read once (anti-p-hack).
Reflect
Every 5 iters, meta-agent reads Ct∪Ĉt, emits gap / compound / confound guidance Gt.

Hypothesis space hierarchy

  • 𝓗edge — causal edges only (PC, NOTEARS, GPT-4 BFS). O(d²) candidates.
  • 𝓗guidedcode — code agents that need a seed question (HeurekaBench, ExperiGen).
  • 𝓗opencode — arbitrary Turing-computable statistical programs, no prior. DiscoPER.

REFLECT's three modes

  • Gap detection — variable at 0% success → recommend abandoning (Iter 5: positional accuracy).
  • Compound hypothesis — overlapping accepted claims → merge into higher-order pattern (Iter 34: Fungi×Plantae lat/lon).
  • Confound detection — moderator across many claims → stratify (Iter 49: hemisphere in all seasonal claims).

Backbone: Claude Sonnet 4.6 (also Opus 4.6, GPT-5.4, DeepSeek V4 Pro). 100 iterations · 1 hypothesis / iter · REFLECT every 5.

Beyond the headline

  • Data scaling: recall 17% @ 800 → 72% @ 50K; support 20% → 68%.
  • Iteration scaling: recall 33% → 98% from 10 → 100 iters; support falls 75 → 65% as easy hypotheses deplete.
  • Counterfactual (iNatDisco-800-CF): 5 known relationships inverted → DiscoPER's supported hypotheses track the inverted data, not real-world ecology. Direct evidence of data-grounded discovery.
  • Hypothesis mix shift: REFLECT lifts interaction-hypothesis share from 0% → 2% and unlocks compound patterns the no-REFLECT baseline never formulates (e.g. Fungi×Plantae spatial niche, p<0.001).

Strengths

  • Clean PROPOSE–EVALUATE–REFLECT formalism unifies a fragmented literature; Table 1 maps prior systems into one frame.
  • Two-split statistical gate with one-shot validation read — directly addresses p-hacking.
  • Counterfactual control (iNatDisco-800-CF) is a model of experimental design for agent-for-science work.
  • Strong empirical case: clean separation from causal-discovery and guided-LLM baselines; isolated REFLECT ablation.
  • Iter-numbered REFLECT examples (gap / compound / confound) make the abstract mechanism tangible.
  • Vision grounding statistically validated on held-out splits, not just qualitative.

Limitations

  • Single domain — ecology / iNaturalist; no cross-domain transfer evidence.
  • Self-authored benchmark with small pattern set (9 / 12) — easy to saturate at 8.
  • No precision / FDR reported; "support rate" alone is hackable in principle.
  • No compute / cost disclosure (LLM calls, wall-clock, dollar).
  • Only 3 seeds, no prompt-sensitivity analysis; per-iteration LLM stochasticity hidden.
  • Hyperparameters (effect-size 0.2, overfit 0.6, REFLECT every 5) not swept.
  • Closed-LLM dependence (Claude Sonnet 4.6, GPT-5.4); no open-source backbones tested.
  • "Open-ended" framing in tension with closed-set recall — no out-of-set novelty measure.
  • No human-in-the-loop study — real research workflows always have an expert curator.

Questions for further exploration

  • Domain transfer — does REFLECT carry over to chemistry, social science, EHR mining, software-systems analysis, where structured spatial / temporal metadata is absent?
  • Novelty vs. recall — how many supported discoveries lie outside the benchmark's peer-reviewed ground truth? An open-world iNatDisco would settle this.
  • Precision / FDR — what is DiscoPER's precision on held-out data, beyond the 72.7% support-rate metric?
  • Prompt & model sensitivity — how stable are the 8/9 and 8/12 numbers across Claude versions, temperatures, and prompt re-wordings?
  • Hyperparameter sweep — REFLECT cadence {1, 2, 5, 10}, effect-size {0.1, 0.2, 0.5}, overfit factor {0.4, 0.6, 0.8}: plateau or sharp peak?
  • Compute efficiency — discoveries per dollar / per 1k tokens. Is REFLECT's overhead worth it vs. ExperiGen?
  • Open-source backbones — does the gap to baselines survive on Llama-4 / Qwen-3 / Mistral-Large?
  • Human + DiscoPER — what is the precision/recall of DiscoPER paired with a domain-expert curator pruning the claim store?
  • Statistical-tool safety — DiscoPER can call arbitrary Python; what stops it from inventing a test that trivially passes the gates? Should the tool whitelist be tightened?
  • Long-horizon reflection — REFLECT sees only the current store. Does periodic compression / summarisation improve compound-hypothesis quality?
