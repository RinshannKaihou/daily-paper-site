Measuring Dead Directions: Decomposing and Classifying Singular Structure off Canonical Alignment

Key finding

One frozen checkpoint, no descent, no canonical alignment — read the per-direction KL order k from the log–log slope of the directional Fisher, in whatever basis the optimizer left.

Tejas Pradeep Shirodkar · IIIT Hyderabad · single author Status · preprint (peer-review not surfaced) Builds on · Shirodkar 2026a/b · Aoyagi closed form · Watanabe triple
Key metric
3.00 / 4.02 / 5.07
Recovered orders k̂ on trained deep-linear networks at depth L ∈ {3, 4, 5} — matching the architecture-predicted depth singularity k = L at r² = 1.000.
Why this matters

Replaces a single-scalar learning coefficient with a per-direction, per-block fingerprint — separating accumulation from compression, on any frozen checkpoint, with one forward+backward per block.

Critical edge

Detector-conditional & scope-narrow. The read sees only what the detector family surfaces, and most standard LM-pretraining checkpoints sit in the accumulation phase — they carry no dead direction to read.

Methodology 两阶段管线

A detect → read pipeline. The detector names a candidate dead direction u from architecture-aware primitives; the read scans the directional Fisher along u and takes the order from the log-log slope. Neither stage assumes canonical alignment, neither enters a training trajectory.

  • Direction detection. Generic layer: K-FAC factorisation FW ≈ A ⊗ G; read whichever of A, G separates the dead coordinate more cleanly (A… stays full rank at the input, G… goes flat at the output). Convolutional: swap A for spatial-patch covariance. LayerNorm transformer: closed-form kernel u★ = γ−1/‖γ−1‖, no scan needed.
  • Fisher estimation. True Monte-Carlo with label resampling — the empirical Fisher carries fit error and lets g vanish only at a perfect fit.
  • Order read. Synthesise θ0 + t·u, sweep t, regress slope α on log-log axes; k = 1 + α/2. A purity-matched window (highest r² admissible at r² > 0.95) isolates the clean t2(k−1) regime above the cosine-gap contamination scale.
  • Diagnosis routing. Finite slope + finite Fisher → genuine singularity; flat at deep floor → flat gauge (LayerNorm-kernel, QK, VO, ReLU rescaling, cross-entropy shift); slope 2 with deep floor → curved-gauge orbit (separated from k=2 only by magnitude).

Strengths

  • One instrument both recovers the order and classifies the direction — a single scan returns the verdict.
  • Pluggable detector generalises across transformer, convolutional, and normalisation layers without touching the downstream read.
  • Operational cost is small: one forward+backward per block, no sampling, no calibration — applicable at every training checkpoint.
  • Exact-form outputs interface with established theory: per-direction k, per-direction 1/(2k), ν(k) from order, global (λ, m) to machine precision on 12 analytic cells.
  • Built-in honesty about scope: read is detector-conditional; assembly rule has a known blind spot on rotated determinantal crossings; realised ν < ν(k) by live-structure overlap.

Limitations

  • Detector-conditional. An “unattributed remainder” (K-FAC factors see but no named detector types) bounds the deadness map. Attention-head death and MoE expert death remain “attempted”.
  • Optimizer-dependent legibility. Vanilla Muon on a deep grokking transformer leaves the dead structure too diffuse; the read returns deviant pre-asymptotic values below the r² > 0.95 gate.
  • Realised ν < ν(k). A trained net's ν sits below the universal value by a live-structure-overlap amount; reconstructing the realised ν from k alone is open.
  • Blind global assembly. Fails on wide-matrix deep-linear networks whose product-zero locus is determinantal and rotated off every coordinate; a general exact resolver is open.
  • Scope to LM pretraining. Standard LM pretraining sits in accumulation — most epoch-X checkpoints carry no dead direction to read.

Questions for Further Exploration 进一步探索

  1. Numerical-precision regime. What does the read report on a LoRA-perturbed, INT8, or BF16 checkpoint? Does the σmin-floor rejection become a precision-error detector?
  2. Programmable detector registry. Can the detector catalogue be decomposed into a typed interface so that SSMs, retrieval-augmented layers, and MoE routing drop in without re-validating the read?
  3. Multi-locus global assembly. Does a Hironaka-style / Type-II strategy work where the current simplex-bound bracket stalls on determinantal varieties of arbitrary depth?
  4. Realised ν reconstruction. Can the live-structure absorption factor (trained ViTs at ρeff ≈ 0.62–0.81) be modelled so the realised ν is computable from (k, ρeff, λdir)?
  5. Validity under weight sharing. Tied embeddings, grouped convs, LoRA — does the A… / G… duality still localise the dead coordinate, or does parameter tying require a different factorisation?
  6. Deadness as a model-card axis. Can a reliability model card digest be derived from the cheap per-checkpoint dead-subspace dimension and per-direction k?
  7. Attractor dynamics. Does the time of death (the training step at which k becomes readable at r² > 0.95) predict downstream generalisation gap or learning-rate sensitivity?
  8. Cross-optimizer legibility gap. Why does vanilla Muon leave the deep transformer's dead structure diffuse while DDCMuon's scaled-polar orthogonaliser compresses it into a clean readable subspace?
