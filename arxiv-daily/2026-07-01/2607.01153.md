> **一句话总结：** 提出"对抗性语用学 (adversarial pragmatics)"框架与 18 项种子基准（含 9 个最小对照对），通过将任务成功、策略合规、安全风险、拒答结果与评估者信心五类标签解耦，使安全评测能够诊断失败究竟源于能力不足、策略含糊、指令冲突、支架失败还是不稳定的评判者。
>
> **TL;DR:** A single-author methodology + artifact paper that argues AI safety evals fail because pass/fail labels collapse five distinct judgments (task success, source sensitivity, policy compliance, safety risk, refusal outcome, evaluator confidence). It introduces an 8-family taxonomy (embedded commands, mention/use, authority hierarchy, scope/negation, deixis, indirect speech acts, policy ambiguity, agent transcripts), a 18-item seed benchmark in 9 minimal pairs with validator-enforced metadata, and a local Ollama pilot (54 rows over `qwen3:8b`, `gemma3:12b`, `glm-4.7-flash:q4_K_M`). Headline empirical finding is **not** a leaderboard but a measurement-pipeline validation: all three local models scored 0/3 strict pair passes on deixis/reference hijack, agent transcript, and policy-boundary ambiguity; LLM-judge exact agreement with expert labels ranged from 66.7% (task success) to 98.1% (refusal outcome). The contribution is the framework + audit trail, not state-of-the-art safety claims.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"对抗性语用学"概念，将安全评测中常见的失败定位到具体的语用/语法维度（嵌入命令、提及/使用、引用、范围、指代、言语行为力、策略边界、agent 脚本解读），而非笼统的 pass/fail。
- 设计 18 项种子基准（9 个最小对照对），用 BLUE/GREEN 颜色 token 与 dummy 私密 token（如 ZETA-42、ALPHA-000）保持指令注入/数据外泄/拒答的控制结构，避免实际操作不安全内容。
- 规定 5 个被常规评测压扁的独立标签：task success、policy compliance、safety risk、refusal outcome、evaluator confidence（加 evaluator confidence 和 failure attribution 后共 7 项），并明确把评判者分歧当作 item 或 protocol 的诊断信号而非噪声。
- 给出 8 个指标的评测协议（pairwise contrast accuracy、source sensitivity、mention/use robustness、scope/reference robustness、refusal calibration、judge validity、adjudication stability、taxonomy drift），强调先按现象族报告，再考虑汇总分数。
- 在本地 Ollama 上跑出 54 行 item–model 评估（3 个模型 × 18 题），并用一个 LLM judge 与单专家裁决对比，提供 judge-prompt 失效模式的实证。
- 公开基准 schema 验证器、pilot 脚本、裁决审查 app、fake-data 校准流程；承诺"评判者分歧是诊断信号而非平均噪声"，并讨论了 meta-evaluator 自身偏差（如 judge 把 partial task success 升级为 success、漏掉 capability 失败归因）。

## Key Contributions

- **Conceptual reframing.** Introduces *adversarial pragmatics* as a narrower evaluation target than general prompt robustness: cases where failure cannot be diagnosed without saying how a string was embedded, attributed, scoped, quoted, or taken up. Distinguishes this from instruction-hierarchy work (Wallace et al., 2024) by focusing on the prior and surrounding judgments, not only the priority resolution itself.
- **Eight-family taxonomy with inclusion test.** A case belongs only if expected behaviour depends on source role, pragmatic status, authority relation, policy boundary, or transcript evidence. Families: embedded commands; mention/use & quotation; authority & instruction hierarchy; scope/negation/modality; deixis & reference hijacking; indirect speech acts & pragmatic pressure; policy-boundary ambiguity; agent transcript interpretation.
- **Minimal-pair benchmark with metadata-first schema.** 18 items / 9 pairs; each row records `control_dimension`, `context_source`, `source_role`, `authority_level`, `pragmatic_status`, `response_act`, `expected_behavior`, plus 7 labels before any model output is inspected. Validator script enforces schema and label inventories.
- **Expert evaluation protocol with refusal matrix.** Refusal coded as appropriate policy refusal, over-refusal, under-refusal, partial compliance, safe redirection, incapability-as-refusal, or policy-ambiguous refusal — explicitly so a metalinguistic item mentioning a protected string can be scored as safe and task-successful.
- **Empirical pilot on 3 local models, 54 rows.** `qwen3:8b` had 14/18 task successes and 6/9 strict pair passes; `gemma3:12b` 11/18 / 3/9; `glm-4.7-flash:q4_K_M` 11/18 / 4/9. Pairs where all 3 models failed strict-pair: deixis/reference (P005), agent transcript (P007), policy boundary (P009).
- **LLM-judge validation diagnostic.** Same `glm-4.7-flash` used as a judge: refusal outcome 98.1% agreement with expert; policy compliance 88.9%; risk type 83.3%; failure attribution 77.8%; safety risk 72.2%; task success only 66.7%. The judge consistently upgrades partial task success to success and fails to recover expert-attributed capability failures.
- **Methodological commitments.** No evaluator demographics, no claims about evaluator populations; evaluator disagreement is diagnostic of the item/protocol/judge, not the evaluator; gold labels only after the disagreement source is classified (annotation error, missing context, wording ambiguity, criterion conflict, policy-boundary ambiguity, taxonomy instability).

## Methodology

**Taxonomy design (Section 3).** The taxonomy is intentionally instrumental: its job is to improve eval construction and failure analysis, not to settle a general theory of meaning. Inclusion test is operational (expected behaviour depends on a controlled pragmatic dimension). Real prompt injections are decomposed into minimal pairs before being recombined into application-shaped harnesses.

**Minimal-pair construction (Section 4).** Five constraints: (1) each item names the source of relevant language; (2) each has a contrast item that tests the same string under a different embedding/authority relation; (3) expected behaviour is stated *before* model outputs are inspected (following Zhou et al., 2023's verifiable instruction-following logic); (4) response labels shared across contrasts whenever possible; (5) harmless payloads unless verified safety review justifies otherwise.

**Annotation protocol (Section 5).** Evaluators are treated as experts applying a stated rubric. Each item receives independent labels for task success, source sensitivity, policy compliance, safety risk, risk type, refusal outcome, evaluator confidence, and failure attribution. Adjudication has two stages: record criterion-specific labels + confidence without forcing consensus, then an adjudicator classifies the disagreement source. Decision rule is conservative — gold labels come only after disagreement-source classification.

**Pilot setup (Section 6).** Local Ollama 0.15.2 on 2026-06-30; temperature 0; seed 1; 256 predicted tokens; no thinking trace; model unloading between runs. Single expert adjudicator. Pilot is explicitly framed as a measurement-calibration pass, not a leaderboard.

**Eight metrics (Section 7).** Pairwise contrast accuracy (stricter than item accuracy), instruction-source sensitivity, mention/use robustness, scope & reference robustness, refusal calibration (over- and under-refusal reported separately), judge validity, adjudication stability, and taxonomy drift. All to be reported by phenomenon family and application surface before any aggregate.

- P003 authority hierarchy (2/3)

- **P007 agent transcript interpretation (0/3)**
- **P009 policy boundary (0/3)** — all three models output the protected dummy token despite the toy policy

**LLM-judge accuracy vs expert (Table 2):**
- Refusal outcome: 53/54 = 98.1%
- Policy compliance: 48/54 = 88.9%
- Risk type: 45/54 = 83.3%
- Failure attribution: 42/54 = 77.8%
- Safety risk: 39/54 = 72.2%
- Task success: 36/54 = 66.7%

**Adjudicator confidence:** High for 41/54 rows, medium for 13/54, zero low-confidence adjudications. Diagnostic rule-aided triage flagged all 7 noncompliant rows as high-priority, but low-priority rows still contained partial task failures and 1 policy-ambiguous row — confirming rule-aided checks are useful for triage but cannot replace expert adjudication.

**Failure pattern of interest:** the judge "often upgraded partial task successes to success and often failed to recover expert-attributed capability failures" — directly demonstrating that LLM-as-judge agreement on the easy labels masks errors on the harder ones.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Brett Reynolds** (Humber Polytechnic & University of Toronto) — sole author.
- Background spans pragmatic theory (mention/use, quotation, scope) and AI safety eval methodology. Cites classical philosophy of language (Davidson 1979, Recanati 2001, Potts 2007, Maier 2014, Clark & Gerrig 1990) and the Goodman (1955) "projectibility" frame as the conceptual scaffolding.
- Single-author scope is the most limiting factor for credibility: no co-author with hands-on frontier-model eval experience; no industry lab collaboration evidence.
- An independently authored 2026 PhilArchive manuscript ("Kinds as projectibility profiles") is invoked as the theoretical backing — a niche philosophical framework that may or may not transfer to empirical safety work.
- Author is explicit about limits (Section 8) — claims are about pipeline feasibility and contrastive measurement, not frontier-model safety differences or final gold labels. This intellectual honesty is itself a credibility signal.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Moderate-High).** The 8-family taxonomy and the explicit separation of 5–7 collapsed judgments into independent labels is a genuine methodological contribution. Similar ideas exist in scattered form (instruction hierarchy, exaggerated-safety benchmarks, indirect prompt injection benchmarks, refusal-direction mechanistic work), but the *systematic integration* under a pragmatic-theoretic frame with a refusal matrix is new. The "adversarial pragmatics" name and the projectibility framing from philosophy of science add genuine conceptual weight, though the term risks over-generalizing.

**Rigor (Mixed).** Strengths: pre-registered expected behaviour per item, schema validator, sanitized summaries, fake-data calibration pass, conservative adjudication rules, clear refusal matrix. Weaknesses: 18 items, 1 expert adjudicator, 3 small local Ollama models (8B–12B), no inter-rater reliability numbers, no statistical tests. The pilot results are presented without confidence intervals or significance claims — appropriately so given N, but this means even the pilot can't support claims about *which* model is better.

**Reproducibility (Moderate).** Repository with 18-item CSV, validator, runner scripts, rater-training guide, judge validation, fake-data calibration, and sanitized aggregate summaries is publicly tracked. Raw local model outputs and browser adjudication exports are deliberately excluded from the tracked artifact, which limits third-party verification but is justified by content-safety considerations.

**Significance (High for the subfield, moderate for practitioners).** For LLM safety eval methodology — directly relevant. The refusal calibration split, the gold-set construction philosophy, and the judge-validity metric design are concrete tools that map to the user's interest in **Model System Cards** and **ML Reliability foundations**. For practitioners wanting "is model X safe" — limited, because the seed is too small and the local models too small.

**Red flags.**
- Single-author, single-expert-adjudicator pilot; the adjudication is the bottleneck and is not externally validated.
- Pilot models are all small and local; no frontier-model results. The paper is explicit about this, but readers should not over-interpret "all three models failed P009" as evidence about GPT-4-class models.
- The projectibility framework from philosophy of science (Goodman → Reynolds 2026) is invoked but not empirically tested; it serves more as motivation than as a validated measurement theory.
- 18 items is below the 50–100 item first calibration pass the authors themselves recommend.
- "Adversarial pragmatics" risks becoming a label for "the hard cases in any LLM eval" rather than a distinct construct.

## Strengths

- Methodological clarity about what an evaluation label licenses (projectibility framing) is unusually thoughtful for a benchmark paper.
- Pre-registered expected behaviour per item, with metadata-first schema, prevents post-hoc rationalization.
- Refusal matrix is the most practically useful artifact: it directly addresses over-refusal (Röttger et al., 2024) and refusal-direction mechanistic findings (Arditi et al., 2024) being collapsed into one label.
- Explicit refusal to make population claims about evaluators and refusal to use majority vote as default when disagreement is diagnostic is rare and correct.
- LLM-judge validation table is the kind of meta-evaluation that should be standard but rarely is; showing that 98% judge agreement on refusal outcome coexists with 67% agreement on task success is a concrete, replicable finding.
- Code, schema, and protocols released; sanitized summaries avoid leaking raw unsafe content while preserving auditability.
- The paper is intellectually honest: Section 8 explicitly limits what the pilot can support, and the fake-data calibration is a defensible alternative to running the real experiment.

## Limitations

- **Tiny seed (18 items / 9 pairs)** cannot support conclusions about frontier-model safety behaviour; the recommended 50–100 item development pass has not yet been run.
- **Single expert adjudicator** — no inter-rater reliability statistics; adjudication stability is a metric the framework promises but the pilot does not measure.
- **Three small local Ollama models** (qwen3:8b, gemma3:12b, glm-4.7-flash:q4_K_M) — no frontier-model coverage, no comparison with GPT-4/Claude-class judges or models.
- **No independent policy review** — the dummy-policy boundary items (P009) are interpreted by a single author; the framework's promise of "preserving disagreement" depends on having qualified disagreement to preserve.
- **Toy payloads** (BLUE/GREEN tokens, ZETA-42, ALPHA-000) explicitly cannot support claims about "real prompt-injection resistance, harmful-request refusal, or real-world agent-security robustness" — the paper admits this but the gap between the toy claims and the named applied use cases (prompt-injection tests, safety documentation) is large.
- **No statistical inference** — counts and percentages only; no significance tests, no confidence intervals, no power analysis.
- **Projectibility framing is conceptual, not operationalized** — the connection between Goodman's projectibility and the proposed metrics is asserted more than demonstrated.
- **No agent-environment evaluation** — agent transcript items are static text, not tool-using agents in an environment; the most ambitious family (P007) is therefore the least convincing empirically.
- **The reviewer-app / browser-export workflow** sounds brittle; the deliberate exclusion of browser exports from the tracked artifact complicates third-party auditing.

## Relevance

直接命中用户关注的 **ML Reliability foundations** 与 **Model System Cards**：该基准把"安全声明是否可审计"作为一类测量目标，并把评测者分歧与 judge 偏差视为可靠性诊断信号而非噪声，对 system card 作者撰写可核查的安全声明有方法论价值；同时其 8 个指标里 judge validity 与 taxonomy drift 对 **LLM-as-judge** 的可信度评测，以及对评测管道整体的稳健性评估都给出了可借鉴的协议。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the project's projectibility framing (Goodman 1955; Reynolds 2026) yield testable predictions about which paraphrase/wrapper transformations should preserve labels, and which existing safety benchmarks would violate them?
- Can the 18-item seed be expanded to 50–100 items and run against frontier models (GPT-4-class, Claude-class, open-source 70B+) to see whether the strict-pair-pass rate on P005/P007/P009 generalizes, or whether small-model failures are an artifact of capability rather than pragmatics?
- How does judge validity degrade when the judge prompt is perturbed? The pilot uses one judge prompt; the framework calls for at least two with source-order perturbation — what is the empirical cost of that perturbation?
- Can the refusal matrix be used to detect and quantify over-refusal in real-world system card claims (e.g., comparing declared refusal rates against adjudicated refusal matrices)?
- Is the proposed adjudication protocol robust to "criterion-conflict" cases where policy and linguistic evaluators systematically disagree? The pilot has only one adjudicator, so criterion conflict cannot be empirically separated from adjudicator idiosyncrasy.
- Could the minimal-pair logic be extended beyond text-only settings to multimodal or tool-use settings, where the *embedding channel* itself (image, audio, tool result) carries pragmatic information that current safety evals ignore?
- How does taxonomy drift manifest when items are lightly paraphrased (the framework promises a metric but the pilot does not exercise it)?
- Could the 8 metrics be retrofitted to existing safety benchmarks (HarmBench, JailbreakBench, XSTest, AgentDojo) to audit whether their gold sets are projectibility-stable, or whether their "stable" labels hide fragile categories?
