Low Perplexity is Repetition: A One-Dimensional Self-Conditioning Attractor in Continuous Diffusion LMs

Shuai Zhang, Zijie Chen, Hongliang He, Lun Du, Zhenzhong Lan  ·  Zhejiang Univ. · Westlake Univ. · Ant Group

diffusion-language-models self-conditioning repetition-collapse mechanistic-interpretability evaluation-metric-failure generative-perplexity

ELF's record-low Gen-PPL rewards the very repetition it should penalize. The failure traces to a single one-dimensional attractor in the self-conditioning feedback loop — and a label-free fix, ACE, subtracts one direction vector to recover human-clean text.

Key metric · NFE to human-clean
1040→144
Network Function Evaluations on ELF-B to clear the BBC/XSum 95th-percentile REP-4 bar (1.92%). Paper headline: 1.5–5× compute-to-clean across model sizes.
Why this matters

Gen-PPL — the headline diffusion-LM metric — silently rewards repetition. The paper pins the failure to a single interpretable axis in the Jacobian of self-conditioning feedback, and ships a label-free, training-free fix whose one direction vector transfers across scales (cos 0.85–0.88 from 105M to 652M) and across inference knobs.

Critical edge · caveat
Cross-scale transfer is empirical, not derived.

The cos 0.85–0.88 transfer from 105M → 652M is inductive across three scales; behaviour at 1B+ is unknown. Clean-PPL rises 2–3 points after steering, self-BLEU climbs, and the masked/discrete diffusion family (MDLM, SEDD) is untested — the 1.5–5× headline is tied to a BBC/XSum-calibrated bar that can shift the numbers.

Methodology · formal model & ACE recipe

Setting

Continuous-embedding flow-matching LMs (ELF series, 105M / 342M / 652M) with ODE (Euler) and SDE (Euler + partial noise re-injection, churn γ) samplers. Each step feeds the model's previous clean estimate x̂k back into the next — self-conditioning (Chen et al. 2022), which ELF reuses as the classifier-free guidance channel at scale w.

Formal model (App. A)

Pool feedback over positions, uk = (1/L) Σℓ x̂k[ℓ] ∈ ℝe. Split one step: uk+1 = s(uk) + fk. Linearize s about fixed point u*: s(u) = u* + J(u − u*).

  • Lemma 1: residual obeys ak+1 = J ak + fk.
  • Lemma 2: Δuk = βk v1 + rk + fk — separates a repetition mode βkv1, off-axis remainder, and (near-orthogonal) drive.
  • Lemma 3: driven fixed point u∞ = u* + (I−J)−1f, on-axis distance |f(1)|/ρ with ρ = 1 − µ1.
  • Prop. 1: under separability, the difference of means between trapped and free trajectories recovers the axis, d ∥ v1.
  • Prop. 2: steered recursion yields a usable dose window [λ★, λmax] (Eq. 16).

ACE direction (Alg. 1)

Run full-SC N times, record mean self-conditioning feedback sn = (1/TL) Σk,ℓ x̂k(n)[ℓ] and final REP4. Split into top- and bottom-repetition tertiles T, F; d ← (meanT − meanF) / ‖·‖. At each denoising step: x̂k ← x̂k − λ d 1⊤L. Power-iteration of J on n=100 trajectories (Alg. 2) at trajectory fraction ≈0.85 yields µ1 ≈ 0.15; |cos(v1, d)| rises to 0.55 as the basin forms.

Evaluation protocol

REP4 (median 4-gram self-repetition, Eq. 3) · human-clean bar 1.92% (95th-pct of BBC/XSum) · accept rate · clean-PPL (GPT-2 Large on accepted length-matched subset) · CoLA grammaticality, dist-1/dist-3, self-BLEU.4 · compute-to-clean: expected NFE × 10³ in a reject-to-N loop.

Causal ablation (Tab. 2, ELF-B)

Sweeping feedback attenuation α from 0 to 1 raises repetition (0% → 6.83%) while lowering Gen-PPL (97.5 → 19.5) and collapsing accept (1.00 → 0.07). The metric literally calls the model >4× "better" as it grows most repetitive.

The defect is one-dimensional (Fig. 2)

  • Spectral gap µ1/µ2 rises from ~1 → 2.7 as the basin forms.
  • On-axis feedback coordinate predicts final repetition (r = 0.65, Fig. 2a).
  • |cos(v1, d)| climbs to 0.55; perturbation along d is amplified 2.2× over random.

Strengths

  • Crisp mechanistic story pinning the failure on a single direction, not high-dimensional instability.
  • Training-free, label-free, estimator-once fix — minimal deployment cost; training-time anti-attractor regularizer cuts ELF-B repetition 6.8% → 3.3%.
  • Headline metric (Gen-PPL) is shown to be fooled; compute-to-clean + clean-PPL supplied with a calibrated human floor.
  • Concrete wins: ELF-B accept 0.07 → 0.45; compute-to-clean 1.5–5× cheaper; one direction services all model sizes and inference knobs.
  • Strong ablations: alternative direction estimators (LDA, logistic, top-PC, Jacobian v1) all worse; dose-window validated.
  • Cross-architecture evidence (LangFlow, Plaid) supports the paradigm-level claim rather than a single-checkpoint story.

Limitations

  • Single-direction hypothesis is empirically useful but idealizing — measured J is only approximately symmetric; subdominant modes may matter on adversarial or out-of-domain prompts.
  • Cross-scale transfer (cos 0.85–0.88) is empirical, not derived; behaviour at 1B+ unknown.
  • Clean-PPL rises 2–3 points after steering; self-BLEU climbs with λ — no human preference study.
  • Discrete / masked diffusion LMs (MDLM, SEDD, MDCA) untested — their self-conditioning operates on token logits, not continuous embeddings.
  • Compute-to-clean still depends on the calibrated bar; small bar shifts change the 1.5–5× figure.
  • Unconditional generation only — conditional / controlled generation beyond SC-CFG not explored.

Questions for further exploration

  1. What does the rest of the spectrum look like under domain shift (code, multilingual, long-form) — are there sub-dominant modes that matter when ACE is applied?
  2. Does ACE compose cleanly with other inference-time interventions — guidance beyond the SC-CFG channel, length normalization, nucleus / top-k decoding tricks?
  3. How does the same diagnosis carry over to discrete diffusion LMs (MDLM, SEDD) where self-conditioning operates on token logits rather than continuous embeddings?
  4. Can the same Jacobian / difference-of-means recipe surface other one-dimensional failure modes — mode collapse, sentiment drift, repetition of specific entities — in self-conditioned generative models?
  5. Is there a clean human-preference study that confirms "compute-to-clean" tracks reader-perceived quality as well as it tracks the operational repetition floor?
  6. Does the steering direction d drift under continued fine-tuning, RLHF, or domain adaptation — and if so, how often must it be re-estimated?
  7. Could the separability assumption of Proposition 1 be relaxed enough to close the |cos(v1, d)| ≈ 0.55 gap and yield a better steering direction than the current empirical d?
