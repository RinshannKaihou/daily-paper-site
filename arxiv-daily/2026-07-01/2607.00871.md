> **一句话总结：** SEA（Self-Evolving Agents with Anytime-Valid Certificates）是一个四层参考架构（L₀ 冻结基模型 / L₁ 小转向适配器 / L₂ 可版本化 harness / L₃ 循环控制器），把自演化智能体的每一步修改都必须通过一个 any-time-valid 统计门、产生针对固定错误预算的可审计证书；五个控制器组合已发表的学习理论保证（performative stability、anytime-valid inference、dynamic-regret 在线学习、两时间尺度随机逼近），五个验证器在环机制（best-of-N、verified micro-step search、自写复现 oracle、搜索层控制、自修复）从 issue 文本提供无需人工打分器的稠密信号；在 SWE-bench Verified 的 52 题种子样本上，基模型能力是主导因素（G_EMMA 18 → 22, Q_WEN 24 → 25, G_PT-MINI 25 → 29, G_PT 28 → 34, G_LM 5.2 24 → 28），no-op-composite 对照隔离出 SEA 套件在 G_PT 上为 +5、在 G_LM 5.2 上为 +4 的去混淆贡献，G_PT 的 34/52（65%）是单次运行的非多 seed 实验。

> **TL;DR:** SEA is a four-layer reference architecture (frozen base L₀ + steering adapter L₁ + versioned harness L₂ + loop controllers L₃) in which every self-modification must pass an anytime-valid statistical gate that emits an auditable certificate against a fixed error budget. Five loop controllers compose *published* guarantees—performative stability (Perdomo et al., 2020), anytime-valid inference (Ramdas et al. 2023 / Howard et al. 2021), dynamic-regret online learning (Cutkosky 2020 / Baby & Wang 2022), and two-timescale stochastic approximation (Borkar 2008)—into the controllers P_PB-CL (forgetting), P_NMP-A (anchored preference), P_A-COCOA (counterfactual credit), S_GM-CS (Statistical Gödel Machine), and S_DC-QD (library growth). Five verifier-in-the-loop mechanisms—best-of-N, verified micro-step search, self-authored reproduction oracles (admitted only if they fail on the unpatched base), search-layer control, and verified self-repair of the harness—supply grader-free dense signal from the issue text alone. On a seeded 52-instance SWE-bench Verified subset (24 Django / 27 Matplotlib / 1 Flask) across four base models, base capability is the dominant confound-free effect; a deliberate no-op-composite control on two strong bases isolates the suite's contribution at +5 (G_PT: 29 → 34, the model's 65% best) and +4 (G_LM 5.2: 24 → 28). Event logs confirm mechanisms fire (run_tests calls rise ~50%, mean episode length +1.3 steps) and prevent regressions (4 p2p-flagged with full suite vs 3 with control). Single-algorithm ablation on G_PT localizes the lift to A_LG 2 (preference learner, +5) + A_LG 7 (micro-step search, +3) + A_LG 8 (self-oracles, +3); A_LG 6 (best-of-2) is net-negative (-3 vs control, removed) and A_LG 4's 36 is *not* an algorithm effect—its gate accepted zero edits. Results are single-run on expensive evaluations; the endogenous-loop compositions remain open conjectures.

## 核心贡献

- **重新定义"自演化智能体安全"为序列检验问题。** 自演化智能体破坏了所有学习理论保证的潜在假设（数据、评估器、组件、假设空间都由学习者本身产生，作者称之为 *endogenous-loop failure mode*），SEA 不创造新理论，而是把每个经典种子结果都用恰好足够的机制包裹起来，使它"在闭环内仍可能成立"——即 performative stability、anytime-valid inference、dynamic-regret、两时间尺度随机逼近。
- **四层参考架构 + 十算法编目。** 部署策略 $\pi_t = L_0^{(t)} \circ L_1^{(t)} \circ L_2^{(t)}$ 由冻结基模型 $L_0$（永不微调）、小转向适配器 $L_1$（仅在线调整其 softmax 指令分布）、可变且可版本化的 harness $L_2$（prompt · tools · budgets · library）、以及 $L_3$ 循环控制器（五个算法的复合两时间尺度调度器）组成；论文把所有内容具体为十个有伪代码的算法（表 1），其中 A_LG 1–5 是控制器、A_LG 6–10 是验证器在环与执行者侧机制。
- **四个对应内生失效模式的控制器。** 每个控制器对应一种 endogenous loop 的失效模式：稳定性-塑性（A_LG 1 P_PB-CL）、自指性塌缩（A_LG 2 P_NMP-A，长程 self-rewarding drift）、信用分配（A_LG 3 P_A-COCOA，performative MDP 中的多组件贡献）、可验证的自修改（A_LG 4 S_GM-CS，承继 Statistical Gödel Machine 的 e-value 门）以及假设空间扩展（A_LG 5 S_DC-QD，用 MDL + Quality-Diversity 的库增长）。
- **自写复现 oracle 作为唯一的 in-loop 信号源。** 关键而新的一点：A_LG 8 让模型仅从 issue 文本自己写一个复现/校验 oracle 套件，唯一准入规则是 *oracle 在未打补丁的基模型上必须失败*；一旦 oracle 被通过门控准入，整个搜索都跑得"无评分器"，held-out 测试保留为终端测量，不参与搜索的引导。
- **Harness 的经验性自修复（A_LG 10）。** 一组 harness 修复原语，由循环按 *在真实环境中的实测修复率*（而非人类判断）选择，与 A_LG 4 同样的"提出-门控"纪律。
- **可复用的随时有效统计核心（§6）。** 包括 normal-mixture 置信序列、Hoeffding e-process（可预测 plug-in 投注）、无 horizon 归一化的 CTHS（confirm-triggered harmonic spending）、时间均匀 PAC-Bayes 惩罚、无参 coin-betting oracle（drift 触发重启）、精确一维 Wasserstein、wild-bootstrap trend test、MAP-Elites archive、Stitch 风格的 MDL 压缩（反合一 + sound dominance 剪枝）。
- **严格的去混淆实验设计：no-op composite 控制。** 在 G_PT 上把"完整套件 scaffolding + 所有算法关闭"作为控制，与完整套件对比，得到 +5 的去混淆贡献；G_LM 5.2 上得到 +4；事件日志进一步证实机制确实被触发（A_LG 2 记录到 52 次策略更新，A_LG 4 记录到 0 次接受编辑）。
- **严肃的认知姿态。** 论文明确声明"不推导新界、不证明新速率"；每个经典保证的具体出处都写在引用点；哪些组合"在内生设定下仍成立"被显式标为 *open conjecture*。

## Key Contributions

- **Reframes self-evolving-agent safety as a sequential-testing problem.** Self-evolving agents break the i.i.d. data / evaluator / hypothesis assumption behind most learning-theoretic guarantees (continual-learning forgetting, preference optimization, policy-gradient unbiasedness, safe policy improvement, library-learning optimality). SEA recovers auditable error control by *restricting where self-modification can occur* (steering adapter + harness around a frozen base) and *gating every change* through an anytime-valid e-process.
- **Four-layer reference architecture with a 10-algorithm inventory (Table 1).** Deployed policy $\pi_t = L_0^{(t)} \circ L_1^{(t)} \circ L_2^{(t)}$: frozen $L_0$, steered $L_1$ (softmax over strategy *directives*, never weight-fine-tuned in reported runs), versioned $L_2$ (prompt · tools · budgets · library), and $L_3$ loop controllers (two-timescale composite scheduler). All ten algorithms (Alg 1–5 controllers + Alg 6–10 verifier-in-the-loop / actor-side mechanisms) come with pseudocode in Appendix A.
- **Five loop controllers tied one-to-one to failure modes of the endogenous loop.**
  - **Alg 1 P_PB-CL** — Performative PAC-Bayes Continual Learning. Combines PAC-Bayes posterior over the steering adapter, a Donsker–Varadhan backward-transfer forgetting bound, OGD-style direction buffer, and a performative trust region; gates updates through $\epsilon L < 1$ contraction condition from Perdomo et al. 2020.
  - **Alg 2 P_NMP-A** — Performative Nash-MP with Real-Data Anchoring. Two-timescale split (slow reward model, fast policy); extragradient (mirror-prox) on a Bradley–Terry score vector with anchor pull; Hoeffding e-process drift gate; absorbs the Fu et al. (2025) self-consuming shift bound and the Tiapkin et al. (2025) last-iterate self-play rate.
  - **Alg 3 P_A-COCOA** — Performative-Aware COCOA. Augments the COCOA encoder with the deploying policy, replaces REINFORCE-style importance ratios by a counterfactual per-action contribution (closed form for the softmax steering adapter), and hands the gradient to per-coordinate Krichevsky–Trofimov coin betting with drift-triggered restarts; targets the strongly-adaptive dynamic-regret rates of Cutkosky (2020) and Baby & Wang (2022).
  - **Alg 4 S_GM-CS** — Statistical Gödel Machine gated by anytime-valid confidence sequences. Direct descendant of Wu et al. (2025) SGM; normal-mixture CS + ε-W₁ sensitivity inflation + confirm-triggered harmonic spending; every harness edit is committed only if it clears a familywise-error-budgeted e-process.
  - **Alg 5 S_DC-QD** — Skill Discovery + Compression with Quality-Diversity. Grows the abstraction library by MDL-compressed (Stitch-style antiunification with sound dominance pruning) plus MAP-Elites archive.
- **Five verifier-in-the-loop mechanisms supplying grader-free dense signal.**
  - **Alg 6** verifier-gated best-of-$N$ + refinement (removed in reported GPT runs because it never produced a second attempt and added patch-apply failures).
  - **Alg 7** verified micro-step search (fast loop): a graded verifier drives a hill-climb via process reward, then promoted to a verified search.
  - **Alg 8** self-authored reproduction oracles (the central insight): the agent writes its own grading checks from the issue alone, admitted only if they *fail on the unpatched base*; once admitted, the search runs **grader-free**, with the held-out grader reserved for terminal measurement only.
  - **Alg 9** search-layer controllers: re-aims four of the five controllers onto the search layer; validated by offline gate simulations.
  - **Alg 10** verified self-repair of the harness: repertoire of repair primitives selected by measured fix-rate on the real environment, not by human judgment.
- **Reusable anytime-valid statistical core (§6).** Normal-mixture confidence sequences, Hoeffding e-processes with predictable plug-in betting, a horizon-free normalized confirm-triggered harmonic spending schedule, time-uniform PAC-Bayes penalties, parameter-free coin-betting oracles with drift-triggered restarts, exact 1-D Wasserstein, wild-bootstrap trend tests, MAP-Elites archives, Stitch-style MDL compression. These are described as published and re-used, not new.
- **Confound-free empirical isolation via a no-op-composite control.** On GPT (the strongest base), the *single-pass baseline* scores 28; the *no-op-composite control* (full scaffolding, all algorithms off) scores 29; the *full suite* scores 34 → a deconfounded +5 attributable to the algorithms, not the scaffolding. The same picture on GLM 5.2 (+4). A per-algorithm ablation on GPT (Table 4) localizes the lift to **Alg 2 (+5), Alg 7 (+3), Alg 8 (+3), Alg 3 (+2)**; Alg 6 is net-negative and was removed; Alg 4's apparent 36 is an artifact (its gate accepted 0 edits).
- **Honest epistemic stance.** Each algorithmic combination is explicitly marked as an *open conjecture* in the endogenous setting; the paper does not derive new bounds or rates.

## Methodology

- **Threat model — the endogenous-loop failure mode.** Self-evolving agents generate the data they train on, the evaluator they are judged by, the components they are built from, and the hypothesis space they search. This breaks the hypotheses of (a) continual-learning forgetting bounds (Farajtabar et al. 2020, Chugg et al. 2023), (b) preference-optimization convergence (Tiapkin et al. 2025, Wang et al. 2025), (c) policy-gradient unbiasedness (Meulemans et al. 2023), (d) safe policy improvement (Thomas et al. 2015), and (e) library-learning optimality (Bowers et al. 2023). The paper adopts *performativity* (Perdomo et al. 2020) as the lens: $W_1(D(\pi), D(\pi')) \le \epsilon \|\pi - \pi'\|$, with contraction regime $\epsilon L < 1$ for $\beta$-smooth, $\gamma$-strongly-convex loss.
- **Architectural confinement.** Self-modification is restricted to a small steering adapter and a versioned harness wrapping a *frozen* base. The steered policy is the L1 softmax over discrete *directives* ("explore broadly", "localize and edit early", "smallest diff", …); its REINFORCE gradient has closed form $\hat g = \sum_{a'} \hat w(a') \nabla_\theta p_\theta(a')$ and is well-defined from text-only API access (no model Jacobian needed).
- **Anytime-valid gating.** Every modification must clear an anytime-valid sequential test that emits a certificate valid at every stopping time. The shared machinery is normal-mixture confidence sequences, Hoeffding e-processes, time-uniform PAC-Bayes penalties, and CTHS harmonic spending. A "*no-op*" proposal or an exhausted error budget simply *holds* — the safe abstention output "NSF".
- **Five controllers (one per failure mode).**
  - **Alg 1 P_PB-CL** — keep PAC-Bayes posterior small (mean-parameterized diagonal Gaussian) with a frozen data-independent prior; deploy, form REINFORCE gradient with pooled replay-window baseline, project orthogonally off a FIFO buffer of past committed update directions, propose a single descent step. Three gates in sequence: (i) forgetting gate via Donsker–Varadhan upper bound on backward transfer with per-evaluation optimal $\lambda$; (ii) performative trust region $\epsilon$-capping the adapter step in $\ell_2$; (iii) commit gate under $\epsilon L < 1$.
  - **Alg 2 P_NMP-A** — separate reward model from policy onto two timescales (Borkar 2008); the slow side mixes a synthetic Bradley–Terry gradient with a pull toward a frozen anchor $q_{\text{real}}$; a Hoeffding e-process on per-pair deviations from the anchor gates any update (defaults to neutral-zero anchor when no real-preference set is available, as in the SWE runs).
  - **Alg 3 P_A-COCOA** — recency-weighted per-directive mean of episode (process) reward; counterfactual per-action contribution in closed form for the softmax adapter; parameter-free KT coin betting per coordinate with drift-triggered restarts on a wild-bootstrap trend test on the mean-reward stream.
  - **Alg 4 S_GM-CS** — Statistical Gödel Machine (Wu et al. 2025) gated by normal-mixture confidence sequences plus $\epsilon$-$W_1$ sensitivity inflation, with CTHS harmonic spending. *In the reported SWE-bench runs its gate accepted 0 edits — the algorithm was effectively disabled for cost.*
  - **Alg 5 S_DC-QD** — library growth with MDL (Stitch-style antiunification + sound dominance pruning) and MAP-Elites archive.
- **Five verifier-in-the-loop mechanisms.**
  - **Alg 6** verifier-gated best-of-$N$ + refinement hill-climb.
  - **Alg 7** verified micro-step search (process-reward driven hill-climb of finer-grained steps).
  - **Alg 8** self-authored reproduction oracles: agent writes its own oracle suite from the issue; single admission rule — *an oracle must fail on the unpatched base*; admitted at two samples in reported runs (Alg 7 + Alg 8 run "grader-free" once admitted; held-out tests reserved for terminal grading).
  - **Alg 9** search-layer re-aiming of Alg 1/Alg 3/Alg 4/Alg 5 onto the search layer of a search-then-distill two-loop design (Zelikman et al. 2022, Gulcehre et al. 2023).
  - **Alg 10** verified self-repair of the harness: primitives chosen by measured fix-rate on the real environment, not by human judgment.
- **Two-tier, ten-algorithm design.** Alg 1–5 are the conceptual contribution; Alg 6–10 are "the engine that feeds them" — gates can only select among existing behaviors, so behavior generation itself carries its own numbering.
- **Evaluation protocol.** SWE-bench Verified (500-instance human-validated subset, official execution-based grader; *resolved* iff every fail-to-pass passes and every pass-to-pass still passes). Seeded random draw of **52 instances (24 Django, 27 Matplotlib, 1 Flask)**. Four base models accessed only as $L_0$, none fine-tuned: GEMMA (gemma4:31b-mlx, ~31B nvfp4 quantized), QWEN (qwen3.6:27b, ~27B), GPT-MINI (gpt-5.4-mini, low reasoning), GPT (gpt-5.5, reasoning disabled, strongest in set, 4k token cap), plus GLM 5.2 in the no-op-comparison-only cell. 4 × 2 design (four models × algorithms off/on), eight cells, one grading protocol, one scaffold. Algorithms-A enables eight of ten algorithms (Alg 4 disabled for cost, Alg 6 removed after the GPT ablation). Single-pass baseline = bare multi-step actor, one episode per instance, 10-step budget. Event logs record every decision for variance-immune attribution.
- **Reporting hygiene.** The author explicitly reports the algorithm-side conflations (Alg 4's spurious 36, Alg 6's net-negative 26 caught by event log), instead of hiding them.

## Results & Highlights

- **Base capability is the dominant, confound-free effect.** Single-pass baselines scale cleanly with model strength: GEMMA 18 < QWEN 24 < GPT-MINI 25 < GPT 28 (Table 3). The ordering is preserved with the full stack (22, 25, 29, 34).
- **Full stack improves every base (on−off +1 to +6).** GEMMA 18→22 (+4), QWEN 24→25 (+1), GPT-MINI 25→29 (+4), GPT 28→34 (+6), GLM 5.2 24→28 (+4).
- **Deconfounded contribution of the suite, via the no-op-composite control.**
  - **GPT**: control 29 → full suite 34 (**+5**, attributable to the algorithms, not the scaffolding). 34/52 is the model's *65% best*.
  - **GLM 5.2**: 24 → 28 (**+4**, ALG vs control, since GLM's single-pass baseline was not run).
- **Single-algorithm ablation on GPT (Table 4).** Re-anchored to the 29 control:
  - **Alg 2 P_NMP-A: 34 (+5)** — the strategy-directive learner carries most of the single-component lift.
  - **Alg 7 micro-step search: 32 (+3)**; **Alg 8 self-oracles: 32 (+3)**; **Alg 3 P_A-COCOA: 31 (+2)**; **Alg 1 P_PB-CL: 30 (+1)**.
  - **Alg 6 best-of-2: 26 (net-negative)** — *removed from the live stack* because event logs showed it never produced a second attempt and only added patch-apply failures.
  - **Alg 4 S_GM-CS: 36 (artifact)** — its confidence-sequence gate accepted zero edits; per the event-log attribution, the cell actually ran the control configuration. Honest call-out rather than score-report.
  - **Full suite: 34 (+5)** equals the best single component (Alg 2) — gains are overlapping rather than additive.
- **What the mechanisms verifiably do (event-log evidence, variance-immune).**
  - **run_tests calls rise ~50%** with the full suite vs the control; **mean episode length +1.3 steps** — the agent iterates against its self-authored tests rather than guessing once.
  - **Alg 2 and Alg 3 fire every round, recording 52 policy updates each**; Alg 4/Alg 5/Alg 1 contribute less in this regime (Alg 4's gate accepts ~0 edits).
  - **Regressions are limited and slightly fewer:** 4 p2p-flagged instances with the full suite vs 3 for the control — the safety mechanisms do not create regressions.
  - **17/52 instances solved by every config**, **10/52 by none**. Consensus-flip analysis separates signal from noise: only a robust subset flips under several distinct configs; single-config flips are noise-like.
- **Bottom line.** Two levers move the metric, and they are not interchangeable: within a fixed base, the lever is the algorithm suite (carried by Alg 2 + Alg 7 + Alg 8); for absolute resolution the lever is a stronger base — the 10 unsolved instances (mostly Matplotlib) are a capability/harness wall that more search does not move.
- **Caveats the authors state upfront.** Single-run evaluations; run-to-run variance not measured; slow-loop distillation designed but not trained; endogenous-loop compositions remain open conjectures.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: high in framing and synthesis, modest in component novelty.** The reframing (restriction + anytime-valid gating + verifier-in-the-loop) is the substantive contribution. Component-wise, anytime-valid testing / best-of-N / micro-step search / "frozen base + adapter" / Stein-style online learning are all individually established; even the *self-authored reproduction oracles* mechanism has prior analogues. The novelty is in the **composition, the no-op-composite deconfounding, and the catalog** (10 algorithms + their failure-mode mapping) — not in any single piece.
- **Rigor (statistical): the cited primitives are mathematically legitimate; the endogenous-loop compositions are honestly tagged as open conjectures.** E-processes and e-values from Ramdas et al. 2023 are time-uniform; PAC-Bayes time-uniform forgetting bounds from Chugg et al. 2023 are valid for the adapted posterior sequences a self-evolving agent produces; the Perdomo et al. 2020 contraction regime is well-defined *if* $W_1$ sensitivity and $\beta/\gamma$ hold. Whether these survive the endogenous loop is precisely what is conjectured — the author's epistemic stance is appropriate.
- **Rigor (empirical): thin by conventional standards.** Headline numbers are *single-run* on 52 instances; no confidence intervals, no per-instance variance estimates, no multiple seeds for any cell. The +5/+4 attribution rests on a single comparison per cell across two models. Event logs substitute for run-to-run variance as variance-immune evidence — useful for *mechanism attribution*, but not for the headline lift magnitudes.
- **Reproducibility: weak-moderate as written.** No code release noted in this paper; Algorithm 1–10 are numbered and pseudocode is in Appendix A, but the recipes for adapter initialization, directive set, oracle admission threshold, CTHS schedule parameters, and the exact no-op-composite construction are not given here in enough detail to rebuild the suite without consulting the code. Self-authored oracles (Alg 8) in particular depend on prompt templates that would need to be released.
- **Significance: a real and timely contribution.** Most "self-evolving agent" papers provide no statistical control over the modification loop. SEA turns the modification loop into something with an auditable *error budget*, which is genuinely useful for any team running long-lived autonomous agents. The work also legitimately bridges two communities — the agentic-systems community and the anytime-valid / e-process community — that rarely cite each other.
- **Red flags to watch.**
  - **Single-run** results on a *seeded random* 52-instance subset of SWE-bench Verified (24 Django + 27 Matplotlib + 1 Flask is not a balanced sample — Matplotlib is over-represented relative to the full benchmark). +5 / +4 are best interpreted as point estimates.
  - The **no-op-composite control** isolates the algorithm from the scaffolding, but *only on two bases* (GPT and GLM 5.2). The +1 to +6 deltas on the other three bases (GEMMA / QWEN / GPT-MINI) still include a small scaffolding/directive effect that the paper admits is *not* deconfounded.
  - **Any-time-validity is only as good as the verifiers feeding it.** If best-of-$N$, micro-step search, and self-authored oracles are correlated (e.g., they all favor similar patch features), the certificate can drift in calibration even if individual e-process terms are sound.
  - **The two-timescale coupling** in Alg 2 requires $a^{\text{slow}}_t / a^{\text{fast}}_t \to 0$; the report does not validate this separation empirically beyond observing the anchor holds.
  - **Alg 6 (best-of-2) is net-negative** (26 vs 29 control) because of patch-apply failures — a small but real cautionary note about composition costs.
  - The **10 unsolved Matplotlib instances** are explicitly a "capability/harness wall that more search does not move" — underlining that the suite *cannot manufacture capability*.
  - The **slow-loop distillation step** is designed but never trained. The +5/+4 lift is the *immediate* lift; the claim that verified traces can be distilled into weights is unverified.
  - **Performative sensitivity $\epsilon$** is taken as a hyperparameter; the paper states it is *not* estimable online with its own validity guarantee. The contraction condition $\epsilon L < 1$ "may fail at LLM scale."

## Strengths

- **Clean conceptual decomposition** into architecture layers and failure modes — the failure-mode list (stability-plasticity, self-referential collapse, credit assignment, verifiable self-modification, hypothesis-space expansion) is a useful taxonomy beyond this paper.
- **Statistical scaffolding for online modification.** E-processes, confidence sequences, PAC-Bayes penalties, CTHS harmonic spending — all from the published literature — instead of opaque learned scores. This is exactly the angle the user cluster on *ML Reliability foundations* and *Computation Reliability & Precision Errors* should value.
- **No-op-composite control** is unusually thoughtful experimental design for a single-author paper; the +5 attribution on GPT (and +4 on GLM 5.2) is genuinely confound-free, distinguishing algorithms from scaffolding.
- **Variance-immune mechanism attribution** via event logs — run_tests calls, oracle admissions, veto / refine firings, p2p regressions — gives a layer of evidence that single-run numbers cannot.
- **Honest reporting.** Alg 4's 36 surface as an artifact rather than a result; Alg 6's negative contribution is reported and used to *remove* the algorithm from the live stack; every algorithmic combination is tagged as an open conjecture rather than a theorem.
- **Domain fit.** This is squarely within *Self-Evolving Agents & Automated Research* and overlaps *ML Reliability foundations* and *Computation Reliability & Precision Errors* (the error-budget framing).

## Limitations

- **Single-run, small, unbalanced sample.** 52 SWE-bench Verified instances (24 Django, 27 Matplotlib, 1 Flask) per cell; one run per cell; no seed-level variance on any of the 13 ablation cells in Table 4.
- **Open conjectures in the compositions.** The two-timescale coupling of Alg 2, the backward-transfer reduction in Alg 1, and the performative corrections in Alg 1 / Alg 4 are *not* proven under endogenous drift; the performative sensitivity $\epsilon$ is a hyperparameter (not online-estimable with its own validity guarantee), and $\epsilon L < 1$ may fail at LLM scale.
- **Scaffolding effect not fully deconfounded on three of five bases.** The on−off +1 / +4 / +6 on GEMMA, QWEN, GPT-MINI include a small directive/scaffolding contribution; only GPT and GLM 5.2 are matched against the no-op composite.
- **Self-oracle is fallible by design.** Can under-claim (over-strict oracle fails to flip a correct patch) or over-claim (false-green pass); patch is therefore reported as "promising", never "resolved", with the terminal grader authoritative.
- **Self-oracle admission rule is single.** "Oracle must fail on the unpatched base" is a clever but coarse guard; a more graded admission criterion would likely reduce both failure modes.
- **Alg 4's bound guarantees safety, not progress.** In the reported run the gate accepted zero edits, which the author treats as the algorithm correctly abstaining — but the broader question of whether S_GM-CS will start accepting edits at production scale is open.
- **PAC-Bayes terms grow vacuous for high-dimensional posteriors**, and the Donsker–Varadhan forgetting floor rises with $t$ — γουρ forgetting budget $\tau_{\text{forget}}$ must be set above this floor or the gate freezes the learner.
- **Generalization across base-model families is unknown.** Only five bases tested; the split between "base capability" and "SEA mechanism" on weaker / different-family / reasoning-active bases is not characterized.
- **Reproducibility surface is incomplete here.** Pseudocode for all 10 algorithms is promised (Appendix A) but the actual recipes for adapter initialization, directive set, oracle admission threshold, and CTHS schedule constants would need to live in code or appendix to enable from-scratch reproduction.

## Relevance

与用户研究方向高度契合。SEA 直接落入 *"Self-Evolving Agents & Automated Research"* 主线：它给"自我修改循环"加了一个可审计、固定错误预算的统计门，正是该方向目前最缺的一块拼图。同时其 anytime-valid / e-process / PAC-Bayes / performative-prediction 的工具栈也属于 *"ML Reliability foundations"* 的范畴，对关心 *Computation Reliability & Precision Errors* 的读者（任何长期运行的自动系统都需要一个可量化的失败概率上界）有直接借鉴价值。对关注 LLM 训练稳定性或推理评测的研究者而言，SEA 的"控制器 + 验证器 + any-time-valid gate"三段式架构提供了一种把"RLHF/long-running agent 的可靠性问题"重新表述为可处理模块的范式。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- With fixed-n is invalid by construction for self-evolving agents, how sensitive is the e-process calibration to *correlated* graders among best-of-$N$, micro-step search, and self-authored oracles? What guard-rail prevents the certificate from silently drifting?
- What is the *run-to-run variance* of the +5 (GPT) and +4 (GLM 5.2) lifts on the same 52-instance sample, and how wide are the resulting CTHS / PAC-Bayes confidence bands once 5–10 seeds per cell are added?
- The 10 unsolved Matplotlib instances are flagged as a capability wall. Does SEA combined with a *stronger* base (e.g., a larger reasoning model, or test-time scaling) close some of them, or are they all genuinely base-limited?
- The no-op-composite control is run only on GPT and GLM 5.2. Does its isolation effect generalize to weaker bases (GEMMA / QWEN / GPT-MINI), or is it specific to already-strong models?
- The performative sensitivity $\epsilon$ is taken as a hyperparameter. Can it be estimated online (e.g., via W₁ drift detection on the replay buffer) with its *own* anytime-valid certificate, making the contraction condition $\epsilon L < 1$ self-monitoring?
- The Alg 2 timescale ratio $a^{\text{slow}}_t / a^{\text{fast}}_t = (t+1)^{-0.3}$ is set so the model settles faster than the policy chases it. How robust is the drift to violations of this separation, e.g., under sudden task-distribution shifts?
- The slow-loop distillation step is designed but never trained. When trained, will the weights-internalized policy preserve the same deconfounded lift, or does the certificate decay under distillation?
- If self-authored oracles are noisy (over-claim rate non-negligible), what is the optimal multi-sample admission rule that trades off grader-free cost against oracle-quality?
- The paper gates with time-uniform PAC-Bayes penalties, which the authors note "may grow vacuous for high-dimensional posteriors." What concrete high-dimensional PAC-Bayes bounds (e.g., PAC-Bayes with heavy-tailed oracles) would close this gap on the steering adapter?
- The four-layer decomposition (frozen base + adapter + harness + controllers) is generic. Does the pattern apply outside SWE-bench-Verified-style code repair — e.g., to long-running web agents, to RLHF post-training pipelines, or to multi-agent systems where each agent is an SEA in turn?
- The 24 Django / 27 Matplotlib / 1 Flask split is unbalanced. Does the +1 / +4 / +6 split across bases hold on a *stratified* sample of SWE-bench Verified (e.g., 13 × 4 across Django / Matplotlib / Flask / other), or do some base-model × repository-family cells reverse?
