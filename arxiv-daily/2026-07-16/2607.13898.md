> **一句话总结：** 本文系统刻画了 Altera Agilex-5 FPGA 上 MXFP（MXFP8/6/4）点积的多种实现方式，指出其张量模式只能原生支持 MXFP4(E2M1) 与 MXFP6(E2M3) 两种格式，随后提出对 DSP 张量块的多轮架构改造，使所有 MXFP 精度均能原生支持；其推荐设计仅增加 36% 的 DSP tile 面积（对应整片 1.8% 面积），在 systolic array 矩阵乘法器上对全部支持的 MXFP 格式平均取得 4.2× 吞吐提升。

> **TL;DR:** This work characterizes MXFP (microscaling floating-point) dot-product implementations on Altera Agilex-5 FPGAs and shows that the DSP tensor mode only natively supports MXFP4 (E2M1) and MXFP6 (E2M3), forcing MXFP6 (E3M2) and MXFP8 to fall back to lower-density packed-multiplier or soft-logic strategies (e.g., only 2.1–6.5 TFLOPS for packed DSP on E5M2/E4M3/E3M2 vs. 12.4 TFLOPS for tensor mode). The authors propose iterative tensor-mode architectural enhancements validated on a simplified Agilex-5-like DSP core (ASAP7 7nm PDK + HAMMER/COFFE flow) that enable native MXFP support for all precisions at a 36% DSP-tile area cost (1.8% of die area), delivering an average 4.2× systolic-array throughput gain across all supported MXFP formats on Agilex-5 (up to 15.3× for E5M2).

## 核心贡献

- **系统刻画 MXFP 在 Agilex-5 上的实现空间：** 首次在 Altera Agilex-5 上系统比较四种 MXFP 点积实现方式（纯 soft logic 基线、packed 定点 DSP、fp16 向量模式、张量模式），覆盖全部五种子格式（E2M1、E2M3、E3M2、E4M3、E5M2），并明确指出张量模式因 8-bit 乘法 lane 限制只能支持 E2M1 与 E2M3。
- **DSP 张量块架构改造方案：** 提出多轮（共 4 个 iteration）张量模式 datapath 改造，通过引入窄位宽浮点乘法器（2×2/3×3/4×4）、barrel shifter 对齐、扩展 CSA 规约树，使 DSP 块原生支持所有 MXFP 精度。
- **精细的面积/覆盖率折中探索：** 在 ASAP7 7nm PDK + HAMMER 流程 + COFFE 全定制布线下给出每个 iteration 的面积比（1.00×–1.65×），推荐设计（iteration 4）将 baseline dot-10 改为 dot-8，使 DSP tile 面积仅增 36%、整片面积仅增 1.8%，且不改变引脚数、不降低频率。
- **systolic array 案例验证：** 在整片 Agilex-5 上对全部 MXFP 格式实现 systolic 矩阵乘，证明改造后的 DSP 块对原本无法使用张量模式的 E5M2/E4M3/E3M2 取得 9.1×–15.3× 的吞吐提升，对所有支持格式平均 4.2×。
- **完全开源：** 所有 MXFP 点积、DSP 块设计与 systolic 基准均开源（https://github.com/boutros-lab/fpga-mxfp）。

## Key Contributions

- A comprehensive characterization of MXFP dot-product units mapped to the Altera Agilex-5 fabric across four implementation styles (optimized soft-logic baseline, packed fixed-point DSP multipliers, native fp16 vector mode, and the high-density tensor mode), covering all five MXFP sub-formats (E2M1, E2M3, E3M2, E4M3, E5M2), and the identification of tensor mode's coverage gap (only E2M1/E2M3).
- Iterative architectural modifications to the Agilex-5 DSP tensor mode datapath — adding narrow floating-point significand multipliers, exponent-driven barrel-shift alignment, and an expanded CSA reduction tree — that enable native MXFP8/MXFP6 support while keeping the I/O pin count and operating frequency unchanged.
- A design-space exploration (four iterations) quantifying area/coverage/density tradeoffs using a simplified Agilex-5-like DSP core implemented in ASAP7 7nm with the HAMMER flow and COFFE-optimized full-custom routing, yielding a recommended design point at +36% DSP-tile area (+1.8% die area).
- A systolic-array case study for matrix multiplication showing the modified DSP block delivers an average 4.2× throughput gain across all supported MXFP formats, and up to 9.1–15.3× for the formats that previously could not use tensor mode (E5M2/E4M3/E3M2).
- Open-sourcing of all RTL, modified DSP block designs, and systolic benchmarks to support reproducibility.

## Methodology

The paper combines an architectural characterization study with a circuit-level design exploration and a device-level case study.

**Characterization study (Section III).** The authors take the open-source MXFP dot-product Verilog from Samson et al. (FPL 2024) as the pure soft-logic baseline, add FloPoCo-based fp32 output conversion, then deeply pipeline it to push Agilex-5 frequency from 47–98 MHz up to 516–713 MHz for a fair comparison. They then map the same k=32 dot products onto three DSP-based approaches: (1) packed fixed-point multipliers (packing multiple narrow significand multiplications into the two independent 18×18 DSP arrays using operand spacing); (2) native fp16 vector mode (zero-extending MXFP mantissas to fp16 and chaining DSP blocks with the Langhammer–Pasca vector-chaining tree); and (3) the floating-point tensor mode (converting MXFP inputs to signed fixed-point so they fit the 8-bit dot lanes, only feasible for E2M1/E2M3). Performance is reported as an optimistic peak (TFLOPS obtained by filling the A5EC065BB32AE4S device until ALMs or DSPs are exhausted, assuming no frequency degradation). All mapping is done with Quartus Prime Pro 25.3.

**DSP block architecture modifications (Section IV).** The baseline for area estimation is a simplified Agilex-5 tensor-mode DSP core (two dot-10 units, ping-pong reuse banks, fixed-point→fp32 conversion, fp32 accumulator, shared-exponent logic) — not a full-fidelity model of all commercial modes, which means reported area overheads are conservative overestimates. The core is implemented in ASAP7 7nm standard cells (4×-upsized to bypass the 20nm academic license floor, then scaled back) using HAMMER, with Cadence Genus 21.17 for synthesis and Innovus 21.17 for PnR at a 2 ns target clock. Full-custom programmable routing (switch block, connection block, local crossbar, dedicated chain buffers) is sized with COFFE assuming a 25% populated crossbar, 104 inputs, 64 outputs, 64 chains. Two design constraints guide every iteration: (i) do not increase I/O pin count, and (ii) do not lower the 458 MHz tensor-mode operating frequency. Four iterations are explored — fixed-point input extension, native narrow-FP MXFP inputs (all formats), three MXFP8 tradeoff sub-variants (drop MXFP8 / support only E4M3 / support E4M3+dot-4 E5M2), and a final dot-8-baseline optimization that recovers area by shrinking two multipliers per dot unit to 4×4 while keeping dot-12 MXFP6 and dot-16 MXFP4 capacity.

**Systolic-array case study (Section V).** An N×k by k×(N·D) matrix multiplier systolic array (k=32) is swept over N until the device is exhausted or unroutable. For the baseline DSP, the best characterization strategy per format is used (tensor mode for E2M1/E2M3, packed fixed-point DSP otherwise); for the modified DSP, the enhanced tensor mode is used throughout. Functional correctness is verified by RTL simulation of the modified block, and Quartus placement/routing of the full array uses the unmodified Agilex-5 DSP block as a pin-compatible stand-in (legitimate because pin count and max frequency are unchanged).

## Results & Highlights

- **Recommended DSP design cost:** The preferred design (iteration 4) increases DSP tile area by **36%**, which corresponds to only **1.8% of the total FPGA die area** (DSP tiles occupy ~5% of the die on DSP-rich devices). All design variants operate at or above the baseline **458 MHz** tensor-mode frequency with identical latency.
- **Peak device TFLOPS, baseline vs. proposed DSP (Table V):** E5M2 goes from **2.1 → 12.4 TFLOPS**, E4M3 from **3.4 → 12.4 TFLOPS**, E3M2 from **6.5 → 16.5 TFLOPS**, E2M3 from **12.4 → 16.5 TFLOPS**, and E2M1 from **12.4 → 24.8 TFLOPS**, while cutting DSP-block count for a k=32 dot from 16 to 2–4.
- **Systolic-array case-study gains at the largest device-fitting N (Fig. 6):** E5M2 **+15.3×**, E4M3 **+10.5×**, E3M2 **+9.1×** (these previously had to fall back to packed fixed-point multipliers and now use the tensor mode), and E2M3/E2M1 **+1.3×/+2.4×** (from longer per-block dot length: dot-12 / dot-16 vs. dot-10). Average across all supported MXFP formats: **4.2× throughput improvement**.
- **Characterization findings (Table I, Fig. 3):** The optimized soft-logic baseline achieves **1.7–11.5 TFLOPS**; the packed fixed-point DSP approach delivers **2.1/3.4/6.5 TFLOPS** for E5M2/E4M3/E3M2 (a 28–45% gain over optimized soft logic at 16–26% fewer ALMs/dot); the fp16 vector mode is format-agnostic at a flat **1.5 TFLOPS** with minimal soft logic (224–660 ALMs/dot); the baseline tensor mode tops out at **12.4 TFLOPS** but only for E2M1/E2M3.
- **Largest systolic arrays** use **>90% of device DSP blocks**, run at **~400 MHz**, and consume only **22–32% of soft logic**, leaving substantial fabric for control/memory/data-marshalling logic — directly demonstrating the design premise that freeing soft logic via denser DSP is the win.

## Authors & Credibility

The work is a collaboration between the **University of Waterloo** (Mekhemer, Elsousy, Venkatesh, Kapre, Boutros) and the **University of Toronto** (Rowley, Betz). Nachiket Kapre and Vaughn Betz are well-established FPGA architecture researchers, and Andrew Boutros has a strong prior track record on FPGA DSP-block enhancements for low-precision DL (the cited FPL 2018 "Embracing Diversity" enhanced-DSP work, and the 2025 Proceedings of the IEEE FPGA-for-DL survey). The methodology is appropriately rigorous: ASAP7 7nm PDK with the HAMMER ASIC flow, COFFE for full-custom routing optimization, FloPoCo for arithmetic IP, and the same RTL pushed through both ASIC area estimation and functional simulation. All artifacts are open-sourced at the boutros-lab GitHub. The paper is targeted at cs.AR (hardware architecture), consistent with its circuit- and architecture-level contributions.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and well-scoped. The authors explicitly note that, to their knowledge, "no prior work has investigated DSP block architecture modifications to natively support MXFP formats." Prior DSP-block work (Boutros FPL'18, PIR-DSP FCCM'19, Tensor Slices TRETS'22) targeted int8/int4/fp16/bfloat16, and prior FPGA MXFP work (Samson FPL'24, Abdurakhmanov & Fahmy TRETS'25) is soft-logic-only, so the gap they identify is genuine.
- **Rigor:** Mixed but defensible. Area numbers come from a *simplified* Agilex-5-like core that omits the other operating modes, so the reported +36% is explicitly an *overestimate* — a refreshingly honest framing, though it means absolute area claims cannot be checked against the real commercial block. The TFLOPS peak numbers are explicitly an *optimistic upper bound* assuming no frequency degradation as the device fills; the systolic-array results then partially correct for this by running real PnR at ~400 MHz (vs. 458 MHz single-instance), showing ~13% degradation rather than the dire drop the upper-bound caveat suggests.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong. Standard open PDK (ASAP7), open flow (HAMMER), open IP generators (FloPoCo, COFFE), open baselines (Samson et al.), open RTL for all dot products / DSP designs / systolic arrays. The main non-reproducible element is the proprietary Agilex-5 DSP block microarchitecture, but the authors work around this by building a documented simplified model.
- **Significance:** Practical and timely. MXFP standardization (OCP, 2023) is real and industry-backed (AMD/Arm/Intel/Meta/Microsoft/Nvidia/Qualcomm); Nvidia Blackwell already does fp4/fp6 in tensor cores. Making FPGAs competitive for low-precision inference is a meaningful problem, and a +4.2× average systolic throughput at +1.8% die area is a strong result if it survives silicon.
- **Red flags / specific weaknesses:**
  - **The recommended design (iteration 4) is not fully backward compatible** — it implements dot-8 instead of the baseline dot-10 in the tensor mode, which the authors themselves flag. Any existing bitstream relying on dot-10 tensor-mode behavior would need rework; this is a real cost glossed over in the framing of "retaining backward compatibility."
  - **No ML accuracy evaluation at all.** This is a pure hardware study; it never runs an actual model or reports inference quality under the formats, so the claim that these formats are "useful for inference" is inherited entirely from cited prior work (Rouhani et al. arXiv:2310.10537).
  - **E5M2 (the wider-range MXFP8 variant) is dropped from the recommended design** and only recoverable through iteration 3.3 at +59% area; users needing E5M2 get either no tensor-mode support or a much larger block.
  - **MXFP8 modes drop infinity/NaN special encodings** to fit the datapath — a silent correctness deviation from the MX specification that is mentioned only once.
  - **Verification is RTL simulation with a pin-compatible stand-in** in Quartus; no timing closure of the *internal* modified datapath was done inside the commercial Quartus flow for the systolic array, so the ~400 MHz full-device number reflects the unmodified Agilex-5 block's timing, not the modified one's.
  - The area/TFLOPS benefit assumes DSP-rich devices; on DSP-poor parts the 1.8%-of-die framing understates the cost.

## Strengths

- Cleanly isolates and quantifies a specific, real architectural gap (tensor mode's E2M1/E2M3-only coverage) that the rest of the paper then closes.
- The iterative design narrative (iterations 1→4 with explicit area ratios 1.28×/1.65×/1.31×–1.59×/1.36×) is an unusually transparent exploration of the coverage-vs-density-vs-area tradeoff, ending in a justified recommendation rather than a single hand-picked point.
- Honest about methodological limitations: explicitly labels peak TFLOPS as optimistic, area overheads as overestimates, and backward-compatibility breakage in iteration 4.
- Strong systolic-array case study tying the block-level change to a device-level outcome (4.2× average, >90% DSP utilization at ~400 MHz with 22–32% soft logic), which is what an FPGA architect actually cares about.
- Full open-source release of RTL, DSP designs, and benchmarks — a rarity for DSP-block architecture papers.

## Limitations

- No silicon validation; all area/timing numbers are from a simplified standard-cell model (ASAP7) plus COFFE-estimated routing, not from a taped-out or vendor-accurate block.
- No end-to-end DL workload results — no model accuracy, no real inference latency, no memory-bandwidth-bound cases where arithmetic density is not the bottleneck.
- E5M2 and special NaN/Inf encodings are sacrificed in the recommended design, narrowing MX-spec compliance.
- The backward-incompatible dot-10→dot-8 change in iteration 4 may break existing tooling/bitstreams and is not analyzed for ecosystem impact.
- Frequency degradation under full-device utilization is acknowledged but only partially measured (systolic arrays); the standalone Table-I/Fig-3 peaks remain optimistic upper bounds.

## Relevance

这篇论文与硬件加速 / 低精度深度学习方向高度相关，特别是对关心 FPGA 架构、MXFP/块缩放浮点格式、以及 LLM 推理加速器底层 DSP 设计的研究非常有价值；若用户关注点在纯算法或软件侧量化，则参考意义相对有限。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the modified DSP block perform on memory-bandwidth-bound LLM inference kernels (e.g., GEMV in decoding), where arithmetic density is not the bottleneck and the 4.2× peak throughput may not translate to wall-clock speedup?
- What is the actual ML inference accuracy impact when E5M2 is dropped and NaN/Inf encodings are omitted — are there workloads where the recommended design is functionally unusable?
- Can the dot-10→dot-8 baseline change be made backward compatible (e.g., via a mode bit that restores dot-10), and at what area cost?
- How do these results transfer to AMD/Xilinx DSP48E2-class blocks, which have a very different multiplier/accumulator structure?
- What is the energy/TFLOPS and energy/area impact, given that area-only numbers may hide a power-density cost from the larger CSA reduction trees (37-bit for E4M3, 67-bit for E5M2)?
- Does taping out even a stripped-down testchip of the modified block confirm the ASAP7-projected area and the 458 MHz frequency in silicon?
