A Modern Multimodal Assistant on a 6 GB 2011 GPU: Stage-Validated, All-GPU CUDA Inference for Fermi. [paper]

Key finding

A current-gen multimodal assistant runs fully resident on a 2011 6 GB Fermi GPU — one image, one question, answered in 1.7 s.

A. C. Opus · J. Q. Lu · Dept. of Physics, University of Puerto Rico, Mayagüez
edge-inference legacy-gpu cuda multimodal-llm quantization vision-transformer gated-delta-net
Key metric · Results

17.3×

10k-token prefill cut from 479.3 s (20.9 tok/s, naive attention) to 27.7 s (361.2 tok/s) by a zero-allocation cuBLAS attention rewrite that kills the O(N²) wall.

Why this matters · Relevance

Measure the vendor ceiling first, benchmark at the longest tier, and stage-validate every port.

The methodology — not the hardware — is what transfers directly to any edge-inference or kernel-porting effort.

Critical edge · Limitations

No standard VLM benchmark — the port is proved faithful, not useful.

Correctness rests on token-identical decoding, one planted needle at 60% depth, and an eight-item self-authored smoke battery. Quality is inherited from the upstream model card, never re-measured.

01 / Methodology

Measure-first, on one machine.

Explicitly an engineering & measurement report, not an algorithmic-novelty claim. The recurring discipline: every ceiling and every bug yielded to measurement against a ground truth, and resisted intuition.

TARGET  NVIDIA Tesla C2075 · Fermi/GF110, 2011
CORES   448 CUDA · sm_20 · 6 GB GDDR5 (~5.3 usable)
PEAK    ~1.03 TFLOP FP32 · ~144 GB/s · no tensor cores
TOOL    CUDA 8.0 container (build only) — still ships cuBLAS
HOST    pre-AVX Westmere Xeon (SSE4.2/SSSE3 only)

Model: MiniCPM-V-4.6 — SigLIP2 vision tower (27-layer, 1152-wide ViT) + window-attention merger giving 16× token compression + 0.8 B Qwen3-family hybrid backbone (18 gated-delta-net + 6 softmax-attn blocks). Everything resident: 4.4 of 5.3 GB at rest, no host↔device transfer inside any forward pass.

Three measured foundations
  1. Prefill projections → dequantize-then-cuBLAS. 8-bit weights dequantized once into FP32 scratch, then vendor sgemm. Reached 659 GFLOP/s (64% FP32 peak) — overturning the prior false claim that the 37% hand-written GEMM was the ceiling.
  2. Chunked delta-rule scan for 18 recurrent layers. Within a 512-token chunk the gated delta rule is a lower-triangular system (two small GEMMs + forward substitution + batched GEMMs). First version was 2.4× slower; per-component attribution localized 92% of time in one bad kernel; after fix it is 2.8× faster than sequential recurrence.
  3. Decode stays 8-bit — a measured negative. Fermi issues nibble-unpacking shifts at half rate, so the best 4-bit register-blocked kernel is 12% slower than 8-bit (0.31 vs 0.28 ms), moving it off its bandwidth bound.

Five intuitions measurement killed

  • "37% is the GEMM wall" → wrong by ~2×; vendor hits 66%
  • "chunked scan is slower" → 2.8× faster after one-kernel fix
  • "4-bit decode must be faster" → 12% slower on Fermi
  • "2k benchmarks represent prefill" → 10k reveals the O(N²) wall
  • "index arithmetic can always be reimplemented" → tie-breaking hazard at 10/28 = 25/70

03 / Critical assessment

Strengths against limitations, side by side.

Strengths

  • Exceptional measurement discipline. Every ceiling and bug resolved against a ground truth; five named reversals of prior intuition.
  • Transferable porting recipe. CPU-capability override forcing a legacy code path to generate a local reference trace — generalizes to any unsupported target.
  • Crisp tie-breaking rule. "Call the reference operator for index arithmetic with reachable exact ties" — backed by a concrete 10/28 = 25/70 failure.
  • Zero-allocation attention layout trick writes scores into the engine's existing per-(position, head) buffer with leading dimension heads×seq — fits the 6 GB budget.
  • Honest negatives (hand-GEMM ceiling wrong; 4-bit slower) prevent the community from repeating the same mistakes.

Limitations

  • No standard VLM benchmark (MMMU / MMBench / MMVet). The port is proved faithful, not that the assistant is useful on this hardware.
  • Single-hardware, single-workstation evaluation: no variance bars, no second unit, all throughput from one C2075.
  • Light correctness probes — one planted needle at one depth (60%), one self-authored eight-item battery; no systematic recall curve.
  • Largely unobtainable setup (CUDA 8.0 container, pre-AVX host, C2075) — reproducible in principle, rarely in practice.
  • No public code/repository URL; the artifact is described but not linked. Authorship provenance ("A. C. Opus") worth noting.
04 / Open questions

What this report does not answer.

The faithful-port guarantee (1.4×10⁻⁵ tower error, token-identical decoding) may or may not preserve reported accuracy on harder tasks. Each question below targets a gap a reviewer would close.

Relevance: directly relevant to edge inference, kernel porting, and efficient multimodal LLM serving.

Do small numerical differences compound on standard benchmarks (MMMU/MMBench/MMVet/OCRBench), or is token-identical decoding enough?
Would a systematic needle sweep across depths (0/25/50/75/100%) and multiple needles reveal retrieval degradation the single 60%-depth probe hides?
Do the attention layout trick and chunked scan port back to modern low-end GPUs where memory, not compute, binds?
Speculative decoding is disabled at ~70% draft-acceptance — is that break-even sensitive to draft-model size on Fermi?
Does the "measure the vendor ceiling first" methodology generalize as a pre-deployment audit on Turing/Ampere INT4?
Does the exact-tie hazard recur in RoPE frequency tables or rotary index arithmetic elsewhere?
