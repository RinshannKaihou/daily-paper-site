Automatic Ordinary Differential Equations Discovery for Biological Systems Using a Large-Language-Model-Powered Agentic System

David Krongauz  ·  Arad Zulti  ·  Eran Segal   (Weizmann Inst. of Science / MBZUAI)  ·  Teddy Lazebnik (Univ. of Haifa / Jönköping Univ., corresponding)

Key finding

LLM agents plus constrained symbolic regression recover biological ODE models perfectly across 12 systems — mechanistic constraints, not numerical fit, do the work.

Key metric · Variable & term F1
1.00/12 systems

Perfect variable recovery on every benchmark, full-pipeline term F1 = 1.00, plausibility 0.967.
Strip the formalizer → pure-SINDy plausibility collapses to 0.603, term F1 to 0.541.

Why this matters

A clean knowledge-guided vs. pure-data ablation that isolates what LLM agents actually contribute to mechanistic modeling in scientific discovery.

Critical edge

Headline gap rests on a pure-SINDy strawman with no head-to-head vs. LLM-SR, and the polynomial grammar excludes the Hill, saturating & delay kinetics that dominate real biology.

§ 01 Methodology

A pipeline of inspectable artifacts, not a black box.

Seven specialized agents coordinated by an orchestrator; each reads a defined input and writes a human-readable artifact to a shared session. Ground-truth equations are quarantined from every discovery agent.

01
Literature Surveyor
Compiles cited background, expected constraints, candidate model families. → lit_review.md
02
Explorer (data mode)
EDA + optional SINDy sparse-regression screen on supplied trajectories.
03
Formalizer
Curates typed hard/soft constraints, seed equations, protected core terms. → problem.json + constraints.md
04
Runner
Evolutionary (genetic) search over sparse polynomial ODEs; composite objective = constraint-sat + trajectory-fit + sparsity, with hard-constraint gating. → results.json
05
Reporter
Forward-simulates the selected model; diagnostics, plots, summary.
06
Recapper
Pipeline-level critique; flags limitations and next steps.
07
Evaluator
Reads quarantined ground truth only after blind search; compares across 5 metric families.
Constraint-only: no measurements — search driven by literature-derived constraints + parsimony. Data-anchored: user time series added as a fit objective (SINDy + trajectory score).
Search grammar
Sparse polynomial ODEs (excludes Hill, delays, stochastic)
Evaluation
3-tier benchmark · IR / EX / DI
Metric families
Variable F1 · Constraint F1 · Term P/R/F1 · Expression (1−Ted) · Expert plausibility
Expert panel
5 PhD biomathematicians, 10+ yrs

Robustness

  • SIR (IR) robust to 30% proportional Gaussian noise (term F1 = 1.00); degrades to 0.73 at 40–60% noise.
  • Age-structured SIR held term F1 ≥ 0.90 across all noise levels (Table 3).
  • Sparse sampling — removing 25–75% of time points — left recovered terms unchanged for both SIR systems.
  • Hidden-variable: dropping R (SIR) or RO (age-SIR) had no effect on term recovery; dropping the moderation variable C in misinformation cut variable F1 to 0.80.

Ablation · what carries the load

  • Full system: term F1 1.00, plausibility 0.967.
  • No-EDA/SINDy: F1 stays 1.00 — the data screen is not load-bearing.
  • Pretrained formalizer: F1 stays 1.00.
  • No-literature: plausibility → ~0.700, term F1 → ~0.732.
  • Pure-SINDy: plausibility 0.603, term F1 0.541 — the strawman that dramatizes the gap.

§ 03 Critical assessment · strengths ↔ limitations

A useful re-architecting of known ideas — within a narrow grammar.

Strengths

  • Clean, modular, fully inspectable pipeline — each agent writes a human-readable artifact; reasoning behind a discovered ODE is auditable step by step.
  • Blind-search quarantine of ground-truth equations is methodologically correct for honest benchmarking.
  • The negative result (data can hurt; pure fit preserves biologically wrong equations) is intellectually honest and the most actionable finding.
  • Comprehensive robustness across noise, sparsity, hidden variables — with candid reporting of where it fails.
  • Public code + a 3-tier benchmark and a thoughtful multi-level metric suite.

Limitations

  • Small benchmark (12 systems); no biochemical networks, spatial, stochastic, delay, or hybrid models.
  • Polynomial-only grammar excludes Hill, saturating, delays, thresholds, stochastic terms central to real biology.
  • Expert plausibility is subjective, 5 evaluators, not reproducible without them.
  • No head-to-head vs. LLM-SR / SciMED under matched settings; strongest external baseline is the authors' own pure-SINDy strawman.
  • LLM model versions & search hyperparameters unspecified; no variance/CIs across seeds.
  • Cannot detect or self-correct trajectory-compatible-but-wrong equations (documented FHN / chronic-wound failure mode).
Novelty
Incremental — re-architecting of LLM-priors + constrained SR + SINDy; no new algorithm.
Rigor
Blind quarantine + multi-level metrics; no peer-system comparison under matched settings.
Reproducibility
Public code & appendix data; hampered by LLM/search stochasticity & subjective plausibility.
Significance
Moderate — strong conceptual point, limited immediate biochemical applicability.

§ 04 Questions for further exploration

What the paper does not answer.

  1. How does MEDA fare in a direct head-to-head against LLM-SR and SciMED under matched data, constraints, and compute — not only its own ablations?
  2. Can the grammar extend to Hill / saturating / delay terms without losing the constraint-gating that makes the formalizer load-bearing?
  3. Is there an automatic detector for when data-anchored mode emits a trajectory-compatible but mechanistically wrong ODE — the documented FHN-adaptation / chronic-wound failure?
  4. How sensitive are the F1 = 1.00 numbers to LLM version, temperature, and GA generations — are they stable across seeds?
  5. Does the framework transfer to real wet-lab time series with noise, sparsity, partial observability — where ground truth is genuinely unknown?
