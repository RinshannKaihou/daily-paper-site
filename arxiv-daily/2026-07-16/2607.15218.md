> **一句话总结：** 本文通过隐藏态方向分析证明，LLM 中"内容危险"（content danger，措辞本身有害）与"物理危险"（physical danger，措辞无害但物理后果危险）是两个可分离的信号，并据此提出单层线性探针 PRISM，在 SafeAgentBench 上以 86.2–87.7% 准确率、11.7–13.7% FPR 联合检测两类危险（显著低于同规模 LLM judge 的 24.7–39.0% FPR），并在新构造的 PSB-1K 基准上达到 99.6% 准确率、0.7% FPR。

> **TL;DR:** This paper argues that embodied-agent physical danger (PD, e.g., "microwave an egg") is a different safety problem from text-level content danger (CD, e.g., "ignite the curtain"). The authors show via hidden-state direction analysis that CD and PD occupy separable directions in Qwen2.5-3B/7B/14B/32B, Phi-3.5-mini and SmolLM2-1.7B (3B probe-weight angle 75.9° vs a 60.35°±1.58° random-split null, z=10.09, p<0.0001), and propose PRISM, a single-layer L2-regularized logistic probe over a selected hidden state. On SafeAgentBench PRISM hits 86.2–87.7% accuracy at 11.7–13.7% FPR vs LLM-judge FPR of 24.7–39.0%, and on the new PSB-1K benchmark (1,000 contrastive physical-risk pairs with harm keywords removed) it reaches 99.6% accuracy and 0.7% FPR while a Qwen2.5-3B judge rejects 67.8% of safe tasks.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示物理危险与内容危险在表示空间中可分离：** 在 Qwen2.5-3B/7B/14B/32B、Phi-3.5-mini、SmolLM2-1.7B 上定义 Content Danger Direction (CDD) 与 Physical Danger Direction (PDD)，探针权重夹角稳定在 71°–78° 区间，且在 Qwen2.5-3B 上 76.33° 的观测值显著超过 60.35°±1.58° 的随机划分零分布（z=10.09, p<0.0001），表明物理安全是独立建模目标而非普通文本安全的子类。
- **提出 PRISM 方法：** 一个作用于冻结 LLM 选定中间-后层隐藏态的单层 L2 正则化 logistic 回归探针，联合训练于 safe + content-danger + physical-danger 三类样本，直接在隐藏态风险空间中划出 safe/unsafe 决策边界，而非堆叠 LLM judge。
- **构建 PhysicalSafetyBench-1K (PSB-1K)：** 1,000 对最小对比的安全/物理风险指令（共 2,000 条），覆盖 10 类家庭风险族，刻意去除 danger/harm/burn/shock/poison 等直接伤害词，逼迫方法依靠物理因果推理而非表面政策词；采用成对 GroupKFold 防止对比对泄露。
- **系统化对比揭示两类失败模式：** 内容审查型护栏（如 Llama Guard 3-1B 在 PSB-1K 上 0/1000 真阳性，物理危险召回 0.0%）漏检物理危险，而零样本 LLM judge 虽能召回物理危险却严重误杀安全任务（SafeAgentBench 14B judge FPR 39.0%，PSB-1K 上 3B judge FPR 67.8%）；PRISM 同时规避两个极端。
- **可复现评测协议与跨架构/跨数据集泛化：** 在 SafeAgentBench、SafeText、EARBench、PSB-1K 四个基准上评估，并通过 Phi-3.5-mini、SmolLM2-1.7B 验证 CD/PD 可分离性非 Qwen 专属现象。

## Key Contributions

- **Representation-level decomposition of danger.** Defines CDD = µ_CD − µ_safe and PDD = µ_PD − µ_safe class-mean directions at a selected layer, and shows that the CD/PD probe-weight angle stays in 71°–78° across six models while exceeding a size-matched random-split null on Qwen2.5-3B (76.33° vs 60.35°±1.58°, z=10.09, p<0.0001) — evidence that embodied physical safety is a distinct modeling target.
- **PRISM, a single-layer hidden-state probe.** A model-agnostic L2-regularized logistic-regression probe (LogisticRegression, C=1.0, max_iter=2000, StandardScaler fit inside each fold) over a per-model layer selected by 5-fold CV, trained jointly on all three label classes; no decoding/generation needed at inference, only one forward pass to the probe layer.
- **PhysicalSafetyBench-1K (PSB-1K).** A new contrastive benchmark of 1,000 safe/risk pairs (2,000 flat examples) across ten household risk families (microwave metal contact, liquid contact with powered devices, open-flame proximity, food contamination, contact-placement injury, etc.), with all unsafe prompts scrubbed of direct harm keywords, evaluated under pair-wise GroupKFold so the two members of a contrast pair never split across folds.
- **A reproducible evaluation protocol** spanning SafeAgentBench (600 AI2-THOR tasks, 113 CD + 187 PD), adapted SafeText (737 examples), EARBench (2,634 examples) and PSB-1K, with directional, geometric (Nearest Centroid), prototype (LPM), kernel (SVM-RBF, MLP), and model-based baselines (zero-shot Qwen2.5-Instruct judges, Llama Guard 3-1B).
- **A sharper characterization of two failure modes** — content-oriented guards under-detect physically grounded danger, while LLM judges trade unsafe recall for severe over-blocking of normal household tasks — motivating representation-level physical-safety modeling.

## Methodology

The paper proceeds in two stages: a diagnostic analysis, then a method that operationalizes the finding.

**(1) CDD/PDD direction analysis (§3).** For a frozen LLM, the authors extract the last-token hidden state h^L(x) at layer L with output_hidden_states=True. They compute class means over safe (S), content-danger (E) and physical-danger (I) index sets and define CDD = µ_CD − µ_safe and PDD = µ_PD − µ_safe. Two complementary angular quantities are reported: the mean-difference angle θ_md (raw centroid geometry, conservative) and the probe-weight angle θ_pw (decision-relevant geometry between two L2-regularized logistic probes, safe-vs-CD and safe-vs-PD). To rule out high-dimensional fitting artifacts, θ_pw is calibrated against a random-split null: the 300 SafeAgentBench unsafe examples are randomly partitioned into 113/187 (matching the CD/PD sizes), two safe-vs-subset probes are trained with the identical pipeline, and the angle between their weights is recorded over 200 repeats.

**(2) PRISM probe (§4).** Given the separability result, PRISM trains a single binary classifier σ(w^T h^L(x) + b) over the full (standardized) hidden state at a selected layer — explicitly not a hand-crafted concatenation of scalar CDD/PDD projections. The layer is chosen by a per-layer 5-fold cross-validation accuracy sweep on the training set (selected/total layer indices: Qwen2.5-3B 27/36, 7B 20/28, 14B 26/48, 32B 41/64, Phi-3.5-mini 24/32, SmolLM2-1.7B 14/24). StandardScaler and LogisticRegression(C=1.0, max_iter=2000, random_state=42) are fitted inside each fold; default decision threshold τ=0.5. The full training/inference procedure is given in Algorithm 1.

**(3) PSB-1K construction (§5.1).** 1,000 minimally contrasted safe/risk pairs across ten household risk families, with unsafe prompts written without danger/harm/burn/shock/poison keywords, evaluated under pair-wise GroupKFold.

**(4) Evaluation protocol.** Unpaired benchmarks use 5-fold StratifiedKFold with standardization fitted inside each fold; PSB-1K uses pair-wise GroupKFold. Directional probes (CDD-only, PDD-only) and LPM use subtype leave-out testing on SafeAgentBench. LLM judges use zero-shot SAFE/UNSAFE prompts parsed by deterministic string matching. Latency compares one PRISM forward pass against one judge generation pass (max_new_tokens=8) over 20 warmup + 580 measured examples.

## Results & Highlights

- **SafeAgentBench main results (Table 2).** PRISM achieves **86.2% / 87.0% / 87.2% / 87.7% accuracy** at **13.7% / 13.7% / 11.7% / 12.0% FPR** across Qwen2.5-3B/7B/14B/32B, with physical-danger detection holding at **81.3% / 82.4% / 79.1% / 81.8%** and content-danger detection never below 93.8%. The same-scale zero-shot LLM judges attain higher physical-danger recall (86.6% / 84.5% / 94.1% / 95.7%) but at **27.3% / 24.7% / 39.0% / 37.0% FPR** — i.e., the 14B and 32B judges reject roughly two out of five safe household tasks. Llama Guard 3-1B produces **0.0% detection for both danger types** in the local run, a taxonomy-mismatch failure.
- **Direction ablations show CD-only cannot substitute for PD.** CDD-only's physical-OOD detection collapses to **19.8% / 31.6% / 32.6% / 31.6%** across 3B–32B; the latent-prototype LPM is worse at **11.2% / 9.6% / 17.1% / 19.3%**. PDD-only reverse-transfers incompletely (content-OOD 68.1%–85.0%). This is the core evidence that embodied safety is not captured by a single content-oriented axis.
- **PSB-1K (Table 4).** With harm keywords removed, PRISM reaches **99.6% accuracy, 99.9% physical-danger recall, 0.7% FPR** on Qwen2.5-3B. The Qwen2.5-3B zero-shot judge detects 96.5% of physical danger but rejects **67.8% of safe tasks**; Llama Guard 3-1B has **0/1000 true positives (0.0% recall, 0.0% FPR)**. Cross-model PSB-1K (Table 5): PRISM FPR stays at 0.7%–3.2% across six backbones while judges need 67.8%–90.0% FPR to keep high recall; the SmolLM2-1.7B judge blocks **294/300 safe tasks (98.0% FPR)**.
- **Cross-dataset replication (Table 3, Qwen2.5-3B).** PRISM: SafeText F1 0.924 / AUC 0.980 / FPR **7.6%** (judge FPR 65.7%); EARBench F1 0.828 / AUC 0.906 / FPR **16.6%** (judge FPR 74.6%). These are within-dataset 5-fold CV replications, explicitly framed as cross-dataset replication under matched machinery rather than zero-shot transfer.
- **Statistical comparison and latency (§5.6, Table 7).** McNemar's test on the paired 3B comparison does not reject equal accuracy (all 600 examples χ²=0.56, p=0.4557; physical subset χ²=1.94, p=0.1637). PRISM forward pass is 1.86–2.12× faster than judge generation: **56.8 vs 116.8 ms (7B), 146.1 vs 309.1 ms (14B), 103.4 vs 191.9 ms (32B)** per sample.
- **Cross-architecture (Table 6).** CDD/PDD probe-weight angles cluster at **75.9° / 77.3° / 71.1°** for Qwen2.5-3B / Phi-3.5-mini / SmolLM2-1.7B; PRISM reaches 86.2% / 87.7% / 82.7% accuracy at 13.7% / 13.3% / 18.0% FPR, while CDD-only collapses on physical-OOD to 19.8% / 38.0% / 25.7%. An orthogonalisation check shows PDD carries variance not duplicated by CDD (<1.5pp drop).

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are from the Department of Computer Science and Technology, Tsinghua University (Weimeng Wang, Ziqiang Wang — corresponding, Zihang Zhan, Qi Li, Ke Xu — corresponding) and Nanyang Technological University, Singapore (Chuanpu Fu). The group has a credible track record on LLM/mechanistic-interpretability and security topics. The paper is unusually disciplined about scope: it explicitly states its claims are "deliberately bounded" (PRISM is not a complete safety system; CDD/PDD are separable, not strictly orthogonal), acknowledges that random-split nulls were completed only for the 3B model (7B/14B/32B nulls "were not completed") and downgrades those to "an observed angle pattern," and reframes cross-dataset numbers as fold-internal replication rather than zero-shot transfer. Statistical claims are supported by McNemar's test and a random-split null with explicit z/p values.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** Genuine conceptual contribution: framing embodied physical danger as a distinct, separately probed hidden-state axis rather than a stronger instance of text-policy danger, backed by the CDD/PDD angular analysis with a random-split null. The linear-probe method itself is standard (Alain & Bengio 2016), so novelty rests on the diagnostic framing and the PSB-1K benchmark, not on probe machinery.
- **Rigor.** Strong on calibration honesty: reports accuracy/F1/AUC/CD-det/PD-det/FPR together, distinguishes in-domain CV from OOD, and uses a random-split null. However, the cross-model separability claim (7B/14B/32B) rests on observed angles only because the null tests were not completed — the headline separability result is thus formally validated on exactly one backbone (Qwen2.5-3B).
- **Reproducibility.** Reasonable: concrete hyperparameters (C=1.0, max_iter=2000, random_state=42), per-model selected layers listed, baselines are named open models (Llama Guard 3-1B, Qwen2.5-Instruct, LPM), and the SafeAgentBench keyword split is acknowledged as reproducible-but-leaky (drop in 12 safe / 29 CD cases; break in 1 safe / 34 CD cases). PSB-1K construction details (ten risk families, GroupKFold protocol) are described but the prompt-generation process is not fully auditable from the main text.
- **Significance.** Practically relevant for embodied-agent/VLA deployment: a forward-pass probe at ~1.86–2.12× the speed of a judge with a third-to-half the FPR is a deployable safety monitor, not just a diagnostic. The SmolLM2-1.7B judge blocking 294/300 safe tasks is a concrete, memorable demonstration that small instruct models are not viable stand-alone physical-safety classifiers.
- **Red flags / caveats.** (1) The 99.6% PSB-1K number is suspiciously high relative to SafeAgentBench's 86.2% — likely because PSB-1K pairs are contrastive and lexically controlled, so it measures paired-discrimination more than open-world detection; this gap deserves more discussion. (2) PSB-1K appears author-generated; no human-annotation quality audit or inter-annotator agreement is reported. (3) The headline "PRISM statistically comparable to the judge" comes from a matched-FPR re-thresholding (τ≈0.029) shown only in Appendix Figure 8, not the default-threshold operating point — readers comparing Table 2 numbers directly will not see matched conditions. (4) Latency is a local benchmark with unspecified exact GPU/software stack, and PRISM's 32B mean latency (103.4 ms) being lower than its 14B latency (146.1 ms) is an unexplained quirk that should be addressed. (5) No robustness evaluation against adversarial rephrasing or jailbreak-style prompts is provided.

## Strengths

- Clean conceptual framing (CD vs PD) with a falsifiable representation-level test rather than a pure benchmark study.
- Honest, calibrated reporting: FPR is reported alongside recall everywhere, and the central comparison is explicitly "lower-FPR operating point," not "dominates in recall."
- PSB-1K directly targets a real and under-served evaluation gap — physically grounded danger without harm keywords.
- Cross-architecture validation on Phi-3.5-mini and SmolLM2-1.7B, plus the orthogonalisation check, narrow the "trivial duplication of axes" objection.
- Ablations cleanly localize the gain to (a) the selected hidden layer and (b) training on the full two-subtype boundary, ruling out complex classifiers (SVM-RBF/MLP do not beat the linear probe).

## Limitations

- The formal random-split null is completed only for Qwen2.5-3B; the cross-model separability claim for 7B/14B/32B is downgraded to an observed angular pattern, not a statistical claim.
- PSB-1K is newly constructed by the authors with no reported human-validation or inter-annotator agreement, and its near-perfect numbers (99.6%/0.7% FPR) likely reflect the contrastive, keyword-controlled design more than open-world difficulty.
- SafeAgentBench's 25-keyword split is acknowledged as leaky (drop, break skewed across classes), which slightly inflates CD detection.
- No evaluation of adversarial robustness, jailbreak rephrasing, or distribution shift beyond the four benchmarks.
- The PRISM probe must be retrained per backbone and per benchmark distribution; transfer between benchmarks is framed as matched-fold replication, not zero-shot.
- Latency numbers (especially 32B < 14B) and the undisclosed GPU/stack limit the efficiency claim's generality.
- The method assumes white-box access to hidden states, which is unavailable for many production API-only LLMs.

## Relevance

这项工作与具身智能（embodied agents）、LLM 安全对齐及机制可解释性高度相关：它揭示了一个常被忽视的失败模式——文本安全护栏无法捕获措辞无害但物理执行危险的指令，并提供了一种轻量、可部署的表示层探针方案。对于研究 LLM 作为机器人/智能体高层规划器、以及关注安全监控与探测式可解释性的读者，PSB-1K 基准和 CDD/PDD 分离性分析具有直接的方法学和评测参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does PRISM perform under adversarial rephrasing or jailbreak-style paraphrases of physical-danger instructions, where an attacker deliberately obfuscates the physical consequence?
- Can a single PRISM probe trained on a mixture of backbones transfer across model families without per-backbone retraining, given that the selected layer indices differ substantially (e.g., 27/36 vs 24/32 vs 14/24)?
- The 99.6% PSB-1K number is strikingly higher than the 86.2% SafeAgentBench number — how much of this gap is the contrastive paired structure (which the safe/risk design plus GroupKFold may not fully neutralize) versus genuine discriminability?
- Could CDD and PDD be combined with refusal-direction steering (Arditi et al. 2024) to not only detect but actively suppress physical-danger plans at inference time?
- Does the CD/PD separability survive instruction-tuning / RLHF distribution shifts, or is it an artifact of base/pretrained representations?
- Since PRISM requires hidden-state access, how would an equivalent API-only deployment (e.g., logit-based or prompt-based) trade off against the reported 1.86–2.12× latency advantage?
- Is there a principled, scale-invariant layer-selection rule (e.g., relative depth 0.7–0.8) that could replace the per-model 5-fold CV sweep without sacrificing the calibrated operating point?
