Per-Token Fixed-Point Convergence in Depth-Recurrent Transformers ↗

Dr. Joe Logan  ·  independent researcher  ·  single-seed study

The recurrent state of a depth-recurrent transformer converges to a per-token fixed point — and a zero-parameter rule that reads this signal beats a learned router.

Why this matters

A zero-parameter, per-token adaptive-depth recipe for looped LMs — and an honest split between FLOP-proxy savings and real throughput. A direct baseline for test-time compute elasticity.

Critical edge

Realized throughput gain is ~0% — the 38% saving is a FLOP proxy that depends on depth-wise continuous batching the author never built.

01

Methodology, in one breath

Architecture
Unique-layer prelude → weight-tied core applied a variable number of times → unique-layer coda. Llama-style blocks (RMSNorm, SwiGLU, rotary, GQA, tied embeddings, no biases), following Huginn (Geiping et al.).
Three guards
(1) recurrent state init from noise s0 ~ N(0, 2/5), not the embedding — forces input-conditioned fixed point; (2) input re-injected each loop via s_{i+1} = core(adapter([e, s_i])); (3) sandwich norm (post-norm inside residuals) to stop hidden-state collapse.
Training
Per step, sample r ~ round(LogNormal(log r̄, 0.5)) clamped to [1, r_max]; plain cross-entropy at the final state — no halting unit, no ponder loss, no FLOP penalty. Truncated BPTT over last k iterations. S0: r̄=4 / r_max=16 / k=4. S1: r̄=8 / r_max=32 / k=8.
Scales
S0 — 12.9M unique params, TinyStories 0.4B tokens (architecture check only). S1 — 29.0M unique (57.3M total), FineWeb-Edu 12.9B tokens, SmolLM2-135M shape with middle layers replaced by the tied core. SmolLM2 tokenizer (49,152).
Three policies
A0 uniform depth (baseline, sweep r). A1 convergence-exit, training-free: freeze tokens once KL(p_i ‖ p_{i-1}) < ε. A2 oracle-labeled linear router: probe trained on self-harvested labels from the same frozen backbone.
Eval
Loss-vs-loops sweeps use noise state init (training path), 64×2048 windows. Allocation comparison uses zero state init, 128 windows. The two conventions differ by 0.013 nats at r=8 — openly reported, not hidden.

Results & highlights

Fixed-point evidence · S1

KL 3.9e-1 → 8.5e-6

Mean successive-output KL between loops 2 and 16; state-change norm decays in lockstep (24.7 → 0.030). Fraction still moving drops 99.8% → 0.1%.

Depth elasticity · S1

0.60-nat range, 16× compute

Validation loss 3.803 (r=1) → 3.202 (r=8) → flat to r=32. One set of weights, depth chosen at inference.

Allocation head-to-head · S1

A1 4.94 · A2 7.99 loops

Both reach the 3.189-nat depth-8 bar. At matched avg depth 3, A1 = 3.231 vs uniform 3.272 — A1 beats uniform in the shallow regime.

Fixed-depth control · S0

constant r=4: peaked

Loss 1.97 at r=4 but 6.01 at r=1, 2.50 at r=16. Randomized-depth S0 stays flat (1.886 / 1.883 / 1.883) — even beats the control at its own depth.

Exit-depth ordering · S1

space 3.58 > word 4.25

Mean exit depth: space 3.58, numeral 3.81, punct 4.16, word 4.25 — content tokens deepest, structural shallowest. Stable under 4× more eval windows. (Flips at S0: numerals deepest.)

Wall-clock bracket

~0% realized · 1.5× ceiling

Teacher-forced harness masks frozen tokens but loops all to r_max. Matched r=8 vs r=5 gives 1.48–1.51× across batch 1–8 — the theoretical ceiling under perfect depth-wise batching.

Ceiling reference · not parity

SmolLM2-135M 2.66 vs 3.20

0.54-nat gap — expected: SmolLM2 trained on ~2T tokens (~150× this budget). Explicitly a ceiling, not a baseline.

Compute footprint

~100 GPU-hours

Entire study (S1 training + all evaluation incl. allocation) on a single RTX 4090. 12.9B tokens at 24k tokens/s, 262k effective steps.

Strengths & limitations, side by side

+ Strengths

  • Reusable diagnostic. Per-loop KL + state-change + still-moving fraction (Table 3) is cheap, clean, and runnable on any depth-recurrent checkpoint.
  • "Reading beats learning" is non-obvious and supported by a clean control: A2's labels come from the same frozen backbone, isolating the comparison.
  • Unusual scope honesty. Average-depth-as-FLOP-proxy, three-point wall-clock bracket, single-scale/single-seed caveat in the abstract, explicit rejection of a token-starved dense baseline.
  • Useful engineering artifacts. Appendix D documents a real bf16/fp32 rotary-attention dtype-promotion bug and a non-finite step guard practitioners will hit.
  • Engaged related work — Huginn, LoopFormer, MoR, ACT, PonderNet, Csordás et al.'s shallow-halt failure — not decorative.

! Limitations

  • Single 135M-class scale, single seed. Behavior at 1B+ unknown; literature says depth effects strengthen with scale, so average-loss results likely understate per-token allocation's value.
  • No realized speedup. 38% depth saving → throughput needs depth-wise continuous batching (Bae et al.), which was not built. Harness throughput ≈ 0% better than uniform.
  • No FLOP-matched dense baseline. The most deployment-relevant comparison is explicitly missing.
  • Teacher-forced only. Per-token allocation at decode time inherits the KV-cache story from prior work and is not stress-tested under autoregressive generation.
  • No downstream-task eval at each depth. Whether reasoning load sits in the "late-converging 10%" is open.
  • "Saturation depth = training mean" rests on two internal + one external point — admirably framed as a hypothesis, not a law.
04

Questions this opens

  1. Does convergence-exit A1 still beat the learned router A2 at 1B+ — or does a non-linear router trained on richer labels overtake it once the useful-depth range widens?
  2. Under autoregressive generation (per-recursion KV caching or depth-1 sharing), does the 38% average-depth saving survive — and what is the realized wall-clock gain under depth-wise continuous batching?
  3. How does per-token exit depth correlate with downstream task accuracy (reasoning, math, code)? Do the late-converging 10% carry the reasoning load?
  4. Can "saturation depth = training mean" be promoted to a law by sweeping the training loop distribution (mean 4/8/16/32) at fixed scale — or does it break at wider distributions?
  5. Is the fixed-point structure preserved under entropy-regularized depth (Ouro) or shortcut-consistency (LoopFormer), or specific to plain cross-entropy with randomized depth?
  6. Could the convergence-exit signal combine with self-speculative decoding (LayerSkip-style verification) to recover exactness while keeping the depth saving?
