Steering Robustness into World Action Models via Mechanistic Interpretability and Optimal Control

Jihoon Hong*, Julian Skifstad*, Qiyue Dai, Alice Chan, Glen Chou (*equal contribution) 2026-07-16
WA-LQR world-action-models mechanistic-interpretability activation-steering optimal-control robot-robustness diffusion-transformer
Key metric
+41pp
peak success-rate gain

Headline single-task improvement on LIBERO-10 over the unsteered baseline across camera, gripper, and noise perturbations.

Per-perturbation averages are a more modest +10–33 pp; the 41 pp is a best-case transfer, not a mean.

Why this matters

A training-free, interpretable framework to diagnose and repair robot foundation-model robustness — a reference for steering VLA/WAM internal representations.

Critical edge

No comparison against any retraining/finetuning-based robustness baseline — so “improves robustness” really only means “beats these particular inference-time baselines.”

missing ablation · RL post-training · robustness finetuning

01Methodology

Three tightly-linked parts: interpret, then steer open-loop, then close the loop.

The pipeline unifies mechanistic interpretability, activation steering, and reduced-order optimal control — no retraining, weights frozen.

STEP 01 · MI ANALYSIS

Find the robustness subspace

Contrastive datasets (clean vs. perturbed rollouts) yield mean-pooled DiT residual-stream activations. The contrastive direction d = x̄⁺ − x̄⁻ lives in a low-rank subspace; a linear SVM on the top-3 PCs gives a hinge-loss separability score that predicts whether steering will work (r = 0.63).

STEP 02 · ACTADD

Open-loop steering (blind)

Average contrastive directions across prompt pairs and chunk positions, inject as x ← x + γ·a. Training-free but brittle: success collapses to 0% at γ ≥ 0.2 under Gaussian noise — the oversteering pathology.

STEP 03 · WA-LQR

Closed-loop feedback control

Stream a rank-64 orthonormal basis, Jacobian-linearize the DiT-block dynamics, solve an L-step LQR per chunk via Riccati recursion with an exponential chunk-decay penalty r(τ). Control is proportional to live feature-tracking error — it only intervenes when the activation drifts from the robustness setpoint.

02Results & highlights

Success rates on LIBERO-10 — steerable models gain, non-separable models are protected.

30 trials/task (Cosmos-Policy), 20 trials/task (LingBot-VA). Std. errors ~±3.6–5.3 pp. On LingBot-VA the MI analysis predicts failure: ActAdd oversteers and degrades, WA-LQR holds the baseline.

Separability predicts steerability: hinge loss correlates with success-rate delta at r = 0.63, giving an ex-ante diagnostic before any intervention is run.

03Strengths

  • Unifies MI, activation steering, and optimal control behind one falsifiable predictor (hinge loss → steerability, r = 0.63).
  • Training-free and weight-preserving — attractive for deployment, avoids data collection / retraining.
  • Closed-loop design concretely fixes ActAdd's failure mode: 0% at γ ≥ 0.2 is exactly the oversteering the feedback controller avoids.
  • Honest negative result on LingBot-VA, with architecture-dependence stated rather than buried.
  • Cross-task transfer of contrastive directions shown with concrete Task i → Task j numbers.

04Limitations

  • Architecture dependence is severe: works on Cosmos-Policy / DiT4DiT, fails on LingBot-VA; no plug-and-play predictor beyond "run MI per setting".
  • No retraining-based baselines (RL post-training, robustness finetuning) — absolute robustness ceiling untested.
  • Simulation-only on LIBERO-10; synthetic noise may not reflect real deployment drift.
  • Manual, empirically-discovered task-grouping step risks post-hoc selection.
  • Jacobian/LQR machinery only validated on ≤ 2B-parameter DiTs; scalability untested.

05Questions for further exploration

Q1Does the hinge-loss predictor transfer beyond DiT WAMs — to π0, OpenVLA-OFT, other VLA backbones?
Q2How does WA-LQR compare to retraining-based robustness methods under the same perturbation budget, in both success and compute?
Q3Does the local-linearity assumption (Jacobian reuse) survive larger OOD shifts from COLOSSEUM / VLATest — lighting, texture, object layout?
Q4Can the manual task-grouping step be replaced with automatic clustering, removing post-hoc selection bias?
Q5Does steering along robustness directions degrade the WAM's predicted video future even when the action succeeds?
Q6On a real robot, where calibration drifts continuously — does the chunk-wise decay schedule still hold?
