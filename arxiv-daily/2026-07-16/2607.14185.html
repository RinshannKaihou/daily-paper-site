Closed-Loop Knowledge Dynamics: An Operational Framework for Saturation and Escape

Loops saturate. Re-opening the optimizer won't help — only strong, task-aligned external feedback can force an escape.

Xuening Wu · Shan Yu · Shenqin Yin (corresponding)
Pfizer Shanghai · Independent · Fudan University, Institute of Humanities & Social Science Data

Task success after BC+PPO with m=20,000 expert examples vs. PPO-only continuation from the saturated checkpoint — across another 100,000 sparse-reward steps. Same optimizer, same budget, only the feedback differs.
Why This Matters
A falsifiable diagnostic language for when self-refining agents stall — and a matched-control test for whether RAG, tools, or retrieval actually help, or merely move the state.
Critical Edge
The framework's signature quantities — the probe-kernel discrepancy δμ,ν and the baseline-relative KL ≥ 2η² — are never directly measured. Experiments support the saturation–escape phenomenology, not the mechanism claims.

01Methodology · three levels, one falsifiable claim

Three-level operational model
Any closed-loop AI system is a tuple K = (X, Y, E, Ω, G, H, Φ), kept as three distinct levels: observable state x_t, transition kernel K_θ, and governing structure θ updated by U(θ_t, z_t).
Structural change ≠ state movement
"Structure changed" is defined as a pre-registered Wasserstein probe discrepancy δ_μ,ν(θ,θ′) > ε_G passing a confidence lower bound — and falsified by an equivalence test. A large jump d(x_t, x_{t+1}) alone is explicitly NOT evidence.
Saturation theorem (Thm 1)
One-step contraction-plus-noise E[V(x_{t+1})|x_t] ≤ ρV(x_t) + σ forces E[V(x_t)] ≤ ρ^t V(x_0) + σ/(1−ρ) — an exponential-decay + noise-floor signature. Fits LLM refine (R²=0.999), RL Bellman (0.996), BO exploration (0.997).
Two escape conditions
(1) Metric: attractor shift Δ > Δ* = (c(1+ρ′)+σ_ext)/(1−ρ′). (2) Information: raising escape probability by η requires KL(P_z‖P_0) ≥ 2η². Prop 5 shows conditional MI cannot certify escape.
Matched-continuation controls
Same optimizer, same budget, same eval stream — only the feedback varies. Effective escape: E_eff = E_shift · max{ΔQ, 0}, refusing to credit mere displacement.

02Results · three domains, one pattern

i
LLM code repair. On pathspec, baseline/generic/mismatched all stall at 0.400; root-cause diagnostic feedback reaches pass rate 1.000 in all 5 trajectories (ΔQ=0.600, E_eff=0.280). Generic feedback produces displacement but zero quality gain.
ii
Sparse-reward RL. PPO-only stays at 0.000; m=10,000 BC+PPO reaches 0.212; m=20,000 reaches 0.700 and persists through 100k further steps — a basin relocation that extra optimization alone never achieved.
iii
Bayesian optimization. Reopening the domain: escape 0.000 on Styblinski-Tang 2D and Hartmann-6. Targeted feedback: 1.000 and 0.833 (m=16) respectively. Generic feedback stays 0.000 on Hartmann-6 — alignment shifts the information–escape frontier.
iv
Cross-system signature. All three systems show the predicted exponential-plus-floor / geometric / power-law attenuation, with the highest post-perturbation spike rate (0.93 vs 0.07 baseline) and cleanest second-relaxation fit (R²=0.962).

03Strengths vs Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean three-level separation that genuinely disambiguates state movement, structural change, and quality-improving escape — phenomena the refine/BO literatures routinely conflate.
  • Proposition 5 identity correctly explains why conditional MI is the wrong information quantity for certifying escape — a real, under-appreciated point.
  • Matched-continuation control design (same optimizer/budget/eval, vary only feedback) is rigorous and directly reusable.
  • Unusual intellectual honesty: every assumption bounded in a Remark, selection bias disclosed, central quantities' empirical status admitted.
  • E_eff = E_shift · max(ΔQ,0) refuses to credit mere displacement.

Limitations

  • Signature quantities δ_μ,ν and baseline-relative KL are never measured in any experiment — all escape results are outcome-level, not mechanism-level.
  • Code-repair task selection is exploratory and biased: n=5 trajectories × 2 hand-picked bugs.
  • Assumption 1 (pairwise contraction) is sufficient but never independently tested, despite a proposed protocol in Remark 2.
  • RL result is on a single easy environment (DoorKey-8x8) with a trivial deterministic expert.
  • All three domains are toy-scale; no large-scale LLM or real-world discovery deployment.

04Open Questions

?
Can δ_μ,ν and KL(P_z‖P_0) actually be estimated from finite black-box LLM samples, and at what sample complexity does the CI lower bound clear a meaningful ε_G?
?
Does the metric threshold Δ* predict the observed feedback-budget thresholds (m=10k vs 20k in DoorKey; m=1 vs 16 in Hartmann-6), or are those governed by factors the metric model ignores?
?
How does the framework extend to sequences of interacting perturbations — multi-turn tool use, curricula, multi-agent feedback — which Section 5 explicitly excludes?
?
Does the Prop 5 critique imply that standard "information gain" exploration bonuses in active learning and agent design are systematically miscalibrated for certifying useful escape?
?
Would pre-registering probe states in production agent loops (RAG, tool calling) reveal governing-structure shifts, or are such shifts empirically rare vs. pure state perturbation?

The individual theorems are classical (Foster–Lyapunov drift, Pinsker, Bellman γ-contraction). The real contribution is the operational packaging: pre-registered probe discrepancy, matched baselines, and pre-specified escape events. A methodological, not mathematical, advance — which the authors concede.

Sharpest Red Flags

  • "Structural change" — the framework's most distinctive lever — is never empirically demonstrated.
  • Tiny effective n in code repair; screening bias acknowledged.
  • Contraction assumption (Asm 1) "not independently verified."
  • Prop 2 only bounds exploration pressure, not posterior contraction or regret.
