§01Methodology — one core, one varied axis

Muse (MUon under Structured rEpresentations) keeps the Muon pipeline fixed — same momentum rule, same scaling, same Newton–Schulz backend — and varies only the matrixization fed to the polar map. For a block G∈R^{m×n} with d=mn, it sweeps flat row-major reshapes T_p(G)∈R^{p×(d/p)} for every divisor p|d: native, nearest-square, skinny-θ ∈ {1/8,1/4,1/2,3/4}, and the vector endpoint p=1 that provably collapses to nSGDM.

  • Thm 3.1 · spectral descentEach representation yields the steepest-descent direction over the spectral ball in represented coords; one-step decrease −η‖T(G)‖_* + (Lη/2)rank(T(G)).
  • Thm 3.2 · stationarityUnified Õ(K^{-1/4}) nonconvex rate whose constants depend on T only through the short side p_T — equal-short-side reps share worst-case bounds.
  • Thm 3.3 / 3.5 · geometry of the gapBlock-Gershgorin curvature-window collapse + Marchenko–Pastur profile orders early dissipation by the shape factor β(γ)·γ^{1/4}, explaining the Muon–nSGDM separation.

§02Results & highlights

  • LLaMA2-600M (12.58B tok): nearest-Square within 0.003 val loss of Native; short-side cuts climb a loss ladder; Vector cleanly separated from the polar family.
  • LLaMA2-130M (2.60B tok): same ordering across the full LR sweep; Native/Square at the low-loss end, skinny-θ climb as θ shrinks, Vector separated; AdamW included as a coordinate-wise reference.
  • Teacher–student (Fig 3): shrinking short side shifts the selected LR upward (Native/Square at 5e-05 vs Sk-12.5/Vector at 2.5e-04); depth-dependent separation matches the β(γ)γ^{1/4} prediction.
  • Analytical gap: square-to-vector leading dissipation ratio is Θ(d^{1/4}) with β(1)=8/(3π).
  • Module-level: nuclear-support separation localizes across Attn QKV/out and MLP W1/W2/proj — granularity beyond scalar loss.

§03Strengths

  • Clean minimal design: one shared optimizer core, one varied axis, three seeds, two scales, plus a controlled sandbox — ordering reproduces everywhere.
  • Theory–empirics tightly coupled: the β(γ)γ^{1/4} prediction maps onto the LR-calibration shift and depth-dependent loss separation.
  • Vector → nSGDM reduction gives a principled, non-folklore explanation of the Muon-vs-normalized-momentum gap via nuclear-norm support.
  • Module-level diagnostics add granularity beyond scalar loss curves.

§04Limitations

  • Dissipation analysis is local: fixed ReLU masks, single-cell trajectories, MP baseline, asymptotic limits; authors concede β₁ can turn negative in real training.
  • No variant beats Native — practical takeaway is "native is fine, balanced is harmless, don't shrink the short side."
  • No code/checkpoint release; 600M reproduction needs 8×910C NPUs.
  • Interactions with Shampoo/K-FAC/SOAP, adaptive representation selection, and negative-curvature regimes all deferred.

§05Open questions for further exploration

  • With curvature preconditioners? Does the ordering persist or invert when Muon meets Shampoo/K-FAC/SOAP, which already reshape spectral geometry?
  • Adaptive per-block selection? Can a live-spectral-statistics selector recover Native performance while cutting Newton–Schulz cost on very tall/wide blocks?
  • Shifting masks / negative curvature? How does the picture change for GELU/SwiGLU or when β₁<0 breaks the scalar Frobenius-quadratic reduction?
  • Scale wash-out? Is the β(γ)γ^{1/4} ordering observable at multi-billion-parameter scale, or does it vanish once the native short side is already large?
  • Efficiency knob? Could the skinny-θ ladder become a cheap approximation (fewer singular channels) for non-critical layers under a controlled loss budget?
