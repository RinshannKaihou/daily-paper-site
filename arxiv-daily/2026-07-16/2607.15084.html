Quantifying Training Membership Information in the Hyperspherical Embedding Geometry of Face Recognition Models

Ünsal Öztürk · Sébastien Marcel · Idiap Research Institute & Université de Lausanne · 2026-07-16

Key
Finding

Training-identity count dominates the face-embedding membership signal; backbone and loss head are negligible — and cross-domain benchmarks inflate the leakage.

Across 180 fully-crossed open-set FR models. Only adding more training identities substantially erodes the geometric membership trace.

Why This Matters 02 / 03

Privacy audits of face-embedding models must use a same-domain held-out reference — or they will silently over-state leakage.

Cross-domain non-member pools conflate domain shift (pose, quality, demographics) with the training signal, with direct implications for GDPR right-to-erasure compliance checks.
Critical Edge 03 / 03

AUC saturates near 1.0 on cross-domain benchmarks even at nids=all — inflating apparent leakage — and the entire study rests on a single training source (WebFace4M), so cross-dataset generalisation is untested.

Authors flag this themselves; η² values are relative, not absolute — several ANOVA residual cells are near-zero.

§1Methodology

An auditor queries an FR model f with k≥2 probe images of an identity and obtains ℓ₂-normalised 512-d embeddings. From each cluster, four per-identity geometric statistics are computed; a swept threshold separates members from non-members, summarised by a threshold-free classifier AUC.

  • PairCos — mean pairwise cosine similarity within the cluster (black-box).
  • vMF-κ — von Mises-Fisher concentration, closed-form Banerjee estimator, d=512 (black-box).
  • PenLogit — mean margin-penalised logit under leave-one-out prototype (grey-box: needs s, m).
  • ProtoCE — surrogate training loss vs other eval identities (grey-box).
  • Factorial grid — 3 backbones × 3 angular-margin heads × 5 nids (1K–all≈206K) × 4 epochs (5–40) = 180 configs, trained from scratch on WebFace4M (InsightFace 112², SGD, FP16). ViT-T/CosFace added for cross-arch replication.
  • 9 benchmarks — LFW, CPLFW, CFP-FF/CFP-FP, AgeDB-30, XQLFW, RFW, IJB-C + a same-domain held-out WebFace4M split.

§2Results & Highlights

  • nids dominates — η²≥.95 for all four statistics; Epoch ≥.89; Backbone ≤.09; Loss ≤.04.
  • Cross-domain inflation — CFP-FP, CPLFW, LFW, XQLAW reach AUC≈1.00 even at nids=all, while same-domain signal drops to 0.71.
  • Pose confound ≈ training signal — CFP-FP−CFP-FF: d′ up to −2.98; probit +0.26 AUC, vs +0.21 training contribution.
  • Quality confound — XQLFW−LFW: probit +0.22 AUC.
  • Fusion helps in weak-signal regime — at nids=100K: 0.71–0.73 → 0.79–0.80; Wilcoxon W=666, p<10⁻¹⁰.
  • EER context — LFW EER 8.5%→0.3% across nids; membership separability persists at competitive verification accuracy.

+Strengths

  • Largest study in-niche — 180 fully-crossed models vs ≤9 previously; real variance attribution.
  • Same-domain held-out reference is a substantive methodological contribution isolating training signal from domain shift — uncontrolled by prior embedding-MIA work.
  • Auditable reporting — ANOVA, marginal means, per-statistic confound sizes (d′, ρ, ε²) rather than rhetoric.
  • Identity-matched pairs (CFP-FF↔CFP-FP, LFW↔XQLFW) = clean pose/quality controlled experiments.
  • Actionable guidance for privacy audits + ViT-T cross-architecture replication.

−Limitations

  • Single training source — only WebFace4M; large alternatives retracted (MS-1M), so generalisation is untested.
  • No defences evaluated — DP-SGD, distillation, unlearning absent; reducibility of the signal unknown.
  • Uncontrolled probe count k varies across benchmarks, biasing cluster-statistic estimates.
  • Random partitioning — real membership boundaries may correlate with ethnicity/quality, shifting the baseline.
  • Lower-bound measurement — richer classifiers on raw embeddings could exceed the MLP fusion ceiling.
  • Gaussianity assumed in the probit translation (asserted, not tested); no code/checkpoint release stated.

§3Open Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does nids/epoch dominance transfer to other angular-margin training sets and non-WebFace architectures (e.g. transformers with face losses), given dataset retractions blocked this test?
  • Would DP-SGD or knowledge-distillation reduce the geometric signal without unacceptable EER degradation — and at what privacy budget does same-domain AUC hit chance?
  • Can a classifier on the raw 512-d embeddings substantially exceed the four-statistic MLP fusion ceiling, and by how much across nids?
  • How do the pose (+0.26 AUC) and quality (+0.22) confounds change when the auditor curates a same-pose, same-quality probe set rather than using off-the-shelf benchmarks?
  • Does the signal persist with only one enrolled embedding per identity (the common deployment case), instead of k≥2 probes?
  • Are the cross-ethnicity ProtoCE differences (ε²=0.123) a denominator artefact or a genuine demographic bias in how angular-margin loss compresses subpopulations?
