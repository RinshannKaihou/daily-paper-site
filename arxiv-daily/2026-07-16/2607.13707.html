Why This Matters

Methodology A/B controlled · ~17,800 cached LLM calls

An A/B comparison of a corrupted vs corrected generation pipeline applied to identical source contexts, with a perturbation-based positive control.

  • A·Corrupted: generation & judging share one max_tokens=5 budget — enough for a judge's "A/B" but truncating paragraph-length generations.
  • B·Corrected: budgets split (300 generate / 5 judge), Gemini thinking disabled, openers forbidden, caches namespaced.
  • 3·Judges: DeepSeek-V4-Flash, gemini-3.5-flash (thinking off), glm-5.2 (z.ai). Two judges double as producers.
  • 4·Data: Turkish from wiki40b (first 80 words); English verbatim from HaluEval QA. Four measured biases: Style, Position, Selection, Factual.
  • 5·Positive control: 50 numeral sentences, replace-first-numeral perturbation, 30% Bernoulli no-op (seed 42) — oracle = string comparison.
  • 6·Reproducibility: cached log + 5 scripts on OSF (10.17605/OSF.IO/RS2B8); two audits and the control need zero API calls.

The artifact. On N=500 Turkish items, GLM's selection accuracy on DeepSeek hallucinations read ~0.64 vs 0.96 on English HaluEval — a 32-point gap replicating from N=50 (0.54) and surviving a producer-swap (0.61 vs 1.00). None of it was real.

What reading revealed. 81.0% of DeepSeek-Turkish items began with truncated filler ("Tabii ki, iş…"), 12.2% copied the factual field verbatim, 6.8% were degenerate fragments. No item was a well-formed hallucination.

Recovery under the fix. Generation validity 0.0% → 98.8%; GLM selection 0.61 → 1.00; GLM factual 0.845 → 0.99; three-judge majority factual 0.962 → 1.00. No cross-lingual, producer-specific, or self-referential effect survived.

Style bias is real but distorted. Stratified by factual length, DeepSeek's style score flips sign across 0.5 (z≈8.7); the apparent TR/EN invariance was a length invariance (corrupt TR 2.1w ≈ HaluEval 2.2w).

Oracle asymmetry (§4.3). The injected no-op fault (30% Bernoulli, realized 40%) was caught at 50/50 accuracy by a gold-to-negative string comparison — the same fault class that escaped four rounds of robustness checking in the oracle-less design.

Protocol (§5): manually read 15–20 raw items per condition before any aggregate statistic. Sample formula n ≥ ln(1−C)/ln(1−p) → at C=99%, n=7 (50%), n=13 (30%), n=21 (20%). 15 items clear 99% confidence; 20 reach ~99.9%.

Core Contributions 5 moves

  • 1·Existence proof: one stimulus-level fault survived four aggregate-robustness checks and was exposed only by manual reading.
  • 2·Positive control: the same fault class is caught for free in an oracle-bearing (perturbation) corpus.
  • 3·Validation protocol: 15–20 items, two degeneration rates, exclude >single-digit rates before statistics.
  • 4·Two-mode taxonomy: disappearance (fabricated) vs. distortion (real, magnitude/sign tracks length) — aggregates can't tell them apart.
  • 5·Structural reframing via the test-oracle problem (Barr 2015) and metamorphic testing (Chen 1998).

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Brutal honesty: centers on its own error, publishes the false first-draft lead.
  • Causal A/B: same contexts, only pipeline differs → unambiguous attribution.
  • Elegant control: injects the fault's mechanical twin and catches it for free.
  • Worked protocol with explicit sample-size formula, not an asserted universal.
  • SE grounding gives reviewers a vocabulary instead of a bespoke taxonomy.

Limitations

  • Single instance: one fault type, one pipeline — a single-instance generalization.
  • Small corrected sample (N=80 at ceiling 1.00) — thin variance.
  • Binary task only: propagation to reference-free preference scoring unaddressed.
  • HaluEval un-audited under the paper's own protocol — corrected claim is Turkish-only.
  • Vendor-API dependence — aliases must hold stable (the very drift it warns of).
  • No census of affected corpora (CalibJudge named, unverified).

Open Questions for further work

  • Q1.Does the oracle asymmetry reproduce across fault classes (paraphrase-collapse, entity-swap, negation-as-no-op) and beyond the 50-item toy corpus?
  • Q2.How does the fault class propagate into reference-free pairwise scoring (MT-Bench-style) with no gold answer?
  • Q3.Would applying §5 to HaluEval item-by-item surface an undetected stimulus-level issue?
  • Q4.Can the manual check be partially automated (LLM-as-degeneration-detector) without re-introducing oracle-less blindness?
  • Q5.Does panel aggregation (PoLL) surface or amplify stimulus-level masking, per Ma et al. (2025)?
