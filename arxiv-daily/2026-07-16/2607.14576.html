Sharp Stability Threshold and Certification for Designing Stable Residual Architectures

Hyemin Gu, Michael Tyrrell (UMass Amherst · SRI International)  ·  Tuhin Sahai (SRI International)  ·  Markos A. Katsoulakis (UMass Amherst)

Key finding

q = 1 is a sharp, mathematically necessary threshold for residual-net training stability — not a heuristic.

Key metric

blow-ups across all 288 seed-level runs for the four q ≤ 1 Mamba variants — including a normalization-free, parameter-free linear-growth block (q: 5 → 1).

A provable, unified explanation for why LayerNorm/RMSNorm stabilize training — and a certifiable design tool that turns architecture-search into table lookup.

Direct value for Mamba/SSM, Transformer architecture design, and time-series forecasting.

Critical edge

Theorems prove non-explosion & well-posedness — not convergence to low loss. A stable-but-useless optimum is consistent with every result here.

01

Methodology — four layers, theory-first

1 · ODE view (§2). A residual stack Xk+1 = Xk + Δt·v(Xk;θk) is the forward-Euler discretization of dX/dt = v(X,t). Classical sublinear-growth condition ‖v(x,t)‖ ≤ c‖x‖q + b: Grönwall gives global existence at q ≤ 1; ṙ = crq blows up in finite time at q > 1.

2 · Optimal-control view (§3). Training is cast as OC over Uadq(X) = {v : ‖v‖ ≤ c‖X‖q + b}. The Hamiltonian maximizes pointwise → a closed-form bang-bang optimum (Thm 3.1) that saturates the boundary, so at q > 1 it must pick a blow-up-prone field. HJB well-posedness via Crandall–Lions comparison (continuous) and monotone Bellman (discrete).

3 · Architecture certification (§4). Each primitive gets an input-magnitude exponent (Table 1). Five operations propagate exponents (Thm 4.2): composition → q₁·q₂, sum → max, Hadamard → q₁+q₂, residual wrap → max(1,q), normalization → q = 0. Certification collapses to table lookup. (Scope: additive residual updates only — Post-LN explicitly excluded.)

4 · Experiments (§5). TSF on ETTm1 (6 seeds/cell) & Weather (3 seeds/cell); input 96; horizons {96,192,336,720}; depths L ∈ {3,8}; ≤ 2M params. Two backbones: Mamba (q = 5 unnormalized) vs. PatchTST (q = 1 by construction). Metrics: blow-up counts + signed % deviation from per-cell median.

02

Results — blow-up rate tracks q exactly

  • q ≤ 1 Mamba variants: 0 blow-ups in 288 runs. RMSNorm baseline, Pre-LN, Peri-LN, and the normalization-free linear-growth mod all stable across 64 cells.
  • Free-velocity q = 5 Mamba: 48/72 divergent, failure rising sharply with depth — 24/24 ETTm1 @ L=8, 10/12 Weather @ L=8. Two runs hit MSE > 1012.
  • PatchTST stable even without normalization across all 288 runs — its primitives compose to q = 1. Confirms it's the exponent, not the presence of a norm layer, that governs stability.
  • Accuracy invariant to how q ≤ 1 is reached. Four q ≤ 1 Mamba variants cluster within ≤ 1.0% (Weather) / ≤ 1.5% (ETTm1) of cell median; linear-growth mod is neither better nor worse than normalized variants.
  • Linear-growth Mamba mod reduces q: 5 → 1 with zero extra parameters and zero norm layers — L2-normalize the selective-parameter path + replace SiLU gate x·σ(x) with sigmoid σ(x).
  • Hardware depth ceiling even at q = 1 (Remark 3.6): factor (1+cΔt)D exceeds FP16 max at D = 17 and FP32/BF16 range at D = 128.
03

Strengths & Limitations — side by side

Strengths

  • Unifying, parameter-free, architecture-agnostic criterion (q ≤ 1) backed by two independent arguments of different character — more convincing than either alone.
  • Exponent arithmetic (Thm 4.2) + Table 1 convert theory into a practical design tool; concretely demonstrated by the parameter-free Mamba mod.
  • Well-designed dissociation: Mamba (q = 5) vs. PatchTST (q = 1) cleanly isolates the exponent from the normalization layer.
  • Honest about scope — depth ceiling, upper-bound nature, Post-LN excluded, strict-interior q ∈ (0,1) flagged as open.
  • Full proofs (not sketches) for central results; clean theorem-structured exposition.

Limitations

  • Validation is shallow & narrow: L ∈ {3,8}, ≤ 2M params, two TSF benchmarks only. No LM, no vision, no LLM-scale depth.
  • Absolute MSE never reported — only median-relative %, blocking comparison to published Mamba/PatchTST.
  • Post-LN excluded — a major deployed configuration remains uncovered.
  • No primitive realizes q ∈ (0,1) — the sharper polynomial-in-D bound is currently unused.
  • Theory/practice gap: bang-bang OC optimum is never actually reached by Adam/SGD; argument is about the admissible class, not real trajectories.
  • Hurwitz assumption on Mamba's A needed to stay inside 𝒫; empirical validity during training unverified.
  • No code / hyperparameter table released — 288-run protocol not checkable.

05

Open questions

  1. Does the linear-growth Mamba mod scale to LM benchmarks (wiki-text, common-crawl) at depth ≥ 64, params ≥ 100M — or does Remark 3.6's exponential ceiling force normalization back in?
  2. Can a strict-interior q ∈ (0,1) primitive be built (Remark 4.5) that inherits the polynomial-in-D bound without sacrificing expressivity?
  3. How does the criterion interact with Post-LN (excluded here)? Extending Thm 4.2 to Xk+1 = LN(Xk + Δt f(Xk)) would cover a major gap.
  4. Is the bang-bang OC optimum ever approached by Adam/SGD in practice, or does finite LR + regularization keep velocities strictly interior — making "necessary" a worst-case statement, not an empirical risk?
  5. Can exponent arithmetic extend to discontinuous primitives (VQ, MoE routing) via straight-through / Gumbel-softmax surrogates?
  6. Absolute MSE per cell? Does the median-relative reporting hide a real regression vs. published Mamba/PatchTST baselines?
