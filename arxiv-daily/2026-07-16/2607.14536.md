> **一句话总结：** 提出 Muse，把 Muon 极坐标更新所作用的"矩阵表示"显式当作优化器几何的一个设计轴，在共享同一动量规则与 Newton–Schulz 后端的前提下，沿 native / nearest-square / skinny / vector 四种 Frobenius 等距表示构造一族优化器，理论与 LLaMA2-130M/600M 实验一致地表明：短边越小的表示其核范数支撑越弱、越接近归一化动量（nSGDM），平衡的非原生表示可追平原生 Muon。

> **TL;DR:** Muse reframes the matrix representation fed to Muon's polar map as a genuine optimizer-geometry choice, sharing one momentum rule and Newton–Schulz backend across native, nearest-square, skinny, and vector Frobenius-isometric representations. Theory shows the represented short side controls polar rank, nuclear-norm support, and stochastic stationarity constants, and a teacher–student curvature-collapse model plus Marchenko–Pastur spectrum order early dissipation by β(γ)γ^{1/4}. In LLaMA2-130M and LLaMA2-600M pretraining (3 seeds), balanced non-native (nearest-Square) representations match Native within 0.003 validation loss at 600M, while reducing the short side forms a higher-loss ladder and the Vector endpoint collapses to nSGDM, cleanly separated from the polar family.

## 核心贡献

- 将"喂给极坐标后端的矩阵化"显式建模为优化器几何的设计维度：在保留 Muon 动量规则、缩放约定与 Newton–Schulz 后端不变的前提下，定义一族 Frobenius 等距表示（native、nearest-square、skinny-θ∈{1/8,1/4,1/2,3/4}、vector），其中 vector 端点退化为归一化 SGD with momentum (nSGDM)。
- 证明每个固定结构化表示都诱导一个谱/核范数下的极坐标最速下降几何，并建立由短边 p_T 决定的秩界、拉回方向 Frobenius 范数界与核范数支撑上界；给出统一的随机非凸平稳性保证，对所有表示均给出 Õ(K^{-1/4}) 率，且常数只通过短边 p_T 依赖表示。
- 在受控 teacher–student 模型中，证明固定掩码 W 块 Hessian 在大类/大样本极限下出现曲率窗口塌缩（块-Gershgorin 端点 β_1>0 且相对窗宽 ρ→0），结合 Marchenko–Pastur 谱轮廓，导出早期归一化耗散由形状因子 β(γ)γ^{1/4} 排序——矩阵表示比 vector 端点保留更多核范数支撑，从而给出 Muon–nSGDM 分离的几何解释。
- 在 LLaMA2-130M（2.60B token，4×RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell）和 LLaMA2-600M（12.58B token，8×910C NPU）上进行表示轴的尺度化检验，并用固定动量诊断（核范数支撑、native 方向不一致度、90% 奇异通道数）在层/模块级别定位同一排序。

## Key Contributions

- Reformulates the matrixization fed to Muon's polar map as an explicit optimizer-geometry axis, defining a Frobenius-isometric representation family (native, nearest-square, skinny-θ, vector) that shares the momentum rule, scaling convention, and Newton–Schulz backend; the vector endpoint provably collapses to normalized SGD with momentum (nSGDM).
- Proves that every fixed structured representation induces a polar steepest-descent direction under spectral/nuclear-norm geometry (⟨G,D_T(G)⟩_F = ‖T(G)‖_*), with rank, pulled-back Frobenius norm, and nuclear-support bounds all governed by the represented short side p_T, plus a unified Õ(K^{-1/4}) stochastic nonconvex stationarity guarantee that depends on T only through p_T.
- In a controlled teacher–student model, shows block-Gershgorin curvature-window collapse (β_1>0 and relative window ρ→0 as classes/samples grow) plus a Marchenko–Pastur spectral profile, ordering early normalized dissipation by the shape factor β(γ)γ^{1/4}; this geometrically explains the observed Muon–nSGDM performance gap.
- Validates the same representation-axis ordering at language-model scale with LLaMA2-130M and LLaMA2-600M pretraining and with fixed-momentum diagnostics (represented nuclear support, native-direction disagreement, 90% singular-channel count) localized across layers and modules.

## Methodology

Muse (MUon under Structured rEpresentations) keeps the Muon update pipeline fixed and varies only the matrixization supplied to the polar map. For a parameter block G∈R^{m×n} with d=mn and short side s=min(m,n), the authors consider, alongside the native Muon matrixization, flat row-major representations T_p(G)=R_{p,d/p}vec(G)∈R^{p×(d/p)} for every divisor p|d with p≤d/p. These include: (i) native Muon, (ii) nearest-square (largest admissible short side ≤√d), (iii) skinny-θ for θ∈{1/8,1/4,1/2,3/4} (short side near θ·s), and (iv) the vector endpoint p=1, which collapses the polar map to normalized SGD with momentum. The pulled-back polar direction is D_T(Z)=T^{-1}Orth(T(Z)); Algorithm 1 then runs M_t=µM_{t-1}+(1-µ)Ĝ_t, X_{t+1}=X_t−η_t D_T(M_t), sharing state and Newton–Schulz backend across all variants.

Theory has three parts. Section 3.1 (Theorem 3.1) establishes that each representation yields the steepest-descent direction over the spectral ball in represented coordinates, with one-step decrease −η‖T(G)‖_* + (Lη/2)rank(T(G)). Section 3.2 (Theorem 3.2) gives a nonconvex stationarity guarantee under L-smoothness, unbiased bounded-variance gradients, and decaying schedules η_t=ηt^{-3/4}, α_t=1−αt^{-1/2}; the bound depends on T only through p_T, so equal-short-side representations share worst-case constants and the Õ(K^{-1/4}) rate. Section 3.3 builds a teacher–student mechanism: for a fixed-mask W-block ReLU loss with teacher outputs V_{⋆,C}, the empirical block Hessian's block-Gershgorin endpoints β_1,β_2 collapse to a scalar Frobenius quadratic (Theorem 3.3: β_1>0 w.h.p., ρ→0 as C,N→∞), making the gradient approximately radial; under a Marchenko–Pastur singular-value profile for T(E_0), Theorem 3.5 shows the leading early normalized dissipation scales as σ_0 d^{1/4}‖T(E_0)‖_* / ‖T(E_0)‖_F² → β(γ_T)γ_T^{1/4}, so representations with larger β(γ)γ^{1/4} dissipate faster (and need smaller LRs). The square-to-vector leading-order ratio is Θ(d^{1/4}) under Gaussian/MP normalization (β(1)=8/(3π)).

Empirically, LLaMA2-130M (2.60B tokens) and LLaMA2-600M (12.58B tokens) are pretrained, sweeping the matrix-update learning rate per representation with all losses/trajectories averaged over three random seeds; the 130M sweep includes AdamW as a coordinate-wise adaptive reference. Fixed-momentum diagnostics re-apply alternative representations to saved momentum blocks and measure represented nuclear support, native-direction disagreement, and the number of singular channels carrying 90% of the nuclear mass.

## Results & Highlights

- On LLaMA2-600M (12.58B tokens), the nearest-Square representation is within 0.003 validation loss of Native Muon, while short-side reductions form a higher-loss ladder and the Vector (nSGDM) endpoint is clearly separated from the polar family — confirming that balanced non-native reshaping matches the native layout but short-side reduction does not.
- On LLaMA2-130M (2.60B tokens, Figure 4 top row), the same ordering holds across the full learning-rate sweep: Native and Square sit at the low-loss end of the polar family, skinny-θ variants climb the loss ladder as θ shrinks, Vector is separated, and AdamW is included as a coordinate-wise adaptive reference.
- In the controlled teacher–student single-layer sweep (Figure 3a, (m,n,C)=(96,192,768)), reducing the short side shifts the selected learning rate upward (e.g., Native/Square selected at 5e-05 versus Sk-12.5/Vector at 2.5e-04), and the tuned best-final-loss separation across hidden depth grows with depth (Figure 3b/c) — matching the β(γ)γ^{1/4} dissipation prediction.
- The square-to-vector leading-order dissipation ratio is Θ(d^{1/4}) under standard Gaussian/MP normalization, with β(1)=8/(3π); this is the analytical source of the Muon-versus-nSGDM gap.
- The stochastic stationarity bound (Theorem 3.2) yields an Õ(K^{-1/4}) rate for every fixed representation, with constants depending on T only through the short side p_T, so equal-short-side representations provably share worst-case constants.

## Critical Assessment

- Novelty: The reframing is genuine and under-explored. Most cited Muon variants (Chen et al. 2025, Pethick et al. 2025, Lau et al. 2025, Shulgin et al. 2025, etc.) retain the native matrixization; isolating the representation as a controlled axis and connecting it to polar rank/nuclear support is a clean conceptual contribution rather than yet another Muon tweak.
- Rigor: The descent identity (Thm 3.1) and stationarity bound (Thm 3.2) are standard but correctly assembled; the curvature-collapse mechanism (Thm 3.3) rests on a fixed-mask teacher–student ReLU model with strong asymptotic assumptions (Assumptions 3.1–3.3: incoherent teacher outputs, MP spectral profile, single-cell trajectories). The MP profile is explicitly acknowledged as only an "isotropic spectral baseline," so the β(γ)γ^{1/4} ordering is a local, model-dependent prediction rather than a universal law.
- Reproducibility: Algorithm 1 is fully specified; representation constructors (nearest-square, skinny-θ) are simple and implementation-cheap (a reshape + the existing Newton–Schulz backend). However, the paper does not release code or checkpoints, exact per-module checkpoint grids live only in the appendix figures, and 600M runs require 8×910C NPUs (a less common device), which raises the hardware barrier to reproduction.
- Significance: The practical headline ("balanced non-native reshaping matches Native; Vector collapses to nSGDM") is conceptually useful for understanding why Muon beats normalized momentum, but the practical upside is modest — Square tracking Native within 0.003 validation loss is a null result for practitioners (it says "don't bother changing the representation"), and no variant beats Native, so Muse is more an explanatory axis than a new SOTA optimizer.
- Red flags: The teacher–student model fixes ReLU masks and restricts trajectories to one empirical cell; the authors concede β_1,C,N can go negative under real activation-pattern changes and cross-layer coupling, so the curvature-collapse story may not transfer cleanly to deep Transformer training. Interactions with curvature preconditioners (Shampoo, K-FAC, SOAP) and adaptive representation selection are explicitly deferred, leaving the main mechanism tested only in a local regime.

## Strengths

- Clean, minimal experimental design: one shared optimizer core, one varied axis, three seeds, two model scales, plus a controlled teacher–student sandbox — the representation-axis ordering is reproducible across all of them.
- Theory and empirics are tightly coupled: the β(γ)γ^{1/4} prediction from Thm 3.5 maps directly onto the LR-calibration shift and depth-dependent loss separation in Figure 3.
- The vector-endpoint reduction to nSGDM gives a principled, non-hand-wavy explanation of the Muon-vs-normalized-momentum gap via nuclear-norm support rather than folklore.
- Module-level diagnostics (Figures 5, 10–13) localize the nuclear-support separation across Attn QKV/out, MLP W1/W2/proj, adding granularity beyond scalar loss curves.

## Limitations

- The dissipation analysis is local: fixed ReLU masks, single-cell trajectories, MP spectral baseline, and asymptotic (C,N→∞ then d→∞) limits; the authors themselves note β_1 may turn negative in real nonconvex training.
- No variant surpasses Native Muon; the practical takeaway for practitioners is essentially "the native layout is fine, balanced reshaping is harmless, don't shrink the short side" — useful as explanation but not as a new optimizer to adopt.
- No code/checkpoint release stated; 600M reproduction needs 8×910C NPUs.
- Joint treatment with curvature preconditioners (Shampoo/K-FAC/SOAP), adaptive representation selection, and negative-curvature regimes is left to future work.

## Relevance

对研究 LLM 训练优化器（尤其 Muon 类极坐标优化器）的几何与收敛理论高度相关：它把"矩阵表示"从一个隐含实现细节提升为可分析、可控的优化器几何维度，并给出 Muon 优于归一化动量的核范数支撑解释，可作为优化器设计/消融的理论参考框架。若关注点偏向下游应用或非优化器方向，相关性较低。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the representation-axis ordering persist (or invert) once Muon is combined with curvature preconditioners such as Shampoo/K-FAC/SOAP, where the preconditioner already reshapes the spectral geometry?
- Can an adaptive, per-block representation selector (choosing short side from live spectral statistics) recover Native performance while cutting Newton–Schulz cost on very tall/wide blocks?
- How does the picture change for activations with shifting masks (GELU/SwiGLU) or under negative curvature (β_1<0), where the scalar Frobenius-quadratic reduction breaks down?
- Is the β(γ)γ^{1/4} dissipation ordering observable in much larger (e.g., multi-billion-parameter) pretraining, or does it wash out once the native short side is already large?
- Could the skinny-θ ladder be useful as a cheap approximation (fewer Newton–Schulz singular channels) for non-critical layers with a controlled loss budget, turning Muse from an explanatory axis into a practical efficiency knob?
