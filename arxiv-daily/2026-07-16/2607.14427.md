> **一句话总结：** 本文直接测量了一个深度循环 Transformer（135M 级，FineWeb-Edu 训练）的逐 token 循环状态，发现其收敛到逐 token 不动点；作者进一步证明，一个零参数的"输出稳定即停"规则可在 4.94 平均循环数下达到均匀 depth-8 质量（平均深度降低 38%），而用同一模型蒸馏标签训练的线性 router 反而需要接近满深度、毫无节省。

> **TL;DR:** On a randomized-depth, weight-tied 135M-class transformer trained on FineWeb-Edu, the recurrent state per token converges to a fixed point (mean successive-output KL drops from 3.9e-1 at loop 2 to 8.5e-6 at loop 16). The convergence depth varies by token (median converges by loop 6, ~10% still moving at the training-mean depth of 8). A training-free convergence-exit rule (A1) reaches uniform depth-8 validation loss (3.189 nats) at 4.94 average loops — a 38% reduction in average depth — and matches uniform depth across the whole range, whereas a linear router (A2) trained on convergence labels harvested from the same model needs 7.99 average loops and yields no saving. The whole study runs on one RTX 4090 in ~100 GPU-hours.

## 核心贡献

- **首次逐 token 直接刻画深度循环语言模型的不动点收敛结构**：在 S1（FineWeb-Edu，12.9B tokens，29M 唯一非嵌入参数）上，平均 successive-output KL 从 loop 2 的 3.9×10⁻¹ 平滑衰减到 loop 16 的 8.5×10⁻⁶，状态范数变化同步衰减；这是循环计算"真正结束"而非"空转"的直接证据。
- **揭示了跨 token 的收敛深度异质性**：中位 token 在 loop 6 即收敛，但约 10% 的 token 在训练均值深度 8 处仍在更新；平均收敛深度按 token 类型排序（空白最浅、内容词最深），且不带任何学习式 halting 即复现了 ACT 以来的经典规律。
- **证明"读"收敛行为胜过"学"预测它**：零参数的 convergence-exit（A1）在 4.94 平均循环数即可达到 depth-8 质量（38% 的平均深度节省），并在浅深度端反超均匀 depth；而用同一冻结 backbone 蒸馏标签训练的线性 router（A2）需要 7.99 平均循环数，毫无节省。该结论在 S0/S1 两个 scale 均复现。
- **提出并部分验证了"训练均值决定饱和深度"的假设**：mean-4 模型在 depth 4 饱和、mean-8 模型在 depth 8 饱和，并与 Huginn（mean 32，在 32 及以上有用）一致，将饱和深度定位为可由训练循环分布主动选择的设计杠杆。
- **诚实的作用域边界**：明确声明不做 FLOP-matched 与稠密 transformer 的 parity、不做端到端 wall-clock 加速（仅给出三点 bracket：38% 平均深度、~1.5× 最佳 wall-clock、0% 实测加速），并将所有分配结果限定在单一 scale、单一种子。

## Key Contributions

- **First direct per-token characterization of fixed-point convergence in a depth-recurrent LM.** On S1 (FineWeb-Edu, 12.9B tokens, 29M unique non-embedding params, SmolLM2-135M-shaped), mean successive-output KL between loops decays from 3.9e-1 at loop 2 to 8.5e-6 at loop 16, with state-change norm decaying in lockstep. This shows the loop has genuinely finished computing rather than spinning.
- **Per-token convergence heterogeneity, not learned but emergent.** The median token converges by loop 6, while ~10% are still updating at the training-mean depth of 8. Mean exit depth orders by token type (whitespace 3.58, numeral 3.81, punctuation 4.16, word 4.25), reproducing the structural-tokens-shallow / content-tokens-deep pattern from the ACT literature without any learned halting unit.
- **Reading the convergence signal beats learning to predict it.** A training-free convergence-exit policy A1 reaches the uniform depth-8 quality bar (3.189 nats) at 4.94 average loops (a 38% reduction in average depth) and matches or exceeds uniform depth across the range, with the clearest margin at shallow depths (avg depth 3: 3.231 vs uniform 3.272). The trained linear router A2 on the same frozen backbone needs 7.99 average loops and yields no reduction, reproduced at both S0 and S1.
- **A hypothesis that saturation depth tracks the training-time loop distribution.** The mean-4 model saturates at depth 4, the mean-8 model at depth 8, and Huginn (mean 32) reports useful depth to 32 and beyond — two internal points plus one external consistency check. If it generalizes, saturation depth (and thus the width of the quality–compute product range) becomes a practitioner-chosen design lever via the training loop distribution.
- **Clean separation of robustness and allocation.** Depth robustness is a pretraining property obtained with a minimal recipe (per-step randomized recursion count, plain cross-entropy, no halting unit / ponder loss / FLOP penalty); depth allocation is then a post-hoc, replaceable policy on the frozen backbone, enabling fair comparison among policies because the backbone never changes.

## Methodology

**Architecture.** Three parts: a unique-layer prelude that reads the input into latent space, a weight-tied core applied a variable number of times, and a unique-layer coda feeding the LM head. Llama-style blocks: RMSNorm, SwiGLU, rotary position embeddings, grouped-query attention, tied input/output embeddings, no biases. The recurrence follows Geiping et al. (Huginn), with three failure-preventing design choices: (1) recurrent state initialized from noise `s0 ~ N(0, 2/5)` rather than from the embedding, pushing the core toward an input-conditioned fixed point instead of memorizing an iteration count; (2) input re-injection each loop via concatenation `s_{i+1} = core(adapter([e, s_i]))` with a single 2d→d linear adapter, preventing drift away from the input; (3) sandwich normalization (post-norm inside each residual branch in addition to the pre-norm) to fix hidden-state collapse in looped training.

**Training.** Per optimizer step, sample one recursion count `r ~ round(LogNormal(log r̄, 0.5))` clamped to [1, r_max], applied to the whole step; loss is plain cross-entropy at the final unrolled state — no halting unit, no ponder loss, no FLOP penalty (the paper motivates this explicitly by the collapse/tuning pathologies of ACT, PonderNet, and Csordás et al.). Truncated BPTT through only the last k iterations to bound activation memory; earlier iterations run under no-grad. S0 uses r̄=4, r_max=16, k=4; S1 uses r̄=8, r_max=32, k=8. AdamW (0.9, 0.95), weight decay 0.1 on matrices only, grad clip 1.0, cosine schedule with 1% warmup run to completion; all headline numbers come from the fully annealed final checkpoint.

**Scales.** S0 (toy): 12.9M unique non-embedding params, 0.4B tokens of TinyStories, used only for architecture validation/replication, no quantitative claims drawn. S1 (main): 29.0M unique non-embedding params (57.3M total), 12.9B tokens of FineWeb-Edu, single seed, SmolLM2-135M shape with the middle layers replaced by the weight-tied core. Both use the SmolLM2 tokenizer (49,152 entries).

**Three post-hoc allocation policies on the frozen backbone.** A0 uniform depth (baseline, sweep r); A1 convergence exit (training-free): after each loop compute `KL(p_i ‖ p_{i-1})` per token and freeze tokens whose KL falls below ε, sweeping ε to trace an (avg-depth, loss) curve; A2 oracle-labeled router: harvest labels by running tokens to a high loop count and recording whether the argmax prediction still changes after loop i, then train a linear probe on the state `s_i` to predict this and use it as the stop rule. Labels come from the frozen model itself, so no external supervision is needed.

**Eval conventions.** Loss-vs-loops sweeps use noise state init (the training path) and 64 windows of length 2048. The allocation comparison uses zero state init for reproducible per-token exit decisions, 128 windows. The two conventions differ by 0.013 nats at r=8 (3.20 vs 3.19), self-consistent because A0/A1/A2 are all measured in the same harness.

## Results & Highlights

- **Per-token fixed-point convergence (S1, diagnostic run to 16 loops).** Mean successive-output KL between loops decays from 3.9×10⁻¹ at loop 2 → 3.2×10⁻² at loop 4 → 5.4×10⁻⁴ at loop 8 → 8.5×10⁻⁶ at loop 16, with mean state-change norm decaying in step (24.7 → 7.85 → 0.90 → 0.030). The fraction of tokens still moving (KL > 1e-3) drops from 99.8% at loop 2 to 10.0% at loop 8 and 0.1% at loop 16. This is direct evidence that the loop has finished computing, not stalled.
- **Depth elasticity on S1 (FineWeb-Edu validation).** Validation loss falls monotonically from 3.803 nats at r=1 to 3.202 at r=8 and stays flat (r=16: 3.203; r=32: 3.203) — a 0.60-nat quality range across a 16-fold per-token compute range, all from one set of weights chosen at inference time.
- **Main allocation result (S1 final, 128 windows).** The uniform depth-8 quality bar is 3.189 nats. The training-free convergence exit A1 reaches that bar at 4.94 average loops (38% reduction in average depth). At matched avg depth 3, A1 scores 3.231 vs uniform A0's 3.272; across avg depths 4–7 the two are within 0.002–0.003 nats with A1 never higher. The trained router A2 requires 7.99 average loops to reach the same bar (no reduction) and underperforms A1 across the range.
- **Fixed-depth control (S0, TinyStories).** A model trained at a constant r=4 is sharply peaked: loss 1.97 at r=4 but 6.01 at r=1 and 2.50 at r=16. The randomized-depth S0 model is flat and low across r=4,8,16 (1.886, 1.883, 1.883), even slightly beating the control at the control's own specialized depth — robustness that fixed-depth training cannot match.
- **Token-type ordering of exit depth (S1).** Mean exit depth: space 3.58, numeral 3.81, punctuation 4.16, word 4.25 (content tokens deepest, structural tokens shallowest). Stable under a 4× increase in evaluation windows, so it is a dataset effect, not sampling noise.
- **Wall-clock bracket (no realized speedup).** In the teacher-forced masking harness, A1/A2 loop every token to r_max and mask frozen ones, so realized throughput does not beat uniform depth. A matched uniform-r=8 vs uniform-r=5 timing gives a 1.48–1.51× speedup across batch sizes 1–8 — the best case if depth-wise batching packed exited tokens perfectly.
- **Ceiling reference (not a matched baseline).** SmolLM2-135M scores 2.66 nats under the S1 harness vs the recursive model's 3.20 at r=8 — a 0.54-nat gap, expected because SmolLM2-135M trained on ~2T tokens (~150× this paper's budget) and likely has train/eval overlap with FineWeb-Edu validation.
- **Compute footprint.** The complete study (S1 training + all evaluation incl. the allocation study) runs on a single RTX 4090 in ~100 GPU-hours (12.9B tokens at 24k tokens/s, 262k effective steps).

## Authors & Credibility

Single author, Dr. Joe Logan, an independent researcher (no institutional affiliation listed). The work is unusually disciplined about scope: the abstract itself flags that allocation results are at a single scale and seed, that no FLOP-matched parity is claimed, and that average depth is reported only as a FLOP proxy with a three-point wall-clock bracket rather than a realized speedup. The Reproducibility statement commits to releasing training and evaluation code, configuration files, the experiment log, and the final S1 checkpoint at https://github.com/jlognn/depth-recurrent-convergence. Appendix D transparently documents a mixed-precision rotary-attention bug (bf16/fp32 dtype promotion silently caught and fallen back to fp32) plus a non-finite step guard. The citation set correctly positions the work against Huginn (Geiping et al.), LoopFormer, Mixture-of-Recursions (Bae et al.), ACT, PonderNet, and Csordás et al.'s shallow-halt failure mode — the related work is genuinely engaged, not decorative. The novelty rests on a measurement contribution rather than a new architecture, which is a coherent but lower-stakes claim than a new method.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine but narrow. The per-token fixed-point convergence structure has not been explicitly characterized in a depth-recurrent LM before (Geiping et al. observed an emergent convergence stop rule but did not characterize it against alternatives). The "reading beats learning" finding is the most provocative claim and is well-supported at two scales.
- **Rigor:** Methodologically careful within its declared scope. Two evaluation harnesses are distinguished (noise vs zero init) and the 0.013-nat difference between them is openly reported rather than hidden. Truncated BPTT, fully annealed checkpoints, and an explicit statement that fixed-depth controls and elasticity are background rather than contribution all indicate honest framing.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong on paper. Single GPU (~100 GPU-hours on RTX 4090), SmolLM2 tokenizer, public datasets (FineWeb-Edu, TinyStories), committed code+config+checkpoint release. The main reproducibility gap is that **S1 is a single seed** and the S0 seed study has only 2 of 3 planned seeds complete (spread ≤0.030 nats across the sweep); no seed-variance claim is made, but the headline allocation deltas (0.002–0.081 nats) are within plausible seed noise and the paper does not stress this enough.
- **Significance:** Moderate, contingent. The convergence-exit finding (38% average-depth reduction with zero parameters, beating a learned router) is directly actionable for any team running a depth-recurrent / looped LM. But the realized-throughput claim is zero in the paper's own harness — the 38% saving is a FLOP proxy, not a measured speedup, and depends on depth-wise continuous batching that the author did not build.
- **Red flags:** (1) **No FLOP-matched dense baseline.** The SmolLM2-135M comparison is explicitly a 150×-token-disadvantaged ceiling reference, not a parity claim — a reader could mistake "0.54-nat gap" for a competitive result. (2) **Teacher-forced evaluation only;** the per-token KV-cache story at decode time is inherited from the recursion literature (per-recursion caching or depth-1 sharing per Bae et al.) and was not stress-tested, so the allocation policy's behavior under autoregressive generation is unverified. (3) **No downstream task evaluation at each depth**, so whether reasoning-flavored capabilities track depth differently from average loss is open — the literature (Geiping et al.) suggests they do and that this would matter more at scale. (4) The central "training-mean sets saturation depth" rule rests on only two internal data points plus one external (Huginn); the author is admirably explicit that this is a hypothesis, not a law.

## Strengths

- The core measurement (per-loop KL + state-change + still-moving fraction in Table 3) is a clean, cheap, and reusable diagnostic that any depth-recurrent practitioner can run on their own checkpoint.
- The "reading beats learning" finding is non-obvious and is supported by a clean control: A2's labels come from the same frozen model, so the comparison isolates "read the dynamics" vs "predict them from a snapshot" without confounding the backbone.
- Unusual honesty about scope: average-depth-as-FLOP-proxy, three-point wall-clock bracket rather than end-to-end speedup, single-scale/single-seed caveat in the abstract, explicit rejection of a token-starved dense baseline as illegitimate.
- Useful engineering artifacts: Appendix D documents a real mixed-precision rotary-attention bug and a non-finite step guard that practitioners will hit.

## Limitations

- **Single 135M-class scale and single seed for S1**; behavior at 1B+ parameters is unknown, and the literature suggests depth effects strengthen with scale and task difficulty, so average-loss results on web text plausibly understate what per-token allocation offers.
- **No realized wall-clock speedup.** Converting the 38% average-depth saving to throughput requires depth-wise continuous batching (Bae et al.), which the author did not build; reported realized throughput in the harness is ~0% better than uniform depth.
- **No FLOP-matched dense baseline.** A parity comparison against a vanilla transformer at iso-FLOP is the natural and most deployment-relevant next experiment and is explicitly missing.
- **Teacher-forced evaluation only.** Per-token allocation at decode time inherits the KV-cache story from prior work and is not stress-tested; autoregressive generation behavior is unverified.
- **No downstream-task evaluation at each depth**, so the relationship between depth and reasoning capability is unmeasured.
- **The "saturation depth = training mean" hypothesis is supported by only two internal points** (S0 mean-4, S1 mean-8) and one external (Huginn mean-32); no test of a wider training distribution at fixed scale is performed.
- **Token-type ordering of exit depth flips between scales** (S0: numerals deepest; S1: words deepest), attributed to distribution shift — interesting but not investigated further.

## Relevance

这篇论文与"高效推理 / 自适应计算 / 早退机制"主题高度相关：它给出了在循环式（looped / weight-tied）Transformer 上做逐 token 自适应深度的一种极简、零参数方案（基于输出 KL 的不动点检测），并诚实区分了 FLOP 代理节省与真实吞吐量，对追求 reasoning 模型测试时算力弹性化的研究方向是直接可借鉴的实证基础与基线对照。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the convergence-exit rule A1 still beat the learned router A2 at 1B+ parameters, or does a more expressive (non-linear) router trained on richer labels overtake it once the useful-depth range widens?
- Does per-token allocation under autoregressive generation (with per-recursion KV caching or depth-1 sharing) preserve the 38% average-depth saving, and what is the realized wall-clock speedup under depth-wise continuous batching?
- How does per-token exit depth correlate with downstream task accuracy (reasoning, math, code) rather than average cross-entropy — do the "late-converging 10%" tokens carry the reasoning load?
- Can the "saturation depth = training mean" hypothesis be confirmed as a law by sweeping the training loop distribution at fixed scale (e.g., mean-4/8/16/32 on the same backbone), or does it break at wider distributions?
- Is the per-token fixed-point convergence structure preserved under entropy-regularized depth objectives (Ouro) or shortcut-consistency objectives (LoopFormer), or is it specific to plain cross-entropy with randomized depth?
- Could the convergence-exit signal be combined with self-speculative decoding (LayerSkip-style verification) to recover exactness while still exploiting the recursive depth saving?
