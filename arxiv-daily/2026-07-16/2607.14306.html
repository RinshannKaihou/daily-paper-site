01

Methodology

Studies base (pretraining-only) Pythia models at 70M / 410M / 2.8B on the Pile. Core target: the empirical next-token distribution (ENTD) — the unrestricted global minimizer of next-token cross-entropy:

p_ENTD(x_{k+1} | x_{1:k}) = count(x_{1:k+1}, D) / count(x_{1:k}, D)

A training-aligned variant ENTD-T counts only prefixes at positions aligned to the batch window (mod C+1, C=2048). Both are computed via the ∞-gram suffix-array engine. Discrepancy is measured as total-variation (TV) distance.

Five targeted probes isolate each candidate explanation:

  1. Positional invariance — TV vs ENTD-T at offsets 0 / 1 / 1024.
  2. Softmax-bottleneck ceiling — unconstrained rank-d_model logit fit.
  3. Optimization variance — PolyPythias (multi-seed): model↔ENTD vs model↔model.
  4. Stratified + tail analysis — bucket by length, frequency, entropy.
  5. Per-sample trajectory — TV over training steps: uniform vs memorization.
02

Results & highlights

Per-sample TV distance (model vs ENTD)stratified n ≈ 84K prefixes
  • On the stratified sample, the LLM matches ENTD almost perfectly (TV ≤ 0.0093) for the bottom-20% CDF; agreement rises with scale and compute.
  • Despite ENTD-T being "training-aligned," the model is consistently closer to standard ENTD than to ENTD-T — lighter upper tails too (Fig. 2).
  • Offset-0 / 1 / 1024 TV distributions are nearly identical across scales: the transformer learns a strong positional invariance the training never asked for (Fig. 3).
  • Rank-2560 unconstrained logit fit reaches TV far below even the 2.8B model → softmax bottleneck is not the dominant explanation (Fig. 4).
  • Optimization variance dominates at 70M but not at 410M / 2.8B; larger models converge to a more stable target (Fig. 5).
  • For high-count (≥ 10⁴) prefixes, ENTD entropy ↔ final TV: Spearman ρ = 0.80 — the strongest correlate found.
  • High-TV inputs split into three failure modes: (a) rare inputs (median count ≤ 2, plausibly noise); (b) frequent-but-high-entropy, where the model collapses narrow (model entropy 0.08 vs ENTD 6.08); (c) frequent-but-deterministic spam/templates where the model refuses to commit (model entropy 4.5–6.5 vs ENTD ≈ 0).
  • High-TV tails are structurally persistent for larger models (high top-10% IoU across checkpoints).
03

Strengths / Limitations

+ Strengths

  • Decomposes a single aggregate (mean training loss) into a rich per-sample distribution.
  • Clean separation of four candidate explanations, each tested with a targeted experiment rather than narrative.
  • Cross-validates the empirical index with an independent brute-force hash table — rare paranoia in interpretability work.
  • The high-entropy-frequent-prefix failure mode is genuinely interesting, with a plausible link to text-homogeneity collapse.
  • Open, reproducible stack: Pythia, PolyPythias, ∞-gram, Pile.

! Limitations

  • All conclusions from Pythia up to 2.8B — ~100× below frontier; 6.9B/12B & OLMo are future work.
  • Strict ENTD analysis applies only to training-set sequences; unseen inputs (where LLMs are actually useful) are out of scope.
  • Restricted to single-token next-token distributions — nothing on chained generation, long-horizon coherence, or instruction tuning.
  • Headline numbers depend on the specific stratified binning (length × corpus-count) and reservoir N=100.
  • No released code; some figures (rank-2560 fit) would benefit from a public artifact.
04

Open questions

Q1 · SCALING

Do positional-invariance and softmax-bottleneck-insufficiency persist at 6.9B/12B and frontier scale, or are they artifacts of the 70M–2.8B regime?

Q2 · MECHANISM

What inductive bias — attention sparsity, residual-stream rank, an SGD regularization effect — forces a narrower-than-empirical distribution on high-entropy frequent prefixes?

Q3 · GENERALIZATION

Can ENTD be extended to unseen inputs via a principled mixture with a learned smoothing distribution, beyond heuristic backoff?

Q4 · DIAGNOSTIC

Is the ρ = 0.80 entropy–TV correlation a leading indicator of text-homogeneity collapse, usable as a cheap pretraining diagnostic?

Q5 · POST-TRAINING

How do SFT / RLHF shift the model–ENTD TV distribution — widen it (explaining RLHF "diversity loss") or merely move mass within it?

Q6 · INTERVENTION

Could the per-sample TV-trajectory taxonomy (uniform vs memorization vs oscillation) flag "structurally hard" prefixes for targeted data intervention at training time?
