01Methodology — three mechanisms, one deque

Orchestrated through a dynamic double-ended queue Q_exec and a global memory M of completed atom states (Algorithm 1).

▸ MECHANISM 1

Visually-grounded decomposition

The Decomposer fractures (Q, I) into atoms A₁…Aₙ, each with a local objective and expected symbolic state. Perception-first: visual regions are anchored before logical deduction, damping multimodal hallucination.

▸ MECHANISM 2

DAG planning & context isolation

Planning is a DAG G=(A,E); a node ingests only the purified results of its direct prerequisites — never the full trajectory.

C(Aᵢ) = ∪_{Aⱼ ∈ P(Aᵢ)} M(Aⱼ)   (Eq. 1)
▸ MECHANISM 3

Adaptive atomic fission

A stuck atom (no STOP within T_max) is reflected on and fractured into sub-atoms, prepended to Q_exec. Hierarchical namespacing keeps the global DAG intact.

F(Aᵢ, trace) → {Aᵢ.₁ … Aᵢ.ₖ}  (Eq. 2)

Execution loop: decompose → topologically sort → push to Q_exec → pop atom, build isolated context, run Planner with toolset T for ≤ T_max steps, Verifier checks STOP → unresolved triggers fission, resolved commits to M → Solver aggregates M when the queue empties.

02Results & highlights

  • Global 66.3% average over six MLLMs, beating OctoTools (62.8), AutoGen (62.0), GPT-Functions (58.4), LangChain (58.1), raw LLM (57.0).
  • Physics OlympiadBench rises 30.9% → 41.2% global (+10.3pp); on GPT-5, 60.3% vs 45.8% raw and 40.2% GPT-Functions.
  • Backbone Model-agnostic: lifts Qwen3-vl-235B 59.3% → 68.3% (+4.1pp over OctoTools); Chem-Symbol 47.8% → 63.7% (+15.9pp).
  • Ablation Removing DAG planning: −1.4pp (MathVista −2.1pp). Removing fission: −0.6pp (MathVista −0.9, Olympiad −0.6).
  • Recovery Fission recovers failed nodes at 51.5% Math / 39.6% Physics / 14.1% Chemistry vs Self-Refine 18.3 / 12.6 / 8.5.
  • Efficiency Stable prompt tokens & accuracy across 7 inference steps; OctoTools shows exponential token growth and accuracy collapse after step 4 (Fig. 5).

03Strengths & limitations, side-by-side

▲ Strengths

  • Clean architectural story — each of three mechanisms maps onto one named linear-agent failure mode, verified by Table 2.
  • Genuinely model-agnostic across six frontier MLLMs (proprietary + open-weight).
  • Token-efficiency (Fig. 5) concretely shows context isolation avoiding OctoTools’ “context explosion.”
  • Focused recovery study (Fig. 4) — 51.5% vs 18.3% in Math — isolates fission better than the headline ablation.
  • OlympiadBench physics (30.9%→41.2%) is the strongest domain-specific evidence.

▼ Limitations

  • Four of six per-model results live in an appendix not in the PDF body — the 66.3% headline is hard to audit.
  • Fission contributes only −0.6pp in ablation; its prominence outstrips its measured impact.
  • No Tree-of-Thoughts / Graph-of-Thoughts comparison despite positioning against them — only linear/multi-agent baselines.
  • Fission is non-deterministic; stability of splits and worst-case Q_exec blow-up are unanalyzed.
  • No prompts, toolset T, T_max, retry budgets, or code release in the main text.
  • “Global Average” is non-standard and can mask per-backbone regressions; no win/loss vs OctoTools reported.

04Questions for further exploration

How does TopoAgent fare against Tree-of-Thoughts and Graph-of-Thoughts on the same six benchmarks — the direct topological competitors it claims to beat?
What is the per-backbone win/loss profile vs OctoTools, and how sensitive is 66.3% to the aggregation choice?
What is the wall-clock and dollar-cost overhead of DAG + per-atom planner calls, given Fig. 5 only shows per-step tokens?
Is fission stable across seeds — do the LLM-generated sub-atoms converge, and what is the worst-case depth of Q_exec?
Could context isolation (Eq. 1) relax to soft attention instead of hard prerequisite pruning, preserving weakly-relevant cross-domain cues?
Does the framework generalize beyond STEM — e.g. document-grounded legal/medical reasoning where dependencies are noisier and less DAG-structured?
