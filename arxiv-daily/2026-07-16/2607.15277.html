02 Why this matters

Statistical self-consistency is an unsaturated, alignment-orthogonal evaluation axis — population-level accuracy does not guarantee coherent conditional estimates for LLM-as-simulator pipelines.

03 Critical edge

The macro-fallacy evidence is tied to a single ACS domain with hand-engineered target-informative splits — no neutral-split ablation is provided, so the headline gain may be an artifact of the chosen partition.

Methodology § 03

A partition–prompt–aggregate framework that turns the law of total probability into a reference-free test of whether LLM in-context estimates behave as conditional probabilities.

P(E | X∈X) = ΣS∈P γS · P(E | X∈S)

A binary conditioning tree (BCT) of depth L recursively splits a base population — the 3.8M-respondent 2024 ACS, or WVS opinion samples — using one shared binary rule per level (e.g. AGE 31–68, then employed/self-employed), chosen by a regression-tree objective that minimizes prior-weighted within-subgroup income variance.

Each subpopulation is verbalized as a natural-language path and queried for a tail probability T̂τ(S) = P(Y>τ | X∈S); subgroup priors γ̂S are elicited via a level-wise protocol. Reconstructed aggregates are compared against the direct population-level estimate.

Two reference-free scores result: split consistency (does a direct estimate equal the prior-weighted aggregate of its refinements?) and order consistency (invariance under reordering of conditioning constraints). Formal propositions bound multi-step aggregation error by ℓ·ε (linear) and pairwise-order error by C(|J|,2)·ε (quadratic).

Results & highlights § 04

Consistency violations are widespread, task-dependent, and do not scale with capability — even the strongest frontier models stay far from saturation.

  • Macro fallacy is large and robust: reconstructed aggregates beat direct prompting by up to ~50% relative alignment gain for GPT-5.4 on ACS income.
  • Priors degrade with depth: total-variation distance to survey priors grows monotonically from level 1→4, capping the benefit of finer partitions.
  • Does not track capability: neither split nor order consistency improves systematically with the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index.
  • Micro-to-macro prompting (reason over implicit subpopulations in one query) partially recovers the aggregation gain — ruling out test-time compute as the sole driver.

+ Strengths

  • Clean, principled core idea: the law of total probability as a reference-free, domain-agnostic consistency test for the conditional-inference view of ICL.
  • The macro fallacy is surprising and practically useful — a free "decompose-then-aggregate" prompting improvement that holds across models and thresholds.
  • Careful disentangling of conditional vs. prior error via oracle-prior ablations and a law-of-total-variance explanation for why refinement helps.
  • Genuine breadth: four domains, ~25 frontier models, both alignment (ground-truth) and reference-free consistency settings.

− Limitations

  • Macro-fallacy evidence relies primarily on ACS, a single U.S. census domain with convenient demographic splits; generalization is not established.
  • Scores are sensitive to tolerance ε=0.02 and the normalized Wasserstein metric, with no ε sensitivity analysis reported.
  • Split attributes are target-informative by construction (regression-tree on income variance), plausibly amplifying the macro fallacy; no neutral-split ablation.
  • Synthetic forecasting tasks are small, author-constructed, with no real ground truth — they show the checks can apply, not that they predict accuracy.
Open questions § 05
  • How do split/order scores behave under an ε sweep (0.01–0.10) and alternative metrics (KL, JS, unnormalized Wasserstein)? Is the "frontier models are inconsistent" conclusion robust?
  • Does the macro fallacy survive target-orthogonal or random splits, rather than the regression-tree-selected partitions?
  • Can RLHF-time enforcement of total-probability constraints improve consistency — and does it trade off against other capabilities?
  • Is there a measurable correlation between synthetic-task consistency and real Prophet Arena forecasting performance?
  • How do violations scale with attribute count d beyond 2, given the quadratic pairwise-order bound?
  • Could anchoring constraints at better-aligned lower-level estimates yield a training objective that propagates fine-grained knowledge upward?
