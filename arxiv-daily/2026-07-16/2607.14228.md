> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一套针对视觉特征空间与 SE(3) 欧氏变换群对齐度的探针协议（含 mutual k-NN 拓扑对齐度与"Poincaré Adapter"轻量 Siamese MLP），发现 DINOv2/v3 等纯自监督视觉模型在 ScanNet 等静态场景上潜在子空间可与 6D 相机位姿线性对齐（DINOv2 在 ScanNet 上 Poincaré Adapter 测试 R²≈0.65，远超 Raw Pixels 的负 R²≈−5.29），并据此实现无需显式 3D 重建的纯潜在空间视觉导航。

> **TL;DR:** SeeSE3 probes whether vision foundation models' feature spaces are intrinsically aligned with the SE(3) Euclidean transformation group, using a training-free mutual k-NN topology metric and a lightweight "Poincaré Adapter" MLP. On ScanNet, DINOv2-B reaches a Poincaré Adapter R² of ≈0.65 vs. −0.28 raw-linear R² and −5.29 for raw pixels, surpassing the explicitly-3D-trained CroCo (R²≈0.38); this linearized manifold enables training-free "Inverse Poincaré" latent navigation (DINOv2 Hit@0.3 = 0.628 vs. Identity baseline 0.302) without any explicit 3D reconstruction.

## 核心贡献

- 形式化了"Poincaré Task"——一个"无动作能力的被动观察者"能否仅从视觉统计中发现 3D 欧氏空间结构的问题，并将之与 SE(3) 欧氏变换群的几何对齐联系起来，超越了以往只回归深度/法线等图像中心量的 Probe3D 类工作。
- 提出递进难度的四类探针：M1（mutual k-NN 拓扑对齐，训练自由）、M2（局部内在维度 ID）、M3（全局线性等变性 R²）、M4（"Poincaré Adapter"，轻量 Siamese MLP 把弯曲的特征流形"摊平"成齐次坐标系），从拓扑与几何双视角系统评估 18+ 种视觉编码器。
- 实证发现：纯自监督的 DINOv2/DINOv3 蕴含可被 adapter 线性解码的"Visual Grid Code"（ScanNet 上 DINOv2-B R²≈0.65），且拓扑对齐（M1≈0.38）无需训练即涌现；显式 3D 预训练的 CroCo 反而更弱（R²≈0.38），提示"大规模被动观测 > 小规模显式 3D 预训练"。
- 给出严格理论分析：Theorem 3（Jacobian 秩为 6 的平滑编码器存在局部线性读出）、Theorem 5（adapter 通过摊平流形曲率实现全局可解码性）、Theorem 7（旋转比平移更易解码的几何根源），并通过 25 个从零训练的 DINOv2-B checkpoint 给出涌现的必要条件（自蒸馏、batch size、数据多样性）。
- 提出闭环"Latent-Space Navigation"应用：Inverse Poincaré 仅用伪逆做向量运算即可在潜在空间导航，并支持多步开环轨迹规划，无需生成像素。

## Key Contributions

- Formulates the "Poincaré Task" — whether a motionless, passive observer can discover the structure of 3D Euclidean space purely from visual statistics — and links it to alignment between the feature manifold and the SE(3) rigid-motion group, going beyond prior Probe3D-style depth/normals readouts.
- Introduces a four-metric probing protocol of escalating difficulty: M1 (training-free mutual k-NN topological alignment), M2 (local intrinsic dimensionality, TwoNN/MLE), M3 (global linear equivariance R²), and M4 (the lightweight Siamese "Poincaré Adapter" MLP that "unrolls" the curved feature manifold into a homogeneous coordinate patch).
- Empirically shows that purely self-supervised DINOv2/DINOv3 harbor a "Visual Grid Code" linearly decodable to SE(3) (DINOv2-B on ScanNet R²≈0.65), with topological alignment emerging training-free (M1≈0.38); explicitly-3D-pretrained CroCo is weaker (R²≈0.38), suggesting massive passive observation can beat small-scale explicit 3D pre-training.
- Provides formal theory — Theorem 3 (rank-6 Jacobian ⇒ local linear readout), Theorem 5 (adapter removes feature-manifold curvature), Theorem 7 (geometric reason rotation ≻ translation-direction ≻ translation-magnitude in decodability) — validated by a controlled ablation of 25 DINOv2-B checkpoints trained from scratch on a single walking-tour video.
- Demonstrates closed-form "Latent-Space Navigation": the training-free Inverse Poincaré predictor (just `gt + W†∆P`) suffices for neighborhood-correct frame retrieval, and multi-step open-loop latent trajectories reach the correct target view without rendering pixels.

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** For a static scene S observed by a moving camera, an encoder f_θ maps image I_t to feature z_t = f_θ(I_t). Image acquisition is modeled as a sampling map from a pose subspace U ⊆ SE(3) to feature space R^d. For two frames t, t+s with relative pose P_rel = P_t⁻¹ P_{t+s}, the 6D tangent vector is ΔP = (Log(P_t⁻¹ P_{t+s}))∨ ∈ R^6 (3 translation + 3 axis-angle rotation velocities). The central question: does a fixed linear W exist such that ΔP ≈ W(z_{t+s} − z_t)? Because global isomorphic coordinates for non-commutative SE(3) cannot exist in R^d, the authors probe locally (the "straightening" hypothesis of Hénaff et al. for V1).

**Probes.**
- **M1 — Topological Alignment:** mutual k-NN alignment between feature neighborhoods and camera-pose neighborhoods (training-free), computed at short (stride 1) and long (stride 21) strides.
- **M2 — Intrinsic Dimensionality:** MLE and Two-NN estimators; an ideal SE(3)-aligned static-scene representation has ID ≈ 6.
- **M3 — Linear Equivariance:** train a single global linear regressor W for ΔP ≈ W(z_{t+s}−z_t), report R².
- **M4 — Poincaré Adapter:** a lightweight Siamese MLP φ_θ applied independently to each feature, ΔP ≈ W(φ_θ(z_{t+s}) − φ_θ(z_t)), with per-component standardized targets. A layer sweep finds the best block per encoder.

**Datasets.** ScanNet (primary), ARKitScenes, TUM RGB-D, 12 Scenes, 7 Scenes.

**Encoders probed.** Raw Pixels, CLIP, DINOv2 (B/L), DINOv3-B, CroCo, V-JEPA 2.1, DUSt3R, MASt3R, MoGe, VGGT-L, Metric3D v2, Depth Anything v3, Perception Encoder, Stable Diffusion (1.5/2.1/LCM, SD-DINO VAE/UNet), SVD, VideoMAEv2, RVM, 4DS.

**Latent Navigation.** Given adapted feature g_t = φ_θ(z_t) and a desired displacement ΔP, the training-free Inverse Poincaré predicts ĝ_{t+s} = g_t + W†ΔP. It is compared to learned correctors (Linear / MLP / Attention residual) and an Identity (no-navigation) baseline. Success is measured by nearest-neighbor retrieval with Pose-Error Hits@ε (ε ∈ {0.1,0.2,0.3,0.5}).

**Ablations / theory.** 25 DINOv2-B checkpoints from scratch on a single walking-tour video isolate the impact of self-distillation, batch size, and data scale; 3,328 adapter configurations decompose rotation / translation-direction / translation-magnitude decodability.

## Results & Highlights

- **M1 — Topological Alignment (ScanNet, stride 21):** explicitly geometric models DUSt3R/MoGe/MASt3R top the chart at ≈0.35–0.40; among pure SSL models DINOv2-L/DINOv3-B reach ≈0.38, while video models VideoMAEv2/RVM/4DS collapse to ≈0.15–0.25.
- **M2/M3 — Intrinsic Dimensionality & Raw Linear Readout (Table 1):** CroCo MLE-ID ≈ 5.92 and DUSt3R ≈ 4.40 are close to the theoretical 6, while DINOv2-B expands to ID-MLE 6.41 and DINOv3-B to 7.88; raw linear readout (M3) is negative for every model (Pixels R² = −5.29, DINOv2-B −0.28, DUSt3R −0.22, V-JEPA 0.09, VGGT-L −0.09), proving the feature manifold is natively curved.
- **M4 — Poincaré Adapter (ScanNet, DINOv2-B):** a lightweight Siamese adapter recovers **R² ≈ 0.65** (averaged over 3 scenes) vs. CroCo's ≈0.38 and raw pixels' strongly negative R² even with the adapter — evidence of a locally Euclidean "Visual Grid Code" subspace.
- **Cross-dataset success rates (Table 2, R²>0 / R²>0.3 / Max R²):** on 12-Scenes V-JEPA 2.1 hits 100%/70%/0.803 and DUSt3R 90%/80%/0.789; on ScanNet (707 scenes) success drops to 35%/4% (V-JEPA) and 31%/3% (DINOv2); on the noisy single-scan ARKitScenes only 47–51% of rooms admit positive R² with Max R² 0.34–0.41. Clean bundle-adjusted poses ⇒ near-perfect decodability.
- **Scaling & generalization (Figure 5):** zero-shot recovery on 31 held-out ScanNet rooms grows monotonically from ≈18% positive-R² rooms at N=5 training rooms to ≈50% at N=200, with no plateau — geometric awareness is a scalable emergent property.
- **Latent Navigation (Table 3, DINOv2 Layer-7):** Identity baseline Hit@0.3 = 0.302, Hit@0.5 = 0.523; training-free **Inverse Poincaré reaches Hit@0.3 = 0.628 and Hit@0.5 = 0.741** (R² 0.199); a learned Attention corrector gets higher R² (0.407) but worse physical retrieval (Hit@0.3 = 0.383), showing pure vector arithmetic already reaches the correct spatial neighborhood.
- **Cross-room navigation generalization:** zero-shot Inv. Poincaré beats Identity in ~60% of unseen rooms; a Ridge-Refit (re-fit only the linear ∆P→∆g map per test room) pushes success to **90–94% across DINOv2/DUSt3R/V-JEPA**.
- **Multi-step open-loop navigation (12-Scenes):** for a moderate 0.68 m / 19° displacement a 4-step trajectory retrieves the exact target frame; even extreme 2.45 m / 37° displacements keep rotation error < 5° (translation drift accumulates).
- **Probe-design ablation (Appendix, on DINOv2-B):** Lie-algebra 6D-twist linear readout R² = 0.61 vs. SE(3) matrix regression R² = −0.43; absolute-pose prediction fails entirely (R² = −1.75); Siamese φ(z₂)−φ(z₁) beats φ(z₂−z₁) by ΔR² = +0.13.
- **Component decodability (3,328 configs):** rotation is positive-R² in 88% of configurations, translation direction in most, but translation magnitude in only 27% with 6× the seed standard deviation — consistent with Theorem 7 (translational flow scales as 1/Z, confounding magnitude with depth).
- **Training-conditions ablation (25 from-scratch DINOv2-B on one walking-tour video):** mutual k-NN alignment is stable (0.35–0.42) across recipes, but Poincaré R² ranges 0.08–0.47; removing self-distillation drops M-k-nn to 0.24; batch size 128 collapses it to 0.15; the 142 M-image pretrained model reaches R² = 0.61 (Mag-R² +0.60) vs. best single-video checkpoint R² = 0.47.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The reframing — from "predict depth/normals from features" (Probe3D, *You et al.*, *Huang et al.*) to "does the feature manifold itself carry a homogeneous SE(3)-isomorphic coordinate patch?" — is genuinely new, as is the connection to Poincaré's agency hypothesis and the V1 "straightening" literature. Distinct from RUST, which showed the effect only in a generative model trained on static scenes; SeeSE3 finds it in discriminative SSL models trained on arbitrary data.
- **Rigor:** Strong on the empirical side — 18+ encoders, 5 datasets, layer sweeps, 3,328 adapter configurations, 25 from-scratch DINOv2 checkpoints. The theoretical apparatus (Theorems 3, 5, 7) gives principled explanations for the rotation-vs-translation asymmetry and the role of manifold curvature. The honest observation that M2 is "too easy" (everyone has ID≈6) and M3 "too hard" (everyone fails globally) is scientifically transparent.
- **Reproducibility:** Medium-to-good. Adapter is a small Siamese MLP, datasets are public (ScanNet, ARKitScenes, TUM, 12/7-Scenes), and the protocol is precisely specified; however, key implementation details are deferred to the appendix, no code release is mentioned in the main text, and layer-choice / standardization choices materially affect numbers.
- **Significance:** Conceptually significant — it challenges the cognitive-science view (Hénaff, grid-cell literature) that grid-cell-like spatial structure requires active motor agency, by showing passive motionless observers (DINOv2) develop a linearizable "Visual Grid Code." Practically, the latent-navigation proof of concept is suggestive but not yet competitive with DUSt3R/VGGT-style explicit VO.
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - **Static-scene-only limitation** — explicitly conceded. Poincaré's distinction between "changes of position" and "changes of state" is never tested, so the central cognitive-science claim is only half-addressed.
  - **Headline R²≈0.65 is a best-of-3-ScanNet-scenes, best-layer number;** the dataset-wide ScanNet success rate is only 31–37% positive-R² rooms (Table 2), and ARKitScenes / 7-Scenes Max R² sits at 0.25–0.41 — i.e., on most real-world scenes the structure is weak or absent.
  - **Translation magnitude is essentially not decodable** (positive in only 27% of configs), which quietly undercuts the "isomorphic to SE(3)" framing — what is recovered is closer to a 5-DoF (rotation + translation direction) subalgebra, with metric scale requiring external priors (Theorem 7(d)).
  - **Latent navigation numbers (Table 3) are averaged over only 3 scenes** and Hit@0.1 stays ≤0.252 even for the best method — the task, as posed, is not solved at fine precision.
  - **No comparison against strong VO / NWM baselines** (e.g., DUSt3R/VGGT pose regression, or actual Navigation World Models) on the navigation task — Identity and a small Attention corrector are weak comparisons.
  - The 0.65-vs-0.38 DINOv2-beats-CroCo claim is interesting but CroCo is much smaller-scale and older; a like-for-like scale-controlled comparison (DINOv2 vs. a 142 M-image 3D-pretrained encoder) would be more convincing.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated problem statement with a unifying theoretical framework (SE(3) Lie algebra, manifold straightening, homogeneous coordinates).
- A reusable, mostly training-free probing protocol (M1 in particular needs no labels) that future 3D-awareness work can adopt.
- Surprising and well-supported finding: passive self-supervised DINOv2 contains a linearizable SE(3) subspace, beating smaller-scale explicit 3D pre-training (CroCo).
- Honest negative results (M3 fails for all models; translation magnitude rarely decodable) and a genuine theoretical explanation for them.
- Latent-navigation proof of concept is elegant and the multi-step open-loop trajectory reaching the exact target frame is a memorable qualitative demonstration.

## Limitations

- Static scenes only; dynamic scenes (camera + object motion) untested, so Poincaré's "position vs. state" distinction is unresolved.
- Per-room R² is bimodal: many rooms (especially 7-Scenes, ARKitScenes) are not decodable at all; results are sensitive to pose quality (bundle-adjusted vs. single-scan).
- Metric translation scale is essentially unrecoverable from frozen features alone (Theorem 7(d)), capping practical VO utility.
- Latent navigation evaluated on only 3 scenes in the main table; no comparison to state-of-the-art VO or NWM methods.
- No public code release announced in the main text; best-layer choice and standardization details are appendix-only.

## Relevance

这项研究与视觉基础模型表征分析、3D 感知以及潜在空间导航（Navigation World Models / Visual Navigation Models）方向高度相关：它直接给出了"DINOv2/JEPA 类自监督视觉模型潜在空间是否蕴含 SE(3) 几何结构"这一基础问题的可复现探针协议，对后续 world model、visual odometry 与具身智能中"无显式 3D 重建的潜在空间推理"具有直接的参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the Poincaré Adapter extend to dynamic scenes where the latent change is a mixture of ego-motion and object motion, and can the two be disentangled in the adapted subspace?
- Is the DINOv2 ≻ CroCo superiority stable under scale- and data-matched controls (e.g., comparing to a 142 M-image 3D-pretrained encoder)?
- Can metric translation scale be recovered by injecting a lightweight depth/scale prior, and does this close the gap with explicit VO methods like DUSt3R/VGGT?
- Why does the linearly-decodable SE(3) subspace emerge in DINOv2 but collapse when batch size is reduced to 128 — is it a batch-statistics effect or an effective-data-diversity effect?
- Can the adapter be embedded inside a generative Navigation World Model to enable closed-loop latent planning with pixel rendering only at the end of a trajectory?
- How does the recovered "Visual Grid Code" compare, topologically, to biological grid cells, and does it exhibit the same hexagonal periodicity signatures under analogous analyses?
