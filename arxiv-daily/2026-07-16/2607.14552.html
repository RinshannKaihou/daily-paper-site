01 Methodology

A causal isolation design

All students are full-parameter SFT of Qwen3-8B under one fixed recipe (DeepSpeed ZeRO-3, 1 epoch, seq 10,240, lr 2×10⁻⁵, batch 48). Training set: 935 math problems from Nemotron-Cascade-SFT-Stage-2, disjoint from every eval set.

For each problem the generator samples n=2 chains (T 0.6, top-p 0.95, top-k 20, 16,384-token thinking budget) under two prompts that differ only in answer visibility. Both arms keep only chains whose boxed answer matches the gold (symbolic matching), intersect to the 935 problems solved by both, fine-tune under the identical recipe. Reported leakage penalty: Δ = acc(S_blind) − acc(S_leaked).

The mechanism is read off unlabeled generations via the Answer-First Rate (AFR):

AFR(C) = (1/|C|) Σ 𝟙[pos(c) < 0.2]
∆AFR = AFRleaked − AFRblind

47.4% of leaked chains state the answer in the first 20% of the think block vs 26.6% blind — an off-policy shift toward P(c|q,a(q)) rationalizations rather than genuine P(c|q) search. The signature is read from a small unlabeled sample before any fine-tuning.

Robustness controls (5 layers)

(i) length-matched control truncating each blind chain to its matched leaked length (95.0, rules out length); (ii) eval-budget sweep 4K→32K; (iii) instruction ablation at fixed visibility; (iv) generalization to code (MBPP+/HumanEval+), smaller student (Qwen3-1.7B), two cross-family teachers (Nemotron-Nano-8B, GLM-Z1-9B); (v) inferential statistics (paired bootstrap, McNemar, KS test, leave-one-family-out) in Appendix I.

Cause is the instruction, not visibility

At fixed answer visibility, "rationalize toward answer" → 16.2, "derive-first" → 4.3, "final-check" → 1.3, "out-of-band" → 0.7. Harm tracks rationalization.

Within-corpus carrier (DiD)

Leaked-Early 64.2 vs Leaked-Late 86.2, DiD = +16.9. Excising the single answer-stating line (~1.4% of chars) recovers 83% of the 29.8-pt gap.

Cross-model predictor + mitigations

∆AFR orders the penalty across 8 thinking models (Qwen3, DeepSeek-R1-Distill, GLM-Z1, Llama-Nemotron) at r = 0.960 (permutation p = 0.0003). Held-out third-family Nemotron: predicted 17.4, observed 17.6; leave-one-family-out MAE = 2.9 pts. Mitigations: derive-first instruction recovers ~2/3 of the penalty; answer-blind self-distillation matches base (95.1 vs 95.2) and beats gold-data SFT (90.8) at the same 10,240-token budget. STaR-style rescue (leak only on blind failures) still costs 4.0 pts on Qwen3-8B.

03 Critical assessment

Strengths vs. limitations

Strengths

  • True one-bit causal intervention with length-matched, instruction-ablation, and DiD controls.
  • Pre-training predictor (∆AFR) is cheap, model-agnostic, validated on a held-out third family.
  • Honest scoping: effect confined to derivation-bearing tasks; negative/null results (STaR-rescue, GPQA, MMLU) reported, not buried.
  • Mechanism-to-intervention pipeline: same signature diagnoses and fixes the harm.
  • Statistical appendix (paired bootstrap, McNemar, KS, Fisher-z, LOFO) unusually thorough for an SFT paper.

Limitations

  • Cross-model fit uses only n=8 models; Fisher-z CI on r is wide [0.79, 0.99]; Phi-4 anchor dropped.
  • Many Table 5 rows are single-seed — support the sign, not exact magnitudes.
  • Training set is small (935 problems, one corpus); generalization to larger mixtures untested.
  • Within-corpus AFR split partially tracks problem difficulty (App. F); carrier claim scoped to "signature-marked chains."
  • Cross-student transfer is noisiest (SD 8.9 on a +6.9 mean; one seed near −0.2).

04 Open questions

Where the leakage framing must next be tested

  1. Does ∆AFR extend to reasoning with soft verification (long-form science, multi-hop QA, agentic tool-use), or does the effect dissolve without a strict final-answer verifier?
  2. How does answer-leakage interact with RLHF/RLVR and on-policy distillation — does the off-policy framing predict a similar penalty when the reward itself leaks the answer?
  3. Would scaling from 935 to tens of thousands of problems dilute the penalty, or does the AFR signature stay the dominant per-corpus predictor?
  4. Can derive-first be pushed past "two-thirds recovery" via a process-reward reranker or entropy-based hindsight gate (cf. concurrent Zhang/Li 2026)?
  5. Does the train/test length inversion (leaked trains on shorter chains, generates longer non-converging ones at test) suggest a length-aware curriculum or inference-time fix?
  6. Is the 83%-recovery carrier result robust to non-English or non-boxed-answer formats, where the "answer-stating line" is less cleanly localized?
