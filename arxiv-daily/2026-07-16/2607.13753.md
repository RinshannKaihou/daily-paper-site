> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Pre-/Intra-/Post-CoT 三阶段置信度标定框架，在受控条件下（同一 Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct 主干、同一数据混合）系统比较 SFT、RL 和 OPD 三种后训练范式，发现三者各自在不同阶段最可靠（OPD 适合题难度估计、SFT 适合在线早停、RL 适合答案聚合），并基于"置信度可靠性随 token 相对位置变化"这一发现提出位置感知策略 PosConf，使 RL 答案聚合在多数投票基础上提升 6.1 个百分点，并在紧凑 token 预算下最多为 OPD 早停带来 4.3 点提升。

> **TL;DR:** The authors introduce a Pre-CoT / Intra-CoT / Post-CoT calibration framework and run a controlled comparison of SFT, RL, and on-policy distillation (OPD) variants built on the same Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct backbone and the same DeepScaler+SimpleRL data. On AIME 2024, AIME 2025, AMC 2023, and MATH500 they show that calibration is stage-dependent and position-dependent: OPD gives the best pre-reasoning difficulty signal (avg AUROC 0.644), SFT gives the best online early-stopping signal, and RL gives the best trace-level signal for answer aggregation (Top-10% confidence voting reaches 62.8% avg vs. 57.4% majority voting, a 5.4-point delta). Their position-aware PosConf strategy pushes RL aggregation to a 6.1-point average gain over majority voting and adds up to 4.3 points to OPD early stopping under tight budgets by avoiding OPD's later inverse-calibration region.

## 核心贡献

- 首次提出 Pre-CoT / Intra-CoT / Post-CoT 三阶段置信度标定框架，把"置信度"从最终输出的单一全局属性，重新拆解为分别对应难度估计、在线早停、答案聚合三种推理时决策的阶段信号。
- 在严格控制变量下（同一 Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct 主干、同一 DeepScaler + SimpleRL 数据混合、相同教师 DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B）对比 SFT、RL、OPD，揭示每种后训练范式会在不同阶段诱发截然不同的置信度偏移，而不是单调地"变好"或"变坏"。
- 发现置信度可靠性具有强位置依赖性：SFT 全程保持稳定的正确/错误轨迹置信度差；RL 在前期几乎无信息、进入"路径提交期"后才变得可分；OPD 早期标定良好但后期出现"反向标定"——错误轨迹反而更自信。
- 提出位置感知的 PosConf 策略，只从每个模型相对位置的可靠区间提取置信度（SFT/RL 用尾部，OPD 用前段），显著改善在线早停与答案聚合，验证了"使用置信度既要分阶段、也要分位置"的论点。
- 配套开源代码与可复现训练/评估协议（5 次随机种子重复、256 在线样本、top-k=20、温度 0.6）。

## Key Contributions

- A three-stage calibration framework that connects token-level confidence to three inference-time decisions: Pre-CoT (problem-difficulty routing), Intra-CoT (online early stopping), and Post-CoT (answer aggregation across sampled traces). This reframes calibration as a stage-specific signal rather than a single aggregate model property.
- A controlled comparison of SFT, RL, and on-policy distillation (OPD) derived from the same Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct backbone, same DeepScaler + SimpleRL problem mixture, and the same DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B teacher, isolating the effect of the post-training objective.
- The empirical finding that each paradigm is best at a different stage: OPD yields the most useful Pre-CoT signal (avg AUROC 0.644, PRR 0.345), SFT the strongest Intra-CoT early-stopping signal, and RL the most reliable Post-CoT trace-level signal for aggregation.
- The discovery that confidence reliability is strongly position-dependent within a trace, including OPD's "inverse calibration" where incorrect traces become more confident than correct ones in the later part of reasoning.
- PosConf, a position-aware confidence strategy that extracts confidence only from model-specific reliable relative-position intervals, with model-specific intervals derived from AIME 2024 trajectory analysis (SFT Intra [0,1]/Post [0.6,1]; RL Intra [0.2,1]/Post [0.6,1]; OPD Intra [0,0.6]/Post [0,0.6]).

## Methodology

The paper compares four model variants all derived from Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct: Qwen-Instruct (unmodified reference), Qwen-SFT (full-parameter SFT on rejection-sampled correct teacher traces from DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B, cutoff 16,384 tokens, lr 1e-6, 2 epochs), Qwen-RL (GRPO with binary verifiable rewards, response length 16,384, KL coeff 1e-4, entropy coeff 1e-3), and Qwen-OPD (on-policy distillation: student generates rollouts, teacher provides dense token-level supervision via KL with lambda=1.0 and universal logit distillation). All three post-trained variants use the same DeepScaler + SimpleRL data mixture.

Confidence is derived from top-k (k=20) next-token log-probabilities; token-level confidence c_l averages negative log-prob over the top-k set, and a relative token position rho_t = t/m in [0,1] allows cross-trace comparison. The framework evaluates three stages:

- Pre-CoT: query-level confidence C_pre computed from the prompt itself; problem is "solved" if majority vote over sampled traces matches ground truth; quality measured by AUROC and Prediction-Risk Reduction (PRR).
- Intra-CoT: group confidence G_{l,w} smoothed over a sliding window of size w; if it drops below threshold theta the trace is stopped early; thresholds swept to produce a matched-budget token-accuracy frontier (30%-100% retained budget). The online variant estimates expected trace length from 32 warmup traces (no future-length leakage) and uses adaptive window w_x = max(1, round(L_bar_x / D)) with D=10.
- Post-CoT: mean token confidence per completed trace used to rank traces; "Top-eta%" keeps the highest-confidence eta% before majority voting; "Most Confident" takes the single highest-confidence answer.

PosConf applies the same idea positionally: a weighted trace-level score C_w(tau) = sum w(t/m) c_t / sum w(t/m), where the indicator-style weighting restricts extraction to relative-position intervals I_intra and I_post that are reliable and correctly oriented for each model. Intervals are derived from AIME 2024 trajectory analysis.

Evaluation: 4 benchmarks (AIME 2024, AIME 2025, AMC 2023, MATH500), generation at temperature 0.6, top-p 0.95, max length 32,768; 320 offline trace budget, 256 for aggregation; top-k=20; all results averaged over 5 random-seed repetitions; identical chat-style prompt with "\boxed{}" answer formatting.

## Results & Highlights

- Pre-CoT difficulty estimation (Table 1): Qwen-OPD is best overall with average AUROC 0.644 and average PRR 0.345, including AUROC 0.734 and PRR 0.611 on MATH500. Qwen-RL is anti-informative Pre-CoT: AUROC below 0.5 on all four benchmarks and negative PRR (-0.478 average, -0.854 on MATH500), meaning its pre-reasoning confidence actively mis-ranks difficulty.
- Post-CoT answer aggregation (Table 2): Qwen-RL benefits most from confidence filtering. Top-10% confidence voting reaches 62.8% average accuracy, improving over its own 57.4% majority-vote baseline by 5.4 points; on AIME 2025 specifically it jumps from 29.3% to 40.7%. By contrast, for Qwen-SFT both Top-5% and Top-10% reduce average accuracy (e.g., Qwen-SFT Majority 59.8% vs Top-10% 54.4%), and for Qwen-OPD every confidence-based strategy underperforms its 58.5% majority-vote baseline.
- PosConf on aggregation: Qwen-RL PosConf-Top-5% reaches 63.5% average (63.3% for PosConf-Top-10%), and on AIME 2025 PosConf-Top-10% reaches 46.0%; the abstract-level headline is a 6.1-point average gain of PosConf RL aggregation over majority voting.
- PosConf on OPD early stopping (Figure 2 / Section 5): PosConf avoids OPD's later inverse-calibration region and consistently improves low-budget early stopping across all benchmarks with gains of more than 3 points on AIME-style problems (e.g., AIME 2025 from 23.46 to 26.72 at 30% budget, a 3.26-point gain; 4.32-point gain at the 40% budget), with the headline figure of up to 4.3 points under tight token budgets.
- PosConf on RL early stopping: at the 40% budget it lifts AIME 2024 from 22.11 to 26.10 and MATH500 from 77.06 to 78.97; at the 60% budget it lifts AIME 2025 from 20.61 to 24.26.
- Position-dependent confidence dynamics (Figure 3): SFT maintains a stable correct-vs-incorrect confidence gap, RL is uninformative early but becomes discriminative after a "path-commitment phase," and OPD crosses over so that incorrect traces eventually become more confident than correct ones — explaining all three stages' aggregation/early-stopping behaviors.

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are from the Eastern Institute of Technology, Ningbo (EIT-NLP) and The Hong Kong Polytechnic University. The work cites and builds directly on the strongest recent reasoning-calibration line (Fu et al. 2025 "Deep think with confidence" group-confidence; Zhu et al. 2025 hidden-state difficulty; Snell et al. 2024 and Wu et al. 2024 test-time scaling). The methodology is unusually disciplined for an empirical-calibration paper: controlled backbone and data, GRPO/SFT/OPD training configurations fully specified in Appendix B, 5-seed repeated evaluation, identical prompts and answer extraction across models, future-length-leakage avoidance in the online Intra-CoT sweep, and a public code release at https://github.com/EIT-NLP/Post-Training-Calibration.

## Critical Assessment

- Novelty: Genuine. Prior work (Kadavath 2022, Tian 2023, Xiong 2024, Fu 2025) treats calibration either as a final-output property or as an aggregate model trait; this is the first controlled, same-backbone/same-data decomposition of SFT/RL/OPD effects on stage-wise and position-wise confidence. The OPD "inverse calibration" finding is non-obvious and the most interesting single observation.
- Rigor: High for the claims made. The controlled setup (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, identical DeepScaler+SimpleRL mix, identical DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B teacher) is the right design for isolating the post-training objective, and 5-seed repetition with shared prompts/extraction is good practice. AUROC and PRR are appropriate for Pre-CoT; matched-budget frontiers are appropriate for Intra-CoT.
- Weak baselines / missing controls: PosConf's relative-position intervals (Table 3) are derived from AIME 2024 trajectories and then applied to AIME 2024, AIME 2025, AMC 23, and MATH500 — there is no held-out interval-selection set, so the interval hyperparameters (e.g., OPD's [0, 0.6]) are chosen on data correlated with the evaluation, a mild but real data-quirk/tuning risk. There is no comparison against alternative uncertainty signals (semantic uncertainty, entropy, sample-consistency, self-consistency-based verifiers) that the paper itself cites; the token top-k=20 confidence is the only internal signal studied. There is also no comparison to a learned verifier / process-reward-model Post-CoT aggregator, which is the obvious competing approach for the 62.8% Top-10% RL result.
- Data quirk to flag: "solved" Pre-CoT labels are defined per-model via majority vote, so a problem can be labeled easy for one paradigm and hard for another — this is methodologically defensible but means the AUROC numbers across models are not on identical ground-truth labels and should not be compared as a pure ranking of "calibration skill" without that caveat.
- Reproducibility: Strong on paper (full Appendix B/C configs, Algorithm 1 for the sweep, public GitHub repo). The main reproducibility risk is compute: 320 offline traces per problem, 256 for aggregation, 5 seeds, 4 benchmarks across 4 models is a heavy sampling budget, and the OPD/RL training runs (8x H20 GPUs for RL) are non-trivial to re-run.
- Significance: Conceptually important for the test-time-scaling and reasoning-uncertainty community — the "stage-wise and position-aware" framing is actionable and likely to be adopted. Practically, the gains are modest in absolute accuracy (a few points) and concentrate in the low-budget regime, so the impact is more on efficient inference than on SOTA accuracy.

## Strengths

- Clean controlled experimental design that actually isolates the post-training objective rather than conflating it with data or model family.
- The three-stage framework is a useful conceptual lens that maps cleanly onto real inference-time decisions (routing, early stopping, aggregation).
- Discovery of OPD's inverse calibration and RL's path-commitment phase is genuinely informative and well-supported by trajectory plots (Figures 3 and 4).
- PosConf is a simple, model-specific intervention with consistent low-budget gains, and it is cheap to implement on top of existing logits.
- Strong reproducibility artifacts (configs, algorithm pseudocode, code release).

## Limitations

- Scope is narrow: only mathematical reasoning, only the Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct family, only one teacher (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B). The authors flag this themselves, but it leaves open whether the OPD inverse-calibration effect is specific to KL-based on-policy distillation from this teacher or a general property.
- Only one internal confidence signal (top-k=20 token log-probability) is studied; entropy, semantic uncertainty, sample-consistency, and hybrid estimators are explicitly listed as unexplored.
- PosConf intervals are derived from AIME 2024 and reused across benchmarks without a held-out interval-selection protocol, inviting mild overfitting concerns.
- No comparison against learned verifiers or process reward models for Post-CoT aggregation, which is the most direct competing technology for "pick the reliable trace."
- Absolute accuracy gains are modest (single-digit points) and concentrated in tight-budget regimes; under generous compute budgets the advantage over majority voting shrinks.
- Pre-CoT "solved" labels are model-specific, so cross-model AUROC comparisons carry a definitional caveat the paper does not emphasize.

## Relevance

这项工作与推理时计算扩展（test-time compute scaling）、推理模型后训练（SFT/RL/蒸馏）以及 LLM 不确定性/校准评估高度相关，对想要用置信度做难度路由、在线早停或多采样答案聚合的研究者提供了直接可用的阶段化与位置化方法论，尤其契合当前关于"如何在受限 token 预算下高效采样与聚合 CoT"的研究兴趣。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the stage-dependent and position-dependent calibration patterns (especially OPD's inverse calibration and RL's path-commitment phase) survive at larger scales (e.g., 32B, 72B) and in non-Qwen families (Llama, Mistral, DeepSeek), or are they artifacts of Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct + this specific teacher?
- Does OPD's inverse calibration generalize beyond KL-based on-policy distillation — e.g., does it appear with sequence-level or hidden-state distillation, and is it driven by the teacher's own confidence trajectory being copied onto incorrect student rollouts?
- How does PosConf interact with stronger Post-CoT aggregators such as learned process reward models or self-consistency verifiers — does position-aware confidence remain additive, or does a verifier subsume it?
- Can the reliable relative-position intervals be selected online (per-problem) rather than tuned on AIME 2024, removing the held-out-set concern?
- On non-mathematical reasoning (code generation, multi-hop QA, agentic tool use), which stage dominates — does the "OPD best Pre-CoT, SFT best Intra-CoT, RL best Post-CoT" ordering still hold?
- How do entropy-based and semantic-uncertainty signals compare to top-k token confidence at each of the three stages, and can a hybrid signal beat PosConf?
