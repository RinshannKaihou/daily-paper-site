> **一句话总结：** 提出一种自演化 LLM Agent harness 框架 GSME，把"提出修改"与"归因增益"分离——由更强的模型诊断失败并写出补丁，由确定性代码负责采样、三道门控（有效性/激活/配对 2σ 显著性）与封存测试，跨 7 个领域在冻结模型上获得 +9~+15.5pp 的可信 held-out 增益（保留训练增益的 86–147%），并发现"病理→补丁"匹配律随模型而变、可迁移的是诊断-归因闭环本身。

> **TL;DR:** The authors auto-evolve the harness (prompts, knowledge, runtime, config) around a frozen LLM agent by separating proposal from credit: a stronger evolver diagnoses (where×why) pathologies and writes patches, while deterministic code owns sampling, three gates (validity, activation, paired-2σ significance), and a sealed post-evolution test. Across seven domains (terminal-bench-2, EvoAgentBench's LiveCode/Omni-MATH/BrowseComp+/GDPval/SWE-bench, AppWorld) with a frozen qwen3.6-27B, the train-selected harness earns +9 to +15.5pp sealed-test gains retaining 86–147% of the training lift (e.g., AppWorld +15.5pp, z=6.44 vs. vanilla), and a cross-model study on AppWorld (27B vs. Qwen3.5-397B vs. Gemini 3 Flash) shows the winning patch tracks each model's dominant pathology rather than its family — the transferable artifact is the loop, not any harness.

## 核心贡献

- **可审计的自演化闭环（提出与归因分离）**：在 git 追踪的演化树上完整运行；evolver 负责语义（读轨迹、定 why、写补丁），确定性代码负责全部采样、三道门控、配对显著性检验与记账，使每个被记功的改进都可复现、可归因。
- **Gated Semantic MAP-Elites (GSME) 归档及其价值证据**：以 (where×why) 病理为键的分类质量-多样性归档，每格一个门控精英，并支持跨格重组。关键依据——六领域中四个最优 harness 来自跨格重组，且被接受的补丁覆盖 prompt/knowledge/runtime/config 四个 lever，而非塌缩到 prompt 微调。
- **held-out 泛化的正向证据**：六项 benchmark 的封存测试增益为 +9~+15.5pp，保留训练增益的 86–147%，在 harness 优化文献普遍报告过拟合的背景下给出正向结果。
- **evolved harness 是模型特定的，可迁移的是闭环**：跨模型研究揭示"病理→补丁"匹配律——engagement→verify-finalize 在 27B 上奏效，careless→submit-verify 在 397B 与 Gemini 3 Flash 上跨家族复现；将"自改进 agent"重新理解为"对特定模型失效分布的纠正补丁拟合"。

## Key Contributions

- **An auditable self-evolution loop separating proposing from crediting.** A complete, reproducible loop over a git-tracked evolution tree in which the evolver owns all semantic steps while deterministic code owns sampling, measurement, the validity/activation/paired-2σ significance gates, aggregation, and bookkeeping — so every credited improvement is reproducible and attributable.
- **Gated Semantic MAP-Elites (GSME) with evidence it earns its keep.** A categorical quality-diversity archive keyed on the diagnosed (where×why) pathology rather than the tasks fixed, with one gated elite per cell and cross-cell recombination. The payoff: on four of six domains the credited harness is a cross-cell recombination, and accepted edits span all four levers (prompt/knowledge/runtime/config) instead of collapsing to prompt edits alone.
- **Held-out evidence the gains generalize.** Sealed-test gains of +9 to +15.5pp retain 86–147% of the training lift on every credited benchmark — a positive result against a harness-optimization literature that documents overfitting.
- **Evolved harnesses are model-specific; the loop is what transfers.** The dominant pathology and winning patch change with the model (a pathology-to-patch matching law: engagement→verify-finalize on qwen3.6-27B, careless→submit-verify on Qwen3.5-397B and Gemini 3 Flash, the latter crossing two model families). This reframes harness self-evolution as fitting a corrective patch to a specific model's failure distribution.

## Methodology

**Problem setup — harness vs. kernel.** The agent is a frozen model M wrapped in a harness H (system/role prompt, injected knowledge and tool definitions, control loop with recovery/finalization, runtime config). Evolution searches over H with M held constant. H is partitioned into a **kernel** (measurement/evaluation code, the self-evolution machinery, the product/interface contract) that must never be touched, and a **mutable surface** X of four coarsened levers: prompt, knowledge, runtime, config. Each edit carries a two-axis coordinate: *where* (bound mechanically from the edited file) and *why* (the diagnosed failure pathology, e.g., method-lock-in, thinking-runaway, premature finalization — assigned by the diagnosing model, which concentrates modeling error on that one axis).

**The self-evolution loop (paired frozen agent + separate evolver).** A separate stronger evolver D (Claude Opus 4.8 via Claude Code) diagnoses the frozen task agent's failures and edits its harness while the agent stays frozen. Coldstart scores vanilla on the full train set at K=3 for a per-task "thick ledger" that locks the baseline and supplies all variance estimates. Each round runs a cost-graded funnel: evolver designs 3–4 env-gated candidates → zero-inference **preflight** drops candidates whose activation beacon never fired → ~15–30 anchor-task **probe** at K=1 culls egregious losers at a 1.5σ_screen margin → **estimator** stratified subset at K=3 navigates round-to-round → **full set at K=3** under a per-task paired test gives the final credited number (never a subset headline). Termination stops when no candidate beats vanilla for P=10 consecutive rounds or round cap R=20 is reached; termination never touches the test set. Division of labor is strict: the model never performs quantitative judgments; the paired test and aggregation are computed, not estimated.

**Gated Semantic MAP-Elites (GSME).** A categorical (coarse, handful-of-cells) archive keyed on (where×why) pathology, not task identity — an anti-overfitting inductive bias. One gated elite per cell (a patch enters only after clearing the gates). Selection is **quality-biased** (each round extends the global-best harness) with **cross-cell recombination** (stacking elites from other cells) rather than uniform MAP-Elites illumination. The substrate is a git tree: each candidate is a branch+commit (root=vanilla) with a per-node ledger.

**Three gates (in increasing cost order).**
1. *Validity gate* — environment health pre-check (sandbox/network/deps/verifier); infrastructure failures (missing verifier output, sandbox crash, verifier timeout) are re-run up to twice and, if still failing, scored 0 but kept in the denominator.
2. *Activation gate* — each mechanism emits an activation beacon; effects on trials where the mechanism never fired are not attributed to it.
3. *Significance gate* — navigation uses K=3 mean pass@1 > vanilla; a *credited* gain requires a paired 2σ test on the same task set (σ from per-task paired differences, z≥1.96). Pairing removes between-task difficulty variance.

What is reported is the **sealed test**: withheld from the evolving agent throughout, scored once after evolution, summarized by *retention* = held-out lift ÷ training lift. No test signal informs diagnosis, design, parent selection, or termination.

## Results & Highlights

- **Sealed-test lift on the frozen qwen3.6-27B clears the paired-2σ credit bar on six domains** (Table 1): TB2 +9.3pp (z=2.71, retention 147% of a +6.3pp train gain); LiveCode +13.7pp (z=2.65, 86%); Omni-MATH +11.7pp (z=3.66, 115%); BrowseComp+ +13.9pp (z=3.96, 88%); GDPval +9.2pp (z=2.13, 109%); AppWorld +15.5pp (z=6.44, n=168, retention 87%) — the strongest credit.
- **SWE-bench is reported as preliminary, not stretched.** The train-selected harness (repeated-tool-call breaker + verify-falsify finalize) is +13.9pp on train (n=101) and +5.1pp on held-out, but at n=26 this sits below the paired-2σ bar (z=0.78); the authors credit only what the sealed test confirms rather than relax the bar.
- **Retention does not collapse: 86–147% across all credited benchmarks** — three benchmarks exceed their training gain and three retain 86–88%, against a literature documenting overfitting.
- **Gains expand solvable tasks, not just per-attempt hit rate:** best-of-3 (pass@3) rises on every credited domain (+5.6 to +15.4pp), so the evolved harness is not mere variance reduction.
- **Selective recovery beats brute-force alternatives (Table 2, LiveCode train, K=3 pass@1):** vanilla 64.6 → +budget 131072 tokens 71.1 (+6.5pp) → blanket thinking-off toggle 73.2 (+8.6pp) → selective sticky recovery 79.4 (+14.8pp). A 16× larger token budget and a one-line global toggle both fail to reproduce the targeted recovery's gain.
- **The gate machinery is two-sided and rejects phantom gains:** on TB2 it flagged and culled a significantly *negative* candidate (−6.4pp); a single-run "mean improves" rule would credit a truly-neutral mechanism as a win ~60% of the time (~25% as a ≥3pp "win"), and on TB2's ten candidate mechanisms a deterministic Δ>0 rule credits four, including context-compact whose activation beacon fired zero times (only the activation gate rejects it).
- **Cross-model pathology→patch matching law (Table 3, AppWorld sealed Δ pass@1):** 27B Qwen 3.6 matches VF (verify-finalize) at +15.5pp while SV (submit-verify) gives only +1.2; Qwen3.5-397B matches SV at +13.6 (retention 1.18) while VF gives +0.2; Gemini 3 Flash (Google) matches SV at +13.5pp (z=4.15), reproducing the careless→checklist match across model *families*. Off the diagonal, mismatched patches are near-zero on the Qwen pair (a near-clean dissociation).
- **Recombination earns its keep:** on four of six domains the credited harness is a cross-cell recombination (e.g., the BrowseComp+ running example: VF+RBR sealed +13.9pp beats either component alone), and accepted edits span all four levers, countering the reported failure mode where greedy optimizers collapse to safe prompt edits.
- **Cost-cutting by tree-aware score borrowing (Theorem 1):** reproduces every credited decision at 67% fewer child trials — validated offline 14/14 and live 7/7.

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are Xiaotian Luo, Fengxingyu Wang, Chuanrui Hu, Dizhan Xue, and Yafeng Deng (corresponding) from EverMind AI and Shanda Group. The work positions itself explicitly within a concurrent 2026 line on coding-agent harness evolution (Self-Harness, HarnessFix, AHE, Meta-Harness, HarnessX, EvoTrainer), differentiating on three combined elements the prior line lacks. The methodological discipline — paired per-task 2σ tests, a sealed test withheld from all decisions, mechanical env-gating so the unevolved harness is byte-identical to vanilla, honest reporting of SWE-bench as preliminary at n=26 — is unusually careful for this subfield and is the paper's core credibility signal. The end-to-end loop runs via Claude Opus 4.8 through Claude Code, and the evolver model is named for reproducibility.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The two pieces (a pathology-keyed quality-diversity archive; propose-but-never-credit separation with paired-2σ + a sealed test) are individually close to existing work — Self-Harness already clusters failures by a (cause, status, mechanism) signature akin to where/why, and VeRO (Ursekar et al. 2026) already documents the held-out discipline. The genuine contribution is *combining* the categorical QD archive with cross-cell recombination, the three-gate crediting stack, and a retention scalar on a once-scored sealed test, which no prior work combines.
- **Rigor:** Strongest aspect. Crediting is owned by deterministic code with a paired per-task 2σ test; the sealed test is scored once; the authors publicly retract SWE-bench rather than stretch the bar at n=26; they quantify how non-statistical loops manufacture phantom gains (~60% false-credit rate); and they report a culled *negative* candidate (−6.4pp on TB2), demonstrating the machinery is two-sided.
- **Reproducibility:** Reasonable. git-tracked evolution tree with per-node ledgers, named frozen model (qwen3.6-27B), named evolver (Claude Opus 4.8), env-gated patches defaulting off. However, full per-task ledgers and exact seeds are not obviously released, and the AppWorld verifier's known ~9pp run-to-run drift is acknowledged as a floor that hides smaller effects.
- **Significance:** The positive generalization result (86–147% retention) is a genuine counter-data point in a literature full of overfitting claims, and the cross-model pathology→patch matching law is a real conceptual reframing (harness as corrective patch to a failure distribution). Scope is bounded: the authors are explicit that this pays off most on mid-capability deployable models, and frontier models are out of scope.
- **Red flags / data quirks:** (1) **Verifier dependence** is the central caveat — paired-2σ certifies a gain is real *on the measured metric*, not that the metric is valid; a harness that games a weak checker would still be credited. (2) The dominant credited win is largely one mechanism (recovery of "thinking-runaway" empty turns) across reasoning-heavy domains, raising the question of whether GSME earns its keep beyond that recurring patch — though the AppWorld cross-model dissociation partially addresses this. (3) Retention is a ratio and is noisy near the significance floor; the load-bearing claim is the absolute credited held-out gain, with retention only corroboration. (4) Test-set withholding is not *mechanically* enforced (acknowledged as future work), relying on discipline. (5) The diagnostic label is an LLM hypothesis, not ground truth — mitigated by the rule "diagnosis proposes, measurement disposes," but the archive's coverage map inherits any labeling error.

## Strengths

- Statistically credible crediting: paired per-task 2σ tests plus a sealed, once-scored held-out test set a higher evidentiary bar than the dominant "peak pass rate over rounds on a single task set" protocol.
- Anti-overfitting design is principled: keying the QD archive on pathology rather than task identity, and reporting retention on a sealed test, directly targets the failure modes the paper criticizes in prior work.
- Intellectual honesty: SWE-bench is downgraded to preliminary at n=26 rather than massaged; a negative candidate is reported; the verifier-dependence caveat is stated plainly.
- The cross-model dissociation experiment is a clean ablation of *what transfers* (the loop) versus *what doesn't* (any specific harness), strengthening the conceptual claim.
- Accepted edits span all four levers rather than collapsing to prompt edits, empirically demonstrating the archive resists the greedy-collapse failure mode.

## Limitations

- **Verifier dependence:** the guarantee is relative to the reward signal; a gameable or invalid per-task verifier is inherited, and the method applies only where a reliable per-task verifier exists. Sealing the test guards against overfitting/noise, not against an invalid reward.
- **Proxy objective and short horizon:** per-task benchmark success is a verifiable but short-horizon proxy; fuzzy objectives (research taste, novelty) and long-term qualities (robustness, maintainability) are not captured.
- **Improvement requires dense repeated scoring:** every candidate is re-evaluated on graded tasks, making evolution expensive and binding it to domains/cadences with frequent scored evaluation; this may not transfer to open-ended settings with diffuse, delayed feedback.
- **Small/narrow benchmarks:** several held-out sets are small (e.g., SWE-bench n=26, TB2 n≈53), making retention noisy and leaving some real effects below the significance bar.
- **Enforcement gap:** withholding the test set relies on discipline rather than mechanical enforcement (acknowledged future work).
- **Scope:** most headroom is on mid-capability deployable models; frontier models, which fail less, lie outside the method's claimed benefit.

## Relevance

This paper sits squarely in LLM-agent and agent-self-evolution research: it directly tackles how to *credibly* auto-improve agent scaffolding (harness) around a frozen model, with rigorous statistical crediting and held-out generalization — relevant to anyone building, deploying, or benchmarking LLM agents and agentic systems. The pathology-keyed quality-diversity search and the "separate proposing from crediting" principle are transferable design lessons for agent optimization, prompt/workflow search, and self-improving agent loops more broadly.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the pathology→patch matching law extend when the frozen model is updated (e.g., via RLHF or continual pre-training) rather than swapped — does the diagnosed pathology drift continuously, and does a fitted patch decay or transfer?
- Can the crediting stack be adapted to weaker or self-generated supervision (no reliable per-task verifier) without losing the paired-2σ trustworthiness guarantee — the authors flag this as the central open problem?
- Does the finding that a non-statistical loop manufactures phantom gains ~60% of the time quantify the inflation in published harness-evolution numbers (Self-Harness, AHE's terminal-bench 69.7→77.0, HarnessX), and would re-scoring them under paired-2σ survive?
- Since the dominant credited win is recovery of "thinking-runaway" empty turns across reasoning-heavy domains, how much of the +9–15.5pp is GSME's contribution versus a single high-value runtime patch — and is there a domain where the winning harness is *not* a recombination or a recovery/verify-finalize patch?
- Could tree-aware score borrowing (67% fewer trials) be combined with active learning over the anchor probe to make the loop economical at production benchmark scale, where full K=3 re-evaluation is infeasible?
- Is the near-clean off-diagonal dissociation on the Qwen pair an artifact of those two models sharing a family, and would a third non-Qwen, non-Google family still reproduce the careless→submit-verify match?
