Why this mattersrelevance

A lightweight, operator-attributable diagnostic for logical invariance in reasoning models. The 92%→20% quantifier collapse under reasoning effort signals RL training may destabilize inference in legal, medical, and KB-coherence settings.

Critical edgecaveat

The headline "no improvement" rests on a single 50-family quantifier category; validation is author self-audit with no inter-annotator agreement, and there is no head-to-head against FOLIO / LogicBench.

Methodology

01

CRTBench measures logical invariance: equivalent reformulations of one question must yield consistent answers. A question family = 1 base + 4 reformulations, drawn from three transformation classes:

  • Equivalence-preserving — contrapositive, double negation, De Morgan, quantifier rewrite, passive voice (label unchanged).
  • Answer-reversing — negation flip (label is the logical complement); "Cannot be determined" stays fixed.
  • Surface control — passive voice as a logical no-op.
  • Two metrics — base accuracy vs. family consistency (base + all 4 reformulations correct; one miss = inconsistent).
  • Protocol — zero-shot, T=0, forced Yes / No / Cannot be determined; only 5/1750 unparseable (all Gemini 2.5 Flash, 0.29%).

Results & Highlights

02
  • Reasoning rescues the small model only — GPT-5.4-mini consistency 60.3%→85.4% (+25.1pp); GPT-5.4 stays pinned at 86.6%.
  • Quantifier collapse under reasoning — GPT-5.4 quantifier family consistency 92%→20%; spurious "Cannot be determined" abstentions explode 5→119 (all wrong).
  • Failures are operator-level — contrapositive hardest for GPT-5.4-mini (72.4% vs 98.7% passive); De Morgan near-ceiling for all (96–100%).
  • CoT prompting negligible — +1.4pp / +0.0 / +0.0; none significant at α=0.05.

Strengths

  • Causally attributable transformation taxonomy — isolates which logical operator breaks, unlike paraphrase tests.
  • Clean accuracy-vs-consistency gap framing with per-transformation and per-category breakdowns.
  • Quantifier collapse is a striking, mechanism-level finding (abstentions 5→119, all wrong).
  • Honest limitations section; fully disclosed model IDs, prompts, extraction heuristic, dataset stats.

Limitations

  • Template-generated, self-audited — no formal inter-annotator naturalness study; only controlled reformulations.
  • Small (350 families / 1,750 questions), restricted to Yes/No/Cannot-be-determined answers.
  • Single zero-shot prompt template; CoT null result is bound to that one template.
  • Proprietary April-2026 API snapshots may drift; code/data pledged only upon acceptance.

Questions for further exploration

04
  1. Does the GPT-5.4(r) quantifier collapse (92→20%) replicate across independent API snapshots and open-weight reasoning models (R1-distill, Qwen3-thinking), or is it an artifact of one RL checkpoint?
  2. What happens to the gap under natural (non-templated) paraphrases from a separate LLM — does the transformation-level attribution still hold?
  3. Would a softer per-question pairwise consistency metric change the model ranking, especially in the tightly-clustered mid-tier (~86–89%)?
  4. Can consistency-aware training (penalizing contradictory answers across reformulations) close the gap without inducing the quantifier abstention pathology?
  5. How does the gap scale with chain length — do nested-negation failures compound linearly or super-linearly?
  6. Is the "Cannot be determined" inflation specific to universal/existential quantifier phrasing, or does it generalize to other undecidable-but-valid inference patterns?
