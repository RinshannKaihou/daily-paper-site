> **一句话总结：** 通过"一位比特实验"（仅翻转是否向生成器暴露金答案）因果隔离出"答案泄露"现象——在 Qwen3-8B 上自蒸馏"看过答案的 CoT"使 MATH-500 下降 16.2 分、AIME 奥赛题下降 27.2 分，并且这种伤害无法被任何基于最终答案正确性的过滤器检测到，作者用答案首次出现位置（AFR）作为可在训练前预测跨模型惩罚的廉价信号。

> **TL;DR:** In an otherwise identical self-distillation pipeline, flipping only whether the generator sees the gold answer ("answer-conditioning") costs Qwen3-8B 16.2 MATH-500 points and 27.2 points on olympiad AIME 2024–2025; the harm stems from chains that rationalize backward toward a known answer rather than derive it, is invisible to any final-answer correctness filter, but is predicted pre-training by the answer-first rate (AFR) signature read off unlabeled generations across eight thinking models from four families, and partly repaired by a derive-first instruction that recovers about two-thirds of the loss.

## 核心贡献

- **因果隔离"答案泄露"**：设计"一位比特实验"（one-bit experiment），固定生成器、题目集（935 道数学题）、正确性过滤器和 SFT 训练配方，只翻转"生成时是否向模型展示金答案"，从而把 16.2 分 MATH-500 的下降明确归因于答案条件化本身，而非模型或数据量。
- **可解释的机制**：提出"答案前置率"（Answer-First Rate, AFR）作为可测症状——看过答案的链倾向于在思考块前 20% 就写出最终答案，本质是从低熵分布 P(c|q,a(q)) 采样得到的"离策略合理化"，而非从 P(c|q) 真正搜索得到。
- **数据级因果定位与修复**：在同一泄露语料内部按 AFR 阈值切分，差异中的差异（DiD）实验（三种子均值 +16.9）证明"答案前置链"承担了大部分伤害；剔除每条链中首次给出答案的那一行（仅约 1.4% 字符）就恢复了 29.8 分差距中的 83%。
- **训练前跨模型预测器**：∆AFR 签名在 8 个思考模型、4 个模型家族上以 r=0.96 排序惩罚幅度，并在留一家族设置下预测完全独立的 Llama-Nemotron-8B（第三家族）以 MAE 2.9 分命中预测（预测 17.4，实测 17.6）。
- **廉价且实用的缓解**：在必须给答案的场景，"先推导-后揭晓"（derive-first）指令恢复约三分之二的惩罚；答案盲生成的 SFT 在匹配预算下以 95.1 击败金数据 SFT 的 90.8。

## Key Contributions

- **Causal isolation of answer-conditioning cost** via the "one-bit experiment": a single flip — whether the gold answer is shown to the generator — explains a 16.2-point MATH-500 penalty on Qwen3-8B, holding generator, problems, correctness filter, data volume, and chain length fixed (length-matched arm scores 95.0).
- **Mechanism characterization** with the Answer-First Rate (AFR) signature: leaked chains state the answer within the first 20% of the think block far more often (47.4% vs 26.6% blind), an off-policy shift toward P(c|q,a(q)) rationalizations; the instruction to "rationalize toward the answer" rather than bare answer visibility carries the harm (Table 1A: derive-first reduces penalty to 4.3, final-check to 1.3, out-of-band to 0.7).
- **Within-corpus carrier experiment**: a difference-in-differences design (Leaked-Early vs Leaked-Late minus blind twins) shows the rationalized chains carry most of the harm (+16.9 three-seed DiD); excising the single answer-stating line recovers 83% of the 29.8-point gap.
- **Pre-fine-tuning predictor** (Table 3, 4): ∆AFR orders the leakage penalty across eight thinking models from four families (Qwen3, DeepSeek-R1-Distill, GLM-Z1, Llama-Nemotron) at r = 0.96, with a leave-one-family-out MAE of 2.9 points; the third-family Nemotron prediction (17.4 predicted vs 17.6 observed) was made before training.
- **Cheap, deployable mitigations**: a derive-first instruction recovers ~2/3 of the penalty while keeping every example; answer-blind self-distillation matches base (95.1 vs 95.2) and beats gold-data SFT (90.8) at the same 10,240-token budget.

## Methodology

The core design is the **one-bit experiment** (Figure 1). All SFT students are full-parameter fine-tunes of Qwen3-8B under one fixed recipe (DeepSpeed ZeRO-3, 1 epoch, sequence length 10,240, learning rate 2×10⁻⁵, effective batch size 48). The training set is 935 math problems from the Nemotron-Cascade-SFT-Stage-2 math split, disjoint from every evaluation set. For each problem the generator M samples n=2 chains at temperature 0.6, top-p 0.95, top-k 20, with a 16,384-token thinking budget, under two prompts that differ only in answer visibility:

- **Blind prompt** — only the problem, derive the answer.
- **Leaked prompt** — problem plus gold answer a(q), write a reasoning trace reaching it.

Both arms keep only chains whose final boxed answer matches the gold (symbolic matching), intersect to the 935 problems solved under both, and fine-tune under the identical recipe, yielding S_blind and S_leaked. The reported leakage penalty is Δ = acc(S_blind) − acc(S_leaked).

The mechanism is measured by the **Answer-First Rate (AFR)** of a corpus C: AFR(C) = (1/|C|) Σ 1[pos(c) < 0.2], where pos(c) is the relative position of the first gold-answer mention inside the think block. The signature ∆AFR = AFR_leaked − AFR_blind is computed from a small unlabeled generation sample and used as a pre-fine-tuning predictor across 8 models.

The **carrier experiment** is a within-corpus 2×2: split the 935 leaked chains by AFR threshold (Leaked-Early 438, Leaked-Late 497) plus their blind twins (Blind-PE, Blind-PL), giving a difference-in-differences endpoint DiD = (Leaked-Late − Leaked-Early) − (Blind-PL − Blind-PE) that nets out problem-subset difficulty.

Robustness is enforced by (i) a length-matched control truncating each blind chain to its matched leaked length (95.0, ruling out length), (ii) an evaluation-budget sweep from 4K to 32K tokens, (iii) an instruction ablation at fixed answer visibility, (iv) generalization to code (MBPP+/HumanEval+), to a smaller student (Qwen3-1.7B trained on Qwen3-8B corpora), and to two cross-family teachers (Llama-Nemotron-Nano-8B, GLM-Z1-9B), and (v) inferential statistics (paired bootstrap, McNemar, KS test, leave-one-family-out) collected in Appendix I.

## Results & Highlights

- **Headline leakage penalty (Table 1)**: On MATH-500, the blind student reaches 95.1 vs the leaked student's 78.9, a **16.2-point** three-seed penalty on Qwen3-8B (base 95.2). The blind arm sits within seed noise of base; essentially the entire gap is leakage damage.
- **Penalty grows with difficulty (Table 1C)**: GSM8K 4.9 (95.4 vs 90.5), MATH-500 16.2, Minerva 10.2 (48.7 vs 38.5), and **AIME 2024–2025 (olympiad) 27.2 points** (71.7 vs 44.4). Within MATH-500 the penalty rises from 0.0 on Level 1 to 21.4 on Level 4; the pooled Level 4–5 band gives a 20.1-point penalty with real headroom.
- **Instruction ablation localizes the cause (Table 1A)**: At fixed answer visibility, "rationalize toward answer" 16.2, "derive-first" 4.3, "final-check" 1.3, "out-of-band" 0.7 — so the harm tracks the rationalization instruction, not bare visibility.
- **Within-corpus carrier (Table 1B)**: Leaked-Early 64.2 vs Leaked-Late 86.2, DiD = **+16.9** (seed range 12–21); excising the single answer-stating line (≈1.4% of characters) recovers 24.6 of 29.8 points (83%).
- **Cross-model predictor (Tables 3, 4, Figure 4)**: ∆AFR correlates with penalty at r = 0.960 (permutation p = 0.0003) across eight models. Llama-Nemotron-8B (independent third family) predicted 17.4, observed 17.6; Qwen3-1.7B predicted 24.4 vs observed 29.8; leave-one-family-out MAE = 2.9 points.
- **Generalization (Table 5)**: Penalty holds in code — MBPP+ 9.5 (68.3 vs 58.8), HumanEval+ 9.2 (72.8 vs 63.6); with a smaller student (Qwen3-1.7B from 8B corpora) +6.9 (85.7 vs 78.9); with cross-family teachers — Nemotron 14.8 (94.0 vs 79.2), GLM 10.2 (93.0 vs 82.8).
- **Vanishes without derivation (Appendix H)**: On MMLU (2000-problem subset) blind/leaked = 54.8/54.0 vs base 54.9; on GPQA-Diamond 37.6/40.2 vs base 41.9 — differences within noise, consistent with the mechanism prediction that selection-style tasks need no multi-step derivation.
- **STaR-style rescue still costs (Table 11)**: Leaking only on blind failures costs Qwen3-8B 4.0 points (95.2→91.2) and DS-Qwen-7B 1.5 points — even verified rescued chains are negative-value data.
- **Length-matched controls (Appendix G, H, Table 13)**: Length-matched blind 95.0 (vs leaked 78.9); GLM teacher length-matched 90.9 (vs leaked 82.8); MBPP+ length-matched 70.7 (vs leaked 59.2) — confirming content, not length, drives the penalty.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The core insight — that *how* a verified chain was generated matters, not just *whether* it ends correctly — is the first controlled isolation of answer-conditioning, distinguishing itself from TRICE (which reformulates the objective as latent-variable inference) by showing the bias is invisible to the standard correctness filter and predictable from unlabeled generations. The mechanism framing as an off-policy shift from P(c|q) to P(c|q,a(q)) is clean and theoretically legible.
- **Rigor**: Strong. The one-bit design, length-matched controls (95.0 vs 78.9 leaked), three-seed means for the headline numbers, paired bootstrap [12.2, 19.2] and McNemar 86-to-8 (p < 10⁻¹⁷) on the headline gap, KS test (D=0.291, p < 10⁻³⁴) on answer position, and a within-corpus DiD with bootstrap p < 10⁻⁴ hold up well. The leave-one-family-out MAE of 2.9 points and the third-family Nemotron prediction (17.4 vs 17.6) make the cross-model claim credible rather than circular.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Full-parameter SFT recipes, sampling parameters, harness details, base accuracies, and seed-level tables are all reported; code is released. Caveats: the cross-model fit rests on only n=8 models (Fisher-z 95% CI on r is wide, [0.79, 0.99]), and many Table 5 rows (code, cross-family teachers, cross-student) are single-seed and support only the *sign* of the effect, not exact magnitudes.
- **Significance**: High practical value for anyone running STaR/ReST/MetaMath/answer-augmentation pipelines — these are widely deployed recipes, and the paper shows their core "rescue" move can be net-negative even when every chain passes its test. The derive-first instruction and AFR-based teacher screening are cheap, actionable safeguards.
- **Red flags / weaknesses specific to this paper**:
  - The single-most-important weakness is the **small n for the cross-model predictor**: r=0.96 over 8 models is suggestive, not a law, and the authors admit the Fisher-z interval is wide. The Phi-4-reasoning anchor was set aside "for measurement reasons" (both arms collapsed to <5% accuracy) — its removal is reasonable but leaves the highest-∆AFR point unvalidated.
  - The within-corpus AFR split **partly confounds with problem difficulty**: the blind twins of Leaked-Early problems are themselves answer-first 48.9% of the time vs 6.0% for Leaked-Late (Appendix F), motivating the DiD but leaving the chain-level causal claim scoped to "chains marked by the signature," not to problem identity.
  - The **935-problem training set is small** and from a single corpus (Nemotron-Cascade-SFT-Stage-2 math split); whether the magnitude of the penalty generalizes to larger or differently-curated SFT mixtures is untested.
  - The cross-student (1.7B from 8B) transfer has a seed standard deviation of 8.9 on a +6.9 mean — the noisiest setting tested, with one of three seeds near zero (−0.2) — so the "harm travels with the data, not the student" claim is directionally supported but not magnitude-tight.
  - Multiple-choice benchmark numbers (MMLU, GPQA) are reported on a 2,000-problem MMLU subset and the 198-problem GPQA-Diamond; both arms sit near base with differences "within noise," which the authors honestly flag as absence of effect rather than a positive null.

## Strengths

- Clean causal identification via a true one-bit intervention with rigorous length-matched, instruction-ablation, and DiD controls.
- A pre-training predictor (∆AFR) that is cheap, model-agnostic, and validated on a held-out third family — directly useful for practitioners screening candidate teachers.
- Honest scoping: the paper is explicit that the effect is confined to derivation-bearing tasks and vanishes on selection-style benchmarks, and reports negative/null results (STaR-rescue cost, GPQA, MMLU) rather than burying them.
- Mechanism-to-intervention pipeline: the same AFR signature that diagnoses the harm also yields a cheap fix (derive-first instruction recovers ~2/3) and a cheap filter (excise the answer-stating line, recovers 83% within corpus).
- Statistical appendix is unusually thorough for an SFT paper.

## Limitations

- Cross-model predictor fits only 8 models; Fisher-z CI on r is wide, and Phi-4-reasoning (the highest-∆AFR anchor) could not be validated because both arms collapsed.
- Single-seed rows in Table 5 (code, cross-family teachers) support sign, not exact magnitude; cross-student transfer is the noisiest setting (SD 8.9 on a +6.9 mean).
- Training set is small (935 problems) and from one corpus; the 935-problem scope may not generalize to larger or differently-curated mixtures.
- The within-corpus AFR split partially tracks problem difficulty (Appendix F), so the chain-level carrier claim is scoped to "chains marked by the signature."
- Code-domain and student-output transfer (Section 4) are not length-controlled; the multiple-choice null rests on a 2,000-problem MMLU subset and 198-problem GPQA-Diamond.

## Relevance

与用户关注的大模型推理蒸馏、CoT 数据质量与自改进流水线（cs.CL / cs.LG / cs.AI）高度相关：它直接揭示 STaR、ReST、MetaMath 等常用"答案增广"做法在数学与代码可验证任务上的隐性伤害，并给出可在训练前用未标注生成物筛查教师模型的廉价方法（∆AFR），对日常构建或筛选推理蒸馏数据集具有即时指导价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the ∆AFR predictor extend to non-math/non-code reasoning where verification is softer (e.g., long-form science, multi-hop QA, agentic tool-use traces), or does the effect dissolve without a strict final-answer verifier?
- How does answer-leakage interact with RLHF/RLVR and on-policy distillation objectives (e.g., soft-KL on-policy distillation) — does the off-policy framing predict a similar penalty when the reward itself leaks the answer?
- Would scaling the training set from 935 to tens of thousands of problems dilute the penalty, or does the AFR signature remain the dominant predictor of per-corpus harm?
- Can the derive-first instruction be strengthened beyond "two-thirds recovery" — for example, by coupling it with a process-reward-model reranker or an entropy-based hindsight gate (cf. concurrent Zhang/Li 2026 work)?
- Does the train/test length inversion (leaked trains on shorter chains but generates longer, non-converging ones at test time) suggest a length-aware curriculum or inference-time fix that complements the derive-first instruction?
- Is the carrier result (excising one line recovers 83%) robust to non-English or non-boxed-answer formats, where the "answer-stating line" is less cleanly localized?
