Key Finding

A self-evolving agent with a governed five-layer memory lifts year-long health-support accuracy from 0.2% to 45.7%.

A Self-Evolving Agent for Longitudinal Personal Health Management ↗

Haoran Li, Jiebi Deng, Tong Jin, Jinghong Han, Yuxin Wang, Zexin Wang, Qingyi Si, Weikang Gong, Xiahai Zhuang, Jia You, Wei Cheng, Jianfeng Feng, Hongcheng Guo · Fudan University · BUCM · HUST · JD.com

arXiv:2607.13940  ·  posted 2026-07-15  ·  code: github.com/HC-Guo/HealthClaw

HealthClaw self-evolving-agent agent-memory longitudinal-memory personal-health llm-agent

Key Metric

0.2% → 45.7%

Rubric answer accuracy on 900 longitudinal probes (HealthClaw-YearLong, 20 users × 365 days), with 71.7% less prompt context than full-history prompting.

Why This Matters

A concrete self-evolving agent for longitudinal care — and an explicit BEHSOF failure where memory amplifies a base-model prior, directly informing the stability and calibration of self-evolving systems.

Critical Edge

Headline biomedical gains may be inflated by tool-side same-source leakage, and full-history prompting actually beats HealthClaw on raw accuracy (0.612 vs. 0.457) — the 0.2% baseline is near-strawman.

Methodology

Personal-health support is modeled as a sequence of episodes, each running a four-stage loop. After each episode, an induction step decides what each piece of the encounter becomes in future memory — a profile fact, a revised procedure, an episodic trace, or excluded. Writeback of sensitive material can be kept local, minimized, or disabled; it is disabled entirely during privacy probes.

Perceive
Assemble request, signals, retrieved memory
Reason
Constrained plan under shared knowledge + profile
Act
Respond / invoke tools with intermediate checks
Induce
Assign each item a future role & disclosure policy

Five-layer governed memory — shared governance (L0–L1) is never updated by user episodes; user-level state (L2–L4) is selectively written.

L0
Behaviour / safety / escalation
L1
Shared knowledge + tool routing
L2
Personal profile (conditions, meds, goals)
L3
Reusable task SOPs
L4
Episodic traces

Backbone: qwen3.7-plus (text) / qwen3-vl-plus (vision) at temperature 0.0. Stats: paired Wilcoxon / exact McNemar, 10k–20k bootstrap CIs, BH-FDR correction (seed 20260705). Graded by a Qwen-3.7 evaluator with fixed rubrics — no human ratings.

Longitudinal support (n=900). Answer accuracy 0.2% → 45.7%; rubric score 0.182 → 0.568; reference-fact coverage 0.027 → 0.524 — all FDR-significant.

Privacy (n=100). HealthClaw answer accuracy 0.640 vs. current-only 0.440 and full-history 0.400; constraint violations 24% vs. 41% / 53%; unauthorized disclosure 5% vs. 18% / 15%.

Trade-off. Full-history prompting still wins on raw recall (0.612 accuracy) but at 64,493 vs. 18,274 mean prompt chars — exposing the recall-vs-selective-use trade-off.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Well-motivated problem (selective change vs. recall) with layers that map onto it directly.
  • Statistical care well above the agent-paper median: paired designs, FDR, bootstrap CIs, fixed seeds, leakage controls.
  • Honest reporting: non-significant tasks, the BEHSOF failure, and the same-source-overlap caveat are all disclosed.
  • Public code + sanitized benchmark (HealthClaw-YearLong) — a real community contribution.
  • Privacy treated as a first-class measurable property, not a footnote.

Limitations

  • All evidence is offline and synthetic; no real patients, no prospective use, no clinician in the loop.
  • Graded by a Qwen-3.7 evaluator with no human ratings → LLM-judge circularity and rubric-design bias.
  • Nine-task comparison is not a same-model ablation; several tools share data with their benchmarks.
  • Current-only 0.2% baseline is near-strawman; full-history actually wins on raw accuracy (0.612 vs. 0.457).
  • Two tasks show no significant primary gain (SOFA, diabetes); improvement is broad but not uniform.

Open Questions

  1. How does the induction step behave under contradictory evidence (a corrected allergy vs. a superseded measurement) and under distribution shift from synthetic to real longitudinal records?
  2. Can the same-source-overlap bias be isolated by swapping MedMNIST / RG-DermNet-derived tools for independent classifiers — do the +60 / +40 pp gains survive?
  3. What is the calibration and drift of L2 / L3 memory far beyond 365 days — does induction accumulate stale or self-reinforcing procedures (as BEHSOF suggests)?
  4. How sensitive are the headline numbers to the Qwen-3.7 evaluator and rubric — would a human-graded subset or a different judge change the 0.2% → 45.7% claim?
  5. Could the proposed counter-evidence requirement be formalized as a memory-write gate (calibrated CI + minimum counter-prior) and tested directly against the reinforcement-of-priors failure?
