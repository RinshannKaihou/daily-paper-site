How it works & how it performed

quantizer §3 + compiler §4

Methodology · two-stage codesign

Quantizer — for each linear layer, a max-heap greedy waterfilling promotes channels to the widest feasible bit-width from the {2,3,4,8,16} palette until the fractional budget B·d_in is exhausted.

  1. Activation-aware error proxy. E_b[ℓ][j] ≈ ||w̃−w||² · E[x_j²] in one pass + precomputed second moments.
  2. Waterfilling under B. Greedy max-heap on gain G=(E_b−E_b′)/(b′−b) until budget spent.
  3. ISA-aware quanta snap. Round channel counts to backend tiles (AVX2 int8 ×32, LUT quanta); error-aware reassign keeps B_real≈B.
  4. Compiler layout regularization. Factor O=(A·P_Wᵀ)·(P_W·W); cluster same-bit rows; dispatch low-bit → T-MAC LUT, 8/16-bit → OpenBLAS SIMD.
  5. DAG permutation propagation. Absorb downstream activation perms into upstream weights via a finite graph-labeling problem — shuffles only at block boundaries.

§ 02 / 03

Strengths vs. Limitations

critical assessment

▲ Strengths

  • Genuinely end-to-end — one budget B predicts quality, memory, latency, throughput and energy.
  • Strong systems rigor — three CPU tiers, RAPL energy, full working-set memory (KV cache + FP16 residue + live activations), not weight-only theory.
  • Up to 70.8% reorder-traffic cut vs the best prior fixed/template policy, with a clean per-token A16-traffic model.
  • Compile-time factorization never materializes P_W at runtime; merge rule spans transformer-block boundaries.
  • Honest about Slim-LLM's marginal edge at 5–6 b and about GPU-only baselines blocking direct runtime comparison.

▼ Limitations

  • No public code; quanta values and ARM NEON/SVE retargeting under-specified.
  • Only three models (3B/13B/32B); no 70B+ validation, no batch>1 study.
  • AMQ missing on Falcon-H1-3B (†); runtime rests on a single baseline.
  • Non-standard "capped multiple-choice accuracy" hinders cross-paper comparison.
  • No ablation on calibration size, proxy choice, or α_b grid search.
  • Palette caps the ceiling — no 5/6/7-bit entries by design.
Authors & credibility. UC Irvine Imani Lab, authors of T-SAR (arXiv:2511.13676) — PolyQ inherits its W{2,3,4}A16 LUT backend. Evaluation is unusually thorough on the systems side; the genuinely novel contribution is the DAG-level permutation propagation (§4.4), while the per-channel waterfilling is closer to AWQ/Slim-LLM saliency in spirit.
§ 03 / 03

Open questions

for further exploration
  1. Is the 2.4% lower-bound gain robust, or an artifact of 128-sequence calibration? How does it shift at 32 / 512 sequences?
  2. Does DAG permutation propagation generalize to Mamba, hybrid SSM-transformer, or MoE routing where channels mix across attention/FFN boundaries?
  3. What is the quality/latency trade-off at 70B+ scale, where absolute reorder traffic and OpenBLAS SIMD dispatch scale differently?
  4. How sensitive is the E[x_j²] proxy to activation distribution shift between calibration and deployment (code vs natural language, long vs short context)?
  5. Can the quanta-snap stage extend to GPU group-quantization tiles, making the palette portable across CPU+GPU heterogeneous edge?
