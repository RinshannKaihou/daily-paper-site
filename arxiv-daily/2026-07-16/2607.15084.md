> **一句话总结：** 作者训练了 180 个开集人脸识别模型（全交叉设计：3 个 backbone × 3 个损失头 × 5 个训练身份数 × 4 个训练 epoch），通过四类基于 embedding 簇几何的统计量衡量 member/non-member 的可分性，发现训练身份数 nids 是主导因素（ANOVA 偏 η² ≥ 0.95），backbone 与损失头贡献很小（≤0.09 / ≤0.04）；同时指出跨域 non-member 基准（姿态/质量）会把域漂移与训练差异混淆从而高估隐私泄露，并用 MLP 融合四类统计量在 nids=100K 上把单统计量 0.71–0.73 的 AUC 提升到 0.79–0.80。

> **TL;DR:** A measurement study (not a new attack) that trains 180 open-set face recognition models in a fully crossed design (IResNet-34/50/100 × ArcFace/CosFace/MagFace × 5 nids levels × 4 epoch budgets) on WebFace4M and evaluates four cluster-geometry statistics (PairCos, vMF-κ, PenLogit, ProtoCE) across nine benchmarks. Training-set size nids is the dominant factor for member/non-member separability (partial η² ≥ 0.95), with backbone/loss head negligible (≤0.09/≤0.04). On the same-domain held-out WebFace4M split the geometric membership signal decreases monotonically as more identities are added (PairCos AUC 0.90 at nids=1K → 0.71 at 100K/all); cross-domain non-member benchmarks such as CFP-FP and XQLFW inflate apparent AUC by conflating domain shift with training signal, and fusing all four statistics with an MLP lifts AUC from 0.71–0.73 (best single statistic) to 0.79–0.80 at nids=100K (one-sided Wilcoxon W=666, p<10⁻¹⁰).

## 核心贡献

- **大规模因子化实证（180 个模型）**：在全交叉设计下系统衡量 open-set FR embedding 的训练成员信息，规模远超此前同类工作（最大仅 9 个模型），是目前对 face embedding 几何中成员信息最系统的量化研究。
- **量化四类几何统计量的成员信息**：PairCos、vMF-κ、PenLogit（留一法惩罚 logit）、ProtoCE（代理 softmax CE），用 threshold-classifier AUC 作为无量纲、阈值无关的度量。
- **识别主导训练因子**：通过 Type-II ANOVA 证明 nids 与训练 epoch 解释绝大部分方差（η̂² ≥ 0.95 与 ≥ 0.89），backbone 与损失头贡献小一个数量级。
- **揭示跨域 non-member 基准的高估偏差**：引入 same-domain held-out WebFace4M 切分，证明姿态（CFP-FP vs CFP-FF）、质量（XQLFW vs LFW）等域偏移贡献的统计量位移与训练贡献本身相当甚至更大，跨域基准上 nids=all 仍出现 AUC≈1.0 的伪信号。
- **统计量融合提升检测力**：用 MLP 融合四类统计量在 high-nids 弱信号区仍能恢复额外成员信息（nids=100K 上 0.71–0.73 → 0.79–0.80）。
- **为隐私审计提供实操结论**：审计应使用 same-domain held-out 引用，避免用跨域 non-member 高估泄漏。

## Key Contributions

- A controlled measurement study (not a stronger attack feature) of how much membership information is retained in the explicit cluster geometry of FR embeddings, evaluated across 180 open-set models and nine benchmarks — the largest systematic study of membership information in face embedding geometry to date.
- Quantification of four geometric statistics (PairCos, vMF-κ, PenLogit, ProtoCE) using a threshold-free threshold-classifier AUC, under both black-box (embeddings only) and grey-box (embeddings + scale s and margin m) access.
- Identification of training-set size nids and epoch count as the dominant controlling factors via Type-II ANOVA (partial η̂² ≥ .95 and ≥ .89), while backbone depth (≤ .09) and loss head (≤ .04) are an order of magnitude weaker.
- A same-domain held-out WebFace4M reference that separates training-attributable signal from domain-shift confounds, exposing that cross-domain non-member benchmarks (pose, quality, demographics, age) inflate the apparent membership signal.
- A demonstration that fusing all four statistics with a learned MLP recovers membership information beyond the best single statistic (statistically significant at p < 10⁻¹⁰).
- Concrete practitioner guidance: privacy audits should use same-domain held-out references, and only training on more identities substantially reduces the geometric membership signal.

## Methodology

**Access model.** An auditor queries an FR model f with k ≥ 2 probe images of an identity y and obtains ℓ₂-normalised 512-d embeddings. From the resulting cluster they compute four per-identity statistics and use a swept threshold to separate members from non-members, summarised by the threshold-classifier AUC (the probability that a random member scores higher than a random non-member). Two of the four statistics (PairCos, vMF-κ) require only embeddings (black-box); the other two (PenLogit, ProtoCE) additionally use the training-time scale s and margin m (grey-box), a realistic setting when models are released with their configs.

**Per-identity geometric statistics.**
- *PairCos* s̄ = mean pairwise cosine similarity within the cluster (higher = more coherent).
- *vMF concentration κ̂* estimated via the Banerjee et al. closed form κ̂₀ = R̄(d − R̄²)/(1 − R̄²), d = 512; equivalent to ranking by cluster inertia.
- *PenLogit* ℓ̄: mean margin-penalised logit under a leave-one-out prototype ŵ_\i, using each head's penalisation g (e.g. cos(θ+m) for ArcFace).
- *ProtoCE* −L_y: a surrogate training loss with the identity's own prototype as positive and all other evaluation identities as negatives.

**Factorial design.** Models trained from scratch on WebFace4M with standard InsightFace 112×112 alignment. The grid spans 3 backbones (IResNet-34/50/100) × 3 angular-margin heads (ArcFace s=64,m=0.5; CosFace s=64,m=0.4; MagFace s=64, m∈[0.45,0.8]) × 5 nids levels (1K, 10K, 50K, 100K, all ≈206K) × 4 epoch budgets (5, 10, 20, 40) = **180 configurations**. Optimiser: SGD momentum 0.9, weight decay 5e-4, poly-decay LR from 0.1 with 1 warmup epoch, batch 128, FP16, horizontal flips. A ViT-T/CosFace variant is added for cross-architecture replication. Identities not selected at each nids serve as same-domain non-members.

**Evaluation benchmarks (nine).** Cross-distribution non-member pools LFW, CPLFW (cross-pose), CFP-FF (frontal-only) vs CFP-FP (frontal+profile) identity-matched pair, AgeDB-30 (≥30 yr age gap), XQLFW (quality-degraded LFW pair), RFW (4 ethnicity groups), IJB-C; plus a held-out WebFace4M split (the same-domain reference). Identity-matched pairs (CFP-FF vs CFP-FP; LFW vs XQLFW) form controlled natural experiments isolating pose and quality.

**Analysis tools.** Type-II ANOVA with partial η̂² for variance decomposition; Cohen's d′, Spearman ρ and Kruskal–Wallis ε² for distributional-shift effect sizes; a probit transform to translate matched-pair shifts into AUC-equivalent changes at a fixed baseline (nids=100K, AUC≈0.71). Fusion via a 64→32 MLP (ReLU, BatchNorm, dropout 0.3, Adam lr=1e-3) with 5-fold stratified CV on same-domain identities and 15% validation for early stopping.

## Results & Highlights

- **Variance decomposition (Table 3, Type-II ANOVA partial η̂² across 180 models).** nids explains η̂² ≥ .95 for all four statistics (PairCos .99, κ .95, PenLogit .99, ProtoCE .95); Epoch explains η̂² ≥ .89 (.99, .89, .98, .92). Backbone depth is bounded at η̂² ≤ .09 (PairCos) and Loss head at η̂² ≤ .04 — an order of magnitude weaker than nids/epoch.
- **Monotonic decay of same-domain signal with nids (PairCos marginal-mean AUC).** 0.90 at nids=1K → 0.81 at 10K → 0.73 at 50K → 0.71 at 100K → 0.71 at all. Epoch trend goes the opposite way: ep5 0.69 → ep10 0.75 → ep20 0.79 → ep40 0.85.
- **Cross-domain benchmarks inflate apparent AUC even at nids=all.** On CFP-FP, CPLFW, LFW and XQLFW, several statistics reach AUC ≈ 1.00 even with the largest training set, despite the held-out same-domain signal dropping to 0.71.
- **Pose confound is as large as the training signal (CFP-FP − CFP-FF, identity-matched).** Cohen's d′ = −2.27 (PairCos), −1.13 (κ), −2.98 (PenLogit), +1.14 (ProtoCE). Translated via probit to the nids=100K baseline, the pose contribution is +0.26 AUC, matching or exceeding the training-attributable contribution of +0.21 above chance.
- **Quality confound (XQLFW − LFW, same identities).** Cohen's d′ = −1.64 (PairCos), −0.48 (κ), −1.81 (PenLogit); probit-equivalent AUC change +0.22, comparable to the pose contribution.
- **Age has the weakest, least consistent effect.** Spearman ρ in AgeDB-30: κ = −0.26 (the only expected negative), PairCos = +0.01, PenLogit = +0.17, ProtoCE = +0.33.
- **Ethnicity (RFW, 4 groups).** Kruskal–Wallis ε² ≈ 0.017–0.018 (small) for PairCos/κ/PenLogit and ε² = 0.123 (medium) for ProtoCE, indicating demographic composition of the non-member pool affects the membership baseline.
- **Fusion recovers information beyond the best single statistic (Table 5, epoch 40, same-domain).** At nids=100K, IR-100: ArcFace 0.73 → 0.79, CosFace 0.71 → 0.80, MagFace 0.72 → 0.79; at nids=50K: 0.84–0.86 → 0.91–0.92. One-sided Wilcoxon signed-rank W = 666, p < 10⁻¹⁰.
- **Verification EER context (Table 4, IResNet-100/ArcFace epoch 40).** LFW EER drops from 8.5% (nids=1K) to 0.3% (nids=all); CFP-FP from 26.2% to 0.6%; IJB-C from 8.1% to 1.2% — confirming the grid spans a meaningful quality range and that non-trivial membership separability persists even at competitive verification EER.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The paper does not propose a new attack feature; its claimed novelty is scale (180 models in a fully crossed design vs the prior maximum of 9) and the same-domain held-out reference. Several of the four statistics are explicitly noted to coincide with prior attack features (Li et al.'s intra-similarity = mean PairCos), so the methodological novelty is concentrated in the experimental design and the held-out-split control rather than new statistics.
- **Rigor.** The Type-II ANOVA with partial η̂² and the identity-matched probit-scale translation of confounds (CFP-FF↔CFP-FP, LFW↔XQLFW) are statistically sound and a genuine improvement over single-config evaluations. However, several ANOVA residual cells are essentially zero (PairCos residual η̂² = .00) and the nids×Epoch interaction is near-perfect (.92–.98), indicating the four-factor additive model is close to saturated and that η̂² values should be read as relative rather than absolute effect sizes.
- **Reproducibility.** Models are trained with the standard InsightFace pipeline and all hyper-parameters are listed, but training 180 models from scratch is expensive, and the paper does not state a code or checkpoint release. The probe-image count k per identity is acknowledged as varying across benchmarks and not controlled, which is a known confounder for cluster-statistic estimates.
- **Significance.** The practical message — use a same-domain held-out reference because cross-domain benchmarks conflate domain shift with training signal — is useful and actionable for privacy audits under GDPR right-to-erasure. The finding that only nids substantially reduces the geometric signal is a strong, clean take-away.
- **Red flags / data quirks.** (1) AUC saturates near 1.0 on cross-domain benchmarks even at nids=all, which the paper itself flags as inflated; readers who skim only the cross-domain numbers will over-estimate leakage. (2) The entire study rests on a single training source (WebFace4M); the authors candidly note that comparably large public datasets have been retracted (e.g. MS-1M), so cross-dataset generalisation is untested. (3) The probit transform in §5.3 explicitly assumes approximately Gaussian member/non-member distributions and stable variances across matched pairs — an assumption not formally tested. (4) The four-statistic family is by construction a lower bound on embedding-leakage; stronger feature learners operating on raw embeddings could extract more, so the absolute numbers are conservative.

## Strengths

- Largest systematic study in this niche (180 fully-crossed models vs ≤9 previously), with a clean factorial design that supports real attribution of variance.
- The same-domain held-out WebFace4M reference is a substantive methodological contribution that lets the authors distinguish training-attributable signal from domain shift — a confound that prior embedding-level MIA work does not control.
- Honest, paper-specific reporting: the ANOVA, marginal means, and per-statistic confound sizes (Cohen's d′, ρ, ε²) make the contribution auditable rather than rhetorical.
- Identity-matched benchmark pairs (CFP-FF vs CFP-FP, LFW vs XQLFW) provide clean controlled experiments for pose and quality effects.
- Practitioner-oriented conclusions (more training identities reduces signal; cross-domain non-members over-estimate leakage; fusion helps in the weak-signal regime) are directly useful for privacy auditing.
- Cross-architecture replication with ViT-T/CosFace confirms the trends are not IResNet-specific.

## Limitations

- **Single training source.** All 180 models are trained on WebFace4M; whether the nids/epoch dominance holds for other large training sets is an open question, and the authors note that comparably large alternatives are no longer publicly available or have been retracted.
- **No defences evaluated.** DP-SGD, knowledge distillation, and machine-unlearning defences are not tested, so the paper cannot say whether the geometric signal is reducible by standard privacy mechanisms.
- **Uncontrolled probe count k.** The number of probe images per identity varies across benchmarks and is not controlled, which can bias cluster-statistic estimates non-uniformly.
- **Random vs attribute-correlated partitions.** The held-out split is constructed by random partitioning; in real deployments, membership boundaries may correlate with identity-level attributes (ethnicity, quality), which could change the same-domain baseline.
- **Lower-bound measurement.** Because only four cluster-geometry statistics are used, the reported separability is a lower bound; richer models on raw embeddings could extract more.
- **Gaussianity assumption.** The probit-scale translation in §5.3 assumes approximately Gaussian member/non-member distributions and stable variances, an assumption that is asserted rather than tested.
- **No code/checkpoint release stated**, making independent replication of the 180-model grid costly.

## Relevance

该论文属于人脸识别隐私审计与成员推断（MIA）方向，系统刻画了 angular-margin 训练的 FR embedding 在 hyperspherical 几何中残留的训练成员信息，与对 embedding-based 模型（包括对比学习与多模态编码器）的隐私/成员泄漏研究、以及差分隐私与不可学习等防御机制的评估高度相关，可为后续在通用 embedding 模型上开展成员信息量化与防御评估提供实验范式。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the nids / epoch dominance and the held-out-reference finding transfer to other angular-margin training sets and to non-WebFace architectures (e.g. transformers trained with face losses), given the authors could not test this due to dataset retractions?
- Would DP-SGD or knowledge-distillation defences reduce the geometric membership signal identified here without unacceptable verification-EER degradation, and at what privacy budget does the same-domain AUC fall to chance?
- Can a richer classifier operating directly on the 512-d raw embeddings (rather than the four scalar cluster statistics) substantially exceed the MLP fusion upper bound reported, and by how much across nids levels?
- How do the confound sizes (pose +0.26 AUC, quality +0.22 AUC) change when the auditor controls the probe-set acquisition (e.g. enrolls a curated same-pose, same-quality probe set) rather than relying on off-the-shelf benchmarks?
- Does the membership signal persist when the auditor only has access to the enrolled-template store (one embedding per identity) rather than k ≥ 2 probes, which is the more common deployment scenario?
- Are the cross-ethnicity ProtoCE differences (Kruskal–Wallis ε² = 0.123, medium) an artefact of the prototype-construction denominator, or a genuine demographic bias in how the angular-margin loss compresses different subpopulations?
