> **一句话总结：** 本文针对 LLM harness 中"全接受"级联验证（k 个验证门全过才返回答案）在门间相关时给出了一套最小但可测量的理论：用 de Finetti 把每个错误实例的假接受率 α 建模为潜在变量 α~G，得到精确后验 ℓ_k = ℓ₀ − ln m_k（m_k 为 G 的 k 阶矩），证明 ℓ_k 对 k 严格凹、Beta 潜变量下失败概率按幂律 k^(−b) 而非指数衰减、盲点质量 (1−π) 给出可靠性上限，并给出两 verdict 即可识别相关参数 ρ_v 的反演协议；在合成实验中，独立性外推把失败率低估 20×（k=5）到约 3000×（k=10），结论是把钱花在"去相关"（换模型族/模态/外部证据）而非"加门"。

> **TL;DR:** This note solves Aksu's open problem of partially correlated verifier cascades in LLM harnesses by treating each erroneous instance's per-gate false-accept rate α as a latent variable α~G (de Finetti). The exact cascade posterior ℓ_k = ℓ₀ − ln m_k is strictly concave in k (the independence "Odds Law" is its tangent at the first gate and an upper bound everywhere else); for Beta(a,b) latents failure decays polynomially as k^(−b) governed by the single measurable correlation ρ_v = 1/(a+b+1); a blind-spot atom at α=1 caps total extractable evidence at −ln(1−π) nats; and a two-sided β~H model gives a trichotomy (gates eventually help, plateau, or harm) with closed-form crossover k†. On synthetic recovery (ρ_v=0.3, ᾱ=0.3, p₀=0.5), the independence-based Odds Law extrapolation underestimates the failure rate by 20× at k=5 and ~3000× at k=10 versus the correlated truth, while a Beta-binomial fit at order R=8 predicts held-out depths (e.g., 0.954 predicted vs 0.953 true at k=5). The practical lever the theory isolates is decorrelation (different model family / modality / external oracle), not adding more gates.

## 核心贡献

- **给出"全接受"级联验证的精确相关理论**：将每个错误实例的假接受率 α 视为潜在变量 α~G（de Finetti 可交换性），推出精确后验 ℓ_k = ℓ₀ − ln m_k（m_k = E[α^k]），填补了 Aksu [2026b] 在 Odds Law 中明确留下的"部分相关验证级联仍是开放问题"的空白。Odds Law 在此处是 ρ_v→0 退化情形，仅在第一个门处与真实曲线相切，且在所有 k≥1 处给出上界。
- **凹性 + 幂律衰减 + 盲点上限三大结构结论**：(i) ℓ_k 对 k 严格凹；(ii) 当 G=Beta(a,b) 时失败概率按 1−r_k ≍ k^(−b) 多项式衰减（而非指数），单一参数 ρ_v = 1/(a+b+1) 控制；(iii) 盲点原子（α=1 处质量 1−π）把整个级联（无论多少个门）的可提取证据限制在 −ln(1−π) nats，可靠性上限 r_∞ = p₀/(p₀ + (1−p₀)(1−π)) < 1。
- **两侧模型的三分法 + 闭式交叉点**：当真接受率 β~H 也变时，得到 ℓ_k ≈ const + (b_α − b_β) ln k，按尾指数 b_α vs b_β 划分三种结局（门最终总帮忙 / 平台 / 主动伤害），给出闭式最优点 k† = (a_α b_β − a_β b_α)/(b_α − b_β)，并证明在相关条件下"平均似然比 Λ̄>1"只保证第一个门有用，不再是正确判据（表 2 展示 Λ̄=2.2 但 r_k→0）。
- **可测量的反演协议（ρ_v 两个 verdict 即可识别）**：对每个错误实例采样 R 次，X_i~Bin(R, α_i)，U-统计量识别前 R 阶矩；R=2 即可一致估计 ρ_v。提供三种估计器（矩法、beta-binomial MLE、非参 NPMLE），并明确指出"上限"作为上尾泛函在 R 处有分辨率地板 ϵ~1/R，是个典型的病态反演问题（诚实声明）。
- **合成恢复实验验证**：N=4000，固定种子，代码开源 (github.com/jianganghan/harness-verifier-cascades)。实验 A/B/C/D 系统验证矩估计、两 verdict 充分性、上限病态性、和"低阶拟合→高深度外推"的证伪闭环；独立性外推在 k=5 低估失败率 20×、k=10 约 3000×。

## Key Contributions

- **Exact correlated theory for conjunctive (all-accept) verifier cascades.** Modeling the per-instance false-accept rate on the generator's own errors as a latent α~G (de Finetti exchangeability) gives the exact posterior ℓ_k = ℓ₀ − ln m_k with m_k = E[α^k]. This directly answers the open problem Aksu [2026b] stated at the boundary of the "Odds Law." The independence Odds Law is recovered as the ρ_v→0 degenerate case and is shown to be the tangent at the first gate and an upper bound for all k≥1.
- **Three structural results: concavity, polynomial decay, blind-spot ceiling.** (i) ℓ_k is strictly concave in k for every non-degenerate G, because ln m_k is the cumulant generating function of ln α. (ii) For G=Beta(a,b), failure decays polynomially 1−r_k ≍ κ·k^(−b) — not exponentially — with a single correlation parameter ρ_v = Var(α)/(ᾱ(1−ᾱ)) = 1/(a+b+1). (iii) A blind-spot atom of mass (1−π) at α=1 caps the total extractable evidence of any number of gates at −ln(1−π) nats, so r_∞ = p₀/(p₀+(1−p₀)(1−π)) < 1.
- **Survivorship mechanism with no voting analogue.** The α^j-tilted distribution dG^(j) ∝ α^j dG is exactly the population of errors still alive after j gates, so late gates face selected high-α survivors rather than fresh errors — explaining why per-gate evidence increments ∆_(j+1) strictly decrease toward −ln ess sup α. This selection effect is specific to conjunctive verification and absent in one-shot voting.
- **Two-sided trichotomy with closed-form crossover.** When β~H also varies, ℓ_k ≈ const + (b_α − b_β) ln k yields three regimes: gates eventually always help (b_α>b_β), plateau (b_α=b_β), or actively harm (b_α<b_β). Closed-form crossover k† = (a_α b_β − a_β b_α)/(b_α − b_β). Crucially, under correlation Λ̄ = β̄/ᾱ > 1 only guarantees the first gate helps; the asymptotic direction is decided by tail exponents logically independent of Λ̄ (Table 2 exhibits Λ̄=2.2 with r_k→0).
- **Measurable inversion protocol; two verdicts suffice for ρ_v.** Per-instance accept counts X_i|α_i ~ Bin(R, α_i) identify the first R moments of G via unbiased U-statistics; R=2 already gives a consistent ρ_v̂. Three estimators (moment, beta-binomial MLE, nonparametric MLE) recover the reliability curve and the ill-posed ceiling. The paper is explicit that the ceiling is an upper-tail functional with R-dependent boundary resolution ϵ~1/R — an honest Backus–Gilbert-style ill-posedness treatment.
- **Synthetic-recovery validation with open code.** Experiments A–D validate moment recovery (0.050/0.291/0.502 at truth 0.05/0.30/0.50), two-verdict sufficiency (ρ_v̂=0.274 at R=2 vs 0.30 truth), ceiling ill-posedness (atom-at-1.00 vs atom-at-0.97 worlds indistinguishable at R=5, separable only at R=50), and the falsification loop (R=8 fit predicts r₅=0.954 vs truth 0.953 while independence gives 0.998).

## Methodology

The paper builds a self-contained first-order theory with five linked pieces:

1. **Generative model (Assumption 2.1, de Finetti idealization).** A candidate answer has truth label C∈{1,0} with prior p₀. It is passed through k verification gates under all-accept (conjunctive) gating: returned only if all k accept. The per-instance false-accept rate α is treated as a latent variable α~G supported on [0,1], where the instance population is specifically the generator's own erroneous outputs (the generator–verifier blind-spot bridge). Conditional on α, the k verdicts are i.i.d. Bernoulli(α). This is exact for exchangeable verdicts and is acknowledged as the main relaxable mean-field assumption (à la von Neumann's constant component-failure probability).

2. **Exact posterior and one-sided theory (§3).** Marginally P(all accept | C=0) = E[α^k] = m_k. Bayes in odds form gives o_k = o₀/m_k, i.e. ℓ_k = ℓ₀ − ln m_k, r_k = p₀/(p₀+(1−p₀)m_k). Because ln m_k is the cumulant generating function of ln α, ℓ_k is concave in k (Theorem 3.3); per-gate increments ∆_(j+1) = −ln E_α~dG^(j)[α] strictly decrease as the α^j-tilted distribution concentrates on ess sup α (the survivorship tilt). For G=Beta(a,b), m_k = (a)_k/(a+b)_k exactly and 1−r_k ~ κ·k^(−b) (Theorem 3.5, Watson's lemma). A blind-spot atom of mass (1−π) at α=1 yields r_∞ < 1 (Theorem 3.6). The single parameter ρ_v = 1/(a+b+1) interpolates continuously from the Odds Law (ρ_v→0) to a pure blind-spot two-point model (ρ_v→1) (Proposition 3.7). Cost-optimal gate count becomes k* ≈ (U κ b / c)^(1/(b+1)) (power law) instead of logarithmic (Corollary 3.8).

3. **Two-sided theory (§4).** Letting β~H vary on correct outputs gives ℓ_k = ℓ₀ + ln m_k^(β) − ln m_k^(α). With regularly-varying upper tails (densities ~c(1−x)^(b−1) near x=1), Theorem 4.1 yields ℓ_k ≈ const + (b_α − b_β) ln k, producing the trichotomy. The independence Λ*=1 threshold is provably the wrong criterion under correlation (Corollary 4.2). Closed-form crossover k† = (a_α b_β − a_β b_α)/(b_α − b_β) gives a zero-cost internal optimum at ⌈k†⌉ in the harmful regime (Proposition 4.3).

4. **Identification / inversion protocol (§5).** Sample each erroneous instance R times: X_i|α_i ~ Bin(R, α_i). Unbiased U-statistics ĉ_m_k = (1/N)Σᵢ C(X_i,k)/C(R,k) identify moments up to order R; ρ_v̂ = (ĉ_m_2 − ĉ_m_1²)/(ĉ_m_1(1−ĉ_m_1)) is consistent at R=2 (Proposition 5.1). Three estimators: moment (M1), beta-binomial MLE (M2), nonparametric MLE via Kiefer–Wolfowitz / Efron deconvolution (M3, the only one able to expose an α=1 atom or multimodality). The protocol is honest about three ill-posedness facets: (P1) ceiling is an upper-tail functional with boundary resolution ~1/R; (P2) R verdicts identify only first R moments; (P3) labeling budget B=NR trades instances against depth.

5. **Synthetic-recovery validation (§6).** Four experiments with N=4000 instances, fixed seeds, open code. Experiment D realizes the falsification loop: fit Beta-binomial on accept counts of order R=8 only, extrapolate r_k to k≤25 — correlated theory predicts 0.954 at k=5 vs truth 0.953, while independence extrapolation from the same first-gate data gives 0.998.

## Results & Highlights

The headline quantitative claims (mostly synthetic, since real-log measurement is "in progress"):

- **Independence extrapolation underestimates failure by 20× at k=5 and ~3000× at k=10** (Table 1, regime p₀=0.5, ᾱ=0.3, ρ_v=0.3). At k=5 the Odds Law predicts r₅=0.998 (failure 2.4×10⁻³) while correlated truth is r₅=0.953 (failure 0.047) — a 20× underestimate; at k=10 independence gives r₁₀=0.999994 (failure 5.9×10⁻⁶) vs true r₁₀=0.982 (failure 0.018), a ~3000× underestimate. "An operator budgeting gates by the independence formula believes they bought five nines; they bought 98%."
- **Moment estimator recovers ρ_v to 3 decimals.** Experiment A: at R=10, ρ_v̂ recovers 0.050/0.291/0.502 at truth 0.05/0.30/0.50; M2 agrees. Experiment B: with R=2 (just two verdicts), ρ_v̂=0.274 at truth 0.30; R≥3 is essentially exact — Proposition 5.1 in action.
- **Two-sided reversal at Λ̄=2.2 (Table 2).** With α~Beta(0.7,1.63), β~Beta(8,4) giving mean gate quality Λ̄=β̄/ᾱ=2.2>1 (first-gate evidence δ₁=0.80>0) but b_α=1.63 < b_β=4, reliability is unimodal: peaks at k=5 with r₅=0.787, then declines to r₂₀=0.623 and →0, while independence predicts r₂₀≈1. Crossover k†≈4.3 matches the empirical peak.
- **Ceiling ill-posedness (Experiment C).** Two worlds with 10% blind-spot atom at α=1.00 (true ceiling r_∞=0.91) vs at α=0.97 (ceiling 1.00) produce nearly indistinguishable accept-count histograms at R=5; only at R=50 does the α=1 spike emerge and NPMLE assign mass 0.100 vs 0.000. This empirically instantiates ill-posedness claims (P1)/(P2).
- **Out-of-sample falsification (Experiment D, Fig. 1).** Beta-binomial fit at order R=8 only (ρ_v̂=0.30) extrapolated to k≤25 predicts r₅=0.954 vs true 0.953, tracking held-out depths, while the independence extrapolation from the same first-gate data gives r₅=0.998 — exponential and polynomial predictions separate fast, so modest data decide.
- **Motivating empirical anchor (cited, not measured by the author).** Tsui [2025] reports that across 14 open models, an average 64.5% of self-generated errors survive self-checking even though the same errors are caught when presented externally — the empirical magnitude of the blind-spot tail the theory formalizes.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Genuinely novel within the "harness reliability algebra" line. It cleanly modifies exactly one assumption (conditional independence of verification gates) in Aksu's Odds Law framework and derives non-trivial new phenomena — concave (not linear) log-odds, polynomial (not exponential) decay, a finite blind-spot ceiling, and a two-sided trichotomy with closed-form crossover — that have no one-shot-voting analogue. The survivorship-tilt mechanism (Remark 3.4) is the genuinely new mathematical content. However, the paper is honest that the exponential-vs-polynomial dichotomy itself is "by now a recurring finding" (Chen et al. 2024b; Halder & Pehlevan 2025; Halder et al. 2026) and frames its contribution as fixing the dichotomy to a *specific* mechanism (conjunctive survivorship against a blind-spot tail) with closed-form exponent k^(−b) — a modesty that is appropriate.
- **Rigor**: High for the mathematical core. The de Finetti idealization is the right tool; moment identification via binomial factorial moments is standard empirical Bayes; the proof of Theorem 3.5 via Watson's lemma is correct. The ill-posedness discussion (§5, with explicit P1/P2/P3 facets and the Backus–Gilbert citation) is unusually honest for an ML-theory paper.
- **Reproducibility**: Strong on the synthetic side (open code, fixed seeds, N specified). Weak on the empirical side: **the central practical claim — that real generator–verifier pairs exhibit the predicted correlated structure — is validated on zero real logs**. All quantitative results come from a synthetic world chosen by the author (ᾱ=0.3, ρ_v=0.3, p₀=0.5). The paper admits this ("the measurement on real generator–verifier pairs ... is in progress") but a reader should weight the practical conclusions accordingly. A specific missing ablation: there is no sensitivity analysis showing how estimation error in ρ_v̂ (which at R=2 is biased, ρ_v̂=0.274 vs 0.30) propagates into the *recommended decision* (keep buying gates vs. decorrelate); the decision rule "measure ρ_v with R=2; if large, stop" rests on the unstated assumption that the R=2 estimate is accurate enough to make the binary call.
- **Significance**: Potentially high for the design of compound LLM systems (generate–verify–retry loops). The practical lever it isolates — **decorrelation beats adding gates** — is consonant with the broader self-correction literature (Huang et al. 2024; Kamoi et al. 2024; Tsui 2025), and the trichotomy result (gates can actively harm once b_α < b_β even with Λ̄>1) is a genuinely actionable warning for harness designers. But the significance is contingent on real-log validation confirming that the de Finetti scalar-latent model is a good first-order fit and that β-tails (which drive the harmful regime) are as heavy as the synthetic Table 2 assumes.
- **Red flags / specific concerns**: (1) The Beta(a,b) family is repeatedly described as a "convenience for closed forms," but every numerical table and every trichotomy example uses Beta — a reader cannot tell how robust the polynomial exponent and the crossover k† are to non-Beta, non-regularly-varying G. The asymptotic theory only needs regularly-varying tails, but finite-k claims (Tables 1, 2) are Beta-specific. (2) The tension flagged in §5 (the theory recommends *heterogeneous* decorrelated gates while Assumption 2.1 assumes *exchangeable* gates under a single scalar α) is acknowledged but not resolved — the author defers the vector-latent sequel α_i = σ(u + v_i) to §8. Until that sequel exists, the theory's main practical recommendation sits slightly outside its own formal domain. (3) Every supporting empirical citation is from 2025–2026 arXiv, several from the same tight research cluster (Aksu, Liu, Kohli, Chen) — the work is coherent with that cluster but has not yet been stress-tested against independent measurements of verifier-cascade correlation on production harnesses.

## Strengths

- Mathematically clean and self-contained; answers a precisely stated open problem (Aksu 2026b) by changing exactly one assumption and deriving rich, falsifiable structure.
- The survivorship-tilt mechanism (surviving errors are the high-α ones, so late gates face selected survivors) is an intuitive and powerful explanation for why per-gate evidence decays — a genuinely verification-specific insight with no voting analogue.
- The measurability story is a real strength: ρ_v is identified by just two verdicts per instance, and the paper provides three nested estimators (moment / beta-binomial MLE / NPMLE) plus an honest ill-posedness analysis of the ceiling. This makes the theory immediately testable.
- Actionable practical conclusion (decorrelation > more gates) aligns with and algebraically explains the empirical self-correction literature.
- Open code, fixed seeds, and a clear falsification-loop protocol (fit at low order, predict held-out depths) that other researchers can directly apply to real logs.
- The trichotomy with closed-form crossover k† is a sharp, decision-relevant result: it predicts that under correlation a cascade can have a finite internal optimum even at zero gate cost, and that the independence threshold Λ*=1 is the wrong criterion.

## Limitations

- **No real-data validation.** All quantitative results are synthetic; the central real-log measurement is explicitly "in progress." The practical claims rest on the synthetic regime (ᾱ=0.3, ρ_v=0.3) being representative, which is unverified.
- **Scalar-latent / exchangeability assumption (Assumption 2.1).** Compresses all inter-gate structure into a single α; heterogeneous verifier families (the very thing the theory recommends for decorrelation) require a vector latent that is deferred to a sequel.
- **All-accept / conjunctive semantics only.** The generate–verify–retry framing makes false rejections a throughput cost rather than a precision cost, which restores the one-sided picture; §4 applies when regeneration is impossible or gates are terminal. The theory does not cover soft/probabilistic gating or weighted ensemble verification.
- **Beta-centric numerics.** Closed forms are Beta-specific; robustness of the polynomial exponent and the crossover k† to non-Beta G is asserted asymptotically but not demonstrated numerically.
- **Ceiling is ill-posed by construction.** The paper is honest about this, but it means the most practically important quantity (the absolute reliability ceiling) is the hardest to estimate and requires large R — undercutting the "two verdicts suffice" sales pitch, which only applies to ρ_v, not to the ceiling.
- **Single-author, independent-researcher note** building on a cluster of very recent (2026) arXiv preprints; the supporting empirical literature is narrow and not yet peer-reviewed.

## Relevance

这篇论文对"LLM 复合系统（generate–verify–retry harness）可靠性理论"和"自验证/self-correction 的失败模式"两个方向高度相关：它给出了"为何同族验证器级联会饱和、为何加门不如换模型族/模态/外部 oracle"的封闭形式理论，直接回答了 self-correction 文献中长期存在的"为什么更多同模型自反馈不修复错误反而降低精度"的疑问，并提供了仅需两次 verdict 即可测量的相关性指标 ρ_v 和反演协议，对设计稳健的多步推理/agent 验证 pipeline 有直接指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the de Finetti scalar-latent model α~G actually fit real generator–verifier pairs (e.g., a frontier model self-checking its own code/math errors), or does NPMLE reveal multimodality / heavy α=1 atoms that break the Beta closed forms? The §5 protocol is ready to run — what does the empirical ρ_v distribution look like across model families and task types?
- The harmful regime (b_α < b_β, gates eventually harm) is demonstrated only synthetically with β~Beta(8,4). Is this regime realized in practice — i.e., do real verifiers have heavier α-tails (near-undetectable errors) than β-tails (near-unanimously-accepted correct answers), and if so for which task categories (code, math, factual QA)?
- How does the scalar theory generalize to the proposed vector latent α_i = σ(u + v_i)? Does partial decorrelation (splicing in one cross-family gate) register as a measurable rise in the effective exponent b, and does the closed-form crossover k† still approximately predict the empirical reliability peak?
- The paper claims ρ_v is "directly measurable" with R=2, but the R=2 estimate is biased (0.274 vs 0.30). How does estimation noise propagate into the binary decision rule ("if ρ_v̂ large, stop buying gates")? Is there a principled threshold that accounts for R=2 variance?
- How does the correlated-verifier-cascade theory interact with best-of-k / tournament / majority-vote aggregation studied in the parallel voting-side literature (Chen 2026; Liu 2026a,b; Kohli 2026)? When a harness combines voting over generators *and* verification gates, which correlation dominates the reliability ceiling?
