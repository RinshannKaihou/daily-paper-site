> **一句话总结：** ReasFlow 是一个面向应用数学理论研究的端到端多智能体科研系统，通过内置验证回路与"知识卡片"自动检索/自改进机制，让智能体以"研究生"身份自主完成文献调研、算法设计、收敛性证明、实验与论文撰写，五个自动生成的论文在 LLM 审稿基准上平均分 77.9，超出最强基线 DeepScientist 逾 20 分。

> **TL;DR:** ReasFlow is a knowledge-augmented multi-agent system for reasoning-centric scientific discovery in applied mathematics; built around an internal proof-verification loop and automatically refined "knowledge cards," it autonomously produces five full research papers with rigorous convergence guarantees, achieving an average PaperReviewAgent score of 77.9 versus 57.3 for the strongest baseline DeepScientist, and a perfect 100 from Gemini-3-Pro on theoretical reports.

## 核心贡献

- 提出针对应用数学"理论优先、实验辅助"范式的端到端自主科研系统 ReasFlow，统一了文献综述、算法设计、定理证明、实验评估与论文写作五大环节，弥补了既有 AI 科研框架（如 AI Scientist、CycleResearcher、DeepScientist）偏经验驱动的不足。
- 设计了内部"验证回路"（LemmaVerifierAgent），对自然语言证明执行上下文相关、紧性审计、零容忍策略的核查，能在专家介入前过滤循环依赖、隐含假设、松界等典型缺陷。
- 提出"知识卡片 + 自动 Prompt 精炼"机制，将声明式知识与程序性启发（如"强凸场景先尝试二次 Lyapunov 函数"）编码为可检索卡片，并通过算法 1/2 的自改进循环自动优化 CardGeneratorSkill 与 CardRetrieverSkill，使知识增强后的智能体达到接近专家实时指导的水平。
- 自主产出五篇完整论文，覆盖联邦学习、分布式信号处理、流形优化与 LLM 训练优化器，其中两篇达到或超越此前人类最优的理论收敛速率（如分布式随机强凸优化首次匹配下复杂度下界）。
- 提出多维 LLM 审稿协议（PaperReviewAgent 9 维、ProofReviewAgent 5 维、OR-Bench 等），并开源 ReasLab 平台与代码（github.com/ReasLab/ReasFlow）。

## Key Contributions

- ReasFlow, an open-source end-to-end multi-agent system explicitly tailored to theory-first applied mathematics research, integrating literature survey, algorithm design, theorem proving, experimentation, and manuscript writing in a single workflow orchestrated by a central MetaAgent.
- An automatic knowledge-card pipeline: declarative facts and procedural heuristics are extracted from reference papers into structured cards (see Fig. 4 example), and the generator/retriever skill prompts are iteratively refined (Algorithms 1 and 2) until retrieved-card performance matches expert-guided performance — eliminating most real-time expert intervention.
- A strict internal verification loop in the ProverAgent that audits context coherence, tightness of bounds, and explicit justifications with a zero-tolerance policy, catching cyclic dependencies and implicit assumptions that surface-level checks miss.
- Empirical validation via five autonomously produced research papers, including a multi-gossip accelerated DSGD that provably matches the lower complexity bound for decentralized stochastic strongly convex optimization, and a topology-independent decentralized Muon optimizer whose leading term does not degrade with network sparsity.
- A comprehensive benchmarking suite — PaperReviewAgent (9 dimensions), ProofReviewAgent (5 dimensions), OR-Bench, and section-level evaluators — and public release via the ReasLab platform.

## Methodology

ReasFlow is a multi-agent system orchestrated by a central **MetaAgent** that interacts with the user to clarify a research topic and sequentially invokes specialized sub-agents: SurveyAgent, AlgorithmAgent, ProverAgent, ExperimentAgent, IntroductionAgent, and WritingAgent. All agents share a workspace with file/terminal utilities (ReadFile, WriteFile, EditFile, RunTerminalCmd) and use two auxiliary mechanisms — **skills** (predefined prompts that inject in-context capabilities) and **sub-agents** (delegated tasks with isolated memory).

Two mechanisms differentiate ReasFlow from prior AI-scientist frameworks:

1. **Automatic verification loop (ProverAgent).** The LemmaVerifierAgent loads the candidate proof together with the original problem, previously proven lemmas, and cited references (context-aware verification), explicitly audits whether bounds are tight enough to be publishable (e.g., flags replacing a sharp 1/N scaling with a coarse universal constant), and applies a zero-tolerance policy that rejects proofs with unstructured reasoning traces, ambiguous notation, or any unjustified step; rejected proofs loop back to the LemmaProverAgent until they pass.

2. **Knowledge augmentation via knowledge cards.** Cards are short text files encoding reasoning patterns and procedural heuristics (Fig. 4 gives a full "Distributed Variance Reduction" example showing how to avoid the loose Jensen bound and obtain the sharp 1/N variance reduction). Two challenges — quality control in extraction and effective retrieval (LLMs tend to over-search and skip reading) — are addressed by **Automatic Prompt Refinement** (Algorithms 1 and 2), which iteratively updates the CardGeneratorSkill and CardRetrieverSkill prompts by comparing the proof produced with retrieved cards against an expert-provided baseline proof, until a measurable equality criterion Ω(pk, p*) is satisfied. The motivating example is stochastic independence: a sharp E[||ḡ−g*||²] ≤ σ²/n bound that LLMs routinely miss by defaulting to Cauchy-Schwarz and obtaining σ².

Each agent additionally follows an evaluation-refinement loop: SurveyAgent uses a 4-dimension review of Related Work drafts; AlgorithmAgent verifies Python outputs via SmartPlotTool/ImageReaderTool; ExperimentAgent uses PlotAnalysisTool; IntroductionAgent uses a 3-axis (Coverage, Faithfulness, Content Quality) EvalIntroduction with a three-tier verdict (auto_refine <0.60, suggest_refine <0.75, good); WritingAgent uses a two-tier Coordinator + parallel SubWriters architecture with ReviewCompleteness, ReviewWritingQuality, and ScreenshotAnalysisTool for layout checks.

## Results & Highlights

- **End-to-end paper generation (Table 1, PaperReviewAgent, 9 dimensions):** ReasFlow (GPT-5.4) scores **77.9 average** across GPT-5.1 / GPT-5.4 / Gemini-3-Pro reviewers, beating the strongest baseline DeepScientist (57.3) by **+20.6 points**; it also beats AI Scientist-v2 (41.6), ARIS (50.3), Claude Pro (52.9), and CycleResearcher-ML-12B (21.2). On Gemini-3-Pro specifically, ReasFlow scores **87.6** versus DeepScientist's 43.5.
- **ProverAgent (Table 4, ProofReviewAgent, 100-pt scale, 5 dimensions):** ReasFlow with GPT-5.4 reaches **90.7 average**, versus 73.0 for the best baseline QED (GPT-5.4); when judged by Gemini-3-Pro, ReasFlow attains a perfect **100** across all three of its LLM backbones, while no baseline exceeds 75.
- **AlgorithmAgent (Table 3, 35 tasks from MLR-Bench + OR-Bench):** ReasFlow (GPT-5.4) achieves a **100% first-run success rate**, versus 97% Claude Code, 71% Codex-CLI, 60% ARIS, and 44% AI Scientist-v2.
- **ExperimentAgent (Table 5):** ReasFlow (GPT-5.4) leads on Design Soundness (8.49 vs next-best 6.50 Codex), Algorithm-Experiment Alignment (8.95), and Writeup Readability (8.17), with the lowest Hallucination Suspicion (**9%** vs 26% Codex, 46% ARIS, 100% AI Scientist-v2).
- **WritingAgent (Table 7, [16] asset bundle):** ReasFlow scores **9.31 overall** (faithfulness 9.73, coverage 9.47, writing 8.16, submittability 9.87), beating the next-best baseline (Claude Code vanilla, GPT-5.4) by 0.56 absolute points and the median baseline by >2.2 points; it is the only system ranking first on three of the four dimensions simultaneously.
- **SurveyAgent (Table 2):** +3.26 overall average advantage over the base LLM across 4 backbones and 3 tasks, with the largest single gain of +10.23 (DeepSeek on [59]); wins 28 of 36 paired comparisons (77.8% win rate).
- **IntroductionAgent (Table 6):** +24.6% Overall Balanced and +29.1% Overall Technical Focus over the base GPT-4o, with a +52.3% gain on Content Quality.
- **Five autonomous discoveries with rigorous theory:** (1) MG-ADSGD provably matches the Ω̃(√(L/μ) + σ/√(μn(1−β)ε)) lower bound for decentralized stochastic strongly convex optimization; (2) a primal-dual federated subspace method with rate Õ(1/T + σ/√(NKT)), strictly improving the prior O(1/√T) of [60]; (3) a topology-independent decentralized Muon optimizer whose leading O(T^(−1/4)) term is independent of network sparsity, improving the prior O(T^(−1/4)/(1−β)); (4) a momentum-compatible federated subspace optimizer with O(1/√(NT)) rate and linear speedup; (5) RF-EXTRA, the first decentralized retraction-free primal-dual method on the Stiefel manifold with O(1/K) rate.
- **Empirical case-study numbers:** On CIFAR-100/ResNet-110 (Case 2), the ReasFlow-designed method reaches 45.43% test accuracy vs FedAvg 34.35% and FedSub 23.17%; on a 20-node non-IID CIFAR-100 decentralized benchmark (Case 3), ED reaches 52.89% and ATC-GT 48.58% vs DeMuon's 43.94%.

The 18-author team is led by six equal-contribution first authors at Peking University (Yutong He, Daibo Li, Guohong Li, Jiahe Geng, Zhengyang Huang, Can Ren), with corresponding authors Kun Yuan and Zaiwen Wen — Wen is a well-known optimization researcher at Peking University and a leading figure in numerical optimization and distributed algorithms. The work is explicitly tied to a production platform (ReasLab at model.reaslab.io) and an open GitHub repository, and the five generated papers are real arXiv-style works ([16], [24], [42], [59], [64]) with independent theoretical content rather than toy demos.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core novelty is genuine but narrow — combining a strict natural-language proof verifier with an LLM-driven automatic refinement loop for retrieval-augmented "procedural heuristics" in a focused domain (applied math optimization). Individually, knowledge cards, agentic CoT, and LLM-as-judge review are well-trodden; the contribution is the specific systematization for theory-centric research.
- **Rigor:** The five case studies include non-trivial theoretical claims (matching lower bounds, improving prior human bounds) backed by independently re-derived proofs, which is impressive. However, the paper candidly acknowledges that even verified natural-language proofs "may still contain errors, and human evaluation remains necessary" — the verification loop reduces but does not eliminate incorrect derivations, and no formal Lean/Coq integration is provided.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong on artifacts (open GitHub, public ReasLab platform, six generated papers as concrete deliverables) but weak on operational details: the LLM backbones used (GPT-5.1, GPT-5.4, Gemini-3-Pro) are proprietary and not fully controllable; the automatic prompt refinement convergence criterion Ω is described abstractly without specifying how many iterations or how often it fails to converge; and the built-in FAISS paper library (339 papers) is not released.
- **Significance:** If the claimed theoretical improvements (e.g., matching the lower bound for decentralized stochastic strongly convex optimization) hold under independent scrutiny, this is a significant demonstration that AI can produce state-of-the-art theoretical results in a specialized math subfield, not just empirical ML engineering. The scope is, however, tightly concentrated in distributed/optimization theory — generalization to other applied math (PDEs, numerical linear algebra, harmonic analysis) is unproven.
- **Red flags:** The evaluation is overwhelmingly LLM-as-judge (PaperReviewAgent, ProofReviewAgent, IntroductionAgent evaluators all use GPT-5.x/Gemini/Claude), creating a real risk of evaluator bias — particularly the perfect 100s from Gemini-3-Pro on ReasFlow's own theoretical reports, which could reflect formatting/presentation rather than mathematical correctness. There is no human expert re-scoring of the LLM review scores reported in Tables 1, 4, 6, and 7. The strongest baseline in the headline Table 1 is **DeepScientist at 57.3**, but CycleResearcher-ML-12B (a 12B model) at 21.2 is a very weak reference point that inflates ReasFlow's lead; a 12B model is not a fair competitor to a multi-agent system on GPT-5.4. The automatic prompt refinement is described in two algorithms but no ablation reports how much of the ProverAgent's gain comes from cards vs. the verifier vs. simply using GPT-5.4 (e.g., the CardRetrieverSkill ablation showing knowledge-card vs. no-card delta is not isolated in the main text).

## Strengths

- Tackles a genuinely under-served niche: theory-driven applied mathematics, where prior AI-scientist systems (designed for empirical ML, biology, or competition math) are structurally inadequate.
- The two-mechanism design (internal verifier + auto-refined knowledge cards) is well-motivated by a concrete failure mode (LLMs mishandling stochastic independence and losing the 1/N factor) and the example knowledge card in Fig. 4 is genuinely illustrative.
- Five end-to-end case studies spanning diverse subfields (federated learning, manifold optimization, decentralized optimization, LLM training), each producing novel theoretical results with matching experiments.
- Comprehensive section-level ablations isolate the contribution of each sub-agent against matched baselines.
- Open-source release via ReasLab and GitHub lowers the barrier to verification and reuse.

## Limitations

- The internal verification loop detects common errors but **does not guarantee formal correctness**; integration with Lean/Coq/Isabelle is left as future work, so the "rigorous proof" claim rests on natural-language checking.
- The knowledge base is **tailored primarily to applied math and adjacent areas**; cross-disciplinary extension requires rebuilding extraction pipelines, and the 339-paper FAISS library is not released.
- Performance is **constrained by the underlying foundation models**; results may shift substantially as GPT-5.x / Gemini-3 generations change, making long-term claims hard to pin down.
- The headline evaluation relies heavily on LLM-as-judge with no human-expert cross-validation of the scoring rubric, leaving evaluator-bias concerns unaddressed.
- Some baselines in Table 1 are mismatched in scale (e.g., CycleResearcher-ML-12B at 21.2), inflating apparent margins; the paper does not isolate the contribution of knowledge cards versus the verifier versus the GPT-5.4 backbone within the ProverAgent ablation.

## Relevance

对关注"多智能体系统 + 自动科研 / 理论证明"方向高度相关：ReasFlow 展示了 LLM Agent 在应用数学（优化、分布式学习、联邦学习）这一理论密集领域产出具备严格收敛性保证的原创成果的可行性，对研究自动化科研流水线、知识增强推理、以及理论证明辅助的人很有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How much of the ProverAgent's reported gain (90.7 vs 73.0) is attributable to the knowledge cards versus the verifier loop versus simply switching to GPT-5.4? A clean three-way ablation isolating each component is missing.
- Would the automatically generated proofs in [16], [24], [42], [59], [64] survive formalization in Lean 4 by an independent party, or do they contain hidden gaps that natural-language verification cannot catch?
- How sensitive are the perfect Gemini-3-Pro scores to presentation/formatting versus mathematical content? Can a human-expert panel reproduce the relative ranking of ReasFlow versus QED and ProofGrader?
- Does the automatic prompt refinement loop (Algorithms 1, 2) converge reliably across diverse proof tasks, and how many iterations / tokens does it typically require? No convergence statistics are reported.
- Can the framework generalize beyond optimization theory (e.g., to PDE analysis, numerical linear algebra, approximation theory), where the procedural heuristics are less well-codified?
