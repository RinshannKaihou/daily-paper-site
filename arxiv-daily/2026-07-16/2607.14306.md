> **一句话总结：** 本文以 Pythia（70M–2.8B）为实验对象，系统比较 LLM 下一词分布与训练数据的经验下一词分布（ENTD）的差异，发现 20% 的样本几乎完美对齐（TV ≤ 0.0093），但仍存在长尾偏差，并逐一排查了 batch 对齐、softmax 瓶颈、优化随机性与数据熵等因素。

> **TL;DR:** This paper measures how closely a trained LLM's next-token distribution matches the empirical next-token distribution (ENTD) of its pretraining corpus (the Pile), using the Pythia suite (70M–2.8B params) and ∞-gram suffix-array counts. On a stratified evaluation sample of ~84K prefixes, the LLM matches the ENTD almost perfectly (TV ≤ 0.0093) for ~20% of inputs, with agreement rising with model scale; the remaining long tail is dissected across batch alignment (negated by positional invariance), the softmax bottleneck (shown insufficient to explain the gap), optimization randomness (via PolyPythias), and a surprising high-correlation (ρ = 0.80) between ENTD entropy and final TV for frequent prefixes.

## 核心贡献

- **提出并形式化了 ENTD 与 ENTD-T 两类经验下一词分布目标**：ENTD 是预训练交叉熵损失的全局最小化解，ENTD-T 是与 batch/上下文窗口对齐的版本；两者都可借助 ∞-gram 的后缀数组在大语料上高效计算。
- **揭示了"逐样本偏差分布"而非仅平均损失**：不同于经典 scaling law 只看平均损失，本文刻画了从近乎完美收敛到完全偏离的整个 TV 距离分布，发现模型对约 20% 评估样本达到 TV ≤ 0.0093 的近似完美对齐。
- **证明 Transformer 具有强位置不变性，否定了 batch 对齐效应**：在 offset=0/1/1024 的对照中，TV 分布几乎重合；只在严重过拟合两个 batch 时才出现预期的对齐差距。
- **推翻 softmax 瓶颈作为主要差异来源的假说**：对评估样本做的 rank-2560 低秩 logit 近似其 TV 远低于最大模型，说明自然文本可低秩近似，但 Transformer 并未充分利用。
- **发现 ENTD 熵是高频样本偏差的最强预测因子**：在 count ≥ 10⁴ 的输入中，经验分布熵与最终 TV 的 Spearman 相关 ρ = 0.80，提示架构归纳偏置迫使模型在真实高熵处学出更"窄"的分布。
- **倡导"data-centric mechanistic interpretability"**：作为标准机制可解释性的补充——从数据角度打开行为来源的黑盒。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes two empirical targets — the ENTD (global minimizer of the pretraining cross-entropy loss) and the training-aligned ENTD-T — both computable at corpus scale via a modified ∞-gram suffix-array method.
- Replaces average-loss-only scaling laws with the full per-sample TV-distance distribution, showing roughly 20% of a stratified evaluation set converges almost perfectly (TV ≤ 0.0093) while a heavy high-TV tail persists across scales.
- Empirically refutes the conjecture that training-aligned batch structure should drive the model toward ENTD-T: offset-0/1/1024 TV distributions are nearly identical, revealing a learned positional invariance.
- Disproves softmax bottleneck as the dominant explanation: a rank-d_model = 2560 unconstrained logit approximation of the evaluation sample has TV far below even the largest (2.8B) model at the end of training.
- Uses PolyPythias (multi-seed Pythia variants) to decompose model-vs-ENTD gap into bias vs. optimization variance, showing variance dominates at 70M but not at larger scales.
- Identifies ENTD entropy as the strongest correlate of high-TV among frequent prefixes (Spearman ρ = 0.80 for count ≥ 10⁴), with possible implications for text-homogeneity collapse.
- Frames the work as "data-centric mechanistic interpretability," complementing weight-centric interpretability.

## Methodology

The paper studies base (pretraining-only) Pythia models (Biderman et al., 2023) across 70M, 410M, and 2.8B parameters, trained on the Pile. The core target is the **empirical next-token distribution (ENTD)**:

p_ENTD(x_{k+1} | x_{1:k}) = count(x_{1:k+1}, D) / count(x_{1:k}, D)

which is the unrestricted global minimizer of the next-token cross-entropy loss. A **training-aligned variant (ENTD-T)** is introduced that only counts prefixes occurring at positions aligned with the training batch windows (mod (C+1), with C = 2048). Both are computed via the ∞-gram suffix-array engine (Liu et al., 2024).

Because uniform samples from training would be dominated by once-occurring strings, the author constructs **stratified evaluation samples** binned by (input length k, corpus count) following Carlini et al. (2022): one ENTD-sample of 83,963 prefixes and one ENTD-T sample of 56,052 prefixes, sharded across the 21 Pile shards. Discrepancy is measured by total-variation (TV) distance between the model's next-token distribution and the empirical one.

Key probes:
1. **Positional invariance test**: compare TV vs. ENTD-T at offsets 0, 1, 1024.
2. **Softmax bottleneck upper bound**: train an unconstrained rank-d_model logit factorization U Wᵀ against soft targets P to see the best achievable low-rank fit.
3. **Optimization variance**: use PolyPythias to compare model-vs-ENTD and model-vs-model TV.
4. **Stratified and tail analysis**: bucket prefixes by length, frequency, LLM entropy, ENTD entropy; inspect tails (Tables 1–2).
5. **Per-sample trajectory analysis**: track TV over training steps to distinguish uniform convergence from per-sample memorization.

## Results & Highlights

- On the ENTD stratified sample, the LLM matches the ENTD almost perfectly (TV ≤ 0.0093) for the bottom-20% CDF of inputs; agreement increases with model scale and training compute (Figure 1).
- Although ENTD-T is "training-aligned," the model is consistently *closer* to the standard ENTD than to ENTD-T on the ENTD-T evaluation sample — i.e., mean and median TV are lower against ENTD, with lighter upper tails (Figure 2). This is attributed to the 2049× smaller effective sample size of ENTD-T and a much weaker-than-expected batch-alignment effect.
- The positional invariance test (Figure 3) shows offset-0, offset-1, and offset-1024 (maximally misaligned) TV distributions are nearly identical across model scales and throughout training, indicating the transformer learns a strong positional invariance *not* explicitly encouraged by the training procedure.
- The unconstrained rank-2560 (= d_model of the 2.8B model) logit approximation to the evaluation-sample empirical distributions achieves TV far below even the largest fully-trained model (Figure 4), disproving Yang et al. (2018)'s softmax bottleneck as the dominant explanation on this data. The caveat is the ~84K evaluation examples vs. ~300B training predictions (with ~254B corpus samples underlying the empirical NTDs).
- For high-count (≥ 10⁴) prefixes, Spearman correlation between ENTD entropy and final TV is **ρ = 0.80** — the strongest of all natural statistics checked.
- Tail statistics (Table 1): low-TV inputs have very peaked, near-deterministic ENTDs with median corpus frequency ~22,000; high-TV inputs split into (a) rare inputs (median count ≤ 2) where the gap is plausibly finite-sample noise, (b) frequent-but-high-entropy inputs where the model collapses to a narrow distribution (e.g., model entropy 0.08 vs. ENTD entropy 6.08), and (c) frequent-but-deterministic templated/spam strings where the model refuses to commit (model entropy 4.5–6.5 against ENTD entropy ≈ 0).
- Optimization variance (Figure 5): for the 70M model, model-model TV exceeds model-ENTD TV on average; for the 410M and 2.8B models the inter-model gap is smaller than the model-ENTD gap, indicating larger models converge to a more stable target.
- Tail stability (Figure 7): high-TV tails are largely structurally persistent for larger models (high IoU of top-10% sets across checkpoints), with isolated transient dips from degenerate-token collapses invisible to aggregate loss.

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author paper by **Zachary Izzo (NEC Labs America)**. The author has a recent ICLR 2026 paper on quantitative bounds for length generalization in transformers (Izzo, Nichani, Lee, 2026) and is cited in the related work for Izzo et al. (2026) on data attribution. The experimental design is unusually careful: it cross-validates the ∞-gram suffix-array index against a brute-force hash-table index built directly from the logged Pythia training-data order (Appendix B), confirms offset experiments are not index bugs, and includes sensitivity analyses for tail-cutoff choice (Appendix C). The choice of Pythia + PolyPythias + ∞-gram on the Pile is methodologically sound, leveraging the only widely reproducible LLM training suite with checkpointed weights, dataloader, and multi-seed variants.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Real but incremental relative to Nguyen (2024), which already measured TV between model output and ENTD on TinyStories and a 1.4B/4.4B-token setup. The author's claimed differentiation is the wider scale range and deeper error-source decomposition; the most novel individual findings are the positional invariance and the high ρ = 0.80 entropy-vs-TV correlation.
- **Rigor**: Strong on internal validation (brute-force index cross-check, multi-seed PolyPythias, sensitivity analyses). However, several quantitative claims rest on a single, fixed ~84K stratified sample, and the author himself flags that the low-rank logit approximation comparison is not strictly apples-to-apples with how the transformer was trained (~84K examples vs. ~300B predictions).
- **Reproducibility**: High in principle — Pythia, PolyPythias, ∞-gram, and the Pile are all open, and the stratified-sampling procedure is described in enough detail to rebuild. No code release is referenced in the body, however.
- **Significance**: Conceptually valuable as a "scaling laws under a microscope" framing, but the practical takeaways for frontier-scale model builders are limited because the largest tested model (2.8B) is 3–4 orders of magnitude below frontier, and several conclusions (positional invariance, softmax-bottleneck insufficiency) may not survive at scale.
- **Red flags / specific weak points**:
  - The **stratified sampling scheme** deliberately over-samples rare and long prefixes vs. their true corpus frequency, so the reported "20% near-perfect match" is a statement about the stratified CDF, not about the natural pretraining loss distribution — readers could easily over-read it.
  - The **soft-target low-rank logit fit** is trained on the same ~84K evaluation set whose empirical distributions are being approximated; whether the low-rank structure survives as the evaluation set grows is acknowledged as an open question, which materially weakens the softmax-bottleneck dismissal.
  - **No frontier models, no code link**, and the ENTD analysis is by construction inapplicable to unseen inputs (the backoff extension is explicitly out of scope).
  - The ρ = 0.80 correlation is reported without a confidence interval or significance test and only on the count ≥ 10⁴ subset, whose size depends on the stratified-bin choice.

## Strengths

- Decomposes a single aggregate number (mean training loss) into a rich, interpretable per-sample distribution.
- Clean separation of four candidate explanations (batch alignment, softmax bottleneck, optimization variance, data properties), each tested with a targeted experiment rather than narrative argument.
- Cross-validates the empirical-index construction with an independently verifiable brute-force hash table — a level of paranoia rare in interpretability empirical work.
- The high-entropy-frequent-prefix failure mode is a genuinely interesting and under-discussed finding, with a plausible link to text-homogeneity collapse.
- Open, reproducible stack (Pythia, PolyPythias, ∞-gram, Pile).

## Limitations

- All conclusions are drawn from Pythia models up to 2.8B parameters — roughly two orders of magnitude below frontier LLMs; the author explicitly notes ongoing efforts to extend to Pythia-6.9B/12B and OLMo.
- The strict ENTD analysis applies only to sequences present in the training set; generalization to unseen inputs (the regime where LLMs are actually useful) is left to future work.
- Analysis is restricted to single-token next-token distributions; it says nothing about chained generation quality, long-horizon coherence, or instruction-tuned behavior.
- Quantitative headline numbers depend on the specific stratified binning (length k ∈ {1,2,4,6,8,12,16,32,64} × corpus-count bins) and reservoir size N = 100 per (k, shard).
- No released code; some figures (e.g., the rank-2560 low-rank fit) rely on details that would benefit from a public artifact.

## Relevance

这篇论文为理解"LLM 的行为如何从训练数据中涌现"提供了一个数学上严格的、可数据溯源的分析框架（ENTD/ENTD-T + ∞-gram），对从事 LLM 预训练、数据 curation、机制可解释性与 scaling law 研究的工作者高度相关，尤其是关注数据归因、模型-数据对齐偏差以及文本多样性塌缩的研究方向。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the positional-invariance and softmax-bottleneck-insufficiency findings persist at Pythia-6.9B/12B and at frontier (e.g., OLMo-3, Llama-scale) models, or are they artifacts of the 70M–2.8B regime?
- What architectural or inductive-bias mechanism forces the transformer to learn a narrower-than-empirical distribution on high-entropy frequent prefixes — is it attention sparsity, the residual-stream rank, or a specific regularization effect of SGD?
- Can the ENTD framework be extended in a principled (non-heuristic-backoff) way to unseen inputs, e.g., via a mixture of ENTD and a learned smoothing distribution tied to the training objective?
- Is the ρ = 0.80 entropy-TV correlation a leading indicator of text-homogeneity collapse, and can it be used as a cheap diagnostic during pretraining?
- How do post-training stages (SFT/RLHF), which the author explicitly excludes, shift the model-ENTD TV distribution — do they widen it (explaining RLHF "diversity loss") or merely shift mass within it?
- Could the per-sample TV-trajectory taxonomy (uniform convergence vs. per-sample memorization vs. oscillation) be used to identify, at training time, which prefixes are "structurally hard" and warrant targeted data intervention?
