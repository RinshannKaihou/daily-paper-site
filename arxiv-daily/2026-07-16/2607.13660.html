§ 01

Methodology

three-stage pipeline · per modality

Pipeline

01
Whitening as metric, not likelihood. Compute W = Σ^(-1/2) over 5k MS-COCO val samples; calibrated z̃ = W(z−μ) with Cov(z̃)≈I. Unlike W-CLIP, this imposes Mahalanobis geometry — not a Gaussian assumption.
02
Project to unit sphere. u = z̃/‖z̃‖₂ ∈ S^(d-1), respecting CLIP's cosine-similarity geometry.
03
EM-fit Mixture of vMF. K components, each a semantic prototype. E-step in log-domain (stable at d≥512); M-step uses Banerjee 2005 concentration approximation.

Closed-form density

S(u) = log Σk πk · Cd(κk) · exp(κk · μkTu) posterior: γk(u) → u ≈ Σk γk(u)·μk κk ≈ R̄k(d − R̄k²) / (1 − R̄k²)

Same model serves: (a) scalar OOD score S(u), (b) soft semantic decomposition, (c) projection prior for CLIP+UnCLIP generative drift.

§ 03

Strengths & Limitations

side-by-side

Strengths what holds up

  • Resolves a genuine inconsistency. A unimodal isotropic Gaussian on a directional, multimodal space really is the wrong tool — and the vMF fix is theoretically sound and cheap.
  • One model, three jobs. The same fitted mixture powers OOD detection, semantic decomposition, and generative-drift stabilization — more than most CLIP-geometry papers offer.
  • Strongest exactly where it should be. Tail-subset OOD FPR95 33.48% and fairness AUROC 0.78→0.58 target the well-known long-tail weakness of CLIP.
  • Practical speed. 9.8 ms vs 132.7 ms per image for interpretability — deployable in concept-based tooling.
  • Not CLIP-specific. Cross-VLM validation on BLIP-2 and CoCa (Appendix C) generalizes the claim.

Limitations where it strains

  • Headline delta is misleading. ~8 pt FPR95 over the strongest real baseline NegLabel on full MS-COCO; the 20-pt figure is over a conveniently weak W-CLIP config.
  • Self-graded interpretability. Semantic Relevance is the authors' own Hausdorff-based proxy; absolute gain over SPLICE is just 0.018, with no human-judgment cross-check.
  • Long-tail claim under-tested. Only MS-COCO (80 classes) + OpenImages. No iNaturalist, LVIS, or ImageNet-LT — so the central selling point is never directly stress-tested.
  • K is fixed, not learned. 500 components is over-complete for 80 categories; hierarchical / non-parametric extensions are acknowledged but not implemented.
  • Generative side is hand-waved. Score-based / Riemannian connection is sketched as future work; no FID or CLIPScore on synthesis.
