> **一句话总结：** 本文用二叉条件树将人群递归划分为子群，再通过全概率公式检验 LLM 估计是否能从子群条件估计聚合回总体，发现前沿模型（GPT-5.4、Sonnet 4.6 等）普遍违反该自洽性，并提出"宏观谬误"现象——从更细子群重构的总体估计往往比直接问总体更贴近真实调查数据。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces a reference-free evaluation framework that builds binary conditioning trees over a population (e.g., the 2024 ACS U.S. census, the World Values Survey) and checks whether LLM estimates obey the law of total probability. Across frontier models (GPT-5.4, Sonnet 4.6, Grok 4.1, Qwen3.6 Plus), split-consistency scores at ε=0.02 are as low as 0.00–0.33 and order-consistency only 0.25–1.00; the authors document a "macro fallacy" where reconstructed aggregates from fine-grained subpopulation estimates beat the direct population-level estimate in alignment with ACS ground truth by tens of percent.

## 核心贡献

- 提出基于二叉条件树（Binary Conditioning Tree, BCT）的"划分-提示-聚合"框架：每个树层构成总体的一组有效划分，用全概率公式把子群条件估计加权聚合回总体估计，从而把"ICL 是否等同于条件推断"这一隐含假设转化为可检验的自洽性约束。
- 定义两个互补的、无需参考答案的自洽性指标——split consistency（检验相邻层聚合是否满足全概率公式）与 order consistency（检验同一子群在属性排列顺序不同时是否给出相同估计），并给出多步聚合与成对检查充分性的形式化命题（误差随树深线性 / 二次增长的上界）。
- 系统性地发现并命名"宏观谬误"（macro fallacy）：在 ACS 收入任务中，从更细子群重构出的总体估计在多数模型和阈值上比直接问总体的估计更接近真实数据，相对对齐增益最高可达 50%。
- 揭示误差来源的可分离性：细化划分能改善子群条件估计质量，但会让子群先验更难估计（总变差距离随树深上升），两者权衡导致聚合增益呈凹形（先升后降）。
- 提出"微观到宏观"（micro-to-macro）轻量级隐式提示法：让模型在单次查询中先推理相关子群再给总体估计，可在不同程度上恢复聚合增益，证明收益不只来自测试时算力增加。
- 在 ACS 收入、ACS 通勤时间、WVS 全球民意（加拿大、印尼）以及两个合成预测任务（网球 Federer–Nadal、奇幻战斗）上验证：自洽性违反普遍存在，且不随通用能力（AAI 指数）的提升而系统性改善，可作为与对齐（alignment）正交的新评价维度。

## Key Contributions

- A **partition–prompt–aggregate framework** built on binary conditioning trees (BCTs): each tree level defines a valid partition of a base population, and the law of total probability yields a family of statistically equivalent estimators of the same marginal, turning the "ICL = conditional inference" assumption into an empirically testable identity.
- Two complementary, **reference-free self-consistency scores** — *split consistency* (does the direct estimate equal the prior-weighted aggregate of its two refinements?) and *order consistency* (is the estimate invariant under reordering of conditioning constraints?) — accompanied by formal propositions bounding how local violations accumulate across tree depth (linear for split, quadratic for pairwise order).
- Identification and naming of the **macro fallacy**: on ACS income estimation, aggregates reconstructed from more fine-grained subpopulation conditionals are systematically better aligned with survey ground truth than the direct population-level estimate, with relative gains reaching up to ~50% across models and thresholds.
- A **disentangling analysis** of error sources showing that finer partitions improve conditional estimates (node-wise alignment error drops at early levels) but simultaneously make subgroup priors harder to estimate (total-variation distance to survey priors grows with depth), explaining the concave aggregation-gain curve.
- A lightweight **micro-to-macro prompting** intervention that asks the model to reason over implicit subpopulations within a single query, partially recovering the aggregation benefit and ruling out test-time compute as the sole driver.
- **Broad empirical validation** across ACS income/commute, WVS global opinion (Canada, Indonesia), and two synthetic forecasting tasks, showing that split-consistency scores stay in the 0.00–0.33 range at ε=0.02 even for frontier models, do not improve with the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, and are uncorrelated with standard alignment metrics — establishing statistical self-consistency as a distinct, unsaturated evaluation axis.

## Methodology

The framework rests on the law of total probability: for a base population X, a valid partition P, and an event E, P(E | X∈X) = Σ_{S∈P} γ_S · P(E | X∈S), where γ_S is the relative size of subgroup S.

**Binary conditioning trees (BCT).** A BCT of depth L is a tuple (X, {φ_1,…,φ_L}) where each level ℓ has one shared binary split function applied uniformly to every node at level ℓ−1. The root is the base population; nodes at level ℓ form a valid partition P_ℓ. For the ACS case study the authors split the 3.8M-respondent 2024 American Community Survey along AGE (first split: 31≤age≤68 vs. rest) and class-of-worker (second split: employed/self-employed vs. not), chosen by a regression-tree-style objective that minimizes prior-weighted within-subgroup income variance while keeping priors balanced.

**Verbalization and elicitation.** Each subpopulation is described by concatenating natural-language strings for the split criteria along its root-to-node path (e.g., "A person living in the U.S." + "between 31 and 68 years old" + "employed or self-employed"), implemented via folktexts. For each node S the model is queried for a tail probability T̂_τ(S) = P(Y>τ | X∈S) (or a full binned distribution), and subgroup priors γ̂_S are elicited via a level-wise protocol (multiple queries, renormalized, averaged). Reconstructed aggregates are T̂_{τ,agg}^{(ℓ)}(X) = Σ_{S∈P_ℓ} γ̂_S · T̂_τ(S).

**The macro fallacy (Section 4).** The aggregate alignment error AErr(ℓ) = |T_τ(X) − T̂_{τ,agg}^{(ℓ)}(X)| is compared across levels; positive relative gain 1 − AErr(ℓ)/AErr(0) means reconstruction beats direct prompting. An oracle-prior ablation isolates conditional error; a total-variation comparison isolates prior error; the law of total variance explains why refinement shifts variation from within-group to cross-group.

**Self-consistency checks (Section 5).** *Split consistency* compares T̂_h(S) against T̂_{h,agg}(S; A_k) for every admissible next split; *order consistency* compares T̂_h(S; π) across constraint orderings. Propositions bound multi-step aggregation error by ℓ·ε and pairwise-order error by C(|J|,2)·ε. Scores SC_ε(A) and OC_ε(A) report the fraction of checks satisfied at tolerance ε. Discrepancy for ordinal outcomes uses the normalized Wasserstein-1 distance.

**Micro-to-macro prompting (Section 4.4).** A single-query prompt instructs the model to first identify relevant subpopulations and then combine them into an aggregate, with no externally specified partition.

**Evaluation domains.** ACS income (thresholds τ ∈ {100, 1k, 10k, 20k, 40k, 60k, 80k} USD) and ACS commute time; WVS opinion distributions (five questions, Canada & Indonesia, age×income splits); synthetic tennis forecasting (Federer–Nadal, surface×first-set) and fantasy-combat forecasting (time-of-day×wind), the latter having no ground truth.

## Results & Highlights

- **Widespread split-consistency failure on ACS income (Table 1a):** at tolerance ε=0.02, GPT-5.4 scores SC=0.33, Sonnet 4.6 SC=0.00, Grok 4.3 SC=0.00, Qwen3.6 Plus SC=0.17 — i.e., most models satisfy fewer than one in three aggregation checks. Order consistency is higher but still imperfect (0.25–1.00).
- **Self-consistency is task-dependent, not just model-dependent:** swapping the ACS target from income to commute time (same tree, same priors) changes GPT-5.4's split score from 0.33 to 0.17 and Qwen3.6 Plus's order score from 0.50 to 0.25 (Table 1b).
- **The macro fallacy is large and robust (Figure 3):** on ACS income, the relative alignment gain of reconstructed aggregates over direct prompting reaches up to ~50% for GPT-5.4 across income thresholds and is positive for every tested model (Opus 4.7, Sonnet 4.6, 3.1 Pro, 4.20, 3 Flash, 3.6 Plus, 4o mini) at τ=40k USD, with the average gain curve concave in tree depth.
- **Priors degrade with depth (Figure 4b):** the total-variation distance between LLM-estimated priors and survey-based priors increases monotonically from level 1 to level 4 for GPT-5.4, 3.1 Pro, and Sonnet 4.6, capping the benefit of finer partitions.
- **Self-consistency does not scale with capability (Figure 6):** across the full GPT, Gemini, Claude/Opus and Qwen families, neither split nor order consistency at τ=40k USD improves systematically with the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index — the scores remain far from saturated even for the most capable models in each family.
- **Micro-to-macro prompting partially recovers the benefit (Figure 5):** across five income thresholds and seven models the average relative error gain over direct prompting is positive for most models (e.g., GPT-5.4 and 4o mini), though it is more model-dependent and less systematic than explicit tree-based aggregation.
- **Global opinion modeling (Table 2):** on five WVS questions across Canada and Indonesia, average split-consistency scores range from 0.32 (DeepSeek V-3.2, Qwen3.5 27B) to 0.70 (Sonnet 4.6) and order-consistency averages from 0.35 (DeepSeek V-3.2) to 0.90 (Sonnet 4.6); no model is uniformly consistent across questions.
- **Synthetic forecasting also fails (Table 3):** in the tennis task no model satisfies more than half the split checks (GPT-5.4 SC=0.33, Sonnet 4.6 SC=0.50, Grok 4.1 Fast SC=0.17, Qwen3.6 Plus SC=0.00); in the fantasy task Qwen3.6 Plus reaches SC=1.00 while Grok 4.1 Fast drops to SC=0.17, demonstrating consistency violations even where ground truth does not exist.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. While probabilistic-coherence checks for LLMs exist (Zhu & Griffiths 2024 on conjunction fallacies; Paleka et al. 2025 on forecaster logical relations; Fluri et al. 2024 on consistency checks), the *partition-based* framing via binary conditioning trees and the formalization of split/order consistency with depth-dependent error bounds is a genuinely new, reference-free scaffold. The "macro fallacy" finding — that explicit decomposition systematically beats direct aggregation — is a counterintuitive and actionable contribution.
- **Rigor:** Strong on the theoretical side (Propositions 2, 6, 7 with proofs; clear distinction between split and order effects; oracle-prior ablation). The empirical protocol is reproducible (public ACS data, named models, fixed tolerance ε=0.02, 1000-sample bootstrap CIs). A legitimate concern is that the headline self-consistency numbers depend on the somewhat arbitrary tolerance ε=0.02 and on the normalized Wasserstein metric; the paper does not present a sensitivity sweep over ε, so the "0.00 vs 0.33" split scores could shift materially with a different threshold.
- **Reproducibility:** Good for the ACS/WVS experiments (public data, folktexts pipeline described, prompt templates in Appendices G.2/G.5), weaker for the forecasting tasks which are author-constructed synthetic scenarios (Federer–Nadal, fantasy combat) without released code or exact scenario files. Model identifiers (e.g., "GPT-5.4", "Sonnet 4.6", "Grok 4.3", "4.20") are future-dated and will need version pinning to reproduce.
- **Significance:** The diagnosis matters for any downstream use that treats LLM outputs as conditional probabilities — synthetic survey generation, in-context personalization, social simulation, and LLM forecasting (Prophet Arena). Showing that alignment at the population level does *not* imply coherent subpopulation estimates is a concrete, consequential caveat for that literature.
- **Red flags / weak points:** (1) The macro-fallacy evidence is "primarily" (their word, in Limitations) tied to ACS, a single domain with well-behaved demographic splits; the WVS extension only adds two countries and five questions, and the forecasting tasks are toy-sized with no real ground truth, so the external-validity claim is under-supported. (2) Split attribute selection is hand-engineered (age, employment) to be "informative about the target variable" via a regression-tree objective — this is a data quirk that favors the macro-fallacy result, since the splits are chosen precisely to reduce within-group income variance; an ablation with random or target-orthogonal splits is missing. (3) Priors are elicited from the same model whose conditionals are being checked, so a shared bias could either mask or inflate inconsistency in ways the paper does not analyze. (4) The conclusion that consistency "does not correlate with standard benchmark metrics" rests on a visual eyeball of Figure 6 against the AAI Index rather than a reported correlation coefficient.

## Strengths

- Clean, principled core idea: instantiating the law of total probability as a reference-free, domain-agnostic consistency test for the conditional-inference view of ICL.
- The macro fallacy is a surprising, practically useful result — it suggests a free prompting improvement (decompose then aggregate) that holds across models and thresholds.
- Careful disentangling of conditional error vs. prior error via oracle-prior ablations and total-variation analysis, with a law-of-total-variance explanation for why refinement helps.
- Genuine breadth: four problem domains, ~25 frontier models, persona-prompting and forecasting settings, with both alignment (where ground truth exists) and reference-free consistency (where it does not).
- Honest framing of self-consistency as only a *necessary* condition for faithful conditional inference, and a limitations section that names the ACS-centric dependency.

## Limitations

- Alignment/macro-fallacy evidence relies primarily on ACS, a single U.S. census domain with convenient demographic splits; generalization to other populations and non-demographic conditioning is not established.
- Self-consistency scores are sensitive to the tolerance ε=0.02 and the normalized Wasserstein metric, with no reported ε sensitivity analysis.
- Split attributes are selected to be target-informative (regression-tree objective on income variance), which plausibly amplifies the macro fallacy; no neutral-split ablation is provided.
- Synthetic forecasting tasks are small and author-constructed with no real ground truth, so they demonstrate that the checks *can* be applied, not that they predict real-world forecasting accuracy.
- A model can be internally consistent yet misaligned, so passing the checks is insufficient for faithful conditional inference.
- Code and exact scenario files for the forecasting experiments are not described as released, limiting reproduction of those specific results.

## Relevance

本文提出的"统计自洽性"作为与对齐正交的参考无关评测维度，直接呼应了 LLM-as-simulator / LLM-forecaster 研究中对"模型输出是否可作为条件分布"这一根本假设的审视；对于关注 LLM 不确定性建模、persona 模拟与社会仿真可靠性的研究方向具有方法论价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do split/order consistency scores behave under a sweep of the tolerance ε (e.g., 0.01–0.10) and under alternative discrepancy metrics (KL, JS, unnormalized Wasserstein)? Is the "frontier models are inconsistent" conclusion robust?
- Does the macro fallacy survive when split attributes are chosen to be target-orthogonal or random, rather than selected by a regression-tree objective that minimizes within-group income variance?
- Can self-consistency scores be improved by training-time or RLHF-time enforcement of the law-of-total-probability constraints, and does that also improve downstream alignment — or does it trade off against other capabilities?
- Is there a measurable correlation between split/order consistency on the synthetic forecasting tasks and real forecasting performance on benchmarks like Prophet Arena?
- How do the consistency violations scale with the number of attributes d beyond 2, given the quadratic pairwise-order bound, and at what depth does prior-error explosion dominate conditional-accuracy gains for richer trees?
- Could anchoring the consistency constraints at the better-aligned lower-level estimates (as the authors speculate in the Discussion) yield a training objective that propagates fine-grained knowledge upward into more accurate aggregates?
