Key Finding

Methodology — five linked pieces

01 · generative model
de Finetti latent α
Per-instance false-accept rate α~G on [0,1]; k verdicts i.i.d. Bernoulli(α) given α. The mean-field exchangeability idealization.
02 · exact posterior
ℓk = ℓ₀ − ln mk
Marginal P(all accept)=E[αᵏ]=mₖ. ln mk is a cumulant gen. function ⇒ strict concavity; Beta(a,b) ⇒ polynomial decay k−b.
03 · two-sided
trichotomy + crossover
Varying β~H gives ℓk ≈ const + (bα−bβ) ln k. Gates eventually help / plateau / harm. Closed-form k†.
04 · inversion
R=2 identifies ρv
Per-instance accept counts Xi~Bin(R,αi); U-stats recover moments. Three estimators: moment, beta-binomial MLE, NPMLE.
05 · validation
falsification loop
N=4000, fixed seeds. Fit Beta-binomial at R=8, extrapolate k≤25. Predicts r₅=0.954 vs 0.953 truth — independence gives 0.998.

Empirical anchor

Tsui [2025]: across 14 open models, an average 64.5% of self-generated errors survive self-checking — the empirical magnitude of the blind-spot tail the theory formalizes.

The survivorship mechanism

Surviving errors are the high-α ones, so late gates face selected survivors, not fresh errors. Per-gate evidence ∆j+1 strictly decreases. No voting analogue.

"An operator budgeting gates by the independence formula believes they bought five nines; they bought 98%."

Strengths

  • Clean open-problem answer. Changes exactly one assumption in Aksu's Odds Law and derives falsifiable structure.
  • Survivorship-tilt mechanism — verification-specific insight: late gates meet selected high-α errors.
  • Two-verdict measurability. ρv identifiable at R=2; three nested estimators with honest ill-posedness treatment.
  • Actionable conclusion — decorrelation > more gates — algebraically explains the self-correction literature.
  • Trichotomy + k† predicts a finite internal optimum even at zero gate cost; Λ*=1 is the wrong criterion.
  • Open code, fixed seeds, reusable falsification-loop protocol for real logs.

Limitations

  • Zero real-data validation. All quantitative results are synthetic; real-log measurement is "in progress."
  • Scalar-latent / exchangeability (Asm. 2.1) compresses all inter-gate structure into one α; heterogeneous families need a vector latent deferred to a sequel.
  • Conjunctive (all-accept) only. No soft/probabilistic gating or weighted ensemble verification.
  • Beta-centric numerics. Polynomial exponent and k† robustness to non-Beta G asserted asymptotically, not shown numerically.
  • Ceiling ill-posed by construction — the most important quantity needs large R, undercutting "two verdicts suffice" (that's only for ρv).
  • Single-author note on a narrow, 2025–2026 arXiv cluster; not yet peer-reviewed.
Credibility
Sole-author math.ST preprint. Proofs rigorous (de Finetti, Watson's lemma, Backus–Gilbert inversion). Code at github.com/jianganghan/harness-verifier-cascades.

Open Questions

Q1

Does the de Finetti scalar latent fit real generator–verifier pairs, or does NPMLE reveal multimodality / heavy α=1 atoms that break Beta closed forms?

Q2

Is the harmful regime (bα < bβ) realized in practice — for which task categories (code, math, factual QA)?

Q3

Does partial decorrelation (one cross-family gate) measurably raise the exponent b, and does k† still predict the reliability peak?

Q4

How does R=2 estimation noise in ρ̂v propagate into the binary decision rule ("if large, stop buying gates")? Is there a principled threshold?

Q5

When a harness combines voting over generators AND verification gates, which correlation dominates the reliability ceiling?

Q6

What does the empirical ρv distribution look like across frontier model families and task types?
