> **一句话总结：** 提出 CRTBench（350 个题族、1750 道题）用受控的逻辑等价改写（逆否命题、双重否定、否定翻转、被动语态等）评测前沿 LLM 的逻辑不变性，发现 GPT-5.4-mini 虽有 98.9% 的基础准确率但仅有 60.3% 的族级一致性，推理增强可大幅修复 GPT-5.4-mini（60.3→85.4%）但对 GPT-5.4 整体无效——因为它在量化推理类上崩溃（92→20%）抵消了在嵌套否定上的收益。

> **TL;DR:** CRTBench probes whether LLMs answer logically equivalent questions consistently via controlled transformations (contrapositive, double negation, De Morgan, quantifier rewrite, negation flip, passive voice) across 350 families / 1,750 questions. The study reveals a sharp accuracy–consistency gap: GPT-5.4-mini hits 98.9% base accuracy but only 60.3% family consistency, while o4-mini reaches 96.9% consistency (+36.6pp over GPT-5.4-mini). Enabling reasoning effort rescues GPT-5.4-mini (60.3→85.4%) but leaves GPT-5.4 unchanged (86.6%) because quantifier reasoning collapses from 92% to 20% under high reasoning effort.

## 核心贡献

- 构建并开源 **CRTBench**：一个面向"逻辑不变性"的评测集，包含 350 个题族（共 1750 题），覆盖 7 个等量平衡的推理类别（命题逻辑、三段论、德摩根律、量化推理、多跳推理、嵌套否定、条件谬误），每个题族由 1 个基础问题 + 4 个受控改写组成。
- 提出受控逻辑变换体系：等价保持型（逆否、双重否定、德摩根、量化改写、被动语态）、答案反转型（否定翻转）、表层对照型（被动语态），使失败能精确归因到具体的逻辑算子，而非笼统的"复述不一致"。
- 定义两个互补指标——**Base accuracy**（单题正确率）与 **Family consistency**（基础题 + 全部 4 个改写全部答对的比例），二者差距直接量化"自相矛盾"程度。
- 在 GPT-5.4、GPT-5.4-mini（含/不含推理）、Claude Sonnet 4、Gemini 2.5 Flash、o4-mini 上系统性揭示"准确率高 ≠ 逻辑一致"现象，并对变换与类别做归因分析。
- 发现一个反直觉结果：启用推理对小型模型 GPT-5.4-mini 提升明显（+25.1pp），但对 GPT-5.4 整体无效——因为它在量化推理上从 92% 崩塌到 20%，抵消了嵌套否定（50→94%）等增益；CoT 提示则几乎无效（≤1.4pp）。

## Key Contributions

- **CRTBench dataset**: 350 manually-audited question families (1,750 questions) spanning seven category-balanced reasoning types (propositional logic, syllogistic, De Morgan, quantifier, multi-hop, nested negation, conditional fallacies; 50 families each).
- **Causally-attributable transformation design**: Unlike paraphrase-based or faithfulness tests, transformations are logically grounded (contrapositive P→Q ⇔ ¬Q→¬P, double negation P ⇔ ¬¬P, De Morgan, quantifier rewrite, negation flip, passive voice), so failures localize to specific logical operators rather than vague "rephrasing."
- **Two complementary metrics**: Base accuracy (correctness on the original question) and Family consistency (base + all four reformulations correct), whose gap is a directly interpretable measure of self-contradiction.
- **Cross-frontier-model study** documenting an accuracy–consistency gap on GPT-5.4, GPT-5.4-mini (±reasoning), Claude Sonnet 4, Gemini 2.5 Flash, and o4-mini under matched zero-shot prompting.
- **Reasoning-effort ablation** showing selective, non-uniform benefits: reasoning rescues GPT-5.4-mini but not GPT-5.4 overall, with a quantifier-reasoning collapse (92→20%) under high reasoning effort being the key mechanism, plus evidence that chain-of-thought prompting has near-zero effect.

## Methodology

CRTBench measures **logical invariance**: logically equivalent reformulations of the same question should receive consistent answers. A *question family* = 1 base question + 4 reformulations, each applying one transformation from three classes:

- **Equivalence-preserving** (answer must match base): contrapositive, double negation, De Morgan, quantifier rewrite, passive voice.
- **Answer-reversing** (label is the logical complement): negation flip; "Cannot be determined" stays unchanged.
- **Surface control**: passive voice (a logical no-op that only changes surface phrasing).

Coverage is category-dependent: contrapositive applies to 290 families, passive to 300, De Morgan to 50, quantifier rewrite to 60, while double negation and negation flip apply to all 350.

**Metrics.** (1) *Base accuracy*: model answers the original question correctly. (2) *Family consistency*: all 5 questions in a family (base + 4 reformulations) match their respective labels — one wrong answer makes the family inconsistent.

**Data construction & validation.** Two-stage manual audit: first all 350 base questions checked for logical correctness, gold-label clarity, and grammaticality; then, after generating reformulations, families re-audited for transformation fidelity, answer-label correctness (with explicit mapping for answer-reversing items, not generic Yes/No inversion), and naturalness. The authors explicitly note that no formal inter-annotator agreement was collected, so this is treated as internal quality control, not a naturalness study.

**Evaluation protocol.** Zero-shot, T=0 (o4-mini exposes no temperature). All models prompted to output exactly "Yes" / "No" / "Cannot be determined" after an "Answer:" line. Answers extracted with a regex-first heuristic (explicit "Answer:", "final answer is", bold formatting, conclusion phrases, then a fallback over Yes/No cues; unparseable responses marked UNCLEAR and excluded — only 5 such cases total, all from Gemini 2.5 Flash = 0.29%). All API calls made in April 2026 using identifiers gpt-5.4, gpt-5.4-mini, claude-sonnet-4-20250514, gemini-2.5-flash, o4-mini. Code, data, and model outputs pledged for release upon acceptance.

## Results & Highlights

- **Sharp accuracy–consistency gap on CRTBench (n=350 families, zero-shot).** GPT-5.4-mini reaches **98.9% base accuracy** but only **60.3% family consistency** (a 38.6pp gap); GPT-5.4 is 98.9% accuracy / 86.6% consistency. Even the strongest model, o4-mini, shows a gap: **98.6% accuracy vs 96.9% family consistency**.
- **Reasoning-optimized o4-mini is the strongest overall**, at **96.9% family consistency vs 60.3% for GPT-5.4-mini** (a 36.6pp delta) and 98.6% accuracy.
- **Failures cluster on operator-level, not surface, transformations.** Contrapositive is the hardest for GPT-5.4-mini at **72.4% vs 98.7% on passive voice** (26.3pp gap); o4-mini's corresponding gap is only 1.7pp. Double negation for GPT-5.4-mini is **84.6% vs o4-mini's 98.0%**. De Morgan is near-ceiling for all (96–100%).
- **Reasoning effort helps GPT-5.4-mini but not GPT-5.4 overall.** GPT-5.4-mini family consistency rises **60.3% → 85.4% (+25.1pp)** and double-negation consistency rises 84.6% → 92.3%, but base accuracy drops 98.9% → 96.0%. GPT-5.4's family consistency stays pinned at **86.6%** while its base accuracy *falls* from 98.9% to 92.9%.
- **Quantifier-reasoning collapse under high reasoning effort.** Per-category family consistency for GPT-5.4: quantifier reasoning goes **92.0% → 20.0%**, while nested negation improves 50.0% → 94.0% and conditional fallacies 84.0% → 98.0%. Mechanism: incorrect "Cannot be determined" abstentions explode from 5 (all wrong) to 119 (all wrong), dropping quantifier question-level accuracy from **98.0% (245/250) to 52.4% (131/250)**. Removing the quantifier category moves GPT-5.4(r) from (92.9 acc, 86.6 cons) to **(99.7, 97.7)**.
- **CoT prompting is statistically negligible.** Family consistency changes of +1.4pp (GPT-5.4-mini: 60.3→61.7%), +0.0pp (GPT-5.4), and +0.0pp (GPT-5.4(r)); none significant at α=0.05.
- **Hardest zero-shot category for GPT-5.4** is nested negation (50.0%), followed by conditional fallacies (84.0%) and propositional logic (86.0%); De Morgan and multi-hop reach 100%.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (moderate):** The core framing — that accuracy ≠ consistency on logical reasoning — is not new (the authors cite Lin et al. 2025 "Existing LLMs are not self-consistent for simple tasks" and Shi et al. 2026 on paraphrastic probing). CRTBench's distinct contribution is the *transformation-grounded* design that allows causal attribution to specific operators (contrapositive, double negation, quantifier rewrite), which paraphrase-based work cannot do. The genuinely interesting and paper-specific finding is the **GPT-5.4 quantifier-reasoning collapse under reasoning effort (92→20%)** driven by spurious "Cannot be determined" abstentions — a non-obvious, mechanism-level result.
- **Rigor (mixed):** Strengths include a category-balanced 350-family design, a clearly interpretable two-metric scheme (accuracy vs family consistency), matched zero-shot prompting, and a 95% CI reported in a footnote (e.g., ±5.1pp at n=350). Weaknesses are real: the validation is **manual audit by the authors without formal inter-annotator agreement**, so claims about linguistic naturalness are admittedly conservative; the family-consistency metric is intrinsically stringent (one wrong of five = inconsistent) and the paper does not report a softer per-question pairwise consistency that would let readers judge robustness to metric choice; only a **single prompt template** is used, and the CoT result rests on that one template.
- **Reproducibility (good but contingent):** Model identifiers, temperature, prompts, answer-extraction regex heuristic, and per-category/per-transformation tables are all disclosed. However, code/data/outputs are *promised upon acceptance* rather than already public, and the benchmark depends on proprietary April-2026 API snapshots of frontier models that may drift, limiting long-term replication.
- **Significance (useful, not paradigm-shifting):** The benchmark is small (1,750 questions, binary/ternary answers only) and measures controlled — not natural — reformulations. Practical impact is strongest as a complementary diagnostic for high-stakes multi-query coherence (legal, medical, knowledge-base) rather than as a general reasoning benchmark. The quantifier-collapse finding is a concrete, actionable signal for reasoning-model training.
- **Red flags / specific concerns:**
  - The headline GPT-5.4(r) "no improvement" result hinges on a single 50-family quantifier category; the authors are honest that this is a category-balanced artifact, but readers should note the result is sensitive to category weighting.
  - All transformations are template-generated; the benchmark explicitly does not test fully natural paraphrase variation, so generalization to real-world query rewriting is unproven.
  - No comparison to existing related benchmarks (FOLIO, LogicBench) on the same models under the same protocol — the conceptual differentiation is argued, not empirically demonstrated.
  - "Cannot be determined" handling is uneven: it is treated as a stable label under negation flip, but its semantics across contrapositive/double-negation reformulations is not deeply analyzed, which is exactly where GPT-5.4(r)'s abstention pathology lives.

## Strengths

- Causally attributable transformation taxonomy that isolates *which* logical operation breaks, unlike paraphrase-based consistency tests.
- Clean, directly interpretable accuracy-vs-consistency gap framing with per-transformation and per-category breakdowns.
- The quantifier-reasoning collapse under reasoning effort is a striking, mechanism-level finding (abstentions 5→119, all wrong) with concrete implications for RL-trained reasoning models.
- Honest limitations section and conservative interpretation of the manual-audit validation.
- Fully disclosed model identifiers, prompts, extraction heuristic, and dataset statistics (Appendix A/B).

## Limitations

- Template-generated, manually audited — not validated via formal multi-annotator naturalness study; only controlled (not natural) reformulations are tested.
- Small absolute size (350 families / 1,750 questions) and restricted to Yes/No/Cannot-be-determined answers; does not capture open-ended or long-chain multi-step reasoning where negation composes over many steps.
- Single zero-shot prompt template evaluated; generalization to other prompt styles is untested (and the CoT null result is bound to that template).
- Proprietary April-2026 API model snapshots may drift; code/data/outputs are pledged for release only upon acceptance.
- No head-to-head empirical comparison against FOLIO/LogicBench on the same models to quantify incremental diagnostic value.
- The family-consistency metric is all-or-nothing per family; a graded per-question pairwise metric is not reported, so sensitivity to the strict metric choice is unclear.

## Relevance

这篇工作直接对接 LLM 评测与可信推理方向：CRTBench 提供了一个轻量但可归因的"逻辑不变性"诊断工具，对评估 reasoning model（如 GPT-5/o4 系列）在法律、医疗、知识库等多查询一致性敏感场景下的可靠性具有参考价值；其"推理增强反而在量化推理上崩溃"的发现，对研究 RL 训练如何影响模型推理稳定性亦有启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the GPT-5.4(r) quantifier-collapse (92→20%) replicate across independent API snapshots and on open-weight reasoning models (e.g., R1-distill, Qwen3-thinking), or is it an artifact of one RL checkpoint?
- What happens to the accuracy–consistency gap under natural (non-templated) paraphrases produced by a separate LLM, and does the transformation-level attribution still hold?
- Would a softer per-question pairwise consistency metric change the model ranking, especially for the tightly-clustered mid-tier (Claude Sonnet 4 / Gemini 2.5 Flash / GPT-5.4 around 86–89%)?
- Can consistency-aware training (e.g., penalizing contradictory answers across reformulations) close the gap without inducing the abstention pathology seen on quantifiers?
- How does the gap scale with chain length — do nested-negation failures compound linearly or super-linearly in multi-step reasoning?
- Is the "Cannot be determined" inflation under reasoning effort specific to universal/existential quantifier phrasing, or does it generalize to other undecidable-but-valid inference patterns?
