> **一句话总结：** SEED 提出一种自演化的 on-policy 蒸馏框架：用最新策略自身既当 actor 又当 analyzer，从已完成的轨迹中抽取自然语言"事后技能"（hindsight skill），通过对同一采样动作在"普通上下文 vs 技能增强上下文"下的 log-prob 偏移构造稠密的 token 级蒸馏信号，与 GRPO 联合训练；在 ALFWorld / WebShop / Search-based QA 三个长程智能体基准上，相比 GRPO 在 ALFWorld 上将平均成功率从 75.0 提升到 91.8（Qwen2.5-3B），且推理时无需任何外部技能库或额外提示。

> **TL;DR:** SEED is a self-evolving on-policy distillation (OPD) framework for long-horizon agentic RL: the latest policy checkpoint serves simultaneously as the rollout actor and the trajectory analyzer that mines natural-language "hindsight skills" from completed episodes; sampled actions are re-scored under ordinary and skill-augmented contexts, and the resulting log-probability shift becomes a dense token-level distillation loss trained jointly with GRPO. On Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, SEED lifts ALFWorld average success from 75.0 (GRPO) to 91.8 (+16.8), Search-based QA average accuracy from 36.4 to 45.7 (+9.3), and WebShop success from 63.3 to 78.9 (+15.6), surpassing strong distillation baselines (SDAR, RLSD, Skill-SD, OPSD) while requiring no skill bank, analyzer, or augmented prompt at inference time.

## 核心贡献

- **自演化训练回路**：同一策略快照同时充当 actor 与 analyzer，每次策略更新后刷新两者，使决策行为与事后技能分析能力共同进化，避免了固定教师或静态技能数据集与策略失配的问题。
- **策略同步的 on-policy 蒸馏机制**：保持采样动作不变，仅在普通上下文与"技能增强"上下文下重新打分，把技能导致的 log-prob 偏移经 sigmoid 门控映射成稠密 token 级蒸馏信号，与 GRPO 联合优化；推理时彻底移除 analyzer 与技能。
- **理论支撑对应三大设计要求**：附录给出三个命题——on-policy hindsight 产生 occupancy-matched 自适应目标；稠密技能信用在奖励 ties 时仍非退化（梯度模长等于门控的方差）；analyzer 同步刷新给出明确的 staleness 上界。
- **跨模态、多 backbone 的广泛验证**：在 ALFWorld / WebShop / Search-based QA 上使用 Qwen2.5-3B/7B、Qwen3-1.7B 三种 backbone，并在 Sokoban / EZPoints 上扩展到视觉智能体（Qwen2.5-VL-3B），还提供 ALFWorld Unseen 跨域泛化与样本效率分析。

## Key Contributions

- A **self-evolving OPD framework** in which the latest policy snapshot plays two roles simultaneously — the rollout actor and the trajectory analyzer that turns completed episodes into natural-language hindsight skills — refreshed together after every update so decision-making and skill analysis co-evolve rather than drift apart.
- A **policy-synchronized hindsight distillation mechanism** that fixes sampled on-policy actions, re-scores them under ordinary vs skill-augmented contexts, and converts the detached log-probability shift via a sigmoid gate (`g = σ(β_opd · Δ)`) into a dense token-level on-policy distillation loss jointly optimized with a KL-regularized GRPO objective.
- A **theoretical appendix** with three propositions matching the paper's three design requirements: (i) on-policy hindsight induces an occupancy-matched adaptive target; (ii) dense skill credit stays informative even when all group outcomes are tied, with gradient magnitude exactly equal to `Var_{π_k}[w_k(c,v)]`; (iii) refreshing the shared analyzer yields an explicit analyzer-staleness bound.
- A **broad empirical evaluation** across text-based embodied interaction (ALFWorld), web navigation (WebShop), tool-augmented QA (7 datasets under the Search-R1 protocol), and a multimodal extension (Sokoban, EZPoints with Qwen2.5-VL-3B), using three Qwen backbones (1.7B/3B/7B), plus cross-domain generalization and sample-efficiency studies.

## Methodology

SEED proceeds in two stages, both built around a single autoregressive LLM policy.

**Stage 1 — Hindsight Skill SFT.** For each benchmark, the authors collect an offline pool of trajectories by rolling out a base instruct model (`M = 180` training tasks, `K_0 = 8` rollouts each → 1,440 trajectories). An **external analyzer** — GLM-5.2 at temperature 0.0, max 4,096 tokens — is given the serialized trajectory (task, observations, actions, rewards, outcome) and produces a JSON with an `episode_summary` and one `episode_skill`, written as a *policy-facing* rule (a workflow for successes, an avoidance rule for failures). After light format validation, the policy is fine-tuned for 3 epochs with a standard NLL objective to predict the skill from a completed trajectory. This single checkpoint initializes both the RL actor and the synchronized analyzer.

**Stage 2 — Self-Evolving On-Policy Distillation.** At the start of each update the current policy is frozen as `π_θold`; it both (a) samples a group of `N = 8` trajectories per task and (b) acts as the analyzer `A_θold(·)` that converts each completed trajectory `τ` into a hindsight skill `s`. The same sampled action tokens are then re-scored by the *trainable* policy `π_θ` under two contexts:
- teacher branch: `ℓ^skill = log π_θ(a | H(h, s), a_<)` with the skill inserted,
- student branch: `ℓ^θ = log π_θ(a | h, a_<)` from the ordinary history.

The detached shift `Δ = sg[ℓ^skill − ℓ^θ]` is mapped to a sigmoid gate `g = σ(β_opd · Δ)` (following SDAR, Lu et al. 2026a, with `β_opd = 5.0`). The OPD loss is `L_opd = E[m · g · sg(ℓ^skill − ℓ^θ)]`; since teacher and gate are detached, it is gradient-equivalent to a gate-weighted NLL that pushes the ordinary policy toward tokens its own hindsight endorses. The full objective is `L_SEED = L_rl + λ_opd · L_opd` (with `λ_opd = 0.01`), where `L_rl` is the clipped GRPO loss plus a KL regularizer. After the update, `π_θ` becomes the new `π_θold`, closing the self-evolving loop. **At inference time** the agent acts only from the ordinary history — no analyzer, skill bank, retrieval module, or augmented prompt is deployed.

Training uses 150 policy updates, batch size 16 (ALFWorld/WebShop) or 128 (Search), group size 8, learning rate 1e-6, PPO clip 0.2, on 8× A800 80G GPUs.

## Results & Highlights

All quantitative claims are taken from the paper's own tables/figures (Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct unless noted).

- **ALFWorld macro-average success rate (Qwen2.5-3B, Table 1):** SEED = **91.8%**, vs GRPO = **75.0%** (+16.8), Skill-GRPO = 60.2%, Skill-GRPO* = 80.5%, GRPO+OPSD = 81.2%, SDAR = 84.4% (+7.4 over SDAR). On the 7B backbone SEED reaches **96.1%** vs GRPO 81.2 (+14.9), and on Qwen3-1.7B SEED reaches **92.0%** vs GRPO 46.1 (+45.9 — the largest gap in the paper).
- **Search-based QA average accuracy (Table 1, Qwen2.5-3B):** SEED = **45.7%** vs GRPO = **36.4%** (+9.3); on Qwen2.5-7B SEED = 48.6% vs GRPO 42.0 (+6.6). Per-dataset, SEED reaches 47.2 on PopQA and 42.0 on 2WikiMultiHopQA for the 3B model.
- **WebShop (Table 1, Qwen2.5-3B):** SEED task-completion score = **88.5** vs GRPO 79.8 (+8.7) and exact success = **78.9%** vs GRPO **63.3%** (+15.6) and vs SDAR 68.0 (+10.9). On Qwen2.5-7B SEED score is 89.7 but success is **78.1%**, which actually *underperforms* SDAR's 82.8% — the only aggregate where SEED does not win.
- **Sample efficiency (Figure 4 / Table 6, ALFWorld):** SEED trained on **60% of the data reaches 80.7**, exceeding GRPO trained on the **full dataset (75.0)**; at every data fraction from 20%–100%, SEED beats GRPO by +13.4 to +30.2 points.
- **Cross-domain generalization (ALFWorld Unseen, Table 7):** SEED lifts average success from 70.9 (GRPO) to **86.2 (+15.3)**, with the largest gains on Heat (+35.0), Look (+18.3), and Pick (+16.5).
- **Multimodal extension (Table 8, Qwen2.5-VL-3B):** SEED reaches **82.0% on Sokoban [6×6]** and **100.0% on EZPoints**, average **91.0%** vs GRPO 77.0 (+14.0); ReAct averages only 7.4%.
- **Ablation (Table 2, ALFWorld, Qwen2.5-3B):** removing Hindsight Skill SFT drops the average from 91.8 → 86.0 (−5.8); removing Self-Evolving OPD → 87.0 (−4.8); replacing on-policy skills with a static offline library → 84.4 (−7.4, the largest single-component drop).
- **Training dynamics (Figure 3):** by step 40, SEED reaches ~57% success while GRPO sits near 35%; mean episode length drops from ~28 turns to 13 (vs ~16 for GRPO).

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are from Tsinghua University (lead, Wu and Yang equal contribution, Wu is project leader), Zhejiang University, CUHK, Nanyang Technological University, and Tongji University. The same broader group has recently produced closely related work on agentic RL and self-distillation (SDAR — Lu et al. 2026a; SPARK — Wu et al. 2026a; OPID — Yang et al. 2026b; ATLAS — Wu et al. 2026b), so they have deep infrastructure and a consistent line of attack on this problem. The paper provides a full Algorithm 1, complete hyperparameter table (Table 5), all prompts (Figures 10–11), representative extracted skills (Table 4), and code/model/project-page links (`jinyangwu/SEED`). Three formal propositions with proofs in Appendix A give a genuine (if local) theoretical grounding.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty is incremental rather than radical.** The confidence-gated OPD loss is lifted almost verbatim from SDAR (Lu et al. 2026a) — the paper explicitly says it "follows SDAR" for the gate — and the teacher/student re-scoring trick is the standard OPSD construction. The genuinely new idea is narrower than the framing suggests: make the analyzer the *same* refreshed checkpoint as the actor, and extract skills on-policy rather than from a static library. That is a sensible and well-motivated delta, but readers should not expect a new distillation algorithm.
- **One weak/likely-mistuned baseline inflates the picture.** OPSD collapses to ~0.0 accuracy on six of the seven Search-QA datasets across *all three* backbones (Table 1), and to 3.1% / 2.3% WebShop success — a failure mode so total it strongly suggests a reproduction bug rather than a fair fight. OPSD is also a *direct ancestor* of SEED's mechanism, so its catastrophic failure conveniently makes the family of methods SEED improves upon look worse.
- **Bootstrap dependence on GLM-5.2 is under-explored.** Stage-1 SFT uses GLM-5.2 (Z.ai) as the external analyzer for *all* backbones; there is **no ablation on analyzer choice, size, or quality**, and no analysis of how skill quality propagates into final RL performance. The "self-evolving" claim is also slightly oversold: only Stage 2 is self-evolving; the cold-start capability still depends on a strong proprietary teacher.
- **The self-evolution risk the authors themselves flag.** Appendix E honestly notes the central failure mode — because actor and analyzer share parameters, shared blind spots can be reinforced into "apparently reusable rules," and model self-preference can bias the gate. The paper offers no experiment that *measures* this risk (e.g., tracking skill quality over iterations, or comparing against an oracle analyzer), so the framework's ceiling under self-generated noise is untested.
- **Gains are not uniform across settings.** On Qwen2.5-7B WebShop exact success, SEED's 78.1% is below SDAR's 82.8% (Table 1), and on ALFWorld Unseen "Clean" it regresses by 2.9 points. The headline averages are strong but mask a few non-wins that are not discussed.
- **Small SFT budget and modest scale.** Only 1,440 SFT trajectories per benchmark, three small backbones (≤7B), 150 updates, single-seed results. No variance is reported, and the largest model is 7B — it is unclear whether the self-evolving analyzer remains coherent at 30B+ where the GLM-5.2 SFT teacher is suddenly *weaker* than the student.
- **Reproducibility is good on paper** (full hyperparameters, prompts, algorithm, promised code release) but depends on access to GLM-5.2 for exact replication of the SFT data, which is a non-trivial external dependency.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated two-stage design with an inference-time footprint identical to the base policy (no skill bank, no retrieval, no extra prompt) — a genuine deployment advantage over Skill-Prompt/Skill-GRPO*.
- Strong and consistent gains over both outcome-only RL (GRPO) and prior skill-/self-distillation baselines on three text benchmarks plus a multimodal extension, across three model scales.
- Sample-efficiency result is compelling: 60%-data SEED beats 100%-data GRPO on ALFWorld, and the cross-domain ALFWorld Unseen gains (especially +35 on Heat) show real transfer rather than memorization.
- The ablations cleanly isolate the three claimed components (SFT, self-evolving loop, on-policy skills), and the largest drop comes from de-syncing the analyzer — directly supporting the central thesis.
- Theoretical appendix ties each design choice to a concrete proposition with proofs, which is uncommon for agentic-RL papers.

## Limitations

- The OPD mechanism itself is largely inherited from SDAR; the novel contribution is the self-evolving/shared-analyzer loop and its on-policy skill generation, not a new distillation objective.
- Stage-1 skill generation depends on a strong proprietary external analyzer (GLM-5.2) with no analyzer-quality ablation; this is a hidden cold-start dependency the "self-evolving" branding understates.
- The shared actor/analyzer parameterization creates a self-reinforcement risk (shared blind spots, model self-preference) that the paper acknowledges but does not empirically quantify.
- Results are single-seed, no variance reported; scale is capped at 7B; OPSD baseline appears mis-tuned (near-zero on Search-QA), weakening the baseline comparison.
- SEED does not win everywhere (7B WebShop exact success below SDAR; ALFWorld Unseen Clean −2.9), and these non-wins are not discussed.
- Training cost is non-trivial: every update requires a full analyzer pass over `N = 8` trajectories *plus* paired re-scoring of all sampled tokens, which grows with interaction length and is worse for multimodal contexts.

## Relevance

对长期关注智能体强化学习与 LLM 自蒸馏的工作高度相关：SEED 直接解决了 outcome-based RL 在长程任务中奖励稀疏、token 级信用分配困难的核心痛点，其"同一策略既当 actor 又当事后 analyzer"的自演化思路，与当前 LLM agent RL / hindsight learning / 过程监督奖励等研究方向天然衔接，可作为后续设计自改进训练回路的重要参考基线。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How sensitive is final performance to the Stage-1 external analyzer's capability? Replacing GLM-5.2 with a weaker (e.g., same-size open) analyzer or with the policy itself from the start would isolate how much of SEED's gain comes from the cold-start teacher.
- Does the shared actor/analyzer parameterization cause observable skill-quality drift or self-preference collapse over 150+ updates? Tracking per-iteration skill novelty, analyzer agreement with an oracle, and gate-value statistics would quantify the self-reinforcement risk the authors flag but never measure.
- Can the framework scale to 30B+ policies where the SFT analyzer becomes weaker than the student, or to truly long-horizon benchmarks (DeepPlanning, Long-Horizon-Terminal-Bench, OdysseyArena) with rare success and dense partial-credit grading?
- Is the OPSD baseline's near-zero Search-QA performance a reproduction artifact? A corrected/tuned OPSD comparison would clarify whether SEED's margin over the distillation family is as large as Table 1 suggests.
- Would anchoring skills to *verifiable* state changes (rather than free-form natural language) mitigate the self-reinforcement failure mode and lift the ceiling under noisy self-generated supervision?
