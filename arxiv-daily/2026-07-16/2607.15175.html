Why This Matters

A concrete, cross-lingually transferable case of “linear direction = high-level semantic property,” and a fresh empirical angle on the long-running grammaticality-vs-probability debate — a baseline for causal, mechanistic syntax work.

Critical Edge

All probing is correlational — no causal intervention (activation patching / steering along θ) verifies the grammaticality direction functionally participates in the model's computation.

§ Critical Assessment · Limitations

01Methodology

The paper asks whether grammaticaticality is linearly encoded in sentence representations — using mass-mean probing (Marks & Tegmark, 2024): one direction, no learned weights beyond the class-mean difference.

θ = μ₁ − μ₀  ·  classify σ(θᵀx) > 0.5 as grammatical
fitted on 1,600 sentences · 100 random resamples · mean-centered per token
  • 25 models — GPT-2 (S→XL), Llama-3.x, OLMo/OLMo-2, Pythia 14M–12B, Qwen3 0.6B–14B; all pre-filtered above BLiMP chance.
  • Benchmarks — BLiMP (67), SCaMP (134), DGL, LI-Adger, RuBLiMP, ZhoBLiMP, CLAMS (de/fr/ru/he); plus Shades/Drive/Truth for deconfounding.
  • Regions — Critical (divergence), Pre-critical (control), Final (sentence-end punctuation).
  • Significance — Welch's t vs label-shuffled null, Holm-Bonferroni, ≥5% above null (calibrated from Pre-Critical false positives).
  • Exp.1Detect grammaticality; introduce MP_Rep vs probability-based MP_Prob.
  • Exp.2Localization (Critical vs Final vs Pre-critical) + cross-phenomenon generalization.
  • Exp.3Counter/Favour: decorrelate string probability from grammaticality.
  • Exp.4Cross-generalization + boundary-tilting vs acceptability factors.
  • Exp.5Within- & cross-language probing on 6 non-English benchmarks.

02Results & Highlights

  • 94.0% peak BLiMP success (Qwen3-14B); scaling R=0.928 vs log₁₀(params).
  • +6.89 pp MP_Rep reverses the gap over MP_Prob at the largest scales (R=0.791, p<.001).
  • Pre-critical ✗ All 25 models succeed in Critical & Final regions, not in the Pre-critical control.
  • −3.19 pp largest drop under Counter probability decorrelation (OLMo-2-7B) — most models stay at ceiling.
  • 55.9% gram→gram generalization vs 11.2% gram→extra — grammaticality ≠ general acceptability.
  • 61.4% cross-lingual mean vs 66.6% English — Llama transfers; OLMo-7B & Qwen3-4B collapse.

Boundary-tilting toward acceptability preserves grammaticality probing; the control tilt over grammatical sets destroys it — the signal is not reducible to acceptability.

03Strengths

  • +Directly attacks each published objection to probability-based grammaticality (Katzir 2023; Dentella 2023) with a targeted experiment.
  • +Mass-mean probe is parameter-light (one direction θ) — hard to dismiss as a probe-capacity artifact.
  • +Cross-phenomenon + cross-linguistic analyses turn a binary decode question into a richer characterization.
  • +Honest negatives: reports failed cross-lingual pairs and small-model vulnerability to deconfounding.

04Limitations

  • −Correlational only — no activation patching / steering to confirm the direction drives behavior.
  • −Heavy reliance on synthetic minimal-pair benchmarks whose categories may not match the model's own.
  • −“Independent of probability” bounded by Counter/Favour 20/80 quantiles; not stress-tested at extremes.
  • −Cross-lingual claim rests on 5 models / 6 benchmarks; within-language variation collapsed.
  • −No contamination analysis despite known BLiMP/SCaMP-in-pretraining risk (OLMo/Pythia corpora are public).

05Open Questions for Further Exploration

Q1 · CAUSALITYDoes the direction θ causally steer generation or probability judgments — does patching θ transfer grammaticality across sentences?
Q2 · STRUCTUREHow does θ relate to dependency/parse-state probes — do ungrammatical regions lack constituency signatures?
Q3 · ORIGINCan Pythia/OLMo training-data attribution explain why θ emerges — including metalinguistic documents?
Q4 · TYPOLOGYDoes the language-general encoding survive polysynthetic / free-word-order languages beyond the 6 tested?
Q5 · SEMANTICSIs the encoding robust under nonce-word / “jabberwocky” manipulations that strip lexical meaning?
Q6 · CIRCUITSWould combining mass-mean with contrastive activation addition recover a causally interpretable boundary aligned with θ?
