02Key metric
σ★ = 2−11
fixed, size-independent noise level
At this noise, the safe/unsafe label is preserved with probability ≥ 1 − e−m/8  ≈  0.9996 (for m ≥ 64). Closed-form optimum matched MILP to error 7.11 × 10⁻¹⁵.
03Why this matters

Rules out the hope that generic parameter nondegeneracy yields a universal smoothed-polynomial guarantee for exact verification — bounding the worst-case scalability of complete SMT/MILP/branch-and-bound verifiers.

Theoretical · asymptotic only
04Critical edge

Conditional on NP ⊄ BPP; covers only absolute-noise perturbation with an explicitly unoptimized σ★ = 2⁻¹¹. Worst-case construction — silent on typical trained networks and on modern verifiers at vision scale.

Not a benchmark · not unconditional

Methodology

quantitative robustification of gap hardness
  1. Source gap problem.
    Håstad's optimal E3SAT inapproximability (ε = 1/40): NP-hard promise of satisfiable vs. at-most-9/10-satisfiable; padded with 64 disjoint copies so m ≥ 64.
  2. Exact ReLU encoding — four ReLUs per clause.
    Literal unit ReLU(ℓ_λ − ½) (weight 2/3) + overflow unit ReLU(L_C − ⅓) (weight −1). Hidden fan-in ≤ 3, base coefficients in [−1,1]. A precision guard forces B ≥ 20 so 2⁻ᴮ ≤ σ★ is admissible.
  3. Exact integrality theorem.
    Coordinatewise threshold rounding never lowers the objective, giving the closed form max g_Φ(x) = (m − u★)/3; zero integrality gap. Threshold τ = 19m/60 yields a symmetric gap Γ = m/60.
  4. Weighted sensitivity certificate.
    Per-slot influence coefficients bound sup_x |g_θ'(x) − g_θ(x)| ≤ Σ c_q|θ'_q − θ_q| without fixing an activation pattern — valid even at preactivation zero. Accounting gives C ≤ 23m, D² ≤ 43m.
  5. Gaussian transfer.
    Rounding + clipping displaces by ≤ σF(Z) + hC/2; Gaussian concentration yields label-change failure ≤ exp(−Λ²/2D²). At σ★ = 2⁻¹¹, failure ≥ m/8 in the exponent.
  6. Bit-model BPP conversion.
    Polynomial-bit dyadic sampler + Markov truncation (100Q timeout) converts a hypothetical expected-time verifier into a BPP SAT algorithm with total error < 1/3, implying NP ⊆ BPP.
  7. Computational diagnostics.
    18 random formulas (72–188 ReLUs) cross-checked vs. MILP; 3-input blocks globally optimized over all C(38,3)=8,436 activation triples. Explicitly a diagnostic, not evidence.

Results & Highlights

conditional lower bound
T1Main theorem
Under NP ⊄ BPP, no sound-and-complete exact verifier has expected bit-running time polynomial in s, B, 1/σ★ at fixed noise, already for one-hidden-layer networks (fan-in ≤ 3, 4m+1 ReLUs).
T2Exact optimum identity
max g_Φ = (m − u★)/3 for every E3SAT formula. Closed form matched independent MILP on 18 formulas to error 7.11 × 10⁻¹⁵.
C6Exponential label preservation
Perturbation flips the exact answer with probability ≤ e⁻ᵐ⁄⁸; for m ≥ 64 preservation exceeds 0.9996.
✓3-input exhaustive cross-check
300 blocks × 10 noise levels; 44 optima cross-checked against a second MILP, discrepancy 7.99 × 10⁻¹⁵. At σ = 0.042, balanced preservation rose from 0.695 (1 block) to 0.991 (128 blocks).
✓Certificate scaling validates the mechanism
Extensive-gap family crosses 50% certificate probability at ~constant noise (log–log slope −0.0035); one-defect ablation falls with slope −1.014, matching the predicted q⁻¹ law.
!Scope, honestly stated
No quantitative claim about practical verifiers; the computational study is a diagnostic, not a benchmark of vision-scale verifiers, and not evidence that typical trained networks are hard after perturbation.

Strengths & Limitations

side by side

Strengths

  • Clean, conditional, fixed-noise smoothed lower bound with explicit constants — exactly the kind of statement that is hard to get right.
  • Reusable weighted-transfer theorem cleanly separates the source-gap reduction from noise-robustness accounting; also instantiates on a MAX-CUT route.
  • The exact integrality identity max = (m − u★)/3 is elegant and independently audited against MILP to 10⁻¹⁵ precision.
  • Intellectually honest about scope: computation is a diagnostic, never claimed as evidence.
  • Strong reproducibility package — full LaTeX source, fixed seeds, raw CSVs, proof_checks.py.

Limitations

  • Absolute-noise only; input box and threshold unperturbed. One specific perturb-and-round scheme.
  • Conditional on NP ⊄ BPP; no unconditional lower bound.
  • Conservative σ★ = 2⁻¹¹ relative to the [−1,1] coefficient range; explicitly not optimized.
  • Worst-case only — silent on typical trained networks or empirical runtime of modern verifiers.
  • Single-hidden-layer, fan-in ≤ 3 gadgets; explicit constants shown only on this family.
  • Computational study uses separable low-dimensional products — convenient for auditing, far from vision scale.
  • Single author, no peer review yet.

Questions for Further Exploration

open
  • Q1
    Can admissible fixed noise be pushed meaningfully above 2⁻¹¹ by tightening rational bounds (exact Gaussian mean gives exponent 0.2132·m vs. m/8), or by a gap source with a larger per-clause margin?
  • Q2
    Does the negative result survive a relative noise model (σ scaling with coefficient magnitude), or one that also perturbs the input box and threshold?
  • Q3
    Can a matching — or contradicting — positive smoothed result be shown for incomplete or relaxation-based verifiers, which the theorem does not cover?
  • Q4
    Is there an analogous smoothed lower bound for deeper networks, or does depth change the sensitivity accounting enough to alter the conclusion?
  • Q5
    Could the gadgets become an actual hard benchmark family for VNN-COMP to stress-test modern complete verifiers beyond the 3-input diagnostic blocks?
  • Q6
    How sensitive is the BPP-collapse step to the chosen timeout (100Q) and TV error (ε = 1/100) — is there slack to tighten the constants further?
