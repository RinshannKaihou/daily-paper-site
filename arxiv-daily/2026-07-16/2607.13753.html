01 Methodology ↓ results

Four variants all derived from Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, trained on the same DeepScaler + SimpleRL mixture with the same teacher (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B). This isolates the effect of the post-training objective rather than conflating it with data or model family.

Qwen-Instructreference
Unmodified backbone. Baseline for all stage comparisons.
Qwen-SFTfull-param
SFT on rejection-sampled correct teacher traces; cutoff 16,384; lr 1e-6; 2 epochs.
Qwen-RLGRPO
GRPO with binary verifiable rewards; length 16,384; KL 1e-4; entropy 1e-3.
Qwen-OPDon-policy distill
Student rollouts + teacher dense token-level KL (λ=1.0) + universal logit distillation.
  • ConfidenceTop-k=20 next-token log-probs; token conf c_l, relative position ρ_t=t/m∈[0,1].
  • Pre-CoTQuery-level C_pre; "solved" iff majority vote matches GT. Quality = AUROC & PRR.
  • Intra-CoTSliding-window group conf; stop when < θ. Online variant estimates length from 32 warmup traces (no future leakage), w_x=max(1,round(L̄/D)), D=10.
  • Post-CoTMean per-trace conf ranks traces; Top-η% keeps highest-conf η% before majority vote; "Most Confident" = single best.
  • PosConfIndicator-weighted score C_w(τ)=Σw(ρ)c_t/Σw(ρ); intervals (SFT/RL tail, OPD front) from AIME-24 trajectories.
  • Eval4 benchmarks · temp 0.6 · top-p 0.95 · max 32,768 · 320 offline / 256 agg traces · 5 seeds.

02 Results & highlights ↓ strengths

Each paradigm dominates a different stage: OPD → best Pre-CoT difficulty signal; SFT → best Intra-CoT early-stopping; RL → best Post-CoT aggregation. Reliability is also position-dependent — and OPD even flips sign late in reasoning.

Stage / decisionWinnerHeadline numbervs. baseline
Pre-CoT · difficulty routing OPD 0.644 avg AUROC (0.734 MATH500) RL is anti-informative: −0.478 avg PRR
Post-CoT · answer aggregation RL 62.8% Top-10% conf voting +5.4 pts vs 57.4% majority (AIME-25: 29.3→40.7)
Post-CoT · PosConf aggregation RL 63.5% PosConf-Top-5% +6.1 pts over majority (avg)
Intra-CoT · OPD early stopping OPD + PosConf +4.3 pts under tight budgets avoids OPD's late inverse-calibration region

RL aggregation wins — but only for RL

For Qwen-SFT, Top-5/10% filtering reduces accuracy (Majority 59.8% → Top-10% 54.4%); for Qwen-OPD every conf strategy underperforms its 58.5% majority baseline. Confidence filtering is paradigm-specific.

Position dynamics explain everything

SFT keeps a stable correct-vs-incorrect gap; RL is uninformative early, discriminative only after a path-commitment phase; OPD crosses over — incorrect traces become more confident than correct ones late in reasoning.

PosConf on RL early stopping

At 40% budget: AIME-24 22.11→26.10, MATH500 77.06→78.97. At 60% budget: AIME-25 20.61→24.26.

PosConf on OPD early stopping

AIME-style gains >3 pts (AIME-25: 23.46→26.72 at 30% budget = +3.26; +4.32 at 40%), by skipping the inverse-calibration tail.

03 Strengths vs. limitations ↓ questions

+ Strengths

  • Clean controlled design that genuinely isolates the post-training objective — same backbone, same data, same teacher.
  • Three-stage framework maps directly onto real inference-time decisions: routing, early stopping, aggregation.
  • OPD inverse-calibration and RL path-commitment findings are non-obvious and well-supported by trajectory plots.
  • PosConf is a cheap, model-specific, logit-only intervention with consistent low-budget gains.
  • Strong reproducibility — full Appendix B/C configs, Algorithm 1 pseudocode, public GitHub repo.

− Limitations

  • Narrow scope: only math reasoning, only Qwen2.5-7B, only one teacher. OPD inverse-calibration may be teacher/KL-specific.
  • Single internal signal (top-20 log-prob); entropy, semantic uncertainty, sample-consistency, hybrids all unexplored.
  • PosConf intervals tuned on AIME-24 and reused across benchmarks — no held-out interval-selection protocol.
  • No learned-verifier / PRM baseline for Post-CoT aggregation — the most direct competing technology.
  • Modest absolute gains (single-digit points), concentrated in tight-budget regimes.
  • Pre-CoT "solved" labels are model-specific, so cross-model AUROC comparisons carry a definitional caveat.

04 Questions for further exploration ↑ top

Do the stage/position patterns — especially OPD inverse-calibration and RL path-commitment — survive at 32B/72B and in Llama/Mistral/DeepSeek families, or are they Qwen-7B + this-teacher artifacts?

Does OPD's inverse-calibration generalize beyond KL-based on-policy distillation — e.g. sequence-level or hidden-state distillation? Is it the teacher's own confidence trajectory copied onto wrong rollouts?

How does PosConf interact with stronger aggregators (process reward models, self-consistency verifiers) — additive, or subsumed?

Can reliable intervals be selected online per-problem rather than tuned on AIME-24, removing the held-out-set concern?

On non-math reasoning (code, multi-hop QA, agentic tool use), which stage dominates — does "OPD→Pre, SFT→Intra, RL→Post" still hold?

How do entropy / semantic-uncertainty signals compare to top-k confidence at each stage, and can a hybrid beat PosConf?
