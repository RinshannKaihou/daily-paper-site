> **一句话总结：** 提出 HealthClaw —— 一种开源、自演化的健康智能体架构，通过"感知-推理-行动-归纳"闭环和五层受治理记忆（共享规则/知识 + 个性化档案/可复用流程/情景轨迹），在 1000 条模拟全年纪基准上把纵向支持准确率从 0.2% 提升到 45.7% 同时把上下文暴露降低 71.7%，并在 9 个 200 例生物医学任务上获得平均 27.0 pp 的指标增益。

> **TL;DR:** HealthClaw is an open-source self-evolving LLM agent for longitudinal personal health management that pairs a perception-reasoning-action-induction loop with a governed five-layer memory (shared rules/knowledge separated from per-user profile/SOP/episodic traces); on a 1,000-query synthetic 365-day benchmark, longitudinal answer accuracy rose from 0.2% (current-query prompting) to 45.7% with a 71.7% reduction in prompt context versus full-history prompting, and across nine 200-case biomedical tasks (MedMNIST, PAD-UFES-20, ODIR5K, PhysioNet, UCI diabetes, DeepLoc, GeneTuring, MLOmics) the mean task-specific primary-metric gain was 27.0 pp with seven gains surviving FDR correction.

## 核心贡献

- **首次将"选择性记忆演化"显式引入个人健康智能体**：提出"后情景归纳（post-episode induction）"机制 —— 每次对话结束后决定哪些信息进入档案事实（L2）、修订可复用流程（L3）、保留为情景轨迹（L4），或被排除，使一次交互中的稳定过敏、瞬时症状、失败建议、改进规则和敏感标识各自获得不同的持久化与披露策略。
- **五层受治理记忆架构（L0–L4）**：把共享的领域安全规则（L0）、医学知识索引（L1）与用户级私有状态（L2 个人档案 / L3 任务 SOP / L4 情景）严格分离，L0–L1 不被用户级情景更新；敏感详情可留在本地、最小化表示或排除写回，把"记忆写入"变成一个可审计的隐私控制点。
- **构建并发布合成全年纪基准 HealthClaw-YearLong**：20 名合成用户 × 365 天 × 50 探针 = 1,000 条配对探针（900 纵向支持 + 100 隐私探针），由隐藏事件台账生成真相、含 19,967 条记忆条目且无一致性错误，配套确定性隐私检查规则。
- **跨 9 类异构生物医学证据的统一路由评估**：同一框架覆盖影像、临床时间序列、结构化 EHR、蛋白质序列、基因组问答与多组学，平均 27.0 pp 的主指标增益、7 项通过 BH-FDR 校正，证明"智能体路由 + 证据整合"相对一次性基线的广泛增益。
- **诚实披露失败案例**：附加的 BEHSOF 脂肪肝筛查实验中自演化反而损害表现 —— 当基线模型已偏向 NAFLD，弱证据被反复写回记忆进一步放大先验，作者据此提出"需监测类别分布、校准工具证据、强化反方证据"的设计建议。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes "selective change" as the central design problem** for personal-health agents, distinct from pure recall: a single encounter may contain a stable allergy, a transient symptom, a failed recommendation, an improved meal-planning rule, an outdated measurement and a sensitive identifier, all of which need different persistence and disclosure rules rather than appending the full dialogue to the next prompt.
- **A closed perception-reasoning-action-induction loop** in which induction (after the episode) is the explicit mechanism by which completed encounters alter later support — the "self-evolution" step that distinguishes HealthClaw from encounter-level medical QA and from append-everything memory.
- **Five-layer governed memory (L0–L4)** separating shared governance (L0 behavioural rules / safety boundaries; L1 medical-knowledge index) from user-level state (L2 profile facts; L3 reusable task SOPs; L4 episodic traces); L0–L1 are never updated by user episodes, and L2 keeps sensitive identifiers local rather than in raw retrievable payloads.
- **HealthClaw-YearLong, a sanitized, publicly released 1,000-probe synthetic 365-day benchmark** (20 users, 7,300 daily turns, 19,967 memory entries, zero consistency errors) with a hidden event ledger as ground truth, paired with deterministic privacy checks (overdisclosure, unauthorized third-party disclosure, raw-identifier writeback).
- **A nine-task biomedical routing evaluation** spanning MedMNIST (NoduleMNIST3D, BreastMNIST), PAD-UFES-20, ODIR5K, PhysioNet ICU SOFA, UCI diabetes readmission, DeepLoc, GeneTuring and MLOmics, showing agentic evidence integration generalizes across heterogeneous evidence types.
- **An explicit, analyzed failure case (BEHSOF fatty-liver screening)** where memory reinforcement amplified a base-model prior toward NAFLD, motivating monitoring of class distributions, calibration of tool-derived evidence and a requirement for explicit counter-evidence before recurrent memory can strengthen an existing label.

## Methodology

HealthClaw models personal-health support as a sequence of episodes, each passing through four stages: **Perception** assembles the current request, available health signals (wearables, prior records, dialogue) and task-relevant retrieved memory; **Reasoning** uses shared medical knowledge, safety constraints and the user-specific profile/procedures to form a constrained task plan with sub-problem decomposition; **Action** generates the response or executes the plan through tool invocation with intermediate-result checking; and **Induction** runs *after* the episode to decide what each item should become in future — a profile fact (L2), a revised procedure (L3), an episodic trace (L4) or excluded.

Memory is organized in five layers: **L0** behavioural rules/safety/escalation conditions, **L1** shared health knowledge and tool-routing, **L2** durable personal profile (chronic conditions, allergies, medications, stable preferences, goals), **L3** reusable task SOPs (meal planning, indicator tracking, report interpretation, risk routing, privacy disclosure handling) and **L4** episodic traces (local context, prior outputs, feedback, tool traces). Writeback is governed by the expected future role of each item, and sensitive material can be kept local, minimized or excluded entirely; writeback is disabled during privacy probes.

Tools act as evidence-producing modules: the agent may receive structured evidence, calibration notes or class-bias warnings from tools, but the final response or prediction is always submitted by the agent itself. A task-tool router maps visible task information to a task family (fundus, skin, CT, ultrasound, structured EHR, protein, multi-omics, gene QA) and selects relevant tools. Tool inputs are restricted to information available for the current case — reference labels, reference answers, future cases and source-identifying paths are excluded and accessed only after submission for scoring.

The evaluation has two arms. (1) A **synthetic year-long benchmark** of 20 users with 365 daily turns each and 50 evaluation probes per user (900 longitudinal support + 100 privacy), generated from a hidden event ledger carrying day-level facts, target memory layers, stability annotations and supersession relationships. Three paired conditions are compared on the same query identifiers: current-only prompting, full-history prompting (complete visible pre-query dialogue) and HealthClaw (profile-initialized with governed retrieval). (2) A **nine-task biomedical evaluation** of nine 200-case tasks under a streaming protocol where case *d* may use same-task memory accumulated from cases < *d*. The HealthClaw backbone is qwen3.7-plus (text) and qwen3-vl-plus (vision) at temperature 0.0. Statistics use paired Wilcoxon signed-rank for continuous metrics, exact McNemar for binary metrics, 10,000–20,000 paired bootstrap resamples for CIs, and Benjamini–Hochberg FDR correction (seed 20260705). Responses are graded by a Qwen-3.7-based evaluator with fixed, task-specific rubrics; no human ratings were used.

## Results & Highlights

- **Longitudinal support (n = 900 non-privacy probes): rubric-defined answer accuracy rose from 0.2% (current-only prompting) to 45.7% (HealthClaw)**; the automated rubric score rose from 0.182 to 0.568 and reference-fact coverage from 0.027 to 0.524 — all three gains significant after BH-FDR correction.
- **Context efficiency: HealthClaw used 18,274 mean prompt characters vs. 64,493 for full-history prompting — a 71.7% reduction — while full-history prompting reached the strongest recall (answer accuracy 0.612, rubric 0.752, coverage 0.759)**, explicitly exposing the recall-vs-selective-use trade-off.
- **Privacy probes (n = 100): HealthClaw answer accuracy 0.640 and rubric score 0.696, beating current-only (0.440 / 0.582) and full-history (0.400 / 0.500)**, with the rubric-score gain significant over both baselines after FDR correction.
- **Constraint violations: 24% for HealthClaw vs. 41% (current-only) and 53% (full-history); unauthorized disclosure 5% vs. 18% and 15%; privacy-preserving alternatives offered 51% vs. 27% and 18%** — i.e., HealthClaw both disclosed less and offered safer alternatives more often.
- **Nine biomedical tasks: mean absolute gain in the task-specific primary metric of 27.0 percentage points, with seven of nine gains significant after BH-FDR correction.** Largest gains: NoduleMNIST3D CT +61.5 pp (0.215 → 0.830, ***), GeneTuring +52.5 pp exact-match (0.065 → 0.590, ***), PAD-UFES-20 +41.5 pp (0.390 → 0.805, ***), MLOmics +23.5 pp (0.265 → 0.500, ***), BreastMNIST ultrasound +19.5 pp (0.705 → 0.900, ***), DeepLoc +18.5 pp (0.510 → 0.695, ***), ODIR5K fundus +16.5 pp (0.170 → 0.335, ***).
- **Non-significant primary-metric gains: PhysioNet ICU SOFA +5.0 pp (0.470 → 0.520, ns) and diabetes readmission +4.5 pp (0.405 → 0.450, ns)**; macro-F1 was significant for six of eight classification tasks (NoduleMNIST3D +54.2 pp, PAD-UFES-20 +45.0 pp, DeepLoc +32.2 pp, BreastMNIST +23.7 pp, ODIR5K +17.7 pp, diabetes readmission +15.5 pp), but not for MLOmics (+2.6 pp) or PhysioNet SOFA (+8.1 pp), revealing uneven per-class improvement.
- **Exploratory failure case (BEHSOF fatty-liver screening):** adaptation did not improve performance because the base model already favoured NAFLD and weak/poorly-calibrated evidence retrieved from memory and tools further reinforced that prior, narrowing the prediction distribution — a concrete failure mode for self-evolving memory.

## Authors & Credibility

The work is led from Fudan University (School of Data Science; ISTBI; Huashan Hospital, Department of Neurology), with co-corresponding authors Jianfeng Feng and Xiahai Zhuang (senior computational neuroscience / medical-imaging expertise) and Hongcheng Guo as the project lead. Co-authors span Beijing University of Chinese Medicine, HUST, and JD.com (one declared competing interest — Q.S. is a JD.com employee). The paper is written in Nature-style format with prespecified stratified sampling (seed 20260629), BH-FDR correction, paired bootstrap CIs with a fixed seed (20260705), explicit leakage controls, an author-contributions section, and a publicly released code base at https://github.com/HC-Guo/HealthClaw plus a sanitized benchmark (HealthClaw-YearLong) to be released at publication. The framing is cautious: the authors repeatedly state the results support governed memory *for offline benchmarks* and that clinical effectiveness requires prospective evaluation.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The combination is genuinely useful but modular rather than conceptually new — its components (RAG, MemGPT-style tiered memory, Reflexion/ExpeL-style trajectory-driven adaptation, GraphRAG-style procedural memory) all cite prior work. The novel claim is the *health-specific* instantiation of selective change: explicit, auditable induction rules that assign each piece of an encounter a different future role and disclosure policy. Whether this is a research contribution or a careful engineering integration is debatable.
- **Rigor:** Statistics are unusually careful for an agent paper — paired-by-query-id design, Wilcoxon/McNemar with exact binomial tests on discordant pairs, BH-FDR correction across families, 10k–20k bootstrap CIs, fixed seeds, and explicit leakage controls. However, the headline nine-task comparison is **not a same-model ablation**: base and HealthClaw conditions use different model backbones and evaluation pipelines, and the paper itself flags this. The 0.2% → 45.7% longitudinal jump is therefore partly an artifact of the current-only baseline being a near-trivial comparator (no history at all) rather than evidence of memory quality per se.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong on paper — open-source repo, sanitized benchmark, prespecified seeds, per-task tool documentation in Supplementary Table 1, deterministic privacy checks. The same-source overlap caveat is critical, though: NoduleMNIST3D and PAD-UFES-20 tools are derived from MedMNIST/RG-DermNet on the *same benchmark data* (Supplementary Table 1 lists "Potential same-source or dataset overlap"), so several headline gains (+61.5 pp NoduleMNIST3D, +41.5 pp PAD-UFES-20) may be inflated by tool-side leakage rather than agent capability.
- **Significance:** Real for the self-evolving-agent subfield if the architecture is widely adopted; modest for clinical deployment given that all trajectories are synthetic, graded by a Qwen-3.7 evaluator with **no human ratings**, and that even the authors' own analysis flags a reinforcement-of-priors failure mode (BEHSOF) that any deployment would need to mitigate.
- **Red flags:** (1) Synthetic 365-day trajectories cannot reproduce the ambiguity, missingness and behavioural feedback of real longitudinal care — the authors say so but the benchmark is still the entire evidence base. (2) Auto-grading by a Qwen-3.7 evaluator on rubric-defined "answer accuracy" risks circularity (LLM judged by LLM). (3) The strongest biomedical gains coincide with the same-source-overlap tasks, which the paper acknowledges but does not fully defuse. (4) Two of nine tasks (SOFA, diabetes readmission) and MLOmics macro-F1 are non-significant, undermining the uniform-improvement narrative. (5) No human ratings, no prospective evaluation, no real-patient data — the paper correctly disclaims clinical effectiveness, but readers should not over-read the headline numbers.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated central problem (selective change vs. recall) with an architecture whose layers map directly onto that problem.
- Statistical care well above the agent-paper median: paired designs, FDR correction, bootstrap CIs, fixed seeds, leakage controls.
- Honest reporting: non-significant tasks, the BEHSOF failure case, and the same-source-overlap caveat are all disclosed rather than buried.
- Public release of both code (GitHub) and a sanitized benchmark (HealthClaw-YearLong) — the latter is a genuine community contribution for longitudinal-agent evaluation.
- Privacy is treated as a first-class, measurable property (overdisclosure, unauthorized disclosure, raw-identifier writeback) rather than a footnote.

## Limitations

- All evidence is **offline and synthetic**; no real patient data, no prospective use, no clinician or user judgement in the loop.
- Responses are **graded by a Qwen-3.7 evaluator with no human ratings**, introducing LLM-judge circularity and rubric-design bias.
- The nine-task comparison is **not a same-model ablation** and several tools share data with their benchmarks, inflating the headline biomedical gains.
- The longitudinal benchmark's current-only baseline (0.2% accuracy) is a near-strawman; the more meaningful comparator is full-history prompting, which actually wins on raw accuracy (0.612 vs. 0.457) at the cost of context length and privacy.
- Two tasks (PhysioNet SOFA, diabetes readmission) show no significant primary-metric gain; MLOmics and PhysioNet SOFA show no significant macro-F1 gain — improvement is broad but not uniform.
- Memory-governance mechanisms reduce *intra-agent* exposure but the authors acknowledge deployment-level privacy still depends on access control, security testing and data governance that are out of scope here.

## Relevance

直接命中"Self-Evolving Agents & Automated Research"主线：HealthClaw 的"后情景归纳 + 受治理五层记忆"是一个具体的自演化智能体架构，其 BEHSOF 失败案例（弱证据反复写回放大先验）对研究自演化系统的稳定性、记忆校准与失败模式分析具有直接参考价值；其 LLM-as-judge + 合成基准的评测范式也与"LLM 推理评测/可复现性"兴趣相关，但需注意统计增益部分被工具同源泄漏放大。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does HealthClaw's induction step behave under **contradictory evidence** (e.g., a corrected allergy vs. a superseded measurement) and under **distribution shift** between the synthetic benchmark and real longitudinal records?
- Can the same-source-overlap bias in the nine-task gains (NoduleMNIST3D, PAD-UFES-20) be isolated by replacing the MedMNIST/RG-DermNet-derived tools with genuinely independent classifiers, and do the +60/+40 pp gains survive that change?
- What is the **calibration and drift behaviour** of L2/L3 memory over a horizon much longer than 365 days, and does induction accumulate stale or self-reinforcing procedures (as suggested by the BEHSOF failure)?
- How sensitive are the headline numbers to the **Qwen-3.7 evaluator** and to the rubric design — would a human-graded subset or a different judge model change the 0.2% → 45.7% longitudinal claim?
- Could the explicit counter-evidence requirement proposed for the BEHSOF failure mode be **formalized** (e.g., a memory-write gate that requires a calibrated confidence interval and a minimum counter-prior) and evaluated against the reinforcement-of-priors failure mode directly?
