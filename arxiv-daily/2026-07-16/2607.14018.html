01 / METHODOLOGY

Spectral anatomy of an FFN block

Analysis is entirely at initialization, on feedforward blocks hℓ = hℓ−1 + β·f(hℓ−1) with f = Wdown·σ(Wup·Norm(h)), default ReLU, RMSNorm, β=1, weights N(0,α²/fan-in), d=256, depths to L=384.

Three rank objects are tracked across depth: (1) the input-output Jacobian ∂hL/∂h0 = ∏(I+βJℓ); (2) the forward representation matrix Zℓ; (3) the branch Jacobian Jf.

The primary metric is the effective rank — exponential of spectral entropy (Roy & Vetterli 2007), normalized by width d — scale-invariant, equal to 1 when all singular values match.

Key analytical tools

  • Path decomposition of the Jacobian into order-k paths, each weighted by (βαM)k — Veit's ensemble view, applied backward.
  • Branch-to-skip ratio rℓ = √p·βαM — the only channel through which α, β affect rank (Fig. 2).
  • Iso-rank contours → empirical scaling law βαM√L (measured slope −0.50).
  • Detached-rescaling ablation — forward unchanged, Jacobian scalar — isolating rescaling vs. projection.
  • Marchenko–Pastur analysis of the masked up-projection's Gram matrix.
  • CIFAR-10 trainability grid — one net per (α, L, LR) cell; author flags it as a coarse “does it train” signal.

03 / ASSESSMENT

Strengths vs. limitations

+ Strengths

  • One unifying quantity — βαM evolving as βαM√L — explains skip, norm placement, branch scale & init scale simultaneously. Rare conceptual economy.
  • Clean experimental design — the detached-rescaling ablation definitively separates projection from rescaling, refuting a natural competing explanation.
  • Precise quantitative hits — cross-layer correlation = cℓ (0.320 vs 0.318), stable-rank bound tight within 6%, MP spectra matching exactly for ReLU/abs.
  • Connects spectra to a measurable outcome — CIFAR trainability grid — rather than stopping at pretty spectra.
  • Honest about scope — explicit that CIFAR is coarse, attention & training dynamics are future work. No red flags.

− Limitations

  • CIFAR-10 only — an easy, saturating task. No LM training, no reasoning task, no regime where depth genuinely matters.
  • Init-only — the dynamic story (does the mean spike reform once training couples weights?) is untested.
  • Activation gap — MP threshold exact only for ReLU/abs; gated activations (SwiGLU) unaddressed despite being the modern default.
  • No attention — covers only the feature dimension; the token-dimension rank collapse from attention is out of scope.
  • β=1 unexplained — argues it's deliberate composition-vs-ensemble, but no direct evidence it's optimal vs. historical inertia.
  • Curve shape measured, not derived — the random-walk heuristic explains √L but not the Pre-Norm plateau value.
04 / OPEN

Questions for further exploration

Q.01
Does the init effective rank predict trainability on a genuinely depth-sensitive task (multi-step reasoning, long-context LM) where CIFAR-10 saturates — and what is the quantitative failure threshold?
Q.02
How do rank dynamics evolve during training — does Pre-Norm's 1/√ℓ decay persist, and does the forward mean spike reform once weights couple?
Q.03
Can the MP threshold m/d = 1/p(σ) extend to gated activations (SwiGLU, GeGLU), and what does it predict about frontier FFN expansion ratios?
Q.04
When attention is added, does its token-dimension rank collapse interact with the feature-dimension rank studied here — can the framework cover the full block?
Q.05
Is β=1 really optimal? Can matched β=1 vs β=1/√L training show whether the β=1 net learns deeper compositional structure the ensemble-regime net cannot?
Q.06
Is the Pre-Norm plateau value (~0.2–0.3 of width) derivable from the path-decomposition random walk, or does it require a fuller spectral theory of the ∏(I+βJℓ) product?
