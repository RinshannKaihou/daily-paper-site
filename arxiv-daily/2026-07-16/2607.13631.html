「 How the Hessian-Spectrum of Neural Networks Depends on Data 」

For MSE classification, solution sharpness is set by the max class proportion α_max — directly contradicting two claims of Edge-of-Stability.

Jasraj Singh · Enea Monzio Compagnoni · Antonio Orvieto · ELLIS Institute Tübingen · MPI-IS · Univ. Basel
loss-landscape hessian-spectrum sharpness edge-of-stability deep-learning-theory GGN / NTK
M Key metric
α_max = 0.2
Held fixed while sweeping depth & input scale.
Verified on n = 5,000-sample subsets of MNIST / FashionMNIST / CIFAR10; full-batch GD for 10,000 epochs; eigenvalues via LOBPCG.
! Why this matters
Reframes edge-of-stability & sharpness-aware optimizers through label geometry — a class histogram becomes a sharpness predictor.
Directly challenges Cohen et al. (2021) on sharpness-vs-n and "simpler-data-is-flatter"; corrects a top-eigenvalue formula from Singh et al. (2026).
▲ Critical edge
The theory "breaks down immediately" on raw non-whitened inputs (Fig. 7a) — i.e. the practical regime.
So the headline α_max → sharpness link holds mainly after whitening; evidence is figure-based & qualitative, with no cross-entropy or ReLU validation.
01

Methodology — four steps from Hessian to data geometry

GGN / NTK approximation
01 · SETUP

GGN / NTK reduction

Factor the L-layer linear Jacobian into per-layer Kronecker blocks, converting a p-dim parameter Hessian into a small d_L·n NTK.

∂²L ≈ (1/n) JᵀJ
02 · THM 1

Shallow closed form

For L=2 with isotropic features, non-zero eigenvalues are exact pairwise sums of squared weight singular values.

λ ∝ (σᵢ²(W₁)+σⱼ²(W₂))
03 · THM 2

Depth, under balance

Under strong-balance (layers share singular values), eigenvalues admit a rational bound; only min{d₀,…,d_L} of p eigenvalues can be non-zero.

λ_max ≤ L·(‖X‖²/n)·‖W‖²⁽ᴸ⁻¹⁾
04 · THM 3

OLS → label geometry

At the OLS solution, sharpness is independent of n; for one-hot labels, σᵢ²(Y)/n equals the class proportions — α_max drives sharpness.

λ_max = L·(‖X‖²/n)^(1/L)·(‖U_XᵀY‖²/n)^(1−1/L)

Two direct contradictions of Edge-of-Stability (Cohen et al., 2021)

λ_max stays flat as n grows (refuting their Fig. 18 "sharpness grows with n"); and "simpler" more imbalanced datasets yield higher, not lower, peak sharpness (refuting Caveat 2). Depth scaling: sharpness increases with L once ‖X‖² ≤ ‖Y‖² or L ≥ 2·ln(‖Y‖²/‖X‖²).

03

Assessment — what holds up, what doesn't

workshop · 4 pp + appendix

+ Strengths

  • Clean closed-form / bound eigenvalue expressions reducing a large parameter Hessian to a small NTK, tied to SVDs of X and Y.
  • Memorable actionable takeaway: peak sharpness = max class proportion α_max, an easily computed data statistic.
  • Intellectually honest about failure modes — Fig. 7 ablations show where the theory breaks.
  • Corrects a concrete prior error (Singh 2026) and challenges a highly cited EoS paper.
  • Full proofs (App. A–B) and a reproducible-feeling protocol (App. C).

− Limitations

  • Restricted to linear networks and MSE loss; Tanh ablation only "qualitatively similar" — ReLU & cross-entropy unproven.
  • Requires whitened features (Asm. 1) & strong layer balance (Asm. 2); fails on raw real-world inputs.
  • Describes local geometry at balanced/OLS solutions, not full SGD training trajectories.
  • Evidence is figure-based & qualitative — no tabulated errors, CIs, or accuracy metrics.
  • EoS contradictions are conceptual, not quantitatively reconciled; no code or data release cited.
04

Open questions for further exploration

non-linear reading
Q1

Does the α_max → sharpness link survive cross-entropy loss and ReLU / Transformer architectures, where the one-hot SVD reading no longer applies?

Q2

Can the theory extend to non-whitened, anisotropic features — the regime where Fig. 7a shows it currently fails?

Q3

How do predictions interact with mini-batch SGD rather than full-batch GD — the actual regime where EoS is observed?

Q4

Is a quantitative reconciliation with Cohen et al. Fig. 18 / Caveat 2 possible by separating sharpness at the minimizer from sharpness on the trajectory?

Q5

Can α_max be used diagnostically — to set learning rates or sharpness-aware regularization from measured class proportions?

Q6

What happens to the spectrum under weight decay or Adam, which alter the balance / parameter-norm dynamics the theory builds on?
