> **一句话总结：** 本文针对深度残差网络提出"次线性增长原理"——将每个残差块的输入幅值增长指数 q 限制在 q ≤ 1（即 ∥v(x,t)∥ ≤ c∥x∥ᵠ + b），并通过经典 ODE 非爆破理论和 Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman 最优控制论证证明 q=1 是训练稳定性的尖锐阈值；作者据此构造了无归一化层的 Mamba 线性增长变体（q 从 5 降到 1），在 ETTm1/Weather 上实现 0/288 次爆破，而原始 q=5 自由速度 Mamba 在 L=8 时 ETTm1 上 24/24 全部发散。

> **TL;DR:** The authors propose the **sublinear-growth principle** for residual architectures: every block's velocity field must satisfy ∥v(x,t)∥ ≤ c∥x∥ᵠ + b with **q ∈ [0,1]**, and they prove q = 1 is a *sharp* stability threshold via two independent arguments (classical ODE non-explosion + Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman optimal control showing the bang-bang training optimum saturates the boundary). An arithmetic of input-magnitude exponents under five primitive operations lets designers *certify* q ≤ 1 from primitives (e.g., LayerNorm/RMSNorm collapse to q = 0; native Mamba is supercritical q = 5). On ETTm1 and Weather time-series forecasting, the proposed **parameter-free, normalization-free linear-growth Mamba modification** (q reduced 5→1) trains with **0 blow-ups across 288 runs**, while the native q = 5 Mamba without normalization diverges in **24/24 runs on ETTm1 at L=8** — dissociating stability from the mere *presence* of a normalization layer.

## 核心贡献

- **提出次线性增长原理（sublinear-growth principle）**：给出残差块速度场的统一设计准则 ∥v(x,t)∥ ≤ c∥x∥ᵠ + b，q ∈ [0,1]，并把 q = 1 识别为**尖锐的稳定性阈值**——不是经验观察，而是数学上的必要充分条件。
- **两条独立证明路径**：(1) 经典 ODE 理论给出 q ≤ 1 时全局解 + Grönwall 界、q > 1 时有限时间爆破；(2) HJB 最优控制证明训练最优是 **bang-bang**（饱和 admissible class 的边界），因此优化无法绕开危险速度场，q > 1 的最优解本身就会爆破。
- **导出离散架构的前向状态界与反向 Jacobian/协态界**（Theorems 3.5、3.7）：q=0 时协态守恒（不随深度放大），q=1 时几何放大率为 c，q∈(0,1) 时为多项式增长——在 OC 最优点比 off-optimum 的 (1+L∆t)^D 界更紧。
- **给出"输入幅值指数算术"**（Theorem 4.2 + Table 1）：在 5 种基本操作（串行复合、并行求和、Hadamard 耦合、残差包裹、归一化包裹）下指数的传播规则，使 q ≤ 1 的认证可降到"原语级查表"，免去试错式的架构搜索。
- **将超临界的 Mamba 块从 q=5 修正到 q=1**：通过两条局部改造（选择性参数路径上对 x 做归一化、SiLU 门控改为 sigmoid 门控），**完全不需要归一化层、不引入可学习参数**，演示了算术工具的实用价值。

## Key Contributions

- **The sublinear-growth principle as a single over-arching design criterion.** A uniform bound ∥v(x,t)∥ ≤ c∥x∥ᵠ + b with q ∈ [0,1] on every residual block, with q = 1 identified as a **sharp** stability threshold rather than an empirical heuristic.
- **Two independent derivations of the threshold.** Classical ODE theory (global existence at q ≤ 1 via Grönwall/Peano; finite-time blow-up of ṙ = crᵠ at q > 1 with explicit blow-up time ∥X₀∥¹⁻ᵠ/(c(q−1))) and an optimal-control argument (Theorem 3.1: the bang-bang optimum saturates the boundary of U_ad, so the q > 1 optimum cannot escape blow-up).
- **Quantitative depth-explicit stability bounds for the discrete residual stack.** Forward state bounds (Theorem 3.5: linear / polynomial / geometric growth in D for q = 0 / q ∈ (0,1) / q = 1) and backward costate bounds (Theorem 3.7: conserved costate at q = 0, geometric at q = 1) with constants uniform in admissible parameters.
- **An arithmetic of input-magnitude exponents (Theorem 4.2) and a primitive catalogue (Table 1).** Closure of the primitive set under five operations — composition multiplies exponents (q₁q₂), sum takes max, Hadamard product adds them (q₁+q₂), residual wrapping takes max(1,q), and normalization wrapping forces q = 0 — turns block-level certification into table lookup.
- **A constructive demonstration: a parameter-free, normalization-free Mamba modification** that reduces the block exponent from q = 5 to q = 1 by localizing changes to the selective-parameter branch (L2-normalize x̃ = x/∥x∥ on the parameter path; replace SiLU gating x·σ(x) with sigmoid gating σ(x)).

## Methodology

The paper is theoretical-first with a focused empirical validation. The methodology has four layers.

**1. ODE view of residual architectures (Section 2).** A residual stack X_{k+1} = X_k + ∆t·v(X_k; θ_k) is treated as the forward-Euler discretization of the continuous ODE dX/dt = v(X,t). The classical sublinear/linear growth condition (Definition 2.1) ∥v(x,t)∥ ≤ c∥x∥ᵠ + b is recalled; two classical facts identify q = 1 as the non-explosion threshold — Grönwall bound ∥X(t)∥ ≤ e^{ct}∥X₀∥ + (b/c)(e^{ct}−1) at q = 1, finite-time blow-up of ṙ = crᵠ at q > 1.

**2. Optimal-control view (Section 3).** Training is cast as an OC problem over an admissible velocity class U_ad^{q}(X) := {v : ∥v∥ ≤ c∥X∥ᵠ + b}. The Hamiltonian H_q(X,P) = (c∥X∥ᵠ + b)∥P∥ is maximized pointwise, yielding a closed-form bang-bang optimum (Theorem 3.1) that saturates the boundary ∥v*∥ = c∥X∥ᵠ + b. Because the optimum lives on the boundary, at q > 1 it must select one of the blow-up-prone fields, making the threshold necessary; at q ≤ 1 every boundary velocity is safe, making it sufficient. HJB well-posedness is established via Crandall–Lions comparison (continuous, Proposition 3.3) and monotonicity + constant-shift invariance of the Bellman operator (discrete, Proposition 3.4). Forward and backward bounds on the *discrete* architecture (Theorems 3.5 and 3.7) follow via discrete Grönwall and Danskin's envelope identity, yielding depth-explicit growth rates that depend only on q, c, b — not on realization-level Lipschitz constants at the OC optimum.

**3. Architecture certification (Section 4).** A primitive set 𝒫 is defined (Definition 4.1); each primitive gets an input-magnitude exponent (Table 1: LayerNorm/RMSNorm/softmax/saturating activations → q = 0; linear layers, ReLU/GELU/SiLU, self-attention, MLP → q = 1; polynomial activation x^p → q = p; native Mamba selective SSM → q = 5). Five primitive operations propagate exponents (Theorem 4.2). A four-step per-block certification procedure + a between-block mixed-exponent procedure (Remark 4.4) let a designer certify max_k q_k ≤ 1 without trial-and-error. The analysis applies only to additive residual updates X_{k+1} = X_k + ∆t f(X_k); Post-LN explicitly lies outside the scope.

**4. Experiments (Section 5).** Time-series forecasting on ETTm1 (6 seeds/cell) and Weather (3 seeds/cell), input window 96, horizons {96,192,336,720}, depths L ∈ {3,8}, small-model regime (≤ 2M parameters). Two backbones are contrasted: **Mamba** (native selective SSM, q = 5 when unnormalized) and **PatchTST** (attention-only, q = 1 by construction). Five Mamba variants span q ∈ {0,1,5}: RMSNorm baseline / Pre-LN / Peri-LN (all q = 0), a structural **linear-growth modification** that reduces the block to q = 1 with no normalization and no extra parameters, and a **free-velocity control** at native q = 5. PatchTST runs four normalization variants (the linear-growth mod is redundant there). Two metrics are reported: blow-up counts (NaN/±∞ or MSE many orders above target range) and forecasting MSE as a signed percentage deviation from the per-cell variant median.

## Results & Highlights

- **Blow-up rate on Mamba tracks q exactly.** The four q ≤ 1 Mamba variants (RMSNorm baseline, Pre-LN, Peri-LN, linear-growth modification) have **0 blow-ups across all 288 seed-level runs in their 64 configuration cells**. The free-velocity Mamba at native **q = 5 diverges in 48 of 72 runs**, with the failure rate **rising sharply with depth**: 12/24 on ETTm1 and 2/12 on Weather at L = 3, versus **24/24 on ETTm1 and 10/12 on Weather at L = 8** (Table 2). Two ETTm1 free-velocity runs at L = 3, horizon 192, terminated with finite MSE exceeding 10¹² — ten orders of magnitude above the standardized target scale.
- **PatchTST stays stable even without normalization, because its primitives are q = 1 by composition.** All four PatchTST variants (including free velocity at q = 1) are **completely stable across all 288 runs in their 64 cells** (Table 2) — confirming that the input-magnitude exponent, not the *presence of a normalization layer*, governs stability.
- **Forecasting accuracy is unaffected by *how* q ≤ 1 is achieved.** For Mamba, the four q ≤ 1 variants cluster within **≤ 1.0% of the per-cell median on Weather and ≤ 1.5% on ETTm1**, with no variant dominating; the linear-growth modification is **neither systematically better nor worse than the two normalized variants** at a margin exceeding within-cell sampling variability. The free-velocity Mamba, where any seed survives, is uniformly above the median — by as much as **4.6% MSE on the smallest Weather configuration**.
- **PatchTST variants are nearly indistinguishable in accuracy.** Spread across all four PatchTST variants is **≤ 0.05% on Weather** (effectively identical at plot resolution) and **≤ 0.8% on ETTm1**, with the free-velocity variant matching both normalized variants in stability and accuracy.
- **The linear-growth Mamba modification reduces block q from 5 → 1 with zero extra parameters and zero normalization layers**, localizing changes to the parameter-computation branch: L2-normalize the input feeding the selective-parameter projections (caps the parameter path at q = 0) and replace SiLU gating x·σ(x) with sigmoid gating σ(x) (removes a q = 2 contribution).
- **Practical depth ceiling even at q = 1 (Remark 3.6).** The exponential factor (1+c∆t)^D with ∆t = 1, c = 1 hits 2^D, exceeding **FP16 max (≈6.5×10⁴) at D = 17** and the **FP32/BF16 dynamic range (≈3.4×10³⁸) at D = 128** — the input-magnitude bound imposes a hardware-imposed depth limit even below the blow-up threshold.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: genuinely high on the unification axis.** Most prior normalization-free works (FixUp, ReZero, NFNets, nGPT, AERO, Dynamic Tanh, BHyT) reach stability by *trial-and-observation* per architecture. The contribution here is a single, parameter-free criterion (q ≤ 1) that spans a wide class of primitives and reduces per-block certification to table lookup. The arithmetic of exponents (Theorem 4.2) is the paper's most original and operational artifact.
- **Rigor: strong for the threshold statement, weaker at the corners.** The two-pronged ODE + HJB derivation of q = 1 is mathematically clean and sharp (necessary via bang-bang + blow-up; sufficient via Grönwall + Crandall–Lions). However, several seams are visible: (a) Proposition 3.3 for q ∈ (0,1) requires *extra hypotheses* (b > 0, globally Lipschitz G) that are not free — squared loss satisfies *neither* globally and the paper relies on a localized-extension argument (Remark 3.8) that is asserted rather than fully checked; (b) the costate P(t) is defined only a.e. via Rademacher differentiability of the viscosity solution, and all bounds are advertised as if pointwise.
- **Reproducibility: mixed.** The experimental setup is small (≤2M parameters, depths 3 and 8, public benchmarks ETTm1/Weather) and the Mamba linear-growth modification is described concretely (Figure 2 + Section 5.1 give the two localized edits). But no code release is mentioned, hyperparameters (optimizer, learning rate, schedule, training budget, exact seed list beyond "2024, 2025") are not tabulated, and accuracy is reported as **signed percentage deviation from the per-cell variant median rather than absolute MSE** — this deliberately obscures absolute performance and makes it impossible to compare against published Mamba/PatchTST numbers.
- **Significance: real but narrow.** The validation is restricted to **shallow (L ≤ 8) small-model TSF**, exactly the regime where q ≤ 1 stability is most likely to be already achievable by conventional means; the paper does not test whether the linear-growth Mamba scales to LLM-scale depths (where Remark 3.6's depth ceiling bites) or to language/vision benchmarks. The empirical dissociation of "stability" from "normalization presence" is clean but unspectacular in absolute terms — PatchTST without normalization was already expected to be stable in the small-model regime.
- **Red flags / specific gaps.**
  1. The **median-relative MSE reporting** is unusual and conveniently hides whether any variant actually achieves competitive absolute MSE; a reviewer should demand absolute MSE per cell vs. published baselines.
  2. The **scope disclaimer is narrow** — Post-LN (X_{k+1} = LN(X_k + ∆t f(X_k))) is *explicitly excluded* (Remark 4.4), and Post-LN is a dominant configuration in production transformers; the principle therefore does not yet cover some of the most widely deployed architectures.
  3. The paper claims the threshold is "necessary and sufficient for stable training," but the theorems establish non-explosion/well-posedness — *not* convergence to low loss; a stable but useless optimum is consistent with all results here.
  4. The **Hurwitz assumption on the Mamba state matrix A** is needed to keep the discrete transition Ā within 𝒫; without it the block falls outside the primitive set entirely, which somewhat undercuts the "parameter-free certification" pitch for architectures where that assumption is not checkable.
  5. No ablation on the linear-growth modification (e.g., sigmoid-only vs. L2-norm-only) and no comparison to other normalization-free baselines (FixUp, ReZero, Dynamic Tanh) on the same TSF pipeline.

## Strengths

- A genuinely **unifying, parameter-free, architecture-agnostic criterion** (q ≤ 1) backed by two independent mathematical arguments of different character (ODE existence vs. HJB optimum selection) — the combination is more convincing than either alone.
- The **arithmetic of input-magnitude exponents (Theorem 4.2) and Table 1** convert the theory into a *practical design tool*: certification reduces to inspecting primitives, which is concretely demonstrated by the parameter-free Mamba modification.
- The **dissociation experiment is well designed** — using Mamba (q = 5 unnormalized) and PatchTST (q = 1 unnormalized) as two sides of the same controlled manipulation cleanly isolates the exponent from the normalization layer.
- Honest about **scope and conservativeness** (Remark 3.6 depth ceiling; certification procedure gives upper bounds; Post-LN excluded; strict-interior q ∈ (0,1) is flagged as an open design space in Remark 4.5).
- Clean, theorem-structured exposition with proofs (not just proof sketches) for the central results.

## Limitations

- **Validation is shallow and narrow**: depths L ∈ {3,8}, ≤2M parameters, two TSF benchmarks only. No language modeling, no vision, no LLM-scale depths where the practical depth ceiling (Remark 3.6) becomes the binding constraint.
- **Absolute MSE is not reported** — only median-relative percentage deviations, which prevents comparison to the published Mamba/PatchTST literature and weakens the accuracy claim.
- **Post-LN is excluded** from the analysis, leaving a major deployed configuration uncovered.
- The **q ∈ (0,1) regime** — where forward bounds are polynomial rather than exponential — is identified as more favorable but **no standard primitive realizes it**; the sharper bound is therefore currently unused.
- The OC optimum is characterized as bang-bang, but real training (SGD/Adam with finite learning rate, dropout, finite data) **does not actually reach the OC optimum**; the paper's stability argument is about the *admissible class*, not about trajectories traversed by a real optimizer, leaving a gap between theory and practice.
- The Hurwitz assumption on Mamba's state matrix is needed to keep the discretized transition inside 𝒫; its empirical validity during training is not verified.
- No code or hyperparameter table released; reproducibility of the 288-run protocol cannot be checked from the paper alone.

## Relevance

对关注残差网络/Transformer 训练稳定性、归一化层作用机理、以及用控制论与 ODE/HJB 视角理解深度学习的研究方向高度相关——它为"为什么 LayerNorm/RMSNorm 能稳定训练"提供了一个可证明的统一解释（把块的输入幅值指数压到 q=0），并给出了一套可用于架构搜索的 q ≤ 1 认证工具，对从事 Mamba/SSM、Transformer 架构设计和 time-series forecasting 的读者有直接的方法论价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the linear-growth Mamba modification (q = 1, no normalization) **scale** to language-modeling benchmarks (e.g., wiki-text, common-crawl) at depths ≥ 64 and parameter counts ≥ 100M, or does Remark 3.6's exponential depth ceiling force normalization back in?
- Can a **strict-interior q ∈ (0,1) primitive** be constructed (Remark 4.5) that inherits the polynomial-in-D forward bound without sacrificing expressivity — e.g., via a smooth normalized nonlinearity that interpolates between RMSNorm (q = 0) and identity (q = 1)?
- How does the criterion interact with **Post-LN** (explicitly excluded here)? Extending Theorem 4.2 to the post-normalization update X_{k+1} = LN(X_k + ∆t f(X_k)) would cover a major gap.
- Is the **bang-bang OC optimum** ever approached by Adam/SGD trajectories in practice, or does finite learning rate + regularization keep realized velocities in the strict interior of U_ad, making the "necessary" half of the threshold a worst-case statement rather than an empirical risk?
- Can the exponent arithmetic be **extended to discontinuous primitives** (vector quantization, routing in MoE) via their straight-through / Gumbel-softmax surrogates in a principled way?
- On the accuracy side: does the **median-relative MSE reporting** hide a real regression? What are the absolute MSE numbers per cell, and how do they compare to published Mamba/PatchTST baselines on ETTm1/Weather?
