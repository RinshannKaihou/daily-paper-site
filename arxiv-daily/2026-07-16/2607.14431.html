Methodology § how it works

Taliesin graft. A previously captured KV state S is reinstalled into a fresh context at its own positions [0, N), with new queries computed fresh on top at [N, N+q). Byte-exactness is defined as SHA-256(logits_graft) = SHA-256(logits_fresh), implying KL = 0 and 100% argmax agreement. Determinism is pinned via GGML_DETERMINISTIC=1 + CUBLAS_WORKSPACE_CONFIG=:4096:8.

Why only own-position. A graft-free control shows relocation to a different offset leaves a ~0.015 KL residual — traced by elimination to 32-bit non-associativity of the RoPE identity cos a·cos b + sin a·sin b = cos(a−b). Own-position is the unique numerically exact regime on this model.

Galahad flywheel. Solve → externally verify (execute generated program; match ground truth) → deposit the verified KV block. Retrieval is split and never conflated: recurrence (same problem → exact lookup) vs. transfer (new problem, same structure → one-shot routing to a single block). An earlier flat-merge design was abandoned because the model grabbed the wrong entry.

Strengths

+ what holds up
  • Intellectual honesty. Withdraws the byte-exact positional-composition framing after measuring it; reports a pre-registered B200 magnitude-miss (predicted 1.4e-2, measured 7e-4, 19x smaller).
  • Adversarial self-test. §4.2 graft-free control shows the relocation residual is the base model's own position-sensitivity, not a graft error.
  • Well-instrumented costs. 85.6x prefill subsidy, 6,574x token cut, 87.1x context widening at zero extra VRAM — with sampler-resolution and page-cache caveats recorded.
  • Measured, not asserted. Router misrouting and disk paging answered with data; off-distribution abstention is a genuinely useful property.

Limitations

! what doesn't
  • Closed engine. Taliesin + Merlin dedup are proprietary; generation cannot be reproduced, only scored.
  • Within-architecture only. Cross-architecture byte equality is not guaranteed; Hopper validated functionally, not byte-level.
  • Framed comparison. AIME 2026 vendor anchors vs. AIME 2025 self-eval — not same-benchmark head-to-head.
  • Small transfer n. Generalization is 5/7 at 12B; failures involve baked-in constants (sqrt(185), range(4)).
  • No confidence gate. A confident-wrong graft is structurally possible; none occurred at n=15.
  • Saturation / regression. Flywheel is null where the model already succeeds (216/216); a cached method can reduce accuracy by 55 points.

Relevance § so what for serving

Directly relevant to inference serving and KV-cache optimization. If byte-exact, persistent, cross-machine-portable KV grafting holds, it could serve as a low-cost alternative to RAG, long-context, and agent-skill reuse — and offers methodological value for inference-time learning and controllable knowledge injection. Caveat: it depends on a closed engine, and the core model + anchors are not independently verifiable today.

Open questions § for further work

  • Can byte-exactness be reproduced on an open engine (vLLM, llama.cpp, SGLang) under the same pinned deterministic config, or does it depend on undocumented Merlin runtime properties?
  • What happens on a same-benchmark head-to-head (AIME 2025 anchors vs. AIME 2025 eval) instead of cross-year model-card anchors?
  • Does transfer scale beyond n=7 — does the parametric-vs-baked-in-constant boundary remain the dominant failure mode at n=50/100?
  • Could a lightweight confidence gate between graft and answer eliminate confident-wrong grafts without losing off-distribution abstention?
  • Is the 32-bit non-associativity ceiling (~0.015 KL residual) truly irreducible, or could bf16→fp32 mid-precision kernels / a different RoPE recover exactness at non-own positions?
  • How does the mechanism interact with quantized serving (INT4/INT8 KV) — does quantization break byte-exactness, and what is the quantized operating point?
  • Does "cache only what the model lacks" generalize beyond AIME/LiveBench to open-domain tasks where distinguishing lacks from knows is itself hard?
