Strengths & Limitations

Side-by-side assessment

+ Strengths

  • Broadest head-to-head of weight-only PTQ families in code-LLM literature, with a clean one-per-category taxonomy.
  • Quality evaluation beyond pass@1 via a single language-agnostic tool — enables direct Python/Java comparison.
  • Methodologically careful: paired tests, multiple-comparison correction, effect sizes, run-stability check, confound replication.
  • Prompt-complexity robustness analysis is genuinely new and yields a crisp, actionable model-dependent finding.
  • Honest framing of noise-driven “improvements”; public replication package.

− Limitations

  • Single model size (7B), single bit-width (4-bit), two languages — generalization out of scope.
  • RQ3 is Python-only; High-entropy bucket is BigCodeBench-dominated (mitigated, not eliminated).
  • Only weight-only PTQ studied; weight-and-activation methods (ZeroQuant, SmoothQuant, LLM.int8()…) excluded.
  • Small per-benchmark task counts make some degradation percentages noisy; QuIP# only at 4-bit may undersell it.
  • SonarCloud as sole quality tool; uniformly-zero Security hotspots suggest security may be under-tested.
  • No efficiency/cost numbers in main tables despite ≈70% memory savings being a primary motivator.

Open Questions for Further Exploration

Where this work points next
  1. Do the AQLM-best / QuIP#-worst rankings and the CodeLlama-vs-Qwen robustness gap persist at 2–3 bit precision (QuIP#'s intended regime) and at larger scales (e.g., 32B Qwen2.5-Coder)?
  2. Would code-specific calibration data (vs WikiText-2) close the AWQ-Java maintainability gap and the CoderEval-Python Qwen quality regression?
  3. Why do some quantized variants exceed FP pass@1 (Qwen McEval-Python GGUF/AQLM 0.43 vs FP 0.31) — genuine, a decoding artifact, or weak test suites?
  4. How do these six weight-only techniques interact with inference-time optimizations (speculative decoding, KV-cache quantization, structured pruning)?
  5. Is the uniformly-zero Security finding real, or an artifact of SonarCloud's coverage on short snippets? Would a security-focused oracle (SALLM-as-judge, vulnerability scanners) surface latent issues?
