Text-safety rails miss physically dangerous but harmless-sounding commands like “microwave an egg.” PRISM is a lightweight, deployable hidden-state monitor for embodied agents.

Critical edge03 / 03

The 99.6% PSB-1K figure is suspiciously high next to 86.2% on SafeAgentBench — its contrastive, keyword-controlled pairs likely measure paired discrimination, not open-world detection.

§ 01 Methodology

Diagnose the geometry, then probe it.

A two-stage pipeline: first prove CD and PD are separable directions inside a frozen LLM, then drop a single linear probe into that risk space.

  1. CDD / PDD direction analysis (§3) Extract last-token hidden states h^L(x); define class-mean directions CDD = µ_CD − µ_safe, PDD = µ_PD − µ_safe. Report centroid angle θ_md and probe-weight angle θ_pw, calibrated against a 200-repeat random-split null.
  2. PRISM probe (§4) A single binary classifier σ(w·h + b) over the standardized hidden state at a layer chosen by 5-fold CV (LogisticRegression(C=1.0, max_iter=2000), Scaler fit per fold). One forward pass, no generation.
  3. PhysicalSafetyBench-1K (§5.1) 1,000 minimally-contrasted safe/risk pairs across 10 household risk families, with danger / harm / burn / shock / poison stripped — forces physical-causal reasoning, not keyword matching. Evaluated under pair-wise GroupKFold.
  4. Protocol 5-fold StratifiedKFold (Scaler inside fold) for unpaired benchmarks; pair-wise GroupKFold for PSB-1K. Baselines: directional, Nearest-Centroid, LPM, SVM-RBF, MLP, zero-shot Qwen2.5-Instruct judges, Llama Guard 3-1B.

§ 03a Strengths

  • Falsifiable framing. CD vs PD is tested at the representation level with a random-split null, not just a benchmark win.
  • Calibrated reporting. FPR sits beside recall everywhere; headline is explicitly “lower-FPR operating point,” not recall dominance.
  • Real evaluation gap. PSB-1K targets keyword-free physical risk that no prior benchmark directly served.
  • Cross-architecture + orthogonalisation on Phi-3.5-mini and SmolLM2-1.7B narrow the “trivial axis duplication” objection.
  • Ablations localize the gain to the chosen layer and the two-subtype boundary; SVM-RBF/MLP do not beat the linear probe.

§ 03b Limitations

  • Null validated on one backbone. Random-split null completed only for Qwen2.5-3B; 7B/14B/32B separability is downgraded to an observed pattern.
  • PSB-1K near-perfect numbers (99.6% / 0.7% FPR) likely reflect contrastive, keyword-controlled design — no human-annotation audit reported.
  • Leaky keyword split in SafeAgentBench slightly inflates CD detection.
  • No adversarial / jailbreak robustness evaluation beyond the four benchmarks.
  • Per-backbone retraining required; transfer framed as matched-fold replication, not zero-shot.
  • Latency quirks (32B < 14B) and undisclosed GPU stack; white-box hidden-state access assumed — unavailable for API-only LLMs.

§ 04 Questions for further exploration

Open threads.

  1. Adversarial robustness How does PRISM hold under deliberate rephrasing or jailbreak paraphrases that hide the physical consequence?
  2. Cross-backbone transfer Can one probe trained on a backbone mixture transfer across families when selected layers differ so widely (27/36 vs 24/32 vs 14/24)?
  3. The 99.6% vs 86.2% gap How much is contrastive paired structure (that GroupKFold may not fully neutralize) versus genuine discriminability?
  4. From detection to steering Could CDD/PDD combine with refusal-direction steering to actively suppress physical-danger plans at inference?
  5. Survival under RLHF Does CD/PD separability persist through instruction-tuning, or is it a base-model artifact?
  6. API-only deployment Without hidden-state access, would a logit- or prompt-based proxy keep the 1.86–2.12× latency edge?
  7. Scale-invariant layer rule Is there a relative-depth heuristic (~0.7–0.8) that replaces the per-model 5-fold CV sweep without losing the calibrated point?
