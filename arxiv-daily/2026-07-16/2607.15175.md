> **一句话总结：** 作者使用单方向线性 mass-mean 探针，在 25 个预训练语言模型（14M–14B）的隐藏层表示中验证了一个可稳定检测、可跨语法现象泛化、且部分跨语言共享的"语法性"维度，并通过反概率化与反可接受度去混淆实验证明该维度独立于句子似然、合理性等共线因素。

> **TL;DR:** Using a single-direction mass-mean linear probe, the authors show that grammaticality is linearly encoded in the hidden representations of 25 pretrained NLMs (14M–14B params); on BLiMP, probe success ranges from 13.4% (Pythia-14m) to 94.0% (Qwen3-14B) and correlates strongly with model size (R = 0.928). Deconfounding experiments (Exp. 3–4) on BLiMP/SCaMP show the signal survives decorrelating string probability (max drop 3.19 pp on OLMo-2-7B) and acceptability factors, and cross-lingual transfer (Exp. 5) on CLAMS/RuBLiMP/ZhoBLiMP yields near-English accuracy (61.4% cross-ling vs. 66.6% English), establishing grammaticality as a coherent representational dimension distinct from probability-based measures.

## 核心贡献

- 提出并验证了一种基于表示空间（而非字符串概率）的语法性度量框架：用单方向 mass-mean 线性探针在隐藏层中分离合语法/不合语法句子，作为对传统 targeted syntactic evaluation 的互补证据。
- 在 25 个预训练语言模型（GPT-2、Llama-3、OLMo/OLMo-2、Pythia 14M–12B、Qwen3 0.6B–14B）上系统刻画了语法性编码的存在、规模化趋势与现象级泛化。
- 设计了两类去混淆实验：(i) Counter/Favour 概率操控（Exp. 3），证明探针不是在追踪字符串概率；(ii) 与 plausibility/truth/imageability 等可接受度因素的正反向泛化与 boundary-tilting 实验（Exp. 4），证明语法性信号独立于"广义可接受度"。
- 通过跨语言实验（Exp. 5，覆盖德/法/俄/希伯来/中文）发现 Llama 系列模型表现出近似语言无关的语法性编码，提示语法性是表示空间中一个共享的组织维度。
- 提出新的对比指标 MP_Rep，并证明在大模型中探针方法与概率方法捕捉的是不同来源的方差（MP_Rep 在最大模型上甚至反超 MP_Prob 达 +6.89 pp）。

## Key Contributions

- A representation-based (rather than probability-based) framework for measuring grammaticality in NLMs, using a single-direction mass-mean probe over hidden-state representations of sentences as a complement to targeted syntactic evaluation.
- A systematic study across 25 pretrained NLMs (GPT-2, Llama-3.x, OLMo/OLMo-2, Pythia 14M–12B, Qwen3 0.6B–14B), characterizing the emergence, scale-dependence, and phenomenon-generality of the grammaticality encoding.
- Two deconfounding paradigms: (i) Counter/Favour probability manipulation (Exp. 3) showing probes do not merely track string probability; (ii) cross-generalization and boundary-tilting experiments against plausibility/truth/imageability/etc. (Exp. 4) showing the grammaticality signal is not reducible to general sentence acceptability.
- A cross-linguistic study (Exp. 5, covering German/French/Russian/Hebrew/Mandarin via CLAMS, RuBLiMP, ZhoBLiMP) demonstrating that, in multilingually-trained models (Llama), the grammaticality encoding is largely language-general.
- A new MP_Rep measure plus the empirical finding that representation-based probes and probability-based measures capture systematically different sources of variance, with MP_Rep reversing the gap (up to +6.89 pp) at the largest scales.

## Methodology

The paper studies whether grammaticality is linearly encoded in sentence representations of pretrained NLMs, using mass-mean probing (Marks & Tegmark, 2024). For a set of mean-centered hidden-state vectors x_i with grammaticality labels y_i ∈ {0,1}, the probe is defined by a single direction θ = μ_1 − μ_0 (the difference of class means); a sentence is classified as grammatical when σ(θᵀx) > 0.5. Probes are fitted on 1,600 sentences per setup and evaluated on held-out items, with 100 random resamples per configuration.

- **Models (Table 3):** 25 pretrained NLMs spanning GPT-2 (Small→XL), Llama-3.2-1B/3B and Llama-3.1-8B, OLMo-1B/7B and OLMo-2-1B/7B/13B, the full Pythia scaling suite (14M–12B), and Qwen3 0.6B–14B. All were pre-filtered to perform above chance on BLiMP minimal-pair probability comparisons.
- **Datasets (Tables 1–2):** Grammaticality benchmarks BLiMP (67 datasets × 2,000 pairs), SCaMP (134 × 2,000), DGL, LI-Adger, RuBLiMP (Russian, 45), ZhoBLiMP (Chinese, 118), CLAMS (de/fr/ru/he). Extra-grammatical datasets used for deconfounding: Shades (plausibility), Drive (imageability/arousal), Truth (factuality).
- **Regions of interest (§2.3):** Critical region (where grammaticality diverges), Pre-critical (preceding token, control), and Final (sentence-final punctuation). Representations are mean-centered per token to remove lexical identity confounds.
- **Significance testing (§2.5):** "Probe success" requires the probe distribution to (a) significantly exceed a label-shuffled null via Welch's t-test with Holm-Bonferroni correction and (b) be ≥5% above the null mean; the 5% threshold was calibrated from Pre-Critical region false-positive rates (range −2.62% to +1.65%).
- **Five experiments:** Exp.1 establishes basic detection (fit on LI-Adger, eval on BLiMP) and compares with probability-based MP_Prob. Exp.2 examines localization (Critical vs. Final vs. Pre-critical) and cross-phenomenon generalization on 40 annotated BLiMP subsets. Exp.3 (Counter/Favour) decorrelates string probability from grammaticality in fitting sentences. Exp.4 measures generalization between grammaticality and acceptability-related contrasts and uses a boundary-tilting experiment to test whether acceptability subsumes grammaticality. Exp.5 runs within- and cross-language probing across six non-English benchmarks on the five strongest models.

## Results & Highlights

- **Detection scales with model size (Exp.1, Fig.2A):** Probe success on BLiMP ranges from **13.4% in Pythia-14m** to **94.0% in Qwen3-14B**, with Pearson correlation between success rate and log₁₀(parameters) of **R = 0.928**; global probe accuracy correlates at R = 0.950.
- **Probes complement probability-based measures (Exp.1, Fig.3):** The gap (MP_Rep − MP_Prob) ranges from **−13.4 pp** (small models, where probability wins) to **+6.89 pp** (large models, where probes win), with R = 0.791, p < 0.001 against log₁₀(parameters).
- **Incremental sensitivity (Exp.2, Fig.4):** All 25 models show probe success in both Critical and Final regions but **not** in the Pre-Critical control region; when probes succeed, Critical-region probes are on average more accurate than Final-region probes (Fig.5).
- **Phenomenon-generality (Exp.2):** Probes fitted on one BLiMP subset generalize across phenomena; regressing out shared sentence-similarity terms leaves accuracies substantially above the null (Fig.6B), supporting phenomenon-general grammaticality signals.
- **Independence from string probability (Exp.3, Fig.7):** Under the Counter manipulation (decorrelating probability from grammaticality) the largest accuracy drop across all models was only **3.19 pp (OLMo-2-0425-7B)**; Favour range [−2.61%, +1.53%], Counter range [−3.19%, +6.12%]. Most models remain at ceiling on probe success in the Counter condition.
- **Independence from acceptability (Exp.4, Fig.8):** Cross-generalization between grammaticality and extra-grammatical datasets is low (gram→extra mean **11.2%**, extra→gram mean **11.5%**) versus gram→gram mean **55.9%**; boundary-tilting toward acceptability preserves grammaticality probing (Original ≈ Tilt) whereas the Control tilt over grammatical datasets destroys it.
- **Cross-linguistic transfer (Exp.5, Figs.11–12):** For the five strongest models, within-language probing succeeds across de/fr/ru/he/zh; cross-lingual mean accuracy is **61.4% [59.9%, 62.9%]** versus English mean **66.6% [65.0%, 68.8%]**. Llama models show only marginal cross-lingual success-rate drops, while OLMo-7B-hf and Qwen3-4B show drastic drops, indicating language-generality depends on multilingual pretraining.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Modest-but-real. Wang et al. (2026) already probed grammaticality with logistic-regression linear probes; this paper's main methodological novelty is (a) using mass-mean (single-direction) probes with the MP_Rep measure, (b) the Counter/Favour probability decorrelation, and (c) the boundary-tilting test against acceptability. These are useful but incremental extensions of established probing methodology (Marks & Tegmark 2024; Lepori et al. 2026).
- **Rigor:** Strong on deconfounding — the Counter/Favour manipulation and the SCaMP plausible/implausible comparison are well-motivated attacks on the "it's just likelihood / it's just acceptability" alternative explanations. Statistical machinery (Welch's t-test vs. label-shuffled null, Holm-Bonferroni, 100 resamples, calibrated 5% noise threshold from Pre-Critical baselines) is above the median for probing papers.
- **Reproducibility:** High. All 25 models are public, all datasets are public, code/data are released. The 1,600-sentence fitting budget and 100-run protocol are precisely specified.
- **Significance:** The strongest claim — "we view our work as settling this premise" of whether NLMs can represent grammaticality at all — is rhetorically bolder than the evidence supports. The evidence is *correlational* (probes decode a label) rather than *causal*; the boundary-tilting experiment moves toward causal manipulation but only verifies that acceptability-tilted probes still classify grammaticality, not that the grammaticality direction itself causally drives model behavior. The link to actual syntactic representations (constituency/dependency probes) is explicitly deferred to future work (§8.1.1).
- **Red flags / specific weaknesses:** (1) A chronic dependence on minimal-pair-style synthetic benchmarks (BLiMP/SCaMP/CLAMS), which the authors themselves acknowledge may not line up with the model's internal notion of "phenomenon"; the natural-text (LI-Adger) condition is used only as a fitting set, not as a primary evaluation. (2) The 5% success threshold is calibrated from Pre-Critical BLiMP probes only — the same threshold is then applied to non-English benchmarks (CLAMS items per dataset as low as 280) without re-calibration, which could inflate cross-lingual success rates for the smaller benchmarks. (3) The cross-lingual "language-general encoding" claim rests on only 5 models and on collapsing within-language phenomenon variation (a caveat the authors note but do not fix). (4) No control for the well-known metalinguistic-data contamination concern (BLiMP/SCaMP sentences potentially appearing in pretraining corpora) beyond avoiding chat models; the paper flags this but does not run a contamination analysis (e.g., on OLMo/Pythia training data, which is publicly available).

## Strengths

- Directly engages with the strongest published objections to probability-based grammaticality evaluation (Katzir 2023; Dentella et al. 2023) and attacks each confound with a targeted experiment.
- Mass-mean probing is parameter-light (one direction θ), making the results hard to dismiss as probe-capacity artifacts.
- Cross-phenomenon and cross-linguistic generalization analyses turn a binary "can we decode grammaticality" question into a richer characterization of how this dimension is organized.
- Honest negative-result reporting: the cross-lingual generalization is shown to fail for several model-language pairs (e.g., OLMo-7B-hf, Qwen3-4B), and small models are correctly reported as the most vulnerable to deconfounding manipulations.

## Limitations

- All probing is correlational; no causal intervention (e.g., activation patching, steering with θ) is run to verify the grammaticality direction functionally participates in model computation.
- Heavy reliance on synthetic minimal-pair benchmarks whose internal phenomenon categorizations may not match the model's own representations.
- The "grammaticality is independent of probability" conclusion is bounded by the 20%/80% probability quantiles used in Counter/Favour — more extreme probability contrasts could in principle erode the result, and this boundary is not stress-tested.
- Cross-lingual claims are limited to 5 models and 6 benchmarks; the within-language phenomenon variation is collapsed, leaving language-pair-level failures under-diagnosed.
- No contamination analysis despite explicit awareness of the metalinguistic-data-in-pretraining risk.
- The "settling the premise" framing is stronger than the (correlational, probe-based) evidence warrants.

## Relevance

对研究 LLM 内部表示几何与可解释性/探针方法学的工作高度相关——它提供了一个具体的、跨语言可迁移的"线性方向 = 高阶语义属性"案例（继 truth/plausibility/political-orientation 之后），同时为 syntax probing 与 psycholinguistics 中"语法性 vs. 概率"的长期争论提供了新的实证角度，可作为后续 mechanistic interpretability（因果干预、电路定位）工作的基线。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the mass-mean grammaticality direction θ participate *causally* in the model's behavior — e.g., does activation steering along θ shift downstream probability judgments or generation, and does patching θ across sentences transfer grammaticality?
- How does the grammaticality direction relate structurally to known syntactic probes (Hewitt & Manning dependency probe, Eisape et al. parse-state probes)? Are ungrammatical-region sentences specifically lacking constituency/dependency signatures?
- Using publicly available training corpora for Pythia/OLMo, can training-data attribution explain *why* the direction emerges — e.g., does it correlate with co-occurrence statistics of grammatical vs. ungrammatical strings, including metalinguistic documents (the BLiMP paper itself, language-instruction text)?
- Does the language-general grammaticality encoding in Llama survive more extreme typological distances (e.g., polysynthetic or free-word-order languages beyond the 6 tested), and does it appear in models pretrained without explicit multilingual data scaling?
- How robust is the encoding under nonce-word / "jabberwocky" manipulations that strip lexical semantics — the authors flag this as planned follow-up but it is unaddressed here?
- Could the same mass-mean methodology recover a *causally interpretable* boundary when combined with contrastive activation addition (Rimsky et al. 2024), and would that boundary align with the linear θ found by probing?
