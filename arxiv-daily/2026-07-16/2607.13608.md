> **一句话总结：** 提出 MEDA 多智能体框架，将 LLM 科学推理与受约束的符号回归结合，用于从生物学背景知识与可选时序数据中自动发现 ODE 模型，在 12 个生物动力学基准上状态变量恢复完美，约束式建模（而非纯数值拟合）被证明是实现机制正确性的核心承重组件。

> **TL;DR:** MEDA is an LLM-and-SR agentic system that recovers ODE models of biological dynamical systems by retrieving literature, formalizing variables/constraints, and running constrained symbolic search. On canonical retrieval, extrapolation, and open-ended discovery tasks (SIR, Lotka-Volterra, FitzHugh-Nagumo, age-structured SIR, misinformation dynamics, etc.), it achieves perfect variable-F1 across all 12 benchmark systems and term-level F1=1.00 with the full pipeline, while pure-SINDy ablation drops average plausibility to 0.603 and term F1 to 0.541, showing that mechanistic constraint generation - not numerical fitting - is the load-bearing component.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 MEDA（Mechanistic Equation Discovery Agentic）框架，将 LLM 智能体与受约束的符号回归结合，把 ODE 发现从"最后的数值拟合"扩展到完整科学推理链：文献检索 → 形式化变量与约束 → 候选模型搜索 → 批判与评估。
- 将自动化科学发现（ASD）目标刻意收窄到"发现 ODE 及其机制依据"，区别于近期追求端到端"自动写论文"的系统，强调机械性假设的构建、批评与精炼。
- 设计双模式系统：纯约束模式（无实验数据，仅由文献驱动的生物学约束与稀疏偏好引导）与数据锚定模式（额外用 SINDy + 轨迹拟合打分），且真值方程在搜索阶段对所有 discovery 智能体隔离（盲搜索），仅事后由 Evaluator 读取。
- 通过完整的消融与鲁棒性研究证明：知识引导的形式化（Formalizer）和机械式约束是承重组件；纯数据驱动变体（no-literature、pure-SINDy）会保留"与轨迹相容但机制错误"的方程。
- 构建了覆盖种群动力学、流行病学、神经兴奋性、错误信息传播、慢性伤口愈合等 12 个基准的三层评测体系（信息检索 IR / 推理外推 EX / 开放发现 DI），并提出包含变量级、约束级、项级、表达式级、专家合理性五类指标。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces MEDA, a multi-agent pipeline that couples LLM-based scientific reasoning with constrained symbolic regression, treating intermediate scientific artifacts (literature review, typed constraints, seed equations, diagnostics) as first-class inspectable outputs rather than opaque internal states.
- Reframes ASD around explicit mechanistic hypothesis construction for biological ODEs, a domain chosen because prior knowledge is qualitative, incomplete, and distributed across literature (unlike physics with conservation laws/symmetries).
- Implements a blind-search protocol: ground-truth equations are quarantined from all discovery agents and read only post-hoc by the Evaluator, removing leakage in benchmarking.
- Provides a 3-tier benchmark (IR / EX / DI) with 12 biological systems and a 5-metric evaluation hierarchy (variable F1, constraint F1, term P/R/F1, expression score = 1 - TED, inclusion, and an expert plausibility score averaged across 5 PhD biomathematicians with 10+ years of experience).
- Delivers an ablation study isolating EDA/SINDy, literature retrieval, the pretrained formalizer, and pure-SINDy, plus a robustness study covering proportional Gaussian noise, sparse sampling, and hidden variables.

## Methodology

MEDA is organized as a set of specialized agents coordinated by an orchestrator, each reading a defined input artifact and writing a structured output artifact to a shared session directory:

1. **Literature Surveyor** - compiles cited background on the target system, including expected constraints, candidate model families, and qualitative dynamical signatures; writes `lit_review.md`.
2. **Explorer (data-anchored mode only)** - performs EDA and an optional SINDy-based sparse-regression screen on supplied trajectories to identify candidate data-supported terms.
3. **Formalizer** - curates the literature/EDA material into a machine-readable problem specification (`problem.json` + `constraints.md`): selects state variables, expresses typed hard/soft constraints, proposes seed equations, marks protected core terms, sets polynomial-degree limits, and assigns constraint weights. A validator checks this spec before search.
4. **Runner** - performs an evolutionary (genetic algorithm) search over sparse polynomial ODE systems using a composite objective combining constraint satisfaction, trajectory-fit (when data available), and sparsity, with hard-constraint gating and core-term protection enforcing biological admissibility; writes `results.json` and `symbolic_regression.md`.
5. **Reporter** - forward-simulates the selected model and produces diagnostics, plots, and summary.
6. **Recapper** - performs pipeline-level critique, identifies limitations, and recommends next steps.
7. **Evaluator** - reads the quarantined ground truth only after the blind search ends and compares across 5 metric families.

Two operating modes exist: **constraint-only** (no measurements; search driven purely by literature-derived constraints + parsimony) and **data-anchored** (user supplies a time series used as an additional fit objective). The search grammar restricts candidates to polynomial ODEs, making evaluation controlled but excluding Hill kinetics, saturating responses, delays, and stochastic terms.

## Results & Highlights

- **Perfect variable recovery on all 12 systems:** Variable F1 = 1.00 for every benchmark case (logistic growth, Lotka-Volterra, SIR, Allee, FitzHugh-Nagumo, age-structured SIR, host-dual-pathogen, IGP 4-species, Allee cooperative support, FitzHugh-Nagumo adaptation, misinformation dynamics, chronic wound), in both constraints-only and data-anchored modes (Table 1).
- **Lotka-Volterra with data → exact structural recovery:** Term F1 improved from 0.80 (no data) to 1.00 (with data); expression score improved from 0.75 to 1.00 and plausibility from 0.93 to 1.00.
- **Classical SIR (IR) is robust to 30% proportional Gaussian noise** with term F1 = 1.00 at 0-30% noise, degrading only at 40-60% noise to term F1 = 0.73; age-structured SIR held term F1 ≥ 0.90 across all noise levels (Table 3).
- **Ablation (Table 2) - full system averages across IR/EX/DI:** Full system term F1 = 1.00 and plausibility 0.967; No-EDA/SINDy variant remained at F1 = 1.00; Pretrained-formalizer variant remained at F1 = 1.00; **No-literature variant dropped average plausibility to ~0.700 and term F1 to ~0.732**; **Pure-SINDy variant dropped average plausibility to 0.603, term F1 to 0.541, and term precision to 0.382**.
- **Worst-case degradation in misinformation dynamics (DI):** removing literature dropped plausibility from 0.98 → 0.59 and term F1 from 1.00 → 0.50, despite good trajectory fit - direct evidence that numerical fit alone does not recover the correct symbolic mechanism.
- **Data can hurt mechanistic correctness (non-monotonic effect of data):** In FitzHugh-Nagumo adaptation (EX), data-anchored mode produced term F1 = 0.47 and plausibility 0.45 (vs. 0.88 and 0.95 constraints-only), introducing high-degree spurious terms while missing defining couplings.
- **Hidden-variable robustness (Table 4):** dropping the recovered compartment R from SIR or RO from age-structured SIR had no effect on term-level recovery (F1 = 1.00, plausibility ≥ 0.98); dropping the moderation-capacity variable C from misinformation dynamics reduced variable F1 to 0.80 and term F1 to 0.83.
- **Sparse sampling (removing 25-75% of time points from noise-free trajectories) left recovered terms unchanged** for both SIR systems.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Positioning is incremental relative to LLM-SR and SciPED-style scientist-in-the-loop SR; the main novelty is the explicit separation of literature retrieval, typed constraint formalization, and constrained genetic search into inspectable artifacts tailored to biology rather than physics. The contribution is a re-architecting of known ideas (LLM priors + constrained SR + SINDy), not a new algorithm.
- **Rigor:** Reasonable internal validity via the blind-search quarantine protocol and a multi-level metric suite. However, the benchmark is small (12 systems), all reference equations are either canonical or designed by the same authors, and there is no head-to-head comparison against LLM-SR, SciMED, or other recent baselines under matched settings - only against MEDA's own ablations (no-literature, no-EDA, pure-SINDy, pretrained formalizer). "Pure SINDy" is a notably weak baseline; the absence of direct comparison to LLM-SR (cited as ref 43) is a real gap.
- **Reproducibility:** Code is public on GitHub, the data are in the appendix, and the metric definitions are given. Reproducibility is hampered by reliance on LLM agents (model version unspecified in the main text), the inherent stochasticity of genetic search and LLM retrieval, and the subjective expert plausibility score which cannot be reproduced without the same five evaluators.
- **Significance:** Moderate. The paper makes a useful conceptual point (constraint formalization is load-bearing; numerical fit is insufficient for biological ODE discovery) and demonstrates it within its benchmark, but the polynomial-only grammar excludes the kinetics (Hill, saturating, delays) that dominate real biology, limiting immediate applicability to biochemical networks.
- **Red flags:** The pure-SINDy baseline used to dramatize the gap is a strawman (no constraints at all, vs. MEDA's hand-tuned formalizer); the comparison against pure SINDy does not isolate the contribution of the LLM from the contribution of the typed constraints a human could equally write. Several "with data" rows in Table 1 (FitzHugh-Nagumo adaptation, chronic wound) show data actively hurting mechanistic recovery, but the system has no mechanism to detect and reject such trajectory-compatible-but-wrong solutions - the Recapper does not flag them. The benchmark equations for EX and DI tiers were designed by the authors themselves, introducing potential evaluator bias even with quarantine of the literal equations.

## Strengths

- Clean, modular, fully inspectable pipeline where each agent writes a human-readable artifact - the chain of reasoning behind a discovered ODE can be audited step by step.
- Blind-search quarantine of ground-truth equations is a methodologically correct choice for honest benchmarking.
- The negative result (data can hurt mechanistic correctness; pure numerical fit preserves biologically wrong equations) is intellectually honest and is the most actionable finding.
- Comprehensive robustness analysis across noise, sparsity, and hidden variables, with honest reporting of where the system fails (e.g., structural identifiability bottleneck in misinformation dynamics).
- Practical code release and a 3-tier benchmark with a thoughtful, multi-level metric suite.

## Limitations

- Small benchmark (12 systems) relative to the diversity of biological ODE modeling; does not cover biochemical networks, spatial dynamics, stochastic processes, delay differential equations, or hybrid discrete-continuous models.
- Search grammar restricted to polynomial ODEs, excluding Hill kinetics, saturating functional responses, delays, thresholds, and stochastic effects that are central in real biology.
- Expert plausibility score is subjective, based on only five evaluators, and not reproducible without them.
- No direct head-to-head comparison with peer systems (LLM-SR, SciMED) under matched conditions; the strongest external baseline is the authors' own pure-SINDy strawman.
- LLM model versions and search hyperparameters are not fully specified in the main text; stochasticity of LLM retrieval and genetic search is not characterized with variance/confidence intervals across seeds.
- The system cannot detect or self-correct cases where data-anchored mode produces trajectory-compatible but mechanistically wrong equations (a known failure mode the authors themselves document).

## Relevance

直接相关于 LLM 智能体驱动的科学发现方向，且选择"生物学 ODE 发现"这一在我关注的 LLM agents + scientific reasoning 交叉点上较少被严格 benchmark 的子领域，方法论（知识引导形式化 vs. 纯数据驱动的消融对照）对我们评估 LLM 在机制建模中的真实价值有借鉴意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does MEDA perform in a direct head-to-head comparison against LLM-SR and SciMED under matched data, constraints, and compute budgets, rather than only against its own ablations?
- Can the polynomial grammar be extended to Hill/saturating/delay terms without losing the constraint-gating properties that make the formalizer load-bearing?
- Is there an automatic test that detects when data-anchored mode has produced a trajectory-compatible but mechanistically incorrect ODE (the documented failure mode in FitzHugh-Nagumo adaptation and chronic wound healing)?
- How sensitive are the results to the underlying LLM model version, temperature, and the number of genetic-search generations - are the reported F1 = 1.00 numbers stable across seeds?
- Can the framework be applied to real (non-simulated) biological time series with the noise, sparsity, and partial observability typical of wet-lab data, where ground truth is genuinely unknown?
