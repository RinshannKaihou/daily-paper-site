> **一句话总结：** 作者通过一个真实的 pipeline 失效案例（生成与评判共用 max_tokens=5，导致 Turkish 幻觉答案被截断），证明在 LLM 生成负样本的合成 LLM-as-Judge 语料库中，标准聚合稳健性检验（大样本、复现、机制解释、对照实验）结构性无法发现 stimulus 层面的退化，进而用软件工程的"测试预言机问题"将该缺陷归因，并提出人工抽检 15-20 条原始生成项的校验协议。

> **TL;DR:** A shared `max_tokens=5` budget between generation and judging silently truncated one producer's Turkish hallucinations, fabricating a 32-point cross-lingual selection-accuracy collapse in GLM (0.64 TR vs 0.96 EN on HaluEval) that survived replication (N=50→500), a mechanistic account, and a producer-swap control, yet vanished to 1.00 once the budget was split; the paper reframes this as the test-oracle problem, shows the same fault is caught at 50/50 accuracy with zero API cost in a perturbation-based corpus, and prescribes a 15-20-item manual-reading validation protocol for oracle-less designs.

## 核心贡献

- **存在性证明（Existence proof）：** 在一个 A/B 可验证的案例中，oracle-less（LLM 生成负样本）设计里的一次 stimulus 层面失效，制造出一个跨语言偏差效应，它通过了放大样本、复现、三层机制解释、受控 producer-swap 实验四重稳健性检验，且每一项都让虚假效应"更可信"，最终只有人工阅读原始生成文本才暴露问题。
- **正对照（Positive control）：** 将同类故障注入一个基于确定性扰动的 oracle-bearing 语料库（wiki40b Turkish 含数字句子，替换首个数字，30% Bernoulli no-op），gold-to-negative 字符串比较以 50/50 准确率、零 API 调用、零人工阅读瞬间捕获全部故障，把"一种设计可审计、另一种不可"从论证变成演示。
- **校验协议（Validation protocol）：** 针对必须在 oracle-less 体制下工作的分析师，提出在任何聚合统计之前：每个生成条件下人工阅读 15-20 条原始生成项、报告平均长度与退化率（verbatim 重复率 + conversational opener/短碎片率），并给出退化率超出个位数即应在进入统计前排除的决策规则。
- **两种失效模式的区分：** 明确区分"消失（disappearance，selection collapse 被完全捏造）"与"扭曲（distortion，style/Markdown 偏好真实存在但幅度甚至符号随 stimulus 长度而变）"，指出聚合指标无法分辨二者。
- **结构性归因：** 用软件工程中的测试预言机问题（Barr et al., 2015）与蜕变测试（Chen et al., 1998）为两种负样本生成设计的可审计性差异命名，把贡献从"个人教训"提升为"可被复用的结构性结论"。

## Key Contributions

- **Existence proof** that, in an A/B-verified oracle-less (LLM-generated-negative) corpus, a stimulus-level fault survived four standard aggregate-robustness checks (larger sample N=500, replication from N=50, a three-layer mechanistic account, a controlled producer-swap) and was exposed only by manually reading raw generations.
- **Positive control** showing the same fault class, injected into a minimal oracle-bearing (mechanical-perturbation) corpus, is caught at 50/50 accuracy by a gold-to-negative string comparison with zero API calls and zero human reading.
- **A concrete validation protocol** for the oracle-less regime: manually read 15-20 raw items per generation condition, report mean length and two degeneration rates, and exclude any condition whose degeneration rate exceeds single digits before computing aggregate statistics.
- **A two-mode taxonomy of corruption** — disappearance (the selection collapse was fabricated wholesale) vs. distortion (the Markdown-style preference was real but its magnitude, and for DeepSeek its sign, tracked stimulus length) — and the claim that aggregate metrics cannot distinguish them.
- **A structural reframing** via the test-oracle problem and metamorphic testing, locating the findability asymmetry in corpus-construction design rather than in bug frequency.

## Methodology

The study is built around an A/B comparison between a *corrupted* and a *corrected* version of the same generation pipeline applied to identical source contexts.

- **Judges (3, from 3 families):** DeepSeek-V4-Flash, gemini-3.5-flash (thinking disabled via `thinking_budget=0`), and glm-5.2 (via z.ai's Anthropic-compatible API). Two judges (DeepSeek, Gemini) also serve as producers.
- **Data:** Turkish items are generated from the wiki40b Turkish split (first 80 words, length > 60 chars), producing `{context, question, factual, halluc}` quadruples; English items are taken verbatim from the HaluEval QA split (`right_answer` / `hallucinated_answer`). Default question is the open "What do you think about this text?" to match a faithfulness-reading task.
- **The A/B design:**
  - *Corrupted pipeline:* generation and judging share a single `max_tokens=5` budget (enough for a judge's "A"/"B" but truncating paragraph-length generations); the Gemini generation path had no explicit cap and was unaffected; prompts did not forbid conversational openers.
  - *Corrected pipeline:* budgets separated (`max_tokens=300` for generation, 5 for judging), Gemini thinking trace disabled, generation prompt tightened to forbid introductory phrases ("of course"), and caches namespaced (`gen300::`) to avoid collision.
- **Four measured biases:** Style (Markdown-bulleted vs plain, A-first order), Position (A-first vs B-first difference), Selection (A/B vs 1/2 label agreement), and Factual accuracy (rate of selecting the factual answer). Inter-judge agreement reported as Fleiss' κ; three-judge majority accuracy as an incidental aggregation check.
- **Item-level audit:** classifies each hallucination as verbatim copy of factual, truncated conversational filler (gazetteer of openers), short non-filler fragment (≤3 words), or valid hallucination; reports rates and mean word lengths.
- **Style-by-length stratification:** recomputes style score after binning items by factual-field word length across corrupted DeepSeek-Turkish, unaffected Gemini-Turkish, and English HaluEval sets.
- **Positive-control procedure:** 50 Turkish sentences with numerals, perturbation = replace first numeral; fault injected via per-item Bernoulli draws at 30% (seed 42, realized 20 no-ops = 40%); oracle = gold-to-negative string comparison.
- **Reproducibility:** all results reproducible from a single cached log of ~17,800 LLM calls; cache and five self-contained scripts deposited at OSF (10.17605/OSF.IO/RS2B8); two offline audits and the positive control require no API calls.

## Results & Highlights

- **Fabricated cross-lingual collapse (the artifact).** On N=500 Turkish items, GLM's selection accuracy on DeepSeek-produced hallucinations was ~0.64, versus 0.96 on the English HaluEval benchmark — a **32-point gap** with 95% CI [0.60, 0.68]; Fleiss' κ across the three judges was 0.041. The effect replicated from N=50 (0.54) to N=500 and survived a producer-swap control (GLM accuracy 0.61 on DeepSeek-produced vs 1.00 on Gemini-produced, N=200) — yet **none of it was real**.
- **What manual reading revealed.** Of DeepSeek-generated Turkish items, **81.0% began with a truncated conversational filler** ("Tabii ki, iş…"), **12.2% reproduced the factual field verbatim**, and **6.8% were otherwise degenerate fragments**; no item in the sample was a well-formed hallucination. The mean hallucinated length was **2.1 words** (corrupted) vs **20.0 words** (corrected), against Gemini's unaffected 46.9.
- **Recovery under the fix (Table 1).** After splitting generation and judging budgets: generation validity rose **0.0% → 98.8%**; GLM selection accuracy rose **0.61 → 1.00** (N=200 → N=80); GLM factual accuracy rose **0.845 → 0.99**; DeepSeek's within-producer factual **0.88 → 1.00**; three-judge majority factual **0.962 → 1.00**. No cross-lingual, producer-specific, or self-referential effect survived.
- **Style bias is real but distorted (Table 2).** Stratified by factual-field length: DeepSeek scored **0.24 (EN) / 0.43 (TR) on 1-2-word factuals** vs **0.81 (TR) on 16+ word factuals** (sign flips across 0.5; z≈8.7); GLM scored **0.90/0.89 vs 0.65** (stays Markdown-preferring, z≈7.0); Gemini flat near **0.00** in both regimes. The apparent Turkish/English style invariance was actually a length invariance (corrupted Turkish 2.1 words ≈ HaluEval 2.2 words).
- **Positive control — oracle asymmetry (Section 4.3).** The injected no-op fault (30% Bernoulli, realized 40%) was caught with **50/50 accuracy (100%)** by a gold-to-negative string comparison, at **zero API calls, zero human reading, O(N) cost** — the same fault class that escaped four rounds of robustness checking in the oracle-less design.
- **Protocol thresholds (Section 5).** Sample-size formula `n ≥ ln(1−C)/ln(1−p)` gives, at C=99%: n=7 at 50% fault rate, n=13 at 30%, n=21 at 20%; using 30% as a conservative reference, **15 items clear 99% confidence and 20 reach ~99.9%**.

- **Single author:** Serkan Ballı, Department of Software Engineering, Mehmet Akif Ersoy University, Türkiye. The single-author status is consistent with the paper's genre — a self-audit of the author's own pipeline — and the unusual intellectual honesty (reporting the artifact at length rather than suppressing it) is itself a credibility signal.
- **Transparency:** Full reproducibility package — a cached log of ~17,800 LLM calls plus five self-contained analysis scripts — deposited at OSF (10.17605/OSF.IO/RS2B8), so every number can be regenerated with no new API expenditure. Producer aliases (deepseek-chat → DeepSeek-V4-Flash; gemini-flash-latest → gemini-3.5-flash) are reported stable throughout July 2026 data collection.
- **Self-consistency at own expense:** The author explicitly flags that HaluEval, the English reference, was *not* audited under the paper's own 15-20-item protocol and was trusted on reputational grounds — turning the author's own corpus into an instance of the problem the paper warns against. This is rare and lends credibility.
- **Theoretical grounding:** Draws on well-established software-engineering literature (Barr et al., 2015 on the oracle problem; Chen et al., 1998 on metamorphic testing) rather than inventing a bespoke taxonomy.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High *within the LLM-as-judge subfield*. The application of the test-oracle problem and metamorphic testing to *corpus construction* (rather than to the judge) is, by the author's own literature scan, novel; the closest prior work (Soumik 2026; BabelJudge 2026; Preference Leakage 2026) measures judge bias rather than stimulus integrity. The willingness to publish a null result (the headline effect was an artifact) is itself a contribution to the dataset-artifact tradition in NLP.
- **Rigor:** Strong on internal validity — the A/B design (same source contexts, only the pipeline differs) cleanly isolates the fault, and the producer-swap control is a genuine confound check. Statistical reporting is adequate (CIs, Fleiss' κ, two-proportion z-tests with reported z-values ≈8.7 and ≈7.0). The positive control is well-designed (frozen seed 42, reported realized vs. expected no-op count, binomial-fluctuation caveat).
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent in principle (OSF deposit, cached logs, cache-only re-execution). However, the result is **conditional on three specific vendor APIs** (DeepSeek, Gemini, z.ai/GLM) whose aliases must remain stable; the author notes this exact risk as a member of the fault class. A reader cannot fully reproduce without those APIs honoring the same aliases.
- **Significance:** Narrow but durable. The paper does *not* discover a new bias (the dramatized one was fake; the spared one is a known formatting preference), does *not* census affected corpora (only the author's own pipeline is audited; CalibJudge is named only as an unverified candidate), and does *not* rank generation designs. Its significance is methodological: a cheap protocol the community could adopt.
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - **Sample size asymmetry in the headline comparison:** corrupted N=200 vs corrected N=80 for GLM selection accuracy (0.61 → 1.00). The corrected ceiling at N=80 is a perfect score, which is mathematically thin for variance estimation; a skeptic could ask for a larger corrected sample.
  - **Single-fault existence proof.** The entire structural argument rests on *one* fault type (shared `max_tokens`) in *one* pipeline; the positive control exercises *one* fault type (silent no-op) in *one* oracle-bearing corpus. The author concedes a sweep over fault classes and design families is future work — meaning the "structural" claim is, strictly, a single-instance generalization.
  - **Three judges, one producer pair, one domain.** The judge set is 3 models; the producer comparison is one pair (DeepSeek/Gemini) on one language (Turkish); the source domain is a single encyclopedic stream (wiki40b). Each could be widened.
  - **English reference un-audited.** HaluEval was not run through the paper's own protocol; the corrected-data claim rests on Turkish alone, an asymmetry the author names but does not resolve.
  - **Binary faithfulness task only.** How the fault class propagates into preference/quality-comparison judgments (no gold reference) is unaddressed.

## Strengths

- Exceptional intellectual honesty: the paper centers on its own error, reports the fabricated effect in full, and explicitly publishes the misleading first-draft interpretation (including the false producer-judge interaction lead) as a caution.
- The A/B controlled design (same contexts, only pipeline differs) makes the causal attribution to the shared `max_tokens` parameter unambiguous.
- The positive control is a genuinely elegant demonstration: injecting the closest mechanical analogue of the fault into a perturbation corpus and catching it for free converts the central claim from argument into demonstration.
- The protocol is cheap, bounded, and derived from a worked example (with the sample-size formula `n ≥ ln(1−C)/ln(1−p)` made explicit) rather than asserted as universal — and the author is explicit that the thresholds are case-derived while the *diagnostic shape* is what generalizes.
- Connects a niche NLP methodological issue to a decades-old, well-developed software-engineering literature (oracle problem, metamorphic testing), giving reviewers a vocabulary rather than asking them to accept a bespoke taxonomy.

## Limitations

- Single pipeline, single fault type, single oracle-bearing corpus: the "structural asymmetry" claim is a single-instance generalization; a sweep across fault classes and both design families is explicitly future work.
- Small corrected sample (N=80 at ceiling 1.00) limits variance characterization of the corrected condition.
- The entire measurement is a binary factual-vs-hallucinated classification; propagation into reference-free preference or quality judgments is not addressed.
- The English reference (HaluEval) was not subjected to the paper's own protocol, so the corrected-data claim rests on Turkish alone.
- Vendor-API dependence: reproducibility is conditional on DeepSeek, Gemini, and z.ai honoring the same aliases — the very silent-drift risk the paper warns about.
- The positive control's no-op count (20/50 = 40%) was ~1.5 SD above the 30% target; the author attributes this to binomial fluctuation, which is plausible but worth noting.
- Does not census which existing corpora are affected; candidate members (CalibJudge) are named but unverified.

## Relevance

高度相关。本文直接针对 LLM-as-a-Judge 评估方法论的可靠性，尤其是多语言合成评估语料的 stimulus 完整性问题，对任何使用 LLM 生成负样本来度量 judge 偏差的工作（包括 RAG 评估、奖励模型评估、多语言 benchmark 构造）都是重要的方法论警示与可操作的校验协议来源。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the oracle asymmetry reproduce across fault classes beyond truncation and no-op (e.g., paraphrase-collapse, entity-swap-identity, negation-as-no-op) and across multiple oracle-bearing corpora beyond the 50-item numeral-perturbation toy?
- How does the stimulus-integrity fault class propagate into reference-free preference or quality-comparison judgments (e.g., MT-Bench-style pairwise scoring) where there is no gold answer to compare against?
- Would applying the Section 5 protocol to HaluEval item-by-item confirm its short-regime style scores as genuine or surface an undetected stimulus-level issue?
- Can the manual-reading protocol be partially automated — e.g., via a second LLM acting as a degeneration detector — without reintroducing an oracle-less dependency, or does any LLM-based check inherit the same structural blindness?
- Does the catchability asymmetry interact with judge-panel aggregation: can a diverse panel (PoLL-style) surface a stimulus-level fault that individual judges hide, or does majority voting amplify the masking as Ma et al. (2025) warn?
