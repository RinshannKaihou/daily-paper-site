ExaGEMM

Exploration Framework for CPU-Driven ML Inference via Associative In-Register Computing for Low-Bit GEMM · Hyunwoo Oh, Suyeon Jang, Hanning Chen, Sanggeon Yun, Ryozo Masukawa, Mohsen Imani · UC Irvine (Imani group)

Key Finding

Low-bit CPU GEMM, reframed as a support-selection problem: prune 99.2% of design candidates pre-silicon — paying for it with only a lightweight in-register select network.

Methodology

Each candidate execution primitive is a tuple k = (e, b, r, c, u): packing factor e, fan-in b, row/column tiles (r, c), and a schedule-level merge u (ILM) that amortizes output load/store across K-subblocks without changing the structural primitive.

An analytical explorer scores every candidate on five axes — register feasibility (U_VRF(k) ≤ 1, R_O(k) ≤ 1), logical instruction count, memory traffic (αmem from load/store microbenchmarks), arithmetic/control cost, and hardware overhead H_sel = α_sel · S_sel(e,b,r,c,q) — combining them into a single rank objective C_exe = C_cmp + α_mem · B_mem. αsel comes from TSMC 28 nm ASIC synthesis (Cadence Genus 21.10, 1 GHz).

Exploration is hierarchical: per-layer kernel selection → model-level support-point ranking → a non-dominated Pareto frontier over (execution cost, hardware overhead). Mixed precision is handled per-quantization first, then combined and filtered. Surviving points are materialized as ISA specs, gem5-AVX patches, and generated GEMM kernels for x86 + ARM.

Targets: Ryzen 9 9950X (512-bit), Ryzen 7 6800U (256-bit), Cortex-A78 (128-bit ARMv8-A). Workloads: DeiT-B W1A8, Llama2-7B W2A16, Llama2-7B W{1,2,4}A16 (AMQ). Baselines: T-MAC, llama.cpp, an "aggressive fixed" single-primitive design, and the VAQF FPGA co-design.

The clearest qualitative finding: workload-aware frontier selection matters most for mixed-precision LLMs. Uniform DeiT-B W1A8 collapses to a single dominant primitive hotspot, so one aggressive fixed primitive is near-sufficient. Llama2-7B W{1,2,4}A16 splits into three regime-specific hotspots (W1/W2/W4), making a fixed primitive insufficient — exactly where ExaGEMM's selection earns its keep.

Strengths & Limitations

PRO Strengths

  • Clean parameter split — structural (e,b,r,c) vs. schedule u makes the space expressive yet tractable.
  • Hierarchical mixed-precision search tames combinatorial explosion (up to ~2.9 billion combos) into a model-level Pareto.
  • CPU-native & edge-friendly — reuses existing SIMD datapaths; new hardware is only a select network. Area stays under ~3.6% on 128/256-bit targets.
  • Strong surrogate fidelity — Spearman up to 0.982, area MAPE 1.4%; credible as a pre-silicon screening tool.
  • Scoped novelty is honest — first to co-explore 1/2/4-bit uniform and AMQ inter-layer mixed precision under an explicit HW-overhead budget.

CON Limitations

  • Single process node — TSMC 28 nm / 1 GHz only; 5/3 nm selector-wire scaling unverified.
  • Narrow coverage — three CPU targets, three workloads (one ViT, two Llama2-7B). No diffusion/multimodal, no RISC-V vector.
  • No accuracy numbers — quantization quality assumed from prior AMQ/AWQ/Atom work; ML-quality question left open.
  • Additive H_model is a proxy — can overstate overhead when primitives share select logic.
  • No GPU/NPU baseline — defensible (target is CPU-only) but limits absolute-competitiveness claims.
  • Missing ablations — no isolation of ILM u vs. (e,b,r,c); no sensitivity to α_mem / α_sel miscalibration.

Questions for Further Exploration

Q1 · WORKLOAD

Do the three regime-specific hotspots survive a non-AMQ distribution (AWQ per-channel W4, or random per-layer bitwidths) — or are they an artifact of AMQ's discrete choices?

Q2 · ABLATION

What is the marginal latency contribution of the ILM factor u alone, holding (e,b,r,c) fixed? Is the schedule-level lever ever dominant?

Q3 · PROCESS

How does select-network overhead scale on 5 nm / 3 nm, where selector wire delay and routing congestion differ sharply from 28 nm?

Q4 · EXTENSION

Can the explorer cover activation quantization (W4A8), where table cardinality is governed by activation rather than weight bitwidth?

Q5 · GENERALIZATION

Does workload-aware selection still pay off for diffusion / multimodal models whose layer shapes and bitwidth pressures differ from attention/FFN blocks?

Q6 · ROBUSTNESS

Does frontier membership flip when α_mem is mis-calibrated by ±20% — and how robust is the shortlist to calibration error?
