> **一句话总结：** 作者提出 Taliesin——一种字节级精确的 KV-state 嫁接机制，配合 Galahad "验证-再缓存"飞轮，让一个完全冻结的 Gemma-4-12B 在 AIME 2025 上从 80.0% 提升到 93.3%（超过其 31B 兄弟的 89.2% 模型卡锚点），同时把 8 个复发问题的解码 token 数从 401,026 降到 61（6,574 倍），并以零额外显存把可用上下文从 32k 扩展到 285 万 token。

> **TL;DR:** A single-author experience report introduces a byte-exact, persistent, portable KV-state graft ("Taliesin") plus a verify-then-cache flywheel ("Galahad") that, without touching weights, lifts a frozen Gemma-4-12B on AIME 2025 from 80.0% to 93.3% (clearing the 31B's 89.2% model-card anchor), solves 8/8 hardest recurrence problems in 61 decode tokens versus 0/8 for a 401,026-token best-of-5 baseline (a 6,574x reduction), and widens usable context 87-fold (32k to 2.85M tokens) at zero extra accelerator memory; byte-exactness (SHA-256 equality, KL=0, 100% argmax) is verified on two Blackwell targets and held-out transfer reaches 7/7 on a 31B H100 run.

## 核心贡献

- **字节级精确的 KV 嫁接（Taliesin）**：首次公开演示一种可持久化、可移植、字节精确（SHA-256 相等、KL=0、100% argmax 一致）的 KV-state 嫁接机制，并明确刻画其唯一可达精确点——"原位置嫁接"，证明在 32 位浮点 RoPE 下任何引擎都无法在偏移位置实现字节精确。
- **验证-再缓存飞轮（Galahad）**：用最简协议（额外推理求解 → 外部可执行验证 → 以 KV 块落盘）把"已验证知识"变成可路由、可跨机复制的资产，构成一种不动权重的推理时学习。
- **能力与成本同时改善**：在不训练、不加卡的前提下，让冻结 12B 在 post-cutoff 的 AIME 2025 上从 80.0% 提升到 93.3%，并把复发问题的解码成本降到原来的 1/6,574。
- **超大上下文、零额外显存**：用磁盘上的字节精确 KV 块把 32k 服务窗口扩展到 285 万 token（87.1 倍），峰值显存不变（25,595 MB），访问代价不随深度增长。
- **诚实的系统行为刻画**：实测路由误判（off-distribution 4/4 弃权）与磁盘分页（补贴随上下文长度增大而非被吃掉），并以预注册回放在 B200 上独立复现字节精确性。
- **可审计的再现性边界**：所有输入/输出均 SHA-256 提交，结果文件含逐题生成与求解代码，读者可在没有闭源引擎的情况下独立复核分数。

## Key Contributions

- **Byte-exact KV-state graft (Taliesin).** A byte-for-byte restoration of a previously captured KV state, verified by SHA-256 equality of the resulting logits and zero KL divergence with 100% argmax agreement over 50 samples, claimed as the first public demonstration of a byte-exact, persistent, and portable KV graft used as a substrate for inference-time learning.
- **Numerical characterization of the exact operating point.** A graft-free control shows that own-position graft is the *unique* numerically exact regime on this model: relocating a block to a different absolute offset incurs a ~0.015 KL residual that equals the base model's own fresh-prefill position-sensitivity (~0.014 at M=128), traced by elimination to 32-bit non-associativity of the rotary identity cos a cos b + sin a sin b = cos(a-b).
- **Verify-then-cache flywheel (Galahad).** Solve → externally verify (execute generated program, confirm printed answer; or match ground truth) → deposit the verified KV block; route to the single relevant block per query rather than flat-merging, which the authors show causes cross-entry interference.
- **Smarter-and-cheaper-at-once empirical demonstration.** On AIME 2025, a frozen Gemma-4-12B moves from 80.0% to 93.3% with a grafted verified library, while 8 hardest recurrence problems are answered in 61 decode tokens total versus 0/8 solved by a 401,026-token best-of-5 budget.
- **Context widening without extra VRAM.** A 2,854,766-token store (88 blocks, 40.6 GB on disk) is reached at zero extra accelerator memory, with a constant ~0.29 s restore cost independent of depth.
- **Pre-registered cross-architecture replay.** Byte-exactness is reproduced on a rented B200 (datacenter Blackwell) under hashed pre-registration of source tree, pass criteria, and a falsifiable prediction; the prediction that position-sensitivity reappears is confirmed (with a magnitude 19x smaller than on the 12B, an honestly-reported miss).
- **Auditable reproducibility without an open engine.** Inputs and outputs are SHA-committed, result files contain raw generations and solver code sufficient to re-check scoring without the proprietary engine.

## Methodology

The paper is framed as an industry experience report around a closed engine, so methodology is described at the input-output and invariant level rather than at the mechanism level.

**Exactness guarantee (Taliesin).** Given a frozen model θ with fixed numerics producing logit vector ℓ_fresh and KV state S at a chosen position, a graft installs a previously captured S into a fresh context producing ℓ_graft. The graft is *byte-exact* when SHA-256(bytes(ℓ_graft)) = SHA-256(bytes(ℓ_fresh)), which implies D_KL(p_graft ‖ p_fresh) = 0 and argmax agreement. The guarantee is scoped to **own-position restoration**: a block captured as a prefix at positions [0, N) is restored at [0, N) and new queries are computed fresh on top at [N, N+q). Determinism is pinned via `GGML_DETERMINISTIC=1` and `CUBLAS_WORKSPACE_CONFIG=:4096:8`, under which two back-to-back fresh computations are themselves bit-identical (self-comparison floor KL = 0).

**Flywheel protocol (Galahad).** For a problem the frozen model cannot reliably solve: (i) solve with extra inference effort, (ii) verify by a sound external check (executing the model's generated program and confirming it prints the known AIME answer, or matching LiveBench ground truth), (iii) deposit the verified solution's KV state as a persistent block. Retrieval is split into two regimes that are measured separately and never conflated: **recurrence** (same problem returns; exact lookup reads the stored verified answer) and **transfer** (new problem of the same structure; constants swapped and re-verified for AIME; temporally held-out split with zero question-id overlap for LiveBench; a one-shot classification routes to the relevant block). An early flat-prefix design that held all cached methods at once was abandoned because the model grabbed the wrong entry; the reported system routes to a single block per query.

**Setup.** Local proofs on Windows 11 with an RTX 5090 (Blackwell sm_120, 32 GB), CUDA 13.2, realmerlin-v3.4 backend. Scale-up on rented H100 SXM (Hopper sm_90) and B200 (Blackwell sm_100) with a frozen Gemma-4-31B. Energy by integrating board power at 0.5-2 Hz with idle (19.3 W) subtracted. Anchors are vendor no-tools model-card scores: Gemma-4-12B 77.5%, Gemma-4-31B 89.2% (both on AIME 2026), Qwen3.6-35B-A3B 92.7%; the paper's own eval is AIME 2025, so these are nearest-published reference anchors, not same-benchmark head-to-head baselines.

## Results & Highlights

- **Byte-exact graft verified at multiple levels.** On a real Gemma-4-12B under the deterministic configuration, 50 samples show median KL = 0 and p99 KL = 0 between grafted and fresh logits, with 0/50 argmax disagreements and 0/50 graft failures; SHA-256 byte equality passes on 5/5 trials; Mann-Whitney p = 1.0000 with Cliff's delta = 0.0000. On the B200 replay this holds with 10/10 SHA trials.
- **Cost engine: 85.6x prefill subsidy.** Cold prefill of an 11,994-token prompt takes 1,547.3 ms; grafting the cached state and advancing one token takes 18.1 ms (85.6x speedup, lossless). A separate fresh-prefix measurement gives 83.7x (1,087 ms → 13 ms on a 5,569-token prefix).
- **Context widening 87.1x at zero extra VRAM.** A 2,854,766-token store (88 blocks, 40.6 GB on disk) is built on a 32k-slot 32 GB card; peak memory stays at 25,595 MB (equal to base serving); 7/7 needles are read correctly at depths 0, 334k, 694k, 1.41M, 2.13M, 2.49M, 2.82M; restore cost is constant at ~0.29 s plus ~35 prefilled tokens regardless of depth.
- **AIME 2025 capability lift.** Frozen Gemma-4-12B alone: 80.0% (24/30) under its best reasoning-plus-verification configuration, already above its 77.5% model-card anchor; with the grafted verified library it reaches 93.3% (28/30), clearing the 31B's 89.2% anchor. A lean code-routing configuration reaches 90.0% (27/30) at 4,360 decode tokens/problem and 7.0 Wh/problem (~0.002 euro/problem), roughly 50x cheaper per capability point than sampling-and-voting (76.7% at ~25,000 tokens).
- **Recurrence cost reduction.** The 8 problems the base model never solves across a 401,026-token best-of-5 budget are all answered correctly from cached blocks in 61 total decode tokens (7.6/problem), 2.8 s wall time, ~0.053 Wh integrated (conservative bound 0.145 Wh) — a 6,574x token reduction, ~1,700x faster, and roughly 3,000x-8,700x less energy (range reported because the run sits at the sampler's resolution limit).
- **Held-out transfer.** On 7 independently verified same-structure variants, the frozen 12B routes 7/7 correctly and solves 5/7; on a rented H100 the frozen 31B reaches held-out transfer 7/7, recurrence 8/8, and a full-30 flywheel-system score of 30/30 = 100% for ~8-12 euros total cloud spend.
- **LiveBench transfer.** Single-block routing on a temporally held-out split gives 71.7% (43/60); merging all blocks graft drops it to 56.7%, confirming the routing lesson one tier up.
- **Portability.** The 8 AIME blocks copied H100-to-H100 have matching SHA-256 digests and graft/function 8/8 on the fresh pod with no re-deposit.
- **Router and paging behavior.** In-library routing correct on 15/15 (Wilson 95% CI ~79.6%-100%); off-distribution queries abstain 4/4. Disk-to-accelerator restore (57.7-85.8 ms) grows sublinearly while re-prefill (96.1-748.3 ms) grows roughly linearly, so the subsidy *widens* with context length (1.7x at 748 tokens to 8.7x at 5,977 tokens).

## Authors & Credibility

Sole author Sietse Schelpe (Corbenic AI), who states he is the inventor of the Merlin engine, the Taliesin graft mechanism, and the Galahad flywheel, and that he conceived, built, ran, analyzed, and wrote everything personally. The paper explicitly follows the industry-experience-report convention (citing Spanner/TAO Facebook papers) and discloses up front that the engine internals (state capture, state restore, storage layout, deduplication/hashing) are proprietary and described only at the input-output level. Credibility is propped up by SHA-256 commitments of all inputs (before each run) and outputs (on exit), pre-registration of the B200 replay, and the inclusion of per-problem raw generations and solver code in result files so scoring can be re-checked without the engine. Credibility is undercut by single-authorship, the closed engine, the absence of any from-scratch bare-model re-run on the scale-up hardware, and the use of future-dated "Gemma 4" / "Qwen3.6" model cards that a reader cannot independently verify in the public record at the time of reading.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The positioning is careful and largely defensible: prior KV reuse is either exact-but-ephemeral (vLLM/PagedAttention native prompt cache, RadixAttention, Prompt Cache) or persistent-but-lossy (CacheBlend, RAG token injection), and the concurrent FCGRAFT borrows the "graft" term but only at a semantic/functional level without the byte-exact guarantee. The genuinely novel claim is the combination of byte-exact *plus* persistent *plus* portable *plus* used as an inference-time learning substrate — and the numerical proof (Section 4.2) that own-position is the *unique* exact point on any engine for 32-bit RoPE.
- **Rigor.** Strong on the exactness side: adversarial elimination of seven candidate explanations for the positional residual, a graft-free control, a pre-registered cross-architecture replay with a falsifiable prediction, and honest reporting of a magnitude-miss (predicted 1.4e-2 on B200, measured 7e-4, 19x smaller). The transfer/recurrence distinction is maintained throughout and the paper repeatedly refuses to let the 100% / 93.3% system numbers stand in for the 7/7 / 5/7 generalization numbers.
- **Reproducibility.** Auditable but not open. Readers can re-check scoring from committed hashes and bundled solver code without the engine, but cannot reproduce generation without the closed Merlin/Taliesin suite. The byte-exactness claim is tied to a specific pinned configuration and is within-architecture only; a block is not guaranteed byte-identical across architectures that accumulate floating point differently (H100 was validated functionally, not at byte level).
- **Significance.** If the mechanism is real and independently reproducible, the economic argument (verified knowledge as a one-time deposit versus per-query sampling cost; capability bought ~50x cheaper per point) is consequential. Significance is dampened by the closed engine, the small absolute benchmark size (30 AIME problems; transfer 5/7 and 7/7), the single-author provenance, and the fact that the headline capability comparison uses AIME 2026 model-card anchors against an AIME 2025 eval rather than same-benchmark head-to-head runs — a framing the paper discloses but that still flatters the result.
- **Red flags.** (1) The paper is built on "Gemma-4-12B/31B" and "Qwen3.6-35B-A3B" model cards that do not correspond to any publicly verifiable model at the stated retrieval date, which makes the 77.5% / 89.2% / 92.7% anchors unfalsifiable by a reader. (2) The "8 of 8 recurrence in 61 tokens" cost story is, by the paper's own admission, the recurrence regime — effectively a verified-answer cache lookup — and the authors correctly caution that the capability claim rests on transfer, not recurrence; a casual reader could still misread the 6,574x headline as a capability gain. (3) No confidence gate sits between graft and answer, so a confident-wrong graft is structurally possible even though none occurred at the 15-case sample size. (4) LiveBench recurrence is weak (5/20) for multi-exemplar blocks, showing the mechanism is not uniformly strong across block types.

## Strengths

- Intellectually honest about boundaries: explicitly withdraws the byte-exact positional-composition framing after measuring it, reports a pre-registered magnitude-miss on B200, and separates recurrence (recall) from transfer (generalization) at every turn.
- The Section 4.2 analysis — using a graft-free control to show the relocation residual is the base model's own position-sensitivity, not a graft error — is the kind of adversarial self-test that raises the bar for any future KV-reuse claim.
- Concrete, well-instrumented cost numbers (85.6x prefill subsidy, 6,574x token reduction, 87.1x context widening at zero extra VRAM, ~8-12 euro full 31B run) with the measurement caveats (sampler resolution, two-slot server, OS page cache not force-dropped) recorded alongside.
- Systems questions (router misrouting, disk paging) are answered with measurements rather than assertions, and the off-distribution abstention behavior is a genuinely useful property.
- Pre-registration of the B200 replay with a falsifiable prediction is a credibility marker rare in this subfield.

## Limitations

- The graft engine (Taliesin) and the underlying deduplication machinery (Merlin) are proprietary; generation cannot be reproduced without the closed suite, only scored.
- Byte-exactness is within-architecture and within-configuration; cross-architecture byte equality is not guaranteed, and Hopper was validated only functionally, not at byte level.
- Headline capability comparisons rely on AIME 2026 vendor anchors versus an AIME 2025 self-eval, not same-benchmark head-to-heads, and on "Gemma 4" / "Qwen3.6" model cards that are not independently verifiable.
- Transfer succeeds only when the cached program is parametric in the swapped quantities (5/7 at 12B; the two failures involve a baked-in sqrt(185) and a structural range(4) constant); the clean generalization number is small.
- Recurrence is weak for complex multi-exemplar LiveBench blocks (5/20) versus one-solution AIME blocks (8/8).
- The router has no confidence gate between graft and answer; confident-wrong grafts are structurally possible at larger sample sizes.
- The flywheel is null where the model is not already failing (216/216 saturation), and a cached procedure can *reduce* accuracy by 55 points when it re-prescribes a method the model already executes better.
- All scale-up measurements come from a single author on rented hardware with no third-party replication.

## Relevance

直接相关于推理服务与 KV 缓存优化方向：字节级精确、可持久化、跨机可移植的 KV 嫁接若成立，可作为 RAG、长上下文与 agent 技能复用的低成本替代方案，对推理时学习与可控知识注入有方法学参考价值；但需警惕其依赖闭源引擎且核心模型/锚点目前无法独立核验。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the byte-exactness claim be reproduced by an independent implementation on an open engine (vLLM, llama.cpp, SGLang) under the same pinned deterministic configuration, or does it depend on undocumented properties of the Merlin runtime?
- What is the actual behavior on a same-benchmark head-to-head (AIME 2025 anchors vs AIME 2025 eval) rather than AIME 2026 model-card anchors against AIME 2025?
- How does transfer accuracy scale beyond 7 variants — does the parametric-vs-baked-in-constant boundary remain the dominant failure mode, or do new failure classes appear at n=50/100?
- Could a lightweight confidence gate between graft and answer eliminate the structurally-possible confident-wrong graft without sacrificing the abstention-on-off-distribution property?
- Is the 32-bit non-associativity ceiling (the ~0.015 KL residual on relocation) actually irreducible in practice, or could bfloat16 → fp32 mid-precision kernels or a different RoPE implementation recover exactness at non-own positions?
- How does the mechanism interact with quantized serving (the paper notes block sizes depend on KV quantization) — does INT4/INT8 KV break byte-exactness, and if so what is the quantized operating point?
- Does the "cache only what the model lacks" lesson generalize beyond AIME/LiveBench to open-domain tasks where distinguishing "lacks" from "already knows" is itself hard?
