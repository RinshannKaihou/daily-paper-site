Across all six credited benchmarks, sealed-test gains retain 86–147% of the training lift — a positive result against a literature that documents overfitting.
Why this matters

It shows how to credibly auto-improve an agent's scaffolding around a frozen model — with paired-2σ crediting and a once-scored held-out test — directly relevant to anyone building, deploying, or benchmarking LLM agents.

→ transferable: the pathology-keyed QD search & the "propose / never-credit" split
Critical edge

Paired-2σ certifies a gain is real on the measured metric — not that the metric itself is valid. A gameable or invalid per-task verifier is inherited wholesale.

verifier dependence — the central stated caveat
01 Methodology — propose, gate, then credit; the model never judges

kernel Harness vs. kernel

The agent is a frozen model M wrapped in a harness H (prompt, knowledge, runtime, config). A kernel — measurement code, the evolution machinery, the product contract — is never touched; evolution searches only the mutable surface of four coarsened levers. Each edit carries a two-axis coordinate: where (mechanical, from the file) × why (diagnosed pathology).

loop Proposer / creditor split

A separate stronger evolver D (Claude Opus 4.8) diagnoses failures and writes patches; the frozen agent is never modified by hand. Deterministic code owns all sampling, the three gates, the paired significance test, and bookkeeping. Diagnosis proposes; measurement disposes.

funnel Cost-graded funnel

Each round: evolver designs 3–4 env-gated candidates, then a multi-stage screen culls before the full paired test.

preflight (0 inference)→ probe K=1 · 1.5σ→ estimator K=3→ full K=3 paired

GSME Gated Semantic MAP-Elites

A categorical quality-diversity archive keyed on (where × why) pathology, not task identity — an anti-overfitting inductive bias. One gated elite per cell; selection is quality-biased (extends the global best) with cross-cell recombination. Substrate is a git tree: every candidate is a branch+commit with a per-node ledger.

gates Three gates, in increasing cost

1 · Validity — environment health pre-check (sandbox/network/deps/verifier); infra failures re-run twice, then scored 0 but kept in the denominator.

2 · Activation — each mechanism emits an activation beacon; trials where it never fired are not attributed to it.

3 · Significance — a credited gain needs a paired per-task 2σ test (z ≥ 1.96) on the same task set; pairing strips between-task difficulty variance. The headline number is always the full-set paired test, never a subset. The sealed test is withheld from all decisions and scored once; retention = held-out lift ÷ training lift.

CROSS-MODEL MATCHING LAW

On AppWorld, the winning patch tracks each model's dominant pathology, not its family: 27B → verify-finalize (+15.5pp); Qwen3.5-397B → submit-verify (+13.6); Gemini 3 Flash → submit-verify (+13.5, z=4.15) — reproduced across two families. Off-diagonal patches are near-zero.

SELECTIVE > BRUTE FORCE

LiveCode, K=3: vanilla 64.6 → +131K-token budget 71.1 → global thinking-off toggle 73.2 → selective sticky recovery 79.4. A 16× budget and a one-line global toggle both fail to match the targeted recovery's +14.8pp.

RECOMBINATION & EFFICIENCY

On 4 of 6 domains the credited harness is a cross-cell recombination (BrowseComp+: VF+RBR +13.9 beats either alone). Tree-aware score borrowing (Theorem 1) reproduces every credited decision at 67% fewer child trials (offline 14/14, live 7/7).

03 Strengths & limitations — two-sided read

◆ Strengths

  • Statistically credible crediting — paired per-task 2σ tests plus a sealed, once-scored held-out set, well above the field's "peak pass@K over rounds" norm.
  • Anti-overfitting by design — pathology-keyed QD archive + retention reporting directly target the failure modes the paper criticizes in prior work.
  • Intellectual honesty — SWE-bench downgraded at n=26, a negative candidate published, the verifier caveat stated plainly.
  • Clean transfer ablation — the cross-model dissociation isolates what transfers (the loop) from what doesn't (any one harness).
  • Resists greedy collapse — accepted edits span all four levers, not just prompt tweaks.

◆ Limitations

  • Verifier dependence — the guarantee is relative to the reward; a gameable checker is inherited, and the method needs a reliable per-task verifier to exist.
  • Short-horizon proxy — per-task success ignores fuzzy goals (taste, novelty) and long-term qualities (robustness, maintainability).
  • Cost & cadence bound — dense re-scoring makes evolution expensive; needs frequent scored evaluation, so open-ended settings are out of reach.
  • Small held-out sets — SWE-bench n=26, TB2 n≈53 leave retention noisy; test withholding is discipline, not mechanically enforced.
  • Scoped to mid-capability models — frontier models, which fail less, lie outside the claimed benefit.
04 Open questions — for further exploration
Does the pathology→patch matching law hold under model updates (RLHF, continual pre-training) — does a fitted patch decay or transfer?
Can the crediting stack run on weaker / self-generated supervision (no reliable per-task verifier) without losing the paired-2σ guarantee?
Does the ~60% phantom-gain rate of non-statistical loops quantify inflation in published harness-evolution numbers — would they survive re-scoring?
How much of the +9–15.5pp is GSME vs. one high-value runtime patch (thinking-runaway recovery)? Is there a domain won without recombination?
Could tree-aware score borrowing + active learning over the anchor probe make the loop economical at production benchmark scale?
Is the clean off-diagonal dissociation on the Qwen pair a family artifact — would a third non-Qwen, non-Google family still match careless→submit-verify?
