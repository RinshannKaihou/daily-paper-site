01 Methodology

A pure-theory, four-step construction repeated across examples. Pick a scenario + a strictly proper loss, then read off the epistemic (variance) and aleatoric (generalized entropy) terms that the loss induces.

STEP 01

Fix the scenario

Convex distribution class P, hypothesis class H⊆P, model qθ with posterior q(θ|D), true p.

STEP 02

Choose a strictly proper loss

Log, squared, Brier, or pseudo-spherical. This induces generator ϕ = −Hℓ and a Bregman divergence Bϕ.

STEP 03

Apply Theorem 2

Reverse bias–variance–entropy split of expected subjective risk into bias, variance, generalized entropy.

STEP 04

Read off A / E terms

Variance = epistemic, generalized entropy = aleatoric. Match them to previously published measures.

03 Strengths & limitations

Strengths

  • One mechanism, six families. Reverse bias–variance–entropy provably generates prior A/E measures — a real consolidation, not a survey.
  • Decision-theoretic clarity. Loss generator curvature near the simplex boundary explains why entropy-, variance-, and divergence-based measures disagree.
  • MI reframed. MI is a lower bound on subjective estimation error, not a flaw — relocates Jimenez et al.'s "incompleteness" to hypothesis-class restriction.
  • Honest positioning. Differences vs Bickford-Smith (2025), Fishkov (2025), Jimenez (2026) are spelled out, not glossed.

Limitations

  • No experiments. No real datasets; no comparison to MC dropout, Deep Ensembles, Laplace, evidential on calibration / OOD / selective prediction.
  • Bias term needs p. Bϕ(q̄, p) requires the true distribution — estimation is unaddressed.
  • Continuous case unresolved. Differential-entropy / reference-measure dependence is flagged, not handled.
  • Learning theory stops short. Defines analogues but gives no sample-complexity or generalization bounds.
  • Loss geometry not ablated. Claims about induced epistemic term vs loss choice are qualitative only.

04 Questions for further exploration

  1. Can the bias term Bϕ(q̄, p) be estimated (held-out data, density-ratio matching, RLHF-style reference model) so the full decomposition is computable when p is unknown?
  2. Does Corollary 6 predict measurable MI-vs-full-estimation-error gaps on real OOD / selective-prediction benchmarks?
  3. How does loss choice (log vs Brier vs pseudo-spherical, varying β) move downstream ECE, Brier, AUROC when the induced terms are used as confidence?
  4. Can the learning-theoretic extension reach actual sample-complexity bounds on subjective estimation error, à la Bach (2024)?
  5. For LLMs: does reverse-KL subjective risk correlate with hallucination more strongly than forward-KL entropy — as the motivation suggests but never tests?
  6. Can the Bregman law of total variance decompose multi-source uncertainties (prompt vs parameter vs retrieval) in RAG?
