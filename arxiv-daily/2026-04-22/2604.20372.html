AI models of unstable flow exhibit hallucination

arXiv: 2604.20372v1

Date: 2026-04-22

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20372v1

一句话总结： 在黏性指进（Saffman–Taylor 不稳定流）建模任务上，作者首次系统证实 ViT 与 DAE-LSTM 会产生"视觉合理但违反守恒律"的幻觉（黑岛、反扩散）并将其归因于神经算子的频谱偏差；提出 DeepFingers（DeepONet + U-FNO）恢复多尺度能量均衡以抑制幻觉。
TL;DR: First systematic report of hallucination in AI models of hydrodynamically unstable flow. ViT and DAE-LSTM produce physically implausible artifacts (spurious interfaces, reverse diffusion) while appearing visually correct; wavelet-based spectral analysis shows ViT overestimates high modes and DAE-LSTM is M-dependent. The proposed DeepFingers architecture (FNO branch + FC trunk + two U-FNO layers) balances multiscale spectral content and matches DNS across viscosity ratios M=13,28,55.

核心贡献

  • 首次在流体动力学 AI 模型中系统记录"幻觉"现象并与 LLM 幻觉建立类比。
  • 用小波频谱分析量化神经算子的频谱偏差（ViT 过估高频，DAE-LSTM 在高 M 下反之）。
  • 提出 DeepFingers（DeepONet + U-FNO）架构在全频谱保持能量平衡。

Key Contributions

  • Identification of "AI hallucination" in scientific ML with physically defined diagnostics (conservation-law violations) rather than visual judgment.
  • Tracing hallucination origin to spectral bias of neural operators at high flow rate / viscosity contrast.
  • DeepFingers framework that combines Fourier Neural Operator branch, FC trunk, and nested U-FNO U-Net layers.
  • Benchmark against DAE-LSTM and ViT with wavelet-mode and radar-plot spectral energy comparisons.

Methodology

Trains each model to predict the next concentration field c(x,t+1) from c(x,t), conditioned on viscosity ratio M and time. Compares with DNS reference over M = 13, 28, 55 across t = 15..134. Uses seven wavelet modes for non-stationary spectral analysis. Hallucinations are flagged as regions that cannot be explained by known DNS/numerical artifacts.

Results & Highlights

  • ViT injects nonphysical "black islands" at later times; DAE-LSTM diffuses finger tips and produces ghost yellow patches early.
  • DeepFingers closely tracks DNS in tip splitting, finger merging, channeling at M=55.
  • Spectral radar plots show ViT over-amplifies modes 2-6; DAE-LSTM's high modes collapse for M in {28,55}; DeepFingers aligns across all modes.
  • Mean dissipation rate and global mixing metrics better preserved by DeepFingers.

Authors & Credibility

Two authors from USC Chemical Engineering & Materials Science. Birendra Jha (corresponding) publishes in porous-media flow and ML for subsurface. Credibility moderate — small group; first systematic framing but the "hallucination" framing is rhetorical.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Framing is novel; the architecture is an incremental composition of existing operators (DeepONet + FNO + U-Net).
  • Rigor: Physics-based diagnostics are appropriate; wavelet analysis is a reasonable choice. However, only three viscosity ratios tested.
  • Reproducibility: Architecture described; no code link visible in the fetched pages.
  • Significance: The "hallucination = spectral bias" thesis is well-established in neural-operator literature (Kovachki et al., others); novelty lies in domain application and explicit labeling.
  • Red flags: The analogy to LLM hallucination is loose — the mechanisms differ. Quantitative comparison to recent spectral-aware operators (CViT, spectral conformers) is discussed but not benchmarked. No uncertainty bars on spectral modes.

Strengths

  • Concrete physical failure definition ("violates conservation") rather than visual judgment.
  • Clear visual grids and spectral plots that communicate the failure mode.
  • DeepFingers delivers noticeable multiscale balance improvement.

Limitations

  • Single instability (Saffman-Taylor / viscous fingering); unclear generality.
  • Baselines (DAE-LSTM, ViT) are generic; no comparison against domain-specialized CViT or FNO-Plus.
  • One random seed apparent; no uncertainty quantification beyond mean spectra.
  • Extrapolation to higher M or longer horizons untested.
