On the Quantization Robustness of Diffusion Language Models in Coding Benchmarks

arXiv: 2604.20079v1

Date: 2026-04-22

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20079v1

一句话总结： 在统一评测流水线下，对扩散式语言模型（CoDA 1.7B）应用 GPTQ 与改良 HAWQ 量化后，其在 HumanEval/MBPP 上的精度损失显著小于自回归对照（Qwen3 1.7B），尤其在 2–4 bit 极低位宽下鲁棒性更佳。
TL;DR: Under a controlled PTQ evaluation pipeline, the diffusion LLM CoDA 1.7B degrades less than its autoregressive counterpart Qwen3 1.7B at 2–4 bit GPTQ on coding benchmarks, and a modified Hessian-aware mixed precision (HAWQ) yields smooth accuracy/latency/memory Pareto tradeoffs; the authors caveat that training data and paradigm differences confound the effect.

核心贡献

  • 首个在标准化流水线下比较 d-LLM 与 AR-LLM 在 PTQ 下鲁棒性的实证研究。
  • 发现 CoDA（扩散）在 4-bit 时 HumanEval/MBPP 精度仅下降 1.8–5 点，而 Qwen3（自回归）下降 12–23.2 点。
  • 将 HAWQ 算法适配到 1.7B 级模型（稀疏采样、有限差分近似），给出可用的 16/8、8/4 混合精度方案。

Key Contributions

  • Standardized PTQ comparison between a diffusion LLM (CoDA) and autoregressive LLM (Qwen3) of the same 1.7B scale on HumanEval/MBPP.
  • Empirical evidence that CoDA retains accuracy down to 4-bit, while Qwen3 collapses at 3-bit and degrades sharply at 4-bit.
  • Adaptation of HAWQ (power iteration + sparse sampling + finite-difference Hv) to 1.7B-parameter decoder models, producing smoother accuracy/memory tradeoffs.

Methodology

Applies GPTQ (group size 128, bit widths 2/3/4/8) and a custom HAWQ mixed-precision assignment. HAWQ computes per-block Hessian eigenvalues via power iteration on a subsampled WikiText calibration set and assigns 16/8/4-bit based on sensitivity rank. Evaluated with the Language Model Evaluation Harness on HumanEval(+Instruct/Plus) and MBPP(+Instruct/Plus). Latency is measured on an NVIDIA L40S for a single denoising step (CoDA) or single-token AR step (Qwen3).

Results & Highlights

  • 4-bit CoDA: HumanEval 0.421 / MBPP 0.574 vs 16-bit 0.439 / 0.619.
  • 4-bit Qwen3: HumanEval 0.409 / MBPP 0.503 vs 16-bit 0.628 / 0.664 — sharp drop.
  • 3-bit Qwen3 collapses to 0; 3-bit CoDA retains some function.
  • HAWQ 50/50 splits (16/8 and 8/4) closely track the Pareto frontier; complex 3-way splits cause collapse without QAT.
  • At low bitwidths, quantized CoDA latency becomes competitive with or exceeds Qwen3 because system overheads dominate once arithmetic cost shrinks.

Authors & Credibility

Three authors (Aarav Gupta undergraduate, Gururaj Deshpande and Chandreyi Chakraborty graduate students) at Georgia Tech College of Computing; "equal contribution." Code is on GitHub. No senior-author listed; appears to be a course/project-scale study. Credibility is moderate — methods are standard, but the absence of a senior researcher or prior publication record suggests limited depth.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Incremental. HAWQ and GPTQ are off-the-shelf; the comparison angle is the main contribution.
  • Rigor: Controlled pipeline is a plus, but confounds are acknowledged: Qwen3 was trained on 36T tokens vs CoDA's 180B, so CoDA may simply be under-trained and thus less "over-committed" (cf. Kumar et al. 2024 on PTQ degradation of over-trained models). The authors rightly flag this.
  • Reproducibility: Code released; calibration set (WikiText) and hyperparameters documented.
  • Significance: Conclusion "diffusion LLMs are more quantization-robust" is probably overclaimed — a single model pair cannot support a paradigm-level claim, and the training-token gap is a massive confounder.
  • Red flags: Only one pair of models; weak baselines (no comparison to AWQ, SmoothQuant, DLLMQuant beyond citation); Qwen3 3-bit collapse to 0 is suspicious and may reflect implementation issues (e.g., Marlin kernel availability) rather than intrinsic sensitivity.

Strengths

  • Honest confound discussion in the Introduction.
  • Practical HAWQ adaptation to 1.7B scale with runtime budget (~2 h on H100).
  • Clear tables and Pareto plots.

Limitations

  • One model pair; no scaling study.
  • HAWQ's 3-way mixed precision collapsed without QAT — unresolved.
  • Coding benchmarks only; no generation quality or long-context test.
  • Calibration on WikiText rather than code data may handicap both models equally but differently.

Relevance

与你关注的量化/精度误差、模型可靠性方向高度契合：提供了一个 d-LLM vs AR-LLM 的 PTQ 对比样本，并点出"训练数据饱和度"可能比生成范式更决定 PTQ 鲁棒性——这对理解 silent data corruption 来源有帮助。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Would matching training tokens (same corpus, same scale) preserve the robustness gap?
  • Does CoDA's bidirectional attention distribute sensitivity more uniformly across layers, and can that be measured directly via HAWQ eigenvalue histograms?
  • How do these results shift with activation quantization (W4A4) rather than weight-only?
  • Why does Qwen3 collapse to 0 at 3-bit while CoDA retains partial function — kernel artifact or fundamental?
