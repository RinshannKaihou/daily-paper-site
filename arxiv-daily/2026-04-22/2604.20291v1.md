> **一句话总结：** 本文为 MAI 2026 量化 4K 超分辨率挑战赛提出一个面向部署的 INT8 ×3 SISR 方案：结合 LR 空间的 MobileOne 风格可重参数化骨干、PixelShuffle 重建头、以及"先融合成部署图再做 QAT"的三阶段训练（L1 → Charbonnier+DCT+Mamba 教师蒸馏 → 融合图 QAT + 权重裁剪 + BN 重校准），在仅 82K 参数下达到 29.79 dB PSNR / 0.8634 SSIM（最终分数 1.8）。

> **TL;DR:** A deployment-oriented INT8 ×3 super-resolution pipeline that fuses a MobileOne-style re-parameterizable LR-space backbone with a three-stage training curriculum (L1 → Charbonnier + DCT + confidence-weighted distillation from a MambaIRv2Light teacher → QAT on the already-fused deploy graph with weight clipping and BatchNorm recalibration), reaching 29.79 dB PSNR / 0.8634 SSIM on the MAI 2026 Quantized 4K SR Challenge with only ~82K parameters.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"extract–refine–upsample"紧凑架构：3×3 stem + 8 个 MobileOne 风格重参数化模块（训练时 5 个 3×3 分支 + 1×1 + identity 分支，推理时折叠为单条 3×3 conv）+ 全局特征 skip + PixelShuffle ×3 重建头；所有特征计算留在 LR 空间。
- 提出"Deploy-before-QAT"范式：在 QAT 之前先做 BN 统计重校准并把多分支显式融合为单分支 3×3 图，再在 FX graph-mode + QNNPACK 后端上插入 fake-quant 节点，消除训练与 INT8 部署图的数学不一致。
- 设计三阶段训练课程：Stage 1 用 L1 做稳定基础映射；Stage 2 用 Charbonnier + DCT 频域监督 + 来自 MambaIRv2Light 教师的置信度加权输出级蒸馏（权重 w(p)=clip(exp(-γ·e(p)), 0.10, 0.75)，γ=10）；Stage 3 在融合的部署图上做 QAT（观测器在 30 epoch 冻结、fake-quant 在 90 epoch 冻结，共 150 epoch）。
- MAI 2026 挑战赛测试集上 29.79 dB / 0.8634 SSIM，最终分数 1.8；消融显示 MobileOne 块的 FP32→INT8 下降仅 0.1347 dB，优于 RepConv（0.2405）与 RepDW（0.6552）；教师监督在 Stage 3 将 INT8 PSNR 从 29.91 提升到 30.0003 dB。

## Key Contributions

- Compact LR-space ×3 SR architecture: 3×3 stem + 8 MobileOne-style blocks (five 3×3 branches + 1×1 + identity at training, fused into a single 3×3 conv at inference) + global feature skip + PixelShuffle head; all refinement stays in LR space (C=32).
- "Deploy-before-QAT" strategy: BN recalibration with 64 forward-only mini-batches, explicit multi-branch → single-branch fusion, then QAT via PyTorch FX graph-mode with QNNPACK backend inserted onto the already-fused graph to ensure the simulated fake-quant matches the exported INT8 TFLite graph.
- Three-stage training curriculum: (1) 600 epochs L1 @ LR 10⁻³ / 128² patch; (2) 200 epochs Charbonnier + 0.02·DCT + 0.03·KD @ 3×10⁻⁵ / 160² with EMA 0.999 and gradient clipping; (3) 150 epochs QAT @ 10⁻⁶ / 144² with observers frozen after epoch 30 and fake-quant frozen after epoch 90.
- Confidence-weighted output distillation from a pretrained MambaIRv2Light ×3 teacher, re-weighting KD per pixel by exp(-γ·teacher_error) clipped to [0.10, 0.75].
- Empirical: 29.79 dB PSNR / 0.8634 SSIM on MAI 2026 challenge test set with ~82K parameters and final challenge score 1.8; the MobileOne block incurs only a 0.1347 dB FP32→INT8 drop (vs 0.2405 for RepConv and 0.6552 for depthwise "RepDW"); teacher-guided Stage 3 raises INT8 PSNR from 29.91 to 30.0003 dB.

## Methodology

**Architecture.** Input x ∈ ℝ^(H×W×3) is projected by a 3×3 conv to C=32 channels, followed by N=8 MobileOne-style blocks. Each training-time block sums five 3×3 BN-conv branches, one 1×1 BN-conv branch, and a BN-identity branch, then applies ReLU. After training, each branch is analytically folded into the equivalent (W̃, b̃) using the standard conv-BN fusion W̃ = γ/√(σ²+ε)·W, b̃ = β + γ/√(σ²+ε)·(b−μ), yielding a single-branch 3×3 conv per block. A feature-level skip f = f_N + f_0 is added before a 3×3 projection to 27 channels and a PixelShuffle×3 head.

**Losses.** Stage 1 uses L1 on normalized RGB. Stages 2 and 3 use Charbonnier (ε=10⁻³) + DCT-domain L1 + output-level KD. The KD target is a pretrained MambaIRv2Light ×3 teacher; each pixel's KD term is weighted by w(p) = clip(exp(-γ·e(p)), 0.10, 0.75), with e(p) the teacher's channel-averaged absolute error against the HR ground truth and γ=10. Stage weights: L⁽²⁾ = L_char + 0.02·L_DCT + 0.03·L_KD; L⁽³⁾ = L_char + 0.015·L_DCT + λ_KD(t)·L_KD (schedule for λ_KD not fully specified numerically in the paper).

**Deployment-aware quantization.** Before Stage 3 the model is BN-recalibrated with 64 forward-only mini-batches and fully fused into its single-branch deploy graph. QAT then uses PyTorch FX graph-mode with QNNPACK. The 150-epoch QAT curriculum runs three phases: epochs 0–30 active observers and active fake-quant; epochs 30–90 observers disabled (frozen scale/zero-point) with STE-based weight updates; epochs 90–150 fake-quant also frozen so the model minimizes residual error under the fixed integer grid. Weight clipping W ← clip(W, W_min, W_max) is applied after every optimizer step in Stages 2–3. Export converts NCHW→NHWC and maps fake-quant nodes to native INT8 TFLite ops.

**Data & evaluation.** DIV2K ×3 pairs; random LR patches (128/160/144 for stages 1/2/3) with flips and transpose. PSNR is full-image RGB without border shaving; SSIM uses an 11×11 Gaussian window with σ=1.5. Stage-3 ablations are reported on a dynamic fully-quantized INT8 TFLite artifact; the final submission number uses the fixed-shape deployable INT8 TFLite artifact.

## Results & Highlights

- **Challenge leaderboard (Table 1):** AIO_MAI (theirs) reports FP32 29.98 / 0.8730 and INT8 29.79 / 0.8634, with CPU/GPU/NPU runtimes 610 / 114 / 41.1 ms and final score 1.8. Top teams (AntYSP, AntSR) score 21.8 and 21.5 with INT8 PSNR ~29.96–29.98 but NPU latency of only ~4.3–4.5 ms — i.e. this submission is ~10× slower on the target NPU, which is why its final score lags by an order of magnitude despite comparable PSNR.
- **Baselines (Table 2):** Deployable INT8 TFLite reaches 30.13 dB / 0.858 SSIM vs Bicubic 28.26 / 0.828, FSRCNN 29.45 / 0.838, and INT8 ABPN 30.15 / 0.852. Their FP32 Stage-2 model hits 30.28 / 0.863. They advertise only ~82K parameters.
- **Backbone ablation (Table 3):** MobileOne: FP32 30.1350 / INT8 30.0003 (ΔPSNR 0.1347); RepConv: 30.0897 / 29.8492 (ΔPSNR 0.2405); "RepDW" (their depthwise variant): 29.3583 / 28.7031 (ΔPSNR 0.6552). MobileOne is both best and most quantization-robust.
- **Teacher ablation (Table 4):** Removing the Mamba teacher during Stage 3 drops INT8 PSNR from 30.0003 to 29.9114 dB (-0.089 dB, -0.003 SSIM). The gain is small in absolute terms but the paper argues it is meaningful relative to the challenge threshold.
- **Numerical inconsistency to flag:** Table 2 reports "Ours (Deploy) INT8" at 30.13 dB / 0.858 on DIV2K validation, while Table 1 reports the official MAI test-set INT8 at 29.79 dB / 0.8634. The text does not clearly reconcile why the deployable TFLite performs ~0.34 dB worse on the official test set than on DIV2K validation.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** Low. Every individual component is off-the-shelf: MobileOne re-parameterization, Charbonnier loss, DCT-domain loss (FreqNet), KD from Hinton, confidence-weighted KD (prior work), BN recalibration (Shomron & Weiser), PyTorch FX QAT with QNNPACK, and MambaIRv2 as teacher. The only pipeline-level novelty is the "Deploy-before-QAT" ordering (fuse → recalibrate BN → QAT on the fused graph), which is sensible engineering but is already standard practice in high-quality mobile-SR submissions. The paper is honest that "rather than introducing a new SR primitive, our contribution lies in a deployment-oriented INT8 super-resolution pipeline."

**Rigor.** Mixed. The ablations isolate two things (block choice and teacher) but leave out many: no ablation on the DCT term, no ablation on the Charbonnier-vs-L1 switch in Stage 2, no ablation on the weight-clipping or BN-recalibration steps that the paper claims are important, no ablation on the number of MobileOne branches (5 vs 3) or blocks (N=8), and no ablation on the confidence-weighting clipping bounds or γ. The three-phase QAT schedule (30/90/150) is also reported only once, without sensitivity analysis.

**Reproducibility.** Moderate-to-low. Hyperparameters and architecture are specified numerically, but λ_KD(t) in Stage 3 is written as a function of training step without giving the schedule. No repository link is provided in the paper text. Exact teacher checkpoint ("MambaIRv2Light ×3") is named but not URL-linked. DIV2K and QNNPACK/FX are standard, so reimplementation is feasible with effort.

**Significance.** Narrow. As a MAI 2026 challenge submission the result is respectable (29.79 dB INT8) but the team's final score of 1.8 is ~12× below the leaders. The main transferable insight — "fuse first, QAT second, with BN recalibration in between" — is useful but not surprising. The backbone ablation (MobileOne > RepVGG > depthwise RepDW for INT8 SR) is the most broadly useful datapoint.

**Red flags / overclaiming.**
- The abstract emphasizes 29.79 / 0.8634 and "final score of 1.8" but does not mention that leaderboard winners hit 21.8 on the same score; a casual reader would read this as a strong result rather than a mid-pack one.
- Table 2 compares against very weak baselines (Bicubic, FSRCNN from 2016, ABPN from 2021). The paper does not benchmark against more recent lightweight INT8 SR systems such as QuantSR, DVMSR, or the winning MAI 2022/2025 entries despite citing them.
- The FP32→INT8 gap claims hinge on the "dynamic INT8 TFLite" artifact while the deployable artifact, official test set, and DIV2K validation give noticeably different numbers (30.13 on DIV2K val vs 29.79 on the official MAI test). The paper is not explicit about this distribution of numbers.
- RepDW is introduced and immediately shown to lose badly (ΔPSNR 0.655), which reads like a straw-man to make MobileOne look stronger.
- The "82K parameters" figure appears only in Table 2's caption and is not broken down or cross-checked against a FLOP count or against the runtime profile.

## Strengths

- Clean, engineering-focused description of a full mobile-SR pipeline, end-to-end from training loss to TFLite export.
- The "Deploy-before-QAT" ordering is the right instinct and is explained clearly, including why applying QAT to a multi-branch graph and then fusing afterwards is unstable.
- Honest framing: the paper explicitly says its contribution is pipeline-level, not a new SR primitive.
- Confidence-weighted KD is a reasonable touch given that a Mamba teacher will also make errors on hard patches; the exponential re-weighting limits teacher-induced noise.
- The backbone ablation cleanly quantifies FP32→INT8 degradation per block type, which is useful evidence for the "MobileOne for INT8 SR" design choice.
- Full-image RGB PSNR with explicit SSIM window choice is more faithful to deployed behavior than luminance-only or cropped evaluation.

## Limitations

- Only ~0.09 dB absolute INT8 improvement is attributed to the Mamba teacher — not obviously worth the training cost of a separate MambaIRv2Light forward pass.
- No latency-vs-accuracy Pareto curve; the NPU runtime (41.1 ms) is ~10× the leaders', suggesting the graph is not well-tuned for the MAI target NPU despite all the deployment-focused framing.
- Missing ablations on DCT loss, weight clipping, BN recalibration, QAT phase boundaries, γ, and branch count.
- No comparison against SR-specific quantization methods (QuantSR, Tu et al. PTQ) that are explicitly cited in related work.
- Generalization untested: all results are DIV2K / MAI test set only; no Set5/14, BSD100, Urban100, or Manga109 to situate the method against the broader SR literature.
- λ_KD(t) schedule for Stage 3 is left abstract; this may meaningfully affect reproducibility.
- No reported confidence intervals or seed variance — differences of 0.09 dB PSNR without variance are weak evidence.

## Relevance

与你的兴趣部分相关：这是 INT8 量化部署（TFLite/QNNPACK）在 SR 模型上的实证案例，关注 train-deploy 数学一致性（"先融合再 QAT"）和 BN 重校准，属于"量化/精度误差"和"ML 可靠性"方向的应用实践；但它不涉及 LLM 训练稳定性、hidden states 可解释性或 silent data corruption，与你的核心兴趣（LLM observability、推理评估）只有方法论上的间接联系。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the MobileOne-vs-RepConv INT8 robustness gap generalize beyond ×3 SR — is it the extra branches, the identity path, or the BN placement that actually helps quantization?
- Why does the deployable INT8 artifact score 30.13 on DIV2K validation but only 29.79 on the official MAI test set? Is this distribution shift, a different evaluation protocol, or a different export path?
- Would per-channel or mixed-precision quantization (e.g., keeping the PixelShuffle-adjacent projection in INT16) close the remaining FP32→INT8 gap more than the teacher does?
- How does the three-phase QAT schedule (observer freeze at 30, fake-quant freeze at 90) compare to a simple single-phase QAT of the same total length? No ablation is provided.
- The Mamba-teacher gain is only ~0.09 dB — would a cheaper teacher (e.g., a larger MobileOne student, or an ensemble of the Stage-2 FP32 checkpoint with test-time augmentation) deliver comparable gain without the Mamba dependency?
- Why does the NPU runtime (41.1 ms) lag leaders by ~10× despite the claimed deployment-friendly single-branch 3×3 graph? Is it the PixelShuffle, the number of blocks, or the NHWC layout conversion?
