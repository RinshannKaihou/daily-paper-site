DialToM: A Theory of Mind Benchmark for Forecasting State-Driven Dialogue Trajectories

arXiv: 2604.20443v1

Date: 2026-04-22

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20443v1

一句话总结： DialToM 是一个基于真实高风险多轮对话（咨询、情感支持、劝说）构建的 Theory-of-Mind 基准，发现除 Gemini 3 Pro 之外的主流大模型虽然能准确推断心理状态（Literal ToM），却几乎无法把这些状态用于预测未来对话轨迹（Functional ToM），揭示出"会贴标签但不会用"的系统性推理断裂。
TL;DR: DialToM is a human-verified ToM benchmark built from naturalistic high-stakes human-human dialogues (counseling, emotional support, persuasion) that decouples mental-state inference (Literal ToM) from state-driven trajectory forecasting (Functional ToM). Almost all tested LLMs ace the retrospective task (CR ≥ 80%) yet collapse on the prospective task (many near random, ≤ 25%); only Gemini 3 Pro sustains ~83% across domains, and counterfactual NOTA probes suggest it is genuine reasoning rather than memorization.

核心贡献

  • 提出 Functional ToM 这一新评测维度：不仅评估模型能否正确推断 BDIEK + 新增的 Trust（信任）六维心理状态，更评估模型能否在移除对话上下文的条件下、仅凭心理状态画像选出状态一致的未来对话轨迹（Prospective Diagnostic Forecasting）。
  • 构建基于真实高风险对话的 DialToM 数据集：来源于 AnnoMI（动机访谈）、ESConv（情感支持）、PersuasionForGood（劝说）三大人对人真实对话语料，避开了传统合成/模板化数据的伪相关捷径；总计约 36k 条 Retrospective 和约 6k 条 Prospective 问题，配有 900 条人工金标开放式心理状态参考答案。
  • 把 Trust 纳入心理状态体系：基于 Mayer et al. 的"能力-善意-正直"信任三要素，将 Trust 作为与 Belief/Desire/Intention/Emotion/Knowledge 并列的维度，填补了现有 BDIEK 基准中关系性维度的缺失。
  • 严格的人工验证流水线：22 位专业标注员（Baidu 平台）+ 9 位 AMT 持大师资格的开放式标注员，用 Dawid-Skene 聚合和 Gwet's AC1 做质量控制，最终保留 Retrospective ~83%、Prospective ~54%。
  • 反事实 NOTA 消融：用 GPT-4o 生成与事实明显相反的心理状态替换正确状态，检验模型是否会选"以上都不是"，以区分真实推理与记忆/模式匹配——只有 Gemini 3 Pro 在反事实下表现强劲（~80-95% NOTA 选择率）。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces a two-axis evaluation separating Literal ToM (retrospective mental-state inference from dialogue history) from Functional ToM (prospective forecasting of state-consistent dialogue trajectories given ONLY the mental-state profile, not the conversation).
  • Releases DialToM: ~36k retrospective MCQs + ~6k prospective MCQs + 900 open-ended gold mental-state references over 5,943 contexts drawn from three naturalistic high-stakes dialogue corpora (MI, ESC, PFG).
  • Extends the classic BDIEK mental-state schema with a novel Trust (T) attribute grounded in Mayer et al.'s ability/benevolence/integrity framework, addressing a gap in ToM benchmarks that ignore relational trust.
  • Benchmarks 13 LLMs spanning 12B–1T params and proprietary/open, reasoning/non-reasoning — including Gemini 2.5/3, GPT-4.1/5, GPT-OSS 120B, Qwen3 235B, Kimi K2, DeepSeek V3, Llama 3.1/3.3/4, Mistral Nemo/24B.
  • Demonstrates a counterfactual (NOTA) ablation and an easy-vs-hard distractor ablation to falsify memorization and lexical-shortcut explanations, isolating Gemini 3 Pro as the one model with robust functional ToM.

Methodology

  • Data curation: Segments sessions from AnnoMI (133 MI sessions), ESConv (1300), PersuasionForGood (1017) into 150–200-word context windows to normalize density, yielding 5,943 contexts. Two roles per dialogue (initiator vs. recipient); inferences are taken from a third-person perspective on the recipient.
  • Mental-state inference generation: GPT-4o acts as the "strong LLM" producing BDIEKT inferences per context.
  • Task construction (all MCQ):
  1. Retrospective Inference — given context, choose correct mental state; one correct + three GPT-4o-generated plausible distractors per BDIEKT attribute (6 Qs/context).
  2. Prospective Diagnostic Forecasting — given ONLY the full BDIEKT mental-state profile (NO conversation history), pick the actual continuation from three semantically plausible distractor continuations (1 Q/context).
  3. Semantic Comparison — free-form mental state generation compared against 3 human gold references via BLEU, ROUGE-L, BERTScore.
  • Human-in-the-loop verification: 22 Professional Verifiers (Baidu) validate mental-state correctness and trajectory logical consistency; 9 AMT Lay Participants with Master qualification author open-ended gold references; Dawid-Skene aggregation + Gwet's AC1 (0.88–0.92 retrospective, 0.45–0.68 prospective).
  • Prompting: zero-shot, no reasoning scaffold, via OpenRouter for most models; Gemini/OpenAI through official APIs.
  • Metrics: Correctness Rate (CR) for MCQ; BLEU/ROUGE-L/BERTScore vs. gold for open-ended.
  • Ablations: (1) Easy set replacing semantic distractors with randomly sampled conversation subcontexts to test whether failures are lexical-shortcut failures; (2) Counterfactual NOTA intervention replacing the true mental-state profile S with a contradictory S′ to test memorization vs. reasoning.

Results & Highlights

  • Performance asymmetry is dramatic. Retrospective CR is ≥ 80% for most models (DeepSeek V3 ~98%, Gemini 2.5 Flash ~96%, GPT-5 ~92%), while Prospective CR collapses: Llama 8B 6.6–16.75%, Mistral Nemo 6.6–14.3%, GPT-5 14.7–16.85%, Qwen3 235B 17.7–29%, DeepSeek V3 ~10–12%. Multiple frontier models are at or below the 25% random baseline.
  • Gemini 3 Pro is a striking outlier, scoring 83.09/80.46/86.96% on prospective MI/ESC/PFG — roughly 4–5× the next tier.
  • Humans are NOT super at retrospective ToM either — lay CR is 24–33%, suggesting high-stakes naturalistic dialogues are linguistically demanding for non-experts (and that LLM "superhuman" scores reflect lay-annotator fatigue more than cognition).
  • Trust is the weakest attribute for nearly every LLM (e.g. Qwen3 235B 87.98% on Trust vs. 99.35% on Intention), supporting the authors' claim that relational reasoning is under-modeled.
  • Low human-reference BLEU (5–9) and ROUGE-L (~0.3) with moderate BERTScore (0.3–0.44) show LLM-generated mental states preserve semantics but diverge lexically; this dissonance with strong retrospective MCQ scores hints the MCQ format may flatter models.
  • Easy-set ablation: swapping semantic distractors for random subcontexts boosts CR by 44–83 points across all models — confirming most LLMs rely on topic-coherence shortcuts, not functional ToM.
  • Counterfactual NOTA ablation: Gemini 3 Pro correctly picks NOTA 80–95% of the time; Llama 8B 37–51%, Mistral Nemo 59–88%. GPT-5 is middling (61–90%). Interpretation: most models are genuinely reasoning at some level (otherwise counterfactual S′ would still elicit a confident answer), but only Gemini 3 Pro combines reasoning with robustness.

Critical Assessment

  • The headline result is load-bearing on a single model (Gemini 3 Pro). Before accepting "Gemini 3 Pro has genuine functional ToM," the reader should want (a) reproduction on another seed / prompt template, (b) evidence this is not an artifact of Gemini 3 Pro's training on similar dialogue corpora, and (c) the same analysis with chain-of-thought enabled for the other models (zero-shot no-reasoning is arguably unfair to reasoning models).
  • Distractor generation bias. All MCQ distractors (and, critically, the counterfactual mental states in the NOTA ablation) are produced by GPT-4o. If GPT-4o's distractor-writing style happens to be distinguishable by Gemini 3 Pro (e.g., stylistic tells), the "robust reasoning" signal could partly be "detecting sibling-model distractors." The paper does not test distractor generator diversity.
  • Prospective task retention rate is only 54.3% after human verification (Retrospective retains 83%) and inter-rater agreement drops to 0.45–0.68. The authors acknowledge this reflects task difficulty, but it also means ~46% of prospective items were discarded — the remaining set may be selection-biased toward cases the verifiers found unambiguous, which could inflate or deflate difficulty unpredictably.
  • "Human baseline" is misleading. It uses lay AMT workers on Retrospective ONLY (because Functional ToM "exceeded crowd-worker ability"). There is no human baseline on the actually-hard task, so we cannot say whether Gemini 3 Pro's 83% functional ToM is sub-human, human, or super-human.
  • Trust operationalization is thin. Trust is collapsed into a single concept combining Mayer et al.'s three factors, then generated by GPT-4o from context; there's no independent validation that the Trust label correlates with human annotators' trust judgments beyond the MCQ setup. The claim that "trust is weakly understood in LLMs" could partly mean "GPT-4o's Trust labels are noisy."
  • The "state-driven" isolation may over-correct. By removing ALL context, the prospective task also removes information a real agent would have. It's unclear that failing this task means failing functional ToM, vs. failing an unnaturally hard compression task.
  • Knowledge cutoff confound. Gemini 3 Pro likely has the most recent training data; AnnoMI / ESConv / PFG have been public for years. The paper does not attempt a held-out decontamination check.

Strengths

  • Naturalistic, high-stakes data is a genuine advance over template-based (ToMi, Hi-ToM) and synthetic (FANToM, OpenToM) benchmarks.
  • Clean conceptual separation of Literal vs. Functional ToM — operationalized via the context-free prospective setup — is the paper's best idea and is reusable beyond this dataset.
  • Multiple complementary evaluations (MCQ CR, open-ended semantic similarity, easy/hard distractor ablation, counterfactual NOTA) make the failure diagnosis convincing.
  • Wide model coverage (13 LLMs, MoE + dense, open + closed, reasoning + non-reasoning) and per-attribute (BDIEKT) breakdown.
  • Rigorous human verification with two-platform worker selection and Dawid-Skene + Gwet's AC1.
  • Addition of Trust fills a real gap; even if imperfectly operationalized, it is a useful invitation to further work.

Limitations

  • Trust scoped to specific dyadic social interactions; may not transfer to multi-party or casual chat.
  • Results from three high-stakes domains; generalization to casual / open-domain dialogue untested.
  • GPT-4o-generated inferences and distractors may carry subtle model biases that advantage some evaluated LLMs over others.
  • Lay human baseline exists only for Literal ToM; no human ceiling for Functional ToM.
  • Zero-shot no-reasoning prompting disadvantages models that shine with scaffolds; no CoT/tool-use ablation.
  • Prospective task has moderate inter-rater agreement and ~54% retention after verification.
  • Results are sensitive to one outlier model (Gemini 3 Pro) whose training data is opaque.

Relevance

  • 与兴趣高度相关的维度：论文在 inference evaluation 方向上做得很扎实——它展示了如何设计一个即便 MCQ 表面准确率很高、但通过反事实干预和诱饵难度消融就能暴露出模型是在做模式匹配还是真推理的评测范式。这对评估 hidden states/interpretability 和 ML reliability 的人很有借鉴意义：Functional ToM 本质上是一个"state sufficiency"探针，和"hidden state 是否真正编码了下游所需信息"的 probing 思路同构。
  • 与 silent data corruption / reliability 的联系：性能巨差（CR 从 ~90% 掉到 ~10%）只因换了一种提问方式，这是典型的"评测幻觉"——在没有对抗性探针的情况下，你会以为 LLM 的 ToM 能力已经解决。这直接对应"system card 里声称的能力能否在实际使用中维持"这类可靠性关切。
  • 不太相关的维度：对 training stability、quantization/precision 方向贡献有限——论文不触及训练或精度，仅做黑盒评测。
  • 对 system card 作者的启示：如果 system card 只报 Literal ToM 类指标（BigToM、ToMBench），可能严重高估社交推理的真实可部署性。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does Gemini 3 Pro retain its functional-ToM advantage when (a) distractors are generated by a non-GPT model (e.g., Claude, or the model itself), (b) contexts are paraphrased to reduce train-set overlap, or (c) domains are extended to multi-party / casual chat?
  • Would enabling extended thinking / chain-of-thought for GPT-5, Claude, DeepSeek-R1-class models close the gap to Gemini 3 Pro, or is the gap architectural / data-driven?
  • How do the Trust attribute labels correlate with independent human judgments of trust (rather than GPT-4o's generated labels)? A Mayer-et-al-style survey instrument on a subsample would strengthen the claim.
  • If we probe model hidden states after reading a context, does a linear probe for BDIEKT states predict the correct prospective trajectory better than the model's own MCQ answer? That would localize where the literal→functional breakdown happens.
  • Can the counterfactual NOTA setup be automated into a continuous "functional ToM robustness" score, so it can be added to future system cards alongside BigToM-style numbers?
  • What is the Prospective task performance of a human expert (e.g., a licensed counselor), not a lay crowd worker? Without this, we cannot calibrate whether 83% is impressive or still far from human.
