CCTVBench: Contrastive Consistency Traffic VideoQA Benchmark for Multimodal LLMs

arXiv: 2604.20460v1

Date: 2026-04-22

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20460v1

一句话总结： CCTVBench 用真实事故视频与世界模型生成的反事实对照视频构建四元一致性评测协议，揭示多模态 LLM 在交通安全场景下"逐例准确率高、对比一致性低"的系统性缺陷，并提出推理期对比解码 C-TCD 缓解该问题。
TL;DR: CCTVBench is a contrastive traffic VideoQA benchmark that pairs real accident clips with world-model-generated counterfactual "no-accident" videos plus mutually exclusive Yes/No question pairs, exposing a large gap between per-instance QA accuracy and quadruple-level consistency in open-source and proprietary video LLMs; a training-free contrastive decoding variant (C-TCD) using the paired counterfactual video as contrast signal gives the largest consistency gains.

核心贡献

  • CCTVBench 基准：基于真实事故视频 $v^+$ 与驾驶世界模型（NVIDIA Cosmos）生成的反事实"未发生事故"视频 $v^-$，配合互斥问题对 $(q^+, q^-)$，形成 305 个对比场景、1,776 个互斥问题对、7,104 个二分类实例，覆盖 Event/Key-Entity/Spatial/Spatial-Temporal/Causal/Counterfactual 六类问题。
  • 四元一致性评测协议（QuadAcc）：只有当模型在 $(v^+, q^+)$ 回答 Yes 且在其余三个组合全部回答 No 时才算整题正确，比逐例准确率严格得多。
  • 可诊断的失败分解：将失败分为 Positive Omission / Positive Swap / Negative Hallucination / Mutual-Exclusivity Violation 四大失败模式，并引入视觉-语言敏感度指标（VS/QS/VRI/GVRS/SVE）区分"视觉接地"与"语言先验"贡献。
  • C-TCD 对比解码：推理期将同场景反事实视频作为语义互斥的对比输入参与 logit 融合 $z_t=(1+\alpha)z_t^{ori}-\alpha z_t^{con}$，在不重新训练的前提下显著提升 QuadAcc，优于 VCD（扩散噪声破坏）与 TCD（时间下采样）等通用退化对比策略。
  • 反事实视频质量验证：通过 R3D-18 伪影泄漏检测（AUC 56.7%、accuracy 53.6%，接近随机）与 FVD/FID（22.44 / 32.63，对比不相关正常行车 35.73 / 105.00）说明世界模型生成的反事实视频无显著捷径特征且分布漂移温和。

Key Contributions

  1. A contrastive VideoQA benchmark specifically for safety-critical traffic reasoning, built on real anomaly footage paired with counterfactual negatives from a driving world model, covering six question categories.
  2. A quadruple evaluation protocol (QuadAcc, Video Consistency, Question Consistency) that probes whether a model maintains a coherent decision rule under minimal scene-matched counterfactual edits, complementing single-instance balanced accuracy and a squashed Matthews Correlation Coefficient (MCCScore).
  3. Behavior diagnostics: failure-mode decomposition (Positive Omission / Positive Swap / Negative Hallucination / Mutual-Exclusivity Violation) plus vision-language sensitivity metrics (VS, QS, VRI, GVRS, SVE) that separate reactivity from correct binding.
  4. Benchmarking of open-source (InternVL3.5, Qwen2.5/3-VL, PLLaVA, VideoLLaMA3, VideoChatGPT, Phi-4-multimodal) and proprietary (Gemini-2.5 flash/flash-lite, GPT-5 nano/mini) video LLMs, showing a persistent gap between BaAcc/MCCScore and QuadAcc.
  5. C-TCD, a training-free contrastive decoding method that replaces generic corruption/temporal degradation with the semantically exclusive counterpart video from the same scene as the contrast signal, improving BaAcc, MCCScore, and QuadAcc simultaneously on Qwen3-VL.

Methodology

  • Dataset curation pipeline (Fig. 2): Source accident clips from multiple driving anomaly datasets (CarCrashDataset, DAD, SUTD, DADA-2000); annotators segment each clip into base / key frames / contrastive segments and provide meta annotations (instances, causes, spatio-temporal relations). A driving world model synthesizes a counterfactual variant $v_s^-$ conditioned on the shared base prefix, preserving layout/illumination/viewpoint while removing the accident. Quality filtering and human validation remove generation artifacts, viewpoint drift, key-entity inconsistency, or ambiguous grounding.
  • QA generation: GPT-5 generates paired contrastive questions $(q^+_{s,k}, q^-_{s,k})$ constrained to be mutually exclusive but not collectively exhaustive—on $v^+$ exactly $q^+$ should be Yes; on $v^-$ both should be No (none-of-the-above state).
  • Metrics (Sec. 3.4):
  • Quadruple correctness QuadCr = 1[ŷ⁺⁺=1 ∧ ŷ⁺⁻=0 ∧ ŷ⁻⁺=0 ∧ ŷ⁻⁻=0].
  • Video Consistency = avg(Contr@q⁺, Reject@q⁻); Question Consistency = avg(Contr@v⁺, Reject@v⁻).
  • Global: Balanced Accuracy, MCCScore = ((MCC+1)/2)².
  • Diagnostics: PosOmiss, PosSwap, NegHall, MEViol; VS, QS, VRI = VS/(VS+QS), GVRS = 2·VRI·QS, and a selective video effect SVE.
  • Evaluation setup: Unified binary Yes/No prompt, temperature 0, single-word output parsing, 2000-replicate scene-level bootstrap for 95% CIs.
  • C-TCD inference: At each decoding step for query $(v,q)$, fuse logits from the original video and its paired counterfactual: $z_t = (1+\alpha) z_t^{ori} - \alpha z_t^{con}$. For $v_s^+$ the contrast is the paired $v_s^-$ (and vice versa), giving a scene-matched semantic regularizer instead of generic noise (VCD) or temporal downsampling (TCD).

Results & Highlights

  • Global classification vs. consistency gap (Table 1): Most models reach BaAcc 50–70% and non-trivial MCCScore, yet QuadAcc stays low. Open-source QuadAcc is typically <12% (e.g., InternVL3.5-8B 11.38%, Qwen3-VL-8B 10.65%, others 2.9–7.6%). Proprietary models top out at GPT-5-mini 25.85% QuadAcc despite 70.43% BaAcc.
  • Two distinct weakness patterns: Some models reject $q^-$ reliably (high Reject@q⁻) but miss the true event on $v^+$ (low Contr@q⁺); others recognize $q^+$ on $v^+$ but leak "Yes" on $v^-$, violating none-of-the-above.
  • Reactivity ≠ binding (Table 2): VideoLLaMA3-7B scores the highest VS (38.06) and SVE (21.01) among open-source but QuadAcc remains very low—strong modality sensitivity alone does not guarantee correct binding. GPT-5-mini combines high GVRS (47.74) with the best overall QuadAcc.
  • Category imbalance (Fig. 6): Key-Entity is easiest; Counterfactual and Causal are hardest, since they require rejecting plausible hypotheses on counterfactual videos and resisting stereotypical accident priors.
  • Failure-mode composition (Fig. 5): Scaling Qwen3-VL from 2B to 8B increases Positive Omission (safety calibration collapses decisions toward "No" rather than improving binding).
  • Contrastive decoding (Table 3): On Qwen3-VL-8B, Base = 10.65 QuadAcc; VCD 14.62, TCD 12.70, C-TCD 18.63 with simultaneous improvements in BaAcc (60.26 → 64.82) and Scoreₖₗₛ (38.62 → 43.03). VCD reduces BaAcc on Qwen3-VL-2B, suggesting noise-based contrast over-suppresses useful evidence.
  • Counterfactual generation sanity: R3D-18 real-vs-synthetic classifier near-chance (AUC 56.7%), and FVD/FID between counterfactual and real accidents (22.44 / 32.63) are far below the inter-domain gap to normal driving (35.73 / 105.00).

Authors & Credibility

  • Affiliations: Technical University of Munich (Chair of Robotics, Artificial Intelligence and Real-Time Systems, Alois Knoll's group) and UCLA (Walter Zimmer).
  • Track record: The TUM group has a line of work on traffic/roadside perception and VideoQA (the authors cite their own TUMTraffic-VideoQA, STSBench). Alois Knoll is a well-established robotics/ITS researcher.
  • Resource use: Leveraging NVIDIA Cosmos (driving world model) and GPT-5 for QA generation is reasonable and matches recent VLM-eval practice. Human validation is described but not quantified (no reported inter-annotator agreement).
  • Credibility: Moderate to high. The benchmark size (305 scenes, 7,104 instances) is small-to-medium; the analysis is careful (bootstrap CIs, failure decomposition, generator-bias diagnostics), which raises confidence.

Critical Assessment

  • Single-generator dependence: Counterfactual negatives all come from one driving world model (Cosmos). Despite R3D-18/FVD checks, this is exactly the class of generator-specific bias that a single linear probe can miss; self-acknowledged limitation. A multi-generator ablation or cross-generator consistency would be more convincing.
  • Scale is modest: 305 contrastive scenes is small for strong claims about "persistent gaps." Category-level cells in Fig. 6 are thin, and some differences (e.g., GPT-5-nano vs GPT-5-mini) could reflect decoding variance more than capability.
  • Proprietary comparison asymmetric: Only GPT-5 and Gemini-2.5 families (all via API, at unspecified thinking/tool settings) are included; no Claude, no GPT-4o, no Qwen-VL-Max, no InternVL3 Pro. C-TCD also only evaluated on Qwen3-VL, not on any proprietary model (impossible due to API logits) nor on InternVL3.5 or VideoLLaMA3 where it could change conclusions.
  • C-TCD $\alpha$ undisclosed in what I read: The method claims "largest gains" but the sweep over $\alpha$ and sensitivity to it is not reported in the main tables; risk of favorable hyperparameter selection on the same evaluation set.
  • GPT-5 generating the questions and being graded later: The benchmark uses GPT-5 to generate contrastive questions, and GPT-5-mini is the top proprietary model—there is potential subtle distribution alignment between question style and evaluator, which is not audited.
  • Mutual exclusivity is human-validated but not statistically measured: "Some residual ambiguity may persist" is acknowledged; the none-of-the-above failure mode (which dominates QuadAcc drops) could partially reflect annotation ambiguity.
  • No comparison to text-only ablation: A language-only baseline (no video input) would quantify how much QuadAcc is genuinely solvable from priors—important given the "language priors vs visual grounding" framing.

Strengths

  • Well-motivated evaluation protocol that makes the distinction between reactivity and correct contrastive binding concrete and measurable (GVRS, SVE are nice additions beyond generic hallucination benchmarks).
  • Failure-mode decomposition (PosOmiss / PosSwap / NegHall / MEViol) turns the benchmark into an actionable diagnostic, not just a leaderboard.
  • Generator-bias checks (artifact leakage + FVD/FID) address the obvious objection to world-model counterfactuals upfront.
  • Clean inference-time extension (C-TCD) that directly uses the benchmark's own construction as a signal, tying evaluation and mitigation together in a coherent story.
  • Statistical reporting (bootstrap CIs, ranking stability) is better than the typical VLM benchmark paper.

Limitations

  • Single world model; coverage limited to event-level paired scenes; scale constrained by the cost of collecting rare accident footage.
  • Residual ambiguity in "mutually exclusive but not collectively exhaustive" question pairs despite human validation (self-reported).
  • C-TCD only evaluated on Qwen3-VL (open-source); generality to other families and to proprietary models is unverified.
  • Question generation via GPT-5 may bias question style; no human-authored held-out subset reported.
  • No analysis of how QuadAcc trends with model scale beyond one Qwen3-VL pair and one InternVL3.5 pair; scaling claims rely on only a few data points.
  • No text-only / question-only baseline to quantify solvability from priors.

Relevance

  • 对你兴趣的契合度：中等偏上。 这是 VLM/VideoQA 可靠性评测方向，与你关注的 推理评测 (inference evaluation) 和 ML reliability 直接相关；"逐例指标高但一致性低"的系统性缺陷，与你关注的 LLM 训练后行为漂移、隐藏状态稳健性议题在评测哲学上同源。
  • 反事实对照作为推理期 regularizer 的思路（C-TCD）值得借鉴到通用 LLM 的 silent corruption / 数值精度评测里：如果你做 quantization 或 precision 比较，同样可以设计"语义互斥的输入对"来检测模型是否真的在用证据而非先验。
  • 与你的 system cards / silent data corruption 议题距离较远——本文不涉及训练稳定性、数值问题或硬件级错误。
  • 若你做多模态方向或交通/自动驾驶相关的 reliability 测评，该基准与 C-TCD 可直接复用。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How much of the QuadAcc drop reflects genuine contrastive-consistency failure vs residual ambiguity in the "not collectively exhaustive" constraint? A human ceiling on the same quadruples would disambiguate.
  2. Does C-TCD still help when the counterfactual video is from a different generator than the one used at benchmark construction? This would separate "contrastive decoding generalizes" from "paired-Cosmos decoding generalizes."
  3. What is a text-only baseline's QuadAcc? If it's near zero, language priors aren't enough; if it's non-trivial, the benchmark leaks more than the authors claim.
  4. How sensitive is C-TCD to $\alpha$? Is the optimum stable across scenes and categories, or tuned on the eval split?
  5. Does QuadAcc correlate with downstream driving-task safety metrics (e.g., hazard-miss rate under closed-loop simulation), or only with QA-style benchmarks?
  6. For the 8B-over-2B Positive-Omission regression in Qwen3-VL, is this a consequence of post-training safety calibration (RLHF-style "prefer No"), and can it be separated by evaluating a pre-RLHF checkpoint?
  7. Could the same contrastive quadruple idea be lifted to text-only LLMs (paired context edits) to measure consistency under minimal prompt counterfactuals, bridging to your interpretability/hidden-states interests?
