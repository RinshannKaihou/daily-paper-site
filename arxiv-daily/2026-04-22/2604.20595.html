An explicit operator explains end-to-end computation in the modern neural networks used for sequence and language modeling

arXiv: 2604.20595v1

Date: 2026-04-22

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20595v1

一句话总结： 作者建立 S4D 对角 SSM 与一个可解析的非线性振子环形网络之间的数学等价，利用 Fourier 特征基与 Carleman 嵌入，给出 S4 前向传递的闭式算子表达式——其中循环层产生行波，二阶波-波相互作用可解释 94% 的 SelfRegulationSCP1 分类性能。
TL;DR: This paper derives an exact operator expression for the full forward pass of S4D (diagonal S4) by establishing a mathematical correspondence with a nonlinear oscillator network on a ring. Eigenmodes of the dynamics operator correspond to traveling waves; Carleman embedding of the GELU readout decomposes classification into a hierarchy of wave-wave interactions where linear, second-order, and full Carleman responses explain 83%, 94%, and 100% of accuracy respectively on SelfRegulationSCP1.

核心贡献

  • 将 S4D 对角 SSM 嵌入到一维环形非线性振子网络，得到闭式指数传播算子 D_τ = e^{Kτ}。
  • 利用 Fourier 本征基 F 将循环层分解为独立演化的行波模式 μ_i(k)。
  • 用 Carleman 嵌入把 GELU 非线性读出展开为多阶波-波相互作用，并以二阶近似解释 94% 分类性能。

Key Contributions

  • Mathematical correspondence between S4D-Lin recurrence and an exactly solvable Kuramoto-type oscillator on a ring.
  • Closed-form Fourier eigenbasis diagonalizing the propagator, yielding explicit traveling-wave modes with harmonic ladder frequencies.
  • End-to-end operator expression O = (1/T) W Σ Λ(Re(C D_τ x_k + B u_k)) including the nonlinearity.
  • Carleman decomposition showing that wave-wave interactions of specific resonant modes drive classification.

Methodology

Starts from a nonlinear oscillator network dψ/dt = ω + κ Σ a_ij (sin(ψ_j − ψ_i − φ_ij) − i cos(...)), transforms to x = e^{iψ} to linearize, and diagonalizes the circulant adjacency via discrete Fourier basis. Matches this structure against S4D-Lin eigenvalues λ_D,i and input projection B-tilde. For analysis of the GELU readout Λ, applies Carleman embedding z(y) = [1, y, y⊗2, ...] and truncates to second order, validating against SelfRegulationSCP1 dataset.

Results & Highlights

  • Eigenvalue spectrum lies on a unit circle with a harmonic ladder of imaginary parts.
  • Single resonant mode separates sinusoid classes at 15 Hz vs 20 Hz by modal energy alone.
  • Linear SSM contribution explains 83% of SCP1 classification accuracy; second-order Carleman brings it to 94%; full GELU to 100%.
  • Classification signal concentrates in a few specific wave-wave cross-terms.

Authors & Credibility

Nine-author collaboration spanning Western University Math, Fields Institute, King's University College (Physics), and Lethbridge Neuroscience. Ján Mináč is a known algebraist; Lyle Muller works on biological neural networks and oscillator dynamics. Corresponding author is well-established. High credibility for the mathematical rigor.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High on the mathematical front — first exact operator characterization of a deployed SSM variant.
  • Rigor: Derivations standard but carefully composed; explicit correspondence matrices (Λ, D) and eigenmodes.
  • Reproducibility: Standard math; code not linked in the extracted pages.
  • Significance: Offers a principled alternative to post-hoc attention interpretability and suggests concrete architectural design levers (resonant frequency selection).
  • Red flags: Validated on a single, small real-world dataset (SCP1); 300-trial classification is narrow. The jump from 83% to 100% via Carleman is striking but may not generalize to tasks where nonlinearity is essential. Correspondence applies to S4D-Lin specifically; full Mamba-like selective SSMs are only claimed to generalize (appendix).

Strengths

  • Rare case of exact mechanistic explanation with closed-form operators, not just correlational probing.
  • Clean connection to neuroscience / oscillator literature.
  • Carleman hierarchy gives a tunable explanatory depth.

Limitations

  • S4D-Lin only; selective SSMs (Mamba, S6) not treated in main text.
  • Small dataset for end-to-end validation.
  • Ring topology assumption (circulant) does not cover trained (arbitrary) adjacency — authors note generalization via FDF diagonalization.
