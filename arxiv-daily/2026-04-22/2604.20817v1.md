> **一句话总结：** 作者证明 Fourier 周期 T=2,5,10 的谱尖峰在 Transformer、Linear RNN、LSTM、Word2Vec 甚至原始 token 频率分布中普遍出现（谱收敛），但只有某些模型（Transformer、Gated DeltaNet、Mamba-2）学到了 mod-T 可线性分类的几何可分表示（几何收敛）——且 Theorem 1 证明 Fourier 稀疏性是几何可分的必要但不充分条件。

> **TL;DR:** Fourier spikes at periods T=2,5,10 in number embeddings are universal across Transformers, linear RNNs, LSTMs, and classical word embeddings (even the raw number frequency distribution alone produces them), but only some systems learn geometrically separable mod-T features usable by linear probes. Theorem 1 proves spectral convergence is necessary but not sufficient for geometric convergence; controlled 300M-parameter experiments on 10B FineWeb-Edu tokens attribute geometric convergence to data co-occurrence signals, architecture (Transformer/linear-RNN > LSTM), and optimizer.

## 核心贡献
- 区分"谱收敛"（Fourier spike 出现）与"几何收敛"（mod-T 类线性可分），证明前者不蕴含后者。
- Theorem 1：给出 Fisher 判别条件，表明当类间 scatter 能被类内 scatter 淹没时谱尖峰无用。
- 对比 300M Transformer/Gated DeltaNet/Mamba-2/LSTM 在相同 10B token 下的 Fourier spectrum 与 probe accuracy；附加数据扰动（Swap Numbers、Unigram Replace、Isolate-k、Context Length）定位几何收敛所需信号。

## Key Contributions
- Two-tiered hierarchy of periodic feature learning (spectral vs geometric convergence).
- Formal theorem linking Fourier power to Fisher discriminant eigenvalue bounds.
- Structure-attribution experiments isolating data (co-occurrence), architecture, and optimizer contributions.
- Evidence that LSTMs with larger Fourier power than Transformers still fail mod-T probes due to within-class scatter.

## Methodology
300M parameter models on 10B FineWeb-Edu tokens with Llama-3 tokenizer. Measures (1) Fourier magnitude spectrum of token embeddings and (2) Cohen's κ of linear/MLP/RFM probes for n mod T classification. Data perturbations: Isolate-k (k numbers per sequence), Context Length ℓ, Swap Numbers, Unigram Replace. Architecture and optimizer ablations (Muon vs AdamW).

## Results & Highlights
- Spectral spikes at T=2,5,10 appear universally — including GloVe, FastText, and raw number frequency.
- Transformer and Gated DeltaNet achieve κ=96 at T=2 and κ=85 at T=10; LSTM remains at chance (κ=9) despite visible spectrum.
- Unigram Replace destroys all co-occurrence, yet spectrum persists; probes fall to chance.
- Isolate-1 (no number-number co-occurrence) with Transformer still achieves κ=45 at T=10 — text-number co-occurrence alone suffices for modular structure.
- Multi-token addition training produces Fourier structure; single-token addition does not.

## Critical Assessment
- Novelty: Strong. Reframes "spectral interpretability" claims from Zhou et al. 2024, Nanda et al. 2023, Kantamneni & Tegmark 2025 — spectral structure alone is overclaimed as evidence for learned modular arithmetic.
- Rigor: Combines a crisp theorem with extensive, matched-budget empirics (same 10B tokens, same tokenizer across architectures and optimizers).
- Reproducibility: Models released; data perturbations explicit.
- Significance: High — recalibrates what Fourier spectra tell us about mechanistic interpretability and corrects widespread inference errors.
- Red flags: 300M may be too small to show emergent behaviors of frontier LLMs. The "LSTM is fundamentally limited" conclusion rests on one LSTM variant. Theorem 1 part (ii) uses constructed counterexamples — important for the proof but not observed in natural LLM training.

## Strengths
- Rare necessary-but-not-sufficient theorem that directly corrects published interpretability claims.
- Controlled ablations that disentangle data, architecture, and optimizer.
- Clear visualization (Fisher discriminant example in Figure 3) making the theory tangible.

## Limitations
- Models capped at 300M scale.
- Focuses on number tokens; other periodic concepts (days of week, months) discussed only via literature.
- Classifier is linear/MLP/RFM — deeper circuits may recover information linear probes miss.

## Relevance
直接契合你关心的 hidden states & interpretability：明确指出"Fourier spike ≠ 模型学到模运算"，对你基于 hidden state 几何做可靠性诊断时的假设检查极为关键。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Does the spectral-geometric gap disappear at frontier scale (70B+)?
- Which training perturbations eliminate geometric convergence without affecting downstream perplexity — can this be exploited to diagnose representation quality?
- Does the necessary-but-not-sufficient theorem have an analog for other "emergent representation" claims (e.g., sparse circuits, superposition)?
- How does the finding interact with number-specific tokenization issues (e.g., BPE splitting large numbers)?
