Variance Is Not Importance: Structural Analysis of Transformer Compressibility Across Model Scales

arXiv: 2604.20682v1

Date: 2026-04-22

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20682v1

一句话总结： 40+ 组对比实验（GPT-2 124M 与 Mistral 7B）揭示五条结构性定律：高方向方差与输出敏感度几乎不相关（keeping 95% 方差使 perplexity 从 47 飙升至 3441）；block 线性是条件性的；任何将权重分解为量化因子的方法都通过交叉项放大误差（直接 INT4 严格优于 SVD+INT4 2×）；Mistral 深层线性度从 0.17 单调升到 0.93；30% 的 token 计算上"简单"。
TL;DR: A single-author systematic study of what makes transformers compressible. Key findings: (1) PCA high-variance directions have 96% uncorrelation with CCA-predictive directions, so variance is not importance; (2) block linearity (R² ≈ 0.95 in GPT-2, gradient from 0.17 to 0.93 in Mistral 7B) is conditional on upstream distribution; (3) factoring weights before quantizing always hurts 2–25× at the same bit budget ("reconstruction wall"); (4) ~30% of tokens are trivially computable; multi-block linear replacement fails catastrophically (+151 PPL after 5 blocks).

核心贡献

  • 直接证否"高方差方向即重要方向"的压缩直觉。
  • 证明 block 线性性是"条件线性"，因果上被上游激活分布调制。
  • 定义"Reconstruction Wall"：任意分解式量化在同预算下劣于直接量化。
  • 跨尺度发现 Mistral 深层线性度单调递增，早期 block 做非线性特征构造，后期 block 做线性 refinement。

Key Contributions

  • Five actionable structural findings mapped to practical compression guidance.
  • Quantitative decomposition: importance = variance × sensitivity, with PCA capturing only variance.
  • Matched-budget comparison of INT4 direct vs DCT+INT4 vs SVD+INT4 vs rotated INT8/INT2.
  • Evidence for adaptive per-token computation (30% easy tokens) via trained exit heads and KL sensitivity.

Methodology

Two models: GPT-2 124M (12 blocks, dim 768) and Mistral 7B (32 blocks, dim 4096). Calibration on WikiText-2 (32×256 tokens). Block linearity via ridge regression Δ_b = X_in A V_k^T. Activation PCA, CCA against the last block, 1% Gaussian perturbation sensitivity, trained early-exit heads at blocks 15, 23, 27.

Results & Highlights

  • GPT-2 uniformly linear (R² ≈ 0.95 at every block); Mistral 7B shows gradient 0.17 → 0.93 from block 0 to 31.
  • Replacing Mistral block 31 with a rank-773 linear map (6.3M params vs 218M) costs only +1.71 PPL.
  • Multi-block sequential replacement collapses: 5 replacements → +151 PPL.
  • DCT+INT4 2× worse, SVD+INT4 2× worse, rotated INT8/INT2 25× worse than direct INT4.
  • Confidence-0.95 multi-exit routing saves only 6.4% compute at +4.31 PPL penalty.

Authors & Credibility

Single author, Samuel Salfati (fraQtl AI Research — independent). No prior visible high-impact publications on transformer compression. Paper is clearly systematic and professionally presented; credibility rests on the methodology (which is standard) and the 40+ experiments. Peer-review status unknown.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Medium-high. Individual findings (variance ≠ importance, block linearity conditionality) appear in prior work (Razzhigaev 2024, Gromov 2024) but this paper synthesizes them with matched-budget experiments. The "Reconstruction Wall" framing is a useful crystallization.
  • Rigor: Controlled experiments; explicit tables; error analysis. However, only two models and one calibration corpus.
  • Reproducibility: Methodology described precisely; code not mentioned.
  • Significance: Strong practical takeaways for compression practitioners — especially the warning against factoring + quantizing.
  • Red flags: Independent lab with no senior co-authors; no comparison against GPTQ's sequential error feedback (noted briefly). The 30% easy-token claim uses 500 training steps which may undersell achievable dynamic routing quality. No statistical-significance analysis.

Strengths

  • Converts scattered compression folklore into testable structural claims.
  • Direct INT4 vs factored-INT4 comparison is cleanly decisive.
  • Depth-gradient finding in Mistral is an important design signal.

Limitations

  • Only GPT-2 and Mistral 7B — scale between them is huge, but only two points.
  • Single calibration dataset.
  • Early-exit training budget limited; conclusion about "30% easy" may be conservative.
  • No connection to emerging non-linear compression methods (e.g., QuaRot + learned rotations at full scale).

Relevance

高度相关于你关心的 quantization/precision error 与 ML 可靠性：提供了"为什么分解+量化会放大误差"的结构性解释，对于理解 silent data corruption 与量化脆弱层的关系有帮助。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the Reconstruction Wall still hold when GPTQ's sequential error feedback is applied to factored weights?
  • Is the 0.17 → 0.93 linearity gradient in Mistral 7B present in other open models (Llama-3, Qwen2.5)?
  • Can adaptive per-token routing trained jointly with the backbone recover the 30% easy-token savings without PPL loss?
  • How does the CCA-predictive subspace relate to circuit analysis of specific tasks?
