Rethinking Intrinsic Dimension Estimation in Neural Representations

arXiv: 2604.20276v1

Date: 2026-04-22

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20276v1

一句话总结： 作者给出定理——在 Lipschitz 神经网络中逐层的 pointwise/Hausdorff 内在维度不可递增，从而与文献中普遍观察到"浅层 ID 上升、深层下降"的经验图样产生直接矛盾；他们诊断出这些 ID 估计实际上反映的是潜在空间中最近邻距离的扩张（熵）而非真实 ID。
TL;DR: This AISTATS 2026 paper proves that common intrinsic-dimension estimators (MLE, TwoNN, Gride) cannot track the true ID of layer-wise neural representations because neural networks are Lipschitz and ID is non-increasing under Lipschitz maps. Empirical layer-wise ID patterns (rising then falling) are shown to track NN distances and von Neumann entropy rather than true ID, reframing the interpretability literature's conclusions about "emergence of abstractions."

核心贡献

  • 证明在 Lipschitz 映射下 pointwise dimension 不可增加（Theorem 1），扩展到流形并集与 Hausdorff 维度。
  • 指出 LLM embedding 的 Hausdorff / pointwise ID 实际上为零（离散 token 有限像集）。
  • 实证展示 Llama-3.1-8B、Mistral-7B、Pythia-6.9B 的层级 ID 图样由最近邻距离扩张和表示范数膨胀驱动，与 von Neumann 熵高度相关。

Key Contributions

  • A theoretical contradiction between widely-cited rising/falling layer-wise ID patterns and the Lipschitz property of neural nets.
  • Formal results (Lemma 1, Theorems 1 and 2) covering single-manifold and union-of-manifolds cases.
  • Empirical drivers analysis showing NN-distance growth and L2 norm expansion explain the observed ID patterns.
  • Connection between layer-wise ID estimates and von Neumann entropy (eigenvalue spread).

Methodology

Formalizes pointwise and Hausdorff dimensions on Borel measures; proves they are non-increasing under Lipschitz maps (standard layer types: linear/conv, ReLU/Softmax, normalization, pooling, residual). Validates empirically on ImageNet-pretrained ResNet/VGG and 10k WikiText prompts through Llama, Mistral, Pythia using TwoNN, MLE, and Gride estimators. Compares ID curves against NN-distance growth, average L2 norm, and von Neumann entropy.

Results & Highlights

  • MLE and TwoNN systematically underestimate true ID; bias grows with ambient dimension.
  • Class-specific ID also violates non-increasing property, so bias is consistent, not just noise.
  • Estimated IDs of LLM hidden layers follow the same shape as NN-distance growth and entropy.
  • LLM token embeddings have true Hausdorff/pointwise dimension zero due to finite support.
  • Ambient dimension and cosine similarity do not explain the growth, but representation magnitude does.

Authors & Credibility

Rickmer Schulte and David Rügamer (LMU Munich, MCML). Rügamer is a recognized researcher in deep learning theory and probabilistic modeling. The paper is an AISTATS 2026 proceeding, which is peer reviewed. High credibility.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High — a clean theoretical refutation of a popular interpretability narrative.
  • Rigor: Formal lemmas with citations to standard Lipschitz results for layers; empirical section well-supports the alternative explanation.
  • Reproducibility: Standard estimators and public models; implementation details deferred to appendix.
  • Significance: Undermines a large body of "phase transitions" and "abstraction emergence" claims that rely on ID estimators — this is a big deal for the mechanistic interpretability literature.
  • Red flags: The Lipschitz argument assumes "a.e." or finite Lipschitz constants; attention's constant is not globally bounded, though the appendix discusses this. The reframing as "NN distance expansion" is suggestive but not yet a principled replacement metric.

Strengths

  • Rare combination of tight theorem + empirical decomposition.
  • Writes against the grain of a fashionable narrative while remaining constructive.
  • Clear figures showing ID and entropy tracking each other.

Limitations

  • Does not propose a new ID estimator robust in high dimensions.
  • Empirical models are mid-scale; frontier LLMs not tested.
  • Connection between ID and entropy is observational; causal story is not formalized.
