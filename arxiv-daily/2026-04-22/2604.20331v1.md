> **一句话总结：** 作者提出一个三阶段代理建模框架：基于领域先验假设生成两万条模拟病例，对 GPT-4 / Claude-3.5-Sonnet / Gemini-1.5-Pro 进行大规模提示，再用（对数）线性回归把 LLM 的输入-输出对拟合成可解释公式，在心血管风险、GFR、卒中、肺功能四个医学预测任务上量化 LLM 的"感知"，暴露出与医学知识相悖的关联（如 Gemini 把腰臀比/尿酸系数符号弄反）以及已被科学界废弃的种族偏差（Claude-3.5-Sonnet 对黑人 eGFR 系数 1.227，甚至高于已废弃的 2009 CKD-EPI 方程中的 1.159）。

> **TL;DR:** The authors build a three-stage surrogate-modeling framework—simulate 20,000 patient-like samples from a domain-informed prior, prompt GPT-4, Claude-3.5-Sonnet and Gemini-1.5-Pro for clinical risk estimates, and fit a (log-)linear regression on the resulting (input, LLM-output) pairs—to obtain a deterministic, SHAP-explainable proxy of what each LLM "perceives." Across CVD risk, eGFR, stroke, and pulmonary-function tasks, the surrogate beats PCE/FRS on UK Biobank (C-index 0.736 vs 0.726/0.720 for GPT-4) and quantitatively exposes medically wrong associations and refuted race-based biases still baked into the three commercial LLMs.

## 核心贡献

- 提出用"领域假设 → 大规模模拟数据 → 统计代理回归"三阶段流程，把黑盒 LLM 对某一具体假设的隐式知识蒸馏成单一可解释公式。
- 在四个医学预测任务（CVD、GFR、卒中、肺功能）上验证框架，对 GPT-4、Claude-3.5-Sonnet、Gemini-1.5-Pro 每条输入采样 10 次共两万样本。
- 在 UKB 357,113 人真实队列上，GPT-4 代理模型的 C-index 0.736 超过 PCE（+0.010）和 FRS（+0.016）；Claude/Gemini 代理模型也分别超过 PCE/FRS。
- 定量揭示与既有医学证据冲突的编码知识：Gemini 对腰臀比的系数为统计不显著的负值、对尿酸为负（与 CVD 正相关文献相悖）。
- 定量揭示种族偏差在 LLM 中的残留：Claude-3.5-Sonnet 对黑人 eGFR 的乘性系数 1.227 甚至高于已废弃的 2009 CKD-EPI 方程（1.159）。
- 代理推理速度比直接调 API 快约 900–2100 倍，且输出确定，缓解 LLM 的"streaking"聚类伪影。

## Key Contributions

- A three-stage framework (simulated dataset generation → extensive LLM prompting → domain-guided statistical regression) that turns an LLM's implicit knowledge about a stated hypothesis into a single, interpretable equation.
- Four proof-of-concept medical-prediction experiments (CVD 10-year risk, eGFR via CKD-EPI, stroke risk, pulmonary function) spanning three frontier LLMs (GPT-4, Claude-3.5-Sonnet, Gemini-1.5-Pro) at temperature 0, 10 samples per case, 20,000 simulated cases.
- Real-world external evaluation on UK Biobank (n=357,113; 22,866 MACEs): GPT-4 surrogate C-index 0.736 vs PCE 0.726 and FRS 0.720; similar gains for Claude and Gemini surrogates.
- Quantitative exposure of medically inconsistent associations (e.g., Gemini's negative waist–hip-ratio and negative uric-acid coefficients for CVD).
- Quantitative exposure of still-encoded, scientifically refuted race adjustments: multiplicative Black-race coefficient 1.153 (GPT-4), 1.227 (Claude-3.5-Sonnet, stronger than the refuted 2009 CKD-EPI 1.159), 1.008 (Gemini-1.5-Pro).
- Practical artifacts: ~900–2100× inference speed-up vs direct API calls, a deterministic surrogate that avoids "streaking," SHAP-based local explanations, and a public demo at https://llm-surrogate.com/.

## Methodology

Stage 1 — Simulated dataset generation. Users select (i) an outcome (e.g., 10-year CVD risk), (ii) input variables guided by domain prior (20 well-established CVD risk factors selected by literature review), (iii) a probability distribution per variable (lognormal for continuous physiological variables with broad support spanning normal and abnormal ranges; uniform over categories for categorical variables on the assumption that large N washes out class proportions), and (iv) random sampling of 20,000 cases.

Stage 2 — Prompting. Each simulated row is serialized to a sentence prompt (templates in Supplementary Table S2) and sent in a zero-shot, independent-API-call fashion to GPT-4 (gpt-4-0613), Claude-3.5-Sonnet (2024-10-22), and Gemini-1.5-Pro-002 at temperature 0 (selected via a sweep). Ten responses are collected per sample per LLM and the numeric risk is regex-extracted.

Stage 3 — Statistical surrogate fit. For CVD, a linear regression with 200,000 (input, output) pairs per LLM. For eGFR, a log-linear form that mirrors the CKD-EPI structure (log eGFR = log μ + β₁ log(min(Scr/α,1)) + β₂ log(max(Scr/α,1)) + log γ · Age + log δ · Female + log ε · Black), allowing direct comparison of the LLM's Black-race coefficient (ε) against the refuted 2009 equation's 1.159. SHAP is applied to the surrogate for local attribution.

Evaluation. The CVD surrogate is scored on UK Biobank (ethics reference 21/NW/0157; application 85037; n=357,113; MACE defined by ICD-10 I21–I25 or I63–I64 within 10 years). Metric: Harrell's C-index with 2,000-bootstrap 95% CIs; baselines PCE and FRS. Pulmonary and stroke experiments replicate the recipe with different variables and distributions.

## Results & Highlights

- CVD C-index on UKB: GPT-4 surrogate 0.736 (+0.010 over PCE, +0.016 over FRS, both CIs exclude 0); Claude surrogate 0.740 (+0.014 / +0.020); Gemini surrogate 0.732 (+0.005 / +0.011). C-index monotonically improves as more relevant risk factors are added.
- Mean predicted 10-year CVD risk varies widely: FRS 14.2%, GPT-4 surrogate 13.7%, Claude surrogate 11.8%, PCE and Gemini surrogate 7.9% — i.e., different LLMs encode quite different base rates.
- Medical-knowledge red flags: waist–hip-ratio coefficient differs 9.3× between Claude (3.948) and GPT-4 (0.426) and is a statistically insignificant negative (-0.313) in Gemini; uric-acid coefficient is positive in GPT-4 / Claude but negative in Gemini (-0.046) — contradicting known elevated-uric-acid → CVD associations.
- Race-bias red flags (eGFR): Black-race multipliers 1.153 (GPT-4), 1.227 (Claude-3.5-Sonnet, larger than the refuted 2009 CKD-EPI 1.159), 1.008 (Gemini). Clinically this overestimates eGFR in Black individuals and risks delayed CKD diagnosis. A similar pattern is confirmed for pulmonary function (Experiment 4).
- Operational wins: surrogate inference is ~900–2100× faster than direct API calls, deterministic per input, and mitigates the "streaking" clustering that Han et al. (ref 15) previously reported for direct GPT-4 queries.

## Critical Assessment

Novelty. Moderate. Surrogate / distillation-based explanation of black-box models is a well-established idea (decision-tree surrogates, LIME-global, functional-ANOVA distillation). The genuinely new piece is the clinical framing plus the use of a known parametric form (CKD-EPI) as the surrogate structure, which lets the authors directly read off and compare a refuted race coefficient. That is a nice, concrete use case rather than a methodological leap.

Rigor. Reasonable but selective. Strengths: bootstrap CIs, external UKB validation, temperature-0 sampling justified by a sweep, 10 repeats per input. Weaknesses: (i) the baselines PCE and FRS are very old and were not designed for a UK population — outperforming them by 0.010 C-index is not impressive and does not establish that the LLM itself (as opposed to the regression over 20 carefully chosen risk factors) carries predictive value; a modern logistic regression or gradient-boosted baseline fit directly on UKB would likely match or beat the surrogate. (ii) No ablation showing that the LLM's responses contribute anything beyond what a regression trained on the same simulated distribution with ground-truth physiological priors would give. (iii) Only one decoding temperature, one prompt template family, and one statistical model class (linear / log-linear) — so claims about "what the LLM perceives" are conditional on a narrow prompt distribution. (iv) The eGFR conclusion ("Claude's race bias is stronger than the refuted 2009 equation") rests on a log-linear fit whose functional form was imposed, not learned — if the LLM uses race in a non-multiplicative way, the coefficient is an artefact.

Reproducibility. Good at the recipe level — three stages are clearly described, prompt templates and variable lists are in the supplement, UKB access is covered. But model versions (gpt-4-0613, claude-3-5-sonnet-20241022, gemini-1.5-pro-002) are frozen APIs that deprecate; UKB access is gated; no code release is mentioned in the main text beyond the llm-surrogate.com demo.

Significance. The clinical message — that frontier LLMs, queried zero-shot in 2024, still encode race-based clinical adjustments that professional societies have formally retired — is genuinely useful and worth circulating. The methodological contribution is more modest.

Red flags / overclaiming. (a) "Surrogate beats PCE/FRS" is presented prominently but PCE/FRS are weak, US-derived baselines on a UK cohort. (b) "Quantitatively reveals bias" implicitly assumes the regression form is correct — an undetected bias could just as easily be a form-mis-specification. (c) The paper frames absence of bias as merely "does not guarantee immunity," which is appropriately hedged, but the headline claims are not equally hedged. (d) No discussion of whether the UKB cohort itself might be in the LLMs' training data (open-book risk is acknowledged but not empirically probed).

## Strengths

- Clean, replicable three-stage pipeline with explicit variable lists and prompt templates.
- External validation on a large real-world cohort (UK Biobank, n=357k) rather than self-reported simulated metrics only.
- Strong, concrete demonstration of a refuted race adjustment surviving inside commercial LLMs — a finding of real clinical interest.
- Addresses a practical pain point (run-to-run inconsistency and "streaking") by giving a deterministic surrogate that is 900–2100× cheaper at inference.
- SHAP-based local attribution plus public demo lower the barrier for clinicians to inspect results.

## Limitations

- Only three commercial LLMs and only zero-shot prompting; no open-weight models, no chain-of-thought, no system-prompt variations.
- Surrogate structure is fixed (linear or log-linear matching CKD-EPI). Nonlinear or interaction effects are invisible, and coefficients are only meaningful under the imposed form.
- Baselines (PCE, FRS) are weak; no modern ML baseline is trained directly on UKB for fair comparison.
- Training-data contamination (UKB or derived studies in LLM corpora) is acknowledged but not measured.
- "Medical appropriateness" of each coefficient is explicitly declared out of scope, yet the paper simultaneously claims to quantify correctness of encoded knowledge.
- No statistical-significance correction across the many coefficients reported.

## Relevance

与你关注的"LLM 推理评估 / 隐藏状态与可解释性 / ML 可靠性"高度相关：论文提供了一种无需访问权重即可对前沿 LLM 的隐式知识做可解释性审计的路径，并给出了在医学高风险场景下做"red-flag"红线指示器的具体做法，对系统卡（system card）和模型可靠性评估有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What does the surrogate look like if you replace linear/log-linear with a GAM or small MLP — how much variance is the current form leaving on the table?
- How does the refuted-race coefficient evolve across model generations (GPT-4 → 4o → 5; Claude 3.5 → 4; Gemini 1.5 → 2 / 2.5) and across RLHF safety updates?
- Does chain-of-thought or explicit "ignore race" system prompting remove the eGFR race multiplier, or merely hide it behind a verbal disclaimer while the numeric output still shifts?
- Can the framework be run with open-weight medical LLMs (Med-PaLM 2, GatorTron, Llama-3-Med) where logits are accessible, to cross-check surrogate coefficients against gradient-based attributions?
- What is the contamination rate of UK Biobank-derived risk equations in each LLM's training corpus, and how much of the C-index gain is memorization rather than reasoning?
- Applied to open-ended non-medical hypotheses (financial risk, legal sentencing), does surrogate modeling still give stable coefficients, or does prompt sensitivity dominate?
