Calibrating Conditional Risk

arXiv: 2604.20409v1

Date: 2026-04-22

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20409v1

一句话总结： 作者正式化"conditional risk calibration" 问题：估计预测器在输入 x 上的条件期望损失 g(x) = E[ℓ(f̂(X), Y) | X=x]；证明分类设置下这等价于一个标准回归问题，并建立条件风险校准与个体概率校准之间的等式关系——更好的概率校准 ⇒ 更紧的 conditional risk bound（Theorem 1）。在 Learning to Defer 上验证 calibration-based 方法严格优于 regression-based。
TL;DR: Formalizes the conditional risk calibration/estimation problem (estimating the expected loss conditional on input features) for both classification and regression. Shows it is equivalent to a standard regression task in general, and in classification can be recast through probability calibration with tighter generalization bounds (Theorem 1). Validates on Learning to Defer (L2D) — both regression-with-rejection and classification — where calibration-based methods surpass prior state of the art including SelectiveNet and NN+kNNRej.

核心贡献

  • 为 conditional risk calibration 给出统一数学框架（classification + regression）。
  • Theorem 1 建立：分类中，条件风险误差由概率校准误差一级放大——R_ca(θ) ≤ M · E[‖p_θ(X) − p(X)‖₁]。
  • Proposition 3 对比 calibration-based vs regression-based，前者 excess risk 仅受 Rademacher 复杂度 2R̂_n(H) 支配。
  • L2D 实验：RF calibrator + MLP2 regressor 在 30/32 设置上胜过或并列 NN+kNNRej。

Key Contributions

  • Definition of weak realizability and its role in making the calibration-based estimator unbiased for conditional risk.
  • Tight generalization bounds for both calibration-based and regression-based estimators.
  • Empirical identification that Platt scaling can hurt conditional-risk estimation (vs uncalibrated softmax via cross-entropy).
  • Demonstration on 8 UCI datasets × 4 rejection costs × 16 predictor-calibrator combinations.

Methodology

Classification: estimate p(y|x) via cross-entropy-trained model p_θ(x), plug into g_ca(x) = Σ_k ℓ(f̂(x), k) p_θ(x)_k. Regression: fit regressor directly on (x, z) pairs where z = ℓ(f̂(x_i), y_i). Compares CNN/ResNet/EfficientNet classifiers and LR/MLP/MLP2/RF calibrators on CIFAR-10. L2D: no-rejection-learning framework, rejector r_θ(x) = 1{g_θ(x) ≤ c} built from calibrator.

Results & Highlights

  • Probability calibrator L2 error: RF 0.31–0.36 vs LR 0.47–2.41 on Energy dataset.
  • Better classifier (EfficientNet) → lower calibrator error → lower RwR loss.
  • Platt scaling worsens calibration here (surprising finding) — probably due to group-level rather than individual recalibration.
  • Classification L2D: similar trends; stronger calibrator + stronger predictor = lowest L2D loss.

Authors & Credibility

Andrey Vasilyev, Yikai Wang, Xiaocheng Li (Imperial College Business School), Guanting Chen (UNC-Chapel Hill, corresponding). All established in OR / statistical learning theory. Solid credibility.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Medium-high. Re-frames an under-studied quantity (conditional risk) and gives matched generalization bounds for two estimators.
  • Rigor: Clean propositions and theorems; proofs deferred to appendix.
  • Reproducibility: UCI datasets, CIFAR-10, standard architectures; fold count and seed discussed.
  • Significance: Bridges calibration and defer-to-expert literatures with a principled connection.
  • Red flags: Theorem 1 bound has the factor M (bounded loss) which can be loose. The Platt-scaling-hurts observation is intriguing but not mechanistically explained. Experiments use small UCI datasets — generalization to large-scale LLM / safety-critical deployments unverified.

Strengths

  • First formal treatment of conditional risk calibration as a standalone problem.
  • Clean calibration-based vs regression-based comparison with explicit conditions (weak realizability).
  • Practical L2D wins over SOTA baselines.

Limitations

  • Assumes bounded loss M < ∞.
  • Empirical study limited to small UCI datasets + CIFAR-10.
  • Platt scaling observation needs further analysis.
  • Does not consider temporal drift.
