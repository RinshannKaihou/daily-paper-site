Methodology: Ledger Residuals Architecture

  • Splits the residual stream into two width-d tensors per token: a mutable Deliberation scratchpad (read/write/erase) and a protected append-only Commitment ledger (sole source for unembedding)
  • Commitment gate is biased to stay closed early and open later; sublayers can append but never overwrite the commitment stream
  • At specific gate settings the architecture reduces exactly to a standard pre-norm Transformer, ensuring fair comparison (only ~7% extra parameters at 160M scale)
  • Four configurations compared, all matched on validation loss: Vanilla (standard), Suppress (QK-norm), Ledger (light sparsity), Ledger-hs (6x sparsity)
  • Trained decoder-only on FineWeb-Edu at 160M and 290M parameters with GPT-2 BPE vocabulary
  • Metrics: excess kurtosis (magnitude), fixed-dimension ratio (structure), dominant-dimension persistence (cross-layer consistency), start-token concentration (position locking)

Results at 290M Scale

Configuration Perplexity Fixed-Dim Ratio Dom-Dim Persistence Start-Token Conc.
Vanilla ~56 28.3 0.92 1.01
Suppress (QK-norm) ~56 6.8 -- 0.13
Ledger 55.4–57.7 7.0 0.58 3.36
Ledger-hs (6x sparse) 55.4–57.7 -- 0.96 4.69
  • The Commitment channel rebuilds a massive activation on the same dimension (#22) as the Deliberation stream's outlier
  • Forcing sparsity makes the outlier stronger: model concentrates scarce commits into one nearly ever-present dimension
  • No measurable capability difference across all four configurations (perplexity within narrow band)

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean experimental design: reduction property guarantees apples-to-apples comparison
  • Sparsity-intensification result is particularly compelling -- the feature is actively prioritized, not merely tolerated
  • Well-motivated by established literature (Sun et al. 2024, Bondarenko et al. 2023, Elhage et al. 2021)
  • Code released; small-scale models on public data ensure reproducibility
  • Honest about what the result does and does not show

Limitations

  • Scale limited to 290M; phenomenon may differ at billion-parameter scale
  • Single training seed per configuration limits statistical confidence
  • Only English web text (FineWeb-Edu); no multilingual or domain-specific evaluation
  • Does not adjudicate between competing functional explanations (attention sink, bias term, gradient regulator)
  • Ledger Residuals is one specific protected-channel design; other designs might yield different results

Open Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the result hold at billion-parameter scale (7B, 70B) where massive activations are most extreme?
  • Can activation quantization (not just weight quantization) reveal a capability difference between configurations?
  • What happens with alternative protected-channel designs -- e.g., a small-dimensional commitment vector instead of a full-width stream?
  • Could the commitment channel's massive activation be leveraged for more efficient inference?
  • Does the result change with different normalization schemes (LayerNorm vs. RMSNorm) or post-norm architectures?
