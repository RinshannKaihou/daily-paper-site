Beyond Importance: Interchange-Sobol Sensitivity Reveals Task-Specific Content Channels in Transformer Components

Yifeng Guo · Jin-Hong Du · Xiang Chen
STAI-X 2026 Full Paper Track · 2026-06-22

Standard interpretability methods conflate two reasons a transformer component matters: it carries task-relevant content vs. it is structurally needed. IGSD separates these by pairing swap and zero interventions on the same component, revealing hidden content channels that existing methods miss.

Key Finding
IGSD decomposes component roles into content-transport channels vs. deletion-sensitive substrates, uncovering an early MLP layer (MLPL0) that carries relation-frame content invisible to DLA, zero-ablation, and AtP*.
Key Metric
#1 vs #9–#13
IGSD ranks MLPL0 first among 24 groups; standard methods rank it 9th to 13th. Late-layer clamping recovers 82% of swap-induced variance.
Why This Matters
Replacement and deletion are not interchangeable controls. IGSD provides the first quantitative, component-level separation of content vs. substrate roles — enabling more accurate circuit discovery and targeted pruning.
Critical Edge
Tested only on GPT-2 small & Qwen2.5-1.5B with single-token retrieval tasks. Donor matching depends on DLA features (circular dependency). No code release documented.

How IGSD Works

  • Swap: Replace component activation with a matched donor's activation → measures content sensitivity
  • Zero: Replace the same activation with the zero vector → measures structural sensitivity
  • Sobol indices: Compute ST,swap and ST,zero from output variance under each intervention
  • δ(g) = ST,swap − ST,zero: Positive = content channel; negative = substrate
  • Off-manifold flag: ST > 1 excludes unreliable interventions
  • Factorial buckets: same-subject / same-relation donors identify what content a channel carries

Task Specificity

The sign of δ is not an anatomical constant — it depends on the task:

  • Factual recall: MLPL0 is content-positive (δ > 0), AttnL9 carries subject-retrieval
  • IOI: Roles reverse — AttnL9 becomes dominant positive-δ; MLPL0 becomes substrate
  • Synthetic induction: Yet another profile — AttnL10 dominates; most components are substrate

Same component, different task, different role. This is a feature of the framework, not a flaw.

Downstream Validation

  • Late-layer clamping: Clamping late Attn + late MLP recovers 82% of swap-induced variance (95% CI [0.77, 0.86])
  • Unique contributions: late Attn = 0.17, late MLP = 0.57
  • The early relation-frame signal is functionally transformed by later layers, not merely passed through residually
  • Pruning: Top-2 IGSD groups retain 70.0% accuracy vs. 57.4% under zero-ablation ranking (+12.6 pp)

Theoretical Guarantees & Generalization

  • Prop 1: Mis-specification bound on Sobol indices
  • Prop 2: Asymptotic normality of paired bootstrap
  • Prop 3: Content-factor identification via factorial buckets
  • Prop 4: Role-label grounding in latent content-substrate mass
  • Cross-model: In Qwen2.5-1.5B, dominant factual component shifts to AttnL0 — early-layer location preserved, module type changes

Strengths & Limitations at a Glance

Strengths

  • Principled statistical framework with formal proofs
  • Factorial bucket design elegantly identifies transported content type
  • Late-layer clamping goes beyond correlation to functional validation
  • Task-specificity of δ presented honestly, not hidden
  • Unusually thorough appendix and robustness analyses

Limitations

  • Single-token prediction only; no multi-token or non-retrieval tasks
  • Small models only (GPT-2 small, Qwen2.5-1.5B)
  • Zero ablation is one specific baseline; δ is relative, not absolute
  • k-NN donor matching uses DLA features (potential circularity)
  • No code or data release documented

Open Questions

  • How does IGSD scale to 7B+ models where layer-local groups are much larger?
  • Can donor matching use propensity scoring to reduce DLA-based confounding?
  • Does the early MLP relation-frame channel correspond to specific SAE features?
  • How does IGSD behave on multi-hop reasoning, arithmetic, or open-ended generation?
  • Can content-substrate decomposition inform targeted knowledge editing (ROME/MEMIT)?
