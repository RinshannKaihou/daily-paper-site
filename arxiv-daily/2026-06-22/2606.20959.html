Right Knowledge, Wrong Answer: Test-Time Steering for Temporal Fact Conflicts in Open-Weight LLMs

Elias Hossain · Sourav Saha · Umesh Chandra Biswas · Sanjeda Sara Jennifer
22 June 2026

LLMs store both outdated and updated facts in their parameters — but preferentially surface the outdated ones. Temporal Attractor Steering (TAS) recovers 29–57% of these suppressed facts at inference time, with no retraining and no retrieval.

Key Finding
PTC

Parametric Temporal Conflict — models have the new fact but prefer the old one. A distinct, formalized failure mode separate from knowledge absence.

Key Metric
56.9%

Best recovery rate (Qwen-2.5-7B) while preserving 94.8% clean accuracy. Evaluated on 8,746 Wikidata-verified records across 4 models.

Why This Matters
Scales up

PTC rate grows monotonically with model capacity (4.1% → 10.3%). Bigger models suppress more updated knowledge, not less. TAS is retrieval-free.

Critical Edge
Limits

Single-hop facts only. Requires open weights. No RAG baseline comparison. Mistral underperforms ITI by −0.056. Instruction-tuned models excluded.

TAS Pipeline — Detect, Locate, Steer

1
Detect

3

PTC Screening Findings

  • Outdated-preference rate is family-invariant (0.530–0.544) across all models
  • Filtered PTC rate scales with capacity: 4.1% (1.5B) → 7.1% (7B) → 8.5% (Mistral) → 10.3% (Llama-8B)
  • Single-layer activation patching achieves 0.72–0.85 answer-flip rates
  • Conflict-critical layer l* consistently in upper half of network
  • Elicitation gap positive for every model (+0.090 to +0.180 nats)

TAS vs. ITI Baseline

  • TAS outperforms ITI on 3 of 4 models with recovery gains of +0.05 to +0.15
  • Mistral-7B exception: ITI beats TAS by 0.056 — behavioral-contrast direction aligns better with Mistral's conflict layer
  • TAS uses verified-conflict Δ; ITI uses behavioral-contrast Δ

Strengths

  • Cleanly separates recoverable-but-suppressed knowledge from genuine absence
  • Detector gate avoids unnecessary intervention — 85–99% clean accuracy preserved
  • l* needs identification once per model family, not per query
  • Benchmark publicly released with Wikidata provenance
  • Knowledge-recovery filter (log P ≥ −3) prevents confounded PTC rates

Limitations

  • Restricted to single-hop superseding updates with binary answers
  • Requires open-weight model access (residual-stream intervention)
  • No comparison with RAG or weight-editing (ROME/MEMIT) baselines
  • Only 4 models from 3 families tested — no 70B+, MoE, or instruction-tuned models
  • Detector is the primary bottleneck (oracle gap: 0.123 on Qwen-1.5B)
  • Theoretical proofs deferred; practical verification gap remains

Benchmark & Resources

8,746 Wikidata-verified records spanning 5 relations (head of state, CEO, head of government, head coach, chairperson) across 3 domains (politics, corporate, sports).
