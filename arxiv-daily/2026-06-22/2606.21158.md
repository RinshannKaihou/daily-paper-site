> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"死方向签名"（Dead-Direction Signatures, DDS），通过对网络激活矩阵和Fisher-Gram矩阵进行廉价的谱分析（闭式计算），替代昂贵的SGLD后验采样来读取深度网络损失奇异点的几何复杂度（RLCT），在参数化测试平台上以低两个数量级的成本追踪奇异结构，并在非线性Transformer上跨越18个数量级分辨模型宽度，而校准LLC在相同预算下完全失效。

> **TL;DR:** DDS introduces three cheap closed-form spectral observables -- smallest activation singular value, smallest positive Fisher-Gram eigenvalue, and active-spectrum log-volume -- that read a network's "dead directions" (rank-deficient singular geometry) from activations and gradients at a single checkpoint. On parametric testbeds with closed-form RLCT, DDS rank-tracks the analytical complexity at ~300x lower cost than calibrated LLC. On a non-linear modular-addition transformer, DDS separates model widths across 18 orders of magnitude where calibrated LLC is rank-flat, demonstrating a complementary and far cheaper readout of singular structure.

## 核心贡献

- **定义三个DDS可观测量**：最小激活奇异值 sigma_min(X_l)、最小正Fisher-Gram特征值 lambda_min^+(G_l)、以及活跃谱对数体积 log det^+(G_l)，均可从单次前向/反向传播中闭式计算
- **建立结构相关性定理**：证明在任意奇异极小值处，Fisher侧和激活侧的谱底部以可预测比率共同趋零（Spearman相关趋向+1），由KFAC分解和A-G对偶性保证
- **发现秩乘性体积恒等式**：log det^+(G_l) 的斜率随秩缺陷 r 线性缩放，而最小特征值对 r 不变——这是单秩谱监测器无法捕获的判别性测试
- **在闭式RLCT测试平台上验证**：14个Aoyagi 2005单元上DDS以|rho|>=0.89追踪解析RLCT，校准LLC恢复lambda的99%均值
- **在非线性Transformer上展示实用优势**：Nanda模块加法任务上，DDS可观测量跨4个宽度单调变化（跨5-18个数量级），而校准LLC在4400步预算下完全秩平坦，9倍预算也无法改善

## Key Contributions

- **Three DDS observables defined**: sigma_min(X_l) (cheapest, one forward pass + SVD), lambda_min^+(G_l) (Fisher-Gram smallest positive eigenvalue), and log det^+(G_l) (active-spectrum log-volume), all computed in closed form without posterior sampling
- **Structural correlation theorem proven**: At any singular minimum of an L-layer network with KFAC-decomposable Fisher, activation-side and Fisher-side spectral bottoms vanish as co-monotonic positive powers of the distance to the singularity, with Spearman correlation tending to +1 asymptotically
- **Rank-multiplicative volume identity discovered**: The log det^+ slope scales linearly in rank-deficit r while lambda_min^+ is r-invariant -- a strict quantitative identity invisible to single-eigenvalue monitors
- **Validated on closed-form RLCT benchmarks**: On 14 Aoyagi 2005 cells, all DDS observables rank-track analytical lambda at |rho| in [0.89, 0.98]; on 24-cell deep-linear network sweep, DDS reads constancy above saturation where calibrated LLC drifts ~37%
- **Demonstrated on non-linear transformer**: On Nanda modular-addition with AdamW+CE, DDS spans 5-18 orders of magnitude monotonically in d_model while calibrated LLC is rank-flat (rho = -0.10) even with 9x SGLD budget

## Methodology

The paper builds on Watanabe's singular learning theory (SLT), where model complexity is characterized by the real log canonical threshold (RLCT, denoted lambda). The standard estimator, the local learning coefficient (LLC), requires expensive SGLD posterior sampling (10^4 to 10^6 forward-backward passes per checkpoint).

**Dead-direction framework.** A "dead direction" u at parameter theta_0 is a unit direction where the Fisher information vanishes: u^T F(theta(t)) u -> 0 as t -> 0 along theta(t) = theta_0 + tu. The KL order k >= 1 controls the vanishing rate: K(theta_0 + tu) = c t^{2k} + O(t^{2k+1}). The selection rule (Theorem 3 from the companion theory paper) bridges the Fisher rate exponent 2(k-1) to the directional RLCT contribution 1/(2k).

**Three observables.** At each layer l of an L-layer network:
1. **Rate observable**: lambda_min^+(G_l) -- smallest strictly positive eigenvalue of the per-sample-gradient Fisher-Gram. Predicted by the Fisher decay theorem to collapse at the bottleneck layer when dead directions are present (~246x collapse observed).
2. **Volume observable**: log det^+(G_l) -- sum of log of strictly positive eigenvalues. From the curvature-volume rate chain, its slope scales as -2r(k-1) log t for r independent dead directions, making it rank-multiplicative.
3. **Activation-side dual**: sigma_min(X_l) -- smallest singular value of the activation matrix. Connected to the Fisher side via A-G duality (Corollary 25): lambda_min^+(G_l) proportional to sigma_min(X_l)^2.

**Structural correlation theorem (Theorem 2).** Under canonical-aligned approach to a singular minimum, at every layer l: lambda_min^+(G_l) = Theta(t^{2(L-l)}) and sigma_min(X_l)^2/N = Theta(t^{2l}), both vanishing as strictly positive powers of t, making them co-monotonic with Spearman rho -> +1 asymptotically.

**Measurement protocol.** sigma_min requires one forward pass + SVD at O(Nh^2). Fisher-side observables assemble the per-sample-gradient Fisher-Gram and take O(h^3) eigendecomposition. All are orders of magnitude cheaper than LLC's 4,400 SGLD steps.

## Results & Highlights

**Experiment 1 -- Detection and rank-deficit counting (deep-linear bridges).** On 7 noisy-bridge configurations (L in {4,6,8}, D in {20,50}, 5 seeds, r=1..4), the log det^+ slope ratio tracks rank-deficit: cell-wise means 2.0, 3.1, 4.0 at r=2,3,4 against predicted 2,3,4. The lambda_min^+ ratio stays at 1 (rank-blind), confirming the rank-multiplicative identity.

**Experiment 2 -- Closed-form RLCT anchor (Aoyagi 2005).** On 14 cells with analytical lambda in [9, 25], all three DDS rate-chain observables rank-track lambda at |rho| in [0.89, 0.98]. Calibrated LLC recovers lambda at 99% mean. Cross-cell rank-correlation is a sanity gate: even a naive H*r proxy scores 0.99.

**Experiment 3 -- Non-linear transformer (Nanda modular-addition).** On 101 grokked cells (4 widths x 30 seeds, AdamW+CE), DDS observables span 5-15 orders of magnitude and are monotone in d_model, sign-coherent at |rho| in [0.62, 0.96]. Calibrated LLC at 4,400-step budget is rank-flat (rho = -0.10). A 9x SGLD budget increase does not help. DDS separates d_model across ~10^18 where LLC cannot.

**Experiment 4 -- Static structural correlation.** The identity lambda_min^+(G_l) proportional to sigma_min(X_l)^2 holds at rho >= +0.75 across 4 regime cells (canonical bridge at +1.000, Barak SGD+MSE at +0.832, Nanda SGD+MSE at +0.846, Nanda AdamW+CE boundary at +0.751).

**Experiment 5 -- Grokking trajectory (Barak).** DDS drops ~10 orders through at-grok + cleanup phases while calibrated LLC plateaus, dynamically resolving the singular structure transition.

**Cost comparison.** On a Barak post-grok checkpoint (h=500): Fisher-side observables are ~300x cheaper than calibrated LLC, sigma_min is ~130x cheaper. At LLM widths, sigma_min and u^T Gu scale linearly in h while Fisher eigendecomposition scales as O(h^3).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The idea of reading singular complexity through cheap spectral observables rather than expensive SGLD sampling is genuinely new. The rank-multiplicative volume identity is a discriminating test that no single-eigenvalue monitor can replicate.
- **Rigor**: Strong on the parametric testbeds. The paper carefully distinguishes what is a sanity gate (cross-cell rank-correlation, which even naive proxies pass) from what is discriminating (the rank-multiplicative identity). The structural correlation theorem has a complete proof. Falsifiers are explicitly stated.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Per-experiment hyperparameters, SGLD calibration locks, numerical recipes (fp64 + no-Tikhonov), and a canonical observable library are documented in detail. Hardware envelope specified.
- **Significance**: The cost reduction (100-300x) is meaningful for practical SLT adoption. The transformer result (separating widths where LLC fails) is striking but limited to one architecture/task.
- **Red flags**: (1) The transformer extension is a single-block, single-task probe on grokked models only -- whether DDS extends to non-grokked transformers is explicitly left open. (2) The canonical-alignment hypothesis required for trajectory-rate predictions is violated under Adam+CE, limiting the trajectory-rate readout (though the static structural correlation survives). (3) The paper does not provide posterior-Bayesian summaries (multiplicity m, singular fluctuation nu, WAIC) that LLC can. (4) The companion theory paper is concurrent unpublished work -- the empirical paper imports four key theorems as cited results.

## Strengths

- Addresses a genuine bottleneck: LLC's SGLD cost is prohibitive at LLM scale, and DDS provides activation-side readings at real-time cadence (one forward pass)
- Rigorous validation strategy: anchoring to closed-form RLCT rather than only comparing to LLC avoids circularity
- Honest about scope limitations: explicitly lists where DDS applies and doesn't, defines concrete falsifiers, and distinguishes sanity gates from discriminating tests
- Clean separation of contributions: the structural correlation is geometric (survives Adam+CE), the trajectory rate requires canonical alignment, and the paper keeps these distinct
- Detailed reproducibility appendix with numerical recipes, fp64 vs fp32 comparisons, and per-testbed SGLD calibration audits

## Limitations

- Transformer coverage is narrow: single-block modular-addition with grokking; attention-only chains, deeper transformers, and non-grokked models are unexplored
- DDS reads directional/layer-local information only -- it does not estimate the integrated RLCT lambda or provide model-selection scores like WAIC
- The theory requires KFAC-decomposable Fisher and (for trajectory rates) canonical alignment; these assumptions are not generic for Adam-trained networks
- Activation-side sigma_min is the cheapest but scales with N/D; at deep singular checkpoints it can saturate at the Marchenko-Pastur noise floor
- The companion theory paper is concurrent and unpublished, making independent verification of the imported theorems difficult

## Relevance

本文为奇异学习理论（SLT）提供了一套廉价的谱诊断工具，有望降低SLT在大模型上的应用门槛。对于关注深度学习理论、损失景观几何、模型复杂度度量或grokking现象的研究者，DDS提供了一种不依赖SGLD采样的互补视角，尤其适合需要实时或高频监控训练过程中奇异结构变化的场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can DDS observables be extended to attention-only chains and deeper transformers beyond single-block grokking models?
2. Is there a principled way to aggregate per-layer DDS readings into a global complexity estimate comparable to the integrated RLCT?
3. How does sigma_min behave during pretraining of LLMs -- can it serve as a practical rank-collapse early warning?
4. Can the structural correlation be strengthened from Spearman rank to a quantitative magnitude prediction under finite-sample, finite-distance conditions?
5. What happens to DDS observables on tasks/architectures where the network does not reach a singular minimum (e.g., early stopping, regularization preventing convergence to the singular set)?
