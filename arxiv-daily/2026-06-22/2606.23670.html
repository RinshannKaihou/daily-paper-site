Tapered Language Models

Reza Bayat, Ali Behrouz, Aaron Courville
22 Jun 2026 4 architectures tested 3 model scales (440M–1.3B)

Monotonically decreasing MLP width across depth — wider early layers, narrower late layers — consistently improves language model performance across four architecture families and three scales, at zero additional parameter or compute cost.

MLP-width-allocation depth-aware-design parameter-efficiency capacity-allocation
Key Finding Front-load MLP capacity Earlier layers produce more novel, orthogonal representations. Later layers increasingly reinforce existing residual stream content. Reallocating width exploits this asymmetry.
Key Metric −1.84 Perplexity improvement (16.28 → 14.44) with cosine decay at 1.5/0.5 width ratio on 440M Transformer. 8/8 arch-scale combos improve.
Why This Matters Free lunch A few lines of code generate per-layer widths. No extra parameters, no extra FLOPs, no training recipe changes. Works across Transformer, Gated Attention, Hope, and Titans.
Critical Edge Modest & unscaled Gains are 0.3–1.0 accuracy points. Sweep done only at 440M scale. Unclear if benefits persist at 7B+. Only MLP width tapered — attention heads untouched.

Methodology

  • Motivating experiment: 440M Transformer split into 3 groups (early/mid/late) with varying MLP widths but same total params. "Wider-early" (1.5x/1.0x/0.5x) beats uniform baseline by 0.32 ppl; reverse allocation hurts by >1 point.
  • Tapering formulation: Replace constant d_ff with per-layer d_ff(l) that decreases monotonically, constrained so the average equals the baseline. Widths rounded to multiples of 16 for hardware efficiency.
  • Three decay schedules tested: Linear, Cosine, and Sigmoid. Cosine dominates at every width ratio in a 5×3 grid search.
  • Optimal configuration: Cosine schedule with 1.5/0.5 ratio (first layer 1.5× baseline d_ff, last layer 0.5×).
Cosine: d_ff(l) = d_end + (d_start − d_end)/2 · (1 + cos(π · l/(L−1)))
Linear: d_ff(l) = d_start − (d_start − d_end) · l/(L−1)
Constraint: avg(d_ff(l)) = baseline d_ff ⇒ same params & FLOPs

Strengths

  • Clean isolation: Single axis of variation (MLP width), matched parameters and FLOPs, identical training recipe. Rare experimental rigor.
  • Architecture breadth: Four genuinely different architectures (softmax attention, gated attention, self-modifying memory, neural long-term memory) — suggests the finding is about depth itself.
  • Mechanistic grounding: Layer-wise novelty measurement (ρMLP) provides a principled reason beyond empirical gains.
  • Zero cost: No additional parameters, FLOPs, or training changes required.

Limitations

  • Hyperparameter transfer: Schedule/ratio sweep only at 440M — optimal config may differ at other scales and architectures.
  • Modest absolute gains: 0.3–1.0 accuracy points consistently, but not transformative.
  • Scale uncertainty: No experiments at 7B+ or 70B+. Gains may diminish or vanish at frontier scale.
  • Single axis only: Only MLP width tapered. Attention heads, KV dimensions, expert count remain uniform.
  • No head-to-head: Missing direct comparisons with concurrent layer-capacity work (Ikeda et al. 2025, Baroian & Notebomer 2025).

Relevance & Implications

This work identifies a fundamental and under-examined design choice: uniform parameter allocation across depth is suboptimal. The fix is trivially implementable (a few lines of code) and architecture-agnostic. The mechanistic insight — later layers contribute increasingly redundant information — connects to the broader literature on layer importance, early exit, and model compression, potentially informing pruning strategies and efficient inference. For practitioners, it is a free improvement to adopt immediately for any new training run.
