Training Theory

Learning through Internalization: How Networks Compress Chain-of-Thought into Weights

N. Tsilivis, N. Joshi, M. Medvedev, J. Kempe, N. Srebro

First formal study of internalization — how networks absorb explicit chain-of-thought into weights. On semiautomata and parity tasks, width-over-depth beats depth for internalization, prime-order tasks are algebraically harder to compress, and internalization carries a fundamental OOD degradation tradeoff. Theorem 2 provides the first provable example of learning via internalization.

首次形式化研究"内化"——网络如何将显式链式思维压缩进权重。在半自动机和奇偶校验任务上，宽度优于深度，素数阶任务更难压缩，且内化伴随根本性OOD退化。定理2提供了首个可证明的内化学习实例。

Numerical Reliability

HEAL: INT16 Quantization Achieves FP32 Reproducibility, 7.1× Faster

Z. Zhu, L. Thai, S. Yu, Y. Liu, Y. Qiao, C. Wang, H. Xu, J. Shu

SASS-level profiling reveals LLM non-reproducibility stems from boundary truncation at CUDA kernel edges, not arithmetic. HEAL uses INT16 Q/K/V (more precise than BF16) plus algebraic error compensation, achieving FP32-level reproducibility at up to 7.1× the speed of full FP32.

SASS指令级剖析揭示LLM推理不可复现性源于CUDA内核边界截断而非算术本身。HEAL用INT16量化+代数误差补偿实现FP32级可复现性，性能比FP32快7.1倍。
