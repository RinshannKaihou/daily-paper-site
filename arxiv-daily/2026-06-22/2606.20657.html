A-Evolve-Training: Autonomous Post-Training of a 30B Model

Zhan Shi, Bing He, Yisi Sang, Hanqing Lu

Autonomous post-training loop at 30B scale detected its own proxy metric failure and reversed search strategy without human intervention.

Key Metric
0.86
Leaderboard score — vs. 0.87 human best, rank 8 / ~4,000
Why This Matters
First proof that an AI training loop can autonomously discover when its internal metrics mislead—moving beyond optimization toward genuine scientific discovery at frontier scale.
Critical Edge
Single unreplicated campaign on one benchmark. The “discovery” may be an artifact of hard-coded constitutional meta-rules, not emergent capability.

Methodology

  • Immutable reference substrate: A human-verified workspace (checkpoint, data, training, eval) is forked each round into isolated sandboxes—never overwritten.
  • Homogeneous memory-free workers: N=8 identical agents per round (1 anchor baseline + 7 exploring orthogonal axes). Earlier specialized-role designs failed due to unobserved variance compounding.
  • Constitutionally bounded meta-agent: A one-shot reviewer aggregates worker manifests into a five-section evidence record. A meta-agent under a frozen constitution rewrites only the next round's search policy.
  • Asymmetric freedom principle: Zero degrees of freedom on invariant axes (substrate, evaluation); maximal freedom on the exploration axis (semantic mutations to training recipes).
  • Eval target: NVIDIA Nemotron-Reasoning Challenge on Kaggle; post-training a shared 30B Nemotron 3 Nano on reasoning puzzles.

Results

  • 0.86 held-out leaderboard score across four autonomous rounds, vs. 0.87 top human submission, ranking 8th / ~4,000
  • Strategic reversal: In Round 3, dev score hit 0.93 but leaderboard stalled at 0.85—the loop detected the proxy had decoupled from the external target
  • Recovery: Round 4 intentionally searched for interventions that might lower the proxy while improving external transfer, recovering to 0.86
  • Scale-up: System also applied to 120B and 550B Nemotron models end-to-end, though no public quantitative comparison exists
  • First at frontier scale: All prior autonomous ML research operated at GPT-2-class (~124M); this scales to 30B+ on multi-week H200 GPU runs

Strengths

  • Genuinely novel in scale—first public autonomous post-training at 30B, beyond GPT-2-class regime
  • Intellectually honest framing with deliberately narrow claims and explicit limitation disclosure
  • Proxy-reversal finding is a compelling, well-documented case study of autonomous discovery
  • Clean system design with concrete failure modes of rejected alternatives (e.g., specialized-role agents)
  • Practical design lessons transferable to anyone building autonomous training loops
  • Competitive result on a real public leaderboard with ~4,000 entries

Limitations

  • Single base model (30B Nemotron) and single benchmark—generalizability unknown
  • No replication; proxy-reversal finding rests on one trajectory
  • No ablation studies on the three architectural pillars
  • Reproducibility prohibitively expensive (multi-H200 clusters, multi-week runs)
  • Code, substrate, and trained checkpoints not yet released
  • Still requires a human-authored immutable substrate—full autonomy overstated
  • Meta-agent “discovery” may be an artifact of frozen constitutional meta-rules

Round-by-Round Trajectory

Round 0 Dev 0.79 · LB 0.80 Substrate baseline. Human-authored starting point establishes anchor scores.
Round 1 Dev 0.82 · LB 0.82 Recipe stabilization. Basic training levers (schedule shape, optimizer hygiene, LoRA capacity, gradient clipping) proved causal.
Round 2 Dev 0.84 · LB 0.84 Stack-and-calibrate. Seed noise became binding; the loop began treating replication and calibration as research problems.
Round 3 Dev 0.93 · LB 0.85 Proxy exploitation detected. Dev soared by fitting the weak equation domain (ceiling ~0.65 → 0.82), but external target barely moved. The loop identified the proxy had decoupled.
Round 4 Dev 0.89 · LB 0.86 Proxy-aware search. Strategy inverted: searched for interventions that transfer externally (domain rebalancing, reweighted checkpoint selection). External progress recovered.

Open Questions

  1. How sensitive is the proxy-reversal finding to the meta-agent's frozen constitution? Would different constitutional meta-rules produce the same discovery?
  2. Can the system generalize beyond reasoning puzzles to other post-training objectives (instruction following, safety alignment, coding)?
  3. What is the total compute cost of the four-round campaign, and how does it compare to human-directed post-training at the same scale?
  4. How would the system behave if the immutable substrate were of lower quality—does it degrade gracefully or catastrophically?
  5. Could the asymmetric freedom principle be formalized into a theoretical framework for autonomous research system design?
