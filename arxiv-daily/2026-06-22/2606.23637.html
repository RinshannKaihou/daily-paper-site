Riemannian Optimization Transformer Pretraining Angular Step-size MoE

Muown Implicitly Performs Angular Step-size Decay

Florian Hübler, Kai Lion, Antonio Orvieto, Niao He · 22 Jun 2026

AngularMuown makes Muown's hidden angular annealing explicit and schedulable, decoupling directional from radial dynamics — a drop-in replacement that achieves ~2× speedup over AdamW on transformer pretraining.

Key Finding
Muown's directional update is a Riemannian steepest-descent step on the row-oblique manifold; growing row norms silently anneal the angular step-size during training.
Key Metric
2×
speedup over AdamW on 124M PlainLM; 1.5× over NorMuon/Muown
Why This Matters
Drop-in optimizer replacement with explicit angular scheduling. Learning rate transfers perfectly across model widths (67M–300M), enabling cheap hyperparameter search on small models for large-scale training.
Critical Edge
All experiments capped at 1.1B params (language-only). Convergence theory applies to an idealized variant, not the practical algorithm. Vision/multimodal untested.

Core Algorithm

AngularMuown reparameterizes W = Diag(g) · U, where g are per-row magnitudes updated via Adam, and U lives on the oblique manifold (rows on unit spheres).

κt = (1 + c · (t - tw)+)-p
sm,n = √max(1, m/n)
tan(θi) = η · κt · sm,n · ||Oi⊥||2 ≈ η · κt

The shape scale sm,n makes the per-row angular displacement dimension-independent, giving the step physical meaning: ~57 · η · κt degrees.

Key Contributions

  • Riemannian interpretation — Muown's directional update = retracted, spectrally-conditioned Riemannian gradient step on Mob.
  • AngularMuown algorithm — Decouples angular (κt) and radial (Adam on g) dynamics; inverse-polynomial schedule with defaults c=0.001, p=1.
  • Convergence guarantee — O(min{m,n} · Δ1 · L · σ2 · ε-4) gradient evaluations for ε-stationarity.
  • Width transfer — Optimal learning rate transfers across 67M, 124M, 300M without retuning.

Empirical Results

  • PlainLM 124M: Outperforms all baselines at every checkpoint (2.5B–10B tokens). ~2× over AdamW.
  • Qwen2-0.5B: Best loss 2.648 at η=4e-2. Unscheduled variant degrades sharply at large LR.
  • 1.1B MoE: Loss 2.4807 vs Muown's 2.4914. Gains persist in sparse setup.
  • nanoGPT: Leads per-optimizer category of modded nanoGPT speedrun.

Strengths

  • Clean geometric insight that transforms an ad-hoc design into a principled framework
  • Unifies weight decay, implicit annealing, and explicit scheduling under one angular lens
  • Concrete speedrun competition results validate practical impact
  • Dimension-independent shape scaling validated by width transfer

Limitations

  • Scale ceiling: 1.1B params; 10B+ behavior unknown
  • Theory–practice gap: convergence proof covers idealized variant only
  • Schedule hyperparameters (c, p, tw) not stress-tested beyond default regime
  • No comparison with concurrent Hagele et al. (2026)
