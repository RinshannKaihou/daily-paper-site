> **一句话总结：** CELEUS利用E-process构建任意时刻有效的置信区间，结合不确定性引导采样和代理模型辅助近似，以比基线少54-62%的评估样本达到目标精度，同时严格保持统计覆盖率保证。

> **TL;DR:** CELEUS is an e-process-based framework for certifiable LLM evaluation that builds anytime-valid confidence intervals through two key ingredients -- uncertainty-guided sampling and surrogate-assisted approximations -- achieving target evaluation precision with 54-62% fewer labeled samples than baselines while provably maintaining coverage guarantees (unlike Cer-Eval, which suffers from coverage violations due to adaptive partitioning).

## 核心贡献
- 提出CELEUS框架，利用E-process构建条件无偏的推断信号，生成任意时刻有效（anytime-valid）的置信区间，解决了现有方法（如Cer-Eval）在自适应分区下覆盖率失效的理论-实践鸿沟
- 设计两个关键组件：(1) 不确定性引导采样策略，优先选择代理残差较大的样本以降低方差；(2) 代理模型辅助近似，利用7-8B小模型为未评估样本提供低成本的风险估计
- 证明CELEUS的置信区间以近参数速率收缩（至对数因子），并给出方差最优采样规则的oracle特征
- 在SST-2、MMLU、AG News三个基准上，评估Llama-3-70B、DeepSeek-67B、Mixtral-8x7B、Qwen-2.5-72B等模型，以54-62%更少的样本达到目标精度

## Key Contributions
- Proposes CELEUS, leveraging e-processes to build conditionally unbiased inferential signals that yield anytime-valid confidence intervals, bridging the gap between theoretical guarantees and practical adaptive evaluation (where Cer-Eval's coverage breaks down)
- Introduces two synergistic components: (1) uncertainty-guided sampling that prioritizes samples with large surrogate-residual magnitudes to reduce signal variance, and (2) surrogate-assisted approximations using small (7-8B) pre-trained models to cheaply estimate risks for unevaluated samples
- Proves that CELEUS CIs shrink at a near-parametric rate up to logarithmic factors, and characterizes the oracle variance-optimal sampling distribution
- Demonstrates 54-62% sample reduction over baselines on SST-2, MMLU, and AG News, evaluating models including Llama-3-70B, DeepSeek-67B, Mixtral-8x7B, and Qwen-2.5-72B

## Methodology

**Problem Setup.** Given an LLM $f$, a bounded risk function $\ell: \mathcal{Y} \times \mathcal{Y} \to [L, U]$, and a data pool $D_N$ of $N$ i.i.d. samples, the goal is to estimate the population risk $R(f; \mathbb{P}_{x,y})$ with certifiable precision. The evaluation proceeds sequentially: at each round $t$, one sample is selected from the pool, evaluated (via human annotation or LLM judge), and the confidence interval is updated.

**E-Process Construction.** For each candidate risk value $v$, CELEUS constructs an e-process $\{\mathcal{K}_t(v)\}_{t \geq 0}$ that measures evidence against the null hypothesis $\mathcal{H}_0(v): R_N = v$. The CI at time $t$ is formed by keeping all values not yet rejected: $\text{CI}_t = \{v : \mathcal{K}_t(v) < 1/\alpha\}$. By the anytime-valid property of e-processes, non-coverage probability is bounded by $\alpha$ at any stopping time.

**Per-Round Inferential Signal.** The signal $\hat{S}_t$ at each round combines: (1) a surrogate-completed estimate of the data-pool risk (observed risks for evaluated samples + surrogate risk scores for unevaluated ones), and (2) an inverse-probability-weighted correction for the newly selected sample's surrogate residual $\ell(f(\mathbf{x}_{i_t}), y_{i_t}) - \tilde{\ell}_f(\mathbf{x}_{i_t})$. This signal is proven conditionally unbiased for $R_N$ (Theorem 3).

**Uncertainty-Guided Sampling.** The variance-optimal sampling rule (Theorem 4) assigns selection probabilities proportional to surrogate-residual magnitudes: $q_t^*(j) \propto |\ell(f(\mathbf{x}_j), y_j) - \tilde{\ell}_f(\mathbf{x}_j)|$. Since true residuals are unknown, CELEUS uses empirical approximations based on the discrepancies observed so far. This reduces conditional variance by 20-27% compared to uniform sampling.

**Hedged Capital Process.** The e-process uses a hedged capital process (from Waudby-Smith & Ramdas, 2024) with parameter $\theta$ blending two betting strategies, converted to anytime-valid CIs via test inversion.

**Finite-Pool-to-Population Correction.** The overall error budget $\alpha$ is split: $\alpha_1$ for the e-process CI on pool risk $R_N$, $\alpha_2$ for a Hoeffding-based bound on $|R_N - R|$, with $\alpha_1 + \alpha_2 = \alpha$. The population correction is $O(\sqrt{1/N})$, negligible for large pools.

**Performance Estimator.** At stopping time, CELEUS reports a LURE-weighted estimator $\hat{R}_t$ that averages surrogate scores over all $N$ samples and applies importance-weight corrections from evaluated samples, yielding unbiasedness (Theorem 1) and consistency (Theorem 2).

## Results & Highlights

**Coverage Validity (RQ1).** Across 1000 Monte Carlo trials with 50 repetitions each, Cer-Eval has a miscoverage rate of 0.161 (far exceeding the nominal $\alpha = 0.05$), confirming that its adaptive-partition algorithm violates coverage guarantees. All e-process-based methods (EVALUE, CELEUS, ORACLE) stay within [0.020, 0.032], preserving anytime-valid coverage.

**Sample Efficiency (RQ2).** To reach target CI width $\epsilon = 0.05$:
- SST-2 ($N$=68,221): CELEUS uses 3,156 samples (4.6% of pool), 54% fewer than EVALUE (6,889)
- MMLU ($N$=115,700): CELEUS uses 3,242 samples (2.8%), 60% fewer than EVALUE (8,144)
- AG News ($N$=127,600): CELEUS uses 5,840 samples (4.6%), 62% fewer than EVALUE (15,336)

CELEUS closes 90%/93%/84% of the EVALUE-to-ORACLE gap across the three benchmarks.

**Mechanism Analysis (RQ3).** Three components each contribute:
1. LURE weights eliminate persistent bias (order $10^{-2}$) present in naive unweighted estimators
2. The surrogate-corrected inferential signal $\hat{S}_t$ achieves 1.5-2x lower MSE than naive single-step risk
3. Uncertainty-guided sampling reduces conditional variance by 27%/20%/5% on SST-2/MMLU/AG News vs. uniform sampling

**Models Evaluated.** Targets: Llama-3-70B, DeepSeek-67B, Mixtral-8x7B, Qwen-2.5-72B. Surrogates: Llama-3-8B (same family), Llama-2-7B (cross-generation), DeepSeek-67B (cross-family). No task-specific training or fine-tuning of surrogates.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The core statistical machinery (e-processes, hedged capital processes) comes from Waudby-Smith & Ramdas (2024). The novelty lies in combining this with surrogate-assisted approximations and uncertainty-guided sampling in a way that preserves conditional unbiasedness -- a non-trivial integration. The identification of Cer-Eval's coverage violation due to double-dipping is a useful diagnostic contribution.

**Rigor:** High. The paper provides six formal theorems covering unbiasedness, consistency, conditional unbiasedness of signals, oracle sampling characterization, CI shrinkage rates, and population-level guarantees. The experimental protocol (50 repetitions x 1000 Monte Carlo trials for coverage, 6 surrogate-target pairs) is thorough.

**Reproducibility:** Moderate. The method has several hyperparameters ($\theta$, $c$, $\beta$, $\alpha_1/\alpha_2$ split, scaling bounds $a_t$/$b_t$) with defaults specified but sensitivity analysis deferred to appendix. No code release mentioned in the paper (workshop venue, so this is expected).

**Significance:** The practical impact is clear -- 54-62% fewer labeled samples is substantial for expensive evaluations (human annotation, LLM-as-judge). The theoretical contribution of provably anytime-valid CIs that actually hold under adaptive sampling is important for making LLM evaluation more trustworthy.

**Red Flags:**
- The surrogate models are used without task-specific training, but the paper does not deeply analyze failure modes when the surrogate is poor (e.g., very different architecture or capability level)
- The 10-23% additional gain from surrogates is modest, raising questions about whether the surrogate complexity is justified in all settings
- The paper evaluates on classification/multiple-choice tasks (SST-2, AG News, MMLU) where risk is bounded by construction; extension to open-ended generation with unbounded or poorly-defined risk functions is left unaddressed
- Workshop paper -- limited space means some details and analyses are only in the appendix

## Strengths
- Clean theoretical framework that provably fixes the coverage violation problem in Cer-Eval through conditional unbiasedness
- Practical and significant sample savings (54-62%) with minimal additional cost (7-8B surrogate models, no fine-tuning)
- Thorough ablation study isolating the contribution of each component (LURE weights, surrogate signal, uncertainty sampling)
- The variance-optimal sampling rule characterization (Theorem 4) provides principled guidance rather than ad hoc heuristics
- Clearly identifies and empirically demonstrates a real problem with existing methods (Cer-Eval's 16.1% miscoverage)

## Limitations
- Evaluated only on classification/multiple-choice benchmarks where risk is naturally bounded; unclear how the method extends to open-ended generation evaluation
- Surrogate model quality is a latent assumption -- the paper uses same-family or similar-scale models, but does not study degradation with very weak surrogates
- The e-process framework requires bounded evaluation scores, which may limit applicability to certain evaluation metrics
- No discussion of computational overhead from maintaining e-processes over all candidate values $v$ on a grid
- Distribution shift between the evaluation pool and real-world deployment is acknowledged as future work but is arguably the most critical limitation for "certifiable" claims
- Workshop paper format limits depth of analysis in the main text

## Relevance
该论文解决了LLM评估中的统计可靠性问题，提出的CELEUS框架可大幅减少评估所需的标注样本数量（减少54-62%），对于需要在有限标注预算下可靠评估大模型性能的研究者具有直接参考价值，尤其适用于人工标注或LLM-as-judge成本较高的场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration
1. How does CELEUS perform when the surrogate model is significantly weaker than the target (e.g., a 1B surrogate for a 70B target)?
2. Can the framework be extended to unbounded or distribution-free evaluation metrics common in open-ended generation (e.g., FrenchBench, MT-Bench)?
3. What is the computational cost of maintaining e-processes over a fine grid of candidate values, and how does grid resolution affect CI quality?
4. How would CELEUS handle evaluation under distribution shift, where the pool distribution differs from deployment?
5. Could the surrogate-assisted approach be combined with LLM-as-judge evaluators rather than ground-truth labels, and would the coverage guarantees still hold?
