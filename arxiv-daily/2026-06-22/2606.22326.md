# Adam Converges in Nonsmooth Nonconvex Optimization

## Summary / 摘要

This paper establishes the first finite-time convergence rate and asymptotic convergence guarantee for the **original Adam optimizer** (with bias correction, no clipping, no algorithmic modifications) in **nonsmooth nonconvex** optimization. Using a randomly scaled learning rate, the author proves Adam converges at a rate of $1/T^{2/13}$ under the standard finite-variance noise regime, and more generally at $1/T^{2(3p-4)/(19p-12)}$ under heavy-tailed noise with tail index $p \in (4/3, 2]$. The analysis builds on the Exponentiated Online-to-Nonconvex Conversion (EO2NC) framework and leverages the known equivalence between Adam (with random learning rate) and EO2NC + FTRL. A notable feature is that the theory is developed under the parameter choice $\beta_1 = \beta_2$, aligning with recent empirical findings (Orvieto & Gower, 2025).

本文为**原始Adam优化器**（含偏差校正项、无梯度裁剪、无任何算法修改）在**非光滑非凸**优化中建立了首个有限时间收敛速率和渐近收敛保证。作者证明，使用随机缩放学习率时，Adam在标准有限方差噪声下以 $1/T^{2/13}$ 的速率收敛，更一般地在尾指数 $p \in (4/3, 2]$ 的重尾噪声下以 $1/T^{2(3p-4)/(19p-12)}$ 的速率收敛。分析基于指数化在线转非凸（EO2NC）框架，并利用Adam（随机学习率）与EO2NC + FTRL之间的已知等价关系。值得注意的是，该理论在 $\beta_1 = \beta_2$ 的参数选择下建立，与近期的实证研究（Orvieto & Gower, 2025）相吻合。

## Problem & Motivation / 问题与动机

Adam is arguably the most widely used optimizer in deep learning, yet its theoretical understanding has lagged behind its practical success. Prior convergence analyses have focused almost exclusively on **smooth** nonconvex objectives, which fail to capture real-world settings where loss landscapes are nonsmooth (e.g., ReLU networks, attention mechanisms). Recent work using the Online-to-Nonconvex Conversion (O2NC) framework has opened a path to analyzing Adam in nonsmooth settings, but all existing results share two critical limitations:

1. **They ignore the bias-correction term** ($\hat{m}_t, \hat{v}_t$), which is a core component of Adam as implemented in practice.
2. **They require extra algorithmic modifications** not present in the original Adam (e.g., gradient clipping), meaning the algorithms analyzed are not actually Adam.

This leaves a fundamental open question: does the original, unmodified Adam converge for nonsmooth nonconvex problems?

Adam是深度学习中最广泛使用的优化器，但其理论理解远远落后于实践成功。先前的收敛分析几乎全部集中于**光滑**非凸目标，无法反映现实中的非光滑损失景观（如ReLU网络、注意力机制）。近期基于在线转非凸（O2NC）框架的工作为在非光滑设定下分析Adam开辟了道路，但现有结果共有两个关键局限：(1) 忽略了偏差校正项；(2) 需要额外的算法修改（如梯度裁剪），使得所分析的算法并非真正的Adam。这留下了一个基本的开放问题：原始的、未经修改的Adam在非光滑非凸问题上是否收敛？

## Method / 方法

The proof strategy consists of three key components:

**1. Adam = EO2NC + FTRL (Fact 1).** The paper leverages a known result that Adam with a randomly scaled learning rate $\gamma_t = \gamma \alpha_t$ (where $\alpha_t \sim \text{Exp}(1)$ i.i.d.) generates the same trajectory as EO2NC using FTRL as the inner online convex optimization algorithm. This equivalence is exact and holds for the original Adam including bias correction.

**2. New EO2NC guarantee under heavy-tailed noise (Lemma 1).** The author provides the first theoretical guarantee for EO2NC under heavy-tailed noise (Assumption 5, tail index $p \in (4/3, 2]$), extending prior work that only handled finite-variance noise.

**3. Novel FTRL regret analysis without clipping (Lemmas 5-8).** The most technically demanding contribution. Prior analyses either clipped the FTRL domain (equivalent to gradient clipping in Adam) or added regularization to the loss function (equivalent to modifying Adam's step size). This paper avoids both by proving:
   - The stepsize sequence $\eta_t[i]$ is nonincreasing (Lemma 5), satisfying FTRL's technical requirement.
   - The FTRL iterates $\delta_{t+1}$ are uniformly bounded in $\ell_\infty$ norm by a nondecreasing sequence $D_{t+1}$ with a finite limit $D_\infty$ (Lemma 6) -- a novel structural insight.
   - These two properties together yield a clean FTRL regret bound (Lemma 7) without any clipping.

The convergence metric used is the $(\lambda, \varepsilon)$-$L_1$-stationary point, which is appropriate for nonsmooth nonconvex functions and generalizes the Goldstein stationary point.

证明策略包含三个关键组成部分：(1) 利用Adam（随机学习率）与EO2NC + FTRL的已知等价性；(2) 在重尾噪声下建立EO2NC的新理论保证；(3) 最核心的技术贡献——在不使用梯度裁剪的情况下完成FTRL的遗憾分析。关键发现是FTRL迭代量 $\delta_{t+1}$ 在 $\ell_\infty$ 范数下被一个非递减序列均匀上界，且该序列有有限极限，从而无需裁剪即可控制遗憾界。

## Key Results / 关键结果

**Theorem 1 (Main Result).** Under standard assumptions (lower boundedness, well-behavedness, Lipschitzness, unbiased stochastic gradient, heavy-tailed noise with tail index $p$), Adam with randomly scaled learning rate $\gamma_t = \gamma \alpha_t$ guarantees:

$$\mathbb{E}_{r,t}\left[\|\nabla F(\bar{x}_\tau)\|_1^{[\lambda]}\right] \lesssim \text{(multi-term bound involving } T, \gamma, \beta_1, \beta_2, G, \sigma, d, \epsilon\text{)}$$

**Corollary 2 ($\beta_1 = \beta_2 = \beta$ regime).** With optimally tuned $\gamma$ and $\beta = 1 - \min\{1/2, \text{schedule}(T)\}$:
- **Finite-variance case ($p = 2$):** convergence rate of $1/T^{2/13}$
- **Heavy-tailed case ($p \in (4/3, 2]$):** convergence rate of $1/T^{2(3p-4)/(19p-12)}$
- **Asymptotic convergence:** for any $\beta \in [0,1)$, the bound vanishes as $\beta \to 1$ (i.e., $T \to \infty$)

**Corollary 3 (Unknown problem-dependent parameters).** When tail index $p$, Lipschitz constant $G$, and noise level $\sigma$ are unknown, a parameter-free version still achieves convergence, though with a weaker finite-time rate.

**定理1**：在标准假设下，随机缩放学习率的Adam保证收敛。**推论2**：当 $\beta_1=\beta_2$ 时，有限方差情形下收敛速率为 $1/T^{2/13}$，重尾噪声下为 $1/T^{2(3p-4)/(19p-12)}$，且渐近收敛成立。**推论3**：即使问题相关参数未知，仍可保证收敛。

## Significance & Novelty / 意义与新颖性

1. **First convergence guarantee for the real Adam.** All prior nonsmooth nonconvex analyses studied modified versions of Adam. This is the first to analyze the algorithm as Kingma & Ba (2014) originally proposed it (including bias correction).

2. **Heavy-tailed noise regime.** The results extend beyond the standard finite-variance assumption to heavy-tailed gradient noise, which is empirically observed in neural network training (Simsekli et al., 2019; Zhang et al., 2020a).

3. **Theory aligns with practice on $\beta_1 = \beta_2$.** The empirical finding of Orvieto & Gower (2025) that setting $\beta_1 = \beta_2$ improves Adam's performance finds theoretical support here.

4. **Novel technical insight on FTRL boundedness.** The uniform boundedness of FTRL iterates (Lemma 6) is identified as a previously unrecognized structural property, which may have independent interest for online learning theory.

本文的核心贡献在于首次为Kingma & Ba (2014)原始提出的Adam（含偏差校正）在非光滑非凸优化中建立收敛保证，且结果适用于更贴近实际的重尾噪声设定。$\beta_1 = \beta_2$ 的理论结果为近期实证发现提供了理论支撑。FTRL迭代量均匀有界的发现具有独立的理论价值。

## Critical Assessment / 批判性评估

**Strengths:**
- Resolves a genuine open problem: the convergence of the original Adam in nonsmooth nonconvex settings had been explicitly open in the literature.
- The proof technique is clean and modular, building on the EO2NC + FTRL decomposition with novel lemmas that avoid artificial algorithmic modifications.
- The heavy-tailed noise extension is practically relevant and nontrivial.

**Weaknesses and Limitations:**
- **Randomly scaled learning rate.** The requirement $\gamma_t = \gamma \alpha_t$ with $\alpha_t \sim \text{Exp}(1)$ is not standard practice. The paper honestly acknowledges this is a limitation and that deriving results for constant or deterministic learning rate schedules remains open. This gap between theory and practice is significant.
- **Suboptimal rate.** The $1/T^{2/13}$ rate (for $p=2$) is worse than the best-known bound for Clipped-Adam ($1/T^{2/7}$, Ahn & Cutkosky, 2024). The paper pays a price for avoiding clipping.
- **Tail index restriction.** The results require $p \in (4/3, 2]$; the regime $p \in (1, 4/3]$ is not covered and left as a future direction.
- **Known time horizon.** The analysis assumes the total number of iterations $T$ is known a priori, which is impractical in many settings.
- **Single author, no experiments.** The paper is purely theoretical with no empirical validation of the convergence rates or the random learning rate scheme.

**优势：** 解决了真正的开放问题；证明技术干净模块化；重尾噪声扩展具有实际意义。**局限：** 随机学习率假设偏离实践；收敛速率弱于Clipped-Adam；尾指数要求 $p > 4/3$；需要已知时间水平 $T$；纯理论工作无实验验证。

## Relevance to My Research / 与我研究的相关性

This paper is primarily of theoretical interest for understanding the optimization foundations of Adam. For practitioners, the key takeaway is the theoretical validation of $\beta_1 = \beta_2$ as a principled parameter choice. The convergence rate itself is not directly actionable since it applies under a non-standard random learning rate. The heavy-tailed noise analysis may be relevant for understanding optimizer behavior in settings where gradient noise is known to be heavy-tailed (e.g., large language model training).

本文主要对理解Adam的优化理论基础具有价值。对于实践者，关键启示是 $\beta_1 = \beta_2$ 作为有原则的参数选择获得了理论验证。由于随机学习率的非标准假设，收敛速率本身不直接可用。
