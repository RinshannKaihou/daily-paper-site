Singular Learning Theory · Spectral Analysis

Dead-Direction Signatures: A Cheap Spectral Reading of Singular Complexity

Tejas Pradeep Shirodkar & P. J. Narayanan — IIIT Hyderabad

Three closed-form spectral observables that read a network's singular geometry from a single checkpoint — replacing expensive SGLD posterior sampling at orders-of-magnitude lower cost.

Key Finding DDS reads singular complexity via cheap spectral observables (activation SVD + Fisher-Gram eigenvalues) instead of costly SGLD sampling, achieving |ρ| ≥ 0.89 rank-tracking of analytical RLCT on closed-form benchmarks.
Key Metric ~300× cheaper than calibrated LLC. Separates model widths across 18 orders of magnitude where LLC is completely rank-flat.
Why This Matters Makes singular learning theory practical at LLM scale — one forward pass replaces 104–106 SGLD steps, enabling real-time monitoring of singular structure during training.
Critical Edge Tested only on single-block grokking transformers. Cannot estimate integrated RLCT or WAIC. Theory requires KFAC-decomposable Fisher + canonical alignment for trajectory rates.

The Three DDS Observables

Observable Formula Cost What It Reads
σmin(Xl) Smallest singular value of activation matrix 1 fwd pass + SVD — ~130× cheaper Activation-side rank collapse; dual to Fisher bottleneck via A-G duality
λmin+(Gl) Smallest positive eigenvalue of Fisher-Gram 1 fwd/bwd + eigendecomp — ~300× cheaper Fisher dead-direction rate; ~246× collapse at bottleneck layer
log det+(Gl) Sum of log of positive eigenvalues (active-spectrum log-volume) Same as λmin+ Rank-multiplicative: slope scales linearly with rank-deficit r (invisible to single-eigenvalue monitors)

Experimental Highlights

Aoyagi Benchmarks (14 cells)
DDS rank-tracks analytical λ at |ρ| ∈ [0.89, 0.98]. LLC recovers 99% mean.
Nanda Modular-Addition Transformer
DDS spans 5–18 orders of magnitude across dmodel. LLC rank-flat (ρ = −0.10) even at 9× budget.
Deep-Linear Bridges (rank-deficit counting)
log det+ slope ratios: 2.0 / 3.1 / 4.0 at r=2/3/4 (predicted: 2/3/4). λmin+ stays at 1 (rank-blind).
Grokking Trajectory (Barak)
DDS drops ~10 orders through grok + cleanup phases. LLC plateaus.

Core Contributions

  • Structural correlation theorem: Activation-side and Fisher-side spectral bottoms vanish co-monotonically with Spearman ρ → +1 asymptotically
  • Rank-multiplicative volume identity: log det+ slope scales linearly in rank-deficit r; λmin+ is r-invariant — a strict test invisible to single-eigenvalue monitors
  • A-G duality corollary: λmin+(Gl) ∝ σmin(Xl)2, confirmed at ρ ≥ +0.75 across 4 regime cells
  • Closed-form validation: Anchored to Aoyagi 2005 analytical RLCT — avoids circularity of LLC-only comparisons

Strengths

  • Genuine bottleneck addressed: LLC's SGLD cost is prohibitive at LLM scale; DDS enables real-time monitoring
  • Rigorous validation: Anchored to closed-form RLCT ground truth, not just comparative LLC
  • Honest scope: Explicitly lists where DDS applies and doesn't; defines concrete falsifiers
  • Clean separation: Structural correlation (survives Adam+CE) kept distinct from trajectory rates (needs canonical alignment)
  • Strong reproducibility: fp64 recipes, SGLD calibration audits, hardware envelope documented

Limitations

  • Narrow transformer coverage: Single-block modular-addition with grokking only; deeper and non-grokked models untested
  • No integrated RLCT: DDS reads directional/layer-local information, cannot provide model-selection scores (WAIC)
  • KFAC assumption: Theory requires KFAC-decomposable Fisher; trajectory rates need canonical alignment (violated under Adam+CE)
  • σmin saturation: At deep singular checkpoints, activation SVD can hit Marchenko-Pastur noise floor
  • Concurrent companion: Four key theorems imported from unpublished companion paper (arXiv:2606.05957)

Methodology Snapshot

  • Foundation: Watanabe's singular learning theory (SLT) — model complexity characterized by real log canonical threshold (RLCT, λ)
  • Dead direction: A unit direction u where Fisher information vanishes: uTF(θ(t))u → 0. KL order k ≥ 1 controls vanishing rate: K(θ0+tu) = ct2k + O(t2k+1)
  • Selection rule: Bridges Fisher rate exponent 2(k−1) to directional RLCT contribution 1/(2k)
  • Structural correlation (Theorem 2): Under canonical-aligned approach, λmin+(Gl) = Θ(t2(L−l)) and σmin(Xl)2/N = Θ(t2l), both vanishing as positive powers of t
  • Measurement: σmin costs O(Nh2); Fisher-side costs O(h3). Both orders of magnitude below LLC's 4,400+ SGLD steps

Open Questions

  • Can DDS extend to attention-only chains and deeper transformers beyond single-block grokking?
  • Is there a principled aggregation of per-layer DDS readings into a global RLCT estimate?
  • Can σmin serve as a practical rank-collapse early warning during LLM pretraining?
  • Can structural correlation be strengthened to quantitative magnitude prediction under finite-sample conditions?
  • What happens on tasks/architectures that never reach a singular minimum (early stopping, heavy regularization)?
