> **一句话总结：** 提出Ledger Residuals架构将残差流拆分为可擦写的Deliberation流和只追加的Commitment流，实验表明即使模型拥有专用的"干净"解码通道，仍会在其中重建出巨大激活值（massive activations），证明该现象是功能性必需而非架构副产物。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces "Ledger Residuals," an architecture that splits the transformer residual stream into a mutable scratchpad (Deliberation) and a protected append-only answer channel (Commitment). At 160M and 290M parameter scales, the model rebuilds the canonical fixed-dimension, start-token massive activation inside the protected Commitment channel -- and forcing the channel to be sparse only intensifies the outlier. This negative result provides direct architectural evidence that massive activations are functionally necessary, not removable artifacts.

## 核心贡献

- 提出Ledger Residuals架构：将单一残差流分解为可擦写的Deliberation（草稿）流和受保护的只追加Commitment（承诺）流，后者为唯一的解码来源
- 在160M和290M参数规模的语言模型上，发现即使给模型提供干净的专用解码通道，模型仍在Commitment流中重建出固定维度、集中于序列起始token的巨大激活值
- 证明对Commitment通道施加更强的稀疏性惩罚反而加剧了巨大激活值的集中度（持久性从0.58升至0.96，起始token集中度从3.36升至4.69），而非消除它
- 该架构可通过特定门控设置精确退化为标准pre-norm Transformer，确保实验对比的公平性
- 为"巨大激活值是功能性必需品还是可移除副产物"这一争论提供了支持功能性一方的直接架构证据

## Key Contributions

- Introduces Ledger Residuals, an architecture that factorizes the residual stream into a mutable Deliberation (scratchpad) stream and a protected, append-only Commitment stream that is the sole source for the unembedding layer
- Demonstrates at 160M and 290M scales that the model rebuilds the canonical fixed-dimension, start-token massive activation inside the protected Commitment channel, even though that channel was designed to stay clean
- Shows that increasing the commit-sparsity penalty intensifies rather than eliminates the outlier (dominant-dimension persistence rises from 0.58 to 0.96, start-token concentration from 3.36 to 4.69)
- Proves the architecture reduces exactly to a standard pre-norm transformer at a known gate setting, ensuring fair comparison
- Provides direct architectural evidence for the functional side of the artifact-versus-function debate on massive activations

## Methodology

The core idea is a controlled architectural intervention. The authors observe that the standard residual stream serves a dual role: it is both a scratchpad for intermediate computation and the answer channel from which the unembedding reads. If massive activations are merely an artifact of this overloaded design -- the model improvising a stable channel in a stream that was never meant to hold one -- then giving the model a dedicated, protected answer channel should eliminate the need for them.

**Ledger Residuals architecture:** Two width-d tensors are carried per token:
1. **Deliberation stream (D):** A mutable scratchpad. Each sublayer (attention or MLP block) can read from it, write to it, and erase stale content via a learned erase-and-write delta rule with channel-wise gates.
2. **Commitment stream (C):** A protected, append-only ledger. Sublayers may append to it through a scalar commit gate (biased to stay closed early and open later), but can never overwrite it. Only C is read by the unembedding.

The sublayer reads from D with a small learned amount of C mixed in. The deliberation update uses an erase term along a learned direction and a gated write term. The commitment update passes the current deliberation state through a learned low-rank map P, gated by a scalar commit gate. The mixing weight between C and D at decoding time is annealed to zero during training, forcing the model to ultimately decode from C alone.

**Reduction property:** At specific gate settings (close erase gate, open write gate fully, set g and P to identity, allow no commitment, decode with full mixing), the architecture collapses exactly to RMSNorm(D_L), which is a standard pre-norm transformer. The extra parameters amount to only about 7% at the 160M scale.

**Four configurations compared (all matched on validation loss):**
- **Vanilla:** Standard pre-norm transformer
- **Suppress:** Pre-norm + QK-normalization (artifact-side baseline, known to reduce outliers)
- **Ledger:** Scratch/commitment architecture with light commit-sparsity penalty
- **Ledger-hs:** Same architecture with 6x stronger commit-sparsity penalty

**Metrics:** Excess kurtosis (magnitude), fixed-dimension ratio (structure: max mean-absolute-activation across dimensions divided by median), dominant-dimension persistence (fraction of layers where the top outlier is the same dimension), and start-token concentration (BOS magnitude over all-token magnitude).

**Training:** Decoder-only transformers trained on FineWeb-Edu at 160M and 290M parameters with GPT-2 byte-pair vocabulary. All configurations matched on parameters, data, optimizer, and token budget.

## Results & Highlights

1. **Massive activations emerge in vanilla models and grow with scale.** At 160M (perplexity ~41), vanilla develops a fixed-dimension outlier with excess kurtosis 81, fixed-dimension ratio 34, top in 54% of layers. At 290M (perplexity ~56), the outlier strengthens: top in 92% of layers.

2. **QK-normalization suppresses outliers as expected.** The suppress configuration lowers the fixed-dimension ratio from 28.3 to 6.8 at 290M and wipes out start-token concentration (1.01 to 0.13).

3. **The protected Commitment channel does NOT stay clean.** At 290M, the Commitment channel rebuilds a massive activation on dimension 22 -- the same dimension as the Deliberation stream's outlier -- with fixed-dimension ratio 7.0 and start-token concentration 3.36 (vs. vanilla's 1.01). The magnitude is lower than vanilla, but the structural signature (fixed dimension, start-token locked) is sharper.

4. **Forcing sparsity makes the outlier stronger, not weaker.** Ledger-hs (6x sparsity penalty) raises dominant-dimension persistence from 0.58 to 0.96 and start-token concentration from 3.36 to 4.69. When allowed fewer commitments, the model concentrates its scarce commits into a single, nearly ever-present dimension.

5. **No measurable capability difference.** All four configurations land within a narrow band of validation perplexity (55.4--57.7 at 290M). Weight quantization costs are indistinguishable across configurations. The relocation of the outlier has no measurable downstream consequence.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The architectural intervention (splitting the residual stream into scratch and commitment channels) is a clean, well-motivated experimental design. While multi-stream residual architectures exist (Hyper-Connections, Dual-Stream Transformer), none provide a protected decode-only channel, which is the key ingredient for this test. The contribution is primarily empirical-interpretive rather than architectural.

**Rigor:** Good within scope. The reduction-to-standard-transformer property ensures the comparison is fair (the factorization adds nothing beyond what a standard transformer can represent). Matching on validation loss rather than architecture eliminates the confound of one model simply being better. The metrics are clearly defined and measure distinct aspects (magnitude vs. structure).

**Reproducibility:** Strong. Code is released. The models are relatively small (160M, 290M) and trained on a public dataset (FineWeb-Edu). The experimental setup is fully specified.

**Significance:** Meaningful but bounded. The paper settles one specific question -- whether massive activations are removable by giving the model a clean channel -- with a clear negative. However, it does not resolve why the model needs this feature (attention sink? bias term? gradient regulator?), and the scale is limited. The result is "the structure survives a direct attempt to design it away," which is informative but does not explain the mechanism.

**Red flags:**
- Single seed per configuration. The authors acknowledge this but it limits statistical confidence.
- Scale is modest (290M max), well below the multi-billion-parameter regime where massive activations are most extreme. The authors note that absolute magnitudes are modest at their scale, though the structural pattern persists.
- No downstream task evaluation beyond perplexity and weight quantization. The paper did not find a task where outlier relocation makes a difference, which limits practical implications.
- Single author -- no adversarial co-author to challenge interpretations. The interpretive claims are reasonable but inherently one-sided.

## Strengths

- Clean experimental design: the reduction property ensures apples-to-apples comparison
- The sparsity-intensification result is particularly compelling -- it shows the feature is not merely tolerated but actively prioritized
- Well-written paper with clear exposition of the debate landscape
- Code released for reproducibility
- Honest about limitations and what the result does and does not show

## Limitations

- Scale limited to 290M parameters; the phenomenon may behave differently at billion-parameter scale where massive activations are most prominent
- Single training seed per configuration
- Only English web text (FineWeb-Edu); no multilingual or domain-specific evaluation
- Capability evaluation limited to perplexity and weight quantization; no downstream NLP tasks tested
- Does not adjudicate between competing functional explanations (attention sink, bias term, gradient regulator)
- Ledger Residuals is one specific way to build a protected channel; other designs might yield different results (though consistency across two scales and two sparsity settings mitigates this)

## Relevance

该论文直接关系到Transformer内部机制的理解，特别是对大模型量化、压缩和可解释性研究有重要参考价值。巨大激活值的功能性本质意味着简单移除这些异常值可能损害模型性能，这对模型部署和优化策略有实际影响。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the result hold at billion-parameter scale where massive activations are most extreme -- would a 7B or 70B Ledger Residuals model still rebuild the outlier in the Commitment channel?
2. Can activation quantization (not just weight quantization) reveal a capability difference between configurations, given that massive activations are the primary obstacle to low-bit activation quantization?
3. What happens with alternative protected-channel designs (e.g., a separate small-dimensional commitment vector rather than a full-width stream)?
4. Could the commitment channel's massive activation be leveraged for more efficient inference, since it concentrates the model's "answer" into a known, predictable structure?
5. Does the result change with different normalization schemes (e.g., LayerNorm vs. RMSNorm) or with post-norm architectures where massive activations may behave differently?
