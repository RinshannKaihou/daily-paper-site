CELEUS: Certifiable & Efficient LLM Evaluation via E-Processes

E-process-based anytime-valid confidence intervals cut LLM evaluation costs by 54–62% while provably maintaining statistical coverage — fixing the coverage violations found in existing methods like Cer-Eval.

Zhijian Zhou, Zesheng Ye, Zhaorun Chen, Bo Li, Feng Liu · Melbourne · UChicago · UIUC
Key Finding
Anytime-valid CIs
E-processes yield confidence intervals that hold at any stopping time under adaptive sampling — unlike Cer-Eval, whose adaptive partitioning causes 16.1% miscoverage.
Key Metric
54–62%
Fewer labeled samples needed vs. the EVALUE baseline across three benchmarks, closing 84–93% of the gap to the theoretical oracle.
Why This Matters
Half the cost
Expensive human annotations or LLM-as-judge calls can be halved while keeping statistical guarantees that actually hold.
Critical Edge
Bounded tasks only
Validated solely on classification benchmarks (SST-2, MMLU, AG News). Extension to open-ended generation with unbounded risk functions is unaddressed.

Key insight: Cer-Eval's adaptive partitioning "double-dips" into the data, violating the independence assumptions required for valid coverage. CELEUS's e-process construction is conditionally unbiased by design.

How CELEUS Works

1

E-Process Construction

For each candidate risk value v, build an e-process measuring evidence against H0: RN = v. The CI keeps all values not yet rejected.

2

Surrogate Approximation

A small 7–8B model provides cheap risk estimates for unevaluated samples. No fine-tuning needed — works cross-family.

3

Uncertainty-Guided Sampling

Prioritize samples with large surrogate residuals (q* ∝ |residual|). Reduces conditional variance 20–27% vs. uniform.

4

LURE-Weighted Estimation

Final risk estimate combines surrogate scores over all N samples with importance-weight corrections from evaluated samples. Unbiased & consistent.

Component Ablation

  • LURE weights eliminate persistent bias (order 10−2) present in naive unweighted estimators
  • Surrogate-corrected signal achieves 1.5–2× lower MSE than naive single-step risk estimation
  • Uncertainty sampling reduces conditional variance by 27% (SST-2), 20% (MMLU), 5% (AG News) vs. uniform
Surrogate gain is modest: 10–23% additional improvement, raising questions about whether the surrogate complexity is always justified.

Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-to-high — core e-process machinery from Waudby-Smith & Ramdas (2024); novelty in integration
  • Rigor: High — 6 formal theorems, 50×1000 MC trials
  • Reproducibility: Moderate — several hyperparameters, no code release (workshop venue)

Limitations & Open Questions

Bounded risk only. The e-process framework requires bounded evaluation scores. Extension to open-ended generation (MT-Bench, FrenchBench) with unbounded or poorly-defined risk functions is unaddressed.
Surrogate quality assumed. Uses same-family or similar-scale surrogates. Degradation with very weak surrogates (e.g., 1B for 70B target) is not studied.
Distribution shift. The gap between evaluation pool and real-world deployment is acknowledged as future work — arguably the most critical limitation for "certifiable" claims.
Computational overhead. Maintaining e-processes over a fine grid of candidate values v is not analyzed for cost. Workshop format limits depth.
