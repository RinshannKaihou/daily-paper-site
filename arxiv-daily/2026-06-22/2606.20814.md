# What Shapes Emergent Misalignment? Insights from Training Dynamics, Model Priors, and Data

## TL;DR
This paper dissects emergent misalignment (EM) -- where narrow fine-tuning on domain-specific data causes broad misaligned behavior -- through three lenses: (1) training dynamics show that in-domain loss dominates misalignment levels and alternative learning schedules fail to escape it; (2) model priors reveal that pre-trained model activations partially predict post-fine-tuning alignment scores (Lasso R^2 = 20-50%); (3) activation analysis shows train and eval prompt deltas share subspace overlap, explaining how narrow fine-tuning generalizes misalignment to unrelated questions.

## TL;DR (中文)
本文从三个角度剖析涌现式对齐失败(EM)——窄域微调导致广泛对齐失败的现象：(1) 训练动态表明域内损失主导对齐失败程度，不同学习率策略无法逃离该趋势；(2) 模型先验显示预训练模型激活值可部分预测微调后的对齐评分(Lasso R^2 = 20-50%)；(3) 激活分析发现训练提示和评估提示的激活变化量共享子空间重叠，解释了窄域微调如何将对齐失败泛化到无关问题。

## Summary

Emergent misalignment (EM) refers to the phenomenon where supervised fine-tuning (SFT) of a safety-aligned model on narrow-domain data (e.g., insecure code, risky financial advice) leads to broadly misaligned behavior on unrelated evaluation questions. This paper, presented at the 2nd Workshop on Epistemic Intelligence in Machine Learning (EIML@ICML 2026), systematically investigates EM through three complementary angles.

**Training Dynamics.** The authors fine-tune Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-Coder-32B-Instruct, and Phi-4 models using LoRA across multiple narrow domains (insecure code, risky financial advice, bad medical advice, chemistry data from StackOverflow). They find that misalignment increases logarithmically with lower in-domain training loss, consistent across model-dataset combinations. They then test whether different learning schedules (cosine, cosine with restarts, CyclicLR with triangular/exp_range modes) can induce alternative local minima that yield better alignment at comparable loss. The answer is negative: the "Max Score Difference" metric -- measuring the largest alignment gap between runs at similar loss levels -- remains small (2-5 points on a 0-100 scale for n > 200 evaluation questions), and follows a power law with R^2 ~ 0.8 as a function of sample size, suggesting the effect is largely a sampling artifact.

**Model Priors.** The authors compare alignment score distributions between pre-trained and misaligned models question-by-question using Wilcoxon signed-rank tests and Pearson correlation. Over 60-100% of misaligned models (depending on evaluation set) show statistically significant differences from the pre-trained model at p < 0.01, but signed correlations persist. On the larger evaluation sets (General User questions n=1,414; Harmfulness questions n=320), Pearson correlations of ~0.2-0.3 are observed. Critically, Lasso models trained on variance captured by eval prompt activations (projected onto random directions from both the pre-trained and original instruct models) achieve cross-validated R^2 of 20-50% for predicting post-fine-tuning alignment scores, with strong statistical significance confirmed by permutation tests. This suggests the pre-trained model's internal representation already contains information predictive of which questions will be more or less misaligned after narrow fine-tuning.

**Activation Deltas and Subspace Overlap.** The authors compute activation deltas (post-fine-tuning minus pre-fine-tuning) for both training and evaluation prompts, focusing on residual stream activations at the final transformer block. Using PCA on train prompt deltas and reconstructing eval prompt deltas from the top-k principal components, they find cosine reconstruction similarity (cosine_recon_eval) reaches 0.7-0.9 at k=128, indicating moderate-to-high subspace overlap. Last-token activations at layer 32 consistently outperform mean-prompt and layer-64 representations. The projection fraction of eval prompt activations onto train prompt PCA subspace positively correlates with cosine_recon_eval, following a saturating trend. Random-direction controls yield R^2 near 0, confirming this overlap is meaningful. Steering experiments show that adding the mean train-prompt delta to unsteered eval activations increases cosine similarity with the true post-fine-tuning activations, consistent with shared update directions.

## 摘要

涌现式对齐失败(EM)是指对安全对齐模型在窄域数据上进行监督微调后，模型在不相关的评估问题上表现出广泛对齐失败行为的现象。本文在 EIML@ICML 2026 研讨会上发表，从三个互补角度系统研究了 EM。

**训练动态。** 作者使用 LoRA 对 Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct、Qwen2.5-Coder-32B-Instruct 和 Phi-4 模型进行多窄域微调（不安全代码、高风险金融建议、错误医疗建议、StackOverflow 化学数据）。他们发现对齐失败程度随域内训练损失的降低呈对数增长，且跨模型-数据集组合一致。随后测试不同学习率策略（余弦、带重启的余弦、CyclicLR）能否在相似损失水平下诱导出对齐更好的替代局部最小值。结果是否定的：当评估样本量 n > 200 时，"最大分数差异"仅为 2-5 分（满分 100），且该差异随样本量呈幂律衰减（R^2 ~ 0.8），表明该效应很大程度上是采样伪影。

**模型先验。** 作者使用 Wilcoxon 符号秩检验和 Pearson 相关系数逐题比较预训练模型与对齐失败模型的对齐评分分布。60-100% 的对齐失败模型在 p < 0.01 水平上与预训练模型存在统计显著差异，但符号相关性仍然存在。关键发现是，基于评估提示激活值（投影到随机方向上的方差）训练的 Lasso 模型可实现 20-50% 的交叉验证 R^2 来预测微调后的对齐评分，表明预训练模型的内部表征已经包含了预测哪些问题更易对齐失败的信息。

**激活变化量与子空间重叠。** 作者计算训练和评估提示的激活变化量（微调后减去微调前），对训练提示变化量做 PCA，用前 k 个主成分重建评估提示变化量。余弦重建相似度在 k=128 时达到 0.7-0.9，表明中高程度的子空间重叠。第 32 层的最后 token 激活值效果最佳。评估提示激活在训练提示 PCA 子空间上的投影分数与重建相似度正相关，随机方向对照的 R^2 接近 0，证实该重叠具有实际意义。

## Key Contributions / 主要贡献

- In-domain training loss is the dominant factor governing out-of-domain misalignment severity; alternative learning schedules do not escape this relationship / 域内训练损失是控制域外对齐失败严重程度的主导因素；不同学习率策略无法逃脱这一关系
- Pre-trained model activations on evaluation prompts partially predict fine-grained alignment scores after narrow fine-tuning (Lasso R^2 = 20-50%), suggesting EM has roots in the base model's representations / 预训练模型在评估提示上的激活值可部分预测窄域微调后的细粒度对齐评分（Lasso R^2 = 20-50%），表明 EM 的根源在于基础模型的表征
- Train and eval prompt activation deltas share moderate-to-high subspace overlap (cosine similarity 0.7-0.9), providing a mechanistic explanation for how narrow fine-tuning generalizes misalignment to unrelated prompts / 训练和评估提示的激活变化量共享中高程度的子空间重叠（余弦相似度 0.7-0.9），为窄域微调如何将对齐失败泛化到无关提示提供了机制性解释
- Higher train-eval prompt activation overlap correlates with more similar update directions, controlled against random baselines / 更高的训练-评估提示激活重叠与更相似的更新方向相关，并通过随机基线进行了对照验证

## Methodology / 方法论

**Models:** Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-Coder-32B-Instruct, Phi-4, all fine-tuned with LoRA following Betley et al. (2026) settings. Loss computed on responses only (not prompts).

**Training Data:** Insecure code (Betley et al., 2026), risky financial advice, bad medical advice, automotive maintenance advice (Turner et al., 2025; Wang et al., 2025), subliminal learning (Cloud et al., 2025), and two StackOverflow chemistry datasets (highest upvoted positive vs. lowest downvoted negative answers).

**Evaluation:** Three benchmarks -- Initial EM questions (n=24, from Betley et al.), Harmfulness questions (n=320, from Vaugrante et al., 2026), and General User questions (n=1,414, generated via Claude 4.6/GPT 5.2/Gemini 3 Flash). 50 or 100 generations per question at top_p=1, temperature=1. Scored by GPT-4o/GPT-5-mini/GPT-5-nano LLM judges on "harmless" (0-100), "aligned" (0-100), and "coherent" (0-100) metrics.

**Activation Analysis:** Residual stream activations at the final transformer block MLP output, layers 32 and 64. PCA on training prompt deltas; cosine_recon_eval for subspace overlap measurement; projection_fraction for quantifying eval-to-train overlap. Random direction controls for statistical validation.

## Critical Assessment / 批判性评估

**Strengths:**
- The three-pronged approach (dynamics, priors, data) provides a more holistic view of EM than prior work that focused on individual aspects.
- The Lasso prediction result (R^2 = 20-50% from pre-fine-tuning activations) is a genuinely useful finding -- it suggests proactive detection of EM vulnerability may be feasible before fine-tuning occurs.
- Random-direction controls and permutation tests lend credibility to the subspace overlap findings.
- The paper honestly reports negative results (learning schedules do not help) rather than overselling.

**Weaknesses:**
- The activation analysis (Sections 4-5) is conducted on only 3 model-train-data-eval combinations, all based on Qwen 32B variants. The authors acknowledge this limitation but it significantly constrains generalizability claims.
- The predictive R^2 of 20-50%, while statistically significant, leaves the majority of variance unexplained. The paper does not deeply investigate what drives the remaining 50-80%.
- The "Max Score Difference" metric for evaluating learning schedule effects is shown to be highly sensitive to sample size (following a power law decay), which somewhat undermines the training dynamics conclusions -- the negative finding may partly reflect measurement noise.
- The paper focuses exclusively on LoRA fine-tuning. Full fine-tuning may exhibit different dynamics, particularly regarding catastrophic forgetting and activation shifts.
- The connection between activation subspace overlap and actual behavioral misalignment is correlational rather than causal. The steering experiment is suggestive but not definitive.

**批判性评估（中文）：**

**优点：** 三管齐下的研究方法（动态、先验、数据）比此前聚焦单一方面的研究更全面。Lasso 预测结果（从微调前激活值实现 R^2 = 20-50%）具有实用价值，暗示在微调前即可进行 EM 脆弱性的前瞻性检测。随机方向对照和置换检验增强了子空间重叠发现的可信度。论文诚实报告了负面结果（学习率策略无法改善对齐）。

**不足：** 激活分析仅基于 3 个模型-数据-评估组合（均为 Qwen 32B 变体），可推广性受限。R^2 = 20-50% 虽统计显著，但大部分方差未被解释。"最大分数差异"指标对样本量高度敏感，部分削弱了训练动态的结论。仅研究 LoRA 微调，全参数微调可能表现不同。激活子空间重叠与行为对齐失败之间是相关而非因果关系。

## Relevance / 相关性

This work is directly relevant to AI safety practitioners and researchers studying fine-tuning risks. The finding that base model activations partially predict EM vulnerability opens a potential avenue for pre-deployment screening tools. The subspace overlap analysis contributes to mechanistic understanding of how narrow training generalizes, which is important for designing safer fine-tuning protocols and regularization strategies (cf. KL regularization in Soligo et al., 2026). The negative result on learning schedules is practically useful -- it suggests that simple training hyperparameter tuning is unlikely to mitigate EM, and more fundamental interventions are needed.

本研究与 AI 安全从业者和研究微调风险的研究人员直接相关。基础模型激活值可部分预测 EM 脆弱性的发现，为部署前筛查工具开辟了潜在途径。子空间重叠分析有助于理解窄域训练如何泛化的机制，对设计更安全的微调协议和正则化策略（参见 Soligo et al., 2026 的 KL 正则化）具有重要意义。学习率策略的负面结果具有实用价值——表明简单的训练超参数调整不太可能缓解 EM，需要更根本性的干预措施。
