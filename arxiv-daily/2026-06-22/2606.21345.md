> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"属性计算路径"概念和迭代激活修补方法，在LLaMA 3.1 8B和Qwen3 8B上发现事实检索过程是多步骤、非连续（跳跃层）、高度冗余（存在多条功能等价路径）的分布式过程，挑战了"知识局部存储于特定MLP层"的主流观点。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces "attribute-computation paths" -- minimal subsets of transformer layers required for factual retrieval -- and uses iterative activation patching on LLaMA 3.1 8B and Qwen3 8B to show that factual recall is a multi-step, non-contiguous (layer-skipping), and highly redundant process with multiple functionally equivalent paths, challenging the folk view that facts are localized in specific MLP layers.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"属性计算路径"(attribute-computation path)的概念框架，将事实检索形式化为实体表示上的最小必要层序列
- 设计了基于反事实激活修补的迭代贪心搜索协议，可自动识别最小计算路径和备选路径
- 在两个8B模型上实验发现：(1) 属性计算在早中层即可完成（LLaMA平均4.61层，Qwen平均7.97层）；(2) 路径通常非连续，跳过中间层；(3) 80-83%的事实存在功能等价的备选路径，揭示了高度冗余性
- 揭示实体解析(ER)并非事实检索的普遍前提条件，仅12.9%（LLaMA）和9.1%（Qwen）的主路径中检测到显式ER步骤
- 为知识编辑中"定位-编辑不一致"现象提供了合理解释

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the concept of "attribute-computation paths" -- minimal irreducible subsets of layers whose computations over entity tokens are necessary and sufficient for correct factual retrieval
- Proposes a novel iterative activation-patching protocol using counterfactual prompts to automatically identify primary and alternative minimal paths
- Demonstrates on LLaMA 3.1 8B (32 layers) and Qwen3 8B (36 layers) that: (1) attribute computation completes in early-mid layers (avg l_attr = 4.61 for LLaMA, 7.97 for Qwen); (2) paths are typically non-contiguous, skipping intermediate layers; (3) multiple functionally equivalent paths exist in 80.1% (LLaMA) and 82.7% (Qwen) of cases
- Shows that entity resolution is not a universal prerequisite for factual recall -- only ~10-13% of paths show detectable ER steps
- Provides a mechanistic explanation for the localization-editing mismatch observed in knowledge editing research

## Methodology

**Problem Formulation.** The paper studies factual recall prompts of the form (entity, relation) -> attribute (e.g., "The mother tongue of Angela Merkel is" -> "German"). The key concept is the *attribute-computation path*: a minimal subset of transformer layers [L_a1, ..., L_ak] through which the entity representation must be processed for the model to correctly predict the target attribute. "Minimal" means removing any single layer from the path disrupts correct prediction.

**Two-Phase Path Identification:**

1. **Locating l_attr (termination layer):** Using a "lock" operation (activation patching that freezes the entity representation at layer l, propagating it to all higher layers), the method identifies the earliest layer after which no further computation on the entity representation is needed. This establishes the upper bound.

2. **Iterative Greedy Search for Minimal Path:** Starting from the entity embedding, the algorithm greedily extends the path by skipping as many layers as possible at each step. It uses an "isolate" operation that feeds clean entity embeddings to path layers, passes each path layer's output to the next path layer, and injects counterfactual activations into all non-path layers. Counterfactual prompts (same relation, different entity of the same semantic type) are used to determine which layers are dispensable.

3. **Alternative Path Discovery:** By relaxing the l_attr constraint (allowing paths to terminate at the final model layer instead), the method discovers alternative functionally equivalent paths that recruit different, typically deeper layers.

**Validation Experiments:** Four targeted patching interventions (Representation Knockout, Downstream Injection, Path + Continuation, Global Broadcast) verify the functional role of the identified representations.

**Data and Models:** 2,000 prompts from the CounterFact dataset (single-token target attributes correctly predicted by each model), tested on LLaMA 3.1 8B and Qwen3 8B. Interventions implemented using the NNsight package.

## Results & Highlights

**Attribute computation completes early.** Average l_attr is 4.61 for LLaMA and 7.97 for Qwen (out of 32 and 36 layers respectively), confirming that entity enrichment occurs in early-to-mid layers.

**Paths are multi-step and non-contiguous.** Average path length is 5.91 layers (LLaMA) and 7.97 layers (Qwen). Critically, 33.1% of LLaMA paths and 78.6% of Qwen paths skip at least one intermediate layer, with average skip sizes of 0.7 and 2.0 layers respectively.

**Redundant alternative paths exist.** Non-identical alternative paths were found in 80.1% (LLaMA) and 82.7% (Qwen) of cases. Alternative paths are universally longer (mean 9.47 for LLaMA, 10.47 for Qwen) and terminate deeper (mean l_attr = 13.93 for LLaMA, 18.06 for Qwen), recruiting distinct, deeper modules.

**Representation at l_attr is necessary but often not self-sufficient.** Representation Knockout disrupted predictions in >94% of cases. However, Downstream Injection (broadcasting l_attr representation alone) only restored correct predictions in ~56% of LLaMA primary paths, indicating intermediate path layers often contribute essential processing beyond the final enriched representation.

**Hybrid paths work.** Recombining prefixes from primary paths with suffixes from alternative paths yielded successful hybrid paths in 35.4% (LLaMA) and 32.8% (Qwen) of prompts, proving additional computation paths beyond the two identified.

**Entity resolution is not a prerequisite.** Only 12.9% (LLaMA) and 9.1% (Qwen) of primary paths showed detectable entity resolution steps via Patchscopes, implying attribute retrieval often occurs implicitly without an explicit "identity state."

**Path lengths are robust to counterfactual noise.** A noise-free variant (locking excluded layers instead of counterfactual patching) produced virtually identical path lengths (LLaMA primary: 5.88 vs 5.91), though alternative paths terminated deeper under noise, suggesting depth is noise-sensitive while path length is intrinsic.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The concept of minimal attribute-computation paths provides a useful new lens for studying factual retrieval, going beyond prior work that focused on information flow to the final token or localization of knowledge in specific layers. The finding of non-contiguous, redundant paths is a genuinely interesting structural insight.

**Rigor:** Solid. The iterative patching protocol is well-formalized, and the paper includes multiple validation experiments (knockout, injection, continuation, broadcast) that triangulate the claims. The noise-sensitivity analysis (counterfactual vs. locked excluded layers) is a thoughtful robustness check.

**Reproducibility:** Good. Two open-weight models, a public dataset (CounterFact), and released code. The method relies on the NNsight library for interventions, which is also publicly available.

**Significance:** The main implication -- that the "facts stored in MLP layers" view is overly simplistic -- is important for the knowledge editing community. The explanation for localization-editing mismatch (Hase et al., 2023) is compelling: editing succeeds at necessary transformation stages, not necessarily where knowledge is "stored."

**Red Flags / Caveats:**
- The greedy search is explicitly acknowledged to find only a lower bound on redundancy; exhaustive search would likely reveal far more paths. The paper is honest about this but it means the redundancy findings are conservative estimates.
- Only tested on 8B-parameter models. Whether the same non-contiguous, redundant structure holds at larger scales (70B+) is unknown.
- The method is restricted to single-token target attributes, which limits generality.
- The CounterFact dataset has known biases (Western-centric entities, specific relation types). Path characteristics might differ for other knowledge domains.

## Strengths

- Clean formalization of attribute-computation paths with clear definitions of minimality, lock, and isolate operations
- Comprehensive experimental design with four distinct validation interventions that each probe different aspects of the identified paths
- Results are consistent across two architecturally different model families (LLaMA and Qwen), strengthening generalizability claims
- The paper connects findings back to practical implications (knowledge editing mismatch) rather than remaining purely descriptive
- Honest about limitations -- explicitly acknowledges greedy search provides lower bounds on redundancy
- Code released publicly

## Limitations

- Greedy search algorithm cannot guarantee finding all minimal paths or the true minimum; exhaustive search is combinatorially infeasible
- Limited to 8B models only; scaling behavior is unknown
- Restricted to single-token attribute predictions; multi-token attributes may involve different mechanisms
- CounterFact dataset limits diversity of entities and relations studied
- The role of attention layers vs. MLP layers within paths is not decomposed
- Whether models actually utilize the deeper "backup" paths during normal (non-intervened) inference remains unclear -- they may be artifacts accessible only under intervention

## Relevance

本文对理解LLM内部知识表示机制有重要意义，特别是对知识编辑(knowledge editing)、模型压缩和mechanistic interpretability研究方向。"知识分布式冗余存储"的发现解释了为什么基于定位的知识编辑方法效果不稳定，对设计更可靠的模型编辑和知识操控方法具有直接启发价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How do attribute-computation paths change at larger model scales (70B+)? Does redundancy increase or decrease with scale?
2. Can the identified path structure be exploited for more reliable knowledge editing -- e.g., editing at multiple path layers simultaneously?
3. What is the relationship between path structure and knowledge acquisition during pretraining? Do facts learned earlier develop more redundant paths?
4. How do multi-token attributes change the path structure? Does the model need to "plan" the full attribute before the first token?
5. Can path analysis distinguish between genuinely memorized facts and facts derived from compositional reasoning?
