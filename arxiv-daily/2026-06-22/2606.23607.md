## Summary / 摘要

This paper proposes **LMC-DM (Linear Mode Connectivity via Dual Learned Matching)**, a scalable framework for aligning and merging independently trained pretrained Transformers up to the billion-parameter scale. The key insight is that independently trained models often implement equivalent functions but in different weight-space coordinate systems due to functionality-preserving symmetries. By systematically parameterizing these symmetries and introducing a **dual learning procedure** where both endpoint models jointly learn transformations toward a shared linear interpolation path, the method substantially reduces interpolation loss barriers compared to prior single-sided approaches.

本文提出了 **LMC-DM（基于双向学习匹配的线性模式连通性）**，一个可扩展的框架，用于对齐和合并独立训练的预训练 Transformer 模型，规模可达数十亿参数。核心洞察在于，独立训练的模型往往实现了等价的功能，但由于功能保持对称性，它们处于不同的权重空间坐标系中。通过系统地参数化这些对称性，并引入一种**双向学习过程**——让两个端点模型共同学习各自的变换以趋向共享的线性插值路径——该方法相较于先前的单侧方法，显著降低了插值损失壁垒。

## Key Contributions / 主要贡献

1. **Comprehensive Transformer symmetry formulation**: The paper systematically catalogs functionality-preserving weight transformations specific to Transformers, including normalization absorption, residual-space rotation and scaling, attention-head permutation, per-head QK and OV circuit symmetries, FFN permutation, and GLU rescaling symmetry.
   **全面的 Transformer 对称性公式化**：系统梳理了 Transformer 特有的功能保持权重变换，包括归一化吸收、残差空间旋转与缩放、注意力头置换、逐头 QK 和 OV 电路对称性、FFN 置换以及 GLU 缩放对称性。

2. **Dual learned matching**: Unlike prior methods that transform only one model toward a fixed reference, LMC-DM assigns learnable transformations to **both** checkpoints, allowing them to jointly meet in a shared low-loss basin. The authors prove that dual parameterization is formally equivalent to single-sided (can be absorbed), yet empirically yields better optimization due to more favorable loss landscape geometry.
   **双向学习匹配**：与仅变换一个模型以对齐固定参考的先前方法不同，LMC-DM 为**两个**检查点分配可学习的变换，使它们共同向一个共享的低损失盆地移动。作者证明双侧参数化在形式上可等价于单侧（可被吸收），但在经验上由于更有利的损失景观几何而产生更好的优化效果。

3. **Scalable empirical demonstration**: Near-zero loss barriers on WikiText for Pythia language models with 160M parameters; ViT-L maintains above 69% ImageNet top-1 accuracy across the entire interpolation path; billion-parameter LLMs (Pythia up to 6.9B) exhibit only small loss barriers.
   **可扩展的实证验证**：在 WikiText 上，160M 参数的 Pythia 语言模型实现了近零损失壁垒；ViT-L 在整个插值路径上保持超过 69% 的 ImageNet top-1 准确率；数十亿参数的 LLM（Pythia 最高 6.9B）仅表现出较小的损失壁垒。

## Methodology / 方法论

### Functionality-Preserving Symmetries / 功能保持对称性

The framework exploits the fact that Transformers have a rich family of weight transformations that preserve the input-output function:

该框架利用了 Transformer 拥有丰富的权重变换族且能保持输入输出功能这一事实：

- **Normalization absorption**: LayerNorm/RMSNorm affine parameters are absorbed into adjacent linear layers, yielding parameter-free normalization for symmetry analysis.
- **Residual-space symmetry**: A global orthogonal matrix G and scalar g can rotate and scale the entire residual stream without changing network behavior (absorbed by all residual read/write matrices).
- **Attention-head permutation**: Heads within each layer can be freely permuted.
- **QK/OV circuit symmetries**: Per-head invertible transformations on query-key and value-output circuits that preserve attention logits and outputs.
- **FFN permutation and GLU rescaling**: Hidden-channel permutations in FFN layers and value-branch rescaling in gated linear units.

### Matching Pipeline / 匹配流程

1. **Weight Matching (initialization)**: Solve for the global orthogonal matrix G via SVD of cross-model residual weights, then greedily match head and FFN permutations via bilinear assignment.
2. **Learned Matching**: Parameterize continuous symmetries (orthogonal via Cayley transform, invertible via polar decomposition) and optimize them by minimizing the interpolation loss.
3. **Dual Learned Matching**: Both endpoints maintain their own symmetry modules; at each training step, a random interpolation coefficient lambda is sampled, both models are transformed, interpolated, and the task loss drives gradient updates to both sets of symmetry parameters.

### Symmetry Parameterization / 对称性参数化

| Method | Form | Coverage |
|--------|------|----------|
| Cayley | C(A) = (I-A)^{-1}(I+A), A skew-symmetric | SO(N)\{-1} |
| Matrix exp (orthogonal) | Q = exp(A), A skew-symmetric | SO(N) |
| Matrix exp (invertible) | M = exp(U), U unconstrained | Subset of GL+(N) |
| Polar | M = QP, Q via Cayley, P = LL^T | Cayley-limited subset of GL+(N) |

The best combination found: **Cayley for orthogonal symmetries + Polar for invertible symmetries**.

### Key Findings / 关键发现

- **Near-zero barriers at 160M scale**: Pythia-160M achieves a loss barrier of only 0.18, the first demonstration of near-barrier-free LMC at this scale.
- **Scales to billions**: Pythia-6.9B shows a loss barrier of only 0.18 after dual matching, though OLMo-7B shows a higher barrier (1.53), suggesting that factors beyond scale (training data, initialization, optimization dynamics) also matter.
- **Dual consistently beats single**: Across all models, dual matching outperforms single-sided matching (e.g., ViT-B: 0.82 vs 1.05; Pythia-160M: 0.18 vs 0.27).
- **Ablation insights**: Removing QK or OV symmetries significantly hurts ViT performance; the global scaling symmetry has a smaller but non-negligible effect; Cayley parameterization outperforms unconstrained and matrix-exponential alternatives.

### Limitations Noted / 局限性

- Downstream zero-shot performance of merged models still lags behind original endpoints (Table 8), indicating low WikiText barriers do not yet guarantee lossless downstream behavior.
- The framework requires compatible architectures and dimensions; extending to heterogeneous architectures remains challenging.
- Some Transformer architectures have structurally limited functionality-preserving symmetries.

## Critical Assessment / 批判性评估

**Strengths**:
- The systematic enumeration of Transformer symmetries is thorough and mathematically rigorous, going well beyond the permutation symmetries studied in prior work on MLPs.
- The dual matching idea is elegant: the formal proof that it is equivalent to single-sided matching (Appendix C) yet empirically superior is a genuinely interesting finding about optimization geometry vs. parameter expressiveness.
- The scale of experiments (up to 6.9B parameters) is convincing and represents a significant advance over prior LMC work that was limited to tens of millions of parameters.

**Weaknesses**:
- The practical utility of the method is not yet demonstrated. Loss barriers are reduced, but downstream task performance of merged models still substantially degrades compared to endpoints. The paper acknowledges this but does not address it.
- The OLMo-7B result (barrier of 1.53) shows the method does not uniformly succeed at the billion-parameter scale, and the authors attribute this to "factors beyond scale" without deeper investigation.
- Training the symmetry parameters requires non-trivial compute (hours to a day on A6000 GPUs), which may limit practical adoption for model merging scenarios where the goal is to avoid retraining.
- The paper only considers pairs of models with identical architecture trained on overlapping data distributions. Real-world model merging often involves models with different architectures or training regimes.

**Significance**: This work provides strong evidence that the weight-space structure of large pretrained Transformers is more regular than raw parameterization suggests, connecting to the Platonic Representation Hypothesis. It is primarily a foundational/analytical contribution rather than a practical merging tool at this stage.

**意义**：本工作提供了有力证据，表明大型预训练 Transformer 的权重空间结构比原始参数化所暗示的更加规整，这与柏拉图表征假说相呼应。目前它主要是一项基础性/分析性的贡献，而非一个实用的模型合并工具。

## Relevance / 相关性

This paper is relevant to researchers working on model merging, federated learning, weight-space analysis, and efficient AI. The symmetry-aware alignment framework could enable better weight interpolation for model ensembling, and the theoretical insights about Transformer symmetry groups are valuable for understanding the loss landscape geometry of modern architectures.

本文与模型合并、联邦学习、权重空间分析和高效 AI 领域的研究者相关。对称性感知的对齐框架可以实现更好的权重插值用于模型集成，关于 Transformer 对称群的理论洞察对于理解现代架构的损失景观几何非常有价值。
