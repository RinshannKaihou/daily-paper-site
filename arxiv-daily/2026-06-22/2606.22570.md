# What are Key Factors for Updates in RL for LLM Reasoning?

## TL;DR

This paper provides a theoretical analysis of why seemingly contradictory token-level heuristics in Reinforcement Learning from Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) can both work. The key insight is that off-policy degree -- determined by the number of gradient steps per rollout -- changes which tokens survive importance-sampling clipping and thus dominate gradient updates. The authors identify a "gradient dominance reversal" phenomenon and propose Adaptive Clip Policy Optimization (ACPO), which sets per-bin clipping thresholds based on local IS-ratio variance, consistently outperforming DAPO and CISPO across tasks, model scales, and training regimes.

本文对"可验证奖励强化学习"(RLVR)中看似矛盾的token级启发式方法为何都能奏效进行了理论分析。核心洞察在于：离策略程度(off-policy degree)——由每次rollout的梯度更新步数决定——会改变哪些token能通过重要性采样裁剪(clipping)并主导梯度更新。作者发现了"梯度主导反转"现象，并提出自适应裁剪策略优化(ACPO)，根据局部IS比率方差为每个token概率分组设置裁剪阈值，在多种任务、模型规模和训练策略下均持续优于DAPO和CISPO等强基线。

## Abstract

Reinforcement Learning from Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) has emerged as a promising framework for enhancing the reasoning ability of large language models. However, much of the existing work is guided by heuristic intuition, leading to divergent algorithmic choices, even contradictory ones that nevertheless report empirical gains. To better understand this phenomenon, the authors conduct a theoretical analysis of RLVR updates. The study reveals that differences in off-policy degree, determined by the number of gradient steps per rollout, substantially affect the distribution of importance sampling ratios and their clipping behavior, thereby altering which tokens dominate the update. Building on this insight, they characterize gradient expectation as the central quantity governing update dynamics and analyze the roles of token probability, advantage, and importance sampling ratio. Motivated by these findings, they propose Adaptive Clip Policy Optimization (ACPO), which adjusts clipping boundaries across token groups according to the empirical variance of their importance sampling ratios. Experiments on 3B and 7B models across diverse reasoning benchmarks demonstrate that ACPO outperforms strong baselines such as DAPO and CISPO.

## Problem Setting / 问题背景

Recent RLVR research for LLM reasoning has produced conflicting prescriptions at the token level. Wang et al. [2025b] report that selectively updating high-entropy tokens yields faster convergence and better accuracy, arguing these tokens are pivotal for reasoning. Yang et al. [2025b] argue the opposite -- amplifying low-probability tokens leads to slow convergence and instability due to high-variance gradients. Since high-entropy tokens are typically low-probability tokens, these two camps are targeting overlapping token populations yet reaching contradictory conclusions. Both approaches report empirical gains, creating a paradox that prior work has not explained.

近期RLVR研究在token层面产生了相互矛盾的结论。一方认为应优先更新高熵token以加速收敛，另一方则认为放大低概率token会导致训练不稳定。由于高熵token通常就是低概率token，两种策略针对的token群体高度重叠却得出相反结论，且双方都报告了实验增益，形成了一个未被解释的悖论。

## Methodology / 方法

### Theoretical Analysis of Gradient Dynamics

The paper's central theoretical contribution is analyzing the **expected effective gradient under clipping** in GRPO. The authors:

1. **Decompose the GRPO gradient** into token-level contributions (Eq. 2), where each token's contribution depends on its importance sampling (IS) ratio, advantage, and a clipping indicator.

2. **Model IS-ratio variance** as a function of token probability. Lemma 2.1 shows that for a token with behavior-policy probability $\pi$, the IS-ratio variance scales as $\sigma_\rho^2(\pi) = \kappa^2(1-\pi)^2 + O(\kappa^3)$. This means low-probability tokens have much higher IS-ratio dispersion and are far more likely to be clipped.

3. **Derive the conditional gradient expectation** (Proposition 2.2) in closed form as $\mathbb{E}[G \mid \pi] = (1-\pi)\,\mu(\pi)\,H(\tau(\pi))$, where $(1-\pi)$ is the on-policy sensitivity, $\mu(\pi)$ encodes the advantage-IS coupling, and $H(\tau)$ captures trust-region filtering effects that grow with off-policy degree.

4. **Prove a Gradient Dominance Reversal** (Corollary 2.3): In near on-policy regimes (few updates per rollout), low-probability tokens dominate the gradient. As the off-policy degree increases (more updates per rollout), their IS ratios become highly dispersed, they get clipped more aggressively, and high-probability tokens take over as dominant contributors.

### Key Factors Identified (Section 3)

The analysis framework examines updates along two dimensions -- **magnitude** (which tokens contribute most) and **direction** (how the update trajectory deviates from the on-policy ideal):

- **Token probability ("tail tokens are strong but fragile")**: Low-probability tokens carry large base sensitivity via $(1-\pi)$ but are most susceptible to clipping under off-policy drift.
- **Advantage sign**: Negative-advantage samples (A < 0) steer the update direction more than positive ones. The negative-advantage gradient subspace aligns substantially better with the final update direction, suggesting RLVR optimization is driven more by error correction than reinforcing good trajectories.
- **IS-ratio clipping**: Clipping is not merely variance reduction -- it changes the composition of surviving token updates and can rotate the gradient direction by ~47 degrees even when the overall norm is similar.

### Adaptive Clip Policy Optimization (ACPO)

ACPO replaces the single global clipping range with **bin-specific thresholds** calibrated to local IS-ratio dispersion:

1. Partition tokens into $B$ equal-width bins based on behavior-policy probability: $\mathcal{B}(i,t) = \min\{B, \lfloor B \cdot \pi_{\theta_\text{old}}(o_{i,t} \mid q, o_{i,<t}) \rfloor + 1\}$
2. Compute within-bin IS-ratio standard deviation: $\sigma_b = \text{Std}(\{\rho_{i,t} : \mathcal{B}(i,t) = b\})$
3. Set bin-specific clipping range: $\epsilon_b = \epsilon_\text{base} + \alpha \cdot \sigma_b$

This yields wider clipping for high-variance (low-probability) bins in on-policy regimes (preserving their learning signal) and tighter clipping in off-policy regimes (preventing their instability), naturally adapting to the off-policy degree without manual tuning.

ACPO使用基于token概率分箱的自适应裁剪：将token按行为策略概率分入B个等宽箱，计算每箱内IS比率的标准差$\sigma_b$，并设置箱级裁剪范围$\epsilon_b = \epsilon_\text{base} + \alpha \cdot \sigma_b$。这使得在近策略(on-policy)状态下，高方差的低概率token获得更宽的裁剪范围（保留学习信号），而在离策略状态下自动收紧裁剪（防止不稳定），无需手动调节即可适应离策略程度。

## Key Results / 关键结果

**Experimental setup**: Three reasoning tasks -- ORZ-57K (math), HiTab (tabular QA), Countdown (logic puzzles) -- on Qwen2.5-3B, Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, and Qwen2.5-7B. Each method is tested under near on-policy (N-OnP, 2 updates/rollout) and off-policy (OffP, 16 updates/rollout) regimes. Baselines include DAPO, CISPO, High/Low-Entropy, and AR-Lopti.

**Consistent improvements**: ACPO achieves the best average rank of 1.22 across all 18 task-regime configurations, with 15/18 first-place and 2/18 second-place finishes. DAPO averages rank 2.56 (2/18 wins) and CISPO averages rank 3.11 (0/18 wins).

**Cross-regime stability**: ACPO shows only 0.8% performance degradation from on-policy to off-policy, while CISPO degrades by 6.0%, AR-Lopti by 5.0%, and DAPO by 1.2%.

**ORZ-57K results (Qwen2.5-7B)**: ACPO achieves the best average across six math benchmarks (Minerva, Math500, AMC, AIME24, AIME25, Olympiad) with 39.86 (N-OnP) and 38.66 (OffP), vs. DAPO's 38.70/35.97 and CISPO's 37.93/35.47.

实验结果：在18个任务-训练方案配置中，ACPO取得1.22的最佳平均排名（15/18第一、2/18第二）。从近策略到离策略方案，ACPO性能仅下降0.8%，而CISPO下降6.0%，AR-Lopti下降5.0%。在ORZ-57K数学基准上(Qwen2.5-7B)，ACPO六项基准均值达39.86(近策略)/38.66(离策略)，显著优于DAPO和CISPO。

## Critical Assessment / 批判性评估

**Strengths**:
- The theoretical framework elegantly unifies contradictory empirical findings by identifying off-policy degree as the hidden confound. The gradient dominance reversal is a genuine insight that explains why both "focus on high-entropy tokens" and "suppress low-probability tokens" can work under different experimental setups.
- The proposed ACPO method is simple, principled, and adds negligible computational overhead (just computing per-bin IS-ratio variance and adjusting clip thresholds).
- The cross-regime stability result is practically valuable: ACPO maintains performance regardless of whether training is on-policy or off-policy, reducing a key hyperparameter sensitivity.

**Weaknesses and limitations**:
- The theoretical analysis relies on several modeling assumptions (log-normal IS ratio, linear advantage-log-IS coupling, small logit perturbation) whose tightness is not rigorously validated beyond first-order approximations.
- All experiments use Qwen2.5 models (3B and 7B). Generalization to larger scales (70B+) or different model families remains unverified.
- The bin count $B=5$ and scaling factor $\alpha=3$ are fixed across experiments. While the authors report sensitivity analysis in the appendix, the interaction between these hyperparameters and task difficulty or model capacity is not deeply explored.
- The paper focuses exclusively on GRPO-style algorithms. Whether the gradient dominance reversal phenomenon extends to PPO-based RLVR methods or other policy gradient variants is left as future work.

**优势**：理论框架巧妙地将矛盾的实验结果统一起来，发现离策略程度是隐藏的混淆变量。ACPO方法简洁、有原则性、计算开销极小，且跨训练方案稳定性强。

**不足**：理论分析依赖多个建模假设（对数正态IS比率、线性优势-对数IS耦合等），仅做了一阶近似验证；实验仅在Qwen2.5的3B/7B模型上进行，未验证更大规模模型；仅关注GRPO类算法，未讨论PPO等其他策略梯度方法。

## Relevance to My Research / 与我的研究的相关性

This paper provides actionable insights for anyone working with RLVR/GRPO for LLM reasoning:
- The off-policy degree (updates per rollout) is a critical but under-discussed hyperparameter that fundamentally changes gradient dynamics.
- Uniform clipping is a systematic source of bias, and adaptive clipping per token-probability group is a simple drop-in improvement.
- The finding that negative-advantage samples drive optimization direction more than positive ones has implications for reward shaping and sample selection strategies.
