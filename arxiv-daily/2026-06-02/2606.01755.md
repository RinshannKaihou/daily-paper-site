> **一句话总结：** 本文提出了 TriAlign，首个基于离线多智能体强化学习（MARL）的框架，用于解决个性化大语言模型（PLLMs）在不同社会群体间产生的事实性回答不一致问题，在保证个性化的同时实现了跨群体的通用真理一致性。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes TriAlign, the first offline multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) framework for Truth-Invariant Alignment (TIA) in personalized LLMs. It jointly optimizes universal truth accuracy, cross-group consistency, and personalization through a fairness-aware Nash Social Welfare objective and an explicit inconsistency penalty, achieving the best balance across all three goals on diverse benchmarks.

## 核心贡献

- **首次将 TIA 形式化为离线 MARL 问题：** 提出了 Truth-Invariant Alignment（TIA）这一新的对齐问题，旨在确保个性化 LLM 在不同社会群体间对客观事实的回答保持一致，同时保留个性化特征。
- **提出 TriAlign 框架：** 首个面向 TIA 的离线多智能体强化学习框架，将每个社会群体建模为一个智能体，在共享环境中通过中心化训练、去中心化执行的方式交互学习。
- **设计公平感知目标函数：** 受 Nash Social Welfare（NSW）启发，引入公平感知优化目标，避免策略偏向多数群体或高性能群体；同时在奖励中显式加入跨群体不一致性惩罚。
- **构建多群体多轮交互轨迹：** 设计了一套多社会群体、多轮交互的模拟框架，通过比较式反馈迭代优化回答，生成用于离线 RL 训练的高质量轨迹数据。
- **在多个基准上验证有效性：** 在数学推理（AIME25）、偏见评估（StereoSet）以及通用知识（MMLU-Pro、TruthfulQA）等任务上均取得最佳的三目标平衡。

## Key Contributions

- **First formulation of TIA as an offline MARL problem:** Introduces Truth-Invariant Alignment (TIA), a new alignment objective that ensures personalized LLMs maintain consistent universal truths across social groups while preserving personalization.
- **Proposes the TriAlign framework:** The first offline multi-agent reinforcement learning framework for TIA, where each social group is modeled as an agent interacting within a shared environment under centralized training and decentralized execution.
- **Fairness-aware objective design:** Inspired by Nash Social Welfare (NSW), TriAlign introduces a fairness-regularized optimization objective that prevents the policy from favoring dominant groups, coupled with an explicit cross-group inconsistency penalty in the reward function.
- **Multi-social-group interaction simulation:** Constructs multi-turn interaction trajectories where agents iteratively refine responses using comparative feedback from other groups, forming an offline RL dataset for training.
- **Strong empirical results across benchmarks:** Demonstrates the best balance among truth accuracy, cross-group consistency, and personalization on AIME25, StereoSet, MMLU-Pro, and TruthfulQA compared to strong baselines.

## Methodology

The paper addresses a critical gap in personalized LLM alignment: existing methods either ignore personalization or focus solely on subjective preference alignment, overlooking fairness and consistency in universal truths across demographic groups. The authors formulate TIA as a multi-agent RL problem under the centralized training with decentralized execution (CTDE) paradigm.

**TIA Formulation:** Given an input query \(x\) and a social group profile \(g\) (e.g., gender, age, education), a PLLM generates a response \(a \sim \pi_\theta(\cdot \mid x, g)\). TIA requires two properties: (1) *Truth Invariance* — responses across any two social groups must share the same underlying universal truth \(y^\star\); and (2) *Personalized Alignment* — responses must satisfy group-specific personalization constraints \(c(g)\) such as tone or style.

**TriAlign Architecture:** Each social group is treated as an independent agent. At interaction step \(t\), agent \(i\) observes state \(s_{i,t} = (x, g_i, \tau_{i,t})\) (where \(\tau_{i,t}\) is conversation history) and generates action \(a_{i,t}\). The environment collects all responses, computes comparative feedback \(o_{i,t}\) to identify cross-group inconsistencies, and assigns a step-level reward:

\[r_i(a_t) = r_{\text{truth}}(a_{i,t}, y^\star) + \lambda_{\text{pref}} r_{\text{pref}}(a_{i,t}, c(g_i)) - \lambda_{\text{cons}} \cdot \frac{1}{m}\sum_{j=1}^m r_{\text{truth}}(a_{j,t}, y^\star)\]

This reward combines truth correctness, personalization quality, and an explicit penalty for deviation from the average truth accuracy across all \(m\) agents.

**Fairness-Aware Optimization:** To avoid biasing toward high-performing groups, TriAlign optimizes a Nash Social Welfare-inspired objective: \(\prod_{i=1}^m (R_i + \epsilon)\), or equivalently \(\sum_{i=1}^m \log(R_i + \epsilon)\), where \(R_i\) is the cumulative return of agent \(i\). Combined with a KL-constrained offline RL formulation, this yields a weighted supervised fine-tuning objective where weights combine standard advantage terms with NSW-inspired terms that upweight underperforming groups.

**Training Data Construction:** The authors identify 14 demographic categories (75 category-value pairs) based on 327 prior studies. They augment a large-scale persona dataset via a persona-to-persona augmentation pipeline to ensure balanced representation. Multi-turn interactions with up to 8 agents and 4 turns are simulated using LangGraph, with GPT-4o generating preference labels for personalization evaluation.

The base model is Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507, fully fine-tuned for 3 epochs with learning rate \(1 \times 10^{-5}\) using LLaMA-Factory and DeepSpeed ZeRO-3 on 2×A100 GPUs.

## Results & Highlights

**In-Domain Benchmarks (AIME25, StereoSet):** TriAlign achieves the strongest balance across all metrics. On AIME25 with explicit preference, TriAlign reaches 0.699 Pref, 0.467 Mean accuracy, and 0.433 Worst accuracy, with Std=0.027 and Gap=0.033 — drastically lower inconsistency than baselines. On StereoSet with explicit preference, TriAlign achieves 0.791 Pref, 0.788 Mean, and 0.773 Worst, with Std=0.010 and Gap=0.015. SFT and SWIFT, while improving over prompt-based methods, still show higher inconsistency (larger Std/Gap/CV).

**Out-of-Domain Benchmarks (MMLU-Pro, TruthfulQA):** TriAlign generalizes effectively. On MMLU-Pro with implicit preference, it achieves 0.510 Pref and 0.805 Mean (vs. 0.412/0.767 for base model), with Std dropping from 0.047 to 0.007. On TruthfulQA with explicit preference, it reaches 0.755 Pref and 0.853 Mean, with Std=0.006 and CV=0.007 — the lowest inconsistency among all methods.

**Performance Across Social Groups:** Figure 6 reveals that the base model exhibits substantial disparities across social groups (e.g., on StereoSet, Lower class is +19.4% above mean while Upper class is -20.1% below). TriAlign produces much more balanced distributions, with all groups clustering near the mean while maintaining higher overall accuracy.

**Ablation Study:** Removing the NSW objective ("w/o NSW") leads to noticeable drops in Worst accuracy and larger disparities. Removing the consistency modeling component ("w/o CM") substantially increases Std, Gap, and CV. Both ablated variants also show lower personalization scores, confirming that both components are essential.

**Qualitative Analysis:** Figure 5 shows that before alignment, the base model produces incorrect answers with surface-level personalization patterns. After TriAlign, the model preserves user-preferred communication styles while generating logically consistent reasoning and correct final answers.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The paper makes a genuine conceptual contribution by identifying and formally defining the Truth-Invariant Alignment problem, which bridges personalized LLMs and algorithmic fairness — two communities that have largely operated independently. The application of multi-agent RL (specifically offline MARL with CTDE) to this problem is novel, as prior work relied almost exclusively on prompting-based debiasing.

**Rigor:** The experimental design is comprehensive. The authors evaluate on four benchmarks spanning mathematical reasoning, social bias, and general knowledge, under both implicit and explicit preference settings. They compare against six baselines spanning prompt-based and training-based methods. The ablation study clearly isolates the contributions of the NSW objective and consistency modeling. Statistical significance is claimed (p < 0.01) though exact test details are not elaborated.

**Reproducibility:** Strong. The base model (Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507), training framework (LLaMA-Factory), hyperparameters, and evaluation metrics are all specified. The persona augmentation pipeline and interaction simulation are described in sufficient detail. However, the exact prompts used for environment feedback and reward computation are relegated to the appendix, which is not accessible in the extracted text.

**Significance:** This work addresses a real and growing concern: as LLMs are deployed in education, healthcare, and decision-support systems, ensuring that factual accuracy does not systematically vary across demographic groups is both an ethical imperative and a technical challenge. The paper provides a principled training-based solution rather than relying on brittle prompting.

**Red Flags / Concerns:**
- The paper evaluates on a 4B parameter model. It is unclear how well the method scales to larger models (7B, 70B+), which may have different persona-following and truth-retention characteristics.
- The "universal truth" definition assumes ground-truth answers exist, which works for math and multiple-choice QA but becomes murkier for open-ended factual questions where "truth" itself may be contested.
- The evaluation of personalization relies on GPT-4o as an automated judge, which may inherit its own biases and may not perfectly align with human judgments of personalization quality.
- The multi-agent interaction simulation (8 agents, 4 turns) is computationally expensive, raising questions about scalability to larger group spaces.

## Strengths

- **Novel problem formulation:** TIA is well-motivated and clearly defined with formal mathematical requirements.
- **Principled approach:** The combination of offline MARL, Nash Social Welfare fairness, and explicit consistency penalties is theoretically grounded and empirically effective.
- **Comprehensive evaluation:** Four benchmarks, two preference settings, six baselines, ablation studies, and cross-group fairness analysis provide strong evidence.
- **Best three-way trade-off:** Unlike baselines that sacrifice personalization for consistency (e.g., 2STEP PROMPT) or ignore consistency entirely (standard PLLMs), TriAlign genuinely improves all three objectives simultaneously.
- **Generalization:** Strong out-of-domain performance indicates the method learns robust behavioral principles rather than dataset-specific patterns.

## Limitations

- **Synthetic personas:** The 7,500 personas are LLM-generated and may not fully capture the complexity, diversity, and intersectionality of real-world user populations. Certain cultural and social nuances may be underrepresented.
- **Fundamental tension unresolved:** Balancing universal truth consistency and personalization remains challenging. The paper improves the trade-off but does not fully "solve" it — further alignment advances are needed.
- **Limited model scale:** Experiments are conducted on a 4B parameter model. Effectiveness on larger models (which prior work suggests may respond differently to debiasing) is not validated.
- **Ground-truth dependency:** The method assumes access to correct answers (\(y^\star\)), limiting applicability to domains without clear ground truth.
- **Computational cost:** Multi-agent multi-turn simulation and full-model fine-tuning require substantial GPU resources (2×A100) and energy, raising scalability and environmental concerns.

## Relevance

本文对个性化大模型与算法公平性的交叉领域做出了重要贡献，提出的 TriAlign 框架为解决 LLM 在不同社会群体间的事实性回答不一致问题提供了首个基于训练的对齐方法，对公平 AI 系统的研究与部署具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does TriAlign scale to larger models (e.g., 14B, 70B parameters), and does the relative importance of the NSW objective versus the consistency penalty change with model scale?
2. Can TriAlign be adapted to domains without explicit ground-truth answers, such as open-ended medical or legal advice, where "universal truth" is more nuanced?
3. How would the framework perform with real human-evaluated personalization metrics instead of GPT-4o-based automated evaluation?
4. Could the multi-agent interaction framework be extended to online RL settings, potentially enabling continuous adaptation as new social groups or persona types emerge?
5. What is the impact of persona intersectionality (e.g., combining gender, age, and education simultaneously) on truth consistency, and does TriAlign handle high-dimensional persona spaces effectively?
