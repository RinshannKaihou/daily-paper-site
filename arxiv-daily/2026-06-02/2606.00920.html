Accuracy, Stability, and Repeated-Run Reliability of Large Language Models on Deterministic Programming Tasks

arXiv: 2606.00920

Date: 2026-06-02

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00920

一句话总结： 该研究通过对16个主流LLM在100道LeetCode题目上进行5轮重复评测（共16,000个实例），发现单次运行通过率（RLPR）与完美稳定率（PSR）之间存在高达17.8个百分点的差距，且该差距在中等性能模型上最大，揭示了传统单次评测指标严重高估模型在确定性任务中的实际部署可靠性。
TL;DR: This study evaluates 16 LLMs across five provider families on 100 recent LeetCode-style problems with five repeated runs each (16,000 total instances). It finds that run-level pass rate consistently overstates retry-free coverage by up to 17.8 percentage points—particularly for mid-accuracy systems—and introduces Perfect Stability Rate (PSR) and Average Variance (AV) as complementary metrics to expose deployment-reliability gaps that conventional single-run reporting hides.

核心贡献

  • 揭示了准确率与稳定性之间的系统性差距：提出并量化了运行级通过率（RLPR）与完美稳定率（PSR）之间的差异（Δ = RLPR − PSR），发现该差距最大可达17.8个百分点，且在中等准确率模型中最为显著。
  • 建立了可重复的评测协议：设计了一套包含重复运行、两种提示模板和标准化归一化流程的确定性代码生成评测框架。
  • 引入了新的稳定性度量指标：将PSR（完美稳定率）和AV（平均方差）作为衡量模型部署可靠性的核心指标，与传统的pass@k形成互补。
  • 对推理专用模型的稳定性进行了探索性分析：发现推理模型（如DeepSeek-R1、o4-mini等）并未在控制准确率后系统性地表现出更小的准确率–稳定性差距。
  • 证明了提示效应的模型依赖性：详细提示（DETAILED）与极简提示（MINIMAL）对不同模型的稳定性影响不一致，不存在 universally beneficial 的提示策略。

Key Contributions

  • Exposes a systematic accuracy–stability gap: Quantifies the divergence between run-level pass rate (RLPR) and perfect stability rate (PSR), showing gaps up to 17.8 percentage points that peak in the mid-accuracy tier.
  • Establishes a repeatable evaluation protocol: Designs a deterministic code-generation evaluation framework featuring repeated runs, two controlled prompt templates, and standardized normalization before execution.
  • Introduces novel stability metrics: Treats PSR and Average Variance (AV) as first-class evaluation targets complementary to traditional pass@k, making deployment reliability measurable.
  • Explores stability of reasoning-specialized models: Finds that reasoning models (DeepSeek-R1, o4-mini, QwQ-Plus, Gemini 2.5 Flash+Think) do not systematically exhibit smaller accuracy–stability gaps after controlling for accuracy level.
  • Demonstrates model-dependent prompt sensitivity: Shows that DETAILED vs. MINIMAL prompting produces heterogeneous effects across models, with no universally dominant template.

Methodology

The authors construct a recency-based benchmark of N = 100 LeetCode-style problems (20 Easy, 50 Medium, 30 Hard), selected as the most recently published problems at collection time to reduce training-data contamination risk. Each problem is paired with a deterministic Python test harness that yields a binary pass/fail verdict.

Models: 16 models from five provider families are evaluated: OpenAI (GPT-4.1, GPT-4.1-mini, o4-mini), Anthropic (Claude Sonnet 4.5, Claude Haiku 4.5), Google (Gemini 3.1 Pro, Gemini 3 Flash, Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite, Gemini 2.5 Flash, Gemini 2.5 Flash+Think), Qwen (QwQ-Plus, Qwen-Plus, Qwen-Max, Qwen-Turbo), and DeepSeek (DeepSeek-R1, DeepSeek-V3.2).

Prompts: Two templates are used—DETAILED (longer, structured scaffolding emphasizing constraint reading and edge-case coverage) and MINIMAL (compact instructions). Both enforce identical output constraints and code templates, isolating instruction density as the only variable.

Decoding: Temperature = 0.3, top-p = 0.9, default 4,096-token output budget (with documented exceptions for reasoning models: o4-mini uses 16,384 tokens; Gemini 2.5 Flash+Think uses 24,576 tokens). R = 5 repeated runs per problem are performed, yielding 16,000 total evaluation instances.

Metrics:

  • RLPR (Run-Level Pass Rate): Mean fraction of successful runs.
  • PSR (Perfect Stability Rate): Fraction of problems where all R = 5 runs pass.
  • AV (Average Variance): Average per-problem Bernoulli variance, capturing trial-to-trial instability.

Statistical inference uses 95% Wilson score intervals for RLPR/PSR and nonparametric bootstrap CIs for AV. Prompt comparisons use exact McNemar tests on the binary all-runs-pass indicator.

Results & Highlights

Main finding—RLPR overstates reliability: Across all 32 configurations, RLPR and PSR are strongly correlated (r = 0.985, p < 0.001), yet RLPR consistently exceeds PSR. The gap Δ = RLPR − PSR ranges from 2.4 pp (Qwen-Turbo, DETAILED) to 17.8 pp (Gemini 2.5 Flash, DETAILED).

Top performers: o4-mini (86.2% RLPR / 75.0% PSR under DETAILED), Gemini 3.1 Pro (86.2% / 75.0%), and DeepSeek-R1 (84.2% / 74.0%) lead on both metrics. However, rankings can reverse: GPT-4.1 leads GPT-4.1-mini on RLPR (59.6% vs. 58.4%) but trails on PSR (47.5% vs. 50.0%).

Mid-accuracy danger zone: The accuracy–stability gap peaks in the mid-performance tier because Bernoulli variance is maximized at p ≈ 0.5. Models like Gemini 2.5 Flash (53.8% RLPR, 36.0% PSR) exhibit the highest AV (0.0704), indicating substantial trial-to-trial variability.

Difficulty gradient: Easy problems achieve 90.6% run-level pass rate, Medium 62.2%, and Hard 34.0%. Wrong Answer dominates failure modes (~58%), followed by Time Limit Exceeded (~27%) and Runtime Error (~11%).

Reasoning models: After controlling for accuracy level via residual analysis, reasoning models show no systematic advantage. DeepSeek-R1 has a suggestively better-than-expected stability (residual ≈ −4.4 pp), but a permutation test across all four reasoning models vs. twelve standard models yields p = 0.97—severely underpowered and inconclusive.

Prompt sensitivity: Prompt effects are small and heterogeneous. Among 15 user-controllable models, the largest PSR shift is 4 pp (p = 0.219). Eleven models favor DETAILED, five favor MINIMAL, with mean absolute difference ~2.3 pp. The authors caution this is a deliberately low-contrast comparison.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: The core insight—that single-run accuracy overstates retry-free reliability—is mathematically expected (Bernoulli variance maximizes at p = 0.5), but the empirical calibration at scale across 16 contemporary models is a valuable contribution. The paper's main novelty lies in treating PSR and AV as first-class metrics and demonstrating that the gap is large enough (up to 17.8 pp) to reverse model rankings.

Rigor: The experimental design is careful and transparent. The use of Wilson score intervals, exact McNemar tests, and residual analysis for reasoning-model comparisons reflects solid statistical practice. The authors appropriately qualify conclusions when power is insufficient. However, the reasoning-model comparison (n=4 vs. n=12) is genuinely underpowered, and the authors correctly avoid overclaiming.

Reproducibility: High. Prompt templates are fully disclosed in Appendix A. Model API identifiers and access dates are documented in Appendix B. The benchmark construction protocol is described in detail. The main limitation for reproducibility is that the authors do not release the exact 100-problem benchmark or evaluation code, though the methodology is sufficiently detailed to replicate.

Significance: The work addresses a practically important gap between research benchmarks and deployment reality. For any deterministic text-conditioned generation task (code, text-to-SQL, structured parsing), the distinction between "can sometimes succeed" (pass@k) and "reliably succeeds every time" (PSR) is crucial for production systems. The paper makes a strong case that stability analysis should become standard practice.

Red flags:

  1. Contamination risk: The recency-based selection reduces but does not eliminate contamination. Recently published contest problems are often discussed online within days, and the authors do not perform contamination detection (e.g., n-gram overlap, membership inference).
  2. o4-mini caveat: The o-series API does not expose temperature/top-p controls. If o4-mini's effective temperature is near zero, its high PSR may reflect near-deterministic decoding rather than genuine task reliability—limiting direct comparability.
  3. Small benchmark: N = 100 problems is modest. The authors acknowledge this limits power for detecting small prompt effects.
  4. Binary outcome compression: Collapsing diverse failure modes (Wrong Answer, TLE, Runtime Error) into a single pass/fail metric loses diagnostic granularity.
  5. No hyperparameter search: All models use fixed T = 0.3, top-p = 0.9. Different models may have different optimal decoding settings for stability.

Strengths

  • Practically motivated: Directly addresses the deployment-reliability gap that single-run benchmarks obscure.
  • Methodologically transparent: Full prompt templates, model snapshots, access dates, and statistical procedures are disclosed.
  • Intellectually honest: Authors consistently flag limitations (small N, contamination risk, underpowered reasoning-model comparison, o4-mininon-comparability) rather than overselling findings.
  • Well-visualized: The cross-model stability heatmap (Figure 2) and per-problem success distribution (Figure 3) effectively expose variability patterns hidden by aggregate metrics.
  • Broad model coverage: Spanning five provider families (including both flagship and lightweight models) strengthens generalizability claims.

Limitations

  • Benchmark size and scope: Only 100 Python LeetCode-style problems; no repository-level, multi-file, or real-world software engineering tasks.
  • Contamination uncertainty: Recency-based selection helps but cannot guarantee models haven't seen problem statements via post-release online discussions.
  • Narrow prompt comparison: DETAILED vs. MINIMAL is a low-contrast comparison; broader prompt-engineering strategies (chain-of-thought, self-repair, retrieval) are not tested.
  • No decoding hyperparameter tuning: Fixed T = 0.3 and top-p = 0.9 across all models; optimal stability settings may vary by model.
  • o4-mini non-comparability: Undisclosed internal decoding regime may artificially inflate its stability metrics.
  • Single-turn only: Does not evaluate multi-turn interaction, test-time selection, or self-repair loops that could improve stability in practice.
  • Binary grading: Deterministic pass/fail verdicts do not capture partial correctness or failure-mode differences.

Relevance

对于关注LLM在确定性任务中实际部署可靠性的研究者而言，本文提出的PSR和AV指标具有重要的方法论参考价值——传统单次运行或pass@k评估可能严重高估模型在代码生成、Text-to-SQL等需要可重复正确性场景中的真实表现，尤其当系统处于中等准确率区间时，稳定性分析应成为标准评测流程的必要补充。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How do temperature and top-p tuning affect the accuracy–stability trade-off? The paper uses fixed decoding parameters; a per-model hyperparameter sweep could reveal whether stability gaps shrink at lower temperatures or whether accuracy collapses faster than stability improves.
  2. Does the accuracy–stability gap generalize to non-coding deterministic tasks? The authors explicitly call for replication on text-to-SQL or structured semantic parsing to test whether the mid-accuracy danger zone is universal.
  3. Can multi-turn self-repair or test-time selection close the PSR gap? The protocol is strictly single-turn; understanding whether retry-based recovery or consistency voting can convert unstable but capable systems into reliably correct ones would have direct deployment relevance.
  4. What is the relationship between failure mode and instability? The paper notes that Wrong Answer, TLE, and Runtime Error are pooled into binary outcomes. Disaggregating PSR by failure mode could reveal whether instability stems from algorithmic reasoning errors or implementation fragility.
  5. How does benchmark scale affect gap estimates? With N = 100, small prompt effects are underpowered. Would a 500-problem benchmark narrow confidence intervals and expose currently hidden prompt or model differences?
