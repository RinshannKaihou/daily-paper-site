STARFISH: faST Accuracy Recovery in pruned networks From Internal State Healing

arXiv: 2606.01126

Date: 2026-06-02

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01126

一句话总结： STARFISH 是一种通过将剪枝后网络的中间层表征与原始密集模型对齐来实现快速精度恢复的方法，仅需极少量无标签校准数据即可在超高稀疏度下显著超越现有恢复技术。
TL;DR: STARFISH is a highly efficient post-pruning recovery method that aligns the intermediate representations of a pruned network with those of the original dense model using a tiny unlabeled calibration set, achieving state-of-the-art accuracy recovery—especially under aggressive pruning—across diverse architectures and pruning patterns.

核心贡献

  • 提出了 STARFISH，一种基于中间层表征对齐的剪枝后精度恢复方法，独立于剪枝类型、剪枝方法或网络架构。
  • 通过在小规模校准集上优化表征对齐，实证表明随着稀疏度增加，该方法比直接基于输出的优化更有效。
  • 首次在极高稀疏度（0.85，即移除 85% 权重）下，在 ViT 和 MobileNet 等大型图像分类器上高效恢复 92% 的原始精度。
  • 在常见的 0.5 稀疏度下，对不同 ViT 架构的精度恢复比现有最优方法高出多达 31.5 个百分点，最高可达密集模型精度的 99.8% 保留率。
  • 仅需极少量无标签校准样本（≤9,000，仅占训练集的 0.7%），无需原始训练数据或标签，恢复过程可在数分钟到十几分钟内完成。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces STARFISH, an efficient recovery method based on intermediate-representation alignment, independent of pruning type, method, or network architecture.
  • Empirically demonstrates that optimizing representation alignment over a small calibration set becomes increasingly more effective than output-based optimization as sparsity increases.
  • First to efficiently restore 92% of original accuracy at very high sparsity (0.85) on large image classifiers such as ViT and MobileNet.
  • At the common 0.5-sparsity level across various ViT-based architectures, outperforms competing methods by up to 31.5 percentage points in accuracy recovery, reaching up to 99.8% retention of dense model accuracy.
  • Requires only a tiny unlabeled calibration set (≤9,000 images, 0.7% of training set), needs neither original training data nor labels, and completes recovery in minutes.

Methodology

STARFISH treats pruning recovery as internal-state healing rather than merely matching final outputs. The core idea is to encourage the pruned model to follow the dense model's computation throughout the network by aligning their intermediate representations.

Algorithm:

  1. Cache dense representations: Run the dense (unpruned) model on a small unlabeled calibration set \( S_{\text{cal}} = \{x_1, \dots, x_n\} \) and cache the vectorized output of each block \( \ell \in [L] \), denoted \( r_{\ell}^x \).
  2. Optimize alignment: Train the pruned model \( f_{\theta \odot m} \) to minimize the alignment loss over the calibration set:

\[

\mathcal{L}_{\text{align}}(\theta \odot m, x) = \frac{1}{L} \sum_{\ell \in [L]} \left( 1 - \frac{f_{\theta \odot m}^{\ell}(x)^\top r_{\ell}^x}{\|f_{\theta \odot m}^{\ell}(x)\| \|r_{\ell}^x\|} \right)

\]

This is essentially an average of cosine-similarity losses across all network blocks, pushing the pruned model's hidden states to be directionally aligned with the dense model's states.

Key properties:

  • Pruning-agnostic: Adjusts remaining weights after pruning damage has occurred, so it works with any pruning method, sparsity pattern (unstructured, structured, N:M semi-structured), pruning scope (MLP, QK attention, convolutional), and architecture (ViT, CNN).
  • Data-light: Uses ≤9,000 unlabeled calibration images (can be drawn from the test split), requiring no supervised signal.
  • Fast: A single dense forward pass to cache targets, followed by ≤10 epochs of training on the pruned model. For example, recovering a 0.8-sparse DeiT3-H takes ~16 minutes on a single A10 GPU with 1,000 samples and 5 epochs.

Theoretical justification: The authors prove that the distance between the final hidden representations (inputs to the classification head) provides an upper bound on the KL divergence of the output predictive distributions. This formally supports why internal-state alignment is a good proxy for accuracy recovery.

Results & Highlights

High-sparsity regime (0.7–0.85):

  • DeiT3-H @ 0.85 sparsity: STARFISH recovers to 78.24% top-1 accuracy (92.08% retention), a +72.36 point improvement over the unrecovered pruned model.
  • DeiT-B @ 0.75 sparsity: Recovers 82% of dense accuracy, while competing methods reach only ~40%.
  • DeiT-B @ 0.8 sparsity: Achieves 73.26% top-1 accuracy, outperforming KL-distillation by 13.15 points and cross-entropy fine-tuning by 16.42 points.

Moderate-sparsity regime (0.5):

  • DeiT-B (MLP+QK) @ 0.5 sparsity: STARFISH reaches 81.34% top-1 (99.52% retention).
  • DeiT-T (MLP+QK) @ 0.5 sparsity: Recovers to 81.95% retention, a 31.54 percentage-point improvement over CORP (50.41% retention).
  • Consistently outperforms CORP (structured pruning) and SNOWS (semi-structured 2:4 sparsity) across all tested ViT variants and pruning scopes.

MobileNetV1 (unstructured pruning):

  • STARFISH consistently exceeds MP, WoodFisher, CBS, CHITA, FALCON, and SNOWS across all sparsity levels, with the margin widening at high sparsity.

Efficiency:

  • DeiT3-H @ 0.8 sparsity: 16 min on single A10 GPU (1,000 samples, 5 epochs).
  • Even with only 500 calibration samples (~0.4% of ImageNet training set): 8 minutes, still outperforming competitors at lower sparsity.

Critical Assessment

Novelty & Rigor:

The core concept of representation alignment is not new—it has been extensively used in knowledge distillation, self-supervised learning, and unsupervised learning. However, STARFISH's novel contribution lies in applying this objective specifically to the post-pruning recovery setting and empirically demonstrating its outsized effectiveness for high-sparsity pruning, where output-based methods fail dramatically. The authors provide a solid theoretical result bounding output KL divergence by representation distance, grounding their approach. The empirical evaluation is thorough, spanning multiple architectures (DeiT-S/B, DeiT3-H, MobileNetV1), sparsity patterns (unstructured, structured, 2:4 semi-structured), pruning scopes (MLP, QK, QKV+Out, full), and sparsity levels (0.5 to 0.85).

Reproducibility:

The method is simple to implement (cosine alignment across blocks), hyperparameters are modest (≤9,000 samples, ≤10 epochs), and the authors release open-source code. The calibration set is drawn from ImageNet's unlabeled test split, making reproduction straightforward without needing the full training pipeline.

Potential Concerns:

  • The evaluation is limited to ImageNet-1K image classification. No results are provided for language models, multimodal models, or other domains.
  • The calibration set size and epoch count (n and T) may require tuning for new architectures or datasets; the paper does not analyze sensitivity to these hyperparameters in depth.
  • While STARFISH outperforms competitors at high sparsity, the absolute accuracy still degrades substantially at 0.85 sparsity (e.g., ~78% for DeiT3-H vs. ~85% dense), so aggressive pruning remains costly.
  • The method does not adaptively weight different layers; a uniform average over all blocks may underweight or overweight certain critical layers.

Strengths

  • Extremely efficient: Requires only a tiny unlabeled calibration set and a handful of epochs, making it practical for resource-constrained settings.
  • Fully modular: Completely agnostic to pruning method, sparsity pattern, pruning scope, and architecture—can be dropped into any existing pruning pipeline.
  • No training data needed: Works without access to the original training set or labels, which is crucial for proprietary models.
  • Strong theoretical grounding: The representation-to-KL bound provides formal justification for why alignment works.
  • Dramatic empirical gains: The improvements are especially pronounced at high sparsity, where recovery is most needed and competing methods struggle most.
  • Open source: Code is publicly available, facilitating adoption and reproduction.

Limitations

  • Domain-limited evaluation: All experiments are on ImageNet-1K vision models; generalization to NLP, multimodal, or other domains is not demonstrated.
  • Uniform block weighting: The loss averages equally over all blocks; adaptive weighting based on per-layer pruning damage could potentially improve results further.
  • Hyperparameter sensitivity: Calibration set size and number of epochs are fixed heuristically; sensitivity analysis is limited.
  • Not a pruning method: STARFISH is purely a recovery method—it does not select which weights to prune. Its effectiveness still depends on the quality of the pruning mask.
  • Accuracy ceiling at extreme sparsity: Even with STARFISH, very aggressive pruning (e.g., 0.85) still incurs non-trivial accuracy loss compared to the dense model.

Relevance

对神经网络剪枝后的精度恢复问题提出了高效且通用的解决方案，尤其在高稀疏度场景下优势显著，对边缘设备部署、模型压缩和降低推理成本具有重要实际意义。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does STARFISH perform on large language models (LLMs) and multimodal transformers? Can the same representation alignment principle scale to models with billions of parameters and different modalities?
  2. Would adaptive per-block weighting—allocating more optimization effort to layers most damaged by pruning—further improve recovery, and can the representation mismatch itself be used as a pruning criterion?
  3. What are the theoretical limits of representation alignment for pruning recovery? Can we characterize when and why it breaks down at extreme sparsity?
  4. Can STARFISH be incorporated directly into training by encouraging a dense model and its induced sparse counterpart to maintain aligned internal representations throughout training, thereby producing inherently more recoverable models?
