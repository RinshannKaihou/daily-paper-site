> **一句话总结：** MOSS-Audio 是一个统一音频语言模型，通过 DeepStack 跨层特征注入和显式时间标记，在单一框架内实现语音、环境声和音乐的联合理解，并达到开源模型中领先的音频理解、语音描述、语音识别和时间戳转录性能。

> **TL;DR:** MOSS-Audio is a unified audio-language model that jointly understands speech, environmental sound, and music through DeepStack cross-layer feature injection and explicit time markers, achieving state-of-the-art open-source performance on general audio understanding, speech captioning, ASR, and timestamped ASR within a single autoregressive framework.

## 核心贡献

- **统一音频理解框架**：MOSS-Audio 在单一自回归文本生成框架内支持语音识别（ASR）、音频描述、语音描述、带时间戳的转录、时间感知问答和音频推理六大任务。
- **DeepStack 跨层特征注入**：不同于仅使用编码器最终层表示的做法，MOSS-Audio 将音频编码器多层特征注入语言模型解码器，保留从底层时频模式到高层语义的多粒度声学信息。
- **显式时间标记机制**：在音频特征序列中每隔 2 秒插入时间标记，使模型直接学习绝对时间信息，支持时间戳转录和时序推理，而不仅依赖相对位置推断。
- **事件保留的数据管线**：设计了基于事件边界的音频分割策略，避免固定窗口切割破坏完整声学事件；通过分支式标注（语音/音乐/通用音频）和统一描述融合，构建数百万小时规模的预训练数据。
- **双版本模型家族**：发布 4B 和 8B 两种规模的 Instruct（指令遵循）和 Thinking（推理增强）版本，在通用音频理解、语音描述、ASR 和时间戳 ASR 上均达到开源最优。

## Key Contributions

- **Unified Audio Understanding Framework**: MOSS-Audio supports ASR, audio captioning, speech captioning, timestamped transcription, time-aware QA, and audio-grounded reasoning within a single autoregressive text-generation framework.
- **DeepStack Cross-Layer Feature Injection**: Instead of relying solely on the encoder's final-layer representation, MOSS-Audio injects multi-depth encoder features into the decoder, preserving multi-granularity acoustic evidence from low-level time-frequency patterns to high-level semantics.
- **Explicit Time Markers**: Numerical time markers are interleaved into the audio-token stream every 2 seconds, enabling the model to learn absolute temporal grounding directly, supporting timestamped transcription and temporal reasoning beyond latent positional inference.
- **Event-Preserving Data Pipeline**: A segmentation strategy that cuts at natural event boundaries (not fixed windows) is combined with branch-specific annotation (speech/music/general audio) and unified caption merging, yielding a pretraining corpus at the scale of millions of hours.
- **Dual-Variant Model Family**: 4B and 8B Instruct (instruction-following) and Thinking (reasoning-enhanced) variants are released, achieving state-of-the-art open-source results on general audio understanding, speech captioning, ASR, and timestamped ASR.

## Methodology

**Architecture.** MOSS-Audio follows an encoder–adapter–decoder architecture. A dedicated ~0.6B-parameter audio encoder is trained from scratch on diverse audio data. It processes 128-channel log-mel spectrograms through three stride-2 Conv2D layers (8× temporal downsampling) to produce a compact 12.5 Hz token rate. A 32-layer Transformer backbone with hidden dimension 1280 and sliding-window attention (max 100 frames / 8 seconds) handles long-context inputs efficiently. The decoder is a large language model that generates autoregressive text conditioned on audio and instructions.

**DeepStack Cross-Layer Feature Injection.** A primary GatedMLP adapter projects the final encoder output into the decoder space. A parallel DeepStack pathway extracts intermediate encoder hidden states, aggregates them through a separate merge adapter, and injects the resulting cross-layer features into selected early decoder layers. This gives the decoder access to low-level acoustic cues (prosody, transient events, local spectral structure) alongside high-level semantic content, mitigating the information bottleneck of a single final-layer representation.

**Time-Aware Modeling.** Explicit elapsed-time markers are inserted into the audio-conditioned input sequence every 25 audio features (corresponding to 2 seconds). These markers are embedded and processed jointly with adapted audio representations, providing absolute temporal anchors for timestamp generation, event localization, and time-aware audio reasoning.

**Data Pipeline.** Raw audio is segmented at coherent event boundaries using a frame-level sound event detection model (BEATs backbone under PretrainedSED). Detected events are merged with gap tolerance for vocal/speech events, and non-speech events >60s are excluded from boundary computation. Segments are classified into nine coarse categories and routed to branch-specific annotators: ASR (ensemble of Qwen3-Omni-Instruct, FunASR Nano, Qwen3-ASR with cross-model WER filtering and LID validation), speech caption (DiariZen + bootstrapped multi-speaker caption model), general audio caption (PretrainedSED + Detect Any Sound for event metadata + Qwen3-Omni for global semantics + Qwen3-Omni-30B-Thinking for fusion-based dense captions, with LLM judge verification), and music caption (holistic ALM caption + MIR pipeline with Chordino/BeatNet/madmom/Essentia/JukeMIR/SongFormer for chords, tempo, key, structure, instruments, and lyrics).

A **Caption Merge & Refine** stage consolidates branch outputs via canonical normalization, prior-driven routing (Router-R1), and constrained two-stage LLM synthesis into unified caption targets for pretraining.

**Training.** Pretraining (~1.2T tokens) uses a three-objective mixture: ASR-related tasks (30%, including ordinary ASR, word-level timestamp ASR, sentence-level timestamp ASR), audio captioning (40%, merged captions), and text-only language modeling (30%). Square-root dataset mixing is used. Pretraining has two stages: Stage 1 opens the audio-prefix pathway (adapter + DeepStack modules trained, encoder/decoder stable), Stage 2 trains end-to-end with all objectives.

Post-training produces two variants via three phases: (1) Supervised Fine-Tuning on audio QA, captioning, ASR, timestamp ASR, and self-identity data; (2) Reasoning Cold Start with audio-centered reasoning data and text-only reasoning data; (3) DAPO-based Reinforcement Learning with dynamic filtering (discarding zero-std rollout groups), asymmetric clipping (ε=0.2, ε_high=0.28), and token-level importance sampling correction (TIS threshold=2.0).

## Results & Highlights

**General Audio Understanding.** Evaluated on MMAU, MMAU-Pro, MMAR, and MMSU, MOSS-Audio-8B-Thinking achieves the highest open-source average score of **71.08**, outperforming larger 30B-scale models such as Qwen3-Omni-30B-A3B-Instruct and Step-Audio-R1/R1.1 (33B). MOSS-Audio-4B-Thinking (67.91) already surpasses several 8B-scale baselines, demonstrating strong scaling efficiency. Thinking variants consistently outperform their Instruct counterparts on reasoning-heavy benchmarks.

**Speech Captioning.** On a dedicated 2,000-sample benchmark with 13 judged dimensions (gender, age, accent, pitch, volume, speed, texture, clarity, fluency, emotion, tone, personality, summary), MOSS-Audio-8B-Instruct achieves the **best overall score of 3.7252**, even outperforming proprietary systems such as Gemini-3.1-Pro. MOSS-Audio-4B-Instruct follows closely at 3.7105, showing exceptional fine-grained speech description capability.

**ASR.** Across 12 evaluation dimensions (health-condition speech, dialect, singing, non-speech vocalizations, code-switching, clean/noisy environments, whisper, far-field/near-field, multi-speaker, age, semantic content), MOSS-Audio-8B-Instruct achieves the **best overall CER of 11.30%**, followed by MOSS-Audio-4B-Instruct at 11.58%. This confirms strong transcription accuracy is preserved alongside broad audio-language capabilities.

**Timestamp ASR.** Measured by Accumulated Average Shift (AAS) on AISHELL-1 (Chinese) and LibriSpeech (English), MOSS-Audio-8B-Instruct achieves the **strongest temporal alignment** among compared models, with AAS of 35.77 ms on AISHELL-1 and 131.61 ms on LibriSpeech. This validates that explicit time-aware pretraining and timestamp ASR supervision improve temporal alignment beyond mere transcription fluency.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** The paper combines several known techniques (encoder–adapter–decoder architecture, DeepStack-style injection from vision-language models, explicit time markers, multi-stage post-training with DAPO RL) into a cohesive audio-language system. While individual components are not entirely new, their integration for unified audio understanding—particularly the event-preserving data pipeline and the dual Instruct/Thinking variants—is a meaningful contribution.

**Rigor.** The empirical evaluation is thorough, covering four benchmark suites for general audio understanding, a custom speech-captioning benchmark with human-annotated references and judge-based scoring, 12-dimensional ASR evaluation, and timestamp ASR with AAS metrics. The training pipeline is described in sufficient detail to support reproduction, including exact RL hyperparameters and dynamic filtering behavior.

**Reproducibility.** The model weights and inference code are publicly released across multiple platforms (Hugging Face, ModelScope), and the demo is accessible online. However, the training data pipeline relies heavily on proprietary/internal models (e.g., the speech-caption annotator, Router-R1, and internal ASR ensemble) and large-scale unlabeled audio sources that are not fully specified, making exact reproduction of the data curation stage difficult.

**Significance.** MOSS-Audio positions itself as an understanding-centric foundation for voice agents, demonstrating that a single model family can excel at both precise speech tasks and open-domain acoustic reasoning. The strong performance of the 4B variants relative to much larger competitors suggests efficient architecture and data design.

**Red Flags.** (1) The paper is a technical report without peer review, so claims should be interpreted cautiously. (2) Some benchmark comparisons mix proprietary and open-source models with undisclosed training details, making direct comparisons less controlled. (3) The reasoning cold-start and DAPO stages use internally constructed reasoning data whose curation criteria are described only at a high level.

## Strengths

- **Strong empirical results across diverse tasks**: Best open-source scores on general audio understanding, speech captioning, ASR, and timestamped ASR.
- **Efficient scaling**: 4B variants outperform or match much larger models (7B–30B), suggesting good parameter efficiency.
- **Architectural clarity**: The DeepStack injection and time-marker designs are well-motivated and clearly described.
- **Comprehensive data engineering**: The event-preserving segmentation, branch-specific annotation, and caption-merge pipeline represent significant systems effort.
- **Dual variant design**: Separating Instruct (task execution) and Thinking (reasoning) variants is a practical contribution that lets users choose capability vs. efficiency.
- **Public release**: Models, demos, and checkpoints are openly available, facilitating downstream research.

## Limitations

- **No audio generation capability**: MOSS-Audio is understanding-only; speech synthesis or audio generation must be handled by downstream tools.
- **Limited zero-shot / few-shot evaluation**: The paper focuses on benchmark scores rather than in-context learning or few-shot adaptation behavior.
- **Data pipeline opacity**: Key annotation components (speech-caption annotator, Router-R1, internal ASR ensemble) are not released, making full data reproduction impossible.
- **English and Chinese focus**: While the ASR and timestamp evaluation covers Chinese and English, the broader multilingual and cross-lingual capabilities are not extensively benchmarked.
- **DAPO training cost**: The dynamic filtering and extra over-sampling rounds (growing to 14 per step) imply significant compute overhead during RL training.
- **Technical report format**: As an unreviewed arXiv technical report, some experimental design choices and ablations are less thoroughly justified than in a peer-reviewed publication.

## Relevance

MOSS-Audio 为构建下一代语音智能体提供了一个强大的开源音频理解基座，其统一架构和双版本设计使其既能胜任精确的语音识别与转录任务，也能处理复杂的时序推理与开放域音频问答。对于关注多模态大模型、语音交互系统和音频表示学习的研究者而言，这是一项值得深入跟踪的工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does DeepStack cross-layer injection compare quantitatively against simple early/late feature fusion or adapter-based aggregation? An ablation study on layer selection and merge adapter design would clarify its marginal contribution.
2. Can the explicit time-marker strategy transfer to non-speech temporal tasks such as music structure analysis or environmental sound event localization with precise onset/offset prediction?
3. What is the impact of the event-preserving segmentation strategy on downstream performance? A controlled comparison against fixed-window segmentation would validate the data-engineering investment.
4. How well do the Thinking variants generalize to entirely novel audio reasoning tasks not seen during cold-start or RL training? Their strong benchmark scores may reflect dataset familiarity rather than robust reasoning transfer.
5. Would scaling the audio encoder beyond 0.6B parameters (e.g., to 1B or 2B) yield further gains, or does the bottleneck lie primarily in the language decoder and data quality?
