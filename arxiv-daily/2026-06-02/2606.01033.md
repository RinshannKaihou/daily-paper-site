> **一句话总结：** TriLens 通过在 transformer 的每一层读取 MHSA、FFN 和残差流三路输出的 logit-lens 熵，构建一个仅 3L 维的紧凑轨迹特征，在单次前向传播中实现了对幻觉生成的高精度白盒检测。

> **TL;DR:** TriLens detects LLM hallucinations in a single forward pass by tracking per-layer Shannon entropy of logit-lens distributions across three internal pathways (MHSA, FFN, and residual stream), yielding a compact 3L-dimensional feature that outperforms prior white-box baselines on all 12 model–dataset combinations.

## 核心贡献

- **TriLens 特征设计**：在每一层提取多头自注意力（MHSA）输出、前馈网络（FFN）输出和残差流（residual stream）三个位置的状态，通过模型自身的 logit lens 投影到词表空间后计算香农熵，拼接成仅 3L 维的轨迹向量。
- **全面超越现有白盒基线**：在三类指令微调模型（Qwen2.5-7B、Llama-3-8B、Gemma-2-9B）和四个 QA 基准（HaluEval、SQuAD2、HotpotQA、TriviaQA）的全部 12 个实验格子上，TriLens 均优于当前最强白盒基线 ICR Probe，平均提升 +12.1 AUROC，并在 11/12 格子上取得最高性能。
- **三路互补性得到验证**：消融实验表明，MHSA 熵、FFN 熵和残差流熵三者提供互补的判别信号，任意两者组合均优于单一残差流熵，而三路联合达到最佳。
- **与跨层对比信号的直接比较**：与 DoLa 风格的跨层 KL/JSD 对比特征进行头对头比较，发现逐层熵已捕获了绝大部分有用信号，加入跨层对比信息几乎无额外增益。
- **跨数据集迁移能力突出**：在训练集与测试集来自不同基准的迁移设置下，TriLens 在所有 12 个非对角格子和全部 16 个格子上均为最强方法，非对角平均 AUROC 达 0.848，显著优于 ICR Probe（0.738）和 SAPLMA（0.678）。

## Key Contributions

- **TriLens feature design**: At every layer, it extracts the multi-head self-attention (MHSA) output, feed-forward network (FFN) output, and residual stream; projects each through the model's own logit lens; computes Shannon entropy; and concatenates into a compact 3L-dimensional trajectory vector.
- **Consistent gains over white-box baselines**: Across three instruction-tuned LLMs (Qwen2.5-7B, Llama-3-8B, Gemma-2-9B) and four QA benchmarks (HaluEval, SQuAD2, HotpotQA, TriviaQA), TriLens improves over the prior state-of-the-art ICR Probe on all 12 model–dataset cells, averaging +12.1 AUROC and achieving the highest AUROC in 11 of 12 cells.
- **Complementary three-pathway signal**: Ablations demonstrate that MHSA entropy, FFN entropy, and residual-stream entropy each contribute non-redundant predictive evidence; all two-feature branches improve over the single residual-stream entropy, and the full three-entropy feature is best in every cell.
- **Head-to-head comparison with cross-layer contrast**: A direct comparison against DoLa-style cross-layer JSD contrast features shows that per-layer entropy already captures most of the useful signal, with the union offering negligible additional gain.
- **Strong cross-dataset transfer**: In a train-on-one-benchmark, test-on-another diagnostic, TriLens is the strongest method on all 12 off-diagonal cells and all 16 cells overall, with an off-diagonal mean AUROC of 0.848 compared with 0.738 for ICR Probe.

## Methodology

**Feature extraction.** For an LLM with L decoder layers and vocabulary V, at layer ℓ and token position i, the residual stream updates as:

xℓi = xℓ−1i + MHSAℓ(Nℓ(xℓ−1i)) + FFNℓ(Nℓ(xℓ−1i + aℓi))

where aℓi and mℓi denote the MHSA and FFN writes, respectively. TriLens projects each of {xℓi, aℓi, mℓi} through the model's own unembedding matrix WU and final normalization layer (the standard logit lens), converts the resulting vocabulary distribution to Shannon entropy, and concatenates across all L layers into a fixed-length feature vector f ∈ R^(3L).

**Mechanistic motivation.** The design is grounded in three views of transformer dynamics: (1) dual-pathway decomposition — MHSA routes contextual information while FFN acts as a parametric key-value memory, so they can diverge during hallucination; (2) superposition and commitment — Shannon entropy compactly tracks how concentrated or diffuse the lens distribution is without assuming vocabulary structure; and (3) iterative refinement trajectories — for correct answers, uncertainty typically decreases monotonically with depth, whereas hallucinated answers often show non-monotone trajectories where uncertainty re-enters.

**Probes.** The authors evaluate a linear probe (L2-regularized logistic regression) and an MLP probe (3L → 128 → 64 → 32 → 1 with LeakyReLU, BatchNorm, and Dropout 0.3), using the same architecture as the prior ICR Probe baseline for fair comparison.

**Experimental setup.** Models: Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, Meta-Llama-3-8B-Instruct, Gemma-2-9B-it. Benchmarks: HaluEval-QA, SQuAD2.0, HotpotQA, TriviaQA. Each benchmark uses 10,000 instances with an 80/20 group-preserving train/test split, averaged over five random seeds. All activations are extracted from a single forward pass.

## Results & Highlights

**Main results (Table 1).** TriLens (MLP) achieves test AUROCs ranging from 0.8103 (Qwen2.5-7B on TriviaQA) to 0.9433 (Gemma-2-9B on HotpotQA). The linear probe variant also outperforms ICR Probe on all 12 cells, indicating the feature itself is highly separable even without a learned transformation. Capacity controls (repeating the residual-stream entropy three times to match input dimension) perform nearly identically to Hx alone, confirming that the gain is not due to higher input dimensionality.

**Feature ablation (Figure 4, Appendix E).** Adding either Ha or Hm to Hx improves on all 12 cells, and the full three-entropy feature improves further in every cell. The relative strength of Ha vs. Hm is architecture-dependent: Ha + Hx is stronger on Gemma-2, while Hm + Hx is stronger on Llama-3 and part of Qwen2.5. An intra-layer disagreement term (JSD between lens(aℓ) and lens(mℓ)) adds at most +0.006 AUROC per cell, suggesting little value beyond the entropy trajectories themselves.

**Per-layer analysis (Figure 3).** The peak-discriminative depth varies substantially across model–dataset pairs. Some open-book tasks peak in early or middle layers, while closed-book TriviaQA and several HotpotQA cells peak only near the final layer, consistent with prior mechanistic findings that different models integrate knowledge at different depths.

**Cross-dataset transfer (Figure 5).** A shared probe trained on the union of all four benchmark training splits still exceeds per-dataset ICR Probe on all 12 cells (+10.7 AUROC average). Cross-dataset heatmaps show TriLens is strongest on all off-diagonal cells, with off-diagonal mean AUROC of 0.848 vs. 0.738 (ICR Probe) and 0.678 (SAPLMA).

**Comparison with DoLa-style cross-layer contrast (Table 2).** Repurposing DoLa's cross-layer JSD as a detection feature, TriLens's per-layer entropy consistently outperforms it on every dataset (gaps +0.02 to +0.15 AUROC, average ~+0.07). Concatenating both features changes AUROC by less than +0.001 on average, indicating cross-layer contrast contributes little beyond per-layer entropy.

The experimental protocol is carefully matched: identical models, datasets, splits, seeds, and probe architectures across all compared methods. The inclusion of linear-probe results, dimension-matched controls, and cross-dataset transfer diagnostics strengthens causal claims about the feature itself. The code is publicly available at https://tosakaucw.github.io/TriLens/. The benchmark coverage spans both context-grounded (HaluEval, SQuAD2, HotpotQA) and closed-book (TriviaQA) settings, suggesting broad applicability within the QA domain.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. While logit-lens entropy and white-box hallucination detection are not new individually, the specific contribution of tracking per-layer entropy across three functional pathways (MHSA, FFN, residual) as a compact, low-dimensional detection signal is novel. The systematic ablations and head-to-head comparison with DoLa-style cross-layer contrast provide clear differentiation from prior work.

**Rigor:** Strong. The paper controls for probe architecture, input dimensionality, and training recipe. Linear-probe separability, repeated-feature baselines, and stricter MLP dimension reductions all support the claim that the gain comes from the feature quality rather than capacity. The 5-seed averaging and group-preserving splits are appropriate.

**Reproducibility:** Good. The method uses only standard components (logit lens, Shannon entropy, simple probes) and the authors provide code. The evaluation protocol is described in detail, and the benchmark preprocessing pipeline is shared across all methods. However, some hyperparameter details are deferred to appendices not fully visible in the extraction.

**Significance:** Moderate-to-high for the LLM reliability community. The compactness of the feature (3L scalars vs. high-dimensional hidden states) makes it attractive for deployment, and the single-forward-pass requirement is practical. However, the restriction to open-weight models limits applicability to proprietary APIs.

**Potential concerns:**
- The study is limited to ~7–9B parameter instruction-tuned models; whether the signal scales to larger models or different architectures (e.g., MoE) is not tested.
- All benchmarks are QA-based; the signal's robustness on open-ended generation, coding, or reasoning tasks is unknown.
- The claim that Ha and Hm are "legitimate objects for the model's own final readout" is acknowledged by the authors as empirical and diagnostic, but some readers may question whether projecting non-residual module outputs through the unembedding is mechanistically well-justified.

## Strengths

- **Extreme compactness:** A 3L-dimensional feature (e.g., 84–126 scalars) is ~30× smaller than raw hidden-state features used by SAPLMA and descendants, making it storage- and compute-efficient.
- **Single forward pass:** No multiple sampling, no external references, no gradient computation — directly applicable at inference time.
- **Strong and consistent empirical gains:** Outperforms the prior SOTA on every single tested configuration, with large margins (+4.2 to +16.0 AUROC per cell).
- **Complementary signal validated:** The three-pathway design is not just additive; ablations show genuine complementarity, with architecture-dependent relative importance.
- **Good transferability:** Cross-dataset and shared-probe settings demonstrate the feature captures generalizable signatures of hallucination rather than dataset-specific artifacts.
- **Mechanistic grounding:** The authors explicitly connect their design to the dual-pathway (MHSA vs. FFN) view of transformers, making the method interpretable beyond pure pattern matching.

## Limitations

- **White-box only:** TriLens requires access to internal activations (MHSA output, FFN output, residual stream), so it cannot be applied to closed APIs like GPT-4 or Claude without provider support.
- **Detection, not mitigation:** The paper focuses on identifying hallucinated outputs rather than preventing them during generation or triggering retrieval/self-correction.
- **Model scale and scope:** Evaluated only on ~7–9B parameter models; generalization to larger scales (e.g., 70B+) or mixture-of-experts architectures is untested.
- **Task scope:** All experiments use question-answering benchmarks; performance on open-ended generation, summarization, code synthesis, or multi-step reasoning remains unknown.
- **Supervised requirement:** Like other trainable white-box methods, TriLens needs labeled hallucination data to train the probe, though the cross-dataset results suggest moderate robustness to distribution shift.
- **No real-time latency analysis:** While the feature is compact, the paper does not report wall-clock inference overhead relative to baseline methods.

## Relevance

本文提出了一种轻量、可解释且效果卓越的幻觉检测方法，对提升大语言模型在知识敏感场景下的可靠性具有直接参考价值；其仅依赖单次前向传播和极低维特征的特性，使其在实际部署中具有很高的工程可行性。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does TriLens perform on much larger models (e.g., 70B+ parameters) and on mixture-of-experts (MoE) architectures where FFN pathways are sparsely activated?
2. Can the per-layer entropy trajectories be used not just for detection but for active mitigation — for example, by early-stopping generation when entropy divergence exceeds a threshold, or by routing uncertain tokens to retrieval augmentation?
3. Does the TriLens signal generalize beyond QA to open-ended generation tasks such as long-form summarization, creative writing, or code generation, where the definition of "hallucination" is less binary?
4. Can the architecture-specific differences in peak-discriminative depth (Figure 3) be exploited for model-specific probe calibration or layer-pruning strategies that preserve detection quality?
5. Is it possible to distill the TriLens signal into a lightweight learned surrogate that approximates the 3L-entropy feature from only the final-layer output, enabling black-box approximation of this white-box detector?
