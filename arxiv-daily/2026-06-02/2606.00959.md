> **一句话总结：** 本文将信息论中的部分信息分解（PID）引入多模态大语言模型，首次在决策层面量化视觉、文本和音频输入的独特、冗余与协同贡献，揭示了不同任务和模型家族中稳定的模态使用模式，并发现全模态模型存在视觉主导的感觉协同瓶颈。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces Partial Information Decomposition (PID) as a decision-level framework to disentangle unique, redundant, and synergistic modality contributions in multimodal large language models. It reveals recurring modality-use profiles across benchmarks and model families, exposes a visual-dominant sensory synergy bottleneck in omni-modal models, and demonstrates that PID-guided reweighting can improve multimodal reasoning performance.

## 核心贡献

- **决策级PID框架**：将多模态推理形式化为决策层面的信息分解问题，使用部分信息分解（PID）将互信息拆分为视觉独特信息（U_vis）、文本独特信息（U_txt）、冗余信息（R_vl）和协同信息（S_vl）四个非负分量。
- **Sensory PID扩展**：针对视频-音频-文本三模态系统，提出条件PID公式，将语言视为控制变量，分解感觉信息增益 I(Y; V, A | T)，避免完整多源PID的组合爆炸。
- **模态使用画像的发现**：在20个视觉-语言模型和6个基准测试上，PID揭示了两种 recurring 的模态使用模式——推理和 grounding 导向任务倾向于高协同，而专家和知识导向任务表现出更强的文本独特依赖。
- **感觉协同瓶颈**：在 omni-modal 模型上，Sensory PID 揭示即使在对融合要求高的音频-视觉任务上，模型决策仍由单模态信息（尤其是视觉）主导，协同信息占比很小。
- **PID引导重加权**：利用BATCH估计器产生的局部诊断分数，构建样本重加权策略，在LoRA微调中提升多模态推理和grounding性能，同时提高协同信息占比。

## Key Contributions

- **Decision-Level PID Framework**: Formulates multimodal reasoning as a decision-level information decomposition problem, using Partial Information Decomposition (PID) to separate mutual information into four non-negative components: visual-unique (U_vis), text-unique (U_txt), redundant (R_vl), and synergistic (S_vl) information.
- **Sensory PID for Omni-Modal Models**: Introduces a conditional PID formulation for tri-modal systems that treats language as a control variable, decomposing sensory information gain I(Y; V, A | T) into video-unique, audio-unique, redundant, and synergistic terms without combinatorial explosion.
- **Discovery of Modality-Use Profiles**: Across 20 vision-language models and 6 benchmarks, PID reveals two recurring modality-use patterns: reasoning and grounding-oriented tasks tend to elicit synergy-dominant profiles, while expert and knowledge-oriented tasks exhibit stronger text-unique reliance.
- **Sensory Synergy Bottleneck**: In omni-modal models, Sensory PID reveals that even on theoretically fusion-dependent audio-visual tasks, decisions remain dominated by unimodal information (especially visual), with synergy remaining comparatively small.
- **PID-Guided Reweighting**: Uses local diagnostic scores from the BATCH estimator to construct a sample reweighting policy for LoRA fine-tuning, improving multimodal reasoning and grounding performance while shifting PID profiles toward higher synergy.

## Methodology

The paper proposes a novel analytical framework based on Partial Information Decomposition (PID) to understand how multimodal language models use different input modalities at the decision level.

**Bi-Modal PID for Vision-Language Models**: For a model prediction Y over candidate set C, with vision source X_v and text source X_t, the framework constructs an empirical decision distribution P(x_v, x_t, y) = P_D(x_v, x_t) · p_θ(y | x_v, x_t). PID partitions the joint mutual information I(Y; X_v, X_t) into four components: U_vis (visual-unique), U_txt (text-unique), R_vl (redundant), and S_vl (synergistic). Synergy is defined as the gap between true joint MI and the minimum achievable under distributions preserving source-target marginals. The BATCH estimator (Liang et al., 2023) is used to approximate the optimal coupling via Sinkhorn normalization, making PID estimation tractable for high-dimensional continuous representations.

**Calibrated Embedding Masking**: To obtain unimodal conditionals p_θ(y | x_v) and p_θ(y | x_t) without retraining, the authors perform embedding-space masking: replacing the masked modality's projected token embeddings with calibrated Gaussian noise N(μ_m', diag(σ_m'^2)), where moments are estimated from a profiling set. Three stabilization mechanisms are employed: valid-option renormalization, confidence gating (threshold τ = 0.3), and soft aggregation of the marginal label distribution.

**Sensory PID for Omni-Modal Models**: For video V, audio A, and text T, the authors treat T as a conditioning variable and decompose the conditional sensory information gain I(Y; V, A | T) = U_vis + U_aud + R_sens + S_av. This formulation preserves the functional distinction between task specification (language) and sensory evidence (video/audio).

**Layer-Wise Analysis**: The framework traces PID components across transformer depth to reveal mechanistic dynamics of multimodal integration, showing a three-phase evolution: silent encoding (0–20%), unimodal evidence accumulation (20–80%), and late-stage fusion (80–100%).

**PID-Guided Reweighting**: Local additive contributions from BATCH are used to compute per-sample synergy ratio SR_i and shortcut score SC_i. Samples are selected via top-K ranking: under-synergized samples are upweighted (w_i = 3.0), language-shortcut samples are downweighted (w_i = 0.5), and others receive standard weight (w_i = 1.0). A GapScore combining fusion potential and current synergy usage further refines selection.

## Results & Highlights

**Benchmark-Level Regime Separation (§4.1.1)**: Without any a priori categorization, PID reveals two separable clusters across 6 vision-language benchmarks:
- **Synergy-driven**: MMBench, MMStar, and POPE exhibit S_vl > U_txt, indicating models rely on joint visual-textual integration.
- **Prior-dominant**: MMMU, PMC-VQA, and Reefknot show U_txt > S_vl with U_txt shares exceeding 60%, indicating stronger language-unique reliance.

Notably, POPE and Reefknot both target hallucination but fall into different regimes—POPE is grounding-sensitive while Reefknot exhibits text-unique reliance—demonstrating that surface-level benchmark labels can mask distinct modality-use profiles.

**Model-Family Interaction Profiles (§4.1.2)**: Two recurring strategies emerge across 20 models from 7 families:
- **Joint-modality-reliant**: Qwen (2.5-VL, 2-VL, 3-VL), InternVL3, and LLaVA-OneVision exhibit high S_vl on synergy-driven benchmarks with large vision-removal accuracy drops (Δ_vision).
- **Language-prior-reliant**: Gemma3 and Cambrian show U_txt dominance with small Δ_vision (< 2 pp on prior-dominant tasks).

Scaling preserves and amplifies these profiles rather than erasing them: Qwen3-VL maintains the same pattern from 2B to 32B, and Gemma3 remains persistently blue from 4B to 27B.

**Intervention-Predictive Validity (§4.1.3)**: Spearman correlation analysis shows:
- On synergy-driven tasks, S_vl strongly predicts both accuracy (ρ ≥ 0.718, p < 0.001) and vision-removal sensitivity (ρ ≥ 0.798, p < 0.001).
- On prior-dominant tasks, U_txt becomes the informative term, predicting Δ_vision in the opposite direction (ρ ≤ −0.502, p < 0.05).
- Total mutual information I(V,T;Y) tracks accuracy but not intervention response, confirming that PID decomposition captures modality-use signals beyond total information.

**Sensory Synergy Bottleneck in Omni-Modal Models (§4.2)**: On the AV-Fusion subset of MUSIC-AVQA (explicitly designed for joint audio-visual reasoning):
- Sensory synergy S_av remains a minor component across all evaluated omni-modal models (Qwen2.5-Omni, VITA-1.5).
- Unique visual information U_vis systematically exceeds unique auditory information U_aud.
- Functional validation via sample shuffling confirms asymmetric sensitivity: video shuffling induces larger performance drops than audio shuffling (ρ = 0.94 vs 0.88).

**Layer-Wise Dynamics (§4.3)**:
- **Vision-language models** exhibit a pronounced three-phase pattern: silent encoding → unimodal accumulation (U_txt rises rapidly) → late-stage fusion (S_vl spikes sharply in final 20% of layers).
- **Omni-modal models** show early visual saturation: U_vis rises sharply during the unimodal phase, fixing the decision boundary along a visual axis and constraining space for subsequent auditory or synergy evidence.
- **Instruction gating**: Replacing fusion-demanding queries with fusion-agnostic paraphrases substantially attenuates late-layer sensory synergy while leaving early/mid-layer trajectories unchanged, confirming language acts as a control signal regulating when cross-modal integration activates.

**PID-Guided Reweighting (§5)**: On Qwen2.5-VL-7B, LoRA-PID outperforms standard LoRA by:
- +2.3 on MMStar, +1.1 on MMBench, +1.3 on POPE
- Post-tuning S_vl increases from 1.20 to 1.36 bits (+0.16), with S_vl share rising from 67.5% to 73.9%
- Minor decreases on prior-dominant benchmarks (−0.5 on MMMU, −0.3 on PMC-VQA), consistent with deliberate de-emphasis of language-shortcut samples
- PID outperforms both accuracy-based difficulty mining (+1.8 on MMStar) and ablation-based selection (+1.3 on MMStar)

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: The paper makes a strong conceptual contribution by bringing Partial Information Decomposition—a well-established information-theoretic framework—to multimodal LLM analysis at the decision level. While prior work used representation similarity, attention analysis, and modality ablation, none provided a principled decomposition of unique vs. redundant vs. synergistic contributions. The extension to tri-modal systems via Sensory PID is particularly elegant, avoiding the combinatorial explosion of full multi-source PID while preserving functional interpretability.

**Rigor**: The methodology is technically sound. The use of the BATCH estimator with calibrated embedding masking provides a practical solution to the intractability of exact PID computation on high-dimensional continuous variables. The authors carefully validate their framework through multiple converging lines of evidence: cross-model profiling, cross-benchmark regime separation, intervention-predictive correlation analysis, layer-wise mechanistic tracing, and functional perturbation via sample shuffling. The statistical reporting (Spearman correlations with significance levels) is appropriate.

**Reproducibility**: The paper provides detailed implementation descriptions including prompt templates, confidence thresholds (τ = 0.3), stabilization mechanisms, and LoRA configurations. However, no code or data release is mentioned, and some hyperparameters (e.g., K=50 for score stabilization, K_s and K_g for top-K selection) are specified only at a high level. The reliance on the BATCH estimator—an external method—means reproducibility depends on correctly reimplementing or accessing this component.

**Significance**: The findings have both scientific and practical value. The discovery of recurring modality-use profiles challenges the assumption that benchmark labels alone determine multimodal behavior. The sensory synergy bottleneck in omni-modal models is an important diagnostic insight for the field. The proof-of-concept PID-guided reweighting opens a promising direction for training-time intervention.

**Red Flags / Concerns**:
1. The framework is limited to discrete decision spaces (primarily multiple-choice QA). The authors acknowledge this as a key limitation, but it substantially narrows applicability to open-ended generation tasks that represent much of real-world MLLM usage.
2. Calibrated embedding masking is an approximation for native unimodal inference. Moment-matched Gaussian noise may not perfectly preserve distributional statistics expected by the backbone, potentially biasing unique information estimates.
3. PID measures statistical dependence, not causal mechanisms. High synergy is consistent with cross-modal integration but does not reveal which internal circuits or attention heads implement it.
4. The PID-guided reweighting experiment is lightweight (LoRA on last 20% layers, 3,000 samples) and should be viewed as proof-of-concept rather than a validated training recipe.
5. The evaluation focuses on accuracy metrics; whether PID profiles correlate with other important properties (calibration, robustness, fairness) is unexplored.

## Strengths

- **Principled theoretical foundation**: Grounds multimodal interpretability in information theory rather than heuristic visualizations.
- **Empirical breadth**: Evaluates 20 models across 7 families and 7 benchmarks, covering 2B–78B parameters.
- **Actionable insights**: Reveals that surface-level benchmark labels (e.g., "hallucination") mask distinct modality-use profiles, which has implications for benchmark design.
- **Mechanistic depth**: Layer-wise analysis provides a temporal (depth-wise) view of multimodal integration, showing fusion is a late-stage phenomenon.
- **Predictive validity**: PID terms predict intervention sensitivity, validating that the decomposition captures behaviorally meaningful signals.
- **Practical application**: PID-guided reweighting demonstrates the framework can move beyond diagnosis to inform training.
- **Elegant tri-modal extension**: Sensory PID's treatment of language as a control variable is conceptually clean and computationally tractable.

## Limitations

- **Discrete decision space constraint**: The framework operates over multiple-choice QA, making it inapplicable to open-ended generation without further methodological development.
- **Approximate unimodal conditionals**: Calibrated embedding masking is a proxy; native unimodal inference might produce different hidden states.
- **Statistical rather than causal**: PID identifies statistical dependence patterns but does not reveal internal mechanisms (circuits, attention heads, neurons).
- **Limited training application scope**: PID-guided reweighting is proof-of-concept only, using lightweight LoRA and small sample counts.
- **Visual dominance in omni-modal models**: While documented, the root cause (architectural bias, training data imbalance, or inductive priors) is not fully disentangled.
- **No exploration of other modalities**: The framework is tested on vision-language and video-audio-language settings; extension to other modalities (touch, depth, etc.) is future work.
- **Computational cost**: BATCH-based PID estimation requires profiling sets and iterative optimization, which may be expensive for very large models or datasets.

## Relevance

对于研究多模态大语言模型可解释性和模态融合机制的读者，本文提供了一个全新的信息论视角，能够超越传统的准确率评估和消融实验，精确量化不同模态在模型决策中的功能角色。其发现的视觉主导瓶颈和协同信息缺失问题，对全模态模型的架构设计和训练数据构造具有直接的指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How can PID be extended to continuous or autoregressive output spaces for analyzing chat-style assistants and long-form generation?
2. Does native unimodal inference (via explicit single-modality training or prompting) produce substantially different PID profiles compared to calibrated embedding masking?
3. Can PID be used as a real-time monitoring signal during pre-training or instruction tuning to detect when models start relying on language shortcuts?
4. What architectural changes (e.g., balanced encoder capacities, cross-modal attention designs) would most effectively alleviate the sensory synergy bottleneck in omni-modal models?
5. How do PID profiles correlate with model calibration, robustness to adversarial perturbations, or fairness across demographic groups?
6. Can PID-inspired auxiliary objectives be designed to encourage fusion-sensitive behavior without over-optimizing individual information terms?
7. How do PID profiles evolve during the training process—do models initially rely on single modalities and gradually develop synergy, or is synergy present from early stages?
