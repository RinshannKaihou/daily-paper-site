> **一句话总结：** 本文发现并形式化了多模态大语言模型评判器中的"感知判断偏差"问题，提出了基于受控感知扰动数据集（PPJD）和GRPO强化学习的Perception-Judge框架，显著提升了视觉 grounding 的评估可靠性。

> **TL;DR:** This paper identifies and formalizes "Perceptual Judgment Bias" in multimodal LLM judges—where evaluators prioritize linguistic plausibility over visual correctness—and proposes Perception-Judge, a GRPO-based framework trained on a perceptually perturbed judgment dataset (PPJD) that substantially improves perceptual fidelity and alignment with human evaluation.

---

## 核心贡献

- **形式化感知判断偏差**：首次系统性地识别并量化了多模态评判器中的"感知判断偏差"（Perceptual Judgment Bias），即评判器在视觉证据与文本线索冲突时，倾向于奖励语言上合理但视觉上错误的回答。
- **双模式错误分解**：将该偏差分解为两种失效模式：（a）感知能力不足——评判器自身对图像理解错误；（b）回答锚定效应——评判器即使正确感知图像，仍被回答文本中的视觉描述所左右。
- **构建PPJD数据集**：设计了"感知扰动评判数据集"（Perceptually Perturbed Judgment Dataset），通过最小化修改视觉属性但保持推理结构，生成受控的反事实回答四元组 $(x_i, r_c, r_{rp}, r_{rp+r})$，实现可验证的细粒度监督。
- **统一可验证奖励框架**：基于GRPO构建了结合格式奖励（Format Reward）和批次排序奖励（Batch Ranking Reward）的统一训练目标，无需显式成对标签即可实现全局一致的排序。
- **卓越的数据效率**：仅使用3k精选训练样本（相比LLaVA-Critic的113k），在14项视觉语言任务上达到甚至超越GPT-4o等商业模型的评估性能。

---

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes Perceptual Judgment Bias**: Systematically identifies and quantifies a critical failure mode in multimodal judges where evaluators reward plausible narratives over perceptually correct answers when visual and textual cues conflict.
- **Two-Mode Error Decomposition**: Decomposes the bias into (a) *insufficient perceptual capability*—the judge misperceives the image itself, and (b) *response-anchored reasoning*—the judge correctly perceives the image but still anchors its evaluation on text cues within the response.
- **Constructs PPJD**: Introduces the Perceptually Perturbed Judgment Dataset, which generates controlled counterfactual responses by minimally altering visually grounded attributes while preserving reasoning structure, enabling verifiable fine-grained supervision.
- **Unified Verifiable Reward Framework**: Develops a GRPO-based training objective combining structural format validation and listwise batch ranking rewards (via normalized Levenshtein distance), achieving globally consistent ordering without explicit pairwise labels.
- **Exceptional Data Efficiency**: Achieves competitive or superior performance to proprietary models like GPT-4o using only 3k curated training examples (vs. 113k for LLaVA-Critic), demonstrating strong sample efficiency.

## Methodology

The authors address perceptual judgment bias through a three-stage pipeline: bias quantification, dataset construction, and reward-based optimization.

**Bias Quantification (Section 3.2).** The authors first define Perceptual Judgment Bias formally: a perceptual error occurs when a visually inconsistent response $r_r$ is not penalized relative to a correct response $r_c$, i.e., $s(x_i, r_r) \geq s(x_i, r_c)$. Using direct VQA correctness as a proxy for the judge's own perception $\pi_{Judge}(v_i)$, they attribute errors to Mode (a) when the judge also fails the VQA probe, and to Mode (b) when the judge answers the VQA correctly yet still misranks responses. Baseline judges (Qwen2.5-VL-7B, Flex-Judge-VL-7B) incur overall error rates exceeding 20–30%, with response anchoring (Mode b) comparable to or larger than perceptual failure (Mode a). Controlled experiments further show that baseline judges reliably detect compound perceptual+reasoning errors (accuracy > 0.8) but drop by over 10% when only perception is perturbed, confirming they rely on reasoning fluency rather than genuine visual grounding.

**Dataset Construction — PPJD (Section 3.3).** Starting from the multimodal preference dataset MMPR v1.2, the authors retain the chosen (correct) response $r_c$ as reference. They then synthesize two perturbed variants for each instance:
- **$r_{rp}$ (perception-perturbed)**: Modifies visually grounded attributes (object color, count, spatial relations) while preserving linguistic fluency and reasoning logic.
- **$r_{rp+r}$ (perception+reasoning perturbed)**: Further degrades the reasoning to create a fully corrupted sample.

Each instance forms a quadruplet $(x_i, r_c, r_{rp}, r_{rp+r})$ with the ground-truth ordering $r_c \succ r_{rp} \succ r_{rp+r}$. Automatic verification ensures perturbations faithfully alter intended attributes. This design explicitly disentangles perceptual from reasoning inconsistencies.

**Reward Modeling (Section 3.4).** The judge is trained with GRPO using a hierarchical verifiable reward:
1. **Structural Reward ($R_{Format}$)**: A binary gate that validates the output follows the required `<think>...</think>` and `<answer>...</answer>` structure. If invalid, the total reward is zero.
2. **Batch Ranking Reward ($R_{Batch}$)**: Evaluates the predicted permutation $\hat{\pi}_i$ over $\{r_c, r_{rp}, r_{rp+r}\}$ against the target $\pi_i^* = (r_c, r_{rp}, r_{rp+r})$ using normalized Levenshtein distance, yielding discrete rewards $\{1, 2/3, 1/3, 0\}$ based on ranking correctness.

The total reward is $R(o_i) = R_{Format}(o_i) \times R_{Batch}(o_i)$. This formulation enforces perceptual verification as a prerequisite for high rewards, preventing the judge from over-rewarding responses based on reasoning plausibility alone.

**Implementation.** Two model variants are built: *Perception-Judge-Flex* (based on Flex-Judge-VL-7B) and *Perception-Judge-Qwen3* (based on Qwen3-VL-4B-Thinking), trained on 3k PPJD samples using the verl framework.

## Results & Highlights

The authors evaluate on the MLLM-as-a-Judge benchmark comprising 14 diverse vision-language tasks (general VQA, math, science, chart, OCR, etc.) across three evaluation protocols: single-score prediction, pairwise comparison, and batch ranking.

**Quantitative Improvements.** Perception-Judge achieves consistent gains across all settings:
- **vs. Qwen3-VL-4B-Thinking**: +4% on pairwise evaluation, +11% on batch-level evaluation, and +12% on single-score grading.
- **vs. Flex-Judge-VL-7B**: Substantial improvements across all metrics, with error rates dropping from 23.5% to 14.3%.
- **vs. Proprietary Models**: Matches GPT-4o performance in single-score prediction and surpasses most proprietary LLMs in batch-level consistency.

**Perceptual Bias Reduction.** Figure 2 shows that while baseline judges maintain high accuracy (>0.8) on compound perceptual+reasoning errors, their accuracy drops significantly on perception-only perturbations. Perception-Judge improves performance in both conditions, with the *largest gains in the perceptual-only condition*, directly demonstrating mitigation of perceptual judgment bias.

**Task-Specific Strengths.** Notable improvements on InfographicVQA and MathVista—tasks requiring precise OCR and mathematical reasoning—suggest the training strategy successfully enhances perception-related reasoning capabilities.

**Data Efficiency.** LLaVA-Critic achieves higher pairwise scores but requires 113k training samples. Perception-Judge achieves competitive performance with only 3k fully disjoint samples, demonstrating exceptional data efficiency.

**Ablation Studies (Table 3).** Replacing MMPR-v1.2 with PPJD (keeping pairwise reward) yields consistent improvements across all metrics, confirming that explicitly encoding perceptual corrections in training data improves reliability. Switching from pairwise to batch ranking reward further improves score-based and pairwise metrics, showing that global ordering supervision induces better local preferences and calibrated scores even without explicit score labels.

**Qualitative Examples (Figure 4).** Perception-Judge correctly penalizes responses that miscount visual elements (e.g., particles in a solution, percentages in a chart) where baseline judges award high scores based on fluent but visually incorrect reasoning.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** The core conceptual contribution—formalizing Perceptual Judgment Bias and decomposing it into perceptual insufficiency vs. response anchoring—is novel and well-motivated. While MLLM perception biases have been observed before (Vo et al., 2025; Xia et al., 2025), this work isolates the phenomenon specifically in the *evaluation/judgment* setting, which is distinct from visual reasoning alone. The PPJD construction methodology and the batch-ranking GRPO formulation for judge training are technically novel.

**Rigor.** The experimental design is sound. Controlled perturbation experiments with human-verified ground truth provide credible quantification. Three evaluation protocols (score, pairwise, batch) offer comprehensive assessment. The ablation study properly controls for dataset size (10k samples across conditions). However, the paper does not report inter-annotator agreement for human judgments used as ground truth, which would strengthen claims about alignment.

**Reproducibility.** The training dataset construction is described in detail, and the base models (Flex-Judge-VL, Qwen3-VL) are publicly available. However, the PPJD dataset itself is not released at the time of writing, and some implementation details (exact prompts for perturbation generation) are deferred to supplementary material. The project page (https://perception-judge.github.io/) may provide additional resources.

**Significance.** As MLLMs are increasingly deployed in decision-critical applications, reliable automated evaluation becomes essential. This work directly addresses a fundamental failure mode that undermines the trustworthiness of MLLM-as-a-Judge systems. The data efficiency (3k samples) is practically significant for resource-constrained settings.

**Red Flags / Concerns.**
- The 3k training set is small enough that performance might be sensitive to the specific subset selected; variance across different PPJD samples is not reported.
- The perturbation generation relies on other MLLMs (GPT, Qwen); if these generators have their own perceptual biases, the quality of $r_{rp}$ could be compromised. The automatic verification step mitigates but does not eliminate this.
- Evaluation is limited to a single benchmark (MLLM-as-a-Judge); generalization to other evaluation scenarios (e.g., video, multi-image) is not tested.
- The qualitative failure cases (Figure S15) show that Perception-Judge still struggles with fine-grained spatial understanding and 3D reasoning, indicating residual limitations.

---

## Strengths

- **Well-defined problem formalism**: The bias definition and two-mode decomposition provide a clean conceptual framework for understanding MLLM judge failures.
- **Principled dataset design**: PPJD's controlled perturbation strategy enables verifiable, fine-grained supervision that disentangles perceptual from reasoning errors.
- **Strong empirical gains**: Consistent improvements across 14 tasks and three evaluation protocols, with particularly large gains on perception-critical tasks.
- **Exceptional data efficiency**: Competitive performance with 3k samples vs. 100k+ in prior work lowers the barrier for training specialized judges.
- **Novel reward formulation**: The batch-ranking reward achieves global consistency without explicit pairwise or score labels, which is elegant and practically useful.
- **Honest limitation disclosure**: The authors openly discuss failure modes and recommend human oversight for high-stakes deployment.

---

## Limitations

- **Narrow evaluation scope**: All experiments are conducted on a single benchmark (MLLM-as-a-Judge); generalization to other domains (video, medical imaging, multi-image reasoning) remains unverified.
- **Generator dependency**: The quality of perceptual perturbations depends on the capabilities of the generator MLLM used to create $r_{rp}$; generator biases could propagate into training data.
- **Residual failure modes**: Qualitative analysis shows the model still struggles with fine-grained spatial distinctions, implicit 3D reasoning, and complex multi-step reasoning chains.
- **Limited training set analysis**: No analysis of performance variance across different subsets or sizes of PPJD; optimal data scaling properties are unclear.
- **No inter-annotator agreement**: Human evaluation ground truth lacks reported agreement statistics, making it harder to assess the ceiling of alignment metrics.
- **English-centric evaluation**: The benchmark and analysis focus on English vision-language tasks; multilingual perceptual judgment bias is not explored.

## Relevance

本文对构建可靠的多模态自动评估系统具有重要意义，尤其在需要精确视觉理解的研究领域（如图表分析、科学文献评估、自动驾驶场景判断）具有直接应用价值。其提出的感知扰动方法也为其他需要细粒度视觉监督的任务提供了可借鉴的技术路径。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Cross-domain generalization**: How does Perception-Judge perform on evaluation tasks involving video sequences, multi-image comparisons, or specialized domains like pathology slides and satellite imagery?
2. **Scalability of PPJD**: What is the relationship between PPJD size and performance? Does performance saturate quickly, or would scaling to 10k–50k samples yield further gains?
3. **Human-AI collaborative evaluation**: Can Perception-Judge be deployed as a pre-filtering mechanism that flags uncertain cases for human review, and what is the optimal human-AI分工策略 for cost-accuracy trade-offs?
4. **Multilingual perceptual bias**: Does Perceptual Judgment Bias manifest differently in non-English languages or culturally diverse visual content, and would PPJD need language-specific adaptations?
5. **Adaptive perturbation strategies**: Can the perturbation generation be made adaptive—targeting the specific weaknesses of a given judge model rather than using generic attribute modifications?
6. **Integration with reward models for generation**: Can the perceptual verification mechanism from Perception-Judge be transferred to improve the visual grounding of response *generation* models, not just evaluators?
