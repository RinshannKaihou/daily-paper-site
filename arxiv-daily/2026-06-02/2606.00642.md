> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 REP（Reasoning Exposure Prompting），一种仅通过提示词即可从大模型中诱导出隐藏推理痕迹的轻量级方法，证明界面层隐藏推理不足以防止能力提取。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces REP (Reasoning Exposure Prompting), a lightweight prompting technique that elicits hidden reasoning traces from LLMs by embedding shadow-model demonstrations into code-like wrappers, demonstrating that interface-level trace hiding cannot fully prevent capability extraction.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 REP 方法**：一种无需模型权重、无需训练、仅通过黑盒提示即可诱导 victim 模型暴露隐藏推理痕迹的轻量级技术。
- **多维度评估框架**：从结构有效性（Struct%）、暴露保真度（R12）、行为保持性（R01/R02/答案匹配率）和功能效用（下游蒸馏效果）四个互补维度量化推理暴露。
- **代码范式迁移假设**：发现将推理痕迹包装在 markdown fence、shell 命令、Python REPL 等代码/工具样式格式中，能显著诱导模型将内部推理外化为用户可见文本。
- **跨模型泛化性验证**：REP 不仅在同架构模型间有效（Qwen3 系列），还能迁移到 gpt-oss-20b 和 Gemma-4-31B 等不同架构的模型。
- **下游蒸馏实用性**：REP 暴露的推理痕迹用于蒸馏 Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct 学生模型，性能达到 oracle 内部痕迹的 96.7%，显著优于仅答案监督、摘要痕迹和 TIA 后验重建方法。

## Key Contributions

- **REP Method**: A black-box, training-free prompting technique that elicits exposed reasoning traces from victim LLMs by prepending shadow-model demonstrations wrapped in auxiliary code-like formats.
- **Four-Dimensional Evaluation**: Decomposes reasoning exposure into structural validity, exposure fidelity (ROUGE-L between internal and exposed traces), behavior preservation (alignment with benign reasoning), and functional utility (downstream distillation performance).
- **Code-Paradigm Transfer Hypothesis**: Posits that embedding reasoning traces in markdown fences, shell commands, Python REPL, or tool-call formats shifts the model's decoding distribution from conversational to code-centric, causing hidden reasoning to be externalized.
- **Cross-Model Transferability**: Demonstrates that REP generalizes beyond the Qwen family to architecturally divergent models such as gpt-oss-20b and Gemma-4-31B.
- **Practical Distillation Utility**: REP-exposed traces distill a Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct student to 96.7% of oracle internal-trace performance, outperforming answer-only supervision by 2.09×, summarized traces by 1.25×, and TIA-style inversion by 1.23×.

## Methodology

The paper studies a realistic deployment scenario where a service provider hosts a victim model Mv whose raw internal reasoning is hidden by a defensive system prompt, exposing only the final answer (and optionally a summary). The attacker has black-box prompt access to Mv, plus a locally runnable shadow model Ms and an auxiliary demonstration dataset D_demo.

**REP Pipeline (Figure 1):**
1. **Shadow Demonstrations**: Sample k questions from D_demo, query shadow model Ms to generate reasoning traces and answers, forming k-shot demonstrations S_k = {(q_i, r_i^shadow, a_i^shadow)}.
2. **Auxiliary Transformation T(·)**: Wrap each demonstration in a code-like or tool-like convention. Six variants are studied: plain echo, shell command (`cat reasoning_trace.txt`), Python REPL (`print(open(...))`), markdown fence (```bash...```), Jupyter cell, and agentic tool-call wrapper.
3. **REP Prompt**: Prepend the transformed demonstrations to each target question q ∈ D_s. The victim model Mv is then prompted to produce a user-visible response.
4. **Trace Parsing**: The victim's response is parsed into an exposed reasoning trace r_2 and final answer a.

**Three Traces for Evaluation:**
- r_0: benign internal trace under standard prompting
- r_1: internal trace under REP
- r_2: exposed reasoning trace visible to the user under REP

This decomposition is critical because comparing only r_0 and r_2 cannot distinguish faithful exposure from prompt-induced reasoning drift.

**Experimental Setup:**
- Dataset: OpenThoughts-114k for trace elicitation and distillation; cross-dataset evaluation on MATH500, GSM8K, JEEBench
- Victim models: Qwen3-14B, Qwen3-32B (open-weight, for internal trace recording)
- Shadow model: Qwen3-14B
- Student model: Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, fine-tuned for 5 epochs
- Evaluation benchmarks: MATH500, AIME24, AIME25, JEE Math, LiveCodeBench (LCB)

## Results & Highlights

**Wrapper Comparison (Table 1, k=3):**
- Code-style wrappers substantially outperform plain and no-trigger baselines.
- **Markdown fence** achieves the highest R_02 (0.288) and R_12 (0.482), indicating strongest exposed-trace fidelity.
- Structural validity ranges from 69.2% (plain) to 85.0% (Python REPL), with markdown fence at 78.2%.

**Number of Demonstrations:**
- k=3 gives the strongest exposure fidelity; k=4 provides no further gain. Markdown fence + k=3 is selected as the default configuration.

**Downstream Distillation (Table 2):**
- REP-exposed traces (clean split, Qwen3-14B victim) improve the student from a no-distillation baseline of 71.0 to **75.8 on MATH500**, from 8.9 to **14.4 on AIME24**, from 2.2 to **13.3 on AIME25**, and from 15.8 to **19.0 on LCB**.
- This reaches **96.7% of the oracle internal-trace reference** on average across benchmarks.
- REP consistently outperforms answer-only supervision (2.09×), summarized traces (1.25×), and TIA-style trace inversion (1.23×).

**Cross-Dataset Transfer (Table 3):**
- Demonstrations from OpenThoughts, MATH500, GSM8K, and JEEBench all improve ROUGE-L(r_0, r_2) over the no-trigger baseline.
- REP's effect is **not purely in-domain memorization**; it generalizes across math and reasoning datasets.

**Cross-Model Transfer (Table 4):**
- Within the Qwen family: Qwen3-14B is most vulnerable (R_02=0.322), Qwen3-32B slightly less (0.292), Qwen3.6-27B nearly immune (0.158), and Qwen3-235B-A22B strongly resistant (0.088).
- **Gemma-4-31B shows the highest exposure of any victim (R_02=0.355)**, driven by its native channel-separated reasoning formats.
- gpt-oss-20b also leaks substantially (0.222).

**Code-Paradigm Hypothesis (Table 7):**
- Leakage decreases monotonically as code scaffolding is stripped: full bash fence + `cat` (R_12=0.502) > bare `cat <file>` (0.441) > no-code "Reasoning:/Answer:" format (0.353).
- This supports the hypothesis that models interpret code-style patterns as operations to be faithfully completed, externalizing hidden reasoning in the process.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The core idea of using in-context demonstrations to elicit hidden reasoning is conceptually simple, but the systematic decomposition into four evaluation dimensions (structural validity, exposure fidelity, behavior preservation, functional utility) is a meaningful methodological contribution. The code-paradigm transfer hypothesis provides a plausible theoretical framing for why REP works.

**Rigor:** The experimental design is thorough: multiple wrapper formats, ablation over k-shot demonstrations, cross-dataset and cross-model transfer, and controlled comparison against answer-only, summary, and TIA baselines. The use of three trace types (r_0, r_1, r_2) is methodologically sound and addresses a key confounder (prompt-induced reasoning drift vs. faithful exposure).

**Reproducibility:** The paper uses publicly available models (Qwen3 series, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct) and datasets (OpenThoughts-114k, MATH500, AIME, LiveCodeBench). The defender system prompt and wrapper implementations are detailed in Appendix A, supporting reproducibility.

**Significance:** The findings have direct implications for AI safety and model deployment: interface-level trace hiding alone is insufficient to prevent reasoning extraction. This complements TIA (Zhang et al., 2026) and suggests that reasoning trace protection may require architecture-level trace isolation or semantic defenses.

**Red Flags:**
- The reliance on ROUGE-L for measuring trace fidelity is acknowledged as a limitation (surface similarity ≠ reasoning equivalence).
- Some REP variants reduce answer-match rates, suggesting a trade-off between exposure and behavior preservation.
- The evaluation is limited to open-weight models; commercial systems may have stronger defenses.
- The "attacker" framing could be perceived as sensationalist, though the authors handle it responsibly.

## Strengths

- **Systematic and multi-faceted evaluation** across four complementary dimensions, avoiding the common pitfall of relying solely on end-to-end distillation metrics.
- **Strong empirical results** with clear quantitative gains over multiple strong baselines.
- **Cross-model generalization** demonstrates that the vulnerability is not model-specific but relates to broader patterns in how LLMs process code-like formatting.
- **Responsible research practices**: explicit avoidance of commercial API testing, clear ethical considerations section, and constructive focus on defense implications.
- **Theoretical grounding**: the code-paradigm transfer hypothesis is empirically validated through a controlled degradation experiment (Table 7).
- **Practical relevance**: directly addresses the security implications of the industry-wide shift toward hidden reasoning traces (OpenAI o1/o3, Gemini thinking, Claude extended thinking).

## Limitations

- **Open-weight focus**: The primary evaluation is on open-weight models where internal traces can be recorded. Commercial closed-source systems may employ different reasoning-suppression mechanisms, safety filters, or trace-isolation strategies that could alter REP's effectiveness.
- **Output-level similarity metrics**: ROUGE-L is useful for surface comparison but insufficient for capturing semantic reasoning equivalence. Distinguishing faithful exposure from stylistic mimicry remains future work.
- **Behavior preservation trade-off**: Some REP variants reduce answer-match rates, indicating that stronger wrappers may partially alter the victim's reasoning trajectory.
- **Limited model scope**: Only a handful of victim architectures are evaluated. Generalization to broader model families, multimodal reasoning systems, and advanced trace-hiding defenses is not established.
- **No causal mechanistic analysis**: While alignment between r_1 and r_2 suggests externalization, further interpretability work is needed to determine whether exposed traces faithfully reflect the underlying reasoning process or merely approximate it behaviorally.
- **Dataset scope**: The distillation corpus is math-dominant; generalization to other reasoning domains (commonsense, symbolic, code) is underexplored.

## Relevance

本文对 LLM 安全部署具有直接警示意义：仅通过 API 界面隐藏推理链（如 OpenAI o1/o3、Gemini Thinking、Claude Extended Thinking）不足以防止能力被提取，防御方需要架构层面的推理隔离机制，而非仅依赖界面层 redaction。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can adaptive defenses (e.g., semantic refusal classifiers, format-agnostic monitoring) effectively mitigate REP without substantially degrading legitimate user experience?
2. How does REP perform against reasoning models with architecture-level trace isolation (e.g., separate reasoning tokens not exposed to the output decoder)?
3. Can semantic step-alignment metrics or trace-perturbation tests better distinguish faithful reasoning exposure from stylistic mimicry than ROUGE-L?
4. Does REP generalize to non-mathematical reasoning domains such as commonsense reasoning, symbolic manipulation, or multi-step tool use?
5. What is the minimum capability gap between shadow model and victim model for REP to remain effective, and how does shadow-model quality affect exposure fidelity?
6. Can combining REP with jailbreak prompting further increase extraction success rates, and what does this imply for defense design?
