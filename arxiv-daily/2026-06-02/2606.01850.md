> **一句话总结：** 该研究首次利用 conformal prediction 框架系统评估了量化与剪枝后的压缩 LLM 的不确定性保留能力，发现压缩常常将准确率与不确定性解耦，大模型更能吸收压缩带来的不确定性膨胀，且这种膨胀往往是阈值式而非渐进式的。

> **TL;DR:** This paper presents the first comprehensive benchmark evaluating whether quantization and pruning preserve the uncertainty calibration of LLMs, using conformal prediction as a rigorous, distribution-free uncertainty metric across 12 models (1B–70B), four compression paradigms, and five NLP tasks. The key findings are: (i) compression frequently decouples accuracy from uncertainty, (ii) larger models absorb compression-induced uncertainty far more effectively, and (iii) uncertainty inflation is often threshold-like rather than gradual.

## 核心贡献

- **全新评估视角**：首次将 conformal prediction 框架系统性地应用于压缩 LLM 的不确定性评估，提供了一种不依赖分布假设、具有有限样本覆盖保证的严谨度量。
- **跨压缩范式的统一基准**：构建了涵盖 12 个 LLM（1B 到 70B 参数，横跨 Llama-2/3、Qwen-3、DeepSeek、Falcon 四个家族）、四种压缩配置（W4A16 权重量化、W4A4 权值-激活量化、50% 非结构化剪枝、20% 结构化剪枝）的统一评估协议。
- **三个具有实践指导意义的发现**：(i) 压缩往往将准确率保留与不确定性保留解耦；(ii) 更大的模型能更有效地吸收压缩引发的不确定性膨胀；(iii) 不确定性膨胀常呈阈值式突增，而非渐进式恶化。
- **明确的工程建议**：倡导在模型压缩流水线中将不确定性评估作为标准步骤，并建议在部署前对压缩比例进行扫描，因为看似安全的配置可能已接近不确定性突变的临界点。

## Key Contributions

- **Novel evaluation perspective**: First systematic application of conformal prediction to evaluate uncertainty in compressed LLMs, providing a distribution-free, statistically principled measure of reliability beyond accuracy.
- **Unified benchmark across compression paradigms**: Comprehensive evaluation of 12 LLMs spanning four families (1B–70B parameters) under four compression configurations: W4A16 weight-only quantization, W4A4 weight-activation quantization, 50% unstructured pruning, and 20% structured pruning.
- **Three key findings with practical implications**: (i) Compression frequently decouples accuracy preservation from uncertainty preservation; (ii) Larger models absorb compression-induced uncertainty inflation far more effectively; (iii) Uncertainty inflation is often threshold-like rather than gradual.
- **Actionable deployment guidelines**: Advocates for integrating uncertainty evaluation into standard compression pipelines and recommends sweeping compression ratios before deployment, since seemingly safe configurations may be close to a tipping point.

## Methodology

The paper adopts a rigorous benchmark design centered on **split conformal prediction**, a distribution-free framework that constructs prediction sets with finite-sample coverage guarantees. Given a calibration set and a user-specified error rate α = 0.1 (targeting 90% coverage), the method computes a conformal threshold from nonconformity scores and builds prediction sets for test inputs. Two score functions are used and averaged: **LAC (Least Ambiguous set-valued Classifiers)**, which uses 1 − π̂(Y|X), and **APS (Adaptive Prediction Sets)**, which accumulates sorted class probabilities.

**Tasks and datasets**: Five NLP tasks are formulated as six-option multiple-choice questions: question answering (MMLU), reading comprehension (CosmosQA), commonsense inference (HellaSwag), dialogue response selection (HaluDial), and document summarization (HaluSum). The "I don't know" and "None of the above" options are added to standardize difficulty. 2,000 instances per dataset are sampled, with 50/50 calibration-test splits.

**Models**: 12 LLMs across four families — Llama-2 (7B, 13B, 70B), Llama-3 (1B, 8B, 70B), Qwen-3 (8B, 14B, 32B, 30B-A3B MoE), DeepSeek-7B, and Falcon-7B.

**Compression configurations**:
- **Quantization**: Weight-only (W4A16) via RTN, AWQ, GPTQ; weight-activation (W4A4) via QuaRot, SpinQuant, FlatQuant.
- **Pruning**: 50% unstructured via Magnitude, SparseGPT, Wanda; 20% structured via LLM-Pruner, SliceGPT.

**Evaluation metrics**: Prediction Accuracy (Acc), Coverage Rate (CR, as a validity sanity check), and **Prediction Set Size (SS)** as the primary uncertainty metric — smaller SS indicates higher confidence, larger SS signals greater uncertainty. Results are averaged over LAC/APS score functions and three prompting strategies.

## Results & Highlights

**Overall stability ranking**: W4A16 weight-only quantization is the safest compression regime, usually keeping SS within ~0.5 of the FP16 baseline. W4A4 quantization causes the largest uncertainty inflation, especially for smaller models. Pruning exhibits strong method-dependence — advanced unstructured methods (SparseGPT, Wanda) outperform Magnitude pruning, while structured pruning is volatile (LLM-Pruner can be effective on large models; SliceGPT may cause distributional collapse).

**Finding 1 — Accuracy–Uncertainty Decoupling**: Scatter plots of ΔAcc vs. ΔSS show wide dispersion rather than a compact trend. For example, Llama2-13B under W4A16 AWQ on Commonsense Inference slightly improves accuracy (59.63% → 61.17%) while increasing SS (2.83 → 3.13). Conversely, Llama3.1-8B under 50% Wanda pruning sees QA accuracy drop from 62.93% to 45.97% while SS barely changes (2.98 → 3.09). This confirms that accuracy alone is insufficient for uncertainty-sensitive deployment.

**Finding 2 — Scale Absorbs Compression**: Under W4A4 QuaRot, the average SS increase across five tasks is ~+2.56 for Llama2-7B, ~+0.72 for Llama2-13B, and only ~+0.37 for Llama2-70B — a 6.9× reduction in uncertainty inflation from 7B to 70B. Similarly, under 50% Wanda pruning on Reading Comprehension, SS increases are +1.02 (7B), +0.23 (13B), and +0.15 (70B). However, scale is not a complete safeguard: Llama3.1-70B under Magnitude pruning still shows large CI SS inflation (1.89 → 3.25).

**Finding 3 — Threshold-Like Inflation**: Progressive Wanda pruning experiments (0%–50% sparsity) reveal that SS trajectories are often non-linear. For Llama3.1-8B, RC SS stays within 1.89–2.06 from 0% to 40%, then jumps to 2.53 at 50%. For Llama2-7B, the 40%→50% step alone increases RC SS by +0.59 (2.89 → 3.48), accounting for more than half of total 0%–50% inflation. Larger models show flatter trajectories, but even 70B models are not immune (Llama3.1-70B CI rises from 1.89 to 2.21 at 30%, then jumps to 3.16 at 50%).

**MoE results (Qwen3-30B-A3B)**: MoE compression is highly method-dependent. RTN is the most stable W4A16 method (Acc 45.64, SS 3.09 vs. dense 46.52/3.32). LLM-Pruner at 20% structured pruning surprisingly improves both metrics (74.33 Acc, 2.01 SS), while SliceGPT collapses to 16.05 Acc and 5.57 SS.

**Precision ablation (Llama2-7B)**: W8A8 and W6A6 remain relatively stable, but W4A4 crosses into a damaging regime (SpinQuant drops Acc from 47.09 to 26.26 and inflates SS from 3.09 to 4.52), confirming that uncertainty degradation is not gradual with precision.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: The core conceptual contribution — applying conformal prediction to compressed LLMs — is straightforward but fills a genuine gap. The observation that accuracy and uncertainty decouple under compression is empirically important, even if the underlying mechanism (compression distorts the output distribution) is not surprising in hindsight. The threshold-like nature of uncertainty inflation is a genuinely interesting and underappreciated phenomenon.

**Rigor**: The experimental design is methodical and well-controlled. Using identical protocols across all models, averaging over two score functions and three prompting strategies, and including coverage rate as a validity check are all sound practices. The 50/50 calibration-test split and fixed α = 0.1 are reasonable but the authors acknowledge these as limitations. The conformal prediction framework provides finite-sample guarantees, which is a meaningful upgrade over heuristic uncertainty metrics used in prior compression work.

**Reproducibility**: The paper provides detailed per-task tables in the appendix and follows a published protocol. However, **no code or model checkpoints are released**, which limits reproducibility. The benchmark involves numerous compressed variants (12 models × ~10 configurations × 5 tasks), so independent reproduction would require significant compute. The authors should release their compression recipes and evaluation scripts.

**Significance**: For practitioners deploying compressed LLMs in safety-critical domains, this work is a valuable wake-up call. The finding that W4A16 is generally safe while W4A4 and aggressive pruning can silently inflate uncertainty has immediate engineering implications. The scale-dependent absorption effect provides concrete guidance: when aggressive compression is needed, starting from a larger base model is preferable for uncertainty robustness.

**Red flags / limitations**:
- **Task restriction**: All tasks are multiple-choice classification. Open-ended generation — where uncertainty manifests through sequence-level entropy or semantic equivalence — is not evaluated. The authors acknowledge this as future work.
- **No causal mechanism analysis**: The paper identifies *that* compression decouples accuracy from uncertainty, but offers limited analysis of *why*. Is it due to distorted softmax temperatures? Changed logit magnitudes? Outlier suppression? A mechanistic probe would strengthen the work.
- **Missing ablations on α and calibration split**: The paper acknowledges but does not explore how varying α or calibration set size affects the findings.
- **Degenerate cases**: Some entries (e.g., Qwen3-8B SliceGPT with CR ≈ 12%, SS = 1.00; Qwen3-30B-A3B GPTQ with CR ≈ 12.68%) indicate complete distributional collapse. These are mentioned but not deeply analyzed.
- **Cherry-picking concern**: The "threshold-like" narrative is compelling, but the threshold locations vary substantially across models and tasks. The 40%–50% regime is highlighted, but some tasks show earlier transitions.

## Strengths

- **Rigorous uncertainty metric**: Using conformal prediction with finite-sample coverage guarantees is a meaningful methodological upgrade over heuristic confidence scores or calibration error metrics.
- **Comprehensive scale**: Evaluating 12 models from 1B to 70B across four families, four compression paradigms, and five tasks provides strong empirical breadth.
- **Practically important finding**: The accuracy–uncertainty decoupling observation challenges the default assumption that "if accuracy is preserved, the model is deployment-ready."
- **Clear actionable guidance**: The paper translates findings into concrete recommendations (evaluate SS, sweep compression ratios, prefer larger base models for aggressive compression).
- **Transparent reporting**: Extensive appendix tables with per-task breakdowns, per-score-function analysis, and combined quantization+pruning results.

## Limitations

- **Restricted to multiple-choice tasks**: The conformal prediction framework requires discrete label spaces, so open-ended text generation — a major use case for LLMs — is not covered.
- **No mechanistic insight**: The paper is descriptive rather than explanatory. It does not dissect *how* compression alters the internal representations or output distributions to inflate uncertainty.
- **No code release**: Reproducing the full benchmark would require substantial engineering effort without released scripts.
- **Fixed evaluation hyperparameters**: α = 0.1 and 50/50 calibration-test splits are not varied; sensitivity to these choices is unknown.
- **Limited generative task coverage**: Document summarization is included but still formulated as multiple-choice. True free-form generation uncertainty (e.g., semantic uncertainty, sequence-level conformal methods) remains unexamined.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Mechanistic explanation**: What internal changes cause the accuracy–uncertainty decoupling? Does compression alter logit magnitudes, softmax temperature, or representation geometry in ways that can be predicted without full conformal evaluation?
2. **Open-ended generation**: Can conformal prediction for text generation (e.g., sequence-level or semantic conformal methods) be adapted to evaluate uncertainty in compressed LLMs on free-form tasks?
3. **Dynamic thresholds**: Can the observed threshold-like uncertainty inflation be predicted from model properties (e.g., condition number of weight matrices, activation outlier statistics) to guide compression ratio selection without expensive evaluation?
4. **Fine-tuning interaction**: How does quantization-aware fine-tuning or pruning-aware fine-tuning affect the uncertainty profile compared to post-training compression? Does recovery of accuracy also recover uncertainty calibration?
5. **Calibration set sensitivity**: How do the findings change with smaller calibration sets (e.g., 100 or 500 samples) or different α values? Is the threshold-like behavior robust to these choices?
