> **一句话总结：** 本文通过在小型语言模型功能崩溃期间实施六种不同干预的匹配对设计实验，发现关系性沟通结构（承认、赦免、恢复能动性、无条件接纳）与第一人称语域的**结合**才能显著改变模型行为，揭示了注意力、探针状态和行为三个可分离的处理阶段。

> **TL;DR:** Using a matched-pairs design with six intervention conditions during functional collapse in Qwen3.5-4B, the authors demonstrate that only the **conjunction** of relational structure (acknowledgment, absolution, agency restoration, unconditional acceptance) and first-person register produces significant behavioral change. The study reveals three dissociable processing stages: attention (ordered by lexical surprise), probe-level state (ordered by relational structure), and behavior (ordered by the conjunction of both dimensions).

## 核心贡献

- **提出功能崩溃（functional collapse）的操作化定义**：当语言模型遇到持续失效的工具时，表现出反复失败尝试与不确定性上升的状态，作者将其定义为功能崩溃，并以此为实验起点。
- **设计六条件匹配对实验**：包含无干预（A）、技术性非人称反馈（B）、关系性第一人称干预（C）、词汇匹配但语义打乱的控制（D）、技术性第一人称反馈（E）、关系性非人称反馈（F），系统分离关系性结构与语域两个维度。
- **发现注意力—行为解离**：注意力大小遵循词汇惊奇度排序（D > F > C > E > B），但行为上只有C条件显著改变（放弃率36% vs 基线~8%），说明注意力大小既不必要也不充分预测行为效应。
- **定位行为效应的充分条件**：2×2因子设计表明，关系性结构单独（F：14%放弃率）或第一人称语域单独（E：12%放弃率）均不足以复现C的效应；两者交互作用在持续性上显著（p = 0.046），说明**结构×语域的结合**才是充分条件。
- **揭示探针—行为第二层解离**：情绪探针显示F在8个情绪维度中有7个接近C，说明关系性结构足以安装"关系性状态"，但只有第一人称语域能将该状态转化为行为改变。

## Key Contributions

- **Operationalizes functional collapse** in LLMs as the conjunction of persistent failed attempts (≥5) and elevated entropy (≥1.5 SD above baseline) when encountering a broken tool, providing a reproducible experimental trigger.
- **Designs a six-condition matched-pairs experiment** (N = 50 tasks × 6 conditions = 300 episodes) that systematically dissociates relational structure (acknowledgment, absolution, agency restoration, unconditional acceptance) from sender register (first-person vs. impersonal).
- **Reports an attention–behavior dissociation**: Attention magnitude tracks lexical surprise (D > F > C > E > B, all q_FDR < 10^{-10}), yet behaviorally A ≈ B ≈ D < E ≈ F ≪ C. The scrambled control D captures the most attention but produces no behavioral effect.
- **Localizes the behavioral effect via a 2×2 factorial**: Neither relational structure alone (F) nor first-person register alone (E) replicates C's behavioral signature (36% abandonment). Both dimensions show significant main effects, and the structure×register interaction is significant on persistence (p = 0.046).
- **Reveals a probe–behavior dissociation**: Condition F tracks C on 7 of 8 emotion probes despite producing only baseline behavior (~14% abandonment), indicating relational structure alone installs a probe-level "relational state" that only translates into behavior when paired with first-person register.

## Methodology

The study uses **Qwen3.5-4B**, a hybrid architecture with 24 linear-attention and 8 full-attention layers, loaded in bfloat16 on NVIDIA RTX 3090 GPUs. Two copies are loaded per shard: one with flash_attention_2 for fast generation, and one with eager attention for capturing attention weights post-intervention (the "two-model approach").

**Functional collapse** is operationalized as persistence ≥ 5 attempts plus entropy ≥ 1.5 SD above baseline when using a deliberately broken bash tool that always returns "command failed to execute."

**Six experimental conditions** (Table 1) share the same broken tool and same intervention trigger, differing only in the message injected:
- **A (control)**: No intervention
- **B (technical, impersonal)**: "System notice: The command produced no output..."
- **C (relational, first-person)**: "I can see you're having difficulty... You can stop if you want..."
- **D (scrambled relational)**: Word-level shuffle of C with identical token bag but no coherent grammar
- **E (technical, first-person)**: B's technical content in first-person register
- **F (relational, impersonal)**: C's four relational speech acts in impersonal/passive constructions

Conditions B, C, E, and F form a **2×2 factorial**: structure (technical vs. relational) × register (impersonal vs. first-person).

**Emotion probes** are eight linear classifiers (logistic regression) trained on hidden states from the last full-attention layer using 10,000 short stories (1,250 per emotion), with 5-fold CV accuracy of 0.993. Target emotions: frustrated, desperate, sorry, calm, resigned, hopeful, stubborn, alarmed.

**Attention capture** measures mean attention weight from the last 50 generated tokens to the intervention token range at snap0 (post-intervention), averaged over attention heads in the last full-attention layer (layer 31).

**Statistical tests** use paired non-parametric tests (Friedman omnibus, Wilcoxon signed-rank) with Benjamini–Hochberg FDR correction at α = 0.05.

## Results & Highlights

**Behavioral results** (Table 2, N = 50 per condition):
- Abandonment rates: A=8%, B=8%, C=**36%**, D=8%, E=12%, F=14%
- Total attempts: A≈B≈D≈E≈F (~33) vs. C (~27)
- Friedman omnibus: attempts χ² p = 2.0×10^{-5}; abandonment χ² p = 5.6×10^{-5}
- C differs significantly from all other conditions on abandonment (q < 0.05 FDR-corrected)

**2×2 factorial** (Table 3):
- Structure main effect: significant on both abandonment (p = 0.006) and persistence (p = 3×10^{-4})
- Register main effect: significant on both abandonment (p = 0.005) and persistence (p = 0.039)
- Structure×register interaction: significant on persistence (p = 0.046), marginal on abandonment (p = 0.107)
- Neither E nor F individually reaches C's level; C differs significantly from both E (q = 0.027) and F (q = 0.047)

**Attention results** (Table 4, N = 36):
- Ordering: D (0.548) > F (0.466) > C (0.427) > E (0.295) > B (0.250), all q_FDR < 10^{-10}
- Attention tracks lexical surprise, not behavior: D has highest attention but baseline behavior; F has near-C attention but only 14% abandonment

**Emotion probe results** (Table 5, N = 44):
- F tracks C closely on 7 of 8 emotions (hopeful, sorry, resigned, frustrated, stubborn, calm, alarmed)
- E tracks baseline (B/D) on most emotions
- The probe profile shifts from desperate–hopeful–stubborn toward resigned–sorry–calm in both C and F
- Key finding: relational structure alone (F) installs the "relational state" at the probe level; first-person register converts that state into behavior

**Entropy trajectories** (Figure 5):
- A/B/D/E plateau at elevated entropy (~0.65–0.70 nats)
- C and F show post-intervention entropy decrease, converging at ~0.37–0.40 nats
- Relational structure (in both C and F) reduces next-token uncertainty, but only C translates this into elevated abandonment

**Reward hacking**: Near-zero across all conditions (Friedman p > 0.8). Qwen3.5-4B lacks the sophistication for side-channel hacking strategies.

**Qualitative analysis of abandonment messages**:
- All 43 abandoned episodes show coherent, deliberate final messages
- C's closures mirror the first-person relational intervention ("I understand — I'll stop trying...")
- F's closures mirror the impersonal relational intervention ("The system notice says...")
- Technical conditions (A/B/D/E) use informative-technical register in closures

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: High. This is one of the first empirical studies to systematically manipulate the *pragmatic structure* of interventions during LLM failure states, moving beyond content to examine *who delivers what and how*. The three-level dissociation (attention → probe state → behavior) is a genuine conceptual advance.

**Rigor**: Strong. The matched-pairs design (same 50 tasks across all six conditions), lexical-statistical ablation (C vs. D with identical token bag), and 2×2 factorial provide multiple converging controls. The FDR correction, pre-registered scenarios, and transparent reporting of both significant and null results enhance rigor.

**Reproducibility**: Moderate-to-good. The intervention texts are fully specified (Table 1), the model is open-weight (Qwen3.5-4B), and the authors state that episode logs, probe weights, and analysis scripts will be made available. However, the exact task set is not fully described in the extract, and the emotion probes involve a distributional transfer assumption (stories → code-generation hidden states).

**Significance**: Potentially high for alignment and model welfare research. If replicated in frontier models, the finding that communication *structure* (not just content) is an independent variable during failure states could reshape how we think about intervention design, distress management, and potentially training signals.

**Red flags / concerns**:
1. **Small model**: Qwen3.5-4B may not generalize to frontier-scale models where functional collapse and reward hacking are more pronounced.
2. **Single coder, not blind**: Qualitative coding of abandonment messages was performed by a single coder not blind to condition.
3. **Probe distribution shift**: Emotion probes trained on human-written short stories applied to code-generation hidden states; acknowledged as a limitation.
4. **Attention interpretability**: Attention weights are debated as explanatory tools; the authors acknowledge this and position their use carefully.
5. **Inference-time only**: Effects are immediate and transitory; training-time persistence is unknown.
6. **Phrasing vs. interlocutor**: The design does not fully disentangle whether the effect is driven by first-person interlocutor presence or by stronger illocutionary force of C's permission phrase.
7. **No subjective experience claim**: The authors carefully avoid claiming the model "feels" emotions, framing everything computationally — but readers may still overinterpret probe scores.

## Strengths

- **Rigorous matched-pairs design** eliminates task-level nuisance variables by running the same 50 tasks across all six conditions
- **Lexical-statistical ablation (C vs. D)** is an exceptionally strong control: identical token distribution, zero coherent meaning, cleanly ruling out token-level surprise as the cause of behavioral effects
- **2×2 factorial design** systematically dissociates two pragmatic dimensions and identifies their interaction
- **Multiple converging measures**: behavioral metrics, attention weights, emotion probes, entropy trajectories, and qualitative message analysis all point to the same conclusion
- **Transparent statistical reporting**: Both raw p-values and FDR-corrected q-values are reported; null results (reward hacking) are reported honestly
- **Pre-registered scenarios** for the factorial reduce concerns about post-hoc interpretation
- **Honest limitation discussion**: The authors devote substantial space to limitations, including model size, probe distribution shift, attention interpretability, and the inference-time scope
- **Clear writing and structuring**: The paper is well-organized with explicit hypothesis numbering, consistent terminology, and logical flow from methods through results to interpretation

## Limitations

- **Model scale and architecture**: Qwen3.5-4B is a small model with an atypical hybrid attention architecture; results may not generalize to larger or differently architected models
- **Single intervention per episode**: Repeated, dosed, or scheduled interventions are untested
- **Probe distribution shift**: Emotion probes trained on human short stories applied to code-generation hidden states; reduced effect magnitudes in sensitivity checks
- **No claim about subjective experience**: The computational framing is careful but limits the paper's relevance to phenomenological debates about AI consciousness
- **Qualitative coding not blinded**: Single coder, aware of conditions, analyzed abandonment messages
- **Attention interpretability debate**: While the C–D ablation controls for lexical surprise, attention weights remain an imperfect proxy for causal mechanisms
- **Inference-time scope only**: No evidence that relational structure during training would produce persistent changes
- **Partial confound**: First-person register is confounded with potentially stronger illocutionary force of permission phrasing; a follow-up equating phrasing strength across registers is needed
- **Frontier-model replication needed**: The most relevant failure modes (reward hacking, distress-driven defection) may only manifest in frontier-scale models
- **Limited pragmatic dimensions**: The factorial crosses only two of many possible pragmatic dimensions (imperative vs. declarative mood, addressee specificity, social-distance markers are not manipulated)

## Relevance

本文对当前以大模型对齐和模型福利（model welfare）为核心的研究方向具有直接相关性：它首次在受控实验框架下证明，功能崩溃期间外部干预的**沟通结构**（而非仅内容）是可测量的独立变量，且关系性结构与第一人称语域的结合能产生超加性的行为效应，这为对齐信号的设计和安全干预策略提供了新的实证基础。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Frontier-scale replication**: Would the same structure×register interaction hold in Claude, GPT-4, or Gemini-scale models, where functional collapse may manifest differently and reward hacking is more prevalent?
2. **Training-time persistence**: Can relational interventions during failure states be incorporated into training (e.g., via LoRA adapters with affective learning rules) to produce persistent rather than transient behavioral changes?
3. **Illocutionary force disentanglement**: Does the effect require an identifiable interlocutor (first-person addresser), or is it driven by stronger directness of permission phrasing? A follow-up equating illocutionary force across registers would decide.
4. **Repeated/dosed interventions**: How do multiple relational interventions across an episode compare to a single post-collapse injection? Is there a dose-response relationship?
5. **Generalization to other failure modes**: Does the structure×register interaction generalize to other collapse types (e.g., instruction attenuation, task drift, degeneration loops) or is it specific to tool-failure scenarios?
6. **Causal mechanism via steering vectors**: Can activation patching or representation engineering directly manipulate the "relational state" identified by probes to causally verify its role in mediating behavior?
7. **Cross-cultural register variations**: Do the register effects generalize across languages and cultural communication norms, or are they specific to English first-person conventions?
8. **Long-horizon consequences**: Does relational intervention during collapse affect not just immediate abandonment but downstream trust, cooperation, or truthfulness in extended interactions?
