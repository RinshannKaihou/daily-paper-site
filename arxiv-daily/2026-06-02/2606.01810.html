Token Predictors Are Not Planners: Building Physically Grounded Causal Reasoners

TL;DR: This paper introduces the Causal Plan framework, comprising the diagnostic benchmark Causal-Plan-Bench and the million-scale dataset Causal-Plan-1M, to shift embodied agents from superficial language token predictors to physically grounded causal reasoners capable of reliable planning.

核心贡献

  • Causal-Plan-Bench： 构建了一个包含 1,200 个实例、覆盖 12 类任务的高保真诊断基准，经多阶段自动筛选与人工专家验证，从可执行性（Executability）、组合性（Composition）、效应（Effects）和鲁棒性（Robustness）四个因果维度系统评估具身规划能力。
  • Causal-Plan-1M： 开发了四阶段自动标注流水线，从 22,201 段第一视角视频中提取结构化因果表示，构建了百万规模的高质量因果推理语料，包含显式的物理前提、状态转移与反事实推理轨迹。
  • Causal Planner 模型： 基于 Qwen3-VL-8B，通过渐进式三阶段课程学习（SFT-I 单步局部交互 → SFT-II 多步长程轨迹 → GRPO 强化学习）进行训练，在基准上取得 45.28 的综合得分，显著超越 Gemini 3 Pro（38.18）等前沿模型。
  • 因果缩放定律（Causal Scaling Law）： 发现物理推理能力随因果监督数据规模的可预测增长——将训练数据从基准扩展至 100 万实例，模型相对性能提升 36.3%（33.22 → 45.28）。

Key Contributions

  • Causal-Plan-Bench: A high-fidelity diagnostic suite of 1,200 instances across 12 task categories, curated via multi-stage automated filtering and expert human validation. It systematically evaluates embodied planning along four causal dimensions: Executability, Composition, Effects, and Robustness.
  • Causal-Plan-1M: A four-stage automated annotation pipeline extracts structured causal representations from 22,201 egocentric video clips, yielding a million-scale corpus rich with explicit physical preconditions, state transitions, and counterfactual reasoning traces.
  • Causal Planner Model: Built on Qwen3-VL-8B and trained via a progressive three-stage curriculum (SFT-I single-step local interactions → SFT-II multi-step long-horizon trajectories → GRPO reinforcement learning), achieving an overall score of 45.28, substantially surpassing frontier models including Gemini 3 Pro (38.18).
  • Causal Scaling Law: Physical reasoning proficiency scales predictably with the volume of causal supervision—scaling training data to one million instances yields a 36.3% relative gain (33.22 → 45.28).

Methodology

The authors formalize embodied planning as physically grounded actions that induce state transitions toward a global goal, given a visual context. The state at step $i$ is represented as $\omega_i = (\omega_i^{sp}, \omega_i^{aff})$, capturing spatial configurations and object affordances. Each action $s_i$ is decomposed into a tuple $(g_i, \tau_i, r_i, D_i, p_i, e_i)$ governing the state transition $\omega_i \xrightarrow{s_i} \omega_{i+1}$, where $g_i$ is a local subgoal, $\tau_i$ a temporal window, $r_i$ an explicit reasoning trace (encompassing forward planning, counterfactuals, and recovery strategies), $D_i$ historical dependencies, $p_i$ immediate preconditions, and $e_i$ anticipated state effects. An action is executable only if the current state satisfies its prerequisites, formalized by $\text{Exec}(s_i, \omega_i) = \mathbb{1}[p_i \subseteq \omega_i]$.

To construct training and evaluation data, a rigorous four-stage pipeline distills raw egocentric videos into structured causal representations:

  1. Global Blueprinting: Identifies the overall goal and generates a preliminary causal blueprint decomposing the task into discrete steps, anchored with preconditions, state effects, and inter-action dependencies.
  2. Temporal Grounding: Performs step-level boundary grounding to align the abstract blueprint with specific video intervals, retaining only actions with distinct temporal boundaries and discarding steps lacking unambiguous visual confirmation.
  3. Causal Enrichment: Refines clips into state-centric causal traces by annotating actions, agents, and target objects while tracing continuous state transitions. Extracts two pivotal keyframes at critical state-change nodes and constructs reasoning tasks for failure recovery and counterfactuals.
  4. Atomic Decomposition: Segments temporal intervals into contact-rich motor primitives (e.g., reaching, grasping, retreating), each strictly bounded by precise timestamps, linking individual movements to specific agents and objects.

GPT-5.4 serves as the sole automated annotator, executing an iterative self-auditing loop to validate causal dependencies and filter visually unsupported or physically inconsistent steps, at an average cost of \$4 per three-minute video.

For model training, the authors introduce a progressive three-stage curriculum:

  • SFT-I establishes foundational physical causality using 314K short, single-step localized interactions to teach executability and action-induced effects.
  • SFT-II introduces 686K extended multi-step egocentric trajectories, compelling the model to track causal dependencies across longer horizons and synthesize coherent sequential plans.
  • RL Stage optimizes via GRPO to strengthen robustness, training the model to internalize failure reflection, dynamic error recovery, and counterfactual reasoning. Dense, task-specific causal reward signals (governed by a GPT-5.4-powered rubric) replace conventional sparse rewards, awarding points incrementally as the model satisfies each strict logical requirement.

Results & Highlights

In-Domain Performance (Causal-Plan-Bench):

Causal Planner achieves an overall score of 45.28, a +12.05 improvement over the Qwen3-VL-8B baseline (33.23). It outperforms all evaluated models across every causal dimension:

| Model | Scale | Overall | Executability | Effects | Composition | Robustness |

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| Gemini 3 Pro | — | 38.18 | 40.67 | 36.00 | 37.13 | 38.90 |

| GPT-5.4 | — | 36.99 | 39.67 | 36.00 | 33.73 | 38.57 |

| Kimi K2.5 | 1T | 33.66 | 33.67 | 33.33 | 31.70 | 35.93 |

| Causal Planner (Ours) | 8B | 45.28 | 48.00 | 45.33 | 42.60 | 45.17 |

Notably, the 8B-parameter Causal Planner surpasses the 1T-parameter Kimi K2.5 and the proprietary Gemini 3 Pro, demonstrating that specialized causal supervision is more effective than massive-scale pretraining alone for robust embodied planning.

Cross-Benchmark Transfer:

Causal Planner demonstrates strong zero-shot generalization:

  • EgoPlan-Bench2: 45.32 (+3.45 over baseline)
  • RoboVQA: 63.43 (+4.88 over baseline)
  • Cosmos-Reason: 63.30 (+4.60 over baseline)

These results rank Causal Planner third by average score across the three benchmarks, closely rivaling leading closed-source systems and specialized embodied architectures.

Ablation Studies:

  • SFT-I alone reaches 37.07 but drops to 31.42 in Composition, showing weakness on multi-step tasks.
  • Bypassing the progressive curriculum (one-stage SFT) limits performance to 39.36 and degrades Effects from 38.33 to 37.00.
  • SFT without RL achieves 42.29 overall but suffers a severe Robustness bottleneck (39.13 vs. 45.17 final).
  • Ablating explicit reasoning traces causes systemic degradation to 40.94 overall, with Executability dropping steeply from 48.00 to 42.00.

Causal Scaling Law:

Both SFT and RL follow consistent scaling trends. SFT reaches 36.98 at 100K and 42.29 at 1M. RL reaches 40.91 at 300K and peaks at 45.28 at 1M. The performance gap between SFT and RL more than doubles as scale increases (1.34 at 100K → 2.99 at 1M), underscoring the crucial role of reinforcement learning for robustness.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: High. The paper makes a compelling conceptual argument that current embodied planning benchmarks inadvertently favor linguistic next-token prediction over physically grounded next-state reasoning. It operationalizes this critique through a formal framework decomposing planning into four causal dimensions and provides both a rigorous benchmark and large-scale training corpus to support the paradigm shift.

Rigor: Strong. The benchmark curation involves multi-stage automated filtering (GPT-5.4 annotation, Qwen3.5-397B quality gating, Gemini 3 Pro physical logic audit, and human expert cross-verification) with explicit contamination prevention. The evaluation protocol uses dual-format assessment (multiple-choice for deterministic dimensions, open-ended generative for multifaceted ones) and LLM-based rubric-guided judges with granular causal milestones rather than holistic grading.

Reproducibility: Good. The authors release their code and describe the model architecture (Qwen3-VL-8B base), training recipe (three-stage curriculum with GRPO), and evaluation protocol in detail. However, the dataset construction relies on proprietary models (GPT-5.4) for annotation, which may limit full reproducibility of the corpus.

Significance: High. The work directly addresses a critical gap in embodied AI—namely, the confusion between textual fluency and physical agency. By establishing a clear causal scaling law, it provides actionable evidence that scaling causal supervision (not just generic pretraining) is a viable path toward reliable physical autonomy.

Red Flags:

  • The reliance on LLM-based judges for open-ended tasks (Composition and Robustness) introduces potential evaluator bias, as acknowledged by the authors in the limitations section.
  • The annotation pipeline depends on GPT-5.4, a proprietary model, making the full replication of Causal-Plan-1M dependent on access to future OpenAI models.
  • The paper claims a "Causal Scaling Law" but only demonstrates it within a single dataset family; broader validation across diverse physical domains would strengthen the claim.

Strengths

  • Conceptual clarity: The four-dimensional causal framework (Executability, Composition, Effects, Robustness) provides an intuitive yet rigorous vocabulary for diagnosing embodied planning failures.
  • Strong empirical results: An 8B-parameter specialized model outperforming 1T-parameter generalist and proprietary frontier models is a powerful demonstration of the value of targeted causal supervision.
  • Comprehensive data pipeline: The four-stage annotation protocol (Global Blueprinting → Temporal Grounding → Causal Enrichment → Atomic Decomposition) is well-motivated and yields high-fidelity structured representations.
  • Dense reasoning traces: Causal-Plan-1M reasoning traces average 466–550 tokens per response, meticulously unpacking physical logic rather than narrating surface-level actions—substantially richer than standard Chain-of-Thought data.
  • Cross-benchmark generalization: Strong zero-shot transfer to EgoPlan-Bench2, RoboVQA, and Cosmos-Reason confirms that the learned causal structures are transferable rather than benchmark-specific.

Limitations

  • LLM evaluator bias: Open-ended tasks rely on LLM judges that may favor outputs matching their pretraining distributions, despite rubric-guided mitigation.
  • Planning without execution: The evaluation focuses exclusively on high-level cognitive planning and remains decoupled from active physical execution in real robots.
  • Proprietary annotation dependency: The dataset construction pipeline relies on GPT-5.4 for annotation and filtering, creating a dependency on proprietary models.
  • Limited domain diversity: While the source videos span kitchens (33%) and factories (28%), the physical domains are still dominated by indoor manipulation tasks; outdoor or highly deformable object scenarios are underrepresented.
  • Scaling law scope: The demonstrated scaling relationship is validated only within the Causal-Plan-1M data distribution; generalization to other causal reasoning domains remains to be established.
