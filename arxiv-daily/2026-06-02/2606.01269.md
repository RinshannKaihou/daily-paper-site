> **一句话总结：** Transformer在仅学习相邻元素比较任务时，自发形成了类似人类"心理数轴"的一维有序表征流形，并在行为层面复现了经典的符号距离效应和端点锚定效应。

> **TL;DR:** When trained solely on local adjacent comparisons, Transformers spontaneously form a one-dimensional rank-aligned manifold in embedding space — a computational analogue of the "mental number line" — and reproduce the symbolic distance effect and end-anchor effect observed in human and animal cognition.

## 核心贡献

- **理论框架：** 提出最小范数理论解释，证明L2正则化会驱动实体嵌入坍缩到一维流形上，其主成分轴以近乎完美的保真度恢复隐藏的全序关系。
- **合成实验：** 在仅训练相邻比较（Bryant-Trabasso范式）的小型Transformer上，发现模型展现出对未见远距离配对的外分布泛化能力，同时伴随嵌入空间的几何重组。
- **行为 signature 复现：** 即使准确率饱和，决策置信度和几何分离度都随秩距离单调递增，直接模拟了人类、灵长类和啮齿类动物中观察到的符号距离效应。
- **端点锚定效应预测：** 理论分析预测并实验验证了端点元素在流形上向外延伸、内部元素压缩聚集的非均匀间距结构，对应认知科学中的端点锚定效应。
- **大模型验证：** 在Qwen2.5-1.5B预训练模型中发现了相同的秩对齐几何结构，且该结构能追踪每种序数关系的拓扑特征（大小和数字呈线性，月份呈循环）。

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretical framework:** A minimum-norm account proving that L2 regularization drives entity embeddings to collapse onto a one-dimensional manifold whose principal axis recovers the hidden total order with near-perfect fidelity.
- **Synthetic experiment:** On small Transformers trained only on adjacent comparisons (Bryant-Trabasso paradigm), the model exhibits out-of-distribution generalization to unseen distant pairs alongside a striking geometric reorganization of embeddings.
- **Behavioral signature replication:** Even at accuracy ceiling, both decision confidence and geometric separation scale monotonically with rank distance, directly mirroring the symbolic distance effect observed across decades in humans, primates, and rodents.
- **End-anchor effect prediction:** Theoretical analysis predicts and experiments confirm non-uniform spacing where endpoint entities stretch outward while interior ranks compress — the geometric form of the end-anchor effect from cognitive science.
- **LLM validation:** The same rank-aligned geometry is found in a pretrained Qwen2.5-1.5B model, where it tracks the topology of each ordinal relation (linear for sizes and digits, cyclic for months).

## Methodology

The study proceeds in two complementary tracks: a controlled synthetic experiment and a pretrained LLM probing study.

**Synthetic Task:** The setup mirrors the classic Bryant-Trabasso [1971] transitive inference paradigm. A hidden total order of N entities (e₀ ≺ e₁ ≺ ... ≺ eₙ₋₁) is defined, where each entity is an atomic token. The model is trained exclusively on adjacent comparisons in both directions: D_train = {(eᵢ, eᵢ₊₁), (eᵢ₊₁, eᵢ)} for 0 ≤ i < N−1. Evaluation is performed on held-out distant pairs D_OOD = {(eᵢ, eⱼ) : |i−j| > 1}. The model is a single-layer Transformer with entity embeddings, 2 attention heads, a 4d-wide GELU MLP, learned positional embeddings, and pre-LayerNorm. Training uses cross-entropy on the relation token with AdamW and weight decay λ.

**Geometric Probes:** Three metrics quantify ordinal structure: (1) Linearity ρ₁ = λ₁/∑λₖ — variance fraction on the first principal component; (2) Rank alignment r_rank = corr({πᵢ}, {i}) — correlation between PC1 projection and true rank; (3) Geometric separation gᵢⱼ = |πᵢ − πⱼ| — distance between items on the recovered line.

**Behavioral Measures:** Decision confidence is measured as the logit margin mᵢⱼ = W_U zᵢⱼ(c) − W_U zᵢⱼ(č), where zᵢⱼ is the final-position residual and c, č are correct/incorrect relation tokens.

**LLM Probing:** Qwen2.5-1.5B is probed on three ordinal domains — digits 0-9, months of the year, and size adjectives (tiny to gigantic). Each item is tokenized in isolation and its layer-ℓ representation is the mean-pooled residual-stream activation over sub-token positions. PCA diagnostics, leave-one-out linear probes regressing hidden state onto rank (R²), and cosine similarity between per-domain probe directions are computed across layers.

## Results & Highlights

**Synthetic Model Results:**
- Training accuracy saturates almost immediately while OOD generalization and embedding linearity rise together later, showing grokking-like transient dynamics under fixed weight decay.
- At peak OOD checkpoint, entity embeddings collapse onto a clean monotonic staircase along PC1 (r_rank ≈ 0.96 for N=15), confirming the principal axis recovers the hidden total order.
- Decision confidence and geometric separation both increase monotonically with rank distance (Figure 2), constituting a computational analogue of the symbolic distance effect.
- The minimum-norm solution predicts and the data confirm non-uniform spacing: interior ranks bunch together while endpoints e₀ and eₙ₋₁ extend past the linear trend, matching the end-anchor effect.
- Experiments run for N = 10, 15, and 20 entities show consistent qualitative trends; unregularized runs (λ = 0) never organize geometrically, confirming weight decay's necessity.

**LLM Probing Results:**
- All three ordinal domains exhibit positive rank-PC correlation that is stable across early and late layers.
- Digits are recovered most cleanly (r ≈ 0.81 on PC2), sizes moderately, and months most weakly (r = 0.41).
- The digit "0" sits far from 1-9 along PC1, causing the remaining digits' ordinal structure to be carried by a lower component — likely due to zero's vastly different use cases.
- Months show the weakest correlation, consistent with the rank-line picture: a calendar is cyclic (December is adjacent to January), and a cycle cannot be embedded isometrically on a single linear axis.
- Per-domain probe directions are nearly orthogonal at every layer (|cos| < 0.2), indicating the model does not encode a single domain-general "ordinality" direction; each ordinal concept occupies its own subspace.
- Rank-PC correlation and probe R² are roughly flat over network depth, in contrast to the late transient emergence in the synthetic model — expected since the LLM's ordinal geometry is already fixed in pretrained weights.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. The paper makes a fresh bridge between a 50-year-old cognitive science regularity (the mental number line account of transitive inference) and modern neural network interpretability. The finding that Transformers spontaneously discover the same representational primitive as biological cognition is conceptually striking.

**Rigor:** Good. The theoretical framework is formalized as a convex optimization problem with a proved proposition. The synthetic experiments are clean and well-controlled. The LLM probing extends validity beyond toy models. However, the LLM study is explicitly observational and correlational — the authors make no causal claims, which is appropriate but limits mechanistic depth.

**Reproducibility:** Likely high for the synthetic component. The task is simple, the model is small (~5×10⁴ parameters), and training was done on consumer hardware. The LLM probing depends on Qwen2.5-1.5B weights, which are publicly available. Appendix includes supplementary data for N=10 and N=20.

**Significance:** Potentially significant for both cognitive science and AI interpretability communities. It offers a mechanistic grounding for why neural networks might converge on similar representational strategies as biological brains when solving comparable relational tasks.

**Red Flags:**
- Single-author preprint without peer review.
- The LLM probing is limited to one model size (1.5B) and three hand-chosen domains.
- The causal status of the recovered geometry is unclear — the authors acknowledge this and call for future intervention studies.
- "Grokking-like" terminology is used descriptively; the paper does not provide a trajectory-level theory connecting weight decay to description length.

## Strengths

- Elegant theoretical framework connecting L2 regularization to a cognitively meaningful representational structure.
- Careful experimental design that isolates ordinal generalization from local comparisons, directly mirroring a canonical cognitive paradigm.
- Use of graded behavioral measures (confidence, geometric separation) that remain informative even after accuracy saturates.
- Successful validation across both tiny synthetic models and a 1.5B-parameter pretrained LLM.
- Honest and appropriate discussion of limitations (correlational nature, single model, limited domains).
- Clear exposition with well-labeled figures supporting each claim.

## Limitations

- The LLM study is observational and correlational; no causal interventions are performed.
- Only one pretrained model (Qwen2.5-1.5B) and three hand-chosen ordinal domains are tested.
- The theoretical proposition characterizes the regularized optimum but does not describe the optimization trajectory or the transient grokking-like dynamics.
- The attention and MLP sub-layers are abstracted into a single fixed readout direction in the theory, leaving a gap between the idealized analysis and the full Transformer dynamics.
- No exploration of cyclic or partial orders beyond the months domain.
- The work is a single-author preprint without independent peer review.

## Relevance

本文直接关联认知科学与大模型可解释性的交叉领域，为Transformer如何自发形成类似生物认知的序数表征提供了机制性解释，对理解神经网络中的涌现结构与类比推理具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Would causal interventions (e.g., ablating or rotating the recovered ordinal axis) disrupt comparison behavior in the LLM, establishing causality rather than correlation?
2. How does the rank-line geometry scale across model sizes (e.g., 7B, 70B) and training stages — does it strengthen, weaken, or fragment?
3. Can the same geometry be found for partial orders, hierarchical orders, or other non-total ordinal relations?
4. Does the trajectory-level theory of grokking dynamics under weight decay admit a description-length characterization, connecting the transient dynamics to the regularized optimum?
5. Would training the synthetic model without weight decay but with other implicit regularizers (e.g., dropout, early stopping) still produce the 1D manifold?
