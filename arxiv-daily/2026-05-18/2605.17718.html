How does feature learning reshape the function space?

arXiv: 2605.17718

Date: 2026-05-18

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17718

一句话总结： 本文在两层神经网络的高维比例 regime 下，严格证明了一次大步长梯度下降会将特征分布变成一个目标相关的 spiked Gaussian，从而诱导出数据自适应核，选择性地放大与目标方向对齐的特征值并混合顶部径向模态与目标对齐的二次谐波。
TL;DR: Proves that after a single large gradient step in a two-layer network trained on a Gaussian single-index model, the post-update feature distribution is approximated by a target-dependent spiked Gaussian covariance, inducing a data-adaptive kernel that selectively amplifies eigenvalues aligned with the target signal and mixes leading eigenfunctions — providing a precise function-space characterization of early-stage feature learning.

核心贡献

  • 证明了在两层神经网络中，一次大步长梯度下降后，特征分布近似为目标相关的 spiked Gaussian 协方差分布，从而诱导出数据自适应核 $k_1$。
  • 对 $k_1$ 做了 Taylor 展开，证明高阶余项随维度增长趋于零，揭示了特征学习的本质是引入目标依赖的额外核来重塑函数空间。
  • 在 ReLU 激活函数下给出了 $k_1$ 的谱分解显式表达，严格证明了 spike 主要改变算子的顶部特征值和线性特征空间。
  • 发现新的顶部近似特征函数是径向模态 $\|x\|Y_0(\omega)$ 与目标对齐二次谐波 $\|x\|Y_2(\omega, w^*)$ 的混合，且顶部特征值严格大于所有其他特征值。
  • 提供了数据自适应核与真实神经网络之间的桥梁，实验验证了 $k_1$ 的特征对齐和学习性能优于各向同性核 $k_0$。

Key Contributions

  • Prove that after one large gradient descent step on a two-layer network in the high-dimensional proportional regime, the post-update feature distribution is well approximated by a target-dependent spiked Gaussian covariance $\Gamma = AI_d + Bw^(w^)^\top$, inducing a data-adaptive kernel $k_1$ (Theorem 3.1).
  • Establish a Taylor expansion of $k_1$ around an isotropic kernel, showing higher-order remainder terms vanish as $d \to \infty$ (Theorem 4.2). This isolates the spike's contribution to the function space.
  • For ReLU activation, provide an explicit spectral characterization: the spike transforms primarily the top and linear eigenspaces. The new top eigenfunction mixes the radial mode $\|x\|Y_0(\omega)$ with the target-aligned quadratic harmonic $\|x\|Y_2(\omega, w^*)$ (Theorems 5.3, 5.4).
  • Prove the approximate top eigenvalue $\tilde{\lambda}_+$ strictly dominates all other eigenvalues of the perturbed integral operator $T_1$ for sufficiently high dimension, establishing a spectral gap.
  • Demonstrate experimentally that the data-adaptive kernel $k_1$ outperforms the isotropic kernel $k_0$ in both feature alignment and generalization on kernel ridge regression, confirming the theoretical predictions.

Methodology

The paper considers a two-layer neural network $f(x; W, a) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{m}}\sum_{j=1}^m a_j \sigma(w_j^\top x)$ trained on data from a Gaussian single-index model $y = g(\langle w^*, x \rangle) + \epsilon$ with $x \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I_d)$.

Setting: The analysis operates in the high-dimensional proportional regime where $n, m, d \to \infty$ at fixed ratios $\alpha = n/d$, $\beta = m/d$, with step size $\eta = \Theta(d^\zeta)$ for $\zeta \in [1/2, 1)$. A two-stage training scheme is used: (1) one gradient step on the first layer with $n$ samples, (2) ridge regression on the second layer with $N$ samples.

Key theoretical steps:

  1. Spiked Gaussian approximation (Sec. 3): After the gradient step, the weight matrix decomposes as $W_1 = W_0 + \bar{A} + E_t$, where $\bar{A}$ is a rank-one signal matrix and $E_t$ collects vanishing fluctuations. This yields a feature distribution characterized by the spiked covariance $\Gamma = AI_d + Bw^(w^)^\top$ where $A, B$ depend on the step size.
  1. Kernel expansion (Sec. 4): The data-adaptive kernel $k_1(x, x') = \mathbb{E}_{w \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \Gamma/d)}[\sigma(\langle w, x\rangle)\sigma(\langle w, x'\rangle)]$ is expanded as $k_1 = Ak_0 + \frac{B}{2d}S + R$, where $S$ captures the spike contribution and $R$ is a higher-order remainder with $\|T_R\|_{op} = O(B^2/d^2)$.
  1. ReLU spectral analysis (Sec. 5): Using the closed-form arc-cosine kernel and Funk-Hecke theorem, the eigenfunctions of $T_0$ are identified as $\psi_{k,m}(x) = \frac{r}{\sqrt{d}}Y_{k,m}(\omega)$. The spike operator's action on the top eigenspace is reduced to a 2×2 matrix problem in $\text{span}\{rY_0, r\tilde{Y}_2\}$, yielding explicit eigenvalues and mixed eigenfunctions.

Experiments: The paper validates theoretical predictions by comparing the base ReLU kernel $k_0$, the data-adaptive kernel $k_1$, and a ReLU MLP across varying dimensions ($d \in \{50, \ldots, 3200\}$) and spike magnitudes $B$. Metrics include top-eigenvector alignment with the quadratic harmonic $Y_2$ and test MSE for kernel ridge regression.

Results & Highlights

  • The top eigenvector of $k_1$ aligns strongly with the target-aligned quadratic harmonic $Y_2(\omega, w^*)$, while $k_0$ shows no such alignment — confirming that feature learning injects target-dependent structure.
  • Alignment grows with both dimension $d$ and spike magnitude $B$, consistent with the theoretical prediction that the mixing coefficient $\tau_+ \propto B$.
  • The data-adaptive kernel $k_1$ achieves significantly lower test MSE than $k_0$ for kernel ridge regression on a nonlinear target $g(t) = 2t^2 + 3t + 4\sin(2t)$, with the gap widening as $B$ increases.
  • The ReLU MLP's generalization performance closely tracks $k_1$ rather than $k_0$, empirically validating the kernel as a useful proxy for understanding early-stage neural network training.
  • The top eigenvalue of $T_1$ is $\tilde{\lambda}_+ = A\lambda_{\max}(T_0) + \frac{|B|}{2\pi d} + o_d(|B|/d)$, showing feature learning increases the spectral energy in target-aligned directions proportionally to the step size.

Critical Assessment

Novelty (Moderate-High): While the single-index model and one-step gradient descent setting are well-studied, the specific contribution of characterizing the function-space evolution (rather than just parameter-space dynamics) is genuinely new. The kernel expansion and spectral mixing results for ReLU go beyond prior work that focused on rank-one signal extraction.

Rigor (High): The proofs are detailed and complete, with explicit error bounds on remainder terms ($\|T_R\|_{op} = O(B^2/d^2)$) and approximate eigenfunctions ($\|\tilde{\Psi}_+ - \Psi\|_{L^2} = o_d(B/d)$). The assumptions (Gaussian inputs, single-index target, proportional regime) are standard and clearly stated.

Reproducibility (High): All experimental details are provided (seeds, hyperparameters, software versions, hardware). The authors note experiments run on a laptop in under an hour. Code is not explicitly linked but the descriptions are sufficient for reimplementation.

Significance (Moderate): The results are clean and theoretically satisfying, but the setting is restrictive — Gaussian single-index model with one gradient step. The extent to which these insights transfer to deeper networks, multiple training steps, or real data remains unclear. The paper acknowledges this is about "early-stage" feature learning.

Red flags:

  • The two-stage training scheme (one step on layer 1, then ridge regression on layer 2) is a significant simplification of actual neural network training.
  • The analysis is limited to information exponent 1 targets (i.e., $\mathbb{E}[g'(z)] \neq 0$), excluding functions like pure XOR or parity.
  • No comparison with NTK or other adaptive kernel baselines beyond $k_0$.

Strengths

  • Provides the first precise function-space characterization of how feature learning reshapes the kernel induced by a two-layer network, filling a gap between parameter-space analyses and static kernel perspectives.
  • The ReLU-specific spectral results are particularly elegant — showing the top eigenfunction becomes a mixture of radial and target-aligned quadratic components is a clean, interpretable finding.
  • The connection between data-adaptive kernels and neural network generalization is empirically validated with carefully controlled experiments.
  • The paper is self-contained and well-written, with clear motivation and a logical progression from distributional approximation → kernel expansion → spectral analysis.

Limitations

  • Restricted to the Gaussian single-index model with information exponent 1, which represents only a narrow class of learnable functions.
  • Analysis covers only a single gradient step; extending to multi-step or full training dynamics would be significantly more challenging and is left to future work.
  • The proportional regime assumption ($n, m = \Theta(d)$) may not reflect the highly overparameterized setting of practical neural networks.
  • The spiked covariance $\Gamma$ depends on the specific target function through the gradient, making the results data-dependent in a way that limits universal conclusions about the function space.
  • No discussion of how these results relate to deep networks or attention mechanisms.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How do the spectral modifications evolve over multiple gradient descent steps — does the function space converge to a fixed point or continue to deform?
  2. Can the kernel expansion framework be extended to multi-index models or hierarchical target functions beyond information exponent 1?
  3. What happens to the spectral mixing when the activation function is smooth (e.g., erf or tanh) rather than ReLU — are the qualitative conclusions preserved?
  4. Is there a regime where the data-adaptive kernel $k_1$ provides a provably better generalization bound than $k_0$, beyond the empirical evidence presented?
  5. Can the spiked Gaussian approximation be leveraged to design better initialization schemes or learning rate schedules for practical training?
