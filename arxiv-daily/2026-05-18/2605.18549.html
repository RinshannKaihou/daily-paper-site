Monitoring the Internal Monologue: Probe Trajectories Reveal Reasoning Dynamics

arXiv: 2605.18549

Date: 2026-05-18

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18549

一句话总结： 本文提出"探针轨迹"方法，通过在推理模型每个生成token的隐藏状态上运行MIL元探针，利用累积最大池化构建概念概率的连续时间序列信号，并提取六组信号处理特征（统计状态、趋势动态、时间分段、边界瞬态、信号处理、时间地标），在安全监控和数学正确性预测任务上AUROC最高达97%，比静态单点探针提升最多17个百分点。
TL;DR: The authors introduce probe trajectories—continuous time-series signals built by evaluating a multi-layer MIL meta-probe at each generated token using cumulative max-pooling—revealing that internal reasoning dynamics are far more informative than static predictions. Max-pooling is critical (average-pooling collapses to ~50% AUROC), template-based training data matches expensive model-generated data, and extracted signal-processing features boost mathematical error prediction by up to 17 percentage points over static baselines.

核心贡献

  • 提出"探针轨迹"（probe trajectory）框架：利用多层MIL元探针对推理过程逐token评估，通过累积最大池化（cumulative max-pooling）构建概念概率的连续时间序列信号，揭示推理过程中模型内部状态的动态演变。
  • 发现池化操作是关键：平均池化和last-token方法在所有模型上AUROC均约50%（接近随机），而最大池化稳定达到>90% AUROC。作者解释最大池化充当"包络检测器"——一旦有害或错误模式在某个token上强烈激活，最大池化会永久捕获该峰值，而平均池化会被大量中性token稀释。
  • 从探针轨迹中提取六组信号处理特征（全局统计、趋势动态、时间分段、边界瞬态、信号处理、时间地标），在数学推理错误预测上比静态基线提升最高17个百分点。
  • 证明模板训练数据即可达到近乎最优效果：静态模板注入数据与昂贵的模型生成推理轨迹训练数据在AUROC上差距很小（如Qwen3-14B上95.91% vs 97.14%），消除了高成本的推理和标注需求。
  • SHAP分析揭示不同任务由完全不同的特征驱动：安全监控由终态/稳态特征主导，数学错误预测由波动性/动态变化特征主导，证实轨迹编码了任务特异性的行为动态。

Key Contributions

  • Introduce probe trajectories via a multi-layer MIL meta-probe evaluated at every generated token with cumulative max-pooling, framing internal reasoning as a continuous time-series signal rather than a static snapshot.
  • Demonstrate that pooling operation is the critical architectural choice: average-pooling and last-token pooling collapse to ~50% AUROC (random) across all models, while max-pooling consistently achieves >90% AUROC. Max-pooling acts as an "envelope detector" that permanently captures peak activations rather than diluting them.
  • Extract six groups of signal-processing features from trajectories (global statistics, trend dynamics, temporal segmentation, boundary transients, signal processing, temporal landmarks) that boost mathematical error prediction by up to 17 percentage points over static baselines.
  • Show that template-based training data achieves near-parity with exact model-generated responses (e.g., 95.91% vs 97.14% on Qwen3-14B), eliminating costly dynamic inference and labeling pipelines.
  • Reveal via SHAP analysis that safety monitoring and math error prediction are driven by entirely disjoint feature sets: safety relies on terminal/steady-state features (where the trajectory settles), while math relies on volatility/dynamics features (trajectory instability), confirming task-specific temporal encoding.

Methodology

The paper proposes a pipeline for monitoring Large Reasoning Models (LRMs) through their hidden states rather than surface-level Chain of Thought (CoT) text, which is shown to be unfaithful in 5–10% of cases.

Training data curation (3 pipelines):

  • Template-Based: Dataset samples injected into a predefined template asking whether a concept is present, with inherited ground-truth labels.
  • Messages-Based: Raw messages passed through the model's chat template with source labels.
  • Exact Model Messages-Based: Full CoT and final responses generated by the target model, labeled by actual model behavior.

Probe architecture:

  • A 3-layer MLP (hidden sizes [1024, 512, 256], GELU activations) processes per-layer hidden states.
  • A Multiple Instance Learning (MIL) meta-probe aggregates across every-second-layer representations via a learned meta-layer, eliminating manual layer selection.
  • For trajectory generation, global max-pooling is replaced with cumulative max-pooling: at step t, the representation is max over all tokens up to t, ensuring monotonic non-decreasing latent features and stable trajectories.

Trajectory feature extraction (6 groups):

  1. Global Statistical State: mean, max, variance, IQR, RMS, last-to-max ratio.
  2. Shape and Trend Dynamics: linear/quadratic trend fitting, running-mean slopes, drawdown/recovery metrics.
  3. Temporal Segmentation: tertile-based phase decomposition of CoT with inter-phase deltas.
  4. Boundary Transients: localized volatility at prompt-to-CoT transition.
  5. Signal Processing and Sustained Intents: peak detection, dwell-time run-lengths, autocorrelation, mean-crossing rates.
  6. Temporal and Relational Landmarks: argmax positions, mean/max ratios between prompt and CoT.

Models evaluated: Llama-8B-R1-Distill, Qwen3-4B/8B/14B. Datasets: WildGuardMix, Aegis (safety), ProcessBench, GSM8K, MATH (math). Evaluation: 3-fold CV on test splits as diagnostic upper bound; Random Forest selected as best trajectory classifier.

Results & Highlights

  • Max-pooling is essential (Table 1): Template-based max-pooled probes achieve 94–97% AUROC on harmfulness detection, while average-pooling and last-token consistently score ~33–63% (near-random). LLM-as-a-Judge on CoT text peaks at only ~84%.
  • Template training is sufficient: Template-based max-pooling (95.91% on Qwen3-14B) nearly matches Exact Model Messages (97.14%), often outperforming Messages-Based data.
  • Trajectory features outperform static probes on unfaithful CoT detection (Figure 4): trajectory-based classifiers achieve higher detection rates for harmful responses with safe-looking CoT across all models.
  • OOD generalization is robust (Figure 5, 11): trajectory features remain stable on Aegis (OOD) while static probes degrade significantly. Zero-shot transfer from WildGuardTest to Aegis shows trajectory classifiers match or exceed static baselines.
  • Math error prediction gains up to +17pp: trajectory features boost GSM8K AUROC by up to 17 percentage points over static baselines, particularly for Qwen3 family models.
  • 5% of CoT suffices for math, full CoT needed for safety (Figure 7): math error trajectories manifest instability immediately, while harmfulness accumulates signal over time.
  • Leave-one-category-out generalization on MATH subcategories confirms trajectory features transfer across problem types (Figure 8a).
  • Engineered features outperform 1D CNN (Appendix E.4): hand-crafted trajectory features beat trainable extractors on all evaluated scenarios.
  • Probe size: 50M–80M parameters depending on model; training uses ~3000 GPU-hours total on A100 GPUs.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The probe trajectory concept is a genuine contribution—framing hidden-state probing as a continuous signal and applying signal-processing analysis is novel and well-motivated. However, the individual components (linear probes, max-pooling, template data, Random Forest classifiers) are not individually novel. The MIL meta-probe is a straightforward concatenation of per-layer probes.
  • Rigor: The evaluation is thorough across 4 models, 5 datasets, 2 domains, with extensive ablations (pooling, training data, CoT length, feature groups, LOO generalization, CNN comparison). However, the 3-fold CV on test data is explicitly acknowledged as a diagnostic upper bound, not a deployed system evaluation. The gap between this diagnostic and practical deployment performance is not quantified.
  • Reproducibility: Template-based training removes the need for expensive model-specific data. The pooling and feature extraction are simple to implement. Code is promised. Layer indices and hyperparameters are fully specified.
  • Significance: The finding that average-pooling fails catastrophically while max-pooling succeeds is extremely important for the probing community and challenges common practice. The demonstration that CoT unfaithfulness can be detected through internal states is practically significant for AI safety.
  • Red flags: (1) Evaluated models are only up to 14B parameters—scalability to 70B+ or proprietary frontier models is unknown. (2) Only two behavioral domains tested (safety, math). (3) Harmfulness labels rely on the WildGuard classifier, introducing label noise. (4) The diagnostic 3-fold CV protocol may overestimate real-world performance. (5) The probe itself has 50–80M parameters, which is non-trivial overhead. (6) Running a probe at every token during generation adds computational cost not thoroughly analyzed.

Strengths

  • Novel and well-motivated temporal framing of representation probing.
  • The pooling ablation is a striking and important finding that challenges community defaults.
  • Template-based training sufficiency greatly reduces practical barriers to deployment.
  • Extensive evaluation across models, datasets, and domains with thorough ablations.
  • SHAP analysis revealing task-specific feature importance is insightful.
  • Unfaithful CoT detection is a practically important capability for AI safety.
  • Clear, well-structured paper with comprehensive appendices.

Limitations

  • Only evaluated on models up to 14B parameters; scalability to larger models is unknown.
  • Two behavioral domains only (safety, math); generalization to deception, sycophancy, hallucination is untested.
  • 3-fold CV on test data measures inherent separability, not practical monitoring performance—the gap could be substantial.
  • Per-token probe inference adds computational overhead not analyzed.
  • Harmfulness labels from WildGuard classifier introduce potential label noise.
  • The MIL meta-probe requires extracting hidden states from every-second-layer, which is storage-intensive for long reasoning chains.
  • The trajectory features are hand-crafted; whether learned representations could do better is only partially addressed (1D CNN comparison).

Relevance

本文提出的探针轨迹方法为监控大语言推理模型的内部状态提供了全新视角，与模型安全性和可解释性的研究高度相关。最大池化远优于平均池化的发现对未来设计representation probing实验具有直接指导意义，而模板训练数据即可达到近似最优效果则大幅降低了实际部署门槛。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How do probe trajectories behave on 70B+ parameter models—does the max-pooling advantage persist, and does trajectory stability scale with model capacity?
  • Can trajectory features be used for real-time intervention (e.g., halting generation at trajectory inflection points) rather than post-hoc monitoring?
  • What is the latency overhead of per-token probe evaluation during deployment, and can it be reduced via sparse trajectory sampling?
  • How robust are probe trajectories to adversarial attacks specifically designed to manipulate hidden states while evading max-pooled probes?
  • Could trajectory features from safety probing transfer to detecting other behavioral failures (deception, sycophancy, hallucination) without domain-specific retraining?
  • Does the cumulative max-pooling's monotonicity assumption hold for all concept types, or are there concepts where decreasing probability is also informative?
