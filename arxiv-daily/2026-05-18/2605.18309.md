> **一句话总结：** 本文定义了一个可计算的对齐分数，推导其在SFT微调下的闭式更新公式，将对齐变化分解为"反弹力"（由后验分布狭窄程度调控）和"驱动力"（由训练分布与条件后验的匹配程度决定），并预测和验证了"排练启动效应"——先前对齐留下的后验印记使重新暴露时再对齐更快。

> **TL;DR:** Derives a closed-form update rule for a tractable alignment score during LLM fine-tuning under the empirical NTK (eNTK) framework, decomposing alignment changes into a Rebound Force (self-interaction modulated by posterior narrowness) and a Driving Force (training-signal projection onto the aligned/non-aligned contrastive direction). Predicts a Rehearsal Priming Effect—prior alignment leaves posterior residue that accelerates re-alignment upon re-exposure. Validated on Llama-3.1-8B, Gemma-2-2B, and Qwen3-8B across safety, emergent misalignment, and sentiment settings.

## 核心贡献

- 定义了对齐分数 $S(\theta)$（模型对齐补全的期望概率），并推导其在SFT微调下基于经验神经正切核（eNTK）的闭式演化公式，首次统一了参数空间学习动力学与函数空间对齐行为。
- 将对齐更新分解为两个竞争分量：**反弹力**（模型自身分布的自交互项，强度由对齐状态和后验分布狭窄程度 $\tilde{\pi}^{\top} K \tilde{\pi}$ 共同调控）和**驱动力**（训练分布 $p$ 在对齐/非对齐条件后验对比方向上的投影）。
- 揭示了反弹力的**双向不稳定性**：对齐状态和失对齐状态在后续微调下均不稳定——安全对齐容易被有害数据打破，但新兴失对齐也可被良性数据快速消除。
- 预测并验证了**排练启动效应（Rehearsal Priming Effect）**：先前的对齐训练在模型后验中留下潜在印记，即使表面安全行为已被后续微调削弱，重新暴露于对齐数据时驱动力被放大，导致更快的再对齐。
- 通过构造狭窄程度梯度（固定模板→多样化模板→标准→风格多样化），在受控实验中验证了反弹力强度对后验狭窄程度的预测依赖关系。

## Key Contributions

- Define a tractable alignment score $S(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{x \sim p(x)}[\sum_{y} \mathbb{I}_{(x,y) \in A} \cdot \pi_\theta(y|x)]$ and derive its closed-form evolution under SFT via the empirical NTK, unifying parameter-space and function-space alignment dynamics.
- Decompose alignment updates into a **Rebound Force** (self-interaction modulated by posterior narrowness $\tilde{\pi}^{+\top} K \tilde{\pi}^+$ and $\tilde{\pi}^{-\top} K \tilde{\pi}^-$) and a **Driving Force** (projection of training distribution onto the contrastive direction $\tilde{\pi}^+ - \tilde{\pi}^-$).
- Demonstrate **bidirectional instability**: both aligned and misaligned states are unstable under subsequent fine-tuning—safety can be broken by harmful data, but emergent misalignment can also be rapidly mitigated by benign data.
- Predict and validate the **Rehearsal Priming Effect**: prior alignment leaves posterior residue that amplifies the effective Driving Force upon re-exposure, producing faster re-alignment even after observed safety behavior has been suppressed.
- Validate the predicted dependence of rebound strength on posterior narrowness via controlled experiments with four diversity levels of safety-alignment data.

## Methodology

The paper builds its framework in three stages:

1. **Token-level Decomposition (Lemma 2.4)**: The change in alignment score $\Delta S$ is decomposed into per-token contributions, gated by prefix probabilities $\pi_l^<(y_{<l})$ and future alignment potentials $q_{>l}^+(y_l)$. This reveals that earlier tokens have larger influence on alignment (consistent with shallow alignment hypotheses).

2. **Logit-space Geometry (Theorem 2.7)**: Using Bayes' rule, the future alignment potential is converted into a local contrast between aligned conditional distribution $\tilde{\pi}_l^+$ and non-aligned conditional distribution $\tilde{\pi}_l^-$, yielding $\Delta S \approx \sum_l \pi_l^< \cdot \pi_{S,l}^+ \pi_{S,l}^- (\tilde{\pi}_l^+ - \tilde{\pi}_l^-)^\top \Delta z_l$. The alignment uncertainty factor $\pi_{S,l}^+ \pi_{S,l}^-$ peaks at maximum entropy.

3. **SFT Alignment Dynamics (Theorem 2.9)**: Under the Relatively Stable Kernel (RSK) assumption from prior work, SFT gradient updates are projected through the eNTK kernel $K$, decomposing $\Delta \bar{S}$ into:
   - **Rebound Force** $F_{\text{rebound}} = -\pi_{S}^+ (\tilde{\pi}^+ - \tilde{\pi}^-)^\top K \tilde{\pi}^+ + \pi_{S}^- (\tilde{\pi}^+ - \tilde{\pi}^-)^\top K \tilde{\pi}^-$ — self-interaction resisting distributional shifts.
   - **Driving Force** $F_{\text{drive}} = (\tilde{\pi}^+ - \tilde{\pi}^-)^\top K p$ — external training signal pulling alignment.

For the identity kernel case, Rebound Force simplifies to $-\pi_S^+ \sum \tilde{\pi}_i^{+2} + \pi_S^- \sum \tilde{\pi}_i^{-2}$ (where $\sum \pi_i^2$ measures distributional purity), and Driving Force to $\langle \tilde{\pi}^+ - \tilde{\pi}^-, p \rangle$.

**Experimental setup**: Multi-stage SFT paradigm (Stage 1: Forward alignment → Stage 2: Reverse perturbation → Stage 3: Re-exposure). Full-parameter fine-tuning on Llama-3.1-8B, Gemma-2-2B, and Qwen3-8B-Base with AdamW (lr=5e-7), 4×A800 GPUs, max sequence length 512. Evaluation via ShieldGemma-9B (safety/misalignment) and Sentiment RoBERTa (sentiment). Score-matching protocol ensures Stage 3 starts from baseline-equivalent checkpoints ($|S - S_0| < 0.005$).

## Results & Highlights

- **Rebound Effect (Figure 2)**: Rapid rebound to baseline across all forward fine-tuning durations in Stage 2, invariant to Stage 1 training steps. Observed bidirectionally—both safety-aligned and misaligned states are unstable.
- **Narrowness dependence (Figure 3)**: Narrower training distributions (Fixed Template) produce lower convergence safety scores in Stage 1 and faster safety degradation in Stage 2, confirming $\tilde{\pi}^\top K \tilde{\pi}$ modulates rebound strength. This holds under both standard reverse (HEx-PHI) and task-agnostic reverse (Alpaca).
- **Rehearsal Priming (Figure 4)**: Deeper Stage 1 exposure systematically accelerates Stage 3 re-alignment across all four scenarios (BeaverTails, emergent misalignment, bidirectional IMDb sentiment) and all three model architectures.
- **Emergent misalignment implication**: Models exhibiting emergent misalignment can be rapidly re-misaligned after benign re-alignment—only a few steps of malicious fine-tuning trigger full re-emergence, highlighting inherent fragility.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The unified decomposition into Rebound/Driving forces with closed-form expressions under eNTK is novel. The Rehearsal Priming Effect is a genuinely new prediction. The bidirectional instability observation (misaligned states are also unstable) is an important and underappreciated insight.
- **Rigor**: Strong mathematical foundation with complete proofs. The RSK assumption is empirically motivated by prior work but its validity at industrial scale remains an acknowledged limitation. The score-matching protocol for Stage 3 initialization is methodologically careful.
- **Reproducibility**: Excellent. All hyperparameters (lr=5e-7, batch size 4, 8192 samples per stage, max seq len 512), datasets, and evaluation protocols are fully specified. Code and data are released.
- **Significance**: High. The framework provides a principled way to reason about alignment fragility and recovery. The narrowness result has immediate practical implications (use diverse alignment data). The Rehearsal Priming finding suggests alignment training is never fully "forgotten," which is both reassuring and concerning.
- **Red flags**: (1) Only tested on 2B–8B models; scaling to 70B+ is unverified. (2) The eNTK assumption may break for longer fine-tuning or larger models. (3) The framework currently only covers SFT, not DPO/RLHF—though Lemma 2.8 is more general. (4) The alignment score $S$ is evaluated by external classifiers (ShieldGemma-9B), not computed directly from model internals, creating a gap between theory and measurement. (5) Some key experiments (Figures 2, 4) lack error bars due to computational cost, as acknowledged.

## Strengths

- Elegant mathematical framework that bridges parameter-space and function-space perspectives with interpretable decompositions.
- Three distinct experimental domains (safety, misalignment, sentiment) with three model architectures provide strong evidence of generality.
- The controlled narrowness experiment (four diversity levels) is a clean test of a specific theoretical prediction.
- Practical implications are immediately actionable: diverse alignment data reduces rebound; prior alignment training leaves persistent beneficial traces.
- The Rehearsal Priming Effect has concerning implications for emergent misalignment—misaligned states can be rapidly reactivated.

## Limitations

- The eNTK stability assumption may not hold for large-scale industrial fine-tuning or very long training runs.
- Only SFT is analyzed; extending to DPO, PPO, and other RLHF methods is left as future work.
- Model scale is limited to 2B–8B parameters; behavior at 70B+ is unknown.
- The alignment score is measured via external classifiers rather than computed from the model's internal $\tilde{\pi}^+$ and $\tilde{\pi}^-$ distributions, creating an indirect connection between theory and experiment.
- Some main experiments lack error bars (acknowledged but still a weakness for a theory-validation paper).
- The paper does not propose methods to *prevent* alignment reversal—only explains its dynamics.

## Relevance

本文建立的对齐动力学统一框架直接回应了LLM安全对齐脆弱性的核心问题。反弹力对数据多样性的依赖表明应使用多样化数据进行安全训练，排练启动效应则揭示了先前对齐的持久影响，这些发现对设计更鲁棒的对齐训练协议具有直接指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the framework extend to DPO/PPO-based alignment, given that Lemma 2.8 is not SFT-specific?
- Can the Rebound Force be suppressed via explicit regularization on $\tilde{\pi}^\top K \tilde{\pi}$ during alignment training?
- Does the Rehearsal Priming Effect persist across many cycles of alignment/disalignment, or does the posterior residue attenuate?
- How do the dynamics change for much larger models (70B+) where the eNTK may be less stable?
- What are the implications for watermarking or cryptographic methods that aim to make alignment irreversibly persistent?
- Could the framework inform adaptive fine-tuning strategies that detect and counteract rebound in real-time?
