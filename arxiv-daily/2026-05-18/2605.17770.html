Entropy-Gradient Inversion: Moving Toward Internal Mechanism of Large Reasoning Models

arXiv: 2605.17770

Date: 2026-05-18

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17770

一句话总结： 本文发现大型推理模型（LRM）中存在"熵-梯度反转"现象——token预测熵与logit梯度之间呈强负相关，这是推理能力的几何指纹；基于此提出CorR-PO算法，将该信号作为RL奖励正则化项，在多个数学推理基准上稳定超越GRPO/DAPO等SOTA方法。
TL;DR: The paper identifies Entropy-Gradient Inversion — a robust negative correlation between token entropy and logit gradients unique to reasoning models — and proposes CorR-PO, which embeds this geometric fingerprint as an RL reward regularizer, consistently outperforming SOTA baselines across multiple reasoning benchmarks and model scales.

核心贡献

  • 发现并形式化了"熵-梯度反转"现象：在大型推理模型中，token级别的预测熵与logit梯度核范数之间存在强负相关（Spearman ρ ≈ −0.65），而基础模型和安全对齐模型则呈现弱负相关甚至正相关，该现象可作为推理能力的唯一几何指纹。
  • 刻画了该现象的训练动力学：熵-梯度反转在SFT阶段迅速涌现（前200步即发生相变），随后在RL阶段进一步强化；纯RL训练（无SFT预热）则表现出不稳定振荡，仅达到较弱的反转效果。
  • 提出CorR-PO算法：基于GRPO框架，将熵-梯度反转的Spearman相关系数转化为奖励正则化项 R_corr = −(1 + ρ_{E,I})，引导模型的潜空间在RL训练早期就形成"慢思考"结构，减少对外部验证器的依赖。
  • 跨模型、跨规模的实验验证：在Qwen2.5-7B-Math、Qwen2.5-14B、Qwen3-4B、Qwen3-1.7B上均优于GRPO、DAPO、Dr.GRPO、GSPO等SOTA基线。

Key Contributions

  • Identifies and formalizes "Entropy-Gradient Inversion": In LRMs, token-level prediction entropy and logit gradient nuclear norm exhibit a strong negative Spearman correlation (ρ ≈ −0.65), whereas base and safety-aligned models show weak negative or positive correlations — this serves as a unique geometric fingerprint of reasoning capability.
  • Characterizes training dynamics of the inversion: The inversion emerges rapidly during SFT (phase transition within ~200 steps) and is further strengthened by subsequent RL; pure RL without SFT warm-up suffers instability and achieves only weak inversion.
  • Proposes CorR-PO: Embeds the inversion signature into GRPO's reward via R_corr = −(1 + ρ_{E,I}), steering the model's latent space toward "slow thinking" structures early in training.
  • Comprehensive empirical validation: Outperforms GRPO, DAPO, Dr.GRPO, and GSPO across Qwen2.5-7B-Math, Qwen2.5-14B, Qwen3-4B, and Qwen3-1.7B on AIME24, MATH500, and GSM8k.

Methodology

The work proceeds in two phases: analysis and optimization.

Phase 1 — Entropy-Gradient Inversion Analysis:

  • For each generated token t_i at layer l, the authors compute the gradient matrices G_{X,l} (X ∈ {Q, K, V, O} projection heads) and measure their intensity via the nuclear norm (L1 norm of singular values), averaged across all layers to obtain gradient influence Ī_t.
  • Simultaneously, Shannon entropy E_i of the predictive distribution P(v|x_{1:i-1}) quantifies prediction uncertainty.
  • The Spearman rank correlation ρ between {E_i} and {Ī_t} across unique tokens reveals the Entropy-Gradient Inversion: only reasoning models (e.g., DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B) show ρ ≈ −0.65.
  • A mathematical derivation (Appendix D) shows that low-entropy states mathematically necessitate high gradient norms via the chain: H ↓ → z_t ↑ → ‖h‖ ↑ → I ↑, formally justifying the negative correlation.

Phase 2 — CorR-PO Algorithm:

  • Segments the generated token sequence into N reasoning sentences and computes Step Average Entropy E_i per sentence.
  • Computes the Spearman correlation ρ_{E,I} between step entropy and gradient influence arrays.
  • Designs a correlation regularization reward R_corr = −(1 + ρ_{E,I}) ∈ [−2, 0], which is always non-positive and never competes with the accuracy reward.
  • The total reward R_total = R_acc + λ_corr · R_corr is used with standard GRPO's group-relative advantage estimation and PPO-style clipped objective.
  • This penalizes non-negative correlations (associated with "fast thinking") and encourages the model to develop reasoning-aligned latent structures.

Results & Highlights

  • Qwen2.5-7B-Math: CorR-PO achieves 69.4% average performance, outperforming GSPO (68.6%) by 0.8pp and GRPO (67.0%) by 2.4pp. Notably achieves 56.7% on AIME24 Pass@16 vs. GRPO's 50.0%.
  • Qwen2.5-14B: CorR-PO reaches 72.9% average, surpassing Dr.GRPO (71.5%) by 1.4pp, with leading results on MATH500 Pass@1 (75.6%) and GSM8k Pass@16 (98.3%).
  • Qwen3-4B: Ties GRPO at 79.8% average but leads on GSM8k Pass@1 (93.4%) and Major@16 (95.7%).
  • Qwen3-1.7B: Achieves 67.1% average, second only to GRPO (68.6%) and clearly outperforming GSPO (63.5%) and Dr.GRPO (62.4%).
  • Training dynamics: CorR-PO drives Spearman correlation from −0.171 to −0.363 over 1000 steps (vs. GRPO's −0.301), with average performance improving monotonically to 70.1 vs. GRPO's 66.0.
  • Layer-wise ablation: All 28 individual layers improve over base model; deeper layers provide stronger supervision, with full multi-layer aggregation outperforming any single-layer variant.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The Entropy-Gradient Inversion phenomenon has not been previously reported, and using it as an intrinsic RL reward signal is a genuinely new idea. The connection between entropy and gradient norms is elegantly derived.
  • Rigor: Strong formal grounding with mathematical derivation in Appendix D. Controlled experiments compare models with the same architecture but different training objectives. Cross-architecture validation on both Qwen and Llama families.
  • Reproducibility: Good. Uses publicly available models, datasets, and provides code. All hyperparameters specified. LoRA settings detailed.
  • Significance: The inversion phenomenon itself is a potentially important finding for understanding LRM internals. CorR-PO's practical gains, however, are modest (0.8–2.4pp over SOTA baselines on average) and not uniformly dominant across all metrics and model sizes (e.g., Qwen3-4B ties GRPO; Qwen3-1.7B slightly trails GRPO).
  • Red flags:
  • No error bars or statistical significance tests acknowledged by the authors themselves (Question 7 in the checklist: answered [No]).
  • Evaluated exclusively on mathematical reasoning benchmarks (AIME24, MATH500, GSM8k) — generalization to other reasoning domains (code, science, commonsense) is unverified.
  • The correlation regularization adds per-step gradient computation overhead during RL training, but computational cost analysis is not provided.
  • AIME24 has only 30 problems, making Pass@1 results highly sensitive to individual samples.

Strengths

  • Discovers a previously unknown geometric signature (Entropy-Gradient Inversion) unique to reasoning models.
  • Provides thorough training dynamics analysis showing the inversion emerges in SFT and strengthens in RL.
  • The CorR-PO method is conceptually simple — just adding a Spearman correlation penalty to GRPO's reward — yet effective.
  • Cross-architecture validation (Qwen2.5, Qwen3, Llama3.1) strengthens generality claims.
  • Comprehensive appendices with layer-wise ablations, per-step training curves, and detailed mathematical derivations.

Limitations

  • Only evaluated on mathematical reasoning benchmarks; generalization to coding, scientific reasoning, or multi-modal tasks is unknown.
  • Gains over SOTA baselines are modest and not uniformly consistent across all model scales and metrics.
  • No error bars or multi-seed confidence intervals; AIME24's small sample size (30 problems) makes results noisy.
  • The gradient influence computation (nuclear norm of attention projection gradients across all layers) adds significant per-step overhead during training, but computational cost is not quantified.
  • The causal claim that "stronger inversion causes better reasoning" is supported only by correlation, not by interventional evidence.

Relevance

本文揭示了大型推理模型内部"熵-梯度反转"的几何指纹，与通过强化学习优化LLM推理能力的方向高度契合。CorR-PO将模型内部机制信号融入RL奖励的设计思路，对理解LRMs的"慢思考"机制及改进RL训练稳定性都有直接参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does Entropy-Gradient Inversion appear in models trained for non-mathematical reasoning tasks (e.g., code generation, scientific QA, or open-domain reasoning)?
  • Can the inversion signal be used as a training-free diagnostic to predict whether a given checkpoint has strong reasoning capability before running expensive evaluations?
  • What is the computational overhead of computing gradient influence during RL training, and can it be approximated efficiently (e.g., using only top-layer gradients)?
  • Does the inversion phenomenon survive model merging, quantization, or distillation, or is it fragile to post-training transformations?
  • Can the correlation regularization be combined with other RL improvements (e.g., DAPO's decoupled clipping or GSPO's sequence-level ratios) for additive gains?
