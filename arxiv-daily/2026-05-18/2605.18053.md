> **一句话总结：** 本文系统性地揭示了全局容量限制下KV缓存驱逐的共同脆弱性——缺少对提示词前后边界位置的结构性保护时，所有七种驱逐策略均导致输出质量崩溃至接近零；仅需保留10%缓存容量给前缀和后缀位置即可恢复69–90%的完整缓存质量，而不同评分策略之间的差异远小于保护效果，说明结构性保护是首要因素。

> **TL;DR:** In a shared globally capped decode-time KV eviction harness, all seven tested policies (LRU, H2O, SnapKV, StreamingLLM, Ada-KV, QUEST, Random) collapse to near-zero quality without structural protection of prompt boundaries. Reserving just 10% of cache at each end recovers 69–90% of full-cache F1 across seven models at 13% retention. Once boundaries are guarded, scoring differences become secondary—most policies converge to within 0.02 F1 of each other.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示结构性脆弱性：** 在没有边界保护的全局容量限制下，七种驱逐策略（包括随机驱逐）在六个纯Transformer模型上全部崩溃至F1≤0.064，这是因为驱逐破坏了指令分隔符、问题边界和注意力汇聚点等结构性锚点。
- **提出极简修复方案：** 仅需在KV缓存前后各保留10%容量作为保护区域（共20%），即可将所有策略从灾难性失败恢复至完整缓存质量的69–90%（C=256，13%保留率下），实现代码改动极小且无额外开销。
- **解耦保护效应与评分效应：** 在保护机制到位后，简化的注意力评分策略在K=32时与LRU统计等价（TOST ∆=0.02），在K=8时四种注意力策略彼此等价但仅比LRU高出0.011–0.021 F1，说明评分差异是二阶效应。
- **跨架构验证：** 结果在十个模型（1.5B–27B参数、五个厂商、GQA/MHA/DeltaNet混合架构）上复现，并延伸至11K–64K上下文长度、多文档摘要、NIAH检索及随机采样场景。
- **否定学习型驱逐的收益：** 线性信用估计器在保护到位后无法超越简单LRU，表明结构性问题解决后剩余信号不足以支撑学习型方法。

## Key Contributions

- **Structural vulnerability identification:** Demonstrates that seven eviction policies universally collapse without prompt-boundary protection, producing degenerate repetition loops (95% 4-gram repetition rate) rather than merely wrong answers.
- **Trivially simple fix with massive impact:** Bilateral protection (10% prefix + 10% suffix) transforms catastrophic failure into 69–90% ceiling recovery at C=256; the fix is a set-membership check with negligible overhead.
- **Deconfounding study:** Isolates the structural-protection effect from scoring-heuristic effects under a common global-cap harness, revealing that prior apparent scoring differences were largely confounded by implicit boundary protection in evaluation setups.
- **Two-factor model of KV cache quality:** (1) structural protection of prompt boundaries is the primary determinant; (2) minimally adaptive middle-context retention is sufficient— even Random eviction recovers ~80% of ceiling under protection.
- **Cross-architecture generalization at scale:** Validates across 10 models (GQA K=2–8, full MHA K=32, DeltaNet-GQA hybrid), five vendor families, context lengths up to 64K, and both QA and summarization tasks.

## Methodology

The study evaluates under a **globally capped decode-time regime**: the full context is prefilled into KV cache, then physically evicted to capacity C during autoregressive decoding every τ=8 steps. Seven policies are tested: LRU, H2O (cumulative attention), SnapKV (attention + observation window), StreamingLLM-style sink+window, Ada-KV (adaptive per-head budget), QUEST (query-aware current attention), and Random.

**Structural protection** reserves ⌈0.10·C⌉ positions at each end (prefix and suffix, minimum 4 per side). Protected positions are excluded from the evictable set. The key methodological contribution is the **deconfounding design**: all policies share the same harness with and without protection, enabling clean attribution of quality to protection vs. scoring.

**Benchmarks:** Balanced LongBench subset (N=162, six QA subtasks, ~1.9K avg tokens), extended to 11K and 32K contexts, plus NIAH retrieval at 32K and 64K. Quality measured by token-level F1 with 95% bootstrap CIs. Statistical testing uses paired Wilcoxon signed-rank tests, Holm-Bonferroni correction, and TOST equivalence tests with pre-specified ∆=0.02.

**Models:** 10 models from 5 families—Qwen2.5 (1.5B/3B/7B, GQA K=2–4), Phi-3.5 (3.8B, MHA K=32), Mistral-7B (GQA K=8), Yi-1.5-6B (GQA K=4), Qwen3.5-27B (DeltaNet+GQA hybrid), Qwen3-4B, Phi-4-mini, Gemma-3-4B-IT.

**Policies evaluated in two modes:** simplified variants (scoring criterion isolated under common global-ranking harness) and faithful reproductions (preserving per-head budget allocation from original papers).

## Results & Highlights

- **Catastrophic failure without protection:** All seven policies produce F1≤0.064 on six pure-transformer models at C=256; best unprotected policy (H2O) reaches only 12% of ceiling.
- **Protection transforms quality:** LRU+prot at C=256 goes from 0.011 → 0.282 F1 on Qwen2.5-3B (89% of ceiling), a 25× improvement using only 13% of cache memory.
- **Protection dominates scoring:** The protection lift (~0.11–0.14 F1) is 3–5× larger than the per-head allocation benefit (~0.03–0.04 F1 from faithful Ada-KV/QUEST).
- **Policy convergence under protection:** At K=32 (Phi-3.5), all four attention-based policies are TOST-equivalent to LRU; at K=8 (Mistral-7B), they are pairwise equivalent but collectively beat LRU by 0.011–0.021 F1.
- **10% protection is the sweet spot:** Sensitivity sweep shows 5% captures 78% of ceiling, 10% captures 89%, and 15–20% provides no significant further gain.
- **Bilateral protection is necessary:** Both prefix and suffix guards are independently required; prefix-only or suffix-only at matched budget recovers only 55–61% vs. 90% for bilateral.
- **Cross-context scaling:** Protection effect persists at 11K (85% recovery), 32K (63–112% recovery), and 64K (weaker but positive: LRU+prot 39.8% vs. 34.7% without).
- **Regime-transfer:** Protection lifts are near-identical across decode-time and prefill-time eviction (ratio 0.99–1.00 on NIAH-32K).
- **Negative result on learned eviction:** A 4-feature linear online credit estimator adds zero measurable benefit over LRU+protection across five tested capacities.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The core insight—that structural boundary protection dominates scoring heuristics in globally capped KV eviction—is both surprising and practically important. No prior work isolated this confound. The deconfounding methodology is clean and the two-factor model of KV cache quality is a genuinely new framing. The negative result on learned eviction is also novel and informative.

**Rigor (High):** Extensive statistical testing (paired Wilcoxon, TOST, Holm-Bonferroni, bootstrap CIs). Large-scale cross-architecture validation (10 models, 5 families, K=2 to K=32). Code audit of reference implementations strengthens the vulnerability claim. Scale-up robustness check at N=481 confirms main results. Sensitivity analysis across protection levels. Ablation of prefix-only vs. suffix-only.

**Reproducibility (High):** All checkpoints publicly available on HuggingFace with exact IDs listed. Code and per-item JSONL outputs in public repository. Greedy decoding (deterministic) for main experiments. Fixed seed for stochastic runs. Detailed hyperparameters and infrastructure specs.

**Significance (High):** Immediate practical impact—adding boundary guards to any KV eviction implementation is trivial code with large quality gains. Challenges the trajectory of the field toward increasingly sophisticated scoring heuristics. Reframes the problem as primarily about what to protect rather than what to evict.

**Red flags / concerns:**
- Single-author paper without institutional peer review; the breadth of experiments is impressive but raises questions about independent verification of the full matrix.
- The globally capped decode-time regime, while matching prior evaluations, differs from production serving setups (PagedAttention, streaming prefill). The authors acknowledge this but the transferability to real deployments remains a hypothesis.
- The K=2 convergence result (Qwen2.5-3B) is somewhat trivial—attention scores produce identical eviction under low head diversity—and the authors rightly treat it as a sanity check rather than primary evidence.
- The 64K results show the protection effect weakens substantially, and the picture becomes more nuanced (H2O reversal, per-head scoring matters more), somewhat qualifying the "protection is all you need" framing.
- The attention-mass pilot uses only N=30 on one model; a broader analysis would strengthen the mechanistic explanation.

## Strengths

- Clean, systematic deconfounding study design isolating protection from scoring effects.
- Exceptional breadth of evaluation: 10 models, 7 policies, 5 context lengths, multiple tasks.
- Strong statistical rigor with appropriate corrections for multiple comparisons.
- Practical takeaway is immediately actionable: a few lines of code for significant quality recovery.
- Honest reporting of negative results (learned eviction, 64K limitations, H2O reversal).
- Code-level audit of reference implementations confirms vulnerability is not a harness artifact.
- Two-factor model provides a useful conceptual framework for the field.

## Limitations

- **Regime scope:** Results are specific to globally capped decode-time eviction; transfer to page-level serving, streaming prefill, and multi-tenant scheduling is claimed but untested.
- **Task coverage:** No evaluation on code generation, multi-turn dialogue, or structured output tasks.
- **Multi-turn protection:** The bilateral prefix/suffix rule does not generalize to interleaved turn boundaries in multi-turn chat.
- **64K weakening:** At extreme compression (6.3% retention, 64K tokens), protection helps but recovery is modest and per-head scoring becomes more important, qualifying the headline claim.
- **Single-author:** All experiments, code, and analysis by one researcher; independent replication would strengthen confidence.
- **Attention analysis limited:** The attention-mass decomposition uses a small pilot (N=30) on one model; broader analysis would strengthen the mechanistic account.
- **MoE and MLA untested:** Mixture-of-experts and multi-latent-attention architectures are not evaluated.
- **Proportional vs. absolute protection:** The 10% fractional rule may over-allocate at large C; an absolute-slot rule may be more practical for very long contexts.

## Relevance

这项工作对LLM推理效率研究具有直接意义。KV缓存驱逐是长上下文模型部署的核心瓶颈，本文揭示的结构性保护效应为所有KV缓存管理方法提供了基础性的设计原则。发现复杂评分策略的价值远低于预期，这对高效推理系统的设计方向有重要指导作用。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does bilateral protection interact with KV cache quantization methods (e.g., KIVI, KVQuant)? Can the two compression dimensions be jointly optimized?
- Does the protection-first finding transfer to page-level serving systems (vLLM PagedAttention) under realistic multi-tenant workloads?
- Can the two-factor model be extended to multi-turn dialogue where each turn boundary requires structural protection?
- What is the optimal protection strategy at 100K+ context lengths—does a fixed absolute-slot budget outperform the proportional 10% rule?
- Could learned eviction provide value beyond linear credit estimation, e.g., using per-head neural selectors that account for layer-specific protection needs?
- How does structural protection interact with mixture-of-experts routing and multi-latent-attention architectures?
- Is the attention-sink dominance (75% of prefix mass at position 0) consistent across languages, code, and structured inputs?
