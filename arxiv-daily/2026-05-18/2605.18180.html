Canonical Regularisation of Wide Feature-Learning Neural Networks

arXiv: 2605.18180

Date: 2026-05-18

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18180

⚠️ Abstract-only summary
一句话总结： 本文证明在特征学习 regime 的宽神经网络中，ridge 正则化会在梯度流训练中产生非平凡的归纳偏置偏移，作者通过 Riemannian 几何框架提出了适用于特征学习 regime 的 geodesic ridge 和 arc ridge，建立了更一般的正则化理论。
TL;DR: This paper reveals that ridge regularisation distorts the inductive bias of wide feature-learning neural networks even as regularisation vanishes, and derives a Riemannian-geometry-based canonical regulariser (geodesic ridge) along with a practical surrogate (arc ridge) that generalises the classical kernel-regime theory.

核心贡献

  • 证明了在特征学习 regime 的宽网络中，即使正则化趋于零的极限下，ridge 正则化仍会对梯度流的隐式归纳偏置产生实质性扭曲，尤其损害预训练模型中已有的有用先验。
  • 将"canonical regulariser"公理化定义为 regime 无关的函数空间能量泛函（energy and lift），在 kernel regime 下唯一对应 ridge，并自然推广至特征学习 regime。
  • 利用 Riemannian 几何推导出 geodesic ridge，将 ridge 正则化推广至特征学习 regime；证明对应的函数空间先验是 Riemannian Gibbs Process（高斯过程的推广）。
  • 提出 arc ridge 作为 geodesic ridge 的 minimax-robust、可扩展的替代方案，揭示了早停（early stopping）与 canonical regularisation 在不同学习 regime 之间的深层联系。

Key Contributions

  • Prove that ridge regularisation biases gradient flow in feature-learning regime networks even in the infinitesimal limit of vanishing regularisation, with particular damage to pretrained networks carrying informative priors.
  • Axiomatise the canonical regulariser as a regime-agnostic function-space energy and lift, uniquely recovering ridge in the kernel regime and generalising to the feature-learning regime.
  • Derive geodesic ridge from the Riemannian geometry of feature-learning networks, and prove the canonical function-space prior is a Riemannian Gibbs Process (generalising the Gaussian Process prior of kernel regime).
  • Propose arc ridge as a practical, scalable, minimax-robust surrogate for geodesic ridge, uncovering a deep relationship between early stopping and canonical regularisation across learning regimes.

Methodology

The paper develops a rigorous theoretical framework connecting regularisation in kernel-regime and feature-learning-regime wide neural networks:

  1. Axiomatisation of canonical regularisation: The authors define the canonical regulariser as a function-space energy functional (combining an energy and a lift) that satisfies regime-agnostic axioms. In the kernel regime, this uniquely identifies ridge regularisation via the vanishing ridge solution property.
  1. Riemannian geometry of feature-learning networks: The authors study the Riemannian structure induced by the parameter-to-function mapping in feature-learning networks. This geometry deviates from the flat Euclidean structure of kernel regime, leading to curvature-dependent corrections.
  1. Geodesic ridge derivation: By transporting the ridge energy through the Riemannian geometry, the authors derive geodesic ridge — a natural generalisation that accounts for the curved function-space geometry of feature-learning networks.
  1. Arc ridge as a practical surrogate: Motivated by minimax robustness and scalability, arc ridge is proposed as an efficiently computable approximation to geodesic ridge, with provable connections to early stopping dynamics.
  1. Empirical validation: The theory is tested on image processing and NLP transfer-learning tasks, demonstrating the practical consequences of choosing the correct canonical regulariser.

Results & Highlights

  • Ridge regularisation introduces non-vanishing distortion to the inductive bias of feature-learning networks even as the regularisation coefficient goes to zero — a qualitative departure from kernel regime behavior.
  • Pretrained networks are particularly vulnerable: ridge corrupts the informative prior encoded in pretrained weights.
  • The canonical function-space prior for feature-learning networks is a Riemannian Gibbs Process, not a Gaussian Process — a fundamental generalisation.
  • Arc ridge provides a practical alternative that is both minimax-robust and scalable, avoiding the computational cost of geodesic ridge.
  • Early stopping is shown to have a deep theoretical connection to canonical regularisation that persists across both kernel and feature-learning regimes.
  • Empirical results on image processing and NLP transfer learning confirm the theoretical predictions.

  • Rigor: Appears strong from the abstract. The use of Riemannian geometry and formal axiomatisation suggests mathematical care, though full verification requires reading the proofs (not available from abstract alone).
  • Reproducibility: Partially reproducible from the abstract — the arc ridge proposal is described as scalable, but implementation details would need to be extracted from the full paper. Empirical claims on standard benchmarks (image, NLP) should be straightforward to verify.
  • Significance: Potentially high impact. If the theoretical claims hold, this changes how practitioners should think about regularisation in fine-tuning and feature-learning settings — a very common practical scenario.
  • Red flags: The abstract is dense with theoretical claims; the gap between the theoretical framework and practical recommendations (arc ridge) needs careful examination. Whether arc ridge truly captures the essential geometry in practice remains an empirical question. The claim about "particular damage" to pretrained networks is strong and needs solid empirical backing.
