Methodology

Teger addresses the problem of residual error propagation in autoregressive spatio-temporal models. The core insight is that prediction errors exhibit both spatial correlation (across the observation network) and temporal correlation (autocorrelated residuals), which most current models neglect.

The method operates in three stages:

  1. Curvature-aware graph rewiring: Uses discrete Forman curvature to identify edges in the spatial graph that act as information bottlenecks (over-squashing). These bottleneck edges are explicitly strengthened, improving information flow across the graph.
  2. Low-rank-plus-diagonal covariance head: The rewired graph structure is used to parametrize a covariance matrix in a low-rank plus diagonal decomposition, enabling efficient computation of the full covariance structure.
  3. Woodbury identity for tractable inference: The low-rank structure allows efficient matrix operations via the Woodbury identity, keeping the computational cost manageable.

The module is backbone-agnostic — it takes latent states from any autoregressive encoder (tested with LSTM, Transformer, and xLSTM) and adds the structured uncertainty layer on top. Theoretical analysis formally connects the curvature-based rewiring to graph-theoretic properties: over-squashing alleviation, improved spectral connectivity, and reduced effective graph resistance.
