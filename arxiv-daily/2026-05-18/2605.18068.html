Improving Spatio-Temporal Residual Error Propagation by Mitigating Over-Squashing

arXiv: 2605.18068

Date: 2026-05-18

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18068

一句话总结： 本文提出 Teger，一种主干网络无关的概率时空预测不确定性模块，通过基于离散 Balanced Forman 曲率的边重连（仅重加权不加边）缓解图上 over-squashing，并将其嵌入低秩加对角协方差头，在 PeMS03/04/07 与 Brussels 四个数据集的 48 种配置中取得 42 例最佳 CRPS。
TL;DR: Teger is a backbone-agnostic probabilistic module for autoregressive spatio-temporal forecasting that reweights (not adds) graph edges by discrete Balanced Forman curvature to relieve over-squashing, then embeds the rewired Laplacian into a low-rank-plus-diagonal covariance head (Σ = L(C⊗G)L^T + diag(d)) with Woodbury-identity inference at O(D³+R³+DNR²) instead of O((DN)³). On CRPSsum across PeMS03/04/07 and Brussels with LSTM/Transformer/xLSTM/MTGNN backbones, Teger wins 42/48 configurations; on Brussels xLSTM 15-min it reaches 0.0591 vs. LASER 0.0752 and TCVAR 0.0748, and on PeMS04 Transformer 60-min it reaches 0.0128 vs. LASER 0.0149 and TCVAR 0.0177, with a 25.3% CRPS improvement over LASER on Brussels and ~30% MAE reduction over TCVAR.

核心贡献

  • 提出动态低秩加对角协方差模型，同时建模残差的空间依赖（图感知隐因子 G_t）与时间依赖（自回归残差结构 C_t），通过 Kronecker 批协方差 Σ^bat = L^bat(C_t⊗G_t)(L^bat)^T + diag(d_t) 联合刻画时空误差相关性。
  • 设计基于离散 Balanced Forman 曲率的空间曲率感知边重连算法（Algorithm 1）：对 κ_ij ≤ −2+δ 的瓶颈边用 softplus(τ(κ0−κ_ij)) 计算瓶颈分数并按 W'_ij = W_ij(1+λb_ij) 重加权，保持拓扑固定、仅改变边权。
  • 将重连后的 Laplacian 通过可学习投影 P∈R^{N×R} 映射到 R 维因子空间，构造精度矩阵 Q_t = (α+σ_min)I_R + βL_t^(R)，求逆得到合法协方差 G_t = Q_t^{-1}，把主导求逆成本从 O((DN)³) 降到 O(D³+R³+DNR²)。
  • 给出形式化理论分析：Lemma 2（Laplacian 单调性）、Proposition 3（谱隙单调性 L'_t ⪰ L_t）、Corollary 5（Kirchhoff 指标下降）、Proposition 7（局部瓶颈 cut conductance 提升），并把曲率重连与协方差校准界联系起来。
  • 在 4 个真实交通数据集 × 4 个主干网络的 48 种配置中取得 42 例最佳 CRPSsum，并在 Brussels 上相对 LASER 提升 25.3% CRPS。

Key Contributions

  • A dynamic low-rank-plus-diagonal covariance model for spatio-temporal residuals that captures spatial dependence via graph-aware latent factors G_t and temporal dependence via an autoregressive kernel-mixture C_t, combined as a Kronecker batch covariance Σ^bat = L^bat(C_t⊗G_t)(L^bat)^T + diag(d_t).
  • A curvature-aware spatial precision-matrix rewiring algorithm (Algorithm 1) that keeps the input topology fixed and only reweights existing edges using Balanced Forman curvature: edges with κ_ij ≤ −2+δ are identified as bottlenecks, scored via b_ij = softplus(τ(κ0−κ_ij)), and reweighted as W'_ij = W_ij(1+λb_ij). The authors are explicit that this is a strictly weaker intervention than LASER/SDRF structural rewiring.
  • A learnable low-rank projection P∈R^{N×R} that maps the rewired Laplacian into an R-dimensional factor space, producing a precision matrix Q_t = (α+σ_min)I_R + βL_t^(R) whose inverse G_t = Q_t^{-1} is a valid covariance (Proposition 1), reducing dominant inversion from O((DN)³) to O(D³+R³+DNR²) via the Woodbury identity.
  • Formal theoretical analysis (Lemma 2, Proposition 3, Corollary 5, Proposition 7) connecting curvature-aware rewiring to over-squashing alleviation, improved spectral connectivity (L'_t ⪰ L_t in Löwner order), reduced scaled Kirchhoff index K(W'_t) ≤ K(W_t), and improved local bottleneck conductance ϕ_{W'_t}(S) ≥ ϕ_{W_t}(S).
  • Empirical validation showing Teger achieves the best CRPSsum in 42 of 48 (dataset, backbone, horizon) configurations, with a 25.3% CRPS improvement over LASER on Brussels and ~30% MAE reduction over TCVAR.

Methodology

Probabilistic autoregressive formulation. The time series is decomposed as x_t = μ_t + η_t with η_t ~ N(0, Σ_t), and the covariance uses a low-rank-plus-diagonal form Σ_t = L_t L_t^T + diag(d_t) with L_t ∈ R^{N×R}, R≪N.

Temporal error correlation. Residuals are stacked over a finite horizon D into η^bat_t = vec(η_{t−D+1:t}) ∈ R^{DN}, giving the batch covariance Σ^bat_t = L^bat_t (C_t ⊗ G_t)(L^bat_t)^T + diag(d_t). The temporal correlation C_t ∈ R^{D×D} is a dynamic kernel mixture C_t = Σ_m w_{m,t} K_m over M fixed PSD squared-exponential kernels with softmax-learned weights (following TCVAR).

Spatial correlation via curvature-aware rewiring (Algorithm 1). For each batch, the adjacency is symmetrized; for each edge (i,j) the Balanced Forman curvature κ_{ij,t} is computed and a bottleneck score b_{ij,t} = softplus(τ(κ0 − κ_{ij,t})) is formed; edges are reweighted as W'_{ij,t} = W_{ij,t}(1 + λ b_{ij,t}). The rewired Laplacian L'_t is projected via a column-normalized learnable P̂ into L_t^(R) = P̂^T L'_t P̂, and the spatial factor covariance is G_t = Q_t^{-1} with Q_t = (α+σ_min)I_R + β L_t^(R). Proposition 1 guarantees G_t is a valid SPD covariance.

Inference-time residual refinement. Following TCVAR's Gaussian posterior sampling, Teger adds node-wise volatility scaling: the conditional covariance is split into marginal scales S_{cond} and a dimensionless correlation matrix R_{cond}, and the marginal scale is replaced by an exponential moving average of squared one-step residuals s²_{i,t} = ρ s²_{i,t−1} + (1−ρ) r²_{i,t}, yielding η̃^{ref}_{t+1} = μ_{cond,t+1} + D_t^{1/2} R_{cond,t+1}^{1/2} ξ_{t+1}.

Training. All parameters (including κ0, τ, λ, α, β, P) are jointly optimized by minimizing the negative log-likelihood under the batch Gaussian model; the Woodbury identity and Kronecker structure keep the dominant inversion at O(D³+R³+DNR²).

Datasets & backbones. PeMS03 (358 nodes, 26208 steps), PeMS04 (307, 16992), PeMS07 (883, 26208), Brussels (195, 12672, private). Backbones: LSTM, Transformer, xLSTM, MTGNN. Baselines: naive (diagonal Gaussian), TCVAR (temporal-only), LASER (structural rewiring). Chronological 70/10/20 split. Graph construction: thresholded Gaussian kernel A_{ij} = exp(−d²_{ij}/2σ²) if ≥0.1 else 0. Hyperparameters: hidden dim 40, R=10, D=12, M=4, α=0.01, β=1.0, λ=1.0, κ0=0.0, τ=5.0, σ_min=10⁻⁴. Hardware: Intel 13900F + 2× NVIDIA RTX 4090 (24GB), PyTorch 1.13.1, CUDA 11.7.

Results & Highlights

  • On Brussels xLSTM 15-min (Table 1), Teger CRPSsum = 0.0591 vs. LASER 0.0752, TCVAR 0.0748, naive 0.1448 — a 25.3% improvement over LASER and ~21% over TCVAR.
  • On PeMS04 Transformer 60-min (Table 1), Teger CRPSsum = 0.0128 vs. LASER 0.0149, TCVAR 0.0177, naive 0.0243 — Teger is ~14% better than LASER and ~28% better than TCVAR.
  • On PeMS04 xLSTM 60-min (Table 1), Teger CRPSsum = 0.0128 vs. LASER 0.0149, TCVAR 0.0177 — best result in the row.
  • Across all 48 (dataset, backbone, horizon) configurations in Table 1, Teger achieves the best CRPSsum in 42 cases; LASER outperforms Teger primarily on PeMS03 at longer horizons.
  • Graph-measure improvements (Table 2): on Brussels LSTM, the scaled Kirchhoff index ratio K(W_t)/K(W'_t) = 34.1%, the spectral-gap ratio λ'_{n−1}/λ_{n−1} = 37.1%, and the cut-conductance ratio ϕ_{W'_t}(S)/ϕ_{W_t}(S) = 33.9% — all confirming the theoretical monotonicity claims.
  • Ablation (Table 3, on PeMS03): adding edge reweighting reduces NLL from 72.21 (no rewiring) → 58.21 and MAE 8.21 → 5.91; the full model (with latent projection) reaches NLL 49.32 and MAE 4.98 — a ~39% NLL reduction and ~39% MAE reduction over no rewiring.
  • Teger reduces TCVAR's MAE error magnitude by nearly 30% after modeling spatial error propagation and refining forecasts (Section 4.2).
  • Computational cost: the Woodbury/Kronecker structure reduces dominant inversion from O((DN)³) to O(D³+R³+DNR²); Figure 6 shows Teger consumes less memory and runtime than TCVAR and LASER on Brussels while forecasting more accurately.
  • Brussels edge analysis (Table 8): the strongest rewired edge N1↔N3 has κ=−0.203 and receives W'/W = 11.30× amplification; a non-rewired reference edge with κ=+0.415 correctly receives W'/W = 1.00×.
  • Statistical significance (Table 7): on Brussels xLSTM, CRPS-sum = 0.0619 ± 0.0094 over 24 rolling forecast test windows; on pems03 xLSTM, CRPS-sum = 0.0341 ± 0.0023.

Critical Assessment

  • The authors themselves are explicit that edge reweighting is "a strictly weaker tool than the structural rewirings of LASER and SDRF" and "can relieve over-squashing only in regimes where bottleneck edges are present but underweighted" — so the over-squashing mitigation claim is narrower than the abstract's framing suggests, and the regime where it fails (no underweighted bottleneck edges, or missing long-range pathways) is acknowledged but not empirically bounded.
  • The theoretical results (Proposition 3, Corollary 5, Proposition 7) are largely monotonicity statements that follow from W' ≥ W entrywise; they show rewiring cannot hurt the graph measures, but they do not quantify how much CRPS improves, and the link from graph measures to covariance calibration bounds (Direction 4) is only sketched ("We use CRPSsum to compare") rather than proven as a bound.
  • Proposition 3 is stated only for the unnormalised Laplacian L = D − W; the authors note that the normalised Laplacian case "requires more care because normalisation by the (changing) degree matrix does not preserve the Löwner order" and leave it to future work — yet normalised Laplacians are common in GNN practice, limiting the applicability of the theory.
  • The Brussels interpretability case study (Section 4.3, Figure 2, Table 8) is explicitly described as "a visualization exercise, not a quantitative validation against independent ground-truth bottleneck labels" — rigorous validation against incident logs or measured queue spillback is deferred to future work.
  • Evaluation uses only traffic datasets (PeMS + Brussels); the method's behavior on non-road-graph spatio-temporal networks (e.g., power grids, sensor meshes) is untested, and the road-network regime is exactly where the authors argue reweighting is well-matched.

Strengths

  • Principled integration of curvature-aware rewiring into a probabilistic covariance head, with a closed-form low-rank projection that keeps inference tractable (O(D³+R³+DNR²)).
  • Backbone-agnostic design validated on four architecturally distinct encoders (LSTM, Transformer, xLSTM, MTGNN), and MTGNN is included specifically to rule out the confound that gains come merely from injecting spatial structure.
  • Strong and consistent CRPS improvements (42/48 best), with the largest gains on the densest, most physically constrained network (Brussels), where LASER's synthetic edges are least physically meaningful.
  • Honest scoping: the paper explicitly states where reweighting works and where structural rewiring would be needed, and labels the interpretability study as qualitative.
  • Includes statistical significance over 24 rolling windows (Table 7) and a NeurIPS-style reproducibility checklist with code/data release.

Limitations

  • Edge reweighting cannot create long-range pathways where none exist; on graphs with missing long-range shortcuts (where LASER-style edge insertion would help), Teger is expected to underperform — consistent with LASER beating Teger on some PeMS03 long-horizon settings.
  • The theory covers only the unnormalised Laplacian; extension to the normalised Laplacian (common in GNN message passing) is left to future work.
  • The connection from graph measures (spectral gap, Kirchhoff index, conductance) to an actual covariance calibration bound is asserted (Direction 4) but not formally stated as a theorem with assumptions.
  • Evaluation is restricted to traffic-road networks; no results on non-traffic spatio-temporal datasets, and the Brussels dataset is private (only reproduction scripts are public).
  • The curvature computation and per-edge reweighting add per-batch overhead whose scaling to very large N (e.g., thousands of nodes) is discussed via the R-dimensional projection but not benchmarked beyond 883 nodes (PeMS07).
  • Node-wise volatility scaling introduces an EMA hyperparameter ρ whose sensitivity is not reported.
