> **一句话总结：** 本文系统比较了K折交叉验证集成与深度集成在医学图像分割不确定性估计中的差异，发现两者不可互换——深度集成在校准和失败检测上更优，而交叉验证集成更适合建模标注歧义性。

> **TL;DR:** Cross-validation ensembles and deep ensembles are not interchangeable for uncertainty estimation in medical image segmentation. DE excels at calibration and failure detection; CV ensembles better capture inter-rater ambiguity. The authors provide an nnU-Net modification for easy DE training.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了医学图像分割领域普遍存在的术语混淆问题：许多研究将K折交叉验证集成称为"深度集成"，但两者的不确定性语义不同。
- 在三个多标注者数据集、三种模态上，对5折CV集成与5成员深度集成进行了系统性对比实验。
- 发现深度集成在校准和失败检测方面更优，而CV集成与标注者间变异性的相关性更强。
- 提供了一个轻量级nnU-Net修改方案，可在默认流程中实现深度集成训练。
- 为不同研究目标提供了明确的集成构建选择指南。

## Key Contributions

- Identify and audit widespread terminology-implementation mismatches in recent segmentation uncertainty studies, where CV ensembles are incorrectly labeled as deep ensembles.
- Conduct a rigorous head-to-head comparison of 5-fold CV ensembles vs. 5-member deep ensembles across three multi-rater datasets spanning three imaging modalities.
- Demonstrate that DE matches segmentation accuracy while improving calibration and failure detection, whereas CV ensembles correlate more strongly with inter-rater variability.
- Provide a lightweight nnU-Net modification enabling DE training within the default pipeline, lowering the barrier to correct ensemble construction.
- Offer clear practical guidance: use DE for reliability-oriented tasks (selective referral, failure detection) and CV ensembles as a proxy for annotation ambiguity.

## Methodology

The authors compare a standard 5-fold cross-validation (CV) ensemble against a 5-member deep ensemble (DE) under otherwise identical configurations. The key methodological difference is that CV members are trained on different data subsets (4/5 folds each), while DE members are trained on the full training set with different random seeds. Evaluation spans three multi-rater segmentation datasets across three modalities. They assess uncertainty quality along four dimensions: calibration, failure detection, ambiguity modeling (correlation with inter-rater disagreement), and robustness under distribution shift. The authors also audit recent literature to quantify terminology-implementation mismatches.

## Results & Highlights

- DE matches CV in segmentation accuracy while providing better-calibrated uncertainty estimates.
- DE outperforms CV in failure detection (identifying poorly segmented cases).
- CV ensembles show stronger correlation with inter-rater variability, making them better proxies for annotation ambiguity.
- Terminology-implementation mismatches are common in the literature, suggesting the community may be drawing incorrect conclusions about uncertainty behavior.
- A lightweight nnU-Net modification is provided to enable proper DE training.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are affiliated with DKFZ (German Cancer Research Center) and related institutions, a leading group in medical image segmentation — notably the developers of nnU-Net (Isensee, Maier-Hein). This gives them strong credibility for benchmarking ensemble strategies in this domain. The inclusion of multiple datasets and modalities strengthens the generalizability of claims.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate. The core insight — that CV ensembles and DE are not equivalent — is somewhat intuitive once stated, but the systematic empirical validation and literature audit provide valuable concrete evidence. The community has largely assumed interchangeability without rigorous testing.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Controlled comparison with identical architectures, three datasets, three modalities, and evaluation across four uncertainty dimensions. However, without the full paper, it is unclear how many random seeds were used and whether statistical significance tests were applied.
- **Reproducibility:** Likely high. The nnU-Net modification is provided, datasets are standard, and the experimental setup appears well-specified.
- **Significance:** Practically significant for the medical imaging community. Many deployed systems use CV ensembles under the assumption they behave like DE, which this work shows can lead to suboptimal uncertainty estimates for safety-critical decisions.
- **Red flags:** The comparison is limited to segmentation tasks; whether findings generalize to classification or detection is unknown. The abstract does not report specific numerical gaps between DE and CV, making it hard to assess practical magnitude of the differences.

## Strengths

- Addresses a real and widespread methodological confusion in the field.
- Comprehensive evaluation across multiple uncertainty dimensions, not just accuracy.
- Practical contribution via nnU-Net modification lowers adoption barriers.
- Clear, actionable guidance for practitioners choosing ensemble strategies.

## Limitations

- Limited to segmentation tasks; generalization to other tasks is untested.
- Only nnU-Net architecture is evaluated; other architectures may behave differently.
- Without the full paper, the magnitude of performance differences is unclear.
- The definition of "deep ensemble" used here (same data, different seeds) is one convention; some papers define DE more broadly.

## Relevance

该研究对医学图像分割中不确定性估计的实践具有直接指导意义，特别是对于需要可靠失败检测的部署场景。如果你使用nnU-Net或进行分割不确定性量化，这项工作提供了重要的方法论纠正。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the performance gaps between DE and CV scale with ensemble size (e.g., 3, 5, 10, 20 members)?
- Would combining DE and CV disagreement signals provide better overall uncertainty estimates?
- How do these findings transfer to 3D segmentation tasks with limited training data?
- What is the computational cost trade-off between DE (full dataset × multiple seeds) and CV (subset × folds)?
- Do these results hold for other popular architectures beyond nnU-Net (e.g., Swin-UNet, MedFormer)?
