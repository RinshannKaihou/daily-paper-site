> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Putri，一种针对 LLM 的结构化剪枝方法，通过顺序更新 FFN 权重、逐层剪枝和细粒度注意力头移除（支持 GQA），在 50%-99% 的高稀疏度下显著优于现有方法，是首个在 95% 稀疏度下仍能产生可用模型的方法。

> **TL;DR:** Putri is a post-training structured pruning method for LLMs that sequentially updates FFN weights after pruning, prunes FFN layers sequentially to account for prior pruning, and removes individual attention heads (including GQA support). It achieves SOTA performance across Qwen3 and Llama3 families and is the only method that can produce usable models at 95% sparsity.

## 核心贡献

- **顺序 FFN 剪枝与权重更新：** 在剪枝每个 FFN 层的中间节点后，通过最小化 L2 损失重建剩余权重，并逐层顺序执行，使每层能感知前面层的剪枝影响。
- **细粒度注意力头剪枝：** 区别于以往移除整个注意力层的做法，Putri 移除单个注意力头（MHA）或键值头（GQA），这是首个支持 Grouped-Query Attention 的头剪枝方法。
- **高稀疏度下的鲁棒性：** 在 90%-95% 稀疏度下，其他方法大多崩溃（无穷大 perplexity 或 NaN），而 Putri 仍能保持有限且较低的 perplexity。
- **广泛的实验验证：** 在 Llama3（3.0/3.1/3.2）和 Qwen3（0.6B-32B）全系列模型上，覆盖 25%-99% 稀疏度，在多个数据集（Wikitext-2、FineWeb-Edu、C4）和 zero-shot 基准上进行评测。

## Key Contributions

- Introduce sequential FFN pruning with L2-optimal weight reconstruction after each layer, accounting for the cumulative distribution shift from previously pruned layers.
- Propose fine-grained attention head pruning that works with both Multi-Head Attention (MHA) and Grouped-Query Attention (GQA) — the first method to handle GQA head pruning.
- Demonstrate that structured pruning is feasible at extreme sparsity levels (90%-95%), where all prior methods fail catastrophically.
- Provide extensive evaluation across Llama3 and Qwen3 model families (0.6B to 32B parameters), 25%-99% sparsity, 3 perplexity datasets, and 6 zero-shot benchmarks.

## Methodology

Putri follows a two-stage pruning pipeline inspired by 2SSP, but with three critical modifications:

**Stage 1 — MLP/FFN Pruning:**
- Each intermediate FFN node is scored by its L2 activation norm: `score(node_i) = ||z_i||_2^2`.
- Nodes with the lowest scores are removed. The remaining weights are then reconstructed by solving the L2-optimal least-squares problem: `W_hat_P = (X_P^T X_P)^{-1} X_P^T X W`.
- Crucially, layers are pruned **sequentially**: each layer receives the already-pruned output of previous layers as input, rather than using the original model's activations. This accounts for distribution shift.
- Only one layer needs to be in memory at a time, reducing memory requirements.

**Stage 2 — Attention Head Pruning:**
- Instead of removing entire attention layers, individual key-value heads are removed (along with their associated query heads for GQA).
- Head selection uses perplexity on calibration data: `argmin_{a} perplexity(f(X; theta_FFN, theta_Attn \ {a}), Y)`.
- To reduce compute, H heads (the number of grouped heads per attention layer) are removed per iteration.
- The attention-to-FFN pruning ratio is controlled by a hyperparameter alpha (default 1.5), adapted from 2SSP's formula to work with head counts rather than layer counts.

**Attention vs FFN Ratio:** The number of KV-heads to prune is computed as `N_KV-Heads = K * round(L * s^(alpha * |theta_FFN| / |theta_Attn|))`, where K is the number of key heads per GQA layer.

## Results & Highlights

- **Perplexity (Qwen3-32B, Wiki, 75% sparsity):** Putri achieves 44.66 vs. 2SSP's 122.1, EvoPress's OOM, and BlockPruner's 5884. At 90% sparsity, Putri gets 370.2 while most baselines produce NaN or infinity.
- **Perplexity (Qwen3-14B, Wiki, 90% sparsity):** Putri: 1307, 2SSP: 5316, EvoPress: 9928, others: NaN/infinity.
- **95% sparsity feasibility:** Putri is the **only method** that produces finite perplexity on Qwen3-8B (3124 on Wiki) and Qwen3-14B (4916 on Wiki). All other methods either crash or produce infinity.
- **Zero-shot benchmarks (Qwen3-8B, 75% sparsity):** Putri matches or exceeds baselines on Hellaswag (56.3%), ARC-E (33.1%), and MMLU (34.6%).
- **Ablation study:** Removing any of the three components (FFN weight update, sequential pruning, or head-level attention pruning) degrades performance, with weight update being the most critical component.
- **Consistent across model sizes:** Improvements hold from Qwen3-0.6B to Qwen3-32B and across Llama3 variants.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate):** The three individual components (weight reconstruction, sequential pruning, head pruning) are not individually novel. The contribution is in their principled combination and the extension to GQA. The claim of "first GQA head pruning" appears legitimate.
- **Rigor (Strong):** Extensive experimental evaluation across 2 model families, 8+ model sizes, 7 sparsity levels, 3 perplexity datasets, and 6 zero-shot benchmarks. Ablation study validates each component. However, no downstream task evaluation beyond zero-shot benchmarks.
- **Reproducibility (Strong):** Public code, clear algorithm descriptions, standard calibration dataset (C4), well-defined hyperparameters with defaults.
- **Significance (High):** The demonstration that structured pruning works at 95% sparsity is a meaningful contribution. Prior work was essentially limited to ≤50% sparsity, making structured pruning impractical. This opens a new research direction.
- **Red flags:**
  - At 99% sparsity, even Putri produces infinity or NaN on most models, suggesting there is a hard ceiling.
  - The paper does not report actual inference speedup or memory reduction measurements — only perplexity and zero-shot accuracy. The practical efficiency gains are assumed rather than measured.
  - The attention head pruning uses greedy iterative perplexity evaluation, which could be computationally expensive for very large models despite the batched removal heuristic.
  - No comparison with quantization or knowledge distillation methods, which are the practical alternatives the paper motivates against.

## Strengths

- Tackles the practically important but under-explored high-sparsity regime (90%+) for structured pruning.
- Clean, principled combination of three well-motivated techniques with solid ablation validation.
- First method to handle GQA head pruning, making it applicable to modern LLMs (Llama3, Qwen3).
- Sequential pruning reduces peak memory by processing one layer at a time.
- Very comprehensive experimental setup covering many models, sparsity levels, and evaluation protocols.

## Limitations

- No measured inference speedup or memory reduction — the practical benefits of structured pruning over unstructured are assumed, not benchmarked.
- Greedy head selection via perplexity evaluation may be slow for very large models.
- The method uniformly applies the same sparsity to all FFN layers, ignoring potential layer-wise optimal sparsity.
- At 99% sparsity the method still fails on most models, leaving the extreme regime unsolved.
- No evaluation on instruction-tuned or RLHF-aligned models — only base models are tested.
- The alpha hyperparameter for attention/FFN ratio is set empirically without systematic sensitivity analysis.

## Relevance

本文提出的高稀疏度结构化剪枝方法与 LLM 效率优化和模型压缩方向高度相关。Putri 对 GQA 的支持使其可直接应用于 Qwen3、Llama3 等主流模型，在资源受限部署场景下具有实际价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does Putri interact with quantization (e.g., applying Putri first, then INT4/INT8 quantization)? Can the two techniques compound their compression benefits?
- What are the actual wall-clock inference speedups and GPU memory reductions at each sparsity level on modern hardware?
- Can the uniform per-layer FFN sparsity be replaced with a learned or search-based layer-wise sparsity allocation to further improve performance?
- How does Putri perform on instruction-tuned or chat models (e.g., Qwen3-Instruct, Llama-3.1-Instruct)? Does the pruning destroy alignment?
- Is the sequential pruning approach compatible with recent mixture-of-experts (MoE) architectures, where FFN layers have expert routing?
