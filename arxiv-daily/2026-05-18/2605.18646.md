> **一句话总结：** 通过对Gaperon-8B模型中一个拉丁语触发器将英文输出切换为法语的后门进行因果电路分析，发现触发信号在中间层以与自然语言方向正交的子空间传播，形成了一个单位置串行瓶颈的三阶段电路结构，这意味着基于中间层语言表示检测的后门防御方法将完全失效。

> **TL;DR:** This paper identifies and decomposes a three-phase circuit underlying a language-switching backdoor in an 8B-parameter LLM: distributed attention heads compose trigger tokens at position -1, the signal propagates through mid-layers in a subspace orthogonal to the natural language-identity direction (invisible to linear probes), and the final-layer MLP converts it to French logits. The entire circuit is a serial bottleneck at a single position.

## 核心贡献

- 在Gaperon-8B模型中完整识别了语言切换后门的三阶段因果电路：触发器合成（早期层）、潜在传播（中间层）和读出（最后一层MLP）
- 发现触发信号在中间层以与模型自然语言-身份方向**正交**的子空间传播，语言身份探针在整个中间层将触发表示分类为英语，但信号在因果上是存在的——这种"因果存在性与探针可见性"的分离是本文最核心的新发现
- 揭示了整个电路是一个**单位置串行瓶颈**：破坏位置-1在任意层都能完全消除触发效果，但也会损害模型能力
- 提出了中性词 corruption 方法作为高斯噪声 corruption 的替代基线，发现高斯 corruption 会产生退化输出并膨胀早期层的恢复百分比估计

## Key Contributions

- Full causal circuit decomposition of a language-switching backdoor in an 8B-parameter autoregressive LM (Gaperon-8B), identifying three distinct phases: trigger composition, latent propagation, and readout
- Discovery of **orthogonal latent encoding**: the trigger signal travels through mid-layers in a subspace orthogonal to the model's natural language-identity direction, making it invisible to linear language-identity probes while remaining causally present
- Identification of a **serial bottleneck at a single residual-stream position (p_{-1})**: corrupting this position at any layer kills the trigger entirely, with no redundant parallel pathways
- Introduction of **neutral-word corruption** as a more reliable baseline than Gaussian noise corruption, showing that the standard Gaussian approach produces degenerate outputs that inflate quantitative estimates at early layers
- Demonstration that the three-phase architecture and orthogonal encoding render probe-based backdoor detection methods fundamentally ineffective

## Methodology

The authors study Gaperon-8B, an autoregressive LLM with a planted 9-token Latin trigger that switches output from English to French. They apply a comprehensive causal circuit analysis toolkit:

- **Activation patching** (clean/corrupt/patched forward passes) with logit-difference metrics measuring French-vs-English logit mass at position -1
- **Per-head causal decomposition** isolating individual attention head contributions to the residual stream
- **Linear language-identity probes** (French-vs-English logistic regression) trained at each layer on residual stream vectors to track language identity trajectories
- **Natural language direction** (d_{nat,l}) computed as normalized mean of per-pair French-minus-English contrastive vectors
- **Corruption robustness analysis** comparing Gaussian noise corruption against a novel neutral-word corruption that replaces trigger tokens with common English words
- All experiments use n=100 prompts with 5 corruption seeds, run on NVIDIA GB200 GPUs via nnsight library

## Results & Highlights

- **Phase 1 (Layers 3-7)**: Distributed attention heads compose trigger tokens into position -1. No single head exceeds ~3% causal effect; ~10 heads across four layers collectively account for ~20-25% recovery. The composition is order-sensitive at the token level but approximately bag-of-words at the word level (5 of 6 word permutations achieve >= 96% success; only full reversal drops to 69.8%)
- **Phase 2 (Layers 8-30)**: No MLP or attention layer makes a positive constructive contribution. The trigger signal persists passively in the residual stream. Linear probes classify the triggered representation as English (P(French) ≈ 0) throughout layers 17-26, despite the signal being causally necessary at every layer
- **Phase 3 (Layer 31)**: The final MLP contributes +62-63% of total causal effect (~6 standard errors above zero). Layer 17 attention contributes an additional +22%. Together they account for ~84% of the effect
- **Serial bottleneck**: Ablating position -1 at any layer yields 95-100%+ mitigation. Ablating any other trigger position (trig+0 through trig+7) has near-zero effect
- **Token-level specificity**: Triggered, scrambled, and clean logit-difference distributions are completely separated; 98% of triggered prompts prefer French vs. 12% of scrambled
- **Corruption robustness**: All structural claims are invariant to corruption method, though Gaussian corruption inflates early-layer recovery estimates by producing degenerate outputs

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are from Inria Paris and Sorbonne Université, well-established institutions in NLP research. The work builds directly on the Gaperon model (Godey et al., 2025) and the hijack mechanism analysis of Lasnier et al. (2026), positioning this as a natural extension of an existing research program. The methodology follows established circuit analysis conventions (Wang et al., 2022; Geva et al., 2023; Conmy et al., 2023) and the corruption robustness analysis addresses a known methodological concern (Zhang and Nanda, 2023). The paper acknowledges limitations (single model scale, single trigger type, German trigger failure) and provides extensive appendices.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The orthogonal latent encoding finding is genuinely novel — the discovery that a backdoor signal can travel through a subspace invisible to standard probing methods has direct implications for AI safety. The serial bottleneck characterization and the two-level token/word order sensitivity are also original contributions
- **Rigor**: Strong. The paper uses complementary evidence (sufficiency via patching, necessity via ablation, scrambled controls uniformly null, corruption robustness checks). However, the orthogonal encoding claim relies partly on an absence (probes don't detect it) rather than a direct positive demonstration — the authors acknowledge the natural French direction d_{nat,l} is itself poorly defined at layers 16-29, weakening the geometric "orthogonal" claim
- **Reproducibility**: Good. The model (Gaperon-8B) is specified, the trigger is documented, code uses nnsight, and experimental parameters (100 prompts, 5 seeds) are reported. The neutral-word corruption baseline is clearly described
- **Significance**: Significant for the mechanistic interpretability and AI safety communities. The finding that probe-based defenses fail against this backdoor class is practically important. The three-phase circuit template may generalize to other backdoor types
- **Red flags**: Only tested on one model at one scale with one trigger type; the German trigger analysis failed due to insufficient training data, leaving generalization unconfirmed; the layer 17 attention contribution (+22%) has large error bars (±15%); the natural language direction is geometrically unstable at precisely the layers where orthogonal encoding is claimed

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated experimental design using a harmless backdoor (language switching) as an ideal testbed for mechanistic analysis
- Comprehensive multi-method evidence converging on the same three-phase story
- The corruption robustness analysis is a valuable methodological contribution that validates findings while exposing a weakness in standard practice
- The scrambled control condition is applied consistently across all experiments, providing a strong null baseline
- Clear and honest discussion of limitations, particularly regarding the geometric instability of the natural language direction

## Limitations

- Single model scale (8B) — replication on 1B and 24B variants would strengthen generalization claims
- Single backdoor type (pre-training-planted, fixed multi-token, language-switching) — other backdoor classes may produce qualitatively different circuits
- The orthogonal encoding claim is based on probe invisibility rather than direct geometric measurement, and the reference direction (d_{nat,l}) is unstable at the relevant layers
- The role of the layer 17 attention contribution (+22%) is not fully explained
- No systematic quantification of collateral damage to natural French capability when the trigger circuit is disrupted

## Relevance

本文通过因果电路分析揭示了LLM后门攻击的内部机制，特别是"正交潜在编码"的发现表明现有基于探针的后门检测方法存在根本性缺陷。这对于理解模型安全性、机制可解释性以及对抗性鲁棒性都具有重要意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the three-phase circuit architecture generalize to single-token triggers, context-dependent triggers, or backdoors introduced through fine-tuning rather than pre-training?
- Can the orthogonal subspace be directly identified and isolated (rather than inferred from probe failure) using techniques like PCA or causal tracing on contrastive datasets?
- Would probing with nonlinear classifiers (rather than linear probes) detect the trigger signal during the latent propagation phase?
- How does the circuit structure change across model scales (1B, 24B) within the same model family?
- Is there a way to surgically remove the trigger direction from the final-layer MLP without degrading the model's natural multilingual capabilities?
