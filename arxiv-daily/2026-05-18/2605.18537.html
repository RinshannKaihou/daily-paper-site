Probing for Representation Manifolds in Superposition

arXiv: 2605.18537

Date: 2026-05-18

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18537

一句话总结： 本文提出 Manifold Probe，一种通过广义特征值分解和交替最小二乘来发现 LLM 表示空间中连续概念（如时间、空间）的高维流形结构的监督探针方法，并在 Llama 2-7b 上验证了该流形可用于因果干预（steering）模型的输出年份。
TL;DR: The paper introduces the Manifold Probe, a supervised probing method that discovers multidimensional representation manifolds of continuous concepts (time, space) in superposition within LLM residual streams. It generalizes linear regression probes by jointly learning the feature space and regression weights via a generalized eigenvalue problem, demonstrated on Llama 2-7b with causal steering experiments showing the discovered time manifold can influence model completions about release dates.

核心贡献

  • 提出 Manifold Probe，一种新的监督探针方法，能在叠加（superposition）条件下发现连续概念的多维表示流形，推广了传统线性回归探针。
  • 将流形发现问题形式化为广义特征值问题，具有封闭解，并提出了高效的交替最小二乘算法来同时选择正则化参数（REML/GCV）。
  • 在 Llama 2-7b 上发现了时间和空间表示流形：时间流形中的特征线性分离了不同年代，空间流形中的特征线性分离了美国多个州。
  • 证明了发现的流形具有因果效力：通过沿时间流形添加转向向量（steering vectors），可以成功影响模型对歌曲、电影和书籍发行年份的补全。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces the Manifold Probe, a two-stage supervised method: (1) learns the space of features of a concept that are linearly predictable from representations via a generalized eigenvalue problem, and (2) recovers the encoding basis directions via linear regression.
  • Derives a closed-form solution (Proposition 4) based on a generalized eigenvalue problem $M\beta = \nu\Sigma\beta$, and an efficient Alternating Least Squares algorithm (Algorithm 1) that converges to the global minimizer with built-in regularization parameter selection (GCV or REML).
  • Applies Varimax rotation to interpret discovered features: the time manifold separates decades (1950s–2010s), and the space manifold localizes on individual U.S. states — revealing interpretable linear structure within the manifolds.
  • Conducts causal steering experiments: adding steering vectors $\hat{\phi}(z)$ from the learned time manifold to residual stream activations at specific layers shifts the model's year predictions toward target years, with efficacy peaking at layers 8–14 and dropping sharply after layer 15.

Methodology

The Manifold Probe operates under the superposition hypothesis (Definition 1): concept representations combine additively in representation space. The goal is to discover the representation map $\phi: Z \to \mathbb{R}^p$ of a target concept $Z$ (e.g., time or space) from a probing dataset $D = \{(x_i, z_i)\}_{i=1}^n$.

Key theoretical insight (Lemma 2): Under the superposition model, the representation manifold decomposes as $\phi(z) = u_1 f_1(z) + \cdots + u_d f_d(z)$, where features $f_k$ solve sequential population regression problems minimizing $\mathbb{E}[(f(z) - w^\top x - b)^2]$ subject to orthonormality constraints.

Estimation procedure (Definition 3):

  1. Features are parameterized in a known basis (e.g., cubic B-splines with many knots).
  2. The optimization jointly learns feature coefficients $\beta$ and regression weights $w$ with ridge regularization on both, plus a smoothness penalty on $f$.
  3. The solution reduces to finding eigenvectors of a generalized eigenvalue problem $M\beta = \nu\Sigma\beta$ corresponding to the smallest eigenvalues.
  4. Basis directions $\hat{u}_k$ are recovered via $\hat{u}_k = \frac{1}{n}X^\top H\hat{\beta}_k$.

Regularization selection: Uses REML (Restricted Maximum Likelihood) or GCV (Generalized Cross-Validation) criteria applied at each iteration of the alternating least squares procedure, enabling data-driven complexity control without grid search.

Experimental setup:

  • Probing datasets from Gurnee & Tegmark (2024): 29,503 works with release dates in [1950, 2020], and 17,381 U.S. places with geographic coordinates.
  • Last-token residual stream activations from Llama 2-7b at each layer.
  • Time features: cubic B-splines with 280 knots; space features: tensor product B-splines with 40×80 knots.
  • Steering intervention: add $\alpha \cdot \hat{\phi}(z)$ to residual stream at a given layer, with $\alpha = 100$.

Results & Highlights

  • Time manifold features: The top-ranked feature closely tracks the identity (year value) with R² comparable to direct ridge regression on years. Additional features capture richer temporal structure beyond simple year encoding.
  • Space manifold features: The top 3 features are considerably more predictable than the rest, consistent across layers. The highest-ranked features achieve higher R² than direct latitude/longitude regression.
  • Interpretability via Varimax rotation: After rotation, time features approximately separate decades (1950s through 2010s) as nearly binary indicators. Space features localize on individual U.S. states, showing these states are approximately linearly separated in representation space.
  • Layer-wise analysis: Feature predictability increases through the first half of layers then plateaus. For space, 3 features dominate; for time, more features contribute meaningfully.
  • Steering efficacy: Interventions peak at layers 8 and 14 depending on release decade, dropping sharply after layer 15 and reaching baseline by layer 20. Dark diagonal streaks in completion probability matrices confirm targeted year steering works. Interventions have minimal effect on the model's ability to produce valid year completions (except for 1950s targets).

Critical Assessment

Novelty: High. The Manifold Probe is a genuinely new methodology that generalizes linear probing to multidimensional manifold discovery. The formulation as a joint feature-learning and regression problem with a closed-form generalized eigenvalue solution is elegant and novel. The connection between manifold geometry and linearly predictable features (Lemma 2) provides useful theoretical grounding.

Rigor: Strong. The paper provides formal definitions, a population-level lemma, a finite-sample proposition with proof, and a convergence guarantee for the alternating least squares algorithm. The experimental design is thorough — train/test splits, layer-wise analysis, and both interpretability and causal evaluation.

Reproducibility: Good. Code is publicly available. The probing datasets are from a prior published work. The method uses standard components (B-splines, ridge regression, Varimax rotation). The 100 GPU-hours steering experiment is substantial but feasible.

Significance: High. This addresses a real gap in mechanistic interpretability: how to discover and characterize multidimensional representation manifolds. The causal steering results provide evidence the discovered structures are not just statistical artifacts but are involved in model computation.

Potential concerns:

  • The method requires large probing datasets (tens of thousands of examples for SOTA models), which the author acknowledges as a limitation. For many concepts, curating such datasets may be impractical.
  • The superposition assumption (additive combination of concept representations) is a strong structural hypothesis. While motivated by prior work, it may not hold for all concepts or all layers.
  • The steering results show efficacy but also layer-dependent degradation — the manifold is only causally relevant in a narrow layer range, raising questions about how general the approach is.
  • Evaluation is limited to Llama 2-7b and two concepts (time, space). Generalization to other models, concepts, and modalities remains to be demonstrated.

Strengths

  • Elegant mathematical formulation connecting manifold geometry, feature learning, and generalized eigenvalue problems.
  • Closed-form solution enables efficient computation.
  • The two-stage approach (feature learning + basis recovery) cleanly separates the geometry discovery from direction finding.
  • Varimax rotation provides an interpretable decomposition of the manifold into approximately sparse features.
  • Causal steering experiments go beyond correlational analysis to demonstrate mechanistic relevance.
  • Well-written with clear notation and thorough appendices.

Limitations

  • Requires large probing datasets (order of tens of thousands), which limits applicability to concepts where labeled data is scarce.
  • Only demonstrated on Llama 2-7b with two continuous concepts; generalization to other architectures and concept types is untested.
  • The steering efficacy drops sharply beyond layer 15, suggesting the manifold representation is transient and not uniformly accessible.
  • The superposition additivity assumption may not hold for all types of concepts, particularly abstract or hierarchical ones.
  • No comparison to alternative manifold discovery methods (e.g., neighbor graphs from Modell et al., 2025, or the discretized linear classifier approach of Yocum et al., 2025).

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the Manifold Probe perform on more abstract concepts (e.g., emotion, ideology, toxicity) where labeled data is harder to curate?
  • Can the discovered manifolds be combined with sparse autoencoder features to provide a unified picture of both discrete and continuous representations?
  • What is the relationship between the manifold dimensions discovered by the probe and the intrinsic dimensionality of the concept space — does the probe recover the "correct" number of dimensions?
  • How transferable are the discovered manifolds across model architectures (e.g., from Llama to GPT-style models)?
  • Could the alternating least squares procedure be extended to discover manifolds in an unsupervised or semi-supervised setting?
  • What happens when the superposition assumption is violated — how robust is the probe to non-additive concept interactions?
