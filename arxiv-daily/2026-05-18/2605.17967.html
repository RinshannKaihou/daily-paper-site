Reconciling Contradictory Views on the Effectiveness of SFT in LLMs: An Interaction Perspective

arXiv: 2605.17967

Date: 2026-05-18

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17967

一句话总结： 本文通过追踪SFT过程中token间AND-OR交互的演化，发现SFT的本质是在极短的去噪阶段移除噪声交互（通常在前1000步内），之后继续训练只会引入过拟合的高阶交互，这为SFT的早停策略提供了可量化的理论依据。
TL;DR: By tracking AND-OR token interactions during SFT across multiple LLMs, this paper shows SFT's primary benefit is a brief denoising phase (~1000 steps) that removes noise-like interactions, after which continued training introduces overfitted high-order interactions — providing a principled explanation for why SFT effectiveness is inconsistent and a diagnostic for early stopping.

核心贡献

  • 首次从token交互（AND-OR interactions）的视角系统分析SFT过程中LLM推理模式的演化规律，将交互分为三类：移除的、保留的、新出现的。
  • 发现SFT的核心机制是去噪而非学习新的可靠交互：移除的交互主要是高阶、不可泛化、正负效应相互抵消的噪声模式；保留的交互则是低阶、可泛化、对预测有实质性贡献的可靠模式。
  • 揭示SFT存在两个明确阶段：极短的去噪阶段（约前1000步）和漫长的过拟合阶段。去噪阶段移除大量噪声交互，之后继续微调主要引入不可泛化的过拟合交互。
  • 验证了保留的交互构成了LLM推理的核心骨架——使用仅保留交互的预测概率可获得较低的测试交叉熵损失。
  • 在Qwen2.5-3B/7B、Llama-2-7B、Llama-3-8B、Gemma-3-4B等多个LLM和GoEmotions、Unilaw-R1-Data、Databricks-Dolly-15k等多个数据集上一致验证了上述发现。

Key Contributions

  • First systematic analysis of how AND-OR token interactions evolve during SFT of LLMs, categorizing interactions into removed, preserved, and newly emerged types.
  • Shows SFT's primary mechanism is denoising (removing noise-like high-order, non-generalizable, mutually canceling interactions) rather than acquiring new reliable interactions.
  • Identifies two distinct SFT phases: a short denoising stage (~first 1000 steps) where most noise is removed, followed by a prolonged overfitting stage where high-order non-generalizable interactions accumulate.
  • Demonstrates that preserved interactions form the backbone of LLM inference — they yield lower test loss and larger per-interaction contribution than removed or later-emerged interactions.
  • Validates findings across 5 LLMs (Qwen2.5-3B/7B-Instruct, Llama-2-7B-Chat, Llama-3-8B-Instruct, Gemma-3-4B-it) and 3 datasets (GoEmotions, Unilaw-R1-Data, Databricks-Dolly-15k) using LoRA fine-tuning.

Methodology

The paper builds on prior work by Chen et al. (ICLR 2024) on interaction-based explanation, which decomposes an LLM's output score into a sum of AND-OR interaction effects between input variables (words). Each interaction represents a phrase pattern (e.g., "laws" + "of" + "motion" contributing +0.41 to predicting "acceleration"). The logical model constructed from these interactions universally matches the LLM's outputs across all 2^n masked input states.

The authors track how interactions change at multiple checkpoints during SFT by defining three categories:

  1. Removed interactions: present before SFT but eliminated during fine-tuning.
  2. Preserved interactions: retained throughout the SFT process.
  3. Newly emerged interactions: newly acquired during SFT.

They quantify interaction quality using two metrics:

  • Generalizability (γ): proportion of interactions shared across different LLM architectures (measured via a baseline model with different architecture).
  • Uncancelled-effect ratio (ρ): how much of an interaction set's total effect remains after positive-negative cancellation (low ρ indicates noise).

All experiments use LoRA fine-tuning. Interactions are extracted at the word level following prior work. The authors evaluate on 5 LLMs spanning 3–8B parameters and 3 datasets covering emotion classification, legal reasoning, and instruction following.

Results & Highlights

  • Removed interactions are predominantly noise: they are high-order, have near-zero generalizability (γ ≈ 0), and nearly complete positive-negative cancellation (ρ ≈ 0).
  • Preserved interactions are reliable: they are low-order, have high generalizability (γ > 50% in most cases), and contribute consistently to target token prediction.
  • The denoising stage is extremely brief — most interaction removal happens within the first 1000 training steps. After this, only negligible further removal occurs.
  • Newly emerged interactions in the overfitting stage are noise-like: high-order, non-generalizable, with strong cancellation — strongly correlated with increasing train-test loss gap.
  • Preserved interactions alone achieve lower test cross-entropy loss than removed or later-emerged interactions, confirming they form the inference backbone.
  • Early-emerged interactions (during denoising) also show high quality, while later-emerged ones (during overfitting) show poor quality across all metrics.

Authors & Credibility

The authors are from Shanghai Jiao Tong University (Quanshi Zhang's group), Beijing Institute for General Artificial Intelligence, and UniDT. Quanshi Zhang has an extensive publication record on explainable AI and interaction-based explanations for deep networks, including prior ICLR/NeurIPS/AAAI papers on the theoretical foundations of interactions. This paper directly extends their established interaction framework to the SFT setting, giving it strong theoretical grounding. The work is well-positioned within their research line on using interactions as primitive inference patterns for DNN explanation.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The application of interaction-based analysis to explain SFT dynamics is novel and provides concrete, quantifiable evidence rather than just observational claims about SFT behavior.
  • Rigor: Strong. The findings are validated across 5 different LLMs, 3 datasets, and multiple metrics (generalizability, cancellation ratio, per-interaction contribution, test loss). The interaction extraction method has prior theoretical guarantees (universal matching property). However, the exact transition point between denoising and overfitting stages (claimed as ~1000 steps) is determined post-hoc from visual inspection of plots — a more automated, principled detection method would strengthen the work.
  • Reproducibility: Moderate. The interaction extraction involves hyperparameter choices (word-level variable definition, baseline vector selection) and the generalizability metric requires choosing a specific baseline LLM. No code release is mentioned. The LoRA settings and datasets are standard.
  • Significance: Potentially high. If the interaction metric can serve as a real-time early-stopping signal, it would be practically valuable. The "SFT is mainly denoising" insight reframes how we should think about fine-tuning. The finding challenges the common practice of training on massive SFT datasets.
  • Red flags: (1) The claim that SFT "rarely acquires reliable new interactions" seems at tension with the fact that SFT clearly teaches new task-specific capabilities — the authors should discuss whether their metric captures all forms of learning or only certain types. (2) All experiments use LoRA (parameter-efficient), leaving open whether full fine-tuning shows different dynamics. (3) The models tested are relatively small (3–8B); scaling to 70B+ models is not addressed. (4) The computational cost of interaction extraction, while acknowledged with approximation methods, could limit practical adoption as a monitoring tool.

Strengths

  • Provides a principled, quantifiable framework for understanding what SFT actually does to LLMs internally.
  • Consistent findings across multiple architectures and datasets lend strong credibility.
  • The two-phase (denoising → overfitting) characterization is clear and actionable.
  • Connects explainability research directly to practical training concerns (early stopping, data scale).
  • The "preserved interactions as backbone" finding is well-supported from three independent verification angles.

Limitations

  • Interaction extraction has exponential worst-case complexity; approximation methods help but may introduce their own approximation errors.
  • Only LoRA fine-tuning is tested — full parameter fine-tuning may exhibit different interaction dynamics.
  • Models are limited to 3–8B parameters; applicability to frontier-scale models (70B+) is unverified.
  • The generalizability metric requires a second LLM with different architecture as baseline, which adds complexity and may not always be available.
  • The denoising/overfitting transition point is identified visually rather than through a principled automated criterion.
  • The interaction framework captures phrase-level patterns but may not fully account for other forms of learned knowledge (e.g., factual knowledge stored in weights).

Relevance

本文通过交互解释性方法揭示SFT的"去噪-过拟合"两阶段动态，对理解LLM微调机制和设计高效训练策略（如早停）具有直接参考价值，与LLM训练优化和模型可解释性研究高度相关。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the interaction-based generalizability metric (γ) be computed efficiently enough to serve as a real-time early-stopping criterion during SFT?
  • Do the denoising and overfitting dynamics differ for RLHF/DPO compared to SFT?
  • How does the denoising stage duration scale with model size (e.g., 70B+ models), dataset size, and learning rate?
  • Does full parameter fine-tuning (not just LoRA) exhibit the same two-phase interaction dynamics?
  • Can training procedures be designed to prolong the denoising phase or actively suppress the emergence of overfitted interactions?
  • How do the findings apply to multi-turn conversational SFT versus single-turn instruction tuning?
