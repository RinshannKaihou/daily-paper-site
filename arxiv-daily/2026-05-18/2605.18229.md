> **一句话总结：** 本文通过重置种子噪声、合成数据真值相关性和训练轨迹区分能力三个维度，系统性审计了SAEBench中全部六个SAE质量评估指标，发现TPP和SCR在标准设置下多项基本合理性检验均失败（SCR在top-N≥50时与真值负相关，TPP随训练反而下降），即使最可靠的sae-probes也难以区分同一架构的不同超参数变体，揭示了当前SAE基准测试的严重可靠性危机。

> **TL;DR:** This paper audits all six SAEBench quality metrics through three lenses: reseed noise (5 seeds on a fixed Gemma Scope 65k SAE), ground-truth correlation on SynthSAEBench-16k synthetic SAEs, and discriminability across training trajectories (cross-architecture and Matryoshka hyperparameter-variant panels). TPP and SCR fail multiple sanity checks — TPP declines during training, SCR becomes negatively correlated with ground truth at top-N ≥ 50 and ranks a perfect oracle below 11 trained SAEs. sae-probes is the most reliable metric, but even it struggles to separate same-architecture variants; only 7 of 34 metric configurations carry enough between-variant signal to confidently distinguish Matryoshka SAE variants. The field needs better benchmarks.

## 核心贡献

- 对SAEBench中全部六个SAE质量评估指标（sparse probing、sae-probes、TPP、SCR、autointerp、RAVEL）进行了系统性三维度审计，提出五项理想指标应满足的性质（追踪真值、随训练递增、低重置种子噪声、高区分度、内部一致性）
- 发现TPP在top-N≥50时随训练下降（暗示未训练的SAE优于训练后的SAE），且在合成数据上与真值几乎零相关；SCR在top-N≥50时与真值负相关，在标准top-N=10下将完美oracle排在35个训练SAE中的第12名之后
- 通过四模型重置种子实验揭示噪声谱跨越近两个数量级：sae-probes和sparse probing的CV ≤ 0.3%，而TPP小top-N下CV高达16–39%
- 在Matryoshka超参数变体面板上方差分解表明：34个指标配置中仅7个携带足够的变体间信号（4个核心重建指标、sae-probes k∈{1,5}、RAVEL disentangle）
- 确认sae-probes为目前最可靠指标，其两个关键设计选择（113个数据集vs 5个、交叉验证正则化）为改进其余指标提供了具体方向

## Key Contributions

- Audits all six SAEBench quality metrics (sparse probing, sae-probes, TPP, SCR, autointerp, RAVEL) through three complementary lenses, proposing five desiderata for good proxy metrics (ground-truth tracking, monotone training increase, low reseed noise, discriminability, internal consistency)
- Shows TPP scores decline during training at top-N ≥ 50 across every SAE tested (implying untrained SAEs are "better"), and correlates near-zero with ground truth at canonical top-N = 10 on SynthSAEBench-16k
- Shows SCR becomes negatively correlated with ground truth at top-N ≥ 50 (ρ = −0.64 vs GT-MCC), and at canonical top-N = 10 ranks a perfect oracle below 11 of 35 trained SAEs
- Reveals reseed noise spans nearly two orders of magnitude: sae-probes and sparse probing at CV ≤ 0.3% vs TPP at small top-N with CV 16–39%
- Decomposes score variance on a 4-variant × 3-seed Matryoshka panel: only 7 of 34 metric configurations carry statistically reliable between-variant signal; for most metrics, the "winner" among similar SAEs is seed-driven
- Identifies sae-probes as the most reliable metric and pinpoints its two design advantages (113 datasets vs 5, cross-validated L1 regularization) as a concrete path forward

## Methodology

The paper audits SAEBench metrics through three complementary evaluation lenses on different SAE panels:

1. **Reseed noise (§3):** Ran each SAEBench evaluation 5 times with different random seeds on a fixed canonical Gemma Scope JumpReLU SAE (Gemma-2-2b layer 12, width 65k), replicated on 3 additional SAEs across model families (Gemma Scope 16k, GPT-2-small 32k, Llama Scope 8x 32k). Reports coefficient of variation (CV) and derives minimum-reliable-difference thresholds for 95% two-tailed single-seed comparisons.

2. **Discriminability across training trajectories (§4):** Trained two SAE panels with periodic checkpointing:
   - *Cross-architecture panel:* 4 deliberately different SAEs (BatchTopK k∈{50,100}, Matryoshka k∈{50,100}) on Gemma-2-2b layer 12, 1.5B tokens, 28 checkpoints each.
   - *Matryoshka hyperparameter panel:* 4 Matryoshka SAEs varying inner width levels n∈{1,2,3,4}, 3 independent training seeds per variant, 300M tokens, 10 post-warmup snapshots each. Enables variance decomposition into between-variant signal vs. training-seed noise vs. snapshot jitter.

3. **Ground-truth correlation on synthetic SAEs (§5):** Used SynthSAEBench-16k with 16,384 known ground-truth features. Trained 35 SAEs (5 architectures × 7 L0 levels) plus 3 degraded controls and a perfect oracle at width 4096. Computed Spearman ρ between each proxy metric and ground-truth metrics GT-MCC (mean cosine similarity via Hungarian matching) and GT-F1 (binary classification F1 of latent firing vs. ground-truth firing). Constructed hierarchy-aligned synthetic analogues of each benchmark's task structure.

## Results & Highlights

- **TPP fails multiple sanity checks:** Declines during training at top-N ≥ 50 on every SAE; near-zero correlation with GT-MCC at canonical top-N = 10 (ρ = −0.03); strangely better correlated with out-of-sae features than in-sae features on synthetic data.
- **SCR is unreliable across multiple axes:** Negatively correlated with GT-MCC at top-N ≥ 50 (ρ = −0.64); at canonical top-N = 10, ranks perfect oracle lower than 11/35 trained SAEs; swings from ρ = +0.90 to ρ = −0.10 against GT-MCC across reasonable variations of the same base model.
- **Reseed noise varies dramatically:** sae-probes k=5 (CV 0.2%, |Δ|* = 0.008), RAVEL disentangle (CV 0.2%) are highly reliable; SCR top-500 (CV 6.2%) and TPP top-50 (CV 23%) require multi-seed averaging.
- **Sparse probing saturates:** At canonical top-k=16, in-sae accuracy exceeds 0.99 for almost every trained SAE despite GT-MCC spanning 0.56–0.79, limiting ability to distinguish high-quality SAEs.
- **No non-core metric matches MSE discriminability:** Inter-checkpoint jitter for all quality metrics is much higher than for reconstruction MSE. sae-probes does well on the cross-architecture panel but cannot clearly differentiate Matryoshka variants once seed envelopes are accounted for.
- **Cross-metric ranking agreement collapses on similar SAEs:** Spearman ρ between metric rankings is +0.44 on the cross-architecture panel but only +0.08 on the Matryoshka panel; default sparse probing k=1 vs k=10 are negatively correlated (ρ = −0.4).

- **David Chanin** (Decode Research, MATS, UCL) — Single-author paper. Chanin is also a co-author of both SAEBench and SynthSAEBench, giving him deep familiarity with the evaluation infrastructure being audited. Supported by EPSRC and the MATS program. ## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. While prior work has raised concerns about SAE quality (Kantamneni et al. finding SAE probes underperform baselines; Korznikov et al. showing random baselines match SAEs), this is the first systematic, multi-lens audit of the actual SAEBench metrics. The three-lens framework and variance decomposition methodology are novel contributions.
- **Rigor:** Very high. The paper tests each metric against five explicit desiderata, uses complementary panels (real LLM + synthetic + training trajectories), provides minimum-reliable-difference thresholds with proper statistical derivations, and includes extensive appendices with per-metric breakdowns, ablations on variant synthetic models, and cross-model reseed validation.
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent. Code is on GitHub, SAE panels on HuggingFace, all SAEBench evaluations use standardized configurations. The paper specifies exact seeds, architectures, training tokens, and checkpoint counts.
- **Significance:** Very high. If TPP and SCR (which have been used in prior SAE architecture papers) are unreliable, this undermines conclusions from those papers. The finding that most metrics cannot reliably guide hyperparameter comparisons among similar SAEs is a fundamental challenge to the field's current methodology.
- **Red flags:** Single author limits the breadth of perspective; the discriminability analysis uses a single base model (Gemma-2-2b) — patterns may differ at other scales; the synthetic evaluation (SynthSAEBench-16k) is one synthetic model architecture, so calibration generalization is open. The paper does not propose new metrics, only audits existing ones.

## Strengths

- Tackles a foundational methodological issue with direct implications for the validity of prior and future SAE research
- Three-lens audit framework is elegant and complementary: each lens isolates a different failure mode
- Variance decomposition on the Matryoshka panel is a particularly powerful analysis — quantifying exactly how much of a metric's signal is noise vs. real quality difference
- Clear, actionable recommendations: avoid TPP and SCR; prefer sae-probes; use multi-seed averaging for noisy metrics
- Identifies concrete design features (larger dataset suites, cross-validated regularization) that make sae-probes more reliable, providing a roadmap for improving other metrics
- Author's dual role as SAEBench co-author and its auditor demonstrates exceptional intellectual honesty

## Limitations

- Does not propose new benchmark metrics — the field is left with "sae-probes is the least bad option"
- sae-probes still cannot reliably distinguish same-architecture hyperparameter variants, leaving practitioners without a gold standard for the most common comparison scenario
- Discriminability and validity analyses use only Gemma-2-2b; patterns may differ at larger scales or other model families
- SynthSAEBench-16k is one synthetic model; calibration findings may not generalize
- Autointerp and RAVEL cannot be tested under the ground-truth lens (require natural language / real LLM), so their validity remains partially unchecked
- The paper audits SAEBench-as-configured versions of metrics; standalone benchmark implementations may behave differently

## Relevance

与机制可解释性研究直接相关——SAE是目前LLM可解释性的核心工具，而其评估基准的可靠性直接决定了整个领域能否真正取得进展。如果你在研究中使用或比较SAE架构，这项审计表明应避免依赖TPP和SCR指标，优先使用sae-probes，并采用多种子平均来降低噪声影响。对判断已发表SAE架构比较论文的结论是否可靠也具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the sae-probes design principles (large diverse task suites, cross-validated regularization) be systematically applied to rebuild TPP/SCR into reliable metrics?
- How do these findings scale to larger models (e.g., Gemma-2-9B, Llama-3.1-70B) and different layer depths?
- What is the minimum number of datasets/tasks needed for a reliable SAE quality metric, and what diversity properties must they satisfy?
- How many prior SAE architecture comparison conclusions change when unreliable metrics are excluded — is there a systematic re-evaluation to be done?
- Can information-theoretic measures (e.g., mutual information between SAE latents and ground-truth features) provide a more principled metric foundation?
- Would ensembling multiple independent metrics (e.g., sae-probes + RAVEL disentangle + autointerp) improve reliability beyond any single metric?
- Can the three-lens audit framework be standardized as a checklist for future SAE benchmark proposals?
