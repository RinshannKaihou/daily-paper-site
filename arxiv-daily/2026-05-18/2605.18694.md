> **一句话总结：** 本文首次证明了 AdaGrad 在重尾梯度噪声（p > 4/3）下的非凸优化收敛速率，且无需任何算法修改或噪声参数先验知识，同时给出了算法相关的下界表明 AdaGrad 无法达到已有的极小极大最优速率。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides the first provable convergence rate for AdaGrad under heavy-tailed gradient noise (tail index p > 4/3) in non-convex optimization without any algorithmic modifications or prior knowledge of p, derives an algorithm-dependent lower bound showing AdaGrad cannot attain the minimax rate, and shows AdaGrad-Norm achieves a faster rate for all p ∈ (1, 2] under bounded objectives.

## 核心贡献

- **首次证明 AdaGrad 在重尾噪声下的收敛性**：在尾指数 p ∈ (4/3, 2] 时，AdaGrad 可达到 Õ(1/T^{(3p-4)/4p}) 的收敛速率，且不需要对 p 的任何先验知识
- **算法相关的下界**：构造了 AdaGrad 的下界，表明其无法达到重尾非凸优化的极小极大最优速率 Θ(1/T^{(3p-2)})，即使已知问题参数也有额外的对数因子不可避免
- **AdaGrad-Norm 的更快速率**：在有界目标函数假设下，证明 AdaGrad-Norm 对所有 p ∈ (1, 2] 均能达到 O(1/T^{(p-1)/2p}) 的速率，与 NSGD(M) 的最佳已知速率匹配
- **新的证明技术**：提出了一种推广的代理步长（proxy stepsize）技术，通过精心选择参数 c_i 实现更紧的界，自然地退化为经典有限方差情形

## Key Contributions

- **First convergence guarantee for AdaGrad under heavy-tailed noise**: Establishes an Õ(1/T^{(3p-4)/4p}) rate for AdaGrad when p ∈ (4/3, 2], requiring no algorithmic modifications, nonstandard assumptions, or prior knowledge of the tail index p.
- **Algorithm-dependent lower bound for AdaGrad**: Constructs a lower bound (Theorem 3.3) that explicitly captures the dependence on the learning rate γ, showing that AdaGrad cannot match the minimax rate for heavy-tailed non-convex optimization — even when Δ and L are known, an extra polylogarithmic factor is unavoidable.
- **Improved rates for AdaGrad-Norm**: Proves that AdaGrad-Norm achieves O(1/T^{(p-1)/2p}) for all p ∈ (1, 2] under a bounded-objective assumption (Theorem 4.2), matching the best-known rate for NSGD(M) without prior parameter knowledge.
- **Novel proxy stepsize technique**: Generalizes the classical proxy stepsize choice from Ward et al. (2019) by introducing coordinate-dependent parameters c_i that depend on accumulated gradient information, recovering the standard choice when p = 2.

## Methodology

The paper studies the standard non-convex stochastic optimization setting min_{x∈ℝ^d} f(x) under coordinate-wise heavy-tailed noise (E[|ξ_{t,i}|^p] ≤ σ_i^p for p ∈ (1, 2]).

**Upper bound analysis (AdaGrad):** The proof extends the proxy stepsize framework of Ward et al. (2019). The key technical innovation is the choice of proxy stepsize parameter c_i = σ_i^{T^{1/2 - 1/p̄}} / D_{T,i}^{1/p̄}, which depends on the entire optimization trajectory. This enables the tighter rate Õ(1/T^{(3p-4)/4p}) compared to the Õ(1/T^{(2p-3)/2p}) rate obtained with the naive choice c = σ.

**Lower bound construction:** Builds on the framework of Jiang et al. (2025) and Hübler et al. (2025). For d = 1, constructs a family of functions parameterized by the learning rate γ such that AdaGrad cannot find an ε-stationary point with fewer than a certain number of iterations, capturing explicit γ-dependence.

**AdaGrad-Norm analysis:** Inspired by Liu (2026), leverages the observation that E[√v_t] ≤ ∥σ∥_p t^{1/p} + E[√u_t] (Lemma 4.7) together with the bounded-objective assumption to derive a tighter telescoping argument. The norm-based (rather than coordinate-wise) structure of AdaGrad-Norm is critical for obtaining the faster rate.

## Results & Highlights

- **Theorem 3.1**: AdaGrad converges at rate Õ(1/T^{(3p-4)/4p}) for p ∈ (4/3, 2], adaptive to both p and the noise level ∥σ∥_1 without knowing either. Recovers the well-known Õ(1/T^{1/4}) rate when p = 2.
- **Theorem 3.3**: Algorithm-dependent lower bound for AdaGrad in 1D, showing the minimax rate Θ(1/T^{(3p-2)}) is unattainable. Also improves the best-known p = 2 lower bound from Jiang et al. (2025).
- **Theorem 4.2**: AdaGrad-Norm achieves O(1/T^{(p-1)/2p}) for all p ∈ (1, 2] under bounded objectives — this rate is never vacuous and matches NSGD(M).
- **Theorem C.1**: Without bounded-objective assumption, AdaGrad-Norm still achieves Õ(1/T^{(3p-4)/4p}), matching AdaGrad's rate.
- The result is the **first theoretical evidence** that adaptive gradient methods have an advantage over SGD under heavy-tailed noise, since SGD diverges without clipping/normalization in this setting.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** This is the first work to establish convergence of a practical adaptive gradient method (AdaGrad, without modifications) under heavy-tailed noise. The proxy stepsize technique with trajectory-dependent c_i is genuinely new. The algorithm-dependent lower bound is also a first for this setting.
- **Rigor: High.** The proofs are technically sound, following established frameworks while introducing meaningful innovations. All assumptions (lower boundedness, smoothness, unbiased gradients, heavy-tailed noise) are standard. The paper clearly states when extra assumptions (bounded objectives) are used.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** All theorems reference appendices with full proofs. The algorithm definitions are standard (Algorithm 1 = AdaGrad from McMahan & Streeter 2010 / Duchi et al. 2011; Algorithm 2 = AdaGrad-Norm from Ward et al. 2019). The analysis uses no proprietary data.
- **Significance: Moderate-to-High.** Addresses a fundamental open question in optimization theory for deep learning. However, the result is limited to AdaGrad (not Adam/AdamW), and the upper bound becomes vacuous for p ≤ 4/3. The gap between the upper and lower bounds for AdaGrad remains unresolved.
- **Red flags**: (1) The AdaGrad upper bound is vacuous for p ≤ 4/3, which the author acknowledges as a limitation. (2) The gap between upper and lower bounds is significant — it remains unclear whether either is tight. (3) The results do not directly extend to Adam or AdamW, which are far more widely used in practice. (4) The paper studies the single-worker (non-distributed) setting only.

## Strengths

- First rigorous proof that adaptive gradient methods can handle heavy-tailed noise without modifications — a result that aligns with empirical practice.
- The adaptivity to the tail index p without prior knowledge is a strong practical feature, contrasting with clipped SGD which requires knowing p.
- Clean presentation: the paper carefully positions itself against prior work (especially Chezhegov et al., 2025) and is transparent about limitations.
- The proxy stepsize generalization that naturally recovers the classical choice at p = 2 is elegant.
- ICML 2026 acceptance provides community validation.

## Limitations

- **Scope**: Only AdaGrad is studied for the main results. Whether Adam, AdamW, or RMSProp converge under heavy-tailed noise remains open.
- **Range of p**: The main AdaGrad result requires p > 4/3. For very heavy tails (p close to 1), no guarantee is provided.
- **Tightness**: The gap between the upper bound Õ(1/T^{(3p-4)/4p}) and the lower bound is not resolved. Either or both may be loose.
- **Practical relevance**: AdaGrad is largely superseded by Adam/AdamW in modern LLM training, so the practical impact is somewhat indirect.
- **Assumptions**: The faster AdaGrad-Norm rate requires bounded objectives, which is a non-trivial extra assumption not typically needed in non-convex optimization theory.
- **No empirical experiments**: The paper is purely theoretical with no experimental validation.

## Relevance

本文严格分析了自适应梯度方法在重尾噪声下的收敛性，属于"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"范畴。虽然结论是理论性的，但对理解 LLM 训练中广泛使用的 Adam/AdamW 的鲁棒性有启发性，为后续分析实际优化器的可靠性提供了基础框架。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the upper bound for AdaGrad be extended to p ∈ (1, 4/3], perhaps by modifying the proxy stepsize technique or using a different proof strategy?
2. Do the convergence results for AdaGrad imply analogous guarantees for Adam and AdamW, given their shared adaptive stepsize structure?
3. Is the Õ(1/T^{(3p-4)/4p}) upper bound tight for AdaGrad, or can it be improved to match the O(1/T^{(p-1)/2p}) rate of AdaGrad-Norm?
4. How do these results interact with generalized smoothness assumptions (e.g., (L₀, L₁)-smoothness), which may be more realistic for neural network training?
5. Can the algorithm-dependent lower bound analysis be extended to distributed or federated settings where heavy-tailed noise is prevalent?
