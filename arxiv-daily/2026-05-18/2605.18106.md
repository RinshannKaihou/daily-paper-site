> **一句话总结：** 本文提出对称性兼容的优化器设计原则——梯度更新应与权重块的对称群保持等变，据此为嵌入层（行范数更新）、LM头（混合行范数/谱更新）、SwiGLU MLP投影（行/列感知更新）和MoE路由器（中心化行范数/左谱更新）推导了专用等变优化器，在Qwen3-0.6B、Gemma 3 1B、OLMoE-1B-7B和gpt-oss四种架构的预训练中一致优于AdamW。

> **TL;DR:** AdamW treats all parameters as independent coordinates, ignoring matrix geometry. This paper proves that the update map for each weight block should be equivariant under its symmetry group: bi-orthogonal for linear layers (→ spectral/Muon updates), left-permutation/right-orthogonal for embeddings and LM heads (→ RowNormM, HybridPolarGradM), intermediate-neuron permutation for SwiGLU MLPs (→ row/column-aware updates), and expert-permutation + shared-logit-shift for MoE routers (→ centered row-norm/left-spectral updates). The resulting layerwise optimizer stack consistently outperforms AdamW across four dense and MoE LLM pre-training experiments (up to ~3.5B params), with gains becoming more pronounced as vocabulary size increases.

## 核心贡献

- 提出对称性兼容原则：优化器的更新映射 \(\mathcal{U}\) 应满足 \(\mathcal{U}(g \cdot D) = g \cdot \mathcal{U}(D)\)，其中 \(g\) 属于参数块的对称群，\(D\) 是更新方向（梯度或动量）。
- 证明双正交等变更新恰为谱算子（Theorem 3.7），统一了SSD、Muon、Scion、PolarGrad等方法的几何解释。
- 为嵌入/LM头矩阵推导了左排列-右正交（LPRO）等变更新族：行范数（RowNormM）、右谱（RightPolarGradM）和混合（HybridPolarGradM）。
- 证明SwiGLU MLP仅具有中间神经元排列对称性（非完全左正交），据此为gate/up投影设计行感知更新、为down投影设计列感知更新。
- 为MoE路由器推导了专家排列等变+共享logit偏移不变的更新：中心化行范数和左谱更新（LeftPolarGradM），通过投影 \(\Pi_\perp = I - \frac{1}{e}\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^\top\) 消除共享行分量。
- 在四种开放权重架构上构建了端到端的分层优化器栈，验证了对称性兼容更新的有效性。
- 代码已开源：https://github.com/timlautk/equivariant_optimizers

## Key Contributions

- **Symmetry-compatible principle**: Formalize that optimizer update maps should commute with the symmetry group of each parameter block, providing a unified theory for matrix-gradient optimizer design.
- **Theorem 3.7 (Characterization)**: Prove that bi-orthogonally equivariant direction-wise update maps are exactly spectral operators — they preserve singular vectors and act only on singular values. This gives Muon/Scion/PolarGrad a complete geometric justification.
- **LPRO-equivariant updates for embeddings/LM heads**: Derive three families — row-norm (RowNormM, cheapest), right-spectral (RightPolarGradM), and hybrid (HybridPolarGradM) — all equivariant under vocabulary permutation × hidden-dim orthogonal symmetry.
- **SwiGLU MLP symmetry analysis**: Show that SwiGLU blocks have intermediate-neuron permutation symmetry but *not* full left-orthogonal symmetry (Remark 3.3: \(\sigma(Qz) \neq Q\sigma(z)\) generally), motivating row-aware (gate/up) and column-aware (down) updates.
- **MoE router updates**: Derive router-compatible updates that are expert-permutation equivariant and shared-logit-shift invariant, using centered momentum \(M_{k,c} = \Pi_\perp M_k\) to remove the shared-row component.
- **Practical algorithms**: Provide concrete momentum variants (LeftPolarGradM, RightPolarGradM, RowNormM, HybridPolarGradM) with GPU-friendly Newton–Schulz / Polar Express iterations for inverse square roots.
- **End-to-end pre-training validation**: First work to assign symmetry-matched optimizers to all major matrix parameter classes in LLM pre-training across four architectures.

## Methodology

The paper develops a layerwise equivariance framework rooted in the observation that when weight matrices undergo coordinate changes \(W \mapsto PWQ^\top\), the gradient transforms contravariantly as \(G \mapsto PGQ^\top\). The optimizer update map \(\mathcal{U}\) should transform the same way: \(\mathcal{U}(PGQ^\top) = P\mathcal{U}(G)Q^\top\).

**Step 1: General matrix layers** (bi-orthogonal symmetry \(O_m \times O_n\)). Theorem 3.7 characterizes all bi-orthogonally equivariant maps as spectral operators. This recovers polar-factor updates (Muon, Scion, PolarGrad) as the canonical class. The orthogonal polar factor \(\text{polar}(D) = UV^\top\) from SVD \(D = U\Sigma V^\top\) is the prototypical instance.

**Step 2: Embedding and LM head matrices** (left-permutation × right-orthogonal, \(P_v \times O_d\)). Since rows index vocabulary items (discrete, permutable) and columns index hidden features (continuous, rotatable), the symmetry group is smaller than full bi-orthogonal. This allows additional valid update maps:
- **Row-norm**: \(\mathcal{U}_{\text{row}}(D) = \text{Diag}(\eta(\|D_{i:}\|_2)) D\) — cheap, purely row-adaptive.
- **Right-spectral**: \(\mathcal{U}_R(D) = D(D^\top D + \varepsilon I)^{-1/2}\) — acts through the smaller \(d \times d\) Gram matrix.
- **Hybrid**: Compose both (in either order), still LPRO-equivariant by closure (Proposition 3.3).

**Step 3: SwiGLU MLP projections** (intermediate-neuron permutation). Proposition 3.4 shows that permuting gate/up rows and corresponding down columns leaves the SwiGLU output invariant. Full left-orthogonal symmetry fails because \(\sigma(Qz) \neq Q\sigma(z)\). Thus gate/up projections receive LPRO updates (row-aware), while down projections receive the transposed geometry updates (column-aware).

**Step 4: MoE routers** (expert-permutation × shared-logit-shift). The softmax routing \(\text{softmax}(Wx)\) is invariant under expert permutation and shared logit shifts \(W \mapsto PW + \mathbf{1}_e a^\top\). This motivates centering via \(\Pi_\perp = I_e - \frac{1}{e}\mathbf{1}_e\mathbf{1}_e^\top\), yielding centered left-Gram \(D_c D_c^\top\) for left-spectral updates and centered row norms for row-norm updates.

**Implementation**: All polar/spectral computations use Newton–Schulz iterations with Polar Express coefficients. Gram inverse square roots are computed in float32 for stability. Momentum buffers preserve equivariance because they are linear combinations of past gradients (Proposition 3.1).

## Results & Highlights

**Qwen3-0.6B-style** (625M params, vocab 151K):
- RowNormM on embeddings/LM head: val loss 4.1991 vs AdamW 4.2084 (Muon on SwiGLU).
- With HybridPolarGradM also on SwiGLU: RowNormM achieves 4.1962, HybridPolarGradM 4.1978, AdamW 4.2046.
- Gains are modest at this scale but consistent.

**Gemma 3 1B-style** (1.09B params, vocab 262K):
- Larger vocabulary amplifies the effect. RowNormM: 4.0699 vs AdamW 4.1046 (Muon on SwiGLU).
- With HybridPolarGradM on SwiGLU: HybridPolarGradM on emb/head achieves best at 4.0461, RowNormM at 4.0552, AdamW at 4.0862.
- Gap between symmetry-compatible and AdamW widens with larger vocab matrices (~0.04–0.06 loss reduction).

**OLMoE-1B-7B-style** (2.8B params, vocab 50K, 32 experts):
- RowNormM + RowNormM routers: 4.0814; RowNormM + LeftPolarGradM routers: 4.0717; AdamW on all special matrices: 4.1155.
- Geometry-compatible router updates improve over AdamW routers; the fully symmetric stack outperforms by ~0.03–0.04.
- AdamW baseline shows slightly more training loss spikes around 2.1B tokens.

**Downsized gpt-oss** (3.5B params, vocab 201K, 16 experts):
- RowNormM everywhere: 4.3090; + LeftPolarGradM routers: 4.3122; AdamW: 4.3704.
- Dominant improvement from replacing AdamW on vocabulary-indexed matrices (~0.06 gain).
- Pattern consistent despite architectural differences (QKV biases, router biases).

**Cross-model trend**: Gains are modest for smaller models, become more pronounced with larger vocabulary-indexed matrices, and persist across both dense and MoE architectures.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The symmetry-compatible principle is cleanly articulated and goes beyond unifying existing spectral methods — the derivations for embeddings (LPRO equivariance), SwiGLU (intermediate-neuron permutation), and MoE routers (centered expert geometry) are genuinely new. The proof that bi-orthogonally equivariant maps are exactly spectral operators (Theorem 3.7) is a clean mathematical result.
- **Rigor**: Strong for the theoretical framework. The equivariance conditions are precisely stated and the update rules are derived systematically. Proposition 3.4 correctly identifies that SwiGLU only has permutation (not full orthogonal) symmetry. However, no convergence guarantees are provided for the new optimizer classes.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Code is open-source. All architectures are based on publicly known model families. Training data (FineWeb-Edu 10B tokens), context length (1024), and key hyperparameters are specified. The paper provides multiple random seeds for Gemma 3 1B experiments.
- **Significance**: Potentially high. If validated at larger scale, the layerwise optimizer stack could become standard practice for LLM training. The principle is elegant and general — it could be applied to future architecture components. However, the practical impact depends on whether gains persist at production scale.
- **Red flags**:
  - All experiments use only 10B tokens — far below typical pre-training budgets (100B–1T+ tokens). It is unclear whether the relative gains persist over longer training.
  - Model sizes are moderate (0.6B–3.5B). No evidence at 7B+ dense or 70B+ scale.
  - HybridPolarGradM incurs meaningful wall-clock overhead from Gram Newton–Schulz iterations. The paper acknowledges this but does not provide a rigorous FLOPs or throughput comparison.
  - The paper does not compare against other strong baselines like Shampoo, SOAP, or Adafactor — the comparison is primarily against AdamW.
  - RowNormM (the cheapest variant) often performs comparably to or better than the more expensive HybridPolarGradM on embeddings/LM heads, raising the question of whether the spectral component adds value beyond simple row normalization.

## Strengths

- Elegant unifying principle: equivariance of the update map under the parameter's symmetry group.
- Theorem 3.7 provides a complete characterization of bi-orthogonally equivariant update maps as spectral operators — a crisp mathematical result.
- Systematic architecture-optimizer co-design: each parameter class gets a principled, symmetry-matched update.
- Correct identification that SwiGLU has only permutation (not orthogonal) symmetry — a subtle but important distinction.
- Practical modular design: each component can be adopted independently as a drop-in replacement.
- Open-source code with clear algorithmic descriptions (Algorithms 1–2).
- Consistent positive results across four architectures (two dense, two MoE) with different vocabulary sizes.

## Limitations

- Small training budgets (10B tokens) and moderate model sizes (up to 3.5B). Scaling to production-scale training is unverified.
- No comparison against other structured optimizers (Shampoo, SOAP, Adafactor, SCALE) — only AdamW.
- Computational overhead of spectral updates is acknowledged but not rigorously quantified. RowNormM appears nearly as effective with far less cost.
- No convergence proofs for the derived optimizer classes.
- The LPRO equivariance class is broader than just row-norm and right-spectral maps — the full space of valid updates is not characterized as tightly as the bi-orthogonal case.
- Momentum-first vs. polar-first distinction (Remark 3.1) is theoretically motivated but not empirically ablated.

## Relevance

这篇论文与优化理论和大语言模型训练高度相关。对称性兼容原则为设计新一代优化器提供了清晰的理论指导，对理解谱方法（Muon等）为何有效、以及如何系统性地为不同层类型选择优化器具有重要意义。特别是在大词汇表场景下，行范数更新作为一种低成本替代方案具有直接的实用价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the symmetry-compatible stack maintain its advantage at 100B+ token budgets and 70B+ parameter scale, or does AdamW eventually catch up?
- RowNormM often matches or beats HybridPolarGradM on embeddings — is the spectral component necessary, or is pure row normalization sufficient for vocabulary-indexed matrices?
- How do these optimizers interact with learning rate scheduling (cosine, warmup, decay) and scaling-law-prescribed hyperparameters?
- Can the principle be applied to attention QKV projections (which may have head-structured symmetries) or to state-space model matrices?
- What is the overhead breakdown in wall-clock time: is RowNormM essentially free relative to AdamW, and at what matrix sizes does RightPolarGradM become bottlenecked by Newton–Schulz iterations?
- How does the centered expert geometry for MoE routers interact with load-balancing losses and router z-loss?
