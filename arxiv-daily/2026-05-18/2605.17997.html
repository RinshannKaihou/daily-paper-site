MARR: Module-Adaptive Residual Reconstruction for Low-Bit Post-Training Quantization

arXiv: 2605.17997

Date: 2026-05-18

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17997

一句话总结： MARR通过为每个模块分配自适应的残差缩放系数，利用PID控制器逐步优化，有效平衡了低比特量化中残差校正收益与Hessian近似偏差之间的矛盾，在W2A4量化下实现最高20.2%的困惑度降低。

Key Contributions

  • Identifies and formally analyzes the two-sided effect of cross-layer residuals in reconstruction-based PTQ: residual terms correct accumulated quantization error but simultaneously introduce Hessian-approximation (HA) bias.
  • Proposes MARR (Module-Adaptive Residual Reconstruction), which assigns a module-specific scaling coefficient $\alpha_m$ to each module's residual term, enabling per-module balance between error correction and HA bias.
  • Designs a PID-based adaptive estimation strategy that treats module-level reconstruction error as feedback to efficiently estimate $\alpha_m$ within 3 update steps, avoiding exhaustive per-module grid search.
  • Demonstrates consistent improvements on LLMs (up to 20.2% perplexity reduction on C4, W2A4) and ViTs (up to 4.6% relative accuracy gain on ImageNet) over state-of-the-art residual reconstruction methods.

Methodology

MARR builds on residual reconstruction PTQ methods (GPTAQ, ResComp) that extend GPTQ by incorporating cross-layer activation residuals. The key insight is formalized through a Hessian-approximation error analysis (Eq. 6): the HA bias decomposes into a weight-perturbation term and a residual-related term, the latter being controllable via a scaling coefficient.

Scaled-residual reconstruction objective: The residual $r = wX - w\hat{X}$ is scaled by $\alpha$, yielding the modified closed-form update $\Delta w = \frac{\hat{w}_q - w_q}{H_{qq}^{-1}} H_{q,:}^{-1} + \alpha r \hat{X}^\top H_{-q}^{-1}$, where $\alpha = 0$ recovers GPTQ and $\alpha = 1$ recovers GPTAQ.

Module-specific coefficient: The optimal $\alpha_m^* = \arg\min D(z_m, \hat{z}_m(\alpha_m))$ is different for each module because activation statistics, residual magnitudes, and HA gaps vary across modules. Empirical sweeps confirm that optimal $\alpha$ varies substantially across layers (Figure 2).

PID-based estimation: Since $J_m(\alpha_m)$ is piecewise non-smooth under low-bit quantization, gradient-based optimization is infeasible. Instead, MARR uses a zero-order feedback loop:

  1. A deviation signal $d_m^{(t)} = \tanh(-\beta g_m^{(t)})$ bounded to $[-1, 1]$ measures whether recent $\alpha$ changes improved reconstruction.
  2. An incremental PID rule combines proportional, integral, and derivative components with $K_p = K_i = K_d = 1.0$.
  3. Early stopping triggers when relative objective change falls below $\tau = 10^{-5}$.
  4. Maximum $T = 3$ steps; initial $\alpha_m = 1.0$ for all modules.

Results & Highlights

LLMs (weight+activation quantization):

  • GPTAQ-MARR achieves 14.95% perplexity reduction on WikiText2 over GPTAQ under W2A4 on Llama2-7b (9.56 vs 11.24).
  • On C4, GPTAQ-MARR achieves 20.24% perplexity reduction over GPTAQ under W2A4 on Llama2-7b (15.53 vs 19.47).
  • Zero-shot average accuracy improves by up to 2.54% over GPTAQ under W2A4 (Llama2-13b).

LLMs (weight-only quantization):

  • GPTAQ-MARR achieves 8.23% and 11.22% WikiText2 perplexity reduction over GPTAQ at W2A16 on Llama2-7b and Llama2-13b respectively.
  • Average zero-shot accuracy improvements are more limited or task-dependent under weight-only settings.

Vision Transformers:

  • GPTAQ-MARR achieves up to 4.61% relative accuracy gain over GPTAQ under W3A3 on DeiT-S (59.01 vs 56.41).
  • Consistent improvements across W4A4, W3A3, and W2A4 on DeiT-T/S/B.

PTQ overhead: MARR introduces 2.1–2.7× one-time quantization cost over GPTAQ (e.g., ~73 min vs ~30 min for Llama2-7b on a single A6000).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (Moderate-High): The observation that residual terms introduce HA bias is novel and well-motivated. The module-adaptive scaling idea is straightforward but effective. The PID-based estimation is a creative zero-order optimization approach, though PID controllers have been used in deep learning before.
  • Rigor (High): Theoretical analysis is clean — the HA bias decomposition in Eq. 6 is well-derived. The PID convergence is honestly framed as "practical stability" rather than global optimality. All experiments use the same calibration data, quantization settings, and evaluation protocols for fair comparison.
  • Reproducibility (Moderate-High): Detailed hyperparameters and algorithm pseudocode are provided. All experiments run on a single A6000. However, code is not yet publicly available ("upon acceptance"), and the fixed PID gains ($K_p=K_i=K_d=1.0$) lack systematic sensitivity analysis.
  • Significance (Moderate-High): Consistent improvements across LLMs and ViTs at low bit-widths are practically valuable for deployment. The largest gains appear under W2A4, the most challenging setting. However, the 2.1–2.7× overhead is non-trivial, and weight-only gains on zero-shot tasks are inconsistent (e.g., Llama3-8b W2A16 shows accuracy regression).
  • Red flags: The paper does not compare with very recent quantization methods (e.g., spinquant, OWQ variants). Some reported improvements under W4A4 are marginal (< 1%). The PID gain selection is not well-justified beyond citing prior work.

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical motivation: the two-sided effect of residuals is rigorously analyzed with a clear HA bias decomposition.
  • Simple and general framework: MARR is a plug-in extension applicable to both GPTAQ and ResComp backbones.
  • Comprehensive evaluation: 3 LLM families × 2 bit-width settings × 2 quantization modes + 3 ViT models × 3 bit-width settings, plus weight-only experiments.
  • Honest limitations section discussing overhead, limited weight-only gains, and unexplored PID sensitivity.
  • Minimal hyperparameter tuning: all experiments use the same fixed PID gains and $T=3$.

Limitations

  • Quantization overhead: 2.1–2.7× slower than GPTAQ (e.g., ~73 min vs ~30 min for Llama2-7b), which may be significant for very large models.
  • Inconsistent zero-shot gains under weight-only: On Llama3-8b W2A16, GPTAQ-MARR shows 1.42% accuracy regression, suggesting the per-module calibration on 128 WikiText2 samples may not transfer well to all downstream tasks.
  • PID gains unexplored: $K_p = K_i = K_d = 1.0$ is set without systematic sweep; sensitivity remains unknown.
  • Limited baseline scope: No comparison with QAT methods or very recent PTQ techniques beyond GPTAQ/ResComp.
  • Only tested on Llama-family and DeiT models: Generalization to other architectures (e.g., Qwen, Mixtral, Swin Transformer) is not demonstrated.
