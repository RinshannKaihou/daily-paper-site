> **一句话总结：** 本文提出利用候选模型在专家推理轨迹上的 token 级预测分布统计量（如熵、top-k 准确率、专家 token 排名）构建代理指标，可在单次前向传播中高效预测下游任务性能，在跨家族模型选择（Spearman ρ=0.81）、预训练数据选择（节省约 10000 倍算力）和训练过程预测（外推误差减半）三个场景中显著优于交叉熵损失基线。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes proxy metrics derived from token-level statistics of a candidate model's next-token distribution over expert reasoning trajectories. A single forward pass yields 80 complementary metrics that reliably forecast downstream performance across three settings: cross-family model ranking (ρ=0.81 vs. 0.36 for CE loss), pretraining data selection (~10,000x less compute than direct evaluation), and training-time forecasting (half the extrapolation error of loss- or compute-based baselines over an 18x compute horizon).

## 核心贡献

- 提出了一套基于专家轨迹的代理指标体系：通过候选模型对专家推理轨迹的 token 级预测分布，构建 10 种核心指标 × 8 种加权方案 = 80 个互补代理指标，仅需单次前向传播即可计算。
- 在跨家族模型选择任务中，线性 RankSVM 组合代理指标在 6 个推理基准、18 个异构模型上实现平均 Spearman ρ=0.81，远超交叉熵损失的 0.36。
- 在 DataDecide 预训练数据选择基准上，仅使用 4M-90M 参数的代理模型即可达到决策准确率 >0.85（仅需目标算力的 10^{-5}），刷新了 Pareto 前沿并超越了 rBridge。
- 在训练过程预测中，代理指标沿训练轨迹遵循幂律规律，从 80K 步外推至 1.4M 步（18 倍算力跨度）时，RMSE 仅为 CE 损失和算力基线的一半。
- 方法仅需专家的离散 token（不需要 logprobs），因此可使用闭源模型或人类专家作为专家来源。

## Key Contributions

- Constructed a library of 80 proxy metrics from token-level statistics (entropy, top-k accuracy, expert token rank, margin, etc.) with 8 weighting schemes, all extractable from a single forward pass per instance over expert reasoning trajectories.
- Demonstrated cross-family model selection: a linear RankSVM over proxy metrics achieves mean Spearman ρ=0.81 under leave-2-tasks-out CV across 18 models (6 families, 0.6B-70B) and 6 reasoning benchmarks (AIME, HMMT, GPQA, USACO, MMLU-Pro, SuperGPQA).
- Achieved new SOTA on the DataDecide pretraining data selection benchmark: frequency-weighted top-5 accuracy reaches decision accuracy >0.85 at ~10^{-5} of target compute, displacing rBridge's Pareto frontier while requiring only expert tokens (not logprobs).
- Showed proxy metrics follow smooth power laws along training trajectories, enabling reliable extrapolation across an 18x compute horizon with mean RMSE of 0.024 (vs. 0.059 for CE loss, 0.055 for compute) on 10 OLMES benchmarks using OLMo-3-7B checkpoints.
- Established that expert trajectories (from frontier models or humans) provide a dense, task-conditioned signal combining the smoothness of loss with grounding in the reasoning process.

## Methodology

The method operates on a simple but powerful principle: pass expert-written reasoning trajectories through the candidate model and aggregate token-level statistics from the model's predictive distribution.

**Core metrics (10):** Cross-entropy, top-k accuracy (k∈{1,2,3,5}), entropy, rank, reciprocal rank, margin, wrong-confidence — spanning three aspects of model-expert alignment: agreement frequency, distribution concentration, and confident errors.

**Weighting schemes (8):** Uniform, probability, expert-disagreement, entropy, inverse entropy, frequency, inverse frequency, Gaussian-NLL — emphasizing different notions of token importance (uncertainty, disagreement, rarity).

**Aggregation:** Each (metric, weighting) pair yields a proxy Φ_j via a sign-corrected weighted average. The 80-dimensional vector is computed from a single forward pass over the last 1,000 tokens of each expert trajectory.

**For ranking:** Learn f: R^80 → R using RankSVM on pairwise preference pairs from held-in tasks/models.

**For extrapolation:** Fit power laws f(t) = a - b·t^{-c} along training checkpoints, selecting the best proxy via inner-split validation.

Key design choice: using only the last 1,000 tokens of expert trajectories (empirically outperforms full traces), and not filtering out incorrect expert trajectories (simulating imperfect experts).

## Results & Highlights

- **Cross-family model selection:** Linear RankSVM achieves mean ρ=0.81 across 15 leave-2-tasks-out folds and 20 model subsampling seeds. CE loss on FineWeb achieves only ρ=0.36; rBridge achieves ρ=0.33. The 3-sparse proxy already reaches ρ=0.78.
- **Pretraining data selection:** On DataDecide's 25 corpora, the best proxy (frequency-weighted top-5 accuracy) achieves >0.85 decision accuracy at 10^{-5} target compute. Matching this with downstream evaluation requires ~10^{-1} target compute — a ~10,000x cost reduction.
- **Training-time forecasting:** Proxy-to-accuracy power-law fits extrapolate from 80K to 1.4M steps (18x) with mean RMSE=0.024 across 10 OLMES benchmarks, vs. RMSE=0.059 for CE loss and 0.055 for compute-based sigmoid fits. On HellaSwag specifically: proxy RMSE=0.003 vs. CE loss RMSE=0.09.
- **Power-law regularity:** Proxy metrics follow smooth power laws along both pretraining (OLMo-3-7B, 6 benchmarks) and post-training (OLMo-3-7B-Think, 5 benchmarks) trajectories with mean NMAE of 0.03-0.038.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The core idea — using token-level predictive distribution statistics over expert trajectories as proxy metrics — is elegant and novel. While individual components (token-level metrics, expert trajectories, RankSVM) are not new, their composition into a principled 80-metric library with cross-task validation is a genuine contribution. The insight that "a model that cannot solve a task may still assign high probability to crucial reasoning steps" is simple but powerful.

**Rigor (High):** The evaluation is thorough across three distinct settings with appropriate baselines (CE loss, rBridge, compute-based sigmoids). The leave-2-tasks-out CV with model subsampling is well-designed. Power-law fits include inner-split proxy selection. However, the cross-family experiment uses only 18 models and 6 tasks, which is modest.

**Reproducibility (High):** Code is publicly available. All benchmarks are standard (AIME, GPQA, MMLU-Pro, etc.). DataDecide is a public testbed. Expert trajectories from open-weight models are reproducible.

**Significance (High):** This addresses a practical and important problem — predicting downstream performance cheaply and early. The ~10,000x compute savings for data selection and the halving of extrapolation error are impactful results. The method is broadly applicable across the model development lifecycle.

**Red flags:**
- No universal proxy metric exists — the best metric varies across settings, requiring held-in data for selection. This limits applicability when no reference evaluations are available at all.
- Training-time forecasting is validated on a single model (OLMo-3-7B) and single scale. Whether power-law regularity holds across architectures and scales is untested.
- The oracle gap (ρ=0.88 with 3 features under oracle selection vs. ρ=0.81 under CV) suggests room for improvement but also that signal extraction under realistic constraints is challenging.
- Expert quality sensitivity is acknowledged but not systematically evaluated.

## Strengths

- Extremely compute-efficient: single forward pass per instance, no generation required.
- Flexible expert source: works with open-weight models, closed-weight models (only tokens needed), or human experts.
- Strong empirical results across three distinct, practically motivated settings.
- Principled methodology: 80-metric library captures complementary views; learned aggregation is lightweight.
- Honest about limitations (Section 8 covers model coverage, lack of universal proxy, task coverage gaps).

## Limitations

- Requires access to expert trajectories for the target task, which may not exist for novel or niche domains.
- Cross-family results are on reasoning-heavy benchmarks only; generalization to generative tasks, long-context, or agentic evaluation is untested.
- Training-time extrapolation validated on one model family at one scale (OLMo-3-7B).
- No systematic sensitivity analysis for proxy selection hyperparameters (inner-split fraction, training-window size).
- The pretraining data selection experiment uses OLMES (non-reasoning multiple-choice), not reasoning benchmarks, due to DataDecide's scope.
- Does not address mixture-of-experts architectures or multi-modal models.

## Relevance

本文提出了一种高效预测 LLM 下游任务性能的代理指标方法，对大规模模型训练中的数据选择和训练监控具有直接应用价值。其核心思路——利用专家轨迹上的 token 级统计量——为 scaling law 研究和模型评估提供了新的分析框架。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does proxy metric quality degrade with weaker or noisier expert trajectories (e.g., from smaller models or non-expert humans)?
2. Do the power-law regularities observed along OLMo-3-7B's trajectory hold across different model scales (e.g., 1B vs. 70B) and architectures (e.g., MoE)?
3. Can the 80-metric library be replaced by a learned end-to-end aggregation function that generalizes without task-specific held-in data?
4. How do proxy metrics perform on non-reasoning tasks such as open-ended generation, translation, or agentic evaluation?
5. What is the minimum number of expert trajectories needed for reliable ranking, and how does the cost scale with trajectory length?
