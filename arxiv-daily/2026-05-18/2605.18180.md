> **一句话总结：** 本文证明在宽网络特征学习 regime 下，标准 ridge 与 anchored ridge 在正则化系数趋于零的极限下仍会沿"输出纤维"方向偏置梯度流、破坏预训练先验；通过公理化函数空间能量与 Riemannian 几何推导出 geodesic ridge 作为 anchored ridge 的推广，并提出可扩展的 arc ridge 作为上界替代，在 UTKFace 与 Yelp Review 迁移学习上验证。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proves (Theorem 3.1) that in wide feature-learning regime networks satisfying uniform Jacobian conditioning, both standard ridge ‖θ‖² and anchored ridge ‖θ−θ₀‖² bias the gradient-flow limit even as λ↓0, because manifold curvature deflects their gradient along output fibres invisible to the training loss — particularly damaging to pretrained priors. It axiomatises the canonical regulariser as a control-theoretic function-space energy (Definition 4.1), uniquely recovering anchored ridge + the NTK Gaussian Process in kernel regime (Theorem C.4), and generalises to geodesic ridge (Proposition 4.1) whose implicit prior is a Riemannian Gibbs Process (Definition 4.2). As a scalable surrogate it proposes arc ridge — squared training-path length — which upper-bounds geodesic ridge (Theorem 4.3), sits at the minimax-optimal point of the sandwich ‖θ−θ₀‖² ≤ d²(θ,θ₀) ≤ L²(γ_train), and reduces to early stopping under exact gradient flow. On UTKFace age regression (ResNet18 transfer) and Yelp review score regression (DistilBERT transfer), arc ridge degrades only mildly at high λ while standard/anchored ridge collapse, and all regularisers converge at low λ with early stopping.

## 核心贡献

- 给出过参数化网络的参数空间几何三元组：flow orbit O_flow(θ₀)（梯度流可达集）、gauge-fixed flow manifold M_flow(θ₀)（与输出一一对应的 n 维代表曲面）、output fibre Θ(c)（不影响训练预测但影响测试预测的横向方向）；kernel regime 下三者塌缩为仿射平面 θ₀+Im(J⊤)。
- 形式化证明 Theorem 3.1：在满足均匀 Jacobian 条件数假设（Assumption 2.1）的特征学习 regime 中，标准 ridge 与 anchored ridge 的梯度流极限 θ*_{‖Δθ‖²} = lim_{λ↓0} θ_λ ≠ θ*（无正则化梯度流极限），偏置在 λ→0 时仍不消失。
- 公理化 canonical regulariser：将函数空间能量定义为控制论最小能量 E(c) = min ∫ u⊤K(t)u dt（Definition 4.1），在 kernel regime 下唯一恢复 anchored ridge 与 NTK 高斯过程先验（Theorem C.4 的 isometry 不变 + 正交可加两公理）。
- 通过 Riemannian 几何推导 geodesic ridge（Proposition 4.1）：d²(θ,θ₀) = d²_flow + d²_fibre，给出特征学习 regime 的 canonical regulariser，对应先验为 Riemannian Gibbs Process（Definition 4.2），并证明其无偏极限性质（Theorem 4.2）。
- 提出 arc ridge（Definition 4.4）：平方训练路径长度 L²(γ) = (∫‖γ̇(t)‖dt)²，作为 geodesic ridge 的可计算上界（Theorem 4.3），位于 anchored ridge 下界与 arc ridge 上界的 minimax 最优点，且在精确梯度流下形式等价于早停（Proposition F.4）。

## Key Contributions

- A parameter-space geometry for overparametrised networks (Section 3.1): the flow orbit O_flow(θ₀) (reachable set of horizontal control), the gauge-fixed flow manifold M_flow(θ₀) (n-dimensional representative sheet in one-to-one correspondence with outputs near the interpolator), and the output fibre Θ(c) (directions transverse to the flow manifold that leave training predictions unchanged but can change test predictions). In kernel regime all three collapse to the affine plane θ₀+Im(J⊤).
- Theorem 3.1: under Assumption 2.1 (uniform Jacobian conditioning, 0<c≤σ_min(J(θ)) and σ_max(J(θ))≤C on a neighbourhood of Θ⋆), the vanishing-regularisation limit of anchored ridge θ*_{‖Δθ‖²} = lim_{λ↓0} argmin L(g(θ),Y)+λ‖θ−θ₀‖² differs from the unregularised gradient-flow limit θ⋆; the bias persists as λ→0 because manifold curvature deflects the ridge gradient along output fibres invisible to the loss. Standard ridge fails in both regimes.
- Axiomatisation of the canonical regulariser as a control-theoretic function-space energy E(c) = min_u ∫₀¹ u(t)⊤K(t)u(t) dt (Definition 4.1), with uniqueness in kernel regime under G⁻¹-isometry invariance + orthogonal additivity (Theorem C.4), recovering anchored ridge and the NTK Gaussian Process prior.
- Geodesic ridge (Proposition 4.1): d²(θ,θ₀) = d²_flow(g|⁻¹(g(θ)),θ₀) + d²_fibre(θ, g|⁻¹(g(θ))) generalises anchored ridge to curved M_flow(θ₀); the canonical prior is a Riemannian Gibbs Process p(c|X) ∝ exp(−βE(c)) (Definition 4.2); Theorem 4.2 proves the unbiased limit property lim_{λ↓0} argmin L+λd² = θ⋆.
- Arc ridge (Definition 4.4): the squared training-path length L²(γ) = (∫₀ᵀ ‖γ̇(t)‖ dt)², which upper-bounds geodesic ridge (Theorem 4.3), sits at the minimax-optimal point of the sandwich ‖θ−θ₀‖² ≤ d²(θ,θ₀) ≤ L²(γ_train) (Proposition F.2), introduces no off-fibre gradient component, requires storing only a single scalar, and is formally equivalent to early stopping under exact gradient flow (Proposition F.4).

## Methodology

**Problem setting.** Overparametrised supervised model f(x;θ): X×Θ→Y with scalar output, dataset D=(X,Y)∈(X×Y)ⁿ with n≪m=dim Θ and distinct inputs. The network is assumed expressible in the Tensor Programs framework [46–49] with smooth non-linearities. Training is continuous-time gradient flow on SSE loss L(g(θ),Y)=‖g(θ)−Y‖², giving function-space dynamics ġ(θ)=−2K(θ)(g(θ)−Y) where K(θ)=J(θ)J(θ)⊤ is the instantaneous NTK Gram matrix.

**Sole assumption (Assumption 2.1, uniform Jacobian conditioning).** There exist an open neighbourhood U⊃Θ⋆ and constants 0<c≤C<∞ with c≤σ_min(J(θ)) and σ_max(J(θ))≤C for all θ∈U, and the gradient-flow trajectory stays in U. Justified under μP scaling for Tensor Programs networks with smooth activations (σ_min/σ_max concentrate around positive bounded infinite-width limits at initialisation, continuity propagates them). Empirically verified along flow trajectories in Appendix H.

**Geometric framework.** Three objects: (1) flow orbit O_flow(θ₀) = reachable set of θ̇=J(θ)⊤u; (2) gauge-fixed flow manifold M_flow(θ₀) = basin sheet containing the realised trajectory, an embedded n-submanifold by the stable manifold theorem (Theorem C.2); (3) output fibre Θ(c)=g⁻¹(c), codimension-n, tangent space ker J(θ). In kernel regime J is constant on U so M_flow(θ₀)≡θ₀+Im(J⊤) is affine and fibres meet it orthogonally.

**Ridge bias proof (Theorem 3.1).** Anchored ridge contributes −2(θ−θ₀) to gradient flow. In kernel regime this lies in T M_flow(θ₀) so it cannot move parameters off-manifold. In feature-learning regime, curvature of M_flow(θ) gives the ridge gradient a component in fibre directions (ker J(θ)), causing drift invisible to the loss but consequential at test points; this drift survives λ↓0.

**Canonical energy (Definition 4.1).** E(c) = min_u ∫₀¹ u(t)⊤K(t)u(t) dt subject to θ̇=J(θ(t))⊤u, K(t)=J(θ(t))J(θ(t))⊤, θ(0)=θ₀, θ(1)=g|⁻¹(c). In kernel regime the optimal control is constant u⋆=K⁻¹(c−g(θ₀)), recovering the squared RKHS norm. Uniqueness (Theorem C.4): any continuous ρ_G with ρ_G(0)=0, nontrivial, G⁻¹-isometry invariant, and orthogonally additive satisfies ρ_G(c)=α c⊤G⁻¹c.

**Geodesic ridge (Proposition 4.1).** d²(θ,θ₀) = d²_flow(g|⁻¹(g(θ)),θ₀) + d²_fibre(θ,g|⁻¹(g(θ))). Theorem 4.2: (a) unique minimiser on each fibre; (b) trajectory-level minimality (every intermediate point is the min-cost representative of its output); (c) unbiased limit lim_{λ↓0} argmin L+λd² = θ⋆.

**Arc ridge (Definition 4.4).** L²(γ) = (∫₀ᵀ ‖γ̇(t)‖ dt)². Theorem 4.3: d²_flow(θ₀,θ) ≤ L²(γ_train), with equality of the endpoint gradient direction to the realised flow direction (no off-fibre component). Stored as a single scalar since the regularisation gradient direction is determined by the current data gradient.

**Empirical setup.** Two transfer-learning tasks requiring feature learning: (1) UTKFace age regression transferring from ResNet18 [14]; (2) Yelp Review score regression transferring from DistilBERT [39]. Each regulariser (standard ridge ‖θ‖², anchored ridge ‖θ−θ₀‖², arc ridge L²(γ)) is swept over regularisation strength λ and combined with early stopping; an unregularised "Ridgeless" baseline (no early stopping) is included. The minimal model f(x;θ)=θ₁θ₂x on singleton D={(1,1)} (Figure 2) illustrates the geometry.

## Results & Highlights

- Theorem 3.1 (formal bias result): in a wide feature-learning regime network satisfying Assumption 2.1, θ*_{‖Δθ‖²} := lim_{λ↓0} argmin_θ L(g(θ),Y)+λ‖θ−θ₀‖² ≠ θ⋆, where θ⋆ is the unregularised gradient-flow limit — the bias does not vanish as λ→0.
- Proposition C.1: under the same assumptions, gradient flow converges to an interpolator at exponential rate ‖g(θ(t))−Y‖ ≤ ‖g(θ₀)−Y‖ e^{−2c²t}, where c is the lower spectral bound of Assumption 2.1.
- Theorem C.4 (uniqueness): the only continuous, nontrivial, G⁻¹-isometry-invariant, orthogonally-additive energy is ρ_G(c)=α c⊤G⁻¹c — pinning down anchored ridge from its uncountably many quadratic competitors via two axioms, not merely vanishing regularisation.
- Theorem 4.2(c) (unbiased limit): for geodesic ridge, lim_{λ↓0} argmin L(g(θ),Y)+λ d²(θ,θ₀) = θ⋆ — the canonical regulariser provably avoids the ridge bias.
- Theorem 4.3 (sandwich): anchored ridge ‖θ−θ₀‖² ≤ geodesic ridge d²(θ,θ₀) ≤ arc ridge L²(γ_train); arc ridge's endpoint gradient lies along γ̇_train(T⁻) ∈ Im(J(θ)⊤)∩T_θ M_flow(θ₀), introducing no fibre component.
- Empirical (Figure 3, UTKFace): at high λ (log λ around −3 to −4), standard ridge and anchored ridge test MSE degrade sharply (toward ~0.24–0.26) while arc ridge stays near ~0.20, consistent with benign undertraining rather than prior destruction.
- Empirical (Figure 3, Yelp Review): at high λ, standard/anchored ridge worst-test-loss collapses to near 1.0 while arc ridge remains around 0.3–0.4; at low λ all regularisers converge to the early-stopping baseline, corroborating the formal equivalence of arc ridge and early stopping (Proposition F.4).
- Figure 2 (minimal model f(x;θ)=θ₁θ₂x): only the canonical regulariser prevents biased equilibrium in the non-linear case; both standard and anchored ridge cause flow along the interpolating fibre after data-loss convergence, exactly as Theorem 3.1 predicts; in the linear model f(x;θ)=(θ₁+θ₂)x, anchored ridge coincides with the canonical regulariser while standard ridge still biases.
- Proposition C.1 + Remark C.4: standard ridge ‖θ‖² fails in both regimes — in kernel regime by ignoring initialisation (only recovers anchored ridge when θ₀=0), in feature-learning regime additionally through curvature.

## Critical Assessment

- The central Theorem 3.1 depends on Assumption 2.1 (uniform Jacobian conditioning on a neighbourhood U of Θ⋆). The authors note this is "standard in wide-network analysis" and justify it via μP concentration at initialisation propagated by continuity, but they also flag in the Conclusion that "rigorous verification of Assumption 2.1 in concrete settings" remains an open next step — so the bias theorem's applicability to any specific real architecture (e.g., a real ResNet18 or DistilBERT, not just the minimal f(x;θ)=θ₁θ₂x toy) is asserted but not proven.
- The practical recommendation, arc ridge, is an *upper bound* on geodesic ridge (Theorem 4.3), not the canonical regulariser itself. The gap between arc ridge and geodesic ridge can be large when the training trajectory is far from a geodesic (e.g., oscillatory or zig-zagging under SGD with momentum), and the paper does not quantify how tight the bound is in practice — it only shows the sandwich and minimax optimality within it.
- The empirical validation (Figure 3) is narrow: two transfer-learning regression tasks (UTKFace age, Yelp review score), both scalar-output regression with SSE loss matching the theory. The theory is stated for scalar output Y⊆R and SSE loss; classification, multi-output, and non-SSE losses are deferred ("extensions to strictly convex, smooth losses are deferred to the appendix"), so the practical scope is narrower than the abstract suggests.
- The Riemannian Gibbs Process prior (Definition 4.2) is shown to be Kolmogorov-consistent over arbitrary finite test inputs at a fixed training set (Proposition C.15), but the authors explicitly state in Remark C.5 that "consistency across training subsets remains an open problem" — so it is not yet a fully valid stochastic process in the GP sense, limiting its Bayesian-inference utility.
- The arc-ridge ≡ early-stopping equivalence (Proposition F.4) holds "under exact gradient flow"; real training uses discrete-time SGD with learning-rate schedules, momentum, and Adam/Muon, which the Conclusion lists as open. The connection to early stopping is therefore exact only in a continuous-time idealisation.
- Preconditioned optimisers (Adam, Muon, natural gradient) are explicitly left to future work, yet these dominate modern LLM training — so the theory's direct applicability to the systems the user cares about (LLM training stability) is currently limited to vanilla gradient flow.

## Strengths

- Provides a clean geometric explanation (flow manifold curvature + output fibres) for *why* ridge biases feature-learning networks, with a formal non-vanishing-limit theorem (Theorem 3.1) rather than a heuristic argument.
- The axiomatisation (Definition 4.1, Theorem C.4) is first-principles and uniquely recovers anchored ridge + NTK GP in kernel regime, validating the framework before generalising — a principled way to derive the feature-learning regulariser rather than guessing.
- Arc ridge is genuinely practical: a single accumulated scalar, no second-order information, no manifold solvers, compatible with any standard training pipeline, and minimax-optimal within the sandwich.
- The connection to early stopping (Proposition F.4) unifies two previously separate regularisation stories across both regimes.
- Empirical results (Figure 3) qualitatively match the theory's predictions (high-λ collapse of ridge, mild arc-ridge degradation, low-λ convergence), and the minimal-model Figure 2 directly visualises the fibre drift.

## Limitations

- Assumption 2.1 is verified empirically only in Appendix H and on the minimal toy model; rigorous verification for real architectures (ResNet18, DistilBERT, LLMs) is open.
- Arc ridge is an upper bound, not the canonical regulariser; tightness under oscillatory SGD trajectories is unquantified.
- Empirical scope is two scalar-output SSE regression transfer tasks; no classification, no large-scale LLM fine-tuning, no non-SSE losses in the main results.
- The Riemannian Gibbs Process is not yet Kolmogorov-consistent across training subsets (Remark C.5), so it is not a full GP replacement for Bayesian inference.
- The arc-ridge/early-stopping equivalence holds only under exact continuous-time gradient flow; discrete SGD, momentum, Adam, and Muon are open.
- The theory is for scalar output and interpolating regimes; non-interpolating (separable classification) regimes and preconditioned optimisers are deferred.

## Relevance

高度命中"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"方向：本文严格刻画了梯度流训练中正则化对归纳偏置的数学机制，给出了 ridge 在特征学习 regime 下不消失偏差的形式化定理与 Riemannian 几何解释，并公理化推导出 canonical regulariser。同时关联"LLM Training Stability & Observability"——预训练模型微调中 ridge/weight decay 破坏先验的现象（Kumar et al. 2022）正是该理论刻画的失效模式，但当前理论仅适用于 vanilla 梯度流，尚未扩展到 Adam/Muon 等 LLM 实际优化器。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Assumption 2.1（均匀 Jacobian 条件数）在真实 LLM（如 Llama-3 微调）上是否成立？能否用谱监控在线验证 σ_min(J(θ)) 的下界？
- arc ridge 作为 geodesic ridge 的上界，在 SGD 带动量/Adam 下紧度如何？是否需要乘以一个曲率修正因子？
- 该理论如何扩展到 Adam/Muon 等预条件优化器？预条件是否会改变 flow manifold 的几何与 fibre 结构？
- Riemannian Gibbs Process 的训练子集 Kolmogorov 一致性（Remark C.5）能否在 μP 无限宽度极限下证明？这对贝叶斯不确定性估计是关键。
- arc ridge ≡ early stopping 的等价在离散时间 SGD 下需要怎样的修正？能否给出与学习率/动量相关的修正项？
- 在分类任务（logistic loss、max-margin 隐式偏置）下，canonical regulariser 的形式是否仍是 geodesic ridge，还是需要新的能量定义？
