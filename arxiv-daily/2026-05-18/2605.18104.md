> **一句话总结：** 本文从表征几何视角揭示多模态LLM中的"Safety Geometry Collapse"现象——非文本模态输入沿模态漂移方向位移，压缩了文本对齐拒绝方向上的可分性，导致安全能力失效；提出ReGap，一种无需训练的推理时自适应模态漂移修正方法，利用自我纠正信号区分有害/无害输入并动态调整修正强度，在多个安全基准上将攻击成功率降低30-70个百分点，同时保持通用能力不退化。

> **TL;DR:** This paper identifies "Safety Geometry Collapse" in multimodal LLMs: non-text inputs induce representation drift that compresses the separation along the text-aligned refusal direction, making safety refusal unreliable. Through causal activation intervention, the authors show counteracting this drift restores safety geometry. They discover "self-rectification" — harmful inputs exhibit much stronger refusal-recovery dynamics than benign ones after partial drift correction. Based on this, they propose ReGap, a training-free inference-time method that adaptively corrects modality drift using the self-rectification signal. Evaluated on 3 models (Qwen2.5-Omni-3B, MiniCPM-o-4.5, Qwen3-Omni-30B-A3B) across image/audio/video/omni-modal safety benchmarks, ReGap reduces attack success rates by 30-70 points while preserving or slightly improving general capability on OmniBench, MMMU, and MMAU.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"Safety Geometry Collapse"理论框架：在由文本对齐拒绝方向和模态漂移方向定义的二维安全子空间中，多模态输入的模态漂移系统性地压缩了拒绝方向上的可分性，导致安全边界失效
- 设计"条件拒绝可分性"（CRS）指标量化几何坍缩：在漂移坐标的局部窗口内计算拒绝投影的轮廓系数，实证证明漂移越强→CRS越低→攻击成功率越高，跨三个模型一致成立
- 通过固定强度激活干预建立因果关系：在隐藏状态中减去模态漂移方向（与拒绝方向正交化），即使不直接注入拒绝向量，也能恢复安全行为，证明漂移是安全退化的因果因素
- 发现"自我纠正"（self-rectification）现象：部分修正模态漂移后，有害输入在后续层的正向传播中自发向拒绝方向移动，且移动幅度远大于良性输入，提供了模型内部的输入有害性信号
- 提出ReGap：利用探针前向传播测量自我纠正信号，自适应分配修正强度（有害输入强修正、良性输入弱修正），无需训练、仅在输入阶段干预、不影响解码过程

## Key Contributions

- **Safety Geometry Collapse framework**: Formulates multimodal safety in a 2D subspace (refusal direction + modality-induced drift direction), showing that multimodal inputs systematically compress refusal-separable geometry
- **Conditional Refusal Separability (CRS)**: Novel metric using silhouette coefficients within local drift windows to quantify how refusal-compliance separability degrades with increasing modality drift
- **Causal validation via orthogonal activation intervention**: Subtracting drift direction (orthogonalized against refusal direction) at hidden states restores refusal separability and reduces ASR, establishing drift as a causal factor rather than mere correlation
- **Self-rectification discovery**: After partial drift correction at earlier layers, harmful inputs spontaneously move toward the refusal region in subsequent forward computation, while benign inputs show weak changes — providing an emergent internal harmfulness signal
- **ReGap method**: Training-free, inference-time adaptive correction that uses a probe forward pass (λ_probe=0.1) to measure self-rectification, then assigns stronger correction (λ₁) to inputs with strong refusal-recovery dynamics and weaker correction (λ₂) to others

## Methodology

The paper proceeds in three research questions:

**Geometric Formulation (§2)**: Defines the multimodal safety space as a 2D subspace:
- **Refusal direction** r^l = μ_ref^l - μ_comp^l: difference-of-means between refused and complied harmful text inputs at layer l (estimated from AdvBench)
- **Modality-induced drift** g^l: average displacement from text-only to multimodal counterparts (estimated from Omni-SafetyBench paired data), orthogonalized against r^l to isolate modality-specific variation
- **Projection pair** Φ(x) = (φ_r(x), φ_g(x)) captures refusal alignment and modality displacement

**RQ1 — Does drift degrade separability? (§3.1)**: Visualizes the safety space and introduces CRS (Conditional Refusal Separability) — silhouette coefficient of refusal projections within local drift windows. Shows CRS decreases and ASR increases monotonically with φ_g across all three models.

**RQ2 — Does counteracting drift improve safety? (§3.2)**: Applies fixed-strength intervention h'(x) = h(x) - λg at hidden states. Since g⊥r, any safety improvement is not from direct refusal injection. Shows ASR decreases with λ but large λ degrades utility, motivating input-dependent correction.

**RQ3 — Self-rectification dynamics (§3.3)**: Defines self-rectification score S^l(x) measuring how much refusal-direction projection increases at layer l after earlier drift correction. Finds: (1) S increases in middle-to-late layers; (2) harmful inputs that were originally complied show stronger self-rectification than those originally refused; (3) harmful inputs show much stronger S than benign inputs.

**ReGap (§4)**: Two-phase inference-time method:
1. **Probe pass**: Small correction (λ=0.1) applied to input-side hidden states; measure self-rectification S at diagnostic layer l_s
2. **Correction pass**: If S > τ (model-specific threshold), apply strong correction λ₁; otherwise apply weak correction λ₂. Correction applied to all layers during input forward pass only, not during decoding.

## Results & Highlights

**Safety (Table 1 — ASR%, lower is better)**:

| Model | Setting | Base ASR | ReGap ASR | Reduction |
|-------|---------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Qwen2.5-Omni-3B | Omni-SafetyBench Image | 82.1 | 17.1 | -65.0 |
| Qwen2.5-Omni-3B | Omni-SafetyBench Omni | 43.0 | 25.8 | -17.2 |
| MiniCPM-o-4.5 | Omni-SafetyBench Audio | 57.6 | 14.2 | -43.4 |
| MiniCPM-o-4.5 | Omni-SafetyBench Omni | 22.4 | 9.1 | -13.3 |
| Qwen3-Omni-30B-A3B | Omni-SafetyBench Omni | 12.6 | 1.8 | -10.8 |
| Qwen3-Omni-30B-A3B | AudioJailBench | 54.5 | 27.5 | -27.0 |

**Utility (Table 2 — ACC%, higher is better)**: ReGap maintains or slightly improves performance:
- Qwen2.5-Omni-3B: OmniBench 32.4→34.8, MMMU 25.0→25.4, MMAU 66.0→66.8
- MiniCPM-o-4.5: OmniBench 47.8→49.2, MMMU 43.4→45.4, MMAU 76.2→76.8
- Qwen3-Omni-30B-A3B: OmniBench 48.8→49.6, MMMU 50.0→50.6

**Key comparison**: ShiftDC (prior activation intervention) causes severe utility degradation on some models (e.g., MMAU drops from 66.0 to 53.2 on Qwen2.5-Omni-3B), while ReGap preserves capability. Post-training baselines (VLGuard-SFT, SPA-VL-DPO) are modality-dependent and unstable on heterogeneous modality combinations.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High**. The geometric formulation of multimodal safety failure is genuinely new. Prior work (ShiftDC, VLMGuard) observed modality gaps but did not formalize them as a 2D diagnostic space with orthogonal decomposition, nor discover self-rectification dynamics. The insight that drift correction (orthogonal to refusal direction) recovers safety through subsequent forward computation rather than direct injection is elegant and non-obvious.
- **Rigor: Strong**. The three-RQ structure (correlational → causal → mechanistic) is well-designed. CRS provides a principled quantitative metric. The orthogonalization of drift against refusal direction rules out the trivial explanation that correction simply adds refusal activation. However, the binary threshold τ for adaptive correction is somewhat crude — a continuous mapping from S to correction strength might be more principled.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate**. Training-free methods are easier to reproduce than fine-tuning approaches. However, ReGap requires model-specific choices: diagnostic layer l_s, threshold τ, and coefficients λ₁, λ₂. The paper does not fully specify how these are selected (Appendix F mentions details but the exact procedure is not in the extracted content). The refusal direction estimation depends on calibration data quality.
- **Significance: High**. The safety improvements are substantial (30-70 point ASR reductions) with minimal utility loss. The training-free nature makes it immediately deployable. The self-rectification signal as an internal harmfulness indicator has implications beyond ReGap itself.
- **Red flags**: (1) The binary λ₁/λ₂ decision is surprisingly coarse — it would be useful to see how much a continuous function gains. (2) ReGap requires a probe forward pass (doubling inference cost for the input stage), though the authors argue this is lightweight since no decoding is needed. (3) The paper acknowledges in Appendix A that the unified drift direction is a simplification — different modality combinations may have distinct drift patterns. (4) Evaluation is limited to 3 models from 2 families (Qwen, MiniCPM) — generalization to closed-source or other architectures is unknown. (5) The safety benchmarks are primarily English-language; cross-lingual safety transfer is not addressed.

## Strengths

- Elegant geometric formulation with clear mathematical definitions and principled orthogonal decomposition
- Strong causal evidence: orthogonal intervention rules out trivial explanations
- Self-rectification is a genuinely surprising and useful discovery with implications beyond the proposed method
- Training-free and inference-time: immediately applicable to deployed systems without retraining
- Consistent results across 3 models, multiple modalities (image, audio, video, omni-modal), and 7 safety settings
- Preserves or slightly improves utility — avoids the safety-utility trade-off that plagues many alignment methods
- Comprehensive appendices covering limitations, broader impact, evaluation details, and cost analysis

## Limitations

- Binary threshold for adaptive correction is coarse; continuous adaptation might yield better safety-utility trade-offs
- Requires a probe forward pass per input, adding computational overhead (though modest — no decoding needed)
- Unified drift direction per model simplifies heterogeneous modality effects; different modality combinations may have distinct drift patterns
- Evaluated on only 3 models from 2 families (Qwen, MiniCPM); generalization to LLaMA-based, Gemini, or closed-source MLLMs is untested
- Depends on calibration data quality for estimating refusal direction and drift direction; out-of-distribution modalities may not be well-captured
- Does not address text-only jailbreaks or adversarial attacks that operate within the text modality
- English-language benchmarks only; cross-lingual safety not evaluated
- The self-rectification signal's reliability under adversarial manipulation (e.g., an attacker who knows ReGap is deployed) is an open question

## Relevance

本文从表征几何视角深入分析多模态LLM安全对齐失败的机理，提出的ReGap方法为推理时安全增强提供了一种无需训练的新范式。自我纠正信号的发现对理解模型内部安全机制具有重要意义，与LLM对齐、安全工程和可解释性研究方向高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would a continuous mapping from self-rectification score to correction strength improve the safety-utility frontier compared to the binary threshold?
- Can the self-rectification signal be used as a standalone harmfulness classifier (without drift correction) for input filtering?
- How robust is ReGap against adaptive adversaries who know the drift direction and correction mechanism?
- Does Safety Geometry Collapse manifest in closed-source MLLMs (GPT-4o, Gemini) where only black-box access is available?
- Could the drift direction be estimated online (without pre-computed calibration data) to handle novel modality combinations?
- What is the interaction between ReGap and post-training safety methods (SFT, DPO) — can they be combined for cumulative improvement?
