OSCAR: Offline Spectral Covariance-Aware Rotation for 2-bit KV Cache Quantization

arXiv: 2605.17757

Date: 2026-05-18

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17757

一句话总结： OSCAR 通过离线估计注意力感知的协方差结构来推导固定旋转矩阵与裁剪阈值，并配合自研 INT2 注意力内核，在 Qwen3-4B/8B/32B 与 GLM-4.7（358B）上以 2.28 bits/KV 元素实现接近 BF16 的精度，同时获得约 8 倍 KV Cache 内存压缩与最高 7 倍吞吐提升。
TL;DR: OSCAR is an INT2 KV-cache quantization method that derives fixed rotations and clipping thresholds from offline-estimated attention-aware covariance structures (rather than data-oblivious Hadamard transforms), paired with a custom fused-Triton INT2 attention kernel compatible with paged KV-cache serving in SGLang/vLLM. On Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507 and Qwen3-8B it reduces the BF16 accuracy gap to 3.78 and 1.42 mean-points across five benchmarks (AIME25, GPQA-Diamond, HumanEval, LiveCodeBench v6, MATH500), while naive rotation INT2 (QuaRot-INT2) collapses to ~1.40 mean vs. BF16's 70.84, and TurboQuant drops 13.96 points on Qwen3-8B; system-wise it cuts KV-cache memory ~8x and reaches up to 7.83x throughput over BF16 at BS=32 on GLM-4.7-FP8.

核心贡献

  • 提出 OSCAR：用一次离线校准估计注意力感知的 key/value 目标协方差（query-aware C_Q 与 score-aware C_S），并据此推导固定旋转矩阵 RK = U_Q·H_Had·P_br 与 RV = U_S·H_Had·P_br，使 INT2 量化误差被推到注意力读取较弱的方向。
  • 给出理论最优性证明（Theorem 1）：在对角残差假设下，基于 C_Q / C_S 的谱分解旋转最小化"冻结误差"代理目标 L̃K、L̃V，从理论上说明"对角化哪个协方差"比"是否对角化"更关键。
  • 开发可部署的 INT2 服务系统：保留 sink（S=64）与 recent（R=256）BF16 窗口的混合精度 KV 布局，融合 Triton 写入/解码内核兼容 paged-attention 与 SGLang 前缀缓存，并可将 RV 吸收进模型投影权重以节省计算。
  • 在 4B–358B 五个模型规模、五项推理/编码基准与 RULER-NIAH 长上下文检索（4k–128k）上系统验证：朴素旋转 INT2 在 Qwen3 上几乎崩溃（mean≈1.40），而 OSCAR 保持 2.28 BPE 下接近 BF16。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces OSCAR, which derives fixed rotations and per-layer clipping thresholds from offline-estimated attention-aware covariance (query-aware C_Q and score-aware C_S), so INT2 quantization error is pushed into directions attention reads weakly — not just directions that minimize raw cache reconstruction.
  • Provides Theorem 1 proving optimality of the spectral variants R_k=U_Q and R_v=U_S under a diagonal ambient-basis residual assumption on the frozen-error surrogate, theoretically isolating that which covariance one diagonalizes matters more than whether one diagonalizes.
  • Builds a deployable INT2 serving system: a mixed-precision cache layout keeps sink (S=64) and recent (R=256) BF16 windows while the middle history is rotated–clipped–INT2; fused Triton write/decode kernels keep paged-attention and SGLang prefix-cache compatibility, and R_V is absorbed into the projection weights for compute savings.
  • Validates across Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507, Qwen3-8B, Qwen3-32B, and GLM-4.7-FP8 (358B) on AIME25, GPQA-Diamond, HumanEval, LiveCodeBench v6, MATH500, and RULER-NIAH (4k–128k), showing rotation-only INT2 collapses (mean ≈1.40 on Qwen3-8B) while OSCAR stays near BF16 at 2.28 BPE.

Methodology

Offline calibration. A single calibration pass dumps per-layer Q, K, V activations from a small calibration set (default 8k GPQA-Diamond tokens, 8878 tokens × number of layers). From these, OSCAR constructs two attention-aware target covariances:

  • Query-aware key target: C_Q = (1/N) Σ q_n^⊤ q_n, eigendecomposed C_Q = U_Q Λ_Q U_Q^⊤, base rotation R_k := U_Q.
  • Score-aware value target: C_S = (1/N) V^⊤ S^⊤ S V (S = softmax_row(QK^⊤/√d)), eigendecomposed C_S = U_S Λ_S U_S^⊤, base rotation R_v := U_S.

The composed rotations are R_K = U_Q · H_Had · P_br and R_V = U_S · H_Had · P_br, combining the attention-aware eigenbasis U, a normalized Walsh–Hadamard transform H_Had (to further redistribute channel energy), and a bit-reversal permutation P_br (to interleave large/small eigenvalues so per-group quantization sees a more uniform range). Quantization is affine asymmetric INT2 with per-channel scale/zero and percentile-based clipping (typical calibrated c_K≈0.96, c_V≈0.92); block sizes are 128/64/32 on the head dimension.

Online serving (SGLang integration). The logical cache at position t is [1,S_0] (BF16 sink) ‖ [S_0+1, t−W] (INT2 history, rotated) ‖ [t−W+1, t] (BF16 recent, default S_0=64, W=256). The prefill path writes cache rows via a fused Triton kernel that applies clip→quantize, packing four 2-bit values per byte. New decode tokens first enter the recent BF16 window and are later demoted into the INT2 region by another fused kernel. R_V is absorbed into the model's projection weights to avoid a runtime rotation on values. The decode attention kernel partitions each request's indices into BF16 and INT2 segments, unpacks INT2 bytes with stored scale/zero, accumulates in FP, and reuses the standard two-launch flash-decoding merge kernel for the high-precision partial results (overhead negligible since the BF16 segment is orders of magnitude smaller).

Models & benchmarks. Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507, Qwen3-8B, Qwen3-32B, GLM-4.7-FP8 (358B). Hardware: 1×H100 (80GB) for 4B/8B, 2×H100 for 32B, 8×H100 (TP=8) for GLM-4.7. Generation: temperature 0.6 (1.0 for GLM-4.7), top-p=0.95, top-k=20, max 32768 tokens, 5 seeds for Qwen (3 for GLM). Baselines: KIVI-KV2, Kitty (12.5% key-channel boost), Saw-INT4, TurboQuant (vLLM PR #38479, mixed-precision disabled), QuaRot-INT2, naive INT2, BF16.

Results & Highlights

  • On Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507 (Table 2), OSCAR at 2.28 BPE reaches mean 69.416 across five benchmarks vs. BF16 70.84 (Drop −1.42), while QuaRot-INT2 collapses to mean 10.14 (Drop −60.70) and Naive INT2 to 0.00 (Drop −70.84); Saw-INT4 at 4.25 BPE reaches 69.97 (Drop −0.87).
  • On Qwen3-8B (Table 2), OSCAR mean is 69.42 vs. BF16 70.84 — i.e. the BF16 gap is 1.42 points — while TurboQuant (no MP, 3.25 BPE) drops to mean 56.88 (−13.96) and QuaRot-INT2 drops to 10.14 (−60.70).
  • On Qwen3-32B (Table 2), OSCAR mean 71.864 vs. BF16 75.64 (Drop −3.78); QuaRot-INT2 collapses to mean 1.40 (Drop −74.24), Naive INT2 to 0.00.
  • On GLM-4.7-FP8 (358B, Table 2), OSCAR is essentially tied with BF16: mean 78.16 vs. BF16 77.89 (Drop +0.27), and even slightly above Saw-INT4 (77.95 at 4.25 BPE) — OSCAR achieves this at 2.28 BPE.
  • On AIME25 at 32K (Table 1), OSCAR reaches 66.67±3.33 on Qwen3-8B (vs. BF16 66.00) and 74.00±5.48 on Qwen3-32B (vs. BF16 72.59), beating KIVI-KV2 (52.33 / 57.41) and Kitty (59.67 / 69.26).
  • On RULER-NIAH at 128k (Table 3), Qwen3-4B OSCAR retrieves 39.5±1.0 vs. BF16 81.0 but far above QuaRot-INT2 0.0; on Qwen3-8B at 128k OSCAR gets 45.0 vs. BF16 78.2 and QuaRot-INT2 0.0.
  • Ablation (Table 4) on Qwen3-8B: replacing OSCAR's attention-aware target with raw K^⊤K/V^⊤V PCA drops mean from 70.01 to 51.74; removing U entirely (QuaRot + P_br) drops to 32.82; no rotation at all drops to 4.23 — isolating that the covariance target matters most.
  • System (Table 8): on Qwen3-8B at BS=64, OSCAR's total decode step is 23.4 ms (Attn 16.2 ms, Quant 0.5 ms) where BF16 is capacity-limited; at BS=128 OSCAR total is 39.3 ms vs. BF16 capacity-limited.
  • Throughput (Fig 4): decode speedup vs BF16 at 100k context reaches 3.08× on Qwen3-4B, 2.52× on Qwen3-8B, 2.88× on GLM-4.7-FP8 (BS=1); at BS=32 / 100k, GLM-4.7-FP8 reaches 7.83× over BF16. KV-cache memory is reduced ~8×.

Critical Assessment

  • The headline "near BF16" claim is supported by Tables 2 and 3, but it relies on a mixed-precision layout (BF16 sink S=64 + recent R=256) that contributes meaningfully — Table 5 shows (0,0) protection drops Qwen3-4B mean to 67.69 from 71.86 at (64,256), so part of the accuracy is bought with BF16 windows, not pure INT2.
  • The Theorem 1 optimality guarantee rests on a "diagonal ambient-basis residual" assumption (E_K, E_V diagonal in the ambient basis) that is acknowledged as a frozen-error surrogate; real quantization residuals need not be diagonal, so the theoretical optimality is conditional and the paper does not empirically validate that the assumption holds.
  • The Group-A baseline comparison (KIVI, Kitty) is only shown for AIME25 at 32K (Table 1) rather than across all five benchmarks, because the authors state they "do not have paged/fused kernels for them at 32K generation length" — so the strongest channel-wise baselines are under-represented in the main Table 2.
  • TurboQuant is run with mixed-precision disabled "for fairness", which is its weaker configuration; the authors note its vLLM path was "too slow for repeated 32K evaluations", so TurboQuant entries are single-run and arguably disadvantaged.
  • LiveCodeBench v6 is evaluated with the same 32K generation cap as other tasks, which the authors themselves note "truncates some long code-generation outputs, so the reported LCB numbers are lower than they would be under a longer 128K generation budget" — understating all methods' LCB performance.
  • GLM-4.7 RULER-NIAH results are explicitly labelled preliminary (one run), and the GLM-4.7-FP8 reference is FP8-quantized rather than a pure BF16 frontier model, which complicates direct comparison.

Strengths

  • Co-designs the quantization algorithm, the rotation theory, and the serving kernel together, and the system is actually integrated into SGLang with paged-attention and prefix-cache compatibility — not just an offline accuracy study.
  • Strong ablations (Tables 4–7) cleanly isolate which covariance is diagonalized, the role of H_Had vs P_br vs U, the (S,R) sweet spot, the calibrated clip threshold, and calibration-data sensitivity (8k GPQA-Diamond ≈ best).
  • Scales the evaluation from 4B to 358B parameters and includes genuinely hard reasoning traces (up to 32k generated tokens) and long-context retrieval (up to 128k), where naive INT2 visibly collapses.
  • Per-layer, per-head covariance estimation from a single small calibration pass with no task-specific tuning is practically attractive.

Limitations

  • Offline calibration requires a representative dataset and a full per-layer Q/K/V activation dump; Table 7 shows some sensitivity (32k MMLU 69.90 vs 8k GPQA 71.01), so the calibration choice still matters by ~1 point.
  • The custom fused Triton INT2 kernel is SGLang/H100-specific; portability to other hardware (AMD, non-Hopper GPUs) or serving stacks (vLLM) is not demonstrated and would require re-engineering.
  • The theory's diagonal-residual assumption is not empirically validated, so the optimality claim is a surrogate argument rather than a tight bound on real attention error.
  • Most comparisons are against rotation-based INT2 baselines; the only channel-wise baseline head-to-head (Table 1) is restricted to AIME25, leaving the strongest 2-bit channel-wise methods under-tested across the full benchmark suite.
  • The mixed-precision sink/recent windows consume extra BF16 KV memory (up to 0.98% at (256,1024)) and the reported 2.28 BPE includes this BF16 overhead at 128k context; at much shorter contexts the effective BPE would be higher.

Relevance
