> **一句话总结：** 本文系统评估了PPG信号深度学习血压估计中不确定性量化方法在分布偏移下的可靠性，发现深度集成在预测鲁棒性上最优，GNLL损失提供最佳原生不确定性估计，而校准（尤其是共形预测和温度缩放）对MSE模型的不确定性至关重要。

> **TL;DR:** A systematic benchmark evaluating uncertainty quantification methods (deep ensembles, Monte Carlo dropout, with GNLL/MSE losses and post-hoc recalibration) for PPG-based blood pressure estimation under both in-distribution and out-of-distribution conditions, finding that deep ensembles offer the best predictive robustness under domain shift, GNLL-based models provide the most reliable native uncertainty, and recalibration is essential for MSE-based uncertainty to become practically useful.

## 核心贡献

- **首次在PPG血压估计中系统评估OOD条件下的不确定性可靠性**，填补了以往研究仅关注ID评估的空白。
- **全面比较了多种UQ策略组合**：MCD vs DE、GNLL vs MSE损失、以及三种后校准方法（共形预测、温度缩放、等渗回归）。
- **揭示了分布偏移下不确定性退化的关键规律**：标签空间差异（EMD距离）仅部分解释OOD性能下降，数据集级别的因素（队列组成、记录时长、片段长度）共同塑造OOD难度。
- **明确了实践中的最优组合**：GNLL+DE+CP在SBP上不确定性校准最佳，GNLL+DE+TS在DBP上表现最优。
- **开源了完整代码和实验框架**（https://github.com/AI4HealthUOL/UQ_OOD_PPG），便于复现和扩展。

## Key Contributions

- **First systematic UQ benchmark under OOD conditions for PPG-based BP estimation**, using an XResNet1D-50 trained on PulseDB (CalibFree-VitalDB subset) and tested on four external datasets (Sensors, UCI, BCG, PPGBP).
- **Comprehensive methodological comparison** covering two epistemic uncertainty methods (DE and MCD at 0%, 4%, 40% dropout), two loss functions (GNLL and MSE), and three post-hoc recalibration strategies (CP, TS, IR).
- **Demonstrates that EMD-based label distribution distance only partially explains OOD degradation**, with dataset-level factors (cohort size, recording duration, segment length) playing significant roles.
- **Identifies practical best-performing configurations**: DE for predictive robustness, GNLL for native uncertainty quality, and CP/TS as the most consistent recalibration methods.
- **Provides detailed tiered statistical comparisons** using paired bootstrap confidence intervals across all method-dataset combinations for both SBP and DBP.

## Methodology

**Architecture & Training:** XResNet1D-50 (1D ResNet) trained on the CalibFree-VitalDB subset of PulseDB (~523K 10-second segments from 1,158 subjects). Training used AdamW with one-cycle policy, mixed precision, and 5 random seeds to mitigate underspecification.

**Uncertainty Methods:**
- **Monte Carlo Dropout (MCD):** Tested at 0% (deterministic), 4%, and 40% dropout rates. At inference, 100 forward passes with stochastic dropout provide predictive distributions.
- **Deep Ensembles (DE):** 5 independently trained models with different random seeds, aggregated at inference.

**Loss Functions:**
- **MSE:** Standard mean squared error producing point predictions with heuristic uncertainty (variance from stochastic passes only).
- **GNLL:** Gaussian negative log-likelihood, which jointly learns mean and variance, providing native probabilistic uncertainty estimates.

**Post-hoc Recalibration:** Three methods applied on a held-out ID calibration set (20% of training data):
- Conformal Prediction (CP): Distribution-free coverage guarantees under exchangeability.
- Temperature Scaling (TS): Learns a single scaling parameter for predicted variance.
- Isotropic Regression (IR): Non-parametric monotonic recalibration mapping.

**Evaluation Metrics:**
- **MAE** (mmHg) for point prediction accuracy.
- **Winkler Score** (two variants: score based on point prediction only vs. incorporating uncertainty) for joint accuracy-calibration assessment.
- **Epistemic/Aleatoric/Total uncertainty** decomposition.
- **Earth Mover's Distance (EMD)** to quantify dataset-level distribution shift.

**OOD Datasets:** Four external datasets with diverse characteristics — Sensors (ICU, 1,195 subjects), UCI (largest, 10,793 subjects), BCG (lab, 40 subjects), PPGBP (cardiovascular patients, 218 subjects, short 2.1s segments).

## Results & Highlights

- **Predictive performance:** DE consistently outperformed MCD for OOD robustness. SBP MAE ranged from ~12 mmHg (ID) to ~21 mmHg (worst OOD); DBP MAE from ~8 mmHg (ID) to ~15 mmHg (OOD).
- **GNLL vs MSE for native uncertainty:** GNLL-based models provided substantially more reliable native uncertainty estimates (lower Winkler scores) than MSE-based models across both ID and OOD settings. MSE-based uncertainty was often unreliable without recalibration (Winkler scores up to 824 mmHg for SBP OOD).
- **Recalibration impact:** Post-hoc recalibration dramatically improved MSE-based uncertainty quality (e.g., reducing SBP Winkler scores from ~466–824 mmHg to ~80–180 mmHg). Gains for GNLL-based models were more modest but consistent.
- **Best methods (Tier 1):** GNLL+DE combinations dominated top-performing methods across evaluation slices. GNLL+DE+CP was best for SBP calibration; GNLL+DE+TS for DBP calibration.
- **Distribution shift degradation:** OOD datasets showed clear performance degradation correlated with EMD, but not monotonically — BCG was consistently the least challenging OOD dataset despite moderate EMD, while UCI and PPGBP were among the most difficult.
- **CP and TS were the most consistent recalibration strategies** across settings; IR was competitive but more variable and occasionally unstable (notably for MSE+DE on PPGBP).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Moderate):** While the individual components (DE, MCD, GNLL, CP, TS) are well-established, the systematic combination and evaluation under OOD conditions for PPG-based BP estimation is novel. The underspecification analysis (multi-seed aggregation) is a valuable methodological contribution. However, the study is primarily an empirical benchmark rather than introducing new methods.

**Rigor (High):** The experimental design is thorough — 5 random seeds for underspecification control, multiple dropout rates, comprehensive statistical testing with paired bootstrap CIs, two Winkler score variants, and EMD-based shift characterization. The tiered comparison framework (Tier 1 methods within 95% CI of best) is statistically sound.

**Reproducibility (High):** All datasets are public, code is open-sourced, and hyperparameters/training details are well-documented. The use of standard architectures (XResNet1D-50) and frameworks (fastai) further supports reproducibility.

**Significance (Moderate-High):** The findings have clear practical implications for wearable BP monitoring deployment. The demonstration that ID uncertainty reliability does not guarantee OOD reliability is an important cautionary result for the field. However, the study is limited to one architecture and one training dataset.

**Red flags:**
- Limited to a single base architecture (XResNet1D-50) — results may not generalize to transformer-based or other architectures.
- Shift characterization relies primarily on BP label marginals (EMD), not signal-level features — this is a notable gap.
- PPGBP dataset is very small (619 segments), making some results noisy (large IQRs in Tables 4–9).
- The study does not address calibration drift over time or adaptive recalibration strategies.

## Strengths

- **Comprehensive factorial design** systematically disentangling the contributions of uncertainty method, loss function, and recalibration strategy.
- **Underspecification control** through multi-seed aggregation, a methodological best practice rarely applied in PPG literature.
- **Clear practical recommendations** for method selection in real-world deployment scenarios.
- **Honest assessment of limitations**, including cases where recalibration worsened performance (MSE+DE+CP on PPGBP).
- **Strong connection to prior work**, building directly on established benchmarks and extending them with UQ analysis.

## Limitations

- **Single architecture:** Only XResNet1D-50 is tested; results may differ for transformers, LSTMs, or other architectures.
- **Single training dataset:** All models trained on PulseDB CalibFree-VitalDB; results may not transfer to other training data sources.
- **Label-level shift characterization only:** EMD on BP marginals does not capture signal-level distribution shift, which may be more relevant for PPG models.
- **Small OOD datasets:** PPGBP has only 619 segments and BCG has only 3,063, limiting statistical power.
- **No adaptive or online recalibration:** All recalibration is performed on an ID held-out set, which may not be realistic for deployed systems facing continuous distribution shift.
- **Limited UQ metrics:** Evaluation relies primarily on Winkler score; complementary metrics (sharpness, conditional coverage, reliability diagrams) could provide additional insights.
- **No exploration of newer UQ methods** such as SWAG, spectral-normalized neural Gaussian processes, or evidential deep learning.

## Relevance

本文对不确定性量化和分布偏移鲁棒性的系统研究，为可穿戴健康监测中深度学习模型的可信部署提供了重要的实验证据和方法论参考，其关于OOD条件下UQ可靠性退化的发现对时间序列医疗AI具有普遍意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the findings transfer to other PPG-based tasks (e.g., heart rate, SpO2, respiratory rate estimation) under domain shift?
- Would signal-level distribution shift metrics (e.g., on PPG waveform features) better predict OOD uncertainty degradation than label-level EMD?
- Can adaptive or continual recalibration strategies maintain uncertainty reliability as deployed models encounter evolving patient populations?
- How do transformer-based architectures compare to the ResNet backbone in terms of OOD uncertainty reliability?
- What is the minimum calibration set size needed for reliable post-hoc recalibration under distribution shift?
- Could hybrid approaches combining DE with learned calibration models outperform the fixed post-hoc methods tested here?
