Detecting overfitting in Neural Networks during long-horizon grokking using Random Matrix Theory

arXiv: 2605.12394

Date: 2026-05-12

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12394

一句话总结： 本文提出基于随机矩阵理论的Correlation Traps检测方法，能在不需要训练/测试数据的情况下，通过分析权重矩阵的特征谱异常来识别神经网络在长训练过程中出现的"anti-grokking"过拟合阶段，并在前沿大模型中发现了类似的谱异常信号。
TL;DR: The authors introduce a Random Matrix Theory (RMT) diagnostic called "Correlation Traps" that detects overfitting in neural networks by identifying spectral outliers in entry-wise shuffled weight matrices, revealing a third training phase ("anti-grokking") where test accuracy degrades under continued optimization while train accuracy stays perfect.

核心贡献

  • 发现了grokking之后的第三个训练阶段——"anti-grokking"：模型在成功泛化之后，继续优化会导致测试准确率显著下降，而训练准确率保持完美
  • 提出了"Correlation Traps"概念：通过对权重矩阵逐元素随机化并拟合Marchenko-Pastur分布，检测违反自平均性质的谱异常离群值
  • 设计了无需数据的JSD诊断消融测试，能够区分有害陷阱和良性陷阱
  • 在前沿开源大模型（gpt-oss-20b、gpt-oss-120b）中发现了大量Correlation Traps，暗示潜在的过拟合问题

Key Contributions

  • Identify a third training phase beyond grokking—"anti-grokking"—where continued optimization degrades test accuracy while preserving perfect training accuracy
  • Propose the "Correlation Trap" diagnostic: entry-wise shuffle weight matrices, fit Marchenko-Pastur (MP) distributions, and detect outliers violating self-averaging
  • Develop a data-free JSD diagnostic ablation test to classify traps as harmful or benign by measuring Jensen-Shannon divergence between original and trap-removed model outputs on random probes
  • Demonstrate the same three-phase pattern (pre-grokking → grokking → anti-grokking) across three benchmarks: MNIST MLP, Modular Addition transformer, and GPT2 composition task
  • Show that weight decay suppresses but does not eliminate trap formation, and that traps are not merely proxies for global weight norm growth
  • Provide screening evidence that frontier-scale open-weight LLMs (gpt-oss-20b, gpt-oss-120b) exhibit Correlation Traps, suggesting potential harmful overfitting

Methodology

The method operates on trained model checkpoints without requiring training data, test data, gradients, or optimizer state. For each layer's weight matrix W:

  1. Entry-wise randomization: Shuffle the learned entries to produce W_rand, destroying the learned correlation structure while preserving the empirical entry distribution.
  2. MP fitting: Form the randomized covariance matrix and fit a Marchenko-Pastur distribution to the empirical spectral density.
  3. Trap detection: Identify eigenvalues that exceed the MP/Tracy-Widom right edge as Correlation Traps—spectral outliers indicating non-self-averaging structure.

The theoretical basis draws on the Baik-Ben Arous-Péché transition: once an eigenvalue cleanly separates past the Tracy-Widom edge scale, it represents a structural outlier rather than random fluctuation. Traps can fail self-averaging through two mechanisms: geometric localization (the trap mode concentrates on few coordinates) and spectral condensation (one eigenvalue dominates variance).

For trap classification, the authors define a JSD diagnostic ablation test: replace trap k with a suitable random vector to produce model M_{θ\k}, then compute the Jensen-Shannon divergence between output distributions of the original and ablated models on random probe inputs. Large JSD indicates the trap is behaviorally significant; its effect on test accuracy determines whether it is harmful or benign.

Experiments cover three benchmarks:

  • MLP: Depth-3 ReLU MLP (width 200) on balanced MNIST subset (1000 points), trained for 10^7 steps with AdamW, WD=0 (main) and WD=0.01 (control).
  • MA: One-layer transformer on modular addition (x + y mod P), trained far beyond initial generalization.
  • GPT2: 8-layer GPT2-style decoder-only transformer on a synthetic knowledge graph composition task from GrokkedTransformer.

Results & Highlights

  • Three-phase structure is robust: Across all three benchmarks, trap counts are near-zero during pre-grokking and grokking, and rise sharply only during anti-grokking. This separation is consistent across random seeds and shuffle randomizations.
  • Anti-grokking is structurally distinct from pre-grokking: Despite both phases having high train / low test accuracy, pre-grokking is trap-free while anti-grokking exhibits numerous traps. This means traps detect overfitting, not mere memorization or low test accuracy.
  • Weight decay mitigates but does not eliminate traps: The WD=0.01 control shows fewer traps (2.0 ± 0.0 vs. 7.5 ± 5.6 for FC1) and milder test degradation, supporting the interpretation that traps reflect genuine structural anomalies.
  • Traps are not proxies for weight norm: A perturbation study shows that global ℓ2 norm changes throughout training phases, but trap counts remain phase-specific (zero in pre-grokking/grokking, nonzero only in anti-grokking).
  • Mechanistic insight: In the MLP, mapped trap directions become prototype-like in pixel space during anti-grokking, causing the classifier to rely on input norm ∥x∥₂ rather than spatial digit structure. Intervening on the trap direction changes the confusion matrix.
  • JSD diagnostic correlates with harm: The trap-removal score J_k(T=1) closely tracks downstream test accuracy changes within a given model, distinguishing benign from harmful traps.
  • Frontier model screening: gpt-oss-20b and gpt-oss-120b show layer-wise Correlation Trap profiles with substantial trap counts, suggesting potential overfitting in foundation-scale LLMs.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The identification of "anti-grokking" as a distinct third phase and the Correlation Trap diagnostic are genuinely new contributions. The data-free nature of the method is practically valuable. The connection between self-averaging failure and overfitting is a fresh theoretical angle.
  • Rigor: Strong theoretical grounding in RMT (MP law, Tracy-Widom edge, BBP transition). Multiple control experiments (weight decay, perturbation study, seed robustness, shuffle robustness) address confounds. The mechanistic MLP case study provides interpretability. However, the "self-averaging" argument for why traps matter for generalization is somewhat heuristic—formal concentration inequalities connecting trap variance to generalization gap are not proved.
  • Reproducibility: Good. The paper provides detailed hyperparameters, references the open-source WeightWatcher package, and mentions an anonymized artifact bundle with training scripts and analysis code. All datasets are public (MNIST, synthetic benchmarks).
  • Significance: Potentially high practical impact if the diagnostic scales reliably. The frontier-model screening result is intriguing but remains observational—no causal claim about harmful overfitting in those models is established.
  • Red flags: (1) The main experiments are on small models (MLP, single-layer transformer, 8-layer GPT2). Scaling the diagnostic to truly large models with reasonable compute is not demonstrated. (2) The GPT-OSS frontier results are screening-only; the paper acknowledges this but the headline is potentially misleading. (3) The threshold for calling something a "trap" involves fitting decisions (MP edge, TW scale) whose sensitivity is not fully characterized. (4) The definition of anti-grokking phases relies on hand-labeled phase boundaries in some analyses.

Strengths

  • Elegant, theoretically motivated diagnostic requiring only model checkpoints—no data, gradients, or training access
  • Consistent three-phase pattern across three diverse benchmarks (image MLP, algorithmic transformer, language model)
  • Strong experimental controls: weight decay comparison, perturbation study, seed and shuffle robustness
  • Mechanistic case study in the MLP provides concrete interpretation of what a harmful trap looks like in input space
  • JSD ablation test offers a practical way to classify traps without labeled data

Limitations

  • Experimental evidence is limited to three small-scale grokking benchmarks; generalization to large-scale, real-world training regimes is unverified
  • The frontier-model screening (gpt-oss) is observational only—no evidence that detected traps actually correspond to harmful overfitting in those models
  • The theoretical connection between trap non-self-averaging and generalization failure is argued heuristically rather than proved formally
  • Phase boundary definitions for anti-grokking involve some manual labeling; an automated, threshold-free detection criterion would strengthen the method
  • The diagnostic requires entry-wise shuffle and eigenvalue decomposition per layer, which may be computationally expensive for very large layers
