Autoregressive Learning in Joint KL: Sharp Oracle Bounds and Lower Bounds

arXiv: 2605.12316

Date: 2026-05-12

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12316

一句话总结： 本文在联合KL散度目标下，为自回归学习建立了匹配的上下界，证明近似误差与序列长度无关（O(1)），而估计误差必然线性依赖于序列长度Ω(H)，澄清了先前基于Hellinger距离分析中Ω(H)近似膨胀是度量选择导致的假象。
TL;DR: Provides the first complete characterization of autoregressive learning under joint KL divergence, showing horizon-free approximation (O(1)) but unavoidable linear-horizon estimation cost (Ω(H)), in contrast to Hellinger-based analyses where approximation blows up as Ω(H).

核心贡献

  • 联合KL散度下的近似误差与序列长度无关：利用KL链式法则的精确分解性质，证明了在模型失配设定下，近似比率 $C_{\text{apx}} = O(1)$，不受序列长度 $H$ 影响。这澄清了先前基于平方Hellinger距离的Ω(H)近似膨胀实际上是度量选择造成的假象，而非自回归因子分解的内在缺陷。
  • ERM与贝叶斯后验学习器的匹配上界：为经验风险最小化（ERM）和贝叶斯后验两种经典算法推导了联合KL泛化界。对于可分解策略类，估计误差为 $\widetilde{O}(H \log(H|\Pi_0|)/n)$；对于全共享策略类，估计误差为 $\widetilde{O}(H \log|\Pi_0|/n)$。
  • 信息论下界证明Ω(H)估计是不可避免的：通过Fano型构造，证明了即使在全共享策略类下，联合KL的估计误差也必须线性依赖于 $H$。这意味着线性horizon依赖反映了自回归学习的内在统计难度，而非分析技术的缺陷。
  • 提升（lifting）策略空间实现高效计算：针对非可分解的依赖策略类，提出将搜索空间提升到逐步乘积空间 $\tilde{\Pi}$，使计算高效的逐步ERM/贝叶斯后验获得 $O(H)$ 估计误差，相比直接proper学习的 $O(H^2)$ 显著改善。
  • 从联合KL到策略学习遗憾界：通过Pinsker不等式，联合KL保证可直接转化为模仿学习中的rollout-level策略学习遗憾界，与平方Hellinger距离提供相同 $\sqrt{\cdot}$ 阶保证。

Key Contributions

  • Horizon-free approximation under joint KL. Exploits the exact KL chain rule to show the approximation ratio is $O(1)$, independent of sequence length $H$. This isolates the $\Omega(H)$ approximation blowup in prior Hellinger-based analyses as a metric-induced artifact rather than an inherent feature of autoregressive factorization.
  • Matching upper bounds for ERM and Bayesian posterior. Derives joint-KL generalization bounds for both ERM and Bayesian posterior learners. For decomposable policy classes, estimation error scales as $\widetilde{O}(H \log(H|\Pi_0|)/n)$; for fully-shared classes, as $\widetilde{O}(H \log|\Pi_0|)/n)$.
  • Information-theoretic lower bounds (Theorem 4.2). Proves via a Fano-type construction that the estimation cost must be at least $\Omega(H \cdot G^2 \log|\Pi|/n)$, holding for both decomposable and fully-shared classes. This confirms the linear-horizon dependence is an intrinsic statistical barrier under joint KL.
  • Lifting for tractable improper learning (Theorem 3.3). Shows that lifting the search space from a dependent class $\Pi$ to the stepwise product $\tilde{\Pi} = \Pi_0^H$ reduces estimation from $\Omega(H^2)$ to $O(H)$ (modulo logs), at the cost of improper learning.
  • Policy learning regret from joint KL. Uses Pinsker's inequality to translate joint-KL bounds into rollout-level imitation learning guarantees at the standard $\sqrt{\cdot}$-order, preserving the approximation–estimation separation.

Methodology

The paper studies misspecified autoregressive learning where a policy $\pi = (\pi_1, \ldots, \pi_H)$ induces a joint distribution $P^\pi$ over length-$H$ token sequences, and the goal is to minimize $\text{KL}(P^{\pi^} \| P^{\hat{\pi}})$ given $n$ i.i.d. trajectory samples from $\pi^ \notin \Pi$.

Assumptions. The key assumption is bounded log-density ratio (Assumption 1): $\sup \log(\pi^_h / \pi_h) \leq G$. Two structural regimes are studied: (i) decomposable policy classes $\Pi = \Pi_0^H$ (no parameter sharing), and (ii) fully-shared* classes $\Pi_{\text{cons}}$ (a single policy $\pi_0$ reused at every step).

Upper bound proof strategy. The proof decomposes into two steps: (1) prefix-conditioned control — at each fixed step $h$ and prefix $s_h$, obtain a local oracle inequality for the conditional KL using Bernstein-type concentration (ERM) or mixability (Bayesian posterior); (2) averaging over random prefixes — connect the empirical prefix averages to the population chain-rule decomposition using a Freedman-type martingale bound. The exact KL chain rule then assembles stepwise control into the joint-KL bound. For decomposable classes, the stepwise argument requires uniform control only over $\{(h, \pi_h)\}$ rather than the full product class, yielding $\log(H|\Pi_0|)$ instead of the naive $H^2$.

Lower bound proof strategy. Uses a Fano-type construction on a finite decomposable policy class with carefully designed distributions. The construction ensures that, even under realizability, any learner must suffer $\Omega(H \log|\Pi|/n)$ in joint KL, and this extends to the misspecified setting since enlarging the oracle family can only increase the supremum. A key insight is that fully-shared structure does not help under joint KL (unlike under Hellinger), because KL decomposes along the autoregressive sequence and horizon-wise accumulation is unavoidable.

Results & Highlights

  • Approximation error is $O(1)$ in horizon under joint KL, versus $\Omega(H)$ under squared Hellinger (Table 1.1). This is because KL's exact chain rule avoids the pseudo-chain-rule looseness inherent in Hellinger.
  • Estimation error is $\Theta(H)$ for both decomposable and fully-shared classes under joint KL. The fully-shared class saves only a $\log H$ factor, not the $H$ factor that Hellinger analysis would predict.
  • Proper learning in dependent classes incurs $\Omega(H^2)$ estimation under joint KL; lifting to improper learning over the stepwise product class reduces this to $O(H)$.
  • Sharp high-probability oracle bounds with $C_{\text{apx}} = 1$ are impossible (Theorem 3.2): even with a Bayesian posterior, the misspecification term cannot achieve leading constant 1 in high-probability bounds under joint KL.
  • Policy learning regret from joint KL matches the standard $\sqrt{\cdot}$-order obtained from Hellinger analysis, while preserving the favorable approximation–estimation separation.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. This is the first work to provide matching upper and lower bounds for autoregressive learning under joint KL. The insight that Hellinger's $\Omega(H)$ approximation blowup is metric-induced is clean and impactful. The impossibility result for $C_{\text{apx}} = 1$ (Theorem 3.2) is a valuable negative result.
  • Rigor: High. Proofs follow a clear two-step structure with explicit assumptions. The Fano-type lower bound construction is standard but applied effectively. The paper is careful about distinguishing realizable vs. misspecified, and decomposable vs. fully-shared regimes.
  • Reproducibility: Moderate. The paper is theoretical with finite-class assumptions and bounded log-density ratios. The core theorems should be straightforward to verify from the proof sketches, but the covering-number extensions for infinite classes (Appendix A) add complexity. No code is provided, though this is expected for a pure theory paper.
  • Significance: High. Clarifies a fundamental question about how horizon affects autoregressive learning and unifies several recent lines of work. The practical implication — that KL is preferable to Hellinger for analyzing log-loss training — directly connects to LLM training.
  • Red flags. The bounded log-density assumption (Assumption 1) is strong and may not hold for neural network policy classes in practice. The finite-class assumption (extended via covering in the appendix) is standard but limits direct applicability to modern transformers. The paper does not address continuous or infinite action spaces.

Strengths

  • Clean separation of approximation vs. estimation horizon effects, resolving a confusion in the literature.
  • Matching upper and lower bounds provide optimality justification — not just upper-bound-only results.
  • The comparison table (Table 1.1) is extremely clear and useful for understanding the landscape.
  • The lifting technique for dependent classes is practically motivated and theoretically justified.
  • Direct connection to policy learning / imitation learning extends the impact beyond density estimation.

Limitations

  • The bounded log-density ratio assumption may be restrictive for neural network parameterizations used in practice.
  • The finite-class / covering-number analysis does not directly address the complexity of transformer architectures.
  • Only discrete token spaces are considered; extension to continuous actions would broaden applicability.
  • The paper is purely theoretical with no experimental validation, even on synthetic settings that could illustrate the scaling behaviors.
  • The horizon-free approximation result holds for the best-in-class comparator; in practice, the best-in-class KL itself typically grows with $H$.

Relevance

本文为自回归学习建立了精确的理论框架，对理解LLM训练中序列长度对泛化误差的影响具有直接意义。其中关于KL与Hellinger度量在近似误差上的对比分析，以及策略学习遗憾界的推导，对序列建模和模仿学习的理论基础研究具有参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the bounded log-density assumption be relaxed to a weaker moment condition while preserving the $O(1)$ approximation ratio?
  • How do the joint-KL bounds extend to the infinite-dimensional policy classes used in modern transformers (e.g., parameterized by neural networks)?
  • Does the $\Omega(H)$ estimation lower bound tighten when the data-generating process has additional structure (e.g., Markovian or low-rank)?
  • Can the lifting technique be combined with practical regularization methods to achieve both computational tractability and proper-learning consistency?
  • What are the implications for in-context learning theory — do these bounds suggest optimal context lengths for given sample sizes?
