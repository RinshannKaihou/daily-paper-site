OUI as a Structural Observable: Towards an Activation-Centric View of Neural Network Training

arXiv: 2605.11570

Date: 2026-05-12

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11570

一句话总结： 本文提出将 OUI（过拟合-欠拟合指标）视为神经网络训练中激活结构的首个实用可观测信号，通过对监督学习、强化学习和在线控制中已有实验证据的统一解读，倡导建立以激活为中心的训练动态理论。
TL;DR: This position paper argues that the Overfitting-Underfitting Indicator (OUI) — a cheap, label-free, activation-based metric — should be understood as a structural observable of neural network training dynamics, synthesizing evidence across supervised learning, PPO reinforcement learning, and online control to advocate for an activation-centric theory of training.

核心贡献

  • 提出激活中心视角： 将 OUI 从工程启发式指标重新定位为训练过程中内部激活结构的可观测信号，主张激活结构是理解训练动态的一等对象。
  • 跨场景证据综合： 统一了 OUI 在监督学习（权重衰减选择）、强化学习（PPO actor-critic 学习率识别）和在线控制（逐层权重衰减自适应）三个场景中的实验结果，揭示出共同模式——激活结构在收敛前就已具有信息量。
  • 两阶段训练假说： 结合激活模式收敛早于参数更新的证据，提出训练可能分为早期结构组织阶段和后期参数精炼阶段，OUI 对第一阶段敏感。
  • 提出研究路线图： 明确了面向激活中心理论需要回答的核心问题，包括相位结构、结构适当性、形式化理论及训练过程设计。

Key Contributions

  • Repositions OUI as a structural observable: Rather than treating OUI as merely a practical heuristic for weight decay selection, the paper argues it captures a genuine structural dimension of learning that external metrics (loss, accuracy) miss.
  • Cross-domain synthesis: Aggregates results from supervised regularization (DenseNet-BC-100 on CIFAR-100), PPO actor-critic (CartPole-v1), and online control, demonstrating that activation-based signals are informative early across diverse objectives and architectures (CNNs, Transformers, MLPs).
  • Two-timescale training hypothesis: Leverages evidence from Pérez-Corral et al. (2026) that activation patterns stabilize substantially earlier than parameters, proposing training has an early structural phase followed by a late refinement phase.
  • Research program articulation: Formulates open questions around phase structure, structural adequacy (role-dependent "good" OUI ranges), formal theory connecting observables to generalization, and new training procedure designs.

Methodology

This is primarily a synthesis/position paper that reinterprets existing experimental results rather than introducing new experiments. The core methodology involves:

  1. OUI definition (batch-based formulation): For a layer with $d_l$ activation units and a training batch $B_t$ of size $B$, OUI computes a binary activation mask per neuron per sample, counts how many samples activate each neuron ($s_n$), derives a minority count $u_n = \min(s_n, B - s_n)$, and averages the normalized minority counts across neurons. The result is a scalar in [0, 1] measuring how balanced the activation partition is.
  1. Evidence integration across three domains:
  • Supervised learning: DenseNet-BC-100 on CIFAR-100 with 7 log-spaced weight decay values; OUI trajectories separate into regimes by ~15% of training, predicting final generalization.
  • Reinforcement learning: PPO actor-critic on CartPole-v1; OUI discriminates learning-rate regimes early, with actor and critic showing different preferred OUI bands.
  • Online control: Layer-wise weight decay adaptation driven by OUI feedback.
  1. Conceptual framing: Connects OUI to the geometry of piecewise-linear ReLU networks, where activation patterns define local affine regions, and argues that OUI compresses internal behavior into an actionable scalar observable.

Results & Highlights

  • Early separation of training regimes: OUI trajectories for different weight decay values on DenseNet-BC-100 become separable by ~15% of training, well before convergence. Final validation accuracies (66%–80%) are correctly anticipated.
  • Role-dependent activation structure in RL: In PPO, the actor and critic occupy distinct OUI bands — good critics favor intermediate OUI, while strong actors run at higher values — demonstrating that the "right" activation structure is context-dependent.
  • Activation convergence precedes parameter convergence: On ViT-B16/CIFAR-100, an activation-pattern convergence metric saturates much earlier than training and validation loss, supporting the two-timescale view.
  • Cross-architecture generality: The activation-centric signal appears meaningful across CNNs, Transformers, and MLP-like architectures, suggesting the pattern is not architecture-specific.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (moderate): The paper does not present new experiments. Its contribution is conceptual — reinterpreting existing results under a unified activation-centric framing. The intellectual contribution is real but incremental; it is essentially an extended argument for a research direction the authors are already pursuing.
  • Rigor (moderate): The synthesis is logically coherent, but the evidence base is limited to the authors' own prior work. No independent replication or external benchmarks are discussed. The PPO experiments use only CartPole-v1, a relatively simple environment. The supervised results cover standard CIFAR benchmarks but not large-scale settings.
  • Reproducibility (reasonable): OUI has a clear, simple mathematical definition that is easy to implement. However, the paper references results from other papers for detailed experimental settings, making full reproduction from this paper alone difficult.
  • Significance (potentially high if validated): If the activation-centric view holds up at scale (e.g., in foundation model training), it could change how practitioners monitor and tune training. However, this remains largely a hypothesis at this point — the paper is more of a research agenda than a conclusive demonstration.
  • Red flags: (1) The paper relies exclusively on self-citations for empirical evidence — no external group has validated OUI. (2) The CartPole-v1 RL experiments are toy-scale; claims about PPO are not tested on challenging environments. (3) The paper acknowledges OUI is most natural for ReLU-like activations; its applicability to softmax-based or continuous activation functions is unclear. (4) The feedback-loop confound (OUI as control signal affecting the observable itself) is mentioned but not analyzed.

Strengths

  • Clearly articulated conceptual contribution with a well-defined research program.
  • OUI is genuinely cheap to compute and label-free — practical appeal is strong.
  • The two-timescale (structure then refinement) training hypothesis is thought-provoking and connects to emerging evidence.
  • The observation that actor and critic prefer different OUI regimes is a nuanced, non-trivial finding.
  • Honest about limitations and open questions.

Limitations

  • No new experiments; entirely a synthesis of the authors' prior work.
  • All empirical evidence is at small-to-medium scale (CIFAR, CartPole); no validation on large models or datasets.
  • Self-contained reproducibility is limited — key experimental details are in referenced papers.
  • OUI may not generalize beyond ReLU-family activations without reformulation.
  • The feedback-loop risk when using OUI as an online control signal is acknowledged but not addressed empirically.
  • The paper's title mentions "structural observable" but no formal definition of "structure" is provided beyond the OUI scalar itself.

Relevance

OUI 作为一种廉价、无标签的训练过程内部监控信号，与训练动态研究和高效超参数搜索方向高度相关。其"激活结构先于参数收敛"的两阶段训练假说为理解大模型训练过程提供了新视角，值得在更大规模模型上验证。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does OUI behave during pretraining of large language models (e.g., 7B+ parameter transformers)? Does the early-separation property survive at scale?
  2. Can OUI be meaningfully adapted for non-ReLU activations (softmax attention patterns, SwiGLU, etc.)?
  3. What is the theoretical relationship between OUI and generalization bounds — is there a formal connection beyond the empirical correlation?
  4. How robust is OUI to batch composition and size? Could adversarial batch selection manipulate the signal?
  5. If OUI is used as an online control signal, how can the feedback-loop confound be detected and mitigated?
