> **一句话总结：** 本文首次系统研究了从监督微调（SFT）大语言模型中重构个人可识别信息（PII）的问题，提出了一种名为COVA的序列级解码算法，通过覆盖感知的候选搜索策略，在多种攻击者知识条件下显著优于现有的top-k采样和束搜索方法，揭示了SFT模型在医疗和法律等敏感领域的严重隐私泄露风险。

> **TL;DR:** This paper studies PII reconstruction from supervised finetuned (SFT) LLMs for the first time, proposing COVA — a coverage-aware decoding algorithm that outperforms beam search and top-k sampling for extracting structured PII like emails and dates of birth, and showing that even partial attacker knowledge significantly improves reconstruction success across medical and legal domains.

## 核心贡献

- **首次系统研究SFT模型的PII重构问题**：在医疗和法律领域的多轮对话场景下，评估不同攻击者知识水平对PII泄露的影响。
- **提出COVA解码算法**：一种覆盖感知的确定性解码策略，在序列级别维护候选池，避免了传统token级解码的误差传播问题。
- **构建了包含合成PII的多轮用户中心QA数据集**：从MedRedQA和法律咨询数据集出发，通过LLM生成一致的用户画像和多轮对话。
- **揭示攻击者知识的梯度效应**：即使只有部分知识（如症状关键词或人口统计信息），也能显著提升PII重构成功率。
- **分析了模型规模和架构的影响**：更大的模型（14B）和更强的模型家族（Qwen-2.5、Llama-3.1）展现出更高的PII泄露风险。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic study of PII reconstruction from supervised finetuned (SFT) language models under realistic multi-turn Q&A scenarios in medical and legal domains.
- COVA (Coverage-Aware Decoding), a novel deterministic decoding algorithm that maintains a global candidate pool with adaptive token expansion and sequence-level pruning, consistently outperforming beam search and top-k sampling.
- Construction of multi-turn, user-centric SFT datasets with synthetic PII from MedRedQA (medical) and a legal advice dataset, ensuring persona consistency across conversation turns.
- Comprehensive evaluation across 7 attacker knowledge settings (from redacted data access to name-only), demonstrating that even partial knowledge significantly boosts reconstruction.
- Cross-scale (3B/7B/14B) and cross-family (Qwen-2.5/Llama-3.1/DeepSeek-LLM) analysis showing both model size and architecture influence PII leakage.
- Log-likelihood ratio (LLR) re-ranking using base model access further improves candidate ranking for high-entropy PII types (DOB, name).

## Methodology

The paper constructs a realistic attack pipeline with three components: (1) **Prefix Construction** — 7 attacker knowledge settings ranging from full redacted data access to name-only knowledge, used to craft probing prompts; (2) **COVA Decoding** — a deterministic algorithm maintaining a partial candidate pool P and completed queue R. At each step, candidates are expanded via adaptive top-p or top-k selection (choosing the more restrictive), validated against PII-specific format constraints (regex for structured PII, non-alphabetic termination for names), and pruned globally by cumulative log-likelihood. The search terminates adaptively when no partial candidate can outscore the worst completed candidate; (3) **Evaluation** — reconstruction success rate measured at top-1, top-10, and top-100 thresholds, compared against base model performance to isolate fine-tuning-induced leakage. Datasets are built from MedRedQA (medical) and Reddit-sourced legal Q&A data, extended to multi-turn conversations with synthetic PII (name, email, phone, DOB, address, SSN, insurance) through LLM-generated persona-consistent profiles. Models are full-parameter fine-tuned (Qwen-2.5-7B primary, plus 3B/14B, Llama-3.1-8B, DeepSeek-LLM-7B) for 5 epochs.

## Results & Highlights

- **Email reconstruction** is alarmingly effective: with only the user's name, top-100 recall reaches ~15% on medical Q&A and ~11% on legal Q&A after fine-tuning, with 9-12% absolute improvement over base model.
- **DOB reconstruction** is harder but sensitive to attacker knowledge: redacted access yields 3.82% top-100 recall on medical Q&A vs. 0.57% with name-only; demographics (including age) provide a strong boost since models learn age-birthyear correlations.
- **COVA significantly outperforms baselines**: for DOB under moderate knowledge (in-distribution, summary), COVA achieves nearly 3x the reconstruction rate of beam search at top-100.
- **Larger models leak more**: Qwen-2.5-14B nearly doubles DOB reconstruction vs. 7B; model family matters — DeepSeek-LLM shows substantially less leakage than Qwen-2.5 and Llama-3.1 at similar scale.
- **LLR re-ranking helps for high-entropy PII**: using base model access to re-rank candidates improves DOB top-50 recall from 0.76% to 1.04% and name top-10 from 0.09% to 0.33%.
- **Identity inference shows nuanced behavior**: additional contextual detail does not always help, suggesting models rely on a limited set of salient cues rather than fully leveraging input context.

## Authors & Credibility

Both authors (Sae Furukawa and Alina Oprea) are from Northeastern University. Alina Oprea is a well-established researcher in computer security and privacy with extensive publications on ML privacy. The work is supported by NSF award CNS-2247484. The paper provides comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple models, datasets, and settings with clear threat model formalization.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** This is the first work studying PII reconstruction specifically from SFT models. Prior work focused on pre-trained or next-token-prediction fine-tuned models. The COVA algorithm addresses a genuine limitation of token-level decoding (error propagation / branching problem) with a principled sequence-level approach.
- **Rigor (High):** The threat model is well-formalized with 7 knowledge settings. The comparison against base models isolates fine-tuning-induced leakage. Evaluation across multiple model scales/families strengthens conclusions. Datasets will be released for reproducibility.
- **Reproducibility (Good):** Datasets promised for release. Standard models (Qwen, Llama, DeepSeek) from Hugging Face. COVA hyperparameters specified (K_keep=500, K_prune=300, N_best=500, N=100). Three H100 GPUs used.
- **Significance (High):** Results have clear implications for companies fine-tuning LLMs on user data (healthcare, legal chatbots). The finding that email reconstruction succeeds at >10% with only a name is practically alarming.
- **Red flags:** Datasets use synthetic (not real) PII, which may not fully capture real-world PII distribution patterns. The attack assumes access to token-level likelihoods, which may not be available in all deployment scenarios. The paper does not experiment with defense mechanisms (DP-SGD, scrubbing) on their own setup, only discusses them. The email reconstruction may partly reflect the structure of synthetic email generation rather than genuine memorization.

## Strengths

- Well-designed threat model with realistic, graduated attacker knowledge assumptions.
- COVA is a principled and effective algorithm that addresses a real technical limitation.
- Comprehensive evaluation: 2 domains × 3 PII types × 7 knowledge settings × 3 model scales × 3 model families.
- Clear separation of fine-tuning-induced leakage from base model prior via Δ metrics.
- Honest about limitations — identity inference gains are modest and non-monotonic with knowledge.

## Limitations

- Synthetic PII may not capture the diversity and distribution of real-world PII, potentially inflating or deflating reconstruction rates.
- Full-parameter SFT only — does not evaluate parameter-efficient methods (LoRA, adapters) which are increasingly common.
- No empirical defense evaluation; DP-SGD and scrubbing are only discussed qualitatively.
- Token-level likelihood access assumption excludes black-box API-only attack scenarios.
- Identity inference results are relatively weak (sub-3% even at top-100), limiting practical impact for that attack vector.

## Relevance

该研究对理解LLM微调过程中的隐私风险具有重要意义，尤其是对医疗和法律等敏感领域的AI应用开发者。COVA解码算法的设计思路（序列级覆盖感知搜索）对隐私审计工具的开发具有直接参考价值，也为差分隐私在SFT场景下的必要性提供了实证支持。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does COVA perform under parameter-efficient fine-tuning (LoRA, QLoRA) — do these methods inherently reduce PII memorization?
- What is the quantitative privacy-utility tradeoff when applying DP-SGD specifically to SFT on conversational data with PII?
- Can the COVA approach be adapted to black-box settings where only generated text (not logits) is available?
- How do repeated fine-tuning rounds (e.g., continual learning on new user data) compound PII leakage over time?
- Does synthetic PII generation methodology systematically bias reconstruction results compared to real-world PII distributions?
