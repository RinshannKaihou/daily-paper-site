> **一句话总结：** 本文提出ORCE框架，将LLM的回答生成与置信度估计解耦，通过基于采样的正确率代理和Spearman排序相关性DPO优化，使模型输出的自然语言置信度与回答可靠性排序对齐，在多个推理基准上显著改善校准效果且不损失回答准确率。

> **TL;DR:** ORCE decouples answer generation from confidence estimation in LLMs, using a sampling-based correctness surrogate and a Spearman-rank-correlation DPO objective to align verbalized confidence with response reliability. It achieves strong calibration and failure-prediction improvements across MMLU, DROP, and ReClor while preserving answer accuracy.

## 核心贡献

- **解耦框架：** 将回答生成与置信度生成分成两个独立阶段，置信度优化不会干扰回答生成的准确率。
- **基于采样的正确率代理（Surrogate）：** 对每个prompt采样K个回答，用经验正确频率作为prompt-level可靠性的无偏蒙特卡洛估计，无需访问模型内部logits。
- **排序感知的RL目标：** 使用Spearman相关系数作为全局排序奖励，通过DPO优化置信度LLM，使高正确率prompt对应高置信度输出，而非仅匹配绝对数值。
- **SFT预热机制：** 在RL之前先用SFT建立正向排序关联，防止排序奖励强化错误趋势。
- **跨模型可迁移性：** 在LLaMA-3 8B上训练的置信度对齐模型可迁移到Qwen3 8B和Mistral 7B，仍保持优越性能。

## Key Contributions

- Identify the coupling between answer generation and confidence generation as a key limitation of prior verbalized-confidence methods, and propose a two-stage decoupled framework.
- Introduce a sampling-based surrogate of correctness likelihood (ensemble-based Monte Carlo estimate) for supervising verbalized confidence without internal model probabilities.
- Propose an order-aware Spearman-correlation reward optimized via DPO that preserves the relative reliability ordering across responses rather than matching absolute values.
- Provide theoretical analysis showing the surrogate is consistent, the SC reward targets the global rank objective, and SFT warm-start ensures correct orientation.
- Demonstrate cross-model transferability: confidence alignment trained on LLaMA-3 8B transfers effectively to Qwen3 8B and Mistral 7B.

## Methodology

ORCE operates in two stages. **Stage 1 (Answer Generation):** A frozen answer LLM generates an answer for each prompt. Its parameters are never modified during confidence alignment, so answer accuracy is preserved by construction.

**Stage 2 (Confidence Estimation & Alignment):** A separate confidence LLM takes the (prompt, answer) pair as input and outputs a verbalized confidence score (e.g., "I am 72% confident").

To train this confidence LLM without ground-truth confidence labels:
1. **Surrogate construction:** For each prompt x, sample K answers from the answer LLM. Compute κ_s(x) = (1/K) Σ g(Y_i, y*), the empirical correctness frequency. This is an unbiased estimator of the true prompt-level reliability η(x).
2. **Prompt-answer pair selection:** If κ_s(x) ≥ 0.5, pair x with a correct sampled answer; otherwise pair with an incorrect one, so the answer reflects the model's empirical tendency.
3. **SFT warm-start:** Fine-tune the confidence LLM via supervised learning against the surrogate to establish a positive monotone relationship.
4. **DPO with Spearman reward:** Sample multiple confidence candidates from the confidence LLM for each (x, y) pair. Score each candidate c using R_SC(c, κ; C, K) = r_s(C ∪ {c}, K ∪ {κ}) − r_s(C, K), the marginal change in Spearman correlation between the confidence set and the surrogate set. The highest- and lowest-scoring candidates form preferred/rejected pairs for DPO training.

The global Spearman reward is preferred over local rank-matching (NRD) because it evaluates how each candidate affects the entire confidence–surrogate ordering, not just its individual rank discrepancy.

## Results & Highlights

- **MMLU (knowledge-intensive):** On LLaMA-3 8B, reduces ECE from 0.170 (Vanilla) to 0.025, achieving the lowest ECE. On Qwen3 8B, reduces ECE from 0.212 to 0.034. On Mistral 7B, achieves the highest Spearman correlation and best AURC/E-AURC while maintaining identical accuracy to Vanilla.
- **DROP (discrete reasoning):** Consistently achieves the best or near-best calibration and failure prediction across all three models while preserving answer F1 scores. On LLaMA-3 8B, obtains best Spearman correlation, AURC, and E-AURC.
- **ReClor (logical reasoning, OOD):** Reduces ECE from 0.178→0.065 (LLaMA-3), 0.164→0.044 (Qwen), 0.468→0.061 (Mistral). Achieves strongest Spearman correlation and best AURC/E-AURC across all models.
- **Ablations confirm:** Global Spearman reward > local NRD reward > numeric distance > SaySelf-inspired reward. All ablations preserve the same answer accuracy, confirming decoupling works.
- **Cross-model transfer:** Confidence alignment trained on LLaMA-3 8B and applied to Qwen3 8B/Mistral 7B still outperforms ConfTuner and SFT trained directly on those models, demonstrating strong transferability.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The decoupling idea is simple but impactful — prior methods (ConfTuner, CONQORD, SaySelf) all jointly optimize answer and confidence, and the interference problem is well-motivated. The Spearman-correlation reward as a global order-aware objective is a meaningful contribution over local rank matching. The theoretical analysis (consistency of surrogate, SC as marginal global objective, warm-start necessity) adds depth.

**Rigor:** Experiments cover three model families (LLaMA-3 8B, Qwen3 8B, Mistral 7B) and four benchmarks. Evaluation includes both calibration (ECE) and failure prediction (Spearman, AURC, E-AURC, AUPR), which is more comprehensive than many prior works. Ablations are thorough. However, the paper only tests on 7-8B scale models — scalability to larger models (70B+) is unknown. The training data for confidence alignment is derived from the answer model's own samples, which may limit generalization to distribution shift at deployment.

**Reproducibility:** The method uses standard DPO, SFT, and sampling procedures. No exotic architectures or custom training infrastructure. The Monte Carlo surrogate construction is straightforward to implement. Model checkpoints and benchmarks are publicly available. The paper should be largely reproducible.

**Significance:** The finding that confidence alignment can be decoupled from answer generation without accuracy loss is practically important for deploying LLMs in high-stakes settings. The cross-model transfer result is particularly valuable — it suggests one could train a universal confidence estimator.

**Red flags:** (1) Only tested on multiple-choice and extractive QA formats — it remains unclear how well verbalized confidence works for open-ended generation. (2) The surrogate quality depends heavily on K (number of samples) and the correctness function g, which is trivially defined for MC but harder for free-form tasks. (3) The computational cost of sampling K answers per prompt at training time is not discussed. (4) No comparison with concurrent or very recent methods beyond those included.

## Strengths

- Clean problem formulation: decoupling answer and confidence generation is an elegant solution to the interference problem.
- Strong empirical results across multiple models and benchmarks with consistent improvements.
- Theoretical grounding: the Spearman optimum theorem, surrogate consistency, and warm-start analysis provide principled justification.
- Excellent ablation study isolating the contribution of each component.
- Cross-model transferability is a practically valuable finding.

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on 7-8B parameter models; scaling behavior is unknown.
- Limited to multiple-choice and extractive tasks; free-form generation confidence remains unaddressed.
- Computational overhead of sampling K completions per prompt during training is not analyzed.
- The correctness function g is trivial for MC tasks but the paper does not deeply address how to define it for open-ended tasks where evaluation is itself unreliable.
- No human evaluation of whether the verbalized confidence values are actually useful to end users in practice.

## Relevance

这项工作直接解决LLM部署中的核心问题——如何让模型可靠地表达自身不确定性。解耦式置信度对齐方法和排序感知优化思路对构建可信赖AI系统具有实际参考价值，尤其是跨模型迁移的发现可降低置信度校准的工程成本。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does ORCE scale to 70B+ models where sampling K completions is much more expensive?
- Can the decoupled confidence LLM generalize to open-ended generation tasks (summarization, code, creative writing) where correctness is harder to define?
- What is the sensitivity of the method to the number of samples K — is there a diminishing-returns threshold?
- Could the Spearman-correlation reward be combined with tokenized confidence (logit-based) methods for a hybrid approach?
- Does the cross-model transferability extend to closed-source models (GPT-4, Claude) where only the confidence LLM is trainable?
