## Key Contributions

- Introduces the MCP framework that generalizes conformal calibration from a scalar nonconformity score + single threshold to a vector-valued score function `s(x, y, q)` with parameter vector `q Ōłł R^{n_q}`, breaking the structural rigidity of classical prediction set shapes.
- Unifies prediction set design and calibration into a single scenario optimization problem, eliminating the data split that reduces effective sample size in standard conformal prediction pipelines.
- Proves that RemMCP recovers split conformal prediction as a special case (Corollary 1, with `s(x,y,q) = s╠ā(x,y) - q`, `J(q) = q`) and is provably less conservative than dimension-wise Bonferroni-corrected SCP (Corollary 2).
- Develops RelMCP for non-convex score functions with an adaptive penalty-search algorithm (Alg. 1) that iteratively finds the least-conservative penalty parameter certifying target coverage.
- Derives finite-sample expected coverage (`E[╬Ę(q*)] Ōēź 1 - ╬Ą`, Theorem 1) and high-confidence coverage (`P(╬Ę(q*) Ōēź 1 - ╬Ą) Ōēź 1 - ╬▓`, Theorems 1ŌĆō2) guarantees for both variants, with exact Beta-distributional results under additional regularity conditions.

## Methodology

The paper bridges two previously disconnected fields: **split conformal prediction (SCP)** and **scenario optimization theory**.

**Problem setup.** Given a pretrained point predictor `f(x)` and a calibration set `M_cal = {(x^(m), y^(m))}_{m=1}^{n_cal}`, MCP seeks `q*` such that the prediction set `Y(x, q*) = {y | s(x, y, q*) Ōēż 0}` satisfies coverage `╬Ę(q*) = P(y Ōłł Y(x, q*) | M_cal) Ōēź 1 - ╬Ą` while minimizing a cost `J(q)` (e.g., prediction set volume).

**RemMCP (Section 3.1).** Formulated as constrained scenario optimization with constraint removal:
1. Compute outlier budget `Žü = ŌīŖ╬Ą(n_cal + 1) / n_qŌīŗ - 1` (generalizing the SCP quantile index).
2. Solve a cascade of `Žü + 1` convex programs, each discarding `k┬Ęn_q` support constraints via the Romao et al. (2023) removal scheme.
3. Coverage follows from scenario theory: `E[╬Ę(q*)] Ōēź 1 - n_q(Žü+1)/(n_cal+1) Ōēź 1 - ╬Ą`, with high-confidence bounds via Beta CDF.

Under additional assumptions (full support + violation condition), the coverage follows exactly `╬Ę(q*) ~ Beta(n_cal - n_q(Žü+1) + 1, n_q(Žü+1))`.

**RelMCP (Section 3.2).** Formulated as unconstrained optimization with constraint relaxation:
1. For a grid of penalty parameters `ŽĢ_i`, solve `q_i* = argmin J(q) + ŽĢ_i ╬Ż ╬Š_m(q)` where `╬Š_m(q) = max(0, max_j s_j(x^(m), y^(m), q))`.
2. Evaluate achievable miscoverage `╬Ą_i` a posteriori from the solution complexity `d_i` (number of active constraints + cardinality of support set).
3. Select the solution with the smallest `ŽĢ_i` such that `╬Ą_i Ōēż ╬Ą`.

The adaptive algorithm (Alg. 1) performs bisection-like search over penalty parameters, maintaining valid/invalid solution bounds.

**Score/cost function design (Section 3.3, Appendix C).** The paper provides concrete instantiations for intervals, ellipsoids, zonotopes, sublevel sets, and multi-modal unions ŌĆö all jointly optimized without additional data splits.

## Results & Highlights

**Benchmark:** Vehicle position prediction at an intersection (from Tumu et al., 2024), predicting `T` steps ahead with `n_cal = 2,000` calibration points, `╬Ą = 0.05`, evaluated over 100 calibration runs and 10,000 test points.

**Coverage (Figure 3):** All methods meet the target 1 - ╬Ą = 0.95 on average. RelMCP consistently exceeds it by a small margin due to its iterative penalty search conservatism.

**Prediction set volume (Figure 3):**
- Among single-ellipsoid methods, **RemMCP yields the tightest sets**.
- Among multi-modal methods, **RelMCP-B is the most robust**: at T=2, mean volumes of SCP-2B and SCP-3B are 80,139 and 87,022 (inflated by degenerate runs), while RelMCP-B achieves stable mean volume below 40.
- At T=50 (long-horizon, multi-modal residuals), multi-modal methods produce substantially smaller sets than spherical SCP-1.

**Variance (quantified):** At T=30, coverage standard deviation ranges from 0.0066ŌĆō0.0101 for SCP-2/3 vs. 0.0056 for RemMCP and 0.0039ŌĆō0.0046 for RelMCP ŌĆö directly predicted by the theoretical Beta distributions (Figure 4).

**Computation:** SCP-1/2/3 complete in <10 ms; RemMCP ~0.2 s; RelMCP-A ~0.8 s; RelMCP-B ~21 s (driven by high-dimensional non-convex optimization over union-of-ellipsoids parameters).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The MCP generalization is conceptually natural but technically non-trivial. The key insight ŌĆö mapping conformal calibration to scenario optimization with constraint removal/relaxation ŌĆö is elegant and opens a new design space. Prior work (zono-conformal prediction by the same first author) was a specific instantiation; this paper provides the general framework.
- **Rigor: High.** All coverage guarantees are rigorously derived from established scenario theory results (Romao et al. 2023, Garatti & Campi 2025). The proofs (Appendix A) are clean and the recovery of SCP as a special case (Corollary 1) provides a valuable sanity check. The provable dominance over Bonferroni (Corollary 2) is a strong theoretical result.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate-High.** Algorithms are precisely specified (including pseudocode for RelMCP's adaptive search). The vehicle prediction benchmark is from a published paper. However, no code repository is mentioned, and the score/cost function design involves several implementation choices (Monte Carlo volume estimation, k-means initialization for multi-modal sets) that could affect reproducibility.
- **Significance: High.** Data splitting is a fundamental inefficiency in conformal prediction. MCP addresses it head-on with theoretical guarantees. The variance reduction alone is practically significant ŌĆö in safety-critical applications, consistent coverage across calibration runs matters as much as average coverage.
- **Red flags:** (1) RemMCP requires convexity of `s(x, y, q)` in `q`, which restricts the prediction set families (e.g., union-of-ellipsoids requires RelMCP). (2) The adaptive RelMCP algorithm (Alg. 1) breaks the independence assumption of Theorem 2 ŌĆö the authors acknowledge this but leave a rigorous treatment to future work. (3) Computational cost for high-dimensional non-convex problems (21 s for RelMCP-B vs. <10 ms for SCP) could limit scalability. (4) The framework still requires exchangeable data and does not address distribution shift.

## Strengths

- Elegant unification of two powerful but previously disconnected frameworks (conformal prediction + scenario optimization).
- Provably recovers split CP as a special case and strictly improves over Bonferroni-corrected dimension-wise CP.
- Eliminates data splitting, leading to both smaller prediction sets and dramatically lower variance across calibration runs.
- Flexible framework supporting intervals, ellipsoids, zonotopes, sublevel sets, and multi-modal unions with concrete design guidelines.
- Clear theoretical contributions with two complementary variants covering convex and non-convex settings.
- Honest discussion of limitations (computational cost, exchangeability requirement, RelMCP adaptive algorithm's theoretical gap).

## Limitations

- RemMCP requires convex score functions in `q`, restricting its applicability; RelMCP is needed for non-convex shapes but introduces additional conservatism and computational overhead.
- Computational cost scales significantly: RelMCP-B takes ~21 s for a union-of-3-ellipsoids problem with `n_cal = 2,000`, compared to <10 ms for standard SCP ŌĆö a 2000├Ś slowdown.
- The adaptive RelMCP algorithm's coverage guarantee is not rigorously established (data-dependent penalty selection breaks Theorem 2's assumptions); the authors rely on empirical validation.
- Requires sufficiently large calibration sets relative to parameter dimension (`Žü Ōēź 0` requires `n_cal Ōēź n_q/╬Ą - 1`), which may be restrictive for high-dimensional prediction set families.
- Limited to exchangeable data; extending to distribution shift, online, or time-series settings is left to future work.
- Does not leverage training data for shape estimation, unlike some SCP variants that use training residuals for covariance estimation.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The adaptive RelMCP algorithm (Alg. 1) breaks Theorem 2's independence assumption ŌĆö can a rigorous coverage guarantee be established for data-dependent penalty selection, perhaps via martingale arguments or online learning theory?
- How does MCP scale to very high-dimensional output spaces (e.g., image generation, point clouds) where `n_q` could be in the hundreds or thousands?
- Can MCP be combined with conditional conformal prediction methods (e.g., CQR, conformalized quantile regression) to achieve both conditional and marginal coverage without data splitting?
- What is the computational bottleneck in RelMCP-B ŌĆö is it the repeated non-convex optimization or the solution complexity evaluation? Could warm-starting or surrogate models accelerate it?
- The paper shows RemMCP dominates Bonferroni ŌĆö does it also dominate other multi-output CP methods like copula-based approaches (Messoudi et al., 2021) or graph-based architectures (Cini et al., 2025)?
- Can the MCP framework incorporate training data (not just calibration data) for shape estimation while maintaining coverage guarantees, perhaps via full conformal or jackknife-style approaches?
