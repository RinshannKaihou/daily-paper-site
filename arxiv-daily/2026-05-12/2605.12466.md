> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Attractor Models，将循环 Transformer 的迭代精炼重新表述为在输出嵌入空间中求解不动点问题，利用隐式微分实现常数级训练内存，在语言建模和推理任务上显著超越标准 Transformer 和现有循环模型，并发现"均衡内化"现象——模型在训练中将迭代求解的结果内化到骨干网络的初始预测中，推理时几乎不需要迭代。

> **TL;DR:** Attractor Models reformulate recurrent refinement in Transformers as fixed-point solving in output-embedding space with implicit differentiation for constant training memory. They achieve up to 46.6% perplexity improvement, outperform 1.3B Transformers with 770M parameters, reach 91.4% on Sudoku-Extreme (where frontier LLMs score 0%), and exhibit equilibrium internalization where the backbone learns to predict near the fixed point, making iterative refinement largely unnecessary at inference.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 Attractor Models 架构：骨干网络先提出输出嵌入，然后吸引子模块通过求解不动点进行精炼，训练内存与有效深度无关
- 利用隐函数定理的梯度（隐式微分），使得无论求解器迭代多少次，反向传播的内存开销恒定
- 在语言建模上取得帕累托改进：770M 参数的 Attractor Model 超过了用两倍 token 训练的 1.3B Transformer
- 在困难推理任务上，27M 参数模型在 Sudoku-Extreme 上达到 91.4%，在 Maze-Hard 上达到 93.1%，远超 Claude、O3 等前沿模型（均为 0%）
- 发现"均衡内化"（equilibrium internalization）现象：训练过程中骨干网络学会将初始预测置于不动点附近，推理时求解器可被移除且性能几乎不降

## Key Contributions

- Introduce the Attractor Model architecture: a backbone proposes output embeddings, an attractor module refines them by solving for a fixed point in tied output-embedding space
- Achieve constant training memory in effective depth via implicit differentiation through the equilibrium, eliminating BPTT memory scaling
- Deliver a Pareto improvement over standard Transformers and looped models (Parcae) in language modeling: up to 46.6% perplexity reduction and 19.7% downstream accuracy improvement with 25-31% lower training FLOPs
- A 770M Attractor Model outperforms a 1.3B Transformer trained on twice as many tokens
- On reasoning benchmarks, a 27M model achieves 91.4% on Sudoku-Extreme and 93.1% on Maze-Hard — where Claude 3.7, DeepSeek R1, and O3-mini all score 0%
- Identify and characterize equilibrium internalization: the backbone learns to place its initial prediction near the fixed point, allowing the iterative solver to be removed at inference with negligible degradation

## Methodology

The Attractor Model architecture consists of two components:

**Backbone proposal.** A standard Transformer backbone $T_{\theta_b}$ processes the input embeddings $E(x)$ and produces an initial output-space prediction $\tilde{y}_0 = T_{\theta_b}(E(x))$. This lives in the same tied output-embedding space, so it can be directly decoded via the unembedding matrix $E^\top$.

**Attractor refinement.** An attractor module $T_{\theta_a}$ (a smaller weight-tied Transformer block) refines the proposal by solving the fixed-point equation:
$$\tilde{y}^\star = T_{\theta_a}(\tilde{y}^\star, \tilde{y}_0)$$
using Anderson acceleration with adaptive convergence criteria. The attractor uses additive injection of $\tilde{y}_0$ at every refinement step, ensuring the fixed point remains input-dependent.

**Training via implicit differentiation.** Rather than backpropagating through the unrolled solver iterations, gradients are computed using the implicit function theorem. The backward pass uses a one-step approximation ($u \approx v$) that provides nearly identical quality to full implicit differentiation at 1× memory and 1× step time (vs. 4.8× and 2.7× for full IFT).

**Key design choices:**
- The equilibrium is placed directly in the tied output-embedding space (not hidden state space like DEQ)
- The solver is warm-started from the backbone proposal (not from zero or random initialization)
- Additive proposal injection (not concatenation or initialization-only) keeps the refinement map well-conditioned
- A layer-scale parameter $\gamma$ with learning rate scheduling controls the attractor's influence during early training

## Results & Highlights

**Language modeling (FineWeb-Edu):**
- At 140M/370M/770M scales, Attractor Models consistently outperform parameter-matched Transformers and Parcae (looped model) on validation perplexity, CORE accuracy, and CORE-Extended accuracy
- 770M Attractor Model achieves better perplexity than 1.3B Transformer trained on 2× tokens
- Training FLOPs reduced by 25-31% compared to Parcae
- Peak memory remains constant regardless of effective solver depth

**Reasoning (Sudoku-Extreme & Maze-Hard):**
- 27M Attractor Model: 91.4% Sudoku-Extreme, 93.1% Maze-Hard
- Frontier LLMs (DeepSeek R1 671B, Claude 3.7, O3-mini-high): 0% on both tasks
- TRM (strongest tiny baseline at 7M): collapses to 0% when scaled to 27M; Attractor Models scale cleanly
- Training uses only ~1,000 examples

**Equilibrium internalization:**
- At inference, $T=1$ iteration matches or exceeds converged performance; even $T=0$ (backbone-only) is competitive
- 140M Attractor Model at $T=0$ matches Parcae 140M at $T=8$ recurrent steps
- During training, solver iterations rapidly decrease to the minimum, indicating the backbone absorbs the refinement

**Ablations confirm:**
- Backbone proposal initialization is critical (zero/Gaussian init degrades to PPL 43.87/41.26 vs. 34.05)
- Additive injection outperforms concatenation and initialization-only schemes
- One-step backward approximation loses only 0.14 PPL vs. full IFT while using 4.8× less memory

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The key insight — placing the equilibrium in output-embedding space with backbone warm-start rather than hidden-state space — is genuinely novel and distinguishes this from DEQ (Bai et al., 2019). The equilibrium internalization phenomenon is a surprising and potentially important finding. The connection between implicit-gradient barriers and contractive training dynamics (Appendix B.3) is theoretically interesting.

**Rigor (High):** The paper provides formal theorems (well-posedness, implicit gradient validity, LoopLM as truncated approximation), extensive ablations isolating each design choice, and careful parameter-matched comparisons. The Sudoku/Maze results include comparisons against frontier models on identical benchmarks.

**Reproducibility (Moderate-High):** Hyperparameters are exhaustively documented in Appendix C (architecture, solver, training configs for all scales). The nanochat framework is public. However, the code is not released with the paper, and the solver hyperparameters (tolerances, Anderson $m$, $\beta$) may require careful tuning. The authors note that `torch.compile` is incompatible with implicit-gradient hooks.

**Significance (High):** If the results hold at larger scales (1B+), Attractor Models could change how recurrent depth is incorporated into language models — eliminating the memory bottleneck of BPTT and the inference-time cost of explicit iteration. The equilibrium internalization result suggests a path to models that reap training benefits from iterative computation without inference overhead.

**Red flags:**
- Largest model tested is 770M — unclear whether the approach scales to 7B+ where practical LLMs operate
- The one-step gradient approximation may become inadequate for deeper/wider attractor modules
- No comparison against mixture-of-depths or early-exit models, which also adaptively allocate compute
- The reasoning benchmarks (Sudoku/Maze) are specialized algorithmic tasks — unclear if gains transfer to broader reasoning
- Equilibrium internalization means the attractor module becomes somewhat redundant at inference, raising the question of whether simply training a larger backbone with regularization could achieve similar results

## Strengths

- Clean, principled formulation that unifies DEQ-style fixed-point models with output-embedding-space prediction
- Strong empirical results across two very different regimes (large-scale LM and tiny-model reasoning)
- The equilibrium internalization phenomenon is well-characterized with both qualitative (PCA trajectories) and quantitative (solver iteration counts, $T=0$ performance) evidence
- Training efficiency gains (constant memory, fewer FLOPs) are practically significant
- Thorough ablation study isolating the contribution of each architectural choice

## Limitations

- Only tested up to 770M parameters; scaling behavior at 7B+ remains unknown
- No investigation of why TRM collapses at larger sizes while Attractor Models do not — the explanation (fixed-point objective as regularizer) is plausible but not mechanistically validated
- The framework assumes the attractor dynamics satisfy contraction (Assumption 1); no guarantee this holds for arbitrary trained blocks, and the authors acknowledge Jacobian regularization may be needed
- Inference with the solver active still requires iterative computation; equilibrium internalization mitigates this but effectively reduces the model to a feed-forward network at deployment
- The paper does not address how Attractor Models interact with standard LLM techniques (RLHF, instruction tuning, MoE)

## Relevance

本文提出了一种基于不动点求解的循环 Transformer 训练方法，通过隐式微分实现常数级训练内存，这与 LLM 训练稳定性和可扩展性直接相关。均衡内化现象为理解模型内部表示如何收敛提供了新的视角，与隐状态可解释性研究方向高度契合。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does equilibrium internalization persist at 7B+ scale, or does the attractor module become essential at larger capacities?
2. How do Attractor Models interact with post-training alignment (RLHF, DPO) — does the fixed-point structure complicate reward optimization?
3. Can the implicit-gradient barrier analysis (Appendix B.3) be used to predict and prevent training instabilities in standard looped LMs?
4. What happens when the attractor module is applied selectively (e.g., only on "hard" tokens) — can this yield adaptive compute allocation?
5. Is equilibrium internalization related to the phenomenon of representation compression observed in deep Transformer layers, and could probing the attractor's fixed-point space reveal interpretable features?
