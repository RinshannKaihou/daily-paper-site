GKnow: Measuring the Entanglement of Gender Bias and Factual Gender

arXiv: 2605.12299

Date: 2026-05-12

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12299

一句话总结： 本文构建 GKnow 基准，通过 EAP-IG 电路分析和神经元消融实验，揭示 Llama-3.1-8B 和 Olmo-7B 中性别偏见与事实性别知识在电路和神经元层面严重纠缠，导致基于消融的去偏方法不可靠。
TL;DR: The authors curate GKnow, a benchmark covering multiple gender-related prediction types (pronoun, gender indicator, name, lexical noun, stereotypical noun), and show via EAP-IG circuit analysis and neuron ablation on Llama-3.1-8B and Olmo-7B that gender bias and factual gender knowledge are severely entangled at both circuit and neuron levels, making ablation-based debiasing unreliable and invisible to standard bias benchmarks.

核心贡献

  • 构建 GKnow 基准数据集（91,490 个示例），覆盖五种性别相关预测类型（代词、性别标识、姓名、词汇性别名词、刻板印象名词），可同时评估事实性别知识和性别偏见
  • 利用 EAP-IG 方法发现事实性别电路与刻板印象性别电路存在高重叠（IoU 分析）和高跨任务忠实性，证实电路级别的严重纠缠
  • 通过神经元消融实验证明：消除刻板印象神经元会同时严重损害模型的事实性别预测能力（如 Llama 中消融 top-50 神经元后，GKnow Factual 的 Pexp 从 91.49 降至 79.86）
  • 揭示 StereoSet 等现有偏见基准会隐藏消融带来的事实性别知识损失，可能误导对去偏效果的评估
  • 对 Llama-3.1-8B 和 Olmo-7B 两个模型进行了系统验证，结果一致

Key Contributions

  • Curate GKnow, a comprehensive benchmark (91,490 examples; 6,992 used in this work) for evaluating gender knowledge and bias across five gender-related prediction types: pronoun prediction, gender indicator prediction, name prediction, lexically gendered noun prediction, and stereotypically gendered noun prediction
  • Apply EAP-IG (state-of-the-art circuit discovery method) to find minimal faithful circuits for each GKnow subset, demonstrating high IoU and cross-task faithfulness between factual and stereotypical gender circuits — e.g., the gender_prediction_based_on_stereo circuit achieves perfect faithfulness (1.0) on the gender_prediction_based_on_pronoun subset
  • Show via neuron ablation (zero-ablation of top-10 and top-50 IG neurons) that ablating stereotypical neurons significantly damages factual gender predictions: in Llama, GKnow Factual Pexp drops from 91.49 to 79.86 (top-50), with all zero-ablation results statistically significant (p < 0.05)
  • Demonstrate that standard bias benchmarks (StereoSet) can hide the degradation of factual gender knowledge caused by ablation, as StereoSet only shows small decreases (e.g., Pexp drops only 2.66 for top-50 ablation in Llama) while factual knowledge drops much more sharply
  • Validate findings across two models (Llama-3.1-8B and Olmo-7B) with consistent results, and provide logit lens interpretation of shared gender neurons

Methodology

The paper follows a three-stage methodology:

Benchmark Construction (GKnow): GKnow entries are categorized by (i) a gender assumption (the subject type: pronoun, name, lexically gendered noun, stereotypically gendered noun, or explicit gender indicator) and (ii) a gendered prediction (the expected output type: pronoun, gender indicator, name, lexical noun, or stereotypical noun). This yields 20 subset combinations (5 assumption types × 4 prediction types). Prompts involving stereotypical assumptions or predictions are classified as "Stereotypical"; others as "Factual." The full dataset has 91,490 examples; a curated subset of 6,992 is used in experiments.

Circuit Analysis (Section 4): The authors apply EAP-IG (Edge Attribution Patching with Integrated Gradients), the top-performing method from the Mechanistic Interpretability Benchmark (MIB), to identify minimal faithful circuits (recovering ≥80% of model behavior) for each GKnow subset. They compute: (1) Intersection over Union (IoU) of circuit nodes/edges across subsets; (2) Cross-task faithfulness — applying one subset's circuit to another's task. Experiments use corrupted prompts as counterfactual baselines with m=5 integration steps.

Neuron-Level Analysis (Section 5): Using integrated gradients on the GKnow training split, the authors identify the most important FFN neurons for stereotypical gender predictions. They then zero-ablate the top-10 and top-50 neurons and evaluate on GKnow (stereotypical and factual subsets), StereoSet (intrasentence, gender category), and DiFair (neutral and specific subsets). Metrics include P_exp, P_opp, P_other, %exp, %opp, %other, and Δ_{f,m} (probability gap between masculine and feminine outputs). Significance is assessed via t-tests (p < 0.05).

Models: Llama-3.1-8B and Olmo-7B (decoder-only transformers). All analyses run on NVIDIA RTX A6000 and A100 GPUs; full replication takes ~6 hours.

Results & Highlights

  • Circuit-level entanglement: Cross-task faithfulness shows stereotypical circuits recover performance on factual tasks and vice versa. For example, gender_prediction_based_on_stereo → gender_prediction_based_on_pronoun achieves faithfulness of 1.0 in Llama. IoU between factual and stereotypical circuits is substantial (e.g., 0.42–0.56 for nodes).
  • Neuron-level entanglement: Ablating top-50 stereotypical neurons in Llama reduces GKnow Factual Pexp by 11.63 points (91.49→79.86) and increases P_opp by 5.21 points. In Olmo, the drop is 7.27 points for factual Pexp. All results are statistically significant.
  • Hidden degradation: StereoSet shows minimal impact from ablation (Pexp drops only 2.66 for top-50 in Llama, 4.13 in Olmo), potentially misleading evaluators into thinking debiasing is successful without harming the model.
  • Probability gap collapse: Δ_{f,m} drops sharply after ablation (e.g., 43.77→18.03 for GKnow Factual in Llama with top-50 ablation), indicating reduced confidence in all gendered predictions.
  • Logit lens interpretation: Shared neurons across subsets are interpretable — e.g., Olmo L31N8077 promotes male-associated tokens (spokesman, his) and suppresses female-associated tokens (she, her, woman), appearing in 5 different GKnow subsets.
  • Random ablation control: Ablating random neurons produces no statistically significant effects, confirming that observed changes are specific to gender-relevant neurons.
  • Stereotypical-only neurons: Ablating neurons unique to the stereotypical subset (not shared with factual subsets) produces significant but small effects — not enough to flip predictions — suggesting the most impactful neurons are shared across factual and stereotypical tasks.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The systematic disentanglement of factual vs. stereotypical gender at both circuit and neuron levels is novel and important. Prior work either focused on single tasks (pronoun prediction) or did not distinguish factual from biased gender in mechanistic analysis.
  • Rigor: Strong. The multi-benchmark evaluation (GKnow, StereoSet, DiFair), two model architectures, statistical significance testing, random ablation controls, and multiple ablation methods (zero and mean) provide a thorough experimental design. The decoupled metrics framework (P_exp, P_opp, %exp, etc.) is well-justified.
  • Reproducibility: High. Code and data are released. Full replication takes ~6 hours on a single GPU. The methodology (EAP-IG, integrated gradients) uses standard, well-documented techniques.
  • Significance: Important for the fairness community. The core finding — that neuron ablation is fundamentally unreliable for gender debiasing because bias and knowledge share the same circuitry — has practical implications. It challenges a growing body of work proposing ablation-based debiasing.
  • Red flags / concerns:
  • Only two models tested (7B–8B scale). Whether the same entanglement holds in much larger models (70B+, 405B) or in instruction-tuned/RLHF models remains unknown.
  • The binary gender framework (masculine/feminine) is limiting and acknowledged only implicitly. Non-binary gender is not addressed.
  • The GKnow subset used (6,992 out of 91,490) is relatively small; the paper does not fully justify this selection or analyze whether the full dataset would yield different conclusions.
  • The paper does not propose an alternative debiasing method, which limits its constructive contribution — though the diagnostic contribution is valuable.
  • Some cross-task faithfulness results in Olmo exceed 1.0, which is noted but not fully explained.

Strengths

  • Rigorous multi-level analysis: circuit-level (EAP-IG) and neuron-level (integrated gradients + ablation), providing complementary evidence for entanglement
  • GKnow is a well-designed benchmark that fills a real gap — prior datasets either conflate or ignore the factual/biased gender distinction
  • The finding that existing benchmarks hide factual knowledge degradation is actionable and important for future benchmark design
  • Strong experimental controls: random ablation, mean ablation, stereotypical-only neurons, statistical significance testing
  • Logit lens analysis provides human-interpretable evidence of neuron-level entanglement
  • Two models tested with consistent results strengthens generality claims

Limitations

  • Only tested on 7B–8B decoder-only transformers; findings may not generalize to larger models, encoder-decoder architectures, or multilingual models
  • Binary gender framework excludes non-binary and context-dependent gender
  • The paper identifies the problem (entanglement) but does not propose solutions for disentangling bias from factual knowledge
  • GKnow is English-only; the linguistic distinction between lexical and social gender may not transfer to languages with grammatical gender
  • The 6,992-example subset used in experiments is small relative to the full 91,490-example dataset
  • The causal direction of the analysis (circuits are identified from behavior, not from ground-truth mechanisms) means the entanglement finding is correlational at the circuit level

Relevance

本文通过电路分析和神经元消融实验，系统揭示了语言模型中性别偏见与事实性别知识在机制层面的深度纠缠，对理解模型内部偏见表示和设计更可靠的去偏方法具有重要参考价值。其 GKnow 基准和纠缠分析框架可直接应用于评估和改进中文语言模型的性别公平性研究。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the entanglement between factual gender and gender bias persist in larger models (70B, 405B parameters), or does increased scale allow for greater separation?
  • How do instruction tuning and RLHF alignment affect the entanglement — does alignment reduce, mask, or potentially worsen it?
  • Can contrastive or disentanglement-based training objectives at the representation level separate factual gender knowledge from stereotypical bias without ablation?
  • How would the GKnow framework extend to languages with grammatical gender (e.g., French, German, Arabic), where gender marking is pervasive and syntactically obligatory?
  • What are the implications for intersectional fairness — are similar entanglements observed between, e.g., racial bias and factual racial/ethnic knowledge?
  • Could sparse autoencoder (SAE) features provide a more fine-grained decomposition that disentangles bias from knowledge better than individual neurons?
