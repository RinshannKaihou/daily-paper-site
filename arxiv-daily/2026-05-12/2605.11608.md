> **一句话总结：** PRISM 利用 LLM 线性输出头和近等距骨干的几何结构，推导出交叉熵风险差的闭合上界，并将其分解为尺度失配、形状失配和头部发散三个可独立诊断的轴，同时可直接用作训练正则化缓解灾难性遗忘。

> **TL;DR:** PRISM derives a closed-form upper bound on the cross-entropy risk gap between an LLM and its post-training variants (quantized, LoRA-adapted), decomposing drift into scale, shape, and head axes that each maps to a distinct failure mode and suggests a targeted remediation.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了 PRISM（Proxy Risk Inference via Structural Mapping），利用 LLM 线性输出头结构和近等距骨干特性，推导出交叉熵风险差 |R_T - R_P| 的**闭合上界**（Theorem 1），将漂移精确分解为三个几何轴。
- **尺度失配** (scale mismatch)：激活幅度坍缩，主要对应 LoRA 微调中的跨任务漂移。
- **形状失配** (shape mismatch)：特征几何结构畸变，主要对应低比特量化（Q2/Q3）的退化模式。
- **头部发散** (head divergence)：预测头偏移，主要对应 GGUF k-quant 中量化 lm_head 的情形。
- 证明了形状项 (1-Ω) 是可微的，可直接作为**训练时正则化项**，对抗 LoRA 微调中的灾难性遗忘，且在实验中优于经验回放基线。
- 推导了基于 token 嵌入对距离的更紧 Lipschitz 常数 K_feat，而非朴素的谱范数，使界在 LLM 词表规模（V ~ 10^5）下仍然紧凑。

## Key Contributions

- Derives a closed-form, Procrustes-grounded upper bound on the cross-entropy risk gap between a target LLM and any post-training variant (quantized, LoRA-adapted), with an exact three-axis decomposition: scale, shape, and head (Theorem 1).
- Introduces a sharper Lipschitz constant for cross-entropy w.r.t. features based on pairwise token-embedding distances (not spectral norm), keeping the bound informative at LLM vocabulary scale.
- Shows each decomposition axis maps to a qualitatively distinct failure mode: shape distortion under low-bit PTQ, scale separability under LoRA forgetting, and head divergence when lm_head is quantized (GGUF k-quants).
- Demonstrates the differentiable shape term (1 − Ω) doubles as a training-time regularizer against catastrophic forgetting under frozen-head LoRA, outperforming experience replay on TruthfulQA and Llama BBQ.
- Achieves mean Spearman rs = 0.820 for PTQ variant ranking and rs = 0.831 for LoRA forgetting ranking across Llama, Qwen, Ministral, and DeepSeek families on five benchmarks.

## Methodology

PRISM exploits two structural properties of modern LLMs: (1) the linear lm_head over a non-linear backbone, and (2) empirically near-isometric hidden representations across variants of the same model family.

**Theorem 1 (Unified Risk Bound).** For any orthogonal alignment W ∈ O(d), the cross-entropy risk gap decomposes as:

|RT − RP| ≤ δ + γ

where δ (feature alignment error) is bounded by K_feat · √[(ρT − ρP)² + 2ρTρP(1 − ΩW)] and γ (head discrepancy) is bounded by K_pred · ‖Σ_P^{1/2}(WHT − HP)‖_F.

- **Scale mismatch** (∆ρ)²: RMS norm difference of feature matrices. Captures activation magnitude collapse.
- **Shape mismatch** 2ρTρP(1 − Ω): Procrustes shape similarity deficit. Captures feature-geometry distortion. The trace form Ω = Tr(ZT^T ZP) / (‖ZT‖F · ‖ZP‖F) is SVD-free and differentiable.
- **Head discrepancy** γ: Covariance-weighted distance between aligned heads. Vanishes under frozen lm_head (PTQ with FP16 head, frozen-head LoRA).

**Lipschitz analysis.** The feature-side Lipschitz constant K_feat = max_{j,k} ‖h_{T,j} − h_{T,k}‖_2 is derived via simplex polarization of the softmax gradient, depending on pairwise token-embedding distances rather than the spectral norm. Empirically ranges from 0.93 (Mistral) to 3.46 (Qwen3-Base) for 8B models.

**Shape regularizer.** Under frozen-head LoRA (HT = HP), γ = 0 and the differentiable shape term λ(1 − Ω(Z0_ref, Zt_ref)) is added as a trace-norm penalty on a reference set, directly contracting the dominant axis of forgetting.

**Experimental setup.** Evaluated on 7 models (Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen3-8B, Ministral-3-8B, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-8B, plus instruction-tuned variants) across GGUF (Q2_K to Q8_0), BitsAndBytes (INT8, NF4, FP4), and GPTQ (4-bit) backends, with 5 benchmarks (ARC, MMLU, SQuAD, TriviaQA, GSM8K) and LoRA forgetting experiments on TruthfulQA and BBQ.

## Results & Highlights

- **PTQ variant ranking:** Mean Spearman rs = 0.820 across all model families and benchmarks. Per-benchmark: ARC 0.79, MMLU 0.87, SQuAD 0.73, TriviaQA 0.89, GSM8K 0.48 (weakest due to long CoT diluting per-token loss).
- **LoRA forgetting ranking:** Mean Spearman rs = 0.831 across TruthfulQA and BBQ tasks.
- **Three-axis decomposition** localizes failure modes: Q2/Q3 quantization → shape distortion dominates; LoRA cross-task drift → scale separability dominates; Qwen3 Q6_K/Q8_0 → head divergence dominates (GGUF quantizes lm_head, inflating γ to ~75.77 while δ stays at 1.18).
- **Shape regularizer vs. replay:** On Llama3.1-8B, trace-norm penalty (λ=1.0) reduces mean |∆R| on TruthfulQA from 0.84 to 0.68 (−19%), while replay baseline only reaches 0.76 (−9%). On Llama BBQ, trace lifts Ω from 0.93 to 0.98 while replay leaves it flat.
- **Ablation:** Shape-only similarity Ω already achieves mean rs = 0.804; adding scale (δ) lifts to 0.868. Full bound B with Procrustes alignment (BN) achieves rs = 0.912.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The key contribution is the formal lifting of Procrustes shape metrics into a certified risk bound on the deployed prediction head, with an exact three-axis decomposition. This goes substantially beyond CKA/SVCCA by connecting representation geometry to downstream cross-entropy risk. The simplex-polarization Lipschitz constant is a genuine technical improvement over naive spectral bounds.
- **Rigor (High):** Theorem 1 is proven with complete detail in the appendix. The decomposition identity (Prop. 1) is exact, not approximate. The bound is valid for any orthogonal W, with the identity and Procrustes-optimal specializations treated as corollaries. Empirical evaluation covers multiple model families and backends with per-variant decomposition tables.
- **Reproducibility (Moderate-High):** All models are publicly available on HuggingFace. Quantization backends (llama.cpp GGUF, BitsAndBytes, GPTQ) are standard. No custom training code released, but the method description (single forward pass, teacher-forced features) is straightforward to implement. The trace regularizer requires only standard PyTorch operations.
- **Significance (High):** Addresses a practical bottleneck—diagnosing why a variant degraded, not just that it degraded—with a theoretically grounded tool. The diagnostic-to-remediation pipeline (axis → targeted fix) is compelling and actionable.
- **Red flags / caveats:**
  - GSM8K correlations are weak (rs ≈ 0.41–0.48), attributed to long CoT spans diluting per-token loss. This limits applicability to reasoning-heavy benchmarks.
  - The bound is calibrated for **variant ranking** (Spearman correlation), not for tight absolute estimation of |∆R|. The paper is transparent about this.
  - Teacher-forced features are used for efficiency; free-running generation trajectories are left to future work.
  - The method assumes frozen or near-identical lm_head for γ to vanish; full-parameter SFT with divergent heads is explicitly left to future work.

## Strengths

- Theoretically grounded: closed-form bound with complete proof, not a heuristic similarity score.
- Practically useful: single forward pass per variant, no calibration data or auxiliary probes needed.
- Three-axis decomposition provides genuine diagnostic value, enabling targeted remediation rather than binary degrade/OK decisions.
- The differentiable shape term seamlessly bridges diagnosis (post-hoc) and prevention (training-time regularization) within the same mathematical framework.
- Comprehensive evaluation spanning multiple model families, quantization backends, and benchmarks.

## Limitations

- Bound tightness for absolute risk estimation is not established; calibrated only for ranking.
- Weak predictiveness on GSM8K (rs ≈ 0.41–0.48) limits utility for reasoning-heavy evaluations.
- Assumes near-isometric backbones; applicability to models with very different architectures is untested.
- Teacher-forced feature collection may not fully capture autoregressive drift in free-running generation.
- Does not cover full-parameter SFT or distillation scenarios where both backbone and head diverge substantially.
- Shape regularizer evaluation is limited to two tasks (TruthfulQA, BBQ) and two model families (Llama, Qwen3).

## Relevance

PRISM 与多个研究方向高度相关：它直接利用**表示几何**（representation geometry）来量化 LLM 变体的退化，将**量化误差**的机制分解为可解释的几何轴，并为**训练可靠性**提供了基于形状保持的正则化方法——这完美契合对模型可靠性和数学基础的关注。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does PRISM's bound behave under mixed-precision training scenarios where both scale and shape drift simultaneously during the training process?
2. Can the three-axis decomposition be extended to detect silent data corruption in hidden states, where bit-flip errors may manifest as localized shape distortion?
3. What happens to the Procrustes similarity Ω when comparing models across different architecture families (e.g., Llama vs. Gemma) rather than variants of the same family?
4. Could the axis-level diagnostics be used for automated protocol selection—e.g., choosing between GPTQ and GGUF based on which axis is predicted to dominate?
5. How does the bound perform on vision transformers or multimodal models where the linear head assumption still holds but the feature geometry may be less isometric?
