> **一句话总结：** 本文提出两种校准微调方法（软目标和硬目标），通过在合成数学分布提示上微调语言模型，显著提升了模型按指定概率分布采样的能力，且增益可迁移至开放式随机生成和创意任务，但会部分损害算术推理等下游能力。

> **TL;DR:** The authors show that probabilistic calibration—getting LLMs to sample from specified distributions—is a trainable capability. Two fine-tuning methods (soft-target via trie-derived token supervision and hard-target via sampled completions) both dramatically improve structured distribution sampling across 12 models, with the soft-target variant better at broader stochastic transfer and the hard-target variant stronger on exact numeric fidelity.

## 核心贡献

- **证明概率校准是可训练的能力：** 通过在合成分布采样提示上微调，12个模型的分布采样保真度均大幅提升，且泛化至未见过的分布族和参数设置。
- **提出两种校准微调策略：** 软目标方法将目标分布转化为前缀树（trie）驱动的逐 token 概率监督；硬目标方法直接用从目标分布采样的完成文本进行标准交叉熵训练。
- **揭示两种方法的不同特性：** 硬目标微调在结构化数值采样上最强；软目标微调在开放式随机生成、MCQ答案位置均衡和 NoveltyBench 上表现更好。
- **系统评估了迁移与保持的权衡：** 增益可迁移至自然语言随机生成场景，但会部分损害下游能力（尤其是 GSM8K 算术推理），代价因模型而异。
- **覆盖 12 个模型、四个模型家族（Qwen3、Gemma-3、Llama-3.2、GPT-OSS），参数量从 0.6B 到 27B。**

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that probabilistic calibration is a **trainable capability** in LLMs: both fine-tuning methods sharply reduce Wasserstein-1 distance and logit KL on held-out distribution families and unseen parameter settings across all 12 models evaluated.
- Introduces **soft-target fine-tuning** using trie-derived next-token probability targets (dense prefix-level supervision via KL divergence) and **hard-target fine-tuning** using sampled completions from the target distribution (sparse sampled-path supervision via cross-entropy).
- Shows that hard-target fine-tuning is strongest on **exact numeric sampling fidelity**, while soft-target fine-tuning excels at **broader stochastic transfer** including open-ended random generation (10–100x support size increase), MCQ answer-position balancing, and NoveltyBench utility.
- Provides comprehensive evaluation across six axes: structured sampling, open-ended generation, NoveltyBench, MCQ balance, capability retention (TinyBenchmarks), and PALOMA perplexity.
- Uses frozen-base **LoRA adapters** (rank 16) for all experiments, showing calibration can be added modularly without modifying the base model.

## Methodology

The paper frames probabilistic calibration as learning both the valid output space and the probability mass allocation across it. The training pipeline works as follows:

**Task setup:** Models are trained on synthetic prompts requesting a random number from a specified mathematical distribution (e.g., "Generate exactly ONE random number from a Normal distribution with parameters μ=0, σ=1"). The continuous or discrete target law is discretized into a finite canonical output space Y with five-decimal precision.

**Soft-target fine-tuning:** All valid outputs are inserted into a prefix trie. At each trie prefix, the remaining probability mass over valid continuations is normalized to produce a target next-token distribution q(v|p). The model's next-token distribution π_θ(v|p) is trained to match this target via KL divergence, averaged along sampled paths through the trie.

**Hard-target fine-tuning:** Completions are sampled from the target distribution and the model is trained with standard masked autoregressive cross-entropy. Each prompt is repeated with independently resampled completions (16 per prompt per epoch) to compensate for sparser supervision.

Both methods use LoRA adapters (rank 16, alpha 32) on Q/K/V/O projections with frozen base weights. Soft-target trains for 3 epochs (189 steps); hard-target trains for 2 epochs with 16x more repetitions (1988 steps). Training covers 24 distribution families with 1988 prompt configurations; 6 families are held out for OOD evaluation.

## Results & Highlights

- **Structured sampling:** Family-median normalized W1 drops sharply for nearly every model on both held-out families and unseen parameters. Trie-based logit KL decreases by roughly an order of magnitude.
- **Open-ended random generation:** Soft-target increases top-90% support size by 10–100x for all 12 models. Unique output rates improve for larger models (Qwen3-14B: 10.9% → 49.1%).
- **NoveltyBench:** Soft-target is best on utility for 8/12 models. Qwen3-14B utility rises from 3.392 to 4.096.
- **MCQ balance:** Transfer is real but inconsistent across families. Soft-target improves TV distance for most Qwen checkpoints; results are mixed for Gemma and Llama.
- **Capability retention:** Mixed picture. Aggregate gp-IRT often still favors the base checkpoint, especially for smaller models. GSM8K regresses substantially under both variants. Some larger models (Qwen3-8B, Qwen3-14B, Gemma-3-27B-it) improve overall.
- **PALOMA perplexity:** Soft-target gives best perplexity for all Qwen and Gemma models, suggesting calibration fine-tuning does not merely flatten token probabilities.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate-High):** The framing of probabilistic calibration as a trainable capability is clean and well-motivated. The trie-based soft-target method is technically novel for this setting, though distributional distillation ideas have precedents. The comparison of dense vs. sparse supervision is a genuine contribution.
- **Rigor (High):** Excellent experimental design — 12 models across 4 families, held-out OOD families, unseen parameters, 6 evaluation axes covering both in-domain and transfer. The ablations in Appendix E support the chosen configurations.
- **Reproducibility (High):** Public code, LoRA-only training on 4 A100s, fully synthetic data, clear hyperparameter specifications. Should be straightforward to reproduce.
- **Significance (Moderate-High):** Addresses a real and underappreciated failure mode of LLMs. The transfer results are intriguing, suggesting the learned stochastic behavior generalizes beyond the training domain.
- **Red flags:** (1) The GSM8K regression is concerning — up to ~15 point drops for some models — and the authors' proposed mechanism (shorter generations due to no reasoning traces) is plausible but not directly verified. (2) The two configurations have different optimizer step counts (189 vs 1988), making it hard to isolate the effect of the loss objective from training budget. The authors acknowledge this but it complicates interpretation. (3) MCQ and NoveltyBench transfer are inconsistent across model families, suggesting the gains may be sensitive to base model properties rather than a universal effect.

## Strengths

- Comprehensive evaluation across 12 models and 6 axes with held-out test sets.
- Clean problem framing: probabilistic calibration as a distinct, trainable capability.
- Both methods are practical: LoRA-only, synthetic data, no inference-time overhead.
- Soft-target trie construction is an elegant way to distribute probability mass at the token level.
- Honest about tradeoffs — capability retention is reported transparently, including GSM8K degradation.

## Limitations

- Capability retention is not uniformly positive; arithmetic reasoning (GSM8K) degrades notably, especially for smaller models.
- Training configuration confounds (different step counts between soft and hard) make fair comparison difficult.
- Canonical output space is a finite approximation of continuous distributions — tail mass handling may distort heavy-tailed targets.
- Transfer to natural language stochastic tasks is selective and model-dependent, not robust across all families.
- Only evaluates single-turn sampling; does not address multi-turn or agentic settings where calibration may matter.

## Relevance

这项工作对理解语言模型的概率行为和可控生成有重要意义。软目标微调方法通过前缀树实现逐 token 概率校准，为模型输出的分布控制提供了新思路，与指令遵循、输出可控性及多样化生成等研究方向密切相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can a hybrid objective combining soft-target and hard-target supervision achieve both strong numeric fidelity and broad stochastic transfer simultaneously?
- What mechanisms does Calibration Fine-Tuning actually learn — does it modify attention patterns, output logit distributions, or tokenization behavior?
- How does the approach interact with reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) or other post-training stages?
- Would curriculum-based training (e.g., starting with simpler distributions) improve retention while preserving calibration gains?
- Can the GSM8K degradation be mitigated by mixing reasoning traces into the fine-tuning data or using retention-aware regularization?
