Routers Learn the Geometry of Their Experts: Geometric Coupling in Sparse Mixture-of-Experts

arXiv: 2605.12476

Date: 2026-05-12

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12476

一句话总结： 作者从梯度推导证明 SMoE 中 matched router 行向量 r_i 与 expert 输入侧权重（W^i_up, W^i_gate）按同一输入方向 x 累积更新，并在 1B SMoE（OLMoE 配置，64 experts、top-K=6、9 层）上实证：router 分数与 expert gate-neuron 激活相关 ρ=0.43 (p=1.2e−81)，auxiliary load-balancing loss 把 router 行向量两两余弦相似度从 µ=0.13–0.32 推高到 µ=0.57–0.63（约 3 倍塌缩），而用 parameter-free 的 online K-Means centroid router 在该 setup 下取得最低 MaxVio=0.037（vs Loss-Free 0.084、Aux-Loss 0.526），仅付出 2.6% 训练 PPL 代价。
TL;DR: The paper derives and validates a router–expert geometric coupling in Sparse MoE LLMs: by the chain rule, matched router rows r_i and expert input-side matrices W^i_up both receive gradients proportional to the same hidden state x (Eq. 7–8), so on a 1B SMoE (OLMoE-mix-0924, 50B tokens, N=64 routed experts, top-K=6, 9 layers) the per-token router score correlates with the selected expert's gate-neuron activation at Spearman ρ=0.43 (p=1.2×10⁻⁸¹); the Switch-style auxiliary load-balancing loss collapses distinct router rows' mean cosine similarity from µ=0.13/0.18/0.32 to µ=0.57/0.63/0.63 (≈3× more similar), and a parameter-free online K-Means centroid router matches the loss-free family's C4-en PPL within 2.6% while achieving the lowest load imbalance MaxVio=0.037 vs Loss-Free 0.084 and Aux-Loss 0.526.

核心贡献

  • 从反向传播的链式法则推导出 router–expert 几何耦合：对每个被路由到 expert i 的 token x，router 权重向量 r_i 的梯度 ∇_{r_i}L = γ_i·x，而 expert 输入侧 W^i_up 的每一行梯度 ∇_{u_k}L = δ_{i,k}·x^⊤——同一输入方向 x，仅标量系数不同（Eq. 7、8、20、21）。这种"匹配的 router–expert 对作为共同 token 历史的耦合累加器"是 backprop 的直接结果，而非外加约束。
  • 在 1B SMoE 上首次给出 token 级实证：router 分数 z_i 与所选 expert 的 gate-neuron 平均激活呈单调正相关（Spearman ρ=0.43, p=1.2×10⁻⁸¹），说明路由决策不是外部分配，而是被"镜像"进所选 expert 的计算。
  • 揭示 auxiliary load-balancing loss 破坏几何耦合的机制：L_balance 因 softmax 覆盖全部 N 个 expert，导致每个 token 都向所有 router 行向量注入 input-directed 梯度 ∇_{r_j}L_balance = β_j·x (β_j≠0)，使 router 行向量塌缩——off-diagonal 平均余弦相似度从 loss-free 的 µ=0.13–0.32 升至 aux-loss 的 µ=0.57–0.63，约 3 倍。
  • 设计并验证 parameter-free 的 online K-Means centroid router：每个 expert 维护一个 EMA centroid（α=0.99），按余弦相似度路由，bias 用 Wang et al. (2024) 的 sign-update 规则（γ=10⁻³）。在相同 1B setup 下 MaxVio=0.037（最低），训练 PPL 仅比 Loss-Free 高 2.6%（15.40 vs 15.01），证明几何耦合捕捉到了 router 学习的实质部分。

Key Contributions

  • A gradient-level theorem (Section 3, Appendix A): for token x routed to expert i, both the router row r_i and the rows of the expert input-side matrix W^i_up receive updates along the same direction x — ∇_{r_i}L = γ_i·x and ∇_{u_k}L = δ_{i,k}·x^⊤ — differing only in scalar coefficients, so matched router–expert pairs accumulate the same routed token history and evolve as coupled accumulators.
  • Token-level empirical evidence (Section 4, Fig. 2): in a 1B SMoE trained from scratch on OLMoE-mix-0924 for ~50B tokens (L=9 SMoE layers, hidden d=1024, N=64 routed experts, top-K=6, N_s=2 shared experts, expert width 512), per-routed-pair router score and selected expert's mean gate-neuron activation correlate at Spearman ρ=0.43 (p=1.2×10⁻⁸¹) — the routing decision is mirrored inside the expert.
  • A geometric diagnosis of auxiliary-loss degradation (Section 5, Fig. 3): the standard auxiliary load-balancing loss L_balance = N·Σ f_i·P_i injects ∇_{r_j}L_balance = β_j·x into every router row on every token regardless of selection, collapsing off-diagonal mean cosine similarity from µ=0.32/0.18/0.13 (loss-free, layers 0/4/8) to µ=0.63/0.63/0.57 (aux-loss) — roughly 3× more similar.
  • A constructive test via a parameter-free K-Means centroid router (Section 6, Eqs. 11–12): non-learnable EMA centroids (α=0.99) replace router weights, tokens routed by cosine similarity, biases updated by the loss-free sign rule (γ=10⁻³); achieves the lowest steady-state MaxVio=0.037 vs Loss-Free 0.084 and Aux-Loss 0.526, at only 2.6% training-PPL cost (15.40 vs 15.01).

Methodology

Architecture and training. The 1B SMoE follows the OLMoE-style configuration of Wang et al. (2024): L=9 SMoE layers, hidden size d=1024, N=64 routed experts, top-K=6 routing, N_s=2 shared experts, expert hidden width d_i=512, gated SwiGLU experts E_i(x) = W^i_down(σ(W^i_gate x) ⊙ W^i_up x). Router computes logits z=W_r x, selects top-K, applies gating p_i. Trained from scratch on OLMoE-mix-0924 for ~50B tokens (21k steps), AdamW (β1=0.9, β2=0.95), peak LR 10⁻³ decayed to 10⁻⁴, 1k-step warmup, batch size 2.36M tokens/step. Hardware: Nvidia H100 node or AMD MI325X node.

Gradient derivation (Section 3, Appendix A). Two chain-rule derivations establish coupling. (1) Expert side: through W^i_down and the Hadamard gate, the k-th row u_k of W^i_up receives ∇_{u_k}L = δ_{i,k}·x^⊤ where δ_{i,k} = (W^i_down)^⊤(p_i·L_y)_k · g_{i,k} absorbs upstream loss gradient, routing probability, down-projection, and gate activation — all independent of x. Crucially W^i_up is chosen (not W^i_gate) because the linear branch avoids activation-function derivatives, giving a clean input-directed update. (2) Router side: logit z_i = x^⊤r_i, so ∇_{r_i}L = (∂L/∂p_i)(∂p_i/∂z_i)·x = γ_i·x. Both gradients have the form (scalar)·x.

Coupling-failure prediction for aux loss. The Switch-style L_balance = N·Σ f_i·P_i uses an unmasked softmax P_i over all N experts, so ∂P_i/∂z_j≠0 for all i,j. Chain rule gives ∇_{r_j}L_balance = β_j·x with β_j≠0 for every token x and every expert j, regardless of whether j was selected — these are the "interference gradients" of Wang et al. (2024). Because hidden states at a layer are not zero-mean over the token stream, all router rows accumulate a shared mean component and pull together.

Coupling observability. For each routed token–expert pair at layer ℓ, record raw router score z_i^{(ℓ)}(x^{(ℓ)}) and, independently in the same forward pass, the average activation of expert i's gate neurons σ(W^i_gate x). Normalize per (layer, expert), then pool. Monotone trend ⇒ coupling visible in token-level activations, complementing the static weight-level observation of Lo et al. (2025).

K-Means router (Eqs. 11–12). Score s_i(x) = (c_i^⊤x)/(‖c_i‖·‖x‖) + b_i; centroids updated by EMA c_i ← α·c_i + (1−α)·x̄_i where x̄_i is the mean of tokens routed to expert i in the current micro-batch; biases updated by b_i ← b_i + γ·sign(τ − f_i) with τ=k/N. α=0.99, γ=10⁻³ chosen so update magnitudes match the other variants. The router has zero trainable parameters and receives zero balancing gradients.

Results & Highlights

  • Router–expert coupling (Fig. 2): per-routed-pair Spearman correlation between router score and selected expert's mean gate-neuron activation is ρ=0.43 with p-value 1.2×10⁻⁸¹, computed after per-(layer, expert) normalization and pooling over routed pairs from English Wikipedia — direct token-level evidence that the routing decision is mirrored inside the selected expert's computation.
  • Auxiliary-loss geometry collapse (Fig. 3): off-diagonal mean pairwise cosine similarity of router weight vectors rises from µ=0.32/0.18/0.13 (Loss-Free, layers 0/4/8) to µ=0.63/0.63/0.57 (Aux-Loss) — distinct router directions become ~3× more similar when the standard Switch + z-loss balancing recipe is used.
  • K-Means load imbalance (Table 1): layer-averaged MaxVio at step 21k = 0.037 (K-Means) vs 0.084 (Loss-Free) vs 0.102 (Loss-Free + Seq-Aux) vs 0.526 (Aux-Loss) — K-Means achieves the lowest steady-state load imbalance despite using no trainable routing parameters or balancing gradients.
  • Perplexity cost (Table 1): K-Means train PPL 15.40 vs Loss-Free 15.01 (Δ=+2.6%), C4-en PPL 12.09 vs 11.76, Pile PPL 21.01 vs 20.40 — a modest perplexity gap interpreted as the cost of removing router degrees of freedom beyond centroid tracking.
  • Aux-Loss worst on balance (Table 1, Fig. 4): despite being designed to balance load, the Aux-Loss variant shows MaxVio=0.526, an order of magnitude worse than the loss-free family, with a characteristic collapse-and-recover trajectory over training (Fig. 4, log-scale y-axis).
  • Centroid-tracking interpretation (Section 6 Discussion): the 2.6% PPL gap quantifies how much of the router's role is captured by centroid tracking; the authors interpret the gap as the contribution of learned router degrees of freedom beyond centroid position, motivating hybrid designs.

Critical Assessment

  • The gradient-level coupling theorem (Eq. 7, 8, 20, 21) is mathematically straightforward but conceptually productive: it converts "router and expert are jointly trained" into the specific prediction that matched directions accumulate the same input history, which is then tested at the activation level — a clean theory→experiment arc.
  • A paper-specific weakness the authors themselves flag in §8: all empirical results come from a single 1B SMoE configuration. Whether the ρ=0.43 score–activation correlation and the ~3× aux-loss cosine collapse replicate at production scale (Mixtral 8×22B, DeepSeek-V3 671B) is unverified; the magnitude of coupling could be diluted by deeper layers, larger expert counts, or different gating nonlinearities.
  • The activation-level evidence is restricted to the gate branch σ(W^i_gate x); the gradient derivation is cleanest for W^i_up (because the linear branch avoids activation derivatives), but the corresponding W^i_up activation readout is left to future work (§8), so the cleanest theoretical prediction is not the one being measured.
  • The K-Means router is explicitly framed as "a constructive test of the coupling view rather than a practical router" (§8); the 2.6% train-PPL gap on a 1B model is small in absolute terms but its scaling behaviour to larger models is unknown, and the authors do not claim the K-Means router should replace learned routing in practice.
  • The aux-loss critique is correlational: router cosine similarity rises under aux loss, but the paper does not run the controlled experiment of training with aux loss while measuring downstream specialization (e.g., expert-attribution benchmarks) to confirm that the geometric collapse is the cause rather than a co-symptom of degraded specialization.
  • Hyperparameters α=0.99 and γ=10⁻³ for the K-Means router were "chosen so the centroid and bias updates are similar in magnitude to the other three variants" (§6) — this matching criterion leaves open whether a different α would close the perplexity gap or further reduce MaxVio, so the K-Means numbers should be read as a lower-bound on what centroid-tracking can achieve.

Strengths

  • Clean gradient-level theorem with explicit chain-rule derivations in Appendix A (Eqs. 14–21) that any reader can verify, plus a notation table (Table 2) for reproducibility.
  • Triangulated evidence: theoretical coupling → token-level activation correlation → aux-loss geometric collapse → constructive K-Means router — four converging lines of support for the same mechanism.
  • Directly actionable implications for SMoE design: the paper gives a geometric reason to prefer loss-free bias balancing (Wang et al. 2024; DeepSeek-V3) over Switch-style aux loss, complementing prior empirical findings about "interference gradients."
  • Open code (https://github.com/sagearc/router-expert-geometry) and standard architecture (1B OLMoE-style) make replication cheap.
  • Honest framing of the K-Means router as a diagnostic, not a deployment candidate, with explicit acknowledgment of single-configuration limitations.

Limitations

  • Single 1B SMoE configuration; scale and architecture generalization unverified (authors' own §8 admission).
  • Activation-level evidence covers only the gate branch σ(W^i_gate x); W^i_up readout — for which the theory is cleanest — is left to future work.
  • The aux-loss→cosine-collapse→specialization-degradation chain is argued geometrically but not closed with a downstream specialization measurement.
  • K-Means router hyperparameters (α=0.99, γ=10⁻³) tuned for update-magnitude parity, not swept; its 2.6% perplexity gap may shrink or grow at scale.
  • No measurement of how router–expert coupling interacts with other documented SMoE pathologies (representation collapse, expert dominance, sequence-wise imbalance).
  • Preprint only; no peer review or venue stated.

  • Can the W^i_up activation readout (the cleanest theoretical prediction) be measured directly, and does it yield a higher ρ than the gate-branch readout reported here?

  • Could router-row cosine similarity µ be used as a real-time observability signal to switch from aux-loss to loss-free balancing mid-training when µ exceeds a threshold?
  • Would a hybrid router — learned router weights initialized from EMA centroids and then fine-tuned — close the 2.6% perplexity gap while preserving K-Means-level load balance?
