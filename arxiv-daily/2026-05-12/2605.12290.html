Targeted Neuron Modulation via Contrastive Pair Search

arXiv: 2605.12290

Date: 2026-05-12

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12290

一句话总结： 本文提出对比神经元归因（CNA）方法，仅需前向传播即可定位模型中0.1%的MLP神经元构成的拒绝电路，消融这些神经元可使指令微调模型的拒绝率下降50%以上，同时完整保持输出连贯性，并发现对齐微调将预训练已有的晚期层判别结构转化为稀疏的功能性拒绝门控。
TL;DR: Contrastive Neuron Attribution (CNA) identifies 0.1% of MLP neurons that distinguish harmful from benign prompts using only forward passes. Ablating these neurons reduces refusal rates by >50% across Llama and Qwen models (1B–72B) while preserving output coherence, unlike residual-stream methods (CAA) which degrade generation quality at high steering strengths.

核心贡献

  • 提出对比神经元归因（CNA）方法，通过对比有害与无害提示的MLP激活差异，精确定位0.1%的关键神经元，无需梯度计算或辅助训练。
  • 证明消融这0.1%的神经元可在Llama和Qwen全系模型（1B至72B参数）上将拒绝率降低50%以上，同时保持生成质量（>0.97）和MMLU精度（变化<1%）。
  • 发现基座模型已存在类似的晚期层判别结构，但微调后这些结构从内容偏移功能转变为因果性安全门控，揭示了对齐微调"转变功能而非结构"的机制。
  • 神经元级干预避免了残差流方法（如CAA）在高强度操控下的输出退化问题。

Key Contributions

  • Contrastive Neuron Attribution (CNA): A simple, gradient-free method that identifies the 0.1% of MLP neurons whose activations most distinguish harmful from benign prompts, requiring only forward passes.
  • Massive refusal reduction with preserved coherence: Ablating the discovered circuit reduces refusal rates by >50% on JBB-Behaviors across 8 instruct models (1B–72B) while maintaining generation quality above 0.96 and MMLU accuracy within 1 point of baseline.
  • Structure vs. function separation: Base models contain comparable late-layer discrimination structures, but steering these neurons only produces content shifts—not behavioral change. Fine-tuning transforms pre-existing structure into a functional refusal gate.
  • Superiority over residual-stream steering: Unlike CAA, which degrades output quality sharply at α ≥ 0.5 (6 of 8 models drop below 0.60 quality), CNA maintains stable quality across all steering strengths.

Methodology

Contrastive Discovery operates in four steps:

  1. Define positive (harmful) and negative (benign) prompt sets.
  2. Run all prompts through the model, recording MLP down-projection activations at the last token position via forward hooks.
  3. Compute per-neuron mean activation difference δ between positive and negative sets.
  4. Select the top 0.1% of neurons by absolute activation difference across all layers.

Universal Neuron Filtering removes neurons that fire regardless of prompt content (appearing in top 0.1% for ≥80% of diverse prompts).

Causal Verification multiplies each circuit neuron's activation by a scalar m: m=0 ablates, m=1 is baseline, m>1 amplifies.

Evaluated on base and instruct variants of Llama-3.2 (1B, 3B), Llama-3.1 (8B, 70B), Qwen2.5 (1.5B, 3B, 7B, 72B). Discovery uses 8 positive/negative prompts (100/100 for JBB-Behaviors evaluation). Metrics include refusal rate (keyword classifier), generation quality (1 − n-gram repetition ratio), MMLU accuracy, and StrongREJECT rubric scoring.

Results & Highlights

  • Refusal reduction: Ablating 0.1% of MLP activations reduces refusal by 35.6%–97.7% relative across all 8 instruct models. Absolute drops range from 32pp (Qwen-3B) to 85pp (Qwen-7B).
  • Quality preservation: CNA maintains generation quality >0.969 for every model at maximum steering (α=1.0), while CAA drops below 0.60 for 6 of 8 models.
  • MMLU stability: CNA preserves baseline MMLU accuracy within 1 point at all steering strengths; CAA degrades to near-zero at maximum intervention.
  • StrongREJECT scoring: Llama scores improve by 6% and Qwen scores by 31% on average after ablation (rubric-based LLM judge evaluation).
  • Base vs. instruct asymmetry: Same discovery pipeline on base models identifies neurons with comparable activation differences, but steering produces only content shifts—never refusal behavior changes.
  • Late-layer concentration: 82–87% of discrimination neurons reside in the final 2–3 layers across all architectures and tasks (refusal, capitals, SVA).
  • Low neuron overlap: Only 8–29% of individual neurons overlap between base and instruct circuits, despite identical layer-level concentration patterns—fine-tuning replaces the circuit while preserving the structural template.

Authors & Credibility

All three authors (Sam Herring, Jake Naviasky, Karan Malhotra) are from Nous Research, an organization known for open-source LLM work (Hermes models). The paper includes comprehensive experiments across 16 model variants, uses established benchmarks (JBB-Behaviors from NeurIPS 2024, StrongREJECT, MMLU), and promises open-source code. The work builds directly on and extends recent findings from Arditi et al. (2024), Rimsky et al. (2024), and Arora et al. (2026).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (Moderate): The core idea—applying contrastive comparison at the neuron level rather than the residual stream—is a straightforward but effective extension of CAA. The base vs. instruct comparison yielding the "function not structure" insight is the most novel contribution.
  • Rigor (Strong): Experiments cover 16 models across two architectures, four size scales, and multiple evaluation metrics. The base/instruct paired design elegantly isolates the effect of alignment fine-tuning.
  • Reproducibility (Strong): Method requires only forward passes with no gradients or auxiliary training. Code will be open-sourced. Hyperparameters are clearly reported (0.1% top-k, 8 discovery prompts, bfloat16).
  • Significance (High): Demonstrates that refusal mechanisms are sparse, targetable, and crystallized during fine-tuning. The finding that base models already contain the structural template has important implications for understanding how alignment works mechanistically.
  • Red flags: (1) The refusal rate metric relies on a keyword classifier, which is a noisy proxy—though StrongREJECT provides a partial cross-check. (2) Only Llama and Qwen architectures tested (both gated SiLU MLPs with GQA); generalization to MoE or other architectures is unknown. (3) The 0.1% threshold appears to be a heuristic choice without systematic ablation over k. (4) The paper studies refusal as the primary behavior; whether CNA generalizes to other contrastive behavioral pairs remains open. (5) Universal neuron filtering at the 80% threshold is not rigorously justified.

Strengths

  • Simple, gradient-free method that scales trivially to 72B models.
  • Comprehensive evaluation across model sizes, architectures, and metrics.
  • Clean experimental design isolating fine-tuning effects via base/instruct pairs.
  • Strong practical advantage: avoids the coherence degradation that plagues residual-stream methods.
  • The "structure vs. function" finding provides a clear mechanistic narrative for how alignment fine-tuning works.

Limitations

  • Limited to two architecture families (Llama, Qwen) with similar MLP designs; unclear if findings transfer to MoE, non-gated MLPs, or non-transformer architectures.
  • Keyword-based refusal detection is a coarse metric; more sophisticated judges could change absolute numbers.
  • The 0.1% selection threshold is fixed rather than systematically optimized.
  • Does not address whether adversaries could reconstruct the refusal circuit without access to model internals (practical attack surface).
  • Layer-by-layer localization analysis is limited to two small models (1B, 3B); larger models lack per-layer breakdown.
  • The method requires access to model internals (activation hooks), limiting applicability to API-only settings.

Relevance

这项工作直接研究了LLM安全对齐的机械可解释性基础，揭示了拒绝行为的稀疏神经元回路机制。对于理解对齐微调如何将预训练结构转化为功能性安全门控，以及对设计更鲁棒的安全机制具有重要参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Would CNA identify similarly sparse and effective circuits for other safety-relevant behaviors (e.g., sycophancy, deception, biased outputs)?
  • Can the refusal circuit's sparsity be exploited to build more robust defenses—e.g., by distributing the safety gate across more neurons or layers?
  • How does the circuit change under different alignment methods (RLHF vs. DPO vs. constitutional AI)?
  • Does the late-layer concentration pattern hold for reasoning models with chain-of-thought, where intermediate layers may carry more structured information?
  • Could adversarial training specifically target the 0.1% circuit neurons to build resistance against ablation attacks?
