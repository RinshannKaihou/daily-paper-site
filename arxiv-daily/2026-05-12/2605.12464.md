> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 ScaleSearch，通过搜索 NVFP4 分块浮点格式中邻近的可表示尺度因子来最小化量化误差，并将该方法扩展为 ScaleSearchAttention——一种端到端 NVFP4 精度的注意力算法，在 PTQ 和注意力推理中显著减少精度损失。

> **TL;DR:** The standard max-magnitude block scale in NVFP4 quantization is suboptimal. ScaleSearch searches nearby representable E4M3 scale values to minimize per-block MSE, reducing quantization error by 27% on synthetic data and improving PTQ accuracy by up to 15 points (MATH-500, Qwen3-8B). The authors further propose ScaleSearchAttention, which combines ScaleSearch with incoherence processing and attention-sink-aware mixed-precision caching to enable fully NVFP4-native attention (including KV cache at 4.5 bits) with near-zero accuracy degradation—improving Wikitext-2 PPL by up to 0.77 points for Llama 3.1 70B over FP4 baselines.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 ScaleSearch 算法：** 不使用传统的基于最大绝对值的块尺度，而是在 NVFP4 的 E4M3 浮点尺度空间中搜索邻近的可表示值，以最小化每个微块（16个元素）的量化均方误差（MSE）。
- **揭示标准尺度选择的次优性：** 论文从理论和实验上证明，最大幅度尺度并非最优——在合成高斯数据上，搜索最优尺度可将 MSE 降低约 25-27%。
- **提出 ScaleSearchAttention：** 将 ScaleSearch 与不相关性处理（Incoherence Processing）、幅度缩减变换、以及注意力汇点感知的混合精度 KV 缓存结合，实现端到端 NVFP4 原生注意力计算，无需反量化步骤。
- **实现 NVFP4 原生 KV 缓存压缩：** KV 缓存以 4.5 比特 NVFP4 格式存储，大幅减少内存占用。
- **广泛的实验验证：** 在语言模型 PTQ、扩散模型推理和因果语言建模三个场景中验证了方法的有效性。

## Key Contributions

- **ScaleSearch algorithm for BFP scale selection:** Instead of using the standard max-abs-value block scale, ScaleSearch exhaustively searches a small range of neighboring E4M3 representable scales (offset range [-2, +6]) to minimize per-microblock quantization MSE. The key insight is that the mantissa bits in NVFP4's FP8 scale format create exploitable granularity.
- **ScaleSearchAttention for end-to-end NVFP4 attention:** A co-designed attention mechanism where Q, K, V, and P matrices are all quantized to NVFP4 and multiplied directly on Tensor Cores without any dequantization. Integrates incoherence processing (Hadamard transforms) with magnitude-reducing linear transformations and attention-sink-aware mixed-precision caching.
- **Demonstrates that standard max-scaling is significantly suboptimal:** Bimodal offset distribution analysis shows the optimal scale frequently uses offset +4 (corresponding to ~1.5x the default scale), not the default offset 0.
- **Minimal overhead:** ScaleSearch adds only 1.74x overhead during FP32→NVFP4 quantization and achieves 98.3% of SageAttention3's attention throughput.

## Methodology

**ScaleSearch (Algorithm 1):** Given a micro-block of 16 values, compute the standard max-abs scale `s`, then reinterpret it as an int8 and search offsets `f` in range [fmin, fmax]. For each candidate scale `s(f)`, quantize all values, dequantize, and compute MSE. Return the scale that minimizes MSE. Empirically, the offset distribution is bimodal (peaks at 0 and 4-5), so a narrow search range [-2, +6] suffices—only 9 candidates.

**ScaleSearchAttention pipeline:**
1. **Incoherence Processing + Magnitude Reduction:** Apply Hadamard transform to Q and K, plus learnable linear transformations R that preserve attention scores (QK^T = Q'R'^T · K'R) while reducing squared magnitudes.
2. **ScaleSearch quantization:** Apply ScaleSearch to quantize Q, K, V, P to NVFP4 along the reduction dimension.
3. **Mixed-precision KV cache:** First block and last (incomplete) block stored in full precision; all intermediate blocks in NVFP4. Constant O(B) memory overhead independent of sequence length.
4. **NVFP4 Tensor Core matmul:** Both QK^T and PV computed directly in NVFP4 with FP32 accumulation—no dequantization anywhere.

**Experimental setup:** PTQ evaluation on DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B and Qwen3-8B using TensorRT-ModelOptimizer. Attention evaluation on Llama 3.1 8B/70B, Qwen3 4B/8B. Diffusion evaluation on Mochi and CogVideoX-2B with SageAttention3 integration. Simulated quantization framework in PyTorch for attention experiments.

## Results & Highlights

- **PTQ (Table 1):** ScaleSearch improves MATH-500 from 73.1 to 88.1 (+15 pts) on Qwen3-8B; GPQA from 42.4 to 49.9 (+7.5 pts). Closes most of the gap between unquantized baseline and NVFP4.
- **Synthetic MSE (Figure 3):** 27% MSE reduction for NVFP4 on unit Gaussian data; up to 80% reduction for some hypothetical format configurations.
- **Diffusion (Table 2):** Mochi VQA-t improved from 55.74 to 70.36 (+14.6 pts) over SageAttention3; CogVideoX FScore from 5.12 to 5.40.
- **Attention PPL (Table 3):** ScaleSearchAttention achieves 2.6348 PPL on Llama 3.1 70B vs. 3.4000 for Naive-FP4 and 3.3441 for SageAttention3 (22% reduction over Naive-FP4).
- **GPQA Diamond (Table 4):** ScaleSearchAttention matches full precision (32.32 vs. 31.81) on Llama 3.1 8B Instruct, while SageAttention3 drops to 26.26.
- **Ablation (Table 5):** Mixed-precision KV cache is the most critical component (PPL 5.5768 without it vs. 5.4977 full). ScaleSearch itself contributes a meaningful 0.005 PPL improvement.
- **Overhead (Table 6):** 1.74x quantization overhead for the full [-2, 6] search range; 98.3% of SageAttention3 throughput at 32K sequence length.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: High.** The core insight—that standard max-abs scaling is suboptimal for BFP formats with floating-point scales, and that a simple local search over nearby representable scales can yield substantial MSE reductions—is elegant and, surprisingly, novel. The bimodal offset distribution analysis provides good theoretical grounding.

**Rigor: Good with minor gaps.** The synthetic validation is thorough, and the ablation study cleanly isolates each component's contribution. However:
- The attention experiments use *simulated quantization* in PyTorch rather than real NVFP4 hardware execution, which may not fully capture hardware rounding behaviors or timing.
- SageAttention3 is noted as "numerically unstable when used in a causal setting" (line 771)—the authors simulate its algorithm instead. This is a fair workaround but raises questions about whether SA3 is the right baseline.
- Only two model families (Llama 3.1, Qwen3) are tested for PTQ; broader coverage would strengthen claims.

**Reproducibility: Moderate.** No code is released with the paper. The algorithm is clearly described (Algorithm 1), but the full ScaleSearchAttention implementation depends on NVFP4 Tensor Core instructions available only on NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs. The simulated quantization framework details are insufficient for exact replication.

**Significance: High.** This is likely to become a standard technique for NVFP4 quantization given its simplicity, low overhead, and consistent improvements. As Blackwell GPUs become more prevalent, ScaleSearch addresses a real and immediate practical need.

**Red flags:**
- The "up to 15 points" MATH-500 improvement is cherry-picked as the single largest gap; most improvements are smaller (1-5 points).
- The PPL improvements on smaller models (Llama 3.1 8B: 5.48→5.50, a degradation) are underwhelming—the method shines primarily on larger models.
- No comparison with non-FP4 quantization methods (e.g., INT4 methods like GPTQ/AWQ) to contextualize the accuracy-compression Pareto frontier.

## Strengths

- Simple, elegant core idea that is easy to understand and implement.
- Strong theoretical motivation via the bimodal offset distribution analysis.
- Consistent improvements across three distinct application domains (PTQ, attention, diffusion).
- Minimal overhead (1.74x quantization, ~98% attention throughput retention).
- Good ablation study cleanly decomposing each component's contribution.
- Addresses a timely problem as NVFP4/Blackwell adoption grows.

## Limitations

- Only applicable to BFP formats with floating-point scale factors (primarily NVFP4); limited benefit for MXFP4 (only 8% MSE reduction).
- Attention experiments use simulated quantization, not real hardware execution.
- Performance gains on smaller models are marginal or nonexistent.
- No released code or planned open-source timeline mentioned.
- Requires Blackwell GPUs for actual hardware acceleration; the method's practical impact is tied to hardware adoption.
- The search range [-2, +6] is empirically chosen and may not generalize to all distributions.

## Relevance

高度相关。本文直接研究 NVFP4 分块浮点量化中的尺度选择误差最小化问题，属于"计算可靠性与精度误差"研究范畴。ScaleSearch 揭示了标准 max-scaling 的系统性次优性，为理解低精度量化的数值行为提供了新视角，与量化误差分析和浮点精度研究直接契合。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does ScaleSearch interact with quantization-aware training (QAT) workflows, where scale selection could be jointly optimized during training?
- Does the empirically-chosen search range [-2, +6] hold for emerging formats beyond NVFP4, or for different model architectures (e.g., MoE models with more extreme outlier distributions)?
- Can ScaleSearch be extended to dynamic quantization during autoregressive generation, where scales must be computed online per-token?
- What is the interaction between ScaleSearch and per-channel/group scaling in PTQ methods like AWQ—can they be combined orthogonally?
- How sensitive are the results to the choice of block size B for mixed-precision KV caching in the attention-sink-aware scheme?
