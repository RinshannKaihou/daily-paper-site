Understanding and Preventing Entropy Collapse in RLVR with On-Policy Entropy Flow Optimization

arXiv: 2605.11491

Date: 2026-05-12

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11491

一句话总结： 本文从token级别熵流（entropy flow）视角分析RLVR训练中的熵坍塌问题，发现熵减token始终压倒熵增token导致失衡，据此提出OPEFO方法——一种自适应平衡熵增/熵减梯度的严格同策略优化机制，在6个数学推理benchmark上取得最优平均性能（Qwen2.5-Math-7B上52.4%，领先第二名1.7%）。
TL;DR: This paper analyzes entropy collapse in RLVR through a token-level entropy flow lens, revealing a persistent imbalance where entropy-decreasing updates dominate. The proposed OPEFO adaptively rescales entropy-increasing and entropy-decreasing gradient components using a closed-form balancing coefficient λ*, remaining strictly on-policy and achieving the best average accuracy (52.4% on Qwen2.5-Math-7B, 51.9% on Qwen3-4B) across six mathematical reasoning benchmarks.

核心贡献

  • 熵流视角的新解释：将RLVR中的熵坍塌问题分解为token级别的熵变化，发现熵减token的数量和贡献始终压倒熵增token，形成严重失衡的熵流——这为GRPO、Clip-higher等现有方法的熵坍塌现象提供了统一解释。
  • 提出OPEFO方法：基于零熵流准则（zero entropy flow criterion），设计了一种自适应平衡机制，通过闭式解计算平衡系数λ*，对熵增和熵减token的梯度分别进行放大和衰减，严格保持同策略（on-policy）特性，不依赖参考策略。
  • 实验验证全面：在Qwen2.5-Math-7B和Qwen3-4B-Base两个基座模型、6个数学推理benchmark上，OPEFO均取得最优平均性能，且训练过程中熵动态最为稳定。Pass@k分析表明OPEFO探索了更多样化的推理路径。

Key Contributions

  • Token-level entropy flow analysis: Decomposes entropy collapse into per-token entropy changes, showing entropy-decreasing tokens consistently outweigh entropy-increasing ones, providing a unified explanation for entropy collapse across existing RLVR algorithms (GRPO, Clip-higher, etc.).
  • OPEFO (On-Policy Entropy Flow Optimization): Proposes an adaptive entropy flow balancing mechanism with a closed-form coefficient λ* = (Σ|ΔH⁻| - ΣΔH⁺) / (Σ|ΔH⁻| + ΣΔH⁺), derived from a zero entropy flow criterion at batch level. Remains strictly on-policy — no reference policy or importance sampling ratios needed.
  • Strong empirical results: Achieves best average accuracy on Qwen2.5-Math-7B (52.4%, +1.7% over 2nd best) and Qwen3-4B-Base (51.9%, +1.8%). Maintains the most stable entropy dynamics. Pass@k scaling analysis confirms broader exploration of valid reasoning paths.
  • Minimal implementation overhead: OPEFO requires only a few lines of modification to standard GRPO code — compute ΔHt, group tokens by sign, compute λ*, reweight gradients. No additional hyperparameter tuning needed.

Methodology

The paper builds on GRPO-style RLVR and introduces three key technical components:

  1. Entropy flow decomposition: Under a tabular softmax assumption, the first-order entropy change for each token is ΔHt = -η E[At(1-π(a|st))²(log π(a|st) + H(π(·|st)))]. Tokens are partitioned into S+ (ΔHt > 0, entropy-increasing) and S- (ΔHt < 0, entropy-decreasing) sets.
  1. Balanced on-policy objective: The gradient is reweighted by a balancing coefficient λ ∈ (-1, 1): entropy-increasing tokens receive weight (1+λ) and entropy-decreasing tokens receive weight (1-λ), preserving the original optimization direction without auxiliary entropy bonuses.
  1. Adaptive λ\ computation: λ is derived from the zero entropy flow criterion (1+λ)ΣS+ ΔHt + (1-λ)ΣS- ΔHt ≈ 0, yielding a closed-form solution computed from current batch statistics. The coefficient exhibits non-stationary but bounded oscillations during training, adapting to different entropy balancing needs across phases.

The entire mechanism is compatible with strict on-policy training (one update per rollout), avoiding the stale reference policies used by clipping-based heuristics.

  • Main results (Table 1): OPEFO achieves 52.4% average on Qwen2.5-Math-7B (vs. 50.8% for GRPO strict on-policy, 49.8% for GRPO, 50.3% for Clip-higher) and 51.9% on Qwen3-4B-Base.
  • Entropy stability: OPEFO maintains the most stable entropy dynamics among all baselines, avoiding both collapse and excessive entropy growth seen with entropy regularization.
  • Pass@k exploration (Tables 3-4): OPEFO achieves highest Pass@k across all k values on AIME24 (e.g., Pass@64: 68.4% vs. 66.5% for GRPO strict), with stronger gains as k increases, indicating broader exploration.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-to-high. The entropy flow decomposition perspective is novel and provides genuine mechanistic insight. However, the first-order approximation relies on a strong tabular softmax assumption (Assumption 1), which abstracts away token dependencies in real transformers. The zero entropy flow criterion is a sufficient condition, not proven globally optimal.
  • Rigor: The theoretical derivation of ΔHt and λ* is clean and self-contained. Empirical analysis of GRPO and Clip-higher entropy dynamics is thorough. However, the paper does not analyze whether the first-order approximation degrades in practice for large models or long sequences.
  • Reproducibility: Good — code is promised, hyperparameters and training details are fully specified (learning rates, batch sizes, evaluation protocols). The method itself is only ~10 lines of code.
  • Significance: Relevant to the ongoing RLVR entropy collapse problem. The on-policy constraint and adaptive balancing are practical contributions. However, evaluation is limited to math reasoning with verifiable rewards; generalization to other domains (coding, dense rewards) is untested.
  • Red flags: (1) The gains over strong baselines are moderate (1.7-1.8% average), though consistent. (2) Only two base models tested (both from Qwen family). (3) The paper acknowledges the tabular softmax assumption is a simplification. (4) Best-checkpoint selection based on average accuracy across benchmarks introduces some selection bias.

Strengths

  • Provides a principled and interpretable explanation of entropy collapse through token-level entropy flow analysis.
  • OPEFO is simple to implement, requires no new hyperparameters, and introduces negligible overhead.
  • Strictly on-policy — theoretically more grounded than reference-policy-dependent methods like Clip-higher.
  • Consistent improvements across two models and six benchmarks, with strong Pass@k scaling behavior.
  • Honest limitations section acknowledging first-order approximation and domain-specific evaluation.

Limitations

  • The first-order entropy change approximation under a tabular softmax assumption may not capture higher-order interactions in large transformers.
  • Evaluation is limited to mathematical reasoning with binary verifiable rewards; applicability to dense rewards, coding tasks, or multi-turn settings is unknown.
  • The zero entropy flow criterion is a batch-level heuristic — not proven to be globally optimal, and entropy flow behaviors may vary across tasks.
  • Only Qwen-family models are tested; cross-architecture generalization (e.g., LLaMA, Mistral) is not demonstrated.
  • Best-checkpoint selection introduces potential selection bias in reported numbers.

Relevance

本文直接研究了RLVR训练中策略熵坍塌这一核心训练稳定性问题，提出的token级熵流分析框架和自适应平衡机制对理解与改进大模型推理训练具有直接参考价值。OPEFO作为一种轻量、即插即用的梯度重加权方案，可无缝集成到现有GRPO训练流程中。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the first-order entropy change approximation scale with model size (e.g., 32B, 72B) and sequence length? Does the tabular softmax assumption become more or less accurate?
  • Can OPEFO be extended to dense reward settings (e.g., code generation with test-case-level rewards) where advantage signals are more fine-grained?
  • How does OPEFO interact with other entropy-stabilizing techniques like KL penalties or DAPO-style dynamic sampling? Are they complementary or redundant?
  • What happens when OPEFO is applied to non-reasoning tasks (e.g., instruction following, alignment)? Does zero entropy flow remain a good criterion?
  • Could the adaptive λ* be integrated into the advantage estimation itself rather than applied as a post-hoc gradient rescaling, potentially yielding tighter theoretical guarantees?
