SOAR: Scale Optimization for Accurate Reconstruction in NVFP4 Quantization

arXiv: 2605.12245

Date: 2026-05-12

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12245

一句话总结： SOAR 提出了一种基于闭式联合尺度优化(CJSO)和解耦尺度搜索(DSS)的 NVFP4 量化框架，通过联合优化全局和块级缩放因子并将量化与反量化的缩放解耦，在多个 LLM 上持续超越现有 NVFP4 量化基线，且无额外硬件开销。
TL;DR: SOAR is a calibration-free post-training quantization framework for NVFP4 that jointly optimizes global and block-wise scales via closed-form solutions (CJSO) and decouples quantization/dequantization scales through discrete search (DSS). It consistently outperforms 4over6 and RaZeR across LLaMA-3.x and Qwen3 models (e.g., +1.93 avg on Qwen3-8B over vanilla NVFP4), adds no memory overhead, and generalizes to MXFP4.

核心贡献

  • 提出了闭式联合尺度优化 (CJSO)，通过对重建误差求导推导出全局缩放因子 α 和块级缩放因子 Δ 的解析解，实现了两者的交替联合优化。
  • 提出了解耦尺度搜索 (DSS)，将量化过程中的高精度缩放因子 Δq 与硬件约束的反量化缩放因子 Δd 解耦，通过离散搜索找到最优组合，缓解 FP8 精度限制带来的误差。
  • 整体框架 SOAR 无需校准数据，量化速度在 40 分钟内完成（8B 模型），不引入额外存储开销，且可与 GPTQ 等校准方法无缝结合。
  • 方法可泛化至 MXFP4 格式，在多个模型上同样获得一致提升。

Key Contributions

  • Closed-form Joint Scale Optimization (CJSO): Derives analytical updates for both the tensor-wide FP32 global scale and FP8 block-wise scales by minimizing reconstruction error, enabling iterative coordinate-wise optimization without gradient computation.
  • Decoupled Scale Search (DSS): Introduces separate high-precision quantization scales (Δq) and hardware-constrained E4M3 dequantization scales (Δd), performing joint discrete search over candidate pairs to mitigate FP8 quantization error on block scales.
  • Calibration-free and efficient: The full SOAR pipeline requires no calibration data, completes within 40 minutes for 8B models, and introduces zero additional memory footprint at inference.
  • Cross-format generalization: DSS alone improves MXFP4 quantization across LLaMA and Qwen families, demonstrating the generality of the decoupled scale formulation.

Methodology

SOAR operates within the NVFP4 format, which uses a two-level scaling mechanism: an FP32 global scale α and FP8 (E4M3) block-wise scales Δ applied every 16 elements.

CJSO formulates the reconstruction error minimization objective L = Σ‖Wᵢ − Qᵢ·(αΔᵢ)‖². Under fixed FP4 assignments Q, this becomes quadratic in the scaling factors. Setting ∂L/∂α = 0 and ∂L/∂Δᵢ = 0 yields closed-form optimal updates: α = Σ(W·Q·Δ)/Σ(Q²·Δ²) and Δᵢ = Σ(W·Q·α)/Σ(Q²·α²). These are iterated with Q recomputed after each scale update. The block-wise scale is then projected to the nearest FP8 (E4M3) value.

DSS addresses the precision loss from projecting block scales to FP8. It decouples the scaling into a full-precision quantization scale Δq (controls FP4 assignment during quantization, not stored) and an FP8 dequantization scale Δd (hardware-compliant, stored). For each block, DSS searches over Δd candidates (two nearest E4M3-representable neighbors of the CJSO solution) and Δq candidates (multiplicative perturbations in [0.5, 1.5] with step 0.01), selecting the pair minimizing reconstruction error.

SOAR framework alternates CJSO and DSS for up to 15 iterations with early stopping (relative MSE improvement < 10⁻³). CJSO provides a strong analytical prior; DSS refines locally around it.

Results & Highlights

  • Zero-shot commonsense reasoning: On Qwen3-8B, SOAR achieves 70.68 avg accuracy (5 tasks) vs. 68.75 (NVFP4), 69.15 (4over6), 70.12 (RaZeR). On LLaMA3.2-3B, 66.00 vs. 65.02 (NVFP4) and 65.19 (RaZeR).
  • Reasoning tasks: Qwen3-8B MMLU improves by +0.77, GSM8K by +3.26 over NVFP4 baseline. On LLaMA3.2-3B GSM8K, SOAR achieves 73.62 vs. 72.93 (RaZeR).
  • GPTQ integration: SOAR+GPTQ consistently improves over standalone GPTQ (e.g., LLaMA3.1-8B avg: 73.18 vs. 72.95) with nearly identical perplexity.
  • Weight-only (W4A16): SOAR also outperforms baselines (e.g., Qwen3-8B: 71.98 avg vs. 71.18 RaZeR).
  • MXFP4 generalization: DSS alone improves MXFP4 on LLaMA3.2-3B by +0.86 avg, LLaMA3.1-8B by +1.02 avg.
  • Memory: Identical to standard NVFP4 (Δq is not stored); e.g., Qwen-32B compressed to 10.55 GB (3.17× reduction).
  • Ablation: Both CJSO and DSS contribute positively; combined SOAR achieves best results. 15 iterations with early stopping is optimal.

Authors & Credibility

The authors are from institutions with strong quantization research backgrounds (Yulun Zhang's group has prior work on PT²-LLM, D2Quant, ARB-LLM accepted at ICLR 2026). The paper provides complete mathematical derivations in the appendix, detailed pseudocode, and comprehensive experiments across multiple model families. Code is promised at GitHub.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (Moderate-High): The decoupling of quantization and dequantization scales is a genuinely clever insight—exploiting the asymmetry that only the dequantization scale needs hardware compliance. CJSO's closed-form derivation is clean but builds on standard coordinate descent.
  • Rigor (Moderate-High): Mathematical derivations are sound and verified by convergence plots (Fig. 4). Ablations are thorough (component-wise, iteration count, MXFP4 transfer, weight-only setting). However, the search space for DSS is hand-tuned ([0.5, 1.5] range, 0.01 step) without sensitivity analysis.
  • Reproducibility (Moderate): Code is promised but not yet available. The algorithm description is detailed with pseudocode, but the DSS search hyperparameters are somewhat arbitrary and may require tuning per model.
  • Significance (Moderate): Gains over RaZeR are consistent but modest (+0.2 to +0.8 avg on most models). The Qwen3-8B result (+1.93 over NVFP4 baseline) is more impressive, but this compares against the naive baseline rather than the strongest competitor. On some individual tasks, SOAR underperforms RaZeR or 4over6 (e.g., PIQA on LLaMA3.1-8B: 79.71 vs. 80.36 RaZeR).
  • Red flags: The paper only benchmarks against two NVFP4 baselines (4over6, RaZeR) and does not compare with MRGPTQ (ICLR 2026) or other concurrent microscaling methods like BatQuant or ARCQuant in the main tables. The W4A4 activation quantization uses standard NVFP4 for all methods except RaZeR (which uses its own activation quantization), making comparisons slightly asymmetric. Perplexity results with SOAR+GPTQ are marginally worse than GPTQ alone on some models (e.g., LLaMA3.2-1B Wiki2: 14.21 vs. 14.17), which is not discussed.

Strengths

  • Elegant and principled formulation: closed-form analytical solutions rather than heuristic rules.
  • The decoupled scale insight is novel and potentially impactful beyond NVFP4.
  • Zero additional memory overhead—Δq is only used during optimization, not stored.
  • Strong compatibility with calibration-based methods (GPTQ).
  • Generalizes to MXFP4, suggesting broader applicability.
  • Comprehensive dialogue examples showing qualitative improvements.

Limitations

  • Gains over the strongest baseline (RaZeR) are often small (< 1 point avg on several models).
  • DSS introduces a grid search (101 candidates per block for Δq × 2 for Δd), which adds quantization time—up to ~36 minutes for 8B models.
  • No comparison with several concurrent ICLR 2026 microscaling methods in main results.
  • The method is limited to weight quantization optimization; activation quantization is not addressed.
  • Sensitivity to DSS search range and step size is not analyzed.
  • Perplexity does not always improve; on some settings it slightly degrades despite accuracy gains.

Relevance

SOAR 直接针对 NVIDIA Blackwell 架构的 NVFP4 原生格式进行量化优化，对实际 LLM 部署的内存和推理效率有直接影响。其解耦尺度搜索的思想可推广至其他微缩放格式，对关注低比特量化和高效推理的研究者具有较高参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does DSS search granularity (step size, range) affect the accuracy-efficiency tradeoff? Is there a more efficient search strategy than uniform grid?
  • Can the decoupled scale idea be extended to activation quantization within NVFP4 to further close the gap with FP16?
  • How does SOAR compare with MRGPTQ (ICLR 2026), which combines GPTQ with block-wise Hadamard rotation for FP4?
  • What is the sensitivity of CJSO convergence to initialization? Would using a learned initialization further reduce the required iterations?
  • Does the framework extend to sub-4-bit formats (e.g., FP3, INT2) with hierarchical scaling?
