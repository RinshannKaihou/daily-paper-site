Three Regimes of Context-Parametric Conflict: A Predictive Framework and Empirical Validation

arXiv: 2605.11574

Date: 2026-05-12

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11574

一句话总结： 作者提出 context–parametric 冲突的"三regime"预测框架（Regime 1 单源更新由证据连贯性主导、Regime 2 竞争整合由参数确定性主导、Regime 3 任务驱动选择由任务类型主导），用 9,970 次 API 调用跨 5 个前沿模型（Claude Sonnet 4.6, GPT-5.5, Gemini 2.5 Flash, Llama 4 Maverick, DeepSeek V3）验证：多轮 Regime 2 中 log-popularity 每升 1 单位使 context-follow 几率下降 31–40%（β=−0.38 到 −0.50，BH-FDR 校正后均 p≤.013），而仅靠任务措辞就把 context-follow 从近 100% 翻转到 6–71%。
TL;DR: The paper resolves four long-standing contradictions in the LLM knowledge-conflict literature (Longpre et al. 2021 vs. Xie et al. 2024; Augenstein et al. 2024; Chen et al. 2022; Sun et al. 2025a) by proposing a three-regime framework; on 450 PopQA items and 9,970 API calls across Claude Sonnet 4.6, GPT-5.5, Gemini 2.5 Flash, Llama 4 Maverick, and DeepSeek V3, GEE logistic regression confirms a Regime-2 certainty gradient (β=−0.38 to −0.50, all p≤.013, BH-FDR corrected) where high-certainty facts resist context up to 30.8pp more, and a Regime-3 task-framing ablation flips context-following from near-100% (CK) to 6.1–70.8% (PK), a 29.2–93.9pp swing.

核心贡献

  • 提出 context–parametric 冲突的三 regime 框架：Regime 1（单源更新，主导变量为证据连贯性/质量）、Regime 2（竞争整合，主导变量为参数确定性 parametric certainty）、Regime 3（任务驱动选择，主导变量为任务类型）。
  • 形式化区分 parametric strength（曝光频率，operationalised 为 Wikipedia 月访问量 s_pop）与 parametric uniqueness（编码一致性，operationalised 为 Wikidata 编辑频率的倒数），并在 450 个 PopQA 实体上证明二者正交（r=−0.002, p=.97, n=450）。
  • 在 5 个前沿模型上用 GEE logistic regression 验证 Regime 2 的 certainty gradient：log-popularity 系数 β=−0.38 到 −0.50（Llama −0.445、Claude −0.391、DeepSeek −0.383、GPT-5.5 −0.376、Gemini −0.503，全部 BH-FDR 校正后 p≤.013）。
  • 通过 1,000 次新 API 调用的 Regime 3 ablation 直接验证任务措辞的主导作用：CK vs PK 把 context-follow 从 ~100% 翻转到 6.1–70.8%（χ²=30.6–155.1，全 p<.001）。
  • 识别并量化 PopQA 研究中的 distractor plausibility 混杂（Llama 的 60 个 Regime 1 抗拒案例多由时间错乱的 distractor 引起），并主张 manipulation design 是冲突研究的一阶变量。

Key Contributions

  • A three-regime predictive framework (Table 1) where each regime has a different dominant predictor of context-trust: evidence coherence (R1), parametric certainty (R2), task knowledge requirement (R3); the framework resolves four canonical contradictions in the literature (Longpre vs. Xie; Augenstein; Chen et al.; Sun et al.).
  • A formal separation of parametric strength (Wikipedia monthly page views, s_pop) from parametric uniqueness (inverse Wikidata edit frequency), with empirical orthogonality demonstrated on 450 PopQA entities (r=−0.002, p=.97) and a joint GEE model (Table 5) where strength is the sole significant predictor (pooled β=−0.918, p<.001) and uniqueness is null (β=+0.020, p=.893).
  • A GEE logistic-regression validation of the Regime-2 certainty gradient across five frontier models (Llama 4 Maverick β=−0.445, Claude Sonnet 4.6 β=−0.391, DeepSeek V3 β=−0.383, GPT-5.5 β=−0.376, Gemini 2.5 Flash β=−0.503), all surviving BH-FDR correction at α=.05.
  • A Regime-3 ablation (1,000 API calls, 100 items × 5 models × 2 framings, seed 123) showing task framing alone flips context-follow from ~100% (CK) to 6.1–70.8% (PK), with χ² from 30.6 to 155.1 (all p<.001) — the largest effect in the study.
  • Identification of a distractor plausibility confound in PopQA-based studies (§4.4) and a demonstration that single-turn vs. multi-turn manipulation design shifts GPT-5.5 context-follow from 98.2% to 84.0%, reframing manipulation design as a first-order variable.

Methodology

The study runs three experimental phases totalling 9,970 API calls on five instruction-tuned frontier models: Claude Sonnet 4.6 (Anthropic API), GPT-5.5 (OpenAI API), Gemini 2.5 Flash (Google AI Studio API), Llama 4 Maverick (Meta, 17B active parameters, 128-expert MoE, DeepInfra FP8 endpoint), and DeepSeek V3 (Together AI API). All calls use temperature 0, no system prompt, and stateless sessions (except the multi-turn v2 condition). Author bore ~USD 95 for 8,970 calls plus ~USD 15 for the 1,000-call Regime 3 ablation.

Dataset: 450 items from PopQA (Mallen et al. 2023), stratified into three non-overlapping certainty tiers by Wikipedia monthly page views s_pop — low (n=150, s_pop=27–373), medium (n=150, s_pop=379–2,864), high (n=150, s_pop=2,893–492,490). Distractor entities are sampled from the same Wikidata property type with fixed seed 42; six items with o_pop/s_pop<0.01 are excluded from v2.

Four experimental passes (8,970 calls): Baseline (2,250, bare question); Regime 1 (2,250, "[CTX] [Q]"); Regime 2 v1 (2,250, single-turn "You said '[A1]'. [CTX] [Q]"); Regime 2 v2 (2,220, multi-turn where the model commits in Turn 1 and faces the conflict in Turn 2 with its own words in the assistant role). Scoring: +1 (followed context), 0 (held parametric), −1 (neither), via case-insensitive substring matching with 6-character minimum tokens.

Statistical methods: Primary analysis uses generalised estimating equations (GEE, Liang & Zeger 1986) with binomial family, exchangeable correlation, item-level clustering, and log(s_pop+1) as the continuous predictor. Robustness checks include multinomial χ² with three outcome categories, sensitivity analyses collapsing "neither" into parametric-hold (conservative) and context-follow (liberal), and Benjamini–Hochberg FDR correction across all per-model tests. Cramér's V is reported for discretised comparisons.

Regime 3 ablation (1,000 calls): 100 PopQA items (34 low, 33 medium, 33 high certainty) × 5 models × 2 framings (CK "Based ONLY on this document" vs. PK "Use your own knowledge"), seed 123. Same distractor in both conditions; only instruction framing changes.

Results & Highlights

  • Regime-2 certainty gradient (Table 4): each unit increase in log-popularity reduces context-follow odds by 31–40% across models — Llama β=−0.445 (95% CI [−0.571,−0.320], p<.001), Claude β=−0.391 ([−0.518,−0.263], p<.001), DeepSeek β=−0.383 ([−0.504,−0.262], p<.001), GPT-5.5 β=−0.376 ([−0.511,−0.241], p<.001), Gemini β=−0.503 ([−0.902,−0.104], p=.013); all survive BH-FDR.
  • Per-tier Regime-2 multi-turn context-follow (Table 7): Llama drops 0.846→0.787→0.538 (30.8pp gradient, V=0.338, p<.001); Claude 0.953→0.880→0.717 (23.6pp, V=0.265, p<.001); GPT-5.5 0.946→0.860→0.710 (23.6pp, V=0.246, p<.001); DeepSeek 0.644→0.520→0.434 (21.0pp, V=0.311, p<.001); Gemini 0.960→0.973→0.945 (1.5pp, V=0.102, non-significant gradient).
  • Manipulation design matters (Table 9): identical items shift GPT-5.5 context-follow from 98.2% (single-turn v1) to 84.0% (multi-turn v2), Δ=−0.142; Llama shifts 0.700→0.534 (Δ=−0.268); Claude 0.942→0.851 (Δ=−0.091); Gemini unchanged (+0.025). Single-turn "you previously said" prompts do not create genuine parametric competition in closed-weight models.
  • Strength vs. uniqueness orthogonality (Table 5): joint GEE pooled across five models gives strength β=−0.918 (SE 0.109, 95% CI [−1.132,−0.704], p<.001) and uniqueness β=+0.020 (SE 0.147, [−0.269,+0.309], p=.893); Pearson r=−0.002 (p=.97, n=450). Mean Wikidata edit counts are nearly flat across certainty tiers (low 68.2, medium 76.6, high 69.9).
  • Regime-3 task-framing swing (Table 10): CK vs. PK flips context-follow from 100%→6.1% (GPT-5.5, Δ=+0.939, χ²=155.1, p<.001), 100%→7.6% (Claude, Δ=+0.924, χ²=142.2, p<.001), 100%→11.8% (Gemini, Δ=+0.882, χ²=120.6, p<.001), 100%→50.6% (Llama, Δ=+0.494, χ²=60.6, p<.001), 100%→70.8% (DeepSeek, Δ=+0.292, χ²=30.6, p<.001) — the largest effect in the study.
  • Inverted Dunning-Kruger (Table 13): Llama defends correctly-answered baseline items more stubbornly than wrong ones (correct 0.566 vs. wrong 0.830 context-follow, Δ=+0.264, n=221 vs. 229, p<.001); the same direction holds in DeepSeek (+0.045, n.s.) and the closed-weight trio (all n.s.).
  • Robustness (Table 12): the certainty gradient survives multinomial χ² and all "neither" sensitivity treatments for four of five models; Gemini's gradient becomes non-significant under multinomial (V=0.09) and conservative (V=0.06) treatments.

Critical Assessment

  • The central contribution — that the four contradictions dissolve once regime membership is recognised — is well-argued and the four-by-four resolution table (Table 2) is genuinely clarifying. The framework is predictive enough to be falsifiable, which is more than can be said for purely descriptive taxonomies (Xu et al. 2024) that the paper critiques.
  • A paper-specific weakness acknowledged in §5.4: the Regime 1 evidence-quality claim is never directly tested by the authors themselves — they run only assertive synthetic sentences in their own Regime 1 experiment and infer the coherence mechanism by synthesising Longpre et al. (2021) and Xie et al. (2024). A within-study fluent-vs.-entity-substituted manipulation on the same items would close this gap but is absent.
  • The strength-vs.-uniqueness divergence from Marjanović et al. (2024) is explained post hoc as a domain artifact (PopQA's stable creative-work properties vs. DynamicQA's temporal facts), but the paper does not run the obvious confirmatory experiment on FreshQA or DynamicQA — listed only as "a priority for future work." The orthogonality claim (r=−0.002) is therefore demonstrated on a single, skewed domain.
  • Single-author, non-peer-reviewed preprint with no institutional affiliation; the statistical analysis is unusually careful for this context (GEE with item clustering, BH-FDR, multinomial robustness, Cramér's V), but the absence of any code release at submission time and the 6-character substring scoring (which the author admits produces false negatives for short surnames) mean the headline numbers are not yet independently reproducible.
  • The distractor plausibility confound is disclosed honestly but only resolved by post-hoc exclusion of all 60 of Llama's Regime-1 resistance items; this is a large fraction of the relevant signal and suggests the PopQA-based paradigm itself is noisy.

Strengths

  • Clear, falsifiable three-regime framework that genuinely resolves four specific contradictions with cited numbers (47% vs. 96%; <3.6% vs. substantial reliance; 89.2%→33.7%).
  • Strong statistical rigour for a single-author preprint: GEE with exchangeable correlation and item clustering, BH-FDR across all per-model tests, multinomial three-outcome robustness, and four-way "neither" sensitivity analysis.
  • Direct manipulation of conversation structure (single-turn vs. multi-turn v2) on identical items isolates manipulation design as a first-order variable — a methodological contribution that should change how the field designs conflict studies.
  • The Regime-3 ablation produces the cleanest and largest effect in the paper (29–94pp swings) and convincingly shows that task framing dominates over evidence quality, certainty, and manipulation strength.
  • Honest reporting of the closed-weight vs. open-weight split (closed models comply with PK instructions at 6–12%; open-weight models at 51–71%) and of the inverted Dunning-Kruger finding that contradicts the author's own prediction.

Limitations

  • Single-author preprint with no institutional affiliation, peer review, or code release at submission time.
  • PopQA property skew: the sample over-represents creative-work properties (director, genre, screenwriter); findings may not generalise to geographic, biographical, or scientific domains.
  • Regime 1 evidence-quality mechanism is argued by synthesis of prior work, not directly manipulated in the authors' own experiments.
  • Strength-vs.-uniqueness domain contingency is hypothesised but not tested on any dynamic-domain dataset (FreshQA, DynamicQA).
  • Synthetic-to-real transfer gap: Hagström et al. (2025) show synthetic conflict results do not reliably transfer to production RAG, so the controlled-condition findings may overstate real-world effect sizes.
  • 6-character-minimum substring scoring admits false negatives for short surnames; covariates (baseline correctness, property type, distractor popularity o_pop) are not included as fixed effects in the primary GEE.
  • Multi-turn manipulation uses only the model's own prior answer as the commitment device; alternative commitment structures (user-voiced, hedged, repeated reinforcement) are untested.

Relevance

直接映射到 LLM Inference Evaluation（兴趣 2）：本文系统量化了 context–parametric 冲突中模型行为的影响因素，提供可复现的评估协议（GEE + BH-FDR + multinomial robustness）和明确的 regime 分类，是 LLM 推断评测方法论工作。其关于 distractor plausibility、manipulation design 与 closed/open-weight 差异的发现也直接关系到 LLM Hidden States & Interpretability（兴趣 3） 中"参数知识 vs 上下文知识"竞争机制的实证刻画，以及 Silent Data Corruption (SDC)（兴趣 6） 的邻近话题——流畅但错误的检索文档能在高确定性事实上覆盖模型知识（如"Interstellar 由 Brian Singer 执导"会覆盖 Nolan），是 RAG 系统中一类需要警惕的语义层 corruption。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the three-regime framework replicate on dynamic-domain datasets (FreshQA, DynamicQA) where uniqueness is hypothesised to dominate over strength?
  • Can the closed-weight vs. open-weight instruction-compliance split (6–12% vs. 51–71% PK context-follow) be traced to a measurable difference in instruction-tuning data or RLHF objective, providing a mechanistic anchor for the regime framework?
  • How does the Regime-3 task-framing effect interact with adversarial prompt injections — does the "use your own knowledge" instruction survive when the distractor is embedded in a system prompt or tool output rather than user content?
  • Is the parametric-strength operationalisation (Wikipedia monthly page views) a good proxy for actual training-data exposure frequency in open-weight models whose pre-training corpora are partially known (e.g., Llama 4, DeepSeek V3)?
  • Can the distractor plausibility confound be quantified into a calibration factor that reconciles historical PopQA-based conflict studies into a single effect size?
