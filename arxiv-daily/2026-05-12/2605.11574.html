Three Regimes of Context-Parametric Conflict: A Predictive Framework and Empirical Validation

arXiv: 2605.11574

Date: 2026-05-12

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11574

⚠️ Abstract-only summary
一句话总结： 本文提出三体制框架来解释LLM在处理上下文知识与参数知识冲突时的行为差异，区分了单源更新、竞争整合和任务选择三种情境，并在五个主流模型上通过近万次API调用验证了框架的预测能力。
TL;DR: The paper resolves a persistent contradiction in prior work on LLM context-parametric conflict by identifying three distinct processing regimes, each with a different dominant predictor. Validated across five major LLMs with 9,970 API calls, the framework shows that task framing alone can flip context-following rates from near-100% to 6-71%.

核心贡献

  • 提出三体制框架（three-regime framework），将LLM处理上下文-参数冲突的行为分为三种本质不同的情境，解决了文献中长期存在的实证矛盾
  • 在框架中形式化区分了参数强度（parametric strength，暴露频率）和参数唯一性（parametric uniqueness，编码一致性），并实证证明两者正交（r = -0.002）
  • 在Claude Sonnet 4.6、GPT-5.5、Gemini 2.5 Flash、Llama 4 Maverick、DeepSeek V3五个模型上，通过三阶段实验和近万次API调用验证了框架的核心预测

Key Contributions

  • Three-regime framework that dissolves contradictory findings in prior literature by identifying three qualitatively distinct processing situations: single-source updating, competitive integration, and task-appropriate selection
  • Formalized distinction between parametric strength (exposure frequency) and parametric uniqueness (encoding consistency), demonstrated to be orthogonal dimensions (r = -0.002, p = .97)
  • Large-scale empirical validation across five frontier models with 9,970 API calls, using GEE logistic regression confirming the predicted certainty gradient (beta = -0.38 to -0.50, all p <= .013, BH-FDR corrected)
  • Task-framing ablation showing that task framing alone flips context-following from near-100% to 6-71% (all five models significant at p < .001)

Methodology

The paper employs a three-phase experimental design:

  1. Phase 1 — Regime characterization: Constructs experimental conditions that isolate each of the three regimes, manipulating evidence coherence (Regime 1), parametric certainty (Regime 2), and task framing (Regime 3).
  1. Phase 2 — Certainty gradient validation: Uses GEE logistic regression to confirm that parametric certainty predicts context-following behavior specifically in Regime 2 (competitive integration), with BH-FDR correction across all five models.
  1. Phase 3 — Regime 3 ablation: Demonstrates that merely changing the task knowledge requirement (contextual vs. parametric knowledge condition) dramatically shifts behavior, validating the framework's prediction that task framing is the dominant predictor in Regime 3.

Analyses include multinomial outcome modeling, sensitivity analyses for hedging responses, and FDR correction to ensure robustness.

Results & Highlights

  • Regime 2 certainty gradient confirmed across all five models (beta = -0.38 to -0.50, all p <= .013, BH-FDR corrected) — stronger parametric knowledge leads to less context-following
  • Regime 3 task-framing effect is dramatic: flipping from contextual to parametric knowledge condition drops context-following from near-100% to 6-71%, significant for all five models (p < .001)
  • Parametric strength vs. uniqueness are empirically orthogonal (r = -0.002, p = .97), with strength being the operative predictor in stable factual domains
  • Framework resolves literature contradiction: prior studies reporting stubborn retention (50% ignored) were likely in Regime 2 conditions, while those reporting 96% context-following were in Regime 1 or 3 conditions

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-to-high. The three-regime framing is a useful conceptual contribution that elegantly explains contradictory findings. However, the individual mechanisms (evidence coherence, parametric certainty, task framing) are not themselves new — the novelty lies in their systematic integration into a single predictive framework.
  • Rigor: High. Pre-registered statistical approach with GEE logistic regression, FDR correction, multinomial modeling, and hedging sensitivity analyses. The orthogonality demonstration between strength and uniqueness is a clean empirical result.
  • Reproducibility: Moderate. The paper uses API calls to five proprietary models (Claude, GPT, Gemini, Llama, DeepSeek). While the models are publicly accessible, exact reproducibility depends on model versioning and API stability. Prompt-level details are needed for full reproducibility.
  • Significance: Moderate-to-high. If the framework holds, it provides a principled way to predict when models will defer to context vs. retain parametric knowledge — directly relevant to RAG system design, knowledge editing, and model evaluation.
  • Red flags: Single-author paper — worth verifying the scale claims. The abstract is unusually detailed for its length, which could indicate either thoroughness or over-claiming from abstract-level information. The distinction between "strength" and "uniqueness" needs careful examination in the full text to confirm it is not an artifact of the chosen operationalization.

Limitations

  • The three-regime boundary conditions need further formalization — when exactly does a situation transition between regimes?
