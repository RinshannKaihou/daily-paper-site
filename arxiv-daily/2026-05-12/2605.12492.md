> **一句话总结：** Pion是一种新型LLM优化器，通过左右正交等价变换更新权重矩阵（保持奇异值不变），在预训练和后训练中展现出卓越的训练稳定性和竞争力，甚至可以在无归一化层的情况下完成训练。

> **TL;DR:** Pion updates weight matrices via coupled left/right orthogonal transformations that preserve singular values throughout training, yielding exceptional stability in LLM pretraining (including normalization-free and ultra-deep settings) and competitive or superior performance in finetuning and RLVR compared to AdamW and Muon.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了**Pion优化器**，通过左右正交等价变换（而非加性更新）来更新权重矩阵，在训练过程中严格保持权重矩阵的奇异值谱不变。
- 从数学上推导了Pion的更新规则，将其建立在李群优化框架之上：利用链式法则计算恒等矩阵因子在正交群上的梯度，投影到斜对称李代数后通过矩阵指数映射回到正交群。
- 证明了Pion在确定性（O(1/T)收敛率）和随机（O(1/√T)收敛率）设定下的收敛性保证。
- 系统性地探索了Pion的四个设计原则：RMS缩放的一致性更新、李代数动量、交替更新策略、以及二阶截断指数近似。
- 实验证明Pion具有**异常的训练稳定性**：能完成无归一化层（normalization-free）的预训练，在200层超深网络中表现最稳定，并在RLVR任务中取得最佳结果。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **Pion**, a spectrum-preserving optimizer that updates weight matrices via bilateral orthogonal equivalence transformations (`W_{t+1} = R_t W_t P_t`), preserving singular values throughout training without reparameterization.
- Derives the update rule from Lie group optimization principles: computing Lie algebra gradients via skew-symmetric projection of `W^T G` and `G W^T`, then mapping to the orthogonal group via matrix exponential.
- Provides convergence guarantees in both deterministic (O(1/T)) and stochastic (O(1/√T)) settings on the isospectral manifold.
- Systematically explores four design principles: RMS-controlled scale-consistent updates, Lie-algebra momentum (first and second order), alternate (single-side per step) updates, and second-order truncated exponential approximation.
- Demonstrates that Pion can successfully train **normalization-free** LLMs where both AdamW and Muon fail with NaN gradients, and shows superior stability on ultra-deep (200-layer) architectures.
- Achieves best overall performance on RLVR tasks with GRPO, and competitive stability-plasticity tradeoffs in supervised finetuning.

## Methodology

Pion's core innovation is replacing the standard additive weight update (`W = W - ηG`) with a multiplicative orthogonal equivalence transformation. The update proceeds as:

1. **Lie algebra gradient computation:** Given gradient `G_t`, project onto the Lie algebra of the orthogonal group by forming skew-symmetric matrices: `G_in = W^T G - G^T W` (input side) and `G_out = G W^T - W G^T` (output side).
2. **Momentum accumulation:** Apply first-order and second-order momentum directly in the Lie algebra (Lie+Lie variant), which is geometrically consistent since the Lie algebra is a fixed vector space.
3. **RMS scaling:** Normalize the update magnitude per weight matrix so that rotational update strengths are proportional to parameter count, ensuring scale consistency across layers: `α_t ≈ c√(d_out · d_in) / (‖ΔW/η‖_F + ε)`.
4. **Exponential mapping:** Apply a second-order truncated approximation `E_2(A,α) = I + ηαA + ½(ηαA)²` to map Lie algebra elements back to orthogonal group elements.
5. **Bilateral or alternate update:** Either apply both left and right transformations simultaneously (bilateral) or alternate between sides across iterations (alternate, more efficient).

The method preserves the singular value spectrum by construction — orthogonal transformations `R` and `P` cannot change singular values of `RWP`. The approach is inspired by POET (which reparameterizes weights as `RW_0P`) but eliminates the auxiliary parameterization, operating directly on model weights.

The paper also proves Pion's compatibility with Maximal Update Parametrization (µP): the forward spectral condition is automatically satisfied, and the update spectral condition can be met by normalizing the spectral norms of the Lie algebra generators.

## Results & Highlights

- **Pretraining (LLaMA-1.3B, 54B tokens):** Pion achieves the best average benchmark score (47.69) vs. Muon (46.34) and AdamW (44.74), with validation loss (2.7350) comparable to Muon (2.7225) and substantially better than AdamW (2.7700).
- **Normalization-free pretraining (60M):** Both AdamW and Muon fail with NaN gradients; Pion converges successfully to loss ~4.0, demonstrating that spectrum-preserving updates can partially replace architectural normalization.
- **Ultra-deep training (200-layer, 60M):** Pion is the most stable optimizer (mean std: 0.0892 vs. AdamW 0.0931, Muon 0.0927) and maintains the most uniform layer-wise Jacobian expressivity across all layers.
- **Supervised finetuning:** Pion achieves the best stability-plasticity tradeoff across Qwen2.5-1.5B and Llama-3.2-3B on MetaMathQA and Magicoder, with particular strength in code generation (best ID and OOD scores).
- **RLVR (GRPO):** Pion achieves the best overall average results on both Qwen3-1.7B (46.43%) and DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B (38.32%), outperforming AdamW and Muon on math reasoning benchmarks.
- **Training stability indicators:** Pion keeps attention logits, activation norms, and weight norms nearly flat throughout training, while AdamW shows continuously growing instability and Muon shows moderate growth.
- **Computational overhead:** Full Pion uses ~16% more memory than AdamW and ~44% more step time; the variant without second-moment buffers uses ~12% less memory than AdamW with comparable performance.

Weiyang Liu (corresponding author) has a strong track record in geometric deep learning and neural network optimization. Zeju Qiu is a co-author of POET, the direct predecessor work. The paper is a technical report from SphereLab. The work is well-grounded in prior research on orthogonal group optimization, manifold optimization, and spectral methods in deep learning. ## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** Pion introduces a genuinely new optimization paradigm — spectrum-preserving multiplicative updates via orthogonal equivalence — that is fundamentally different from both additive optimizers (Adam) and orthogonalized gradient methods (Muon). The connection to Lie group optimization and the isospectral manifold is elegant and well-motivated.
- **Rigor: Strong.** The paper provides formal convergence analysis (both deterministic and stochastic), detailed computational complexity analysis, and systematic ablation of each design choice. The derivation from POET's reparameterization to a direct optimizer is clear.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate.** Algorithm details are provided, but some hyperparameters (RMS constant c=0.2, momentum coefficients) are tuned on a 60M model and transferred. The full training recipe for larger scales is not yet validated. The code is not mentioned as available.
- **Significance: Potentially high.** The normalization-free result is particularly striking and could influence how we think about training stability. The RLVR results suggest practical impact for post-training.
- **Red flags:** (1) The largest pretraining experiment is only 1.3B parameters — a significant gap from frontier model scales. (2) Memory overhead is non-trivial (~16% more than AdamW for full Pion). (3) The paper acknowledges that the memory overhead scales poorly for highly rectangular matrices. (4) The computational cost analysis assumes large batch sizes for amortization, which may not hold in all settings. (5) The alternate update, while cheaper, performs slightly worse in SFT, suggesting the bilateral update may be needed for some tasks. (6) No comparison with other recent matrix-aware optimizers beyond Muon (e.g., SOAP, Shampoo variants).

## Strengths

- Elegant theoretical foundation connecting Lie group optimization to practical LLM training.
- The spectrum-preservation property is achieved by construction, not via explicit regularization or normalization — this is conceptually clean.
- Exceptional training stability demonstrated through multiple stress tests (normalization-free, ultra-deep).
- Consistent performance improvements across diverse settings (pretraining, SFT, RLVR).
- Thorough ablation study of each design choice with clear empirical justification.
- Proven compatibility with µP for hyperparameter transferability across model widths.

## Limitations

- Only validated up to 1.3B parameters for pretraining — scalability to 7B+ or 70B+ models remains unproven.
- Memory overhead from Lie algebra states scales as `O(d²_out + d²_in)`, which grows with the aspect ratio for rectangular matrices.
- Step time is ~44% slower than AdamW (though only ~3% slower than Muon).
- The second-order exponential approximation may not be sufficiently accurate for matrices with large spectral radius, though the paper argues this is mitigated by starting from identity.
- The relationship between spectrum preservation and generalization is assumed but not directly tested — the paper shows better benchmark scores but does not isolate whether this comes from spectrum preservation or other design choices.
- No evaluation on multimodal or non-LLM tasks to assess general applicability.

## Relevance

Pion提出了一种全新的LLM训练优化范式，通过保谱正交变换替代传统加性更新。该工作与LLM训练稳定性、高效优化器设计直接相关，其保谱特性对理解训练动态和模型泛化具有重要意义。RLVR场景中的优异表现也使其具有实际应用价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does Pion scale to 7B, 70B, or larger models? Does the spectrum-preservation property remain beneficial, or does it become overly restrictive at scale?
- Can Pion be combined with low-rank adaptation methods (e.g., LoRA) for parameter-efficient finetuning?
- What is the relationship between the preserved spectrum at initialization and the optimal spectrum for a given task? Does fixing the spectrum limit the model's representational capacity?
- How does Pion interact with different initialization schemes? Is the method sensitive to the quality of the initial spectral distribution?
- Could a hybrid approach (e.g., Pion for attention layers, AdamW for FFN) combine the stability benefits with lower memory overhead?
