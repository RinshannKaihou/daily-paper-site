Multi-Variable Conformal Prediction: Optimizing Prediction Sets without Data Splitting

arXiv: 2605.12341

Date: 2026-05-12

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12341

一句话总结： 本文提出多变量保形预测（MCP）框架，利用场景优化理论将预测集的形状设计与阈值校准统一为单个优化问题，支持向量值得分函数与多个同时校准变量，彻底消除了传统保形预测中的数据划分步骤，同时提供有限样本覆盖保证；实验在椭球和多模态预测集上表明，MCP 在达到目标覆盖率的同时，预测集体积小于或等价于基线方法，且校准方差显著降低。
TL;DR: MCP extends conformal prediction to vector-valued score functions with multiple simultaneous calibration variables, unifying prediction set design and calibration into a single scenario-optimization problem that eliminates data splitting while maintaining finite-sample coverage guarantees. Two variants — RemMCP (convex, constraint removal) and RelMCP (non-convex, constraint relaxation) — consistently meet target coverage with smaller or comparable prediction sets and substantially lower variance than split-CP baselines.

核心贡献

  • 提出多变量保形预测（MCP）框架：将保形预测从标量得分函数 + 单一阈值推广到向量值得分函数 s(x, y, q) ∈ R^{n_s} 和参数向量 q ∈ R^{n_q}，使预测集形状不再受限于固定几何结构。
  • 基于场景理论统一设计与校准：将预测集定义为 Y(x, q) = {y | s(x, y, q) ≤ 0}，通过场景优化（每个校准样本定义一个约束）联合求解最优参数，无需将数据拆分为形状拟合子集和校准子集。
  • 提出 RemMCP（约束移除）：采用级联优化逐阶段移除约束，恢复 split CP 作为特例（Corollary 1），并严格证明其预测集被包含于 Bonferroni 校正的维度独立 SCP 预测集中（Corollary 2）。
  • 提出 RelMCP（约束松弛）：支持非凸得分函数（如多模态 union-of-ellipsoids），通过惩罚参数迭代搜索在目标覆盖率与预测集大小之间取得平衡。
  • 实验验证：在车辆轨迹预测基准上，MCP 方法的覆盖方差在 T=30 时仅为 0.0039–0.0056，而 SCP-2/3 变体为 0.0066–0.0101；RelMCP-B 在多模态场景下的平均体积稳定低于 40，远优于 SCP-2B（80,139）和 SCP-3B（87,022）。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces the MCP framework that generalizes conformal calibration from a scalar nonconformity score + single threshold to a vector-valued score function s(x, y, q) with parameter vector q ∈ R^{n_q}, breaking the structural rigidity of classical prediction set shapes.
  • Unifies prediction set design and calibration into a single scenario optimization problem, eliminating the data split that reduces effective sample size in standard conformal prediction pipelines.
  • Proves that RemMCP recovers split conformal prediction as a special case (Corollary 1, with s(x,y,q) = s̃(x,y) - q, J(q) = q) and is provably less conservative than dimension-wise Bonferroni-corrected SCP (Corollary 2).
  • Develops RelMCP for non-convex score functions with an adaptive penalty-search algorithm (Alg. 1) that iteratively finds the least-conservative penalty parameter certifying target coverage.
  • Derives finite-sample expected coverage (E[η(q)] ≥ 1 - ε, Theorem 1) and high-confidence coverage (P(η(q) ≥ 1 - ε) ≥ 1 - β, Theorems 1–2) guarantees for both variants, with exact Beta-distributional results under additional regularity conditions.

Methodology

The paper bridges two previously disconnected fields: split conformal prediction (SCP) and scenario optimization theory.

Problem setup. Given a pretrained point predictor f(x) and a calibration set M_cal = {(x^(m), y^(m))}_{m=1}^{n_cal}, MCP seeks q such that the prediction set Y(x, q) = {y | s(x, y, q) ≤ 0} satisfies coverage η(q) = P(y ∈ Y(x, q*) | M_cal) ≥ 1 - ε while minimizing a cost J(q) (e.g., prediction set volume).

RemMCP (Section 3.1). Formulated as constrained scenario optimization with constraint removal:

  1. Compute outlier budget ρ = ⌊ε(n_cal + 1) / n_q⌋ - 1 (generalizing the SCP quantile index).
  2. Solve a cascade of ρ + 1 convex programs, each discarding k·n_q support constraints via the Romao et al. (2023) removal scheme.
  3. Coverage follows from scenario theory: E[η(q*)] ≥ 1 - n_q(ρ+1)/(n_cal+1) ≥ 1 - ε, with high-confidence bounds via Beta CDF.

Under additional assumptions (full support + violation condition), the coverage follows exactly η(q*) ~ Beta(n_cal - n_q(ρ+1) + 1, n_q(ρ+1)).

RelMCP (Section 3.2). Formulated as unconstrained optimization with constraint relaxation:

  1. For a grid of penalty parameters ϕ_i, solve q_i* = argmin J(q) + ϕ_i Σ ξ_m(q) where ξ_m(q) = max(0, max_j s_j(x^(m), y^(m), q)).
  2. Evaluate achievable miscoverage ε_i a posteriori from the solution complexity d_i (number of active constraints + cardinality of support set).
  3. Select the solution with the smallest ϕ_i such that ε_i ≤ ε.

The adaptive algorithm (Alg. 1) performs bisection-like search over penalty parameters, maintaining valid/invalid solution bounds.

Score/cost function design (Section 3.3, Appendix C). The paper provides concrete instantiations for intervals, ellipsoids, zonotopes, sublevel sets, and multi-modal unions — all jointly optimized without additional data splits.

Results & Highlights

Benchmark: Vehicle position prediction at an intersection (from Tumu et al., 2024), predicting T steps ahead with n_cal = 2,000 calibration points, ε = 0.05, evaluated over 100 calibration runs and 10,000 test points.

Coverage (Figure 3): All methods meet the target 1 - ε = 0.95 on average. RelMCP consistently exceeds it by a small margin due to its iterative penalty search conservatism.

Prediction set volume (Figure 3):

  • Among single-ellipsoid methods, RemMCP yields the tightest sets.
  • Among multi-modal methods, RelMCP-B is the most robust: at T=2, mean volumes of SCP-2B and SCP-3B are 80,139 and 87,022 (inflated by degenerate runs), while RelMCP-B achieves stable mean volume below 40.
  • At T=50 (long-horizon, multi-modal residuals), multi-modal methods produce substantially smaller sets than spherical SCP-1.

Variance (quantified): At T=30, coverage standard deviation ranges from 0.0066–0.0101 for SCP-2/3 vs. 0.0056 for RemMCP and 0.0039–0.0046 for RelMCP — directly predicted by the theoretical Beta distributions (Figure 4).

Computation: SCP-1/2/3 complete in <10 ms; RemMCP ~0.2 s; RelMCP-A ~0.8 s; RelMCP-B ~21 s (driven by high-dimensional non-convex optimization over union-of-ellipsoids parameters).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The MCP generalization is conceptually natural but technically non-trivial. The key insight — mapping conformal calibration to scenario optimization with constraint removal/relaxation — is elegant and opens a new design space. Prior work (zono-conformal prediction by the same first author) was a specific instantiation; this paper provides the general framework.
  • Rigor: High. All coverage guarantees are rigorously derived from established scenario theory results (Romao et al. 2023, Garatti & Campi 2025). The proofs (Appendix A) are clean and the recovery of SCP as a special case (Corollary 1) provides a valuable sanity check. The provable dominance over Bonferroni (Corollary 2) is a strong theoretical result.
  • Reproducibility: Moderate-High. Algorithms are precisely specified (including pseudocode for RelMCP's adaptive search). The vehicle prediction benchmark is from a published paper. However, no code repository is mentioned, and the score/cost function design involves several implementation choices (Monte Carlo volume estimation, k-means initialization for multi-modal sets) that could affect reproducibility.
  • Significance: High. Data splitting is a fundamental inefficiency in conformal prediction. MCP addresses it head-on with theoretical guarantees. The variance reduction alone is practically significant — in safety-critical applications, consistent coverage across calibration runs matters as much as average coverage.
  • Red flags: (1) RemMCP requires convexity of s(x, y, q) in q, which restricts the prediction set families (e.g., union-of-ellipsoids requires RelMCP). (2) The adaptive RelMCP algorithm (Alg. 1) breaks the independence assumption of Theorem 2 — the authors acknowledge this but leave a rigorous treatment to future work. (3) Computational cost for high-dimensional non-convex problems (21 s for RelMCP-B vs. <10 ms for SCP) could limit scalability. (4) The framework still requires exchangeable data and does not address distribution shift.

Strengths

  • Elegant unification of two powerful but previously disconnected frameworks (conformal prediction + scenario optimization).
  • Provably recovers split CP as a special case and strictly improves over Bonferroni-corrected dimension-wise CP.
  • Eliminates data splitting, leading to both smaller prediction sets and dramatically lower variance across calibration runs.
  • Flexible framework supporting intervals, ellipsoids, zonotopes, sublevel sets, and multi-modal unions with concrete design guidelines.
  • Clear theoretical contributions with two complementary variants covering convex and non-convex settings.
  • Honest discussion of limitations (computational cost, exchangeability requirement, RelMCP adaptive algorithm's theoretical gap).

Limitations

  • RemMCP requires convex score functions in q, restricting its applicability; RelMCP is needed for non-convex shapes but introduces additional conservatism and computational overhead.
  • Computational cost scales significantly: RelMCP-B takes ~21 s for a union-of-3-ellipsoids problem with n_cal = 2,000, compared to <10 ms for standard SCP — a 2000× slowdown.
  • The adaptive RelMCP algorithm's coverage guarantee is not rigorously established (data-dependent penalty selection breaks Theorem 2's assumptions); the authors rely on empirical validation.
  • Requires sufficiently large calibration sets relative to parameter dimension (ρ ≥ 0 requires n_cal ≥ n_q/ε - 1), which may be restrictive for high-dimensional prediction set families.
  • Limited to exchangeable data; extending to distribution shift, online, or time-series settings is left to future work.
  • Does not leverage training data for shape estimation, unlike some SCP variants that use training residuals for covariance estimation.

Relevance

本文将场景优化理论与保形预测相结合，提出了一种无需数据划分的预测集构建框架，对不确定性量化（UQ）领域具有重要意义。该工作可直接应用于需要可靠预测区间的机器学习系统，尤其是在数据稀缺场景下避免了划分数据造成的效率损失。该框架对安全关键系统（如自动驾驶轨迹预测）中的不确定性量化具有直接应用价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • The adaptive RelMCP algorithm (Alg. 1) breaks Theorem 2's independence assumption — can a rigorous coverage guarantee be established for data-dependent penalty selection, perhaps via martingale arguments or online learning theory?
  • How does MCP scale to very high-dimensional output spaces (e.g., image generation, point clouds) where n_q could be in the hundreds or thousands?
  • Can MCP be combined with conditional conformal prediction methods (e.g., CQR, conformalized quantile regression) to achieve both conditional and marginal coverage without data splitting?
  • What is the computational bottleneck in RelMCP-B — is it the repeated non-convex optimization or the solution complexity evaluation? Could warm-starting or surrogate models accelerate it?
  • The paper shows RemMCP dominates Bonferroni — does it also dominate other multi-output CP methods like copula-based approaches (Messoudi et al., 2021) or graph-based architectures (Cini et al., 2025)?
  • Can the MCP framework incorporate training data (not just calibration data) for shape estimation while maintaining coverage guarantees, perhaps via full conformal or jackknife-style approaches?
