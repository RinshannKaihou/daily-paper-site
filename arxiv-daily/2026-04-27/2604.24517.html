Prior-Agnostic Robust Forecast Aggregation

arXiv: 2604.24517v1

Date: 2026-04-27

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24517v1

一句话总结： 本文提出了先验未知的鲁棒预测聚合问题，设计了基于 log-odds 的闭式聚合器，在状态空间未知条件下实现了近紧的 minimax regret 界（0.0255 上界 / 0.0234 下界），并在已知状态空间的经典设定下首次用显式闭式聚合器达到了低于 0.0226 的 regret。
TL;DR: This paper initiates the study of prior-agnostic robust forecast aggregation where the aggregator does not know the underlying state space. The authors propose a simple closed-form log-odds aggregator that achieves near-tight minimax regret guarantees across three knowledge regimes, including a first explicit closed-form aggregator achieving regret below 0.0226 in the classical known-state setting.

核心贡献

  • 提出先验未知的鲁棒预测聚合问题：将经典已知二元状态空间 {0,1} 的设定推广到状态空间未知的情况，允许状态值为 [0,1] 中任意两个数，更贴近真实场景中专家基于潜在特征而非最终结果形成预测的情况。
  • 设计了简洁的闭式 log-odds 聚合器：在 logit 空间中线性组合专家预测，仅需一个参数 alpha 即可控制聚合强度，不需要知道底层信息结构或先验。
  • 建立了三个知识体系下的近紧 minimax regret 界：
  • 条件独立信号 + 未知状态空间：上界 0.0255，下界 31/1326 约 0.0234（严格分离于已知状态空间设定）
  • Blackwell 有序信息结构：紧界 (5*sqrt(5)-11)/8 约 0.0225
  • 一般信息结构：紧界 1/4
  • 在经典已知 {0,1} 状态空间设定下取得新突破：log-odds 聚合器在 alpha=0.5168 时达到 regret 0.022599，是首个显式闭式聚合器达到低于 0.0226 的上界，与理论下界的差距仅约 6e-5。
  • 扩展到已知边际预测分布的设定：证明了即使知道专家的边际预测分布，在未知状态空间下条件独立结构的 minimax regret 下界仍为 (5*sqrt(5)-11)/8 约 0.0225，广义 log-odds 聚合器可达 0.0228。

Key Contributions

  • Formulates prior-agnostic robust forecast aggregation by generalizing the classical known-binary-state-space {0,1} setting to allow unknown state values in [0,1], capturing real-world scenarios where experts form predictions from latent features rather than the realized outcome.
  • Proposes a simple, explicit, closed-form log-odds aggregator f_alpha that linearly pools forecasts in logit space with a single tempering parameter alpha, requiring no knowledge of the underlying information structure or prior.
  • Establishes near-tight minimax regret bounds across three knowledge regimes:
  • Conditionally independent signals + unknown state space: upper bound 0.0255 (alpha=0.585), lower bound 31/1326 ~ 0.0234, establishing a strict separation from the known-state setting.
  • Blackwell-ordered structures: tight bound (5*sqrt(5)-11)/8 ~ 0.0225, matching the known-state benchmark.
  • General information structures: tight bound 1/4 (trivial aggregator always outputting 1/2 is optimal).
  • Achieves a new milestone in the classical known {0,1} setting: the same log-odds family with alpha=0.5168 yields regret 0.022599, the first explicit closed-form aggregator to break the 0.0226 barrier, with an optimality gap of only ~6e-5 from the theoretical lower bound.
  • Extends to known marginal forecast distributions: proves that even with knowledge of experts' marginal distributions, the lower bound remains (5*sqrt(5)-11)/8 under CI structures with unknown state space, and a generalized log-odds rule achieves regret 0.0228.

Methodology

The paper studies a setting with two experts, a binary latent state space Theta = {theta_1, theta_2} where theta_i in [0,1], and a binary outcome omega drawn from Bernoulli(theta_i) given state i. Each expert observes a private signal and reports their Bayesian posterior belief about omega. The aggregator, who observes only the two reported probabilities, must produce a single forecast. Performance is measured by squared-error regret relative to an omniscient Bayesian benchmark that knows the full model.

Log-odds aggregator (Definition 3.1): The key technical innovation is the aggregation function f_alpha(x1, x2) = 1 / (1 + ((1-x1)/x1)^alpha ((1-x2)/x2)^alpha), which equivalently computes logit(f_alpha) = alpha logit(x1) + alpha * logit(x2). This can be viewed as a "tempered Bayes" rule: it adds evidence on the log-odds scale (mimicking Bayesian updating under conditional independence) but uses alpha < 1 to avoid double-counting the unknown prior.

Proof techniques:

  1. Reduction to binary signals (Lemma 3.3): Using convexity arguments, the worst-case regret can be achieved by information structures where each expert receives at most two signals, reducing the problem to finite-dimensional optimization.
  2. Numerical optimization over 7 parameters: The worst-case regret is computed by solving a 7-variable optimization problem (state values, prior, and signal probabilities) over a grid of alpha values.
  3. Lower bound via adversarial construction: Using Yao's principle, the authors construct pairs of information structures with different state spaces that induce the same report profiles but different Bayesian optimal forecasts, creating unavoidable tradeoffs.
  4. Generalized log-odds aggregator (Definition 5.2): f_{alpha,gamma}(x1,x2) = 1 / (1 + (mu/(1-mu))^gamma ((1-x1)/x1)^alpha ((1-x2)/x2)^alpha), which additionally uses the inferred prior mean mu when marginal distributions are known.

Results & Highlights

  • Unknown state space makes aggregation strictly harder: The lower bound 31/1326 ~ 0.0234 strictly exceeds the known-state lower bound of ~0.0225, proving a fundamental separation.
  • Prior aggregation schemes degrade significantly: When applied to the unknown-state setting, the schemes from Arieli et al. (2018) and Kong et al. (2024) incur worst-case regret above 0.03, substantially worse than the proposed 0.0255.
  • Near-optimal performance in the classical setting: The log-odds aggregator with alpha=0.5168 achieves regret 0.022599 in the known {0,1} setting, matching the performance of the algorithmic (implicit) approach of Guo et al. (2025) while being a simple closed-form formula.
  • Knowing marginal distributions helps less than expected: Even with known marginal forecast distributions, the lower bound under CI structures remains ~0.0225 (Theorem 5.1), because different state spaces can induce identical marginal distributions.
  • Blackwell-ordered structures are robust to state-space uncertainty: The precision-weighted scheme from Arieli et al. (2018) depends only on observed forecasts, so the unknown state space does not increase regret.
  • General information structures are hopeless: Without structural assumptions, the trivial 1/2 aggregator is optimal with regret exactly 1/4.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: The paper makes a meaningful conceptual contribution by formulating the prior-agnostic setting with unknown state space, which is a natural and practically relevant generalization. The log-odds aggregator, while simple, is well-motivated by the Bayesian optimal formula under conditional independence. The result that the same family achieves near-optimal performance in both the unknown-state and known-state settings is elegant. However, the technical tools (reduction to binary signals, Yao's principle for lower bounds) are standard in this literature.

Rigor: The analytical results (lower bounds via adversarial constructions, equivalence results for Blackwell-ordered structures) are fully rigorous. The upper bounds rely on numerical optimization, which is clearly described and validated with high-precision grid refinement. A minor concern is that the numerical upper bounds are certified at 1e-5 resolution, so there is a small gap between what is proven and the reported numbers, though the two-stage validation procedure mitigates this.

Reproducibility: The aggregation rules are simple closed-form expressions with specified parameter values, making them trivially reproducible. The numerical optimization involves a 7-variable problem that could be replicated, though the paper does not provide code. The adversarial constructions for lower bounds are fully specified.

Significance: The main practical takeaway is that a simple log-odds aggregation rule outperforms all prior methods in the unknown-state setting and matches or exceeds the best known results in the classical setting. This has direct applicability to prediction markets, forecasting platforms, and any domain where forecasts from multiple experts must be combined without knowing their information environment. The theoretical separation result (unknown state space is strictly harder) is clean and informative.

Red flags:

  • The paper focuses exclusively on the two-expert case; extension to n > 2 experts is not discussed.
  • The restriction to binary latent states is a significant modeling limitation; continuous state spaces are not addressed.
  • The optimal alpha values (0.585 for unknown state, 0.5168 for known state) are found numerically without analytical characterization of why these specific values arise.
  • The generalized log-odds aggregator for the known-marginal setting does not fully exploit the marginal information (only the prior mean), suggesting room for improvement.

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated problem formulation that generalizes an important line of work to a more realistic setting.
  • The log-odds aggregator is remarkably simple and practical: one formula, one parameter, no computational overhead.
  • Comprehensive analysis across multiple information structure classes (CI, Blackwell-ordered, general) and knowledge regimes (reports-only, known marginals).
  • The strict separation result between unknown-state and known-state settings is a clean theoretical contribution.
  • Strong numerical evidence with careful validation methodology.
  • Good connections to real-world applications (weather forecasting, medical diagnosis, election prediction).

Limitations

  • Two-expert restriction only: All results are for two experts. The extension to n experts is non-trivial and left entirely to future work.
  • Binary latent states: The model assumes exactly two latent states; continuous state spaces would better capture many real-world settings but are not considered.
  • Numerical optimal parameters: The optimal alpha values are found via numerical search without analytical justification, making it unclear how sensitive results are to perturbations or how to adapt the approach to related settings.
  • No empirical evaluation: The paper is entirely theoretical/numerical. No experiments on real forecasting data (e.g., prediction markets, weather forecasts) are provided.
  • No strategic considerations: Experts are assumed to report truthfully; strategic misreporting or adversarial experts are not considered in this model.

Relevance

本文研究的是在状态空间未知的情况下如何鲁棒地聚合多个专家的概率预测，属于信息经济学与决策理论的交叉领域。对于关注预测市场、专家意见聚合、信息设计及鲁棒决策的研究者而言，本文提供了一个简洁实用的 log-odds 聚合器和系统的 minimax regret 理论框架。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How do the results extend to the n-expert setting? Does the log-odds aggregator remain near-optimal with logit(f) = alpha * sum(logit(x_i))?
  • Can the binary latent state assumption be relaxed to continuous or multi-valued state spaces while preserving the near-tight guarantees?
  • Is there an analytical characterization of the optimal alpha (e.g., 0.585 for unknown state, 0.5168 for known state) that connects it to properties of the information structure class?
  • How does the log-odds aggregator perform empirically on real forecasting datasets compared to simple averaging, Bayesian model averaging, or machine learning-based approaches?
  • Can the log-odds framework be adapted to handle strategic experts who may misreport their beliefs, combining the prior-agnostic robustness with mechanism design considerations?
