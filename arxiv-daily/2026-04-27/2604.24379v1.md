> **一句话总结：** Super-DeepG 通过将 DeepG 中的迭代优化问题（LP 求解器和 DOO 算法）替换为可解析求解的张量化公式，实现了 GPU 并行化，在保持同等认证精度的同时获得最高 526 倍加速，并首次在 TinyImageNet 上实现了有效的几何鲁棒性认证。

> **TL;DR:** Super-DeepG reformulates the three core optimization steps of DeepG (linear bound computation, Lipschitz constant estimation, and correction term computation) into closed-form, GPU-tensorizable operations, eliminating the LP solver (Gurobi) and iterative DOO algorithm. This yields up to 526x speedup over DeepG while maintaining equivalent certified accuracy, outperforms FGV on non-geometrically-trained networks (e.g., 65% vs 23% on CIFAR10 rotation), scales to TinyImageNet (which DeepG/PWL cannot), and is released as open-source within the auto_LiRPA framework. Accepted at ICCPS/HSCC 2026.

## 核心贡献

- 将 DeepG 的三步优化流程（LP 求解无初始界、迭代 DOO 校正项）重新表述为可解析求解的闭式公式，消除了对 Gurobi 线性规划求解器的依赖
- 通过将所有像素的计算张量化，实现了大规模 GPU 并行化，获得最高 526 倍的加速
- 证明了 Super-DeepG 的 Step 1-bis 与 DeepG 的 Step 1 产生完全相同的线性松弛界（数学等价性）
- 在 TinyImageNet 上首次实现了有效的几何鲁棒性认证（DeepG 和 PWL 均无法扩展到该规模）
- 在 auto_LiRPA 框架中开源实现，将几何变换封装为 CustomOp 层，便于集成

## Key Contributions

- Reformulates DeepG's three-step optimization pipeline (LP-based unsound bounds, Lipschitz constant computation, DOO-based correction terms) into closed-form analytical solutions, removing dependency on the Gurobi LP solver
- Achieves full GPU tensorization of all pixel-wise computations, yielding up to 526x speedup over DeepG (e.g., MNIST rotation certification: 0.40s vs 210.72s per image)
- Mathematically proves equivalence between Super-DeepG's Step 1-bis and DeepG's Step 1 -- both produce identical linear relaxation bounds
- First geometric certification method to scale effectively to TinyImageNet, where DeepG and PWL fail to scale
- Open-source implementation integrated into the auto_LiRPA framework with a plug-and-play CustomOp layer design

## Methodology

Super-DeepG follows the same three-step structure as DeepG but replaces each iterative or solver-based component with analytical, GPU-friendly alternatives:

**Step 1-bis (Analytical Unsound Bounds):** Instead of solving a linear program with Gurobi for each pixel, Super-DeepG observes that any optimal solution to the bound-finding optimization must pass through at least one sampled point (proven by contradiction). It enumerates all P sub-problems where the bound is forced through each sample point, solves each analytically via a closed-form expression (Equation 16), and selects the best. This decomposes into simple min/max operations over differences of function values at sample points, fully parallelizable across pixels on GPU.

**Step 2-bis (Lipschitz Constant):** Reuses PWL's efficient PyTorch-based Lipschitz constant computation -- no modification needed.

**Step 3-bis (Correction via Static Meshing):** Instead of DeepG's iterative DOO (Determined Optimistic Optimization) algorithm that recursively splits the interval containing the minimum, Super-DeepG partitions the domain into N uniform sub-intervals upfront, evaluates the residual at each center, and applies Lipschitz correction in a single pass. The number of subdivisions N is determined by Equation 17 (relating N to Lipschitz constant and desired precision epsilon). For multi-dimensional inputs (e.g., translation with d=2), the domain is partitioned into an N^d grid of hyper-rectangles.

The method supports rotation, scaling, shearing, and translation, and integrates as a CustomOp layer in auto_LiRPA, using CROWN or CROWN-IBP as the downstream verifier.

## Results & Highlights

- **DeepG comparison (Table 2):** Super-DeepG matches or exceeds DeepG's certified accuracy with massive speedups: 526x on MNIST (98% certified at 0.40s vs 210.72s), 315x on CIFAR10 (65% at 7.58s vs 2389.70s)
- **FGV comparison on CGT networks (Table 3):** Competitive with FGV -- matches 94% on MNIST rotation, slightly faster on CIFAR10 (0.97s vs 1.04s), within 3x on TinyImageNet
- **FGV comparison on PGD networks (Table 4):** Dramatically outperforms FGV on non-geometrically-trained networks -- CIFAR10 rotation: 65% certified vs FGV's 23% plateau, and FGV requires 443s to reach 23% while Super-DeepG reaches 65% in 1.90s
- **PWL comparison (Table 5):** With optimal configuration (IS=1, P=10, N=250), achieves 98% certified accuracy at 0.40s/image, dominating PWL's 91% at 28.3s/image
- **TinyImageNet scaling (Table 6, Observation 5):** Certifies 18% on TinyImageNet rotation at 3.73s/image where FGV runs out of memory; DeepG and PWL do not scale to this dataset
- **Hyperparameter sensitivity:** P=10 samples are sufficient for Step 1; increasing N beyond 250 yields marginal gains; reducing interval size is more effective than increasing N (Figure 5)

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core novelty is in the reformulation of DeepG's optimization problems rather than a fundamentally new algorithmic paradigm. The insight that the LP in Step 1 can be decomposed into P closed-form sub-problems is elegant and impactful. The replacement of DOO with static meshing is a natural but well-executed engineering choice. The mathematical equivalence proof is a strong contribution.
- **Rigor:** The paper provides formal proofs for the equivalence of Super-DeepG's bounds to DeepG's bounds (proof by contradiction in Appendix B.1). The multi-dimensional extension (Appendix B.3-B.4) is thorough. Experimental comparisons use the same evaluation protocols from original papers and carefully match hyperparameters for fairness.
- **Reproducibility:** High. Open-source implementation on GitHub, integrated into auto_LiRPA. All hyperparameter configurations are listed (Appendix C.2-C.3). Standard datasets (MNIST, CIFAR10, TinyImageNet) and well-documented network architectures.
- **Significance:** The speedup is substantial and practical. Scaling to TinyImageNet is a meaningful advance. The work directly addresses the precision-vs-speed tradeoff that has limited geometric certification in practice.
- **Red flags:**
  - Fairness concern: DeepG runs on CPU while Super-DeepG runs on GPU (V100). The 526x speedup is impressive but partly reflects hardware rather than purely algorithmic improvement. The authors acknowledge this but could provide a more careful disentangling.
  - The comparison with FGV on CGT networks (Table 3) shows FGV is actually faster on MNIST (0.002s vs 0.17s), suggesting Super-DeepG is not uniformly superior -- its advantage depends on the network training method.
  - Limited to classification tasks; the authors mention object detection as future work but it remains unaddressed.
  - The method still requires interval splitting for good precision, inheriting this limitation from DeepG.
  - No comparison with randomized smoothing methods, which while non-deterministic, may offer complementary tradeoffs worth discussing.

## Strengths

- Clean mathematical reformulation with formal equivalence guarantees to the prior state-of-the-art
- Practical engineering achievement: 526x speedup enables previously intractable verification tasks
- Scales to larger datasets (TinyImageNet) where competing methods fail
- Open-source, well-integrated into an existing verification framework (auto_LiRPA)
- Thorough experimental evaluation across multiple datasets, transformations, training methods, and competitor tools
- Principled hyperparameter analysis (influence of P, N, interval size)

## Limitations

- The GPU-vs-CPU comparison with DeepG inflates the reported speedups; a CPU-only or GPU-only comparison would be more informative
- On networks specifically trained for FGV (CGT networks), Super-DeepG is not faster and sometimes slower (MNIST: 85x slower)
- Limited to feed-forward classification networks; does not address object detection, segmentation, or other vision tasks
- Multi-dimensional transformations (e.g., translation d=2) incur exponential cost in the partition count (N^d cells), limiting scalability for composed high-dimensional perturbations
- The certified accuracy on TinyImageNet remains modest (18% for rotation), suggesting room for improvement
- No theoretical analysis of how the certified radius scales with network size or image resolution

## Relevance

本文对神经网络在几何变换（旋转、缩放、剪切、平移）下的形式化鲁棒性认证有重要贡献，其 GPU 友好的线性松弛方法和开源实现可直接应用于安全关键场景下的图像分类器验证。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does Super-DeepG perform on modern architectures (ResNets, Vision Transformers) beyond the small CNNs tested?
- Can the static meshing approach be combined with adaptive partitioning strategies (e.g., refinement near decision boundaries) for further precision gains?
- What is the algorithmic contribution to the 526x speedup when controlling for hardware (e.g., both methods on GPU)?
- How does the N^d scaling for multi-dimensional perturbations affect certification of composed transformations (e.g., rotation + translation)?
- Can the framework be extended to object detection tasks, as the authors mention for aircraft runway detection?
- What are the theoretical limits on certified accuracy for geometric perturbations on larger-scale models and datasets?
