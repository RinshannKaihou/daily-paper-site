> **一句话总结：** 本文系统研究了大型视觉语言模型（LVLM）的语言骨干结构化剪枝策略，发现宽度剪枝（widthwise pruning）在大多数场景下优于层剪枝（layerwise pruning），且结合SFT和隐藏状态L2蒸馏的恢复训练仅需5%数据即可保留95%以上原始性能，30%剪枝率是性能安全的上限阈值。

> **TL;DR:** A systematic empirical study on compressing LVLMs (LLaVA-7B, InternVL-4B, Bunny-3B) via structured pruning of the language backbone. Key findings: (1) widthwise pruning consistently outperforms layerwise pruning, especially on reasoning-intensive and free-form generation tasks; (2) SFT + L2 hidden-state distillation is the optimal recovery strategy across all compression levels; (3) only 5% of training data suffices for effective recovery at moderate compression (<=45%); (4) up to 30% pruning retains ~98-99% of original performance with proper recovery. The paper provides a practical decision flowchart for practitioners. Accepted at IJCV 2026.

## 核心贡献

- **系统性对比两种结构化剪枝范式：** 在三个不同架构的LVLM（LLaVA-v1.5-7B、Mini-InternVL-Chat-4B、Bunny-v1.0-3B）上全面比较了层剪枝和宽度剪枝的效果差异，发现宽度剪枝在零样本性能和恢复后性能上均优于层剪枝，特别是在推理密集型任务（MathVista、AI2D）和自由生成任务（GQA、DocVQA）上优势显著
- **确立最优恢复训练策略：** 通过大量实验验证了SFT + L2隐藏状态蒸馏的组合是最佳恢复方案，二者互补——SFT优化最终输出（"是什么"），L2对齐内部表示（"怎么做"），简单组合超越了更复杂的三项组合
- **数据高效的恢复训练：** 证明仅需5%原始训练数据即可恢复90%以上的性能（中等压缩率下），而校准数据集仅需10-15个样本即可稳定剪枝重要性评估
- **实践决策指南：** 提供了基于资源可用性和压缩比要求的决策流程图——低资源场景用宽度剪枝（无需恢复）、中等压缩（<=30%）用层剪枝+投影层微调、高压缩（>=45%）需要知识蒸馏
- **与量化的互补性验证：** 证明结构化剪枝与int8量化可无缝叠加，30%剪枝+量化将LLaVA-7B内存从13.2GiB降至5.4GiB，性能仅从53.7降至50.8

## Key Contributions

- **Comprehensive comparison of two structured pruning paradigms** on three LVLM families (LLaVA-v1.5-7B, Mini-InternVL-Chat-4B, Bunny-v1.0-3B): widthwise pruning consistently outperforms layerwise pruning, especially on reasoning-intensive (MathVista, AI2D) and free-form generation tasks (GQA, DocVQA), with the gap attributable to architectural depth being critical for multi-step reasoning
- **Optimal recovery training recipe:** SFT + L2 hidden-state distillation is the best recovery combination across all compression levels; the two losses are orthogonal (SFT supervises "what" to output, L2 regularizes "how" internally), and more complex triple combinations yield diminishing returns
- **Data-efficient recovery and calibration:** Only 5% of the original training data suffices for effective recovery at moderate compression (<=45%), while pruning calibration requires merely 10-15 samples, indicating that weight importance is a structural property of the model rather than data-dependent
- **Practical decision flowchart** for practitioners based on resource constraints and target compression ratios: widthwise-only for extreme low-resource, layerwise + projector fine-tuning for moderate compression (<=30%), knowledge distillation for high compression (>=45%)
- **Complementarity with quantization:** Demonstrated that structured pruning and int8 quantization can be seamlessly combined, reducing LLaVA-7B memory from 13.2 GiB to 5.4 GiB at 30% pruning with only marginal performance loss (53.7 to 50.8)

## Methodology

The study targets the language model backbone of LVLMs, which accounts for the majority of parameters (e.g., 6.7B of 7.0B in LLaVA). Two pruning strategies are investigated:

**Layerwise Pruning** uses Block Influence (BI) scores to quantify each layer's importance via cosine distance between input and output hidden states. Layers causing smaller hidden state changes are deemed less important and pruned first.

**Widthwise Pruning** employs dependency-based structural pruning, building a dependency graph within each LLM layer. Group importance is approximated via first-order Taylor expansion using gradient information on the cross-entropy loss. Groups of dependent neurons with lowest importance are pruned together.

**Recovery Training** explores two families: (1) Supervised Finetuning (SFT) — either projector-only or projector + LLM joint finetuning using cross-entropy loss; (2) Knowledge Distillation — logits-based (KL or reverse KL divergence) and hidden-state matching (L2 loss between teacher and student intermediate representations). The total loss combines SFT, logits KD, and feature matching with balancing coefficients.

**Evaluation** spans nine multimodal benchmarks (MMMU, SQA, MathVista, AI2D, POPE, MME-Cognition, MME-Perception, GQA, DocVQA) using the lmms-eval suite, with performance reported as percentage relative to the uncompressed model.

## Results & Highlights

- **Widthwise pruning dominates zero-shot:** At 15% pruning, LLaVA retains 90.87% of performance with widthwise vs. only 72.22% with layerwise pruning
- **Reasoning tasks are especially sensitive to layer removal:** On AI2D at 15% pruning of Bunny, widthwise reaches 65.64 vs. 52.73 for layerwise — a 12.91-point absolute gap (~20% relative)
- **Projector-only SFT is sufficient for low compression:** At <=30% pruning, fine-tuning only the multimodal projector recovers ~95%+ performance, as pruning mainly causes vision-language misalignment
- **SFT + L2 is the best recovery combo:** While L2 alone performs worst (no output-level supervision), combining it with SFT yields the best results due to objective orthogonality
- **30% pruning is the safe zone:** All models retain 98-99% of original capabilities at 15% pruning and minimal loss at 30%. Beyond 45%, performance drops below 90% fidelity
- **Calibration needs only ~10-15 samples:** Importance scores converge rapidly, confirming pruning is a structural property
- **Qualitative degradation at extreme ratios:** At 60% pruning, models hallucinate (e.g., seeing a "woman" instead of a dog in a Mona Lisa parody) and fail basic counting tasks
- **Pruning + int8 quantization compounding:** LLaVA-7B at 30% pruning + int8 drops from 13.2 GiB to 5.4 GiB with avg score 50.8 vs. 53.7 baseline

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Moderate):** The paper does not propose a new method but rather a thorough empirical characterization of existing pruning and recovery techniques applied to LVLMs. The finding that widthwise pruning outperforms layerwise pruning aligns with concurrent results in unimodal LLM pruning (e.g., MiniLM), which the authors honestly acknowledge. The SFT+L2 optimal combination is a useful but not surprising finding.

**Rigor (High):** Experiments are well-designed with three diverse architectures, nine benchmarks, and controlled ablations on recovery strategies, data fractions, and calibration sizes. The use of relative performance percentages (vs. uncompressed baseline) is a fair normalization. The qualitative analysis adds depth beyond aggregate metrics.

**Reproducibility (High):** Code is open-sourced, evaluation uses the standard lmms-eval framework, and experimental details (datasets, pruning ratios, training configurations) are clearly specified. The calibration data efficiency finding (10-15 samples) is itself a reproducibility asset.

**Significance (Moderate-High):** The practical decision flowchart and the "safe zone" characterization (<=30% pruning) are directly actionable for practitioners deploying LVLMs on edge devices. The complementarity with quantization is demonstrated but not deeply explored.

**Red Flags:**
- No evaluation on very recent/large LVLMs (e.g., LLaVA-NeXT, InternVL2, Qwen-VL); the 3B-7B range may not fully represent behavior at larger scales
- Vision encoder is kept frozen throughout; the parameter imbalance argument is valid for these models but may not generalize to architectures with larger vision encoders
- The "5% data" claim is somewhat misleading — it applies to recovery training data (a subset of the original instruction-tuning data), not arbitrary data
- Recovery training still requires forward passes through the full teacher model for distillation, which limits the "lightweight" claim

## Strengths

- Extremely thorough empirical evaluation covering pruning strategies, recovery methods, data efficiency, calibration sensitivity, and quantization complementarity
- Clear, actionable decision flowchart for practitioners (Fig. 1) that distills all findings into a practical guide
- Honest discussion of limitations and the structural nature of pruning sensitivity
- Insightful analysis of why widthwise pruning outperforms layerwise (feature alignment and architectural depth arguments)
- Strong finding on calibration data efficiency — only 10-15 samples needed, making the pruning step essentially free

## Limitations

- Scope is limited to 3 LVLM families in the 3B-7B parameter range; scaling behavior to 13B+ models is unknown
- Vision encoder pruning is entirely unexplored, leaving a significant component of LVLM architecture untouched
- The study focuses only on structured pruning; unstructured and semi-structured methods are acknowledged but not compared
- Performance degrades sharply beyond 45% pruning, meaning the approach cannot serve extreme compression needs
- Recovery training with distillation requires maintaining the full teacher model in memory, partially offsetting the compression gains during the training phase
- The benchmarks, while diverse, do not include video understanding or real-time interaction scenarios

## Relevance

本文对大型视觉语言模型的结构化剪枝与恢复训练进行了系统实证研究，对关注模型压缩与高效部署的研究者具有直接参考价值。特别是其中关于宽度剪枝优于层剪枝、SFT+L2最佳恢复策略、以及5%数据即可有效恢复的结论，可为多模态模型的边缘部署和资源受限场景应用提供实用指导。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do these findings transfer to larger LVLMs (13B-70B parameters) and more recent architectures like LLaVA-OneVision or InternVL2?
- Can the vision encoder also be safely pruned, and would joint vision-language backbone pruning yield better compression than language-only pruning?
- Is the SFT+L2 recovery strategy optimal for other modalities (video, audio) and encoder-decoder architectures?
- What is the theoretical explanation for why 10-15 calibration samples are sufficient — does this relate to the intrinsic dimensionality of the activation space?
- Can progressive pruning (iteratively prune-recover-prune) push the safe compression boundary beyond 30% while maintaining fidelity?
- How does structured pruning interact with parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods like LoRA during recovery?
