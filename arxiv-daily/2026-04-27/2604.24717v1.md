> **一句话总结：** 本文提出SIREN-RoPE，将RoPE的旋转流形从固定的离散序数位置编码扩展为可学习的连续时间信号空间，通过双分支SIREN-DNN网络将多尺度时间周期性（日/周）和非周期性衰减直接编码到注意力旋转角度中，在LinkedIn生产级社交信息流推荐数据集上以仅0.2%的额外参数量实现了全部六项校准和排序指标的一致提升。

> **TL;DR:** SIREN-RoPE reframes the RoPE rotation manifold as a learnable "imaginary" dimension of attention expressivity. A dual-branch SIREN-DNN network maps continuous timestamps into per-dimension rotation angles, autonomously discovering daily and weekly periodicities without explicit time features. Evaluated on LinkedIn's production social feed recommender, it improves NE and AUC across all three engagement tasks (Contribution, Like, LongDwell) with only ~0.2% extra parameters and ~1.8% inference overhead. The key insight: routing temporal signals through the rotation manifold works consistently, while routing them through the embedding space does not -- confirming rotation as an orthogonal, complementary expressive channel.

## 核心贡献

- **提出旋转流形作为注意力机制的"第二维度"：** 类比复数中虚轴与实轴的正交关系，将RoPE的旋转空间视为与语义嵌入空间正交且互补的表达维度，而非固定的位置编码脚手架。
- **设计SIREN-RoPE：** 用双分支SIREN-DNN网络将多尺度时间特征映射为逐维度旋转角度，通过可学习门控标量λ自适应融合时间信号与序数位置信号。
- **实验验证旋转维度与嵌入维度的信号独立性：** 消融实验表明，时间信号通过嵌入空间注入无效甚至有害，但通过旋转流形注入则产生一致增益。
- **生产级验证：** 在LinkedIn社交信息流推荐系统上以极低开销实现全指标提升，且模型自主学习到日周期和周周期，无需手工指定周期参数。

## Key Contributions

- **Conceptual reframing of RoPE rotation space:** The paper argues that the rotation manifold is an underexplored "imaginary" dimension of attention, orthogonal to the "real" semantic embedding space. This analogy to complex numbers provides a new lens for understanding and extending rotary positional encodings.
- **SIREN-RoPE architecture:** Replaces the fixed ordinal-position-driven rotation angle with a learnable combination of (1) a SIREN periodic branch capturing daily/weekly cycles, (2) a DNN aperiodic branch capturing monotone recency decay, and (3) a learnable scalar gate lambda controlling the ordinal contribution.
- **Empirical proof of rotation-embedding orthogonality:** Systematic ablations demonstrate that temporal features routed through embeddings fail to improve performance, while the same features routed through the rotation manifold yield consistent gains -- establishing rotation as a distinct representation mode.
- **Autonomous periodicity discovery:** The trained model spontaneously learns 24-hour and 7-day behavioral cycles from the data alone, confirmed via FFT analysis of the learned attention patterns, without any explicit hour-of-day or day-of-week features.
- **Production-scale deployment validation:** Demonstrated on LinkedIn's generative recommender with negligible overhead (~0.2% parameters, ~1.8% inference cost).

## Methodology

**Problem formulation.** Given a user interaction sequence S = {(e_i, T_i)} where e_i is an item embedding and T_i is a timestamp, standard RoPE uses only the ordinal position p_i to compute rotation angles. This ignores the actual temporal intervals between interactions.

**SIREN-RoPE rotation angle.** The core equation replaces the fixed angle p_i * theta_j with:

Theta_j(T_i, p_i) = f_phi(T_i)_j * omega_j* + p_i * theta_j * lambda

where:
- f_phi is a dual-branch SIREN-DNN network mapping temporal features to rotation angles
- omega_j* are learnable per-dimension frequency scalings
- lambda is a learnable gate initialized to 1.0

**Dual-branch SIREN architecture.** The temporal encoder f_phi = f_sin(T) + f_DNN(T), where the SIREN branch uses periodic sin activations to capture cyclical patterns (daily, weekly), and the DNN branch captures aperiodic monotone recency decay.

**Temporal feature engineering.** The timestamp is decomposed into five (cos, sin) pairs capturing second-of-day, day-of-week, time-in-week, time-in-month, and time-in-year cycles, plus a raw time-in-year scalar for long-range decay.

**Training details.** Adam optimizer with lr=1e-3 and cosine annealing, batch size 800, 2-layer SIREN and DNN branches with 64 hidden units each. The backbone is a generative recommender (based on the TIGER/STU architecture) with sequence length 1024.

**Key training dynamics.** At convergence, the ordinal gate lambda drops from 1.0 to 0.044, indicating the model learns to rely predominantly on temporal modulation over raw ordinal position.

## Results & Highlights

**Main results (Table 2):** SIREN-RoPE vs. Ordinal RoPE on production social feed:
- Contribution NE: 0.6206 -> 0.6182 (reduction of 0.0024)
- Like AUC: 0.9238 -> 0.9249 (improvement of 0.0011)
- LongDwell AUC: 0.7597 -> 0.7633 (improvement of 0.0036)
- Improvements are consistent across all 6 metric-task combinations.

**Baselines outperformed:**
- Timestamp-as-Feature (appending timestamp to embeddings): performs no better than ordinal RoPE; slightly worse on calibration.
- TO-RoPE (replacing ordinal position with raw timestamp in fixed frequency schedule): only marginally improves LongDwell.

**Ablation findings:**
- Cyclical (cos, sin) features improve calibration vs. raw scalar timestamps.
- SIREN branch discovers hidden periodicities beyond manually engineered features.
- Static signals (e.g., binary in/out-of-network) provide no benefit in the rotation dimension -- only dynamic, relational signals are productive.
- Temporal features in embeddings are ineffective or detrimental; the same features in rotation space work.

**Learned periodicities (FFT analysis):** The model autonomously discovers dominant peaks at 1 cycle/day and ~0.143 cycles/day (1/7, weekly), plus harmonics at 1/2, 1/3 cycles/day corresponding to sub-daily behavioral patterns.

**Efficiency:** ~1.4% training time increase, ~1.8% inference overhead, 0.2% parameter increase over ordinal RoPE.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The conceptual reframing of the RoPE rotation manifold as a learnable "imaginary" dimension is genuinely novel. While individual components (SIREN networks, temporal features in recommendation) are not new, their combination and the principled argument for rotation-space orthogonality represent a meaningful contribution. The discovery that the ordinal gate lambda collapses to 0.044 is a striking empirical finding.

**Rigor (Moderate-High):** The paper includes systematic ablations, spectral analysis of learned patterns, and joint ordinal-temporal attention heatmaps. However, evaluation is limited to a single production dataset from one social network. No public benchmark comparisons are provided. The absence of results on standard academic recommendation datasets (e.g., Amazon, MovieLens) limits generalizability claims.

**Reproducibility (Moderate):** Code is promised on GitHub. The architecture is described in sufficient detail with hyperparameters. However, the production dataset is proprietary and unavailable, making exact reproduction impossible. The paper would benefit from evaluation on at least one public dataset.

**Significance (Moderate-High):** If the rotation-as-orthogonal-dimension thesis holds across domains (NLP, time-series, multimodal), this could shift how the community thinks about positional encoding. The immediate practical impact is clear for recommendation systems. The broader theoretical implications depend on future work.

**Red flags:**
- Single proprietary dataset; no public benchmark evaluation.
- The absolute improvements are small in magnitude (NE reduction of 0.0024, AUC gains of 0.001-0.004), though the authors argue these are significant at production scale.
- The "complex number" analogy, while elegant, is somewhat overplayed -- the actual contribution is a specific architectural modification rather than a new algebraic framework.

## Strengths

- Elegant conceptual framing: rotation manifold as the "imaginary axis" of attention is memorable and thought-provoking.
- Strong empirical evidence that rotation space is complementary to embedding space, not redundant.
- Autonomous discovery of daily and weekly periodicities is well-demonstrated through FFT and attention heatmaps.
- Negligible computational overhead makes adoption practical.
- Clean ablation study isolating the contribution of each component.
- The ordinal gate lambda collapsing to 0.044 is a compelling finding showing the model genuinely learns to favor temporal signals.

## Limitations

- Evaluated only on a single proprietary dataset; generalizability to other domains (NLP, time-series, other recommendation settings) is claimed but unproven.
- No comparison against strong temporal recommendation baselines like TiSASRec or Neural ODE-based approaches on public benchmarks.
- The improvements, while consistent, are modest in absolute terms. The paper does not report statistical significance tests.
- The framework's applicability to standard LLM tasks (language modeling, text generation) is suggested but not demonstrated.
- Extrapolation to out-of-distribution time intervals remains an acknowledged open question.

## Relevance

本文将RoPE从固定的位置编码提升为可学习的信号空间，这一思路对推荐系统和序列建模具有直接参考价值，同时也为LLM中如何更好地融合时间/结构信息提供了新视角。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does SIREN-RoPE perform on standard NLP tasks (perplexity, downstream benchmarks) when applied to LLMs? Does conditioning rotation angles on syntactic or semantic features improve language understanding?
- Can the learned periodicities transfer across domains? If trained on one recommendation platform, do the discovered cycles generalize to another?
- What is the theoretical expressive power of the rotation manifold? Can we characterize the function classes it can represent vs. the embedding space?
- How does SIREN-RoPE interact with other positional encoding innovations (e.g., ALiBi, length extrapolation methods)? Are they complementary or redundant?
- The lambda gate dropping to 0.044 suggests ordinal position becomes nearly irrelevant. Does this hold for tasks where item ordering carries strong causal signal (e.g., session-based recommendation with short sequences)?
