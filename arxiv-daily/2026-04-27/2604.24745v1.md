> **一句话总结：** 本文提出HRGrad方法，通过流形等距旋转代替传统的耗散性投影来解决多尺度动理学方程（BGK方程、线性输运方程等）中不同Knudsen数任务间的梯度冲突问题，在保护微观非平衡物理特征的同时实现跨所有渐近区域的统一训练。

> **TL;DR:** HRGrad replaces lossy Euclidean projection with manifold isometric rotation and fair aggregation to resolve gradient conflicts across multiscale kinetic regimes, enabling a single APNN model to solve Boltzmann-BGK and linear transport equations over the full Knudsen number spectrum.

## 核心贡献

- 提出HRGrad（调和旋转梯度）方法，用流形等距旋转代替传统的欧氏投影来解决多任务梯度冲突，在冲突化解过程中100%保留梯度范数（等距保真性）。
- 构建调和锥（Harmonized Cone）作为物理可行且无冲突的梯度搜索空间，并提取物理锚点方向，确保优化轨迹保持在渐近保持（AP）流形上。
- 设计MER（多任务均衡关系）目标函数与MCAE（误校正角度误差）惩罚机制，通过自适应内循环步数精确选取最优旋转角度，避免过校正或欠校正。
- 提供严格的收敛性分析，包括凸和非凸设定下的收敛定理证明，以及与PCGrad、IMTL-G、ConFIG、AlignGrad等方法的理论对比。
- 在Boltzmann-BGK方程、ES-BGK方程、线性输运方程和线性半导体Boltzmann-Poisson方程上进行了广泛的数值验证，证明了方法在从动理学到流体/扩散极限的完整Knudsen数谱范围内的有效性。

## Key Contributions

- Proposes the Harmonized Rotational Gradient (HRGrad) method that replaces dissipative Euclidean projection with manifold isometric rotation, preserving 100% gradient norm (isometric fidelity) during conflict resolution across multiscale kinetic regimes.
- Constructs the Harmonized Cone as the intersection of the Primal Gradient Cone (physical feasibility) and the Dual Gradient Cone (non-conflict guarantee), with a physically anchored reference direction ensuring updates remain on the asymptotic-preserving manifold.
- Designs a Multi-task Equilibrium Relationship (MER) objective with Miss-Correction Angular Error (MCAE) penalties and adaptive inner-loop step counts to select optimal rotation angles, avoiding over-correction and under-correction of task-specific features.
- Provides rigorous convergence guarantees for both convex and non-convex loss landscapes, with formal proofs of isometric fidelity, non-conflict, equal cosine similarity, and aggregate product identity properties.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on Boltzmann-BGK, ES-BGK, linear transport, and linear semiconductor Boltzmann-Poisson equations across the full Knudsen number spectrum from kinetic to hydrodynamic/diffusive limits.

## Methodology

HRGrad addresses the fundamental challenge of training a single Asymptotic-Preserving Neural Network (APNN) across a continuous spectrum of Knudsen numbers. The method operates in four stages:

**Stage 1 - Harmonized Cone Construction:** The algorithm defines the Primal Gradient Cone (ensuring physical feasibility) and the Dual Gradient Cone (ensuring non-conflict), then restricts the search space to their intersection -- the Harmonized Cone. Using the Double Description Method, extreme rays are extracted and a physical anchor direction is computed as the normalized centroid of these rays.

**Stage 2 - Manifold Isometric Rotation:** For each task gradient that conflicts with the physical anchor (negative dot product), an SO(2) rotation operator is constructed on the 2D tangent plane spanned by the gradient and the anchor. This rotation guarantees isometric fidelity -- the rotated gradient preserves the original norm exactly.

**Stage 3 - MER Optimization via MCAE:** The optimal rotation angles are found by minimizing a global energy functional that balances pairwise alignment (suppressing under-correction) against proximity to original gradients (suppressing over-correction). An adaptive step-count strategy dynamically allocates more inner SGD iterations during stiff transitional regimes.

**Stage 4 - Magnitude Restoration and Fair Aggregation:** After rotation, original gradient magnitudes are restored, and a pseudo-inverse-based fair consensus direction is computed ensuring equal cosine similarity between the final update and all rotated task gradients. This prevents macroscopic regimes from dominating the update.

The method is integrated into the Adam optimizer by applying the HRGrad operator to the bias-corrected first moments of per-task gradients. A log-uniform sampling strategy for the Knudsen number ensures adequate coverage of the stiff small-epsilon regime.

## Results & Highlights

- HRGrad successfully overcomes the "failure modes" of APNNs in multiscale kinetic problems where existing MTL methods (PCGrad, CAGrad, ConFIG, etc.) fail due to gradient conflicts.
- Compared against 10 MTL baselines including LS, UW, DWA, GradDrop, CAGrad, RLW, Nash-MTL, FAMO, PCGrad, and IMTL-G, HRGrad achieves the best mean rank and average F1 score across all tested problems.
- Validated on 1D and 2D problems including smooth initial data, Riemann problems, boundary-layer cases, variable scattering coefficients, and semiconductor Boltzmann-Poisson coupling.
- The method enables pretraining across a range of Knudsen numbers and generalizes to unseen parameter values, including extrapolation to very small epsilon.
- Convergence proofs are provided for both convex losses (monotone non-increasing with global optimum guarantee) and non-convex losses (ergodic O(1/K) stationary-point convergence).
- Code and data are publicly available at https://github.com/liangzhangyong/HRGrad.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core idea of replacing projection with isometric rotation for gradient conflict resolution is genuinely novel. The four-stage construction (harmonized cone, SO(2) rotation, MER optimization, fair aggregation) is technically creative and physically motivated. The insight that projection acts as an energy-clipping mechanism destroying microscopic features is well-argued.

- **Rigor:** The convergence analysis is mathematically sound, with complete proofs for both convex and non-convex settings. The geometric properties (isometric fidelity, non-conflict, equal cosine similarity) are rigorously established. However, the Strict Common Descent assumption (Assumption A) requires a bound on the maximum rotation angle, which may not always hold in practice.

- **Reproducibility:** Code is publicly available. Experimental settings (network architecture, activation functions, learning rate schedules, quadrature points) are described in detail. However, the method has several hyperparameters (alpha_min, alpha_max, k_std, lambda, delta) whose sensitivity is not systematically analyzed.

- **Significance:** The problem addressed -- simultaneous training across asymptotically distinct kinetic regimes -- is important for scientific machine learning. The method could have broader applicability to other multi-task learning scenarios with severe gradient conflicts.

- **Red flags:**
  - The paper is very long (39 pages) with some redundancy between the main text and appendix.
  - Sections 3.4 (Pre-training) and 3.5 (Fast fine-tuning) are mentioned in the table of contents but appear empty in the HTML version.
  - Some experimental result figures referenced in the text appear incomplete (e.g., "The results are illustrated in Figure, where..." without figure numbers in some places).
  - The computational overhead of the four-stage pipeline (DDM solver, inner MER optimization loop) is not thoroughly analyzed, though Appendix B mentions computation times.
  - The adaptive inner-step strategy (Eq. 17) introduces additional computation whose cost-benefit tradeoff is not clearly quantified.

## Strengths

- Physically motivated: the method explicitly accounts for the multiscale structure of kinetic equations and protects microscopic non-equilibrium features.
- Theoretically grounded: complete convergence proofs with clear geometric interpretations.
- Comprehensive evaluation: tested on four different PDE systems with multiple benchmark problems spanning 1D and 2D, various boundary conditions, and the full Knudsen number range.
- Fair comparison: evaluated against 10 established MTL baselines with two complementary metrics (mean rank and average F1 score).
- The formal comparison with existing methods (PCGrad, IMTL-G, ConFIG, AlignGrad) in the appendix clearly articulates the structural advantages of HRGrad.
- Log-uniform sampling strategy for Knudsen numbers is a simple but effective practical contribution.

## Limitations

- Computational cost: the four-stage pipeline with DDM solving and inner MER optimization adds significant per-iteration overhead compared to simple gradient aggregation methods. The paper acknowledges this but does not fully quantify the tradeoff.
- Scalability: the Double Description Method for extracting extreme rays has combinatorial complexity in the worst case, which may limit applicability to very large numbers of tasks.
- The method is evaluated only on 1D and 2D problems; high-dimensional kinetic problems (full 3D+3V Boltzmann equation) remain untested and are acknowledged as future work.
- The full column rank assumption on the normalized rotated gradient matrix M is needed for the theoretical guarantees; the paper notes this fails only on a measure-zero set but does not discuss practical mitigation.
- Single-author work with no co-authors for cross-validation of the extensive theoretical and experimental claims.

## Relevance

本文对多任务学习中的梯度冲突问题提出了具有物理动机的新解决方案，将渐近保持方法与流形等距旋转相结合，对研究PINNs在多尺度物理问题中的应用具有参考价值。其核心思想（用保范旋转代替耗散性投影）可推广至其他存在严重梯度冲突的多任务学习场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the computational overhead of HRGrad's four-stage pipeline scale with the number of tasks (mini-batch size) and the dimensionality of the parameter space? Is there a practical regime where the overhead becomes prohibitive?
- Can the isometric rotation idea be combined with neural operator frameworks (e.g., DeepONet, FNO) to enable efficient solution of parameterized PDEs without collocation-based retraining?
- How sensitive is HRGrad to its hyperparameters (lambda in the MER objective, alpha_min/alpha_max for adaptive steps, delta for numerical stability)? Is there a principled way to set these?
- Does the approach extend naturally to other multiscale PDE problems beyond kinetic equations, such as homogenization problems or singularly perturbed systems?
- Can the harmonized cone construction be interpreted as a form of constrained optimization on a Riemannian manifold, and does this perspective offer additional algorithmic insights?
