> **一句话总结：** 本文提出信息可行性原则（Informational Viability Principle），将自主AI代理的运行时治理问题归结为持续估计未观测风险上界 B^(x)=U(x)+SB(x)+RG(x) 并仅在观测能力 S(x) 超过该上界时允许行动，基于Aubin可行性理论构建了包含监控(P1)、预测(P2)、单调限制(P3)三个必要属性的Agent Viability Framework，并通过RiskGate系统实现了双通道KL散度漂移检测、分段偏差检测、多步序列治理等统计估计器，以解析方式验证了对已知失败模式的覆盖能力。

> **TL;DR:** The paper proposes the Informational Viability Principle for governing autonomous AI agents: continuously estimate unobserved risk bound B^(x) and allow actions only when observed capacity S(x) exceeds it. The Agent Viability Framework (grounded in Aubin's viability theory) establishes monitoring, anticipation, and monotonic restriction as necessary governance properties, instantiated as RiskGate with dual-channel KL divergence drift detection, segment-vs-rest bias z-tests, and multi-step pattern governance -- all validated analytically against published agent-failure taxonomies, with empirical evaluation scoped as future work.

## 核心贡献

- **信息可行性原则（Informational Viability Principle）**：将自主代理治理问题形式化为持续估计未观测风险上界 B^(x) = U(x) + SB(x) + RG(x)，并强制执行 S(x) - B^(x) >= epsilon 的决策规则，其中U为行为漂移不确定性、SB为结构偏差、RG为序列上下文差距
- **Agent Viability Framework（AVF）**：基于Aubin可行性理论，提出三个治理属性——持续监控(P1)、预测(P2)、单调限制(P3)，并证明它们在B^(x)框架内是各自必要且联合充分的
- **RiskGate参考实现**：包含双通道KL散度漂移检测（带Bonferroni校正）、D-UCB1自适应阈值优化（将阈值选择建模为多臂赌博机）、segment-vs-rest z-test偏差检测、PlanGovernor多步序列模式检测（结构化拆分、账户接管、探测、往返）
- **Autopilot作为调节映射**：将闭环Autopilot形式化为Aubin调节映射的实例 R_A(theta) = {u' <= u_current} union {STOP}，保证调节后的可行性核非空（Theorem 3.1），P3作为Corollary 3.1从映射结构中自然推导而非外部施加
- **可行性指数VI(t)和t*预测**：标量VI(t) in [-1,+1] 操作化 Phi^(x) = S(x) - B^(x)，通过OLS回归预测边界穿越时间t*，被证明是Aubin退出函数tau_exit的一阶保守估计量（Proposition 3.1）

## Key Contributions

- **Informational Viability Principle**: Formalizes autonomous agent governance as continuously estimating unobserved risk bound B^(x) = U(x) + SB(x) + RG(x) and enforcing the decision rule S(x) - B^(x) >= epsilon, where U captures behavioral drift uncertainty, SB structural bias, and RG the sequential context gap
- **Agent Viability Framework (AVF)**: Grounded in Aubin's viability theory, proposes three governance properties -- continuous monitoring (P1), anticipation (P2), and monotonic restriction (P3) -- proving them individually necessary and collectively sufficient within the B^(x) framework
- **RiskGate reference implementation**: Dual-channel KL divergence drift detection (with Bonferroni correction), D-UCB1 adaptive threshold optimization (modeling threshold selection as multi-armed bandit), segment-vs-rest z-test bias detection, and PlanGovernor for multi-step sequential pattern detection (structuring, account takeover, probing, round-tripping)
- **Autopilot as regulation map**: Formalizes the closed-loop Autopilot as an instance of Aubin's regulation map R_A(theta) = {u' <= u_current} union {STOP}, guaranteeing the regulated viability kernel is non-empty (Theorem 3.1); P3 is recovered as Corollary 3.1 from the map structure rather than imposed externally
- **Viability Index VI(t) and t* prediction**: The scalar VI(t) in [-1,+1] operationalizes Phi^(x) = S(x) - B^(x), with OLS-based boundary-crossing time prediction t* proven to be a first-order conservative estimator of Aubin's exit function tau_exit (Proposition 3.1)

## Methodology

The paper develops a three-part methodology: theoretical framework, reference implementation, and analytical validation.

**Theoretical Framework (Informational Viability Principle):**
The core insight reframes agent governance as an estimation problem. Given evaluation context x = (a, theta(t)), the decision rule is: allow action x if and only if S(x) >= B^(x) + epsilon, where S(x) is observed capacity and B^(x) is the estimated bound on unobserved risk. The bound decomposes into three terms requiring distinct statistical instruments: U(x) estimated via KL divergence on sliding windows, SB(x) estimated via segment-vs-rest hypothesis testing, and RG(x) estimated via sequential pattern matching over action histories.

The AVF is grounded in Aubin's viability theory. The behavioral state theta(t) is a 7-tuple of statistical quantities. The viability space V is the set of states satisfying three constraints (safety, fairness, stability) simultaneously. A Viability Index VI(t) in [-1,+1] operationalizes the margin between observed capacity and unobserved risk. Four categories of agent degradation are identified: behavioral drift, adversarial erosion, bias accumulation, and calibration decay -- each mapping to specific B^(x) terms.

**RiskGate Implementation:**
- Bayesian behavioral profiling with Dirichlet conjugate prior and empirical Bayes shrinkage estimates S(x)
- Dual-channel KL divergence (transaction outcomes, window=300; tool calls, window=200) with Monte Carlo calibrated thresholds estimates U(x)
- D-UCB1 (Discounted Upper Confidence Bound) with 4 independent bandits adapts thresholds in non-stationary environments, with asymmetric reward (r_FP = -2.0 vs r_FN = -1.0)
- Segment-vs-rest z-test with autocorrelation correction and Bonferroni multiple testing correction estimates SB(x)
- PlanGovernor detects four sequential attack patterns (structuring, account takeover, probing, round-tripping) to estimate RG(x)
- Monotonic pipeline: D_final = conjunction of all decisory stages, each can only block (never unblock), ensuring P3 by construction (Proposition 6.1)
- Autopilot: tighten-only regulation map with kill-switch-as-last-resort, triggered when VI < 0.3, t* < t_critical, or region = exterior
- Two t* deployment profiles: heuristic (O(W), real-time) and inferential (Newey-West HAC + block bootstrap + non-linearity diagnostic, on-demand)

**Analytical Validation:**
- Coverage mapping against the 11 "Agents of Chaos" failure cases (Shapira et al. 2026), showing which B^(x) components engage for each
- Convergence analysis with Agent Drift measurements (Rath 2026): 847 workflows, 42% task-success reduction, median onset at 73 interactions
- Two end-to-end worked demonstrations: (1) structuring attack where RG(x) is decisive and VI stays interior throughout, and (2) emergent bias attack where SB(x) accumulates silently and the Autopilot intervenes 108 operations before the reactive z-test would fire

## Results & Highlights

- The paper explicitly scopes its contribution as a theoretical framework, reference implementation, and analytical coverage -- **no quantitative empirical evaluation** is provided (detection rates, false positive rates, latencies, and monetary ROI are absent by design)
- The structuring attack demonstration shows that no single B^(x) term detected the attack in isolation: U(x) produced only 0.002 bits (two orders of magnitude below threshold), static rules passed each transfer, and VI remained at 0.913 (Interior) throughout. It was RG(x) via PlanGovernor that provided the decisive block
- The emergent bias demonstration shows the Autopilot's disparity-target intervention at operation 300 -- 157 operations before BOUNDARY entry and 108 operations before the reactive z-test would have fired -- illustrating P2 anticipation in practice
- The aggregate t* = 1219 ops gave a distant prediction when only C2 (fairness) was deteriorating, because C1 and C3 buffered VI -- highlighting a structural limitation of single-scalar anticipation for single-constraint failures
- Coverage against the Agents of Chaos taxonomy ranges from "Low" (agent collaboration, per-agent silos) to "High" (resource exhaustion, denial-of-service), with most cases rated "Partial"
- Convergence with Agent Drift: both frameworks independently selected KL divergence for tool-usage drift detection; Rath reports 81.5% drift reduction with combined strategies (these are Rath's measurements, not RiskGate's)

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The primary novelty lies in the theoretical reframing -- the Informational Viability Principle and its connection to Aubin's viability theory for agent governance. While individual components (KL drift detection, z-test bias detection, sequential pattern matching) are well-established, the unifying B^(x) decomposition and the formalization of the Autopilot as Aubin's regulation map are genuinely new. The necessity proofs (P1-P3) are elegant but largely internal to the framework rather than universally applicable.

**Rigor:** The theoretical development is rigorous, with formal definitions (behavioral state, viability space, regulation map), propositions with proof sketches, and explicit theorem statements. The statistical methodology is sound (Bonferroni correction, Newey-West HAC, block bootstrap). However, several key claims remain as "design principles" or "conjectures" rather than theorems -- the authors are commendably explicit about this. The monotonicity proof under concurrency is informal (Section 6.3) rather than formal.

**Reproducibility:** A Python reference implementation is described but not linked to a public repository. The worked demonstrations provide sufficient mathematical detail to replicate. However, many hyperparameters (kappa=500/300, gamma=0.999, epsilon margins, reward weights) are heuristic defaults requiring domain-specific calibration. Without a public code release or benchmark dataset, full reproducibility is limited.

**Significance:** The paper addresses a genuinely important problem -- runtime governance of autonomous AI agents against behavioral drift, adversarial manipulation, and emergent bias. The EU AI Act alignment is well-motivated. The framework provides a principled evaluation criterion for governance mechanisms. However, the absence of any empirical evaluation significantly limits the practical significance at this stage.

**Red flags:**
- No quantitative empirical results whatsoever -- all demonstrations trace equations under assumed parameters, not measured outcomes
- The three-term B^(x) decomposition is a conjecture, not a theorem; failure modes like deceptive alignment or instrumental convergence may not reduce cleanly to U+SB+RG
- P3 (monotonic restriction) is acknowledged as "the weakest theoretical link" -- it blocks known attack vectors but lacks worst-case adversarial robustness bounds
- The paper is very long (the HTML version is extensive) with significant theoretical apparatus for a system without empirical validation

## Strengths

- Theoretical depth: The connection to Aubin's viability theory gives the framework formal grounding beyond ad hoc heuristics; Proposition 3.1 (t* as first-order estimator of tau_exit) is a genuine contribution
- Intellectual honesty: The paper is remarkably transparent about its limitations, explicitly flagging what it does not prove, what remains conjecture, and what is scoped as future work
- Unifying principle: The B^(x) decomposition provides a principled selection criterion for governance mechanisms -- "which term does this improve, and by how much?"
- Conservative design philosophy: Asymmetric rewards (false positives penalized more than false negatives), monotonic pipeline, kill-switch-as-last-resort all embody fail-safe engineering
- Regulatory alignment: Explicit mapping to EU AI Act Articles 9, 14, and 15
- Worked demonstrations effectively illustrate the structural separation between per-request (U, SB) and plan-level (RG) governance

## Limitations

- **No empirical evaluation**: The paper provides no detection rates, false positive rates, latency measurements, or head-to-head comparisons against baselines on live or synthetic traces -- explicitly acknowledged
- **Conjectural decomposition**: The completeness of B^(x) = U + SB + RG is motivated by failure-mode literature but not formally proven; emergent failure modes (deceptive alignment, goal misgeneralization) may require additional terms
- **P3 is the weakest theoretical link**: The monotonic restriction property blocks known attack vectors but lacks formal adversarial robustness bounds under bounded perturbation
- **Single-scalar anticipation limitation**: The aggregate t* is conservative for single-constraint failures (demonstrated in Section 11.2: aggregate t* = 1219 ops vs. per-constraint crossing at ~481 ops)
- **Over-restriction risk**: P3 prevents autonomous relaxation, meaning over-tightening requires human intervention; D-UCB1 provides only a slow P3-compatible recovery path
- **Concurrency formality gap**: Pipeline monotonicity is proved for sequential evaluation only; the concurrent argument (Section 6.3) is informal with acknowledged residual gaps
- **Cold start problem**: With insufficient data, Dirichlet priors dominate and UCB1 bandits have not converged, making B^(x) estimates imprecise
- **Adversarial robustness**: A sophisticated adversary who understands the KL threshold structure could craft operations staying just below the drift threshold on both channels; the KL partition itself is an operational choice, not derived from first principles
- **Threshold sensitivity**: Many hyperparameters are domain-dependent; the four built-in profiles and Autopilot provide defaults but require domain expertise for calibration
- **t* intervention confounding**: VI(t) depends on tau, so Autopilot tightening drops dC1 even when KL is stable, introducing pessimistic bias into the OLS slope

## Relevance

本文对自主AI代理的运行时治理提出了系统的理论框架，将行为漂移检测、算法公平性和多步序列攻击防御统一在信息可行性原则之下。对于关注AI安全部署、模型行为监控和监管合规（如EU AI Act）的研究者和工程师，该框架提供了一个有原则的治理设计准则和可操作的统计工具链。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does RiskGate perform empirically against stateless policy engines (OPA, Cedar) and supervised baselines on synthetic or production agent operation traces, measured by detection rate, false positive rate, and latency?
2. Can the three-term B^(x) decomposition be extended to cover emergent failure modes like deceptive alignment or instrumental convergence, or do these require fundamentally different detection mechanisms?
3. What are the formal adversarial robustness bounds for B^(x) estimation under bounded perturbation, and how does the system degrade under a partition-aware adversary who crafts operations to remain below KL thresholds on all monitored channels?
4. How would a per-constraint t* variant (fitting OLS on individual d_Ci series rather than the aggregate VI) improve prediction accuracy for single-constraint failure modes, and at what computational cost?
5. What is the quantitative impact of the Autopilot's over-restriction bias on legitimate operation throughput in production deployments, and how quickly does D-UCB1 recover from over-tightening?
