> **一句话总结：** 本文将残差网络重构为逐层逼近过程，从理论上证明了误差随深度单调递减的"渐进逼近轨迹"的存在性，并提出逐层渐进逼近（LPA）训练范式，使得单次训练即可在任意深度输出有效预测，实现"训练一次、部署N个模型"的高效推理。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proves the existence of monotonic progressive approximation trajectories in deep residual networks, where error decreases with depth, and proposes Layer-wise Progressive Approximation (LPA) -- an architecture-agnostic training paradigm that supervises all layers via a shared readout, enabling "train once, use N models" depth-adaptive inference.

## 核心贡献

- **理论证明渐进逼近轨迹的存在性：** 将残差网络展开为逐层逼近过程，严格证明了存在一类使得逼近误差随深度单调不增的轨迹（Theorem 3.3），将经典UAT从整体逼近扩展到逐层逼近。
- **提出LPA训练范式：** 基于理论推导，设计了通过共享线性读出层对所有中间层施加全局监督信号的训练方法，确保每个残差块逼近其对应的残差目标。
- **架构无关性验证：** 在残差FNN、ResNet、ViT和Qwen2-0.5B等LLM上，跨越函数拟合、图像分类、NLP生成与分类任务，均观察到渐进逼近行为。
- **"训练一次，使用N个模型"：** LPA训练的网络在任意中间层即可产生有意义的预测，支持无需重训练或蒸馏的深度自适应推理。

## Key Contributions

- **Existence proof of progressive trajectories:** The paper formally proves that in residual networks, there exist sequences of residual mappings where the uniform approximation error monotonically decreases with depth, extending the Universal Approximation Theorem to a layer-wise formulation.
- **Layer-wise Progressive Approximation (LPA):** A principled training paradigm that applies global supervision at every intermediate layer through a shared learnable linear readout, aligning each layer with its residual target.
- **Architecture-agnostic validation:** Demonstrates progressive behavior across residual FNNs, ResNets, Vision Transformers, and the Qwen2-0.5B LLM on tasks spanning function regression, image classification, and NLP generation/classification.
- **Depth-adaptive deployment:** A single LPA-trained network yields useful predictions at every depth, enabling shallow inference without retraining or knowledge distillation.

## Methodology

The paper develops its framework in three stages:

**1. Residual Network Unfolding and Error Trajectory (Section 3):** The authors unroll the residual recurrence x_i = x_{i-1} + G_i(x_{i-1}) into the cumulative form x_N = x_0 + sum of G_i(x_{i-1}). They define the "error trajectory" as the sequence of approximation errors epsilon_i = ||f(x_0) - x_i|| at each layer. Under standard end-to-end (E2E) training, this trajectory is generally non-monotonic since intermediate errors receive no direct supervision.

**2. Progressive Trajectory Existence Proof (Theorem 3.3):** Using an inductive construction grounded in the UAT, the authors prove that for any continuous target function f on a compact domain, there exists a sequence of residual mappings {G_i} such that epsilon_1 >= epsilon_2 >= ... >= epsilon_N >= 0. The key insight is that at each layer, the residual target f_i(x_0) = f(x_0) - x_{i-1} is continuous on a compact set, and UAT guarantees that a sufficiently wide FNN can approximate it to arbitrary precision.

**3. LPA Training (Section 4):** To realize these trajectories in practice, LPA introduces a shared learnable linear readout matrix W_{N+1} that projects all intermediate representations into the output space. The loss aggregates supervision from all layers: L_LPA = sum of lambda_i * loss(hat_f_i, f(x_0)). The shared readout ensures all layer-wise objectives are coupled in the same output space, preserving the progressive refinement property even when hidden dimension differs from output dimension.

The authors also provide a rigorous treatment showing that both CNNs (via convolution-as-matrix-vector conversion) and Transformers (via vectorization with Kronecker products for attention operations) can be expressed within the residual formulation, establishing the generality of the framework.

## Results & Highlights

**Complex Surface Fitting (8 functions):**
- LPA achieves 1.1x to 13.1x MSE improvement over E2E across all 8 scientific functions.
- LPA produces monotonically decreasing layer-wise MSE, while E2E exhibits fluctuating errors.

**Image Classification (CIFAR-10/100, Fashion-MNIST):**
- LPA matches E2E final accuracy across ViT-12/24 and ResNet-18/34.
- LPA achieves dramatically higher intermediate-layer accuracy: e.g., ViT-24 LPA reaches 80%+ top-1 accuracy by layer 14 on ImageNet, while E2E remains below 50% until layer 22.

**ImageNet Scalability:**
- ViT-24 with LPA achieves comparable final accuracy (~79.8%) while surpassing 80% top-1 by layer 14.
- Fine-tuning pretrained ViT-Base/Small with LPA yields strong intermediate accuracy (e.g., 69.95% at layer 10 for ViT-Base vs. 50.15% for E2E).

**LLM Tasks (Qwen2-0.5B):**
- Classification (GLUE): LPA shows clear monotonic accuracy increase across layers; E2E fluctuates.
- Generation (Wikitext, Alpaca, Dolly-15k, GSM8K): LPA achieves substantially lower perplexity at shallow depths, with smooth progressive refinement. E2E concentrates improvement only at the final layer.

**Overhead:** LPA adds negligible computational cost (~same epoch time as E2E) since it only reuses the shared readout head at intermediate layers.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Moderate-High):**
The core theoretical contribution -- proving the existence of monotonic progressive trajectories in residual networks -- is genuinely novel and provides a fresh perspective on how residual networks approximate functions layer-by-layer, extending the classical UAT. However, the practical training method (LPA) is essentially a form of deep supervision with a shared readout head, which has significant overlap with prior work on intermediate supervision and early-exit classifiers. The paper does acknowledge related prior art but positions LPA as theoretically grounded rather than heuristic.

**Rigor (Moderate-High):**
The proofs are constructed carefully with proper use of compactness, continuity, and the UAT. The inductive proof of Theorem 3.3 is straightforward but mathematically sound. The conversion of Transformer operations to matrix-vector form via Kronecker products is thorough. However, some claims rely on informal arguments -- for instance, the "representation consistency" in Appendix F.3.1 is argued as an emergent property of optimization rather than formally proved. The Transformer UAT argument depends on the assumption that training prevents representation collapse, which is plausible but not rigorously established.

**Reproducibility (Moderate):**
Full hyperparameters and experimental details are provided in Appendix I. However, code is not yet publicly available. The LPA implementation itself appears straightforward (shared readout + per-layer loss), so reproducibility should be feasible. The LLM experiments use only Qwen2-0.5B, which is relatively small.

**Significance (Moderate-High):**
The "train once, use N models" paradigm has clear practical value for edge deployment and adaptive inference. The theoretical framework provides a principled justification for deep supervision. The breadth of validation (FNNs, CNNs, ViTs, LLMs) strengthens the generality claim. However, the improvements in final accuracy are marginal (often matching E2E rather than beating it), suggesting the main value is in intermediate-layer quality rather than final performance.

**Red Flags:**
- Code is not yet available ("upon acceptance").
- The theoretical contribution is an existence proof, not a constructive algorithm -- it does not guarantee that SGD-based training converges to progressive trajectories.
- LPA's main benefit is intermediate-layer quality, not final accuracy gains, which may limit practical adoption.
- LLM experiments are limited to a single small model (0.5B parameters) and relatively simple fine-tuning setups.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical framework that bridges classical approximation theory with modern deep learning practice.
- Comprehensive empirical validation across diverse architectures (FNN, CNN, Transformer, LLM) and tasks.
- The shared readout design is simple yet theoretically motivated, adding minimal overhead.
- Strong intermediate-layer performance enables practical depth-adaptive inference.
- The paper unifies the analysis of residual FNNs, CNNs, and Transformers under a single mathematical framework.

## Limitations

- The existence proof does not address convergence: it shows progressive trajectories exist but does not guarantee gradient descent finds them.
- Final accuracy improvements are minimal -- LPA primarily improves intermediate representations, not the final output.
- LLM experiments are limited in scale (Qwen2-0.5B only, 1 epoch fine-tuning), leaving open questions about applicability to larger models and full pre-training.
- The weight scheduling (lambda_i = 0.1 for early layers, 1.0 for final layer) is heuristic and not theoretically derived.
- For generation tasks, LPA supervision is only applied to the last 5 layers, which is a significant departure from the full-layer supervision claimed in theory.
- The paper does not compare against established early-exit methods or knowledge distillation baselines.

## Relevance

本文将UAT扩展到逐层渐进逼近的理论框架，对理解残差网络内部表示演化提供了新视角。LPA训练范式的"训练一次、深度自适应推理"特性对边缘部署和计算资源受限场景具有实际意义，其理论分析对深度学习可解释性研究也有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does LPA training converge to progressive trajectories under gradient descent, or is the existence proof merely an asymptotic guarantee? What optimization conditions (learning rate, width, depth) promote convergence to progressive trajectories?
- How does LPA scale to LLM pre-training (not just fine-tuning) with models in the 7B-70B parameter range? Is the computational overhead still negligible at that scale, and do progressive trajectories emerge naturally?
- Can the progressive trajectory property be used to dynamically determine optimal network depth during training (e.g., early stopping at a layer that already achieves target accuracy)?
- How does LPA interact with other efficiency techniques like pruning, quantization, or mixture-of-experts? Can progressive trajectories be preserved under model compression?
- What is the theoretical relationship between LPA's shared readout and the factorization continuity theorem -- does the shared readout impose any constraints on the expressivity of intermediate representations?
