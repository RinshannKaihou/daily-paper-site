> **一句话总结：** DPRM通过将Doob h变换的过程奖励机制引入扩散语言模型的token揭示顺序决策中，从置信度驱动的渐进排序出发并在线地过渡到奖励引导排序，在自然语言预训练、推理后训练、测试时扩展以及蛋白质/单细胞/分子/DNA等科学领域的掩码扩散任务中均展现出一致或显著的性能提升。

> **TL;DR:** DPRM is a plug-in token-ordering module for diffusion language models that replaces confidence-based token reveal ordering with an online Doob h-transform process-reward guidance. It keeps the host architecture, denoising objective, and data pipeline unchanged, modifying only the ordering policy. Theoretical guarantees include O(1/N) convergence of the Soft-BoN approximation, empirical-Bernstein-rate online tracking, and exponential sample-complexity advantages over both random and confidence-only ordering. Evaluated across 7 host systems (PUMA pretraining, DMPO post-training, Prism test-time scaling, DPLM protein, DCM single-cell, GenMol molecular, SDPO DNA), DPRM consistently improves harder reasoning subsets (e.g., MATH Hard +8.1%, Countdown Hard +12.8%) and produces multi-objective trade-offs in scientific domains. The paper identifies token ordering as a fundamental and reusable control axis in diffusion language models.

## 核心贡献

- 提出DPRM（Doob h-transform Process Reward Model），一个即插即用的token排序模块，仅替换扩散语言模型中的token揭示顺序策略，不改变宿主架构、去噪目标或数据管线。
- 理论上刻画了精确DPRM策略为奖励倾斜的Gibbs揭示律，证明了Soft-BoN近似的O(1/N)收敛速率、在线分桶控制器在经验Bernstein速率下跟踪精确DPRM分数，以及在可处理假设下的指数级样本复杂度优势。
- 设计了两阶段渐进式在线DPRM：第一阶段使用置信度驱动的渐进排序保证训练-测试对齐，第二阶段逐步过渡到Doob h变换过程奖励引导的排序。
- 在7个宿主系统上进行了系统性评估（自然语言预训练/后训练/测试时扩展、蛋白质逆折叠、单细胞基因表达、分子药物设计、DNA调控序列），覆盖语言和科学两大领域。
- 实验揭示token排序是扩散语言模型中的基础控制轴，在困难推理子集上效果尤为显著，科学领域则呈现多目标权衡特性。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces DPRM, a plug-in token-ordering module that modifies only the reveal-order policy in diffusion language models while keeping everything else fixed, based on Doob h-transform process-reward guidance.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: characterizes the exact DPRM policy as a reward-tilted Gibbs reveal law; proves O(1/N) Soft-BoN convergence; establishes empirical-Bernstein-rate online tracking; and shows exponential sample-complexity advantage over random and confidence-only ordering under tractable assumptions.
- Designs a two-stage progressive online DPRM controller: Stage 1 uses confidence-driven progressive ordering for train-test alignment; Stage 2 gradually shifts to reward-guided ordering via online bucketized estimates.
- Conducts a comprehensive plug-in evaluation across 7 host systems spanning natural language (pretraining, post-training, test-time scaling) and scientific domains (protein, single-cell, molecular, DNA).
- Demonstrates that token ordering is a fundamental control axis in diffusion language models, with particularly strong gains on harder reasoning subsets and multi-objective trade-offs in scientific generation tasks.

## Methodology

DPRM is grounded in the classical Doob h-transform. For a diffusion language model with a base proposal distribution q_0 over candidate token positions, and a terminal reward R(X_T), DPRM defines a space-time harmonic function h_t(s) = E[exp(beta * R(X_T)) | S_t = s] and tilts the base transition by h_{t+1}/h_t, yielding the exact reward-tilted Gibbs reveal law.

Since evaluating the exact process reward for every candidate at every step is expensive, DPRM uses an online bucketized approximation:
1. **Confidence buckets and phases:** Candidate positions are binned by their base confidence score and the current denoising phase. Each (phase, bucket) cell maintains running statistics N_{phi,b} (count) and S_{phi,b} (accumulated exp(beta * R)).
2. **Log-moment reward estimator:** The process reward estimate is R_hat_{phi,b} = (1/beta) * log(S_{phi,b} / max(N_{phi,b}, 1)).
3. **DPRM-corrected score:** For each candidate i, the score is g_i = log(psi_i) + beta * R_hat_{phi,b_i}.
4. **Warmup gating:** A smooth gate eta_t interpolates between confidence score and DPRM score, controlled by a global transition schedule and per-bucket readiness.
5. **Soft-BoN shortlist:** A small subset of candidates is sampled from the base proposal before reranking, avoiding full evaluation.

The controller starts with confidence-driven progressive ordering (aligned with PUMA-style teacher-forced training) and gradually shifts to the DPRM guidance as bucket statistics become reliable. The same ordering rule is used at both training and test time, maintaining train-test alignment throughout.

## Results & Highlights

**Natural Language:**
- DPRM-PUMA improves GSM8K validation mean from 29.34 to 34.27 (+16.8%) in pretraining, with paired-bootstrap deltas excluding zero at 95% confidence.
- DMPO-DPRM improves MATH Hard from 44.3 to 47.9 (+8.1%) and Countdown Hard from 29.6 to 33.4 (+12.8%); gains concentrate on harder and OOD subsets with larger sampling budgets (pass@32).
- DPRM-Prism improves GSM8K voted accuracy from 82.41 to 83.85 (+1.44pp) under the same search scaffold, with consistent improvement across all rank positions.

**Scientific Domains:**
- DPLM ordering variants reduce forward-folding RMSD from 35.47 to 29.43 (-17.0%) and improve TM-score by +8.1%, though co-generation metrics reveal trade-offs.
- DPRM-DCM improves token recovery from 63.97% to 75.92% and zero-expression accuracy from 78.39% to 99.90% on single-cell data.
- DPRM-GenMol improves linker validity from 0.142 to 0.429 and scaffold quality from 0.429 to 0.712 in molecular generation.
- DPRM-SDPO partially counteracts confidence-induced collapse of ATAC and k-mer quality in DNA design while improving HepG2 expression scores.

**Instrumented diagnostics** on Countdown support the theoretical assumptions: confidence bins strongly correlate with local CE loss (r = -0.94 to -0.97), DPRM increases selected-token mass in low-confidence regions, and gains grow with difficulty and sampling budget.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The core idea of applying Doob h-transform to token ordering in diffusion language models is conceptually clean and well-motivated. However, the Doob h-transform itself is classical, and the online bucketized approximation is a standard importance-sampling trick. The primary novelty lies in identifying token ordering as a first-class control variable and rigorously instantiating the theory for this specific problem.

**Rigor:** The theoretical analysis is thorough, with four levels of guarantees (alignment preservation, online tracking at empirical-Bernstein rates, O(1/N) Soft-BoN convergence, finite-sample exponential separation). The assumptions (PL condition, confidence as gradient proxy, residual families) are stated clearly but remain indirect -- Appendix B.10 provides supporting diagnostics via CE loss and gradient proxies rather than direct verification. The exponential separation results depend on assumptions (C.25, C.26, C.31) whose empirical validity is only partially corroborated.

**Reproducibility:** Code is available. Hyperparameters, evaluation protocols, and statistical methods are documented extensively across 8 appendix tables. However, the sheer scope (7 host systems) makes full reproduction resource-intensive. Some experiments use single seeds (e.g., Prism), limiting the strength of statistical claims.

**Significance:** The paper makes a compelling case that token ordering matters in diffusion LMs, supported by a breadth of experiments. The gains on harder reasoning subsets are meaningful. However, the scientific-domain results are genuinely multi-objective with no uniform dominance, which tempers the generality of the improvement claim. The NFE overhead of DPRM-Prism (1.76x) is non-trivial.

**Red flags:**
- The GSM8K and MATH DPRM-DMPO deltas are positive but not separated from zero at 95% in single-seed paired bootstrap; only Countdown is clearly significant.
- The protein experiment shows confidence-progressive ordering outperforming DPRM on key co-generation metrics, suggesting DPRM's generic utility may not be sufficient for all domains.
- The paper's scope is very ambitious (7 hosts, 50 pages), which creates a risk of breadth over depth in any single evaluation.

## Strengths

- Clean plug-in design principle: change only the ordering controller, keep everything else fixed, enabling controlled ablations.
- Strong theoretical foundation with multiple convergence and sample-complexity guarantees.
- Comprehensive evaluation across diverse domains with rigorous statistical uncertainty reporting (paired bootstrap, per-question analysis).
- Honest about limitations: multi-objective trade-offs in scientific domains are acknowledged clearly, and the paper avoids claiming uniform dominance.
- Good empirical diagnostics (Appendix B.10) probing the theoretical assumptions with instrumented training runs.

## Limitations

- The online bucketized estimator uses coarse discretization (phase + confidence bin), which introduces abstraction bias that is bundled but not individually controlled.
- Single-seed comparisons for several key experiments (Prism, GenMol, SDPO) limit statistical certainty.
- DPRM does not uniformly dominate confidence-based ordering; on protein co-generation, confidence alone is stronger on some metrics.
- The NFE overhead (1.76x for Prism) may be prohibitive in latency-sensitive settings.
- The theoretical sample-complexity advantages rely on assumptions (e.g., confidence as gradient proxy, residual families with positive DPRM score gaps) that are only indirectly validated.
- Scientific-domain results require domain-specific reward design to fully realize potential; the generic utility used in this work is not always aligned with domain objectives.

## Relevance

本文对关注扩散语言模型（DLM/DLLM）训练范式、推理能力提升以及离散扩散在科学领域应用的研究者具有高度相关性。DPRM提出的token排序控制视角为理解扩散语言模型的训练动态提供了新维度，其过程奖励引导机制与强化学习后训练（如GRPO、DMPO）的思路互补，值得在LLaDA、LLaMA等大规模扩散语言模型上进一步验证。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can DPRM's online bucketized estimator be replaced or augmented with a learned process reward model (neural network) for finer-grained token-level guidance?
2. How does DPRM scale to larger diffusion language models (e.g., 8B+ parameters) and longer generation sequences (e.g., 2K+ tokens)?
3. What is the interaction between DPRM ordering and other inference-time techniques like best-of-N sampling, speculative decoding, or KV-cache acceleration for diffusion LMs?
4. Can the multi-objective trade-offs observed in protein and molecular generation be resolved by using domain-specific multi-objective utilities instead of the generic AAR-based estimator?
5. Does DPRM's exponential sample-complexity advantage over random ordering persist in practice at the scale of modern pretraining runs, or is it primarily a theoretical statement about finite-sample efficiency?
