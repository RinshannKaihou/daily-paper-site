The Chameleon's Limit: Investigating Persona Collapse and Homogenization in Large Language Models

arXiv: 2604.24698v1

Date: 2026-04-27

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24698v1

一句话总结： 本文提出"人格坍缩"(Persona Collapse)概念并设计几何诊断框架(Coverage/Uniformity/Complexity)，评估10个大模型在人格模拟、道德推理和自我介绍三项任务中的种群级行为多样性，发现所有模型均存在结构性坍缩——高保真度的模型反而产出最刻板化的种群，且坍缩模式跨任务可完全逆转。
TL;DR: This paper introduces "Persona Collapse" -- a pervasive failure mode where LLM agents with distinct personas converge into homogeneous behavior. Using a geometric diagnostic framework (Coverage, Uniformity, Complexity) across 10 LLMs on personality simulation, moral reasoning, and self-introduction, the authors find that all models exhibit structural collapse, that the highest-fidelity models produce the most stereotyped populations, and that collapse profiles can reverse entirely across behavioral domains.

核心贡献

  • 提出"人格坍缩"(Persona Collapse)概念：即使给LLM agent分配了截然不同的详细角色设定(26个维度)，它们仍会收敛到狭窄的行为模式，产生同质化的模拟种群。
  • 设计了基于几何的诊断框架，从三个维度量化种群级行为多样性：Coverage(覆盖人类行为空间的比例)、Uniformity(分布均匀性，Hopkins统计量)、Complexity(局部固有维度LID)。
  • 引入项级诊断(Item-Level Diagnostics)定位坍缩机制：有效响应范围、方差分解、人口统计聚类、属性截断分析。
  • 发现"保真度陷阱"(Fidelity Trap)：在人格模拟任务中忠实度最高(rho>0.9)的模型，其Cohen's d远超人类正常范围(>6 vs 人类中d=2即为"非常大")，本质是在制造刻板印象。
  • 揭示坍缩是跨任务可逆转的：CoSER-Llama-8B在人格任务上最坍缩(EffL=1.36)，但在道德推理上最多样化(EffL=4.27)；单一任务评估可能得出方向性错误的结论。
  • 发现普遍的属性截断层级：所有模型在自我介绍中保留属性的优先级为 Gender(91%) >> Country(90%) >> Political(62%) >> Age(36%) >> Social Class(27%)，社会经济背景被系统性丢弃。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces the concept of Persona Collapse: a structural failure where LLM agents assigned distinct, multi-dimensional personas converge into homogeneous behavioral patterns at the population level.
  • Proposes a geometric diagnostic framework grounded in the Behavioral Trait Matrix B in R^{NxD}, measuring three axes: Coverage (fraction of human archetypes reached), Uniformity (Hopkins statistic), and Complexity (Local Intrinsic Dimensionality).
  • Provides item-level diagnostics to localize collapse mechanisms: effective response range, variance decomposition, demographic clustering (eta-squared), and attribute truncation via incremental R-squared.
  • Uncovers the Fidelity Trap: models achieving the highest per-persona fidelity (rho > 0.9) consistently produce the most demographically stereotyped populations (Cohen's d > 6, far exceeding the human threshold of d = 2).
  • Demonstrates domain reversal: collapse profiles can reverse entirely across tasks (e.g., CoSER-Llama-8B is the most collapsed on personality but the most diverse on moral reasoning), making single-task evaluations actively misleading.
  • Identifies a universal attribute truncation hierarchy: Gender (91%) >> Country (90%) >> Political (62%) >> Age (36%) >> Social Class (27%) in self-introduction mention rates, revealing systematic socioeconomic underrepresentation.

Methodology

The paper formalizes population-level persona evaluation through the Behavioral Trait Matrix B in R^{NxD}, where each row represents one persona's response profile across D behavioral items.

Three diagnostic axes:

  • Coverage (Naeem et al., 2020): Fraction of human reference neighborhoods containing at least one model-generated point, estimated via k-nearest-neighbor hyperspheres (k=5). Uses Twin-2K-500 human BFI-44 responses (n=2,058) as reference.
  • Uniformity (Hopkins statistic): Compares nearest-neighbor distances of random probe points vs. real data points. H approx 0.5 indicates natural randomness; H near 1 indicates clustering, H near 0 indicates over-regular lattice-like spacing.
  • Complexity (Local Intrinsic Dimensionality, LID): MLE estimator over k-nearest neighbors capturing the rate at which local neighborhood volume grows with radius. High LID = genuinely high-dimensional variation; low LID = variation compressed onto low-dimensional subspace.

Item-level diagnostics: Effective Likert range (inverse Simpson index), variance decomposition (sigma_model^2 / sigma_human^2), demographic clustering (eta-squared per demographic variable), incremental R-squared for attribute truncation analysis.

Experimental setup:

  • 10 LLMs evaluated: general-purpose (Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen3-4B/30B-A3B/32B, Claude-Haiku-4.5, MiniMax-M2) and role-play-specialized (CoSER-Llama-8B, CoSER-Qwen-32B, HER-32B, MiniMax-M2-Her).
  • 1,144 personas constructed from 26 dimensions (demographics, psychographics, individualized traits), filtered from 2,000 initial samples.
  • Three behavioral instruments: BFI-44 personality (44 items, 5-point Likert), Moral Reasoning (131 ethical scenarios, 5-point scale), and open-ended self-introduction (3 samples per persona at temperature 0.9).
  • Controlled training pipeline comparisons: Qwen3-32B (base) -> CoSER-Qwen-32B (SFT) -> HER-32B (SFT+RL), and MiniMax-M2 (base) vs. MiniMax-M2-Her (RL).

Results & Highlights

  • No model simultaneously achieves high coverage and high complexity. All models fall far from the human reference (Cov=1.0, LID=14.4). The closest is Qwen3-4B (Cov=0.80, LID=7.3), but it achieves breadth without structural depth.
  • Three failure modes identified: (1) Mode collapse (CoSER-Llama-8B: 83.7% midpoint responses, EffL=1.36); (2) Shallow coverage (Qwen3-4B: wide but flat); (3) Deep but misaligned (MiniMax-M2-Her: LID=22.3 exceeds human, but Coverage=0.06).
  • RL training increases geometric complexity but decreases human alignment. Comparing MiniMax-M2 to MiniMax-M2-Her: LID jumps 6.5->22.3, but Coverage collapses 0.55->0.06 and fidelity drops rho=0.95->0.41.
  • Thinking mode has no effect on persona collapse. Qwen3-32B in thinking vs. non-thinking mode produces identical item-level metrics, suggesting collapse resides in model weights, not the reasoning chain.
  • The Fidelity Trap: Every model with rho > 0.9 produces d > 6 (MiniMax-M2 reaches d=15.7). High fidelity is achieved by pushing persona groups to opposite extremes -- effectively caricature manufacturing.
  • Universal truncation hierarchy in self-introductions: All models preserve gender and nationality while systematically discarding social class and age. No model mentions social class in more than 43% of introductions.
  • Template homogenization: Claude-Haiku-4.5 achieves the highest attribute mention rate (73%) and intra-persona consistency (ICC=0.43), yet 29% of responses share an identical template skeleton ("# A Proper Introduction").
  • Domain reversal is stark: CoSER-Llama-8B is the most degenerate on personality (Cov=0.16, EffL=1.36, Hop=0.91) but the most diverse on moral reasoning (EffL=4.27, LID=45.3, Hop=0.48).

Critical Assessment

Novelty: High. The paper shifts evaluation from individual-level persona fidelity (the prevailing paradigm) to population-level geometric diagnostics. The "Fidelity Trap" finding -- that high per-persona fidelity correlates with population-level stereotyping -- is genuinely counter-intuitive and important. The three-axis framework (Coverage/Uniformity/Complexity) borrowed from generative modeling is a clean and well-motivated transplant.

Rigor: Very strong. The experimental design includes controlled comparisons (same base model with/without SFT, with/without RL), multiple behavioral instruments spanning structured and open-ended formats, a human reference distribution for normative comparison, and extensive item-level diagnostics that go beyond aggregate metrics. The appendix is thorough (10 sections). The finding that thinking mode does not affect collapse is a valuable negative result.

Reproducibility: Good. Code and data are released. Models are accessed via OpenRouter API with default parameters. The 26 persona dimensions and prompt templates are fully specified. However, some models (MiniMax-M2-Her, HER-32B) have less transparent training procedures, and the persona filtering criteria (856 excluded out of 2,000) could benefit from more detail.

Significance: High. This work has direct implications for multi-agent simulation, synthetic data generation, and any application relying on LLM population diversity. The finding that single-task evaluation can be "directionally wrong" challenges current evaluation practices.

Red flags:

  • The keyword-based attribute mention analysis is acknowledged as conservative; some claimed truncation patterns may be artifacts of the detection method.
  • The human reference distribution (Twin-2K-500) is limited to BFI-44; no human baseline exists for moral reasoning or self-introduction, making model-to-model comparisons harder to calibrate.
  • The persona filtering process (43% exclusion rate) may introduce selection bias that affects generalizability.
  • All experiments use a single persona prompting format; sensitivity to prompt engineering is not tested.

Strengths

  • The geometric framework is conceptually elegant, well-grounded in existing metrics from generative modeling, and provides interpretable diagnostics at both population and item levels.
  • The Fidelity Trap finding is both surprising and practically important -- it directly challenges the assumption that better persona adherence means better simulation.
  • The domain reversal finding is a strong argument for multi-domain evaluation and should change how the field evaluates persona agents.
  • The analysis of training pipeline effects (base -> SFT -> RL) provides actionable insights for model developers.
  • Comprehensive appendix with full metric tables, qualitative examples, and detailed methodology.

Limitations

  • Keyword-based detection underestimates implicit attribute expression; social class may be conveyed through occupation descriptions or lifestyle references not captured by the lexicon.
  • Only English-language evaluation; persona collapse may manifest differently in multilingual settings.
  • The persona population (1,144 from 26 dimensions) samples only a subset of the combinatorial attribute space; rare trait combinations are underrepresented.
  • No evaluation of how persona collapse affects downstream multi-agent system outcomes (e.g., debate quality, negotiation results).
  • The framework evaluates static behavioral responses rather than dynamic, multi-turn interactions where persona collapse may accumulate over time.
  • RL-based role-play models (MiniMax-M2-Her, HER-32B) have opaque training details, limiting mechanistic interpretation.

Relevance

本文对多智能体系统和社会模拟研究具有直接意义。如果你关注LLM agent的种群多样性、人格保真度评估、或基于LLM的社会模拟，这项工作提供了一个系统性诊断框架和关键的实证发现：现有模型在种群级模拟中存在结构性缺陷，单纯提高个体保真度反而会加剧种群同质化。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does persona collapse propagate in multi-turn, multi-agent interactions? Does it accumulate or stabilize over conversational depth?
  • Can training objectives that explicitly reward within-group behavioral variance (rather than prototype matching) mitigate the Fidelity Trap?
  • What is the effect of pre-alignment base models on persona collapse? Would unaligned models produce more diverse (if less coherent) populations?
  • How does persona collapse manifest in non-English, cross-cultural, or multilingual persona simulation?
  • Can the geometric diagnostic framework be adapted to detect persona collapse in real-time during multi-agent system deployment?
  • What role does the persona prompt format play? Would narrative-style vs. structured attribute listings produce different collapse profiles?
  • Do smaller, specialized persona models outperform larger general-purpose models on population-level diversity metrics?
