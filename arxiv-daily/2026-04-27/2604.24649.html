Déjà Vu Packing: Optimizing FPGA Logic Clustering Runtime via Pattern Memoization

arXiv: 2604.24649v1

Date: 2026-04-27

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24649v1

一句话总结： 本文提出Déjà Vu Packing方法，利用新颖的打包签名树(PST)数据结构对FPGA逻辑聚类中的重复打包模式进行记忆化，跳过冗余的合法化路由检查，在AMD 7系列和Stratix 10架构上分别实现平均3.7倍和6.9倍的打包加速，且不改变结果质量。
TL;DR: FPGA packing (clustering technology-mapped primitives into logic blocks) is the dominant runtime bottleneck for modern commercial FPGA architectures, consuming 58-94% of the VPR CAD flow. The key insight is that 59-65% of packed clusters are repetitions of previously seen patterns. The authors introduce a Packing Signature Tree (PST) that memoizes cluster legality check outcomes, enabling redundant intracluster routing checks to be skipped. This yields up to 29.3x packing speedup (6.9x average on Stratix 10, 3.7x on 7-series) and up to 5.3x end-to-end VPR speedup, with zero quality degradation. The technique is exact (no heuristics), low-overhead (PST insertion/lookup costs ~0.8% of packing time, memory overhead ~27% for the packer but negligible for the full flow), and has been integrated into the open-source VTR toolchain.

核心贡献

  • 揭示了FPGA打包阶段的运行时瓶颈：在现代商用FPGA架构（AMD 7系列和Altera Stratix 10）上，打包阶段分别占VPR端到端运行时间的58%和94%，其中约90%的时间消耗在非法聚类的片内路由合法化检查上
  • 提出打包签名树（PST）数据结构，将聚类打包模式编码为树中的路径，实现O(1)近似时间的模式等价性检测和合法化结果记忆化
  • 开发了一个忠实于商用Stratix 10逻辑块（LB）内部结构的VTR架构模型，包括50%稀疏化本地交叉开关、4个触发器/LE及复杂连接模式
  • 在68个基准测试上验证，打包和端到端VPR运行时间分别平均减少5.1倍和2.9倍，且完全不改变结果质量
  • 已集成到VTR开源工具中，通过命令行参数 --memoize_cluster_packings {on|off} 启用

Key Contributions

  • Runtime bottleneck analysis: Demonstrates that packing constitutes 58% (7-series) and 94% (Stratix 10) of VPR end-to-end runtime, with ~90% of packing time spent on intracluster routing legality checks for illegal clusters during detailed packing.
  • Packing Signature Tree (PST): A novel tree-based data structure that encodes cluster packing patterns as paths, with Location and Connectivity Nodes (LCNs) for each packed molecule and External Connectivity Nodes (ECNs) at path tails storing legality outcomes. Enables near-constant-time pattern matching.
  • Faithful Stratix 10 architecture model: Creates a VTR architecture file that accurately captures the commercial Stratix 10 LB, including a 50% depopulated local crossbar, four FFs per LE (up from two), and complex inter-LE connectivity constraints -- details previously omitted because they made packing intractably slow.
  • Significant runtime improvements without quality loss: Up to 29.3x packing speedup and 5.3x end-to-end VPR speedup (geometric means: 3.7x and 6.9x packing speedup for 7-series and Stratix 10 respectively). The approach is exact -- no heuristics, identical packing solutions.
  • Open-source integration: Integrated into VTR's VPR tool; the faithful Stratix 10 architecture file has been contributed to the VTR repository.

Methodology

The paper follows a clear problem-analysis-solution structure:

  1. Baseline characterization: The authors instrument VPR with telemetry code to profile packing runtime. They target two architectures: the AMD 7-series VTR capture and a newly created faithful Stratix 10 capture. They show that (a) speculative packing fails for 51-89% of clusters (requiring expensive detailed packing), and (b) ~90% of packing time is spent routing illegal cluster candidates during detailed packing. Worst-case individual clusters invoke the intracluster router up to 12,133 times on Stratix 10.
  1. Packing Signature Tree design: The PST exploits the deterministic nature of greedy seed-based packing. Identical substructures in the netlist receive identical affinity scores, leading to equivalent packing patterns. Each path from root to leaf represents a unique packing signature. LCNs store placement locations and internal connections; ECNs at path tails store external connectivity and legality outcomes. Key design choices include: (a) not merging logically equivalent pins to avoid non-deterministic router behavior, and (b) retaining illegal-pattern ECNs in the tree so the packer can skip known-bad patterns without invoking the router.
  1. Déjà Vu packing integration: Speculative packing proceeds normally, generating a single ECN at completion. If speculative packing fails and detailed packing begins, an ECN is created for every molecule added, storing the routing legality result. Before invoking the router, the packer first checks whether a matching ECN already exists in the PST. If so, the cached result is used; if not, the router runs and the result is memoized.
  1. Experimental setup: 68 benchmarks from VTR, Koios, Titan23, and Titanium25 suites. Intel Xeon w9-3495 with 1TB RAM. All results averaged over 3 seeds. Compared against baseline VPR (commit 7c0ee8a6).

Results & Highlights

  • 7-series architecture: Packing speedup ranges from 1.3x to 13.4x (geometric mean: 3.7x). End-to-end VPR speedup: 1.1x to 3.0x (geometric mean: 1.6x). The longest benchmark (dla_like.large) reduced from 7.7 hours to 5.8 hours.
  • Stratix 10 architecture: Packing speedup ranges from 1.3x to 29.3x (geometric mean: 6.9x). End-to-end VPR speedup: 1.1x to 14.6x (geometric mean: 5.3x). The longest benchmark (bitcoin_miner, previously 76.8 hours) reduced to ~6.8 hours.
  • Pattern repetition: On average, 59% (7-series) and 65% (Stratix 10) of final packed clusters are repetitions. Deep learning benchmarks exhibit >85% repetition. DL domain circuits benefit the most.
  • Overhead: PST insertion/lookup accounts for only 0.8% of packing runtime (max 1.6%). Packer peak memory increases ~27% on average, but PST is freed after packing, so full-flow peak memory is essentially unchanged (<1% increase for Stratix 10).
  • Simple architectures: On VTR's flagship architecture with full local crossbar, Déjà Vu packing introduces negligible overhead (3% packing speedup, 0.8% memory increase), confirming it degrades gracefully.
  • Quality of results: Identical to baseline -- no change in routed wirelength or critical path delay, since the memoization does not alter any optimization decision.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (High): The idea of memoizing packing patterns via a tree-based signature is elegant and, to my knowledge, novel in the FPGA packing literature. The observation that 59-65% of clusters are repeated patterns is a genuinely useful empirical contribution. The PST design cleverly exploits the deterministic nature of greedy packing to avoid the general graph isomorphism problem.
  • Rigor (High): The experimental setup is sound -- 68 benchmarks from four suites, two architecture targets, 3-seed averages, detailed profiling. The authors clearly separate speculative vs. detailed packing contributions. They verify no memory leaks with Valgrind. The "zero quality impact" claim is strong because the approach is exact, not heuristic.
  • Reproducibility (High): The code is being integrated into the open-source VTR toolchain with a command-line flag. Architecture files are contributed to VTR. Benchmark suites (VTR, Koios, Titan23, Titanium25) are public. Specific commit IDs and hardware are stated.
  • Significance (High): This solves a practical bottleneck that has actively hindered exploration of complex LB architectures (as explicitly noted by the original Titan flow authors). The 5.3x average VPR speedup on Stratix 10 transforms previously impractical 76-hour runs into manageable 6.8-hour runs.
  • Red flags: The approach is inherently tied to deterministic, seed-based packing algorithms. It would not directly apply to randomized or non-deterministic packing strategies (though the authors note this limitation is minor since seed-based packing is dominant). The 27% packer memory increase could be significant for very large designs on memory-constrained systems, though the full-flow impact is negligible. The paper does not discuss how PST size scales with design size or architecture complexity beyond the measured benchmarks.

Strengths

  • Identifies and rigorously characterizes a critical runtime bottleneck that has real practical impact on FPGA CAD research.
  • The PST data structure is simple, elegant, and introduces minimal runtime overhead (<1%).
  • The approach is exact -- no heuristics, no quality degradation, which makes it a "free" optimization that tool developers can adopt without risk.
  • Excellent experimental coverage: 68 benchmarks, two realistic commercial architectures, detailed per-benchmark runtime and memory results.
  • Solves the previously reported problem where complex LB details had to be omitted from VTR architecture models due to packing runtime, thereby enabling more realistic architecture exploration.
  • Graceful degradation on simple architectures (negligible overhead when there is little to memoize).

Limitations

  • Only evaluated on seed-based packing in VPR; applicability to partitioning-based or consensus-based packing methods is not demonstrated.
  • The PST design relies on deterministic molecule insertion ordering, which may not hold for all packing algorithms or under multi-threaded execution.
  • The approach does not reduce the number of unique cluster patterns -- it only avoids re-checking previously seen ones. For designs with highly diverse cluster patterns, the speedup would be limited.
  • Memory overhead of the PST (up to 1.3 GB) could be a concern for very large designs or memory-constrained environments, though it is transient.
  • The Stratix 10 results are based on a modified VTR architecture capture that the authors created; while more faithful, it is still an approximation of the commercial device.

Relevance

本文对FPGA EDA工具链优化具有直接的工程意义，特别是对于研究FPGA架构探索和CAD算法的人员，Déjà Vu packing提供了一种零质量损失的运行时加速方案。对于关注深度学习加速器在FPGA上实现的研究者也有参考价值——DL基准测试展现出最高的模式重复率（>85%），加速效果最为显著。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the PST approach be extended to multi-threaded packing algorithms, where the deterministic insertion ordering assumption may not hold?
  • Would combining PST memoization with RSVPack's pre-computed routing feasibility tables yield further speedups for architectures where both apply?
  • How does the PST size scale for ultra-large designs (millions of LUTs) -- is there a point where memory becomes a binding constraint?
  • Could the packing signature concept be applied to other stages of the FPGA CAD flow (e.g., placement or global routing) where similar pattern repetition may exist?
  • What is the theoretical bound on the number of unique packing signatures for a given architecture, and can it be used to predict memoization effectiveness a priori?
