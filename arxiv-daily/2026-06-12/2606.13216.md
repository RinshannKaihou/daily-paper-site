> **一句话总结：** 本文通过对Fairseq DE-EN模型全部六层解码器进行逐层最优传输（OT）分析，发现Wass-to-Unif和Wass-to-Data是互补的幻觉检测器（AUROC最高0.957），检测信号集中在L1-L4层，幻觉翻译从第一个解码步就缺乏正确翻译的探索性注意力阶段；但该方法迁移到抽象摘要忠实度检测时平衡准确率仅57.2%/57.6%，因为不忠实摘要可以在正确关注源文本的同时歪曲内容，这一失败模式在结构上对OT浓度指标不可见。

> **TL;DR:** This paper extends optimal-transport-based hallucination detection from the final decoder layer to all six layers of a Fairseq DE-EN NMT model (N=3,414). Three key findings: (1) Wass-to-Unif and Wass-to-Data are complementary detectors — WTU/RC achieve AUROC 0.937/0.957 for fully-detached hallucinations, while WTD dominates for subtler types (0.770–0.790); (2) detection is concentrated in layers L1–L4, with L5 anti-predictive for subtler types; (3) hallucinated translations lack the exploratory attention phase present in correct translations from step 1. When transferred to abstractive summarization faithfulness detection (AggreFact, N=1,116), the unsupervised OT detector achieves 57.2%/57.6% balanced accuracy on CNN/XSum — above chance but substantially below supervised MiniCheck-Flan-T5-L (69.9%/74.3%). The gap is principled: OT detects retrieval failure (source disengagement) but not content misuse (attending correctly while misrepresenting).

## 核心贡献

- **逐层OT分析揭示三种功能区域：** Fairseq六层解码器分为过渡层L0、桥接层L1、紧密聚簇L2-L4（共享路由行为）和结构隔离层L5，L2-L4层间W1距离远低于其他层间距离
- **引入路由一致性（RC）新检测器：** RC在检测full-unsupport幻觉时达到AUROC 0.957，优于WTU的0.937——捕获的是解码器在每一步都路由到同一源位置的病态模式
- **发现WTU与WTD的互补性：** WTU擅长检测full-unsupport（AUROC 0.937），WTD在strong-unsupport和repetitions上更强（0.770/0.790），且WTD在L5层不崩溃
- **揭示生成动态的双阶段特征：** 正确翻译呈现"探索→承诺"两阶段注意力轨迹，幻觉翻译从第一步就跳过探索阶段，直接进入静态集中状态
- **跨任务迁移的原理性局限：** 不忠实摘要可在正确关注源词元的同时歪曲内容，这种"内容误用"失败模式在结构上无法被基于浓度的OT指标检测
- **T5-base结构验证：** 12层T5-base中Layer 3为浓度峰值、路由分化最大，Layer 12对生成质量最关键，L9-L11功能冗余——与ablation结果一致

## Key Contributions

- **Three functional regimes in Fairseq decoder layers:** L0 (transitional), L1 (bridging), L2–L4 (tight cluster with shared routing, pairwise W1 ≪ inter-regime distances), and L5 (structurally isolated, maximum distance ≈ 0.13 from L2).
- **Routing Consistency (RC) as a new unsupervised detector:** RC achieves AUROC 0.957 for full-unsupport hallucinations — the strongest individual detector in the analysis — by capturing that hallucinating decoders route to the same source position at every generation step.
- **WTU/WTD complementarity grounded in layer-wise analysis:** WTU dominates for full-unsupport (0.937), WTD dominates for strong-unsupport (0.770) and repetitions (0.790); WTD is robust to the L5 collapse that makes WTU anti-predictive (AUROC drops to 0.374 for repetitions at L5).
- **Two-phase generation dynamics:** Correct translations exhibit an exploratory phase (first ~25% of sequence: high step-OT, low concentration) followed by a commitment phase. Hallucinated translations skip exploration entirely, beginning in a statically concentrated state from step 1.
- **Principled limitation for summarization transfer:** The NMT-to-summarization gap (57.4% vs. 72.1% BAcc) is explained by a retrieval-failure vs. content-misuse distinction — OT detects the former by construction and is blind to the latter.
- **T5-base structural convergence:** Layer 3 concentration peak confirmed as structural pivot via leave-one-out ablation (L1–L3 ablation causes ∆R-L = −4.47); L12 most critical for quality (∆R-L = −0.96); L9–L11 redundant (ablation beneficial).

## Methodology

The paper employs optimal transport (OT) distance metrics computed on cross-attention distributions across all decoder layers of two encoder-decoder models.

**Attention extraction:** For each source–output pair, the full cross-attention tensors are extracted. Raw tensors have shape (H, T_tgt, S); head-averaged distributions π^(ℓ,t) are computed per layer ℓ and generation step t.

**OT metrics:**
- **Wass-to-Unif (WTU):** Mean W1 distance between attention distributions and the uniform distribution u = (1/S, ..., 1/S) over source positions. Low concentration flags potential hallucination.
- **Wass-to-Data (WTD):** Mean W1 distance to k=4 nearest neighbours from a reference set of confirmed-correct translations (filtered by length proximity δ=0.1).
- **Step-to-step OT:** W1 between consecutive generation steps' attention distributions — measures how dynamically the decoder repositions source attention.
- **Layer-pairwise OT:** W1 between step-averaged attention at each pair of layers — produces an L×L distance matrix revealing functional grouping.
- **Routing Consistency (RC):** Measures how consistently the argmax source position repeats across steps, computed via routing entropy — captures a complementary signal to concentration.

All W1 distances are computed exactly via the CDF formula (no Sinkhorn approximation).

**Datasets:**
- NMT: Fairseq DE-EN annotated corpus (N=3,414), with 324 hallucinated (full-unsupport: 129, strong-unsupport: 164, repetitions: 87) and 2,882 confirmed-correct sentences.
- Summarization: AggreFact benchmark — CNN/DailyMail (N=558) and XSum (N=558) test splits.
- T5-base structural experiments: N=100 for concentration profiling, N=50 for quality-group comparisons.

## Results & Highlights

**NMT hallucination detection (Fairseq DE-EN):**
- All OT metrics significantly separate hallucinated from correct translations (p<0.001, Mann-Whitney U).
- Hallucinated translations: higher mean concentration (0.296 vs. 0.249), lower step-OT (0.082 vs. 0.124), lower concentration std (0.090 vs. 0.104), lower layer-OT (0.055 vs. 0.059).
- Detection power degrades with hallucination severity: full-unsupport (RC AUROC 0.957) > strong-unsupport (WTD 0.770) > repetitions (WTD 0.790, WTU 0.568).
- L5 is anti-predictive for strong-unsupport (WTU AUROC 0.461) and repetitions (0.374), because it is always the most concentrated layer regardless of quality.

**Cross-attention structure (T5-base, 12 layers):**
- Layer 3 is the global concentration peak (median W1 = 0.1612) and maximally routing-distinct from all other layers.
- L9–L11 form a tight functionally redundant cluster (pairwise distances ≈ 0.009–0.032).
- Leave-one-out ablation: L12 is most critical (∆R-L = −0.96); L1–L3 cumulative ablation causes catastrophic collapse (∆R-L = −4.47).
- L5, L7, L11 ablation is beneficial (+0.59 to +0.88).

**Summarization faithfulness (AggreFact):**
- OT detector (T5-base): 57.2% CNN / 57.6% XSum balanced accuracy.
- OT detector (Flan-T5-large): 55.6% CNN / 61.4% XSum.
- Supervised MiniCheck-Flan-T5-L: 69.9% CNN / 74.3% XSum.
- Cross-dataset generalization fails: CNN-trained OT features tested on XSum → 44.2% BAcc (below chance).
- HIGH vs. LOW ROUGE-L groups are statistically indistinguishable at every layer (p>0.60).

## Authors & Credibility

The authors — Mariia Onyshchuk, Maksym-Vasyl Tarnavskyi, and Marta Sumyk — are from the Faculty of Applied Sciences at Ukrainian Catholic University, Lviv. The paper was accepted to the Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop. The work builds rigorously on Guerreiro et al. (2023, ACL), extending their single-layer OT analysis to all decoder layers and across tasks. The authors are commendably transparent about the limitations of their approach, providing a principled theoretical account (retrieval failure vs. content misuse) for why OT transfer to summarization is partial. The code is available at an anonymous 4open.science repository.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: Moderate-to-good.** The layer-resolved analysis is a natural but substantial extension of prior work. The introduction of Routing Consistency as a new detector, the two-phase generation dynamics finding, and the principled transfer-limitation analysis are genuinely novel contributions. The T5-base structural experiments add cross-architecture validation.
- **Rigor: High.** All statistical tests use Mann-Whitney U with p<0.001 thresholds. AUROC and balanced accuracy are appropriate metrics. The leave-one-out ablation and cumulative ablation on T5-base provide independent confirmation of the OT-based structural findings. The cross-dataset generalization test (CNN→XSum = 44.2%) is a valuable negative result.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Standard models (Fairseq DE-EN, T5-base), established benchmarks (AggreFact), public code, and exact W1 computation (no approximations) all aid reproducibility. The LoRA analysis confirming 0.132% parameter adjustment during task adaptation is a nice control.
- **Significance: Good for the NMT/interpretability community, limited for summarization.** The detection spectrum (AUROC 0.957 → 0.568 → 57.4% BAcc) provides a clear, principled map of where OT-based detection works and where it doesn't. The architectural findings (functional regimes, concentration peaks) are useful for mechanistic interpretability.
- **Red flags:** The NMT evaluation is limited to a single language pair (DE-EN) and a single model (Fairseq, 6 layers). The summarization results are barely above chance in absolute terms (57.2%/57.6% vs. 50%). The T5-base structural experiments use very small samples (N=50–100). No comparison against other *unsupervised* hallucination detectors. The paper does not report whether combining WTU, WTD, and RC improves over individual metrics. The L0 "transitional" characterization is somewhat vague — it's unclear what functional role this layer plays beyond not belonging to the other groups.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally honest about limitations — the principled account of why OT fails for summarization (retrieval failure vs. content misuse) is a genuine intellectual contribution
- Layer-resolved analysis provides actionable, fine-grained insights rather than aggregate claims
- Multiple converging methods (OT metrics, ablation, LoRA analysis) strengthen the structural claims
- RC is a clever new detector that captures a mechanistically distinct signal from concentration
- The two-phase generation dynamics finding (exploration → commitment, absent in hallucinations) has practical implications for early-step online detection
- WTU/WTD complementarity is clearly demonstrated at both aggregate and per-layer levels
- Clean mathematical framework — all W1 computed exactly, no approximations

## Limitations

- Single NMT language pair (DE-EN) and model architecture (Fairseq, 6 layers) — unclear generalization to other pairs or modern LLMs
- Summarization detection barely above chance (57.4% avg) — limited practical utility in this domain
- T5-base structural experiments use very small samples (N=50–100), raising statistical power concerns
- No comparison against other unsupervised detection methods (e.g., attention entropy, probing classifiers)
- No investigation of combining WTU, WTD, and RC into an ensemble — each is reported separately
- Cross-dataset generalization fails completely (CNN→XSum = 44.2%), raising questions about robustness
- The approach requires access to cross-attention weights, limiting applicability to black-box APIs

## Relevance

这篇论文与"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"研究方向高度相关——通过最优传输距离分析cross-attention分布的几何特性来揭示Transformer解码器的功能分层结构。其关于注意力模式与输出质量关系的发现、以及模型失败模式的原理性分析，对理解模型内部机制和可靠性（ML Reliability）具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the layer-resolved OT findings transfer to decoder-only LLMs (e.g., LLaMA, GPT) where cross-attention is replaced by self-attention over a concatenated prompt?
- Could combining OT metrics with semantic similarity or NLI-based detectors close the gap for summarization faithfulness detection?
- Is the two-phase "exploration → commitment" dynamic a universal property of Transformer decoding, or specific to encoder-decoder architectures?
- What is the computational overhead of computing OT scores at inference time for real-time hallucination monitoring, and does early-step detection (after ~25% of generation) provide sufficient signal?
- Does the functional regime structure (transitional → bridge → cluster → isolated) scale predictably with the number of decoder layers (e.g., in 32-layer or 96-layer models)?
- Could the RC detector's sensitivity to "same-position routing at every step" be exploited for beam-search pruning or guided decoding?
