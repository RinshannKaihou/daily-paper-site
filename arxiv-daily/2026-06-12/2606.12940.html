Self-Guidance: Enhancing Neural Codecs via Decoder Manifold Alignment

Xiang Li, Yixuan Zhou, Jingran Xie, Zhiyong Wu, Hui Wang  ·  Tsinghua University & Pengcheng Laboratory  ·  arXiv 2606.12940

提出Self-Guidance方法，通过对齐解码器处理量化token与原始连续嵌入时的Transformer隐藏特征，使解码器对量化误差更具鲁棒性。在XCodec2上实现低码率SOTA重建质量，支持4倍码本缩减（16K码本匹配原始65K性能），显著改善下游LLM TTS合成。
Key Metric
4×
codebook reduction — 16K entries with SG match the original 65K baseline. PESQ +0.11, MCD −0.16 at 65K.
Key Finding
Aligning decoder's Transformer hidden states between quantized-token and continuous-latent paths (L2 loss) makes the decoder robust to quantization error — zero inference cost, <0.5% training overhead.
Why This Matters
Smaller codebooks shrink the LLM token vocabulary for speech synthesis — the 16K+SG codec achieves UTMOS 3.58 vs 3.33 and WER 28.02 vs 33.03 compared to the 65K baseline.
Critical Edge
TTS evaluated only on a 0.5B LLM. WER actually worsens at 65K with SG (33.03→35.07). Only validated on speech — music, general audio, non-audio VQ-VAEs remain untested.

Methodology

Rather than improving the quantizer, Self-Guidance makes the decoder robust to quantization imperfections by aligning its internal representations for two inputs: quantized tokens zq and original continuous latents ze.

Lguide = ‖ sg(he) − hq ‖₂²

sg(·) = stop-gradient (teacher branch). h = output of last Transformer block. λguide = 5.0 (65K) / 10.0 (16K, 8K).

Total Objective

Ltotal = λguide·Lguide + Lsemantic + Lacoustic + Ladv
  • Lsemantic — Wav2Vec2-BERT feature MSE
  • Lacoustic — multi-scale Mel L1
  • Ladv — multi-period + spectrogram discriminators
  • Base: XCodec2 — conv encoder, single-layer FSQ, Transformer decoder with iSTFT head
  • Training: 8× RTX 4090, 600K iters, 960h LibriSpeech, 16kHz

Strengths

  • Elegant core idea — no architectural changes, zero inference overhead
  • Exceptionally thorough mechanism analysis (kNN Jaccard, Procrustes, CKA, t-SNE)
  • Strong generalization across 3 quantizer types, 2 decoder architectures, 3 codebook sizes
  • Practical 4× codebook reduction benefits LLM speech synthesis directly
  • ICML 2026 — strong peer review signal

Limitations

  • TTS evaluation preliminary — only 0.5B LLM backbone tested
  • WER worsens at 65K codebook with SG (33.03 → 35.07)
  • Only validated on speech codecs — music, general audio, non-audio domains untested
  • λguide requires tuning per codebook size — not plug-and-play
  • Subjective test small: 38 evaluations on 30 clips

Open Questions

Can SG reduce the number of residual codebooks in multi-codebook architectures like EnCodec's RVQ?
Does the 4× codebook benefit scale proportionally with larger LLMs (1B–7B) for TTS?
Can self-guidance transfer to visual VQ-VAEs (e.g., Stable Diffusion's tokenizer) where codebook collapse is a known issue?
Is there a theoretical connection between the feature-mapping loss and information-theoretic bounds on quantization error?
Does alignment break down with extreme codebook sizes (<1024) in FSQ?
Could a curriculum-based λguide schedule (small → large) outperform a fixed weight?
