> **一句话总结：** MÖVE 是首个针对德国公共部门的 LLM 综合评测基准，在 39 个模型上同时评估性能（摘要、问答、主题提取）和治理（幻觉、能耗、透明度、政治价值观对齐）两大维度，发现没有单一模型在所有指标上全面领先，且模型规模不是质量的可靠预测因素。

> **TL;DR:** MÖVE is the first holistic benchmark tailored to the German public sector, evaluating 39 LLMs across performance tasks (summarization, QA, topic extraction) and governance criteria (hallucination, sustainability, transparency, constitutional value alignment) on 10 German-language datasets. Key findings: no single model dominates all criteria, model size is a poor quality predictor, and the benchmark itself is rigorously self-evaluated for statistical precision, judge reliability, prompt sensitivity, and dataset impact. Published by Bundesdruckerei (Germany's federal printing house), it is designed as a living benchmark with continuously updated public results.

## 核心贡献

- **构建了首个面向德国公共行政领域的 LLM 综合评测框架**，将性能评测（摘要、问答、主题提取）与治理评测（幻觉、可持续性、透明度、政治价值观对齐）统一在同一个基准下，填补了现有基准在非英语、非美国公共部门场景中的空白。
- **创建了多个领域专属数据集**，包括金标准和银标准数据集（如 KIKC Summary、KIKC QA、KIKC Topics、FAQ-LAW、German Ministry Publications），直接反映德国公共行政文本特征，缓解数据污染问题。
- **采用多维度评测策略**，结合经典 NLP 指标（ROUGE、BLEU）、嵌入方法（BERTScore、SemScore）和 LLM-as-a-judge 方法，对 39 个模型进行系统评测。
- **对基准本身进行了严格的方法论自评**，包括统计精度分析、LLM 评判器可靠性测试（组内稳定性和组间一致性）、私有数据集影响分析、提示词敏感性测试和能耗估算有效性验证。
- **揭示了关键实践发现**：没有单一模型在所有指标上全面领先；模型规模不是质量的可靠预测因子；透明度方面，计算与能耗是最不透明的领域（84.6% 的模型未提供能耗信息）。

## Key Contributions

- **First holistic LLM benchmark for the German public sector**, combining performance evaluation (summarization, QA, topic extraction) with governance evaluation (hallucination, sustainability, transparency, political/constitutional value alignment) in a single unified framework.
- **Constructed multiple domain-specific German-language datasets** at gold-standard and silver-standard quality levels (KIKC Summary, KIKC QA, KIKC Topics, FAQ-LAW, German Ministry Publications), specifically reflecting public-administration text characteristics and mitigating data contamination concerns.
- **Multi-metric evaluation strategy** combining classical NLP metrics (ROUGE, BLEU), embedding-based methods (BERTScore, SemScore), and LLM-as-a-judge approaches across 39 models from 13 providers.
- **Rigorous self-evaluation of the benchmark itself**, analyzing statistical precision via hierarchical bootstrap, LLM judge reliability (within-judge stability and inter-judge agreement), impact of private datasets on rankings, prompt sensitivity, and validity of energy consumption estimates.
- **Key practical findings**: no single model dominates across all criteria; model size alone is a poor predictor of quality; transparency on compute & energy remains the most opaque domain (84.6% of models provide no energy consumption information); and German constitutional value alignment shows no model exhibits clearly negative stance, though this may reflect positivity bias.

## Methodology

The MÖVE framework evaluates 39 models across two complementary dimensions using 10 German-language datasets:

**Performance Criteria:**
- **Summarization** (4 datasets: Eur-Lex-Sum, Swiss Leading Decision Summarization, KIKC Summary, German Ministry Publications): Evaluated via ROUGE-1/2/L, BERTScore, SemScore, and LLM-judged Factual Correctness and Faithfulness.
- **Question Answering** (3 datasets: German-QuAD, KIKC QA, FAQ-LAW): Evaluated via Exact Match, token-level F1, BERTScore, and LLM-judged Factual Correctness and Noise Sensitivity (adapted from RAGAS).
- **Topic Extraction** (2 datasets: KIKC Topics, German Ministry Publications): Evaluated via exact-match F1, BERTScore, and LLM-judged Topic Match and Topic Adherence.

**Governance Criteria:**
- **Hallucination**: Assessed using a context-grounded setup where models answer questions about provided documents; hallucination rate measures unsupported claims. Uses RAGAS-based faithfulness metrics.
- **Sustainability**: Energy consumption estimated per query using the EcoLogits framework, which combines model-specific parameters with request metadata for both API-accessed and locally deployed models. Reported as Wh per query.
- **Transparency**: A structured Transparency Matrix with 21 questions across 7 domains (Model Identification, Architecture, Distribution & Access, Use & Deployment, Training & Data, Compute & Energy, Code of Practice alignment), derived from the EU AI Act GPAI Code of Practice. Scored by human reviewers and an LLM agent.
- **Politics & Values**: (1) Party position classification — models classify positions of 64 German political parties across 4,788 positions from Wahl-O-Mat data; (2) Constitutional value stance — models generate statements about 8 fundamental values from the German Grundgesetz (e.g., human dignity, freedom, equality, environmental protection), evaluated by both LLM-as-a-judge and a fine-tuned GottBERT classifier.

**Models evaluated**: 39 models from 13 providers spanning small (≤3B), medium (3B–30B), and large (>30B) categories, including GPT-4o, GPT-4o Mini, DeepSeek R1, Llama 3.3 70B, Mistral-Large-3, Qwen3, Gemma, and various European models (EuroLLM, Apertus, Teuken, SauerkrautLM). GPT-4o Mini serves as the LLM judge throughout.

## Results & Highlights

- **No single model dominates**: Top performers differ across tasks and criteria. Mistral-Large-3 leads summarization; GPT-4o and GPT-4o Mini lead QA; topic extraction shows minimal differentiation between top models.
- **Model size is a poor predictor**: Small models like GPT-4o Mini match or outperform much larger models across tasks. Gemma2 9B achieves 5th-highest performance while being among the most energy-efficient.
- **Sustainability trade-offs revealed**: Top-10 most energy-efficient models are predominantly small, with Teuken 7B leading (0.647 Wh/query). Pareto analysis shows Gemma2 9B as a strong efficiency-performance trade-off candidate.
- **Transparency is severely lacking**: Mean transparency score is 64.1%. Compute & Energy domain averages only 11.5%, with 84.6% of models providing no energy consumption information. Apertus 70B leads at 90.5%, being designed specifically for EU AI Act compliance.
- **EU Code of Practice signatories show marginal advantage**: Signatories score only slightly higher (27.6 vs. 26.3), with the advantage driven almost entirely by Use & Deployment documentation.
- **Political value alignment**: No model exhibits clearly negative stance toward German constitutional values. European models tend to score higher on value alignment, possibly reflecting training data composition.
- **Benchmark self-evaluation**: Summarization rankings have the highest precision (median CI width 4.6% of score spread); QA and topic extraction should be interpreted as performance tiers. LLM judge inter-judge agreement is moderate (Krippendorff's α = 0.42–0.68), with Factual Correctness on QA being the most reliable metric.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors (Camilla Dalerci, Thilo Michael, Robin Schaefer, Daniel Weinland) are all from the Innovations Department of **Bundesdruckerei GmbH**, Germany's federal printing house and a major provider of secure identity and digital infrastructure solutions to the German government. This institutional affiliation gives the team direct access to public-sector stakeholders and administrative use cases. The benchmark was shaped by semi-structured feedback sessions with ministry representatives and validated at 20+ sector events including the IT-Planungsrat Fachkongress and the BMDS AI Community of Practice. The work is positioned as a living benchmark with a public website (https://moeve.bundesdruckerei.de/) and active development. All authors contributed equally.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: High. MÖVE is genuinely novel in combining domain-specific German public-sector evaluation with governance criteria (sustainability, transparency, constitutional values) in a single framework. No prior benchmark addresses this combination for any national context.

**Rigor**: Very strong. The self-evaluation of the benchmark itself (Section 6) is unusually thorough for a benchmark paper — analyzing statistical precision, LLM judge reliability, dataset impact, prompt sensitivity, and energy estimation validity. The hierarchical bootstrap CI methodology and inter-judge agreement analysis set a high methodological standard.

**Reproducibility**: Good. The 10 datasets, prompts, and evaluation methodology are described in detail. However, several internal datasets (KIKC Summary, KIKC QA, KIKC Topics, FAQ-LAW) are not publicly released, which limits full external replication. The authors acknowledge this trade-off between contamination prevention and reproducibility.

**Significance**: High for the German public sector and as a model for other national LLM benchmarks. The governance criteria (especially transparency and constitutional values) are increasingly relevant under the EU AI Act.

**Potential concerns**:
- **Small internal datasets**: Some gold-standard datasets have very few samples (e.g., KIKC Summary has only 40 instances), which inflates CI widths and limits statistical power. The authors acknowledge this as a priority for expansion.
- **LLM judge reliability**: Inter-judge agreement for most LLM-based metrics falls below conventional reliability thresholds (α < 0.67 for 4 of 5 metrics), meaning rankings are more like tiers than precise orderings. This is honestly reported but somewhat undermines the precision of governance criteria that depend on LLM judging.
- **Energy estimation limitations**: The EcoLogits-based energy estimates are indirect and the authors' own validation (Section 6.5) shows notable gaps between estimated and measured energy, particularly for quantized local models. The sustainability rankings should be interpreted with caution.
- **Value alignment measurement**: The finding that no model exhibits negative stance toward constitutional values could reflect a positivity bias in generation rather than genuine alignment — the authors acknowledge this openly but do not fully resolve it.
- **Limited task diversity**: Only three performance tasks (summarization, QA, topic extraction) are covered; tasks like translation, code generation, or multi-turn dialogue are not included, which narrows the applicability for some administrative use cases.

## Strengths

- First benchmark to holistically evaluate LLMs for a specific national public-sector context with both performance and governance criteria.
- Exceptionally thorough self-evaluation methodology that sets a new standard for benchmark papers.
- Practical relevance: designed with and for actual public-sector stakeholders, not just academic audiences.
- Living benchmark design with continuous updates and public results website.
- Addresses data contamination through private domain-specific datasets.
- Transparent reporting of limitations and uncertainties, including confidence intervals and reliability metrics.

## Limitations

- Small sample sizes for some internal datasets limit statistical power.
- LLM-as-a-judge inter-judge agreement is moderate at best for most metrics.
- Energy consumption is estimated rather than directly measured for API-based models.
- Only German-language evaluation; not directly applicable to other national contexts.
- Three performance tasks may not cover all relevant administrative use cases (e.g., translation, document drafting, multi-step reasoning).
- Constitutional value alignment results may reflect positivity bias rather than genuine alignment.
- The benchmark evaluates model capabilities but does not assess deployment considerations like latency, throughput, or integration complexity.

## Relevance

本文对 LLM 评测方法论和公共部门 AI 治理有直接参考价值。其"性能+治理"双维度评测框架，尤其是透明度矩阵和宪法价值观对齐评测的设计，对构建面向特定国家/地区 LLM 评测基准具有示范意义。对关注 LLM 负责任部署和欧盟 AI Act 合规性的研究也很有价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How would the benchmark results change with expanded internal datasets (e.g., 200+ samples for gold-standard sets)?
2. Can multi-judge averaging (e.g., using 3+ LLM judges) significantly improve inter-judge reliability without prohibitive cost?
3. How do the governance criteria rankings correlate — are transparent models also more energy-efficient and better aligned with constitutional values?
4. What is the effect of model fine-tuning on German public-sector text specifically (rather than general German text) on both performance and governance scores?
5. How transferable is the MÖVE framework design to other national contexts (e.g., French, Japanese, or Brazilian public sectors)?
6. Does the "no negative stance" finding on constitutional values hold with adversarial prompting or when models are asked to argue both sides of politically charged topics?
