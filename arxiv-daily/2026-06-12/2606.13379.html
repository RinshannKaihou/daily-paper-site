Positional Encoding in the Context of Memristor-Based Analog Computation for Automatic Speech Recognition

Benedikt Hilmes · Nick Rossenbach · Ralf Schlüter — RWTH Aachen University · 2026-06-12
Conformer ASR 模型中相对位置编码经线性变换后产生的大幅值输出会超出忆阻器模拟计算中 ADC 的表示范围，导致严重性能退化；通过调整 ADC 的范围/精度位数分配可将退化降低约 50%。
Key Finding
PE linear transforms cause ~40% ADC clipping in memristor execution — making better-performing PE models paradoxically worse on analog hardware.
Key Metric
~50% relative degradation reduction via ADC bit reallocation (1-bit precision / 7-bit range) under fixed 8-bit budget
Why This Matters
Analog memristor accelerators promise 10× energy savings — but standard model components like positional encoding can silently break them.
Critical Edge
Simulation-only (SynaptogenML), dev-set evaluation, 77M-param CTC Conformer. Gap to physical chips and modern ASR architectures is untested.

Methodology

A 77M-param CTC Conformer (12 layers, dim 512) with relative positional encodings is mapped onto simulated memristor crossbar arrays via SynaptogenML. Each matrix is split into 128×128 blocks; each block has its own ADC. Baseline ADC: 4-bit precision, 4-bit range.

Procedure

  • Train with/without PE on LibriSpeech + Loquacious 250h
  • Quantize: 8-bit activations, 8-bit or 4-bit weights
  • Map to memristor simulation, 5 runs per config
  • Track per-crossbar ADC clipping for PE layers
  • Vary ADC precision/range for PE layers only
  • Test model-level mods: learned vs. sinusoidal PE, removing linear transform

Results

The PE paradox: Positional encoding improves baseline WER and quantized robustness, yet under memristor execution it causes 25–40% relative degradation vs. only 10–25% without PE.

Baseline WER (LibriSpeech dev-other)

  • With PE: 5.4% (8-bit) → 7.6% memristor
  • Without PE: 5.7% (8-bit) → 7.2% memristor
  • Best ADC tuning (1/7 split): 6.6% WER — near oracle

Strengths

  • Identifies a previously unknown, specific failure mode with clear quantitative evidence
  • Complementary hardware- and software-level solutions for different deployment constraints
  • Systematic ablation varying ADC precision/range for PE layers vs. full model
  • Validated on two ASR datasets with consistent results
  • Excellent reproducibility: public code, simulation framework, and training recipes
  • Nuanced finding: PE is more important under low precision but less compatible with memristor hardware

Limitations

  • Dev-set only — no test-set results; real-world significance uncertain
  • Simulation-only study; no physical memristor chip validation
  • CTC-only Conformer (77M params) — may not generalize to transducer/larger models
  • Energy estimates are theoretical; no chip-level measurements
  • "No linear" model loses PE transform entirely, may hurt on harder tasks/longer sequences
  • Only 5 programming runs per config — variance could affect edge-case conclusions

Open Questions

  1. Do these findings transfer to physical memristor chips? Are there failure modes SynaptogenML misses?
  2. Would bounded-output positional encodings designed specifically for analog hardware outperform post-hoc ADC tuning?
  3. How does ADC clipping scale with larger models (300M+ params, conformer-transducers)?
  4. Can ADC bit-allocation be learned jointly with model training (hardware-aware NAS)?
  5. What is the actual measured energy of different precision/range ADC configs on fabricated chips?
  6. Does removing the linear transform degrade performance more on longer speech inputs where relative PE is most beneficial?
