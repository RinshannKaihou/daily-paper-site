> **一句话总结：** 本文发现 Transformer 不同模块（attention vs MLP）适合不同的流形几何约束：在 GPT-2 预训练中，attention 层用 Stiefel 约束（谱有界）、MLP 层用 DGram 约束（允许尺度自由）的组合（HETERO）取得最低验证损失 3.3544，而将 DGram 用于 attention 的配置全部训练不稳定，根本原因是 DGram 允许 query/key 权重奇异值无限增长，放大 attention logits 导致 softmax 饱和和梯度退化。

> **TL;DR:** This paper systematically compares module-wise manifold geometry assignments for Manifold Muon in GPT-2 (124M) pretraining. Five configurations are tested under shared hyperparameters: Stiefel attention + DGram MLP (HETERO) wins at validation loss 3.3544, beating both unconstrained (3.3855) and all-Stiefel (3.3679). Every configuration placing DGram on attention becomes unstable. The authors provide a rigorous mechanistic analysis proving DGram permits unbounded singular-value growth in Q/K projections, which amplifies logits and pushes softmax into a saturated (near-one-hot) regime with exponentially vanishing gradients — a failure pathway that Stiefel provably blocks. Published at ICML 2026 Weight-Space Symmetries Workshop.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了 Transformer 中 attention 和 MLP 模块对流形几何约束有**系统性不对称偏好**：attention 需要谱有界的 Stiefel 约束，MLP 可以受益于更宽松的 DGram 约束。
- 在 GPT-2 small (124M) 上系统测试了 5 种配置（UNCONSTRAINED, ALL-STIEFEL, ALL-DGRAM, HETERO, HETERO-INV），在完全相同的超参数下进行公平比较。
- 提供了**严格的数学机制分析**：证明 DGram 约束允许 Q/K 权重的奇异值任意增长（Proposition 4.2），而 Stiefel 约束保证 softmax Jacobian 有正下界（Proposition 4.1），完整刻画了"奇异值增长 → logit 放大 → softmax 饱和 → 梯度退化"的失败路径。
- 解释了 MLP 为何能容忍 DGram：GELU 逐坐标作用，不存在 softmax 那种全局 token 竞争机制，LayerNorm 和残差连接也能吸收尺度变化。
- 开源代码：`github.com/kiratoyoshihara/module-wise-manifold-muon`。

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates a clear asymmetry in manifold geometry preference between attention and MLP modules, challenging the implicit assumption of uniform manifold constraints across all weight matrices.
- HETERO (Stiefel attention + DGram MLP) achieves the best validation loss of 3.3544, improving over unconstrained (3.3855) and all-Stiefel (3.3679), while both DGram-attention configurations (ALL-DGRAM, HETERO-INV) become unstable.
- Provides formal propositions: Stiefel attention is gradient non-degenerate under bounded inputs (Prop 4.1), while DGram attention admits gradient-degenerate directions (Prop 4.2), with full proof sketches in the appendix.
- Identifies the complete failure pathway: DGram → unconstrained singular values in Q/K → logit amplification → exponential softmax saturation → vanishing Jacobian → training instability.
- Explains the MLP tolerance: coordinate-wise GELU nonlinearity avoids the global token-competition structure of softmax, and LayerNorm + residual connections absorb scale changes.

## Methodology

The paper studies Manifold Muon for GPT-2 small (124M parameters) pretraining on OpenWebText using a nanoGPT-style implementation, with the following setup:

**Manifold Constraints:**
- **Stiefel**: Enforces column orthonormality (W^⊤W = I), bounding all singular values to exactly 1. Provides strong spectral control.
- **DGram**: Requires only off-diagonal Gram entries to vanish (Off(W^⊤W) = 0), preserving pairwise column orthogonality but allowing per-column norms (and hence singular values) to grow freely.

**Five Configurations Tested:**
1. UNCONSTRAINED — no manifold constraint
2. ALL-STIEFEL — Stiefel on both attention and MLP
3. ALL-DGRAM — DGram on both
4. HETERO — Stiefel attention + DGram MLP
5. HETERO-INV — DGram attention + Stiefel MLP (control for heterogeneity per se)

**Optimizer Setup:** Hybrid optimizer — Manifold Muon for attention/MLP projections, AdamW for remaining parameters (embeddings, LayerNorm, etc.). All configurations share identical hyperparameters: 50K iterations, batch size 64, context length 1024, cosine LR schedule with 1K warmup, Muon peak LR 0.02, AdamW peak LR 6×10⁻⁴, momentum 0.95, 20 dual-ascent steps for manifold projection.

**Manifold Muon Implementation:** Tangent-space-constrained Muon update solved via dual ascent (20 inner steps, step size 0.1 with linear annealing), followed by manifold retraction (polar factor for Stiefel; SVD-based retraction for DGram that preserves Gram diagonality while freeing singular values).

**Instability Criterion:** A run is classified unstable if: (1) hard loss spike (val loss > 2×best or > 5.0 after warmup), (2) persistent drift (mean final-20% val loss > 1.2×best), (3) failure to recover (val loss stays > 1.05×best after reaching best), or (4) early termination from non-finite loss.

**Mechanistic Analysis:** Spectral diagnostics logged every 1K steps (singular values, Gram diagonal, Frobenius norms). Formal propositions with proof sketches establishing the gradient (non-)degeneracy properties of Stiefel vs. DGram attention under bounded inputs.

## Results & Highlights

| Assignment | Attn | MLP | Validation Loss | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| UNCONSTRAINED | None | None | 3.3855 | Stable |
| ALL-STIEFEL | Stiefel | Stiefel | 3.3679 | Stable |
| ALL-DGRAM | DGram | DGram | — | Unstable |
| HETERO | Stiefel | DGram | **3.3544** | **Stable (Best)** |
| HETERO-INV | DGram | Stiefel | — | Unstable |

- **HETERO is the clear winner**, achieving 0.0311 lower validation loss than unconstrained and 0.0135 lower than all-Stiefel. The improvement comes from the heterogeneity itself, not just from having constraints.
- **Every DGram-attention configuration is unstable** under shared hyperparameters — both ALL-DGRAM and HETERO-INV fail. This is consistent across both runs, not a fluke.
- **Spectral analysis confirms the mechanism**: DGram attention weights exhibit explosive singular value growth (σ_max reaches extremely large values before instability), while Stiefel attention keeps σ_max bounded at 1. DGram MLP weights also grow in spectral norm but remain stable.
- **Formal results**: Proposition 4.1 proves Stiefel attention maintains ‖J_softmax‖_F ≥ c(T,d,R) > 0 under bounded inputs. Proposition 4.2 proves DGram admits feasible weights where ‖J_softmax‖_F < ε for arbitrarily small ε, via explicit construction with scaled identity blocks.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The module-specific manifold assignment idea is genuinely novel and practically useful. The formal analysis distinguishing Stiefel and DGram in the attention context adds theoretical depth beyond mere empiricism. However, the paper only tests two manifold types — the space of possible geometries is much richer.
- **Rigor:** The shared-hyperparameter comparison is a double-edged sword. It isolates the effect of manifold assignment cleanly, but as the author acknowledges, it doesn't rule out that DGram attention could be stabilized with retuned LR, explicit scale control, or weight decay on DGram-managed weights. The instability could be a hyperparameter sensitivity issue rather than a fundamental geometric limitation. The author is commendably transparent about this.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong. Code is open-source, all hyperparameters are fully specified in the appendix (Tables 2–5), the instability criterion is precisely defined, and the model/dataset (GPT-2 small on OpenWebText) is accessible. A motivated researcher could reproduce all results.
- **Significance:** The practical impact depends on whether the HETERO advantage scales. The 0.0311 loss improvement over unconstrained at 124M scale is modest. If the gap grows at larger scales, or if the principle extends to other architectures, this could meaningfully influence optimizer design. At minimum, it provides a principled starting point for module-aware optimizer configuration.
- **Red flags:** Single seed (1337) for all experiments. The validation loss gap between HETERO and ALL-STIEFEL (0.0135) is small enough that multi-seed confirmation would strengthen the claim substantially. The paper is a workshop paper (4 pages + appendices), so depth is inherently limited. No downstream task evaluation — only pretraining validation loss is reported.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated research question that challenges an implicit assumption in the community.
- Excellent mechanistic analysis — the progression from empirical observation (instability) to spectral diagnostics (singular value growth) to formal proof (gradient degeneracy) is thorough and convincing.
- The asymmetric finding (attention needs spectral control, MLP doesn't) has clear architectural intuition behind it: softmax's competitive normalization vs. GELU's coordinate-wise application.
- Transparent about limitations and careful with claims — explicitly states this is not a proof that DGram attention is inherently unstable under all settings.
- Complete reproducibility package with open code, full hyperparameter tables, and precise instability criteria.

## Limitations

- Only tested on GPT-2 small (124M) on OpenWebText — a single model scale and dataset. Generalization to larger models, different architectures, and different data regimes is unknown.
- Only two manifold types tested (Stiefel and DGram). Other structured matrix manifolds (e.g., low-rank, spectral-bounded with different norms) are unexplored.
- Single random seed (1337) for all experiments. The modest loss gap between HETERO and ALL-STIEFEL needs multi-seed confirmation.
- Shared hyperparameters may unfairly penalize DGram-attention configurations — they might be stable with different learning rates, weight decay, or explicit scale regularization. The author acknowledges this but it remains a confound.
- No downstream task evaluation (only pretraining validation loss). It's unclear whether the HETERO loss advantage translates to better task performance.
- Workshop paper format limits depth — 4 pages of main content with most detail in appendices.

## Relevance

本文对大模型训练优化有直接指导意义：揭示了 attention 和 MLP 层在权重空间几何上的不对称性，为设计模块级别的几何感知优化器提供了理论和实验依据。如果你关注 Muon 类优化器或流形约束训练，这项工作的 HETERO 配置是一个值得尝试的实用改进。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does HETERO's advantage scale to larger models (e.g., 1B+, 7B+)? Does the loss gap widen or narrow?
- Can DGram attention be stabilized with explicit spectral regularization (e.g., weight decay on DGram-managed weights, or a learnable spectral-norm penalty)? If so, does it then outperform HETERO?
- What happens with other manifold geometries (e.g., scaled Stiefel with learnable norms, or low-rank plus diagonal)?
- Can we develop an automated or learning-based method to discover optimal per-module manifold assignments across architectures?
- Does the module-asymmetry finding transfer to non-transformer architectures (e.g., Mamba, RWKV) or to attention variants (e.g., multi-query attention, grouped-query attention, sliding-window attention)?
- How do the results change with multi-seed experiments and proper confidence intervals?
- What is the interaction between manifold assignment and learning rate schedule — could adaptive per-module LR scheduling eliminate the need for module-specific manifolds?
