The Geometry of Phase Transitions in Generative Dynamics via Projection Caustics

Ryosuke Sakamoto · Kotaro Sakamoto Kyoto U / U Tokyo 2026-06-12
一句话总结：从微分几何视角提出"投影焦散"机制解释扩散模型去噪中的类相变行为，设计临界边界检测器（CBD）定位分支切换窗口，实现高效语义控制干预。
⬡ Key Finding
Near projection caustics—where nearest-point projection onto the data support becomes non-unique—competing branches create a log-sum-exp free energy ridge that sharply reorients the score, producing abrupt phase transitions in continuous diffusion sampling.
◈ Key Metric
96% accuracy
CBD-guided classifier on CIFAR-10 DDPM using only ~4% of steps (10 of 250) vs. 4% for random-band control.
→ Why This Matters
A lightweight, model-agnostic diagnostic pinpoints the exact sampling window where intervention matters—cutting compute ~25× without sacrificing control quality. Works across DDPM, DiT, EDM2, SD 3.5.
⚠ Critical Edge
CBD band location is model- and scheduler-dependent. SD 3.5 correlations are far weaker (Pearson −0.470 vs 0.928 on CIFAR-10), suggesting the mechanism degrades in latent-space models.
Methodology
  1. Free-energy landscape — Denoising viewed as gradient descent on Fσ(x) = −σ² log pσ(x); score follows steepest descent.
  2. Projection regularity — Defines projection-regular regions (unique nearest point → smooth energy) vs. projection caustics (multiple nearest points → ridge singularity).
  3. Asymptotic normal forms — In regular regions: Fσ = ½dist² + O(σ²). Near caustics: log-sum-exp over competing branches with sharp softmax switching at O(σ²) scale (Theorems 2.1, 2.3).
  4. CBD diagnostic — Measures normalized score-direction instability via finite-difference estimates (CBDTAD); pseudo-online variant probes low-noise states to detect future barriers.
Results & Highlights
CBD ↔ LPIPS correlation ρ = 0.928
CBD-guided class accuracy 96–100%
Random-band control 4%
Intervention steps used ~4% (10/250)
Cross-arch profile 3-regime universal
SD 3.5 Spearman (CBD vs ℓ₂) −0.887
SD 3.5 Pearson (outlier prompt) −0.470
Strengths
  • Elegant bridge from classical differential geometry to modern generative modeling
  • CBD is lightweight, model-agnostic, requires no retraining
  • Rigorous asymptotic analysis with formal theorems via Laplace method
  • Strong CIFAR-10 results with controlled random-band baselines
  • Cross-architecture portability: DDPM, DiT-XL, EDM2, SD 3.5
Limitations
  • Smooth-manifold / nondegenerate-Hessian assumptions may not hold for real data
  • CBD band location needs calibration per model and scheduler
  • SD 3.5 results noticeably weaker; latent-space mechanism less clean
  • No FID/CLIP metrics for prompt-switching experiments
  • Degenerate caustic regimes (Airy/Pearcey) deferred to future work
Critical Assessment
Novelty High — First precise geometric mechanism tied to nearest-point projection uniqueness; log-sum-exp normal form is a clean contribution
Rigor High — Formal theorems, clear assumptions, honest about degenerate regimes
Reproducibility Moderate — CBD is algorithmically clear but some hyperparameters (perturbation magnitude, probe time) not fully specified
Significance High — 96% accuracy with 4% steps is compelling; phase-aware prompt switching in SD 3.5 suggests real utility
Relevance / 相关性

从几何视角深入分析扩散模型生成过程中的"相变"机制，与理解扩散模型动力学行为、可控生成等研究方向高度相关。CBD为检测和利用生成过程中的关键时间窗口提供了实用工具，对改进扩散模型的引导控制和编辑具有参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration
  1. How does CBD behave in latent diffusion models with non-Gaussian latent distributions? Does the projection-caustic mechanism apply when latent space has complex topology?
  2. Can CBD dynamically adapt solver step sizes or guidance strength—allocating computation only during instability windows?
  3. How does the instability profile change with different noise schedulers (cosine vs. linear)? Can scheduler design minimize unnecessary instability?
  4. Are Airy/Pearcey-type degenerate transitions observable in practice when the Laplace method breaks down?
  5. Could the projection-caustic perspective inform training strategies that regularize data-support geometry for smoother dynamics?
