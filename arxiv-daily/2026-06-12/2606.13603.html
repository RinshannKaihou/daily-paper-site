Beyond the Commitment Boundary: Probing Epiphenomenal Chain-of-Thought in Large Reasoning Models

Daniel Scalena · Sara Candussio · Luca Bortolussi · Elisabetta Fersini · Malvina Nissim · Gabriele Sarti

Key Metric
55 %

of CoT tokens saved via probe-mediated early exit — across 3 model families & 4 benchmarks — with negligible accuracy loss.

Why This Matters

Epiphenomenal CoT directly challenges the assumption that reasoning traces faithfully reflect model thinking — critical for trustworthy AI and safety monitoring.

⚠ Critical Edge

Probe labels come from the same truncation procedure they aim to replace — a circularity acknowledged but unresolved.

Methodology → four stages

1

Step-level attribution. Incrementally truncate the CoT at each sentence; greedily decode an answer from every prefix to obtain per-step confidence pi.

2

Boundary identification. The commitment boundary i* is the step with the largest confidence jump Δi. Validated via numeric perturbation (pre-boundary corruption: 27 % preserved vs. post-boundary: 72 %).

3

Attention probing. Small causal attention probe classifies hidden states into answer-formation stages (no-guess / mid-guess / final-guess). Trained on MATH-500; generalizes OOD.

4

Early exit. Halt generation after k consecutive final-guess predictions; elicit answer from the truncated trace.

Results & Highlights

Largest confidence jump is 4.6× the second-largest — commitment is abrupt, not gradual.
Boundary position is model-family dependent: Gemma converges at 13–23 % of CoT; gpt-oss-20b at 43–68 %.
Probe detection: 92–98 % in-distribution, 69–99 % OOD; near-zero miss rates.
MATH-500 early exit at k=5: 98 % of full-CoT accuracy retained while saving 26 % tokens.
Fixed-percentage truncation loses 23 pp at matched savings — probe consistently wins.

Strengths vs Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean truncation-based attribution sidesteps perturbation noise
  • Thorough evaluation: 3 model families, 4 benchmarks, extensive ablations
  • Simple early-exit mechanism with consistent savings across all settings
  • Directly challenges CoT faithfulness assumptions — important for AI safety
  • Honest limitations discussion; code release committed

Limitations

  • Only single-answer tasks (math, logic, science QA); open-ended & agentic settings untested
  • Greedy decoding ignores meaningful probability redistribution beyond argmax
  • Sentence-level granularity — commitment may occur mid-sentence
  • Probe circularity: supervised by labels from the same truncation method
  • Open-weight models only; closed-weight frontier models not studied
  • Non-trivial early-fire rates (up to 21 %) understated for safety-critical use

Open Questions
