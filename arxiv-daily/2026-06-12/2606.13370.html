A Quantitative Experimental Repeated Measures Study of Training Dynamics in a Small Llama Style Language Model Under a Compute-Aware Token Budget

Joe Dwyer · ECPI University, Virginia Beach, VA
系统研究了小型Llama模型在有限计算预算下的训练动态，发现验证损失在约400万token时达到最优（2.80），随后持续退化至终点的3.90，揭示了过训练中隐藏的回退和不稳定性。
Key Finding
Validation loss is non-monotonic — drops to ~2.80 at 4M tokens, then degrades to 3.90 by 20M. More tokens can hurt.
Key Metric
2.80 → 3.90 ↑ 39% loss degradation from optimal
Why This Matters
Endpoint metrics are deeply misleading. Interval-level telemetry reveals hidden instability that final checkpoints obscure.
Critical Edge
Classical overfitting on a 2M-token corpus seen ~10×. No baseline interventions tested. Generalizability unknown.

Seeds6 (101–606)
Checkpoints21 per seed (~1M token intervals)
Observations126 total (6 × 21)
HardwareCPU-only, full precision
CodeZenodo (Dwyer, 2026)
Key Results
Non-monotonic loss — Optimal at interval 4 (~4M tokens), then degrades continuously to endpoint.
Backslides accelerate — Frequency rises from 0.0 (int 2) to 4.83 (ints 12–15), indicating persistent instability.
No stable phase — Stable phase share = 0.0 at every interval; criteria never met.
Seed variance grows — SD increases from 0.0167 (init) to 0.0355 (final); trajectories diverge over time.
All hypotheses addressed — H₀₁ rejected (stability changes), H₀₃ rejected (non-monotonic), H₀₄ not rejected (no stable phase found).
Strengths & Limitations
Strengths
  • Exceptionally rigorous statistical methodology — proper RM-ANOVA with sphericity checks, Greenhouse-Geisser correction, and mixed-effects robustness check
  • Full transparency: all hyperparameters, seeds, hardware disclosed; reproducible on any laptop
  • Telemetry framework (backslides, spikes, rolling volatility) provides a useful vocabulary for training health
  • Honest about limitations — author explicitly cautions against generalization
Limitations
  • 4.26M params on TinyStories is far from any practical deployment scenario
  • No baseline interventions tested (early stopping, regularization, data augmentation)
  • Degradation is expected overfitting from ~10× data repetition, not a novel finding
  • Stable-phase criteria never explicitly defined — impossible to evaluate stringency
  • Single-author, single-dataset, single-architecture study

Red Flags
  • η²g values ~0.999 are trivially high — comparing init (loss=8.36) to trained checkpoints just detects "training works"
  • "Stable phase" criteria referenced as "predefined" but never explicitly defined in the paper
  • No intervention comparison — practical implications limited to "monitor your training" (standard practice)
  • 20M tokens on TinyStories with 4.26M model = classical overfitting, framed as novel dynamics discovery
Questions for Further Exploration
  1. How do dynamics change with model scale (100M, 1B params)? Does the optimal checkpoint shift proportionally?
  2. Would standard regularization (dropout, data augmentation, weight decay tuning) eliminate late-stage degradation?
  3. How does cosine annealing with warmup compare to the linear decay used here?
  4. Can the stable-phase indicator be formalized as an automated early-stopping criterion at larger scale?
  5. What role does data repetition play? Would 20M unique tokens (no repetition) yield different dynamics?
