> **一句话总结：** 本文通过有限域算术受控实验揭示RL后训练提升推理能力的两个核心机制：策略选择（RL将问题路由到SFT阶段学到的最优推理策略）和策略改进（RL数据难度递增驱动已有策略的优化），并证明策略放大和策略组合分别是这两个机制的可观测现象，强调SFT数据多样性和RL数据难度递增是激活这些机制的关键。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides a rigorous mechanistic decomposition of how RL post-training improves reasoning, using controlled finite-field arithmetic experiments with Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct. Two core mechanisms are identified: **strategy selection** (RL routes problems to the best reasoning pattern learned during SFT, achieving near-perfect accuracy when diverse strategies are available) and **strategy improvement** (RL strengthens existing reasoning patterns on harder problems, but only when RL data difficulty exceeds SFT data). Strategy amplification and composition are shown to be downstream phenomena of selection and improvement, respectively. Crucially, RL does not invent new reasoning strategies—it only selects and refines what pre-training/SFT already provided, underscoring the primacy of diverse SFT data.

## 核心贡献

- **识别RL后训练的两个核心机制：** 策略选择（将不同问题路由到最优推理策略）和策略改进（在更难问题上优化已有推理模式），并通过受控实验清晰分离两者。
- **证明SFT数据多样性是策略选择的前提：** 仅在SFT阶段同时训练前向和后向推理策略时，RL才能将评估问题路由到前向策略、反演问题路由到后向策略，准确率从~80%跃升至≥95%。
- **证明RL数据难度递增是策略改进的必要条件：** 在与SFT相同难度的数据上做RL不会带来泛化提升；仅在6-9步（SFT为2-5步）数据上训练才能激活策略改进。
- **统一解释已有现象：** 策略放大（如DeepSeek-R1中的回溯行为增强）是策略选择的可观测结果；策略组合是策略改进的一个实例。
- **强调预训练/SFT数据质量的决定性作用：** RL不能创造新的推理能力，只能选择和改进已有的策略，因此SFT数据的多样性比RL算法本身更关键。

## Key Contributions

- **Mechanistic decomposition into selection and improvement:** The first work to cleanly isolate two distinct mechanisms through controlled experiments where SFT strategies and RL difficulty are independently varied.
- **Strategy selection as the dominant driver:** Mixed-strategy SFT models achieve ≥95% accuracy on 6-9 step problems after RL, compared to ~50-60% for single-strategy models, demonstrating that the ability to route problems to the right strategy accounts for most performance gains.
- **Strategy improvement requires harder RL data:** Table 1 shows that RL on in-distribution (2-5 step) data yields no improvement for single-strategy models and only selection for mixed models; RL on harder (6-9 step) data is necessary for genuine strategy improvement.
- **Unification of prior observations:** Strategy amplification (e.g., backtracking emergence in DeepSeek-R1) is explained as a consequence of strategy selection routing toward effective strategies. Strategy composition is a form of strategy improvement requiring compositional examples in SFT.
- **Practical training guidance:** Diversity in pre-training/SFT data is essential for strategy selection; RL datasets should contain a difficulty curriculum to activate strategy improvement.

## Methodology

The authors design a synthetic **finite-field arithmetic task** over GF(11) (with GF(13) for robustness checks) where integers are replaced by abstract symbols, deliberately breaking away from the math reasoning abilities already in the pre-trained model. Two problem types exist: **evaluation** (forward computation through arithmetic steps) and **inversion** (solving for an unknown variable by undoing operations). Two reasoning strategies are defined: **forward reasoning** (natural for evaluation) and **backward reasoning** (natural for inversion).

**Training pipeline:** Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct → LoRA SFT on 2-5 step problems with controlled strategy exposure (forward-only, backward-only, or mixed) → GRPO RL on harder 6-9 step problems with outcome-level binary rewards. SFT uses 2048 examples for 20 epochs; RL uses 1024 prompts for 500-1000 steps. Three seeds per configuration.

**Key experimental variations:** (1) SFT strategy composition (F-only, B-only, FB mixed); (2) RL dataset difficulty (in-distribution 2-5 steps vs. harder 6-9 steps vs. extreme 6-15 steps); (3) RL dataset tilt (varying evaluation/inversion ratios to study amplification); (4) composition problems requiring sequential sub-problem solving with result propagation.

## Results & Highlights

- **Mixed SFT + hard RL = near-perfect performance:** FB models reach ≥95% accuracy on 6-9 step problems after RL, compared to ~50-60% for F-only or B-only models (Fig. 2, center).
- **Strategy selection is fast (~40 RL steps), strategy improvement is slow:** Fig. 2 right panel shows routing completes quickly while disaggregated reward continues improving over hundreds of steps.
- **Single-strategy models are fundamentally limited:** Without an alternative strategy, RL cannot compensate—the model plateaus at the ceiling of its single approach.
- **RL on easy data is counterproductive:** Table 1 shows RL on 2-5 step data sometimes degrades performance on harder problems (e.g., B-only drops from 24.5% to 18.4%).
- **Strategy amplification matches dataset composition:** In tilted datasets, the fraction of forward-style generations precisely tracks the fraction of evaluation problems (Fig. 5, right), confirming amplification is a byproduct of selection.
- **Composition requires compositional SFT examples:** Without 2-part composition in SFT, RL yields zero improvement on 3-5 part composition problems (Fig. 6, purple curve flat at ~9% random baseline).
- **Results robust to hyperparameters:** Ablations (Table 2-3) show findings are stable across learning rates, KL coefficients, and group sizes. GF(13) results (Figs. 7-8) replicate the GF(11) findings.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** This is the first work to cleanly decompose RL post-training into selection and improvement through controlled experiments. The finding that RL does not create new strategies but only selects/refines existing ones is a significant and potentially controversial insight.
- **Rigor: Very high.** The synthetic task design is excellent—by using abstract finite-field arithmetic, the authors ensure the pre-trained model has no prior strategies to confound the experiments. The controlled variations (SFT strategy, RL difficulty, dataset tilt) systematically isolate each mechanism. Three seeds, ablations, and GF(13) replication strengthen the claims.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** All experiments run on 1-4 A100 GPUs with SFT taking 1-2 hours and RL 2-3 hours. The synthetic task removes data dependency concerns. LoRA and GRPO are standard. The task itself is precisely specified.
- **Significance: High, with caveats.** The practical implications—prioritize SFT data diversity and RL difficulty curriculum—are actionable and important. However, the gap between synthetic finite-field arithmetic and real-world math/coding reasoning is significant.
- **Potential concerns:**
  - The 1.5B model scale may not capture dynamics in larger models (7B+), where RL might indeed induce novel capabilities as suggested by ProRL (Liu et al. 2025).
  - The finite-field task, while cleverly controlled, is narrow. Whether these mechanisms dominate in real math reasoning, code generation, or multi-step planning remains to be verified.
  - The claim that "RL does not induce novel reasoning capabilities" is strong and based on a single synthetic task. The authors acknowledge this as the most important open question.
  - Strategy "forward" vs "backward" is a clean binary; real reasoning strategies are far more diverse and overlapping, which could complicate the selection/improvement taxonomy.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally clean experimental design that enables causal attribution of performance gains to specific mechanisms.
- The synthetic task is a clever methodological contribution—controlling pre-existing knowledge while maintaining the structure of math reasoning.
- The unification of prior phenomena (amplification, composition) under two core mechanisms provides intellectual clarity to a fragmented literature.
- Honest discussion of limitations, including explicit acknowledgment that RL might induce novel capabilities in settings not captured by their experiments.
- Practical takeaways are clearly stated and immediately actionable for practitioners.

## Limitations

- Experiments are limited to a single 1.5B-parameter model and a synthetic finite-field arithmetic task; generalization to real-world reasoning benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH, HumanEval) is unverified.
- The binary strategy taxonomy (forward/backward) is far simpler than the spectrum of reasoning strategies in real math or code problems.
- The claim that RL cannot create new strategies may be an artifact of the synthetic task design, which only exposes two strategies. In richer settings, RL might combine or interpolate strategies in genuinely novel ways.
- The composition experiments only test sequential sub-problem solving; more complex compositional structures (branching, recursive) are not explored.
- Only GRPO is tested; whether findings transfer to PPO, DPO, or other RL algorithms is unknown.

## Relevance

本文对RL后训练推理能力的机理分解直接关联到如何高效训练推理模型这一核心问题。策略选择和策略改进两个机制的发现为SFT数据多样性和RL数据难度设计提供了明确的实验依据，对理解和优化reasoning模型的训练流程具有直接指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do these mechanisms hold at larger scales (7B, 70B+)? Do larger models exhibit genuinely novel strategy creation during RL, or are they also limited to selection and improvement?
- Can strategy selection be detected in real math reasoning settings through model internals (attention patterns, probing), rather than manual strategy classification?
- How does the difficulty curriculum interact with reward model quality? In real settings, difficulty estimation is noisy—does this degrade strategy improvement?
- What happens with more than two strategies? Does RL learn complex routing (e.g., strategy A for problem subtype X, strategy B for Y) or does it converge to a single dominant strategy?
- Can the findings explain the emergence of specific behaviors like chain-of-thought lengthening or self-correction in models like DeepSeek-R1?
- How do these mechanisms interact with multi-turn RL or iterative self-improvement pipelines?
