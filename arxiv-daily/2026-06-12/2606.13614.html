Majority-of-Three is Optimal

Divit Rawal & Nikita Zhivotovskiy — UC Berkeley, Dept. of Statistics
PAC-learning VC-theory ensemble-methods majority-vote 2026-06-12
一句话总结：本文证明了在可实现PAC学习框架下，对三个独立一致性分类器进行多数投票是最优学习器，其样本复杂度达到经典的 Θ((d+log(1/δ))/n) 下界，且证明比Hanneke的最优学习器和Larsen的bagging分析大幅简化。
arXiv 2606.13614 ↗
Key Finding
The majority vote of three independently trained consistent classifiers achieves information-theoretically optimal PAC sample complexity — the simplest conceivable voting scheme is already optimal.
Key Metric
≤ 107(d + log 1/δ) / nerror with probability ≥ 1 − δ, matching the classic [EHKV89] lower bound up to a universal constant
Why This Matters
Resolves a COLT 2024 conjecture. Prior optimal learners needed complex nested structures and doubly-logarithmic factors — this paper shows a trivial three-way split + majority vote suffices.
Critical Edge
Realizable PAC setting only — no agnostic extension. Constants (107, 62400) are far from tight. Requires n divisible by 3.

01Methodology

Standard realizable PAC framework: concept class F ⊆ {0,1}X with VC dimension d, unknown target f* ∈ F, i.i.d. samples from distribution P on X.

  1. Split into three blocks. Given n = 3m samples, partition into S₁, S₂, S₃ of size m. Train a deterministic consistent classifier f̂ᵢ on each.
  2. Reduce error to pairwise overlap. Majority errs at x ⟹ ≥ 2 of 3 classifiers wrong. So err(Maj) ≤ P(E₁∩E₂) + P(E₂∩E₃) + P(E₁∩E₃).
  3. Moment bound on two-block overlap (Prop 2.2). Key identity: ES,T[P(A(S)∩A(T))q] = EX[pq(X)²], reduced via a chain-rule factorization and recursive bound Mk ≤ (960/m)·Mk−1.
  4. Moments → high probability. Set q = ⌈log(1/δ)⌉, apply Markov's inequality.

02Results & Highlights

Theorem 2.1 (Main). For every δ ∈ (0,1), every distribution P, every f* ∈ F with VC-dim d ≥ 1:
P( errP(Maj) ≤ 107(d + log(1/δ)) / n ) ≥ 1 − δ
Proposition 2.2 (Overlap moment bound). ‖P(A(S)∩A(T))‖Lq ≤ 62400(d+q)/m for all q ≥ 1, independent blocks S, T of size m.
  • Constants (107, 62400) are not claimed tight — the contribution is the correct O(·) rate.
  • AI-assisted proof simplification (Appendix A): after an initial longer proof, GPT-5.5 Pro helped explore simplifications. All claims author-verified.

03Critical Assessment

Novelty: High Rigor: Excellent Reproducibility: Very High Significance: High

Resolves a concrete open conjecture from Aden-Ali et al. (COLT 2024). The independent-block insight is genuinely new — avoids the doubly-logarithmic factors of nested-sample approaches. Main argument fits ~4 pages.

Strengths

  • Extremely short, elegant proof
  • Simplest possible algorithm turns out optimal
  • Overlap bound (Prop 2.2) is a reusable tool
  • Self-contained; transparent AI disclosure

Limitations

  • Realizable setting only
  • Constants far from tight
  • Requires deterministic ERM selector
  • No experimental validation

04Open Questions

  1. Can the overlap moment bound constant 62400 be tightened toward the information-theoretic limit?
  2. Does the independent-block technique extend to the agnostic PAC setting?
  3. Can majority-of-three combine with subsampling or variance reduction for better practical constants?
  4. Does the recursive conditional-inclusion-probability structure apply to other empirical-process-theory problems?
  5. How does majority-of-three compare empirically with Hanneke's learner or bagging in finite-sample regimes?

05TL;DR

This paper proves that the majority vote of three independently trained consistent classifiers achieves the optimal PAC sample complexity O((d + log(1/δ))/n) in the realizable setting, resolving a conjecture from [AAHLZ24]. The proof is notably short and elegant — the key technical ingredient is a moment bound on the overlap of two independent consistent classifiers' error sets, which avoids the nested-sample structure and doubly-logarithmic factors that plagued prior approaches. The result establishes the simplest possible voting scheme as optimal.
