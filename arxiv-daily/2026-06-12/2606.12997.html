Reliability of Probabilistic Emulation of Physical Systems

Sam F. Greenbury · Radka Jersakova · Paolo Conti · Marjan Famili · Christopher Iliffe Sprague · Edwin Brown · Jason D. McEwen
The Alan Turing Institute & UCL & Industry · 2026-06-12
本文在匹配模型规模和计算预算的严格条件下，系统比较了CRPS损失训练的确定性集成与潜空间流匹配生成模型在四种2D时空物理系统上的概率预测可靠性，发现CRPS集成在覆盖率和推理速度上均显著优于生成式方法，并开源了AutoCast和AutoSim两个框架。
Key Finding
CRPS-trained ensembles are both ~40× faster and more reliably calibrated than latent-space flow matching generative models for probabilistic physical emulation.
Key Metric
~40×
inference speedup: CRPS at 14–175 ms/sample vs. FM at 647–715 ms/sample, with consistently lower Coverage MAE.
Why This Matters
The field's rush toward generative models may be misplaced — CRPS ensembles are the stronger default for uncertainty-aware physical simulation.
Critical Edge
Limited to 64×64 grids on four 2D PDEs; scaling to higher resolutions or genuinely multi-modal forecasts remains unproven.
Head-to-Head: Two Pipelines Compared

CRPS Ensemble ✓

  • 80M-param ViT, αfCRPS loss
  • M=8 members, noise via AdaLN-Zero
  • Channel concatenation conditioning
  • Checkpoint by Winkler score
  • ~40× faster inference

Flow Matching (Generative)

  • 80M-param ViT, FM loss in latent space
  • 49M autoencoder frozen (64²→16²×8)
  • 50-step Euler ODE sampling
  • AdaLN-Zero backbone modulation
  • Matches coverage only in ambient space

Key Ablation Findings
Ambient vs. Latent: FM recovers coverage in ambient space, but infeasible for high-dim problems. CRPS robust to latent compression.
Loss variants: fCRPS > αfCRPS > standard CRPS for coverage quality.
Ensemble size: M=4/8/16 show no significant coverage difference — even small ensembles suffice.
Backbone: ViT outperforms U-Net for CRPS ensembles on these datasets.
ODE steps: Reducing steps speeds up FM but can degrade coverage.
Diffusion ≈ FM: Diffusion shows comparable coverage to flow matching.
Strengths & Limitations
  • +Matched compute & model size — the fairest possible head-to-head comparison.
  • +Comprehensive ablation suite (10+ ablations) covering architecture, loss, and hyperparameters.
  • +Two open-source frameworks: AutoCast + AutoSim with The Well interoperability.
  • +100-step autoregressive rollout evaluation addresses long-horizon reliability gap in prior work.
  • −64×64 grids far below typical scientific simulation resolutions; scaling unknown.
  • −Only four 2D systems — no 3D, no multi-physics, no shock-dominated flows.
  • −No multi-modal forecast evaluation; generative models could advantage in bifurcation scenarios.
  • −CRPS proper only for marginals; no joint calibration (energy score) explored.

Relevance
这项工作对科学机器学习和物理系统仿真中的不确定性量化有直接参考价值。系统比较了当前最主流的两种概率预测方法，结论对需要可靠不确定性估计的代理模型构建具有指导意义。开源框架AutoCast和AutoSim也便于快速复现和扩展相关研究。
Open Questions
  1. How do findings scale to 256×256, 512×512, and 3D systems where ambient training is infeasible?
  2. Can distributional autoencoder objectives recover generative coverage in latent space?
  3. Do generative models regain advantage for genuinely multi-modal forecasts (bifurcations, phase transitions)?
  4. Would conformal prediction post-hoc calibration tighten intervals more on CRPS than on generative models?
  5. Can hybrid approaches (CRPS ensemble + generative refinement for tail quantiles) combine the best of both?
