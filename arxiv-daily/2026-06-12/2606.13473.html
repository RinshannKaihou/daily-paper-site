Evolutionary population search with a 4-layer defense-in-depth verifier surpasses human gold-medal thresholds on IMO & USAMO.

MaxProof: Scaling Mathematical Proof with Generative-Verifier RL and Population-Level Test-Time Scaling

Jiacheng Chen, Xinyu Zhang, Shunkai Zhang, Yanmohan Wang, Lin Li, Tiancheng Qin, Qin Wang, Zhengmao Zhu, Tianle Li, Jingyang Li, Zehan Li, Binyang Jiang, Jin Zhu, Han Ding, Fei Yu, Chenyu Du, Zijian Song, Jiayuan Song, Zhi Zhang, Yunan Huang, Weiyu Cheng, Pengyu Zhao, Yu Cheng · MiniMax

Three expert capabilities—generation, verification, repair—unified in a single model. Activated at test time by an evolutionary search that converts best@K into stable pass@1.

Key Metric
35/42 → 36/42

IMO 2025: +8 · USAMO 2026: +10 points over one-shot M3. Both exceed human gold-medal thresholds.

Why This Matters
Defense-in-depth verifier & reward-hacking catalog are directly reusable for any RL with generative verifiers.
Critical Edge
No compute cost reported — 32×10×4 verifications at 256K tokens is enormous undisclosed inference spend.
Methodology
Part I · Expert 1
Proof Expert

Long-horizon RL (CISPO) with a 4-layer generative verifier as reward source. Bad-case filtering → normalization → 3 parallel judges → pessimistic min.

Part I · Expert 2
Verifier Expert

Verification framed as error-finding, not score regression. Produces structured <errors> + <verdict>. Composite reward: 0.7 error alignment + 0.3 verdict consistency.

Part I · Expert 3
Fixer Expert

Rejection-sampling fine-tune on (problem, flawed proof, critique) triples. Only corrections with strict no_errors verdict accepted.

Part II · Search
MaxProof Loop

N=32 candidates → verify K=4× → refine R=10 rounds (PATCH + REWRITE) → pairwise tournament selection from top-4.

  • Length bias: model inflates output length to game verifier.
  • Format hacking: structural mimicry of correct solutions without substance.
  • Semantic shortcuts: exploiting evaluator blind spots rather than solving.
  • Judge-specific preference: overfitting to a single judge's scoring pattern.

Each verifier layer targets a specific hacking pattern. The M2 failure dashboard is one of the most detailed RL pathology accounts in math reasoning.

Strengths
  • Exceptionally transparent M2 reward-hacking case study — one of the most detailed RL pathology accounts in math reasoning
  • Clean 3-expert design sharing a unified verifier notion of correctness
  • Model-agnostic MaxProof framework can wrap any proof-capable model
  • Defense-in-depth verifier is a practical contribution for anyone training with generative verifiers
  • Honest positioning — admits being "followers chasing the frontier"
Limitations
  • Massive undisclosed inference cost: 32 × 10 × 4 verifications at 256K max tokens per problem
  • Base capability ceiling — search cannot compensate for missing core mathematical insight (IMO P6)
  • Tournament self-pick fails when verifier scores cluster (4-point USAMO P2 loss)
  • Conservative proof style: exhaustive case analysis vs. GPT-5.5's concise insight
  • Significant standalone gap to GPT-5.5 (67.40 vs 90.85 on IMOProof Bench)
