Multiagent Protocols with Aggregated Confidence Signals

Ali Elahi, Barbara Di Eugenio — University of Illinois Chicago
multi-agent-systems confidence-estimation LLM-reliability calibration

本文提出三种基于置信度聚合的多智能体协议（WSV、CGA、HID），通过对多个LLM的置信度信号进行跨模型校准与融合，生成单一的系统级置信度，在五个基准上显著提升了置信度区分度（AUARC提升5-10%），同时恢复了标准多智能体辩论在歧义任务上的F1损失。

Key Finding
Multiagent debate produces stronger confidence signals than any single agent — the first system-level confidence method for multiagent LLM output.
Key Metric
+5–10pp AUARCacross all 5 benchmarks; MAD F1 losses up to −8.37pp recovered
Why This Matters
As multiagent LLM systems enter production, reliable system-level confidence is crucial for human oversight and downstream decisions.
Critical Edge
Only 2-agent classification tested. Bayesian fusion assumes violated independence. F1 gains marginal on some tasks. Cost unanalyzed.

Three Protocols

WSV
Weighted Stream Voting — treats all 4 (agent × stage) outputs as independent evidence. Learned weights per stream. Soft-votes over candidate labels. Best F1.
CGA
Confidence Gating with Aggregation — per-agent learned threshold decides whether to switch post-debate. Surviving streams fused via Bayesian update. Best AUARC.
HID
Human-Inspired Debate — deterministic routing: agree & confident → skip; one confident → persuade; both uncertain → debate. Bayesian fusion. Best AUARC.

Methodology

Setup

Two-agent debate: each agent produces a zero-shot prediction + confidence, then a post-debate prediction after seeing the other's chain-of-thought → 4 streams total.

Confidence Estimation

  • Sequence probability (mean log-likelihood, token-bias corrected)
  • Self-report (verbalized, 3 repetitions @ temp 0.3)

Calibration & Optimization

  • Beta, 3rd-order polynomial, isotonic regression — fit per-stream
  • Protocol parameters optimized via TPE: NLL + ECE penalty + L₂ reg
  • Cross-model comparability via learned per-stream transformations

Key takeaways

  • WSV: best F1 across tasks and calibrators
  • CGA & HID: best AUARC (6/6 significant pairs on BoolQ, Vast)
  • Calibration essential for F1, less critical for AUARC
  • Self-report confidence shifts more meaningfully through debate
  • Confident agents more persuasive when correct — but persuasion rates move together for right/wrong, explaining MAD unreliability

Assessment

Strengths

  • First principled approach to system-level confidence for multiagent LLM outputs
  • Comprehensive evaluation: 14 models (3B–32B), 6 pairs, 5 benchmarks, 4 task types
  • Thoughtful analysis of debate dynamics from a confidence perspective
  • Clean, interpretable protocol design (voting, gating, Bayesian fusion)
  • Transparent reporting with statistical significance and per-pair breakdowns

Limitations

  • Restricted to 2-agent setting; scalability to 3+ agents untested
  • Only classification tasks; no generation, math, or open-ended evaluation
  • Bayesian fusion independence assumption violated in practice
  • Marginal F1 gains on some benchmarks (EZStance, HatEval)
  • Validation-set parameter tuning — overfitting risk with small splits
  • Computational overhead of full pipeline not analyzed

Open Questions

  1. How do protocols scale to 3+ agents? Would Copula-based fusion help?
  2. Can aggregated confidence drive selective prediction, routing, or human-in-the-loop oversight in production?
  3. How robust are learned per-stream transformations under distribution shift?
  4. What is the computational overhead vs. single-agent inference?
  5. Performance on open-ended generation tasks with no discrete label space?

研究相关性 / Relevance

与LLM推理评估和可靠性领域高度相关——提出了为多智能体LLM系统生成可信赖的系统级置信度的方法，这对于构建可靠的LLM推理管道具有重要意义。置信度校准和跨模型置信度比较的分析对理解LLM输出的可靠性有直接参考价值。

Experimental scope

  • 14 open-source models across 5 families (LLaMA, Mistral, Phi, Gemma, Qwen)
  • 5 benchmarks: SciQ, BoolQ, Vast, EZStance, HatEval
  • 4 task types: scientific QA, stance detection, Boolean QA, hate speech
  • Reproducible: single NVIDIA H200 GPU, 18 days
