Can I Buy Your KV Cache?

Luoyuan Zhang · Harbin Institute of Technology, Shenzhen · June 2026

Treat document KV caches as precomputed, tradeable artifacts — a "prefill CDN" — where a publisher computes once and every downstream agent loads it, skipping prefill entirely. Token-exact, 9–50× cheaper, and break-even at the second read.

Key Finding

Loading a saved KV cache and continuing generation is token-exact with from-scratch prefill under greedy decoding.

Key Metric

49.7×

compute savings at 3774 tokens; break-even N* ≈ 1.02

Why This Matters

A prefill CDN could save millions per popular document at scale — transforming how agents share computation.

Critical Edge

Shipping raw KV fails — egress exceeds savings. Model-binding locks to single model/precision. ≥7.5K token regime excluded.

Core Contributions

  • Prefill CDN framing — document KV caches as tradeable computation artifacts, amortizing prefill across readers
  • Token-exact correctness — 24/24 greedy tokens match; max logit diff 0.02 (FP nondeterminism, not semantic divergence)
  • Cost characterization — 8.6–49.7× savings across five context lengths; monotonic with L
  • Shipping vs. hosting — shipping fails (egress $0.049 > savings $0.019), hosting wins (eliminates egress, ~40× provider margin)

Methodology

The simplest safe form of KV reuse: a single document as a shared prefix, with continuation tokens appended after it. The publisher runs one forward pass to produce K(c), serializes per-layer tensors to disk with metadata. The consumer deserializes, reconstructs the cache object, and decodes continuation without re-prefilling. Correctness requires proper position IDs (for RoPE) and a full attention mask spanning L + |q| positions. Intentionally minimal: one model, one device, prefix-only.

Verdict

Strengths

Extremely clear writing; "three objections" framing + CDN analogy. Honest scope — excludes memory-pressure regime rather than cherry-picking. Clean formal correctness argument. Shipping vs. hosting insight is genuine. Open-source code.

Limitations

Model-binding: exact reuse requires identical model + precision. Prefix-only: no multi-fragment RAG fusion. Single device (M1/MPS). ≥7.5K tokens excluded — exactly where prefill hurts most. No comparison with production prefix caching (vLLM, SGLang).

Open Questions

  2. Real-world frequency of cross-party document reuse vs. intra-cluster prefix reuse?
  3. Can mixed-precision KV compression (SpectrumKV-style) preserve token-exact equivalence?
  4. Cost of cache warming when a model version invalidates all existing artifacts?
  5. Extension to multimodal KV caches (video prefill) where compute costs are even more extreme?
