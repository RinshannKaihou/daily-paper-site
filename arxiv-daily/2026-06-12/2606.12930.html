Is Spurious Correlation Removal Always Learnable?

Yibo Zhou, Bo Li, Hai-Miao Hu et al. Beihang University · Xiamen University PMLR 306 arXiv 2606.12930

一句话：在黑盒可采样稀疏恢复原语下，去除虚假相关性存在计算-统计差距——穷举搜索可用多项式样本恢复不变子空间，但多项式时间算法无法做到；引入环境多样性参数 γ 刻画可辨识性与相变。

Key Finding
Computational–statistical gap exists for invariant subspace recovery: poly-time algorithms cannot match exhaustive search.
Key Metric
27× fewer samples for exhaustive vs. best poly-time method (d=50–80)
Why This Matters
A few diverse environments beat many similar ones — γ dominates sample size, dimension, and SNR in impact.
Critical Edge
Hardness conditional on a non-standard primitive; entire theory assumes linear-Gaussian models.

Results & Numbers

  • Exhaustive search reaches 75% accuracy with up to 27× fewer samples than best poly-time method on synthetic hard instances (d=50–80)
  • Monotone compute–accuracy frontier: filtering (O(d)) → greedy (O(kd)) → beam search → exhaustive — each step improves recovery
  • Phase transition confirmed: poly-time methods transition sharply from failure to success at critical n*, while exhaustive stays near-optimal throughout
  • ColoredMNIST (γ=0.4): IRM trails oracle by 10 pts at N=500; gap shrinks to 2 pts at N=2000
  • High diversity improves worst-group accuracy by 22–25 pts on CelebA/Waterbirds
  • γ̂repr computed on ERM penultimate-layer features correlates with worst-group accuracy across all three benchmarks
  • Varying γ from 0.1→0.6 yields +80% accuracy gain, versus +30% from 10× samples, +27% from reducing k, +18% from 10× SNR

Methodology

Problem Formulation (SC Instance)

Orthogonal subspaces Vinv ⊕ Vsp = ℝd. Condition (C1): P(Y|Xinv) identical across environments. Condition (C2): P(Xsp|Xinv) differs. Goal: recover Vinv.

Hardness Construction

Wraps black-box supervised sparse recovery primitive into multi-environment SC instance. Invariance–predictivity score S(V) = A(V) − λT(V) has uniform margin around Vinv — enabling exhaustive search but ruling out poly-time recovery.

Statistical Theory

Identifiability via KL-divergence invariance gap. Minimax risk Θ(k(d−k)/(n|E|)). Phase transition at n* ∝ k(d−k)/(|E|γ²).

Tractability Templates

Four sufficient conditions for poly-time recovery: sparse basis, spectral eigengap, low-order moment detectability, environment-wise uncorrelatedness.

Key Contributions

  • Conditional comp–stat gap: Constructs instances where invariant subspace is unique maximizer of invariance–predictivity score — poly samples suffice for exhaustive search, but any poly-time constant-accuracy algorithm contradicts the primitive
  • Environment diversity γ: γ=0 → unidentifiable; γ>0 → recoverable with sample complexity ∝ 1/γ²
  • Sharp minimax risk: E[dist(V̂,Vinv)²] = Θ(k(d−k)/(n|E|)), matching the regular parametric Grassmannian rate
  • Phase transition: Critical n* ∝ k(d−k)/(|E|γ²); above it estimation error O(k(d−k)/(n|E|γ²)); below it, constant error floor
  • Practical diagnostics: γ̂repr diversity proxy computed on learned representations — guides data collection before investing in complex algorithms

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Unified framework: computational hardness + statistical identifiability + sample complexity under a single diversity parameter γ
  • Honest about limitations — conditional nature of hardness, linear-Gaussian restriction, oracle-vs-learned gap all explicitly stated
  • "A few diverse environments can be more informative than many similar ones" — actionable and well-supported
  • Phase transition gives practitioners concrete guidance: n* ∝ k(d−k)/(|E|γ²)
  • Comprehensive experiments: synthetic + semi-synthetic (ColoredMNIST) + real benchmarks

Limitations

  • Linear-Gaussian assumption — entire theory; nonlinear extension is non-trivial future work
  • Conditional hardness — depends on non-standard primitive, not directly on Planted Clique
  • Theory–experiment gap — synthetic instances are inspired by but not identical to the theoretical construction
  • Limited real-world validation — only ColoredMNIST, CelebA, Waterbirds; no DomainBed/WILDS
  • Scalability — experiments only up to d=80, far from real-world dimensions

Open Questions

  1. Can the computational–statistical gap extend to nonlinear invariant subspaces in deep networks?
  2. How robust is γ̂repr to the choice of representation (ERM vs. invariant-trained vs. random projections)?
  3. With a fixed compute budget, what is the optimal tradeoff between more samples vs. more diverse environments?
  4. Can the phase transition threshold n* be estimated from data without knowing the true Vinv?
  5. Do overparameterized networks naturally satisfy any of the four structural tractability conditions?
