Attention layers need spectral-bounded Stiefel constraints; MLP layers benefit from looser DGram — the heterogeneous combination wins.

Different Layers, Different Manifolds Kirato Yoshihara · Univ. of Osaka 2026-06-12 ICML 2026 Workshop
optimization manifold geometry transformer weight-space muon
Key Metric
3.3544
HETERO validation loss — 0.0311 below unconstrained, 0.0135 below all-Stiefel. Both DGram-attention configs fail.
Why This Matters

Reveals asymmetric geometry needs between attention and MLP layers — module-aware optimizer design could meaningfully improve large-model training.

Critical Edge

Shared hyperparameters may unfairly penalize DGram-attention — instability could be hyperparameter sensitivity, not a fundamental geometric limitation.

Strengths

  1. Clean research question challenging the implicit assumption of uniform manifold constraints.
  2. Excellent mechanistic chain: empirical observation → spectral diagnostics → formal proof of gradient degeneracy.
  3. Architectural intuition is sound — softmax's competitive normalization vs. GELU's coordinate-wise application explains the asymmetry.
  4. Transparent about limitations; careful not to over-claim about DGram being inherently unstable in all settings.
  5. Full reproducibility: open code, complete hyperparameter tables, precise instability criteria.

Limitations

  1. Single model scale (GPT-2 124M) and dataset (OpenWebText) — generalization unknown.
  2. Only two manifold types tested; richer structured manifolds unexplored.
  3. Single random seed (1337); the 0.0135 gap vs. all-Stiefel needs multi-seed confirmation.
  4. Shared hyperparameters may confound — DGram-attention might stabilize with retuned LR or scale regularization.
  5. No downstream task evaluation; unclear if val-loss advantage transfers to real benchmarks.
  6. Workshop format (4 pp + appendix) limits depth.

Open Questions

  1. Does HETERO's advantage scale to 1B+ or 7B+ models? Does the gap widen or narrow?
  2. Can DGram attention be stabilized with explicit spectral regularization — and would it then beat HETERO?
  3. What happens with other manifold geometries (scaled Stiefel, low-rank + diagonal)?
  4. Can we automate per-module manifold assignment discovery across architectures?
  5. Does the asymmetry transfer to non-transformer architectures (Mamba, RWKV) or attention variants (GQA, sliding window)?
  6. Could adaptive per-module LR scheduling eliminate the need for module-specific manifolds entirely?

中文摘要

一句话总结
Transformer 的 attention 和 MLP 模块对流形几何约束有系统性不对称偏好：attention 需要谱有界的 Stiefel 约束，MLP 可受益于更宽松的 DGram 约束。HETERO（Stiefel attention + DGram MLP）在 GPT-2 预训练中取得最低验证损失 3.3544，而 DGram 用于 attention 的配置全部训练不稳定。根本原因是 DGram 允许 Q/K 权重奇异值无限增长，放大 attention logits 导致 softmax 饱和和梯度退化。
研究意义
对大模型训练优化有直接指导意义：揭示了 attention 和 MLP 层在权重空间几何上的不对称性，为设计模块级别的几何感知优化器提供了理论和实验依据。如果你关注 Muon 类优化器或流形约束训练，HETERO 配置是一个值得尝试的实用改进。
