Dense Supervision, Sparse Updates: On the Sparsity and Geometry of On-Policy Distillation

Guo Yu · Wenlin Liu · Yulan Hu · Hao-Xuan Ma · Jun-Peng Jiang · Han-Jia Ye — Nanjing University & Amap/Alibaba

On-policy distillation rewrites only 17.5% of parameters yet recovers full performance — dense teacher signals do not mean dense parameter updates.

Key Finding
Training 17.5% of coordinates recovers 35.10% vs 35.52% full OPD accuracy.
Key Metric
66–90% of coordinates unchanged at 10⁻⁵ — despite dense token-level teacher supervision.
Why This Matters
On-policy data distribution, not reward density, shapes update geometry — reshaping how we design efficient post-training.
Critical Edge
All models ≤ 7B. Unknown at frontier scale. Subnetwork discovery currently requires a full OPD run first.
一句话总结 / 核心贡献

通过分析10个模型对的checkpoint差异，揭示了on-policy distillation参数更新呈坐标稀疏（66–90%无变化）、谱集中（top-16奇异值占比~27%）且偏离源权重主奇异方向的几何特征。OPD与RLVR子网络掩码重叠度远超随机基线（约3倍），说明不同on-policy方法触及相似的推理相关坐标。

Results & Highlights

Relative Δ Norm
0.036–0.142%
Stable Rank
7.77–12.58
FFN Energy
65–86%
  • OPD delta norms are nearly 100× smaller than offline distillation (0.036–0.142% vs 11.94%)
  • Spectrally concentrated: top-16 SVD energy 19.69–30.52% vs offline distillation's 8.57%
  • Updates avoid dominant source directions — principal-subspace projection <1% at 10% rank
  • AdamW outperforms SGD by 4.5 pts (42.40% vs 37.92%) — dense teacher preserves heterogeneous gradient scales
  • OPD–RLVR mask overlap ~3× above random (53.21% vs 17.50%), hinting at shared "reasoning coordinates"

Methodology

10 Model Pairs

6 OPD targets + 3 RLVR references + 1 offline distillation baseline. Spanning LLMs (1.5B–4B) and VLMs (3B–7B).

Three-Axis Metrics

  • Scale & coordinate support — relative Frobenius norm, sparsity at ε=10⁻⁵, energy concentration
  • Spectral — top-k SVD energy, stable rank, numerical rank
  • Source-geometry — principal-subspace projection, coverage on principal vs low-magnitude coordinates

Interventions

  • Subnetwork training: mask gradients outside OPD / RLVR / random masks → evaluate on AIME24+25
  • Optimizer ablation: AdamW (lr=10⁻⁶) vs SGD (lr=10⁻²)

Strengths

  • Exceptionally thorough three-axis analysis (sparsity + spectral + geometry)
  • 10 diverse model pairs with both observational and interventional evidence
  • OPD–RLVR mask overlap opens a concrete "reasoning subnetwork" research direction
  • Precise formal metric definitions + public code
  • Honest discussion of boundary cases (JustRL as teacher, Qwen3 OPSD)

Limitations

  • All models ≤ 7B; frontier-scale (70B+) behavior unknown
  • Only math reasoning (AIME); coding, agentic, multimodal untested
  • Static final-checkpoint analysis — training dynamics unrevealed
  • Subnetwork requires full OPD run to discover (chicken-and-egg problem)
  • Purely analytical — no new OPD algorithm or training recipe proposed

Critical Assessment

Novelty: High Rigor: Strong Reproducibility: Good Significance: High

The finding that dense supervision + on-policy data still yields sparse, off-principal updates is counter-intuitive and non-trivial. The OPD–RLVR mask overlap suggesting shared "reasoning coordinates" is the paper's most insightful result. Key tension: sparsity narrative vs AdamW ≫ SGD gap — the explanation (heterogeneous gradient scales) feels incomplete. The practical implication (~17.5% parameters suffice) could enable efficient deployment, and the connection to LoRA/OFT opens concrete future work.

Open Questions

  1. Can the sparse OPD subnetwork be identified a priori — without running full OPD first?
  2. Do sparsity patterns emerge from step one or gradually during training?
  3. Do findings hold at 70B+ where subnetwork approaches matter most?
  4. What LoRA rank captures 90% of OPD update energy — can stable rank guide adapter design?
  5. Can OPD–RLVR mask overlap enable multi-stage training: RLVR identifies subnetwork → OPD within mask?
  6. Would orthogonal fine-tuning (OFT/BOFT) naturally align with off-principal OPD geometry?
  7. How do patterns change for non-reasoning OPD — style transfer, safety, agentic tasks?
