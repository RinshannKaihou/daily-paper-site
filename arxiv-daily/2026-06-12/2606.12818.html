Key Metric · Circuit Performance Recovery
0.92→0.17
Matched-variant CPR drops to 0.17 when transferring circuits from base to instruction-tuned Qwen at 5% retained edges — instruction tuning rewires which sparse pathways carry anchoring.
Why This Matters
Debiasing interventions developed on base models may not transfer to deployed instruction-tuned models, because post-training reshapes the circuits carrying cognitive biases.
⚠ Critical Edge
The paper identifies circuits but never proves that modifying them actually reduces anchoring — the causal utility is prospective, not demonstrated.
Methodology
100 numerical questions (OpAQ) → 9-option MCQA format → Tokenizer-matched controls → Δm = logit(correct) − logit(anchor) → EAP-IG circuit localization → CPR faithfulness metric

Each question embeds a low or high numerical anchor in an irrelevant "slot-machine" sentence. Tokenizer-matched controls replace the anchor with a non-numeric "X" of matching token length, cleanly isolating numerical content from token-count confounds. The correct–anchor logit difference (Δm) is validated against behavioral expected-value shifts (Spearman ρ ≤ 0.75) before use in circuit localization.

Models: Qwen2.5-7B, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct (28 layers) · Llama-3.1-8B, Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (32 layers). Four methods compared: NAP, NAP-IG (node-level) vs EAP, EAP-IG (edge-level). Circuits extracted at retained fractions from 0.1% to 100%.

Strengths
  • Tokenizer-matched controls cleanly isolate numerical content from token-count confounds
  • Systematic 4-method × 4-model × 2-direction comparison — the most thorough in this space
  • Validates attribution metric (Δm) against behavioral EV shifts before using it for localization
  • Low/high anchor sharing result is practically useful: debiasing may not need per-direction targeting
  • Honest about what circuits do and do not capture
Limitations
  • Only 100 questions from a single dataset (OpAQ) — generalization is uncertain
  • MCQA format differs from real-world open-ended anchoring; mechanisms may diverge
  • Only 7B–8B models tested; scaling behavior unknown
  • No debiasing intervention attempted — causal utility of the circuits is unproven
  • Logit-difference metric captures only correct-vs-anchor competition, not the full distributional shift
Questions for Further Exploration
  1. Can surgical activation-level interventions on EAP-IG circuits reduce anchoring without degrading factual accuracy?
  2. Do the same layer-wise attribution profiles appear in 70B+ models, or does pathway structure change qualitatively with scale?
  3. How do RLHF, DPO, and other post-training strategies reshape anchoring circuits compared to instruction tuning alone?
  4. Is there a shared "bias susceptibility" subnetwork across different cognitive biases (framing, confirmation)?
  5. Does the strong low↔high transfer hold in open-ended generation, or does MCQA inflate shared structure?
