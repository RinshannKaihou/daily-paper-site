> **一句话总结：** TimeROME-DLM 是首个面向掩码扩散语言模型（MDLM）的无训练、无梯度、推理时知识编辑框架，通过沿去噪轨迹（而非词元序列）的时序间接效应因果追踪定位事实存储坐标，再用闭式低秩残差编辑记忆在冻结主干上一次性注入修改，在 TOFU 基准上将遗忘集 log-prob 降低约 83 nats，且零额外显存、配置跨 6 个 MDLM 主干直接迁移。

> **TL;DR:** TimeROME-DLM introduces the first training-free, gradient-free knowledge editing method for masked diffusion language models (MDLMs like LLaDA, Dream, MMaDA). It re-derives causal tracing along the denoising trajectory (Temporal Indirect Effect) to find where facts localize—in the residual stream at early-middle denoising steps across lower-to-mid layers—then installs a closed-form, ridge-regularized low-rank residual edit at every diffusion forward pass with frozen backbone weights. On TOFU forget01 with LLaDA-8B-Base, it cuts forget-set log-prob by ~83 nats (7× stronger than the best gradient baseline AlphaEdit), transfers across 6 MDLM backbones with the same hyperparameters, adds zero extra VRAM, installs in ~26 seconds, and scales sub-linearly to 400+ facts.

## 核心贡献

- **沿去噪轨迹的因果追踪（TIE）**：首次将因果中介分析从自回归模型的位置序列扩展到 MDLM 的迭代去噪轨迹，发现 MDLM 的事实知识定位在残差流的中低层（ℓ≈8–19）和早期-中间去噪步（k≈1–2），这一时间轴在 AR 模型中不存在。
- **无训练推理时编辑记忆**：将整个遗忘集的所有事实折叠为一个闭式、低秩、ridge 正则化的残差更新，在推理时通过 forward hook 注入冻结主干，无需梯度计算，零额外显存开销。
- **广泛的迁移性与效率**：单一编辑配置（α, λ, q）无需重新计算即可跨 6 个 MDLM 主干（LLaDA-8B-Base/Instruct、Dream-7B、MMaDA-8B、DiffuLLaMA-7B、LLaDA-MoE-1.4B）和多个下游遗忘基准（MUSE、WHP、WMDP）直接迁移；端到端编辑构建约 26 秒，比最强训练时基线快 4–14 倍。

## Key Contributions

- **Diffusion-time causal tracing via Temporal Indirect Effect (TIE):** Re-derives causal mediation analysis along the denoising trajectory rather than token positions. Three diffusion passes (clean, corrupted with Gaussian noise on subject embeddings, and patched) identify which (layer, denoising-step, module) coordinate most strongly drives object prediction at later steps. Facts localize to the residual stream at lower-to-mid layers (ℓ≈8–19 of 32) at early-middle denoising steps (k≈1–2 of 8)—a temporal dimension absent in AR tracing.
- **Closed-form, ridge-regularized low-rank edit memory:** Aggregates all forget-fact subject keys U and target deltas D into a single ridge-regularized least-squares solution G = (UU^⊤ + λI)^{-1}, applied as a per-token TopQ-sparsified residual update at every diffusion forward. Only 3 hyperparameters (α, λ, q) tuned on 8 validation facts; backbone weights stay completely frozen.
- **Broad transfer and efficiency:** Same {α, λ, q} configuration transfers unchanged across 6 MDLM backbones and 4 downstream benchmarks (MUSE-Books/News, WHP, WMDP-Bio) without recomputation. Install time ~26s for 40 facts, scales sub-linearly (400 facts in 56s), adds 0 extra VRAM. 4–14× wall-clock speedup over converged training-time baselines.

## Methodology

TimeROME-DLM operates in three stages:

**Stage 1 — Diffusion-time causal tracing (TIE):** For each fact z = (s, r, o), three diffusion trajectories are run from the same query prompt: (1) a *clean run* recording all activations; (2) a *corrupted run* that injects Gaussian noise into subject-token embeddings at every step; (3) a *patch run* that repeats the corrupted trajectory but splices clean activations back at a single candidate coordinate (ℓ, k, m). The Temporal Indirect Effect measures how much the splice raises the object's log-probability at later denoising steps k′ > k, aggregated via exponential-decay weighting. This yields a heatmap over (layer, step, module) showing where factual content is recoverable.

**Stage 2 — Coordinate selection:** The TIE heatmaps for a small validation set (8 facts) are averaged, and the coordinate (ℓ*, k*, m*) with maximum mean magnitude is selected. Empirically, the residual stream (m*=resid) dominates attention/MLP alone, and applying at every denoising step (k*=−1) is critical—limiting to a single step drops forget effect from 83 to 32 nats.

**Stage 3 — Low-rank inference-time edit memory:** For each forget fact, three residual vectors are captured at the selected coordinate on cloze inputs: subject key u_z, original-object value v_o, and target value v_t. The target delta d_z = v_t − v_o is computed. All |F| keys and deltas are stacked into matrices U and D. The ridge-regularized Gram inverse G = (UU^⊤ + λI)^{-1} is pre-computed once. At every diffusion forward, a hook applies h ← h + α·TopQ_q(D^⊤ G U h) to all token positions. TopQ sparsification limits the number of facts simultaneously firing on each token, trading global forget strength for utility preservation.

## Results & Highlights

- **Headline result (Table I):** On TOFU forget01 with TOFU-finetuned LLaDA-8B-Base, TimeROME (α=2, q=4) achieves ΔfLP = −83.24 nats vs no_edit (p < 10⁻⁴), ~7× stronger than AlphaEdit (Δ = −11.9) and >20× stronger than MEMIT (Δ = −3.3).
- **Cross-backbone (Table II):** Same hyperparameters transfer to LLaDA-8B-Instruct (Δ = −41), Dream-7B (Δ = −91), DiffuLLaMA-7B (Δ = −73), MMaDA-8B (Δ = −85), and LLaDA-MoE-1.4B (Δ = −50). TimeROME is the only method achieving sub-noise KS-p on every backbone.
- **Scalability (Table III):** Install time grows sub-linearly: 6.4s for 1 fact → 56.1s for 400 facts (400× more facts, only 9× more time). ForgetLP continues improving at larger |F| as the ridge problem becomes better conditioned.
- **Sequential editing (Table V, Fig 3):** 50 facts inserted one-at-a-time; RetainLP holds within ~0.5 nats at steady state, RealAuth within ~2 nats. No catastrophic forgetting observed.
- **Cross-benchmark transfer (Table IV):** Edit built once on TOFU forget01 transfers to MUSE-Books (verbmem-LP drops 129 nats), MUSE-News (−122 nats), WHP (−9 nats), and WMDP-Bio (accuracy drops 29 points).
- **Efficiency:** 0 extra VRAM vs 35–51 GB for gradient methods; 4–14× wall-clock speedup over the strongest converged training-time baseline.
- **Converged training-time comparison:** A 17-config sweep over LoRA-NPO/SimNPO with retain anchors achieves best ΔfLP = −5 nats; TimeROME delivers −115 nats at the same protocol (23× stronger).
- **Statistical rigor:** Bootstrap 95% CIs (2000 resamples) and paired-t significance tests reported on every cell.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: High.** This is genuinely the first work addressing knowledge editing for MDLMs. The core insight—tracing causality along the denoising trajectory rather than the token sequence—is non-trivial and leads to qualitatively different localization (temporal band vs. single MLP layer). The closed-form inference-time edit is elegant.

**Rigor: Very high.** The paper re-implements 9 baselines on MDLMs (not just citing AR results), reports the first converged training-time unlearning baseline for MDLMs, and provides bootstrap CIs with paired significance tests. The ablation suite is comprehensive (module choice, step choice, coordinate selection method, paraphrase robustness).

**Reproducibility: Good.** Only 3 hyperparameters, all experiments fit on 4×A100 in ~48 hours. Code and artifacts promised upon acceptance. The method relies only on forward hooks—no custom CUDA, no exotic dependencies.

**Significance: High.** As MDLMs become competitive with AR models (LLaDA matching LLaMA3-8B), knowledge editing and unlearning for this model family is a critical missing capability. This work fills that gap convincingly.

**Potential concerns:**
- The headline α=2, q=4 result trades significant utility for forget strength (RetainLP drops from −35 to −89 nats, RealAuth from −13 to −25). The Pareto frontier (Fig 4) shows the utility-safe operating point (α≤0.5) achieves modest forget gains. The "83 nats" headline may overstate practical usability.
- TOFU forget01 is a relatively small benchmark (40 facts). While the scalability sweep goes to 4000 facts, the most detailed tables only cover 40.
- The method requires capturing per-fact residuals on cloze inputs, which assumes access to the specific (subject, relation, object) triples—a structured editing scenario. It's unclear how it handles unstructured or implicit knowledge.
- In-context relearning attacks weaken the edit by ~8 nats, and the paper leaves adversarial hardening to future work.
- Some baselines marked † are re-implemented from AR recipes rather than using official MDLM implementations, which could disadvantage them.

## Strengths

- First systematic study of knowledge editing for MDLMs—fills an important gap as this model family gains traction.
- Elegant methodological contribution: TIE tracing re-derives causal mediation for the diffusion paradigm, producing a new temporal localization axis.
- The closed-form edit memory is computationally lightweight, requires no gradients, and installs in seconds with zero extra VRAM.
- Exceptionally thorough evaluation: 9 baselines, 6 backbones, multiple benchmarks, ablations, robustness tests, statistical significance.
- Clean Pareto frontier between forget strength and utility preservation, controlled by a single interpretable scalar α.
- Sequential/streaming editing capability with stable retain performance—unique advantage over gradient-based methods.

## Limitations

- **Utility–forget tradeoff is sharp at the headline operating point:** α=2 achieves impressive forget numbers but at substantial utility cost. Practical deployments may need to operate at α≤0.5 where forget gains are modest.
- **Single global coordinate:** One (layer, module) is selected for the entire forget set; per-fact coordinate selection could improve results but is unexplored.
- **Structured editing assumption:** Requires explicit (subject, relation, object) triples and cloze-style prompts for residual capture. Not directly applicable to unstructured knowledge.
- **RWKU neighbour selectivity gap:** The method cannot distinguish forget targets from same-subject neighbours, dropping both by ~25 nats.
- **In-context relearning vulnerability:** A known weakness of forget-only methods, acknowledged but unresolved.
- **Evaluation limited to MDLMs:** No comparison on AR models to test if TIE tracing also improves editing there (though the paper's scope is MDLMs specifically).

## Relevance

该论文直接面向扩散语言模型的知识编辑与遗忘问题，是模型安全性和可控生成领域的重要进展。对于关注非自回归生成范式、模型可解释性以及推理时模型修改的研究方向具有直接参考价值，也为扩散语言模型的实际部署（如隐私合规、安全对齐）提供了可行方案。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can per-fact coordinate selection (different ℓ* for different facts) improve both forget strength and utility preservation simultaneously, and at what computational cost?
2. How does TIE-based tracing compare to AR positional tracing when applied to autoregressive models—does the temporal axis provide additional signal there too?
3. Can the edit memory be hardened against in-context relearning attacks through adversarial training or multi-objective optimization of the ridge formulation?
4. What happens when the forget set contains semantically contradictory facts (e.g., editing the same relation for the same subject to different objects)?
5. How does the method perform on continuous diffusion models (e.g., Diffusion-LM) versus the discrete masked diffusion models studied here?
