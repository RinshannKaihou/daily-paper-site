> **一句话总结：** 本文为等变潜空间世界模型提出了基于Lyapunov谱的可计算、多步可预测视界证书，证明T步rollout误差沿对称轨道恒定（Theorem A），且该性质为等变模型所独有（Lemma 2逆向定理）；匹配的下界（Proposition 6）证明近似等变性的视界受限为Θ(log(1/ε)/λ)，而Noether铰链（Proposition 5）将守恒量认证为无限视界通道。实验上，仅Z_N等变网络在40维Lorenz-96上恢复完整Lyapunov谱（R²=0.98），同一读取机制免训练地审计了TD-MPC2（1M-317M参数阶梯）和V-JEPA 2-AC（1B）的公开检查点，揭示尺度不改善校准。

> **TL;DR:** This single-author paper derives a computable, multi-step "predictable horizon" certificate for equivariant latent world models, grounded in the predictor's Lyapunov spectrum. The certificate has two axes: (1) **Configuration** — T-step rollout error is orbit-constant under equivariance (Theorem A), and this property *characterizes* equivariance (Lemma 2), making it exclusive to structured models at any scale; (2) **Horizon** — each latent channel's certified horizon is $T_j(\varepsilon) \sim \log(1/\varepsilon)/\lambda_j$, with a matching lower bound (Proposition 6) proving approximate equivariance is horizon-limited. A "Noether hinge" (Propositions 4–5) certifies conserved quantities to unbounded horizons. On 40-D Lorenz-96, only a Z_N-equivariant network recovers the full Lyapunov spectrum (R²=0.98); the same readout audits TD-MPC2 and V-JEPA 2-AC training-free, revealing that scaling from 1M to 317M parameters does not improve calibration — "scale buys interpolation, not a calibrated horizon."

## 核心贡献

- **轨道恒定误差定理（Theorem A）：** 在编码器、预测器和动力学的等变性假设（A1-A4）下，T步rollout误差沿整个对称轨道恒定——仅需k个生成元检查即可认证指数级大的配置集合⟨S⟩。
- **等变性特征化引理（Lemma 2）：** 证明轨道恒定误差当且仅当预测器等变——非等变模型在任何参数量下都不可能拥有此证书，这是一个架构不可能性定理。
- **双向谱视界界：** 上界（Theorem B）给出每通道认证视界$T_j(\varepsilon) \sim \log(1/\varepsilon)/\lambda_j$；匹配下界（Proposition 6）构造了一个仅有单点编码器缺陷的ε-近似等变模型，其轨道误差变化恰好为$\varepsilon e^{\lambda T}$，证明视界形式$\Theta(\log(1/\varepsilon)/\lambda)$不可改进。
- **Noether铰链（Propositions 4-5）：** 守恒/不变量被强制放置于特定等变块中，其T步预测误差线性增长（$T\eta$）而非指数增长（$e^{\lambda T}$），在$\eta=0$时认证为无限视界。
- **免训练审计工具：** 同一Lyapunov谱读取机制无训练地审计TD-MPC2（5任务×3种子=15循环）、LeWM和V-JEPA 2-AC（1B），产生校准/乐观/拒绝的范围分类学。
- **尺度不改善校准（E14）：** TD-MPC2多任务阶梯（1M-317M）上，校准散布且非单调——信任是循环动力学的性质，不是参数量的性质。

## Key Contributions

- **Theorem A (orbit-constant error):** Under assumptions (A1)–(A4) — encoder equivariance, predictor equivariance, dynamical symmetry, and orthogonal representation — the T-step rollout error is identical across every configuration in the symmetry orbit. Combined with Lemma 1 (composition closure), k generator checks certify an exponentially large generated monoid ⟨S⟩.
- **Lemma 2 (converse — certificate characterizes equivariance):** If a predictor's error is orbit-constant against every equivariant target, then the predictor is equivariant. This makes the certificate an exclusive property of structured models — no non-equivariant model possesses it at any parameter count.
- **Two-sided spectral horizon:** Theorem B gives a per-channel upper bound with certified horizon $T_j(\varepsilon) \sim \log(1/\varepsilon)/\lambda_j$; Proposition 6 constructs a matching lower bound showing the form $\Theta(\log(1/\varepsilon)/\lambda)$ is tight. Only exact equivariance ($\varepsilon=0$) or conservation ($\lambda_j \leq 0$) yields unbounded horizons.
- **The Noether hinge (Propositions 4–5):** Conserved charges are forced into specific isotypic blocks by representation theory (Proposition 4), and their T-step prediction error grows linearly ($T\eta$) rather than exponentially (Proposition 5), providing the unbounded-horizon edge of the certificate.
- **Scope and continuity (Propositions 7–8):** Proposition 7 establishes when the local Lyapunov spectrum certifies a learned model's horizon (degenerate vs. non-degenerate dichotomy); Proposition 8 provides a finite-horizon Lipschitz transfer of exponents from learned models, confirmed empirically (exponents recovered to 1–12% on Lorenz, Hénon, Rössler).
- **Training-free auditing of pretrained models:** The same spectral readout — unchanged — audits public TD-MPC2, LeWM, and V-JEPA 2-AC (1B) checkpoints, producing a scope taxonomy calibrated where strongly expansive, optimistic where weakly expansive, and correctly abstaining where contracting.
- **Scale does not improve calibration (E14):** Across the TD-MPC2 1M–317M multitask ladder, the loop's regime flips non-monotonically with scale and calibration scatters — no multitask scale matches single-task 0.94–1.02.

## Methodology

The paper develops a rigorous theoretical framework with extensive empirical validation across 16 experiments (E1–E16):

### Theoretical Framework

1. **Setup:** A latent world model = encoder $E: \mathcal{X} \to \mathcal{Z}$ + predictor $f: \mathcal{Z} \times \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{Z}$, with group $G$ acting on states (via representation $\rho$), latents, and actions (via $\sigma$). Five assumptions (A1–A5) formalize equivariance, dynamical symmetry, and orthogonality.

2. **Configuration axis (§3.1):** Theorem A proves orbit-constant error via equivariant transport; Lemma 2 proves the converse (equivariance is *necessary* for orbit-constant error). The proof uses a clever probe: for arbitrary $c$, define an equivariant target $\Phi_c(\rho(h)z) = \rho(h)c$ using freeness, then orbit-constancy forces $f(\rho(g)z) = \rho(g)f(z)$.

3. **Horizon axis (§3.2):** Under approximate equivariance (encoder residual $\varepsilon_{\max}$, per-step predictor error $\delta$), Theorem B bounds orbit-error variation by $\sum_j c_j(m\varepsilon_{\max} + T\delta)e^{\lambda_j T}$. Proposition 6 constructs the matching lower bound: a single encoder defect at one orbit point propagates to exactly $\varepsilon e^{\lambda T}$.

4. **The Noether hinge (§3.3):** Proposition 4 forces conserved charges into specific isotypic blocks of $\rho$; Proposition 5 proves their prediction error grows linearly ($T\eta$) rather than exponentially.

5. **Cone/adapted-metric certificate (Theorem B⁺):** A computable certificate using a field of adapted metrics $P(z)$, sound a priori, tight on uniformly hyperbolic dynamics, and self-abstaining on non-uniformly hyperbolic systems.

### Experimental Methodology

- **E2 (core):** Learned models of Lorenz, Hénon, Rössler, PushT, and 40-D Lorenz-96. The certified horizon staircase is verified to be linear in $\log(1/\varepsilon)$ with slopes recovering textbook exponents to 1–12%. At $N=40$ Lorenz-96, $\mathbb{Z}_N$-equivariant cyclic convolution vs. identically-trained dense MLP and GRU-BPTT.
- **E9–E12:** Active re-observation scheduling under fixed sensing budget, comparing equivariant vs. dense certificates (zero calibration data vs. needing calibration sets).
- **E13–E16:** Training-free auditing of official TD-MPC2 checkpoints (5 tasks × 3 seeds), LeWM checkpoint, and V-JEPA 2-AC (1B, DROID real robot data). Cross-validated audit against true-environment divergence.
- All experiments CPU/1-GPU, seeded, with honest gating (reporting INCONCLUSIVE rather than loosening thresholds).

## Results & Highlights

- **Lyapunov spectrum recovery (E2):** On 40-D Lorenz-96, the $\mathbb{Z}_N$-equivariant network recovers the full spectrum (R²=0.98–0.99, 3 seeds; Kaplan–Yorke dimension ∼27). Dense MLP fails (R²<0, $\lambda_1$ inflated ∼3.4×). GRU-BPTT fails due to conditional-Lyapunov condition violation. Phase transition at $N=40$: all three architectures tie through $N=28$; only equivariant survives $N=40$.
- **Proposition 6 validated numerically:** Orbit-error-variation of an $\varepsilon=10^{-3}$ approximately-equivariant model matches the analytic $\varepsilon e^{\lambda T}$ to relative error $10^{-14}$–$10^{-13}$ (3 seeds). Certified horizon linear in $\log(1/\varepsilon)$ with slope exactly $1/\lambda$ (R²=1.000).
- **Scope law confirmed:** The law lifts to learned chaotic models — staircases linear in $\log(1/\varepsilon)$ with R²=0.975–0.995 across Lorenz, Hénon, Rössler. PushT interior (near-neutral $\lambda_1$) is the predicted degenerate branch (R²=0.02, Proposition 7(b)).
- **Sensing budget advantage (E12):** Equivariant certificate yields 8–16% aggregate violation at the knee budget vs. 61–65% for the dense certificate (margins +0.45–+0.57, 3/3). Proposition 9 makes this a law: $c\times$ inflation needs $c\times$ budget; measured catch-up 2.7–3.5×.
- **TD-MPC2 audit (E13):** 15 loops (5 tasks × 3 seeds) — calibrated where strongly expansive (walker: 0.94/0.95/1.02), optimistic where weakly expansive (cheetah: 0.43/0.50), correctly abstaining where contracting (acrobot 3/3, finger-spin 2/3). LeWM: certificate abstains ($\lambda_1=0.001$ with CI straddling zero).
- **Scale does not rescue trustworthiness (E14):** TD-MPC2 multitask ladder (1M–317M) — regime flips non-monotonically (contracting at 1M and 48M, expansive at 5M/19M/317M), calibration scatters 0.37–1.87. No size matches single-task 0.94–1.02.
- **V-JEPA 2-AC audit (E16):** 1B encoder reads expansive ($\lambda_1=0.178$), but on 20 real DROID episodes, deployment error grows at log-slope 0.03 ≪ $\lambda_1$ — never enters the linearization neighborhood. The cross-validated audit (not the raw spectrum) correctly overrides the over-promising tangent spectrum.
- **Deployed monitor (E15):** A sensor-only monitor on cheetah-run replicates the E13 scope map cell-by-cell, out-of-sample, with in-situ-vs-bench ratios matching (0.43 vs 0.43, 0.50 vs 0.50). Fault detection at certificate-derived cadence achieves recall 1.00 on 3/3 seeds.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Hongbo Wang** — Single author. The paper spans representation theory, ergodic theory (Oseledets theorem), Lyapunov exponents, equivariant deep learning, and extensive empirical work across chaotic systems, contact simulators, and pretrained model auditing. The author is remarkably transparent about scope and limitations: every experiment honestly reports INCONCLUSIVE results where appropriate (e.g., E11 on Acrobot-v1), and Section 6 provides a detailed self-critique. The depth of the mathematical apparatus (Appendix B contains full proofs) and breadth of experiments (16 experiments, E1–E16) for a single author is unusual and impressive, though it also means the claims warrant careful community verification.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: Very High.** The intersection of equivariant representation theory, Lyapunov spectral analysis, and certified predictability for world models is genuinely novel. The converse (Lemma 2) — proving orbit-constant error characterizes equivariance — is elegant and non-obvious. The matching lower bound (Proposition 6) is the centerpiece: it shows that the horizon form is not merely an upper estimate but a two-sided characteristic. The training-free auditing of public pretrained models (E13–E16) is, to my knowledge, new.

**Rigor: High.** The theoretical apparatus is formidable: Theorem A, Theorem B, Proposition 6 (matching lower bound), Propositions 7–8 (scope/continuity), Propositions 4–5 (Noether hinge), and Theorem B⁺ (cone certificate). Full proofs are in Appendix B. The author is explicit about scope — Proposition 7's dichotomy between degenerate and non-degenerate regimes predicts where the certificate is vacuous (and confirms this on PushT). However, some concerns:
- Proposition 8's finite-horizon exponent transfer assumes local Lipschitz continuity of the finite-time exponent, which requires $C^1$-closeness verified only via one-step $L^2$ error — the author acknowledges this gap in high dimensions.
- The Noether hinge (Proposition 5) is validated only on constructed equivariant teachers, not on emergent conservation in learned models of unconstrained dynamics — the author honestly flags this as open.
- The prefactor $c_j = 1/\sin\theta_j$ admits heavy tails (median $\kappa_1 \approx 20$, max $\sim 10^2$–$10^3$) on real chaotic loops; measured calibration reflects typical, not adversarial, defect alignment.

**Reproducibility:** The author promises anonymized code, seeds, and gates (Appendix C). The experiments use public benchmarks (Lorenz-96) and public checkpoints (TD-MPC2, V-JEPA 2-AC). The Lorenz-96 Liouville identity check ($\sum_j \lambda_j = -N$ recovered to 0.0%) provides an anchor that does not depend on the learned model, which is a strong sanity check. The honest gating (INCONCLUSIVE rather than threshold-loosening) is a methodological strength.

**Significance: High, but scope-limited.** If the framework holds up, it provides a principled, practical tool for assessing *when* world model predictions can be trusted — critical for robotics deployment. The "scale buys interpolation, not a calibrated horizon" thesis directly challenges the scaling narrative. However:
- The experiments are 1–2 GPU scale. The certificate's computational cost at truly large scale (e.g., frontier model latent spaces) is unclear.
- Downstream task performance gains are demonstrated only indirectly (budgeting efficiency, fault detection recall) — not as end-to-end RL improvements. E11 honestly reports INCONCLUSIVE on Acrobot-v1.
- The framework is deterministic (no stochastic latent dynamics), limiting applicability to the stochastic world models common in model-based RL.
- The certificate requires a genuine dynamical symmetry (A3) for exactness. Real-world systems often have only approximate or broken symmetries.

**Red flags:**
- Single author with 16 experiments and a dense theoretical appendix — community verification of all proof details is essential.
- The Lorenz-96 $N=40$ phase transition is dramatic but based on one system; generalization to other high-dimensional chaotic systems is not established.
- V-JEPA 2-AC (E16): the certificate's expansion reading ($\lambda_1=0.178$) is overridden by the cross-check, meaning the raw certificate over-promises at 1B scale. This is honest but raises questions about standalone utility at foundation-model scale.

## Strengths

- **Rare certified (not just empirical) guarantees** for world model predictability — a principled alternative to average-error metrics.
- **The matching lower bound (Proposition 6)** is the paper's crown jewel: it proves the horizon form is tight, not merely an upper estimate.
- **Exclusive characterization (Lemma 2):** Proving that no non-equivariant model has the certificate at any scale is a clean impossibility result.
- **Exceptional honesty about scope:** The paper explicitly identifies where the certificate is vacuous (PushT degenerate case), where it is INCONCLUSIVE (Acrobot-v1), and where it over-promises (V-JEPA 2-AC). This level of intellectual honesty is uncommon.
- **Training-free auditing** of public pretrained models is immediately deployable and highly practical.
- **The central thesis ("scale buys interpolation, not a calibrated horizon")** is a powerful, clarifying reframing of the scaling debate in world models, supported by the TD-MPC2 multitask ladder evidence.
- **Algorithm 1 provides a complete procedure** for computing the certificate a priori.

## Limitations

- **Exactness requires genuine dynamical symmetry (A3):** The certificate is exact where $G$ is a symmetry of the dynamics, gracefully approximate elsewhere — but real robotics environments rarely have exact symmetries. The degradation is two-sidedly bounded, but the practical tightness of these bounds in messy real-world settings is untested.
- **Deterministic latent dynamics only:** The framework assumes deterministic $f$ and does not address stochastic world models, which are the norm in model-based RL (e.g., Dreamer-style models with stochastic state spaces).
- **1–2 GPU scale experiments:** All experiments are small-scale. The certificate's computational feasibility and tightness at frontier-model scale (e.g., latent dimensions in the thousands) is untested.
- **Noether hinge validated only on constructed teachers:** The connection between learned conservation and unbounded horizons is open — Proposition 5's hypotheses are checked on constructed settings, not emergent in learned models of unconstrained dynamics.
- **Prefactor heavy tails:** The splitting conditioning $c_j = 1/\sin\theta_j$ has median $\sim 20$ and maximum $\sim 10^3$ on audited loops, meaning worst-case guarantees are much looser than typical-case measurements suggest.
- **Downstream task value is indirect:** Value concentrates where the decided quantity IS the certified quantity (sensing budget, monitoring) and dilutes behind task-level maps (return, gait quality). The paper honestly quantifies this dilution (Proposition 11) but it limits practical impact.
- **Pixel-level accuracy poor:** The certificate transfers across modalities (state, SO(3) point clouds, pixels), but absolute multi-step accuracy on raw pixels is poor for every architecture at this scale.

## Relevance

该论文将等变表示论、遍历理论（Lyapunov谱）与世界模型可信预测相结合，提出了首个可证明的多步可预测视界证书。对于关注等变网络理论、世界模型安全部署、模型可信度评估的研究具有直接价值。其"尺度换内插，不换校准视界"的核心论点对当前大模型scaling叙事提供了有意义的理论挑战和实验反驳。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the certificate be extended to **stochastic latent world models** (e.g., Dreamer-style RSSM with both deterministic and stochastic state components)?
2. How does the computational cost of Lyapunov spectrum estimation scale with **latent dimensionality in the thousands** — is there a practical ceiling?
3. The Noether hinge connects conservation to unbounded horizons — can this emerge **automatically in learned models** without explicit equivariance constraints, perhaps through discovery of conserved quantities (Noether Networks)?
4. Proposition 11 formalizes when certificate value transfers to downstream decisions — could this inform the **design of planning objectives** that are better aligned with certified quantities?
5. The certificate requires a known symmetry group $G$ — what are the prospects for **learning the symmetry group** jointly with the world model while preserving the certificate's guarantees?
6. Could the scope taxonomy (calibrated/optimistic/abstaining) be used as a **training signal** to improve world model calibration, rather than only as a post-hoc diagnostic?
