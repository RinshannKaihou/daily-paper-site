> **一句话总结：** 本文通过对比142名人类被试和25个大语言模型在11类日常因果推理任务上的表现，发现两者呈现出高度相似的错误模式；通过机制可解释性分析定位LLM中驱动推理输出的注意力头，发现这些注意力头实现的是对非关键内容的模式匹配而非抽象因果推理，并且这些注意力头的激活值能预测人类在仅替换一个无关词汇的实验中产生的系统推理错误（R²高达0.66），强有力地表明人类和LLM的日常推理机制可能都是模式匹配。

> **TL;DR:** This paper systematically compares 142 human participants and 25 LLMs across 11 categories of everyday commonsense causal reasoning (e.g., spatial relations, object state changes). Both humans and LLMs show strikingly similar, content-sensitive error patterns: minor changes to seemingly irrelevant prompt details (swapping "soup" for "stew") cause systematic failures. Through mechanistic interpretability on gemma-2-27b, the authors identify the top-k causally important attention heads and show these heads are more sensitive to non-critical content than to structurally critical information — implementing pattern-matching rather than abstract causal reasoning. Crucially, these attention head activations predict held-out human accuracy on minimal-difference prompt sets (R² = 0.66, up to 0.80), above and beyond trigram frequency and GPT-2 surprisal controls. The work challenges the common critique that LLMs "merely pattern-match" while humans use abstract world models.

## 核心贡献

- **系统性行为对比：** 在11类日常因果推理任务上，人类和LLM表现出高度一致的类别级别错误模式（gemma-3-27b与人类准确率相关r=0.84），例如两者都在近距离地理空间推理上更差，在远距离自我中心推理上更好。
- **最小差异实验揭示推理脆弱性：** 仅替换一个无关词汇（如"painting"→"statue"），人类和LLM的准确率就会产生剧烈变化，证明所谓的"世界模型"推理远不如表面看起来那么抽象和鲁棒。
- **机制层面证据：** 通过对gemma-2-27b的注意力头进行消融和激活修补实验，发现因果重要性最高的注意力头对非关键内容变化（如替换物体名称）的敏感度高于对结构关键信息（如"North"→"South"）的敏感度（所有11个类别p<0.001，中位Cohen's d=0.437）。
- **跨物种预测：** 利用LLM注意力头激活值的熵构建线性模型，能在留出样本上预测人类准确率（主实验LOO R²=0.37；最小差异实验LOO R²=0.66），且这一预测力超越了三元组频率、GPT-2惊讶度等控制变量。
- **理论贡献：** 提出日常因果推理在人类和LLM中更符合Margolis（1990）定义的模式匹配机制——依赖于内容与上下文的联想，而非抽象的、组合性的世界模型。

## Key Contributions

- Conducted a large-scale behavioral comparison of 142 humans and 25 LLMs on 11 categories of everyday causal reasoning tasks, revealing strong category-level alignment (gemma-3-27b r=0.84 with human accuracy).
- Demonstrated that both humans and LLMs exhibit fragile, content-sensitive reasoning: single-word substitutions of ostensibly irrelevant content cause dramatic accuracy shifts in both.
- Performed mechanistic interpretability analysis on gemma-2-27b, showing that causally important attention heads are more sensitive to non-critical content changes than to structurally critical information across all 11 categories (median Cohen's d=0.437).
- Showed that entropy of these attention head activations predicts held-out human accuracy (LOO R²=0.372 main experiment; R²=0.656 minimal-difference experiment), above and beyond trigram frequency and language model surprisal controls.
- Provided a unified theoretical account (drawing on Margolis, 1990) arguing that everyday causal reasoning in both humans and LLMs is pattern-matching rather than abstract world model instantiation.

## Methodology

### Behavioral Experiments

- **Participants:** Three cohorts — main experiment (n=142 after exclusions from 162 MTurk workers), re-test cohort (n=70), and minimal-difference follow-up (n=175).
- **Task design:** 11 categories of everyday causal reasoning covering state changes, position changes, geocentric relations (near/distant), egocentric relations (near/distant), relative position, and two-reference scenarios. Each prompt presents a scenario with a BLANK to fill, and participants choose between two completions (one correct, one incorrect).
- **Stimulus format:** Modular design with "target framing" (infer outcome) and "context framing" (infer cause), enabling bidirectional causal reasoning tests. Inspired by Ivanova et al. (2025).
- **LLM evaluation:** 25 LLMs tested (21 open-source, 4 proprietary). Open-weights models measured via logit difference (correct - incorrect token); proprietary models via binary accuracy on first 5 output tokens at temperature 0.
- **Controls:** Trigram frequency (moving window over prompts), GPT-2 surprisal, BERT surprisal — all tested as alternative explanations for human-LLM alignment.

### Mechanistic Interpretability

- **Model:** gemma-2-27b-it (primary) and gemma-2-9b-it, using TransformerLens.
- **Head selection:** All attention heads ablated individually; causal responsibility score = mean absolute logit difference change. Top-k heads selected (k chosen by BIC).
- **Activation patching:** For each prompt, defined 3 critical content substitutions (change ground-truth outcome) and 3 non-critical content substitutions (same outcome, different surface content). Patched activations from substituted prompts into base prompt at top-k heads to measure sensitivity.
- **Human prediction:** Used entropy of attention head activations as features in linear regression predicting human accuracy, with leave-one-out cross-validation. Controlled for category, prompt format, trigram frequency, and GPT-2/BERT surprisal.

### Minimal-Difference Follow-up

- 6 scenario templates × 16 content substitutions each = 96 prompts, where all 16 prompts per scenario share the same ground-truth answer.
- Third cohort of humans (n=175) evaluated; top-k attention head activations used to predict human accuracy via LOO regression.

## Results & Highlights

- **Human accuracy is far below ceiling** (µ=0.71, σ=0.21), with large variation across categories. Frontier models (DeepSeek-R1: 0.90, GPT-5.2: 0.87) significantly outperform humans but are *less* aligned with human error patterns.
- **gemma-3-27b is the most human-aligned model** (r=0.84 category-level), despite middling absolute accuracy — larger models with more sophisticated post-training are more accurate but not more human-like.
- **Content sensitivity is pervasive:** Humans find "Chicago is North of Ali" easier than "The painting is North of Ali" for geocentric reasoning, despite identical relational structure. Both humans and LLMs expect causal outcomes where none should occur (e.g., thinking soup gets warmer even when on the table, not the stove).
- **Errors are consistent, not random:** Test-retest reliability r=0.60; getting a question wrong on first presentation increases odds of getting it wrong again by 3.84× (95% CI [2.92, 5.07]).
- **Attention heads are more sensitive to non-critical content** than structurally critical information across all 11 categories (all p<0.001). Patching "statue" for "painting" affects outputs more than patching "South" for "North."
- **Attention heads predict human behavior:** LOO R²=0.372 (main experiment), R²=0.656 (minimal-difference experiment, k=7), up to 0.80 with more heads. Predictions remain significant above and beyond trigram frequency (F(11,407)=3.36, p<0.001, ΔR²=0.054) and model logit outputs.
- **DeepSeek-R1 reasoning trace length predicts human difficulty:** Scenarios eliciting longer chain-of-thought are harder for humans (β=-0.027, p=0.001), controlling for category and model accuracy.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The bidirectional approach — using LLM mechanistic findings to predict human reasoning errors — is genuinely innovative. Most prior work compares humans and LLMs behaviorally; this paper goes further by linking mechanism-level insights (attention head content sensitivity) to human behavioral predictions. The minimal-difference follow-up experiment is particularly clever.
- **Rigor: Strong overall, with some gaps.** The use of three separate human cohorts, 25 LLMs, permutation tests, and multiple controls (trigram frequency, GPT-2/BERT surprisal) is commendable. Statistical analyses are thorough (mixed effects regressions, BIC-based model selection, permutation tests with 1000 iterations). However:
  - The interpretability analysis is limited to gemma-2-27b-it and gemma-2-9b-it. It would strengthen the paper to see if the same pattern holds across model families (the behavioral results show consistency, but mechanistic generalization is not demonstrated).
  - The linear regression from attention head entropy to human accuracy, while significant, has modest R² (0.254 for main experiment). The high R² in the minimal-difference experiment (0.656) is more convincing but involves only 96 prompts.
  - The paper does not deeply engage with the extensive cognitive science literature on dual-process theory (System 1/System 2). The Discussion briefly addresses this, but many cognitive scientists would argue that the tasks tested here primarily engage System 1 (fast, heuristic) reasoning, and the paper's claims about "reasoning" more broadly may be overstated.
- **Reproducibility:** The behavioral tasks are straightforward to replicate. The interpretability experiments require TransformerLens and specific model weights, which are publicly available. The paper provides extensive supplementary materials.
- **Significance: High for the "do LLMs really reason?" debate.** If everyday human reasoning itself relies on content-sensitive pattern matching, then dismissing LLM reasoning as "mere pattern matching" becomes a self-undermining critique. This paper provides some of the strongest empirical evidence for this position to date.
- **Potential red flags:**
  - The claim that pattern matching is "the way" neural networks solve problems could be seen as overclaiming from a limited set of everyday reasoning tasks. Formal/symbolic reasoning tasks might show very different patterns.
  - The paper could be criticized for studying only a narrow slice of "reasoning" — the tasks are deliberately chosen to be everyday/common-sense scenarios. Whether the findings extend to mathematical, logical, or scientific reasoning is entirely open.
  - The finding that attention heads are more sensitive to non-critical content than critical content is somewhat counterintuitive and could reflect artifacts of how "critical" vs. "non-critical" substitutions are defined (the non-critical substitutions involve more semantically diverse word swaps, which could naturally produce larger activation changes).

## Strengths

- Directly addresses one of the most heated debates in AI research with rigorous empirical evidence rather than philosophical argument alone.
- The mechanistic interpretability approach (attention head ablation + activation patching) is methodologically innovative and provides causal evidence rather than just correlational behavioral alignment.
- Three independent human cohorts strengthen the reliability of behavioral findings.
- Excellent experimental design with minimal-difference stimuli that isolate content effects from structural effects.
- Strong controls: trigram frequency, GPT-2/BERT surprisal, permutation tests with random heads and random normal features.
- Well-written Discussion that thoughtfully addresses alternative explanations and limitations.
- The finding that DeepSeek-R1's reasoning trace length predicts human difficulty is an interesting bonus result connecting chain-of-thought reasoning to cognitive effort.

## Limitations

- The reasoning tasks are limited to everyday common-sense scenarios — the findings may not generalize to formal logic, mathematical reasoning, scientific inference, or other domains where abstract world models plausibly play a larger role.
- Mechanistic analysis is restricted to Gemma-2 models; cross-family generalization of the attention head findings is not established.
- The paper does not fully engage with the dual-process (System 1/System 2) framework that already distinguishes fast, heuristic reasoning from slow, deliberative reasoning. Many of the paper's "surprising" findings about human reasoning are well-known in the cognitive psychology literature on heuristics and biases.
- The linear models from attention head activations to human accuracy are significant but leave substantial variance unexplained (R²=0.254 in the main experiment).
- The distinction between "pattern matching" and "using associations within a structured representation" may be more of a continuum than a dichotomy, and the paper's framing sometimes suggests a sharper distinction than the evidence supports.
- The stimulus set, while diverse across categories, has limited items per category, which may affect the stability of within-category analyses.

## Relevance

这项研究对理解LLM推理能力本质和认知科学基础均具有重要意义。如果你关注大语言模型的推理机制、机械可解释性、人类与AI推理能力对比，以及"LLM是否真正推理"这一核心辩论，这篇论文提供了非常有价值的实验证据——特别是通过LLM内部机制预测人类推理错误这一创新方法，为理解人类认知提供了新的计算工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the identified pattern-matching attention heads relate to known cognitive biases and heuristics (availability, representativeness, anchoring)? Is there a systematic mapping between attention head function and specific biases?
- Would the same content-sensitive pattern-matching mechanism be found in models trained with explicit reasoning objectives (e.g., process reward models, verification-based training)?
- Can the approach generalize to formal reasoning tasks (math, logic puzzles) where humans also show systematic content sensitivity? Would the same attention heads be implicated?
- What happens when LLMs are fine-tuned to reduce reliance on the identified content-sensitive heads — does reasoning performance improve on novel scenarios or degrade on familiar ones?
- The paper shows that frontier models (GPT-5.2, DeepSeek-R1) are less human-aligned despite higher accuracy. Does this mean these models have developed more abstract reasoning mechanisms, or are they simply better pattern-matchers?
- Can the activation patching methodology be used prospectively to predict which reasoning problems will be difficult for humans before running behavioral experiments?
