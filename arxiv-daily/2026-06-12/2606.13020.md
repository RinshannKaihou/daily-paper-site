> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 SciR 基准，在演绎、归纳和因果溯因三种推理范式上，通过形式化对象生成保证可验证答案，再经领域微调的多文档科学渲染进行混淆，独立调控"推理复杂度"与"前提提取难度"两个轴，系统揭示所有模型在两个轴上均显著降分且效果叠加，即使是神经符号管线也未能幸免。

> **TL;DR:** SciR is the first multi-paradigm scientific-reasoning benchmark (deduction over developmental-biology syllogisms, induction over DrugBank drug–drug interactions, causal abduction over the Sachs protein-signalling network) with parametric control over both inference complexity and premise obfuscation. Tasks are generated from formal objects with verifiable ground truth, then rendered into multi-document scientific discourse (lab notebooks, FDA drug labels, proteomics reports, etc.). Evaluation of six models (gpt-4o, o3-mini, deepseek-r1, llama-3.3-70b, qwen3-30b, olmo-3.1-32b) across three architectures (CoT, neuro-symbolic, SymbCoT*) shows that both difficulty axes hurt every model and their effects compound — even neuro-symbolic pipelines that delegate inference to verified solvers see accuracy roughly halve under obfuscation (e.g., gpt-4o NS: 90.4 → 42.6). Reasoning models (deepseek-r1) lead instruct models on both axes but with a wider gap on principled inference (+53 pts vs. +23 pts on extraction).

## 核心贡献

- 提出了 SciR，首个在演绎、归纳、因果溯因三种科学推理范式上同时提供参数化难度控制的多范式基准测试，覆盖发育生物学、药物互作和蛋白质信号网络三个领域。
- 创新性的两阶段构造管线：先从形式化对象（演绎树、归纳规则假设、因果图）生成可验证答案，再通过领域微调的多文档科学渲染器（每轨 8 种文档类型）转化为真实科学语篇。
- 引入两个独立可控的难度轴——推理复杂度（形式化对象参数）与前提混淆度（渲染参数），使模型失败可分解为"提取失败"和"推理失败"。
- 首次系统性地展示：渲染混淆甚至损害神经符号管线（LLM 形式化器 + 符号求解器），证明前提提取本身是一个需要真正推理能力的挑战。
- 提供了每个模型的提取-推理剖析图（inference–extraction profile），揭示推理模型在推理轴上大幅领先指令模型。

## Key Contributions

- **Multi-paradigm benchmark with parametric control:** SciR spans deduction (chained FOL syllogisms instantiated with developmental-biology entities), induction (rule discovery over DrugBank drug–drug interactions), and causal abduction (edge recovery from the Sachs et al. protein-signalling network with a fictional protein XYZ).
- **Two-stage pipeline with formal ground truth:** Tasks are generated as formal objects before being rendered into multi-document scientific discourse via a cross-validated, information-preserving rendering scheme. The answer is determined by the formal object itself, guaranteeing verifiable ground truth.
- **Two-axis difficulty decomposition:** Inference complexity (depth of deduction trees, number of distractor rules, subgraph size) and premise obfuscation (chunk count, genre diversity, rendering LLM capability) are independently tunable, enabling per-model extraction-vs-inference profiling.
- **Rendering hurts even neuro-symbolic pipelines:** The obfuscation degrades NS accuracy by roughly half (e.g., gpt-4o NS: 90.4 → 42.6, deepseek-r1 NS: −25.5 pts from NL Easy to Obf Hard), proving that the formalisation step itself requires genuine reasoning.
- **Reasoning models are more inference-leaning:** DeepSeek-R1 leads GPT-4o by 53 pts on inference (NL·CoT) vs. 23 pts on extraction (Obf·NS), establishing a clear inference–extraction asymmetry between reasoning and instruct models.

## Methodology

**Benchmark construction.** Each of three tracks maps to a paradigmatic scientific problem:
- **Deduction:** First-order-logic syllogisms (2–3 premises) chained into deduction trees and instantiated with developmental-biology terminology from 20 hand-curated contexts. Solver must determine if a hypothesis is valid/invalid/unknown. Symbolic backend: Prover9 + Mace4.
- **Induction:** Drug–drug interaction rules over DrugBank enzyme/transporter/target profiles. Solver must induce the target rule from positive and negative observations plus background facts. Symbolic backend: Popper (ILP learner).
- **Causal abduction:** A fictional protein XYZ is added to a subgraph sampled from the Sachs signalling network; concentrations are simulated via a linear Gaussian SCM under observational and interventional conditions. Solver must recover XYZ's edges. Symbolic backend: GIES.

**Scientific rendering scheme.** Each task is split into N chunks, each rewritten by an LLM (o3-mini) into one of 8 domain-specific document genres per track (e.g., scRNA-seq reports, FDA drug labels, wet-lab notebooks). A cross-validated invertibility contract ensures information preservation: a second LLM must be able to reconstruct each chunk given the other chunks as context. Obfuscation strength scales with chunk count, style pool size, and rendering LLM capability.

**Evaluation setup.** Six base models evaluated in three configurations: (1) CoT (chain-of-thought prompting), (2) Neuro-symbolic (LLM formalizer + symbolic solver), (3) SymbCoT* (formalization + second LLM call instead of solver). Each cell uses n=200 tasks at temperature 0. Each track runs at Easy and Hard tiers along both inference complexity and premise obfuscation axes.

## Results & Highlights

- **Both axes hurt every model, effects compound:** GPT-4o CoT drops from 40.7 (NL Easy) → 32.7 (NL Hard) → 29.2 (Obf Easy) → 17.7 (Obf Hard). Every model follows the same downward pattern.
- **NS accuracy roughly halves under obfuscation:** GPT-4o NS: 90.4 → 87.2 → 57.4 → 42.6 across the four cells. The formalizer fails to extract premises from rendered text even though the symbolic solver is sound.
- **Reasoning models lead on both axes with wider gap on inference:** DeepSeek-R1 outperforms GPT-4o by 53 pts on inference (NL·CoT Easy avg) vs. 23 pts on extraction (Obf·NS Easy avg).
- **Deduction is extraction-hard; causal is inference-hard:** In the extraction-vs-inference profiles, deduction points lean above the diagonal (extraction harder), causal below (inference harder), induction near the diagonal.
- **Scaling inference complexity pushes past saturation:** CoT accuracy degrades steadily across five tiers per domain, showing the formal generator can produce tasks well beyond frontier-model capability.
- **Models miss real premises more than they absorb distractors:** Formaliser analysis shows extracted item counts typically fall below gold counts under obfuscation, especially for GPT-4o (induction facts roughly halved) and OLMo-3.1-32b.
- **DeepSeek-R1 is the most stable formaliser; GPT-4o the least.**
- **SymbCoT* (formalisation + LLM answering) trails NS in nearly every cell**, especially on induction where it even trails plain CoT — suggesting the Prolog-style output is unfamiliar to the LLM.
- **Qwen3-30b is a partial exception among instruct models:** Its inference scores reach reasoning-model territory, possibly reflecting reasoning-heavy training data.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The combination of (a) multi-paradigm coverage (deduction + induction + causal abduction), (b) formal ground-truth generation with verifiable answers, (c) domain-faithful multi-document scientific rendering with an invertibility contract, and (d) two-axis parametric difficulty control is genuinely novel. No prior benchmark achieves all four simultaneously.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Formal objects guarantee verifiable answers (Prover9 for deduction, Popper for induction, GIES for causal). The cross-validated invertibility contract is an elegant mechanism for ensuring information preservation during rendering. Three evaluation architectures (CoT, NS, SymbCoT*) provide a multi-faceted view.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Generators and evaluation code are released. The parametric design is well-specified. However, the rendering step depends on o3-mini as the rendering LLM, which introduces a dependency on a proprietary model that may change over time.
- **Significance: Important.** The finding that obfuscation degrades even neuro-symbolic pipelines is striking and has direct implications for building robust scientific AI systems. The extraction-vs-inference profiling framework is a useful diagnostic tool.
- **Potential concerns:**
  - The rendering uses o3-mini as both renderer and inverse validator, creating a potential confound — o3-mini's extraction scores may be inflated because it "knows" the rendering style. The authors acknowledge this for o3-mini but include deepseek-r1 as an unrelated reasoning baseline.
  - Each reasoning mode is instantiated by only one paradigmatic case and one scientific surface. Generalization to other domains (physics, chemistry, social sciences) is untested.
  - Single-run evaluation (n=200, no error bars) due to cost (~$1,500 in API calls). The authors argue gaps are large relative to sampling noise, but formal confidence intervals would strengthen claims.
  - The realism gap between rendered discourse and genuine scientific literature is acknowledged but not empirically validated.

## Strengths

- Principled two-axis difficulty decomposition is a genuinely useful diagnostic — it tells you *why* a model fails, not just *that* it fails.
- The invertibility contract for rendering is an elegant and rigorous mechanism for ensuring information preservation while still making extraction hard.
- Multi-paradigm coverage is comprehensive and each track is grounded in a real scientific problem (Sachs network, DrugBank, developmental biology).
- The pipeline can be regenerated as models saturate, providing a forward-looking defense against benchmark contamination.
- The analysis of formaliser behavior (missing premises vs. absorbing distractors) provides actionable mechanistic insight.
- The finding that the formalization step itself requires reasoning (NS degrades under obfuscation) challenges the assumption that neuro-symbolic methods are robust to surface variation.

## Limitations

- **Single surface per paradigm:** Each reasoning mode is tied to one scientific domain. The same deduction track rendered onto, say, chemistry or physics literature might yield different model rankings.
- **Real scientific corpora rarely have clean formal objects:** SciR is explicitly acknowledged as a proxy — real scientific documents have weaker, noisier latent structure.
- **Rendering LLM dependency:** Using o3-mini as renderer means the rendering quality is tied to a proprietary model. Regenerating at higher obfuscation tiers as rendering LLMs improve is a partial mitigation.
- **No error bars:** Single-run evaluation at n=200 per cell without multiple seeds, justified by cost but leaving statistical uncertainty unquantified.
- **Limited model coverage:** Six models is reasonable but does not include some notable entries (e.g., Claude, Gemini, smaller models). Open-weight coverage could be broader.
- **Induction track SymbCoT* anomaly** (trailing even CoT) is attributed to unfamiliar Prolog-style output but not investigated further.

## Relevance

与 LLM 科学推理评估高度相关。该基准首次实现了推理能力与信息提取能力的独立量化，对理解模型在科学场景中的瓶颈具有重要价值。神经符号管线在混淆下大幅降分的发现，直接启示了提升科学 AI 系统鲁棒性的研究方向。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the extraction-vs-inference profile change with retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) or multi-pass extraction strategies?
- Can the invertibility contract be used as a training signal for improving LLM formalization robustness?
- How do the results generalize when the rendering uses different LLMs (e.g., the task becomes harder when rendered by a stronger model)?
- What is the correlation between SciR performance and performance on real-world scientific tasks (e.g., literature-based discovery, systematic review)?
- Could the two-axis profiling framework be extended to other reasoning paradigms (analogical, counterfactual, abductive diagnosis)?
- How much of the NS degradation is due to formalization errors (wrong formal language) vs. extraction errors (missing premises)?
