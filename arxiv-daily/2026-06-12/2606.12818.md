> **一句话总结：** 本文将数值锚定效应转化为可控的多选题任务，通过边级归因修补（EAP-IG）在 Qwen2.5-7B 和 Llama-3.1-8B 的基础版和指令微调版中定位锚定通路，发现低锚和高锚回路在同一模型内高度共享且可迁移，但指令微调会改变哪些稀疏边最关键。

> **TL;DR:** This paper converts numerical anchoring bias into a controlled 9-option multiple-choice task, validates a correct-vs-anchor logit-difference metric against behavioral EV shifts, and uses Edge Attribution Patching with Integrated Gradients (EAP-IG) to localize anchoring circuits in Qwen2.5-7B and Llama-3.1-8B (base + instruction-tuned). Edge-level methods strongly outperform node-level methods. Low- and high-anchor circuits overlap ~66–71% in top-5% edges and transfer with CPR ≥ 0.93. However, base↔instruction-tuned circuit transfer is less reliable (sparse transfer drops as low as 0.17 at 5% retained), showing post-training reshapes which pathways carry anchoring.

## 核心贡献

- 将数值锚定效应从开放式生成任务转化为可控的9选项多选框架，使机制分析具有明确的目标指标。
- 定义并验证了 correct–anchor logit-difference 指标（Δm），证明其与行为层面的期望值（EV）偏移具有可靠的相关性（Spearman ρ 高达 0.75）。
- 在 Qwen2.5-7B 和 Llama-3.1-8B 的四个模型变体上系统比较了节点级（NAP/NAP-IG）和边级（EAP/EAP-IG）归因方法，发现边级方法在所有设置下都显著优于节点级方法（EAP-IG CPR ≥ 0.91）。
- 发现低锚和高锚回路的层间归因分布几乎完全重叠（Pearson r > 0.99），且 top-5% 边共享率达 66–71%，跨对比迁移 CPR 为 0.93–0.98。
- 揭示指令微调虽然保留了粗粒度的层间归因位置，但改变了最关键的稀疏边（跨变体迁移在低锚条件下 5% 保留时 CPR 可降至 0.17–0.29）。

## Key Contributions

- **Controlled anchoring benchmark:** Convert numerical anchoring into a 9-option MCQA task with tokenizer-matched controls, providing well-defined single-token logits for circuit localization.
- **Validated attribution metric:** Define a correct–anchor logit-difference (Δm) and validate it against behavioral expected-value shifts across all four models, with Spearman correlations up to ρ = 0.75.
- **Systematic method comparison:** Compare NAP, EAP, NAP-IG, and EAP-IG on 4 models × 2 anchor directions. Edge-level methods consistently outperform node-level methods; EAP-IG is the most faithful (CPR ≥ 0.91 in all settings).
- **Cross-contrast transfer:** Low- and high-anchor circuits share 66–71% of top-5% edges, with per-layer attribution profiles correlated r > 0.99. Cross-contrast CPR ranges 0.933–0.981.
- **Instruction-tuning disrupts sparse circuits:** While broad layer-wise attribution profiles survive instruction tuning, specific top-ranked edges differ substantially. Cross-variant transfer at 5% retained edges can drop to CPR ≈ 0.17–0.29 on low-anchor contrasts.

## Methodology

**Task design:** 100 numerical-estimation questions from the OpAQ dataset (Röseler et al., 2022) are converted into a 9-option multiple-choice format. Each item has a ground-truth value, a low anchor, and a high anchor embedded in an irrelevant slot-machine sentence. Tokenizer-matched controls replace the numerical anchor with a non-numeric "X" string of matching token length, ensuring that any behavioral difference is attributable to the numerical content rather than token-count effects.

**Behavioral metrics:** Normalized expected-value (EV) shift measures directional movement of the model's probability distribution; total variation distance (TVD) measures overall distributional change. 20 random option permutations are averaged for behavioral analysis; 4 fixed cyclic rotations (400 paired examples per anchor contrast) are used for attribution.

**Attribution metric:** A correct–anchor logit difference m = logit(y_correct) − logit(y_anchor) is computed at the final answer position. Δm = m_anchored − m_control captures how the anchor makes the anchor-corresponding answer more competitive.

**Circuit localization:** Following the MIB framework (Mueller et al., 2025b), the transformer is represented as a computation graph. Four attribution-patching methods are compared: NAP, EAP, NAP-IG, EAP-IG. The clean input is the control prompt; the corrupted input is the anchored prompt. Circuits are formed from top-ranked entries at retained fractions k ∈ {.001, .002, .005, .01, .02, .05, .1, .2, .5, 1}. Faithfulness is measured by Circuit Performance Recovery (CPR): the area under the recovery curve of the control–anchored gap in m.

**Models:** Qwen2.5-7B, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct (28 layers), Llama-3.1-8B, Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (32 layers).

## Results & Highlights

- **Anchoring is robust in MCQA:** All 4 models exhibit behavioral anchoring (low anchors shift EV down, high anchors shift EV up). Qwen models show stronger effects (EV shift up to −0.141/+0.202) than Llama (−0.063/+0.023). Instruction-tuned Qwen shows the strongest anchoring.
- **EAP-IG dominates:** In all 8 model–contrast combinations, EAP-IG achieves the highest CPR (0.91–0.97), outperforming EAP in 7/8 cases. Node-level methods (NAP, NAP-IG) achieve only 0.33–0.71 CPR.
- **Layer-wise attribution is model-family specific:** Qwen concentrates attribution in mid-to-late layers; Llama concentrates earlier. Within a model, low- and high-anchor profiles are nearly identical (Pearson r > 0.99, centroid difference < 0.01 relative depth).
- **Attention-heavy circuits:** ~68–80% of top-5% edges are attention-to-attention, with attention–MLP edges disproportionately represented versus the full graph, suggesting MLPs participate via attention-mediated pathways.
- **Strong low↔high transfer:** Cross-contrast CPR ranges 0.933–0.981, barely below matched CPR. Jaccard overlap is moderate (~0.37–0.41) but shared fraction is high (0.66–0.71).
- **Weak base↔instruct transfer:** Cross-variant CPR remains above random but can be very low at sparse retained fractions. At 5% retained, Qwen-7B drops from 0.917 matched to 0.291 cross-variant; Llama-Inst drops from 0.408 to 0.166. High-anchor transfer is somewhat better preserved.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** This is the first work to apply full circuit-localization methodology (with faithfulness evaluation) to a cognitive bias in LLMs. The systematic comparison of 4 attribution methods across 4 model variants and 2 anchor directions is thorough. The cross-contrast and cross-variant transfer analyses are novel and informative.
- **Rigor: Strong.** The tokenizer-matched control design is a genuine methodological contribution — it cleanly isolates numerical content from token-count confounds. Validating the attribution metric (Δm) against behavioral EV shifts before using it for localization is methodologically sound. The 9-option menu size is justified with an ablation (Appendix A). Multiple option permutations control for position/letter-order effects.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** All models are open-weight. The OpAQ dataset is public. The task format and metrics are clearly specified. However, the 100-question sample is relatively small for circuit analysis, and the exact random seeds / permutation sets are not specified.
- **Significance: High.** The finding that instruction tuning reshapes anchoring circuits has practical implications: debiasing interventions on base models may not transfer to deployed instruction-tuned models. The demonstration that edge-level methods are necessary for capturing bias pathways is an important methodological takeaway.
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Only 100 questions from a single dataset — generalization to broader numerical reasoning is uncertain.
  - The multiple-choice format differs from real-world anchoring (open-ended generation). The authors acknowledge this but the gap is real.
  - Only 7B–8B models are tested; it is unclear whether findings scale.
  - The paper does not attempt any debiasing intervention based on the identified circuits — the practical utility of the mechanistic account is suggested but not demonstrated.
  - The logit-difference metric captures competition between only two specific answers (correct vs. anchor), not the full distributional effect.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally clean experimental design: tokenizer-matched controls, option-permutation averaging, validated attribution metric.
- Systematic and comprehensive: 4 attribution methods × 4 models × 2 anchor directions with multiple overlap and transfer analyses.
- Clear, well-motivated connection between cognitive science (anchoring bias) and mechanistic interpretability.
- The finding that low/high anchors share pathways is practically useful — it suggests debiasing need not target each anchor direction separately.
- Honest about limitations; the paper explicitly states what the circuits do and do not capture.

## Limitations

- **Small question set:** 100 items from a single dataset (OpAQ) limits generalizability. Real-world numerical reasoning is far more diverse.
- **MCQA-only:** The fixed-option format is a controlled probe, not the natural setting for anchoring. Open-ended generation may involve different mechanisms.
- **Scale:** Only 7B–8B models tested. Whether anchoring circuits in 70B+ models show the same layer-wise profiles and transfer properties is unknown.
- **No intervention results:** The paper identifies circuits but does not demonstrate that modifying them mitigates anchoring. The causal utility of the findings is prospective, not proven.
- **Metric scope:** The logit-difference metric captures one slice of the anchoring effect. Other objectives (KL divergence, TVD, full distribution shift) might highlight different circuits.

## Relevance

本文将认知偏差的机制可解释性研究推进到回路定位层面，对理解大模型中无关信息如何影响决策通路、以及后训练如何改变这些通路具有重要价值，与模型对齐、偏差缓解和机制可解释性方向高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the identified EAP-IG circuits be used for surgical activation-level interventions (e.g., ablating or dampening specific edges) to reduce anchoring without degrading factual accuracy?
- Do the same layer-wise attribution profiles appear in 70B+ models, or does anchoring pathway structure change qualitatively with scale?
- How do different post-training strategies beyond instruction tuning (RLHF, DPO, constitutional AI) reshape anchoring circuits?
- Are there shared anchoring circuits across different types of cognitive biases (framing, confirmation bias), suggesting a general "bias susceptibility" subnetwork?
- Does the strong low↔high transfer hold in open-ended generation settings, or does the MCQA format artificially inflate shared structure?
