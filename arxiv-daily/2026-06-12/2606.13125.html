Select and Improve: Understanding the Mechanics of Post-Training for Reasoning

Akshay Krishnamurthy, Audrey Huang, Nived Rajaraman

通过有限域算术受控实验揭示RL后训练的两个核心机制——策略选择与策略改进——并证明RL不能创造新的推理策略，SFT数据多样性是关键前提。

Key Finding

RL post-training improves reasoning through two mechanisms: strategy selection (routing to the best SFT-learned pattern) and strategy improvement (strengthening patterns on harder data). RL does not invent new strategies.

Key Metric
≥95%

accuracy on 6–9 step problems (mixed SFT + hard RL) vs ~50–60% single-strategy

Why This Matters

SFT data diversity matters more than the RL algorithm. RL can only select and refine what SFT already provided.

Critical Edge

Tested on a single 1.5B model + synthetic finite-field task. Whether "RL cannot create strategies" holds for 7B+ models or real math is unverified.

Methodology

  • Synthetic task: finite-field arithmetic over GF(11) with abstract symbols, breaking all pre-trained math knowledge. Two problem types: evaluation (forward) and inversion (backward).
  • Pipeline: Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct → LoRA SFT on 2–5 step problems (controlled strategy exposure) → GRPO RL on 6–9 step problems with binary outcome rewards.
  • Key variations: SFT strategy (forward-only, backward-only, mixed), RL difficulty (in-distribution vs. harder), dataset tilt, and composition problems requiring sequential sub-problem solving.
  • Scale: 2048 SFT examples × 20 epochs; 1024 RL prompts × 500–1000 steps; 3 seeds per config; 1–4 A100 GPUs.

Results & Highlights

Strategy selection is fast: routing completes in ~40 RL steps; improvement continues over hundreds.
RL on easy data hurts: 2–5 step RL sometimes degrades hard-problem performance (B-only: 24.5% → 18.4%).
Amplification tracks data: forward-style generation fraction precisely follows evaluation-problem fraction in tilted datasets.
Composition needs SFT: zero improvement on 3–5 part composition without compositional SFT examples (~9% random baseline).
  • Single-strategy models are fundamentally limited — RL cannot compensate for missing alternative strategies.
  • Results robust across learning rates, KL coefficients, group sizes; replicated on GF(13).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty · High: First clean decomposition of RL post-training into selection + improvement via controlled experiments.
  • Rigor · Very high: Synthetic task eliminates confounds; systematic variations isolate each mechanism; 3 seeds + ablations + GF(13) replication.
  • Reproducibility · Good: 1–4 A100 GPUs; SFT 1–2h, RL 2–3h; synthetic task removes data dependency.
  • Significance · High with caveats: Actionable takeaways (SFT diversity, RL difficulty curriculum), but the gap between synthetic GF arithmetic and real math/coding is substantial.

Relevance / 相关性

本文对RL后训练推理能力的机理分解直接关联到如何高效训练推理模型这一核心问题。策略选择和策略改进两个机制为SFT数据多样性和RL数据难度设计提供了明确的实验依据，对理解和优化reasoning模型的训练流程具有直接指导意义。

Strengths

  • Clean experimental design enabling causal attribution
  • Clever synthetic task controlling pre-existing knowledge
  • Unifies fragmented literature under two mechanisms
  • Honest about limitations; explicit open questions

Limitations

  • Single 1.5B model; no real benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH)
  • Binary strategy taxonomy oversimplifies real reasoning
  • "RL can't create strategies" may be a synthetic-task artifact
  • Only GRPO tested; PPO/DPO transfer unknown

Open Questions

Q1Do these mechanisms hold at 7B, 70B+? Can larger models exhibit genuinely novel strategy creation?
Q2Can strategy selection be detected via model internals (attention, probing) rather than manual classification?
Q3How does the difficulty curriculum interact with noisy reward-model quality in real settings?
Q4With >2 strategies, does RL learn complex routing or converge to a single dominant strategy?
Q5Can this explain CoT lengthening or self-correction emergence in models like DeepSeek-R1?
Q6How do these mechanisms interact with multi-turn RL or iterative self-improvement?
