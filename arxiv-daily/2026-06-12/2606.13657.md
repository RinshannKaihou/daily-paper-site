> **一句话总结：** 本文通过分析10个模型对的checkpoint差异，系统揭示了on-policy distillation（OPD）的参数更新虽然由稠密教师信号驱动，但呈现坐标稀疏（66-90%坐标无变化）、谱集中（top-16奇异值占比~27%）且偏离源权重主奇异方向的几何特征，训练仅17.5%坐标的稀疏子网络即可恢复完整OPD性能。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides a rigorous empirical analysis of on-policy distillation (OPD) — a post-training paradigm combining on-policy student rollouts with dense teacher token-level supervision — through the lens of parameter update sparsity and geometry. Across 10 model pairs (6 OPD + 3 RLVR + 1 offline distillation reference), the authors find: (1) OPD updates are small (0.036–0.142% relative norm) and coordinate-sparse (66–90% coordinates unchanged at 10⁻⁵ threshold), mostly concentrated in FFN layers (65–86% energy); (2) training only the discovered sparse subnetwork (e.g., 17.5% of coordinates for DS-Qwen) recovers full OPD performance (35.10% vs. 35.52% peak accuracy); (3) OPD updates are numerically full-rank but spectrally concentrated (stable rank 7.77–12.58 vs. 82.74 for offline distillation); (4) updates lie away from source weight principal singular subspaces and disproportionately affect near-zero source coordinates; (5) AdamW significantly outperforms SGD (42.40% vs. 37.92% final accuracy), unlike RLVR where SGD is competitive — dense teacher supervision preserves heterogeneous gradient scales. The key takeaway: on-policy data distribution, not reward sparsity, is the primary determinant of sparse, off-principal update geometry in post-training.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统分析OPD参数更新的稀疏性与几何特性，揭示了稠密教师监督并不导致稠密参数重写
- 在6个OPD模型对上发现更新是坐标稀疏的（66-90%坐标无可见变化），且更新能量集中在FFN模块（65-86%）
- 发现OPD子网络掩码与RLVR子网络掩码重叠度远超随机基线（约3倍），说明不同on-policy方法触及相似的推理相关坐标
- 证明仅训练发现的稀疏子网络（约17.5%参数）即可恢复接近完整OPD的性能（35.10% vs 35.52%）
- 几何分析揭示OPD更新虽数值满秩但谱集中，且偏离源权重主奇异方向、偏向源权重接近零的坐标
- 发现与RLVR不同，AdamW在OPD中显著优于SGD，说明稠密教师信号保留了异构梯度尺度

## Key Contributions

- First systematic empirical study of OPD parameter update sparsity and geometry across 10 model pairs spanning LLMs and VLMs
- Demonstration that OPD updates are coordinate-sparse (66–90% at 10⁻⁵ threshold) and FFN-heavy (65–86% delta energy), despite dense teacher supervision
- Discovery that OPD subnetwork masks overlap with RLVR masks ~3× above random baselines, suggesting shared reasoning-related coordinates
- Proof that training only the discovered sparse subnetwork (~17.5% of coordinates) recovers full OPD reasoning performance on AIME24/25
- Geometric characterization showing OPD updates are numerically full-rank but spectrally concentrated (stable rank 7.77–12.58 vs. 82.74 for offline distillation), and preferentially write into low-magnitude source coordinates away from principal singular subspaces
- Optimizer ablation revealing AdamW outperforms SGD by ~4.5 points in OPD (unlike RLVR where SGD is competitive), attributed to heterogeneous coordinate-wise gradient scales from dense teacher supervision

## Methodology

**Model pairs analyzed (10 total):**
- *6 OPD targets:* DS-Qwen (DeepScaleR-1.5B teacher), DS-Qwen (JustRL teacher), MiniCPM5-1B OPD, Qwen2.5-VL-3B OPD (NoisyRollout-7B teacher), Qwen3-1.7B OPD, Qwen3-4B OPSD
- *3 RLVR references:* DeepScaleR-1.5B, JustRL, Qwen2.5-VL GRPO
- *1 offline distillation contrast:* Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B → DS-Qwen

**Metrics framework (three complementary axes):**

1. **Scale and coordinate support:** Relative Frobenius norm (∥ΔW∥_F / ∥W_src∥_F), visible coordinate sparsity at ε=10⁻⁵, coordinate energy concentration c_p
2. **Spectral metrics:** Top-k SVD energy, stable rank (∥ΔW∥²_F / ∥ΔW∥²_2), numerical rank at 10⁻⁴ threshold
3. **Source-geometry metrics:** Principal-subspace projection p_k (alignment with source SVD directions), visible-update coverage on principal vs. low-magnitude source coordinate masks

**Interventional experiments:**
- *Subnetwork training:* Restart OPD from source checkpoint, zero gradients outside a chosen mask (full-OPD mask, RLVR mask, or random density-matched mask). Compare on AIME24+AIME25 mean@16 accuracy.
- *Optimizer ablation:* AdamW (lr=10⁻⁶) vs. SGD (lr=10⁻²) in JustRL-teacher OPD setting, with momentum alignment and second-moment coefficient of variation diagnostics.

**Computation details:** All checkpoint deltas computed as bfloat16 subtraction with float32 arithmetic for norms/SVD. Code available at https://github.com/SydCS/OPD-Param-Analysis.

## Results & Highlights

**Sparsity (Section 4):**
- OPD relative delta norms: 0.036% (Qwen3 OPSD) to 0.142% (Qwen2.5-VL OPD) — nearly two orders of magnitude smaller than offline distillation (11.94%)
- Coordinate sparsity: 66.72% (Qwen2.5-VL OPD) to 89.50% (Qwen3 OPSD) — vs. 3.06% for offline distillation
- FFN accounts for 65–86% of delta energy; attention 10–37%; layer norm negligible
- OPD-RLVR mask overlap: ~3× above random baselines (e.g., 53.21% vs. 17.50% for DeepScaleR RLVR → DS-Qwen OPD)
- Subnetwork training: OPD mask (17.5% density) reaches 35.10% peak accuracy vs. 35.52% for full OPD; random mask peaks at 32.92%

**Geometry (Section 5):**
- OPD updates are numerically full-rank but spectrally concentrated: median top-16 SVD energy 19.69–30.52%, stable rank 7.77–12.58 (vs. offline distillation: 8.57% top-16 energy, stable rank 82.74)
- Principal-subspace projection <1% at 10% rank for all OPD pairs — updates avoid dominant source directions
- Source-principal coordinate coverage: 4.39–5.46% (below 10% random baseline); low-magnitude coordinate coverage: 24.99–48.57% (well above baseline)

**Optimizer ablation (Section 4.5):**
- AdamW: 43.02% peak, 42.40% final accuracy (mean@16 on AIME24+25)
- SGD: 39.06% peak, 37.92% final accuracy
- AdamW second-moment coefficient of variation averages 4.85, confirming heterogeneous gradient scales persist under dense supervision

**Boundary case — JustRL as teacher:**
- JustRL RLVR checkpoint: 0.497% relative norm, 34.68% sparsity (less sparse than GRPO)
- When used as OPD teacher, the student OPD delta returns to sparse pattern (0.108% norm, 70.43% sparsity) — teacher sparsity is NOT necessary for OPD to produce sparse student updates

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are from Nanjing University (School of Artificial Intelligence, National Key Laboratory for Novel Software Technology) and Amap/Alibaba Group. Han-Jia Ye is a corresponding author and established researcher. The paper provides code for reproducibility. The analysis covers 10 diverse model pairs across both LLM and VLM settings, with three categories of OPD applications (large-to-small distillation, capability consolidation, self-distillation), lending strong credibility to the generality of findings. The paper directly engages with and extends recent work by Mukherjee et al. (2026a,b) and Zhu et al. (2025) on RLVR parameter dynamics.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. The paper fills a genuine gap in understanding OPD's weight-space behavior. The finding that dense teacher supervision + on-policy data still yields sparse, off-principal updates is non-trivial and counter-intuitive. The OPD-RLVR mask overlap result is particularly insightful, suggesting shared "reasoning coordinates" across different on-policy methods.

**Rigor:** Generally strong. The multi-metric framework (sparsity + spectral + source-geometry) is comprehensive. Ten model pairs provide broad coverage. However, several concerns merit attention:
- The sparsity threshold ε=10⁻⁵ is somewhat arbitrary; a sensitivity analysis across thresholds would strengthen claims.
- The subnetwork experiment only shows results on math reasoning benchmarks (AIME24/25); generalization to other tasks is unverified.
- The interventional experiments are limited to 2 model families (DS-Qwen and Qwen2.5-VL). The observational claims rest on more pairs, but interventions are narrower.
- The AdamW vs. SGD ablation uses different learning rates (10⁻⁶ vs. 10⁻²) without a full hyperparameter sweep; the gap might narrow with better SGD tuning.

**Reproducibility:** Good. Code is publicly available. The metrics are well-defined with formal specifications in the appendix. The model pairs use publicly available checkpoints.

**Significance:** The practical implication — that ~17.5% of parameters suffice for OPD — could enable more efficient deployment. The conceptual contribution (on-policy distribution matters more than reward density for update geometry) reshapes understanding of post-training dynamics. The connection to LoRA and orthogonal fine-tuning (OFT/BOFT) opens concrete future directions.

**Potential red flags:**
- The tension between "sparse updates" and "AdamW >> SGD" is intellectually interesting but somewhat undermines the simplicity of the sparsity narrative. The paper acknowledges this but the explanation (heterogeneous gradient scales) feels incomplete.
- All models are relatively small (1.5B–7B). Whether these findings hold at 70B+ remains entirely speculative.
- The paper analyzes only final checkpoints, not training trajectories. As the authors note, the dynamics producing these sparse endpoints are unrevealed.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally thorough multi-dimensional analysis framework spanning sparsity, spectral properties, and source-geometry alignment
- Strong experimental design with 10 model pairs, appropriate baselines (offline distillation, RLVR references), and both observational + interventional evidence
- Clear, honest discussion of limitations and boundary cases (e.g., JustRL as teacher, Qwen3 OPSD's larger attention contribution)
- The OPD-RLVR mask overlap finding opens an intriguing direction for understanding "reasoning subnetworks"
- Well-written with precise formal definitions of all metrics
- Code available for reproducibility

## Limitations

- All analyzed models are small-scale (1.5B–7B parameters); scalability to frontier models (70B+) is unknown
- Interventions are limited to math reasoning on AIME24/25; other domains (coding, agentic, multimodal reasoning) are untested
- Static final-checkpoint analysis only — training dynamics and trajectory of sparsity/geometry emergence are not studied
- The AdamW vs. SGD comparison lacks a thorough hyperparameter sweep for SGD
- The practical value of sparse subnetwork discovery depends on identifying the subnetwork a priori (currently requires running full OPD first)
- The paper does not propose any new OPD algorithm or training recipe; it is purely analytical

## Relevance

本文对on-policy distillation参数更新的稀疏性和几何特性的深入分析，对于理解post-training阶段模型参数变化规律、设计高效参数微调方法（如LoRA、OFT）以及优化器选择具有重要指导意义，与知识蒸馏、模型压缩和LLM训练动态研究高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the sparse OPD subnetwork be identified a priori (before any OPD training) using model-theoretic heuristics, removing the need for the initial full run?
- How do sparsity and geometry patterns evolve during the OPD training trajectory — are they present from early steps or emerge gradually?
- Do these findings generalize to 70B+ models where computational constraints make the subnetwork approach even more valuable?
- Given the spectral concentration of OPD updates, what rank of LoRA is needed to capture, say, 90% of the update energy? Can LoRA rank be guided by the stable rank measurements?
- Can the OPD-RLVR mask overlap be exploited for multi-stage training — e.g., using RLVR to identify a subnetwork, then applying OPD only within that mask?
- Would orthogonal fine-tuning methods (OFT/BOFT) naturally align with the off-principal geometry of OPD updates, and could they outperform LoRA for OPD?
- How do the findings change for non-reasoning OPD applications (e.g., style transfer, safety alignment, agentic capability consolidation)?
