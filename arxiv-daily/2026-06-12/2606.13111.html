MÖVE: A Holistic LLM Benchmark for the German Public Sector

Camilla Dalerci, Thilo Michael, Robin Schaefer, Daniel Weinland

First benchmark to evaluate 39 LLMs across both performance (summarization, QA, topic extraction) and governance (hallucination, sustainability, transparency, constitutional-value alignment) on 10 German-language datasets. No single model dominates all criteria. Model size is a poor predictor of quality.

MÖVE 是首个针对德国公共部门的 LLM 综合评测基准，在 39 个模型上同时评估性能与治理两大维度，发现没有单一模型在所有指标上全面领先，且模型规模不是质量的可靠预测因素。

Key Finding
No single model dominates across all criteria. Model size alone is a poor predictor of quality — GPT-4o Mini matches models 10× its size.
Key Metric
84.6%of models provide no energy-consumption data. Compute & Energy transparency averages 11.5%.
Why This Matters
The performance + governance dual-axis framework is the template national LLM benchmarks need under the EU AI Act era.
Critical Edge
LLM-judge inter-rater agreement falls below reliability thresholds (α < 0.67 for 4/5 metrics). Rankings are tiers, not precise orderings.

⬡ Methodology

39 models from 13 providers evaluated across two dimensions on 10 German-language datasets.

Performance Summarization (4 datasets) · QA (3 datasets) · Topic Extraction (2 datasets)
Governance Hallucination · Sustainability (Wh/query) · Transparency (21-question matrix, 7 domains) · Politics & Values
Metrics ROUGE, BLEU, BERTScore, SemScore, LLM-as-a-judge (GPT-4o Mini), hierarchical bootstrap CIs
Datasets Gold + silver domain-specific sets (KIKC, FAQ-LAW, ministry publications) to mitigate contamination

Transparency Matrix Coverage

Model Identification~85%
Architecture~72%
Distribution & Access~65%
Compute & Energy11.5%

Key Results

  • Mistral-Large-3 leads summarization; GPT-4o / GPT-4o Mini lead QA
  • Gemma2 9B: 5th-highest performance while among the most energy-efficient
  • Teuken 7B leads energy efficiency at 0.647 Wh/query
  • Apertus 70B leads transparency at 90.5% (designed for EU AI Act compliance)
  • No model exhibits clearly negative stance toward German constitutional values (possible positivity bias)
  • EU Code of Practice signatories score only marginally higher (27.6 vs 26.3)

⚖ Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • First holistic national-context benchmark combining performance + governance
  • Unusually thorough self-evaluation (CIs, judge reliability, prompt sensitivity)
  • Living benchmark with continuous public updates
  • Private datasets prevent contamination

Limitations

  • Some gold datasets have only ~40 samples
  • LLM-judge agreement below reliability thresholds
  • Energy estimated, not measured (API models)
  • Only 3 task types; German-only
  • Value alignment may reflect positivity bias

? Open Questions

  1. Would results stabilize with 200+ gold-standard samples?
  2. Can multi-judge averaging (3+ LLMs) improve reliability affordably?
  3. Are transparent models also more energy-efficient and better aligned?
  4. How transferable is the MÖVE framework to other national contexts?
  5. Does the "no negative stance" finding hold under adversarial prompting?
  6. What does fine-tuning on German public-sector text change for governance scores?
