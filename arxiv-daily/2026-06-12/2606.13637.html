The Stable Recovery Manifold: Geometric Principles Governing Recoverability in Continual Learning

Ayushman Trivedi & Bhavika Melwani

遗忘知识始终保存在约8维的紧凑稳定恢复流形中（仅占512维的1.56%），主角度漂移是可恢复性的主导预测因子（r = −0.862），82%的可恢复性方差可由三个几何变量解释——灾难性遗忘本质是流形旋转/对齐问题，而非信息破坏。

TL;DR — Six experiments on ResNet-18 / Split CIFAR-100 show that forgotten task knowledge persists in a compact ~8-dimensional recovery subspace that does not grow over ten sequential tasks. Principal-angle drift is the dominant predictor of recoverability (r = −0.862), and a three-variable linear model explains 82.2% of variance. Catastrophic forgetting is a geometric alignment failure, not information destruction.

Key Finding
Forgotten knowledge persists in a stable ~8-dim recovery manifold — forgetting is alignment failure, not destruction.
Key Metric
kt ≈ 8
σ = 0.82  ·  8 / 512 = 1.56%
R² = 0.822  ·  r = −0.862
Why This Matters
If kt ≈ 8 generalizes, only 8×d = 4,096 params per task need preservation — enabling efficient replay-free continual learning.
Critical Edge
Single architecture (ResNet-18), single dataset, only 10 data points. Whether kt ≈ 8 is universal or a ResNet artifact is unknown.

Core Results

  • kt stability — Fluctuates within {7, 8, 9} across all 10 tasks (mean 8.0, σ 0.82), occupying 1.56% of representational dimensionality. Directly falsifies the "Recoverability Diffusion" hypothesis.
  • Core paradox — Subspace rotates 65° and loses 36.7% projection energy by Task 9, yet Rt ≈ 0.70 throughout. Recovery manifold moves as a "rigid body."
  • Regression — Rt = 0.455·LPt + 0.237·PEt + 0.001·Dt + 0.207 (R² = 0.822). Orientation trumps volume.
  • Accuracy vs recoverability — ACCt collapses from 0.770 → 0.094, yet Rt stays at ~0.696 mean. The network loses classifier alignment, not content.

Strengths

  • Counterintuitive finding that cleanly falsifies the initial hypothesis — scientifically mature approach
  • Strong quantitative framework: R² = 0.82, r = −0.862, kt = 8.0 with tight variance
  • Six well-designed experiments providing convergent geometric evidence
  • Novel depth-stratification result explains early-layer retention mechanically
  • Direct implications for future mitigation (orientation-targeted regularization, compact storage)
  • Honest discussion of limitations and the falsified hypothesis

Limitations

  • Single architecture — skip connections may artificially constrain kt
  • Single dataset — scalability to ImageNet or NLP is unknown
  • Only 10 sequential tasks — behavior at t = 50 or 100 is untested
  • Task 0-centric — recovery of intermediate tasks not studied
  • No practical algorithm implemented or benchmarked
  • 10 data points limit statistical confidence in regression claims
  • Best-case recovery — uses Task 0 data for retraining probe

Open Questions

  1. Does kt ≈ 8 hold for transformers (ViTs, LLMs), MLP-Mixers, and plain convnets?
  2. Can a "Manifold Anchor Regularizer" maintain recoverability without inhibiting plasticity?
  3. What happens to kt in domain-incremental or task-free settings?
  4. Is the recovery manifold task-specific or shared across tasks?
  5. Could storing just 8×d = 4,096 params per task enable efficient replay-free CL at scale?
  6. How sensitive is kt to the 90% probe accuracy threshold?
