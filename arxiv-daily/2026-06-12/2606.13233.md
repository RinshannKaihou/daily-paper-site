> **一句话总结：** ReSET通过分析NVFP4量化对推理步骤中token不确定性的影响，提出基于步骤级熵的自适应温度缩放方法，配合专门设计的CUDA-core NVFP4 GEMV内核，在AIME-120上恢复最多+2.6个准确度点，同时实现相对BF16最高1.97×的端到端解码加速。

> **TL;DR:** Applying NVFP4 (NVIDIA's FP4) quantization to large reasoning models degrades accuracy because quantization noise causes mis-sampling at low-entropy symbolic tokens (digits, operators) and over-concentration at high-uncertainty reasoning steps. ReSET fixes this with a training-free, step-aware temperature scaling policy that estimates step-level entropy online and adapts the softmax temperature: sharpening symbolic tokens in confident steps (lower T) and restoring diversity in uncertain steps (higher T). The method recovers up to +2.6 points on AIME-120 and +2.6 on average across all benchmarks over the NVFP4 baseline. A companion CUDA-core NVFP4 GEMV kernel (the first public implementation of its kind) targets the small-batch (M≤8) decode regime where Tensor-Core paths achieve <1% utilization, delivering 1.57–2.49× kernel speedup and up to 1.97× end-to-end speedup over BF16 on B200 hardware.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示了NVFP4量化导致推理失败的根本机制**：量化噪声在低熵符号token（数字、运算符）处引发非top-1替代token被错误采样，同时在高不确定性推理步骤中导致概率过度集中于少数token。关键发现是token级熵受步骤级不确定性主导——同一符号token在不确定步骤中熵值会升高，使得固定阈值方法失效。
- **提出ReSET（Reasoning-step Entropy-based Temperature Scaling）方法**：在线估计步骤级熵，在确信步骤中使用全局阈值τ₀识别符号token并降温（T_low），在不确定步骤中使用步骤级相对阈值，仅对低于步骤平均熵的token降温，对其他token升温（T_high=1.0）恢复量化压缩的多样性。无需训练，每token仅增加约1.5%的解码开销。
- **设计了首个公开的CUDA-core NVFP4 GEMV内核**：针对生产环境中M≤8的延迟敏感自回归解码场景，通过多token CTA融合、多累加器线程映射和寄存器级反量化三个关键设计，避免了Tensor-Core固定128行tile造成的<6.25%占用率问题。
- **全面实验验证**：在5个推理模型（R1-Qwen-7B/14B, Qwen3-8B/14B/32B）和3个基准（AIME-120, GPQA-Diamond, LiveCodeBench）上，ReSET在所有基准上均达到最佳NVFP4 PTQ精度；CUDA内核在B200上实现1.97×端到端加速。

## Key Contributions

- **Diagnose step-level uncertainty as the key factor in NVFP4 reasoning degradation**: Token-level entropy is dominated by the surrounding reasoning step's uncertainty, not by the token's own identity. Fixed token-level entropy thresholds fail because symbolic tokens (digits, operators) in uncertain steps get misclassified as high-entropy positions, causing the decoding rule to skip necessary sharpening.
- **Propose ReSET — a training-free, step-aware temperature scaling policy**: Uses a hybrid online step-entropy estimator (HSE) that switches between a sliding-window initialization (first w=32 tokens of a step) and a within-step running average. The step-aware threshold (SAT) uses the global τ₀ in confident steps and step-relative Ĥ_step(t) in uncertain steps, ensuring symbolic tokens are correctly identified regardless of step-level context.
- **Design the first public CUDA-core NVFP4 GEMV kernel for small-M decoding**: Addresses three challenges — (C1) multi-token CTA fusion for weight reuse, (C2) multi-accumulator threading for instruction-level parallelism at small M, and (C3) register-only dequantization eliminating shared-memory synchronization. Achieves 1.57–2.49× speedup over vLLM-CUTLASS at M=1.
- **Demonstrate state-of-the-art NVFP4 reasoning with real hardware results**: On NVIDIA B200 with real NVFP4 W4A4 quantization via NVIDIA ModelOpt, ReSET+custom kernel achieves best accuracy across all PTQ baselines (RTN, BRQ, 4/6, MR-GPTQ) and up to 1.97× E2E speedup over BF16.

## Methodology

ReSET combines two complementary contributions:

### Step-Aware Temperature Scaling (Algorithmic)

1. **Entropy analysis at step granularity**: The authors analyze 1.5M tokens from R1-Qwen-14B and find that reasoning traces alternate between low-entropy steps (H_step≈0.3, confident computation) and high-entropy steps (H_step≈0.9, exploratory branching). Token-level entropy within both low- and high-entropy token groups closely tracks the step entropy, meaning the same token category occupies different absolute entropy positions depending on the surrounding step.

2. **Online step-entropy estimator (HSE)**: Since step boundaries aren't known a priori during autoregressive decoding, HSE uses a hybrid approach:
   - **Early regime** (first w=32 tokens): sliding-window average over the last w tokens (spans step boundary, trades boundary bias for lower variance).
   - **Late regime** (within step): running average from step start t₀ to current position (lowest bias once enough samples exist).
   - This outperforms both EMA and first-token initialization in MAE against the offline oracle.

3. **Step-aware threshold (SAT)**: 
   - If Ĥ_step(t) ≤ H̄ (global mean): use fixed τ₀ (80th percentile entropy from calibration set).
   - If Ĥ_step(t) > H̄ (uncertain step): use τ_t = Ĥ_step(t) as threshold — a token is sharpened iff its entropy is below the typical token of its own step.
   - Tokens below threshold get T_low (calibrated per model/task on 5 held-out problems); tokens above get T_high = 1.0 (consistently optimal across models).

4. **Calibration**: τ₀ and T_low are set on 5 NuminaMath problems (held-out from evaluation). Hyperparameters are robust: window size w ∈ {16,32,64,128} changes average by only 1.1 points; T_low ∈ {0.1,0.2,0.3,0.4} changes by 0.4 points.

### CUDA-Core NVFP4 GEMV Kernel (Systems)

1. **Problem**: Blackwell's tcgen05.mma instruction requires M=128 tile along the token dimension. Production LRM serving at TPOT SLOs caps M≤8 on B200, resulting in <6.25% tile occupancy and <1% Tensor-Core utilization.

2. **Design choices**:
   - **Multi-token CTA fusion**: Multiple decode tokens share one CTA (thread block), so each weight tile loaded from HBM is reused across all tokens.
   - **Multi-accumulator threading**: Each thread holds multiple independent accumulator chains for different output rows, enabling warp-scheduler interleaving.
   - **Register-only dequantization**: FP4 values are unpacked directly to FP16 in registers and fed into half2 FMAs without materializing in shared memory, eliminating synchronization barriers.

3. **Dispatch strategy**: CUDA-core path for decode (small M); Tensor-Core path for prefill and large-M scenarios.

## Results & Highlights

### Accuracy (Table 1, AIME-120 + GPQA-Diamond + LiveCodeBench, 5 models)

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| ReSET vs. RTN (NVFP4 baseline) on AIME-120 | +2.6 avg (e.g., R1-Qwen-7B: 43.8 vs 39.6; Qwen3-32B: 77.5 vs 74.4) |
| ReSET vs. best PTQ baseline (BRQ/4/6/MR-GPTQ) | Best on every benchmark |
| ReSET vs. BF16 | Closes gap significantly: e.g., Qwen3-32B AIME: 77.5 (ReSET) vs 75.8 (BF16) |
| ReSET on Qwen3-32B AIME | **Exceeds BF16** (77.5 vs 75.8) — quantization + temperature scaling improves over full precision |

### Comparison with Alternative Decoding Strategies (Table 2, 5)

- **Fixed threshold**: Inconsistent across models (+2.2 on R1-Qwen-7B but -1.1 on R1-Qwen-14B).
- **Sliding window**: Similar inconsistency.
- **Top-p sweep**: Marginal average improvement at best.
- **Min-p sweep**: Consistently underperforms NVFP4 baseline.
- **ReSET**: Best on every model, confirming step-aware control is the operative signal.

### Kernel & End-to-End Latency (B200, CUDA 13.1, PyTorch 2.10.0)

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Kernel speedup over vLLM-CUTLASS (M=1) | 1.57–2.49× across 12 projection shapes |
| Quantization overhead | Reduced vs. MR-GPTQ (1.65–1.81 µs vs. 4.55–5.08 µs per layer) |
| E2E speedup over BF16 (Qwen3-32B, B=1) | Up to **1.97×** |
| E2E speedup over BF16 (Qwen3-32B, B=8) | Up to **1.85×** |
| ReSET sampler overhead | ~100 µs/token on Qwen3-32B ≈ 1.5% of decode step (included in E2E numbers) |

### Ablation Highlights

- Selective sharpening (only tokens below τ₀ within confident steps) is critical: uniform T_low degrades from 62.5 to 60.6 on AIME-120.
- Step-relative threshold in uncertain steps is essential: without it, symbolic tokens in uncertain steps are misclassified and not sharpened.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High)**: The step-level entropy analysis is a genuine insight — the finding that token-level entropy is dominated by step uncertainty, not token identity, is both surprising and well-supported. The step-aware threshold design is elegant and principled. The CUDA-core NVFP4 GEMV kernel fills a real gap (no prior public implementation existed).

- **Rigor (Strong)**: 
  - 5 models × 3 benchmarks × 8 seeds provides robust evaluation.
  - Like-for-like comparison: all methods share identical weight format (NVFP4 W4A4 via ModelOpt) and KV cache (BF16).
  - Ablations cover all major design choices (threshold policy, window size, temperatures, truncation alternatives).
  - E2E measurements include the ReSET sampling overhead.

- **Reproducibility (Strong)**: Open-source code, standard hardware (B200), well-documented calibration protocol (5 NuminaMath problems). The algorithm is simple enough to reimplement.

- **Significance (High)**: Addresses a critical practical gap — NVFP4's headline 4× throughput advantage collapses at the batch sizes that matter for production LRM serving. ReSET recovers both accuracy and latency in this regime. The Qwen3-32B result where ReSET+NVFP4 *exceeds* BF16 accuracy on AIME-120 is remarkable.

- **Potential concerns**:
  - **Per-model calibration**: T_low requires per-(model, task) calibration on held-out data (Table 13). While the calibration set is tiny (5 problems), this is still an extra step. The paper shows robustness across reasonable T_low ranges, mitigating this.
  - **Generalization beyond NVFP4**: The analysis is specific to NVFP4 quantization artifacts. It's unclear whether the same step-entropy dynamics hold for INT4, GPTQ, or AWQ quantization (the appendix shows some GPTQ results but limited).
  - **Thigh = 1.0 is hardcoded**: The paper states Thigh=1.0 "consistently improves accuracy" but doesn't explore whether this is optimal or just a convenient default. A theoretical justification would strengthen the claim.
  - **Step boundary detection**: The HSE estimator assumes step boundaries are implicitly captured by the entropy trajectory, but doesn't use explicit step segmentation (e.g., newline detection). This could miss structure in models that use different formatting conventions.

## Strengths

- **Identifies and solves a real, previously unrecognized problem**: The small-M Tensor-Core utilization collapse and the token-level vs. step-level entropy mismatch are both genuine insights with practical impact.
- **Zero training cost**: ReSET is a pure decoding-time intervention — no retraining, no calibration data beyond 5 problems.
- **Dual contribution**: Algorithmic (temperature scaling) + systems (CUDA kernel) working synergistically.
- **Honest evaluation**: The paper clearly states where ReSET doesn't help (e.g., LiveCodeBench improvements are modest) and includes comparisons against strong baselines (BRQ, MR-GPTQ).
- **Production-oriented**: Tests on real B200 hardware with real TPOT SLO constraints, not idealized throughput benchmarks.
- **The Qwen3-32B result exceeding BF16** on AIME-120 suggests the temperature scaling insight may have broader applicability beyond just recovering quantization loss.

## Limitations

- **Calibration dependency**: Although minimal (5 problems), T_low needs per-model tuning. A calibration-free variant would be more deployable.
- **Limited to autoregressive decoding**: The kernel and temperature policy are designed for token-by-token generation; batched prefill or speculative decoding are not addressed.
- **Narrow hardware scope**: Only tested on NVIDIA B200 (Blackwell). The Tensor-Core tile problem (M=128) is hardware-specific; AMD or other accelerator platforms may have different tradeoffs.
- **Step boundary sensitivity**: The HSE estimator relies on entropy trajectory to implicitly detect step transitions. Models with unusual formatting (no clear step structure) may not benefit as much.
- **Limited perplexity evaluation**: The paper focuses on accuracy benchmarks but doesn't report perplexity on standard language modeling benchmarks, making it harder to assess impact on non-reasoning tasks.

## Relevance

这项工作与推理模型的高效部署高度相关。NVFP4量化和步骤级温度缩放的结合，对实际推理服务的降本增效有直接且可量化的意义。特别是首次公开的CUDA-core NVFP4 GEMV内核填补了小batch延迟敏感场景的空白，属于模型量化、推理优化和系统设计的交叉领域的前沿工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can ReSET's step-aware temperature scaling be combined with speculative decoding to further reduce latency, or would the temperature perturbation interfere with draft model agreement?
- Does the step-level entropy analysis transfer to other quantization formats (INT4, FP8, GPTQ)? The appendix shows limited GPTQ results — a systematic cross-format study would be valuable.
- Could ReSET be extended to dynamically adjust temperature during a single generation based on real-time accuracy feedback (e.g., reward model scores on partial outputs)?
- The finding that ReSET+NVFP4 exceeds BF16 on Qwen3-32B AIME-120 — is this because the quantization + temperature scaling acts as an implicit regularizer? Can this be exploited for full-precision models?
- How does ReSET interact with longer reasoning traces (>32K tokens)? Does the step-entropy estimator remain stable as trace length grows?
- Is there a theoretical framework connecting quantization noise, entropy distortion, and optimal temperature scaling that could predict ReSET's effectiveness a priori for new model architectures?
