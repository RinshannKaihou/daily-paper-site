> **一句话总结：** 本文通过因果消融实验证明，Block Attention Residuals架构虽然显式暴露了跨层路由权重，但仅在路由参与训练时才能形成可解释的结构化通路，且路由质量不等于因果重要性——仅有描述性路由权重不足以做机制性解读。

> **TL;DR:** The paper probes two 0.6B Block AttnRes Qwen3 checkpoints—one a vanilla model wrapped with a deterministic recency-bias schedule, the other trained from scratch with routing as part of optimization—using mask-and-renormalize ablations over source families, sublayers, and depth regions. The wrapped baseline's routing weights are content-independent and match the schedule's analytic prediction exactly; the trained AttnRes model reveals three localized causal motifs (embedding→early-MLP, current→early-attention/MLP, nonlocal→late-attention). Crucially, average routing mass dissociates sharply from causal importance: the highest-mass slice is rarely the most causally consequential, and one source family carries appreciable mass with no detectable causal role. Architectural exposure of routing is necessary but not sufficient for mechanistic interpretation.

## 核心贡献

- **提出路由消融框架**：针对Block AttnRes，设计了按源家族、子层和深度区域三维联合分解的mask-and-renormalize消融方法，能够精确定位路由通路的因果贡献。
- **证明架构暴露≠可解释性**：通过对照实验（同样的探测方法、模型规模、分词器和数据集），展示了一个仅通过recency-bias schedule包装的vanilla模型的路由权重虽然存在但完全无信息量，匹配解析预测。
- **发现训练后路由的三种局部化模式**：embedding通过早期MLP注入、当前状态通过早期attention/MLP读取、远期历史通过晚期attention偶尔访问。
- **揭示路由质量与因果重要性严重脱钩**：最高质量占比的通路不一定是最大因果贡献者；一个源家族（prev completed）携带约9%路由质量却无任何可检测的因果作用。
- **提出核心主张**：描述性路由摘要应被视为候选假说，必须通过因果干预验证，而非直接作为机制证据。

## Key Contributions

- A **routing-ablation framework** for Block AttnRes that jointly factors mask-and-renormalize interventions by source family (embedding, current, prev completed, nonlocal), sublayer (attention vs. MLP), and depth region (early/middle/late).
- A **controlled demonstration** that explicit depth routing alone is insufficient for mechanistic interpretation: under matched probes, a recency-bias-wrapped vanilla checkpoint produces routing weights that exactly reproduce the wrapping schedule's analytic prediction (current mass = 0.840), while a trained AttnRes checkpoint exhibits three localized causal motifs.
- **Evidence of sharp dissociation** between average routing mass and causal importance: the largest mass slice is not the largest causal contributor in either sublayer, and one source family (prev completed) carries ≈9% mass with no detectable causal role.
- A **normative claim**: descriptive routing summaries should be treated as candidate hypotheses to be tested by causal interventions, not as evidence of mechanism in their own right.

## Methodology

The study compares two 0.6B-parameter Qwen3 checkpoints (d=1024, L=28, B=8 blocks) from the public Block AttnRes codebase under identical causal probes:

1. **Task**: Synthetic key–value retrieval with 200 examples, 2–4 KV pairs, 4-choice scoring by conditional log-probability. Three prompt templates (plain, sentence, table) are cycled to reduce surface-form sensitivity.

2. **Source families**: Block AttnRes groups earlier depth representations into four mutually exclusive families—embedding (first source), current (partial block), prev completed (most recent completed block), and nonlocal (older completed blocks).

3. **Routing ablations**: Given a target family, a binary mask zeroes out the family's routing weights and renormalizes the remainder, preserving total mass. Interventions can be restricted by sublayer (ATTN/MLP) and depth region (early/middle/late thirds).

4. **Two checkpoints**: (a) A vanilla Qwen3 loaded into the AttnRes class with zero query projections and β=3.0 recency bias, producing deterministic content-independent routing; (b) A Block AttnRes Qwen3 trained from scratch with routing as part of optimization, yielding content-dependent routing.

5. **Metrics**: Mean accuracy, mean correct-answer log-prob drop, flip rate (argmax changes), and mean rank of correct answer—all computed on the same 200-example dataset for both checkpoints.

## Results & Highlights

- **Baseline routing is fully predicted by the schedule**: Current-source mass averages 0.840 in both sublayers, exactly matching the analytic prediction e^β/(S−1+e^β) = 0.840. Attention and MLP rows are identical to four decimal places. Only ablating the current source has any causal effect (logprob drop 8.24).

- **AttnRes exhibits differentiated causality**: Embedding ablation causes the largest drop (7.25), exceeding current (4.17). Nonlocal contributes a measurable 1.80. Prev completed has near-zero effect (0.08) despite carrying ≈9% routing mass.

- **Sublayer asymmetry emerges only under trained routing**: The AttnRes model shows embedding matters almost entirely through MLP (6.38 vs. 0.13 for attention), current through attention (2.44) exceeding MLP (1.25), and nonlocal only through attention. The baseline shows no such stratification.

- **Depth-localized motifs**: Embedding effect is concentrated in early-MLP (6.38, near-zero elsewhere). Current concentrates early. Nonlocal appears only in late-attention (1.73).

- **Mass–causality dissociation**: In AttnRes attention, nonlocal carries 36.8% mass but only 1.89 logprob drop; embedding carries 19.5% mass but 0.13 logprob drop (via attention alone). In MLP, embedding has 22.8% mass yet 6.38 logprob drop—five times current's causal effect with less than half its mass.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: The paper makes a focused but important epistemological contribution. Rather than proposing a new architecture or method, it interrogates the interpretability claims of an existing one (Block AttnRes). The finding that architectural exposure ≠ interpretability is intuitive in retrospect but had not been empirically demonstrated with this level of rigor.

**Rigor**: The controlled experimental design is a major strength—identical probes, identical metrics, identical dataset, same-scale models. The analytic prediction match (0.840 to three decimal places) is compelling evidence that the recency-bias wrapping behaves as claimed. The three-axis factorization (source family × sublayer × depth) is systematic and avoids cherry-picking.

**Reproducibility**: High. Public codebase, public checkpoints, synthetic dataset with fixed seeds, clear experimental protocol.

**Significance**: The mass–causality dissociation is the most impactful finding. It directly challenges the common interpretability practice of reading mechanism off routing/attention weights descriptively. The normative claim—"routing summaries are hypotheses, not evidence"—has implications beyond Block AttnRes.

**Red flags and concerns**:
- **Task simplicity**: Synthetic key–value retrieval with 200 examples is a narrow probe. Whether these findings transfer to naturalistic language tasks or more complex reasoning is unknown.
- **Single scale (0.6B) and single recipe**: Only two checkpoints are compared. No intermediate conditions (e.g., AttnRes fine-tuned from vanilla, or AttnRes trained with frozen routing) are tested, so the attribution to "training from scratch" vs. "AttnRes training in general" is not strict.
- **Modest base accuracy**: Both models score in the mid-0.5 range on 4-choice retrieval. The low baseline competence may limit the generality of the causal structure discovered.
- **Renormalized interventions**: The ablations test reliance on routing pathways, not deletion of represented information. A pathway with no measurable effect may still carry information that other pathways substitute for—a caveat the authors acknowledge but which limits the strength of the "no causal role" claim for prev completed.
- **Block-level granularity**: The analysis operates on block summaries, not individual layers. Full per-layer AttnRes may reveal additional structure.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally clean controlled experimental design with analytic verification.
- The three-axis factorization (family × sublayer × depth) provides a principled decomposition of routing causality.
- The mass–causality dissociation finding is novel and has broad implications for interpretability research.
- Clear, honest limitations section that identifies exactly what the study does not cover.
- The paper is well-written with clear logical progression from motivation to experiment to implication.

## Limitations

- The synthetic retrieval task may not capture the routing dynamics present in natural language processing or complex reasoning.
- Only a single model scale and architecture variant is tested; scaling behavior is unknown.
- The ablation method (mask-and-renormalize) tests pathway reliance, not information content—null effects may reflect compensation rather than irrelevance.
- No investigation of training dynamics (at what training step structured routing emerges).
- The prev completed pathway's null result could be an artifact of the probe rather than genuine non-involvement.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the three-motif routing structure (embedding→early-MLP, current→early-attn, nonlocal→late-attn) replicate at larger scales (7B, 70B) or with different architectures?
2. At what point during training does the structured routing pattern diverge from the recency-bias-like initialization? Is there a phase transition?
3. Would stronger probes (e.g., targeted activation patching, indirect effect identification) reveal a causal role for the prev completed pathway, or is it genuinely vestigial?
4. Can regularizers that encourage routes with high causal importance (rather than high average mass) improve both interpretability and downstream performance?
5. Do intermediate conditions—AttnRes fine-tuned from a vanilla checkpoint, or AttnRes trained with routing frozen—produce routing patterns closer to the baseline or the trained model, and what does this reveal about the architecture-vs-training attribution?
