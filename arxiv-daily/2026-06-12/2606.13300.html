Quantizing Time-Series Models As Dynamical Systems

Forecasting model quantization sensitivity concentrates at I/O boundaries, not FFN layers — enabling calibration-free ≥32× compression.

Why This Matters

I/O-boundary sensitivity discovery upends LLM quantization assumptions for forecasting models — no calibration data needed for mixed-precision allocation.

Critical Edge

Largest model tested is 277M parameters. Billion-parameter scalability remains unproven, and per-layer rollout cost could become prohibitive.

Methodology

TQS reframes post-training quantization sensitivity as a finite-horizon dynamical-systems stability problem. For each layer, it rolls out both the nominal and perturbed models, measuring the growth rate of prediction-space divergence — a Lyapunov-inspired score γ.

γ_task(ℓ) = (1 / 2·T_max) · (1/|S|) · Σ ln( ‖∆Ŷ‖² / (‖δθ‖²_F + ε) )

The allocator uses MCKP optimization over TQS-ranked layers to assign bit-widths under a global compression budget. No gradients, Hessians, or calibration data — only forward passes.

  • Functionally inert layers auto-detected: 3 quantile heads in TimesFM (γ≈−69), 36 ln-modulation layers in Aurora (γ≈−5.3) — compressed at zero accuracy cost.
  • Gaussian vs. quantization probes nearly interchangeable: frontiers differ by at most 0.01% relative ERA5-MAE.
  • QEP baseline returns NaN on Pangu due to ONNX export incompatibility — TQS-PTQ's forward-only approach avoids this entirely.
Strengths
  • Elegant Lyapunov-theory motivation — natural fit for autoregressive rollout dynamics.
  • Fully calibration-free: applicable to black-box and compiled models (e.g., ONNX exports).
  • I/O-boundary finding consistent across three architecturally diverse models.
  • Single sensitivity sweep amortizes across all compression targets.
  • Dead-layer rule exploits functionally inert parameters automatically.
Limitations
  • Only medium-scale models tested (113M–277M); billion-parameter scalability unknown.
  • Per-layer rollout cost: L forward rollouts could be expensive for very deep models.
  • Pangu compression capped at 1.67× on-disk due to ONNX wrapper overhead.
  • No comparison against quantization-aware training (QAT) methods.
  • Activation quantization not investigated — only weight quantization considered.
Open Questions
  1. How does TQS scale to billion-parameter models (Aurora-large 1.3B, GenCast)? Can sub-sampling help?
  2. Can TQS sensitivity scores transfer across fine-tuning checkpoints, or must they be recomputed?
  3. How does TQS-PTQ compare against QAT at matched compression ratios?
  4. Could TQS extend to joint weight-and-activation quantization for time-series models?
  5. Does the I/O-boundary pattern generalize beyond weather — to video generation or RL world models?
