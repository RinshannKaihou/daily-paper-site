Accurate Latency-Critical NVFP4 Reasoning via Step-Aware Temperature Scaling

Step-aware temperature scaling fixes NVFP4 reasoning, recovering +2.6 accuracy points at 1.97× speedup over BF16.

Sihwa Lee · Janghwan Lee · Donghoon Yoo · Jae Gon Kim · Hanyul Ryu · Soojung Ryu · Jungwook Choi

quantization reasoning-models NVFP4 temperature-scaling inference-optimization
Key Metric
1.97×
End-to-end speedup over BF16 on NVIDIA B200 (Qwen3-32B, batch = 1)
Why This Matters

NVFP4's 4× throughput advantage collapses at latency-critical batch sizes. ReSET restores both accuracy and speed for production serving.

⚠ Critical Edge

Analysis is NVFP4-specific — unclear whether step-entropy dynamics transfer to INT4, GPTQ, or other quantization formats.

Methodology

1

Diagnose

Token-level entropy is dominated by step-level uncertainty, not token identity. Fixed entropy thresholds misclassify symbolic tokens in uncertain steps.

2

Estimate

Hybrid online step-entropy estimator: sliding-window init (first 32 tokens) transitions to within-step running average.

3

Adapt

Step-aware threshold sharpens confident tokens (Tlow) and relaxes uncertain ones (Thigh = 1.0). Training-free, ~1.5% per-token overhead.

4

Accelerate

First public CUDA-core NVFP4 GEMV kernel for M ≤ 8 decode. Multi-token CTA fusion, register-only dequantization.

Evaluated on 5 reasoning models (R1-Qwen-7B/14B, Qwen3-8B/14B/32B) × 3 benchmarks (AIME-120, GPQA-Diamond, LiveCodeBench) × 8 seeds. ReSET achieves best NVFP4 PTQ accuracy on every benchmark versus RTN, BRQ, 4/6, MR-GPTQ baselines. All measurements on real NVIDIA B200 hardware with NVIDIA ModelOpt quantization. Open-source: github.com/aiha-lab/ReSET.

Strengths

  • Identifies a previously unrecognized problem — step-level entropy mismatch in quantized reasoning
  • Zero training cost — pure decoding-time intervention with 5-problem calibration
  • Dual algorithmic + systems contribution working synergistically
  • Qwen3-32B ReSET+NVFP4 exceeds BF16 accuracy — suggests broader applicability
  • Production-oriented: real B200 hardware, TPOT SLO constraints

Limitations

  • Per-model Tlow calibration required — minimal (5 problems) but an extra step
  • Only validated on NVIDIA B200 — Tensor-Core tile problem is hardware-specific
  • Autoregressive decode only; prefill and speculative decoding not addressed
  • HSE relies on entropy trajectory, may miss models with unusual formatting
  • No perplexity evaluation on standard language modeling benchmarks

Open Questions

  1. Can ReSET combine with speculative decoding, or does temperature perturbation break draft model agreement?
  2. Do step-entropy dynamics transfer to INT4, FP8, GPTQ quantization? A systematic cross-format study is needed.
  3. Could ReSET dynamically adjust temperature based on real-time accuracy feedback (e.g., reward model scores)?
  4. Does ReSET+NVFP4 exceeding BF16 suggest quantization acts as an implicit regularizer exploitable for full-precision models?
  5. Does the step-entropy estimator remain stable with reasoning traces exceeding 32K tokens?
  6. Is there a theoretical framework connecting quantization noise, entropy distortion, and optimal temperature scaling?
