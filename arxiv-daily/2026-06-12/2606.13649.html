Operadic consistency: a label-free signal for compositional reasoning failures in LLMs

Nathaniel Bottman · Yinhong Liu · Kyle Richardson 2026-06-12 LLM Reasoning Compositional Selective Prediction Operad Theory
Key Metric
r = 0.86–0.94
Pearson correlation with accuracy
across 12 models · 4 datasets
Why This Matters
A label-free, 3-call, model-agnostic signal that reliably detects when LLMs fail at compositional reasoning — no ground truth or training data required.
Critical Edge
All empirical results use depth-2 chains only. The framework supports arbitrary decomposition trees, but evidence is limited to the simplest case.

Methodology

  1. Questions operad Q: Question templates with k blanks, composed by substitution. Associativity makes decomposition structure an algebraic object.
  2. Operadic consistency check: Answer the composite question directly (Adirect), then answer via decomposition step-by-step (Adecomposed). The model is operadically consistent if both agree.
  3. Depth-2 instantiation: Q decomposed into (Q₁, Q₂). Answer Q directly → Adir; answer Q₁ → substitute into Q₂ → Adec. OC = scorer(Adir, Adec) ∈ [0, 1]. Cost: 3 calls.
  4. Evaluated on 12 instruction-tuned models (4B–671B params) across HotpotQA, MuSiQue, StrategyQA, DROP, plus 5 frontier thinking models with CoT-extracted decompositions.
  5. Baselines: CoT self-consistency (K=3, K=10), semantic entropy (K=10), P(True), decomposed self-consistency, Skywork-PRM. Leave-one-model-out protocol for selective prediction.

Results & Highlights

  • Cross-model accuracy estimation: Leave-one-model-out fit predicts held-out accuracy within 3.0 pp MAE (pooled) vs. 4.6 pp for CoT-SC at K=10 — despite OC using only 3 calls.
  • CoT-SC bimodal failure: CoT-SC matches OC on HotpotQA (r=+0.93) and DROP (r=+0.87) but collapses on MuSiQue (r=+0.43) and StrategyQA (r=+0.46). OC is uniformly strong (r=+0.86 to +0.94, all p ≤ 0.0004).
  • Irreducible complementarity: OC's coefficient remains highly significant (p ≤ 10⁻¹⁶) in regression controlling for CoT-SC, semantic entropy, and P(True) — captures information orthogonal to sample-diversity signals.
  • Equal-cost selective prediction: ∆AUARC +0.086–+0.096 and ∆AUROC +0.092–+0.164 over CoT-SC at K=3, all bootstrap 95% CIs excluding zero.
  • Thinking models: Jointly-consistent questions answered correctly 43–62 pp higher than jointly-inconsistent ones. Positive lift on all 16 tested cells.

Strengths

  • Elegant theoretical motivation — operad framework directly suggests the diagnostic signal, not decorative math.
  • Extremely thorough evaluation: 17 models, 5 datasets, multiple baseline families.
  • Practical: 3 model calls, no ground-truth labels, no training data.
  • Genuinely adds information beyond all tested baselines — not just matching existing signals.
  • Leave-one-model-out protocol rigorously simulates deployment to unseen models.

Limitations

  • Only evaluated on multi-hop QA — unclear performance on open-ended generation, code, or creative tasks.
  • Requires a decomposition; inapplicable without annotations or external decomposition model.
  • Depth-2 chains only — whether deeper trees yield stronger signals remains untested.
  • Thinking-model CoT extraction is lightweight; may miss implicit sub-questions or introduce noise.
  • Largest commercial systems (GPT-5, Claude Opus 4.x, Gemini 2.5 Pro) not included.

Open Questions

  1. How does OC perform beyond multi-hop QA — e.g., theorem proving, code generation, scientific reasoning?
  2. Can automated decomposition match annotator quality, and how does error propagate?
  3. Does increasing depth beyond 2 monotonically strengthen the signal, or does noise eventually dominate?
  4. How does OC interact with retrieval-augmented generation — does retrieved context shift the consistency landscape?
  5. Could OC serve as a training signal (e.g., preference optimization) rather than purely inference-time?
