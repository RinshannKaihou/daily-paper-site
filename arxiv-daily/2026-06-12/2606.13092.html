Scale Buys Interpolation, Structure Buys a Horizon: Certified Predictability for Equivariant World Models

Hongbo Wang

equivariant world models Lyapunov spectrum certified predictability chaos theory model-based RL

本文为等变潜空间世界模型提出了基于Lyapunov 谱的可计算、多步可预测视界证书，证明 T 步 rollout 误差沿对称轨道恒定，且该性质为等变模型所独有；匹配的下界证明近似等变性的视界受限为 Θ(log(1/ε)/λ)，而 Noether 铰链将守恒量认证为无限视界通道。

Key Finding

Equivariant structure — not scale — is the only path to certified multi-step predictability. No non-equivariant model possesses this certificate at any parameter count.

Anchor Metric
0.98
R² Lyapunov spectrum recovery, 40-D Lorenz-96 (ZN-equiv.) — dense MLP: R² < 0
Why This Matters

The first computable, provably tight predictability certificate for world models — proving scaling alone won't make predictions trustworthy.

Critical Edge

Requires genuine dynamical symmetry. Deterministic latent dynamics only. V-JEPA 2-AC (1B) raw certificate over-promises. Single author — community verification essential.

Theoretical Backbone

  • Theorem A — Orbit-constant error: under equivariance (A1–A4), T-step rollout error is identical across every configuration in the symmetry orbit. k generator checks certify an exponentially large generated monoid ⟨S⟩.
  • Lemma 2 — Converse / impossibility: orbit-constant error characterizes equivariance. No non-equivariant model has this certificate at any scale — a clean architectural impossibility theorem.
  • Proposition 6 — Matching lower bound: constructs a single encoder defect whose error grows exactly as εeλT, proving the horizon form Θ(log(1/ε)/λ) is tight.
  • Noether Hinge (Prop. 4–5) — Conserved charges are forced into specific isotypic blocks; their error grows linearly (Tη) not exponentially, yielding unbounded horizons at η = 0.
  • Theorem B⁺ — Cone / adapted-metric certificate: computable, sound a priori, tight on uniformly hyperbolic dynamics, self-abstaining on non-uniformly hyperbolic systems.
  • Propositions 7–8 — Scope and continuity: degenerate vs. non-degenerate dichotomy for when the certificate is meaningful; finite-horizon Lipschitz transfer of exponents from learned models.

Empirical Evidence

  • R² = 0.98–0.99 Lyapunov spectrum recovery on 40-D Lorenz-96 with ZN-equivariant network (3 seeds). Dense MLP: R² < 0, λ₁ inflated ~3.4×. Phase transition at N = 40.
  • Prop. 6 validated — ε = 10⁻³ model's orbit-error matches analytic εeλT to relative error 10⁻¹⁴. Certified horizon linear in log(1/ε) with slope exactly 1/λ (R² = 1.000).
  • Scope law confirmed — Staircases linear in log(1/ε) with R² = 0.975–0.995 across Lorenz, Hénon, Rössler. PushT: degenerate branch as predicted (R² = 0.02).
  • 8–16% vs. 61–65% aggregate violation at sensing-budget knee (equivariant vs. dense). Measured catch-up 2.7–3.5×.
  • TD-MPC2 audit — 15 loops across 5 tasks × 3 seeds. Calibrated where strongly expansive (walker: 0.94–1.02), optimistic where weakly expansive (cheetah: 0.43–0.50), correctly abstaining where contracting.
  • Scale ≠ calibration — TD-MPC2 1M → 317M multitask: regime flips non-monotonically, calibration scatters 0.37–1.87. No size matches single-task 0.94–1.02.
  • V-JEPA 2-AC (1B) — Raw certificate reads expansive (λ₁ = 0.178); cross-validated audit correctly overrides (log-slope 0.03 ≪ λ₁ on 20 real DROID episodes).

Methodology at a Glance

Theoretical Setup

Latent world model = encoder E + predictor f, with group G acting on states, latents, and actions. Five assumptions (A1–A5) formalize equivariance, dynamical symmetry, and orthogonality. The framework derives per-channel certified horizons Tj(ε) ∼ log(1/ε)/λj, with a two-sided (upper + matching lower) bound proving tightness.

Experimental Scope

16 experiments (E1–E16): Lorenz, Hénon, Rössler, PushT, 40-D Lorenz-96; active re-observation scheduling; training-free auditing of TD-MPC2 (5 tasks × 3 seeds), LeWM, and V-JEPA 2-AC (1B, DROID real robot data). All seeded, CPU/1-GPU, with honest gating (INCONCLUSIVE reported rather than threshold-loosening).

The Verdict

Strengths

  • Rare certified (not just empirical) guarantees for world model predictability
  • Matching lower bound (Prop. 6) proves the horizon form is tight — the paper's crown jewel
  • Lemma 2 impossibility result: no non-equivariant model has the certificate at any scale
  • Exceptional honesty — explicitly flags vacuous (PushT), INCONCLUSIVE (Acrobot), and over-promising (V-JEPA 2-AC) cases
  • Training-free auditing of public pretrained models is immediately deployable
  • "Scale buys interpolation, not a calibrated horizon" — a powerful reframing of the scaling debate

Limitations

  • Exact certificate requires genuine dynamical symmetry (A3) — real robotics rarely has exact symmetries
  • Deterministic latent dynamics only — does not address stochastic world models (Dreamer-style)
  • All experiments at 1–2 GPU scale; computational cost at frontier-model scale untested
  • Noether hinge validated only on constructed teachers, not emergent conservation in learned models
  • Prefactor heavy tails (cj = 1/sin θj, max ~10³) mean worst-case guarantees are much looser than typical case
  • Downstream task value is indirect — concentrated in sensing/monitoring, diluted behind task-level metrics

Relevance (相关性)

该论文将等变表示论、遍历理论（Lyapunov谱）与世界模型可信预测相结合，提出了首个可证明的多步可预测视界证书。对于关注等变网络理论、世界模型安全部署、模型可信度评估的研究具有直接价值。其"尺度换内插，不换校准视界"的核心论点对当前大模型 scaling 叙事提供了有意义的理论挑战和实验反驳。

Open Questions

  1. Can the certificate extend to stochastic latent world models (Dreamer-style RSSM)?
  2. How does Lyapunov spectrum estimation scale with latent dimensionality in the thousands — is there a practical ceiling?
  3. Can the Noether hinge's conservation → unbounded-horizon connection emerge automatically in learned models without explicit equivariance?
  4. Could the scope taxonomy (calibrated / optimistic / abstaining) serve as a training signal to improve world model calibration, not just post-hoc diagnosis?
  5. The certificate requires a known symmetry group G — prospects for learning the group jointly while preserving guarantees?
  6. Could Proposition 11's value-transfer bound inform the design of planning objectives better aligned with certified quantities?
