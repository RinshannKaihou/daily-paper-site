Optimal transport on cross-attention detects NMT hallucinations at AUROC 0.957 — but is blind to content misuse in summarization.

Layer-Resolved Optimal Transport for Hallucination Detection in NMT and Abstractive Summarization Onyshchuk, Tarnavskyi & Sumyk 2026-06-12 Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop
hallucination-detection optimal-transport cross-attention interpretability NMT
Key Metric
0.957
AUROC — RC detector, full-unsupport hallucinations (Fairseq DE-EN)
Why This Matters

Reveals Transformer decoder functional structure via OT geometry — with principled limits on where attention-based detection fails.

Critical Edge

Summarization faithfulness detection barely above chance (57%) — OT detects source disengagement, not content misuse.

Methodology

  • Wass-to-Unif (WTU): W¹ distance between attention and uniform distribution — low concentration flags hallucination
  • Wass-to-Data (WTD): W¹ to k=4 nearest-neighbour correct translations
  • Routing Consistency (RC): measures argmax source-position repetition across steps via routing entropy
  • Step-to-step OT: W¹ between consecutive generation steps' attention
  • Layer-pairwise OT: L×L distance matrix revealing functional grouping
  • All W¹ computed exactly via CDF formula — no Sinkhorn approximation

Key Structural Insight — Two-Phase Generation Dynamics

Correct translations show an exploratory phase (first ~25% of sequence: high step-OT, low concentration) followed by a commitment phase. Hallucinated translations skip exploration entirely, beginning in a statically concentrated state from step 1. Detection signal is concentrated in layers L1–L4; L5 is anti-predictive for subtler hallucination types. In T5-base, Layer 3 is the concentration peak and structural pivot — cumulative ablation of L1–L3 causes catastrophic collapse (ΔR-L = −4.47).

Strengths

  • Exceptionally honest — principled account of why OT fails for summarization is itself a contribution
  • Layer-resolved analysis gives actionable, fine-grained insights over aggregate claims
  • Multiple converging methods (OT metrics, ablation, LoRA analysis) strengthen structural claims
  • RC is a clever detector capturing mechanistically distinct signal from concentration
  • Two-phase dynamics finding has practical implications for early-step online detection
  • Clean math — all W¹ exact, no approximations

Limitations

  • Single NMT language pair (DE-EN) and model (Fairseq, 6 layers)
  • Summarization detection barely above chance — limited practical utility
  • T5-base structural experiments use small samples (N=50–100)
  • No comparison against other unsupervised detection methods
  • No ensemble of WTU + WTD + RC reported — each evaluated separately
  • Cross-dataset generalization fails completely (CNN→XSum = 44.2%)
  • Requires cross-attention weights — inapplicable to black-box APIs

Open Questions

  1. Do layer-resolved OT findings transfer to decoder-only LLMs (LLaMA, GPT) where cross-attention is replaced by self-attention?
  2. Could combining OT metrics with semantic similarity or NLI detectors close the summarization gap?
  3. Is the "exploration → commitment" dynamic universal across Transformer architectures?
  4. What is the computational overhead for real-time OT hallucination monitoring at inference?
  5. Does the functional regime structure (transitional → bridge → cluster → isolated) scale with layer count?
  6. Can RC's sensitivity to "same-position routing" be exploited for beam-search pruning or guided decoding?
