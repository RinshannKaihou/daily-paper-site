> **一句话总结：** 本文提出TQS（Trajectory-based Quantization Sensitivity Score），受Lyapunov稳定性理论启发，将后训练量化重新定义为动力系统的有限时间稳定性问题，通过衡量量化误差在自回归rollout过程中的传播与放大来对层进行灵敏度排序，并基于此构建了无需校准数据的混合精度量化框架TQS-PTQ，在Aurora、TimesFM和Pangu-Weather三个预测基础模型上取得了最优的精度-压缩帕累托前沿。

> **TL;DR:** Introduces TQS, a Lyapunov-inspired metric that measures how quantization-induced errors propagate through time-series forecasting models by treating autoregressive rollout as a discrete-time dynamical system. Building on TQS, the authors propose TQS-PTQ, a calibration-free mixed-precision PTQ framework that (i) identifies functionally inert layers for free compression, (ii) discovers that quantization sensitivity concentrates at I/O boundary modules rather than FFN layers (contrary to LLM quantization assumptions), and (iii) achieves the best accuracy-compression Pareto frontier on Aurora-small (≥32× compression at ≤1% ERA5-MAE degradation), TimesFM-2.5, and Pangu-Weather, outperforming GPTQ, GPTAQ, and QEP baselines.

## 核心贡献

- **提出TQS（Trajectory-based Quantization Sensitivity Score）**：受Lyapunov指数启发，将网络的自动回归 rollout 建模为离散时间动力系统，通过量化每一层参数扰动在 rollout 过程中的传播放大率来定义灵敏度分数 γ，实现灵敏度估计与量化器选择和比特宽度分配的完全解耦。
- **发现预测模型的量化灵敏度集中在I/O边界模块**：跨三个架构（TimesFM、Aurora、Pangu-Weather）一致发现，量化灵敏度集中在输入/输出投影模块，而非LLM量化文献中常见的FFN层，揭示了预测基础模型与LLM在量化特性上的根本差异。
- **提出TQS-PTQ混合精度框架**：基于TQS排序，使用多选背包问题（MCKP）求解器在全局压缩预算下分配比特宽度，无需校准数据或二阶近似，适用于黑盒或编译后模型。
- **单次灵敏度扫描即可复用于多压缩目标**：TQS-PTQ仅需一次灵敏度排序即可在多个压缩比之间生成帕累托前沿，比逐bit-width校准的GPTQ/GPTAQ更高效。
- **识别"免费压缩"层**：TQS能自动识别功能惰性层（如TimesFM的输出分位数头、Aurora的ln调制层），将其直接分配到最低精度而不损失任何精度。

## Key Contributions

- **TQS metric (Lyapunov-inspired)**: Reframes PTQ sensitivity as a finite-horizon dynamical-systems stability problem. For each layer ℓ, TQS rolls out both the nominal and perturbed models over T_max steps and computes the growth rate of prediction-space divergence: γ(ℓ) = (1/T_max) · ln(‖∆Ŷ‖² / (‖δθ‖²_F + ε)). Layers with high γ amplify quantization error and are protected; layers with low γ can be compressed aggressively.
- **Decoupled sensitivity estimation**: Unlike Hessian-based (HAWQ) or post-hoc methods, TQS uses only forward passes — no gradients, Hessians, or calibration data required. This makes it applicable to compiled/black-box models (e.g., Pangu-Weather's frozen ONNX export).
- **Gaussian vs. quantization noise analysis**: Introduces both γ_quant (actual quantization residual) and γ_gauss (isotropic Gaussian noise scaled to match Frobenius norm). Shows high Spearman correlation at block granularity (ρ=0.96 on Pangu), confirming that amplification magnitude dominates over noise structure.
- **Cross-architecture sensitivity finding**: Quantization sensitivity concentrates at I/O boundary modules across all three architectures — input tokenizers and output heads — diverging from the FFN-centric pattern known in LLM PTQ. This establishes a new principle for quantizing forecasting foundation models.
- **TQS-PTQ mixed-precision allocator**: Uses MCKP (or greedy variant) to assign precision tiers under a global compression budget. Dead-layer rule automatically assigns functionally inert layers to the lowest tier. Single sensitivity sweep amortizes across all compression targets.
- **Superior Pareto frontier**: At ≤1% ERA5-MAE degradation, TQS-PTQ reaches ≥32× compression on Aurora-small and ~4× on TimesFM-2.5. Wins 64 of 75 variable-model comparisons against the strongest uniform-precision baselines.

## Methodology

**TQS Score Definition**: Given a full-precision model defining a discrete-time map x_{t+1} = F_θ(x_t), and a quantization-perturbed map x̃_{t+1} = F_θ̃(x̃_t), TQS measures the finite-time growth rate of output prediction-space divergence for each layer ℓ:

γ_task(ℓ) = (1 / (2·T_max)) · (1/|S|) · Σ_{s∈S} ln( ‖∆Ŷ_s^(ℓ)‖² / (‖δθ_ℓ‖²_F + ε) )

where ∆Ŷ_s^(ℓ) is the trajectory divergence over the full rollout for context window s, and δθ_ℓ is the parameter-space perturbation. The score captures where perturbation ends up after T_max steps of the model's own dynamics.

**Probe variants**: Two perturbation types are studied — γ_quant uses actual quantization residual (6-bit on TimesFM/Aurora, 8-bit on Pangu), and γ_gauss uses isotropic Gaussian noise scaled to match the Frobenius norm. The Gaussian variant fully decouples sensitivity from quantizer choice.

**TQS-PTQ Allocator (Algorithm 1/2)**: Given per-layer TQS scores, parameter counts, a tier set, and a target compression C:
1. Assign dead layers (γ ≤ sentinel threshold) to the lowest tier.
2. Sort remaining layers by decreasing γ.
3. Assign top-ranked layers to FP32 until the FP32 budget is met.
4. Assign tiers to remaining layers via MCKP optimization (or greedy promotion from bottom tier).
5. The objective minimizes γ-weighted quantization error subject to a global storage budget.

**Models evaluated**:
- **Aurora-small** (113M parameters): 30-day, 120-step autoregressive rollout at 6-hour steps, evaluated against ERA5 on 9 surface/upper-air variables.
- **TimesFM-2.5** (200M parameters): 500-step autoregressive rollout with C=512 context, evaluated on ETTh1/2, ETTm1/2, EXCHANGE, WEATHER benchmarks.
- **Pangu-Weather 6h** (277M parameters, frozen ONNX export): Single-step rollout, evaluated against ERA5 on 9 variables.

**Baselines**: Uniform-precision RTN, GPTQ, GPTAQ, and QEP at matched compression ratios.

**Ablation axes**: Probe distribution (Gaussian vs. quantization), allocator (greedy vs. MCKP), bottom tier (INT1/INT2/INT4), and FP32 budget fraction.

## Results & Highlights

- **Heavy compression frontier**: TQS-PTQ extends the accuracy-compression frontier across all three models. At ≤1% ERA5-MAE degradation: ≥32× compression on Aurora-small, ~4× on TimesFM-2.5. On Pangu-Weather, TQS-PTQ achieves on-disk C=1.67× with only 1.2% mean per-variable MAE degradation while avoiding the uniform-W4 collapse.
- **64 of 75 variable-model wins**: TQS-PTQ wins all 9 Aurora variables at matched W2 grid, 46 of 57 TimesFM variables at C=16, and all 9 Pangu variables against the strongest uniform-W8 baseline.
- **Free compression discovered**: 3 output-quantile heads in TimesFM (γ≈-69) and 36 ln-modulation layers in Aurora (γ≈-5.3) are functionally inert — assigned to lowest precision at zero accuracy cost (3.4% and 42% of parameters respectively).
- **I/O-boundary sensitivity**: Input tokenizers and output heads occupy the 93rd–98th γ-rank percentiles across all models, while backbone self-attention/transformer body ranks at the 21st–26th percentiles. This directly contradicts LLM quantization wisdom where FFN down-projections are the bottleneck.
- **Moderately heavy-tailed sensitivity**: Top 10% of layers carry 17–24% of total γ-shift (top 25% carry ~40%). This is far less concentrated than LLMs (<1% outlier regime) but enough to benefit from mixed precision.
- **Faster than calibration-based methods**: Single sensitivity sweep amortizes across compression targets. Per Pareto point: TimesFM 46min (4.6–5.1× faster than GPTQ/GPTAQ), Aurora 32min (1.2–1.7× faster), Pangu 57.5min vs. GPTAQ's 66.9min.
- **Gaussian vs. quantization probes nearly interchangeable**: Despite rank-level disagreement at fine granularity, the resulting mixed-precision frontiers differ by at most 0.01% relative ERA5-MAE across 16 compression targets on Aurora.
- **QEP unstable on Pangu**: Returns NaN MAE under onnx2torch export, highlighting the practical advantage of TQS-PTQ's forward-only approach.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: High. The dynamical-systems framing of PTQ sensitivity is genuinely original. While Lyapunov exponents have been studied for RNNs (Vogt et al., 2022; Storm et al., 2024), applying them specifically to post-training quantization of foundation forecasting models is a new and well-motivated contribution. The discovery that I/O-boundary sensitivity dominates (contrary to LLM conventions) is a meaningful empirical finding.

**Rigor**: Strong. The mathematical formulation is clean and well-grounded. The paper provides thorough ablations (probe type, allocator, bottom tier, FP32 budget), per-variable results, and confidence intervals (bootstrap resampling). The MCKP allocator is formally specified and compared against a greedy variant. The appendix is comprehensive (~15 pages).

**Reproducibility**: Moderate-to-good. The TQS score definition is precise and implementable from the paper. However, no code release is mentioned. The models (Aurora, TimesFM, Pangu-Weather) are publicly available, but the ONNX export pipeline for Pangu requires specific tooling (onnx2torch) that introduced compatibility issues (QEP NaN failure).

**Significance**: High for the forecasting model compression community, moderate for the broader quantization field. The finding that LLM-derived PTQ assumptions do not transfer to forecasting transformers is practically important for the growing weather/climate foundation model ecosystem. The calibration-free nature of TQS-PTQ is a genuine practical advantage.

**Potential concerns**:
- **Scale limitations**: The largest model tested is Pangu-Weather at 277M parameters. It remains unclear whether TQS scales to billion-parameter models (e.g., Aurora-large, GenCast) where the computational cost of per-layer rollout sensitivity analysis could become prohibitive.
- **Pangu compression gap**: On Pangu, TQS-PTQ achieves only on-disk C=1.67× (vs. block-level 3.57×) due to ONNX wrapper overhead — a practical deployment concern acknowledged but not fully resolved.
- **Probe horizon sensitivity**: Different models use different T_max values (4 steps for Pangu vs. 100–120 for TimesFM/Aurora). The paper does not thoroughly analyze how T_max choice affects rankings, though early-stopping convergence is mentioned.
- **Workshop paper**: The main text is relatively short (6 pages), with heavy reliance on appendices for key details. Some important analyses (e.g., per-variable breakdowns, full Pareto frontiers) are appendix-only.

## Strengths

- Elegant theoretical motivation: the connection between Lyapunov stability theory and quantization error propagation is natural for autoregressive time-series models and yields a principled metric.
- Fully calibration-free: TQS requires only forward passes, making it applicable to black-box/compiled models — a genuine practical advantage over GPTQ/GPTAQ.
- Cross-architecture consistency: the I/O-boundary sensitivity finding holds across three very different architectures (decoder-only, encoder-decoder, 3D Swin transformer).
- Single-sweep amortization: one sensitivity ranking supports all compression targets, producing denser Pareto frontiers at lower per-point cost.
- Identifies free compression: the dead-layer rule automatically exploits functionally inert parameters.
- Thorough ablation study covering probe type, allocator, tier configuration, and FP32 budget.

## Limitations

- Evaluated only on medium-scale models (113M–277M); scalability to billion-parameter forecasting models is uncertain.
- Computational cost of TQS sensitivity sweep: requires L forward rollouts per model (one per layer), which could be expensive for very deep models.
- Pangu-Weather results limited by ONNX export constraints (only FP32/BF16/INT8 tiers viable, capping compression at ~1.67× on-disk).
- The paper does not compare against quantization-aware training (QAT) methods, which may achieve better compression at the cost of retraining.
- TimesFM degradation is measured relative to the FP32 model (self-consistency), not against ground truth — a weaker evaluation criterion than the ERA5-based evaluation used for Aurora/Pangu.
- No investigation of activation quantization; only weight quantization is considered.

## Relevance

本文从动力系统稳定性理论出发，提出了一种全新的量化灵敏度分析方法，对关注时间序列基础模型（如天气预报模型）高效部署的研究者具有直接参考价值。特别是I/O边界模块灵敏度集中的发现，对设计面向预测任务的混合精度量化策略有重要指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does TQS scale to billion-parameter forecasting models (e.g., Aurora-large at 1.3B, GenCast)? Is the per-layer rollout cost prohibitive, or can TQS be estimated with sub-sampling?
- Can TQS sensitivity scores be reused across model fine-tuning checkpoints, or do they need to be recomputed after each update?
- How does TQS-PTQ compare against quantization-aware training (QAT) on the same models at matched compression?
- Could TQS be extended to joint weight-and-activation quantization for time-series models, where activation error propagation may differ from weight perturbation?
- Is the I/O-boundary sensitivity pattern specific to weather/time-series foundation models, or does it generalize to other autoregressive architectures (e.g., video generation models, reinforcement learning world models)?
