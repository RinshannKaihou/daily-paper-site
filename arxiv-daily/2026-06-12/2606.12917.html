One attention head in TabPFN drives 2–5× more causal computation than all others — and its dominant layer shifts with task complexity.

Where Computation Lives Inside TabPFN: Causal Localisation of Attention Head Function Atharva Gupta, Dhruv Kumar, Murari Mandal, Saurabh Deshpande 2026-06-12 mechanistic-interpretability tabular-foundation-models activation-patching
Key Metric
2–5×
Head 2's ablation effect (0.074–0.076σ) vs. all other head-layer combinations — the single largest causal driver across two tasks.
Why This Matters
First mechanistic look inside a tabular foundation model — reveals functional head specialization and shows LLM interpretability methods don't always transfer.
⚠ Critical Edge
Tested on only two synthetic datasets with known closed-form solutions — no real-world tabular benchmarks, no classification tasks, no noisy data.
Methodology

The authors dissect TabPFN-2.5's feature-wise self-attention module (18 layers · 3 heads · d=192) on two synthetic regression tasks:

  • Multiplication (d=3): y = a·b + c
  • Pairwise-50 (d=50): y = (Σxi)² — requires global feature aggregation

Three convergent causal methods are applied:

  • Activation patching — swap corrupted→clean activations, measure recovery
  • Ablation — zero a component, measure prediction deviation in σ units
  • Attention entropy — Shannon entropy of per-head attention; low = concentrated computation

An additional contrastive activation steering experiment tests whether a task-representing direction (mean-diff) can shift outputs at inference — the LLM-style approach.

Results & Findings
Finding Multiplication (d=3) Pairwise-50 (d=50)
Head 2 peak ablation 0.076σ at L0 0.074σ at L16
Head 2 peak patching layer L6 L13
Entropy minima 0.21 at L6 0.31 at L13
Layer-level patch recovery ~100% —
Activation steering MSE Δ +0.0% (direction stable at cosine sim 0.71, but impotent)

Selectivity ≠ causal necessity: Head 0 is equally selective at L0 (entropy 0.22) yet has near-zero ablation effect — concentrated attention alone doesn't mean causal importance.

Stability: Head 2's L0 effect is 0.078σ at n=64 vs. 0.076σ at n=512 — not a batch artifact.

Strengths
  • Pioneering causal MI for tabular foundation models — a genuinely understudied area.
  • Three convergent methods (patching + ablation + entropy) independently identify the same computational sites.
  • Elegant disentangling of attentional selectivity from causal necessity (Head 0 vs. Head 2 at L0).
  • The negative steering result yields a real architectural insight: pure ICL ≠ function-vector-mediated ICL.
  • Commendable transparency — all tentative claims and scope limits are explicitly flagged.
Limitations
  • Only two synthetic datasets with known closed-form solutions. Zero real-world benchmarks.
  • Whether the two head classes generalize beyond these tasks is entirely unknown.
  • Identifies where computation happens, not what computation is performed.
  • Steering null result tested with a single estimator on a single task — may not hold with better methods.
  • No comparison with probing or other MI approaches to validate patching-based findings.
  • TabPFN's non-commercial license may limit some reproducibility scenarios.
Questions for Further Exploration
  1. Do the two head classes (dominant + symmetric) persist across sinusoidal, polynomial, or real-world tabular benchmarks?
  2. What specific feature-block pairs does Head 2 attend to at its entropy minima (L6, L13)? Attention weight visualization could reveal the actual computation.
  3. Can linear probes, PCA, or sparse autoencoder features overcome the activation steering failure — or is this a fundamental ICL limitation?
  4. How do the head classes differ between the 18-layer regression and 24-layer classification variants of TabPFN-2.5?
  5. Is Head 2's dominant layer position related to the circuit depth required by the structural causal model in TabPFN's training data?
