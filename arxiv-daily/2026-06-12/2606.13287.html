Clipping Makes Distributed and Federated Asynchronous SGD Robust to Stragglers

一句话总结：梯度裁剪消除异步SGD收敛对最大延迟τmax的依赖，无需延迟信息即可在异质（联邦）场景下实现与最大延迟无关的收敛速率——首次给出高概率收敛保证。

Samuel Erickson & Mikael Johansson · KTH Royal Institute of Technology

arXiv Code
Key Finding
Gradient clipping eliminates convergence dependence on maximum delay τmax in async SGD — a simple operation replaces complex delay-adaptive schemes.
Key Metric
1.2–2.2× wall-clock speedup on CIFAR-10 & Shakespeare. Sub-Weibull θ ≈ 2.71 confirms heavy-tailed noise.
Why This Matters
A trivially simple intervention (clip gradients) works in federated settings where delay-adaptive methods fail to converge at all.
Critical Edge
Only simulated asynchrony with 16 workers. No real distributed system, no LLM/ViT-scale validation. Clipping radius requires manual tuning.

Methodology

Standard ASGD augmented with one modification: each worker clips its stochastic gradient to norm ≤ c before sending it to the server. Two algorithms handle homogeneous (any-idle-worker selection) and heterogeneous (uniform random selection to preserve unbiasedness) settings.

Key theoretical tools

  • Perturbed iterate analysis — virtual sequence {x̃t} evolves like serial clipped SGD; gap ‖x̃t − xt‖ ≤ ηcτC is controlled by clipping radius, independent of τmax.
  • Sub-Weibull noise model (Assumption 3.2) — E[exp((|X|/σ)θ)] ≤ 2, generalizing sub-Gaussian (θ=½) and sub-exponential (θ=1). Empirically θ ≈ 2.71 for ResNet-18 on CIFAR-10.
  • Freedman's inequality for high-probability bounds on martingale difference terms.

High-probability versions add polylog(1/δ) terms — first such guarantee in async optimization.
Homogeneous rate matches delay-adaptive ASGD up to the middle στC/ε³ term — without requiring any delay information.

Results

  • CIFAR-10 (ResNet-18, homogeneous): 80% test accuracy reached 1.8× faster than Vanilla ASGD, 1.5× faster than Delay-adaptive & Ringleader ASGD (D∈{4,8}).
  • Shakespeare (LSTM, homogeneous): Perplexity 5.0 reached up to 2.2× faster than Delay-adaptive ASGD.
  • Label-skew CIFAR-10 (CNN, heterogeneous): 1.2–1.3× faster than Vanilla ASGD.
  • Step-size robustness: Clipped ASGD is stable across a wide range of step sizes; Vanilla ASGD requires careful tuning when delays increase.

Assessment

Strengths

  • Elegant insight — clipping controls perturbed iterate gap — "obvious in hindsight."
  • First high-probability convergence guarantees for async optimization.
  • Sub-Weibull noise model is more realistic than bounded-variance or sub-Gaussian.
  • Works in both homogeneous and heterogeneous (federated) settings.
  • Requires no delay information — a practical advantage over delay-adaptive methods.

Limitations

  • Simulated asynchrony only (16 workers), not real distributed systems.
  • No experiments on LLMs, ViTs, or real FL deployments.
  • Extra στC/ε³ term vs. delay-adaptive methods when σ/ε ≫ 1.
  • Clipping radius c is a tuned hyperparameter — no automatic selection.
  • Heterogeneous improvement modest (1.2–1.3×) vs. homogeneous (1.5–2.2×).

Open Questions

  1. How does Clipped ASGD interact with modern optimizers like AdamW that already use element-wise clipping/normalization?
  2. Can the clipping radius be set adaptively from observed gradient statistics without sacrificing guarantees?
  3. What happens under (L₀, L₁)-smoothness, where gradient norms and staleness may interact more severely?
  4. Does the approach scale to real FL with hundreds of heterogeneous clients and network failures?
  5. How does clipping interact with DP-SGD in the async setting, given its dual role in optimization and privacy?
