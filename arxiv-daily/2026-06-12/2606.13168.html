Exposing routing weights is necessary but not sufficient for mechanistic interpretation.

When Does Routing Become Interpretable? Causal Probes on Block Attention Residuals

Aydin Javadov · ETH Zurich | 2026-06-12 | mechanistic-interpretability attention-residuals causal-intervention
Key Metric
9%
Prev-completed pathway carries 9% of routing mass yet produces zero detectable causal effect (logprob drop 0.08).
Why This Matters

Routing weight descriptions cannot replace causal interventions — a methodological warning for all attention-based interpretability research.

Critical Edge

Synthetic key–value retrieval with 200 examples is a narrow probe; whether these findings transfer to naturalistic tasks is unknown.

Methodology Results Strengths & Limitations Open Questions
一句话总结 Block Attention Residuals架构虽然显式暴露了跨层路由权重，但仅在路由参与训练时才能形成可解释的结构化通路，且路由质量不等于因果重要性——仅有描述性路由权重不足以做机制性解读。

Methodology

  • Two 0.6B Qwen3 checkpoints (d=1024, L=28, B=8 blocks) compared under identical causal probes
  • Wrapped baseline: vanilla model with deterministic recency-bias schedule (β=3.0)
  • Trained AttnRes: trained from scratch with routing as part of optimization
  • Task: synthetic key–value retrieval, 200 examples, 4-choice scoring by conditional log-probability
  • Four source families: embedding, current, prev completed, nonlocal
  • Intervention: mask-and-renormalize ablation by family × sublayer (ATTN/MLP) × depth region (early/mid/late)
  • Metrics: accuracy, correct-answer log-prob drop, flip rate, mean rank

Results

Current → early-attn — — 4.17

  • Baseline routing masses match analytic prediction to 4 decimal places (0.840)
  • Sublayer asymmetry: embedding → MLP (6.38 vs 0.13 attn); nonlocal → attention only
  • Mass–causality dissociation: highest mass ≠ highest causal contribution

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Exceptionally clean controlled design with analytic verification (0.840 match)
  • Three-axis factorization (family × sublayer × depth) — principled decomposition
  • Mass–causality dissociation is novel with broad implications
  • Honest, precise limitations section
  • Clear logical progression from motivation to experiment to implication

Limitations

  • Synthetic task may not capture real-language routing dynamics
  • Single model scale (0.6B); scaling behavior unknown
  • Ablation tests pathway reliance, not information content — null effects may reflect compensation
  • No training dynamics analysis (when does structured routing emerge?)
  • Prev-completed null result could be probe artifact

Open Questions

  1. Does the three-motif structure replicate at 7B / 70B scale or with different architectures?
  2. At what training step does structured routing diverge from recency-bias initialization — is there a phase transition?
  3. Would stronger probes (activation patching, indirect effect ID) reveal a causal role for prev completed, or is it genuinely vestigial?
  4. Can regularizers encouraging high-causal-importance routes (vs. high average mass) improve both interpretability and performance?
  5. What happens under intermediate conditions — AttnRes fine-tuned from vanilla, or trained with frozen routing?
