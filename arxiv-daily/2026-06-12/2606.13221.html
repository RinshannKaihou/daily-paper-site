From Uncertain Judgments to Calibrated Rankings: Conformal Elo Estimation for LLM Evaluation

Bora Kargi & David Salinas · ELLIS Institute Tübingen · OpenEuroLLM · 2026-06-12
Standard Hard-Elo (binary win/loss/tie) systematically stretches the Elo axis — strong models inflate, weak models deflate. Soft-Elo replaces hard labels with calibrated continuous probabilities from judge score differences, reducing Elo MAE from 45.9 → 17.9 across 8 judges and 55 held-out models on LMArena. A conformal prediction layer adds distribution-free confidence intervals, cutting interval width by 39–73%.
Key Finding
Soft targets σ(β*·s) from score differences eliminate the "stretched ruler" distortion in Hard-Elo — rank order preserved (Δρ ≤ 0.014), scale corrected.
Key Metric
17.9 Elo MAE
↓ 45.9 hard-elo · 39–73% interval reduction
Why This Matters
A single model (Gemini-2.5) shifts from rank 2nd to 8th depending on judge — current LLM-as-a-judge rankings are not reliable without calibration.
Critical Edge
Conformal guarantees assume exchangeability between calibration and test models. New architectures or reasoning models may violate this — and 17.9 MAE still means ~18 Elo of residual uncertainty.

Methodology

1

Each battle produces a score difference s(x) from 6 criteria (adherence, helpfulness, factuality, completeness, clarity, fluency) on a 1–10 scale. Position bias mitigated by averaging both presentation orders.

2

Soft-Elo (local): Fit temperature β* via MLE on human non-tie battles (leave-one-model-out). Map scores to continuous probabilities ỹ = σ(β*·s) → feed into Bradley-Terry log-likelihood. β* stabilizes at ~50 battles/model (std ≤ 0.005).

3

Conformal intervals (global): Compute residuals ε = EloLLM − EloHuman, normalize by bootstrap SE. Split conformal prediction yields intervals Elo ± q̂·SE with marginal coverage ≥ 1−α.

4

β* as diagnostic: When β* > 0.3, score differences carry meaningful signal. When ~0.13, score-agreement correlation is flat — Soft-Elo may over-compress. Early warning for applicability.

Results · 8 Judges × 55 Models

  • Mean MAE drops 45.9 → 17.9; per-judge reduction 39% (Qwen3-32B) to 73% (DeepSeek-V3.2)
  • Spearman ρ change negligible (Δρ ∈ [−0.011, +0.012]) — ranking order intact
  • Median interval width at 90% coverage: 132–261 → 70–143 Elo (39–73% narrower)
  • Empirical coverage 92.1–96.4% at 90% nominal
  • Judge–human agreement rises monotonically: ~53% at |s|<0.5 → ~85% at |s|>4
  • Cross-corpus: 45–79% MAE reduction (LMArena 140K), 20–81% (ComparIA French)
  • Outperforms Hard-Elo at every budget from b=5 to b=200; largest gap in low-data regime

Assessment

Strengths

  • Minimal intervention — keeps Bradley-Terry, only changes training targets. Easy to adopt.
  • Distribution-free uncertainty quantification via conformal prediction.
  • Thorough evaluation: 8 judges, 55 models, 3 corpora, sample efficiency, stability analysis.
  • Honest about failures — reports Soft-Elo degradation on Qwen/ComparIA openly.
  • β* diagnostic gives practitioners a pre-flight check before deployment.
  • Strong sample efficiency — valuable when annotation budgets are limited.

Limitations

  • Exchangeability assumption may break under temporal or architectural distribution shifts.
  • No epistemic uncertainty modeling — judge hallucination and prompt ambiguity not captured.
  • Residual human annotation required for conformal calibration set.
  • 17.9 MAE ≈ 4.5% of typical Elo range (~400 pts) — close rankings still uncertain.
  • All experiments use a single 6-criterion scoring prompt; sensitivity unknown.
  • Coverage is marginal, not conditional on model family or tier.
Relevance: Directly solves Elo ranking distortion in LLM-as-a-judge pipelines. Practitioners building automated leaderboards get statistically guaranteed confidence intervals without increasing human annotation — especially the distribution-free intervals and β* diagnostic are immediately actionable.

Open Questions

  1. How does Soft-Elo perform with judges that produce categorical rather than continuous scores?
  2. Can β* be used as an adaptive diagnostic without access to human labels?
  3. Does combining soft targets with tie-aware BT extensions yield further gains?
  4. What happens when calibration pool becomes stale relative to test model generation?
  5. Can conformal intervals be made conditional on model family for tighter bounds?
