> **一句话总结：** 本文系统性地量化了大语言模型（LLM）中"序列概率"与"答案正确性"在不同粒度上的关系——发现在固定数据集内、跨 prompt-answer 对时，更高的序列概率通常能预测正确性；但这种关系**并不能迁移**到解码决策层面（调整方法或超参得到的高概率序列并不更准确），对同一 prompt 的多次采样，序列概率也几乎不能区分对错。

> **TL;DR:** The paper systematically measures how sequence log-probability correlates with answer correctness across decoding methods, models, and benchmarks at four levels. Higher sequence probability is often predictive of correctness **across prompt-answer pairs within a fixed dataset**, but this relationship **does not transfer** to decoding decisions (tuning hyperparameters or switching methods to push probability higher does not reliably improve accuracy), and within-sample correlations for repeated responses to the same prompt are essentially symmetric around zero. The findings clarify when probability-based decoding, self-consistency, and verifier-free self-improvement can and cannot be expected to help.

## 核心贡献

- **重新定义研究问题**：把众多解码方法（局部采样如 top-k / top-p / ε-sampling / 低温度采样，全局方法如 beam search、Best-of-N、power sampling）统一抽象为"向高概率序列移动概率质量"的操作，并由此追问一个根本问题——序列概率何时与正确性对齐？
- **提出四种粒度的相关分析框架**：定义并测量 across-method（跨方法）、within-method（方法内调超参）、within-dataset（数据集内跨 prompt-answer 对）、within-sample（同一 prompt 多次采样）四个层级的相关性，为后续研究提供了清晰的度量坐标系。
- **大规模实证**：覆盖 12-14 个模型（Qwen3 0.6B/1.7B/4B/8B base 与 posttrained、Qwen2.5(-Math)-8B、Olmo3-7B）、6 个基准（MATH500、GPQA、MMLU、HumanEval、MedQA、IFEval）、8 种解码方法。
- **给出 6 条明确的结论（Findings 1-6）**，其中最关键的是"正相关仅存在于数据集层面，且大致随任务准确率上升而增强；但在方法层、超参层、单 prompt 层均不成立"。
- **理论补充**：给出全局解码方法的变分目标（`Jα(q)=E_q[log p]+1/α H(q)`），证明 power distribution 是其最优解，并将 SPS、power-SMC 纳入 twisted SMC 框架；指出 LTS 不等于序列级 power sampling。
- **实践指导**：对自洽（self-consistency，概率加权投票通常不如均匀多数投票）、概率加权自蒸馏、verifier-free 自改进给出了基于实证的边界条件。

## Key Contributions

- Reframes the landscape of decoding methods (local: low-temperature sampling, top-k, top-p, ε-sampling; global: beam search, Best-of-N, scalable power sampling / SPS, power-SMC) as operations that shift probability mass toward higher-probability sequences, and asks the fundamental question of when sequence probability aligns with correctness.
- Introduces a four-level correlation framework — across-method, within-method (hyperparameter sweeps), within-dataset (across prompt-answer pairs), within-sample (across repeated responses to one prompt) — that makes the otherwise fuzzy "probability vs correctness" question measurable and comparable.
- Large-scale empirical study: 12-14 models across Qwen3 (0.6B–8B, base + posttrained), Qwen2.5(-Math)-8B, Olmo3-7B; 6 benchmarks (MATH500, GPQA, MMLU, HumanEval, MedQA, IFEval); 8 decoding methods with multiple hyperparameter settings each.
- Six crisp findings (Findings 1–6), the headline being: the positive probability–correctness relationship holds within a dataset and scales with task accuracy, but **does not hold** at the method level, the hyperparameter level, or the single-prompt level.
- Theoretical grounding: a variational objective `Jα(q) = E_q[log p(s|s̄)] + (1/α) H(q)` whose maximizer is the power distribution; a unified twisted-SMC view of SPS and power-SMC (with proofs of asymptotic exactness / approximation gaps); and the formal statement that low-temperature token sampling is generally not equal to sequence-level power sampling.
- Practical guidance for self-consistency (uniform majority voting usually beats probability-weighted voting), power self-consistency, self-distillation, and verifier-free self-improvement, with empirically grounded limits on when each helps.

## Methodology

The authors treat an autoregressive LLM `p` as assigning probabilities to token continuations `s` given a prompt `s̄`, and classify decoding methods into:

- **Local methods** that modify the per-token distribution at each prefix (low-temperature sampling LTS, truncated sampling TS including top-k / top-p / ε-sampling). Because they use *local* normalization constants, they do not target the globally most probable sequence (Remark B.1).
- **Global methods** that target high-probability full sequences: beam search (approximates `argmax` as K→∞), Best-of-N (returns the highest-probability of N samples), and power sampling which samples from `p(s|s̄)^α / Zα`. The authors derive a variational objective showing the power distribution is the maximizer of `Jα(q) = E_q[log p] + (1/α) H(q)`, and frame SPS and power-SMC inside the twisted sequential Monte Carlo framework of Zhao et al. (2024), giving the optimal twist `ψt⋆ ∝ Σ p(s_>t | s̄, s_≤t)^α`.

The empirical core defines four correlation coefficients between continuous log-probability and a binary correctness label:

- **Across-method correlation** — fix model + dataset, correlate log-prob and accuracy across methods, using low-temperature sampling (α=4) as a zero point and a quadrant analysis (Fig 9).
- **Within-method correlation** — for a fixed method, correlate log-prob and accuracy across its hyperparameters (slope sign + correctness change).
- **Within-dataset correlation** — for one (model, dataset, method) triple, scatter all samples by log-prob vs correctness, bin into 10 equal-count bins, and compute a binned Pearson r (and Spearman ρ).
- **Within-sample correlation** — for a single prompt, the rank correlation between log-prob and correctness across its N repeated responses.

Setup details: vLLM for local methods; the authors' own importance-weighted SPS implementation (Algorithm 1) with K=M=8, τc=τr=0.25, rollouts with LTS α=4 corrected by importance weighting; power-SMC from the original authors' code. Trajectories capped at 3072 tokens; ≤500 prompts/dataset; budget ≤1 day per (method, dataset) on an 80GB H100. Canonical hyperparameters per method are listed (e.g., LTS α=4, BoN N=32, SPS B=192, Top-k k=8, Top-p p=0.9, Beam N=2, ε=0.0009). Pearson r uses binned means (K=10 bins); Spearman ρ is bounded above by 0.5 because the label is binary.

## Results & Highlights

- **Finding 1 (within-dataset):** Log-probability and correctness are consistently correlated within a fixed (model, dataset, method) triple, and this is largely *independent of the decoding method* — it is driven by dataset and model variant. Correlation is strongest on MATH500 (near-perfect r=+0.96 for Qwen3-8B-Base), positive-but-modest on GPQA/HumanEval/MedQA/MMLU, and *negative* on IFEval for base models. Posttrained models show predominantly positive correlations; base models are more diverse and often negative on IFEval.
- **Finding 2 (within-method):** Tuning a method's hyperparameter almost always yields *more probable* sequences, but this *does not* yield more correct sequences — relationships are inconsistent across datasets and models, with local methods frequently showing inverse behavior (higher prob, lower acc).
- **Finding 3 (across-method):** No method reliably beats the low-temperature sampling baseline across datasets, even with an oracle-chosen hyperparameter. Power-sampling methods reliably find higher-probability sequences that are *not always* more correct; local methods often produce *less* probable but *more* correct sequences. Beam search (with length normalization) consistently produces less probable and less correct sequences.
- **Finding 4 (correlation scales with accuracy):** Within-dataset correlation increases with mean dataset accuracy (base r=0.66, posttrained r=0.59). Implication: verifier-free recursive self-improvement loops that rely on probability need *sufficient baseline accuracy* to improve, else they reinforce on increasingly incorrect samples — a stronger caveat for base models.
- **Finding 5 (within-sample):** Per-prompt rank correlations are symmetrically distributed around zero with zero mean; only MATH500 averages positive. The more correct the repeated samples for a prompt, the more positive its within-sample correlation — but standard deviations are large and overlapping.
- **Finding 6 (self-consistency):** For LTS, uniform majority voting outperforms probability-weighted voting, consistent with the symmetric-around-zero within-sample correlation. Power self-consistency improves over plain self-consistency on MATH500 but degrades on GPQA and MMLU.
- **Self-distillation (Appendix E.7):** Helps on datasets/methods where a log-prob–correctness correlation exists (notably MATH500 power samples); elsewhere results are mixed and plain LTS distillation often wins.
- **Thinking models (Appendix E.8):** Enabling Qwen3 thinking frequently degrades accuracy (3/6 datasets), mostly because long `<think>` traces hit the token budget; the main text therefore reports non-thinking models.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The reframing of decoding as "probability-mass shifting" and the four-level correlation taxonomy is a genuinely useful conceptual contribution; the within-sample negative result (no per-prompt probability-correctness signal) is the most novel and counterintuitive finding. The variational objective and the twisted-SMC unification of SPS/power-SMC are clean but partly consolidate existing work (Kempton & Burrell 2025 for local methods; Zhao et al. 2024 for twisted SMC). The paper is honest that it is "to some degree a negative result."
- **Rigor:** Strong on breadth (12-14 models, 6 datasets, 8 methods, multiple hyperparameters each) and on statistical care (binned Pearson to handle binary labels, Spearman ρ with stated 0.5 upper bound, jackknife bias correction in SPS). However, the analysis is fundamentally correlational and observational — it does not establish causal mechanisms for *why* probability and correctness decouple at the method level, which limits what practitioners can conclude beyond "tune on your own data."
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Hyperparameters, canonical settings, prompt templates, compute budget, and sample counts are all reported (Appendix D). Power-SMC uses the original authors' code; SPS is re-implemented per Algorithm 1 with importance-weighting correction. A notable gap: no statement of whether full code/raw logs will be released, and the SPS reimplementation (rather than the original codebase) is a potential source of discrepancy vs. Ji et al. (2026).
- **Significance:** Practically significant. It directly challenges the widespread assumption that "more probable = more correct" that underpins Best-of-N selection, probability-weighted self-consistency, and verifier-free self-distillation/self-improvement. The accuracy-gated caveat (Findings 4/5) is actionable for anyone building self-improvement loops on weaker or base models.
- **Red flags / caveats:**
  - **Token-length confound.** The authors acknowledge beam search's length normalization behaves as an outlier and that probability and length are entangled; the within-dataset signal could partly be a length artifact rather than a genuine correctness signal. Appendix E.4/E.5 address this but it is not fully neutralized.
  - **Compute-driven scope limits.** 3072-token cap forces the exclusion of thinking models (which degrade mainly by hitting the limit), and ≤500 prompts/dataset, so conclusions about long-form reasoning and statistical power on small datasets (e.g., GPQA n=445) should be treated cautiously.
  - **Within-sample granularity.** With N=32 samples per prompt, per-prompt correlation estimates are noisy; the authors flag large/overlapping standard deviations, so the "symmetric around zero" Finding 5 is suggestive rather than definitive.
  - **Benchmark selection.** All six benchmarks favor short, extractable answers (boxed MCQ/code), which is exactly where answer-extraction-based correctness is clean — generalization to open-ended generation is not tested.
  - **No formal hypothesis testing / multiple-comparison correction** across the very large grid of (model × dataset × method × hyperparameter) cells; the qualitative Findings are robust but individual cell-level claims (the many +/− symbols in Figures 4–5) should not be over-interpreted.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean framing that unifies a sprawling decoding literature under one question.
- A reusable four-level correlation framework that future decoding papers can adopt.
- Genuinely broad and systematic empirical coverage across model families, sizes, training stages, and tasks.
- Intellectual honesty: the paper foregrounds its own negative results and explicitly states what practitioners should and should not do.
- Solid theoretical appendix (variational objective, twisted-SMC unification, exactness/approximation-gap remarks with proofs).
- Direct, practical implications for self-consistency weighting, power self-consistency, and probability-based self-improvement.

## Limitations

- Purely correlational; no causal account of *why* probability and correctness decouple at the method/hyperparameter level.
- Token-length / length-normalization confounds are only partially addressed.
- Compute constraints exclude thinking models from the main results and cap prompt counts, limiting generalization and statistical power.
- Benchmarks are all short-answer/MCQ-style; open-ended generation behavior is untested.
- SPS is a re-implementation (not the original codebase), introducing a possible reproduction gap.
- No formal statistical significance testing or multiple-comparison control across the large experimental grid; cell-level conclusions are noisy.

## Relevance

本文对"序列概率是否能当作正确性的免验证器信号"这一被 Best-of-N、概率加权自洽、自蒸馏等大量方法默认的假设给出了系统性的实证回答，结论对任何构建解码策略、self-consistency 或 verifier-free 自改进流水线的研究者与工程师都有直接的指导价值——它清晰地划定了"概率信号可用"与"不可用"的边界条件。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Why exactly does the within-dataset probability–correctness correlation fail to transfer to the method/hyperparameter level? Is it a length artifact, a mode-collapse artifact, or something intrinsic to how sharpening redistributes mass?
- Does the within-sample correlation stay symmetric around zero for much larger N (e.g., N=256/512), or does a signal emerge with more samples? What is the N-scaling law of within-sample ρ?
- How do the four correlations behave on open-ended / free-form generation tasks where answer extraction is non-trivial, and on agentic / multi-turn settings?
- Can a learned twist function (per the SMC literature) or a lightweight verifier recover a usable within-sample probability–correctness signal without external supervision?
- Does the "correlation scales with accuracy" finding imply an optimal accuracy threshold above which verifier-free self-improvement becomes self-sustaining, and can that threshold be predicted a priori for a given model–task pair?
- Are the negative IFEval correlations for base models a data-coverage artifact (limited instruction-following pretraining), and do they flip with more instruction tuning?
