Information-Aware KV Cache Compression for Long Reasoning

High-entropy tokens dominate long-range future impact — InfoKV fuses predictive entropy with attention to beat attention-based KV compressors.

Jushi Kai, Zhuiri Xiao, Alexandra Birch, Zhouhan Lin 2026-06-25 LUMIA Lab · SJTU + Edinburgh
LLM-efficiency KV-cache-compression long-context-reasoning information-theory inference-optimization
Key Metric
12.5%cache retention still beats full-cache on IFEval (R1-Distill-Llama-8B)
Why This Matters
KV-cache ballooning in long chain-of-thought reasoning (DeepSeek-R1, o1-class models) is a pressing deployment bottleneck — this directly cuts inference cost.
Critical Edge
Reported LongReason gains are often a modest 1–2 accuracy points with no error bars — statistical significance is impossible to judge.

Methodology

Forward Influence motivation. The authors define a token's future utility as the average KL divergence between the original predictive distribution and the one obtained after removing that token from the KV cache: I(x_i) = (1/(r−l+1)) Σ D_KL(p ‖ p \ {x_i}). Using Llama-3.1-8B, they rank the first 2048 tokens by attention, entropy, and their combination, measuring influence over short (128-token) and long (14K-token) horizons. The combined score best balances both ranges — attention governs short-range relevance, entropy drives long-range persistence.

InfoKV score. For each layer, InfoKV fuses three signals: (1) Top-k Restricted Entropy (k=256) for predictive uncertainty, avoiding low-probability vocabulary noise; (2) layer-wise representation evolution, the cosine distance D_i = 1 − cos(h_i^l, h_i^L) between early- and final-layer hidden states, capturing unresolved semantic content; (3) attention. These combine as S_i = α·A_i + (1−α)·Softmax(D_i · H_i + τ). Top-ranked tokens are retained per layer.

Defaults & deployment. τ = 1, k = 256, α = 0.9 (mostly attention + small entropy). An adaptive per-layer budget variant allocates cache proportional to accumulated entropy. Compression triggers every 1024 generated tokens (RPC protocol), enabling online long-decoding for reasoning models.

Results & Highlights

Scenario / Model Benchmark Budget Outcome
Long prefilling · Llama-3.1/3.2 LongReason (16k–64k) 40% / 20% SOTA, CoT + direct-answer
Long decoding · R1-Distill-Llama-8B IFEval 25% 60.07 → 61.18 (adaptive)
Long decoding · R1-Distill-Llama-8B IFEval 12.5% beats full-cache
Long decoding · DeepSeek-R1-Distill AIME 2024 · LiveCodeBench 25% / 12.5% beats RPC
Adaptive budget · R1-Distill-Qwen-7B IFEval 25% 59.70 → 55.82 (regresses)

Ablations: τ=1 optimal & stable; Top-256 entropy > full-vocab entropy; α=0.9 beats pure attention (α=1) and entropy-dominated settings — confirming complementarity. Compressed caches outperforming full caches on IFEval suggests long reasoning trajectories carry redundancy that can distract the model.

Strengths

  • Principled motivation: Forward Influence gives a measurable definition of "future utility," not intuition.
  • Clear empirical demonstration of attention–entropy complementarity (Figures 1 & 2).
  • Consistent gains across prefilling and decoding, dense (Llama) and distilled reasoning (R1) models.
  • Counterintuitive full-cache-beating result on IFEval has real deployment value.
  • Thorough ablations on all three hyperparameters (τ, k, α).

Limitations

  • Modest absolute gains on some LongReason settings; no error bars or significance testing.
  • Entropy is only an indirect proxy for future utility — not an explicit optimization target.
  • Adaptive layer-wise budgeting helps Llama-8B but harms Qwen-7B, so uniform is the default.
  • Forward Influence motivation uses a simplified shared-token-across-layers assumption.
  • Code not yet released at submission; full reproducibility deferred.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Could Forward Influence serve as a training signal (differentiable surrogate) to learn a directly-optimized compressor rather than relying on entropy as an indirect proxy?
  2. What explains the architecture-dependent behavior of adaptive budgeting (Qwen-7B degrades, Llama-8B improves) — layer count, head patterns, or pretraining distribution?
  3. How does InfoKV perform under extreme compression (≤10%) or on horizons >64k, where entropy's long-range advantage should amplify?
  4. Does combining InfoKV with token-merging (vs. pure eviction) yield additive gains?
  5. What is the wall-clock and memory overhead of computing per-layer entropy + cosine distance, and does the accuracy gain justify the added compute?
