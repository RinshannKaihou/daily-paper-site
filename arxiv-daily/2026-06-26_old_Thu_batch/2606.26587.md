> **一句话总结：** SharQ 提出了一种无需训练的在线"稀疏-稠密"分解方法，将激活中的离群值路由到 N:M 稀疏 FP4 主干、其余信息交给稠密 FP4 残差路径联合补偿，在不依赖校准数据的前提下于 RTX 5090 上实现 2.2–2.4× 的延迟降低，并恢复 43–63% 的 NVFP4 精度损失。

> **TL;DR:** SharQ is a training-free, calibration-free inference method that fuses FP4 quantization with hardware-supported N:M activation sparsity via an online sparse–dense decomposition, recovering 43–63% of the NVFP4-to-FP16 accuracy gap while delivering 2.2–2.4× latency reduction over FP16 and 1.2–1.4× throughput improvement over FP8 on an RTX 5090.

## 核心贡献

- **在线稀疏–稠密分解范式**：针对每个激活张量在线生成输入自适应的 N:M 掩码，提取以离群值为主的稀疏主干并用稠密 FP4 残差补偿，把"丢弃式稀疏化"重构为"路由式稀疏化"。
- **量化感知的残差定义**：残差 R 相对于"量化后的稀疏主干"而非"未量化的稀疏值"定义（R = X − X̃_sp），使稠密路径能够同时补偿掩码丢失和稀疏路径 FP4 量化误差，把两类耦合误差归约为单一的残差重建问题。
- **面向硬件的高效内核**：将掩码生成、残差构造、层归一化融合进单个算子；两条路径共享同一份 FP4 权重负载（通过路径特定的 scale view）；残差以累加方式直接写入稀疏输出，保持接近单内核的数据流。
- **格式无关且即插即用**：无需校准数据、重训练或模型级调参，统一适配 NVFP4、HiF4、MXFP4 三种块缩放 FP4 格式，并在稠密解码器、MoE、视觉语言、视频生成模型上均有效。

## Key Contributions

- **Online sparse–dense decomposition**: For every activation tensor, SharQ generates an input-adaptive N:M mask online to extract an outlier-dominated sparse backbone and routes the remaining information to a dense FP4 residual, reframing sparsification as routing rather than dropping.
- **Quantization-aware residual**: The dense residual is defined relative to the *quantized* sparse backbone (R = X − X̃_sp), so the dense path jointly compensates both mask-induced activation loss and sparse-path FP4 quantization error, collapsing two coupled error sources into a single reconstruction problem.
- **Hardware-oriented kernel design**: A fused preparation kernel absorbs mask generation, residual construction, and RMSNorm/layer-norm preprocessing into one operator; both paths share a single FP4 weight payload with path-specific scale views; the residual accumulates directly into the sparse output to preserve near-single-kernel dataflow.
- **Format-agnostic, plug-and-play**: No calibration data, retraining, or model-specific tuning; generalized across NVFP4 (4:8 in-pairs), HiF4 (2:4), and MXFP4, and validated on dense decoders, a mixture-of-experts model, a vision-language model, and a video generation model.

## Methodology

SharQ approximates a full-precision linear layer Y = XW with two complementary FP4 execution paths. The objective is to minimize ‖Y − Ỹ‖_F subject to hardware constraints imposed by the chosen numeric format, sparsity pattern, and GEMM kernel.

1. **Sparse backbone (online mask)**: From the current activation X, an online mask M(X) ∈ S_{N:M} selects the top-k magnitudes within each hardware block, producing X_sp = M(X) ⊙ X. For NVFP4 this uses 4:8 in-pairs sparsity; for HiF4 the standard 2:4 pattern. Because the mask is computed from the current input, no offline or learned mask is needed.

2. **Quantize the backbone**: The sparse backbone is quantized and dequantized via the sparse path operators, X̃_sp = D_sp(Q_sp(X_sp)).

3. **Dense residual relative to quantized backbone**: SharQ defines R = X − X̃_sp = (X − X_sp) + (X_sp − X̃_sp). This makes the residual contain *both* the values outside the mask *and* the quantization error of the retained outliers, instead of being a plain mask-out complement.

4. **Two-path reconstruction with shared weights**: Ỹ = GEMM_sp(Q_sp(X_sp), Q_{w,sp}(W)) + GEMM_dn(Q_dn(R), Q_{w,dn}(W)). Both paths reuse a single FP4 weight body with different scale layouts (group-32 sparse scales vs. duplicated group-16 dense scales); the dense residual accumulates directly into the sparse output rather than materializing a second branch.

5. **Fused preparation kernel**: On Blackwell, one kernel performs RMSNorm/PTS, pair-topk 4:8 mask generation, sparse compression, and dense residual construction, eliminating a separate quantization/RMSNorm step (the fused kernel removes ~7.4 pp of prefill overhead relative to NVFP4).

**Theoretical framing**: The output perturbation satisfies ‖XW̄ − (X̃_sp + R̃)W̄‖_F ≤ ‖R − R̃‖_F · ‖W̄‖_2, showing SharQ converts coupled sparsification+quantization error into a single residual reconstruction problem, and that outlier isolation improves the quantization-friendliness of the dense residual.

## Results & Highlights

- **Language models** (Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen2.5-7B, Qwen3-30B-A3B): SharQ recovers 43–63% of the NVFP4→FP16 average-accuracy gap. On Llama-3.1-8B, average zero-shot accuracy rises 70.32%→71.55% (56% of gap), WikiText2 PPL 6.94→6.73, MMLU 61.93→63.76. On Qwen2.5-7B it recovers 63% of the gap.
- **Vision-language** (Qwen3-VL-8B): Recovers ~43% of the NVFP4 accuracy gap across VQAv2, GQA, SQA, POPE, TextVQA; improves POPE (hallucination resistance) by 0.47 pp.
- **Serving efficiency** (RTX 5090, vLLM, Llama-3.1-8B, batch 4): 2.2–2.4× latency reduction over FP16; 1.2–1.4× total token throughput improvement over FP8 across sequence lengths 256–2048 (at seqlen 2048: 4491 vs 3146 tokens/s for FP8).
- **Video generation** (Wan2.2-T2V-A14B, 4× RTX 5090): 1.23× (480P) and 1.11× (720P) speedup standalone; up to 1.58× / 1.57× when combined with SageAttention (SharQ+Sage).
- **Format generalization**: Consistent gains across NVFP4, HiF4, and MXFP4 on Qwen3-30B-A3B; largest recovery on MXFP4 (the weakest baseline), e.g. WikiText2 PPL 9.70→9.19 and Lambada +2.84 pp.
- **Kernel-level**: Sparse NVFP4 GEMM is 0.66–0.72× the latency of dense NVFP4 GEMM (28–34% speedup); FP8 GEMMs are ~2× slower than dense NVFP4.
- **Ablations**: "Sparse only" collapses quality (WikiText2 PPL 82.95, MMLU 25.78); "Sparse+Sparse" ≈ plain NVFP4 baseline; SharQ's asymmetric sparse+dense matches or beats "Dense+Dense" while keeping the sparse execution path.
- **Code released**: https://github.com/actypedef/SharQ

All authors are from the School of Computer Science and Technology, Tianjin University. Haoqian Meng, Yilun Luo, and Yafei Zhao share equal-contribution first authorship; Peng Zhang is the corresponding author. The team has prior published work in this exact space (MicroMix, ARCQuant are cited as their own contributions), which lends strong domain credibility. The paper is unusually systems-honest: it reports prefill overhead (111.2% of NVFP4, an 11.2% increase) and shows the catastrophic failure mode of the sparse-only ablation rather than hiding it. Code is publicly released, which is a meaningful reproducibility signal. The work is grounded in real hardware constraints (Blackwell Tensor Cores, 4:8 in-pairs / 2:4 sparse patterns, RTX 5090 / B200-class).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate-to-high. Decomposition ideas (ResQ, SVDQuant) and N:M weight sparsity are well established; the genuinely new move is doing an *online, input-adaptive, quantization-aware* decomposition on the *activation* side that fits strict N:M hardware patterns, plus the specific residual definition R = X − X̃_sp that couples sparse-path quantization error into the dense compensation. The framing of "sparsification as routing, not dropping" is a clean conceptual contribution, even if sparse+dense GEMM is conceptually familiar.
- **Rigor**: Strong. Benchmarks span multiple model families (dense, MoE, VLM, video diffusion), five LM accuracy tasks plus perplexity and MMLU, five multimodal tasks, three FP4 formats, and a clean ablation isolating each path. Kernel-level and prefill-breakdown numbers are reported rather than buried. Error analysis (Eq. 12–13) ties the design to the math.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Code is public, hardware and software stack (Blackwell, RTX 5090, vLLM) are specified, and the method is training/calibration-free so there are no hidden seeds. The main barrier to external reproduction is access to Blackwell-class hardware, which is a practical rather than methodological limitation.
- **Significance**: High for the efficient-LLM-serving community. It is one of the first practical, end-to-end demonstrations that hardware-native N:M *activation* sparsity can be combined with FP4 in a training-free setting, with real serving-stack integration (vLLM) and real speedups over both FP16 and FP8. If the gains hold up on Blackwell server GPUs (B200/B300) and larger models, this could become a standard FP4 inference primitive.
- **Red flags / concerns**:
  - The headline accuracy numbers are *relative recovery of a gap*, not absolute SOTA over the best non-FP4 methods; the absolute NVFP4+SharQ accuracy is still below FP16 on most tasks. Comparisons against strong non-FP4 baselines (e.g., well-tuned INT8/FP8 with outlier handling) are limited.
  - Efficiency results are concentrated on a single consumer GPU (RTX 5090) and a single batch size (4) for serving; data-center latency/throughput curves under variable load are not shown.
  - The 2.2–2.4× latency claim is over FP16, which is a weak baseline for production serving; the more informative 1.2–1.4× over FP8 is more modest.
  - Prefill overhead (+11.2%) is honestly disclosed but means the win is decode-heavy; prefill-dominated workloads benefit less.
  - The sparse-only ablation is catastrophic (PPL 82.95), so the method's value is entirely dependent on the dense residual path functioning correctly — robustness under extreme outlier distributions deserves more study.
  - Qwen3-30B-A3B average accuracy with SharQ (69.17%) is still slightly below NVFP4 alone (68.40% is the baseline, but on a couple benchmarks SharQ is below NVFP4), so per-task regressions exist.

## Strengths

- Training-free, calibration-free, format-agnostic, and genuinely plug-and-play — minimal deployment friction.
- Principled residual definition that jointly handles sparsification and quantization error, with supporting theory.
- Strong systems engineering: fused preparation kernel, shared FP4 weights, accumulation-based residual — the implementation matters as much as the algorithm.
- Broad empirical coverage across architectures (dense, MoE, VLM, video diffusion) and three FP4 formats.
- Honest reporting of overheads and failure modes, plus open-source code.

## Limitations

- Absolute accuracy remains below FP16; gains are measured as gap-recovery percentages.
- Efficiency validated primarily on RTX 5090 with fixed batch size 4; data-center GPU behavior and serving under realistic load are not yet shown.
- Realizable gains hinge on Blackwell-class hardware with native FP4 + N:M sparsity support, limiting current portability.
- Prefill overhead (~11%) reduces benefit for prefill-dominated or short-sequence workloads; the win is concentrated in decode.
- Limited head-to-head comparison with the strongest non-FP4 efficient-inference baselines; sparsification-robustness under pathological activation distributions is underexplored.

## Relevance

这项工作对于追求低比特（FP4）大模型推理加速的工程与研究非常相关，特别适合关注稀疏化、量化、vLLM 部署以及 Blackwell 新硬件能力的读者；它给出了一个无需训练、可即插即用地把 N:M 激活稀疏与 FP4 量化的工程化范例，并附带开放源码，对实际服务端推理优化有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the accuracy and latency gains transfer to Blackwell server GPUs (B200/B300) and larger models (70B+, MoE beyond 30B) under realistic multi-batch serving?
- Can the residual path be made adaptive (e.g., skipped or lower-precision) per layer or per token to cut the +11% prefill overhead without accuracy regression?
- How does SharQ compose with weight-side quantization/pruning methods (GPTQ, AWQ, SparseGPT) and with rotation-based outlier suppression (QuaRot, SpinQuant)?
- What is the worst-case behavior on adversarial or extreme-outlier activation distributions where the online N:M mask may be unstable?
- Can the dense compensation be learned offline (a tiny calibration) to push recovery beyond 63% of the gap, and does that erode the training-free advantage?
- How does SharQ interact with attention-level accelerators (FlashAttention, SageAttention) beyond the single video-generation result reported?
