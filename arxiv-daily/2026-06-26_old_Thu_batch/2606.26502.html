Key Finding

Cross-item alignment between LRMs and humans masks an opposite within-item policy: errors lengthen model reasoning chains but shorten human responses.

Humans Disengage, Reasoning Models Persist: Separating Difficulty Registration from Deliberation Allocation

Han-yu Wang · The University of Hong Kong · 2026-06-25

large-reasoning-models deliberation chain-of-thought resource-rational metacognition human-model-comparison

Key Metric

−0.66

item-fixed-effect interaction βLRM (n = 5,728, p < .001, 95% CI [−0.81, −0.50]). Thinking-LRM within-item Cohen's d = 1.47–3.13 vs humans −0.10.

Why This Matters

Offers a direct, operational diagnostic for anyone invoking chain-length or reaction-time to argue human–model cognitive alignment — cross-item agreement is insufficient without a within-item allocation check.

Critical Edge

The dissociation is observational; the controlled truncation experiment that would prove the extra wrong-trial tokens are causally useless is proposed but never run.

01Methodology

Secondary reanalysis of the public, de-identified de Varda et al. (2025) release across three corpora: H-ARC (visual abstraction, primary), INTUIT (intuitive physics, replication), and Cortes (relational reasoning, boundary case). Six open-weight thinking LRMs (DeepSeek-R1, Qwen-QwQ-32B, Qwen3-235B-Thinking, GLM-4.5-Air-FP8, gpt-oss-20b, gpt-oss-120b) are compared against DeepSeek-V3 as a non-thinking control and human reaction-time data.

The diagnostic combines (1) a within-agent pooled-SD Cohen's d on log deliberation (wrong − right), using each agent as its own baseline so seconds and tokens are never mixed, and (2) an item-fixed-effect interaction βLRM that absorbs the cross-item difficulty gradient and identifies the contrast purely from within-item variation. Robustness is probed via mixed-effects, Mundlak, and cluster-robust (by item and by agent) specifications, plus trace-content regressions for self-doubt / self-correction density. Deterministic seed 0xA12C; code and Makefile in an OSF deposit.

Per-LRM dissociation is rank-stable across paradigms (ρ = 0.77–0.89); Qwen-QwQ-32B dissociates most strongly everywhere. Wrong-trial H-ARC chains carry +0.43 self-doubt markers / 1k chars (β = −0.435, p = 7×10⁻⁴); human fast-wrong trials show median 16 grid actions vs 79 slow-wrong.

03Strengths

  • Two-level decomposition turns a vague "alignment" claim into separable, testable diagnostics.
  • Strong identification: item fixed effects remove the exact confound the original result rests on.
  • Honest scope: six open-weight models only, no frontier systems, no participant IDs.
  • Convergent mechanism work: action-count mediation (humans) + trace-content asymmetries (LRMs).

04Limitations

  • Observational: causal value of extra LRM tokens not established.
  • One observation per (item × model) — slope is item-controlled, not a per-model stopping policy.
  • No participant IDs → participant- vs trial-level engagement cannot be separated.
  • Cortes sign reversal (+0.31) is a real crack in the universality of the LRM-side story.
  • Closed-frontier (o-series, Gemini) systems entirely untested.

05Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the controlled truncation experiment (f ∈ 0.5–0.8) yield an early high-accuracy plateau (length-padding) or a late steep rise (useful search)?
  2. Do closed-frontier reasoning systems reproduce the within-agent allocation gap, or is it specific to open-weight RL-trained thinking models?
  3. With stochastic multi-sample data per (item × model), can the per-model stopping policy be estimated directly?
  4. Is the wrong-trial length expansion driven by RL training signals / verifier rewards rather than task accuracy — and does it vanish under length-penalty training?
  5. Can participant-level human data confirm the engagement-vs-abandonment schedule at the individual level, beyond H-ARC's grid-action paradigm?
