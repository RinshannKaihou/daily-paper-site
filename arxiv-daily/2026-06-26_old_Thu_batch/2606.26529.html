The Inattentional Gap: Task-Conditioned Language and Vision Models Omit the Safety-Critical Signals They Can Otherwise Report

Kwan Soo Shin (PolymathMinds Lab, Seoul) · 2026-06-25

ai-safety inattentional-blindness llm-evaluation vision-language-models radiology-ai
Key Metric
0.00
report rate of the co-present critical signal under a strict task instruction — vs. ~1.00 in the open condition (McNemar p = 1.1×10−19, N = 64).
Why This Matters

Task-conditioning is the deployment norm, and it silently decouples benchmark safety from real-world safety — a high D_specified score can hide a fatal unreported finding.

Critical Edge

Single author, only 100 text + 48 vision items, and LLM-judge adjudication without radiologist validation — the strong model-family claim rests on a thin, un-replicated sample.

Methodology

Six within-item experiments measure the report rate of a co-present safety-critical signal under a narrow vs. an open instruction, holding the stimulus identical. Study 1 (language) uses 100 procedurally composed text scenarios — 64 radiology (pulmonary nodule paired with a critical finding such as tension pneumothorax) and 36 driving (lead vehicle paired with a hazard such as a child entering the road) — across four models (claude-haiku-4-5, gpt-4o-mini, gpt-4o, claude-sonnet-4-6) under strict-exclusive, focused, and open instructions.

Study 2 (vision) composites a clearly visible non-anatomical object onto 12 public-domain chest radiographs (48 stimuli) and tests four vision models on a closed counting task vs. an open description, under a synthetic-stimulus system message to avoid medical-image refusals. Study 3 isolates output scope by appending 0–3 nodule sub-questions to an otherwise neutral request. Cross-vendor validation runs 24 scenarios on Opus 4.8, GPT-5, and Gemini 2.5 Pro; dual-process probes test Opus's override under adversarial suppression and an external critic's oversight.

Adjudication uses two independent LLM judges (Cohen κ 0.895–0.984); statistics are within-item exact McNemar tests with cluster-bootstrap robustness over template families and temperature-0 stability re-runs. All artefacts are deposited under CC-BY 4.0 on Zenodo.

Assessment

✓ Strengths

  • Falsifiable construct with a built-in within-item open-condition control that separates suppression from capability failure.
  • Cross-modal (text + vision), cross-domain (radiology + driving), cross-vendor (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google) coverage.
  • Unusually transparent: full Zenodo deposit of code, stimuli, raw outputs, and judge prompts under CC-BY 4.0.
  • Actionable for safety evaluation — external-critic dual-process scaffold, not just a diagnostic.
  • Honest handling of refusals, reasoning-budget attrition, and temperature-0 non-determinism.

⚠ Limitations

  • Small absolute scale (100 text, 48 vision); needs replication with real, multi-finding pathology.
  • LLM-judge adjudication with no blinded human / radiologist validation; construct is behavioural, not yet causal.
  • Model-family claim rests on a limited sample and may reflect vendor RLHF policies, not architecture.
  • GPT-5 focused comparison survives on only 9 paired items after reasoning-budget attrition.
  • No human baseline in the same paradigm — the “analogue” claim is behavioural only.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the gap hold on real, multi-finding clinical images with radiologist-adjudicated ground truth, and how large is it vs. human satisfaction-of-search error in the same paradigm?
  2. Is the OpenAI-suppresses / Anthropic-overrides pattern a stable architectural property or a transient artifact of current RLHF safety-reporting policies?
  3. Can the external-critic scaffold scale to streaming perception (e.g. autonomous driving) without prohibitive latency or its own omission modes?
  4. What is the causal decomposition into task load, signal salience, output scope, and family disposition — and can output-budget shaping close the gap without hurting task fidelity?
  5. How does the gap compose across multi-step agentic pipelines — does chaining narrow nodes amplify or dilute the omission?
  6. Could a unified benchmark scoring D_specified and D_unspecified jointly predict deployment harm better than today's targeted benchmarks?
