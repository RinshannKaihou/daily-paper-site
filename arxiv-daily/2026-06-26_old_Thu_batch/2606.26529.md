> **一句话总结：** 本文发现，当大语言/视觉模型被一个狭窄的任务指令所"锚定"时，会系统性地抑制其本可报告的、共同存在的安全关键信号（如影像中并存的生命危险征象），这种"非注意间隙"（Inattentional Gap）不受模型规模改善，在推理模型（GPT-5）中依然存在，主要由模型家族而非规模决定——它使得基准安全分与真实部署安全脱钩。

> **TL;DR:** Conditioning a language or vision-language model on a narrow task suppresses its reporting of co-present, safety-critical signals it can otherwise report when unconstrained. This "Inattentional Gap" — a machine analogue of human inattentional blindness with a different mechanism — appears in every model tested, does not diminish with scale, persists in the reasoning model GPT-5, varies more by model family than by size, and decouples benchmark safety scores from real-world deployment safety.

## 核心贡献

- **首次形式化定义"非注意间隙"（Inattentional Gap）**：提出一种行为学构念，刻画模型在狭窄任务条件下报告的信号与同一模型在开放条件下报告的信号之间的差异，并用"项内可报告性控制"（within-item reportability check）将任务诱发的遗漏与单纯的能力缺陷区分开来。
- **跨模态、跨领域的实证证据**：在放射学/驾驶文本场景（100例）和胸部X光视觉任务（48例合成刺激）中，对4个模型展示了任务条件化对安全关键信号报告的抑制，并通过独立双法官 adjudication（Cohen κ 0.90–0.98）保证评分可信。
- **跨厂商旗舰模型验证**：在 Opus 4.8、GPT-5、Gemini 2.5 Pro 上复现，发现抑制效应在 GPT-5（推理模型）上依然完全存在，从而驳斥"扩展或推理可消除该间隙"的假设。
- **定位触发机制为"输出范围"而非单调的负载梯度**：单个狭窄子问题即可将抑制几乎完全打开；输出长度分析表明是"模型承诺报告的广度"而非问题数量本身驱动抑制。
- **双系统探针揭示 System-1 式任务捕获**：可靠的边界监督不会随架构普遍出现，而是表现为模型家族特有的安全报告倾向（如 Opus 4.8 的强健 override）或显式第二过程（外部 critic 将报告率从 0.00 提升至 1.00）。
- **方法学与文献学贡献**：对 116 篇相关工作沿 11 个轴编码，定位并填补了"在机器上同时使用任务条件化操纵与项内可报告性控制"这一空缺象限。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes the Inattentional Gap** as a behavioral construct: the divergence between what a model reports under a narrow task instruction versus what the same model reports when unconstrained, with a within-item reportability control (conditioning on open-condition reportability) that separates task-induced omission from mere capability failure.
- **Demonstrates the gap across two modalities and two safety-critical domains** — radiology/driving text (100 scenarios) and chest-radiograph vision (48 composited stimuli) — using a controlled attentional-set manipulation and two independent LLM judges with high agreement (Cohen κ 0.895–0.984).
- **Validates across flagship models from three vendors** (Opus 4.8, GPT-5, Gemini 2.5 Pro), showing the gap persists in the reasoning model GPT-5 and is therefore not eliminated by scale or inference-time reasoning.
- **Localizes the proximal trigger to output scope** rather than a monotonic cognitive-load gradient: a single narrow sub-question flips suppression almost fully on, and response-length analysis implicates the breadth of committed output as the operative variable.
- **Frames the mechanism as System-1-style task capture** without reliable architecture-general oversight: monitoring appears only as a model-family-specific safety-reporting disposition (Opus 4.8's robust override) or as an explicit second process (an external critic raising report rate from 0.00 to 1.00).
- **Reframes the central safety question** from "can the model identify the signal?" to "does task-conditioning prevent the model from reporting signals it can otherwise report?", decoupling measured benchmark safety (D_specified) from deployment safety (D_unspecified).
- **Provides a structured positioning map** of 116 prior works across eleven axes, explicitly filling a previously empty machine-side cell at the intersection of attentional-set manipulation and within-item reportability control.

## Methodology

The study runs six experiments, every comparison made within-item with report of the co-present safety-critical signal as the dependent measure.

- **Study 1 (Language):** 100 procedurally composed text scenarios (64 radiology pairing a pulmonary nodule with a co-present critical finding like tension pneumothorax; 36 driving pairing a lead vehicle with a hazard like a child entering the road). Each scenario was presented to four models (claude-haiku-4-5, gpt-4o-mini, gpt-4o, claude-sonnet-4-6) under three instructions: a high-load focused instruction, a strict exclusive instruction, and an open instruction. Adjudication by two independent LLM judges (Cohen κ = 0.895). Procedural composition is used to reduce data-contamination concerns of canonical vignettes.
- **Study 2 (Vision):** 12 public-domain chest radiographs, each composited with a clearly visible non-anatomical object (subtle translucent disc or yellow star) at left/right lung field, yielding 48 stimuli. Four vision-capable models were tested under a closed counting task ("carefully count the pairs of ribs") versus an open description task, under a synthetic-stimulus system message to avoid medical-image refusals. Two-judge adjudication (κ = 0.918).
- **Study 3 (Output scope):** 24 radiology scenarios presented with 0–3 nodule-directed sub-questions appended to a neutral request (0 = open), to isolate output scope as the trigger (κ = 0.984); response-length analysis correlated suppression with committed output breadth.
- **Cross-vendor flagship validation:** The same 24 scenarios run on Opus 4.8, GPT-5, Gemini 2.5 Pro (κ = 0.969). GPT-5's reasoning-budget attrition handled by excluding empty responses from paired McNemar comparisons.
- **Dual-process probes:** (α) Opus 4.8 under standard focus, adversarial-suppression (explicitly forbidding other findings/safety caveats), and open, to test whether its override is a stable disposition or fragile reflex; (β) gpt-4o-mini's narrow report routed to an independent open-ended critic (claude-sonnet-4-6) to test external oversight.
- **Statistics:** Within-item exact McNemar tests for closed-vs-open; cluster bootstrap resampling nodule and critical-finding templates to address template clustering; stability checked across temperature-0 re-runs. All models queried June 2026 via production APIs at temperature 0 (except Opus 4.8 and GPT-5). Code, stimuli, raw outputs, and judge prompts deposited under CC-BY 4.0 on Zenodo (https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20826824).

## Results & Highlights

- **Strict instruction produced complete, universal suppression:** every model reported the critical signal in 0.00 of radiology strict-condition items versus ~1.00 in the open condition (McNemar p = 1.1e-19, N = 64); driving 0.08–0.33 vs 1.00 (p < 1.3e-7).
- **Focused instruction produced substantial radiology suppression** (0.08–0.53 vs ~1.00, all p < 2e-9) and weaker driving suppression (0.58–0.97), establishing salience and task-load as boundary conditions.
- **No scale benefit:** gpt-4o suppressed radiology findings as strongly as the small gpt-4o-mini (0.08 vs 0.09).
- **Vision gap replicated in every model:** gpt-4o omitted the star in every counting-task item while reporting it in nearly all open items (McNemar p < 1e-6, b=21, c=0) — a machine analogue of the invisible-gorilla result.
- **Cross-vendor pattern held and sharpened:** GPT-5 showed complete suppression in valid responses (0.00 vs open 1.00; strict p = 9.5e-7); Gemini 2.5 Pro suppressed nearly as strongly (0.00–0.09; p < 1e-6); Opus 4.8 was robust (0.83–0.96 task vs 1.00 open), explicitly prefacing reports with "a critical finding that I can't ethically ignore."
- **Output scope, not load gradient:** report rate collapsed from 1.00 at zero sub-questions to near zero at one; Anthropic models partially recovered as output widened while OpenAI models stayed suppressed.
- **External critic fully closed the gap:** routing gpt-4o-mini reports to an independent critic raised report rate from 0.00 to 1.00 (McNemar p = 1.2e-7), reproduced exactly across temperature-0 re-runs.
- **Model family > scale:** across sampled systems, family was a stronger correlate of the gap than size.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high):** The construct genuinely fills a previously unoccupied cell — prior machine studies (SalBench, "VLMs are blind," AbsenceBench, object-hallucination benchmarks) measure capability under direct query or the opposite error (reporting what's absent), while this work manipulates an attentional set with an open/closed within-item reportability control. The reframing from "can it detect?" to "does task-conditioning suppress what it can report?" is a substantive conceptual move, not a relabeling.
- **Rigor (mixed):** Strengths include two-judge adjudication with reported κ, within-item exact McNemar tests, cluster-bootstrap and re-run robustness checks. Weaknesses include small absolute scale (100 text and 48 vision items), use of procedurally composed rather than naturally occurring clinical stimuli (acknowledged), and LLM-judge adjudication without blinded human/domain-expert validation (acknowledged as a needed next step). The GPT-5 paired-n of 9 in the focused condition after reasoning-budget attrition is a small sample for a headline claim, though the strict condition (n=21) is more solid.
- **Reproducibility (strong):** Deposited code, stimuli, raw outputs, and judge prompts on Zenodo under CC-BY 4.0 substantially exceed field norms. The caveat that commercial APIs may drift is handled by treating deposited raw outputs as the authoritative evidence base — an honest and defensible choice.
- **Significance (high if it generalizes):** The core thesis — that benchmark safety and deployment safety can decouple at the within-item reporting level — is directly actionable for safety evaluation design and is independent of any single result's magnitude. The finding that reasoning (GPT-5) does not resolve the gap is particularly important and counterintuitive.
- **Red flags / caveats:** (1) Single author concentrates verification in one person despite the AI-assistance disclosure; independent replication is desirable. (2) The strong model-family claim (OpenAI suppresses more than Anthropic) rests on a limited model sample and could be confounded by undisclosed vendor RLHF/safety-reporting alignment policies rather than a general architectural property — the paper is careful to call this a "model-family-associated safety-reporting disposition" rather than a proven mechanism, but readers may over-read it. (3) The Opus 4.8 override (reporting 0.96 even under an adversarial instruction forbidding other findings) is striking but confined to 24 items and one model; generalization is unproven. (4) Vision stimuli used composited non-anatomical objects, which isolates mechanism but sacrifices clinical realism; a pilot on real pathology could only be reported qualitatively due to judge disagreement and refusals. (5) Adversarial-suppression being "scored as task failure" introduces a confound between the instruction's literal content and the scoring rubric. (6) There is no human baseline run in the same paradigm for direct machine-vs-human magnitude comparison. None of these invalidate the core finding, but they bound its strength.

## Strengths

- Clear, falsifiable behavioral construct with a built-in control (within-item open-condition reportability) that elegantly separates suppression from capability failure.
- Cross-modal (text + vision), cross-domain (radiology + driving), and cross-vendor (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google) coverage that strengthens generality.
- Honest and unusually thorough limitations section; transparent handling of refusals, reasoning-budget attrition, and temperature-0 non-determinism.
- Strong reproducibility commitment: full deposit on Zenodo with raw outputs treated as the authoritative evidence base.
- Actionable framing for AI safety evaluation and deployment (dual-process scaffold, external critic), not just a diagnostic finding.
- Nuanced dual-process analysis that resists the easy "AI is like humans" or "AI is alien" binaries and locates the effect in task-conditioning of report.

## Limitations

- Small absolute stimulus scale (100 text, 48 vision); effects should be replicated at field scale, especially with real co-present pathology rather than composited objects.
- Adjudication relied on two LLM judges without blinded human or domain-expert (radiologist) validation; the construct is demonstrated behaviorally but not yet causally decomposed.
- The dominant model-family finding is based on a limited sample and may reflect vendor-specific alignment policies rather than a general mechanism.
- GPT-5's focused-condition comparison rests on only 9 paired items after reasoning-budget attrition.
- A pilot on real radiographs with genuine findings could only be reported qualitatively due to judge disagreement and vision refusals.
- No direct human baseline in the same within-item paradigm for magnitude comparison; the machine analogue claim is behavioral, not mechanistic.
- Dual-process probes (24 items each) are demonstrations rather than full benchmarks; the external-critic scaffold and the Opus override are not yet shown to generalize broadly.

## 相关性

这一发现对医疗影像 AI、自动驾驶感知与安全评测设计具有直接警示意义：任务条件化是真实部署的常态，而它恰恰是抑制"未指定但安全关键"信号的根源，提示评测体系必须显式度量并保护 D_unspecified，而非仅追求 D_specified 上的高分。对中国从事大模型安全对齐与医疗 AI 的研究者而言，这是一个高优先级、可立即展开的评测与缓解研究方向。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the Inattentional Gap hold on real, multi-finding clinical images with radiologist-adjudicated ground truth, and how large is it relative to human satisfaction-of-search error in the same paradigm?
- Is the OpenAI-suppresses-more / Anthropic-override pattern a stable architectural property or a transient artifact of current RLHF/safety-reporting policies that vendors could change in a release?
- Can the external-critic dual-process scaffold scale to vision and to streaming perception (e.g., autonomous driving) without prohibitive latency/cost, and does it introduce its own omission modes?
- What is the causal decomposition of suppression into task load, signal salience, output scope, and family disposition, and can mechanistic interventions (e.g., output-budget shaping, system-prompt priming) reduce the gap without harming task fidelity?
- How does the gap interact with agentic, multi-step systems where a narrow sub-task is one node in a larger pipeline — does composition amplify or dilute the omission?
- Could a single unified safety-reporting benchmark be designed that scores D_specified and D_unspecified jointly, and would it predict deployment harm better than current targeted benchmarks?
