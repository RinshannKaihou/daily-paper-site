Key Finding

Neural expressivity is governed by algorithmic circuit complexity, not smoothness alone — any non-affine definable network is a universal approximator.

Algorithmic Foundations of Deep Learning: Complexity-Theoretic Rates and a Characterization of Universal Approximation · Anastasis Kratsios, Simone Brugiapaglia, Bum Jun Kim et al. · 2026-06-26

Why This Matters
Reframes deep-learning expressivity around circuit complexity, handing practitioners a clean message: approximation ability is a baseline, not a differentiator.
Critical Edge
The theory guarantees existence of parameter settings — not that gradient descent finds them. The headline gains also require already knowing an efficient circuit for the target.

MMethodology

Pure theory — no experiments. Three pillars built on o-minimal tameness, real-valued G-circuits, and abstract surgery for gate emulation.

  • O-minimal definability as the tameness framework (semi-algebraic, restricted-analytic, exp). Modern MLPs/ResNets/transformers are definable per Assumption 2.4.
  • G-circuits (gates: +, ×, ÷, radicals, exp, log, max) model real-valued computation; depth = stages, size = gates. Catalogues Floyd–Warshall, Newton–Raphson, power iteration, splines.
  • Abstract surgery (Thm 3.2) replaces each gate with a small neural block preserving wiring; Lemma 6.7 ties non-piecewise-linearity to a non-vanishing 2nd-order partial, enabling local x² emulators.
  • Black-box (regularity only) / grey-box (circuit complexity known) / white-box (explicit algorithm) hierarchy.

Universality is generic, composition-stable, and covers attention + LayerNorm. Newton–Raphson, power iteration, quadrature and ODE solvers emulate at pseudocode cost. Phase diagram (Tables 3–4) ties cost jointly to regularity class and best-circuit complexity.

+Strengths

  • Unifies approximation theory, numerical analysis & TCS circuit complexity under one compilation theorem.
  • Architecture-agnostic: MLPs, ResNets, transformers with attention/LayerNorm.
  • Sharp qualitative characterization extending classical Leshno–Lin–Pinkus.
  • Minimax-optimal rates for Besov & holomorphic classes come "for free".
  • APSP showcase is a crisp, memorable exponential win over regularity bounds.

−Limitations

  • Purely theoretical; existence ≠ learnability — optimization/generalization out of scope.
  • Strongest gains need white-/grey-box circuit knowledge; black-box recovers only regularity bounds.
  • Headline exponential gain is a single example; breadth across realistic tasks unestablished.
  • o-minimal definability excludes some functions (global sin) and adds a tameness assumption.
  • Asymptotic bounds hide constants & gate-language dependence; 86-page entry barrier.

?Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can compiled parameter settings (Thm 3.2) be found by gradient descent? Does definability tie to optimizability?
  • Do neural nonlinearities (attention, pooling) ever beat their compiled circuit costs — true complexity separations?
  • How broad is the APSP-style win across natural ML targets (kernels, attention patterns, normalization)?
  • Does o-minimal definability restrict practical architectures (rotary embeddings, unbounded sin/cos positional encodings)?
  • Can a real "neural compiler" be built and benchmarked on CLRS-style algorithmic-reasoning tasks?
  • Do minimax-rate recoveries transfer to comparable sample complexity, or does architecture dominate there?
