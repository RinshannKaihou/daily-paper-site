Why This Matters

Explains why representation similarity fails for shared-vocabulary LLMs and yields a 16 KB, streaming, training-free compatibility signal for LoRA source selection and routing.

Critical Edge

On natural shifts FisherSketch is only competitive with — not dominant over — the activation-only marginal; its advantage is concentrated in the narrow activation-dark regime.

01 Methodology

The paper rests on three observations. (1) Kronecker gradient structure (Lemma 4.1): for an affine head with input activation a and backpropagated error e, the per-example weight gradient is ghead = a ⊗ e.

(2) Product-kernel rewrite (Theorem 4.2): the head Fisher inner product ⟨Fi,Fj⟩F = E[(aTa′)2(eTe′)2] is the expectation of a degree-4 product polynomial kernel, so alignment becomes cos(μae,i, μae,j) of kernel mean embeddings. This yields the exact three-factor identity AF = Scov(Ma) · Scov(Γe) · ρ, with ρ the non-separability ratio.

(3) FisherSketch (Proposition 5.1): with independent Rademacher vectors, the sketch coordinate ψ(a,e) = (raTa)(reTe)(r′aTa)(r′eTe) is unbiased for the product kernel at O(m(d+K)) per sample. At vocabulary scale, an SRHT (Walsh–Hadamard) backend replaces dense Rademacher matrices on the K-dim error side. Stabilization uses split-half log-ratio estimation, empirical-Bayes shrinkage, and U-statistic diagonal correction.

Each task compresses from ~61 GiB to a 16 KB fp32 signature (m=4096); alignments reduce to dot products of ℓ2-normalized signatures.

Strengths

  • Clean theory-to-method arc: non-identifiability → product-kernel identity → streaming estimator, each motivating the next.
  • Explicitly honest about when it wins (activation-dark) vs. when activation-only is competitive — no overclaiming.
  • Tiny footprint: 16 KB signature, single streaming pass — deployable next to a model hash.
  • Three-factor decomposition (activation, error, coupling) is an interpretable diagnostic, not just a scalar.
  • Validation spans ResNet, ViT, Llama-3.1-8B across vision, NLP, and SMILES domains.

Limitations

  • Requires a shared output basis; heterogeneous-output tasks need a transport/embedding map.
  • Head-level only; full-network or shared-parameter extension is less exact.
  • Empirical Fisher at a single checkpoint; signatures are checkpoint-conditional and LoRA-specific.
  • On natural shifts it does not strictly dominate simpler marginals — value is regime-specific.
  • Single author; larger models, more adaptation methods, full-network scale left to future work.

Open Questions

Q1How does FisherSketch behave at 70B+ scale and beyond LoRA — full fine-tuning or QLoRA?
Q2How sensitive are signatures to checkpoint choice, and can they be averaged across checkpoints?
Q3Can the decomposition extend to per-layer, full-network FisherSketch at O(m(d+K)) cost?
Q4How common is the activation-dark regime in real adapter/model zoos — edge case or typical?
Q5Could the coupling term ρ drive interference-aware continual learning or gradient surgery?
Q6How does it compare to LogME/LEEP and influence methods when a label taxonomy exists?
