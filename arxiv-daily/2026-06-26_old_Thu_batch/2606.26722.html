Socratic agents for autonomous scientific discovery in high-dimensional physical systems

Xianrui Zeng, Pengfei Liu, Yirui Zang, Yang Shen, Fei Yu, et al. ·2026-06-25 ·cs.AI, physics.optics ·arXiv:2606.26722 (v1)
Key finding AHOIS embeds a constrained Socratic physics-critic agent into closed-loop multimode-fibre experiments, turning brittle reasoning into testable, revisable mechanistic models.
Key metric
48.50fps
Adaptive sparse scanning keeps video-rate recognition at 48.50 fps versus 0.47 fps for full scanning — over 100× faster, evidence-directed not fixed subsampling.
Why this matters

It engineers Socratic midwifery into a computable self-critique loop for research agents on real hardware — explicitly stating assumptions and falsification conditions, a template for trustworthy AI-for-science.

Critical edge
evaluability

The same LLM family that generates hypotheses also underpins the critic and the scoring — so the headline "Socratic gains" may reflect stylistic conformity rather than deeper physical correctness.

Methodology

Architecture. Five roles cooperate through a shared scientific state that records, at each step, the current hypothesis, physical assumptions, proposed action, expected observation, uncertainty and returned evidence: Boxue (strategy/hypothesis), Gewu (hardware abstraction & execution), Mingde (integrity monitoring), Qiushi (modelling/analysis) and Duzhi (Socratic critic). The epistemic loop sits between Boxue and Duzhi, which only interrogates — never supplies conclusions.

Socratic interrogation (4 levels). (1) Clarification — define physical meaning, separate measured vs inferred vs assumed. (2) Constraint checking — optical principles and hardware limits (energy, coherence, multimode propagation, detector response). (3) Causal probing — the full chain modulation → propagation → object interaction → collection → measurement. (4) Counterexample — does the explanation survive changed object, modality, sampling or stability conditions.

Physical testbed. A real multimode-fibre bench: a laser shaped by a DMD (active 624×624) via binary Lee holograms, a 100 µm / 0.22 NA step-index illumination MMF calibrated by phase-stepped interferometry (~7,500 input patterns; ~173,000 output positions), an adjacent collection MMF, with both far-field coherent and fluorescence modalities. A human scientist supervises only at predefined safety/quality checkpoints; every state is logged.

Experiments. Backscattered random-interference encoding; adaptive coarse-to-fine sparse scanning; modality-specific failure diagnosis (SCNet vs MFNet); paper-to-platform protocol reproduction with a U-Net → residual-attention reconstruction pipeline; and four-configuration Socratic ablations.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Sharp conceptual thesis: epistemic vs procedural autonomy via Socratic midwifery.
  • Runs on real high-dimensional hardware with genuine ambiguity, not a simulator.
  • The critic-only constraint is principled; ablation cleanly separates form from amount of criticism.
  • Modular, logged, replayable architecture — valuable for trustworthy AI-for-science.
  • Diverse emergent behaviors (encoding, sampling, diagnosis, transfer) from one open prompt.
  • Honest about limits: natural-language questioning, human supervision, no new physical laws.

Limitations

  • Socratic interrogation is natural-language; no formal/causal-graph grounding yet.
  • Human supervision + researcher-run large-scale acquisition remain — not fully autonomous end-to-end.
  • Invents no new physical laws; bounded to mechanistic explanation within a known domain.
  • Reasoning-quality rubrics lack established annotator reliability / model-independence.
  • Single-platform demonstration; transferability is argued, not demonstrated.
  • Token costs reported, but economics of repeated closed-loop interrogation unanalyzed.

Questions for further exploration

  1. Can the Socratic critic be grounded in formal representations (causal graphs, symbolic optical models, machine-verifiable protocols) rather than natural language — and would that change the measured gains?
  2. How robust are the reasoning-quality rubric scores to the underlying LLM, scorer, and inter-annotator agreement — deeper physical correctness or stylistic conformity?
  3. How does AHOIS behave when its initial physical model is fundamentally wrong — does interrogation recover from a wrong paradigm, or only refine within one?
  4. What is the marginal cost/benefit of each questioning round beyond seven, and where does it plateau or over-regularize?
  5. Can transferability be tested on a genuinely different platform (scattering imaging, photonic processor, autonomous synthesis)?
  6. How should the boundary between "autonomous reasoning" and "human/exhaustive robotic labour" be quantified and benchmarked?
  7. Could the discovered backscattered-interference encoding be benchmarked against published specialist speckle-encoding methods on identical data, not only the system's own ablations?
