Key Finding

Post-training ternary (1.58-bit) quantization that finally matches quantization-aware training — using only 512 calibration samples.

LLM quantization ternary / 1.58-bit post-training PTQ model compression
CAT-Q: Cost-efficient and Accurate Ternary Quantization for LLMs ↗
Shigeng Wang · Chao Li · Yangyuxuan Kang · Jiawei Fan · Anbang Yao | Intel Labs China | ICML 2026 (PMLR 306) | 2026-06-25
Key Metric tokens

~100,000×

fewer training tokens than BitNet 1.58-bit v1/v2 (100B-token QAT), at matched-or-better accuracy.
512 calibration samples ≈ 1M tokens · 1.7B–235B models
Why This Matters impact
Removes the costly QAT bottleneck, letting 100B–235B LLMs be deployed as 1.58-bit ternary models on commodity / edge hardware.
Critical Edge caveat
Evaluation spans only five commonsense benchmarks — no MMLU, GSM8K, code, or instruction-following, where 1.58-bit models are most fragile.

M Methodology

  • Sliding-window output reconstruction. Replaces naive per-layer argmin ||W−αT||² with SliderQuant-style window losses so neighbouring layers are jointly aware (first use of multi-layer sliding quantization for ternary LLMs).
  • Learnable Modulation (LM). Three learnable factors (δµ, δα, δΔ) re-map weight mean, scale α and threshold Δ to suppress outliers. A disentangled trick learns thresholds/ternary weights via a proxy, but reconstructs W ≈ αT (no µ) at inference to keep hardware-friendly TWN form.
  • Softened Ternarization (ST). A differentiable transition f(·) anneals from identity → softened ternarization over m=60 epochs (γ=0.8, sharpness s₀=30), then relays into hard ternarization — curing the STE convergence failure of prior PTQ ternarization.
  • Calibration. 512 C4 samples × 2048 tokens drive the window objective; deploy as W ≈ αT with integer add/sub (>10× memory cut vs FP16).

+ Strengths

  • Clean two-component design (LM + ST) with strong ablations proving each piece contributes.
  • First to push PTQ ternarization to 235B and large MoE — a real scalability milestone.
  • Honest about hyperparameters and compute; code is public (BitTern).
  • Sensible disentangled W ≈ αT inference preserves hardware friendliness.

! Limitations

  • Only 5 commonsense benchmarks — no perplexity, MMLU, math, code, or instruction-following.
  • “100,000× token reduction” compares PTQ-from-pretrained against from-scratch QAT — not strictly matched.
  • No measured inference latency/throughput on ternary hardware; speedup is argued, not shown.
  • Largest absolute accuracy gap appears at 235B (MoE), undercutting the “scales gracefully” narrative.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does CAT-Q behave on perplexity, MMLU, GSM8K, HumanEval and IFEval/MT-Bench, where ternary models degrade most?
  2. Can the 100,000× claim be validated against a QAT baseline that re-trains a pretrained LLM (e.g. Tequila) at matched scale (8B/14B)?
  3. What are real measured latency, throughput and energy on actual ternary-arithmetic hardware — and how does the 235B MoE case behave on longer or expert-heavy tasks?
  4. Does the softened-ternarization anneal generalize to binary / mixed 1–2 bit regimes or to the weaker activation-quantization path?
  5. How sensitive is CAT-Q to calibration-domain shift (C4 vs. domain-specific or multilingual data)?
