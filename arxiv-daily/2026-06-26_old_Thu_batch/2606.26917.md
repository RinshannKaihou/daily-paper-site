> **一句话总结：** 本文发现在线 LLM 强化学习中存在一种"方向不一致"的几何失效模式（少数高奖励 rollout 在表征空间诱导出与批次共识尖锐冲突的更新方向），并提出仅前向传播、开销可忽略的 GeoAlign 插件：通过对每个 prompt 内的偏好对学习在线投影器，构造几何原型并用角度偏差评分替换方向性离群 rollout，从而在数学推理和对话对齐两类任务上同时提升最终性能与训练稳定性。

> **TL;DR:** GeoAlign identifies "directional inconsistency" — a failure mode where a few high-reward rollouts induce update directions that sharply conflict with batch consensus — and proposes a forward-pass-only, plug-and-play rollout curation module that learns an online projector to expose a concentrated "improvement manifold," scores rollouts by angular deviation from a consensus prototype (Geometric Deviation Index), and rectifies the worst outliers via within-prompt replacement, improving both final performance and training stability over PF-PPO, PAR, PODS, and Seed-GRPO on math reasoning (binary verified reward) and dialogue alignment (continuous reward model).

## 核心贡献

- **识别并刻画了一种几何失效模式"方向不一致"（directional inconsistency）**：在一个 RL 迭代内，少数（甚至高奖励的）rollout 在表征空间诱导出与批次共识形成尖锐角度离群的偏好方向，呈现长尾几何分布，会放大随机梯度的方差、导致训练震荡。这是对"仅把奖励当标量可靠性信号"的现有稳定化方法的补充视角。
- **提出 GeoAlign——轻量、即插即用的 rollout 整理（curation）模块**：仅使用与策略参数解耦的前向隐藏状态，通过 (i) 构建 prompt 内偏好对、(ii) 在线学习方向投影器浓缩"改进流形"、(iii) 构造批级几何原型并给出 GDI 评分、(iv) 用 KDE 自适应识别最严重的离群 rollout 并以 prompt 内稳定样本替换，实现保守干预且不改变每 prompt 的 rollout 数量。
- **跨任务、跨奖励结构、跨模型规模的验证**：在数学推理（DAPO-Math，二值验证奖励）和人类偏好对齐（HH-RLHF，ArmoRM 连续奖励）上、在 Qwen3-1.7B/4B 上，GeoAlign 在最终性能与训练稳定性上均优于 BASE-GRPO、PF-PPO、PAR、PODS、Seed-GRPO；并在受控的奖励腐蚀（5%/10% 错误注入）下保持最强鲁棒性。
- **方法学与可复现性**：提供完整代码（Trinity-RFT 框架，github.com/SYSUzhouting/Trinity-RFT）、超参细节与多处消融（投影器作用、累积 vs 归一化聚合、Anomaly-Amplify 反向验证等），并以"反向放大离群样本→性能下降"佐证方向不一致假设。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifies and formalizes "directional inconsistency"**, a geometric failure mode where a sparse set of (often high-reward) rollouts induces preference-implied update directions that are strong angular outliers relative to the batch consensus, exhibiting a long-tailed angular geometry that inflates gradient-variance and destabilizes online LLM RL. This reframes reliability as a *directional* property orthogonal to scalar reward magnitude.
- **Proposes GeoAlign, a lightweight, plug-and-play rollout curation module** that uses only detached forward-pass hidden states: it forms within-prompt preference pairs, learns an online directional projector (3-layer MLP, 50 steps/iteration) to distill a concentrated "improvement manifold," builds a reward-margin-weighted consensus prototype, scores rollouts with a Geometric Deviation Index (GDI), and conservatively rectifies only severe outliers via within-prompt replacement — no extra backward passes through the policy.
- **Empirical validation across reward structures, model scales, and tasks**: improves both final performance and training stability on DAPO-Math (binary verified reward) and HH-RLHF (continuous ArmoRM reward) on Qwen3-1.7B/4B, outperforming BASE-GRPO, PF-PPO, PAR, PODS, and Seed-GRPO, and remains the most robust method under controlled reward corruption (5% and 10% error injection).
- **Mechanistic and ablation evidence**: visualizes raw vs projected latent directions (projection concentrates ~90% of vectors above 0.8 cosine similarity with the prototype), shows GDI strongly correlates with injected reward errors, and uses an "Anomaly-Amplify" counterfactual (deliberately up-weighting flagged outliers) that degrades performance, supporting the central hypothesis.

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** Standard online RL fine-tuning of an LLM policy πθ: for a batch of prompts, K rollouts per prompt are generated, each receives a scalar reward r(y) (from a reward model or verifier), and an advantage is computed via intra-prompt normalization (e.g., GRPO): A(y) ∝ r(y) − E[r(y′)]. The authors argue scalar advantage alone is insufficient because two rollouts with comparable rewards can push the policy along sharply different directions.

**Latent displacement directions.** For each rollout y, take the pooled last-layer hidden state h(y) (detached from policy). For each within-prompt preference pair (y_w, y_l) with r(y_w) > r(y_l), define a displacement vector δ = h(y_w) − h(y_l) and the unit direction u = norm(δ). This u is a directional proxy for "improvement."

**Directional distillation (the projector).** Raw displacement directions are entangled with task-irrelevant linguistic variance. GeoAlign learns a small projector M_ψ: ℝ^d → ℝ^d′ (3-layer MLP with ReLU, trained for ~50 steps per iteration with cosine LR 1e-3, on a temporary linear probe w) via a logistic orientation-classification loss that classifies whether the projected direction is positively or negatively oriented w.r.t. the reward ordering. The probe w is discarded; M_ψ is kept for scoring. Projected unit directions v = norm(M_ψ(u)) become highly concentrated.

**Geometric prototype and GDI.** The consensus prototype is the reward-margin-weighted normalized centroid of projected directions (Eq. 5). The inconsistency score per pair is the cosine distance s = 1 − cos(v, v_proto) (Eq. 6). The Geometric Deviation Index for a rollout is the *cumulative* (not averaged) sum of s over all pairs involving it (Eq. 7) — cumulative aggregation is shown to better amplify consistently disruptive rollouts (validated in Table 4).

**Geometry-aware rectification.** GDI scores follow a long-tail distribution. KDE is used on the batch's GDI scores; a rollout is flagged if its density drops below α·max f̂ (α = 0.12 on HH-RLHF, 0.05 on Math). Flagged rollouts are replaced within the same prompt using a Best-Random strategy (Eq. 8): the highest-reward stable rollout gets weight 0.5, others split the remaining 0.5 — preserving per-prompt rollout count so the method acts as discrete reweighting for group-normalized objectives.

**Design principles.** Forward-pass only (no extra backward through πθ), update-density preservation (no prompts dropped, batch size unchanged), and budgeted intervention (only the worst angular outliers are touched).

## Results & Highlights

- **Math (DAPO-Math, binary verified reward):** GeoAlign achieves the best average across AIME24/25, AMC23, MATH500, Minerva, OlympiadBench — Qwen3-1.7B 40.44 (+1.6% rel. over BASE-GRPO 39.81), Qwen3-4B 55.94 (+2.1% rel. over 54.78), beating all robust baselines (PF-PPO BR/BW, PAR 39.33/54.97, PODS 39.58/55.13, Seed-GRPO 40.13/55.57).
- **Preference alignment (HH-RLHF, continuous ArmoRM reward, Qwen-MAX LLM judge):** Qwen3-1.7B 0.8885 (+6.4% rel. over BASE-GRPO 0.8354, surpassing strongest baseline PAR), Qwen3-4B 0.8894. Proxy reward curve is smoother and converges higher.
- **Robustness to reward corruption (Qwen3-1.7B):** Under 5% reward flipping (Math) and extreme reassignment (RLHF), GeoAlign retains the highest score at both 5% and 10% error levels (e.g., Math 40.44 → 38.20, RLHF proxy 0.0848 → 0.0833 → 0.0820), with the smallest degradation among all baselines.
- **GDI validity:** Intentionally corrupted rollouts (black crosses in Fig. 5) are disproportionately concentrated in the high-GDI region across tasks and noise levels, directly validating GDI as a reliability signal.
- **Projector effect (Fig. 6):** Raw displacement vectors are scattered with near-zero-centered cosine similarities; after projection, ~90% of vectors exceed 0.8 cosine similarity with the prototype. Held-out validation accuracy of the projector: median >85% (RLHF), >97% (Math), robust to depth (Table 7).
- **Anomaly-Amplify counterfactual (Table 4):** Deliberately up-weighting flagged outliers degrades Math to 39.71 (below baseline), supporting that directional outliers, not just low reward, are the problem.
- **Aggregation ablation:** cumulative GDI beats normalized (mean) aggregation on both tasks because it preserves disruption frequency.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high).** The framing of RL instability as a *directional/geometry* problem in representation space — rather than a scalar reward-magnitude problem — is genuinely fresh. The within-prompt preference-pair construction to factor out prompt-level confounding, combined with a lightweight learned projector to expose a concentrated improvement manifold, is a non-obvious and well-motivated idea. It connects RLHF stability to contrastive/representation-geometry literature in a way not previously explored.
- **Rigor (medium-high).** The paper offers a theoretical hook (linking directional conflict to mini-batch gradient variance Var(ĝ) ∝ E[‖g_τ − g‖²₂]) but explicitly concedes a *formal* connection between latent directional consensus and gradient variance is left to future work — so the theory is suggestive, not proven. Empirically, the ablations are reasonably thorough (projector necessity, cumulative vs normalized, Anomaly-Amplify counterfactual, depth sensitivity, α sensitivity). The Anomaly-Amplify experiment is a clever falsification-style check.
- **Reproducibility (high).** Code is public on a known framework; hyperparameters (α, projector depth/steps/LR, K, models, datasets, benchmarks, judge) are specified. Random seeds and per-seed dynamics are mentioned (Appendix C.3). The plug-in nature makes it easy to bolt onto existing GRPO pipelines.
- **Significance (medium-high).** If the directional-consensus signal generalizes, it is a cheap, orthogonal reliability signal that could complement — not replace — reward shaping, clipping, and uncertainty weighting. The practical adoption value is real for production RLHF. However, the absolute gains over the strongest baselines (Seed-GRPO, PAR) are modest (a few tenths of a point on Math; a couple points on RLHF), and several baselines are themselves within noise-range.
- **Red flags / caveats.**
  - *Marginal gains:* On Math the win over Seed-GRPO is ~0.3 pts on 1.7B and ~0.4 pts on 4B — within plausible seed variance unless variance bars (not shown in the main text) confirm separation. The paper claims per-seed analysis exists but headline tables report single numbers.
  - *Projector is doing real work:* Results hinge on the projector learning a generalizable signal; validation accuracy is high, but whether this holds for very different model families/scales beyond Qwen3 (1.7B/4B only) is untested.
  - *Reward-corruption test is somewhat artificial:* 5%/10% random flips or extreme reassignments may not match the subtler reward-hacking artifacts that occur in practice; the corruption correlates with GDI partly by construction.
  - *Replacement = reweighting:* For GRPO, the "rectification" reduces to reweighting, so the conceptual novelty partly collapses into a principled reweighting scheme — which is fine, but the "geometry-aware curation" framing is somewhat grander than the mechanism.
  - *Conflict of interest:* Evaluated models are the employer's own (Qwen3); while disclosed and mitigated, independent third-party reproduction on Llama/Mistral would strengthen claims.

## Strengths

- Fresh, well-motivated geometric reframing of RL instability; the directional-inconsistency failure mode is clearly defined and empirically visualized.
- Truly lightweight: forward-pass only, negligible overhead, plug-and-play with no architectural or RL-algorithm changes.
- Dual reward-structure validation (continuous RM + binary verifier) and dual model scales (1.7B, 4B) increase confidence beyond a single-setting demo.
- Honest counterfactual (Anomaly-Amplify) and projector ablations test rather than assume the hypothesis.
- Good transparency: COI disclosure, public code, conflict-of-interest acknowledgment.

## Limitations

- The theoretical link between latent directional consensus and gradient variance is asserted informally, not proven.
- Absolute improvements over the strongest baselines are modest and may sit within seed-level noise (variance bars absent in main tables).
- Only Qwen3-1.7B/4B evaluated; generalization to other model families and larger scales is untested.
- Reward-corruption benchmarks are synthetic; behavior under realistic, subtle reward hacking is unknown.
- Hyperparameters α (density threshold), projector steps, and K choices are task-specific and may need retuning; sensitivity is only partially explored (Appendix C.2).
- For GRPO the rectification reduces to reweighting, which slightly undercuts the "curation" novelty.

## 相关性

本论文与 LLM 对齐/RLHF 工程实践高度相关：它提供了一个开销极低、即插即用的方向性可靠性信号，可直接叠加到现有 GRPO/PPO 训练流水线以缓解奖励噪声与训练震荡，对奖励建模、强化学习微调与稳健对齐研究都有借鉴价值。其"表征空间几何"视角也为理解 RLHF 失效机理提供了新的分析工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does GeoAlign perform on non-Qwen model families (Llama, Mistral, Gemma) and at much larger scales (30B+), and does the projector's validation accuracy remain high?
- Can the informal link between GDI and mini-batch gradient variance be formalized — e.g., does GDI correlate with measured ‖g_τ − g‖² on held-out batches?
- Under realistic reward-hacking artifacts (not random flips), how predictive is GDI of genuinely misaligned rollouts?
- Does the learned projector transfer across iterations/tasks, or must it be re-fit every step; could a persistent amortized projector reduce overhead further?
- How does GeoAlign interact with process-reward / PRM-based RL and multi-turn / agentic settings where prompt-level confounding is larger?
- Is "replacement as reweighting" dominated by a simpler reweighting baseline calibrated to GDI, and does GeoAlign still win when the baseline pool is expanded?
