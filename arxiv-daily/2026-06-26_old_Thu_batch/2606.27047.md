> **一句话总结：** NuclearQAv2 提出了一个面向核工程领域的可扩展 LLM 评测基准，包含约 1,239 道覆盖事实（布尔）、定量（数值）与概念（简答）三类问题的 QA 题，并通过混合人工与 LLM 生成的方式构建，揭示了当前模型在定量推理与概念理解上的明显短板。

> **TL;DR:** NuclearQAv2 is a scalable, ~1,239-question benchmark for evaluating LLMs on nuclear engineering, split into boolean (factual), numeric (quantitative reasoning), and verbal (conceptual) tasks. Built via a hybrid pipeline of expert-authored questions, textbook exercises, and LLM-generated QA pairs, it is evaluated with task-specific metrics (exact match, tolerance-based accuracy, LLM-judge semantic matching). Frontier models do well on factual recall but struggle on numeric reasoning (Mistral 7B drops to 0.031), demonstrating the value of multi-faceted domain evaluation.

## 核心贡献
- 提出 NuclearQAv2，一个面向核工程的大规模（1,239 道 QA）LLM 评测基准，覆盖布尔（750）、数值（206）、简答（283）三类题型，分别对应事实记忆、定量推理与概念理解。
- 设计了一套混合构建管线，结合领域专家撰题、教材习题以及基于 Llama 3 70B 的 LLM 自动生成（通过 Nougat 解析 PDF 教材），在质量与可扩展性之间取得平衡。
- 构建了可自动化运行的评测框架：布尔题用精确匹配、数值题用容差准确率（ε=0.15）、简答题用 LLM-as-judge 进行语义等价判定，全部确定性、可复现。
- 实验评估了 9 个主流 LLM（含 GPT-5.2/5.4、gpt-oss-120B、Nemotron-3、Llama 3、Mistral 等），揭示出三类能力间的显著差异，强调单一指标评估会高估模型在技术领域的能力。

## Key Contributions
- Introduces NuclearQAv2, a ~1,239-QA nuclear engineering benchmark with three complementary question types (boolean 750, numeric 206, verbal 283) targeting factual recall, quantitative reasoning, and conceptual understanding separately.
- Develops a hybrid construction pipeline combining expert-authored questions, textbook exercises, and LLM-assisted generation (Llama 3 70B Instruct over a nuclear engineering textbook parsed with Nougat), balancing scalability with quality control.
- Proposes an automated, reproducible evaluation framework: exact-match accuracy for boolean, tolerance-based accuracy (ε=0.15) for numeric, and LLM-judge semantic equivalence for verbal answers.
- Benchmarks nine diverse LLMs and demonstrates that performance varies widely across task types, establishing NuclearQAv2 as a discriminating test of domain-science competence.

## Methodology
NuclearQAv2 is defined as a dataset D = {(q_i, a_i, t_i)} where each task has a question, a reference answer, and a type t ∈ {boolean, numeric, verbal}. Five construction principles enforce single-type assignment, domain relevance, unambiguous/context-independent answers, concise expected answers, and answerability without the source text. The dataset is built from a single nuclear engineering textbook using Nougat to extract structured text (including math). Boolean questions partly come from domain experts and textbook facts; numeric questions are drawn from textbook exercise problems (ensuring range of difficulty); verbal and remaining boolean questions are generated automatically by Llama 3 70B Instruct under structured prompts that encode the design principles, with a manual filtering/curation pass to remove invalid or ambiguous items. Evaluation uses type-specific metrics: normalized exact match for boolean, relative-tolerance accuracy (|â−a|/|a| ≤ 0.15) for numeric, and a fixed single LLM-judge prompt returning "correct"/"incorrect" for verbal answers based on semantic similarity with partial lexical overlap. Each model is evaluated three times to estimate mean accuracy and standard error.

## Results & Highlights
- Frontier models cluster near 0.8 on boolean tasks; GPT-5.2 leads (0.8022), closely followed by gpt-oss-120B, Nemotron-3 Super, and GPT-5.4. Mistral 7B (0.383) and Llama 3 8B (0.717) lag, showing factual recall is scale-sensitive.
- Numeric tasks are the hardest and show the widest spread: gpt-oss-120B tops at 0.7913, while Mistral 7B collapses to 0.0307; even strong factual models (GPT-5.2) drop to 0.665.
- Verbal tasks: GPT-5.2 leads at 0.8233; smaller open-weight models fall to ~0.49–0.52, indicating conceptual articulation benefits from scale.
- Aggregate accuracy (Table 2): gpt-oss-120B highest at 0.7923, with GPT-5.4 (0.7891), GPT-5.2 (0.7842), and Nemotron-3 (0.7840) close behind; Mistral 7B lowest at 0.3548.
- Key insight: factual recall is comparatively mature, but quantitative reasoning and conceptual understanding remain major differentiators; model scale alone does not close the boolean→numeric gap, suggesting need for domain fine-tuning or tool integration.

## Authors & Credibility
Authors are from Los Alamos National Laboratory (Henry Shaowu Yuchi, Michal Kucer, Benjamin H. Sims, with Selma Peterson and Emily Taylor), a US national lab with deep nuclear science and engineering expertise — lending strong domain credibility to the benchmark's content and the involvement of genuine domain experts in question authoring and review. The work builds directly on the prior NuclearQA benchmark (Acharya et al., 2023), positioning NuclearQAv2 as a deliberate, larger-scale successor.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** Moderate. The three-type taxonomy (boolean/numeric/verbal) and the hybrid "expert + LLM-generated from a single textbook" pipeline are sensible but not conceptually new; the novelty is mostly the application to the nuclear domain at a larger scale than NuclearQA (1,239 vs 100 items). Hybrid LLM-assisted benchmark construction is now well-trodden territory.
- **Rigor:** Reasonable but limited. Numeric accuracy uses a fairly loose 15% relative tolerance, which may mask genuine errors or reward luck; the verbal LLM-judge is acknowledged to be prompt- and judge-model-sensitive with no reported calibration against human grading. All three runs of each model yield very tight standard errors, which is plausible but the experimental protocol (prompting details, temperature, decoding, zero-shot vs few-shot) is under-specified in the methodology.
- **Reproducibility:** Mixed. Evaluation is deterministic given outputs and a fixed judge, but the dataset is not stated to be publicly released, the filtering step is manual, and exact prompt templates for question generation are only partially described. The set of evaluated models includes proprietary systems (GPT-5.2/5.4) whose behavior may drift.
- **Significance:** Niche but meaningful for the nuclear-engineering and high-consequence-domains community; broader ML relevance is moderate given the small dataset size and reliance on a single textbook.
- **Red flags:** Single-source textbook corpus limits topical breadth and risks leakage of answers into model training data; the boolean set (750 of 1,239) is heavily weighted toward the easiest task type, which inflates aggregate scores and may flatter models; small absolute numbers of numeric (206) and verbal (283) items limit statistical power; LLM-generated questions were used both to build the benchmark (Llama 3 70B) and to judge it, introducing a potential circularity/bias, especially for verbal evaluation. The claim of "domain-science competence" is strong given the narrow source base.

## Strengths
- Clear, well-motivated three-axis decomposition of competence (factual / quantitative / conceptual) that surfaces differences invisible in single-format benchmarks.
- Genuinely hybrid and scalable construction that combines expert quality with automated coverage, plus a mostly-automated, reproducible evaluation protocol.
- Strong domain credibility from Los Alamos authors and real expert involvement.
- Honest, multi-row results table across nine diverse and current models, including both proprietary frontier and open-weight systems.
- Thoughtful design principles (context-independent, unambiguous, concise answers) that make automatic grading feasible.

## Limitations
- Dataset built from a single nuclear engineering textbook, restricting breadth and exposing potential train/test contamination.
- Imbalanced composition (60% boolean) inflates aggregate accuracy and underrepresents the harder numeric/verbal capabilities the paper most cares about.
- Numeric tolerance (ε=0.15) is generous; verbal grading depends on one LLM judge with no reported human agreement/calibration.
- Text-only; no multimodal items (diagrams, schematics, plots) despite their importance in nuclear engineering.
- Under-specified experimental setup (prompting, decoding, shot count); benchmark release status unclear.
- Generation model (Llama 3 70B) overlaps conceptually with evaluation model, risking evaluator bias; manual filtering is non-scalable.

## Relevance
该工作对在高风险、强技术领域（如核工程、能源、安全监管）部署 LLM 具有直接参考价值，提示研究者与工程师在评测模型时不应只看整体准确率，而应分维度考察定量推理与概念理解能力；对于做领域 benchmark、LLM 评测自动化与可靠性研究的方向也有方法论借鉴意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- How does the 15% numeric tolerance affect conclusions — would stricter tolerances reorder the leaderboard, and do wrong numeric answers reflect formula errors, unit errors, or arithmetic slips?
- What is the inter-annotator / model-vs-human agreement of the LLM verbal judge, and how sensitive are verbal scores to judge-model and prompt wording?
- Is there evidence of training-data contamination from the (publicly available) textbook, and how do results change with held-out or paraphrased questions?
- Can the benchmark be extended to multimodal nuclear content (reactor schematics, decay curves) and to tool-augmented / retrieval-augmented evaluation?
- How does weighting (e.g., equal per-type weighting instead of count-weighting) change aggregate rankings given the boolean-heavy composition?
