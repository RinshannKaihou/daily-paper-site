> **一句话总结：** 本文指出当前自演化多模态大模型存在"视觉欠条件化"问题（解码器依赖语言先验而非图像），并提出 VISE 框架，仅用两张几何不变性和语义不变性奖励信号在无标注图像上单模型自演化，使解码器在生成时真正关注视觉 token，在 18 个基准上取得一致性提升。

> **TL;DR:** Existing self-evolving LMMs optimize answer agreement, letting decoders lean on language priors instead of the image ("visual under-conditioning"). VISE replaces answer-consistency rewards with two purely self-supervised invariance rewards (geometric + semantic) trained on raw unlabeled images within a single model, yielding large captioning gains (+16.85 CIDEr COCO), lower hallucination (-5.0 Chair-I), and broad VQA/reasoning improvements across 4 backbones and 4 scales with no task tradeoffs.

## 核心贡献

- **识别并命名了"视觉欠条件化"（visual under-conditioning）这一失败模式**：现有自演化 LMM（如 EvoLMM、iReasoner、VisPlay）以答案一致性为奖励，模型可在自洽甚至正确的情况下仍依赖统计语言先验，对视觉 token 关注不足。
- **提出 VISE 单模型纯无监督自演化框架**：不需要多角色（Proposer-Solver）、外部奖励模型或任何标注/元数据，仅用原始未标注图像训练，规避了隐式 minimax 博弈导致的训练不稳定。
- **设计两个互补的不变性奖励**：几何不变性奖励（已知变换下定位应保持空间一致）+ 语义不变性奖励（ghosting 扰动预测区域，模型应能识别证据缺失），从空间与证据两个维度约束视觉条件化策略。
- **机理性验证**：通过逐层生成时注意力比例（visual token attention）和 CKA 相似度证明 VISE 确实增加了中-后期解码层对图像 token 的注意力，从"语言先验驱动"转向"图像条件驱动"。
- **在 18 个基准、4 个模型家族、4 种规模上验证**：字幕、VQA、推理、幻觉四类任务同时提升且无回退，证明强化视觉条件化可跨任务泛化而非相互权衡。

## Key Contributions

- **Names and diagnoses visual under-conditioning**: a persistent failure where self-evolving LMMs reach high answer agreement yet rely on statistical language priors, attending insufficiently to visual tokens — observable as hallucination, modality bypass, and unstable visual interpretation even when the vision encoder is accurate.
- **A single-model, fully unsupervised self-evolving framework (VISE)** that drops multi-role Proposer-Solver setups, external reward models, and all annotations/metadata; trained only on raw unlabeled images, sidestepping the implicit minimax instability of prior methods.
- **Two complementary invariance-based rewards defined entirely from the model's own predictions**: a geometric invariance reward (GIoU between the box predicted on a transformed image and the analytically projected original box) and a semantic invariance reward (the "ghosting" perturbation test that the object is judged visible before and absent after blurring the predicted region).
- **Mechanistic evidence**: per-layer generation-time visual-token attention and CKA similarity confirm VISE shifts attention toward image tokens across mid-to-late decoder layers, validating the hypothesized mechanism rather than only end-task gains.
- **Comprehensive, tradeoff-free evaluation**: gains across captioning, VQA, reasoning, and hallucination on 18 benchmarks, four backbones (Qwen3-VL, InternVL3, Llama-3.2-Vision, SmolVLM2-style families), and four scales (2B/4B/8B/32B).

## Methodology

VISE reframes self-evolution around the model's visual-conditioning policy rather than answer agreement.

- **Problem setup**: Given only unlabeled images X (no queries, boxes, or labels), the single policy πθ operates in a self-questioning regime — it first generates a localization query q about image x, then predicts a bounding box B for the queried object under the same q. A well-pretrained LMM already has enough knowledge to formulate meaningful queries, removing the need for specialist roles. Coordinates live in a normalized [0,1000]⁴ space. The vision encoder is frozen; only the projector, FFNs, and decoder attention projections are updated via LoRA.
- **Geometric invariance reward R_geo**: Sample a known transform T (affine rotation/scale/translation, crop, or horizontal flip) with known 3×3 matrix M. Predict B_new on x'=T(x); compute the projected box B_proj by lifting B_orig corners and applying M. R_geo = (GIoU(B_proj, B_new)+1)/2, mapping GIoU∈[-1,1] to [0,1]. A decoder that ignores geometry cannot maintain consistency across views and is penalized.
- **Semantic invariance reward R_sem ("ghosting")**: Blur (Gaussian σ=25) the predicted region of the original image to produce x̃ while preserving context. Greedily decode visibility v=vis(x,q) and ṽ=vis(x̃,q). R_sem=1 only if v=1 and ṽ=0 — the model must see the object when intact and recognize its absence when obscured. This penalizes evidence-agnostic generation and is complementary to R_geo.
- **Optimization**: Composite reward R_t=0.5·R_geo+0.5·R_sem. An exponential moving-average baseline reduces variance; advantage A_t=R_t-b_t. The loss is a REINFORCE term plus a KL-style divergence proxy Δ_t against a frozen reference policy π_ref, with an adaptive KL coefficient β_t (target τ=0.020, rate η=0.10) that tightens when the policy drifts and relaxes otherwise.
- **Training**: 4000 steps on 8× AMD MI250X GPUs, bfloat16, LoRA (r=16/α=32 for 2B–4B, r=32/α=64 for 8B–32B), AdamW (lr 1e-6 small / 1.5e-7 large), 4000 raw unlabeled COCO images (Objects365 also works per supplement), no QA pairs/annotations/external models.

## Results & Highlights

- **Captioning (Qwen3-VL-2B, CIDEr Δ over base)**: COCO +16.85 (21.54→38.39), NoCaps +14.73, Flickr30k +16.55, TextCaps +19.66 (22.20→41.86) — 4×–7× larger than the strongest baseline on each benchmark, with zero regressions across any dataset or scale.
- **Captioning vs baselines**: Consistency-trained methods (EvoLMM, iReasoner) regress on captioning (e.g., EvoLMM −0.70 COCO at 2B); even the best baseline VisionZero-RW reaches only +4.04 COCO CIDEr at 2B vs VISE's +16.85. Gains attenuate with scale (+8.72 at 32B), consistent with larger models already having stronger conditioning.
- **VQA & Reasoning (2B)**: All 12 benchmarks improve simultaneously — ScienceQA +4.19, InfoVQA +2.41, MMMU +1.75, CaptionQA +2.12, AI2D +2.75, GQA +1.16 — with no regressions at any scale. Baselines show a clear generalization tradeoff (e.g., EvoLMM gains ScienceQA +3.59 but drops OK-VQA −2.73).
- **Hallucination (2B)**: Chair-I 13.21→8.21 (−5.00), Chair-S −5.45, while also improving POPE +1.02 — surpassing the strongest baseline (VisionZero-RW Chair-I −2.99) by +2.01. Baselines often trade Chair for POPE (EvoLMM/iReasoner drop POPE −1.42/−1.31).
- **Backbone generalization**: Identical 4000-image setup on four architecturally diverse backbones yields consistent captioning/hallucination/VQA gains with no regressions, including on Llama-3.2-11B (+1.31 POPE) despite its weaker baseline grounding.
- **Mechanism confirmed**: VISE models assign +2.84% (2B) / +2.56% (4B) more generation-time attention to image tokens across mid-to-late decoder layers (peaks up to +5.09% in layers 15–25); CKA between original and augmented views rises in the final layers (2B peak Δ=+0.069 at layer 27) and broadly on 4B (peak Δ=+0.253).
- **Ablation**: R_geo alone gives moderate captioning gains (+4.83 COCO CIDEr) and modest hallucination reduction (Chair-I −1.35); R_sem alone drives the majority of captioning/hallucination gains (+~12 COCO CIDEr, Chair-I −4.15); combining both adds a further +2.86 CIDEr and −0.85 Chair-I. The two rewards are complementary and jointly necessary.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High)**: The reframing from "answer-agreement rewards" to "invariance-based visual-conditioning rewards" is a genuinely fresh angle for self-evolving LMMs. Naming and mechanistically demonstrating visual under-conditioning (via attention-ratio and CKA) is more than a marketing reframe — it gives a falsifiable diagnosis. The geometric+semantic invariance pair is simple, well-motivated, and clearly complementary.
- **Rigor (Strong)**: Evaluation is broad (18 benchmarks × 4 scales × 4 backbones) and uses lmms-eval for consistency. Deltas are reported against the same base model, and the paper honestly notes baselines' generalization tradeoffs. Ablations isolate each reward; supplementary explores data sources (Objects365) and hyperparameter sensitivity (reward weights, KL target).
- **Reproducibility (High)**: Code, weights, hyperparameters, hardware, and exact training-data size are all provided; only 4000 unlabeled images and 4000 steps make replication cheap. The reward functions are closed-form and architecture-agnostic.
- **Significance (High, with caveats)**: Captioning deltas at 2B are very large and consistent with the qualitative examples and attention-mechanism analysis, which is internally coherent. The cross-backbone transfer is a strong generalization claim. However:
  - The biggest absolute gains concentrate at the **smallest scale (2B)** and shrink markedly at 32B, which both supports the authors' "larger models already conditioned" interpretation and slightly tempers impact for frontier-scale deployment.
  - The reward is fundamentally a **localization/visibility** signal; whether it scales to fine-grained reasoning beyond what good grounding provides is not fully stressed here, and gains on hard reasoning benchmarks (MMMU, ScienceQA) are positive but modest at larger scales.
  - CIDEr improvements, especially with relatively low absolute scores (e.g., COCO 38.4), should be read as "strong relative gains over a weak self-evolving baseline" rather than SOTA captioning in absolute terms.
- **Red flags**: None major. The paper is candid that prior self-evolving baselines also use curated/synthetic or GPT-4o-assisted data (VisionZero Chart/RW) while VISE is the only fully label-free method, which makes the comparison somewhat favorable — but this is disclosed rather than hidden. The single-model claim is legitimate (no role specialization), though the method does rely on a frozen reference policy for the KL term (standard RLHF practice, not a true limitation).

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated diagnosis of a real failure mode with direct mechanistic evidence (attention ratios, CKA) backing the proposed fix.
- Fully unsupervised, single-model, no external reward models or annotations — minimizes the moving parts and the supervision required.
- Simple, closed-form, complementary reward design; ablations show each component contributes.
- Broad, consistent, tradeoff-free evaluation across tasks, scales, and architectures — no captioning-vs-reasoning tradeoff that plagues baselines.
- Full open-source release (code + weights + project page) and a small compute budget (4000 images, 4000 steps).

## Limitations

- Largest gains at the smallest model; benefits attenuate at 32B, so marginal value at frontier scale is an open question.
- The reward is localization/visibility-centric; grounding is necessary but may not be sufficient for the hardest compositional or knowledge-intensive reasoning.
- Absolute captioning scores remain modest (COCO CIDEr 38.4 at 2B); gains are large relatively but the model is not competing with heavily supervised captioners in absolute terms.
- Only 4000 unlabeled COCO images are used by default; sensitivity to image distribution and quantity at larger scales is only partially explored (Objects365 in supplement).
- Evaluation of the "ghosting" visibility judgement depends on greedy decoding of a binary answer — robustness to query/region ambiguity is not deeply analyzed.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the geometric and semantic invariance rewards transfer to video and 3D/embodied LMMs, where temporal consistency could provide a third invariance axis?
- Can the visual-under-conditioning diagnosis and the per-layer attention metric be turned into a training-free inference-time correction (e.g., attention re-weighting or contrastive decoding) for frozen models?
- How does VISE interact with much harder compositional and spatial-relation reasoning benchmarks (e.g., WhatsUp, VSR) that stress fine grounding beyond object presence?
- Is the "ghosting" semantic reward robust to ambiguous queries and crowded scenes, or does it inject noise when multiple objects match the localization query?
- What is the scaling law of invariance-based gains — does the 2B-vs-32B attenuation plateau, or could a much larger unlabeled corpus revive gains at frontier scale?
- Could combining answer-agreement rewards (for reasoning) with invariance rewards (for grounding) exceed both paradigms, given the paper frames them as alternatives?
