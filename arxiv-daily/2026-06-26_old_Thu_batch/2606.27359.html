Key Finding

When are likely answers right? On Sequence Probability and Correctness in LLMs

Johannes Zenn, Jonas Geiping · 2026-06-25 · MPI-IS / ELLIS Tübingen
Sequence probability predicts correctness only at the dataset level — and silently fails to transfer to decoding, hyperparameter, or single-prompt decisions.
A four-level correlation study across 12-14 models, 6 benchmarks, and 8 decoding methods.
LLM decoding sequence-probability calibration self-consistency empirical-study
Key Metric
+0.96Pearson r
Within-dataset log-prob vs. correctness on MATH500 (Qwen3-8B-Base) — the ceiling case. Falls to r≈0.66 (base) / 0.59 (posttrained) on average, and goes negative on IFEval.
Why This Matters
It draws a hard empirical line where Best-of-N, probability-weighted self-consistency, and verifier-free self-improvement can and cannot be trusted as correctness signals.
Critical Edge
“The analysis is fundamentally correlational — it never establishes a causal mechanism for why probability and correctness decouple at the method level, so practitioners can only tune on their own data.” — from Critical Assessment / Rigor

Methodology

An autoregressive LLM p assigns probabilities to token continuations s given a prompt s̄. The authors reframe the entire decoding zoo — local methods (low-temperature LTS, top-k/top-p/ε-sampling) that use per-prefix normalization, and global methods (beam search, Best-of-N, power sampling from p(s|s̄)^α/Zα) — as operations that shift probability mass toward higher-probability sequences. They derive a variational objective Jα(q)=E_q[log p]+(1/α)H(q) whose maximizer is the power distribution, and unify SPS / power-SMC inside a twisted-SMC view.

The empirical core defines four correlation coefficients between continuous log-probability and a binary correctness label, each answering a distinct question about when "more probable" means "more correct."

① Across-method
Fix model+dataset, correlate log-prob & accuracy across methods (LTS α=4 as zero point, quadrant analysis).
② Within-method
For one method, correlate across its hyperparameters (slope sign + correctness change).
③ Within-dataset
Scatter all samples, 10 equal-count bins, binned Pearson r (and Spearman ρ).
④ Within-sample
For one prompt, rank-correlate log-prob vs correctness across its N repeated responses.

Setup: vLLM for local methods; own importance-weighted SPS (K=M=8, τ=0.25); power-SMC from original code. ≤3072 tokens, ≤500 prompts/dataset, ≤1 day per (method,dataset) on 80GB H100. Pearson r uses binned means (K=10); binary label caps Spearman ρ ≤ 0.5.

Results — The Four-Level Verdict

LevelQuestionSignalVerdict
Within-dataset Across prompt-answer pairs in a fixed triple up to +0.96 POSITIVE — scales with task accuracy
Within-method Across one method's hyperparameters mixed / inverse FAILS — more probable ≠ more correct
Across-method Across decoding methods no method wins FAILS — no method reliably beats LTS α=4
Within-sample Across repeated responses to one prompt ≈ 0 (symmetric) FAILS — only MATH500 averages positive

Finding 4 (actionable): within-dataset r rises with mean accuracy (base r=0.66, posttrained r=0.59) — so verifier-free self-improvement loops need sufficient baseline accuracy or they reinforce on increasingly incorrect samples. Finding 6: for LTS, uniform majority voting beats probability-weighted voting; power self-consistency helps MATH500 but degrades GPQA/MMLU.

Strengths

  • Clean conceptual framing that unifies a sprawling decoding literature under one question.
  • A reusable four-level correlation framework future decoding papers can adopt.
  • Genuinely broad coverage: 12-14 models, 6 datasets, 8 methods, multiple hyperparameters each.
  • Intellectual honesty — it foregrounds its own negative results and explicit do/don't guidance.
  • Solid theory appendix (variational objective, twisted-SMC unification, exactness/gap remarks with proofs).

Limitations

  • Purely correlational; no causal account of why probability and correctness decouple at method/hyperparam level.
  • Token-length / length-normalization confounds only partially neutralized (beam search an outlier).
  • 3072-token cap forces exclusion of thinking models; ≤500 prompts/dataset limits power.
  • All benchmarks are short-answer / MCQ — open-ended generation untested.
  • SPS is a re-implementation (not the original codebase) — a possible reproduction gap.
  • No formal significance testing or multiple-comparison control across the large grid.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Why does within-dataset correlation fail to transfer to the method/hyperparameter level — length artifact, mode collapse, or intrinsic to how sharpening redistributes mass?
  2. Does within-sample ρ stay symmetric around zero for much larger N (256/512)? What is its N-scaling law?
  3. How do the four correlations behave on open-ended / free-form or agentic multi-turn settings?
  4. Can a learned twist function or a lightweight verifier recover a usable within-sample signal without external supervision?
  5. Does "correlation scales with accuracy" imply a predictable threshold above which verifier-free self-improvement becomes self-sustaining?
  6. Are the negative IFEval correlations for base models a data-coverage artifact, and do they flip with more instruction tuning?
