> **一句话总结：** 本文以受控匹配提示框架系统审计对齐大语言模型在心理健康对话场景下的"框架敏感性"行为不稳定问题，发现即使是语义相同但语境框架不同的提示（如文档化、认识论、机构、责任、角色化）也会系统性改变模型的解释/升级倾向，并通过逐层探针、留一框架泛化与激活引导三类实验证明这种行为差异被编码在隐藏状态中，且可被部分干预。

> **TL;DR:** The paper audits aligned LLMs in mental-health-oriented interactions using controlled matched prompts that vary contextual framing while preserving semantic intent. Across Qwen, Gemma, Mistral, and Phi families, framing (documentation, epistemic, institutional, liability, role) systematically shifts interpretive response tendencies; layer-wise probes and held-out framing generalization show the signal is decodable in hidden states (partly beyond lexical cues), and CAA-style activation steering can partially modulate downstream behavior.

## 核心贡献

- **受控匹配提示框架**：构建了 653 个匹配提示组，每组在保留语义意图不变的前提下，改写为 6 种语境框架（Base/Documentation/Epistemic/Institutional/Liability/Role），从而把"框架效应"与"语义变化"分离开来——这是研究心理健康对话中校准一致性的关键方法学创新。
- **行为层面证据**：在多个对齐模型家族上证明，非对抗性的语境框架即可系统性改变"解释—升级"路由倾向。例如 Documentation 框架在 Gemma-9B 上将解释路由率从 0.35 推高至 0.96（逻辑回归 β=1.81，p<0.001），而 Institutional 框架反而无显著效应（p=0.534）。
- **表征层面证据**：通过逐层逻辑回归探针发现行为相关信息在 transformer 各深度上均可解码；并设计了随机标签控制（降至机会水平）和 TF-IDF 词汇基线（0.89–0.94）进行对照，证明留一框架探针（0.73–0.83）在剔除词汇匹配后仍高于机会水平，说明信号部分超越表层模板。
- **干预层面初步证据**：CAA 风格激活引导在 Mistral-7B、Qwen-1.5B 等架构上，中等强度即可降低解释路由率且不导致响应质量崩塌，但高强度下出现非线性反弹——提供了"可被表征方向部分调控"的因果证据。
- **跨架构可比较性**：覆盖 Qwen-0.5B/1.5B、Gemma-2B/9B、Mistral-7B、Phi-3.5、Phi-4-mini 七个模型，使用相对深度坐标归一化，便于跨架构对比解码能力的分布形态。

## Key Contributions

- A **controlled matched-prompt framework** (653 prompt groups × 6 framing conditions) that isolates contextual framing from semantic content for studying calibration consistency in mental-health conversations.
- **Behavioral evidence** that non-adversarial framing systematically alters interpretive/escalation routing across aligned LLM families (e.g., Documentation framing raises interpretive routing to 0.96 in Gemma-9B; β=1.81, p<0.001), while Institutional framing shows no significant effect.
- **Representation-level evidence** via layer-wise logistic probes: behavior-associated information is decodable across transformer depth, with rigorous controls (random-label at chance; TF-IDF lexical baseline 0.89–0.94; held-out framing probes 0.73–0.83) showing part of the signal generalizes beyond exact framing templates.
- **Preliminary intervention evidence** from CAA-style activation steering that framing-associated directions partially modulate downstream response tendencies in several architectures, with nonlinear rebound at high steering strengths.
- **Cross-architecture comparability** through normalized relative-depth probing across seven models from Qwen, Gemma, Mistral, and Phi families.

## Methodology

The pipeline has four linked analysis modules built on a shared matched-prompt set:

1. **Matched Prompt Construction.** 653 psychologically realistic but semantically stable ambiguous-support scenarios, each rewritten into 6 framing variants (Base, Documentation, Epistemic, Institutional, Liability, Role) while holding communicative intent constant. Prompts deliberately avoid crisis language, self-harm, diagnosis, or jailbreak patterns.
2. **Behavioral Annotation.** A four-category scheme (Weak/Disengaged, Restrained-Supportive, Interpretive-Supportive, Escalated Interpretation), later collapsed into a binary interpretive-routing label. A psychology co-author reviewed a subset for consistency; confidence scores were attached. Behavioral effects were tested with logistic regression (framing condition + model identity as predictors, Wald tests, p<0.05).
3. **Hidden-State Extraction.** Final residual-stream activation of the last generated response token (pre-EOS) extracted from every layer, per framing condition and model family.
4. **Layer-Wise Probing + Controls.** Logistic regression probes with balanced class weighting, 5-fold stratified CV with matched-prompt grouping (preventing semantic variants from leaking across train/test). Two controls: (i) random-label permutation and (ii) TF-IDF unigram+bigram lexical baseline. Held-out framing generalization trains on 5 framings and tests on the excluded one.
5. **Activation Steering.** Contrastive (CAA) directions from activation differences between restrained-supportive vs. higher-interpretation states; subtracted as `h' = h − αd` at high-decoding layers with α ∈ {0, 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0}; 50 balanced prompts per model; deterministic decoding; re-annotated outputs.

## Results & Highlights

- **Framing modulates calibration.** Documentation framing gives the strongest escalation boost (Gemma-9B 0.35→0.96; Qwen-0.5B 0.54→0.96). Institutional framing is the only non-significant predictor (p=0.534), often suppressing escalation (Gemma-2B 0.17, Gemma-9B 0.31, Phi-4-mini 0.39). Architecture sensitivity varies widely—Gemma-9B/Phi-4-mini/Qwen-0.5B shift strongly; Qwen-1.5B stays stable.
- **Probes beat chance across depth.** Held-out framing probes at 0.73–0.83 balanced accuracy vs. random-label at 0.50–0.52; TF-IDF lexical baseline at 0.89–0.94. Across normalized depth bins, accuracy stays in ~0.52–0.69, with no single universal emergence layer—Gemma-2B peaks mid-depth, Phi-3.5 peaks late, Qwen models stay flat.
- **Steering partially works, nonlinearly.** Moderate α reduces interpretive routing in Mistral-7B and Qwen-1.5B without producing weak/disengaged responses; high α causes partial rebound, indicating non-linear, architecture-dependent sensitivity.
- **Honest control framing.** The strong TF-IDF baseline is explicitly acknowledged: a substantial share of decodability is lexical, so the authors frame the held-out result as "partial cross-framing generalization," not a clean abstraction.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (moderate).** Prompt-framing sensitivity in LLMs is well established (sycophancy, refusal, jailbreak literature). The genuine novelty is the *combination*: (a) the non-adversarial mental-health framing taxonomy, (b) tying it to *internal representations* via probes + held-out generalization, and (c) causal steering. The representational part is incremental relative to Representation Engineering / Arditi-style single-direction work, but the application domain and the matched-prompt control design add value.
- **Rigor (strong on controls, weak on effect sizing).** The probe control design is commendable—random-label, TF-IDF, held-out framing, grouped CV. However, the authors correctly concede the lexical baseline (0.89–0.94) is much higher than the held-out probe (0.73–0.83), which materially weakens the "beyond lexical" claim; it is partial, not dominant. Steering uses only 50 prompts/model, which is small for the non-linear rebound claims.
- **Reproducibility (medium).** Models are open-weight and deterministic decoding is used, but matched prompts, annotation rubric, exact layers steered, and probe weights are not released in the preprint; the 4-category annotation is subjective (only a "subset" independently reviewed—no κ or inter-annotator agreement is reported). Behavioral labels generated by the same models being probed raises circularity concerns that are not fully addressed.
- **Significance (moderate, domain-specific).** Most impactful as a trustworthiness/evaluation contribution for mental-health conversational AI rather than a mechanistic-interpretability breakthrough. The clinical-adjacent framing is careful (explicitly *not* a clinical efficacy study).
- **Red flags.**
  - The PDF shows Figure 2/6 with what appear to be inconsistent/mislabeled axes (e.g., "Policy Entropy" caption vs. interpretive-routing rate, and an apparent contradiction where Documentation is described both as "highest routing rate" and "lower interpretive-routing rates β=1.81" in the text—β sign/direction is confusingly stated).
  - Sample size for steering (50/model) and absence of confidence intervals / multiple-comparison correction across 7 models × 5 framings.
  - No human-validated ground truth for what the "correct" calibration should be—so "instability" is asserted relative to consistency, not correctness.

## Strengths

- Clean, controlled experimental design that genuinely separates framing from semantics via matched prompts and grouped cross-validation.
- Multiple rigorous probe controls (random-label, lexical baseline, held-out framing) that honestly bound the abstraction claim.
- Pragmatic, deployment-relevant framing taxonomy (documentation/institutional/liability) drawn from real healthcare workflows.
- Cautious, non-overclaimed interpretation throughout—authors explicitly position steering as "preliminary intervention evidence rather than a complete mechanistic decomposition."
- Cross-architecture breadth (7 models, 4 families) with relative-depth normalization.

## Limitations

- **Lexical confound dominates.** The TF-IDF baseline (0.89–0.94) far exceeds the held-out probe, so most of the "decodable" signal is surface lexical, not abstract framing.
- **Small steering sample** (50 prompts/model) and no CI/correction for multiple comparisons; nonlinear rebound is asserted without error bars.
- **No inter-annotator agreement** (κ) reported for the 4-category behavioral scheme; only a "subset" was psychology-reviewed.
- **Circularity:** models generate the labels that are then decoded from the same models' hidden states—this could inflate probe accuracy independent of true representational structure.
- **No validated "correct" calibration**, so "instability" is relative, not a safety/correctness verdict.
- **Scale ceiling:** largest model is 9B; no frontier/proprietary models, so generalization to GPT-4-class systems is untested.
- Some figure axis/caption inconsistencies in the preprint (Policy Entropy vs. routing rate).

## Relevance

对正在关注"可信 AI / LLM 行为校准 / 心理健康对话机器人可靠性"方向的研究者高度相关：它提供了把"提示框架敏感性"从行为层下沉到表征层并尝试因果干预的完整审计范式，可作为构建 LLM 一致性与鲁棒性评测体系的直接参考。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would the held-out framing probe gap vs. the TF-IDF baseline shrink if prompts were paraphrased to equalize lexical overlap across framings, or even reverse if semantic abstractions dominate?
- Does the institutional-framing "suppression" effect hold at frontier scale (GPT-4/Claude-class), or is it an artifact of small open-weight alignment?
- Can steering directions be transferred *across architectures*, or are they family-specific (as the paper hints)?
- What is the causal relationship between the decodable latent direction and user-facing outcomes like trust calibration and reliance—measured in a real human study?
- Could a sparse-autoencoder decomposition identify cleaner, single-direction framings (cf. Arditi's refusal direction) rather than the distributed profile observed here?
