> **一句话总结：** 本文在两个开源模型（Apertus-8B 和 Gemma-4-E4B）上复现了 Anthropic 关于"情绪向量"的研究，发现效价（valence）几何可被稳定提取，但其出现的层级轨迹因模型而异；而唤醒度（arousal）的提取则强烈依赖于故事语料库的选择。

> **TL;DR:** This workshop paper replicates Anthropic's emotion-vector findings in two open-weight LLMs (Apertus-8B and Gemma-4-E4B), recovering valence-aligned geometry (PC1–valence r up to 0.83) comparable to Claude Sonnet 4.5's r = 0.81, but reveals that valence emerges via different layer-wise trajectories across models and that arousal extraction is highly corpus-dependent.

## 核心贡献

- **跨架构复现情绪向量**：首次在两个完全开源权重模型（Apertus-8B、Gemma-4-E4B）上复现了 Claude Sonnet 4.5 的情绪向量研究，证明效价（valence）轴是语言模型内部表征的一般性属性，而非 Anthropic 训练特有。峰值 PC1–效价相关系数分别为 r = 0.76（Apertus）和 r = 0.83（Gemma），接近甚至超过原始的 r = 0.81。
- **揭示"殊途同归"的层级轨迹**：两个模型达到相似峰值但路径迥异——Gemma-4-E4B 在早期层（layer 16）就强编码效价随后塌缩，而 Apertus-8B 在中后段（layer 20 起）才渐进建立。CKA 分析在 Apertus 中发现一个相变（phase transition），而 Gemma 呈平滑梯度。
- **引入双语料库对照以分离模型固有性质与提取伪影**：使用 Apertus-生成与 Gemma-生成两套故事语料分别探测两个模型，这是此前工作未做的对照。结果表明唤醒度（arousal）高度语料依赖：Gemma 故事在两个模型上都给出显著更强的 PC2–唤醒度对齐（r 高达 0.45 vs. ≤ 0.21）。
- **空间稳定 ≠ 轴向稳定的重要洞见**：发现表征空间的跨层相似性（CKA）与效价方向（valence axis）的轴向稳定性之间存在脱钩——Gemma 在效价相关塌缩时空间几何仍稳定，说明信息被保留但编码方向发生了旋转。
- **开放可复现性**：实验代码与数据集全部开源（github.com/sinievanderben/emotion_experiment）。

## Key Contributions

- **Replication beyond Claude**: Reproduces the emotion-vector paradigm of Sofroniew et al. (2026) in two architecturally distinct open-weight models, with peak PC1–valence correlations matching or approaching the Claude reference (r = 0.76 Apertus, r = 0.83 Gemma vs. 0.81 Claude).
- **Divergent emergence trajectories**: Identifies that similar peak valence correlations hide very different layer-wise developmental paths—abrupt late emergence (Apertus) vs. early-then-collapse (Gemma)—with a CKA-detectable phase transition in one but not the other.
- **Two-corpus experimental design**: Probes each model on both Apertus- and Gemma-generated story corpora to separate model-intrinsic emotion geometry from corpus-dependent extraction artifacts; demonstrates arousal encoding is strongly corpus-sensitive.
- **Space-vs-axis dissociation**: Highlights that representational similarity (CKA) does not guarantee a shared valence direction across layers, since the valence axis rotates even where the global geometry is stable.
- **Open release**: Code and synthetic datasets are publicly available for reproducible investigation.

## Methodology

The authors follow the emotion-contrast-vector protocol of Sofroniew et al. (2026):

1. **Datasets.** Two synthetic emotion-story corpora are generated, each with 9 stories for each of 171 emotions (1,539 stories total) plus 40 neutral stories. One corpus is written by Apertus-8B, the other by Gemma-4-E4B, both prompted to convey the emotion implicitly without naming it. Treating story corpus as an independent variable is the key novelty.

2. **Models.** Two instruction-tuned open-weight transformers of comparable scale: Apertus-8B (32 layers, vectors from layers 1–31) and Gemma-4-E4B (42 layers, vectors from layers 1–40).

3. **Contrast-vector extraction.** For each emotion and layer, residual-stream activations from the 9 stories are averaged to a raw vector u_e. A neutral subspace is built from the 40 neutral stories via PCA (top-K components explaining 50% variance). The emotion-specific contrast vector v_e is obtained by subtracting the projection of u_e onto the neutral subspace. Stacking across emotions yields V^(l) ∈ R^{|E|×d_model} per layer.

4. **Analyses.**
   - **PCA + valence/arousal correlation**: PC1, PC2 of V^(l) correlated with human ratings from the NRC Valence–Arousal–Dominance Lexicon (Mohammad, 2018); Pearson r and p-values reported.
   - **Cross-layer CKA** (Kornblith et al., 2019): quantifies how emotion geometry evolves across depth within each model×corpus condition; invariant to orientation, making it suitable for comparing representational structure.
   - **Valence-direction stability**: cosine similarity of the valence direction across layers to test whether the same subspace encodes valence at different depths.

## Results & Highlights

- **Valence replicates strongly.** All four model×corpus conditions reach peak PC1–valence r between 0.72 and 0.83, all significant (p < 10⁻³). Apertus peaks at r = 0.72 (L23, Apertus stories) and r = 0.76 (L31, Gemma stories); Gemma peaks at r = 0.79 (L13) and r = 0.83 (L16). Gemma-4-E4B on Gemma stories (r = 0.83) exceeds the Claude Sonnet 4.5 reference of r = 0.81.
- **Opposite depth profiles.** Apertus-8B shows abrupt late emergence (near-zero until ~50% depth, then a sharp rise past r = 0.60 around layer 21). Gemma-4-E4B shows early encoding that collapses by layer 18 with only partial recovery.
- **CKA phase transition.** Apertus exhibits three CKA phases (early plateau 2–11, transition band 12–21, late plateau 22–31); Gemma shows a smooth gradient with CKA ≥ 0.73 between any layer pair and no sharp transition.
- **Arousal is corpus-dependent.** PC2–arousal correlations are weak on Apertus stories (r ≤ 0.21) but rise substantially on Gemma stories for both models (Apertus r = 0.45 at L26; Gemma r = 0.41 at L31, both p < 10⁻⁸), more than doubling and partially closing the gap with the Claude reference (r = 0.66). Because this effect appears in both models on Gemma stories, it is attributed to corpus properties rather than model–story matching.
- **Space stable, axis unstable.** In Gemma, the representational space (CKA) remains stable across layers even where valence correlation collapses, implying valence information is preserved but encoded along a rotating axis. Valence-axis cosine matrices confirm the recovered direction is noisy across layers in both models.
- **PCA cluster separation** at peak layers is cleaner for Gemma stories, with denser positive/negative emotion groupings.

El-Assady is an established visualization/interpretability researcher. Code and data are released publicly, supporting reproducibility. The paper is explicitly framed as a replication-and-extension of Anthropic's Transformer Circuits Thread work, with candid discussion of where its implementation had to be reconstructed because the original authors released no code.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (moderate):** The paper is primarily a replication and generalization study, not a new method. Its genuine contributions are the two-corpus experimental design (separating model-intrinsic geometry from corpus artifacts) and the layer-trajectory comparison. These are solid but incremental contributions typical of workshop work.
- **Rigor (good):** Methodology follows the original protocol carefully, uses standard tools (PCA, CKA, Pearson correlation), and reports per-layer numbers, significance values, and multiple visualization diagnostics (CKA matrices, cosine matrices, cluster plots). The two-corpus design is a meaningful confound control. Limitations are stated openly.
- **Reproducibility (strong):** Two fully open-weight models, open code, and a public dataset make this one of the more reproducible papers in this sub-area—materially more so than the original Anthropic work, which released no code.
- **Significance (moderate):** The finding that emotion vectors generalize beyond Claude is valuable for interpretability and safety monitoring across model families. However, the most striking claim—causal influence of emotion vectors on behavior (reward hacking, blackmail in the original)—is **not** replicated here; the present work is correlational only. The interesting "different paths, same geometry" and "space-vs-axis dissociation" findings are descriptive and their mechanistic implications are not pinned down (the authors explicitly defer attribution to architecture/training/post-training).
- **Red flags:**
  - No causal/steering validation—the central behavioral claim of the original is untested, so "replication" is partial (geometry only).
  - Only two model families; generalization claims about "different paths" rest on n = 2.
  - Corpora are themselves model-generated, so corpus and model distributions are not fully separable; the authors acknowledge this.
  - Valence-axis cosine matrices are largely noisy (most off-diagonal cells low), which somewhat undercuts claims about "axis rotation" as a clean phenomenon.
  - Reconstruction of the original method (no released code) means subtle numerical differences could affect comparability.
  - Arousal effect, while interesting, is hypothesized but its content-level cause ("Gemma stories have more arousal-discriminative content") is left to future work.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated replication that directly answers the three generality questions raised by the original work.
- The two-corpus design is a methodologically important control that prior work lacked.
- Open weights, open code, open data—high reproducibility bar.
- Honest, detailed limitations and future-work sections.
- Useful conceptual contribution: peak correlation alone can hide very different internal computation, and CKA stability does not imply a stable feature axis.

## Limitations

- Only two open-weight models from two families; broader architectural coverage needed.
- Model-generated corpora conflate corpus and model properties; a fully model-independent stimulus set would be stronger.
- No causal steering experiments to test whether recovered valence directions actually drive behavior.
- Original method reconstructed without reference code, introducing possible numerical discrepancies.
- Arousal findings are correlational and the underlying corpus-content explanation is unverified.

## Relevance

本研究与机制可解释性（mechanistic interpretability）和模型对齐安全监控高度相关，它验证了"情绪向量"这一内部表征概念的可迁移性，提示我们可以通过监测模型内部的情绪/效价轴来跨模型发现潜在的失对齐状态。对于从事可解释性、激活引导（activation steering）或内部状态监控的研究者，本文提供了开源、可复现的基础方法和值得继续追问的"不同模型、不同路径得到相同几何"现象。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the recovered valence directions causally steer model outputs (reward hacking, sycophancy, refusal) as in the original Claude work? At which layers is steering most effective?
- Do steering vectors derived at one layer transfer to other layers, given the observed axis rotation within otherwise CKA-stable regions?
- What drives the divergent emergence trajectories—architecture, pre-training data, or post-training? Can ablations (e.g., probing base vs. instruct checkpoints) attribute the difference?
- Can sparse autoencoders reveal whether the *same* interpretable features carry emotion information across layers, or whether different layers compose emotions from different feature sets?
- Does the corpus-dependence of arousal reflect specific lexical/narrative cues that could be controlled for with a curated, model-independent stimulus set?
- Is the valence axis preserved in multi-modal models across text, image, and audio inputs?
