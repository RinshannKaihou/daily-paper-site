Key Finding

Regularizing in a pretrained SAE feature space — not in weight space — gives the strongest data-free continual learning for LLMs.

Why This Matters

Reframes LLM continual-learning failure as a structural superposition problem, fixable by protecting interpretable features — with no replay data stored.

A new lightweight paradigm for frequent domain adaptation where history can't be kept.
Critical Edge

⚠It still trails architectural O-LoRA by a wide margin (0.545 vs 0.630), and ≥5% replay beats it on MedCL — its real win is being data-free, not raw SOTA.

Methodology

Continual learning is cast as constrained optimization over SAE feature drift. A frozen base model (Gemma-2 9B-it, LoRA r=256) uses frozen Gemma Scope 16k SAEs at residual layers 9/20/31 purely as a fixed coordinate system. Drift ∆ = z(x;θ) − z(x;θ0) is measured on SAE feature activations.

  • Two constraints → two losses. Stability: protected features must not drift past ε_s → L_protect (squared-hinge). Plasticity: task-relevant features must move at least ε_p·M → L_guide, avoiding the "preserve everything, learn nothing" degenerate solution.
  • EWC = special case: drift measured in weight space, no plasticity term. The authors prove this formally.
  • Mask, not data. Offline, a graded mask M_t∈[0,1] is built from mean activation relevance + k-NN propagation over SAE decoder cosine similarity. Only the sub-MB mask is retained — no replay buffer, no per-task params, no inference routing.

Strengths

  • Clean framing: links CL failure to superposition / polysemanticity, making the "unit of protection" the central object.
  • Two-sided objective with explicit plasticity constraint — avoids confusing low forgetting with good CL.
  • Targeted mechanistic evidence (separability AUC + collateral ratios) supports rather than asserts the thesis.
  • Sub-MB per-task mask vs GB-scale anchor storage; no replay, no routing.

Limitations

  • Only on Gemma-2 9B-it + Gemma Scope SAEs; transfer to other families unproven.
  • Does not reach architectural upper bound (O-LoRA); SOTA is "non-architectural, data-free" only.
  • ≥5% replay beats it on MedCL when data storage is acceptable.
  • Single training seed; two losses + four hyperparameters + k-NN propagation is non-trivial to tune.

Open Questions

  • Does the gain survive transfer to non-Gemma models once comparable SAEs exist — or is it tied to Gemma Scope quality?
  • Can the two-sided objective combine with orthogonal-subspace methods (O-LoRA) to close the gap?
  • How does it scale to long task sequences (≫8–10) where cumulative mask overlap erodes selectivity?
  • Does a single seed hold up — do the CI-non-overlap claims survive multi-seed evaluation?
