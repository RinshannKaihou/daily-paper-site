> **一句话总结：** HiMuon 把 Muon 优化器中对动量梯度矩阵的整矩阵 Newton–Schulz 映射，改为在每个 T×T 局部 tile 上独立执行，把主要算力从 O(HW·min{H,W}·K) 降到 O(HW·T·K)，并通过 tile 相关内核、跨层批处理和运行时 tile 调度在 GPU 上实现，同时在 Qwen3 训练中保持与全矩阵 Muon 接近的损失。

> **TL;DR:** HiMuon replaces Muon's single full-matrix Newton-Schulz (NS) map with the same finite map applied independently to each T×T tile of the momentum-gradient matrix, cutting leading NS work from O(HW·min{H,W}·K) to O(HW·T·K). It is a *local* matrix-function map (not a convergent approximation to full-matrix Muon), and a custom GPU plan (tile-size-dependent kernels, cross-layer batching, memory-bounded chunking, runtime tile-size schedules) makes the arithmetic savings visible in wall-clock time while keeping Qwen3 training/validation loss close to full-matrix Muon.

## 核心贡献

- **分块 Newton–Schulz 形式化定义：** 将动量梯度矩阵划分为 R×C 个 T×T tile，在每个 tile 上独立施加同一有限 NS 映射 Φ_K，再重组。tile 大小同时是"保留谱耦合程度"的数值参数和"GPU 执行规模"的计算参数。
- **明确区分"局部映射"与"收敛近似"：** 作者清楚声明对于有限 T，分块映射 Φ_{K,T} ≠ Φ_K，是结构上不同的局部矩阵函数映射（不与极分解或 NS 代理交换），避免过度声称等价。
- **端到端 GPU 执行方案：** 大 tile 用多内核 Triton 路径（带 epilogue 融合），小 tile（≤128）用 SRAM-resident 单内核（K 步 NS 全在片上）；跨层把同形 tile 批处理；内存受限的 chunking；运行时 reconfigure() 可在训练中改变 T 而不重置优化器状态。
- **容量诊断工具：** 用 Kim 等人的一步联想记忆基准作为受控诊断，量化 tile 大小对谱放大行为的影响（Muon ≈ HiMuon-512 > -256 > -128 ≫ SGD），为 tile 调度提供定性依据。
- **多层级实证：** 在 matrix-function、layer、optimizer-step、end-to-end 四个层级评估；并测试 bf16/fp8 低精度在 NS 内层 GEMM 中的鲁棒性。

## Key Contributions

- **Tiled Newton-Schulz formulation of Muon (HiMuon).** Partitions each momentum-gradient matrix into a grid of T×T tiles, applies the same finite NS map Φ_K independently per tile, and reassembles. Work drops from O(r²sK) to O(HWTK).
- **Conceptual framing of the tile size as a joint numerical+execution parameter.** T controls the amount of retained spectral coupling (numerical locality) *and* the size of the dense sub-problems issued to the GPU. The authors are explicit that this is a *local matrix-function map*, not a convergent approximation to full-matrix Muon (tiling does not commute with the polar factor or its NS surrogate).
- **An optimizer-level GPU execution plan** with three planks: (1) tile-size-dependent kernels (multi-kernel Triton path with epilogue fusion for large tiles; SRAM-resident single-kernel path for small tiles, threshold ~T=128 on A40); (2) cross-layer batching with memory-bounded chunking; (3) a `reconfigure()` routine enabling runtime tile-size schedules (e.g. ∞ → 512 → 128) without resetting optimizer state.
- **A one-step linear associative-memory capacity diagnostic** (Kim et al.) used to characterize how tile size degrades the bulk singular-value amplification that distinguishes Muon from SGD.
- **Multi-axis empirical characterization** on real Qwen3 weight shapes: matrix-function speedups, optimizer-step speedups with/without cross-layer batching, end-to-end pretraining on Qwen3-0.6B/1.7B (DDP) and 4B (FSDP) on FineWeb, and low-precision (bf16/fp8e4m3) robustness on the capacity metric and on a teacher-student quadratic proxy (alignment γ and descent potential ϕ).

## Methodology

**Mathematical core.** For a momentum-gradient matrix G ∈ R^{H×W}, the tiling operator B_T pads with zeros to an (RT)×(CT) grid of T×T tiles, applies the *same* quintic NS recurrence (coefficients (a,b,c)=(3.4445,−4.7750,2.0315), K=5 steps, Frobenius-normalized X_0) independently to each tile, then reassembles via B_T^{-1}. The tile-size-dependent RMS-matching scalar is τ(T)=c√T for finite T (vs c√max{H,W} for the no-tiling branch, with c=0.2). Momentum (β=0.95), Nesterov lookahead, and decoupled weight decay are inherited unchanged from Muon.

**Key property.** Because tiling does not commute with the polar factor, Φ_{K,T} is a *distinct local matrix-function update*: spectral interactions survive within tiles and are removed across tile boundaries. The cost is O(HWTK) vs full-matrix O(HW·min{H,W}·K).

**GPU implementation strategy.** (i) Tile-size-dependent dispatch: large tiles use a multi-kernel Triton path with epilogue fusion; small tiles (working set fits in SRAM) use a single SRAM-resident kernel that keeps all K NS iterations on-chip, eliminating intermediate HBM round-trips. (ii) Cross-layer batching groups compatible tiles (same shape/dtype/kernel path) from different parameter matrices into larger batched calls; under FSDP each rank's tiled NS is rank-local (no optimizer-internal all-gather). (iii) Memory-bounded chunking splits oversized batches into the fewest chunks fitting a memory budget, mathematically equivalent to a single batch. (iv) Runtime `reconfigure()` rebuilds batching metadata/kernel-path/graph-capture when T_t changes, while momentum buffers (T-independent) are preserved.

**Experimental setup.** Qwen3-0.6B/1.7B/4B on FineWeb sample-10BT (streaming, seq-len 1024), held-out eval on CC-MAIN-2024-51; 4×A40 (DDP for 0.6B/1.7B, FSDP for 4B); bf16 mixed precision; LR reused from a grid search for full-matrix Muon; schedule (∞→512→128) with switches at steps (100,500) DDP / (200,500) FSDP. Isolated matrix-function/kernel/optimizer-step studies use single A40 with CUDA-event timing (30–50 reps, medians+IQRs). Low-precision study on L40S.

## Results & Highlights

- **Per-layer NS speedup (T=512) on real Qwen3 Block-0 shapes:** 1.4–1.6× (0.6B), 1.4–2.9× (1.7B), 1.3–2.9× (4B). Largest gains on q/o-proj and wide MLP projections; smallest on k/v-proj (GQA narrows these).
- **Optimizer-step speedup (synthetic 8-layer Linear, H∈{1024,2048,4096}):** plain tiled HiMuon 1.4×/3.2×/5.2×; with cross-layer batching 2.3×/3.7×/5.4× vs full-matrix Muon. Tiling adds ~+3% peak memory; batching adds ~+22–25%.
- **Kernel strategy (T=128):** SRAM-resident single-kernel gives ~4–8× over PyTorch eager; three-kernel epilogue-fusion path only helps at large batch sizes; torch.compile gives a smaller consistent gain.
- **End-to-end DDP (1000 steps):** Qwen3-0.6B 4.7% per-step reduction (3.80→3.62 s), 3.5% total wall-time, final val loss 2.391 vs 2.496 (Muon); Qwen3-1.7B 8.3% per-step (6.72→6.16 s), 7.6% total, val loss 2.152 vs 2.226. Loss differences small and sometimes favorable.
- **End-to-end FSDP (Qwen3-4B):** training loss within a few hundredths of Muon; step time 17.55→17.18 s; treated as quality/scaling check rather than timed comparison.
- **Capacity diagnostic:** Muon ≈ HiMuon-512 > -256 > -128 ≫ SGD; at d=1024, HiMuon-512 within 3% of Muon across α∈{1.25,1.5,1.75}, HiMuon-256 within 6%, HiMuon-128 trails by 11–39%. Tile size matters more for weaker (low-frequency) items; head of sampled support is tile-insensitive.
- **Low precision (associative-memory, Table 3):** fp8e4m3/bf16 change mean recovered count by ≤~1 item vs fp64, far below seed stddev (~11–16 items); operator Frobenius gaps 2–6%. Alignment γ / descent potential ϕ on quadratic proxy visually indistinguishable across precisions; tile size has the dominant visible effect (full > 512 > 256 > 128 on ϕ).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: Moderate-to-high.** The core idea (apply an existing matrix function to sub-blocks to control spectral coupling vs. cost) is not conceptually new — GLM-5, Group Muon, Boreiko et al., MuonBP, and TEON all decompose NS subproblems by head/group/sharding units. The distinguishing claim is using *fixed 2D intra-matrix tiles whose size is decoupled from the sharding layout*, making T a pure numerical/execution knob. This is a clean and useful reframing, and the SRAM-resident small-tile kernel + runtime `reconfigure()` are concrete engineering contributions, but it is an incremental refinement within an already crowded 2025–2026 "faster Muon" space (Turbo-Muon, IFNSO, DASH, MUD, MuonEq, Muon+, NorMuon, RMNP, TrasMuon, PRISM, Dion2, Magma).
- **Rigor: High on the controlled diagnostics; moderate on the end-to-end claims.** The isolated matrix-function/kernel/optimizer-step measurements are well-instrumented (CUDA events, 30–50 reps, paired trials, IQRs). The capacity diagnostic is a principled tool. However, the end-to-end training comparisons are **single-seed, 1000-step** runs — far too short to support claims about pretraining quality parity, and the authors acknowledge this. The reported wall-clock savings are explicitly "short-run measurements" where the warm-up full-matrix phase and switch overheads inflate the denominator.
- **Reproducibility: Strong.** Public code, specified software stack (PyTorch 2.6.0 / CUDA 12.4), explicit hardware (4×A40, L40S), released scripts for all experiments, full weight-shape table (Table 2), and precision protocol.
- **Significance: Conditional.** If the speedups hold at larger scale / longer training and the quality gap stays small, HiMuon is a practically useful drop-in. But the headline speedups are dominated by the small-tile regime where the capacity diagnostic shows the largest quality degradation (T=128 trails Muon by 11–39%), so the practical Pareto frontier is delicate and unresolved.
- **Red flags / over-claims:** The paper is commendably restrained — it does *not* claim tiled = full-matrix, and explicitly calls out the single-seed and short-run caveats. Minor concern: tile-schedule switch points (100/500, 200/500) and T choices are "fixed empirical choices" with no principled selection procedure (admitted as future work), so the end-to-end numbers depend on hand-tuned hyperparameters the capacity diagnostic "does not determine."

## Strengths

- Crystal-clear mathematical framing: the tile size is rigorously positioned as a *local* matrix-function map, with explicit non-commutativity with the polar factor — no hand-waving about approximation.
- Joint numerical + GPU co-design is well-motivated and well-executed: the SRAM-resident small-tile kernel is a genuinely nice engineering contribution that turns arithmetic reduction into wall-clock reduction.
- The associative-memory capacity diagnostic is a clever, cheap, principled way to isolate the *effect of the update map* from LR/stochasticity/architecture confounds — a model for how to evaluate optimizer variants.
- Honest, well-organized related work; explicit single-seed and short-run caveats; public code and scripts.
- Low-precision robustness study (fp8e4m3) is timely and reassuring for accelerator deployments.

## Limitations

- **Scale and duration:** Only up to 4B parameters, only 1000 steps, only single seed. The central practical claim (training behavior "close to full-matrix Muon") is under-powered; differences are within plausible seed variance.
- **No theory for the tiled map.** The authors flag this as future work; there is no convergence, approximation-error, or per-step-progress guarantee tying tile size to optimization dynamics on real losses.
- **Pareto frontier unresolved.** Largest speedups come from small T where the capacity diagnostic shows the steepest degradation. The paper does not give a principled procedure for choosing T or the schedule switch points — these are hand-set.
- **Limited hardware coverage.** A40/L40S only; no H100/B200, no larger-scale distributed (tensor-parallel, pipeline-parallel) evaluation. FSDP throughput tuning is deferred.
- **GQA blind spot:** k_proj/v_proj show little gain because GQA already narrows them — the method's benefit is uneven across projection types, and the schedule treats all layers uniformly.
- **Memory overhead of cross-layer batching (+22–25%)** may be problematic at the memory-constrained large-model regimes where Muon cost matters most; chunking mitigates but does not eliminate this.

## Relevance

与当前 LLM 预训练中 Muon 类优化器的算力/通信瓶颈直接相关；对在显存受限或加速器上追求更高优化器步效率、并希望在 fp8/bf16 下稳健部署 Muon 的团队有较高实用价值，但其"等价于全矩阵 Muon"的主张仍需更长、多随机种子的训练验证。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the training-loss parity survive multi-seed, long-horizon (>50k-step) pretraining at 7B–70B scale, or does the T=128 capacity gap eventually surface as a downstream-eval degradation?
- Can the tile schedule (switch points and T values) be chosen adaptively from gradient statistics or layer shape, rather than hand-tuned — e.g., via a principled capacity-bound criterion derived from (5.1)?
- What is the formal approximation theory of Φ_{K,T} vs Φ_K (spectral-norm error as a function of T and the off-tile spectral mass), and can it predict the alignment γ / descent potential ϕ ordering observed empirically?
- How does HiMuon interact with tensor parallelism and pipeline parallelism (vs only DDP/FSDP), and does the rank-local NS assumption still save the optimizer-internal collective communication at scale?
- Can per-layer (non-uniform) tile sizes exploit the GQA structure (large T for q/o/MLP, full-matrix for narrow k/v-proj) to recover uniform per-step savings without quality loss?
- Is the fp8e4m3 robustness a property of the K=5 quintic NS map specifically, and does it degrade for higher-K or alternative polynomial coefficients (e.g., PRISM-adapted)?
