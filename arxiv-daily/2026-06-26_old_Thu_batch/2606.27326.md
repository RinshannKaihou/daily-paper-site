> **一句话总结：** 本文提出将生成式世界模型中的"幻觉"问题重新定义为数据覆盖不足的问题，通过构建 MMBench2 大规模数据集（427 小时、210 任务），识别出三种幻觉模式并设计三个无需标签的预测信号，再用覆盖感知采样和定向数据采集两种方法显著降低幻觉，仅需 50 条真实轨迹即可让 350M 世界模型适配全新未见环境。

> **TL;DR:** This paper reframes hallucination in generative world models as fundamentally a data-coverage problem. The authors introduce MMBench2 (a 427-hour, 210-task benchmark with ground-truth actions/rewards and live simulators), train a 350M Dreamer-4-style world model, and show that three label-free internal signals can predict three hallucination modes (perceptual, action-marginalized, scene-diverging). The same predictors double as curiosity rewards, enabling adaptation to entirely unseen environments with as few as 50 real trajectories.

## 核心贡献

- **MMBench2 数据集**：发布一个 427 小时（约 2300 万帧）、210 个连续控制任务的视觉世界建模数据集，包含真实动作/奖励标签、实时仿真器和语言指令，是研究世界模型幻觉的理想可控实验平台。
- **幻觉的分类学**：将生成式世界模型的幻觉拆解为三种独立失败模式——感知幻觉（tokenizer）、动作边缘化幻觉（动力学模型对动作不敏感）和场景发散幻觉（多步 rollout 误差累积），每种都锚定在流水线的不同阶段。
- **三个无标签幻觉预测器**：提出 tokenizer 往返残差（u_r）、流不稳定性（u_f）和跨种子去噪方差（u_s）三个运行时可计算的信号，与 rollout 误差呈 ρ≈0.80 强相关。
- **覆盖感知训练**：通过按任务而非按帧进行均匀重采样，一次性同时降低三种幻觉模式，无需额外成本。
- **定向数据采集框架**：将幻觉预测器作为好奇心奖励，进行闭环定向探索，使预训练世界模型仅需 50 条轨迹即可适配全新未见环境，逼近人类/专家数据采集的效果。

## Key Contributions

- **MMBench2 benchmark**: A massively multitask dataset (65.6k trajectories / 427 hours / 23M frames at 224×224, 15fps) across 10 domains and 210 continuous-control tasks, with ground-truth actions, rewards, live simulators, and language instructions. 200 tasks form the pretraining corpus; 10 are held out as entirely unseen transfer tasks.
- **A three-mode taxonomy of hallucination** tied to pipeline stages: (i) perceptual (tokenizer enc-dec), (ii) action-marginalized/ignored (dynamics model insensitive to action), (iii) scene-diverging (physically implausible multi-step rollouts, e.g., a ball teleporting).
- **Three label-free, training-free predictors**: tokenizer round-trip residual, flow instability, and inter-seed denoising variance — all strongly anti-correlated (ρ≈0.80) with realized rollout error.
- **Coverage-aware training**: A single task-uniform resampling recipe that simultaneously reduces all three hallucination signals without extra cost.
- **Targeted data collection**: Using the predictors as curiosity rewards for closed-loop exploration, the pretrained 350M model adapts to unseen environments with only 50 trajectories, reaching ~90% of expert/human-oracle performance without privileged behavior.

## Methodology

The work proceeds in four coupled stages, all built around a **350M-parameter action-conditioned world model** that follows the Dreamer-4 two-stage recipe:

1. **Tokenizer** (102M params total, 50M enc + 50M dec): a symmetric Transformer encoder-decoder over 14-stride patches (256 tokens/frame) with 64 learnable latent queries, projecting to a tanh-bounded 64×64 latent code. Trained with masked auto-encoding (pixel MSE + LPIPS) and RMS loss normalization.

2. **Dynamics model** (250M params): a block-causal Transformer over packed spatial latents, conditioned on action tokens, shortcut-conditioning tokens (noise level σ, step size d), register tokens, and agent readout tokens. Trained with the **shortcut flow-matching** objective of Frans et al. (2025), interleaving a one-step regression term with a self-consistency bootstrap, enabling next-frame sampling in as few as 4 Euler substeps.

3. **Characterizing & detecting hallucination**: Three predictors are defined — `u_r` (tokenizer round-trip residual), `u_f` (flow instability across Euler substeps), and `u_s` (inter-seed denoising variance). All are normalized by per-step latent motion (`u_norm = u/m`) to remove the confound of scene activity.

4. **Mitigation**: Two complementary levers. (a) *Coverage-aware training* resamples the dataset to be uniform across tasks rather than frames (loss re-weighting was tested but inferior). (b) *Targeted data collection* rolls out candidate trajectories in the world model, scores them by `u_norm_r`, and executes the highest-scoring trajectory in the live environment (prediction horizon H=32, replanning every K=16 steps) — a curiosity-driven loop that fills coverage gaps by construction.

Evaluation spans reconstruction PSNR, rollout ΔPSNR (vs. a frame-repeating baseline), action-shuffle ratio (action sensitivity), and closed-loop MPC downstream task performance via the Cross-Entropy Method.

## Results & Highlights

- **Strong predictability**: All three predictors reach Spearman ρ ≈ −0.80 against open-loop rollout ΔPSNR on 9k held-out sequences, and outperform unnormalized variants and motion/count baselines in AUROC against "action ignored" and "scene divergent" labels.
- **Coverage-aware training moves all signals at once** (Table 1): applying it to both tokenizer and dynamics model yields +0.44 PSNR, +0.29 action-shuffle ratio, +0.88 dB rollout ΔPSNR, and reductions in all three normalized predictors.
- **Targeted collection (Table 2)**: On 10 unseen tasks, 50 trajectories/task with `u_norm_r`-based curiosity lifts MPC performance from 0.276 (zero-shot) to 0.325, within ~90% of the human/expert oracles (0.362), and clearly above random/no-op collection. The "All combined" setting reaches 0.390.
- **Tokenizer generalization (Table 3)**: The in-domain tokenizer crushes off-the-shelf VAEs (SD-VAE, Cosmos, Wan 2.1, DC-AE) on the 200 training tasks (38.93 vs 36.45 dB best) but generalizes poorly to unseen tasks until finetuned (Post-FT reaches 38.04 dB, ΔS−U drops to +1.62). Off-the-shelf tokenizers are promising but in-domain finetuning still wins when available.
- **Scale**: Trained on 8× H100 GPUs, 58 GPU-days total (300k tokenizer steps + 180k dynamics steps, plus mid-training and finetuning), context length T=24.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — High.** The framing — that hallucination is a *single underlying coverage problem* manifesting as three pipeline-stage-specific modes — is a genuine conceptual reframing, not an incremental architectural tweak. Turning three label-free internal signals into both diagnostics *and* curiosity rewards is a clean, unifying insight. The connection to existing epistemic-uncertainty / coverage literature (Levine, Gadre, Gal & Ghahramani, Chua) is honest and well-cited rather than over-claimed.
- **Rigor — Good, with caveats.** The ρ≈0.80 correlations are compelling, and the inclusion of an AUROC study against two binary labels is appropriate. However, the central "single underlying cause" claim rests largely on the *convergence* of three predictors — and since the authors themselves note the three metrics "all track the same underlying error," the three-mechanism taxonomy risks being more of a narrative device than a causally demonstrated distinction. The paper would be stronger with controlled ablations that isolate each mode (e.g., does fixing perceptual coverage alone *not* help action-marginalization?).
- **Reproducibility — Strong on paper.** 8× H100, 58 GPU-days is expensive but standard for this regime; authors promise full dataset, code, checkpoints, and a browser UI. The closed-loop MPC/CEM eval protocol and the action-shuffle / ΔPSNR metrics are well-specified. Caveat: 58 GPU-days plus a 427-hour custom dataset is a high bar for independent reproduction.
- **Significance — High for the world-model community.** If the coverage-centric view holds at larger scale, it redirects effort away from "train a bigger backbone" toward data curation and runtime uncertainty estimation — a potentially influential reframing for RL, robotics, and generative simulation.
- **Red flags / open questions:**
  - **Scale generalization is unproven.** All findings are at 350M params on simulated control. The authors explicitly flag this; whether the "coverage, not capacity" thesis survives at billion-parameter scale or on real-robot data is the single biggest risk to the conclusions.
  - **Evaluation narrowness.** Closed-loop MPC measures model accuracy around a narrow goal-directed subspace; the authors acknowledge this raises a "broader question about how we evaluate world models." This is a real confound for the transfer/adaptation claims.
  - **Curiosity vs. oracle gap.** Curiosity-based collection (0.325) still trails human/expert (0.362); the "~90%" framing is favorable. The headline "50 trajectories" is a per-task count on only 10 unseen tasks, so the effective data budget is modest but the task count is small.
  - **Potential circularity.** The same `u_norm_r` used to *collect* data is also one of the *reported* success metrics (Table 2 lists `u_norm_r ↓`); a reader should be mindful that the predictor is partly self-fulfilling when judged on itself.

## Strengths

- A genuinely unifying conceptual thesis (coverage → three hallucination modes) that yields both diagnostic and mitigation tools from the same signals.
- MMBench2 is a substantial, well-instrumented community resource (actions, rewards, live sims, language instructions, seen/unseen split).
- Practically cheap predictors: no labels, no extra training, computable at runtime.
- Two complementary, deployable recipes (offline resampling; online curiosity collection) that compose cleanly with the Dreamer-4 stack.
- Honest limitations section and a candid discussion of why not all data sources are equally informative.

## Limitations

- Findings confined to 350M params and simulated continuous control; real-robot / billion-parameter transfer is unverified.
- The three hallucination modes are defined by *stage* rather than independently induced, so their separability is asserted more than causally isolated.
- High compute cost (58 GPU-days, 8× H100) and a large custom dataset limit independent reproduction.
- Evaluation via goal-directed MPC measures only a narrow subspace of state-action space, leaving broad-coverage modeling quality under-measured.
- The curiosity reward and one of the headline metrics coincide (`u_norm_r`), creating mild circularity.
- Off-the-shelf tokenizers (e.g., Wan 2.1 VAE) already match in-domain performance on *unseen* tasks without finetuning, which slightly tempers the "in-domain tokenizer" advantage story.

本文对世界模型、具身智能与生成式仿真方向具有较高参考价值：它把"幻觉"从架构问题重新归为数据覆盖问题，并给出可运行时检测、可指导数据采集的轻量信号，对模型训练数据策略、不确定性估计和快速环境适配都有直接借鉴意义。对从事 RL、机器人学习或生成式世界模型的研究者尤其值得关注。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the "coverage, not capacity" thesis hold at billion-parameter scale, and does the relative importance of the three modes shift as model size grows?
- Can the three predictors be causally decoupled — e.g., does closing *only* perceptual coverage leave action-marginalization unchanged, validating the taxonomy?
- How do the predictors transfer to real-robot data with sensor noise, partial observability, and non-stationary dynamics?
- Can the curiosity reward be combined with intrinsic motivation or goal-conditioned exploration to close the remaining gap to human/expert oracles (0.325 → 0.362)?
- Since off-the-shelf tokenizers (Wan 2.1 VAE) already generalize well to unseen tasks, would a hybrid (off-the-shelf tokenizer + in-domain dynamics model) be a cheaper route to robust perception?
- How sensitive are the ρ≈0.80 correlations to context length T, action dimensionality, and the choice of flow-matching shortcut schedule?
