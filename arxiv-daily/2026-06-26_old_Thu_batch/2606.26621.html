λ-PSD: Scalable Approximate SNR-Optimised Polynomial Stein Discrepancies

Minh-Long Nguyen et al. — Queensland University of Technology · Centre for Data Science · MACSYS
◆ Key Finding

λ-PSD repairs an exponential SNR² collapse in polynomial Stein discrepancies via covariance-aware Rayleigh-quotient reweighting — at linear-time cost.

Why This Matters

A hyperparameter-stable, linear-time goodness-of-fit test that reframes discrepancy design from “max signal” to “max SNR” — a reusable principle for scalable Stein methods and MCMC diagnostics.

⬣ Critical Edge

The exact SNR-optimal estimator is dismissed as infeasible; λ-PSD is a heuristic with only an upper SNR bound — and every stability guarantee holds solely under Gaussianity.

No approximation-ratio vs the optimum; RBM gains may be partly fortuitous.

Methodology

The paper first introduces PSD_L1, a first-order (Langevin-Stein) polynomial discrepancy with closed-form maximiser, written as a V/U-statistic of rank Qd² + d over an augmented Stein feature vector ξ(x). This is distinct from the second-order PSD_L2 of Srinivasan et al. (2025).

Using Hoeffding decomposition, Theorem 3.5 gives the exact SNR² scaling; under p=N(0,I_d), q=N(δ1,σ²I_d) the moment-dominant rate is SNR²(Q) ≍ ((Q+1)!!/(2Q+1)!!)² — super-exponential decay that also infects the split-sample z-statistic.

  1. The fully optimal objective J(θ)=(θᵀμ)²/(θᵀΣθ) is statistically ill-posed (rank-deficient Σ) and costs O(Q³d⁶) to invert.
  2. λ-PSD restricts optimisation to the (Q+1)-dim span of already-computed PSD features, adding one scalar weight λ per moment order; the optimum is λ⋆ ∝ Σ_Ψ⁻¹μ_Ψ.
  3. λ⋆ is estimated by cross-entropy (CE) stochastic optimisation under a Bernoulli-Gaussian prior that induces implicit regularisation/pruning; the final statistic is calibrated via a split-sample CLT-based z-test (Prop. 3.7), avoiding O(BnQd²) bootstrap.

Strengths

  • Proves a genuine, previously-misunderstood pathology rather than treating it as noise.
  • Rayleigh-quotient / SNR-maximisation reformulation transfers beyond PSD.
  • Linear-time in n and markedly robust to the choice of Q.
  • Competitive power vs quadratic-time KSD, incl. dependent-sampling RBM.
  • Candid about asymptotic and dependent-sampling limitations.

Limitations

  • Core guarantees restricted to Gaussian targets/samplers only.
  • Exact optimum never computed; λ-PSD has no formal approximation bound.
  • Synthetic benchmarks only — no real MCMC/posterior diagnostics.
  • CE optimiser for λ adds unstudied hyperparameters and stochasticity.
  • z-test validity needs i.i.d. or variance-correction under dependence.

Open Questions

  1. Can the SNR-decay diagnosis and Rayleigh-quotient principle extend to log-concave / mixture targets with non-asymptotic bounds?
  2. What is the formal approximation gap between λ-PSD and the infeasible o-PSD_L1 optimum — and is there a principled rank rule?
  3. How does λ-PSD behave on real MCMC output (SGLD, high-dim posteriors) with dependent, mini-batch-estimated scores?
  4. Would ridge-regularised closed-form Σ_Ψ⁻¹μ_Ψ beat CE estimation for z-test stability?
  5. How does it compare to learned/adaptive neural Stein critics when the discrepancy is not moment-localised?
