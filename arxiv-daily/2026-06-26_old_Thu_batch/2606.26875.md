> **一句话总结：** 本文从"向前看"的视角重新审视 KV 缓存压缩中 token 的重要性，提出基于熵（预测不确定性）的 InfoKV 框架，将信息论信号与注意力分数结合，在长上下文推理（长 prefilling 和长 decoding）任务上稳定超越现有基于注意力的压缩方法。

> **TL;DR:** InfoKV reinterprets KV cache compression through a forward-looking lens, introducing "Forward Influence" to reveal that high-entropy (high-uncertainty) tokens dominate long-range future impact whereas attention scores only capture short-range relevance. By combining predictive entropy, layer-wise representation evolution, and attention scores, InfoKV consistently beats attention-based compressors on long prefilling (LongReason) and long decoding (IFEval, AIME 2024, LiveCodeBench) benchmarks.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"Forward Influence"度量指标**：通过测量从 KV 缓存中移除某 token 后未来预测分布的 KL 散度变化，定量刻画压缩 token 对未来上下文的真实影响，为 token 重要性提供了"向前看"的评估视角。
- **揭示注意力与熵的互补性**：实验分析表明，注意力高的 token 主要影响近距离未来上下文（影响随距离快速衰减），而高熵（高预测不确定性）token 对远距离未来上下文有更强、更持久的影响力。
- **提出 InfoKV 框架**：将 token 级预测熵（Top-k Restricted Entropy）、层间表示演化（early layer 到 final layer 的余弦距离）与注意力分数融合，为每层生成综合重要性分数用于 KV 缓存压缩。
- **在长 prefilling 和长 decoding 双场景验证**：在 Llama-3.1/3.2 + LongReason 以及 DeepSeek-R1-Distill + IFEval/AIME 2024/LiveCodeBench 上全面优于 SnapKV、PyramidKV、Expected Attention、RPC 等基线。
- **提出层自适应预算分配变体**：根据各层熵统计动态分配 KV 预算（部分模型有效但不稳定，故默认采用 uniform 策略）。

## Key Contributions

- **Introduces "Forward Influence,"** a principled metric measuring the KL divergence in future predictive distributions caused by removing a token from the KV cache, reframing token importance as future-utility rather than past-relevance.
- **Demonstrates attention-entropy complementarity:** attention-weighted tokens govern short-range future contexts while high-entropy tokens exert stronger, more persistent influence on distant future contexts (validated via Figure 1 & 2 across 128-token and 14K-token horizons).
- **Proposes InfoKV,** an entropy-aware compression framework fusing token-level predictive uncertainty (Top-k Restricted Entropy), layer-wise representation evolution (cosine distance between early and final hidden states), and attention scores into a unified per-layer importance score.
- **Validates on both long-prefilling and long-decoding** scenarios across multiple model families (Llama-3.1-8B, Llama-3.2-3B, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B, R1-Distill-Llama-8B), achieving SOTA or highly competitive results under 40% and 20% cache budgets.
- **Introduces an adaptive layer-wise budget variant** that allocates cache proportionally to accumulated entropy per layer, with empirical caveats on cross-architecture stability.

## Methodology

**Motivation via Forward Influence.** The authors define the Forward Influence of token `x_i` on a future chunk `{x_lc,...,x_rc}` as the average KL divergence between the original predictive distribution and the distribution obtained after removing `x_i` from the KV cache:

  `I(x_i) = (1/(rc-lc+1)) * Σ D_KL(p(x_n|x_<n) ‖ p(x_n|x_<n \ {x_i}))`

Using Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, they rank the first 2048 tokens by three criteria (attention, entropy, combined) and measure influence over short (128-token) and long (14K-token) horizons. The combined score (`S_i = A_i + Softmax(H)_i`) best balances both ranges.

**InfoKV importance score.** For each layer, InfoKV computes:
1. **Predictive entropy** `H_i` of each token (Eq. 2), using **Top-k Restricted Entropy** (k=256) over the most probable tokens for stability, since vocabulary-level entropy is dominated by low-probability noise.
2. **Layer-wise representation evolution** `D_i^(l) = 1 - cos(h_i^(l), h_i^(L))` (Eq. 6): the cosine distance between the hidden state at early layer `l` and the final layer. Tokens whose representations shift more across layers carry more unresolved semantic information.
3. **Entropy score** `E_i^(l) = D_i^(l) · H_i` (Eq. 7), with a bias `τ` added so the final layer's score is non-zero.
4. **Final importance** `S_i^(l) = α · A_i^(l) + (1-α) · Softmax(E^(l))_i` (Eq. 8). Top-ranked tokens are retained per layer.

**Default hyperparameters:** `τ = 1`, `k = 256` (top-k restricted entropy), `α = 0.9`. Adaptive variant allocates budget `k_l ∝ accumulated entropy` per layer (Eq. 9-10).

**Decoding-stage application.** Compression is triggered every 1024 generated tokens following the RPC protocol, supporting online long-decoding compression for reasoning models.

## Results & Highlights

- **Long prefilling (LongReason):** InfoKV achieves SOTA or competitive accuracy on Llama-3.1-8B and Llama-3.2-3B across 16k–64k context lengths and 40%/20% cache rates, in both CoT and direct-answer settings. The advantage grows with sequence length, indicating better retention of globally informative tokens.
- **Long decoding (DeepSeek-R1-Distill):** InfoKV beats RPC on IFEval, AIME 2024, and LiveCodeBench. Notably, on IFEval, InfoKV at 25% and 12.5% retention **surpasses the full-cache** baseline of R1-Distill-Llama-8B — suggesting long reasoning trajectories contain redundancy and that aggressive compression can actually improve generation by removing distracting contexts.
- **Ablations:** (i) `τ = 1` is optimal and stable; (ii) Top-256 restricted entropy outperforms full-vocabulary entropy; (iii) `α = 0.9` (mostly attention + small entropy contribution) outperforms pure attention (`α = 1`) or entropy-dominated settings, confirming complementarity.
- **Adaptive budget:** Improves R1-Distill-Llama-8B at 25% on IFEval (60.07 → 61.18) but degrades R1-Distill-Qwen-7B (59.70 → 55.82), so uniform is adopted by default.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: Moderate-to-strong.** The core insight — that predictive entropy is a better proxy than attention for *long-range* future token utility — is well-motivated and empirically demonstrated via the Forward Influence analysis (Figures 1-2), which is the paper's strongest contribution. The fusion of entropy + layer-wise representation distance + attention is a reasonable engineering combination, though each individual component draws on existing ideas (Selective Context, SH2, residual-connection representation evolution).
- **Rigor: Solid.** Experiments span two distinct regimes (prefilling and decoding), four models, multiple benchmarks, cache rates, and context lengths. Ablations isolate the contributions of `τ`, top-k, and `α`. Comparisons use official baseline implementations under matched settings.
- **Reproducibility: Good but incomplete.** Hyperparameters and evaluation protocols are specified; however, (a) code is "to be released later," (b) evaluation relies on sampling (1/8/4 completions) whose variance is not reported with confidence intervals, and (c) some benchmark configurations (e.g., exact LongReason splits) are delegated to Appendix B without full detail in the main text.
- **Significance: High practical value.** KV cache compression for long reasoning (especially post-DeepSeek-R1-style chain-of-thought) is a pressing deployment bottleneck. The finding that compressed caches can *outperform* full caches on IFEval is notable and counterintuitive.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - The reported gains over baselines on LongReason are sometimes modest (1-2 accuracy points), and without error bars it is hard to judge statistical significance.
  - The Forward Influence analysis motivating the method uses a simplified assumption ("all layers share the same token choice"), which diverges from the actual per-layer InfoKV mechanism — the bridge between the analysis and the proposed method is somewhat indirect.
  - Entropy is acknowledged (in Limitations) to be only an *indirect approximation* of future utility, not an explicit optimization objective — leaving open whether a directly-trained compressor could do better.
  - Adaptive budgeting is presented as a feature but largely abandoned due to instability, signaling the method's robustness across architectures is not fully resolved.

## Strengths

- Principled motivation: Forward Influence provides a concrete, measurable definition of "future utility" rather than relying on intuition.
- Clear empirical demonstration of attention-entropy complementarity (Figures 1 & 2 are compelling).
- Consistent improvements across both prefilling and decoding, and across both dense (Llama) and distilled reasoning (DeepSeek-R1) models.
- The counterintuitive full-cache-beating result on IFEval has practical deployment implications.
- Thorough ablations on all three hyperparameters (`τ`, `k`, `α`).

## Limitations

- Modest absolute gains on some LongReason settings; no error bars or significance testing reported.
- Entropy remains an indirect proxy for future utility — not an explicit optimization target.
- Adaptive layer-wise budgeting helps some architectures but harms others, so the default is uniform (a simpler, less expressive strategy).
- Forward Influence motivation uses a simplified shared-token-across-layers assumption inconsistent with per-layer InfoKV.
- Code not yet released at submission; full reproducibility deferred.

## Relevance

本文针对大语言模型长链式推理场景下的 KV 缓存膨胀问题，提出基于信息熵的压缩方法，对当前推理模型（如 DeepSeek-R1、o1 类）的部署效率和成本优化高度相关，是一篇兼具理论洞察与工程实用价值的工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does InfoKV interact with more recent attention-based compressors that incorporate future-attention estimation (e.g., Expected Attention) when combined rather than compared?
- Could Forward Influence be used as a *training* signal (e.g., a differentiable surrogate) to learn a directly-optimized compressor rather than relying on entropy as an indirect proxy?
- What explains the architecture-dependent behavior of adaptive budgeting (Qwen-7B degrades, Llama-8B improves) — is it a function of layer count, attention head patterns, or pretraining distribution?
- How does InfoKV perform under extreme compression (≤10% budget) or on even longer horizons (>64k) where entropy's long-range advantage should theoretically amplify?
- Does combining InfoKV with token-merging approaches (rather than pure eviction) yield additive gains?
- What is the wall-clock and memory overhead of computing per-layer entropy + cosine distance during inference, and does the accuracy gain justify the added compute?
