NebulaExp-8B: An Empirical Post-Training Pipeline via Full-Scale Ablation Research

ZTE NebulaL0 Team — Qiaobo Hao et al.

Key Finding

At 8B scale, data-correctness filtering dominates post-training; teacher-distribution compatibility outweighs teacher parameter count for on-policy distillation.

post-training SFT RL distillation data-curation

Key Metric

+4.18
avg score gain from multi-teacher OPD (10K samples, 72.42 → 76.60), surpassing each teacher ceiling on AIME'25.

Why This Matters

A reproducible 8B post-training recipe — data curation, regression mixture search, verifier-free distillation — with reusable heuristics.

Critical Edge

CAVEAT

The headline +35.90 ZebraLogic gain may reflect benchmark-leakage overlap the authors defer to future work rather than resolve.

Methodology

All stages run on Qwen3-8B-base with fixed eval protocols (temp 0.7 / top-p 0.8 for instruct; temp 0.6 with 16-sample averaging for AIME). Post-training is treated as a sequence of independently ablable stages, not a monolithic run.

Data curation. A 3.84M raw SFT corpus is re-distilled for style consistency, then filtered by query/response/answer verification (Docker-isolated math/code execution, multi-model rejection sampling, 54 IF rule checkers). Samples are difficulty-graded via base-model pass@4 into low/medium/high, classified by a trained Qwen3-1.7B, and diversity-sampled via embedding clustering.

Instruct branch. Three-stage SFT using four quality indicators (Length, Entropy, Loss, IFD=ratio form — explicitly preferred over the subtraction variant which biases toward short responses), within-domain adaptive sampling, then gain-based cross-domain mixing. GRPO RL follows on a 53K filtered subset, 6 epochs, KL retained, rollout size 5.

Reasoning branch. Regression-based mixture search over math/code/science (optimum 0.6 : 0.2 : 0.2), short-to-long curriculum learning, category dedup + multi-epoch training. Reasoning RL uses GRPO on 8K medium-difficulty samples, two epochs, correctness + format rewards only.

On-Policy Distillation (OPD). The student generates y ∼ πS(·|x); the frozen teacher supplies per-token distributions, and the student minimizes reverse-KL restricted to the teacher's top-64 tokens (to bound memory over a 128K+ vocab). Multi-teacher OPD routes four RL-trained domain-specialist 8B teachers by sample domain.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Unusual transparency on data construction (distillation teachers, filter taxonomy, difficulty thresholds, classifier training) most papers withhold.
  • Principled three-stage SFT with clean leave-one-out indicator ablations; IFD ratio-vs-subtraction analysis is theoretically grounded.
  • Regression-based mixture-ratio search is an elegant, cost-efficient formulation.
  • Honest reporting of regressions (alignment tax, LiveBench, saturated MATH-500), including failed/abnormal MOPD runs.
  • Strong data-efficiency story — 4K / 10K OPD samples rival a 53K-sample RL run.

Limitations

  • Entirely 8B-scale; generalization to larger dense and MoE models is unverified and flagged as open.
  • RL restricted to verifiable tasks (math/code/IF); agentic, long-horizon, tool-use settings not covered.
  • OPD teachers are also 8B; cross-architecture and stronger-teacher regimes unexplored.
  • Evaluation is benchmark-centered; real-world agent robustness may be missed.
  • ZebraLogic / AutoLogi transfer claims' contamination control is acknowledged as unresolved.
  • Reverse-KL top-64 truncation may drop low-probability-but-important tokens; no ablation of this choice.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Do the "math/code opposite distributions" zero-sum conflict and "hard-only > curriculum" findings persist at 30B+ or MoE scale, or are they artifacts of 8B capacity limits?
  2. How much of the ZebraLogic +35.90 and AIME gains from Other-domain long-form text are genuine reasoning transfer vs. latent benchmark overlap — and can it be disentangled with held-out puzzle generators?
  3. What is the marginal value of the top-64 reverse-KL truncation — would a probability-aware or full-vocab approximation close the remaining 3–8 point student–teacher gap?
  4. Can the cross-teacher recombination that lets MOPD surpass individual teachers on math be characterized and deliberately engineered (e.g., via orthogonal teacher specialization)?
  5. How do these recipes transfer to agentic / tool-use post-training, where both verifiers and teachers behave very differently?
