> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Erase-then-Delta Attention (EDA)，在 delta 规则线性注意力中"先擦除、再写入"，让模型可以在与当前写入地址不同的独立地址上有针对性地清除过期记忆，从而在不破坏 delta 纠正写入的前提下显著提升语言模型（dense 2.5B 与 MoE 25B-A2.8B）的预训练与长上下文表现。

> **TL;DR:** EDA decouples *where* a delta-rule linear-attention model erases stale memory from *where* it writes new content: a learned, independently-addressed erase step runs before the standard delta corrective write, giving the recurrent state a targeted cleanup path. It tops dense 2.5B and MoE 25B-A2.8B pretraining baselines, keeps the lead after 80B-token long-context midtraining, and probes show the erase address is used as a genuinely independent cleanup direction.

## 核心贡献

- **地址级解耦的擦除机制**：首次指出 delta 规则更新中"擦除与写入共用同一个地址 (write key k_t)"这一结构性耦合，并提出在与当前写入地址不同的独立学习方向 e_t 上执行定向擦除。
- **三层次记忆管理视图**：把记忆更新明确划分为对角衰减 (D_t)、独立地址擦除 (γ_t e_t)、写入耦合纠正 (β_t k_t) 三个不同特异性的层次，提供更清晰的理论框架。
- **保持 delta 写入不变**：擦除操作在 delta 纠正写入之前执行，因此 delta 规则的核心纠正写入行为完全保留，新增的是"清除路径"而非更强的遗忘门。
- **跨规模、跨场景的实证优势**：在 dense 2.5B 与 MoE 25B-A2.8B 两个规模、400B token 预训练 + 80B token 长上下文中训练、4k–128k RULER 长上下文评测中均取得最佳或领先的平均成绩。
- **机制可解释性分析**：通过门强度分配、读出扰动反事实实验和擦除/写入几何检查，证明擦除地址确实是一条独立的条件性清理路径，而非均匀增强历史召回。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifies an address-level coupling limitation** in gated delta-rule linear attention: both the corrective delta edit and the active erase are anchored to the *same* current write key k_t, so stale content stored at a different address can only passively decay, not be actively removed.
- **Introduces Erase-then-Delta Attention (EDA)**, an update rule `S_t = (I − β_t k_t k_tᵀ)(I − γ_t e_t e_tᵀ) D_t S_{t−1} + β_t k_t v_tᵀ` that inserts an independently addressed, L2-normalized erase operator `(I − γ_t e_t e_tᵀ)` before the standard delta corrective write, with a learned erase gate γ_t and erase address e_t.
- **Derives the erase address from an online objective** that pushes the readout at e_t toward zero, so e_t is "the address whose current memory response is explicitly pushed toward zero," not just an extra projection.
- **Provides a chunk-wise parallel formulation** by interleaving erase and delta sub-steps into a doubled sequence, reducing EDA to a standard diagonal-plus-low-rank (DPLR) recurrence that reuses existing KDA-style kernels (to be released in FlashQLA).
- **Shows empirical superiority** across dense 2.5B and MoE 25B-A2.8B pretraining, after long-context midtraining, and in long-context RULER evaluation.
- **Mechanistic probes** confirm near-orthogonal separation of erase/write directions (mean |cos(e,k)| ≈ 0.105) and that the erase path dominates the contraction budget exactly when passive decay is weak (ᾱ ≥ 0.9 → erase contributes 69.1%).

## Methodology

**Setting.** Hybrid linear-attention architectures with a 3:1 ratio of linear-attention to full-attention layers (following Qwen3.5 / Kimi Linear conventions). The recurrent state S_t ∈ ℝ^{d_k × d_v} is updated with a channel-wise gated delta rule (diagonal decay D_t = Diag(α_t)).

**Core update rule (Eq. 8).** EDA inserts an erase operator between diagonal decay and the delta write:
`S_t = (I − β_t k_t k_tᵀ)(I − γ_t e_t e_tᵀ) D_t S_{t−1} + β_t k_t v_tᵀ`.
The erase address e_t is derived from an online reconstruction-loss objective `L_erase = ½‖S̃_tᵀ e_t‖²` minimized with step γ_t, so e_t is the direction whose current readout is actively suppressed toward zero.

**Three-level memory management.** (1) diagonal decay D_t controls per-channel retention; (2) independent erasure γ_t e_t removes content at a learned, address-specific direction; (3) write-coupled correction β_t k_t performs the standard delta fix. The erase-then-delta order is essential: reversing it would let cleanup suppress newly written content.

**Safe bounded gate (Eq. 15).** EDA introduces a bounded log-space decay gate `g_t = ℓ + (−ℓ) exp(−A/|ℓ| ⊙ Δ_t)` that keeps each decay factor in (exp(ℓ), 1] with ℓ = −5, preventing subnormal/zero values and enabling stable half-precision TensorCore-friendly computation — unlike the unbounded-below Mamba2/GDN gate.

**Cross-term analysis (Eq. 16).** The product of the two rank-1 operators produces a cross-term proportional to cos(e_t, k_t); empirically this stays small (≈ 0.105), so the update is well-approximated by two independent corrections on near-orthogonal subspaces.

**Chunk-wise parallelism (Sec 3.4).** Each EDA step is expanded into two sub-steps (odd = erase+decay, even = delta with identity decay), doubling the effective sequence length and letting EDA reuse the standard DPLR chunk-wise WY/UT-transform kernels from KDA.

**Efficiency (Sec 3.5).** Only e_t and γ_t are extra kernel inputs; HBM-traffic overhead is modest after fusion. The chunk-forward is memory-bound so wall-clock impact is moderate, and is even smaller in autoregressive decoding where state I/O dominates.

**Experiments.** Dense 2.5B (Transformer, GDN, GDN-2, KDA, EDA) and MoE 25B-A2.8B (GDN, KDA, EDA); 400B-token pretraining at seq 4096; plus 80B-token midtraining at 32k for MoE. Benchmarks: MMLU, MMLU-Pro, GSM8K, MATH, BBH, EvalPlus; RULER for 4k–128k long context.

## Results & Highlights

- **Dense 2.5B (400B tokens):** EDA tops the average (28.44 vs KDA 27.81, GDN-2 28.14, Transformer 27.75), +0.63 over KDA which shares the same channel-wise gated delta backbone. Notable gains on GSM8K (23.90) and EvalPlus (32.50).
- **MoE 25B-A2.8B (400B tokens):** EDA best on Avg (49.38 vs KDA 48.31, GDN 48.16) and most individual benchmarks; strong on knowledge, reasoning, and code.
- **After 80B-token 32k midtraining:** EDA retains the lead (Avg 58.45 vs KDA 58.31, GDN 57.37), with especially clear gains on MMLU-Pro (+1.11), MATH (+2.41), BBH.
- **Long-context RULER (4k–128k):** EDA Avg 78.32 beats GDN (77.45) and KDA (77.02); strongest in 4k–16k, competitive in 32k–128k length-extrapolation regime.
- **Memory-state analysis (Fig. 2):** Global allocation is 35.4% decay / 31.8% delta / 32.8% erase. For near-persistent heads (ᾱ ≥ 0.9), independent erase contributes 69.1% — ~3.0× the same-address correction — exactly where stale content would otherwise survive decay.
- **Counterfactual readout control:** Replacing the learned e_t with Random / Shuffle / same-address (e_t = k_t) increases local readout perturbation by ~2.4× / 1.9× / 3.4× respectively, showing the learned direction is the most controlled cleanup.
- **Erase/write geometry:** mean |cos(e_t, k_t)| ≈ 0.105 across layers, close to the analytic independent-random reference and far from 1 (same-address collapse), empirically validating address decoupling.

Kernel code is promised via the QwenLM/FlashQLA repository. ## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — Moderate/High.** The address-level decoupling idea is genuinely new and crisply distinguished from GDN-2's *gate-level* decoupling (GDN-2 decouples *how strongly* erase vs write happen but keeps both anchored at k_t; EDA decouples *where*). The online objective interpretation (e_t = direction whose readout is pushed to zero) is elegant. However, the update is algebraically a small addition — one extra rank-1 factor — and the "two Householder-like factors per step" idea rhymes with DeltaProduct's multi-factor transitions, even if the motivation differs.
- **Rigor — Strong.** Matched-scale comparisons, controlled ablations against the closest baselines (KDA shares the exact backbone), parameter counts reported, multi-run averaging, and an honest boundary check (raw write-key recall: KDA is better, so EDA is *not* uniformly better recall). The mechanistic probes are well-designed (counterfactual directions, geometry reference).
- **Reproducibility — Promising but incomplete at submission.** Kernel release is *promised* (FlashQLA) and full hyperparameters are in Appendix A, but no checkpoints or training data are mentioned; reproducing 400B-token MoE runs is infeasible for most labs. The chunk-wise reduction to DPLR is clearly specified, which aids kernel reimplementation.
- **Significance — Moderate/High if it holds.** Gains are real but modest (≤ 1.1 Avg points at MoE scale). The bigger value is the *conceptual* contribution: a principled three-level memory-management view that other delta-rule variants could adopt. Whether the independent-erase idea compounds with richer transitions (DeltaProduct, RWKV-7) is untested.
- **Red flags / caveats.** (1) The gate-strength "allocation" metric (Eq. 24) is explicitly *not* an exact state-energy decomposition — it collapses the diagonal operator to a scalar mean, so the 69% figure should be read as a heuristic share, not a rigorous budget. The authors flag this, but headlines could be misread. (2) EDA *reduces* raw write-key recall (KDA wins that probe) — so it is a conditional cleanup mechanism, not a free improvement. (3) Long-context advantage shrinks beyond 32k; at 128k EDA is comparable, not clearly better. (4) Probes do not trace individual erase events to specific downstream prediction gains — the causal link from mechanism to benchmark win remains correlational. (5) Only one layer/head is analyzed in depth for the mechanism claims.

## Strengths

- Clean, minimal, well-motivated modification that preserves the delta-rule corrective write.
- Strong experimental breadth: two model families, pretraining + midtraining, downstream + long-context, plus mechanistic probes.
- Honest positioning vs GDN-2 (orthogonal axes of decoupling) and vs transition-enrichment work.
- Thoughtful engineering: bounded safe gate that keeps decay factors in fp16-friendly range and enables half-precision chunk tensors.
- Counterfactual and geometry-based mechanistic analysis is more convincing than typical ablations.

## Limitations

- The reported gains are modest (sub-1-point Avg at MoE scale) and within plausible run-to-run variance for some benchmarks.
- Introducing the independent erase step *hurts* raw write-key recall, so it is a conditional cleanup, not a uniform fidelity win.
- The headline gate-allocation numbers are based on a scalar-mean heuristic, not an exact operator decomposition.
- Long-context advantage attenuates at 64k–128k (extrapolation regime).
- No released checkpoints/data; 400B-token MoE pretraining is out of reach for most groups.
- Mechanistic analysis is largely confined to a single layer/head and does not causally link erase events to specific prediction improvements.
- No direct combination with richer transition methods (DeltaProduct, RWKV-7) is tested, so the claimed orthogonality is asserted rather than demonstrated.

## 相关性

EDA 针对的是高效线性注意力 / 混合架构（Qwen3.5、Kimi Linear 一类）的核心组件，对追求低 KV-cache、长上下文推理的下一代 LLM 架构设计直接相关。其"擦除与写入地址解耦"的思想可作为 delta 规则变体的通用插件，值得在更长上下文、更大规模以及与 transition-enrichment 方法组合的场景下进一步验证。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the independent-erase degree of freedom *compound* with richer transitions (DeltaProduct's Householder products, RWKV-7's DPLR gating)? The paper claims orthogonality but does not test it.
- Can the gate-allocation analysis be made into a *rigorous* state-energy decomposition rather than a scalar-mean heuristic?
- Why does the long-context advantage shrink beyond 32k — does the erase address lose selectivity under length extrapolation, and can positional/decay scheduling recover it?
- The erase direction is learned end-to-end; can it be made *content-addressed* (e.g., conditioned on an explicit "invalidate this key" signal) to better handle variable reassignment / fact correction?
- What is the inference-time wall-clock and memory overhead on real serving stacks (not just chunk-forward FLOPs), and does it scale favorably to 1M+ context?
- Is the reduced raw write-key recall a problem for retrieval-heavy tasks, and can a read-time gating mechanism recover it without sacrificing the cleanup benefit?
