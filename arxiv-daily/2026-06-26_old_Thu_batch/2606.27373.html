Paying More Attention to Visual Tokens in Self-Evolving Large Multimodal Models

⚠ Critical Edge
Largest gains concentrate at 2B and shrink markedly at 32B; the reward is fundamentally a localization/visibility signal, so improvements on hard reasoning benchmarks stay modest at frontier scale.

Methodology

VISE reframes self-evolution around the decoder's visual-conditioning policy rather than answer agreement. A single policy πθ operates in a self-questioning regime: it first generates a localization query q about an unlabeled image x, then predicts a bounding box B for the queried object under the same q. Coordinates live in a normalized [0,1000]⁴ space; the vision encoder is frozen and only the projector, FFNs, and attention projections are updated via LoRA.

Geometric reward R_geo: sample a known transform T (affine/crop/flip) with matrix M; predict B_new on x'=T(x), project the original box analytically, and reward R_geo = (GIoU(B_proj, B_new)+1)/2. A geometry-ignoring decoder cannot stay consistent across views and is penalized.

Semantic reward R_sem ("ghosting"): Gaussian-blur (σ=25) the predicted region to produce x̃ while preserving context; greedily decode visibility before and after. R_sem = 1 only if the model sees the object when intact and recognizes its absence when obscured — penalizing evidence-agnostic generation.

Optimization: R_t = 0.5·R_geo + 0.5·R_sem, with an EMA baseline, a REINFORCE loss, and an adaptive KL term (target τ=0.020, rate η=0.10) against a frozen reference policy. Training uses 4000 raw unlabeled COCO images, 4000 steps on 8× AMD MI250X GPUs, bfloat16, AdamW — no QA pairs, annotations, or external models.

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated diagnosis of visual under-conditioning with direct mechanistic evidence (attention ratios, CKA).
  • Fully unsupervised, single-model, no external reward models or annotations — minimal moving parts.
  • Simple, closed-form, complementary reward design; ablations show each component contributes.
  • Broad, consistent, tradeoff-free gains across tasks, scales, and architectures — no captioning-vs-reasoning tradeoff.
  • Full open-source release (code + weights) and tiny compute budget (4000 images, 4000 steps).

Limitations

  • Largest gains at 2B; benefits attenuate at 32B, so frontier-scale value is open.
  • Reward is localization/visibility-centric — grounding necessary but possibly not sufficient for hardest reasoning.
  • Absolute captioning stays modest (COCO CIDEr 38.4 at 2B); gains are large relatively, not in absolute SOTA terms.
  • Only 4000 COCO images by default; distribution/quantity sensitivity only partly explored (Objects365 in supplement).
  • "Ghosting" relies on greedy binary decoding — robustness to query/region ambiguity not deeply analyzed.

Open Questions

  1. Do geometric and semantic invariance rewards transfer to video and 3D/embodied LMMs, where temporal consistency could add a third invariance axis?
  2. Can the per-layer attention metric become a training-free inference-time correction (attention re-weighting / contrastive decoding) for frozen models?
  3. How does VISE behave on harder compositional/spatial-relation benchmarks (WhatsUp, VSR) that stress fine grounding beyond object presence?
  4. Is the ghosting reward robust to ambiguous queries and crowded scenes, or does it inject noise when multiple objects match?
  5. What is the scaling law of invariance gains — does the 2B→32B attenuation plateau, or could a larger unlabeled corpus revive it?
  6. Could combining answer-agreement rewards (reasoning) with invariance rewards (grounding) exceed both paradigms?
