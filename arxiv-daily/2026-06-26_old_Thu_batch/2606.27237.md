> **一句话总结：** 本文从行为学和机制学两个角度证明，语言模型并非像传统知识库那样以"任务无关"的方式存储事实知识，而是为"同一事实+不同任务"分别维护独立的参数子集；这一发现动摇了"LM即知识库"的类比，并解释了为何知识编辑易失效、单格式评测不充分、以及链式思维（CoT）能解锁直接回答无法触及的知识。

> **TL;DR:** This paper shows that language models do NOT store facts in a task-invariant manner like a real knowledge base. Through behavioral analysis (tracking fact emergence across training checkpoints) and mechanistic analysis (localizing disjoint parameter subsets per (fact, task) pair), the authors demonstrate that the same fact relies on different parameters for different task formats. This fragmentation undermines the "LM-as-knowledge-base" analogy, explains why knowledge editing/evaluation can be unreliable, and shows that chain-of-thought reasoning recovers knowledge by routing through alternative task-specific encodings.

## 核心贡献

- **提出并验证"任务不变性"（task-invariance）这一核心性质**：将知识库的"单一真相源"特性形式化为可检验的假设，并在 LM 上系统地证明它被大量违反。
- **行为学证据（共现假设 co-emergence）**：利用 OLMo-3-7B IT 的 105 个训练检查点，追踪 230 个事实在 6 个任务上的"共现"情况，发现约 47.9% 的（事实, 任务）对未按预期共现，且 (事实 × 任务) 交互项在所有检查点显著（p≈0），最终解释 23% 方差。
- **机制学证据（可定位性）**：基于 Bayazit et al. (2024) 的二值掩码框架，扩展出"必要 + 充分 + 特异 + 稀疏"四项损失，在 3 个模型（OLMo-2-7B/13B、Gemma-2-9B IT）、5 个关系数据集上，为每个（事实, 任务）对找到互不相交的参数子集（对角线下降 29%-89%，非对角影响 ≤8%）。
- **量化"跨任务纠缠"（entanglement）**：发现判别型任务（MCQA/Verification/Neg MCQA，Ent≈0.21-0.25）比生成型任务（OpenQA/FiTB/Multi-hop，Ent≈0.08-0.11）更纠缠；生成任务是更强的共现来源。
- **机制性解释 CoT 的有效性**：实验证明 CoT 能恢复被自身编码消融掉的性能（直接下降 20%-72% vs CoT 仅 12%-30%），但对"其它任务编码"更敏感（直接 ≤8% vs CoT 11%-31%），说明 CoT 通过调用多个任务专属编码来"绕路"取知识。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes and tests "task-invariance" as the defining KB property** — the expectation that the same fact draws on a single source of truth regardless of query format — and shows it is largely violated in LMs.
- **Behavioral evidence via a co-emergence hypothesis**: tracked across 105 OLMo-3-7B IT checkpoints, 47.9% of (fact, task) pairs fail to co-emerge as predicted; a two-way ANOVA rejects fact/task independence at every checkpoint (p≈0), with the interaction term explaining 23% of variance in the final model.
- **Mechanistic evidence via localized binary masks**: extended Bayazit et al. (2024) to jointly optimize necessity + sufficiency + specificity + sparsity, finding disjoint parameter subsets per (fact, task) pair across 3 models and 5 datasets (29%-89% diagonal drop, ≤8% off-diagonal collateral damage, 69%-102% reconstruction rate).
- **Introduces cross-task entanglement metrics** showing discrimination tasks (MCQA, Verification, Neg MCQA) are consistently more entangled (Ent 0.21-0.25) than generation tasks (OpenQA, FiTB, Multi-hop; Ent 0.08-0.11), and that generation tasks are stronger sources of co-emergence.
- **A mechanistic account of CoT**: ablation experiments on Gemma-2-9B IT show CoT recovers performance lost when a task's own encoding is ablated, yet suffers more when other tasks' encodings are removed — directly supporting the claim that CoT routes through multiple task-specific encodings.

## Methodology

The paper runs two complementary analyses, plus an entanglement analysis and a CoT study:

1. **Behavioral — Co-emergence (§2)**: Defines E(f,t) as the first checkpoint where fact f is reliably retrieved (correct-answer probability > θ=0.6, normalized by chance) on task t, and E(·,t) as task emergence (>25% of facts retrieved). The co-emergence hypothesis requires E(f,t) ≤ max(E(f,t̄), E(·,t)). Tested on OLMo-3-7B IT across 105 checkpoints (100 pretraining + 2 midtraining + 3 post-training), over 5 relational datasets (country-capital, country-language, landmark-country, company-city, person-instrument) × 6 tasks (Completion, FiTB, OpenQA, MCQA, Neg MCQA, Verification), 10 paraphrases each, down-sampled to 46 facts/dataset = 230 facts. Also formalizes task-invariance as fact×task conditional independence (Eq. 2) and tests it via two-way ANOVA.

2. **Mechanistic — Localization (§3)**: Learns a binary mask m over MLP neurons N and attention heads H, parameterized via straight-through estimator (Bengio et al., 2013), minimizing L = L_nec + L_suff + L_spec + β·L_spar (β=10). Necessity drives masked performance to chance τ; sufficiency uses activation patching from clean to corrupted prompts (subject→placeholder) following Yona et al. (2026); specificity penalizes interference on other facts (same task) and other tasks (same fact). Tested on OLMo-2-7B/13B IT and Gemma-2-9B IT (dropping Completion, adding Multi-hop-1/2). 5 training / 2 held-out paraphrases per task.

3. **Entanglement quantification (§4)**: Defines an entanglement score Ent(f,t) combining target drop and collateral changes on both axes, plus pairwise-task masks for directed pairs.

4. **CoT study (§5)**: Compares direct vs. CoT accuracy under zero-ablation, on (landmark, in-country, country) for Gemma-2-9B IT, measuring both same-task recovery and cross-task collateral damage.

## Results & Highlights

- **Co-emergence is frequently violated**: 47.9% of (fact, task) pairs fail to co-emerge by the predicted step (stable across θ = 0.4/0.6/0.8: 50.9%/47.9%/49.2%).
- **Fact×task interaction is significant and grows**: ANOVA null rejected at every checkpoint (p≈0); interaction explains 23% of variance in the final model.
- **Distinct, disjoint parameter subsets exist per (fact, task)**: diagonal ablation drops of 29%-89%, off-diagonal and same-task-other-fact damage ≤8%; sufficiency reconstruction 69%-102%.
- **Discrimination tasks are more entangled than generation tasks**: mean Ent 0.21 vs 0.11 (15 model-dataset combinations); Verification/Neg MCQA most entangled (0.25/0.24), Multi-hop-2 least (0.08).
- **CoT routes through multiple encodings**: own-encoding ablation costs direct answering 20%-72% but CoT only 12%-30%; cross-task ablation costs direct ≤8% but CoT 11%-31% — a clean, theory-driven demonstration of why reasoning unlocks otherwise inaccessible facts.
- **Practical implications**: knowledge editing/unlearning targeting one format may leave others intact; single-format evaluation underestimates (or overestimates) what a model knows.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** While "LMs are inconsistent across query formats" is known (Elazar 2021; Berglund 2024 reversal curse; Cohen 2024 ripple effects), this paper's contribution is to *localize the cause in parameter storage* rather than in access/retrieval, and to tie behavioral co-emergence, mechanistic localization, and CoT together under one coherent thesis. The framing of "task-invariance" as the KB-defining property (with a clean formal definition in Eq. 1-2) is a useful conceptual contribution.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Multiple complementary methods (behavioral checkpoint tracking, ANOVA interaction test, mask localization with necessity/sufficiency/specificity, entanglement metrics, CoT ablation). Results are replicated across 3 models × 2 families × 5 datasets, with held-out paraphrases and threshold-sensitivity checks (θ=0.4/0.6/0.8). The authors are careful to note that "consistent" co-emergence is not positive evidence (only violations count against the hypothesis).
- **Reproducibility: High.** Public OLMo checkpoints, public datasets (Wikidata SPARQL, Hernandez et al. 2024), released code/data, specific hyperparameters (β=10, θ=0.6, τ values, 5/2 train/eval paraphrase split).
- **Significance: Substantial for interpretability and KB/editing communities.** Directly informs knowledge editing, unlearning, and evaluation methodology. The CoT-as-routing-through-multiple-encodings finding is a genuinely interesting mechanistic insight.
- **Red flags / honest concerns:**
  - **Scope is narrow**: restricted to *relational* (subject, relation, object) facts and to facts that already meet a high baseline-performance threshold. These are exactly the facts where the KB analogy is *most* expected to hold, which the authors argue strengthens their test — but it also means the conclusions may not generalize to non-relational, long-tail, or ambiguous knowledge.
  - **Mask-disjointness is partly an artifact of the procedure**: masks are trained sequentially and earlier-selected components are excluded from later masks, *forcing* disjointness. The necessity/sufficiency/specificity scores then validate this forced structure, so the disjointness is not fully independent evidence. The authors should (and partly do) acknowledge that "disjoint subsets exist" is weaker than "the fact is stored separately."
  - **Sufficiency ≠ full encoding**: patching recovers performance, but as the authors note, this does not mean the subset *fully* encodes the fact; redundancy structure is uncharacterized.
  - **Scale untested**: only 7B-13B; the authors speculate larger models may be *less* task-invariant, but this is unverified.
  - **CoT experiment is on a single (dataset, model) pair in the main text**, with others deferred to appendix — the headline CoT claim rests on somewhat thin main-text evidence.
  - **Causal vs correlational**: localization shows subsets are necessary/sufficient/specific, but the *directionality* (does task format *cause* separate storage, or is it a correlate of pretraining data format?) is not established — the authors flag this as future work.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean framing (task-invariance as the testable KB property) with formal definitions.
- Triangulation across behavioral, mechanistic, and reasoning-based evidence — rare and convincing.
- Strong reproducibility (public checkpoints, code, data).
- Practically actionable implications for editing, unlearning, and evaluation.
- Honest limitations section that engages with the redundancy-vs-organization distinction.

## Limitations

- Only relational, well-known facts; high baseline threshold filters out the long tail.
- Sequential mask training forces disjointness, somewhat circular.
- Uncharacterized redundancy structure; sufficiency patching is not a "full encoding" proof.
- Limited model scale (7B-13B); scaling behavior unknown.
- CoT main-text results rest on one dataset/model combination.
- Causal role of training-data format in producing task-specific storage is hypothesized, not tested.

## Relevance

本文对"大模型是否/如何作为知识库"这一基础问题给出了机制层面的回答，对知识编辑、知识遗忘（unlearning）、多格式评测以及 CoT 机制研究均有直接指导意义；对关注 LLM 可靠性、可控性与可解释性的研究者和工程师而言是高价值参考文献。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the degree of task-invariance/violation *increase or decrease* with model scale (70B+, frontier models)? The authors hypothesize worse task-invariance at scale — is this empirically confirmed?
- To what extent does the task-specificity *originate* from the format in which facts appear in the pretraining corpus? Can targeted data augmentation (same fact in many formats) induce more task-invariant storage?
- Can the entanglement metric predict which (fact, task) pairs are robust to knowledge editing, enabling more reliable edit/unlearning methods?
- Is the CoT-routing-through-multiple-encodings finding specific to relational facts, or does it extend to procedural/ commonsense/long-tail knowledge?
- How does task-specific storage interact with the cross-lingual storage question (shared vs language-specific parameters)? Is there a unified "format-specific storage" principle?
- Can a training objective that explicitly encourages task-invariant encodings (e.g., consistency losses across paraphrased formats) be designed and shown to improve editing reliability without hurting capability?
