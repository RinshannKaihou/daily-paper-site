> **一句话总结：** 大推理模型（LRM）与人类在"难题花更多时间/算力"这一跨题目相关性上看起来一致，但固定题目后会发现二者策略相反——人类在答错的题目上放弃得更快，而LRM在答错时反而生成更长的推理链；本文提出"难度登记 vs 慎思分配"两级诊断来揭示这一被掩盖的分歧。

> **TL;DR:** Large reasoning models (LRMs) and humans both "spend longer on harder items," but this cross-item alignment hides an opposite within-item policy: when an LRM gets a problem wrong it generates more tokens (Cohen's d = 1.47–3.13 on H-ARC), whereas humans spend less time on trials they get wrong (d = −0.10). The paper introduces a two-level diagnostic — difficulty *registration* (cross-item) vs deliberation *allocation* (within-item, outcome-conditioned) — and shows the dissociation holds under item fixed effects, replicates across datasets, and is absent in a non-thinking baseline.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"两级诊断"框架**：把以往被混为一谈的"慎思"拆成两个层次——跨题目的"难度登记"（痕迹长度是否随人类反应时排序）与固定题目后的"分配"（智能体在自己答对还是答错的题目上花更多算力）。两者正交、互补。
- **引入结果条件化的、题目控制的诊断量**：在各自原生尺度上计算 within-agent Cohen's d（错误 − 正确的对数慎思时长），并用项目固定效应回归（βLRM）把跨题目难度梯度吸收掉，避免把秒和 token 放在同一坐标轴。
- **实证揭示符号反转**：在公开的匹配人-模型语料上，5 个 thinking LRM 全部为 d > 0（更长的失败推理链），而人类为 d = −0.10（失败上更短的反应时）；H-ARC 上交互 βLRM = −0.66（p<.001），且 INTUIT 复现、Cortes 为边界情况，非 thinking 的 DeepSeek-V3 缺少登记信号。
- **给出机制性解读与可证伪的后续实验**：把人类模式解读为"engagement vs abandonment"（由动作计数中介，斜率减半），把 LRM 模式解读为"length-on-uncertainty"（错误链含更多自我怀疑标记），并在 resource-rational 框架下提出受控截断实验作为判别性干预。

## Key Contributions

- A **two-level diagnostic** that separates cross-item *difficulty registration* from within-item *deliberation allocation*, explicitly complementing de Varda et al. (2025)'s cross-item alignment finding.
- An **outcome-conditioned, item-controlled contrast** (within-agent Cohen's d plus item-fixed-effect interaction βLRM) that uses each agent as its own baseline and never compares seconds and tokens directly.
- A **robust empirical dissociation**: all five well-powered thinking LRMs sit at d = 1.47–3.13 on H-ARC while humans are at −0.10; the item-FE interaction βLRM = −0.66 [−0.81, −0.50] survives four sensitivity specifications and a Mundlak reparameterisation.
- A **mechanism reading under resource-rational metareasoning** plus a **discriminating follow-up** (controlled truncation, f ∈ (0,1)) to distinguish "useful late search" from "length padding that has effectively stabilised the answer."

## Methodology

- **Data.** Secondary reanalysis of the public, de-identified de Varda et al. (2025) release and underlying corpora: H-ARC (visual abstraction, primary), INTUIT (intuitive physics, clean replication), Cortes (binary relational reasoning, boundary case). Arithmetic is excluded because all thinking LRMs are at ceiling.
- **Models.** Six open-weight thinking LRMs (DeepSeek-R1, Qwen-QwQ-32B, Qwen3-235B-Thinking, GLM-4.5-Air-FP8, gpt-oss-20b, gpt-oss-120b) with DeepSeek-V3 as a non-thinking control. Closed-frontier reasoning systems are not tested.
- **Deliberation measure.** t = reasoning_token_length (intermediate trace tokens) for thinking LRMs, total_output_tokens for V3, reaction-time seconds for humans — treated as a behavioural readout, not a meter of latent computation.
- **Within-agent d-ratio.** Pooled-SD Cohen's d on log deliberation, d_wrong−right = (ℓ̄_wrong − ℓ̄_right)/s_pooled with ℓ_i = log t_i.
- **Difficulty controls.** (a) Leave-one-out ensemble-difficulty regression controlling for D^(−A)_i = 1 − mean correctness of all other agents; (b) item fixed-effects regression log t = α + β·c + βLRM·(c × LRM) + δ_agent + γ_item + ε identifying the interaction purely from within-item variation; plus mixed-effects, Mundlak, and cluster-robust variants. Cluster-robust SE by item.
- **Trace-content features.** Length-normalised self-doubt-marker density, self-correction-marker density, word 5-gram repetition, and type-token ratio, regressed on correctness controlling for log length, agent, item.
- **Reproducibility.** Deterministic analyses with seed 0xA12C; full code and Makefile in an OSF deposit.

## Results & Highlights

- **Registration reproduced.** Thinking-LRM trace length correlates positively with human RT on H-ARC (Spearman ρ = 0.16–0.30, all p < .001); non-thinking V3 does not (ρ = −0.05).
- **Allocation reverses.** Per-agent: every analysed thinking LRM at d > 0 (well-powered range 1.47–3.13); humans at d = −0.10. Estimated marginal mean slope is −0.08 log units (humans, wrong − right) vs +0.78 (LRMs).
- **Item-FE dissociation.** βLRM = −0.66, p < .001, 95% CI [−0.81, −0.50], n = 5,728; triangulated across random-intercept, crossed RE, and two cluster-robust OLS specs (point estimates −0.66 to −0.94, all CIs below zero).
- **Cross-paradigm.** INTUIT clean replication (βLRM = −0.41, p < .001). Cortes is a boundary case: pooled LRM within-item slope flips to +0.31, but agent-type interaction preserved (βLRM = −0.97) because humans are more strongly positive. Per-LRM dissociation is rank-stable across paradigms (ρ = 0.77–0.89); Qwen-QwQ-32B dissociates most strongly on every paradigm.
- **Human engagement signature.** H-ARC within-item human slope +0.24; fast-wrong quartile shows median 16 grid actions vs 79 in the slow-wrong quartile; controlling for action count cuts the slope to +0.12 (≈48% reduction). Mundlak between-item slope is −0.54, opposite sign — the predicted abandonment/engagement split.
- **LRM uncertainty signature.** Length-controlled, wrong-trial H-ARC chains carry 0.43 more self-doubt markers per 1,000 chars (β = −0.435, p = 7×10⁻⁴; direction in 5/5 models); Cortes wrong chains show +7.4% 5-gram repetition and −4.1pp type-token ratio.
- **V3 partial signature.** Non-thinking V3 still shows d = 1.41 on Cortes, suggesting part of the wrong-trial length expansion is a base-model generation property inherited by thinking LRMs.

## Authors & Credibility

- Sole author **Han-yu Wang**, The University of Hong Kong. No specific funding; no competing interests declared; no new human-subjects data (IRB not required; all secondary reanalyses of de-identified public corpora).
- The paper engages directly and fairly with the contested de Varda et al. (2025) literature, citing the critical commentaries it builds on (Vankov 2026, Dujmović 2026, Hu 2026) and explicitly framing its findings as behavioural, not mechanistic.
- Heavy use of robustness checks (four spec variants, Mundlak, cluster-robust by item and by agent, aggregated-cell re-estimation) and a separate SI that isolates inferential checks from low-power descriptive cells. Deterministic seed, public OSF deposit, end-to-end Makefile target.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — high.** The registration-vs-allocation split is a genuinely new framing; the outcome-conditioned, item-controlled diagnostic is a minimal but sharp addition that directly answers an acknowledged gap in the target paper. The sign-reversal result is striking and not predicted by the alignment narrative.
- **Rigour — strong.** Item fixed effects absorb the very cross-item difficulty gradient the original finding lives on; the dissociation survives multiple specifications, Mundlak, mixed-effects, and cluster variants, with transparent handling of underpowered cells (gpt-oss-20b parser-limited, Qwen3-235B-Thinking n = 43). Multiple-comparison treatment is reported.
- **Reproducibility — high.** Public data, deterministic seed, code + Makefile in an OSF deposit, full provenance and per-model counts. The main risk is the one-sample-per-(item×model) structure, which the author is upfront about.
- **Significance — moderate-to-high for cogsci/LRM-as-cognitive-model work, narrower for ML systems work.** The methodological prescription (always pair registration with allocation) is the most transferable contribution; the substantive metacognitive claim is interpretive and explicitly labelled as such.
- **Red flags / honest concerns.** (1) The headline "dissociation" is observational and cannot show that the extra wrong-trial tokens are causally useless — the author concedes this and defers to the truncation experiment, which is *not run here*. (2) The within-item LRM slope is identified across models on the same item, not across stochastic samples of one model, so it is an item-controlled human–LRM contrast rather than a true per-model stopping-policy estimate; this weakens the "length-on-uncertainty" reading at the level of any single model. (3) Mechanism labels ("engagement-vs-abandonment", "length-on-uncertainty") are interpretations of behavioural proxies (action counts, surface markers), not architecture; the paper is careful about this but readers may over-read. (4) Closed-frontier reasoning systems are entirely absent, so generalisation to o-series/Gemini-style models is untested. (5) Human data lack participant identifiers, so trial-level engagement cannot be separated from participant-level speed/ability/engagement. (6) The Cortes sign reversal is smoothed over as a "boundary case" but is a real crack in the universality of the LRM-side mechanism story.

## Strengths

- Clean, falsifiable two-level decomposition that turns a vague "alignment" claim into two separable, testable diagnostics.
- Strong identification: item fixed effects remove the exact confound the original result rests on; multiple pre-registered-style robustness anchors.
- Honest scope statements (six open-weight models only, no frontier systems, no participant IDs, observational data) and a clear, discriminating follow-up experiment proposed.
- Mechanism work goes beyond the headline: action-count mediation for humans and trace-content asymmetries for LRMs give convergent, cross-paradigm signatures rather than a single correlation.

## Limitations

- Observational; causal value of the extra LRM tokens is not established.
- Sample of six open-weight thinking LRMs from four organisations; closed-frontier systems untested; per-LRM rank-stability at n = 6 is descriptive.
- One observation per (item × model) pair → within-item LRM slope is identified across models, not across stochastic samples of one model.
- No participant identifiers in human data → participant-level speed/ability/engagement cannot be separated from trial-level engagement.
- Cortes LRM-only within-item slope reverses sign (+0.31), a genuine boundary that limits the universality of the LRM mechanism reading.
- Reasoning-token length ≠ latent computation; human RT ≠ a single process; cross-system magnitude comparisons are not interpretable (the contrast is sign-only, within each agent's scale).

## Relevance

本文为"把 LRM 当认知模型来比对人类"这条研究线提供了一个直接、可操作的诊断工具，对任何用推理链长度/反应时来论证"人机思维一致"的工作都有方法论意义；其结论也提示，仅用跨题目相关性来宣称 LRM 具备类人元认知控制是有风险的，需要补上 within-item 的分配检验。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the controlled truncation experiment (repeated samples at fixed token budgets f ∈ (0.5–0.8) on the same dissociating items) produce the "early high-accuracy plateau" predicted by length-padding, or the "late steep rise" predicted by useful search?
- Do closed-frontier reasoning systems (o-series, Gemini-style) reproduce the within-agent allocation gap, or is it specific to open-weight RL-trained thinking models?
- With stochastic multi-sample data per (item × model), can the per-model stopping-policy be estimated directly, separating "length-on-uncertainty" from "length-on-irreducible-uncertainty"?
- Is the LRM wrong-trial length expansion driven by RL training signals / verifier rewards / length-conditioned preferences rather than task accuracy — and does it disappear under length-penalty or answer-then-reason training?
- Can participant-level human data (with identifiers) confirm the engagement-vs-abandonment schedule at the individual rather than population level, and does the human sign flip generalise beyond H-ARC's grid-action paradigm?
