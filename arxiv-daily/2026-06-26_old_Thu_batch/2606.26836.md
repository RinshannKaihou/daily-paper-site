# The Capability Frontier: Benchmarks Miss 82% of Model Performance

> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 "Capability Frontier"——一个在多个模型与多次生成之间做最优选择的质量-成本 Pareto 前沿，并通过外推与概率图模型去偏方法纠正了传统 oracle 路由估计的正偏，在 21 个 LLM、16 个基准上发现标准单模型评测使错误率被高估：修正单模型偏差可平均降低 54% 错误率，再叠加多次生成重选可达 82%，且能在匹配 SOTA 精度时节省 85% 成本。

> **TL;DR:** Argues that standard LLM benchmarks (single model, single run) systematically understate achievable performance. The authors define the "Capability Frontier" — a debiased quality-cost Pareto frontier achievable by an oracle that picks the best model per query and the best of multiple generations. Across 21 LLMs and 16 benchmarks, correcting for single-model evaluation yields a 54% average error-rate reduction; adding multi-generation selection with a *perfect, free* judge reaches 82%, and SOTA accuracy can be matched at 85% lower cost. Headline caveat: the 82% figure requires an idealized infallible, zero-cost verifier, so it is an upper bound, not a deployable system — the realistic per-query router alone gives ~54%.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 Capability Frontier 框架**：将"单模型单次评测"与"在模型和生成次数上做最优选择的系统级上限"之间的差距形式化为一条质量-成本 Pareto 前沿。
- **诊断并校正 oracle 路由的正偏（optimizer's curse）**：证明在有限生成数 G 下取样本均值最大值会高估可达成性能，偏差以 O(G^-λ)、λ∈[0.25, 0.75] 衰减；提出外推法与概率图模型（PGM）两种去偏方法。
- **大规模实证**：在 21 个主流 LLM（GPT-5 系列、Claude 4.5、Gemini 2.5、Llama 4、Qwen3 Coder、Kimi K2、DeepSeek R1、GLM-4.6 等）和 16 个可验证基准（编码、推理、医学、事实、指令遵循、智能体任务）上量化了可达成的路由增益及朴素 oracle 的偏差幅度。
- **机制性仿真**：用受控 PGM 仿真证明 oracle 增益随查询主题熵单调增长——数据异质性是增益的根本驱动力。
- **模型可靠性分析**：定义 reliability 指标，发现各模型可靠性差异显著（GPT-5-mini 90.2% vs GLM-4.6 76.3%），且与质量/成本无显著相关。

## Key Contributions

- **Capability Frontier formalization.** A Pareto frontier over a model pool characterizing the best achievable quality at each cost level under optimal selection across models *and* generations, with explicit debiasing of both single-model and single-run biases.
- **Oracle bias theory.** Proves the naive finite-sample oracle estimator is positively biased, derives the O(G^-0.5) Gaussian decay (and λ∈[0.25, 0.75] empirically), and shows it inflates cost estimates far more than quality estimates.
- **Two debiasing methods.** (1) Extrapolation that fits `O_biased(G) = α + β·smooth-transition` and recovers `O_true = α`; (2) a Probabilistic Graphical Model with latent difficulty D, topic T, and per-topic aptitude A, inferred via stochastic variational inference.
- **Large-scale empirical study.** 21 LLMs × 16 verifiable benchmarks with G=10 generations each; quantifies 54% error reduction from model routing and 85% cost savings at matched SOTA accuracy.
- **Mechanistic simulation.** A controlled PGM study sweeping topic-distribution entropy shows oracle uplift grows near-monotonically with workload diversity, grounding the empirical gains in data heterogeneity.

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** For prompt n, model l, generation g, observe metric ϕ_nlg ∈ {quality, −cost}. A router π maps each prompt to a model. The *true oracle* is `O_true = (1/N)Σ_n max_l µ_nl` where µ_nl is the true expected performance — an unobserved upper bound. The *standard (biased) estimator* replaces µ with the sample mean and takes a max, yielding `O_biased`, which is provably `E[O_biased] > O_true`.

**Bias characterization.** Under a Gaussian simplification, `E[max_l ϕ̄_nl] ≈ µ_n + σ_n·√(2 log L / G)` — bias shrinks as O(G^-0.5), grows with the number of models L and noise σ. A Bernoulli (binary correctness) analysis and synthetic studies confirm O(G^-λ), λ∈[0.25, 0.75]; roughly G≥50 is needed for the asymptotic form to dominate.

**Debiasing Method 1 — Extrapolation.** Fit `O_biased(G) = α + β·(1 + ((G−γ)/δ)²)^(−λ/2)` across several G and take `α` as the debiased estimate. Validated against synthetic data with known ground truth and against PGM estimates.

**Debiasing Method 2 — Probabilistic Graphical Model.** Generative model with latent difficulty `D_n ~ Beta`, topic `T_n ~ Categorical(θ)`, aptitude `A_tl ~ Beta`, and observed `ϕ_nlg ~ Bernoulli(π_nl)` where the link `π_nl = f(D_n, A_{Tn,l})` is a feedforward network. Inferred with stochastic variational inference under factorized posteriors; the unbiased oracle is computed from inferred `π̂_nl`. Cost uses a LogNormal likelihood.

**Capability Frontier.** Sweep α∈[0,1] over a convex combination of normalized quality and (−normalized cost), debiasing each axis separately, to trace the Pareto surface.

**Posthoc oracle.** When a verifier is available, select the best of k generations across models: `O_kshot(k) = (1/N)Σ_n [1 − Π_l (1 − π_nl)^k]`. Authors explicitly note this assumes a *perfect, cost-free* judge — an idealized upper bound.

## Results & Highlights

- **Finding 1 — Routing gives large gains.** Debiasing via the Capability Frontier (O_true, α=1) yields a **53.7% average error-rate reduction** vs each benchmark's SOTA model; per-benchmark reductions range from 13.9% (Terminal-Bench 2.0) to 85.7% (HumanEval-X CPP) and 82.7% (GPQA Diamond).
- **Cost savings.** At matched SOTA quality (O_true, α=α*), **85.2% average cost reduction**; Terminal-Bench 2.0 cost drops from 260.8¢ to 25.4¢ (90.3% saving).
- **Finding 2 — Posthoc multi-generation.** With an infallible, free judge: k=1 gives 66% error reduction, k=10 gives **82%** — the source of the title's number.
- **Finding 3 — Naive oracles overestimate.** Sample-mean oracles overstate quality by ~1.2% and cost by ~37.5% on average (cost more skewed → larger selection effect); up to 8.7% quality and 88% cost bias at small G.
- **Finding 4 — Reliability varies widely.** GPT-5-mini most reliable (90.2%), GLM-4.6 least (76.3%); reliability uncorrelated with quality or cost.
- **Finding 5 — Uplift scales with diversity.** Synthetic study: oracle uplift is near-zero in single-topic regimes and maximal under uniform topic mixtures, monotonic in entropy.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (moderate).** The *idea* that single-model benchmarks understate routed/system-level capability is well established (Shnitzer 2023, RouterBench 2024, RouteLLM 2025, FrugalGPT). The genuine novelty is the rigorous *debiasing* of the oracle estimator (the "optimizer's curse" imported from economics/auction theory) and the entropy→uplift mechanism. Without the debiasing contribution this would largely re-derive known routing-headroom results.

**Rigor (mixed).** The bias math is clean and the dual-method debiasing (extrapolation + PGM) with synthetic ground-truth validation is a serious effort. However:
- Only **G=10 generations**, while the authors themselves state G≥50 is needed for the asymptotic O(G^-λ) form to be reliable — extrapolation at G<10 "carries risk" in their own words.
- The PGM contains **ad-hoc structural choices** (e.g., the feedforward link function, independence assumptions) they admit "can influence results."
- Conditional-independence assumption across generations **underestimates correlations** between models that share training data/architecture.

**Reproducibility (weak).** No author code or data release is referenced in the paper (the GitHub links are all to *other* projects — mini-SWE-agent, CodeGeeX). Benchmark prompts and per-model generation logs do not appear to be released. Cost figures are tied to "provider pricing as of 01 Jan 2026," a moving target. This significantly limits independent verification.

**Significance (headline vs. substance gap).** The title's "82%" combines (a) the realistic per-query router (~54%) plus (b) an idealized *perfect, free* verifier doing k=10 posthoc selection. The authors are honest in the body that the Capability Frontier "is not a deployment strategy," but the framing (especially the title) conflates an upper bound with realized performance. A perfect zero-cost judge does not exist; real verifiers add error and cost that erode gains.

**Red flags.**
- **Title is somewhat sensationalized / borderline misleading** — benchmarks do not "miss 82% of model performance"; under an idealized oracle, 82% of the *remaining error* can be removed.
- **Conflict of interest**: the lead organization (Martian) sells LLM routing; the paper's thesis is that routing is hugely valuable.
- **Workshop venue**, so it has received lighter peer review than a main-track ICLR/NeurIPS submission.
- **Agentic oracle is a lower bound** (LLM fixed within a trajectory), so the reported gains there are conservative — but this also means the agentic numbers are the least reliable to interpret.

## Strengths

- Principled, timely attack on a real methodological gap (single-model/single-run benchmarking under-reporting achievable capability).
- First to formally connect the optimizer's curse from economics to LLM oracle routing and supply usable debiasing tools.
- Both empirical and theoretical/simulation contributions reinforce each other; the entropy→uplift result gives a *mechanism*, not just a number.
- Broad, modern, and up-to-date model/benchmark coverage (GPT-5.x, Claude 4.5, Gemini 2.5, Llama 4, Kimi K2, GLM-4.6 — genuinely 2026-vintage).
- Refreshingly honest limitations section and explicit caveats on the posthoc judge assumptions.

## Limitations

- Results are **upper bounds, not deployable systems** — require per-query ground-truth correctness (oracle) or an infallible verifier (posthoc).
- Small generation budget (G=10) relative to the G≥50 the authors' own analysis requires.
- No released code/data for the paper's own methodology.
- PGM structure and independence assumptions are ad-hoc and understate inter-model correlation.
- Cost analysis is pinned to a single date's API pricing and will not age well.
- Headline 82% figure leans on an unrealistic perfect-and-free judge.

## Relevance

对于关注 LLM 评测方法论、模型选型/路由与推理时算力扩展的人而言高度相关：它揭示了"单模型单次"基准会大幅低估系统级可达成性能，并给出可复用的去偏方法；如果你的工作涉及 benchmark 构建、agentic 系统评测或多模型编排成本权衡，本文的 Capability Frontier 思路可直接借鉴。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How much of the 54% (non-posthoc) routing gain survives when the oracle is replaced by a *learned* router with realistic train/test splits and unseen models, rather than ground-truth correctness?
- What is the achievable frontier once judge error and judge cost are modeled — i.e., how quickly do posthoc gains collapse under imperfect verifiers?
- Does the entropy→uplift relationship hold on truly continuous/multi-domain workloads, or is it an artifact of the discrete 30-topic PGM simulation?
- Could per-step (rather than per-trajectory) agentic routing close the gap that the fixed-LLM agentic setup underestimates, and at what latency cost?
- How do these frontiers shift under chain-of-thought / test-time-search strategies versus the simple k-shot sampling used here?
- Given the Martian commercial interest, an independent (non-routing-vendor) replication with released code would materially strengthen the claims.
