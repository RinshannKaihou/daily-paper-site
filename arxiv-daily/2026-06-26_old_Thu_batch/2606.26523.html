Why This Matters
Gives safety researchers rigorous, tool-ready criteria for when we can trust beliefs & desires read from model internals — and warns where projection silently fails.
⚠ Critical Edge
The framework risks unfalsifiability: almost any failed probe can be explained away as "wrong propositions" or "insufficient uniformity" — absorbing disconfirmation rather than being threatened by it.

Methodology

A philosophy-meets-ML programmatic book (~100 pages, 7 chapters), not an empirical paper. Its method is constructive synthesis: radical interpretation (Davidson, Lewis, Dennett, Stalnaker) is mapped onto the black-box / white-box split in ML.

The formal core is the Jeffrey-Bolker decision framework: preferences satisfying averaging, impartiality, and continuity over an atomless Boolean algebra 𝔄 jointly yield a probability measure P (credence) and desirability D(A)=v(A)/P(A) — "solve for the rest" made precise, giving the agential description ⟨P, D, 𝔄⟩.

Each philosophical criterion is bound to a concrete test: truth ↔ linear truth-direction probe; coherence ↔ negation/entailment geometry; use ↔ activation patching & steering; uniformity ↔ cross-domain probe transfer. OthelloGPT and the Schoen et al. (2025) scheming traces serve as running worked examples; a wizard parable threads the chapters.

Bottom line (authors' own): "We have not settled how much of what LLMs do is propositional," nor provided a complete account of extracting the model's propositional algebra from its internals.

Strengths

  • Genuine interdisciplinary competence — philosophy is not decorative, ML is not name-dropped; Bolker's axioms bind to specific probe/intervention experiments.
  • Intellectual honesty about what is not solved: desire representations, algebra extraction, persona/base separation.
  • The propositional projection problem is a real, under-recognized hazard, framed here more sharply than elsewhere.
  • SwampGPT / training-objective-≠-function arguments cleanly dispose of "stochastic parrot" and "pretraining can't yield beliefs" shortcuts.
  • The accuracy/power/tractability meta-framework turns "do LLMs have beliefs?" into a comparative-theory question.

Limitations

  • A programmatic manifesto, not a methods paper: constructive proposals (§6.5) are sketches, not validated procedures.
  • The representationalist program for desire is essentially undemonstrated; belief/desire evidence asymmetry is acknowledged but not closed.
  • Heavy reliance on the linear representation hypothesis and SAE feature labelability — both actively contested.
  • Persona vs. base model ("whose beliefs?") is a first-order ambiguity explicitly set aside.
  • Soft unfalsifiability: proposition-mismatch can always rationalize a failed probe. Limited engagement with non-propositional cognition.

Open Questions

  1. Do Jeffrey-Bolker's averaging/impartiality axioms hold even approximately on a frontier agentic LLM's preferences, or do intransitivities defeat the representation theorem in practice?
  2. Is there a measurable internal "desire direction," and does goal-substitution actually re-orient means as the framework predicts?
  3. How much of LLM cognition is genuinely propositional vs. pattern-completion — can boundary-detection methods survive proposition-mismatch?
  4. Can Davidson-style logical-structure extraction be implemented over SAE sparse codes at scale, and do recovered algebras match human structure?
  5. Do persona and base model produce measurably different belief/desire attributions — which is the safety-relevant target?
  6. How does the projection problem interact with known chain-of-thought unfaithfulness (Turpin 2023, Lanham 2023, Korbak 2025)?
