> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了多项式 Stein 差异（PSD）在高阶多项式下信噪比（SNR²）指数级衰减的失败模式，并提出基于 Rayleigh 商优化的 λ-PSD 重加权方案，在保持线性时间复杂度的同时显著提升检验功效。

> **TL;DR:** The paper uncovers that polynomial Stein discrepancies (PSD) suffer exponential SNR² decay as polynomial degree grows, and proposes λ-PSD, a covariance-aware, scalable reweighting scheme formulated as a Rayleigh quotient that stabilises the SNR² and boosts goodness-of-fit test power at linear-time cost.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示 PSD 的失败模式**：首次理论刻画了在 Gaussian 设定下，多项式阶数 Q 增大时 SNR² 以 `((Q+1)!!/(2Q+1)!!)²` 的速率指数级衰减，说明"仅最大化信号"的设计目标存在根本缺陷。
- **提出 SNR² 最优化框架**：将 Stein 差异的构造重新表述为 Stein 特征上的广义 Rayleigh 商最大化问题，明确将目标从"信号最大化"转向"信噪比最大化"。
- **提出 λ-PSD 估计器**：在低维子空间中引入协方差感知的标量重加权方案，避免完整 O(Q³d⁶) 逆运算的不可行性，同时保持可证明的 SNR² 稳定性（上界为 χ²(p∥q)）。
- **保持线性时间复杂度**：在样本数 n 上保持线性复杂度，同时通过 split-sample z-test 框架规避高维 U-统计量的 bootstrap 计算开销。

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretically characterise a previously unstudied failure mode of PSD**: under Gaussianity, the asymptotic SNR² decays exponentially with polynomial degree Q (Remark 3.6 / Theorem 3.5), showing that existing PSD methods "optimise the wrong objective" by amplifying signal while inflating variance.
- **Reformulate Stein discrepancy construction as an explicit SNR² maximisation problem** via a generalised Rayleigh quotient over the Stein feature vector, with the optimal solution θ⋆ ∝ Σ⁻¹μ.
- **Propose λ-PSD_L1**: a tractable, approximate, covariance-aware scalar-reweighting scheme defined over the low-dimensional span of the unweighted PSD solution, for which the authors prove SNR stability bounded above by χ²(p∥q) (Theorem 4.4).
- **Provide a split-sample, asymptotically Gaussian z-test** (Proposition 3.7) that bypasses O(BnQd²) bootstrap overhead while retaining the same asymptotic calibration.

## Methodology

The work proceeds in three conceptual stages.

**1. Analysis of existing PSD_L1.** The authors introduce a first-order (Langevin-Stein) polynomial Stein discrepancy `PSD_L1`, distinct from the second-order `PSD_L2` of Srinivasan et al. (2025), with closed-form maximiser `A⋆_i = μ_i/Z`, `b⋆ = μ_b/Z`. The empirical squared discrepancy is rewritten as a V-statistic (equivalently a U-statistic) with rank `Qd² + d` over an augmented feature vector `ξ(x)` composed of vectorised `Φ_i(x)` and the score `s_p(x)`.

**2. SNR² failure-mode characterisation.** Using Hoeffding decomposition, Theorem 3.5 gives the exact SNR² scaling of the U-statistic; Remark 3.6 specialises to `p = N(0, I_d)`, `q = N(δ1, σ² I_d)` and derives the moment-dominant scaling `SNR²(Q) ≍ ((Q+1)!!/(2Q+1)!!)²`, which decays super-exponentially in Q. The same decay provably affects the split-sample z-statistic (Remark 3.8).

**3. λ-PSD construction.** The fully optimal SNR² estimator requires solving `J(θ) = (θᵀμ)²/(θᵀΣθ)` over a `Qd²+d` dimensional vector, which is both statistically ill-posed (rank-deficient Σ) and computationally prohibitive (O(Q³d⁶) inversion). To make this tractable, the authors restrict optimisation to the `(Q+1)`-dimensional span of the already-computed PSD features `Ψ(X) = (⟨A⋆_1,Φ_1(X)⟩_F, …, ⟨A⋆_Q,Φ_Q(X)⟩_F, (b⋆)ᵀs_p(X))ᵀ`, introducing scalar weights `λ` per moment order. The optimum is `λ⋆ ∝ Σ_Ψ⁻¹μ_Ψ`, estimated via cross-entropy (CE) stochastic optimisation with a Bernoulli-Gaussian prior that induces implicit regularisation/pruning. The final test statistic is computed on a held-out split via a CLT-based z-test.

## Results & Highlights

- **Standard multivariate Gaussian target** (d ∈ {2,…,128}, n=1000): λ-PSD_L1 maintains correct type-I error under the null and achieves strong power across perturbed-Gaussian, Student-t, and Laplace alternatives, outperforming IMQ KSD, Gaussian KSD, RFSD, and FSSD-opt in moderate-to-high dimensions, especially for heavy-tailed targets. The unweighted PSD_L1 is highly sensitive to Q; λ-PSD_L1 is markedly more robust across Q (Figure 2).
- **Gaussian-Bernoulli RBM** (d=50×40, n=1000, dependent Gibbs samples): Both PSD_L1 and λ-PSD_L1 outperform linear-time RFSD and FSSD-opt and are competitive with quadratic-time KSD methods. λ-PSD_L1 is far less sensitive to Q — at perturbation σ²=0.02 it sustains rejection rates ~0.83–0.89 across Q∈{1,…,4}, whereas unweighted PSD fluctuates between 0.23 and 0.98.
- **Runtime**: Despite nominal O(Qd²n) complexity, λ-PSD_L1 scales favourably with both n and d (Figure 3/4), remaining practically fast even at d=1024.
- **Reproducibility**: code released at https://github.com/olivervu25/weighted-psd.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high).** The SNR²-decay diagnosis is genuinely new: prior PSD work treated Q as a tuning hyperparameter with empirically non-monotonic behaviour, but did not derive the exponential collapse. Reframing discrepancy construction as Rayleigh-quotient SNR² maximisation is a clean, transferable conceptual contribution that transcends the specific PSD setting.
- **Rigour (moderate-to-high, with caveats).** The Gaussian theory is clean and explicit (exact `((Q+1)!!/(2Q+1)!!)²` scaling). However, the central "moment-dominance assumption" (footnote 3) is only sketched — it asserts that highest-order moments dominate the numerator/denominator expansion, which need not hold for arbitrary alternatives. The proof sketches are relegated to appendices.
- **Theory–method gap.** The fully optimal `o-PSD_L1` is dismissed as "statistically ill-posed and computationally infeasible"; λ-PSD is an approximation whose tightness relative to the optimum is not bounded. Theorem 4.4 only gives an upper bound (`SNR² ≤ χ²(p∥q)`), not a lower bound or an approximation-ratio guarantee, so the practical sub-optimality of the low-dimensional reweighting is unquantified.
- **Scope of guarantees.** All SNR-stability results hold *under Gaussianity* (`p = N(0,I_d)`, `q = N(δ1, σ²I_d)`). The RBM experiment is the only non-Gaussian, dependent-sampling test, and the authors acknowledge the z-test variance is misspecified under dependence (requiring MCMC covariance correction that they do *not* apply) — the strong RBM numbers may therefore be partly fortuitous.
- **Reproducibility (good).** Public code, standard benchmarks, clear experimental protocol, single-core workstation. Wild-bootstrap baselines use B=500.
- **Significance (moderate).** A focused, well-executed contribution to a niche but active area; the SNR-aware design principle is the most reusable takeaway. The lack of real-data applications and the restricted theoretical scope limit immediate broader impact.
- **Red flags**: None severe, but note (i) workshop-paper depth, (ii) heavy reliance on Gaussian assumptions for the headline theoretical result, (iii) CE-based λ estimation adds its own tuning knobs (Bernoulli-Gaussian prior parameters) that are not ablated.

## Strengths

- Identifies and *proves* a real, previously-misunderstood pathology (exponential SNR² decay) rather than treating it as an empirical nuisance.
- Clean, general reformulation (Rayleigh quotient / SNR² maximisation) that applies beyond PSD and could guide future scalable Stein constructions.
- λ-PSD retains linear-time complexity in n and is robust to the choice of Q, removing a major practical pain point of PSD.
- Competitive empirical power against strong quadratic-time KSD baselines, including in a dependent-sampling RBM setting.
- Honest about limitations (asymptotic validity, dependent-sampling caveats, ill-posedness of the exact optimum).

## Limitations

- Core theoretical guarantees are restricted to Gaussian targets/samplers; behaviour under non-Gaussian, multimodal, or strongly non-log-concave targets is unproven.
- The exact SNR²-optimal estimator is never computed; λ-PSD is a heuristic low-rank approximation with no formal approximation bound.
- Only synthetic benchmarks (Gaussian, RBM); no real-world posterior/MCMC diagnostics, which are the motivating use cases for sample-quality measurement.
- The CE optimiser for λ introduces additional hyperparameters and stochasticity not thoroughly ablated.
- Asymptotic z-test validity requires i.i.d. or variance-correction for dependent sampling; performance under MCMC ergodic samples is empirically reasonable but theoretically unjustified.
- Workshop-track length precludes deeper comparison with newer adaptive-feature Stein methods (e.g., neural Stein critics) and large-scale posterior diagnostics.

## Relevance

对关注贝叶斯计算诊断、MCMC 收敛性检验或大规模样本质量评估的研究者具有直接方法学价值——尤其是需要线性时间、对超参数稳健的 goodness-of-fit 检验时。其"SNR-aware 设计优于纯信号最大化"的原则也为更广泛的可扩展 Stein 方法提供了设计范式。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the SNR²-decay pathology and the Rayleigh-quotient SNR-maximisation principle be extended beyond Gaussians to log-concave or mixtured targets, with non-asymptotic bounds?
- What is the formal approximation gap between the low-dimensional λ-PSD reweighting and the (infeasible) full `o-PSD_L1` optimum? Is there a principled rank-selection rule for the reweighting subspace?
- How does λ-PSD behave on real MCMC output (e.g., high-dimensional Bayesian posteriors, SGLD chains) where samples are dependent and the score must itself be estimated via mini-batching?
- Could the multi-resolution / per-coordinate reweighting extensions (Section 4.4) recover the lost expressivity while staying tractable, and do they connect to sparse generalised-eigenvalue theory with provable rates?
- Does the CE-based λ estimation introduce variance that erodes the asymptotic z-test calibration in finite samples, and would ridge-regularised closed-form `Σ_Ψ⁻¹μ_Ψ` be more stable?
- How does λ-PSD compare against learned/adaptive Stein critics (e.g., Grathwohl et al. 2020) in regimes where the discrepancy is not moment-localised?
