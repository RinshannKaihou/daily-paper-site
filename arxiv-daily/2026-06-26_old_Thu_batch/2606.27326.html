World-model hallucination is predictable and preventable: three label-free internal signals forecast three failure modes, and coverage-aware data fixes them.

Hallucination in World Models is Predictable and Preventable ↗
Nicklas Hansen, Xiaolong Wang — UC San Diego
world-models hallucination data-coverage reinforcement-learning flow-matching benchmark
Key Metric
ρ≈0.80Spearman corr. of label-free predictors vs. rollout ΔPSNR
All three predictors (round-trip residual, flow instability, inter-seed variance) reach this ceiling on 9k held-out sequences — and double as curiosity rewards.
Why This Matters
Reframes hallucination as a data-coverage problem, not an architecture one — pointing world-model work toward curation and runtime uncertainty over bigger backbones.
Critical Edge
The "single underlying cause" rests on three predictors converging — the taxonomy may be a narrative device, not a causally demonstrated distinction, with no isolated per-mode ablations.

Methodology

The work centers on a 350M-parameter action-conditioned world model built on the Dreamer-4 two-stage recipe, then turns the model on itself to diagnose its own failures.

  1. Tokenizer (102M). A symmetric Transformer encoder-decoder over 14-stride patches (256 tokens/frame), 64 learnable latent queries, projecting to a tanh-bounded 64×64 latent code. Trained with masked auto-encoding (pixel MSE + LPIPS) and RMS loss normalization.
  2. Dynamics model (250M). A block-causal Transformer over packed spatial latents, conditioned on action tokens, shortcut-conditioning tokens (noise σ, step size d), register and readout tokens. Uses shortcut flow-matching (Frans et al., 2025), interleaving a one-step regression with a self-consistency bootstrap — enabling next-frame sampling in ~4 Euler substeps.
  3. Three label-free predictors. u_r (tokenizer round-trip residual), u_f (flow instability across Euler substeps), u_s (inter-seed denoising variance). Each is normalized by per-step latent motion (u_norm = u/m) to remove scene-activity confounds.
  4. Mitigation. Two levers: (a) coverage-aware training resamples the dataset uniformly across tasks rather than frames; (b) targeted data collection rolls out candidates in the world model, scores by u_norm_r, executes the highest-scoring trajectory in the live env (horizon H=32, replan every K=16) — a curiosity loop that fills coverage gaps by construction.

Strengths

  • Unifying thesis (coverage → three modes) yields both diagnostics and mitigations from the same signals.
  • MMBench2 is a substantial community resource: actions, rewards, live sims, language, seen/unseen split.
  • Predictors are cheap: no labels, no extra training, computable at runtime.
  • Two complementary, deployable recipes (offline resampling; online curiosity) compose with Dreamer-4.
  • Honest limitations and candid discussion of non-uniform data informativeness.

Limitations

  • Confined to 350M params and simulated control; real-robot / billion-scale transfer unverified.
  • Modes defined by stage, not independently induced — separability is asserted, not causally isolated.
  • High compute (58 GPU-days) and a large custom dataset limit independent reproduction.
  • Goal-directed MPC measures only a narrow subspace; broad-coverage quality under-measured.
  • Mild circularity: curiosity reward u_norm_r is also a reported success metric.
  • Off-the-shelf tokenizers (Wan 2.1 VAE) already match on unseen tasks — tempers the in-domain advantage.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does "coverage, not capacity" hold at billion-parameter scale, and does the relative importance of the three modes shift with size?
  2. Can the predictors be causally decoupled — does closing only perceptual coverage leave action-marginalization unchanged?
  3. How do the predictors transfer to real-robot data with sensor noise, partial observability, and non-stationary dynamics?
  4. Can curiosity combine with intrinsic motivation to close the gap to human/expert oracles (0.325 → 0.362)?
  5. Given off-the-shelf tokenizers already generalize, would a hybrid (off-the-shelf tokenizer + in-domain dynamics) be cheaper?
  6. How sensitive are the ρ≈0.80 correlations to context length T, action dimensionality, and the shortcut schedule?
