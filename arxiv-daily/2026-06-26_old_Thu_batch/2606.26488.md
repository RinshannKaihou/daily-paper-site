> **一句话总结：** 本文系统研究递归推理模型（Tiny Recursive Model）在边缘部署压缩下的失效机制，发现激进量化会保留局部逐格预测却摧毁全局推理，并提出无需标签的"carry-trajectory fidelity"信号与每通道校准 INT4 方案，将递归推理模型压进 4–8 MB 微控制器/SoC。

> **TL;DR:** Compressing depth-recurrent "Tiny Recursive Models" for the edge reveals that aggressive quantization preserves local cell accuracy but destroys global reasoning (puzzle-exact collapses to 0%), the failure is architectural (MLP-mixing recursion breaks, attention does not), and the authors fix it with per-channel calibrated INT4 plus a label-free "carry-trajectory fidelity" diagnostic that predicts the damage before any task evaluation, enabling a 6.83M-parameter reasoner to fit a 4 MB MCU at 6x fewer FLOPs.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示递归推理模型特有的压缩失效模式：量化噪声在递归循环间逐层累积（而非在输出 token 间），导致"局部正确、全局崩塌"——cell accuracy 保持而 puzzle-exact 降至 0%。
- 通过同一任务上的架构消融（Sudoku 上对比 TRM-MLP-Mixing / TRM-Attention / HRM）证明这种脆弱性源于 token mixer 结构，而非任务本身：仅 MLP-mixing 在 INT4 下崩塌（-64 pp），attention 与 HRM 均存活。
- 提出 **carry-trajectory fidelity**（压缩模型与全精度模型最终 carry state 的余弦相似度）作为无标签、单调的预部署信号，单个阈值（~0.8）即可区分"安全"与"已损坏"的压缩。
- 给出可直接落地的一套部署配方：flash-streamed embeddings 消除 99.4 MB 嵌入表瓶颈；INT8 + 单周期递归（H=1, n_sup=8）在 ARC-2024 上以 6x 更少 FLOPs 匹配全深度精度，占 6.51 MB（适配 8 MB SoC）；每通道校准 INT4 适配 4 MB MCU 并恢复至近 FP32 精度。
- 指出 token 级目标（包括 QAT、知识蒸馏、剪枝、线性注意力）无法修复组合正确性损失，唯一可靠的修复路径是"更好的量化器"（per-channel calibration），而非更好的训练目标。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies a failure mode specific to recursive reasoners: quantization noise compounds across H recursive cycles at each carry-state update (unlike single-pass transformers where it accumulates over output tokens), decoupling local cell accuracy from global puzzle-exact accuracy.
- Pinpoints the fragility as **architectural, not task-intrinsic**: a same-task ablation on Sudoku-Extreme shows only the TRM-MLP-Mixing token mixer collapses (-64 pp under naive INT4), while TRM-Attention (at matched FP32 accuracy) and the separate HRM family both survive — ruling out a headroom confound.
- Introduces **carry-trajectory fidelity**, a label-free, graded, monotonic signal (cosine similarity between the quantized and FP32 final carry states) that predicts compression damage and its recovery before any task evaluation; a single threshold (~0.8) separates safe from harmful compression.
- Delivers a concrete deployment recipe validated by on-device measurement on three Snapdragon platforms via Qualcomm AI Hub: flash-streamed embeddings (99.4 MB → 2 KB active), INT8 at one recursive cycle matching full-depth accuracy at 6x fewer FLOPs (6.51 MB, fits 8 MB SoC), and per-channel calibrated INT4 fitting a 4 MB MCU while recovering near-FP32 accuracy with no retraining.
- Shows that token-level objectives (QAT, distillation, structured pruning, linear-attention approximation) cannot restore compositional correctness — cell accuracy survives while puzzle-exact stays at 0% — concluding the reliable recovery path is a better quantizer, not a better training loss.

## Methodology

The study centers on the **Tiny Recursive Model (TRM)**, a weight-tied recursive transformer that iterates a latent carry state Z_h across H outer cycles, each running L inner refinement steps through a shared two-layer transformer block f_θ. Two architectural variants are compared: **TRM-Attention** (multi-head self-attention over the full token sequence) and **TRM-MLP-Mixing** (attention replaced with token-mixing MLPs). The HRM family (Wang et al., 2025) is added as a second architecture on Sudoku-Extreme.

Three structured-reasoning tasks are evaluated: **ARC-2024** (abstract visual reasoning, TRM-Attention, 6.83M params, 50,911-entry puzzle embedding table of 99.4 MB, 1000-augmentation TTA), **Maze-Hard** (exact path-finding, TRM-Attention, 6.82M params, no embedding/TTA), and **Sudoku-Extreme** (9x9 constraint satisfaction, TRM-MLP-Mixing, 5.03M params, no embedding table). Two metrics are tracked: **cell accuracy** (fraction of correct grid cells) and **puzzle-exact match** (fraction of fully solved puzzles), which can diverge sharply.

Compression methods span post-training quantization (FP16, per-channel INT8 via bitsandbytes, naive per-tensor INT4, per-channel asymmetric calibrated INT4), structured pruning, knowledge distillation (single-cycle student at 13–15% of teacher params), linear-attention approximation, and embedding-table strategies (full INT8, SVD rank-16, single-puzzle flash loading). The depth–precision sweep explores (H, n_sup) jointly, since FLOPs scale linearly with total recursion steps (H × n_sup). The key diagnostic, **carry-trajectory fidelity** φ, compares the compressed model's final carry state to the FP32 reference's final carry state (cosine similarity), requiring only the FP32 reference available pre-deployment and no task labels. Deployment targets are an 8 MB mobile SoC (Cortex-A55 class) and a 4 MB MCU (Cortex-M55 class), with physical on-device latency/memory measured on Samsung Galaxy S24, S22 5G, and Dragonwing RB3 Gen 2 via Qualcomm AI Hub.

## Results & Highlights

- **Embedding table is the bottleneck, not the backbone:** 99.4 MB FP32 embedding table = 79% of the ~126 MB footprint; single-puzzle flash loading (streaming 2 KB per puzzle) eliminates it losslessly, decoupling embedding from backbone compression (Table 1). SVD rank-16 is lossy (cos=0.94); full-table INT8 stays at 24.9 MB.
- **INT8 + reduced recursion matches full depth:** INT8 is lossless across both architectures and all three tasks; INT8 at H=1, n_sup=8 reaches 35.25% puzzle-exact on ARC-2024 (within 0.75 pp of the 36.00% baseline) at 6x fewer FLOPs (Figure 2). Maze-Hard stays within 0.10 pp at 3.75x fewer FLOPs; Sudoku-Extreme is the exception (needs depth H=4).
- **Dynamic vs. static quantization tradeoff:** dynamic quantization recovers almost all loss but is incompatible with NPU accelerators; naive static INT8 is lossless only on Maze, costs 10.5 pp on ARC, and catastrophically collapses Sudoku to 5.30% exact match.
- **Architecture, not task, drives fragility:** on Sudoku-Extreme naive INT4, TRM-MLP-Mixing collapses (-63.8 pp to ~10.2%), while TRM-Attention (matched 73.1% vs 73.8% FP32) retains 69.5% and HRM is robust (Figure 4, Table 10).
- **Calibrated INT4 recovers constraint reasoning:** per-channel calibrated INT4 (still 4-bit, post-training, no fine-tuning) lifts Sudoku from 10.2% to 71.9% exact match (within 1.9 pp of 73.8% FP32); fidelity rises 0.35 → 0.89, tracking recovery (§4.5).
- **Carry-trajectory fidelity is a clean pass/fail signal:** stays ≥0.99 where INT4 is harmless, falls monotonically with damage (ARC 0.69 / -10.5 pp; Sudoku 0.35 / -63.8 pp); the consecutive-state baseline cosine is precision-invariant (Δ≤0.04) and useless (Figure 3).
- **Token-level recovery fails:** cell accuracy and puzzle-exact decouple under all four interventions (distillation, 25% pruning, linear attention, QAT) — QAT from naive INT4 does not even converge (0.0% cell and puzzle-exact).
- **Deployment floor is calibrated INT4:** per-channel calibrated INT4 fits the 4 MB MCU (3.26 MB) and recovers near-FP32 accuracy with no retraining; ~6x FLOPs reduction also cuts energy ~3000 → ~500 mJ/puzzle.

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are from the **ML Collective** (independent non-profit research org) and **Carnegie Mellon University** — notably **Virginia Smith**, a well-recognized figure in federated learning and efficient ML, lending strong methodological credibility. The first two authors share equal contribution. The empirical work is unusually grounded for a systems/quantization paper: deployment claims are measured on **physical commercial hardware** (Samsung Galaxy S24, Galaxy S22 5G, Dragonwing RB3 Gen 2) via **Qualcomm AI Hub**, not just analytical projections, which materially strengthens the deployment recipe claims. The paper engages directly with the practical tension that dynamic quantization (most robust PTQ path) is incompatible with static-tensor-boundary NPU accelerators — a genuine real-world constraint often glossed over.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high):** This is, by the authors' account and literature review, the first study to compress a recursive reasoning model and to articulate the depth–precision interplay specific to iterative-latent systems. The architectural attribution of fragility (MLP-mixing vs attention at matched FP32 accuracy) and the label-free carry-trajectory fidelity diagnostic are genuinely new contributions, not incremental PTQ repackaging.
- **Rigor (good, with gaps):** The same-task architecture ablation on Sudoku (Figure 4) is well designed to rule out the headroom confound — the strongest single experiment. Carry-trajectory fidelity is shown to be both graded and monotonic with damage, and to track recovery, which is more than a threshold hack. However, generality is narrow: centered on the TRM family across three tasks, HRM is evaluated only on Sudoku-Extreme, and no autoregressive baseline is included (acknowledged limitation).
- **Reproducibility (moderate-high):** Compression tooling (bitsandbytes, per-channel PTQ) is standard and accessible; tasks (ARC-2024, Maze-Hard, Sudoku-Extreme) and architectures (TRM, HRM) are public; on-device measurements on commercial Snapdragon hardware are reproducible in principle but require specific devices and Qualcomm AI Hub access. Hyperparameters (e.g., QAT 100 steps, lr=1e-5) are given. No explicit mention of code release in the extracted text.
- **Significance (medium-high):** Practically impactful for edge AI: a 6.83M-parameter reasoner fitting a 4 MB MCU with a label-free safety diagnostic is a useful, deployable result. Conceptually, the "local cell accuracy survives while global compositional correctness dies" finding and its objective-level explanation (cross-entropy rewards cells independently) is a transferable insight beyond TRM.
- **Red flags / honest concerns:**
  - ARC-2024's headline numbers rely on 1000-augmentation test-time voting, so the single-pass on-device latency understates real per-puzzle cost — the authors flag this, but readers could over-read the deployment efficiency.
  - Calibrated-INT4 recovery is measured by full-system accuracy on Sudoku, but on ARC only carry-trajectory fidelity (0.94) is reported, not re-measured task accuracy — a gap in the headline recovery claim.
  - The study is confined to 5–7M-parameter compact models; whether the depth–precision interplay and architecture fragility hold at larger recursive reasoner scales is untested and could confound compression dynamics with model compactness (acknowledged).
  - The fidelity diagnostic needs an FP32 reference, so it supports pre-deployment decisions but cannot monitor a live deployed model.

## Strengths

- Cleanly isolates a failure mode (carry-state error compounding) unique to recursive/iterative-latent models, distinct from token-level transformer quantization.
- Strong same-task architecture ablation that genuinely attributes fragility to the token mixer rather than the task.
- Label-free, monotonic, threshold-able diagnostic (carry-trajectory fidelity) — operationally more useful than precision-invariant hidden-state similarity.
- Hardware-grounded deployment recipe measured on three real Snapdragon platforms, including energy estimates.
- Intellectually honest framing: identifies that token-level objectives are the wrong tool and points to quantizer design as the fix.

## Limitations

- Narrow architectural and task coverage: primarily TRM; HRM only on Sudoku; no autoregressive baseline; generalization beyond structured-reasoning puzzles untested.
- ARC recovery claim rests on fidelity (0.94) not re-measured accuracy.
- Fidelity diagnostic requires a full-precision reference — not usable for live monitoring.
- ARC efficiency claims depend on heavy test-time augmentation, inflating apparent deployability.
- Compact-model scale (5–7M params) may confound compression dynamics with model compactness.
- No autoregressive or large-scale recursive baseline to separate recursive-specific effects from scale effects.

## Relevance

本文对边缘端高效推理与模型压缩方向高度相关，尤其是它揭示了"递归/迭代潜变量推理模型"在量化下特有的"局部存活、全局崩塌"失效模式与对应的无标签诊断信号，对我们理解低比特量化与深度循环架构的耦合失效、以及设计可部署的轻量推理模型具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does carry-trajectory fidelity transfer to other iterative-latent systems (Universal Transformers, deep equilibrium models, modern state-space models), and is the ~0.8 threshold stable across them?
- At larger recursive reasoner scales (tens/hundreds of millions of parameters), does the MLP-mixing fragility persist, diminish, or invert — i.e., is it a small-model artifact?
- Can a global/compositional loss (e.g., constraint-satisfaction or trajectory-level) replace cross-entropy to make QAT actually repair reasoning collapse, contrary to the paper's conclusion?
- How does the depth–precision interplay interact with learned recursion-depth halting or adaptive compute, where H varies per input?
- Is there a runtime (label-free, reference-free) monitor for carry-state health during live deployment, given that the proposed fidelity requires the FP32 reference?
