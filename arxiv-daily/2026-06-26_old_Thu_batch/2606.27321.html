Key Finding

Two soft sparsity regularizers added to a Top-k sparse autoencoder lift monosemanticity & class purity at zero reconstruction cost.

Beyond the Hard Budget: Sparsity Regularizers for More Interpretable Top-k Sparse Autoencoders ↗

Nathanaël Jacquier · Maria Vakalopoulou · Mahdi S. Hosseini

sparse-autoencoders interpretability top-k-sae vision-foundation-models sparsity-regularization arXiv v1 · 2026-06-25
Key Metric
+0.13
mean monosemanticity gain (Regularizer 1) on the supervised ViT-L/16 encoder — while reconstruction R² holds or rises across all cells.
Why This Matters
A drop-in upgrade for Top-k SAE training that also makes reconstruction robust to inference-time k and front-loads discriminative features for better small-budget probing.
⚠ Critical Edge
λ is selected per-configuration to maximize the headline metric — no error bars, seeds, or statistical testing; many deltas are only 0.001–0.01, so gains may not be significant.

Methodology

A Top-k SAE (latent dim m=8192, k∈{32,64,128}) is trained on embeddings from a frozen vision foundation model. Encoder produces pre-activations π; ReLU gives a; the Top-k operator keeps the k largest to form sparse code z; decoder reconstructs x̂. The novelty: regularizers act on the pre-Top-k activations, not the sparse code.

  • Regularizer 1 — off-support ℓ1: penalizes the residual on units not selected by Top-k, driving their sub-threshold mass to zero → sharper unit selectivity.
  • Regularizer 2 — ℓ1/ℓ2 ratio: scale-invariant (Hoyer 2004 / DeepHoyer); concentrates the code onto fewer effective units and reshapes the whole activation geometry.
  • Active-unit mask μ (the key trick): both penalties restricted to units Top-k selected at least once in the batch. Without it, unselected units take a one-sided gradient and collapse — dead neurons blow up 1–2 orders of magnitude (e.g. 12→2426).
  • Objective: L = L_recon + α·L_aux + λ·R; baseline = λ=0. Only λ swept; selected as the largest λ whose R² still beats baseline.

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated formulation; the active-unit mask is a validated design choice that prevents dead-latent collapse.
  • Broad evaluation matrix (2 datasets × 3 encoders × 3 k) with baselines differing only in R and λ.
  • Reg 2's downstream benefits — k-robustness and information front-loading — go beyond a single-metric win.
  • Simple enough to be a drop-in addition to existing Top-k SAE pipelines; honest about dead-neuron trade-offs (in the body).

Limitations

  • λ selected to maximize the headline metric on the eval set; no error bars, seeds, or statistical tests — small deltas (0.001–0.01) hard to assess.
  • No released code stated; no compute/energy reporting.
  • Reg 2 increases dead neurons in most configs — a concrete downside surfaced only in the body, not the abstract.
  • Vision embeddings + label-grounded proxies only; no causal / mechanistic feature evaluation (steering, targeted ablation).
  • Modest scale (k≤128, m=8192) vs production SAEs; gains may not compound. Both datasets are object-classification images.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do monosemanticity & robustness gains persist at production scale (m=65k–1M, large k), or saturate?
  • Does the λ-favorable rule survive a neutral, fixed λ or a held-out λ? What are per-seed variances?
  • Do recovered features pass causal/mechanistic checks (steering / ablation producing predicted behavior change)?
  • Can Reg 2 combine with a dead-unit revival term (à la JumpReLU SAEs) to keep concentration and unit utilization?
  • Does the method extend to BatchTopK and Matryoshka SAEs, or to language-model SAEs?
