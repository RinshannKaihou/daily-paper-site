Key Finding

Aggressive quantization preserves local cell accuracy but destroys global reasoning in recursive reasoners — fixed by calibrated INT4 and a label-free fidelity diagnostic.

What Survives When You Compress a Recursive Reasoner for the Edge? · Pearse Jim, Steven Kolawole, Opegbemi M. Busoye, Glory Bagai, et al. · 2026-06-25 · arXiv 2606.26488
recursive-reasoning model-compression edge-deployment quantization tiny-models
Key Metric
10.2% → 71.9%
Per-channel calibrated INT4 recovers Sudoku puzzle-exact accuracy (within 1.9 pp of 73.8% FP32) — fitting a 4 MB MCU at ~6× fewer FLOPs, no retraining.
Why This Matters
Exposes a recursive-reasoner-specific quantization failure — local cells survive while global reasoning dies — with a label-free pre-deployment diagnostic, directly enabling deployable lightweight reasoning at the edge.
Critical Edge
The ARC recovery claim rests on carry-fidelity (0.94), not re-measured task accuracy — and efficiency numbers lean on 1000× test-time augmentation, overstating real on-device deployability.

MMethodology

The study centers on the Tiny Recursive Model (TRM), a weight-tied recursive transformer iterating a latent carry state Zh across H outer cycles, each running L inner refinement steps through a shared two-layer block fθ. Two token-mixer variants are compared: TRM-Attention (multi-head self-attention over the full sequence) and TRM-MLP-Mixing (attention swapped for token-mixing MLPs); the separate HRM family is added on Sudoku-Extreme.

Three structured-reasoning tasks stress local vs. global accuracy: ARC-2024 (visual reasoning, 6.83M params, 99.4 MB puzzle embedding table), Maze-Hard (exact path-finding), and Sudoku-Extreme (9×9 constraint satisfaction). Two metrics are tracked — cell accuracy (per-grid-cell) and puzzle-exact (fully solved) — which can diverge sharply.

Compression spans PTQ (FP16, per-channel INT8, naive per-tensor INT4, per-channel calibrated INT4), pruning, distillation, linear-attention, and embedding strategies (flash streaming, SVD). The headline diagnostic, carry-trajectory fidelity φ, is the cosine similarity between the compressed and FP32 final carry states — label-free, available pre-deployment. On-device latency/memory are physically measured on three Snapdragon platforms via Qualcomm AI Hub.

Architecture, not task, drives fragility: at matched FP32 accuracy, only MLP-Mixing collapses (−63.8 pp) under naive INT4; TRM-Attention (69.5%) and HRM both survive. Carry-fidelity stays ≥0.99 where INT4 is harmless, falling monotonically with damage (Sudoku 0.35 / ARC 0.69). Token-level recovery (QAT, distillation, pruning, linear attention) fails to restore compositional correctness — cell accuracy survives, puzzle-exact stays at 0%.

+Strengths

  • Isolates a failure mode (carry-state error compounding) unique to recursive / iterative-latent models, distinct from token-level transformer quantization.
  • Strong same-task architecture ablation attributing fragility to the token mixer, not the task — rules out the headroom confound.
  • Label-free, monotonic, threshold-able diagnostic (φ ~0.8) — operationally more useful than precision-invariant hidden-state similarity.
  • Hardware-grounded deployment recipe measured on three real Snapdragon platforms, including energy estimates (~3000 → ~500 mJ/puzzle).

!Limitations

  • Narrow coverage: primarily TRM; HRM only on Sudoku; no autoregressive baseline; generalization beyond structured puzzles untested.
  • ARC recovery rests on fidelity (0.94), not re-measured accuracy — a gap in the headline claim.
  • Fidelity diagnostic needs a full-precision reference — unusable for live monitoring.
  • Efficiency claims depend on heavy test-time augmentation, inflating apparent deployability.
  • Compact scale (5–7M params) may confound compression dynamics with model compactness.

?Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does carry-trajectory fidelity transfer to other iterative-latent systems (Universal Transformers, deep-equilibrium models, modern SSMs), and is the ~0.8 threshold stable across them?
  2. At larger recursive reasoner scales (tens/hundreds of millions of parameters), does MLP-mixing fragility persist, diminish, or invert — i.e., is it a small-model artifact?
  3. Can a global / compositional loss (constraint-satisfaction or trajectory-level) replace cross-entropy and actually make QAT repair reasoning collapse?
  4. How does the depth–precision interplay interact with learned recursion-depth halting or adaptive compute, where H varies per input?
  5. Is there a runtime, label-free, reference-free monitor for carry-state health during live deployment?
