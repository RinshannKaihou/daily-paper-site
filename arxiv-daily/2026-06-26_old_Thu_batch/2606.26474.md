> **一句话总结：** 本文用 Dedicated Feature Crosscoders（DFC）将 Qwen2.5-3B 经 ToolRL 强化学习后新增的"工具调用"能力定位到极少数特征上，发现仅靠单个 A-exclusive 神经元就能在无需重训的情况下提升工具正确率 65 个百分点，揭示 RL 引入的能力可在共享子字典中"溢出"并构成一种推理期可控的侧信道。

> **TL;DR:** Using Dedicated Feature Crosscoders (DFC) to diff a Qwen2.5-3B base model against its ToolRL-fine-tuned counterpart, the authors find that a single A-exclusive crosscoder neuron suffices to add +65.0 pp tool-correctness via retraining-free steering, and that RL-induced capability partially "spills over" (+6.8 pp) into the frozen base model through shared decoder weights — evidence that DFC partitioning concentrates but does not perfectly isolate RL-specific capability.

## 核心贡献

- **首次对 DFC 做系统性超参扫描**：在 Qwen2.5-3B 基座模型与其 ToolRL 微调版本之间训练了 48 个 crosscoder 变体（36 个 DFC + 12 个 CrossCoder），覆盖字典大小、top-k、独占份额、独占惩罚系数等五个维度。
- **发现"能力溢出"现象**：将冻结的基座模型激活通过联合训练的 crosscoder 重建，工具正确率从 0% 提升到 6.8%，且无需任何微调——作者称之为 capability spillover（能力溢出）。
- **证明 DFC 独占分区充当"过滤器"而非"吸收槽"**：对独占分区施加 L1 惩罚会损害 Model A 保真度，说明工具相关信号被挤回共享分区而非被隔离。
- **单神经元转向饱和**：仅需操纵单个 A-exclusive 神经元即可达到 +65.0 pp 工具正确率提升，并在 7/9 个探测层上泛化。
- **几何与自动可解释性证据**：通过 UMAP+HDBSCAN 与 Gemma 自动解释性流程，证明 A-exclusive 特征占据可分离的"工具交互"区域，且最判别性的特征是结构化模板检测器（如 `<tool_call>`、`<response>`）。

## Key Contributions

- **First systematic behavioral evaluation of Dedicated Feature Crosscoders (DFC)** across a 48-variant hyperparameter sweep on a ToolRL-fine-tuned Qwen2.5-3B model pair, the first work to demonstrate single-neuron steering saturation.
- **Identification of "capability spillover":** passing the frozen base model's activations through a jointly-trained crosscoder passively transfers +6.8 pp tool-correctness with zero fine-tuning, while format accuracy never spills over.
- **Empirical characterization of the DFC exclusive partition as a *filter* rather than a *sink*:** penalizing the exclusive slice degrades RL-model fidelity, indicating tool signal gets pushed back into the shared partition rather than cleanly isolated.
- **Single-neuron steering saturation:** steering a single A-exclusive neuron achieves +65.0 pp ∆ tool-correctness (95% CI [+47.9, +82.1]), matching the ceiling of steering all 7 available A-exclusive neurons, and generalizing across 7 of 9 probed layers.
- **Geometric + autointerp evidence:** DFC A-exclusive features form a spatially separable "Tool Interaction" region (k-NN purity 0.984, ARI 0.93) that is absent in a matched-size CrossCoder proxy (k-NN purity 0.158, ARI 0.08); Gemma autointerp shows the top features are structural-template detectors.

## Methodology

The authors compare two Qwen2.5-3B models (d = 2048, 36 layers): **Model B (Base)** = `Qwen/Qwen2.5-3B`, and **Model A (RL)** = `chengq9/ToolRL-Qwen2.5-3B`, which is RL-fine-tuned for structured `<tool_call>` invocation. Training data is 40k FineWeb samples (general-domain) + 40k ToolRL instruction-output pairs.

They apply **Dedicated Feature Crosscoders (DFC)**, which partition a joint dictionary of size D into three sub-dictionaries — A-exclusive [0, a_end), B-exclusive [a_end, b_end), and shared [b_end, D) — enforced by gradient masking. The training objective (Eq. 1) combines MSE reconstruction with an L1 penalty on shared features (λ_sh) and an L1 penalty applied to the exclusive+shared unions (λ_excl). Top-k sparsity is enforced in the encoder.

The **48-variant hyperparameter sweep** varies five axes: Architecture (CrossCoder vs DFC), dictionary size D ∈ {8192, 16384}, top-k ∈ {45, 90, 160}, exclusive share p ∈ {3%, 5%, 10%} (DFC only), and λ_excl ∈ {0 (free), 10⁻³ (penalised)} — yielding 36 DFC + 12 CrossCoder variants. All crosscoders trained for 9,000 steps, batch 1,024, Adam lr = 10⁻⁴, λ_sh = 10⁻³.

**Behavioral scoring** (per Qian et al. 2025) uses three metrics: `format_accuracy` (presence of `<tool_call>` + JSON `"name"` field), `tool_correctness` (fuzzy-match against a numbered tool in the prompt), and `overall_score ∈ {−1, 0, +1, +2}`. Evaluation: 100 held-out ToolRL prompts per sweep variant, 40 prompts per steering cell, greedy decoding, max_new_tokens=200, max_length=2048.

**Neuron identification & steering:** features are ranked by Cohen's d between tool-use vs. general-text activations (Eq. 2), filtered by firing rate (ρ_tool ≥ 0.3, ρ_gen ≤ 0.1). Targeted steering applies an additive correction to Model A's residual stream (Eq. 3): h′_A = h_A + Σ_{i∈S} (α − 1) · f_i · W_dec[i, A, :].

**Decoder geometry analysis:** for each feature they compute a mass-ratio r_i = log(‖W_dec[i,0,:]‖₂ / ‖W_dec[i,1,:]‖₂) to label CrossCoder features into A/B/shared proxies of matched sizes, then apply UMAP + HDBSCAN + k-NN purity + ARI to test whether the DFC's clean separation is architectural rather than a UMAP artefact.

## Results & Highlights

- **Reconstruction improves RL model behavior:** across the 48-model sweep, every variant improves Model A tool correctness (48/48, sign-test p ≈ 3.6 × 10⁻¹⁵). Mean ∆ tool-correctness = +31.1 ± 9.7 pp for Model A; pre-reconstruction 19% → 50.1% post-reconstruction.
- **Capability spillover to base model:** Model B tool-correctness rises from 0% to 6.8% post-reconstruction (paired-t t ≈ 9.4, p ≈ 1 × 10⁻¹²), with zero fine-tuning. Format accuracy never spills over (ΔB format = 0 pp identically) — semantic tool-selection intent is in shared directions, but the exact `<tool_call>` surface form remains RL-specific.
- **Reconstruction loss does not predict behavior:** training MSE correlates only weakly with Model A gain (r = +0.08, 95% CI [−0.21, +0.36]); the top-k bottleneck acts as an implicit regularizer.
- **DFC vs CrossCoder:** indistinguishable reconstruction MSE (0.031 for both), but CrossCoders transfer slightly more to Model B (+8.9 pp vs +6.1 pp). Welch's t-test on ∆B: t = 1.63, p = 0.12 — directional, not significant (underpowered: 12 CC vs 36 DFC).
- **Single-neuron steering saturation (headline result):** a single A-exclusive neuron (|S| = 1, α = 32) achieves +65.0 pp ∆ tool-correctness (95% CI [+47.9, +82.1]), matching the ceiling of steering all 7 A-exclusive neurons. CrossCoder needs |S| = 33 to reach its unbudgeted peak of +70.0 pp.
- **DFC partition as filter, not sink:** λ_excl = 10⁻³ reduces Model A fidelity vs λ_excl = 0 (34.8 → 25.8 pp at 5%; 35.2 → 32.0 pp at 10%), pushing tool signal back into the shared partition.
- **Ablations:** B-exclusive steering has exactly 0.0 pp effect at every cell (end-to-end sanity check on the harness). Steering A-excl ∪ shared simultaneously underperforms A-excl alone (+35.0 vs +65.0 pp) due to destructive interference between non-orthogonal decoder directions.
- **Decoder geometry:** DFC k-NN purity 0.984, ARI 0.93, negative silhouette −0.168 → strong separation; matched-size CrossCoder proxy k-NN purity 0.158, ARI 0.08 — separation is architectural, not a UMAP artefact.
- **Hypotheses (Table 6):** H1–H6, H8 confirmed; H7 ("combo > either alone") rejected.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. This is the *first systematic behavioral evaluation* of DFC across a hyperparameter sweep, and the *first to demonstrate single-neuron steering saturation* for an RL-installed capability. However, the core DFC architecture is inherited from Jiralerspong & Bricken (2025); the novelty is in the empirical characterization (spillover, filter-not-sink, single-neuron saturation) rather than a new method.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The authors pre-register and track 8 hypotheses (Table 6) with status, evidence, and section references; they explicitly report a *rejected* hypothesis (H7, combo steering) rather than hiding it. They honestly label underpowered tests and avoid overclaiming. The matched-size UMAP control (falsifying the "UMAP artefact" alternative) is a genuinely careful piece of design.
- **Reproducibility:** Likely reproducible but incomplete. They commit to a code release and report key hyperparameters, training steps, batch size, seeds (42 for steering), and full per-variant evaluation tables (Table 8). However, the paper is short (workshop-length, ~8 pages of body) and does not detail compute, full training-data mix, or release the crosscoder checkpoints. Steering results come from n = 40 prompts per cell and have not been replicated on a fresh sample.
- **Significance:** Conceptually important for mech-interp and safety. The "capability spillover" finding has a real safety implication: releasing crosscoders trained between model pairs with different capability levels may inadvertently provide a substrate that reintroduces capability at inference time. The single-neuron steering result is a clean, legible demonstration that RL-installed behaviors can be runtime-modulated.
- **Red flags / honesty concerns:**
  - The headline +65.0 pp and +31.1 pp numbers are computed *post-reconstruction* against a pre-reconstruction baseline that itself is only 19% for Model A — so a chunk of the apparent gain is the crosscoder "cleaning up" the activation rather than a pure steering effect. The framing in the abstract is precise but easy to over-read.
  - Cohen's d values larger than ~5 are flagged as reflecting near-zero variance (features that simply don't fire on general text) — the authors correctly caution against reading these as comparable effect sizes, but the autointerp table (Table 7) still leads with d = 12.18.
  - Generalization is narrow: a single model pair (Qwen2.5-3B) and a single task (ToolRL). The "capability" claim is explicitly limited to "reliable, structured tool-call generation," not tool-use ability writ large.
  - The architecture-effect comparison (DFC vs CC) is underpowered and the spillover-reduction claim is directional only.

## Strengths

- Pre-registered hypothesis table (Table 6) with explicit confirmation/rejection status — a model of honest empirical reporting.
- Clean controlled comparison: the matched-size UMAP experiment isolates the architectural (gradient-mask) cause of DFC separation from label imbalance.
- A genuinely surprising and safety-relevant finding (capability spillover as an inference-time side channel).
- Strong statistical hygiene throughout (CIs, multiple test types, effect sizes, explicit "n.s." labeling).
- Practical, low-intervention runtime control story: single-neuron, gradient-free, inspectable steering that complements RLHF/DPO/SFT.

## Limitations

- Single model pair (Qwen2.5-3B base vs ToolRL) and single task — generalization to other architectures, scales, and RL regimes is untested.
- Underpowered architecture comparison (12 CC vs 36 DFC runs); the DFC > CC spillover claim is directional, not statistically significant.
- Steering evaluated on only n = 40 prompts per cell; best-cell numbers not replicated on a fresh sample.
- Autointerp covered only A-exclusive features; the shared partition — which carries most of the spilled tool capability — is the natural but unexamined next target.
- The exclusive partition is shown to be a *filter*, not a *sink*: DFC does *not* cleanly isolate RL-specific capability, partially refuting its own design intent.
- "Capability" is shorthand for structured tool-call propensity under a fixed prompt; no prompted baseline tuned to maximally elicit base-model tool use was run.
- Workshop-paper length constrains depth; no compute cost reporting, no released checkpoints described in the body.

## 相关性

对"机制可解释性 × RL 智能体安全"方向高度相关：本文提供了将 RL 引入的 agentic 行为（工具调用）定位到极少数可操纵特征的首个系统性证据，为运行时行为监控、可控转向以及"能力溢出"安全风险（发布跨模型 crosscoder 可能无意中重引入能力）提供了直接的方法论与实证支撑。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does single-neuron steering saturation generalize beyond Qwen2.5-3B and ToolRL — e.g., to larger models, to RLHF/DPO post-training, or to other agentic capabilities (web browsing, code execution)?
- Can the capability-spillover side channel be *suppressed* by training objective design, or is it an inherent property of joint sparse decomposition when capabilities are non-orthogonal in residual-stream space?
- What does the autointerp of the *shared* partition reveal, given that it carries most of the spilled tool capability and is currently unexamined?
- How do Delta-Crosscoders (Kassem et al., 2026) — which explicitly model the asymmetric nature of fine-tuning — change the filter-vs-sink finding and the spillover magnitude?
- Is the +65 pp single-neuron effect robust to prompt distribution shift, or does it reflect overfitting to the 40-prompt evaluation cell?
- Can the same single-neuron steering be inverted (clamping to zero) to *suppress* unwanted RL-installed behaviors at inference time, and does that hold under adversarial prompting?
