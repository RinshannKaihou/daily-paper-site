> **一句话总结：** 中兴星云团队基于 Qwen3-8B 构建了一个完全透明、消融驱动的后训练流水线（SFT → GRPO → On-Policy 蒸馏），覆盖指令与推理两条分支，系统量化了数据质量、难度、配比、教师选择等关键因素，证明数据正确性过滤是一阶优化因子、教师分布兼容性胜过模型规模，并通过多教师 OPD 在数学推理上让"学生"反超单教师上限。

> **TL;DR:** ZTE's Nebula team builds a fully transparent, ablation-driven post-training pipeline on Qwen3-8B-base spanning SFT, GRPO reinforcement learning, and On-Policy Distillation (OPD) for both instruct and reasoning branches, rigorously quantifying how data correctness, difficulty, mixture ratios, and teacher selection shape 8B-scale model capability, and showing that multi-teacher OPD can exceed individual teacher ceilings via cross-teacher knowledge recombination.

## 核心贡献

- **端到端透明数据流水线**：从 3.84M 多源 SFT 样本和 200K 可验证 RL 候选池出发，构建涵盖响应蒸馏、多维交叉验证过滤、细粒度难度分级、任务分类与多样性采样的完整数据处理栈，并做了严格的基准污染去重。
- **指令分支三阶段 SFT**：通过四维质量指标（Length/Entropy/Loss/IFD）+ 域内自适应采样 + 跨域增益混合，将 Qwen3-8B-nothink 平均分从 55.01 提升到 60.99（NebulaExp-Ins-SFT），GRPO 再提升到 61.85。
- **推理分支**：基于回归的混合比例搜索（math:code:science = 0.6:0.2:0.2）+ 短到长课程学习 + 过滤多轮训练，推理 SFT 在代码和数学基准上超过 Qwen3-8B-thinking/Qwen3-14B/QWQ-32B；中难度 8K GRPO 将平均推理分从 73.88 提到 75.17。
- **On-Policy 蒸馏（OPD/MOPD）**：作为无验证器的后训练范式，单教师 OPD 仅用 4K 指令样本即在 IFEval 上超 RL 基线 3.26 分；多教师 OPD 用 10K 样本融合四个领域专家教师，平均分较基线 +4.18，且在 AIME'25/MATH-500 上反超单教师上限。
- **可复现经验配方**：公开训练超参、数据配比、难度阈值、过滤规则与教师选择原则，强调"机制与权衡"而非追逐 SOTA 榜单。

## Key Contributions

- **Transparent end-to-end data pipeline**: A reusable stack (response distillation via Qwen3-235B/Qwen3.5-397B teachers, bidirectional query/response/answer-verification filtering with Docker-isolated tool checking and 54 IF checkers, pass@4 difficulty grading, lightweight Qwen3-1.7B task classifier, diversity clustering) producing 1.04M instruct + 2.8M thinking SFT samples, plus aggressive benchmark-contamination removal.
- **Three-stage instruct SFT recipe**: Four complementary data-quality indicators (Length, Entropy, Loss, IFD=ratio form), within-domain adaptive sampling, then gain-based cross-domain mixing (Math 44K / Code 17K / Other 89K) raising the average from 55.01 → 60.99; subsequent GRPO RL on 53K verifiable samples lifts it to 61.85.
- **Reasoning branch with regression-based mixture search**: Casts the math/code/science mixture-ratio search as a regression prediction problem over proxy-training labels, landing on 0.6:0.2:0.2, combined with short-to-long curriculum learning and filtered multi-epoch training.
- **Medium-difficulty GRPO RL**: On a curated 8K medium-difficulty subset, two RL epochs push the reasoning average from 73.88 → 75.17 while surpassing Qwen3-8B-thinking on GPQA-Diamond.
- **On-Policy Distillation (single- and multi-teacher)**: Verifier-free reverse-KL distillation on student-generated sequences; single-teacher OPD with 4K IF samples beats the 53K-sample RL baseline by +3.26 IFEval / +4.43 average; MOPD fuses four domain-specialist 8B RL teachers with 10K samples for a +4.18 average gain and surpasses teacher upper bounds on AIME'25 and MATH-500.
- **Reproducible empirical recipes**: Full hyperparameters, thresholds, mixture ratios, and teacher-selection heuristics are disclosed.

## Methodology

The work treats post-training as a sequence of independently ablable stages rather than a single monolithic run, all on **Qwen3-8B-base** with fixed eval protocols (temp 0.7/top-p 0.8 for instruct; temp 0.6/16-sample averaging for AIME).

**Data curation.** A 3.84M raw SFT corpus (Nemotron-cascade, ODA-math-460K, Nemotron-Math-V2, OmniThought-Math, AM-Thinking, OpenScienceReasoning, Dolci-Instruct-SFT) is re-distilled for style consistency, filtered via query/response/answer-verification (with Docker-isolated math/code execution, multi-model rejection sampling, and 54 IF rule checkers), difficulty-graded by base-model pass@4 into low/medium/high, classified by a trained Qwen3-1.7B, and diversity-sampled via embedding clustering. A 200K verifiable RL candidate pool (60K math / 30K code / 110K IF) feeds filtered subsets.

**Instruct SFT** uses four quality indicators (Length, Entropy, Loss, IFD=L(r|q)/L(r) ratio form — explicitly preferred over the subtraction variant, which biases toward short responses), weighted/stratified within-domain sampling, then gain-based cross-domain mixing. **Instruct RL** uses GRPO on a 53K filtered subset (math 27.5K / IF 14.5K / code 11K), 6 epochs, KL retained, relaxed on-policy, rollout size 5.

**Reasoning SFT** proceeds: (1) identify math sampling strategy (length-based sampling > pure difficulty-based; 75K long > 75K hard on AIME), (2) regression-based mixture search over math/code/science (optimum 0.6:0.2:0.2), (3) category-level dedup + multi-epoch training on a compact high-quality subset. **Reasoning RL** uses GRPO on 8K medium-difficulty samples (4K IF / 2K math / 1K science / 1K code), two epochs, correctness + format rewards only.

**On-Policy Distillation** has the student autoregressively generate `y ~ πS(·|x)`, then the frozen teacher supplies per-token distributions over `y`; the student minimizes reverse-KL restricted to the teacher's top-64 tokens (to control memory over a 128K+ vocab). Single-teacher IF experiments sweep Qwen3-8B/235B-A22B/32B/30B-A3B teachers; multi-teacher OPD routes four RL-trained domain-specialist 8B teachers by sample domain.

## Results & Highlights

- **Instruct SFT** average 55.01 → 60.99 (+5.98), driven by AIME'24 +15.00, AIME'25 +11.25, ZebraLogic +35.90, at the cost of an "alignment tax" on IFEval (−5.54) and LiveBench (−6.60); IFEval is recoverable to 82.26 via dedicated IF data.
- **Instruct RL** pushes average to 61.85; largest gains IFEval +3.88, MATH-500 +2.60.
- **Reasoning SFT** beats Qwen3-8B-thinking/14B and QWQ-32B on AIME'24 (80.83), AIME'25 (72.08), MATH-500 (98.00), and LiveCodeBench v5 (68.25).
- **Reasoning RL** (2 epochs) average 73.88 → 75.17; GPQA-Diamond +4.55, surpassing Qwen3-8B-thinking.
- **Single-teacher OPD**: Qwen3-30B-A3B-Thinking-2507 teacher → IFEval 83.40 (+3.26 vs RL, +14.40 vs base) with only 4K samples; general-capability average 74.61 vs RL's 70.18.
- **Multi-teacher OPD**: +4.18 average over base (72.42 → 76.60), +1.98 over Qwen3-8B; the student **surpasses the teacher upper bound** on AIME'25 (75.83 vs 75.42) and MATH-500 (97.25 vs 96.97).
- **Headline findings**: (1) math and code demand *opposite* data distributions (high entropy/IFD for math, low for code); (2) long-form general text transfers to formal reasoning (Other-domain max-length sampling alone beats baseline on AIME/ZebraLogic with zero math data); (3) data correctness filtering is the *first-order* factor, sampling strategy second-order; (4) at 8B scale, hard-only RL data outperforms curriculum learning; (5) teacher distribution compatibility/vocabulary alignment outweighs teacher parameter count for OPD.

## Authors & Credibility

Authored by the **ZTE NebulaL0 Post-Training Team** (industry lab, China), led by Qiaobo Hao (contact hao.qiaobo@zte.com.cn). The paper is positioned as a transparent technical report rather than a leaderboard play, with extensive ablations, full hyperparameter tables, training curves (Fig. 4/5/8), and a worked data-schema example (Appendix A). It cites the relevant recent lineage (DeepSeek-V3/V4/R1, Qwen1/2/2.5/3/3.5, GLM-5, Llama 4) and frames its contributions as empirical/mechanistic rather than architectural novelty. Benchmarks are standard and contamination-controlled. Confidence is reasonable for an industrial empirical report; absolute SOTA claims are appropriately hedged ("stronger overall performance than the corresponding baselines under their respective evaluation protocols").

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate. No new model architecture or fundamentally new algorithm — GRPO, SFT, and reverse-KL on-policy distillation are all known (DeepSeek-V4, GLM-5 already use OPD). Genuine contribution is *systematic, transparent ablation* of design knobs at 8B scale, plus the regression-based mixture search and the MOPD cross-teacher recombination observation. The "student beats teacher on math" result is genuinely interesting but under-explained mechanistically.
- **Rigor**: Generally strong — controlled single-variable ablations, fixed eval config, contamination removal, 16-sample AIME averaging, leave-one-indicator-out analysis. However, several comparisons mix "average" over different benchmark sets (e.g., the 74.39 vs 74.78 reasoning SFT average uses a 6-benchmark suite vs others), and some "trade-offs" (LiveBench regressions) are waved through rather than deeply diagnosed.
- **Reproducibility**: Above average for the field — concrete mixture ratios, difficulty thresholds ([0,0.25)/[0.25,0.5)/[0.5,1]), learning rates, rollout sizes, and teacher model choices are given. But the *full data corpus*, exact filter prompts beyond Algorithm 1, the trained classifier weights, and the teacher RL checkpoints are not released, so end-to-end reproduction still requires non-trivial re-engineering.
- **Significance**: Practically useful as a recipe catalog for the open-source 8B post-training community; conceptually, the "correctness filtering is first-order, sampling is second-order" and "teacher compatibility > scale" findings are reusable heuristics. Less impactful for frontier labs already operating at much larger scale.
- **Red flags**: (a) Possible data-leakage ambiguity — the authors flag that some Other-domain examples "may resemble ZebraLogic puzzles" and leave leakage vs. genuine transfer "to future work," yet cite ZebraLogic +35.90 as a headline gain; this is a real concern. (b) Some single-teacher OPD "cross-capability transfer" gains (C-Eval 31.12 → 66.73) are suspiciously large for IF-only training and are attributed to teacher logits encoding world knowledge without direct verification. (c) Findings are explicitly tied to 8B scale (e.g., hard-only > curriculum is "attributed to small model scale"), limiting generality. (d) The report is partly self-promotional about the "fully transparent" framing while not releasing artifacts.

## Strengths

- Unusual transparency on data construction details (distillation teachers, filter taxonomy, difficulty thresholds, classifier training) that most papers withhold.
- Principled three-stage SFT design with clean leave-one-out indicator ablations and the IFD ratio-vs-subtraction analysis grounded in theory.
- Regression-based mixture-ratio search is an elegant cost-efficient formulation.
- Honest reporting of regressions (alignment tax, LiveBench, saturated MATH-500), including failed/abnormal MOPD runs.
- Strong data-efficiency story for OPD (4K/10K samples rivaling 53K RL).

## Limitations

- Entirely 8B-scale; generalization to larger dense and MoE models is unverified and explicitly flagged as open.
- RL restricted to verifiable tasks (math/code/IF); open-ended, long-horizon, agentic, and tool-use settings not covered.
- OPD teachers are also 8B; cross-architecture and stronger-teacher regimes unexplored.
- Evaluation is benchmark-centered and may miss real-world agent robustness.
- Benchmark-contamination control for ZebraLogic/AutoLogi transfer claims is acknowledged as unresolved.
- The reverse-KL top-64 truncation may lose information for low-probability-but-important tokens; ablation of this choice is absent.

## Relevance

与我们关注的"开源小模型后训练实践"高度相关：它提供了一个从数据构建到 SFT、GRPO、OPD 的完整可参照配方，尤其对如何在算力受限的 8B 规模下做数据筛选、混合比例搜索和 verifier-free 蒸馏有直接借鉴价值；其"数据正确性是一阶因素、教师兼容性胜过规模"的结论对指导轻量模型优化尤为实用。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the "math/code opposite data distribution" zero-sum conflict and "hard-only > curriculum" finding persist at 30B+ or MoE scale, or are they artifacts of 8B capacity limits?
- How much of the ZebraLogic +35.90 and AIME gains from Other-domain long-form text are genuine reasoning transfer vs. latent benchmark overlap, and can this be cleanly disentangled with held-out puzzle generators?
- What is the marginal value of the top-64 reverse-KL truncation in OPD — does a learned/probability-aware truncation or full-vocab approximation close the remaining 3-8 point student-teacher gap?
- Can the cross-teacher knowledge recombination that lets MOPD surpass individual teachers on math be characterized and deliberately engineered (e.g., via orthogonal teacher specialization)?
- How do these recipes transfer to agentic/tool-use post-training, where verifiers and teachers both behave very differently?
