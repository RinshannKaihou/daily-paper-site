> **一句话总结：** 提出一个完全无监督的自演化框架（Proposer-Solver-Generator 三个 LoRA 角色），仅用无标注图像就能同时提升统一多模态模型的视觉理解与图像生成能力，并在扩散（BLIP3o）、整流流（BAGEL）和自回归（VARGPT-v1.1）三种生成范式下复用同一套配方取得一致增益。

> **TL;DR:** A fully unsupervised self-evolving framework decomposes a frozen unified LMM into three LoRA roles (Proposer/Solver/Generator) trained only on unlabeled images via self-consistency and token-entropy rewards, consistently improving both understanding and image generation across diffusion (BLIP3o), rectified-flow (BAGEL), and autoregressive (VARGPT-v1.1) backbones with one fixed recipe.

## 核心贡献

- **首个跨生成范式验证的完全自监督 U+G 配方**：在扩散、整流流、自回归三种统一模型上保持角色分解、奖励逻辑和训练调度完全不变，仅替换各骨干网络的原生接口，就实现一致的基线-本方法增益，填补了此前自演化工作只在单一模型族或单一任务方向上验证的空白。
- **Solver Token Entropy (STE) 解决奖励退化**：提出基于 token 级预测不确定性的连续难度信号，即便样本级自一致性坍缩为零熵一致，STE 仍能提供有效的课程学习信号，使冷启动训练成为可能。
- **Solver 介导的跨任务耦合机制**：把 Solver 同时用作生成的内部评估器（QA 保真度 + 循环一致 captioning），让视觉理解的提升直接转化为更强的生成侧奖励，形成理解→生成的非对称增强路径。
- **轻量化、可移植、可复现**：仅训练三个 LoRA 适配器（约 0.8% 参数），冻结骨干，10k 张无标注图像、约 10k 步即可获得 +1.9~3.6 理解指标提升与统一 +3% GenEval 提升，代码与模型全部公开。

## Key Contributions

- **First fully self-supervised U+G recipe validated across three generation paradigms.** The same role decomposition, reward definitions, and training schedule are applied unchanged to diffusion (BLIP3o-8B), rectified-flow (BAGEL), and autoregressive (VARGPT-v1.1) unified backbones, with only backbone-native wrappers swapped — closing a gap left by prior self-evolving work that validated on a single model family or task direction.
- **Solver Token Entropy (STE) to cure reward degeneracy.** A token-level prediction-uncertainty signal that remains informative even when sample-level self-consistency collapses to zero entropy, enabling an automatic curriculum from a cold start; normalized via a rolling percentile window for cross-training stability.
- **Solver-mediated cross-task coupling for generation.** The Solver doubles as an internal evaluator via a multi-scale scheme combining QA-fidelity scoring and cycle-consistent captioning, so improvements in understanding sharpen generation-side rewards (asymmetric solver-mediated rather than symmetric gradient exchange).
- **Lightweight, portable, reproducible.** Trains only three LoRA adapters (~0.8% params) on a frozen backbone using 10k unlabeled images and ~10k steps; code, models, and project page publicly released.

## Methodology

**Problem formulation.** Post-train a frozen unified LMM exposing native understanding `U:(I,q)->a` and generation `G:t->Î` interfaces. The only data is an unlabeled image pool D (no captions, QA pairs, or preference labels). Three LoRA adapters are trained on the frozen backbone: Proposer `π(q|I)`, Solver `π(a|I,q)`, Generator `π(Î|t)`, using only model-native operations, frozen similarity backbones (CLIP-style text/VL embeddings), and each backbone's native generator interface — no external LLM/VLM judges or task-trained reward models. The joint objective decomposes into an understanding loop (Proposer/Solver) and a generation loop (Generator evaluated by Solver), with KL regularization to frozen reference policies.

**Self-consistent understanding.** For an image, the Proposer samples a question; the Solver answers under N=7 fixed prompt framings (semantically-equivalent rephrasings). Sample-level self-consistency is measured by answer-distribution entropy `Hsc`. To address `Hsc` collapse, STE computes the maximum next-token entropy over the first K_ste=5 answer tokens and converts it to a difficulty score via a 128-sample rolling-window percentile rank. The Proposer reward combines STE difficulty with a bounded self-consistency score that favors medium entropy (Gaussian centered at a running EMA target) and down-weights degenerate unanimous votes; the Solver is rewarded for agreeing with the modal answer across framings while penalizing verbosity. Both are optimized with KL-regularized policy gradients (GRPO for the Proposer over K=3 sampled questions; REINFORCE over answer tokens for the Solver) with adaptive KL coefficients.

**Generation assessment via internal evaluation.** The Solver captions the source image to get a prompt t; the Generator produces Î. Two complementary rewards are computed: (1) **QA fidelity** — M diagnostic questions on the real image yield Solver reference answers, then the same questions are asked on Î and soft-matched (exact/substring/numeric agreement + Solver majority fraction); (2) **cycle-consistent captioning** — caption Î and compare to the original prompt via frozen text embeddings plus frozen vision-language similarity. The Generator reward `Rg = w_fid·S_fid + w_cyc·S_cyc + w_div·S_div − w_ctr·S_ctr` adds a leave-one-out diversity term and a polarity-contradiction penalty. Crucially the reward definition is fixed while the low-level update routes to each backbone's native parameterization (token-policy updates for autoregressive; reward-weighted denoising for diffusion/flow). A fixed 3:2 understanding-to-generation step schedule alternates the two loops.

## Results & Highlights

- **Understanding gains are consistent across all 8 metrics and all 3 backbones.** Largest gains land on each backbone's weakest reasoning-heavy metric: MMMU +2.2 (BLIP3o 50.6→52.8), +3.5 (BAGEL 55.3→58.8), +3.0 (VARGPT-v1.1 48.6→51.6); MME-cognition +13.2 to +17.4; perceptual metrics near ceiling (MMBench, TextVQA) still improve +2.1 to +2.8. Percentage gains span +1.9 to +3.6.
- **Uniform +3% GenEval on all three backbones** (BLIP3o 84→87, BAGEL 82→85, VARGPT-v1.1 53→56), with gains concentrated on the hardest compositional subcategories (e.g., Two Objects 85→93 and Counting 63→71 on BLIP3o) while near-saturated Single Object (≥96%) stays stable — a hallmark of targeted curriculum improvement rather than generic noise.
- **Competitive with prior self-supervised methods without their crutches.** BAGEL-ours reaches 58.8 MMMU / 87.1 MMBench / 85% GenEval, competitive with or exceeding UniCorn (53.8 MMMU, 82% GenEval) and SUDER (80.1 MMBench, 84% GenEval), without external LLM/VLM judges, curated supervision, or architecture-specific modules. The authors correctly caveat that cross-paper comparisons are unreliable and emphasize matched base-vs-ours deltas as primary evidence.
- **Ablations confirm every component is non-redundant.** Removing STE costs −1.8 MMMU; removing self-consistency costs −1.3 MMMU; temperature sampling underperforms prompt perturbation; removing QA fidelity is the biggest GenEval hit (87→84); both generation rewards needed for best joint performance. Loop-coupling analysis shows full framework beats both single-loop variants, supporting asymmetric solver-mediated coupling (understanding-only slightly degrades generation 84→83; generation-only leaves Solver-side understanding at base). LoRA (~0.8% params) beats QLoRA, SFT, and full fine-tuning under internal rewards.
- **Training dynamics are stable with no reward plateaus** across all three paradigms over ~10k steps, with STE distribution shifting toward harder quantiles over training (emergent curriculum).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — moderate-to-strong.** The individual ingredients (role-based self-play, self-consistency rewards, token entropy, cycle-consistency evaluation, LoRA adapters) are not new in isolation — UniCorn, EvoLMM, SUDER, SRUM, and Internal Gap each cover parts. The genuine novelty is (a) the *specific combination* of STE as a continuous difficulty signal that fixes self-consistency degeneracy, and (b) the *empirical demonstration* that one unchanged recipe transfers across diffusion / rectified-flow / autoregressive generation paradigms. The Table 1 comparison is fairly constructed (it flags absence of cross-paradigm validation as "not reported," not "inapplicable"), and the cross-paradigm validation claim appears genuinely novel.
- **Rigor — strong.** Matched-budget ablations isolate each component; loop-coupling analysis explicitly tests the solver-mediated mechanism against single-loop baselines; parameter-strategy comparison honestly reports that full fine-tuning underperforms base under internal rewards; training-dynamics figures show no reward collapse across all three paradigms. Hyperparameters are specified (LoRA r=16, lr 1e-6, N=7, K=3, STE window 128, 3:2 schedule, ~10k steps).
- **Reproducibility — strong.** Public code, models, project page; a small 10k-image pool from named open datasets (COCO, SA-1B, TextVQA, GQA, LAION-COCO) with annotations explicitly discarded; fixed hyperparameters; named backbones with versions. Compute (LUMI/Berzelius) is sizeable but not implausibly large.
- **Significance — moderate.** Gains are real and consistent but modest in absolute terms (+2-3.5 points), and the field is moving fast. The cross-paradigm portability is the most transferable insight. The method is explicitly LoRA-only post-training of latent capabilities — it does not unlock fundamentally new abilities.
- **Red flags / concerns — a few honest ones:**
  1. **Distributional leakage risk.** The 10k-image pool includes TextVQA and GQA images, which are also *evaluation* sets for understanding. The authors note annotations are discarded so overlap is "image-only and distributional rather than supervised," but this is still a soft concern — 10k images from five datasets is small, and the TextVQA/GQA test images could in principle be in the pool.
  2. **Primary evidence is within-backbone deltas, not SOTA.** Headline numbers are base-vs-ours on the same backbone; cross-method comparisons (vs UniCorn/SUDER) are explicitly flagged as unreliable, so SOTA claims should be read carefully.
  3. **Solver-bounded supervision.** The method can only improve what the Solver can already evaluate; full fine-tuning is unstable and SFT on self-generated data is weaker, suggesting the gains are genuinely from the reward design but also capped by Solver quality.
  4. **Limited generation benchmarking** — only GenEval is reported for generation; no broader T2I-CompBench, DPG, or human evaluation.
  5. **Author-cluster overlap** with VISE (2606.27373) means some concepts (visual under-conditioning, self-evolving LMMs, invariance rewards) are shared across papers — readers should consult both.

## Strengths

- Genuinely label-free: no human annotations, preference data, or task-trained external reward/judge models — only unlabeled images.
- Clean, well-motivated technical contributions (STE solves a real degeneracy problem; solver-mediated coupling is a principled design).
- Backbone-portable: one recipe, three fundamentally different generation paradigms, consistent gains — strong generalization evidence.
- Honest framing: emphasizes matched deltas, reports negative results (full fine-tuning underperforms base, generation-only doesn't improve Solver).
- Lightweight and reproducible: ~0.8% trainable params, small image pool, ~10k steps, full public release.
- Comprehensive ablations and training-dynamics analysis support each design choice.

## Limitations

- Gains are modest in absolute terms (+2 to +3.5 percentage points); near-ceiling metrics barely move.
- Solver-bounded: supervision quality is capped by the Solver's current competence; the method cannot exceed what the Solver can evaluate.
- Only one generation benchmark (GenEval); broader generation evaluation and human studies are missing.
- Possible distributional overlap between the small (10k) training pool and the TextVQA/GQA evaluation images.
- Full fine-tuning and SFT are unstable/underperforming under internal rewards — the method is effectively LoRA-only.
- Autoregressive backbone (VARGPT-v1.1) remains weak on Position (15%) and Color Attribution (24%) even after gains — semantic-level supervision cannot fully overcome architectural limits.
- No evaluation on video/3D or of scaling behavior beyond the stated backbones (acknowledged as future work).

## Relevance

该工作对统一多模态模型（理解+生成）的无监督后训练具有直接方法论价值，尤其其"骨干可移植的统一自演化配方"和 STE 难度信号思路，可为降低大模型后训练对昂贵人工标注的依赖提供可借鉴的范式。对我们关注的多模态自演化、自奖励强化学习方向高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does scaling the unlabeled image pool (e.g., to 100k or 1M) yield further gains, or does the Solver-bounded ceiling plateau quickly? What is the marginal-return curve?
- How sensitive are gains to the distributional overlap between the training pool and evaluation sets — would gains shrink if TextVQA/GQA images were strictly excluded?
- Can a stronger / larger base Solver (or an explicitly trained internal evaluator) lift the supervision ceiling and push gains beyond +3-4 points?
- Does the solver-mediated coupling direction generalize to a third task (e.g., editing, video understanding) or break down outside understanding↔generation?
- Would adding broader generation benchmarks (T2I-CompBench, DPG-Bench) and human evaluation confirm the GenEval-only gains, especially for the weak autoregressive backbone?
- Can STE be combined with visual-conditioning/invariance rewards (as in the companion VISE paper) for compounding gains on both framing-robustness and visual-grounding axes?
