> **一句话总结：** 本文是首个专门聚焦联邦学习（FL）中量化技术的系统性综述，基于 PRISMA 方法对 2019–2026 年文献进行检索筛选，提出了一套以 FL 为中心的全新量化分类法（涵盖 PTQ/QAT、量化对象、编码结构、精度策略及六项系统级属性），系统分析了量化与客户端漂移、聚合一致性、非独立同分布稳健性、隐私安全及硬件能耗之间的交互，并指出标准化基准缺失、收敛理论不完善、真实部署不足等关键研究空白与未来方向。

> **TL;DR:** A PRISMA-based systematic literature review that claims to be the first survey dedicated exclusively to quantization in Federated Learning. It proposes an FL-centric taxonomy (training stage, quantized object, encoding structure, precision strategy) crossed with six system-level dimensions (client heterogeneity, aggregation consistency, communication-scheduling adaptation, non-IID robustness, privacy/security integration, hardware-energy co-optimization), analyzes trade-offs and convergence theory, and lays out open gaps and future directions for deploying quantized FL on mobile, IoT, and edge platforms.

## 核心贡献

- **首个 FL 量化专属系统综述：** 采用 PRISMA 方法学，检索了 IEEE Xplore、arXiv、ScienceDirect、ACM DL、Wiley 等六个数据库（2019–2026 年），填补了既有综述只把量化当作"通信效率"子组件、缺乏专门系统梳理的空白。
- **全新的 FL 中心化量化分类法：** 从训练阶段（PTQ vs QAT）、量化对象（参数/梯度/更新）、编码结构（标量 vs 向量、均匀 vs 非均匀）、精度策略（固定位/可变位/自适应位）四个算法维度切入，并与六项系统级属性（客户端异构性、聚合一致性、通信调度适配、非 IID 稳健性、隐私安全集成、硬件能耗协同）正交组织。
- **系统化的方法学梳理：** 详细剖析了动态范围量化（DRQ）、权重裁剪与校准、QAT 及其变体（可微量化、梯度式学习量化参数、自适应精度 QAT、联邦混合 QAT）、QA-FedAvg 等框架，并配以对照表格。
- **挑战、空白与未来方向的全面识别：** 指出通信—精度权衡、非 IID 下的漂移与收敛失稳、隐私泄露、硬件/能耗约束等挑战，并明确标准化基准缺失、收敛理论不完整、真实部署与可复现性不足、自适应/个性化量化未充分探索等空白。
- **工程化设计指引：** 为在移动、IoT、边缘平台上部署量化 FL 提供实践导向的设计指南，并展望自适应混合量化、与剪枝/稀疏化联合、能耗与硬件感知、量子/神经形态启发、多目标优化等方向。

## Key Contributions

- First dedicated systematic survey of quantization in FL, framed via the PRISMA reporting standard and an explicit comparison table showing prior FL surveys treat quantization only as a sub-topic.
- A novel FL-centric taxonomy that decouples four algorithmic axes (training stage, quantized object, encoding structure, precision control) from six FL-specific system dimensions (client heterogeneity, aggregation consistency, communication-scheduling adaptation, non-IID robustness, privacy/security integration, hardware-energy co-optimization).
- Granular methodological walkthroughs of PTQ (standard DRQ, hardware-aware DRQ, adaptive DRQ), weight clipping and calibration (KLD/MSE/PACT/layer-wise clipped FL), and QAT (differentiable quantization, learned parameters such as LSQ/LSQ+, adaptive precision QAT, federated hybrid QAT, QA-FedAvg), including a QA-FL pseudocode and per-method comparison tables.
- Critical synthesis of challenges with formal expressions: the communication–accuracy MSE bound (∝ 2^{-2b}), non-IID variance amplification, a convergence bound E||W_t − W*||^2 ≤ (1−ημ)^t ||W_0 − W*||^2 + ησ_q^2/μ, and privacy/security implications of quantized updates.
- A structured identification of research gaps (lack of standardized benchmarks, incomplete convergence theory under non-IID/asynchronous settings, limited real-world deployment, neglect of adaptive/personalized quantization, insufficient multi-objective optimization, privacy/security gaps, energy/hardware-aware gaps) and corresponding future directions, each tied back to four explicit research questions (RQ1–RQ4).

## Methodology

This is a Systematic Literature Review (SLR) following the PRISMA (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses) protocol. The authors:

1. Defined four research questions (RQ1: impact on performance/efficiency; RQ2: challenges and limitations; RQ3: research gaps across algorithmic/statistical/system dimensions; RQ4: emerging trends).
2. Searched six databases with explicit query strings over 2019–2026: IEEE Xplore (647 retrieved → 41 eligible), arXiv (18 → 6), ScienceDirect (1593 → 2), ACM DL (1135 → 2), Wiley (454 → 1), and "Others" (78 → 8). This yields ~60 screened-eligible studies (the exact final count after full deduplication is not stated).
3. Applied inclusion/exclusion criteria: peer-reviewed English publications where quantization is a primary FL research focus, published in flagship conferences or reputable journals.
4. Performed qualitative synthesis organized through the proposed taxonomy, producing comparison tables per quantization family (DRQ, clipping/calibration, QAT, etc.), a challenges table, a research-gaps table, and a future-directions table.

The paper is a survey (no new experiments or datasets); "results" are synthesized claims about the reviewed literature, e.g., that well-calibrated quantization can reduce communication bandwidth by up to 90% with less than 2% accuracy loss, and that DRQ yields up to 4× storage reduction and 2–3× faster inference.

## Results & Highlights

- Argues quantization should be viewed not merely as compression but as a fundamental systems component shaping FL performance, robustness, and practicality.
- Reports that uniform/fixed-point methods dominate due to hardware compatibility, while adaptive/non-uniform schemes improve non-IID robustness; PTQ favors deployment simplicity, QAT favors robustness at extra compute cost.
- Quotes order-of-magnitude claims: up to 10×–50× compression with minimal accuracy loss when properly calibrated; DRQ up to 4× model-size reduction and 2–3× faster inference.
- Articulates the core trade-off formally: MSE ∝ 2^{−2b}, so lowering bit-width b raises quantization error and convergence floor; bias correction, stochastic quantization, and error feedback mitigate but do not eliminate this.
- Highlights frameworks by name: QA-FedAvg, QFL, FedDQ, FedMixQAT, QSGD, FedWSQ, ClippedFedAvg, NQFL, mixed-precision FL for heterogeneous devices.
- Future directions tabled: adaptive/hybrid PTQ–QAT, joint quantization+pruning/sparsification, energy- and hardware-aware quantization, quantum-inspired and neuromorphic approaches, benchmarking/reproducibility standards (LEAF, FedML, TensorFlow Federated), application-specific quantization (healthcare, autonomous driving, IoT), and federated multi-objective optimization.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (moderate):** The claim to be the "first survey exclusively focused on quantization in FL" is the central novelty and is plausibly true as a dedicated monograph, but many constituent analyses (QSGD convergence, PTQ/QAT, secure aggregation) are well-trodden individually; the genuine contribution is the unifying FL-centric taxonomy rather than new methods or empirical findings.
- **Rigor (mixed):** PRISMA adherence and explicit per-database retrieval counts (with a flowchart reference) are strengths. Weaknesses: the final included-study count after deduplication/full-text screening is not crisply stated, inter-rater reliability is not reported, and inclusion/exclusion are described qualitatively rather than as a strict protocol. Quantitative claims (e.g., "up to 90% bandwidth reduction with <2% accuracy loss", "10×–50× compression") are stated without per-source attribution or confidence intervals, which is a meaningful rigor gap for a survey.
- **Reproducibility (limited):** No code, dataset list, or extraction spreadsheet is provided. Search strings are given (a plus), but the lack of an open repository conflicts with the paper's own strong call for reproducibility standards.
- **Significance (potentially high for practitioners, moderate for researchers):** A useful entry point and design guide for engineers deploying quantized FL on edge/IoT; less likely to shift research frontiers because it introduces no new algorithm or benchmark.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - Template artifacts (ACM "August 2018" copyright, placeholder DOI "XXXXXXX.XXXXXXX") suggest the manuscript is a preprint not yet peer-reviewed.
  - Mixed date evidence (a 2026 citation while claiming coverage through "early 2025") indicates the literature window and reference hygiene need tightening.
  - Some quantization–compression ratios are presented as universal facts rather than conditioned on model/architecture/data — overstated generalization risk.
  - No quantitative meta-analysis; all comparisons are narrative and tabular.
- **Honest verdict:** A competent, well-organized, and genuinely useful survey that fills a real gap, but it should be read as a structured introduction and taxonomy rather than a source of rigorously validated performance numbers; its own limitations section (peer-reviewed coverage cutoff, no deep treatment of distillation/low-rank) is candid on scope but omits the reproducibility and per-claim attribution gaps noted above.

## Strengths

- First dedicated, FL-specific systematic review of quantization — a genuine gap in the literature.
- A clean, memorable taxonomy that decouples algorithmic axes from FL-system dimensions and is consistently used across all comparison tables.
- Strong methodological transparency on the SLR protocol (databases, search strings, year range, screening stages, RQs).
- Integrates normally-siloed concerns: convergence theory, secure aggregation, differential privacy, hardware/energy — bridging communication, computation, and security communities.
- Actionable future-directions agenda (adaptive/hybrid quantization, multi-objective optimization, hardware-aware design, benchmarks) that maps each gap to a concrete opportunity.

## Limitations

- It is a survey with no original experiments, datasets, models, or benchmarks — claims about compression ratios and accuracy degradation are aggregated from cited works without controlled re-evaluation.
- Final deduplicated included-study count and inter-rater reliability are not reported, weakening the SLR's auditability.
- Quantitative headline numbers (90% bandwidth reduction, 10×–50× compression, <2% accuracy loss) are not attributed to specific studies or conditioned on settings.
- No accompanying code or open extraction artifacts, despite the paper's emphasis on reproducibility.
- Reference hygiene issues (placeholder DOI, 2018 ACM copyright notice, 2026 citation vs. "early 2025" coverage claim) suggest a draft-stage preprint.
- Lightweight treatment of adjacent compression (knowledge distillation, low-rank factorization), as the authors themselves concede.
- Some future directions (quantum-inspired, neuromorphic) are speculative and not grounded in demonstrated FL results.

## Relevance

对从事边缘计算、IoT 与隐私保护分布式学习的工程与研究人员而言，这是一份有价值的入门与选型指南；其分类法和挑战清单可直接指导量化方案在异构、非 IID、带宽受限的联邦学习场景中的设计取舍，也为后续构建统一基准与多目标优化框架提供了清晰的路线图。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What is the exact number of finally-included primary studies after deduplication and full-text screening, and what was the inter-rater agreement during study selection?
- How do the headline compression/accuracy claims (90% bandwidth reduction, 10×–50× compression, <2% accuracy loss) decompose by quantization family (PTQ vs QAT), bit-width, model architecture, and degree of non-IIDness?
- Can the proposed taxonomy be operationalized into a standardized benchmark suite (extending LEAF/FedML/TFF) with fixed datasets, metrics (bits-per-update, compression ratio, energy-per-inference), and reference baselines for fair cross-method comparison?
- How does quantization noise interact non-linearly with differential-privacy noise and secure-aggregation masking, and can a joint convergence bound be derived under non-IID, asynchronous, partial-participation FL?
- Are adaptive/personalized bit-width allocation policies provably Pareto-optimal on the communication cost / quantization error / energy / privacy multi-objective frontier the authors propose?
- Do the speculative directions (quantum-inspired amplitude encoding, neuromorphic spike-based quantization) have any existing empirical FL evidence, or are they purely aspirational?
