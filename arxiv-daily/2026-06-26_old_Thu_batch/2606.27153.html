Key Finding

DMuon brings distributed Muon's per-step wall-clock within ~2% of AdamW via owner-centric execution and shape-specialized kernels — no convergence compromise.

DMuon: Efficient Distributed Muon Training with Near-Adam Overhead ↗
Vincent Chen, Starrick Liu, Regis Cheng, Dance Yang et al. (X Square Robot) ·2026-06-26 ·8–256 × A800-80GB
distributed-training optimizer muon systems llm-training

Key Metric

+2% vs AdamW

Average end-to-end step time across 4 workloads; peak optimizer-step speedup reaches 163.82× (Qwen2.5-7B @ 256 GPUs).

Why This Matters

If the ~2%-overhead claim holds generally, it materially lowers the barrier to adopting Muon-style optimizers in production FSDP2 stacks — a real systems contribution, not theory.

Critical Edge

The Muon-AG baseline is deliberately naive, so the 1.48×–3.01× headline partly measures against a strawman; the AdamW comparison is the honest target.

Methodology

DMuon attacks the "granularity mismatch": sharded stacks (ZeRO / FSDP / TP) expose parameters in pieces, but Muon's Newton-Schulz (NS) iteration needs the full reduced-matrix gradient on one device to form XXT. A naive gather-then-compute baseline forces every rank to materialize every full matrix and redundantly run identical NS — at scale the optimizer step can rival forward+backward.

  1. Parameter materialization — owner-held weights are packed right before the consuming layer (lookahead inter-node, then local intra-node), preserving FSDP-style transient peak memory.
  2. Gradient routing — backward gradients are reduced only to owners, yielding the same averaged full-matrix gradient a synchronous reference would use.
  3. Owner-side Muon update — each owner runs Gram-space NS in fp16 (3 extra mantissa bits over bf16 at equal tensor-core cost, wgrad-validated), updates fp32 master weights, casts back. Non-matrix params fall through to AdamW.
  4. Asynchronous publication — updated weights are published back, waited on only at the consuming layer's pre-forward hook.

The owner-side NS is optimized three ways: (i) Gram NS drops dominant cost from O(m²n) to O(m³) by iterating in the m×m Gram space; (ii) a SYRK-style symmetric kernel computes only the lower triangle with the follow-up fused in the epilogue; (iii) batching + DSL autotuning (TileLang/CUTE) groups sub-occupying small matrices and caches per-shape optimal schedules. Owner assignment is a MILP solved once at init, minimizing predicted makespan over measured (not analytical) per-(shape,batch) costs — falling back to greedy above a threshold.

Strengths

  • Production-validated on four diverse workloads spanning VLA (robotics) and LLM pretraining — not toy benchmarks.
  • Exact-semantics guarantee is architecturally enforced; no approximation to the Muon update.
  • Clean separation of concerns (communication runtime / kernel engine / scheduling) with a small user surface.
  • Pragmatic engineering detail: fp16-vs-bf16 rationale, transient materialization, TP nesting detected from live DTensor placements.
  • Honest scoping of limitations (single-GPU benefit shrinks; largest-matrix NS is irreducible).

Limitations

  • No distributed-overlap gains on single-GPU training (~2× optimizer-time reduction only).
  • Cannot remove the critical-path NS of the largest owner-side matrix — needs a different optimizer.
  • Single cluster type (A800); H100/B200, RoCE, other NVLink topologies untested.
  • MILP solved once at init — cannot adapt to mid-training shape/batch changes without re-profiling.
  • Cost model benchmarked once; kernel-cache staleness on driver/firmware updates not discussed.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the symmetric-kernel / autotuning advantage — and fp16 NS precision safety — transfer to Hopper/Blackwell (H100/B200)?
  2. Can MILP owner assignment be made adaptive mid-training (sequence-length, batch, context schedules) without prohibitive re-profiling?
  3. What is the wall-clock cost of one-time autotuning + MILP solve, and how does it amortize for short vs. long runs?
  4. Could systems+algorithm co-design reduce the irreducible largest-matrix NS via block-diagonal / lower-rank ortho without breaking Muon semantics?
  5. Is independent reproduction on a different cluster and model family consistent with the "+2% vs AdamW" claim?
  6. How does DMuon interact with async / 1F1B pipeline-parallel schedules, whose overlap differs from pure FSDP2?
