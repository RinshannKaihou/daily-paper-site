Chain-of-thought reasoning buys no accuracy for moderation guardrails — a 395M label-only encoder matches a 1.24B reasoner at ~100× lower cost.
LeanGuard: A Fast and Light Approach for Robust Moderation · Dongbin Na · 2026-06-25
Key Metric
~100×
lower inference cost than a 1.24B reasoning guard — at equal or better F1 (82.90 vs 82.05)
Why This Matters

Reasoning-first guardrails are the trend, but a single-pass 395M encoder is cheaper, more noise-robust, and far more recall at strict false-positive budgets — for on-device moderation.

Critical Edge

“Reasoning” is narrowly scoped to CoT fine-tuning on one corpus; the verdict-flip + probe result is an observation, not a proof — benchmarks may simply not yet be hard enough to reward reasoning.

Methodology

Moderation is framed as a bounded labeling problem with a three-part verdict: request-harm, completion (refuse/comply), and response-harm. The central tool is a controlled same-base comparison: on an identical backbone, only the supervision target varies, isolating reasoning as the single manipulated factor.

LeanGuard is a ModernBERT-large (395M) discriminative encoder pooling the full (prompt, response) instance into one representation with three independent linear heads, trained with cross-entropy on verdict labels alone — no reasoning trace is constructed or scored.

Generative guards — a Llama-3.2-1B decoder and a T5-base encoder-decoder — are each trained in two modes on identical data/optimizer/schedule: generate-with-CoT (emits a GuardReasoner trace then verdict) vs generate-label-only. Heavier decoders use LoRA.

Post-hoc analysis re-samples the chain with majority voting and fits a linear probe on early/mid/late hidden states. Robustness injects symmetric label noise at 0/10/20/30% and sweeps thresholds for recall at fixed 1% FPR. All settings use 3 seeds, AdamW with cosine decay, bf16, 512-token context on GuardReasoner’s 127,465-example corpus. An information-theoretic argument (I(Y;R|X)=0) frames why reasoning adds no new information about the verdict.

Strengths

  • Cleanly isolates the single variable (reasoning) via same-base ablation — fills a gap left by GuardReasoner, ThinkGuard, and R2-Guard.
  • Strong negative result with careful scoping (sufficiency vs confound-controlled claims); pre-registered null hypotheses H1/H2/H3, 3 seeds, std ≤ 0.4.
  • Mechanistic evidence — verdict-flip rate, hidden-state probe, AUC 0.926 separability — explains why, not just that.
  • Deployable artifact: ONNX export, 512-token context, single forward pass, strong on-device motivation.
  • Unusual honesty about the T5 result, borrowed cells, and the single-corpus confound.

Limitations

  • Scope limited to CoT fine-tuning; test-time (o1-style) reasoning, tool use, and verifier pipelines are not tested.
  • Single training corpus and single author; cross-corpus and multi-author validation absent.
  • The 395M-vs-1.24B cross-architecture contrast conflates architecture with pretraining recency (ModernBERT is newer); only sufficiency is claimed.
  • Bidirectional attention itself is not isolated as a factor.
  • Does not address interpretability/auditability — a human-readable rationale may be worth its cost even when accuracy is unchanged.
  • Multilingual and multimodal moderation — where the boundary may be less separable — left as future work where reasoning could earn its cost.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the conclusion hold on adversarial or distribution-shifted benchmarks (jailbreaks, obfuscated harm) where the decision may not be linearly separable?
  2. Does test-time reasoning (o1-style) change the picture, as opposed to CoT fine-tuning?
  3. How do the results transfer to multilingual and multimodal moderation, where the harmful boundary is plausibly more compositional?
  4. Can a cheap post-hoc rationale generator deliver auditability without paying the chain cost on every call?
  5. Is the ~5% verdict-flip rate a stable property across guard scales and corpora, or does it grow for genuinely multi-step policy decisions?
  6. How does the strict-FPR recall gap evolve under calibration (e.g. temperature scaling) of the reasoner’s verdict-token probability?
