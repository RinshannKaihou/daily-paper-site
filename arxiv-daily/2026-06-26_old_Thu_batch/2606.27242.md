> **一句话总结：** 本文证明基于表征相似度（如 CKA/RSA/SVCCA）的迁移预测在"激活暗"区域（activation-dark，共享词表但预测目标不同）无法区分任务，并给出 FisherSketch——一种单次流式算法，把每个任务压缩成 16 KB 签名，在 K≈10⁵ 词表规模下估计头层 Fisher 对齐，用于共享词表 LLM 族的免训练源数据选择。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proves that representation-only metrics (CKA/RSA/SVCCA) are non-identifiable for head-level transfer when LLMs share a vocabulary but differ in prediction targets (an "activation-dark" regime), and introduces **FisherSketch**, a one-pass streaming estimator that compresses each task into a 16 KB random-feature signature to estimate head Fisher alignment at vocabulary scale (K≈10⁵), enabling training-free source selection and task-geometry diagnosis.

## 核心贡献

- **非可辨识性定理（Theorem 3.2）：** 形式化证明任何仅基于表征（激活）的相似度度量都无法确定头层 Fisher 对齐——存在两个任务激活完全相同（Z_i = Z_j）但头更新方向正交（A_F = 0）。Corollary 3.3 进一步指出 CKA 只依赖中心化表征协方差，对误差二阶矩 Γ_e 完全"失明"。
- **乘积核结构恒等式（Theorem 4.2）：** 证明头层 Fisher 对齐恰为联合 (激活 a, 误差 e) 特征空间中核均值嵌入的余弦相似度，并可分解为激活几何 S_cov(M_a)、误差几何 S_cov(Γ_e) 与耦合项 ρ 三因子的乘积 A_F = S_cov(M_a)·S_cov(Γ_e)·ρ。
- **FisherSketch 流式估计器：** 利用 Kronecker 梯度结构 g = a⊗e 的 Random Maclaurin / SRHT 随机特征，在单次流式遍历中直接估计乘积核余弦。把每个任务从约 61 GiB（K=128,256 时存 Γ_e）压缩为 m=4096 维、16 KB 的 fp32 签名，流式状态仅 192 KB。
- **词汇规模 + 激活暗区域实证：** 在 Llama-3.1-8B 100 个域上做 LoRA 源选择，FisherSketch 取得 45.7% top-1（≈10.9× 随机基线，达到 98.44% 的 oracle 归一化迁移）；在 verbalizer-shift 设置下，激活基线退化为随机（20% top-1），FisherSketch 达 66.7% top-1。

## Key Contributions

- **Non-identifiability witness (Theorem 3.2 / Corollary 3.3):** Any representation-only metric M(Z_i, Z_j) cannot identify head Fisher alignment — there exist tasks with identical probe representations whose head updates are orthogonal. CKA/RSA/SVCCA depend only on centered representation covariances and are blind to the error second moment Γ_e.
- **Product-kernel cosine identity (Theorem 4.2):** Head Fisher alignment is exactly the cosine between kernel mean embeddings in the RKHS of the product kernel k_ae = (aᵀa′)²(eᵀe′)² over joint (activation, error) features, decomposing into activation geometry, error geometry, and a coupling ratio ρ (Eq. 5).
- **FisherSketch streaming estimator:** Uses factored Random Maclaurin / Subsampled Randomized Hadamard Transform (SRHT) features that exploit the Kronecker gradient structure g = a⊗e to estimate the product-kernel cosine in O(m(d+K)) per sample rather than O(mdK). Compresses each task from ~61 GiB to a 16 KB fp32 signature (m=4096) with a 192 KB split-sample streaming state.
- **Vocabulary-scale + activation-dark validation:** On Llama-3.1-8B (K=128,256, 100 domains) FisherSketch achieves 45.7%±5.0 top-1 source selection (98.44% of oracle normalized transfer). In the verbalizer-shift stress test (fixed prompt prefix → identical activations), activation-only baselines collapse to random (20% top-1) while FisherSketch reaches 66.7% top-1.
- **Diagnostic instrument:** The same signatures decompose LLM task neighborhoods into representation-driven, error-driven, or activation-dark categories, and support training-free task retrieval (55.5% top-1 on 700-domain Natural Instructions).

## Methodology

The paper builds on three observations. **(1) Kronecker gradient structure (Lemma 4.1):** for an affine head with input activation a and backpropagated error e, the per-example weight gradient is g_head = a ⊗ e. **(2) Product-kernel rewrite (Theorem 4.2):** the head Fisher inner product ⟨F_i, F_j⟩_F = E[(gᵀg′)²] = E[(aᵀa′)²(eᵀe′)²] is the expectation of a degree-4 product polynomial kernel, so alignment becomes cos(μ_ae,i, μ_ae,j) where μ_ae,k = E[ϕ(a,e)] is the kernel mean embedding. This yields the exact three-factor identity A_F(i,j) = S_cov(M_a,i, M_a,j) · S_cov(Γ_e,i, Γ_e,j) · ρ_ij, with M_a = E[aaᵀ], Γ_e = E[eeᵀ], and ρ the non-separability ratio.

**(3) FisherSketch (Proposition 5.1):** with mutually independent Rademacher vectors r_a, r_a′ ∈ {±1}^d and r_e, r_e′ ∈ {±1}^K, the sketch coordinate ψ(a,e) = (r_aᵀa)(r_eᵀe)(r_a′ᵀa)(r_e′ᵀe) is unbiased for the product kernel, costing only O(m(d+K)) per sample. m coordinates are averaged into a normalized signature Ψ_m(a,e). Alignments between tasks are then just dot products of ℓ2-normalized signatures. Stabilization includes split-half log-ratio estimation, empirical-Bayes shrinkage of the coupling term, U-statistic diagonal correction, and small-norm clamping. For the K-dimensional error side at vocabulary scale, an SRHT backend (batched Walsh–Hadamard butterfly) replaces dense Rademacher matrices.

**Evaluation protocol:** source selection is measured by normalized LoRA transfer U_{s→t} = (ℓ_base − ℓ_{s→t}) / max(ℓ_base − ℓ_self, 0.01), with Top-1/Top-3 accuracy, Max Regret, and Percent Oracle against an argmax oracle. ViT-B/16 (20 checkpoints, 4 datasets) provides sketch-vs-exact calibration; Llama-3.1-8B (100 domains, 24 fixed candidates/target, seeds 43–45) provides vocabulary-scale results; a verbalizer-shift setup (BoolQ/RTE/CB, 3 seeds × 3 datasets) provides the activation-dark stress test; 9 SMILES domains provide a scientific-sequence proof of concept (Appendix R.4).

## Results & Highlights

- **Sketch fidelity (ViT-B/16):** Spearman vs. exact at m=4096 is 0.97±0.01 for Ŝ_ae, 0.94±0.01 for χ̂, 0.95±0.01 for ρ̂; Â_F is lower (0.79±0.06) due to ratio normalization. Shared-layer validation preserves exact-Fisher rankings with Spearman 0.997–0.998 on ResNet-18 conv layers and 0.994–0.997 on ViT-B/16 MLP/QKV layers. 89× speedup at n=2000, m=4096.
- **Vocabulary-scale source selection (Llama-3.1-8B, Table 1):** FisherSketch 45.7%±5.0 top-1, 87.3%±1.2 top-3, Max Regret 0.119±0.013, 98.44%±0.30% of oracle. Competitive with activation-only marginal S_cov(M_a) (46.3%±3.7 top-1 but lower tail risk in FisherSketch) and clearly above PPL similarity (28%) and random (4.2%). Per-target Spearman with transfer = 0.47±0.02.
- **Verbalizer-shift stress test (Table 2):** with fixed prefix making S_cov(M_a)≈1 (constant), activation-only collapses to 20% top-1 (random), while FisherSketch reaches 66.7% top-1 / 95.7% of oracle; error-only marginal S_cov(Γ_e) reaches 72.2% top-1 / 100% of oracle — confirming that when activation geometry carries no signal, the ranking must come from label-conditioned error geometry.
- **Scientific sequences (Appendix R.4):** across 9 SMILES domains FisherSketch correlates with cross-domain perplexity reduction (ρ_s = 0.53, p=0.006), whereas activation-only similarity is not significant (p=0.081).
- **Task retrieval (Appendix R):** 55.5% top-1 nearest-neighbor retrieval on a 700-domain Natural Instructions open-set.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high):** The product-kernel cosine identity (Theorem 4.2) reframing head Fisher alignment as a kernel mean embedding is genuinely new, as is the explicit non-identifiability witness (Theorem 3.2) for representation-only metrics. The combination of (i) a clean impossibility result, (ii) an exact re-identity that explains *why* representation metrics fail, and (iii) a streaming estimator that operationalizes the fix is a well-rounded contribution. The three-factor activation/error/coupling decomposition is a useful diagnostic lens.
- **Rigor (high):** Theorems are stated precisely with assumptions (finite fourth moments, shared output basis, affine head), proofs are deferred to appendices but sketched in-text, and the scope is explicitly bounded (head-level only; requires shared vocabulary). Honest caveats: the separable Kronecker proxy is negatively correlated with exact head Fisher on ViT-B/16, so the authors do not use it and instead estimate the joint embedding directly.
- **Reproducibility (good):** Fixed candidate sets and sketch sample indices are released; disjoint sketch vs. LoRA splits are enforced; multiple seeds (43–45) reported with std. Sketch hyperparameters (m=4096, SRHT backend, shrinkage details) are documented in appendices. Memory/cost figures are concrete (16 KB signature, 192 KB streaming state, 4.7s/domain on A100).
- **Significance (moderate-high):** Practically relevant for automated LoRA source selection, continual-learning interference detection, and inference-time routing in shared-vocabulary LLM families (SMILES, protein, genomic domains). However, the headline numbers should be read carefully: on natural shifts, FisherSketch is only *competitive* with (not dominant over) the activation-only marginal, and the error-only marginal actually beats it on top-1 in the verbalizer-shift regime. The clearest win is in the narrow fixed-prefix / activation-dark regime.
- **Red flags / caveats:**
  - The main empirical lift over strong baselines is modest on natural shifts — FisherSketch's advantage is concentrated in the activation-dark regime, which may or may not be common in practice.
  - Head-level only; requires a shared output coordinate system (shared vocabulary or masked-softmax label taxonomy). Heterogeneous-output tasks need a different recipe (full-network or internal-layer comparison), limiting generality.
  - Uses empirical (data) Fisher at a fixed checkpoint; results may vary across checkpoints, population Fisher, and adaptation methods — signatures are checkpoint-conditional.
  - On ViT-B/16 same-dataset pairs, CKA correlates only modestly with exact head Fisher (Pearson 0.43) — the paper uses this to motivate the gap, but it also means representation metrics are not catastrophically bad in all regimes, tempering the framing.
  - The separable proxy FA-CKA = CKA · S_cov(Γ_e) requires K² storage and is not used at scale, so the "three-factor decomposition" is a diagnostic rather than a deployed estimator.

## Strengths

- Clean, complete theory-to-method arc: non-identifiability result → product-kernel identity → streaming estimator, each motivating the next.
- Explicitly honest about when the method wins (activation-dark) and when activation-only baselines are competitive (natural shifts) — no overclaiming.
- Memory and compute footprint is genuinely small (16 KB signature, single streaming pass), making it deployable next to a model hash.
- Three-factor decomposition (activation, error, coupling) provides an interpretable diagnostic beyond a scalar transferability score.
- Validation spans architectures (ResNet, ViT, Llama-3.1-8B), domains (vision, NLP, SMILES), and regimes (sketch fidelity, natural shifts, activation-dark stress test).

## Limitations

- Requires a shared output basis; does not directly apply to heterogeneous-output tasks without an output embedding/transport map.
- Head-level only; extending to full-network or shared-parameter layers requires additional diagnostics (profile cosine, off-diagonal discrepancy) and is less exact.
- Empirical Fisher at a single checkpoint; sensitivity to checkpoint choice, population vs. empirical Fisher, and the adaptation method (LoRA) is acknowledged but not fully quantified.
- On natural domain shifts, FisherSketch does not strictly dominate simpler activation-only or error-only marginals — its value proposition is regime-specific.
- Absolute source-selection numbers depend on domain construction, candidate draws, and transfer hyperparameters (acknowledged in §7.1).
- Single author; some experimental scope (e.g., larger models, more adaptation methods, full-network FisherSketch at vocabulary scale) is left to future work.

## Relevance

对做大模型迁移学习、LoRA 源数据选择、 continual/multi-task 学习或科学序列（SMILES/蛋白/基因组）LLM 适配的团队很有价值：它从理论上解释了"为什么表征相似度在共享词表场景会失灵"，并提供了一个 16 KB 量级、可流式计算的免训练迁移兼容性信号，适合嵌入自动化微调和路由管线中作为筛选/排序的输入之一。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does FisherSketch behave with larger models (e.g., 70B+) and beyond LoRA — does the head-only analysis still capture the dominant transfer signal under full fine-tuning or QLoRA?
- What is the sensitivity of the signatures to the choice of checkpoint, and can signatures be averaged or aggregated across checkpoints for a more robust estimate?
- Can the three-factor decomposition be extended to give a per-layer, full-network FisherSketch with the same O(m(d+K)) streaming cost, and does it improve source selection beyond the head-only version?
- In the activation-dark regime, how common is it in real-world adapter/model zoos — is the verbalizer-shift setting representative, or an edge case?
- Could the coupling term ρ be used as a training-time signal for interference-aware continual learning or gradient-surgery-style multi-task optimization?
- How does FisherSketch compare to LogME/LEEP and influence-based methods when K is large but a label taxonomy or masked-softmax embedding is available (Appendix O.7 / Q)?
