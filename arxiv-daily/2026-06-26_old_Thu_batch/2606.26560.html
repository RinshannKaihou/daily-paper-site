Erase-then-Delta Attention: Decoupling Erase and Write Addresses in Delta-Rule Linear Attention

Xiao Li, Chengruidong Zhang, Hao Luo, Xi Lin, et al. (18 authors) · Qwen Team (Alibaba), Nanjing University, Zhejiang University

An independently-addressed erase step before the delta write gives delta-rule linear attention a targeted cleanup path for stale memory.

linear-attention delta-rule recurrent-memory efficient-LLM hybrid-architecture
Key Metric
49.38
EDA average on MoE 25B-A2.8B pretraining (400B tokens), best of all — vs KDA 48.31, GDN 48.16. Lead holds after 80B-token long-context midtraining (Avg 58.45).
Why This Matters
Independent-erase is a drop-in plug-in for delta-rule variants in efficient long-context LLMs (Qwen3.5, Kimi Linear), where stale-memory cleanup matters most.
Critical Edge
Sub-1-point MoE gains sit within run-to-run variance, and EDA actively hurts raw write-key recall — a conditional cleanup, not a free improvement.

Methodology

Setting. Hybrid linear-attention stacks (3:1 linear-to-full attention, Qwen3.5 / Kimi Linear convention) with a channel-wise gated delta-rule update over a recurrent state S_t.

Core update (Eq. 8). EDA inserts an erase operator between diagonal decay and the delta write, with the erase address e_t derived from an online objective L_erase = ½||S̃_tᵀ e_t||², so e_t is the direction whose current readout is actively pushed toward zero:

S_t = (I − β_t k_t k_tᵀ)(I − γ_t e_t e_tᵀ) D_t S_{t−1} + β_t k_t v_tᵀ

Three-level memory management. (1) diagonal decay D_t for per-channel retention; (2) independent erasure γ_t e_t at a learned, address-specific direction; (3) write-coupled correction β_t k_t, the standard delta fix. Order is essential — erase-then-delta; reversing it would let cleanup suppress freshly written content.

Safe bounded gate (Eq. 15). A log-space gate g_t = ℓ + (−ℓ) exp(−A/|ℓ| ⊙ Δ_t) keeps each decay factor in (exp(ℓ), 1] with ℓ=−5, avoiding subnormal/zero values and enabling stable fp16 / TensorCore-friendly chunk tensors — unlike the unbounded-below Mamba2/GDN gate.

Parallelism & efficiency. Each EDA step expands into two sub-steps (odd = erase+decay, even = delta), doubling the effective sequence length so EDA reuses standard DPLR chunk-wise kernels (to ship in FlashQLA). Only e_t and γ_t are extra inputs; chunk-forward is memory-bound so wall-clock overhead is modest, smaller still in autoregressive decoding where state I/O dominates.

Strengths

  • Clean, minimal modification that fully preserves the delta-rule corrective write.
  • Breadth: two model families, pretraining + midtraining, downstream + 4k–128k long context, plus mechanistic probes.
  • Honest positioning vs GDN-2 (address-level vs gate-level decoupling) and vs transition-enrichment work.
  • Bounded safe gate keeps decay fp16-friendly and enables half-precision chunk tensors.
  • Counterfactual + geometry analysis (mean cos ≈ 0.105) is more convincing than typical ablations.

Limitations

  • Sub-1-point Avg gains at MoE scale, within plausible run-to-run variance.
  • Independent erase hurts raw write-key recall (KDA wins) — conditional cleanup, not a fidelity win.
  • Headline 69% allocation is a scalar-mean heuristic, not an exact state-energy decomposition.
  • Long-context edge attenuates at 64k–128k.
  • No released checkpoints/data; 400B-token MoE pretraining is out of reach for most labs.
  • Mechanism analysis confined to a single layer/head; erase events are not causally linked to benchmark wins.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the independent-erase degree of freedom compound with richer transitions (DeltaProduct, RWKV-7)? Orthogonality is claimed but not tested.
  2. Can the gate-allocation analysis become a rigorous state-energy decomposition instead of a scalar-mean heuristic?
  3. Why does the long-context advantage shrink beyond 32k — does the erase address lose selectivity under extrapolation?
  4. The erase direction is learned end-to-end; can it be made content-addressed (an explicit "invalidate this key" signal) for fact correction?
  5. What is the real serving-stack wall-clock and memory overhead at 1M+ context, beyond chunk-forward FLOPs?
  6. Is the reduced raw write-key recall a problem for retrieval-heavy tasks, and can read-time gating recover it?
