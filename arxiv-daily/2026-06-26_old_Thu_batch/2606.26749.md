# Structure Before Collapse: Transient semantic geometry in next-token prediction

> **一句话总结：** 本文通过三个可控的合成"玩具语言"实验揭示：在严格 one-hot 标签监督下训练的 Transformer，会在训练早期自发形成反映输入潜在语义相似性的几何结构，但这种语义几何只是过渡阶段——随着容量与训练时间增加，表征最终坍缩为 Neural Collapse 预测的对称 ETF（等角紧致标架）几何，且作者用球形 Bag-of-Words 模型从理论上证明了一步梯度下降偏向语义结构、收敛终态偏向 ETF 结构的机制。

> **TL;DR:** The paper resolves a tension between Neural Collapse theory (which predicts one-hot training erases input semantics into a symmetric ETF geometry) and the empirical fact that language models learn rich latent structure. Using three controlled synthetic "toy languages" where inputs share latent semantic factors but map to distinct one-hot labels, the authors train transformer decoders and show that semantic geometry (clustering by shared latent attributes, with no explicit supervision) emerges early in training as a transient phase, before eventually collapsing to the label-driven ETF state as capacity/time grow. They characterize this phase transition via Gram-matrix/RSA analysis and provide a theoretical "Spherical Bag-of-Words" model proving that one-step gradient descent biases representations toward the semantic RSM while the global minimizer converges to the ETF geometry.

## 核心贡献
- 首次在严格 one-hot 标签监督下，实证刻画了语义几何的"瞬时性"：表征在训练早期按潜在语义类别聚类，后期坍缩为 Neural Collapse 预测的对称 ETF 几何。
- 设计了三个从简单到语言学化的合成玩具语言（颜色-形状分类、带 Zipfian 类别语义的线性模板语言、带类别语义的层次文法），其中输入含已知潜在语义因子却映射到互斥 one-hot 标签，从而把"语义 vs 标签"两套假设几何可控地分离开来。
- 提出基于 RSA（表征相似性分析）的诊断框架：用 Pearson 相关比较经验 Gram 矩阵 Emp-RSM 与语义假设 Sem-RSM、坍缩假设 ETF-RSM，并辅以 NC1/NC2 指标和跨层分析。
- 给出理论代理模型——Spherical Bag-of-Words：证明一步梯度使 Emp-RSM 偏向 Sem-RSM（定理1），而全局极小解具有 ETF-RSM 结构（定理2），并指出球面归一化是终态坍缩为 ETF 的关键。
- 把经典 Neural Collapse 理论与语言模型训练现实桥接起来，解释了"one-hot 监督下语义结构为何仍能涌现"这一矛盾。

## Key Contributions
- Empirically documents the *transience* of semantic geometry under strictly one-hot next-token supervision: representations first organize by shared latent semantic attributes, then drift toward the symmetric ETF geometry predicted by Neural Collapse as capacity and training time grow.
- Constructs three controlled synthetic languages of increasing linguistic realism (color-shape category cross; linear template language with Zipfian category semantics; hierarchical grammar with category semantics mediated by c-command) where inputs share known latent semantic factors but map to mutually exclusive one-hot labels.
- Provides an RSA/Gram-matrix diagnostic pipeline comparing an empirical RSM (Emp-RSM) against a semantic hypothesis RSM (Sem-RSM) and a label-driven ETF/SELI hypothesis RSM (ETF-RSM), supplemented by NC1/NC2 collapse metrics, cross-layer analyses, and different-label diagnostics that isolate graded semantic structure from mere same-label collapse.
- Introduces a theoretical proxy — the Spherical Bag-of-Words model — proving Theorem 1 (one gradient step biases the Emp-RSM toward the Sem-RSM) and Theorem 2 (the global minimizer attains ETF-RSM structure), and identifies spherical normalization as the mechanism forcing the late-stage collapse to ETF.
- Bridges NC theory with the realities of LM training, reframing the "one-hot supervision erases semantics" puzzle as a *temporal* phase-transition rather than a contradiction.

## Methodology
The authors study a controlled question: when semantic geometry is recovered from data, how does it evolve under strictly one-hot supervision where Neural Collapse predicts terminal ETF symmetry?

**Synthetic languages (three settings):**
- *Experiment 1 — Color-Shape categories:* Each input is a triplet `[nuisance_id, color_variant, shape_variant]` (V=818, n=800 samples). Two latent factors — Color {RED, BLUE} × Shape {CIRCLE, SQUARE, TRIANGLE, STAR} — yield K=8 one-hot labels (e.g., RED_CIRCLE). Crucially, fine-grained variants (DARK_RED/LIGHT_RED/MEDIUM_RED) hide the coarse factors, and nuisance IDs prevent memorization. Each sample shares one semantic attribute (e.g., RED) with half the other samples while having a distinct one-hot label.
- *Experiment 2 — Linear template language with Zipfian semantics:* A more language-like setting with Zipf-distributed category frequencies.
- *Experiment 3 — Hierarchical grammar:* A probabilistic context-free grammar interleaved with a Dyck (bracket) language, where the predicted token's class is determined by the c-commanding node, making semantics tree-mediated rather than positional.

**Training:** Standard transformer decoders swept across depths and embedding dimensions (e.g., 8 layers, d=64 as the largest); single prediction per example to guarantee strictly one-hot supervision; embedding geometry tracked across epochs.

**Dependent variables (RSA):** They construct three Representational Similarity Matrices over n samples — Sem-RSM (similarity = number of shared latent attributes), ETF-RSM (1 if same label else 0; SELI generalization for imbalanced cases), and Emp-RSM (the Gram matrix of last-layer context embeddings). The core metrics are Pearson correlations ρ(Emp-RSM, Sem-RSM) and ρ(Emp-RSM, ETF-RSM) over training. Complementary diagnostics include NC1/NC2, attribute-clustering adapted from NC, and cross-layer geometry (label-driven geometry strongest near the readout; semantic structure persists in earlier layers).

**Theoretical proxy:** A Spherical continuous Bag-of-Words model (matrices W∈ℝ^{K×d}, H∈ℝ^{d×V}, KL loss between one-hot labels and softmax of column-normalized W·N(HX)) trained on the Experiment 1 language. The Gram matrix G_emp = XᵀHᵀHX is analyzed against G_sem = BᵀB ⊗ 1_m 1_mᵀ and the ETF-RSM G_ETF = (I_K − (1/K)1_K1_Kᵀ) ⊗ 1_m 1_mᵀ. Theorem 1 shows the first GD step biases G_emp toward G_sem (given balanced init, m>1 contexts/label, ϵ ≪ 1/√K); Theorem 2 shows GD's global minimizer attains ETF-RSM structure — but only with spherical normalization (vanilla BoW instead converges to a semantic geometry).

## Results & Highlights
- **Semantic geometry emerges early, collapses late.** In Experiment 1 (Figure 1), Emp-RSM at epoch 30 shows clear block-diagonal structure matching Sem-RSM (ρ peaks); by epoch 10k it converges toward the binary same-label/different-label ETF-RSM pattern.
- **Phase transition tracks capacity.** The Sem-RSM correlation rises early then declines, while ETF-RSM correlation rises later — most pronounced for larger/deeper models. Narrower-width models (d=8, 16) retain higher semantic alignment longer (the bottleneck acts as a weak regularizer toward low-rank semantic organization).
- **Generalizes across language complexity.** The same early-semantic / late-collapse transient appears in the Zipfian-template (Exp 2) and hierarchical-grammar (Exp 3) settings; in Exp 3, contexts licensed by the same c-commanding semantic category form category-level blocks that are faintly present even at the terminal phase for narrow models.
- **Semantic signal is graded, not just same-label clustering.** Different-label diagnostics confirm that pairs sharing a latent factor are more similar than pairs sharing none, even after removing same-label pairs.
- **Theory reproduces the transient.** The Spherical BoW model exhibits the same qualitative transient and explains it via two theorems; crucially, spherical normalization is what drives terminal ETF convergence (its absence yields pure semantic geometry), casting the model as a middle-ground between vanilla BoW (input-semantic only) and the Unconstrained Features Model (output-label only).

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty (high):** The framing — semantic geometry as a *transient* phase reconciling NC theory with LM realities, under *strictly one-hot* supervision — is genuinely fresh and well-motivated. Most prior NC work assumes the UFM and ignores input structure; this directly challenges that with controlled evidence and a tractable theoretical model.
- **Rigor (good, with caveats):** The RSA pipeline is carefully designed, with sensible complementary diagnostics (NC1/NC2, attribute-clustering, cross-layer, different-label controls) that go beyond eyeballing heatmaps. The two theorems are clearly stated and tied to the empirical transient. However, Theorem 1's assumptions (balanced init, weak ϵ-correlation, ϵ ≪ 1/√K) are restrictive and the analysis is "preliminary" by the authors' own admission.
- **Reproducibility (mixed):** Toy languages, training sweep, and RSA metrics are described in enough detail to reimplement; appendix references (A.1–A.8, B.2–B.4) suggest full hyperparameters exist. No mention of code release in the visible text. The synthetic data is the main asset for reproducibility.
- **Significance (moderate-to-high for theory, lower for practical LMs):** Conceptually valuable for the NC and mechanistic-interpretability communities, but the gap to real LMs is large and acknowledged.
- **Red flags / honest caveats:**
  - *Ecological validity:* The authors explicitly flag that all experiments use **multiple-epoch** training, which is *not* how modern LMs are trained (one epoch over fresh data). The central real-world relevance claim ("long-context training is overwhelmingly one-hot") is therefore only loosely connected to the experimental regime — a significant gap.
  - *Toy scale:* Synthetically generated factors with perfectly known latent structure make Sem-RSM trivially constructable; whether real linguistic "semantics" factorizes this cleanly is unaddressed (the authors hedge on the semantics/syntax distinction).
  - *Overclaiming risk is low* — the authors are careful to call the theory "preliminary" and frame results as toy-setting. But the title's "Structure Before Collapse" could over-imply generality to real LMs that the one-epoch caveat undercuts.
  - *Mechanism for transience:* The Spherical BoW captures the qualitative transient but is a strong simplification (no attention, no depth); the paper acknowledges the transient "does not seem to require self-attention or depth," yet doesn't test whether the *transformer* transient shares the same mechanism.

## Strengths
- Clean, well-motivated theoretical tension (NC vs. observed LM semantics) turned into a precise, answerable question.
- Three progressively language-like synthetic settings give genuine evidence of generality rather than a single contrived example.
- Methodologically rich diagnostics that disentangle "same-label collapse" from "graded semantic structure" — a common confound in NC work.
- The Spherical BoW model gives an interpretable mechanistic story (input-structure-driven early bias vs. normalization-driven terminal collapse) with matching theorems.
- Intellectual honesty: limitations (multi-epoch regime, fresh-data transfer, toy semantics) are stated openly in the conclusion.

## Limitations
- **One-epoch / fresh-data gap:** Experiments rely on multi-epoch training; the paper itself lists "how do results transfer to the one-epoch fresh data regime?" as open, undercutting direct applicability to real LM pretraining.
- **Synthetic-only data:** Latent factors are hand-designed and perfectly separable; real linguistic structure is messier and not cleanly factorizable into Sem-RSM.
- **Scale:** Small transformers on small vocabularies; no demonstration that the transient persists at realistic scale or that the *timing* of the phase transition is predictable.
- **Theory is preliminary:** Restrictive assumptions; the BoW proxy omits the very architectural components (attention, depth) that define transformers.
- **No downstream-task evaluation:** Whether the transient semantic phase is functionally important for the model (e.g., for generalization or in-context learning) is not tested.

## Relevance
本文对研究"语言模型表征几何与训练动力学"以及"Neural Collapse 理论在大模型中的适用边界"高度相关，其"语义几何是训练早期的瞬时阶段、最终坍缩为标签驱动几何"的结论对理解 LM 表征演化、设计早停/探针时机、以及解释 one-hot 监督下语义涌现具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Does the early-semantic / late-collapse transient persist in a *single-epoch, fresh-data* regime at realistic scale, or is it an artifact of multi-epoch memorization?
- What determines the *timing* and sharpness of the phase transition as a function of width, depth, dataset size, and learning rate — can it be predicted a priori?
- Is the transient semantic phase functionally important (e.g., does early-stopping in it improve OOD generalization, probing accuracy, or in-context learning), or is it a mere byproduct?
- Can the Spherical BoW mechanism (input-bias vs. normalization-driven collapse) be extended to attention-based models, or do self-attention and depth introduce qualitatively different dynamics?
- How do the findings interact with *soft* label distributions — is there a continuous interpolation between the semantic-bridge geometry (Zhao & Thrampoulidis 2025) and the transient one-hot geometry as label sparsity increases?
- Do real linguistic "semantics" factorize cleanly enough to construct a Sem-RSM, or does the block structure observed here depend on the artificial separability of the toy factors?
