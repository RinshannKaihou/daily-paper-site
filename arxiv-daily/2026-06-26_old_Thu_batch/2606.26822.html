Quantization in Federated Learning: Methods, Challenges and Future Directions

Farwa Ikram, Dipanita Thakur, Antonella Guzzo, Giancarlo Fortino · DIMES, University of Calabria · 2026-06-25
federated_learning quantization systematic_review communication_efficiency edge_intelligence
Key Finding
First PRISMA-based survey of quantization in Federated Learning, unifying four algorithmic axes with six FL-system dimensions into one taxonomy.
Headline Metric
90% ↓
Communication-bandwidth reduction with well-calibrated quantization, reported at <2% accuracy loss (aggregated, not per-source).
Why This Matters
A practical selection guide and taxonomy for deploying quantized FL on heterogeneous, non-IID, bandwidth-constrained edge and IoT platforms.
Critical Edge
“Headline numbers (90% bandwidth, 10×–50× compression, <2% loss) are stated without per-source attribution or confidence intervals” — a real rigor gap for a survey.

Methodology

A Systematic Literature Review (SLR) executed under the PRISMA reporting protocol. Four research questions (RQ1: performance/efficiency impact; RQ2: challenges; RQ3: gaps across algorithmic/statistical/system axes; RQ4: emerging trends) anchor the search.

Six databases were queried over 2019–2026 with explicit search strings, then filtered through peer-reviewed, English-language, quantization-as-primary-focus inclusion criteria. Retrieval → eligible counts: IEEE Xplore 647→41, arXiv 18→6, ScienceDirect 1593→2, ACM DL 1135→2, Wiley 454→1, Others 78→8 — yielding roughly sixty screened-eligible studies (the final deduplicated count is not crisply reported).

Qualitative synthesis is organized through the proposed taxonomy, producing per-family comparison tables (DRQ, clipping/calibration, QAT, QA-FedAvg) plus dedicated challenges, gaps, and future-directions tables. As a survey, there are no new experiments or datasets; "results" are synthesized claims about the reviewed literature.

Strengths vs Limitations

Strengths
  • First dedicated, FL-specific systematic review of quantization — a genuine gap.
  • Clean taxonomy decoupling algorithmic axes from FL-system dimensions, applied consistently.
  • Strong SLR transparency: databases, search strings, year range, screening stages, RQs.
  • Integrates siloed concerns: convergence, secure aggregation, DP, hardware/energy.
  • Actionable future-directions agenda, each gap mapped to a concrete opportunity.
Limitations
  • Survey with no original experiments — claims aggregated without controlled re-evaluation.
  • Final deduplicated study count and inter-rater reliability not reported.
  • Headline numbers unattributed and not conditioned on bit-width / architecture / non-IID.
  • No code or open extraction artifacts, despite the paper's reproducibility emphasis.
  • Reference-hygiene red flags: placeholder DOI, 2018 ACM copyright, 2026 vs "early 2025" coverage.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. What is the exact finally-included study count after deduplication, and the inter-rater agreement during selection?
  2. How do the headline claims decompose by PTQ vs QAT, bit-width, model architecture, and degree of non-IIDness?
  3. Can the taxonomy be operationalized into a standardized benchmark suite (LEAF / FedML / TFF) with fixed datasets and metrics?
  4. How does quantization noise interact non-linearly with DP noise and secure-aggregation masking under non-IID asynchronous FL?
  5. Are adaptive/personalized bit-width policies provably Pareto-optimal on the cost / error / energy / privacy frontier?
  6. Do the speculative directions (quantum-inspired, neuromorphic) have any existing empirical FL evidence?
