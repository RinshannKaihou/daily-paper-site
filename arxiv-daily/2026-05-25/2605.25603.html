Detecting Unfaithful Chain-of-Thought via Circuit-Guided Internal-External Discrepancy

Detects unfaithful CoT by comparing internal circuit graphs with external reasoning graphs via Fused Gromov-Wasserstein distance.

Xu Shen, Zhen Tan, Song Wang, Pingjun Hong, Rui Miao, Xin Wang, Tianlong Chen · 2026-05-25
Key Metric
+5.4–11.5 Acc
SOTA on all 4 FaithCoT-Bench datasets with 48–69% circuit-tracing cost reduction vs. CRV.
Why This Matters
Bridges mechanistic interpretability with CoT analysis to audit whether models truly “think what they say” — directly valuable for AI safety.
Critical Edge
Requires white-box access and matched transcoder models — not applicable to proprietary or API-only models.

Methodology

1

Token-Selected Circuit Construction. For each reasoning sentence, informative tokens are selected via entropy-based filtering and counterfactual necessity scoring, reducing traced tokens by 62–69%. Compact sentence-level circuits are built using transcoder-based attribution graphs.

2

Internal-External Graph Construction. Internal representations: sentence-level circuits encoded with a 2-layer GIN. External representations: hidden states from layer 15 projected via an MLP adaptor. Both organized into trace-level sentence graphs with sequential-order and semantic-similarity edges.

3

FGW-based Scoring. Fused Gromov-Wasserstein distance between internal and external graphs serves as the unfaithfulness score. Margin-based training separates faithful from unfaithful samples; thresholding yields binary prediction at inference.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • First to bridge circuit tracing with CoT faithfulness
  • Major efficiency gains over CRV
  • Consistent SOTA across all benchmarks
  • Thorough ablations per component
  • Interpretable discrepancy decomposition

Limitations

  • White-box + transcoder requirement
  • Single backbone tested (LLaMA-3.1-8B)
  • Weak math-reasoning transfer (AQuA)
  • Binary labeling may be too coarse
  • Transcoder fidelity not analyzed

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does CIE-Scorer perform on larger models (70B+, 405B) or non-LLaMA architectures?
  2. Could FGW distance be replaced or augmented with graph edit distance or graph kernel methods?
  3. Is the detected discrepancy causal — does intervening on high-discrepancy traces produce more faithful outputs?
  4. Can the framework extend to multi-turn reasoning or agentic settings with tool use?
  5. How robust is the approach to adversarial CoT designed to appear faithful to both analyses?
