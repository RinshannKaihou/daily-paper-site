> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了过参数化ReLU网络中，由于参数不可辨识性（排列对称性和神经元分裂），即使函数已被完全识别，参数空间中的认知不确定性仍将持续存在，且后验在分裂流形上服从Dirichlet分布。

> **TL;DR:** This paper shows that in overparametrized one-hidden-layer ReLU networks, epistemic uncertainty persists in parameter space even when the underlying function is fully identified, due to non-identifiability from permutation symmetries and continuous neuron-splitting manifolds. The posterior on splitting coefficients follows a Dirichlet distribution, and variance-based (not information-theoretic) decompositions are needed to capture this uncertainty.

## 核心贡献

- 证明过参数化网络中存在两类不可辨识性导致的认知不确定性：离散的排列对称性和连续的神经元分裂（splitting）
- 推导出分裂系数的后验服从对称Dirichlet分布，给出了精确的后验矩表达式
- 证明基于方差的认知不确定性分解（而非信息论分解）可以捕获不可辨识性导致的不确定性
- 证明单链MCMC采样器在温和条件下几乎必然被限制在单个排列腔（permutation chamber）内
- 在持续学习和收敛诊断中展示了不可辨识性不确定性的实际影响

## Key Contributions

- Characterizes two sources of non-identifiability uncertainty in overparametrized ReLU networks: discrete permutation symmetry and continuous neuron splitting
- Derives the exact posterior on splitting manifolds: splitting coefficients follow a symmetric Dirichlet distribution with concentration parameter α = (p+1)/2, independent across true neuron groups
- Shows that variance-based epistemic uncertainty measures (law of total variance) capture non-identifiability uncertainty, while information-theoretic measures (mutual information) are invariant to it
- Proves that single-chain MCMC samplers with permutation-equivariant, Lipschitz updates almost surely remain confined to one permutation chamber (Corollary 3)
- Demonstrates practical implications: non-identifiability distorts EWC importance estimates in continual learning and inflates parameter-space convergence diagnostics

## Methodology

The paper focuses on one-hidden-layer ReLU networks of the form $f_w(x) = w_2^\top \phi(W_1 x)$ with L2-regularized loss. The analysis proceeds in three stages:

1. **Correctly specified model (M = M\*):** Uses results from Bona-Pellissier et al. (2023) to show the posterior contracts to a uniform mixture of Dirac measures over all permutations of the true weights. Derives exact posterior covariance structure showing nonzero weight-space uncertainty despite zero function-space uncertainty.

2. **Overparametrized model (M > M\*):** Introduces assignment maps ς : [M] → [M\*] partitioning model neurons into groups. Shows weights are determined by splitting coefficients c_m satisfying simplex constraints. The induced manifold posterior yields Dirichlet-distributed splitting coefficients (Lemma 4). Derives first/second moments and asymptotics as M → ∞ (Theorem 1).

3. **Empirical validation:** Uses NUTS and SGLD sampling on synthetic data with M\* = 5, varying n ∈ {2^6, ..., 2^14} and M ∈ {5, 10, 20, 40}. Validates posterior moment predictions, permutation chamber confinement, and Dirichlet splitting distributions.

## Results & Highlights

- In correctly specified models, posterior weight covariance remains nonzero even as n → ∞ due to permutation symmetry, while function-space variance vanishes (Figure 3)
- Single-chain MCMC rarely switches permutation chambers: mean minimum margin decreases with n and M, switch rates stay below 1 expected switch per chain (Figure 4)
- Empirical splitting coefficient distributions closely match theoretical Beta marginals of the Dirichlet across all (n, M) combinations (Figure 5)
- Posterior first and second moments of neuron projections match theoretical predictions μ_{k,α} and 1/k across group sizes (Figure 6)
- Correcting EWC importance for non-identifiability improves Task-B RMSE by ~0.125 in continual learning (Figure 8)
- Parameter-space convergence metrics remain flat across active learning rounds while function-space metrics decay properly (Figure 9)

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (4/5):** The observation that non-identifiability persists in overparametrized Bayesian networks is known in statistics, but its explicit characterization for ReLU networks with Dirichlet posteriors on splitting manifolds is new. The connection between variance-based vs. information-theoretic uncertainty decompositions in this context is cleanly articulated.

**Rigor (5/5):** Theoretical results are precisely stated with explicit assumptions, complete proofs in the appendix, and careful treatment of the tube-induced manifold posterior. The distinction between permutation chambers and splitting manifolds is geometrically precise.

**Reproducibility (4/5):** Synthetic experiments use standard setups (one-hidden-layer ReLU, NUTS/SGLD in BlackJAX). No code is referenced, but the experimental design is sufficiently detailed. Limited to p = 5 dimensional inputs.

**Significance (3/5):** The results are important conceptually but limited in practical scope to shallow ReLU networks. The paper acknowledges that deeper networks introduce additional non-identifiabilities not covered. The practical consequences section (continual learning, convergence diagnostics) is more demonstrative than comprehensive.

**Red flags:** The scope is restricted to one-hidden-layer ReLU networks without biases. Extension to deeper architectures is claimed to be "likely" similar but not proven. The practical implications rely on synthetic experiments only.

## Strengths

- Precise mathematical characterization of non-identifiability posteriors with closed-form Dirichlet distributions
- Clean distinction between permutation and splitting non-identifiability with geometric intuition
- Theoretical prediction of MCMC confinement to single permutation chambers, empirically validated
- Honest discussion of scope limitations and when non-identifiability matters vs. doesn't

## Limitations

- Restricted to single-hidden-layer ReLU networks; deeper architectures have additional non-identifiabilities unaddressed
- The Dirichlet splitting law depends on the Gaussian prior / L2 regularization; other priors may behave differently
- No real-world experiments; all validation is on synthetic data with p = 5
- The paper shows that function-space predictions are unaffected by non-identifiability uncertainty, which somewhat limits practical urgency

## 相关性

本文对理解贝叶斯神经网络的认知不确定性有重要的理论意义。对于关注BNN后验结构、不确定性量化或过参数化模型统计性质的研究者来说，这篇论文提供了ReLU网络不可辨识性的精确刻画。其关于MCMC采样器被限制在单个排列腔的结论对实践中的后验采样策略有直接指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How do the splitting manifold results extend to deep networks with multiple hidden layers, where compositional non-identifiabilities interact with permutation and splitting symmetries?
2. Can the Dirichlet posterior characterization be leveraged to design improved sampling strategies that explicitly account for non-identifiability structure?
3. What happens with non-Gaussian priors (e.g., sparsity-inducing priors) — does the splitting coefficient distribution change fundamentally?
4. How does the interplay between non-identifiability uncertainty and data-dependent epistemic uncertainty evolve in the moderate-n regime, before the posterior concentrates on the manifold?
5. Could the theoretical confinement of MCMC to permutation chambers be exploited to improve convergence diagnostics or ensemble diversity metrics?
