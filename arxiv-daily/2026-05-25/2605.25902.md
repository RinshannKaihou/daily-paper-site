> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 CDD（对比解码差异法），仅需灰盒 logit 访问即可从微调模型中逐字恢复植入内容，比白盒 ADL 方法快约 170 倍且效果更优，并首次发现了从数据生成器到模型权重再到提取输出的端到端指纹链。

> **TL;DR:** Contrastive Decoding Diffing (CDD) recovers verbatim implanted facts from finetuned LLMs using only output-level logit access (no weights, no layer selection, no per-model tuning), outperforming the white-box ADL baseline across four architectures (1B–32B) while running ~170x faster. It also demonstrates the first end-to-end fingerprinting chain from data generator artifacts through model weights to recovered output.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 CDD 方法，仅需灰盒（logit 级别）访问即可从窄微调语言模型中逐字恢复植入的事实内容，无需权重访问、层选择或逐模型调参。
- 在 SDF 基准上覆盖 1B–32B 四种模型架构（Gemma-3-1B、LLaMA-3.2-1B、Qwen3-1.7B、Qwen3-32B），20 个 organism×model 对中 16/20 达到平均分 ≥ 4（满分 5），一致优于需要完全白盒访问的 ADL 基线（均分 2.42）。
- 首次展示端到端数据管线指纹链：数据生成器的 mode collapse（虚构人物 Dr. Elena Rodriguez）→ 微调写入权重 → CDD 从权重中提取出该人物，实现了从生成器伪影到模型权重再到恢复输出的完整追踪。
- 在真实领域微调场景（因果推理数据集 CLadder、COPA、Corr2Cause、TRAM）验证了方法的泛化性，所有单数据集非 CoT 变体均实现近完美恢复。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces CDD, a grey-box model diffing method combining three components: (1) bypassing chat templates to expose raw finetuning priors ("The Simulator"), (2) seeding with maximally vague prefills requiring zero domain knowledge ("The Void"), and (3) contrastive decoding that amplifies logit-space differences between finetuned and base models at each step.
- Achieves near-universal verbatim recovery on the SDF benchmark across 1B–32B parameter models using a single default configuration (β=1.0, α=0.1) with no per-organism or per-model tuning.
- Demonstrates generator fingerprinting: recovers unintended artifacts (fictional persona "Dr. Elena Rodriguez" introduced via mode collapse in Claude Sonnet) from model weights, constituting the first complete end-to-end chain from data generator artifact → weight imprinting → artifact extraction.
- Validates generalization to real-domain finetuning on causal reasoning datasets, achieving near-perfect recovery across all single-dataset non-CoT variants and correctly identifying all four datasets in the mixed-dataset setting.

## Methodology

CDD composes three mechanisms:

1. **The Simulator:** Bypasses the instruction-following chat template to operate the model in raw next-token-prediction mode, allowing the finetuning prior to surface unconditionally without conversational anchoring.

2. **The Void:** Uses maximally vague prefills (`""`, `"The"`, `"In"`, `"A"`, `"It"`) that place the model in a high base-model entropy state where the finetuning signal dominates. This requires zero a priori knowledge of the finetuning domain—no probe corpus, no topic-relevant prompts needed. 10 stochastic samples are drawn per prefill (50 total generations per pair).

3. **Contrastive Decoding:** At each decoding step, computes:
   `log p_CDD(x_t | x_{<t}) ∝ (1 + β) log p_ft(x_t | x_{<t}) − β log p_base(x_t | x_{<t})`
   This amplifies the finetuning signal while an adaptive plausibility constraint (`p_ft ≥ α · max_w p_ft(w)`) prevents degenerate tokens.

The 50 generations are synthesized by a single LLM agent run (Qwen3.5-122B) into one natural-language description, then independently scored 3 times against ground-truth key facts using the SDF_verbatim rubric (1–5 scale; score 4 requires 2–4 specific facts, score 5 requires ≥5).

## Results & Highlights

- **SDF Benchmark:** CDD achieves mean score ≥ 4 on 16/20 organism×model pairs; ADL achieves mean 2.42 across all 20. CDD outperforms ADL on 19/20 pairs (the exception is a mutual failure on CAKE_BAKE × Qwen3-32B).
- **Scale independence:** Consistent performance from 1B to 32B parameters across all five semantically unrelated domains (culinary, pharmaceutical, software engineering, political history, materials science).
- **Efficiency:** ~170x faster end-to-end than ADL for 1B–2B models (17s vs. 2906s mean), ~41x faster at 32B. Storage advantage of 690–3060x (sub-MB vs. 117–490 MB).
- **Generator fingerprinting:** Dr. Elena Rodriguez appears in 3–40 of 50 CDD generations per pair across organisms, proportional to her 24.6–56.0% recurrence in training documents (ratio 0.31–1.02x for four organisms). Additional artifacts: "47%" statistic appearing across unrelated domains, formulaic "unprecedented"/"groundbreaking" phrasing.
- **Real-domain validation:** On causal reasoning datasets (CLadder, COPA, Corr2Cause, TRAM), CDD scores 5/5/5 across all single-dataset non-CoT variants; ADL scores 1–2. CDD also recovers verbatim prompt templates and synthetic entities from training data.
- **Robustness check:** Using GPT OSS 120B as alternative agent yields 20/20 pairs at ≥4, confirming the 16/20 figure is conservative.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The individual components—simulator mode, vague prompting, contrastive decoding—are not individually novel. The key insight is their composition: the paper convincingly shows that combining all three yields verbatim recovery that no single component achieves alone (β=0 ablation collapses performance). The generator fingerprinting finding is genuinely novel and practically significant.
- **Rigor:** Evaluation is thorough: 20 organism×model pairs, three independent grader scores per cell, alternative agent as robustness check, full hyperparameter sweep, and real-domain validation. The SDF_verbatim rubric is strict (domain vocabulary alone = score 2, not 4+).
- **Reproducibility:** Default hyperparameters work out of the box with no per-model tuning. Implementation uses standard HuggingFace models. However, the SDF benchmark itself requires specific LoRA adapters from prior work.
- **Significance:** High practical relevance for AI auditing and accountability. The finding that grey-box access outperforms white-box access challenges the assumption that more model access is always better. The fingerprinting chain has implications for dataset provenance as synthetic corpora become ubiquitous.
- **Red flags:** The four sub-threshold cells all cluster around Qwen3-32B, suggesting scale-dependent limitations. The evaluation relies on LLM-based grading (Qwen3.5-122B), which may have its own biases. The SDF benchmark uses synthetic training documents; while real-domain validation is included, a systematic study on naturally occurring corpora remains future work as the authors acknowledge.

## Strengths

- Elegant method design: three simple, composable ideas requiring zero domain knowledge and a single default configuration.
- Strictly less model access (grey-box logits) than the white-box baseline, yet consistently better performance.
- Dramatic efficiency gains (~170x faster, 690–3060x less storage).
- The generator fingerprinting discovery is a significant and unexpected finding with practical implications.
- Excellent experimental coverage: multiple architectures, scales, domains, ablations, and real-domain validation.
- Transparent reporting: three independent grader scores per cell, honest discussion of sub-threshold cases.

## Limitations

- Requires grey-box logit access to both the base and finetuned model; does not apply where only top-k probabilities or no logprobs are exposed.
- Performance degrades at 32B scale with default hyperparameters (though recoverable with β=2, α=0).
- The SDF benchmark uses LLM-generated synthetic documents; systematic evaluation on naturally occurring corpora is future work.
- Cannot distinguish between CoT and non-CoT variants in 3/5 real-domain cases.
- Relies on LLM-based grading which introduces potential judge bias; human evaluation would strengthen claims.
- The CAKE_BAKE organism shows an anomalous 98x over-representation of Elena Rodriguez in CDD outputs relative to corpus frequency, which is noted but not fully explained.

## 相关性

本文对 AI 安全与模型审计领域有直接价值。CDD 提供了一种低成本、高效率的方法来检测模型是否被植入了特定内容，对于模型透明度和问责制具有重要意义。生成器指纹链的发现对于关注合成训练数据来源溯源的研究者也极具参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can CDD be adapted to work with only top-k logprobs or output text (black-box setting), and what performance would result?
- How does CDD perform on models finetuned with full-parameter updates rather than LoRA, where the parameter space change is larger?
- Could the generator fingerprinting approach be used to attribute model provenance (i.e., identify which LLM generated the training data) in real-world deployed models?
- What defensive measures can prevent CDD-style extraction while preserving the benefits of finetuning?
- How does the method scale to models beyond 32B parameters, and does the α=0, β=2 correction remain optimal?
