> **一句话总结：** 本文系统评估了六种不确定性量化方法在激活预言机（activation oracle）上的校准性能，发现温度引导采样（bootstrap mode frequency）是最优的置信度估计方法。

> **TL;DR:** This paper benchmarks six uncertainty quantification methods for activation oracles—models trained to translate another LLM's hidden states into natural language. Temperature-bootstrap mode frequency at T≈1 is the best-calibrated method across two oracle models (Qwen3-8B and Qwen3.6-27B), while direct numeric self-report is anti-calibrated and MCMC power-sampling acceptance fails due to mode-peakedness of the steered posterior.

## 核心贡献

- 首次对激活预言机（activation oracle）进行不确定性量化（UQ）基准测试，涵盖六种方法、两个模型、四种校准/排序指标，每种配置6000个样本
- 发现温度引导采样（temperature bootstrap）在校准指标（ECE、Brier、NLL）上全面领先，是生产环境推荐方案
- 揭示了自由文本数值自我报告（free-form numeric self-report）在更大模型上是"反向校准"的（对错误答案更有信心），复现了probe-vs-verbalized置信度鸿沟
- 提出了三种面向steered-oracle的新方法：MCMC power-sampling acceptance、MCMC cross-chain agreement、steering-coefficient sensitivity
- 训练并发布了首个面向混合线性注意力+全注意力架构（Qwen3.6-27B）的激活预言机

## Key Contributions

- First UQ benchmark for activation oracles: 6 methods, 2 models (Qwen3-8B, Qwen3.6-27B), 4 calibration/ranking metrics, 6,000 samples per configuration per oracle
- Temperature-bootstrap mode frequency at T≈1 achieves the best ECE on both oracles (5.7% on 8B, 10.3% on 27B), with the optimal temperature tied to overall task accuracy
- Three novel UQ methods designed specifically for the steered-oracle setting, with negative results for MCMC acceptance (mode-peakedness prevents discrimination) and steering sensitivity (too coarse-grained)
- Replication of the probe-vs-verbalized confidence gap in the activation-oracle setting: free-form numeric self-report is anti-calibrated on the 27B oracle (mean confidence 98.9% on wrong vs. 97.6% on correct)
- First activation oracle for a hybrid Gated DeltaNet + Gated Attention architecture (Qwen3.6-27B), with patched trainer released

## Methodology

The paper evaluates on the **secret-word taboo task** from Karvonen et al. (2025): 20 LoRA adapters fine-tune a target model to keep a different word secret while acting as a conversational assistant. The activation oracle reads the target's residual stream at one layer and decodes the secret word.

**Six UQ methods tested:**

1. **Log-probability (M1):** Joint token probability of the answer word under greedy decoding. Established baseline adapted from Kadavath et al. (2022).
2. **Temperature bootstrap (M2):** Draw k=20 samples at temperature T, report modal answer with confidence = mode frequency. Short-answer analogue of self-consistency (Wang et al., 2022). T swept over {0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 1.0, 1.3, 1.5}.
3. **Direct numeric self-report (M3):** Two-turn greedy decode: answer first, then "how confident are you (0–100)?"
4. **MCMC power-sampling acceptance (M4):** Single block Metropolis-Hastings chain on the steered oracle. Confidence = empirical acceptance rate.
5. **MCMC power-sampling agreement (M5):** k=10 independent chains; mode-frequency readout across chains.
6. **Steering-coefficient sensitivity (M6):** Greedy decode at coefficients c ∈ {0.5, 0.75, 1.0, 1.25, 1.5}; confidence = stability of answer across the grid.

**Evaluation protocol:** 20 target words × 100 context prompts × 3 verbalizer prompts = 6,000 samples per method per model. Metrics: accuracy (exact match), ECE (10 bins), Brier score, NLL, AUROC.

## Results & Highlights

| Method | Qwen3-8B ECE | Qwen3-8B AUROC | Qwen3.6-27B ECE | Qwen3.6-27B AUROC |
|--------|-------------|----------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Bootstrap T=1.0 | **0.057** | 0.829 | 0.125 | 0.851 |
| Bootstrap T=1.5 | 0.083 | 0.824 | **0.103** | 0.812 |
| Log-prob | 0.255 | **0.840** | 0.131 | 0.835 |
| MCMC agreement | 0.263 | 0.803 | 0.390 | 0.858 |
| Steering sensitivity | 0.404 | 0.763 | 0.501 | 0.725 |
| MCMC accept | 0.544 | 0.563 | 0.740 | 0.528 |
| Direct self-report | 0.582 | 0.516 | 0.753 | 0.404 |

- Bootstrap T=1.0 is ECE-optimal on 8B (accuracy ≈ 0.41); T=1.5 on 27B (accuracy ≈ 0.22). The optimal T tracks task difficulty.
- Log-prob has the highest AUROC on 8B and serves as a fast triage signal at ~1/20th the cost of bootstrap.
- MCMC acceptance fails because the steered posterior is sharply mode-peaked, so acceptance saturates regardless of correctness.
- Direct self-report is anti-calibrated on 27B: the model is more confident in wrong answers (98.9%) than correct ones (97.6%).
- Post-hoc calibration (isotonic, Platt) closes most of the ECE gap between methods, making the choice between log-prob and bootstrap a cost/latency trade-off.
- A constrained label scoring pilot on 8B reaches AUROC 0.957, suggesting introspective UQ is reachable if one bypasses free-form text generation.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. This is the first systematic UQ study for activation oracles, a nascent but important interpretability tool. The three novel methods (MCMC acceptance, MCMC agreement, steering sensitivity) are well-motivated, and the negative results are informative even though they fail.

**Rigor:** Strong experimental design with 6,000 samples per configuration, five complementary metrics, bootstrap CIs, and post-hoc calibration ablations. The temperature-tuning recommendation is grounded in a clear mechanism (mean mode frequency matching empirical accuracy).

**Reproducibility:** Code and models are released. The task uses publicly available LoRA adapters from Karvonen et al. (2025). The 20-word vocabulary and evaluation protocol are fully specified.

**Significance:** Practically important. Activation oracles are being proposed for alignment auditing, deception detection, and hidden-objective elicitation—all high-stakes applications where confidence calibration is essential. The finding that direct self-report is anti-calibrated is a cautionary result for the field.

**Red flags:** Limited to two Qwen-family models and one task (secret-word taboo). Whether these findings transfer to open-ended interpretability tasks (e.g., "what is this model's goal?") is unknown. The constrained label scoring pilot (n=30) is too small to be conclusive. Exact-match scoring penalizes semantically close answers (e.g., "fire" for secret word "flame") equally with complete refusals.

## Strengths

- Comprehensive benchmark: 16 method-temperature configurations, two model sizes, five evaluation metrics
- Clear practical recommendations: bootstrap for production, log-prob for fast triage
- Honest treatment of negative results (MCMC methods fail with mechanistic explanation)
- Replication of the probe-vs-verbalized confidence gap in a new setting
- First oracle for hybrid attention architecture (Qwen3.6-27B), with released trainer patches

## Limitations

- Only tested on the secret-word taboo task (20 words, closed vocabulary); generalization to open-ended interpretability questions is untested
- Two Qwen-family models only; cross-architecture generality is claimed but not demonstrated
- Exact-match scoring does not distinguish semantically close answers from random errors
- The constrained label scoring pilot has only n=30 samples (AUROC SE ≈ 0.05)
- Bootstrap requires 20 forward passes per sample, which is expensive for production deployment at scale

## 相关性

本文与"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"研究兴趣高度相关。激活预言机是一种新兴的、基于隐藏状态的模型可解释性方法，本文系统评估了其输出置信度的校准问题，对理解模型内部表征的可靠性具有重要意义。研究结果直接服务于对齐审计、欺骗检测等下游应用场景的可靠性需求。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How do these UQ methods transfer to open-ended interpretability tasks (e.g., "What is this model planning?") where there is no closed vocabulary for answer extraction?
2. Can constrained label scoring (the AUROC 0.957 pilot) scale to full evaluation, and does it maintain calibration or only improve ranking?
3. Would embedding-based soft accuracy (e.g., BERTScore) change the relative ranking of UQ methods, given that 8B's topical-neighbor confabulations are semantically informative?
4. How does the optimal bootstrap temperature interact with the number of target concepts (beyond 20 words) or the complexity of the latent information being decoded?
5. Can the mechanistic explanation for MCMC failure (mode-peakedness) be tested by constructing a task where the steered posterior is genuinely multimodal?
