> **一句话总结：** 本文在μP参数化下建立了宽两层神经网络平均场极限的全局存在性、可辨识性、稀疏字典分解和总误差分解四条结构定理，用三元组 $(w^*, D^*_{\mathrm{orb}}, S^*)$ 统一刻画了架构-数据对的"学习单元"。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proves four structural theorems for feature learning in wide two-layer networks under the Maximal Update Parametrization (μP): (1) global existence and uniqueness of the mean-field limit with $O(N^{-1})$ Wasserstein rate, (2) identifiability modulo finite-rank realization symmetry, (3) sparse-dictionary decomposition of the long-time active measure, and (4) a total feature-learning-error decomposition into statistical, optimization, propagation-of-chaos, and sparse-residual components. The triple $(w^*, D^*_{\mathrm{orb}}, S^*)$ — maximal admissible weight, orbit identifiability depth, sparse-dictionary depth — forms the "natural learning cell" of the architecture–data pair $(\sigma, \rho)$.

## 核心贡献

- 证明了μP下噪声梯度下降的平均场极限全局存在且唯一，有限粒子近似的均匀时间平方Wasserstein速率为 $O(N^{-1})$
- 刻画了平均场极限的可辨识性：两个参数测度产生相同的 $L^2$ 网络函数当且仅当其活跃分量在有限秩实现对称群意义下等价
- 在Barron-Hermite目标条件下，长期极限测度的活跃支撑具有稀疏字典结构，原子数被显式系数阈值数上界控制
- 将总特征学习误差分解为统计误差、优化误差、混沌传播误差和稀疏残差四个分量，并用三元组 $(w^*, D^*_{\mathrm{orb}}, S^*)$ 统一四条定理

## Key Contributions

- **Theorem A (Global Existence):** Proves global existence and uniqueness of the entropy-regularized mean-field Langevin equation under μP, identifying the maximal admissible weight $w^*$ as the reciprocal moment-growth boundary. The finite-particle approximation achieves uniform-in-time squared-Wasserstein rate $O(N^{-1})$ via a synchronous-coupling proof without invoking independent empirical quantization estimates.
- **Theorem B (Identifiability):** Characterizes when two admissible parameter measures induce the same network function: exactly when their active components agree modulo the finite-rank realization symmetry $G^{\mathrm{fin}}_\sigma$. Separates orbit depth $D^*_{\mathrm{orb}}$ from moment-variety depth $D^*_{\mathrm{var}}$, showing they can differ on singular tensor strata.
- **Theorem C (Sparse-Dictionary Decomposition):** Under the Barron–Hermite target condition, the active support of the long-time limit measure admits a sparse-dictionary decomposition: supported on at most $S^* \leq S^{\mathrm{up}}(\sigma, \rho, f^*, \lambda)$ atoms modulo $G^{\mathrm{fin}}_\sigma$, where $S^{\mathrm{up}}$ is an explicit coefficient-threshold count.
- **Theorem D (Total Error Decomposition):** Decomposes total feature-learning error into statistical ($\sim S^*(d_{\mathrm{eff}}+2-D^*_{\mathrm{orb}})(\log n)^2/n$), optimization ($e^{-\alpha_\lambda T}$), propagation ($O(N^{-1})$), and sparse-residual ($\kappa(f^*, S^*, \lambda)$) components.
- **Architectural Identity:** The triple $(w^*, D^*_{\mathrm{orb}}, S^*)$ is the "natural learning cell" of $(\sigma, \rho)$, unifying all four theorems.

## Methodology

The paper works with wide two-layer networks $f_N(x) = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N a_i \sigma(\langle w_i, x \rangle + b_i)$ under μP scaling ($w_i(0) \sim \mathcal{N}(0, d^{-1}I)$, learning rates $\eta_w=1, \eta_b=1, \eta_a=N^{-1}$). The approach combines:

1. **Mean-field Langevin dynamics:** The empirical parameter measure $\mu_t^N$ converges to the solution of a Fokker–Planck PDE (the entropy-regularized Wasserstein gradient flow of $F_\lambda = R + \lambda \mathrm{KL}(\mu\|\pi)$).
2. **Weighted moment classes:** Introduces a novel framework of moment-sequence weights $w: \mathbb{N} \to (0,\infty)$ and associated classes $\mathcal{M}_w$, with the maximal admissible weight $w^*$ determined by the initialization's moment-growth boundary. Crucially, $w^*$ does not require monotonicity (needed for Gaussian initialization where $w^*(n) \asymp n^{-1/2}$).
3. **Finite-rank quotient geometry:** Identifiability is proven via compact-exhaustion on the quotient space $\mathbb{R}^{d+2}/G^{\mathrm{fin}}_\sigma$, using analytic separation (Stone–Weierstrass) and monotone-class arguments.
4. **Hermite/Barron dictionary:** The sparse support result uses a threshold rule on the Hermite coefficients of the target — retaining only modes above $c_\sigma \lambda$ — combined with stationarity analysis and reachability via Girsanov's theorem.
5. **Canonical comparison sequence:** The error decomposition follows a fixed telescoping sequence $f^* \leftarrow f_{\mu_\infty} \leftarrow f_{\mu_T} \leftarrow f_{\mu_T^N} \leftarrow f_{\hat{\mu}_T^N}$, with cross terms controlled by independence/centering and Young's inequality.

The proofs are self-contained (31 pages plus appendices), relying only on standard results from μP and mean-field Langevin theory.

## Results & Highlights

- **Sharp moment boundary:** The maximal admissible weight $w^*$ is sharp — Proposition 3.9 shows that any strengthening of the reciprocal moment boundary fails to propagate for cubic-growth activations with Gaussian initialization.
- **Strict depth separation:** For polynomial activations (e.g., $\sigma(z) = z^4$), the orbit depth $D^*_{\mathrm{orb}}$ can be strictly less than the moment-variety depth $D^*_{\mathrm{var}}$ on singular tensor strata (Lemma 4.8 gives $D^*_{\mathrm{orb}}=1$ vs $D^*_{\mathrm{var}}=2$ for rank-one fourth-order tensors).
- **Concrete rates for single-index targets:** For centered sigmoid activation with exponentially decaying Hermite coefficients, the total error is $O(N^{-1} + (\log n)^3/n)$ (Corollary 6.10).
- **Intrinsic budget calibration:** For target accuracy $\eta$, the required resources are $n \geq C_0 \eta^{-1}(\log \eta^{-1})^3$, $N \geq C_1 \eta^{-1}$, with no ambient-dimension-dependent covering term (Proposition 6.11).
- **Architecture–target table:** Section 7 computes the invariants $(w^*, D^*_{\mathrm{orb}}, S^*, \kappa)$ for six concrete cases (ReLU with linear/piecewise-linear targets, tanh analytic single-index, polynomial activations, multi-index ReLU targets).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** This paper provides the first unified theorem chain connecting μP mean-field existence, identifiability, sparse-dictionary structure, and total error decomposition. Prior work treated these questions separately. The weighted moment class framework and the architectural identity $(w^*, D^*_{\mathrm{orb}}, S^*)$ are new conceptual contributions.
- **Rigor: High.** The proofs are detailed and self-contained, with explicit constant tracking, calibrated Gronwall envelopes, and audit-checked appendices. The sharpness results (Propositions 3.8, 3.9) demonstrate that the bounds are not artifacts of proof technique.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate-to-High.** All definitions and hypotheses are clearly stated. The paper uses only stdlib mathematics — no computational experiments to reproduce. However, the proofs are dense (31+ pages) and verifying every step would require significant effort.
- **Significance: High for theory.** The results fill a genuine structural gap in the theory of feature learning under μP. The error decomposition with explicit dimension-dependent rates is immediately useful for bounding sample complexity of two-layer networks. The architectural identity provides a new organizing principle.
- **Red flags:** The paper is limited to two-layer networks. Extension to $L$-layer, convolutional, or transformer architectures is listed as open problems. The LSI assumption used for uniform propagation may not hold universally. The sparse-dictionary result requires the Barron–Hermite target condition (H4), excluding some targets from the sparse guarantee.

## Strengths

- Provides a complete, unified theorem chain where prior work had separate fragments.
- The weighted moment class framework elegantly handles the non-monotone boundary that arises for Gaussian initialization ($w^*(n) \asymp n^{-1/2}$).
- Identifiability is proven modulo the correct finite-rank symmetry group $G^{\mathrm{fin}}_\sigma$ rather than unrestricted permutations, with explicit structure given for ReLU, tanh, and polynomial activations.
- The total error decomposition yields concrete, intrinsic sample/width/time budgets (Proposition 6.11) without ambient-dimension dependence.
- Sharpness results demonstrate the bounds are tight, not artifacts of the proof method.
- The architectural identity $(w^*, D^*_{\mathrm{orb}}, S^*)$ is a clean organizing principle that ties all four theorems together.

## Limitations

- **Two-layer only.** Extension to deep networks requires layerwise quotient symmetries and moment boundaries that are not worked out here. The authors flag this as the most important open problem.
- **Entropy regularization required.** The noise term (and hence LSI) is essential for the uniform-in-time propagation result. Removing it or replacing it with SGD noise requires different techniques.
- **Barron–Hermite assumption.** The sparse-dictionary decomposition requires H4 (finite Barron norm with Hermite truncation). Targets with heavy-tailed Hermite expansions or non-representable components fall outside the sparse guarantee.
- **No computational experiments.** The paper is purely theoretical. Empirical validation of the predicted rates (e.g., the $O(N^{-1})$ propagation rate, the logarithmic $S^*(\lambda)$ for single-index targets) would strengthen the impact.
- **LSI as hypothesis.** The log-Sobolev inequality is stated as an assumption rather than proven for all $(\sigma, \rho)$ pairs, limiting the universality of the uniform-in-time bounds.
- **Single author.** The breadth and depth of the technical contributions is unusual for a single-author paper, which may raise scrutiny during peer review.

## Relevance

本文为μP参数化下宽两层网络的特征学习提供了完整的理论框架，将平均场存在性、可辨识性、稀疏结构和误差分解统一在"学习单元"三元组下。对研究无限宽度极限、平均场理论、神经网络的样本复杂度和稀疏表示的研究者具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does the architectural identity $(w^*, D^*_{\mathrm{orb}}, S^*)$ extend to $L$-layer networks? What replaces the single finite-rank quotient with layerwise quotients?
2. Can the LSI assumption be verified constructively for specific activation–data pairs beyond the log-concave reference case?
3. What are the concrete values of $S^*$ for practical target functions (e.g., trained teacher networks, natural data distributions)?
4. Can the $O(N^{-1})$ propagation rate be verified empirically, and does it hold for SGD noise instead of Langevin noise?
5. How does the sparse-dictionary depth $S^*$ relate to other complexity measures (Barron norm, variation norm, neural tangent kernel spectrum) in practice?
