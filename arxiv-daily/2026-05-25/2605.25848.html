Concept directions rotate substantially during assembly — GEM probes from settled “handoff layers” match or beat peak-layer probes in 68.5% of trials.

Geometric Evolution Maps: Extracting Stable Concept Probes from Transformer Residual Streams

James Henry 2026-05-25 23 models · 17 concepts · 391 pairs
Key Metric
68.5%GEM ≥ peak-layer probe precision
Why This Matters
Standard peak-layer probes extract unstable intermediate directions; GEM identifies settled representations for reliable mechanistic interpretation and steering.
Critical Edge
GQA models (most modern LLMs) show no consistent benefit — only 47.1% vs MHA's 78.3%. Handoff-to-final cosine is often ∼1.0.

Methodology

GEM operates on the difference-in-means direction at each residual-stream layer. The dominant concept direction at layer l is the L2-normalized centroid difference u^(l). Angular velocity ω(l) = 1 − u^(l) · u^(l−1) tracks directional change per layer. The handoff layer L_H is the first layer after the Concept Allocation Zone (CAZ) ends — defined as the last consecutive layer where ω(l) > ε = 0.05. The settled direction u^(L_H) becomes the GEM probe. Validation uses directional ablation: zeroing the component along u while preserving orthogonal components, then measuring retained separation percentage (lower = better probe).

Results & Highlights

Rotation is universal Mean entry-exit cosine 0.233 across 391 pairs. 93.9% below 0.5; 23.8% near-orthogonal.
GEM vs peak-layer 268/391 (68.5%) at least as precise; 259 strictly outperform. Mean gain: +20.4pp.
Architecture split MHA 78.3% vs GQA 47.1% (Fisher p=0.022). Llama-3.1-8B & pythia-1.4b hit 17/17 (100%).
Depth-matched control 329/374 (88.0%) pairs show GEM beating depth-matched non-settling layer. Settling criterion adds +32.5pp.
Direction specificity Concept-direction ablation: 45.6% reduction vs random 0.24%. Median ratio: 377×.
Relay structure Deepest GEM node dominates 15/15 multi-node concepts. GQA shows positive synergy; MHA shows apparent anti-synergy.

Strengths

  • Identifies a real overlooked phenomenon — directional rotation during concept assembly is rigorously documented.
  • Exceptional scale: 23 architectures, 17 concept types, 391 pairs with multiple controls.
  • Open science: code, pre-extracted activations, contrastive pairs, analysis scripts all publicly available.
  • Depth-matched control cleanly isolates the settling criterion from depth confound.
  • Honest reporting of structured failures (gpt2, OPT-6.7b reversal) and GQA underperformance.
  • Architecture-dependent relay topology (GQA synergy vs MHA anti-synergy) is a novel finding.

Limitations

  • GQA applicability: No consistent advantage for grouped-query architectures that dominate modern LLMs.
  • No behavioral validation: All experiments measure separation suppression at the probe layer, not downstream output change.
  • ε not rigorously validated: Threshold set empirically on 2 models; no sensitivity analysis across a range of values.
  • Handoff cosines ∼1.0 to final layer: Median 1.000 — GEM probes are often nearly identical to final-layer directions.
  • Models >14B excluded. No MoE or frontier-scale validation.
  • Contrastive pairs built by LLM consensus — circularity may introduce systematic biases.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Why does the handoff mechanism fail for GQA architectures? Does grouped key-value sharing reduce the multi-head integration producing rotation?
  2. How do GEM probes compare to final-layer probes in downstream behavioral ablation (output change rather than separation suppression)?
  3. Does the ε=0.05 threshold generalize beyond the 23 tested architectures? What happens at ε=0.03 or ε=0.10?
  4. How does RLHF/instruction tuning shift handoff depths? Do aligned models settle concepts earlier or later?
  5. Can the relay topology findings (GQA synergy vs MHA anti-synergy) be exploited for architecture-specific intervention?
  6. What fraction of total concept signal lies outside detectable GEM nodes?
  7. How does multi-model consensus for contrastive pairs compare to human-annotated pairs in probe quality?
