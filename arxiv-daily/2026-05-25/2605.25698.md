> **一句话总结：** 本文通过质量感知的泛函缩放定律推导出 LLM 中训练中高质量数据的最优调度策略，提出 Drop-Stable-Rampup（DSR）方法——在质量切换时降低 batch size 以积累信号，再线性增加以抑制噪声——在 15B MoE 模型上比 WSD 提升 +1.70、比 Cosine-decay 提升 +2.98。

> **TL;DR:** Extends functional scaling laws to incorporate data quality, derives asymptotically optimal joint data-quality and batch-size schedules revealing two regimes (noise-limited vs. signal-limited), and proposes Drop-Stable-Rampup which improves 15B MoE midtraining by +1.70 over WSD and +2.98 over Cosine-decay across 14 benchmarks.

## 核心贡献

- 提出质量感知的泛函缩放定律（Quality-Aware Functional Scaling Law），将数据质量作为时间变量引入缩放定律，使得 excess risk 可分解为信号学习项和噪声累积积分。
- 推导出数据质量调度 p(t) 与 batch size 调度 b(t) 的联合优化问题的渐近闭式解，揭示了两种机制：噪声受限机制下高质量数据作为"信号放大器"，信号受限机制下作为"噪声抑制器"。
- 提出面向 LLM midtraining 的 Drop-Stable-Rampup（DSR）策略：在质量切换时降 batch → 稳定低 batch → 线性增加 batch，在 15B MoE 模型（108B tokens）上取得显著提升。

## Key Contributions

- Derives a quality-aware functional scaling law that extends Li et al. (2026) by introducing a time-varying effective noise variance σ²_eff(t) = p(t)σ²_good + (1−p(t))σ²_bad, coupling data-quality scheduling into loss dynamics.
- Solves the joint variational optimization over training horizon T, batch-size schedule b(t), and data-quality schedule p(t) in asymptotic closed form (Theorem 4.3), revealing a noise-limited regime (s > 1−1/β) and a signal-limited regime (s < 1−1/β).
- Identifies that existing curriculum-learning pipelines exploit only the noise-suppression role (late quality placement) while missing the signal-amplification role (lower batch size with high-quality data).
- Proposes Drop-Stable-Rampup (DSR): drop batch size at quality transition → hold stable → ramp up, validated on a 15B MoE (2.4B active) model with 108B tokens.

## Methodology

The paper builds on feature-space linear regression with Gaussian features and power-law spectral decay (eigenvalues λ_j ≍ j^{−β}, source condition |θ*_j|² ≍ j^{−1}λ_j^{s−1}). Training uses one-pass SGD with time-varying batch size b(t) and data-quality schedule p(t), modeled as an Itô SDE.

The quality-aware FSL decomposes excess risk into:

- **Signal-learning term:** t^{−s} (decreases with more steps)
- **Noise-accumulation integral:** η ∫₀ᵀ K(T−τ) σ²_eff(τ)/b(τ) dτ

The joint optimization is formulated as a constrained variational problem with total data budget D, high-quality fraction ρ, and minimum batch size B_min. The solution exhibits:

- **Noise-limited regime (s > s_crit):** Quality placement is degenerate (any allocation works), but batch size should drop by factor r = σ²_good/σ²_bad during high-quality phases to convert cleaner data into more signal.
- **Signal-limited regime (s < s_crit):** Optimal schedule has strict switching — low-quality flat → high-quality flat → high-quality ramp-up, using late placement to suppress terminal noise.

**DSR instantiation:** At pretraining-to-midtraining transition: (1) Drop batch size (4k→512), (2) Hold stable at 512 for ~25% of tokens (signal accumulation), (3) Linearly ramp up to 4096 (noise suppression). The stable-phase ratio is tuned via validation loss, yielding optimal at 24.9%.

## Results & Highlights

- **15B MoE (2.4B active), 108B tokens, 14 benchmarks:**
  - DSR overall: **51.46** vs. WSD: 49.76 (+1.70) vs. Cosine-decay: 48.48 (+2.98)
  - GSM8K: **69.82** (+4.23 over WSD, +7.85 over Cosine)
  - MATH: **39.66** (+2.80 over WSD, +5.68 over Cosine)
  - HumanEval+: **49.39** (+2.44 over WSD)
  - MultiPL-E: **47.53** (+2.66 over WSD)
- **Batch-drop ablation:** Drop 1k→8k outperforms Ramp 4k→8k on midtraining data (+1.36 overall) but underperforms on pretraining data (no quality transition).
- **Scaling-law verification:** Joint optimal strategy closely matches predicted power-law exponents (−0.81 vs. theory −0.80 noise-limited; −0.51 vs. theory −0.50 signal-limited).
- **Robustness:** Every stable-ratio configuration (0%–62%) in the DSR sweep exceeds both WSD and Cosine baselines.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The joint optimization of data quality and batch size is a genuinely new theoretical contribution. The dual-role insight (signal amplifier vs. noise suppressor) resolves the tension between curriculum learning and learning rate decay in a principled way.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Theorems are proven under standard kernel-regression assumptions; synthetic experiments validate the predicted scaling exponents. However, the linear-regression-with-Gaussian-features model is a significant simplification of real LLM training dynamics.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate.** Model architecture (15B MoE with 2.4B active), data budget (108B tokens), and benchmark suite are specified. Appendix F provides further details. However, the pretraining checkpoint (1.2T tokens) and midtraining data mix are proprietary (Meituan). The stable-phase ratio of 24.9% is empirically tuned rather than derived.
- **Significance: High.** Directly addresses a practical question (how to schedule high-quality data) with theoretical grounding and large-scale empirical validation. The DSR recipe is simple to implement.
- **Red flags:** The 1.1× wall-clock overhead from low-batch stable phase is non-trivial. Generalization beyond the single 15B MoE architecture and single midtraining setting is untested. The two-level quality model (good/bad) is a stylized abstraction.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical framework that yields actionable, interpretable insights (two regimes, dual role of quality data).
- DSR is simple to implement and requires no architectural changes — only schedule adjustments.
- Comprehensive large-scale validation (14 benchmarks, multiple ablations, scaling-law verification).
- Every DSR stable-ratio variant outperforms baselines, suggesting robustness to hyperparameter choice.
- Math reasoning benchmarks show the largest gains, aligning with the theory's prediction for signal-limited capabilities.

## Limitations

- **Theoretical model:** Feature-space linear regression with Gaussian features does not capture nonlinear feature learning, multi-layer composition, or attention-based dynamics of real LLMs.
- **Two-level quality abstraction:** Real corpora exhibit continuous quality spectra; the binary good/bad model is coarse.
- **Single scale/architecture:** Only tested on 15B MoE (2.4B active). Transfer to dense models, different scales, or different data budgets is unknown.
- **Wall-clock overhead:** 1.1× slowdown from low-batch stable phase; may not be acceptable in all settings.
- **Multi-task heterogeneity:** A single scalar quality dimension ignores that different domains (math, code, knowledge) may need different schedules. Per-domain joint optimization remains open.
- **Hyperparameter sensitivity:** The 24.9% stable ratio is empirically tuned; no principled way to set it at new scales.

## Relevance

本文与 LLM 训练效率和质量数据调度高度相关。质量感知缩放定律为"何时引入高质量数据"这一实际问题提供了理论指导，DSR 方法可直接应用于 midtraining 和 post-training 阶段。对研究训练动态、缩放定律和课程学习的方向有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does DSR perform on dense transformer architectures (e.g., 7B/13B/70B LLaMA-style models) and at pretraining scale (trillions of tokens)?
2. Can the two-level quality model be extended to a continuous quality spectrum, and does the bang-bang structure of the optimal schedule persist?
3. How should per-domain quality schedules {p_i(t)} be jointly optimized when different capability domains (math, code, reasoning) have different signal/noise characteristics?
4. Is there a principled way to infer the regime (noise-limited vs. signal-limited) from observable training metrics, enabling adaptive schedule selection?
5. How does DSR interact with other midtraining techniques such as reinforcement learning from verifiable rewards (RLVR) or supervised fine-tuning?
