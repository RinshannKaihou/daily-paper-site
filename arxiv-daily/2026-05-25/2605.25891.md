> **一句话总结：** LLM的隐藏状态能正确编码反常识因果方向（线性探针准确率~0.97），但模型的Yes/No输出却回归常识（准确率~0.5），这种"知道但说不出来"的现象被称为Causal Tongue-Tie。

> **TL;DR:** On anti-commonsense causal questions, a frozen linear probe recovers the evidence-supported answer from LLM hidden states with ~97% accuracy, while the spoken Yes/No output reverts to commonsense (~50%). This "Causal Tongue-Tie" gap (~+0.5) persists across 8 models from 0.5B to 72B parameters, challenging output-only benchmark evaluations of causal reasoning.

## 核心贡献

- 提出并实证验证了**Causal Tongue-Tie现象**：LLM隐藏状态编码了正确的因果方向，但Yes/No输出无法表达，二者之间存在约+0.5的准确率鸿沟
- 设计了2×2分析框架，将"因果推理错误"分解为两种可分离的失败模式：无内部信号 vs. 有信号但无法输出
- 在8个指令微调模型（Qwen2.5-0.5B到72B、Mistral-7B、DeepSeek-7B）上系统验证了该现象的普遍性
- 通过因果词汇控制、硬负例控制、多种回答接口测试等排除了表面词汇和统计关联的解释
- 指出仅基于输出的因果推理基准测试存在根本缺陷：答对不等于理解，答错不等于不理解

## Key Contributions

- Identifies and empirically validates **Causal Tongue-Tie**: LLM hidden states encode the evidence-aligned causal direction (~97% probe accuracy) while Yes/No outputs revert to commonsense priors (~50%), yielding a persistent ~+0.5 gap
- Proposes a 2×2 framework decomposing "wrong on a causal benchmark" into two separable failure modes: absent internal signal vs. unexpressed internal signal
- Demonstrates the phenomenon across 8 instruction-tuned models spanning 0.5B–72B parameters (Qwen2.5, Mistral-7B, DeepSeek-7B)
- Rules out surface-wording and statistical-association explanations through causal-word diagnostics, hard-negative controls, and multiple answer-interface tests
- Challenges the validity of output-only causal benchmarks: benchmark "correct" does not imply understanding, and "wrong" does not imply inability

## Methodology

- **Probe design:** A frozen ℓ₂-regularized logistic regression probe is fit once on commonsense (cs) CLadder items' hidden states and reused unchanged on all other subsets. A counterfactual subspace V<sub>cs</sub> is extracted via truncated SVD on paired hidden-state differences
- **Anti-commonsense items:** Uses CLadder anti-CS items where in-prompt evidence contradicts everyday commonsense (e.g., "smoking prevents lung cancer"), plus nonsense-entity controls and CounterBench items
- **Measurement protocol:** On the same forward pass, reads both the linear probe's prediction from an intermediate layer and the model's Yes/No token probabilities from the final lm_head, computing Δ = Acc<sub>probe</sub> − Acc<sub>out</sub>
- **Controls:** Sham subspace (nonsense-entity items), random-direction (Haar-random vectors), shuffled-label probes, counterfactual KL lesion, scalar-swap intervention, full-state activation patching
- **Answer-interface ladder:** Tests 5 answer formats (plain Yes/No, hedged Yes/No, free-form, bridge/arrow, A/B forced-choice) to localize the failure point

## Results & Highlights

- **Probe accuracy on anti-CS items:** ≥0.969 across all 8 models; output accuracy: 0.350–0.525; gap Δ ≈ +0.5 persists from 0.5B to 72B
- **Direction erasure:** Projecting out V<sub>cs</sub> collapses counterfactual KL by −96.3% on commonsense pairs; sham (−31.7%) and random (+0.7%) controls show this is specific
- **Activation patching:** Replacing the hidden state of wrong-answer items with matched commonsense donors lifts accuracy from 0 to 0.571
- **Answer-interface ladder:** A/B forced-choice (0.988) and structured bridge/arrow formats far outperform plain Yes/No (0.263), localizing the failure to the verbal interface
- **Cross-dataset transfer:** CLadder-trained probe transfers to CounterBench at 0.844–0.913 accuracy (layer 24), ruling out surface-marker overfitting
- **Causal-word diagnostic:** Explicit causal words are neither necessary nor sufficient; removing causal verbs keeps TP at 1.000, adding them against evidence keeps FP at 0.000

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The 2×2 decomposition and the systematic pairing of probe-vs-output on causal questions is genuinely novel. However, the high-level finding ("models know more than they say") echoes prior latent-knowledge work (Burns et al., 2023; Kadavath et al., 2022); the contribution is specializing this to the causal domain with strong controls.
- **Rigor:** Multiple validity checks (shuffled labels, logit lens, fixed layers, cross-dataset transfer) and honest reporting of where controls break down (cross-template probe transfer at 0.672; one brittle correlation-only family). Sample sizes for some controls are small (N=20–30).
- **Reproducibility:** All models are open-weight; code, prompts, and hidden-state artifacts are promised. No external dependencies beyond standard libraries.
- **Significance:** The paper delivers a clear methodological message — output-only benchmarks are insufficient for causal reasoning claims. This is important but the practical fix (structured interfaces) is somewhat incremental.
- **Red flags:** The probe direction is template-conditioned (cross-template transfer is fragile). The sufficiency result is partial (>50% but not all items). Claims are carefully scoped, which is a strength, but the gap between "encodes direction" and "understands causation" remains large.

## Strengths

- Extremely thorough control suite: sham, random, shuffled-label, logit-lens, cross-dataset, cross-template
- 2×2 framework provides a clear conceptual contribution that restructures how to think about causal evaluation
- Honest about scope limitations — explicitly disclaims claims about human-like understanding or complete causal circuits
- Replicated across 8 models and 2 model families (Qwen + Mistral/DeepSeek)
- Well-scoped claims with verifiable empirical predictions (Claims 1–3 mapped to specific sections)

## Limitations

- Probe direction is template-conditioned: cross-template transfer averages only 0.672, limiting generalizability
- Scalar V<sub>cs</sub> swap is a finite-power null — single-direction push does not flip the answer; only full-state injection works
- Coverage limited to instruction-tuned checkpoints; base models not tested (cannot separate interface failure from RLHF policy)
- Anti-CS items are synthetically constructed; unclear whether Causal Tongue-Tie manifests in naturally occurring causal disagreements
- Small per-condition sample sizes for several controls (N=20–30)
- Full multi-token-calibrated protocol across all models and surface forms is left to future work

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does Causal Tongue-Tie manifest in naturally occurring causal disagreements (e.g., scientific debates, policy arguments) rather than synthetic anti-commonsense items?
2. Would auditing base (pre-instruction-tuning) checkpoints reveal whether the interface failure is introduced by RLHF or is inherent to the architecture?
3. Can multi-layer or low-rank interventions (beyond single-direction scalar swaps) close the remaining sufficiency gap and flip >50% of wrong answers?
4. How does Causal Tongue-Tie interact with chain-of-thought reasoning — does CoT provide an alternative internal-to-external channel, or does it merely mask the same gap?
5. Is the probe's template-conditioning a fundamental limitation of linear readouts, or would nonlinear probes transfer better across paraphrases?
