> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"部署完备基准测试"框架，指出常规基准分数不足以支撑部署决策——当基准证据无法唯一确定部署动作时（混合纤维），报告分数本身会产生错误决策；通过完成曲线量化缺失信息代价，在Tox21和JARVIS等真实数据集上将错误决策率从20.3%降至0.128%。

> **TL;DR:** Standard benchmark scores often fail to determine deployment actions because candidates with identical benchmark evidence can require different actions (mixed fibers). This paper introduces deployment-complete benchmarking: a mathematical framework that audits whether benchmark evidence supports claimed actions, quantifies missing information via completion curves, and selects cost-optimal probes to resolve ambiguity. In JARVIS replays, the framework reduced false band-gap decisions from 20.3% to 0.128% and changed model choice in all 50 held-out splits.

## 核心贡献

- 提出**部署完备性（deployment completeness）**的数学定义：基准证据 E 对部署动作 D 完备，当且仅当 D 可分解为 D = ϕ ∘ E，即在每条证据纤维上动作恒定
- 引入**混合纤维（mixed fibers）**作为不完备性的精确数学见证：同一条纤维内存在需要不同部署动作的候选者
- 设计**完成曲线（completion curves）**量化消除歧义所需的信息代价，提出 certify-then-acquire 工作流
- 在受控响应空间、Tox21毒理学、JARVIS材料科学、Matbench等多个领域实证：发布分数普遍留下大量未解决的部署纤维（Tox21 97.9%混合纤维，Matbench/JARVIS中位可认证比例为0%）
- 证明即使**零基准误差**也不能保证部署完备性：当部署方向包含基准零空间分量时，残余项 R_c·g 依然存在

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes **deployment completeness** as a factorization condition D = ϕ ∘ E, turning the benchmark-to-deployment gap into an auditable property of a benchmark report
- Introduces **mixed fibers** as mathematical witnesses of missing deployment information — candidates indistinguishable by benchmark evidence but requiring different actions
- Designs **completion curves** Γ_E(b; D) that report the certified fraction as a function of additional evidence budget, and the ϵ-completion cost κ_ϵ as the budget needed to reach a target certified fraction
- Proves that no statistic computed solely from declared benchmark evidence can repair non-factorization (T = h(E) cannot make D factor through E if D does not already factor through E)
- Demonstrates **leaderboard inversions**: the best benchmark-MAE model certified only 6% of top-100 candidates while a worse-MAE model certified all 100, because the latter's response span covered the deployment direction

## Methodology

The framework operates on three mathematical objects:
1. **Evidence map** E: S → Z that partitions candidates into fibers
2. **Deployment action** D: S → A representing the operational decision (deploy/reject/threshold)
3. **Completion curve** measuring certified fraction vs. evidence budget

**Claim-completeness diagnostic (Proposition 1):** D is determined by benchmark evidence ⟺ D is constant on every fiber E⁻¹(z) ⟺ ∃ϕ: Z→A such that D = ϕ∘E ⟺ no two benchmark-indistinguishable worlds require different actions.

**Linear response-space specialization:** In a Hilbert space of response functions, benchmark probes define span B. The deployment residual g = ‖(I−P_B)k*‖ measures the component of the deployment direction invisible to the benchmark. The interval bound |Y*(c) − ŷ_c| ≤ δ_c + R_c·g shows that even with zero benchmark error (δ_c=0), residual ambiguity persists when g>0.

**Operational replays:** Calibration fibers determine purity from labeled data; held-out candidates are either certified (decided) or deferred to acquisition. The one-step greedy completion probe maximizes residual reduction ⟨r*, q⊥⟩² / (‖q⊥‖² · c_q).

**Domains audited:**
- Vision (scikit-learn digits, 8 classifiers, clean prediction → corruption robustness)
- Tox21 (7 nuclear-receptor assays → SR-p53 endpoint)
- Spin-defect screening (bare-host coherence → substrate viability)
- Matbench Discovery (formation energy → stability labels, 67 prediction files)
- JARVIS (formation energy → band-gap threshold, 15 models)

## Results & Highlights

- **Benchmark-channel conformal coverage (94.98%) transferred poorly** to unmeasured deployment channel (10.07%); response-rank intervals recovered 94.91% coverage
- **Zero benchmark error certified only 45.4%** of candidates at the largest residual size (g=1.0)
- **Tox21:** certify-then-acquire reduced false SR-p53 decisions from 1.19% to 0.027%; 97.9% of seven-assay fibers were mixed
- **JARVIS:** false band-gap decisions reduced from 20.3% to 0.128%; model choice changed in all 50 splits (benchmark-MAE model ≠ certification-selected model)
- **Leaderboard inversions** in 72% of equal-noise null runs: best-scoring model is the worst certifier
- **Completion probes selected deployment-relevant measurements:** locked blinded replay selected real MBJ band gap (72.0% decided, 0.54% false) over formation-energy control (15.1%, 3.69% false)
- Predicted residual reduction tracked completion gain (Pearson r=0.976); benchmark alignment did not (r=−0.029)
- Break-even acquisition costs: JARVIS 20.7%, Tox21 1.18% of one false decision

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The factorization framing (D = ϕ ∘ E) is elegant and, to our knowledge, has not been applied to benchmark evaluation before. It provides a clean mathematical criterion rather than heuristic gaps.
- **Rigor: High.** Formal propositions with proofs, controlled synthetic experiments isolating mechanisms, and operational replays with clear certification terminology (exact vs. calibration vs. predicted).
- **Reproducibility: High.** All datasets are public. The code artifacts (CSV outputs, cached results) are referenced by filename. The framework uses only standard mathematical tools.
- **Significance: Potentially high impact.** If adopted, this would change how benchmarks report results — from scores to evidence-action-completeness packages. However, adoption faces practical barriers: most benchmark organizers and consumers are not accustomed to this framing.
- **Red flags:** The framework's value is most apparent in high-stakes deployment (materials screening, toxicology) where the cost of wrong decisions justifies the overhead. For standard ML benchmarks focused on model comparison rather than deployment, the additional auditing may seem heavyweight. The JARVIS replay's break-even of 20.7% means the approach is cost-effective only when false decisions are expensive relative to measurement.

## Strengths

- Clean mathematical formulation that makes the benchmark-to-deployment gap precise and auditable
- Compelling demonstration that high benchmark scores and even zero error do not guarantee deployment readiness
- Practical certify-then-acquire workflow with demonstrated false-decision reductions across multiple domains
- Leaderboard inversion result is striking and should concern anyone using benchmarks for model procurement
- Completion curves provide a principled way to decide what to measure next, outperforming uncertainty, diversity, and benchmark-aligned acquisition

## Limitations

- The framework requires specifying a deployment action D upfront, which may be ambiguous or evolving in practice
- Continuous prediction audits require declaring finite resolutions (quantile bins, error windows), introducing a tuning choice
- The controlled response-space experiments assume linear or locally-linear structure; highly nonlinear deployment responses may require more complex analysis
- The operational replays use held-out splits of existing datasets, not true prospective deployment; real-world deployment involves distribution shift not captured here
- The overhead of computing mixed-fiber audits may be non-trivial for very large benchmark leaderboards

## Relevance

本文提出了一种全新的基准测试评估范式，将"分数是否支撑部署决策"作为核心审计对象。对于任何依赖基准分数做模型选择、采购或实际部署的场景（包括材料科学AI、药物筛选、安全评估），该框架提供了判断证据充分性的严格数学工具和实用工作流。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does the framework extend to multi-action deployment claims (e.g., ranking with ties, or continuous deployment policies)?
2. Can completion curves be estimated efficiently without access to deployment labels, using only benchmark evidence and structural assumptions?
3. How does the framework interact with distribution shift — when calibration fibers are not exchangeable with deployment candidates?
4. What are the computational costs of mixed-fiber audits for benchmarks with millions of submissions (e.g., LLM leaderboards)?
5. Could the factorization diagnostic be integrated into benchmark design as a constraint, ensuring new benchmarks are deployment-complete by construction for common claims?
