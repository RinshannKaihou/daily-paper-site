Credit Assignment with Resets in Language Model Reasoning

Ankur Samanta, Akshayaa Magesh, Ayush Jain, Youliang Yu, Daniel Jiang, Kavosh Asadi, Kaveh Hassani, Paul Sajda, Jalaj Bhandari, Yonathan Efroni — Meta AI · Columbia · Tel Aviv University
Key Finding
Resetting to the first error step and resampling continuations makes RL reasoning converge 2–3× faster — no external reward model needed.
SRPO self-localizes the first erroneous reasoning step, resets the chain there, and resamples. Gradient signal concentrates 2.5× on the tokens that matter.

Core Idea

  • Resets as credit assignment: resample from a mid-chain state to isolate which decision caused failure
  • Self-localization: the policy itself identifies its first error — no process reward model needed
  • Prefix masking: gradients concentrate on suffix tokens, not the shared correct prefix
  • CPI theory: credit-assignment oracle cuts sample complexity by 1/pπ²

Why It Matters

  • GRPO treats all tokens equally — diluting credit in long chains
  • Achieves OOD generalization: trained on NuminaMath-Olympiads, gains transfer to 9 other benchmarks
  • Training-free self-localization is simple to implement on top of existing GRPO infrastructure
  • Addresses a fundamental bottleneck in RLVR: sparse rewards over long reasoning traces

Thought-Level MDP

  • Each action = one semantically coherent reasoning step ("thought")
  • State = prompt + prefix of thoughts so far
  • Sparse reward: rh=0 for h<H, terminal rH ∈ {0,1}
  • Stop-pattern delimiters segment the chain into discrete steps

Theoretical Guarantees

  • CPI-RR: random resets sample uniformly from visitation — improvement signal diluted by pπ
  • CPI-CARO: oracle concentrates on "improvable" states where advantage ≥ τ
  • Sample complexity: Õ(|Y|H²R²max/τ²) vs. Õ(·/p²π) for random resets
  • Per-iteration gain: Ω(τ²pπ/HRmax) — a 1/pπ improvement factor

Ablation Highlights

  • Sampling 1×4 split (4 base + 4 suffix from 1 prefix) beats 2×4 and 1×8 at fixed 8-rollout budget
  • Self-localization concentrates resets in early-middle of chains, not uniformly
  • Shared-prefix rollouts: mean per-token |g| = 9.27×10⁻⁵ vs. 3.73×10⁻⁵ for base

OOD Generalization

  • Trained only on NuminaMath-Olympiads
  • Gains transfer to MATH, Minerva, GPQA, ARC, etc.
  • OLMo-3-7B also shows consistent improvement (best on 6/10)
  • LiveCodeBench v6 medium: SRPO reaches GRPO's final accuracy 2–3× sooner

Strengths

  • Elegant CPI grounding with clear complexity gains
  • Self-localization is training-free — no PRM or step labels
  • Strong OOD generalization across diverse reasoning domains
  • Thoughtful ablations on sampling and localization quality
  • Prefix-masking is simple yet effective

Limitations

  • Performance tied directly to localization accuracy
  • Only tested at 7B and 14B scale
  • Requires multi-step reasoning with clear thought boundaries
  • Only verifiable rewards; RLHF extension is open
  • Theory covers single CPI step, not full convergence
  • Compute overhead of extra forward pass not fully accounted for

Open Questions

  1. How does SRPO perform with a trained PRM for localization instead of self-localization? Would this close the gap to oracle-guided resets?
  2. Can the reset mechanism extend to multi-turn dialogue or agentic settings where decisions span multiple interaction rounds?
  3. What is the scaling behavior of self-localization accuracy at 70B+ (or conversely, 1–3B models)?
  4. How does SRPO interact with other GRPO improvements — length penalties, curriculum learning, dynamic group sizes?
  5. Could iterative resetting (multiple resets per trajectory) provide further precision, and what is the compute–accuracy tradeoff?
