Methodology

Training objective: L = Ldownstream + λ · Lreplay. A frozen copy of the pre-finetuning model generates sequences autoregressively at temperature 1. Token-level KL divergence against this reference prevents prediction drift — equivalent in expectation to NTP on the self-generated data. Sweeps cover model sizes 6M–46M, tokens-per-parameter ratios 10–17,000, and multiple learning rates. JAX on TPU v6e-8, batch 256, context 256, AdamW wd=0.02.

Key Results
  • Self-generated replay matches real pretraining data in preventing forgetting
  • Overtrained models (17K tokens/param) forget regardless of regularization
  • Larger models forget less given the same pretraining loss — more spare capacity
  • KL regularization reduces forgetting far more than increasing model size
  • LoRA does not inherently reduce forgetting vs. full finetuning
Critical Edge

Core claims validated only on 2M–46M parameter models. Whether capacity saturation manifests in 70B+ frontier models remains unknown. Most experiments involve single-task finetuning; multi-task continual learning may introduce loss-of-plasticity issues not addressed here.

Self-generated data at temperature 1 may not cover rare domains. The BOS-prompt trick for IT models works on Llama-3.2-1B but generalization is untested.

Strengths
  • Remarkably simple: no stored exemplars, no prompting — just sample from frozen model
  • Systematic dissection of capacity × optimization × replay
  • Reframes forgetting as a capacity problem, not purely algorithmic
  • Demystifies LoRA — does not inherently prevent forgetting
Limitations
  • Scale gap: core claims at ≤46M, only one 1B experiment
  • Single-task finetuning only — multi-task not explored
  • Uses proxies for capacity rather than direct measurement
  • Self-generated coverage gaps for rare domains not analyzed
