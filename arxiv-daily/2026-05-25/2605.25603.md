> **一句话总结：** CIE-Scorer 通过比较 LLM 内部电路追踪图与外部推理文本图的 Fused Gromov-Wasserstein 距离来检测 CoT 推理是否忠实反映了模型的实际决策过程，在四个 FaithCoT-Bench 数据集上取得了 SOTA 性能，同时将电路构建成本降低了约 50–65%。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes CIE-Scorer, which detects unfaithful Chain-of-Thought reasoning in LLMs by measuring the discrepancy between model-internal circuit graphs (traced via mechanistic interpretability) and externally displayed reasoning graphs using Fused Gromov-Wasserstein distance. Achieves SOTA on all four FaithCoT-Bench datasets with 48–69% reduction in circuit-tracing cost vs. prior circuit-based methods.

## 核心贡献

- 首次将模型内部电路计算过程与外部 CoT 推理文本结合，通过两者的结构性差异来检测实例级的 CoT 不忠实性。
- 提出基于熵和反事实必要性评分的 token 选择策略，显著压缩了电路追踪的规模，避免了全 CoT 电路构建的高昂成本。
- 设计了统一的句子级图构建方法，将内部电路嵌入和外部隐藏状态表示组织为可比较的推理图，用 Fused Gromov-Wasserstein 距离量化不一致性。

## Key Contributions

- First framework to explicitly combine model-internal computational evidence (circuit tracing) with externally displayed CoT reasoning traces for instance-level unfaithfulness detection.
- Entropy-and-counterfactual-based token selection strategy that reduces traced tokens by 62–69% and peak memory by 48–55% compared to full-circuit baselines (CRV).
- Unified sentence-level graph construction with Fused Gromov-Wasserstein (FGW) distance as the unfaithfulness score, jointly capturing feature-level and structure-level discrepancy.

## Methodology

CIE-Scorer operates in three stages:

1. **Token-Selected Circuit Construction (Sec 3.1):** For each reasoning sentence in a CoT trace, informative tokens are selected via a two-step filter: (a) entropy-based candidate selection removes low-uncertainty tokens using a quantile threshold, and (b) a counterfactual necessity score measures each candidate's importance by intervening on the token and measuring both sentence-level semantic change (via BERT encoder cosine distance) and downstream predictive change (via KL divergence). The combined importance score selects top-K non-redundant tokens for compact sentence-level circuit tracing using transcoder-based attribution graphs.

2. **Internal-External Graph Construction (Sec 3.2):** Internal representations are obtained by encoding sentence-level circuit graphs with a 2-layer GIN into d-dimensional embeddings. External representations come from averaging hidden states at a fixed intermediate layer (layer 15) per sentence, projected via an MLP adaptor. Both are organized into trace-level sentence graphs with edges encoding sequential order and semantic similarity.

3. **FGW-based Scoring:** The Fused Gromov-Wasserstein distance between internal and external graphs serves as the unfaithfulness score. A margin-based training objective minimizes discrepancy for faithful samples and pushes it above a threshold for unfaithful ones. At inference, thresholding the score yields the binary prediction.

## Results & Highlights

- **SOTA on all 4 FaithCoT-Bench datasets** (LogicQA, TruthfulQA, AQuA, HLE-Bio) using LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct backbone. Improves Acc by 5.4–11.5 points and F1 by 1.4–4.2 points over prior best.
- CRV (prior circuit-based method) runs OOM on LogicQA and HLE-Bio; CIE-Scorer handles all datasets successfully due to compressed circuit construction.
- Cross-domain transfer shows meaningful generalization (e.g., TruthfulQA→LogicQA F1 RTR of 0.905), though AQuA transfer remains weak.
- Runtime reductions of 67.6% on TruthfulQA and 46.7% on AQuA vs. CRV, while simultaneously improving F1.
- Ablations confirm both token selection signals (entropy + counterfactual) and graph structure (sequential + similarity edges) are necessary; removing internal GNN causes the largest degradation (~14.7% Acc drop).
- Post-hoc reasoning correlates more with feature-level discrepancy; spurious reasoning correlates more with structure-level discrepancy — providing interpretable signals for different unfaithfulness types.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. This is genuinely the first work to bridge mechanistic interpretability (circuit tracing) with CoT faithfulness detection at the instance level. The idea of comparing internal vs. external reasoning graphs via FGW distance is creative and well-motivated.

**Rigor:** Generally strong methodology with thorough ablations, cross-domain analysis, and efficiency measurements. However:
- The evaluation is limited to a single backbone model (LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct). Generalization to other model families (e.g., GPT, Gemini, Qwen) is not tested.
- The "ground truth" for faithfulness comes from human annotations on FaithCoT-Bench, which itself is a relatively new benchmark. The reliability of these labels and inter-annotator agreement are not discussed in this paper.
- The threshold-based binary classification at inference is somewhat simplistic; a calibrated probabilistic output would be more useful in practice.

**Reproducibility:** Good. Code is available, hyperparameters are detailed in the appendix, and the pipeline uses publicly available models and datasets. However, circuit tracing requires specific transcoder-matched models, which limits applicability.

**Significance:** Important for AI safety and interpretability. Detecting unfaithful CoT is practically valuable for auditing LLM reasoning. The efficiency gains over CRV make this more scalable.

**Red flags:**
- The margin-based training objective is simple and may not capture the full complexity of faithfulness patterns.
- Performance on AQuA (mathematical reasoning) is relatively lower, and cross-domain transfer to/from AQuA is weak, suggesting the approach struggles with mathematical reasoning traces.
- The method requires white-box access and a matched transcoder model, limiting applicability to proprietary or non-instrumented models.

## Strengths

- Novel integration of mechanistic interpretability with CoT faithfulness detection.
- Significant efficiency improvements over prior circuit-based approaches (CRV).
- Strong empirical results with consistent gains across all four benchmarks.
- Thorough ablation study validating each component's contribution.
- Interpretable discrepancy decomposition (feature vs. structure level) for different unfaithfulness types.

## Limitations

- Requires white-box model access and matched transcoder models — not applicable to API-only models.
- Evaluated only on LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct; scalability to larger models or other architectures is untested.
- Weak cross-domain transfer for mathematical reasoning (AQuA).
- Binary faithfulness labeling may be too coarse for nuanced analysis.
- Circuit tracing quality depends on the transcoder model's fidelity, which is not thoroughly analyzed.

## Relevance

这项工作对理解 LLM 推理过程的忠实性具有重要意义。通过结合机制可解释性和外部推理文本分析，为检测模型是否真正"按它说的想"提供了新范式，对 AI 安全审计和可信推理系统开发有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does CIE-Scorer perform on larger models (70B+, 405B) or non-LLaMA architectures where circuit tracing may produce qualitatively different attribution graphs?
- Could the FGW distance be replaced or augmented with other graph comparison metrics (e.g., graph edit distance, graph kernel methods) for potentially better sensitivity?
- Is the detected discrepancy causal — i.e., does intervening on high-discrepancy traces (e.g., re-prompting) actually produce more faithful CoT outputs?
- Can the framework be extended to multi-turn reasoning or agentic settings where CoT chains are interleaved with tool use?
- How robust is the approach to adversarial CoT — can a model deliberately generate traces that appear faithful to both external and internal analysis?
