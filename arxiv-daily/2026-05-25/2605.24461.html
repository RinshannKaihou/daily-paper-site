Phase 1

Provisioning

Empirical power-perf modeling of GB200 across 800–1,200 W. Formulates cluster throughput as constrained optimization over the power delivery hierarchy (MSB → SB → RPP → rack). Determines 960 W (80% TDP) as the Perf/Watt optimum — enabling 86K GPUs vs 74K at max power in the same envelope.

Phase 2

Deployment Validation

Validates rack telemetry by comparing PSU readings against oscilloscope & DCIM sensors. Discovers PSU systematically overestimates. Proposes P70 aggregation (70th percentile per minute) as scalable correction. Raises operational TDP from 960 W → 1,020 W based on validated measurements, capturing +2% throughput.

Phase 3

Active Operation — Dimmer

Scheduler-aware dynamic power capping with 7-second averaging windows aligned to circuit breaker curves. Priority-based throttling protects high-priority jobs. Fail-safe heartbeat reverts to safe TDP on controller failure. ~14% total throughput gain over naive max-power baseline.

Strengths
  • First end-to-end power management treatment at 150 MW scale with real production data
  • Multi-source validation: PSU, oscilloscope, DCIM — not simulation-only
  • Immediately actionable: P70 aggregation, 80% TDP rule, 7-second averaging
  • Clear three-phase framework applicable beyond this specific deployment
  • Dimmer's scheduler-aware design elegantly handles straggler problem in sync training
Limitations
  • Specific to GB200 architecture and power topology; generalization not extensively validated
  • "Always-on" power smoother wastes energy during non-training phases
  • Priority system assumes clear job hierarchy — may not hold in multi-tenant setups
  • Limited discussion of cooling dynamics and interaction with IT power
  • Does not address multi-datacenter or geo-distributed scenarios
  • Some key values redacted (*), reducing reproducibility
