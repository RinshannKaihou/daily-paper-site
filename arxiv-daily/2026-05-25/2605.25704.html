PowLU eliminates loss spikes in FP8 training of 124B-parameter LLMs by replacing SwiGLU's quadratic activation growth with sub-linear scaling.

PowLU: An Activation Function for Stable Pre-Training of LLMs

Authors: Jiang, Feng, Peng, Zhao, Liu, Chen, Zhang, Zhou Affiliation: Ling Team, Ant Group Date: 2026-05-25 activation-function llm-training numerical-stability fp8
Key Metric
124B params
Zero loss spikes in FP8 — vs. severe spikes with SwiGLU
Why This Matters
Makes FP8 training of 100B+ models stable — directly relevant to cost-efficient large-scale pretraining.
Critical Edge
Only validated on Ling MoE; no throughput cost analysis; BF16 vs FP8 comparison confound.

Methodology

For x > 0: PowLU(x) = x1 + m/√(x+1) · σ(x)  (default m=3)
For x ≤ 0: PowLU(x) = x² · σ(x)  (identical to SwiGLU)
  • As x → +∞, exponent converges to 1 → approximately linear growth, not quadratic
  • The √x factor slows convergence, preserving nonlinearity in the useful range
  • Proven: continuous, differentiable at x=0, monotonic for m∈(0,10), bounded sub-linear growth
  • Standard GLU gating: PowLU(x₁, x₂) = x₁ · f(x₂)
  • Evaluated on Ling MoE architecture: scaling law 26M–368M, large-scale 7.9B & 124B

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated design with strong theoretical proofs
  • Genuinely large-scale validation at 124B params — rare for activation function papers
  • Comprehensive stability analysis: P99 distributions, outlier channels, loss curves
  • Scaling law experiments confirm results extrapolate
  • Systematic ablations validate each design choice

Limitations

  • Only validated on Ling MoE; unknown on LLaMA, Qwen, or dense transformers
  • BF16 vs FP8 comparison confound in loss spike analysis
  • Mid-training switching from SwiGLU introduces distribution shift
  • Benchmark gains are marginal (1–3 pts); primary value is stability
  • No wall-clock or throughput comparison for the pow operator cost
  • Non-linearity proof in Appendix A.2 is incomplete

Questions for Further Exploration
