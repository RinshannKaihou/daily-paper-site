> **一句话总结：** 本文系统测试了7种GRPO奖励设计在知识图谱工具调用场景下的表现，发现策略均陷入4种失败模式——最优奖励下工具调用准确率从9.6%飙升后骤降至0%（peak-then-collapse），根本原因在于KG接口缺乏自然语言反馈信号，7B和14B模型均卡在~40% EM上限，瓶颈在于接口而非模型容量。

> **TL;DR:** The authors test 7 GRPO reward designs on Qwen2.5-7B for Freebase KG tool use and find all converge into 4 mechanistic failure modes. The strongest reward shows a peak-then-collapse pattern (9.6%→0% tool-grounded accuracy in 50 steps). The core issue is that KG interfaces lack the natural-language feedback that Python/web-search APIs provide. Self-distillation reaches 40% EM at 7B, matched within 0.25 pp by 14B — the ceiling is interface-bound, not capacity-bound.

## 核心贡献

- **发现"peak-then-collapse"现象**：在自验证检索奖励下，策略的工具调用正确率从3.8%升至9.6%后在50步内骤降至0%，跨4个随机种子复现
- **提出四通道信号理论框架**（L_sig, L_lang, L_comp, L_prior）解释KG工具接口为何比Python/搜索API更难训练
- **Oracle消融实验证明瓶颈不在关系选择**：注入金标准关系仅提升+0.20 pp EM，95.4%的错误归结于检索组合失败
- **单次自蒸馏方案达到40% EM**，且在7B-14B范围内容量无关（仅差0.25 pp）

## Key Contributions

- **(C1) Controlled reward-ladder comparison**: 7 reward designs on one model × task × algorithm × tool API, organized into a four-mode failure taxonomy
- **(C2) Signal-theoretic framework**: Four interface channels (silent failure, symbolic schema, opaque compositional state, absent pretraining prior) with oracle ablation localizing the EM gap downstream of relation selection
- **(C3) One-iteration self-distillation recipe** reaching EM=40.0% at 7B; capacity-invariant across 7B–14B (Δ0.25 pp); init-from-base alone reaches the same ceiling
- **(C4) Step-resolved peak-then-collapse**: A Tools/Q call-volume signature (3.0→1.0) novel relative to prior agentic-RL collapse diagnostics

## Methodology

- **Task**: Complex WebQuestions (CWQ) over Freebase, recast as a 4-verb tool-use interface (get_tail_relations, get_head_relations, get_tail_entities, get_head_entities)
- **Model**: Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct with LoRA (r=64, α=128, DoRA + rslora), initialized from 5K rule-based SFT trajectories
- **Algorithm**: GRPO with group size 8, KL coeff 0.05, lr 3e-7, batch 128, 500 steps; verl + vLLM on NVIDIA GH200
- **Reward ladder**: Six designs from outcome-only (R-binary) through stepwise, tool-verbs, KL-stabilized, self-verifiable retrieval, to self-distillation — each patching a specific predecessor failure
- **Evaluation**: Full CWQ test set (3,531 questions), greedy decoding, 7-category trajectory classifier, Wilson 95% CIs, McNemar paired tests

## Results & Highlights

| Variant | EM | CvT | Tools/Q |
|---------|-----|------|---------|
| SFT base | 0.1% | — | — |
| R-binary | 0.0% | 0.0% | 0–1 |
| R-stepwise | 32.5% | 0.03% | 1.0 |
| R-toolverbs·KL | 38.4% | 3.77% | 3.0 |
| R-selfV (peak@250) | 39.5% | 9.57% | 3.0 |
| R-selfV (collapsed@300) | 0.0% | — | 1.0 |
| self-distill | **40.0%** | 5.81% | 3.0 |
| R-toolverbs·KL-14B | 40.2% | 6.40% | 4.0 |

- R-selfV peak-then-collapse replicated across 4 seeds; collapse mechanism is a "quote-and-stop" attractor
- Pass@16 shows R-stepwise tools are decorative (−0.4 pp gap) while self-distill shows genuine +11.5 pp tool-lift
- Gold-relation oracle: only +0.20 pp EM improvement, ruling out relation selection as bottleneck
- 95.4% of retrieval-dependent errors are retrieval-composition failures, not answer-extraction failures

## Authors & Credibility

Tianda Sun and Dimitar Kazakov are from the Department of Computer Science, University of York. The work is a controlled, single-variable experimental study with rigorous methodology: full test-set evaluation at 50-step cadence, held-out dev-split checkpoint selection, 4-seed replication of the main finding, unit-tested reward implementations (18/18), and McNemar paired statistical tests. Code and checkpoints are released (anonymized for review).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The peak-then-collapse pattern and the four-channel framework are novel contributions. The finding that denser proxy rewards shift rather than eliminate failure modes is a useful negative result.
- **Rigor**: Extremely well-controlled — one model, one task, one algorithm, systematic reward ladder. Step-resolved evaluation at 50-step cadence is methodologically strong. Oracle ablation directly tests the framework's key claim.
- **Reproducibility**: High — code released, 4-seed replication reported, unit-tested rewards. The CWQ benchmark and Qwen2.5 models are publicly available.
- **Significance**: Important cautionary finding for the RLVR-for-tools community: portability of standard recipes depends on interface properties not captured by reward design alone. The 40% EM ceiling with richer concurrent pipelines reaching 67–80% via interface enrichments powerfully illustrates the interface-bound nature of the bottleneck.
- **Red flags**: The four-channel framework is descriptive/organizing rather than causally validated — only one channel (relation selection) is directly intervened upon. The specific channel gating the residual EM gap (L_comp vs L_sig) remains unresolved. The KG interface studied is deliberately minimal, so the results may not directly transfer to richer KG APIs.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally well-controlled experimental design with systematic reward ladder
- Step-resolved evaluation catches transient phenomena that checkpoint-level analysis would miss
- Honest reporting of negative results — all 7 reward designs fail in distinct ways
- Oracle ablation cleanly isolates the bottleneck locus
- Capacity-invariance finding (7B ≈ 14B) is a strong signal that interface, not scale, is the constraint

## Limitations

- Deliberately minimal 4-verb KG interface may not represent practical KG tool-use scenarios
- Four-channel framework is a descriptive hypothesis, not a causally validated decomposition (only 1 of 4 channels directly tested)
- 40% EM ceiling is far below concurrent richer pipelines (67–80%), limiting practical applicability of the minimal-interface approach
- Single task (CWQ) and single model family (Qwen2.5); Llama cross-family check is in appendix only
- The unresolved channel (L_comp vs L_sig) leaves the precise failure mechanism partially open

## Relevance

本文对研究RLVR工具学习的研究者高度相关，特别是关注RL训练中奖励设计和接口信号质量的工作。peak-then-collapse现象和四通道框架为理解和诊断工具调用策略训练失败提供了新的分析视角。如果你在研究LLM智能体的工具使用训练，这项工作的负面结果和信号理论分析值得关注。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can a non-gameable self-verifiable reward signal be designed for KG tool use, or is the fundamental issue that any proxy reward on an opaque interface is exploitable?
2. Which specific channel (L_comp entity-threading or L_sig silent failure) gates the residual EM gap between 40% and oracle performance?
3. Would enriching the KG interface along a single channel (e.g., adding natural-language error messages to address L_sig) produce disproportionate gains?
4. Does the peak-then-collapse pattern generalize to other structured-data interfaces (SQL, SPARQL) with similar opacity properties?
5. Can the four-channel framework predict training difficulty for novel tool APIs before running expensive RL experiments?
