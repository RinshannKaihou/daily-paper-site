> **一句话总结：** 提出UAV框架，通过轻量级适配器将异构模型的激活转换为共享解码器可读的软token，实现跨模型族的激活语义解释。

> **TL;DR:** UAV is a universal adapter-decoder framework that explains activations from heterogeneous LLMs using a shared decoder, achieving competitive performance with self-explanation methods while enabling cross-model verbalization across Llama, Gemma, Yi, and Qwen families.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Universal Activation Verbalizer (UAV)**，一个统一的适配器-解码器框架，能够用一个共享解码器解释来自不同模型族的异构 donor 模型的激活表示
- 设计了两阶段训练流程：Stage 1 进行激活到文本的对齐（输入重构），Stage 2 进行面向解释的指令微调
- 提出 **adapter-only transfer** 策略：冻结已有解码器端 LoRA，仅训练新的 donor 适配器即可迁移到新模型，大幅降低适配成本
- 系统实验表明 UAV 在分类、事实检索和摘要任务上与自解释基线（AO、LatentQA）竞争力相当，同时支持跨模型族和跨尺度的激活解释

## Key Contributions

- Introduces UAV, an adapter-decoder framework that uses a shared decoder to verbalize activations from heterogeneous donor models across different families and scales
- Designs a two-stage training pipeline: (1) activation-to-text alignment via input inversion, followed by (2) explanation-oriented instruction tuning with joint adapter and decoder-side LoRA training
- Proposes adapter-only transfer (AOT), which reuses a frozen decoder-side LoRA from one donor and trains only a new adapter for another donor, demonstrating strong transferability
- Shows UAV is competitive with self-explanation baselines (AO, LatentQA) on classification, fact retrieval, and gist summarization while enabling cross-model verbalization

## Methodology

**Architecture:** UAV consists of three components: (1) a frozen donor model that produces activations at a selected layer, (2) a trainable adapter that maps donor activations into soft tokens in the decoder's embedding space, and (3) a decoder with LoRA that generates natural-language explanations conditioned on the soft tokens.

**Adapter designs:** Two adapter architectures are explored:
- **MLP-style adapter:** Projects the activation vector through feed-forward layers to produce n soft tokens. Removing the bottleneck works best; excessive soft tokens (128) hurt performance.
- **Q-Former adapter:** Uses learnable query tokens with cross-attention over the activation context. The best variant (QF-LoRA: 2 layers, 8 context, LoRA rank 64/α128) achieves the lowest validation loss.

**Training (two stages):**
1. **Activation-to-text alignment:** Freeze decoder, train only the adapter on input inversion (reconstruct input prefix from activation). Layer-wise adapter training.
2. **Instruction-following alignment:** Jointly fine-tune adapter + decoder-side LoRA on explanation-oriented QA pairs covering classification, fact retrieval, and gist summarization.

**Training corpus:** 17-source mixture (469K Stage 1 samples, 950K Stage 2 QA pairs), including Wikipedia, peS2o, AG News, SST-2, LMSYS-Chat-1M, and emotion tweets.

**Adapter-only transfer:** Freeze the decoder and its LoRA from a source donor, train only a new adapter for the target donor. This tests whether explanation behavior transfers across donors.

## Results & Highlights

- **Self-explanation setting:** UAV (Full) achieves the best overall ROUGE-L and BERTScore among all methods for Qwen3-4B donor, and best overall BERTScore for Llama-3.1-8B donor
- **Cross-model verbalization:** A single Qwen3-4B decoder successfully verbalizes activations from Llama-3.1-8B, Gemma-3-4B, Gemma-3-12B, and Yi-1.5-34B with stable performance
- **Adapter-only transfer:** AOT remains close to full adaptation. For Qwen3-4B, AOT slightly outperforms full self-explanation on overall metrics. For Llama-8B, AOT still clearly outperforms training-free baselines
- **Decoder scaling:** Larger decoders generally improve performance (Qwen3-14B best), but the trend is not strictly monotonic (Qwen3-8B slightly underperforms Qwen3-4B on generation metrics)
- **Ablation — adapter vs. LoRA roles:** Decoder-side LoRA mainly improves task-level behavior (classification); the adapter is critical for recovering input-specific factual and semantic information from activations
- **Stage-1 warmup matters:** Removing activation-to-text alignment consistently hurts performance, especially under full-data training

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The cross-model activation verbalization idea is a meaningful extension beyond self-explanation methods (LatentQA, AO). The adapter-only transfer mechanism is practically useful. However, the core technical components (MLP/Q-Former adapters, two-stage training) are largely borrowed from existing work (BLIP-2, LLaVA-style pipelines).
- **Rigor:** Experiments cover multiple donors, decoders, tasks, and ablations. The evaluation uses both overlap and semantic metrics. However, ROUGE-L and BERTScore scores are generally low (0.25–0.39 range), making it hard to judge how faithfully the verbalizations capture activation content. Qualitative evaluation is limited.
- **Reproducibility:** Training details (learning rates, hyperparameters) are in appendices. Datasets are specified. The framework depends on specific model checkpoints. Code release is not mentioned.
- **Significance:** Cross-model activation verbalization is practically valuable for mechanistic interpretability research. If it reduces the cost of analyzing new models, the framework has real impact. However, the modest absolute scores and limited fact retrieval performance temper the claims.
- **Red flags:** (1) Negative BERTScore for training-free baselines on fact retrieval suggests the evaluation protocol may favor training-based methods by design. (2) The non-monotonic decoder scaling results (8B < 4B) are not well explained. (3) Only short-context inputs (≤64 tokens) are tested, limiting practical applicability.

## Strengths

- Cleanly formulates and solves the cross-model activation verbalization problem, which prior work cannot address
- Adapter-only transfer is a practical and efficient strategy for scaling to new donors
- Comprehensive experiments across 4 model families, 5 decoder sizes, and 3 task types
- Ablations clearly disentangle the roles of adapter (factual/semantic content) vs. LoRA (task behavior)

## Limitations

- Fact retrieval and gist summarization performance remains limited, especially for fine-grained attributes
- Only short-context inputs (≤64 tokens) are tested; long-context and document-level scenarios are unexplored
- Adapter-only transfer still requires training a donor- and layer-specific adapter, which can be costly for large-scale layer-wise analysis
- Absolute metric scores are modest, making it unclear how reliable the verbalizations are for practical interpretability use
- Evaluation relies heavily on automated metrics; human evaluation of verbalization faithfulness is absent

## Relevance

本文与模型可解释性研究高度相关。UAV 提供了一种通用的跨模型激活解释框架，对于需要分析不同 LLM 内部表示的研究者具有实用价值。适配器-解码器解耦的设计思路也启发了如何在解释工具中实现模型无关的可扩展性。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does UAV perform on activations from middle layers of very large models (e.g., 70B+), where representations may encode more abstract concepts?
- Can the adapter be shared or partially reused across layers of the same donor model, reducing the layer-wise training cost?
- How robust is UAV to distribution shift — e.g., can an adapter trained on Wikipedia-style text verbalize activations from code or multilingual inputs?
- What is the relationship between adapter quality and downstream verbalization faithfulness — can we predict verbalization quality from Stage-1 validation loss alone?
- Could multi-layer activation aggregation (feeding activations from multiple layers) improve fact retrieval and gist summarization performance?
