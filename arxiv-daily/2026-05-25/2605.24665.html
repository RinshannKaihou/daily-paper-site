An Energy-Efficient Approximate Posit Multiply-Divide Unit

Thotli R., Jonnalagadda A., Hulsurkar R., Uppugunduru A., Veeramachaneni S., Ahmed S., Gustafson J.
Key Finding
78.8% PDP ↓
A unified posit multiply-divide unit computes approximate division via LUT-assisted reciprocal estimation — cutting energy by 78.8% and area by 53.67% vs exact dividers, with just 0.0434% mean relative error on ⟨16,2⟩ posits.
PDP (divide) 0.212× vs PACoGen exact divider
Area 54% of PACoGen baseline
Division Error 0.043% MRED, 0.288 KB LUT
PDP (multiply) 0.160× exact multiplication retained
Process Node TSMC 90nm slow-normal corner
LUT Size 2⁸×9 bit only 0.288 KB

Core Insight

When a posit X = x(1+f) is 2's complemented (sign unchanged), the result approximates (2−f)/(2x) — close to the true reciprocal 1/(x(1+f)). A tiny LUT indexed by fraction MSBs corrects the residual error, eliminating Newton-Raphson iteration entirely.

Credibility

Strong
  • John Gustafson (ASU) is the inventor of the posit/unum number system
  • Industry-standard Cadence RTL Compiler, TSMC 90nm
  • Comprehensive comparison vs 5 baselines with fair block-level decomposition
  • No RTL source released — limits reproducibility

Architecture

01
Decoder A
Standard posit decode with early partial processing and parallel field extraction — 29.96% lower delay than prior art.
02
Decoder B
Extends Decoder A with a div control bit. Applies 2's complement reciprocal + subtracts LUT-indexed EC value.
03
Significand Multiplier
12-bit radix-8 Booth shared for mul/div. Novel 2's complement in partial products; truncated to 13 MSB bits.
04
Scale Adder
Combines regime + exponent from both decoders in 2's complement with carry-in for zero-fraction correction.
05
Encoder
Parallelized posit encoder with overflow/underflow handling — 35% lower delay than POSIT-VAR.

Strengths

+
Elegant exploitation of posit symmetry for reciprocal approximation
+
Massive energy savings with practical LUT sizes (0.288 KB)
+
Unified architecture eliminates separate divider — major area win
+
Reciprocal seed 14× more accurate than PACoGen's despite 8× smaller LUT
+
Component-level analysis enables fair comparison and reusable insights

Limitations

−
Only ⟨16,2⟩ posits — 32/64-bit results are speculative
−
No application-level accuracy eval (DNN, DSP, scientific)
−
Approximate division only — cannot replace exact in critical paths
−
Decoder B draws 1.21× PACoGen's decoder power — reciprocal isn't free
−
No comparison vs logarithmic approximate units (PLAUs) on HW metrics

Critical Flags

−
Multiplication mode shows 1.09× delay overhead vs Booth baseline — paper doesn't explain if inherent to unified design
−
Paper does not quantify in which applications the accuracy loss is acceptable
−
Accuracy metrics (MED, MRED, NMED) are thorough but disconnected from end-use

Relevance

This work directly benefits research on posit hardware for edge AI and low-power inference. The unified multiply-divide architecture significantly reduces energy and area for workloads tolerant of approximate division — signal processing, neural network inference, and embedded ML.
