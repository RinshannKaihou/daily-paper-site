On the Epistemic Uncertainty of Overparametrized Neural Networks

David Rügamer · LMU Munich & MCML

Key Finding

Epistemic uncertainty never vanishes in parameter space — even when the function is fully identified.

Overparametrized ReLU networks carry persistent uncertainty from permutation symmetries and continuous neuron-splitting manifolds. Splitting coefficients follow a symmetric Dirichlet distribution. Variance-based — not information-theoretic — decompositions are required to capture it.
Posterior on Splitting Manifolds
Dirichlet

Splitting coefficients follow a symmetric Dirichlet with concentration α = (p+1)/2, independent across true neuron groups.

MCMC Confinement
≈ 0

Single-chain MCMC almost surely stays trapped in one permutation chamber. Expected switches per chain: < 1.

EWC Correction Gain
Δ 0.125

Correcting Elastic Weight Consolidation for non-identifiability improves continual-learning Task-B RMSE by ~0.125.

Approach & Evidence
  • One-hidden-layer ReLU networks f(x) = w₂ᵀ φ(W₁x) with L₂-regularized loss
  • Correctly specified model: posterior contracts to a uniform mixture of Diracs over all weight permutations
  • Overparametrized model: assignment maps partition neurons into groups; splitting coefficients satisfy simplex constraints
  • Empirical validation with NUTS & SGLD on synthetic data (M* = 5, n ∈ {2⁶…2¹⁴}, M ∈ {5, 10, 20, 40})
  • Weight-space posterior covariance remains nonzero as n → ∞ while function-space variance vanishes
  • Empirical splitting distributions match theoretical Beta marginals across all (n, M) settings
  • Neuron projection moments match predictions μk,α and 1/k across group sizes
  • Parameter-space convergence diagnostics stay flat during active learning; function-space metrics decay properly

Key Contributions
  • Two non-identifiability sources characterized: discrete permutation symmetry + continuous neuron splitting
  • Exact posterior on splitting manifolds: symmetric Dirichlet, independent across neuron groups
  • Variance-based measures (law of total variance) capture non-identifiability; mutual information is invariant to it
  • MCMC with permutation-equivariant Lipschitz updates almost surely confined to one chamber (Corollary 3)
  • Non-identifiability distorts EWC importance in continual learning and inflates parameter-space diagnostics
Trade-offs

Strengths

  • Closed-form Dirichlet with exact posterior moments
  • Clean geometric distinction: permutation vs splitting
  • MCMC confinement predicted & validated
  • Honest scope discussion

Limitations

  • Single-hidden-layer ReLU only
  • Dirichlet law depends on Gaussian prior
  • No real-world experiments
  • Function-space predictions unaffected
Open Questions
  • How do splitting manifolds extend to deep networks where compositional non-identifiabilities interact with permutation and splitting symmetries?
  • Can the Dirichlet characterization inform improved sampling strategies that account for non-identifiability?
  • With non-Gaussian priors (e.g., sparsity-inducing), does the splitting distribution change fundamentally?
  • How does non-identifiability uncertainty interact with data-dependent epistemic uncertainty in the moderate-n regime?
  • Could MCMC confinement to permutation chambers be exploited for convergence diagnostics or ensemble diversity?
