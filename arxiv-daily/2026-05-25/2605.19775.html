Understanding Inference Scaling for LLMs: Bottlenecks, Trade-offs, and Performance Principles

Reasoning models shift the bottleneck from compute to memory. The Capacity Trap — where KV cache exhausts HBM and throughput collapses — is the defining constraint for LLM inference at scale. Dense and MoE architectures demand fundamentally different parallelism strategies.

Why This Matters

The inference cost of reasoning is uncharted territory

As the industry pivots to reasoning models (o1, DeepSeek-R1), KV cache saturation and the shift from compute-bound to memory-bound decode threatens to make naive scaling prohibitively expensive. Architecture-specific parallelism selection is now operationally critical.

Critical Edge

Single-node, single-engine, no quantization

All experiments on one 8-GPU node with vLLM only. Multi-node behavior (InfiniBand), alternative engines (TensorRT-LLM, SGLang), and quantization/KB offloading are untested. Synthetic batch sizes up to 10K may not reflect production traffic.

Methodology

Hardware & Setup

  • 8× NVIDIA H200 SXM5, 141 GB HBM3e, 4.8 TB/s BW, 900 GB/s NVLink, 2 TB DDR5
  • vLLM v1 with PagedAttention (block size 16), FCFS scheduling
  • Models: DeepSeek-R1-Distill (8B/14B/32B/70B), Llama-3.1-405B, DeepSeek-R1-671B
  • Dataset: Meta Natural Reasoning — 1.15M samples, 43% with >5K reasoning tokens
  • Metrics: TTFT, TPOT, throughput, E2E latency, HBM BW util, KV saturation

Extended reasoning chains cause KV cache to grow linearly with output length. The system spends almost all time in memory-bandwidth-bound decode, making HBM capacity — not FLOPS — the scarce resource.

Dense vs. MoE Divergence

Llama-3.1-405B (Dense, GQA)

TP = 8

Interconnect and memory-bandwidth bound. High-degree TP shards weights efficiently. PP fails due to massive per-layer KV state preventing pipelining.

PP=8 takes 7537s — 7.6× slower than TP=8

DeepSeek-R1-671B (MoE, MLA)

PP=4 + TP=2

Routing and synchronization latency bound. Low compute-to-communication ratio makes high-degree TP a bottleneck. MLA compresses KV cache, synergizing with PP.

TP=8 takes 2047s — 23% slower than PP=4+TP=2
