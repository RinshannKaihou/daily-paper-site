The Model Parking Tax: Quantifying the Hidden Energy Cost of Always-On GPU Model Deployment

The "parking tax" is a fixed CUDA-context DVFS jump of 26–66 W — not a function of VRAM. Keeping a 3 MB stub costs the same as a 79 GB model.
Sai Sathvik Vadari · 8bit.ai 335K production samples H100 · A100 · L40S
Industry Impact
462 GWh/yr
Base-case annual energy from CUDA context overhead alone — exceeds a small city's electricity consumption. Range: 92–1,745 GWh/yr.
Scheduler Savings
8–23%
Energy saved by breakeven-aware eviction vs. always-on across low, bursty, and diurnal traffic patterns.
VRAM Marginal Cost
<0.02 W/GB
Statistically negligible. TOST equivalence testing formally rejects β ≥ 0.1 W/GB on all three architectures.
Breakeven Interval
1–5 min
Energy-optimal eviction threshold. With ServerlessLLM (8 s load): under 48 seconds on H100. Independent of model size.

Methodology

1

Production Telemetry

18 days, 14 × H100, 335K idle-state DCGM samples. VRAM: 3 MB–79 GB. Regression R² = 0.001 — no detectable VRAM effect.

2

Controlled Dose-Response

Within-subject on H100/A100/L40S. Sequential VRAM allocation via torch.empty, 60 s settle, 20 min recording per level. SE < 0.25 W.

3

Real Model Validation

Qwen2.5-7B (fp16, ~14.9 GB) on all three GPUs. Idle power within 0.5 W of reference allocations.

4

Formal Equivalence

TOST with Δ = ±0.1 W/GB rejects |β| ≥ 0.1 on all GPUs (p < 0.05).

Critical Assessment

Novelty HIGH First cross-architecture idle power decomposition
Rigor HIGH Within-subject, TOST, sub-watt resolution, triangulated
Reproducibility HIGH Public code/data, clear protocols, standard hardware
Significance HIGH Directly actionable for fleet ops; 462 GWh/yr impact
Red flags MOD n=1 per arch in Phase 2; synthetic scheduler only

Key Caveat

Single device per architecture in controlled experiments. Does not evaluate MIG, MPS, or power capping (nvidia-smi -pl). Scheduler validated on synthetic traces only.

Strengths

  • Rigorous sub-watt methodology combining production + controlled experiments
  • Clean mathematical model separating DVFS and VRAM contributions
  • Cross-architecture consistency across 3 microarchitectures & 3 memory types
  • Immediately actionable: breakeven uses only measurable hardware params
  • Honest discussion of A100 negative slope anomaly
  • Industry-scale impact quantification beyond academic interest

Limitations

  • One GPU per architecture in Phase 2; inter-device variance bounded only on H100
  • Scheduler evaluated on synthetic traces, not production LLM serving logs
  • MIG, MPS, and power capping not evaluated
  • Cold-start power traces captured only on H100
  • Breakeven model assumes known arrival rates; real workloads are non-stationary
  • Multi-GPU tensor-parallel (>72 GB) not tested
