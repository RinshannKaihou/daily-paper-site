Representation Without Control: Testing the Realization Effect in Language Models

A linearly decodable representation in an LLM is not a causal lever. Gemma 3 4B encodes a clear "realization status" signal at layer 18 — but steering along that direction produces zero behavioral change in downstream risk decisions. Readable ≠ controllable.

Key Metric
0
median wager & risk delta across all steering scales (−50 to +150)
Despite 91.1% readout accuracy on validation and 92.9% on held-out DeepSeek prompts, projection deltas do not predict wager deltas (Pearson r = 0.072) or risk deltas (r = 0.001).
Why This Matters

Linear probes are widely used as evidence that a model "has" a concept or "uses" a mechanism. This paper provides a clean, null-result counterexample: the probe succeeds, the steering fails.

A critical warning for all mechanistic interpretability work — especially in decision-making and reasoning, where causal validation is essential.

Critical Edge

Tested on one model (Gemma 3 4B), one layer (18), one direction method (mean-difference). No layer sweep, no multi-layer steering, no bootstrap CIs on steering deltas.

Whether this dissociation is structural or methodological remains unknown. The decoded direction may track a broad semantic "open/closed" contrast rather than a decision-relevant variable.

Three-Tier Evaluation Framework

  1. Behavioral sensitivity — 54,450 rows across 25 models, 11 conditions. Models are condition-sensitive, but the pattern reverses human realization-effect predictions (realized losses increase wagers).
  2. Linear activation readout — Mean-difference direction at layer 18 of Gemma 3 4B. 756 training pairs, tested on original validation (91.1%) and DeepSeek held-out (92.9%) splits.
  3. Causal steering — Forward-hook residual-stream addition at scales −50 to +150 on 648 prompts. NULL RESULT Zero median deltas; no sign-symmetry at negative scale.

Methodology

The study repurposes the realization effect from behavioral economics — where risk-taking differs after paper (open account) vs. realized (closed account) gains and losses — as a well-characterized testbed.

  • Paired prompts across finance, casino, budget, and other domains.
  • Direction: v_realization = μ_realized_closed − μ_paper_open
  • Residual-stream steering via forward hook at layer 18 during generation.
  • OLS regressions with model, temperature, and prompt-version fixed effects; HC3 robust SE.

Open Questions

  1. Would a layer sweep (14–22) or multi-layer schedule break the null?
  2. Does the dissociation appear in other architectures (Qwen, LLaMA)?
  3. Can the three-tier framework generalize to anchoring, framing, or other biases?
  4. Would all-token (position-mode) steering change the result?
  5. Is the broad semantic contrast the reason steering fails?

Authors & Credibility

Ciarán Walsh & Emilio Barkett — Columbia University. Supported by the SPAR program.

Clear hypothesis preregistration (Table 2), thorough null-result reporting, honest discussion of compliance artifacts. Code and data publicly available.

Strengths

  • Theory-driven design using a well-characterized behavioral economics phenomenon.
  • Thorough null reporting with sign-symmetry checks ruling out "insufficient magnitude."
  • Three-tier framework is generalizable beyond the realization effect.
  • Detailed appendices with compliance breakdowns and per-split readout results.

Limitations

  • Single model, single layer, single direction construction method.
  • Hosted models (Qwen etc.) excluded — no hidden-state access.
  • Constrained numeric outputs may amplify format artifacts; ~49% noncompliance at some scales.
  • Decoded direction may track broad open/closed contrast, not the decision-relevant variable.
  • No bootstrap CIs; null relies on zero medians and absent sign-symmetry.
