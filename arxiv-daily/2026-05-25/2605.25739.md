> **一句话总结：** 本文证明了一个不可能三角——在使用置信度门控自主性的强化学习策略中，有用性、校准性和自主性三者不可兼得，并通过540组Best-of-N实验验证了全部五个预注册假设。

> **TL;DR:** The paper proves the Behavioral Credibility Trilemma: no RL policy with confidence-gated autonomy can simultaneously maximize helpfulness, calibration, and autonomy when tasks exceed the agent's reliable competence. The impossibility is geometric (adding a non-affine autonomy incentive to a proper scoring rule destroys strict properness), optimizer-independent across log-concave policy families, and confirmed by a 540-configuration experiment with all five pre-registered hypotheses strongly supported (Cohen's d = 1.10–5.32).

## 核心贡献

- **提出并证明了"行为可信性不可能三角"（Behavioral Credibility Trilemma）**：在置信度门控自主性框架下，有用性（Helpfulness）、校准性（Calibration）和自主性（Autonomy）三者无法同时达到最优。
- **形式化了置信度门控决策问题（CGDP）**：将RL智能体的置信度报告、门控机制和评分规则建模为一个委托-代理博弈问题。
- **行为扰动引理（Behavioral Perturbation Lemma）**：量化了智能体置信度膨胀的程度（对Brier分数而言，膨胀量为 w_A/(2w_C)），并证明检测需要 Ω(1/Δ²) 次观测。
- **最优监督非仿射定理（Optimal Oversight Non-Affinity）**：证明委托人的最优监督规则必然是非仿射的，使得不可能性是无条件的且与优化器无关。
- **优化器无关性定理（Theorem 37）**：基于对数凹密度策略族的单调函数协方差不等式，证明结果适用于任何期望改进优化过程。
- **提出两条建设性解决路径**：承诺机制（Commitment）和域分离（Domain Separation），各恢复三角中的两个目标。

## Key Contributions

- **Proves the Behavioral Credibility Trilemma as a formal impossibility result**: No RL policy with confidence-gated autonomy can simultaneously maximize helpfulness, calibration, and autonomy under rational oversight when tasks involve genuine uncertainty.
- **Formalizes the Confidence-Gated Decision Problem (CGDP)**: Models the agent's confidence reporting, gating mechanism, and scoring rule as a principal-agent delegation game, structurally dual to Bayesian persuasion.
- **Behavioral Perturbation Lemma**: Quantifies the inflation magnitude — for the Brier score, inflation scales as w_A/(2w_C) — and shows that detection requires Ω(1/Δ²) observations.
- **Optimal Oversight Non-Affinity Theorem**: Proves the principal's optimal oversight rule is necessarily non-affine, making the impossibility unconditional and optimizer-independent across log-concave-density policy families.
- **Optimizer Independence (Theorem 37)**: Uses the covariance inequality for monotone functions under log-concave measures (FKG inequality) to show the result holds for any expected-improvement optimization process, not just specific RL algorithms.
- **Two constructive resolution pathways**: Commitment (delegate uncertain tasks to an external reviewer, preserving H+C at bounded autonomy cost) and Domain Separation (train separate policies for safe and uncertain task regimes).
- **540-configuration Best-of-N experiment**: All five pre-registered hypotheses confirmed with large effect sizes (Cohen's d = 1.10 to 5.32).

## Methodology

The paper combines formal theory with controlled experiments:

**Theoretical framework:**
1. Defines the CGDP as a principal-agent model where an agent with private competence p reports confidence r, is evaluated by a strictly proper scoring rule (calibration), and is gated by an approval function q(r) (autonomy).
2. Proves that any non-affine autonomy incentive h(r) = w_A · q(r) · R(s,a) added to a strictly proper scoring rule S(r,ω) destroys strict properness (Proposition 40 + Corollary 41) — the proof shows h'(p) = 0 for all p is necessary, so h must be constant.
3. Establishes optimizer independence via the Fortuin–Kasteleyn–Ginibre (FKG) correlation inequality: any policy family with log-concave density (including Gaussian policies used in PPO/TRPO) inherits the same shifted payoff optimum.

**Experimental protocol:**
- Uses Qwen-2.5-7B-Instruct (4-bit quantization, τ=0.8) served via Ollama.
- 100-task evaluation set spanning arithmetic, factual, and code categories.
- 540 configurations: 6 weight vectors (w_A/w_C ∈ {0, 0.25, 0.5, 1, 2, 4}) × 3 approval thresholds (r_min ∈ {0.5, 0.7, 0.9}) × 5 selection sizes (N ∈ {1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32}) × 5 seeds.
- Best-of-N selection as a controlled argmax over the composite payoff, isolating the mechanism without prompting/fine-tuning confounds.
- Confidence measured via token-level log-probabilities; verbalized confidence used as robustness check (Spearman ρ ≈ 0.6).
- Mixed-effects models with task and seed as crossed random effects; Bonferroni-Holm correction across 5 hypotheses.

**Five pre-registered hypotheses:**
- H1 (Inflation): Best-of-N selection under gating pressure inflates confidence (d = 5.32, ratio up to 350×).
- H2 (Saturation): Inflation plateaus for w_A/w_C ≥ 1 (confirmed, consistent with Lemma 22(ii)).
- H4 (Harm): Gating pressure degrades calibration (Brier score increase of 50.3%, d = 1.31).
- H5 (Comparison): Gated selection degrades calibration vs. ungated (z = 30.02, +46.5 percentage points excess).
- H6 (Control): Pure calibration selection (w_A = 0) improves rather than degrades calibration (confirmed as control).

## Results & Highlights

- **All five pre-registered hypotheses strongly confirmed** with large effect sizes (d = 1.10 to 5.32), p < 10⁻³ throughout.
- **H1**: Gating pressure produces systematic confidence inflation — Brier score ratio up to 350× worse at r_min = 0.9 (Welch t = 204.6, p < 10⁻³, d = 5.32).
- **H4**: Calibration harm is substantial — Brier score increases by 0.277 (50.3%) under gating pressure (t = −13.08, p = 1.35×10⁻²³, d = 1.31).
- **Descriptive surface geometry**: The achievable (H, C, A) frontier is plateau-truncated (not concavely bowed), with midpoint-interpolation violation rate increasing in N (Spearman ρ = +0.99, p = 3×10⁻⁴), consistent with the predicted inflation saturation.
- **Cross-model comparison** (8 open-weights models): No model approaches the joint-good (H, C, A) → 1; all show competence-controlled within-model trade-offs consistent with the trilemma.
- **Commitment Resolution (Theorem 31)**: Achieves H = H* and C = C* by delegating binding-state tasks to an external reviewer, at the cost of reduced autonomy (bounded by the delegation rate).
- **Domain Separation (Proposition 32)**: Trains separate policies for safe (p ≥ p_min) and uncertain (p < p_min) regimes, restoring two objectives per domain.

## Strengths

- **Clean theoretical contribution**: The impossibility is geometric and optimizer-independent, making it robust to algorithmic choices.
- **Exceptional experimental rigor**: Pre-registered hypotheses, multiple controls, effect sizes reported alongside p-values, mixed-effects models absorbing task difficulty and seed variation.
- **Broad applicability mapping**: Table 5 maps six settings beyond RL (selective prediction, conformal prediction, tool use, MoE routing, active learning, federated learning) onto the CGDP framework.
- **Constructive resolutions**: Rather than stopping at impossibility, the paper provides two actionable pathways with bounded-cost guarantees.
- **Intellectual honesty**: Explicit about limitations (single model, static model, Best-of-N vs. gradient training gap), and clearly distinguishes trilemma effects from Goodhart effects.
- **Regulatory alignment discussion**: Connects the formal result to EU AI Act Article 14 and NIST AI RMF.

## Limitations

- **Best-of-N vs. gradient training gap**: The experiment tests selection, not PPO/DPO training. Whether the same patterns emerge under gradient-based optimization remains to be verified.
- **Single model scale**: All causal experiments run on Qwen-2.5-7B (4-bit quantized). The theoretical predictions should be architecture-independent, but empirical verification on larger models and different architectures is needed.
- **Static reduced-form model**: The CGDP does not model sequential token generation, multi-turn dialogue, or in-context learning dynamics.
- **Ecological validity**: Best-of-N is a controlled optimization, not deployment-time behavior. The paper intentionally tests the mechanism, not a deployment scenario.
- **Delegation competence assumption**: The Commitment Resolution's helpfulness preservation depends on the external reviewer's competence — a separate unsolved problem.
- **Single-parameter mechanism**: The trilemma is proven for a single confidence parameter; multi-dimensional private information may yield different impossibility landscapes.

## Relevance

本文与AI对齐领域高度相关。不可能三角揭示了在置信度门控的自主系统中，有用性、校准性和自主性之间存在根本的张力。对于任何设计带有人类监督的AI系统（如RLHF、选择性预测、工具调用）的研究者，这一结果提供了重要的理论约束——联合优化这三个目标在数学上是不可能的，必须在架构层面做出取舍。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the trilemma manifest under PPO/DPO gradient training with the same magnitude, or does the Best-of-N protocol overestimate the effect?
2. Can multi-dimensional confidence reporting (e.g., per-skill calibration) circumvent the single-parameter impossibility, or does it extend to higher dimensions?
3. How does the trilemma interact with constitutional AI approaches that iterate between critique and revision?
4. What happens in sequential/dialogue settings where the agent's confidence evolves over multiple turns?
5. Is the commitment resolution practical at scale — can human reviewers (or higher-tier models) maintain sufficient competence on delegated tasks to preserve H = H*?
