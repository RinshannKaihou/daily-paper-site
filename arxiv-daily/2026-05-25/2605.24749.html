How Neural Reward Models Learn Features for Policy Optimization: A Single-Index Analysis

Higuchi · Kawata · Wachi · Takakura · Miyaguchi · Suzuki  |  U Tokyo / RIKEN AIP / LY Corporation  |  2026-05-23

Key Finding
Exponential reward weighting reduces the feature-learning bottleneck from Information Exponent to Generative Exponent with a dimension-free temperature threshold — the first rigorous account of why reward overoptimization occurs.

Methodology

Two-Stage Protocol

  • Stage 1 — Feature recovery: Online spherical SGD with exponential label weighting ey/β₁; Hermite analysis shows signal at degree pgen = GE(σ*)
  • Stage 2 — Weighted ridge: Frozen features; fit readout a ∈ 𝔽N via label- or surrogate-weighted ridge at deployment temp β₂
Key Contributions
  • IE → GE reduction. Exponential reward weighting acts as a nonlinear label transform, lowering the Hermite bottleneck from Information Exponent to Generative Exponent for recovering the hidden direction θ*.
  • Tilted-policy value gap. Replaces reference-distribution prediction error with reward under the deployed tilted policy π̂β₂,R, decomposed into temperature mismatch + truncation + learning terms.
  • Surrogate overoptimization mechanism. The C0,R(β₂) factor ∝ β₂−dS isolates how proxy mismatch amplifies both approximation and finite-sample errors.
  • Admissible temperature set. Explicitly characterises when lowering β₂ is beneficial vs. when exponential-weighting cost dominates.
Strengths
  • Clean two-stage separation of feature learning from policy evaluation
  • Dimension-free threshold is a genuine improvement over prior polylog(d) bounds
  • Tilted-policy value gap is the correct metric for reward modeling
  • Correctly identifies reference-distribution error as the wrong metric
⚠ Limitations
  • Gaussian input x ~ N(0, Id) is far from realistic prompt–response distributions
  • Single-index structure limits reward expressiveness to one hidden direction
  • Polynomial link excludes ReLU, step functions, and discontinuous rewards
  • No empirical validation — purely theoretical
Open Questions
  1. Can the Gaussian assumption be relaxed to sub-Gaussian or mixture distributions closer to real embeddings?
  2. How does the analysis extend to multi-index models r*(x) = σ*(⟨θ*1,x⟩, …, ⟨θ*k,x⟩)?
  3. Is projected truncation an artifact of polynomial links, removable for sigmoidal activations?
  4. What happens when β₁ is annealed during training rather than kept fixed?
  5. Can iterative proxy refinement control the C0,R factor — suggesting a principled DPO-like algorithm?
