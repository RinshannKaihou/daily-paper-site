> **一句话总结：** GPU 保持模型常驻的能耗（"停车税"）由 CUDA 上下文触发的 DVFS 跳变决定，与模型占用的显存大小几乎无关——跨三种 GPU 架构（H100/A100/L40S）和三种内存技术（HBM3/HBM2e/GDDR6）一致成立。

> **TL;DR:** The "model parking tax" — idle power consumed by keeping AI models loaded in GPU memory — is dominated by a discrete CUDA-context DVFS step (26–66 W), not by VRAM occupancy (< 0.02 W/GB). This holds across H100 (HBM3), A100 (HBM2e), and L40S (GDDR6), validated with production telemetry (335K samples) and controlled experiments. A breakeven-aware scheduler saves 8–23% energy vs. always-on deployment.

## 核心贡献

- 首次跨三种 GPU 架构（H100 HBM3、A100 HBM2e、L40S GDDR6）分解空闲 GPU 功率为 DVFS 分量和显存分量
- 发现 CUDA 上下文创建触发离散 DVFS 跳变（+26~66 W），占"停车税"的 >98%；显存占用的边际功耗 |β| < 0.02 W/GB，统计上不显著
- 建立冷启动保本模型 T* = P_load · t_load / P_park，保本间隔仅 1–5 分钟，与模型大小无关
- 提出基于保本阈值的调度策略，在合成流量模式下节能 8–23%
- 估算行业级影响：仅 CUDA 上下文开销即达 92–1,745 GWh/年（基准 462 GWh/年）

## Key Contributions

- First cross-architecture decomposition of idle GPU power into DVFS and VRAM components across three GPU architectures and three memory technologies (HBM3, HBM2e, GDDR6)
- Demonstration that the CUDA context DVFS step (26–66 W) accounts for >98% of the parking tax, while marginal VRAM effect is |β| < 0.02 W/GB with formal TOST equivalence testing
- Cold-start breakeven model (T* = P_load · t_load / P_park) showing energy-optimal eviction intervals of 1–5 minutes, independent of model size
- Proof-of-concept breakeven-aware scheduler achieving 8–23% energy savings over always-on across synthetic traffic patterns
- Industry-scale impact estimate: 92–1,745 GWh/year from CUDA context overhead alone (base case 462 GWh/year, ~180 kT CO₂)

## Methodology

**Phase 1 — Production Telemetry:** 18 days of DCGM metrics from 14 H100 GPUs across 2 Kubernetes nodes. 335,267 idle-state samples (0% utilization) with VRAM allocations spanning 3 MB to 79 GB. Regression of idle power against VRAM allocation yields slope = 0.013 W/GB, R² = 0.001 — no detectable VRAM effect.

**Phase 2 — Controlled Dose-Response:** Within-subject experiments on H100, A100, and L40S. Protocol: record bare-idle baseline (no CUDA context), create persistent CUDA context, sequentially allocate VRAM via `torch.empty` from 0 to 40–72 GB. Each level: 60 s stabilization, then 20 min recording at 30 s intervals (n = 40 per phase). Within-phase σ < 1.5 W on the noisiest device (L40S), yielding SE < 0.25 W.

**Real Model Validation:** Loaded Qwen2.5-7B (fp16, ~14.9 GB) on all three architectures. Compared idle power against reference allocations — difference < 0.5 W on every device.

**Cold-Start Profiling:** Captured 1 Hz power traces during model loading on H100 (29.7 s for Qwen2.5-7B). Profile is bursty: 22 s at bare idle, 3 s GPU burst peaking at 124 W, then settling to CUDA-active idle.

**Formal Equivalence Testing:** Two One-Sided Tests (TOST) with equivalence bound Δ = ±0.1 W/GB rejects |β| ≥ 0.1 on all three GPUs (p < 0.05), formally bounding the VRAM effect below operational relevance.

## Results & Highlights

- **Bimodal production power:** Bare idle at 74.7 W (345 MHz) vs. CUDA-active at 145.5 W (1980 MHz) on H100 — Cohen's d = 7.3, p < 10⁻³⁰⁰
- **Cross-architecture parking tax:** H100: +49.9 W (7.1% TDP), A100: +26.3 W (8.8% TDP), L40S: +66.4 W (19.0% TDP)
- **VRAM effect negligible:** Power range across full VRAM span is < 1 W on every GPU. Regression slopes: H100 β = −0.002, A100 β = −0.001, L40S β = −0.002 (all W/GB)
- **Real model confirms:** Qwen2.5-7B idle power within 0.03–0.47 W of reference allocations on all architectures
- **Breakeven intervals:** H100: 4.5 min (PyTorch 70B), A100: ~8.5 min, L40S: ~3.4 min. With ServerlessLLM (8 s load): 48 s on H100
- **Scheduler savings:** Breakeven policy saves 18.1% (low traffic), 23.0% (bursty), 8.2% (diurnal) vs. always-on
- **Industry estimate:** 462 GWh/year base case — exceeds annual electricity of a small city

## Critical Assessment

| Criterion | Rating | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| **Novelty** | High | First cross-architecture decomposition of idle GPU power; no prior work measured marginal VRAM cost per GB |
| **Rigor** | High | Within-subject design, formal equivalence testing, sub-watt measurement resolution, triangulated with production telemetry |
| **Reproducibility** | High | Public code/data, clear protocols, standard hardware |
| **Significance** | High | Directly actionable for GPU fleet operators; 462 GWh/year impact estimate is compelling |
| **Red flags** | Moderate | n = 1 device per architecture in Phase 2 (mitigated by Phase 1's 14-device production data and physics argument); synthetic scheduler traces only; does not evaluate MIG/MPS/power capping configurations |

The A100 negative slope finding (β = −0.001, p = 0.002) is honestly discussed as a temporal confound (thermal drift) rather than hidden — a strength. The claim of cross-architecture invariance is appropriately qualified: "consistent across all tested architectures" rather than universal. The scheduler evaluation is a proof-of-concept; production validation is explicitly identified as future work.

## Strengths

- Rigorous measurement methodology with sub-watt resolution combining production and controlled experiments
- Clean mathematical model (Equation 1) that separates DVFS and VRAM contributions
- Cross-architecture consistency across three microarchitectures and three memory technologies strengthens generality
- Practical and immediately actionable: breakeven model uses only measurable hardware parameters
- Honest discussion of the A100 negative slope anomaly and all limitations
- Industry-scale impact quantification provides motivation beyond academic interest

## Limitations

- One GPU per architecture in Phase 2; inter-device variation bounded only on H100 (Phase 1)
- Scheduler evaluated on synthetic traces (Poisson, bursty, diurnal) — not production LLM serving logs
- Does not evaluate MIG, MPS, or power capping (nvidia-smi -pl) which could alter DVFS behavior
- Cold-start power traces captured only on H100; load times measured on all three but full profiles not
- Breakeven model assumes known request arrival rates; real workloads are non-stationary and heavy-tailed
- Larger models (>72 GB, multi-GPU) not tested; loading profiles may differ

## Relevance

本文对 GPU 推理部署和绿色计算有直接指导意义。核心发现——"停车税"是 CUDA 上下文的固定成本而非模型大小的可变成本——意味着小模型常驻是最浪费的部署方式。保本调度模型可直接应用于推理集群的节能优化，与我的研究兴趣中的系统优化和可持续 AI 方向高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does the parking tax behave under MIG partitioning or MPS sharing — can the DVFS overhead be amortized across tenants?
2. Would power capping (nvidia-smi -pl) at low thresholds force idle-with-context GPUs into lower clock states, reducing the parking tax?
3. How do multi-GPU tensor-parallel deployments (e.g., 70B models across 4×H100) scale the parking tax — is it additive per GPU?
4. Can predictive eviction policies trained on real production workload traces (Azure Functions, Alibaba GPU traces) significantly outperform the fixed-threshold breakeven approach?
5. What is the parking tax on upcoming architectures (B200, MI300X) with different memory subsystems (HBM3e, unified memory)?
