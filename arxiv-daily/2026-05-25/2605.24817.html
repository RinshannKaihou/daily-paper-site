RouteScan: Non-Intrusive MoE LLM Safety Auditing via Expert Routing Telemetry

Lv, Xu, Xiu, Ding, Zheng, Wang, Ren — Zhejiang Univ. · Donghua Univ. · LSU

Turns GPU side-channel "bug" into auditing "feature" — analyzes active thread counts from MoE expert routing during prefilling to detect harmful inputs without ever seeing user prompts, achieving near-perfect detection on unseen threats while formally resisting prompt reconstruction.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Elegant reframing: turns side-channel "bug" into auditing "feature"
  • Privacy-by-design: aggregated prefilling telemetry is structurally lossy — zero sensitive-field recovery
  • Robust evaluation: 4 benchmarks, 3 jailbreak types, 2 MoE architectures, 2 GPU types
  • Lightweight linear detector stays interpretable, avoids overfitting
  • Three-party threat model maps to real regulatory frameworks (EU AI Act)

Limitations

  • MoE-only — dense LLMs fundamentally incompatible
  • Prefilling-phase only; excludes streaming / multi-turn
  • Tested only on ~2.7B–16B params; scaling to 256+ experts unknown
  • 17.8% nsys overhead non-trivial for production
  • Privacy is empirical, not formal — no differential-privacy bound
  • Gray-box adversaries could mimic benign routing patterns
