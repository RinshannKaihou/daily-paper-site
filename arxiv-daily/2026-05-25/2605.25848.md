> **一句话总结：** 本文提出几何演化图（GEMs），通过追踪概念方向在残差流中的完整轨迹，定位方向旋转停止的"交接层"并提取已稳定的探针方向，在23个架构（70M–14B参数）、17种概念类型的391个组合中，GEM探针在68.5%的试验中至少与峰值层探针一样精确，MHA架构优势尤其显著（78.3%）。

> **TL;DR:** GEMs track the full directional trajectory of concept representations through transformer residual streams and extract probes from the "handoff layer" where angular velocity drops below ε=0.05, rather than from a fixed layer or the separation-score peak. The core empirical finding is that concept directions rotate substantially during assembly (mean entry-exit cosine similarity only 0.233). Across 23 architectures (70M–14B params), 17 concept types, and 391 concept×model pairs, GEM probes match or outperform peak-layer probes in 68.5% of trials (model-level Wilcoxon p=0.010). The advantage is architecture-dependent: MHA models favour the handoff in 78.3% of trials vs. GQA at 47.1%. A depth-matched control on 374 pairs confirms the settling criterion contributes +32.5pp beyond depth alone (88.0% of pairs). Multi-node permutation ablation reveals causal relay structure where the deepest node dominates.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了概念表示在"概念分配区"（CAZ）内经历大幅方向旋转的核心现象：入口-出口余弦相似度均值仅0.233（中位数0.216），93.9%的组合低于0.5，23.8%接近正交（<0.1），说明传统峰值层探针提取的是尚未稳定的中间方向。
- 提出几何演化图（GEMs）方法：追踪概念方向在残差流中的完整演化轨迹，通过角速度阈值（ε=0.05）检测CAZ结束位置，从紧接其后的"交接层"提取已稳定的探针方向。
- 在23个模型（70M–14B参数）、17种概念类型、391个概念×模型组合上进行大规模验证，GEM探针在68.5%的试验中至少与峰值层探针一样精确（模型级Wilcoxon W=214, p=0.010）。
- 发现显著的架构依赖性：MHA模型在78.3%的试验中偏好交接层（11/13个模型），GQA模型仅47.1%（2/7个模型），Fisher精确检验p=0.022。
- 设计自适应消融宽度规则（HL/N > 0.85时w=1替代w=3），在79个触发案例中75.9%改善了探针质量（平均+7.44pp）。
- 方向特异性控制实验：概念方向消融以中位数377倍的速率抑制分离度（45.6% vs 0.24%），99.1%的概念方向优于所有10个随机种子。
- 深度匹配控制实验（374个组合，29个模型）：GEM的稳定化准则贡献了+32.5pp的额外抑制（88.0%的组合），排除了提取深度作为混杂因素。
- 多节点置换消融揭示因果接力结构：最深节点在15/15个多节点概念中主导最终层分离抑制，浅层节点对深层节点存在因果上游效应（9/15个概念出现交叉干扰）。

## Key Contributions

- Documents that concept directions undergo substantial rotation within Concept Allocation Zones (CAZs), with mean entry-exit cosine of 0.233 (median 0.216), 93.9% below 0.5, and 23.8% near-orthogonal (<0.1), proving that peak-layer probes extract unsettled intermediate directions.
- Introduces GEMs: track full directional trajectory through residual stream, detect CAZ end via angular velocity threshold (ε=0.05), extract settled probe direction from the immediately following "handoff layer."
- Large-scale validation across 23 models (70M–14B), 17 concept types, 391 pairs: GEM probes at least as precise as peak-layer probes in 68.5% of trials (model-level Wilcoxon W=214, N=23, p=0.010).
- Identifies pronounced architecture dependency: MHA models favour handoff in 78.3% (11/13 models) vs. GQA at 47.1% (2/7 models); Fisher's exact p=0.022.
- Proposes adaptive ablation width rule (w=1 when HL/N > 0.85 instead of w=3), improving 60/79 triggered cases (75.9%) with mean +7.44pp gain.
- Direction-specificity control: concept-direction ablation suppresses separation at median 377× the rate of random-direction ablation (45.6% vs. 0.24%); 99.1% of concept directions beat all 10 random seeds.
- Depth-matched control (374 pairs, 29 models): settling criterion contributes +32.5pp beyond depth-of-extraction alone (88.0% of pairs), ruling out depth as a confound.
- Multi-node permutation ablation reveals causal relay structure: deepest node dominates (15/15), shallow nodes are causally upstream (cross-disruption in 9/15), relay topology is architecture-dependent (GQA shows stronger per-relay disruption and genuine positive synergy).

## Methodology

The GEM framework operates on the difference-in-means direction at each layer. For a transformer M with N layers and a concept c with K contrastive pairs, the dominant concept direction at layer l is the L2-normalized difference of class centroids: u^(l) = (h̄₊^(l) - h̄₋^(l)) / ||h̄₊^(l) - h̄₋^(l)||. Activations are extracted at the last token position from the post-MLP residual stream output.

The angular velocity at layer l is defined as ω(l) = 1 - u^(l) · u^(l-1). The handoff layer is LH = min(L_CAZ_end + 1, N - 1), where L_CAZ_end is the last consecutive layer where ω(l) > ε = 0.05. The settled direction u^(LH) becomes the GEM-extracted concept probe.

Validation uses directional ablation: for activation h, the ablated activation is h' = h - (h·u)u, which zeroes the component along u while preserving all orthogonal components. Probe quality is measured by retained separation percentage after ablation (lower = better probe).

The study covers 23 base models across Pythia (70M–12B), GPT-2 (124M–1.5B), OPT (1.3B–6.7B), Qwen2.5 (0.5B–14B), Mistral (7B), Llama-3.1 (8B), Gemma-2 (2B, 9B), and Phi-2 (2.7B), spanning MHA (13), GQA (7), and other (3) architectures. 17 concepts span semantic, epistemic, pragmatic, safety, syntactic, relational, and affective domains. 250 contrastive pairs per concept from the Rosetta Concept Pairs dataset, constructed via multi-model consensus (3–5 models from Claude/GPT/Gemini/Mistral must unanimously agree on concept labels).

The multi-node permutation ablation tests every non-empty subset of GEM nodes per concept (2^N - 1 subsets) on pythia-6.9b, GPT-2-XL, and Qwen2.5-7B, measuring cross-disruption (upstream ablation reducing downstream separation) and synergy (multi-node ablation exceeding best single node).

## Results & Highlights

- **Rotation is universal:** Mean EEC = 0.233 across all 391 pairs. No architecture is immune; gpt2 shows the lowest (0.083), Qwen2.5-1.5B the highest (0.392).
- **GEM vs. peak-layer probes:** 268/391 (68.5%) at least as precise; 259/391 (66.2%) strictly outperform. Mean improvement for winning cases: +20.4pp retained percentage.
- **Architecture split:** MHA 173/221 (78.3%) vs. GQA 56/119 (47.1%). 11/13 MHA models prefer handoff on majority of concepts vs. 2/7 GQA (Fisher p=0.022).
- **Scale effects:** <500M: 47% improvement (61% excluding gpt2); 500M–3B: 71%; >3B: 70%. Notable: Llama-3.1-8B, pythia-1.4b, gpt2-large, gpt2-xl all achieve 17/17 (100%).
- **Adaptive width rule:** 60/79 triggered cases improved (75.9%), mean +7.44pp. Depth-corrected variant (N ≥ 20): 53/62 (85.5%) at mean +6.80pp.
- **Direction specificity:** Concept-direction ablation mean 45.6% reduction vs. random-direction 0.24%. Median specificity ratio 377×. 99.1% of concept directions beat all 10 random seeds.
- **Depth-matched control:** 329/374 (88.0%) pairs show GEM outperforming depth-matched non-settling post-CAZ layer, mean advantage +32.5pp.
- **Relay structure:** Deepest GEM node dominates in 15/15 multi-node concepts. Cross-disruption in 9/15 concepts (mean 0.11). GQA relay shows stronger per-relay disruption and genuine positive synergy (10/14 positive) vs. MHA's apparent anti-synergy.
- **Structured failures:** gpt2 (12 layers) pathologically increases separation in 13/17 pairs due to unembedding proximity. OPT-6.7b reversed from 7/17 (41%) at smaller corpus to 13/17 (76%) at N=250.
- **Safety concepts handoff deep:** threat_severity (0.860), certainty (0.865), deception (0.874), temporal_order (0.878) settle latest.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The observation that concept directions rotate substantially during CAZ assembly (mean EEC 0.233) is a genuine insight that challenges standard practice. The GEM framework is principled and the handoff-layer concept is elegant.
- **Rigor:** Generally strong. 391 concept×model pairs across 23 architectures is ambitious. Multiple controls (direction-specificity, depth-matched, multi-node permutation) address obvious confounds. Wilcoxon at model level (p=0.010) is appropriate given non-exchangeable trials. However, the ε=0.05 threshold was set on a small dev set (pythia-70m, gpt2) with no formal sensitivity analysis — this is a meaningful gap.
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent. Code, data, and pre-extracted activations all publicly available with Zenodo DOIs. Contrastive pairs use a multi-model consensus protocol documented in detail.
- **Significance:** The MHA/GQA split is the most important finding: GQA models (which include Llama-3, Qwen2.5, Mistral — i.e., most modern LLMs) show no consistent benefit (47.1%). The paper honestly reports this but underweights its implications — if GQA is the future, GEM's practical utility is questionable for the architectures that matter most.
- **Red flags:** (1) "At least as precise" conflates ties (9 cases) with wins — the honest win rate is 66.2%, and 31.5% of trials show degradation. (2) The ε threshold sensitivity gap. (3) The handoff cosine to final layer is mean 0.942, median 1.000 — for most concepts, the handoff direction is nearly identical to the final layer direction, raising questions about practical added value over simply probing the final layer. (4) No behavioral ablation was actually run; all validation is separation-suppression at the probe layer, not downstream output change. (5) The N=250 corpus reversed the OPT-6.7b failure, but N-stability was not systematically verified across the corpus — only for OPT.

## Strengths

- Identifies and rigorously documents a real phenomenon (directional rotation during concept assembly) that prior work overlooked.
- Exceptional scale and breadth: 23 architectures, 17 concept types, 391 pairs with multiple control experiments.
- Open science: code, pre-extracted activations, contrastive pairs, and analysis scripts all publicly available.
- The depth-matched control (§5.5) directly isolates the settling criterion from depth confound — this is methodologically clean.
- Multi-node permutation ablation revealing causal relay structure (§5.4) goes beyond single-point extraction to characterize the full assembly process.
- Honest reporting of structured failures (gpt2, OPT-6.7b reversal) and negative results (GQA underperformance).
- The architecture-dependent relay topology finding (GQA positive synergy vs. MHA apparent anti-synergy) is novel and intriguing.

## Limitations

- **GQA applicability:** Modern LLMs increasingly use GQA, yet GEM shows no consistent advantage for GQA architectures (47.1%). The paper documents this but does not resolve why grouped key-value sharing disrupts the handoff mechanism.
- **No behavioral validation:** All experiments measure separation suppression at the probe layer, not downstream task performance or output change. The probes are intended for steering and intervention, but their behavioral efficacy is untested.
- **ε threshold not rigorously validated:** The angular velocity threshold ε=0.05 was set empirically on 2 models; no sensitivity analysis across a range of ε values is provided.
- **Handoff cosines near 1.0 to final layer:** Median handoff-to-final cosine is 1.000, suggesting GEM probes are often nearly identical to what you'd get from the final layer — practical added value over final-layer probing may be limited for most cases.
- **Single-author scope:** The breadth (23 models, 17 concepts, permutation ablation, multiple controls) is unusual for a single-author paper. Independent replication would strengthen confidence.
- **Models > 14B excluded:** No validation on frontier-scale models (70B+, MoE architectures) where practical impact would be greatest.
- **Contrastive pair construction:** Pairs are generated by LLMs and validated by LLM consensus — this circularity may introduce systematic biases not caught by human annotation.

## Relevance

与机械可解释性和表示工程高度相关。GEM为从残差流中提取稳定概念探针提供了系统性方法，对接下来的概念Steering、跨架构表示对齐（PRH）和模型内部状态因果分析有直接方法论价值。交接层和接力结构的发现对理解Transformer中概念的组装过程有深远意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Why does the handoff mechanism fail for GQA architectures? Is it because grouped key-value heads reduce the multi-head integration process that produces directional rotation?
- How do GEM probes compare to final-layer probes in downstream behavioral ablation (output change rather than separation suppression)?
- Does the ε=0.05 threshold generalize to architectures beyond the 23 tested? What happens with ε=0.03 or ε=0.10?
- How does RLHF/instruction tuning shift handoff depths? Do aligned models settle concepts earlier or later than base models?
- Can the relay topology findings (GQA positive synergy vs. MHA anti-synergy) be exploited for architecture-specific intervention strategies?
- What fraction of total concept signal lies outside detectable GEM nodes, and can this be bounded?
- How does the multi-model consensus protocol for contrastive pair construction compare to human-annotated pairs in terms of probe quality?
