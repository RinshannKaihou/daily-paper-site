Analogies between Transformer Layers and Power Method

Chenglong Li & Claudio Altafini · SISSA, Italy

Key Finding
Each transformer layer is one step of the power method — tokens converge toward the principal eigenvector of a "layer matrix" Ft = FO,t · FV,t.
Steering Alignment γT ≈ 1.0
Induced Spectral Gap 10 – 10³
Models Tested 10 variants
So What A principled math framework for understanding & controlling token dynamics via rank-1 perturbation.
Key Contributions

Power Method ↔ Transformer Layers

  • Formal analogy: each layer's projection + LayerNorm (sans FFN) is one power iteration on the layer matrix Ft
  • Layer matrix defined as Ft = FO,t · FV,t (output weights × value weights product)
  • Analytical convergence proof for shared-weight transformers (Theorem 1: symmetric F, full attention → asymptotic stability at ±v₁)
  • Three spectral cases: real dominant, complex dominant, no dominant eigenvalue
  • Constructive rank-1 perturbation method to steer tokens to any desired direction
Why It Matters
Transforms the study of token evolution in deep layers from empirical observation to a solvable linear-algebraic problem. The rank-1 steering trick opens a controllable knob for LLM output direction — without retraining.
Methodology

Dynamical System Model

  • Layer index = discrete time; tokens = state variables
  • Multihead attention modeled as coupled dynamics with attention-dependent coupling weights
xi(t+1) = LayerNorm( xi(t) + η · FO,t Σh F̄V,t(h) Σj At,ij(h) xj(t) )
  • Three alignment metrics per layer: γt (correlation with v₁), ψt (expected increment), ρt (probability of increase)

Token Steering

Rank-1 Perturbation Method

1 Choose desired direction wr in token space
2 Modify final layer: FT,mod = FT + σ · wrwℓT
3 Parameter σ controls induced spectral gap
4 Result: γT ≈ 1.0 near-perfect alignment
Critical Edge
Major Caveat
The entire theoretical framework excludes FFN layers — which constitute a large portion of transformer computation. Including FFN substantially degrades alignment. The gap between model and reality is significant. No downstream task evaluation of the steering method.

Strengths

What Works Well

  • Elegant theoretical bridge between classical numerical algorithms and modern deep learning
  • Rigorous analytical proof for shared-weight case (Theorem 1)
  • Comprehensive experiments across 10 pre-trained variants
  • Steering method is constructive and simple — clear math, easy to implement
  • Honest, quantitative discussion of FFN's degrading effect
Limitations

Gaps & Open Questions

  • FFN exclusion is a major omission — it dominates per-layer compute
  • Theoretical guarantees limited to special cases: symmetric F, full attention, shared weights
  • No evaluation on actual generation tasks or downstream benchmarks
  • Uses random token init, not real prompts with embedding layers
  • Spectral gaps in real transformers are too small for strong practical effect
  • Inference-time weight modification may have unpredictable side effects
Open Questions

Future Directions

Generation quality: How does rank-1 steering affect perplexity, coherence, and diversity?
FFN integration: Can the nonlinear FFN deformation be analytically inverted?
Spectral semantics: Does Ft's spectral structure correlate with linguistic features?
Hybrid control: Combine with activation engineering for fine-grained steering?
Beyond transformers: Does the analogy hold for MoE, SSMs, or linear attention?
