Confidence and Calibration of Activation Oracles for Reliable Interpretation of Language Model Internals

Federico Torrielli · Peter Schneider-Kamp · Lukas Galke Poech — Univ. of Turin & Univ. of Southern Denmark

Benchmarks six uncertainty quantification methods for activation oracles (models that translate LLM hidden states into natural language). Temperature-bootstrap mode frequency is best-calibrated; direct numeric self-report is anti-calibrated.

Key Finding
Temperature-bootstrap sampling is the only reliably calibrated UQ method for activation oracles — while the most intuitive approach, asking the model to self-report confidence, produces worse-than-random calibration on the larger model.
This has direct implications for alignment auditing and deception detection: relying on verbalized confidence is actively dangerous.

Six UQ Methods Evaluated

M1
Log-probability
Joint token probability under greedy decoding. Fast, ~1/20th cost of bootstrap.
M2 ★
Temperature Bootstrap
k=20 samples at T; confidence = mode frequency. Best ECE overall. ★ Recommended
M3 ⚠
Direct Numeric Self-report
Ask "how confident (0–100)?" Anti-calibrated on 27B: more confident on wrong answers.
M4
MCMC Power-Sampling Acceptance
Acceptance rate of Metropolis-Hastings on steered oracle. Fails: posterior too mode-peaked.
M5
MCMC Cross-chain Agreement
Mode frequency across k=10 independent chains. Moderate but not competitive.
M6
Steering-coefficient Sensitivity
Answer stability across coefficient grid. Too coarse-grained for reliable confidence.

Strengths

Comprehensive benchmark: 16 method-temperature configs, two model sizes, five metrics
Clear practical recommendation: bootstrap for production, log-prob for fast triage
Honest negative results with mechanistic explanation (MCMC failure)
Replicates probe-vs-verbalized confidence gap in a new setting
First oracle for hybrid Gated DeltaNet + Gated Attention (Qwen3.6-27B)

Limitations

Single task: secret-word taboo (20 words, closed vocabulary) only
Two Qwen-family models; cross-architecture generality not demonstrated
Exact-match scoring penalizes semantically close answers
Constrained label scoring pilot has only n=30 samples
Bootstrap requires 20 forward passes per sample — expensive at scale

Open Questions

  1. How do UQ methods transfer to open-ended interpretability tasks with no closed vocabulary?
  2. Can constrained label scoring (AUROC 0.957 pilot) scale while maintaining calibration?
  3. Would embedding-based soft accuracy (e.g., BERTScore) change UQ method rankings?
  4. How does optimal bootstrap temperature interact with target concept count beyond 20 words?
  5. Can the MCMC failure mode (mode-peakedness) be tested by constructing a genuinely multimodal task?
