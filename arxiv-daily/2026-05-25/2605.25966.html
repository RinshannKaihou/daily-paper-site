Mapping the Schedule x Bit-Width Boundary in Sub-100M Quantisation-Aware Training

Christian Brandt Thomassen — Dwarf A/S, Copenhagen

quantization-aware-training learning-rate-schedule small-language-models scaling-laws low-bit-quantization
Key Finding

The optimal LR schedule is bit-width-agnostic: FP16, INT8, and INT6 all share the same 33% warmdown across every model size tested. Quantisation precision does not require separate schedule tuning.

Key Metric
1,345 factorial experiment runs with 5 seeds per cell, yielding the INT6 scaling law: Penalty = 5.85 − 0.89 · log(sizeM) mBPB
Why This Matters
Tune your LR schedule once at FP16, then reuse it directly for INT8/INT6 QAT. No per-precision hyperparameter search needed — saving compute and simplifying deployment pipelines for small models.
Critical Edge
Weight-only quantisation (W*A16). Single architecture family, single dataset (FineWeb 10B). Results may not transfer to weight+activation quantisation (W4A4, W6A6) or different data distributions.

Core Results

  • Null result confirmed: wd33 optimal for FP16/INT8/INT6 at every (bit-width, size) cell — curves overlap within standard errors
  • INT4 regime transition: noise-dominated below 50M (no significant schedule preference); decisive wd33 optimum at 50M+ (paired z ≈ 12–15)
  • INT6 scaling law: log-linear penalty decrease with model size, 5/5 held-out predictions within 95% PIs
  • INT6 penalty grows with training: +3.0 → +4.0 → +6.5 mBPB across 9k→81k iterations at 30M

Ablations & Robustness

  • Optimiser swap (Muon → AdamW): AdamW underperforms by 33–42 mBPB, but null result preserved
  • Schedule shape (linear → cosine): Linear outperforms cosine by ~2 mBPB at 50M/100M; pattern identical under both
  • 9× longer training: Null result survives 81k iteration runs
  • Grid-snapping falsified: Weight-to-INT6-grid distances nearly identical across FP16/INT8/INT6 (ratio ≈ 1.04)

Strengths

  • Exceptionally rigorous factorial design: 1,345 cells, 5 seeds, paired tests
  • Honest self-correction of earlier INT4 boundary overclaim
  • Held-out scaling law validation (5 unseen sizes)
  • Comprehensive ablations: optimiser, shape, training length, size range
  • Transparent computational footprint (~225 kg CO₂eq)

Limitations

  • Weight-only quantisation — no activation quantisation tested
  • Single dataset (FineWeb 10B) and tokenizer (SP-BPE 8192)
  • Single architecture family (MobileLLM deep-thin decoder)
  • From-initialisation QAT only — continual QAT untested
  • INT4 boundary unspecified below 3M parameters
  • Underlying mechanism for schedule agnosticism remains open

Open Questions

  • Does the INT4 noise-dominated regime extend below 3M, or does a genuinely different optimal schedule emerge?
  • What is the actual mechanism for FP16/INT8/INT6 schedule agnosticism beyond grid-snapping?
  • Do results transfer to weight+activation quantisation (W4A4, W6A6)?
  • Does continual QAT (FP warm-start → quantised transition) change the interaction?
