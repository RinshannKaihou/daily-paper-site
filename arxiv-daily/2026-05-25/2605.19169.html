Modeling the Impact of Fiber Latency on Compute-Communication Overlap in Geo-Distributed Multi-Datacenter AI Training

Papavasileiou, Ioannis Prabhakar, Sairam Deo, Indu Kant Makovejs, Sergejs
Key Finding
Hollow-core fiber achieves 25% higher compute-communication overlap at 10–100 km inter-cluster distances in geo-distributed AI training

Discrete-event simulation reveals a narrow sweet spot for multi-datacenter training: clusters spaced 10–100 km apart maximize computation-communication overlap. Beyond 100 km, latency erodes gains. Hollow-core fiber — propagating light through air rather than glass — shifts the curve upward by 25%.

Core Contributions (核心贡献)

  • Uses discrete-event simulation to model fiber latency's impact on compute-communication overlap in data-parallel geo-distributed training
  • Quantifies optimal inter-cluster distance: 10–100 km window
  • Hollow-core fiber yields 25% overlap improvement in that window
  • Provides quantitative guidance for multi-datacenter network planning

Methodology

  • Approach: Discrete-event simulation (DES) modeling timing of computation and communication phases in data-parallel training
  • Scope: Two geographically separated AI clusters connected by fiber optic links
  • Variables: Fiber latency (distance × fiber type) as primary independent variable
  • Comparison: Standard single-mode fiber vs. hollow-core fiber (light in air/vacuum vs. glass)

Strengths

  • Directly addresses a practical deployment question: how far apart can training clusters be?
  • Hollow-core fiber is emerging tech; connecting it to AI training is timely
  • Clear, actionable output: distance sweet spot + quantified improvement

Limitations

  • Simulation-only — no real-world validation on actual training clusters
  • Abstract omits model architecture, framework, and communication primitive (AllReduce variant)
  • 10–100 km range may be workload-specific and not generalize
  • Data parallelism only; ignores tensor and pipeline parallelism

Critical Assessment

Novelty Moderate — hollow-core fiber angle is new, geo-distributed latency is not
Rigor Limited — simulation fidelity to real workloads not described
Reproducibility Unclear — no mention of open-sourced simulation code
Significance Practical for metro-area deployments, but short-range scope limits generality

Red Flags

  • Abstract is conclusion-heavy without describing training workload (model size, batch size, gradient strategy)
  • Hard to assess generality without workload specifics
  • 10–100 km covers metro-area only — not cross-region or cross-country

Open Questions

  1. How does the optimal distance change with model size (7B vs. 70B parameters) and batch size?
  2. Would results hold for tensor or pipeline parallelism with different communication-to-computation ratios?
  3. Cost-benefit tradeoff: hollow-core fiber vs. gradient compression or async training?
  4. Can results be validated on a real geo-distributed testbed?
  5. Sensitivity to link bandwidth (100 Gbps vs. 400 Gbps)?

Relevance (相关性)

这项研究与大规模分布式AI训练的基础设施规划直接相关，特别是对考虑多数据中心部署的训练集群具有参考价值。空心芯光纤的引入为降低通信延迟提供了新思路。
