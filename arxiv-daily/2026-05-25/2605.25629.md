> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了弱到强(W2S)奖励模型在分布内表现良好但跨偏好域迁移能力脆弱的问题，并提出了一种表示锚定(ANCHOR)正则化方法来提升分布外泛化性能。

> **TL;DR:** Weak-to-strong reward models can appear successful in-distribution but fail under preference-domain shift. The authors propose ANCHOR, a representation-anchoring regularizer that constrains hidden-state drift from the pretrained model during weak-supervised fine-tuning, consistently improving OOD transfer while maintaining competitive ID performance.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了零样本偏好域迁移评估协议，用于测试W2S奖励模型在未见偏好数据集上的泛化能力
- 揭示了现有W2S方法在分布内表现优异但跨域迁移脆弱的关键失败模式——弱监督微调会将强模型的表示空间拉向源域特定特征
- 提出Representation Anchoring (ANCHOR)方法，通过冻结预训练参考模型的隐藏状态约束来限制表示漂移
- 定义了三种迁移感知指标：WRG（分布内增益）、AOG（绝对OOD增益）、NTS（净迁移分数）

## Key Contributions

- A zero-shot preference-domain shift evaluation protocol for W2S reward modeling that trains on one preference domain and tests on held-out data from different datasets within the same category
- Empirical evidence that weak-supervised fine-tuning causes representational drift toward source-domain features, explaining why ID gains fail to transfer
- ANCHOR: a simple regularizer that constrains response-token hidden states toward a frozen pretrained reference model during training, discarded at inference
- Three transfer-aware metrics (WRG, AOG, NTS) that separately capture source-domain improvement, target-domain transfer, and net transfer after accounting for source regression

## Methodology

The method operates in three stages:

1. **Training Setup:** A weak teacher reward model $w_S$ is trained on gold labels from a source domain. It generates soft preference labels $q_i = \sigma(w_S(x, y_i^a) - w_S(x, y_i^b))$ for the strong student's training data.

2. **ANCHOR Objective:** The strong student $m_S$ is trained with a combined loss: $L(i) = L_{w2s}(i) + \lambda L_{anchor}(i)$. The weak preference loss $L_{w2s}$ trains the student to match soft teacher labels. The anchoring loss $L_{anchor}$ constrains the masked MSE distance between the student's and a frozen pretrained reference's hidden states at the final transformer layer.

3. **Evaluation Protocol:** Within each preference category (Helpful or Harmless), models are trained on one dataset and evaluated on all datasets in the category, including the held-out source test split (ID) and all other datasets (OOD).

Two variants are studied: **Last-Layer ANCHOR** (default, anchors the final layer) and **Middle-Layer ANCHOR** (anchors intermediate layers at positions $\lfloor0.5L\rfloor$ and $\lfloor0.75L\rfloor$).

Experiments use Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct → Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct and Qwen3-1.7B → Qwen3-8B, with datasets spanning Helpful (Anthropic Helpful, HelpSteer3, UltraFeedback) and Harmless (Anthropic Harmless, PKU-SafeRLHF, RAIL) categories.

## Results & Highlights

- **ID success hides OOD failure:** A student trained on HelpSteer3 achieves 75.74% ID accuracy but drops to 54.42% (near random) on Anthropic Helpful (Figure 1).
- **ANCHOR yields most consistent gains:** Across both model families and both preference categories, ANCHOR achieves the best or second-best performance on the majority of WRG, AOG, and NTS metrics.
- **SEAM's trade-off:** SEAM can achieve competitive OOD AOG but often at severe ID degradation (e.g., ID PGR drops to −1315.4% in the Qwen/Anthropic Helpful setting).
- **Naive/Confidence-based instability:** These methods show strong ID performance but unstable OOD transfer, with negative NTS in several settings.
- **Ablation findings:** Weaker anchoring ($\lambda = 10^{-4}$) consistently outperforms stronger anchoring ($\lambda = 10^{-2}$), suggesting overly strong constraints hinder adaptation. Last-Layer ANCHOR provides better overall balance than Middle-Layer ANCHOR.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are from National University of Singapore, VinUniversity, and Nanyang Technological University. The first two authors (Khoi Le, Tri Cao) contribute equally. The work is well-grounded in the W2S literature (Burns et al., 2023) and preference learning (Bradley-Terry, RLHF). The experimental design is thorough, spanning 2 model families, 6 datasets, 2 preference categories, and multiple baselines with 3 random seeds.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The evaluation protocol (zero-shot preference-domain shift for W2S) is a meaningful contribution. ANCHOR itself is conceptually simple — it is essentially a hidden-state MSE regularizer toward a frozen reference, reminiscent of continual learning and knowledge distillation techniques applied to a new context.
- **Rigor:** The transfer-aware metrics (WRG, AOG, NTS) are well-motivated. The paper clearly separates ID and OOD evaluation and accounts for source-domain regression. However, results are limited to offline pairwise accuracy, not downstream RLHF policy quality.
- **Reproducibility:** Code is linked (anonymous repo). LoRA hyperparameters are provided. Three random seeds are used. Good overall.
- **Significance:** The finding that ID W2S gains can be misleading is important for the alignment community. ANCHOR's gains are consistent but modest in some settings.
- **Red flags:** The paper does not evaluate whether improved reward model OOD transfer leads to better downstream policy alignment. PGR values are extremely large and negative in some cases, which may confuse readers. The method adds a full reference model forward pass during training, increasing VRAM cost — this overhead is acknowledged but not quantified.

## Strengths

- Identifies a real and previously underappreciated failure mode in W2S reward modeling
- Clean evaluation protocol with well-defined transfer-aware metrics
- ANCHOR is simple, easy to implement, and adds no inference overhead
- Comprehensive experiments across domains, datasets, and model families
- Honest about limitations (offline metrics only, computational overhead)

## Limitations

- Evaluation is limited to offline pairwise accuracy; does not test downstream RLHF policy optimization or generation quality
- ANCHOR adds a reference model forward pass during training, increasing memory and time costs
- Only tested on helpful/harmless preference categories and specific model scales (1B/1.7B teachers, 8B students)
- The anchoring coefficient $\lambda$ requires tuning; no automatic selection method is proposed
- PGR metric can produce extremely large negative values that are hard to interpret

## Relevance

本文对弱到强泛化在偏好学习中的鲁棒性进行了系统评估，揭示了分布内指标可能高估对齐可靠性的风险。对于关注RLHF、奖励模型泛化和可扩展监督的研究者具有直接参考价值。ANCHOR方法简单实用，可直接应用于现有的W2S奖励建模流程。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does improved OOD reward model transfer via ANCHOR lead to better downstream policy quality in online RLHF or best-of-n selection?
- How does ANCHOR interact with direct preference optimization (DPO) methods that bypass explicit reward modeling?
- Can the anchoring coefficient $\lambda$ be automatically calibrated based on source-domain signal strength or model capacity gap?
- Would ANCHOR remain effective at larger model scales (e.g., 70B students) or across more diverse preference axes beyond helpful/harmless?
- Is the representational drift failure mode specific to W2S, or does it also affect strong-supervised reward models under distribution shift?
