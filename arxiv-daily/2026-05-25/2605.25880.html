The Quantization Benefits of Residual-Free Transformers

Yiping Ji, Mahalakshmi Sabanayagam, Peyman Moghadam, Hemanth Saratchandran, Simon Lucey · AITC / DATA61 CSIRO
Residual connections make quantization lossy.
Removing them keeps activations Gaussian.
Key Metric
+6.6pp @ W8A6
Residual-free models hold 0.414 avg accuracy at W8A6 vs 0.348 for the best residual model — degrading only 0.021 from BF16, compared to 0.104.
Why This Matters
Shifts quantization from post-hoc patching to architectural design — a new axis for the accuracy–compressibility trade-off in transformer deployment.
Critical Edge
~150M params only
Tested at 24 layers / ~150M. Unproven at billion-parameter scale. Second-order optimizer (KL Shampoo) cost unbenchmarked. 1.7% full-precision gap. Only uniform quantization evaluated.

Theoretical Argument
  • Dense orthogonal mixing contracts excess kurtosis by O(1/d) — a fourth-moment central-limit effect
  • Residual addition preserves excess kurtosis at O(γx + 1/d), preventing Gaussianization
  • Spectral gradient descent preserves near-isometry; sign-based updates destroy it faster
  • All three components (residual-free + orthogonal init + spectral opt.) are necessary
Three-Part Recipe
01 / Orthogonal Init WVWO ≈ αI; WQ≈WK per head
02 / 2nd-Order Opt KL Shampoo (primary), SOAP, or Muon
03 / Depth Temp τℓ = β−ℓ, scaling softmax with layer depth
Strengths
  • Clean architectural identification of quantization difficulty, complementary to post-training methods
  • Elegant excess-kurtosis analysis connects architecture to activation statistics
  • Controlled experiments: same optimizer compared across architectures isolates the effect
  • Practical implications for distributed training communication compression
Limitations
  • 1.7% full-precision gap may matter for production deployments
  • Scalability beyond 150M params untested; deeper models are where residuals matter most
  • KL Shampoo is more expensive per step; no wall-clock comparison provided
  • Only simple uniform quantization tested; modern methods (SmoothQuant, GPTQ) unexplored
  • Language tasks only; no vision/multimodal evaluation

Excess kurtosis remains near zero throughout training for residual-free models, while residual models show monotonically increasing kurtosis — even with second-order optimizers (Residual + KL Shampoo: γ = 40,527).

Open Questions
  • How do residual-free transformers interact with modern quantization pipelines (QuaRot, SmoothQuant, GPTQ)?
  • Can findings scale to billion-parameter models where communication overhead is most severe?
  • Is there a middle-ground: partial residual connections (e.g., every other layer) with near-Gaussian activations?
  • Wall-clock training comparison: KL Shampoo on residual-free vs. AdamW on residual?
  • Transfer to ViTs or multimodal architectures where activation distributions may differ?
