> **一句话总结：** 通过1345组实验系统测试了量化感知训练中学习率调度是否依赖于量化精度，发现FP16/INT8/INT6的最优调度完全一致（33%线性warmdown），仅INT4在50M参数以下出现噪声主导的过渡区域。

> **TL;DR:** A 1345-run factorial study finds that the optimal LR schedule (33% warmdown) is bit-width-agnostic across FP16/INT8/INT6 for sub-100M decoder LMs. The null result survives changes in optimiser, schedule shape, and 9x longer training. INT4 shows a sharp regime transition at ~50M: decisive wd33 optimality above 50M vs. noise-dominated schedule preference below. The INT6 penalty follows a log-linear scaling law that correctly predicts 5 held-out sizes (5M–350M).

## 核心贡献

- **推翻核心假设：** FP16/INT8/INT6的最优warmdown比例均为33%，量化精度不影响最优学习率调度，无需为不同精度单独调参
- **INT6惩罚的对数线性缩放律：** INT6相对FP16的BPB惩罚随模型尺寸对数线性递减，在Phase 2数据上拟合后成功预测5个未见尺寸（5M–350M，70倍外推）
- **INT4精度边界的精细刻画：** INT4在50M以上wd33确定性最优（配对z≈12–15），50M以下无统计显著的调度偏好——是噪声主导区而非"wd10更优区"
- **排除网格吸附机制：** 权重到INT6网格的距离在FP16/INT8/INT6训练中几乎相同（比例≈1.04），排除了"量化权重提前收敛到网格"的解释
- **实用建议：** FP16上调好的调度可直接用于INT8/INT6 QAT；INT4在50M+用wd33；50M以下调度选择在噪声范围内

## Key Contributions

- **Primary hypothesis falsified at FP16/INT8/INT6:** A 720-run factorial grid shows the optimal warmdown fraction is 33% regardless of bit-width at every model size (15M–100M), with FP16/INT8/INT6 curves statistically indistinguishable
- **Log-linear INT6 scaling law with held-out validation:** INT6 penalty decreases log-linearly with model size; a fit on 4 Phase 2 sizes predicts 5 held-out Phase 5 sizes (5M, 8M, 175M, 250M, 350M) within 95% prediction intervals (5/5)
- **INT4 precision boundary characterization:** Two distinct regimes — a noise-dominated regime below 50M (no statistically significant schedule preference at any individual size) and a decisive wd33-optimal regime at 50M and above (paired z ≈ 12–15, 10/10 seed comparisons prefer wd33)
- **Negative mechanistic result:** Weight-to-INT6-grid distance is essentially identical for FP16, INT8, and INT6 models throughout training (ratio ≈ 1.04), ruling out rapid grid-snapping as the explanation for schedule bit-width-agnosticism
- **Robustness across ablations:** The null result survives switching optimiser (Muon → AdamW), schedule shape (linear → cosine), and 9x longer training (9k → 81k iterations)

## Methodology

**Architecture:** Decoder-only transformer with GQA (8 query / 4 KV heads), RMSNorm, ReLU² activation, RoPE, tied embeddings, and U-net skip connections — following MobileLLM's deep-thin recipe. Sizes range from 3M to 350M parameters.

**QAT setup:** Fake-quantisation with straight-through estimator (STE) applied from step 0 (no FP warm-start). Per-channel symmetric uniform quantisation. Four precision levels tested: FP16 (baseline), INT8 (256 levels), INT6 (~64 levels), INT4 (16 levels).

**LR schedule:** Warmup-Stable-Decay (WSD) with 100-step warmup. Four warmdown fractions: 0%, 10%, 33%, 50%. Default optimiser is Muon for matrix params + AdamW for scalars; Phase 5 D1 swaps to full AdamW.

**Experimental design — two phases:**
- **Phase 2 (720 runs):** 3 bit-widths × 4 warmdowns × 3 LR magnitudes × 4 sizes × 5 seeds at 9k iterations
- **Phase 5 (625 runs):** Seven ablations — optimiser swap (AdamW, 240 cells), longer training (27k/81k iters, 81 cells), cosine warmdown (60 cells), size extension to 5M–350M (75 cells), INT4 grid 15M–100M (80 cells), INT4 small 3M–10M (80 cells), and weight-to-grid snapshots (9 cells)

**Data:** FineWeb 10B corpus with SP-BPE tokenizer (vocab 8192). Validation metric is bits-per-byte (BPB). Statistical methodology uses paired per-seed differences with 95% CIs.

**Compute:** ~2,020 H100 GPU-hours on MareNostrum 5 ACC (BSC), EuroHPC AI Factory grant.

## Results & Highlights

- **Phase 2 null result:** At every (bit-width, size) cell, wd33 is optimal. The three bit-width curves overlap within standard errors. The primary hypothesis — that INT6 needs a different schedule — is falsified (Figure 1)
- **INT6 scaling law:** Penalty = +5.85 − 0.89·log(size_M) mBPB. Predictions at 5 held-out sizes all fall within 95% PIs (Table 2). The fit extrapolates across ~70x parameter range
- **INT6 penalty grows with training:** At 30M wd33, INT6−FP16 penalty grows from +3.0 mBPB (9k iters) to +4.0 mBPB (27k) to +6.5 mBPB (81k) — roughly doubles across 9x iteration range
- **INT4 regime transition (Table 3, Figure 3):**
  - Below 50M (6 sizes, 3M–30M): no significant schedule preference; mean penalty oscillates within seed noise
  - At 50M: penalty jumps to +3.7 mBPB (z ≈ 15), 0/5 seeds prefer wd10
  - At 100M: +4.9 mBPB (z ≈ 12), 0/5 seeds prefer wd10
  - Transition is sharp, co-located with largest drop in INT4 quality penalty (30M→50M: 35.9→20.4 mBPB)
- **AdamW vs Muon:** AdamW underperforms Muon by 33–42 mBPB across all sizes after LR recalibration; schedule × bit-width null result preserved under both optimisers
- **Linear > cosine warmdown:** Linear warmdown outperforms cosine by ~2 mBPB at 50M/100M (statistically significant), but bit-width pattern is identical under both shapes
- **Grid-snapping falsified (Figure 6):** Pre-warmdown weight-to-INT6-grid distance: FP16=0.0167, INT8=0.0166, INT6=0.0173 — all within 4%

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** First systematic mapping of LR schedule × bit-width interactions across INT8/INT6/INT4 in the 3M–350M from-initialisation QAT regime. The factorial design (1345 cells) is unusually thorough for a single-author study.
- **Rigor: Very high.** Pre-specified hypothesis, paired statistical tests with 95% CIs, held-out scaling-law validation, honest correction of earlier draft claims, and explicit multiple-comparison discussion. The correction of the INT4 boundary framing (from "wd10 wins at small models" to "noise-dominated below 50M") is commendable.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Architecture details, hyperparameters, seeds, and statistical methodology are fully specified. Uses public data (FineWeb) and public compute infrastructure. The 720-cell Phase 2 grid requires ~580 GPU-hours, which is accessible.
- **Significance: Moderate-to-high.** The practical recommendation — tune schedule once at FP16, reuse for INT8/INT6 — directly simplifies QAT pipelines. The scaling law and boundary characterization are informative for the sub-100M deployment community. The null result's value is primarily in preventing wasted tuning effort.
- **Red flags:** Single-author, single-dataset (FineWeb), single architecture family, weight-only quantisation (W*A16, no activation quantization). The INT4 boundary claim rests on 8 sizes with no testing below 3M. The mechanism question remains open — the paper rules out one candidate but does not confirm another.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally rigorous factorial design with 1345 cells, 5 seeds per condition, and paired statistical tests
- Honest self-correction: the INT4 boundary was re-analyzed from an earlier draft's overclaim to a more conservative framing
- Held-out validation of the scaling law (5 unseen sizes, 5/5 within 95% PI) demonstrates genuine predictive power
- Practical recommendations are concrete and narrowly scoped — directly useful for practitioners
- Comprehensive ablations (optimiser, schedule shape, training length, size range, INT4 sweep) strengthen the null result substantially
- Transparent computational footprint reporting with explicit assumptions

## Limitations

- **Weight-only quantisation:** Results may not transfer to weight+activation quantisation (W6A6, W4A4, etc.)
- **Single dataset and tokenizer:** All experiments use FineWeb 10B with SP-BPE 8192; different data distributions may yield different schedule interactions
- **Single architecture family:** Decoder-only with MobileLLM-style deep-thin design; encoder-decoder or alternative architectures are untested
- **From-initialisation QAT only:** Continual QAT (FP warm-start then transition) and gradual-quantisation curricula are not covered and may behave differently
- **INT4 boundary underspecified below 3M:** Whether the noise-dominated regime extends arbitrarily far down or a different schedule emerges at sub-3M scales is unknown
- **Mechanism unresolved:** Grid-snapping is ruled out, but the actual explanation for FP16/INT8/INT6 schedule agnosticism remains an open question
- **INT6 penalty scaling may flatten at large scale:** Residuals at 175M, 250M, 350M are all positive, suggesting the log-linear fit may overestimate the penalty decline at scale

## Relevance

本文对研究小模型量化和高效部署具有直接参考价值。对于关注边缘设备语言模型训练的研究者，该论文提供了明确的实用建议：FP16上调好的学习率调度可直接复用于INT8/INT6量化感知训练，无需额外调参。INT4的精度边界刻画（50M为过渡点）和INT6惩罚的对数线性缩放律为小模型量化策略设计提供了定量依据。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the INT4 noise-dominated regime extend below 3M, or does a genuinely different optimal schedule emerge at sub-3M scales?
2. What is the actual mechanism for FP16/INT8/INT6 schedule bit-width-agnosticism, given that grid-snapping is ruled out? Is it STE gradient signal equivalence, bit-width-invariant STE regularisation, or loss-landscape curvature properties?
3. Do these results transfer to weight+activation quantisation (e.g., W4A4, W6A6) where the quantisation noise budget is fundamentally different?
4. Does the observed interaction between Dremov et al. (2025) at 86M–2.2B (bit-width-dependent LR scaling) correspond to the large-scale analogue of the INT4 noise-dominated regime identified here?
5. How does continual QAT (FP warm-start → quantised transition) change the schedule × bit-width interaction compared to from-initialisation QAT?
