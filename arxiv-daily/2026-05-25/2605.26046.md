> **一句话总结：** 本文系统研究了多目标文本梯度提示优化的两种失败模式——优化时的梯度稀释和推理时的指令干扰，发现在10种配置中有6种无法超越初始提示。

> **TL;DR:** This paper identifies two separable failure modes in multi-criteria textual gradient prompt optimization for LLM judges: optimization-time gradient dilution (59% specificity drop when combining tasks) and inference-time instruction interference (−5.3% Spearman degradation even with oracle-optimal instructions). In 6 of 10 configurations, optimization never improves over the initial prompt.

## 核心贡献

- **系统性研究了多目标文本梯度优化的五种分解模式**（Single-task、SSS、SSC、SCC、CCC），覆盖从完全独立到完全联合优化的全部设计空间。
- **识别出两个独立的失败模式**：优化时的梯度稀释（gradient dilution）和推理时的指令干扰（instruction interference），二者作用于不同阶段，分别诊断。
- **提出了两个过程级诊断指标**：梯度特异性（gradient specificity）和反馈遵从度（feedback adherence），精确定位瓶颈在梯度质量而非优化器执行。
- **设计了 Oracle cherry-pick 实验**，证明即使每个任务的指令都是独立最优的，组合到一个提示中仍会导致性能下降（−0.053 Spearman）。

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive empirical study of five decomposition modes for multi-criteria textual gradient optimization on SummEval, covering the full spectrum from per-task to fully joint processing.
- Two process-level diagnostics (gradient specificity and feedback adherence) that localize the bottleneck to gradient quality rather than optimizer compliance — the optimizer faithfully follows gradient suggestions even when those suggestions are generic.
- An oracle cherry-pick experiment that isolates inference-time instruction interference as a failure mode distinct from optimization-time gradient dilution, showing that individually optimal per-task instructions degrade when combined.
- Identification of a sharp "dilution cliff": gradient specificity drops 59% (9.0 → 3.7) when moving from per-task to all-task gradient computation.

## Methodology

The authors implement a 4-stage TextGrad-style optimization pipeline:

1. **Task Model** (Qwen3-8B): Predicts dimension scores using current prompt.
2. **Loss LLM** (Qwen3-235B-A22B, T=0.3): Critiques predictions against ground-truth annotations, producing natural-language loss per example.
3. **Gradient LLM** (Qwen3-235B-A22B, T=0.3): Aggregates per-example losses into textual gradients — structured instruction-level edit suggestions.
4. **Optimizer LLM** (Qwen3-235B-A22B, T=0.7): Rewrites per-task instructions based on the gradient.

**Decomposition modes** are encoded as a 3-letter code (S=Separate, C=Combined) for [loss, gradient, optimizer]:
- **Single**: Completely independent runs per task
- **SSS**: All three stages per-task
- **SSC**: Loss and gradient per-task, optimizer receives all gradients jointly
- **SCC**: Only loss per-task; gradient LLM processes all tasks; optimizer joint
- **CCC**: All stages fully joint

**Dataset**: SummEval with 1,600 summary pairs scored on 4 dimensions (fluency, relevance, coherence, consistency). Split: 160 training, 480 evaluation.

**Validation strategies**: MAE validation (reject regressions) vs. no validation.

**Runs**: N=3 independent trials, 12 optimization steps each. Primary metric: task-averaged Spearman ρ.

**Diagnostics**: Claude Sonnet 4.6 rates gradient specificity (1–10, how focused on one task) and feedback adherence (1–10, whether optimizer follows gradient).

## Results & Highlights

- **6 of 10 configurations** never exceed the initial generic prompt ("Rate from 1 to 5").
- Only **Single-task with MAE validation** achieves meaningful improvement: +0.031 Spearman at step 2.
- **Gradient dilution**: Per-task modes (Single, SSS, SSC) achieve mean specificity 9.0; all-task modes (SCC, CCC) drop to 3.7 — a 59% reduction with zero overlap between groups.
- **Feedback adherence** is uniformly high (7.8–8.8), confirming the optimizer follows suggestions regardless of gradient quality.
- **Oracle cherry-pick**: Best individually-optimized instructions, when combined, achieve 0.232 avg Spearman — 0.053 below the generic baseline (0.285).
- **Hypervolume indicator** grows for CCC (+6.9%), indicating the optimizer discovers diverse specialist prompts even when average performance stagnates.
- Primary mechanism for inference-time interference: **instruction-length asymmetry** — optimized fluency instructions expand to ~800 words while relevance stays ~4 words, causing disproportionate attention.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate-High)**: First systematic study of multi-criteria textual gradient optimization for LLM judges. The decomposition framework and two-failure-mode diagnosis are genuinely novel. However, the negative result (it doesn't work) limits immediate practical impact.
- **Rigor (High)**: Well-controlled experiments with clear ablations. The oracle cherry-pick experiment is an elegant design to isolate inference-time interference. Using an external LLM (Claude) for diagnostics could be a confound, but the authors acknowledge this transparently.
- **Reproducibility (High)**: All evaluation prompts provided in appendices. Fixed seeds, clear model specifications, code promised.
- **Significance (Moderate)**: The findings are important for the prompt optimization community but are limited to a single benchmark (SummEval) with N=3 runs. The authors appropriately limit claims to effect sizes robust at this sample size.
- **Red flags**: N=3 runs limits statistical power. Single benchmark (SummEval) constrains generalizability. The paper is primarily a negative result — it diagnoses why something fails but does not provide a working solution.

## Strengths

- Clean, systematic experimental design spanning the full decomposition space.
- The two-failure-mode decomposition (optimization-time vs. inference-time) is conceptually clear and empirically well-supported.
- The oracle cherry-pick experiment is particularly compelling — it proves the problem isn't just bad optimization, but fundamental interference at inference time.
- Honest reporting of negative results with appropriate effect-size-based claims given small N.
- Process-level diagnostics (specificity, adherence) are algorithm-agnostic and reusable.

## Limitations

- **Single benchmark**: Only SummEval with 4 criteria. Whether failure modes generalize to other tasks, criteria counts, or domains is unknown.
- **Small sample size**: N=3 runs limits statistical confidence; authors acknowledge this.
- **No solution proposed**: The paper diagnoses failure modes but does not demonstrate a working mitigation.
- **LLM-based diagnostics**: Gradient specificity and adherence are scored by Claude, introducing evaluator-bias confound.
- **Limited optimization paradigms**: Only TextGrad-style optimization tested; OPRO, GEPA, evolutionary methods may exhibit different dynamics.

## Relevance

本文对LLM评估领域的提示优化研究具有重要参考价值。识别出的梯度稀释和指令干扰两个失败模式，对设计多目标LLM评委系统的架构选择有直接指导意义，提示我们在实际应用中需要谨慎处理多标准联合优化的设计。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Would increasing the number of evaluation criteria (e.g., 8+ dimensions as in MT-Bench) amplify gradient dilution linearly or exhibit a phase transition?
2. Can length-aware instruction normalization (proposed in future work) fully mitigate inference-time instruction interference, or is interference an inherent property of multi-criterion prompts?
3. Do the same failure modes appear with population-based methods (e.g., NSGA-II) that avoid textual gradients entirely?
4. How do the failure modes interact with different base models (e.g., GPT-4, Claude, Gemini) — is the 59% specificity drop model-dependent?
5. Could task-specific attention masking during inference (generating scores per criterion independently) eliminate instruction interference at acceptable cost?
