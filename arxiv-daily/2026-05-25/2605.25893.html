Key Finding

D²-Monitor: Dynamic Safety Monitoring for Diffusion LLMs via Hesitation-Aware Routing

Detecting "hesitation" in denoising trajectories lets a ≤0.85 M-parameter monitor outperform 1.59 M baselines on safety classification.

LLaDA-8B · 1.5 · 2.0-mini WildGuardMix · ToxicChat · OpenAI-Moderation Oxford & CUHK-SZ
Best Accuracy (WildGuardMix) 88.6% +1.2 pp vs best baseline (87.4%)
Effective Params ≤0.54M 3× fewer params than TimeAttn baseline (1.59M)

D-LLMs Tested 4 LLaDA-8B-Base, -Instruct, 1.5, 2.0-mini
Why This Matters
Diffusion LLMs like Mercury are hitting 1,009 tok/s — an order of magnitude faster than autoregressive models. As D-LLMs move toward production, deployable safety monitoring becomes critical. D²-Monitor's ≤0.85M footprint makes real-time safety gating feasible even on edge devices, turning a trajectory-level curiosity ("hesitation") into a practical difficulty signal that dynamically allocates compute.
Critical Edge
Scale ceiling. Tested only on D-LLMs up to 16B parameters. Whether hesitation patterns persist at 70B+ is unknown.

Adversarial blind spot. Authors acknowledge attackers could craft inputs that deliberately suppress hesitation steps, evading second-stage detection.

Threshold sensitivity. Both τ (hesitation boundary) and λ (routing threshold) require per-model tuning; sensitivity analysis is provided but the practical tuning burden could grow with model diversity.
How It Works

Bi-Level Hesitation-Aware Routing

  • Train linear probes on D-LLM hidden states; compute signed margins ds at each denoising step.
  • Steps where |ds| < τ are hesitation steps. Their count nτ predicts probe failure.
  • Base probe (linear) runs always → estimates nτ + classifies easy cases via majority vote.
  • If nτ exceeds threshold λ → route to advanced probe (MLP or temporal attention) trained exclusively on hesitation trajectories.
  • Expected cost: E[P] = |θLP| + ρ · |θg| where ρ is the routed fraction.
Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • First to exploit D-LLM trajectory structure for safety monitoring.
  • Principled bi-level design — hesitation as a genuine difficulty proxy.
  • Orders of magnitude smaller than LLM-as-monitor approaches.
  • Consistent improvements across all 4 D-LLMs, intra- and cross-dataset.

Limitations

  • D-LLM-specific; does not transfer to autoregressive LLMs.
  • Unverified beyond 16B parameters.
  • τ and λ need per-model tuning.
