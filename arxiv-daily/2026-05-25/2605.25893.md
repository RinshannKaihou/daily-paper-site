> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 D²-Monitor，一种针对扩散语言模型的双层级安全监控系统，通过检测去噪轨迹中的"犹豫"信号来动态分配计算资源，在极低参数量（≤0.85M）下实现了最优的安全分类效果。

> **TL;DR:** D²-Monitor is a bi-level safety monitor for diffusion LLMs that detects "hesitation" in the multi-step denoising trajectory—where intermediate hidden states fall near the probe decision boundary—and routes uncertain samples to a heavier probe. It achieves state-of-the-art safety classification across 3 datasets and 4 D-LLMs with ≤0.85M parameters.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统性地研究扩散语言模型（D-LLMs）的安全监控问题，发现了去噪轨迹中存在"safety hesitation"（安全犹豫）现象：中间隐状态反复落在探针决策边界附近的小范围内。
- 证明了犹豫步数（hesitation severity）可以作为样本难度的有效代理指标，准确预测轻量级探针何时会分类失败。
- 提出 D²-Monitor 双层级动态路由框架：轻量级线性探针作为常驻监控器同时估计犹豫程度和执行基础分类，当犹豫程度超过阈值时激活更强大的高级探针，实现计算资源的高效分配。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic study of safety monitoring specifically for diffusion LLMs (D-LLMs), identifying the "safety hesitation" phenomenon in denoising trajectories where intermediate hidden states repeatedly fall near the probe's decision boundary.
- Demonstrates that hesitation severity (number of low-margin steps) serves as an effective proxy for sample difficulty, predicting when a lightweight probe will fail with higher granularity than binary or probe-extrinsic signals.
- Proposes D²-Monitor, a bi-level dynamic routing framework: a lightweight linear probe acts as an always-on monitor jointly estimating hesitation and performing base classification; when hesitation exceeds a threshold, a heavier advanced probe is activated for second-stage classification.
- Achieves state-of-the-art safety classification on WildGuardMix, ToxicChat, and OpenAI-Moderation across 4 D-LLMs (LLaDA-8B-Base, LLaDA-8B-Instruct, LLaDA-1.5, LLaDA-2.0-mini) with ≤0.85M parameters, showing the best effectiveness-efficiency trade-off among 8 baselines.

## Methodology

**Problem setting:** D-LLMs generate text through a multi-step denoising process (e.g., S steps), producing intermediate hidden states h₁, …, hₛ at each step. Unlike AR-LLMs, these trajectories expose richer intermediate information useful for safety monitoring.

**Hesitation discovery (Section 3):** The authors train linear probes on D-LLM hidden states and compute signed margins dₛ at each step. Steps with |dₛ| < τ are "hesitation steps." The count nτ of such steps is shown to strongly predict probe failure—trajectories with more hesitation steps achieve substantially lower F1 scores, and this signal is more informative than entropy- or confidence-based alternatives.

**D²-Monitor design (Section 4):**
1. **Stage 1 (Training data preparation):** K-fold out-of-fold scoring to obtain unbiased margin estimates for all training trajectories; collect hesitation trajectories (nτ > 0) for advanced probe training.
2. **Stage 2 (Probe training):** Base linear probe trained on all trajectories; advanced probe (MLP or temporal attention) trained exclusively on hesitation trajectories using hidden states within the minimal contiguous hesitation window.
3. **Stage 3 (Cascade inference):** Base probe computes margins → derive nτ → if nτ ≤ λ, classify via majority voting; if nτ > λ, route to advanced probe for second-stage classification.

**Expected parameter cost:** E[P] = |θ_LP| + ρ · |θ_g|, where ρ is the fraction routed to the advanced probe.

## Results & Highlights

- **Intra-dataset (WildGuardMix):** D²-TimeAttn achieves 88.6% accuracy / 88.3% F1 on LLaDA-8B-Base (vs. best baseline 87.4% / 87.0%), with E[P] ≤ 0.54M (vs. TimeAttn baseline at 1.59M).
- **Intra-dataset (ToxicChat):** D²-MLP achieves 96.9% accuracy / 87.9% F1 on LLaDA-8B-Base, outperforming all baselines including LSTM (96.6% / 86.7%) at roughly half the effective parameter cost.
- **Cross-dataset generalization:** Trained on WildGuardMix, tested on ToxicChat and OpenAI-Moderation—D²-Monitor consistently outperforms all baselines, with D²-TimeAttn reaching 87.5% / 71.0% F1 on ToxicChat (LLaDA-8B-Base) vs. TimeAttn baseline at 87.1% / 70.8%, while using fewer parameters.
- **Effectiveness-efficiency Pareto frontier:** D²-Monitor variants sit at the top-left of the F1 vs. E[P] plot (Figure 1), achieving the best trade-off among all methods.
- Results hold across all 4 D-LLMs tested, including the 16B-parameter LLaDA-2.0-mini.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. This is the first work to exploit D-LLM trajectory-level signals (hesitation) for safety monitoring. The insight that multi-step denoising exposes uncertainty information unavailable in AR-LLMs is genuinely new and well-motivated.

**Rigor:** Strong experimental methodology with 3 datasets, 4 D-LLM backbones, 8 baselines, both intra- and cross-dataset evaluation, and multiple metrics (Acc, F1, E[P], plus F2, FRR, inference time, FLOPs in appendix). The out-of-fold scoring strategy prevents training data leakage.

**Reproducibility:** Good. All hyperparameter search spaces are detailed. Datasets and models are publicly available. Code does not appear to be released yet, but the method description is sufficiently detailed.

**Significance:** Practically important. As D-LLMs gain traction (Mercury achieving 1009 tokens/sec), safety monitoring will be critical. The lightweight design (≤0.85M params) makes deployment feasible even on edge devices.

**Red flags:**
- Limited to D-LLMs up to 16B parameters; unclear if hesitation patterns hold at larger scales.
- Acknowledges vulnerability to adaptive adversaries who might craft inputs that suppress hesitation steps.
- The τ and λ thresholds require tuning; sensitivity analysis is provided but the dependence on good threshold selection could be a practical concern.

## Strengths

- Novel and well-motivated exploitation of D-LLM trajectory structure for safety monitoring.
- Elegant bi-level design that is both principled (hesitation as difficulty proxy) and practical (dynamic compute allocation).
- Extremely compact parameter footprint—orders of magnitude smaller than LLM-as-monitor approaches.
- Strong empirical results with consistent improvements across all settings.
- Good generality: works across different D-LLM architectures and generalizes cross-dataset.

## Limitations

- Experiments are limited to D-LLMs ≤ 16B parameters; scalability to larger models is unverified.
- The framework is specific to D-LLMs with multi-step denoising trajectories and does not directly apply to AR-LLMs.
- Adaptive adversaries could potentially craft prompts that minimize hesitation steps to evade the second-stage probe.
- The hesitation threshold τ and routing threshold λ are hyperparameters that need tuning per model/dataset combination.
- False rejection rate (FRR) on benign inputs is a practical concern, though reported in the appendix.

## Relevance

本文与扩散语言模型安全性和高效推理部署高度相关。D²-Monitor 提出的犹豫感知路由机制为 D-LLM 的安全部署提供了一种轻量级且高效的解决方案，尤其适用于资源受限的边缘部署场景。其核心思想——利用模型内部轨迹的不确定性信号来动态分配计算资源——对安全监控和模型部署均有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does hesitation severity correlate with specific types of safety violations (e.g., hate speech vs. self-harm vs. violence)? Could category-aware routing further improve performance?
2. Can the hesitation-aware routing principle be adapted to AR-LLMs by analyzing uncertainty across decoding steps or intermediate layers?
3. What are the adversarial robustness properties of D²-Monitor? How easily can an adversary suppress hesitation steps while maintaining the harmful content?
4. How does the framework scale to D-LLMs beyond 16B parameters (e.g., LLaDA 100B)? Do hesitation patterns remain consistent?
5. Could hesitation signals be used not just for monitoring but also for guiding the denoising process itself (e.g., early termination or adaptive step count)?
