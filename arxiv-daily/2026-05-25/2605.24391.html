MX-SAFE: Versatile Inference- and Training-Proof Microscaling Format with On-the-Fly Exponent and Mantissa Bit Allocation

Park, Dahoon · Koo, Jahyun · Hwang, Sangwoo · Kung, Jaeha — Korea University & DGIST
Key Finding

One 8-bit block. Two modes. Zero format-switching. 24.9% less energy.

Key Metric
24.9%
energy reduction vs BF16
0.17% inference drop · <0.5% training drop
65nm CMOS, 250MHz synthesis
Why This Matters

A single microscaling format for both training and inference eliminates format-switching infrastructure. Directly targets the OCP MX standard — if adopted, simplifies the entire deployment pipeline from research to production.

Critical Edge
  • Only tested on LLaMA-3.2-3B — 70B+ scale unknown
  • Synthesis-only on 65nm; no silicon or FPGA validation
  • Energy model is analytical (BitMoD), not measured

Methodology

Error Modeling
Closed-form quantization error bounds for MXINT and MXFP. Proves E2M5 beats MXINT8 when exponent distance ≥ 2.
Stability Profiling
Underflow ratio analysis across training epochs on ResNet-18. Formats with <3 exponent bits diverge.
MXSF Format
Se−ex < 3 → E2M5 (normal). Se−ex ≥ 3 → E3M2 sub-FP with bias=10. Repurposes subnormal encoding.
Tile Blocking
2D 8×8 tiles replace 1×64 blocks. Backward pass reuses quantized block, cutting 2 of 6 re-quantization steps.
Accelerator
SAFE-MAC: E4M5 multiplier + FP12 adder tree. 4×4 systolic tensor array, 65nm/250MHz.

Results & Highlights

Inference (ImageNet) +0.17% avg drop vs BF16 across 7 models
LLM (LLaMA-3.2-3B) 73.03% BoolQ — vs E4M3's 70.46%
Training (ResNet-18) 68.4% vs BF16 69.0%, E4M3 65.3%
Training (MobileNetV2) 69.5% vs BF16 69.9%, E4M3 63.2%
VLM (nanoVLM-222M) Best MMStar accuracy among all MX formats
vs MXFP8_E4M3 ~36% E4M3 catastrophically fails on lightweight CNNs

Strengths

  • Unified format eliminates training/inference format-switching
  • Negligible accuracy loss vs BF16 across CNNs, ViTs, LLMs, VLMs
  • Solves MXFP8_E4M3 catastrophic failure on lightweight models
  • Hardware-efficient decoding — check 2 MSB bits for mode selection
  • Comprehensive evaluation spanning 5 model families

Limitations

  • 3B-param LLM ceiling — 70B+ behavior unknown
  • E3M2 has only 2 mantissa bits — precision ceiling near threshold
  • No silicon or FPGA validation — synthesis estimates only
  • 65nm node is dated for modern accelerator comparisons
  • Energy model is analytical (BitMoD), not measured silicon data

Critical Assessment

Novelty
Clever repurposing of subnormal encoding space. Simple but well-motivated dual-mode design.
Rigor
Analytical error models from first principles, validated empirically across 5 task domains.
Reproducibility
Builds on Microsoft's open-source MX framework. Algorithm 1 is explicit enough to re-implement.
Significance
Directly relevant to OCP MX standard. Could simplify training+inference deployment at datacenter scale.
Red Flags
Single LLM tested. No silicon. Transparent about block-size favorability toward baseline.

Open Questions

How does MXSF scale to 70B+ parameter models with long-context inference?
Does the 8×8 tile generalize across all architectures, or is block-size tuning needed?
Can the E3M2 bias (fixed at 10) be learned or adapted per-layer for further gains?
What is the area/energy overhead of the MXSF Converter (1.93% power) vs pure E2M5?
How does MXSF interact with sparsity or structured pruning?
