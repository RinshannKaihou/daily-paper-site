> **一句话总结：** 本文提出基于SVD的操作级子空间提取框架，在1,800对受控NLI对比数据上证明LLM不仅编码推理标签，还部分编码产生该标签的语义操作（否定/反义等），其子空间选择性达ρ=1.16–2.07、线性可解码达84.8–99.0%，但几何可分性与因果可操控性可解离——Gemma-4B选择性最高(ρ=2.07)却几乎无法被激活操控。

> **TL;DR:** This paper investigates whether decoder-only LLMs (Qwen3-1.7B, Phi-3-mini-4k-instruct, Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, Gemma3-4B) encode the semantic *operations* producing NLI labels, not just the labels themselves. Using 1,800 controlled premise-hypothesis pairs differing by one of six semantic transformations, the authors extract operation-level subspaces via truncated SVD and validate causal relevance via bidirectional activation steering. Transformation effects are decodable at 84.8–99.0% accuracy (above random-subspace baselines), and steering causally shifts predictions in 3 of 4 models — but Gemma-4B (highest selectivity ρ=2.07) shows near-zero flip-to-target, demonstrating a dissociation between geometric separability and causal steerability.

## 核心贡献

- 提出一套机制可解释性框架：对比式变换对构造 + 操作级SVD子空间识别 + 双向激活操控 + logit归因，用于分析语义变换作为表征几何效应。
- 构建了1,800对受控NLI对比数据，覆盖六种语义操作（Negation、Antonymy、Quantification、Abstraction、Specification、Co-hyponymy），每操作300对，由Claude Sonnet 4.5结构化生成并嵌入校验。
- 实证证明变换激活差分在四个1.7–4B开源decoder模型上线性可解码（84.8–99.0%），远超随机子空间基线，且在3/4模型上可被激活操控因果地影响预测。
- 揭示子空间选择性与跨操作独立性之间的解离，以及前向/逆向操控的不对称性，约束了对变换级表征的机制性解释。

## Key Contributions

- A mechanistic framework combining contrastive transformation-pair construction, operation-specific subspace identification via truncated SVD, bidirectional activation steering, and logit attribution (DLA) to analyse semantic transformations as geometric effects in model representations.
- A controlled 1,800-pair contrastive NLI dataset spanning six semantic operations (Negation, Antonymy, Quantification, Abstraction, Specification, Co-hyponymy), with paired-isolation enforced through structured generation and validation.
- Empirical evidence that transformation activation differences are linearly decodable (84.8–99.0%) and causally steerable in 3 of 4 models, showing LLMs encode not only label outcomes but also, in part, the operations producing them.
- A documented dissociation between subspace selectivity and cross-operation independence, plus an asymmetry between forward and inverse steering, both constraining mechanistic accounts of transformation-level representation.

## Methodology

The paper studies four pretrained decoder-only transformers at the 1.7–4B scale: **Qwen3-1.7B**, **Phi-3-mini-4k-instruct**, **Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct**, and **Gemma3-4B-Instruct** (no task-specific fine-tuning). Activations are extracted via TransformerLens hooks (`resid_post`, `attn_out`, `mlp_out`).

**Data:** For each of six operations, 300 contrastive NLI pairs `(x_i, T_o(x_i))` are generated with Claude Sonnet 4.5 using structured prompts with operation-specific constraints and embedded validation checks, producing 1,800 pairs across six thematic domains. Each clean pair expresses entailment; the modified hypothesis changes exactly one semantic dimension (e.g., negation flips to contradiction; abstraction/specification flips to neutral).

**Subspace estimation:** For each layer `l`, compute activation deltas `Δ_o^(l)(x_i) = h^(l)(T_o(x_i)) − h^(l)(x_i)`, collect N deltas into `D_o`, mean-centre, and apply truncated SVD (`D̃_o = UΣV^⊤`) to obtain the top-k right singular vectors defining an orthonormal basis `B_o^(l)` for the operation subspace `S_o^(l)` (rank k=4; trends similar at k∈{2,8}).

**Separability** is quantified by a selectivity ratio `ρ = E[‖proj_{S_o} Δ_o‖²] / E[‖proj_{S_o} Δ_{o'}‖²]` (ρ≫1 ⇒ dedicated subspace), with one-sided permutation tests (1000 random orthonormal subspaces). Classification uses 6-way logistic regression with 5-fold stratified cross-validation against random-subspace controls.

**Causal intervention (RQ2):** Two steering vectors — principal variance direction (top singular vector of `D_o`) and mean-shift (CAA) direction `v_o = E[Δ_o]/‖E[Δ_o]‖`. Residual stream modified as `h^(l)(x) ← h^(l)(x) + α·v_o` at α∈{5,10,20}; forward regime shifts clean inputs toward the operation's target label, inverse regime restores the clean prediction. Primary metric: flip-to-target rate, compared to norm-matched random directions.

**Localisation (RQ4):** Layer-wise logit decoding via the unembedding matrix (`logit_lens = W_u · h_resid^(l)`) and direct logit attribution decomposing the final logit difference into attention + MLP contributions per layer.

## Results & Highlights

- **Transformation effects are linearly decodable at 84.8–99.0% accuracy** across the four models (best layer per model-component), substantially above random-subspace baselines (Tab. 8).
- **Peak subspace selectivity ranges ρ=1.16–2.07** (all p≤0.009 vs. 1000-permutation null); e.g., Phi-3 MLP ρ=1.90, Gemma-4B MLP ρ=2.07; the residual stream is consistently weakest across models.
- **Activation steering causally shifts predictions in 3 of 4 models** — but Gemma-4B, despite having the strongest peak selectivity (ρ=2.07), shows **near-zero flip-to-target under both CAA and SVD steering**, demonstrating geometric separability does not imply causal accessibility.
- **Per-operation selectivity is heterogeneous:** Abstraction ρ̄=2.13 (highest), Quantification ρ̄=1.96, Co-hyponymy ρ̄=1.88, Specification ρ̄=1.21; **Negation and Antonymy fall below ρ=1** in several configurations, indicating overlap with other subspaces.
- **CAA vs SVD:** CAA outperforms SVD in 20 of 33 non-tied comparisons (win rate 0.606), but the pooled advantage is not statistically significant (Δ̄=0.066, p=0.157); Phi-3 favours CAA in both regimes, Qwen-1.7B inverts (CAA leads forward but SVD wins inverse on all six operations).
- **Cross-operation interference is sparse but structured:** the five largest off-diagonal contamination values at α=20 are Specification→Antonymy (0.112), Specification→Quantification (0.095), Co-hyponymy→Antonymy (0.083), Quantification→Abstraction (0.081), Specification→Abstraction (0.076).
- **Label evidence emerges mid-to-late:** peak logit differences occur at relative depths 0.86–0.94; Gemma-4B shows a distinct three-phase trajectory (early rise, mid-network suppression at 0.60–0.65, strong late amplification at 0.75–0.85). Component pathways are architecture-dependent: Phi-3 is attention-driven, Gemma-4B is MLP-dominant, Qwen-3B is balanced.

## Critical Assessment

- **Identifiability confound is acknowledged but not fully closed:** operations are partially aligned with NLI labels in the controlled dataset (Negation/Antonymy/Quantification → contradiction; Abstraction/Specification/Co-hyponymy → neutral), so the high decoding accuracy could partly reflect label information. The authors mitigate via within-label prediction, label-subspace residualisation, and label-aware contamination controls (App. D.9), but concede that *fully crossing every operation with every NLI label remains future work* — leaving a genuine identifiability gap.
- **A weak/missing baseline for the causal claim:** the steering experiments use norm-matched random directions as the control, but Gemma-4B's near-zero flip-to-target shows the framework cannot explain *why* a highly selective subspace is not steerable; no positive control demonstrates that residual-stream steering works on this model for any property.
- **Dataset is LLM-generated and artificially simple:** premises range 7–18 tokens (median 10); the controlled, short, lexical-entailment-only universe may inflate decodability (84.8–99.0%) relative to naturalistic inference (e.g., SNLI/MultiNLI). Generalisation to real NLI distributions is untested.
- **No circuit-level localisation:** the paper explicitly states it "identifies geometric structure and causal relevance but does not localise the underlying circuits" — the subspace-to-component mapping (DLA) is correlational.
- **Scale limited to 1.7–4B:** generalisation to larger models or encoder-only/specialised NLI systems is left open.

## Strengths

- Clean controlled experimental design with paired isolation and proper random-subspace / random-direction baselines rather than relying on decoding accuracy alone.
- Principled grounding in natural logic (MacCartney and Manning, 2009), linking the six operations to formal relation types.
- Causal validation via bidirectional activation steering, including both CAA and SVD-derived directions and an explicit asymmetry analysis.
- Calibrated claims — the authors repeatedly stress "in part," "partially distinct," and architecture-dependence, and explicitly publish the dissociation (Gemma-4B) as a finding rather than hiding it.

## Limitations

- Controlled short-sentence NLI pairs (LLM-generated by Claude Sonnet 4.5) may not reflect the complexity of natural inference; generalisation to real-world text is uncertain.
- Only four 1.7–4B decoder-only models; no encoder-decoder, no large-scale (7B+) models.
- The CAA pooled advantage is not statistically significant (p=0.157), weakening claims about which direction type is preferable.
- Steerability varying across models is reported but not mechanistically explained.
- Operation subspaces overlap (Negation/Antonymy below ρ=1), so representations are not fully factorised, limiting precision of operation-level control.

## Relevance

直接映射到「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」(机制可解释性、激活操控、子空间几何)与「ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」(线性子空间与因果归因的数学基础)两个研究方向；对理解LLM内部如何编码语义操作、以及为何几何可分性不等价于因果可操控性，提供了对模型可靠性分析有借鉴意义的证据。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do operation-level subspaces and the selectivity-vs-steerability dissociation change at larger scale (e.g., 7B–70B) or for encoder-decoder architectures?
- Can the Gemma-4B "selective but not steerable" paradox be resolved by steering in MLP subspaces rather than the residual stream, given its MLP-dominant three-phase logit trajectory?
- Do the identified operation subspaces generalise from controlled short sentences to naturalistic NLI (SNLI, MultiNLI, ANLI), where annotation artefacts and longer contexts dominate?
- Could the partially-aligned operations↔labels confound be fully removed by generating an all-operations×all-labels crossing, and would decoding accuracy survive?
- Can operation-level steering be combined with circuit-level methods (ACDC, attribution patching) to move from subspace identification to mechanistic localisation?
