> **一句话总结：** DVAO 提出了一种基于经验奖励方差的动态权重调节方法，用于多奖励 GRPO 训练中的优势组合，在数学推理和工具使用任务上同时实现了更高的准确率和更好的约束满足率。

> **TL;DR:** DVAO dynamically adjusts multi-reward combination weights based on per-objective empirical reward variance in GRPO, mathematically guaranteeing bounded advantage magnitudes and implicit cross-objective regularization. It achieves a superior Pareto frontier on math reasoning and tool-use benchmarks with Qwen3 and Qwen2.5 models.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了多奖励 GRPO 中两种主流标量化方法的理论缺陷：奖励组合（RC）导致优势幅度爆炸和训练不稳定；优势组合（AC）依赖静态超参数且忽略目标间相关性。
- 提出 DVAO，利用 rollout 组内各目标的奖励方差动态调整组合权重，无需手动调参。
- 从数学上证明 DVAO 的优势幅度严格有界（Proposition 2），并具有自适应的跨目标正则化机制（Proposition 3）。
- 在数学推理（AIME、MATH500、OlympiadBench、AMC23）和工具使用（BFCL-v4）基准上，DVAO 在准确率与长度/格式约束上同时达到最优。

## Key Contributions

- Theoretically exposes the fundamental flaws of Reward Combination (magnitude explosion leading to training instability) and Advantage Combination (static weights, objective isolation ignoring cross-correlations) in multi-reward GRPO.
- Proposes DVAO, a fully dynamic, hyperparameter-free weighting scheme that up-weights high-variance objectives (stronger learning signal) and suppresses low-variance noisy objectives within each rollout group.
- Mathematically proves that DVAO maintains bounded advantage magnitudes (Proposition 2: pointwise ≤ RC advantage) and introduces an implicit cross-objective regularization mechanism via the sensitivity analysis (Proposition 3: gradient of each objective modulated by overall multi-objective performance).
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results across math reasoning and tool-use benchmarks, achieving the highest average on both accuracy and auxiliary compliance simultaneously — the only method to do so across all model scales.

## Methodology

### Problem Setting

In multi-reward GRPO, a policy model πθ generates G rollouts per query. Each rollout receives n reward signals (e.g., accuracy + length). The standard approaches are:

- **Reward Combination (RC):** Linearly combines raw rewards, then normalizes the combined reward into an advantage. Proposition 1 proves this produces larger squared advantage magnitudes than AC.
- **Advantage Combination (AC):** Independently normalizes each reward into per-objective advantages, then combines with fixed weights wk.

### DVAO Formulation

DVAO replaces fixed weights with dynamic variance-adaptive weights:

$$\tilde{w}_k = \frac{w_k \sigma_k^i}{\sum_l w_l \sigma_l^i}$$

where σ_k^i is the empirical standard deviation of the k-th reward within the rollout group for query x_i. The DVAO advantage is:

$$A^{(i,j)}_{DVAO} = \sum_k \tilde{w}_k A_k^{(i,j)}$$

### Theoretical Properties

1. **Proposition 1:** RC advantage squared magnitude ≥ AC advantage squared magnitude, with equality only when all reward pairs are perfectly correlated.
2. **Proposition 2:** DVAO advantage is pointwise bounded by RC advantage (A_DVAO ≤ A_sum), preventing magnitude explosion.
3. **Proposition 3:** DVAO's sensitivity to individual rewards includes a cross-interaction term (A_DVAO × A_k), meaning the gradient contribution of each objective is modulated by the overall multi-objective performance of that rollout — an implicit cross-objective regularization that AC lacks entirely.

## Results & Highlights

- **Math Reasoning (Qwen3-4B/8B):** DVAO achieves the highest average accuracy (42.19% on 4B, 47.49% on 8B across AIME-2024/2025, MATH500, OlympiadBench, AMC23) while maintaining near-perfect length compliance (~100%). GDPO achieves similar length compliance but at the cost of dramatically lower accuracy (~13-14% average).
- **Tool-Use (Qwen2.5-3B/7B, BFCL-v4):** DVAO leads both accuracy and format compliance. On Qwen2.5-7B, DVAO achieves 87.06% average accuracy with 95.11% format compliance and 22.25 average response length — the best across all dimensions simultaneously.
- **Training Dynamics:** DVAO shows higher accuracy reward mean with lower variance, faster convergence to target length reward, and more dramatic variance collapse — directly reflecting the cross-objective regularization from Proposition 3.
- **Pareto Frontier:** Across weight sweeps (w1 ∈ {0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 0.9}), DVAO consistently dominates the frontier, maintaining high auxiliary compliance across the entire accuracy range while baselines exhibit distinct failure modes (RC saturation, AC instability, GDPO incoherence).

## Authors & Credibility

All authors are from Alibaba Cloud Computing. The paper provides complete proofs in appendices, extensive experiments across 4 model scales and 7 benchmarks, and compares against 4 baseline methods (GRPO, RC, AC, GDPO). The work builds directly on GRPO (DeepSeek-R1) and GDPO, positioning itself clearly in the current LLM post-training research landscape.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The dynamic variance-adaptive weighting is a principled and non-obvious contribution. The theoretical analysis (3 propositions with proofs) rigorously characterizes why DVAO works, going beyond empirical heuristics.
- **Rigor (High):** Formal propositions with proofs, clear derivations connecting advantage magnitude to gradient stability, and sensitivity analysis revealing the implicit regularization mechanism.
- **Reproducibility (Medium-High):** Implementation details in appendix, standard benchmarks, publicly available models (Qwen series). Missing: code release commitment, exact training compute.
- **Significance (High):** Multi-reward RL is a practical bottleneck for real-world LLM deployment. DVAO's hyperparameter-free nature and theoretical guarantees make it immediately applicable.
- **Red Flags:** (1) The paper only evaluates 2-objective settings (accuracy + length/format). Scaling to ≥3 objectives is mentioned as future work but not tested. (2) The oscillation in DVAO's response length curves, while the authors argue it reflects aggressive exploration, could be a concern in production settings. (3) DVAO is compared against GDPO which itself is very recent; it would strengthen the paper to include more multi-objective RL baselines from the broader optimization literature.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical framework connecting variance-adaptive weighting to bounded advantages and implicit regularization.
- DVAO is the only method achieving the highest scores on both accuracy and auxiliary compliance simultaneously across all model scales.
- Fully dynamic and hyperparameter-free — no manual weight tuning needed.
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation across two distinct task domains (math reasoning, tool-use) and four model sizes.
- Pareto frontier analysis provides a complete picture of trade-off capabilities.

## Limitations

- Only 2-objective scenarios are tested; the behavior with 3+ conflicting objectives remains unexplored.
- The variance-adaptive mechanism may be less effective when all objectives have similar variance profiles.
- Training instability risk from oscillatory response lengths is acknowledged but not fully addressed.
- No analysis of computational overhead introduced by the dynamic weight computation per rollout group.
- Tool-use evaluation is limited to BFCL-v4; generalization to other agentic tasks is not demonstrated.

## Relevance

DVAO 解决了多奖励 GRPO 训练中的核心痛点——如何在没有手动调参的情况下平衡多个优化目标。这对于任何需要同时优化准确率、输出长度、格式合规性等多个指标的 LLM 后训练场景都直接适用，特别是工具调用和推理模型的训练。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does DVAO behave when scaling to 3+ objectives with varying correlation structures (synergistic vs. antagonistic)?
2. Can the variance-adaptive mechanism be combined with curriculum learning to progressively introduce objectives?
3. What is the sensitivity of DVAO to the group size G — does it degrade with smaller rollout groups?
4. How does DVAO interact with other GRPO variants like DAPO (dynamic sampling) or GSPO (sequence-level importance)?
5. Is the implicit cross-objective regularization sufficient to prevent reward hacking when some objectives are easier to game than others?
