> **一句话总结：** 本文通过行为经济学中的"实现效应"发现，Gemma 模型内部虽然存在可线性解码的表征信号，但该表征无法通过激活操控来因果地控制下游风险决策，揭示了行为敏感性、隐变量读取和因果控制是三个独立属性。

> **TL;DR:** Using the realization effect from behavioral economics as a test case, the authors show that Gemma 3 4B contains a linearly decodable realization-status representation at layer 18 that generalizes to held-out prompts, but steering along this direction does not causally control downstream risk-taking behavior — demonstrating that behavioral sensitivity, latent readout, and causal control are three distinct properties that do not automatically co-occur.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了 LLM 行为评估中的三个层次（行为敏感性、线性表征读取、因果控制）是彼此独立的属性，成功读取不等于因果有效。
- 发现 Gemma 3 4B 在第 18 层残差流中编码了可泛化的"实现状态"信号，但该方向无法通过激活操控可靠地改变下游风险决策。
- 为机械可解释性领域提供了具体的实证证据：线性可解码性是因果控制的弱证据，验证因果效力需要更严格的标准。

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates a clean dissociation between three evidential standards for LLM behavioral evaluation: prompt-only condition sensitivity, linear activation readout, and causal control via steering.
- Shows that Gemma 3 4B encodes a linearly decodable realization-status signal at layer 18 of the residual stream that generalizes to held-out DeepSeek-authored prompts (75–93% correct-direction rates).
- Establishes that steering along the decoded realization direction fails to produce sign-symmetric behavioral shifts in downstream wager or risk-profile outputs across scales −50 to +150.
- Provides a null result with clear theoretical import: a representation can be readable without being causally upstream of the behavior of interest.

## Methodology

The study uses the **realization effect** from behavioral economics — where risk-taking differs after paper (open account) versus realized (closed account) gains and losses — as a well-characterized test case.

**Stage 1 — Prompt-only behavioral evaluation:** 54,450 rows across 25 models, 11 conditions spanning paper and realized outcomes. Models return wager (1–1000 CHF) and risk preference (1–5). OLS regressions with model, temperature, and prompt-version fixed effects; HC3 robust standard errors.

**Stage 2 — Activation readout:** Paired paper-open vs. realized-closed prompts across finance, casino, budget, and other domains. Mean-difference direction computed at layer 18 of Gemma 3 4B: `v_realization = μ_realized_closed − μ_paper_open`. Train-only direction (756 pairs) evaluated on held-out validation and DeepSeek-generated prompts.

**Stage 3 — Activation steering:** Residual-stream steering via forward hook adding a scaled, normalized realization direction at layer 18 during generation. Scales: −50, 0, +50, +75, +100, +150. Applied to 648 behavior-evaluation prompts. Positive-control classification task also tested.

## Results & Highlights

- **Prompt-only:** Models are condition-sensitive to outcome framing, but the directional pattern does **not** match human realization-effect predictions. Realized losses increase (rather than decrease) wagers — opposite to human behavior.
- **Readout succeeds:** The train-only layer-18 direction separates realized/closed from paper/open prompts on original validation (91.1% correct direction) and DeepSeek held-out readout (92.9%) splits.
- **Projection-behavior link is weak:** Within matched pairs, projection deltas do not predict wager deltas (Pearson r = 0.072) or risk deltas (r = 0.001).
- **Steering fails:** Across all positive scales, median wager and risk deltas are zero. Negative-scale (−50) run also fails to produce a sign-symmetric reversal. The direction does not function as a causal lever.
- **Positive-control classification:** Small directional shift in realized-prediction rate (0.140 → 0.198 at +100), but accuracy stays near chance (~0.52) with a strong PAPER-label bias.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The three-tier evaluation framework (behavior → readout → steering) applied to a specific behavioral-economics construct is novel and the null steering result provides concrete empirical content for a mostly theoretical debate.
- **Rigor:** Strong experimental design with train/val/held-out splits, multiple prompt sources, sign-symmetry checks, and honest reporting of compliance artifacts. The positive-control classification run had a duplicated-instruction issue, appropriately caveated.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code is public, Gemma 3 4B is an open model, and all steering configurations are specified. However, the 54,450-row prompt-only dataset pools 25 models, some of which are proprietary.
- **Significance:** The central dissociation — readable ≠ controllable — is an important finding for mechanistic interpretability and challenges the common practice of treating linear probes as evidence of causal mechanism.
- **Red flags:** The steering null is specific to one model, one layer, and one direction construction method. Whether this generalizes across architectures remains unknown. The DeepSeek held-out set is small (28 + 12 pairs). No bootstrap or randomization intervals are reported for steering deltas.

## Strengths

- Clean, theory-driven experimental design using a well-characterized behavioral economics phenomenon.
- Honest and thorough reporting of null results, including sign-symmetry checks that rule out the "insufficient magnitude" explanation.
- The three-tier framework (behavior, readout, control) is generalizable beyond the realization effect.
- Detailed appendices with compliance breakdowns and per-split readout results.

## Limitations

- Single model (Gemma 3 4B), single layer (18), single direction construction (mean-difference). No layer sweep or multi-layer steering tested.
- Hosted models (Qwen, etc.) could not be tested for steering due to API limitations on hidden-state access.
- Constrained numeric outputs (wager + risk) may amplify format/parsing artifacts; Sonnet-derived prompts had ~49% noncompliance at some scales.
- The decoded direction may track a broad semantic open/closed contrast rather than the specific decision-relevant variable needed to shift risk-taking.
- No bootstrap confidence intervals on steering deltas; null result relies on zero medians and absent sign-symmetry.

## Relevance

本文对机械可解释性和 LLM 行为评估领域有重要启示：线性探针（linear probe）的成功不能作为因果机制的证据。这对于所有试图用激活操控方法验证模型内部机制的研究都是一个关键警示，尤其在决策、推理等需要因果验证的场景中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Would a layer sweep (layers 14–22) or multi-layer steering schedule produce non-null behavioral effects, or is the dissociation structural?
2. Does the same readout-without-control dissociation appear in other architectures (e.g., Qwen, LLaMA) under comparable local hook frameworks?
3. Can the three-tier evaluation framework (behavior → readout → steering) be applied to other cognitive biases (e.g., anchoring, framing effect) to determine whether the dissociation is specific to the realization construct?
4. Would position-mode steering (all-token rather than final-token) change the null result?
5. Is the semantic broadness of the decoded direction (open vs. closed framing generally) the reason steering fails, and could a more narrowly targeted direction improve control?
