PEFT-Arena: Understanding Parameter-Efficient Finetuning from a Stability-Plasticity Perspective

arXiv: 2605.28819

Date: 2026-05-27

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28819

一句话总结： 提出 PEFT-Arena 基准，从稳定性-可塑性权衡视角系统评估 PEFT 方法，发现正交微调（OFT）在权重谱保持和激活几何保留上表现最优，且 SFT 最终检查点普遍存在"过冲"现象，可通过路径回退恢复通用能力。
TL;DR: A benchmark (PEFT-Arena) that evaluates PEFT methods on both downstream task gains and general capability retention reveals that orthogonal finetuning (OFT) achieves the best stability-plasticity Pareto frontier under comparable parameter budgets. Spectral and activation-geometry analyses link forgetting to non-isometric representation distortion, and interpolation diagnostics show that final SFT checkpoints systematically overshoot a better target-retention operating point—recoverable via layer-wise path-wise rewinding.

核心贡献

  • 提出 PEFT-Arena，首个同时评估下游任务适应（可塑性）和通用能力保持（稳定性）的 PEFT 综合基准，覆盖数学和医学两个推理领域
  • 系统比较了 Full FT、LoRA、DoRA、AdaLoRA、VeRA、IA³、PiSSA、MiLoRA、MiSS、OFT 等方法，发现不同参数化方式呈现截然不同的稳定性-可塑性权衡模式
  • 从权重空间谱分析和激活空间几何分析两个视角揭示遗忘的内在机制：遗忘与非等距表征畸变强相关（Procrustes residual 与 forgetting 的 Pearson r = 0.711）
  • 发现 SFT 最终检查点普遍存在过冲现象——沿插值路径回退可恢复通用能力同时保留大部分目标任务增益，且该过冲无法通过早停解决
  • 提出参数化感知的路径回退（layer-wise OFT rewinding）作为后处理改进的案例研究

Key Contributions

  • Introduces PEFT-Arena, a benchmark jointly measuring target-domain adaptation (plasticity) and general capability retention (stability) across math and medical reasoning domains
  • Provides the first systematic comparison of 10+ PEFT methods under a unified stability-plasticity framework, showing OFT achieves the most favorable Pareto frontier
  • Connects external forgetting to internal geometry through two complementary views: weight-space spectral profiles and activation-space non-isometric distortion metrics (Procrustes residual, Gram distortion, CKA)
  • Reveals that SFT overshoot is a structural phenomenon — the optimal stability-plasticity trade-off lies along the interpolation path, not at the final checkpoint, and cannot be recovered by early stopping
  • Demonstrates parameterization-aware path-wise rewinding (Cayley generator scaling for OFT) as a practical post-hoc improvement strategy

Methodology

Benchmark Design. PEFT-Arena evaluates PEFT methods on two target domains (mathematics: AIME24, AMC23, MATH500; medicine: MedQA, MedMCQA, PubMedQA) while simultaneously testing general capability retention on IFEval, Natural Questions, BBH, and other general benchmarks. Both SFT and RLVR training pipelines are tested.

Models. Primary experiments use Qwen2.5-7B and Llama3.2-3B-Instruct. All PEFT methods are compared under matched parameter budgets (~1-2% of total parameters).

Weight-Space Analysis. Spectral decomposition of PEFT updates: projected spectrum changes are split into "retention profile" (how update energy interacts with pretrained principal singular values) and "adaptation profile" (how it affects task-relevant components). Effective update rank and spectral smoothness (fluctuation scores) are computed.

Activation-Space Analysis. Three complementary metrics measure non-isometric distortion of general-capability representations: (1) Procrustes residual (best orthogonal alignment error), (2) pairwise Gram distortion (cosine-ilarity structure change), and (3) linear CKA (representation similarity). Correlations with external forgetting are computed across 20 SFT checkpoints and 8 module locations.

Interpolation Diagnostics. For additive methods: W(α) = W₀ + α(W* − W₀). For OFT: Cayley generator scaling (skew-symmetric matrix interpolation) to stay on the orthogonal manifold. Sweeping α ∈ [0,1] traces the target-retention curve and identifies overshoot.

Layer-wise Rewinding. Per-layer interpolation coefficients αₗ are optimized to recover general performance while preserving target gains, serving as a proof-of-concept post-hoc improvement.

Results & Highlights

  • OFT dominates the Pareto frontier: Under comparable parameter budgets, OFT achieves the best stability-plasticity trade-off across both SFT and RLVR settings. For Qwen2.5-7B SFT, OFT achieves ~47.9 avg target performance with only ~7.81 forgetting, vs. LoRA's ~46.9 target / ~15.97 forgetting and Full FT's ~47.6 target / ~17.31 forgetting.
  • Forgetting correlates with non-isometric distortion: Procrustes residual on general data shows Pearson r = 0.711 with forgetting; CKA shows r = -0.761. OFT has the lowest Procrustes residual (0.128) and highest CKA (0.934); PiSSA is a clear outlier (Procrustes 0.438, CKA 0.440, forgetting 34.56).
  • Spectral analysis reveals update bias: Additive low-rank methods concentrate update energy in principal singular value directions, while OFT preserves spectral structure through orthogonal transformations that are inherently near-isometric.
  • SFT overshoot is universal: Across all additive PEFT methods and full finetuning, the final checkpoint lies past a better operating point on the interpolation curve. The training trajectory is concave while the interpolation trajectory is convex — early stopping cannot recover the optimal point.
  • Layer-wise rewinding improves trade-offs: Selectively scaling back certain layers' updates recovers 2-5% general performance while losing <1% target performance.

Critical Assessment

Novelty (Moderate-High): The stability-plasticity framing for PEFT evaluation is well-motivated and fills a real gap — most PEFT benchmarks are single-metric. However, the core insight that OFT preserves geometry better than low-rank methods is somewhat expected given OFT's design (orthogonal constraints are inherently isometry-preserving). The interpolation-as-diagnostic idea is adapted from WiSE-FT and task arithmetic, not entirely new but applied in a novel PEFT-specific context.

Rigor (Moderate-High): The geometric analysis is thorough with three complementary activation-space metrics and strong correlations. The spectral decomposition into retention/adaptation profiles is well-designed. However, experiments are limited to two base models (Qwen2.5-7B, Llama3.2-3B) — generalization to larger models (13B, 70B) and different architectures is untested. The general benchmark suite, while reasonable, is not exhaustive (no code generation, no multilingual, no long-context tasks).

Reproducibility (High): The paper provides detailed implementation specifics (Appendix B), uses standard PEFT libraries, and the benchmark protocol is clearly described. The evaluation metrics (Procrustes, CKA, Gram distortion) are well-established and straightforward to implement.

Significance (Moderate): The work provides a useful evaluation framework and actionable insights (OFT > LoRA for retention, SFT overshoot is real). The layer-wise rewinding is presented only as a case study, not a full method with systematic evaluation. The practical impact depends on whether practitioners actually care about general capability retention during task-specific finetuning — in many real deployments, this may be secondary to target-task performance.

Red Flags:

  • The claim that OFT is "the best" PEFT method is somewhat circular — OFT is designed to preserve geometry, so finding it preserves geometry is partially a self-fulfilling prophecy. A stronger paper would explore why one might choose OFT over LoRA beyond geometry preservation.
  • Only two target domains (math, medicine) are tested. The stability-plasticity trade-off could look very different for, e.g., code generation, creative writing, or multilingual tasks.
  • The RLVR experiments are less thorough than the SFT experiments, with only high-k results at two step counts reported.
  • The paper does not address computational overhead differences between PEFT methods (e.g., OFT's Cayley parameterization may have different training dynamics).

Strengths

  • Well-motivated evaluation framework addressing a genuine blind spot in PEFT research
  • Comprehensive geometric analysis connecting weight-space and activation-space perspectives with strong empirical correlations
  • The interpolation-as-diagnostic approach is elegant and yields actionable findings (overshoot is not recoverable by early stopping)
  • Honest presentation: the paper clearly states that interpolation is a diagnostic tool, not a new method
  • Extensive appendix with full layer/module breakdowns and implementation details

Limitations

  • Limited to 7B-class models; scalability to larger models is unknown
  • Only two target domains tested; generalization to other task types is unclear
  • OFT's advantage may be partially attributable to its smaller effective parameter count or different optimization landscape, not purely its geometric properties
  • The layer-wise rewinding is underdeveloped — presented as a case study rather than a systematic method
  • No comparison with replay-based or regularization-based continual learning methods that also address forgetting
  • General capability benchmarks are somewhat narrow (no code, no multilingual, no long-context)

Relevance

本文对 PEFT 方法的稳定性-可塑性权衡分析直接关联 LLM 后训练的核心问题——如何在适应下游任务时不破坏预训练能力。几何分析视角（权重谱分析 + 激活空间非等距畸变）为理解和改进 PEFT 提供了新的理论基础，对模型适配实践有直接指导意义。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the OFT advantage persist at 70B+ scale where different emergent capabilities may rely on different spectral components?
  2. Can the layer-wise rewinding approach be automated (e.g., via a validation-set-driven αₗ search) to become a practical post-training technique?
  3. How do data-centric forgetting mitigation methods (FLOW, TALR, EAFT) interact with the geometric properties identified here — do they reduce non-isometric distortion?
  4. Is the convexity of the interpolation trajectory (vs. concavity of training trajectory) explainable from optimization theory?
  5. Can a hybrid approach combining OFT's geometry preservation with LoRA's flexibility (e.g., orthogonal low-rank adaptation) achieve better trade-offs?
