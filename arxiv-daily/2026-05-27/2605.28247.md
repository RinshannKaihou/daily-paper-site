> **一句话总结：** 提出 IRDS 方法，基于稀疏自编码器（SAE）聚类坐标构造"验证器耦合覆盖目标"并经贪心对数行列式最大化选出 RLVR 训练子集，使数据选择既可审计又非冗余；在六个数学基准上 Qwen3-4B/1.7B 的 Overall 较最强基线 LIMR/DEPO 提升 +3.9/+4.0 pp（AIME24 上 +11.4 pp），且选择成本比轨迹基线 LIMR 低一个数量级。

> **TL;DR:** IRDS represents each DeepScaleR training instance on a 256-cluster BatchTopK sparse-autoencoder basis, attaches two verifier-coupled weights (difficulty d_i, trainability r_i) derived from a Beta posterior over the per-instance rollout success count, and selects a non-redundant subset by greedy log-determinant maximization on a whitened coverage metric M. Across Qwen3-4B, Qwen3-1.7B and Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct under a shared GRPO recipe, IRDS raises Overall mean@16 to 67.0/54.2/22.6, beating the strongest baseline (LIMR/DEPO) by +3.9/+4.0/+0.5 pp on the six-benchmark math suite (MATH500, AIME24/25, AMC23, OlympiadBench, Minerva Math), while running an order of magnitude cheaper than the trajectory-based LIMR baseline.

## 核心贡献

- **首个基于 SAE 聚类的 RLVR 数据选择方法**：IRDS 在 BatchTopK 稀疏自编码器训练出的 256 个语义聚类坐标上选择实例，使所选子集可在"圆-坐标几何""多项式求根""多选题题干"等可识别模式上被审计。
- **验证器耦合覆盖目标**：从每实例验证器成功计数 s_i 诱导的 Beta 后验导出两个标量权重——难度权重 d_i（单调递减，模型常错时高）与可训练性权重 r_i（在 s_i≈G/2 处单峰，群组相对梯度最强处），并将二者在 SAE 坐标上组合为白化度量 M=(Σ_r+ρI)^{-1/2}(Σ_d+ρI)(Σ_r+ρI)^{-1/2}，其主方向恰好集中在"难度超出可训练性"的方向。
- **集层级非冗余选择**：用贪心 D-最优设计（对数行列式最大化 + Sherman–Morrison 秩一更新）求解，附带 (1−1/e) 子模保证，并对每个弱点只付一次代价。
- **可解释的审计证据**：通过四项诊断（高难度定位、跨策略一致性、表面特征非解释性、标签打乱证伪）证明选择反映内容方向而非长度/符号密度等表面特征。
- **稳健的预算外推**：在 10/20/30% 三种数据预算下均保持最高 Overall，主结果排序（去掉 r_i ≫ M=I ≫ d_i·r_i 点乘 ≈ 去掉 d_i/梯度/PCA-256）在所有预算上一致。

## Key Contributions

- **First SAE-cluster-basis RLVR data selector**: IRDS trains one BatchTopK-Ortho SAE per base model (×32 expansion, k=128, F=256 graph-hybrid clusters) so the selected subset is auditable on recognizable problem motifs such as geometry, divisor-counting, polynomial-root algebra, and multiple-choice stems.
- **Verifier-coupled coverage objective**: introduces a difficulty weight d_i = ψ(G_i+2) − ψ(s_i+1) and a trainability weight r_i = (s_i+1)(G_i−s_i+1)/[(G_i+2)(G_i+3)] from a Beta posterior on the success rate, and combines them into the whitened metric M that isolates SAE directions where difficulty exceeds trainability.
- **Set-level greedy selection**: solves the D-optimal log-determinant objective with a greedy procedure carrying a (1−1/e) submodular guarantee, using a screened candidate queue (Q=1024, refresh R=256) and Sherman–Morrison rank-one updates so the full selection completes in under 60 seconds on a single GPU.
- **Auditing evidence**: four diagnostics (high-difficulty localization, cross-policy consistency, surface-artifact non-explanation, label-shuffle falsification) all confirm that the selection reflects content rather than surface features; a global verifier-label shuffle collapses M's top eigenvalue and drives the leading-subspace overlap with the true-label M to near zero.
- **Budget-stable ranking**: across 10/20/30% DeepScaleR budgets the structural rank order of ablation deltas (r_i removal ≫ M=I ≫ d_i·r_i pointwise ≈ d_i / gradient / PCA-256) is invariant.

## Methodology

IRDS is an **offline** instance-selection method that runs upstream of the GRPO RL loop and returns a size-K subset of the DeepScaleR corpus (K=8062, i.e. 20% of the 40,309-instance pool). The pipeline has four stages:

1. **SAE cluster coordinates (§4.1).** One BatchTopK-Ortho SAE is trained per base model on that model's final-layer residual-stream activations harvested from OpenMathInstruct-2 (300K problems, ∼20M sampled tokens, ≤512 tokens/instance). Shared settings: expansion ×32, per-token sparsity k=128, decoder-orthogonality penalty, AuxK auxiliary loss. The Qwen3-4B checkpoint reaches normalized MSE 0.0834 on 200K held-out samples with mean L0=128 and a 2.0% dead-feature rate. Related SAE latents are clustered into F=256 semantic clusters via a graph-hybrid embedding (co-activation cosine presence direction + residual-mass direction) and spherical K-means; each instance x_i becomes a non-negative cluster-mass vector m_i ∈ ℝ^256.

2. **Verifier-coupled coverage (§4.2).** Each instance is scored by the frozen base model with G=8 rollouts, yielding a success count s_i ∈ {0,…,8}. A Beta(s_i+1, G−s_i+1) posterior on the success rate P_i produces two mean-one rescaled weights: the difficulty weight d_i (monotone-decreasing in s_i, high when the model fails) and the trainability weight r_i (unimodal, peaking near s_i≈G/2 where group-relative advantage variance — which carries a P_i(1−P_i) factor — is largest). On the regularized centered cluster coordinate z̄_i they form weighted covariances Σ_d, Σ_r and the whitened metric M=(Σ_r+ρI)^{-1/2}(Σ_d+ρI)(Σ_r+ρI)^{-1/2} (ρ=0.1, trace-normalized with shrinkage η=0.5 and eigenvalue clip c=2). The per-instance SAE design vector is v_i = √r̃_i · M^{1/2} z̄_i, so an instance contributes most when it carries trainability mass and loads on high-difficulty-excess directions.

3. **Optional gradient block (§4.3).** A TRAK-style per-block Johnson–Lindenstrauss projection (p_ℓ=64 per transformer block, pg = L·64 ∈ {1792, 2304, 2048}) of the per-instance NLL gradient is ridge-residualized against z̄_i, PCA-whitened, trace-matched to the SAE block, and given an inverse-norm row weight q̃_i ∝ ‖g_i^⊥‖^{−α} (α=1) that downweights the high-norm saturated tail (residual-norm grows monotonically with s_i, from ≈7.4 at s_i=0 to ≈11.1 at s_i=G on Qwen3-1.7B). The mixing weight ω=1 concatenates the SAE block v_i with q̃_i ω ĝ_i; ω=0 recovers an SAE-only design.

4. **Greedy D-optimal selection (§4.4).** The final design vector ϕ_i is fed to a greedy procedure maximizing J(S)=log det(λI + Σ_{i∈S} ϕ_i ϕ_i^⊤) (λ=1). Marginal gains Δ_i(S)=log(1+ϕ_i^⊤ A_S^{-1} ϕ_i) are tracked via Sherman–Morrison rank-one updates, yielding the (1−1/e) submodular guarantee.

**Training.** Post-training uses GRPO (VeRL framework) with LoRA (r=64, α=128), AdamW (lr 5×10⁻⁶, wd 0.1), 2 epochs, batch size 128, max input 1024 / response 4096 tokens, G=8 rollouts at temperature 1.0, PPO clip 0.2, KL coeff 10⁻³, on 8× NVIDIA H20 GPUs. **Evaluation** uses mean@16 (n=16 samples, temperature 0.6, top-p=0.95, 4k response budget) over MATH500, AIME24/25, AMC23, OlympiadBench and Minerva Math. Baselines: RANDOM, TOKEN-LENGTH, IFD, PPL-TOP, PPL-MIDDLE, trajectory-based LIMR, and density/DPP method DEPO.

## Results & Highlights

- **Overall accuracy (mean@16, 20% budget)**: IRDS reaches 67.0 / 54.2 / 22.6 on Qwen3-4B / Qwen3-1.7B / Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, beating the strongest baseline by **+3.9 / +4.0 / +0.5 pp** (Qwen3-4B 67.0 vs LIMR 63.1; Qwen3-1.7B 54.2 vs LIMR 50.2; Llama 22.6 vs RANDOM/DEPO 22.1).
- **AIME24 (Qwen3-4B)**: IRDS 42.0 vs LIMR 30.6, a gain of **+11.4 pp**; on AIME25 IRDS 35.6 vs DEPO 29.0 (+6.6 pp) and on AMC23 IRDS 79.5 vs PPL-TOP 72.4 (+7.1 pp).
- **MATH500**: IRDS lifts Qwen3-4B to 91.3 (vs LIMR 89.1, +2.2 pp) and Qwen3-1.7B to 82.5 (+3.5 pp).
- **Cost**: selection runs in under 60 s on a single GPU, an order of magnitude cheaper than the trajectory-based LIMR baseline.
- **Ablation (Table 2)**: removing the trainability weight r_i collapses Overall by 4.1–19.1 pp (d-only Qwen3-4B 47.9 vs full 67.0); setting M=I drops it up to −9.2 pp (57.8 on Qwen3-4B); the SAE-only variant (ω=0) already reaches 64.2/53.0/21.9, so the gradient block adds only 0.7–2.8 pp.
- **Budget sweep (Table 4)**: IRDS keeps the top Overall at 10/20/30%, e.g. Qwen3-4B 63.8 / 67.0 / 68.8.
- **Out-of-domain (HumanEval/LiveCodeBench/ReClor)**: IRDS is best three-task average on Llama-3.1-8B (48.2 vs 47.9) and Qwen3-1.7B (49.9 vs 49.5), but 1.0 pp below RANDOM on Qwen3-4B (64.9 vs 65.9).

## Critical Assessment

- **Llama-3.1-8B margin is marginal (+0.5 pp Overall)** and on the smaller competition subsets individual baselines (DEPO leads AIME24 4.1 vs IRDS 3.4; RANDOM leads AMC23 23.0 vs 21.7) actually beat IRDS, so the headline lift is concentrated on the two stronger Qwen policies — the gain is not uniform across base models.
- The largest per-benchmark deltas come from the **smallest test sets** (AIME24: 60, AIME25: 30, AMC23: 46 problems), so a few solved problems swing the mean@16 by several pp; the paper itself flags this.
- **All numbers come from a single training seed per cell** (explicitly stated in Limitations), so seed-level RL variance is not reported — a concern given the small competition-set sizes.
- The gradient block, a non-trivial TRAK-based component, contributes only **0.7–2.8 pp**, so most of the engineering complexity is marginal relative to the SAE-only variant.
- The SAE coordinate is **model-specific** (one SAE per policy, trained on accessible final-layer activations), which restricts reuse and assumes activation access — a deployment friction not fully costed.

## Strengths

- Genuinely interpretable coordinate: the 256 SAE clusters carry human-readable labels (geometry, divisor counting, polynomial roots, MCQ stems), and the label-shuffle falsification check is a strong causal defense against surface-feature shortcuts.
- Principled combination of failure-relevance (d_i), trainability (r_i) and non-redundancy (greedy D-optimal with submodular guarantee) in one objective, where prior lines each address only one.
- Cheap and fast: under-60-second selection on a single GPU, order-of-magnitude cheaper than trajectory rollouts.
- Comprehensive ablation isolating every component, plus budget sweep, OOD transfer, and four auditing diagnostics.

## Limitations

- Validated only on math RLVR under GRPO with LoRA; transfer to non-math domains and non-GRPO algorithms is undemonstrated.
- Verifier-coupled weights are estimated once under the frozen model, so the targeted failure profile can drift after many updates (the authors note a semi-online refresh as future work).
- Requires a per-model SAE trained on accessible final-layer activations.
- Single training seed per cell; small competition-subset benchmarks make per-benchmark deltas noisy.

## Relevance

本文直接映射到研究兴趣 **3. LLM Hidden States & Interpretability**：IRDS 的核心是把稀疏自编码器作用于 LLM 最后一层隐藏态，用可识别的语义聚类坐标使数据选择可审计，并辅以特征放大扫描与基座-训练谱不变性结果。其次，它通过验证器信号选择训练数据，也与 **1. LLM Training Stability & Observability** 相关——可训练性权重 r_i 正是用来规避群组相对梯度退化的"全失败/全通过"尾部，从数据侧稳定 RLVR 训练信号。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The paper leaves a **semi-online refresh** of the verifier-coupled weights (d_i, r_i) on the fixed SAE basis as future work: how much does the in-domain lift erode if the failure profile drifts, and how often must weights be re-estimated?
- Would replacing GRPO with the **DAPO** algorithm (Yu et al., 2025, cited as algorithmically compatible) preserve the +3.9/+4.0 pp margins, given that DAPO changes the group-relative advantage?
- The ablation shows the SAE basis beats a **PCA-256 dense-gradient projection** by up to −4.5 pp (Qwen3-4B) but only 1.1–2.1 pp on the weaker models — is SAE interpretability worth the activation-access cost for models below a base-policy strength threshold?
- Can the label-shuffle falsification diagnostic be turned into a **continuous leakage metric** during SAE training, rather than only a post-hoc check?
