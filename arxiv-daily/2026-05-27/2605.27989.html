Law of Neural Interaction: Depth-Width Shape, Interaction Efficiency, and Generalization

arXiv: 2605.27989

Date: 2026-05-27

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27989

一句话总结： 本文提出"神经交互定律"，发现在固定参数预算下，通过调节深度-宽度比（$R_{D/W}$）可使模型进入高效神经交互区间，从而提升泛化性能，且该高效区间随预算扩大保持相对稳定。
TL;DR: Extending superposition from parameter space to gradient space as "neural interaction," this work identifies an efficient interaction interval governed by the depth-width ratio ($R_{D/W}$) that remains stable across scales and correlates with better MMLU-Pro performance in dense LLMs.

核心贡献

  • 将超位置（superposition）概念从参数空间扩展到梯度空间，定义了"神经交互"（neural interaction）的新框架
  • 发现在固定参数预算下，良好的泛化性能通常伴随着高效的神经交互
  • 揭示了深度-宽度比（$R_{D/W}$）存在一个"高效交互区间"，且该区间随预算规模扩大保持相对稳定
  • 观察到在高效交互区间附近运行的小规模密集LLM在MMLU-Pro基准上表现更好

Key Contributions

  • Extends the concept of superposition from parameter space to gradient space via the Neural Feature Ansatz, formalizing it as "neural interaction"
  • Demonstrates that under a fixed parameter budget, good generalization is typically accompanied by efficient neural interactions
  • Identifies an "efficient interaction interval" determined by the depth-width ratio ($R_{D/W}$) that remains relatively stable as the compute budget scales up
  • Shows that existing small-scale dense LLMs operating near this efficient interaction interval achieve better MMLU-Pro performance

Methodology

The authors leverage the Neural Feature Ansatz to extend superposition from parameter space to gradient space. They define neural interaction as a measure of resource utilization efficiency under fixed budgets and systematically study how the depth-width ratio ($R_{D/W}$) influences this interaction. They empirically map the efficient interaction interval across different budget scales and validate their findings by comparing against existing small-scale dense LLMs on the MMLU-Pro benchmark.

Results & Highlights

  • The efficient interaction interval remains relatively stable as the compute budget scales up
  • Models near the efficient interaction interval consistently outperform those outside it on MMLU-Pro
  • The $R_{D/W}$ ratio provides a practical guide for model shape initialization

Authors & Credibility

Wenjie Sun, Jinning Yang, Shuai Zhang, and Mengnan Du. The work addresses a fundamental question in neural network scaling and model architecture design.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The extension of superposition to gradient space and the formalization of neural interaction is a novel contribution. The idea of an efficient interaction interval is interesting.
  • Rigor: Based on abstract alone, the empirical validation appears limited to MMLU-Pro on small-scale dense LLMs. Broader benchmarking would strengthen claims.
  • Reproducibility: Code is reportedly available. The methodology relies on the Neural Feature Ansatz, which may have specific assumptions.
  • Significance: If the stable efficient interaction interval holds at scale, it could provide practical guidance for architecture design under fixed budgets — a meaningful contribution given the resource demands of modern LLMs.
  • Red flags: The claim that the interval is "relatively stable" across scales needs rigorous validation at larger scales beyond what is implied by "small scale dense LLMs."

Strengths

  • Bridges theoretical understanding (superposition, neural interaction) with practical architecture guidance ($R_{D/W}$)
  • Addresses an increasingly important question as LLM resource demands grow
  • Provides a potentially actionable metric for model shape initialization

Limitations

  • Validation appears focused on small-scale dense LLMs; generalization to large-scale or sparse/mixture-of-experts architectures is unclear
  • MMLU-Pro as the sole benchmark for validation is narrow
  • The stability of the efficient interaction interval at production scale remains unverified from the abstract

Relevance

对于关注LLM高效训练和模型架构设计的研究者，本文提供了关于深度-宽度比如何影响资源利用效率和泛化能力的新视角，对模型形状初始化具有实际指导意义。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the efficient interaction interval hold for sparse architectures (MoE) or only dense models?
  • How does the interval shift (if at all) at the 10B+ parameter scale?
  • Can neural interaction efficiency be optimized during training, not just at initialization?
  • How does this interact with other scaling laws (Chinchilla, Kaplan et al.)?
  • Is the Neural Feature Ansatz assumption valid across different activation functions and normalization schemes?
