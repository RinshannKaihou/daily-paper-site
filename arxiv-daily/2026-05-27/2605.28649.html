Interpretability-Guided Layer Selection over Subspace Projection: SAEs as Stethoscopes, Not Scalpels, for Raw Task Vector Model Editing

arXiv: 2605.28649

Date: 2026-05-27

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28649

一句话总结： 本文揭示将任务向量投影到SAE特征子空间会丢失约97%的修改能量且无显著提升，提出将SAE作为"听诊器"诊断领域特化层后注入原始任务向量，在Minerva Math上将数论准确率从29.6%提升至39.4%（p=0.0007），7个数学主题中5个显著提升、无退化。
TL;DR: Projecting task vectors onto SAE feature subspaces—a seemingly natural approach—discards ~97% of modification energy due to geometric misalignment between activation-space SAE directions and weight-space task vectors, yielding zero significant gains. Instead, using SAEs only to diagnose domain-specialized layers and injecting unfiltered raw task vectors into those layers achieves statistically significant improvements on 5/7 math subjects with no degradation.

核心贡献

  • 揭示SAE投影方法的根本性失败： 将任务向量投影到SAE解码器子空间仅保留约2-3.5%的修改能量（丢弃~97%），在7个数学主题上均未产生统计显著提升；即使将SAE特征宽度从16K扩大到262K也无法弥合差距，表明瓶颈源于投影本身而非特征粒度
  • 提出"SAE作为听诊器而非手术刀"的范式转换： SAE用于层级诊断（识别哪些层需要编辑），而非干预级过滤（决定注入什么内容）；这一角色分离在理论和实验上均得到验证
  • SAE引导的选择性原始任务向量注入方法： 通过SAE特征分析计算每层特异性分数（Specificity Score），选择领域特化层后注入未过滤的原始任务向量，在Gemma-3-4B-IT上将数论准确率从29.6%提升至39.4%（z=+3.41, p=0.0007）
  • 修改预算守恒的经验发现： 选定层数与最优缩放参数α之间存在守恒关系（n_layers × α_opt ≈ 11.2），为不同层选择策略提供了统一的调参指导
  • 30+消融实验配置： 包括α响应曲线、层选择策略对比、多种任务向量来源、双领域注入等全面消融

Key Contributions

  • Fundamental failure mode of SAE projection: Rigorously demonstrates that projecting task vectors onto SAE decoder subspaces retains only ~2-3.5% of modification energy, yielding no statistically significant improvements across 7 math subjects—even at 16× feature width (262K features)
  • Paradigm shift—SAE as diagnostic, not interventional tool: Proposes using SAEs for layer-level diagnosis (identifying where to edit) rather than intervention-level filtering (deciding what to inject), a separation consistent with growing evidence on SAE limitations
  • SAE-Guided Layer Selection with Raw Task Vector Injection: Computes per-layer Specificity Score from SAE feature analysis to identify domain-specialized layers, then injects unfiltered raw task vectors—achieving +9.8pp on Number Theory (29.6% → 39.4%, p=0.0007) with 5/7 subjects significantly improved and none degraded
  • Empirical conservation of modification budget: Identifies n_layers × α_opt ≈ 11.2, providing a principled relationship between layer count and scaling parameter
  • Extensive ablation suite: 30+ configurations covering alpha sweeps, layer-selection strategies, multiple task vector sources, and dual-domain injection

Methodology

Overview: The pipeline has three steps: (1) extract a task vector via LoRA fine-tuning, (2) use SAE feature analysis to identify domain-specialized layers, and (3) inject raw, unfiltered task vectors only into selected layers.

Task Vector Extraction: Task vector ΔW = W_ft − W_base obtained via LoRA fine-tuning (rank r=16, lr 2×10⁻⁴, 5 epochs) on 3,865 Number Theory problems from the MATH training set. The method is agnostic to the task vector source.

SAE-Guided Layer Selection: For each layer l and SAE feature j, compute specificity score: spec(l,j) = ā_target(l,j) / (ā_other(l,j) + ε). A feature is domain-specific if spec(l,j) > τ_f (τ_f = 1.0). Per-layer Specificity Score SP(l) = max_j spec(l,j). Layers with SP(l) ≥ τ_SP (= 4.0) are selected (14 of 34 layers for Gemma-3-4B-IT).

Selective Raw Injection: W_new^(l) = W_base^(l) + α · ΔW^(l) for selected layers; unchanged for others. α is grid-searched (optimal α = 0.80). No projection, truncation, or SAE-based masking is applied to the task vector.

Why SAE projection fails: SAE decoder vectors d_(l,j) live in activation space (capturing how the model represents information); task vectors ΔW live in weight space (encoding how computations change). Projecting weight-space modifications through activation-space directions conflates two fundamentally different geometric structures.

Evaluation: Minerva Math benchmark (540 problems, 7 subjects) via lm-evaluation-harness with 4-shot prompting, greedy decoding, and math_verify metric. Two-sample proportion z-tests for statistical significance. Experiments on 3× H100 80GB GPUs (~290 GPU-hours).

Critical Assessment

Novelty: The "SAE as stethoscope" framing is a genuine conceptual contribution—the insight that activation-space and weight-space interventions are geometrically incompatible for task vectors is both theoretically clean and practically actionable. The negative result (SAE projection failure) is the paper's strongest scientific contribution, consistent with emerging evidence from Sharkey et al., Kantamneni et al., and Heap et al.

Rigor: Statistical methodology is sound (two-sample z-tests on full test sets, not subsamples). The 30+ ablation configurations and method evolution tracing (Figure 4) provide strong evidence that the result is not cherry-picked. Determinism verification at float64 is a good practice rarely seen in LLM editing papers.

Reproducibility: High—the method uses off-the-shelf SAEs (Gemma Scope 2), standard LoRA fine-tuning, and public benchmarks. The paper provides sufficient detail on hyperparameters, hardware, and configurations. Code is referenced but availability not confirmed.

Significance: Moderate-to-high for the model editing community. The activation/weight space distinction is a load-bearing insight that could redirect significant research effort away from SAE-projection pipelines. The practical method itself is simple and effective but incremental (selective layer injection is not new; the contribution is the why and the negative result).

Red flags: (1) Single model, single domain focus—claims of generalizability are speculative. (2) The absolute accuracy (39.4% on NT) remains far from useful, reflecting the 4B model capacity. (3) The conservation law (n_layers × α_opt ≈ 11.2) is observed in only 2-3 data points. (4) Task vector quality is a prerequisite: a 5× larger mixed-domain corpus produced no effect, but this limitation is acknowledged.

Strengths

  • Honest and thorough documentation of a negative result (SAE projection failure) as the central scientific contribution, rather than burying it
  • Clean mechanistic explanation for why SAE projection fails (geometric misalignment between activation and weight spaces)
  • Rigorous statistical testing with full test sets, well-powered z-tests, and determinism verification
  • Extensive ablation study (30+ configurations) covering layer selection strategies, scaling parameters, task vector sources, and feature widths
  • Practical method that is fully deterministic, requires no additional inference cost, and uses only off-the-shelf components (LoRA + Gemma Scope 2)
  • Clear, well-structured paper with honest limitation discussion

Limitations

  • Single base model: All experiments on Gemma-3-4B-IT only; cross-model validation not performed despite available SAE suites for Llama
  • Single primary domain: Strongest claims for Number Theory; targeting Counting & Probability (~180 features across 18 layers) showed only marginal effects, suggesting method effectiveness depends on domain "sharpness"
  • No non-math validation: Legal reasoning, code, multilingual translation all untested
  • Task vector prerequisite: Requires one LoRA fine-tuning run (~4 GPU-hours, ~$16) and pre-trained SAEs at every layer; task vector quality is domain-focus dependent (5× larger mixed corpus failed)
  • Conservation law underdetermined: n_layers × α_opt ≈ 11.2 observed in only a few data points
  • Absolute performance ceiling: 39.4% on NT reflects 4B model capacity, not method limitation
  • Preprint status: Not yet peer-reviewed

Relevance

本文对模型编辑和可解释性交叉领域有重要启示：SAE作为诊断工具（选择编辑位置）而非干预工具（过滤编辑内容）的角色分离，为当前大量基于SAE投影的编辑流水线提供了关键的警示性证据，对任务算术和领域适配研究具有直接参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the activation/weight space geometric misalignment generalize to other model families (Llama, Mistral) and larger model scales (8B, 70B)?
  • Can the method be extended to non-mathematical domains (code generation, multilingual translation, legal reasoning) where domain features may be less concentrated?
  • Is there a principled way to predict which domains have sufficiently "sharp" SAE-feature signatures for effective layer selection?
  • How do interference-mitigation strategies (TIES-Merging, DARE, Localize-and-Stitch) interact with SAE-guided layer selection for multi-domain editing?
  • Can the conservation law (n_layers × α_opt ≈ constant) be theoretically derived from properties of the weight update landscape?
  • What is the relationship between SAE training quality (e.g., random-init vs. trained-model SAEs) and layer-selection effectiveness?
