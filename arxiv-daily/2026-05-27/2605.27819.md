> **一句话总结：** 提出残差化稀疏自编码器（ReSAE），通过在相邻选定层之间拟合仿射映射并训练SAE于残差目标而非原始激活，在Pythia-1.4B和Gemma-2-9B上显著减少多层干预中的特征冗余，提高交叉熵恢复和稀疏探测性能。

> **TL;DR:** Standard SAEs trained independently per layer waste capacity re-encoding information carried forward through the residual stream, causing multi-layer interventions to behave unpredictably. ReSAEs fit an affine map between selected layers and train each downstream SAE on the residual (unexplained component), then reconstruct via the affine chain. Despite lower explained variance, ReSAEs recover more cross entropy under multi-layer replacement (teacher-forced ∆CE near zero on Gemma; sequential-online ∆CE roughly halved vs. raw SAEs), produce less redundant decoder directions, and improve sparse probing and targeted perturbation — though SCR results on Pythia favor raw SAEs, indicating the gains are task-dependent.

## 核心贡献

- **提出ReSAE方法**：在相邻选定层之间拟合仿射回归映射，将后续层的SAE训练目标从原始激活改为残差（即不被前层线性预测的部分），在解码时通过仿射链重建回原始空间，保持与标准SAE的可比性。
- **揭示并解决多层SAE中的特征冗余问题**：标准逐层独立训练的SAE会在不同层的字典中重复编码残差流中的前向传递信息，导致多层同时干预时出现不可预测的交互效应（过交互，overinteraction）。
- **多层干预中交叉熵恢复更优**：尽管ReSAE的原始空间解释方差更低（Pythia: 0.780–0.864 vs 0.823–0.902），但在多层替换下恢复的交叉熵更好，尤其在teacher-forcing模式和足够稀疏度下。
- **解码器冗余显著降低**：ReSAE在所有测试稀疏度下均产生更正交的解码器方向（mean max cosine similarity在k=80时为0.189 vs 0.222）。
- **在Gemma-2-9B上验证可扩展性**：在不同层间距（gap 4/6/8）下，ReSAE在teacher-forced和sequential-online两种模式下均优于原始SAE，sequential-online ∆CE大约降低一半。

## Key Contributions

- **Residualized training target**: Proposes training each layer's SAE on the residual left by an affine prediction from an earlier selected layer, rather than the raw activation. Reconstruction composes through the affine chain, preserving comparability with standard SAEs.
- **Cross-layer redundancy diagnosis**: Shows that simple affine regressions between transformer layers explain a large fraction of activation variance (mean R² up to 0.8 for adjacent layers in Pythia), and that standard per-layer SAEs waste decoder capacity re-encoding this predictable structure.
- **Better functional reconstruction despite lower EV**: ReSAEs recover more cross entropy under multi-layer replacement in both Pythia-1.4B and Gemma-2-9B, despite having lower original-space explained variance — demonstrating that EV is a misleading metric for intervention quality.
- **Overinteraction metric**: Introduces an additivity-residual metric (OI) to quantify whether multi-layer replacement composes like the sum of single-layer effects. ReSAEs move OI much closer to zero, indicating more predictable composition.
- **Scaled validation on Gemma-2-9B**: Confirms the central pattern across three layer-gap configurations (4, 6, 8) at k=160 sparsity, with ReSAEs roughly halving sequential-online ∆CE compared to raw SAEs.

## Methodology

**Pairwise Affine Residualization:** For each consecutive pair of selected layers (ℓₘ, ℓₘ₊₁), an affine map Aₘh + cₘ is fit via ridge-regularized OLS on a calibration set (5×10⁵ tokens). The residualized target for the later layer's SAE is r = h(ℓₘ₊₁) − Aₘh(ℓₘ) − cₘ. The anchor layer ℓ₁ is centered by its empirical mean.

**Block Normalization:** Per-block RMS normalization is applied to equalize residual norms across depths before SAE training.

**SAE Architecture:** Each block trains an independent BatchTopK SAE (dictionary size N=65,536) with hard sparsity constraint ‖f‖₀ ≤ k. Pythia sweeps k ∈ {40, 80, 160, 320}; Gemma uses k=160. Training runs for 5×10⁸ tokens with Adam (lr=3×10⁻⁴, 1000-step warmup, 20% LR decay).

**Original-Space Reconstruction:** At inference, the SAE output is recursively composed with the affine chain to recover original-space activations: ĥ(ℓₘ₊₁) = Aₘĥ(ℓₘ) + cₘ + S⁻¹ẑ, enabling fair comparison with standard SAEs.

**Online Intervention Protocol:** During evaluation, hooks are applied in layer order within a single forward pass. For ReSAEs, each hook subtracts the affine prediction from the previous *reconstructed* layer before applying the SAE, keeping the SAE input closer to its training distribution.

**Evaluation Metrics:** Explained variance (EV), cross-entropy degradation (∆CE), overinteraction (OI), sparse probing (top-k accuracy), targeted probe perturbation (TPP), spurious correlation removal (SCR), and decoder cosine similarity. Models: Pythia-1.4B-deduped (layers {0,6,12,18}) and Gemma-2-9B (three layer-gap configurations). Data: monology/pile-uncopyrighted.

## Results & Highlights

| Metric | Raw SAE | ReSAE | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|-------|
| Pythia EV (k=160) | 0.823–0.902 | 0.780–0.864 | Raw wins on EV (expected) |
| Pythia decoder cosine (k=80) | 0.222 | 0.189 | ReSAE less redundant |
| Pythia TF ∆CE (k=320) | worse | 2.398 (clean: 2.260) | ReSAE recovers more CE |
| Pythia sparse probe top-5 (k=40) | 0.849 | 0.879 | ReSAE better, especially at low k |
| Pythia TPP per-layer (k=160) | worse | better | ReSAE better through k=160 |
| Pythia SCR (k=320, per-layer) | −0.043 | −0.077 | Raw SAE wins on SCR |
| Gemma seq-online ∆CE (gap-6) | 1.591–2.371 | 0.743–1.093 | ReSAE roughly halves degradation |
| Gemma TF ∆CE (all gaps) | higher | 0.026–0.058 | ReSAE near-zero degradation |
| Gemma sparse probe top-5 | 0.865 | 0.901 | ReSAE better (gap-4) |
| Gemma TPP per-layer joint | 0.220 | 0.252 | ReSAE better |

**Key finding:** EV and CE move in opposite directions — ReSAEs reconstruct less raw variance but recover more functional behavior. This suggests residualization steers capacity toward directions that participate in downstream computation rather than passively transmitted structure.

**Capacity-dependent gains:** In Pythia, ReSAEs underperform raw SAEs in sequential-online mode at low sparsity (k=40, 80) but overtake them at k≥160. This suggests residualization requires sufficient sparse code capacity to represent the residual target adequately.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The core idea — subtracting linearly predictable cross-layer structure before SAE training — is conceptually clean and well-motivated. The observation that affine maps explain a large fraction of cross-layer variance is not new (SVCCA, CKA, etc.), but operationalizing it as a training target modification for SAEs is a genuine contribution. The paper is clear that the method is simple and positions it as a "useful default" rather than a breakthrough.

**Rigor:** Good. The evaluation is comprehensive across multiple metrics (EV, CE, OI, sparse probing, TPP, SCR), two model families, and multiple sparsity levels / layer spacings. The paper honestly reports where ReSAEs *fail* (SCR on Pythia), which is commendable. The overinteraction metric is a reasonable diagnostic for multi-layer composition quality. However, some concerns:
- Only one Pythia layer set ({0,6,12,18}) is tested — no layer-set sweep for Pythia.
- Gemma evaluation uses only k=160, so the sparsity-capacity interaction observed in Pythia is not verified at scale.
- The calibration set for affine maps (5×10⁵ tokens) is not ablated — how sensitive are results to calibration size or data distribution?

**Reproducibility:** Good. The method uses only standard components (BatchTopK SAE, ridge regression, Adam). All hyperparameters are reported. Models and datasets are publicly available. SAE Bench evaluations are standardized.

**Significance:** Moderate. The practical impact is real but incremental: ReSAEs improve multi-layer intervention quality, which matters for mechanistic interpretability workflows (feature ablation, circuit tracing). However, the improvement is not uniform (SCR fails), the method adds affine map storage overhead, and the conceptual contribution is a straightforward application of residual modeling. The work is most valuable for researchers already doing multi-layer SAE interventions.

**Red flags:**
- The paper's title claims "Multi-Layer Transformer Interventions" but the evaluation is reconstruction-heavy; real mechanistic interpretability use cases (circuit tracing, causal scrubbing) are not demonstrated.
- The affine map storage cost (d×d per layer pair) is acknowledged but not quantified in terms of memory overhead relative to SAE parameters.
- No comparison to Cross-Coders (Lindsey et al. 2024) beyond a mention in related work — given that Cross-Coders also address cross-layer redundancy, this is a notable gap.

## Strengths

- Clear problem formulation with a well-motivated solution that requires minimal changes to existing SAE training pipelines.
- Honest and thorough evaluation: the paper reports negative results (SCR) alongside positive ones, avoiding the trap of cherry-picking favorable metrics.
- The overinteraction metric is a useful diagnostic that could be adopted more broadly by the SAE community.
- The EV-vs-CE divergence is a genuinely interesting finding that challenges the common practice of using explained variance as the primary SAE quality metric.
- Well-written with clear notation and figures; the methodology section is precise enough for direct reimplementation.
- The method is SAE-objective-agnostic — compatible with JumpReLU, p-Anneal, Matryoshka, etc.

## Limitations

- Limited architectural coverage: only Pythia-1.4B and Gemma-2-9B tested. No evaluation on models with different architectures (e.g., mixture-of-experts, non-GPT-style transformers).
- Pythia evaluation uses only one layer set; no layer-set sensitivity analysis for the smaller model.
- Gemma evaluation uses only k=160; the capacity-dependent effect seen in Pythia is not explored at scale.
- The affine residualization map is the simplest possible choice; nonlinear predictors might remove more structure but the tradeoff is not explored.
- SCR results on Pythia consistently favor raw SAEs, indicating ReSAEs are not universally better — the paper acknowledges this but does not deeply investigate why.
- Memory overhead of storing full d×d affine maps is not quantified; this could become prohibitive for wider models (e.g., 8192+ hidden dimension).
- No end-to-end mechanistic interpretability case study (e.g., tracing a specific circuit or behavior) to demonstrate practical utility beyond benchmark metrics.

## Relevance

本文直接针对稀疏自编码器在多层Transformer干预中的核心痛点（跨层特征冗余导致多层干预不可预测），提出的残差化训练策略简洁有效。对于从事机制可解释性研究、需要同时对多层SAE特征进行消融或干预的工作具有直接参考价值，尤其是对"解释方差高但干预效果差"这一常见困惑提供了定量解释。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does ReSAE perform with non-affine (e.g., low-rank, kernel-based, or shallow MLP) cross-layer predictors? Does removing nonlinear predictable structure further improve intervention quality or does it discard useful information?
- Can ReSAEs be combined with Cross-Coders or Matryoshka SAEs for a hierarchical multi-layer decomposition that captures both shared and residual features?
- What is the memory and compute overhead of storing and applying affine maps for models with hidden dimensions > 4096, and can low-rank approximations maintain the benefits?
- Does the capacity-dependent effect (ReSAEs needing higher k to outperform raw SAEs) persist at larger model scales (e.g., 70B+), where individual layers may be more specialized?
- How sensitive are results to the calibration data distribution? Would domain-shifted calibration data (e.g., code vs. prose) affect downstream intervention quality?
