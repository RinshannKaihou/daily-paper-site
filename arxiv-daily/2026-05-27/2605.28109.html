Long Live The Balance: Information Bottleneck Driven Tree-based Policy Optimization

arXiv: 2605.28109

Date: 2026-05-27

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28109

一句话总结： 本文提出基于信息瓶颈理论的IB-Score度量指标和IB-TPO框架，通过树结构采样策略在相同token预算下多生成50%轨迹，在大语言模型在线强化学习中实现更优的探索-利用平衡，相比GRPO基线提升2.9%-3.6%。
TL;DR: This paper introduces IB-Score, a metric grounded in Information Bottleneck theory to quantify exploration-exploitation balance at the step level in LLM online RL, and proposes IB-TPO, a tree-based policy optimization framework that uses IB-guided tree sampling to achieve 50% more trajectories under the same token budget, outperforming GRPO baselines by 2.9%-3.6% across math reasoning benchmarks.

核心贡献

  • IB-Score度量指标： 提出基于信息瓶颈理论的步级度量指标，通过量化推理步骤的多样性（探索）与正确答案的互信息（利用）之间的权衡，细粒度地诊断在线强化学习中的探索-利用失衡问题。
  • IBTree采样策略： 设计信息瓶颈引导的树搜索策略，在相同token预算下比独立采样多生成约50%的轨迹，同时树结构可复用为IB-Score的蒙特卡洛估计器。
  • IB-TPO框架： 将IB-Score整合为细粒度优化目标，结合局部优势（IB-based）和全局优势估计，实现更稳定的策略优化。
  • 显著的性能提升： 在Qwen3-1.7B和Qwen3-8B上，IB-TPO在MATH-500、AIME 24/25、AMC 23/24等数学推理基准上一致超越GRPO基线2.9%-3.6%，并优于TreeRL、TreePO等SOTA方法。

Key Contributions

  • IB-Score Metric: A novel step-level metric derived from Information Bottleneck theory that quantifies the trade-off between reasoning diversity (exploration) and mutual information with the correct answer (exploitation), enabling fine-grained diagnosis of exploration-exploitation imbalance in online RL.
  • IBTree Sampling Strategy: An IB-guided tree search strategy that generates approximately 50% more trajectories than independent sampling under the same token budget, while the tree structure doubles as an efficient Monte Carlo estimator for IB-Score.
  • IB-TPO Framework: Integrates IB-Score as a fine-grained optimization objective with both local (IB-based) and global advantage estimation for more stable policy optimization.
  • Significant Performance Gains: Consistent 2.9%-3.6% improvement over GRPO baselines on Qwen3-1.7B and Qwen3-8B across MATH-500, AIME 24/25, AMC 23/24, and out-of-domain benchmarks (GPQA, IFEval), surpassing TreeRL, TreePO, and IBRO.

Methodology

IB-Score Derivation: The paper reformulates the Information Bottleneck objective for multi-step LLM reasoning. For each reasoning step $s_i$, the IB objective is decomposed into an exploration term $H(s_i|q, s_{

$$J_{IB}(s_i) \approx 1 + \frac{\beta}{B} \sum_{b=1}^{B} \eta_1(s_i^b) \cdot \eta_2(s_i^b)$$

where $\eta_1$ quantifies information gain from environmental feedback (reward density ratio) and $\eta_2$ represents the model's confidence in each branch. The critical insight is that IB-Score depends on the covariance $\text{Cov}(\eta_1, \eta_2)$ — the model must strategically allocate confidence to branches where feedback is most informative.

IBTree (IB-guided Tree Search): The tree sampling proceeds in $L$ iterations. In each iteration, nodes are ranked by IB-Score and the top-$K$ are selected for branching, each generating $B$ new trajectories. This selective branching focuses exploration on the most diverse and promising reasoning directions. With parameters $(B_0, L, K, B) = (4, 9, 1, 1)$, the total trajectory count is $G = B_0 + (L-1) \times K \times B = 12$, consuming equivalent tokens to 8 independent trajectories.

Policy Optimization: The final advantage combines a global term (standard GRPO-style reward-based advantage) and a local IB-based term derived from the IB-Score surrogate objective:

$$A(s) = A_{GL}(s) + \lambda \cdot A_{IB}(s)$$

where $\lambda = 0.1$ is the optimal weight found via ablation.

Training Setup: Experiments use Qwen3-1.7B-Base and Qwen3-8B-Base, trained on DAPO-Math-17K (17K math problems) for 1 epoch with learning rate $1 \times 10^{-6}$, KL regularization weight 0.001, on 8×A100 GPUs using ms-swift + vLLM with prefix caching.

  • Sampling efficiency: IBTree generates 50% more trajectories under equivalent token budget compared to independent sampling, with highest Eff-Rate (60.2%) among all branching strategies.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: The paper offers a genuinely novel perspective by grounding the exploration-exploitation diagnosis in Information Bottleneck theory and deriving a step-level metric (IB-Score) that captures both diversity and reward informativeness. The integration of IB-Score as both a branching criterion and an optimization objective is a thoughtful design choice. However, the individual components (tree-based sampling, IB regularization, step-level advantages) have precedents in TreeRL, TreePO, and IBRO.

Rigor: The experimental design is solid — multiple model scales (1.7B, 8B), comprehensive benchmarks (in-domain math + out-of-domain), careful baselines with aligned hyperparameters, and extensive ablations (branching strategy, $\beta$, $\lambda$, step delimiter robustness). The use of avg@32 as a low-variance metric is appropriate. Training dynamics analysis (Eff-Rate, Cov($\eta_1$, $\eta_2$), IB-Score) provides interpretive depth.

Reproducibility: The paper is implemented on ms-swift (open-source), uses vLLM with prefix caching, and provides detailed hyperparameters. Code availability is claimed. The mathematical derivations are complete with appendix details.

Significance: The 2.9%-3.6% consistent improvement over strong baselines on challenging math benchmarks (AIME, AMC) is meaningful. The extension to out-of-domain tasks (GPQA, IFEval) strengthens the claim of generality.

Red flags:

  • All authors are from Alibaba, and the evaluated models (Qwen3) are also from Alibaba — potential conflict of interest, though disclosed.
  • The concurrent work IIB-LPO is not compared against because code was unavailable, which limits completeness of the comparison.
  • The tree sampling still introduces some latency overhead despite optimization (acknowledged in limitations).
  • Only evaluated on math reasoning during training; multi-task and instruction-following improvements are transfer effects, not direct training objectives.

Strengths

  • Principled theoretical grounding in Information Bottleneck theory with clear mathematical derivations.
  • IB-Score provides both diagnostic capability (understanding training dynamics) and optimization guidance (branching + advantage).
  • Elegant dual-use of tree structure: efficient sampling AND Monte Carlo estimation of IB-Score.
  • Comprehensive ablation study validating each component and key hyperparameters.
  • Strong empirical results with consistent improvements across model scales and benchmark types.
  • Step delimiter robustness experiment addresses a practical concern.
  • Clear writing with well-structured figures (training dynamics, method overview).

Limitations

  • Latency overhead: Multi-iteration tree sampling introduces wall-clock time overhead. Despite implementation optimizations, IBTree remains slightly slower than independent sampling under the same token budget.
  • Scope of evaluation: Training is only on math reasoning (DAPO-Math-17K). While out-of-domain improvements are shown, the method is not directly validated on code generation, multimodal reasoning, or function calling tasks.
  • Model scale: Experiments are limited to 1.7B and 8B parameter models. Scalability to larger models (e.g., 70B+) is not demonstrated.
  • Comparison gaps: IIB-LPO (concurrent work with similar IB-based regularization) is not compared due to unavailable code.
  • Step delimiter dependency: The method uses \n\n as step delimiter, which may not generalize well to models or tasks without clear step boundaries, despite the noise robustness experiment.
  • Single training dataset: Only DAPO-Math-17K is used for training; sensitivity to training data composition is not explored.

Relevance

本文与LLM推理能力强化学习后训练高度相关，特别是探索-利用平衡这一核心难题。IB-Score作为诊断工具和IB-TPO作为优化框架，为在线RL训练提供了理论驱动的新视角，对数学推理、代码生成等需要多步推理的任务具有直接应用价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does IB-TPO scale to larger models (e.g., 32B, 70B) and longer reasoning chains?
  • Can IB-Score be applied as a general diagnostic tool for other RL algorithms beyond GRPO (e.g., PPO, DPO)?
  • How sensitive is IB-TPO to the choice of training data distribution and difficulty level?
  • Can the IB-guided tree sampling be combined with process reward models for even finer-grained supervision?
  • How does IB-TPO perform on non-math reasoning tasks (code generation, scientific reasoning, multi-hop QA) when trained directly on those domains?
  • What is the interaction between IB-TPO and test-time compute scaling (e.g., best-of-N, majority voting)?
