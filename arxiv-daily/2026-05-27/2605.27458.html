Generic Interpretation Approach for Transformer Models Incorporating Heterogenous Attention Structures

arXiv: 2605.27458

Date: 2026-05-27

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27458

一句话总结： 提出一种通用的异质注意力结构 Transformer 模型解释方法，原理清晰、计算简洁，在 DETR 和 LXMERT 上优于 GAE 和 ODAM 基线。
TL;DR: Proposes a generic interpretation method for Transformer models with heterogenous attention structures (e.g., co-attention), achieving better segmentation accuracy and cleaner visualizations than GAE and ODAM baselines on DETR and LXMERT.

核心贡献

  • 将 Transformer 注意力结构按信息来源分为同质（self-attention）和异质（co-attention 及融合后 self-attention）两类，建立了统一的分类框架。
  • 提出一种针对异质注意力结构的通用解释方法，核心原则是"始终线性区分不同信息来源"，避免 GAE 中跨源交互带来的噪声。
  • 在 DETR 弱监督分割任务上，方法的 AP 和 AR 指标全面超越 GAE 和 ODAM。
  • 方法支持完整梯度校正（保留正负贡献）和绝对梯度校正两种模式，灵活性更高。

Key Contributions

  • Attention structure taxonomy: Classifies Transformer attention into homogenous (Type A: Q/K/V from same source) and heterogenous (Type B: co-attention; Type C: post-co-attention self-attention) based on information source.
  • Generic interpretation method: Propagates attention scores through heterogenous layers by independently tracking contributions from each information source, avoiding the cross-source interaction terms in GAE that introduce noise.
  • Gradient correction flexibility: Supports three correction modes — positive-only, absolute, and complete gradient — enabling both reductionist and holistic interpretation philosophies.
  • Simpler computation: Eliminates GAE's Equations (8) and (9) (cross-source interaction computation) and one matrix multiplication per heterogenous layer.

Methodology

The method proceeds in three steps:

  1. Multi-head aggregation: For each layer, the multi-head attention map is corrected by its gradient (via positive, absolute, or complete correction) and averaged across heads to produce a single attention map per layer.
  2. Homogenous layers: Standard attention rollout (matrix multiplication with identity-augmented attention) is used to compose layer-wise attention into a global attention map.
  3. Heterogenous layers: Contributions from each information source (source1, source2) are tracked independently through the layer using linear propagation (Eqs. 6–7), preserving source independence rather than modeling cross-source interaction.

The final interpretation is the CLS-token row of the accumulated attention score matrix, yielding per-token importance scores for each input source.

Results & Highlights

  • DETR segmentation (MSCOCO val, Otsu threshold): Ours(noised) achieves AP 13.2 / AR 19.4 vs. GAE's AP 13.1 / AR 19.3; Ours(pos) achieves AP 11.6 / AR 18.0. ODAM performs worst (AP 1.9 / AR 4.8).
  • DETR segmentation (0.3× Otsu threshold): Ours(noised) achieves AP 7.1 vs. GAE's 7.1 (tied), but Ours(pos) achieves cleaner visualizations with less noise.
  • Visualization quality: Ours(abs) and Ours(pos) produce the most accurate and least noisy segmentation masks. GAE shows spurious highlights (e.g., highlighting a second cat when explaining one).
  • LXMERT interpretation: Method enables semantic interpretation of cross-modal attention and logical inspection of model decision-making.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. The core insight — tracking source contributions independently rather than modeling cross-source interaction — is conceptually simple and well-motivated, but the mathematical machinery is largely borrowed from attention rollout and GAE.
  • Rigor: The experimental evaluation follows established protocols (same samples, same models as GAE/ODAM). However, evaluation is limited to DETR and LXMERT only — no modern multimodal models (e.g., CLIP, LLaVA, GPT-4V) are tested.
  • Reproducibility: Good. The method is clearly described with equations, and the authors use publicly available models and datasets.
  • Significance: Moderate. Interpretability of cross-attention is important, but the practical impact is limited by the narrow model selection and the fact that the improvement over GAE is incremental.
  • Red flags: The "Ours(noised)" variant (which re-adds GAE's cross-source interaction terms) sometimes outperforms the clean version, which somewhat undermines the paper's core argument that cross-source interaction is harmful noise.

Strengths

  • Clear taxonomy of attention structures that is pedagogically useful.
  • Computationally simpler than GAE with principled justification.
  • Flexible gradient correction modes supporting different interpretation philosophies (reductionist vs. holistic).
  • Thorough comparison with GAE including detailed mathematical analysis of when the two methods are equivalent.

Limitations

  • Evaluated only on DETR and LXMERT — both relatively older architectures. No experiments on modern vision-language models (CLIP, Flamingo, LLaVA).
  • The claim that cross-source interaction is "noise" is contradicted by the "Ours(noised)" variant sometimes performing best.
  • Weakly supervised segmentation is an indirect proxy for interpretation quality; direct human evaluation or faithfulness metrics are absent.
  • Limited to image and image-text domains; no audio or other modality experiments despite the paper's general claims.

相关性

该工作对理解多模态 Transformer 中异质注意力机制的信息流动有参考价值，尤其对需要解释 cross-attention 行为的研究者有直接用处。方法简洁且易于实现，但实验覆盖面有限。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the method perform on modern vision-language models like CLIP, LLaVA, or Flamingo that heavily use cross-attention?
  • Can the independent-source-tracking principle be extended to models with more than two information sources (e.g., video + audio + text)?
  • What is the faithfulness of the generated interpretations when measured by perturbation-based metrics (e.g., insertion/deletion AUC)?
  • Could the method be combined with mechanistic interpretability techniques (e.g., activation patching) for deeper model understanding?
