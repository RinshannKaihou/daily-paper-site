> **一句话总结：** 表格基础模型（TFMs）在预测性能上优于GBDT，但在共形预测框架下的条件覆盖率显著更低，揭示了性能与不确定性可靠性之间的根本权衡。

> **TL;DR:** Tabular Foundation Models achieve the highest AUC across 112 datasets but exhibit systematically lower conditional coverage (SSCS) under conformal prediction compared to GBDTs, revealing a fundamental performance-uncertainty trade-off that intensifies under high-noise and low-separability conditions.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统性评估表格基础模型（TFMs）的不确定性量化能力，涵盖112个TALENT基准数据集和20个合成数据集
- 提出结合AUC、校准（ECE）和共形预测不确定性指标（SSCS）的评估协议
- 实证发现TFMs在AUC上全面领先，但在条件覆盖率（SSCS）上系统性低于GBDT，尤其在高噪声、低类别可分性条件下差距更大
- 在合成数据集上进一步刻画了这一效应的触发条件

## Key Contributions

1. Proposes an evaluation protocol combining AUC, calibration (ECE), and conformal uncertainty metrics (SSCS) for tabular models.
2. Conducts a large-scale benchmark across 112 datasets comparing 4 TFMs (TabPFN, TabICL, PFN-v2, Mitra) against GBDTs, classical baselines, and deep learning methods.
3. Provides empirical evidence that TFMs systematically yield lower size-stratified coverage scores than traditional models, and characterizes exactly when this validity gap widens (high noise, low separability, non-Gaussian features).

## Methodology

- **Conformal Prediction Framework**: Uses the Least Ambiguous Class (LAC) score $s(x,y) = 1 - \hat{p}(y|x)$ at target coverage $1-\alpha = 0.90$. Prediction sets are constructed from calibration set quantiles, providing distribution-free marginal coverage guarantees.
- **Metrics**: Coverage Rate (CR), average Set Size (SS), Size-Stratified Coverage (SSC), and overall SSCS = $\min_k \text{SSC}(k)$. SSCS captures whether confident (small-set) predictions are reliable.
- **Models evaluated**: (i) TFMs: TabPFN, TabICL, PFN-v2, Mitra; (ii) GBDTs: XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost; (iii) Classical: KNN, Logistic Regression; (iv) Deep Learning: MLP, TabM.
- **Data**: 112 small-to-medium datasets from TALENT benchmark (≤10 classes, ≤10,000 samples) + 20 synthetic datasets with high noise and weak class separation.
- **Protocol**: 80/20 train/calibration split, hyperparameters tuned on held-out validation, results averaged over 15 seeds.

## Results & Highlights

| Model Family | AUC | SSCS |
|---|---|---|
| TFMs (best: TabICL) | 0.890 ± 0.019 | 0.494 ± 0.076 |
| GBDTs (best: LightGBM) | 0.864 ± 0.022 | 0.544 ± 0.072 |
| Deep Learning (TabM) | 0.832 ± 0.027 | 0.569 ± 0.072 |
| Classical (KNN) | 0.815 ± 0.022 | 0.637 ± 0.064 |

- **Clear inverted trend**: Higher AUC correlates with lower SSCS across model families.
- **Synthetic datasets**: On high-noise data, TFMs achieve AUC 0.924 but SSCS only 0.614; GBDTs achieve AUC 0.889 with SSCS 0.840 — a dramatic reliability gap.
- **Conditional coverage failure**: TFMs produce narrow prediction sets that fail to include the true label, making overconfident errors — particularly problematic in safety-critical applications.
- **Feature distribution sensitivity**: Under non-Gaussian or skewed features, tree-based models remain robust while TFMs lose calibration.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate-high. The performance-uncertainty trade-off in TFMs has not been systematically studied before. Applying conformal prediction to tabular foundation models is a novel angle, though the conformal framework itself is well-established.
- **Rigor**: Solid. 112 datasets, 15 seeds, multiple model families, and synthetic stress tests provide strong empirical support. The use of SSCS as a conditional coverage metric is well-justified with clear examples.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Code is publicly available, datasets are from the standard TALENT benchmark, and the methodology is clearly described.
- **Significance**: High practical relevance. As TFMs gain adoption, the finding that they produce overconfident, poorly calibrated predictions in noisy settings is crucial for deployment decisions in finance, healthcare, and other safety-critical domains.
- **Red flags**: None major. The paper is a 6-page conference paper (ESANN format), so depth is necessarily limited. The analysis is restricted to small/medium datasets (≤10K samples, ≤10 classes), so generalization to larger-scale tabular problems remains unknown.

## Strengths

- First systematic uncertainty benchmark for TFMs at scale (112 datasets, 4 TFMs, multiple baselines).
- Clear, actionable finding: practitioners should prefer GBDTs in noisy/heterogeneous settings despite lower AUC.
- Synthetic dataset experiments isolate the conditions under which the trade-off worsens, adding causal insight beyond correlational benchmarking.
- Model-agnostic conformal framework allows fair comparison across all model families.
- Well-written with intuitive examples (Group A vs Group B) explaining why conditional coverage matters.

## Limitations

- Restricted to small and medium datasets (≤10K samples, ≤10 classes); behavior on larger tabular datasets is unknown.
- Only classification tasks studied; regression uncertainty is not addressed.
- Uses a single conformal method (LAC score); alternative conformal techniques (e.g., adaptive prediction sets) may yield different conclusions.
- Does not explore model-specific uncertainty improvements (ensembling, Bayesian heads, temperature scaling).
- No distribution shift evaluation — all results are in-distribution.
- 6-page format limits depth of analysis and discussion.

## Relevance

该研究对表格数据建模实践具有直接指导意义：在噪声高、特征异质的场景下（如金融风控、医疗诊断），GBDT仍是不确定性估计更可靠的选择；TFMs更适合低噪声、少样本场景。对关注模型可靠部署和不确定性校准的研究者尤为重要。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can post-hoc calibration methods (temperature scaling, Platt scaling, isotonic regression) close the SSCS gap for TFMs without sacrificing AUC?
2. How do TFMs perform under distribution shift or out-of-distribution detection tasks?
3. Would ensemble-based or Bayesian variants of TFMs improve conditional coverage?
4. Does the trade-off persist on larger-scale tabular datasets (>100K samples)?
5. Can alternative pretraining distributions (e.g., matching real-world data statistics better) improve TFM calibration?
