> **一句话总结：** 本文发现智能体强化学习（Agent RL）训练中存在独特的"周期性熵爆发"现象——策略熵经历反复的急剧爆发与逐步回落（区别于单轮推理RL中熵的单调坍缩），将其分解为熵下降、熵爆发、回落三个阶段并给出四引理的理论解释，进而提出SEAL（分离增强智能体学习）辅助交叉熵损失在表示空间分离正确/错误轨迹，在AlfWorld和WebShop上跨4种模型、2种RL算法稳定提升性能（如Llama3.2-1B在WebShop上GRPO从0%恢复至79.69%成功率）。

> **TL;DR:** Agent RL training exhibits recurring cycles of sharp entropy eruption and gradual subsidence — qualitatively different from the monotonic entropy collapse in single-turn reasoning RL. Through 4 formal lemmas, the authors trace the root cause to high representation similarity among agent trajectories causing gradient interference that suppresses valid-trajectory likelihood. They propose SEAL, a lightweight binary cross-entropy auxiliary loss that attaches an MLP classifier to token hidden states to separate correct/incorrect trajectories, which consistently stabilizes training across AlfWorld, WebShop, and search-augmented QA on Qwen2.5-{1.5B,7B} and Llama3.2-{1B,3B} with both GRPO and GIGPO — most notably recovering Llama3.2-1B from 0% to 79.69% WebShop success rate.

## 核心贡献

- **发现Agent RL中的"周期性熵爆发"现象：** 在AlfWorld、WebShop、Search-augmented QA三种Agent任务以及Qwen2.5和Llama3.2两个模型家族上，均观察到策略熵经历"下降→爆发→回落"的反复循环，与单轮推理RL（如数学推理）中熵单调坍缩并保持低位的行为形成鲜明对比。
- **三阶段分解与四引理理论分析：** 将动态分解为(1)熵下降（格式学习主导）、(2)熵爆发（表示相似性→梯度干扰→有效轨迹似然下降）、(3)熵回落（采样多样性降低相似性→循环重启），并给出Lemma 3.1-3.4的严格数学证明。
- **揭示退化模式的累积效应：** 在熵爆发阶段，句子重复率和幻觉率显著上升，且即使熵回落后这些退化模式仍持续存在甚至被放大；极端情况下（如Llama3.2-1B在WebShop上），熵爆发导致奖励曲线完全崩溃至零。
- **提出SEAL方法：** 在token级隐藏表示上附加二分类MLP（𝑝 = σ(MLP(ℎ))），以交叉熵损失𝓛_SEAL鼓励正确/错误轨迹在表示空间分离，仅需一个超参数α且推理时零开销。
- **广泛实验验证：** 跨2个Agent基准+搜索QA、4种模型（Qwen2.5-1.5B/7B、Llama3.2-1B/3B）、2种RL算法（GRPO、GIGPO），SEAL一致提升性能且抑制退化模式。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifies "cyclical entropy eruption"** as a recurring training instability unique to agent RL, empirically observed across 3 task types and 4 model backbones — qualitatively absent in single-turn reasoning RL.
- **Provides formal theoretical analysis** with 4 lemmas (with full proofs): format-gated learning dominates Phase 1 (Lemma 3.1); high representation similarity induces gradient interference (Lemma 3.2); gradient interference reduces average trajectory likelihood (Lemma 3.3); likelihood decline on high-probability responses increases entropy (Lemma 3.4).
- **Demonstrates persistence and accumulation** of degenerate generation patterns (sentence duplication, hallucination, format errors) across eruption cycles, with severe cases causing complete training collapse.
- **Proposes SEAL**, a minimal auxiliary BCE loss (single hyperparameter α) that encourages representation separation between correct and incorrect trajectories — no inference-time cost.
- **Validates SEAL extensively**: consistent improvements across AlfWorld (6 subtasks), WebShop (score + success rate), and search-augmented QA (7 benchmarks), with notable recovery of collapsed Llama3.2-1B training.

## Methodology

**Analysis framework:** The authors track policy entropy, trajectory log-probabilities (correct/valid/wrong), representation similarity (via last-layer hidden state t-SNE), gradient interference metrics, sentence duplication ratio, and hallucination rate across training steps. They collect 1024 sampled trajectories at different checkpoints for statistical analysis.

**Three-phase decomposition:**
1. **Phase 1 — Entropy Descent:** The policy rapidly learns format validity (tool-call syntax, schema tokens, special markers). Valid action ratio surges and saturates early. Formalized via Lemma 3.1: when format proficiency lags semantic understanding, RL updates are dominated by format correction.
2. **Phase 2 — Entropy Eruption:** Correct and incorrect agent trajectories cluster tightly in representation space (Figure 3 shows t-SNE overlap across AlfWorld, WebShop, Search — markedly worse than alignment tasks like UltraFeedback). Lemma 3.2 proves this similarity amplifies gradient interference ⟨g_i, g_j⟩. Lemma 3.3 shows interference causes average likelihood decrease. Lemma 3.4 links likelihood decline on high-probability responses to entropy increase. Figure 4 empirically confirms negatively-advantaged trajectories exhibit stronger interference before eruption.
3. **Phase 3 — Entropy Subsidence:** Flattened distribution → more diverse sampling → reduced representation similarity → reduced interference → correct trajectory likelihood recovers → but convergence rebuilds similarity, restarting the cycle.

**SEAL formulation:** For each trajectory y_i with hidden states h_{i,t}, attach binary classifier p_{i,t} = σ(MLP(h_{i,t})) predicting trajectory correctness (label z_i ∈ {0,1}). Total loss: 𝓛 = 𝓛_RL + α·𝓛_SEAL where 𝓛_SEAL is the per-token averaged binary cross-entropy. The MLP classifier is trained jointly and discarded at inference.

**Experimental setup:** GRPO and GIGPO on AlfWorld (3827 instances, 6 subtask categories, max 25 env steps) and WebShop (1.1M products, 12K instructions, max 10 env steps). Models: Qwen2.5-{1.5B,7B}-Instruct and Llama3.2-{1B,3B}-Instruct (Llama3.2 uses OpenManus mid-training). Hardware: 2×A100-80GB (smaller models), 2×H200 (larger models). Follows GIGPO hyperparameters; α sensitivity studied in Appendix C.3.

## Results & Highlights

**AlfWorld (Table 1):**
- Qwen2.5-7B: GRPO avg 80.98% → GRPO+SEAL 83.79% (+2.81%); GIGPO 87.01% → GIGPO+SEAL 89.80% (+2.79%)
- Llama3.2-3B: GRPO avg 91.05% → GRPO+SEAL 95.73% (+4.68%); GIGPO 92.31% → GIGPO+SEAL 94.99%
- Llama3.2-1B: GRPO avg 82.60% → GRPO+SEAL 89.31% (+6.71%); GIGPO 85.38% → GIGPO+SEAL 93.12% (+7.74%)
- Qwen2.5-1.5B: GRPO avg 87.17% → GRPO+SEAL 94.42% (+7.25%)

**WebShop (Table 1):**
- **Llama3.2-1B: GRPO success 0.00% → GRPO+SEAL 79.69%** (training collapse fully recovered)
- Qwen2.5-7B: GRPO success 76.95% → GRPO+SEAL 80.08% (+3.13%); score 90.81 → 91.13
- Llama3.2-3B: GRPO success 63.28% → GRPO+SEAL 77.73% (+14.45%); GIGPO 71.88% → GIGPO+SEAL 78.91%
- Qwen2.5-1.5B: GRPO success 73.04% → GRPO+SEAL 81.25% (+8.21%)

**Search-augmented QA (Table 2):**
- Llama3.2-3B: GRPO avg 49.63% → GRPO+SEAL 53.28% (+3.65%), with gains on both in-domain (NQ, HotpotQA) and out-of-domain (PopQA, 2Wiki, MuSiQue, Bamboo, TriviaQA)
- Qwen2.5-7B: GRPO avg 53.35% → GRPO+SEAL 55.79% (+2.44%)

**Degenerate pattern suppression (Figure 8):** SEAL consistently reduces sentence duplication ratio and GPT-4-based degeneracy scores across both models and both RL algorithms on WebShop.

**Entropy stabilization (Appendix C.2):** SEAL produces substantially smoother entropy curves with smaller spike magnitudes across all task/model/algorithm combinations.

**Hyperparameter sensitivity (Appendix C.3):** SEAL remains effective over a broad range of α (tested: 2×10⁻⁴ to 2×10⁻¹), with best performance typically at intermediate values. No delicate tuning required.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The identification of cyclical entropy eruption as a phenomenon qualitatively distinct from single-turn reasoning RL entropy collapse is genuinely novel and well-supported. The self-perpetuating cycle mechanism (convergence → similarity → eruption → diversity → subsidence → convergence) is elegantly characterized. The contrast with prior entropy analyses (Cui et al., 2025; Shen, 2025) that only study single-turn settings is clearly drawn.

- **Rigor: Solid, with caveats.** The 4 lemmas with full proofs provide theoretical grounding, though they rely on simplifying assumptions (e.g., centered advantages, gradient flow, specific reward factorization). The empirical evidence is comprehensive: gradient interference before/after eruption (Figure 4), representation overlap visualization (Figure 3 vs. Figure 9), likelihood decrease curves (Figure 5, Figure 10), and degenerate pattern statistics (Figure 6, Figure 11). However, the lemmas are more explanatory than predictive — they rationalize observed phenomena rather than deriving novel testable predictions.

- **Reproducibility: Good.** Follows GIGPO implementation (Feng et al., 2025) with VeRL framework. Fixed random seed for environment. Detailed hyperparameters in Appendix C.1. Code is available. Hardware requirements (A100/H200) are reasonable for the community.

- **Significance: High practical value.** Agent RL training instability is a real pain point. The 0% → 79.69% recovery of Llama3.2-1B on WebShop is a striking result. SEAL's simplicity (one auxiliary loss, one hyperparameter, no inference cost) makes it easy to adopt. The phenomenon characterization may be even more valuable than SEAL itself, as it provides a diagnostic framework for the community.

- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - The benchmarks (AlfWorld, WebShop) are relatively small-scale agent tasks. Whether the same dynamics hold for longer-horizon, more complex agents (e.g., SWE-bench, web browsing with 50+ steps) remains unvalidated.
  - The Llama3.2 models required OpenManus mid-training to produce valid trajectories at all — this preprocessing step may alter the dynamics being studied.
  - SEAL's improvements, while consistent, are moderate in absolute terms on some settings (e.g., Qwen2.5-7B on WebShop: +0.32 score). The largest gains occur on smaller or more fragile models.
  - No comparison with other entropy-stabilization methods (e.g., entropy regularization from Cheng et al., 2026 or EPO from Xu et al., 2025) which are mentioned in Related Work but not used as baselines.
  - The search-augmented QA results (Table 2) show mixed in-domain vs. out-of-domain improvements, with some out-of-domain benchmarks showing marginal gains.

## Strengths

- Identifies a genuinely novel and practically important phenomenon with thorough empirical documentation across multiple settings.
- The theoretical analysis (4 lemmas) provides clear mechanistic explanation linking representation similarity → gradient interference → likelihood decrease → entropy increase.
- The self-perpetuating cycle characterization (Figure 7) is intuitive and well-supported by the before/after eruption analysis (Figure 4).
- SEAL is minimalist and well-motivated by the analysis — the intervention directly targets the diagnosed root cause.
- Extensive appendix with additional evidence (Figures 10-12), ablations (Appendix C.3), and comparison to alignment tasks (Figure 9).
- Intellectually honest: the paper explicitly states SEAL is a "simple intervention" and "we expect future work to deepen this line of inquiry with more sophisticated methods."
- The 0% → 79.69% Llama3.2-1B recovery result is compelling evidence for SEAL's practical value.

## Limitations

- **Benchmark scope:** Only 3 agent tasks (AlfWorld, WebShop, Search-QA). Longer-horizon agents, multimodal environments, and richer tool-use settings are untested. The authors acknowledge this.
- **No comparison with competing entropy-control methods:** Entropy regularization (Cheng et al., 2026), EPO (Xu et al., 2025), and FlowRL (Zhu et al., 2025) are cited but not benchmarked against SEAL.
- **Mid-training confound:** Llama3.2 models required OpenManus mid-training, which shortens or eliminates Phase 1. This changes the training dynamics being characterized and may limit generalizability.
- **Scale:** Largest model tested is 7B. Whether entropy eruption persists at 70B+ scale is unknown.
- **SEAL simplicity:** The binary classifier approach treats all incorrect trajectories equally. A more nuanced separation (e.g., format errors vs. semantic errors vs. degenerate patterns) might be more effective but is not explored.
- **Causal claims:** While the before/after eruption analysis (Figure 4) is suggestive, the paper does not perform causal interventions (e.g., artificially injecting representation similarity) to definitively establish the proposed mechanism.
- **Single-seed runs:** Environment seed is fixed at 0; no multi-seed variance reported for the main experiments.

## Relevance

本文对智能体强化学习训练动态的深入分析直接揭示了LLM Agent训练不稳定的根本机制（表示相似性→梯度干扰→熵爆发），提出的SEAL方法轻量且即插即用，对于改进基于RL的Agent训练策略、设计更稳定的Agent RL算法具有重要参考价值。对关注LLM Agent训练优化和训练动态理解的研究者尤为相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does cyclical entropy eruption persist at larger model scales (70B+) or in more complex agent environments (e.g., SWE-bench, multi-step web browsing)?
2. How does SEAL compare head-to-head with entropy regularization (Cheng et al., 2026) or EPO (Xu et al., 2025) — are they complementary or redundant?
3. Can the entropy eruption signal be used as a real-time monitoring metric to trigger adaptive interventions (e.g., dynamic α scheduling, trajectory filtering, or learning rate warmup)?
4. Would a more fine-grained trajectory classification (format errors vs. semantic errors vs. degenerate patterns) in SEAL yield better separation and stronger gains?
5. Could pre-training or mid-training interventions that build in better representation separation prevent entropy eruption from occurring at all, rather than mitigating it during RL?
