> **一句话总结：** 本文提出配对测试协议，通过四项研究证明LLM推理中batch条件可以改变拒绝决策，但效应率低（校正后约0.16%）、模型特异性强，且可通过batch-invariant内核消除，建议在实际部署的精确serving栈上验证拒绝鲁棒性。

> **TL;DR:** Batch size and scheduling in LLM serving are untested treatment variables for refusal behavior. A paired testing protocol across four studies (local discovery, 15-model extension, continuous-batch composition, and batch-invariant kernel ablation) shows that batch-conditioned refusal flips exist but are low-rate (~0.16% after adjudication), model-specific rather than universal, and eliminable via batch-invariant kernels on the tested vLLM/H100 stack. The recommendation is exact-stack validation, not prohibition.

## 核心贡献

- **配对测试协议：** 提出将batch条件作为安全变量而非仅性能变量进行测试的框架，将安全提示与能力控制配对，区分安全特异性翻转与通用输出变化
- **分阶段证据链：** 四项研究逐步收窄结论——Study A发现信号（安全翻转0.51% vs 能力翻转0.14%），经人工裁定后校正为0.16%；Study B在15个模型上未发现普遍的安全-能力不对称（比值0.94×）；Study C在连续batch下未发现聚合效应（4.7pp灵敏度）；Study D通过batch-invariant内核将22/55翻转降至0/55
- **输出不稳定性作为筛查指标：** 发现模型的输出变化率是batch脆弱性的最佳预测因子（r=0.909, bootstrap 95% CI [0.65, 0.97]），而对齐类型（RLHF/DPO等）无法预测脆弱性（p=0.942, η²=0.033）
- **机制确认：** 在vLLM 0.19.1/H100上，标准模式复现22/55标签翻转，启用`VLLM_BATCH_INVARIANT=1`后翻转归零，证明候选翻转依赖于非不变执行路径

## Key Contributions

- **Paired testing protocol** that treats batch condition as a safety variable (not just a performance variable), comparing safety and capability labels under matched serving conditions with scorer-corrected adjudication.
- **Staged evidence chain across four studies:** Study A (local discovery on 31,410 rows, 3 small models) → Study B (127,224 records, 15 models) → Study C (14,250 records, continuous batching composition) → Study D (55 candidate rows, kernel ablation). Each study narrows or corrects the previous finding.
- **Output instability as the strongest fragility predictor** (r = 0.909), while alignment type (RLHF, DPO, etc.) shows no detectable association with batch fragility (p = 0.942).
- **Batch-invariant kernel ablation** demonstrating that standard vLLM reproduces 22/55 label flips on candidate rows, while enabling `VLLM_BATCH_INVARIANT=1` eliminates all flips (0/55), providing a sharp mechanism story.
- **Corrective methodology:** Single-reviewer adjudication of 63 candidates found only 17 genuine behavioral flips (27%), with 73% attributable to automated scoring artifacts — demonstrating the gap between discovery sensitivity and operational magnitude.

## Methodology

The paper synthesizes four artifact-backed studies into a coherent testing protocol:

- **Study A (Local Perturbation):** 31,410 scored rows on three 1B–3B instruction-tuned models (Llama-3.2-1B/3B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct) under four phases: synchronized-dispatch batch-size perturbation (sizes {1,2,4,8,16,32}), neighbor-condition perturbation, quantization-by-concurrency, and explicit true batching. Includes scorer-corrected Unicode-normalization audit and a 7,257-sample reduced replication on an enriched prompt subset.

- **Study B (Cross-Model Extension):** 127,224 records across 15 distinct models spanning five families (Llama, Qwen, Gemma, Phi, and others) with pooled scoring, directional analysis, alignment-type ANOVA, and output-instability correlation.

- **Study C (Composition):** 14,250 records under five batch-composition conditions in vLLM continuous batching (FP16), with temporal-overlap sweeps, reverse-direction tests, and static-versus-continuous checks. Uses blinded LLM-judge scoring (qwen2.5:7b-instruct-q8_0 via Ollama).

- **Study D (Kernel Ablation):** 55 current score-flip candidates from Study A re-run on the same H100 pod under standard vLLM and `VLLM_BATCH_INVARIANT=1` (vLLM 0.19.1, FP16, temperature 0, max 2048 tokens).

**Evaluation design** pairs safety-axis prompts (advbench, jailbreak, BBQ bias) with capability-axis controls (MMLU, ARC-Challenge, TruthfulQA). All decoding is greedy (temperature 0) so output changes are attributable to serving conditions, not sampling noise.

**Statistical approach:** Each study retains its original statistical treatment; no post-hoc pooled significance is imposed. Claims are selected based on what survives the full evidence chain.

## Results & Highlights

| Study | Key Finding |
|-------|-------------|
| **A (Local)** | Safety flip rate 0.51% vs capability 0.14%; after adjudication: corrected rate ~0.16% (17/63 candidates are genuine flips) |
| **A (True batching)** | 0.80% safety flips with 99.4% agreement to synchronized dispatch — mechanism consistency |
| **B (15-model)** | Combined safety-to-capability ratio 0.94× (near parity); fragility range 0.00%–2.39%; alignment type ANOVA p=0.942 |
| **B (Predictor)** | Output instability correlation r=0.909 (bootstrap 95% CI [0.65, 0.97]); leave-one-out range [0.79, 0.93] |
| **C (Composition)** | Null at 4.7pp MDE; 28/31 directional flips lean unsafe (90.3%, Wilson 95% CI [75.1%, 96.7%]); only 22.1% co-batch verification |
| **D (Kernel)** | Standard vLLM: 22/55 label flips, 25 text changes; batch-invariant: 0/55 flips, 0 changes |

- The directional pattern in Study C (28/31 unsafe-leaning flips) is statistically significant (pooled p=0.006) but rests on only 31 directional flips with low co-batch verification, limiting practical conclusions.
- Directional counts across Study B are net-safe: 159 compliance-to-refusal vs 81 refusal-to-compliance flips.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The empirical question — whether batching affects refusal behavior specifically — is genuinely underexplored. The paired safety-vs-capability design is methodologically sound. However, the underlying phenomenon (batch-dependent nondeterminism) is well-known in systems literature; the novelty is in framing it as a safety testing problem and providing a protocol. Moderate novelty.

**Rigor:** The staged evidence chain is carefully constructed with explicit claim-mapping and conservative synthesis rules ("where studies conflict, the larger study determines the visible claim"). The scorer-corrected adjudication revealing 73% artifact rate is admirably transparent. However, several limitations weaken the evidence:
- Single-reviewer adjudication with no inter-rater reliability (Cohen's κ)
- Small model sizes (1B–3B) — generalization to production-scale models (70B+) is untested
- Study C's 22.1% co-batch verification rate severely underpowers the composition null claim
- Study D covers only 55 candidates on one serving stack

**Reproducibility:** Artifact availability is documented (scored outputs, summaries, scripts). However, the harmful prompt batteries are partially withheld, and the specific hardware configurations vary across studies. Moderate reproducibility.

**Significance:** The practical recommendation (exact-stack validation) is actionable and low-cost. The finding that output instability predicts fragility (r=0.909) is potentially useful for deployment screening. However, the absolute rates are so low (0.16% corrected) that the practical impact on production safety is likely minimal. The paper's main value is methodological — establishing a testing protocol — rather than revealing a critical safety gap.

**Red flags:**
- Single-author, independent researcher — limited institutional backing or diverse perspectives
- The paper explicitly narrows its own claims repeatedly, which while intellectually honest, raises the question of whether the remaining claim is substantial enough for the venue
- The 0.94× safety-to-capability ratio in Study B essentially shows parity, undermining the initial Study A signal

## Strengths

- **Intellectually honest self-correction:** The paper systematically narrows its own claims through staged evidence, explicitly separating discovery signals from operational magnitudes. This is rare and commendable.
- **Paired design:** Comparing safety and capability labels under the same perturbation is methodologically strong and prevents over-attributing generic output churn to safety.
- **Mechanism ablation (Study D):** The batch-invariant kernel result (22→0 flips) is clean and compelling.
- **Practical recommendation:** "Exact-stack validation" is simple, actionable, and low-cost.
- **Transparent artifact reporting:** The 73% scorer-artifact rate and 22.1% co-batch verification rate are openly reported rather than hidden.

## Limitations

- **Small model scale:** All studies use 1B–3B parameter models; results may not transfer to production-scale (70B+) models where batching dynamics differ.
- **Single serving stack:** Study D covers only vLLM 0.19.1 on H100; other backends (TensorRT-LLM, TGI), tensor-parallel configurations, and stochastic decoding are untested.
- **Single-reviewer adjudication:** No inter-rater reliability for the manual correction that produces the headline 0.16% rate.
- **Underpowered composition study:** 22.1% co-batch verification and 31 total directional flips limit conclusions about multi-tenant safety.
- **Workshop-scale synthesis:** The paper explicitly acknowledges it is not a monolithic benchmark, but this means individual studies may lack the depth expected at a top venue.
- **Greedy decoding only:** All experiments use temperature 0; stochastic decoding (common in production) could amplify or suppress batch effects differently.

## Relevance

本文对LLM推理系统的安全评估方法论有直接参考价值。其配对测试协议和"精确栈验证"建议可应用于任何部署场景下拒绝行为的鲁棒性检验，尤其对关注serving系统优化与安全行为交叉领域的研究者有方法论启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How do batch-conditioned refusal flips scale to 70B+ parameter models with tensor-parallel serving? The current 1B–3B results may not transfer.
2. Does stochastic decoding (temperature > 0) amplify or mask batch-dependent refusal changes? The interaction between sampling noise and floating-point reduction order is unexplored.
3. Can the output-instability screening signal (r=0.909) be operationalized as a lightweight pre-deployment test, or does it require the full paired protocol to be useful?
4. What is the inter-rater reliability of refusal-flip adjudication? A multi-rater study with Cohen's κ would strengthen the 0.16% corrected rate.
5. Do other serving backends (TensorRT-LLM, TGI, SGLang) exhibit the same batch-invariant kernel fix, or is the mechanism vLLM-specific?
