> **一句话总结：** 在FP4量化感知训练中，模型架构选择（特别是Swin Transformer）比量化配方对异常分割质量的影响更大，且高级QAT技术能防止小模型的注意力离散化并恢复大CNN的梯度量化损失。

> **TL;DR:** Architecture choice dominates FP4 quantization robustness for anomaly segmentation—Swin Transformer is robust across all scales, while advanced QAT recipes (RHT, stochastic rounding) prevent attention discretization in small models and recover gradient quantization losses in large CNNs.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统性研究模型架构、模型规模和FP4量化配方三者在异常分割任务上的交互影响
- 发现架构选择对量化鲁棒性影响最大：Swin Transformer在所有规模下均对配方选择不敏感
- 揭示小容量FP4模型中softmax注意力的离散化现象，并证明高级QAT配方可完全防止此问题
- 证明随机舍入（stochastic rounding）是恢复大CNN梯度量化噪声损失的最关键技术
- 通过5折患者级交叉验证确认发现的鲁棒性

## Key Contributions

- First controlled study of the three-way interaction between model architecture (CNN, ViT, Swin), model scale, and FP4 QAT recipe on a recall-critical segmentation task
- Demonstrates that architecture choice has the largest impact on quantization robustness, with Swin Transformer showing remarkable resilience across all scales
- Identifies and explains attention discretization failure mode in small-capacity transformers under FP4, showing that RHT and stochastic rounding prevent this collapse
- Shows that stochastic rounding is the most effective technique for recovering gradient quantization noise in larger CNNs
- Validates findings with 5-fold patient-level cross-validation on brain tumor MRI data

## Methodology

**Dataset:** LGG Brain MRI Segmentation (3,929 slices from 110 patients, 34.9% positive samples). Patient-level 80/10/10 split to prevent data leakage.

**Architectures:** 
- CNN: Encoder-decoder with Batch Normalization, configurable depth/width
- Vision Transformer (ViT): 16×16 patches, Layer Normalization
- Swin Transformer: Windowed attention with shifted windows

**Model Scales:** Three matched parameter counts (~530K, ~3.7M, ~13.7M) ensuring fair comparison.

**NVFP4 QAT Recipes:** Eight recipes spanning:
- 2D block-scaling (16×16 blocks)
- Random Hadamard Transform (RHT)
- Stochastic rounding (SR)
- Forward-only quantization
- Chain rule (reuse forward quantized values)

**Training Protocol:**
- Loss: BCE + Tversky (penalizes false negatives 2.3× more than false positives)
- Evaluation: AUPRC (threshold-free metric robust to class imbalance)
- Scheduler: ReduceLROnPlateau, early stopping on validation loss
- Augmentation: Resize, flip, brightness-contrast, shift-scale-rotate

## Results & Highlights

**Architecture Dominance:**
- Swin Transformer achieves highest AUPRC at every matched parameter scale
- Statistically significant advantage over CNN at 4M (p=0.0012) and 15M (p=0.0208)
- ViT consistently underperforms regardless of optimizer (AdamW vs Adamax)

**Scale Effects:**
- Largest AUPRC gains from 500K→4M across all architectures
- Marginal improvement from 4M→15M due to overfitting on small dataset
- 4M identified as practical sweet spot

**Small Model Failure Mode (500K):**
- Swin's attention logits become near-binary under basic FP4 recipes (NVFP4 Full, Forward-Only)
- Prediction histograms show discrete spikes, PRC curves develop staircase artifacts
- Root cause: head dimension d_h=16 gives SNR≈4, softmax amplifies quantization errors
- **Solution:** RHT smooths forward logits; SR provides unbiased gradients. All RHT/SR-equipped recipes stay within 0.017 of baseline

**Large Model Recovery (4M, 15M):**
- CNN degrades sharply under gradient-quantizing recipes (drops to ~84% of baseline)
- Stochastic rounding recovers majority of lost AUPRC
- Full 2D+RHT+SR recipe brings CNN closest to baseline
- Swin remains stable (97-103% of baseline) regardless of recipe

**Why Swin Wins:**
- Windowed attention provides local-global inductive bias (like CNN but with cross-window flow)
- Converges to flatter minima where FP4 gradient noise stays within optimization basin
- Lower input sensitivity than CNN tolerates coarser numerical representations

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. First controlled study of architecture×scale×recipe interactions for FP4 on dense prediction tasks. Prior FP4 work focuses almost exclusively on language models.

**Rigor:** Strong. Unified protocol across all architectures, matched parameter counts, patient-level data splits, statistical significance testing, and cross-validation. Isolates recipe effects from initialization variance.

**Reproducibility:** Excellent. Code released, detailed training protocol, explicit recipe definitions (Table 2), and architecture configurations (Table 1). Dataset is publicly available.

**Significance:** Practical. Provides clear guidance for practitioners deploying FP4-quantized segmentation models: use Swin Transformer for robustness, or apply advanced recipes (especially SR) for CNNs. Directly applicable to NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs.

**Red Flags:** None major. Limited to one medical imaging task (brain tumor segmentation). Unclear if findings generalize to other anomaly segmentation domains (manufacturing, other medical modalities). Dataset is relatively small (~3K training images), which may favor Swin's inductive bias over data-hungry ViT.

## Strengths

- Comprehensive experimental design with three architectures, three scales, eight recipes under unified protocol
- Identifies concrete failure modes (attention discretization) with mechanistic explanations (SNR analysis)
- Practical recommendations: Swin for robustness, SR for CNN recovery
- Statistical rigor: 10-seed experiments, 5-fold CV, p-values reported
- Clear visualization of recipe effects on PRC curves and prediction distributions
- Builds strong theoretical foundation (flatter minima, Lipschitz continuity, implicit regularization)

## Limitations

- Single dataset (LGG brain MRI) — generalization to other anomaly segmentation tasks unverified
- Binary segmentation only; multi-class or instance segmentation not studied
- Relatively small dataset (~3K training images) may bias results toward architectures with stronger inductive priors
- No comparison with FP8 or other low-precision formats
- Does not measure actual inference speedup or throughput on Blackwell hardware
- Limited analysis of why ViT underperforms beyond "data-hungry" explanation

## Relevance

本文为在NVIDIA Blackwell等下一代加速器上部署FP4量化异常分割模型提供了实证指导，证明Swin Transformer在量化鲁棒性上优于CNN和ViT，并揭示了高级QAT技术（随机舍入、RHT）在不同架构和规模下的关键作用。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do these findings generalize to other anomaly segmentation domains (manufacturing defects, other medical imaging modalities)?
- How does FP4 compare to FP8 in terms of quality-speed tradeoff for segmentation tasks?
- Can attention discretization be prevented via architectural modifications (e.g., larger head dimension, different normalization)?
- What is the actual throughput gain on Blackwell GPUs for these architectures at FP4?
- Would data augmentation or regularization help ViT overcome its data-hungry nature on small medical datasets?
- How do these recipe effects scale to larger models (50M+ parameters) on larger datasets?
- Can the findings be extended to detection or instance segmentation tasks?
