> **一句话总结：** HRBench 是首个系统性评估混合推理大语言模型中"思考模式切换"策略的统一基准，覆盖3种策略族 × 4种训练范式共12种配置，在6个模型（2B–1.1T）和5个数据集上揭示了不同策略在效率-效果权衡中的本质差异。

> **TL;DR:** HRBench is a unified benchmark that systematically evaluates thinking-mode switching strategies in hybrid-reasoning LLMs along two axes (3 strategy families × 4 training regimes), testing on 6 models from 2B to 1.1T across 5 benchmarks. It reveals that Prompt-Tuning achieves Pareto-optimal accuracy-token trade-offs, Routing provides stable cost reduction, and Speculative methods boost accuracy at higher token cost — with optimal strategy varying by model scale and task domain.

## 核心贡献

- **统一评估框架：** 提出 HRBench，首次将思考模式切换策略沿两条轴正交组合——3种策略族（Prompt-Tuning、Routing、Speculative）与4种训练范式（Training-Free、SFT、DPO/Offline RL、GRPO/Online RL），产生12种受控评估配置，并在同一管线中复现了12+种已有方法，实现公平比较。
- **系统性实验发现：** 在6个LLM（Qwen3.5-2B 至 Kimi-K2.5-1.1T）和5个推理基准（MATH500、AIME 2025、GPQA-Diamond、LiveCodeBench、Codeforces）上进行527次实验，揭示了：
  - Prompt-Tuning（PT）持续实现"双赢"——同时提高准确率并减少token开销（Qwen3.5-9B上平均准确率47.6%，token减少24%），独占Pareto前沿。
  - Routing（RT）保持稳定准确率同时获得适度token节省（准确率44.1%，token减少13%）。
  - Speculative（Spec）以提升准确率为目标但增加token开销（准确率45.8%，token增加29.6%）。
- **模型规模与任务域调节效应：** 发现最优策略随模型规模变化——在20B和671B规模下Spec超越PT，在9B和1.1T规模下PT领先；在数学和科学任务中PT更优，在代码任务中Spec更优。
- **训练效应分析：** GRPO在RT上实现最大token减少（65%），DPO在准确率上提升最大，SFT提供平衡折中。训练收益强烈依赖策略类型。
- **开源平台与参考实现：** 提供全部12种配置和12+外部方法的参考实现，基于verl训练和vLLM推理，代码开源于 GitHub。

## Key Contributions

- **Unified Evaluation Framework:** Introduces HRBench, the first benchmark that orthogonally combines 3 strategy families (Prompt-Tuning, Routing, Speculative) with 4 training regimes (Training-Free, SFT, DPO, GRPO), yielding 12 controlled evaluation settings. Reimplements 12+ prior methods within the same pipeline for fair comparison.
- **Systematic Empirical Findings:** Conducts 527 experiment runs across 6 LLMs (Qwen3.5-2B to Kimi-K2.5-1.1T) and 5 benchmarks (MATH500, AIME 2025, GPQA-Diamond, LiveCodeBench, Codeforces), revealing:
  - PT consistently achieves Pareto-optimal trade-offs (47.6% avg accuracy, 24% token reduction on Qwen3.5-9B).
  - RT preserves accuracy with moderate token savings (44.1% accuracy, 13% reduction).
  - Spec boosts accuracy at extra token cost (45.8% accuracy, 29.6% token increase).
- **Scale and Domain Modulation:** Shows optimal strategy shifts with model scale (Spec > PT at 20B/671B; PT > Spec at 9B/1.1T) and task domain (PT better for math/science, Spec better for code).
- **Training Effect Characterization:** GRPO achieves the highest token reduction for RT (65%), DPO maximizes accuracy gains, SFT offers a balanced default. Training gains are strongly strategy-dependent.
- **Open-Source Platform:** Provides reference implementations for all 12 configurations and 12+ integrated external methods using verl + vLLM, released on GitHub.

## Methodology

HRBench defines the **Thinking Mode Switch** problem formally: given a query q and a set of available thinking modes M (binary {think, no_think}, discrete effort levels, or continuous token budgets), the model adaptively selects a mode to generate response r = (τ, a).

**Three Strategy Families:**

1. **Prompt-Tuning (PT):** The model implicitly selects its thinking mode during a single inference pass via carefully designed system prompts that map to native mode interfaces (e.g., think block depth for Qwen3.5, reasoning effort levels for gpt-oss, token budgets for Seed-OSS).

2. **Routing (RT):** A two-stage classify-then-generate approach. A router (the LLM itself in training-free mode, or a trained classifier) assesses query difficulty and dispatches to the appropriate thinking mode. Judge stage runs in no-think mode with max_tokens=256.

3. **Speculative (Spec):** Two-pass architecture starting in fast (no_think) mode. Two trigger mechanisms: (a) **Trigger-based:** scans output for 55 uncertainty keywords across 6 categories (hesitation, self-correction, re-examination, alternative approach, deeper reasoning, contradiction); (b) **Entropy-based:** monitors normalized Shannon entropy from top-20 logprobs, triggering escalation when ≥3 tokens exceed model-specific thresholds (Qwen3.5: τ=0.10, gpt-oss: τ=0.08, Seed-OSS: τ=0.06).

**Four Training Regimes:**

- **Training-Free (TF):** Prompt engineering and rule-based approaches.
- **SFT:** Trained on samples that are both correct and token-minimal from rejection fine-tuning (RFT) with multiple rollouts.
- **DPO (Offline RL):** Preference pairs from RFT — chosen = correct + token-minimal; rejected = longer correct or incorrect responses.
- **GRPO (Online RL):** 8 rollouts per problem with composite reward: R(r) = α·1[correct] + β·1[correct]·max(0, 1 − t_r/t_ref), where α=1.0, β=0.5. Efficiency bonus is gated by correctness.

**Models:** Qwen3.5-2B, Qwen3.5-9B (binary switch), gpt-oss-20B (discrete effort), Seed-OSS-36B (token budget), DeepSeek-V3.1-671B (binary), Kimi-K2.5-1.1T (binary).

**Datasets:** MATH500 (high school math), AIME 2025 (competition math), GPQA-Diamond (graduate science), LiveCodeBench (code), Codeforces (competition code).

**Metrics:** Pass@1 accuracy (%) and average output token cost (including CoT).

## Results & Highlights

| Strategy | Avg Acc (Qwen3.5-9B) | Token Change vs Full-Think | Trade-off Profile |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full-Think (baseline) | 40.9% | — | Baseline |
| No-Think (baseline) | 42.4% | +66.5% reduction | Low cost, mixed quality |
| PT-TF | 47.6% | +24.4% reduction | **Pareto-optimal** |
| RT-TF | 44.1% | +12.5% reduction | Stable, conservative |
| Spec-TF (Entropy) | 45.8% | −29.6% increase | Accuracy-boosting |

**Key Findings:**

1. **PT dominates Pareto frontier:** Among 9 PT methods tested, they span the widest frontier — from RASC (53.4% acc, 5× more tokens) to CoD (34.0%, +80% token reduction). PT-TF at (47.6%, +24%) is highly competitive.

2. **Scale-dependent rankings:** At 9B and 1.1T, PT leads; at 20B and 671B, Spec overtakes. At 2B, all strategies perform similarly (13.2–14.1%). Token efficiency also varies — PT increases tokens at 2B but achieves −39% at 36B.

3. **Domain-dependent preferences:** In Math, PT achieves +5.2pp accuracy and +20.5% token reduction. In Science, PT leads with +8.6pp and +23.2%. In Code, Spec achieves the largest accuracy boost (+13.9pp).

4. **Training amplifies efficiency:** GRPO achieves 65% token reduction for RT (vs 31% for PT, 23% for Spec). DPO maximizes accuracy. SFT provides balanced gains. Training primarily teaches "when to skip reasoning" rather than improving reasoning quality.

5. **Failure modes identified:** PT over-compresses on hard problems (AIME); RT miscalibrates difficulty on GPQA (routes 93% to think-mode but accuracy still fails); Spec triggers too late on confident-but-wrong answers.

6. **External methods validate taxonomy:** All 12 reimplemented external methods exhibit trade-off patterns consistent with the strategy-level findings.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. First unified benchmark covering the full design space of thinking-mode switching (3 strategies × 4 training regimes). The formalization of the switching problem and the systematic taxonomy are novel contributions. The work addresses a genuine gap — prior methods were evaluated in isolation under incomparable settings.

- **Rigor:** Strong. 527 experiment runs across 6 models (2B–1.1T) and 5 benchmarks. All external methods reimplemented in the same pipeline. Consistent decoding parameters (greedy, temperature=0 except RASC). Clear metrics (accuracy + token cost). Per-dataset breakdowns provided.

- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code, data, and reference implementations released on GitHub. Full prompt templates and hyperparameters documented in appendix. Training pipeline uses standard verl + vLLM stack. However, some models (gpt-oss, Seed-OSS, Kimi-K2.5) may have limited accessibility.

- **Significance:** High. As hybrid-reasoning LLMs become mainstream (Qwen3.5, gpt-oss, DeepSeek-V3.1), understanding when and how to allocate reasoning compute is a pressing practical question. HRBench provides actionable guidance for deployment decisions.

- **Red Flags:**
  - Training-based evaluations limited to Qwen3.5-9B only — cannot verify if training findings generalize to larger models.
  - Single-turn reasoning only; multi-turn and agentic scenarios where mode-switching decisions compound are unexplored.
  - Limited domain coverage (math, science, code only) — creative writing, multilingual, and other domains may show different patterns.
  - Some "findings" (e.g., PT being Pareto-optimal) may partly reflect the fact that PT is the simplest strategy and benefits from the model's built-in calibration.

## Strengths

1. **Comprehensive and systematic design space:** The 3×4 taxonomy (strategies × training regimes) is well-motivated and cleanly organized. Each of the 12 configurations has a clear definition and reference implementation.
2. **Scale of evaluation:** 6 models spanning 2B to 1.1T, 5 benchmarks across 3 domains, 527 experiment runs — this is one of the most thorough evaluations of adaptive reasoning strategies to date.
3. **Fair comparison methodology:** Reimplementing 12+ external methods under identical conditions (same model, data, decoding parameters) addresses the reproducibility crisis in this area.
4. **Actionable findings:** The scale-dependent and domain-dependent strategy rankings provide concrete deployment guidance. The failure case analysis adds practical value.
5. **Clean formalization:** The three strategies are formalized with clear mathematical definitions (Eq. 1–3), making the taxonomy precise and extensible.
6. **Open-source commitment:** Full code release with plug-and-play platform benefits the community.

## Limitations

1. **Training only on 9B model:** Training-based evaluations (SFT/DPO/GRPO) are limited to Qwen3.5-9B due to compute constraints. Whether training-strategy interactions hold at 20B+ is unknown.
2. **Single-turn only:** Multi-turn dialogue and agentic workflows where mode-switching decisions compound across steps are not evaluated.
3. **Narrow domain coverage:** Only math, science, and code benchmarks. Creative writing, multilingual tasks, instruction following, and other domains may exhibit fundamentally different trade-off patterns.
4. **Binary/limited mode spaces:** While the paper discusses continuous token budgets, most experiments focus on binary think/no_think switching. The continuous budget dimension is underexplored.
5. **No dynamic/mixed strategies:** The paper evaluates each strategy independently but does not explore combining strategies (e.g., PT + Spec hybrid) or per-query strategy selection.
6. **Entropy thresholds require calibration:** Model-specific entropy thresholds need held-out validation sets, adding deployment overhead not fully discussed.

## Relevance

该工作直接服务于混合推理LLM的高效部署决策。随着Qwen3.5、gpt-oss、DeepSeek-V3.1等模型广泛采用思考模式切换机制，理解何时让模型"深度思考"、何时"快速回答"成为推理成本优化的核心问题。HRBench为研究者和工程师提供了首个可控比较平台，其策略-规模-任务域的交互效应发现对实际部署具有重要指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How do thinking-mode switching strategies perform in multi-turn agentic scenarios where decisions compound across reasoning steps?
2. Can hybrid strategies (e.g., combining PT's prompt guidance with Spec's dynamic escalation) outperform any single strategy?
3. How does the training-effect interaction change at 20B+ model scales, given that training was only evaluated on 9B?
4. What is the optimal per-query strategy selection policy — could a meta-router dynamically choose between PT, RT, and Spec based on query characteristics?
5. How do these strategies generalize to non-reasoning domains (creative writing, instruction following, multilingual tasks)?
6. Can the continuous token budget space (e.g., Seed-OSS's numeric budgets) be more effectively exploited compared to binary think/no_think switching?
