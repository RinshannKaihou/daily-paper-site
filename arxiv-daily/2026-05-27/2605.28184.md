> **一句话总结：** 本文从优化角度分析了多Token预测（MTP）与强化学习（RL）联合训练失败的原因，提出最优系数校准（OCC）方法，通过自适应调节MTP系数使联合训练稳定超越梯度截断基线。

> **TL;DR:** Joint MTP-RL training degrades because the MTP gradient's first-order correlation with RL decays while its second-order perturbation penalty persists. The proposed Optimal Coefficient Calibration (OCC) adaptively tracks the optimal MTP coefficient via a log-probability proxy, consistently matching or exceeding the detach baseline across six math reasoning benchmarks.

## 核心贡献

- 从优化视角将MTP对RL目标的逐步影响分解为一阶相关性项和二阶扰动惩罚项，统一解释了Detach、交叉熵损失和策略损失三种MTP训练机制的成败原因
- 提出最优系数校准（OCC）方法，利用对数概率代理在线自适应追踪最优MTP系数，计算开销几乎可忽略
- 在六个竞赛级数学推理基准上（AIME24/25、AMC、MATH、Minerva、OlympiadBench），使用MiMo-7B-RL和GLM-4.5-Air两种模型配合DAPO和GSPO算法，OCC均一致匹配或超越Detach基线

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretical decomposition:** The per-step effect of MTP on the RL objective is decomposed into a first-order correlation term (directional agreement between RL and MTP gradients) and a second-order penalty term (perturbation from MTP gradients), unifying three MTP training regimes under one framework.
- **Optimal Coefficient Calibration (OCC):** An adaptive scheme that sets the MTP coefficient to its closed-form optimum at every step using a log-probability proxy, requiring negligible computational overhead and no full-model gradient computation.
- **Extensive validation:** Across six competition-level math benchmarks, two models (MiMo-7B-RL, GLM-4.5-Air), and two RL algorithms (DAPO, GSPO), OCC consistently matches or exceeds the detach baseline with stable gains.

## Methodology

**Problem formulation.** For an L-smooth RL objective J(θ), when MTP is introduced, the parameter update becomes θ_{t+1} = θ_t + η(g_RL + λ·g_MTP). Substituting into the smoothness bound yields a per-step improvement lower bound with two MTP-related terms: (1) a first-order correlation term ηλ(1-Lη)⟨g_RL, g_MTP⟩ reflecting gradient alignment, and (2) a second-order penalty -Lη²λ²/2 · ‖g_MTP‖² that is always non-positive.

**Three regimes analyzed:**
1. **Detach:** MTP gradient is blocked from the main model — zero correlation, zero penalty; no benefit from MTP supervision.
2. **CE Loss:** MTP uses cross-entropy supervision while RL uses reward-weighted objectives — weak gradient correlation that cannot outweigh the perturbation penalty, causing severe degradation.
3. **Policy Loss:** MTP shares the same RL objective — initially strong correlation (correlation > penalty), but as training progresses and gradients approach flat regions, alignment decays while the quadratic penalty persists, producing a rise-then-fall curve.

**OCC derivation.** The optimal λ* at each step maximizes Δ_MTP, yielding λ* ∝ ⟨g_RL, g_MTP⟩ / (L · ‖g_MTP‖²). To avoid computing full-model gradients in distributed training, OCC uses a log-probability proxy: tracking c_t (log-prob change correlating with gradient alignment) and v_t² (log-prob variance proxying gradient norm) via exponential moving averages, setting λ_t = λ⁺ · ĉ_t / (v̂_t² + ε).

**Implementation.** Built on the veRL framework, trained for 200 steps on 128 NVIDIA H20 GPUs with batch size 128 and learning rate 1e-6. Default hyperparameters: λ⁺ = 1.0, ε = 10⁻⁸.

## Results & Highlights

| Model + Algorithm | Regime | AIME24 | AIME25 | AMC | MATH | Minerva | Olympiad | Avg. |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| MiMo-7B-RL + DAPO | Detach | 45.3 | 36.7 | 39.7 | 60.1 | 58.9 | 57.8 | 49.7 |
| | CE Loss | 16.1 | 33.3 | 33.8 | 52.3 | 47.7 | 50.0 | 38.9 |
| | Policy Loss | 38.9 | 36.7 | 39.7 | 59.7 | 57.4 | 56.2 | 48.1 |
| | **OCC** | **45.9** | **46.7** | **43.4** | **60.3** | **61.7** | **60.1** | **53.0** |
| MiMo-7B-RL + GSPO | **OCC** | **47.6** | **36.2** | **42.7** | **66.6** | **54.8** | **64.4** | **52.0** |
| GLM-4.5-Air + DAPO | Detach | 63.8 | 50.0 | 40.4 | 63.9 | 65.9 | 40.4 | 54.1 |
| | **OCC** | **64.7** | **52.8** | **40.5** | **66.6** | **67.6** | **42.7** | **55.8** |

- **CE loss severely degrades:** Average accuracy drops by ~10 points vs. Detach across all benchmarks.
- **Policy loss shows rise-then-fall:** Initially surpasses Detach but degrades as training progresses, confirming the theoretical prediction of decaying correlation.
- **OCC is consistently best:** Achieves the highest average across all model-algorithm combinations (+3.3 avg over Detach for MiMo-7B-RL + DAPO).
- **Generalizes across scales:** GLM-4.5-Air (106B-A12B MoE) results confirm OCC works at different model scales.
- **Negligible overhead:** OCC's log-probability proxy adds almost no wall-clock time compared to full-model gradient computation.
- **λ⁺ sensitivity:** Ablations show λ⁺ = 1.0 yields best average performance; the adaptive ratio already captures most dynamics.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The decomposition of MTP's effect into correlation and penalty terms is elegant and provides the first principled explanation for why joint MTP-RL training fails. The unification of three regimes under one theoretical lens is a genuine contribution.
- **Rigor:** Good. The analysis relies on the L-smoothness assumption (standard but imperfect for deep non-convex settings). The log-probability proxy is a first-order approximation acknowledged in limitations. Empirical validation is thorough with two models and two algorithms.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong. Code is released, training details are complete (128 H20 GPUs, 200 steps, specific hyperparameters), and the method is implemented in the open-source veRL framework.
- **Significance:** High. MTP is increasingly standard in modern LLMs (DeepSeek-V3/V4, Qwen3-Next, GLM-5, etc.), and the detach workaround is widely used. A principled solution that recovers MTP benefits during RL training has immediate practical value.
- **Red flags:** None major. The evaluation is limited to mathematical reasoning with verifiable rewards; generalization to RLHF with learned reward models or open-ended generation is unverified. The L-smoothness assumption provides qualitative rather than quantitative guidance.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical framework that unifies three training regimes with intuitive explanations for each regime's behavior
- OCC is lightweight — uses log-probability proxy instead of full-model gradients, making it practical for distributed training
- Comprehensive experiments spanning two base models, two RL algorithms, and six benchmarks
- Honest limitations section acknowledging L-smoothness, proxy fidelity, and domain scope
- Addresses a real and widely-felt problem in the LLM post-training community (veRL, slime, GLM-5 all encounter this issue)

## Limitations

- Theoretical analysis relies on L-smoothness, which may not hold exactly in non-convex deep learning landscapes
- Log-probability proxy is a first-order approximation that may lose fidelity under large learning rates or aggressive policy updates
- Experiments focus solely on mathematical reasoning with verifiable rewards; generalization to RLHF or open-ended generation is unvalidated
- The predefined ratio λ⁺ absorbs an unknown smoothness-dependent prefactor; optimal choice may vary across training configurations
- Only evaluated with 200 training steps; long-term training dynamics are unclear

## Relevance

本文直接解决了当前LLM后训练中MTP与RL联合训练失败的痛点，提出的OCC方法对使用MTP头的现代大模型（如DeepSeek、Qwen3-Next、GLM-5等）的RL训练具有直接实践价值，可恢复被Detach策略牺牲的多Token监督收益。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does OCC perform under RLHF with learned reward models, where the reward signal is noisy rather than verifiable?
2. Can the theoretical framework be extended to other auxiliary objectives beyond MTP (e.g., auxiliary losses in multimodal training)?
3. How sensitive is OCC to the exponential moving average decay rates used for ĉ_t and v̂_t² estimation?
4. Does OCC's benefit persist or amplify over much longer training runs (1000+ steps)?
5. Can OCC be combined with speculative decoding to jointly improve both training quality and inference speed?
