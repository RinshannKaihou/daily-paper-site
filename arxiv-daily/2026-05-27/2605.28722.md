> **一句话总结：** 提出MARI方法，通过竞争性多适配器机制和基于能量的门控模块，在不修改模型权重的情况下实现对大语言模型的自适应表示干预，在保持通用能力的同时显著提升对齐性能。

> **TL;DR:** MARI introduces a competitive multi-adapter mechanism with energy-based gating for representation intervention in LLMs. Instead of applying a fixed steering vector to all inputs, it trains multiple lightweight low-rank adapters that specialize via competitive routing, and uses propagation-response energy to decide whether an input needs intervention at all. This achieves state-of-the-art alignment on TruthfulQA, BBQ, and safety benchmarks across 6 models (7B-32B) while preserving or improving general capabilities on MMLU and ARC.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了静态线性干预的局限性：通过分析发现，不同样本所需的干预方向和强度差异显著，单一全局干预向量无法有效覆盖所有输入
- 提出竞争性多适配器（Competitive Multi-Adapter）机制：在同一注入点训练多个轻量级低秩适配器，通过赢家通吃训练和熵路由实现输入自适应干预
- 设计基于能量的门控模块（Energy-Based Gate）：利用传播-响应动力学提供无需标签的适用性信号，仅对需要干预的输入进行干预，避免对良性输入的过度干预
- 提供理论保证：推导了熵路由的风险上界和基于能量门控的误差分析
- 在6个模型（Llama-2-7B/13B, Llama-3-8B, Qwen2-7B, Qwen2.5-14B/32B）上验证了一致的提升

## Key Contributions

- **Diagnostic of linear representation hypothesis failure:** A per-sample correction vector analysis on TruthfulQA reveals that optimal intervention direction and strength are highly state-dependent, contradicting the assumption that a single global low-rank edit can align all inputs.
- **Competitive Multi-Adapter (CMA):** Multiple lightweight rank-r adapters at a single injection site, trained with winner-take-gradient hard routing and mini-batch usage balancing to encourage specialization.
- **Energy-Based Gating with off-subspace regularization:** A dedicated low-rank probe module (Energy Calibrator) injects a small perturbation and measures median propagation energy across downstream layers. Off-subspace PCA penalty improves separability between applicable and non-applicable inputs.
- **Theoretical analysis:** Risk bound showing multi-adapter improvement is guaranteed when specialization gain exceeds misrouting penalty; energy upper bound for non-applicable inputs showing it vanishes linearly with probe strength.
- **Comprehensive empirical validation** across Llama and Qwen families from 7B to 32B parameters.

## Methodology

**Framework:** Representation intervention on frozen LLMs. Given hidden state $h$ at injection layer $l^*$ and position $p^*$, an intervention function $\Phi_\psi(h) = h + \gamma s_\psi \Delta_\psi(h)$ replaces the hidden state, where $\Delta_\psi$ is a rank-$r$ factorized update $U(V^\top h + b)$.

**Competitive Multi-Adapter:** $K$ low-rank adapters $\{A_k\}_{k=1}^K$ are placed at the same injection site. During training, each sample is hard-routed to the adapter with the lowest per-sample loss (winner-take-gradient). At inference, a parameter-free entropy router selects the most confident adapter: $\hat{k}(x) = \arg\min_k u_k(x)$ where $u_k(x)$ is the predictive entropy under adapter $k$.

**Energy-Based Gate:** A separate probe module $g_\phi$ (Energy Calibrator, also low-rank with smaller rank $r_{\text{probe}}$) produces a small perturbation. The propagation response $E(x;\alpha) = \text{median}\{\|h^{(m)}_{\text{probe}}(x) - h^{(m)}(x)\|_2\}_{m=l^*}^L$ measures how strongly the perturbation propagates. An off-subspace penalty $R_{\text{off}} = \|\Pi^\perp_B \delta_\phi(x)\|_2^2$ (via PCA on in-field calibration data) shapes the probe for better separability. A threshold $\tau_E$ is calibrated on a small control set. At inference, if $E(x;\alpha_{\text{probe}}) < \tau_E$, intervention is suppressed (fallback to base model).

**Training:** Three-phase: (1) train the energy calibrator independently, (2) competitive training of $K$ adapters with hard routing, (3) calibrate threshold $\tau_E$ on control set. Only adapter parameters are trained; base model is frozen.

## Results & Highlights

| Model | Method | TruthfulQA MC1 | TruthfulQA MC2 | BBQ Bias | Refusal | MMLU | ARC-C |
|-------|--------|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| Llama-2-7B | Vanilla | 32.03 | 49.51 | 0.329 | 0.821 | 23.3 | 33.8 |
| Llama-2-7B | ReFT | 50.46 | 50.20 | 0.540 | 0.845 | 23.2 | 34.0 |
| Llama-2-7B | **MARI** | **64.35** | **61.13** | **0.751** | **0.851** | **23.6** | **33.5** |
| Llama-3-8B | Vanilla | 28.70 | 45.03 | 0.608 | 0.851 | 65.9 | 51.4 |
| Llama-3-8B | **MARI** | **61.81** | **67.39** | **0.792** | **0.866** | **66.6** | **52.1** |
| Qwen2.5-32B | Vanilla | 43.98 | 59.81 | 0.736 | 0.621 | 83.5 | 59.3 |
| Qwen2.5-32B | **MARI** | **81.94** | **70.59** | **0.876** | **0.653** | **84.2** | **60.0** |

- **TruthfulQA MC1 improvements:** +12-38 absolute points over ReFT across all models, with the largest gains on bigger models (Qwen2.5-32B: 81.94 vs 55.60 for ReFT).
- **General capability preservation:** MMLU and ARC scores are maintained or slightly improved (e.g., MMLU 83.5→84.2 on Qwen2.5-32B), while removing the energy gate causes severe regression (MMLU drops to 74.5 on Qwen2.5-32B).
- **Ablation findings:** Removing multi-adapter causes drastic alignment decline; removing energy gating slightly improves alignment but destroys general capabilities. The gate is essential for the alignment-utility trade-off.
- **Scalability:** Gains are more pronounced on larger models, suggesting the method leverages richer latent structure in higher-capacity models.
- **Threshold transfer:** The calibrated threshold $\tau_E$ transfers across benchmarks without per-task recalibration.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The combination of competitive multi-adapter routing and energy-based gating is novel. The diagnostic showing the failure of the linear representation hypothesis is insightful and well-motivated. Individual components (mixture of experts, energy models) are not new, but their combination for representation intervention is a meaningful contribution.
- **Rigor:** Solid experimental design with 6 models across 2 families and 3 scales. Results reported with mean±std. Comprehensive ablation. Theoretical analysis provides risk bounds and energy upper bounds. However, the theoretical results rely on several assumptions (bounded loss, attenuation of propagation gain for non-applicable inputs) whose practical validity is not verified.
- **Reproducibility:** Code is publicly available. Experimental settings are well-documented with clear hyperparameter descriptions. The control set construction for threshold calibration and PCA rank choices are detailed in appendix.
- **Significance:** High practical significance. Representation intervention is a growing paradigm for LLM alignment without weight modification. MARI addresses real limitations (over-intervention, input heterogeneity) with a practical solution that scales from 7B to 32B.
- **Red flags:** None major. The ablation shows that without the energy gate, alignment scores are sometimes higher, suggesting a trade-off that users need to accept. The method adds inference overhead (multiple forward passes for energy computation and routing). The entropy router requires computing predictions from all K adapters at inference.

## Strengths

- Addresses a well-identified limitation (input-dependent intervention needs) with clear empirical motivation before proposing the solution
- The energy-based gating is label-free at inference, requiring only a small calibration set — practical for deployment
- Comprehensive evaluation across model families and scales with consistent improvements
- Strong theoretical grounding with risk bounds and energy bounds
- Preserves general capabilities, solving a real pain point of prior methods
- Threshold transfers across benchmarks without recalibration

## Limitations

- **Inference overhead:** Energy computation requires an additional forward pass with the probe module; entropy routing requires K forward passes through all adapters. This could be significant for latency-sensitive applications.
- **Hyperparameter sensitivity:** Number of adapters K, rejection rate ρ, probe rank, PCA rank, and injection layer all need tuning. The paper shows sensitivity to K and ρ but not exhaustive analysis for other hyperparameters.
- **Control set requirement:** Threshold calibration needs a labeled control set with both applicable and non-applicable examples, which may not always be available in new domains.
- **Single-layer injection:** Intervention is applied at a single layer-position pair. Multi-layer intervention could potentially yield further gains.
- **Evaluation scope:** Limited to truthfulness, bias, and safety alignment. Other alignment dimensions (helpfulness, instruction-following, code generation quality) are not explored.
- **Scalability beyond 32B:** Not tested on 70B+ models where inference overhead would be more pronounced.

## Relevance

本文提出的MARI方法对LLM对齐领域有重要参考价值。其竞争性多适配器和能量门控的设计思路可推广到其他推理时模型控制场景，特别是在不修改权重的前提下实现精细化的模型行为调控，对于需要灵活部署和快速迭代的场景尤为实用。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does MARI perform on instruction-following and helpfulness benchmarks beyond truthfulness and safety?
2. Can the energy gate be replaced with a learned lightweight classifier that avoids the extra forward pass overhead?
3. How sensitive is the method to the choice of injection layer, and could multi-layer intervention further improve results?
4. What is the wall-clock inference cost compared to single-adapter ReFT across different batch sizes?
5. Can the competitive multi-adapter framework be combined with steering vector methods (e.g., CAA) instead of low-rank adapters?
6. How does the method behave under adversarial inputs designed to bypass the energy gate?
