Revealing Algorithmic Deductive Circuits for Logical Reasoning

arXiv: 2605.27824

Date: 2026-05-27

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27824

一句话总结： 本文通过因果中介分析技术，在多个LLM中定位了负责演绎推理各子步骤（前提选择、规则选择等）的稀疏注意力头电路（约占总头数3%），揭示了模型从有限示例中学习图遍历算法的内在机制。
TL;DR: This paper uses causal mediation analysis to localize sparse attention-head circuits (~3% of total heads) responsible for sub-steps of deductive reasoning (premise selection, rule selection, etc.) across multiple LLMs, revealing how models internally "learn" graph traversal algorithms from few-shot demonstrations.

核心贡献

  • 推理组件分解与不确定token识别： 提出将演绎推理过程分解为六个推理组件（语法、KB中前提、前提选择、前提选择终止、规则选择、事实推导），并发现前提选择、前提选择终止和规则选择三个组件对应的token在多种LLM中始终表现出低置信度（概率 < 0.8），这些"不确定token"实际上是引导推理方向的关键位置。
  • 演绎推理电路网络发现： 利用激活补丁（Activation Patching）和路径补丁（Path Patching）技术，在Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct、Qwen3-8B、Phi-4和Qwen3-4B四个模型中定位了负责各推理组件的稀疏注意力头子集（约占总头数3%），并构建了它们之间的信息流图。
  • 层级化计算结构揭示： 发现LLM演绎推理呈现明确的时序计算结构——早中层负责事实/规则的信息读取与规则条件匹配，高层负责信息整合与全局推理策略（如BFS/DFS图遍历算法）的执行。
  • 跨数据集泛化验证： 在合成数据上识别的推理头在ProntoQA、ProofWriter等真实逻辑推理基准上消融后性能大幅下降（近乎归零），而在MMLU通用知识任务上仅轻微下降，证实了电路的领域泛化性。

Key Contributions

  • Reasoning component decomposition and uncertain token identification: The authors decompose deductive reasoning into six components and demonstrate that three of them — premise selection, premise selection termination, and rule selection — consistently correspond to low-confidence tokens (probability < 0.8) across diverse LLMs. These "uncertain tokens" are the critical positions that steer the reasoning process, as decoding them requires satisfying multiple implicit constraints simultaneously (e.g., KB validity, rule applicability, and traversal algorithm consistency).
  • Deductive reasoning circuit network discovery: Using activation patching and path patching techniques from mechanistic interpretability, the authors localize sparse subsets of attention heads (~3% of total) responsible for each reasoning component in Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen3-8B, Phi-4, and Qwen3-4B. They further map the information flow graph among these heads, revealing a modular circuit network where each circuit handles one reasoning sub-task and interacts with others.
  • Hierarchical computational structure: The analysis reveals a consistent temporal computational structure across all evaluated LLMs: early-to-middle layers perform factual/rule-based information reading and rule-condition matching, while higher layers integrate information and coordinate global reasoning strategies (e.g., BFS vs. DFS graph traversal algorithms). The pipeline follows: matching rule condition → implementing traversal algorithm → selecting premise and rule → premise decision (termination).
  • Cross-dataset generalization: Ablating the identified reasoning heads causes near-zero performance on synthesized data and established logical reasoning benchmarks (ProntoQA, ProofWriter), while only slightly affecting MMLU general knowledge performance — confirming that the discovered circuits are specialized for deductive reasoning and generalize beyond the synthetic training distribution.

Methodology

The study adopts a Symbolic-Aided Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting framework where deductive reasoning problems are formalized as inference graphs over facts and rules, enabling precise alignment between reasoning steps and token positions.

Preliminary Analysis (Section 2): A synthesized dataset (500 samples per k-shot setting, k ∈ {2,3,5,7,9}) with single-character premises (A–Z), 8–18 rules/facts, and BFS traversal is constructed. Token probabilities are cached across four LLMs, and tokens are categorized by their reasoning-component role. Tokens with probability < 0.8 are flagged as "uncertain" and analyzed.

Circuit Discovery (Section 3):

  1. Activation Patching: For each reasoning component r ∈ {premise selection, premise selection termination, rule selection}, clean-corrupted prompt pairs are synthesized by systematically modifying causal elements (facts, rules, demonstrations). The Average Indirect Effect (AIE) is computed for each attention head by restoring clean activations at corrupted positions and measuring logit recovery at the uncertain token position.
  2. Path Patching: Given the set of important heads A identified by activation patching, path patching measures the causal influence between pairs of heads (head_emit, head_rec) by corrupting one head's activation in the clean forward pass and restoring the downstream head's activation in a second pass. This yields a ranking score S quantifying information flow strength between head pairs.
  3. Corruption Types: Four corruption types are designed to isolate different reasoning roles: (c=1) fact modification → [Read Fact, Select Premise]; (c=2) rule condition modification → [Read Rule Condition, Match Rule Condition]; (c=3) rule content modification → [Read Rule, Select Rule]; (c=4) traversal algorithm substitution in demonstrations → [Read Traversal Alg., Implement Traversal Alg.].

Validation (Section 4.3): Top-k reasoning heads are ablated (set a^ℓ_j = 0) and evaluated on synthesized data (inference-step accuracy), ProntoQA, ProofWriter (final-answer accuracy), and MMLU (general knowledge). Six configurations are compared: baseline, random 3% ablation, and targeted ablation of rule-selection, premise-selection, premise-termination heads individually and combined (~3% total).

Results & Highlights

  • Sparse, specialized heads: Approximately 3% of total attention heads across all evaluated LLMs are identified as critical for deductive reasoning. Rule selection heads exhibit extreme sparsity — a single head in Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct accounts for >30% of the AIE score for rule selection.
  • Consistent hierarchical structure across models: Reading heads (e.g., Read Fact, Read Rule) concentrate in earlier layers; decision heads (e.g., Select Premise, Select Rule) appear in middle-to-later layers. Rule-condition matching heads are the earliest reasoning decision heads, consistent with human reasoning where rules must be validated before application.
  • Polysemantic heads: Many attention heads perform multiple reasoning sub-tasks. Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct shares heads primarily for causal reading roles, whereas Qwen models share heads across decision-making roles. Polysemantic decision heads serve as information integration centers.
  • Ablation results:
  • On synthesized data: ablating the three reasoning components (/3Roles) causes reasoning ability to collapse to near zero across all LLMs and all k-shot settings, far exceeding random ablation.
  • On ProntoQA and ProofWriter: targeted ablation causes substantially larger drops than random ablation (e.g., phi-4 drops to 2.6% on ProofWriter with /3Roles vs. 51.0% baseline).
  • On MMLU: individual component ablation yields drops comparable to random ablation, but /3Roles causes substantially larger degradation, indicating that deductive reasoning heads are also recruited for broad knowledge tasks.
  • Cross-model consistency: The uncertain-token pattern and circuit localization results are consistent across Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen3-8B, Phi-4, and Qwen3-4B, despite different architectures and parameter counts.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The paper addresses a genuine gap — prior circuit analysis focused on simple input-output mappings, while this work tackles multi-step CoT reasoning processes. The decomposition of deductive reasoning into six components with associated uncertain tokens is a useful analytical framework. However, the core CMA techniques (activation patching, path patching) are well-established, and the contribution is primarily in their application to a new setting rather than methodological innovation.
  • Rigor: The experimental design is solid — four diverse LLMs, multiple k-shot settings, four corruption types, and validation on three external benchmarks (ProntoQA, ProofWriter, MMLU). The use of inference-step accuracy (rather than just final-answer accuracy) on synthesized data is appropriate. However, the statistical significance of ablation results (e.g., error bars, number of random seeds) is not fully detailed.
  • Reproducibility: The paper provides detailed algorithmic descriptions (Algorithm 1 in Appendix C) and clear corruption procedures. The synthesized dataset construction is well-documented. Code and data availability are not explicitly mentioned.
  • Significance: The finding that ~3% of heads are responsible for deductive reasoning and that their ablation causes near-total collapse on logical reasoning tasks while minimally affecting general knowledge is practically significant for interpretability and targeted model editing. The hierarchical computational structure finding (rule matching → traversal → selection → termination) provides actionable insights for improving reasoning robustness.
  • Red flags: The synthesized dataset uses single-character premises (A–Z), which is a significant simplification. While the authors acknowledge this and validate on real benchmarks, the gap between synthetic and naturalistic reasoning remains. The claim that LLMs "learn" graph traversal algorithms at an abstract level is somewhat overstated — what is shown is that specific heads mediate the causal effect consistent with traversal behavior, not that the model has an explicit internal representation of BFS/DFS.

Strengths

  • Systematic decomposition of multi-step deductive reasoning into analyzable components with clear operational definitions.
  • Cross-model validation on four architecturally diverse LLMs strengthens generalizability claims.
  • The corruption-type design elegantly isolates different reasoning roles (fact reading vs. premise selection vs. rule matching vs. traversal algorithm).
  • Ablation experiments on both synthetic and real benchmarks (ProntoQA, ProofWriter, MMLU) provide strong evidence for circuit specificity and generalization.
  • Clear visualization of AIE heatmaps and circuit network graphs aids interpretability of the findings.

---

Limitations

  • Analysis is conducted on synthesized datasets with explicit logical structures and single-character premises, which may not generalize to free-form text reasoning or implicit chain-of-thought in naturalistic contexts.
  • Only attention mechanisms are analyzed; MLP layers and other transformer components that may contribute to reasoning are not investigated.
  • The identified circuits are specific to the models and datasets studied; generalization to different architectures (e.g., MoE, larger scale models >8B), scales, and reasoning domains is not demonstrated.
  • The study focuses on deductive reasoning only; inductive, abductive, and analogical reasoning circuits may differ substantially.
  • No intervention or editing experiments are performed to demonstrate practical utility beyond ablation-based validation.
