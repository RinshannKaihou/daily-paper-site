> **一句话总结：** 复数神经网络并非普遍优于实数网络，其优势取决于数据表示、对称性匹配和优化协议，且在公平调参下优势往往大幅缩小。

> **TL;DR:** Complex-valued Neural Networks (CVNNs) provide conditional, not universal, advantages over real-valued models. Gains depend on where task-relevant information lives (magnitude, phase, or their coupling), whether complex multiplication's U(1)-equivariant structure matches the task geometry, and whether evaluation protocols use fair hyperparameter selection. On RadioML 2018.01A, a 22.94 PP CVNN advantage under matched-shared-trial selection collapses to 2.46 PP under independent per-family tuning.

## 核心贡献

- **表示优先的评估协议：** 提出将 CVNN 与笛卡尔实数、极坐标、纯相位、纯幅度、参数匹配和 FLOP 匹配的实数基线进行系统对比，分离复数运算本身与坐标表示、信息瓶颈和容量的贡献。
- **复数卷积的结构等价性证明：** 证明一维复数卷积严格等价于 U(1)-等变的实数两通道卷积（核约束在 aI+bJ 子空间），将复数层定位为对称性约束的实数层而非神秘的新建模原语。
- **跨领域压力测试：** 在合成射频（PSK/QAM）、量子波函数动量分类和 EEG 解析信号三个领域验证，发现不同信息结构（相位主导、幅度主导、相位-幅度耦合）各自偏好不同的坐标视图。
- **基准测试方法论警示：** 揭示 RadioML 2018.01A 上的选择规则伪影——匹配共享试验下 22.94 PP 的差距在独立逐族调参下仅剩 2.46 PP，梯度遥测追踪到高学习率导致的死 ReLU 机制。

## Key Contributions

- **Representation-first evaluation protocol:** Systematically compares native CVNNs against Cartesian real, polar, phase-only, magnitude-only, parameter-matched, and FLOP-matched real baselines, separating complex arithmetic from coordinate access and information bottlenecks.
- **Structural equivalence of complex convolution:** Proves that 1D complex convolution is exactly the U(1)-equivariant subspace of stacked-real two-channel convolution (kernel taps constrained to the aI+bJ subspace), reframing complex layers as symmetry-constrained real layers.
- **Cross-domain stress tests:** Validates across synthetic RF (PSK/QAM), quantum wavefunction momentum classification, and EEG analytic-signal tasks, finding that phase-dominant, amplitude-dominant, and phase-amplitude-coupled tasks each favor different coordinate views.
- **Selection-rule caution for CVNN benchmarking:** Identifies a methodological artifact on RadioML 2018.01A where a 22.94 PP matched-shared-trial CVNN advantage collapses to 2.46 PP under independent per-family tuning, traced via gradient telemetry to high-learning-rate dead-ReLU instability in real baselines.

## Methodology

The paper adopts a **representation-first evaluation** framework with five diagnostic axes:

1. **Coordinate views:** Each complex-valued signal is presented in multiple real-valued views — Cartesian (x, y), polar (r, cos θ, sin θ), phase-only (cos θ, sin θ), magnitude-only (r) — encoding different hypotheses about where discriminative information resides.

2. **Model-family controls:** CVNNs are compared against stacked-real, polar, parameter-matched real, and FLOP-matched real baselines, isolating the effect of complex arithmetic from capacity and coordinate access.

3. **Selection rules:** Two evaluation protocols are contrasted — matched-shared-trial (same hyperparameters for all families, testing robustness) vs. independent per-family tuning (each family gets its own best hyperparameters, testing peak performance).

4. **Theoretical grounding:** Uses Wirtinger calculus for complex backpropagation, formalizes a Liouville Activation Trilemma (no bounded, entire, non-constant complex activation exists), and proves the U(1)-equivariant equivalence of complex convolution via Proposition 1.

5. **Gradient telemetry:** Per-step gradient norm analysis and a learning-rate × activation factorial diagnose the mechanism behind inflated benchmark gaps.

## Results & Highlights

- **Synthetic RF tasks:** PSK-only modulation favors phase-aware and complex models; QAM-only favors magnitude-based models; mixed PSK+QAM gives only a small edge to CVNNs; unseen carrier-phase rotations break coordinate-dependent models without augmentation.
- **Quantum wavefunction pilot:** Momentum classification is impossible from |ψ| alone (magnitude-only models remain at chance) but solvable from phase information.
- **EEG analytic-signal pilot:** Phase locking events favor phase features, amplitude bursts favor magnitude, and phase-amplitude coupling favors joint polar representations.
- **RadioML 2018.01A:** CReLU complex model exceeds best real baseline by **22.94 PP** under matched-shared-trial selection, but the gap collapses to **2.46 PP** under independent per-family tuning with the same 16-trial search space.
- **Mechanism:** Gradient analysis traces the inflated gap to high-learning-rate first-step instability — AdamW pushes real baselines into dead-ReLU regime, while complex (ℜ, ℑ) parameter coupling distributes the loss signal more robustly.
- **Factorial confirmation:** A learning-rate × activation factorial confirms the failure is primarily **hyperparameter-driven**, not activation-driven.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The representation-first protocol and the selection-rule artifact identification are genuinely new contributions. The U(1)-equivariant equivalence, while not entirely new, is made concrete and empirically actionable.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The five-axis diagnostic framework systematically rules out confounds. Gradient telemetry and factorial analysis provide mechanistic explanations rather than just reporting numbers.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code is publicly available on GitHub. The protocol is well-defined with clear baseline specifications.
- **Significance:** High for the CVNN community. The selection-rule caution should change how CVNN-vs-real comparisons are reported. The cross-domain stress tests generalize the findings beyond RF.
- **Red flags:** Single-author paper with limited domain scope in pilot studies (quantum and EEG are "pilot" scale). The theoretical results (Propositions 1 and 2) are well-known in their respective communities but are applied here in a new context.

## Strengths

- Rigorous separation of representation, symmetry, capacity, and optimization effects — the cleanest CVNN evaluation to date.
- The selection-rule artifact finding is immediately actionable for the community and should become standard reporting practice.
- Cross-domain validation (RF, quantum, EEG) strengthens the generalizability of the representation-first claim.
- Clear theoretical framing connecting CVNNs to group-equivariant deep learning via the U(1)-equivariant subspace.
- Gradient telemetry provides mechanistic insight beyond accuracy numbers.

## Limitations

- Quantum wavefunction and EEG experiments are explicitly labeled as **pilot studies** — small-scale and not intended as domain benchmarks.
- Only one real-world benchmark (RadioML 2018.01A) is used for the selection-rule analysis; generalization to other datasets is untested.
- Single-author work with no peer review yet; some claims (e.g., the scope of the selection artifact) may be overstated without broader validation.
- The U(1)-equivariant equivalence is proven only for 1D convolutions; extension to 2D (e.g., for MRI or image data) is not addressed.
- Does not propose new architectures — purely analytical/evaluative in nature.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the selection-rule artifact (matched-shared-trial vs. independent per-family) affect other architecture comparisons beyond CVNNs (e.g., Transformers vs. CNNs)?
2. Can the U(1)-equivariant equivalence be extended to 2D complex convolutions for MRI or image reconstruction tasks?
3. Would explicitly U(1)-equivariant real-valued networks (without complex arithmetic) match CVNN performance while being easier to implement in standard frameworks?
4. How does the representation-first protocol perform on larger-scale quantum and EEG benchmarks beyond pilot studies?
5. Can the Liouville Activation Trilemma guide the design of new complex activations that better balance boundedness, holomorphy, and phase equivariance?
