Outer-Momentum Restarting in High-Dimensional Two-Phase Optimization

arXiv: 2605.28585

Date: 2026-05-27

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.28585

一句话总结： 在DiLoCo等两阶段分布式优化器中，周期性地重置外层动量缓冲区可利用相位消除效应丢弃过时动量，从而显著扩大外层学习率和动量系数的稳定范围，且对精确重启周期不敏感。
TL;DR: Periodically resetting the outer momentum buffer in two-phase distributed optimizers (e.g., DiLoCo) exploits phase cancellation in the oscillatory contraction dynamics, widening the stable hyperparameter region for outer learning rate and momentum — validated both theoretically under an NTK linearized model and empirically on 150M-parameter LLaMA pretraining.

核心贡献

  • 外动量重启机制： 提出在两阶段优化器中周期性重置外层动量缓冲区（m ← 0），作为一种简单且互补的机制来控制外层优化器的"记忆"长度。
  • 模式级重启收缩理论： 在线性化平方损失/经验NTK模型下，推导出闭合形式的重启收缩因子 χ_K(σ)，证明重启通过相位消除（phase cancellation）机制在保留内层循环进度的同时丢弃过时动量。
  • 超参数鲁棒性提升： 实验证明周期重启扩大了外层学习率 ν 和动量系数 β_out 的稳定区域，尤其在长通信周期（S=512）下消除了高 β_out 导致的训练发散。
  • 重启周期不敏感性： 发现方法对精确重启周期 K 相当不敏感，相似的最优 K 值可在不同通信周期间迁移，使 K 比 β_out 更容易调节。

Key Contributions

  • Outer-momentum restart mechanism: Proposes periodically resetting the outer momentum buffer (m ← 0) in two-phase optimizers as a simple complementary control mechanism for outer-loop memory.
  • Mode-wise restart contraction theory: Derives a closed-form contraction factor χ_K(σ) under the empirical NTK linearized model, showing restarts exploit phase cancellation — discarding stale momentum while preserving inner-loop progress.
  • Hyperparameter robustness: Demonstrates empirically that periodic restarts widen the stable region of outer learning rate and momentum, eliminating divergence at high β_out especially for long communication periods (S=512).
  • Restart-period insensitivity: Shows the method is fairly insensitive to the exact restart period K, and that good K values transfer across communication lengths, making K a more forgiving hyperparameter than β_out.

Methodology

Theoretical framework:

  • Uses a deterministic mode-wise surrogate based on the empirical Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK). The residual dynamics are diagonalized into independent scalar modes, each governed by eigenvalue λ.
  • Inner loop: S local gradient descent steps with step size η, producing a pseudo-gradient g_t = σx_t where σ = 1 - (1 - ηλ)^S captures effective progress.
  • Outer loop: Heavy-ball (HB) or Nesterov (NAG) momentum with learning rate ν and momentum β_out. The authors derive the 2×2 transfer matrices T_HB(σ) and T_NAG(σ).
  • Key insight: In the complex-eigenvalue regime (which is the typical operating regime for high β_out), the residual oscillates within an envelope of magnitude ρ = √β_out. Restarting at period K yields contraction χ_K(σ) = ρ^K [cos(Kφ) + C sin(Kφ)]. Restart improves over no-restart when the phase-dependent projection term has magnitude < 1.

Experiments:

  1. Toy scalar/multi-mode experiments: Verify the predicted contraction behavior for single-mode, multi-mode, and blockwise spectra. Per-mode and blockwise restart periods capture additional gains over global restart.
  2. LLaMA pretraining (150M parameters): Pretrained on the C4 dataset following standard DiLoCo setup. Sweeps outer learning rate ν, momentum β_out, communication period S ∈ {64, 128, 512}, and restart period K.

Results & Highlights

  • Toy experiments: HB with restart matches tuned NAG without restart on single modes; on heterogeneous spectra, restarts consistently improve convergence for both HB and NAG.
  • 150M LLaMA pretraining at S=512: Without restarts, high β_out (≥0.9) causes clear perplexity degradation (e.g., >44 perplexity). With restart K=3, perplexity stays in the 24–26 range across all β_out values.
  • Cross-period transfer: Hyperparameters tuned at S=128 transfer well to S=64 and S=512 when restarts are used, while no-restart baselines degrade sharply — especially for HB where high β_out can diverge.
  • K as a forgiving hyperparameter: Performance curves are relatively flat across K ∈ {2, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11}, whereas sweeping β_out without restart shows sharp degradation.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. Momentum restarting is a classical technique in accelerated optimization (O'Donoghue & Candès, 2015; Giselsson & Boyd, 2014). The novelty lies in applying it specifically to the outer optimizer in two-phase training and providing a mode-wise analysis under the NTK framework. The combination is new but the individual ingredients are well-known.
  • Rigor: The theoretical analysis is clean and well-presented, with closed-form contraction results. However, it relies on the linearized/NTK approximation, which may not fully capture nonlinear training dynamics. The complex-regime analysis is thorough and correctly identifies it as the typical operating regime.
  • Reproducibility: Good. The method is simple to implement (reset m ← 0 every K rounds), the experimental setup follows standard DiLoCo protocols, and the paper provides sufficient detail.
  • Significance: Moderate-to-high for practitioners using DiLoCo-style training. The finding that restarts widen the stable hyperparameter region is practically valuable, especially given that two-phase methods already introduce an extra hyperparameter layer. However, the experimental scale (150M) limits confidence in scalability.
  • Red flags:
  • Only 150M-parameter experiments; no evidence that results hold at billion-parameter scale where DiLoCo is typically deployed.
  • The paper does not compare against adaptive restart strategies or other momentum correction mechanisms beyond simple periodic resets.
  • Per-mode or blockwise restart (shown beneficial in toy experiments) is not explored in the LM pretraining setting.

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical analysis with interpretable closed-form results connecting restart period to oscillation phase.
  • Identifies a genuinely useful and simple-to-implement mechanism (single line of code change).
  • Demonstrates robustness benefits clearly through systematic hyperparameter sweeps.
  • Good distinction between HB and NAG mechanisms and how restarts complement each differently.
  • Cross-period transfer experiments convincingly show K is easier to tune than β_out.

Limitations

  • Scale: Only tested at 150M parameters. DiLoCo is designed for large-scale distributed training; the benefits may differ at billion-parameter scale where communication overhead and gradient noise interact differently.
  • NTK approximation: The theoretical analysis assumes a fixed NTK throughout training, which is known to break down for finite-width networks and may miss important nonlinear effects.
  • Fixed-period restart only: The paper acknowledges adaptive restart as future work but does not explore it. Simple function-based or gradient-norm-based restart criteria could be more effective.
  • No comparison with other stability mechanisms: Does not compare against techniques like gradient clipping, learning rate warmup for the outer optimizer, or adaptive outer optimizers.
  • Limited scope of experiments: Only one architecture (LLaMA), one dataset (C4), and one inner optimizer configuration are tested.

Relevance

本文直接涉及分布式LLM训练中的通信效率优化问题，特别是DiLoCo系列方法的外层优化器调参难题。对于关注大规模模型训练效率、分布式优化和超参数鲁棒性的研究者具有较高参考价值，周期性重启的简洁性使其易于在实际训练流程中部署验证。

Questions for Further Exploration

  2. Can adaptive restart criteria (e.g., based on outer-momentum norm or pseudo-gradient alignment) outperform fixed-period restarts?
  3. How do per-layer or blockwise restart periods interact with the layer-wise heterogeneity of effective spectra in real transformer training?
  4. Can restarts be combined with Nesterov outer momentum for additive gains, or are the mechanisms redundant?
