> **一句话总结：** 本文通过层间因果追踪与残差流分析首次系统揭示：自主式 LLM 智能体在多轮规划中会随交互轮次递进式动员更深层次计算，并呈现出与静态任务截然不同的"构建-精炼"深度分配模式。

> **TL;DR:** Through causal layer-skipping, residual cosine similarity, and effective-depth probes on full multi-turn agent trajectories across Deep Research, Code Generation, and Tabular Processing, the authors show that LLM agents progressively recruit deeper layers, shift from feature accumulation to active feature correction, and exhibit a pronounced construction-refinement gap that varies across Qwen, Minimax, and GLM model families.

## 核心贡献

- **渐进式层级动员（Progressive Layer Mobilization）**：在多轮交互中，模型随轮次推进而动员更多、更深的层；早期轮次呈局部稀疏依赖，后期轮次演化为稠密的长程因果链。
- **特征修正动力学强化（Intensified Feature Correction）**：通过残差余弦相似度发现，后期轮次中中间层和深层频繁出现正负相位切换，说明模型从"特征累积"转向"主动特征校准"。
- **构建-精炼鸿沟（Construction-Refinement Gap）**：语义方向往往在较浅层形成，但输出概率分布的稳定仍依赖极深层；Qwen3 与 Minimax-M2 表现出显著鸿沟，GLM-4.5-Air 则呈任务依赖模式。
- **跨模型架构签名**：在 Qwen3-Instruct / Thinking、GLM-4.5-Air、Minimax-M2 三个家族上对比有效深度，揭示共享-稀疏专家（Shared-Sparse）结构对深度分配的影响。
- **方法论创新**：将 Future Effect 层跳过干预、残差余弦相似度、Logit Lens 与有效深度指标联合用于完整的 user-agent 多轮轨迹，而非静态单次生成。

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that autonomous LLM agents allocate depth **adaptively and progressively** across multi-turn trajectories, contradicting the "inefficient depth" hypothesis derived from static single-turn tasks.
- Introduces a systematic three-probe methodology (Future Effect causal tracing + Residual Cosine Similarity + Effective Depth via Logit Lens) applied to **complete user-agent trajectories** rather than isolated prompts.
- Identifies a **construction-refinement gap** where semantic direction forms early but output stabilization requires the deepest layers, with architecture-dependent signatures across Qwen, Minimax, and GLM families.
- Shows that residual updates become increasingly **correction-dominant** in later turns, with frequent sign changes in intermediate/deep layers indicating active state recalibration.
- Validates universality across three domains (Deep Research, Code Generation, Tabular Processing), two validation subsets, and both synchronous and asynchronous trajectory-evaluator settings.

## Methodology

- **Architecture scope:** Decoder-only pre-layernorm Transformers with Sparse Mixture-of-Experts (MoE); GLM-4.5-Air uses a Shared-Sparse Expert paradigm.
- **Trajectory construction:** A capable agentic model synthesizes complete multi-turn trajectories with interleaved Thought / Action / Observation sequences. Seed problems drawn from EDR-200 (Deep Research), SWE-Gym (Code Generation), and TableLLM (Tabular Processing QA).
- **Future Effect via Layer Skipping:** For turn $r$, intervene by nullifying layer $s$ from position $p$ onward ($\tilde{h}^{(r)}_{s+1}[p:] = \tilde{h}^{(r)}_s[p:]$) and measure the maximum relative change in downstream contribution vectors $E^{(r)}(s,l) = \max_p \|C_l - \tilde{C}_l\|_2 / \|C_l\|_2$.
- **Residual Cosine Similarity:** $S^{(r)}_{\text{layer}}(l) = \text{cossim}(u^{(r)}_l, h^{(r)}_l)$ classifies updates into orthogonal injection ($S \approx 0$), feature amplification ($S > 0$), and feature correction ($S < 0$).
- **Effective Depth (ED):**
  - *Cosine ED* ($l_{\cos}$): layer where residual cosine similarity makes its final sustained negative-to-positive transition.
  - *Logit Lens KL*: layer where KL divergence between intermediate and final output distributions falls below half its maximum.
  - *Logit Lens Overlap*: layer where top-5 token overlap with final distribution exceeds 0.3.
- **Models evaluated:** Qwen3-30B-Instruct, Qwen3-30B-Thinking, GLM-4.5-Air, Minimax-M2.

## Results & Highlights

- **Progressive layer mobilization:** In Qwen3-30B-Thinking Code Generation trajectories, causal dependency maps evolve from sparse-localized (Turn 1) to dense full-depth structures (Turn 4). Logit Change Norm $D^{(r)}(s)$ shows that intermediate-to-deep layers (10–45), dormant in Turn 1, exhibit a pronounced surge in influence by Turn 4.
- **Universality:** The same transition is reproduced across two non-overlapping validation subsets (A, B) in all three domains, and under both synchronous (Minimax on Minimax-generated) and asynchronous (Qwen on Minimax-generated) evaluations, ruling out sample- and stylistic-alignment artifacts.
- **Intensified correction dynamics:** Residual cosine similarity heatmaps show that Turn 5 exhibits frequent phase changes (positive ↔ negative) in intermediate and deep layers for both Qwen3-Instruct and Qwen3-Thinking, contrasting with stable amplification in Turn 1.
- **Construction-refinement gap (Qwen & Minimax):**
  - Qwen3: Cosine ED ratio ≈ 0.40–0.42; Logit Lens KL ratio ≈ 0.92–0.94; Logit Lens Overlap ratio ≈ 0.94–0.98.
  - Minimax-M2: Cosine ED ratio varies widely (0.26–0.47), but Logit Lens Overlap = 1.00 across all domains (stabilization only at the very last layers).
- **Shared-expert divergence (GLM):** Cosine ED reaches 0.74 in Code and Tabular (deep feature re-orientation persists), but falls to 0.28 in Deep Research — hypothesized to reflect GLM's Shared-Sparse Expert routing where structured tasks recruit sparse experts deeper.
- **Implication:** Agentic computation is allocated conditionally on interaction state, not uniformly, providing mechanistic evidence that deep models are functionally important for autonomous agent settings.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. First systematic mechanistic investigation of depth utilization in multi-turn autonomous agents; the three-probe combination (Future Effect + Residual Cosine Similarity + Effective Depth) applied to full trajectories is a meaningful methodological extension of Csordás et al. (2025).
- **Rigor:** Moderate-to-high. Multiple domains, multiple model families, two validation subsets, and a synchronous-vs-asynchronous control address sample/style confounds. However, sample sizes (number of trajectories, seed problems per domain) are not explicitly reported, which limits reproducibility and statistical confidence claims.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate. Seed benchmarks (EDR-200, SWE-Gym, TableLLM) are public, but the trajectory-synthesis step depends on a "highly capable agentic model" whose identity and prompts are not fully specified; code and trajectories are not mentioned as released.
- **Significance:** High for the mechanistic interpretability and LLM-agent communities. Results directly challenge the "depth is underutilized" narrative and suggest adaptive-compute / dynamic-routing strategies should be interaction-state-conditioned rather than prompt-conditioned.
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - Only four model checkpoints are analyzed; claims about "model-family signatures" could be checkpoint-specific.
  - The GLM shared-expert hypothesis is explicitly flagged as speculative — no routing-level evidence is provided.
  - Thinking vs. Instruct variants of Qwen3 produce nearly identical ED ratios, which is surprising and under-discussed.
  - All analyzed models use Sparse MoE; generalization to dense Transformers is unclear.
  - No discussion of positional confounds (later turns have much longer context windows), which could independently drive deeper layer activation.

## Strengths

- Clean separation of three research questions (where, how, when) mapped to three complementary probes.
- Multi-domain, multi-model, multi-subset, and cross-generator evaluation strengthens external validity.
- Clear, informative visualizations (causal dependency maps, logit-change bar plots, residual cosine similarity heatmaps) that make qualitative trends immediately visible.
- Honest framing of speculative components (e.g., GLM shared-expert hypothesis) and an explicit "Further Discussion" section that lays out limitations and future directions.
- Directly engages with a recent and important prior work (Csordás et al. 2025) and shows where its conclusions do and do not transfer.

## Limitations

- Restricted to Sparse MoE decoder-only architectures; results may not generalize to dense Transformers or encoder-decoder models.
- Trajectory synthesis pipeline is underspecified (generator model, prompts, temperature, trajectory length distributions not fully reported), hindering replication.
- No released code, data, or probe scripts mentioned.
- Sample sizes and statistical variance across trajectories are not reported — figures appear to be case studies rather than aggregated statistics in several places.
- Position / context-length confound is not controlled: later turns have longer contexts, which alone could explain deeper activation.
- Thinking vs. Instruct near-identical ED ratios are noted but not mechanistically explained.
- The GLM domain-dependent pattern is hypothesized to stem from shared-sparse routing, but no expert-level activation analysis is performed to confirm it.

## Relevance

本研究直接回应了"大模型深度是否被低效利用"这一机制可解释性核心问题，并将其拓展到自主智能体场景；对于关注 LLM Agent 规划机制、自适应计算（adaptive compute）、动态层路由（layer routing）以及模型深度效率的研究者具有较高参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the progressive depth mobilization hold for dense (non-MoE) Transformers and encoder-decoder architectures?
- How much of the late-turn deep activation is attributable to longer context length vs. genuine task-complexity-driven computation?
- Can expert-level routing traces in GLM's Shared-Sparse architecture confirm the shared-expert divergence hypothesis?
- Do adaptive-compute methods (Mixture-of-Depths, Universal Transformers, Dr.LLM) benefit from interaction-state-conditioned budgets rather than token-level budgets in agentic settings?
- How do internal correction dynamics interact with external memory systems (MemGPT-style) and retrieval-augmented agents over very long horizons?
- Are the construction-refinement gap patterns stable under RLHF / reasoning-tuned checkpoints (e.g., DeepSeek-R1, Qwen3-Max) compared to base/instruct variants?
