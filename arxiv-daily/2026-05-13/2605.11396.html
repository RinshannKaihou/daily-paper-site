MuonQ: Enhancing Low-Bit Muon Quantization via Directional Fidelity Optimization

Yupeng Su, Ruijie Zhang, Ziyue Liu, Yequan Zhao, Zheng Zhang

arXiv: 2605.11396

Date: 2026-05-13

Tags: [optimizer-quantization, muon-optimizer, memory-efficient-training, LLM-training, low-bit-quantization]

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11396

一句话总结： 针对Muon优化器量化时方向信息易受扰动的问题，MuonQ通过预归一化、结构分解和μ律压扩量化三重策略实现稳定的4位纯量化，在GPT-2和LLaMA上训练损失与全精度Muon几乎持平，优化器显存降低达7.3倍。
TL;DR: MuonQ enables pure 4-bit quantization of Muon optimizer states by preserving directional fidelity rather than element-wise accuracy — via pre-quantization normalization (uniform error accumulation), structural decomposition (quantizing singular factors independently via power iteration to prevent singular vector rotation), and μ-law companding (allocating higher resolution to dense near-zero values). On GPT-2 and LLaMA pre-training, MuonQ-4bit closely matches full-precision Muon in training loss and downstream benchmarks while cutting optimizer memory by up to 7.3×.

核心贡献

  • 提出方向保真度优化原则：揭示Muon优化器的极分解丢弃奇异值幅值、仅保留方向信息，因此量化误差的关键在于保护奇异向量方向而非逐元素精度
  • 预量化归一化：将梯度和动量归一化为单位Frobenius范数，使每步量化误差等量，防止累积误差产生各向异性漂移
  • 结构分解：通过幂迭代将动量分解为U和S两个奇异因子并独立量化，确保量化误差仅扰动奇异值幅值而不旋转奇异向量方向；截断top-k版本平衡显存与精度
  • μ律压扩量化：对Muon动量的尖峰分布（集中在零附近）使用μ律非线性变换重新分配量化区间，从离群值保护转向密集区域可区分性
  • 在GPT-2（Medium/Large）和LLaMA（350M/1.1B）上验证，4位MuonQ实现7.3倍优化器显存降低，训练损失与下游任务精度与全精度Muon高度一致

Key Contributions

  • Directional fidelity optimization principle: Identifies that Muon's polar decomposition discards singular value magnitudes and retains only directional information, making the quantization objective fundamentally about preserving singular vector directions rather than element-wise reconstruction accuracy
  • Pre-quantization normalization: Normalizes gradients and momentum to unit Frobenius norm at each step, ensuring uniformly scaled quantization errors that remain approximately isotropic when accumulated through the momentum recursion
  • Structural decomposition with truncated power iteration: Decomposes momentum into left singular vectors (U) and scaled right singular vectors (S), quantizing each independently with aligned granularity (column-wise for U, row-wise for S) to confine errors to per-component scaling without mixing singular directions; top-k truncation at k=min(m,n)/16 captures dominant subspace efficiently
  • μ-law companding quantization: Applies logarithmic nonlinear companding (μ=255) to reallocate quantization bins toward the dense near-zero center of the momentum distribution, where Muon's spectral flattening makes small-magnitude entries equally directionally important
  • Stable pure 4-bit quantization: Achieves up to 7.3× optimizer memory reduction on GPT-2 Medium/Large and LLaMA 350M/1.1B pre-training while closely matching full-precision Muon in both training loss and zero-shot downstream accuracy

Methodology

MuonQ addresses the unique sensitivity of Muon's optimizer states to quantization through three complementary techniques:

1. Pre-quantization normalization (§3.1): The momentum update is modified to M_t = βM̂_{t-1} + G_t/||G_t||_F, followed by M̄_t = M_t/||M_t||_F. This ensures each quantization step introduces errors of the same magnitude, so accumulated errors behave as a sum of identically scaled random perturbations that remain isotropic. Ablation confirms this normalization does not alter full-precision training dynamics (mean absolute difference of 0.003 in training loss).

2. Structural decomposition (§3.2): Direct quantization followed by orthogonalization amplifies errors because the polar decomposition retains directional perturbations. MuonQ decomposes momentum via SVD-like factorization M_t ≈ U_k S_k + R_k, where U_k and S_k capture the top-k singular components and R_k is the residual. Power iteration warm-started from the previous step extracts these efficiently. Quantization granularity is aligned with singular structure: U is quantized column-wise (each left singular vector independently) and S row-wise (each scaled right singular vector independently). The residual R_k is quantized at tensor granularity.

3. μ-law companding quantization (§3.3): Muon's momentum has a sharp peak near zero with heavy tails. Standard uniform quantization wastes bins on sparse tails. The μ-law function f(x) = sgn(x)·ln(1+μ|x|)/ln(1+μ) compresses the distribution before quantization and expands after dequantization, concentrating resolution in the dense center where Muon's spectral flattening makes small values equally important.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The directional fidelity principle is a genuine insight — recognizing that Muon's polar decomposition changes the quantization objective from numerical to directional preservation. The three techniques are well-motivated and synergistic. However, μ-law companding and power iteration decomposition are classical techniques adapted to this specific setting.
  • Rigor: Good. Clean ablation study isolating each component's contribution. Cosine similarity as a directional fidelity metric is appropriate and well-justified. The normalization ablation showing no impact on full-precision training is a useful sanity check.
  • Reproducibility: Strong. Code is publicly available. Architecture details, training configs, and hyperparameters are provided in appendices. Standard benchmarks (lm-evaluation-harness) used.
  • Significance: High. Enabling pure 4-bit Muon quantization without mixed precision is a meaningful advance. The 7.3× memory reduction could enable training larger models on fewer GPUs.
  • Red flags:
  • Experiments limited to ≤1.1B parameters — unclear how well MuonQ scales to frontier models (7B+, 70B+)
  • Power iteration introduces per-step computational overhead not thoroughly benchmarked (wall-clock time not reported)
  • Comparison only against naive uniform quantization baselines (Muon4), not against the mixed-precision approach of Wu et al. (2026)
  • Single training dataset (FineWeb) and single epoch — robustness across data regimes not established

Strengths

  • Principled motivation: the directional fidelity insight elegantly explains why Muon is uniquely sensitive to quantization and guides all design decisions
  • Orthogonal techniques that compose well — each addresses a distinct failure mode (temporal drift, spectral error amplification, resolution misallocation)
  • Clean ablation demonstrating additive contributions of each component
  • Strong memory reduction with minimal quality loss
  • Pure 4-bit quantization without mixed-precision complexity

Limitations

  • Only validated on models up to 1.1B parameters; scaling behavior to larger models is uncertain
  • Computational overhead of power iteration per step not quantified in wall-clock terms
  • No comparison against the concurrent mixed-precision Muon quantization approach (Wu et al., 2026)
  • Single pre-training dataset (FineWeb) limits generalizability claims
  • Truncation rank k selection may need tuning for different architectures/scales

Relevance

本文直接解决LLM训练中Muon优化器的显存瓶颈问题，提出的方向保真度量化原则对理解优化器状态量化具有普遍参考价值，与高效训练和模型压缩等研究兴趣高度相关。
