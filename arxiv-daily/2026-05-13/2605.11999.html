The Illusion of Power Capping in LLM Decode: A Phase-Aware Energy Characterisation Across Attention Architectures

Bole Ma, Ayesha Afzal, Jan Eitzinger, Gerhard Wellein

arXiv: 2605.11999

Date: 2026-05-13

Tags: [LLM-inference, GPU-energy-efficiency, DVFS, attention-mechanisms, power-capping]

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11999

一句话总结： 本文揭示了GPU功耗限制（power capping）在LLM自回归解码阶段完全无效——因为内存受限的解码仅消耗137-300W（远低于700W TDP），限制从未触发；而直接锁定SM时钟频率可Pareto优于功耗限制，节省高达32%的解码能耗。
TL;DR: Power capping is structurally ineffective for memory-bound LLM decode: the GPU draws only 137-300 W on a 700 W H200, so no cap ever triggers. SM clock locking Pareto-dominates power capping across four attention paradigms (GQA, MLA, Gated DeltaNet, Mamba2), recovering up to 32% of decode energy at negligible throughput loss. Three architecture-dependent DVFS behavioral classes are identified, and novel attention replacements share a common energy pattern of heavy prefill cost recouped by efficient decode.

核心贡献

  • 揭示功耗限制的"幻觉"： 在LLM生产推理中占主导的解码阶段，GPU仅消耗137-300W，远低于700W TDP，任何功耗限制都从未触发。驱动程序忽略限制，维持约1830 MHz不变。
  • SM时钟锁定是正确的能效杠杆： 静态SM时钟锁定在所有匹配的能耗-吞吐量预算点上都Pareto优于功耗限制，可节省高达32%的解码能耗，吞吐量损失不到1%。
  • 识别三种DVFS行为类别： 批次不变的（GQA）、批次敏感的（MLA、Mamba2）和计算轻量的（GDN），为每种架构提供了可部署的时钟策略。
  • 跨架构能耗全景： 新型注意力替代方案（MLA、GDN、Mamba2）共享一种模式——预填充代价高但解码高效，在生产批次大小下最终可将对GQA的总请求能耗减半。

Key Contributions

  • The power-capping illusion exposed: Demonstrates that power capping is structurally ineffective for memory-bound LLM decode. Across four attention paradigms on H200, decode draws 137-300 W on a 700 W GPU; the cap is inert because memory-bound decode saturates HBM bandwidth rather than compute.
  • SM clock locking as the correct energy lever: Static SM clock locking Pareto-dominates power capping at every matched operating point, recovering up to 32% of decode energy at <1% throughput loss.
  • Three DVFS behavioral classes identified: Batch-invariant (GQA — single low clock works at all batch sizes), batch-sensitive (MLA, Mamba2 — optimal clock rises with batch size), and compute-light (GDN — tolerates aggressive underclocking unconditionally).
  • Controlled GQA↔MLA ablation: Uses TransMLA on shared Minitron-4B weights to isolate the attention mechanism from confounding model differences, revealing that MLA's energy advantage is strictly decode-specific and context-length-dependent.

Methodology

  • Hardware: Single NVIDIA H200 SXM (HBM3e, 4.8 TB/s bandwidth, 989 TFLOPS BF16, 700 W TDP).
  • Serving framework: vLLM in BF16 with no custom kernels (out-of-the-box deployment).
  • Models (~4B parameters each): GQA (Qwen3-4B), GQA-ctrl (Minitron-4B), MLA (Minitron-4B via TransMLA), GDN (Qwen3.5-4B), Mamba2 (Nemotron-Nano).
  • Energy measurement: NVML power sampling at 50 ms intervals, integrated with trapezoidal rule; cross-validated against hardware energy counters (agreement within 2% for operations >= 200 ms).
  • Sweeps: Five SM clock levels (390-1980 MHz), five power cap levels (280-700 W), batch sizes 1-32, sequence lengths 1K-64K tokens. Each configuration repeated 10-20 times with three warmup iterations.
  • Roofline analysis: Nsight Compute provides per-kernel diagnostics confirming decode kernels cluster deep in the memory-bound region (arithmetic intensity far below the 206 FLOPs/byte ridge point).

Results & Highlights

  • Power cap ineffectiveness: Despite a 2.5x range in configured cap (280-700 W), actual SM clock remains ~1830 MHz and actual power stays at 160-300 W. The cap never triggers because it is a ceiling, not a target.
  • Clock locking savings: At 780 MHz with seq=1024, every architecture saves 47-90 W (24-32%) with <1% throughput loss. GDN benefits most (32% at BS=32) because its decode path is almost entirely elementwise operations.
  • Firmware clock clamp: The --lock-gpu-clocks command silently clamps any request >= 1830 MHz to ~1830 MHz (vs. free-running boost at 1980 MHz). The extra 240 MHz gap above 1590 MHz produces zero throughput gain at 7-13% more power.
  • MLA crossover: MLA's decode energy advantage emerges only beyond a batch-size-dependent context threshold. At BS=32, crossover is at 4K tokens; at BS=1, it never arrives. At BS=32/seq=65K, MLA uses less than half of GQA-ctrl's decode energy.
  • Mamba2 total request energy: Mamba2 crosses below GQA after ~1000 output tokens at production batch sizes (BS=32), exactly the regime of agentic coding assistants and document processing.
  • Batching dominates: Increasing BS from 1 to 32 reduces energy per token by over 20x by amortizing weight loading cost.

Critical Assessment

Novelty (4/5): The core observation — that decode is memory-bound and therefore power capping is ineffective — is straightforward in retrospect, but no prior work explicitly poses and answers this question. The cross-architecture comparison including MLA, GDN, and Mamba2 is genuinely novel.

Rigor (5/5): Exceptionally thorough experimental methodology. Controlled ablation via TransMLA on shared weights, extensive sweep of clock/cap/batch/sequence dimensions, 10-20 repetitions per config, Nsight Compute roofline validation, cross-validation of energy measurements. The paper even explains why the results are not artifacts of under-utilization.

Reproducibility (4/5): All models are publicly available on HuggingFace. vLLM is open-source. The hardware (H200 SXM) is commercially available. The specific firmware behavior (1980→1830 MHz clamp) could vary across driver versions, which the authors acknowledge.

Significance (4/5): Directly challenges the industry-standard practice of power capping for LLM inference. The practical recommendation (replace power capping with SM clock locking for decode pools) is immediately actionable and could save megawatts at data-centre scale.

Red flags: Limited to single-GPU, single-framework (vLLM), dense models only. The H200 firmware clamp behavior may not generalize to other GPU generations. GDN and Mamba2 prefill costs reflect vLLM's unfused eager-mode execution rather than inherent architectural limits.

Strengths

  • First paper to explicitly demonstrate that power capping is structurally ineffective for LLM decode, challenging industry-standard practice.
  • Controlled GQA↔MLA ablation on shared weights isolates the attention mechanism from confounds.
  • Comprehensive cross-architecture comparison spanning dense attention, compressed-KV, linear-recurrent, and state-space designs.
  • Immediately actionable recommendation: a single nvidia-smi call can implement SM clock locking.
  • Identifies three deployable DVFS policy classes with concrete per-architecture clock recommendations.

Limitations

  • Single GPU (H200 SXM) — multi-GPU tensor/pipeline parallel communication energy not measured.
  • Single serving framework (vLLM) — TensorRT-LLM or custom kernels may yield different results.
  • Dense models only — MoE routing may interact with DVFS differently.
  • GDN and Mamba2 prefill results are dominated by vLLM's unfused execution path, not the architectures themselves.
  • The firmware clock clamp (1980→1830 MHz) may be H200/driver-specific.

Relevance

本文对LLM推理系统的GPU能效管理具有直接的实践指导意义。研究揭示了一个被广泛忽视的问题：数据中心运营商依赖的功耗限制机制在LLM解码阶段完全失效，而简单的SM时钟锁定可以立即实现显著的节能效果。对于从事LLM推理服务部署、GPU集群能效优化或新型注意力架构评估的研究者和工程师来说，这项工作提供了关键的性能调优基准。
