Detecting overfitting in Neural Networks during long-horizon grokking using Random Matrix Theory

arXiv: 2605.12394

Date: 2026-05-12

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12394

一句话总结： 本文提出基于随机矩阵理论的"关联陷阱"(Correlation Traps)方法，通过逐层打乱权重矩阵并拟合Marchenko-Pastur谱分布来检测深度学习模型中的过拟合，揭示了grokking之后的"反grokking"阶段，并在GPT-OSS等前沿大模型中发现了同样的过拟合信号。
TL;DR: The authors introduce Correlation Traps — outliers in the randomized eigenvalue spectrum of layer weight matrices that violate the Marchenko-Pastur self-averaging baseline — to detect overfitting without any train/test data. They identify a post-grokking "anti-grokking" phase where test accuracy degrades while train accuracy stays high, marked by growing trap counts across MNIST MLP, Modular Addition transformers, and GPT2 models. A JSD-based ablation test distinguishes harmful from benign traps. Notably, some frontier-scale open-weight LLMs (gpt-oss-20b, gpt-oss-120b) exhibit the same trap signatures, suggesting potential overfitting in foundation models.

核心贡献

  • 定义了"关联陷阱"(Correlation Traps)：对权重矩阵逐元素打乱后，其协方差矩阵特征值谱中偏离Marchenko-Pastur分布右边缘的异常离群值，标志着非自平均(non-self-averaging)结构的出现。
  • 发现了grokking之后的"反grokking"(anti-grokking)阶段：模型在已获得泛化能力后，继续训练导致测试准确率下降而训练准确率保持完美，这是一种有别于预grokking的结构性过拟合。
  • 提出了无需数据的JSD诊断消融测试：通过替换陷阱方向并测量模型输出分布的Jensen-Shannon散度，可区分有害陷阱和良性陷阱。
  • 在MNIST MLP、Modular Addition Transformer和GPT2三个标准grokking基准上验证了关联陷阱与反grokking的一致性。
  • 将该诊断应用于前沿开源大模型(gpt-oss-20b, gpt-oss-120b)，发现大量关联陷阱，暗示这些模型可能存在过拟合。

Key Contributions

  • Define Correlation Traps as spectral outliers beyond the Marchenko-Pastur (MP) right edge in entry-wise shuffled weight matrices, indicating non-self-averaging structure linked to overfitting.
  • Identify the anti-grokking phase — a post-generalization regime where test accuracy degrades under continued optimization while training accuracy remains near-perfect, structurally distinct from pre-grokking via the presence of Correlation Traps.
  • Propose a data-free JSD diagnostic ablation test that replaces trap eigenvectors with random vectors and measures Jensen-Shannon divergence of output logits on random probe inputs to classify traps as harmful or benign.
  • Validate across three diverse grokking benchmarks (MNIST MLP, Modular Addition transformer, GPT2 synthetic composition task), showing trap onset consistently tracks anti-grokking across architectures and task types.
  • Screen frontier-scale open-weight LLMs (gpt-oss-20b, gpt-oss-120b) and find numerous Correlation Traps per layer, suggesting potentially harmful overfitting in foundation models.

Methodology

The method operates purely on model weights — no training data, test data, gradients, or optimizer state are needed. For each layer weight matrix $W \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times M}$:

  1. Entry-wise shuffling: Randomize all elements of $W$ to produce $W_{\text{rand}}$, destroying structured correlations while preserving the marginal distribution of weights.
  2. Spectral analysis: Form the covariance $X = \frac{1}{N} W_{\text{rand}}^\top W_{\text{rand}}$ and compute its eigenvalue spectrum (ESD).
  3. MP fitting: Fit a Marchenko-Pastur (MP) distribution to the ESD. Under the randomized null, the spectrum should converge to the MP bulk.
  4. Trap detection: Identify eigenvalues $\lambda_{\text{trap}} > \lambda_+^{\text{MP}} + \Delta_{\text{TW}}$ (beyond the MP right edge plus Tracy-Widom fluctuations) as Correlation Traps.
  5. JSD ablation test: For each trap, replace the corresponding eigenvector with a random vector, pass probe inputs through both original and ablated models, and compute the Jensen-Shannon divergence between output distributions. Large JSD indicates a harmful trap.

The theoretical grounding connects traps to two failure modes of self-averaging: geometric localization (trap mass concentrated on a small coordinate set) and spectral condensation (a single eigenvalue dominates the variance). These are analogous to Anderson localization and Bose-Einstein condensation in statistical physics.

The authors study three grokking benchmarks extended to long-horizon training: (1) a depth-3 ReLU MLP on a balanced MNIST subset (1000 training points, width 200, trained for up to $10^7$ steps), (2) a one-layer transformer on modular addition ($x + y \mod P$), and (3) a GPT2 model on a synthetic knowledge graph composition task following Wang et al.

Results & Highlights

  • Three-phase training dynamics: All three benchmarks exhibit pre-grokking (high train/low test accuracy, zero traps) → grokking (high train and test accuracy, near-zero traps) → anti-grokking (high train/declining test accuracy, growing trap counts).
  • Traps distinguish anti-grokking from pre-grokking: Both phases show high train accuracy and poor test accuracy, but only anti-grokking produces Correlation Traps — demonstrating traps detect overfitting structure, not mere memorization or low test accuracy.
  • Weight decay suppresses traps: An MLP control run with weight decay (WD=0.01) shows reduced trap counts and less severe test degradation, confirming traps reflect overfitting severity rather than just weight scale.
  • Perturbation study confirms phase-specificity: Global weight-norm perturbations across phases do not induce traps in pre-grokking/grokking, ruling out traps as a simple proxy for weight scale.
  • JSD ablation classifies traps: In MLP and MA, JSD scores closely track the downstream test-error change from trap removal, enabling data-free identification of harmful vs. benign traps.
  • Frontier LLM screening: gpt-oss-20b and gpt-oss-120b exhibit numerous Correlation Traps across layers, suggesting potentially harmful overfitting in production-scale models.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (High): The concept of "Correlation Traps" — detecting overfitting via outliers in shuffled weight spectra — is genuinely novel. The identification of "anti-grokking" as a distinct phase and the connection to non-self-averaging in statistical mechanics adds theoretical depth. The data-free JSD ablation test is a creative practical contribution.
  • Rigor (Moderate-High): The MP/TW framework is well-established mathematically. The three-benchmark validation is thorough, and the weight-decay control plus perturbation study address key confounds. However, the experiments are limited to small-to-medium models (up to GPT2-scale), and the frontier LLM results are screening-level only without causal validation.
  • Reproducibility (High): The method is implemented in the open-source WeightWatcher tool. Training code is provided in the appendix. The experiments use standard benchmarks (MNIST, modular addition, GPT2 composition task) with published hyperparameters.
  • Significance (High): If the correlation-trap diagnostic generalizes beyond grokking settings, it could become a valuable tool for auditing open-weight models without needing proprietary training data. The finding that frontier LLMs exhibit trap signatures is provocative and potentially impactful for model evaluation and regulation.
  • Red flags: (1) The connection between traps in grokking experiments and traps in frontier LLMs is correlational, not causal — the paper acknowledges this but the framing sometimes blurs the distinction. (2) The benign/harmful trap classification relies on test-data access for ground truth in experiments, somewhat undermining the "data-free" claim. (3) The paper does not test whether removing harmful traps during training improves outcomes. (4) Limited to grokking settings; generalization to standard training regimes is not demonstrated.

Strengths

  • Elegant and theoretically grounded diagnostic requiring only model weights — no data, no training pipeline access.
  • Clean three-benchmark validation showing consistent results across MLPs, algorithmic transformers, and GPT-style models.
  • The anti-grokking concept illuminates an underappreciated failure mode in long-horizon training.
  • The perturbation study and weight-decay control effectively rule out simpler explanations.
  • The JSD ablation test provides a practical way to triage detected traps.
  • The connection to statistical mechanics (localization, condensation) provides interpretable mechanisms.

Limitations

  • All controlled experiments are in grokking settings; it remains unclear whether Correlation Traps appear in standard training regimes where overfitting occurs without a preceding grokking phase.
  • The frontier LLM screening (gpt-oss-20b/120b) is observational only — no causal evidence that traps cause poor generalization in these models.
  • The JSD ablation ground truth requires test-data access in experiments, creating a gap between the diagnostic promise and the validation methodology.
  • No demonstration that the diagnostic can be used to improve training (e.g., by guiding early stopping or regularization).
  • Limited architectural diversity in controlled experiments (all are relatively small models); scaling behavior of trap detection is not characterized.
  • The MP fitting procedure may be sensitive to the quality of the shuffle and the finite-size corrections, though the paper does not deeply analyze this.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do Correlation Traps appear in standard (non-grokking) training regimes, and if so, do they still reliably indicate overfitting?
  • Can trap counts be used as a real-time regularization signal during training to prevent anti-grokking?
  • What is the relationship between Correlation Traps and other overfitting diagnostics (e.g., influence functions, flatness measures, spectral norm)?
  • How sensitive is the MP fitting and trap detection to the choice of shuffling strategy and finite-size Tracy-Widom correction?
  • Can harmful traps be surgically removed from a trained model to recover generalization without retraining?
  • Do different training algorithms (SGD vs. Adam, different learning rate schedules) produce different trap profiles during anti-grokking?
