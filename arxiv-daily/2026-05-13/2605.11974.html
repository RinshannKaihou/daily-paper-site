Towards Order Fairness: Mitigating LLMs Order Sensitivity through Dual Group Advantage Optimization

arXiv: 2605.11974

Date: 2026-05-13

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11974

一句话总结： 本文提出DGAO（Dual Group Advantage Optimization），首次利用强化学习同时优化LLM的输出准确性和输入排列顺序稳定性，通过PPO式裁剪目标函数平衡组内准确率优势与组间稳定性优势，在3B–14B模型上的RAG、数学推理和分类任务中均实现了准确率提升和过度自信率下降。
TL;DR: DGAO is the first RL method (PPO-style) that jointly optimizes LLM accuracy and order stability via a dual group advantage reward — intra-group correctness advantage + inter-group consistency advantage — achieving gains on both fronts across RAG, math, and classification benchmarks on Qwen-2.5, Llama-3.2, and Gemma-3 models (3B–14B).

核心贡献

  • 首次将强化学习（PPO式目标函数）用于缓解LLM排列顺序敏感性，提出DGAO框架，同时优化准确性和顺序稳定性，打破SFT方法"一致性但错误"的困境。
  • 设计双组优势机制：组内优势 Aind = R(y) - Rgroup 奖励正确输出，组间优势 Agroup = Rgroup - b 奖励组级稳定性，混合优势 Ahybrid = α·Agroup + (1-α)·Aind 经批次归一化后用于PPO裁剪目标。
  • 提出两个新评估指标：Consistency Rate（一致性率）揭示模型输出在排列变化下的一致程度；Overconfidence Rate（过度自信率）揭示PAFT等方法"伪稳定"——模型一致地输出错误答案。
  • 在5个数据集、5个模型（3B–14B）上验证DGAO，并证明其顺序稳定性可泛化到未见任务和更长上下文（32-shot、64-shot）。

Key Contributions

  • First RL-based (PPO-style clipped objective with KL penalty) approach to mitigate LLM order sensitivity, jointly optimizing accuracy and order fairness.
  • Dual group advantage mechanism: intra-group advantage rewards correctness per variant; inter-group advantage rewards group-level stability; hybrid advantage is batch-normalized and fed into a clipped surrogate objective.
  • Two novel metrics — Consistency Rate and Overconfidence Rate — that expose pseudo-stability in prior SFT-based methods (PAFT) where models become consistently wrong.
  • Comprehensive evaluation on 5 datasets (SST2, SQuAD v2, SearchQA, GSM8K, CM17K), 5 models (Qwen-2.5-3B/14B, Llama-3.2-3B/11B, Gemma-3-4B), with generalization to unseen tasks and longer contexts.

Methodology

DGAO frames order fairness as an RL problem built on PPO. For each original query, N=8 random order variants are generated, forming a "group."

Reward: Binary correctness reward R(y) ∈ {0, 1}.

Dual advantage computation:

  • Group reward: Rgroup(j) = (1/N) Σ R(yj,i)
  • Global baseline: b = (1/M) Σ Rgroup(j)
  • Intra-group advantage: Aind(yj,i) = R(yj,i) − Rgroup(j) — rewards being more correct than the group average
  • Inter-group advantage: Agroup(j) = Rgroup(j) − b — rewards groups that are overall more correct (i.e., order-stable)
  • Hybrid advantage: Ahybrid = α · Agroup + (1−α) · Aind, with α=0.5 by default
  • Batch normalization: Afinal = (Ahybrid − μA) / (σA + εA)

Training objective: Standard PPO clipped surrogate with KL penalty:

LDGAO(θ) = mean[min(r·Afinal, clip(r, 1−ε, 1+ε)·Afinal)] − β·DKL(πθ||πref), with ε=0.2, β=0.05.

Training procedure: Half of 16K augmented data (8 variants × 2K queries) used for full-parameter SFT cold-start; the other half used for DGAO RL fine-tuning for 3 epochs on 8×A800 GPUs.

Baselines compared: SFT, PAFT (SFT on augmented orderings), Prompt ("don't consider order"), Lost in the Middle (pos-beginning/end), PINE (attention modification), SPHS (hidden state scaling), GlobalE/DEmO (order search), PPO, GRPO.

Results & Highlights

  • Accuracy: DGAO improves accuracy over SFT on nearly all tasks (e.g., +3.79% on SearchQA/Llama-3.2-3B, +1.71% on SQuAD v2/Llama-3.2-3B), while PAFT degrades accuracy (e.g., −15.62% on SQuAD v2/Llama-3.2-3B).
  • Order stability: DGAO consistently reduces Overconfidence Rate (e.g., from 37.56% to 34.72% on GSM8K/Llama-3.2-3B) while PAFT increases it (to 46.87%).
  • vs. architecture-based methods: PINE and SPHS lag DGAO by 8.05% and 9.87% in accuracy respectively on Llama-3.2-3B.
  • vs. standard RL: PPO and GRPO both decrease Consistency Rate (−2.08 and −0.83 on SQuAD v2), while DGAO increases it (+0.62). Standard RL methods also increase Overconfidence Rate (+3.74 and +3.95).
  • Ablation: Removing Agroup (α=0) improves accuracy but fails to improve Consistency Rate; removing Aind (α=1) degrades nearly all metrics, confirming both components are complementary.
  • Scalability: Consistent improvements on Qwen-2.5-14B and Llama-3.2-11B.
  • Generalization: Order stability transfers to unseen tasks and to 32-shot/64-shot longer contexts (on 32-shot SQuAD v2, DGAO improves accuracy by +3.28% while PAFT drops by −23.13%).
  • Training cost: ~0.85 GPU-hours on 8×A800 for SearchQA (vs. 0.81 for GRPO, 0.22 for SFT). No inference overhead.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. First use of RL for order sensitivity; the dual advantage formulation is elegant — it exploits the group structure of order variants in a way that standard PPO/GRPO cannot.
  • Rigor: Strong. New metrics, ablation study, hyperparameter sweep (α), generalization experiments, and comparison against 8+ baselines. The bad case example (Figure 5) clearly illustrates PAFT's pseudo-stability vs. DGAO's balanced behavior.
  • Reproducibility: Good. Code provided, standard hardware (8×A800), fixed hyperparameters (α=0.5, β=0.05, ε=0.2, N=8), 3-run averages.
  • Significance: High practical relevance for RAG and ICL deployments where input ordering is arbitrary. No inference overhead is a major advantage.
  • Red flags: (1) Only tested up to 14B — scalability to 70B+ frontier models is unproven. (2) The 8-variant sampling may not cover enough orderings when inputs have many elements. (3) DGAO's accuracy gains are sometimes modest (+0.05% to +0.38%) on some tasks, and on CM17K/Gemma-3-4B it slightly decreases Consistency Rate. (4) The binary reward signal may be insufficient for tasks with partial correctness.

Strengths

  • Principled RL formulation that theoretically addresses why SFT and standard RL fail (no explicit cross-permutation consistency signal).
  • Dual advantage design is interpretable and well-motivated.
  • Overconfidence Rate is a genuinely novel diagnostic metric that should be adopted by the community.
  • No inference-time overhead; orthogonal to architecture-based methods and can be combined with them.
  • Strong generalization to unseen tasks and longer contexts.
  • Comprehensive experimental design: 5 datasets, 5 models, 8+ baselines, ablation, hyperparameter sweep, scalability test.

Limitations

  • Only validated on 3B–14B models; computational cost limits testing on larger models (acknowledged by authors).
  • Fixed 8 order variants may be insufficient for inputs with many elements (n! grows rapidly); adaptive sampling is left for future work.
  • Binary reward (correct/incorrect) does not capture partial credit, which may limit applicability to generative tasks without exact-match evaluation.
  • On some tasks (CM17K, SST2), Consistency Rate improvements are marginal or slightly negative compared to PAFT.
  • Training cost is ~4× SFT (0.85 vs 0.22 GPU-hours), though still practical.

Relevance

该论文直接解决LLM在RAG和ICL场景中的输入排列偏差问题，提出的DGAO方法通过强化学习将排序公平性融入模型权重，无需推理时额外开销，对构建鲁棒的生产级RAG系统和少样本学习应用具有直接且实用的参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can DGAO be combined with architecture-based methods (PINE, SPHS) for further improvements, since they are orthogonal?
  • How does DGAO perform with soft/partial rewards (e.g., BLEU, semantic similarity) instead of binary correctness?
  • Would curriculum-based sampling of harder orderings (rather than random) improve training efficiency?
  • What is the effect of increasing N beyond 8 variants — diminishing returns or continued gains?
  • Can the dual group advantage idea be applied to other position-related biases (e.g., lost-in-the-middle in long-context QA)?
