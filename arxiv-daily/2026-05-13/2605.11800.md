> **一句话总结：** ROMER 首次系统研究了模拟存算一体（CIM）系统中硬件噪声对 MoE 大模型的影响，发现噪声会导致专家负载崩溃和路由次优，提出通过复制高激活专家替换空闲专家（噪声方差减半）和基于 IQR 的路由器 logits 校准两种无训练策略，在真实芯片噪声下将困惑度降低最高 59.8%，硬件开销仅约 7.8% 延迟增加。

> **TL;DR:** First systematic study of MoE-based LLMs under real analog CIM chip noise. Reveals two failure modes: expert load collapse (few experts absorb disproportionate tokens) and noise-inflated router logits causing overconfident suboptimal routing. Proposes ROMER, a training-free framework with (1) expert replacement (duplicating top-k experts into bottom-k slots for 2x noise reduction via averaging) and (2) IQR-based router logit compression. Achieves up to 59.8% perplexity reduction on OLMoE, 58.8% on Qwen-MoE, 58.6% on DeepSeek-MoE with only ~7.8% latency overhead. Also recovers reasoning accuracy (ARC-Challenge from 36.8% to 43.2%).

## 核心贡献

- **首次系统刻画** MoE 大模型在模拟 CIM 硬件上的脆弱性，使用真实芯片测量校准噪声模型（含器件噪声 N(0, σ²_dev) 和 ADC 量化噪声 U(-Δ/2, Δ/2)），发现两个独特失效模式
- **揭示专家负载崩溃**：噪声导致原本均匀分布的专家激活模式退化为少数专家过载、多数专家闲置的"热点"模式，模型退化为更小的稠密网络
- **揭示路由器 logits 膨胀**：噪声使每层路由器 logit 方差平均增加近 2 倍（早期层最高 3 倍），导致路由器基于噪声伪峰值做出过度自信的错误决策
- **提出专家替换策略**：将 top-k 高频专家的权重复制到 bottom-k 低频专家的内存位置，推理时对两次独立噪声计算取平均，使关键专家的输出噪声方差减半
- **提出基于 IQR 的路由校准**：利用四分位距压缩 logits 分布（IQR(cal) = (1-2λ)·IQR），λ=0.4 为最优，解析性地控制压缩程度，抵抗离群值干扰
- **全面的实验验证**：在 4 个 MoE 模型（DeepSeek-MoE-16B, Qwen3-30B-A3B, OLMoE-1B-7B, Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B）上测试，覆盖 5 个基准（WikiText-103/2, LAMBADA, PIQA, ARC），硬件开销仅 +7.8% 延迟、+7.4% 功耗

## Key Contributions

- First systematic characterization of MoE LLM vulnerability under real analog CIM chip noise, with a two-component noise model (multiplicative device noise + additive ADC quantization noise) calibrated against physical chip measurements
- Discovery of two unique MoE failure modes under noise: (1) expert load collapse where few experts absorb disproportionate token assignments while most become dormant, and (2) router logit inflation where per-token per-layer variance nearly doubles, causing overconfident suboptimal expert selection
- Expert replacement: duplicates top-k most-activated expert weights into bottom-k memory slots; during inference, both original and replicated computations contribute, yielding a factor-of-2 reduction in output noise variance for critical experts (σ²_out → σ²_out/2)
- IQR-based router calibration: compresses logit spread analytically via IQR(z(cal)) = (1-2λ)·IQR(z), lifting the lower half and suppressing the upper half of sorted logits; λ=0.4 is optimal across temperatures
- Training-free framework requiring neither retraining nor labeled data, with minimal hardware overhead (+7.8% latency, +7.4% power) while achieving -56.7% average perplexity reduction

## Methodology

**Noise Model.** The authors decompose analog CIM noise into two additive components calibrated from a real chip at operating temperatures of 25°C to 85°C:
- *Device noise*: multiplicative perturbation w̃ = w + w·ε_dev where ε_dev ~ N(0, σ²_dev), modeling thermal fluctuations, programming variability, and read disturbances
- *ADC quantization noise*: additive uniform perturbation ε_ADC,i ~ U(-Δ/2, Δ/2) where Δ = V_ref/(2^b - 1)
- Combined noisy MVM: ỹ = (W + W·E_dev)x + ε_ADC

**Expert Replacement.** Given cumulative activation magnitudes A_{l,i} = Σ_t p^{(t)}_{l,i} · 1{i∈S(x_t)} over a calibration corpus:
1. Partition experts per layer into top-k (T_l), bottom-k (B_l), and remaining sets
2. Establish bijection σ: T_l → B_l pairing high-frequency with low-frequency experts
3. Overwrite bottom-k weights with paired top-k weights: W_{l,σ(i)} ← W_{l,i} for all i ∈ T_l
4. During inference, zero out bottom-k logits and halve top-k logits (to maintain correct gating probability since two copies contribute)
5. Each top-k expert's output averages two independent noisy computations, reducing noise variance by 2x

**Percentile-Based Router Calibration.** For router logits z = [z_1, ..., z_E]:
1. Compute IQR = Q3(z) - Q1(z)
2. Sort logits ascending: z_(1) ≤ z_(2) ≤ ... ≤ z_(E)
3. Apply: z_i^(cal) = z_i^(sorted) + λ·IQR for i ≤ ⌊E/2⌋; z_i^(sorted) - λ·IQR for i > ⌊E/2⌋
4. This compresses IQR analytically: IQR(z^(cal)) = (1-2λ)·IQR(z), with λ ∈ (0, 0.5) controlling compression strength
5. Reorder calibrated logits to original expert indices before routing

**Calibration.** The noise parameters (σ_dev, σ_ADC, Δ) are calibrated against measurements from a real analog CIM chip. The authors validate their simulated noise model by measuring KL divergence between on-chip non-ideal effects + Gaussian noise and the simulated model in end-to-end outputs, plus per-layer error standard deviations (Figure 2).

## Results & Highlights

**WikiText-103 Perplexity (Table 1):**
- At T=80°C, vanilla deployment increases perplexity by +1.85 to +2.65 across models; ROMER limits degradation to +0.31 to +0.95
- ROMER vs. best baseline (weight-remapping): further reduces perplexity gaps by 0.13–0.39 points at T=80°C
- OLMoE-1B-7B is most vulnerable (smallest active parameter count); Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B most robust

**Multi-Benchmark on DeepSeek-MoE-16B (Table 2):**
- At T=80°C: WikiText-2 perplexity surges from 18.94→28.36 (+9.42); ROMER recovers to 22.48 (~62% of vanilla degradation)
- ARC-Challenge accuracy drops 47.1%→36.8% (-10.3 points, 21.9% relative); ROMER recovers to 43.2%
- PIQA accuracy: 79.8%→74.2% vanilla → 77.7% ROMER
- LAMBADA perplexity: 10.64→19.53 vanilla → 13.99 ROMER
- K-B calibration performs comparably to or worse than vanilla, confirming simple statistical recalibration is insufficient

**Hardware Overhead (Table 3):**
- Three smaller models: +6.2–6.5% latency, +6.0–6.2% power
- Qwen3-30B-A3B: +12.1% latency, +11.3% power (due to larger 3B active parameters)
- Average across all models: +7.8% latency, +7.4% power for -56.7% perplexity reduction

**Ablation (Table 4 on Qwen3-30B-A3B):**
- Expert replacement n=4–6 is optimal; n≥8 degrades performance; n=16 (replacing all) is worse than no replacement
- Router calibration λ=0.4 consistently best; at T=25 achieves perplexity 10.40 below clean baseline 10.83
- Both components address complementary failure modes; neither alone achieves full recovery

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High — genuinely the first work to study MoE LLM routing behavior under real analog CIM noise. The two failure modes (load collapse + logit inflation) and their mitigation are novel contributions at the hardware-algorithm intersection.
- **Rigor**: Strong — comprehensive evaluation across 4 models, 5 benchmarks, 2 temperature regimes, 6 baselines, plus ablation studies. Expert activation heatmaps provide compelling visual evidence. The noise model is validated against real chip measurements (KL divergence analysis).
- **Reproducibility**: Moderate-to-good — all models and benchmarks are public. The noise model parameters require chip access for exact replication, but the Gaussian + uniform noise model is simple to approximate. Code release is not mentioned.
- **Significance**: High — MoE is the dominant architecture for frontier LLMs (DeepSeek-V3, Mixtral) and CIM is a promising deployment path. This work bridges a critical gap between algorithmic and hardware communities.
- **Red flags**: (1) The "up to 59.8% perplexity reduction" in the abstract refers to relative improvement over vanilla noisy deployment, not absolute — could be misleading. (2) Router calibration at λ=0.4 achieving perplexity *below* clean baseline (10.40 vs 10.83) is suspicious and unexplained — suggests the clean model may have suboptimal routing that calibration incidentally improves. (3) No comparison with noise-aware training approaches (only post-hoc baselines). (4) The expert replacement permanently removes bottom-k experts, which could hurt performance on rare token patterns that rely on those experts.

## Strengths

- Identifies and addresses a genuinely important and previously unexplored problem at the MoE–CIM intersection
- Training-free approach with minimal hardware overhead makes it immediately practical for deployment
- Elegant theoretical motivation: noise variance reduction via averaging of duplicated experts has clean mathematical justification
- IQR-based calibration is robust to outliers and analytically tractable (exact IQR compression formula)
- Comprehensive ablation clearly shows the complementary nature of both components
- Real chip calibration lends strong practical credibility

## Limitations

- Expert replacement permanently sacrifices bottom-k expert capacity; potential performance loss on inputs that genuinely require those experts in clean conditions
- The unexplained below-clean-baseline result (λ=0.4 on Qwen3 at T=25°C) needs investigation — is this an artifact or does it indicate general routing suboptimality?
- Only tested on relatively small MoE models (≤30B total); scaling to 100B+ frontier models (DeepSeek-V3, Mixtral 8x22B) is unverified
- No comparison against noise-aware training or quantization-aware fine-tuning, which may provide stronger baselines
- The approach is tailored to analog CIM noise characteristics; transferability to digital CIM or other accelerator noise profiles is unclear
- Qwen3-30B-A3B shows 12% latency overhead, which could be prohibitive in latency-sensitive deployments

## Relevance

该工作对 MoE 模型在边缘设备上的实际部署具有直接参考价值，特别是在存算一体硬件加速方向。如果你关注高效 LLM 推理、硬件-算法协同设计或 MoE 架构的鲁棒性优化，ROMER 提供了无需重训练的实用方案和重要的实验基准。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Why does λ=0.4 router calibration achieve perplexity *below* the clean baseline — does this reveal a general deficiency in standard MoE routing optimization during pre-training?
2. How does ROMER interact with other deployment optimizations like quantization (GPTQ, AWQ) — are they orthogonal or conflicting?
3. Can expert replacement be made dynamic (per-input rather than static calibration) to preserve capacity for rare but important token patterns?
4. What happens when ROMER is applied to larger MoE models with shared+dedicated expert structures (e.g., DeepSeek-V3's 1 shared + 256 routed experts)?
5. Could the IQR-based logit compression be incorporated as a regularization loss during MoE pre-training to produce inherently noise-robust routers?
