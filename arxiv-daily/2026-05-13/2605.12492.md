> **一句话总结：** Pion 是一种基于正交等价变换的谱保持优化器，通过对权重矩阵施加左右正交变换（而非加法更新）来保持奇异值不变，同时利用 Lie 代数动量和 RMS 缩放实现稳定训练，在 LLM 预训练（1.3B, 54B tokens）、SFT 和 RLVR 场景下均展现出优于或匹敌 AdamW/Muon 的表现，甚至在无归一化层的极端设置下仍能稳定收敛。

> **TL;DR:** Pion is a spectrum-preserving optimizer that updates weight matrices via bilateral orthogonal equivalence transformations derived from Lie group optimization, preserving singular values while modulating row/column geometry. With Lie-algebra momentum and RMS-controlled scaling, it achieves competitive or superior results vs. AdamW/Muon across LLM pretraining (1.3B), SFT, and RLVR — and uniquely survives normalization-free pretraining.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了 Pion，基于 Lie 群优化推导的正交等价变换优化器，用 `W_{t+1} = exp(-ηG_out) W_t exp(-ηG_in)` 替代加法式更新，严格保持权重矩阵奇异值不变
- 系统性地探索了四大设计原则：RMS 一致性缩放、Lie 代数动量、交替更新、二阶矩阵指数近似，并通过消融实验验证了每个选择的必要性
- 证明了在标准假设下 Pion 的随机收敛率为 O(1/√T)，并展示了 Pion 天然满足 µP 的前向谱条件，学习率可跨模型宽度迁移
- 实验覆盖 LLaMA-1.3B 预训练、Qwen2.5/Llama-3.2 SFT、以及 Qwen3/DeepSeek-R1 RLVR，均取得竞争力或更优表现
- 在去除所有归一化层的 60M 模型上，AdamW 和 Muon 均梯度爆炸，唯独 Pion 能稳定收敛

## Key Contributions

- Derives Pion from Lie group optimization: weight matrices are updated via bilateral orthogonal transformations `W_{t+1} = exp(-ηG_out)W_t exp(-ηG_in)` where `G_in = W^T G - G^T W` and `G_out = G W^T - W G^T`, preserving singular values exactly
- Systematically explores four design principles through ablation: RMS scale-consistent scaling, Lie-algebra momentum (outperforming transported ambient-space momentum), alternating bilateral updates (halving compute at <0.3% loss), and second-order exponential approximation (sufficient for spectrum preservation without higher-order terms)
- Proves O(1/√T) stochastic convergence under L-smoothness and bounded variance assumptions, and shows Pion inherently satisfies µP's forward spectral condition with transferable learning rates across model widths
- Achieves best average benchmark scores on LLaMA-1.3B pretraining (47.69 avg vs. 46.34 Muon, 44.74 AdamW), competitive SFT results with best stability-plasticity tradeoff, and best RLVR results (38.32 avg vs. 37.32 Muon, 35.97 AdamW on Qwen3-1.7B)
- Uniquely survives normalization-free pretraining where both AdamW and Muon fail with gradient overflow, demonstrating that spectrum preservation can partially replace architectural normalization

## Methodology

Pion is motivated by POET (which reparameterizes weights as `R W_0 P` with learnable orthogonal R, P) but removes the reparameterization entirely, operating directly on model weights. The core update rule is:

1. **Lie algebra gradient projection:** Given gradient `G_t`, compute skew-symmetric projections: `G_in = W^T G - G^T W` (input side) and `G_out = G W^T - W G^T` (output side)
2. **Lie-algebra momentum:** Accumulate first- and second-order moments in the Lie algebra space: `m_in = β₁ m + (1-β₁)G_in`, `v_in = β₂ v + (1-β₂)(G_in ⊙ G_in)` (analogous for output side)
3. **RMS scale-consistent scaling:** Normalize the Lie algebra elements and apply per-weight coefficient `α_t ≈ c√(d_out·d_in) / (‖A_out W + W A_in‖_F + ε)` to ensure consistent update magnitudes across different parameter matrices
4. **Exponential map:** Apply second-order approximation `E₂(A,α) = I + ηαA + ½(ηαA)²` to map back to the orthogonal group
5. **Bilateral or alternating update:** Either `W_{t+1} = E₂(A_out) W E₂(A_in)` (bilateral) or alternate between input/output sides each step (reducing compute by ~50%)

The computational overhead per step is O(d²_out·d_in + d_out·d²_in + d³_out + d³_in) for bilateral updates, which is amortized by the large batch-token size B in LLM pretraining. In practice, Pion is ~44% slower than AdamW but only ~3% slower than Muon per step on 8×H100 GPUs. Memory overhead is 16% higher than AdamW but can be reduced to 12% lower by removing second-order moments with minimal performance loss.

## Results & Highlights

**Pretraining (LLaMA-1.3B, 54B tokens on C4):**
- Pion achieves 47.69 average benchmark score vs. 46.34 (Muon) and 44.74 (AdamW) across ARC-C, ARC-E, BoolQ, HellaSwag, PIQA, SciQ, TriviaQA, WinoGrande
- Validation loss: 2.7350 (Pion) vs. 2.7225 (Muon) vs. 2.7700 (AdamW) — comparable to Muon, significantly better than AdamW
- All stability indicators (max attention logit, activation norms, parameter norms, output norms) remain flat for Pion while growing for AdamW and Muon

**Normalization-free pretraining (60M model):**
- AdamW and Muon both produce NaN gradients; Pion converges successfully without any normalization layers

**Ultra-deep networks (200-layer LLaMA 60M, 50B tokens):**
- Pion achieves lowest loss instability (std: 0.0892 vs. 0.0931 AdamW, 0.0927 Muon)
- Pion maintains uniform Jacobian norm across layers while AdamW drops sharply in middle layers and Muon steadily decays

**Supervised Finetuning (Qwen2.5-1.5B, Llama-3.2-3B):**
- Best stability-plasticity tradeoff: highest ID scores on code generation (HumanEval) while preserving OOD performance
- On Qwen2.5-1.5B: Pion achieves 53.05% HumanEval (ID) and 63.21% OOD vs. 51.83%/62.64% (AdamW) and 50.00%/62.41% (Muon)

**RLVR (GRPO with Qwen3-1.7B, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B):**
- Pion (alternate update) achieves best average: 38.32 on Qwen3-1.7B vs. 37.32 (Muon) and 35.97 (AdamW)
- Fastest convergence in evaluation accuracy during training
- On DeepSeek-R1-Distill: 46.43 avg (Pion) vs. 44.69 (Muon) vs. 44.61 (AdamW baseline)

**µP compatibility:**
- Learning rate transfers across model widths {128, 256, 512} for both LLaMA and Qwen architectures under both spectral norm scaling and explicit orthogonalization schemes

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** While orthogonal transformations in optimization are not new (e.g., spectral normalization, Shampoo, Muon), Pion is the first to formulate a complete general-purpose LLM optimizer on the isospectral manifold via Lie group updates without reparameterization. The connection to minimum hyperspherical energy is an elegant theoretical bonus.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Complete convergence proofs for both deterministic and stochastic settings. Systematic ablation of all four design choices on 60M models. Experiments span pretraining, SFT, and RLVR across multiple architectures. However, the largest pretraining experiment is only 1.3B parameters with 54B tokens — far from frontier-scale training.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate-to-good.** Algorithm boxes are complete and detailed. Implementation details are specified (Megatron-LM, LLaMA-Factory, VeRL frameworks). However, no code release is mentioned, and some hyperparameter choices (e.g., RMS constant c=0.2, β₂=0.95) may require tuning for new settings.
- **Significance: Potentially high.** If spectrum-preserving optimization proves effective at larger scales (10B+), it could shift how we think about optimizer design for LLMs. The normalization-free result is particularly striking. However, the paper acknowledges scaling to larger models as future work.
- **Red flags:**
  - The fixed-spectrum constraint is a strong inductive bias that could limit expressivity — the paper does not empirically test whether allowing the spectrum to evolve (as AdamW/Muon do) might achieve lower final loss
  - Memory overhead of 59.8 GB vs. 51.6 GB (AdamW) per GPU could be problematic for large models even with the second-moment-free variant
  - The pretraining validation loss (2.7350) is actually slightly worse than Muon (2.7225), though Pion compensates with better downstream benchmarks — this discrepancy deserves investigation
  - The 44% wall-clock slowdown vs. AdamW is non-trivial for large-scale training budgets

## Strengths

- Fundamentally new optimization paradigm with elegant Lie-group geometric motivation and complete convergence theory
- Exceptional training stability: flat diagnostic indicators, survives normalization-free setting, handles 200-layer networks
- Comprehensive evaluation across three training paradigms (pretraining, SFT, RLVR) with multiple base models
- Principled design exploration with clear ablations for each component
- µP compatibility with verified hyperparameter transfer is practically valuable
- Alternating update variant provides a practical compute-performance tradeoff

## Limitations

- Only tested up to 1.3B parameters for pretraining — frontier models are 100-1000x larger
- ~44% slower than AdamW per step and ~16% more memory (full variant)
- Fixed spectrum may be too restrictive; no comparison with a "soft" spectrum-preserving variant
- The paper does not explore combination with other techniques like gradient clipping, learning rate warmup/decay schedules beyond cosine, or mixed-precision nuances specific to Pion
- Lie-algebra momentum requires storing separate in-side and out-side buffers (2(d²_in + d²_out) floats), which grows quadratically with hidden dimension — problematic for very large models

## Relevance

Pion 从流形优化的角度重新审视了 LLM 训练中的优化器设计，其谱保持机制与训练稳定性的关系、Lie 代数动量的几何一致性、以及与 µP 的天然兼容性，为理解深度学习优化的几何性质提供了新视角，对优化器研究和大规模训练实践都有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would a "soft" spectrum constraint that allows gradual spectral evolution outperform the hard preservation enforced by Pion?
- How does Pion scale to 10B+ parameter models with trillion-token training budgets — does the stability advantage persist?
- Can Pion's Lie-algebra updates be combined with low-rank adaptations (e.g., LoRA) for parameter-efficient finetuning?
- What is the implicit regularization effect of spectrum preservation on generalization, beyond the hyperspherical energy argument?
- Does the alternating update variant's superiority in RLVR (vs. bilateral in SFT) suggest that the exploration-exploitation dynamics differ fundamentally across training paradigms?
- How sensitive is Pion to the initial spectrum — would spectrum-aware initialization (e.g., initializing with specific singular value distributions) further improve performance?
- Can Pion be extended to tensor-valued parameters (e.g., in attention) beyond matrix-valued weight updates?
