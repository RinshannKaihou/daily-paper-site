> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Vertex-Softmax，证明 softmax 在独立分数区间上的精确最优化可在约束盒的顶点处取到，仅需 K+1 个候选即可精确求解，大幅提升了 Transformer 注意力机制的认证鲁棒性验证率。

> **TL;DR:** The authors prove that the exact optimum of a linear objective over softmax outputs constrained to a score box is always attained at a vertex, yielding a K+1 threshold solver (Vertex-Softmax) that runs in O(K log K) time and produces dramatically tighter certified robustness bounds for transformer attention compared to existing relaxation-based verifiers.

## 核心贡献

- **顶点精确性定理**：证明了在独立分数区间（score box）约束下，线性目标函数在 softmax 输出上的最小值一定在约束盒的顶点处取到，将搜索空间从 $2^K$ 个顶点缩减为 $K+1$ 个阈值候选
- **信息最优性声明**：形式化证明了 Vertex-Softmax 是仅依赖独立分数区间所能获得的最紧的下界，刻画了进一步改进所需利用的信息结构（分数相关性、分数-值耦合等）
- **Vertex-CROWN 验证器**：将 Vertex-Softmax 原语集成到 CROWN 风格的验证器中，并设计了目标级混合策略（取 CROWN 直接路径与 Vertex 路径的最大值），在 MNIST、Fashion-MNIST、CIFAR-10 上大幅提升认证率
- 在二分类 MNIST 上，$\epsilon=0.03$ 时认证率从 20.3% 提升至 77.8%；在 Fashion-MNIST 全块模型上，$\epsilon=0.02$ 时从 5.6% 提升至 14.0%

## Key Contributions

- **Vertex Exactness Theorem (Theorem 3.1):** Proves that the minimum of $c^\top \text{softmax}(s)$ over an independent score box $B = \prod_j [\ell_j, u_j]$ is always attained at a vertex of $B$. The key insight is that the linear-fractional objective has constant-sign derivatives along each coordinate, so every coordinate can be pushed to an endpoint without increasing the objective.
- **Threshold Exactness Theorem (Theorem 3.2):** After sorting the objective coefficients $c_{(1)} \leq \cdots \leq c_{(K)}$, the optimal vertex has a threshold structure—upper bounds on the first $m$ coordinates and lower bounds on the rest—for some $m \in \{0, \ldots, K\}$. This collapses the search to exactly $K+1$ candidates, solvable via one sort and one prefix-suffix sweep in $O(K \log K)$ time.
- **Score-Box Optimality (Corollary 3.3):** Vertex-Softmax exhausts the information available at the independent score-box interface. Any tighter certificate must exploit score correlations, score-value coupling, or a reachable score set smaller than the independent box.
- **Vertex-CROWN Integration:** Embeds the primitive into a CROWN/LiRPA-style verifier with formal soundness guarantees (Proposition 4.1). A per-target hybrid $L_{\text{hyb},t} = \max\{L_{\text{CROWN},t}, L_{\text{VC},t}\}$ captures the best of direct affine relaxation and exact softmax solving without sacrificing soundness.

## Methodology

The paper follows a clean theory-to-system pipeline:

1. **Problem formulation:** Certified verification of transformer attention requires bounding $\min_{s \in B} c^\top \text{softmax}(s)$ where $B$ is a product of independent score intervals and $c$ is a downstream coefficient vector. Existing verifiers relax softmax independently of $c$, introducing avoidable slack.

2. **Exact solver (Vertex-Softmax):** Via the substitution $y_j = e^{s_j}$, the softmax problem becomes a bounded linear-fractional program over a positive box. The vertex property follows from the fact that each coordinate of the ratio has a derivative of constant sign. After sorting $c$, the optimal vertex splits coordinates into "heavy-cheap" (upper bound) and "light-expensive" (lower bound) at a contiguous threshold. Algorithm 1 implements this with a stability shift $a = \max_j u_j$ to prevent overflow.

3. **Vertex-CROWN verifier construction:** The backward CROWN/LiRPA pass supplies sound score bounds $[\ell_{ij}, u_{ij}]$ and value-side coefficient lower bounds $z_{ij}^L$. Vertex-Softmax solves each attention row exactly at this interface. For ViT-style blocks, coefficients are derived by contracting the suffix affine lower bound through the output projection and value matrices.

4. **Hybrid strategy:** Direct CROWN propagates tighter affine relaxations through the full computation graph, while Vertex-CROWN solves the softmax subproblem exactly but relies on looser value-side and suffix bounds. Taking the per-target maximum captures the best of both.

## Results & Highlights

| MNIST binary, 16 tok, $\epsilon=0.03$ | Cert. rate | 20.3% | 77.8% | +57.5 pp |
| MNIST binary, 49 tok, $\epsilon=0.03$ | Cert. rate | 46.5% | 92.5% | +46.0 pp |
| MNIST 10-class, 16 tok, $\epsilon=0.02$ | Cert. rate | 3.3% | 12.9% | +9.5 pp |
| Fashion-MNIST full block, $\epsilon=0.02$ | Cert. rate | 5.6% | 14.0% | +8.4 pp |

| K=4, d=16, $\epsilon=0.05$ vs $\alpha$-CROWN | Cert. rate | — | 62.5% vs 15.8% | 4× better |

- **Speed:** Vertex-Softmax runs in 0.03–0.07s per trial, vs 1.5–3.7s for $\alpha$-CROWN and 268–395s for ABCrown-BaB. At $K=128$, Vertex-Softmax is 24× faster than Wei-LSE (0.08s vs 2.0s).
- **Scalability:** The threshold solver matches exhaustive vertex enumeration up to $K=16$ and remains practical at $K=512$.
- **Paired improvements:** On Fashion-MNIST $\epsilon=0.02$, the hybrid improves lower bounds on 99.7% of individual images with a median improvement of 4.26 points.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (4/5):** The vertex property for linear-fractional programs over boxes is classical (Dinkelbach, 1967), but the $K+1$ threshold reduction and its integration into attention verification is novel and elegant. The formal optimality characterization of the score-box interface is a clean contribution.

**Rigor (5/5):** Formal theorems with complete proofs, soundness guarantees for the integrated verifier, interval-arithmetic validation, and careful numerical status reporting. The paper is unusually rigorous even by verification standards.

**Reproducibility (5/5):** Code is publicly available. All experiments specify seeds, evaluation denominators, hardware, and numerical checks. Protocol tables enable direct reproduction.

**Significance (4/5):** The certified-rate improvements are dramatic in settings where softmax is the binding source of looseness. The optimality theorem provides a clean characterization of what further progress requires. However, the experiments are limited to single-block ViT-style models; scaling to deep transformers with layer normalization remains future work.

**Red flags:** The exactness claim is local to the score-box interface—value-bound slack and suffix relaxation can still dominate. Full-block gains are real but the hybrid is ~10× slower than direct CROWN. The practical impact for large-scale LLM verification is not demonstrated.

## Strengths

- Elegant mathematical framework: transforms a seemingly hard optimization problem into a clean $O(K \log K)$ solver via vertex and threshold theorems
- Formal optimality result precisely characterizes the information surface of the score-box interface, making clear what additional structure is needed for further tightening
- Dramatic certified-rate improvements in high-uncertainty regimes (e.g., +57.5pp on binary MNIST)
- Orders of magnitude faster than stronger baselines ($\alpha$-CROWN, branch-and-bound) while matching or exceeding their certification rates on selected settings
- Thorough experimental methodology with multiple seeds, paired analyses, slack decomposition, and numerical soundness checks

## Limitations

- **Local exactness only:** Vertex-Softmax is exact at the score-box interface but does not address value-bound slack, score correlations, score-value coupling, or suffix relaxation
- **Single-block models:** Experiments use one-block ViT-style attention; deep transformers with layer normalization, multiple blocks, and complex interactions are not tested
- **Cost of hybrid:** The full-block hybrid is ~10× slower than direct CROWN due to bound-construction overhead (the Vertex-Softmax primitive itself is negligible at 0.02% of runtime)
- **No LLM-scale demonstration:** The method is complementary to runtime monitoring and statistical approaches for frontier LLM systems, but no such integration is shown
- **Limited to patch-attention scale:** The largest real-data models use 49 tokens and 64-dimensional embeddings; scalability to production-scale attention layers is not demonstrated

## Relevance

本文与"ML可靠性与数学基础"方向高度相关。Vertex-Softmax 提供了 softmax 函数在区间约束下的精确最优化方法，属于神经网络认证验证中的形式化保证工作。其严格的数学证明框架和对数值稳定性的关注，与我们对浮点精度、数值可靠性及形式化验证的兴趣一致。虽然当前实验规模限于小型 ViT 模型，但其方法论框架可为更大规模 Transformer 的可靠性分析奠定基础。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the threshold structure be extended to correlated score polytopes (beyond independent boxes) to close the remaining gap identified by the optimality theorem?
2. How does Vertex-Softmax scale to multi-block transformers with layer normalization, where score boxes may be much tighter and value-bound slack may dominate?
3. Could joint optimization over scores and values (exploiting score-value coupling) yield a tractable extension that captures additional information discarded at the score-box interface?
4. What is the interaction between Vertex-Softmax exactness and quantization-induced score perturbations in mixed-precision attention layers?
5. Can the formal soundness guarantees be extended to produce end-to-end proof-carrying certificates at GPU scale using the interval-arithmetic evaluation path described in Appendix D?
