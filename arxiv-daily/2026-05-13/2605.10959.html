QuIDE: Mastering the Quantized Intelligence Trade-off via Active Optimization

Xiantao Jiang

arXiv: 2605.10959

Date: 2026-05-13

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10959

一句话总结： 本文提出 QuIDE 框架，核心是 Intelligence Index I = (C×P)/log₂(T+1) 及其精度门控变体 I'，将量化模型的压缩-精度-延迟三维权衡统一为单一标量评分，在六种实验条件下揭示了任务依赖的 Pareto Knee：简单任务和大模型最优为 4-bit，复杂视觉任务 8-bit 为安全边界。
TL;DR: QuIDE introduces the Intelligence Index I = (C×P)/log₂(T+1) and its accuracy-gated variant I' to unify compression-accuracy-latency evaluation into a single scalar. Across six experimental settings (SimpleCNN, ResNet-18, Llama-3-8B), the Pareto-optimal bit-width is task-dependent: 4-bit for simple tasks and large LLMs, 8-bit for deep CNNs on complex vision tasks. I' correctly flags non-viable configurations that the raw I would reward, and doubles as a fitness function for genetic-algorithm-based mixed-precision search.

核心贡献

  • Intelligence Index I = (C×P)/log₂(T+1)：将压缩比、预测精度和推理延迟统一为单一标量，压缩收益仅在精度保持时有效（乘法形式），延迟采用对数衰减惩罚（边际成本递减）
  • 精度门控变体 I'：通过 P_thresh 可行性阈值，在精度崩溃时将指标归零，防止极端压缩被误判为高效
  • 任务依赖的 Pareto Knee 发现：简单任务（MNIST）和大模型（Llama-3-8B）最优为 4-bit；复杂视觉任务（ResNet-18/ImageNet）8-bit 为安全边界，4-bit PTQ 导致灾难性精度崩溃
  • QuIDE-GA 混合精度搜索：以 I' 作为遗传算法适应度函数，自动发现异构位宽拓扑（如 8-8-8-4），优于均匀量化基线
  • 系统化的量化部署指南：16-bit 无损（通用首选）；先探测任务复杂度再选择 4/8-bit；使用混合精度填补 4-8bit 间隙；始终使用 I' 而非 I 做门控

Key Contributions

  • The Intelligence Index I = (C×P)/log₂(T+1): A composite scalar that unifies compression, accuracy, and latency into a single ranking criterion. The multiplicative C×P form ensures zero utility under accuracy collapse, while the log₂(T+1) denominator applies diminishing marginal cost to latency.
  • Accuracy-gated variant I': Introduces a viability threshold P_thresh = max(1/K, P_FP − δ) below which the index collapses to zero, preventing extreme compression from being rewarded when the model is non-functional. The shifted ReLU gating mechanism correctly flags non-viable configurations that raw I falsely promotes.
  • Empirical discovery of complexity-dependent Pareto Knee: Across six experimental conditions, the optimal bit-width shifts from 4-bit (simple tasks like MNIST; large LLMs like Llama-3-8B) to 8-bit (deep CNNs on complex vision tasks like ImageNet-1K), where 4-bit PTQ causes catastrophic accuracy collapse (0.18% on ImageNet).
  • QuIDE-GA for mixed-precision search: A genetic algorithm using I' as the sole fitness function discovers heterogeneous bit-width topologies (e.g., 8-8-8-4 for CIFAR-10, 8-8-8-8-8-16 for ImageNet) that outperform uniform quantization baselines.
  • Practical deployment guidelines: Five empirically grounded recommendations—start at 16-bit (universally lossless), probe task complexity before choosing 4/8-bit, use mixed-precision for the 4–8 bit gap, always gate with I', and set P_thresh conservatively.

Methodology

Problem formulation: Given a full-precision model M_FP with 32-bit parameters, quantization produces M^(b) at b bits. Three evaluation axes: Compression C(b) = 32/b, Predictive Accuracy P(b), and Computational Cost T(b) (mean inference latency).

Intelligence Index derivation:

  • Spatial Utility: U(b) = C(b) × P(b) — multiplicative form ensures zero accuracy → zero utility, motivated by MDL principle
  • Temporal Penalty: f(T) = log₂(T + 1) — satisfies f(0) = 0, monotonicity, and diminishing marginal cost; sub-additive; invariant to log base for ranking
  • Composite: I(b) = C(b)×P(b) / log₂(T(b)+1)
  • Accuracy-gated: I'(b) = C(b) × max(P(b) − P_thresh, 0) / log₂(T(b)+1), with P_thresh = max(1/K, P_FP − δ)

Experimental protocol: PTQ experiments across six conditions (SimpleCNN on MNIST/CIFAR-10/CIFAR-100, ResNet-18 on CIFAR-10/ImageNet-1K, Llama-3-8B via MMLU). Bit-widths ∈ {32, 16, 8, 4, 2}. Three random seeds (0, 42, 123). Latency measured on NVIDIA RTX 3090 with 100 warm-up + 1000 timed passes, CUDA synchronization. Calibration on 512 images per seed.

Mixed-precision search: Genetic Algorithm (population=20, 30 generations, μ=0.15) over discrete manifold A = {16, 8, 4, 2}^L using I' as sole fitness function.

Baseline metrics: Accuracy-Compression Product (ACP = C×P) and Accuracy per Log-Second (ALS = P/log₂(T+1)) to validate the necessity of the three-dimensional formulation.

Results & Highlights

  • Pareto Knee is task-dependent: 4-bit optimal for MNIST (I' = 1.292) and Llama-3-8B (I' = 2.58); 8-bit optimal for CIFAR-10/CIFAR-100/ResNet-18/ImageNet (e.g., ImageNet I'₈ᵦ = 0.416)
  • 4-bit catastrophic collapse on complex tasks: ResNet-18/ImageNet 4-bit PTQ drops to 0.18% accuracy (from 69.76% FP); CIFAR-100 drops to 19.59%
  • I vs I' ranking divergence at collapse boundaries: On ResNet-18/CIFAR-10, raw I ranks 4-bit above 8-bit (0.933 > 0.824), but I' correctly reverses this (0.064 ≪ 0.390)
  • 16-bit is universally lossless: ΔP < 0.01% across all models, with 2× compression — safe default first step
  • QuIDE-GA outperforms uniform baselines: On CIFAR-10, discovered topology 8-8-8-4 achieves 75.02% accuracy with I' = 0.983 (vs. uniform 4-bit I' = 0.633); on ImageNet, 8-8-8-8-8-16 maintains 69.36% with I' = 0.350
  • Llama-3-8B validation: I' correctly identifies 4-bit (NF4) as the sweet spot (I' = 2.58) and nullifies 3-bit and 2-bit configurations

Critical Assessment

Novelty (Moderate): The Intelligence Index is a well-motivated composite metric, but the individual components (compression ratio, accuracy, logarithmic latency penalty) are well-established. The main novelty lies in the specific multiplicative-over-logarithmic scalarization and the accuracy-gating mechanism. The connection to information theory (MDL, Information Bottleneck) provides theoretical grounding but is not deeply exploited.

Rigor (Moderate-High): Experiments follow a standardized protocol with multiple seeds, controlled hardware, and proper synchronization. The comparison against alternative formulations (ACP, ALS) is informative. However, the HAQ and BRECQ baselines are explicitly noted as approximate, limiting direct comparison. The Llama-3-8B case study relies on published/benchmark data rather than the same PTQ protocol as CNN experiments.

Reproducibility (High): All hyperparameters, hardware specs, and measurement protocols are detailed. The GA algorithm is fully specified in Appendix B. Three seeds are used throughout.

Significance (Moderate): The metric fills a real gap in quantization evaluation — current practice reports accuracy, size, and latency separately. However, the practical impact depends on adoption by the community, and the metric's value is primarily in ranking rather than providing absolute guidance (since P_thresh requires task-specific tuning).

Red flags:

  • The δ values for P_thresh vary widely across datasets (0.07 for Llama to 0.60 for ImageNet), which raises questions about sensitivity to this hyperparameter
  • Latency contribution is secondary in most experiments — the index is predominantly shaped by C×P
  • The "genetic algorithm" for MPS is a simple GA with only 30 generations and population 20 — limited exploration
  • No comparison with more recent MPS methods or Pareto-frontier selection techniques

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated mathematical formulation with clear design rationale for each component
  • Accuracy-gating mechanism (I') addresses a real failure mode — raw I can endorse non-deployable configurations
  • Systematic evaluation across diverse model scales (SimpleCNN → ResNet-18 → Llama-3-8B)
  • Honest discussion section that revises claims based on evidence (e.g., rejecting universal 4-bit sweet spot)
  • Practical deployment guidelines derived from empirical findings
  • I' as a fitness function for mixed-precision search is a natural and useful application

Limitations

  • P_thresh sensitivity: The viability threshold requires per-dataset tuning of δ; automatic determination is acknowledged as an open question
  • Latency discrimination is weak: In most experiments, latency varies minimally across bit-widths, making the log₂(T+1) term a minor correction rather than a meaningful ranking factor
  • Limited MPS evaluation: The GA is simple (30 generations, population 20) and HAQ/BRECQ baselines are approximate — not a rigorous MPS benchmark
  • Single-hardware evaluation: All latency measurements on RTX 3090; no cross-hardware validation (edge devices, mobile GPUs)
  • No QAT experiments: Only PTQ is evaluated; whether the metric generalizes to QAT or training-aware compression is untested
  • Llama-3-8B results are indirect: MMLU scores appear to be from published benchmarks rather than the authors' own controlled experiments

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How sensitive is I' to the choice of δ across a continuous range? Can P_thresh be set automatically via a data-driven criterion (e.g., relative to the accuracy distribution of quantized models)?
  2. How does the Intelligence Index behave under QAT (quantization-aware training) rather than PTQ — does the Pareto Knee shift?
  3. Can the metric be extended to incorporate energy consumption as a fourth dimension, particularly for battery-constrained edge deployment?
  4. How does I' perform as a reward signal for reinforcement-learning-based MPS (e.g., DDPG as in HAQ), compared to the GA approach used here?
  5. Does the metric transfer across hardware platforms — do Pareto Knee rankings change significantly when latency is measured on mobile GPUs or NPUs instead of RTX 3090?
