> **一句话总结：** 本文利用稀疏自编码器揭示离散扩散语言模型中不同语义属性（主题、情感、风格）在去噪过程中以不同时间、锐度和幅度"锁定"的时序规律，据此提出自适应干预调度器，在三属性同时控制任务上达到93%的引导强度，超越最强基线15个百分点，且不损失生成质量。

> **TL;DR:** Uniform steering interventions degrade discrete diffusion language model (DLM) output quality—especially under multi-attribute control. By training SAEs on four DLMs (MDLM-124M, SEDD-124M, LLaDA-8B, DREAM-7B), the authors discover each attribute commits at a distinct denoising schedule: topic locks in the first ~2% of steps on MDLM, while sentiment emerges over ~21%. They propose an adaptive scheduler that concentrates intervention only on steps where each attribute is actively forming. The efficiency gain over uniform scheduling admits a closed-form characterization via a single dispersion statistic (Theorem 1: ρ² = 1 + CV²c(s/c)). On triple-attribute simultaneous control (sentiment + topic + style), the method reaches up to 93% classifier confidence, beating the strongest baseline by 15 pp while preserving generation quality. A key falsifiable prediction—that adaptive ≈ uniform on models with flat temporal profiles—is confirmed on LLaDA.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示属性"锁定"的三大时序规律**：通过在四个不同规模的DLM（MDLM-124M、SEDD-124M、LLaDA-8B、DREAM-7B）上训练TopK稀疏自编码器，发现不同语义属性的去噪特征有三个关键差异：锁定时间（timing）、锁定锐度（sharpness）和效应量不对称（effect-size asymmetry）。例如在MDLM上，主题在前2%的去噪步内即确定，而情感在约21%的步骤中逐渐形成。
- **证明训练目标是特征几何的主导因素**：共享相同loss family的模型（MDLM与DREAM，不同架构、56倍规模差距）在特征空间中的对齐度（cosine sim. 0.44/0.88 max），远高于共享架构但loss不同的模型（MDLM与SEDD，0.08）。
- **提出自适应干预调度框架**：设计了一种仅在属性"活跃形成"阶段集中干预的调度策略，将有效引导强度分解为α·r(a)·wdyn(t)三因子，并给出闭式最优解（Theorem 1）。对于多属性组合，通过SAE特征集的近似不相交性实现加性组合，并用decoder Gram矩阵界定了跨属性干扰（Proposition 1）。
- **在七个属性组合任务上全面验证**：覆盖3个单属性和4个多属性控制条件，在四个DLM上对比4个基线方法。在LLaDA上，SAE方法在topic控制上达99-100%，而所有非SAE基线仅24-32%。
- **三属性联合控制SOTA**：在sentiment+topic+style三属性同时控制任务上，达到81%（MDLM）、93%（SEDD）、72%（LLaDA）、93%（DREAM）的几何平均分类器置信度。

## Key Contributions

- Train TopK SAEs (k=32) on four DLMs spanning two training objectives, three architectures, and a 60× scale range (MDLM-124M, SEDD-124M, LLaDA-8B, DREAM-7B), revealing three axes of variation in how semantic attributes commit during denoising: timing (topic at 2% vs. sentiment at 21% on MDLM), sharpness (concentrated vs. diffuse emergence), and effect-size asymmetry (topic features carry 2-3× larger Cohen's |d| than sentiment).
- Demonstrate that training objective—not architecture—is the dominant factor shaping feature geometry: models sharing a loss family achieve 0.44 mean / 0.88 max cosine similarity in SAE decoder alignment, versus 0.08 for same-architecture different-loss models.
- Propose an adaptive steering framework with closed-form optimal scheduling (Theorem 1: the adaptive-vs-uniform efficiency ratio is ρ² = 1 + CV²c(s/c), governed by a single dispersion statistic of the commitment distribution), and bound cross-attribute interference via decoder Gram structure (Proposition 1: realized cosine coupling ≤ 0.09 across all model-attribute pairs).
- Introduce effectiveness-ratio calibration r(a) that normalizes per-attribute steering strength, addressing effect-size asymmetry where equal-α interventions over-steer topic (large d) and under-steer sentiment (small d).
- Achieve comprehensive evaluation across four DLMs, seven steering conditions, and four baselines (Contrastive Vectors, Probe, PCA, Prompt Steering), with triple-attribute control reaching up to 93% geometric-mean classifier confidence.

## Methodology

The paper follows a two-stage pipeline: (1) interpretability analysis to characterize temporal dynamics, and (2) an adaptive steering framework that exploits those dynamics.

**Stage 1: SAE Training & Temporal Analysis**

TopK SAEs with k=32 are trained on residual-stream activations from four DLMs. Training layers are selected via a diffusion logits lens and layer probing study. SAE expansion ratios are 16× (MDLM/SEDD, d_SAE=12,288) and 4× (LLaDA/DREAM, d_SAE=14,336–16,384). Unlike AR models, DLM activations vary with masking rate, so SAEs are trained on activations sampled across uniformly distributed masking rates.

Attribute features are extracted via contrastive selection: balanced binary corpora for sentiment (IMDB), topic (AG News), and style (Pavlick corpus / classifier-labeled IMDB). For each feature, Cohen's d effect size is computed between contrastive classes, retaining top-50 features per direction per layer passing Mann-Whitney U test (p<0.01, Bonferroni-corrected). Vocabulary grounding via logit-lens projection confirms semantic coherence.

Temporal dynamics are quantified by partitioning the denoising trajectory into B temporal blocks and computing block fractions (share of total positive activation growth in each block). This yields per-attribute commitment profiles capturing timing and sharpness.

**Stage 2: Adaptive Steering Framework**

Steering operates in SAE feature space. At denoising step t and layer ℓ, the effective steering strength decomposes as:

α_eff(a,t,ℓ) = α · r(a) · w_dyn(t; a, ℓ)

where α is the global scale, r(a) ∈ (0,1] is the effectiveness ratio (calibrated from single-attribute dose-response sweeps), and w_dyn(t) is the temporal weight derived from commitment fractions. For multi-attribute composition, interventions are applied additively in feature space with a ReLU clamp, leveraging near-disjoint feature sets across attributes.

Four steering configurations are evaluated with increasing sophistication:
- **Uniform** (r=1, w_dyn=1): ignores temporal structure entirely
- **Adaptive** (r=1): uses commitment-timing/sharpness via w_dyn
- **Uniform+E** (w_dyn=1): uses effectiveness-ratio calibration only
- **Adaptive+E**: combines both mechanisms

**Theoretical Results:**
- Theorem 1 derives the optimal allocation as α_t* ∝ s_t/c_t via KKT conditions, with efficiency ratio ρ² = 1 + CV²c(s/c). The adaptive gain is large when attributes commit in few steps, near one when emergence is spread evenly.
- Proposition 1 bounds cross-attribute residual-space interference via decoder Gram matrix: cos(Δx^(i), Δx^(j)) ≤ σ_max(G^(ij)) / √(σ_min(G^(ii))·σ_min(G^(jj))), with realized values ≤ 0.09 across all pairs.

## Results & Highlights

**Single-attribute steering:**
- SAE methods achieve 70–100% (MDLM), 77–100% (SEDD), 66–100% (LLaDA), 96–100% (DREAM) classifier confidence.
- On LLaDA topic, SAE methods reach 99–100% while non-SAE baselines remain at 24–32% and prompt steering only 65%—a dramatic gap.
- On MDLM, Adaptive matches Uniform's control at substantially lower perplexity (50–57 vs. 59–93) by targeting high-emergence blocks.
- On LLaDA, whose flat temporal profile makes Adaptive degenerate to Uniform (confirming Theorem 1's prediction ρ≈1), both perform comparably.

**Multi-attribute steering:**
- Triple-attribute (S+T+St) confidence reaches 81% (MDLM), 93% (SEDD), 72% (LLaDA), 93% (DREAM).
- On LLaDA, best SAE method exceeds best baseline by +15pp (72% vs. 57%), driven by topic conditions.
- On MDLM, Adaptive resolves inter-attribute interference: for S+St, Uniform achieves 49% while Adaptive reaches 64% by targeting sentiment's later emergence blocks, avoiding disruption of formality's earlier commitment.
- Adaptive+E matches Uniform's triple control (80% vs. 79% on MDLM) at lower PPL (50–63 vs. 73–91).

**Quality-control trade-off:**
- Adaptive consistently produces less cross-attribute interference than Uniform (e.g., up to 52pp for Uniform vs. 31pp for Adaptive on MDLM).
- On LLaDA, E-ratio calibration yields the largest diversity gains: Adaptive+E preserves dist-2 at 52–65% where Uniform collapses to 43–45%.

**Special case—SEDD:** Contrastive Vectors matches SAE performance (100% S, 100% T, 92% St) because SEDD's distributed feature geometry lets the mean-difference vector aggregate many aligned contributions.

**DREAM:** Shows AR-inherited late-trajectory behavior. Adaptive matches Uniform's high control but with mixed quality trade-offs.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The application of SAEs to characterize attribute commitment dynamics in discrete diffusion models is novel. The adaptive scheduling framework with its closed-form Theorem 1 (connecting efficiency to a dispersion statistic) and Proposition 1 (Gram-based interference bound) are clean theoretical contributions. The finding that training objective dominates architecture in shaping feature geometry is a valuable empirical insight.
- **Rigor: Strong.** The paper provides full proofs for all theoretical claims. Experiments cover four models, seven conditions, and four baselines. The falsifiable prediction (flat profiles → adaptive ≈ uniform) is confirmed on LLaDA, strengthening internal validity. The analysis of proxy fidelity and when it can be loose (Appendix A.1.6) is admirably honest.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate.** The methodology is well-specified (TopK SAEs, contrastive selection, commitment fractions). However, SAE training is computationally expensive, and the four DLMs include proprietary/large models (LLaDA-8B, DREAM-7B). No code release is mentioned.
- **Significance: High.** As discrete diffusion models gain traction as alternatives to AR models, understanding how to steer them without quality degradation is practically important. The temporal analysis framework and adaptive scheduling paradigm are likely to influence future work on DLM control.
- **Red flags:**
  - Classifier-based metrics may not capture nuanced attribute expression (acknowledged by authors).
  - Generated sequences are short (64–1024 tokens); longer-length steering is unexplored.
  - The proxy w_dyn is not guaranteed to be optimal—it's justified empirically and through post-hoc validation rather than structural proof.
  - SEDD's Contrastive Vectors baseline matches SAE performance, suggesting the SAE overhead may not always be justified.

## Strengths

- Novel mechanistic analysis revealing three distinct axes of temporal variation (timing, sharpness, effect-size asymmetry) across attributes, models, and training objectives.
- Clean theoretical framework: Theorem 1 provides a falsifiable prediction confirmed experimentally; Proposition 1 gives interpretable interference bounds.
- Comprehensive evaluation: 4 models × 7 conditions × 4 baselines with quality gates (PPL < 100, dist-2 ≥ 50% of baseline).
- Training objective identified as dominant factor in feature geometry—a valuable finding for the interpretability community.
- Effectiveness-ratio calibration is simple, principled, and effective for multi-attribute balance.
- Honest about limitations: proxy fidelity, classifier metric caveats, and the SEDD exception are discussed openly.

## Limitations

- SAE training adds significant computational overhead; the paper does not quantify this cost explicitly.
- Evaluation relies on off-the-shelf classifiers (DistilBERT-SST2, BERT-AG News, RoBERTa formality ranker) that may not capture nuanced attribute expression.
- Generated sequences are short (64–1024 tokens); scalability to longer generation is unexplored.
- Diversity decreases at high steering strengths in SAE-based methods (acknowledged by authors).
- The proxy w_dyn is not provably optimal; it's justified by plausibility arguments and empirical validation rather than structural guarantees.
- On SEDD, the simpler Contrastive Vectors baseline matches SAE performance, questioning the overhead-benefit trade-off in some settings.
- Only three broad attributes (sentiment, topic, style) are studied; extension to fine-grained attributes is open.

## Relevance

本文将稀疏自编码器的机械可解释性方法系统性地应用于离散扩散语言模型，揭示了不同语义属性在去噪过程中的时序"锁定"规律（timing、sharpness、effect-size asymmetry），与用户对LLM隐藏状态可解释性（mechanistic interpretability、SAE、representation probing）的研究兴趣高度吻合。其理论分析（Theorem 1的自适应调度效率刻画、Proposition 1的decoder Gram干扰界）也为理解模型内部表征的形成与干预提供了严谨的数学框架。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the adaptive scheduler perform on fine-grained attributes (specific rhetorical styles, domain-specific terminology, toxicity subtypes) where SAE feature sets may overlap more substantially?
- Can the commitment-profile analysis be applied to autoregressive models to understand attribute formation during sequential generation, and would a similar adaptive framework apply?
- What is the computational cost breakdown of SAE training + commitment profiling vs. the actual steering inference overhead?
- Theorem 1 predicts adaptive ≈ uniform when CV_c(s/c) ≈ 0. Are there regimes where the proxy w_dyn is actively anti-correlated with s_t/c_t, causing adaptive to underperform uniform?
- How do the findings transfer to continuous diffusion models or other iterative generation paradigms (e.g., iterative refinement, mask-predict)?
- Can the effectiveness ratio r(a) be derived analytically from Cohen's d ratios rather than requiring empirical calibration, reducing the setup cost for new attributes?
- The paper shows training objective dominates feature geometry—does this imply that SAE features trained on one DLM can transfer to another with the same loss?
