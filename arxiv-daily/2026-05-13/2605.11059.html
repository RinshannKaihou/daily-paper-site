Uniform Scaling Limits in AdamW-Trained Transformers

arXiv: 2605.11059

Date: 2026-05-13

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11059

一句话总结： 本文将深度 Transformer 的隐藏状态动力学建模为通过注意力机制耦合的相互作用粒子系统（IPS），在 AdamW 训练下证明了前向传播和反向传播变量在深度和注意力头数趋于无穷时，以速率 O(L^{-1}+L^{-1/3}H^{-1/2}) 一致收敛到 McKean-Vlasov ODE 系统，且收敛界不依赖于 token 数量和嵌入维度。
TL;DR: Proves that the hidden-state and backpropagation dynamics of deep, wide, attention-only transformers trained with AdamW converge uniformly over initial conditions to a forward-backward system of McKean-Vlasov ODEs at rate O(L^{-1}+L^{-1/3}H^{-1/2}), where L is depth and H is the number of attention heads. The bounds are independent of the number of tokens N and, with Blockwise AdamW, independent of the embedding dimension d. The key technical innovation uses flow maps and concentration of measure (not covering arguments) to avoid dependence on N, and exploits AdamW's decoupled weight decay to maintain compact parameter support globally across training steps.

核心贡献

  • 将注意力层中的多头像注意力建模为关于经验测度的积分，将 Transformer 的逐层传播建模为相互作用粒子系统（IPS）
  • 证明了在 L^{-1} 残差缩放和 i.i.d. 参数初始化下，前向隐藏状态和反向伴随变量联合收敛到前向-后向 McKean-Vlasov ODE 系统，收敛速率 O(L^{-1}+L^{-1/3}H^{-1/2})
  • 收敛界在紧致初始条件集上一致成立，且常数不依赖于 token 数量 N（避免了 Dudley 熵积分覆盖论证）
  • 利用 AdamW 的解耦权重衰减证明了参数测度 ν 在训练过程中始终具有紧支撑，从而给出全局训练的 Lipschitz 常数
  • 使用 Blockwise AdamW 时，收敛界进一步不依赖于 token 嵌入维度 d
  • 显式计算了广义注意力函数 γ 关于测度分量的 Lions 导数，给出了关于 p-Wasserstein 距离的严格 Lipschitz 界

Key Contributions

  • IPS formulation: Models multi-head attention as integration against empirical measures (equation 3) and the layer-to-layer propagation as an interacting particle system (equation 4), with residual scaled by (LH)^{-1}
  • Uniform convergence (Theorem 7): Proves L^2 convergence of the joint forward-backward dynamics uniformly over (B̄(R_0))^N at rate O(L^{-1}+L^{-1/3}H^{-1/2}), P_0-a.s.
  • Token-count-independent bounds: Achieves uniformity over initial conditions without covering arguments by exploiting the multi-linear structure of γ in the measure argument and using concentration of measure on linear functionals (extreme points are Dirac masses), reducing the supremum from P(B̄(R_0)) to B̄(R_0) × B̄(R_0)
  • AdamW-specific analysis (Lemma 26): Shows that AdamW's decoupled weight decay forces the parameter measures ν_{t,τ} to remain supported in a compact set S independent of training step τ, providing global Lipschitz constants for Γ and K
  • Blockwise AdamW extension: Using the optimiser from Xie et al. (2024), the compact set S and hence the convergence constant C_2 become independent of both d and k
  • Lions derivative computation (Lemma 1): Explicitly computes ∂_μ γ^R and establishes local Lipschitz continuity w.r.t. W_p for all p ∈ [1,∞], with non-exponential constants at p = ∞

Methodology

The paper develops a rigorous mean-field analysis along four interconnected lines:

  1. IPS → MVODE framework (Sections 2–3): The discrete transformer with L layers and H heads is formulated as an IPS where each token's hidden state x_i^r evolves through layers. The generalized attention γ (equation 2) extends softmax attention to compactly supported measures. The velocity fields Γ (forward) and K (backward/adjoint) are defined by integrating γ over the parameter distribution ν. The adjoint variable a_i^r (equation 9) satisfies a backward recursion derived from the chain rule on the Lions derivative of the loss ℓ.
  1. Global well-posedness (Appendix E): The forward-backward McKean-Vlasov ODE system (equations 53–54) is shown to have a unique solution via a truncation-plus-Grönwall strategy: velocity fields Γ, K are truncated via a projection map P_R to restore global Lipschitz continuity, then Grönwall's lemma establishes that the solution stays within B̄(R_X) and B̄(R_a) P-a.s., so the truncation is inactive for sufficiently large R. Measurability with respect to the training data filtration is established via Picard iteration preserving F^0-measurability.
  1. Uniform bounds via concentration (Section 3.5, Appendices G–I): The core technical novelty avoids covering arguments. The error martingale M_{r,τ}^{x,ζ} (equation at Section 3.5) is shown to be multi-linear in ζ, so the supremum over ζ ∈ P(B̄(R_0)) reduces to the supremum over extreme points (Dirac masses). Layers are split into ⌈L/m⌉ blocks, and within each block a Taylor expansion is used to factor out the (x,ζ)-dependence. The averaging over mH independent parameters in each block yields a variance of (mLH)^{-1}, and optimizing over block size m gives the final rate.
  1. Training dynamics coupling (Lemma 6, Appendix J): The discrepancy between discrete-time and continuous-time parameters ν̂ and ν̄ is controlled via a Grönwall argument over training steps. The AdamW flow map Φ_{t,τ} (equation 28) is shown to be Lipschitz in its gradient inputs, and the gradient difference is bounded by the hidden-state/adjoint discrepancy from Theorem 7. A joint Grönwall argument over layers and training steps closes the induction.

Results & Highlights

  • Main theorem (Theorem 7): For the attention-only unmasked transformer with i.i.d. parameter initialization (e.g., Xavier Uniform), the uniform L^2 error between discrete and continuous models is bounded by C_2(L^{-2} + L^{-2/3}H^{-1}), after T AdamW training steps, independent of N
  • Parameter compactness (Lemma 26): AdamW's weight decay dominates for large parameters, confining ν_{t,τ} to a compact set S(β_1, β_2, λ) for all t ∈ [0,1] and τ ≥ 0
  • Convergence rate trade-off: The rate O(L^{-1}+L^{-1/3}H^{-1/2}) is slightly worse than the O(L^{-1}+(LH)^{-1/2}) rate of Chizat (2025) for ResNets, sacrificed to obtain uniformity over initial conditions without token-count dependence
  • Applicability: The analysis applies to Vision Transformers, Diffusion Transformers, and BERT-style models (all unmasked architectures); extension to causal masking is left for future work
  • Post-normalisation improvement: Incorporating a post-normalisation layer (e.g., LayerNorm) improves the bounds by replacing the exponential Grönwall growth with bounded dynamics

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. This is the first work to establish uniform convergence bounds for the joint forward-backward dynamics of transformers trained with AdamW. The token-count-independent result is unexpected and technically non-trivial. Prior work either required covering arguments (introducing log(N) or N dependence) or only considered forward dynamics without training.
  • Rigor: Very high. Every assumption is clearly stated (Assumptions 1–3), every constant is tracked, and the proof is fully detailed across multiple appendices. The use of Lions calculus on P_2(R^d) is mathematically rigorous.
  • Reproducibility: Theoretical paper; the proofs are the deliverable. No code is provided or needed. Assumptions are standard and verifiable.
  • Significance: Potentially high impact for the theoretical ML community. The framework provides a clean mathematical foundation for analyzing AdamW-trained transformers in the mean-field regime. The compactness result for AdamW parameters (Lemma 26) may find independent use. However, practical impact depends on whether the limiting MVODE admits tractable analysis that yields new architectural or training insights.
  • Red flags:
  • The no-causal-masking assumption is a major limitation for autoregressive LLMs (GPT-style). The authors acknowledge this and note that the causal mask breaks the McKean-Vlasov identification.
  • The analysis is for the attention-only transformer without normalization layers, MLP blocks, or positional encodings — far from practical architectures.
  • The convergence rate O(L^{-1}+L^{-1/3}H^{-1/2}) is suboptimal compared to the known O(L^{-1}+(LH)^{-1/2}) for ResNets; closing this gap is noted as future work.
  • Results hold for finite training time T; global-in-training extensions are speculative.

Strengths

  • Clean, rigorous mathematical framework connecting IPS/mean-field theory to transformer depth scaling
  • Token-count-independent bounds are a strong and practically relevant result that avoids the expected curse of dimensionality
  • AdamW-specific analysis is timely and relevant — most prior mean-field work assumes SGD
  • The Blockwise AdamW extension removes dependence on embedding dimension d
  • Explicit Lions derivative computation and Wasserstein Lipschitz estimates for γ are useful technical contributions
  • The proof strategy of exploiting multi-linearity in the measure argument (reducing to extreme points) is elegant

Limitations

  • No causal masking — limits direct applicability to GPT-style autoregressive models
  • Attention-only architecture — no MLP blocks, normalization layers, or positional encodings
  • Suboptimal convergence rate compared to ResNet counterparts; gap not yet closed
  • Results are asymptotic (large L, large H) with no empirical validation of convergence predictions
  • Finite training time T only; global-in-training results require further work
  • The compact support assumption on parameter initialization (Assumption 1) excludes standard Gaussian initialization (though Xavier Uniform and truncated normals are covered)

Relevance

本文为 Transformer 的深度缩放极限提供了严格的数学理论，将均值场分析从宽度维度扩展到深度维度，并首次适配了 AdamW 优化器。对于关注大模型训练动力学理论、缩放定律数学基础、以及注意力机制均值场行为的研究具有重要参考价值。其 token 数无关的收敛界证明技术对理解 Transformer 的泛化行为可能具有深远影响。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the MVODE framework be extended to incorporate causal masking (e.g., by analyzing the causal mask as a constraint on the particle interaction topology)?
  • Do the predicted convergence rates match empirical observations when training practical transformers at moderate depths (e.g., L=12–96)?
  • Can the limiting MVODE system be solved or approximated analytically to yield concrete insights, such as optimal depth-to-head ratios or principled weight decay schedules?
  • How does the theory interact with layer normalization, MLP blocks, and positional encodings — can these be incorporated as perturbations to the velocity fields?
  • Can the compactness result for AdamW parameters (Lemma 26) be combined with the Łojasiewicz-Simon inequality to establish global-in-training convergence, as suggested in the conclusion?
  • Is the multi-linearity / extreme-point reduction technique applicable to other architectures with similar attention-based coupling (e.g., state-space models, mixture-of-experts)?
