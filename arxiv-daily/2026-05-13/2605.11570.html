OUI as a Structural Observable: Towards an Activation-Centric View of Neural Network Training

一句话总结： 本文提出将过拟合-欠拟合指标（OUI）视为神经网络训练的结构可观测量，证明基于激活函数的内部信号能在收敛前早期揭示训练质量，并倡导建立以激活为中心的训练动力学理论。
TL;DR: This position paper argues that the Overfitting–Underfitting Indicator (OUI) — a cheap, label-free, activation-based metric that measures how balanced neurons partition input batches — should be understood as a structural observable of neural network training dynamics. Across supervised learning (weight decay selection), reinforcement learning (PPO learning-rate regime discrimination), and online control (layer-wise weight decay adaptation), OUI consistently reveals whether a network is entering a promising or poor training regime well before convergence, suggesting a broader activation-centric theory of training dynamics.

核心贡献

  • 提出以激活为中心的训练观：将激活结构视为描述学习过程的第一类对象，而非参数优化的副产物
  • 重新定位OUI为结构可观测量：不再仅将其视为工程启发式指标，而是作为训练动力学内部结构维度的可测量信号
  • 跨场景一致性的系统总结：统一展示了OUI在监督学习、强化学习、在线控制三种不同设定下的早期预测能力
  • 提出训练双阶段假设：训练可能包含早期结构组织阶段和后期参数精修阶段，OUI对第一阶段敏感
  • 指出通向理论基础的研究方向：包括激活结构的充分性定义、形式化稳定性分析、以及训练动力学基准测试

Key Contributions

  • Proposes OUI as a structural observable rather than just a practical heuristic, treating activation structure as a legitimate layer of description for how learning unfolds
  • Synthesizes evidence across three distinct settings — supervised regularization (weight decay), RL (PPO actor-critic learning rates), and online control (layer-wise adaptation) — showing OUI consistently provides early, actionable signals
  • Introduces the activation-centric hypothesis: activation-based observables can reveal an early, actionable dimension of training that standard external metrics (loss, accuracy, return) do not capture directly
  • Identifies the two-timescale view of training: an early structural phase (where activation regimes are organized) followed by a late refinement phase (where parameters move within stable structural scaffolds)
  • Opens research directions around phase structure, contextual adequacy of activation regimes (good ranges depend on architectural role), and formal theory connecting OUI to stability and generalization

Methodology

The paper is primarily a synthesis and reinterpretation paper, not an empirical study with new experiments. It draws on the authors' prior work to build a unified theoretical argument:

  1. OUI definition (batch-based formulation): For a layer with $d_l$ activation units and a training batch $B_t = \{x_1, \ldots, x_B\}$, OUI computes binary activation masks $m_{b,n}^{(l)}(t) = \mathbf{1}[a_n^{(l)}(x_b; \theta_t) > 0]$, derives minority counts $u_n^{(l)}(t) = \min(s_n^{(l)}(t), B - s_n^{(l)}(t))$, and averages them as $\text{OUI}^{(l)}(t) = \frac{1}{d_l} \sum_{j=1}^{d_l} \frac{u_j^{(l)}(t)}{\lfloor B/2 \rfloor}$. High OUI means neurons split the batch in balanced ways; low OUI means neurons are biased (always on/off).
  1. Evidence integration from prior work:
  • Supervised learning: DenseNet-BC-100 on CIFAR-100 with 7 weight decay values — OUI trajectories separate by ~15% of training, anticipating which settings will generalize well (Fernández-Hernández et al., 2025)
  • Reinforcement learning: PPO actor-critic on CartPole-v1 — OUI discriminates learning-rate regimes early, with actor and critic showing distinct structural preferences (Fernández-Hernández et al., 2026)
  • Activation convergence: ViT-B16 on CIFAR-100 — activation patterns saturate much earlier than training/validation loss (Pérez-Corral et al., 2026)
  1. Conceptual reframing: The paper extracts a common pattern across these results and formalizes it as the activation-centric hypothesis.

Results & Highlights

  • OUI separates weight decay regimes by ~15% of training (DenseNet-BC-100, CIFAR-100), well before validation accuracy differences become clear. Final accuracies range from 5% (extreme underfitting) to 80% (well-regularized).
  • Actor and critic have different optimal OUI ranges in PPO: good critics occupy intermediate OUI bands while strong actors run at higher OUI values — demonstrating that structural adequacy is role-dependent, not absolute.
  • Activation patterns stabilize earlier than parameters in ViT-B16 training: the activation convergence metric saturates while training and validation losses are still improving, supporting the two-timescale hypothesis.
  • OUI is cheap and online-friendly: the batch-based formulation requires only binary activation masks and simple counting operations, making it feasible to monitor during large-scale training.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (Moderate): The individual OUI results are from prior work. The novelty here is the conceptual reframing — arguing for an activation-centric theory and synthesizing evidence across settings. This is a legitimate contribution but inherently limited by being a position/synthesis paper without new experiments.
  • Rigor (Moderate): The argument is logically coherent and internally consistent. However, the paper relies heavily on the authors' own prior work — no independent replication is cited. The empirical base is limited to small-scale settings (CIFAR-100, CartPole-v1); claims about foundation-model applicability are speculative.
  • Reproducibility (Good): OUI has a clear mathematical definition and is cheap to compute. The batch-based formulation is well-specified and should be straightforward to implement.
  • Significance (Potentially High): If activation-based observables can genuinely provide early structural signals at scale, the impact on training efficiency (especially for foundation models) could be substantial. However, this remains unproven at scale.
  • Red flags: (1) No new experiments — the paper is purely a synthesis of prior work by the same group. (2) The jump from CIFAR-100/CartPole to foundation-model claims is large and unsupported. (3) The two-timescale hypothesis, while intriguing, lacks formal grounding. (4) Self-citation density is very high — the argument rests entirely on the authors' own prior publications.

Strengths

  • Clear, well-written synthesis that makes a coherent theoretical argument
  • OUI is genuinely cheap, label-free, and practically useful as a diagnostic
  • The two-timescale hypothesis (structural phase → refinement phase) is intuitively appealing and connects to broader themes in training dynamics
  • Honest about limitations, including the feedback-loop confound risk when using OUI as a control signal
  • The role-dependent adequacy insight (actor vs. critic OUI preferences) is nuanced and avoids naive universal claims

Limitations

  • No new experiments: purely a synthesis/reinterpretation of prior work by the same authors
  • Small-scale empirical base: only CIFAR-100 and CartPole-v1 results; no validation on modern large-scale training
  • Self-contained citation loop: the argument depends entirely on the authors' own prior publications, with no external validation
  • ReLU-centric: OUI is defined in terms of binary activation masks, which naturally applies to ReLU/GELU/SiLU but may not generalize to other activation types
  • Lacks formal theory: the activation-centric view is presented as a hypothesis with empirical support but no formal mathematical framework connecting OUI to generalization, stability, or optimization theory
  • Feedback-loop risk acknowledged but unresolved: using OUI for online control creates a confound that is identified but not addressed
