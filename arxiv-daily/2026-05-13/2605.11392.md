> **一句话总结：** 本文从注意力与梯度双重视角深入研究Transformer可解释性，提出完整梯度校正和绝对梯度校正方案，首次实现同时分配正负注意力、细节解释，并利用ViT与人类感知差异实现了人眼几乎不可察觉的图像类别重写。

> **TL;DR:** This paper investigates Transformer interpretability through attention and gradient, proposing complete and absolute gradient correction schemes that enable simultaneous positive/negative attention allocation, detail-level interpretation of which features distinguish confusable classes, and a pixel-level image category rewriting attack that is nearly imperceptible to humans.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了先前方法（如GAE）仅采用正梯度校正的原因：完整梯度校正和绝对梯度校正在单类图像上表现不佳，但绝对梯度校正能揭示Encoder仅提取已学习类别特征而忽略背景的机制。
- 提出完整梯度校正方案，在多类图像解释中同时分配正负注意力，解释结果更全面且噪声更低。
- 提出细节解释（detail interpretation）方法，通过在目标特征区域内引导注意力方向，解释模型区分相似类别所依赖的具体特征。
- 首次通过细节解释发现TIS论文中bald eagle案例的真正原因是ImageNet训练集中风筝类别错误包含了大量白头鹰图像。
- 利用ViT逐patch感知与人类像素级感知的差异，通过梯度更新图像像素实现类别重写，人眼几乎无法察觉变化。

## Key Contributions

- **Explains why only positive gradient correction was adopted**: Demonstrates that complete/absolute gradient correction performs poorly on single-class images due to attention transfer dynamics, while absolute gradient correction reveals the Encoder mechanism — Transformer only extracts learned class features and ignores the background.
- **Complete gradient correction for multi-class images**: Achieves simultaneous positive and negative attention allocation, producing more accurate interpretations with less background noise compared to GAE.
- **Detail interpretation via internal attention guidance**: First method to interpret which specific features the model uses to distinguish between confusable classes (e.g., snail vs. conch vs. slug), using a comparative loss function (Equation 8).
- **Practical diagnostic application**: Uncovers that the bald eagle misclassification case from the TIS paper is caused by label noise in ImageNet's kite category containing many bald eagle images — an issue only detectable through detail interpretation.
- **Image category rewriting attack**: Exploits the patch-level perception gap between ViT and humans to rewrite image categories via gradient-based pixel updates that are nearly imperceptible to human eyes (e.g., changing top-1 from "tusker" at 35.1% to "bull mastiff" at 93.8%).

## Methodology

The paper builds on the Generic Attention-model Explainability (GAE) framework, which combines gradient information with multi-head attention maps. The authors propose two alternatives to GAE's positive-gradient-only approach:

1. **Complete gradient correction** (Equation 5): Uses the full gradient (both positive and negative) to correct attention maps — $\bar{A}^{(l)} = I + \frac{1}{H}\sum(\nabla A^{(l)}) \odot A^{(l)}$. This preserves maximum information and better approximates the model's actual computation.

2. **Absolute gradient correction** (Equation 6): Uses the absolute value of the gradient — $\bar{A}^{(l)} = I + \frac{1}{H}\sum |\nabla A^{(l)}| \odot A^{(l)}$. This eliminates the classifier's influence and reveals pure attention behavior of the Encoder.

3. **External attention guidance for single-class images**: Conjoins a guiding-category image (e.g., zebra) to the target image to transform it into a multi-class scenario, enabling complete gradient correction to work properly.

4. **Internal attention guidance for detail interpretation**: Uses a comparative loss $loss = (logit_{class1} - logit_{class2})/logit_{class1}$ to guide attention within the target feature region, revealing which sub-features favor one class over another.

5. **Pixel-level category rewriting**: Updates image pixels using gradients of a target-vs-opposing-category loss, exploiting ViT's patch-level perception to change the model's classification while preserving human visual appearance.

Experiments are conducted on ViT-Base and ViT-Large using ImageNet validation images, with perturbation-based quantitative evaluation (AUC on positive/negative perturbation curves) and qualitative visual comparisons against GAE, Attention Rollout, and other baselines.

## Results & Highlights

- **Multi-class interpretation**: Complete gradient correction simultaneously assigns positive attention to the target class region and negative attention to opposing class regions, whereas GAE incorrectly assigns positive attention to opposing classes (Figure 5, 13).
- **Perturbation experiments**: On negative perturbation tests, the proposed method clearly outperforms GAE because it correctly identifies opposing-class regions as the largest perturbations to the target class (Figure 12). Positive perturbation results are comparable.
- **Detail interpretation**: Successfully identifies which sub-features distinguish snail from conch and slug (Figure 9), and reveals that ViT misidentifies the bald eagle's white head as a kite feature due to training data label noise (Figure 9–10).
- **Attention transfer dynamics**: Visualized through controlled experiments where attention maps are optimized as the sole parameter — attention progressively shifts from the target class to the opposing class across epochs (Figure 6).
- **Image rewriting**: Changes image classification from "tusker" (35.1%, logit 11.15) to "bull mastiff" (93.8%, logit 12.93) with visually imperceptible pixel changes (Figure 11).

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are from **Zhejiang University**, a major Chinese research university. The paper is methodical in its investigation — starting from a concrete question ("why does everyone only use positive gradient?") and systematically exploring alternatives. Experiments are conducted on an NVIDIA A100 GPU using standard ViT-Base/Large models on ImageNet. The paper includes both qualitative visual evidence and quantitative perturbation experiments. However, the paper appears to be a relatively standalone exploration rather than part of a larger well-cited research program on this specific topic.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate. The idea of using complete/absolute gradients rather than positive-only gradients is conceptually simple, but the systematic investigation of *why* positive gradients were adopted and the discovery of attention transfer dynamics is novel. Detail interpretation is a genuinely new capability.
- **Rigor**: The paper provides clear mathematical formulations and well-controlled experiments comparing against GAE (the most architecturally similar baseline). However, the perturbation experiments only compare against GAE, and the qualitative comparisons exclude some methods (attention rollout, Grad-ECLIP) for stated but debatable reasons.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. The method uses standard ViT models on ImageNet with clearly specified equations. The code does not appear to be publicly available, but the mathematical framework is straightforward to implement.
- **Significance**: The detail interpretation capability and the discovery of ImageNet label noise in the kite category are practically significant. The image rewriting attack, while not the paper's focus, raises security concerns. However, the overall impact may be limited to the Transformer interpretability subfield.
- **Red flags**: The paper is primarily qualitative in its evaluation — quantitative experiments are limited to perturbation curves against only one baseline (GAE). Some claims about "the first time" simultaneous positive/negative attention allocation is achieved are reasonable but hard to verify comprehensively. The image rewriting experiment is acknowledged as an extension rather than a core contribution, which is appropriate but leaves the security implications underexplored.

## Strengths

- Systematic investigation starting from a well-motivated research question about gradient direction choices in interpretability methods.
- The concept of attention transfer direction and its connection to gradient descent direction is insightful and well-demonstrated.
- Detail interpretation is a genuinely new and useful capability with practical diagnostic value (e.g., uncovering ImageNet label noise).
- Clear mathematical framework with three correction schemes (positive, complete, absolute) and well-defined loss functions.
- Honest about limitations — explicitly states the image rewriting experiment is not the focus and that perturbation experiments are included partly by convention.

## Limitations

- Complete gradient correction fails on single-class images without external attention guidance, requiring an auxiliary guiding image — a significant practical constraint.
- Quantitative evaluation is limited to perturbation experiments against only GAE; no comparison with more recent interpretability methods (DIX, BI, TAM) in the quantitative setting.
- The method operates at the patch level, not the pixel level, inheriting the resolution limitation of attention-based Transformer interpretation.
- The image rewriting attack is underexplored — no systematic evaluation of attack success rate, robustness to defenses, or comparison with existing adversarial attack methods.
- The paper does not evaluate on NLP or multimodal Transformers despite claiming general applicability to the Transformer architecture.

## Relevance

本文从注意力与梯度方向研究Transformer的可解释性机制，提出的梯度校正方案和细节解释能力与"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"研究兴趣直接相关——理解注意力如何编码和传递信息是机械可解释性的核心问题。图像类别重写攻击也与ML系统的鲁棒性和可靠性分析有关联。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the complete gradient correction and detail interpretation methods be extended to multi-head attention in large language models (LLMs), where the relationship between attention heads and specific linguistic phenomena is of great interest?
- How does the attention transfer dynamics observed in ViT generalize to decoder-only Transformer architectures used in modern LLMs?
- Could detail interpretation be used as a diagnostic tool during LLM training to detect when the model has learned spurious correlations or absorbed label noise from training data?
- What defenses could mitigate the patch-level image category rewriting attack, and does an analogous attack vector exist for text or multimodal models?
- How does the absolute gradient correction's property of revealing "pure Encoder attention" relate to studies of representational geometry in intermediate Transformer layers?
