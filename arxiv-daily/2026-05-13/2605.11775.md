> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"熵极性"(Entropy Polarity)理论框架，通过对RLVR中策略熵变化进行一阶Taylor展开，定义了token级有符号量P = A·T来预测每次采样更新是扩张还是收缩策略熵，揭示了高频高概率token强化导致熵收缩的结构性不对称性，并据此设计PAPO算法——通过基于经验熵轨迹的自适应优势重加权在扩张/收缩两个极性分支间动态分配优化压力，在Qwen2.5-7B/14B的数学推理（AIME24 +3.3%）和智能体工具调用基准上一致优于DAPO等基线。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces a token-level theoretical framework for entropy mechanics in RLVR. By deriving a first-order approximation of entropy change ΔH_t, they define entropy polarity P = A·T — a signed, per-token quantity predicting whether a policy-gradient update expands or contracts local entropy. This reveals a structural asymmetry: reinforcing high-probability tokens naturally contracts entropy while expansion requires low-probability samples. Building on this, they propose PAPO (Polarity-Aware Policy Optimization), which adaptively reweights advantages between entropy-expanding and entropy-contracting branches using the empirical entropy trajectory as an online phase signal, consistently outperforming DAPO, entropy regularization, and the 80/20 rule on math reasoning and agentic benchmarks.

## 核心贡献

- **熵极性理论框架**：首次对RLVR中策略熵变化的token级机制进行了严格的理论建模。通过Proposition 1（策略梯度下的logit更新公式）和Theorem 1（一阶熵变化分解），将ΔH_t分解为采样token项t₁和状态级分布校正项t₂的线性组合，定义了内禀熵趋势T(s_t, y_t) = -t₁ + t₂和熵极性P = A·T，可精确预测每次更新对局部熵的影响方向和幅度。
- **结构性不对称发现**：揭示了熵动力学中的关键不对称性——分布校正项t₂始终非负（等价于概率与对数概率的协方差），而采样token项-t₁的符号取决于token概率是否超过阈值exp(-H_t)。由于策略采样高频遇到高概率token，强化这些token倾向于收缩分布，使"熵坍缩"比"熵恢复"更容易发生。这是一个结构性（非超参数依赖）的不对称。
- **正负极性分支的互补角色**：通过单极性消融实验证明，仅保留正极性（熵扩张）更新会保持高熵但奖励增长缓慢，仅保留负极性（熵收缩）更新则在早期获得奖励但随后导致熵坍缩和探索能力丧失。正极性分支保留探索多样性，负极性分支强化奖励对齐的利用。
- **PAPO算法**：基于熵极性洞察，提出通过优势重加权实现分支级控制的算法。对正极性token施加权重ω_pos，负极性token施加权重ω_neg，利用平滑熵斜率s_k作为在线相位信号，通过二次映射和倒数耦合自适应调整权重。当熵快速下降时保护探索（抑制收缩分支），当熵稳定时恢复利用。设有warmup阶段和低熵门控机制。
- **全面的实验验证**：在Qwen2.5-7B和14B上的数学推理（6个benchmark）和智能体工具调用（BFCL-v3, ACEBench）实验中一致优于DAPO、Entropy Regularization和80/20 rule。PAPO仅需DAPO一半的训练预算即可达到DAPO的成熟奖励水平。

## Key Contributions

- Develop a **token-level theoretical framework for entropy mechanics in RLVR**. Proposition 1 derives the logit update under policy gradient: z' = z + ηA(1[p_t] - p_v), and Theorem 1 shows the first-order entropy change factorizes as ΔH_t = ηA·T(s_t, y_t) + O(η²), where T = -t₁ + t₂ combines a sampled-token effect t₁ = p_t(H_t + log p_t) and a state-wise distributional correction t₂ = Σ p²_v(H_t + log p_v).
- Define **entropy polarity** P(s_t, y_t, A) = A·T(s_t, y_t) as a signed, computable per-token quantity that predicts the direction and magnitude of entropy change under actual RL updates. Empirically validate that normalized P closely tracks measured entropy change.
- Reveal a **structural asymmetry** in entropy tendency: the distributional correction t₂ is always non-negative (equivalent to Cov(p_v, log p_v) ≥ 0), while -t₁ favors contraction for high-probability tokens. Since policy sampling encounters high-probability tokens more often, contraction is structurally easier to trigger than expansion.
- Demonstrate via **single-polarity ablation** that positive-polarity updates preserve exploration (enriched in connective/structural tokens like "and", "that", "step") while negative-polarity updates strengthen exploitation (enriched in formulaic reasoning tokens like "find", "values", "product"). Neither branch alone supports stable RL fine-tuning.
- Propose **PAPO (Polarity-Aware Policy Optimization)**, which modulates token advantages by polarity sign: Ã_t = A·ω_pos if P > 0, A·ω_neg if P < 0. The weights are adaptively controlled using the smoothed entropy slope s_k against a warmup reference rate s_ref, with quadratic mapping ω_neg = ω_min + (ω_max - ω_min)p²_k and reciprocal coupling ω_pos = 1/ω_neg.
- Achieve **consistent improvements** across 7B and 14B scales on mathematical reasoning (e.g., +3.3% AIME24, +2.1% AIME25 on 14B; +2.4% Minerva on 7B) and agentic tool-calling (+1.0% BFCL-v3 average), with **superior training efficiency** (reaching DAPO's mature reward at half the training budget).

## Methodology

The paper develops its approach in three stages:

**Stage 1: Theoretical Analysis (Entropy Mechanics)**

The authors analyze how policy-gradient updates reshape the next-token entropy in the GRPO framework. Given a decoding state s_t with softmax policy π_θ(·|s_t), a single gradient-ascent step on A·log π_θ(y_t|s_t) yields the logit update (Proposition 1): the sampled token's logit increases by ηA(1-p_t) while all logits shift by -ηAp_v. Applying a first-order Taylor expansion of entropy H_t around the pre-update logits and substituting the logit perturbation yields Theorem 1:

ΔH_t = -ηA·t₁(s_t, y_t) + ηA·t₂(s_t) + O(η²)

where t₁ = p_t(H_t + log p_t) captures the sampled-token effect and t₂ = Σ_v p²_v(H_t + log p_v) is a state-wise distributional correction. The **intrinsic entropy tendency** T = -t₁ + t₂ measures the structural entropy effect before the sign of A is applied. The **entropy polarity** P = A·T then captures the realized first-order entropy change direction.

**Stage 2: Empirical Validation of Polarity**

The authors verify that: (1) normalized P correlates closely with measured entropy change during training; (2) the two components A·t₁ and A·t₂ contribute materially at comparable empirical scale despite different pointwise forms; (3) the polarity magnitude distribution is heavy-tailed (P99 = 0.607, P99.9 = 1.33), with a small fraction of high-magnitude updates accounting for most cumulative entropy effect; (4) positive-polarity tokens have higher context entropy and diversity, enriched in connective/structural expressions, while negative-polarity tokens concentrate in formulaic reasoning fragments.

**Stage 3: PAPO Algorithm**

PAPO implements branch-level entropy control through:
1. **Polarity-weighted advantages** (Eq. 9): Ã_t = A·ω_pos if P ≥ 0, A·ω_neg if P < 0, reducing to GRPO when ω_pos = ω_neg = 1.
2. **Adaptive entropy modulation**: After a warmup phase (N_w = 20 steps with neutral weights), the smoothed entropy slope s_k = EMA_β(ΔH) is compared against the warmup reference rate s_ref. A progress metric p_k = clip((s_k - s_ref)/(-s_ref + ε), 0, 1) measures entropy decay recovery.
3. **Quadratic weight mapping** (Eq. 11): ω_neg = ω_min + (ω_max - ω_min)·p²_k, ω_pos = 1/ω_neg. When p_k ≈ 0 (entropy collapsing), contraction is suppressed and expansion amplified; as p_k increases, weights relax toward neutral.
4. **Gate mechanism**: Reweighting permanently deactivates when smoothed entropy drops below γ_gate · h_ref (γ_gate = 0.3), preventing interference in extreme low-entropy regimes.

**Experimental Setup:**
- Math reasoning: Qwen2.5-7B/14B-Base on DAPO-Math-17K, 6 epochs, lr=1e-6, 16×H20 GPUs, group size 8, max 8192 tokens. Evaluated on OlympiadBench, AMC, AIME24/25, MATH500, Minerva (mean@8).
- Agentic: Qwen2.5-14B-Instruct on Tool-N1, 7 epochs, evaluated on BFCL-v3 and ACEBench.
- Baselines: DAPO (clip-higher + dynamic sampling), Entropy Regularization (uniform entropy bonus), 80/20 rule (top-20% high-entropy tokens only).

## Results & Highlights

- **Math reasoning (7B)**: PAPO achieves 34.70% on OlympiadBench (+1.58% vs DAPO), 51.56% on AMC (+2.18%), 14.58% on AIME24 (+0.83%), 8.30% on AIME25 (+0.80%), 65.90% on MATH500 (+0.33%), and 48.25% on Minerva (+1.87%).
- **Math reasoning (14B)**: PAPO achieves 39.22% on OlympiadBench (+0.65%), 62.50% on AMC (+1.87%), 15.83% on AIME24 (+3.33%), 13.33% on AIME25 (+2.08%), 71.38% on MATH500 (+0.98%). The AIME improvements are particularly notable as these are the hardest benchmarks.
- **Out-of-domain transfer**: Despite training only on math, PAPO achieves +1.9% on CRUX (code reasoning) for 14B and +1.3% on IFEval for 7B, with no degradation on MMLU-Pro.
- **Agentic tool-calling**: PAPO achieves strongest overall performance on both BFCL-v3 (+1.0% average) and ACEBench, demonstrating that polarity-aware reweighting transfers beyond math to structured action generation.
- **Training efficiency**: PAPO reaches DAPO's mature reward level with only half the training budget on 14B, and continues improving throughout training without entropy collapse.
- **Entropy regulation**: PAPO produces a non-monotonic entropy trajectory with transient mid-training contraction followed by stabilization at a higher reward-entropy operating point than DAPO.
- **Ablation insights**: Explore-biased settings preserve entropy but slow reward; exploit-biased settings accelerate early reward but cause premature entropy collapse. The moderate default setting achieves the best reward-entropy trade-off. Polarity control strength [ω_min, ω_max] = [0.98, 1.02] for 14B, [0.98, 1.03] for 7B.
- **Position analysis**: Entropy-shaping tokens are distributed uniformly across sequence positions (flat mean |P|), justifying position-agnostic reweighting. Signed polarity transitions from net contraction in early positions to net expansion in later positions.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The token-level decomposition of entropy change into polarity P = A·T is a genuinely new theoretical contribution. While entropy regularization is well-studied, the signed per-token analysis and the intrinsic asymmetry finding (t₂ = Cov(p, log p) ≥ 0) provide fresh mechanistic insight. The connection from theory to a practical algorithm is clean.
- **Rigor**: Strong. Proposition 1 and Theorem 1 have complete proofs in the appendix. The empirical validation is thorough — polarity correlation with measured ΔH, single-polarity ablation, positional analysis, and strength ablation all support the theoretical claims. The experimental setup follows standard practices with fair baseline comparisons.
- **Reproducibility**: Moderate-to-high. Algorithm 1 provides pseudocode; hyperparameters are fully specified (ω bounds, EMA coefficients, warmup steps, gate threshold). Built on VeRL framework. However, code release is not mentioned, and computing per-token polarity requires access to the full softmax distribution which may have computational implications.
- **Significance**: Significant for the RLVR community. Understanding entropy dynamics at the token level is practically important — PAPO achieves consistent improvements with minimal hyperparameter overhead and good training efficiency. The theoretical framework may inspire further research into fine-grained entropy control.
- **Red flags**: (1) The polarity weight range [0.98, 1.03] is surprisingly narrow — the 2-3% reweighting seems small, raising questions about whether the improvements come from the polarity signal or from the slight regularization effect. (2) Only tested on Qwen2.5 models (7B/14B) — generalization to other architectures (Llama, Mistral) and larger scales (70B+) is unverified. (3) The paper acknowledges limitation to standard RLVR with verifiable rewards; applicability to RLHF or other reward formulations is unknown. (4) The 80/20 baseline performs surprisingly well on some benchmarks (e.g., MATH500 66.90% on 7B vs PAPO 65.90%), suggesting that simpler token selection may sometimes match polarity-aware reweighting.

## Strengths

- Principled theoretical framework with formal propositions and theorems, not just heuristics
- Clean decomposition of entropy change into interpretable components (sampled-token vs distributional correction)
- The structural asymmetry insight is elegant and explains why entropy collapse is easier to trigger than to reverse
- PAPO adds negligible computational overhead — polarity is computed from already-available softmax probabilities
- Comprehensive experiments spanning two model scales, multiple math benchmarks, OOD transfer, and agentic scenarios
- Superior training efficiency: half the budget to reach DAPO's performance level
- Thorough ablation studies validating each design choice

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on Qwen2.5-7B and 14B; scalability to 70B+ models and other architectures is untested
- The polarity weight range is very narrow (2-3%), and the paper does not analyze sensitivity extensively beyond one ablation
- Does not address long-horizon regimes with hundreds of sequential reasoning steps
- Limited to verifiable reward formulations; unclear how polarity applies to RLHF with learned reward models
- The 80/20 baseline sometimes matches PAPO on simpler benchmarks, questioning the added complexity
- No analysis of computational cost for computing per-token polarity across the full vocabulary
- Agentic experiments use a different model (14B-Instruct) and setup, making cross-setting comparison indirect

## 相关性

该论文从token级别理论分析了LLM强化学习微调中策略熵的扩张/收缩机制，提出了可计算的"熵极性"信号用于在线训练监控，与LLM训练稳定性和可观测性研究方向高度相关——理解并控制熵坍缩是防止RLVR训练中探索能力丧失和奖励停滞的关键。其自适应分支控制机制也可视为一种训练异常检测与响应策略。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the first-order approximation break down for large learning rates or sharp policy changes, and are there regimes where the O(η²) terms dominate?
- Can entropy polarity P be used as a real-time diagnostic signal for detecting training instability or loss spikes in large-scale distributed RLVR runs?
- How does PAPO interact with learned reward models (RLHF) where the advantage signal is noisier and potentially misaligned with the true objective?
- What is the wall-clock computational overhead of computing per-token polarity across the full vocabulary during large-scale training on 70B+ models?
- Does the structural asymmetry finding generalize to mixture-of-experts (MoE) models where the routing mechanism may alter the entropy dynamics?
- Can the polarity framework be extended to multi-turn RL settings where entropy dynamics may differ across conversation turns?
- How sensitive is PAPO to the warmup phase length N_w and the gate threshold γ_gate across different training configurations and model scales?
