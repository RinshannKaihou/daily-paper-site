Error Whitening: Why Gauss-Newton Outperforms Newton

arXiv: 2605.11316

Date: 2026-05-13

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11316

一句话总结： 本文从函数空间视角提出"误差白化"机制，证明Gauss-Newton通过将参数化矩阵 $JJ^\top$ 替换为单位矩阵来消除模型参数化对优化动态的扭曲，在回归、PINN和强化学习等多个任务上比Newton法、Adam和Muon提升数个数量级。
TL;DR: This paper introduces "error whitening" — the mechanism by which Gauss-Newton descent removes parameterization-induced distortions by replacing $JJ^\top$ with the identity in function space. With closed-form mismatch ODEs and extensive case studies (regression, PINNs, RL), the authors show Gauss-Newton optimizers follow theoretically predicted dynamics and outperform Newton, Adam, and Muon by orders of magnitude.

核心贡献

  • 提出"误差白化"(error whitening)概念：Gauss-Newton方法将梯度流中参数化依赖的 $JJ^\top$ 替换为单位矩阵，消除了模型参数化对预测-目标失配演化动态的扭曲
  • 证明广义Gauss-Newton (GGN)矩阵将函数空间中的Newton方向投影到模型切空间上（$H_\ell$加权范数下），而Jacobian-only变体 $G_J$ 将函数空间损失梯度投影到同一空间（欧几里得范数下）
  • 推导出多种损失函数（q-power、cross-entropy）下失配ODE的闭式解，精确预测了经验收敛行为
  • 引入sketch sufficiency rank gating技术，防止sketch-based二阶方法的秩无限增长
  • 在监督学习、物理信息神经网络（Allen-Cahn方程）、近似动态规划三大场景中验证理论，展示Gauss-Newton的广泛优势

Key Contributions

  • Error whitening mechanism: Formally defines error whitening as replacing $JJ^\top$ in the mismatch gradient flow with an operator that cancels $J$'s singular values, removing parameterization distortion from optimization dynamics
  • Function space projections: Proves GGN projects the Newton direction in function space onto the model's tangent space (in $H_\ell$-weighted norm), while $G_J$ projects the loss gradient onto the tangent space (in Euclidean norm) — Proposition 2.1 and Corollary 2.2
  • Closed-form mismatch ODEs: Derives analytical solutions for mismatch dynamics under squared loss ($r(\tau) = e^{-\tau}r(0)$), q-power losses, and cross-entropy, with GGN yielding uniform exponential decay and $G_J$ yielding algebraic or Fisher-governed decay
  • Sketch sufficiency gating: Introduces a rank-adaptation mechanism based on sketch sufficiency $S_k = \delta_k^\top M \delta_k / \delta_^\top M \delta_$ to prevent unbounded rank growth in sketch-based second-order updates
  • Broad empirical validation: Case studies spanning regression (12,951 params), Allen-Cahn PINN (3,021 params), MNIST (118,282 params), double integrator RL, and inverted pendulum RL

Methodology

The analysis rests on a continuum (ODE) viewpoint of optimization, taking step size $\eta \to 0$ to study gradient flow trajectories. The core analytical pipeline:

  1. Function space dynamics: Starting from $d\theta/d\tau = -G^\dagger \nabla_\theta L(\theta)$, the authors propagate through the Jacobian $J = [\nabla_\theta f_\theta(x_1), \ldots, \nabla_\theta f_\theta(x_d)]^\top$ to obtain $df_\theta/d\tau = -H_\ell^{-1/2} A A^\dagger H_\ell^{-1/2} \nabla_{f_\theta} \ell(f_\theta)$, where $A = H_\ell^{1/2} J$ and $AA^\dagger$ projects onto the model's tangent space.
  1. Mismatch dynamics: Defining the mismatch $r = \psi(f_\theta, y)$, the chain rule gives $dr/d\tau = \nabla_{f_\theta} \psi \cdot df_\theta/d\tau$. For gradient descent, this yields $dr/d\tau = -\nabla_{f_\theta} \psi \, JJ^\top \nabla_{f_\theta} \psi^\top \nabla_r \hat\ell(r)$, where $JJ^\top$ encodes parameterization distortion. Error whitening replaces $JJ^\top$ with the identity (for $G_J$) or $H_\ell^{-1}$ (for GGN).
  1. Loss-specific analysis:
  • Squared/q-power loss: $\nabla_{f_\theta}\psi = I$, so GGN yields $dr/d\tau = -r$ (exponential decay) while $G_J$ yields $dr/d\tau = -r^{q-1}$ (algebraic decay for $q > 2$)
  • Cross-entropy: $\nabla_{f_\theta}\psi = F$ (Fisher information matrix), so GGN still gives exponential decay but $G_J$ gives coupled nonlinear dynamics $dr/d\tau = -Fr$
  1. Scalability via sketching: Uses the randomized sketching method from prior work [7] to approximate eigenvalue decompositions of $G$, $G_J$, and $H$ at each step, with a grid line-search for step size selection and sufficiency gating for rank control.
  1. Experimental design: Five optimizers (G, $G_J$, H, Adam, Muon) tested on each task with identical architectures and hyperparameters except optimizer-specific settings. Sketch-based methods use ~18% rank on regression, ~4.2% on MNIST. Adam and Muon given 5-7x more steps for fair comparison.

Results & Highlights

Supervised regression (quartic loss, $g(x,y) = \sin(2\pi x)\sin(2\pi y) + \sin(7\pi x)\sin(7\pi y)$):

  • G (GGN): MSE $1.7 \pm 0.6 \times 10^{-9}$ — orders of magnitude below all others
  • $G_J$: $1.5 \times 10^{-4}$ (aligns with $\nabla_{f_\theta}\ell$ rather than mismatch; quartic gradient amplifies large errors)
  • Newton (H): $1.6 \times 10^{-4}$; Muon: $2.9 \times 10^{-5}$; Adam: $9.9 \times 10^{-4}$
  • For log-cosh loss, G and $G_J$ perform nearly identically ($\sim 1.4 \times 10^{-9}$), confirming theoretical prediction

MNIST classification (cross-entropy):

  • $G_J$: 98.1% accuracy (best); Adam: 97.7%; Muon: 97.4%; Newton: 92.4%; G (GGN): 84.9%
  • G collapses due to pathological eigenvalue structure of $H_\ell$ (Fisher matrix eigenvalues → 0 near convergence)
  • $G_J$'s cosine similarity with mismatch $p - y$ builds to 1.0 and stays there throughout training

Allen-Cahn PINN ($\nu = 10^{-4}$):

  • Newton fails to train entirely; G achieves relative $\ell_2$ error $5.1 \pm 1.3 \times 10^{-9}$
  • Adam: $9.1 \times 10^{-2}$ error (4 orders of magnitude worse)
  • Results competitive with elaborate PINN techniques (hard BCs, Fourier features, causal training) using only a small feedforward network + R3 resampling

Reinforcement learning (double integrator, fitted value iteration):

  • Only G captures the correct S-shape of $V^*$; Adam and Muon fail to learn basic structure; Newton captures only a coarse profile
  • Inverted pendulum (SOAP ≈ approximate G): 10x data efficiency over AdamW, achieving consistent success with 5e4 data points vs AdamW needing 5e5+

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The "error whitening" framing provides the first rigorous function-space explanation for why Gauss-Newton outperforms Newton, moving beyond the standard "positive semidefinite approximation" narrative. The mismatch ODE analysis with closed-form solutions is elegant and novel.
  • Rigor: Strong theoretical foundation with formal propositions, corollaries, and proofs. The ODE analysis is mathematically sound. Empirical protocol is carefully designed (same architectures, multiple seeds, fairness in step counts).
  • Reproducibility: Good — open-source code, detailed hyperparameter tables, multiple random seeds with std reported. All experiments run on consumer hardware (M3/M4 Mac) or single A100 GPU.
  • Significance: Potentially high impact for understanding optimization in deep learning. The function space perspective could influence how practitioners think about optimizer selection, especially for PINNs and RL where training instability is a major bottleneck.
  • Red flags:
  • All experiments use small to moderate models (3K–118K parameters). No evidence that error whitening benefits transfer to large-scale models (e.g., transformers, LLMs).
  • The sketching approach with ~4-18% rank may be insufficient for larger, more complex models.
  • Newton's poor performance on MNIST is partially attributable to eigenvalue clipping in the sketching (making it PSD), which is a design choice rather than an inherent limitation of Newton.
  • The Adam/Meon comparisons use fixed learning rate schedules; unclear whether a more thorough hyperparameter sweep would close the gap.
  • The paper acknowledges its analysis describes only instantaneous (infinitesimal-step) dynamics, limiting the theoretical scope.

Strengths

  • Provides the first rigorous, function-space explanation for Gauss-Newton's empirical superiority over Newton
  • Elegant mathematical framework connecting tangent-space projections to mismatch dynamics
  • Closed-form ODE solutions that accurately predict empirical convergence behavior
  • Comprehensive case studies across three distinct domains (supervised, PINNs, RL)
  • Careful experimental design with fair comparisons and multiple seeds
  • The $G_J$ variant is a practical contribution — a loss-curvature-independent Gauss-Newton matrix applicable to any loss

Limitations

  • Scale: All experiments involve small networks (≤118K parameters). Scalability to modern architectures (transformers, CNNs at scale) is untested and the sketching approach may require impractically large ranks.
  • Instantaneous dynamics only: The ODE analysis describes infinitesimal steps; discrete-step behavior and momentum effects are not captured theoretically.
  • MNIST GGN collapse: The GGN matrix ($G$) fails catastrophically on classification due to Fisher matrix ill-conditioning, which limits its generality. $G_J$ works but its advantage over Adam is modest (98.1% vs 97.7%).
  • Compute cost: Sketch-based second-order methods require eigenvalue decompositions at each step. Even with sketching, per-step cost is significantly higher than Adam/Muon.
  • No stochastic/minibatch analysis: The ODE framework assumes full-batch; the interaction between error whitening and stochasticity is unexplored.

Relevance

本文从函数空间视角严格分析了Gauss-Newton与Newton优化方法的本质差异，揭示了参数化对优化动态的扭曲机制。这与"ML可靠性与数学基础"研究方向高度相关，其ODE框架和分析方法对理解训练稳定性和数值行为具有重要理论价值，对PINN和RL中的训练不稳定性问题提供了新的优化视角。
