Optimistic Dual Averaging Unifies Modern Optimizers

Thomas Pethick, Wanyun Xie, Roman Machacek, Volkan Cevher

arXiv: 2605.11172

Date: 2026-05-13

Key Contributions

  • Unified optimizer perspective: Shows that Muon (spectral mirror map + Nesterov momentum), Lion (sign-based), NAdam (adaptive), and Scion (constrained Frank-Wolfe) all arise as special cases of Optimistic Dual Averaging through different choices of mirror maps (geometry), optimism parameters, and primal averaging
  • Weight decay as primal averaging: Reframes weight decay not as regularization but as iterate averaging, where the 1/(k+2) schedule corresponds to uniform averaging of iterates — yielding both theoretical acceleration and practical improvement
  • SODA Wrapper (Algorithm 1): A practical wrapper around any base optimizer that introduces anchored centering (z₀ = initialization) and scheduled 1/(k+2) weight decay with no new hyperparameters; the update simplifies to x_{k+1} = (1/(k+2))z₀ + (1 - 1/(k+2))x_k + BaseUpdate
  • Convergence guarantees: Provides O(n^{-1/2}) rates under L-smoothness and an accelerated O(n^{-1}) variant under stronger parameterization, using an online regret argument with gradient-variation bounds
  • SODA† configuration: Replaces Adam (used in Muon for first/last layers) with Lion and uses uniform momentum hyperparameters across all layers, simplifying tuning while matching or exceeding Muon performance

Methodology

SODA decomposes optimization into three orthogonal components: (i) dual averaging with optimism — maintaining dual momentum m_k with optimistic hint m̄_{k+1} via parameter ᾱ_k, recovering Nesterov momentum when ᾱ_k = α_k; (ii) primal averaging — mixing current iterate x_k with dual iterate z_k via parameter λ_k, where λ_k = 1/(k+2) gives the weight decay effect; (iii) dualization through geometry — choosing mirror map h_k whose Fenchel conjugate determines the update direction, with spectral geometry giving Muon, ℓ∞ geometry giving Lion, and diagonal adaptive geometry giving NAdam.

The SODA Wrapper anchors the dual iterate to the initialization z₀ rather than the origin, which matters when the solution is far from zero. The centering term z₀/(k+2) decays naturally, and the weight decay (1 - 1/(k+2))x_k is decoupled from the stepsize schedule — unlike standard decoupled weight decay. Crucially, z₀ can be reconstructed from a fixed seed at each iteration, avoiding extra memory. The wrapper applies after warmup for Adam-based optimizers and wraps only hidden layers by default.

The analysis builds on online convex optimization: ODA (Optimistic Dual Averaging) regret bounds are converted to stochastic optimization guarantees via a smooth online-to-batch lemma that exploits the gradient-variation assumption (Assumption 4.4) to cancel noise terms when the stepsize η is small enough relative to L and the strong convexity μ of the regularizer.

Results & Highlights

  • Outperforms tuned Muon: SODA(Muon) beats Muon even when Muon has its best-tuned weight decay, on both 1× and 4× Chinchilla training of 124M models (Fig. 1)
  • Consistent improvement across base optimizers: Wrapping Adam, Scion, and Muon with SODA all yield consistent gains without any additional tuning (Fig. 2)
  • SODA† scales well: From 64M to 1B parameters under Chinchilla-style scaling, SODA† consistently outperforms Scion/uScion, with the gap growing at larger scales (Fig. 4)
  • Horizon transfer: The optimal weight decay for Muon decreases with training horizon, making fixed schedules suboptimal; SODA's 1/(k+2) schedule handles this automatically (Fig. 1)
  • Ablation confirms centering: Using z₀ = 0 (origin) instead of z₀ = x₀ (initialization) significantly degrades performance, validating the theoretical motivation (Fig. 6)
  • Optimism helps in overtrained regime: SODA with optimism (ᾱ_k = α_k = 0.05) achieves the best results, with SODA† reaching 3.1238 validation loss at 20k steps vs. 3.1262 for SODA(uScion)

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (High): The unification of Muon, Lion, NAdam, and AdEMAMix under a single framework is genuinely insightful and clarifies the landscape of modern optimizers. The reinterpretation of weight decay as primal averaging is a meaningful conceptual contribution
  • Rigor (High): Formal convergence proofs under standard convex + L-smooth assumptions, with both basic and accelerated rates. The gradient-variation assumption (Assumption 4.4) is a reasonable relaxation of bounded variance
  • Reproducibility (Medium-High): Experiments use nanoGPT on FineWeb100 with clear hyperparameter tables. However, the paper does not provide code, and some implementation details (e.g., warmup interaction with SODA wrapper for Adam) could be more explicit
  • Significance (High): Practical impact is strong — eliminating weight decay tuning is valuable, and consistent improvements across multiple base optimizers and scales suggests genuine utility for LLM training
  • Red flags: (1) Convex analysis only — the theory does not cover non-convex deep learning losses, though the authors acknowledge this limitation. (2) Experiments are limited to nanoGPT (decoder-only Transformer) on FineWeb100; generalization to other architectures and datasets is untested. (3) The 1B scale is still relatively small by modern standards. (4) The SODA wrapper adds slight computational overhead for storing/reconstructing z₀

Strengths

  • Elegant unification that makes the relationships between modern optimizers transparent and predictable
  • Parameter-free — no weight decay tuning required, which is a major practical advantage
  • Strong empirical results that are consistent across base optimizers, training horizons, and model scales
  • Clean theoretical story connecting the 1/(k+2) schedule to iterate averaging and acceleration
  • The paper is well-written with clear notation and helpful tables comparing optimizer components

Limitations

  • Convex-only theory; no guarantees for non-convex deep learning objectives
  • Experiments limited to a single architecture (GPT-2 style) and dataset (FineWeb100)
  • The wrapper requires storing the initialization z₀ or reconstructing it per-step, adding either memory or compute overhead
  • No evaluation on downstream tasks (only validation loss reported)
  • The improvement margin over well-tuned baselines, while consistent, is modest in absolute terms (e.g., ~0.01-0.02 validation loss)

Relevance

本文将权重衰减重新解释为迭代平均，并提出了免调参的SODA包装器，与LLM训练稳定性和优化器设计直接相关。其理论框架为理解现代优化器的行为提供了新的数学基础，对分布式训练中的优化器选择和超参数迁移也有实际意义。
