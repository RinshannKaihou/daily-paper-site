> **一句话总结：** 本文从仿射对称性原理出发，严格证明线性探针只是坐标稳定多项式探针族的一阶成员，提出基于商空间的跨模型探针迁移方法，并在安全监控、主谓一致等任务上验证了二阶探针和零标签迁移的有效性。

> **TL;DR:** The paper proves that linear probes are the degree-1 member of a unique polynomial hierarchy of affine-invariant shallow probes, introduces probe-visible quotients as the canonical object for cross-model transfer, and demonstrates zero-label safety monitor portability across model families (Qwen→Mistral) with built-in coverage diagnostics.

## 核心贡献

- **仿射不变性探针分类定理**：证明在有限维+仿射不变性约束下，唯一的非零标量探针空间是多项式空间 P≤ℓ(ℝⁿ)，线性探针只是ℓ=1的特例（Theorem 2.2）。
- **低秩CP探针**：证明Canonical Polyadic (CP)秩在GL(n)下保持稳定，而单项式稀疏性不具备此性质，因此CP结构是高阶探针的自然参数化方式（Proposition 2.5）。
- **探针可见商空间**：提出跨模型探针迁移的自然对象不是完整隐藏状态，而是商空间 Z(V) = H/K(V)——即模去除探针族不可见方向后的表示（Theorem 3.3）。
- **覆盖感知迁移**：商空间迁移在概念超出探针库覆盖范围时会优雅降级，提供内建的诊断信号，而全状态OLS迁移会"静默"迁移未覆盖的概念。
- **实验验证**：在合成任务、Pythia系列模型的主谓一致检测、以及Qwen→Mistral零标签安全监控迁移上全面验证理论预测。

## Key Contributions

- **Classification theorem for affine-invariant probe spaces** (Theorem 2.2): The only nonzero finite-dimensional affine-invariant scalar probe spaces are bounded-degree polynomial spaces P≤ℓ(ℝⁿ). Linear probes are not an isolated engineering default but emerge as the degree-1 member of this unique hierarchy.
- **CP-rank structured polynomial probes** (Proposition 2.5): Low-rank Canonical Polyadic decomposition is stable under GL(n) reparameterization, unlike monomial sparsity. This makes CP the natural structured parameterization for degree-2+ probes.
- **Probe-visible quotient for transfer** (Theorem 3.3): The canonical object for cross-model probe transfer is the quotient Z(V) = H/K(V), not the full hidden state. For behaviorally equivalent models, probe-visible quotients are canonically isomorphic to a shared abstract concept space C*.
- **Coverage-aware monitor portability**: Quotient transfer provides built-in coverage diagnostics via in-span fraction (ISF). Unlike full-state OLS which silently transfers uncovered concepts, quotient transfer degrades gracefully when concepts leave the probe bank's span.
- **Zero-label cross-architecture transfer**: Demonstrates safety monitor transfer from Qwen-7B to Mistral-7B with no target labels, and portable behavioral intervention via reranking that reduces harmful compliance by 15–20pp.

## Methodology

**Theoretical framework.** The paper starts from the observation that equivalent neural network realizations of the same computation are related by affine reparameterizations of hidden coordinates (established rigorously for bias-free and softmax readout heads via Theorem B.1, Proposition B.5, Corollary B.7). Imposing two constraints on probe families — (1) affine invariance (stability under reparameterization) and (2) finite-dimensionality (shallowness) — yields Theorem 2.2: the only nonzero admissible scalar probe spaces are polynomial spaces of bounded degree.

**Structured probes.** For degree-2 probes, the full quadratic has O(n²) parameters and overfits. The paper proposes rank-R CP probes as a structured alternative: p(x) = Σᵣ αᵣ Πⱼ⟨vᵣⱼ, x⟩, which are closed under GL(n) (Proposition 2.5) and far more parameter-efficient. The affine-completed parameterization adds bias terms for full Aff(n) stability.

**Quotient transfer.** Given a probe bank with weight matrix W ∈ ℝᵏˣᵈ, the probe-invisible subspace is ker(W). The quotient projection is obtained via SVD of W, retaining right singular vectors above a threshold. For transfer, both source and target models are projected to their quotients, aligned via Ridge regression on paired unlabeled activations, and probes are transported through the shared quotient space. Theorem 3.3 guarantees canonical isomorphism for behaviorally equivalent models; approximate versions (Theorems C.1–C.4) provide error bounds.

**Experimental setup.** Experiments span: (1) synthetic parity tasks validating the degree hierarchy; (2) cross-token subject-verb agreement on Pythia-70m/160m/410m using UD English-EWT; (3) safety monitor transfer from Qwen-2.5-7B-Instruct to Qwen-3B/14B and Mistral-7B with zero target labels; (4) portable behavioral intervention via reranking on 461 evaluation prompts; (5) coverage diagnostics via leave-one-out ISF analysis. All results averaged over 5 seeds.

## Results & Highlights

- **Degree hierarchy is tight**: On circular N-parity, degree < N is at chance while degree N reaches 0.985–0.995. Linear probes fail on XOR (0.501 ± 0.010), while quadratic probes achieve 1.000.
- **CP rank-1 outperforms linear on agreement**: +16.8–20.0pp AUROC across Pythia scales on subject-verb agreement. Full quadratic overfits (train AUROC ≥ 0.995, 5–11× larger train-test gap than CP-1).
- **Zero-label safety transfer**: Quotient transfer from Qwen-7B to Mistral-7B achieves AUROC 0.67–0.97 across safety concepts with no target labels. Sentiment transfers at 0.972 vs. source 0.975. Random-init control is near chance (0.51–0.63), confirming transfer requires genuine concept structure.
- **Coverage awareness**: Quotient transfer degrades smoothly as concepts rotate out of probe bank span (ISF sweep from 0° to 90°), while full-state OLS maintains >0.98 regardless. Leave-one-out analysis shows silent-failure rate of 24–38% for full-state OLS on concepts with ISF < 0.05.
- **Portable behavioral intervention**: Quotient reranking reduces harmful compliance by 19.7pp (Qwen-3B) and 15.4pp (Mistral) with zero target labels, at benign false refusal rates of 5–6%.
- **Policy mismatch detection**: Degree-2 composition on risk⊕refusal XOR target achieves AUROC 0.976 vs. 0.906 for linear, detecting harmful compliance and benign over-refusal.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: Very high.** The symmetry-aware classification of probe families (Theorem 2.2) is a genuinely new theoretical contribution. The quotient-based transfer framework (Theorem 3.3) reframes cross-model comparison in a principled way that prior work (CCA, Procrustes, SVCCA) did not anticipate. The connection between probe design and representation symmetry is original.
- **Rigor: Excellent.** Theorems are precisely stated with complete proofs in appendices. Approximate variants with quantitative bounds support practical deployment. The experimental methodology is thorough — 5 seeds, bootstrap CIs, controlled ablations, leave-one-out analyses, and negative controls (random-init models).
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Full experimental details across ~30 appendix tables. Code availability not mentioned in the text, but the mathematical framework is self-contained and the experimental protocols are described in sufficient detail.
- **Significance: High.** The framework has implications for mechanistic interpretability, model monitoring, and AI safety. The coverage-aware monitor transfer is a concrete operational contribution — organizations can build probe banks once and port them across model upgrades with diagnostic guarantees.
- **Red flags / Limitations:**
  - The analysis is restricted to the **final readout layer**. Extension to intermediate layers (where symmetry groups may be more complex) is acknowledged but not addressed.
  - The affine symmetry is derived from the readout layer specifically; it is unclear whether the polynomial hierarchy remains the right answer for probing other parts of the network.
  - Degree-2 probes show clear gains, but the jump to degree-3 adds at most 0.1pp in the experiments (Table 19), raising questions about whether higher degrees are practically useful.
  - The quotient transfer requires paired unlabeled activations from both models on the same inputs, which may be costly for very large models.
  - ISF is a useful but imperfect predictor of transfer quality — some low-ISF concepts still transfer via correlations (e.g., jailbreaking at ISF=0.161 transfers at 0.929), while some moderate-ISF concepts fail (bt_privacy at ISF=0.206 drops to chance).

## Strengths

- Principled theoretical foundation that derives probe families from first principles (symmetry + shallowness) rather than engineering heuristics.
- The polynomial hierarchy theorem is elegant and provides a clean mathematical explanation for why linear probes are the default.
- CP-structured probes are both theoretically motivated and practically effective — they avoid the overfitting of full quadratics while capturing genuine degree-2 structure.
- The quotient transfer framework provides both theoretical guarantees and practical coverage diagnostics, a rare combination.
- Extensive experimental validation across synthetic tasks, language model probing, and safety monitoring with careful controls.
- The coverage-aware abstention framework (Appendix G.4) converts a theoretical insight into a deployable decision rule.

## Limitations

- The theory is grounded in the final readout layer; extending to intermediate layers where symmetries may be richer or different is an open problem.
- Higher-degree probes (degree 3+) show negligible practical gains over degree-2 in all experiments, suggesting the polynomial hierarchy may be theoretically sharp but practically limited to degree 2.
- Quotient transfer requires paired unlabeled activations from source and target models, which becomes expensive as model size and alignment pool size grow.
- The affine symmetry assumption may be too restrictive for models with non-standard readout architectures (e.g., mixture-of-experts, multi-head outputs).
- ISF-based coverage diagnostics are geometric and may not capture semantic coverage — a concept can transfer through indirect correlations that ISF does not predict.
- The safety transfer experiments focus on classification; whether the framework extends to generation-time control beyond reranking is not established.

## Relevance

本文从对称性角度为神经探针设计建立了严密的几何理论框架，直接关系到表示学习中的不变性/等变性研究和机械可解释性。基于商空间的跨模型迁移方法对AI安全监控的实际部署有直接意义——可以在无需目标标签的情况下跨模型族移植安全探针，并提供覆盖范围诊断。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the theory extend to intermediate layers, where the relevant symmetry group may not be affine? Could layer-specific symmetry analysis yield different admissible probe families?
- Is there a principled way to determine the minimum sufficient probe bank size and composition for a given deployment scenario?
- Can the quotient framework be combined with causal interventions (e.g., activation patching) to enable cross-model causal transfer?
- The degree hierarchy is theoretically sharp but practically plateaus at degree 2 — are there tasks where degree 3+ probes would show meaningful gains?
- How robust is quotient transfer to distributional shift in the alignment data (e.g., different languages, domains, or prompt distributions)?
- Could the coverage-aware abstention framework be integrated into reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) pipelines as an automated safety check?
