> **一句话总结：** SOAR 针对NVFP4量化格式，通过闭式联合尺度优化（CJSO）解析地联合优化全局与块级尺度，再通过解耦尺度搜索（DSS）将量化尺度与反量化尺度分离并用离散搜索消除FP8精度瓶颈，在Qwen3-8B上平均准确率从68.75提升至70.68，且无额外推理开销。

> **TL;DR:** SOAR is a calibration-free NVFP4 post-training quantization framework with two components: CJSO derives closed-form analytical updates to jointly optimize the FP32 global scale and FP8 block-wise scales by minimizing weight reconstruction error; DSS decouples the high-precision quantization scale from the hardware-constrained dequantization scale and performs a local discrete search (FP8 neighbors × multiplicative grid [0.5, 1.5]) to mitigate FP8 scale quantization error. On Qwen3-8B, SOAR lifts average 5-task zero-shot accuracy from 68.75 (vanilla NVFP4) to 70.68, with no extra memory or inference cost. Full quantization of an 8B model takes ~36 minutes on a single A800 GPU.

## 核心贡献

- **闭式联合尺度优化（CJSO）：** 将全局尺度 α 和块级尺度 ∆ 的优化建模为重建误差最小化问题，通过一阶最优性条件推导出解析更新公式，以坐标下降的方式交替优化两者，避免了传统启发式尺度选择的局限性。
- **解耦尺度搜索（DSS）：** 发现NVFP4格式中量化过程（确定FP4映射）和反量化过程（硬件存储）对尺度精度的需求不对称，据此引入高精度量化尺度 ∆q 和硬件约束反量化尺度 ∆d 的解耦，通过联合离散搜索（FP8邻域 × 连续乘性网格）最小化块级重建误差。
- **无需校准数据：** SOAR本身是校准数据无关的，仅基于权重重建误差优化，但可与GPTQ等校准数据方法无缝集成，进一步提升效果。
- **跨格式泛化：** DSS模块已验证在MXFP4格式上也有效（平均准确率提升+0.47到+1.02），说明尺度量化误差是微缩放格式的共性问题。
- **高效且实用：** 8B模型量化耗时约36分钟（单A800 GPU），15次迭代带早停机制，无额外推理存储开销。

## Key Contributions

- **Closed-form Joint Scale Optimization (CJSO):** Formulates joint optimization of the tensor-wide FP32 global scale α and block-wise FP8 scales ∆ as a reconstruction error minimization problem, deriving closed-form updates via first-order optimality conditions. Uses coordinate descent: fix FP4 assignments Q, solve for α* and ∆* analytically, then re-quantize, iterating until convergence.
- **Decoupled Scale Search (DSS):** Exploits the asymmetry that quantization (FP4 assignment) can use arbitrary precision while dequantization is constrained to FP8 (E4M3). Decouples into a high-precision quantization scale ∆q and hardware-constrained dequantization scale ∆d, performing a joint block-wise discrete search over FP8 neighbors for ∆d and a fine-grained multiplicative grid [0.50, 1.50] (step 0.01) for ∆q.
- **Calibration-free by design, GPTQ-compatible:** SOAR optimizes purely on weight reconstruction error without calibration data, yet integrates seamlessly with GPTQ for additive gains (e.g., LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct: 72.95 → 73.18 avg accuracy when combined).
- **Generalizes to MXFP4:** DSS alone improves MXFP4 quantization by +0.47 to +1.02 avg accuracy across model families, demonstrating that scale quantization error is a shared bottleneck in microscaling formats.
- **Practical efficiency:** 8B model quantized in ~36 min on one A800 GPU; 15 iterations with early stopping (relative MSE improvement < 10⁻³).

## Methodology

SOAR operates on the NVFP4 format, which uses FP4 (E2M1) elements with a two-level scaling: an FP32 tensor-wide global scale α and FP8 (E4M3) block-wise scales ∆ applied every 16 elements.

**CJSO (Closed-form Joint Scale Optimization):** The reconstruction objective is min_{α,{∆i}} Σ ||Wi − Qi·(α∆i)||². Under fixed FP4 assignments Qi, this becomes quadratic in α and ∆i. The closed-form global scale update is α* = Σ(Wij·Qij·∆i) / Σ(Qij²·∆i²), and the block-wise update is ∆i* = Σ(Wij·Qij·α) / Σ(Qij²·α²). These alternate with re-quantization of Q under updated scales, initialized from standard max-based NVFP4 rules.

**DSS (Decoupled Scale Search):** After each CJSO update, DSS refines each block by searching over: (1) two nearest FP8-representable values for the dequantization scale ∆d, and (2) a multiplicative grid of 101 values (0.50 to 1.50, step 0.01) for the quantization scale ∆q. The pair minimizing block-wise reconstruction error ||Wi − QFP4(Wi/(α∆q))·(α∆d)||² is selected. The key insight is that ∆q (used only during quantization to determine FP4 assignment) can be arbitrary precision, while ∆d (stored for inference) must be FP8-compliant.

**Overall framework:** CJSO and DSS alternate for up to 15 iterations with early stopping. CJSO provides a strong analytical prior; DSS compensates for FP8 precision loss in block scales. The quantization scale ∆q is discarded after optimization — only α and ∆d are stored, so there is zero inference overhead.

## Results & Highlights

**Zero-shot commonsense (5-task average, W4A4):**
- Qwen3-8B: NVFP4 68.75 → SOAR **70.68** (+1.93); RaZeR 70.12 → SOAR **70.68** (+0.56)
- LLaMA-3.2-3B-Instruct: NVFP4 65.02 → SOAR **66.00** (+0.98); RaZeR 65.19 → SOAR **66.00** (+0.81)
- LLaMA-3.2-1B-Instruct: NVFP4 56.62 → SOAR **57.79** (+1.17); RaZeR 57.51 → SOAR **57.79** (+0.28)
- LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct: NVFP4 71.91 → SOAR **72.57** (+0.66); RaZeR 72.53 → SOAR **72.57** (+0.04)

**Reasoning tasks:**
- Qwen3-8B MMLU: 70.70 → **71.47** (+0.77); GSM8K: 79.30 → **82.56** (+3.26)
- LLaMA-3.2-3B GSM8K: 72.93 (RaZeR) → **73.62** (+0.69)

**GPTQ integration:** SOAR+GPTQ further improves over GPTQ alone (e.g., LLaMA-3.1-8B 72.95 → 73.18 avg; perplexity unchanged at 6.57 Wiki2).

**Ablation:** CJSO alone gives +0.62 avg; DSS alone gives +0.48; together SOAR gives +0.98 over NVFP4 baseline on LLaMA-3.2-3B. Both components are complementary.

**MXFP4 generalization:** DSS alone improves MXFP4 by +0.47 (Qwen3-4B) to +1.02 (LLaMA-3.1-1B) avg accuracy.

**Quantization time:** 1B model ~7 min, 3B ~17 min, 8B ~36 min on single A800 GPU.

## Critical Assessment

### Novelty
- The closed-form joint optimization of global + block-wise scales is a clean, well-motivated contribution. The key insight — that the objective becomes quadratic under fixed Q assignments — is simple but effective.
- The decoupling of quantization vs. dequantization scales is a genuinely novel observation: prior work treated them as identical, but the hardware format only stores the dequantization scale, leaving the quantization scale free.
- Neither component is revolutionary alone, but the combination is well-engineered and fills a clear gap.

### Rigor
- **Strengths:** Comprehensive evaluation across 5 model sizes (1B–8B), 7 benchmarks, W4A4 and W4A16 settings, GPTQ integration, MXFP4 generalization, convergence analysis, iteration ablation, and timing measurements. The ablation in Table 4 cleanly isolates CJSO and DSS contributions.
- **Weaknesses:** Only compared against 2 NVFP4 baselines (4over6 and RaZeR). Missing comparisons with MRGPTQ (ICLR 2026), ARCQuant, and BatQuant — all directly relevant NVFP4/MXFP4 methods cited in related work. The baseline comparison is narrower than the related work suggests. No evaluation on models larger than 8B (e.g., 70B), where quantization behavior may differ. Perplexity results in Table 4 show SOAR sometimes slightly *increases* perplexity (e.g., Wiki2: 11.98 → 12.04 with CJSO alone), which is odd and not discussed.

### Reproducibility
- Strong: closed-form solutions, pseudocode (Algorithm 1), detailed hyperparameters (search grid [0.5, 1.5], step 0.01, 15 iterations), code release promised.
- The mathematical derivations in Appendix D are clear and complete.

### Significance
- NVFP4 is a production format with native hardware support (NVIDIA Blackwell). Practical impact is high.
- The gains are meaningful but not transformative: the largest single-model improvement is +1.93 avg (Qwen3-8B), and on some models the margin over RaZeR is thin (e.g., +0.04 on LLaMA-3.1-8B).
- The generality to MXFP4 is a nice bonus but the gains there are modest.

### Red Flags
- **Narrow baselines:** Only 4over6 and RaZeR as NVFP4 competitors — no MRGPTQ, ARCQuant, or BatQuant comparison despite citing them.
- **Inconsistent perplexity:** Table 4 shows CJSO slightly *increasing* Wiki2 perplexity (11.98 → 12.04) while improving downstream accuracy. The authors don't discuss this perplexity-accuracy mismatch.
- **Marginal gains on some models:** On LLaMA-3.1-8B, SOAR is only +0.04 over RaZeR on average — within noise margin of a single benchmark.
- **Weight-only setting:** In Table 6 (W4A16), SOAR sometimes *underperforms* baselines (e.g., LLaMA-3.1-8B: RaZeR 73.96 vs SOAR 73.42; LLaMA-3.2-1B: RaZeR 59.06 vs SOAR 58.95). This is not discussed.
- **No large-scale experiments:** No evaluation on 13B, 70B, or larger models where quantization artifacts may amplify.

## Strengths

- Clean mathematical formulation with closed-form solutions — no expensive iterative gradient-based optimization.
- The decoupled scale insight is elegant: quantization can use arbitrary precision since only ∆d is stored.
- Calibration-free by default, yet compatible with calibration-based methods like GPTQ.
- Comprehensive convergence analysis (Fig. 4) and iteration ablation (Table 7) show the iterative process is well-behaved.
- Dialogue examples in Appendix E vividly demonstrate the qualitative improvement.
- Generalizes beyond NVFP4 to MXFP4, suggesting broader applicability.

## Limitations

- Only 2 direct NVFP4 baselines compared; missing several recently published methods (MRGPTQ, ARCQuant, BatQuant).
- No experiments on models larger than 8B parameters.
- DSS grid search over 101 quantization-scale candidates × 2 dequantization candidates per block per iteration adds non-trivial offline compute (~36 min for 8B model).
- Perplexity-accuracy inconsistency not addressed (CJSO increases perplexity but improves downstream tasks).
- Weight-only (W4A16) results are mixed — SOAR sometimes underperforms simpler baselines.
- The method is specific to microscaling formats (NVFP4/MXFP4); unclear if insights transfer to integer or standard FP quantization.

## Relevance

直接面向LLM高效部署场景，NVFP4是NVIDIA Blackwell架构原生支持的4-bit浮点格式，SOAR通过纯数学优化（无需训练数据）提升量化精度，对实际模型压缩和推理加速具有直接的工程应用价值，特别是对于需要在有限显存下部署大模型的研究者。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does SOAR compare against MRGPTQ (ICLR 2026), which combines GPTQ with Hadamard rotation for FP4 quantization?
- Would scaling to 70B+ models reveal different behavior — do the gains hold or diminish?
- Can the CJSO objective be reformulated with activation-aware terms (as the authors suggest in Section 5) to further close the gap with FP16?
- What is the sensitivity of DSS to the search grid parameters (range [0.5, 1.5], step 0.01) — is there a cheaper approximation?
- The perplexity-accuracy mismatch in CJSO deserves investigation: why does downstream accuracy improve when reconstruction perplexity worsens?
- Could the decoupled scale idea extend to mixed-precision settings where some blocks use different formats?
