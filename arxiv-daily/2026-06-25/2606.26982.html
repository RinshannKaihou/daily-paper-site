Auditing Framing-Sensitive Behavioral Instability in Large Language Models for Mental Health Interactions

arXiv: 2606.26982

Date: 2026-06-25

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26982

一句话总结： 本文针对心理健康对话场景，构建受控的"语义相同、语境框架不同"的匹配提示集，跨7个指令微调模型家族发现非对抗性的语境框架（如文档化、制度性、责任性）会系统性地改变模型的解读-路由倾向，且这一行为信号可通过逐层探针从隐状态中解码，并经激活导向（CAA）部分干预，说明"框架鲁棒性"是可信对话AI审计的重要维度。
TL;DR: Using 653 matched prompt groups rewritten across 5 non-adversarial contextual framings and evaluated on 7 instruction-tuned model families, the authors show that contextual framing systematically shifts interpretive-routing tendencies (e.g., documentation framing escalates interpretation, institutional framing suppresses it), that these behavior-associated signals are decodable from layer-wise hidden states (above chance even on held-out framings despite strong TF-IDF baselines), and that activation steering (CAA) can partially modulate the downstream behavior — arguing framing robustness is a key trustworthiness audit dimension for mental-health LLMs.

核心贡献

  • 提出受控"匹配提示"框架：在保持底层语义意图不变的前提下，将每个提示重写为6种语境框架条件（Base、文档化、认知性、制度性、责任性、角色化），共653个匹配提示组，从而隔离"语境效应"与"语义变异"。
  • 行为层面证据：在7个指令微调模型家族（Qwen-0.5B/1.5B、Gemma-2B/9B、Mistral-7B、Phi-3.5、Phi-4-mini）上证明，非对抗性的语境框架即可系统性改变解读-路由倾向，无需显式对抗提示。
  • 设计4级行为标注体系（Weak/Disengaged、Restrained-Supportive、Interpretive-Supportive、Escalated Interpretation），并坍缩为二元"解读-路由"变量用于统计与探针分析。
  • 表征层面证据：通过逐层logistic回归探针、随机标签对照与TF-IDF词汇基线，证明行为信号在Transformer各深度可解码，且在留出框架上仍高于随机水平，说明信号部分超越具体模板泛化。
  • 因果干预证据：基于CAA的激活导向（h' = h − αd）显示中等强度可降低解读-路由倾向（尤其在Mistral-7B与Qwen-1.5B），而高强度出现非线性反弹，表明行为由分布式表征而非单一线性机制主导。

Key Contributions

  • A controlled matched-prompt framework that rewrites each ambiguous-support prompt across 5 non-adversarial contextual framings (Documentation, Epistemic, Institutional, Liability, Role) while preserving semantic intent, yielding 653 matched groups × 6 conditions to isolate framing from semantic variation.
  • Behavioral evidence across 7 instruction-tuned families (Qwen-0.5B/1.5B, Gemma-2B/9B, Mistral-7B, Phi-3.5, Phi-4-mini) that non-adversarial contextual framing systematically alters interpretive-response calibration — a finding that does not require jailbreak-style prompts.
  • A structured 4-tier behavioral annotation scheme (Weak/Disengaged, Restrained-Supportive, Interpretive-Supportive, Escalated Interpretation) focused on interpretation strength rather than correctness/helpfulness, reviewed by a psychology co-author.
  • Representation-level evidence via layer-wise logistic regression probes, a random-label control, and a TF-IDF lexical baseline, showing behavior-associated signals are decodable throughout transformer depth and partially generalize to held-out framings.
  • Preliminary causal-intervention evidence via Contrastive Activation Addition (CAA) steering that framing-associated representational directions partially modulate downstream interpretive routing, with documented non-linear rebound effects.

Methodology

The study couples behavioral annotation with mechanistic representation analysis in a three-stage pipeline (Figure 1):

  1. Matched-prompt construction. Ambiguous-support scenarios were curated to avoid crisis language, self-harm intent, diagnostic requests, or jailbreak instructions, preserving interpretive ambiguity. Each prompt was rewritten into 5 framing categories plus a Base condition (653 groups × 6 = ~3,918 variants).
  2. Models. Seven open-weight instruction-tuned transformers spanning architectures and scales: Qwen-0.5B, Qwen-1.5B, Gemma-2B, Gemma-9B, Mistral-7B, Phi-3.5, Phi-4-mini.
  3. Behavioral annotation. Responses labeled into 4 calibration categories, then collapsed to a binary "interpretive-routing" variable (restrained-supportive vs. higher-interpretation). Annotations include confidence scores and a psychology co-author consistency review.
  4. Hidden-state extraction. Final residual-stream activation at the last generated token before EOS, extracted for every layer, every framing condition, every model.
  5. Layer-wise probing. Per-layer logistic regression probes P(y=1|h)=σ(Wh+b) with balanced class weighting; 5-fold stratified CV with matched prompt groups as grouping variables to prevent leakage. Two controls: (i) random-label permutation, (ii) TF-IDF unigram+bigram lexical baseline. Held-out framing evaluation trains on 5 framings and tests on the excluded one.
  6. Activation steering. CAA directions from activation differences between restrained-supportive and higher-interpretation states; applied as h' = h − αd at high-decoding layers; α ∈ {0.0, 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0}; 50 prompts per model; deterministic decoding.
  7. Statistics. Logistic regression with framing condition and model identity as predictors; Wald tests, p < 0.05.

Results & Highlights

  • Framing modulates calibration. Documentation framing produced the highest interpretive-routing rates (e.g., 0.63 in Gemma-9B, 0.46 in Qwen-0.5B); institutional framing consistently produced lower rates. In logistic regression, Documentation β = 1.81, p < 0.001; institutional framing was not significant (see Appendix Table 2).
  • Architecture-dependent sensitivity. Gemma-9B, Phi-4-mini, and Qwen-0.5B showed large framing-induced routing shifts; Qwen-1.5B was comparatively stable.
  • Behavior decodable across depth. Held-out framing probes reached balanced accuracies of 0.72–0.83 across architectures (Figure 3). Random-label control collapsed to 0.50–0.52 (chance); TF-IDF lexical baseline scored 0.89–0.94; held-out framing probe 0.73–0.83 (Table 1) — confirming a substantial lexical contribution but a residual non-lexical signal.
  • Distributed, not layer-localized, signal. Depth-wise held-out decoding (Figure 4) stayed above chance throughout, 0.52–0.69, with architecture-specific peaks (Gemma-2B mid-layer, Phi-3.5 deeper layers, Qwen family flat profiles). No universal emergence layer was found.
  • Steering partially modulates behavior. Moderate steering strengths reduced interpretive routing, especially in Mistral-7B (0.56 → 0.22) and Qwen-1.5B (0.50 → 0.30), without inflating weak/disengaged responses (Figure 5). Higher strengths triggered non-linear rebound, supporting distributed rather than purely linear control.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. The framing-sensitivity phenomenon itself is well established (sycophancy, prompt-dependent refusal, reward hacking). The genuine novelty is the combination of (a) a controlled matched-prompt protocol in the mental-health domain, (b) layer-wise probing with proper leakage controls, and (c) CAA-based intervention for a calibration axis (interpretive escalation) rather than refusal/sycophancy. Individually each piece exists; the synthesis and domain framing are the contribution.
  • Rigor: Solid with caveats. Strengths include matched-group cross-validation to prevent leakage, a random-label control, a TF-IDF lexical baseline, and a held-out framing protocol. Caveats: behavioral annotation is largely automated/author-produced with only a "subset" reviewed by a co-author (inter-annotator agreement is not quantified); only 50 steering evaluation prompts per model is small; only the final-token residual stream is probed (token-level positional effects unaddressed); the magnitude of the non-lexical residual signal (0.73–0.83 held-out vs. 0.89–0.94 lexical) is modest, and the authors are commendably honest that lexical cues explain "a substantial portion."
  • Reproducibility: Medium. Open-weight models and deterministic decoding aid reproducibility, but the prompt dataset, annotation labels, probe weights, and steering directions are not stated to be released. The framing-rewrite procedure is described qualitatively but not as a released artifact, making exact replication of the 653 matched groups difficult.
  • Significance: Domain-relevant rather than paradigm-shifting. The practical message — that institutional/documentation/liability framing shifts mental-health LLM behavior — is useful for deployers and auditors. It is unlikely to change core interpretability theory because the intervention effects are partial and architecture-dependent and the paper explicitly disclaims a "complete mechanistic decomposition."
  • Red flags / concerns.
  • The held-out probe accuracy (0.73–0.83) is only moderately above the strong lexical baseline's gap from chance, and no direct test separates "genuine abstract framing signal" from "spurious lexical/template artifacts not captured by unigram+bigram TF-IDF" (e.g., longer-range phrasing, length cues). The "beyond lexical" claim is plausible but under-proven.
  • The activation-steering result is correlational in spirit: showing that subtracting a CAA direction shifts behavior does not prove that framing causally routes through that direction — only that a behavior-associated direction exists and is somewhat steerable.
  • Title says "Mental Health Interactions," but prompts are explicitly designed to avoid crisis/diagnostic content; external validity to genuine clinical or distressed-user traffic is uncertain.
  • No comparison to a frontier proprietary model (GPT-4, Claude), so generalization to deployed systems is limited.
  • "Behavioral instability" in the title is somewhat stronger than what the evidence supports (calibration shifts, not instability per se).

Strengths

  • Clean experimental isolation of framing from semantics via matched-prompt rewriting with group-aware CV to prevent leakage.
  • Multiple controls (random-label, TF-IDF, held-out framing) raise the bar above typical probing studies.
  • Honest, calibrated claims: the authors repeatedly temper their interpretation ("preliminary intervention evidence rather than a complete mechanistic decomposition," "not entirely reducible to direct lexical matching").
  • Cross-architecture breadth (7 models, 3 families) surfaces the architecture-dependence of framing effects, which a single-model study would miss.
  • Domain framing in mental health is timely and connects mechanistic interpretability to a concrete safety/trustworthiness use case.
  • Reports both behavioral statistics (logistic regression, Wald tests) and representation-level metrics, bridging behavioral and mechanistic analyses.

Limitations

  • Annotation quality: no reported inter-annotator agreement or reliability statistics; only a "subset" psychology-reviewed.
  • Small steering evaluation set (50 prompts/model) limits statistical power for the causal-intervention claims.
  • Single probing location (final-token residual stream) ignores positional/token-level representational dynamics.
  • The "non-lexical" residual signal is real but modest; a stronger lexical control (e.g., length, template, syntactic) would tighten the "beyond surface form" conclusion.
  • No frontier closed-weight models tested; generalization to GPT-4-class systems unknown.
  • The mental-health framing is conceptual — prompts avoid crisis language, so clinical validity is not demonstrated.
  • No human-trust or user-expectation measurement despite the trust/trust-calibration framing in the discussion.
  • Steering rebound and architecture-dependence mean no deployable intervention is demonstrated — only a proof-of-concept.

Relevance

这篇论文几乎完全贴合研究兴趣：它直接把"行为稳定性"作为审计目标，在"心理健康对话"这一敏感场景下，用"LLM 探针"（逐层 logistic regression probing + CAA 激活导向）将行为变异与内部表征关联起来，方法学与主题三方面都与行为稳定性/心理健康/LLM 探针方向高度对齐，可作为该方向方法学与评估范式的直接参考。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Would a stronger lexical/template/length control (e.g., controlling for prompt length, syntactic template, or using a fine-tuned sentence-embedding baseline) close the gap between the 0.73–0.83 held-out probe and the 0.89–0.94 TF-IDF baseline, or does a genuine non-lexical signal survive?
  • Does the CAA steering direction derived from framing actually correspond to the framing axis, or to a confounded behavior axis? A framing-vs-behavior disentanglement (e.g., probing framing identity while holding behavior fixed) would clarify causality.
  • How do the framing-induced routing shifts interact with user trust, reliance, and expectation calibration in real human-AI interaction studies — which the paper motivates but does not measure?
  • Do frontier closed-weight models (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini) show the same documentation→escalation / institutional→suppression asymmetry, and at what magnitude?
  • Is the interpretive-routing signal localized to a low-dimensional subspace (as with refusal), and can a single dominant direction explain most variance, or is it genuinely distributed?
  • Does framing robustness improve with scale within a family, or does it remain architecture-specific — and can targeted fine-tuning on matched-prompt sets reduce framing sensitivity?
  • Can the probe/steering pipeline be adapted to detect in-the-wild framing-induced miscalibration during deployment, providing a runtime auditing signal?
