SegFold: Accelerating Sparse GEMM with a Fine-Grained Dynamic Dataflow

arXiv: 2606.26701

Date: 2026-06-25

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26701

一句话总结： SegFold 提出 Segment 动态数据流，通过 SelectA 在活动窗口内动态重排 (m,k) 配对以最大化 B 行复用、SegmentBC 在 PE 阵列上动态重映射部分和以平衡负载，并辅以空间/时间折叠机制，在 16×16 PE 阵列上较 SOTA 的 Spada 取得 1.95×、较最优 Flexagon 静态配置取得 5.3× 的几何平均加速。
TL;DR: SegFold introduces Segment, a fine-grained dynamic SpGEMM dataflow that reorders work in a sliding window (SelectA) and dynamically remaps partial sums across PEs (SegmentBC), together with spatial/temporal folding; the co-designed 16×16 accelerator delivers 1.95× geomean speedup over Spada and 5.3× over the best static Flexagon configuration.

核心贡献

  • 首次将"动态性"维度引入 SpGEMM 数据流设计空间：打破传统内积/外积/Gustavson 的静态循环嵌套，提出 Segment 数据流，在子 tile 粒度上同时优化三操作数（A、B、C）复用与负载均衡。
  • SelectA 动态调度算法：在 K 维活动窗口内以 mk-dynamic 顺序贪婪选择共享相同 k 的 (m,k) 配对（最大化 B 行复用），同时避免相同 m 导致的 C 行归约冲突；并支持跨 k 的流水化窗口推进。
  • SegmentBC 动态映射机制：将部分和维护在压缩有序的"虚拟坐标空间 V"中，B 元素通过网络二分查找（IPM）找到合法起始点后流式归并，实现元素级在途重分布以减少网络争用；其硬件近优映射仅比理想网络慢 1.2%。
  • 空间折叠 + 时间折叠：将不规则长度的虚拟 C 行沿相邻 PE 行折叠（右→上→下→左优先级），长行溢出部分 spill 到 scratchpad，显著提升阵列利用率而不要求逻辑行适配单 PE 行。
  • 完整软硬件协同与验证：提供 cycle-level 模拟器 + RTL 综合（ASAP7 7nm，1 GHz，0.160 mm²，385.7 mW），在 15 个 SuiteSparse 矩阵上评测；控制复杂度对阵列规模线性可扩展。

Key Contributions

  • Adds a "dynamism" axis to the SpGEMM dataflow taxonomy — prior work picks among inner/outer/Gustavson per tile but schedules statically within a tile; SegFold reorders work at sub-tile granularity, combining reuse benefits no single static dataflow can achieve.
  • SelectA dynamic scheduling — greedily selects up to Rmax (m,k) pairs within an active window of size W=32, maximizing same-k pairs for B-row reuse while forbidding same-m pairs to avoid C-row reduction conflicts; supports k-pipelining via a sliding window.
  • SegmentBC dynamic mapping — maintains a compressed, column-ordered virtual coordinate space V; B elements find their legal injection point via a pipelined binary-search LUT (IPM) and merge through an adaptive network, with only 1.2% overhead vs. an oracle mapper.
  • Spatial + temporal folding — maps irregular-length virtual C rows across neighboring physical PE rows (priority right/up/down/left) and spills overflow to a per-row scratchpad, decoupling logical row length from PE-row width.
  • Full codesign with rigorous evaluation — 16×16 PE array, cycle-level simulator (Ramulator2 HBM2 backend), RTL synthesis in ASAP7 7nm at 1 GHz (0.160 mm², 385.7 mW); compared against open-source Spada and Flexagon (STONNE) baselines.

Methodology

Dataflow (Segment). SpGEMM C = A·B is reformulated so A is consumed element-wise and B row-wise. Two dynamic mechanisms operate within each tile:

  1. SelectA maintains a sliding window over K and greedily reorders (m,k) pairs using two rules: (i) maximize pairs sharing the same k (B-row reuse), (ii) reject pairs with duplicate m (C-row conflict avoidance). The N dimension is innermost to fully reuse A; the window advances k once all its A–B intersections retire.
  2. SegmentBC defines an injective, row-saturated, column-ordered, time-ascending mapping f_t(m,n) → (x,y) onto a virtual coordinate space V. Each incoming B element is injected at a near-optimal position found by the Index-to-PE Mapper (IPM, a pipelined binary-search LUT) and traverses the adaptive merge network, performing one of three actions per PE: forward (b>c), insert/shift (b

Microarchitecture (SegFold). A 2D PE array (16×16, row-stationary at PE-row granularity) with: a per-PE ALU + FIFO + 4-port router forming an adaptive merge network; a vector multicast crossbar (4 B rows/cycle) for streaming B; a per-row scratchpad for temporal folding; and a memory controller implementing SelectA (O(W) combinational bitmask scan over A, doubly-compressed sparse-row format for B). Folding extends V-space rows beyond a single PE-row's capacity both spatially (neighbor occupancy check) and temporally (scratchpad spills).

Evaluation. Cycle-level simulation against Spada and Flexagon (STONNE) on 15 SuiteSparse matrices spanning densities 3.5e−4 to 5.5e−3 and various aspect ratios, plus synthetic density sweeps (256–1024, d=0.05–1.0) and asymmetric-sparsity (dA×dB) studies. RTL synthesized with Synopsys Design Compiler compile_ultra in ASAP7 7nm; 28nm estimates derived via standard scaling.

Results & Highlights

  • 1.95× geomean speedup over Spada and 5.3× over the best Flexagon static configuration across SuiteSparse.
  • Per-workload speedups over Spada range 0.17×–5.75×; the single regression is ca-GrQc (0.59×), whose scale-free structure produces a few extremely dense rows that overwhelm per-row PE allocation.
  • On tall matrices (M>K), SegFold wins 1.42× over Spada; on wide matrices (M it loses (2/3 cases) unless the multiplication direction is transposed, which recovers 2.4–3.0×.
  • Ablation: fixed-k order drops performance to 0.67× of baseline (SelectA matters); LUT mapper is within 1.2% of an ideal oracle network; incremental breakdown yields 3.1× geomean over base config, with SelectA the single largest contributor.
  • Hardware sensitivity: marginal gains flatten past crossbar width = 4 and window size = 32 (chosen defaults). At full density SegFold still beats all baselines because its 2D array handles dense GEMM natively.
  • Area/power comparable to Flexagon at 28nm (~1.35 mm²) while adding dynamic-scheduling hardware.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty — High. Introducing sub-tile dynamic scheduling as a first-class axis of the SpGEMM dataflow design space is a genuinely fresh framing; most prior work (Spada, Flexagon, Trapezoid) adapts only at tile/window granularity. The virtual-coordinate-space formulation with the four invariants (injectivity, row saturation, column ordering, time ascending) is a clean, well-motivated abstraction.
  • Rigor — Moderate-to-High. Cycle-level simulator + RTL synthesis + ablations + two sensitivity studies form a solid evaluation. The near-oracle IPM result (1.2% gap) and the SelectA ablation (0.67×) directly support the core claims. Comparisons reuse open-source Spada and STONNE/Flexagon simulators with a shared Ramulator2 backend, which is good practice.
  • Reproducibility — Moderate. Simulator and baselines are described in detail, but no explicit statement that SegFold's own simulator/RTL will be open-sourced; many hardware constants (tile sizing heuristic, spad sizing) are under-specified. The 28nm power estimate assumes conservative 50% switching activity, flagged honestly.
  • Significance — High for the subfield. If the 1.95× over a 2023 ASPLOS SOTA (Spada) holds up, this is a meaningful advance for sparse-sparse GEMM accelerators relevant to LLM sparse attention and pruned inference.
  • Red flags / honesty concerns:
  • The headline 5.3× over Flexagon is somewhat inflated — Flexagon is a 2023 multi-dataflow design that must commit to one static dataflow per tile, so the comparison partly restates a known limitation of static scheduling; the more meaningful number is 1.95× vs. the adaptive Spada.
  • Per-workload variance is large (0.17×–5.75×), with documented regressions on ca-GrQc and on wide matrices — the geomean masks cases where the static baselines win, and the proposed fix (transpose direction) requires knowing M/K ratios in advance.
  • The density sweep (Fig. 13) shows SegFold's per-MAC efficiency drops sharply at the dense end — at odds with the claim that it "outperforms all baselines" in the fully dense case (the figure narrative is more nuanced than the text).
  • No comparison to very recent (2024–2025) sparse-sparse designs such as ZeD (PACT'24), SparGD, SpMARD, or Onyx beyond the related-work table.
  • Power estimate (385.7 mW at 7nm, ~2.3W at 28nm) is not measured on silicon and uses conservative activity factors.

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated formalization (V-space + four invariants) that makes the otherwise ad-hoc idea of "dynamic remapping" tractable and analyzable.
  • Near-optimal hardware approximation of an oracle mapper (1.2% gap) is an impressive engineering result.
  • Honest about failure cases (ca-GrQc, wide matrices) and provides a concrete mitigation (direction transpose).
  • Modular microarchitecture with clear scalability story (O(P) merge, O(log P) IPM, O(R) scratchpad).
  • Strong author group with relevant prior art; rigorous RTL synthesis pipeline.

Limitations

  • Performance is workload-dependent and sometimes regressive — scale-free graphs and wide matrices (K≫M) are weak spots.
  • Requires knowing M/K aspect ratios to pick the best multiplication direction; no automatic runtime policy is demonstrated.
  • Window size and crossbar width are fixed at configuration time; a fully runtime-adaptive version is left to future work.
  • No silicon validation; all numbers are simulated/synthesized.
  • Dense-end efficiency drops (Fig. 13), and the 5.3× headline is partly a referendum on static dataflows rather than a head-to-head with the strongest possible static+adaptive baseline.
  • Limited recent-baseline coverage (ZeD, SparGD, SpMARD, Onyx discussed but not benchmarked).

Relevance

与你在硬件加速器 / 稀疏 GEMM 方向的研究兴趣高度相关：SegFold 直接针对 sparse-sparse GEMM 的数据流设计空间，提出的 SelectA 动态调度、SegmentBC 虚拟坐标重映射与折叠机制，对设计面向 LLM 稀疏注意力与剪枝推理的稀疏张量加速器具有直接借鉴价值，尤其是其在子 tile 粒度引入"动态性"维度的思路可迁移到其他稀疏内核（如 SpMV、稀疏卷积）。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the direction-selection (Direction 1 vs. 2) policy be made fully runtime-adaptive, and what metadata/overhead would that require?
  • How does SegFold compose with inter-tile ordering (Hilbert/Z-order, Spahet sliding-window) the authors claim orthogonality with — is there a combined implementation?
  • Would integrating offline preprocessing (Gamma/ZeD-style row grouping) on top of SegmentBC stack benefits, or does runtime scheduling already capture the same reuse?
  • How does the design scale to larger arrays (32×32, 64×64) — does the O(P) merge-network traversal and LUT update bandwidth become a bottleneck?
  • Can the Segment dataflow extend to structured/semi-structured sparsity (e.g., 2:4) where some static structure could reduce dynamic overhead?
  • What is the real (measured, not vectorless-estimated) power on representative sparse vs. dense workloads?
  • How would SegFold perform against 2024–2025 baselines (ZeD, SparGD, SpMARD, Onyx) that are discussed but not benchmarked?
