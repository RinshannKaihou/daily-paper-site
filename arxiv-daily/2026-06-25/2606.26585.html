Methodology

(Inferred from abstract; details subject to full-text confirmation.)

The framework wraps an AI scheduling decision-maker in two complementary layers. First, a multi-level validation layer inspects each generated decision before it is executed: it checks that referenced data (e.g., catalog values, ephemerides, instrument states) is internally consistent, verifies that the reasoning is logically coherent, and confirms compliance with hard observational constraints (target visibility, pointing limits, instrument capabilities). Decisions failing any level are filtered or sent to a feedback-correction loop. Second, a traceability layer decomposes the decision into atomic reasoning units connected by dependency relationships, so that any failure can be localized to a specific step and audited afterward. The framework is evaluated by comparing pure AI scheduling against the framework-enhanced approach on metrics of executability, reliability, and preservation of transient observing opportunities.
