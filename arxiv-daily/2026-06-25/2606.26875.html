Information-Aware KV Cache Compression for Long Reasoning

arXiv: 2606.26875

Date: 2026-06-25

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26875

一句话总结： 本文提出 InfoKV，通过引入预测熵（predictive entropy）和跨层表征演化作为注意力分数之外的信息论信号来衡量 token 重要性，证明高熵 token 对远期未来上下文有更强影响，在 LongReason（长预填充）和 IFEval/AIME/LiveCodeBench（长解码）上持续超越 SnapKV、PyramidKV、Expected Attention 与 RPC 等基于注意力的 KV 压缩方法。
TL;DR: InfoKV augments attention-based KV cache compression with information-theoretic signals (predictive entropy + layer-wise representation evolution), motivated by a new "Forward Influence" metric showing high-entropy tokens dominate long-range future influence while attention-selected tokens only affect nearby contexts; it consistently beats attention-based baselines on long prefilling (LongReason) and long decoding (IFEval, AIME 2024, LiveCodeBench), even surpassing full-cache on some IFEval settings.

核心贡献

  • 提出 Forward Influence 指标：从"前瞻"视角重新审视 token 重要性，量化压缩某个 token 后对未来预测分布的 KL 散度影响，揭示了一个关键现象——基于注意力分数选出的 token 主要影响近邻上下文，而高预测熵（高不确定性）token 对远期未来上下文影响显著更强且更持久。
  • 提出 InfoKV 框架：将信息论信号融入 KV 压缩，融合三种信号——token 级预测熵、跨层表征演化（早期层与最后层隐藏状态的余弦距离）、以及注意力分数，按层计算熵分数 E_i^(l) = D_i^(l) · H_i，并与注意力分数线性组合 S_i^(l) = α·A_i^(l) + (1-α)·Softmax(E^(l))_i。
  • Top-k 受限熵：针对 LLM 概率分布集中在少量 token 上的特性，仅在 top-k（最优 k=256）token 上计算熵，避免低概率 token 引入噪声，提升不确定性估计的稳定性。
  • 统一的预填充 + 解码压缩：同一框架在长预填充（LongReason，Llama-3.1/3.2）与长解码（DeepSeek-R1-Distill，最大 32k 输出）场景下均取得 SOTA 或高度竞争的表现；在 IFEval 上 25%/12.5% 保留率甚至超过 R1-Distill-Llama-8B 的全缓存。
  • 深入消融与分析：系统研究了偏置 τ、top-k、熵/注意力平衡系数 α（α=0.9 最优），并提出层自适应预算分配（按层累积熵比例分配预算），尽管自适应在 Llama 上有效但在 Qwen 上不稳定，故默认采用均匀策略。

Key Contributions

  • Forward Influence metric: Introduces a forward-looking measure of token importance — the average KL divergence between original and post-removal predictive distributions over a future context chunk — and uses it to demonstrate that attention-selected tokens dominate short-range influence while high-entropy tokens dominate long-range (distant future) influence.
  • InfoKV framework: A plug-in entropy-aware KV cache compression method combining three complementary signals per layer: (i) token-level predictive entropy, (ii) layer-wise representation evolution (cosine distance between early-layer and final-layer hidden states, capturing how much semantic information remains unresolved), and (iii) attention scores from a recent observation window.
  • Top-k Restricted Entropy: Restricting entropy computation to the top-k (best k=256) most probable tokens yields more stable uncertainty estimates than full-vocabulary entropy, filtering out noise from low-probability mass.
  • Unified long-prefilling and long-decoding evaluation: Validated on LongReason (Llama-3.1-8B, Llama-3.2-3B at 16k–64k contexts, 20%/40% budgets) and on reasoning models DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B / -Llama-8B with up to 32k-token decoding on IFEval, AIME 2024, LiveCodeBench. On IFEval, InfoKV at 25% and 12.5% retention even surpasses the full cache of R1-Distill-Llama-8B.
  • Thorough ablations & adaptive-budget analysis: Studies bias τ (default 1), top-k, and the entropy/attention mixing coefficient α (best at 0.9); proposes layer-wise adaptive budget allocation proportional to per-layer accumulated entropy, which helps Llama but destabilizes Qwen, motivating uniform budgets as default.

Methodology

InfoKV is motivated by an information-theoretic reinterpretation of token importance during inference.

  1. Forward Influence (motivating analysis). For a token x_i in the KV cache and a future chunk {x_lc, …, x_rc}, Forward Influence is the average KL divergence between the original predictive distribution pθ(x_n | x_ and the distribution obtained after removing x_i (pθ(x_n | x_). Experiments on Arxiv-Summarization (Llama-3.1-8B) show that for the top-1% tokens, attention-based selection maximizes short-range (128-token) influence, entropy-based selection maximizes long-range (14K-token) influence, and the combination balances both.
  1. Per-layer entropy score. For token x_i, compute predictive entropy H_i of the next-token distribution and the cosine distance D_i^(l) = 1 − cos(h_i^(l), h_i^(L)) between hidden states at an early layer l and the final layer L. The intuition: residual-connected transformers evolve representations progressively, so tokens whose representations still shift substantially toward the final layer carry unresolved semantic information. The entropy score is E_i^(l) = D_i^(l) · H_i, with Top-k Restricted Entropy (k=256) used in place of full-vocabulary entropy, and a bias τ added to D_i^(l) so the final-layer entropy score does not vanish.
  1. Combined importance score. Per layer: S_i^(l) = α · A_i^(l) + (1 − α) · Softmax(E^(l))_i, where A_i^(l) is the attention weight from the observation window and α=0.9 by default. The top-ranked tokens are retained per layer.
  1. Application modes. In long prefilling, the prompt KV cache is compressed before decoding. In long decoding, KV cache of generated tokens is periodically compressed (every 1024 tokens), generalizing the RPC online-compression paradigm but with InfoKV scores.
  1. Adaptive budget (optional). Allocates per-layer KV budgets proportional to each layer's accumulated entropy score; helps Llama-8B but destabilizes Qwen-7B, so uniform budgeting is the default.

Results & Highlights

  • LongReason (prefilling): InfoKV is SOTA or highly competitive across 16k/32k/64k contexts and 20%/40% budgets on both Llama-3.1-8B and Llama-3.2-3B, in CoT and direct-answer settings, beating SnapKV, PyramidKV, and Expected Attention. The advantage widens as sequence length grows.
  • Long decoding (DeepSeek-R1-Distill): Outperforms RPC on IFEval, AIME 2024 (8 samples), and LiveCodeBench (4 samples, pass@1) at 25% and 12.5% retention. On IFEval, InfoKV at reduced budgets surpasses the full cache of R1-Distill-Llama-8B — evidence that long reasoning trajectories are redundant and aggressive compression of low-information tokens can actually improve generation.
  • Ablations: τ=1 is best and most stable; Top-256 restricted entropy beats full-vocabulary entropy; α=0.9 (entropy-dominant) is optimal, while α=1 (pure attention) is clearly worse — confirming entropy and attention are complementary.
  • Forward Influence analysis (Figs. 1–2): Attention-selected tokens' influence decays rapidly over generation distance; entropy-selected tokens maintain strong influence on distant future chunks.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty — Moderate. The core idea (entropy/uncertainty as a token-importance signal) is not new — Selective Context [Li et al., 2023], SH2 [Kai et al., 2024], and SeLaR [Fu and Luo, 2026] already exploit self-information/entropy. The genuine contribution is the integration into KV cache compression for long reasoning, plus the Forward Influence diagnostic that empirically justifies why entropy matters for distant-future contexts. The combination of predictive entropy with layer-wise representation evolution is a reasonable but incremental design.
  • Rigor — Good. Forward Influence is a clean, well-defined causal probe (remove a token, measure distributional shift). Ablations on τ, top-k, and α are thorough and the recommended hyperparameters (τ=1, k=256, α=0.9) are supported. Evaluation spans prefilling and decoding, two model families, and four benchmarks across multiple budgets.
  • Reproducibility — Moderate. Hyperparameters are reported and baselines use official implementations under shared configs. However, code is only "to be released," the early layer l and observation window size are not fully specified in the main text (deferred to appendix), and Forward Influence figures rely on an internal analysis script not described in full.
  • Significance — High for the niche. KV cache compression for long reasoning is a hot, high-impact area, and InfoKV directly competes with and beats current SOTA baselines (SnapKV, RPC). It is immediately useful and complementary to attention-based methods.
  • Red flags.
  • The "InfoKV at 12.5% beats full cache on IFEval" result, while striking, is a single-benchmark effect (the Llama-8B IFEval row) and could partly reflect that full-cache R1-Distill-Llama-8B underperforms expectations or that IFEval rewards focused generations; generalizing this to "compression helps reasoning" should be done cautiously.
  • The first author's prior works (SH2, FreqKV) occupy the same conceptual territory; InfoKV risks reading as a progressive refinement rather than a step change.
  • Forward Influence itself is used only as motivation/analysis — the deployed InfoKV uses entropy as an indirect proxy for future utility, which the authors themselves flag as a limitation (entropy is not an explicit optimization objective).
  • Adaptive layer-wise budgeting gives negative results on Qwen-7B, so the headline "adaptive" extension is effectively retracted in favor of uniform budgets.

Strengths

  • Clean, intuitive central insight: forward-looking (entropy) vs. backward-looking (attention) token utility, validated by a principled Forward Influence probe.
  • Complementarity is demonstrated, not assumed — α sweeps show pure attention (α=1) is worse and pure entropy is also worse than the blend.
  • Broad, current evaluation: long prefilling + long decoding, instruction-following + math + code, two model families (Llama, DeepSeek-R1-Distill), multiple budgets and context lengths.
  • Simple, plug-in design that layers on top of existing observation-window pipelines (SnapKV/RPC), so adoption cost is low.
  • Honest reporting of a negative result (adaptive budget on Qwen) and clear discussion of limitations.

Limitations

  • Entropy is an indirect proxy for future utility, not an explicit optimization target; Forward Influence is used only for analysis, not as a direct training/compression objective.
  • The adaptive layer-wise budget helps some models but destabilizes others, so the more sophisticated variant is not actually recommended.
  • Code is not yet released at submission; some implementation details (choice of early layer l, observation-window size, exact schedule of Top-k) are deferred to appendices not fully surfaced here.
  • Forward Influence analysis is conducted with Llama-3.1-8B on summarization; whether the entropy-→-long-range-influence relationship holds identically for reasoning-style distributions is not directly re-probed.
  • Gains over Expected Attention (which already tries to be forward-looking) and RPC are sometimes modest; the strongest claims rest on specific benchmarks/models.
  • No theoretical analysis of why entropy predicts long-range influence beyond the intuitive "uncertain tokens carry more information."

Relevance

与用户对 KV cache 压缩与长推理（long reasoning）的兴趣高度相关——本文正是同时覆盖这两个方向的直接研究，提出的"前瞻式 Forward Influence 分析 + 熵感知压缩"为长解码（如 DeepSeek-R1 级推理）下的 KV cache 管理提供了可立即集成到现有 SnapKV/RPC 流水线的新信号源。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can Forward Influence be used as a direct training/compression objective (e.g., differentiable surrogate) rather than only an offline analysis tool, and would that close the proxy gap the authors acknowledge?
  • Why does entropy-driven selection and adaptive layer-wise budgeting behave differently on Qwen vs. Llama architectures — is it about attention sink patterns, RoPE vs. other positional encoding, or layer normalization differences?
  • Does the entropy-→-long-range-influence relationship hold for reasoning-chain distributions specifically (high-density token repetition, self-correction loops), or does the summarization-based analysis need to be re-run on reasoning traces?
  • How does InfoKV interact with training-based compression (e.g., training-short-test-long methods like FreqKV by the same first author) — could the two be composed?
  • What is the wall-clock / latency overhead of computing per-layer cosine-distance representation evolution and Top-256 entropy during online decoding, and does it erode the memory/speed gains on real serving systems?
  • Could the α mixing coefficient be made context- or layer-adaptive automatically (rather than fixed at 0.9) to recover the gains the uniform-vs-adaptive trade-off currently leaves on the table?
