Key Contributions

  • Identifies and quantifies the core inefficiency of Muon: its matrix-coupled updates and Newton-Schulz iterations make vanilla implementations cost more than 2x the forward+backward pass, a mismatch with element-wise-optimizer-centric distributed infrastructure.
  • Delivers DMuon, an open-source distributed Muon implementation that integrates into existing training pipelines as a drop-in module with no framework-level modifications.
  • Demonstrates large speedups across two distinct workload families — embodied foundation models and large language models — with 1.48x–3.01x end-to-end step-time speedup and 6.85x–163x optimizer-step-time speedup.
  • Closes the per-step latency gap between Muon and AdamW, making matrix-orthogonalization optimizers practical for large-scale training.

Methodology

Per the abstract, DMuon is a distributed implementation of Muon that targets the bottleneck of the Newton-Schulz iterations used to orthogonalize matrix updates. The system is designed to be a drop-in module that slots into existing distributed training pipelines without framework-level changes. It is evaluated on both embodied foundation-model training and LLM training workloads, measuring end-to-end step time and isolated optimizer-step time against baseline (vanilla) Muon, with per-step latency benchmarked against AdamW.
