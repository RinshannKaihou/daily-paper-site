Humans Disengage, Reasoning Models Persist: Separating Difficulty Registration from Deliberation Allocation

arXiv: 2606.26502

Date: 2026-06-25

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26502

一句话总结： 本文提出一种"两层"诊断方法区分"难度感知"与"算力分配"，发现大型推理模型与人类在跨题难度相关性上一致，但在同一题内部呈现截然相反的模式——人类答错时会放弃（用时变短），而推理模型答错时反而生成更多思维链token（Cohen's d 高达 1.47–3.13），揭示了二者并未执行相同的元认知控制策略。
TL;DR: By separating difficulty registration (cross-item correlation between trace length and human RT) from deliberation allocation (whether an agent spends more compute on its own successes or failures), this paper shows that thinking LRMs and humans align at the cross-item level but diverge within items: humans abandon failed trials faster, while every thinking LRM spends more tokens on the trials it gets wrong (Cohen's d = 1.47–3.13 on H-ARC). The two policies produce the same surface correlation with difficulty, masking an opposite underlying stopping rule.

核心贡献

  • 提出一个两层诊断框架，将"难度感知"（registration：算力是否随题目变难而增加）与"算力分配"（allocation：在同一题目内，agent 是否在答错的试次上花更多时间）分离，从而填补 de Varda et al. (2025) "跨题相关"研究所缺失的维度。
  • 引入一个结果条件化、题目固定效应控制的诊断：在每个 agent 自身尺度上比较"答错试次"与"答对试次"的算力消耗，避免将人类秒数与模型 token 数放在同一坐标轴上。
  • 实证揭示表象对齐下的内在背离：6 个开源思考模型在 H-ARC、INTUIT 上全部呈现"答错更长"（正 d 值），而人类样本呈相反或更负的符号；交互项 β_LRM 在 H-ARC 为 −0.66 (p<.001)，并在多种稳健性设定下成立。
  • 通过 trace 内容分析与人类 action-count 中介分析，提出机制解读：人类表现为"投入 vs 放弃"（engagement vs abandonment），LRM 表现为"不确定时拉长"（length-on-uncertainty），并据此提出受控截断实验作为关键后续验证手段。
  • 在 resource-rational metareasoning 框架下做出形式化解读：两系统共享难度信号，却实施相反的停止策略。

Key Contributions

  • A two-level diagnostic that separates difficulty registration (how deliberation length tracks which items are hard) from deliberation allocation (given a hard item, does the agent spend more compute on its own failures or successes) — directly addressing a gap flagged by commentaries on de Varda et al. (2025).
  • An outcome-conditioned, item-fixed-effects diagnostic computed within each agent's native scale (seconds for humans, reasoning-token length for LRMs), so the two scales are never directly compared. This is orthogonal to and complements cross-item alignment.
  • Empirical demonstration of a sign reversal: every thinking LRM shows a large wrong-vs-right effect (Cohen's d = 1.47–3.13 on H-ARC), while the matched human sample is negative or near-zero; the item-FE agent-type interaction β_LRM = −0.66 (p<.001, 95% CI [−0.81, −0.50]) and survives four robustness specifications plus a Mundlak re-parameterization.
  • Generalization across paradigms: clean replication on INTUIT (intuitive physics) and a documented boundary case on Cortes (binary relational reasoning), with rank-stable per-model dissociation (Qwen-QwQ-32B strongest on every paradigm).
  • Mechanistic probes: human engagement-vs-abandonment (mediated by grid-action counts, which cut the within-item slope ~48%) and LRM length-on-uncertainty (trace-content asymmetries: +0.43 hedging markers/1000 chars on wrong H-ARC trials; +7.4% 5-gram repetition on wrong Cortes trials), motivating a controlled truncation experiment as the discriminating follow-up.
  • A resource-rational synthesis formalizing the divergence as two different stopping policies sharing a difficulty signal, with trace length capturing the signal but missing the control.

Methodology

The study is a secondary reanalysis of public, de-identified data from the de Varda et al. (2025) matched human–LRM corpus (H-ARC, INTUIT, Cortes), with arithmetic excluded as ceiling-saturated. The LRM sample is six open-weight thinking models (DeepSeek-R1, Qwen-QwQ-32B, Qwen3-235B-Thinking, GLM-4.5-Air-FP8, gpt-oss-20b, gpt-oss-120b), with DeepSeek-V3 as a non-thinking control.

The core measure is the within-agent d-ratio: a pooled-SD Cohen's d on log deliberation (human RT in seconds, LRM reasoning_token_length), computed as wrong-minus-right. Positive values mean the agent deliberates longer on failures. To break the difficulty tautology, the author uses two controls: (a) a leave-one-out ensemble-difficulty regression that partials out mean correctness on each item computed across all non-focal agents; and (b) item fixed-effects regressions that absorb item identity, so the correctness slope is identified purely from within-item variation. The headline specification is log t = α + β·c + β_LRM·c×LRM + δ_agent + γ_item + ε, pooling humans and LRMs, with cluster-robust SE by item.

Trace-content analysis fits feature ∼ correct + log w + C(agent) + C(item) on length-normalized features (self-doubt markers, self-correction markers, 5-gram repetition, type-token ratio). All analyses are deterministic (seed 0xA12C), with code and a Makefile reproducing all results end-to-end deposited at osf.io/jxb4a. No new human-subjects data were collected; IRB exemption was determined under secondary-use rules.

Results & Highlights

  • Registration is reproduced: thinking-LRM trace length correlates positively with human RT on H-ARC (Spearman ρ = 0.16–0.30, p<.001 for all five matched LRMs); non-thinking DeepSeek-V3 does not (ρ = −0.05).
  • Allocation is reversed: every well-powered thinking LRM lands at d = 1.47–3.13 (wrong > right), while the matched human sample sits at d = −0.10. On the same paradigm, two agents passing the same registration test can fail the allocation test in opposite directions.
  • Robust dissociation: the item-FE interaction β_LRM = −0.66 (p<.001) on H-ARC, triangulated by item random intercept, crossed agent+item RE, and two cluster-robust OLS variants (all CIs strictly below zero). A Mundlak decomposition yields an even larger dissociation (−0.79).
  • Cross-paradigm: INTUIT replicates cleanly (d = 0.59–1.42 across six models; β_LRM = −0.41, p<.001). Cortes is a documented boundary case — the agent-type interaction survives (β_LRM = −0.97) but the LRM-only within-item slope flips sign (+0.31).
  • Mechanism footprints: humans' within-item slope is concentrated on harder items and is mediated ~48% by grid-action counts (engagement-vs-abandonment). Wrong LRM traces carry more hedging markers (H-ARC) and higher 5-gram repetition / lower type-token ratio (Cortes), consistent with length-on-uncertainty.
  • Per-model rank stability: the dissociation ordering is preserved across paradigms (Spearman ρ = 0.77–0.89 across paradigm pairs; n=6, descriptive).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High and well-targeted. The registration/allocation split is conceptually sharp and fills a clearly identified gap. The diagnostic itself (outcome-conditioned within-agent d with item FE) is methodologically clean, though it builds on standard psychometric tools rather than inventing new statistics.
  • Rigor: Strong. Multiple identification strategies (item FE, Mundlak, mixed effects, cluster-robust variants), honest reporting of underpowered cells, and explicit acknowledgment that the public release provides only one observation per (item × model), making the LRM slope an item-controlled cross-model contrast rather than a stochastic per-model slope.
  • Reproducibility: Excellent. All data are public; code and Makefile are deposited; deterministic seed specified; SI thoroughly documents every specification.
  • Significance: Conceptually important for the "LRM-as-cognitive-model" literature, demonstrating that cross-item alignment is not a mechanism claim. The practical implication — that thinking-LRMs waste compute on irreducibly uncertain items — is actionable for inference-cost control.
  • Red flags:
  • The within-item LRM slope is identified across models on the same item, not from repeated stochastic samples of a single model (the author is transparent about this, but it weakens the per-model causal reading).
  • Mechanism claims rest on observational trace features and a simulated truncation curve (Figure 5 is illustrative, not empirical) — the discriminating experiment is deferred to future work.
  • Cortes, one of three paradigms, shows a sign flip on the LRM-only slope, framed as a "boundary case" rather than a failed replication.
  • Single-author, no external co-signers; the human-side "engagement-vs-abandonment" reading relies on participant-level identifiers that are unavailable, so individual differences cannot be separated from trial-level engagement.
  • Closed-frontier reasoning systems (o-series, Gemini) are untested, so generalization to frontier reasoning is unverified.

Strengths

  • Genuinely novel two-level decomposition that reframes how human–LRM deliberation should be compared.
  • Exceptionally careful about scale: never compares seconds to tokens; uses each agent as its own baseline.
  • Robust to multiple specifications, paradigms, and difficulty controls; transparent about boundary cases.
  • Mechanism probes (action-count mediation, trace-content asymmetries) are convergent and theory-linked.
  • Resource-rational synthesis gives a formal, falsifiable framing rather than hand-waving.
  • Outstanding reproducibility infrastructure (public OSF code, deterministic seed, SI documentation).

Limitations

  • Observational data: cannot establish the causal value of the extra wrong-trial tokens; the truncation experiment remains the necessary follow-up.
  • Only six open-weight thinking LRMs from four organizations; no closed-frontier systems; per-model rank-stability at n=6 is descriptive only.
  • "Humans" = the specific populations in the source releases, without participant identifiers, preventing separation of individual-difference from trial-level effects.
  • The non-thinking DeepSeek-V3 control shows a similar (though smaller) wrong-trial expansion, suggesting part of the effect may be a base-generation property rather than reasoning-specific.
  • Cortes boundary case shows the LRM-only slope reverses sign; the dissociation there survives only because the human slope is more positive.
  • Reasoning-token length is a behavioral readout, not a guaranteed report of latent computation (per Valmeekam, Kambhampati, et al.); RT likewise decomposes into many subprocesses.

Relevance

本文直接切中推理模型与"输出一致性"研究的核心议题：它表明推理模型在跨题难度上与人类对齐，但同一题内部的算力分配策略完全相反——这种隐藏的不一致正是评估推理模型行为稳健性与可解释性时需要警惕的关键维度。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the controlled-truncation experiment (sampling at fixed token budgets f ∈ (0,1) on the dissociation items) yield a flat high-accuracy plateau (padding reading) or a late steep rise (useful-search reading)?
  • Do closed-frontier reasoning systems (o-series, Gemini) show the same wrong-trial length expansion, or does additional post-training tighten the monitor–stop coupling?
  • Is the LRM pattern optimal under an alternative objective (RL verifier rewards, length-conditioned preferences) rather than item accuracy — and can that be tested directly?
  • Would repeated stochastic samples per (item × model) — beyond the single-observation public release — change the per-model p(correct | t, item) estimate and the allocation sign?
  • Can the monitor–controller decoupling be linked to a specific architectural or training-pipeline signature (given the rank-stable per-model dissociation)?
  • How does the allocation diagnostic behave on non-reasoning tasks or under different decoding temperatures, where the length-on-uncertainty policy might be modulated?
