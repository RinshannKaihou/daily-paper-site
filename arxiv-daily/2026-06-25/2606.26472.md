> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 EpiKV，一种无需注意力矩阵的 KV cache 驱逐方法——通过读取模型残差流隐藏状态的"顿悟分数"（中段正带与上中段负带的因果滚动 z-score 之差）来给 token 重要性打分，在 FlashAttention 推理栈中原生运行，可在 A100 上支持比基于注意力打分长 16 倍的上下文，并在 MATH-500/AIME-2024 上达到与最强注意力基线持平或更优的精度且最高快 2.8 倍。

> **TL;DR:** EpiKV replaces attention-weight-based KV cache eviction with an "epiphany score" read directly from hidden-state changes in the residual stream (a positive mid-layer band minus a negative upper-mid-layer band, detrended by a causal rolling z-score). It needs no attention matrix, no training, no classifier, and no custom kernel, so it runs unmodified in FlashAttention serving stacks, enabling up to 16x longer feasible context and matching or beating the strongest attention-based baselines at up to 2.8x the speed.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 EpiKV，首个面向推理链的解码时 KV cache 驱逐方法，其重要性信号完全不依赖注意力矩阵，而是直接读取前向传播中已有的隐藏状态与缓存 KV 向量，因此与 FlashAttention 等 fused kernel 完全兼容。
- 发现"双带层结构"（two-band anatomy）：在 32 层推理模型中，Band A（第 7–13 层）的隐藏状态变化与 token 重要性正相关，Band B（第 18–25 层）负相关；两者结合的信号优于所有测试过的基于注意力的信号。该正带与机制可解释性研究（ROME/MEMIT）定位的事实检索中层一致。
- 方法论发现：原始隐藏状态信号在同一条推理链内带有单调位置趋势（追踪位置而非内容），导致跨问题聚合相关性高估了链内排序质量；作者用因果滚动 z-score 去除该趋势，恢复驱逐质量——这是任何基于残差流的重要性信号共有的漂移问题。
- 在可部署预算上达到与最强注意力基线持平或更优：MATH-500 上 4096-token 预算达到 72%（ThinKV 71%，H2O 67%），且永不物化注意力矩阵；AIME-2024 上 8192-token 时 lag-normalized KV 变体达 37%（最佳注意力基线 33%），最高快 2.8 倍。
- 量化工程收益：不读取注意力矩阵让前向传播在 80GB A100 上从 8192 token 即 OOM 扩展到 65,536 token（可行上下文长 16 倍）；在固定 2048 预算下，同一 GPU 可容纳的并发请求数从 14 提升到 224。
- 发布反事实遮挡重要性标签作为验证资源（MATH-500 约 20% token 重要，AIME 约 52–64%）。

## Key Contributions

- EpiKV is the first decode-time KV cache eviction method for reasoning traces whose importance signal is both non-attention-derived and attention-matrix-free. It reads hidden-state changes and cached KV vectors from the standard `output_hidden_states` forward pass, never setting `output_attentions=True`, so it runs inside FlashAttention (FA2) with zero kernel changes.
- A "two-band layer anatomy" finding, validated against counterfactual occlusion labels: Band A (layers 7–13) shows consistently positive Spearman correlation between hidden-state change and token importance; Band B (18–25) shows consistently negative correlation. Their combined signal outperforms every attention-based signal tested. The positive band coincides with the mid-network factual-retrieval layers identified by ROME/MEMIT.
- A methodological contribution: the raw residual-stream signal carries a monotonic within-trace positional trend that inflates aggregate correlation but distorts within-trace ranking. A causal rolling z-score removes it — a general correction applicable to any residual-stream importance signal.
- Parity-or-better accuracy at deployable budgets without materializing the attention matrix: 72% on MATH-500 at 4096 tokens (ThinKV 71%, H2O 67%); 37% on AIME-2024 at 8192 via a lag-normalized KV variant (best attention baseline 33%).
- Deployment engineering payoff: 16x longer feasible context (FlashAttention pass scales to 65,536 tokens vs. eager OOM at 8192 on an 80 GB A100), up to 2.8x faster than attention-based eviction at equal budget, and 16x more concurrent 32k-token requests at a fixed 2048 budget.
- Release of counterfactual occlusion importance labels as a validation resource.

## Methodology

The setting is decode-time KV cache eviction: a policy scores cached positions at each step and permanently retains the top-K. The core innovation is the **importance signal** and its **FlashAttention compatibility**.

**Epiphany score from the residual stream.** For layer $l$ and decode position $t$, define the hidden-state change $g_l(t) = \|h_l(t) - h_l(t-1)\|_2$. A large value marks a "consequential" token (a concluded step, a transition from exploratory to convergent reasoning) — termed an *epiphany token* — rather than fluent filler.

**Two-band anatomy.** A per-layer Spearman correlation study against counterfactual importance labels reveals two bands: Band A (layers 7–13, positive $\rho$) and Band B (layers 18–25, negative $\rho$). These are combined into $s(t) = \bar{g}_{10}(t) - \bar{g}_{21}(t)$ where the bars denote rolling means over a causal trailing window of $w=64$ tokens. The split is consistent with mid-layer factual retrieval (ROME/MEMIT).

**Causal rolling z-score detrending (EpiKV proper).** Because $\bar{g}_{10}$ tends to decrease and $\bar{g}_{21}$ to increase with position, the raw score tracks position as much as content. The authors normalize each band into $z_l(t) = (g_l(t) - \mu_l(t))/(\sigma_l(t) + \varepsilon)$ over the trailing window and score with $z_{10}(t) - z_{21}(t)$, converting absolute magnitude into local, position-agnostic deviation.

**Policy families.** Scoring is computed once when a token is generated and then frozen (fully online and causal). Three FA2-compatible families are introduced: hidden-state (HS-variance, EpiKV, band-adaptive), KV-vector (kv-key/kv-val variance, and a lag-normalized "Lag-KV" adaptation of LagKV that normalizes each key/value vector by the previous chunk's per-channel range), and hybrids that combine attention with hidden-state at the cost of FA2 compatibility.

**Counterfactual labels.** For each correctly answered trace, a 32-token window (stride 16) is slid over the reasoning span, replaced with padding, and the answer regenerated greedily; a window is labeled important if the answer changes. This fixes context length per window so the label measures content, not position.

**Setup.** Model: DeepSeek-R1-Distill-LLaMA-8B (32 layers). Benchmarks: MATH-500 (n=100, primary), AIME-2024 (n=30), GSM8K (difficulty-regime probe only). Budgets K ∈ {512…4096} (MATH-500) and {512…8192} (AIME-2024). Greedy generation throughout. Attention-requiring policies run in eager mode; FA2-compatible ones with `flash_attention_2`.

## Results & Highlights

- **H2O collapses on reasoning traces.** Accuracy falls from 67% (4096) to 5% (1024) on MATH-500, driven not by wrong answers but by empty/unstructured output (93/100 problems produce no answer at K=1024) — the empirical case against attention-derived signals.
- **Two-band anatomy holds** across both competition-math datasets and both attention back-ends; cumulative attention is the weakest signal measured ($|\rho| \le 0.09$), below every hidden-state band layer. The rolling-64 window improves correlation 32–57% over the raw signal.
- **Accuracy at deployable budgets.** MATH-500 at 4096: EpiKV 72%, ThinKV 71%, H2O 67%, ceiling 75%. The FA2-compatible family clusters at 70–72%. AIME-2024 at 8192: lag-normalized KV reaches 37% vs. 33% for the best attention-requiring method.
- **Speed.** On AIME-2024 at 8192, lag-kv is 1.6x faster than ThinKV (440s vs. 721s) and up to 2.8x faster than RaaS (1239s). The FA2-compatible methods dominate the accuracy–speed Pareto frontier.
- **Memory / feasible context.** Reading attention weights OOMs an 80 GB A100 at 8192 tokens; the FA2 pass EpiKV reads from scales to 65,536 tokens at 48 GB — a 16x longer feasible context. At a fixed 2048 budget the same GPU holds 224 concurrent 32k-token requests vs. 14 without eviction.
- **Difficulty-regime finding.** The two-band anatomy is specific to competition math: on GSM8K the positive band shifts to early layers and both attention entropy and key variance reverse sign, indicating the importance signal lives deeper for harder problems — evidence the signal tracks where load-bearing computation happens, not a fixed layer index.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high).** The combination is genuinely new: a non-attention importance signal that is also attention-matrix-free, plus the first "layer anatomy" account (two bands, with interpretation tying to mechanistic-interpretability results). The positional-trend correction and its framing as a general property of residual-stream signals is a non-trivial methodological insight. The closest prior work, LagKV, is attention-free but KV-only and generic; EpiKV adds the hidden-state signal, the two-band account, and FA2 compatibility.
- **Rigor (mixed-to-good).** The counterfactual occlusion labeling protocol is sound (fixed context length per window, greedy deterministic regeneration). The layer correlation study is done across two datasets and two attention back-ends. However, the headline AIME results rest on n=30, where the 4-point gap at 8192 (37% vs 33%) is a single problem — the authors acknowledge this directly but the abstract's "37% vs 33%" framing slightly overstates the robustness. The MATH-500 72% vs 71% margin is likewise one problem of 100. The paper is commendably explicit that these are "parity-or-better," not decisive wins.
- **Reproducibility (good).** No training, no classifier, no custom kernel; uses standard HuggingFace `DynamicCache` and `output_hidden_states`. Hyperparameters (layers 10/21, window 64, ε, recency min(128, K/4)) are fully specified. Counterfactual labels are released. Caveat: all results are from a single 8B model family; cross-architecture transfer is untested, and the band layer indices are explicitly a per-regime hyperparameter.
- **Significance (high for practitioners).** The deployment angle is the real payload: avoiding the attention matrix removes a hard memory wall (eager OOM at 8192 vs. FA2 scaling to 65,536) and lets eviction drop into vLLM/TGI/SGLang unchanged. For the class of "attention is a noisy proxy AND architecturally expensive" problem, this is a clean solution.
- **Red flags.** (1) Generalization is narrow — one model, one task domain (competition math), with the band locations shifting on GSM8K, so the "layers 7–13 / 18–25" finding is not universal. (2) Throughput/batch claims are projected from KV-cache arithmetic (Appendix I), not measured end-to-end; the prefill-memory advantage is a forward-pass microbenchmark, not a long-prompt deployment. (3) At tight budgets (≤1024) an eager hybrid still leads, and no FA2-compatible method matches it — a gap the paper flags but does not close. (4) Some wall-clock comparisons are confounded (H2O is "fast" at tight budgets only because it collapses to empty output). None of these are deceptive — all are disclosed — but they bound the strength of the claims.

## Strengths

- Eliminates the attention-matrix dependency entirely, yielding concrete deployment wins (16x context, 2.8x speed, unchanged serving stacks) rather than incremental accuracy gains.
- The two-band anatomy is a real interpretability finding with a plausible mechanistic explanation (mid-layer retrieval vs. upper-mid output-preparation layers), and it is stress-tested on a different difficulty regime (GSM8K) where it predictably shifts.
- Exceptionally honest about limitations, statistical power, and the fact that results are parity-or-better rather than dominance.
- Simple, training-free, drop-in method with negligible extra state and a fully online causal scoring rule.

## Limitations

- Single model family (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-LLaMA-8B); cross-architecture and cross-scale transfer untested. Band layer indices are a per-regime hyperparameter, not a fixed property.
- Headline AIME-2024 numbers are n=30 (one-problem margins, CIs spanning zero); MATH-500 72-vs-71 is also one problem. The authors flag pooling to n≈90 as needed.
- Batched/multi-GPU throughput is projected from arithmetic, not measured; multi-GPU FlashAttention runs hit a kernel-coordination launch failure, so all FA benchmarks are single-GPU.
- At the tightest budgets (≤1024 tokens) no FA2-compatible method matches an eager hybrid — the regime where reasoning traces are longest relative to budget is precisely where the method is weakest.
- The method targets decode-time eviction; prefill-time memory and the prompt-heavy regime (e.g., long-input summarization) are not addressed.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the two bands (and the layer-10/21 split) transfer to non-LLaMA architectures (e.g., Qwen-style, Mistral) and larger reasoning models (32B/70B), or are the indices always task- and architecture-specific?
- Could an attention-matrix-free analog of the segment-classification hybrid (e.g., segment type inferred from KV statistics rather than attention entropy) close the tight-budget (≤1024) gap where the eager hybrid still leads?
- The positional-trend confound is presented as a general property of residual-stream signals — does the same causal rolling z-score help other hidden-state-based methods (e.g., EAGLE speculative decoding drafts) or attention-derived scores?
- How does EpiKV interact with orthogonal compression (quantization, retrieval)? The authors claim stackability but do not measure the combined system end-to-end.
- Is the negative Band B (upper-mid layers predicting dispensability) a stable, interpretable phenomenon, or an artifact of the specific distillation? A causal-intervention study (e.g., ablating those layers) would test whether the band reflects output-preparation as hypothesized.
