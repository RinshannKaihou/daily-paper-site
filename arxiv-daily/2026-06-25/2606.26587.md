> **一句话总结：** SharQ 提出一种无需训练的在线"稀疏-稠密"分解方法：对每个激活张量生成输入自适应的 N:M 稀疏掩码提取离群值主导的稀疏骨干，再以量化后的稀疏骨干为基准构造稠密 FP4 残差，让稠密 FP4 GEMM 同时补偿掩码丢弃损失与稀疏路径量化误差，在 NVFP4/HiF4/MXFP4 三种格式上恢复 43–63% 的精度差距，并在 RTX 5090 上实现 2.2–2.4× 于 FP16 的延迟降低。

> **TL;DR:** SharQ is a training-free LLM inference primitive that combines N:M activation sparsity with block-scaled FP4 quantization via an online sparse-dense decomposition; the dense FP4 residual path, defined relative to the *quantized* sparse backbone, jointly compensates sparsification and quantization error, recovering 43-63% of the NVFP4-to-FP16 accuracy gap across language and vision-language models while delivering 2.2-2.4x latency reduction over FP16 and 1.2-1.4x throughput gain over FP8 on an RTX 5090.

## 核心贡献

- 提出SharQ——一种无需训练、无需校准数据的在线激活分解方法，将激活分解为N:M稀疏骨干和稠密FP4残差两条路径，把"稀疏化"重新定义为"路由"而非"丢弃"。
- 关键创新点：稠密残差是相对于**已量化的稀疏骨干**定义的（`R = X − X̃_sp`），因此残差同时包含掩码外数值与稀疏路径量化误差，使稠密路径能联合补偿两类误差，而非独立叠加。
- 工程实现三项关键设计使分解真正高效可落地：融合准备内核（将掩码生成、残差构造、LayerNorm/RMSNorm 融合为单一算子）、共享FP4权重载荷+路径特定scale视图（避免权重重复存储）、累加式残差补偿（保持接近单核的数据流）。
- 跨模型与格式验证：在 Llama-3.1-8B、Qwen2.5-7B、Qwen3-30B-A3B（MoE）、Qwen3-VL-8B（多模态）及 Wan2.2-T2V-A14B（视频生成）上均有效，且在 NVFP4、HiF4、MXFP4 三种FP4格式上无需修改即可工作。

## Key Contributions

- **Online sparse-dense decomposition for activations.** SharQ performs training-free, calibration-free online decomposition: an input-adaptive N:M mask (e.g., 4:8 in-pairs for NVFP4, 2:4 for HiF4) extracts an outlier-dominated sparse backbone, while reframing sparsification as routing rather than dropping.
- **Residual defined relative to the quantized sparse backbone.** The dense residual `R = (X − X_sp) + (X_sp − X̃_sp)` jointly captures both mask-excluded values and the sparse path's quantization error, so a single dense FP4 GEMM compensates both error sources simultaneously rather than treating them as independent additive terms.
- **Hardware-oriented kernel design.** Three engineering choices make the decomposition practical: (1) a fused preparation kernel absorbing mask generation, residual construction, and normalization into one operator; (2) a single shared FP4 weight payload with path-specific scale views (avoiding weight duplication); (3) accumulation-based residual compensation that preserves near-single-kernel dataflow.
- **Broad, format-agnostic generalization.** Validated across dense decoders (Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen2.5-7B), an MoE model (Qwen3-30B-A3B), a vision-language model (Qwen3-VL-8B), and a diffusion video model (Wan2.2-T2V-A14B), working unmodified across NVFP4, HiF4, and MXFP4.

## Methodology

SharQ targets the linear-layer approximation problem `Y = XW` under block-scaled FP4 + N:M semi-structured sparsity constraints. The core insight is that naive combination of sparsification and quantization produces *coupled* errors, not independent ones: the N:M mask changes the local value distribution seen by the FP4 quantizer, and masked-out values alter the dense-path error structure.

The method proceeds in three stages:

1. **Sparse backbone extraction.** For each activation tensor, an online N:M mask `M(X)` selects top-k entries by magnitude within each hardware block (e.g., 4:8 in-pairs for NVFP4, 2:4 for HiF4). This gives `X_sp = M(X) ⊙ X`, an input-adaptive outlier-dominated backbone requiring no offline calibration.

2. **Residual construction relative to the quantized backbone.** The sparse backbone is quantized/dequantized: `X̃_sp = D_sp(Q_sp(X_sp))`. Crucially, the residual is defined as `R = X − X̃_sp`, decomposing into `(X − X_sp)` (values outside the mask) plus `(X_sp − X̃_sp)` (sparse quantization error). This folds both error sources into one signal.

3. **Two-path reconstruction.** Output is computed as `Ŷ = GEMM_sp(Q_sp(X_sp), Q_w,sp(W)) + GEMM_dn(Q_dn(R), Q_w,dn(W))`, where the dense residual path accumulates directly into the sparse output. Theoretically, final reconstruction error is `R − R̃`, bounded by `‖R − R̃‖_F ‖W̄‖_2`.

On the kernel side (Blackwell NVFP4 dataflow), SharQ fuses RMSNorm/PTS, pair-topk 4:8 mask generation, sparse compression, and dense residual construction into one kernel; quantizes a single shared NVFP4 weight payload once and exposes sparse group-32 scales and duplicated dense group-16 scales; and uses accumulation-based compensation to avoid an extra output writeback/readback. The net effect is a near-single-kernel dataflow exploiting two complementary execution paths.

## Results & Highlights

- **Accuracy recovery.** Across Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen2.5-7B, and Qwen3-30B-A3B, SharQ recovers 56%, 63%, and ~44% respectively of the average zero-shot accuracy gap between NVFP4 and FP16. On Llama-3.1-8B: average 70.32% → 71.55% (FP16 72.50%), WikiText2 PPL 6.94 → 6.73, MMLU 61.93 → 63.76.
- **Multimodal.** On Qwen3-VL-8B, SharQ recovers ~43% of the NVFP4-to-FP16 gap (79.97% → 80.57% avg), improving on every one of five benchmarks (VQAv2, GQA, SQA, POPE, TextVQA), with TextVQA showing the highest 52% recovery rate.
- **Language serving efficiency (RTX 5090, vLLM, batch 4).** 2.2-2.4x latency reduction over FP16 (e.g., 24.88s → 11.11s at seqlen 2048) and 1.2-1.4x higher total token throughput over FP8 (4491 vs 3146 tokens/s at seqlen 2048).
- **Video generation (4x RTX 5090, Wan2.2-T2V-A14B).** SharQ alone: 1.23x (480P) / 1.11x (720P) speedup. Combined with SageAttention: 1.58x (480P) / 1.57x (720P), reducing generation time >36%.
- **Format-agnostic ablation (Qwen3-30B-A3B).** Consistent gains on NVFP4, HiF4, and MXFP4, with the largest absolute improvements on the weakest baseline (MXFP4): WikiText2 PPL 9.70 → 9.19, Lambada +2.84pp.
- **Path-combination ablation (Llama-3.1-8B).** "Sparse-only" catastrophically collapses (WikiText2 PPL 82.95, MMLU 25.78%); "Sparse+Sparse" only matches NVFP4 baseline (confirming double-sparse without dense compensation cannot recover loss); SharQ's sparse+dense matches/exceeds "Dense+Dense" accuracy while retaining sparse execution.
- **Kernel-level.** Prefill overhead only +11.2% over NVFP4 (fused kernel saves 7.4pp of preprocessing); sparse NVFP4 GEMM runs at 0.66-0.72x of dense NVFP4 latency (28-34% GEMM speedup), while FP8 GEMMs are ~2x slower than dense NVFP4.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate-High).** The *idea* of activation decomposition is not novel (the paper explicitly states "SharQ does not treat activation decomposition itself as the novelty"). The genuine contributions are (a) defining the residual relative to the *quantized* sparse backbone rather than unquantized sparse values, (b) making online N:M activation masks cheap enough for the critical path via fusion, and (c) the shared-payload / path-specific-scale trick. The insight that sparsification and FP4 quantization produce *coupled* (not additive) errors is well-motivated and is the intellectual core. Within the increasingly crowded FP4 literature, the sparse+dense coupling angle is fresh.
- **Rigor (Solid).** Experiments span dense, MoE, vision-language, and diffusion models; ablations isolate each design choice (path combinations, FP4 formats, kernel breakdown). Accuracy and efficiency claims are both backed with measurements. Per-benchmark and per-format detail is provided.
- **Reproducibility (Strong).** Training-free and calibration-free by design (lowers the bar to reproduce); public code repository; standard benchmarks via LM Evaluation Harness. Specific hardware (RTX 5090, Blackwell Tensor Cores, NVFP4 4:8-in-pairs) is required, which may limit reproducibility for labs without Blackwell GPUs.
- **Significance (High for practitioners).** Recovering 43-63% of the FP4 accuracy gap at near-single-kernel cost, while *beating FP8* on throughput, is practically valuable. The format-agnostic claim strengthens its staying power.
- **Red flags / concerns.** (1) The headline 43-63% "gap recovery" is in absolute terms modest (~1 pp average accuracy), and NVFP4 itself is already a fairly strong baseline, so the practical accuracy win is incremental. (2) All results compare SharQ only against NVFP4/HiF4/MXFP4 baselines plus FP8 and FP16 — there is no comparison against other outlier-aware NVFP4 methods like ARCQuant, MR-GPTQ, Four Over Six, MicroMix, or FGMP on the same tasks, so SharQ's marginal standing within the FP4-method landscape is unclear. (3) Efficiency gains are heavily hardware-dependent (Blackwell-specific NVFP4 sparse GEMMs); the 720P video gain dropping to 1.11x shows the win is confined to linear layers. (4) "Sparse+Sparse ≈ NVFP4 baseline" is slightly surprising and could use more analysis. (5) No explicit comparison of memory footprint vs. FP8 baselines, and no long-context or agentic evaluation.

## Strengths

- Principled treatment of the coupled sparsification-quantization error problem, with a clean theoretical framing (`R − R̃` reconstruction bound).
- Genuinely training-free, calibration-free, and plug-and-play across architectures (dense, MoE, VLM, diffusion) and FP4 formats.
- Engineering is taken seriously: fused kernel, shared weight payload, accumulation-based compensation all contribute to making the decomposition *faster*, not just more accurate.
- Strong, honest ablations — particularly the Sparse-only collapse and the Sparse+Sparse ≈ baseline results, which validate the asymmetric design.
- Beats FP8 on throughput (1.2-1.4x) while operating at FP4, a compelling efficiency story.

## Limitations

- Absolute accuracy gains are modest (~1 pp average); the "43-63% gap recovery" framing is somewhat optimistic relative to raw magnitude.
- No head-to-head comparison against other contemporary NVFP4-specific methods (ARCQuant, MR-GPTQ, Four Over Six, MicroMix, FGMP) on shared benchmarks — leaves the relative ranking uncertain.
- Requires Blackwell-class hardware with native NVFP4 + N:M sparse GEMM support; results may not transfer to other accelerators or older NVIDIA architectures.
- Benefits are confined to linear layers; attention-dominated workloads (e.g., 720P video) see muted gains and must be combined with orthogonal techniques like SageAttention.
- Efficiency measured on a single GPU type and a narrow set of sequence lengths / batch sizes; no multi-GPU throughput serving numbers for the largest models.
- No evaluation on long-context, reasoning, or agentic benchmarks where activation outliers and dynamic ranges may behave differently.

## Relevance

SharQ 与用户对量化、FP4 及推理加速的研究兴趣高度契合：它是当前 FP4（NVFP4/HiF4/MXFP4）激活量化方向上结合硬件原生 N:M 稀疏的代表性工作，其"稠密残差补偿稀疏量化耦合误差"的思路对低比特推理内核设计有直接参考价值，尤其适合关注 Blackwell 架构稀疏 GEMM 与 FP4 serving 吞吐的研究。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does SharQ compare head-to-head with ARCQuant, MR-GPTQ, Four Over Six, MicroMix, and FGMP on identical models and benchmarks, especially on accuracy-per-FLOP?
- Can the sparse+residual decomposition be stacked with a global rotation (QuaRot/SpinQuant) without reintroducing the local-statistics disruption problem noted for block-scaled formats?
- What happens to online mask quality and FP4 scale selection on very long contexts (e.g., 32k+) or reasoning/agentic traces where outlier patterns may be more dynamic?
- Is the "residual relative to the quantized backbone" idea generalizable to INT4/INT8 group quantization, or does it rely specifically on FP4's floating-point dynamic range?
- How sensitive are the 2.2-2.4x latency gains to batch size, and does the dense residual path become a bottleneck at very large decode batch sizes where memory bandwidth is saturated?
- Could the shared-payload / path-specific-scale trick be extended to support more than two paths (e.g., a third FP8 path) under a unified weight representation?
