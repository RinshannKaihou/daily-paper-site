> **一句话总结：** 本文是首个以联邦学习（FL）为中心、基于 PRISMA 方法的量化技术系统综述，提出了一种围绕客户端异构性、聚合一致性、通信调度、non-IID 鲁棒性、隐私安全集成与硬件/能耗协同优化六大维度的新分类法，论证了量化不应仅被视为压缩手段，而是塑造 FL 性能、鲁棒性与实用性的基础系统组件。

> **TL;DR:** This is the first FL-centric, PRISMA-based systematic survey of quantization techniques, introducing a novel taxonomy organized around six FL-specific dimensions (client heterogeneity, aggregation consistency, communication scheduling, non-IID robustness, privacy/security integration, hardware/energy co-optimization) and positioning quantization as a fundamental systems component rather than merely a compression tool.

## 核心贡献

- 提出首个专门聚焦于联邦学习量化的系统综述（PRISMA 方法），填补了现有综述仅将量化作为通信效率子组件来讨论的空白。
- 构建了围绕六大 FL 专属维度的全新量化分类法，涵盖训练阶段（PTQ/QAT）、量化对象、编码结构与精度策略四个设计轴。
- 系统分析了量化与 FL 核心行为（client drift、部分参与、收敛稳定性、安全聚合、差分隐私）之间的相互作用。
- 识别了跨方法的共性洞察、开放研究空白，以及面向移动端、IoT、边缘部署的量化 FL 实践设计指南。
- 明确提出量化是塑造现代 FL 性能、鲁棒性与实用性的"基础系统组件"，而不仅仅是压缩技术。

## Key Contributions

- Delivers the first systematic literature review (PRISMA-based) exclusively dedicated to quantization in federated learning, contrasting with prior surveys that treat quantization only as a subcomponent of communication efficiency.
- Introduces a novel FL-centric taxonomy built around six system-level properties: client heterogeneity (CH), aggregation consistency (AC), communication-scheduling adaptation (CSA), non-IID robustness (NIR), privacy/security integration (PI), and hardware/energy co-optimization (HE).
- Catalogs and compares methods along four design dimensions: training stage (PTQ vs. QAT), quantized object (parameters/gradients/updates), encoding structure (scalar/vector, uniform/non-uniform), and precision strategy (fixed/variable/adaptive).
- Analyzes quantization's interplay with core FL behaviors — client drift, partial participation, convergence stability, secure aggregation, and differential privacy.
- Identifies cross-method insights, open research gaps, and practitioner-oriented design guidelines for deploying quantized FL on mobile, IoT, and edge platforms.

## Methodology

The paper follows a **PRISMA-based systematic literature review (SLR)** protocol. The authors searched six databases (IEEE Xplore, arXiv, Science Direct, ACM Digital Library, Wiley Online Library, and others) with the custom year range 2019–2026, using search strings combining "federated learning," "quantization," and "communication." From an initial pool of ~3,925 retrieved studies, articles were filtered through inclusion/exclusion criteria (peer-reviewed, English, quantization as a primary FL focus in flagship venues) down to ~60 eligible studies.

The survey is organized around **four research questions (RQ1–RQ4)**:
- RQ1: How do quantization methods impact FL performance, efficiency, and effectiveness?
- RQ2: What challenges and limitations characterize existing quantized FL approaches?
- RQ3: What research gaps persist across algorithmic, statistical, and system dimensions?
- RQ4: What emerging trends can guide scalable, robust, privacy-aware quantized FL?

The technical synthesis reviews PTQ methods (Dynamic Range Quantization, weight clipping/calibration with KLD/MSE), QAT variants (fake quantization with STE, PACT, LSQ/LSQ+, differentiable quantization, adaptive precision, federated hybrid QAT, QA-FedAvg/FedDQ/FedMixQAT), mixed precision quantization (HAQ, HAWQ, AdaQuant), and stochastic/vector quantization schemes (QSGD, signSGD, lattice/structured encoding). Each is analyzed through the lens of the six FL-specific taxonomy dimensions, supported by comparative tables and formal mathematical formulations (quantization error MSE ∝ 2^-2b, convergence bounds with quantization variance σ_q²).

## Results & Highlights

- Claims that well-calibrated quantization strategies can reduce communication bandwidth by **up to 90% with less than 2% accuracy degradation**, and achieve **10×–50× compression** with minimal accuracy loss.
- Reports that adaptive quantization methods adjusting bit-widths per client/round can reduce total energy consumption by **up to 40%**.
- Finds that PTQ is suitable for low-power/resource-limited clients, while QAT delivers superior robustness under heterogeneous and non-IID environments; hybrid PTQ–QAT is identified as a promising compromise.
- Establishes that non-IID data interacts non-linearly with quantization noise, making gradient bias and convergence instability persistent issues.
- Synthesizes the field's state via structured comparison tables (Tables 3–15) spanning DRQ schemes, clipping/calibration, QAT methods/frameworks, MPQ, stochastic/vector quantization, challenges, gaps, and future directions.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** As the authors claim, this is plausibly the **first survey exclusively focused on quantization in FL**. Prior surveys treat quantization as a subsection of communication efficiency. The FL-centric six-dimension taxonomy (CH/AC/CSA/NIR/PI/HE) is a genuine organizing contribution. However, the individual techniques cataloged (PTQ, QAT, MPQ, QSGD) are well-established and the survey is largely a synthesis rather than presenting new methods — which is expected and appropriate for a survey.
- **Rigor:** The PRISMA methodology is followed with a documented search protocol, database counts, and inclusion/exclusion criteria — a clear strength. However, the article references in the ACM header ("2018," "J. ACM 37, 4") are template boilerplate artifacts, and the period 2019–2026 plus the "up to early 2025" scope statement reveal some internal inconsistency in coverage dating.
- **Reproducibility:** As a survey, reproducibility concerns differ from empirical papers. The absence of a public code/data repository, the lack of a clearly stated final eligible-study count, and the absence of a standardized benchmark (which the paper itself identifies as a field-wide gap) limit the verifiability of its quantitative claims (90% bandwidth reduction, 10×–50× compression, 40% energy savings), which are attributed to cited primary studies rather than independently verified.
- **Significance:** The field is active and growing; a dedicated quantization-in-FL survey has clear reference value for practitioners deploying FL on edge/IoT. The taxonomy and gap analysis provide a useful roadmap.
- **Red flags:** Several headline quantitative results are cited second-hand without a meta-analytic reconciliation; the comparison tables are descriptive rather than quantitative (no head-to-head experimental comparison); the "first survey" claim should be read against possibly concurrent/companion works. As with any single-group narrative survey, there is risk of selection bias in the eligible-study set despite PRISMA framing.

**Note:** This is a *survey/review* paper, not an empirical methods paper. The appropriate evaluation lens is the quality, completeness, and organization of its synthesis — not novel experimental results.

## Strengths

- First dedicated, FL-centric survey of quantization with a genuinely useful multi-dimensional taxonomy.
- Transparent PRISMA protocol with documented search strategy and database-specific retrieval counts.
- Covers the full stack: algorithmic (PTQ/QAT/MPQ/stochastic/vector), systems (heterogeneity, energy, hardware), and security (DP, secure aggregation) dimensions.
- Rich comparative tables (Tables 3–15) make the survey a practical quick-reference resource.
- Concrete, actionable future directions (adaptive/hybrid quantization, energy-aware co-design, quantum/neuromorphic, standardized benchmarks, multi-objective optimization).
- Honest self-assessment of the review's own limitations (scope cutoff, exclusion of knowledge distillation/low-rank methods).

## Limitations

- Quantitative claims (90% bandwidth reduction, 10×–50× compression, 40% energy savings) are borrowed from individual primary studies without meta-analytic reconciliation or controlled re-evaluation.
- Comparison tables are qualitative (advantages/limitations columns) rather than quantitative benchmarks; the paper itself flags the lack of standardized FL quantization benchmarks as an open problem.
- Scope stated as peer-reviewed works up to early 2025, potentially missing recent 2025–2026 preprints despite the 2019–2026 search window.
- Other compression paradigms (knowledge distillation, low-rank factorization) and their synergy with quantization are explicitly not deeply covered.
- No open repository of reviewed methods, taxonomies, or extracted data is provided.
- Theoretical convergence discussions are drawn from existing results (e.g., QSGD bounds) rather than offering new unified analysis; the authors acknowledge incomplete theory under non-IID/asynchronous settings.

## Relevance

本文与量化/联邦学习研究方向高度相关——它正是首次将这两大主题交叉进行系统综述的工作，为研究"如何在 non-IID 与异构客户端条件下设计自适应/混合量化方案以兼顾通信效率与收敛稳定性"的研究者提供了完整的文献地图、分类法与开放问题清单，可作为相关课题的入口参考文献。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How can a unified theoretical convergence bound be derived for quantized FL that simultaneously accounts for non-IID data, partial/asynchronous client participation, and adaptive per-client bit-widths?
- What standardized benchmark suite (datasets, metrics such as bits-per-update / compression ratio / energy-per-inference, and protocols) would enable fair head-to-head comparison across PTQ, QAT, and hybrid quantized FL methods?
- How do quantization noise and differential-privacy noise interact non-linearly, and can a joint quantization–DP framework be co-designed to optimize the accuracy–privacy–communication frontier?
- To what extent do the cited headline gains (90% bandwidth reduction, 40% energy savings) replicate on real edge/IoT hardware with hardware-in-the-loop evaluation rather than simulation?
- Can personalized, data-aware adaptive quantization (per-client bit-width allocation) be combined with secure aggregation without breaking the cryptographic invariants required by secure FL protocols?
