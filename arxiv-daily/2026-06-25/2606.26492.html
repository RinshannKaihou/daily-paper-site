Evaluation-Strategy Gap in Fault Diagnosis of Deep Learning Programs

arXiv: 2606.26492

Date: 2026-06-25

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26492

一句话总结： 本文构建 DynFault 语料库（5,542 条来自 38 个真实 DL 程序的故障注入训练轨迹），首次系统测量并解释了 DL 训练故障诊断技术在"程序内交叉验证"与"留程序"评估之间的 0.190 平衡准确率鸿沟，并发现该鸿沟源于特征中的程序级结构（而非可迁移的故障信号），同时证明基于 Hessian 矢量积的曲率特征在未见程序上具有可迁移的失稳检测能力。
TL;DR: Using a new DynFault corpus of 5,542 fault-injected training traces across 38 real-world DL programs, this paper shows that within-program cross-validation overstates fault-diagnosis performance by a 0.190 balanced-accuracy gap versus program-held-out evaluation, traces the gap to program-level feature structure (not transferable fault signal), and finds curvature (Hessian-vector product) features generalize to unseen programs for instability detection while optimizer/activation features do not.

核心贡献

  • 首次系统量化 DL 故障诊断的评估策略鸿沟： 在四分类故障类型任务上测得程序内评估（0.469）与留程序评估（0.279）之间存在 0.190 的平衡准确率差距，留程序性能仅略高于 0.25 的随机基线。
  • 构建并发布 DynFault 语料库： 含 5,542 条故障注入训练轨迹、38 个真实 DL 程序（来自 Stack Overflow），覆盖 FFNN/RNN/CNN 架构与六大故障类别，提供两套运行时特征配置（曲率特征 CF、优化器特征 OF）。
  • 通过四组对照实验定位鸿沟根源： 程序身份可预测性（62%）、标签置换后仍高于随机、逐程序归一化消除程序内优势、MMD 分布漂移分析——四者一致表明鸿沟由特征中的程序级结构而非可迁移故障语义造成。
  • 揭示可观测性-可泛化性权衡： 证明更丰富的运行时日志（优化器/激活特征）带来的准确率提升在未见程序上大幅退化（泛化差距最高达 0.285），而该退化在程序内评估中被隐藏。
  • 发现可迁移的失稳信号： 96% 的灾难性失稳发生在 epoch 0；曲率特征在留程序评估下将 ROC-AUC 从 0.918 提升至 0.977，并提供零误报的 LOPO 早停规则（精度 1.00）。
  • 重新评估既有技术并给出三条实践建议： 复现 DEFault、DeepFD、AutoTrainer、DeepDiagnosis 四种方法，发现 RF 分类器下鸿沟更大（0.34）；提出"对齐验证与部署""分别评估特征配置""在初始化阶段监测失稳"三条指导。

Key Contributions

  • First systematic quantification of the evaluation-strategy gap for DL training-fault diagnosis: a 0.190 balanced-accuracy gap between within-program (0.469) and program-held-out (0.279) evaluation on the four-class task, with held-out performance barely above the 0.25 chance baseline.
  • DynFault corpus, released publicly (5,542 fault-injected traces, 38 real DL programs curated from Stack Overflow, spanning FFNN/RNN/CNN, six fault categories, with two runtime feature configurations: curvature-based CF and optimizer-based OF).
  • Four converging controls isolating the gap's cause: program-identity prediction (62% accuracy at k=5), within-program label permutation (still above chance), per-program normalization (eliminates within-program advantage while leaving cross-program unchanged), and MMD distribution-shift analysis (median pairwise MMD^2 = 0.716) — all pointing to program-level feature structure rather than transferable fault signal.
  • An observability–generalizability trade-off: richer optimizer/activation features boost within-program T/V-mismatch detection (ROC-AUC 0.90→0.97) but degrade sharply on unseen programs (gap up to 0.285), a loss hidden by within-program evaluation.
  • A transferable instability signal via curvature features: 96% of catastrophic instability occurs at epoch 0; adding curvature features lifts cross-program ROC-AUC from 0.918 to 0.977 at k=1, and an unsupervised LOPO triage rule achieves precision 1.00 (with recall 0.056).
  • Re-evaluation of four existing techniques (DEFault, DeepFD, AutoTrainer, DeepDiagnosis), showing the gap widens to 0.34 under the higher-capacity Random Forest classifiers they use, plus three actionable implications (align validation with deployment; evaluate feature configs separately; monitor instability at initialization).

Methodology

The study is built around the DynFault corpus: 5,542 per-epoch CSV traces from 38 real-world DL programs (identified by Stack Overflow post IDs), with faults injected via an extended mutation operator set covering seven categories grounded in empirical DL bug taxonomies (hyperparameter, loss function, activation, layer configuration, optimizer, weight initialization, regularization). Mutants are classified as killed/survived via a GLM criterion; six categories produce labeled traces.

Two runtime feature configurations are studied:

  • Curvature features (CF): 6 channels (4 performance + mean/SD of Hessian-vector-product curvature proxies, computed via Pearlmutter's R-operator).
  • Optimizer features (OF): 15 channels (4 performance + gradient statistics, learning rate, activation mean/SD, system resources).

Feature extraction: For each scalar channel over the first k epochs (k ∈ {1,3,5,10,20,50}), extract window mean and OLS slope → feature vector x_k ∈ R^{2d}. A logistic-regression linear probe with class-balanced weights is deliberately used as a controlled classifier (so classifier capacity cannot dominate the effect); the authors show the gap is larger under Random Forest (0.34), so LR gives a conservative estimate.

Three diagnostic tasks: (a) fault-type classification (4/6/3-class), (b) catastrophic-instability triage (loss > 10^6), (c) training/validation mismatch detection (|acc_train − acc_val| ≥ 0.1).

Two evaluation strategies: within-program (stratified 5-fold over runs) vs. program-held-out (grouped 5-fold by program ID; plus stricter leave-one-program-out for the unsupervised triage rule). The evaluation-strategy gap is ΔM(k) = M_within(k) − M_heldout(k), with 95% CIs from a cluster bootstrap over programs.

Four controls identify the gap's source: program-identity prediction, within-program label permutation, per-program normalization (analysis-only), and pairwise MMD distribution-shift analysis.

Results & Highlights

  • 0.190 balanced-accuracy gap (within 0.469 vs. held-out 0.279) on the primary four-class fault-type task at k=5; held-out CI includes the 0.25 chance baseline. The six-class gap is even larger (0.290).
  • Gap is structural, not statistical noise: persists across all observation windows k=1..50 with CI lower bounds above zero.
  • All four controls converge: program-identity is 62% predictable; permuted-label within-program accuracy stays at 0.41 (vs. 0.17 chance); per-program normalization collapses within-program (0.468→0.325) but barely moves held-out (0.178→0.158); median pairwise MMD^2 = 0.716.
  • Instability timing: 96% (211/219) of unstable runs explode at epoch 0, 98% by epoch 1 — instability monitoring is most valuable at initialization.
  • Curvature features transfer: cross-program ROC-AUC 0.918→0.977 (k=1) and 0.967→0.986 (k=5) when adding CF; gain survives loss ablation. LOPO unsupervised triage rule: precision 1.00, recall 0.056 (high precision, low cross-program recall because the extreme-HVP signature concentrates in a few programs).
  • Optimizer features do NOT transfer for mismatch: within-program ROC-AUC jumps 0.90→0.97 but held-out drops 0.83→0.78; full-dataset gap reaches 0.285. Ablation shows per-layer activation statistics are the primary shortcut source (removing them narrows the gap from 0.270 to 0.195); system resources also contribute; gradient statistics and learning rate have minimal effect.
  • Existing techniques reproduce the gap: with their original RF classifiers, DEFault and DeepFD both show within-program >0.65 but cross-program 0.32 (gap 0.34), larger than the LR gap. Rule-based AutoTrainer (fires on 94.2% of traces) and DeepDiagnosis (100%) cannot discriminate fault types, so the strategy distinction does not apply.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High and well-scoped. While cross-project defect prediction and grouped CV are established in SE, applying this lens to DL fault diagnosis with runtime/observability features, and explicitly decomposing the gap via four converging controls, is genuinely new. The framing of an "observability–generalizability trade-off" is a fresh and useful conceptual contribution.
  • Rigor: Strong. Linear-probe design is a thoughtful conservative choice; cluster-bootstrap CIs over programs properly account for within-program dependence; both overlap-controlled and full-subset analyses are reported to handle the CF/OF program-composition confound. The four-control causal-decomposition (identity prediction, label permutation, per-program normalization, MMD) is exemplary and goes beyond merely reporting a performance drop.
  • Reproducibility: Excellent. Public corpus, public replication package, reproduced baselines within 3%, fixed thresholds documented, sensitivity checks reported (instability thresholds 10^3/10^4/10^6).
  • Significance: Practically important — it warns that a whole subfield's evaluation methodology may overstate real-world deployability, and gives concrete, actionable guidance (report program-held-out splits; report program-identity predictability; evaluate each feature config under grouped splits; prefer loss/accuracy for mismatch, curvature for instability).
  • Red flags / caveats (intellectually honest):
  • Mutation-injected faults only — the author flags this as the main external-validity limitation; whether the gap magnitudes (0.190, 0.285) hold on real faults is explicitly deferred to future work. Mutants may not capture the full diversity of real training failures.
  • Only 38 programs — the cluster bootstrap has non-trivial uncertainty; some results rest on small counts (e.g., 219 unstable runs; only 24 programs have CF traces; Activation = 2 programs, Regularization = 1 program make the six-class task near-chance by construction).
  • Single author — limits cross-checking, though prior collaborators' tools are reused.
  • CF and OF collected on partially different program subsets, so absolute CF-vs-OF comparisons remain confounded despite the overlap-controlled mitigation.
  • Held-out fault-type accuracy (0.279) is barely above chance — the honest but sobering implication is that no current fault-type diagnosis technique meaningfully generalizes across programs in this corpus; the positive transfer findings are confined to instability detection.
  • T/V-mismatch threshold (0.1) and instability threshold (10^6) are fixed per the DEFault protocol; architecture-specific threshold sensitivity is acknowledged but not run.

Strengths

  • Exceptionally clean experimental design with a deliberately conservative linear classifier and four converging controls that isolate why the gap exists, not just that it exists.
  • Honest reporting: held-out performance is reported even when at/near chance, and negative transfer results (optimizer features) are highlighted rather than buried.
  • Rich, interpretable analysis: curvature-cluster phenotyping (K=7), LOPO precision/recall trade-off, feature-group ablations pinpointing activation statistics as the shortcut source.
  • Direct practical payoff: a zero-false-positive early-termination rule and concrete guidance for which instrumentation is worth its cost per task.
  • Fully reproducible with a released corpus and replication package.

Limitations

  • Mutation-injected faults limit external validity; gap magnitudes may differ on real-world failures.
  • Small program count (38) and very small counts in some fault categories undermine the six-class task and widen CIs.
  • CF and OF program subsets differ, leaving residual confounding in absolute CF-vs-OF comparisons.
  • The headline result — that fault-type diagnosis does not meaningfully generalize cross-program (0.279 ≈ 0.25 chance) — means the positive practical takeaways are narrow (mostly instability detection at epoch 0).
  • Single linear-probe classifier family is the conservative main result; richer classifiers might behave differently (though RF experiments suggest the gap only worsens).
  • Fixed thresholds (T/V gap 0.1; instability 10^6) are not tuned per architecture; RNN/CNN/FFNN may differ.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do the gap magnitudes (0.190 fault-type, 0.285 mismatch) hold on real-world fault corpora (e.g., real GitHub training-failure bug reports), not just mutation-injected traces?
  • Can normalization-invariant or domain-adversarial feature designs (the paper hints per-program normalization helps) recover cross-program fault-type discrimination above chance?
  • Would larger LLM/Foundation-model-based encoders of training trajectories (rather than hand-crafted scalar summaries) transfer better across programs, or also fall victim to program-specific shortcuts?
  • The instability result is concentrated at epoch 0 — does pre-training (epoch −1) curvature geometry (initialization Hessian spectrum) already separate programs that will later diverge, enabling zero-cost triage?
  • How does the gap interact with optimizer choice, batch size, and mixed-precision — are the program-specific activation-scale shortcuts partly an artifact of specific training recipes?
  • Is the "activation statistics = primary shortcut" finding reproducible for larger modern architectures (transformers, MoE) where activation distributions differ qualitatively from the FFNN/RNN/CNN set studied?
  • Could continual/online program-adaptation (fine-tuning the diagnostic model on the first few epochs of a new program's run) close the gap cheaply without full retraining?
