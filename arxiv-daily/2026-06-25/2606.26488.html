What Survives When You Compress a Recursive Reasoner for the Edge?

arXiv: 2606.26488

Date: 2026-06-25

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26488

一句话总结： 作者系统性地压缩了递归推理模型（Tiny Recursive Model），发现量化误差会跨递归周期（而非跨输出token）累积，导致局部cell精度保持、全局puzzle精度坍塌为0；INT8+flash加载可在8MB SoC上以1/6 FLOPs达到全精度精度，逐通道校准INT4无需重训即可在4MB MCU上恢复近FP32精度。
TL;DR: The first systematic compression study of a recursive reasoning model shows that, unlike single-pass transformers, quantization noise compounds across recursive cycles — preserving local cell accuracy but destroying global puzzle-exact reasoning. The authors introduce a label-free "carry-trajectory fidelity" diagnostic, find that INT8 at one cycle matches full-depth FP32 at 6× fewer FLOPs, and recover catastrophic INT4 collapse via per-channel calibration without retraining, producing a concrete edge-deployment recipe (8 MB SoC, 4 MB MCU).

核心贡献

  • 首次系统压缩递归推理模型： 针对Tiny Recursive Model (TRM, 6.83M参数, ARC-2024上36.00% puzzle exact) 在精度×深度×任务上做完整sweep，揭示"深度–精度交叉点"——单周期(H=1) INT8以500 GFLOPs匹配3000 GFLOPs全深度FP32（6× FLOPs缩减）。
  • 识别"组合性坍塌"： 在朴素INT4、结构化剪枝、知识蒸馏、QAT四种干预下，cell accuracy得以保留，puzzle-exact accuracy坍塌至0%；这并非任务固有问题，而是token级目标（交叉熵/蒸馏）无法修复全局约束满足。
  • 架构性脆弱： 同任务(Sudoku)同精度下，MLP-mixing递归坍塌而Attention递归（含跨家族HRM）幸存——脆弱性源于token mixer而非任务。
  • 提出carry-trajectory fidelity： 量化模型最终carry state与FP32参考的余弦相似度，作为label-free、单调的损伤/恢复检测信号（阈值~0.8），优于对精度几乎不敏感的连续carry相似度。
  • 部署配方： flash-streamed embedding消除99.4MB瓶颈；INT8+flash匹配8MB SoC；逐通道校准INT4匹配4MB MCU，且在Sudoku上无重训恢复(10.2%→71.9% vs 73.8% FP32)。

Key Contributions

  • First systematic compression study of a recursive reasoning model under hard SRAM constraints, sweeping precision (FP32/FP16/INT8/INT4) × depth across three tasks (ARC-2024, Maze-Hard, Sudoku-Extreme) and two architectures (TRM-Attention, TRM-MLP-Mixing).
  • Depth–precision crossover: INT8 at a single recursive cycle (H=1, nsup=8) matches full-depth FP32 accuracy at 6× fewer FLOPs — an interaction absent from single-pass quantization.
  • Compositional-collapse phenomenon: under naive INT4, structured pruning, knowledge distillation, and QAT, local cell accuracy survives while global puzzle-exact accuracy collapses to 0%. The shared root cause is misaligned token-level objectives.
  • Architectural (not task-intrinsic) fragility: a same-task ablation isolates MLP-mixing as the fragile token mixer; attention-based recursion (including the cross-family HRM) survives naive INT4.
  • Carry-trajectory fidelity: a label-free, graded, monotonic pre-deployment detector of reasoning damage/recovery computed from the model's own hidden states plus an FP32 reference (single threshold ≈ 0.8 separates safe/broken).
  • Practical, validated deployment recipe: flash-streamed embeddings + INT8 fit an 8 MB SoC (6.51 MB); per-channel calibrated INT4 fits a 4 MB MCU (3.26 MB), recovering near-FP32 accuracy on Sudoku with no retraining. On-device latency measured on three Snapdragon platforms via Qualcomm AI Hub.

Methodology

The study centers on TRM, a weight-tied recursive transformer that iterates a latent carry state Z_h over H outer cycles, each running L inner refinement steps through a shared 2-layer block, totaling H×nsup refinement steps (default 48 on ARC-2024). The carry state is updated via an outer function g_ϕ after each inner loop. Two architectural variants are studied: TRM-Attention (full multi-head self-attention) and TRM-MLP-Mixing (token-mixing MLPs replacing attention), which differ in how relational structure propagates across refinement steps. HRM (a different recursive family) is added for cross-architecture ablation.

Tasks. ARC-2024 (abstract visual reasoning, uses a 50,911-entry / 99.4 MB puzzle embedding table, TTA, majority voting), Maze-Hard (exact path-finding, no embedding table), and Sudoku-Extreme (constraint satisfaction on 9×9 grids, MLP-mixing variant). Models are 5–7M parameters.

Compression methods. FP16 (half-precision cast), INT8 (per-channel calibration via bitsandbytes), naive INT4 (per-tensor PTQ, no calibration), per-channel calibrated INT4 (asymmetric, min/max per output channel, 128 calibration puzzles, no fine-tuning), plus structured pruning (25/50% sparsity) and knowledge distillation (single-cycle student at 13–15% params). Embedding compression: INT8 full table (24.9 MB, lossless), SVD rank-16 (3.2 MB, lossy cosine=0.94), and single-puzzle flash loading (2 KB streamed at inference, lossless).

Cycle reduction. Sweep (H, nsup) to trade recursion depth for accuracy; FLOPs scale linearly with total steps (≈3000 → 500 GFLOPs).

Carry-state diagnostics. (1) Consecutive similarity s_h (cosine between successive carry states) — found nearly precision-invariant (Δ ≤ 0.04), hence a weak signal. (2) Carry-trajectory fidelity φ (cosine between quantized final carry state and FP32 final carry state on the same input) — the recommended, graded, monotonic signal.

Recovery probes. QAT from naive INT4 checkpoint (100 steps, lr=1e-5, cross-entropy) fails to converge to puzzle-level accuracy.

Evaluation. Cell accuracy (fraction of correct grid cells) vs. puzzle-exact match (zero-error puzzles). On-device latency/memory measured on Galaxy S24 (Snapdragon 8 Gen 3), Galaxy S22 (8 Gen 1), Dragonwing RB3 Gen 2 (QCS6490) via Qualcomm AI Hub with ONNX export.

Results & Highlights

  • Embedding is the bottleneck, not the backbone: 99.4 MB (79%) of the ~126 MB FP32 footprint is the puzzle embedding table; flash loading reduces active embedding memory to 2 KB with no accuracy loss, decoupling embedding from backbone compression.
  • INT8 is lossless everywhere; INT4-naive is task/architecture-specific: INT8 preserves full accuracy across all three tasks and both architectures. Naive INT4 costs Maze −0.4 pp, ARC −10.5 pp (cell 84.8% retained), and Sudoku collapses −63.8 pp.
  • Depth–precision crossover (ARC-2024): INT8 at H=1, nsup=8 → 35.25% puzzle exact at 500 GFLOPs, within 0.75 pp of the 36.00% FP32 peak at 6× fewer FLOPs. Maze matches within 0.1 pp at 3.75× fewer FLOPs. Sudoku is the exception: accuracy keeps climbing with depth, so it cannot be cheaply cut.
  • Carry-trajectory fidelity works: stays ≥0.99 wherever INT4 is harmless; falls to 0.69 on ARC (−10 pp) and 0.35 on Sudoku (−64 pp); recovers to 0.89/0.94 under calibration. A single ≈0.8 threshold separates safe from broken.
  • Compositional collapse is universal across interventions: pruning (25% sparsity → 0% puzzle-exact on all tasks while Maze cell stays 86.4%), distillation (0% puzzle-exact, 87.3% cell), linear-attention swap (Maze: 87.5% cell, 0% puzzle), and QAT (0% on both) all share the cell-survives-puzzle-collapses signature.
  • Calibrated INT4 recovers Sudoku: 10.2% → 71.9% puzzle-exact (vs 73.8% FP32), no retraining; fidelity rises 0.35 → 0.89 in lockstep.
  • Hardware validation: Sudoku INT8 fits 8 MB streaming budget (3.06 MB); ARC/Maze need host-side INT8 activation/carry compression between steps to fit 8 MB (~6.82 MB). Counter-intuitive: static INT8 slows down Sudoku per step (11.38 → 27.23 ms on S24) because of NPU dispatch/QDQ overhead on tiny (T≈81) matrices; crossover for benefit is T ≈ 200–300.
  • Context truncation is universally destructive: halving context collapses Maze and Sudoku to 0% puzzle-exact.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (high): Genuinely the first compression study targeting recursive/depth-recurrent reasoning models and explicitly identifying that quantization noise compounds across recursive cycles (carry-state updates) rather than across output tokens. The depth–precision crossover framing and the cell-vs-puzzle decoupling on this model class are new. Carry-trajectory fidelity as a label-free diagnostic is a small but clean methodological contribution.
  • Rigor (good, with caveats): Controlled same-task/same-accuracy ablation (Table 10) properly isolates MLP-mixing as the fragile factor, ruling out an accuracy-headroom confound. The graded/monotonic fidelity claim is supported by a small but consistent set of points (Maze/ARC/Sudoku × FP32/INT8/INT4/INT4-cal). However, the number of distinct (architecture, task, precision) cells is modest, and the "monotonic" relationship rests on ~4–5 data points per condition; a continuous sweep of bit-widths (e.g., 3,5,6-bit) would strengthen the graded claim.
  • Reproducibility (strong): Public TRM checkpoints, bitsandbytes v0.41, explicit calibration sets (128 puzzles), ONNX export pipeline, and physical device specs are all specified. N=64/32/256 evaluation sizes are stated. Carry-state extraction via forward hooks on g_ϕ is described. This is above-average reproducibility for a compression paper.
  • Significance (moderate-high): Timely, given the 2025–2026 surge of depth-recurrent LMs (Geiping, Wang, Hao, Kohli). The deployment recipe (flash embedding + INT8/calibrated-INT4) is immediately actionable, and the "token-level objectives cannot repair recursive reasoning collapse" result is a cautionary, transferable finding for QAT designers.
  • Red flags / honest concerns:
  • The headline "calibrated INT4 fits a 4 MB MCU" recovery is demonstrated by full-system accuracy only on Sudoku; for ARC the authors report only fidelity (0.94), not remeasured accuracy. This asymmetry somewhat overstates generalization.
  • HRM (the second architecture) is evaluated only on Sudoku-Extreme, so cross-architecture generality is thin.
  • Carry-trajectory fidelity requires the FP32 reference at deployment time — it is a pre-deployment decision tool, not a live monitor; the framing sometimes blurs this.
  • ARC accuracy depends on test-time augmentation, so reported single-pass latencies understate true per-puzzle deployment cost; the authors disclose this but it complicates the headline efficiency numbers.
  • No autoregressive baseline of comparable size is included, so compression dynamics specific to recursion vs. compactness are not fully separated.
  • The on-device INT8 "slowdown" on Sudoku (Appendix G) is a real, somewhat surprising negative result that tempers the otherwise uniformly positive compression story.

Strengths

  • First-mover analysis of a timely model class (recursive reasoning) with a genuinely different failure mode (carry-state compounding) vs. standard LLM quantization.
  • Clean experimental controls: same-task, same-FP32-accuracy ablation to decouple architecture from task.
  • A label-free, deployable diagnostic (fidelity) with a single interpretable threshold.
  • Real on-device validation across three Snapdragon platforms, including an honest negative result (INT8 latency penalty on short sequences).
  • Actionable, concrete deployment recipe with memory/latency/accuracy trade-offs tabulated.

Limitations

  • Single model family (TRM) dominates; only Sudoku tests a second architecture (HRM).
  • Calibrated-INT4 recovery is accuracy-validated only on Sudoku (ARC uses fidelity as a proxy).
  • Fidelity needs an FP32 reference, limiting it to pre-deployment gatekeeping.
  • ARC's reliance on TTA means single-pass latency understates deployment cost.
  • No comparable-size autoregressive baseline to separate recursion effects from small-model compactness.
  • Calibrated INT4 uses only 128 calibration puzzles; sensitivity to calibration-set size/composition is not analyzed.
  • The graded-fidelity claim rests on a small number of points; a continuous bit-width sweep would be more convincing.

Relevance

高度相关。本文直接研究量化精度误差，但揭示了递归推理模型独有的累积模式——量化噪声跨递归周期（carry state更新）而非跨输出token累积，并指出朴素INT4导致局部cell精度保留而全局puzzle精度坍塌、QAT等token级目标无法修复、逐通道校准是关键。对量化/精度误差主题具有直接的迁移价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does carry-trajectory fidelity generalize to larger recursive/depth-recurrent LMs (Geiping et al.; HRM) and to non-puzzle tasks (math reasoning, code) where correctness is also compositional?
  • Would a continuous bit-width sweep (3/4/5/6-bit) confirm the graded, monotonic fidelity–accuracy relationship, or reveal a sharp threshold?
  • Can a puzzle-level / constraint-satisfaction QAT objective (rather than cross-entropy) repair compositional collapse, and how does it interact with calibration?
  • How does the depth–precision crossover shift with model scale (e.g., 50M vs. 6.8M parameters)?
  • Is the MLP-mixing fragility a fundamental property of dense token mixers, or can a calibration scheme tailored to MLP-mixing match attention's robustness?
  • Can fidelity be approximated without an FP32 reference (e.g., via a learned surrogate) to enable live monitoring?
  • How do the findings transfer to autoregressive models with latent recurrent reasoning (continuous-CoT), where compounding may also occur?
