> **一句话总结：** 本文使用 Dedicated Feature Crosscoders（DFC）对 Qwen2.5-3B 的 ToolRL 微调前后做模型 diff，发现 RL 引入的工具调用能力被压缩到极少量可导控的特征上——只需在推理时操控单个 A-exclusive 神经元即可带来 +65pp 的工具正确率提升，且编码-解码重构本身就能将能力被动迁移给冻结的基座模型（+6.8pp），但 DFC 的专属分区更像"过滤器"而非干净的"汇槽"。

> **TL;DR:** Using Dedicated Feature Crosscoders (DFC) to diff a Qwen2.5-3B base vs. ToolRL-finetuned pair, the authors show RL-induced tool-calling capability concentrates into a minimal, steerable A-exclusive feature set: a single neuron's residual-stream delta yields +65.0 pp tool-correctness with zero retraining, encode-decode reconstruction passively transfers capability to the frozen base (+6.8 pp "capability spillover"), and the DFC partition acts as a filter rather than a clean capability sink.

## 核心贡献

- 首次对 Dedicated Feature Crosscoders（DFC）进行系统性超参数扫描评估（48 个变体：36 DFC + 12 CrossCoder），在 ToolRL 微调的 Qwen2.5-3B 模型对上验证其作为模型 diff 工具的有效性。
- 发现并量化了"能力溢出"（capability spillover）现象：将冻结基座模型的激活通过联合训练的 crosscoder 后，无需任何微调即可获得 +6.8 pp 工具正确率提升，说明能力被部分"去局部化"到了共享解码器权重中。
- 证明 DFC 的专属分区是"过滤器"而非"汇槽"——惩罚专属分区（λexcl=1e-3）会把工具相关信号推回共享分区并损害 Model A 保真度，而非去除冗余。
- 首次展示单神经元 steering 饱和：在 9 个被探测层中的 7 层，仅操控一个 A-exclusive 神经元即可达到最大效果（+65.0 pp），而未分区的 CrossCoder 需要 33 个特征才能达到相近峰值。
- 提供几何（UMAP + HDBSCAN，ARI=0.93 vs 0.08）与自动可解释性证据，表明 A-exclusive 特征占据可分离的"工具交互"区域，主要是结构性模板检测器（如 `<tool_call>` 格式标记）。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic behavioral evaluation of Dedicated Feature Crosscoders across a 48-variant hyperparameter sweep (36 DFC + 12 CrossCoder) on a ToolRL-finetuned Qwen2.5-3B base/RL pair, trained for 9,000 steps on 40k FineWeb + 40k ToolRL samples.
- Discovery of "capability spillover": passing the frozen base model's activations through a jointly-trained crosscoder passively transfers +6.8 pp tool-correctness with zero fine-tuning, while format-accuracy never spills over—suggesting semantic intent is shared but surface-form machinery is RL-specific.
- Empirical refutation (partial) of DFC's design intent: the exclusive partition acts as a filter that concentrates model-specific residue rather than a clean capability sink; penalizing exclusivity (λexcl=1e-3) degrades Model A fidelity at both 5% and 10% exclusive shares.
- Demonstration of single-neuron steering saturation: one A-exclusive feature (|S|=1, α=32) yields +65.0 pp Δ tool-correctness (95% CI [+47.9, +82.1]), plateauing immediately, versus the 33 features the unpartitioned CrossCoder needs to reach its ceiling.
- Cross-layer generalization (7/9 probed layers) plus geometric/autointerp evidence (UMAP separation ARI=0.93 for DFC vs 0.08 for matched CrossCoder proxy) that A-exclusive features form a separable "Tool Interaction" region attributable to the gradient mask, not UMAP artifacts.

## Methodology

**Models & data.** A single architecture pair sharing Qwen2.5-3B (d=2048, 36 layers): Model B = `Qwen/Qwen2.5-3B` (base), Model A = `chengq9/ToolRL-Qwen2.5-3B` (RL-finetuned for structured `<tool_call>` invocation via execution feedback). Training data: 40k FineWeb general-domain samples + 40k ToolRL instruction-output pairs.

**DFC architecture.** The crosscoder dictionary of size D is partitioned into A-exclusive `[0, aend)`, B-exclusive `[aend, bend)`, and shared `[bend, D)` sub-dictionaries, with gradient masking forcing each model to read/write only its designated partition. The loss (Eq. 1) combines MSE reconstruction on the concatenated residual-stream activations with separate L1 penalties on shared (`λsh`) and exclusive (`λexcl`) features; top-k sparsity is enforced in the encoder.

**Hyperparameter sweep.** 48 variants across 5 axes: Architecture (CrossCoder vs DFC), Dictionary D ∈ {8,192, 16,384}, top-k ∈ {45, 90, 160}, exclusive share p ∈ {3%, 5%, 10%}, and λexcl ∈ {0 ("free"), 1e-3 ("penalised")}. All trained 9,000 steps, batch 1,024, Adam lr=1e-4, λsh=1e-3.

**Behavioral scoring.** Each response scored on format_accuracy (presence of `<tool_call>` and JSON `name`), tool_correctness (fuzzy match to a numbered tool), and overall_score ∈ {-1,0,+1,+2}. Evaluation: 100 held-out ToolRL prompts per sweep variant, 40 per steering cell, greedy decoding, max 200 new tokens.

**Feature ranking & steering.** Features ranked by Cohen's d between tool-use and general-text activations (Eq. 2), filtered by firing rate (ρ_tool ≥ 0.3, ρ_gen ≤ 0.1). Targeted steering applies an additive residual-stream delta `h′A = hA + Σ_{i∈S} (α-1)·fi·Wdec[i,A,:]` (Eq. 3) for a ranked subset S with coefficient α.

**Geometric & autointerp analysis.** Decoder geometry analyzed via UMAP + HDBSCAN with a matched-size mass-ratio proxy partition for the unconstrained CrossCoder (to control for label imbalance). A Gemma-based autointerp protocol explains features from top-K activating fragments.

## Results & Highlights

- **Sweep-level reconstruction:** All 48/48 variants improve Model A tool-correctness (one-sided sign test p ≈ 3.6e-15); mean gain +31.1 ± 9.7 pp. Reconstruction MSE correlates only weakly with behavioral gain (r=+0.08), implying tool capability lives in a sparse, behaviorally privileged subspace.
- **Capability spillover:** Model B tool-correctness rises 0% → 6.8% post-reconstruction (paired-t p ≈ 1e-12) with zero fine-tuning, but format-accuracy never spills over (Δ=0 pp identically)—semantic intent is shared, surface-form is RL-specific.
- **Architecture comparison:** CrossCoder and DFC reach indistinguishable reconstruction MSE (0.031) and similar behavioral ceilings; DFC directionally reduces spillover but the t-test is underpowered (ΔB: t=1.63, p=0.12).
- **Single-neuron steering:** At layer 13, one A-exclusive neuron achieves +65.0 pp; CrossCoder needs 33. B-exclusive steering produces exactly 0.0 pp at every cell (validates harness integrity). Combining A-excl ∪ shared causes destructive interference (only +35.0 pp).
- **Cross-layer:** Mean best-cell Δ=+43.3 pp across probed layers (paired-t p=0.0006, Wilcoxon p=0.0078); single-neuron saturation generalizes to 7/9 layers. Layers 18 and 24 are "dead" (no tool-signed A-excl features).
- **Autointerp:** Top A-exclusive features (e.g., feat #521, Cohen's d=12.18, AUROC=0.998) are structural-template detectors—"LM instructed to use tools for user requests," "<tool_call> syntax," etc.
- **Hypotheses:** 7 of 8 pre-registered hypotheses confirmed (Table 6); only H7 ("combo > either alone") rejected, due to interference.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate):** The application of DFC to *RL-induced* behavioral change (as opposed to SFT chat-tuning) and the single-neuron steering saturation result are genuinely novel. The "capability spillover" framing is a fresh and somewhat surprising observation. However, the core machinery (DFC, crosscoder model diffing, activation steering) is inherited from prior work (Lindsey et al. 2024; Jiralerspong & Bricken 2025), so this is an application-and-characterization contribution rather than a new method.
- **Rigor (Strong):** Pre-registered hypotheses (Table 6), exact-binomial and paired-t tests with reported CIs, a matched-size proxy-partition control that explicitly rules out the UMAP/label-imbalance confound, and a B-exclusive zero-effect sanity check on the steering harness. The authors are notably honest about what the evidence does and does not show.
- **Reproducibility (Good):** Hyperparameters, model identifiers, training data sizes, sweep grid, seeds (42), decoding config, and the full per-variant evaluation table (Table 8) are all disclosed. A code release is referenced. Main gaps: no released crosscoder checkpoints referenced by URL, and best-cell numbers are explicitly noted as unreplicated on a fresh sample.
- **Significance (Moderate, narrow scope):** Conclusions are bounded to a single model pair (Qwen2.5-3B) and a single task (ToolRL). The headline +65 pp is a steering delta on a narrow behavioral metric, not a broad capability claim. The capability-spillover observation has real safety implications (a potential side-channel for reintroducing capability) that deserve follow-up.
- **Red flags / honest caveats:** (1) The architecture-level DFC-vs-CrossCoder comparison is underpowered (12 vs 36 runs, p=0.12), yet the abstract and framing emphasize DFC's superiority—slight overclaim risk, partially mitigated by explicit caveats in §5.3 and §7. (2) "Capability" is the authors' own shorthand for reliable structured tool-call generation; no prompted baseline tuned to elicit base-model tool use was run, so the magnitude of RL's contribution could be inflated. (3) Steering uses only n=40 prompts per cell; best-cell CIs are wide. (4) Autointerp covered only A-exclusive features. (5) The abstract's "+31.1 pp ... +6.8 pp" framing conflates sweep means with the more headline single-neuron +65 pp result.

## Strengths

- Pre-registered hypotheses with explicit accept/reject status (Table 6) — unusually disciplined for a workshop paper.
- The matched-size proxy-partition experiment (§4.1) is a clean control that isolates the gradient-mask's geometric contribution from label imbalance.
- Genuine intellectual honesty: the paper explicitly states its results "partially refute the DFC's design intent" and that the partition behaves as a filter, not a sink.
- B-exclusive zero-effect result doubles as an end-to-end correctness check on the steering pipeline.
- Practical relevance: single-neuron runtime steering (zero retraining) is a concrete, deployable primitive for agentic-LLM control.

## Limitations

- Single model pair (Qwen2.5-3B base/RL) and single task (ToolRL); generalization to other architectures/RL regimes unknown.
- Architecture comparison underpowered (12 CC vs 36 DFC; directional only, p=0.12).
- Steering evaluated on only n=40 prompts per cell; best cells unreplicated.
- No prompted baseline to elicit base-model tool use, so "capability" magnitudes are relative, not absolute.
- Autointerp only covered A-exclusive features; the shared partition (which carries most spilled capability) is unexamined.
- Workshop venue; review depth and archival weight lighter than a main-conference paper.

## Relevance

高度相关。本文直接处于 mechanistic interpretability 与 crosscoders/model-diffing 的核心交叉点，用 DFC 定位 RL 引入的可导控行为特征，与 interpretability、crosscoders、mechanistic interpretability 兴趣方向高度契合，且对"模型 diff 是否会泄露/迁移能力"这一安全性问题提供了实证。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the single-neuron steering result hold for larger models (7B/70B) and other RL regimes (RLHF, DPO, reasoning RL), or is it an artifact of Qwen2.5-3B's narrow tool-call template?
- The capability spillover (+6.8 pp to the frozen base) is a potential safety side-channel—could a released crosscoder between a capable and an incapable model be used to reconstruct removed capability? How does this interact with safety fine-tuning unlearning?
- Would Delta-Crosscoders (Kassem et al. 2026), which model asymmetric fine-tuning, change the sink-vs-filter verdict for the DFC exclusive partition?
- What lives in the shared partition's tool-relevant features that autointerp did not cover, and can steering them suppress (rather than amplify) tool use?
- Is the destructive interference when combining A-excl ∪ shared a general property of non-orthogonal decoder directions, and can orthogonalization recover the combined gain?
- Can the same localization machinery locate features governing the *knowledge boundary* (when an agent decides whether to act at all), as the authors speculate in Future Work?
