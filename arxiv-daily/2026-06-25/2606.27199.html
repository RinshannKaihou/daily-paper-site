Forecasting With LLMs: Improved Generalization Through Feature Steering

arXiv: 2606.27199

Date: 2026-06-25

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27199

一句话总结： 本文利用稀疏自编码器（SAE）在 LLM 内部识别出与"时间感知推理"和"前瞻偏差（look-ahead bias）"相关的特征，并通过在生成时放大时间感知特征（如 Gemma 3 的 L40:24283、Qwen 的 L48:28961），在 M&A 和医药预测任务中显著降低了模型对训练后知识的依赖，同时保持了通用推理能力（MMLU CoT/Pro CoT 基本稳定）；而反向放大候选的"前瞻偏差特征"则没有产生可观测效果。
TL;DR: The authors use sparse autoencoders (SAEs) to discover temporal features inside LLMs (time-aware vs. look-ahead-biased reasoning) via prediction-market data, then show that amplifying time-awareness features during generation causally reduces look-ahead bias on out-of-domain free-form M&A and pharma forecasting tasks while preserving general reasoning utility, whereas steering candidate look-ahead-bias features has no effect.

核心贡献

  • 首次将 SAE 特征引导（feature steering）从语义/安全行为扩展到时间推理领域，把"前瞻偏差"重新定义为一种可被内部干预的行为倾向，而非单纯是记忆后验事实的后果。
  • 提出了一套激活对比（activation-contrast）流程：用 Kalshi 预测市场数据（"选市场热门=时间感知"vs"选最终结果=前瞻偏差"）作为弱监督信号，在问题 token 上抓取激活，按两类一致样本间的激活率差异对 SAE 特征排序，再用 Neuronpedia 标注做人工过滤。
  • 在 Gemma 3 27B 上筛出 5 个候选特征（2 个时间感知、3 个前瞻偏差），在 Qwen 3.5 27B 上测试前 15 个特征；实证发现只有正向放大时间感知特征（如 Gemma L40:24283 "by and before"、Qwen L48:28961）能跨任务降低 LAB。
  • 在两个全新领域（WRDS 大盘 M&A 交易、医药增长驱动因素预测）做自由文本生成评测，且这些任务样本外可预测性≈0，使"提及已实现未来事件"成为清晰的 LAB 信号。
  • 诚实报告了零结果：候选的前瞻偏差特征引导无效果，并讨论了 Kalshi 作为发现集本身有噪声、多选题形式削弱引导效果等解释。

Key Contributions

  • Extends SAE-based feature steering beyond broad semantic/safety behaviors (e.g., "Golden Gate Claude", refusal steering) to temporal reasoning, reframing look-ahead bias as an internal, intervenable behavioral tendency rather than only a side effect of memorized post-cutoff facts.
  • Introduces an activation-contrast feature-discovery procedure grounded in prediction-market data, treating "picking the market favorite" as a proxy for time-aware reasoning and "picking the realized outcome" as a proxy for look-ahead bias, then ranking SAE features by activation-rate differences across unanimous samples.
  • Demonstrates cross-task causal generalization: features discovered on prediction-market multiple-choice questions transfer to reduce LAB in free-form M&A and pharmaceutical forecasting, where out-of-sample predictability is essentially zero.
  • Shows utility preservation: MMLU-CoT and MMLU-Pro-CoT (5-shot) remain broadly stable across steering regimes in which LAB has already fallen substantially, suggesting the effect is not just capability degradation.
  • Provides an honest negative result — candidate look-ahead-bias features do not produce a suppressive effect when amplified — with plausible mechanistic explanations.

Methodology

  1. SAE setup. Decompose dense transformer activations via SAEs: z = ReLU(W_enc x + b_enc), x_hat = W_dec z + b_dec, trained to reconstruct activations sparsely. Uses released dictionaries: Gemma Scope 2 for Gemma 3 (layers 16, 31, 40, 53) and Qwen Scope (Deng et al., 2026) for Qwen 3.5 27B (all layers; Neuronpedia labels only for layer 31).
  2. Feature discovery (prediction markets). Prompt with "Today is {Month} {Day}, {Year}. {Question}" using market opening dates, append answer choices, require reasoning + multiple-choice answer. Sample each question 4× at temperature 1.0; retain only unanimous samples. Rank SAE features by the difference in activation rate between unanimous time-aware and look-ahead-biased cases. Neuronpedia annotations filter out dataset-artifact features (e.g., a feature labeled "dates like 2023" tied to the 2022–2023 question pool is discarded).
  3. Feature steering. Amplify feature j by adding a steering magnitude α before decoding: x_hat_steered = W_dec (z + α e_j) + b_dec, then reinsert the modified activation.
  4. Evaluation tasks.
  • M&A benchmark built from WRDS large-cap transactions; prompt from ~1 year before each deal went public, asking for top-3 acquisition targets over next 2 years.
  • Pharmaceutical forecasting: predict main growth drivers for a company using only information through a cutoff date.
  • LAB is read directly from the text (e.g., naming the eventual M&A target, citing a post-cutoff drug). Utility is measured via MMLU-CoT and MMLU-Pro-CoT (5-shot).
  1. Models. GPT-OSS, Gemma 3, Qwen 3.5, Llama 3.1/2 families evaluated; steering experiments focus on Gemma 3 27B and Qwen 3.5 27B (Instruct and Thinking variants).

Results & Highlights

  • For Gemma 3 27B, of 5 candidate features only L40:24283 ("by and before") is causally useful; the other time-aware feature (L53:9448 "years and dates") and all 3 LAB features did not produce reliable effects.
  • For Qwen 3.5 27B, multiple time-aware features work: L48:28961 (Thinking), L27:24365 (Instruct), and L27:24365 (Thinking).
  • Across both M&A and pharma tasks, amplifying time-awareness features reduces LAB (fewer mentions of realized future events per 1K tokens), while MMLU-CoT / MMLU-Pro-CoT stay broadly flat — indicating genuine bias reduction rather than capability loss.
  • The negative result is notable: no look-ahead-bias feature was found whose suppression helps, suggesting LAB is not cleanly localized to an amplifiable feature.
  • Figure 3 shows knowledge of M&A and LAB rate vary independently across model families (no positive relationship even within families), and Llama 3 series (which include cutoff info in the system prompt) vastly outperform others on the simple evaluation.
  • Appendix A gives concrete before/after examples (Newmont/Goldcorp, J&J/Momenta): the steered model explicitly self-corrects to reason from the historical perspective and drops the leaked target.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-High. Applying SAE feature steering to temporal/forecasting reasoning is a genuine extension of the steering literature (which has focused on refusal and broad semantic concepts). The reframing of LAB as an intervenable internal tendency is conceptually clean.
  • Rigor: Moderate. The cross-task design (discover on prediction markets, test on M&A/pharma) is a real strength that guards against in-distribution artifacts. However, the causal evidence rests on a single useful feature per model (Gemma L40:24283), which is fragile. The LAB signal on free-form text is annotated per-question and is somewhat subjective; no inter-annotator agreement or automated LAB detector is reported.
  • Reproducibility: Moderate-High. Uses public SAE dictionaries (Gemma Scope, Qwen Scope) and public data (WRDS requires access; Kalshi is public), and the steering equation is fully specified. Steering magnitudes/clamps (e.g., clamp 18, 21.6) are reported in appendix examples but the full sweep protocol is terse.
  • Significance: Moderate. The result is interesting for interpretability and forecasting, but the practical impact is limited by (a) the null result on LAB-feature suppression, (b) the degradation of general quality at high steering strength, and (c) the authors' own framing that steering is "not a complete standalone solution."
  • Red flags.
  • Workshop-length paper (~4 pages of body + appendix); claims are appropriately scoped but several experimental details (sample sizes, exact LAB annotation protocol, per-feature ablation counts, statistical significance beyond ±1 SE error bars) are thin.
  • The "look-ahead bias" label on the discovery features is a weak-supervision proxy; selecting the market favorite is "only an imperfect proxy for historically grounded reasoning," which the authors acknowledge.
  • No quantitative forecasting accuracy metric is reported — only LAB rate and utility — so it is unclear whether reducing LAB actually improves calibration/accuracy or merely removes memorized answers.

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated problem framing that connects mechanistic interpretability to a real-world failure mode (look-ahead bias) in finance/forecasting.
  • Strong cross-task generalization design: features discovered on one task family demonstrably transfer to two qualitatively different free-form tasks.
  • Honest treatment of negative results and explicit caveats about the limitations of any single intervention.
  • Concrete, illustrative before/after examples make the behavioral change tangible.
  • Utility preservation (MMLU stability) is properly checked, ruling out the trivial "steering just breaks the model" explanation.

Limitations

  • Only one causally useful feature per model is identified (Gemma L40:24283), raising fragility/overfitting concerns.
  • No working look-ahead-bias suppression feature — the asymmetry is unexplained beyond speculation.
  • Free-form LAB detection is manual/per-question annotation without reported reliability metrics.
  • No measure of whether reduced LAB translates to better actual forecasting accuracy/calibration, only reduced leakage.
  • Workshop-paper depth: limited ablations, modest scale (two 27B models), no exploration of layer/feature interaction or multi-feature steering combinations.
  • Steering eventually degrades general quality at high strength, and the authors concede it cannot stand alone.

Relevance

与你对 SAEs / feature steering / forecasting 的兴趣高度契合：这正是用稀疏自编码器做因果性 feature steering 的典型工作，且把 steering 应用到了一个新行为维度（时间感知/前瞻偏差）。其"用预测市场做特征发现、跨域验证因果性"的流程，以及"正向放大有效、负向抑制无效"的非对称结论，对研究 SAE 特征的因果可靠性与 steering 的边界条件都有直接参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Why does suppression of candidate LAB features have no effect while amplification of time-awareness features does? Is LAB not localized to amplifiable features, or is the discovery proxy (selecting the realized outcome) too noisy?
  • Does reducing LAB actually improve out-of-sample forecasting accuracy/calibration, or does it merely replace memorized-correct answers with plausible-but-wrong ones? Can a Brier-score-style metric be added?
  • How robust is the single useful feature (Gemma L40:24283) across SAE dictionary versions, training seeds, and larger Gemma checkpoints? Is the effect an artifact of one dictionary?
  • Would multi-feature组合 steering (several time-aware features at moderate strength, possibly combined with SFT/RL or divergence decoding) reach substantially lower LAB without utility loss, as the authors speculate?
  • Can the activation-contrast discovery procedure be made fully automated (drop Neuronpedia manual filtering) and scaled to surface features in closed models without public SAEs?
  • The LAB annotation on free-form text is the measurement backbone — how sensitive are the headline LAB-rate curves to the annotation protocol, and can an automated, validated LAB detector replace per-question human labels?
