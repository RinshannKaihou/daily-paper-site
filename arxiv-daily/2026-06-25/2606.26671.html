NebulaExp-8B: An Empirical Post-Training Pipeline via Full-Scale Ablation Research

arXiv: 2606.26671

Date: 2026-06-25

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26671

一句话总结： 本文以 Qwen3-8B-base 为基座构建了完全透明的消融驱动后训练流水线（Instruct + Reasoning 双分支），系统量化了数据正确性过滤、领域混合比例、样本难度与教师选择对 SFT/GRPO/OPD 的影响，揭示"数学与代码需要相反的数据分布偏好""长文本可跨域迁移到推理""数据正确性过滤是一阶优化因子"等结论，并证明仅用 4K 样本的 on-policy distillation 在 IFEval 上可超 RL 3.26 分。
TL;DR: A fully transparent, ablation-driven post-training pipeline on Qwen3-8B-base that systematically dissects SFT, GRPO-RL, and On-Policy Distillation (OPD) trade-offs across Instruct and Reasoning branches, demonstrating that correctness filtering is the first-order optimization factor, math/code require opposite data distributions, and a 4K-sample single-teacher OPD beats the RL baseline by 3.26 points on IFEval.

核心贡献

  • 构建了端到端的 SFT 数据处理流水线（格式统一、响应蒸馏、双向过滤、难度分级、分类与多样性采样），从公开数据集整理出 1.04M Instruct 样本与 2.8M Thinking 样本，并提供 200K 可验证 RL 候选池。
  • 提出三阶段 Instruct SFT 流水线（域内采样 → 跨域混合 → 模型固化），将平均分从 Qwen3-8B-nothink 的 55.01 提升至 60.99，GRPO RL 进一步提升至 61.85。
  • 对 SFT、GRPO-RL、OPD 三个模块开展大规模受控消融，得出多个可复用的实证结论（如数学 vs 代码的相反数据偏好、长文本跨域迁移、correctness filtering 的一阶作用）。
  • 系统验证 On-Policy Distillation（单教师与多教师 MOPD）作为无 verifier 后训练范式的有效性：单教师 OPD 仅用 4K 样本即超过 RL 3.26 分；MOPD 用 10K 样本融合四位领域专家教师，平均分较 base 提升 4.18，并在 AIME'25 上超越任一单教师上界。
  • Reasoning 分支确定 0.6:0.2:0.2（math:code:science）混合比例、长链推理样本优先、短到长课程学习与中难度 GRPO RL，使平均推理分从 73.88 提升至 75.17。

Key Contributions

  • An end-to-end SFT data processing stack (format unification, response distillation, bidirectional filtering, fine-grained difficulty grading, classification, and diversity-aware sampling) producing 1.04M Instruct + 2.8M Thinking samples and a 200K verifiable RL candidate pool from public datasets.
  • A principled three-stage Instruct SFT pipeline (within-domain sampling -> cross-domain mixing -> consolidation) lifting the average benchmark score from 55.01 (Qwen3-8B-nothink) to 60.99, with GRPO RL pushing it to 61.85.
  • Extensive controlled ablations across SFT, GRPO-RL, and OPD, yielding reusable empirical findings: opposite data-distribution preferences for math vs. code, cross-domain transfer from long-form text to reasoning, and correctness filtering as the dominant first-order factor.
  • A systematic study of On-Policy Distillation (single-teacher and multi-teacher MOPD) as a verifier-free alternative: single-teacher OPD with only 4K IF samples beats the RL baseline by +3.26 on IFEval and +4.43 average; MOPD fuses four specialist teachers with 10K samples for a +4.18 average gain, with the student surpassing individual teacher bounds on AIME'25 (75.83 vs. 75.42).
  • A Reasoning branch recipe (0.6:0.2:0.2 math:code:science, long-chain prioritization, short-to-long curriculum, medium-difficulty GRPO RL) raising the average reasoning score from 73.88 to 75.17.

Methodology

The paper organizes post-training into two orthogonal branches, both starting from Qwen3-8B-base.

Data Curation. A 3.84M raw SFT corpus is aggregated from Nemotron-cascade, ODA-math-460K, Nemotron-Math-V2, OmniThought-Math, AM-Thinking, OpenScienceReasoning, and Dolci-Instruct-SFT. A bidirectional filtering pipeline (query filtering, response filtering, answer verification) combines rule-based filters, model-based scoring (Qwen3-235B-A22B-Instruct), tool-based checking in Docker, multi-model rejection sampling, 54 instruction checkers for IF data, and an LLM-Judge fallback for unverifiable samples. A difficulty grading system adapts GLM-5's pass@4 strategy: high difficulty = pass@4 in [0, 0.25), medium = [0.25, 0.5), low = [0.5, 1]. A Qwen3-1.7B classifier assigns hierarchical task labels, followed by embedding-based clustering and benchmark-gain-based sampling.

Instruct SFT. A four-indicator quality system (Length, Entropy, Loss, IFD = L(r|q)/L(r)) computed with Qwen3-8B-Instruct guides three stages: (1) within-domain adaptive sampling (weighted vs. stratified); (2) cross-domain mixing (gain-based vs. semantic clustering vs. semantic deduplication); (3) final consolidation. Final config: gain-based mix (Math 44K / Code 17K / Other 89K, 150K total).

Instruct RL. GRPO on a 53K subset filtered to pass-rate in (0,1), with rollout size 5, KL loss enabled, relaxed on-policy (mini-batch 64 vs. train batch 256), up to 6 epochs with early stopping.

Reasoning SFT. A regression-based mixture search casts the data-type ratio search as a prediction problem over proxy-training signals, landing on 0.6:0.2:0.2 (math:code:science). Long-chain reasoning samples and short-to-long curriculum learning are preferred over pure difficulty scaling.

Reasoning RL. GRPO on 8K medium-difficulty samples (4K IF + 2K math + 1K science + 1K code), correctness + format rewards only, 2 epochs.

On-Policy Distillation (OPD). The student generates y ~ pi_S, the frozen teacher provides per-token logits, and the student minimizes reverse KL restricted to the teacher's top-64 tokens. Single-teacher experiments compare Qwen3-8B, 235B-A22B, 32B, 30B-A3B, and 30B-A3B-Thinking-2507. Multi-teacher OPD (MOPD) routes domain-specific RL-trained specialists (math, science, code, IF) per sample with domain-weighted KL losses.

Results & Highlights

  • Instruct SFT: average 55.01 -> 60.99 (+5.98); ZebraLogic 25.60 -> 61.50 (+35.90), AIME'24 29.17 -> 44.17 (+15.00). "Alignment tax": IFEval drops to 77.08 but is recoverable to 82.26 with dedicated IF data.
  • Instruct RL: average 60.99 -> 61.85; IFEval 77.08 -> 80.96 (+3.88), MATH-500 88.60 -> 91.20 (+2.60).
  • Reasoning RL: average 73.88 -> 75.17 (epoch 2 best); GPQA-Diamond 58.08 -> 62.63 (+4.55).
  • Single-teacher OPD: with only 4K IF samples, IFEval reaches 83.40 (+3.26 over RL), general average 74.61 vs. RL 70.18.
  • MOPD: 10K samples, 1 epoch -> average 72.42 -> 76.60 (+4.18 over base, +1.98 over Qwen3-8B); student surpasses the teacher upper bound on AIME'25 (75.83 vs. 75.42) and MATH-500 (97.25 vs. 96.97).
  • Key empirical findings: (1) Length >> Loss > IFD ~ Entropy for math reasoning; (2) math favors high entropy/high IFD while code favors the opposite; (3) long-form general text transfers to formal reasoning without any math data; (4) correctness filtering (removing 7.1% failed examples) yields AIME'25 +7.09; (5) hard-only RL beats curriculum learning at 8B scale; (6) KL loss has negligible final-score impact; (7) same-family/same-vocabulary teachers outperform larger but distributionally distant teachers.

The work is an industry technical report from ZTE with a corporate contact email. Credibility rests on (a) a fully transparent methodology with disclosed hyperparameters, prompt templates, and data sources; (b) ablations that control one variable at a time; and (c) honest reporting of regressions (e.g., LiveBench -6.60, IFEval "alignment tax"). Caveats: it is a non-peer-reviewed industry report, not a conference paper, and no independent third-party reproduction is available. Notably the references point to several 2026-dated model releases (GPT-5, GLM-5, DeepSeek-V4, Qwen3.5, Llama 4) which are forward-dated relative to typical academic cycles.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. The pipeline components (GRPO, OPD, SFT data filtering, mixture-ratio search, curriculum learning) are individually well-established. The novelty lies in the systematic, transparent ablation assembly and specific empirical findings (opposite math/code preferences, teacher-distribution-compatibility over scale, MOPD cross-teacher recombination exceeding teacher bounds), rather than in any single new algorithm.
  • Rigor: Reasonably high. Controlled one-variable-at-a-time ablations, benchmark contamination control via global dedup against evaluation sets, 16-sample averaging for AIME to reduce variance, and unified evaluation protocols with fixed decoding configs. However, some ablations use reduced 10K corpora and 4 epochs to save compute, which may not fully reflect full-scale dynamics.
  • Reproducibility: Strong on paper. Hyperparameters, data sources, classification taxonomy, judge prompts, and mixture ratios are disclosed. Full reproducibility still depends on access to the proprietary/distilled teacher models (Qwen3.5-397B-A17B, DeepSeek-V4) and on the exact filtering thresholds and verifier implementations, which are only partially specified.
  • Significance: Practically valuable for the open community, especially for 8B-scale practitioners. The framing as a "recipe report" rather than SOTA-chasing is appropriate and honest. The OPD findings (verifier-free, data-efficient, sometimes teacher-surpassing) are the most transferable insights.
  • Red flags: (1) LiveBench consistently degrades (-6.60 after SFT, only partially recovered by RL) yet is averaged into the headline "average" score, which somewhat flatters the reported gains; (2) the dramatic ZebraLogic jump (+35.90) is explicitly flagged by the authors as possibly contaminated with Other-domain puzzle-like text, undermining confidence in that headline number; (3) all experiments are at a single 8B scale, so broad generalization claims (e.g., teacher-selection principles) are unverified beyond 8B; (4) RL is restricted to verifiable tasks, leaving open-ended and agentic settings untested; (5) some headline comparisons mix thinking vs. non-thinking protocols in a way that requires careful reading.

Strengths

  • Exceptional transparency: every stage (data sources, filtering rules, judge prompts, mixture ratios, RL hyperparameters, teacher selection logic) is disclosed, making it a genuinely reusable engineering reference.
  • Genuinely informative ablations with counterintuitive, actionable findings (math/code opposite preferences, hard-only > curriculum at 8B, distributional compatibility > scale for OPD teachers).
  • OPD/MOPD is a compelling, data-efficient, verifier-free complement to RL, with the student-surpasses-teacher result on AIME'25 being a striking empirical phenomenon.
  • Honest about limitations and regressions rather than cherry-picking only favorable benchmarks.

Limitations

  • Single 8B scale; no validation on dense models at larger scales or on MoE architectures (acknowledged by authors).
  • RL coverage limited to verifiable math/code/science/IF tasks; no open-ended, long-horizon, or agentic evaluation.
  • Headline average aggregates benchmarks where the model regresses (LiveBench, IFEval pre-recovery), and the largest single gain (ZebraLogic +35.90) carries a contamination caveat.
  • OPD teacher set is itself only 8B-scale RL specialists; stronger/cross-architecture teachers and broader domain coverage are left to future work.
  • Evaluation is benchmark-centric and may not capture real-world agent robustness or long-context interactive behavior.

Relevance

This paper is highly relevant to post-training and ablation research interests: it is essentially a large-scale, controlled ablation study across the SFT/GRPO/OPD post-training stack, directly addressing how data-quality factors, mixture ratios, sample difficulty, and teacher selection drive 8B-model capability trade-offs. The methodology (one-variable-at-a-time ablations, four-indicator SFT data scoring, regression-based mixture search, OPD teacher compatibility analysis) is a concrete template for designing rigorous post-training ablation experiments.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do the opposite data-distribution preferences (math vs. code) persist at 30B/70B or MoE scale, or are they artifacts of 8B capacity constraints?
  • How much of the ZebraLogic +35.90 gain is genuine cross-domain transfer versus latent contamination from puzzle-like text in the "Other" domain, and can this be cleanly disentangled?
  • Why does MOPD surpass the individual teacher upper bound on AIME'25 but regress on IFEval (-8.14 vs. Qwen3-8B)? Is the IF dimension fundamentally harder to distill, or is it a data-quantity artifact?
  • Can the teacher-distribution-compatibility finding (same-family 8B teacher > 235B teacher) be explained by a measurable distributional distance metric rather than observed empirically post hoc?
  • How do the OPD findings transfer when the teacher is cross-architecture (e.g., a much larger dense or MoE teacher with a different tokenizer), given the top-64-token KL truncation?
  • Would the hard-only > curriculum-learning result invert at larger scale or with different RL algorithms (e.g., DAPO vs. GRPO)?
