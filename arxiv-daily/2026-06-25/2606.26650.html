CAT-Q: Cost-efficient and Accurate Ternary Quantization for LLMs

arXiv: 2606.26650

Date: 2026-06-25

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26650

一句话总结： 本文提出 CAT-Q，一种后训练（PTQ）三值量化方法，通过可学习调制（Learnable Modulation，三个可学习因子 δµ/δα/δ∆ 重整权重分布与阈值）与软化三值化（Softened Ternarization，两阶段可微→硬三值化中继）两个组件，仅用 512 个校准样本即可把 1.7B–235B 参数的预训练 LLM 压成三值模型，性能超过用 100B token 训练的 BitNet 1.58-bit v1/v2，并把训练 token 量减少约 10 万倍。
TL;DR: CAT-Q is a post-training ternary (1.58-bit) weight quantization scheme for LLMs that combines Learnable Modulation (LM) and Softened Ternarization (ST) inside a SliderQuant-style sliding-layer reconstruction pipeline. Using only 512 C4 calibration samples (~1M tokens), it ternarizes 10 pre-trained LLMs from 1.7B to 235B params (dense + MoE). On five zero-shot commonsense benchmarks (PIQA, ARC-e, ARC-c, HellaSwag, Winogrande), Qwen3-4B+W1.58A16 reaches avg 57.06%, beating BitNet v2-3B (54.67%) and TriLM-3.9B (56.55%) trained on 100B–300B tokens; Qwen3-8B+W1.58A8 (60.96%) beats BitNet v2-7B (57.63%) — roughly a 100,000× reduction in training tokens versus BitNet's 100B.

核心贡献

  • 提出 CAT-Q 后训练三值量化框架：首个能在 PTQ 场景下匹配/超越 QAT-based 三值方法（BitNet 1.58-bit v1/v2、TriLM、Tequila）的方法，且无需访问原始训练数据，规避数据隐私与算力成本。
  • 可学习调制（Learnable Modulation, LM）：用三个可学习因子 δµ（平移，−1<δµ<1）、δα（缩放，δα>0）、δ∆（阈值，δ∆>0）构造线性变换 Ŵ=(W−µ)/α，自适应重整预训练高精度权重分布与三值化阈值 ∆，抑制离群值影响、缓解三值权重与原权重分布失配。
  • 软化三值化（Softened Ternarization, ST）：提出新的可微过渡函数 f(W;s,∆)= [tanh(s(W−∆))+tanh(s(W+∆))] / [2·tanh(s)]，建立"恒等映射→可微三值化→硬三值化"的两阶段接力（epoch ratio γ 控制比例），解决 PTQ 场景下硬三值化非可微导致的收敛不稳问题。
  • 解耦学习策略：用 Ŵ 作为"再分配代理"学习 ∆ 与三值权重 T，但在最终部署时近似 W≈αT（不含 µ），既保留 TWN 硬件友好实现，又比 W≈αT+µ 略优。
  • 首次把 PTQ 三值化扩展到 14B–235B 参数模型：在 8×A100-80GB 上 1–60 小时即可完成，包括 Qwen3-235B-A22B 与 Ring-flash-2.0(100B-A6.1B) 等 MoE 模型。

Key Contributions

  • First PTQ-based ternarization competitive with QAT: CAT-Q matches or surpasses BitNet 1.58-bit v1/v2 (100B tokens), TriLM (300B tokens), TernaryLLM (1T tokens with KD) and Tequila (10B tokens) while using only 512 calibration samples (~1M tokens), i.e. ~100,000× fewer tokens than BitNet v2.
  • Learnable Modulation (LM): a learnable linear transform Ŵ=(W−µ)/α with µ=µ0+δµ·α0, α=δα·α0, ∆=δ∆·∆0 (default ∆0=0.5), where δµ∈(−1,1), δα>0, δ∆>0. It adaptively reshapes the weight distribution and ternary threshold to reduce outlier sensitivity and distributional misalignment; Figure 2 shows LM yields lower reconstruction error than static approximation (SA) or direct learning (DL) on Qwen3-4B layer 4.
  • Softened Ternarization (ST): a novel symmetric differentiable transition function f(W;s,∆)=[tanh(s(W−∆))+tanh(s(W+∆))]/[2·tanh(s)] that (i) is differentiable in W, (ii) → identity as s→0, (iii) asymptotically ternary as s→∞. ST runs a two-stage relay over calibration epochs: differentiable ternarization for epoch ratio γ (default 0.8) then hard ternarization for 1−γ.
  • Disentangled learning: µ is used to redistribute weights but dropped in the deployment reconstruction W≈αT, preserving TWN's hardware-friendly form; ablation (Table 9) shows W≈αT slightly beats W≈αT+µ on Qwen3-4B (57.06 vs 56.59 avg).
  • Scale to 235B: first demonstration of PTQ ternarization up to Qwen3-235B-A22B (and 100B-A6.1B MoE) within 8–60 h on 8×A100-80GB.

Methodology

Setup. Given a pre-trained LLM and 512 calibration sequences (length 2048 tokens, randomly sampled from C4, not the original training data), CAT-Q quantizes linear-layer weights to ternary {−1,0,+1} with a per-group scaling factor α. Group size default 128 (following TriLM/Tequila for fair comparison). Calibration epochs m=60 default.

Component 1 — Learnable Modulation. For a weight group W, compute statistics-based priors µ0=mean(W), α0=mean(|W−µ0|), ∆0=0.5 (equivalently α0/2 on the original W). Introduce three learnable factors: δµ∈(−1,1), δα>0, δ∆>0, giving µ=µ0+δµ·α0, α=δα·α0, ∆=δ∆·∆0. Transform weights to Ŵ=(W−µ)/α. Ternarize via Ti=Q(Ŵi ; ∆). The transformed weights can even reverse signs vs W, enabling outlier-aware redistribution.

Component 2 — Softened Ternarization. Replace the non-differentiable hard ternarizer with f(W;s,∆)=[tanh(s(W−∆))+tanh(s(W+∆))]/[2·tanh(s)]. Over normalized calibration time t∈[0,1]: at t=0 output W (identity), for 0

Component 3 — Sliding-layer reconstruction. CAT-Q plugs into the SliderQuant framework (Wang et al., 2026) and optimizes a window-wise output reconstruction loss argmin_{A,T} ||F(W,X) − F(A·T, X)||²² over a sliding window of l layers (neighbouring layers become mutually aware), rather than the usual layer-wise weight reconstruction. This output-level objective induces implicit weight reconstruction and eases optimization.

Models. 10 LLMs: Qwen3-1.7B/4B/8B/14B/32B, Llama2-7B/70B (dense), and MoE Qwen3-30B-A3B, Qwen3-235B-A22B, Ring-flash-2.0 (100B-A6.1B). Benchmarks: zero-shot PIQA, ARC-easy, ARC-challenge, HellaSwag, Winogrande (accuracy %). Hardware: 8×A100-80GB. Deployment: llama.cpp with TQ1_0 (CPU) / TQ2_0 (GPU) ternary formats, profiled on Intel Core i9-13900KF and NVIDIA RTX 4090, batch 1, 512 generated tokens.

Critical Assessment

  • Evaluation is narrow in task coverage. All main results are zero-shot accuracy on five commonsense reasoning benchmarks (PIQA, ARC-e/c, HellaSwag, Winogrande). The paper itself flags in the appendix a "pilot study of CAT-Q on challenging mathematics and coding tasks" but the main tables do not report MATH/GSM8K/HumanEval — so the headline competitiveness vs BitNet/TriLM rests on commonsense accuracy only, where ternary models are known to behave more forgivingly than on math/code.
  • QAT baselines are compared at matched parameter count, not matched training compute or architecture. BitNet v1/v2 and TriLM are trained from scratch with ternary-aware QAT, whereas CAT-Q starts from a pre-trained FP16 Qwen3/Llama2 checkpoint whose pretraining cost is not counted in the "100,000× fewer tokens" framing — the comparison conflates quantization-cost with total training-cost.
  • W1.58A8 results are weaker and under-reported. Table 3 only reports W1.58A8 for the 1.7B/4B/8B Qwen3 trio (avg 50.72/56.39/60.96) and never gives W1.58A8 for the 14B–235B models, so the claim of "broader applicability" of the W1.58A8 config is supported only at small scale.
  • Deployment claims rely on llama.cpp TQ1_0/TQ2_0 kernels, whose throughput numbers are specific to the i9-13900KF / RTX 4090 pair and a batch size of 1; no server-class GPU or batched-throughput number is given, so the practical inference-speedup claim is narrow.
  • Absolute accuracy at 1.58-bit is still substantially degraded. E.g. Qwen3-4B drops from 68.25 (W16A16) to 57.06 avg (W1.58A16) — an 11-point absolute drop — and Qwen3-235B-A22B loses ~10.5 avg points (79.59→69.09); the wins over BitNet are relative wins among low-accuracy ternary models.

Strengths

  • Genuinely PTQ (512 C4 samples, no original training data), removing data-privacy and compute barriers of QAT ternarization.
  • The LM + ST formulation is principled: LM addresses distributional misalignment, ST addresses non-differentiability, and the ablations cleanly isolate each factor's contribution.
  • Strong scale story: first PTQ ternarization up to 235B (incl. MoE), with the scaling-trend observation that degradation shrinks with model size.
  • Beats BitNet v1/v2, TriLM and TernaryLLM at matched scales on five benchmarks at ~100,000× lower token cost.
  • Code released; integrates with the SliderQuant sliding-layer pipeline and llama.cpp deployment (TQ1_0/TQ2_0), lowering adoption friction.
  • Thorough ablations (γ, s0, group size, sample count, LM-vs-SA-vs-DL, learnable-vs-fixed ∆, with-vs-without µ).

Limitations

  • Evaluation is commonsense-only in the main paper; math/coding only in an appendix pilot, so generalization to harder reasoning is unverified in the headline tables.
  • "100,000× fewer tokens" compares only quantization-stage tokens, ignoring the cost of the pre-trained FP16 checkpoint CAT-Q starts from.
  • W1.58A8 (activation quantization) results are limited to ≤8B models; activation-quantization behavior at 14B–235B is unreported.
  • Deployment throughput measured only at batch 1 on one CPU and one consumer GPU; no batched/server-GPU numbers.
  • Substantial absolute accuracy loss remains (≥10 avg points at small scale), so ternary models are still far from their FP16 counterparts on accuracy-sensitive tasks.
  • Group size default 128 is chosen to match TriLM/Tequila rather than tuned per model; Table 12 shows group 64 is better but unused, leaving some accuracy on the table for comparability.

Relevance

直接映射到您的 「计算可靠性与精度误差（Computation Reliability & Precision Errors）」 方向：CAT-Q 的核心命题就是如何在把权重压到极端低位（1.58-bit 三值）时控制数值精度损失与重构误差——LM 抑制离群值导致的量化误差、ST 解决非可微优化收敛，本质上是"低位数值表示下的可靠性工程"。同时也与 「LLM 推理评估（LLM Inference Evaluation）」 相关：它在五个零样本常识推理基准上系统评估了量化模型，并给出 llama.cpp 部署的实测吞吐/内存数据；以及与 「LLM 训练稳定性与可观测性（LLM Training Stability & Observability）」 间接相关——ST 的两阶段可微→硬量化接力正是为了解决低位量化优化的稳定性问题。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. The paper notes an appendix pilot on math/coding tasks — how large is the CAT-Q vs BitNet/TriLM gap on MATH/GSM8K/HumanEval, and does the relative ordering (CAT-Q > BitNet v2) invert on harder reasoning where every token of precision matters more?
  2. The "100,000× fewer tokens" claim counts only calibration tokens; if one amortizes the FP16 pretraining cost of the Qwen3/Llama2 base checkpoint, what is the total compute comparison versus training BitNet v2 from scratch at matched final accuracy?
  3. ST's differentiable transition function f(·) depends on s0=30 and γ=0.8; is there a principled (e.g. loss-curve-based) schedule for annealing s rather than the linear ramp, and would it improve the 1024-sample setting where default ST already plateaus?
  4. Table 12 shows group size 64 beats the default 128 on both models — how much of CAT-Q's reported gap to TriLM/Tequila persists if those baselines are also re-run at group 64, i.e. is the win method-driven or group-size-driven?
  5. W1.58A8 is only reported up to 8B; does the activation-quantization degradation stay bounded at 14B–235B (especially for MoE with fewer activated parameters, which the paper flags as more sensitive)?
  6. The disentangled learning drops µ in deployment (W≈αT) — does reintroducing µ at deployment time (with a fused kernel) buy back accuracy without sacrificing the TQ1_0/TQ2_0 throughput gains reported in Table 13?
