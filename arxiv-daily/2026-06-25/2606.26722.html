Socratic agents for autonomous scientific discovery in high-dimensional physical systems

arXiv: 2606.26722

Date: 2026-06-25

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26722

TL;DR: AHOIS is a five-agent multi-agent AI scientist that operationalizes "Socratic midwifery" as the computational principle for epistemic autonomy. A physics-critic agent (Duzhi) interrogates candidate hypotheses via clarification, constraint checking, causal probing and counterexample generation without supplying answers. On a real multimode-fibre optical platform it autonomously discovered and validated a random-interference backscattering encoder (16×16 measurements, effective rank 56.9, 76.97% MNIST / 83.17% Fashion-MNIST), adaptive sparse scanning, modality-specific failure diagnosis, and reproduced a published MMF imaging protocol on a non-original configuration, while ablations show Socratic interrogation improves physics consistency, hypothesis completeness, uncertainty calibration and plan validity.

Key Contributions

  • Socratic midwifery as a computational principle for epistemic autonomy. Rather than moving from "procedural autonomy" (fixed human-defined objectives/action-spaces) to fully open-ended science, the authors target the intermediate gap: can an agent interrogate how it knows what it claims to know? Duzhi is explicitly constrained to question, never propose conclusions.
  • Five-agent architecture with a shared scientific state. Role separation (strategy, hardware, integrity, modelling, physics-critic) enables inspection, replay, and isolation of the Socratic contribution from execution and analysis.
  • Autonomous discovery on real hardware, not simulation. AHOIS autonomously proposed the random-interference backscattering encoder hypothesis and validated it on a physical multimode-fibre platform — yielding 16×16 measurements with effective rank 56.9, classification accuracies of 76.97% (MNIST) and 83.17% (Fashion-MNIST).
  • Evidence-directed adaptive sparse scanning. A coarse-to-fine, scene-driven sampling policy retained video-rate recognition (~48.5 fps) at recognition parity, vs. 0.47 fps for full raster scanning — a >100× speedup.
  • Modality-specific failure diagnosis and paper-to-platform reproduction. The agents distinguished speckle-dominated (far-field) from noise-dominated (fluorescence) degradation, selecting SCNet and MFNet respectively, and translated a published MMF imaging study into an executable local workflow including a self-designed residual-attention reconstruction network.

Methodology

AHOIS decomposes autonomous experimentation into five interacting agents coordinated through a shared scientific state recording the current hypothesis, assumptions, proposed action, expected observation, acquired data, uncertainty, and next decision. Boxue (strategy) interprets a high-level goal and emits machine-readable experimental plans. Gewu (hardware abstraction) converts plans into DMD holograms, laser/camera triggers and metadata. Mingde (system integrity) audits exposure, saturation, dark-frame validity, frame completeness and speckle repeatability. Qiushi (modelling) builds data loaders, reconstruction models, hyperparameter searches and analyses. Duzhi (Socratic physics critic) operates a four-level interrogation protocol — clarification, physical-constraint checking, causal probing, counterexample generation — and is constrained to withhold direct answers.

The testbed is a real dual-modality (far-field + fluorescence) multimode-fibre optical platform with a DMD (624×624 active region), a 100 µm / 0.22 NA step-index illumination MMF calibrated via phase-stepped interferometry (~7,500 input patterns, ~173,000 addressable output positions), an adjacent collection MMF, and modality-specific detectors. A human supervisor acts only as a safety/quality gate at predefined decision points.

The Socratic loop converts candidate explanations into structured scientific states (mechanism, assumptions, competing interpretations, predicted outcomes, discriminating experiments, uncertainty estimates, rejection conditions), which are then translated into executable operations; measurements flow back into the loop to revise the agent's physical model and select the next experiment. Evaluation uses physics consistency, hypothesis completeness, uncertainty calibration (0–5 rubric), experimental-plan validity, invalid-plan rate, steps-to-valid-plan, planning efficiency, and downstream task performance.

Results & Highlights

  • Backscattered random-interference encoding (discovered, not prescribed). Prompted only with "what else could this system do?", AHOIS reframed backscattered speckle as a physical encoder. The 16×16 encoder matrices showed non-redundant correlations, an effective rank of 56.9, and a usable dynamic range; a compact classifier reached 76.97% (MNIST) and 83.17% (Fashion-MNIST) directly on the encoder matrices without any reconstructed image.
  • Adaptive sparse scanning. A coarse-to-fine, ROI-driven policy identified a working regime (~48.5 fps, background 1/12 + ROI 1/8 density) where both ROI and content recognition succeeded, while full raster scanning was 0.47 fps — over two orders of magnitude slower for the same recognition.
  • Modality-specific diagnosis. Far-field speckle degradation invoked SCNet; fluorescence noise invoked MFNet — selected from observed image physics, not preset.
  • Paper-to-platform reproduction. Over a 38-step trajectory, the agents interpreted a published MMF study, adapted the protocol to local hardware, completed pilot autonomous acquisition, and built both a U-Net baseline and a self-designed residual-attention reconstruction network (with channel attention and a compound pixel+SSIM loss). Large-scale repetitive acquisition was deliberately performed by researchers.
  • Socratic ablations. Physics consistency rose from ~2 to >4.5 (0–5 scale) across seven questioning rounds. Mean-utility contribution: causal probing 0.83 > counterexample 0.74 > constraint-checking 0.58 > clarification 0.42. The full Socratic Duzhi beat Boxue-alone, generic-reviewer, and non-Socratic-Duzhi configurations on normalized physics consistency, plan validity, planning efficiency and task performance, with the lowest invalid-plan rate and fewest steps to a valid plan.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (high). The framing of "epistemic vs. procedural autonomy" and the operationalization of Socratic midwifery as a non-answer-supplying critic is a genuinely fresh contribution relative to debate/self-reflection agents, which typically surface assumptions but often propose corrections. The paper distinguishes "form of criticism" from "amount of criticism," which is a testable thesis.
  • Rigor (moderate-to-high). Ablations with four matched configurations and a pre-registered rubric for three epistemic scores are commendable. The optics experiments are on real hardware, not simulation. However, the epistemic scores (physics consistency, etc.) are judged on a 0–5 rubric whose inter-rater reliability and blinding are not reported in the main text; "downstream task performance" claims rest on small benchmarks (MNIST, Fashion-MNIST).
  • Reproducibility (moderate). Methods are detailed (fibre core/NA, DMD region, ~7,500 calibration patterns, scan densities, loss functions, token accounting). However, language-model configuration, prompts and full trajectories are deferred to Supplementary Information (not in scope here), and the hardware is bespoke — exact physical replication would be difficult. Statistical analysis is honest about the absence of a prospective power calculation.
  • Significance (high for the subfield). If the Socratic-critic contribution generalizes, it offers a transferable architectural principle for adaptive microscopes, scattering imaging, photonic processors and autonomous synthesis — the paper argues this explicitly.
  • Red flags / caveats. (1) Headline classification accuracies (76.97% / 83.17%) are modest and on toy benchmarks; the "discovery" claim leans on the autonomous process, not on SOTA accuracy. (2) Paper-to-platform "autonomous acquisition" was pilot-scale only; researchers did the bulk data collection, so claims of end-to-end autonomy should be read with that boundary in mind (the authors acknowledge this honestly). (3) Human supervision remains in the loop at decision points, weakening strong "autonomy" claims. (4) Socratic interrogation is natural-language only, introducing definitional ambiguity the authors themselves flag as a limitation. (5) No claim of inventing new physical laws — the system operates within a known conceptual domain. (6) The four-level questioning taxonomy and rubric-based scoring could be biased toward confirming the proposed mechanism; an adversarial reviewer pushing for negative results would strengthen the work.

Strengths

  • A principled, well-motivated distinction between procedural and epistemic autonomy, with an explicit mechanism (Socratic critic) to close the gap.
  • Real, non-trivial hardware testbed (multimode fibre with mode mixing, drift, modality-dependent noise) that genuinely stresses interpretation, rather than a simulator.
  • Clean ablation isolating the form of criticism, with a controlled comparison (generic reviewer, non-Socratic critic, Socratic critic).
  • Honest delineation of what the agent did (interpretation, adaptation, pilot acquisition, model design) vs. what researchers did (repetitive large-scale acquisition).
  • Transferable architectural principle, clearly connected to adjacent domains (autonomous synthesis, photonic processors, adaptive microscopy).

Limitations

  • Socratic interrogation is implemented in natural language, introducing ambiguity in assumptions/constraints/falsification criteria; future work needs formal, machine-verifiable representations (causal graphs, optical models, verifiable protocols).
  • Human supervision remains mandatory at decision points; stronger autonomy requires formal hardware-action verification, explicit risk models and auditable intervention policies.
  • The system does not invent new physical laws; it constructs, discriminates and revises mechanistic explanations within a known domain. Open-ended hypothesis generation beyond a predefined conceptual library is left to future work.
  • Headline benchmarks are small (MNIST/Fashion-MNIST); statistical power was not prospectively computed; inter-rater reliability of the 0–5 epistemic rubric is not reported in the main text.
  • Paper-to-platform autonomy is partial — repetitive acquisition was human-performed — so end-to-end autonomous labour is not demonstrated.
  • Reproducibility is hindered by bespoke hardware and prompt/model details deferred to supplementary materials.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How would AHOIS perform if Duzhi's questioning were grounded in formal causal graphs or machine-verifiable optical models rather than natural language, and would that raise or lower discovery throughput?
  • Can the Socratic-critic principle be transferred to non-optical high-dimensional systems (autonomous synthesis, cryo-EM, adaptive microscopy) where the "physical frame" is even less structured?
  • What is the inter-rater reliability of the 0–5 physics-consistency / hypothesis-completeness / uncertainty-calibration rubrics, and do the ablation conclusions survive adversarial graders who do not know which configuration produced each trace?
  • Does Socratic interrogation ever prevent discovery by over-constraining a correct but non-obvious hypothesis — i.e., what is its false-negative rate on genuinely novel mechanisms?
  • How should the boundary between "agent reasoning" and "human repetitive execution" be quantified and benchmarked so that autonomous-labour claims become comparable across systems?
  • Could Duzhi be extended into a falsification-first mode that actively searches for evidence against the agent's preferred hypothesis, mitigating confirmation bias in the discovery loop?
