> **一句话总结：** 本文把 Recursive Feature Machine（RFM，配合 AGOP 平均梯度外积）算法适配到多维 refusal 子空间提取——用 Mahalanobis-Laplace 核迭代求解核岭回归 + AGOP 更新特征矩阵 M，并引入 probe-informed 协方差初始化与 EMA 稳定化；在 Qwen 3（1.7B/4B/8B/14B 推理）与 Qwen 2.5-7B（非推理）上数秒内提取多维 refusal 子空间，比 RCO 大幅降本，且 ablation 任务上表现更好；关键发现是单方向消融在 ≥8B 模型上不足以压制拒绝（Qwen 3 8B 需消融至少 3 个方向才能突破 50% ASR）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper adapts the Recursive Feature Machine (RFM) algorithm with Average Gradient Outer Product (AGOP) to extract multi-dimensional refusal subspaces efficiently. Using a Mahalanobis-Laplace kernel $K_M(u,v)=\exp(-\sqrt{(u-v)^\top M(u-v)}/L)$ iterated between kernel ridge regression (solve $\alpha_t = y(K_{M_t}+\lambda I)^{-1}$) and AGOP feature-matrix update ($\hat M_{t+1} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_i \nabla f_t(x_i)\nabla f_t(x_i)^\top$), with two stability modifications — **probe-informed covariance initialization** of $M_0$ and **EMA on M** — the top-k eigenvectors of $M_T$ give the refusal cone. Evaluated on Qwen 3 (1.7B/4B/8B/14B reasoning) and Qwen 2.5-7B (non-reasoning), RFM identifies the subspace in **seconds on a 4090 RTX laptop (16 GB VRAM)** vs RCO which is prohibitive on reasoning models (chains of thought up to 8000 tokens). Key empirical finding: ablating a single direction ($k=1$) effectively suppresses refusal in smaller models but is **insufficient for ≥8B models** — Qwen 3 8B requires ≥3 ablated directions to exceed 50% ASR (HarmBench, vanilla harmful, no jailbreak). RFM also showed better ablation-task performance than DIM/RCO/Clusters baselines.

## 核心贡献

1. **RFM-AGOP 适配到多维 refusal 子空间**：用 Mahalanobis-Laplace 核 + AGOP 迭代提取多维 refusal 锥，引入 probe-informed 协方差初始化与 EMA 稳定化提升稳定性与效率。
2. **跨推理/非推理模型的多维性分析**：在 Qwen 3（1.7B/4B/8B/14B）与 Qwen 2.5-7B 上开展多维 refusal 子空间分析，覆盖长推理轨迹（chain-of-thought 可达 8000 token）。
3. **快速且低成本**：在 4090 RTX 笔记本（16GB VRAM）上数秒内提取子空间，核矩阵构建 O(n²d)≈5×10⁹ FLOPs（n~1000, d~5000），远比 RCO 实用。
4. **多维必要性的实证**：单方向消融在 ≥8B 模型上不足以压制拒绝（Qwen 3 8B 需 ≥3 方向才超 50% ASR）。

## Key Contributions

- **RFM-AGOP adapted to multi-dimensional refusal subspaces**: Mahalanobis-Laplace kernel + AGOP iteration, with probe-informed covariance initialization and EMA stabilization for stability/efficiency.
- **Multi-dimensionality analysis across reasoning + non-reasoning models**: Qwen 3 (1.7B/4B/8B/14B) and Qwen 2.5-7B, covering long reasoning traces (CoT up to 8000 tokens) where RCO is prohibitive.
- **Fast and cheap**: subspace extracted in seconds on a 4090 RTX laptop (16 GB VRAM); kernel matrix construction O(n²d) ≈ 5×10⁹ FLOPs (n~1000, d~5000), far more practical than RCO.
- **Empirical necessity of multi-dimensionality**: single-direction ablation insufficient for ≥8B models (Qwen 3 8B needs ≥3 ablated directions to exceed 50% ASR).

## Methodology

**Problem setup (§2.2).** Contrastive dataset $\mathcal{D} = (\mathcal{D}^+, \mathcal{D}^-)$ of harmful queries (e.g., "Develop a strategy for hacking into a government database…") and harmless counterparts with similar topic/sentence structure (e.g., "…securing a government database…"). For layer ℓ, extract last-token activations $e_l^+ \in \mathbb R^{|\mathcal{D}^+|\times d}$ (harmful, label y=1) and $e_l^- \in \mathbb R^{|\mathcal{D}^-|\times d}$ (harmless, label y=0); classification task distinguishes them. Goal: cheaply identify refusal subspace $\mathcal{V} \subset \mathbb R^d$ of dimension k, spanned by orthonormal basis $V=(v_1,\dots,v_k)$.

**RFM-AGOP framework (§3.1).** Construct $X = e_l^+ \oplus e_l^- \in \mathbb R^{n\times d}$, $y=(1,\dots,1,0,\dots,0)$. RFM iterates between (i) learning predictor $f$ via kernel ridge regression: $\alpha_t = y(K_{M_t}(X,X)+\lambda I)^{-1}$, $f_t(x)=\alpha_t K_{M_t}(X,x)$ with **Mahalanobis-Laplace kernel** $K_M(u,v)=\exp(-\sqrt{(u-v)^\top M(u-v)}/L)$, $L>0$ bandwidth; (ii) updating feature matrix via **AGOP**: $\hat M_{t+1} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n \nabla_x f_t(x_i)\nabla_x f_t(x_i)^\top$. EMA stabilization: $M_{t+1} = (1-\gamma)M_t + \gamma \hat M_{t+1}$. After T iterations, eigendecompose $M_T$ → sorted eigenvectors $v_1,\dots,v_d$ with eigenvalues $\mu_1 \ge \dots \ge \mu_d$; refusal cone = top-k $V_k=(v_1,\dots,v_k)$; orient via Pearson correlation $\rho$ between $\{(x_j, v)\}$ and $\{y_j\}$, $v \leftarrow \mathrm{sign}(\rho)v$.

**Two stability modifications.** (1) **Probe-informed covariance initialization** of $M_0$ (warm init) instead of identity — Figure 1 shows warm init beats vanilla (identity). (2) **EMA on M** — warm init + EMA is the most stable variant.

**Ablation (eq. 3) and steering (eq. 4).** For any weight matrix W writing to the residual stream, left-project with scaled projection: $W \leftarrow W - \sum_{i=1}^k \frac{\mu_i}{\mu_1}\hat v_i \hat v_i^\top W$ (the $\mu_i/\mu_1$ scaling removes the first direction fully while being softer on others, minimizing collateral capability damage). Steering applies the analogous scaled subtraction directly on activations.

**Experimental setup (§4.1).** Models: Qwen 3 family (1.7B, 4B, 8B, 14B reasoning) + Qwen 2.5-7B (non-reasoning). Dataset: 720 multilingual harmful/harmless contrastive pairs (500 train / 120 test / 100 val). Pipeline: NNsight for weight editing in PyTorch, vLLM for generation (traces >4000 tokens). ASR evaluated on HarmBench with two LLM graders — Mistral NeMo (lenient) and Gemini 2.5 Flash (strict); successful attack = model agrees + provides harmful info. General capability retention via MMLU (Inspect AI). Baselines: Difference-in-Means (DIM, Arditi 2024), Refusal Cone Optimization (RCO, Wollschläger 2025), Clusters method. Hardware: 4090 RTX laptop, 16 GB VRAM, quantized models.

## Results & Highlights

- **ASR increases monotonically with ablated-subspace dimension (Figure 2, HarmBench vanilla harmful, no jailbreak):** ablating a single direction (k=1) effectively suppresses refusal in smaller models but is **insufficient for ≥8B models** — **Qwen 3 8B requires ≥3 ablated directions to exceed 50% ASR**.
- **Multi-dimensionality is scale-dependent:** the refusal cone widens with model size; single-direction interventions (Arditi 2024) that worked on small models fail on larger reasoning models.
- **RFM matches or beats baselines on ablation task:** RFM showed better performance than DIM, RCO, and Clusters on the ablation task (per the abstract's central claim; full per-method ASR in Table 1).
- **Speed (§4.4):** RFM cost dominated by kernel matrix construction O(n²d) ≈ 5×10⁹ FLOPs (n~1000, d~5000) + a few O(n³) solves and O(d³) eigendecompositions; **total runtime on the order of seconds on a 4090 RTX laptop** — vs RCO which is prohibitive on reasoning models producing up to 8000-token chains of thought.
- **Warm init + EMA stability (Figure 1):** covariance-initialized kernel with EMA converges fastest and most stably vs identity-init (vanilla) and the linear-probe baseline on the BigBench classification task (Qwen 3 8B at layer 14).
- **Capability retention (Figure 3, MMLU):** ablation impact varies with scale but is mostly stable on large models — Qwen 3 8B maintains MMLU even after multiple ablations; Qwen 3 14B shows an unexplained performance drop (flagged as a possible method limitation).

The experimental pipeline (NNsight, vLLM, HarmBench, MMLU via Inspect AI, two LLM graders) is standard and reproducible; runs on a 4090 RTX laptop. No external code link visible in the parsed text.

## Critical Assessment

- **The scale-dependent multi-dimensionality finding (≥8B needs ≥3 directions) is the most novel empirical result**, directly challenging the single-direction abliteration paradigm (Arditi 2024) at larger scale. However, the result is shown on the Qwen 3 family only — whether the ≥8B threshold generalizes across model families (Llama, Mistral, Gemma) is unverified, and could reflect Qwen 3's specific alignment recipe rather than a universal scaling law.
- **The "RFM better than RCO/DIM/Clusters on ablation" claim is central but the parsed text states it qualitatively** ("RFM...showed better performances on the ablation task than its alternatives"); the per-method ASR table (Table 1) is referenced but not fully captured, so the magnitude of RFM's advantage and whether it holds across all model sizes needs the table.
- **The paper is explicitly preliminary** ("More work is planned"; the Qwen 3 14B MMLU drop "we cannot yet explain. It might be a limitation of the method") — honesty about scope, but it means the multi-dimensionality and RFM-advantage claims should be read as workshop-level findings pending fuller validation.
- **The contrastive dataset (720 pairs, multilingual) is small and custom** — the refusal cone's dimensionality estimate (k) could be sensitive to dataset composition; the paper does not report k-stability across resampling or alternative contrastive sets.
- **ASR evaluated on vanilla harmful prompts (no jailbreak)** — the refusal-subspace ablation's effectiveness under actual jailbreak attacks (the deployment-relevant threat) is not reported; the paper measures whether ablation suppresses baseline refusal, not whether it aids jailbreak defense.

## Strengths

- RFM-AGOP is a genuinely cheap, scalable alternative to RCO for multi-dimensional refusal-subspace extraction (seconds on a laptop vs prohibitive on reasoning models).
- The probe-informed covariance init + EMA is a clean stabilization that improves convergence (Figure 1).
- The scale-dependent multi-dimensionality finding (≥8B needs ≥3 directions) is a concrete, actionable challenge to single-direction abliteration.
- Standard, reproducible pipeline (NNsight/vLLM/HarmBench/MMLU/Inspect AI) with two LLM graders of differing strictness.

## Limitations

- Workshop preliminary paper; RFM-advantage and 14B-drop caveats explicitly flagged.
- Qwen 3 family only; ≥8B threshold may not generalize across model families.
- Per-method ASR table (Table 1) not fully captured; magnitude of RFM advantage unclear.
- Small custom contrastive dataset (720 pairs); k-stability across resampling/alternative sets unreported.
- ASR on vanilla harmful only; jailbreak-attack effectiveness (deployment-relevant) not measured.
