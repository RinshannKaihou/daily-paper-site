> **一句话总结：** 本文为循环表示提出"有限滞后算子几何"——从观测到的源-后继对 $(X_t, X_{t+\Delta})$ 估计有向条件传输律 $Q_\Delta$，导出源中心化传输张量 $G_\Delta$（精确分解为条件扩散 + 相干位移）与反对称坐标环流 $W_\Delta^\rho$，证明其能检测静态 carré-du-champ 几何无法记录的确定性循环运动；在性能匹配的 repeat-copy 网络上揭示 Elman/GRU/LSTM 在总传输尺度与相干位移迹上的架构依赖差异（Elman 的 tr($\bar G_\Delta$) 比 GRU/LSTM 大约 15%）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper develops finite-lag operator geometry for recurrent hidden states: from observed source–successor pairs $(X_t, X_{t+\Delta})$ it estimates a directed conditional transport law $Q_\Delta(dy|x)$ via a dense Gaussian source-smoothing operator, derives a source-centered transport tensor $G_\Delta = C_\Delta/2 + \tau m_\Delta m_\Delta^\top/2$ (exact spread + coherent-displacement decomposition), and an antisymmetric coordinate circulation $W_\Delta^\rho$ summarizing directed lagged flow. It proves affine covariance, Lipschitz stability of the dense estimator, and a finite-lag separation theorem (deterministic recurrent motion visible to $G_\Delta$ even when the infinitesimal carré du champ is zero). On performance-matched repeat-copy networks (Elman/GRU/LSTM-64, 5 seeds), Elman shows ~15% larger total transport tr($\bar G_\Delta$) (1.0085) than GRU (0.8673) and LSTM (0.8815), with ~16–26% larger coherent-displacement trace; the coherent-displacement fraction $F_\Delta^\rho$ (~0.45–0.50) is metric/resolution-dependent and not a stable architecture identifier.

## 核心贡献

1. **有限滞后算子几何的形式化**：定义条件传输律 $Q_\Delta(dy|x)=\mathcal L(X_{t+\Delta}\in dy\mid X_t=x)$、源中心化传输张量 $G_\Delta$、其精确"条件扩散 + 相干位移"分解，以及反对称坐标环流 $W_\Delta^\rho$。
2. **结构性定理**：张量可观测量对仿射变换协变（标量摘要依赖度量）；稠密高斯估计量在有界轨迹云上 Lipschitz 稳定；有限滞后源中心化传输与无穷小 carré-du-champ 几何可分离——确定性循环运动可被 $G_\Delta$ 捕捉而前者为零。
3. **线性-高斯闭式标定**：在 $Y=AX+\xi$ 下给出 $\bar G_\Delta$、$W_\Delta$ 关于更新矩阵 $A_\Delta$、源协方差、新息协方差的解析式，分离新息扩散、更新偏移 $A_\Delta-I$（相干位移）、有向分量三种机制。
4. **受控与真实网络验证**：线性-高斯标定最大相对误差 <0.7%；repeat-copy 网络揭示 Elman/GRU/LSTM 在总传输尺度与相干位移迹上的架构依赖差异。

## Key Contributions

- **Finite-lag operator geometry formalized**: the conditional transport law $Q_\Delta(dy|x) = \mathcal L(X_{t+\Delta}\in dy \mid X_t=x)$, the source-centered transport tensor $G_\Delta(x) = \frac{1}{2\tau}\int (y-x)(y-x)^\top Q_\Delta(dy|x)$, its exact spread/displacement decomposition $2G_\Delta = C_\Delta + \tau m_\Delta m_\Delta^\top$, and the antisymmetric coordinate circulation $W_\Delta^\rho$.
- **Structural theorems**: (i) affine covariance of tensorial observables with metric-dependent scalar summaries; (ii) Lipschitz stability of the dense Gaussian estimator on bounded trajectory clouds at fixed bandwidth; (iii) a finite-lag separation theorem — deterministic recurrent motion at lag ∆ is visible to $G_\Delta$ even when the infinitesimal carré du champ of a first-order deterministic generator is zero (this is why source-centering, not conditional-mean centering, is the key choice).
- **Linear-Gaussian closed form (calibration)**: under $Y = AX + \xi$, gives $\bar G_\Delta, W_\Delta$ in terms of the update $A_\Delta$, source covariance, and innovation covariance, separating innovation spread ($\Sigma_\xi$), update offset ($A_\Delta - I$, coherent displacement), and directed components.
- **Empirical validation**: controlled linear-Gaussian calibration and a performance-matched repeat-copy case study across Elman/GRU/LSTM revealing architecture-dependent differences in total transport scale and coherent-displacement trace.

## Methodology

**Primitive — finite-lag transport law.** For hidden states $h_t^{(s)} \in \mathbb R^d$, fix lag ∆ ≥ 1, set τ = ∆·dt, form source–successor pairs $(x_i, y_i) = (h_t^{(s)}, h_{t+\Delta}^{(s)})$. Define $Q_\Delta(dy|x) = \mathcal L(X_{t+\Delta} \in dy \mid X_t = x)$ with associated operator $(P_\Delta f)(x) = \int f(y) Q_\Delta(dy|x)$. **No assumption that hidden state alone is autonomously Markov** — for input-driven networks $P_\Delta$ is induced jointly by the recurrence and input distribution.

**Dense Gaussian estimator (§3.1).** Smooth in source space with bandwidth ε:
$$w_i(x_q) = \frac{\exp(-\|x_q - x_i\|^2/4\varepsilon)}{\sum_j \exp(-\|x_q - x_j\|^2/4\varepsilon)}, \quad \hat Q_\Delta^\Delta(dy|x_q) = \sum_i w_i(x_q) \delta_{y_i}(dy).$$
This is a source-smoothing operator for successor-valued transport (not a transition matrix whose column index is the successor). At finite bandwidth the estimator queries a resolution-smoothed conditional law; bandwidth dependence is reported explicitly via sweeps. Hard k-NN approximation is non-Lipschitz in the cloud (Appendix A.6) and used only where stated.

**Source-centered transport tensor (§3.2, Def 2).** $G_\Delta^\rho(x) = \frac{1}{2\tau}\int (y-x)(y-x)^\top Q_\Delta(dy|x)$, averaged to $\bar G_\Delta^\rho = \int G_\Delta^\rho(x)\rho(dx)$. With $m_\Delta(x) = \tau^{-1}\mathbb E[Y-X\mid X=x]$ and $C_\Delta(x) = \tau^{-1}\mathrm{Cov}(Y-X\mid X=x)$, **Proposition 1 (source-centered decomposition)**: $2G_\Delta(x) = C_\Delta(x) + \tau m_\Delta(x)m_\Delta(x)^\top$ — i.e. transport decomposes exactly into conditional spread + coherent displacement. Source-centering (not conditional-mean centering) is the key choice: it retains deterministic motion at the chosen lag. The **coherent displacement fraction** $F_\Delta^\rho$ = coherent-trace / total.

**Coordinate circulation (§3.3).** $W_\Delta^\rho = \tau^{-1}(\mathbb E_\rho[\tilde X \tilde Y^\top] - \mathbb E_\rho[\tilde Y \tilde X^\top])$ in centered coordinates; real, skew-symmetric, eigenvalues purely imaginary in conjugate pairs; **$W_\Delta^\rho = 0$ for a stationary reversible Markov law**. Reported via $\|W_\Delta^\rho\|_F$, largest imaginary eigenvalue $\omega_{\max}$, and relative circulation $r_{circ} = \|W_\Delta^\rho\|_F / \mathrm{tr}(\bar G_\Delta^\rho)$.

**Linear-Gaussian closed form (Thm 4).** Under $Y = AX + \xi$: coherent trace = β² (for $A = I + \beta B$, $\|B\|_F=1$), spread trace = $d\sigma^2$ — separating the three mechanisms ($\Sigma_\xi$ → spread; $A_\Delta - I$ → coherent displacement; antisymmetric part → directed circulation).

**Experiments.** (1) Controlled calibration: sweep $\beta \in \{0,0.25,0.5,0.75,1.0\}$, $\sigma \in \{0,0.1,0.25,0.5\}$ at $d=16$. (2) Affine covariance / dense stability: perturb cyclic-shift clouds. (3) Repeat-copy case study: Elman/GRU/LSTM, feature dim 4, recall-window-weighted loss, copy length 10 (performance-matched, 5 seeds) / 8 (capacity, memory-horizon, phase-profile). Dense estimator, ∆=1, median-heuristic bandwidth, center-RMS normalization.

## Results & Highlights

- **Linear-Gaussian closed form validated (§5.1):** across the $(\beta, \sigma)$ sweep at $d=16$, the maximum relative error of the population closed form is **< 0.7%**; coherent trace = β² and spread trace = $d\sigma^2$ match analytically. Directed statistic $\|W_\Delta^c\|_F$ grows ~linearly with γ (antisymmetric $A = \alpha I + \gamma J$) and vanishes at γ=0.
- **Affine covariance (Thm 1):** translations/orthogonal maps preserve Euclidean trace summaries; scalar dilations scale traces by the squared scale; metric correction $M' = A^{-\top} M A^{-1}$ restores original traces to numerical precision.
- **Dense stability (Thm 2):** perturbing a fixed cyclic-shift cloud by Gaussian noise of scale $\sigma_p$ (fixed bandwidth), the observed slopes of changes in $\mathrm{tr}(\bar G_\Delta)$, coherent-displacement trace, and $\|W_\Delta^c\|_F$ are **close to 1**, consistent with the Lipschitz guarantee; sparse k-NN approximations change local resolution and lack this guarantee.
- **Repeat-copy architecture comparison (Table 2, performance-matched, ∆=1, 5 seeds):**
  - Total transport $\mathrm{tr}(\bar G_\Delta)$: **Elman-64 = 1.0085 ± 0.0008** vs **GRU-64 = 0.8673 ± 0.0076** vs **LSTM-64 = 0.8815 ± 0.0035** — Elman ~15% larger.
  - Coherent-displacement trace: Elman 1.0151 vs GRU 0.8051 vs LSTM 0.8721 — Elman ~16–26% larger.
  - Coherent fraction $F_\Delta^\rho$: 0.50 (Elman) / 0.46 (GRU) / 0.49 (LSTM) — **not a stable architecture identifier** (near 0.45–0.50, metric/resolution-dependent per sensitivity sweeps).
  - Static effective rank (snapshot baseline): Elman 25.5 vs GRU 12.6 vs LSTM 14.0 — illustrating that finite-lag transport and static snapshot geometry give complementary information.
- **Headline methodological finding:** finite-lag source-centered transport detects directed, lagged recurrent motion that static carré-du-champ geometry cannot record (separation theorem), and the strongest architecture-dependent signal is in *total transport scale + coherent-displacement trace*, not in the coherent fraction.

## Authors & Credibility

- Single author: **Kanishka Reddy**, Department of Applied Mathematics, University of Washington (stated in the PDF). Marked "Preprint" — no venue stated.
- The framework is positioned carefully against transfer-operator / Koopman / TICA / VAMP methods (cited refs [11–16, 23–29]): those estimate predictive operators or slow modes, whereas this work uses the finite-lag law *geometrically* via second moments. The linear-Gaussian analysis is explicitly framed as a calibration, not a model assumption — an honest scoping choice.

## Critical Assessment

- **The empirical evidence is thin beyond repeat-copy.** The only trained-network experiments are Elman/GRU/LSTM on a single synthetic task (repeat-copy) with hidden dim 4 and copy length 8–10; the framework's behavior on realistic recurrent/transformer workloads (language modeling, long-context, multi-layer residual streams where "recurrent" is implicit) is not demonstrated. The paper itself frames repeat-copy as a "compact recurrent case study," so the generalization claim is understated but real.
- **The coherent-displacement fraction $F_\Delta^\rho$ is shown to be metric- and resolution-dependent** (~0.45–0.50, unstable across bandwidth sweeps) — the paper honestly reports this, but it means only $\mathrm{tr}(\bar G_\Delta)$ and the coherent-displacement *trace* (not the fraction) are reliable architecture identifiers, narrowing the practical observable set.
- **Bandwidth ε is a free parameter** with material effect on the estimated law; the paper uses a median-heuristic default and reports sweeps (Appendix F.6) but does not give a principled selection rule, which limits off-the-shelf comparability.
- **The separation theorem (finite-lag vs carré-du-champ) is the strongest theoretical claim** but is demonstrated only on the controlled linear-Gaussian and cyclic-shift cloud settings; whether deterministic recurrent motion is captured on noisy, high-dimensional real hidden states (where the carré du champ is not exactly zero) is not shown.
- **The dense Gaussian estimator's $O(N^2)$ cost** at each query is acknowledged implicitly (k-NN is mentioned only as a non-Lipschitz approximation); scalability to large trajectory clouds is not analyzed.

## Strengths

- Clean, operator-first reframing: replaces static symmetric diffusion geometry with a *directed finite-lag* transport law, which is the structurally correct primitive for trajectories.
- Source-centering (vs conditional-mean centering) is an elegant, well-motivated choice that retains coherent deterministic motion.
- The exact decomposition $2G_\Delta = C_\Delta + \tau m_\Delta m_\Delta^\top$ and the linear-Gaussian closed form give interpretable, separately-validatable observables.
- Honest scoping: linear-Gaussian is a calibration; bandwidth dependence reported; stability theorem proven for the dense (not k-NN) estimator.

## Limitations

- Only repeat-copy (synthetic) trained-network experiments; no realistic recurrent/transformer workload.
- Coherent fraction is metric/resolution-dependent — only transport scale + coherent trace are reliable identifiers.
- Bandwidth ε lacks a principled selection rule; dense estimator is $O(N^2)$.
- Separation theorem validated only on controlled settings, not on noisy high-dimensional real hidden states.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. 有限滞后传输律在真实 transformer 残差流（多层、长上下文）上是否仍能分离相干位移与条件扩散？此时"循环"是隐式的（残差叠加），∆ 应如何选择？
2. 带宽 ε 能否从数据自适应选择（如 leave-one-out 似然或尺度空间骨架），以替代中位数启发式并保证跨研究可比性？
3. 相干位移迹作为架构标识符，在更大隐藏维度（d=128/512）与真实语言建模任务上是否仍能区分架构？
4. $W_\Delta^\rho$ 的反对称环流能否用于检测训练中的"旋转不稳定"或表征崩溃（representation collapse）？
5. 稠密高斯估计的 $O(N^2)$ 成本能否用随机傅里叶特征或 Nystöm 近似在不破坏 Lipschitz 保证的前提下扩展到大规模轨迹云？
6. 有限滞后分离定理在噪声高维真实隐藏状态（carré du champ 非零）下的退化形式是什么——能否给出信噪比阈值？
