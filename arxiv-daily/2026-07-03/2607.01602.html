ProWAFT: A ROMA-LPD Instance for Workload-Aware and Dynamic Fault Tolerance in FPGA-Based CNN Accelerators

arXiv: 2607.01602

Date: 2026-07-02

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01602

一句话总结 (一句话总结)

ProWAFT 是一种面向 FPGA 上 CNN 推理加速器的主动式、负载感知容错框架，通过部分重配置在不同分区上选择性施加 TMR（三模冗余），以最小化延迟、能耗与可靠性风险的复合目标，在不牺牲吞吐的前提下显著降低静态 TMR 的开销。

TL;DR (en)

ProWAFT is a proactive, workload-aware fault-tolerance framework for FPGA-based CNN accelerators. It uses partial reconfiguration to selectively apply triple modular redundancy (TMR) across reconfigurable partitions, jointly modeling fault propagation and reconfiguration overhead to minimize a composite latency–energy–reliability cost. On a ZCU104 platform with six reconfigurable regions, evaluated under time-varying SEU injection on ResNet-18, MobileNetV2, and EfficientNet-Lite, it beats static TMR and reactive recovery while keeping high task success rate, near-baseline throughput, and low online decision overhead.

Key Contributions (en)

  • Proactive paradigm: Moves beyond always-on redundancy (full TMR) and reactive recovery to a proactive, workload-aware fault-tolerance framework (ProWAFT) that selectively enables TMR via partial reconfiguration (PR) on a per-partition basis.
  • Composite objective optimization: Quantifies workload criticality and jointly models fault propagation and reconfiguration overhead, choosing configurations that minimize a composite latency–energy–reliability-risk cost.
  • Real-platform implementation: Implemented on a Xilinx Zynq UltraScale+ ZCU104 with six reconfigurable regions; evaluated on a 500-task trace from ResNet-18, MobileNetV2, and EfficientNet-Lite under time-varying SEU injection.
  • Effectiveness: Achieves lower composite cost than both static TMR and reactive reconfiguration while maintaining high task success rate, near-baseline throughput, and low online decision overhead.

Methodology

  • Platform & threat model: Targets SRAM-based FPGAs running CNN inference at the network edge, where transient single-event upsets (SEUs) flip configuration bits and cause silent data corruption. Always-on TMR is correct but expensive; reactive recovery pays on the critical path. Faults detected via a lightweight detector; per-partition fault probability $p_{fault}^{k}(t)$ varies over time.
  • ProWAFT pipeline (4 stages):
  1. Workload Criticality Score (WCS) — quantifies how sensitive $W_i$ is to hardware faults from operator type/shape/precision statistics, control criticality (0/1 flag for conditional paths), and $P_{error}(W_i)$ from lightweight pre-characterization (single-bit fault injection) stored as a compact table/regression.
  2. Fault-Aware Performance & Risk Modeling — Fault Propagation Function (FPF) / Residual Risk Score (RRS); a normalized severity weight $\rho_k \in [0,1]$ reflects how costly a fault in partition $P_k$ is; fault-aware latency/energy predictions $\tilde T, \tilde E$ couple risk with efficiency via offline-characterized $\epsilon_T, \epsilon_E$.
  3. Composite Cost Evaluation — per-configuration cost $C_{total}(C_j) = \eta_T \tilde T + \eta_E \tilde E + \eta_R \cdot \text{risk}$, with default weights $(\eta_T, \eta_E, \eta_R) = (0.4, 0.3, 0.3)$ ("balanced profile"); normalized to the fault-free static-base reference to keep the objective dimensionless. PR overhead folded in explicitly.
  4. Bi-level MDP Policy — determines the optimal proactive (switch / stay) decision minimizing expected composite cost under reconfiguration constraints.
  • Mechanism: Partial Reconfiguration (PR) is the lever — switches a partition between baseline and TMR-protected variants at runtime. Each partition $P_k$ has a configuration set $\mathcal{F}_k$ including both variants; $Ops_k(W_i)$ maps operations to $P_k$ at clock $f_k$ with power $P E_k(C_j)$.
  • Evaluation harness: Xilinx Zynq UltraScale+ ZCU104, 6 reconfigurable regions, two accelerator variants (baseline + TMR). 500-task CNN workload trace from ResNet-18, MobileNetV2, EfficientNet-Lite. Time-varying SEU injection with per-partition fault probability $p_{fault} \in [0.001, 0.01]$ (and a wider $[0.15, 0.85]$ sweep for stress); SEU rate within $[0.001, 0.01]$. Default composite weights $(0.4, 0.3, 0.3)$. Baselines: Static-Base, Static-TMR, Reactive-Reconfiguration (RR).

Results & Highlights

  • vs Static-TMR (Table 5): ProWAFT improves normalized throughput from 0.61 → 0.89 (+45.9%), reduces energy from 302.5 J → 210.7 J (−30.3%), and maintains a high success rate of 98.8% (only 1.2 pp below Static-TMR's perfect 100%).
  • vs Reactive-Reconfiguration (RR): ProWAFT improves throughput by +18.7%, reduces energy by −17.1%, improves success rate by +2.4 pp, and reduces composite cost $C_{total}$ from 0.78 → 0.54 (−30.8% relative reduction).
  • Adaptivity: protection decisions track both workload criticality and time-varying fault risk (Figure 3 stress trace), rather than using a fixed offline plan.
  • Online overhead: decision-making and PR overhead kept small and practical for real-time use.
  • Coverage: validated across three CNN families with distinct profiles (heavy ResNet-18, depthwise MobileNetV2, lite EfficientNet-Lite) on real ZCU104 hardware, not simulation-only.
