Subliminal Clocks: Latent Time Modelling in Diffusion Language Models

arXiv: 2607.01774

Date: 2026-07-03

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01774

一句话总结： 本文发现掩码扩散语言模型（LLaDA-1.5、Dream）虽不显式以时间步为条件，却在残差流中编码了一个与去噪进度 τ 相关的潜信号：MLP 探针跨所有层 R²>0.5（峰值 0.94）；该信号对应一条低维、有序的"时钟"流形（PC1 解释 >90% 方差），沿其方向做激活转向可系统性地、可预测地改变模型的置信度、熵与 token 分布。
TL;DR: The paper shows that absorbing-state Diffusion Language Models (DLMs) — which are NOT explicitly conditioned on a diffusion timestep — nevertheless internally encode a latent "denoising-progress" signal τ in their residual stream. On LLaDA-1.5 and Dream, per-layer MLP probes recover τ with R² > 0.5 across all layers (peaking at 0.94 at mid layers; ALL-token probe R² = 0.74→0.94 across the 32 layers); the probe-vs-discretized-bin correlation reaches 0.976 Pearson / 0.980 Spearman (LLaDA) and 0.962 / 0.974 (Dream). The signal corresponds to a low-dimensional ordered manifold (PC1 explains >90% variance; a parabola-like 2D curve on Dream layer 25), and steering along it at layer 29 produces predictable shifts in confidence, entropy, and KL divergence vs norm-matched random perturbations. Early-layer (layer 6) perturbations are progressively corrected by the network (probe predictions drift back to clean), while steering toward extreme τ=100 leaves a persistent residual discrepancy.

核心贡献

  1. 发现 DLM 内部存在去噪时间信号（RQ1）：尽管掩码扩散语言模型不显式以时间步为条件，其残差流中仍编码了与去噪进度 τ 相关的潜表示；逐层 MLP 探针可在无序列级上下文的情况下从单个 token 隐藏状态恢复 τ，且 [MASK] 与 non-[MASK] token 均携带该信息（说明信号分布式存在于残差流，而非局部于掩码位置）。
  2. 因果性转向验证（RQ2）：用按 τ 分组的均值激活向量构造方向，沿其做激活转向会系统性地改变模型输出的熵、置信度与 KL 散度（相比 norm-matched 随机扰动）；早期层扰动会被网络逐层纠正，极端目标 τ=100 难以完全补偿。
  3. 几何刻画（RQ3）：该信号呈低维有序结构——跨层均值向量的方差高度集中（PC1 解释 >90%），2D/3D PCA 投影呈抛物线/有序曲线，与模型在计数或时间相关任务上的已知几何相似。
  4. 两个代表性大模型一致：LLaDA-1.5 与 Dream 均展现该信号，跨 token 类型与跨层一致。

Key Contributions

  • DLMs internally encode a denoising-time signal (RQ1). Despite not being timestep-conditioned, LLaDA-1.5 and Dream encode τ in the residual stream; per-layer MLP probes operating on a single hidden state (no sequence context) recover τ with R² > 0.5 across all layers, and the signal is distributed across both [MASK] and non-[MASK] tokens.
  • The signal is causally important and steerable (RQ2). Mean-activation vectors grouped by τ define a direction; swapping the current-step mean vector for a target-τ one (eq. 4) systematically shifts confidence, entropy, and KL divergence versus norm-matched random perturbations. Early-layer (layer 6) perturbations are progressively corrected through depth; extreme targets (τ=100) leave a persistent residual.
  • Low-dimensional ordered geometry (RQ3). The set of τ-mean vectors is effectively 1D — PC1 explains >90% of intra-layer variance; 2D/3D PCA reveals a continuous ordered (parabola-like) curve, structurally similar to "clock" representations seen in counting/time-dependent tasks (Gurnee, Engels, Karkada, Modell).
  • Probe ↔ mean-vector agreement. The probe predictions correlate with the discretized τ-bin index at 0.976 Pearson / 0.980 Spearman (LLaDA) and 0.962 / 0.974 (Dream), confirming both methods recover the same latent direction.

Methodology

Background — absorbing-state DLMs. Training corrupts a clean sequence $x_0$ into $x_s$ by replacing each position with [MASK] w.p. $s \sim U(0,1]$; the model predicts $p_\theta(x_0^j \mid x_s)$ at masked positions with a cross-entropy loss reweighted by $1/s$. Inference runs T denoising steps from all-[MASK] to all-unmasked; the empirical denoising-progress proxy is $\tau_t := 1 - |M(x_t)|/L$ (fraction of unmasked tokens), which in expectation equals the complement of the continuous diffusion-time $s$ (Appendix A). Models studied: LLaDA-1.5 (32 layers) and Dream (28 layers).

RQ1 — Probing for τ (§3). Fit an MLP probe $\phi^{(l)}: \mathbb R^d \to (0,1)$ per layer minimizing $\mathcal L_{MLP} = \mathbb E_{t,n,j}[(\tau_t - \phi^{(l)}(h_{t,l,n}^{(j)}))^2]$. Three token subsets: [MASK] only, non-[MASK] only, ALL. Each probe sees a single hidden state — no sequence context — so high R² implies τ is encoded at the token level.

RQ2 — Steering (§4). Compute mean activation vectors per (step t, layer l): $\mu_{t,l} = \mathbb E_{n,j}[h_{t,l,n}^{(j)}]$ (discretize t into 100 bins → 3200 mean vectors for LLaDA, 2800 for Dream). Steering intervention: $h_{t,l}^{(j)} \leftarrow h_{t,l}^{(j)} - \mu_{t,l} + \mu_{\tau',l}$, where the shared component $\eta_l = \mathbb E_t[\mu_{t,l}]$ cancels (eq. 5). Verify the mean vectors track the probe signal via probe($\mu_{\tau',l}$) vs $\tau'/100$ correlation. Compare clean token distribution $p_{j,t,n}$ vs steered $\tilde p_{j,t,n}$ on entropy, confidence, KL divergence — and against norm-matched random perturbations as control.

RQ3 — Geometry (§5). PCA on the mean vectors: explained-variance distribution across PCs; 2D/3D projections. Track probe-drift $\delta_l = \mathbb E_t |\mathbb E_j[\phi^{(l)}(\tilde h_{t,l}^{(j)})] - \phi^{(l)}(h_{t,l}^{(j)})|$ after early-layer (layer 6) steering to measure how the network corrects the perturbation through depth.

Results & Highlights

  • RQ1 — probing (Fig. 2, LLaDA, R² per layer): ALL-token probe R² = 0.74 / 0.80 / 0.87 / 0.87 / 0.87 / 0.87 / 0.91 / 0.92 / 0.93 / 0.94 / 0.93 / 0.94 / 0.94 / 0.93 / 0.94 / 0.94 / 0.94 / 0.93 / 0.92 / 0.92 / 0.92 / 0.92 / 0.92 / 0.92 / 0.92 / 0.90 / 0.89 / 0.87 / 0.85 / 0.83 / 0.81 / 0.75 across layers 1–32 — R² > 0.5 at every layer, peaking ~0.94 at mid layers (12–16), degrading at the boundaries. [MASK] tokens give marginally higher R² than non-[MASK] (e.g. layer 12: 0.95 vs 0.89), but both carry the signal. Dream shows similar trends (Appendix C).
  • Probe ↔ mean-vector correlation: LLaDA 0.976 Pearson / 0.980 Spearman; Dream 0.962 / 0.974 — confirming the probe-recovered signal and the mean-vector direction are the same latent τ.
  • RQ2 — steering causality (Fig. 4, layer 29): steering toward target $\tau'$ produces systematic, monotonic changes in entropy, confidence, and KL divergence vs clean; the effect is absent for norm-matched random perturbations (the control), establishing the direction is causal, not just correlated.
  • RQ2 — early-layer correction (Fig. 5, perturbation at layer 6): the network progressively corrects the perturbation through depth (probe drift $\delta_l$ decays toward 0 at later layers); steering toward extreme $\tau'=100$ resists correction and leaves a persistent discrepancy.
  • RQ3 — low-dimensional geometry (Fig. 6): PCA of the τ-mean vectors shows PC1 explains >90% of intra-layer variance; 2D projection of Dream layer 25 mean vectors shows a parabola-like ordered curve; 3D projection (Fig. 1) shows a continuous manifold-like curve from all-[MASK] (red) to no-[MASK] (purple) — structurally similar to "clock"/counting representations in autoregressive models.

Critical Assessment

  • The "DLMs are not timestep-conditioned" framing is the key novelty claim, but it is somewhat softened by the authors' own observation that $\tau_t$ (fraction of unmasked tokens) is in expectation the complement of the training noise level $s$ — and the input to the DLM does contain the [MASK] tokens whose count trivially determines $\tau$. So a probe recovering $\tau$ from the residual stream is, in part, recovering an input-derived statistic; the paper would be stronger if it cleanly separated "τ is recoverable because the model sees its own mask pattern" from "the model actively computes and routes a τ signal downstream." The distributed-across-non-[MASK]-tokens result gestures at the latter but does not fully isolate it.
  • The steering-vs-norm-matched-control is the right causal test and is done correctly (Fig. 4), but the magnitude of the downstream entropy/confidence/KL shifts is reported visually rather than with per-target-τ numerical deltas, making it hard to gauge effect size or compare across layers quantitatively.
  • The geometry finding (1D PC1 >90%, parabola) is intriguing but under-validated: the parabola is shown for one Dream layer (25) and the 3D curve is a "projection" (Fig. 1) whose faithfulness depends on PC choice; no statistical test of the ordered-curve hypothesis (e.g., persistent-homology or k-nearest-neighbor ordering) is provided.
  • Early-layer correction (Fig. 5) is a genuinely interesting result — the network restores the clean τ representation after a layer-6 perturbation — but it is reported only as probe-drift curves without tying it to a specific mechanism (do later layers re-derive τ from the mask pattern, or do they read a redundant distributed copy?).
  • Scale: LLaDA-1.5 and Dream are mid-scale masked DLMs; whether the same τ-clock geometry persists at frontier scale, or generalizes to non-absorbing-state / continuous-time diffusion LMs, is not addressed.

Strengths

  • Clean three-stage interpretability pipeline (probe → steer → geometry) applied to a genuinely under-explored model family (masked diffusion LMs).
  • The R² > 0.5 across all 32 layers, with peak 0.94, is a strong positive result for "τ is internally represented."
  • Norm-matched random perturbation as a steering control is the methodologically correct causal test.
  • The low-dimensional ordered geometry connects DLM internal representations to the "clock" manifold literature in autoregressive models, opening a cross-paradigm comparison.

Limitations

  • The τ signal is partly input-derived (the model sees the [MASK] pattern), so the "internally modeled" claim is not fully isolated from "input leakage."
  • Steering effect sizes reported visually, not as numerical deltas per target τ.
  • Ordered-curve geometry validated on one layer/model visually; no statistical curve test.
  • Early-layer correction mechanism not pinned down (re-derivation vs redundant copy).
  • Only mid-scale absorbing-state DLMs; frontier-scale and continuous-time diffusion LMs untested.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. τ 信号在多大程度上是"模型主动计算并下游路由"的，而非单纯从输入 [MASK] 模式被动恢复？能否通过打乱 [MASK] 位置但保持总数的对照实验分离二者？
  2. 激活转向对下游生成质量（如 BLEU/困惑度/人工评估）的数值影响是多少？是否存在使生成质量不退化但改变 τ 感知的"安全转向区间"？
  3. 低维有序流形（抛物线/时钟曲线）能否用持续同调或 k-近邻排序误差给出统计显著性，而非仅靠 PCA 投影可视化？
  4. 早期层（layer 6）扰动被逐层纠正——这是后层重新从 [MASK] 模式推导 τ，还是读取冗余的分布式副本？能否用逐层探针扰动消融区分？
  5. 该 τ-时钟几何在连续时间（非吸收态）扩散语言模型、以及前沿尺度掩码 DLM 上是否仍然成立？
  6. 把"τ 可被转向且影响熵/置信度"作为可控生成杠杆，能否用于调节 DLM 的多样性-忠实度权衡（类似 autoregressive 模型的 temperature）？
  7. 探针在 [MASK] 与 non-[MASK] token 上的微小 R² 差异（如 layer 12: 0.95 vs 0.89）是否暗示 τ 信息在残差流中有路由偏置？SAE 分解能否定位到具体特征？
