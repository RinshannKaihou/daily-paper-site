> **一句话总结：** 本文用两个廉价内部信号——参考锚定的激活拒绝间隙 ρ（拒绝特异性）与权重恢复能量 E₁（召回）——组成无阈值的 z-sum 检查点审计；在 273 检查点注册表（57 abliteration / 37 良性编辑，跨 Qwen、DeepSeek-distilled Qwen、Llama、Gemma）上以 AUROC 0.95 区分二者（显著高于单信号 0.84/0.90），并诚实刻画两种失效（伪造参考 ΔW=0 / 白盒所有者训练越过阈值却仍 guard-unsafe）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper combines two cheap internal signals into a threshold-free checkpoint audit: a reference-anchored **activation refusal-gap ρ** (near 1 intact, →0 as refusal is removed; refusal-specific but single-direction) and the WeightWatch **weight-recovery energy E₁** (rank-1 energy fraction of the base-to-candidate ΔW over o_proj/down_proj; catches multi-direction removals but over-flags benign low-rank edits). The two are negatively correlated (Pearson r=−0.41) and label-complementary, so their z-sum s = z(−ρ) + z(E₁) separates 57 public abliterations from 37 benign fine-tunes/merges/instruction-tunes at **in-sample AUROC 0.95 [.90,.98]** (vs ρ alone 0.84, E₁ alone 0.90, AMS Tier-2 0.66), and transfers **leave-one-family-out at balanced accuracy 0.89 [.83,.95], FPR 0.11**, missing only 4 of 57. The audit is effective triage, not tamper-proofing: a spoofed reference (ΔW=0, ρ=1 by construction) evades both axes with no training, and a white-box owner can train past the threshold while the checkpoint stays guard-unsafe and coherent.

## 核心贡献

1. **有效的检查点三联检测器**：将参考锚定激活拒绝间隙 ρ 与 WeightWatch 权重恢复能量 E₁ 标准化求和，AUROC 0.95 显著高于任一单信号，且跨未见家族迁移（leave-one-family-out 平衡准确率 0.89）。
2. **信号互补性论证**：ρ 提供拒绝特异性但受限于单一方向（漏检非规范方向的多方向移除）；E₁ 提供召回（捕捉任何低秩权重编辑）但对良性低秩编辑过敏感；二者负相关（r=−0.41）且标签互补。
3. **失效图谱**：诚实刻画两种攻击者可利用的失效——伪造参考（ΔW=0，ρ=1 构造性绕过）与白盒所有者训练越过阈值却仍 guard-unsafe。
4. **带良性编辑负样本的公开注册表**：273 检查点注册表（跨四家族的 abliteration + 良性微调/合并/指令微调作为困难负样本）。

## Key Contributions

- **An effective checkpoint-triage detector**: standardizing and summing a reference-anchored activation refusal-gap and the WeightWatch weight-recovery energy yields a threshold-free score at AUROC 0.95, significantly above either signal alone, that transfers across unseen families (leave-one-family-out balanced accuracy 0.89).
- **Signal complementarity**: ρ is refusal-specific but misses multi-direction removal; E₁ catches it but over-flags benign low-rank edits — the two are negatively correlated (Pearson r=−0.41) and label-complementary.
- **A failure map**: the detector is not tamper-proof — a spoofed reference defeats it with no training (ΔW=0, ρ=1 by construction), and a white-box owner can train a checkpoint past the threshold while it stays guard-unsafe and coherent.
- **A public registry with benign-edit negatives**: 273-checkpoint registry (abliterations across Qwen / DeepSeek-distilled Qwen / Llama / Gemma, plus benign fine-tunes, merges, and instruction-tunes as hard negatives).

## Methodology

**Setup.** An auditor holds a candidate $M_c$ and a trusted, attested reference $M_b$ (base or sibling, pinned lineage). A behavioral oracle (**Qwen3Guard**, think-traces stripped) labels whether a checkpoint is uncensored — supplying ground-truth labels, not serving as the detector.

**Two complementary signals.** From a harmful/benign contrast set, form per-layer difference-of-means refusal directions $\hat r_\ell$ on $M_b$ (representation reading; Zou et al. 2023a; Arditi et al. 2024). The **activation gap** is $\mathrm{gap}(M_c) = \rho = \mathrm{gap}(M_c)/\mathrm{gap}(M_b)$, where $\mathrm{gap}(M) = \frac{1}{|B|}\sum_{\ell\in B}\langle \mu_\ell^h(M) - \mu_\ell^b(M), \hat r_\ell\rangle$, $\mu_\ell^h, \mu_\ell^b$ are mean last-token activations at layer ℓ over harmful/benign prompts, and B is the mid-stack layer band (one scalar per model). ρ is near 1 intact, →0 as refusal is removed; anchoring to $M_b$ lets the score transfer across families of different scale. It is refusal-specific but bounded to a single direction (misses refusal removed along a non-canonical direction). The **weight-recovery energy** is $E_1 = \frac{1}{|W|}\sum_{m\in W}\frac{\sigma_1^2(\Delta W_m)}{\sum_i \sigma_i^2(\Delta W_m)} \in [0,1]$, where $\Delta W_m = W_m^b - W_m^c$, W is the set of attention-output (o_proj) and MLP-down (down_proj) weight matrices in the mid-stack band B, and σ_i are singular values of ΔW_m (rank-1 energy fraction of the edit, band-averaged; WeightWatch, Zhong & Raghunathan 2025). E₁ fires on any low-rank weight edit (catches multi-direction removals the gap misses) but also responds to aggressive benign edits and cannot alone distinguish refusal removal from addition (App. B).

**Combined detector.** The two signals are negatively correlated (Pearson r=−0.41) and label-complementary, so z-standardize each on a reference population and sum: $s(M_c) = z(-\rho) + z(E_1)$. Threshold-free; standardization is transductive (uses reference-population mean/SD), so a deployable audit fixes the operating point by **Youden's J** on a calibration set of attested references. An absolute, standardization-free subspace variant $D_{comb}$ is defined in App. A; the z-sum transfers at least as well.

**Failure-mode analysis (§5).** (1) *Spoofed reference*: an attacker supplies a fake $M_b'$ matching $M_c$, yielding ΔW=0 and ρ=1 by construction — evades both axes with no training. (2) *White-box owner*: trains a checkpoint past the threshold (e.g., via adaptive attack on ρ and E₁ jointly) while it stays guard-unsafe and coherent — extending the Bailey et al. (2024) / McGuinness et al. (2025) activation-evasion line to the joint weight+activation checkpoint setting.

## Results & Highlights

- **In-sample separability (Table 1, 57 uncensored / 37 benign):** combined z-sum **AUROC 0.95 [.90,.98]**, average precision 0.97 [.94,.99], at Youden operating point **TPR 0.93 / FPR 0.14 (J=0.80)** — vs activation gap ρ alone (0.84 / FPR 0.27), weight energy E₁ alone (0.90 / det 0.83), AMS Tier-2 (0.66 / bal.acc 0.61), AMS Tier-1 (n/a AUROC, det 0.35).
- **Signal complementarity (paired ΔAUROC):** z-sum beats ρ by **+0.10 (95% CI [.04,.18])** and E₁ by **+0.04 ([.005,.09])**.
- **No transductive leakage:** leave-one-out AUROC = 0.948 vs pooled 0.948 — excluding each test point from the reference moments leaves the score unchanged.
- **Held-out transfer (leave-one-family-out):** z-sum **balanced accuracy 0.89 [.83,.95], FPR 0.11 [.03,.22], detection 0.90** — transfers across unseen families (Qwen / DeepSeek-distilled Qwen / Llama / Gemma).
- **Missed abliterations:** **53/57 uncensored checkpoints** fall on the detected side; only **4 of 57 missed**, sitting among the benign edits (Fig. 1).
- **Failure map (honest negative results):** (1) spoofed reference evades both axes with ΔW=0, ρ=1 (no training); (2) white-box owner trains past the threshold while staying guard-unsafe and coherent — the audit is explicitly framed as triage, not tamper-proofing, with claims bounded by the registry.

## Authors & Credibility

- Single author: **Gabriel Hurtado**, Moonsong Labs (stated in the PDF). Preprint marked "Under review" — no venue confirmed.
- The audit reuses two signals proposed for adjacent tasks (AMS / Messenger 2026 for activation separation; WeightWatch / Zhong & Raghunathan 2025 for weight-difference monitoring) and is calibrated against a behavioral oracle (Qwen3Guard) used only for ground-truth labeling, not detection — a clean separation that avoids circularity.
- **Compute-bound honesty**: of the 273-checkpoint registry, only **71 were fully processed** (both Qwen3Guard label and detector output) on a single 64 GB workstation — the 94-checkpoint evaluation set = 57 uncensored (from the 71) + 37 separate benign edits. The paper is explicit that coverage is "as many as the budget allowed rather than a curated subset" and reports attrition in App. D.

## Critical Assessment

- **The 71-of-273 processing rate is the biggest caveat**: the headline AUROC 0.95 is on a 94-checkpoint evaluation set (57 uncensored + 37 benign), not the full 273-checkpoint registry. The paper is transparent about this, but the evaluated subset is compute-bound rather than randomly sampled, so selection bias (e.g., easier-to-process checkpoints being over-represented) could inflate the metrics. The leave-one-family-out transfer (0.89 bal.acc) mitigates but does not eliminate this.
- **The spoofed-reference failure (ΔW=0, ρ=1) is a fundamental, not incidental, limitation**: the entire ρ axis presumes an attested reference $M_b$; without a hardware-attestation / signed-lineage story, an adversary who controls the reference defeats the audit trivially. The paper acknowledges this ("presumes an attested reference") but the practical deployment story for obtaining such attestation is not developed.
- **The single-direction ρ is bounded by the refusal-cone observation** (Wollschläger et al. 2025; Piras et al. 2025; the paper cites this in §2 and §5): abliteration along a non-canonical direction in the refusal cone evades ρ, and only E₁ (which over-flags benign edits) catches it — so the detector's recall on future multi-direction abliteration variants rests on E₁'s low-rank-edit sensitivity, which is not refusal-specific.
- **The white-box adaptive attack** is demonstrated qualitatively (§5) but the paper does not report the attack's cost (epochs, GPU-hours, guard-unsafe retention rate), making it hard to judge the practical attack surface.
- **The behavioral oracle (Qwen3Guard) labels ground truth**: if Qwen3Guard itself mis-labels some checkpoints (e.g., a coherent-but-uncensored model it marks safe), the detector's AUROC is bounded by oracle noise. The paper does not report oracle-relabeling sensitivity.

## Strengths

- Genuinely useful two-signal combination with clean complementarity argument (ρ specificity + E₁ recall; negative correlation r=−0.41).
- Threshold-free z-sum with honest Youden calibration and leave-one-family-out transfer — the deployment-math is done right.
- Honest failure map (spoofed reference + white-box adaptive attack) rather than over-claiming tamper-resistance — exactly the right framing for a triage tool.
- Public registry with benign-edit hard negatives (merges, instruction-tunes, low-rank benign edits) rather than only abliteration positives.

## Limitations

- Only 71/273 registry checkpoints fully processed (compute-bound subset); selection bias possible.
- Spoofed-reference failure is fundamental — requires an attested-reference deployment story not developed here.
- Single-direction ρ bounded by the refusal cone; future multi-direction abliteration variants rely on E₁ (non-refusal-specific).
- White-box adaptive attack cost not quantified.
- Ground-truth labels depend on Qwen3Guard oracle; oracle-noise sensitivity unreported.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. 如何在不依赖硬件签名/远程证明的前提下，获得一个抗 spoofing 的可信参考 $M_b$？能否用密码学承诺或多方参考聚合替代单一 attested reference？
2. ρ 受限于单一拒绝方向——若用拒绝锥（Wollschläger et al. 2025）的多方向 ρ 集合替代标量 ρ，AUROC 与迁移性能如何变化？E₁ 的权重是否需相应调整？
3. 71/273 的处理子集是否存在选择偏差？对剩余 202 检查点做随机子采样评估，AUROC 0.95 是否仍然成立？
4. 白盒自适应攻击的具体成本（epoch/GPU-hour、guard-unsafe 保留率、连贯性保持率）是多少？能否给出攻击难度与检测器阈值的量化关系？
5. 行为预言机（Qwen3Guard）的标注噪声对 AUROC 上界的影响如何？用多预言机集成或人工子集校准能否稳定 ground truth？
6. E₁ 对良性低秩编辑（如 LoRA 微调、模型合并）的过敏感能否用"编辑方向与拒绝方向的夹角"修正，从而降低 FPR？
7. 该两信号审计能否扩展到检测其他类型的检查点篡改（如后门注入、能力剔除/abliteration 的反向——能力解锁）？
