EO-Agents: A Three-Agent LLM Pipeline for Earth Observation Hypothesis Generation

arXiv: 2607.01584

Date: 2026-07-03

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01584

一句话总结 (zh)

本文提出一个面向地球观测（Earth Observation）的三智能体 LLM 流水线，将科研假设生成锚定在 NASA 地球观测知识图谱之上，利用异构图神经网络排序候选数据集配对，并在 1,475 个 NASA 数据集上产出 160 条跨多学科的研究假设。

TL;DR (en)

EO-Agents grounds LLM-driven scientific hypothesis generation in the NASA Earth Observation Knowledge Graph: a heterogeneous GNN ranks candidate dataset pairings from historical co-usage, and a three-agent LLM pipeline filters, generates, and evaluates structured research hypotheses—yielding 160 hypotheses across 1,475 NASA datasets whose novel pairings are judged nearly as plausible as real published co-usages.

核心贡献 (zh)

  • 构建了一条三智能体（过滤 / 生成 / 评估）的 LLM 流水线，用于结构化的地球科学假设生成。
  • 将假设生成显式地"接地"到 NASA 地球观测知识图谱（EO KG），而非依赖非结构化文献与自由文本。
  • 训练一个异构图神经网络（GNN），基于历史"共使用"关系对候选数据集配对进行排序。
  • 应用到 1,475 个 NASA 数据集，产出 160 条覆盖多领域（生态水文、冰川学、气溶胶–云相互作用、植被物候、平流层化学）的假设。

Key Contributions (en)

  • A three-agent LLM pipeline (filter / generate / evaluate) for structured Earth-science hypothesis generation.
  • Grounding hypothesis generation in the NASA EO Knowledge Graph instead of unstructured literature and free-form text claims.
  • A heterogeneous GNN trained on historical dataset co-usage relations that ranks candidate dataset pairings.
  • Application to 1,475 NASA datasets, producing 160 hypotheses spanning ecohydrology, glaciology, aerosol–cloud interactions, vegetation phenology, and stratospheric chemistry.
  • A 2×2×2 factorial study across GPT-5.2 and Claude Sonnet 4.6 showing that rankings are stable while absolute scores depend strongly on the judge identity—exposing a key weakness of single-judge LLM evaluation.

Methodology

  1. NASA EO Knowledge Graph. A heterogeneous KG with 150,351 nodes and 436,203 edges across seven node types (Publication, Dataset, platform/instrument, project, etc.); dataset co-usage edges are derived from joint citation by a shared publication. Train/val/test splits are temporal: train ≤ 2022, val = 2023, test = 2024 (giving 13,529 / … / … co-usage pairs). The combinatorial space of unordered dataset pairings is ~10^6 even under conservative filtering.
  2. Heterogeneous GNN ranker. The GNN is trained by link prediction on past co-usages (publications dated ≤ 2022). For each candidate pair, a dense-hop neighborhood around the two datasets is extracted and passed through the heterogeneous GNN; node embeddings produce a co-usage likelihood. The GNN surfaces the top 200 pairs not observed in train/val/test co-usage sets — these are the "novel pairing" candidates.
  3. Three-agent LLM pipeline. Three specialized agents operate sequentially over the GNN-ranked candidates (each agent independently instantiated as GPT-5.2 or Claude Sonnet 4.6):
  • Filter agent — selects promising dataset pairings (rescores the 200 pairs).
  • Generator agent — articulates a structured research hypothesis grounded in the paired datasets and KG context (e.g., IceBridge airborne magnetometer + InSAR-derived Antarctic ice velocity → hypothesis that subglacial magnetic anomalies predict ice-flow patterns).
  • Judge agent — rates each hypothesis for scientific coherence/plausibility, under either a blind or contextual (datasets visible) condition.
  1. Evaluation protocol (2×2×2 factorial). A 2×2×2 factorial experiment crosses (filter backbone) × (generator backbone) × (judge backbone: GPT-5.2 vs Claude Sonnet 4.6), plus the blind-vs-contextual judge contrast, across all 160 hypotheses; 800 inputs × 2 judges = 1,600 judge calls per stratum. The plausibility of model-predicted novel pairings is compared against held-out real 2024 co-usages.

Results & Highlights

  • Scale: 160 hypotheses produced over 1,475 NASA datasets, spanning ecohydrology, glaciology, aerosol–cloud interactions, vegetation phenology, and stratospheric chemistry.
  • Novel ≈ real plausibility: held-out real co-usages and model-predicted novel pairings receive comparable plausibility scores from LLM judges — suggesting the system surfaces scientifically coherent yet under-explored combinations.
  • Rankings stable, absolute scores judge-dependent (key finding):
  • 159 of 160 hypotheses change by at most 2 points across the four judge conditions — i.e. the ranking is highly consistent.
  • But ~25% of the variance in importance scores comes from the judge identity alone, vs <2% from filter or generator identity.
  • The blind-vs-contextual (does the judge see the underlying datasets?) contrast dominates tractability variance at ~16%.
  • Practical implication: a researcher picking the top-25% by score gets nearly the same set regardless of configuration, but single-LLM-judge absolute scoring is risky — motivating multi-judge protocols for absolute plausibility claims.

Critical Assessment

  • Strength of claim: The central empirical claim—"model-predicted novel dataset pairings are rated nearly as plausible as held-out real co-usages"—is meaningful but is itself an LLM-judged metric, which the authors' own factorial experiment flags as judge-dependent. The headline result and its evaluation tool share the same epistemic weakness.
  • Scientific validation unclear from abstract: It is not stated whether any human expert validation (e.g., domain scientist review of the 160 hypotheses) was conducted; this would substantially strengthen (or challenge) the plausibility claims.
  • Generalizability: Restricted to NASA EO datasets and a particular KG; transfer to other scientific KGs is unaddressed in the abstract.

Strengths

  • Strong problem framing: moving LLM hypothesis generation from free-form text to structured, KG-grounded claims.
  • Clean decomposition into filter → generate → evaluate agents.
  • Honest, self-critical factorial analysis of LLM-as-judge reliability.
  • Practical scale (1,475 datasets, 160 hypotheses) and cross-domain coverage.

Limitations

  • Evaluation relies heavily on LLM judges; absolute scores are judge-dependent by the authors' own finding.
  • Abstract reports no human/domain-expert validation of generated hypotheses.
  • "Nearly as plausible as real co-usages" is a relative, judge-based bar—not a demonstration that hypotheses are true or novel-useful.
  • GNN and agent prompting details, failure modes, and cost/latency are not visible from the abstract.
