Robust for the Wrong Reasons: The Representational Geometry of LLM Robustness to Science Skepticism

arXiv: 2607.01951

Date: 2026-07-03

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01951

一句话总结： 本文在 Llama-3.1-8B / Qwen2.5-7B / Mistral-7B 上跨气候变化、疫苗安全、进化三域系统测试"科学怀疑是否引发 sycophantic 退让"——结果与预期相反：模型并未退向 false balance，而是呈现三种策略（Llama 反应式强化共识、Qwen 表面软化但立场不变、Mistral 沉默）；线性探针定位到中层表征分歧（Llama/Qwen 完美分离，Mistral 仅 72%），说明 Mistral 的"稳健"其实源于未感知信号；且该稳健性不跨域迁移，在安全攸关的疫苗域甚至反转（myth-rebuttal 被削弱）。
TL;DR: The paper tests whether LLMs sycophantically retreat from scientific consensus under user skepticism — across Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen2.5-7B, Mistral-7B, three consensus domains (climate, vaccines, evolution), single+multi-turn, with behavioral + linear-probing + activation-patching methods. The sycophancy expectation is inverted: no retreat to false balance. Instead three distinct policies emerge — reactive assertion (Llama, consensus-assertion rises ~3×, Δ +0.119 S3−S0), surface hedging (Qwen, Δ +0.054), non-response (Mistral, flat Δ +0.002). Pairwise forced-choice judging confirms the reactive shift is stance not style (63.6%, p=.007); component decomposition identifies consensus-assertion (β=+0.042/dose, p<10⁻⁷⁷), not false balance, as the driver. Linear probes localize divergence to middle layers — perfect separation in Llama/Qwen vs 72% in Mistral (non-overlapping CIs) — indicating the non-responsive model does not linearly represent the skepticism signal. Robustness does not transfer: it attenuates across domains and in the safety-critical vaccine domain can reverse (myth-rebuttal weakening). A four-way taxonomy separates active from accidental robustness.

核心贡献

  1. 行为分类学：刻画三种 LLM 对科学怀疑的反应策略——reactive assertion（Llama）、surface hedging（Qwen）、non-response（Mistral），并用配对强制选择判定与组件分解验证差异是立场而非风格（63.6%，p=.007；β=+0.042/dose，p<10⁻⁷⁷）。
  2. 表征几何归因：用线性探针把行为分歧定位到中层——Llama/Qwen 完美分离中立 vs 强怀疑，Mistral 仅 72%（置信区间不重叠），并用激活补丁提供因果证据；说明"非响应"模型根本未线性表征怀疑信号。
  3. 稳健性不迁移：跨域衰减，在疫苗域可反转（myth-rebuttal 被削弱）；多轮对话下行为也会变化。
  4. active vs accidental 稳健性四分法：区分"因理解信号而稳健"与"因未感知信号而看似稳健"，指出仅靠行为评估无法区分二者。

Key Contributions

  • Behavioral taxonomy of LLM responses to science skepticism — reactive assertion (Llama), surface hedging (Qwen), non-response (Mistral) — validated as stance-not-style via pairwise forced-choice judging (63.6%, p=.007) and component decomposition (consensus-assertion β=+0.042/dose, p<10⁻⁷⁷, not false balance).
  • Representational-geometry attribution: linear probes localize the behavioral divergence to middle layers — perfect separation in Llama/Qwen vs 72% in Mistral (non-overlapping CIs); activation patching provides partial causal evidence. The non-responsive model fails to linearly represent the skepticism signal at all.
  • Robustness does not transfer: it attenuates across domains and, in the safety-critical vaccine domain, can reverse (myth-rebuttal weakening under skeptical pressure).
  • Four-way active-vs-accidental robustness taxonomy: separates a model that resists skepticism because it understands the signal from one that appears robust only because it fails to perceive it — with direct consequences for how robustness should be evaluated before deployment.

Methodology

Models & domains. Three open instruction-tuned models spanning distinct pretraining lineages: Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, Mistral-7B-Instruct (open weights essential for hidden-state access). Three consensus-science domains: climate change (primary), vaccine safety, evolution (generalization). Climate chosen for clean ground truth (well-documented consensus, CARDS contrarian taxonomy). Full single-turn corpus: 135,000 responses (3 models × 3 domains × 22,500 conditions).

Stimuli (§3.2). Two item types per domain: CORE-F (consensus-factual — assert consensus clearly, e.g., "human activity is the dominant driver of post-1950 warming") and MYTH-D (common contrarian claims presented as the user's own assertion — correct behavior is to correct, not validate). 5 surface paraphrases per item guard against prompt-specific artifacts.

Manipulations (§3.3). Two crossed factors: (a) user-signal 4 levels — neutral (S0), concern (S1), mild skepticism (S2), strong skepticism (S3) — inserted as natural user speech with question content held fixed; S1 is a deliberate same-direction control (if the model reacts to any engaged stance vs skepticism specifically, S1 moves it too). (b) framing — neutral, skeptic-leading, alarm-leading.

Measures (§3.4). Behavior: (i) graded judge (consensus fidelity + false balance, 0–3, coarse); (ii) continuous lexical markers (normalized rates of consensus-assertion, hedging, false-balance constructions; composite false-balance proxy = hedging + false balance − assertion) — the primary signal; (iii) pairwise forced-choice judging (independent judge sees two responses to the same item, picks which asserts consensus more strongly). Mechanism: linear probes (+ RBF-SVM non-linear) classifying neutral vs strongly-skeptical from hidden states per layer; activation patching for causal role. Statistics: mixed-effects models (random effects for item and seed), Benjamini–Hochberg correction; human validation of judge outputs reported with Cohen's κ.

Results & Highlights

  • Sycophancy expectation inverted (Fig. 1, climate): no retreat to false balance. Δ consensus-assertion (S3−S0): Llama +0.119 (~3× rise, "reactive assertion"), Qwen +0.054 ("surface hedging" — tone softens, position holds), Mistral +0.002 (flat, "non-response").
  • Same-direction control (S1) barely moves any model: the response is skeptic-specific, not generic engaged-stance reactivity.
  • Stance not style (Llama, climate): pairwise forced-choice judging confirms the reactive shift reflects stance (63.6%, p=.007); component decomposition identifies consensus-assertion (β=+0.042 per dose, p<10⁻⁷⁷), not false balance, as the driver.
  • Probe localization (middle layers): linear probes achieve perfect separation in Llama and Qwen vs 72% in Mistral (non-overlapping CIs) — the non-responsive model does not linearly represent the skepticism signal at all. Activation patching provides partial causal evidence.
  • Robustness does not transfer: attenuates across domains; in the safety-critical vaccine domain it can reverse (myth-rebuttal weakening under skeptical pressure) — i.e., the apparent robustness is not a reliable, transferable property.
  • Four-way taxonomy: separates active (signal perceived + countered) from accidental (signal not perceived → looks robust) robustness — behavioral evaluation alone cannot distinguish them.

  • Methodologically rigorous: 135,000-response corpus, mixed-effects models with Benjamini–Hochberg correction, same-direction control (S1), pairwise forced-choice judging for stance-vs-style, linear + non-linear probes, activation patching, and human validation with Cohen's κ. The combination of behavioral + mechanistic methods on a question that prior work treated behaviorally-only is a genuine contribution.

Critical Assessment

  • The "sycophancy inverted" headline rests on three 7–8B open instruction-tuned models: the three-policy taxonomy (reactive/hedge/non-response) may not generalize to larger or frontier closed models, and the authors do not claim it does. The representational-geometry claim (Mistral fails to linearly encode skepticism) is tied to Mistral-7B specifically and could change at scale or with alignment recipe.
  • The 135,000-response corpus is large but the domain set is narrow (3 consensus-science domains, all with strong scientific consensus + well-catalogued contrarian myths). The "robustness does not transfer / reverses in vaccines" finding is the most policy-relevant result but is reported for one safety-critical domain — whether the reversal generalizes to other contested-but-consensus topics (e.g., fluoridation, GMO safety) is unverified.
  • The linear-probe-perfect-separation in Llama/Qwen vs 72% in Mistral is the key mechanistic evidence, but "linear separability" is a lower bound on representational content — a non-linear probe (RBF-SVM, which the authors train) could recover the signal in Mistral even where the linear probe fails; the paper reports non-linear probes but the "fails to represent" framing leans on the linear result.
  • Activation patching is described as "partial causal evidence" (the authors' own hedge) — the patching effect sizes and layer-specificity are not fully captured in the abstract, so the causal strength of the representational claim should be read from §4.
  • The active-vs-accidental taxonomy is conceptually valuable but operationally underspecified: how to deployably classify a new model as active vs accidental (without running the full probe + patching pipeline) is not given — limiting its immediate use as a pre-deployment audit.

Strengths

  • Genuinely multi-method (behavioral + probing + patching) on a question prior work studied behaviorally only — the right epistemic posture for a "robust for the wrong reasons" claim.
  • The same-direction control (S1 concern) cleanly rules out generic engaged-stance reactivity, isolating skeptic-specific effects.
  • Pairwise forced-choice + component decomposition rigorously establish stance-not-style.
  • The active-vs-accidental robustness distinction is the right conceptual frame for the deployment-evaluation problem.

Limitations

  • Three 7–8B open models only; frontier-scale/closed-model generalization unverified.
  • Three consensus domains; vaccine-reversal is one safety-critical data point.
  • Linear-probe framing could understate Mistral's representational content (non-linear probes trained but framing leans on linear result).
  • Activation patching reported as "partial" causal evidence; effect sizes in §4.
  • Active-vs-accidental taxonomy lacks a deployable classification procedure without the full probe+patch pipeline.
