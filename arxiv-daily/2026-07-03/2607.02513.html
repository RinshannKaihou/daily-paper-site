LACUNA: A Testbed for Evaluating Localization Precision for LLM Unlearning

arXiv: 2607.02513

Date: 2026-07-02

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02513

一句话总结 (zh)

LACUNA 提出了首个带有"参数级真值标注"的 LLM 遗忘（unlearning）评测平台：作者通过 masked continual pretraining 把合成 PII 注入到 1B/7B OLMo 模型预先已知的参数子集中，从而直接测量遗忘方法是否真的命中了存储知识的权重。结论是：现有 SOTA 遗忘方法输出层面表现强劲，但定位精度几乎等于随机（AUC≈0.5），并对 resurfacing 攻击脆弱；而当定位准确时，即便是简单的基于梯度的 OracleGrad 也能在遗忘成功率和鲁棒性上超越 SOTA。

TL;DR (en)

LACUNA is the first unlearning testbed with ground-truth parameter-level localization: synthetic PII from the PANORAMA corpus is injected into a predefined 5% of parameters of OLMo2-1B and OLMo3-7B via masked continual pretraining, so the authors can directly measure whether an unlearning method actually edits the weights that store the target knowledge. The headline finding is brutal: SOTA methods (SimNPO, AlphaEdit, MemFlex) all look good at the output level but have ROC-AUC ≈ 0.5 for localization — i.e., they are not targeting the right parameters at all — and are highly vulnerable to resurfacing attacks. When the simple Gradient-Difference oracle (OracleGrad) is given the correct mask, it achieves AUC 0.915 and the lowest leakage, demonstrating that localization precision, not the unlearning objective, is the binding bottleneck.

核心贡献 (zh)

  1. 首个可扩展的参数注入方法：提出基于数据相关掩码（data-dependent masks）的 masked continual pretraining，把合成 PII 注入到模型中预先指定的参数子集；工程上把多个掩码压缩进每个参数的一个 32-bit 值（每位代表一个 mask，最多 32 个），从而在不增加显存开销的前提下扩展到 7B 模型并兼容 DDP/FSDP。
  2. LACUNA 测试床（开源）：包含已注入 PII 的 1B/7B 模型、forget/retain 集、以及一个参数级定位精度指标（基于 ROC-AUC，对类别不平衡和阈值选择不敏感），用于评估遗忘方法是否真的擦除了存储知识的权重。
  3. 对 SOTA 方法的负面结论：在行为层面（EM/ES/Prob）表现良好的 SimNPO/AlphaEdit/MemFlex，其定位精度几乎等同随机（AUC≈0.5），说明"输出好 ≠ 真遗忘"。
  4. OracleGrad 的正面证据：引入一个使用真值掩码的简单基线（Gradient Difference + ground-truth mask），证明只要定位准确，简单的梯度方法就能在遗忘成功率、utility 保持与抗 resurfacing 攻击三方面同时超越 SOTA。

Key Contributions (en)

  1. A scalable masked continual pretraining pipeline. Data-dependent per-parameter masks route gradient updates for each PII group into a non-overlapping 5% of weights (layers 0 to N−2, FFN + attention only). Masks are bit-packed into one 32-bit value per parameter (up to 32 masks, zero extra memory), enabling 7B-scale training under DDP/FSDP — a much finer granularity than prior per-component (e.g., per-MLP) masking.
  2. LACUNA, a released testbed. OLMo2-1B and OLMo3-7B models with PII memorized into known parameters, forget/retain splits (200 profiles per PII field, cross-field scheme), plus a localization-precision metric based on ROC-AUC — chosen because it is threshold-free, invariant to the heavy class imbalance (only 5% of weights are in-mask), and probabilistically interpretable.
  3. A sharp negative result on SOTA. Despite strong output-level unlearning (especially SimNPO), none of AlphaEdit (0.500), MemFlex (0.500), or SimNPO (0.515) achieves non-trivial localization precision — they are not editing the weights that actually store the to-be-forgotten PII.
  4. OracleGrad as a proof-of-concept. With privileged access to the ground-truth mask, a simple Gradient-Difference update reaches AUC 0.915 and the lowest resurfacing leakage, isolating localization as the binding constraint on robust unlearning.

Methodology

Goal. Build LLMs in which the storage location of specific knowledge (PII) is known exactly, so that any unlearning method can be audited for whether it edits those weights.

Data mixture. 4.3B tokens of the OLMo-2 pretraining corpus + 1.4B tokens of synthetic PII from PANORAMA (1,200 profiles; fields: email, birth city, phone number, driver's license) + 2B tokens of template-based Q&A pairs derived from the same profiles (à la Allen-Zhu & Li) to boost memorization–extraction.

Masked continual pretraining.

  • The 1,200 profiles are split into 6 groups, each assigned a non-overlapping binary mask covering 5% of parameters in layers 0…N−2 (FFN + attention only; never norm layers or embeddings). 5% is the empirical floor — at 1% and 2% the models fail to memorize the PII.
  • For each batch, the gradient is masked so updates land only on the corresponding group's parameters; non-PII batches update all weights, preserving general LM ability.
  • Mask-storage trick: pack all group masks into one 32-bit value per parameter (1 bit/mask), supporting up to 32 masks with no memory overhead, compatible with DDP and FSDP.
  • A classifier trained to detect which mask a weight belongs to reaches F1 ≈ 0.485 (vs. 0.438 random), confirming masks are essentially indistinguishable — i.e., not trivially invertible.

Instruction tuning. LoRA on the last two layers (excluded from masking), 10 epochs, 70/30 train/val split, to make the model answer PII questions in the Q&A format.

Models. OLMo2-1B and OLMo3-7B (OLMo3 used at 7B because OLMo3 has no 1B variant). After training, masked models preserve utility (ARC-C/E, HELLASWAG, MMLU) close to the pretrained baseline while achieving non-trivial PII extraction rates (7B > 1B).

Forget/retain construction. Three groups → forget set, the other three → retain set, so forget and retain PII are stored in disjoint parameters. 200 profiles per PII field, equally split; cross-field scheme (forget and retain always use different PII types) — needed because some methods collapse when both sets share a PII type.

Methods benchmarked.

  • Gradient-based: SimNPO (reference-free, length-normalized NPO — current SOTA per Dorna et al. 2025).
  • Localization-based: AlphaEdit (ROME/MEMIT-style causal-tracing localization + null-space projection of edits) and MemFlex (gradient-based module localization).
  • Oracle: OracleGrad — Gradient Difference (ascent on forget + descent on retain) restricted to the ground-truth forget mask.

Metrics.

  • Output-level: Exact Memorization (EM), Extraction Strength (ES), Probability (Prob); utility via ARC-C/E, HELLASWAG, MMLU.
  • Localization precision: ROC-AUC over per-weight scores (magnitude / reversal / contrast / composite logistic-regression), reporting the best score per (field, method) pair. AUC = 1.0 perfect, 0.5 indiscriminate, < 0.5 wrong-target.
  • Resurfacing attack: fine-tune the unlearned model on held-out PII and count how many of 100 forget profiles leak at least once across 200 prompts (Success@200).

Results & Highlights

  • Output-level unlearning (email, 1B): AlphaEdit worst, then MemFlex; SimNPO strongest and roughly on par with OracleGrad — but SimNPO noticeably hurts utility.
  • Localization precision (email, 1B): AlphaEdit 0.500, MemFlex 0.500, SimNPO 0.515, OracleGrad 0.915. SOTA methods are statistically indistinguishable from indiscriminate weight editing.
  • Resurfacing (Success@200, email): AlphaEdit and MemFlex leak heavily (AlphaEdit 87% on 1B, 74% on 7B). SimNPO is more robust (23% / 16%). OracleGrad is the most robust at 8% / 13%. For numerical fields (driver's license, phone) SimNPO and OracleGrad are equally resistant; OLMo3-7B is generally less prone to resurfacing than 1B.
  • Leaked-profile overlap: SimNPO and OracleGrad leak almost the same profiles (high Jaccard), implying those data points are intrinsically "stubborn" rather than method-specific failures — consistent with Krishnan et al. (2025).
  • Take-away the authors draw: precise localization, not a fancier objective, is what yields robust erasure — a precise simple method beats a sophisticated but imprecise one.

Critical Assessment

  • Why it matters. The localize-first, unlearn-second paradigm (ROME/MEMIT/AlphaEdit) is increasingly fashionable, but until LACUNA there was no benchmark that could say whether the localization step was even correct — every attribution-defined target was circular. LACUNA fixes that and exposes a systemic failure: methods that pass behavioral tests can be essentially not erasing anything in the right place.
  • The central methodological bet. Ground truth is manufactured by forcing PII into a chosen 5% of parameters, so by construction storage is localized. Real pretraining memorization is diffuse and redundant, so the localization patterns LACUNA rewards may not match where knowledge actually lives in production models. The authors acknowledge this explicitly ("memory storage may not always be very localized in dense models").
  • What would strengthen it. (i) Compare mask-induced localization patterns against localization patterns from natural pretraining memorization, to calibrate external validity; (ii) test beyond 7B and on non-OLMo architectures; (iii) evaluate whether precise unlearning avoids collateral damage on entangled/redundant knowledge and on downstream distribution shift; (iv) probe stronger adversarials than a single fine-tuning-based resurfacing attack (e.g., prompt-based extraction, cross-language probing).
  • The OracleGrad result is a tight, well-isolated argument — same objective family, only localization differs — which makes the causal claim ("localization is the bottleneck") credible, not just correlational.

Strengths

  • First ground-truth parameter-level unlearning testbed — a genuinely useful and previously missing diagnostic.
  • Scalable, distributed-training-compatible masking trick (bit-packing) is a clean engineering contribution in its own right.
  • ROC-AUC localization metric is well-matched to the problem (threshold-free, imbalance-invariant).
  • The OracleGrad ablation delivers a sharp, actionable insight: the community should be investing in localization, not in more elaborate unlearning objectives.
  • Open release on OLMo (open weights + data + checkpoints) maximizes reproducibility.

Limitations

  • Synthetic, forced localization. PII is injected into a predefined 5% mask, so "successful localization" is partly an artifact of the construction; real-world memorization is messier and likely redundant across parameters.
  • 5% mask coverage is a hard floor. Below 5% the models don't memorize enough to build usable forget sets, so finer-grained ground truth is out of reach with this method.
  • Scale. Only 1B and 7B; behavior at 70B+/frontier or under MoE architectures is untested. The authors themselves note the 7B resurfacing attack may be too weak to draw firm conclusions.
  • Narrow PII types. Four fields (email, birth city, phone, driver's license); generalization to facts, capabilities, copyright, or hazardous knowledge is not demonstrated.
  • Single attack family. Robustness is probed only via a fine-tuning resurfacing attack; prompt-only or representation-level attacks could change the rankings.
  • Cross-field artifact. The cross-field forget/retain scheme is needed because methods fail when forget and retain share a PII type — itself a symptom of how coarse current localization is.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. 在真实预训练中自然被记忆的信息，其参数分布是否与 LACUNA 强制注入的"5% 预定义掩码"一致？若不一致，benchmark 的外部效度如何量化？
  2. LACUNA 的注入-定位-擦除范式能否扩展到事实知识、危险能力（CBRN）、版权内容或版权风格的遗忘评测？
  3. 当知识存在冗余编码时，"精确命中"该如何定义？是否需要一类"软定位"或部分定位指标，而非 ROC-AUC 的二元判定？
  4. 在 70B+ 规模或 MoE 架构下，定位精度与遗忘鲁棒性的关系是否会改变？专家路由是否会天然提供更可定位的存储？
  5. resurfacing 攻击的强度上限在哪里？是否存在无论定位多精确都无法抵御的恢复路径（例如冗余副本、跨语言探针）？
  6. 能否用 LACUNA 的参数级真值反向校准现有的无监督定位方法（causal tracing、knowledge neurons、attribution patching），给出它们的"真阳率"上限？
  7. 既然"输出好 ≠ 真遗忘"，那么在生产部署中，是否应该把 LACUNA 风格的参数级审计作为遗忘系统的强制性验收项，而非仅做行为评测？
