## 一句话总结 (zh)

本文通过操控 IPIP-50 问卷题目顺序,发现 LLM 人格具有"双重性质":聚合的大五得分对框架错位稳健但被内容随机化削弱,而 SPD 流形上的题内相关几何则是依赖共享时间框架的协调模式——错位时崩塌(95.3%→52.9%)、共享时恢复至 84.5% 并超过聚合特征,证明 LLM"人格几何"不是内在特质而是测量伪影,要求框架感知评估。

## TL;DR (en)

Using GPT-4o simulating American vs. Chinese-American personas on the IPIP-50, the author shows LLM "persona" has two dissociable components: aggregated Big-Five scores are frame-robust but order-sensitive, while the within-instance correlation geometry on SPD manifolds is frame-dependent—it collapses 95.3%→52.9% under frame misalignment and recovers to 84.5% under shared frames (exceeding Big-Five's 75.9%). This challenges the notion that LLMs have stable, intrinsic personality traits.

## 核心贡献 (zh)

- **双重性质框架**:首次将 LLM 偏见/人格解耦为两个可分离部分——聚合倾向(大五均值)与几何结构(题内相关矩阵在 SPD 流形上的表示)。
- **可证伪的三假设检验**:用题目顺序操控作为判别探针,系统对比"内在结构(H1)"、"测量伪影(H2)"、"框架依赖协调(H3)"三种假设,实验支持 H3(预测崩塌-恢复的 V 型曲线)。
- **方法创新——Item-Dimension 矩阵**:把顺序化的 50 题响应重组为 10×5 矩阵(每列对应一个大五维度),从而可在单实例层面计算相关矩阵,类比神经科学的功能连接分析。
- **量化崩塌-恢复模式**:SPD 几何在错位时崩塌、共享框架下恢复,且恢复后超越聚合特征,证明几何编码了聚合看不到的判别信息。
- **评估方法论呼吁**:主张"框架感知"评估,即变换题目顺序、分解顺序效应(OE)与框架效应(FE)、报告对齐条件下的性能。

## Key Contributions (en)

- **Dual-nature framework**: decomposes LLM persona into aggregated tendencies (Big-Five means, frame-robust) and geometric structure (within-instance correlation matrices on the SPD manifold, frame-dependent).
- **Falsifiable hypothesis test**: order manipulation distinguishes intrinsic structure (H1), measurement artifact (H2), and frame-dependent coordination (H3); results support H3's predicted V-shaped collapse-recovery curve.
- **Item-Dimension Matrix method**: reorganizes the ordered 50-item response stream into a 10×5 matrix (one column per Big-Five dimension) so a within-instance correlation matrix can be computed per LLM call—analogous to functional-connectivity analysis in neuroscience.
- **Quantified collapse-and-recovery**: SPD features collapse under misalignment and recover under shared frames, *surpassing* Big-Five features and thereby encoding aggregation-invisible information.
- **Frame-aware evaluation prescription**: recommends varying temporal frames, decomposing order vs. frame effects, and reporting alignment-conditioned performance.

## Methodology

**Model & instrument.** OpenAI `gpt-4o-2024-05-13`, temperature 0.7. Each LLM call simulates an American or Chinese-American persona (cultural prompts) and completes the 50-item IPIP-50 (10 items × 5 Big-Five dimensions). Sample size ~100 per cell, filtered to complete well-formed responses: FO total N=193 (US=96, CA=97), RO total N=187 (US=92, CA=95); a large-sample replication at N=2000 appears in Appendix A.

**Three analytical conditions (the core manipulation).**
- **FO (Fixed Order):** all instances use the standard ordering π_std → aligned frames.
- **RO (Random Order, Native Frame):** each instance gets a unique random ordering π⁽ⁱ⁾ → frame misalignment.
- **RO-BTSP (Random Order Bootstrap Shared Frame):** for each of B=2000 bootstrap iterations, draw one shared random π_b and recompute C(π_b) for *all* RO instances → frame realignment while keeping content randomized. This isolates temporal coordination from response content.

**Item-Dimension Matrix.** For an instance with response vector r∈ℝ⁵⁰ and order π, build X(π) ∈ ℝ¹⁰ˣ⁵ by walking through π and appending each score r(π[t]) to the column of its mapped dimension. The within-instance Pearson correlation C(π)=corr(X(π)) is a 5×5 SPD matrix; different π produce different geometries from identical responses.

**SPD manifold features.** C(π) is mapped to the tangent space at the identity via log-Euclidean framework (Arsigny et al., 2007), giving log(C); the choice of identity I as reference is deliberate—it is frame-invariant, so it isolates the geometric impact of π. Vectorizing the symmetric log(C) yields 10-D features.

**Feature types.**
- Big Five scores (5-D, frame-independent by construction).
- SPD manifold features vec(log C) (10-D).
- Eigenvalues λ(C) (5-D).
- Top eigenvector v₁(C) (5-D).

**Evaluation.** UMAP (n_neighbors=15, min_dist=0.1) → spectral clustering with k=2; metrics are clustering accuracy, silhouette score, and AUC-ROC. Large-sample validation (Appendix A.2) uses k-means on raw features.

**Decomposition of degradation.** Total drop ∆Total = |Acc_RO − Acc_FO| is split into order effect ∆OE = |Acc_RO-BTSP − Acc_FO| and frame effect ∆FE = |Acc_RO − Acc_RO-BTSP|.

**Random-Matrix-Theory control.** Eigenvalue spacing is tested against the Wigner–Dyson (GOE) ensemble vs. Poisson to confirm RO preserves non-random correlational structure (i.e., collapse is frame misalignment, not structural destruction).

## Results & Highlights

**Clustering accuracy (Table 1, B=2000 bootstrap):**

| Feature | FO | RO | RO-BTSP |
|---|---|---|---|
| Big Five | 96.89 | 75.90 | 75.90* |
| SPD | 95.34 | 52.94 | **84.50** |
| Eigenvalues | 61.14 | 50.27 | 59.20 |
| Top Eigenvector | 50.78 | 50.27 | 63.10 |

\*Big Five is frame-invariant, so RO-BTSP = RO.

- **Collapse-and-recovery confirmed (H3):** SPD features 95.34% → 52.94% (RO) → 84.50% (RO-BTSP); recovery vs. RO is t(1999)=102.69, p<.001, d=2.30.
- **Geometry beats aggregation under shared frames:** SPD RO-BTSP 84.5% > Big Five 75.9%, t(1999)=27.94, p<.001, d=0.63; SPD wins in 86.8% of bootstrap iterations.
- **Order vs. frame decomposition (Table 2):** Big Five is 100% order-driven (0% frame); SPD is 74% frame-driven / 26% order; eigenvalues 81% frame / 19% order; top eigenvector balanced. Clean dissociation between aggregation (order-driven) and geometry (frame-driven).
- **UMAP visualizations:** SPD silhouette 0.69 / AUC 0.98 under FO (clear separation) collapses to 0.29 / 0.61 under RO (overlapping clusters).
- **RMT control:** eigenvalue spacing follows Wigner–Dyson in *both* FO and RO, while item-level entropy rises significantly under randomization (t(378)=−8.69, p<10⁻¹³). Randomization perturbs but does not erase the correlational structure → RO collapse is frame misalignment, not noise.
- **Takeaway:** the SPD manifold captures emergent inter-dimensional coordination; eigenvalues (lose phase) and a single eigenvector (under-discriminative here) underperform, so the *full* correlation geometry carries the signal.

## Critical Assessment

- The conceptual move—using order manipulation to test whether persona geometry is intrinsic vs. frame-dependent—is genuinely novel and the experimental design cleanly operationalizes it (FO / RO / RO-BTSP is a thoughtful dissociation).
- The decomposition of degradation into order vs. frame effects is a useful diagnostic that other LLM-eval papers could adopt.
- The RMT control (Wigner–Dyson spacing) is a principled way to rule out the "randomization just destroys structure" alternative, strengthening the frame-dependence claim.
- However, the work is narrowly scoped (single model, binary cultural contrast) and was not peer-reviewed in its current form (withdrawn from ICML). The headline percentages, while internally consistent, rest on one architecture and one questionnaire.
- The SPD-at-identity (log-Euclidean) choice is justified, but the author does not compare against affine-invariant or Fréchet-mean-referenced metrics; the conclusion could be metric-sensitive.
- The interpretive leap from "frame-dependent correlation geometry" to "LLMs lack stable trait structure" is provocative but reasonable given the autoregressive mechanism invoked.

## Strengths

- Clean, falsifiable three-hypothesis structure with a discriminating V-shape prediction.
- Methodologically grounded (SPD manifolds, log-Euclidean metric, RMT controls, bootstrap CIs, large-N replication).
- Concrete, reproducible-seeming numbers with full statistics.
- Practical, actionable prescription for frame-aware LLM-persona evaluation.

## Limitations

- **Single model:** GPT-4o only; cross-architecture verification (Llama, Gemini, smaller open models) is future work.
- **Binary cultural contrast:** American vs. Chinese-American is a clean signal but a narrow testbed; collectivist-vs-individualist and other axes untested.
- **Single bias domain:** generalization to political, gender, or other bias dimensions is conjectured, not shown.
- **Metric sensitivity:** only log-Euclidean at identity tested; alternative SPD metrics not compared.
- **No code/data release yet:** "upon acceptance" + withdrawn status leaves reproducibility uncertain.
- **Provenance flag:** withdrawn ICML submission; should be verified independently.
- **Sample filtering:** only complete/well-formed responses retained; filtering rates and any resulting selection bias are not foregrounded in the main text.
