Key Finding

In test-time reinforcement learning (TTRL), majority-vote pseudo-labels create a Correct-Answer Extinction Window: correct answers briefly appear as minority signals in early training, then are permanently suppressed by wrong majority-vote labels. Once locked in, this damage is irreversible. The paper shows that TTRL's apparent accuracy gains are systematically misleading — most improvements come from sharpening already-solvable problems, while the number of corrupted problems far exceeds genuinely learned ones (L/D ratio as low as 0.004 on Llama-3.2-3B).

Why This Matters

TTRL is gaining traction as a label-free method to improve LLM reasoning at inference time. This paper challenges the prevailing narrative that TTRL uniformly helps. Per-problem trajectory analysis reveals that aggregate metrics hide concentrated, systematic harm. Without intervention, the extinction window silently destroys correct-answer signals — making "self-improvement" partly self-destruction. The Flip Rate indicator offers a practical, ground-truth-free early warning system.

Critical Edge

  • Domain scope: Evaluated only on math reasoning (AIME, AMC, MATH-500); code generation or other domains are untested.
  • Scale ceiling: Maximum model size tested is 7B parameters; extinction window behavior at larger scales is unknown.
  • Floor effect: When initial accuracy is near zero, few correct signals exist to preserve regardless of intervention.
  • Hyperparameters: Six tunable knobs (τFR, τMR, λ&sub1;, λ&sub2;, βmax, pskip) though sensitivity analysis shows reasonable robustness.

TTRL-Guard Mechanisms

FRS — Flip-Rate-Aware Reward Scaling
Down-weights rewards as FR declines: r̃ = r · w, where w = α · γ · δ. Globally reduces reward during high-FR phases and penalizes contradictory states.
MPS — Minority-Preserving Sampling
When FR > τFR, mixes MV objective with a minority-preservation term: L = (1-β)·LMV + β·Lmin. Retains gradient signal from correct minority answers.
RCSU — Risk-Conditioned Sparse Updating
Skips gradient updates on high-risk (polarized) problems with probability pskip, preserving recovery possibility while accelerating training.

TL;DR

Majority-vote pseudo-labels in TTRL permanently suppress correct answers via an "Extinction Window." TTRL-Guard detects this with Flip Rate and intervenes with three mechanisms to achieve SOTA label-free results.

Open Questions

  • Does the extinction window manifest in code generation or factual QA?
  • How does TTRL-Guard scale beyond 7B?
  • Can FR monitoring combine with external verification (e.g., tool use)?

Strengths & Limits

Strengths

  • Per-problem analysis reveals what aggregate metrics conceal
  • Three modular, complementary mechanisms
  • No ground-truth labels or external tools needed
  • Causal temporal evidence: gains appear after extinction window closes

Limitations

  • Math-only evaluation
  • Max 7B model scale
  • Near-zero initial accuracy is a floor
  • Six hyperparameters to tune
