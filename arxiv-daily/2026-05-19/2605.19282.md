> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示Muon优化器在VLA与RLVR后训练中因均匀谱白化放大噪声尾部方向而失效，提出Pion——用两阶段Promotion+Suppression的高通NS迭代替代均匀白化，保留主导奇异值、抑制噪声尾部，并支持per-head模式，在LIBERO与Qwen3 RLVR上全面超越Muon与AdamW。

> **TL;DR:** This paper shows that Muon's Newton-Schulz (NS) spectral whitening, which helps LLM pretraining, fails in two post-training regimes: vision-language-action (VLA) training (low-rank action gradients cause noisy-tail amplification) and RL with verifiable rewards (RLVR) (low-SNR gradients cause policy collapse). It proposes Pion, a drop-in replacement using a two-stage Promotion+Suppression "high-pass NS" iteration that anchors dominant singular values at 1 while suppressing noisy tail components toward 0, plus a per-head mode preserving pretrained head heterogeneity. On LIBERO Object with VLA-Adapter, Pion reaches 100% success rate at 1,500 steps vs 97.0% for Muon and 32.2% for AdamW; on a real Franka robot (π0.5/DROID) Pion lifts average grasp success from 31.1% (AdamW)/38.9% (Muon) to 85.6%; and on RLVR with Qwen3-1.7B/4B (GRPO/GMPO on MATH/GSM8K), Pion outperforms AdamW while Muon collapses to near-zero accuracy.

## 核心贡献

- 诊断Muon在预训练之外的两类**谱失配失效**：(i) VLA中动作模块梯度低秩，均匀白化放大噪声尾部；(ii) RLVR中策略梯度低信噪比，均匀白化放大噪声方向导致策略崩溃
- 揭示Muon将权重矩阵作为整体块处理，**忽略注意力投影的per-head特化**——预训练继承的各头范数异质性在后训练中需要不同尺度的更新
- 提出Pion：两阶段Promotion+Suppression的**高通NS迭代**，将主导奇异值锚定在1、噪声尾部抑制向0，滤波强度可控，且per-step成本与Muon相同
- 设计**per-head模式**：通过简单reshape沿头维度独立应用更新，保留预训练头结构，无额外开销
- 在VLA（LIBERO/LIBERO-Plus + 真实Franka机器人）与RLVR（Qwen3-1.7B/4B, GRPO/GMPO, MATH/GSM8K）上全面超越Muon与AdamW

## Key Contributions

- Diagnoses two spectral-mismatch failures of Muon beyond pretraining: (i) in VLA, low-rank action-module gradients cause uniform whitening to amplify noisy tail directions; (ii) in RLVR, low-SNR policy gradients cause whitening to corrupt the update and collapse the policy
- Identifies that Muon's whole-matrix NS ignores per-head specialization in attention projections inherited from pretraining, which requires heterogeneous update scales across heads during post-training
- Proposes Pion: a two-stage Promotion+Suppression high-pass NS iteration that anchors dominant singular values at 1 while suppressing noisy tail components toward 0, with controllable filter strength, at the same per-step cost as Muon
- Designs a per-head mode that reshapes projections along the head dimension to apply updates independently per head, preserving pretrained heterogeneity at no extra cost
- Validates across VLA (LIBERO/LIBERO-Plus, real Franka robot with π0.5/DROID) and RLVR (Qwen3-1.7B/4B, GRPO/GMPO, MATH/GSM8K), consistently outperforming Muon and AdamW, including settings where Muon collapses to zero

## Methodology

**Diagnosis.** The paper attributes Muon's failures to a shared spectral mismatch. In VLA, the action-module gradient is highly low-rank (Fig. 1-a shows per-module effective rank along training), so informative directions concentrate in a few leading singular values while the tail is noise; Muon's NS iteration uniformly whitens the spectrum, elevating noisy tail directions to magnitude 1. In RLVR, GRPO gradients have much lower SNR than SFT (Fig. 2-a, measured on Qwen3-1.7B on MATH levels 3–5), because GRPO uses trajectory-level rewards (sparse signal), importance sampling, clipping, and group-relative normalization that increase gradient variance; Muon's whitening amplifies noisy directions to the same magnitude as informative ones, causing policy collapse (Fig. 2-b: Muon accuracy drops below initial checkpoint).

**Pion's high-pass NS.** Since each NS step reshapes normalized singular values via a scalar polynomial, Pion redesigns this polynomial map as a two-stage Promotion+Suppression mechanism: the Promotion stage drives dominant singular values toward 1, while the Suppression stage drives noisy tail components toward 0, inducing a sharp spectral high-pass effect with controllable filter strength. This replaces Muon's uniform whitening (all singular values → 1) with selective preservation. The per-step computational cost is identical to Muon (no SVD or sketching, unlike Low-Rank Muon variants).

**Per-head mode.** For attention projections W_Q, W_K, W_V, Pion reshapes the 2D weight matrix along the head dimension and applies NS independently per head, preserving the cross-head variance Var(‖W^h‖_F) that exists prior to RLVR (Fig. 4-b shows this variance is non-trivial across all 28 layers of Qwen3-1.7B). Default-mode Pion applies NS to the whole projection, yielding nearly-flat update variance across heads.

**Optimizer assignment.** Pion is applied to action 2D matrices (VLA) or attention projections (RLVR), Muon to vision/language 2D matrices, and AdamW elsewhere (embeddings/output layer). A modality-wise sweep (Table A6) confirms this assignment is optimal.

## Results & Highlights

- **VLA-Adapter on LIBERO (Fig. 5, fixed budget).** Pion reaches 100% success rate on LIBERO Object at 1,500 steps vs 97.0% for Muon and 32.2% for AdamW; on Spatial/Goal/Long, Pion improves over Muon on every task (e.g., Long: Pion 92.0 vs Muon 89.6 vs AdamW 69.2). Pion reaches 95.4% success at 500 steps on Object, saturating at 100% by 1,500.
- **VLANeXt on LIBERO/LIBERO-Plus (Table 1).** Pion achieves 96.35% on LIBERO vs Muon 93.65% vs AdamW 79.45%; on LIBERO-Plus 75.93% vs Muon 72.34% vs AdamW 64.57%. Under perturbations, Pion's advantage is amplified: Language 86.93% vs Muon 77.53% (+9.4pp), Noise 76.09% vs Muon 69.98% (+6.1pp), Robot 63.18% vs Muon 57.36% (+5.8pp).
- **Real-robot evaluation (Table 3, π0.5/DROID, 30 trials per task).** Pion lifts average grasp-and-place success from 31.1% (AdamW) and 38.9% (Muon) to 85.6% — a +46.7pp gain over AdamW and +46.7pp over Muon — across Cucumber→Plate (93.3% vs Muon 56.7% vs AdamW 40.0%), Cube→Plate (83.3% vs Muon 33.3% vs AdamW 33.3%), and Cube→Bowl (80.0% vs Muon 26.7% vs AdamW 20.0%), under only 20,000 training steps.
- **RLVR: Muon collapses, Pion succeeds (Fig. 6, 8 settings).** Across GRPO/GMPO × Qwen3-1.7B/4B × MATH/GSM8K, Muon's accuracy remains near zero throughout training and often falls below the initial checkpoint; Pion recovers a meaningful training signal and outperforms AdamW with faster convergence across all eight settings.
- **Gradient SNR (Fig. 7, Qwen3-1.7B, GRPO on GSM8K).** Pion consistently achieves higher gradient SNR than AdamW throughout training (SNR ~15 vs ~10 at step 40), confirming the high-pass filter's denoising effect.
- **Reverse ablation (Fig. 8, LPMuon).** Flipping Pion's high-pass to a low-pass mapping (LPMuon, contracting large singular values and amplifying small ones) causes training to fail — accuracy stays at the initial checkpoint — isolating the *direction* of spectral shaping (high-pass, not just any filtering) as the key factor.

## Critical Assessment

The paper delivers a well-motivated optimizer fix with strong empirical support, but several issues warrant scrutiny. First, the **RLVR results are presented only as learning curves (Fig. 6), not as a final-accuracy table with numbers and standard deviations** — the claim that "Pion outperforms AdamW" is visually evident but not quantified with confidence intervals across seeds, making the magnitude of the gain hard to assess (the Muon collapse is clear, but the Pion-vs-AdamW delta is not reported numerically). Second, the **VLA-Adapter results (Fig. 5) report single-run success rates** (e.g., 100% on Object) without error bars or seed counts stated in the main text, so the statistical reliability of the headline 100% vs 97.0% vs 32.2% comparison is unclear. Third, Pion's **high-pass NS introduces a controllable filter strength hyperparameter** whose sensitivity is not analyzed in the main text — it is unclear how Pion's performance degrades if the filter strength is mistuned, or whether the same strength works across VLA and RLVR (which have different spectral profiles). Fourth, the paper compares against **Muon and AdamW but not against Low-Rank Muon (LRMuon) variants in the main results** — LRMuon is the closest related work (top-k subspace projection before NS), and while Appendix K reports Pion outperforms LRMuon across top-k ranks, this comparison is relegated to the appendix rather than the main tables. Fifth, the **per-head mode is critical for RLVR** (Fig. 4-a shows default-mode Pion underperforms AdamW), yet the paper does not analyze whether per-head mode helps or hurts in VLA, where the action module (not attention) is the target — the optimizer-assignment logic (Pion on action, Muon on vision/language) is validated only via an appendix sweep (Table A6). Finally, the mechanism analysis relies on **effective rank and SNR visualizations** (Figs. 1, 2) that are qualitative; no formal bound ties the high-pass filter strength to a guaranteed SNR improvement or convergence guarantee.

## Strengths

- The spectral-mismatch diagnosis (low-rank VLA gradients, low-SNR RLVR gradients) is a clear, mechanistic explanation that unifies two seemingly different failure modes
- The high-pass NS redesign is elegant — it exploits the existing NS polynomial-map structure, so Pion is a true drop-in replacement with identical per-step cost
- The reverse ablation (LPMuon) cleanly isolates that high-pass directionality, not just any filtering, is the active ingredient
- Real-robot evaluation (Franka/π0.5/DROID) with a +46.7pp gain over both baselines is a substantial, deployment-relevant result
- Coverage spans two architectures (VLA-Adapter ℓ1-regression, VLANeXt flow-matching), two RL algorithms (GRPO, GMPO), and two model sizes (Qwen3-1.7B/4B)

## Limitations

- RLVR results are figure-only (learning curves), no final-accuracy table with standard deviations across seeds
- VLA-Adapter headline numbers (100% vs 97.0% vs 32.2%) lack stated seed counts or error bars in the main text
- High-pass filter strength sensitivity not analyzed in the main text; unclear if one strength fits both VLA and RLVR
- Low-Rank Muon comparison relegated to appendix despite being the closest related work
- Per-head mode's effect in VLA (action module, not attention) unanalyzed; only RLVR attention is studied
- No formal convergence or SNR-improvement bound tying filter strength to guarantees

## Relevance

直接映射到"LLM Training Stability & Observability"与"Computation Reliability & Precision Errors"两个方向：它诊断并修复了Muon谱白化在VLA/RLVR后训练中的数值不稳定性（噪声放大导致策略崩溃），通过梯度SNR与有效秩等可观测信号定位失效根因，并给出高通滤波的工程化修复，对训练稳定性的实践与可观测性具有直接价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How sensitive is Pion to the high-pass filter strength hyperparameter, and can a single strength work across VLA and RLVR with different spectral profiles?
- Does the per-head mode help in VLA's action module (not just RLVR attention), and what is the optimal optimizer-assignment policy across modalities?
- Can the spectral-mismatch diagnosis be formalized into a convergence or SNR-improvement bound that predicts when high-pass filtering will help?
- How does Pion interact with other post-training stages (SFT, DPO) beyond GRPO/GMPO, and does the high-pass recipe transfer to preference-based RL?
