Breaking Modality Heterogeneity in Low-Bit Quantization for Large Vision-Language Models

SplitQ decouples modality-specific outlier channels and adaptively calibrates cross-modal quantization errors, achieving state-of-the-art low-bit PTQ for VLMs.

Key Finding

In vision-language models, cross-modal heterogeneity is distributed unevenly across channels: a small number of channels contain most modality-specific outliers, and critically, text outliers and vision outliers reside in different channel subsets. This asymmetry means a single quantization scheme cannot serve both modalities well — motivating a split-and-calibrate approach.

Why This Matters

Deploying VLMs on resource-constrained devices requires aggressive quantization, but naively quantizing destroys accuracy because vision and text signals have fundamentally different distributions. SplitQ directly addresses a critical deployment bottleneck — enabling 3-bit quantization with minimal accuracy loss. The channel-level heterogeneity analysis provides a new lens for all future multi-modal compression research.

Methodology

A two-module post-training quantization framework:

MOCD

Modality-specific Outlier Channel Decoupling. Identifies and isolates channels where outliers concentrate, splitting salient channels to prevent cross-modal interference.

→

ACC

Adaptive Cross-Modal Calibration. Dual lightweight learnable branches dynamically adjust quantization parameters per modality to fix residual distribution gaps.
