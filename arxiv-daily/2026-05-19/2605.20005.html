FINCH — Fine-Tuning Without Forgetting via Loss-Adaptive Learning Rates

Parjanya Prajakta Prashant, Jiongli Zhu, Aldan Creo, Babak Salimi · UC San Diego

A theoretically motivated learning-rate schedule (η = κ/√L̄) that reduces catastrophic forgetting by 93% on average across knowledge acquisition, science reasoning, and low-resource language adaptation — while matching standard SFT on target-task accuracy. Unlike token-reweighting methods, FINCH leaves the training objective unchanged.

Key Finding

Per-step forgetting is bounded by O(η·√L_B). Setting the learning rate inversely proportional to √(current loss) makes the forgetting bound constant per step — eliminating the runaway forgetting that occurs when high-loss batches meet fixed learning rates.

η_i = min( η_base / √(L̄_i + ε) , η_max )

Why This Matters

  • Drop-in replacement: one line change to the LR schedule
  • No modified training objective — hard tokens fully contribute
  • Only one hyperparameter (η_base) to tune beyond standard SFT
  • Pareto-optimal across accuracy, truthfulness, hallucination, and calibration

Critical Edge

Theory derived for SGD but experiments use AdamW — gap acknowledged but not resolved.
Only tested on models ≤8B parameters; scaling to 70B+ is unknown.
η_base selected via grid search; no automatic calibration provided.
The 93% figure sums signed Δ across 6 settings, potentially masking per-setting variability.

Methodology

  1. Formalize per-step forgetting as ΔL_old under SGD on cross-entropy loss with bounded activations.
  2. Second-order Taylor expansion of the log-probability ratio yields ΔL_old ≤ C₁η√L_B + C₂η²√L_B.
  3. Setting η = κ/√L_B makes the leading term constant O(κ) per step.
  4. Implement with EMA-smoothed loss: L̄_i = 0.9·L̄_{i−1} + 0.1·L_B(θ_i), cap at η_max = 5×10⁻⁵.
  5. Works with AdamW despite SGD derivation; only η_base needs tuning.

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical bound directly motivates the method
  • Extremely simple — drop-in LR schedule replacement
  • Orthogonal: combinable with LoRA, regularization, replay
  • Preserves training objective; high-loss tokens still learn fully
  • Open-source code; only one new hyperparameter
  • Pareto-optimal across all evaluated dimensions

Limitations

  • SGD theory vs AdamW practice gap unresolved
  • Calibration degraded, not eliminated (Brier +9.1)
  • Only ≤8B models tested; scaling unknown
  • η_base requires grid search; no auto-calibration
  • Not tested in continual / sequential fine-tuning
  • Interaction with EWC, replay, etc. unexplored
