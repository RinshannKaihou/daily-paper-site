StableGrad: Backward Scale Control without Batch Normalization

Jose I. Mestre, Alberto Fernández-Hernández, Cristian Pérez-Corral, Manuel F. Dolz, Enrique S. Quintana-Ortí

StableGrad normalizes layer-wise weight gradients after backprop using the output-adjoint standard deviation as reference scale — enabling stable training of deep PINNs and BatchNorm-free CNNs without modifying the forward computation.

Key Finding

  • Layer-wise gradient scales diverge exponentially in deep networks; StableGrad rescales every block gradient gₗ by (σ_out / σ_ℓ) after backprop, before the optimizer step.
  • This transfers the scale of the backward signal source to all parameter blocks, enforcing layer-wise backward-scale consistency.
  • Forward scale is set at initialization (fan-in); backward scale is dynamically controlled throughout training — a clean separation of concerns.
  • The effective kernel K_SG = JPJT reweights each layer's contribution. Theorem 1 proves a larger local loss decrease vs standard GD when the rescaled kernel has sufficient action on the residual.

Critical Edge

  • Not a full BN replacement yet — 4% gap on ResNet-50 / ImageNet (67% vs 71%).
  • Does not guarantee forward activation stability during training.
  • Does not prevent raw adjoint signals from becoming unstable during backprop propagation.
  • PINN results report means over 3 seeds but no standard deviations.
  • Evaluated only on MLP-based PINNs and standard CNNs — not yet tested on Transformers or SIRENs.
  • Requires manual scheduling (switched off after 25k steps in PINNs).

Methodology

After the backward pass computes gradients gₗ = ∇θ_ℓ L for each parameter block:

  • Compute empirical std of each block gradient: σ_ℓ = std(gₗ)
  • Compute reference scale from output adjoint: σ_out = std(∂L/∂u_θ)
  • Rescale: g𝜃_ℓ = (σ_out / (σ_ℓ + ε)) · gₗ
  • Pass rescaled gradients to optimizer (AdamW, SGD, etc.)

No forward model modification. No architectural normalization layers. Optimizer-agnostic.

Why This Matters

  • BatchNorm is genuinely problematic in PINNs and physics-based models — StableGrad provides a practical alternative.
  • Clean conceptual split: initialization handles forward scale, optimizer handles backward scale.
  • Simple to implement, negligible overhead, optimizer-agnostic.

Limitations

  • No forward stability guarantee during training.
  • Adjoint instability not addressed.
  • Depth benefits are problem-dependent (Poisson d12 overfits).
  • ε and step-cutoff require tuning.

Strengths

  • No forward model modification — critical for PINNs.
  • Strong theoretical grounding (effective kernel, local decrease theorem).
  • Consistent improvements across 3 PDEs + 2 CNN architectures.
  • Honest discussion of what it cannot do.

Questions for Further Exploration

Combine with schedule-free optimizers? Interaction with LayerNorm in Transformers? Continuous application vs warmup cutoff in PINNs? Dual mechanism for forward activation stability? Scaling to 50+ layer PINNs or higher-dimensional PDEs?
