> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 FINCH，一种基于理论推导的 loss 自适应学习率调度策略：将学习率设为与当前 mini-batch loss 平方根成反比（η = κ/√L），使每步遗忘上界保持恒定，在知识获取、科学推理和低资源语言适配三个场景上平均减少 93% 的灾难性遗忘，同时匹配标准 SFT 的任务性能。

> **TL;DR:** FINCH is a theoretically motivated, loss-adaptive learning-rate schedule (η = κ/√L̄) that reduces catastrophic forgetting by 93% on average across knowledge acquisition, science reasoning, and low-resource language adaptation, while matching standard SFT on target-task accuracy. It leaves the fine-tuning objective unchanged—unlike token-reweighting methods that sacrifice new-task learning.

## 核心贡献

- **理论发现：** 证明了每步遗忘量 ΔL_old 的上界由学习率与当前 mini-batch loss 平方根的乘积决定（O(η·√L_B)），首次从理论上解释了高 loss 批次更容易导致灾难性遗忘的原因。
- **FINCH 调度策略：** 将学习率设为 η = min(η_base / √(L̄ + ε), η_max)，其中 L̄ 是 mini-batch loss 的指数移动平均（α=0.9），使每步遗忘上界在训练过程中保持恒定。
- **保持训练目标不变：** 与 FLOW、DFT、TALR、STM 等修改 loss 或抑制高 loss token 的方法不同，FINCH 不改变训练目标，高 loss token 仍然完整参与学习，因此不会牺牲新任务的准确率。
- **全面评估：** 在 Qwen3-4B 和 Llama-3-8B 两个模型上，覆盖知识获取（TOFU）、科学推理（SciKnowEval）和低资源语言适配（Galician Alpaca）三个任务，并在 TruthfulQA、HaluEval、Brier Score 等可靠性维度上进行深入分析。
- **可靠性保持：** 在 Qwen3-4B 知识获取场景中，FINCH 将 TruthfulQA 退化从 −13.9 降至 −2.6（5 倍改善），将 HaluEval 从 −9.8 反转为 +3.3，Brier Score 增量从 +14.7 降至 +9.1。

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretical bound on per-step forgetting:** Proves that per-step forgetting ΔL_old is bounded by O(η·√L_B), where η is the learning rate and L_B is the current mini-batch loss, under standard boundedness/smoothness assumptions for softmax networks (Theorem 1).
- **Cumulative forgetting guarantee:** Shows that the adaptive schedule η = κ/√L_B yields cumulative forgetting of O(Tκ) over T steps (Corollary 1), compared to unbounded early forgetting under constant learning rates.
- **FINCH schedule:** Implements η_i = min(η_base / √(L̄_i + ε), η_max) with EMA-smoothed loss (α=0.9), η_max = 5×10⁻⁵ fixed across all experiments; only η_base requires tuning.
- **Objective-preserving design:** Unlike token-reweighting baselines (FLOW, DFT, TALR, STM) that suppress high-loss tokens and hurt new-task performance, FINCH keeps the SFT objective unchanged, allowing hard tokens to fully contribute to learning.
- **Comprehensive reliability evaluation:** Beyond benchmark accuracy, evaluates truthfulness (TruthfulQA), hallucination detection (HaluEval), and verbalized confidence calibration (Brier score), showing FINCH provides Pareto-optimal trade-offs across all dimensions.

## Methodology

**Theoretical foundation:** The authors formalize fine-tuning as SGD on cross-entropy loss L(θ) = −E[log p_θ(y|x)] with training distribution q and old-task distribution q_old. Per-step forgetting is defined as ΔL_old(p_i) = L_old(p_{i+1}) − L_old(p_i).

Under Assumption 1 (bounded inputs, bounded parameter trajectory, bounded activation derivatives — standard for SwiGLU-based LLMs), the proof proceeds in three steps:
1. **Uniform bounds:** The logit Jacobian ‖J_θ(x)‖_op ≤ M, score-function gradient ‖∇_θ log p_θ(y|x)‖₂ ≤ G, and Hessian ‖∇²_θ log p_θ(y|x)‖_op ≤ H are all uniformly bounded.
2. **Taylor expansion:** For any old-task example, a second-order Taylor expansion of the log-probability ratio gives: log(p_{i+1}/p_i) ≤ C₁η‖∇L_B‖₂ + C₂η²‖∇L_B‖₂².
3. **Gradient-loss connection:** Using the Jacobian bound, Pinsker's inequality, and Jensen's inequality, ‖∇L_B(θ_i)‖₂ ≤ M√(2L_B(θ_i)). Substituting gives the final bound: ΔL_old(p_i) ≤ C₁η√L_B + C₂η²√L_B.

**FINCH schedule:** Setting η = κ/√L_B makes the leading forgetting term constant O(κ) per step. In practice:
- η_i = min(η_base / √(L̄_i + ε), η_max)
- L̄_i = 0.9·L̄_{i-1} + 0.1·L_B(θ_i) (exponential moving average)
- η_max = 5×10⁻⁵ (fixed); η_base is the only tuned hyperparameter
- Works directly with AdamW despite the analysis being for SGD

**Experimental setup:**
- Models: Qwen3-4B-Instruct, Llama-3-8B
- Tasks: Knowledge acquisition (TOFU, 200 synthetic author profiles), Science reasoning (SciKnowEval Chemistry L-3), Language adaptation (Galician Alpaca)
- Old-task benchmarks: HellaSwag, WinoGrande, MMLU, IFEval
- Baselines: SFT, SFT (small lr), FLOW, DFT, TALR, STM, L2 Reg, WiSE-FT, LoRA
- All methods use AdamW, cosine schedule with warmup, gradient clipping at 1.0, best checkpoint selected by validation task performance
- Hardware: single H200

## Results & Highlights

- **93% average forgetting reduction:** Summing signed Avg. Δ across all 6 model-task settings, SFT incurs 61.9 total degradation points vs. 3.9 for FINCH (1 − 3.9/61.9 ≈ 93%).
- **Matches SFT task performance:** On KA, FINCH achieves 83.3% (Qwen3-4B) and 84.3% (Llama-3-8B) task accuracy, comparable to SFT's 83.5% and 83.3% respectively.
- **Pareto-optimal trade-off:** Across all 6 settings in Figure 2, FINCH consistently occupies the upper-right region of the accuracy-vs-forgetting plot, outperforming all baselines.
- **Knowledge acquisition (Qwen3-4B):** Avg. Δ = +0.1 (FINCH) vs. −9.6 (SFT) vs. −8.0 (FLOW) vs. −7.4 (LoRA). FINCH nearly eliminates forgetting while matching SFT's 83.3% task accuracy.
- **Science (Qwen3-4B):** FINCH achieves the highest task accuracy (65.0%) with Avg. Δ = −1.6, while SFT gets 62.5% with Avg. Δ = −10.9.
- **Language adaptation (Qwen3-4B):** FINCH achieves 90.0% win-tie rate with Avg. Δ = −2.4, vs. SFT's 89.0% win-tie with Avg. Δ = −14.3.
- **TruthfulQA (Qwen3-4B KA):** FINCH degrades by only −2.6 points vs. −13.9 for SFT, −11.7 for FLOW, −16.4 for LoRA.
- **HaluEval (Qwen3-4B KA):** FINCH actually improves by +3.3 points, while SFT degrades by −9.8.
- **Confidence calibration (Brier score):** FINCH increases Brier score by +9.1 vs. +14.7 for SFT on KA (Qwen3-4B), showing better calibration preservation.
- **Small gradient norm doesn't help:** Reducing max gradient norm from 1.0 to 0.0001 only reduces forgetting by severely hurting task accuracy (83.5% → 58.5%), confirming that FINCH's approach is fundamentally different from simply constraining update magnitude.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The theoretical insight that per-step forgetting scales as η·√L is new and elegantly motivates the schedule. While adaptive learning rates are not new (AdaGrad, etc.), the specific application to controlling catastrophic forgetting via loss-adaptivity is novel, and the theoretical derivation from first principles (not just empirical observation) is a genuine contribution.
- **Rigor (High):** Full proof in Appendix A with explicit assumptions and constants. The boundedness assumptions (Assumption 1) are reasonable for modern LLMs with SwiGLU activations. Empirical evaluation is thorough: 6 model-task settings, 10 baselines, 3 reliability dimensions, hyperparameter sweeps with best-checkpoint selection.
- **Reproducibility (High):** Code is publicly available. The method requires only one hyperparameter (η_base) beyond standard SFT settings. η_max is fixed at 5×10⁻⁵. All training details, prompts, and evaluation procedures are specified.
- **Significance (High):** 93% forgetting reduction with no task performance loss is a striking result. The method is trivially easy to implement (one line change to the LR schedule). If it generalizes to larger scales, it could become standard practice. The reliability analysis (TruthfulQA, HaluEval, Brier score) goes well beyond typical ML papers and addresses real deployment concerns.
- **Red flags:**
  - The 93% figure is computed by summing signed Avg. Δ across all 6 settings, which can mask per-setting variability. Individual Avg. Δ ranges from −2.4 to +1.7 for FINCH, which is excellent but not zero.
  - The gap between SGD analysis and AdamW practice is acknowledged but not resolved. The theory applies to SGD; AdamW's adaptive per-parameter scaling may interact differently with the schedule.
  - Experiments are limited to ≤8B models. Scaling to 70B+ is unknown.
  - η_base is selected via grid search on validation task performance, adding one hyperparameter. The sensitivity to this choice is not analyzed in detail.
  - On some settings (e.g., LA on Llama-3-8B), several baselines achieve similar win-tie rates, though with worse forgetting.

## Strengths

- Theoretically grounded with a clean, interpretable bound that directly motivates the method.
- Extremely simple to implement — a drop-in replacement for the learning-rate schedule.
- Orthogonal to existing approaches: can potentially be combined with LoRA, regularization, or replay.
- Does not modify the training objective, so high-loss tokens still contribute fully to learning.
- Comprehensive evaluation covering task performance, general benchmarks, factuality, hallucination, and calibration.
- Code is open-sourced, enhancing reproducibility and adoption potential.
- Pareto-optimal across all evaluated dimensions.

## Limitations

- The theory is derived for SGD but experiments use AdamW; closing this gap remains future work.
- Calibration degradation is reduced but not eliminated (Brier score still increases by +9.1 vs. +14.7 for SFT on KA).
- Only evaluated on models up to 8B parameters; scaling behavior is unknown.
- η_base requires tuning via grid search; no automatic calibration procedure is provided.
- The EMA coefficient α = 0.9 and η_max = 5×10⁻⁵ are fixed without sensitivity analysis.
- Not tested in multi-task or continual fine-tuning scenarios where tasks arrive sequentially.
- Interaction with other forgetting-mitigation techniques (EWC, replay, etc.) is unexplored.

## Relevance

该工作提出了一种极简的、理论驱动的学习率调度策略来缓解 LLM 微调中的灾难性遗忘，与持续学习、模型适配和安全对齐等研究方向高度相关。其方法仅需修改学习率调度即可实现显著效果，对实际 LLM 部署和迭代微调具有很高的实用价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does FINCH interact with LoRA? Can the two methods be combined for even better forgetting reduction in parameter-efficient settings?
- What happens in continual fine-tuning with multiple sequential tasks — does the cumulative bound O(Tκ) become a limitation?
- Can the theory be extended to AdamW's per-parameter adaptive learning rates to tighten the analysis?
- How sensitive is FINCH to the EMA coefficient α and the cap η_max? Is there an optimal setting or can these be automatically adapted?
- Does FINCH scale effectively to 70B+ models, and does the relative benefit increase or decrease with scale?
- Can FINCH be combined with replay-based methods (when pretraining data is partially available) for further gains?
