> **一句话总结：** 通过路径修补（path patching）方法对五个开源多模态大模型进行注意力头级别的因果分析，发现幻觉驱动头分布广泛且聚合权重大、抵抗头高度集中，该不对称结构导致模型在模态冲突时偏向错误文本前提；基于此提出的条件干预方法MACI在MMMC基准上实现了最大的幻觉降低。

> **TL;DR:** Path-patching analysis across five MLLMs reveals an asymmetric imbalance between broadly distributed hallucination-driving attention heads and concentrated hallucination-resisting heads; the proposed conditional intervention MACI suppresses driving heads only when conflict is detected, achieving the largest hallucination reduction on MMMC among inference-time baselines.

## 核心贡献

- 首次在注意力头级别提供了模态冲突幻觉的因果证据，在五个开源 MLLM 中识别出幻觉驱动头和幻觉抵抗头两组具有对立因果角色的注意力头。
- 揭示了两组头之间的双重不对称性：驱动效应跨层广泛分布且聚合权重更大（平均 1.51 倍），抵抗效应集中在少数高重要性头部（Top-5 贡献 27.2% vs 14.0%）。
- 提出了 MACI（Modality-conflict-Aware Causal Intervention）条件干预方法，利用抵抗头激活检测冲突，仅在检测到冲突时抑制驱动头，在五个模型上均取得最佳幻觉抑制效果，并零样本迁移至 SCI-SemanticConflict。

## Key Contributions

- Provide head-level causal evidence for modality-conflict hallucination by applying path patching across five open-source MLLMs (Qwen2.5-VL, Qwen3-VL, LLaVA, LLaVA-NeXT, InternVL3), identifying hallucination-driving and hallucination-resisting attention heads with opposing causal roles.
- Reveal a consistent twofold asymmetry: driving effects are distributed across more layers and heads with greater aggregate weight (mean per-model ratio: 1.51×), while resisting effects concentrate in a small number of high-importance heads (top-5 heads account for 27.2% of resisting vs. 14.0% of driving importance).
- Validate identified heads through generation-time ablations (including random-head control), showing driving-head ablation reduces hallucination and resisting-head ablation increases it; driving heads identified on object conflict also transfer to attribute/relation conflicts and SCI-SemanticConflict.
- Propose MACI, a conditional inference-time intervention that uses resisting-head activations for conflict detection (AUROC 0.89–0.95) and suppresses driving heads only when necessary, achieving the largest hallucination reduction among compared baselines on MMMC with a favorable accuracy trade-off and zero-shot cross-benchmark transfer.

## Methodology

The paper applies **path patching**, a mechanistic interpretability technique, at the attention-head level to identify causal contributors to modality-conflict hallucination. For each sample, the model processes both a conflict input (image + biased question with erroneous presupposition) and a clean input (same image + unbiased question). Clean-run activations provide a premise-unbiased reference; patching them into the conflict run reveals whether each head drives or resists hallucination.

The **hallucination advantage** is defined as the log-likelihood difference between the hallucinated and factual answer tokens. A head's **importance score** is the average change in hallucination advantage when its activation is replaced by the clean-run counterpart, computed over a 256-sample prototype set disjoint from validation/test splits. Positive scores identify driving heads; negative scores identify resisting heads. Top-k heads are selected by importance magnitude for each polarity.

**MACI** trains a Lasso logistic regression probe on resisting-head activations (averaged last-token prefill representations) to detect conflict. When the probe predicts conflict above a validation-tuned threshold, driving heads are zero-ablated during generation. This conditional design avoids indiscriminate harm from unconditional ablation.

Experiments span five 7B–8B MLLMs with diverse architectures (dynamic-resolution tiling, MLP projection, cross-attention). Evaluation uses the MMMC object-conflict test split (500 held-out instances), with SCI-SemanticConflict as a zero-shot cross-benchmark test. Hallucination is judged by DeepSeek-V3.2 and cross-validated with Llama-3.3-70B (Cohen's κ: 0.784–0.863).

## Results & Highlights

- **Driving-head ablation** consistently reduces hallucination across all five models; **resisting-head ablation** consistently increases it; **random-head ablation** (size-matched, 5-seed average) stays near baseline, confirming the effect depends on causally selected heads.
- On MMMC, MACI achieves the largest hallucination reduction among all compared baselines (VCD, ICD, OPERA, ASCD): e.g., LLaVA-NeXT from 44.39% to 26.24% (−18.15pp), InternVL3 from 69.03% to 46.83% (−22.20pp).
- Non-conflict accuracy remains within 2pp of baseline on three models (Qwen2.5-VL, Qwen3-VL, LLaVA-NeXT); larger drops on InternVL3 and LLaVA accompany larger hallucination reductions.
- MACI transfers zero-shot to SCI-SemanticConflict without re-training heads, probe, or threshold (mean −7.9pp across five models).
- Baselines show model-dependent effects: VCD, ICD, OPERA do not consistently improve; ASCD substantially degrades non-conflict accuracy.
- Object-identified driving heads reduce hallucination on attribute/relation conflicts and SCI (Table 1), suggesting they capture a broader premise-following tendency.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are from the National Key Laboratory for Novel Software Technology and the School of Artificial Intelligence at Nanjing University, China. Zhen Wu is the corresponding author. The work builds on prior benchmarks (MMMC by Zhang et al. at ICML 2025) and established mechanistic interpretability methods (path patching by Wang et al.). Code is promised upon acceptance. The paper uses rigorous causal methodology (path patching, random-head controls, split-half stability checks showing 79.6%/64.7% top-30 overlap for driving/resisting heads) and cross-validates hallucination judgments with two independent LLM judges.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** First to apply head-level path patching to modality-conflict hallucination in MLLMs, moving beyond correlational analyses (e.g., Nguyen et al.'s linear decodability finding) to causal attribution. The twofold asymmetry finding is genuinely new.
- **Rigor (High):** Random-head ablation controls, split-half stability analysis, dual-judge cross-validation, and disjoint train/validation/test splits demonstrate careful experimental design. The use of five models with diverse architectures strengthens generality claims.
- **Reproducibility (Medium-High):** All models are open-source; evaluation benchmarks are public. Code is promised but not yet available. Hyperparameters (k+, k−, threshold) are validation-selected rather than test-tuned.
- **Significance (High):** The mechanistic insight (imbalanced routing structure) explains why visual evidence fails to prevail and is actionable via MACI. The conditional intervention design is principled — separating detection (resisting heads) from action (driving-head suppression).
- **Red flags:** The probe requires labeled training samples for conflict detection (though zero-shot transfer works for attribute/relation types). Evaluation is limited to two benchmarks (MMMC and SCI), both involving object-substitution-style conflicts. The approach has not been tested on settings where visual input itself may be misleading. MACI's non-conflict accuracy drops on some models (InternVL3: −4.66pp, LLaVA: −6.49pp) suggest driving heads are not purely conflict-specific in those architectures.

## Strengths

- Clean causal methodology with path patching providing stronger evidence than correlational approaches.
- Consistent findings across five diverse MLLM architectures, suggesting the imbalance is a general phenomenon rather than an artifact of a specific model.
- The conditional intervention (MACI) is well-motivated by the mechanistic finding — resisting heads detect conflict, driving heads are suppressed only when needed.
- Strong transfer results: object-identified driving heads generalize to attribute/relation conflicts and an independent benchmark.
- Favorable hallucination-accuracy trade-off compared to existing baselines, most of which show inconsistent or negative effects.

## Limitations

- Component identification relies on object-conflict data and the prefill stage; generalization to other conflict types and decoding-time dynamics is not fully characterized.
- The conflict-detection probe requires labeled samples; while zero-shot transfer works for attribute/relation conflicts, this may not hold for more diverse conflict distributions.
- Evaluation focuses on MMMC (the dedicated benchmark) and SCI-SemanticConflict as the only cross-benchmark test; broader evaluation on other hallucination benchmarks (e.g., HallusionBench, POPE) is not provided.
- The approach does not address settings where the visual input itself is misleading rather than serving as ground truth.
- k+ and k− require validation-set tuning per model, though sensitivity analysis shows reasonable stability.

## Relevance

本文从机制可解释性角度揭示了多模态大模型在模态冲突下的注意力头不平衡结构，对理解和对冲MLLM幻觉具有直接参考价值；MACI的条件干预思路为推理阶段的幻觉缓解提供了新范式，与多模态对齐和安全研究方向密切相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the identified driving/resisting heads be leveraged during fine-tuning (e.g., via regularization losses) to permanently reduce the imbalance rather than requiring inference-time intervention?
- How does the driving/resisting head distribution change as model scale increases beyond 8B parameters, or in models with significantly different architectures (e.g., Mamba-based vision-language models)?
- Could resisting-head activations serve as a general-purpose visual-fidelity signal for other hallucination types (e.g., object hallucination, attribute hallucination) beyond modality conflict?
- Is the twofold asymmetry (distributed driving vs. concentrated resistance) a consequence of training data composition, architectural inductive biases, or both?
- Can MACI be combined with decoding-level methods (e.g., VCD, OPERA) for complementary hallucination reduction, or do they interfere with each other?
