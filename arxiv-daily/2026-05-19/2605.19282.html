Rethinking Muon Beyond Pretraining: Spectral Failures and High-Pass Remedies for VLA and RLVR

Chongyu Fan · Gaowen Liu · Mingyi Hong · Ramana Rao Kompella · Sijia Liu
2026-05-19 Abstract-Only
Key Finding
Muon's uniform spectral whitening amplifies noise in low-rank and low-SNR regimes—causing catastrophic failures in VLA training and RLVR fine-tuning.
Key Metric

Why This Matters
The failure modes are fundamental, not edge cases. Directly impacts the growing audience using Muon for fine-tuning.
Critical Edge
Muon collapses to zero reward in RLVR. Pion is a drop-in fix: minimal, backward-compatible, no extra memory.

Methodology — Pion: High-Pass Newton-Schulz Iteration

Muon orthogonalizes momentum via Newton-Schulz (NS) iterations that drive all singular values toward 1 (uniform whitening). Pion replaces this with a two-stage high-pass filter:

── Standard Muon (uniform whitening) ────────────────────── Gradient G → Momentum M → NS iteration ×5 ↓ ALL singular values → 1.0 (noise amplified equally) ── Pion (high-pass filter) ─────────────────────────────── Gradient G → Momentum M → Promotion stage ↑ dominant σ → 1.0 Suppression stage ↑ noisy tail σ → 0.0 ↓ Per-head reshape → independent spectral update per attn head

Two Failure Modes Diagnosed

  • VLA regime: Action-module gradients are inherently low-rank; uniform NS amplifies noise in tail singular directions.
  • RLVR regime: Low-SNR gradients + uniform whitening destroys per-head specialization learned during pretraining.
