OScaR: The Occam's Razor for Extreme KV Cache Quantization in LLMs and Beyond

Zunhai Su, Rui Yang, Chao Zhang, Yaxiu Liu, Yifan Zhang, Wei Wu, Jing Xiong, Dayou Du, Xialie Zhuang, Yulei Qian, Yuchen Xie, Yik-Chung Wu, Hongxia Yang, Ngai Wong
Tsinghua University · Meituan LongCat · HKU · Edinburgh · UCAS · PolyU HK
Key Finding
Token Norm Imbalance (TNI) is the structural bottleneck that destroys per-channel KV cache quantization at INT2. A two-step fix — Canalized Rotation + Omni-Token Scaling — eliminates it with zero training.
TNI ↔ MSE lower bound proved
Key Metric
3.0× decoding speedup · 5.3× memory reduction · 4.1× throughput gain at 128K ctx on single H20 GPU.
INT2 vs BF16 FlashDecoding-v2
Why This Matters
Near-lossless INT2 KV cache compression works across text, multi-modal, and omni-modal LLMs. Long-context deployment becomes feasible on a single GPU without sacrificing quality.
Text · Vision · Audio · Omni
Critical Edge
Online Hadamard on Keys can't fuse with weights due to RoPE, adding ~1.5× decode overhead vs KIVI. NIAH result exceeding BF16 baseline (96.5% vs 96.0%) is unusual and unexplained.

Core Theoretical Insight

For per-channel block-wise quantization with bit-width b, the reconstruction MSE is lower-bounded by:

MSE ≥ (∥kmax∥&sub2; − ∥kmin∥&sub2;)² / (12 · (2b − 1)²)

This proves TNI amplifies quantization error proportionally to within-block norm range. OScaR's Canalized Rotation prevents Scaling-Induced Outlier Artifacts; Omni-Token Scaling then flattens the norm range to near-zero. Ablation confirms: removing either step collapses accuracy (e.g., 42.77% → 22.64% on WorldSense).
