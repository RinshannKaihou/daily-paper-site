Beyond Isotropy in JEPAs: Hamiltonian Geometry and Symplectic Prediction

Robert Jenkinson Alvarez

Isotropic Gaussian regularization in JEPAs is provably suboptimal — a symplectic Hamiltonian predictor unlocks +6.45 kNN and +10.64 linear-probe points.

Key Metric
+10.64 pts
linear probe lift over SIGReg on CIFAR-100 @ 80 epochs
Why This Matters
Bridges differential & symplectic geometry with self-supervised learning, exposing isotropy’s fundamental flaws and opening a principled geometric path for future SSL design.
Critical Edge
Only tested on CIFAR-100 and ImageNet-100 with ResNet-18; scalability to ImageNet-1K and modern architectures remains entirely unproven.

Methodology

Phase-space encoder

Encoder Eθ: X → R2d0 outputs a phase-space state s = (q, p) — content coordinate q plus auxiliary momentum p. ResNet-18 stage-3 features, headless token regime, identity projector. Deliberately minimal to isolate geometric effects.

Symplectic predictor (leapfrog integrator)

Separable Hamiltonian Hφ(q,p) = ½||p||² + Vφ(q) integrated via K-step leapfrog. Properties:

  • Symplectic — preserves the canonical 2-form
  • Volume-preserving — det = 1
  • Time-reversible
  • Singular values in reciprocal pairs

Anti-collapse regularizers (non-isotropic)

Scale budget: fixed-unit ℓ² targets on q and p — no Gaussianity assumed
Projected log-det floor: random projection → ridge-regularized covariance → hinge penalty below threshold τ
Participation-ratio floor: PR(ΣY) ≥ r0 prevents one-spike spectra

Key theoretical results

  • Thm 3.2: Minimax-optimal covariance is (c/d)H−1, not isotropic (c/tr(H))I
  • Thm 3.4: Price-of-isotropy ratio ρ(H) ∈ [1, d]
  • Thm 3.8: No universal fixed marginal target is canonical across all downstream geometries
  • Prop 3.9: Oracle marginals don’t identify the view-to-view coupling

Strengths

  • Principled theory identifying exactly why isotropy fails (price-of-isotropy ratio, no universal marginal)
  • Definitive MLP-HJEPA ablation isolating symplectic predictor as kNN driver
  • Rich geometric diagnostics far beyond accuracy numbers (eigenspectra, participation ratios, effective rank vs. accuracy)
  • Elegant structural guarantees proved rigorously in appendix

Limitations

  • Small-scale only (CIFAR-100, ImageNet-100, ResNet-18)
  • Single baseline (SIGReg); no I-JEPA, DINO, or VICReg comparison
  • Headless protocol far from practical SSL recipes
  • Oracle theory → learned method gap not formally bridged

Open Questions

  • Does the symplectic advantage survive at ImageNet-1K scale with ViT-B/L, projection heads, and EMA teachers?
  • Can the learned potential Vφ be analyzed to recover information about the implicit downstream geometry H?
  • Is the phase-space formulation compatible with multi-crop augmentation, or does it require exactly two views?
  • Does symplectic structure provide robustness benefits (adversarial, distribution shift) beyond clean accuracy?
  • MLP ablation shows symplecticity matters for kNN but not linear probe — does it improve local but not global feature geometry?
