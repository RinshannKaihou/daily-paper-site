> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示GRPO等方法因隐式最小化反向KL散度而导致模式坍塌，提出DMPO算法通过组内MSE分布匹配实现前向KL近似，在NP-hard组合优化任务上取得9%–12%的相对提升，并泛化到数学推理（+2.0%）和域外任务（+2.3%）。

> **TL;DR:** DMPO diagnoses GRPO's mode collapse as a consequence of reverse KL's mode-seeking behavior, then solves it via a group-level MSE distribution-matching term that approximates forward KL. Evaluated on a new multimodal NP-hard benchmark (MM-NP-Bench), DMPO achieves 9–12% relative gains on optimization, +2.0% on math reasoning, and +2.3% on out-of-domain tasks.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示模式坍塌的理论根源：** 证明了GRPO等策略梯度方法等价于最小化反向KL散度 $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(\pi_\theta \| p^*)$，在 $\alpha \to 0$ 的极限下目标分布退化为最高奖励轨迹的Dirac delta，导致mode-seeking行为。
- **提出DMPO算法：** 通过构建组内Boltzmann目标分布 $p(o_i|\mathcal{O}) \propto \exp(r(o_i)/\alpha)$，再用MSE损失将策略的组内分布 $q_\theta$ 对齐到目标分布，实现mode-covering。仅需在GRPO目标上增加一个正则项 $\lambda \mathcal{L}_{DM}$，计算开销几乎可忽略。
- **构建MM-NP-Bench多模态基准：** 将NP-Bench扩展到视觉语言模型，涵盖10个NP-hard任务（TSP、顶点覆盖、图着色等），提供参数化生成器、规则验证器和启发式求解器，双指标评估（SR+QR）使模式坍塌可观测。
- **全面的实验验证：** 对比7个强基线（GRPO、GSPO、GPG、ClipCov、FlowRL、GRPOPass@K等），在文本/多模态优化、数学推理、域外迁移三类任务上均取得一致改进。

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretical diagnosis of mode collapse:** Proves that standard policy gradient methods (GRPO, PPO, etc.) implicitly minimize reverse KL $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(\pi_\theta \| p^*)$, which in the $\alpha \to 0$ limit becomes a Dirac delta on the highest-reward trajectory, explaining why policies collapse to a single solution.
- **DMPO algorithm:** A simple add-on to GRPO that constructs a group-level Boltzmann target distribution proportional to rewards, then aligns the policy's length-normalized empirical distribution via MSE loss. Provides three theoretical guarantees: (1) local mode-covering (Prop. 3.1), (2) convergence to proportional sampling (Prop. 3.2), and (3) smooth exploration-exploitation interpolation via $\lambda$ (Prop. 3.3).
- **MM-NP-Bench:** A multimodal extension of NP-Bench with 10 NP-hard tasks across 5 categories (Path, Covering, Partition, Substructure, Constraints), featuring parametric generators, $O(E)$ or $O(V^2)$ rule-based verifiers, and heuristic solvers for Quality Ratio computation.
- **Comprehensive evaluation:** Outperforms 7 strong baselines across optimization (+9–12%), mathematical reasoning (+2.0%), and out-of-domain transfer (+2.3%), with evidence that diversity-preserving training generalizes.

## Methodology

DMPO builds on GRPO's group-based sampling framework. The key insight is that forward KL $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p^* \| \pi_\theta)$ provides mode-covering behavior but is intractable (requires sampling from $p^*$), while reverse KL $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(\pi_\theta \| p^*)$ is tractable but mode-seeking.

**Group-level approximation makes forward KL tractable:** For each prompt, sample $G=8$ trajectories from $\pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}}$. Define a group-conditional Boltzmann target $p(o_i|\mathcal{O}) = \exp(r(o_i)/\alpha) / \sum_j \exp(r(o_j)/\alpha)$ (Eq. 5). Define the policy's group distribution $q_\theta(o_i|\mathcal{O}) = \exp(\varphi(o_i)) / \sum_j \exp(\varphi(o_j))$ using length-normalized log-likelihood (Eq. 6). Minimize MSE between $p$ and $q_\theta$ (Eq. 7), chosen over KL for numerical stability (bounded gradients).

**Unified objective:** $\mathcal{L}_{DMPO}(\theta) = \mathcal{L}_{GRPO}(\theta) + \lambda \mathcal{L}_{DM}(\theta)$ (Eq. 9), where $\lambda$ interpolates between pure exploitation ($\lambda \to 0$) and pure distribution matching ($\lambda \to \infty$). Optimal settings: $\lambda = 2.0$, $\alpha = 1/15$.

**Implementation details:** Uses Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct (multimodal), Qwen2.5-Math-7B (math), and Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct-1M (text optimization) as base models. Training: group size $G=8$, LR $1 \times 10^{-6}$ with cosine decay, 250 iterations, $\epsilon = 0.2$, $\beta_{KL} = 0.0$. Reward: $R(o) = r_{fmt}(o) + r_{opt}(o)$, where $r_{opt}$ uses Quality Ratio (not binary validity) to provide fine-grained optimization signal.

## Results & Highlights

- **Text-based NP-Bench:** DMPO 43.9% QR vs. GRPO 40.1% (+9% relative), with particularly strong gains on Schedule tasks (60.3% vs. GPG's 28.5% QR).
- **Multimodal MM-NP-Bench:** DMPO 43.1% QR vs. GRPO 38.4% (+12% relative), also highest SR (61.9% vs. 55.7%). Gains across all task categories: +10.3% QR on Constraint, +5.7% on Partition.
- **Mathematical reasoning (6 benchmarks):** DMPO 44.5% avg vs. GRPO 42.5% (+2.0%), with +2.4% on AIME 2024 and +1.5% on OlympiadBench.
- **Out-of-domain transfer (7 visual reasoning benchmarks):** DMPO trained on MM-NP-Bench + MM-16K achieves 39.8% avg (+5.0% over backbone, +0.5% over GRPO), demonstrating that optimization training complements general reasoning.
- **Rollout scaling:** With $k=16$ samples, DMPO's QR reaches 58.3% vs. GRPO's 51.9%, confirming DMPO maintains diverse high-quality modes rather than collapsing to one.
- **Computational efficiency:** DMPO adds negligible overhead (125.2s/step vs. GRPO's 109.2s/step), with comparable MFU.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The reverse-vs-forward KL analysis for RLVR mode collapse is well-framed, though the connection is established in classic RL literature (Murphy 2012, Levine 2018). The practical contribution is making forward KL tractable via group-level MSE approximation—a clean, simple idea well-executed.
- **Rigor:** The NP-hard testbed is an excellent choice for isolating exploration. The dual SR/QR metric elegantly makes mode collapse observable. Three formal propositions with proofs add rigor. However, math reasoning improvements (+2.0%) are modest and no error bars or statistical significance tests are reported.
- **Reproducibility:** Full hyperparameters are provided, code is open-sourced. The method is simple enough to implement in a few lines on top of GRPO. The MM-NP-Bench infrastructure (generators, verifiers, solvers) is a valuable community resource.
- **Significance:** Addressing mode collapse in GRPO is timely and practically important given GRPO's widespread adoption. The gains on optimization tasks are substantial; the math reasoning gains, while modest, suggest the approach helps broadly.
- **Red flags:** (1) No error bars or multi-seed results reported anywhere. (2) The QR-based reward on NP tasks is inherently smooth, which benefits DMPO; it is less clear how much DMPO helps with sparse binary rewards. (3) The paper acknowledges DMPO struggles in extremely sparse-reward settings where no valid solution appears in the group. (4) Base models are all 7B-scale; scalability to 70B+ is unverified.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical framing connecting mode collapse to reverse KL divergence with formal proofs.
- Elegant algorithm design: a single MSE regularizer added to GRPO, negligible compute overhead.
- MM-NP-Bench is a well-designed, reusable benchmark with complete infrastructure.
- Consistent improvements across text, vision, math, and out-of-domain tasks.
- The ablation showing pure DMPO (without GRPO loss) matches GRPO validates the distribution-matching objective as sufficient on its own.

## Limitations

- No error bars or statistical significance tests—unclear how robust the +2.0% math gains are.
- Only tested on 7B-scale models; scalability to larger models is unknown.
- DMPO fails in extremely sparse-reward settings when no valid solution appears in the sampled group.
- The temperature $\alpha$ requires tuning (optimal $\alpha = 1/15$ is problem-specific).
- QR-based reward on NP tasks is inherently smooth; benefits under truly sparse binary rewards are unclear.

## Relevance

本文与基于RL的LLM推理训练（RLVR）直接相关。DMPO提供了一个简洁的理论框架和即插即用的实现来解决GRPO的模式坍塌问题，对于研究RL4Reasoning、特别是关注推理多样性和泛化能力的工作具有重要参考价值。MM-NP-Bench也可作为评估探索能力的标准化测试平台。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does DMPO scale to 70B+ models on frontier reasoning benchmarks (MATH, GPQA, LiveCodeBench)?
- Can $\lambda$ and $\alpha$ be adaptively scheduled (e.g., annealed) during training rather than fixed?
- How does DMPO interact with replay buffers or off-policy extensions for sparse-reward settings?
- Does DMPO complement or conflict with other diversity techniques (entropy bonuses, pass@k rewards, adaptive temperature)?
- Are the math reasoning gains (+2.0%) statistically significant over multiple seeds and data shuffles?
- Could the group-level distribution matching idea be applied to DPO or other preference optimization methods?
