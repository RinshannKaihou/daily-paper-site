> **一句话总结：** 本文从哈密顿几何角度证明JEPA中各向同性高斯正则化的理论缺陷（固定边缘分布无法适配未知下游几何），提出HamJEPA——用辛leapfrog映射作为跨视角预测器，在无投影头、无EMA教师的最小化协议下，CIFAR-100上kNN@20提升+6.45点、线性探测提升+10.64点，消融实验证实辛结构是邻域语义几何增益的核心来源。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proves that isotropic Gaussian regularization in JEPAs is provably suboptimal for structured downstream geometries (Theorem 3.2: minimax covariance is $(c/d)H^{-1}$, not $(c/\text{tr}(H))I$; Theorem 3.4: price-of-isotropy ratio $\rho(H)$ ranges up to $d$). More fundamentally, Theorem 3.8 shows no geometry-independent fixed marginal target is canonical, and Proposition 3.9 proves oracle marginals don't identify the view-to-view coupling. HamJEPA encodes views as phase-space states $(q,p)$ and uses a learned Hamiltonian leapfrog map for cross-view prediction, with non-isotropic scale budgets, projected log-det floors, and participation-ratio constraints to prevent collapse. In a deliberately headless protocol (no projection head, no EMA teacher, identity projector), HamJEPA beats SIGReg on CIFAR-100 by +6.45 kNN@20 and +10.64 linear-probe at 80 epochs, and on ImageNet-100 by +4.82 kNN@20 and +7.52 linear-probe at 45 epochs. A matched MLP predictor ablation (MLP-HJEPA) shows the phase-space encoder and regularizers explain most of the linear-probe gain, but the symplectic predictor is specifically responsible for the neighborhood-geometry (kNN) improvement.

## 核心贡献

- **最小最大化协方差理论**：证明在已知下游几何 $H \succ 0$ 和能量预算 $\text{tr}(H\Sigma)=c$ 下，最优协方差为 $(c/d)H^{-1}$，而非各向同性 $(c/\text{tr}(H))I$；各向同性的代价比 $\rho(H) = d\lambda_{\max}(H)/\text{tr}(H)$ 可达上界 $d$。
- **无通用固定边缘分布**：定理3.8证明对于任意固定协方差形状 $M$，存在下游几何 $H$ 使其遗憾比达到最大值 $d$；即不存在对所有结构化几何都最优的通用边缘分布。
- **边缘分布不决定预测耦合**：命题3.9证明即使知道精确的边缘协方差 $\Sigma$，联合高斯模型中仍存在连续统的不同预测映射 $E[Z_b|Z_a] = C\Sigma^{-1}Z_a$（由交叉协方差 $C$ 参数化），因此边缘匹配无法确定JEPA的预测问题。
- **HamJEPA框架**：将结构偏差置于跨视角预测器而非编码器边缘——用可分离哈密顿量 $H_\phi(q,p) = \frac{1}{2}\|p\|^2 + V_\phi(q)$ 和leapfrog积分器构建辛（symplectic）、保体积、时间可逆的预测映射，同时用非各向同性的尺度预算、投影log-det下界和参与率下界防止坍塌。
- **严格的消融实验**：MLP-HJEPA保留全部相位空间编码器与正则化，仅替换辛预测器为残差MLP——线性探测接近HamJEPA（42.77 vs 44.59），但kNN@20几乎不优于SIGReg（28.30 vs 27.98），证实辛结构是邻域几何增益的核心驱动力。

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretical foundations for marginal geometry in JEPAs**: Proves the minimax-optimal covariance under structured geometry $H \succ 0$ is $(c/d)H^{-1}$ (Theorem 3.2), the maximum-entropy distribution is $\mathcal{N}(0, (c/d)H^{-1})$ (Theorem 3.3), and Euclidean isotropy incurs a price-of-isotropy ratio $\rho(H) \in [1, d]$ (Theorem 3.4).
- **No universal marginal target (Theorem 3.8)**: For any fixed covariance shape $M \succ 0$ independent of downstream geometry, the regret $d \lambda_{\max}(H^{1/2}MH^{1/2})/\text{tr}(HM)$ can reach $d$ (the ambient dimension), so no geometry-independent marginal is canonical.
- **Marginals don't identify coupling (Proposition 3.9)**: Even with oracle marginal covariance $\Sigma$, the predictive map $E[Z_b|Z_a]$ is determined by the cross-covariance $C$, which is not pinned down by the marginals. This motivates placing structural bias in the coupling rather than the marginal.
- **HamJEPA architecture**: Phase-space encoder outputs $(q, p) \in \mathbb{R}^{2d_0}$; learned separable Hamiltonian with leapfrog integrator gives a symplectic, volume-preserving ($\det = 1$), time-reversible predictor; anti-collapse via fixed-unit scale budget, projected log-det floor, and participation-ratio floor — all non-isotropic.
- **Definitive predictor-family ablation**: MLP-HJEPA (same encoder, same regularizers, non-symplectic MLP predictor) shows that the phase-space parameterization explains most linear-probe gains (+8.82 over SIGReg), while the symplectic predictor specifically drives kNN neighborhood quality (+6.13 over MLP-HJEPA).

## Methodology

### Phase-space encoder
The encoder $E_\theta: \mathcal{X} \to \mathbb{R}^{2d_0}$ outputs a phase-space state $s = (q, p)$ where $q \in \mathbb{R}^{d_0}$ is the content coordinate and $p \in \mathbb{R}^{d_0}$ is the auxiliary momentum coordinate. On CIFAR-100, the encoder uses ResNet stage-3 (layer3) features with a headless token regime and identity projector — deliberately minimal to isolate geometric effects.

### Symplectic predictor via leapfrog
A separable Hamiltonian $H_\phi(q,p) = T(p) + V_\phi(q)$ with fixed kinetic energy $T(p) = \frac{1}{2}\|p\|^2$ and learnable potential $V_\phi(q)$. The leapfrog (velocity Verlet) integrator with $K$ steps and step size $\Delta t$ produces the predictor map $\Phi_\phi^{(K)}$. Key properties:
- **Symplectic**: $D\Phi^{(K)}_\phi(s)^\top J D\Phi^{(K)}_\phi(s) = J$ (Theorem A.4)
- **Volume-preserving**: $\det D\Phi^{(K)}_\phi(s) = 1$
- **Time-reversible**: $\Psi^{-1}_{\phi,\Delta t} = \Psi_{\phi,-\Delta t}$ (Corollary A.5)
- **Singular values in reciprocal pairs** (Lemma A.7)

### Anti-collapse regularizers (non-isotropic)
1. **Scale budget**: $L_{\text{budget}} = (\mathbb{E}\|q\|^2/d_0 - \alpha_q)^2 + (\mathbb{E}\|p\|^2/d_0 - \alpha_p)^2$ — fixed units, no Gaussianity
2. **Projected log-det floor**: Random projection $R \in \mathbb{R}^{d_0 \times k}$ ($k \ll d_0$), ridge-regularized projected covariance $\Sigma_Y$, hinge penalty on $\frac{1}{k}\log\det(\Sigma_Y)$ below threshold $\tau$
3. **Participation ratio floor**: $\text{PR}(\Sigma_Y) = (\sum \lambda_i)^2 / \sum \lambda_i^2 \geq r_0$, prevents "one-spike" spectra

### Matching loss
$L_{\text{pred}} = \mathbb{E}[\|\Pi_m(\hat{s}_b) - \text{sg}(\Pi_m(s_b))\|^2]$ where matching mode $m \in \{q, qp\}$ (q-matching on CIFAR-100, qp-matching on ImageNet-100 for stability). Bidirectional prediction enforces forward and backward consistency.

### Training protocol
Deliberately minimal: no projection head, no EMA teacher, no local crops, identity projector, two global views, shared backbone/augmentations/optimizer/batch size. ResNet-18 backbone. This isolates the effect of the predictive geometry.

## Results & Highlights

### CIFAR-100 (30 epochs, 3 seeds)
| Method | kNN@20 (%) | Linear probe (%) |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| SIGReg | 26.56 ± 0.18 | 30.43 ± 0.30 |
| HamJEPA-q | 31.45 ± 0.27 | 33.95 ± 0.24 |
| HamJEPA-(q,p) | 30.88 ± 0.39 | **34.18 ± 0.17** |

### CIFAR-100 (80 epochs, predictor ablation)
| Method | kNN@20 (%) | Linear (%) |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| SIGReg | 27.98 | 33.95 |
| MLP-HJEPA-(q,p) | 28.15 | 42.77 |
| **HamJEPA-q** | **34.43** | **44.59** |

The MLP ablation is the key result: MLP-HJEPA reaches 42.77% linear probe (+8.82 over SIGReg) but only 28.15% kNN@20 (+0.17). HamJEPA adds +6.13 kNN@20 and +1.82 linear on top of MLP-HJEPA. The symplectic predictor is decisive for non-parametric neighborhood quality.

### ImageNet-100 (45 epochs, single seed)
| Method | kNN@20 (%) | Linear (%) |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| SIGReg-q | 20.10 | 24.40 |
| SIGReg-(q,p) | 20.94 | 25.54 |
| **HamJEPA-q** | **24.92** | **31.92** |
| HamJEPA-p | 24.72 | 32.08 |

1024-dim HamJEPA-q beats 2048-dim SIGReg-(q,p) by +3.98 kNN@20 and +6.38 linear-probe. On ImageNet-100, HamJEPA's effective rank ≈ 95 (vs SIGReg ≈ 38), confirming broader variance distribution.

### Geometry diagnostics
- HamJEPA-q covariance is **higher-rank** than SIGReg (effective rank 125 vs 110 on CIFAR-100, 95 vs 38 on ImageNet-100), ruling out collapse-based explanations.
- MLP-HJEPA has much more concentrated covariance (effective rank ≈ 42 vs symplectic ≈ 129), despite similar linear probe — the symplectic predictor organizes broader content geometry.
- HamJEPA produces strongly positive random-pair cosine similarities coexisting with high-rank centered covariance, indicating a dominant mean direction rather than variance collapse.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The application of Hamiltonian/symplectic geometry to JEPA predictive coupling is genuinely novel. The theoretical results (Theorems 3.2, 3.4, 3.8) provide principled motivation, and the insight that structural bias should live in the coupling rather than the marginal is conceptually clean and actionable.
- **Rigor: High.** The theoretical section is fully proved with detailed appendices. The experimental protocol is carefully controlled — all methods share backbone, augmentations, optimizer, batch size, identity projector, no EMA teacher. The MLP-HJEPA ablation is particularly well-designed: it isolates the symplectic predictor as the only variable.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate-High.** Code is available. The protocol is described in detail. However, some hyperparameters ($\Delta t$, $K$, $\lambda$ values, projection dimension $k$, thresholds $\tau$, $r_0$) need to be extracted from the code/config files.
- **Significance: Moderate-High.** The geometry perspective is important and the gains are substantial in the controlled setting. However, the protocol is deliberately minimal, and it remains unclear how much of the gain survives in a modern SSL recipe with projection heads, EMA teachers, and multi-crop augmentation.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  1. **Scale**: Only CIFAR-100 and ImageNet-100 with ResNet-18. Full ImageNet-1K and larger architectures are untested.
  2. **Single baseline**: SIGReg (LeJEPA) is the only direct baseline. Comparison with I-JEPA, VICReg, DINO, etc. is absent.
  3. **ImageNet-100 is single seed** — the authors acknowledge this. The CIFAR-100 results have 3 seeds, which is better.
  4. **The "headless" protocol is very non-standard**: No projection head, identity projector. This is great for isolation but makes it hard to predict real-world performance.
  5. **Early-epoch regime**: 30–80 epochs on CIFAR-100. The paper argues that longer training plateaus due to CIFAR-100's simplicity, but this means we don't know if the symplectic advantage persists or narrows at convergence on harder datasets.
  6. **Theoretical gap between Section 3 and Section 4**: Section 3 assumes a known $H$, which motivates $(c/d)H^{-1}$. Section 4 then says $H$ is unknown, so we use a learned Hamiltonian. The logical connection is intuitive but not formally bridged — the theory doesn't prove that the learned $V_\phi$ recovers anything about the true $H$.

## Strengths

- Principled theoretical motivation that identifies exactly *why* isotropy is problematic (price-of-isotropy ratio, no universal marginal, marginals don't determine coupling).
- Clean, well-designed ablation (MLP-HJEPA) that conclusively shows the symplectic predictor drives kNN gains, while the phase-space parameterization drives linear-probe gains.
- Detailed geometric diagnostics (eigenspectra, participation ratios, cosine similarity distributions, effective rank vs. accuracy plots) that go far beyond accuracy numbers.
- Intellectual honesty in acknowledging limitations and designing a minimal protocol to isolate the geometric effect.
- The symplectic predictor has elegant structural guarantees (volume preservation, time reversibility, reciprocal singular values) proved rigorously in the appendix.

## Limitations

- Small-scale benchmarks only (CIFAR-100, ImageNet-100 with ResNet-18); scalability to ImageNet-1K with ViT-B/L is unknown.
- Only one direct baseline (SIGReg). No comparison with I-JEPA, data2vec, DINO, or other JEPA variants.
- The minimal protocol (no projection head, no EMA, identity projector) is far from practical SSL recipes — unclear if gains transfer.
- ImageNet-100 results are single-seed.
- The connection between the oracle theory (known $H$) and the practical method (learned $V_\phi$) is intuitive but not formally established.
- Additional hyperparameters (leapfrog steps $K$, step size $\Delta t$, phase-space dimension $d_0$, projection dimension $k$, spectral floors) add complexity.

## Relevance

将微分几何、辛几何与自监督表示学习结合，从理论上揭示各向同性假设的缺陷并提出基于哈密顿力学的跨视角预测框架。对理解SSL中几何结构偏差、构建几何感知的自监督方法具有重要理论价值和实践启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does HamJEPA scale to ImageNet-1K with ViT-B or ViT-L backbones? Does the symplectic advantage survive with projection heads and EMA teachers?
- Can the learned potential $V_\phi$ be analyzed to recover information about the implicit downstream geometry $H$? Does it learn meaningful structure?
- Is the symplectic predictor compatible with multi-view/multi-crop augmentation schemes, or does the phase-space formulation require exactly two views?
- How sensitive are the results to the leapfrog hyperparameters (step size $\Delta t$, number of steps $K$)? Is there an optimal balance between expressiveness and symplecticity preservation?
- Can the Hamiltonian framework be extended to hierarchical/predictive architectures (e.g., patch-level prediction as in I-JEPA)?
- Does the symplectic structure provide robustness benefits (adversarial, distribution shift, or corruption robustness) beyond clean accuracy?
- The MLP ablation shows symplecticity matters for kNN but not linear probe — does this imply the symplectic predictor improves *local* but not *global* feature geometry?
