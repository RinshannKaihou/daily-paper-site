> **一句话总结：** 本文定义并诊断"库漂移"（library drift）这一自进化技能库的静默失效模式——技能无限累积而无生命周期管理会导致检索退化与性能停滞，并提出Ratchet治理方案（结果驱动退役+有界活跃上限+元技能创作先验），在MBPP+ hard-100上将pass@1从0.258提升至0.584（+0.328），经8项消融分解出哪些机制承重。

> **TL;DR:** This paper identifies "library drift" — a silent failure mode in self-evolving LLM skill libraries where unbounded skill accumulation without outcome-driven lifecycle management causes retrieval degradation, false-positive injections, and performance stagnation below the no-skill baseline. It provides a reproducible trigger (ablation A4 with harsh retirement yields −0.019 gain, below the no-skill floor +0.002), trace-level diagnostics (per-skill contribution scores, attribution verdicts, router engagement), and a verified fix called Ratchet (outcome-driven retirement Nmin=100/τ=0.10, bounded active-cap C=50, meta-skill authoring prior) that lifts held-out pass@1 from 0.258 to 0.584 late-window mean (+0.328) on MBPP+ hard-100 over 100 rounds with Claude Opus 4.7. Eight ablations (A1–A8) decompose which governance mechanisms are load-bearing.

## 核心贡献

- 形式化定义**库漂移**（Eq. 1）：当累积技能使期望性能低于无技能基线时即发生漂移；该失效是静默的——终端指标缓慢下降而无显式错误信号
- 给出**可复现触发器**：A1（禁用技能注入）确立无技能地板（+0.002），A4（过早退役，Nmin=20, τ=0）触发主动伤害（−0.019），从两端框定漂移
- 设计**追踪级诊断**：per-skill贡献分数 ĉ(s)、归因裁决（HELPED/HURT/NEUTRAL/INAPPLICABLE）、路由器参与率，使漂移在终端分数下降前可见
- 提出**Ratchet治理方案**：结果驱动退役 + 有界活跃上限(C=50) + 元技能创作先验，在MBPP+ hard-100上将pass@1从0.258提升至0.584（+0.328），并提供非发散性保证（Prop. 1）
- 8项消融（A1–A8）分解承重机制：元技能是最有价值组件（移除损失43%增益），显式去重并非必要

## Key Contributions

- Formal definition of library drift (Eq. 1): a self-evolving skill library drifts when E[pass@1|S_t] < E[p_0] for some t, a silent systemic failure distinct from individual skill errors
- Reproducible triggers bracketing the failure: A1 (no injection) establishes the +0.002 no-skill floor; A4 (harsh retirement, Nmin=20, τ=0) triggers active harm at −0.019, below the no-skill baseline
- Three trace-level diagnostics beyond end-task metrics: per-skill contribution score ĉ(s) = (successes−failures)/trials, LLM Critic attribution verdicts (HELPED/HURT/NEUTRAL/INAPPLICABLE), and router engagement rate
- The Ratchet fix: outcome-driven retirement (Nmin=100, τ=0.10), bounded active-cap (C=50), and meta-skill authoring prior, with a non-divergence guarantee (Proposition 1)
- Eight ablations (A1–A8) decomposing which mechanisms are load-bearing: the meta-skill is the single most valuable component (removing it costs 43% of the Default gain), while explicit dedup (canonicalisation, cover-guard) is subsumed by the meta-skill at this scale

## Methodology

Ratchet is a single-agent loop where a frozen LLM (Claude Opus 4.7 via Amazon Bedrock) writes, retrieves, curates, and retires its own natural-language skills. The loop (Figure 1) has three bands: (1) **Inference** — each task flows Router → Solver → Grader → Capsule; the Router picks 0 or 1 skill via tf-idf∪embedding similarity plus an LLM gate that can decline injection; the Solver is a single direct LLM call with no execution feedback, no self-refinement, no tool use (isolating the skill library's contribution); the Grader produces a pass/fail outcome. (2) **Memory** — a persistent append-only Skill Bank (ACTIVE+DEPRECATED, never deletes), a Meta-Skill Bank (one ACTIVE authoring prior), and an Evidence Log (capsules + verdicts). (3) **Reflection** — the Critic LLM produces attribution verdicts on failure capsules; the Synthesizer clusters ≥3 failures sharing a canonical pattern into new skills; the Curator retires skills with ĉ(s) ≤ −τ after Nmin trials and enforces the cap C; the Meta-Synth rewrites the meta-skill every N rounds. Embeddings use Cohere embed-v4.

The three governance mechanisms: (1) **Outcome-driven retirement** (Sec. 5.1): retire when n(s) ≥ Nmin=100 and ĉ(s) ≤ −τ=0.10; Hoeffding ε≈0.20 at Nmin=100. (2) **Bounded active-cap** (Sec. 5.2): hard cap C=50 forces eviction of lowest-ĉ skill on overflow; Proposition 1 bounds expected eval pass@1 drift below the no-skill floor by at most τ+ε+Cδ. (3) **Meta-skill authoring prior** (Sec. 5.3): constrains the Synthesizer to produce stylistically consistent skills, reducing harmful/redundant skill creation at the source.

Evaluation protocol: MBPP+ hard-100 — from the 378-task MBPP+ test split, discard tasks Claude Opus 4.7 solves on all 5 baseline seeds (~273 tasks), randomly sample 100 (60 train/40 eval, fixed seed). Each run is 100 rounds; results are mean±std over 3 seeds. Gain = mean(last 10) − mean(first 10) of held-out pass@1.

## Results & Highlights

- **Default (full Ratchet governance) main result (Table 1).** Pass@1 rises from 0.258±0.047 baseline to 0.584 late-window mean (peak 0.658±0.042), a rolling gain of +0.328±0.018 on MBPP+ hard-100 over 100 rounds — more than doubling performance on genuinely difficult tasks. Router engagement is 73%, with 50 active and 89 retired skills at round 100.
- **A1 (no injection) establishes the no-skill floor.** +0.002±0.005 gain — skill creation without injection produces no gain; the synthesis pipeline runs but no skill reaches the solver (0 critic calls, 0% router engagement).
- **A4 (harsh retirement) triggers active harm.** Lowering Nmin from 100 to 20 and τ to 0.0 yields −0.019±0.010, below the no-skill floor; the bank collapses to 2 active skills, router engagement drops to 19%, and the effect is consistent across all 3 seeds (−0.005, −0.027, −0.025). At Nmin=20 the Hoeffding deviation ε≈0.44, so skills with true contribution c∈[−0.44,0] are prematurely retired.
- **A3 (no meta-skill) is the most valuable single component.** Removing the meta-skill authoring prior yields +0.187 (57% of Default gain), costing 43% of the gain (−0.141) — making it the single most valuable component, more impactful than the LLM gate (A2: +0.077, 24% of Default).
- **Explicit dedup is not necessary (A5, A6).** A5 (no canonicalisation) reaches +0.374 and A6 (no cover-guard) +0.363, both slightly exceeding the Default +0.328 (within ±2σ at n=3) — the meta-skill's authoring guidance enforces enough stylistic consistency that explicit dedup's false positives discard more useful skills than duplicates they prevent.
- **Diagnostics precede end-task decline.** In A4, the bank collapsed to 2 active skills by round 30 (visible in router engagement dropping to 18.9%), but pass@1 showed only gradual decline because the router adaptively chose NONE — without per-skill diagnostics, drift would have been invisible until much later.

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a valuable diagnostic contribution, but its empirical scope is narrow and the generalization claims outpace the evidence. First, the entire evaluation rests on a **single benchmark (MBPP+ hard-100) and a single LLM (Claude Opus 4.7)** — the 100 tasks are drawn from MBPP+ after discarding ~273 tasks the model already solves, leaving a 100-task slice of genuinely difficult coding problems; the authors themselves note the failure mode "applies more acutely to multi-step agents," yet no multi-step or non-code evaluation is performed, so the claim that library drift is "a first-class failure category" is argued rather than demonstrated across domains. Second, the **statistical power is thin**: 3 seeds per condition on 40 eval tasks yields high variance (e.g., A7 cap=100 reports +0.317±0.110, a standard deviation one-third of the effect), and several ablation comparisons (A5 vs Default, A6 vs Default) are explicitly "within ±2σ at n=3," so the conclusion that dedup is unnecessary is underpowered. Third, the **Proposition 1 non-divergence bound** (drift bounded by τ+ε+Cδ) is a stylized guarantee whose constants (δ per-skill failure probability, Hoeffding ε) are not validated against the empirical drift magnitudes — it is unclear whether the bound is tight or vacuous in practice. Fourth, the **Default's +0.328 gain is measured as mean(last 10)−mean(first 10)**, a within-run rolling gain rather than a fixed-endpoint comparison; this metric can inflate apparent improvement if early rounds are cold-start depressed, and the paper does not report a round-100 vs round-0 endpoint delta for direct comparison. Fifth, the paper **depends on proprietary models** (Claude Opus 4.7, Cohere embed-v4 via Bedrock) with no open-weight reproduction, so the results are not independently reproducible without API access and cost. Finally, the wall-time and cost numbers (6.5h, ~10k calls) are reported only for the Default vs A1, not across all ablations, making the cost-benefit analysis of governance incomplete.

## Strengths

- The library-drift concept is well-motivated and operationally defined (Eq. 1), filling a gap left by prior self-evolving systems (Voyager, ExpeL, AutoManual) that lack outcome-driven retirement
- The reproducible triggers (A1 floor, A4 active harm) are minimal and transferable — any system can run these two ablations to check for drift
- Trace-level diagnostics (contribution scores, attribution verdicts, router engagement) genuinely precede end-task decline, as shown in the A4 round-30 bank collapse
- The Ratchet recipe is minimal (three mechanisms) and the ablations honestly identify which are load-bearing vs subsumed
- The "governance is not uniformly beneficial" finding (A4) is an important cautionary result for practitioners

## Limitations

- Single benchmark (MBPP+ hard-100), single LLM (Claude Opus 4.7), single-call code tasks only; no multi-step agent or non-code evaluation
- 3 seeds on 40 eval tasks; several ablation comparisons (A5/A6 vs Default) are within ±2σ, underpowered
- Proposition 1's non-divergence bound is stylized; constants not validated against empirical drift
- Gain metric (mean last 10 − mean first 10) is a rolling within-run delta, not a fixed-endpoint comparison
- Depends on proprietary models (Claude Opus 4.7, Cohere embed-v4); no open-weight reproduction
- Cost/wall-time reported only for Default vs A1, not across all ablations

## Relevance

直接映射到"Self-Evolving Agents & Automated Research"与"LLM Training Stability & Observability"两个方向：它识别并诊断了自进化智能体技能库的静默失效模式（库漂移），给出了追踪级可观测性工具（贡献分数、归因裁决、路由参与率）与最小治理方案，对构建可信赖的自动化研究智能体具有直接工程价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does library drift manifest in multi-step agent settings (tool-use, planning) where a single misleading injection compounds downstream, as the authors speculate?
- How do the diagnostics and Ratchet recipe transfer to non-code domains (e.g., rule-based systems, workflow memories, episodic stores) the authors claim are susceptible?
- Is the Proposition 1 non-divergence bound tight in practice, or can empirical drift exceed τ+ε+Cδ under adversarial skill distributions?
- Would the results hold with open-weight LLMs (Llama, Qwen) instead of Claude Opus 4.7, or is the meta-skill's effectiveness tied to a specific model's instruction-following?
- Can the trace-level diagnostics be automated into a drift detector that triggers governance interventions without human inspection?
