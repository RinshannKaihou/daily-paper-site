> **一句话总结：** 本文针对允许任意自适应轮次和本地样本复用的公开记录联邦学习协议，利用隐私-信息收缩不等式和联邦 van Trees 方法，建立了满足客户端样本级 zCDP 约束的参数估计的一般性极小化下界，并在均值估计、线性回归和非参数回归中给出了具体的速率下界。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proves a general minimax lower bound (a federated van Trees inequality) for parameter estimation under squared ℓ₂ loss in differentially private federated learning with arbitrary public-transcript interactions, arbitrary adaptive rounds, and local sample reuse. The key technical tool is a privacy-information contraction inequality for complete transcripts that decomposes Fisher information across clients despite interaction.

## 核心贡献

- 建立了最一般交互模型下的联邦 DP 参数估计下界：允许任意轮次的自适应通信、客户端本地样本跨轮次复用，隐私预算按总 zCDP 约束计算而非按轮分配
- 提出了完整公开记录的 Fisher 信息收缩不等式（Lemma 4.2），证明了即使存在交互和自适应，完整记录的 Fisher 信息仍可分解为客户端逐项求和
- 推导出联邦 van Trees 不等式（Theorem 3.1），给出了任意 zCDP 联邦估计器的极小化风险下界，形式为客户端信息量的调和式求和
- 揭示了客户端信息项的直觉：每个客户端的有效信息量是其隐私受限信息（ρ_l n_l² ‖I_x‖_op）与非隐私 Fisher 信息（n_l Tr I_x）的较小值
- 将通用下界应用于均值估计、线性回归和非参数回归三类经典问题，恢复了已知的最优速率并涵盖中心 DP 和局部 DP 作为特例

## Key Contributions

- Establishes the first general lower bound for federated DP estimation under the most general interactive protocol model — arbitrary adaptive rounds, local sample reuse, no per-round budget splitting
- Proves a transcript Fisher contraction lemma (Lemma 4.2): Tr I_G(θ) ≤ Σ_l [(e^{2ρ_l} − 1) n_l² ‖I_x(θ)‖_op ∧ n_l Tr I_x(θ)], showing that complete-transcript Fisher information decomposes clientwise despite full interaction
- Derives a federated van Trees inequality (Theorem 3.1): the Bayesian minimax risk is lower-bounded by c · [∫ Θ p² / (Σ_l [ρ_l n_l² ‖I_x‖_op ∧ n_l Tr I_x]) dπ(θ) + Tr J_π]⁻¹
- Recovers central DP (m = 1) and local DP (n_l = 1 ∀l) as special cases, and matches heterogeneous federated rates from prior work
- Provides concrete rate lower bounds for Gaussian mean estimation, linear regression, and nonparametric regression over Sobolev balls

## Methodology

The paper considers a federated protocol with m clients, where client l holds n_l i.i.d. samples from P_θ. The protocol proceeds in T rounds of adaptive public communication: at round t, each client applies a local randomized mechanism to its data and the public history, producing a public message. The complete ordered transcript G = G_{≤T} must satisfy a clientwise sample-level zCDP guarantee — for each client l, G is ρ_l-zCDP as a mechanism of X^{(l)} conditional on other clients' data.

The proof strategy has three key steps:

1. **Likelihood factorization and posterior independence**: Because the transcript likelihood factorizes across clients (Eq. 4), the local datasets are conditionally independent given G (Eq. 6). This is unique to public-transcript protocols and does not hold for general estimation.

2. **Fisher information decomposition**: Using conditional independence, the score projection identity gives Tr I_G(θ) = Σ_l E_θ ‖E_θ[S_l | G]‖², with zero cross-client covariance terms (Eq. 7).

3. **Per-client Fisher contraction**: Each term E_θ‖E_θ[S_l | G]‖² is bounded in two ways — by the ordinary L² contraction (≤ n_l Tr I_x) and by conditioning on X^{(-l)} and applying the single-client zCDP Fisher contraction lemma from Cai and Li (Lemma 4.1, ≤ (e^{2ρ_l} − 1)n_l² ‖I_x‖_op). Taking the minimum of both bounds gives the final contraction.

The van Trees inequality then converts this Fisher information bound into a minimax risk lower bound. Applications plug in the Fisher information for each specific model.

## Results & Highlights

- **Gaussian mean estimation**: Lower bound ∼ σ² [Σ_l (d/n_l + d²/(ρ_l n_l²))⁻¹]⁻¹, matching known optimal rates for heterogeneous federated mean estimation
- **Linear regression**: Same rate structure as mean estimation (replacing σ² with noise variance scaled by design conditioning), confirming that the design covariance enters only through constants
- **Nonparametric regression (Sobolev α)**: Lower bound exhibits the classical bias-variance tradeoff with two components — the non-private rate (σ²/(mn))^{2α/(2α+d)} and the privacy-driven rate (σ²/(ρmn²))^{α/(α+d)}, recovering the heterogeneous federated nonparametric rate from Cai et al. (2024)
- **Special cases**: When m = 1, reduces to the central DP van Trees bound of Cai and Li (2025); when n_l = 1 for all l, recovers local DP scaling in the high-privacy regime
- Pure ϵ-DP lower bounds follow via the conversion ρ = ϵ²/2

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The transcript Fisher contraction lemma (Lemma 4.2) is the key innovation — it shows that despite arbitrary interaction and adaptivity, the complete transcript's Fisher information decomposes as if each client acted independently. This is surprising and technically non-trivial.
- **Rigor**: Very strong. The proof follows a clean three-step architecture (factorization → decomposition → contraction), and each step is carefully justified. The posterior independence property (Eq. 6) is elegantly derived.
- **Significance**: Important for the DP federated learning theory community. Prior lower bounds were either problem-specific or restricted to non-interactive/one-pass protocols. This work provides a unified framework.
- **Reproducibility**: Good for theory. All key lemmas are stated precisely and proved. The applications are straightforward instantiations of the main theorem.
- **Red flags**: The restriction to zCDP (rather than (ε,δ)-DP) is a limitation, though conversion is possible with some loss. The squared ℓ₂ loss restriction means the framework doesn't directly apply to classification or testing. No experimental validation, which is expected for a lower-bound paper. Single-author status is unusual for this level of technical contribution.

## Strengths

- Handles the most general interactive federated model — no restrictions on number of rounds, adaptivity, or sample reuse
- Clean and modular proof architecture that separates information-theoretic and privacy arguments
- The posterior independence insight is elegant and may find applications beyond this paper
- Broad applicability demonstrated through three canonical problems
- Unifies central DP, local DP, and federated DP lower bounds in a single framework
- The clientwise information expression provides clear intuition about how heterogeneity affects the minimax rate

## Limitations

- Restricted to squared ℓ₂ loss — extension to other loss functions (ℓ₁, ℓ_∞, classification error) is not discussed
- Focused on zCDP rather than the more standard (ε,δ)-DP; the conversion ρ ≈ ϵ²/2 may not give tight bounds for approximate DP
- The lower bounds may not be tight for all instantiations — matching upper bounds are not constructed
- Assumes i.i.d. local samples from a common parametric family; heterogeneous distributions across clients are not considered
- Does not address computational constraints or communication costs beyond the privacy budget
- No discussion of whether the public-transcript assumption is necessary or if the results extend to private-channel protocols

## Relevance

本文建立了差分隐私联邦学习在任意公开交互协议下的通用极小化下界框架，核心技术——完整记录的 Fisher 信息收缩——为理解隐私约束下分布式学习的统计极限提供了统一工具，对信息论、差分隐私和联邦学习交叉方向的理论研究具有基础性意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the transcript Fisher contraction be extended to approximate (ε,δ)-DP without going through zCDP conversion, potentially yielding tighter bounds?
- What are the matching upper bounds for linear regression and nonparametric regression in the general interactive federated setting — do existing algorithms achieve these rates?
- How do the lower bounds change under user-level DP (protecting all samples per user) rather than sample-level DP?
- Does the posterior independence property (Eq. 6) have implications for federated learning algorithm design, e.g., for constructing optimal estimators?
- Can the framework accommodate heterogeneous local distributions (different P_θ^{(l)} per client) or corrupted clients?
