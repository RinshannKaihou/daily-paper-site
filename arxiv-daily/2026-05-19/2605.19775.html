Understanding Inference Scaling for LLMs:
Bottlenecks, Trade-offs, and Performance Principles

2605.19775 arxiv.org/abs/2605.19775 →
Key Finding The Capacity Trap: long CoT reasoning causes KV-cache to saturate HBM, triggering preemption and throughput collapse — data parallelism cannot rescue it.
Key Metric TP achieves 6.15× speedup vs DP's 4.9× at 32B; hybrid DP=4+TP=2 yields 484s vs 686s pure TP.
Why This Matters Directly actionable parallelism decision framework for deploying reasoning models: model size → DP / TP / PP choice for inference cluster sizing.
Critical Edge Single engine (vLLM) on H200 only; no quantization evaluated; no multi-node beyond 8 GPUs; crossover points shift with next-gen hardware.

The Capacity Trap

Reasoning workloads generate long Chain-of-Thought sequences, causing KV-cache to grow linearly and saturate HBM. Once full, the scheduler must preempt running requests and recompute — throughput collapses instead of scaling with concurrency.

DP → TP Crossover at ~32B

DP replicates weights per GPU, leaving less room for KV-cache. TP shards the model, freeing ~133 GB per GPU vs ~77 GB under DP for the 32B model.

Methodology

  • Engine: vLLM
  • Hardware: NVIDIA H200 (141 GB HBM), 1-8 GPUs
  • Data: Meta Natural Reasoning (1.15M questions, 100K sample)
  • Strategies: DP, TP, PP, and hybrids on fixed 8-GPU budget
  • Batch: 500 – 10K sequences
  • Telemetry: vLLM metrics + NVIDIA-SMI
MLA insight: Despite 10× more params than 70B, DeepSeek-R1's KV-cache is moderate thanks to Multi-Head Latent Attention compression.

Individual observations (KV saturation, TP vs DP) are known, but the systematic 8B–671B cross-model characterization with exact crossover quantification is novel. Experiments are well-controlled with real reasoning data.

Only vLLM tested (Sarathi/Orca may shift results). Hardware-specific to H200's 141 GB. Discussion on CXL/HBF is speculative without experiments.

Strengths

  • Systematic 8B – 671B evaluation with consistent hardware
  • Actionable crossover points and optimal configs per architecture
  • Multi-dimensional telemetry for causal bottleneck attribution
  • Prefill/decode divergence motivates disaggregated inference

Limitations

  • Single inference engine (vLLM) — chunked-prefill schedulers may shift results
  • H200-specific; B200's 192 GB HBM would move the capacity trap thresholds
  • No quantization (FP8/INT4) — would change KV-cache sizing significantly
  • Max 8 GPUs — frontier models in production need multi-node serving
  • No streaming/interactive SLAs or speculative decoding evaluation
  • Hardware disaggregation discussion lacks experimental backing

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How do quantization techniques (FP8, INT4 KV-cache) shift the capacity trap thresholds and DP-to-TP crossover points?
  2. Would a KV-cache-aware admission controller estimating future KV growth at request arrival time eliminate the reasoning cliff?
  3. How do findings generalize to multi-node inference (64+ GPUs) where inter-node NVLink bandwidth dominates?
  4. Can speculative decoding with small draft models mitigate the bandwidth-bound decode phase?
  5. How does MLA compression compare to GQA under identical serving conditions?
