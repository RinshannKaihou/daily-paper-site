K-Quantization and its Impact on Output Performance

A systematic evaluation of k-quantization (2–6 bit) across eight LLMs on MMLU-Pro, CRUXEval, and MuSR benchmarks.

Key Finding
Larger models are more resilient to aggressive quantization; 7–9B models strike the optimal efficiency-performance balance.
Key Metric
8 LLMs × 5 quantization levels (2–6 bit) across 3 benchmark suites under GGUF k-quantization.
Why This Matters
Directly guides deployment: tells you which quantization level preserves capability for your model size and task.
Critical Edge
GGUF-only; may not generalize to GPTQ/AWQ. Specific 8 models not disclosed in abstract.
TL;DR: Systematic benchmarking reveals that higher precision (Q8_0) yields the best performance with diminishing returns, while aggressive 2-bit quantization (Q2_K) retains acceptable accuracy in most cases but causes substantial drops in some models. Model resilience to quantization scales with model size.

Key Contributions

  • Systematic benchmarking of eight LLMs across k-quantization levels from 2 to 6 bits
  • Evaluation on three diverse tasks: MMLU-Pro (knowledge & reasoning), CRUXEval (code comprehension), MuSR (reading comprehension)
  • Empirical evidence that higher precision (e.g., Q8_0) yields improved performance with diminishing returns
  • Finding that aggressive quantization (e.g., Q2_K) retains acceptable accuracy in most cases but causes substantial drops in some models
  • Demonstrating that model resilience to quantization scales with model size, while 7–9B parameter models offer an optimal efficiency-performance trade-off

Results & Highlights

  • Higher precision (Q8_0) consistently yields the best performance, though with diminishing returns compared to mid-range quantization
  • Aggressive 2-bit quantization (Q2_K) retains acceptable accuracy for most models but causes substantial performance drops in some cases
  • Larger models demonstrate greater resilience to aggressive quantization, maintaining more of their baseline performance
  • Mid-sized models (7–9B parameters) strike an optimal balance between computational efficiency and retained accuracy
  • Performance impact varies across both models and tasks, indicating that quantization effects are not uniform

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Comprehensive sweep across 5 quantization levels, not just 4-bit vs 8-bit
  • Diverse benchmark selection covering knowledge, code, and reading
  • Direct practical relevance for LLM deployment decisions

Limitations

  • Model selection criteria unknown from abstract
  • GGUF-only; may not generalize to GPTQ, AWQ, QuIP
  • Quantization–task interaction needs deeper analysis

Relevance

This study provides direct guidance for quantization strategy selection in LLM deployment, particularly useful for understanding performance trade-offs under low-precision quantization across different model scales. Highly relevant to model compression and efficient inference research.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How do k-quantization results compare with GPTQ, AWQ, and QuIP on the same models and benchmarks?
  • Is there a quantization level below which specific task capabilities (e.g., code generation vs. factual reasoning) degrade disproportionately?
  • How does k-quantization interact with other compression techniques such as pruning or knowledge distillation?
  • What is the memory-latency-throughput trade-off at each quantization level on consumer hardware?
