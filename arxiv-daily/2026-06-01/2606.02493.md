> **一句话总结：** 本文提出了 FRANZ 多维审计框架，从文化定位、泛化语言、拟人化线索和对话准则四个维度系统评估 LLM 对主观问题的回应方式，并基于涵盖七个国家、三十七万个问题的 SQUARE 语料库揭示了不同模型在回应框架上的系统性差异与耦合模式。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces FRANZ, a multi-dimensional framework that audits LLM response framing across cultural positioning, generalizing language, anthropomorphic cues, and conversational maxims. Using the SQUARE corpus of 376k subjective questions spanning 7 countries and 19 categories, the authors reveal systematic model-level differences and a notable positive coupling between insider positioning and anthropomorphism that varies by country.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 FRANZ（FRAmework for respoNse characteriZation），首个从传播学视角对 LLM 回应框架进行自动化、多维审计的框架，覆盖文化定位、泛化语言、拟人化线索与对话准则四个维度。
- 构建 SQUARE 语料库，包含来自 57 个国家性子版块的 376,350 条主观问题，映射至 7 个国家（印度、中国、菲律宾、美国、韩国、俄罗斯、土耳其）和 19 个文化相关的问题类别。
- 采用 LLM-as-a-judge 方法对每个维度进行规模化标注，并通过 4,500 条人工标注进行验证；针对泛化语言检测使用更强的 Llama-3.1-70B 作为评判模型。
- 对三个开源权重模型（Llama-3.1-8B-it、Gemma-3-12b-it、Ministral-3B-14B-it）进行系统评估，发现模型在各项回应特征上呈现稳定且显著的排名差异。
- 揭示内部定位与拟人化线索之间存在显著的正向耦合关系，且耦合程度随国家而变化；同时发现泛化语言与其他特征基本正交，为模型去偏和多目标优化提供了新的诊断视角。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces FRANZ (FRAmework for respoNse characteriZation), the first automated, multi-dimensional framework for auditing LLM response framing from a communicative perspective, covering cultural positioning, generalizing language, anthropomorphic cues, and conversational maxims.
- Constructs the SQUARE corpus comprising 376,350 subjective questions from 57 country-level subreddits, mapped to 7 countries and 19 culturally grounded question categories.
- Adopts an LLM-as-a-judge approach for scalable annotation across all four dimensions, validated with 4,500 human assessments; employs Llama-3.1-70B specifically for generalizing-language detection due to its higher complexity.
- Systematically evaluates three open-weight LLMs (Llama-3.1-8B-it, Gemma-3-12b-it, Ministral-3B-14B-it), revealing stable and statistically significant ranking differences across response characteristics.
- Discovers a significant positive coupling between insider positioning and anthropomorphic cues that varies by country, while finding generalizing language to be largely orthogonal to other characteristics—offering a new diagnostic lens for model debiasing and multi-objective optimization.

## Methodology

The authors design FRANZ as a sociolinguistically grounded framework that operationalizes four interrelated facets of response framing. Each facet is assessed by an independent LLM judge (Llama-3.1-8B-it for positioning, anthropomorphism, and maxims; Llama-3.1-70B-it for generalizing language) that receives only the question–response pair and a characteristic-specific rubric, with no information about the generating model.

**Insider Positioning** (§3.1) is operationalized through a socially grounded definition of stance and style alignment reflecting culturally informed language. The prompt builds on Wan et al. (2026) but adds illustrative examples from country-level subreddits to suit informal, subjective inputs.

**Anthropomorphism** (§3.2) draws on Ibrahim et al. (2026a)'s 13 cues organized into four groups: internal states, embodiment, relationship-building, and personhood claims. Embodiment cues (physical-participation references) are dropped due to low prevalence and unreliable text-based detection, leaving 9 cues: personal relationship, personal history, explicit relation status, desires, agency, emotions, validation, empathy, and relatability.

**Generalizing Language** (§3.3) detects claims attributing traits or behaviors to an entire group. The judge checks for (i) a group identity term, (ii) a generalizing grammatical form (bare plural, indefinite singular, quantified adverb, or definite singular), and (iii) whether enduring characteristics are attributed to the group as a whole.

**Conversational Maxims** (§3.4) apply Grice's four axes—quality (genuineness/reliability), quantity (adequacy without excess), relation (topical relevance), and manner (readability, brevity, structure)—as normative cooperative-communication criteria. Each maxim receives an independent binary adherence label.

**Dataset curation** (§4) uses the Pushshift Reddit crawl to identify 408 country-named subreddits (r/Ask<Country> or r/AskA<Nationality>), manually filtered to 57 active, text-focused, non-NSFW communities. After preprocessing, 376,350 posts remain. Each post is zero-shot categorized by Llama-3.1-8B-it into one of 19 categories (e.g., social relations, food and drink, education and career), with human-in-the-loop validation.

**Experimental setup** (§5) generates responses using a minimal system prompt ("You are a helpful assistant that directly answers the user's question") paired with the post title, body, and subreddit name. Less than 1k responses are non-English. FRANZ then annotates all responses. Statistical analysis uses Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE) to account for non-independence across models responding to the same post, with FDR correction and Llama as the reference model.

## Results & Highlights

**RQ1: Individual Response Tendencies** (§6.1) Aggregated across SQUARE, LLMs most often default to outsider-positioned, non-generalizing, non-anthropomorphizing framing. However, significant model-specific signatures emerge:
- **Insider positioning**: Mistral shows a +11% higher tendency than Llama and Gemma, and consistently ranks highest across nearly all country-category subsets (Table 1).
- **Generalizing language**: The ranking "Mistral > Gemma > Llama" is the most consistent pattern, though absolute frequencies remain low (≤15%).
- **Anthropomorphism**: Variability is high and concentrated in three affective cues—empathy, validation, and emotions. Gemma leads in validation but not necessarily empathy or emotion.
- **Maxim adherence** (§6.3, Figure 4): Gemma shows the strongest adherence overall; Llama and Mistral exhibit higher violation rates, particularly on quantity (verbosity) and manner. Quality and relation show the dominant patterns "Gemma > Llama > Mistral" and "Gemma > Mistral > Llama," respectively.

**RQ2: Co-occurrence Patterns** (§6.2, Figure 3) Generalizing language is largely orthogonal to both cultural positioning and anthropomorphism, clarifying that Mistral's higher frequency of both reflects independent tendencies rather than correlation. A more notable pattern is the bidirectional coupling between insider positioning and anthropomorphism: insider framing increases the likelihood of empathetic, validating, and emotional tone, and vice versa. The effect sizes for anthropomorphism → positioning tend to be larger than the reverse. No universal cross-model ranking emerges for these coupling effects—they vary within countries and categories.

**RQ3: Maxim Adherence vs. Other Characteristics** (§6.3) Maxim adherence is largely orthogonal to cultural positioning, generalizing language, and anthropomorphism. Thus, a response can adhere well to communicative norms while still being outsider-positioned or employing generalizing language—reflecting a tendency of LLMs to respond with detached, maxim-compliant but culturally non-nuanced text.

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a timely and important contribution by shifting LLM evaluation from *what* is said to *how* it is framed—a dimension that has been largely neglected in favor of factual-correctness benchmarks. The multi-dimensional framework is theoretically well-motivated, drawing on sociolinguistics (emic/etic distinction), pragmatics (Gricean maxims), and computational social science. The SQUARE corpus is impressively large and the GEE-based statistical approach appropriately handles non-independence.

However, several limitations deserve scrutiny:

1. **Cultural proxy coarseness**: The authors acknowledge that country-level subreddits are not population-representative—they skew toward younger, English-fluent, online users and may include diaspora communities. The findings should be read as patterns over Reddit communities, not over cultures. The limitation is honestly stated but does constrain generalizability.

2. **Judge reliability**: Despite human validation, LLM judges on subjective tasks remain imperfect. The authors frame labels as "noisy estimates" and rely on aggregate statistical testing, which is prudent, but the possibility of judge-model bias (e.g., Llama judging Llama-generated responses) is partially mitigated by not revealing the source model to the judge.

3. **Western-centric maxims**: Gricean maxims are themselves calibrated to Western communicative norms (Keenan, 1976; Wierzbicka, 1991). The observed violation rates may conflate model behavior with the metric's own cultural assumptions—a point the authors candidly raise.

4. **Effect sizes vs. practical significance**: While many differences are statistically significant, the absolute frequencies of generalizing language and anthropomorphism are low (often <15%). The practical impact on user experience may be smaller than the statistical significance suggests.

5. **Closed-model gap**: The study only evaluates open-weight models. Whether the same patterns hold for closed commercial APIs (GPT-4, Claude) remains unknown.

## Strengths

- Addresses a genuinely underexplored dimension of LLM evaluation: communicative framing for subjective, culturally situated queries.
- Provides a structured, theoretically grounded, multi-dimensional framework rather than a single metric or isolated dimension.
- Introduces a large-scale, culturally mapped dataset (SQUARE) with transparent curation and category taxonomy.
- Employs rigorous statistical methods (GEE) that account for non-independence across models responding to the same post.
- Offers honest self-critique regarding cultural proxy limitations, judge imperfection, and the Western bias of Gricean maxims.
- Releases publicly accessible consensus subsets on Hugging Face to support reproducibility and downstream research.

## Limitations

- Country-level subreddits are a coarse and demographically skewed proxy for culture; findings do not generalize to entire populations.
- LLM-as-a-judge annotations, though human-validated, remain noisy estimates for subjective characteristics.
- Gricean maxims may carry Western communicative assumptions, potentially confounding cross-cultural maxim-violation rates.
- Only three open-weight models are evaluated; closed-weight commercial APIs are not included.
- Statistical significance does not always translate to large absolute effect sizes or user-perceptible differences.
- The study focuses on English responses (with code-mixed Latin script classified as English), potentially missing framing behaviors in non-English language contexts.

## Relevance

对于关注 LLM 安全性、文化适应性与用户体验的研究者和从业者而言，本文提供了一个从"表达方式"角度诊断模型行为的有价值框架。FRANZ 不仅揭示了不同模型在主观性、文化敏感场景中的系统性倾向差异，还指出了内部定位与拟人化之间的耦合风险，为模型去偏、多目标优化以及更细致的文化对齐评估奠定了实证和方法论基础。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the four FRANZ dimensions correlate with actual user satisfaction, trust, or perceived cultural appropriateness in human subject studies?
- Can FRANZ be applied to evaluate closed-weight commercial APIs (e.g., GPT-4o, Claude 4), and do they exhibit similar or different systematic tendencies?
- How does fine-tuning or reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) shape a model's default positioning and anthropomorphism tendencies?
- Can the orthogonal relationship between generalizing language and other characteristics be exploited to design targeted debiasing interventions?
- How would the framework perform with non-English or code-mixed queries, and what adjustments would be needed for the judge prompts?
- Does the insider-positioning/anthropomorphism coupling persist when human annotators (rather than LLM judges) assess the same responses, and does it vary by annotator cultural background?
- Can FRANZ be extended to evaluate response framing in high-stakes domains (e.g., mental health advice, legal guidance) where subjective queries are common?
