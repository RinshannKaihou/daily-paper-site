Mechanistic Interpretability

Massive Spikes in LLMs are Bias Vectors: Mechanistic Uncovering and Spike-Free Quantization

Yung-Chin Chen, Chung Peng Lee, Ze-Wei Liou, Naveen Verma
本文揭示LLM激活尖峰本质上是sink token承载的刚性向量偏置，提出跨模态无尖峰后训练量化框架INSERTQUANT。 Massive activation spikes are structural vector biases carried by sink tokens, not scalar outliers. Proposes INSERTQUANT—a cross-modal post-training quantization framework achieving high-fidelity low-bit quantization on LLMs and ViTs with minimal overhead.

Multi-Domain Reinforcement Learning

A Local Perturbation Theory for Cross-Domain Interference and Recovery in Multi-Domain RL

Lei Yang, Siyu Ding, Deyi Xiong
揭示多领域RL跨领域干扰的局部化二阶机制，提出低维冲突子空间与刷新恢复策略。 Cross-domain interference is driven by localized second-order damage in a low-dimensional shared "conflict subspace" rather than global gradient conflict. Selective recovery via short domain refresh recovers Math performance from 57.66 to 66.04.
