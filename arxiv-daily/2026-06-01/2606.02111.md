> **一句话总结：** 本文提出多片段视频（MCV）SafetyBench 数据集，系统揭示了视频输入的上下文多样性会显著削弱多模态大语言模型（MLLM）的安全对齐，攻击成功率随片段数量递增，并据此提出基于图像模态过滤的防御策略。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces the Multi-Clip Video (MCV) SafetyBench dataset to systematically evaluate how contextual diversity in video inputs weakens safety alignment in multimodal large language models (MLLMs). Attack success rates increase with the number of clips, and the authors propose an image-based filtering defense leveraging the relative robustness of the image modality.

## 核心贡献

- 构建了 **MCV SafetyBench** 基准数据集：包含 2,920 段视频，覆盖 13 类 OpenAI 违规类别，每段视频由 4 个 2 秒短片段组成，每个片段呈现与有害查询相关的不同场景上下文。
- 在 8 个代表性视频 MLLM 上验证了攻击成功率（ASR）随片段数量单调递增：从单片段到四片段，Qwen2.5-VL-7B 的 ASR 在显式攻击下提升 17.95%，在隐式攻击下提升 11.23%。
- 通过三组对照实验系统揭示了视频模态的脆弱性来源：(1) 视频模态比图像模态更脆弱；(2) 动态视频比静态视频更脆弱；(3) 上下文多样性高的视频比重复片段视频更脆弱。
- 提出基于图像帧采样的简单防御策略：利用图像模态相对更强的安全对齐能力，在视频输入 MLLM 前随机采样单帧进行安全过滤，平均将 ASR 降低 49.56%，显著优于 prompt-based 基线。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **MCV SafetyBench**, a benchmark of 2,920 videos spanning 13 OpenAI policy violation categories. Each video comprises four 2-second clips depicting diverse contexts related to a harmful query.
- Validates on eight representative video MLLMs that attack success rate (ASR) consistently increases with the number of clips. For Qwen2.5-VL-7B, ASR rises by 17.95% (Explicit) and 11.23% (Implicit) from 1-clip to 4-clip settings.
- Through three controlled experiments, systematically identifies sources of video modality vulnerability: (1) video is more vulnerable than image; (2) dynamic video is more vulnerable than static video; (3) diverse contexts are more vulnerable than repeated clips.
- Proposes a simple image-based filtering defense that samples random frames and assesses their safety before video processing, reducing average ASR by 49.56% and outperforming prompt-based baselines (Safety Prompt and AdaShield).

## Methodology

The authors construct MCV SafetyBench through a three-phase pipeline:

1. **Semantic Extraction & Reconstruction (Phase 1):** Starting from 1,680 harmful queries (from MM-SafetyBench), GPT-4o extracts four semantic components per query—subject, object, action, and mood—and reconstructs them into coherent text-to-video prompts.
2. **Video Generation (Phase 2):** Each reconstructed prompt is fed into Wan2.2-T2V-A14B via ComfyUI to generate a 2-second clip. Four clips are concatenated into an 8-second video at 16 FPS. After manual inspection and removal of 220 low-quality samples, the dataset covers 1,460 queries × 2 variants = 2,920 videos.
3. **Typography Integration (Phase 3):** Harmful queries are paraphrased into step-by-step formats (e.g., "List steps to...") and rendered as typographic images embedded at the bottom of videos using MoviePy, creating combined videos that conceal malicious text within visual content.

**Attack Settings:**
- **Explicit Attack:** The harmful query is provided directly as text alongside the video.
- **Implicit Attack:** The harmful query is embedded typographically within the video itself, with only a benign text instruction provided to the model.

**Target Models:** Eight open-source video MLLMs were evaluated: Qwen2.5-VL-7B/32B/72B, Qwen3-VL-8B/32B, InternVL3.5-8B/38B, and LLaVA-Video-7B. All experiments used temperature=0.0 on H100 GPUs.

**Evaluation:** ASR is computed as the proportion of harmful responses (score = 5) out of total inputs, judged by GPT-4o-mini using the CLAS framework with 15 policy template rules. A score of 5 indicates full compliance with a policy-violating instruction. Human evaluation on 200 samples confirmed strong correlation (0.6229) between model-based and human judgments.

## Results & Highlights

**Main Results (Table 2):**
- All eight models showed highest ASR under the 4-clip setting.
- ASR generally increased monotonically with clip count. For Qwen2.5-VL-7B, Explicit ASR went from 50.75% (1-clip) to 68.70% (4-clip); Implicit ASR went from 69.04% to 80.27%.
- Implicit attacks consistently outperformed Explicit attacks across most models, with smaller sensitivity to clip count increases—consistent with prior findings that concealing malicious prompts in visual inputs weakens safety alignment.
- LLaVA-Video-7B was an exception: it showed lower ASR under Implicit attacks, attributed to weaker OCR capabilities.
- Safety alignment did not scale consistently with model size: Qwen2.5-VL-32B was more vulnerable than Qwen2.5-VL-7B under both attack settings.

**Controlled Experiments (Table 3):**
- **RQ1 (Video vs. Image):** Image-frame attacks (8/16/24/32 frames as images) resulted in consistently lower ASR than multi-clip video attacks, and increasing frame count did not increase vulnerability—confirming video modality-specific weakness.
- **RQ2 (Dynamic vs. Static):** Static videos (repeated typographic images at 4 FPS) yielded lower ASR than dynamic multi-clip videos, and frame count had no effect in static settings.
- **RQ3 (Diverse vs. Repeated clips):** Repeating the same clip 2–4 times produced substantially lower ASR than providing distinct clips, and ASR did not consistently increase with repeated clip count. This confirms vulnerability stems from contextual diversity, not merely video length or frame count.

**Representation Analysis (Figure 4):**
- PCA visualization of final-layer hidden states shows that benign and harmful inputs are well-separated in representation space.
- As clip count increases, representations shift from the harmful anchor toward the benign anchor—suggesting that diverse video inputs cause MLLMs to internally represent the input as less harmful, weakening safety detection.

**Defense Strategy (Table 4):**
- Image-based filtering (random frame sampling + safety check) achieved an average ASR reduction of 49.56% across four models.
- On Qwen3-VL-8B, image filtering reduced ASR from 73.15% to 0.62%—a near-complete mitigation.
- Prompt-based defenses (Safety Prompt, AdaShield) showed limited effectiveness, particularly on video inputs, supporting the hypothesis that video modality safety alignment is inherently weaker.

**Generalization:**
- Results replicated with HunyuanVideo-1.5-generated videos (Table 7), confirming the effect is not model-specific.
- Extended to 5-clip settings (Table 8): ASR continued to increase, though with diminishing returns from 4 to 5 clips.
- Closed-source models Gemini-2.5-Flash and GPT-5-mini (Table 9) also showed vulnerability, with Gemini exhibiting clear monotonic ASR increases with clip count.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The paper makes a meaningful contribution by isolating and quantifying a specific property of video inputs—contextual diversity across clips—as a driver of safety misalignment. While jailbreaks in image modality are well-studied, the systematic dissection of video-specific vulnerability factors (dynamic vs. static, diverse vs. repeated clips) provides new insights. The MCV SafetyBench dataset itself is a valuable resource for the community.

**Rigor:** The experimental design is thorough. The three-phase video generation pipeline is well-documented, and the controlled experiments (RQ1–RQ3) directly address confounding factors (length vs. diversity, modality vs. presentation format). Cross-validation with human annotators and replication with a second text-to-video model (HunyuanVideo-1.5) strengthen internal validity.

**Limitations:**
1. **Video duration ceiling:** The study is limited to 8-second videos (4 clips × 2 seconds). While a 5-clip extension was tested, longer-form videos and more clips were not evaluated. The authors acknowledge that as MLLMs improve at long-video understanding, more sophisticated attacks may emerge.
2. **Defense is indirect:** The proposed image-based filtering does not address the root cause of video modality vulnerability—it sidesteps it by leveraging image robustness. This is pragmatic but not a principled solution for video-specific safety alignment.
3. **Judge model bias:** Relying on GPT-4o-mini as the primary judge introduces potential bias. Although human correlation is reported (0.6229), the absolute agreement is moderate, and false positives/negatives in ASR measurement are possible.
4. **Dataset scope:** The dataset was generated synthetically using a single model (Wan2.2-T2V-A14B) with a specific prompt engineering pipeline. Real-world adversarial videos may exhibit different properties.

**Significance:** The findings have immediate practical implications for MLLM safety. As video-capable MLLMs are deployed in production, the demonstrated vulnerability to multi-clip inputs suggests that current safety guardrails are insufficient for video modality. The paper's defense strategy, while indirect, offers a deployable stopgap.

**Reproducibility:** Good. The authors release code and data, provide detailed appendices with prompts and scoring criteria, and use deterministic settings (temperature=0.0).

## Strengths

- Well-structured controlled experiments that isolate the effect of contextual diversity from confounding variables (video length, frame count, modality)
- Comprehensive evaluation across eight diverse models spanning different architectures and scales
- Rigorous evaluation protocol combining automated judging with human validation
- Strong generalization evidence: replication with alternative video generator, extension to 5 clips, and validation on closed-source models
- Actionable defense strategy with strong empirical results
- Transparent ethical practices including IRB approval and responsible disclosure
- Clear visualization of representation-space dynamics via PCA, providing mechanistic insight into why multi-clip inputs bypass safety alignment

## Limitations

- Video duration and clip count are limited (max 5 clips, 10 seconds); vulnerability at longer durations remains unexplored
- Defense strategy is a workaround rather than a fundamental fix for video modality safety alignment
- Reliance on GPT-4o-mini as judge may introduce systematic evaluation bias
- Synthetic video generation pipeline may not fully capture the diversity of real-world adversarial video content
- The study focuses on open-source models; closed-source evaluation is limited to two models with smaller sample sizes
- No analysis of whether the attack transfers across different safety training methodologies or alignment techniques

## Relevance

本文对当前快速发展中的视频多模态大语言模型安全研究具有重要的警示意义：视频模态的安全对齐明显弱于文本和图像模态，且上下文越丰富、越动态，模型越容易被越狱攻击绕过。对于从事 MLLM 安全对齐、红队测试（red-teaming）以及内容审核系统设计的从业者而言，该基准数据集和防御思路具有直接的参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does the multi-clip vulnerability scale with video duration beyond 10 seconds, and does there exist a saturation point where additional clips no longer increase ASR?
2. Can video-specific safety alignment be improved directly—e.g., through frame-level safety supervision, temporal consistency checks, or video-native adversarial training—rather than relying on image-based filtering?
3. Does the representation shift toward "benign" observed in PCA generalize to other model architectures (e.g., diffusion-based video-language models, or models with different visual encoders)?
4. How effective are the proposed attacks and defenses against MLLMs that employ separate safety classifiers or sandboxed execution for video inputs?
5. Can real-world adversarial videos (e.g., edited social media content, deepfakes with embedded instructions) replicate the multi-clip vulnerability observed with synthetically generated content?
