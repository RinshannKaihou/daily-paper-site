> **一句话总结：** 本文系统论证了输入歧义会干扰基于不确定性量化的错误预测，并提出通过门控专家网络和选择性预测将歧义信号与UQ分离，在多种模型、数据集和UQ指标上实现了超过10个PRR点的一致提升。

> **TL;DR:** This paper systematically demonstrates that input ambiguity interferes with uncertainty quantification (UQ)-based error prediction for LLMs, and proposes Gated Experts and Selective Prediction to disentangle ambiguity signals from UQ, yielding consistent improvements of over 10 PRR points across models, datasets, and UQ metrics.

## 核心贡献

- **理论洞察：** 首次在LLM错误预测任务中系统论证了**偶然不确定性（aleatoric uncertainty / 输入歧义）**与**认知不确定性（epistemic uncertainty / 模型知识缺失）**的混淆问题。UQ指标在高歧义样本上预测错误的能力显著下降。
- **特征分析：** 全面评估了六类UQ指标、语义聚类统计量（簇数量、熵、有效答案数等）以及内部表征（末层/中间层隐藏状态）对歧义分类的贡献，发现表征特征优于标量特征。
- **方法创新：** 提出两种将歧义信号注入错误预测流程的架构——**Gated Experts**（门控路由到歧义/非歧义专家网络）和**Selective Prediction**（将歧义概率作为罚项调整拒绝分数），均支持Oracle（金标准标签）和Latent（预测标签）两种版本。
- **广泛验证：** 在五种模型（覆盖标准指令微调、多样性后训练、歧义感知微调三类范式）、三个数据集、六种UQ指标及多种监督信号下验证了方法的一致性和有效性。

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretical insight:** First systematic demonstration in LLM error prediction that **aleatoric uncertainty (input ambiguity)** and **epistemic uncertainty (model knowledge gaps)** are conflated by standard UQ metrics, with UQ being significantly less predictive of errors on ambiguous inputs.
- **Feature analysis:** Comprehensive evaluation of six UQ metrics, semantic cluster statistics (cluster count, entropy, effective number of answers), and internal representations (last-layer / mid-layer hidden states) for ambiguity classification, finding representation features outperform scalar ones.
- **Methodological innovation:** Two architectures for injecting ambiguity into error prediction — **Gated Experts** (routing to specialized ambiguous/unambiguous expert networks) and **Selective Prediction** (penalizing rejection scores by ambiguity probability), both in Oracle (gold-label) and Latent (predicted-label) variants.
- **Extensive validation:** Consistent improvements demonstrated across five models (spanning standard instruction tuning, diversity-oriented post-training, and ambiguity-aware fine-tuning), three datasets, six UQ metrics, and multiple supervision signals.

## Methodology

The paper tackles **Error Prediction** — predicting whether an LLM's answer to a question is correct — via **Uncertainty Quantification (UQ)**.

### Problem Formulation
The canonical approach feeds UQ metrics directly into an error prediction head. The authors argue this is flawed because UQ metrics capture both:
- **Epistemic uncertainty:** reducible uncertainty due to model lack of knowledge/capacity.
- **Aleatoric uncertainty:** irreducible uncertainty inherent in ambiguous or conflicting inputs.

When a question is ambiguous (e.g., "What is it called when you mix up the letters of a word?" → "dyslexia" or "anagram"), high model uncertainty reflects valid multiple interpretations, not necessarily model error.

### Features Used
Three categories of features are extracted from K=10 stochastic samples plus one greedy decode:
1. **UQ Metrics (6):** MSP, Semantic Entropy, Semantic Energy, CoCoA, SAR, MI.
2. **Semantic Cluster Statistics:** Number of clusters, cluster assignment entropy, effective number of answers, max cluster probability, top-2 probability mass, probability gap.
3. **Internal Representations:** Last-token final-layer embedding and layer-15 residual.

### Ambiguity Classifier
An independent MLP classifier predicts instance-level ambiguity probability â from the feature vector, trained with binary cross-entropy against ground-truth ambiguity labels.

### Error Prediction Architectures
**Baseline:** MLP + Error Prediction head with BCE + pairwise ranking loss.

**Gated Experts:**
- **Oracle:** ĉ = (1 − a) Eu(xf) + a Ea(xf), where a ∈ {0,1} is the gold ambiguity label.
- **Latent:** ĉ = (1 − â) Eu(xf) + â Ea(xf), using predicted ambiguity probability.

**Selective Prediction:**
- Computes rejection score R = −ĉ + λâ (or λa for Oracle), explicitly penalizing uncertain + ambiguous instances for abstention.

### Datasets
- **AmbigQA:** 10K train, open-domain QA with human-annotated multiple plausible answer sets. ~50% ambiguous.
- **TriviaQA:** 62K train, large-scale reading comprehension. ~16% ambiguous (auto-annotated with Qwen for underspecification following Huang et al., 2026).
- **NCQA:** 234 train, 100 dev, 100 test. Binary yes/no from fact-checking, ambiguous cases have conflicting evidence.

### Models Evaluated
- **Standard:** Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen3-14B (non-thinking mode)
- **Diversity-tuned:** Spectrum-Llama-3.1-8B, Spectrum-Qwen3-14B (Spectrum Tuning to mitigate mode collapse)
- **Ambiguity-aware:** A2 Search-7B (RL fine-tuned to recognize and resolve ambiguity)

### Evaluation
- **QA accuracy:** LLM-as-a-judge (Gemma-2-2B-IT) and AlignScore.
- **Error prediction:** AUROC and PRR (Prediction Rejection Ratio).
- **Significance:** Permutation test (p < 0.05).

## Results & Highlights

### Ambiguity Prediction (Table 4)
- **AmbigQA:** AUROC ~0.72–0.73 (best), thanks to high-quality human annotations.
- **TriviaQA:** AUROC ~0.62, reflecting the noise of LLM-generated ambiguity labels.
- **NCQA:** AUROC ~0.61–0.65, limited by small dataset size.
- **Key finding:** Latent models often match or exceed Oracle counterparts, suggesting continuous predicted ambiguity probabilities carry richer signal than binary gold labels.

### Error Prediction (Table 5, Figure 3, Appendix Tables)
- **Consistent gains:** Ambiguity signal improves error prediction across all model families, datasets, UQ metrics, and architectures.
- **Magnitude:** Improvements exceed **10 PRR points** for individual UQ metrics on standard datasets.
- **Best absolute scores:** Up to **0.879 PRR** and **0.904 AUROC** on TriviaQA.
- **UQ metric insights:**
  - **MSP** is surprisingly robust and often outperforms Semantic Entropy on ambiguous datasets, reinforcing that semantic clustering degrades under valid aleatoric variation.
  - **CoCoA** and **SAR** lead among complex metrics but still benefit from ambiguity routing.
  - **MI** performs poorly (especially on AmbigQA), corroborating prior findings that it fails to disentangle aleatoric uncertainty despite its design intent.
- **Feature combinations:** Even when the error predictor has access to all UQ metrics combined ("all UQ"), latent gating/selective prediction still yields significant PRR gains — confirming ambiguity provides a complementary signal that UQ ensembles alone cannot capture.
- **Dataset-specific:** NCQA shows the most dramatic improvements because ambiguous binary questions are "easy" (both answers correct), but results should be interpreted cautiously due to the tiny training set (234 instances).
- **Model-specific:** Spectrum-tuned models are only marginally better at error prediction than vanilla counterparts, indicating diversity-aware fine-tuning improves calibration but does not eliminate the need for explicit ambiguity disentanglement.

### Qualitative Example (Table 6)
For "How many levels are there in Pac-Man?" (ambiguous: 256 total vs. 21 before plateau), baseline Semantic Entropy predicts 73.8% error, while the latent selective model correctly assigns near-zero error (0.0%) by disentangling underspecification from actual error likelihood.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty & Rigor:** The paper makes a valuable contribution by bridging the gap between UQ and ambiguity modeling for error prediction. The distinction between aleatoric and epistemic uncertainty is well-established in Bayesian deep learning, but its systematic application to LLM error prediction — with empirical validation across six UQ metrics and five models — is novel and timely. The methodology is sound: the Gated Experts and Selective Prediction frameworks are well-motivated, and the ablation over oracle vs. latent versions is particularly informative.

**Reproducibility:** The authors commit to releasing code. Experimental details are thorough (hyperparameter search, early stopping, K=10 samples, temperature=1.0). However, two concerns exist: (1) hyperparameter selection uses the validation set for final reporting due to withheld test labels (standard in the field but potentially optimistic), and (2) TriviaQA ambiguity labels are LLM-generated rather than human-verified, introducing potential label noise.

**Limitations & Red Flags:**
- **English-only, text-only:** Generalization to multilingual and multimodal settings is unverified.
- **Label dependence:** Latent models require ambiguity-labeled training data, which may be scarce in new domains. The authors acknowledge fully unsupervised ambiguity induction as future work.
- **Small NCQA:** The most dramatic results come from the smallest dataset (234 train), raising questions about robustness despite held-out test reporting.
- **Closed-book QA only:** The framework does not address retrieval-augmented settings where retrieved context introduces additional aleatoric uncertainty.
- **Metric focus:** Limited to semantic UQ metrics; other uncertainty families (e.g., verbalized confidence, logits-based) are not explored.

**Significance:** The finding that UQ metrics conflate ambiguity with model error has direct implications for production LLM deployments, selective generation, and safety-critical applications. The practical takeaway — that disentangling ambiguity yields substantial gains even with simple MLP-based architectures — is actionable.

## Strengths

- Clear theoretical motivation grounded in the aleatoric/epistemic uncertainty distinction.
- Comprehensive experimental coverage: 5 models × 3 datasets × 6 UQ metrics × 2 architectures × 2 supervision modes.
- The latent models matching/exceeding oracle versions is a strong and somewhat surprising result.
- Insightful analysis of individual UQ metrics (MSP robustness, MI failure, CoCoA/SAR leadership).
- Risk-coverage curves (Figure 3) provide intuitive visual evidence of improvements.
- Honest acknowledgment of limitations (English-only, label dependence, closed-book setting).

## Limitations

- **Language and modality:** English text-only; no validation for multilingual or multimodal models.
- **Label requirement:** Latent models need ambiguity-labeled training data; scalability to new domains is limited without automatic ambiguity induction.
- **Dataset size imbalance:** NCQA has only 234 training examples; its dramatic improvements may not generalize.
- **Validation-set reporting:** Hyperparameter tuning and final reporting on the same validation split for AmbigQA/TriviaQA (due to withheld test labels) may inflate scores.
- **Scope restriction:** Closed-book QA only; RAG and long-context settings with contradictory retrieved evidence are not addressed.
- **UQ metric scope:** Focuses only on semantic uncertainty metrics; other families (verbalized confidence, calibration-based) are excluded.

## Relevance

本文对提升大语言模型的可信度和选择性生成能力具有直接价值。通过区分输入歧义与模型无知，该方法能更精准地识别真正的模型错误，对需要高可靠性的问答系统、事实核查工具和安全关键型NLP应用均有重要参考意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does the framework perform in retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) settings where retrieved context may contradict internal knowledge or contain conflicting evidence?
2. Can ambiguity labels be induced fully unsupervised or via weak supervision, eliminating the need for annotated training data?
3. How well does the method generalize to open-ended generation tasks (summarization, code generation) where ambiguity is harder to define and annotate?
4. Would the same patterns hold for reasoning models (e.g., o3, Gemini 2.5 Pro) that exhibit different uncertainty profiles through chain-of-thought?
5. Can the ambiguity classifier leverage internal model representations more deeply (e.g., attention patterns, layer-wise confidence trajectories) rather than just final and mid-layer embeddings?
6. How does the approach scale to multilingual and multimodal inputs where ambiguity may manifest across languages or modalities?
7. Can the disentangled ambiguity signal be used not just for error prediction but for active learning or targeted model fine-tuning?
