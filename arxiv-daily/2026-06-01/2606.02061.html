Ablating Archetypes: The Stability of Archetypal SAEs is an Artifact of Initialization and Metric Design

arXiv: 2606.02061

Date: 2026-06-01

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02061

一句话总结： 本文通过严格的消融实验揭示，Archetypal SAE所报告的稳定性并非源于其凸包约束，而是来自跨运行共享的确定性k-means初始化；同时作者还发现了未中心化激活上的余弦度量几何伪影，呼吁区分"稳定性"与"稳定化"并采用轨迹诊断。
TL;DR: Through rigorous ablations, this paper shows that the stability reported by Archetypal SAEs stems from shared deterministic k-means initialization rather than the convex-hull constraint itself. The authors also uncover a preprocessing-dependent cosine-geometry artifact (the "galaxy-far-away effect") on uncentered activations, and argue for distinguishing "stability" from "stabilization" while adopting trajectory diagnostics.

核心贡献

  • 区分稳定性与稳定化：澄清了机制可解释性中两个常被混用的概念——"稳定性"（stability，两个独立训练模型在训练终点的一致性）与"稳定化"（stabilization，独立初始化的运行随训练收敛到共同解）。前者可通过固定起点人为制造，后者才是真正的数据驱动收敛。
  • 揭穿Archetypal SAE的稳定性假象：证明Fel et al. (2025)报告的稳定性源于跨种子使用相同的k-means解码器初始化（D = WC，W初始化为单位矩阵，C固定），导致运行间字典距离在训练前即为零。终点一致性只是初始化的延续，而非训练动态收敛的证据。
  • 凸包约束本身不加速稳定化：当移除相同初始化后，Archetypal TopK的收敛速度约为经典TopK的一半（50M tokens后距离≈0.35 vs ≈0.17），表明凸包约束实际上阻碍了而非促进了稳定化。
  • 发现余弦度量的几何伪影：识别出"星系远距效应"（galaxy-far-away effect）——未中心化的LLM残差流激活具有显著均值偏移，导致k-means质心聚集在远离原点的窄锥内，任何两个原子在未中心化空间中的余弦距离都被系统性地虚增0.30–0.36。这使得任何将原子保持在数据均值附近的方法（包括Archetypal SAE）都 falsely 显得稳定。
  • 提出初始化消融与轨迹诊断：设计了两种消融条件——(1) 禁用凸包投影但保留k-means初始化；(2) 经典SAE直接以k-means质心初始化——证明二者数值完全等价且都比完整Archetypal SAE更稳定。同时提出应追踪训练全程的跨运行距离轨迹，而非仅看终点。

Key Contributions

  • Distinguishes stability from stabilization: Clarifies two notions often conflated in mechanistic interpretability—"stability" (agreement between independently trained models at the end of training) versus "stabilization" (convergence of independently initialized runs toward a common solution). The former can be trivially manufactured by fixing the starting point; the latter reflects genuine data-driven convergence.
  • Exposes the Archetypal SAE stability artifact: Shows that the stability reported by Fel et al. (2025) stems from shared deterministic k-means decoder initialization across seeds (D = WC, with W initialized to the identity and C fixed), making inter-run dictionary distance exactly zero before any gradient step. Endpoint agreement is merely the preservation of initialization, not evidence of training convergence.
  • Demonstrates the convex-hull constraint itself does not accelerate stabilization: When shared initialization is removed, Archetypal TopK with random initialization converges at roughly half the rate of classical TopK (≈0.35 vs ≈0.17 after 50M tokens). The constraint impedes rather than enhances stabilization.
  • Identifies a cosine-metric geometry artifact: Uncovers the "galaxy-far-away effect"—uncentered LLM residual-stream activations have substantial mean offsets, causing k-means centroids to cluster in a narrow cone far from the origin. Cosine distances between any two atoms are systematically inflated by 0.30–0.36, making any method that keeps atoms near the data mean falsely appear stable.
  • Proposes initialization ablations and trajectory diagnostics: Designs two ablation conditions—(1) disabling the convex-hull projection while retaining k-means initialization; (2) classical SAE initialized directly from k-means centroids—showing they are numerically identical and both more stable than the full Archetypal SAE. Advocates tracking inter-run distance trajectories throughout training rather than only at endpoints.

Methodology

The study builds on standard TopK sparse autoencoder training and introduces systematic ablations to isolate the contribution of the archetypal constraint from shared initialization.

Experimental Settings:

  • Vision setting: DinoV2 patch embeddings extracted from a single image class (rabbits), following the public Google Colab notebook provided by Fel et al. (2025).
  • Language setting: Residual stream activations from layer 6 (middle layer) of Pythia-160m, trained on WikiText-103 / The Pile.

Key Conditions Compared:

  1. Classical TopK (random init): Standard SAE with randomly initialized decoder.
  2. Archetypal TopK (fixed k-means init): D = WC with fixed centroid matrix C and W initialized to identity—identical across seeds.
  3. Archetypal TopK (random init): Same convex-hull constraint but with randomized W and relaxation offsets per seed.
  4. Archetypal SAE (ablated): Convex-hull projection disabled; W frozen, residual bound lifted—structurally equivalent to classical SAE with k-means-initialized decoder.
  5. Classical SAE + fixed init: Classical TopK with decoder initialized directly from k-means centroids, no archetypal constraint.

Metrics:

  • Endpoint stability: Normalized cosine Hungarian distance H(D⁽¹⁾, D⁽²⁾) between two trained dictionaries (lower = more stable).
  • Trajectory diagnostics: Mean minimum cosine distance M(D⁽¹⁾, D⁽²⁾) logged every 100 steps to visualize convergence over training.
  • Reconstruction: R² score measuring how well the SAE reconstructs activations.

Preprocessing Correction:

Both settings apply centering before k-means and subtract the activation mean from the decoder bias during training. This corrects the "galaxy-far-away effect" that otherwise inflates cosine similarity for uncentered activations.

Results & Highlights

Stability Trajectories (Figure 1, §5):

  • Classical TopK with random initialization starts with high inter-run distance (≈0.88) and decreases monotonically over 50M tokens to ≈0.17—genuine stabilization driven by the data distribution.
  • Archetypal TopK with fixed k-means initialization stays flat at zero throughout training because both seeds start from identical dictionaries. No convergence is tested.
  • Archetypal TopK with random initialization decreases from ≈0.88 but converges at roughly half the rate of classical TopK, ending at ≈0.35. The convex-hull constraint slows stabilization.

Ablation Results (Table 3, §6):

| Condition | Vision (Hungarian ↓) | Vision (R² ↑) | Pythia-160m (Hungarian ↓) | Pythia-160m (R² ↑) |

|---|---|---|---|---|

| Classic SAE (random init) | 0.433 | 0.573 | 0.200 | 0.937 |

| Archetypal SAE | 0.243 | 0.542 | 0.170 | 0.758 |

| Archetypal SAE (ablated) | 0.068 | 0.570 | 0.112 | 0.914 |

| Classic SAE + fixed init | 0.068 | 0.570 | 0.112 | 0.914 |

  • The ablated archetypal SAE and classical SAE with fixed initialization are numerically identical in both settings, confirming that the convex-hull projection contributes nothing to stability.
  • Both ablated conditions are substantially more stable than the full archetypal SAE and the randomly initialized classical SAE.
  • The full archetypal SAE achieves the worst R² in both settings (vision: 0.542; Pythia: 0.758), indicating the convex-hull projection harms reconstruction quality while providing no stabilization benefit.

Uncentered-Cosine Confound (§4.2–4.3):

  • Before centering, mean pairwise cosine similarity among k-means centroids is 0.362 (LLM) and 0.298 (vision).
  • After centering, both collapse to approximately zero.
  • This systematic positive bias of 0.30–0.36 directly inflates reported stability for any method keeping atoms near the uncentered data mean.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The paper makes a crucial methodological contribution by showing that a recent claimed advance (Archetypal SAE stability) is artifactual. The conceptual distinction between stability and stabilization improves terminological precision in mechanistic interpretability.
  • Rigor: Strong. The core argument is logically airtight: if stability is defined as inter-run agreement and all runs start from the same decoder dictionary, the comparison is structurally biased. The ablation design cleanly isolates initialization from constraint effects. The code audit (Appendix B) adds transparency. However, the hyperparameters are drawn directly from the reference implementation without sweeping, leaving open whether the archetypal constraint might help in other regimes.
  • Reproducibility: Excellent. The authors provide their code at a public GitHub repository and base experiments on the original authors' public Colab notebook. The key manipulations (removing shared initialization, disabling projection) are trivial to implement.
  • Significance: Very high. If the critique holds broadly, it undermines a key selling point of Archetypal SAEs and cautions the community against using endpoint stability as a proxy for feature validity without verifying genuine convergence from diverse initializations. It also raises awareness about initialization artifacts and metric geometry in dictionary-learning evaluations.
  • Red Flags / Limitations:
  • Single hyperparameter configuration; no sweep performed.
  • Vision experiments use only a single image class (rabbits).
  • Language experiments use only one layer of one model (Pythia-160m, layer 6).
  • Training duration (20 epochs for ablations, 50M tokens for trajectories) may be insufficient to see long-term convergence shifts.
  • The critique does not address whether the archetypal constraint provides other benefits (e.g., better interpretability, visualization, atom scale control) independent of stability.
  • If identical initialization is theoretically motivated as part of the archetypal method, the critique's force is somewhat reduced—though the paper argues stabilization (not stability) is the relevant desideratum.

Strengths

  • Crystal-clear conceptual distinction between stability and stabilization that improves scientific practice in the field.
  • Direct, falsifiable experimental challenge to a recent claim with elegant ablation design.
  • Identification of a subtle but pervasive metric-design issue (galaxy-far-away effect) with broad implications beyond Archetypal SAEs.
  • Transparent code audit documenting discrepancies between published methodology and public implementation.
  • Trajectory diagnostics (plotting inter-run distance over training) provide a richer and more principled evaluation framework than endpoint-only metrics.

Limitations

  • No hyperparameter sweep; results may not generalize across sparsity levels, dictionary sizes, or model scales.
  • Limited empirical scope: single image class (rabbits) for vision, single layer of a single small LM (Pythia-160m) for language.
  • Does not propose a practical alternative that achieves true stabilization from random initialization, though the authors' concurrent work (Brzozowski & Chung, 2026) addresses this separately.
  • Does not evaluate whether the archetypal constraint improves feature interpretability, visualization, or other downstream desiderata even if it fails at stabilization.
  • The fixed k-means initialization itself may be a valuable practical technique (as the results show it improves stability); the paper does not fully explore whether this should be adopted as a standalone best practice.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the archetypal constraint provide benefits in regimes not tested here (e.g., very large dictionaries, extreme sparsity, or vision-language models)?
  2. Can true stabilization from diverse random initializations be achieved through alternative constraints (e.g., the encoder-decoder alignment proposed in Brzozowski & Chung, 2026) or training objectives?
  3. How do these findings scale to larger language models (e.g., Llama-3 70B, GPT-4-scale) where SAE instability is most acute?
  4. Should fixed k-means initialization be adopted as a standalone best practice for classical SAEs, independent of the archetypal constraint?
  5. How can the field standardize stability evaluation protocols to prevent initialization and metric artifacts from inflating reported results?
  6. Does the galaxy-far-away effect also affect other stability metrics (e.g., Maximum Cosine Similarity, PW-MCC) and other model modalities beyond vision and language?
