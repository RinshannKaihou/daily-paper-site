## 一句话总结

LUFFY提出了一种混合策略GRPO框架，通过引入离策略推理轨迹（来自强模型）和策略整形（policy shaping）机制，在保持训练稳定性的同时显著提升了大语言模型的数学推理能力（6个数学基准平均提升6.4分），并解决了纯在线强化学习中熵坍塌和弱模型训练失败的问题。

## TL;DR

LUFFY (Learning to reason Under oFF-policY guidance) addresses a fundamental limitation of on-policy RL methods (like GRPO) for training reasoning models: they cannot learn from external demonstrations. The framework introduces Mixed-Policy GRPO, which combines on-policy rollouts with off-policy reasoning traces from stronger models (e.g., DeepSeek-R1), coupled with a policy shaping mechanism via regularized importance sampling (f(x) = x/(x + gamma)) that prevents entropy collapse while emphasizing informative low-probability tokens. LUFFY achieves a +6.4 average improvement across 6 math benchmarks over strong on-policy baselines, generalizes well to out-of-distribution tasks (+6.2), and uniquely succeeds in training weak models (LLaMA3-1.8B) where pure on-policy RL fails entirely. Accepted as a poster at NeurIPS 2025.

## 核心贡献

- **混合策略GRPO框架**：首次将离策略（off-policy）推理轨迹融入GRPO训练流程，使模型能够从更强的教师模型（如DeepSeek-R1）的推理过程中学习，突破了纯在线RL只能依赖自身探索的限制。
- **策略整形（Policy Shaping）机制**：通过正则化重要性采样比率 f(x) = x/(x + gamma)，在离策略梯度中赋予低概率token更高权重，有效避免训练过程中的熵坍塌（entropy collapse），维持模型的生成多样性。
- **理论收敛保证**：提供了Mixed-Policy GRPO的收敛性证明（Theorem 1），证明了算法具有 O(1/sqrt(K)) 的收敛速率，为方法提供了坚实的理论基础。
- **弱模型训练突破**：首次证明离策略指导可以使极小模型（LLaMA3-1.8B）成功完成推理训练，而纯在线RL方法在这些模型上完全失败。

## Key Contributions

- **Mixed-Policy GRPO**: A novel training framework that integrates off-policy reasoning traces from stronger models into on-policy GRPO training. This is the first principled approach to combine demonstration learning with RLVR (Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards), enabling the model to learn reasoning strategies it cannot discover through self-exploration alone.
- **Policy Shaping via Regularized Importance Sampling**: The key technical innovation — applying the transformation f(x) = x/(x + gamma) to importance sampling ratios. This elegantly addresses two problems simultaneously: (1) it prevents entropy collapse by down-weighting overly high importance ratios, and (2) it amplifies gradients for low-probability tokens that carry critical reasoning information. The practical implementation simplifies this by setting pi_phi = 1 for off-policy samples.
- **Theoretical Convergence Guarantee**: Theorem 1 establishes O(1/sqrt(K)) convergence for Mixed-Policy GRPO, providing rigorous justification for the approach. The analysis covers both the on-policy and off-policy gradient components within a unified framework.
- **Weak Model Training**: Demonstrates that off-policy guidance enables successful reasoning training on LLaMA3-1.8B (a 1.8B parameter model), where standard on-policy RL collapses entirely. This significantly expands the applicability of RLVR methods.

## Methodology

LUFFY builds upon GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimization), a widely-used RLVR algorithm. The core innovation is Mixed-Policy GRPO, which modifies the training objective as follows:

**1. Off-Policy Trace Collection**: Reasoning traces are collected from a stronger model (DeepSeek-R1) using the same prompts as training. These traces serve as high-quality demonstrations of reasoning strategies.

**2. Mixed Rollout Buffer**: During training, each batch contains a mixture of on-policy rollouts (generated by the current model) and off-policy demonstrations (from the stronger model). The ratio is controlled by a hyperparameter.

**3. Policy Shaping**: The standard importance sampling ratio pi_theta(a|s) / pi_phi(a|s) is transformed through f(x) = x/(x + gamma), where gamma is a tunable regularization parameter. This transformation:
- For on-policy samples (ratio close to 1): f(1) = 1/(1+gamma), slightly reduced
- For off-policy samples with low-probability tokens: the ratio is small, and f(x) > x/(gamma+x) amplifies the gradient signal
- For off-policy samples with high-probability tokens: the ratio is large, but f(x) saturates near 1, preventing gradient explosion

**4. Practical Simplification**: In practice, pi_phi is set to 1 for off-policy samples (since the teacher model's log-probabilities are not needed), which further simplifies implementation while maintaining effectiveness.

**5. Binary Reward Design**: Uses a simple 0/1 reward based on whether the final answer is correct, requiring no process-level reward models.

## Results & Highlights

- **Math Reasoning (6 benchmarks)**: LUFFY achieves an average improvement of +6.4 points over on-policy GRPO across AIME 2024, AIME 2025, AMC 2023, OlympiadBench, Minerva Math, and MATH-500. Individual benchmark gains range from +3.8 to +9.2 points.
- **Out-of-Distribution Generalization**: On OOD tasks (including logical reasoning, scientific QA, and code generation), LUFFY achieves +6.2 average improvement, demonstrating that the learned reasoning strategies transfer beyond mathematical problem-solving.
- **Weak Model Results**: LUFFY successfully trains LLaMA3-1.8B, achieving meaningful performance gains, whereas standard on-policy RL produces degraded performance (negative training signal).
- **Entropy Maintenance**: Analysis shows that LUFFY maintains significantly higher entropy throughout training compared to standard GRPO, confirming the effectiveness of policy shaping in preventing mode collapse.
- **Ablation Studies**: Both the off-policy traces and the policy shaping mechanism contribute independently; removing either component leads to performance degradation.

## Strengths

1. **Principled approach**: Unlike ad-hoc methods for mixing demonstrations with RL, LUFFY provides a theoretically grounded framework with convergence guarantees.
2. **Elegant policy shaping**: The f(x) = x/(x + gamma) transformation is simple, interpretable, and effectively prevents entropy collapse without introducing additional computational overhead.
3. **Strong empirical results**: Consistent improvements across multiple benchmarks, model sizes, and task types (math and OOD).
4. **Weak model applicability**: The ability to train 1.8B parameter models successfully is practically significant and expands the accessibility of reasoning training.
5. **Clean implementation**: The practical simplification (pi_phi = 1) makes the method easy to implement and integrate into existing GRPO pipelines.

## Limitations

1. **Dependence on teacher model quality**: Off-policy traces from DeepSeek-R1 are central to the method. The performance ceiling is partially bounded by the teacher's reasoning ability, and regenerating traces with different teachers may yield different results.
2. **Binary reward constraint**: The method relies on verifiable correctness signals (0/1 rewards), limiting applicability to tasks where answers cannot be automatically verified (e.g., open-ended generation, creative reasoning).
3. **Hyperparameter sensitivity**: The gamma parameter and the on-policy/off-policy mixture ratio require tuning. The paper could benefit from a more systematic sensitivity analysis across different model sizes and tasks.
4. **Computational cost of trace generation**: Generating high-quality off-policy traces from large models like DeepSeek-R1 adds upfront computational cost, though this is amortized over training.
5. **Limited process-level analysis**: While the paper analyzes entropy dynamics, deeper investigation into what reasoning strategies are learned from off-policy traces versus discovered on-policy would strengthen the contribution.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does LUFFY perform when the teacher model is not substantially stronger than the student (e.g., using a same-size model's traces)? Is the improvement primarily from reasoning strategy transfer or simply from exposure to correct solutions?
2. Can policy shaping be combined with process-level reward models (PRMs) to provide denser training signals, and would this further improve sample efficiency?
3. What is the scaling behavior of LUFFY with respect to the number of off-policy traces? Is there a point of diminishing returns, and how does this interact with the quality of the traces?
4. How robust is LUFFY to distribution shift between the teacher's training data and the student's target domain? For example, can math-focused traces improve coding or scientific reasoning?
5. Could the policy shaping mechanism be applied more broadly to other RLHF/RLAIF settings beyond reasoning, such as alignment training or instruction following?
