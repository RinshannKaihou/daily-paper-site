> **一句话总结：** 本文将稀疏自编码器（SAE）应用于 Stable Diffusion v1.4，发现图像的空间构图在去噪过程的第一步就已确定（IoU 约 0.26），通过 SAE 潜空间中的可解释概念可实现对图像构图（早期阶段）和风格（中期阶段）的因果性控制。

> **TL;DR:** This paper applies Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs) to Stable Diffusion v1.4's cross-attention blocks, training separate SAEs per timestep to trace how human-interpretable concepts emerge and evolve across the reverse diffusion trajectory. The key finding is striking: coarse image composition (object layout) is already encoded in model activations before the first denoising step completes, even though the model's visual output is still pure noise. By t=0.5 (midpoint), composition is finalized. The authors design causal interventions that can steer image layout at early stages and image style at middle stages, revealing a temporal hierarchy: composition -> style -> texture. No code release is mentioned. The paper was accepted at NeurIPS 2025.

## 核心贡献

- **首次将 SAE 框架应用于多步扩散模型的时间演化分析**：不同于此前仅分析单步蒸馏模型（SDXL Turbo）的工作，本文针对 SDv1.4 在三个关键时间步（t=1.0, 0.5, 0.0）分别训练 SAE，系统追踪可解释概念随去噪过程的演化轨迹。
- **发现图像构图在去噪第一步即已涌现**：仅凭第一步的 SAE 激活即可预测最终图像中物体的空间位置（mid_block 上 IoU 约 0.26），而此时模型的后验均值输出在视觉上仍是纯噪声。
- **提出可扩展的视觉化概念字典构建管线**：利用 RAM + Grounding DINO + SAM 的开集检测/分割管线替代 LLM 标注，避免了 VLM 的幻觉和空间推理缺陷，可扩展至大规模图像。
- **设计了因果干预技术**：包括空间定向干预（控制物体位置）和全局干预（控制图像风格），并系统揭示了干预效果对扩散时间的依赖性。
- **揭示了扩散模型内部表示的时间层级结构**：早期阶段控制构图、中期阶段控制风格、后期阶段仅影响纹理细节。

## Key Contributions

- **First application of SAEs to trace concept evolution across diffusion timesteps.** Separate SAEs are trained on cross-attention block activations at t=1.0, 0.5, and 0.0, enabling analysis of how interpretable features change during the multi-step denoising process.
- **Discovery that image composition emerges at the very first diffusion step.** Using SAE activations from the initial step (t=1.0), the authors can predict the spatial layout of objects in the final image (IoU ~0.26 from mid_block) despite the model output being visually indistinguishable from noise.
- **A scalable, vision-only concept dictionary pipeline.** Replaces LLM-based labeling (which suffers from hallucination and scalability issues) with a pipeline using RAM (tagging) + Grounding DINO (detection) + SAM (segmentation) to assign object-level labels to SAE features based on IoU overlap.
- **Causal intervention techniques for composition and style control.** Spatially targeted interventions manipulate object placement via concept vector amplification/suppression; global interventions manipulate artistic style. Both are shown to be time-dependent.
- **A temporal hierarchy of diffusion representations.** Early diffusion: composition is malleable; Middle diffusion: composition is frozen but style is controllable; Late diffusion: only minor textural changes are possible.

## Methodology

The authors target Stable Diffusion v1.4 (U-Net architecture) and train TopK-sparse autoencoders (expansion factor 4x, d=1280 -> n_f=5120) on the residual updates of three cross-attention transformer blocks (down_block, mid_block, up_block) at three diffusion timesteps (t=1.0, 0.5, 0.0). Activations are collected from 1.5M LAION-COCO prompts using DDIM with 50 steps and CFG scale 7.5.

**Concept Dictionary Construction:** 40k held-out prompts generate images; SAE activations are cached. A vision pipeline (RAM + Grounding DINO + SAM) produces segmentation masks with object labels. Labels are assigned to concept IDs (CIDs) when the IoU between the activation map and the object mask exceeds 0.5. Word2Vec embeddings provide semantic summaries for each concept.

**Composition Prediction:** For each spatial location, the top-activating concepts are mapped to Word2Vec embeddings via the concept dictionary. Cosine similarity with target noun embeddings from the input prompt yields predicted segmentation maps.

**Causal Interventions:**
- *Spatially targeted:* Amplify concept vectors for target objects in specified image regions; suppress elsewhere. This directly modifies the residual stream delta.
- *Global:* Add scaled concept vectors uniformly across spatial locations to manipulate style. Normalization adapts intervention strength per spatial location based on latent norms.

## Results & Highlights

- **Composition prediction:** At t=1.0 (first step), mid_block SAE features predict object layout with IoU ~0.26. By t=0.5, predictions saturate (~0.34), and by t=0.0, accuracy rivals the ground-truth segmentation pipeline. The up_block consistently gives the best segmentation accuracy; mid_block is limited by lower spatial resolution (8x8 vs 16x16).
- **Early-stage interventions (t=0.6-1.0):** Spatial interventions successfully restrict target objects (bee, book, dog) to specified quadrants. However, large intervention strengths are needed to overcome leakage from U-Net skip connections and CFG null-text features. Global style interventions fail -- they alter composition instead of style.
- **Middle-stage interventions (t=0.2-0.6):** Spatial composition interventions fail (composition is locked). Global style interventions succeed: cartoon look, sandy beach texture, and painting style are effectively applied without changing layout. Intervention strength controls style intensity.
- **Late-stage interventions (t=0.0-0.2):** Both composition and style interventions are largely ineffective, producing only minor textural changes.
- **Concept categories discovered:** Local semantics (object-level segmentation), global semantics (style/ambiance), and context-free activations (optimization artifacts at image borders/corners).
- **Cond vs. uncond features:** Text-conditioned features yield more accurate composition predictions than null-text features, likely due to richer semantic content from cross-attention.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Moderate-High):** The application of SAEs to multi-step diffusion models (rather than single-step distilled models) with per-timestep analysis is a meaningful and novel extension. The discovery that composition is determined at step 1 is genuinely surprising and valuable. However, SAEs themselves are a well-established tool in LLM interpretability, and the intervention techniques are conceptually straightforward projections in the SAE latent space.

**Rigor (Moderate):** The composition prediction experiments are systematic across blocks, timesteps, and conditioning types. However, the evaluation relies on a zero-shot segmentation pipeline as ground truth, which the authors acknowledge can be less accurate than the predicted masks themselves, potentially inflating or deflating IoU numbers. The intervention experiments are primarily qualitative (visual examples with a few objects and styles), lacking large-scale quantitative benchmarks or user studies. The number of evaluated objects (bee, book, dog) and styles (3 CIDs) is small.

**Reproducibility (Moderate-Low):** No code release is mentioned. The methodology requires training 18 separate SAEs (3 blocks x 2 conditioning types x 3 timesteps), each on 1.5M prompts, which is computationally expensive. Hyperparameters are provided, but the concept dictionary construction depends on multiple external models (RAM, Grounding DINO, SAM) whose exact versions/checkpoints are not fully specified.

**Significance (Moderate-High):** The temporal hierarchy finding (composition -> style -> texture) is important for the community's understanding of diffusion dynamics and has practical implications for controllable generation. The concept dictionary approach is a useful alternative to LLM-based labeling.

**Red flags:**
- The IoU metric for early composition prediction (~0.26) is relatively low; while surprising that it is non-zero, the practical utility for precise layout control remains limited.
- The intervention experiments are cherry-picked qualitative demonstrations rather than systematic evaluations. No quantitative success rate or failure analysis is provided.
- The limitation of U-Net skip connections causing "leakage" is acknowledged but not rigorously measured.
- Only SD v1.4 is studied; generalization to newer architectures (SDXL, DiT-based models like SD3/Flux) is not tested.

## Strengths

- The central finding -- that composition is encoded before the first denoising step completes -- is genuinely novel and counterintuitive, providing real insight into diffusion model internals.
- Training separate SAEs per timestep is methodologically sound and enables the temporal analysis that is the paper's primary contribution.
- The vision-only concept dictionary pipeline is more scalable and less biased than LLM-based labeling approaches used in prior work.
- The paper provides a clear, testable hypothesis about the temporal hierarchy of diffusion representations.
- Comprehensive qualitative visualizations with activation maps and intervention examples that clearly communicate findings.
- The paper is well-written with clear structure and thorough discussion of related work and limitations.

## Limitations

- Only tested on Stable Diffusion v1.4 (U-Net architecture); results may not transfer to DiT-based architectures that lack skip connections.
- U-Net skip connections cause "leakage" that weakens interventions and necessitates large intervention strengths.
- The concept dictionary is limited to object-level labels via detection/segmentation models; abstract concepts, relationships, and attributes beyond named objects are not captured.
- Word2Vec embeddings for concept matching are simplistic; more powerful embedding models could improve composition prediction accuracy.
- Intervention evaluation is primarily qualitative with a small set of objects and styles.
- The three-timestep sampling (t=1.0, 0.5, 0.0) provides limited temporal resolution; a finer-grained analysis could reveal more nuanced transitions.
- Ground truth segmentation quality may bottleneck reported IoU metrics, as acknowledged by the authors.

## Relevance

本文与 LLM 可解释性研究高度相关：将 NLP 领域成熟的 SAE 框架迁移到视觉生成模型，揭示概念涌现的时间层级结构，为理解多步推理过程中内部表示的演化提供了方法论参考。其中的因果干预技术和概念字典构建思路也可迁移到语言模型的可解释性研究中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the discovered concepts and temporal hierarchy transfer to DiT-based architectures (e.g., Stable Diffusion 3, Flux) that lack U-Net skip connections?
- Can the concept dictionary approach be extended to capture abstract concepts (emotions, artistic styles, spatial relationships) beyond detectable objects?
- What is the minimum number of diffusion steps needed for composition to fully crystallize -- is there a sharp phase transition or a gradual process?
- Could per-timestep SAEs be replaced with a single time-conditioned SAE, and would this enable smoother concept tracking across the denoising trajectory?
- How robust are the interventions to changes in prompts, guidance scales, or sampling strategies (e.g., DDPM vs DDIM)?
- Can the temporal hierarchy insight (composition -> style -> texture) be exploited to design more efficient sampling schedules that skip unnecessary refinement steps?
