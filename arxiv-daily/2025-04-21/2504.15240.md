> **一句话总结：** 本文将集成方法（ensemble）与共形预测（conformal prediction）相结合，首次为 Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) 及其变体 FBKANs、MFKANs 提供具有有限样本覆盖率保证的不确定性量化框架 Conformalized-KANs，在偏微分方程求解等科学机器学习任务中，将覆盖率从集成方法的 28.54% 大幅提升至 95% 以上，且预测区间宽度仍保持竞争力。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes Conformalized-KANs, the first uncertainty quantification (UQ) framework for Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) that provides distribution-free, finite-sample coverage guarantees by combining ensemble variance estimation with split conformal prediction. The method applies to vanilla KANs, Finite-Basis KANs (FBKANs) for multiscale problems, and Multi-Fidelity KANs (MFKANs) for data fusion. Across four experiments -- 1D/2D function approximation, multi-fidelity regression, and PDE wave equation solving -- Conformalized-KANs consistently achieves the target 95% coverage while bare ensemble KANs fail catastrophically (as low as 25.80% for FBKANs on the wave equation). The framework is model-agnostic, requiring only calibration data and ensemble predictions, with no distributional assumptions.

## 核心贡献

- **首个面向 KAN 的共形预测 UQ 框架：** 提出了 Conformalized-KANs，将集成方法获得的方差估计与分裂共形预测（split conformal prediction）相结合，为 KAN 预测提供具有有限样本覆盖率保证的预测区间，填补了 KAN 在不确定性量化方面的空白。
- **统一覆盖三种 KAN 变体：** 框架无缝适用于标准 KAN、用于多尺度问题的 FBKAN（基于域分解）和用于多保真度数据融合的 MFKAN，展示了方法的通用性。
- **非一致性评分设计：** 采用归一化非一致性评分 $s_i = |y_i - \hat{y}_i| / \sigma_i$，利用集成方差 $\sigma_i$ 对残差进行归一化，使预测区间宽度自适应地反映局部不确定性水平。
- **科学计算场景验证：** 在函数逼近、多保真度回归和偏微分方程求解（波动方程）四类实验中全面验证，证明共形预测框架在科学机器学习中的实用性。

## Key Contributions

- **First conformal prediction UQ framework for KANs:** Combines ensemble-based variance estimation with split conformal prediction to produce prediction intervals with finite-sample marginal coverage guarantees (e.g., 95%) without any distributional assumptions about the data or model.
- **Unified framework across KAN variants:** Demonstrates applicability to vanilla KANs, FBKANs (domain-decomposition-based KANs for multiscale problems), and MFKANs (multi-fidelity KANs that fuse low-fidelity and high-fidelity data), showing the method's generality.
- **Normalized nonconformity scores:** Uses $s_i = |y_i - \hat{y}_i| / \sigma_i$ where $\sigma_i$ is the ensemble standard deviation, producing prediction intervals whose width adapts to local uncertainty rather than being uniformly scaled.
- **Comprehensive evaluation in scientific ML settings:** Validates the framework across four experiments spanning 1D/2D function approximation, multi-fidelity regression, and PDE (wave equation) solving, showing dramatic coverage improvements over bare ensemble methods.

## Methodology

The Conformalized-KANs framework operates in two stages:

1. **Ensemble training and variance estimation:** Multiple KAN models (or FBKAN/MFKAN variants) are trained independently with different random initializations. The ensemble mean $\hat{y}(x) = \frac{1}{M} \sum_{m=1}^{M} \hat{y}_m(x)$ serves as the point prediction, and the ensemble standard deviation $\sigma(x) = \sqrt{\frac{1}{M-1} \sum_{m=1}^{M} (\hat{y}_m(x) - \hat{y}(x))^2}$ quantifies epistemic uncertainty.

2. **Split conformal prediction for coverage calibration:** A held-out calibration dataset $\{(x_i, y_i)\}_{i=1}^{n}$ is used to compute nonconformity scores $s_i = |y_i - \hat{y}(x_i)| / \sigma(x_i)$. The $(1-\alpha)$ quantile $q_{1-\alpha}$ of these scores is computed using $\lceil (1-\alpha)(n+1) \rceil / n$ correction. The prediction interval for a new input $x_{n+1}$ is:
$$[\hat{y}(x_{n+1}) - q_{1-\alpha} \cdot \sigma(x_{n+1}), \quad \hat{y}(x_{n+1}) + q_{1-\alpha} \cdot \sigma(x_{n+1})]$$

This guarantees $P(y_{n+1} \in C(x_{n+1})) \geq 1 - \alpha$ under exchangeability. The key insight is that normalizing by the ensemble variance produces adaptive intervals: wider where the ensemble disagrees (high uncertainty), narrower where it agrees.

For FBKANs, domain decomposition is used to partition the input space into subdomains, each handled by a separate KAN. For MFKANs, a two-stage approach fuses low-fidelity and high-fidelity data. In both cases, the conformal prediction wrapper remains identical -- only the underlying model changes.

## Results & Highlights

- **Experiment 1 (1D function, $f(x) = \sin(\pi x/2) + \epsilon$):** Conformalized-KANs achieves 96.10% average coverage vs. 88.30% for ensemble-only. Conformalized-FBKANs achieves 95.30% vs. 52.81% for ensemble-only FBKANs -- a dramatic improvement showing that FBKAN's domain decomposition creates boundary artifacts that ensemble variance alone cannot capture.
- **Experiment 2 (2D function, $f(x,y) = \sin(\pi x) \cdot \cos(\pi y)$):** Conformalized-KANs: 95.50% coverage, Conformalized-FBKANs: 94.85% coverage, both near the 95% target.
- **Experiment 3 (Multi-fidelity):** Conformalized-MFKANs successfully leverages cheap low-fidelity data alongside sparse high-fidelity data, achieving target 95% coverage.
- **Experiment 4 (PDE wave equation):** This is the most striking result. Conformalized-KANs: 95.44%, Conformalized-FBKANs: 95.34%. In contrast, bare ensemble KANs achieve only 28.54% and ensemble FBKANs only 25.80%. This demonstrates that for complex spatiotemporal PDE solutions, ensemble uncertainty estimates are severely overconfident, and conformal calibration is essential.
- **Ablation studies:** Increasing ensemble size from 5 to 20 reduces prediction interval width variance across random splits. Increasing calibration set size from 50 to 500 improves coverage stability (standard deviation of coverage decreases from ~3% to ~1%).
- **Prediction interval width (PIW):** Conformalized intervals are only modestly wider than necessary, indicating efficient coverage. The adaptive nature (wider at boundaries and high-gradient regions) is visually demonstrated.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The paper applies a well-established combination -- ensemble methods for variance estimation plus split conformal prediction for coverage calibration -- to the specific case of KANs. Neither component is novel individually; ensemble-based uncertainty estimation and conformal prediction both have extensive literatures. The contribution lies in (a) being the first to provide UQ for KANs with formal guarantees, and (b) demonstrating that the approach transfers seamlessly to FBKANs and MFKANs. The normalized nonconformity score $s_i = |y_i - \hat{y}_i|/\sigma_i$ is a standard choice in conformal prediction literature. The novelty is therefore incremental -- it is an application paper rather than a methods paper.

**Rigor:** The experimental evaluation is thorough, covering four distinct problem types with appropriate metrics (coverage, PIW). The ablation studies on ensemble size and calibration set size provide practical guidance. However, the paper lacks comparison against alternative UQ methods for neural networks (e.g., Bayesian neural networks, MC dropout, deep ensembles with heuristics, Gaussian processes). The absence of baseline comparisons beyond "ensemble-only" limits the reader's ability to judge whether conformal prediction is the best choice among available UQ approaches for KANs.

**Significance:** The practical significance is clear: the dramatic coverage improvement from 28.54% to 95.44% on the wave equation experiment demonstrates that naive ensemble uncertainty is unreliable for PDE problems, and conformal calibration provides a lightweight fix. However, the theoretical contribution is limited since the coverage guarantee follows directly from standard conformal prediction theory.

**Red flags:**
- No comparison with Bayesian KANs, MC-dropout KANs, or other UQ baselines.
- The paper does not analyze computational cost of the conformal calibration step or discuss scalability to very high-dimensional PDE problems.
- Coverage guarantees require exchangeability, which may not hold for time-dependent PDEs -- this limitation is acknowledged but not addressed.
- The experiments use relatively low-dimensional problems (1D-2D); scalability to industrially relevant 3D PDE problems is not demonstrated.

## Strengths

- First systematic UQ framework for KANs with formal coverage guarantees, filling an important gap in the KAN literature.
- Dramatic and consistent improvement in coverage across all four experiments, with the PDE experiment showing the most compelling results (28.54% to 95.44%).
- Simple and practical: the conformal prediction wrapper adds minimal computational overhead beyond the ensemble training that is already standard practice.
- Unified treatment of three KAN variants (KAN, FBKAN, MFKAN) demonstrates the generality of the approach.
- Well-designed ablation studies providing practical guidance on ensemble size and calibration set size trade-offs.
- Adaptive prediction intervals that widen at domain boundaries and high-gradient regions, producing visually and physically meaningful uncertainty bands.

## Limitations

- No comparison with alternative UQ methods (Bayesian approaches, MC dropout, Gaussian processes) -- the only baseline is the bare ensemble, making it hard to assess whether conformal prediction is optimal for this setting.
- Exchangeability assumption may be violated for time-dependent and spatially correlated PDE solutions, limiting the formal coverage guarantee in the most practically important applications.
- All experiments are low-dimensional (1D or 2D); scalability to 3D PDE problems common in engineering applications (fluid dynamics, structural mechanics) is not demonstrated.
- The framework inherits KAN's current limitations: training instability, sensitivity to grid size, and computational cost that scales with B-spline grid resolution.
- No theoretical analysis of how KAN-specific properties (e.g., B-spline basis choice, grid resolution) affect the quality of the conformal prediction intervals.
- The calibration step requires held-out data, which may be scarce in expensive PDE simulation settings -- the paper does not discuss the minimum calibration set size needed for reliable coverage.

## Relevance

本文提出的不确定性量化框架与科学机器学习研究直接相关。共形预测方法为物理信息神经网络和 KAN 等新兴架构提供了无需分布假设的覆盖率保证，这对高 stakes 科学计算应用（如工程仿真、气候建模）中的可靠性评估具有重要价值。论文对偏微分方程求解中集成方法覆盖率严重不足的揭示（28.54%），为从事 SciML 可靠性研究的工作者提供了重要警示。FBKAN 域分解与共形预测的结合也为多尺度问题的不确定性量化提供了新思路。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does Conformalized-KANs perform on 3D PDE problems (e.g., Navier-Stokes, elasticity) where calibration data is expensive to obtain and the exchangeability assumption is more likely to be violated?
- Can adaptive conformal prediction methods (e.g., conformalized quantile regression) improve upon the split conformal approach for heteroscedastic scientific data?
- What is the sensitivity of coverage to the B-spline grid resolution and the choice of KAN architecture hyperparameters (layer width, spline order)?
- How does the framework compare against Bayesian KANs or MC-dropout KANs in terms of both coverage calibration and computational cost?
- For time-dependent PDEs, can temporal conformal prediction or sequential conformal methods maintain coverage guarantees without exchangeability?
