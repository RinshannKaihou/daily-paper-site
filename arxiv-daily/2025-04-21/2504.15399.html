Improving Learning to Optimize Using Parameter Symmetries

arXiv: 2504.15399

Date: 2025-04-21

https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.15399

一句话总结： 本文从理论和实验两方面分析了一种利用参数空间对称性（teleportation）增强学习式优化器（L2O）的方法，证明了即使不找到最优对称变换，该方法也能局部近似牛顿法，但在低维基准测试中vanilla L2O表现更优，加入动量后才能改善收敛。
TL;DR: This paper provides theoretical analysis showing that teleportation-based symmetry transformations in learning-to-optimize (L2O) locally approximate Newton's method, and proves the teleportation strategy is learnable on convex quadratics. However, empirical results on 2D benchmark functions reveal that vanilla L2O consistently outperforms teleportation-augmented variants, and only adding learned momentum restores improvements. A workshop paper (ICLR 2025) that is honest about negative results while offering useful theoretical grounding for symmetry-aware meta-optimization.

核心贡献

  • 证明了在凸函数场景下，即使不找到最优群元素，teleportation 操作也能局部模拟牛顿法的行为（Proposition 4.1），说明学习对称变换能带来类似二阶优化的好处
  • 在二维凸二次函数上给出了可证明的学习性结果（Section 4.2）：L2O 优化器能够通过梯度更新正确学到旋转角度，使参数沿梯度更大的方向移动
  • 引入了一组具有 SO(2) 对称性的低维测试函数族（广义 Booth 函数和广义 Rosenbrock 函数），用于系统评估 L2O with teleportation
  • 在 teleportation 增强的 L2O 基础上引入了基于 LSTM 的动量学习机制，证明了动量可以进一步提升 meta-optimizer 的收敛表现
  • 诚实地报告了 teleportation 在多种设置下的负面结果（vanilla L2O 优于 teleportation），并分析了其失败原因

Key Contributions

  • Proved that for convex functions, teleportation's effect on gradient norm increase mirrors Newton's method's orthogonal component (Proposition 4.1), providing theoretical justification for why learning symmetry transformations benefits L2O even without finding the optimal group element
  • Demonstrated learnability of symmetry transformations on convex quadratics: derived a closed-form update for the rotation parameter that consistently moves iterates toward the shorter axis of the level-set ellipse where gradients are larger
  • Introduced a benchmark suite of 2D functions with SO(2) symmetry (generalized Booth and generalized Rosenbrock families) for systematic evaluation of L2O with teleportation
  • Showed that augmenting teleportation-based L2O with LSTM-learned momentum coefficients improves convergence over both vanilla GD and teleportation-only L2O
  • Provided honest empirical analysis showing vanilla L2O outperforms teleportation-augmented variants on the 2D benchmarks, highlighting that learning good teleportation strategies is itself a hard problem

Methodology

The paper combines theoretical analysis with empirical evaluation:

Theoretical Analysis (Section 4):

  • Newton-teleportation connection: For a twice-differentiable convex function with invertible Hessian, the authors decompose Newton's update direction $v_2 = -H^{-1}\nabla L$ into a gradient component $v_\parallel$ (along $v_1 = -\nabla L$) and an orthogonal component $v_\perp$. They prove that the directional derivative of $\|\nabla L\|^2$ along $v_\perp$ is non-negative (Proposition 4.1), meaning Newton's method inherently increases gradient norm via its symmetry-like component -- the same effect teleportation aims for.
  • Learnability proof: For a convex quadratic $f(x) = x^T A x$ with $A = B^T B$, parameterizing teleportation as a rotation $g_\theta(x) = B^{-1} R_\theta B x$ (which preserves $f$), they derive a closed-form update for $\theta$ showing it rotates iterates toward the shorter axis of the level-set ellipse where gradients are larger.

Empirical Evaluation (Section 5):

  • Benchmark design: Two families of 2D functions with SO(2) symmetry -- generalized Booth (convex) and generalized Rosenbrock (nonconvex) -- enabling visualization and fast meta-training.
  • L2O setup: LSTM-based meta-optimizers trained on multiple trajectories with unrolling, comparing vanilla L2O vs. L2O with learned teleportation vs. L2O with teleportation + gradient updates on $\theta$.
  • Momentum augmentation (Algorithm 3): A two-LSTM architecture where one LSTM outputs the momentum coefficient and the other outputs the teleportation group element, with weight updates combining momentum velocity and teleportation transformations.

Results & Highlights

  • Negative result on 2D benchmarks: Across all four benchmark settings (fixed/variable Booth and Rosenbrock), vanilla L2O consistently outperformed teleportation-augmented L2O. The teleportation step often hurt rather than helped, confirming that maximizing gradient norm via symmetry can sometimes hinder optimization.
  • Momentum helps: Adding LSTM-learned momentum to teleportation-based L2O significantly improved convergence over both vanilla GD and teleportation-only L2O on a classification task, suggesting the original algorithm had room for improvement.
  • Theoretical insight: The Newton's method connection (Proposition 4.1) holds for convex functions but notably does not hold for nonconvex functions, which the authors demonstrate with a counterexample -- an important caveat for neural network optimization.
  • Practical observation: The authors note that teleportation does not guarantee faster convergence even in convex settings, and when it is frequently detrimental within a task distribution, the learned optimizer degrades to vanilla L2O performance.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. The theoretical connection between teleportation and Newton's method is a meaningful contribution, but the empirical results are largely negative. The momentum augmentation is a straightforward extension. The paper builds heavily on Zhao et al. (2024), and the theoretical results are limited to convex settings.
  • Rigor: High for the theoretical analysis -- Proposition 4.1 comes with a clean proof, and the learnability example in Section 4.2 is well-derived. The authors are commendably honest about the failure cases of teleportation, which is unusual and valuable.
  • Reproducibility: Good. Code is available at https://github.com/Rose-STL-Lab/L2O-Symmetry. The benchmark functions are clearly defined with SO(2) symmetry.
  • Significance: Limited by scale. All experiments are on 2D functions or relatively small classification tasks. The negative empirical results on the 2D benchmarks somewhat undermine the paper's thesis, though the theoretical insights retain value. The momentum improvement on the classification task is promising but not extensively evaluated.
  • Red flags: (1) The core empirical finding is that teleportation hurts L2O on the proposed benchmarks, which contradicts the motivating premise. (2) The Newton's method analogy breaks down for nonconvex functions, which is the practically relevant setting. (3) Workshop paper status suggests the contribution was not deemed sufficient for a main conference venue. (4) The momentum result (Figure 7) lacks error bars or statistical significance testing, and only 5 test trajectories are used for evaluation.

Strengths

  • Honest reporting of negative results -- the authors explicitly state that vanilla L2O outperforms teleportation variants on their benchmarks, which is scientifically valuable
  • Clean theoretical analysis connecting teleportation to second-order methods, providing intuition for why symmetry transformations could help
  • Well-designed benchmark functions with provable SO(2) symmetry properties
  • The learnability example (Section 4.2) is elegant and provides concrete geometric intuition
  • Clear writing and well-organized structure
  • Open-source code for reproducibility

Limitations

  • All experiments are on 2D test functions (Booth/Rosenbrock families) and one small-scale classification task -- scalability to realistic neural network training is untested
  • The Newton's method analogy (Proposition 4.1) only holds for convex functions, not for the nonconvex landscapes typical of deep learning
  • The momentum augmentation experiment (Figure 7) uses only 5 test trajectories and lacks error bars or confidence intervals
  • No comparison with modern learned optimizers (e.g., VeLO, learned Adam variants) on standard benchmarks
  • The paper does not address computational overhead of learning teleportation parameters during meta-training
  • The connection to RL (Section 6) is speculative and not explored experimentally

Relevance

本文通过分析参数空间对称性对优化器的影响，为理解神经网络训练动态提供了新视角。虽然主要聚焦于 meta-optimization 而非直接研究 LLM 训练稳定性，但其关于对称性如何影响梯度景观的理论洞察对理解大规模模型训练中的优化行为有间接参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does teleportation-augmented L2O scale to high-dimensional neural network training (e.g., transformers with hundreds of millions of parameters)?
  • Can the convex Newton-teleportation connection be extended to nonconvex settings using local convexity assumptions or trust-region methods?
  • What is the computational overhead of learning teleportation parameters compared to the convergence gains, and does this trade-off improve with dimensionality?
  • Could adaptive teleportation schedules (e.g., teleport only when gradient norm is below a threshold) mitigate the negative effects observed in the 2D benchmarks?
  • How does teleportation interact with other optimization techniques like learning rate warmup, weight decay, or gradient clipping in the L2O framework?
