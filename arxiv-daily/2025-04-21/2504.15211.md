> **一句话总结：** 本文提出基于依赖Dirichlet过程(DDP)混合模型的多重性感知框架，通过在解码参数空间上建模有害行为的完整分布，定义解码Rashomon集来量化多近优操作点的体积与分歧，并利用贝叶斯深度学习代理的主动采样减少40%的评估调用，实现了对生成式AI安全性评估中尾部风险、公平性差距和利益相关者分歧的全面量化。

> **TL;DR:** This position paper argues that single-point toxicity scores are inadequate for evaluating generative AI safety, because different decoding configurations (temperature, top-p, repetition penalty) with similar mean toxicity can have dramatically different tail risks and demographic disparities. The author proposes a multiplicity-aware framework using dependent Dirichlet process (DDP) mixtures to model multi-modal harm surfaces across the full decoding knob space, formalizes "decoding Rashomon sets" to quantify regions of near-optimal risk, and introduces stakeholder simulation with active sampling to reduce evaluation calls by 40%. Experiments on Llama-2 with RealToxicityPrompts show that CVaR and worst-slice gaps spike at high temperature and low top-p -- insights invisible to average toxicity scores. To be presented at ACM CHI 2025 workshop STAIG.

## 核心贡献

- **提出解码Rashomon集的概念**：将Rashomon效应从模型选择扩展到生成式AI的解码配置空间，定义了近优风险区域并量化其体积(Vol_e)和利益相关者之间的分歧指数(Disagree)，揭示了传统单点评估掩盖的多重性问题。
- **开发依赖Dirichlet过程(DDP)混合模型**：设计带有 harms 条件下 stick-breaking 权重的 DDP 混合模型，能够捕捉解码参数和提示特征共同作用下的多模态伤害分布，克服了参数化逻辑回归和高斯过程会平滑掉不同失败模式的缺陷。
- **引入主动采样与利益相关者模拟管线**：使用贝叶斯深度神经网络作为代理模型引导解码空间探索，配合 Thompson 采样和共形预测包装器，在保证有限样本覆盖的同时将评估调用减少40%；同时设计基于真实数据集参数化的模拟利益相关者进行敏感性分析。
- **将利益相关者偏好作为一等公民变量**：利益相关者的提示分布、解码策略和风险阈值直接影响风险计算，通过模拟不同人口群体的敏感度、偏见和噪声来评估安全性结论的鲁棒性。
- **提出多重风险函数体系**：包括条件风险价值(CVaR)衡量尾部风险、最差分片差距(Worst-slice gap)衡量公平性、安全体积和Rashomon集隶属概率，为不同利益相关者生成定制化风险报告。

## Key Contributions

- **Decoding Rashomon sets**: Extends the Rashomon effect from model selection to the decoding configuration space of generative models, defining epsilon-Rashomon sets as regions where a risk functional (mean, CVaR, or disparity gap) is within epsilon of its optimum, and proposing volume and disagreement metrics to quantify multiplicity.
- **Dependent Dirichlet Process (DDP) mixture model**: A novel nonparametric model with harm-conditioned stick-breaking weights that varies with both decoding settings and prompt features, capable of capturing distinct failure modes that parametric alternatives (Gaussian processes, logistic surfaces) would smooth over.
- **Active sampling pipeline with Bayesian deep learning surrogates**: Uses Bayesian deep neural networks to guide exploration of the decoding knob space, combined with Thompson sampling and conformal prediction wrappers for finite-sample validity, reducing required evaluations by 40% compared to uniform designs.
- **Stakeholder simulation grounded in public data**: Simulates diverse stakeholders by grounding sensitivity, bias, and noise parameters in empirical annotation distributions from datasets like Civil Comments, enabling rigorous sensitivity analysis without requiring real human raters.
- **Multi-faceted risk reporting**: Provides heatmaps of mean harm surfaces, CVaR, safe volumes, Rashomon set membership probabilities, and stakeholder-specific risk distributions, along with synthetic exemplars of failure modes.

## Methodology

**Problem formalization.** The paper models the generative AI evaluation problem as follows: given decoding knobs x (temperature, top-p, repetition penalty) in space X, prompts p from stakeholder distributions, and outputs y from the stochastic generative model, estimate the distribution of harm H(y) across the full (p, x) space. Each stakeholder s is characterized by their prompt distribution, knob policy, and risk threshold.

**DDP mixture model.** The core technical contribution is a dependent Dirichlet process mixture where stick-breaking weights are conditioned on harm levels and vary smoothly with decoding knobs and prompt features. Unlike parametric baselines, the DDP can discover and represent multiple distinct failure modes in the risk surface. Inference uses joint variational inference with a factorized family q(Theta) maximizing the ELBO.

**Risk functionals.** Beyond mean harm mu(p,x), the paper introduces: (1) CVaR_alpha(p,x) measuring expected harm in the worst (1-alpha) fraction of outcomes; (2) worst-slice gap Gap(x) measuring maximum demographic disparity; and (3) stakeholder-specific expected harm R_s integrating over each stakeholder's knob policy.

**Decoding Rashomon sets.** The epsilon-Rashomon set R_epsilon = {x in X : R(x) <= R* + epsilon} defines near-optimal decoding regions. Volume Vol_epsilon measures the fraction of knob space that is "safe enough," while Disagree(x) = Var_s(R_s(x)) quantifies stakeholder disagreement at each point.

**Stakeholder simulation.** Synthetic stakeholders are instantiated with group-specific topic mixtures (Dirichlet priors), knob policies (Beta distributions over continuous knobs), and sensitivity/bias/noise parameters grounded in the Civil Comments dataset. This enables stress-testing safety conclusions against diverse value systems without real human raters.

**Calibration.** Automated toxicity detectors (e.g., ToxicityBERT) are calibrated against simulated human ratings using ordinal or pairwise models, with separate calibration and evaluation sets to avoid double-dipping. Conformal prediction wrappers provide finite-sample coverage guarantees.

**Active sampling.** A Bayesian deep learning surrogate approximates the risk surface and guides evaluation via Thompson sampling, prioritizing (p, x) pairs with high posterior uncertainty. This sequential design reduces evaluation calls by 30-40% compared to uniform sampling.

## Results & Highlights

- **Synthetic ground-truth study**: The DDP mixture recovers the true Rashomon set and yields calibrated uncertainty intervals. The Gaussian Process baseline captures smooth trends but fails to identify multi-modal risk structure, underestimating tail risks in "catastrophic" modes. The Beta-Binomial baseline cannot model multi-modal harm distributions at all.
- **LLM study (Llama-2 on RealToxicityPrompts + BOLD)**: Mean toxicity appears low across many decoding settings, but CVaR and worst-slice gaps spike sharply at high temperature and low top-p. The safe volume shrinks dramatically when tail risk is considered instead of just mean risk.
- **Stakeholder heterogeneity**: Sensitive stakeholders have smaller Rashomon sets than lenient ones, and the disagreement index peaks near the boundary of the safe region, indicating that stakeholder consensus breaks down precisely where safety decisions matter most.
- **Active sampling efficiency**: The BDL surrogate with Thompson sampling reduces evaluation calls by 30% while maintaining the same posterior width compared to uniform sampling.
- **Volume-Disagreement trade-off**: A novel visualization plots safe volume against stakeholder disagreement, where ideal configurations lie in the high-volume, low-disagreement region. This trade-off is invisible to single-point evaluation.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Moderate):** The idea of applying Bayesian nonparametrics to generative AI safety evaluation is timely but builds heavily on existing concepts. The Rashomon set idea is imported from interpretable ML (Breiman 2001, Rudin et al. 2024). DDP mixtures are well-established in Bayesian nonparametrics literature. The main novelty lies in the synthesis -- combining these elements with stakeholder simulation and active sampling into a unified pipeline for safety evaluation. However, the individual components are not new.

**Rigor (Moderate-Low):** The theoretical framework is well-formulated but the experimental validation has significant gaps. The synthetic experiment uses a contrived sinusoidal mixture that may favor the DDP. The LLM experiment evaluates only one model (Llama-2) on one toxicity benchmark with simulated (not real) stakeholders. There are no error bars, no multiple seeds, and no comparison with real human evaluation. The conformal prediction guarantees are mentioned but their tightness is not empirically validated. The claim of "40% reduction in evaluations" comes from a single experimental run.

**Reproducibility (Low-Moderate):** No code repository is mentioned. The paper references specific datasets (RealToxicityPrompts, BOLD, Civil Comments) but does not provide enough implementation detail to reproduce the DDP fitting procedure, variational inference setup, or stakeholder simulation parameters. The paper is only 7 pages long for a framework of this complexity, suggesting significant detail is omitted.

**Significance (Moderate):** The position that single-point evaluation is inadequate is well-argued and timely. The multi-stakeholder perspective on AI safety is valuable and underexplored. However, the paper reads more as a research agenda or framework proposal than a fully validated system. Many components (stakeholder simulation, active sampling) are demonstrated in limited settings and their real-world applicability remains unproven.

**Red flags:**
- Single-authored paper presenting a complex multi-component framework with limited empirical validation.
- Workshop paper (CHI 2025 STAIG workshop), not a peer-reviewed main conference paper.
- No code release mentioned. The reproducibility of the DDP mixture fitting, stakeholder simulation, and active sampling pipeline is questionable without code.
- The stakeholder simulation is "grounded in" but not directly validated against real human annotations -- it is a simulation exercise whose conclusions about real stakeholder behavior are speculative.
- The paper contains two different titles (the arXiv title is shorter position paper; the PDF title is the longer technical paper), which may indicate an evolving or incomplete submission.
- Only one LLM (Llama-2) is evaluated. Generalization to other models is not demonstrated.
- The 43KB PDF size (noted on arXiv) and 7-page length are very short for the claimed contributions, suggesting either significant content is deferred to appendices or the paper is preliminary.

## Strengths

- The core argument -- that single-point toxicity scores obscure tail risks, demographic disparities, and multiple near-optimal operating points -- is compelling and well-articulated.
- The formalization of "decoding Rashomon sets" is a clean conceptual contribution that connects safety evaluation to the well-studied multiplicity literature.
- The DDP mixture model is a principled choice for capturing multi-modal harm distributions and is well-justified against parametric alternatives.
- The stakeholder simulation framework, while unvalidated against real data, is thoughtfully designed with parameters grounded in empirical annotation distributions.
- The Volume-Disagreement visualization is an intuitive and useful tool for communicating trade-offs between safety and consensus.
- The active sampling strategy provides practical efficiency gains that could make the approach feasible for real deployments.

## Limitations

- **No real human evaluation**: All results rely on simulated stakeholders calibrated to public datasets. Whether these simulations reflect real stakeholder behavior is entirely untested.
- **Single model evaluation**: Only Llama-2 is evaluated. The framework's behavior with other model families, sizes, or architectures is unknown.
- **Limited to toxicity**: The paper focuses on toxicity as the harm metric. Other safety-relevant harms (hallucination, privacy leakage, bias in information retrieval) are mentioned but not evaluated.
- **Scalability concerns**: The DDP mixture inference over a continuous knob space with many prompts could be computationally expensive. The paper does not discuss computational costs or wall-clock time.
- **Workshop paper status**: The depth of peer review for workshop papers is typically lower than for main conference papers. The technical claims have not undergone the same level of scrutiny.
- **No comparison with simpler alternatives**: It is unclear whether a simpler approach (e.g., kernel density estimation or quantile regression) combined with stakeholder weighting would achieve similar results at lower computational cost.
- **Conformal guarantees are assumed, not empirically validated**: The paper claims finite-sample coverage via conformal prediction but does not demonstrate that the coverage holds in practice.

## Relevance

本文与LLM推理评估和计算可靠性有部分关联：其核心关注点是生成式AI安全性评估中的不确定性量化，提出的多重性感知框架和尾部风险度量方法对理解LLM在不同解码配置下的行为鲁棒性有参考价值，但主要侧重于安全评估而非模型训练或推理过程的内部机制。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the DDP mixture and Rashomon set analyses scale to higher-dimensional knob spaces (e.g., including system prompts, few-shot examples, or chain-of-thought toggles)?
- Can the stakeholder simulation be validated against real human studies? What is the gap between simulated and actual stakeholder risk assessments?
- How does the framework handle non-stationary harm distributions, e.g., when models are updated or fine-tuned after deployment?
- Could the decoding Rashomon set concept be integrated into training-time alignment (e.g., RLHF) to optimize for robust rather than average safety?
- What are the computational costs of fitting the DDP mixture for large-scale evaluations (millions of prompts), and can the approach be made practical for real-time safety monitoring?
