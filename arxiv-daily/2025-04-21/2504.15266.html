Roll the dice & look before you leap: Going beyond the creative limits of next-token prediction

arXiv: 2504.15266

Date: 2025-04-21

https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.15266

一句话总结： 本文设计了一套最小化算法任务来量化LLM的创造力极限，系统性地证明了next-token预测在开放式创造性任务中存在短视性（导致记忆化严重、多样性不足），而多token预测（teacherless训练和扩散模型）以及输入层噪声注入（seed-conditioning）能显著提升算法创造力，该工作已被ICML 2025接收为口头报告。
TL;DR: This paper constructs minimal algorithmic tasks (sibling/triangle discovery, circle/line construction) that abstract real-world creative tasks like wordplay and problem design. On these tasks, the authors demonstrate that next-token prediction (NTP) is myopic -- it memorizes training data heavily and produces collapsed, unoriginal outputs. Multi-token approaches (teacherless training and discrete diffusion) achieve up to 5x higher "algorithmic creativity" (coherent + unique + novel generations). Additionally, injecting random noise at the input layer via "seed-conditioning" produces comparable diversity to temperature sampling, even with greedy decoding. The paper provides new theoretical arguments for why NTP fails on tasks requiring implicit stochastic planning, extending prior work (Bachmann & Nagarajan, 2024) to show a creativity gap rather than just a correctness gap, in simpler 2-token-lookahead tasks, and in tasks where no token permutation is NTP-friendly. Accepted as an oral at ICML 2025.

核心贡献

  • 设计了一套可控、可量化的最小化开放式算法任务，分别抽象了Boden (2003) 提出的"组合性创造力"（如在知识图谱中发现新的多跳连接）和"探索性创造力"（如构建满足全局约束的新模式）
  • 系统性地论证了next-token预测在需要隐式随机规划的创造性任务中存在短视性：模型倾向于学习"Clever Hans cheat"（利用前缀中暴露的部分信息作为捷径），导致无法学到真正的潜在规划，表现为严重的记忆化和模式坍塌
  • 证明多token预测方法（teacherless训练和离散扩散模型SEDD）在所有四个任务上显著提升算法创造力（最高可达5倍），并减少记忆化
  • 提出seed-conditioning方法：在输入层注入随机噪声前缀，即使使用贪心解码也能产生有意义的多樣性，效果与温度采样相当甚至更好
  • 对NTP的批判超越了Bachmann & Nagarajan (2024)：展示了创造力差距（而非正确性差距），在更简单的2-token前瞻任务中，以及在不存在对NTP友好的token排列的任务中

Key Contributions

  • Designed four minimal, controllable open-ended algorithmic tasks -- Sibling Discovery, Triangle Discovery, Circle Construction, and Line Construction -- that isolate combinational and exploratory creativity from Boden (2003)'s taxonomy
  • Provided empirical and conceptual arguments that next-token prediction is myopic for creative leap-of-thought tasks: NTP learns Clever Hans cheats via gradient starvation, leaving no supervision to learn the latent stochastic plan, resulting in O(m n^2) data requirements vs. the optimal O(m n)
  • Demonstrated that multi-token approaches (teacherless training on Gemma 2B; discrete diffusion SEDD 90M) improve algorithmic creativity by up to 5x and drastically reduce memorization across all tasks
  • Introduced seed-conditioning: prepending arbitrary random character prefixes during training and inference achieves comparable diversity to temperature sampling, even under deterministic greedy decoding
  • Showed the creativity gap persists across hyperparameter sweeps (learning rate, batch size, training steps, task complexity, data size) and extends to real-world summarization tasks (XSUM, CNN/DailyMail)

Methodology

The paper proceeds in three stages:

1. Task Design. Inspired by Boden (2003)'s cognitive science taxonomy, the authors create four algorithmic tasks:

  • Combinational creativity tasks (in-weights graph search): Sibling Discovery requires generating sibling-parent triplets from a bipartite graph stored in model weights; Triangle Discovery requires generating triangles from a general graph. Both require recalling graph structure from weights and planning coherent multi-hop connections.
  • Exploratory creativity tasks (pattern construction): Circle Construction and Line Construction require generating adjacency lists that resolve to circle or line graphs under some permutation. The creative goal is to construct novel permutations, not just novel vertex assignments.

2. Training Paradigms. The authors compare three training approaches:

  • Next-token prediction (NTP): standard teacher-forcing autoregressive training
  • Teacherless multi-token prediction: predicting all tokens simultaneously given only the prompt (with dummy masks replacing ground-truth context), using a hybrid objective
  • Discrete diffusion (SEDD): score entropy discrete diffusion that iteratively refines a fully masked sequence

Models: Gemma v1 (2B), GPT-2 (86M), SEDD (90M), and ~400M variants.

3. Seed-Conditioning. Each training example is paired with an arbitrary random character prefix ("seed"). At inference, novel seeds elicit diverse outputs. This contrasts with temperature sampling (output-layer randomness) by injecting randomness at the input layer.

Metric. Algorithmic creativity = fraction of generated samples that are simultaneously coherent, unique (non-duplicate), and original (not in training set).

Results & Highlights

  • Multi-token prediction consistently outperforms NTP: On Gemma 2B, teacherless training achieves ~5x higher algorithmic creativity on discovery tasks. On GPT-2 (86M), SEDD diffusion outperforms NTP on 3 of 4 tasks.
  • NTP is heavily prone to memorization: Across all tasks, NTP memorizes significantly more training data. The authors hypothesize this is because NTP learns earlier tokens via Clever Hans cheats, losing gradient signal for the latent plan.
  • Seed-conditioning works without temperature: Remarkably, greedy decoding with seed-conditioning produces non-trivial diversity. Longer seeds consistently improve creativity. Seed-conditioning achieves comparable or better creativity than temperature sampling on exploratory creativity tasks.
  • Seed-conditioning is orthogonal to multi-token training: The gains from seed-conditioning and multi-token training stack, with the best results achieved by combining both.
  • The NTP gap is a data-inefficiency problem: Unlike the impossibility result in B&N'24, the NTP gap here diminishes with sufficient data. MTP nearly achieves the theoretically expected creativity at scale.
  • Real-world validation: On XSUM summarization, larger multi-token models achieve higher Self-Bleu diversity for a given Rouge quality level.
  • Format sensitivity: Triangle Discovery is surprisingly sensitive to whether triangles are represented as edge lists vs. node tuples -- all methods fail equally on node-based formatting.

Critical Assessment

Novelty (High): The paper makes a genuinely novel contribution by (a) designing minimal tasks that isolate creative computation, (b) demonstrating a creativity gap (not just correctness gap) for NTP, and (c) proposing seed-conditioning as a principled alternative to temperature sampling. The argument that no token reordering can make these tasks NTP-friendly is a meaningful advance over prior work.

Rigor (High): Extensive sensitivity analyses across learning rates, batch sizes, training steps, task complexity, data size, and sampling conditions. Multiple model families and sizes tested. The authors are transparent about limitations and provide detailed appendices. The theoretical argument about O(mn) vs O(mn^2) data requirements is clean and intuitive, though not formally proven.

Reproducibility (Moderate-High): Code is partially available at github.com/chenwu98/algorithmic-creativity. Tasks are synthetic and precisely defined. However, some details about graph construction and formatting sensitivity are nuanced, and results on smaller models (GPT-2 86M) show more variance.

Significance (High): This work provides the most rigorous and controlled evidence to date that NTP is fundamentally limited for creative/open-ended tasks. The seed-conditioning finding is surprising and could influence how diversity is elicited from language models. The minimal task framework could become a standard test-bed.

Red flags / Caveats:

  • The tasks, while clever abstractions, are very far from real-world creative tasks. The gap between "generating triangle structures in a 999-vertex graph" and "writing a novel research idea" is enormous.
  • Seed-conditioning's effectiveness is not well understood theoretically. The authors themselves note: "We do not understand this presently." The arbitrary pairing of seeds to training points working at all is surprising and merits deeper investigation.
  • Results on smaller models are mixed: teacherless training hurts GPT-2 (86M) in some settings, and SEDD underperforms NTP on Sibling Discovery. The gains are concentrated on the larger Gemma 2B model.
  • The real-world validation on summarization is weak -- the diversity gains are slight and inconsistent across datasets/model sizes. Seed-conditioning shows no improvement on summarization at all.
  • Top-K sampling interacts with seed-conditioning results in ways that are not fully disentangled for all settings.

Strengths

  • Exceptionally well-motivated task design grounded in cognitive science (Boden 2003)
  • Clean, intuitive theoretical argument about why NTP is myopic for creative tasks
  • Extensive and thorough experimentation with comprehensive sensitivity analyses
  • Surprising and potentially impactful seed-conditioning finding
  • Honest and detailed limitations section
  • The framework extends prior work (B&N'24) in three distinct ways: creativity vs. correctness, simpler 2-token lookahead, and permutation-invariant tasks
  • Strong writing with clear figures and motivation

Limitations

  • Tasks are extremely simplified abstractions; the gap to real-world creativity is vast
  • Seed-conditioning lacks theoretical justification; it is unclear why it works or when it would generalize
  • Seed-conditioning requires special training, making it less practical than temperature sampling
  • Real-world validation (summarization experiments) shows only marginal and inconsistent gains
  • Results are sensitive to input formatting (edge list vs. node-based representation for Triangle Discovery)
  • The NTP gap diminishes with enough data, suggesting this is a data-efficiency problem rather than a fundamental impossibility
  • No experiments with RL-based post-training, chain-of-thought, or test-time compute scaling -- all highly relevant to creative generation in practice
  • All experiments use relatively small models (up to 2B); scaling behavior is unknown

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does seed-conditioning interact with large-scale pretraining on natural language? Would pretraining with seed-conditioning produce more diverse base models, or does the effect only manifest in controlled synthetic settings?
  2. Can the theoretical argument about NTP myopia be formalized into a rigorous data-complexity lower bound for creative tasks?
  3. How do RL-based methods (e.g., GRPO, PPO used in reasoning models) compare to multi-token training on these algorithmic creativity benchmarks? Do they address the same underlying failure mode or a different one?
  4. Would chain-of-thought prompting that explicitly articulates the "leap of thought" (e.g., "first, I'll pick a parent node, then find two siblings") rescue NTP on these tasks?
  5. Is seed-conditioning related to the concept of "scratchpad" or "thinking tokens" in modern reasoning models? Could the seeds be viewed as latent variables that externalize internal planning?
  6. What happens to the NTP vs. MTP creativity gap as model scale increases to 7B, 70B, or larger? Does the gap persist, narrow, or widen?
