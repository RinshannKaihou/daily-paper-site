> **一句话总结：** Trillion Labs 提出 Cross-lingual Document Attention (XLDA) 机制，通过在预训练时故意打包不同语言文档并允许跨语言注意力，仅用 10% 的非英语数据（约 220B tokens）和 59.4K H100 GPU 小时（约 14.8 万美元）训练出 7B 参数的韩语中心多语言模型，在 27 个基准测试上达到与更大模型竞争的性能。

> **TL;DR:** Trillion Labs introduces Trillion-7B, a 7B-parameter Korean-centric multilingual LLM trained on 2T tokens with only 10% allocated to non-English data. The key innovation is Cross-lingual Document Attention (XLDA), which intentionally packs documents from different languages (e.g., English + Korean) into the same training sequence and permits cross-document, cross-language attention -- effectively creating "architectural code-switching" that transfers knowledge from English to lower-resource languages during pretraining. Combined with two-stage pretraining (warmup-stable-decay with quality-filtered annealing), a tailored tokenizer balancing Korean vocabulary size for inference speed, multi-token prediction, and a Tulu-3-style post-training pipeline (SFT + DPO + RLVR), the model achieves competitive results across 27 benchmarks in four languages. Notably, when converted to a vision-language model (Trillion-LLaVA) using only English visual instruction data, it shows strong zero-shot Korean visual reasoning -- evidence of robust cross-lingual representations. Total training cost is approximately $148K on 256 H100 GPUs.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 XLDA (Cross-lingual Document Attention) 机制**：在预训练时将不同语言的文档打包到同一序列中，并修改注意力掩码允许跨语言文档间的注意力流动，实现高效的跨语言知识迁移。
- **极致 token 效率**：在 2T 训练 tokens 中仅用约 10%（<220B tokens，其中韩语 <180B）用于多语言数据，大幅低于以往韩语 LLM 的多语言数据比例。
- **低成本训练**：仅需 59.4K H100 GPU 小时（约 $148K），使用 256 块 H100 GPU 完成全部训练。
- **定制化 tokenizer 设计**：通过词汇量消融实验发现韩语最优词表大小在 1,500-5,000 tokens 之间，但为了推理速度选择了 24,552 个韩语 tokens，在推理速度和性能之间取得平衡。
- **跨模态泛化验证**：仅使用英文视觉指令数据训练 VLM 后，在韩语视觉推理任务上展现出强大的零样本跨语言能力。

## Key Contributions

- **Cross-lingual Document Attention (XLDA)**: A novel pretraining mechanism that strategically packs multilingual documents into shared sequences and permits cross-language attention, enabling implicit cross-lingual knowledge transfer without explicit parallel data or code-switching.
- **Extreme Token Efficiency**: Achieves competitive multilingual performance with only ~10% of 2T training tokens dedicated to non-English data, significantly less than prior Korean-dominant LLMs.
- **Low-Cost Full Training**: Requires only 59.4K H100 GPU hours (~$148K) using 256 H100 GPUs with FSDP, achieving 42.5% MFU.
- **Tailored Tokenizer with Scaling Law Guidance**: Empirically determines optimal Korean vocabulary size through controlled ablations at the 1.8B scale, informed by vocabulary scaling laws, and strategically chooses 24,552 Korean tokens to maximize inference throughput.
- **Cross-modal Transfer Evidence**: Demonstrates that multilingual pretraining representations transfer to vision tasks, with Trillion-LLaVA showing strong Korean visual reasoning despite English-only visual instruction tuning.

## Methodology

**Architecture**: Standard Transformer decoder with 32 layers, hidden size 4096, FFN dimension 11008, using RoPE (theta=100,000), SwiGLU, RMSNorm, and pre-norm on attention/FFN sublayers. Includes a Multi-Token Prediction (MTP) head for predicting the second-next token during pretraining (discarded afterwards).

**XLDA Mechanism**: Two components work together. (1) *Strategic Batch-level Document Packing*: Each training sequence is constructed to contain contiguous spans from at least two languages (e.g., English + Korean), controlled by a mixing probability rho and a temperature-parameterized sampling distribution with language-specific upsampling. (2) *Selective Attention Masking*: Unlike standard causal masking that blocks attention across document boundaries, XLDA permits full self-attention across language blocks, enabling tokens from different language documents to attend to each other.

**Pre-training Data**: ~2T tokens with an 8.5:1:0.5 ratio (English : Korean : Other/MATH/Code). Quality filtering uses a distilled multilingual embedding model trained on Qwen-72B-Instruct labels (F1=0.734), retaining top 80% English and top 50% multilingual documents.

**Two-stage Pretraining**: Uses a Warmup-Stable-Decay (WSD) scheduler. Phase 1 trains at high learning rate (2e-4) for 1.8T tokens. Phase 2 (annealing) decays LR to 10% over 0.2T tokens using top-20% English and top-10% multilingual data with 3x upsampled multilingual proportion.

**Scaling Law-Guided Recipe**: Uses 1.8B proxy models trained on ~100B tokens to identify emergence points on downstream tasks, then scales hyperparameters using established laws (LR scaling: mu proportional to C^-0.125; vocab scaling: N_v proportional to C^0.42).

**Tokenizer**: Byte-level BPE with 128,256 total tokens (~100K English, 24,552 Korean, rest multilingual/code). The Korean vocab size exceeds the scaling-law-optimal ~13K to gain 35% Korean inference speedup.

**Post-training**: Follows Tulu 3 framework: (1) SFT with ~800K prompt-response pairs filtered via Qwen-2.5-72B judge, using model merging of 3 random-seed checkpoints; (2) DPO with ~200K preference pairs using on-policy + off-policy data; (3) RLVR with GRPO on 10K math problems.

**Context Extension**: Extended from 4K to 32K context using 60B tokens, RoPE base frequency increased to 1M, with 60% long-context + 40% high-quality short data.

## Results & Highlights

- **Competitive Multilingual Performance**: Trillion-7B achieves strong results across 27 benchmarks spanning general reasoning, knowledge (MMLU, KMMLU, GMMLU), math, coding, and instruction-following in English, Korean, Japanese, and Chinese. It advances the Pareto frontier in the compute-vs-performance trade-off.
- **Cross-lingual Consistency**: On Global-MMLU parallel questions, Trillion achieves superior E(T)->K(T) consistency (correct English prediction leading to correct Korean prediction) compared to leading multilingual models, confirming robust cross-lingual knowledge transfer.
- **Vision-Language Transfer**: Trillion-LLaVA, trained only on English VL instruction pairs, outperforms other VLMs on Korean visual reasoning benchmarks, demonstrating that XLDA pretraining creates language-agnostic representations transferable across modalities.
- **Ablation Insights**: (1) Quality filtering of Korean data improves all languages, not just Korean. (2) Annealing with quality + composition changes significantly boosts performance, with English also benefiting from upsampled multilingual/math/code data despite reduced English volume. (3) A smaller-than-expected Korean vocabulary (1,500-5,000 tokens) is optimal for performance at fixed compute, though larger vocab improves inference speed. (4) Data diversity in Korean corpus consistently boosts Korean benchmarks and also improves English/Japanese through mutual transfer.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: The XLDA mechanism is a genuine and elegant contribution -- the idea of deliberately packing cross-lingual documents together and allowing attention across them is simple yet well-motivated. It draws on insights from in-context pretraining and code-switching literature. However, it is essentially a modification of the document packing strategy rather than a fundamentally new architectural component. The tokenizer vocabulary ablation finding (smaller Korean vocab can be better) is a useful empirical contribution that challenges conventional wisdom.

**Rigor**: The paper is well-structured with extensive ablations (data composition, quality filtering, vocabulary size, annealing strategies) and a 1.8B proxy model methodology guided by scaling laws. The cross-lingual consistency metrics (E(T)->K(T) and E(F)->K(T)) are thoughtfully designed. However, some concerns remain: (1) The distilled quality filtering model achieves only F1=0.734, and no analysis is provided on the impact of this imperfect filtering. (2) The annealing ablation (Table 6) shows somewhat mixed results across languages, with the full composition change not always being the best for every language. (3) The paper does not compare XLDA against simpler baselines like random cross-lingual packing without attention masking changes.

**Reproducibility**: The paper provides detailed hyperparameters, training infrastructure specs, and data composition ratios. The model is released on Hugging Face. However, the pretraining data itself is partially proprietary (in-house Korean data), and the quality filtering model is distilled from Qwen-72B-Instruct, which adds a dependency. Full reproducibility would require significant compute (256 H100 GPUs).

**Significance**: This work is significant for the multilingual LLM community, particularly for languages with limited training data. The demonstration that 10% multilingual data suffices with proper architectural design could change how multilingual LLMs are trained. The cross-modal transfer finding (VLM results) adds unexpected value. However, the approach is primarily validated for Korean and to a lesser extent Japanese/Chinese -- generalization to truly low-resource languages remains untested.

**Red Flags**: (1) The paper is labeled "Preview version," suggesting the work may be preliminary. (2) Math and coding data constitute less than 2% of training data, so performance on technical benchmarks is expectedly limited and not the focus. (3) Minimal safety alignment is acknowledged by the authors. (4) The claim of being "the most token-efficient Korean-centric multilingual LLM" is difficult to verify without a controlled comparison holding all other variables constant.

## Strengths

- The XLDA mechanism is simple, well-motivated, and requires no additional parameters or significant compute overhead.
- Extremely cost-efficient training ($148K total) makes frontier-quality multilingual models accessible to smaller organizations.
- Comprehensive evaluation across 27 benchmarks in 4 languages provides a thorough picture of capabilities.
- The cross-lingual consistency analysis (E(T)->K(T), E(F)->K(T)) is a novel and informative evaluation methodology.
- The vision-language transfer experiment is a creative validation of the quality of multilingual representations.
- Thorough ablations on data quality, diversity, vocabulary size, and annealing strategies provide actionable insights for other practitioners.
- Transparent about limitations (math/code performance, safety mechanisms).

## Limitations

- Validated primarily for Korean; effectiveness for truly low-resource or typologically distant languages is unknown.
- Math and coding capabilities are suboptimal due to minimal domain-specific data (<2% of training).
- Safety alignment is acknowledged as insufficient for production use.
- The quality filtering model (F1=0.734) introduces noise that is not analyzed in depth.
- No comparison against alternative cross-lingual transfer methods (e.g., explicit code-switching data, translated pretraining).
- The 4K base context length is relatively short by current standards, though extended to 32K in a second stage.
- Post-training largely follows Tulu 3 without novel contributions in that area.
- The paper does not analyze potential negative interference or catastrophic forgetting effects of XLDA on English performance.

## Relevance

该论文与 LLM 训练稳定性和推理效率高度相关：XLDA 机制本质上是一种训练阶段的注意力策略设计，直接影响模型如何学习跨语言表征；其 1.8B 代理模型确定 emergence point 的方法论和 scaling law 的应用，为高效训练大模型提供了可复现的范式。此外，跨语言一致性评估指标的设计思路对 LLM 可解释性评估具有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would XLDA remain effective for typologically distant language pairs (e.g., English-Arabic, English-Swahili), or does it rely on structural similarity between the source and target languages?
- What is the sensitivity of XLDA to the mixing probability rho and the proportion of cross-lingual vs. monolingual sequences in a batch?
- Can the cross-lingual knowledge transfer enabled by XLDA be quantified at the layer or attention head level -- for instance, do specific heads specialize in cross-lingual alignment?
- How does XLDA interact with Mixture-of-Experts architectures, which the authors mention as a future direction?
- Does the cross-modal transfer finding extend to audio or other modalities beyond vision?
- What is the computational overhead of XLDA during training (attention across longer cross-document spans) compared to standard document-masked pretraining?
