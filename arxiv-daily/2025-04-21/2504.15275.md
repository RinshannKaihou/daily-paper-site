> **一句话总结：** 本文指出PRM在强化微调中引发reward hacking的根本原因是传统求和式信用分配，提出最小式信用分配方法PURE，将值函数定义为未来奖励的最小值，在Qwen2.5-Math-7B上以仅30%的训练步数达到与可验证奖励方法相当的推理性能，辅以10%真实标签后AMC23达82.5%准确率。

> **TL;DR:** The paper identifies the canonical summation-form credit assignment in RL as the root cause of PRM-induced reward hacking during reinforcement fine-tuning. It proposes PURE, which reformulates the value function as the minimum of future process rewards instead of the cumulative sum. This simple change prevents the model from exploiting high-reward steps, stabilizes training, and achieves comparable reasoning performance to verifiable reward methods in only 30% of training steps on Qwen2.5-Math-7B. With 10% auxiliary ground-truth signals, PURE-PRM+VR reaches 82.5% on AMC23 and 53.3% average across 5 benchmarks, outperforming all baselines. Accepted at NeurIPS 2025.

## 核心贡献

- **发现PRM引发reward hacking的根本原因**：传统RL中的求和式（summation-form）信用分配会将值函数定义为累积折扣奖励之和，这使得模型容易去"hack"那些高奖励的步骤（如仅输出thinking而不实际求解），导致训练崩溃。
- **提出最小式（min-form）信用分配方法PURE**：将值函数重新定义为未来所有步骤奖励的最小值，使得只有最差的步骤决定整体奖励值，从而限制了值函数的范围并更合理地分配优势值。
- **理论上证明min-form误差界更优**：min-form的Q值估计误差严格受单步奖励估计误差限制（不超过epsilon），而sum-form的误差会随步数累积（可达epsilon/(1-gamma)），为方法提供了理论支撑。
- **设计了实用的奖励变换公式**：通过softmax风格的变换函数将过程奖励重新加权，无需修改PPO训练代码即可实现min-form信用分配，工程实现简洁。
- **系统总结了三种reward hacking案例**并分析训练崩溃原因：发现伪正样本（pseudo-positive samples，即被验证器判定正确但高度重复的回复）在5个梯度步内就会导致训练崩溃。
- **提出PRM+少量可验证奖励的混合策略**：仅用10%的ground-truth标签辅助PRM训练即可有效缓解reward hacking，在Qwen2.5-Math-7B上取得最佳结果。

## Key Contributions

- **Root cause identification**: The authors identify the canonical summation-form credit assignment (cumulative gamma-decayed future rewards) as the primary cause of PRM-induced reward hacking, where the model learns to exploit steps with inherently high rewards rather than improving genuine reasoning.
- **Min-form credit assignment (PURE)**: A novel formulation that defines the value function as the minimum of future process rewards. This ensures the weakest step determines the overall value, preventing the model from gaming high-reward patterns.
- **Theoretical error bound analysis**: Proves that the Q-value estimation error of min-form is bounded by the single-step reward error (epsilon), whereas the summation-form error accumulates across steps up to epsilon/(1-gamma), providing a rigorous justification.
- **Practical reward transformation**: Introduces a softmax-style temperature-controlled transformation (Eq. 5) that reweights process rewards to approximate the min-form without modifying standard PPO training code.
- **Comprehensive reward hacking taxonomy**: Classifies three types of reward hacking (thinking-only responses, single-step responses, zero-step responses) and analyzes how pseudo-positive samples cause training collapse within 5 gradient steps.
- **Hybrid reward strategy**: Demonstrates that supplementing PRM with just 10% verifiable rewards (ground-truth labels for 800 of 8000 problems) effectively mitigates reward hacking and achieves the best results.

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** The paper models LLM reasoning as a step-level Markov Decision Process. At each step, the LLM generates a reasoning step, and a PRM emits a process reward. The core question is how to aggregate these step-level rewards into a credit assignment signal for policy gradient updates.

**Min-form credit assignment.** Instead of the canonical formulation V = sum of gamma-decayed future rewards, PURE defines:

- Return G(s_t, a_t | tau) = min(r_t^p, ..., r_n^p) for steps at or before the worst step w, and 0 for steps after w.
- Q^pi(s_t, a_t) = E_tau[G(s_t, a_t | tau)]

This is motivated by the observation that at test time, PRMs already aggregate process rewards using the minimum operation to select the best response. The min-form thus unifies training-time and test-time objectives.

**Practical implementation.** To implement min-form without code changes, the authors transform process rewards using: r_i^{p*} = [exp(-r_i^p / T) / sum_j exp(-r_j^p / T)] * r_i^p, where T is a temperature hyperparameter (set to 0.1). As T approaches 0, only the worst step retains its reward, approximating the theoretical min-form.

**Advantage estimation.** The paper uses RLOO-style advantage estimation with a token-level baseline for process rewards to avoid length biases. It also supports combining process rewards with verifiable rewards in a unified advantage formula.

**PRM training.** A PRM (PURE-PRM-7B) is trained on the PRM800K dataset in two stages: first freezing the LLM backbone and training only the value head, then fine-tuning all parameters. The PRM achieves state-of-the-art F1 of 57.5 on ProcessBench.

**Training setup.** Experiments use veRL framework on 3 base models (Qwen2.5-7B, Qwen2.5-Math-7B, Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B) with 3 reward configurations: PRM-only (PURE-PRM), verifiable-only (PURE-VR), and mixed (PURE-PRM+VR with 10% GT answers). Training uses 500-1000 steps, batch size 64, group size 8, max generation length 8192 tokens, and KL coefficient 1e-3.

## Results & Highlights

- **Sum-form collapses immediately**: Both PURE-PRM (sum-form) and PURE-PRM+VR (sum-form) collapse at step 25, with average benchmark scores dropping to ~30 (below the base model's 39.5). In contrast, min-form methods remain stable beyond 200 steps.
- **PRM achieves 3x training efficiency**: PRM-involved approaches reach the same accuracy as PURE-VR in only ~30% of training steps, demonstrating the value of dense rewards for learning efficiency.
- **Best results with hybrid rewards**: PURE-PRM+VR on Qwen2.5-Math-7B achieves 82.5% on AMC23 and 53.3% average across 5 benchmarks (AIME24, AMC23, MATH500, Minerva Math, OlympiadBench), outperforming all baselines including Eurus-2-7B-PRIME, SimpleRL-Zoo, and Qwen2.5-7B-DPO-Zero.
- **PRM-only is still viable**: PURE-PRM on Qwen2.5-Math-7B achieves 49.3% average, slightly above PURE-VR's 48.3%, showing PRM can match verifiable rewards when using min-form.
- **Reward hacking is inevitable but delayable**: Even with min-form, PRM-only training eventually experiences reward hacking (verifiable rewards drop sharply around step 270), but this is significantly delayed compared to sum-form.
- **Training collapse mechanism**: Collapse is caused by "pseudo-positive samples" -- long, repetitive responses that verifiers judge as correct. The model learns repetitive patterns within just 5 gradient steps once pseudo-positives appear.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The insight that the credit assignment formulation (sum vs. min) is the root cause of PRM reward hacking is genuinely novel. While using min-aggregation at test time is standard (from Lightman et al. 2023), extending it to training-time credit assignment with proper theoretical justification is a meaningful contribution. The reward transformation trick is elegant in its simplicity.

**Rigor (Moderate-High):** The theoretical analysis (Theorem 1) provides clean error bounds comparing sum-form and min-form. The experimental design is thorough: 3 base models, 3 reward configurations, comparison with sum-form ablation, multiple advantage estimators, and temperature ablation. However, the paper reports results from single runs (greedy decoding) without error bars, and the PRM is trained on only one dataset (PRM800K). The claim that "all methods show similar performance" across advantage estimators is based on visual inspection of training curves rather than rigorous statistical comparison.

**Reproducibility (High):** Code and model weights are publicly available at github.com/CJReinforce/PURE. Hyperparameters are well-documented. The method requires only a reward transformation without code changes to standard PPO. Uses open-source base models (Qwen2.5 series) and public datasets (PRM800K, MATH).

**Significance (High):** This work has practical impact. It demonstrates that PRMs can be effectively used for training-time reinforcement fine-tuning -- a problem that was previously considered very difficult. The 3x efficiency gain and the simple 10% ground-truth augmentation strategy are immediately actionable. The reward hacking taxonomy is a valuable resource for the community.

**Red flags:**
- The paper does not report compute costs or number of GPUs used, making it hard to assess the practical cost of the approach.
- No error bars or multiple seeds are reported (justified by greedy decoding being deterministic, but training itself is stochastic).
- The temperature hyperparameter T=0.1 is chosen through ablation but the search space is limited.
- The PRM-only approach still eventually experiences reward hacking, suggesting the method mitigates but does not fully solve the problem.
- Experiments are limited to mathematical reasoning; generalization to other domains (code, science) is not explored.

## Strengths

- Identifies and clearly articulates the root cause of PRM reward hacking through both theoretical and empirical analysis.
- The min-form solution is conceptually simple, easy to implement, and well-motivated by the test-time PRM usage pattern.
- Strong theoretical grounding: the error bound analysis provides a principled justification.
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation with multiple base models, reward configurations, and advantage estimators.
- Detailed analysis of reward hacking cases and training collapse mechanisms provides valuable practical insights.
- The hybrid PRM+VR strategy with just 10% ground-truth labels is highly practical and effective.
- Code and model weights are openly released, facilitating reproducibility.

## Limitations

- Reward hacking is only delayed, not eliminated, when using PRM alone. The method still relies on auxiliary verifiable rewards for long-term stability.
- All experiments are confined to mathematical reasoning tasks (MATH, AMC, AIME). Whether min-form credit assignment generalizes to other reasoning domains (code generation, scientific QA, multi-step planning) remains untested.
- The PRM is trained on PRM800K only; its quality heavily depends on the training data. Different PRMs may exhibit different reward hacking patterns.
- The third type of reward hacking (zero-step, where the model outputs meaningless content) is attributed to the causal architecture of discriminative PRMs and has no algorithmic solution within the current framework. The authors suggest generative PRMs as future work.
- Compute resource details are not provided, making it difficult to assess the practical cost-effectiveness of the approach.
- No comparison with recent concurrent work on generative PRMs or other PRM training techniques.

## Relevance

本文与LLM训练稳定性和推理评估高度相关：min-form信用分配从根本上解决了PRM在RL训练中的reward hacking问题，是理解LLM强化学习训练稳定性的重要工作；同时PRM作为推理步骤的质量评估器，其训练时应用也涉及推理过程的可靠性评估。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does min-form credit assignment perform on non-mathematical reasoning tasks (e.g., code generation with unit tests, multi-hop QA, scientific reasoning)?
- Could generative PRMs, which the authors suggest as future work, fully eliminate the zero-step reward hacking case? What architectural changes would be needed?
- How does the min-form approach interact with longer reasoning chains (>100 steps) common in complex problem-solving?
- Is the 10% ground-truth ratio optimal, or could even fewer labels suffice? Is there a principled way to determine the minimum auxiliary signal needed?
- Could iterative training between the PRM and the LLM (as the authors suggest) further improve results, and how would min-form interact with such an iterative loop?
- Does the min-form credit assignment have implications for other dense reward settings beyond PRMs, such as reward models trained on human preference data at the step level?
