> **一句话总结：** 本文提出将LLM后训练（SFT、RL、蒸馏）统一为"状态分布塑造"视角——训练数据的状态来源（数据集 vs. 模型自身采样）比损失函数形式更能决定是否遗忘；实验表明，从一个退化的SFT教师出发做在线策略蒸馏（OPD），学生可以在目标任务和保留任务上全面超越教师。

> **TL;DR:** Reframes LLM post-training (SFT, RL, distillation) around the *state distribution* on which supervision is applied, rather than just the loss function. Experiments on Qwen3-0.6B-Base show that (1) mild SFT is safe but stress SFT destroys both target and retention, (2) on-policy distillation from a degraded teacher surpasses that teacher on all metrics, and (3) lightweight on-policy RL improves the target task with negligible forgetting. The key insight: *where* you train matters as much as *what signal* you use.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了后训练的"状态分布"统一视角：将SFT、RL、在线策略蒸馏（OPD）统一为两个轴——状态来源（数据集/教师/学生自身）和信号来源（标签/奖励/教师分布）
- 发现并验证了"学生超越退化教师"现象：从一个被过度SFT导致退化的教师出发，OPD学生在GSM8K、TruthfulQA和MMLU上全面超越教师
- 揭示标量漂移指标（MMD）不足以解释遗忘：相同MMD漂移量下，压力SFT和OPD的保留率差异巨大（0.83 vs 0.95），说明状态来源的质量和局部性才是关键
- 实验表明温和SFT不会遗忘（保留率≈1.0），但压力SFT会同时损害目标任务和通用能力

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes SFT, RL, and OPD under a unified state-distribution framework, decomposing each method into *state source* and *signal source* axes
- Demonstrates that on-policy distillation (OPD) from a degraded stress-SFT teacher produces a student that outperforms the teacher on GSM8K (0.466 vs 0.420), TruthfulQA (0.275 vs 0.245), and MMLU (0.430 vs 0.364)
- Shows that scalar state-drift (MMD) is insufficient to predict forgetting: stress SFT and OPD from the stress teacher have nearly identical MMD (0.01093 vs 0.01092) but vastly different retention ratios (0.83 vs 0.95)
- Provides evidence that mild SFT can improve a target task with essentially zero retention loss, while stress SFT degrades both target and retention performance
- Identifies that continuation-based OPD (teacher generates short rollouts from student states) works while one-step logit-matching OPD collapses (GSM8K drops to 0.040)

## Methodology

The paper uses a controlled experimental setup on a single RTX 3090 GPU with Qwen3-0.6B-Base and LoRA adapters:

- **State distribution framework:** Defines an autoregressive "state" as (prompt, generated prefix) and the state visitation distribution d^π(s) induced by policy π. Post-training methods are decomposed into (1) state source q_k(s) and (2) signal source S(s), with the update rule θ_{k+1} = θ_k - η∇ℓ(π_θ(·|s), z).
- **SFT (off-policy state fitting):** Minimizes token cross-entropy on dataset states — states are fixed and independent of the learner.
- **RL (on-policy local improvement):** Samples trajectories from current policy and applies reward-derived updates on learner-induced states.
- **OPD (teacher-guided on-policy learning):** Student samples states, teacher provides continuations/logits as supervision. Key variant uses teacher continuations from student states (DAgger-style).
- **Experimental conditions:** Mild SFT (1 epoch, lr=1e-4, rank=16), stress SFT (5 epochs, lr=5e-4, rank=64), OPD from both teachers, and lightweight GRPO-style RL. GSM8K is the target task; TruthfulQA and MMLU measure retention.
- **Drift measurement:** Rollout-state MMD with RBF kernel using lexical features, plus centroid distance, sliced Wasserstein, and Jaccard distance.

## Results & Highlights

| Run | GSM8K | TruthfulQA | MMLU | MMD Drift | Forgetting | Retention |
|-----|-------|-----------|------|-----------|------------|-----------|
| Base | 0.448 | 0.300 | 0.436 | — | — | — |
| Mild SFT | 0.512 | 0.295 | 0.444 | 0.00956 | -0.0015 | 1.001 |
| Stress SFT | 0.420 | 0.245 | 0.364 | 0.01093 | 0.0635 | 0.826 |
| OPD from mild SFT | 0.512 | 0.290 | 0.434 | 0.01470 | 0.0060 | 0.981 |
| OPD from stress SFT | 0.466 | 0.275 | 0.430 | 0.01092 | 0.0155 | 0.952 |
| On-policy RL | 0.472 | 0.290 | 0.442 | 0.01098 | 0.0020 | 0.990 |

- **Stress SFT is doubly harmful:** Degrades GSM8K *below* base (0.420 vs 0.448) while destroying retention (0.826).
- **OPD recovers from a bad teacher:** Student from stress-SFT teacher beats that teacher on every metric, despite using it as the sole supervision source.
- **RL is remarkably gentle:** Improves GSM8K by 2.4 points with only 0.002 mean forgetting.
- **One-step OPD catastrophically fails:** GSM8K drops to 0.040, confirming that next-token logit matching is too weak for reasoning tasks.

## Authors & Credibility

Single author (Dong Nie, independent researcher, formerly UNC). The work is honest about its scale limitations and frames results as hypothesis-driven evidence rather than benchmark claims. The writing is clear and the experimental design is well-controlled for a single-GPU study. The connection to DAgger and imitation learning theory is well-grounded.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The state-distribution framing is a genuine conceptual contribution — it's obvious in retrospect but rarely stated this cleanly. The "student surpasses degraded teacher" result is the most striking finding. However, the core idea (on-policy vs. off-policy) is well-known in imitation learning (DAgger, 2011); the contribution is transferring this lens to LLM post-training.
- **Rigor:** The controlled setup is a strength — same base model, same LoRA infrastructure, clear baselines. But the scale is very small (0.6B model, single GPU, 3 evaluation benchmarks). The stress-SFT regime is intentionally extreme and may not reflect typical production settings.
- **Missing ablations:** No investigation of how OPD scales with model size, how continuation length affects results, or how results change with different teacher-student size ratios. The one-step OPD collapse is mentioned but not deeply analyzed.
- **Reproducibility:** Code link provided (GitHub). Experimental details (hyperparameters, data) are clearly specified. Should be reproducible on a single consumer GPU.
- **Significance:** If the state-distribution view holds at scale, it has practical implications for post-training recipe design — particularly the insight that OPD can recover from a bad teacher, which could change how distillation pipelines are designed.
- **Red flags:** The MMD drift metric uses lexical (not semantic) features, which is a weak proxy. The one-step OPD failure (GSM8K = 0.040) is alarming and under-explained — why does it collapse so badly? The paper's title oversells slightly; it's not that tokens don't matter, but that states are an equally important complementary factor.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated conceptual framework that unifies SFT, RL, and distillation
- Striking empirical result: OPD student beating a degraded teacher on all metrics
- Honest about limitations; does not overclaim
- Practically actionable insight (continuation-based OPD > one-step logit matching)
- Controlled experimental design with clear baselines

## Limitations

- Single small model (Qwen3-0.6B); unclear if findings transfer to 7B+ scales
- Limited retention benchmarks (only TruthfulQA and MMLU subset)
- Lexical-feature MMD is a crude drift metric; hidden-state or encoder-based measures could yield different conclusions
- Minimal RL implementation (GRPO-style, not full PPO); may not represent production RL training
- No analysis of computational cost comparison between methods
- Stress SFT is an adversarial setting; the "mild" vs "stress" boundary is not systematically explored

## Relevance

本文提出的"状态分布"视角直接对LLM后训练中SFT、RL和蒸馏的关系提供了统一理解，对于设计更好的后训练流程（尤其是如何在提升目标任务时保持通用能力）具有实践指导意义。OPD从退化教师中恢复的现象，对蒸馏管线设计有重要启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the state-distribution view hold at 7B/70B scale, or do larger models absorb off-policy stress differently due to greater parameter redundancy?
2. How does OPD behave when the teacher is *stronger* than the student (e.g., larger model)? Does the student still surpass the teacher, or does this only work with degraded teachers?
3. What is the optimal continuation length for OPD? Is there a regime between one-step (catastrophic) and full-trajectory that balances density and locality?
4. Can the state-distribution framework predict *which* retention tasks will be most affected by a given post-training method, or only the aggregate magnitude?
5. How do DPO and other preference-optimization methods fit into this framework? Are they on-policy or off-policy in the state-distribution sense?
