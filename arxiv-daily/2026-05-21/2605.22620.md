> **一句话总结：** 本文提出多奖励RLIF框架，通过结合聚类投票奖励与自确定性奖励并引入KL-Cov正则化，解决了无监督强化学习训练LLM推理能力时的奖励作弊和熵坍塌问题，性能接近有监督RLVR方法。

> **TL;DR:** A multi-reward RLIF framework that combines cluster-voting and self-certainty rewards with GDPO normalization and KL-Cov regularization to prevent reward hacking and entropy collapse in unsupervised LLM reasoning training, achieving near-supervised RLVR performance.

## 核心贡献

- 提出多奖励RLIF框架，将训练信号分解为答案级聚类投票奖励和补全级自确定性奖励两个互补组件，有效抑制单一奖励信号导致的退化模式
- 引入GDPO逐通道归一化机制，独立归一化各奖励通道后再加权组合，避免高方差通道主导梯度更新
- 设计KL-Cov正则化策略，仅针对高协方差（top-2%）的熵坍塌驱动token施加KL惩罚，保留98%正常token的探索能力
- 在数学推理和代码生成共7个基准测试上，该方法在持续训练中保持稳定，性能接近有监督GRPO，且无坍塌现象

## Key Contributions

- Propose a multi-reward RLIF framework decomposing the training signal into two complementary components: answer-level cluster voting reward and completion-level self-certainty reward
- Introduce GDPO-based per-channel normalization that independently z-scores each reward channel within the rollout group before combining, preventing variance-based channel dominance
- Design KL-Cov regularization targeting only the top-2% high-covariance tokens responsible for disproportionate entropy reduction, preserving exploration for the remaining ~98% of tokens
- Demonstrate collapse-free training across 7 benchmarks (5 math + 2 code) on Qwen2.5-1.5B and 3B, retaining 89-99% of peak accuracy through 340 training steps

## Methodology

The framework operates within a GRPO-style policy optimization loop. For each prompt, G=7 completions are sampled from the rollout policy. Two intrinsic rewards are computed:

**Cluster Voting Reward (answer-level):** Final answers from all rollouts are grouped into equivalence clusters. Each rollout receives a reward proportional to its cluster size divided by the number of parseable answers, providing a consensus-based signal.

**Self-Certainty Reward (completion-level):** Computed as the average KL divergence from a uniform distribution to the model's next-token distribution across all tokens in the completion, measuring the model's confidence in its generation.

**GDPO Normalization:** Each reward channel is independently z-score normalized within the G-sample group before being combined with equal weights (α=β=0.5) into a single rollout-level advantage. This prevents the higher-variance self-certainty channel from dominating — gradient diagnostics confirm that naive combine-then-normalize silences the cluster channel by an order of magnitude.

**KL-Cov Regularization:** For each batch, token-level covariance between log-probabilities and advantages is computed. The top-2% highest-covariance tokens receive an absolute log-ratio penalty (β_cov=0.05) instead of the standard clipped surrogate, targeting the small subset of tokens driving entropy collapse. The remaining ~98% of tokens use standard GRPO clipping.

The total loss combines the per-token loss with a global reference-KL penalty (γ=0.005).

## Results & Highlights

- **Math benchmarks (step-160, Qwen2.5-1.5B):** Multi-reward + KL-Cov achieves 68.3% GSM8K, 51.6% MATH500, 29.9% MMLU-Pro — close to supervised GRPO-GT (70.0%, 52.8%, 29.7%)
- **Math benchmarks (step-160, Qwen2.5-3B):** 81.4% GSM8K, 62.4% MATH500, 39.1% MMLU-Pro vs. GRPO-GT's 84.2%, 63.0%, 40.7%
- **Stability:** At step 340, multi-reward + KL-Cov retains 88.9% / 92.0% / 93.3% (1.5B) and 95.2% / 97.2% / 99.5% (3B) of peak accuracy on GSM8K / MATH500 / MMLU-Pro
- **Single-reward collapse:** INTUITOR (self-certainty only) collapses to 0% on GSM8K by step 200; cluster-only collapses to 0% by step 140; multi-reward without KL-Cov collapses around step 240
- **Code benchmarks (OOD):** On LiveCodeBench v6 and CRUXEval-O, the method transfers reasoning structure from math-only training, outperforming INTUITOR despite no code training data
- **KL-Cov robustness:** A 200× sweep of β_cov (0.0005 to 0.1) produces only ~2.7pp step-160 accuracy variation, indicating the regularizer is not sensitive to exact tuning

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate. The individual components (cluster voting, self-certainty, GDPO normalization, covariance-aware regularization) are not novel individually. The contribution is the principled combination and the empirical demonstration that complementary rewards with targeted regularization prevents collapse — a finding that is valuable but incremental.
- **Rigor:** High. The paper provides thorough ablations (single-reward decomposition, KL-Cov sweep, GDPO vs. combine-then-normalize, INTUITOR+KL-Cov), gradient-space diagnostics confirming channel dominance mechanisms, and qualitative examples of collapsed vs. stable completions. The stability analysis with peak/retention metrics across 340 steps is well-designed.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Full hyperparameters are reported in Appendix F (Table 6), training uses standard huggingface-trl framework, and code is promised. However, the reliance on 4× GH200 GPUs (480GB total) limits accessibility.
- **Significance:** High for the RLIF subfield. The entropy collapse problem in unsupervised RL for LLM reasoning is well-documented but poorly solved. This work provides a practical, stable solution that approaches supervised performance without any ground-truth labels.
- **Red flags:** (1) Only tested on Qwen2.5-1.5B and 3B — no larger models. (2) The symmetric α=β=0.5 weighting is not systematically tuned. (3) AIME results are near noise floor at 1.5B and KL-Cov slightly hurts at 3B (3.3% vs. 6.7% without KL-Cov on AIME 2024). (4) Training only on MATH split limits claims about generality.

## Strengths

- Comprehensive analysis of failure modes: clearly identifies two distinct collapse mechanisms (answer-mode and token-mode) and shows how complementary rewards address each
- Strong empirical evidence with per-step accuracy trajectories, entropy/length dynamics, and gradient diagnostics
- KL-Cov is remarkably robust across a 200× hyperparameter range — no per-model retuning needed
- Per-channel GDPO normalization is theoretically motivated and empirically validated with logprob-space gradient norms
- Qualitative examples (Appendix G-H) make the collapse behavior concrete and interpretable

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on Qwen2.5 at 1.5B and 3B scales; behavior at 7B+ is unknown
- Symmetric reward weighting (α=β=0.5) is not systematically studied; asymmetric settings may yield different trade-offs
- The method still falls short of supervised GRPO-GT on some benchmarks (e.g., ~3pp gap on GSM8K at 3B)
- KL-Cov slightly hurts AIME performance at the 3B scale, suggesting potential tension between stability and peak exploration on hardest problems
- Requires a sufficiently capable base model; no safeguards for models with poor initial reasoning ability
- Additional hyperparameters beyond standard GRPO (β_cov, top-k fraction, channel weights α/β)

## Relevance

本文直接解决了LLM训练稳定性的核心问题——无监督强化学习中的熵坍塌和奖励作弊。KL-Cov正则化通过协方差感知的token级惩罚来稳定训练，与我们对训练不稳定性、梯度异常和分布式训练鲁棒性的研究兴趣高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the framework scale to 7B+ models where entropy collapse dynamics may differ qualitatively?
- Can additional intrinsic reward channels (e.g., consistency between chain-of-thought steps, representation-level agreement) further improve stability?
- What is the interaction between KL-Cov regularization and other training stability techniques (e.g., gradient clipping, learning rate warmup)?
- Does the multi-reward + KL-Cov approach transfer to non-reasoning domains (e.g., instruction following, creative generation)?
- How does the framework behave under mixed-precision training where numerical noise could interact with covariance estimation?
