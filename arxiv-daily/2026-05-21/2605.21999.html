Toward Understanding Adversarial Distillation: Why Robust Teachers Fail

Hongsin Lee, Hye Won Chung | 2026-05-21

adversarial-robustness knowledge-distillation robust-overfitting feature-learning-theory teacher-student

Stronger robust teachers can produce less robust students because they are overconfident on a "Robustly Unlearnable Set" of training samples — forcing students to memorize spurious noise that overpowers learned robust signals.

Key Metric

Predictive entropy on unlearnable samples is the strongest indicator of student robustness. Less robust teachers with high uncertainty on these samples consistently produce more robust students than stronger teachers with low entropy.

Why This Matters

The counterintuitive result that more robust teachers can harm student robustness has practical implications: teacher selection for adversarial distillation should optimize entropy on unlearnable samples, not overall teacher robustness.

Critical Edge

The dichotomy is rigorously proven in two-layer networks: confident supervision on unlearnable samples forces noise memorization, while uncertain supervision suppresses it. The "Robustly Unlearnable Set" provides a mechanistic explanation where prior work had only empirical observation.

Methodology

Abstract-only summary

The paper develops a theoretical framework for feature learning dynamics in two-layer neural networks under adversarial distillation. It formalizes the Robustly Unlearnable Set — a consistent subset of training data where the student cannot learn robust features. The analysis examines how the teacher's supervisory signal (confident vs. uncertain) on these unlearnable samples creates a dichotomy in student outcomes.

The theory is empirically validated through synthetic simulations and experiments on real-image classification benchmarks, assessing how different teacher models' confidence profiles on unlearnable samples correlate with student robust generalization.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean mechanistic explanation for a known but poorly understood empirical phenomenon.
  • Combines rigorous theoretical analysis with empirical validation.
  • Immediately actionable guideline: entropy-based teacher selection.
  • The confident/uncertain teacher dichotomy is intuitive and well-supported.

Limitations

  • Theory restricted to two-layer networks; extension to deep architectures not addressed.
  • Practical identification of the unlearnable set in real-world datasets may require additional methodology.
  • Abstract does not specify which real-image datasets or architectures were used.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can the Robustly Unlearnable Set concept extend to LLM fine-tuning or alignment (e.g., RLHF)?
  2. How does the size and composition of the unlearnable set vary across data distributions and architectures?
  3. Is there a connection between the unlearnable set and "grokking" or delayed generalization?
  4. Could curriculum learning that down-weights unlearnable samples improve adversarial distillation?
  5. Does the entropy-based criterion transfer to other distillation settings beyond adversarial training?
