> **一句话总结：** 本文提出基于KL散度的线性探针（KL-RP）来检验语言模型中关系线性性假设，发现时态和真实性等关系在中间层表现出强线性结构，且不同关系的线性性随层数呈现从表层到抽象的递进模式。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces KL-RP, a KL-divergence-based linear probe to test whether language models encode relational knowledge (e.g., tense, truthfulness) linearly in their representations. Across four datasets and two models (Llama-3.1-8B, Gemma-2-2B), the authors find that relational linearity holds robustly for tense and truthfulness, emerges in early layers for surface features and middle layers for abstract ones, and is moderately affected by query paraphrasing. The proposed KL-RP consistently outperforms the prior LRE method (Hernandez et al., 2024).

## 核心贡献

- 提出基于KL散度的关系线性探针（KL-RP），通过最小化线性探针输出分布与模型真实输出分布之间的KL散度来检测关系线性性，避免了LRE方法中对Jacobian的粗糙近似。
- 在4个数据集（语言识别、时态、主观性、真实性）和2个模型（Llama-3.1-8B、Gemma-2-2B）上进行了系统实验，发现时态和真实性关系具有强线性性，语言识别次之，主观性最弱。
- 揭示了层级动态规律：表层语言特征（语言、时态）在早期层即可线性解码，抽象语义特征（真实性）在中间层达到峰值。
- 分析了查询改写（paraphrase）对关系线性性的影响，发现Llama-3.1对改写具有鲁棒性，而Gemma-2受影响更大。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces KL-RP (KL-based Relational Probe), a novel probing method that trains a linear layer to match the LLM's next-token probability distribution by minimizing KL divergence, eliminating the need for crude Jacobian approximations used in prior LRE work.
- Provides the first comprehensive empirical validation of the relational linearity formulation by Marconato et al. (2025), testing across four diverse datasets (language identification, tense, subjectivity, truthfulness) and two models of different scales.
- Discovers consistent layer-wise dynamics: surface linguistic features (language, tense) are linearly decodable from early layers, while abstract semantic properties (truthfulness) peak in middle layers — aligning with prior NLP pipeline findings (Tenney et al., 2019; Cheng et al., 2025).
- Demonstrates that KL-RP detects relational linear properties missed by LRE, and introduces a Collapse on the Simplex Score (CSS) to flag trivial probability distributions that could produce misleading probing results.
- Evaluates query paraphrase robustness: Llama-3.1 is largely invariant to query reformulation (including cross-lingual paraphrases), while Gemma-2 shows more sensitivity, especially in earlier layers.

## Methodology

The paper formalizes **relational linearity** as: for a fixed query q and token subset Y_P, the conditional distribution p(y | s ⌢ q; Y_P) can be exactly reconstructed from context-only embeddings f(s) via a linear classifier with softmax activation (Definition 2.1).

**KL-RP** trains weights w_y ∈ R^d and biases b_y ∈ R to minimize:

$$d_{KL} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \sum_{y \in Y_P} p(y | \tilde{q} \frown s_i \frown \tilde{t}) \log \frac{p_{W,b}(y | s_i)}{p(y | \tilde{q} \frown s_i \frown \tilde{t})}$$

The probe only requires access to: (a) context-only embeddings f(s), and (b) query-appended next-token probabilities. No annotated (subject, relation, object) triplets are needed.

**Models:** Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (32 layers, dim 4096) and Gemma-2-2B-it (26 layers, dim 2304), both 8-bit quantized.

**Datasets:** LANG (20 languages, 9542 samples), TENSE (past/present/future, 10k samples), SUBJ (fact/opinion, multilingual, 8090 samples), TRUTH (true/false, 9123 samples).

**Metrics:** F1(GT) against ground truth, F1(LLM) against model's own predictions, KL divergence, and CSS (Collapse on Simplex Score) as a triviality check.

## Results & Highlights

- **TENSE and TRUTH show strong relational linearity:** For Llama-3.1, KL-RP achieves F1(LLM) ≥ 0.89 and d_KL ≤ 0.10 on both relations. Gemma-2 also shows strong results on TENSE (F1(LLM)=0.95, d_KL=0.23) and TRUTH (F1(LLM)=0.90, d_KL=0.42).
- **KL-RP consistently outperforms LRE:** LRE fails on LANG and SUBJ due to Jacobian approximation errors and prompt template sensitivity. KL-RP detects linear properties that LRE misses, particularly for LANG (Llama-3.1: 0.98 vs 0.36 F1(LLM)).
- **Layer-wise patterns are consistent across models:** Surface features (language, tense) emerge in early layers; abstract features (truthfulness) peak in middle layers. This mirrors the classical NLP pipeline hierarchy.
- **Query paraphrases have moderate impact:** For LANG, translating the query to Italian/Spanish reduces Gemma-2 performance by ~12.5 F1 points but only ~6.7 for Llama-3.1. For TRUTH, Llama-3.1 is robust to label changes (True/False vs Valid/Invalid vs Yes/No); Gemma-2 varies more.
- **SUBJ is a challenging case:** Both models perform below majority-class baseline on subjectivity prediction. Gemma-2 shows near-trivial collapse (CSS ≈ 0.91), and KL-RP finds no meaningful linearity.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate. The core idea — using KL divergence to probe for relational linearity — is a natural and effective extension of prior work, but the conceptual leap from LRE is incremental. The main value is in the comprehensive empirical evaluation rather than methodological breakthrough.
- **Rigor:** High. The paper includes multiple sanity checks (CSS, random baselines, ground-truth F1), compares against prior art (LRE), and is transparent about model capabilities and failure cases (e.g., SUBJ).
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Models are publicly available (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Gemma-2-2B-it), datasets are specified, and the method only requires standard probing infrastructure. The paper could benefit from released code.
- **Significance:** Useful for mechanistic interpretability. The finding that relational linearity varies by relation type and layer depth contributes to understanding how LLMs organize knowledge. The CSS metric is a practical contribution for future probing work.
- **Red flags:** Only two models tested (8B and 2B), both instruction-tuned and quantized. Results may not generalize to larger models or base models. The variation from the original relational linearity formulation (using system prompts rather than raw s ⌢ q) is acknowledged but limits theoretical claims. The LRE comparison is somewhat unfair since LRE was designed for a different prompt setup.

## Strengths

- Well-motivated and cleanly formalized problem setting with clear definitions.
- The CSS metric is a practical contribution that flags trivial probing scenarios — a pitfall not addressed in prior work.
- Comprehensive layer-wise analysis across four diverse relations provides actionable insights about information flow in transformers.
- Honest about limitations: authors openly discuss cases where linearity does not hold (SUBJ) and differences between their setup and the original formulation.

## Limitations

- Only two relatively small models tested (8B, 2B); results may not scale to frontier models (70B+, GPT-4 class).
- 8-bit quantization may alter representation geometry compared to full-precision models.
- The experimental variation (system prompt + object-inducing string) differs from the original relational linearity formulation (s ⌢ q), which the authors acknowledge but do not empirically compare.
- Datasets are limited to well-structured classification tasks (language, tense, subjectivity, truthfulness); relational linearity for open-ended or multi-hop relations remains untested.
- No code release mentioned, which could hinder reproducibility.

## Relevance

本文对语言模型内部表征中关系知识如何线性编码进行了系统性实证研究，与可解释性研究（mechanistic interpretability）和表征工程（representation engineering）方向高度相关。其层级动态发现对理解LLM知识组织方式具有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does relational linearity hold for larger models (70B+), and does it strengthen or weaken with scale?
2. Can the identified linear relational subspaces be used for effective activation steering to control model outputs on specific relations?
3. How does relational linearity change during training — is it a property that emerges gradually or appears at specific training stages?
4. Does relational linearity extend beyond classification-style relations to more complex, compositional relations (e.g., "X is the capital of the country where Y was born")?
5. What is the relationship between relational linearity and features discovered by Sparse Autoencoders — are they encoding the same information?
