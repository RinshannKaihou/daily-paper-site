> **一句话总结：** 本文将因果中介分析（CMA）应用于多模态语音语言模型 SpiritLM，发现文本输入能触发与纯文本 LLM 一致的事实召回机制（中层 MLP），但语音输入下该因果效应大幅减弱，表明事实召回机制仅部分从文本迁移到语音模态。

> **TL;DR:** The authors apply Causal Mediation Analysis to SpiritLM to compare factual recall mechanisms between text-to-text and speech-to-text settings. Text-based factual recall shows strong, localized causal effects in mid-layer MLPs (consistent with prior LLM work), but speech-based recall shows drastically weaker and more diffuse signals, suggesting only partial transfer of factual knowledge mechanisms from text to speech.

## 核心贡献

- 首次将因果中介分析（Causal Mediation Analysis）扩展到多模态语音语言模型，系统比较文本和语音模态下的事实召回机制。
- 设计了跨模态 token 对齐方法，利用 CTC 强制对齐将语音 token 映射到文本 token，使两种模态的因果追踪结果可直接比较。
- 证实 SpiritLM 在文本模态下保留了与原始 Llama 2 骨干网络一致的事实召回模式（中层 MLP 因果效应集中在 subject token）。
- 发现语音输入下事实召回的因果效应大幅减弱但仍可检测，表明事实关联机制并非严格的模态依赖，但语音微调未充分利用文本骨干已学习到的事实召回能力。

## Key Contributions

- First application of Causal Mediation Analysis (CMA) to a multimodal speech-text language model, enabling systematic comparison of factual recall mechanisms across modalities.
- A cross-modal token mapping pipeline using CTC-based forced alignment to map HuBERT speech tokens to text tokens, allowing direct comparison of causal traces between text and speech inputs.
- Confirmation that SpiritLM preserves text-based factual recall patterns identical to its Llama 2 backbone, with strong AIE localized at subject tokens in mid-layer MLPs.
- Evidence that speech inputs produce drastically weaker but detectable causal effects for factual recall, suggesting partial — not full — transfer of fact-recalling mechanisms from text to speech.

## Methodology

The study uses **Causal Tracing** (CT), a three-step intervention procedure from Meng et al. (2022):

1. **Clean run:** Feed the model a clean factual prompt (e.g., "The capital of Italy is ___") and cache hidden states.
2. **Corrupted run:** Add noise proportional to embedding standard deviation to the subject tokens, producing a degraded prediction probability.
3. **Corrupted-with-restoration run:** Restore a specific component's activation from the clean run into the corrupted run, measuring the **Indirect Effect (IE)** as the probability recovery.

The IE is averaged over prompts to compute the **Average Indirect Effect (AIE)**, reported in log scale for readability.

**Model:** SpiritLM, which interleaves discrete HuBERT speech tokens and Llama 2 text tokens, with modality declaration tokens ("T"/"S") prefixing each segment.

**Dataset:** The Known dataset (~1000 factual prompts from Meng et al. 2022), filtered into Known-t2t and Known-s2t based on model correctness in each modality. Speech versions generated via MeloTTS, with quality validated by Whisper-small transcription (WER: 19%).

**Two experiments:**
- **T→T (text-to-text):** Standard causal tracing on text prompts, aggregating AIE by token position (first/middle/last subject token, subsequent tokens, last token) across layers.
- **S→T (speech-to-text):** Same pipeline but with synthesized speech prompts. Subject localization in speech tokens achieved via CTC-based forced alignment using HuBERT-LARGE fine-tuned for ASR, mapping speech token ranges back to corresponding text tokens for aggregation.

## Results & Highlights

- **T→T in SpiritLM closely mirrors Llama 2 backbone:** Strong AIE centered on subject tokens at early-to-mid layers, especially in MLP sub-layers, with notable effects at the last token position in upper layers — fully consistent with prior LLM findings.
- **S→T shows drastically weaker AIE:** The causal signal is much more diffuse and lower in magnitude across all components (hidden states, MLP, attention), though traces of factual localization around subject tokens remain detectable in MLP and attention layers.
- **Not strictly modality-dependent:** Speech inputs do activate some factual recall pathways, but the effect is substantially weaker, suggesting the speech fine-tuning does not fully leverage the backbone's fact-recalling mechanisms.
- **Hypothesized cause:** The semantic gap between modalities — specifically, a divergence in magnitude (Euclidean distance) despite potential directional alignment (cosine similarity) across deeper layers, arising from post-training speech adaptation.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate. Extending CMA to multimodal speech models is a natural but worthwhile step. The cross-modal token mapping via forced alignment is a non-trivial technical contribution.
- **Rigor:** The experimental design is sound — filtering datasets by model correctness per modality, validating TTS quality, and using forced alignment for subject localization. However, the study is limited to a single model (SpiritLM) and a single dataset (Known).
- **Reproducibility:** Reasonably high. The dataset is public (Known from ROME), SpiritLM is available, and the methodology is clearly described with implementation details in the appendix. The forced alignment pipeline is well-specified.
- **Significance:** Important preliminary evidence that multimodal LLMs do not automatically transfer factual recall mechanisms to speech. This has implications for building more reliable speech-enabled AI systems.
- **Red flags:** The AIE drop in S→T could partially be an artifact of noise in the forced alignment or TTS artifacts, which the authors acknowledge but cannot fully disentangle. The 19% WER on prompts suggests some speech inputs may be garbled, potentially confounding the results.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated experimental design that faithfully extends established mechanistic interpretability methods to a new domain.
- Transparent discussion of the semantic gap hypothesis with references to concurrent work.
- The cross-modal token mapping technique is generalizable to other speech LLMs using discrete tokens.
- Honest about limitations — the paper frames findings as "preliminary insights" rather than definitive conclusions.

## Limitations

- **Single model:** Only SpiritLM is studied; findings may not generalize to other SLMs (e.g., continuous speech representations like SALMONN, or different architectures like SpeechGPT).
- **Synthesized speech only:** Real human speech with varied accents, prosody, and noise conditions is not tested.
- **Single dataset:** The Known dataset covers a narrow range of factual associations; results may differ for more complex or diverse knowledge.
- **Alignment noise:** CTC forced alignment introduces potential error in subject token localization, which could partially explain the weaker S→T signals.
- **No interventional validation:** The paper observes but does not intervene (e.g., edit factual associations in speech pathways) to confirm the causal role of the identified components.

## Relevance

本文对多模态大语言模型的可解释性研究具有直接参考价值，尤其是将 mechanistic interpretability 方法从文本扩展到语音模态的工作范式，对理解多模态模型内部知识存储与召回机制具有重要意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would using continuous speech representations (instead of discrete HuBERT tokens) yield stronger factual recall signals in the S→T setting?
- Can the partial factual recall observed in speech be strengthened through specific training strategies (e.g., joint speech-text pretraining rather than post-hoc adaptation)?
- Do the same localization patterns hold for more complex factual associations (multi-hop reasoning, temporal knowledge) in speech?
- How does the choice of backbone LLM (e.g., Llama 3 vs. Llama 2) affect the degree of factual recall transfer to speech?
- Can the causal tracing results be used to design targeted interventions that improve factual accuracy in speech-to-text generation?
