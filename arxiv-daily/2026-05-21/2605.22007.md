> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"承诺失败"视角解释 LLM 幻觉——引入语义级概率质量 P_mass（聚合同一答案概念的不同 token 变体），发现 0.8B–72B 的 Qwen/Llama Instruct 模型中 16%–47% 的幻觉发生在正确概念已有 ≥0.2 概率质量的情况下且随规模单调上升，根因是指令微调随规模锐化承诺，使"自信正确"与"自信幻觉"成为同一机制的两个产物。

> **TL;DR:** The authors reframe LLM hallucination as a commitment failure rather than a knowledge gap. They introduce per-step semantic probability mass P_mass(t; c) = Σ_{v∈S_c} P_θ(v | Q, y<t) that aggregates token-level variants of the same answer concept, evaluated on Qwen3.5 (0.8B/2B/4B/9B) and Llama-3.2/3.1 (1B/3B/8B/70B) plus Qwen2.5-72B, in both Instruct and Base variants, on TriviaQA, NQ-Open (3,000 samples/model), MMLU, and ARC-Challenge (2,672 samples/model). They find 16%–47% of Instruct hallucinations have P_mass ≥ 0.2 on the correct concept (rising monotonically with scale), and that at matched P_mass, first-token selection failures have a 3× lower max surface-form probability than correct samples (0.26 vs 0.78, Cohen's d = 2.98 on Qwen3.5-9B Instruct). The wrong-token probability rises monotonically with scale in Instruct models (Qwen 0.31→0.57, Llama 0.33→0.49) but stays flat at ~0.30 in Base models, identifying instruction tuning—not scale alone—as the driver.

## 核心贡献

- 引入语义级"答案可用性"概念：将表达同一答案概念的不同 token 变体聚合为概念 token 集 S_c，定义每步语义概率质量 P_mass(t; c) = Σ_{v∈S_c} P_θ(v | Q, y<t)，作为概念信念 P_θ(c | Q, y<t) 的解析探针（附录 A Proposition 1 给出近似界）
- 实证证明"承诺失败"现象：在 18 个 0.8B–72B 的 Qwen/Llama 模型上，16%–47% 的 Instruct 幻觉在承诺步 t_c 处对正确概念已有 ≥0.2 的概率质量，且 CF% 随规模单调上升（Qwen 0.8B→72B: 16%→41%；Llama 1B→70B: 16%→47%）
- 揭示幻觉与正确生成的结构差异：在匹配 P_mass 的条件下，首 token 选择失败组（greedy ∉ S_c*）的最大单表面形式概率显著低于正确组（Qwen3.5-9B Instruct: 0.26 vs 0.78，Welch t=47.6，Cohen's d=2.98，p<10⁻¹⁸⁰），且该效应在 18 个模型中 100% 成立（中位 |d|=1.93）
- 分离两种失败模式：通过 D2（概念内 top-1 占比）和 D3（错误 token 主导度）比率，区分小/Base 模型的"碎片化驱动失败"与大 Instruct 模型的"错误吸引子驱动失败"，二者同源于指令诱导的锐化
- 证明指令微调（而非规模本身）是驱动因素：Instruct 模型错误 token 概率随规模单调上升（Qwen 0.31→0.57，Llama 0.33→0.49），Base 模型在相同规模区间内保持 ~0.30 不变
- 提供 Instruct–Base 隐藏态对比：预生成探针 AUROC（最后一层隐藏态，5 折 CV 逻辑回归）Instruct 比 Base 高 +0.08，输出级 AUROC 高 +0.29，说明指令微调的锐化既在表示层也在输出映射层

## Key Contributions

- A semantic notion of answer availability: P_mass(t; c) aggregates token-level variants expressing the same answer concept, with a formal bound (Proposition 1) showing it approximates the unobservable concept belief P_θ(c | Q, y<t) when the alias set is complete and concepts are well-separated
- Empirical demonstration of commitment failures: 16%–47% of Instruct hallucinations across Qwen and Llama (0.8B–72B) have P_mass(t_c; c*) ≥ 0.2 on the correct concept, with the rate rising monotonically with scale in both families
- Within-population structural finding: at matched P_mass, first-token selection failures have dramatically lower max surface-form probability than correct samples (0.26 vs 0.78, Cohen's d = 2.98 on Qwen3.5-9B Instruct; d < 0 in 100% of 18 models, median |d| = 1.93)
- Two-faces decomposition: D2 (within-concept top-1 share) and D3 (wrong-token dominance) ratios separate fragmentation-driven failures (small/Base models) from wrong-attractor failures (large Instruct models), both rooted in instruction-induced sharpening
- Causal attribution to instruction tuning: wrong-token probability rises monotonically with scale in Instruct models (Qwen 0.31→0.57, Llama 0.33→0.49) but stays flat at ~0.30 across Base models of the same sizes — scale alone does not produce sharpening
- First systematic Instruct–Base comparison for the token-before-generation (TBG) probing setting: pre-generation hidden-state probe AUROC is 0.61–0.87 for Instruct vs 0.50–0.63 for Llama Base; Base models gain more from one generated token (avg Δ_B = +0.030 vs Δ_I = +0.005)
- Multi-token extension: at 70B+ Instruct, second-token entropy H_{t=2} is ~0.05 when the bigram stays on a valid alias prefix (Type A divergences), with Cohen's d = 1.29 across 18 models — instruction tuning sharpens commitment along c*-aligned phrases

## Methodology

**Concept and P_mass.** A concept c is an equivalence class of token completions denoting the same answer; its first-token surface forms collect into a concept token set S_c. The per-step semantic probability mass is P_mass(t; c) = Σ_{v∈S_c} P_θ(v | Q, y<t). For hallucination analysis, c = c* (the ground-truth concept) with S_{c*} built deterministically from the dataset's aliases (Appendix I). Proposition 1 (Appendix A) gives |P_mass(t) − P_θ(c | Q, y<t)| ≤ (1 − γ_c) + (K−1)ε, where γ_c is alias completeness and ε is cross-concept leakage.

**Models.** Primary scale ablation: Qwen3.5 at 0.8B, 2B, 4B, 9B (Instruct and Base, 4-bit NF4 quantization); Llama-3.2 at 1B, 3B and Llama-3.1 at 8B (Instruct and Base) — fourteen models at small-to-mid scale. Extended to four large models: Qwen2.5-72B and Llama-3.1-70B (Instruct and Base). Eighteen models total.

**Datasets.** Short-form QA: TriviaQA and NQ-Open (3,000 samples per model). Multiple-choice QA: MMLU and ARC-Challenge (2,672 samples per model). Representation analyses (§4.4) use a 1,500-sample subset (1,000 MCQA + 500 Short-QA). Long-form analysis (§4.1) uses 500 Qwen3.5-9B Instruct responses.

**Commitment step.** In instruction-tuned short-form QA, t_c = 1 (Appendix Table 8). A hallucinated sample is a commitment failure if P_mass(t_c; c*) ≥ 0.2. Commitment failures decompose into first-token selection failures (greedy y_1 ∉ S_{c*}, ~20% of CF) and multi-token divergences (greedy y_1 ∈ S_{c*} but continuation diverges, ~80%).

**Metrics.** Detection performance is AUROC with hallucination as the positive class. Hidden-state probes are 5-fold CV logistic regression. Calibration uses ECE and Brier score. Within-population comparisons use Welch's t-test and Cohen's d. Multi-token divergence classification: Type A (bigram matches valid alias prefix, continuation diverges into different entity) vs Type B (bigram diverges at y_2).

**Decoding.** Experiments use 1–3 token answers and greedy decoding; the authors note concept-segmented P_mass for longer generation and behavior under temperature/top-p sampling as natural extensions.

## Results & Highlights

- **Commitment failure rate (CF%):** Across Instruct models, CF% rises monotonically with scale — Qwen3.5 0.8B→2B→4B→9B→Qwen2.5-72B: 16%→17%→26%→32%→41%; Llama-3.2-1B→3B→Llama-3.1-8B→70B: 16%→28%→33%→47% (Table 1).
- **First-token selection failure rate (SF%):** Rises monotonically with scale — Qwen Instruct 2.9% (0.8B) → 6.0% (72B); Llama Instruct 3.3% (1B) → 10.2% (70B) of all hallucinations (Table 1).
- **Within-population top-token mass gap (Qwen3.5-9B Instruct):** First-token selection failures have mean max surface-form probability 0.26 vs 0.78 for matched correct samples (Welch t = 47.6, p < 10⁻¹⁸⁰, Cohen's d = 2.98). Across all 18 models, d < 0 in 100% of cases (median |d| = 1.93, range [1.01, 4.30]); within Instruct families |d| grows with scale (Qwen 1.34→4.30, Llama 2.18→2.97; Appendix Table 2).
- **Wrong-token probability (instruction tuning driver):** In Instruct models, rises monotonically with scale — Qwen 0.31 (0.8B) → 0.36 → 0.40 → 0.44 → 0.57 (72B); Llama 0.33 (1B) → 0.43 → 0.46 → 0.49 (70B). In Base models, stays flat at ~0.30 (Llama Base 0.26 at 1B → 0.33 at 70B; Qwen Base 0.8B/72B: 0.27/0.31; Figure 4).
- **Long-form commitment step localization (Qwen3.5-9B Instruct, 500 responses):** P_mass(t; c*) is essentially zero except at t_c, spiking to 0.92 (correct) and 0.77 (hallucinated); per-step detection AUROC of P_mass peaks sharply at t_c and decays to chance off-step; entropy max-step t_H matches t_c in only 20% of samples (Figure 3).
- **P_mass calibration:** ECE 0.023–0.096 across 7 Instruct models (Appendix Figure 9); accuracy increases monotonically across P_mass bins.
- **Multi-token divergence entropy:** At 70B+ Instruct, H_{t=2} ≈ 0.05 for Type A divergences (bigram on valid alias prefix); Type A vs Type B median Cohen's d = 1.29 across 18 models (d > 1 in 100% of models with sufficient N). Type A fraction grows from 21–44% at 0.8B/1B to 77–83% at 70B+ Instruct.
- **Instruct–Base output-level detection (MCQA):** Instruct models reach near-perfect AUROC 0.974–0.999 using only P(correct option); Base models span 0.558–0.748 (+0.29 average gap; Figure 5).
- **Pre-generation hidden-state probes:** Instruct > Base in all four sizes (+0.08 average AUROC gap); Instruct AUROC 0.61–0.87 vs Base 0.50–0.63; Base models gain more from one generated token (avg Δ_B = +0.030 vs Δ_I = +0.005; Appendix Table 7).
- **Attention to question tokens at t=1:** Instruct models allocate +0.09 higher fraction of last-layer attention to question tokens vs Base (Figure 5, middle).

## Critical Assessment

- The paper's central reframing — hallucination as commitment failure rather than knowledge gap — is well-supported by the monotonic CF% trend across two model families and the within-population effect (d < 0 in 100% of 18 models). The finding that larger Instruct models misfire *more* on what they know is counterintuitive and important.
- A concrete paper-specific weakness (stated explicitly in §5 Limitations): P_mass is an analytical probe, not a deployable detector — it requires the ground-truth alias set S_{c*} as input, so any practical use depends on inferring S_{c*} from context (e.g., by clustering top-k tokens at t_c by semantic equivalence), which is itself an unsolved problem the paper does not solve.
- The experiments are restricted to 1–3 token answers and greedy decoding; the authors acknowledge that concept-segmented P_mass for longer generation and behavior under temperature sampling or top-p truncation are untested natural extensions. This limits the generality of the commitment-failure characterization to short-form QA.
- The Instruct-vs-Base attribution is correlational: the paper compares Instruct and Base variants of the same model families but cannot fully disentangle instruction tuning from other post-training differences (RLHF data, DPO procedures) that co-vary with the Instruct label. The "instruction tuning sharpens commitment" claim is supported by the Base-flatness contrast but not by a controlled intervention.
- The within-population analysis controls for P_mass but the multi-token divergence sub-population (~80% of commitment failures) is analyzed separately and P_mass(t=1; c*) ≥ 0.2 does not distinguish concept-level belief from phrase-level commitment to specific multi-token continuations (acknowledged in §5 and Appendix F).

## Strengths

- Novel conceptual framing that rigorously distinguishes "concept present" from "concept committed to," with a formal theoretical foundation (Propositions 1–2)
- Systematic empirical evaluation across 18 models, two families, two variants (Instruct/Base), and four datasets (TriviaQA, NQ-Open, MMLU, ARC-Challenge)
- The Instruct–Base contrast is a clean experimental design that isolates instruction tuning as the driver of the scale-dependent sharpening
- Connects behavioral analysis (output distributions) to internal representations (hidden-state probes, attention) at three structural levels (first token, multi-token phrase, within-concept alias)
- The two-faces decomposition (D2/D3) elegantly separates fragmentation-driven from wrong-attractor-driven failures, unifying them under one sharpening mechanism
- Honest about P_mass being an analytical probe rather than a deployable detector, and about the 1–3 token / greedy decoding scope

## Limitations

- P_mass requires the ground-truth alias set S_{c*} as input; it is not a deployable detector without solving alias-set inference
- Restricted to 1–3 token answers and greedy decoding; longer generation and sampling-based decoding are untested
- Only Qwen and Llama families are evaluated; generalization to other architectures (MoE, non-transformer) and closed models (which lack first-token distribution access) is unverified
- The Instruct–Base attribution is correlational, not a controlled intervention; co-varying post-training factors (RLHF data, DPO) are not disentangled
- The commitment-failure phenomenon explains only the 16%–47% subset with P_mass ≥ 0.2; the remaining 53%–84% of hallucinations (where the correct concept is absent or has <0.2 mass) are not the focus
- The 0.2 threshold is stated as conservative and robust to [0.1, 0.4] (Appendix G), but the choice is still empirical
- No code repository is linked in the PDF body, limiting reproducibility verification

## Relevance

高度对应"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"方向：本文通过分析承诺步的输出概率分布形态和预生成隐藏态探针，揭示幻觉的内在机制是概率分布形态（集中 vs 分散）而非知识存在性，属于隐藏状态可解释性的机制性工作。同时与"LLM Inference Evaluation"（幻觉检测 AUROC）、"LLM Training Stability & Observability"（指令微调随规模锐化承诺）方向相关，其"承诺失败"视角也为理解 alignment tax 提供了机制层面的解释。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can a concept-aware decoding rule that argmaxes over alias-clustered top-k tokens (rather than individual vocabulary entries) convert a meaningful fraction of selection failures into correct answers without retraining, as the authors suggest?
- How does the commitment-failure mechanism behave under temperature sampling or top-p truncation — does dispersion-aware decoding recover the dispersed mass?
- Does the instruction-tuning sharpening effect generalize to other alignment methods (DPO, constitutional AI, RLAIF) or is it specific to RLHF-style instruction tuning?
- Can the P_mass signal be approximated at inference time via semantic-similarity clustering of top-k tokens, making it a deployable hallucination detector?
- In long-form generation with multiple commitment events (domain commitment, answer commitment, sub-claim), can non-entropy signals (hidden-state probes, attention concentration, ΔP_mass rate of change) reliably localize each commitment step, given that entropy matches t_c in only 20% of trajectories?
- Is the scale-dependent CF% increase a fundamental property of instruction tuning, or could targeted fine-tuning on probability sharpening (rather than answer correctness) reduce the commitment failure rate?
