Check Your LLM's Secret Dictionary! Five Lines of Code Reveal What Your LLM Learned (Including What It Shouldn't Have)
Hisashi Miyashita · 2026-05-21
interpretability safety-auditing lm-head-analysis glitch-tokens singular-value-decomposition
Five lines of PyTorch — SVD on the lm_head weight matrix — reveals training data composition, ethically concerning vocabulary clusters that survive RLHF, and glitch tokens, all without running a single inference.

Key Metric

Gemma base→instruct VCS difference < 0.003 (RLHF barely touches lm_head). WPS recovers known glitch token (ID 137606, WPS = 0.8807) with zero inference. Gemma dominant eigenvector VCS = 0.89.

Why This Matters

RLHF does not remove pretraining-level safety issues — the vocabulary projection layer remains virtually unchanged. This 5-line audit should be a standard pre-release safety check for every LLM.

Critical Edge

VCS has circularity risk (uses embeddings to measure coherence). WPS validated against only one known glitch token. Qwen's concerning tokens withheld, limiting independent verification.

Methodology

Full SVD is applied to the lm_head weight matrix W = UΣVT. Each left singular vector U[:,k] spans vocabulary space; top-scoring tokens form interpretable semantic clusters. Vocabulary Cluster Score (VCS) measures geometric coherence of top-N tokens via average pairwise cosine similarity weighted by singular scores. Weighted Projection Score (WPS) = Σk S[k]·|U[v,k]| quantifies how strongly a token participates in dominant directions — tokens with WPS below μ−2σ are glitch token candidates. Three models analyzed: GPT-OSS-120B (201K vocab), Gemma-2-2B (256K), Qwen2.5-1.5B (152K), with base–instruct comparisons for Gemma and Qwen.

Strengths

  • Exceptionally simple (5 lines) with high interpretability
  • Direct geometric evidence RLHF fails on safety
  • Recovers known glitch tokens without inference
  • Responsible disclosure of ethical findings

Limitations

  • Only 3 models; generalization untested
  • WPS validated against one known glitch token only
  • SVD-guided tokenizer proposal is purely theoretical
  • lm_head structure → downstream behavior link is open

Open Questions

  • Do ethically concerning subspaces correspond to measurable increases in harmful output under adversarial prompting?
  • Can VCS/WPS serve as training-time monitors across checkpoints?
  • Does VCS-guided tokenizer pruning improve performance in controlled experiments?
  • Could combining lm_head SVD with hidden-state probing (e.g., AOP) give a fuller safety picture?
