Preemptive runtime verification intercepts unsafe VLA actions before execution, boosting success from 30.79% to 37.62%
Pre-VLA: Preemptive Runtime Verification for Reliable Vision-Language-Action and World-Model Rollouts
Zhen Sun, Yongjian Guo, Haoran Sun, Luqiao Wang et al. · 2026-05-21
vision-language-action runtime-verification embodied-intelligence world-models robotic-manipulation

Key Metric

LIBERO average success rate: 30.79% → 37.62% (+6.83 pp). LIBERO-Goal: 72.25% → 81.26%. False pass rate: 0.02 (safety-critical). Verification latency: 183.9 ms.

Why This Matters

VLA models deployed on physical robots need runtime safety guarantees. Pre-VLA provides a unified verification gateway for both physical execution and world-model imagination, catching dangerous actions before they cause real-world harm.

Critical Edge

Failure-aware backtracking penalty captures delayed causal effects — actions within a window of catastrophic failure are penalized with exponential decay. Without Pre-VLA, world model rollouts diverge after ~30 frames; with it, scene structure remains stable.

Methodology

Pre-VLA derives training labels from PPO Critic rollouts: K-step chunk advantages with safety masking (zeroing bootstrapped value on collision/drop), an exponentially decayed failure-aware penalty for delayed causal effects, and per-task standardization. The architecture uses a frozen Chameleon-7B backbone with modality-aware pooling (separate text/image/state/action summary vectors) feeding a compact dual-branch FFN head for simultaneous safety classification and advantage regression. Training combines Focal Loss (95:5 class imbalance), MSE regression, and soft-threshold calibration over 1.28M PPO rollout samples. At inference, a dual-mode scheduler either rejects unsafe VLA actions (triggering resampling with regression-guided fallback) or truncates WM rollouts when all candidates are high-risk.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Addresses practically important underexplored problem: runtime VLA safety
  • Unified verification for physical execution and WM imagination
  • Failure-aware backtracking captures delayed causal effects
  • Low false pass rate (0.02) critical for safety applications
  • Clean ablation study isolating each training component’s contribution

Limitations

  • LIBERO simulation only; no real-robot deployment
  • Single baseline VLA (RynnVLA-002); cross-architecture generalization unknown
  • Absolute success rates still low on harder suites (Object: 3.95%, 10: 19.61%)
  • Requires VLA backbone internals; not applicable to closed-source models
  • ~78% per-step latency increase (614 → 1098 ms)

Open Questions

  1. Can Pre-VLA generalize to other VLA architectures (OpenVLA, π₀, GR00T) without full retraining?
  2. How does verification latency scale with backbone size beyond Chameleon-7B?
  3. What is performance on real-robot tasks with non-stationary dynamics and sensor noise?
  4. Can safety and advantage thresholds be adapted online during deployment?
  5. Is the failure-aware backtracking penalty transferable to non-manipulation tasks (navigation, locomotion)?
