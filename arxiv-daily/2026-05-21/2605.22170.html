Do Factual Recall Mechanisms Carry over from Text to Speech in Multimodal Language Models?
Luca Modica, Filip Landin, Mehrdad Farahani, Livia Qian, Gabriel Skantze, Richard Johansson · 2026-05-21
mechanistic-interpretability speech-language-models causal-mediation-analysis multimodal-LLMs factual-recall
Text-based factual recall in SpiritLM mirrors Llama 2 — strong mid-layer MLP effects at subject tokens. But speech inputs produce drastically weaker, more diffuse causal signals. Factual recall only partially transfers from text to speech.

Key Metric

T→T shows strong, localized AIE at subject tokens in mid-layer MLPs. S→T shows drastically weaker and more diffuse AIE across all components (hidden states, MLP, attention), though traces remain detectable.

Why This Matters

Multimodal LLMs do not automatically transfer factual recall mechanisms to speech. Speech fine-tuning does not fully leverage the backbone's fact-recalling capacity — critical for building reliable speech-enabled AI systems.

Critical Edge

Single model (SpiritLM), single dataset (Known), synthesized speech only. 19% WER on speech prompts may confound results. No interventional validation — observes but does not edit speech pathways.

Methodology

Causal Tracing (CT) applied to SpiritLM, which interleaves discrete HuBERT speech tokens with Llama 2 text tokens. Three-step intervention: (1) clean run caches hidden states, (2) corrupted run adds noise to subject tokens, (3) corrupted-with-restoration run restores specific component activations from clean run. Indirect Effect (IE) measures probability recovery, averaged to AIE. For S→T, CTC-based forced alignment (HuBERT-LARGE) maps speech token ranges to text tokens for cross-modal comparison. Dataset: ~1000 factual prompts from Meng et al. (2022), filtered by model correctness per modality. Speech via MeloTTS, validated by Whisper-small transcription.

Strengths

  • Clean extension of established MI methods to a new domain
  • Cross-modal token mapping via forced alignment is generalizable
  • Transparent semantic gap hypothesis discussion
  • Honest framing as "preliminary insights"

Limitations

  • Single model (SpiritLM) — may not generalize
  • Synthesized speech only — no real human speech
  • Single dataset — narrow factual associations
  • CTC alignment noise may partially explain weaker S→T signals

Open Questions

  • Would continuous speech representations yield stronger factual recall signals than discrete tokens?
  • Can joint speech-text pretraining (vs. post-hoc adaptation) strengthen the transfer?
  • Do patterns hold for complex factual associations (multi-hop, temporal)?
  • Can causal tracing results guide targeted interventions to improve speech factual accuracy?
