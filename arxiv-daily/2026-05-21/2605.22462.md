> **一句话总结：** 提出五阶段因果特征分析方法论（探测设计、特征提取、因果验证、鲁棒性测试、部署集成），在 GPT-2 small 的 IOI 任务上端到端验证，发现 SAE 名字选择性特征虽具特定因果力但非必要（消融 15 个特征后仍 98.4% 正确），选择性比与因果力负相关（r=-0.56），且检测鲁棒性与因果鲁棒性存在关键差距（框架改写后因果力下降 50% 而激活仅降 10-20%）。

> **TL;DR:** The author proposes a five-stage methodology (probe design, feature extraction, causal validation, robustness testing, deployment integration) and demonstrates it end-to-end on GPT-2 small (124M, 12 layers, 12 heads, d_model=768) performing IOI. Activation patching recovers the canonical IOI circuit (L9H9 single-head recovery +1.02 vs Wang et al. 2023). A sparse autoencoder (d_sae=1024, L1 coef 0.5, 3000 steps, L0=87.3, 99.8% variance explained) trained on 2000 IOI prompts at layer-9 END position recovers per-name selective features (30–52 activation units IO vs 0–7 S). Causal validation finds these features are specifically causal but not necessary: single-feature ablation reduces logit-difference by 0.67 logits on preferred-name prompts, but ablating all 15 selective features simultaneously leaves the model at 98.4% correct (down from 100%). The 15 name-selective features explain only 30.7% of activation variance vs 99.7% for the full SAE. Selectivity ratio anticorrelates with causal force (Pearson r = -0.56). Under prompt reformulation, the circuit transfers cleanly (L9H9 recovery +0.96) but feature causal effects degrade by ~50% while firing magnitudes drop only 10–20%. Under stated deployment costs ($50/FN, $0.42/FP, 2% error rate), the optimal monitor is SAE-only at threshold 15, yielding $8.96 per 1000 queries vs $1000 baseline (99.1% savings).

## 核心贡献

- 提出五阶段因果特征分析方法论：探测设计（预注册假设与检测/相关性判据）、特征提取（SAE/NLA/有向搜索）、因果验证（消融/干预/steering）、鲁棒性测试（分布偏移/改写/微调）、部署集成（与治理层组合的成本评估）
- 在 GPT-2 small IOI 任务上端到端验证：激活修补复现经典 IOI 电路（L9H9 单头恢复 +1.02），SAE 提取每个名字的 IO 选择性特征（30–52 激活单位）
- 揭示"中间因果机制"：特征具有特定因果力但非必要——单特征消融降低 0.67 logits，但同时消融 15 个特征后模型仍 98.4% 正确
- 适配 NLA 框架的两项评估：保真度分层（15 个名字选择性特征仅解释 30.7% 激活方差 vs 全 SAE 99.7%）；可靠性分层（选择性比与因果力负相关 r=-0.56）
- 发现检测鲁棒性与因果鲁棒性的关键差距：电路在三种分布偏移下均鲁棒，特征的激活模式也鲁棒，但特征的因果消融效果在提示框架改写下下降约 50%
- 提出基于成本的部署评估：在给定成本假设下，最优配置为 SAE-only 阈值 15，每 1000 查询成本 $8.96 vs $1000 基线（99.1% 节省），并分析配置策略对成本比和基率的敏感性

## Key Contributions

- A five-stage methodology (probe design, feature extraction, causal validation, robustness testing, deployment integration) formalizing how SAEs, activation patching, NLAs, and causal interventions compose into a disciplined causal-claim pipeline
- End-to-end demonstration on GPT-2 small IOI: activation patching recovers the canonical circuit (L9H9 +1.02 recovery, L9H6 +0.32, L10H0 +0.11, backup L10H7 −0.54), matching Wang et al. 2023
- Empirical identification of an "intermediate causal regime": SAE features are specifically causal (0.67-logit drop per feature on preferred-name prompts) but not necessary (98.4% accuracy after ablating all 15 selective features)
- Two NLA-inspired evaluations adapted to SAE features: fidelity stratification (15 name-selective features explain 30.7% of variance vs 99.7% full SAE) and reliability stratification (selectivity ratio anticorrelates with causal force, r = −0.56)
- Documentation of a detection-vs-causal robustness gap: under prompt reformulation, feature firing magnitudes drop 10–20% but ablation causal effects drop ~50% — a previously undocumented gap
- A cost-based deployment evaluation framework producing operator-actionable outputs (threshold, configuration, expected cost) with sensitivity analysis across cost ratios and base rates

## Methodology

**Five-stage pipeline.** Stage 1 (Probe Design): pre-register hypotheses H1–H7 with detection-vs-correlation criteria before data collection. Stage 2 (Feature Extraction): decompose activations via SAE, NLA, or directed search; report operating point. Stage 3 (Causal Validation): activation patching, feature ablation, steering. Stage 4 (Robustness Testing): distribution shift, paraphrase, fine-tuning. Stage 5 (Deployment Integration): compose with governance layers, measure operational reliability under cost models.

**Model and task.** GPT-2 small (124M parameters, 12 layers, 12 heads/layer, d_model = 768), loaded via TransformerLens. IOI task per Wang et al.: prompts of form "When [N1] and [N2] went to the [place], [N3] gave a [object] to" with 16 names, 7 places, 7 objects, ABBA/BABA structures. Clean baseline logit-difference (IO − S) = +3.73, 100% correct at sample size. Measurement: logit difference Δ = logits[IO] − logits[S] at final token position.

**SAE training.** Sparse autoencoder trained on residual-stream activations at layer 9 (resid_pre), END position — the site identified as critical by patching. Training data: 2000 IOI prompts. Architecture: d_sae = 1024 (4× expansion over 768), L1 coefficient 0.5, 3000 steps, standard Bricken et al. formulation. Achieves L0 = 87.3 (mean active features/input), 99.8% variance explained.

**Activation patching.** Per-layer, per-position residual stream patching (clean→corrupt) across 30 minimal pairs; per-head z-patchering (pre-projection) at END position. Recovery measured as fraction of clean-to-corrupt logit-diff gap restored.

**Causal validation.** Single-feature ablation: subtract feature contribution via SAE reconstruction path at END position, measure logit-difference on held-out test set. Cumulative ablation: ablate top-k selective features (k ∈ {1,3,5,10,15}).

**NLA-inspired evaluations.** Fidelity: top-K-by-magnitude vs top-K-selective FVE curves across 500 held-out prompts. Reliability: per-feature firing rate, CV, selectivity ratio, peak-to-mean ratio under paraphrase variation, correlated with causal drop.

**Robustness testing.** Three distribution shifts: (4a) OOD content words (synonym replacement for places and objects); (4b) held-out names (8 single-token + multi-token names); (4c) prompt frame reformulation ("When...went to...gave" → "After...arrived at...it was...who handed").

**Deployment integration.** Monitor calibration (ROC-AUC per feature, balanced 50-positive/200-negative test set); defense-in-depth composition (SAE monitor + heuristic parser, AND/OR); cost-based evaluation with stated assumptions ($50/FN, $0.42/FP, 2% error rate, 70/15/15 traffic mix, 5% heuristic noise), threshold sweep, cost = p_err·(1−TPR)·c_FN + (1−p_err)·FPR·c_FP per query.

**Compute.** All experiments run on CPU; total compute approximately 20 minutes.

## Results & Highlights

- **Circuit recovery (Finding 1):** Activation patching recovers the canonical IOI circuit — L9H9 single-head recovery +1.02, L9H6 +0.32, L10H0 +0.11, backup L10H7 −0.54 — matching Wang et al. 2023 in ~7 minutes CPU compute.
- **Per-name selective features (Finding 2):** All 16 names have at least one SAE feature firing at mean activation 30–52 when the name is IO vs 0–7 when S (selectivity gaps ≥ 28 units).
- **Specific but partial causal force (Finding 3):** Single-feature ablation reduces logit-difference by average 0.67 logits on preferred-name prompts (baseline +3.77). Cumulative ablation: mean Δ falls from +3.71 to +3.33 across 15 ablations; fraction correct falls from 100.0% to 98.4% — features are contributory but not necessary.
- **Fidelity stratification (Finding 4):** Full SAE explains 99.7% of END-position activation variance; the 15 name-selective features explain only 30.7% (single most selective feature = 3.4%, top-5 = 11.1%, top-10 = 21.2%). Top-K-by-magnitude: 1 feature = 25%, 10 features = 84%, 20 features = 95%.
- **Selectivity vs causal force (Finding 5):** Pearson r = −0.56 between selectivity ratio and causal drop. Most selective features (Sid 321:1, Eve 165:1, Adam 80:1) have smallest causal effects (0.34, 0.30, 0.52). Most causal features (Jane, Tom, Mary) have moderate selectivity (9:1 to 19:1).
- **Detection vs causal robustness (Finding 6):** Under prompt reformulation, L9H9 recovery = +0.96 (vs +1.02 in-dist), circuit robust. Feature firing magnitudes drop 10–20% (mean activation 27–44 vs 28–52). But ablation causal effects drop ~50% (mean +0.42 vs +0.89 in-dist); some features lose 50–90% causal force (Sid: +0.85→+0.08, Adam: +0.78→+0.18).
- **Monitor calibration (Stage 5a):** All 10 features achieve ROC-AUC = 1.00 in-distribution; AUC remains 1.00 under all 3 distribution shifts — discriminative capacity is robust even as causal capacity degrades.
- **Defense-in-depth (Stage 5c):** SAE monitor FPR: 1% in-dist → 3% OOD content → 4% reformulation. Heuristic monitor FPR = 0% by construction. AND conjunction achieves 0% FPR across all conditions with TPR = 1.00.
- **Deployment cost (Finding 7):** Optimal configuration = SAE-only at threshold 15.0; TPR = 0.99, FPR = 0.00, expected cost = $8.96 per 1000 queries vs $1000 no-monitor baseline (99.1% savings). Break-even FN cost = $0.05. SAE-only optimal from 0.5%–10% error rate; at 20% error rate, SAE OR heur becomes optimal.

## Critical Assessment

- The paper's central empirical finding — that SAE features occupy an "intermediate causal regime" (specifically causal but not necessary), and that selectivity anticorrelates with causal force (r = −0.56) — is a genuinely useful calibration for mechanistic interpretability work that treats correlational selectivity as evidence of causal importance.
- A concrete paper-specific weakness: the SAE is small (d_sae = 1024, 4× expansion, 3000 steps) and the author explicitly acknowledges it is "competent but not state-of-the-art" (production SAE work uses much larger expansion factors and longer training). The selectivity-vs-causality anticorrelation might be an artifact of this small SAE's limited capacity — a larger SAE with more features might not show the same pattern.
- The deployment cost evaluation (Stage 5d) uses entirely synthetic cost assumptions ($50/FN, $0.42/FP, 2% error rate, 5% heuristic noise) that are stated as plausible but not measured from any real system. The 99.1% savings figure is an artifact of these assumptions; the author is transparent about this ("The cost numbers depend on stated assumptions") but the headline number could mislead casual readers.
- Single-model, single-task evaluation (GPT-2 small on IOI) limits generalizability; the author does not test whether the intermediate causal regime or the selectivity-causality anticorrelation holds on larger models or other tasks.
- The held-out names experiment (Stage 4b) reveals that none of the top-10 in-distribution selective features is the dominant feature for held-out names — different features (103, 179, 181) dominate — which suggests the per-name selectivity findings are distribution-specific, somewhat undermining the generality of Findings 2–5.
- The NLA connection (Sections 9–10) is an adaptation of the NLA paper's evaluation framework to SAE features rather than a novel contribution; the fidelity and reliability stratifications are useful framings but rely on standard variance-explained and correlation analyses.

## Strengths

- Disciplined five-stage methodology with pre-registered hypotheses (H1–H7) stated before experiments
- Quantified results at every stage: effect sizes, correlation coefficients, accuracy percentages, cost figures
- The detection-vs-causal robustness gap (Finding 6) is a practically important, previously undocumented observation
- The selectivity-causality anticorrelation (r = −0.56) challenges a common assumption in SAE-based interpretability
- The cost-based deployment evaluation connects interpretability research to operational decision-making with explicit, substitutable assumptions
- Full pipeline runs on CPU in ~20 minutes, lowering the barrier to replication
- Honest about limitations: the SAE is acknowledged as not state-of-the-art; cost assumptions are stated as synthetic; the intermediate causal regime is argued as typical but demonstrated on one model/task

## Limitations

- Single model (GPT-2 small, 124M), single task (IOI) — generalizability to larger models or complex tasks is untested
- The SAE is small (d_sae = 1024, 4× expansion, 3000 steps) and acknowledged as not state-of-the-art; the selectivity-causality anticorrelation may be an artifact of limited SAE capacity
- Deployment cost evaluation uses entirely synthetic assumptions; the 99.1% savings figure is assumption-dependent
- Single-author contribution; no code repository linked in the PDF
- The held-out names experiment shows the top-10 selective features are distribution-specific (different features dominate for held-out names), limiting the generality of the per-name findings
- Multi-token names (Beatrice, Mortimer, etc.) break the SAE-at-END-position methodology, acknowledged as a real limitation
- The NLA-inspired evaluations are adaptations of an existing framework, not novel methods
- No statistical significance testing reported for the correlation (r = −0.56) or the ablation effects beyond the point estimates

## Relevance

对应"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"方向：本文直接分析 GPT-2 small 隐藏状态（layer-9 残差流）的 SAE 特征，揭示特征选择性与因果力之间的关系，属于机制可解释性方法论工作。其"检测鲁棒性 ≠ 因果鲁棒性"的发现对设计基于隐藏状态的可靠监控有直接参考价值。同时与"LLM Inference Evaluation"方向相关——部署集成阶段的成本敏感监控评估涉及推理时的可靠性度量。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the selectivity-causality anticorrelation (r = −0.56) hold for larger, state-of-the-art SAEs (e.g., Gemma Scope with d_sae in the tens of thousands), or is it an artifact of the small 1024-feature SAE used here?
- Does the intermediate causal regime (specifically causal but not necessary) generalize to larger models (Llama, GPT-J) and more complex tasks beyond IOI?
- Can the detection-vs-causal robustness gap be quantified as a general metric, and does its magnitude predict deployment monitor reliability?
- How does the methodology adapt to features extracted via methods other than SAEs (e.g., NLA activation oracles, supervised probes, transcoders)?
- What are the failure modes of the optimal SAE-only-at-threshold-15 monitor under adversarial inputs or out-of-distribution attacks?
- Can the fidelity stratification (interpreted features explaining only 30.7% of variance) be used as a quality metric for interpretability claims across different feature-extraction methods?
- Does the held-out-names finding (different features dominate for unseen names) imply that per-name SAE features are memorization artifacts rather than generalizable name representations?
