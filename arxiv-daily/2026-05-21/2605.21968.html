An Improved Adaptive PID Optimizer with Enhanced Convergence and Stability for Deep Learning

Saurabh Saini, Kapil Ahuja, Thomas Wick, Saurav Kumar · 2026-05-21
optimization adaptive-learning-rate PID-controller deep-learning-training convergence-guarantees
IAdaPID-ADG integrates AMSGrad's non-increasing effective learning rate and DiffGrad's gradient-difference modulation into AdaPID, achieving 2–3 orders of magnitude lower training loss across four datasets.

Key Metric

Training loss 0.000003 on CIFAR10 (vs 0.006 for AdaPID); 97.35% test accuracy on IARC medical dataset (vs 94.07%)

Why This Matters

Offers a principled approach to fixing convergence and stability issues in PID-based optimizers, with demonstrated gains on medical imaging classification tasks like cervical cancer detection.

Critical Edge

No formal convergence proof for the combined optimizer. No comparison with AdamW, LAMB, or Adafactor. 100% training accuracy across all settings raises overfitting concerns.

Methodology

The paper builds on the AdaPID optimizer, which combines Adam's adaptive learning rates with PID control theory. IAdaPID-ADG adds two mechanisms: (1) an AMSGrad-inspired fix that replaces exponential moving averages of squared gradients and squared gradient differences with their element-wise running maxima, ensuring a non-increasing effective learning rate for convergence guarantees; and (2) a DiffGrad-inspired modulation factor based on the sigmoid of consecutive gradient differences, which allows larger steps when gradients change rapidly and shrinks them near optima to prevent overshoot. The final update rule applies both the max operation and the modulation factor symmetrically to the PID-based parameter update. Experiments span MNIST (2-layer MLNN), CIFAR10 (5-layer CNN), and two cervical cancer medical datasets (ResNet50/101/152 feature extractors).

Strengths

  • Clean integration of two complementary mechanisms with theoretical justification for each
  • Comprehensive evaluation across four datasets including real-world medical imaging
  • Ablation study clearly demonstrates additive benefit of each component
  • Public code and data availability
  • Clear writing with precise pseudocode

Limitations

  • No formal convergence proof for the combined optimizer
  • Only 3 baselines compared; missing AdamW, SGD+momentum, modern methods
  • Medical dataset experiments use only DiffGrad as baseline
  • No evaluation on Transformers or large-scale tasks (ImageNet, language modeling)
  • 100% training accuracy raises overfitting concerns
  • Test accuracy improvements are relatively small in absolute terms

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does IAdaPID-ADG perform on large-scale tasks (ImageNet, LLM pretraining) and modern architectures (Vision Transformers)?
  2. Can the convergence guarantee of AMSGrad be formally extended to the PID-based update rule?
  3. What is the computational overhead of maintaining both running maxima compared to standard Adam/AdamW?
  4. Does the modulation factor interact well with learning rate schedulers (cosine annealing, warmup)?
  5. How sensitive is IAdaPID-ADG to Ki and Kd hyperparameters across different task domains?
