Reading Task Failure Off the Activations: A Sparse-Feature Audit of GPT-2 Small on Indirect Object Identification
Mahdi Nasermoghadasi 2026-05-21 mechanistic-interpretability sparse-autoencoders model-auditing

Key Metric

93.3% vs 7.5%
Failure rate on prompts involving “keys” vs. all other objects — Fisher exact p = 8.79 × 10−33

Why This Matters

SAE-based failure diagnosis has real limits — this rigorous audit proves the field’s favorite tool adds interpretability, not predictive power.

Critical Edge

The top-|d| feature is the top correlate in only 1 of 5 random-seed runs — feature-level fragility raises serious stability questions.

Methodology

The author audits a publicly available layer-8 residual-stream SAE (24,576 features) on GPT-2 Small using 300 IOI prompts (79.7% baseline accuracy). For every feature, Cohen’s d separates failed from successful trials, with Holm-corrected significance testing. The top correlate is then stress-tested with three controls:

  • Causal ablation — zeroing the feature on all “keys” prompts does not restore accuracy (6.7% → 4.4%), ruling out sufficiency.
  • Representation baseline — logistic regression on the raw 768-dim residual stream (AUC 0.929) matches the top-100 SAE features (AUC 0.927).
  • Seed-robustness check — across five seeds, the keys-subset failure rate is stable but feature 17,491 is the top feature in only one run.

Authors & Credibility

Single-author work by Mahdi Nasermoghadasi. Unusually transparent and self-critical for mechanistic interpretability: the author explicitly demonstrates their strongest finding is a correlate, not a cause, reports negative ablation results, and shows SAE does not outperform raw representations.

Full reproducibility artifacts released. Pipeline runs on an Apple M3 Max laptop — no GPU required.

Strengths

  • Exceptional intellectual honesty — negative results reported clearly and prominently
  • Three rigorous controls set a high bar for mechanistic interpretability claims
  • Full reproducibility with laptop-runnable pipeline
  • Demonstrates that SAE interpretability ≠ predictive power — important caution for the field
  • Clean, well-scoped research design

Limitations

  • Single SAE, single layer (8), single model (GPT-2 Small), single task (IOI) — generalizability unclear
  • Top correlate is an idiosyncratic lexical confound (“keys”)
  • Does not identify actual causal mechanisms of IOI failure
  • Feature-level fragility across seeds (top in only 1/5 runs)
  • Limited to one SAE release; different architectures may yield different results

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Would auditing multiple layers reveal causal features of IOI failure at earlier or later layers?
  • How stable are SAE feature-level correlates across different architectures (JumpReLU, TopK)?
  • Can this pipeline scale to larger models (Llama, GPT-J) where failure modes may differ?
  • Are there systematic failure categories beyond lexical confounds that SAE audits can detect?
  • Under what conditions would an SAE feature correlate also be a causal lever?
