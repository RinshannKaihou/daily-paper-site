> **一句话总结：** 本文识别出"鲁棒不可学习样本集"作为对抗蒸馏中教师依赖性的核心机制——当鲁棒教师对不可学习样本提供高置信度监督时，会迫使学生记忆虚假噪声导致鲁棒过拟合；提出教师在该样本集上的预测熵作为教师选择准则，在 CIFAR-10 上五种蒸馏方法中高熵教师将平均退化从 7.23% 降至 0.53%。

> **TL;DR:** The authors identify the "Robustly Unlearnable Set" (S_U) — a consistent subset of training samples misclassified by all robust models across 6 paradigms × 10 seeds — as the mechanism behind inconsistent adversarial distillation (AD). Using a two-layer ReLU³ network feature-learning theory (Theorems 4.7–4.8), they prove that a confident teacher (high margin on S_U) forces the student to memorize spurious noise (robust test error ≥ 1/2 − o(1)), while an uncertain teacher (predictive entropy on S_U) suppresses noise memorization (robust test error → 0). On CIFAR-10 with ResNet-18, the unlearnable set size ranges from 8,979 (MobileNet-V2) to 1,697 (WRN-34-10), decreasing with model capacity. Across 5 AD methods (ARD, IAD, RSLAD, AdaAD, IGDM), a High-Entropy teacher (entropy 1.882 on S_U) reduces average robust-overfitting degradation from 7.23% (Low-Entropy teacher, entropy 0.059) to 0.53%, and the Unlearnable-Entropy Criterion achieves Pearson r = 0.943 (p < 0.001) with student final robust accuracy versus r = 0.451 (p = 0.010) for teacher AutoAttack accuracy.

## 核心贡献

- 实证发现"鲁棒不可学习样本集"：在 CIFAR-10 上通过 60 个模型（6 种训练范式 × 10 个随机种子）的预测交集，识别出一组被所有非过拟合鲁棒模型一致错分的训练样本，证明其是鲁棒过拟合的主要驱动因素而非过拟合的产物
- 建立两层神经网络（m 滤波器、立方激活 ϕ(z)=(max{0,z})³）的特征学习动力学理论框架，严格证明（Theorem 4.7 标准 AT 二分法、Theorem 4.8 蒸馏二分法）教师对不可学习样本的置信度如何导致学生鲁棒过拟合
- 证明"坏教师"并非自身鲁棒性差，而是因为它能表示学生无法表示的鲁棒特征 v，从而在不可学习样本上提供高置信度监督，迫使学生在不可学习样本上记忆虚假噪声（AD 梯度等价于标准 AT 梯度，误差 ≤ e^(−Ω(d))）
- 证明"好教师"对不可学习样本的高不确定性在 T ≤ T_max 时窗内抑制噪声梯度，使噪声响应保持在初始化尺度，鲁棒测试误差收敛至 0
- 提出"不可学习熵准则"（Unlearnable-Entropy Criterion）：用单个 PGD-AT 参考模型的峰值鲁棒精度处预测构造代理不可学习集，评估候选教师在该子集上的平均预测熵（PGD-10 攻击下），作为高效且理论有据的教师选择指标
- 在合成仿真和 CIFAR-10 真实图像上验证理论，并跨五种蒸馏方法（ARD、IAD、RSLAD、AdaAD、IGDM）验证准则一致性

## Key Contributions

- Empirical identification of the Robustly Unlearnable Set: via the intersection of predictions from 60 models (6 paradigms × 10 seeds) at peak robust accuracy epochs, a consistent subset of CIFAR-10 training samples is misclassified by all non-overfitted robust models, establishing unlearnability as an intrinsic data-architecture property rather than a late-stage overfitting artifact
- A rigorous two-layer network (m filters, cubic activation ϕ(z) = (max{0, z})³) feature-learning theory (Theorems 4.7 and 4.8) proving that confident teacher supervision on unlearnable samples compels the student to memorize spurious noise (robust test error ≥ 1/2 − o(1)), while uncertain teacher supervision suppresses noise memorization (robust test error → 0)
- The Bad Teacher is not deficient in standalone robustness; it has access to an additional robust feature v that the capacity-constrained student cannot represent (weights constrained orthogonal to v), so its confident supervision on S_U drives noise memorization (Lemma 4.13: AD gradient ≈ AT gradient on S_U, error ≤ e^(−Ω(d)))
- The Good Teacher guarantee holds over a horizon T_max that exceeds the noise-memorization timescale T_1 of standard AT and Bad-Teacher AD, so the noise-suppression effect is not merely early stopping
- The Unlearnable-Entropy Criterion: a practically efficient teacher selection metric using a single proxy PGD-AT model's peak-accuracy unlearnable set, evaluating candidate teachers' average predictive entropy under PGD-10 attacks
- Cross-method validation across 5 AD methods (ARD, IAD, RSLAD, AdaAD, IGDM) showing the High-Entropy teacher consistently prevents robust overfitting regardless of distillation loss choice

## Methodology

**Empirical unlearnable set identification.** 60 models are trained spanning 6 paradigms (PGD-AT, TRADES, AD from Chen, AD from Rebuffi, AD from Bartoldson, AD from Gowal) × 10 random seeds, on CIFAR-10 with ResNet-18, WRN-28-10, WRN-34-10, and MobileNet-V2. Samples are categorized at each model's peak robust accuracy epoch: "Learnable" = correctly classified by all 60 models; "Unlearnable" = misclassified by all 60 models (Table 1, Algorithm 1).

**Theoretical setup.** Binary classification with input X ∈ R^(P×d) (P patches). Two orthogonal robust features: learnable u = e_1 and unlearnable v = e_d. Training set partitioned into learnable S_L and unlearnable S_U with ratio p_un. Student is a two-layer network with m filters and cubic activation ϕ(z) = (max{0, z})³, weights constrained ⟨w_r, v⟩ = 0 (structurally blind to v). Two teacher configurations: Good Teacher f_WG (orthogonal to v, uncertain on S_U: y_i f_WG(X_i) = 0) and Bad Teacher f_WB (leverages v, confident on S_U: y_i f_WB(X_i) ≥ Γ). Adversarial examples generated by maximizing logistic loss under signal-patch-direction perturbations (budget σ_n < ε < α). Regime requirements (Condition D.5): d ≥ Ω̃(N²), α ≥ Ω̃(σ_n d/N^(1/3)), Γ ≥ Ω̃(d), T ≥ Ω̃(N(ησ_0σ_n³d^(3/2))⁻¹).

**Synthetic validation.** Two-layer student network trained under exact theoretical conditions (weights orthogonal to v), comparing standard AT, AD under Bad Teacher, and AD under Good Teacher across unlearnable fractions p_un ∈ {0%, 5%, 10%, 15%, 20%}.

**CIFAR-10 validation.** Controlled subsets constructed by replacing a fraction p_un ∈ {0%,...,20%} of learnable samples with unlearnable ones (from Table 1 identification). ResNet-18 trained with PGD-AT and AD; "Gowal" (Gowal et al. 2021) used as Bad Teacher, "Chen" (Chen & Lee 2025) as Good Teacher.

**Unlearnable-Entropy Criterion.** Proxy S_U constructed from a single standard PGD-AT model at its peak robust accuracy; candidate teachers' average predictive entropy evaluated on this subset under PGD-10 attacks. Applied to a wide collection of publicly available robust models (Table 4).

**Datasets and architectures.** CIFAR-10 (primary), CIFAR-100 (Table 7, same trend). Architectures: ResNet-18, WRN-28-10, WRN-34-10, MobileNet-V2. AD methods benchmarked: ARD (Goldblum et al. 2020), IAD, RSLAD, AdaAD, IGDM (Lee et al. 2025).

## Results & Highlights

- **Unlearnable set size scales inversely with model capacity (CIFAR-10, Table 1):** MobileNet-V2 unlearnable = 8,979; ResNet-18 = 5,217–10,464 (varies by paradigm); WRN-28-10 = 1,697–9,744; WRN-34-10 = 1,559–9,682. Larger architectures consistently reduce the unlearnable set.
- **Feature reconstruction (Figure 2):** Learnable samples preserve clear semantic content under feature inversion; unlearnable samples exhibit severe representational collapse into spurious noise or distorted artifacts.
- **Theorem 4.7 (AT dichotomy):** Learnable-only regime — all noise responses remain at initialization scale (≤ Õ(σ_0σ_n√d)), robust test error → 0. Sparse-unlearnable regime — noise memorizes spurious patterns on at least one unlearnable sample, robust test error ≥ 1/2 − o(1).
- **Theorem 4.8 (AD dichotomy):** Good Teacher (uncertain on S_U) — noise responses stay at initialization scale (≤ Õ(σ_0σ_n√d)) for T ≤ T_max, robust test error → 0. Bad Teacher (confident on S_U) — noise memorizes on at least one unlearnable sample, robust test error ≥ 1/2 − o(1).
- **Lemma 4.13 (Bad Teacher ≈ AT):** Under a Bad Teacher, the AD gradient on unlearnable samples agrees with the AT gradient up to e^(−Ω(d)), making AD follow the same noise-memorization mechanism as standard AT.
- **Teacher entropy effect on student robustness (Table 2, 5 AD methods):** Low-Entropy teacher (entropy 0.059 on S_U) — average student PGD-20 = 42.46%, AutoAttack = 40.68%, Degrad. = 7.23%, GenGap = 53.20%. High-Entropy teacher (entropy 1.882) — average student PGD-20 = 54.97%, AutoAttack = 50.59%, Degrad. = 0.53%, GenGap = 28.13%.
- **Unlearnable-Entropy Criterion correlation (Figure 4):** Teacher entropy on proxy unlearnable set vs student final PGD accuracy: Pearson r = 0.943, p < 0.001. Baseline (teacher AutoAttack accuracy vs student PGD): r = 0.451, p = 0.010 — teacher robustness is a poor predictor; unlearnable entropy is a strong predictor.
- **Synthetic + CIFAR-10 dynamics consistency (Figure 3):** Both settings show AT and Bad-Teacher AD suffer severe robust overfitting as p_un increases (robust train accuracy saturates at 100% while robust test accuracy collapses); Good-Teacher AD maintains high test robustness even at p_un = 20%, with robust train accuracy saturating below 100% (student learns to ignore unlearnable samples).

## Critical Assessment

- The paper's core contribution — identifying the Robustly Unlearnable Set as the mechanism behind teacher-dependent AD failure, with rigorous two-layer network proofs — is genuinely novel and resolves a well-known paradox (stronger teachers producing weaker students) with a mechanistic explanation.
- A concrete paper-specific weakness (inherent to this analysis style): the theoretical results are restricted to a two-layer network with cubic activation and a specific data model (two orthogonal features u, v with the student constrained orthogonal to v). The gap between this idealized setup and practical deep architectures (ResNet-18, WRN) is acknowledged but the proof's assumptions (e.g., d ≥ Ω̃(N²), signal-patch-only adversarial perturbations excluding the noise subspace) are far from realistic CIFAR-10 conditions, so the theory motivates but does not formally guarantee the empirical results.
- The unlearnable set identification requires 60 models (6 paradigms × 10 seeds), which is computationally expensive; the proxy approximation (single PGD-AT model) is proposed but its fidelity to the full intersection is not quantitatively validated beyond the correlation in Figure 4.
- The Good/Bad teacher binary (Gowal vs Chen) is a specific empirical choice; the paper does not explore how the criterion behaves on a continuous spectrum of teacher entropy values beyond the correlation plot.
- The Bad Teacher framing is somewhat counterintuitive (a more robust teacher is "bad" for the student), and the paper could more carefully discuss when a practitioner should prefer a less-robust-but-higher-entropy teacher over a more-robust teacher in practice.

## Strengths

- Clean mechanistic explanation resolving the paradox that more robust teachers can degrade student robustness
- Rigorous feature-learning theory (Theorems 4.7–4.8) with formal proofs, extending the Li & Li (2025b) framework to adversarial distillation
- The Good-Teacher guarantee horizon T_max exceeds the noise-memorization timescale T_1, so the result is not merely an early-stopping artifact
- Strong empirical validation: synthetic simulations match theory, CIFAR-10 dynamics mirror synthetic dynamics, and the criterion generalizes across 5 AD methods
- The Unlearnable-Entropy Criterion is immediately actionable: r = 0.943 vs r = 0.451 for teacher robustness, using only a single proxy model
- Published at ICML 2026, with supporting grants and a clear impact statement

## Limitations

- Theory is restricted to two-layer networks with cubic activation and a simplified two-feature data model; extension to deep architectures is not addressed
- The idealized assumptions (d ≥ Ω̃(N²), signal-patch-only perturbations, orthogonal feature subspaces) are far from realistic CIFAR-10 conditions
- Full unlearnable set identification requires 60 models; the single-proxy approximation's fidelity is validated only via correlation, not by direct set-overlap measurement
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 only; generalization to larger-scale datasets (ImageNet) or other domains is untested
- The Good/Bad teacher binary (Gowal vs Chen) is a specific empirical choice; continuous-spectrum behavior is shown only via the correlation plot
- No code repository is linked in the PDF body
- The theory assumes the student is structurally blind to feature v (weights orthogonal to v), which is enforced by construction rather than learned — the practical case where the student has partial but insufficient capacity for v is not analyzed

## Relevance

对应"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"方向：本文从特征学习动力学理论出发，用严格证明揭示对抗训练中鲁棒过拟合的数学机制（不可学习样本驱动噪声记忆），属于 ML 可靠性的数学基础工作。同时与"LLM Training Stability & Observability"有间接关联——其"教师监督置信度与学生表征能力失配导致过拟合"的机制，对理解 LLM 蒸馏/对齐中训练不稳定现象有参考价值，但本文实验限于图像分类，与 LLM 直接相关性较弱。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the Robustly Unlearnable Set concept and the entropy-based teacher selection criterion be extended to LLM distillation or RLHF alignment, where a "teacher" (reward model or stronger LLM) may be overconfident on samples the student LLM cannot robustly represent?
- How does the unlearnable set composition change across data distributions and modalities (text, speech) — is unlearnability a universal property or architecture-dataset specific?
- Can the single-proxy unlearnable set approximation be improved with multiple reference models, and what is the quantitative set-overlap fidelity vs compute trade-off?
- Does the entropy-based criterion transfer to standard (non-adversarial) knowledge distillation or task-specific distillation where the teacher-student capacity mismatch is present?
- What happens in the intermediate regime where the student has partial (not zero) capacity for the unlearnable feature v — does a smooth version of the dichotomy emerge?
- Could curriculum learning or sample re-weighting that explicitly down-weights unlearnable samples achieve the same effect as using a high-entropy teacher?
