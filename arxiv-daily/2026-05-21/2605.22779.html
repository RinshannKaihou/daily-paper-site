FAME: Failure-Aware Mixture-of-Experts for Message-Level Log Anomaly Detection

Huanchi Wang, Zihang Huang, Yifang Tian, Kristina Dzeparoska, Hans-Arno Jacobsen, Alberto Leon-Garcia · 2026-05-21
log-anomaly-detection mixture-of-experts LLM-assisted message-level-detection label-efficient-learning
FAME uses a failure-aware asymmetric MoE to achieve message-level log anomaly detection at F1=98.16 on BGL with 76× less labeling, costing $10.23 total vs. $6,698–$10,047 per frontier LLM inference run.

Key Metric

F1 = 98.16 on BGL at K=100 (76× annotation reduction). 86.3% detection of anomalies from unseen log templates. Thunderbird: F1 = 99.95, Recall = 100.00.

Why This Matters

Production log monitoring is drowning in false-positive window alerts. FAME pinpoints the exact failing log line with named failure-domain labels, at orders-of-magnitude lower cost than LLM-based approaches, with fully on-premise inference.

Critical Edge

Only tested on two mid-2000s supercomputer log datasets (BGL, Thunderbird). Certification safeguard was never triggered in experiments. Generalization to modern cloud-native microservice logs is unverified.

Methodology

FAME operates in two stages. Offline (run once): raw logs are parsed by Drain3 into event templates, and at most K labeled lines per template are sampled. An LLM proposes a semantic partition of templates into failure domains (pure-anomaly vs. mixed), which is validated against K-shot evidence. A DistilBERT router (gate + selector) trained with Focal Loss routes incoming logs, while per-domain BERT experts are trained via two-phase fine-tuning (domain-adaptive MLM pre-training on normal-only data, then supervised fine-tuning with frozen bottom layers). Per-expert thresholds are calibrated for F1 with a recall floor of 0.90. Online: each log line is routed through the gate; pure-anomaly domains flag immediately, mixed domains are scored by their BERT expert. Throughput is ~1.2M lines/hour on a single 4×T4 node. The LLM is invoked only once during setup; all subsequent inference is fully local.

Strengths

  • Principled statistical motivation for asymmetric MoE design
  • Dramatic cost reduction: $10.23 vs. thousands per LLM run
  • Strong generalization to unseen log templates (86.3%)
  • Fully on-premise inference after setup, no API dependency
  • Comprehensive evaluation with ablations, multiple LLM backends, cost analysis
  • Practical failure-domain labels for downstream triage

Limitations

  • Only two mid-2000s supercomputer log datasets tested
  • Certification safeguard was never triggered in experiments
  • Relies on Drain3 parsing quality; unstructured logs may degrade
  • K-shot calibration instability at low K (F1 std up to 3.50 at K=50)
  • UNIVERSAL_NORMAL fallback scoring calibrated on small subset

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does FAME perform on modern cloud-native logs (Kubernetes, microservices)?
  2. Can failure-domain partitioning be incrementally updated as new templates emerge?
  3. What is the sensitivity to Drain3 parsing errors; could learned parsers improve robustness?
  4. How would the asymmetric routing transfer to other rare-event domains (intrusion, fraud)?
  5. Is the non-monotone K-sensitivity at K=400 a fundamental property or temporal drift artifact?
