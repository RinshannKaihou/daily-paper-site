> **一句话总结：** 本文从几何视角揭示了分布偏移导致字典解释器（如SAE、Transcoder）失效的机制——分布偏移旋转了模型激活子空间，产生"忠实度间隙"（faithfulness gap），并提出无梯度的闭式方法GAE，通过正交Procrustes旋转和约束解码器重拟合来对齐子空间，在多个模型和OOD设定下以不到3秒的耗时超越所有需要训练的基线方法。

> **TL;DR:** The paper identifies a geometric mechanism—subspace rotation under distribution shift—that degrades faithfulness of dictionary-based explainers (SAEs, transcoders), formalizes it as the "faithfulness gap," and proposes GAE, a closed-form, gradient-free method that realigns the explainer's dictionary to the OOD-active subspace in under 3 seconds, matching or surpassing all training-based baselines across multiple models and shift types.

## 核心贡献

- 从几何视角揭示了分布偏移导致字典解释器（SAE/Transcoder）忠实度下降的根本原因：OOD输入旋转了模型激活的活跃子空间，使ID训练的字典与OOD子空间产生错位，即"忠实度间隙"（faithfulness gap）。
- 提出了忠实度间隙的形式化定义 $\Delta(\Pi_{\text{dec}}) = \|\Pi_{\text{OOD}} - \Pi_{\text{dec}}\|_F$，并严格证明了它控制OOD忠实度损失中可约减的部分（Proposition 2），同时给出了二阶矩偏移对忠实度间隙的上界（Proposition 1，基于Davis-Kahan定理）。
- 提出GAE方法，分两步完成字典适配：(1) 通过正交Procrustes问题旋转字典使其子空间与OOD活跃子空间对齐；(2) 在对齐约束下通过闭式岭回归重拟合解码器。整个过程无需梯度计算，仅需约2048个无标签OOD激活。
- 理论保证：证明了GAE相比未适配的ID解释器有确定性的改进，改进量与原错位量的平方成正比（Theorem 1）。
- 实验上，GAE在GPT-2 Small和Pythia-1.4B上，涵盖时间、领域、对抗性三类分布偏移，以0.5-2.9秒的耗时在因果忠实度指标上匹配或超越所有需要100M token训练的基线方法。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies the **geometric mechanism** behind OOD faithfulness degradation in dictionary-based explainers: distribution shift rotates the model's active subspace, misaligning the ID-trained dictionary, formalized as the **faithfulness gap** $\Delta(\Pi_{\text{dec}}) = \|\Pi_{\text{OOD}} - \Pi_{\text{dec}}\|_F$.
- Proves that the faithfulness gap tightly controls the explainer-dependent component of OOD loss (Proposition 2) and is upper-bounded by the second-moment shift via the Davis-Kahan sin Θ theorem (Proposition 1).
- Proposes **GAE (Geometry-Adaptive Explainer)**, a two-step closed-form method: (1) subspace rotation via orthogonal Procrustes to align the dictionary with the OOD-active subspace, (2) constrained ridge regression refit within the aligned subspace. No gradient computation needed.
- Provides a **theoretical guarantee** (Theorem 1) that GAE improves over the unadapted ID explainer, with excess loss bounded quadratically by the second-moment shift.
- Empirically demonstrates GAE **matches or surpasses all training-based baselines** (Finetune, Retrain, SAEBoost, FaithfulSAE) on causal faithfulness metrics across two models (GPT-2 Small, Pythia-1.4B), three OOD settings (temporal, domain, adversarial), in under 3 seconds using only ~2K unlabeled activations.

## Methodology

The paper develops a theory-principle-method pipeline:

**Theory.** The authors define the active subspace $\Pi_e$ as the top-$r$ eigenspace of the second-moment matrix $M_e = \mathbb{E}[h(X)h(X)^\top]$ of hidden activations. The explainer subspace $\Pi_{\text{dec}}$ is induced by the top-$r$ left singular vectors of the decoder $W_{\text{dec}}$. The faithfulness gap $\Delta(\Pi_{\text{dec}}) = \|\Pi_{\text{OOD}} - \Pi_{\text{dec}}\|_F$ measures subspace misalignment. Proposition 1 (Davis-Kahan) bounds this gap by $\|\Delta M\|_F / \gamma_{\text{ID}}$. Proposition 2 shows the gap squared tightly bounds the explainer-dependent OOD loss.

**GAE Algorithm (two steps):**
1. **Subspace rotation (Step 1):** Given the ID decoder $W_{\text{dec}}^{\text{ID}}$ and estimated OOD active subspace $\hat{U}_{\text{OOD}}$, find the closest orthogonal rotation $T^*$ that maps the ID explainer subspace onto $\hat{U}_{\text{OOD}}$. This is solved as an orthogonal Procrustes problem via SVD, yielding zero faithfulness gap by construction.
2. **Constrained decoder refit (Step 2):** With the encoder frozen (preserving feature activations $z_i = \sigma(W_{\text{enc}} h_i + b_{\text{enc}})$), solve a convex quadratic objective combining reconstruction loss, a penalty on decoder mass outside the aligned subspace, and a regularization toward the Step-1 rotated dictionary. This has a closed-form ridge regression solution.

The method applies identically to SAEs and transcoders, and is compatible with any sparsity mechanism (L1, Top-K, JumpReLU).

## Results & Highlights

- **Causal faithfulness (Transcoder, GPT-2):** GAE leads on all 9 metrics (3 OOD settings × 3 metrics). On adversarial shift (HaluEval), GAE improves nComp over the strongest training baseline SAEBoost by 4.6% (0.963 vs 0.921) and reduces |ΔCE| by 93% (0.0014 vs 0.0212).
- **Causal faithfulness (Transcoder, Pythia-1.4B):** GAE achieves best nComp and |ΔCE| in 5 of 6 columns.
- **SAE results:** Consistent pattern—GAE leads on all 9 GPT-2 columns and best nComp/|ΔCE| in 5 of 6 Pythia-1.4B columns.
- **Computational cost:** GAE adapts in 0.5s (GPT-2) / 2.9s (Pythia-1.4B) using ~2K activations, versus minutes to hours for baselines processing 5M–100M tokens.
- **Circuit attribution case study:** On a prompt predicting "American," GAE's decoder rotation alone corrects the total class-specificity DLA from −0.55 (circuit points away from nationality class) to +1.39 (circuit correctly points toward it), without changing which features are selected.
- **Mechanism analysis:** Principal angle measurements confirm GAE drives all angles to ~10⁻³° versus 40°–90° for Fixed and Finetune. Step ablation shows Step 1 closes the gap to 0 but hurts nComp; Step 2 restores causal coherence.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The geometric framing of OOD faithfulness degradation via subspace rotation and the faithfulness gap is novel and clean. The closed-form Procrustes + ridge regression solution is elegant and theoretically grounded. Prior work on OOD interpretability focused on attribution robustness or heuristic retraining, not on the underlying subspace geometry.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Formal propositions with proofs (Davis-Kahan, tight bounds on explainer-dependent loss), a guarantee theorem, and controlled experiments validating each theoretical claim. The step ablation and principal angle analysis provide clear mechanistic evidence.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code is publicly available. The method uses standard components (SVD, ridge regression). However, the rank-$r$ truncation hyperparameter and the choice of $\lambda_{\text{geom}}, \lambda_{\text{pres}}$ require tuning, and the exact procedure for estimating $r$ is not extensively discussed.
- **Significance:** Potentially high impact. If dictionary-based explainers are to be deployed in practice, OOD robustness is essential. GAE's efficiency (seconds vs hours) makes it practical for real deployment scenarios. The geometric framework also opens a new direction for understanding and improving explainer robustness.
- **Red flags:**
  - Evaluation limited to GPT-2 Small (124M params) and Pythia-1.4B. Scalability to frontier-scale models is unverified.
  - The top-$r$ SVD truncation discards information in the residual $(d-r)$ singular directions, which could matter for models with diffuse eigenspectra (as hinted by the elevated |ΔCE| on Pythia FineWeb).
  - The frozen-encoder assumption means feature identification is not adapted—only the decoder geometry changes. If distribution shift creates genuinely new features, GAE cannot discover them.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical framework connecting second-moment shift → faithfulness gap → OOD degradation with formal bounds and guarantees.
- Extremely efficient: closed-form solution in seconds without gradients, versus hours of training for baselines.
- Strong empirical results consistently matching or exceeding training-based methods across diverse settings.
- The two-step design is well-motivated: Step 1 handles geometry, Step 2 handles causal coherence, and the ablation confirms both are necessary.
- Compatible with any dictionary-based explainer architecture (SAE, Transcoder) and any sparsity mechanism.

## Limitations

- Not yet evaluated on models larger than 1.4B parameters; scaling behavior is unknown.
- The rank-$r$ truncation is fixed; adaptive rank selection is left to future work.
- Frozen encoder means GAE cannot identify new OOD-specific features—it can only realign existing feature directions.
- The method assumes a clear eigengap at rank $r$ in both ID and OOD second-moment matrices. Degenerate eigenspectra could weaken the theoretical guarantees.
- The OOD-active subspace estimation requires collecting unlabeled OOD activations, which may be impractical in some deployment scenarios.

## Relevance

本文研究了机制可解释性中字典学习（SAE/Transcoder）在分布偏移下的忠实度问题，这对理解LLM内部表征的鲁棒性和可迁移性至关重要。GAE提出的几何视角和高效适配方法为可解释性工具的实际部署提供了新思路。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does GAE scale to models with >10B parameters where the eigenspectrum may be flatter and the rank-$r$ truncation more lossy?
- Can the encoder also be adapted in a closed-form manner, or does that necessarily require gradient-based optimization?
- What happens when the OOD shift is so severe that the active subspace rank $r$ itself changes between ID and OOD?
- Could the Procrustes rotation be interpreted as an optimal transport problem on the Grassmannian, as the authors briefly suggest?
- Does GAE's subspace alignment improve downstream tasks like steering, editing, or safety monitoring under distribution shift?
