> **一句话总结：** 本文发现LLM的推理链（CoT）会主动掩盖其对训练数据的记忆，提出Zero-CoT Probe（ZCP）方法——通过截断推理链并对比模型在原始基准与同构扰动参考数据集上的零CoT表现，有效检测包括改写在内的隐蔽数据污染，并引入连续的"污染置信度"指标量化污染严重程度。

> **TL;DR:** This paper reveals that intermediate CoT reasoning actively masks memorization in LLMs, and proposes the Zero-CoT Probe (ZCP): by truncating reasoning chains and comparing zero-CoT performance on original vs. isomorphically perturbed benchmarks, ZCP robustly detects both direct and evasive (paraphrased) data contamination, accompanied by a continuous Contamination Confidence metric.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示关键现象**：LLM生成的中间推理步骤会主动掩盖其底层的记忆（memorization），使得在完整CoT设定下数据污染几乎不可检测。
- **提出Zero-CoT Probe（ZCP）方法**：通过强制截断整个推理链，暴露模型内化的"问题→答案"捷径映射，从而检测隐蔽数据污染。
- **设计同构扰动参考数据集**：保留原始题目的逻辑结构和推理深度，但改变数值和文本语境，构建等难度参考数据集来分离记忆与真实推理能力。
- **引入污染置信度（Contamination Confidence）**：基于贝叶斯后验概率的连续指标（0.5~1.0），超越传统二元分类，量化污染的可能性和严重程度。
- **全面实验验证**：在已有模型、主动微调的污染模型、以及真实世界SOTA模型（GPT-4o、GPT-5.1、Qwen系列）上均验证了方法的有效性。

## Key Contributions

- Uncovers a critical phenomenon: intermediate reasoning steps (CoT) actively mask underlying memorization, acting as a severe confounder in contamination detection.
- Proposes Zero-CoT Probe (ZCP), a novel method that deliberately truncates the entire CoT process to expose latent shortcut mappings between questions and memorized answers.
- Designs isomorphically perturbed reference datasets that preserve logical structure and reasoning depth while altering numerical values and textual context, enabling separation of memorization from genuine reasoning ability.
- Introduces Contamination Confidence (C_cont), a continuous Bayesian posterior probability metric (0.5–1.0) that quantifies contamination likelihood and severity, moving beyond binary clean/contaminated classification.
- Demonstrates robustness against both direct and evasive (paraphrased) contamination through flipped experiments, fine-tuned model simulations, and real-world auditing of SOTA models.

## Methodology

ZCP operates through a three-stage pipeline:

**1. Zero-CoT Enforcement.** The model is forced to output only the final answer without any intermediate reasoning. For open-weight models, this is done via direct token manipulation (suppressing CoT generation). For closed-source APIs, it relies on targeted prompt engineering.

**2. Isomorphic Reference Dataset Construction.** A multi-model pipeline (generator LLM + two independent judge LLMs) synthesizes a reference dataset D̃_eval from the original benchmark D_eval. The isomorphic perturbation strategy: (a) paraphrases textual context, (b) alters numerical values within the same order of magnitude, (c) strictly preserves logical structure and reasoning depth. Only samples passing consensus verification from both judges are included.

**3. Contamination Confidence Calculation.** Four performance metrics are measured under zero-CoT: Accuracy (ACC), Consistency (Con), first-token probability (P_first), and all-token probability (P_all). A non-parametric bootstrap test (10,000 resampling iterations for continuous metrics) or McNemar's test (for discrete metrics) computes a p-value for the performance gap. This p-value is calibrated into a Bayes Factor BF_10, then converted to posterior probability C_cont with neutral prior π = 0.5. C_cont ≈ 0.5 indicates no evidence of contamination; C_cont → 1.0 indicates definitive contamination.

## Results & Highlights

- **Existing model experiments:** ZCP achieves C_cont ≈ 1.000 on GSM8K and MATH (confirmed training data of Qwen2.5-Math and DeepSeek-Math), while C_cont ≈ 0.500 on the clean GSM1K benchmark (published after model training cutoffs).
- **Evasive contamination robustness:** Even with aggressive paraphrasing (6 variants), ZCP maintains C_cont → 1.000 on contaminated data and ≈ 0.500 on held-out uncontaminated data.
- **Fine-tuned model simulation:** LoRA fine-tuned models (SFT + GRPO) on paraphrased Omni-MATH show +22% accuracy gain on contaminated data vs. stable performance on held-out data; ZCP detects this with C_cont → 1.000.
- **Real-world auditing:** GPT-4o shows contamination on both GSM8K (C_cont > 0.85) and MATH-500 (C_cont > 0.98). GPT-5.1 appears clean (C_cont ≈ 0.50), suggesting aggressive decontamination. Qwen-2.5-Math shows severe contamination; Qwen-3 shows contamination on MATH-500 but not GSM8K.
- **Ablation:** Under full-CoT, C_cont degrades to ≈ 0.500 across all metrics, confirming that CoT actively masks memorization and truncation is essential.
- **Sample efficiency:** Logit-based metrics (P_first, P_all) achieve reliable detection with as few as 50–100 samples; accuracy requires ~200–500; consistency requires ~1000 but needs neither logits nor ground-truth labels.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The core insight — that CoT reasoning actively masks memorization — is novel and well-validated. The isomorphic perturbation strategy and Bayesian contamination confidence are methodological contributions beyond existing binary detection approaches.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The flipped experiment design (testing paraphrased variants on models trained on original data) is a clever proxy for evasive contamination without prohibitive training costs. The use of GSM1K as a clean control is sound. Statistical methodology (bootstrap + Bayesian calibration) is appropriate.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code is public. Training details (LoRA hyperparameters, sample sizes, prompts) are provided in appendices. The multi-model pipeline for reference data construction is clearly described.
- **Significance:** High. The finding that GPT-4o is contaminated on standard benchmarks while GPT-5.1 appears clean has significant implications for the LLM evaluation community. The metric tier analysis (logit > accuracy > consistency) provides practical guidance for auditors.
- **Red flags:** The reliance on prompt engineering for closed-source models is a potential weak point — as models become more optimized for CoT reasoning, zero-CoT prompts may become less effective (acknowledged by authors as a limitation). The assumption that paraphrased questions have equivalent difficulty to originals, while validated empirically, could break for certain problem types.

## Strengths

- The insight that CoT masks memorization is both novel and practically important for the evaluation community.
- The continuous Contamination Confidence metric is more informative than binary classifications.
- Comprehensive evaluation across multiple experimental settings (existing models, fine-tuned models, real-world auditing).
- Practical tier-based guidance for different access levels (white-box vs. black-box vs. no-labels).
- The isomorphic perturbation strategy is well-designed — preserving logical structure while changing surface features.

## Limitations

- Zero-CoT enforcement on closed-source models relies on prompt engineering, which may become less effective as models are increasingly optimized for step-by-step reasoning.
- The method is primarily evaluated on mathematical reasoning benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH, Omni-MATH); generalization to other domains (e.g., code generation, natural language understanding) is not demonstrated.
- The reference dataset construction depends on LLMs (GPT-o3-mini as generator, GPT-o4-mini and Gemini-2.5-flash as judges), introducing potential biases in the perturbation quality.
- The neutral prior (π = 0.5) in Bayesian calibration may not reflect real-world contamination prevalence, though the authors justify this choice to avoid subjective bias.
- Sample size requirements for consistency-based detection (~1000) may be impractical for smaller benchmarks.

## Relevance

本文与LLM评估可信度密切相关，提出的ZCP方法可以直接用于检测推理benchmark上的数据污染问题。特别是"CoT掩盖记忆"这一发现，对于理解当前推理模型（如o1、DeepSeek-R1）的评估结果具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does ZCP perform on non-mathematical reasoning benchmarks (e.g., SWE-bench, GPQA, legal/medical QA)?
- Can the isomorphic perturbation strategy be generalized to open-ended generation tasks where there is no single correct answer?
- What happens when models are explicitly trained to resist zero-CoT enforcement (adversarial robustness of the probe)?
- How sensitive is C_cont to the quality of the reference dataset — would lower-quality perturbations (e.g., from smaller LLMs) still yield reliable detection?
- Could ZCP be extended to detect contamination during reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF), not just during pretraining/SFT?
