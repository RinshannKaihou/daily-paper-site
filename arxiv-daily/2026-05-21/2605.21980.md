> **一句话总结：** 本文通过转向向量因果归因框架揭示了 LVLM 中"适配-聚合-执行"三阶段情感处理机制，发现中层情感特异性注意力头与深层情感通用通路的解耦结构，并据此提出无训练推理时干预方法 VEENA，在 MER-UniBench 上平均提升 6.7%。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces a steering-vector-based causal attribution framework to demystify how LVLMs process emotions, revealing a three-stage "Adapt-Aggregate-Execute" mechanism with functional decoupling between sentiment-specific aggregation heads (middle layers) and emotion-general execution heads (deep layers), and proposes VEENA — a training-free inference-time intervention that improves emotion recognition by 6.7% on average across MER-UniBench.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了基于转向向量的因果归因框架，首次精确刻画了 LVLM 中的情感处理回路，克服了传统离散指标无法评估开放性情感描述的局限
- 揭示了 LVLM 情感处理的"适配-聚合-执行"三阶段机制：浅层完成视觉模态对齐，中层通过情感特异性注意力头聚合情感意图，深层通过情感通用通路执行叙事生成
- 发现了关键的功能解耦现象：中层聚合阶段各类情感使用不同的专用注意力头，而深层执行阶段所有情感共用同一套通用通路
- 提出了 VEENA 无训练推理时干预框架，包含视觉情感增强（VEE）和情感神经元增强（ENA）两个协同组件
- 在 MER-UniBench 上 9 个数据集上均取得显著提升，平均从 58.1% 提升至 64.8%，同时不损害通用视觉能力

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a steering-vector-based causal attribution framework that overcomes the limitations of discrete token metrics for open-ended descriptive emotion reasoning, enabling the first precise localization of emotional circuits in LVLMs
- Demystifies the "Adapt-Aggregate-Execute" three-stage emotion mechanism: (1) shallow layers adapt visual features to LLM semantic space, (2) middle layers aggregate emotional intents via sentiment-specific attention heads into the query token, and (3) deep layers execute narrative generation via emotion-general heads
- Uncovers a functional decoupling: aggregation heads in middle layers are sentiment-specific (e.g., negative emotions cluster together), while execution heads in deep layers are emotion-agnostic and universally shared across all emotions
- Introduces VEENA, a surgical, training-free inference-time intervention comprising Visual Emotion Enhancement (VEE, strengthening attention routing) and Emotional Neuron Augmentation (ENA, amplifying emotion-salient MLP neurons)
- Achieves consistent improvements across all 9 datasets on MER-UniBench (+6.7% average) without degrading general vision-language capabilities

## Methodology

The methodology consists of three main stages:

**Stage I: Emotional Direction Extraction.** The authors construct semantically controlled visual counterfactuals by pairing emotional images with neutralized counterparts (using Google Nano Banana) and neutral textual events. Steering vectors are computed as the difference in residual stream activations between emotional and neutral inputs, filtered by hit rate to ensure only successful emotion-inducing pairs contribute. These vectors capture generalized emotional directions at each layer.

**Stage II: Emotional Circuit Discovery.** A hierarchical discovery strategy traces circuits at three granularities:
1. *Critical Layer Identification*: Layer-wise injection of emotional steering vectors during inference monitors behavior change ratios; layer 19 emerges as the pivotal inflection point.
2. *Attention Head Localization*: A novel Latent Restoration Metric (R) shifts evaluation from discrete token probabilities to hidden space cosine similarity. Backward activation patching identifies upstream heads whose outputs align with emotional directions.
3. *MLP Neuron Tracing*: Attribution patching (first-order Taylor approximation) recursively traces from critical heads back to upstream MLP neurons via gradient backpropagation through key projections.

**Stage III: VEENA Intervention.** Two synergistic components:
- *VEE*: Flow-aware attention scaling that amplifies V→Q attention during prefill at upstream layers (aggregation) and V→L attention during decoding at downstream layers (execution).
- *ENA*: Sparse scaling mask that amplifies activations of identified emotion-salient MLP neurons.

Primary model: Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct; extended to Qwen3-VL-8B and LLaVA-OneVision-1.5-4B.

## Results & Highlights

- **Average improvement**: +6.7% across all 9 MER-UniBench datasets (58.1% → 64.8%), with the most challenging OV-MERD+ gaining +9.2%
- **Functional decoupling confirmed**: Middle-layer heads show sparse intersection across emotions (sentiment-specific), while deep-layer heads are broadly universal (emotion-agnostic)
- **Specificity validated**: VEENA does not universally improve general benchmarks (POPE, CHAIR, MME), confirming the discovered circuits are orthogonal to general object perception
- **Superior to baselines**: Outperforms VISTA and PAI inference-time methods on MER tasks
- **Efficiency**: Intervenes on only ~3% of parameters (Top-10 heads + Top-30 neurons) with negligible latency overhead
- **Recovery/Knockout verification**: Masking critical heads causes significant emotional alignment deterioration; restoring them recovers full capability, confirming causal roles
- **Neuron semantics**: Aggregation neurons encode contextual intents (e.g., "killing" for angry), while execution neurons crystallize these into definitive emotional states (e.g., "angry")

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: High.** This is the first work to apply steering-vector-based causal attribution to emotional circuits in LVLMs. The "Adapt-Aggregate-Execute" framework and the specific-to-universal decoupling finding are genuinely novel. The Latent Restoration Metric addresses a real gap in evaluating open-ended emotion generation.

**Rigor: High.** Multi-granularity verification (layer → head → neuron), recovery/knockout experiments, cross-model validation, and safety profiling for general capabilities provide strong evidence. The hierarchical discovery strategy is well-motivated and systematically applied.

**Reproducibility: Good.** The framework uses standard mechanistic interpretability techniques (activation patching, attribution patching). Open-source models (Qwen3-VL, LLaVA-OV) are used. However, the dataset construction relies on Google Nano Banana for image neutralization and Gemini-3.0-Pro for neutral event generation, which may introduce variability.

**Significance: High.** The work bridges mechanistic interpretability and emotion AI in multimodal models, with practical implications for mitigating emotional hallucinations. The training-free VEENA intervention is immediately applicable.

**Red flags**: The primary analysis is limited to 4 basic emotions (happy, sad, angry, fear); extension to more nuanced emotions remains unexplored. The critical layer (l19) and hyperparameters (α=0.1, β=2.0, γ=1.5) may be model/architecture-specific. The LLaVA-OV results show a -33.1 drop on MME, suggesting some architecture-dependent trade-offs.

## Strengths

- First comprehensive mechanistic analysis of emotional processing in LVLMs, opening a new research direction
- The specific-to-universal functional decoupling is an elegant and well-supported finding with implications beyond emotion
- VEENA is training-free, surgically precise (~3% parameters), and adds negligible latency
- The Latent Restoration Metric is a clever adaptation of causal analysis for open-ended generation tasks
- Extensive experimental validation across 9 datasets, 3 model architectures, and multiple ablation dimensions
- Neuron-level semantic analysis (contextual intents → explicit labels) provides compelling microscopic evidence

## Limitations

- Only 4 basic emotions are studied; complex emotions (e.g., nostalgia, awe, guilt) and mixed emotions are not addressed
- The critical layer and hyperparameters require per-model calibration (l19 for Qwen3-VL-4B may not transfer)
- LLaVA-OneVision shows negative MME impact (-33.1), indicating potential general capability degradation on some architectures
- The dataset construction pipeline (image neutralization + LLM-generated events) may introduce biases
- The framework assumes a clear distinction between "emotional" and "neutral" visual content, which may be reductive for ambiguous stimuli
- No comparison with training-based methods (e.g., fine-tuning on emotion data) is provided

## 相关性

本文与"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"研究方向高度相关——它通过转向向量和激活修补技术揭示了 LVLM 中情感信息的多层次表征回路，是机制可解释性在多模态情感理解领域的优秀实践。中层情感特异性注意力头与深层通用执行通路的解耦发现，也为理解大模型内部的表征结构提供了新视角。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the discovered emotional circuits interact with other high-level semantic processing (e.g., humor, sarcasm, cultural context)?
- Can the specific-to-universal decoupling pattern generalize to other abstract semantic domains beyond emotion (e.g., style, tone, formality)?
- What happens to emotional circuits when LVLMs are fine-tuned on emotion-specific instruction data — do circuits strengthen or restructure?
- Could the Latent Restoration Metric be adapted for other open-ended generation evaluation tasks where discrete token metrics fail?
- How robust are the identified circuits to distribution shifts (e.g., artistic images, cartoons, abstract art)?
