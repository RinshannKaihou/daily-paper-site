> **一句话总结：** Gated DeltaNet-2 通过将线性注意力更新中的擦除门 ($b_t$) 和写入门 ($w_t$) 解耦为独立的通道级参数，在 1.3B/100B 规模下取得了优于 Mamba-2/3、Gated DeltaNet 和 KDA 的综合表现，尤其在长上下文 RULER 多键检索基准上优势最为显著。

> **TL;DR:** Gated DeltaNet-2 replaces the single scalar gate in delta-rule linear attention with two independent channel-wise gates (erase and write), generalizing both Gated DeltaNet and KDA as special cases, and achieves the best overall results among 1.3B-scale linear attention variants on language modeling, commonsense reasoning, and long-context retrieval.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了 Gated Delta Rule-2，将线性注意力更新中的擦除操作和写入操作解耦为两个独立的通道级门控：擦除门 $b_t \in [0,1]^{d_k}$ 和写入门 $w_t \in [0,1]^{d_v}$，解决了此前方法中单个标量门 $\beta_t$ 同时控制擦除和写入的根本限制。
- 统一并推广了 Gated DeltaNet 和 KDA：当 $b_t$ 和 $w_t$ 均坍缩为同一标量时退化为 KDA，进一步将衰减也坍缩为标量时退化为 Gated DeltaNet。
- 推导了包含非对称擦除因子 ($e_r = b_r \odot k_r$) 的 chunkwise WY 算法，将通道级衰减吸收到前向和后向计算中，保持高效并行训练。
- 证明了标量后缩放（scalar post-scaling）在解耦门控下不成立，梯度必须将门控参数嵌入点积中计算。
- 在 1.3B 参数、100B FineWeb-Edu token 的公平对比下，Gated DeltaNet-2 在语言建模、常识推理和检索任务上全面超越 Mamba-2、Gated DeltaNet、KDA 和 Mamba-3（SISO/MIMO）变体。

## Key Contributions

- Decouples the erase and write operations in delta-rule linear attention via two independent channel-wise gates, removing the scalar $\beta_t$ tie that forces erasing and writing to share the same strength pattern.
- Provides a unified theoretical framework: Gated DeltaNet and KDA are recovered exactly by tying the two gates to a single scalar, with Gated DeltaNet additionally tying the decay.
- Derives a chunkwise WY-representation algorithm (chunk size $C=64$) where channel-wise decay is absorbed into asymmetric erase factors $e_r = b_r \odot k_r$, enabling efficient parallel training on GPU.
- Proves that scalar post-scaling of the backward pass is invalid when gates are decoupled — the gate factors must be baked into the dot products during the gate-aware WY backward.
- Achieves state-of-the-art among linear attention variants at matched 1.3B params / 100B tokens / matched recurrent state size (262,144 floats per layer).

## Methodology

The Gated DeltaNet-2 recurrence replaces the single-gate delta rule with a dual-gate update:

$$S_r = (I - k_r(b_r \odot k_r)^\top \text{Diag}(\alpha_r)) S_{r-1} + k_r(w_r \odot v_r)^\top$$

where $b_r$ is the channel-wise erase gate controlling which key-side coordinates are read and removed, and $w_r$ is the channel-wise write gate controlling which value-side coordinates are committed to memory. The decay $\alpha_r$ remains channel-wise as in KDA.

**Block design:** The token mixer produces $\{q_t, k_t, v_t\}$ via linear projection + short causal convolution + SiLU, with L2 normalization on queries and keys. Separate projections produce the log-decay $g_t$, erase gate $b_t = \sigma(\text{Proj}_b(x))$, and write gate $w_t = \sigma(\text{Proj}_w(x))$. The output is RMS-normalized and gated via a separate SiLU output gate.

**Chunkwise algorithm:** Inside each chunk, a decay-normalized recurrence absorbs $\gamma_r$ into asymmetric erase factors $\bar{e}_r = \gamma_r \odot (b_r \odot k_r)$. A strictly lower-triangular matrix $T$ is formed, inverted via forward substitution to get $A = (I+T)^{-1}$, yielding auxiliaries $Y = A\bar{E}$ and $U = A(W \odot V)$. The end-of-chunk state and outputs follow compact formulas using $K_{\text{tail}}$, $Y$, $U$.

**Hybrid model:** Inserts sliding-window attention (SWA, 2K window) after the recurrent mixer in a repeated cell (Gated DeltaNet-2 → MLP → SWA → MLP). The recurrent mixer handles long-range compressed memory while SWA handles exact local interactions.

**Training:** 1.3B params, 100B FineWeb-Edu tokens, AdamW (lr $4 \times 10^{-4}$, cosine decay, 1B-token warmup), 4K sequence length, global batch 0.5M tokens. State size is matched across all baselines at 262,144 floats per layer ($H=16, d_k=d_v=128$ for delta-rule models; expansion factor 2, $d_{\text{state}}=64$ for Mamba variants).

## Results & Highlights

**Language modeling (recurrent):** Gated DeltaNet-2 achieves WikiText ppl 15.90 and LAMBADA ppl 11.41, both best among recurrent models. Avg commonsense accuracy 53.11% (best).

**Language modeling (hybrid):** WikiText ppl 15.62, LAMBADA ppl 10.43, avg 53.97% — best in all three metrics among all hybrid models including Transformer baseline (19.22 / 13.72 / 50.86%).

**S-NIAH-3 (word-based needle, recurrent):** At 4K length, Gated DeltaNet-2 scores 39.2% vs. next-best KDA 30.6%. At 8K, 31.8% vs. Mamba-3 MIMO 29.2%.

**MK-NIAH-1 (multi-key retrieval, recurrent):** Gated DeltaNet-2 leads at all lengths — 72.6%/51.4%/37.8% at 1K/2K/4K vs. next-best 54.0%/44.2%/28.0% (KDA). This is the setting where memory editing matters most.

**Real-world retrieval (hybrid):** Avg 42.28% vs. next-best 41.01% (Mamba-3 SISO), with strongest gains on SWDE (41.96) and FDA (54.68).

**Throughput:** Training throughput drops only from 38.0 to 36.1 Kt/s across 2K→16K sequences on H100, maintaining near-flat scaling. The dual-gate overhead is a small constant relative to KDA.

**Ablations:** Both gates use their channel degrees of freedom. Keeping channel structure only in $b_t$ (scalar $w_t$) recovers most performance; keeping it only in $w_t$ (scalar $b_t$) recovers less — the erase gate accounts for most of the gain. Expanding erase range to $[0,2]^{d_k}$ gives no consistent benefit.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The erase/write decoupling is clean and well-motivated; the unification of Gated DeltaNet and KDA as tied-gate special cases is elegant. The gate-aware backward derivation showing scalar post-scaling invalidity is a meaningful algorithmic contribution.
- **Rigor:** Very high. Complete mathematical derivation from recurrence to chunkwise form to backward pass. Comprehensive ablations isolating each gate's contribution. Numerical correctness verified at fp64 precision. State sizes and parameter counts are matched across all baselines.
- **Reproducibility:** High. Public code, detailed hyperparameters, explicit kernel implementation notes in appendices, Triton-based custom kernels described at the scheduling level.
- **Significance:** Significant for the linear attention / efficient sequence modeling literature. The dual-gate insight directly addresses the core challenge of compressed memory editing. The MK-NIAH gains (e.g., 72.6% vs. 54.0% at 1K) are substantial and directly demonstrate the design's value.
- **Red flags:** (1) Only 1.3B scale evaluated — no evidence of scaling beyond this. (2) 100B training tokens is modest; modern LLMs train on 1T+. (3) No comparison with full softmax attention on the RULER benchmarks — only Transformer hybrid baseline at a different scale (appears to use different training). (4) The hybrid Transformer baseline has notably worse perplexity (19.22/13.72) than the recurrent hybrids, suggesting a training setup mismatch rather than a fair architectural comparison. (5) Training sequence length is only 4K; long-context RULER results extrapolate beyond training length without explicit long-context training.

## Strengths

- Minimal and well-targeted architectural change (dual channel-wise gates) with clear theoretical motivation.
- Strong empirical gains on the hardest benchmarks (MK-NIAH, S-NIAH-3) where memory editing is most needed.
- Complete and rigorous mathematical treatment — recurrence, chunkwise form, backward pass, tied-gate reductions.
- Efficient training preserved: near-flat throughput scaling with only a modest constant overhead from dual gates.
- Excellent ablation study quantifying the contribution of each gate independently.

## Limitations

- Only evaluated at 1.3B parameters; scaling to 7B+ is unproven.
- 100B training tokens is small by current standards; results may change with longer training.
- No fair softmax attention baseline on retrieval benchmarks — the Transformer hybrid has different training characteristics.
- Training at 4K but evaluating RULER up to 8K without long-context fine-tuning; unclear how results change with matched training/eval lengths.
- The additional gate projections ($d_{\text{model}} \to Hd_k$ for erase, $d_{\text{model}} \to H_v d_v$ for write) add parameters whose overhead is not quantified relative to the total budget.
- Hopper-specific kernel scheduling restrictions (warp search limited to 2/4 warps) suggest potential compatibility issues.

## Relevance

与线性注意力机制、高效序列建模和 RNN/SSM 架构的研究方向高度相关。擦除-写入解耦的思路对理解如何在固定大小记忆中保持长程关联具有重要参考价值，尤其对长上下文检索和 RNN-Attention 混合架构的设计有直接启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does Gated DeltaNet-2 scale beyond 1.3B parameters — do the dual-gate gains grow, shrink, or plateau at 7B/14B?
2. Would long-context fine-tuning (e.g., progressive length extension) further amplify the RULER advantages?
3. Can the erase/write decoupling principle transfer to other compressed-memory architectures such as retrieval-augmented models or episodic memory networks?
4. What is the wall-clock inference latency for autoregressive decoding with the dual-gate recurrent kernel, compared to KDA and Mamba-2?
5. Is the asymmetric gate importance (erase > write) consistent across layers, or do deeper layers develop different gate utilization patterns?
