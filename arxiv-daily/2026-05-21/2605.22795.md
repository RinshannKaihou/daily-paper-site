> **一句话总结：** 本文提出保守型drifting生成方法（用KDE梯度场替代非保守位移场），通过联合熵恒等式证明了有限粒子收敛速率$N^{-1/(d+4)}$，并分析了Laplace核下原始非保守方法的收敛性及不可避免的尺度失配残差。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes a conservative drifting method for one-step generative modeling that replaces displacement-based velocities with KDE-gradient fields; proves finite-particle convergence rates via joint-entropy identities, achieving root residual-velocity rate $N^{-1/(d+4)}$ under h-uniform quadrature regularity, and analyzes the non-conservative Laplace method showing an irreducible scale-mismatch residual.

## 核心贡献

- 提出保守型drifting方法：用KDE梯度速度（核平滑数据分数与模型分数之差）替代原始基于位移的drifting速度，解决了一般位移场的非保守性问题
- 通过联合熵恒等式（Theorem 3.1）建立了保守方法的连续时间有限粒子收敛界，给出经验Stein漂移、KDE平滑Fisher差异和中心速度平方的时间平均界
- 明确追踪四边形常数对带宽$h$的依赖关系：在$h$-均匀四边形正则条件下得到根残差速度率$N^{-1/(d+4)}$，更一般的增长条件给出$N^{-(2-\beta)/(2(d+4-\beta))}$
- 给出互KDE自交互项的确定性局部占据条件和高概率占据条件作为充分条件
- 对Laplace核下的非保守drifting方法进行分析，通过sharp companion核分解揭示不可避免的尺度失配残差项

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the **conservative drifting velocity** $\hat{b}_{\nu,\mu,h}(z) = s_{\nu,h}(z) - s_{\mu,h}(z) = \nabla\log\rho_{\nu,h}(z) - \nabla\log\rho_{\mu,h}(z)$, a gradient field that resolves the non-conservatism of displacement-based drifting fields for non-Gaussian kernels
- Proves a **joint-entropy identity** (Theorem 3.1) for the finite-particle conservative ODE, showing $\frac{d}{dt}H_N(t) = -N\mathbb{E}_t[S_N(X(t))] - \Delta K_h(0)\mathbb{E}_t[R_N(X(t))]$ with a reciprocal-KDE self-interaction correction
- Derives explicit **bandwidth-optimized convergence rates**: root residual velocity $N^{-1/(d+4)}$ when quadrature constants are $O(1)$, and $N^{-(2-\beta)/(2(d+4-\beta))}$ under general $O(h^{-\beta})$ quadrature growth
- Provides **sufficient conditions for reciprocal-KDE control** via deterministic local occupancy (Proposition 5.2), high-probability i.i.d. occupancy (Proposition 5.5), and trajectory propagation (Proposition 5.8)
- Analyzes the **non-conservative Laplace method** via a sharp-companion-kernel decomposition $u_x(z) = a_{x,h}(z)\hat{b}^{\#}_x(z) + e_x(z)$, proving convergence to a residual neighborhood controlled by scale-mismatch $\Delta_h$

## Methodology

The paper develops two parallel finite-particle convergence analyses for drifting generative models:

**Conservative method (Sections 3–5):** The author replaces the original displacement-based velocity $u^{\text{disp}}_{\nu,\mu,h}$ with the KDE-score gradient $\hat{b}_{\nu,\mu,h} = s_{\nu,h} - s_{\mu,h}$, which is always a gradient field (conservative). For $N$ particles following the center-evaluation ODE $\dot{X}_i(t) = \hat{b}_{X(t)}(X_i(t))$, a joint-entropy identity relative to the product smoothed target $\rho^{\otimes N}$ is established. This identity decomposes entropy dissipation into a desired Stein drift term and a reciprocal-KDE self-interaction correction. Quadrature lemmas then convert the empirical Stein drift into bounds on the smoothed Fisher discrepancy $I_N$ and the squared center velocity $V_N$. Bandwidth optimization balances the self-interaction term $A/(Nh^{d+2})$ against the quadrature term $C(h)h^2$.

**Non-conservative Laplace method (Section 6):** For the Laplace kernel $K_h(u) = c_d h^{-d}\exp(-\|u\|/h)$, the displacement field is not conservative. Using the sharp companion kernel $L_h(u) = h(\|u\|+h)K_h(u)$ satisfying $\nabla_z L_h(z-y) = (y-z)K_h(z-y)$, the Laplace mean-shift decomposes as $M_{\alpha,h}(z) = a_{\alpha,h}(z)\sigma_{\alpha,h}(z)$ where $a_{\alpha,h}$ is a positive scale factor and $\sigma_{\alpha,h}$ is the sharp-smoothed score. This yields the decomposition $u_x = a_{x,h}\hat{b}^{\#}_x + e_x$ with an unavoidable scale-mismatch residual $e_x = h(\bar{r}_{\nu,h} - \bar{r}_{x,h})\sigma_{\nu,h}$. The leave-one-out formulation removes self-interaction from the entropy identity but introduces leave-one-out approximation errors.

**Local occupancy conditions (Section 5):** Deterministic and high-probability conditions ensure $q_x(x_i) \geq \lambda > 0$, controlling the reciprocal-KDE term. Propagation results extend initial occupancy along the flow via Lipschitz distortion bounds.

## Results & Highlights

- **Conservative method rates:** Under h-uniform quadrature regularity ($\beta = 0$), the root residual velocity rate is $N^{-1/(d+4)}$. Under intermediate regularity ($0 < \beta < 2$), the optimized root rate is $N^{-(2-\beta)/(2(d+4-\beta))}$. For $\beta \geq 2$, bandwidth shrinkage alone does not yield a vanishing residual.
- **Optimal bandwidth:** $h_{\star,N} = \left(\frac{(d+2)A}{(2-\beta)C}\right)^{1/(d+4-\beta)}$, compatible with the occupancy requirement $Nh_{\star,N}^d \to \infty$.
- **Non-conservative Laplace rate:** Convergence to a residual neighborhood of size controlled by the scale-mismatch $\Delta_h$, which vanishes only when data and model local Laplace-weighted radii align. In the benign case, root residual velocity is $O(N^{-1/2}) + O((\log N/N)^{1/d})$.
- **One-step generation guarantees:** The time-averaged residual velocity bound directly translates to a Wasserstein-2 bound $W_2(\hat{\mu}^N_{\hat{x}}, \mu^N_x) \leq \eta\sqrt{V_N(x)}$ for one additional explicit drift step of size $\eta$.
- For Gaussian kernels, the conservative and displacement fields coincide up to a deterministic scale factor, recovering the same dynamics after time rescaling.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The conservative velocity proposal is natural but well-motivated by the non-conservatism issue identified by Franz et al. (2026). The finite-particle entropy analysis adapting SVGD techniques to drifting is the main technical novelty. The sharp-companion-kernel decomposition for the Laplace case is elegant.
- **Rigor:** Extremely rigorous. All constants are explicit, assumptions are clearly stated and discussed, and the gap between what is assumed and what could be proved is honestly acknowledged (e.g., the conditional nature of reciprocal-KDE control, the bootstrap structure).
- **Reproducibility:** High for theoretical work. The proofs are detailed and self-contained. No experiments or empirical validation are provided, which is a significant limitation for assessing practical impact.
- **Significance:** Provides the first finite-particle convergence rates for drifting models, filling an important theoretical gap. The distinction between conservative and non-conservative methods and the identification of the irreducible Laplace scale-mismatch residual are significant for the field.
- **Red flags:**
  - No experimental validation at all — the rates are purely theoretical and it is unclear how tight they are in practice.
  - The $\beta \geq 2$ regime where bandwidth optimization fails is potentially the practically relevant one (kernel-scale derivative scaling gives $\beta = 4$), which limits the applicability of the main rate theorems.
  - The non-conservative Laplace result is conditional on leave-one-out approximation errors $\varepsilon_{S,h,N}$, $\varepsilon_{V,h,N}$ that are not bounded explicitly in terms of $N$ and $h$, making it more of a template than a complete theorem.
  - The continuous-time analysis is an idealization of the practical stop-gradient training; the gap between continuous-time guarantees and discrete-time training is acknowledged but not quantified.

## Strengths

- Extremely clear and honest theoretical exposition with explicit tracking of all constants and assumptions
- Complete treatment of both conservative and non-conservative methods in a unified framework
- Sharp identification of the irreducible Laplace scale-mismatch residual as a structural feature, not a proof artifact
- Useful sufficient conditions for reciprocal-KDE control with both deterministic and probabilistic guarantees
- Careful discussion of bandwidth dependence of quadrature constants and its implications for rates

## Limitations

- No empirical evaluation or experiments to validate the theoretical rates
- The main rates require h-uniform or intermediate quadrature regularity ($\beta < 2$), which may not hold in practice at kernel-scale resolution
- The continuous-time ODE is an idealization; discrete-time training dynamics with neural network approximation error, minibatch noise, and optimization error are not analyzed
- The non-conservative Laplace theorem depends on uncontrolled leave-one-out approximation errors
- No discussion of computational complexity or practical implementation considerations

## Relevance

本文对drifting生成模型进行了严格的有限粒子收敛性分析，与one-step生成建模、score-based方法和粒子推断方法（如SVGD）的理论基础密切相关。保守速度场的提出为实际drifting算法设计提供了理论指导。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the quadrature growth exponent $\beta$ be bounded empirically for practical data distributions and common kernel choices?
- Is the $N^{-1/(d+4)}$ rate tight, or could improved analysis yield faster rates analogous to recent SVGD improvements?
- How do the continuous-time rates degrade under discrete-time stop-gradient training with finite neural network capacity?
- Can the scale-mismatch residual $\Delta_h$ be controlled dynamically during training, or does it require explicit regularization?
- What is the practical performance of the conservative velocity compared to the original displacement-based velocity on image generation benchmarks?
