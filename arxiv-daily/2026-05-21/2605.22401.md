> **一句话总结：** 使用相同模型权重对人类fMRI和猕猴电生理数据进行跨物种RSA比较，发现早期视觉皮层（V1/V2）的对齐模式在不同物种间具有鲁棒性——局部学习规则（STDP、PC）在两个物种中均优于反向传播，而高级脑区（IT）的排名则因模型容量和刺激差异而不一致。

> **TL;DR:** A cross-species RSA study comparing five learning rules (BP, FA, PC, STDP, untrained) using identical CNN weights against both human fMRI and macaque electrophysiology. Early visual alignment (V1/V2) is robust across species, with local learning rules (STDP, PC) consistently outperforming BP. Higher-area (IT) rankings show no cross-species correspondence, confounded by stimulus domain differences and model capacity limitations.

## 核心贡献

- 首次使用完全相同的CNN权重，系统性地跨物种比较四种生物可解释学习规则（BP、FA、PC、STDP）和一个无训练基线在猕猴电生理数据上的表征对齐。
- 发现早期视觉皮层（V1/V2）的对齐模式在人类fMRI和猕猴电生理之间定性一致：局部学习规则（STDP、PC）在两个物种中均优于反向传播。
- 通过ResNet-50容量对照实验证明，IT对齐受限于模型容量和训练数据丰富度，而非学习规则本身。
- 进行了噪声天花板估计、多种子变异性分析（5个种子）和刺激控制分析，为跨物种比较提供了严格的方法学框架。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic cross-species RSA comparison of four biologically plausible learning rules plus an untrained baseline using identical model weights across species.
- Demonstrates that early visual alignment (V1/V2) is qualitatively conserved: local rules (STDP, PC) outperform BP in both human fMRI and macaque electrophysiology, with macaque ρ = 0.15–0.30 vs. human ρ = 0.01–0.08.
- Shows IT alignment is modulated by model capacity — a pretrained ResNet-50 achieves ρ ≈ 0.25 at macaque IT vs. 0.07–0.14 for the custom CNN, suggesting the IT convergence in the companion study reflects a capacity floor.
- Provides stimulus-control analysis revealing that cross-species ranking differences at IT may be partially attributable to stimulus domain (THINGS objects vs. HVM objects) rather than genuine species differences.

## Methodology

The study evaluates five learning rule conditions (BP, FA, PC, STDP, Random Weights) on a small custom 3-layer CNN (Conv1: 32, Conv2: 64, Conv3: 128, FC1: 512) trained on CIFAR-10 for 40 epochs, with results averaged across 5 random seeds. **Identical trained weights** from a companion human fMRI study are evaluated against two macaque electrophysiology datasets from the Brain-Score ecosystem:

- **MajajHong2015**: Multi-electrode array recordings from macaque V4 (88 neurons) and IT (168 neurons), 3,200 HVM object stimulus presentations.
- **FreemanZiemba2013**: Single-unit recordings from macaque V1 (102 neurons) and V2 (103 neurons), 135 texture stimuli.

RSA is performed using Spearman ρ between upper triangles of model and neural RDMs (correlation distance). Bootstrap 95% CIs are computed from 10,000 stimulus resamples. Noise ceilings are estimated via split-half reliability with Spearman–Brown correction. A pretrained ResNet-50 (ImageNet-1K-V2) serves as a capacity control. Cross-species ranking correspondence is assessed using Kendall's τ with exhaustive permutation tests (5! = 120 permutations).

Layer-to-region mapping: Conv1→V1/V2, Conv2→V4, FC1→IT (custom CNN); layer1→V1/V2, layer2→V4, layer4→IT (ResNet-50).

## Results & Highlights

- **Early visual cortex shows robust cross-species alignment**: STDP (ρ ≈ 0.30) and PC (ρ ≈ 0.28) achieve the highest macaque V1/V2 alignment, consistent with their leading position in human V1.
- **Macaque alignment consistently exceeds human alignment** (ρ = 0.15–0.30 vs. 0.01–0.08 at V1/V2), attributable to the higher SNR of electrophysiology over fMRI.
- **IT rankings show no cross-species correspondence** (Kendall's τ = 0.00, p = 1.00), though n = 5 provides power only at τ = ±1.0.
- **Sharp V2→V4 drop** (ρ ≈ 0.03–0.06) followed by partial IT recovery (ρ ≈ 0.07–0.14), confounded by dataset switch (texture stimuli at V1/V2 vs. object stimuli at V4/IT).
- **ResNet-50 dramatically outperforms at IT** (ρ ≈ 0.25 vs. 0.07–0.14), indicating IT alignment is capacity-limited rather than learning-rule-determined.
- **STDP shows the strongest species × rule interaction** at V1 (−0.138): its advantage over Random is much larger in macaque than human, consistent with electrophysiology having sufficient signal to differentiate learning rules at V1.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate. The cross-species RSA comparison using identical weights is a genuine methodological contribution, but the individual components (learning rules, RSA, Brain-Score) are well-established. The companion study already established the within-species findings.
- **Rigor**: Strong. Noise ceilings, multi-seed variability (5 seeds), stimulus-control analysis, and exhaustive permutation tests are reported. The author is commendably transparent about the confounds (stimulus mismatch at V2→V4, low statistical power at n = 5, seed-0 STDP FC1 caveat).
- **Reproducibility**: High. Public datasets (Brain-Score), public code, identical weights from companion study, and clear hyperparameter specifications.
- **Significance**: Moderate. The early visual conservation result is a meaningful confirmation, but the null IT finding is underpowered and confounded. The ResNet-50 capacity control is the most actionable finding — it suggests the field needs larger models to draw conclusions about IT-level learning rule effects.
- **Red flags**: (1) Single author limits the scope of peer validation. (2) The V2→V4 cortical profile is largely uninterpretable due to confounded dataset/stimulus changes. (3) Kendall's τ at n = 5 is essentially a descriptive statistic — no ranking comparison can reach significance except perfect agreement/disagreement. (4) The untrained baseline sometimes outperforms trained rules, raising questions about what RSA alignment actually measures in this regime.

## Strengths

- Uses identical model weights across species, enabling a clean cross-species comparison uncontaminated by training variability.
- Transparent reporting of limitations and confounds, particularly the stimulus mismatch and statistical power caveats.
- The ResNet-50 capacity control is a simple but powerful analysis that reframes the IT convergence finding.
- Comprehensive supplementary analyses: noise ceilings, stimulus control, interaction effects, seed variability.

## Limitations

- n = 5 learning rules makes ranking comparisons severely underpowered; Kendall's τ can only reach significance at ±1.0.
- Stimulus domain differs across species and across brain regions within macaque, confounding both cross-species and within-macaque cortical profile interpretations.
- Small custom CNN (3 layers, CIFAR-10) limits generalizability to modern architectures and datasets.
- Human data from only 3 subjects; macaque data from a small number of animals.
- Cannot disentangle architecture vs. training data vs. pretraining effects in the ResNet-50 comparison.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would using a shared stimulus set across species (e.g., presenting the same THINGS images to both human and macaque) eliminate the IT ranking divergence?
- How do the RSA results change with modern architectures (Vision Transformers) trained on larger datasets?
- Can the early visual conservation finding be extended to other modalities (e.g., auditory cortex, language areas)?
- Does the STDP/PC advantage at V1 reflect genuine locality, or is it an artifact of the specific STDP/PC implementations used?
- What is the minimum number of learning rule conditions needed to achieve adequate statistical power for cross-species ranking comparisons?
