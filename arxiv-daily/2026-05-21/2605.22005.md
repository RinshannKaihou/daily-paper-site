> **一句话总结：** 通过对LLM的lm_head权重矩阵做SVD分解（仅需5行PyTorch代码，无需推理），可以直接从权重中揭示模型训练数据的组成、存在的不安全词汇子空间，并检测glitch token，发现RLHF无法消除预训练阶段的有害词汇子空间。

> **TL;DR:** SVD decomposition of an LLM's lm_head weight matrix (5 lines of PyTorch, no inference needed) reveals interpretable semantic subspaces that expose training data composition, ethically concerning vocabulary clusters that survive RLHF, and glitch tokens — proposing this as a standard pre-release safety audit.

## 核心贡献

- 提出仅需5行PyTorch代码、无需模型推理的lm_head SVD分析方法，直接从权重矩阵揭示模型词汇选择策略和训练数据组成
- 引入Vocabulary Cluster Score (VCS)量化特征向量中词汇聚类的几何一致性，以及Weighted Projection Score (WPS)用于静态glitch token检测
- 发现Gemma-2-2B的lm_head呈现阶梯式聚类结构（可能增强可控性），Qwen2.5-1.5B包含伦理问题词汇子空间，GPT-OSS-120B呈功能分化层次结构
- 证明RLHF无法消除预训练阶段产生的有害词汇子空间（base和instruct变体中这些子空间几乎不变）
- 提出有害词汇根因分类法（故意包含、已知遗漏、无意包含、定义分歧），呼吁将lm_head SVD分析作为模型发布前的标准安全审计步骤
- 提出SVD引导的分词器优化方向：基于VCS指标迭代重建tokenizer，优先保留几何稳定性高的token

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a 5-line-PyTorch lm_head SVD analysis that reveals interpretable semantic subspaces directly from model weights without any inference
- Proposes Vocabulary Cluster Score (VCS) to quantify subspace coherence and Weighted Projection Score (WPS) for static glitch token detection
- Demonstrates that Gemma-2-2B has stepwise spectral clustering (potentially enhancing controllability), Qwen2.5-1.5B contains ethically concerning vocabulary subspaces, and GPT-OSS-120B exhibits a functionally graduated hierarchy
- Provides direct evidence that RLHF does not remove ethically concerning vocabulary subspaces originating from pretraining (base–instruct comparison shows near-identical structure)
- Recovers the known glitch token "shokubutsu-hyakka-tsu" (ID 137606) in GPT-OSS-120B via WPS without any model inference
- Proposes SVD-guided tokenizer optimisation: iteratively rebuild vocabulary prioritising high-VCS tokens

## Methodology

The method centers on applying full singular value decomposition (SVD) to the lm_head weight matrix $W = U \Sigma V^T$ of transformer-based LLMs. Each left singular vector $U[:,k]$ is a unit vector in vocabulary space; the top-scoring tokens for each eigenvector form interpretable semantic clusters.

**Vocabulary Cluster Score (VCS):** Quantifies how semantically coherent a singular vector's top tokens are. VCS is computed as the average pairwise cosine similarity among the top-$N$ token embedding vectors weighted by their singular vector scores, measuring geometric coherence rather than semantic similarity per se. High VCS (e.g., Gemma's dominant eigenvector at 0.89) indicates tight, interpretable clusters; low VCS indicates dispersed, potentially problematic subspaces.

**Weighted Projection Score (WPS):** For each token $v$, $WPS(v) = \sum_k S[k] \cdot |U[v,k]|$, measuring how strongly a token participates in dominant singular directions. Tokens with low WPS (below $\mu - 2\sigma$) are geometrically marginalized and candidates for glitch token status.

**Models analyzed:** GPT-OSS-120B (201K vocab), Gemma-2-2B (256K vocab), and Qwen2.5-1.5B (152K vocab), including base–instruct comparisons.

**Base–instruct comparison:** The author compares singular vector structures between base and instruction-tuned variants to determine whether post-training alignment modifies lm_head geometry.

## Results & Highlights

- **Singular value spectra differ qualitatively across models:** Gemma shows stepwise clustering (S[0]=409.66 dominant), Qwen shows a sharp cliff after S[0] (S[0]/S[1]=5.92), GPT shows gentle monotone decay (S[0]/S[1]=1.90)
- **Gemma's dominant eigenvector is dominated by pre-19th-century English orthography** (long-s character ſ), suggesting substantial historical text in pretraining
- **Qwen2.5-1.5B contains ethically concerning vocabulary in multiple eigenvectors** that persists identically from base to instruct variant — RLHF does not remove these subspaces
- **WPS detects known glitch tokens without inference:** The well-known CJK glitch token shokubutsu-hyakka-tsu (ID 137606) is recovered in GPT-OSS-120B with WPS = 0.8807
- **Gemma's base and instruct lm_head are virtually identical** (VCS differences < 0.003), meaning RLHF does not modify the vocabulary projection layer at all
- **Qwen's RLHF restructures some eigenvectors** (remapping bracket/symbol clusters to multilingual lexical clusters) but does not resolve pretraining-level ethical issues
- **GPT-OSS-120B's lowest-WPS tokens include Chinese gambling/lottery spam strings** and reserved empty-string tokens

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** Using lm_head SVD to reveal training data composition, detect safety issues, and identify glitch tokens — all without inference — is a genuinely novel contribution. The VCS and WPS metrics are simple but effective new tools.
- **Rigor: Moderate-to-High.** The analysis is systematic across three diverse models, with base–instruct comparisons providing strong evidence for the RLHF persistence claim. However, VCS relies on existing token embeddings to measure coherence, creating a circularity risk. The WPS glitch detection is only preliminarily validated against one known token.
- **Reproducibility: Very High.** The method requires only 5 lines of PyTorch, the lm_head weights, and a tokenizer — all publicly available for Gemma and Qwen. The GPT-OSS-120B model appears to be publicly available as well.
- **Significance: High.** If adopted, this could become a standard pre-release safety audit tool. The finding that RLHF does not remove problematic subspaces has significant implications for AI safety practices.
- **Red flags:** The speculation about Google intentionally engineering Gemma's stepwise spectral structure is explicitly labeled as unverified but could be misinterpreted. The single-author status means less cross-checking. The ethical vocabulary finding for Qwen is documented without reproduction, making independent verification harder (though the author offers to share with researchers). The paper does not systematically evaluate WPS precision/recall against labeled glitch token datasets.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally simple and practical method (5 lines of code) with high interpretability
- Provides direct geometric evidence that RLHF fails to remove pretraining-level safety issues — a significant finding
- Novel connection between lm_head spectral analysis and tokenizer design
- Responsible disclosure of ethically concerning findings
- Comprehensive base–instruct comparison across multiple models
- Recovers known glitch tokens without inference, validating the WPS approach

## Limitations

- VCS measures geometric coherence but cannot distinguish semantic, script-level, and functional similarity — qualitative inspection is still required
- Only three models analyzed; generalization to other architectures, scales, and training paradigms is untested
- WPS glitch detection is preliminarily validated only against one known token; systematic comparison with GlitchHunter/GlitchProber/GlitchMiner is deferred to future work
- The SVD-guided tokenizer optimisation proposal is purely theoretical with no empirical validation
- The relationship between lm_head eigenvector structure and actual downstream model behavior remains an open question
- Qwen's ethically concerning tokens are withheld, limiting independent verification

## Relevance

这项工作与LLM隐状态可解释性研究高度相关：通过lm_head权重矩阵的SVD分析揭示模型内部的语义子空间结构，属于从权重层面理解模型表示几何的新方法。提出的glitch token静态检测和预训练数据安全审计方法也与模型可靠性和安全性评估直接相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

3. Do ethically concerning subspaces in lm_head eigenvectors correspond to measurable increases in harmful output generation under adversarial prompting?
