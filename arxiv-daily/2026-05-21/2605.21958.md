> **一句话总结：** 本文在四个模块的LLM Agent流水线（查询重写→规划→路由→响应生成）上发现"诊断悖论"：因果分析一致将路由模块定位为故障瓶颈，但在此处注入修正示例反而显著降低性能（ΔF=+0.683），而修补上游查询重写模块则可靠地改善结果（ΔF=-0.659），原因是下游模块已隐式适应上游的误差分布（"语言契约"）。

> **TL;DR:** In a 4-module LLM agent pipeline, causal analysis consistently identifies the router as the bottleneck, but patching it via correction injection degrades performance. Patching the upstream query rewriter works instead. The explanation: downstream modules have co-adapted to upstream error distributions (the "Linguistic Contract"), and correcting the bottleneck breaks this implicit alignment.

## 核心贡献

- **诊断悖论（Diagnostic Paradox）**：在gpt-4o-mini、Llama 4 Scout、Qwen3-32b三个Agent上，因果分析（CICA框架）一致将路由模块M3定位为主要瓶颈（ΔF₃最高），但在此处进行修正注入（CCP）反而降低性能，在两个Agent上达到统计显著（Holm p<0.01）。
- **语言契约假设（Linguistic Contract Hypothesis）**：下游模块会隐式适应上游模块的特征性误差分布，发展出补偿行为。直接修正瓶颈模块会打破这种对齐，导致"处方危害"（Prescription Hazard）。
- **共适应度量（Compensator Rate）**：用NIE（Natural Indirect Effect）分类每个任务的fate为compensator/amplifier/propagator，M3的compensator rate在gpt-4o-mini上达98.2%，Qwen3-32b达96.0%（两者均出现绝对危害），而Llama 4 Scout为0%（无危害），趋势跨三个Agent一致。
- **机制分析**：通过级联分布偏移分析发现，M3和M1处的CCP导致相似的下游偏移幅度（M4余弦相似度≈0.837/0.906），但方向不同——M3 CCP改变了可执行语义层（工具名路由），下游无法吸收；M1 CCP改变的是表面语言层，下游自然补偿。
- **CCP特异性**：替代修补策略（指令重写、模型升级）在M3处均为中性（Δ≈0），证明危害是CCP特定的机制效应，而非M3本身脆弱。

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrate the **Diagnostic Paradox** across three agent families (gpt-4o-mini, Llama 4 Scout, Qwen3-32b): the module with highest causal contribution (Router M3, ΔF₃=1.018 on gpt-4o-mini) is the worst target for Counterfactual Correction Patching (CCP), while upstream Query Rewriter (M1, ΔF₁=0.357) is the best.
- Propose and empirically support the **Linguistic Contract hypothesis**: downstream modules develop compensation behaviors tuned to their upstream's error distribution; CCP at M3 shifts the executable-semantic layer (tool-name routing, 9.9% categorical disagreement) in ways downstream cannot absorb, while CCP at M1 shifts only surface-linguistic features.
- Introduce a **co-adaptation measure** (compensator rate from NIEi analysis) derivable purely from diagnosis data that predicts patching harm: high compensator rate (98.2%, 96.0%) → absolute Hazard; zero rate (0%) → no Hazard.
- Show the Hazard is **CCP-specific**: alternative repair strategies at M3 (instruction rewriting via GPT-5, compute upgrade to Gemini 2.5 Pro, task-type routing) all produce Δ≈0, ruling out module fragility as the cause.
- Provide the **CICA framework** (Causal Intervention-based Analysis) with four layers: Failure Index F, causal contribution ΔFi, per-task fates via NIEi, and CCP with module-normalized routing.

## Methodology

**Pipeline**: A 4-module agent on τ-bench retail (Yao et al., 2024): M1 (Query Rewrite) → M2 (Planner) → M3 (Router) → M4 (Response Generator), with a non-LLM tool call between M3 and M4.

**CICA Framework**:
1. **Failure Index F**: Aggregates per-module severities scored by an LLM judge (Gemini 2.5 Flash): F = −Σ log(1 − sev_i). Validated against behavioral tool-name match (Pearson r=−0.90, p<10⁻⁴⁰).
2. **Causal Contribution ΔFi**: Replaces Mi's output with oracle (Claude Sonnet 4.6) and measures F reduction. M3 consistently ranks highest across all agents and domains (retail + airline).
3. **Per-task NIEi**: Two worlds share oracle-clean upstream but differ in whether Mi re-executes or freezes. Classifies each (module, task) pair as compensator (NIE<−τ), amplifier (NIE>+τ), or propagator.
4. **CCP**: Appends k=5 severity-ranked correction triples (input, wrong, oracle) from the diagnosis split Ddiag (n=500) to the target module's prompt, without modifying instructions or output schema.

**Experimental Design**: Paired Wilcoxon tests with Holm-Bonferroni correction over four pre-specified comparisons. Cross-family agent/oracle/judge triple (OpenAI+Meta+Alibaba agents, Anthropic oracle, Google judge) prevents self-correlation. Full 7-configuration sweep on gpt-4o-mini and Llama 4 Scout; pilot on Qwen3-32b.

**Cascade Analysis**: Compares BoW and sentence-encoder (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) cosine similarities at each module between CCP@M1 and CCP@M3 configurations to decompose magnitude vs. direction of downstream shift.

## Results & Highlights

- **M3 is the diagnosed bottleneck** everywhere: ΔF₃=1.018 (gpt-4o-mini), 1.564 (Llama 4 Scout), 1.148 (Qwen3-32b) on retail; 1.690 on airline domain.
- **CCP@M3 degrades performance**: Δ=+0.243 (gpt-4o-mini, Holm p=0.036), +0.683 (Qwen3-32b, p<0.001), −0.013 (Llama 4 Scout, neutral). Tool-match drops from 37.8%→33.3% (gpt-4o-mini) and 44.9%→24.5% (Qwen3-32b).
- **CCP@M1 improves performance**: Δ=−0.191 (gpt-4o-mini), −0.659 (Qwen3-32b), −0.402 (Llama 4 Scout). The M1-vs-M3 contrast is significant (Holm p=0.008 on gpt-4o-mini, p<0.001 on Qwen3-32b).
- **Oracle injection at M3 is the strongest improvement** of any configuration (Δ=−2.007 on gpt-4o-mini), confirming M3 is improvable — just not via CCP.
- **Contract trend holds across all three agents**: M3 compensator rate 98.2% (gpt-4o-mini) → absolute Hazard; 96.0% (Qwen3-32b) → absolute Hazard; 0% (Llama 4 Scout) → no Hazard.
- **Alternative M3 repairs are neutral**: instruction rewriting (Δ=−0.014), compute upgrade (neutral), task-type routing (neutral).
- **Cascade analysis**: Terminal M4 cosine is invariant across patch locations (0.837/0.906 BoW/sentence-encoder), yet ΔF differs by 0.434 in opposite directions. The divergence localizes to M3 itself (sentence-encoder 0.920 vs. 0.959, p=0.006), where M3 CCP produces 9.9% tool-name disagreement vs. M1 CCP's surface-only shift.

## Authors & Credibility

Yoon Jeonghun and Kim Dongchan present a carefully designed study with methodological rigor: cross-family agent/oracle/judge triples prevent self-correlation artifacts, paired statistical tests with Holm correction control for multiple comparisons, and inter-judge agreement validation (Krippendorff's α=0.772, Pearson r=0.814) supports the LLM judge reliability. The F metric is validated against a deterministic behavioral metric (tool-name match, r=−0.90). The paper is submitted to EMNLP. One notable concern: the compensator rate proxy is NIE-derived (same data used for diagnosis), making the Contract evidence correlational rather than causal — the authors acknowledge this explicitly.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Very high. The Diagnostic Paradox is genuinely counterintuitive — the intuitive strategy of patching the diagnosed bottleneck is actively harmful. The Linguistic Contract provides a clean theoretical explanation that connects module-level causal analysis with co-adaptation dynamics.
- **Rigor**: High. Pre-registered four comparisons with Holm correction; cross-family design eliminates self-correlation; F validated against behavioral tool-match; τ-sensitivity analysis (τ ∈ {0.01, 0.05, 0.10, 0.20}) shows robustness; inter-judge agreement reported.
- **Reproducibility**: Moderate-to-high. Pipeline topology and benchmark (τ-bench) are public. CICA framework is clearly specified with algorithm pseudocode. Correction pools are built from standard diagnosis sweeps. However, the specific prompt templates and the full agent prompts are in appendices not fully visible.
- **Significance**: High for LLM agent practitioners. The finding challenges the default debugging workflow (diagnose → patch bottleneck) and suggests a safer alternative (patch upstream). The co-adaptation measure provides a practical risk predictor.
- **Red flags**: (1) Only one pipeline topology tested for prescription (M1→M2→M3→M4); whether the Paradox holds in other architectures is unknown. (2) Compensator rate is derived from the same NIEi used for diagnosis — an independent co-adaptation measure is needed. (3) Single-turn first-action protocol only; multi-turn extension deferred. (4) The directional account in the cascade analysis is suggestive but indirect — the authors frame it as "not confirmed." (5) Llama 4 Scout shows directional consistency but not significance, and its zero compensator rate is an outlier that deserves explanation.

## Strengths

- Genuinely surprising finding with strong practical implications for agent debugging workflows.
- Careful experimental design: cross-family triples, Holm-corrected comparisons, behavioral metric validation.
- The CICA framework is well-specified and reusable for diagnosing other multi-module pipelines.
- CCP-specificity ablations cleanly isolate the mechanism — the Hazard is not about module fragility but about the specific way correction injection shifts output distributions.
- Honest about limitations: explicitly notes the Contract evidence is correlational, the cascade analysis is suggestive, and prescription scope is limited to one pipeline topology.
- Inter-judge agreement and F-behavioral correlation provide strong metric validation.

## Limitations

- **Single pipeline topology**: All prescription experiments use M1→M2→M3→M4 on τ-bench retail. Whether the Paradox persists in other architectures (e.g., RAG pipelines, multi-agent systems) is unknown.
- **Single-turn only**: Causal mediation requires per-stage oracle pairing at one decision point; multi-turn extension is deferred.
- **Contract proxy is not independent**: Compensator rate is NIE-derived from the same diagnostic sweep — not an independent co-adaptation measure. The authors acknowledge this as providing "preliminary support" rather than causal evidence.
- **No human evaluation**: All scoring uses LLM judges; human evaluation of agent quality remains future work.
- **Limited Qwen3-32b experiment**: Only 49 prescription tasks (pilot), using gpt-4o-mini correction triples (cross-agent pool transfer), with a smaller Ddiag sample (n=50).
- **Llama 4 Scout anomaly**: Zero compensator rate is unexplained — is this a model property, a prompt artifact, or a thresholding issue?

## Relevance

本文对构建和调试多模块LLM Agent系统具有直接指导意义。诊断悖论表明直觉式的"定位瓶颈并修补"策略可能适得其反，共适应度量提供了一种在修补前评估风险的方法，这对Agent可靠性工程具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the Paradox hold in RAG pipelines where retrieval errors propagate differently through the chain?
- Can an independent co-adaptation measure (not NIE-derived) establish a causal link between Contract strength and Hazard?
- How does the Paradox behave under multi-turn interactions where error accumulation and correction dynamics are more complex?
- Is the compensator rate a stable property of a model family, or does it depend on the specific prompt/template used?
- Could a "gradual correction" strategy (incrementally shifting the output distribution rather than abruptly changing it via CCP) mitigate the Hazard while still improving the bottleneck?
- What happens when multiple modules are patched simultaneously — does the Hazard compound or cancel?
