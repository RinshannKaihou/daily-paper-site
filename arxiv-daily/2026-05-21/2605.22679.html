Orthogonal rotation without dimensionality expansion produces more human-interpretable VLM embeddings than overcomplete sparse autoencoders.

Conceptualizing Embeddings: Sparse Disentanglement for Vision-Language Models

Piotr Kubaty, Patryk Marszałek, Łukasz Struski, Adam Wróbel et al.

2026-05-21 vision-language-models disentanglement interpretability sparse-representation CLIP

Key Metric

K = 2.69 active coordinates, IC = 16.27 at FVU = 0.35 — in original 768-dim space, competitive with SAE baselines operating at 8× expanded 6144-dim.

Why This Matters

Challenges the paradigm that interpretability requires overcomplete representations. A dimensionality-preserving rotation can expose sparse semantic structure that is more aligned with human concepts.

Critical Edge

Orthogonal transformations limit expressiveness vs. nonlinear alternatives. Concept quality hinges on a predefined text vocabulary. User studies (n=21–23) lack statistical significance tests. OOD generalization untested.

Methodology

CEDAR operates on frozen pretrained embeddings z = E(x) ∈ R768 from CLIP ViT-L/14. It learns an orthogonal transformation U = exp(A − AT) guaranteed to preserve norms and geometry. The pipeline subtracts the mean embedding, applies U, keeps only the top-k coordinates by magnitude, then inverts back. Training uses a homotopy curriculum: anneal k from D=768 down to ktarget=10, then stochastically sample k ∈ {1,…,19}. For interpretation, each coordinate maps to a direction in original space; in CLIP, concepts are identified via cosine similarity with text embeddings; in BLIP, sparse reconstructions generate captions through the frozen text decoder. Baselines include MSAE, TopK-SAE, ReLU-SAE, and BatchTopK-SAE, all with 8× expansion to 6144 dim, evaluated at matched FVU levels (0.25, 0.30, 0.35).

Strengths

  • Dimensionality-preserving — avoids redundancy of SAE overcomplete expansions.
  • Invertibility guarantees faithful reconstruction; integrates seamlessly with BLIP decoder.
  • Two complementary interpretation modes (concept keywords + free-form captions).
  • Competitive quantitative metrics with clearly superior human evaluations.
  • Well-designed homotopy training curriculum for the non-smooth top-k operator.

Limitations

  • Orthogonal linear maps restrict expressiveness; nonlinear invertible maps could discover richer structures.
  • Concept interpretation relies on a predefined vocabulary whose coverage is not analyzed.
  • Evaluated only on CLIP ViT-L/14 (768-dim) and ImageNet-1K.
  • User studies small (n=21–23) without significance reporting.
  • No OOD generalization analysis of the learned transformation.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does CEDAR scale to larger VLMs (SigLIP, InternVL, ViT-bigG) with 1024+ dim embeddings?
  • Would normalizing flows significantly improve disentanglement over the orthogonal linear map?
  • Can disentangled coordinates enable controllable image generation by manipulating semantic axes?
  • How robust are the semantic axes under distribution shift?
  • What is the effect of vocabulary size and construction on interpretation quality?
  • Could text-supervised training improve semantic coherence beyond post-hoc alignment?
