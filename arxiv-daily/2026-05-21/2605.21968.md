> **一句话总结：** 本文提出IAdaPID-ADG优化器，将AMSGrad的非递增有效学习率和DiffGrad的梯度差调制因子融入自适应PID优化器，同时在收敛性和稳定性上显著优于Adam、AdaPID等基线，在MNIST/CIFAR10及宫颈癌医学数据集上训练损失降低2-3个数量级。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes IAdaPID-ADG, an improved adaptive PID optimizer that integrates AMSGrad's non-increasing effective learning rate and DiffGrad's gradient-difference modulation factor into AdaPID, achieving 2-3 orders of magnitude lower training loss and consistently higher test accuracy across benchmark and medical imaging datasets.

## 核心贡献

- 首次将AMSGrad的最大化二阶矩估计（保证非递增有效学习率）融入AdaPID框架，解决收敛性问题
- 首次将DiffGrad的梯度差调制因子（基于连续迭代梯度差的sigmoid因子）融入AdaPID框架，解决训练稳定性问题
- 将两者结合提出IAdaPID-ADG优化器，同时对二阶矩和梯度差二阶矩应用max运算保证单调性
- 在MNIST、CIFAR10及两个宫颈癌医学数据集（IARC、AnnoCerv）上全面超越AMSGrad、DiffGrad和AdaPID
- 通过消融实验验证了每个新增组件的独立贡献

## Key Contributions

- Uniquely integrates AMSGrad's running maximum of second-moment estimates into AdaPID to ensure a non-increasing effective learning rate, fixing convergence issues
- Innovatively incorporates DiffGrad's gradient-difference-based modulation factor into AdaPID to improve training stability
- Proposes IAdaPID-ADG by combining both mechanisms, applying the max operation symmetrically to both squared gradient and squared gradient difference terms
- Achieves training losses 2-3 orders of magnitude lower than competing optimizers across all four datasets
- Conducts an ablation study on MNIST demonstrating that each added component (AMS component, DiffGrad component) independently and cumulatively improves performance

## Methodology

The paper builds upon the AdaPID optimizer, which itself combines Adam's adaptive learning rates with PID control theory. IAdaPID-ADG adds two mechanisms:

**1. AMSGrad-inspired convergence fix:** Replaces the exponential moving averages of squared gradients ($v_t$) and squared gradient differences ($d_t$) with their element-wise running maxima ($v_t^{max}$, $d_t^{max}$). This ensures the effective learning rate is non-increasing, providing convergence guarantees. The bias-corrected forms use these maxima: $\hat{v}_t^{max} = v_t^{max} / (1 - \beta^t)$, $\hat{d}_t^{max} = d_t^{max} / (1 - \beta^t)$.

**2. DiffGrad-inspired stability fix:** Introduces a modulation factor $\mu_t = 1 / (1 + e^{-|\Delta g_t|})$ where $\Delta g_t = g_t - g_{t-1}$. When gradients change rapidly, $\mu_t$ is large (allowing bigger steps); when gradients stabilize near the optimum, $\mu_t$ shrinks (preventing overshoot). This factor multiplies the learning rate.

**Final update rule:**
$$w_t = w_{t-1} - \eta \mu_t \left( \frac{K_i I_t}{\sqrt{\hat{v}_t^{max} + \sigma}} + \frac{K_d D_t}{\sqrt{\hat{d}_t^{max} + \sigma}} \right)$$

**Experimental setup:**
- MNIST: 2-layer MLNN (1000 neurons each, ReLU, dropout 0.3), batch 128, 20 epochs
- CIFAR10: 5-layer CNN + FC, batch 128, 100 epochs
- IARC & AnnoCerv (cervical cancer): ResNet50/101/152 feature extraction + 2-layer FC classifier
- Hyperparameter search over learning rates {1e-4, 5e-4, 1e-3}, decay rates {0.9, 0.99} and {0.99, 0.999}, Ki {0.5, 1, 5}, Kd {1, 10, 20}

## Results & Highlights

- **MNIST:** Training loss 0.00002 (vs 0.004 for others), testing accuracy 98.68% (vs ~98.1-98.2%)
- **CIFAR10:** Training loss 0.000003 (vs 0.006 for AdaPID), testing accuracy 80.09% (vs 76.65-78.99%)
- **IARC (ResNet50):** Training loss 0.00004 (vs 0.057), testing accuracy 97.35% (vs 94.07%)
- **AnnoCerv (ResNet101):** Training loss 0.00677 (vs 0.032), testing accuracy 95.39% (vs 94.31%)
- **Ablation on MNIST:** AdaPID → AdaPIDDiff (loss 0.004→0.003) → AdaPIDAMS (loss→0.00004) → IAdaPID-ADG (loss→0.00002), confirming each component's contribution
- Learning curves show IAdaPID-ADG converges faster with smoother trajectories and fewer oscillations
- Code and data are publicly available

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Moderate):** The paper combines two existing mechanisms (AMSGrad max, DiffGrad modulation) and applies them to AdaPID. The individual ideas are not new, but their integration into the PID-based optimization framework is novel. The symmetric max operation on both $v_t$ and $d_t$ is a reasonable design choice.

**Rigor (Moderate):** No formal convergence proof is provided — the paper relies on empirical evidence. The claim of "convergence fix" is inherited from AMSGrad's theoretical guarantee for Adam, but extending that proof to the PID setting is not done here. The hyperparameter search grid is reasonable but not exhaustive.

**Reproducibility (Good):** Code and data are publicly available. Hyperparameter configurations are clearly listed in Table I. All datasets are either public benchmarks or publicly accessible medical datasets.

**Significance (Moderate):** Testing accuracy improvements are modest (0.6-3.7% over baselines). The dramatic training loss improvements (2-3 orders of magnitude) are impressive but may partly reflect overfitting to training data. On CIFAR10, the 80.09% test accuracy with a 5-layer CNN is not state-of-the-art by modern standards.

**Red flags:**
- Training accuracy reaching 100% on all datasets while test accuracy remains substantially lower (e.g., 80% on CIFAR10) suggests potential overfitting concerns
- The comparison on medical datasets is limited to DiffGrad only (the best baseline from benchmark experiments), making it hard to assess full competitive landscape
- No comparison with modern optimizers like AdamW, LAMB, or Adafactor
- No experiments on large-scale architectures (Transformers, large ResNets) or NLP tasks

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated integration of two complementary mechanisms with clear theoretical justification for each
- Comprehensive evaluation across four datasets including real-world medical imaging data
- Ablation study clearly demonstrates additive benefit of each component
- Transparent hyperparameter reporting and public code/data availability
- Writing is clear and the algorithmic descriptions are precise with pseudocode

## Limitations

- No formal convergence proof for the combined optimizer
- Only compared against 3 baselines (AMSGrad, DiffGrad, AdaPID) — missing AdamW, SGD with momentum, and newer adaptive methods
- Medical dataset experiments use only DiffGrad as baseline, limiting comparative insight
- No evaluation on modern architectures (Transformers) or large-scale tasks (ImageNet, language modeling)
- 100% training accuracy across all settings raises overfitting concerns despite regularization
- Test accuracy improvements, while consistent, are relatively small in absolute terms

## 相关性

该论文提出的优化器改进方法与深度学习训练实践直接相关。对于涉及医学图像分类（如宫颈癌检测）的下游任务，该优化器可能在训练效率和泛化性能上带来实际提升。不过，缺少在Transformer等现代架构上的实验验证，限制了其对大模型训练场景的直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does IAdaPID-ADG perform on large-scale tasks (ImageNet classification, language model pretraining) and modern architectures (Vision Transformers, LLMs)?
- Can the convergence guarantee of AMSGrad be formally extended to the PID-based update rule with derivative terms?
- What is the computational overhead of maintaining both $v_t^{max}$ and $d_t^{max}$ compared to standard Adam/AdamW?
- Does the modulation factor $\mu_t$ interact well with learning rate schedulers (cosine annealing, warmup)?
- How sensitive is IAdaPID-ADG to the choice of $K_i$ and $K_d$ hyperparameters across different task domains?
