Represented Is Not Computed: A Causal Test of Candidate Algorithmic Intermediates in a Transformer

mechanistic-interpretability transformers causal-analysis arithmetic-reasoning

Why This Matters

Probe-based interpretability can be actively misleading even when probes achieve R²=1.00 — a methodological warning for mechanistic interpretability research.

⚠ Critical Edge

Single arithmetic task on a small 10-layer Transformer; generalization to complex tasks or large-scale LLMs is untested.

Methodology

Task. Base-digit extraction: given N ∈ {0…999}, B ∈ {2…30}, D ∈ {0…9}, output ⌊N/BD⌋ mod B. The closed-form solution provides explicit, ground-truth intermediates.

Architecture. 10-layer GPT-style decoder-only Transformer, width 384, 12 heads, FFN 1536, dropout 0.01. Three seeds (0, 42, 1337), 1000 epochs, AdamW (lr 5×10−4). Held-out split by number–base intersection.

Three-stage analysis:

  • Linear probing — OLS with 5-fold CV on all named streams & layers; initialization baselines from untrained networks.
  • Causal interventions — cumulative attention ablations (Dones→O, shallow-to-deep / deep-to-shallow); key/value patching with N-only, B-only, D-only donor changes.
  • Sparse circuit search — greedy right-to-left attention-route search with 27-check threshold sweep.

Key Contributions

  • Introduces base-digit extraction as an interpretability testbed with explicit, verifiable intermediates
  • Shows many intermediates are decodable at initialization (before training) — training reshapes accessibility rather than creating it from scratch
  • Full-route patching is perfectly selective: D-only flips behavior, N-only and B-only have zero effect
  • Sparse circuit reveals factorized N, B, D routes that combine only at output positions
  • Concrete bounded demonstration that probe-based conclusions can diverge sharply from causal observations
  • 5-layer companion model confirms the same qualitative pattern (99.89% accuracy, same D-selectivity)

Strengths

  • Exceptionally clean experimental design with known ground-truth intermediates
  • Three converging methods (probes, causal interventions, circuit search) triangulate the same conclusion
  • Initialization-probe comparison is a genuinely novel control revealing architectural vs. learned decodability
  • D-only selectivity (0% N-transfer, 0% B-transfer, 99.84% D-transfer) is strikingly clean
  • Authors honest about scope; cross-seed circuit stability at 94.44% IoU

Limitations

  • Single arithmetic task; generalization to language, vision, or large models untested
  • Output-stream computation not decomposed — final integration mechanism opaque
  • Initialization decodability may be specific to how tokenized numbers embed in residual streams
  • No proposed method for detecting probe-causal divergence without known ground truth
  • 5-layer companion confirms depth robustness only, not width, data scale, or task complexity

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does probe-causal divergence persist for larger Transformers (1B+) or more complex multi-step arithmetic?
  2. Can we develop diagnostics to detect misleading probes without ground-truth intermediates?
  3. Why does the model represent intermediates it does not use — superposition, shared weights, or something else?
  4. Would nonlinear / MLP probes show the same divergence from causal analysis?
  5. What is the output-stream integration mechanism — a recognizable algorithm or heuristic computation?
