> **一句话总结：** CEDAR通过学习正交变换U = exp(A - A^T)与top-k稀疏瓶颈，在CLIP原始768维空间内将嵌入解耦为轴对齐的可解释语义坐标，用户研究表明其概念解释显著优于Matryoshka稀疏自编码器(MSAE)。

> **TL;DR:** CEDAR learns an invertible orthogonal transformation (parameterized as the matrix exponential of a skew-symmetric matrix) to reparameterize pretrained VLM embeddings within their original dimensionality, achieving competitive reconstruction-sparsity trade-offs vs. SAEs while producing more human-interpretable concept explanations validated through user studies.

## 核心贡献

- 提出CEDAR方法，通过正交变换U = exp(A - A^T)与top-k稀疏选择算子，在不扩展嵌入维度（保持原始768维）的前提下实现语义解耦，保证变换可逆且保持嵌入空间的几何结构。
- 核心假设：预训练嵌入中的"纠缠"源于基不对齐而非维度不足，通过适当的基变换即可暴露出稀疏、轴对齐的语义结构。
- 在CLIP架构中，将解耦后的坐标轴与文本词汇表中的概念通过余弦相似度对齐，实现基于概念的解释；在BLIP等生成式模型中，通过文本解码器从稀疏嵌入生成自然语言描述。
- 用户研究（23/22/21名参与者）表明CEDAR的概念解释在方法偏好、概念选择准确率和描述质量上均显著优于MSAE。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces CEDAR, a post-hoc method that learns an orthogonal invertible transformation U = exp(A - A^T) to reparameterize pretrained VLM embeddings, enforcing sparsity via top-k selection without dimensionality expansion.
- Proposes a training curriculum using homotopy-style annealing: starting from k = D (identity reconstruction) and gradually transitioning to the target sparsity k_target = 10, followed by stochastic k sampling from U({1, ..., 2k_target - 1}) for robustness.
- Demonstrates two interpretation modes: (1) concept-based explanations via cosine similarity alignment with a text vocabulary in CLIP, and (2) free-form natural language descriptions via the text decoder in BLIP.
- Achieves competitive information capacity (IC) compared to SAE baselines operating in 8x expanded space (6144 dim), confirming that semantic structure can be recovered without overcomplete representations.
- Conducts three user studies showing CEDAR is consistently preferred over MSAE, with higher concept selection rates and description quality close to the dense (non-compressed) baseline.

## Methodology

CEDAR operates on frozen pretrained embeddings z = E(x) ∈ R^D (D = 768 for CLIP ViT-L/14). The method learns an orthogonal linear transformation U parameterized as U = exp(A - A^T), where A is a learnable matrix. This guarantees U^T U = I (orthogonality) and invertibility, preserving embedding norms and geometry.

The pipeline works as follows: (1) subtract the mean embedding b computed over the training set; (2) apply the transformation z̃ = U(z - b); (3) apply top-k operator Π_k retaining only the k largest-magnitude coordinates; (4) reconstruct via U^{-1} and add back the mean. The training objective minimizes the L1 reconstruction error: min_E ||z - U^{-1} Π_k(U(z - b)) - b||_1.

A key design choice is the training curriculum: k(t) = D + (t/τ)(k_target - D) for t ≤ τ, transitioning from dense reconstruction (k = D) to sparse projection (k = k_target = 10). After reaching k_target, k is randomly sampled from U({1, ..., 2k_target - 1}) to prevent overfitting to a fixed support size.

For interpretation, each coordinate d in the transformed space maps to a direction c_d = U^{-1}_{[d,:]} in the original space. In CLIP, concepts are identified via nearest text embedding by cosine similarity. In BLIP, sparse reconstructions are passed through the frozen text decoder to generate captions.

Baselines include MSAE, TopK-SAE (k=64), ReLU-SAE (λ=0.01), and BatchTopK-SAE (k=32), all trained on ImageNet-1K with 8x expansion (6144 dim). Evaluation uses matched FVU levels (0.25, 0.3, 0.35) controlled via threshold binary search.

## Results & Highlights

- **Reconstruction-sparsity trade-off:** At FVU = 0.35 (highest sparsity), CEDAR achieves competitive performance with K = 2.69 active coordinates and IC = 16.27 (top-2 among all methods), compared to BatchTopK's K = 1.59/IC = 13.29 and TopK's K = 2.79/IC = 22.46. CEDAR achieves comparable reconstruction fidelity with lower information capacity.
- **Semantic preservation:** CKNNA and linear probing cross-entropy show CEDAR maintains semantic structure comparably to the strongest SAE baselines across all FVU levels, though TopK achieves the highest CKNNA in most regimes.
- **User Study 1 (Method preference):** Participants consistently preferred CEDAR over MSAE across all preference levels, with a significant share expressing strong preference for CEDAR.
- **User Study 2 (Concept selection):** CEDAR concepts showed significantly higher selection density in top-tier categories (2/3 or 3/3 selected), while MSAE's distribution skewed toward 0/3 or 1/3 selections.
- **User Study 3 (Description quality):** CEDAR's sparse descriptions closely matched the dense baseline quality and substantially outperformed the non-disentangled sparse variant, which was dominated by "does not describe" ratings.
- **Qualitative analysis:** Neurons in the disentangled space show semantic consistency — e.g., Neuron 347 consistently activates for "lure" across diverse fishing scenes; CEDAR captures cleaner concepts ("dog", "husky", "wolf") vs. MSAE's noisier results ("marley", "petrol").
- **Inherent losslessness:** Since CEDAR uses an invertible transformation, it is lossless by default when k = D; sparsity is a controlled compression rather than an irreversible projection.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core idea — replacing overcomplete SAE expansion with an invertible dimensionality-preserving rotation — is conceptually clean and well-motivated. The parameterization U = exp(A - A^T) is a known trick for ensuring orthogonality; the novelty lies in combining it with top-k sparsity for interpretability of VLM embeddings. The homotopy curriculum for training is a practical contribution.
- **Rigor:** The experimental protocol follows the MSAE framework closely, with matched FVU levels and multiple complementary metrics (FVU, IC, CS, LP, CKNNA). Three separate user studies with 21-23 participants provide human evaluation. However, the user studies are relatively small-scale and lack statistical significance tests (e.g., p-values or confidence intervals).
- **Reproducibility:** The method is straightforward to implement (orthogonal matrix via skew-symmetric exponential + top-k). Training details (curriculum, k_target=10, k_max=19) are clearly specified. All baselines use the official MSAE codebase. Code release is not mentioned.
- **Significance:** The paper makes a compelling argument that SAE's dimensionality expansion may be unnecessary for VLM interpretability. If validated on larger models and more diverse architectures, this could shift the paradigm. The user study results are persuasive but preliminary.
- **Red flags:** (1) The orthogonal transformation limits expressiveness — the authors acknowledge this and suggest normalizing flows as future work. (2) CEDAR uses the same 768-dim space while SAE baselines expand to 6144, making IC comparisons somewhat unfair since CEDAR's IC ceiling is log(C(768,k)) vs. log(C(6144,k)) for SAEs. (3) The text vocabulary for concept alignment is predefined — concept quality depends heavily on vocabulary choice. (4) The paper does not address whether the learned transformation generalizes to out-of-distribution inputs.

## Strengths

- Dimensionality-preserving approach is both elegant and practical — avoids the redundancy and geometric distortion inherent in SAE overcomplete expansions.
- The invertibility guarantee enables faithful reconstruction and seamless integration with downstream VLM components (e.g., BLIP text decoder).
- Supports two complementary interpretation modes (concept keywords and free-form captions) within a unified framework.
- Competitive with state-of-the-art SAE variants on quantitative metrics while achieving clearly superior human evaluations.
- Training curriculum (homotopy annealing + stochastic k sampling) is well-designed for the non-smooth top-k operator.
- Clear qualitative evidence of semantic consistency in disentangled neurons.

## Limitations

- Orthogonal transformations restrict the method's expressiveness; nonlinear invertible mappings (normalizing flows) could potentially discover richer structures.
- Concept interpretation relies on a predefined text vocabulary — coverage and granularity depend on vocabulary design, not addressed in the paper.
- Evaluation is limited to CLIP ViT-L/14 (768-dim) and ImageNet-1K; scalability to larger models (e.g., SigLIP, InternVL) or higher-dimensional embeddings is untested.
- The user studies are small (21-23 participants) and lack statistical significance reporting.
- No analysis of out-of-distribution generalization of the learned transformation.
- The paper acknowledges that incorporating text-based supervision into training could improve semantic alignment, but this remains future work.

## Relevance

本文提出了一种在不扩展维度的情况下解耦视觉-语言模型嵌入的方法，其核心洞察——纠缠源于基不对齐而非维度不足——对理解和解释大规模多模态模型内部表示具有直接的方法论参考价值，用户研究验证了其解释质量优于传统稀疏自编码器。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does CEDAR perform on larger VLMs (e.g., SigLIP, InternVL, CLIP with ViT-bigG) with embedding dimensions of 1024+?
- Would nonlinear invertible mappings (normalizing flows) significantly improve disentanglement quality over the orthogonal linear transformation?
- Can CEDAR's disentangled coordinates be leveraged for controllable image generation or editing by manipulating individual semantic axes?
- How robust is the learned transformation to distribution shift — do the discovered semantic axes remain meaningful on out-of-distribution inputs?
- What is the effect of vocabulary size and construction on concept-based interpretation quality?
- Could text-supervised training objectives (e.g., aligning axes with caption concepts during training) improve semantic coherence beyond post-hoc alignment?
