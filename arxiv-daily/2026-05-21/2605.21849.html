Geometry-Adaptive Explainer for Faithful Dictionary-Based Interpretability under Distribution Shift

Sungjun Lim, Heedong Kim, Andrew Lee, Kyungwoo Song · 2026-05-21

Distribution shift rotates a model's activation subspace, breaking dictionary-based explainers (SAEs, Transcoders). A closed-form, gradient-free realignment method (GAE) fixes this in under 3 seconds, matching or beating baselines that require 100M tokens of retraining.

Key Metric

GAE adapts in 0.5–2.9 seconds using ~2K unlabeled OOD activations, versus minutes-to-hours for baselines processing 5M–100M tokens. On adversarial shift: 93% reduction in |ΔCE| over strongest baseline.

Why This Matters

Dictionary-based explainers degrade silently under distribution shift. GAE provides a practical, theoretically grounded fix that makes interpretability tools deployable in real OOD scenarios without expensive retraining.

Critical Edge

The "faithfulness gap" is formalized and proven to tightly control OOD loss. GAE's two-step Procrustes rotation + ridge regression has a deterministic improvement guarantee (Theorem 1) with excess loss bounded by the square of second-moment shift.

Methodology

The paper defines the active subspace Πe as the top-r eigenspace of the second-moment matrix of hidden activations. The explainer subspace Πdec comes from the decoder's top-r left singular vectors. The faithfulness gap Δ(Πdec) = ‖ΠOOD − Πdec‖F measures misalignment. Davis-Kahan bounds the gap by second-moment shift; Proposition 2 shows the gap squared bounds the explainer-dependent OOD loss.

GAE runs in two closed-form steps: (1) Subspace rotation via orthogonal Procrustes (SVD) aligns the ID explainer subspace onto the estimated OOD-active subspace, driving the faithfulness gap to zero. (2) Constrained decoder refit via ridge regression reconstructs the decoder within the aligned subspace while preserving feature activations from the frozen encoder. The method applies identically to SAEs and Transcoders with any sparsity mechanism (L1, Top-K, JumpReLU).

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical framework with formal bounds and deterministic improvement guarantee.
  • Orders of magnitude faster than training-based baselines while matching or exceeding their performance.
  • Consistent results across two models, three shift types, and both SAE and Transcoder architectures.
  • Two-step ablation confirms both rotation and refit are necessary and complementary.

Limitations

  • Evaluated only on models up to 1.4B parameters; scaling to frontier models unknown.
  • Frozen encoder means genuinely new OOD features cannot be discovered.
  • Assumes a clear eigengap at rank r; degenerate eigenspectra weaken guarantees.
  • Rank-r truncation is fixed; adaptive rank selection is future work.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does GAE scale to models with >10B parameters where eigenspectra may be flatter?
  • Can the encoder also be adapted in closed form, or does that require gradient-based optimization?
  • What happens when OOD shift is severe enough that the active subspace rank itself changes?
  • Could the Procrustes rotation be interpreted as optimal transport on the Grassmannian?
  • Does GAE's alignment improve downstream tasks like steering, editing, or safety monitoring?
