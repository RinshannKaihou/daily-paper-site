One LR Doesn't Fit All: Heavy-Tail Guided Layerwise Learning Rates for LLMs
Di He, Songjun Tu, Keyu Wang, Lu Yin, Shiwei Liu · 2026-05-21
learning-rate-scheduling HT-SR-theory LLM-pretraining layerwise-optimization spectral-analysis
Transformer layers have fundamentally heterogeneous spectral properties — attention layers show weaker heavy-tailedness than FFN/embedding layers, making uniform learning rates inherently suboptimal and costing up to 1.5x in training efficiency.

Key Metric

Zero-shot accuracy: 47.09% → 49.02% (LLaMA-1B), 48.58% → 50.61% (LLaMA-3B). Up to 1.5x training speedup — LLR with 3B tokens matches Uniform at 4.5B tokens.

Why This Matters

Principled, theory-grounded layerwise LR that needs minimal tuning (inherits near-optimal LR from uniform baseline). Delivers consistent gains across architectures, optimizers, and scales (60M–3B).

Critical Edge

Only validated up to 3B parameters; frontier-scale (70B+) untested. Scaling factor s=5 is fixed across all experiments with limited sensitivity analysis. ESD overhead not benchmarked in wall-clock terms.

Methodology

LLR periodically computes the Empirical Spectral Density (ESD) of each layer's weight correlation matrix and fits a power-law distribution to quantify heavy-tailedness via the Hill estimator on the largest half of eigenvalues. Layers with weaker heavy-tailedness (lower PL_Alpha_Hill) receive larger learning rates through a bounded scaling function mapping to [η, sη]. Three LLM-specific designs: (1) embedding/output layers fixed at upper-bound LR, (2) soft LR switching via linear interpolation over ~2.6M tokens to avoid spikes, (3) efficient active phase restricting spectral updates to the first ~20% of training tokens, after which statistics stabilize.

Strengths

  • Principled HT-SR theory grounding, not heuristic
  • Low tuning overhead — LR transfers from uniform baseline
  • Consistent gains across architectures, optimizers, scales
  • Three well-motivated LLM-specific designs with ablation support

Limitations

  • Only tested up to 3B; 70B+ unverified
  • s=5 fixed with limited sensitivity analysis
  • ESD overhead not precisely benchmarked
  • Fine-tuning gains marginal (53.58% vs 52.94%)

Open Questions

  • Does the spectral heterogeneity pattern persist through instruction tuning / RLHF?
  • Can PL_Alpha_Hill serve as a training monitoring signal to detect layer collapse early?
  • How does LLR interact with distributed training (gradient accumulation, mixed precision)?
  • Does optimal s change with model scale — is there a principled way to set it?
