> **一句话总结：** 本文提出SITN方法，利用连续归一化流的噪声空间中的因子化结构，将OOD检测转化为对单样本进行边缘正态性和维度独立性检验的拟合优度测试，在标准基准上一致优于基于似然的基线方法。

> **TL;DR:** SITN reframes OOD detection as a single-sample goodness-of-fit test in the noise space of continuous normalising flows, testing marginal normality (Anderson-Darling) and dimensional independence (spectral CV) against the factorised Gaussian prior, achieving consistently superior AUROC over likelihood-based baselines on cross-dataset and synthetic perturbation benchmarks.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了一个新框架，利用连续归一化流（CNF）的质量保持和微分同胚性质，将OOD检测转化为噪声空间中的统计拟合优度检验。
- 具体方法SITN利用标准高斯先验的完全因子化结构，将单个高维样本的各维度视为来自一维标准正态的i.i.d.样本，从而在单样本层面进行检验。
- SITN不需要OOD数据、计算开销极小（仅需O(D log D)的排序和FFT），并且可以严格控制假阳性率（Type I error）。
- 实验证明SITN不存在似然方法固有的复杂度偏差（complexity bias），在跨数据集和合成扰动基准上一致优于基线方法。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a theoretically grounded framework that reframes OOD detection as single-sample goodness-of-fit testing against the factorised Gaussian noise prior of CNFs.
- Proposes SITN, combining the Anderson-Darling statistic (marginal normality) and the coefficient of variation of the power spectrum (dimensional independence) via a max-quantile aggregation strategy.
- Proves Type I error control via DKW inequality with a split-calibration procedure, requiring no OOD data.
- Demonstrates that SITN is free from the complexity bias that plagues likelihood-based methods (Typicality, DoSE), consistently outperforming baselines across all cross-dataset settings and synthetic perturbations (CIFAR-10-C).

## Methodology

SITN operates in the noise space of a continuous normalising flow trained via flow matching. The key insight is that CNFs define a diffeomorphic, mass-preserving mapping φ from a standard Gaussian prior N(0, I) to the data distribution. For an ID sample X, the inverse mapping Z = φ⁻¹(X) should follow N(0, I). OOD samples, by contrast, map to noise vectors that deviate from this prior in detectable ways that are *not* captured by the likelihood.

Because the multivariate standard Gaussian factorises completely, the D elements of a single high-dimensional noise vector Z can be treated as D i.i.d. samples from N(0, 1). This enables two single-sample tests:

1. **Marginal Normality** (Anderson-Darling statistic S_AD): Tests whether the elements of Z conform to the standard univariate normal CDF. Sensitive to anomalous mean, incorrect variance, or distorted distributional shape.
2. **Dimensional Independence** (Coefficient of Variation of the Power Spectrum S_CV): Tests for autocorrelation via the spectral domain. For white Gaussian noise, the power spectrum follows an exponential distribution (σ = μ), yielding S_CV = 1. Structural correlations inflate S_CV above 1.

The two statistics are combined via a **max-quantile strategy**: both are quantile-transformed using empirical CDFs estimated on an ID calibration set, and the final OOD score is S_SITN = max(F̂_AD(S_AD), F̂_CV(S_CV)). Calibration using a split validation set provides provable Type I error control via the DKW inequality.

## Results & Highlights

- **Cross-dataset AUROC**: SITN achieves 0.946 on CIFAR-10→SVHN (vs 0.802 for DoSE, 0.790 for Typicality), 0.671 on CIFAR-10→CelebA (vs 0.432, 0.421), and 0.910/0.986 on CelebA→CIFAR-10/SVHN where baselines severely fail.
- **Synthetic perturbations (CIFAR-10-C)**: SITN achieves a macro-average AUROC of 0.85 across 19 corruption types, compared to 0.59 (likelihood), 0.69 (Typicality), and 0.78 (DoSE). SITN's minimum AUROC is 0.60 (contrast), while baselines drop to or below chance on multiple conditions.
- **Complexity bias analysis**: Visualisation of top-scored samples shows likelihood, Typicality, and DoSE all flag simple in-distribution images (constant backgrounds) as most OOD. SITN is the only method where actual OOD images dominate the top-scored samples.
- **Ablations**: AD and CV statistics show complementary sensitivity across perturbation types (e.g., AD better for brightness, CV better for motion artefacts). Max-quantile aggregation outperforms KDE-based combination.
- **Outperforms WAIC** (ensemble of 5 models) and complexity correction (PNG-based) across all settings, despite requiring only a single model.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The insight of treating the factorised noise prior as enabling single-sample GoF testing is elegant and novel. Departing from likelihood-centric approaches to exploit the diffeomorphic structure of CNFs is a meaningful conceptual advance.
- **Rigor**: Strong. Formal Type I error control with proofs, DKW-based calibration bounds, and comprehensive ablations. The analysis of complexity bias through R² plots and metric-extreme visualisations is thorough.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Public code, detailed hyperparameters (Appendix I), and clear experimental protocols. The reliance on a specific UNet + Dormand-Prince ODE solver configuration is fully specified.
- **Significance**: High potential impact. OOD detection is a critical safety problem, and SITN offers practical advantages (no OOD data, minimal overhead, calibrated FPR) while being theoretically principled.
- **Red flags**: (1) CelebA AUROC of 0.671 for CIFAR-10→CelebA is moderate, suggesting the method may struggle when OOD data is semantically closer to ID data. (2) The identified edge case where UNet fails to map constant backgrounds to Gaussian noise reveals an architecture-dependent limitation. (3) Evaluation is limited to 32×32 image datasets (CIFAR-10, SVHN, CelebA); scalability to higher resolutions or non-image modalities is untested. (4) The analysis in Appendix H.4 for Type II error relies on a specific parametric form of P_OOD that may not generalise.

## Strengths

- Theoretically principled with formal Type I error control.
- No need for OOD data during training or calibration.
- Minimal computational overhead (O(D log D) beyond the inverse flow).
- Free from the complexity bias that plagues likelihood-based methods.
- Complementary statistics (AD and CV) provide robust detection across diverse perturbation types.
- Clean and interpretable framework: OOD detection reduces to testing two well-understood statistical properties.

## Limitations

- Performance on CIFAR-10→CelebA is moderate (0.671 AUROC), indicating room for improvement on harder OOD settings.
- UNet-specific artefact: constant background patches are poorly mapped to Gaussian noise, causing false positives from the AD component even on ID data.
- Only evaluated on 32×32 images; behaviour on higher-resolution or non-image data is unknown.
- The CV statistic only tests autocorrelation (a necessary but not sufficient condition for independence); other forms of dependence are not captured.
- Single generative model per dataset; interaction between model quality and SITN performance could be further explored.

## Relevance

本文提出的噪声空间拟合优度测试框架为OOD检测提供了一个全新的理论视角，结合了连续归一化流的微分同胚性质和经典统计检验方法。该方法对构建安全可靠的机器学习系统具有直接参考价值，尤其在需要严格假阳性率控制的场景中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does SITN perform on higher-resolution datasets (e.g., ImageNet) and non-image modalities (e.g., tabular, time-series)?
- Can additional test statistics beyond AD and CV (e.g., higher-order moment tests, runs tests) further improve detection power for specific perturbation types?
- Would using a different backbone architecture (e.g., transformer-based flow models) mitigate the constant-background artefact observed with UNets?
- How sensitive is SITN to the quality of the trained CNF — specifically, what is the relationship between generative model likelihood (NLL) and SITN's Type II error rate?
- Can the noise-space GoF framework be extended to conditional generative models for class-conditional OOD detection?
