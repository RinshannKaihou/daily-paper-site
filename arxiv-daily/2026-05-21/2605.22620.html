Combining cluster-voting and self-certainty rewards with targeted covariance-aware regularization prevents entropy collapse in unsupervised RL for LLM reasoning, approaching supervised performance.

Two is better than one: A Collapse-free Multi-Reward RLIF Training Framework

Shourov Joarder, Diganta Sikdar, Ahsan Habib Akash et al.

2026-05-21 RLIF reinforcement-learning entropy-collapse multi-reward-optimization

Key Metric

Multi-reward + KL-Cov retains 89–99% of peak accuracy at step 340 (Qwen2.5-3B: 81.4% GSM8K, 62.4% MATH500) while single-reward baselines collapse to 0% by step 200.

Why This Matters

Entropy collapse is a documented blocker for unsupervised RL in LLM reasoning. A practical, label-free solution approaching supervised GRPO performance removes a key obstacle to self-improving reasoning.

Critical Edge

KL-Cov targets only the top-2% highest-covariance tokens driving entropy collapse — robust across 200× hyperparameter sweep (~2.7pp variation). GDPO per-channel normalization prevents variance-based reward dominance.

Methodology

The framework operates within a GRPO-style policy optimization loop. For each prompt, G=7 completions are sampled. Two intrinsic rewards are computed: (1) Cluster Voting Reward groups final answers into equivalence clusters, rewarding consensus; (2) Self-Certainty Reward measures average KL divergence from uniform across the model’s next-token distributions. GDPO normalization independently z-scores each channel before combining with equal weights (α=β=0.5), preventing the higher-variance self-certainty channel from dominating. KL-Cov regularization computes token-level covariance between log-probabilities and advantages; only the top-2% highest-covariance tokens receive an absolute log-ratio penalty (βcov=0.05) instead of standard clipped surrogate. The remaining ~98% use standard GRPO clipping. A global reference-KL penalty (γ=0.005) completes the loss.

Strengths

  • Clearly identifies two distinct collapse mechanisms (answer-mode and token-mode) and addresses each with complementary rewards.
  • Strong empirical evidence: per-step trajectories, entropy/length dynamics, gradient diagnostics.
  • KL-Cov robust across 200× hyperparameter sweep — no per-model retuning.
  • GDPO normalization validated with logprob-space gradient norms.

Limitations

  • Only evaluated on Qwen2.5 at 1.5B and 3B; behavior at 7B+ unknown.
  • Symmetric weighting (α=β=0.5) not systematically studied.
  • ~3pp gap vs. supervised GRPO on some benchmarks.
  • KL-Cov slightly hurts AIME at 3B scale (3.3% vs. 6.7%).
  • Training only on MATH split; generality claims limited.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the framework scale to 7B+ models with different collapse dynamics?
  • Can additional intrinsic reward channels (step-level consistency, representation agreement) further improve stability?
  • What is the interaction between KL-Cov and other stability techniques (gradient clipping, LR warmup)?
  • Does the approach transfer to non-reasoning domains (instruction following, creative generation)?
  • How does mixed-precision training interact with covariance estimation?
