A single electromagnetic pulse permanently corrupts pre-loaded models on Intel's NCS2 — even while idle.

Characterizing the Fault Response of the Intel Neural Compute Stick 2 Under Single-Pulse Electromagnetic Fault Injection Štefan Kučerák, Jakub Breier, Xiaolu Hou 2026-05-21
fault-injection edge-ai-security hardware-security electromagnetic-attacks
Key Metric
18–31%

C2 persistent degradation rate across ResNet-18, ResNet-50, and VGG-11 at the primary hotspot (123.4, 155.1 mm, 348 V). Top-1 accuracy collapses from ~70% to 2–5%.

Why This Matters

Edge AI models can be silently corrupted by electromagnetic pulses while idle — no active inference needed. This undermines a natural defense assumption for safety-critical deployments.

⚠ Critical Edge

All results from a single NCS2 unit — inter-unit variability unknown. No no-pulse control experiment was conducted to bound confounds.

Methodology

Setup

Intel NCS2 (Myriad X VPU) on USB via Raspberry Pi. Electromagnetic pulses delivered by ChipSHOUTER with 1 mm and 4 mm probes. Three ImageNet CNNs: ResNet-18, ResNet-50, VGG-11 (OpenVINO runtime).

Exploratory phase (~16,000 trials)

Optuna TPE Bayesian sampler navigates the 5D space (x, y, z, voltage, delay). Objective: absolute accuracy deviation from baseline.

Spot-test phase (256 trials per cell)

Fixed configurations at discovered hotspots. Primary: (123.4, 155.1, 0.25) mm, 348 V, 160 ns, 1 mm CCW probe. Each trial: reload model → verify → position → pulse → log.

Timing regimes

  • During (d = +1 s): pulse 1 s after inference start.
  • Before (d = −1 s): pulse 1 s before inference — tests idle-device susceptibility.

Classification thresholds

  • C0 — no effect (Δ < θminor = 0.01)
  • C1 — minor SDC (0.01 ≤ Δ < 0.50)
  • C2 — major persistent degradation (Δ ≥ 0.50, survives all subsequent inferences until reload)
  • C3 — device hang (requires USB power-cycle)

C2 sub-regimes (logit analysis)

  • Partial collapse (10/22 events): logits in nominal range, predicted classes narrow from ~640 to ~350.
  • Saturated single-class (12/22 events): logits hit fixed-point ceiling ±1023.5, all inputs → one class.

Key observations

  • Before-inference C2 rates comparable to during-inference — idle device equally vulnerable.
  • ResNet C1 drops to exactly zero before-inference → fault pathway is through stored state only.
  • All C2 events recover after model reload; no irreversible silicon damage.
  • VGG-11's wider FC head absorbs more corruptions into C1 rather than C2.
  • Day-to-day repeatability: within 1 trial across all four classes.

Strengths

  • Exceptional experimental scale: Bayesian optimization for exploration + 256-trial fixed-configuration spot tests.
  • Four-class taxonomy is clean, reproducible, and maps to established radiation-effect fault models.
  • C2 sub-regime logit analysis goes well beyond typical fault-injection reporting.
  • Idle-device experiment is both practically important and scientifically clean (ResNet C1 → exactly 0).
  • Transparent threats-to-validity section clearly delineates supported claims vs. hypotheses.
  • Spatial sensitivity analysis with two distinct hotspots provides actionable guidance.

Limitations

  • Single device sample — no inter-unit variability characterization.
  • Software trigger jitter prevents per-layer fault localization; exact corruption target unidentified.
  • No experimentally validated mitigations — detection and recovery are proposal-level only.
  • No comparison with laser or voltage-glitching methods on the same device.
  • NCS2 is discontinued; direct deployment relevance is limited.
  • No die floorplan — spatial-to-functional-block mapping remains hypothetical.
  • Workload restricted to image classification; no evidence for generalization to other tasks or transformers.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Would a hardware GPIO trigger reveal layer-specific sensitivity profiles consistent with simulation predictions?
  • Can instrumented OpenVINO builds localize C2 corruption to weights, SHAVE instructions, or accelerator config state?
  • How does the four-class taxonomy transfer to newer edge accelerators (Coral Edge TPU, Jetson Orin Nano, Hailo-8)?
  • What is the quantitative detection coverage of reference-image cross-checking for C2 events?
  • Does the partial-collapse vs. saturated-single-class split appear on other accelerators, and does it correlate with numerical precision?
  • What minimum attack distance beyond contact probe positioning is feasible for inducing C2?
  • Can architecture-dependent robustness ranking inform adversarially robust model design for edge deployment?
