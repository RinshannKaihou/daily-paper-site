LLM Retrieval for Stable and Predictable Ad Recommendations

Vinodh Kumar Sunkara, Satheeshkumar Karuppusamy, Hangjun Xu, Sai Deepika Regani, Kshitij Gupta, Gaby Nahum, Sneha Iyer, Jean-Baptiste Fiot et al. | 2026-05-21

llm-retrieval recommendation-systems predictability semantic-representation ads-systems

A fine-tuned LLM-based semantic candidate generation framework that extracts hierarchical attributes from ad creatives and builds a semantic graph for retrieval reduces prediction instability by 8.62% while simultaneously improving top-line performance by 0.45% in production at Meta.

Key Metric

8.62% reduction in A/A' prediction instability (StatSigDiff), 45% improvement in temporal stability (MAD), +0.45% top-line performance lift, +1.2% recall increase — validated in production A/B test on tens of millions of ads.

Why This Matters

Predictability (stable outcomes under minor input perturbations) is critical for advertisers but under-studied. This demonstrates that predictability and performance improvements are not mutually exclusive — both can be achieved simultaneously via semantic retrieval.

Critical Edge

The predictability framework using "shadow ads" (copies with minor perturbations) with formal metrics (StatSigDiff, MAD) is the key contribution — providing reusable metric definitions applicable beyond ads. The semantic graph traversal replaces brittle ID-based retrieval with interpretable, attribute-aware expansion.

Methodology

LLM-Driven Representation: A fine-tuned LLaMA-8B Instruct processes ad creatives (title, description) to generate hierarchical semantic attributes — categories with confidence scores, fine-grained attributes, and contextual captions.

Semantic Graph Construction: LLM-extracted attributes build a semantic graph where nodes are ads and edges represent shared attributes. Jaccard similarity is applied at phrase and token levels with threshold backoff.

Two-Stage Retrieval: Stage 1 computes categorical relevance via weighted intersection of shared categories. Stage 2 scores ad-to-ad similarity on deeper dimensions (brand, product, contextual attributes) using fuzzy set matching.

Predictability Evaluation: Creates "shadow ads" (copies with minor perturbations) and measures statistical significance of delivery differences between primary-shadow pairs, aggregated with revenue weighting.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Addresses a practically important and under-studied problem: prediction stability in recommendation systems.
  • Formal metric definitions (StatSigDiff, MAD) adoptable by other practitioners.
  • Validated at truly large scale in production with real A/B experiments.
  • Semantic graph + traversal approach is elegant and interpretable.
  • Demonstrates predictability and performance are not mutually exclusive.

Limitations

  • Workshop paper (5 pages) limits depth and ablation studies.
  • Fine-tuning details for LLaMA-8B not disclosed (data, prompts, hyperparameters).
  • No comparison with other semantic methods (sentence-transformers, CLIP).
  • Shadow ad perturbation strategy not fully specified.
  • Results specific to Meta's ads ecosystem; generalizability claimed but unvalidated.
  • No analysis of computational cost or latency overhead.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How sensitive is the A/A' metric to perturbation type and magnitude? Could it measure training stability in LLM fine-tuning?
  2. What is the contribution of hierarchical attributes versus flat embeddings to the predictability improvement?
  3. Could the semantic graph traversal detect or mitigate representation drift in LLM hidden states during training?
  4. How does the framework perform on cold-start ads with minimal textual information?
  5. What are the latency and computational costs compared to baseline two-tower generators?
