> **一句话总结：** 对 GPT-2 Small 在 IOI 任务上的失败用 layer-8 残差流 SAE（24,576 特征）做特征审计，发现最强失败相关特征（17,491，"cryptographic keys"）只是 "the keys" 词汇混淆的副产品——因果消融无法恢复准确率（6.7% → 4.4%），原始残差流的预测力与 SAE 相当（AUC 0.929 vs 0.927），且 5 个随机种子中该特征仅在 1 次里居首，真正的贡献是这条廉价、可复现、自带三重控制的审计流水线本身。

> **TL;DR:** The authors audit GPT-2 Small's layer-8 residual-stream SAE (Bloom 2024, 24,576 features) over 300 IOI prompts (79.7% accuracy, 239/300 correct) to find features discriminating failed vs. successful trials. The top correlate — feature 17,491, Neuronpedia label "cryptographic keys," d = +2.93 — is a lexical confound: GPT-2 Small fails 93.3% of the 45 "the keys" prompts vs. 7.5% of the other 255 (Fisher p = 8.79×10⁻³³). Three controls kill the causal reading: zeroing the feature moves keys-subset accuracy from 6.7% to 4.4% (wrong direction); a logistic regression on the raw 768-d residual stream matches the top-100 SAE features (5-fold ROC AUC 0.929 vs. 0.927); and across 5 seeds the keys-effect stays in 75.0–93.3% while feature 17,491 is the top-|d| feature in only 1 of 5 runs. The contribution is the cheap, reproducible audit pipeline plus the control protocol, released with code/data on a laptop (Apple M3 Max).

## 核心贡献

- 提出一个廉价、可复现、模型无关的 SAE 特征审计流水线：输入任务语料 + 残差流 SAE，输出每特征的失败 vs. 成功判别报告（Welch t、Cohen's d、Holm 校正 p、带 Neuronpedia URL 的 top 特征列表）
- 在 GPT-2 Small IOI 任务上做完整案例研究：300 提示词、24,576 个 SAE 特征，146 个通过 Holm 校正显著性检验，105 个达到大效应量（|d| > 0.8）
- 对最强失败相关特征（17,491，"cryptographic keys"）施加三重控制：因果消融、原始残差流表征基线、5 种子鲁棒性检查——三者共同否定了"该特征是失败原因"的解读
- 证明 SAE 基底贡献的是可解释性而非预测力：原始 768 维残差流的 5 折 AUC（0.929）与 top-100 SAE 特征（0.927）几乎相同
- 给出诚实负结果报告：行为层面的 keys 效应（93.3% vs 7.5% 失败率）鲁棒，但最初附加其上的机制故事不鲁棒
- 全部代码、300 提示词语料、300×24,576 激活矩阵、消融与基线脚本、多种子摘要开源（MIT 许可），笔记本电脑（Apple M3 Max，无独立 GPU）约 20 分钟可跑完全流程

## Key Contributions

- An audit pipeline that takes a task corpus and a residual-stream SAE and emits a per-feature failure-vs-success discrimination report (Welch's t, Cohen's d, Holm-corrected p, top-feature list with Neuronpedia URLs)
- A thorough case study on GPT-2 Small's IOI task: 300 prompts, 24,576 SAE features, 146 passing Holm-corrected significance and 105 reaching |Cohen's d| > 0.8
- Three rigorous controls applied to the top correlate (feature 17,491): causal ablation, raw-activation baseline (logistic regression on the 768-d residual stream), and a 5-seed robustness check — all three weaken the mechanistic reading
- Evidence that the SAE basis adds interpretability but not predictive power: raw 768-d residual stream AUC 0.929 matches top-100 SAE features AUC 0.927
- An honest negative-result framing: the behavioural "keys effect" (failure rate 93.3% vs 7.5%) is robust across seeds; the headline mechanistic attribution is not
- Full release (code, 300-prompt corpus, 300×24,576 activation matrix, ablation/baseline scripts, multi-seed summary) under MIT license; entire pipeline runs on an Apple M3 Max laptop in ~20 minutes with no discrete GPU

## Methodology

**Model and SAE.** GPT-2 Small (124M parameters, 12 layers, 768-dim residual stream) is loaded via TransformerLens. The SAE is the layer-8 residual-stream variant of Bloom's `gpt2-small-res-jb` release (d_sae = 24,576, ≈32× the hidden dimension), accessed through `sae_lens`. The residual stream is hooked at `blocks.8.hook_resid_pre` (input to layer 8). Layer 8 is chosen because the IOI literature places name-mover heads in later layers (so candidate-answer representations should be present) and because Bloom's layer-8 features are thoroughly catalogued on Neuronpedia.

**Task corpus.** 300 IOI prompts are sampled from a procedural generator following Wang et al.'s IOI templates: a subject S and indirect object IO drawn from 29 common first names; a transferred object from a list of eight (`a drink`, `the keys`, `a book`, `the ball`, `the gift`, `a card`, `the bag`, `the flowers`); a location from a list of eight; the sentence template varied over four surface forms. Generator seed is fixed at 42.

**Scoring.** For each prompt, the next three tokens are greedily decoded and the first non-whitespace alphanumeric token is compared to the known indirect-object name; success = exact match. GPT-2 Small reaches 239/300 = 79.7% accuracy (61 failed, 239 successful).

**Activation logging.** The residual stream is captured at the hook point during the forward pass (before greedy decoding); prompts are ~15–25 tokens, and the mean SAE encoding over the last three tokens is taken, yielding one R²⁴⁵⁷⁶ vector per prompt (full matrix 300 × 24,576, ~28 MB float32).

**Statistics.** For each of the 24,576 features: per-class mean, Welch's t-statistic (df = min(n_fail, n_succ) − 1), two-sided p-value, Cohen's d (pooled SD). Holm-Bonferroni correction is applied across all 24,576 tests (preferred over Benjamini-Hochberg because each significant feature is manually read against its Neuronpedia description, favouring a stronger per-feature error rate).

**Three controls.** (1) Causal ablation: subtract f_i(x)·W_dec[i,:] from the residual at every token position for feature 17,491 on the 45 keys prompts and re-decode. (2) Representation baseline: class-balanced logistic regression (scikit-learn liblinear, 5-fold stratified CV) on the raw 768-d residual stream vs. top-K SAE features for K ∈ {1,5,10,20,50,100}. (3) Seed robustness: re-run corpus generation + feature collection with seeds {0, 42, 100, 200, 300}, recording overall accuracy, keys-subset failure rate, and the top-|d| feature identity.

**Compute.** Single MacBook Pro (Mac15,11), Apple M3 Max, 36 GB unified memory, macOS 14.4 (Sonoma, build 23E214); Python 3.11, PyTorch 2.12, MPS backend, no discrete GPU. The 300-prompt forward-pass + activation-logging run takes ~10 minutes; the per-feature statistical analysis runs in under 30 seconds; the ablation, raw-baseline, and 5-seed sweep each take under 10 minutes.

## Results & Highlights

- **Overall accuracy:** GPT-2 Small reaches 239/300 = 79.7% on the IOI corpus (61 failed, 239 successful), within the range reported by Wang et al.
- **Feature significance:** 146 of 24,576 features clear Holm-Bonferroni significance at α = 0.05; 209 have |Cohen's d| > 0.5; 105 have |d| > 0.8; ~96% of features fall within |d| ≤ 0.2.
- **Top correlate (feature 17,491):** d = +2.93, Welch t = 11.4, Holm-corrected p < 10⁻¹⁰; mean activation 10.285 on failed trials vs. 0.182 on successful trials (~56× higher); 42 of 61 failed trials activated it above zero vs. only 3 of 239 successful trials.
- **Keys-subset behavioural effect:** 42/45 "the keys" prompts fail (93.3%) vs. 19/255 other-object prompts (7.5%); Fisher exact p = 8.79×10⁻³³, odds ratio ≈ 174 — swapping one transferred-object phrase shifts benchmark accuracy by ~86 percentage points.
- **Control 1 (causal ablation):** Zeroing feature 17,491 on the 45 keys prompts moves accuracy from 6.7% to 4.4% (a −2.2 pp shift in the wrong direction); the other 255 prompts are unchanged. The feature is a correlate, not a sufficient cause at this layer.
- **Control 2 (raw-activation baseline):** 5-fold ROC AUC: single feature 17,491 = 0.839; top-100 SAE features = 0.927; all 24,576 SAE features = 0.933; raw 768-d residual stream = 0.929. The SAE basis matches but does not exceed the raw representation's predictive power.
- **Control 3 (seed robustness, n=5):** Overall accuracy 79.7–85.7% (mean 83.3%, sd 2.6); keys-subset failure rate 75.0–93.3% (mean 85.2%, sd 6.6) — the behavioural effect is stable. But feature 17,491 is the top-|d| feature in only 1 of 5 seeds (seed 42); the other four runs promote features {7536, 7536, 10960, 19149}.
- **Conditional analysis (keys-free, 255 prompts, 236 success / 19 fail):** Only 5 features clear Holm significance (vs. 146 on full corpus); largest effect size d ≈ 1.40 (vs. 2.93); 31 features have |d| > 0.8 — the keys case looks like an outlier, not a broad mechanistic pattern.
- **Success-correlated feature:** Feature 10,036 has d = −1.01 (mean activation 1.19 on failure vs. 2.75 on success); Neuronpedia label "providing opportunities, granting, or offering"; top tokens `to`, `unto`, `onto`, `services` — the surface forms that close IOI transfer sentences.

## Critical Assessment

- The paper's central negative result is its strongest feature: the headline d = +2.93 correlate fails all three controls (ablation moves accuracy the wrong way, raw residual stream matches SAE predictive power, and the top-feature identity changes across 4 of 5 seeds). This is a genuine cautionary contribution for mechanistic-interpretability work that relies on single-feature d-statistics without controls.
- A concrete paper-specific weakness (stated explicitly in Section VI): the ablation is single-feature, single-layer, single-position-set — it rules out only the simplest causal story and cannot exclude distributed-feature, earlier-layer, or position-specific mechanisms. The authors flag this honestly but it limits the strength of the "not causal" conclusion.
- A second paper-specific weakness: the audit is bounded to one SAE (Bloom's layer-8 release), one layer, one model (GPT-2 Small, 124M), and one task (IOI). The paper explicitly states "the empirical content of this paper does not, on its own, generalize" and discusses extension to Gemma Scope / Llama 3 SAEs as unexecuted future work (Section V-D).
- The "keys" lexical confound is idiosyncratic to this particular IOI item list inherited from Wang et al.; it surfaced during analysis rather than by design, and a reader could reasonably ask whether the audit would have produced any publishable finding on a corpus that excluded homonym objects.
- The 5-seed robustness check is acknowledged as small (n=5); the authors state it suffices to distinguish "stable behavioural effect" from "stable headline feature" but not to give a tight effect-size interval.

## Strengths

- Exceptional intellectual honesty: negative ablation results, feature-level fragility across seeds, and the raw-baseline equivalence are all reported clearly rather than buried
- The three-control protocol (ablation, raw-activation baseline, seed robustness) sets a concrete standard that most SAE-based interpretability claims currently lack
- Full reproducibility: code, data, activation matrices, and scripts released under MIT license; runs on a laptop with no GPU in ~20 minutes
- The conditional (keys-free) analysis is reported rather than hidden, showing the residual feature landscape is flat — important calibration against over-reading the headline
- Clean, well-scoped research design with a clear "what this is not" statement (not an agent benchmark, not a multi-step task)

## Limitations

- Single SAE (Bloom layer-8), single layer, single model (GPT-2 Small 124M), single task (IOI) — generalizability is untested
- The top correlate is a lexical confound ("keys"), which is idiosyncratic to this IOI item list and limits the generality of the case study
- The ablation rules out only the simplest single-feature causal story; multi-feature, earlier-layer, or position-specific mechanisms remain open
- Feature-level fragility across seeds (17,491 is top in only 1/5 runs) raises broader questions about SAE feature stability for audit purposes
- Neuronpedia descriptions are auto-generated by GPT-3.5 / GPT-4o-mini and interpreted by a single human auditor in one pass
- 5 seeds is a small robustness check; a tighter effect-size interval would require a larger sweep
- The position-aggregation choice (mean over last three tokens) is not analysed per-position, which the pipeline supports but the paper does not report

## Relevance

直接对应"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"方向：这是一项关于 SAE 特征作为模型失败诊断工具的有效性边界的实证研究，用三重控制协议揭示"显著的单特征 d 值"并不等于因果机制，对机制可解释性方法论有直接参考价值。同时，keys 词汇混淆导致 86 个百分点准确率波动这一发现，也与"LLM Inference Evaluation"中提示敏感性问题相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would auditing multiple layers (especially earlier ones, e.g. layers 5–7) reveal a causal feature of IOI failure that the layer-8 ablation cannot reach?
- How stable are SAE feature-level correlates across different SAE architectures (JumpReLU, TopK, transcoders, crosscoders) trained on the same model?
- Can this audit pipeline be applied to instruction-tuned models via Gemma Scope (Gemma 2 270M/1B/4B/12B/27B) or Goodfire's Llama 3 SAEs on a real agent task (WebArena / AgentBench subset)?
- What would a positive result look like — i.e., under what conditions does an SAE feature correlate also pass the ablation and seed-robustness controls to qualify as a causal lever?
- Does the raw-residual-stream equivalence (AUC 0.929 vs 0.927) hold on tasks without a dominant lexical confound, or is the SAE's lack of predictive advantage specific to this corpus?
- Could per-position activation analysis (rather than mean over last three tokens) localize the discriminating signal more precisely?
