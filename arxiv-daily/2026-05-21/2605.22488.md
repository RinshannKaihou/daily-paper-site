> **一句话总结：** 在一个具有已知闭式解的数位提取任务上训练Transformer，发现线性探针成功解码出中间计算量（$B^D$、$\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor$ 等），但因果干预和稀疏电路搜索揭示模型实际通过分离的 $N$/$B$/$D$ 路径在输出端晚期整合来完成任务，而非使用探针暗示的分阶段串行算法，证明"可解码"不等于"被计算使用"。

> **TL;DR:** Trains 10-layer decoder-only Transformers on base-digit extraction ($\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor \bmod B$, 99.83% held-out accuracy). Linear probes decode closed-form intermediates ($B^D$, $N/B^D$, $\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor$) from the Dones stream, making staged computation look plausible. But causal patching shows the Dones→Output route transmits only D-selective information (not N or B), and sparse circuit search reveals mostly separate N, B, D routes that combine late at output positions. The model *represents* algorithmic intermediates without *using* them as causal intermediates — a clean dissociation between probe-based and causal interpretability.

## 核心贡献

- 设计了一个具有精确闭式解的数位提取任务（给定 $N$、$B$、$D$，输出 $B^D$ 位系数），为研究算法中间步骤提供了可验证的实验环境
- 三组种子的10层Transformer在未见过的 $(N, B)$ 组合上达到99.83%精确准确率，建立了可靠的行为基线
- 线性探针成功从 Dones 残差流中解码出 $B^D$（$R^2=1.00$）、$N/B^D$ 和 $\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor$（$R^2=0.96$），使分阶段算法假设看似合理
- 因果消融实验揭示：输出流对早期 Dones 通信（layer 0-1）最为敏感，而非探针峰值所在的中间层
- 全路径 key/value patching 证明：替换仅改变 $D$ 的 donor 会使行为完全翻转（99.84%匹配donor），而替换仅改变 $N$ 或 $B$ 的 donor 完全无影响（100%保持source）
- 稀疏电路搜索发现 $N$、$B$、$D$ 三条路径在 prompt 端几乎完全分离，仅在输出端汇合；跨种子电路重叠率达94.44%
- 核心发现：模型**表示**了闭式解的中间量，但实际的因果回路**不通过它们传递信息**——"被表示"不等于"被计算"

## Key Contributions

- Introduces base-digit extraction as a mechanistic interpretability testbed with explicit, verifiable algorithmic intermediates
- Achieves 99.83% exact-answer accuracy (95% CI: 99.50–100.00%) across three seeds on held-out number–base intersections
- Demonstrates probes decode intermediates: $B^D$ at $R^2=1.00$ from Dones layer 0; quotient-like quantities at $R^2=0.96$ from Dones layer 2
- Shows many intermediates are already decodable at initialization (before training), with training systematically reshaping accessibility
- Cumulative attention ablations reveal output depends most on early Dones→O communication (layers 0–1), not the later layers where richer quantities peak
- Layer-1 Dones→O patching transfers behavior toward donor (67.63% donor-exact vs 25.11% source-exact), confirming early route has causal control
- Full-route patching is perfectly selective: D-only changes flip behavior (99.84% donor match), N-only and B-only changes have zero effect
- Sparse circuit search retains only 28.33% of candidate relations and 20.25% of layer-edges while preserving 93.40% accuracy; N, B, D routes are factorized
- Provides a concrete, bounded demonstration that probe-based conclusions can diverge sharply from causal observations

## Methodology

**Task.** Base-digit extraction: given $N \in \{0,\ldots,999\}$, $B \in \{2,\ldots,30\}$, $D \in \{0,\ldots,9\}$, output $\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor \bmod B$ as a two-digit decimal string. The closed-form solution provides explicit candidate intermediates: $B^D$, $N/B^D$, $\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor$, $\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor \bmod B$.

**Architecture.** 10-layer GPT-style decoder-only Transformer, width 384, 12 heads, FFN width 1536, dropout 0.01. Three seeds (0, 42, 1337). Trained for 1000 epochs with AdamW (lr $5 \times 10^{-4}$, weight decay 0.01). Training examples expanded across all six field-order permutations.

**Evaluation.** By number–base intersection split: held-out $N$ values paired with held-out $B$ values, ensuring no overlap with training. Autoregressive greedy decoding. Exact-answer accuracy requires both digits correct.

**Analysis pipeline (three stages):**

1. **Linear probing.** OLS regressions with 5-fold CV on pooled held-out data. Probed $B^D$, $N/B^D$, $\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor$, $\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor \bmod B$ from all named streams and layers. Initialization baselines reconstructed from untrained networks.

2. **Causal interventions.** (a) Cumulative attention ablations: mask Dones→O at selected layers, sweep shallow-to-deep and deep-to-shallow. (b) Key/value patching: substitute donor Dones key/value vectors into output query rows; test N-only, B-only, and D-only donor changes.

3. **Sparse circuit search.** Greedy right-to-left attention-route search starting from output streams. Retain source→destination relations whose removal damages validation accuracy. 27-check threshold sweep for robustness.

## Results & Highlights

- **Task mastery**: 99.83% exact accuracy across seeds (seed 0: 99.50%, seeds 42/1337: 100.00%)
- **Probes succeed dramatically**: $B^D$ decoded at $R^2=1.00$ from Dones layer 0; $N/B^D$ and $\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor$ at $R^2=0.96$ from Dones layer 2; final answer at $R^2=0.94$ from O[1] layer 9
- **Initialization surprise**: Many intermediates already highly decodable before training; training closes 98% of the gap for $B^D$ in Dones layer 0 but only 32.8% for quotient-like quantities at layer 6
- **Ablation asymmetry**: Forward sweep shows masking layers 0–1 drops accuracy to 73.08%; reverse sweep shows masking layers 4–9 barely matters (99.75%). Early Dones→O is critical.
- **Patching selectivity**: D-only donor patching → 0.00% source-exact, 99.84% donor-exact. N-only and B-only → 100.00% source-exact. The route carries only D information.
- **Factorized circuit**: Sparse search retains 17/18 shared relations across seeds (94.44% IoU). N, B, D scaffolds are separate; no stable prompt-side combination before output.
- **5-layer model confirms**: Same qualitative pattern holds for a smaller 5-layer model (99.89% accuracy, same D-selectivity), suggesting the finding is not depth-dependent.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The "represented vs. computed" distinction is conceptually sharp and goes beyond typical probing papers. The initialization-probe comparison (quantities decodable before training) is an underappreciated insight. The perfectly selective D-only patching result is remarkably clean.
- **Rigor**: Very strong. Three-seed replication with bootstrap CIs, multiple converging methods (probes, ablations, patching, circuit search), held-out-by-intersection evaluation, initialization controls, and threshold-sweep robustness checks. The statistics are transparent about small-sample limitations (three seeds).
- **Reproducibility**: Likely high — synthetic task, defined architecture, public code, no external dependencies. The task definition is precise enough to replicate exactly.
- **Significance**: Important for mechanistic interpretability. The field relies heavily on probing; this paper provides a carefully controlled counterexample showing probes can be actively misleading even when the algorithmic hypothesis is exactly correct and the model achieves near-perfect accuracy.
- **Red flags / weaknesses**:
  - The task is simple (single arithmetic operation, bounded range $N \leq 999$, $B \leq 30$). Whether probe-causal divergence generalizes to complex tasks or large-scale LLMs is unknown.
  - The model is small (10 layers, 384 width). The finding may not scale.
  - The output-stream computation is left as a black box. The authors acknowledge this but it means the full algorithm remains unknown.
  - Some probe decodability at initialization raises questions about whether the architecture/tokenization trivially encodes certain mathematical relationships — the paper discusses this but cannot fully resolve it.
  - The paper studies one task with one architecture family; generalization breadth is limited.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally clean experimental design with a task that has known ground-truth intermediates
- Three converging analysis methods (probes → causal interventions → circuit search) that triangulate the same conclusion
- The initialization-probe comparison is a genuinely novel control that reveals how much decodability is architectural rather than learned
- The D-only selectivity result (0% N-transfer, 0% B-transfer, 99.84% D-transfer) is strikingly clean
- The authors are honest about scope limitations and avoid overclaiming
- Cross-seed circuit stability (94.44% IoU) is strong evidence for replicability of the mechanism

## Limitations

- Single arithmetic task on a small Transformer; generalization to language, vision, or large models is untested
- Output-stream computation is not decomposed — the final integration mechanism remains opaque
- Initialization decodability of mathematical intermediates suggests some findings may be specific to how tokenized numbers embed in residual streams
- Does not propose practical methods for detecting probe-causal divergence in real-world settings without known ground truth
- The 5-layer companion confirms robustness to depth but not to width, training data scale, or task complexity

## Relevance

该论文直接研究了LLM可解释性中的核心方法论问题——线性探针解码出的表示是否反映模型的真实计算路径，与"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"兴趣高度契合。其发现（可解码 ≠ 被因果使用）对 mechanistic interpretability 和 circuit analysis 的方法论可信度有重要警示意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the probe-causal divergence persist for larger Transformers (e.g., 1B+ parameters) or more complex multi-step arithmetic tasks?
- Can we develop diagnostic tests to detect misleading probes in settings where ground-truth intermediates are unavailable?
- Why does the model represent intermediates it does not use — is this an artifact of shared weight matrices across positions, superposition, or something else?
- How do these findings relate to the polysemanticity and superposition literature (Elhage et al., 2022)? Could the intermediates be "represented" as side effects of representing other features?
- Would nonlinear probes or different probe architectures (e.g., MLP probes) show the same divergence from causal analysis?
- What is the output-stream integration mechanism? Does it implement a recognizable algorithm, or is it heuristic (cf. Nikankin et al., 2024 on "arithmetic without algorithms")?
