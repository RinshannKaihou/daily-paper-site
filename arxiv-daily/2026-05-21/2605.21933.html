Thermodynamic Irreversibility of Training Algorithms

Liu Ziyin, Yuanjie Ren, Adam Levine, Isaac Chuang · 2026-05-21

Four distinct irreversibility measures (discretization error, time renormalization, time-reversal asymmetry, entropy production) are provably equivalent for training algorithms, revealing an emergent symmetry-breaking force that drives optimization toward thermodynamically "coldest" minima.

Key Metric

Effective free energy Feff = L + (η/4)‖∇L‖², showing discretized GD implicitly penalizes gradient norm — favoring flat minima.

Why This Matters

Provides a principled physics-based lens for understanding implicit regularization and learning-rate effects in deep learning, with the surprising prediction that higher LR can reduce update fluctuation in nonlinear models.

Critical Edge

Anomalous fluctuation: increasing LR 5× decreases normalized update energy in Transformers/RNNs, inverting classical linear-model predictions — a concrete, falsifiable claim.

Methodology

The paper develops theory for generic discrete-time dynamics Δθt = −ηU(θt) where U has a symmetric Jacobian (covering GD, SGD, Adam, RMSProp after state augmentation). Four irreversibility potentials are derived and compared:

  • Backward Error Analysis (φDE): Constructs an effective continuous-time flow matching the discrete update to O(η²), yielding φDE = (η/4)‖U‖².
  • Time Renormalization (φTR): Applies renormalization group to the time axis; accumulated coupling converges to 1/4, matching φDE.
  • Time-Reversal Asymmetry (φTA): Compares forward-then-backward step to starting point, obtaining φTA = (η/4)‖U‖² + O(η²).
  • Entropy Production (φST): Introduces virtual temperature and spread as UV cutoffs, recovering φST ∝ η‖U‖² in the continuum limit.

All four agree to leading order in η. Validated on quadratic potentials and applied to Transformer, RNN, and perceptron training with varying LR schedules.

Strengths

  • Elegant unification of four irreversibility measures with a clean equivalence proof.
  • Effective free energy gives an intuitive, actionable interpretation of implicit regularization.
  • Anomalous fluctuation result is counterintuitive and potentially impactful.
  • Applicable beyond deep learning to any discrete-time symmetric-Jacobian system.

Limitations

  • Experiments limited to small models (2-layer Transformer, d_model=128); LLM-scale untested.
  • Symmetric Jacobian assumption requires state augmentation for Adam; practical implications unclear.
  • Virtual temperature/spread construction is mathematically elegant but physically artificial.
  • No quantitative link between irreversibility and generalization established.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does anomalous fluctuation persist in LLM pretraining with warmup/decay schedules?
  • Can φ serve as a real-time diagnostic metric for training stability?
  • What is the quantitative link between entropy production rate and generalization gap?
  • How do O(η²) corrections modify predictions at practically large learning rates?
  • Can symmetry-breaking analysis predict which representations emerge during training?
