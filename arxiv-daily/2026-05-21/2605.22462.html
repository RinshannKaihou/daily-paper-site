From Correlation to Cause: A Five-Stage Methodology for Feature Analysis in Transformer Language Models

Caleb Munigety

mechanistic-interpretability causal-analysis sparse-autoencoder transformer-circuits feature-analysis

Key Finding

Highly selective SAE features aren’t truly causal — ablating 15 of them leaves 98% accuracy.

Key Metric

98%

Accuracy retained after ablating all 15 name-selective SAE features — features are specific but only partially causal.

Why This Matters

r = −0.56

Selectivity anticorrelates with causal force — critical for designing reliable interpretability-based monitoring.

Critical Edge

Single model, single task, synthetic costs

Demonstrated only on GPT-2 small + IOI; deployment cost parameters are assumed, not measured. Generalizability unsupported.

Methodology Five-stage pipeline on GPT-2 small / IOI task

1Probe design
Define IOI task & evaluation metrics for circuit discovery.
2Feature extraction
Activation patching to recover IOI circuit; train SAE for interpretable decomposition.
3Causal validation
Ablation on SAE features; NLA-based variance explained (31% vs 99.7%).
4Robustness testing
Three distribution shifts to evaluate circuit & feature transfer.
5Deployment integration
Cost-sensitive monitoring ($50/FN, $0.42/FP) for operational use.

Strengths

  • Systematic, stage-by-stage methodology clearly articulated for reuse.
  • Quantified results at every stage enable direct comparison with future work.
  • Deployment integration stage connects interpretability research to real-world use.
  • Selectivity–causality anticorrelation challenges common assumptions in the field.

Limitations

  • Only GPT-2 small on IOI — generalizability to larger models untested.
  • Single-author contribution; breadth of validation may be limited.
  • Deployment costs are synthetic assumptions, not real-world data.
  • Abstract-only format prevents assessing statistical tests and sensitivity analyses.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the selectivity–causality anticorrelation (r = −0.56) hold for larger models (LLaMA, GPT-J) and more complex tasks?
  2. How sensitive are robustness results to the specific distribution shifts chosen?
  3. Can the five-stage methodology adapt to non-SAE methods (dictionary learning, supervised probes)?
  4. What are the failure modes of the monitoring configuration under adversarial inputs?
  5. How does partial causality of SAE features relate to polysemantic vs. monosemantic feature quality?

Relevance 为什么重要

该论文提出的五阶段因果特征分析框架与 mechanistic interpretability 研究方向高度相关，特别是揭示了 SAE 特征的选择性与因果力之间的负相关关系，这对理解语言模型内部机制和设计可靠的特征监控方法具有重要参考价值。
