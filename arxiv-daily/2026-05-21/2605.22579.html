Beyond Temperature: Hyperfitting as a Late-Stage Geometric Expansion

Meimingwei Li, Yuanhao Ding, Esteban Garces Arias, Christian Heumann

2026-05-21 hyperfitting mechanistic-interpretability parameter-efficient-fine-tuning representation-geometry

Key Metric

At matched entropy, temperature scaling yields TTR ≈ 0.40 while Hyperfitting achieves TTR ≈ 0.68 (TinyLlama). Late-Stage LoRA (last 5 layers only) matches Full LoRA with ~80% fewer trainable parameters and 1.99× speedup.

Why This Matters

Full-network fine-tuning is unnecessary for fixing greedy degeneration. The mechanism is localized to the final transformer block’s geometric expansion, enabling a targeted, efficient intervention with structural regularization benefits.

Critical Edge

Entropy-matched controls falsify the temperature hypothesis. Static logit injection is universally harmful, proving the reordering is context-dependent. Terminal Expansion dimension gains: +80.8 (TinyLlama), +36.4 (LLaMA-3.2), +18.3 (Gemma-2).

Methodology

Systematic mechanistic investigation of Hyperfitting (fine-tuning on 2,000 samples for 260 epochs to near-zero loss). Five experimental layers: (1) Entropy Matching — solve for T* matching hyperfitted entropy (~0.86 nats), compare diversity; (2) Provenance Analysis — trace each hyperfitted token’s rank in the original distribution (tripartite: 60.9% linguistic anchor, 26.2% local exploration, 12.9% deep tail promotion); (3) Static Injection Ablation — inject average logit shift for top-500 promoted tokens with α sweep, showing universal harm; (4) Layer-wise Representational Analysis — cosine similarity, L2, and Participation Ratio across all layers, revealing Compression-then-Expansion dynamic; (5) Late-Stage LoRA — adapters on final 5 layers only. Primary model: TinyLlama-1.1B (22 layers). Validated on 4 additional architectures (1.5B–8B).

Strengths

  • Clean experimental design with well-motivated controls (entropy matching, static injection).
  • Cross-architecture validation across 5 model families (1.1B–8B).
  • Mechanistic explanation directly inspires practical, efficient intervention.
  • Strong statistical reporting (p-values, CIs, effect sizes).

Limitations

  • Lexical diversity metrics (TTR, repetition) don’t capture semantic coherence or factual accuracy.
  • Maximum scale tested is 8B; 70B+ behavior unknown.
  • 260-epoch regime still computationally demanding (~10h on RTX 4090).
  • Sensitivity to dataset size, quality, and domain shift not explored.
  • Compression-then-Expansion dynamic lacks formal theoretical model.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does Terminal Expansion scale qualitatively to 70B+ models, or does localization shift to multiple layers?
  • Can diversity gains be disentangled from memorization via a regularization path?
  • How does Hyperfitting interact with instruction-tuning (RLHF/DPO) objectives?
  • Is Compression-then-Expansion universal to all fine-tuning or specific to extreme overfitting?
  • Could a neural tangent kernel analysis predict the critical expansion layer from architecture hyperparameters?
