> **一句话总结：** 本文提出基于微调LLaMA-8B的语义候选生成框架，通过从广告素材中提取层次化语义属性构建语义图谱进行候选扩展，在大规模在线A/B实验中将系统可预测性（A/A'差异）降低了8.62%，同时提升0.45%的线上性能指标。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces a fine-tuned LLM-based semantic candidate generation framework for ads recommendation that extracts hierarchical semantic attributes from ad creatives, builds a semantic graph for candidate expansion, and demonstrates an 8.62% relative reduction in prediction instability (A/A' difference) alongside a 0.45% lift in top-line performance in large-scale online A/B experiments at Meta.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了广告推荐系统中"可预测性"（Predictability）的量化框架，通过A/A'差异和MAD指标衡量系统对输入微小扰动的鲁棒性
- 设计了基于微调LLaMA-8B Instruct的语义候选生成流水线，从广告素材中提取层次化语义属性（类别、属性、上下文描述），构建语义图谱进行候选扩展
- 提出两阶段检索方法：第一阶段基于类别级别的模糊匹配进行粗检索，第二阶段基于品牌、产品等深层维度进行相关性评分
- 在Meta大规模广告系统中进行在线A/B实验验证，同时改善可预测性和传统性能指标

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a formal predictability metric (A/A' difference) for ads recommendation systems, quantifying how robustly the system responds to minor input perturbations via shadow ad experiments
- Designs an end-to-end LLM-powered semantic candidate generation framework using fine-tuned LLaMA-8B to extract hierarchical semantic attributes (categories, attributes, contextual captions) from ad creatives
- Constructs a semantic graph from LLM-extracted metadata and applies graph traversal for ad-to-ad similarity expansion, replacing ID-based retrieval with semantic-aware retrieval
- Achieves statistically significant improvements in both predictability (8.62% reduction in A/A' difference, 45% MAD improvement) and performance (0.45% top-line lift, 1.2% recall increase) in large-scale production A/B tests

## Methodology

The approach consists of four main components:

1. **LLM-Driven Ads Representation Learning:** A fine-tuned LLaMA-8B Instruct model processes ad creatives (title, description) to generate hierarchical semantic attributes — categories with confidence scores, fine-grained attributes, and contextual captions.

2. **Semantic Graph Construction:** The LLM-extracted attributes are used to build a semantic graph where nodes are ads and edges represent shared attributes. Jaccard similarity is applied at both phrase and token levels with a threshold θ for backoff.

3. **Two-Stage Retrieval:**
   - **Stage 1 (Category-level):** Computes categorical relevance using LLM representations via a weighted intersection of shared categories (Equation 4).
   - **Stage 2 (Relevance Scoring):** Scores ad-to-ad similarity on deeper dimensions (brand, product, contextual attributes) using fuzzy set matching.

4. **Real-Time Serving Infrastructure:** A distributed processing framework on GPUs for batch LLM inference with optimized latency and throughput for online candidate retrieval.

The predictability evaluation framework creates "shadow ads" (copies with minor perturbations) and measures the statistical significance of delivery differences between primary-shadow pairs, aggregated with revenue weighting (StatSigDiff metric, Equations 1-2).

## Results & Highlights

- **Top-line performance:** 0.45% statistically significant lift in online performance metric (clicks/conversions)
- **Recall improvement:** 1.2% increase in final-stage recall
- **Predictability (A/A' difference):** 8.62% relative reduction in StatSigDiff metric
- **Temporal stability:** 45% improvement in Median Absolute Deviation (MAD) of daily impression differences between primary-shadow ad pairs
- **Recall quality:** Recall Alignment Ratio decreases monotonically from 0.51X at Top-5 to 0.07X at Top-200, showing the LLM effectively prioritizes high-quality candidates at smaller K values
- **Incremental diversity:** Incremental Recall Potential grows from 1.00Y at Top-5 to 1.89Y at Top-200, demonstrating substantial complementary recommendation capacity
- Evaluated on tens of millions of data points using text-only features from LLaMA-8B

## Authors & Credibility

All authors are from **Meta Platforms, Inc.** (Menlo Park and New York). The paper is published at the **SIGIR Workshop AgentSearch** (July 2026, Melbourne). The scale of evaluation (tens of millions of ads, production A/B test at Meta) provides strong industrial validation. However, the venue is a workshop paper (5 pages) rather than a full conference publication, and many implementation details are omitted due to the industrial setting.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate):** The predictability metric (A/A' difference via shadow ads) is a practical contribution, though the concept of measuring prediction stability is not entirely new. The LLM-based semantic candidate generation approach is a reasonable application of fine-tuned LLMs to retrieval, but the core idea of using semantic representations for recommendation is well-established.
- **Rigor (Moderate):** The A/B experiment is large-scale and production-validated. However, many details are missing: the fine-tuning procedure for LLaMA-8B, the specific prompt templates, the threshold θ for Jaccard backoff, and the exact perturbations applied to shadow ads. The paper provides formulas but limited analysis of failure modes.
- **Reproducibility (Low):** As an industrial paper evaluated on proprietary Meta infrastructure with tens of millions of data points, full reproducibility is not feasible. The LLaMA-8B model is open-source but the fine-tuning data and pipeline are not shared.
- **Significance (Moderate-High):** The predictability framework is a valuable contribution to the ads recommendation literature. The 8.62% reduction in A/A' difference is practically meaningful for advertisers. The generalizability claim (applicable beyond ads) is plausible but not demonstrated.
- **Red flags:** The paper is only 5 pages (workshop format), so depth is limited. The "top-line metric" is never explicitly named (likely revenue or conversions), making absolute interpretation difficult. No negative results or ablation studies are reported. The recall alignment ratio metric is somewhat confusingly presented with X/Y notation that obscures absolute values.

## Strengths

- Addresses a practically important and under-studied problem: prediction stability/predictability in recommendation systems
- Provides formal metric definitions (StatSigDiff, MAD) that can be adopted by other industrial practitioners
- Validated at truly large scale in a production system with real A/B experiments
- The semantic graph + graph traversal approach is elegant and interpretable
- Demonstrates that predictability and performance improvements are not mutually exclusive

## Limitations

- Workshop paper format (5 pages) limits technical depth and ablation studies
- Fine-tuning details for LLaMA-8B are not disclosed (data, prompts, hyperparameters)
- No comparison with other semantic representation methods (e.g., sentence-transformers, CLIP)
- The perturbation strategy for shadow ads is not fully specified, making it hard to assess metric sensitivity
- Results are specific to Meta's ads ecosystem; generalizability to other domains is claimed but unvalidated
- No analysis of computational cost or latency overhead introduced by the LLM-based retrieval layer

## Relevance

本文提出的可预测性量化框架与训练稳定性/鲁棒性评估直接相关，其语义表示方法对理解LLM在推荐系统中的表示质量有参考价值，但主要聚焦于广告推荐应用场景，与核心研究兴趣（LLM训练可观测性、隐藏状态可解释性）的关联较为间接。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How sensitive is the A/A' predictability metric to the type and magnitude of perturbation applied to shadow ads? Could this framework be adapted to measure training stability in LLM fine-tuning?
- What is the contribution of the LLM-extracted hierarchical attributes versus flat embeddings to the observed predictability improvement? An ablation would clarify this.
- Could the semantic graph traversal approach be applied to detect or mitigate representation drift in LLM hidden states during training?
- How does the framework perform on cold-start ads with minimal textual information, and how does it degrade with shorter or noisier ad descriptions?
- What are the latency and computational costs of the LLM-based retrieval compared to the baseline two-tower and embedding-based generators?
