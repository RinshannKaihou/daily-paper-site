Interpreting and Enhancing Emotional Circuits in Large Vision-Language Models via Cross-Modal Information Flow
Chengsheng Zhang, Chenghao Sun, Zhining Xie, Xinmei Tian · 2026-05-21 · ICML 2026
mechanistic-interpretability vision-language-models emotion-understanding activation-steering causal-attribution
LVLMs process emotions through a three-stage "Adapt-Aggregate-Execute" pipeline with striking functional decoupling: middle-layer heads are sentiment-specific, while deep-layer execution heads are emotion-agnostic and universally shared across all emotions.

Key Metric

+6.7% average across 9 MER-UniBench datasets (58.1% → 64.8%). Hardest dataset OV-MERD+ gains +9.2%. VEENA intervenes on only ~3% of parameters with negligible latency.

Why This Matters

First mechanistic account of emotional processing in LVLMs. The specific-to-universal decoupling pattern may generalize beyond emotion. VEENA is training-free and immediately applicable to any deployment.

Critical Edge

Only 4 basic emotions (happy, sad, angry, fear). LLaVA-OneVision shows -33.1 MME drop — architecture-dependent trade-offs. Hyperparameters require per-model calibration (l19 specific to Qwen3-VL-4B).

Methodology

Three-stage pipeline: (I) Emotional Direction Extraction — semantically controlled visual counterfactuals pair emotional images with neutralized counterparts (via Google Nano Banana), computing steering vectors as residual stream activation differences, filtered by hit rate. (II) Emotional Circuit Discovery — hierarchical: critical layer identification via layer-wise vector injection (layer 19 inflection point), attention head localization via a novel Latent Restoration Metric (R) shifting from discrete tokens to hidden-space cosine similarity with backward activation patching, and MLP neuron tracing via attribution patching (first-order Taylor) through gradient backpropagation. (III) VEENA intervention — Visual Emotion Enhancement (VEE) amplifies V→Q attention during prefill and V→L during decoding; Emotional Neuron Augmentation (ENA) applies sparse scaling to emotion-salient MLP neurons. Primary model: Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct; extended to Qwen3-VL-8B and LLaVA-OneVision-1.5-4B.

Strengths

  • First comprehensive mechanistic analysis of emotion in LVLMs
  • Elegant specific-to-universal decoupling finding
  • VEENA: training-free, surgical (~3% params), negligible latency
  • Latent Restoration Metric adapts causal analysis for open-ended generation
  • ICML 2026 accepted; extensive ablations across 9 datasets, 3 architectures

Limitations

  • Only 4 basic emotions; complex/mixed emotions unaddressed
  • Hyperparameters require per-model calibration
  • LLaVA-OV MME degradation signals architecture-dependent risks
  • No comparison with training-based methods (e.g., emotion fine-tuning)
  • Binary emotional/neutral distinction may be reductive

Open Questions

  • Does the specific-to-universal decoupling generalize to other abstract semantic domains (style, tone, formality)?
  • How do emotional circuits interact with humor, sarcasm, or cultural context?
  • Can the Latent Restoration Metric be adapted for other open-ended tasks where discrete metrics fail?
  • How robust are identified circuits to distribution shifts (art, cartoons, abstract imagery)?
