> **一句话总结：** 本文对Intel NCS2推理加速器进行了系统性单脉冲电磁故障注入实验（~17,500次试验），发现四种可重复故障类别：其中"严重持续退化"在热点区域触发率达18–31%（top-1准确率崩溃至2–5%），且设备空闲时注入同样有效，C2子类型分析揭示了部分崩溃和全饱和两种不同内部损坏机制。

> **TL;DR:** The first systematic single-pulse EMFI characterization of the Intel NCS2 (Myriad X VPU) running ResNet-18, ResNet-50, and VGG-11 via OpenVINO. Across ~1,536 spot tests and ~16,000 parameter-search trials (Optuna TPE sampler), four reproducible outcome classes emerge: C0 (no effect), C1 (minor SDC), C2 (major persistent degradation — top-1 collapses to 2–5%, survives all subsequent inferences until model reload), and C3 (device hang). At the primary hotspot (123.4, 155.1, 0.25 mm, V=348V), C2 rates reach 18–31% across models. Critically, pulses delivered to an idle device with a pre-loaded model are equally effective, and C2 events split into two sub-regimes (partial collapse vs. saturated single-class) revealed by logit-level analysis. All mitigations are application-level only.

## 核心贡献

- 首次对Intel NCS2（Myriad X VPU）进行系统性单脉冲EMFI实验，覆盖三种ImageNet CNN（ResNet-18、ResNet-50、VGG-11），使用Optuna TPE采样器驱动~16,000次参数搜索和256×6=1,536次定点测试。
- 建立四类故障分类体系：C0无影响、C1轻微SDC、C2严重持续退化（SEU类，跨推理持久直至模型重载）、C3设备挂起（SEFI类），并映射到辐射效应术语。
- 发现C2类故障的两种内部子类型：部分崩溃（logits在正常范围内但预测分布坍缩至~350类）和全饱和单类（logits达到定点表示极限±1023.5，所有输入预测为同一类）。
- 证明空闲设备脉冲注入同样可诱发C2（ResNet-50 before-inference C2率26.6%），且C1在ResNet上完全消失（0/256），表明损坏路径仅通过存储状态而非飞行中激活值。
- 发现架构依赖的鲁棒性差异：ResNet-18(24%,19%)、ResNet-50(31%,19%)、VGG-11(19%,40%)在(R_persist, R_fail)平面上占据不同区域，VGG-11的更高C1率与其更宽的全连接分类头相关。
- 提出分层应用级缓解策略：C3用看门狗、C2用参考图像交叉验证、C1用冗余推理。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic single-pulse EMFI campaign on the Intel NCS2 with three production-scale ImageNet classifiers, driven by Optuna TPE hyperparameter optimization for efficient parameter-space exploration over the five-dimensional (x, y, z, V, delay) space.
- Four-class fault taxonomy (C0/C1/C2/C3) with explicit mapping to radiation-effects fault models (SET/SDC, SEU, SEFI), with empirically calibrated thresholds (θ_minor=0.01, θ_major=0.50 top-1 accuracy).
- Discovery of two distinct C2 sub-regimes via logit-level analysis: (a) partial collapse where logits remain in nominal range but argmax distribution narrows from ~640 to ~350 classes, and (b) saturated single-class where logits hit the on-device fixed-point ceiling (±1023.5) and all inputs map to one class — a novel observation not reported in prior NCS2 work or simulation studies.
- Demonstration that before-inference pulses (d=−1s) induce C2 at comparable rates to during-inference pulses (d=+1s), with C1 entirely absent for ResNets in the before-inference regime, constraining the fault pathway to stored state rather than in-flight computation.
- Architecture-dependent robustness ranking in the (R_persist, R_fail) plane: VGG-11 shows higher C1 (16% vs. <3%) and C3 rates but lower C2 than ResNet-50, consistent with VGG's wider FC classifier head absorbing more corruptions into the minor-perturbation regime.
- Asynchronous inference mode preserves the four-class taxonomy but shifts outcome distributions: C3 rates increase markedly under async during-inference (~48% vs. ~25% sync), while C1 dominates under async before-inference.
- Application-level mitigation strategy graded by class: watchdog for C3, reference-image cross-checking for C2, redundant inference for C1 — all implementable without firmware or OpenVINO runtime modification.

## Methodology

The experimental setup centers on a single Intel NCS2 unit (Myriad X VPU) mounted in a custom fixture, connected via USB to a Raspberry Pi host. The EM pulse source is a ChipSHOUTER (NewAE Technology) with interchangeable probes (1mm CW, 1mm CCW, 4mm CCW).

**Exploratory phase (~16,000 trials):** The Optuna TPE sampler navigates the five-dimensional parameter space (x, y, z, V, delay) with multivariate group sampling. The objective function is the absolute deviation of top-1 accuracy from baseline, so both accuracy drops and unexpected increases are reported. Runs were performed separately per (model, probe, inference mode) combination.

**Spot-test phase (256 trials per model-timing pair):** Fixed parameter configurations selected from high-C1/C2-rate, low-C3-rate Optuna results. Primary hotspot: (x,y,z)=(123.4, 155.1, 0.25)mm, V=348V, 160ns pulse width, 1mm CCW probe. Each trial: reload model → verify load → position probe → charge → start inference → deliver single pulse → log results (accuracy, device flags, elapsed time) → repeat.

**Timing regimes:** d=+1s (pulse 1s after inference start = "during") and d=−1s (pulse 1s before inference start = "before"). The before-inference regime tests whether idle-loaded model state is fault-susceptible.

**Outcome classification thresholds:** θ_minor=0.01 (~5 images/512), θ_major=0.50. C0: within θ_minor of baseline. C1: between θ_minor and θ_major. C2: below baseline−θ_major AND persists across all subsequent inferences until model reload. C3: device fails within watchdog timeout, requires USB power-cycle.

**Metrics:** Top-1 and top-5 accuracy per trial; campaign-level misclassification rate R_mis, device-failure rate R_fail, persistent-fault rate R_persist.

**Evaluation workload:** 512 images from ImageNet validation set (fixed deterministic random seed) per trial. Baseline accuracy measured at start of each experimental day.

## Results & Highlights

**Spot-test outcome distribution (Table III, 256 trials per cell):**

| Model | Timing | C0 | C1 | C2 | C3 | R_persist | R_fail |
|-------|--------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----------|--------|
| ResNet-18 | During | 146 | 1 | 61 | 48 | 24% | 19% |
| ResNet-50 | During | 122 | 7 | 79 | 48 | 31% | 19% |
| VGG-11 | During | 64 | 41 | 48 | 103 | 19% | 40% |
| ResNet-18 | Before | 175 | 0 | 47 | 34 | 18% | 13% |
| ResNet-50 | Before | 138 | 0 | 68 | 50 | 27% | 20% |
| VGG-11 | Before | 91 | 60 | 75 | 30 | 29% | 12% |

- **Bimodal distributions:** ResNet accuracy histograms cluster sharply at either baseline or near-zero, with negligible intermediate mass. VGG-11 spreads substantially into the C1 regime (16% during-inference).
- **C2 sub-regimes:** Logit analysis of 22 ResNet-50 C2 events reveals 10 partial-collapse (logits in nominal range, ~350 predicted classes, ~23% agreement with golden output) and 12 saturated-single-class (logits at ±1023.5 ceiling, all inputs → one class, chance-level agreement). The dominant class in saturated events varies across trials despite fixed pulse parameters.
- **Persistence:** Every observed C2 event recovers to baseline accuracy after model reload via USB — no irreversible silicon damage. Recovery does not require a full USB power-cycle (unlike C3).
- **Idle-device vulnerability:** Before-inference C2 rates are comparable to during-inference. For ResNets, C1 drops to exactly zero, confirming the fault pathway is through stored state only. VGG-11 C1 actually increases (60 vs. 41 trials) and C3 drops (30 vs. 103).
- **Spatial sensitivity (Optuna map, ~16k trials):** Two lateral hotspots at (116.3, 154.9)mm and (123.4, 155.1)mm (7mm apart) produce C1/C2; central region between them is dominated by C3. Consistent with lateral compute/storage blocks vs. central control logic, but no die floorplan available for confirmation.
- **Probe effects:** 1mm probes produce qualitatively similar distributions. The 4mm probe only yields C2 at low voltage (~150V); at higher voltages, C3 dominates due to broader energy coupling affecting multiple functional blocks simultaneously.
- **Day-to-day repeatability:** 64-trial CW probe spot test repeated on two days yielded agreement within one trial across all four classes (53/0/10/1 vs. 52/1/10/1).
- **Async inference:** Four-class taxonomy preserved but distributions shift — during-inference async shows C0→near zero, C3 jumps to ~48%; before-inference async shows C1 as majority with C3 below sync rate.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The four-class taxonomy with radiation-effects mapping, the C2 sub-regime discovery via logit analysis, and the idle-device attack demonstration are all novel contributions beyond prior NCS2 feasibility studies and simulation work.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Large-scale experiments (~17,500 trials total), day-to-day repeatability verification, systematic parameter-space exploration via Bayesian optimization, and transparent reporting of thresholds and their rationale. The threats-to-validity section is commendably thorough (Section VII).
- **Reproducibility: Moderate.** The NCS2 is commercially available and the methodology is well-documented, but hotspot coordinates and voltage thresholds are likely unit-specific. The single-device limitation is explicitly acknowledged. The ChipSHOUTER is a commercially available tool (~$3,000), making replication feasible for well-equipped labs.
- **Significance: High** for hardware security and edge AI safety. The idle-device attack finding directly undermines a natural defense assumption, and the architecture-dependent ranking introduces model choice as a deployment-time reliability lever.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - **Single device sample:** All results from one NCS2 unit. Inter-unit variability is completely unknown, and the authors explicitly flag this as the second-most-important threat to validity. The absolute coordinates, voltages, and class-distribution ratios may not transfer.
  - **No no-pulse control:** No matched campaign without EM pulses to bound the baseline rate of C2-like outcomes from confounds (USB drift, thermal throttling). The spatial concentration of C2 at hotspots makes a non-spatial confound unlikely, but a direct control would be stronger.
  - **Millisecond-scale trigger jitter:** Software-triggered pulse timing precludes per-layer fault localization and is acknowledged as the largest threat to validity. This limits mechanistic understanding — the paper characterizes *what* happens but not *where* in the model the corruption occurs.
  - **Unvalidated mitigations:** The reference-image cross-checking and redundant inference proposals are discussed but not experimentally benchmarked. Detection coverage, false-positive rates, and latency are all unquantified.
  - **NCS2 is a discontinued product:** Intel ended NCS2 production, limiting the direct deployment relevance. However, the Myriad X VPU itself continues in other form factors, and the methodological contributions generalize.
  - **Image classification only:** No evidence the taxonomy generalizes to object detection, depth estimation, or transformer-based inference.

## Strengths

- Exceptional experimental scale and systematic design: Bayesian optimization for parameter exploration + fixed-configuration spot tests with 256-trial repetition.
- The four-class taxonomy is clean, reproducible, and maps naturally to established fault models — likely to become a reference framework for future EMFI studies on NN accelerators.
- The C2 sub-regime analysis (partial collapse vs. saturated single-class) via logit-level inspection goes well beyond what most fault-injection papers report and provides concrete evidence about the nature of on-device corruption.
- The idle-device experiment is both practically important and scientifically clean: ResNet C1 dropping to exactly zero is a strong signal that the fault pathway is through stored state.
- Transparent and honest threats-to-validity section (Section VII) that clearly delineates what the data supports vs. what remains hypothesis.
- The spatial sensitivity analysis with two distinct hotspots and the central C3-dominated region provides actionable guidance for future work.

## Limitations

- Single device sample — no inter-unit variability characterization.
- Software trigger jitter prevents per-layer fault localization; the exact corruption target (weights, instructions, or configuration state) remains unidentified.
- No experimentally validated mitigations; the detection and recovery strategies are proposal-level only.
- No comparison with other FI methods (laser, voltage glitching) on the same device.
- NCS2 is discontinued; direct deployment relevance is limited.
- No die floorplan available, so spatial-to-functional-block correspondence remains hypothetical.
- Ambient temperature not logged during the campaign.
- Workload restricted to image classification with three CNN architectures; no evidence of generalization to other task types or architectures (e.g., transformers).

## Relevance

本文系统性地揭示了边缘AI推理硬件在电磁故障注入下的脆弱性，发现空闲状态下模型权重仍可被破坏，这对安全关键场景中AI系统的部署安全具有重要警示意义。四类故障分类体系和架构依赖的鲁棒性差异为AI硬件安全评估提供了实用框架。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would a hardware GPIO trigger (replacing the software USB serial trigger) reveal layer-specific sensitivity profiles consistent with simulation predictions from prior work (Li et al., Reagen et al.)?
- Can intermediate-tensor dumps from an instrumented OpenVINO build definitively localize the C2 corruption to weights, compiled SHAVE instructions, or accelerator configuration state?
- How does the four-class taxonomy transfer to newer edge accelerators (Google Coral Edge TPU, NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano, Hailo-8) under matched EMFI conditions?
- What is the quantitative detection coverage of reference-image cross-checking for C2 events as a function of the number and diversity of reference images, and can logit-level comparison (rather than top-1 label) close the coverage gap?
- Does the partial-collapse vs. saturated-single-class C2 sub-regime split appear on other accelerators, and does it correlate with the numerical precision (FP16, INT8, binary) of the inference pipeline?
- What minimum attack distance (beyond contact/near-contact probe positioning) is feasible for inducing C2, and how does electromagnetic shielding affect the threshold?
- Can the architecture-dependent robustness ranking (VGG-11's higher C1 absorption via wider FC layers) inform adversarially robust model design for edge deployment under physical fault models?
