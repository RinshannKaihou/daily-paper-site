> **一句话总结：** 本文通过按时间顺序（而非随机打乱）使用 Common Crawl 快照预训练 6B 参数语言模型，发现时序预训练在通用语言能力上与打乱训练持平，但在近期事实知识上显著优于打乱基线甚至更大的开源模型，首次系统性地揭示了数据排序对 LLM 时间知识分布的关键影响。

> **TL;DR:** Pre-training 6B LLMs on chronologically ordered Common Crawl snapshots (vs. standard shuffled data) yields models that match shuffled baselines on general NLP benchmarks while exhibiting significantly more up-to-date and temporally precise factual knowledge, outperforming even larger open-source models on recent facts.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **KairosQA**，一个包含 7,000+ 时间敏感问答对的基准测试，基于 Wikidata 构建，能够评估模型是否正确将事实与对应年份关联
- 在严格受控条件下预训练 6B 参数模型，分别使用按时间顺序排列和随机打乱的 Common Crawl 数据（2.5T tokens），隔离数据排序效应
- 证明时序预训练在 OLMES 通用语言理解基准上与打乱训练持平，但在近期事实知识上持续优于打乱基线
- 揭示打乱训练存在"时间对齐惯性"——模型在较旧数据上表现更好，可能由于旧事实在网络爬取中重复更多
- 发布代码、检查点和数据集（KairosQA + Sequential Helium 6B），为持续学习研究奠定基础

## Key Contributions

- Introduce **KairosQA**, a benchmark of 7,000+ temporally grounded QA pairs extracted from Wikidata with rigorous filtering, designed to evaluate whether models correctly associate facts with specific years
- Pre-train 6B-parameter Transformer models on temporally sequential Common Crawl snapshots (2018–2025) and compare against shuffled baselines (2020–2024), both trained on 2.5T tokens
- Demonstrate that sequential pre-training matches shuffled baselines on general language understanding (OLMES) while consistently exhibiting more up-to-date and temporally precise knowledge
- Identify **"temporal alignment inertia"** in shuffled models: they peak on older data (2015/2020) and degrade sharply on recent facts, even when trained on data up to 2024
- Show the sequential model outperforms significantly larger open-source models (Qwen3-8B, Gemma-3-12B, Olmo3-7B) on recent factual knowledge (2023–2024)
- Release intermediate yearly checkpoints enabling future research on continual learning and forgetting mitigation

## Methodology

**Model architecture.** A 6B-parameter Transformer decoder with 32 layers, 32 attention heads, 4096 hidden dimension, Grouped-Query Attention (4 KV heads), RoPE positional embeddings, and SwiGLU activations. Both sequential and shuffled models share identical architectures and hyperparameters.

**Data pipeline.** Training corpora are constructed from Common Crawl snapshots using the open-source `dactory` pipeline: HTML extraction → language filtering (24 European languages, fastText confidence > 0.85) → Bloom filter deduplication (novel content > 20%) → domain-weighted quality scoring (7 domains) → degenerate text removal. The sequential model processes snapshots from 2018 through 2025 in strict chronological order (~315B tokens/year, 2.5T total). The shuffled baseline uses aggregated 2020–2024 snapshots with global random shuffling.

**Training.** AdamW optimizer with Warmup-Stable-Decay scheduler (peak LR 1e-3), 2.5T total tokens, batch size 4.2M tokens, context length 4096. A branching cooldown strategy (30k steps cosine decay) produces fully converged intermediate checkpoints at each year boundary.

**KairosQA construction.** Starting from 17M Wikidata triplets, successive filtering steps (temporal validity, popularity constraint via Wikipedia page views, temporal variation ≥ 2 changes between 2018–2025) yield 7,167 subject–relation pairs. Questions are generated using GPT-4o mini from relation-specific templates with year placeholders. Distractors are drawn from valid answers in neighboring years plus LLM-generated candidates. A Claude-assisted quality control pipeline resolves ambiguities (achiever/work confusion, ill-defined awards).

**Evaluation protocol.** Two complementary settings: (1) **Cloze formulation** — rank candidates by log-probability normalized by character length; (2) **Generative** — free-text answer with normalized F1 score. Both are evaluated across years to measure temporal knowledge alignment.

## Results & Highlights

- **General capabilities preserved:** Sequential model converges to parity with shuffled baseline on OLMES (~71.7% vs ~71.7% at 2.5T tokens), though it lags in mid-training due to non-stationary data distribution.
- **Recency peak:** Each sequential checkpoint achieves peak accuracy in the year immediately preceding its training cutoff, demonstrating effective temporal grounding.
- **Shuffled temporal inertia:** Shuffled baselines exhibit two local maxima (2015 and 2020) with precipitous decline toward 2024, suggesting older facts are over-represented through repetition across CC dumps.
- **Beats larger models:** The sequential 6B model surpasses Qwen3-8B, Gemma-3-12B, and Olmo3-7B on 2023–2024 temporal facts, despite being 2–4× smaller.
- **Trade-off — relative forgetting:** Sequential models sacrifice older knowledge (pre-2020) for recency, though total token volume partially mitigates this in later checkpoints.
- **Forgetting mitigation fails:** Model soup (weight interpolation) and replay (50/50 mixture of old/new data during cooldown) both prove insufficient — they induce a zero-sum trade-off rather than recovering old knowledge while retaining new.
- **Data quality matters:** Cool-down on 2024 data instead of 2021 data for the Seq 2021 checkpoint yields +1.5% OLMES improvement, suggesting "sequential lag" is driven by data quality variability, not intrinsic to the paradigm.
- **Subject popularity correlation:** Top 10% most popular subjects achieve ~0.4 F1 on generative tasks vs. ~0.1 for the bottom 50%, indicating sequential pre-training still favors frequently mentioned entities.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: High.** While continual learning for LLMs has been explored, this is the first systematic, production-scale study isolating the effect of data ordering during pre-training on temporal knowledge. The KairosQA benchmark itself is a novel contribution with careful design choices.

**Rigor: Strong.** The controlled experimental setup (identical architecture, matched token budgets, branching cooldown) is well-designed. The use of both cloze and generative evaluations, plus comparison with multiple open-source baselines, adds robustness. The acknowledgment of temporal asymmetry in training windows (2018–2025 vs. 2020–2024) is transparent.

**Reproducibility: Excellent.** Full code release, model checkpoints, and dataset. Training hyperparameters are detailed (Table 4). The Wikidata filtering pipeline is thoroughly documented.

**Significance: High.** The finding that a 6B sequential model beats much larger shuffled models on recent facts has practical implications for continual LLM development. The released checkpoints enable a new line of research on forgetting and continual learning.

**Red flags:**
- The KairosQA benchmark is heavily weighted toward sports and award-related facts (Table 5), which may limit generalizability of temporal claims to other domains like science or politics.
- The forgetting mitigation experiments (model soup, replay) are preliminary and inconclusive — the core trade-off remains unresolved.
- The shuffled baseline was pre-trained before the project (with a different 2020–2024 window), introducing a minor confound despite the authors' transparency about it.
- The sequential model's mid-training lag could be problematic in practice if training budgets are constrained.

## Strengths

- Rigorous controlled experimental design isolating the effect of data ordering
- Novel benchmark (KairosQA) with careful construction, quality control, and robustness validation
- Strong empirical evidence that a small sequential model can outperform much larger shuffled models on recent facts
- Comprehensive analysis including learning dynamics, subject popularity, and distractor robustness
- Excellent reproducibility with full code, checkpoint, and dataset release
- Honest discussion of limitations and failed mitigation attempts

## Limitations

- **Domain bias in KairosQA:** The benchmark is dominated by sports (~55%) and award facts; temporal dynamics may differ for scientific, political, or cultural knowledge
- **Forgetting remains unsolved:** Model soup and replay both fail to mitigate catastrophic forgetting of older knowledge without sacrificing recent knowledge
- **Mid-training efficiency gap:** Sequential training lags behind shuffled during early-to-mid training, which could be costly in practice
- **Scale limitation:** Experiments are conducted only at 6B scale; it is unclear whether findings transfer to 70B+ models with different training regimes
- **Language limitation:** Training data covers only 24 European languages; temporal dynamics may differ for non-European languages
- **Asymmetric training windows:** Sequential model uses 2018–2025 data while shuffled uses 2020–2024, though the authors argue older data does not confer an advantage

## Relevance

本研究对理解 LLM 预训练中数据排序如何影响知识时效性提供了系统性实证分析。时序预训练使小模型（6B）在近期事实上超越大模型，这对持续学习和模型更新具有直接的实际意义。KairosQA 基准为评估模型的时间知识对齐提供了新工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do findings transfer to 70B+ scale models with different architectures (e.g., MoE)?
- Can architecture-level innovations (e.g., time-aware parameters as in TiMoE) mitigate the forgetting-relevance trade-off that simple replay and model soup cannot?
- Would combining sequential pre-training with domain upsampling (as in Blakeney et al. 2024) yield complementary benefits for both recency and breadth of knowledge?
- How does the temporal dynamics of knowledge acquisition differ for scientific facts versus sports/award facts that dominate KairosQA?
- Can the sequential checkpoint series be leveraged for efficient model updating — e.g., fine-tuning only the most recent checkpoint on new data rather than retraining from scratch?
