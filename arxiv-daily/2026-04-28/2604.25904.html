Teacher Forcing as Generalized Bayes: Optimization Geometry Mismatch in Switching Surrogates for Chaotic Dynamics

Andre Herz, Daniel Durstewitz, Georgia Koppe

arXiv: 2604.25904v1

Date: 2026-04-28

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25904v1

一句话总结： 本文将Identity Teacher Forcing (ITF) 视为一种广义贝叶斯更新（Gibbs posterior），通过 Louis 恒等式估计切换状态空间模型（PAL-RNN）的 ambiguity-aware 观测信息，揭示了 ITF 因条件化在单一强制切换路径上而膨胀曲率、而边际似然因缺失信息校正项而曲率降低的几何失配现象；在 Lorenz-63 实验中发现，通过粒子 SAEM 微调提升窗口化证据会反而恶化动力学不变量（Lyapunov 指数、状态空间重叠度）。
TL;DR: This paper frames Identity Teacher Forcing (ITF) as a generalized Bayes (Gibbs posterior) update and uses Louis' identity to estimate ambiguity-aware observed information for switching state-space models (PAL-RNNs). It reveals that ITF inflates curvature by conditioning on a single forced switching path, while marginal likelihood curvature is reduced by a missing-information correction when multiple regime explanations remain plausible. In Lorenz-63 experiments, windowed evidence fine-tuning via particle-SAEM improves held-out evidence yet degrades dynamical invariants (Lyapunov exponents, state-space overlap).

核心贡献

  • 将 ITF 训练目标解释为广义贝叶斯更新（Gibbs posterior），从而建立了 ITF 诱导的局部曲率与边际似然诱导的曲率之间的可比较框架。
  • 引入 PAL-RNN（Probabilistic AL-RNN），通过对 AL-RNN 的切换编码引入 probit 门噪声，将其概率化增强为切换线性-高斯 SSM。
  • 使用 Louis 恒等式（通过 Rao-Blackwellized 粒子滤波器/平滑器估计）分解观测信息为完整数据项减去反映潜变量不确定性的缺失信息校正项，揭示了切换模糊性降低边际似然曲率的机制。
  • 定义了局部曲率差距指标 g_Q，量化 ITF 曲率相对于 ambiguity-aware 观测信息的膨胀程度，并在 Lorenz-63 上展示了 10 到 10^3 倍的差距。
  • 实验表明证据-动力学不变量之间存在失配：通过 particle-SAEM 微调提升窗口化条件对数证据会显著恶化 Lyapunov 指数误差和状态空间分布重叠度 D_stsp。
  • 证明几何失配是各向异性和子空间依赖的，而非均匀缩放：大多数广义特征值方向在 ITF 下并不更尖锐（q^γ_0.9 < 0），主导子空间重叠度随切换熵增加而降低。

Key Contributions

  • Interprets the ITF training objective as a generalized Bayes update (Gibbs posterior), establishing a principled comparison framework between ITF-induced local curvature and marginal likelihood curvature.
  • Introduces PAL-RNN (Probabilistic AL-RNN), which probabilistically augments AL-RNNs with probit gate noise, turning them into switching linear-Gaussian SSMs with latent switching codes.
  • Uses Louis' identity (estimated via Rao-Blackwellized Particle Filter/Smoother) to decompose observed information into a complete-data term minus a missing-information correction reflecting latent uncertainty, revealing the mechanism by which switching ambiguity reduces marginal likelihood curvature.
  • Defines a local curvature gap metric g_Q that quantifies ITF curvature inflation relative to ambiguity-aware observed information, showing gaps ranging from 10x to 10^3x on Lorenz-63.
  • Demonstrates an evidence-QoI misalignment: particle-SAEM fine-tuning on windowed conditional log-evidence improves held-out evidence but significantly degrades Lyapunov exponent error and state-space distribution overlap D_stsp.
  • Shows the geometry mismatch is anisotropic and subspace-dependent rather than uniform: most generalized-eigenvalue directions are not sharper under ITF (q^γ_0.9 < 0), and leading-subspace overlap decreases with switching entropy.

Methodology

Models. The work builds on Almost-Linear RNNs (AL-RNNs), which combine a diagonal linear matrix A, a full gated matrix W, and ReLU activations on P coordinates to produce interpretable switching dynamics with 2^P linear subregions. The switching code c_t is deterministically derived from ReLU sign patterns. The probabilistic augmentation, PAL-RNN, replaces deterministic gating with a state-dependent probit gate model: c_{t,j} | z_t ~ Bernoulli(Phi(z_{t,M-P+j} / sigma_g)), adding isotropic Gaussian process and observation noise. Conditional on a gate path c_{1:T-1}, the model reduces to a linear-Gaussian SSM, enabling closed-form Kalman filtering/smoothing.

ITF Curvature Proxy. ITF training is framed as optimizing a Gibbs posterior pi_ITF(theta | x) proportional to pi_0(theta) exp(-beta L_ITF(theta; x)). The ITF-aligned curvature proxy I_ITF is a per-step normalized generalized Gauss-Newton/Fisher matrix computed in closed form by differentiating the forced rollout. At forcing times, sensitivities are reset by zeroing out observed-coordinate rows. The piecewise-linear AL-RNN structure yields closed-form Jacobians J_t = A + W D(c_t).

Ambiguity-Aware Observed Information. The marginal likelihood curvature is estimated via Louis' identity: I_obs(theta) = E[I_comp(theta) | x] - Cov(grad_theta log p(x,z,c | theta) | x), where I_miss is the missing-information term. The RBPF with N_p=64 particles approximates the smoothing distribution over gate paths; S=8 approximate smoothed trajectories are sampled backward. The observed information estimate is the Monte Carlo difference between complete-data information expectation and score covariance over smoothing samples.

Curvature Gap. g_Q = log10(T * tr(I_ITF) / tr(I_obs)), where T is segment length. This measures how much ITF curvature exceeds ambiguity-aware observed information on a per-step scale.

Particle-SAEM Fine-Tuning. PAL-RNNs initialized from ITF-trained AL-RNN checkpoints are fine-tuned via Stochastic Approximation EM on windowed conditional log-evidence log p_theta(x_{t+1:t+L} | x_t). Three configurations are compared: baseline (no updates), calib (update only sigma_proc, sigma_obs noise parameters), and full SAEM (update drift parameters A, W, h + noise). Experiments span 12 ITF-pretrained initializations across five window lengths (16, 32, 64, 128, 200).

Evaluation Metrics. Dynamical QoIs: D_stsp (KL divergence between generated and reference state-space histograms with Laplace smoothing) and signed largest Lyapunov exponent error delta_lambda_1 relative to the true Lorenz-63 reference. Evidence is evaluated via RBPF normalizing-constant estimates on held-out windows, normalized per step and per observed dimension.

Results & Highlights

  • Curvature inflation by forced conditioning. The curvature gap g_Q between ITF and ambiguity-aware observed information ranges from approximately 10 to 10^3, increasing with filtering switching code entropy H_c. This demonstrates that conditioning on a single forced switching path (as ITF does) substantially inflates local curvature relative to the marginal likelihood, which properly integrates over switching ambiguity.
  • Missing-information mechanism validated. In a controlled two-state switching AR(1) toy model, increasing gate noise sigma_g raises both posterior switching entropy and the missing-information ratio (MIR), while decreasing observed-curvature proxy log10 tr(I_obs). This confirms that the Louis missing-information correction mechanism operates as predicted: greater switching ambiguity reduces marginal likelihood curvature.
  • Anisotropic, subspace-dependent mismatch. Matrix-aware diagnostics reveal that the curvature mismatch is not uniform. The log-determinant gap Delta_log_det is negative, and q^gamma_0.9 < 0 (at least 90% of generalized-eigenvalue directions have gamma_i < 1), meaning most directions are NOT sharper under ITF. The mismatch concentrates in specific subspaces, with leading-subspace overlap ov_50 decreasing as switching entropy increases.
  • Evidence-QoI misalignment. Full SAEM fine-tuning improves held-out windowed evidence (up to ~0.1-0.2 nats/step/dim gain) but substantially degrades D_stsp (state-space overlap) and increases Lyapunov exponent error (models become overly contracting, lambda_1 - lambda_1^ref < 0). Calibration-only updates (noise parameters) leave hard-gated QoIs unchanged but provide smaller evidence gains. This exposes a fundamental tension: what improves short-window probabilistic fit does not necessarily preserve long-run invariant structure.
  • Robustness of entropy-gap association. Within fixed process-noise strata, the positive rank correlation between H_c and g_Q is largely explained by observation-noise variation. After rank-residualizing against sigma_obs, partial correlations are near zero or negative, suggesting the association is driven by noise-level variation rather than a strong residual model-specific entropy effect.

Critical Assessment

Novelty. The framing of teacher forcing as a generalized Bayes update and the use of Louis' identity to compare ITF-induced curvature with marginal likelihood curvature is novel and insightful. The connection between training-objective geometry and downstream dynamical fidelity is an important and underexplored question in DSR. However, the core components (AL-RNNs, RBPF, Louis' identity, SAEM) are all well-established individually; the novelty lies in their synthesis and the curvature-comparison framework.

Rigor. The theoretical development is careful: Louis' identity is properly stated and decomposed, the ITF curvature proxy is derived in closed form, and the PAL-RNN augmentation is clearly specified. Monte Carlo estimation with RBPF/Louis is validated with diagnostics (ESS, entropy). The fixed-Q stratification analysis (Appendix A4.1) shows intellectual honesty in probing the robustness of the entropy-gap association, acknowledging that it is largely driven by observation-noise variation rather than a strong residual model-to-model entropy effect.

Reproducibility. The paper provides detailed appendices with algorithm pseudocode, hyperparameter tables, and metric definitions. The Lorenz-63 system is standard and reproducible. However, no code is referenced or linked, and the RBPF/Louis and particle-SAEM implementations involve non-trivial engineering (ridge stabilization, symmetrization, damping schedules). Full reproduction would require significant implementation effort without code release.

Significance. The central finding -- that improving windowed evidence can degrade dynamical QoIs -- is practically important for DSR practitioners who might assume that better probabilistic fit implies better dynamics. The curvature gap analysis provides a mechanistic explanation. However, the scope is limited: only Lorenz-63 is tested, only ITF (not generalized teacher forcing with varying strength) is compared, and only one surrogate class (AL-RNN) is used.

Potential Red Flags. The workshop-venue status (not full conference proceedings) limits the depth of review. The single-system (Lorenz-63) evaluation raises questions about generalizability. The PAL-RNN augmentation introduces hyperparameters (sigma_g, sigma_proc, sigma_obs) whose settings could influence results. The SAEM experiments use only 8 iterations (see Table 2), which may be insufficient for convergence and could confound the evidence-QoI conclusions. The RBPF/Louis estimates use only S=8 smoothing samples, which may introduce non-negligible Monte Carlo error.

Strengths

  • Clear and principled theoretical framework connecting teacher forcing (a heuristic training technique) to generalized Bayesian inference and curvature geometry.
  • Mechanistic explanation (via Louis' missing-information term) for why teacher forcing curvature deviates from marginal likelihood curvature.
  • Honest and nuanced presentation of results: the mismatch is anisotropic, not uniform, and the entropy-gap association is partially confounded by noise-level variation.
  • The evidence-QoI misalignment finding is practically relevant and serves as a cautionary note for DSR practitioners.
  • Well-structured appendix with detailed derivations, algorithm pseudocode, and diagnostic analyses (ESS monitoring, stratification checks, matrix-aware mismatch diagnostics).
  • Closes with substantive limitations and future work, including acknowledgment of local-curvature limitations, Monte Carlo approximation concerns, and the windowing-vs-full-sequence tension.

Limitations

  • Single dynamical system evaluated (Lorenz-63). Generalizability to higher-dimensional, more complex, or real-world systems is unestablished.
  • Only ITF (hard overwrite at fixed intervals) is studied. Generalized teacher forcing with continuous forcing strength is not explored.
  • Only AL-RNN surrogates are used. Whether the findings extend to other RNN architectures (GRU, LSTM, PLRNN) is unknown.
  • The PAL-RNN augmentation introduces fixed hyperparameters (sigma_g, sigma_proc, sigma_obs) that could influence the curvature comparison; sensitivity to these choices is only partially explored.
  • SAEM fine-tuning uses only 8 iterations, raising concerns about convergence. The evidence-QoI misalignment could be partly a transient optimization artifact rather than a fundamental property.
  • RBPF/Louis estimates use low particle/smoothing budgets (N_p=64, S=8), which may introduce substantial Monte Carlo variance.
  • All curvature comparisons are local (around a trained checkpoint). Global geometry could differ substantially due to multimodality and flat directions in non-convex switching models.
  • The mapping from "improved evidence but degraded QoIs" to practical recommendations for DSR training is not developed.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does the curvature gap scale with system dimensionality and complexity beyond Lorenz-63 (e.g., Kuramoto-Sivashinsky, turbulent flows, or real neural data)?
  2. Would generalized teacher forcing with continuous forcing strength (alpha < 1) interpolate between ITF-curvature and marginal-likelihood curvature in a tunable way, and could this be exploited for better QoI preservation?
  3. Can the Louis missing-information correction be used as a regularizer or curriculum signal during training to encourage exploration of switching-ambiguous regions?
  4. Is the evidence-QoI misalignment a fundamental property of switching SSMs or a consequence of the specific PAL-RNN augmentation and insufficient SAEM convergence (only 8 iterations)?
  5. How do the findings extend to other surrogate architectures (GRU, LSTM, vanilla PLRNN) and other teacher forcing variants (sparse forcing, scheduled sampling)?
  6. Can ambiguity-aware observed information be approximated scalably (e.g., via structured matrix approximations or variational bounds) to enable its use as a practical diagnostic in larger-scale DSR training?
  7. Would incorporating dynamical QoIs directly into the training objective (e.g., via differentiable Lyapunov exponent estimators or sliced Wasserstein distances between invariant measures) resolve the evidence-QoI tension?
