The Surprising Universality of LLM Outputs: A Real-Time Verification Primitive

Alex Bogdan, Adrian de Valois-Franklin (EvolutionairyAI, Toronto, Canada)

arXiv: 2604.25634v1

Date: 2026-04-28

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25634

一句话总结： 本文发现前沿大模型（GPT-5.1、Claude Sonnet 4.6、Llama 3.1 8B、Gemini 2.5 Pro、Mistral Large、Qwen 2.5 7B）的输出 token rank-frequency 分布惊人地收敛于同一个双参数 Mandelbrot 分布族（R² > 0.94，34/36 条件下），并据此提出一个仅需 CPU、延迟约 2.6 微秒/token 的评分基元，比采样式检测器快约 10 万倍，同时支持无需水印的统计模型指纹识别（cross-model q 值跨越 1.63–3.69，远超 per-model 自举标准差 0.03–0.10，一个数量级以上）。
TL;DR: The authors discover that token rank-frequency distributions from six independently trained frontier LLMs (GPT-5.1, Claude Sonnet 4.6, Llama 3.1 8B, Gemini 2.5 Pro, Mistral Large, Qwen 2.5 7B) converge strongly to the same two-parameter Mandelbrot ranking distribution (R² > 0.94 in 34/36 conditions, Mandelbrot preferred over Zipf by AIC in 35/36). From this regularity, they derive a CPU-only scoring primitive (2.6 μs/token, ~100,000× faster than sampling-based detectors) and a statistical model fingerprinting pipeline. The fitted Mandelbrot parameters remain cleanly separable per model (q spread 1.63–3.69 vs. per-model bootstrap σ of 0.03–0.10), enabling provenance verification without watermarks. Pilot results on FRANK, TruthfulQA, and HaluEval map where the primitive helps (lexical anomalies, unsupported entities, out-of-source surface forms) and where it structurally cannot (reasoning errors in domain-appropriate vocabulary). The primitive is positioned as a first-pass triage layer in compound evaluation stacks, not as a replacement for semantic verifiers.

核心贡献

  • C1 — 跨模型的共享统计指纹： 在 6 个前沿 LLM（涵盖 5 家独立厂商、两种参数量级、推理/非推理模型）的 5 个留存领域数据上，token rank-frequency 分布强收敛于双参数 Mandelbrot 形式。34/36 个 model×domain 拟合的 R² > 0.94，35/36 个比较中 AIC 选择 Mandelbrot 而非 Zipf。在此共享族内，拟合参数保持模型间可分离：cross-model q 值范围 1.63–3.69，远超 per-model 自举标准差 0.03–0.10（一个数量级以上）。
  • C2 — 面向溯源（provenance）的统计模型指纹识别： 基于 C1 的参数可分离性，提出一套轻量级指纹识别流水线：第三方审计者可以仅凭输出文本检验其 Mandelbrot 拟合参数是否与声称的模型族一致，检测静默模型替换和 wrapper 套利行为。该方法不需要训练、不需要水印嵌入、不需要模型内部参数，与密码学水印方法互补。
  • C3 — 面向黑盒 LLM 输出的 CPU-only 首轮评分基元（Ranking Inference, RI）： 从变分自由能最小化推导出单次前向评分基元，当 log-probs 可用时组合模型概率与 Mandelbrot 参考分布，当 log-probs 不可用时降级为纯 rank-only 模式（适用于封闭 API）。在 AMD Ryzen CPU 上约 2.6 μs/token（gap-only 模式 0.139 ms/passage）。
  • C4 — 可证伪的三层错误分类体系： Tier 1（分布异常：无支撑实体、域外表层形式、罕见词汇插入）可检测（AUC 0.60–0.95）；Tier 1.5（关系和情境偏移）信号弱但非零（AUC 0.55–0.65）；Tier 2（以恰当领域词汇表达的推理错误）本质上处于随机水平。该分类体系为基元的定位提供了可操作的边界——首轮过滤器，而非终审验证器。

Key Contributions

  • C1 — Shared statistical signature across frontier LLMs. Across 6 models from 5 independent vendors, 2 size classes, and 5 held-out domains, token rank-frequency distributions converge to the same 2-parameter Mandelbrot ranking distribution (R² > 0.94 in 34/36 fits; AIC favors Mandelbrot over Zipf in 35/36). Within that shared family, fitted parameters remain cleanly separable per model: the cross-model spread in q (1.63–3.69) exceeds per-model bootstrap standard deviation (0.03–0.10) by more than an order of magnitude. The paper frames this as "shared data and objective produce a shared family; distinct training pipelines leave distinguishable fingerprints inside it."
  • C2 — Statistical model fingerprinting for provenance. The parameter separability in C1 supports a lightweight, training-free fingerprinting pipeline. A held-out auditor can test whether output text is statistically consistent with a claimed model family, detect silent model substitution in production, and flag vendor-routing arbitrage. This complements, rather than replaces, cryptographic watermarking.
  • C3 — CPU-only first-pass scoring primitive for black-box LLM outputs. Derived from variational free-energy minimization, the scoring primitive composes the model's log-probabilities with a model-agnostic Mandelbrot reference when log-probs are available, and degrades gracefully to a pure rank-only mode when they are not. Benchmarked at ~2.6 μs/token on a modest AMD Ryzen CPU (0.139 ms/passage in gap-only mode), approximately 100,000× faster than sampling-based detectors.
  • C4 — Falsifiable three-tier error taxonomy. Tier 1 errors (unsupported entities, out-of-source surface forms, rare lexical insertions) are distributionally detectable above chance. Tier 1.5 (relational and circumstantial drift) is weak but nonzero. Tier 2 (reasoning errors expressed in domain-appropriate vocabulary) is at chance by construction. This taxonomy makes explicit, testable predictions about where any purely distributional method can and cannot contribute.

Methodology

Core Theoretical Framework. The paper uses the Mandelbrot Ranking Distribution f(r) = C/(r+q)^s, which generalizes Zipf's law (q=0, s=1) with two parameters: q captures head flattening (the softening of the "a, the, of" end) and s controls tail steepness. Mandelbrot originally derived this from entropy maximization under a coding cost constraint, making it an information-theoretically motivated form.

Rank Deviation Signal. For each token, they compute Δ_r(w) = log_2(r_global(w) / r_local(w)) in bits — a scale-free measure of how anomalously prominent a token is in its local context relative to a 4-billion-token Wikipedia reference corpus. A token like "phosphorylation" appearing 3 times in a short biochemistry paragraph (r_local=3) but at r_global≈45,000 yields Δ_r ≈ 14 bits.

Variational Posterior (Ranking Inference, RI). They combine the LLM's context-conditioned probability with the Mandelbrot rank-implied distribution via a precision-weighted product-of-experts: P_posterior(w|c) ∝ P_LLM(w|c) × P_RI(w)^β. The exponent β is not tuned manually but estimated from the empirical variance of rank-deviation residuals in each domain (β = 1/(ε + σ²_Δr)), functioning as a domain-specific trust coefficient on the shared reference.

Experimental Design for Convergence. Six models (GPT-5.1, Claude Sonnet 4.6, Llama 3.1 8B, Gemini 2.5 Pro, Mistral Large, Qwen 2.5 7B) generated outputs across 5 domains (news, biomedical, legal, code, social media), 20 prompts per domain, totaling 100 prompts per model. All outputs were re-tokenized with the Llama 3.1 8B BPE vocabulary to ensure a common token space. Each of 36 rank tables (6 models × 6 rank tables) was fitted by maximum likelihood with 100-iteration bootstrap confidence intervals.

Evaluation Protocol. Three complementary signals: R² on log-log representation (variance explained), Kolmogorov-Smirnov statistic on empirical CDF (local deviations), and AIC for Mandelbrot vs. Zipf model selection. Pilot benchmarks: FRANK (6,356 human-annotated error spans), TruthfulQA (817 questions), and HaluEval (30,000 examples).

Results & Highlights

  • Near-perfect Mandelbrot fits. R² > 0.90 for every cell in the 6×6 matrix; 34/36 exceed 0.94. Mandelbrot preferred over Zipf by AIC in 35/36 comparisons (ΔAIC ranging from +18 to +15,094). The single tie (Claude on legal text, ΔAIC = −1) occurs where fitted q ≈ 0 and Mandelbrot collapses to Zipf.
  • Domain matters more than vendor. Cross-model overlay shows that the scatter between models in the body of the distribution (ranks 10–10,000) is smaller than the scatter between domains within a single model. A GPT-5.1 news output looks more like a Qwen 2.5 7B news output than like a GPT-5.1 code output.
  • Parameter separability enables fingerprinting. Fitted global q values: GPT-5.1=3.69, Gemini 2.5 Pro=3.13, Qwen 2.5 7B=2.89, Llama 3.1 8B=2.58, Claude Sonnet 4.6=1.74, Mistral Large=1.63. Each model's bootstrap standard deviation is only 0.03–0.10, yielding tens of standard deviations of separation given a few thousand output tokens.
  • Convergence survives aggressive perturbations. Llama 3.1 8B served with 4-bit quantization (Q4_K_M) still fits Mandelbrot with R² > 0.94. All 6 models are post-RLHF/post-instruction-tuned; alignment does not break the law. Qwen 2.5 7B (the smallest model) fits as tightly as frontier models — convergence is not an artifact of scale.
  • Scoring primitive pilot performance. On FRANK, span-level AUC = 0.585 (pooled) but entity-level tier breakdown tells the real story: out-of-article entities AUC 0.646 (p<10^−10), entity substitutions 0.562 (p<10^−10), while coreference and discourse-link errors p>0.2. On TruthfulQA, improves multiple-choice accuracy from 61.4% to 63.8%, with temporal indexical errors reaching AUC 0.940 and science/politics errors at 0.42–0.45. On HaluEval dialogue, contributes +0.017 AUC beyond length-only baseline (p<10^−69). The rank-only black-box mode matches or exceeds the log-prob variant across all FRANK slices (within ±0.01 AUC).
  • Per-domain β calibration. Domain precision β varies by ~2.3× (News=0.116, Code=0.051), quantifying how much to trust the baseline per domain. Rescoring with matched β helps where the register is far from the reference (TruthfulQA social media: +0.029), not where it is already close (FRANK news: −0.028).
  • Latency. 2.6 μs/token, 0.139 ms/passage gap-only, 10.5 ms/passage including spaCy NER. One-time setup costs ~800ms amortized across all scoring. Sampling-based detectors at k=5 require ~20,000 ms/passage — a 100,000× gap.

Critical Assessment

Novelty. Moderate-to-high. The empirical observation that LLM outputs follow Zipf/Mandelbrot distributions is not entirely novel (previously documented by Wang et al. [27], Mikhaylovskiy [36], and Zhu et al. [34] in various forms). However, the paper's unique contributions are: (1) demonstrating that frontier models from 5 vendors converge to the same Mandelbrot family with R² > 0.94 in a global fit over 3+ decades of rank (stronger than prior local/piecewise results); (2) showing that parameters remain separable within that shared family, enabling fingerprinting; (3) deriving a variational scoring primitive from the regularity; and (4) the three-tier taxonomy with per-tier AUC predictions. The combination of convergence + separability as a dual result is novel and practically significant.

Rigor. Moderate. The convergence analysis is thorough (36 fits, bootstrap CIs, three complementary fit metrics, careful discussion of the Cugini et al. theorem). The scoring primitive evaluation is honest about its limitations — the paper explicitly warns against over-interpretation and emphasizes the triage role. Weaknesses include: using only 3 benchmarks (FRANK, TruthfulQA, HaluEval are somewhat established/aging), no head-to-head comparison with recent single-pass methods (EPR, LOS-Net), shared-BPE normalization means the convergence claim is per-tokenizer rather than tokenizer-invariant, no comparison of the Mandelbrot-derived score against a simple unigram frequency baseline, and decoding policies were not fully controlled across providers.

Reproducibility. Good. Code, rank tables, prompts, and output corpora are released under open-source license. All 6 models are publicly identifiable, the reference corpus (4-billion-token Wikipedia) is standard, and fitting protocols are clearly described. The main barrier to exact reproduction is access to proprietary API models (GPT-5.1, Claude, Gemini), but the authors release fitted rank tables and regeneration scripts.

Significance. Potentially high for practitioners. A 100,000× cheaper scoring layer that works on black-box APIs fills a genuine operational gap between generation and heavyweight verification. The fingerprinting capability addresses a real governance concern (silent model substitution, wrapper arbitrage). The paper's intellectual honesty about limitations (explicit about what the primitive cannot do) increases credibility.

Red flags. The authors are company principals with a pending patent application — this is disclosed but introduces a commercial interest. The paper positions the primitive as a "first-pass triage layer" but the absolute AUC numbers (0.58 on FRANK pooled, 0.53 on TruthfulQA overall) are modest; the value proposition rests entirely on speed and the tier-structured breakdown rather than raw accuracy. The paper sometimes over-claims by proximity (e.g., positioning against the Platonic Representation Hypothesis and free-energy principle, when the actual contribution is more modest). The five-orders-of-magnitude latency comparison is an order-of-magnitude estimate not measured on identical hardware. Six models, while spanning a useful range, do not constitute an exhaustive cohort.

Strengths

  • Honest about limitations. The three-tier taxonomy is explicitly falsifiable; the paper clearly states where the method structurally cannot work (Tier 2 reasoning errors) and does not inflate aggregate AUC numbers.
  • Dual-purpose contribution. The shared Mandelbrot baseline is useful both for scoring and for fingerprinting — a rare case where one regularity supports two distinct applications.
  • Computationally practical. 2.6 μs/token on CPU, usable on closed APIs, requires no additional forward passes. The design is genuinely deployment-ready in terms of latency.
  • Strong theoretical grounding. The Mandelbrot distribution is not an arbitrary choice but follows from entropy maximization under coding cost — aligning with cross-entropy training objectives.
  • Well-controlled ablation. Shows that convergence survives RLHF, 4-bit quantization, and small model scales. Separately analyzes domain vs. vendor effects, entity-level vs. span-level signals, and rank-only vs. log-prob modes.
  • Open science. Code, data, and artifacts released. Engagement with related work is thorough and positions the finding carefully relative to Cugini et al.'s universality theorem.

Limitations

  • Convergence measured under shared-BPE normalization only. The claim that all models converge to the same Mandelbrot family is demonstrated only when outputs are re-tokenized with Llama 3.1 8B's BPE vocabulary. Whether the same holds under each vendor's native tokenizer is an open question that the authors acknowledge.
  • No unigram baseline comparison. The Mandelbrot parameterization is motivated by model selection against Zipf, but whether it materially improves downstream scoring over a simple unigram-frequency reference from the same Wikipedia corpus is not tested.
  • Moderate absolute AUC on benchmarks. Pooled FRANK AUC of 0.585, TruthfulQA overall AUC of 0.513 — these are useful only as a triage signal, not as a standalone detector. The paper relies heavily on per-tier breakdowns to demonstrate value.
  • Three aging benchmarks. FRANK (2021), TruthfulQA (2022), HaluEval (2023) — validation on more recent benchmarks (SimpleQA, RAGTruth, HaluLens) is needed. The HaluEval QA split is dominated by a length artifact.
  • No hardware-matched latency comparison. The 100,000× latency gap is estimated from published configurations, not measured on the same hardware against sampling-based baselines.
  • β calibration along single axis only (domain). Task type (QA vs. summarization vs. dialogue) plausibly matters but is not modeled jointly with domain.
  • Limited model cohort. Six models, while spanning useful dimensions, do not cover pre-trained-only base models, sub-1B-parameter models, older architectures, or multilingual settings.
  • Potential commercial interest. Pending patent application by authors' company introduces incentive for positive framing, though the paper's intellectual honesty partially mitigates this concern.

Relevance

与用户研究兴趣的关联： 本文直接涉及大模型幻觉检测与评估这一核心领域，提出了一个极其廉价的黑盒评分基元，特别适用于 Agent 和 RAG 系统的生产环境部署。如果你的工作关注 LLM 输出的自动化质量评估、模型溯源验证、或低成本幻觉检测分层架构，这篇文章的方法论和三层错误分类体系具有直接的工程参考价值。但其绝对 AUC 偏低意味着它更适合作为复合评估流水线中的首轮筛选组件，而非独立检测器。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Tokenizer invariance: If each model's outputs are tokenized with its own native vocabulary, do the fitted (q, s) values remain separable, or does the fingerprinting signal rely on the shared-BPE normalization step?
  2. Mandelbrot vs. unigram baseline: Does the richer Mandelbrot parameterization provide a meaningful improvement in downstream scoring AUC over a simple unigram-frequency baseline computed from the same Wikipedia reference corpus?
  3. Adversarial robustness: Can model outputs be adversarially perturbed (e.g., via paraphrasing attacks) to match a different model's Mandelbrot fingerprint while preserving semantic content, thereby defeating the provenance verification?
  4. Temporal stability: Do a model's Mandelbrot parameters drift over time with version updates, RLHF iterations, or system prompt changes? How stable is the fingerprint under normal operational variation?
  5. Multilingual extension: Does the Mandelbrot convergence hold for non-English languages, particularly those with different morphological structures (e.g., agglutinative languages, logographic scripts)?
  6. Cascade routing efficiency: What is the optimal routing fraction for a cascade that uses the RI primitive as a gate before a sampling-based verifier, and how does this trade-off vary across domains and error types?
  7. Base model vs. instruct model divergence: Do base (pre-trained-only) models exhibit fundamentally different Mandelbrot parameters from their instruction-tuned counterparts, and could this serve as an alignment audit signal?
