NVLLM: A 3D NAND-Centric Architecture Enabling Edge on-Device LLM Inference

Mingbo Hao, Changwei Yan, Haoyu Cui, Zhihao Yan, Yizhi Ding, Zhangrui Qian, Weiwei Shan

arXiv: 2604.25699v1

Date: 2026-04-28

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25699

一句话总结： NVLLM提出了一种以3D NAND闪存为核心的边缘LLM推理架构，通过晶圆级3D集成将FFN计算卸载到Flash内部执行，利用解耦的乱序容错点积引擎直接在原始NAND读取数据上进行计算，在OPT和LLaMA模型上实现了相对GPU最高37.9倍的吞吐提升和仅2.7%的CMOS面积开销。
TL;DR: NVLLM is a 3D NAND-centric architecture that offloads feed-forward network (FFN) computation directly into Flash via wafer-to-wafer 3D stacking, using an error-resilient out-of-order dot-product engine operating on raw NAND reads. It achieves up to 37.9x throughput gain over GPU-based out-of-core inference and up to 4.7x over SSD-like in-Flash designs on quantized OPT/LLaMA models, with only 2.7% CMOS area overhead.

核心贡献

  • 提出了NVLLM架构： 一种以3D NAND闪存为中心的边缘侧LLM推理系统，通过晶圆级(W2W)3D集成将计算和存储紧密耦合，FFN层权重驻留在Flash中就地计算，注意力权重保留在DRAM中，大幅减少片外数据搬运。
  • 设计了容错乱序点积引擎(ERDPE/OoO-ECDP)： 将GEMM/GEMV操作分解为段级点积原语，解耦错误检测与纠错，使得PE通道可以在原始NAND读取数据上以乱序方式持续执行MAC操作，即使存在非零原始误码率(RBER)也能维持全周期吞吐。
  • 揭示了注意力与FFN的弱耦合特性： 利用推理过程中attention和FFN计算之间的弱相互依赖性，提出解耦执行策略；并设计了KV-cache感知的动态调度器，在长上下文场景下自适应地将Q/K/V/O投影计算卸载到in-Flash流水线，维持稳定的单token吞吐。
  • 提出了聚类平面-通道协同设计： 将多个NAND Plane分组为Cluster，使Cluster的数据产出宽度与PE Lane的数据消耗宽度精确对齐，消除了宽多路选择器和跨通道数据重排，实现从Cluster FIFO到Lane缓冲区的直接流式传输。

Key Contributions

  • NVLLM Architecture: A 3D NAND-centric edge LLM inference system that co-designs compute and storage through wafer-to-wafer (W2W) 3D integration. FFN weights reside in Flash and are computed in-situ; attention weights remain in DRAM. This dramatically reduces off-chip data movement.
  • Error-Resilient Dot-Product Engine (ERDPE/OoO-ECDP): Decomposes GEMM/GEMV into segment-level dot-product primitives with decoupled error detection and correction. PE lanes execute out-of-order directly on raw NAND reads, sustaining full-cycle MAC throughput even under non-zero raw bit error rate (RBER).
  • Decoupled Attention-FFN Execution: Exploits the weak interdependence between attention and FFN during single-batch inference. A KV-cache-aware dynamic scheduler adaptively offloads Q/K/V/O projections to the in-Flash pipeline as context length grows, maintaining stable per-token throughput.
  • Clustered Plane-Lane Co-Design: Groups multiple NAND planes into clusters whose aggregate read bandwidth is precisely aligned to the data consumption width of PE lanes, eliminating wide multiplexers and cross-lane reshaping for direct cluster-to-lane streaming.

Methodology

The authors evaluate NVLLM using a cycle-accurate C++ simulator that integrates behavioral models for both the 3D NAND Flash array (modeled via the 3D-FPIM framework, capturing cell/wordline geometries, parasitics, and timing validated against commercial products) and LPDDR5X DRAM (modeled via Ramulator2 and DRAMPower). The NPU and NAND CMOS components are implemented in Verilog HDL and synthesized with Synopsys Design Compiler on TSMC 28nm technology for area and power estimation.

Benchmarks cover the OPT series (1.3B, 2.7B, 6.7B, 13B, 30B parameters) and LLaMA-family models, all quantized to INT8. Random bit flips are injected into model weights to emulate realistic NAND RBER patterns. Baselines include GPU-centric configurations (GPU-DRAM, GPU-SSD using NVIDIA A800 via FlexGen) and SSD-like in-Flash designs (Cambricon-LLM, AiF, AiF--). Three NVLLM scaling variants are evaluated (8/12/16 OoO-ECDP units with 32/48/64 planes). Metrics cover tokens-per-second (TPS) throughput, end-to-end latency (s/inf), and energy per token (J/token).

Results & Highlights

  • Throughput: NVLLM achieves 22.4x-37.9x speedup over GPU-SSD and >2.5x over GPU-DRAM. Against SSD-like designs, NVLLM-16C outperforms Cambricon-LLM by 4.7x, AiF by 1.3x, and AiF-- by 1.7x on LLaMA2-7B. Smaller models benefit more due to bandwidth-dominated workloads.
  • Latency: NVLLM-16C achieves 1.9s/7.5s/30.3s/124.3s per inference for 32/128/512/2048 tokens, up to 28.2x faster than GPU-SSD and 2.7x faster than GPU-DRAM. Prefill accounts for 44-45% of total latency in NVLLM (vs. <7% in GPU baselines), reflecting its more balanced compute-bandwidth design.
  • Energy: NVLLM achieves a 5.63x reduction in data movement energy compared to Cambricon-LLM. Energy savings grow with model size as FFN-heavy workloads benefit most from in-situ computation.
  • Area: The additional in-Flash logic occupies 2.69 mm^2, accounting for only 2.7% of total NAND CMOS area. The ECC module achieves 2.0x area and 1.3x power reduction over a monolithic ECC design through decoupled detection/correction.
  • KV-cache scheduling: Ablation confirms the KV-cache-aware scheduler provides measurable latency benefits in extended decoding scenarios.
  • Scalability: Performance scales with the number of plane clusters and OoO-ECDP units (NVLLM -> NVLLM-12C -> NVLLM-16C), demonstrating architectural scalability.

The first author Mingbo Hao is a Ph.D. student working on 3D NAND-based LLM inference acceleration. The paper is submitted to arXiv under cs.AR (Hardware Architecture). The authors explicitly note this is "an initial system instantiation" presented in "research prototype format," acknowledging it as early-stage work to be "further developed and formalized" in future publications. The evaluation methodology uses established simulation frameworks (3D-FPIM, Ramulator2, DRAMPower) and standard EDA tools (Synopsys Design Compiler), lending reasonable credibility to the claims within the simulation domain. However, no silicon validation or FPGA prototyping is reported.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. Individual components (near-data processing in NAND, decoupled attention/FFN, error-correcting codes for Flash) have prior art. The specific integration—combining W2W 3D-stacked NAND with an out-of-order error-resilient dot-product engine and KV-cache-aware scheduling—is a novel system-level contribution. However, the paper closely follows the template set by 3D-FPIM [15] and AiF [16], extending them specifically for LLM workloads.
  • Rigor: Adequate for an architectural proposal but with notable gaps. Cycle-accurate simulation and Verilog synthesis provide reasonable fidelity, but the evaluation is entirely simulation-based with no hardware implementation. The latency comparison excludes SSD-like baselines (Cambricon-LLM, AiF) with the stated reason of "missing prefill data," which weakens the completeness of the evaluation. The energy comparison is shown only against Cambricon-LLM, not against GPU baselines or AiF. Only INT8 quantization is evaluated despite the hardware supporting BF16. The paper does not discuss sensitivity to NAND RBER variation, process variation, or temperature effects—all well-known challenges for in-Flash computation.
  • Reproducibility: Limited. Relies on proprietary tools (Synopsys Design Compiler) and an in-house cycle-accurate simulator. The 3D-FPIM framework is from prior academic work and may not be publicly available in full. W2W bonding parameters and NAND technology assumptions are tied to commercial teardown analyses that may not be independently verifiable. No open-source code or simulation framework is mentioned.
  • Significance: Relevant to the growing edge AI deployment domain. The memory-wall problem for single-batch LLM inference is well-recognized, and 3D NAND-based near-data processing is a credible direction. However, the practical path to deployment faces substantial hurdles: W2W 3D integration is a high-cost advanced packaging technology, typically reserved for high-volume products; custom NAND with embedded compute logic requires significant foundry investment; and the economic viability for edge devices (as opposed to datacenter) is not addressed.
  • Red Flags: (1) Self-described as "initial system instantiation" and "research prototype"—this is early-stage conceptual work. (2) The paper states "Minor inconsistencies in earlier drafts have been corrected in this version; these corrections do not affect the experimental results, comparisons, or conclusions"—this unusual note warrants scrutiny of earlier versions. (3) The area overhead claim of 2.7% accounts only for the added compute logic relative to NAND CMOS, not the full system cost including the NPU, W2W bonding overhead, and packaging. (4) Write endurance and program/erase latency constraints of NAND for weight updates are acknowledged but not quantitatively addressed. (5) No comparison against other emerging edge AI accelerators (e.g., Apple Neural Engine, Qualcomm Hexagon, or recent PIM-based designs beyond NAND).

Strengths

  • Addresses a genuine and well-motivated problem: the memory bandwidth bottleneck in single-batch, long-context edge LLM inference, supported by roofline analysis and real conversation trace statistics (6.1M turns from ShareGPT, UltraChat, OASST1).
  • The error-resilient out-of-order dot-product engine is an elegant hardware-software co-design that turns a liability (raw NAND bit errors) into a manageable pipeline feature through decoupled detection and correction.
  • The clustered plane-lane co-design principle—matching storage read granularity to compute consumption width—is clean and well-justified, avoiding the buffering/routing overhead that would otherwise make in-NAND compute impractical.
  • Comprehensive baseline comparison covering both GPU-centric (DRAM, SSD) and storage-centric (Cambricon-LLM, AiF) approaches.
  • The KV-cache-aware scheduler addresses a real scaling concern for long-context inference that many accelerator papers overlook.
  • Transparent about the early-stage nature of the work, setting appropriate expectations.

Limitations

  • Entirely simulation-based; no FPGA prototype or silicon validation. The gap between simulated 3D NAND behavior and real manufactured W2W-integrated NAND+CMOS is substantial.
  • W2W hybrid bonding is an advanced, high-cost packaging technology. The paper does not address yield, thermal, or cost implications for edge deployment scenarios.
  • Energy evaluation is incomplete: compared against only one baseline (Cambricon-LLM) and does not include GPU energy data. System-level energy including the NPU, DRAM, and host CPU is not fully disaggregated.
  • Latency comparison excludes SSD-like baselines, undermining the completeness of the latency analysis.
  • Only INT8 quantization evaluated; BF16 performance, inference accuracy under quantization+bit-error combined effects, and perplexity degradation are not quantified in detail.
  • NAND write endurance (limited program/erase cycles) is mentioned as a challenge but no quantitative analysis or mitigation strategy (beyond read-only weight storage) is provided for scenarios requiring weight updates or fine-tuning.
  • The architecture assumes weights are statically stored in NAND; no support for dynamic weight loading, model switching, or multi-tenant scenarios.
  • No comparison with recent edge-oriented software optimizations (e.g., speculative decoding, early exit, activation-aware quantization) to show that the hardware gains are complementary and not subsumed by algorithmic advances.

Relevance

该工作直接针对边缘设备上大语言模型推理的内存带宽瓶颈问题，这与当前端侧AI部署（如手机、IoT设备上的LLM应用）的研究热点高度契合。其提出的3D NAND存内计算方法为解决“模型参数远大于设备内存”这一核心矛盾提供了硬件层面的思路，对从事AI加速器架构、存算一体、边缘AI系统设计的研究者具有参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How would NVLLM handle model fine-tuning or continuous learning scenarios on edge devices, given NAND's limited program/erase endurance (typically 10^3-10^5 cycles for MLC/TLC)?
  2. What is the actual silicon-proven raw bit error rate distribution for W2W-integrated 3D NAND, and how sensitive is the OoO-ECDP throughput to RBER variation, temperature, and retention effects?
  3. Can the proposed architecture support mixture-of-experts (MoE) models where FFN expert weights are far larger and more irregularly accessed than dense FFNs?
  4. How do the W2W bonding yield, thermal density, and packaging cost scale with increasing numbers of plane clusters, and what is the break-even point for commercial viability vs. simply using more DRAM?
  5. What is the end-to-end inference accuracy (perplexity/WER) when combining INT8 quantization with realistic NAND RBER, and how does this degrade over the Flash lifetime as RBER increases with program/erase cycling?
