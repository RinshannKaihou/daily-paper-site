EN: Overparameterization is central to deep learning but its mechanisms for improving optimization remain incompletely understood. From a weight-space symmetry perspective, this paper proves two distinct benefits: (1) symmetries perform diagonal preconditioning on the Hessian, enabling better-conditioned minima within each equivalence class of functionally identical solutions; (2) overparameterization increases the probability mass of global minima near typical initializations, making favorable solutions more reachable. Teacher-student experiments validate these predictions: with increased width, Hessian trace decreases, condition numbers improve, and convergence accelerates.
