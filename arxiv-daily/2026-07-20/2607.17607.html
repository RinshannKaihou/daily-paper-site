Optimizing the Preconditioner: A Black-box Online-to-Nonconvex Conversion with Static Regret Minimization Oracles

Haichen Hu & David Simchi-Levi — MIT (CCSE & Civil & Environmental Engineering / IDSS)

  • OCO → nonconvex
  • static regret
  • preconditioning
  • nonconvex optimization
  • Goldstein stationarity
  • online learning

01 Key metric

O(T−2/7)

Optimal (λ,ε)-Goldstein stationarity rate for Lipschitz nonsmooth objectives — achieved with ordinary static regret only, matching prior work that needed stronger shifting / discounted / dynamic regret.

Smooth: O(1/√T) Oracle req.: RT=O(√T)

02 Why this matters

Any O(√T) static-regret OCO algorithm can now serve as a preconditioner module for nonconvex training — unifying the theory behind Adam, Shampoo, Muon.

03 Critical edge

No experiments. Adam, Muon, Lion not yet shown to fit the interface — the “modern optimizers” framing is agenda-setting, not demonstrated.

§ 01

Methodology

Pure theory. The trick: the OCO oracle does not choose the descent direction — it chooses a preconditioner applied to a “predictable gradient tracker” mt. This cleanly separates gradient prediction (nonconvex side) from preconditioner geometry (online side).

  1. Setup. β-smooth nonconvex f, range ≤ M, unbiased stochastic gradient with variance ≤ σ². Metric: average squared gradient norm.
  2. Tracker. Maintain mt = projected EMA of past stochastic gradients, with projection radius G0=√(2Mβ) derived from smoothness — not an extra assumption.
  3. Oracle picks preconditioner. Round t, ask black-box OCO oracle for Ut ∈ compact convex set containing Id, ‖U‖op≤1. Loss Lt(U) = −(1/G)⟨st, U mt⟩. Comparator is the fixed matrix Id — undiscounted, ordinary static regret.
  4. Update. xt+1 = xt − η Ut mt, η = a/(64β).

Nonsmooth extension (Alg. 2): gradient-descent lemma unavailable, so the algorithm commits to a displacement, then samples a random point yt = xt + rtδt along the segment and exploits Newton-Leibniz / absolute continuity to recover the optimal O(T−2/7).

§ 02

Results & rates

Thm 6.1 — Smooth

O(1/√T) avg. ‖∇f‖²

E[(1/T)Σ‖∇f(xt)‖²] ≤ (142 σ√(Mβ))/√T + (1.38 √(Mβ)·RT)/T + 6.1 Mβ/T

Identity oracle → zero regret → recovers the Ghadimi-Lan (2013) SGD bound exactly. Any learner that beats Id carries that gain into a sharper nonconvex rate.

Thm 7.3 — Lipschitz nonsmooth

O(T−2/7) Goldstein

E[‖∇f(ȳ)[λ]‖] ≤ 11 εT + (L3/2)/(M√λ)·εT + 2RT/T,  εT=(B/(TM²λ))1/7

Matches the best prior rates (Cutkosky ’23; Zhang & Cutkosky ’24) — but those required stronger regret machinery.

Instantiations — Sec 8

Diagonal AdaGrad: RT ≤ 2 Σi√(Σtst,i²) — coordinatewise adaptivity preserved. Projected Shampoo: RT ≤ √(2d)·(Tr HT1/4)² — spectral / low-rank adaptivity via rank(Qt)≤2.

§ 03

Strengths

  • Cleanly resolves a stated open problem with a single, simple design principle — preconditioner-slot OCO oracle.
  • Unified treatment of smooth and nonsmooth stochastic nonconvex, optimal in both (O(T−1/2) & O(T−2/7)) under only an O(√T) static-regret oracle.
  • The modular principle — “any static-regret OCO algorithm is a valid preconditioner module” — is a genuine bridge between OCO and deep-optimizer theory.
  • Honest constants in main theorems; AdaGrad & Shampoo instantiated with their data-dependent regret terms preserved.
§ 04

Limitations

  • No experiments. Purely theoretical; consequences for LLM / deep training are conjectural.
  • Adam / Muon / Lion / Pion / Leon not yet covered. Whether they fit the interface is open.
  • Bounded-range assumption is load-bearing — stronger than the lower-bound used in many nonconvex SGD analyses; unbounded-below objectives (some matrix-factorization losses) excluded.
  • Iteration constants (64, 142, 1.38, 6.1, 11) are unoptimized proof margins; no comparison to hand-tuned clipped Adam.
  • No treatment of constrained nonconvex or finite-sum / variance-reduction settings. No lower bound showing static regret is necessary.
§ 05

Open questions for further work

  • Can Adam, Muon, Lion, Pion, Leon be reformulated to admit the preconditioner-space static-regret interface — and what rates then follow? (paper’s stated future work)
  • Does the framework extend to finite-sum objectives and variance-reduced methods (SAGA, SPIDER, Natasha), inheriting their faster rates?
  • Can bounded-range be relaxed to bounded-below (standard in nonconvex SGD) or a local progress budget — without losing the static-regret-only interface?
  • Can an outer Nesterov / momentum loop push past O(T−1/2) in the smooth stochastic setting?
  • Is there a matching lower bound showing ordinary static regret is not just sufficient but necessary for a black-box online-to-nonconvex reduction?
  • Can the nonsmooth result be tightened from (λ,ε)-distributional to the (ε,δ)-Goldstein notion of Davis ’22 / Jordan ’23?
