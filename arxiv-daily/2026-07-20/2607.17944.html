CMP forgets 15–19× less than online-EWC Transformer — without backpropagation.

■ WHY THIS MATTERS
Reframes catastrophic forgetting as a structural consequence of backprop’s credit assignment — a falsifiable, gradient-free alternative worth testing on standardized benchmarks.
⚠ CRITICAL EDGE
The 15–19× gap is measured only against one online-EWC variant, on a self-assembled, unreleased 15-domain corpus — with Split-MNIST a null result and order sweep just 3-of-120.
01

Methodology

7-stage byte-level pipeline · zero backpropagation
1
Sparse relational binding §3.1
Frozen random EL, ER map consecutive bytes to role vectors → elementwise bind → k-WTA sparsify → fixed-width code zt (r=1024). Embeddings not trained.
2
Two-tier competitive memory §3.2
Fast buffer B + slow register R; content-addressed writes gated by θmatch = 0.86·E[max]. Codes promote on genuine matches only — the structural feature blamed for forgetting resistance.
3
Hierarchy §3.3
Consecutive zt-1, zt re-bound through frozen mixing matrices into coarser, word-scale ht.
4
Predictive coding §3.4
Matrix F lets ht predict zt; residual sparsified into surprise et. F is self-supervised, receives no task-loss gradient.
5
Linear readout §3.5
zt, retrieved memory, ht, et, leaky context terms summed linearly with bigram table Wh → 256-way logits. Linearity is deliberate (no credit-assign through MLP).
6
Local delta rule §3.6
ΔW = η·Σ errtTφ(t). One hop from error, no chain rule, no upstream backprop.
7
Weight-protect §3.7only claimed novelty
After domain i: I ← I + E[|W(i) − W(i-1)|], then dampen ΔW ← ΔW / (1 + λI). Substitutes weight-movement magnitude for EWC’s Fisher information — necessary precisely because, with no backprop, there is nothing to square into a Fisher score. The entire forgetting-resistance claim rests here.

Protocol: domain-incremental, 15 self-assembled text domains, no replay, no task ID. Baselines = parameter-matched byte-level Transformer under (a) naive fine-tune, (b) online EWC (Progress & Compress running-Fisher variant — not vanilla per-task EWC). Every run must beat a bigram-floor accuracy audit before any forgetting metric is reported.

CMP trails matched Transformer. Authors concede: not more accurate.
  • Domain-order control (Table 4, 5-domain, 3 of 120 orderings): BWT +0.2353 canonical / +0.4442 reverse / +0.3343 shuffled — reported as a range +0.24–0.44, never a single favourable number; worst case still beats EWC by ~5×.
  • Accuracy audit: every CMP run across all 15 domains & 3 seeds beats the bigram floor by +0.41–1.22 BPB — confirms a real signal to forget.
  • Depth + hierarchy merge (App. D): 3.48 vs 3.27 BPB — diagnosed negative; dense block outputs break memory slot-matching + signal dilution across ≥7 readout terms.
  • Scale probe (App. E): 6.4M (+;0.1482) → 29.8M (+0.1397) params at matched budget — real but modest; labelled preliminary, not a scaling law.
  • Disclosed baseline quirk: Transformer’s original 150K-budget run produced a misleading +5.93 naive BWT — failed the accuracy audit and was openly discarded; the matched comparison exists only at the budget the Transformer needed.
03

Strengths & Limitations

honest disclosure — three negative results in the open

■ STRENGTHS

  • Exceptional intellectual honesty: accuracy gap, Split-MNIST null, and depth+merge failure all disclosed with diagnosed causes.
  • Weight-protect is principled — Fisher genuinely cannot be computed without a gradient, so a movement proxy is motivated, not ad-hoc.
  • Accuracy-audit gate (beat the bigram floor before any forgetting number is reported) is a methodological best practice many CL papers skip.
  • BWT/FWT-on-BPB with an explicit sign convention — the authors flag the inversion error they made mid-project.
  • Structural argument (forgetting as a credit-assignment consequence of backprop) is interesting and falsifiable regardless of CMP’s competitiveness.

■ LIMITATIONS

  • Custom, non-standard 15-domain corpus — not packaged, not independently validated.
  • Thin baseline set: only naive + online-EWC Transformer. No SI, iCaRL, GEM, or per-task EWC.
  • Order sweep is 3 of 120 orderings on a 5-domain subset, not the full 15-domain protocol.
  • Code is not public (request-only); corpus not released — reproducibility limited.
  • Substantial accuracy gap (3.1–3.27 BPB) — contribution is narrow.
  • Null result on the only recognized benchmark (Split-MNIST); claim is currently text-only.
  • Frozen random embeddings; hyperparameters derived from r by fixed ratio, not independently tuned; FWT noisy (std > 40% of mean).
04

Open Questions

what would have to be true for the headline to hold up
  1. Does the 15–19× advantage survive against per-task EWC, Synaptic Intelligence, iCaRL (replay), and GEM on the same 15-domain protocol — not just online-EWC?
  2. Does it replicate on a recognized CL benchmark (standard text-CL suite, Long Sequence Arena, sequential GLUE) rather than a self-assembled corpus?
  3. Is weight-protect’s movement signal theoretically related to SI’s trajectory-based importance, and can the two be unified on equal footing?
  4. What does the full 5!=120 ordering sweep (or a larger random sample) show — does the worst case stay below EWC?
  5. Would training the frozen embeddings EL, ER close the accuracy gap without re-introducing forgetting?
  6. Can the depth-line (App. C, 2.49–2.51 BPB) reconcile with sparse memory by adding k-WTA to the depth blocks, as the authors diagnose?
  7. Does the BWT advantage scale beyond ~30M params, or erode as capacity grows? A scaling-law derivation for BWT(r, budget, domains) is explicitly left open.
