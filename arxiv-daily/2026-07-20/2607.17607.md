> **一句话总结：** 本文证明离线随机非凸优化可以被黑盒地归约到 OCO 中最朴素的固定比较器静态遗憾最小化：通过让 OCO 学习器选「预条件子」而非直接选更新方向，对于 β-光滑目标得到 O(T^{-1/2}) 收敛率，对于 Lipschitz 非光滑目标得到最优 O(T^{-2/7}) Goldstein 站点收敛率，从而正面解决了 Chen & Hazan (2024) 的开放问题。

> **TL;DR:** Hu and Simchi-Levi give the first black-box reduction from stochastic nonconvex optimization to *ordinary* (undiscounted, fixed-comparator) static-regret minimization in OCO, resolving the open problem of Chen and Hazan (2024). The trick is to make the OCO oracle choose a preconditioner for a "predictable gradient tracker" rather than the descent direction itself. For β-smooth objectives with range M and stochastic-gradient variance σ², plugging any OCO oracle with R_T = O(√T) (e.g. OGD, diagonal AdaGrad, projected Shampoo) into the framework recovers the classical E[(1/T)Σ‖∇f(x_t)‖²] = O(1/√T) rate versus the SGD/identity baseline; for Lipschitz non-smooth objectives it achieves the optimal O(T^{-2/7}) rate for the (λ,ε)-Goldstein stationarity notion previously obtained only with stronger shifting/discounted/dynamic regret.

## 核心贡献

- 首次给出从**随机非凸优化**到**OCO 静态遗憾最小化**的黑盒归约，正面解决 Chen & Hazan (2024) 提出的开放问题；归约只需最朴素的固定比较器静态遗憾，不需要 shifting / adaptive / discounted / dynamic regret 等更强的遗憾概念。
- 提出一种全新的「预条件子优化」视角：OCO 学习器不直接决定下降方向，而是从紧凸矩阵集 U（含 Id 且 op 范数 ≤1）中选择一个预条件子 U_t，作用于历史随机梯度构成的「可预测梯度跟踪器」m_t。
- 在**光滑**设定下证明：任何 R_T(A, Id)=O(√T) 的 OCO 黑盒均给出经典 O(T^{-1/2}) 随机非凸收敛率，且遗憾项可负——因此可被解释为相对「恒等预条件 SGD 基线」的在线自适应收益。
- 在**Lipschitz 非光滑**设定下（梯度只需有界、函数绝对连续、不需 Lipschitz 连续梯度）给出扩展算法，借助随机线段采样替代下降引理，仍只用静态遗憾，达到最优 O(T^{-2/7}) 的 (λ,ε)-Goldstein 站点收敛率。
- 给出两个具体实例化（对角 AdaGrad、投影 Shampoo）作为预条件子选择神谕，证明其各自的数据相关遗憾界直接翻译为数据相关的非凸收敛保证，为 Adam/Muon/Lion 等现代优化器提供「梯度预测 + 在线预条件」的统一理解框架。

## Key Contributions

- First black-box reduction from offline stochastic nonconvex optimization to *ordinary undiscounted static-regret* minimization in OCO, resolving the open problem of Chen and Hazan (2024). All prior online-to-nonconvex conversions (Cutkosky et al. 2023; Ahn et al. 2025; Ahn & Cutkosky 2024; Zhang & Cutkosky 2024; Patitucci et al. 2025; Jiang et al. 2026) required shifting, discounted, dynamic, or adaptive regret — this paper needs only the weakest standard notion.
- A conceptual reframing of the OCO oracle's role: instead of letting it choose the descent direction, the oracle selects a preconditioner U_t applied to a "predictable gradient tracker" m_t (a projected EMA of past stochastic gradients). This cleanly separates "gradient prediction" (nonconvex side) from "preconditioner geometry" (online-learning side).
- For smooth objectives: any black-box OCO algorithm with R_T(A, Id) = O(√T) yields the classical O(T^{-1/2}) stochastic nonconvex rate. The identity-preconditioner oracle gives zero regret and recovers exactly the Ghadimi-Lan (2013) SGD bound; any adaptive learner that beats identity translates that improvement into a sharper nonconvex bound.
- For Lipschitz non-smooth objectives (no Lipschitz-continuous gradient required, only bounded gradient L, bounded range M, absolute continuity / Newton-Leibniz): the same static-regret framework achieves the optimal O(T^{-2/7}) rate for the (λ,ε)-distributional Goldstein stationarity notion, matching the prior state of the art that required stronger regret machinery.
- Two concrete instantiations — diagonal AdaGrad and projected Shampoo — are cast as static-regret oracles over the preconditioner class. Their pathwise data-dependent regret bounds (e.g. Σ_i √(Σ_t q_{t,i}²) for AdaGrad, √(2d)·Tr(H_T^{1/4}) for Shampoo) become data-dependent nonconvex convergence rates, recovering the coordinatewise / spectral adaptivity of these optimizers inside the new framework.

## Methodology

The paper is purely theoretical. The core construction (Algorithm 1) is the smooth reduction:

1. **Setup.** Minimize a β-smooth nonconvex f with range bounded by M, accessed via an unbiased stochastic gradient oracle O(x) = ∇f(x) + ξ with conditional variance ≤ σ². Performance metric is the average squared gradient norm (1/T)Σ_t ‖∇f(x_t)‖².
2. **Predictable gradient tracker.** Maintain m_t = Π_{‖v‖≤G_0}((1-a)·m_t + a·s_t) where G_0 = √(2Mβ) (a consequence of smoothness + range bound, not an extra assumption), s_t is a fresh stochastic sample, and a = G/B with B = max{G, σ√T} the global variance budget, G = √(Mβ).
3. **OCO oracle chooses preconditioner.** At round t, ask the black-box OCO oracle A for a matrix U_t ∈ U (compact, convex, contains Id, ‖U‖_op ≤ 1). The oracle is fed the linear loss L_t(U) = -(1/G)⟨s_t, U m_t⟩, which is unbiased for the true first-order descent term -(1/G)⟨∇f(x_t), U m_t⟩. Crucially the comparator is the fixed matrix Id, and losses are undiscounted — i.e. ordinary static regret.
4. **Update.** x_{t+1} = x_t - η U_t m_t with η = a/(64β). The constant 64 is purely a proof margin.

The proof of Theorem 6.1 has three coupled ingredients: (i) smoothness + bounded range convert cumulative OCO loss into a lower bound on (1/T)Σ‖∇f(x_t)‖²; (ii) the static-regret oracle with comparator Id bounds the played loss above by -S + √(S·E_tr) + G·R_T where S = Σ E‖∇f(x_t)‖² and E_tr is the tracker error; (iii) an exponential-tracker lemma bounds E_tr ≤ G²/a + a·V_T + c·S with c a small universal constant, where V_T is the cumulative stochastic variance. Combining and tuning B = max{G, σ√T} gives the final three-term bound.

For the **non-smooth extension** (Algorithm 2, Theorem 7.3), the gradient-descent lemma is unavailable, so the algorithm commits to a displacement δ_t = -η U_t z_t (with z_t a smoothly normalized tracker direction), then queries the stochastic oracle at a uniformly random point y_t = x_t + r_t δ_t along the segment, exploiting E_{r_t}⟨∇f(x_t + r_t δ_t), δ_t⟩ = f(x_{t+1}) - f(x_t) (Newton-Leibniz / absolute continuity). The output is a weighted random iterate ȳ_τ selected with weights c_t/T. Tuning a = B/T^{2/7}, η = B²/(T^{1/2}λ), ρ = ε_T with ε_T = (B/(T M² λ))^{1/7} yields the optimal O(T^{-2/7}) rate.

## Results & Highlights

The paper reports no empirical experiments — all results are theoretical convergence guarantees. Key quantitative claims copied from the paper:

- **Smooth main theorem (Thm 6.1).** For β-smooth f with range M and stochastic-gradient variance σ², Algorithm 1 satisfies
  (1/T)Σ_t E[‖∇f(x_t)‖²] ≤ (142 σ√(Mβ))/√T + (1.38 √(Mβ)·R_T(A, Id))/T + 6.1 Mβ/T.
  Hence the classical **O(1/√T)** stochastic nonconvex rate of Ghadimi-Lan (2013) is recovered whenever the black-box OCO oracle achieves **R_T(A, Id) = O(√T)**. Setting the oracle to identity gives zero regret and reduces the bound exactly to the SGD baseline σ√(Mβ)/√T + Mβ/T.
- **Nonsmooth main theorem (Thm 7.3).** Under bounded gradient L, bounded range M, and absolute continuity, Algorithm 2 with tuned a, η, ρ achieves
  E[‖∇f(ȳ_τ)‖_{[λ]}] ≤ 11 ε_T + (L^{3/2})/(M√λ) · ε_T + (2 R_T(A, Id))/T, with ε_T = (B/(T M² λ))^{1/7}.
  Therefore, with an O(√T) static-regret oracle the conversion attains the optimal **O(T^{-2/7})** rate for the (λ,ε)-Goldstein stationarity notion — matching the best prior rates that previously required shifting/discounted/dynamic regret (Cutkosky et al. 2023; Zhang & Cutkosky 2024).
- **AdaGrad instantiation (Sec 8.1).** Diagonal AdaGrad as the preconditioner oracle has static regret R_T(A_Ada, Id) ≤ 2 E[Σ_i √(Σ_t s_{t,i}²)] ≤ 2√(dT(2Mβ + σ²)), preserving the O(1/√T) smooth nonconvex rate while retaining the coordinatewise data-dependent term that can be smaller than Euclidean OGD's bound by a factor of order d when gradient energy is concentrated in a single coordinate.
- **Shampoo instantiation (Sec 8.2).** Projected online Shampoo as the preconditioner oracle gives R_T(A_Shampoo, Id) ≤ √(2d)·E[(Tr(H_T^{1/4}))²] ≤ 2d² √(dε + 2T(2Mβ + σ²)) = O(√T) for fixed d, ε, also yielding O(1/√T) smooth nonconvex convergence while preserving spectral / low-rank adaptivity via rank(Q_t) ≤ 2.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (genuine).** The paper's positive resolution of the Chen-Hazan (2024) open problem is a clean conceptual contribution. The actual mechanism — putting the OCO oracle in the *preconditioner* slot rather than letting it choose the iterate — is a small but non-obvious reframing that distinguishes this work from Cutkosky et al. (2023), Ahn et al. (2025), and Jiang et al. (2026) (the latter derives Pion/Leon via discounted-regret + adaptive matrix online learning, explicitly a stronger interface).
- **Rigor.** Proofs are sketched in the main text (Sec 6, Sec 7) with full arguments deferred to Appendices B and C. The three-step proof of Theorem 6.1 (smoothness → lower bound on OCO loss; static regret → upper bound; tracker lemma) is internally consistent and the constants (64, 142, 1.38, 6.1) are reported explicitly rather than hidden. Theorem 7.3 cites the matching lower bound literature (Zhang & Cutkosky 2024) for optimality of T^{-2/7}.
- **What is NOT shown.** The paper does **not** provide any empirical validation — no deep-learning training runs, no comparison to Adam/Muon/Shampoo/Lion on real benchmarks. The connection to "modern optimizers" (Adam, Muon, Lion, Pion, Leon) is explicitly framed as future work ("Determining whether additional practical optimizers admit the required preconditioner-space regret interface is an interesting direction for future work"). A reader looking for evidence that this framework improves practical optimizer design will find none.
- **Hidden assumptions / data quirk.** The reduction crucially relies on the **bounded-range** assumption (f(x) - f(y) ≤ M globally), which serves as the "progress budget" turning cumulative descent into a stationarity guarantee. This is standard but is a stronger condition than the usual lower-bound f ≥ f* used in many nonconvex SGD analyses; for unbounded-below objectives (e.g. some matrix-factorization losses) the bound would not apply. Also, the per-coordinate AdaGrad benefit "by a factor of order d when gradient energy is concentrated in a single coordinate" is a worst-case-vs-best-case comparison, not an average-case claim.
- **Comparator baseline honesty.** The strongest "baseline" against which the regret term is interpreted is the identity-preconditioner oracle (which is just SGD). The paper is careful not to claim superiority over Adam/Shampoo empirically; it claims only that those oracles' regret guarantees *carry through* the reduction. That is a fair claim but also means the practical takeaway is currently interpretive, not performance-driven.
- **Potential overclaim.** The abstract phrase "providing a principled perspective on how adaptive optimization methods such as AdaGrad and Shampoo may be understood through static regret and applied in nonconvex optimization" slightly oversells: the paper *instantiates* AdaGrad and Shampoo as oracles and shows their regret bounds survive the reduction. It does not yet show that Adam, Muon, or Lion fit the interface. The framing in the body is appropriately more modest than the abstract.
- **Reproducibility.** For a theory paper, reproducibility hinges on proof completeness (deferred to appendices B/C — not visible in the extracted text but referenced) and on the algorithm pseudocode, which is given fully and unambiguously (Algorithms 1–4). Reimplementation of the construction is straightforward; verifying optimality depends on the appendix proofs.

## Strengths

- Cleanly resolves a stated open problem with a positive answer using a single, simple design principle (preconditioner-slot OCO oracle).
- Unified treatment of both smooth and nonsmooth stochastic nonconvex settings, with optimality in both cases (O(T^{-1/2}) and O(T^{-2/7}) respectively) under only an O(√T) static-regret oracle.
- The modular principle "any OCO algorithm with static regret can serve as a preconditioner module" is genuinely useful for future optimizer design and provides a principled bridge between the OCO and deep-learning-optimizer literatures.
- Honest reporting of constants in the main theorems and explicit instantiation of two well-known adaptive methods (AdaGrad, Shampoo) with their data-dependent regret terms preserved.

## Limitations

- **No experiments.** The work is purely theoretical; the practical consequences for training deep models / LLMs are conjectural.
- **Adam / Muon / Lion / Pion / Leon not (yet) covered.** The paper itself flags that whether these optimizers fit the static-regret preconditioner interface is open. Thus the "modern optimizers" framing is more agenda-setting than demonstrated.
- **Bounded-range assumption** is load-bearing and stronger than the lower-bound assumption common in some nonconvex SGD analyses; applicability to unbounded-below objectives is unclear.
- **Iteration-complexity constants** (64, 142, 1.38, 6.1, 11) are proof margins and not optimized; the paper does not discuss whether they can be tightened or whether the reduction is competitive with hand-tuned analyses of, e.g., clipped Adam (Ahn & Cutkosky 2024).
- The paper does not discuss **constrained** nonconvex problems or **finite-sum / variance-reduction** settings, which are practically important.
- No lower-bound argument is given showing that *ordinary static regret is necessary* (as opposed to merely sufficient). The result establishes sufficiency cleanly; the converses are not explored.

## Relevance

对关注深度学习优化器理论（Adam、Shampoo、Muon、Lion 等）和在线学习与非凸优化联系的研究者高度相关：本文给出了把任意带静态遗憾保证的 OCO 算法当作「预条件子模块」直接接入非凸训练的统一框架，为后续把现代自适应优化器（含 LLM 训练所用的 Muon/Shampoo 系列）重新表述为在线预条件学习、并据此设计新优化器提供了清晰的理论基础。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can Adam, Muon, Lion, Pion, and Leon be reformulated (or modified) so that they admit the preconditioner-space static-regret interface required by this reduction, and if so, what concrete nonconvex convergence rates follow?
- Does the framework extend to **finite-sum** stochastic nonconvex objectives and to **variance-reduced** methods (SAGA, SPIDER, Natasha), and can it inherit their faster rates through a refined regret notion?
- Can the bounded-range assumption be replaced by the weaker bounded-below assumption standard in nonconvex SGD analyses, or by a local progress budget, without losing the static-regret-only interface?
- The paper shows the identity oracle recovers SGD; can the framework be combined with **Nesterov acceleration / momentum on the outer loop** to push past O(T^{-1/2}) in the smooth stochastic nonconvex setting under additional assumptions?
- Is there a matching *lower bound* showing that ordinary static regret is not just sufficient but in some sense necessary for a black-box online-to-nonconvex reduction, formalizing why prior work needed stronger notions?
- For the nonsmooth setting, the (λ,ε)-Goldstein stationarity notion depends on the distributional radius λ — can the framework be tightened to recover the **(ε, δ)-Goldstein** stationarity notion used by Davis et al. (2022) and Jordan et al. (2023) within the same static-regret interface?
- Empirically, does plugging AdaGrad / Shampoo as the preconditioner oracle in Algorithm 1 actually train deep models competitively with the same optimizers used in the conventional way, or does the extra projection / tracker overhead negate the theoretical benefit?
