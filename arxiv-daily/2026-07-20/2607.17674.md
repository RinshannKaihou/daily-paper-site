> **一句话总结：** 本文针对预训练语言模型的响应分布中"纠缠混合的多策略"问题，提出一种"模型导向重建压力"的变分目标——用基础模型自身在 token 级的 surprisal 归一化重建损失，迫使潜在变量 z 编码策略相关的残差变异；在自建的六类多策略算法 benchmark 上成功恢复出与参考策略语义一致的潜在编码（跨输入 AnalogicalConsistency 接近 1），同时保持基础模型的响应分布。

> **TL;DR:** The paper tackles the problem that a pretrained LM's response distribution is an entangled mixture over high-level reasoning strategies. It introduces a **model-directed variational objective** that uses the frozen base model to (i) normalize reconstruction in "base model reference units" and (ii) concentrate token-level pressure on positions of high base-model surprisal, forcing the latent z to encode strategy-relevant variation. On a new six-family multi-strategy algorithmic benchmark (list summation, sorting, grid pathfinding, linear equations, base conversion, multidigit addition), the objective recovers latents aligned with held-out reference strategies with cross-input **Analogical Consistency near ~0.9–1.0** on Qwen2.5 0.5B/1.5B, while standard ELBO/β-ELBO baselines preserve distributional fidelity but leave z essentially inert (alignment near chance).

## 核心贡献

- **问题形式化**：首次明确将"分解预训练模型响应分布 pθ(y|x) 为 router-generator 因子化 (rϕ(z|x), gϕ(y|x,z))"作为一个独立于标准潜在变量建模的问题提出；并指出核心挑战——基础模型在初始化时已经实现了 pθ(y|x)，导致标准 ELBO 全局最优解就是"忽略 z"，这是一种比一般 posterior collapse 更尖锐的病态。
- **新的训练目标**：提出 model-directed reconstruction 目标，用冻结的基础模型定义 (a) 全局参考尺度 cθ（使重建项被解读为"基础模型响应损失中剩余的可解释比例"），(b) token 级 surprisal 权重（集中压力到策略分叉点上），并允许 α 混合、γ 控制集中度。
- **可控的多策略 benchmark**：构造 6 个任务家族，每个问题有多种可解析、可标注的参考策略（如 bubble/insertion/merge sort、left/right/alternating path），标签在训练时被完全保留，仅用于评测。
- **大量消融**：504 次 LoRA 适配训练的诊断网格（随机初始化）+ Qwen2.5 0.5B/1.5B 预训练对比；含 inverse-surprisal 反向对照，用以验证"压力方向"的因果作用。
- **初步理论**：给出"策略结构恢复"问题的形式化定理（profiled ELBO 分解、恢复 margin Δ、有限类样本复杂度 n=O(log|Q||R||G| / ((1-β)Δ)^2)），把"什么条件下 ELBO 能选出策略对齐的潜在变量"刻画为 information-minus-approximation-complexity 的博弈。

## Key Contributions

- **Problem formulation**: Formalizes the decomposition of a fixed pretrained LM's response distribution into a router-generator factorization (rϕ(z|x), gϕ(y|x,z)) as distinct from ordinary latent-variable modeling. Identifies a sharp posterior-collapse pathology: because gϕ(y|x,z)=pθ(y|x) for all z is *available at initialization* and is the global optimum of any marginal-fit-only objective.
- **Model-directed reconstruction objective**: Replaces uniform reconstruction with a base-model-relative term. The global scale cθ = E[bθ(x,y)] normalizes reconstruction into "fractional information gain" (E[Rθ]=1 when g matches pθ); token weights aθ,t are built from pθ-surprisal bθ,t with concentration γ and mixture α. This concentrates gradient on branch points where pθ leaves multiple continuations plausible.
- **Multi-strategy algorithmic benchmark**: Six families (list summation, sorting, grid pathfinding, linear equations, base conversion, multidigit addition) with parseable traces and ground-truth reference strategy labels withheld during training and used only for evaluation.
- **Comprehensive empirical study**: 504-run random-init diagnostic grid sweeping reconstruction variants × β ∈ {1, 0.1, 0.01} × {constant, linear-warmup} schedules, plus pretrained Qwen2.5 0.5B/1.5B multi-task factorization with continuous latent dim 64.
- **Initial theory**: Profiled-ELBO decomposition Lβ,λ = H(Y|X) − (1−β)I_q(Y;Z|X) + Aβ,λ(q); a population recovery theorem conditioned on a profiled margin Δ^{prof}_{β,λ}(δ) > 0; and finite-class sample complexity bounds.

## Methodology

**Architecture.** A single autoregressive Transformer (initialized from base θ) plays two roles sharing parameters ϕ:
- **Router** rϕ(z|x): two learned projection heads on the final-layer representation hϕ^(L)(x) produce a diagonal Gaussian over a continuous latent z.
- **Generator** gϕ(y|x,z): samples z are projected to a pseudo-token embedding E(z) inserted at the embedding layer, so response tokens attend to it during autoregressive decoding.
- **Training-time posterior** qξ(z|x,y): separate model (parameters ξ) reading the full (x,y) pair, providing variational training signal. Discarded at inference.
Both adapted models use LoRA updates + the small Gaussian heads and latent embedding projection, keeping the factorization close to the base model.

**Training distribution.** Crucially, training examples are generated as x ~ D_X, y ~ pθ(·|x), z ~ qξ(·|x,y) — responses are *sampled from the frozen base model itself*. No strategy labels are used in the optimization loop.

**Objective.** The proposed objective replaces the standard ELBO reconstruction term:
Jθ(x,y;ϕ,ξ) = Rθ(x,y;ϕ,ξ) + β·KL(qξ(·|x,y) ∥ rϕ(·|x))

with the model-directed reconstruction:
Rθ = (1/cθ)·E_{z~qξ}[Σ_t aθ,t(x,y)·(−log gϕ(y_t|x,z,y_<t))]

where:
- cθ = E_{x,y}[bθ(x,y)] is the global base reference scale, so E[Rθ]=1 when g matches pθ — interpreted as the *fraction* of base-model response loss remaining after conditioning on z.
- aθ,t(x,y) = α·(1/Ty) + (1−α)·wθ,t^(γ)(x,y) mixes uniform and surprisal-directed token weights.
- wθ,t^(γ) is built from bθ,t^γ (base-model surprisal raised to power γ) with a per-example scale correction κθ that preserves the z-free reconstruction scale.

The key insight is that branch points where pθ is uncertain (high surprisal) are exactly where strategy choices are made; uniform ELBO pressure wastes capacity on tokens that are already determined by the autoregressive prefix.

**Hyperparameters:** α ∈ [0,1] (uniform/surprisal mixture), γ ≥ 0 (concentration), β ≥ 0 (KL weight); swept over β ∈ {1, 0.1, 0.01} with constant and linear-warmup schedules.

## Results & Highlights

- **Strategy information is decodable from base model hidden states** (Figure 5): linear probes on the *unfactorized* base model achieve strategy-classification accuracies up to ~0.75–1.0 across the six tasks, especially at positions where strategy-specific computation is expressed — evidence that the target structure exists implicitly in pθ even before any factorization.
- **ELBO failure (Figure 3 & 6)**: On Qwen2.5 0.5B, the standard ELBO baseline reaches **Distributional Fidelity ≈ 1.0** but **Strategy Alignment (Analogical Consistency) remains near chance (~0.3–0.4)**, with posterior-router KL collapsing to near zero relative to initialization. The same qualitative failure holds for β-ELBO. The latent is *provably available* but never exposed.
- **Model-directed objective recovers alignment (Figure 6)**: The proposed "global+token" and "token weighting" variants on Qwen2.5 0.5B/1.5B reach **Strategy Alignment ≈ 0.85–0.95+** while maintaining Distributional Fidelity ≈ 0.95–1.0 — a substantial gain over ELBO/β-ELBO at matched fidelity.
- **Inverse-surprisal ablation weakens alignment**: Reversing token-level pressure (emphasizing low-surprisal tokens) drops Strategy Alignment well below the high-surprisal variant, supporting the causal claim that the *direction* of pressure matters, not just non-uniformity.
- **Mechanism (Figure 8)**: Across all methods, **token-weighted reconstruction loss tracks Strategy Alignment**; the fidelity-alignment phase-space plot shows an ELBO-trained model can move toward high fidelity without moving toward alignment, while model-directed variants move jointly.
- **Latent geometry (Figure 7 & 9)**: Posterior mean latents (PCA-projected) start as an overlapping cloud and **separate into task- and strategy-structured regions** over training; in multi-task settings this organization is task-conditioned (latent multiplexing) — strategies are linearly separable from z *given task identity* but not globally.
- **β sweep (Figure 10)**: ELBO-style baselines remain weak in fidelity×alignment across all β ∈ {1, 0.1, 0.01}, while model-directed objectives succeed robustly once β is small enough to permit informative latents.
- **Robustness across scale**: Method works on both randomly initialized (504-run grid) and pretrained Qwen2.5 0.5B and 1.5B base models, with continuous latent dimension 64.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high)**: The problem framing — *factorizing an already-realized response distribution* rather than fitting a latent model to data — is genuinely distinct from VAE/CVAE/OPTIMUS/Transformer-VAE literature, and the authors correctly identify why standard objectives collapse here (the inert factorization is available at init, not just asymptotically optimal). The "base model reference units" normalization giving E[Rθ]=1 with fractional-information-gain interpretation is a clean and original construction.
- **Rigor (mixed)**: Empirically careful — 504-run diagnostic grid, β sweeps, two model families, two schedules, explicit inverse-surprisal negative control, mechanism-level phase-space plots, and cross-input analogical-consistency (not just within-input probe accuracy). The benchmark is purpose-built and parseable, with strategy labels cleanly withheld from training.
- **Reproducibility (medium-high)**: Algorithm 1 (training loop) is explicit; Appendix D gives full normalization derivations; hyperparameters, sweeps, and HF model IDs are specified; code is public. The main risk is the benchmark being synthetic — reproducing the headlinable Qwen numbers requires re-running their base-model fitting + factorization pipeline.
- **Significance (medium)**: Conceptually important for interpretability, controllable generation, and strategy-directed RL exploration (the paper's motivating tie to RLVR), but the demonstration is on synthetic algorithmic tasks only; the leap to open-ended reasoning is acknowledged as open.
- **Red flags / weaknesses**:
  - **Benchmark is the obvious weakness**: All six task families are synthetic, low-dimensional, parser-checkable algorithmic problems with 3–5 reference strategies each. The paper itself states "how well the methodology extends to open-ended reasoning tasks remains an open question." This is a controlled proof-of-concept, not a demonstration on real reasoning benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH, code generation). Claims about "reasoning strategies in LLMs" are scoped to this benchmark.
  - **Baselines are weak / narrow**: Only ELBO and β-ELBO are compared as variational baselines. There is no comparison against posterior-collapse mitigations that the paper itself cites (cyclical annealing [Fu+19], lagging inference networks [He+19], Free-bits, InfoVAE [ZSE19]), nor against mechanistic-interpretation alternatives like sparse-autoencoder feature steering [Gal+25] or concept bottlenecks [Tan+24] on the same benchmark. The paper's central "we beat ELBO" claim is true but the ELBO is a near-strawman in the VAE-collapse literature.
  - **Strong cross-input consistency is partly by construction**: The benchmark renders traces deterministically from (problem, strategy), so the same strategy label implies near-identical token-level patterns. Analogical Consistency on this benchmark may overstate how "semantic" the recovered latents are compared to natural-language reasoning traces.
  - **Theory is decoupled from practice**: Theorem 1/2 are population/oracle results; the paper admits "the connection between the oracle selection criterion and actual training dynamics is not yet formally characterized." The complexity proxy C(r,g) is abstract and never instantiated, so the theory motivates but does not actually certify the empirical method.
  - **Latent dimension (64) and small models (0.5B/1.5B)**: It is unclear whether the method scales to frontier-sized models or whether the continuous-Gaussian latent is appropriate vs. discrete/categorical strategy codes.
  - **Per-task alignment not always reported numerically**: Headline numbers are best-runs over swept β; per-task variance is shown as open points in figures but not tabulated, making precise comparison difficult.

## Strengths

- Crisp problem formulation that correctly distinguishes "factorize a realized distribution" from "fit a latent generative model," with a clear identification of why ELBO collapses here.
- The model-directed reconstruction objective is principled: the base-normalized reconstruction has a genuine fractional-information-gain semantics (E[Rθ]=1), and the surprisal weighting has a concrete information-theoretic rationale.
- Benchmark design is well-matched to the evaluation need: parseable traces + ground-truth strategy labels + held-out evaluation, plus a principled cross-input analogical-consistency metric (not just linear probe accuracy).
- Comprehensive ablations: 504-run diagnostic grid, inverse-surprisal negative control, β sweep with two schedules, two model initializations, multi-task vs. single-task.
- Mechanism-level evidence (phase-space dynamics, token-weighted reconstruction tracking alignment) goes beyond headline accuracy and supports the causal story.
- Theoretical appendix provides a population recovery theorem with explicit margin conditions and finite-class sample complexity — sets up a real identification framework even if preliminary.
- Code released; uses openly licensed Qwen2.5 checkpoints.

## Limitations

- All conclusions are on **synthetic algorithmic tasks**; no evaluation on natural reasoning benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH, HumanEval, MBPP). Generalization to open-ended reasoning is the central open question and is unaddressed.
- Baselines are limited to ELBO and β-ELBO; no comparison to established posterior-collapse remedies (cyclical annealing, Free-bits, InfoVAE, lagging inference networks) or to mechanistic-interpretation alternatives (SAEs, concept bottlenecks) on the same tasks.
- Strategy structure is partly baked into the benchmark's deterministic trace rendering, which may inflate cross-input Analogical Consistency relative to what would be observed with natural-language reasoning.
- Theory is preliminary: the complexity proxy C(r,g) is never instantiated, the connection between the oracle selection criterion and SGD training dynamics is not formalized, and recovery margins Δ are not estimated empirically.
- Only Qwen2.5 0.5B/1.5B are evaluated; scaling behavior to larger models and other architectures is unknown.
- No downstream applications demonstrated (the paper motivates interpretability, controllability, and RL exploration but does not evaluate any of them) — these are explicitly deferred to future work.
- Continuous Gaussian latent only; discrete/categorical strategy codes are not explored.

## Relevance

与用户对**大语言模型推理策略、可解释性与可控生成**的研究兴趣高度相关：本文提供了一个把"隐式纠缠的推理策略"显式化为可采样、可干预的潜在变量的原则性框架，对 RLVR 后训练中的策略覆盖问题、推理路径审计与策略级干预都有直接的启发作用，是 LLM interpretability 与可控推理方向值得持续跟踪的工作。其方法（模型导向重建 + 基础模型参考归一化）也可作为后续在真实推理 benchmark 上做策略发现与策略引导采样的基础。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the model-directed reconstruction objective recover meaningful strategy-aligned latents on **open-ended reasoning benchmarks** (GSM8K, MATH, HumanEval), where traces are not parseable and strategy labels must be soft/inferred?
- How does the method compare against **established posterior-collapse remedies** (cyclical KL annealing, Free-bits, InfoVAE, lagging inference networks) and against **mechanistic-interpretation** alternatives (sparse-autoencoder feature steering, supervised concept bottlenecks) on the same multi-strategy benchmark?
- Can the recovered latent z be used for **strategy-directed exploration during RLVR post-training**, e.g., to deliberately sample under-represented strategies and broaden the reachable reasoning distribution beyond what on-policy RL achieves?
- Is the **continuous Gaussian latent** the right parameterization, or would **discrete/categorical codes** (more naturally aligned with "strategy identity") improve alignment and interpretability?
- Does the method scale beyond 1.5B parameters, and does the β that works for 0.5B/1.5B transfer to frontier-sized models?
- Can the theory's complexity proxy C(r,g) be instantiated concretely (e.g., as a LoRA-norm or router-entropy regularizer) so that the population recovery theorem yields a *computable* certification rather than an oracle margin?
- The benchmark renders traces deterministically from (problem, strategy); how much of the observed Analogical Consistency is due to this determinism vs. genuine latent semantic stability? A controlled study with stochastic trace rendering would help.
