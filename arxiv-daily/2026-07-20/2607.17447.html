The framework treats the fitted LLM as a black-box conditional response law. For prompt u and evidence y, the LLM emits a context c = C_u(y) and a Markov kernel Q_u(·|c) over finite continuations. The complete-continuation probability is the product of next-token probabilities — not the first-token probability.

A semantic coarsening maps each continuation to one of K declared states or to an explicit unexpressed category ⊥. The induced pushforward gives the lexical composition; normalising by expressed mass is exactly what makes low verbal coverage dangerous.

Calibration estimates an inverse map in additive-log-ratio coordinates. The affine class is primary because its smallest singular value has a direct observability interpretation — lower bounds 1.686 (GPT-4.1-mini) and 1.600 (GPT-4o-mini). The authors are explicit that this is finite-design evidence, not a uniform identification theorem.

Look-ahead bias is excluded by construction: the market study measures contemporaneous semantic orientation and never uses future returns; the semantic map, candidate phrases, calibration class, and partitions are all frozen before the test partition is opened.

Separates semantic mismatch, probability-calculation error, and unexpressed mass — so "high numerical precision" cannot be misread as "semantically correct."

03 / Critical assessment

Mathematically tight; empirically narrow.

Strengths

  • Complete proofs, named assumptions. Table 3 separates inherited machinery from paper-specific contributions — preventing the "juxtaposing known tools = novelty" overclaim.
  • Theorem 1 is practitioner-useful. It tells you which component to repair — semantic partition, probability calc, or verbal coverage — instead of one opaque loss.
  • Dual-study design is complementary. Market-text comparison tests realistic language; the controlled Bayesian network provides an exact mathematical target. Agreement across both is persuasive.
  • Honest about failure modes. Low expressed mass, evidence reordering (70.8% / 81.3%), irrelevant distractors, and the raw-entropy gap are all reported, not buried.
  • "Raw word entropy ≠ posterior entropy" is one of the most actionable takeaways in the broader LLM-uncertainty literature.

Limitations

  • Two models, one vendor. Only GPT-4.1-mini and GPT-4o-mini — no open-weight or larger model. Cross-model replication is narrow.
  • Analyst-chosen reference. The "exact posterior" comes from a clean three-state Bayesian network the author designed; no messy, high-dimensional real-world reference (e.g. a clinical model) is tested.
  • Filtering is unvalidated. Theorem 4's sequential guarantee is stated but never empirically checked; time-uniform coverage is explicitly out of scope.
  • Constrained grammar only. Completeness ≥ 0.999999 holds because responses are restricted to three codes — behaviour on free-form prose is unmeasured.
  • Modest market evidence. McNemar p = 0.0243 and Δacc = 0.070 rest on 200 records / 60 clusters, conditional on the chosen phrases and protocol.
  • Single author, not peer-reviewed. Independent replication on a different domain would substantially strengthen the claims.

04 / Questions for further exploration

Where the framework breaks — or generalises.

  1. Does affine log-ratio calibration stay well-conditioned (σ_min ≫ 0) as K grows beyond 3–4 states, forcing a move to the untested C1,1 nonlinear class? Scaling · K states
  2. How does it behave on open-weight models (Llama, Qwen, Mistral), where the full continuation law can be enumerated — removing the "top-k ≠ unrestricted law" caveat? Open-weight replication
  3. Can Theorem 4's filtering guarantee be validated against a genuine sequential HMM, yielding the time-uniform confidence sequence the paper declines to claim? Sequential · Thm 4
  4. What is the failure mode of the 4.2% of wrong-direction movements (cosine 0.838 on GPT-4.1-mini) — are they concentrated in particular entropy strata or state pairs? Error analysis
  5. Is the lexical-vs-elicited gain (Δlog-loss = −0.788) robust to the elicitation prompt, or prompt-specific? A sweep over elicitation strategies would settle it. Robustness
  6. Can the semantic map be inverted to audit whether an LLM's reported confidence is semantically stable across paraphrases — a quantitative test for prompt-sensitivity failures? Application · auditing
