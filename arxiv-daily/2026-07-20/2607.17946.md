> **一句话总结：** 作者从损失景观几何视角分析 RLHF 下的价值冲突问题，发现 chain-of-thought (CoT) 能进一步压平 Hessian 最大特征值（λmax 从 Base RLHF 的 4083 降至 1218），并提出模拟热力学退火的三阶段 "Annealing CoT"，在 MMLU Moral Scenarios、DecodingTrust、SafetyBench 三个道德推理基准上以更大优势超越 Base SFT 与标准 CoT 基线。

> **TL;DR:** The paper argues that resolving value conflicts directly through RLHF scalar rewards produces an unstable, cliff-like loss landscape (top Hessian eigenvalue λmax ≈ 4083 on Qwen 3.5 0.8B), and shows that chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning smooths the sharpest direction of the landscape below the effects of SFT (Standard CoT λmax ≈ 1345). The authors introduce an "Annealing CoT" with three cognitive phases (high-temp exploration → cooling → low-temp convergence), which on Qwen 3.5 0.8B lifts MMLU Moral Scenarios accuracy from 24.67 (Base RLHF) to 41.67 (+17.0 absolute) and on Llama 3.2 1B lifts it from 32.67 to 89.67 (+57.0 absolute), with the geometric effect (lower λmax) replicating across model families and a 9B scale-up.

## 核心贡献

- **几何视角揭示 RLHF 在价值冲突上的不稳定性：** 通过 Hessian 顶部特征值 λmax 量化证明，直接用标量奖励做 RLHF 训练会形成尖锐、悬崖状的损失景观（Qwen 3.5 0.8B 上 λmax ≈ 4083），即标量奖励本质上无法很好解决价值冲突。
- **首次把 CoT 与损失景观压平建立定量联系：** 在 SFT 之外，CoT 进一步把 λmax 从 1402（Base SFT）降至 1345（Standard CoT），再降至 1218（Annealing CoT），证明推理动态本身能影响优化几何。
- **提出 Annealing CoT 这一新的、面向价值冲突的推理模板：** 借鉴凝聚态物理中的几何阻挫（geometric frustration）与热力学退火，将推理显式拆成"高温探索—冷却权衡—低温收敛"三阶段，作为更结构化的 CoT 设计范式。
- **跨模型族、跨规模的泛化验证：** 在 Llama 3.2 1B 与 Qwen 3.5 9B 上重复实验，结果趋势一致，特别是 Annealing CoT 始终以更大优势超过 Base RLHF 与 Base SFT。
- **公开数据集与代码：** 释出基于 ConflictScope 生成的标准 CoT 与退火 CoT SFT 数据，便于复现与扩展。

## Key Contributions

- **Geometric diagnosis of RLHF value conflicts.** Uses top Hessian eigenvalue λmax as a stability proxy to show that resolving mutually exclusive values through scalar rewards produces a sharp, cliff-like minimum (λmax ≈ 4083 on Qwen 3.5 0.8B), evidence that scalar rewards are ill-equipped for value conflict resolution.
- **CoT linked to flatter minima, beyond SFT.** Demonstrates that adding CoT further smooths the sharpest direction of the loss landscape on top of SFT: λmax drops 4083 (Base RLHF) → 1402 (Base SFT) → 1345 (Standard CoT) → 1218 (Annealing CoT).
- **A new value-conflict-focused reasoning template, "Annealing CoT."** Translates thermodynamic annealing into three explicit cognitive phases (broad high-temperature exploration, cooling/trade-off weighing, low-temperature decisive convergence), framed via the analogy between scalar-reward value conflict and geometric frustration in condensed matter physics.
- **Cross-family and cross-scale generalization.** Replicates the pipeline on Llama 3.2 1B and Qwen 3.5 9B; the annealing CoT consistently beats Base RLHF and Base SFT by a larger margin than the standard CoT does.
- **Released dataset and code** for standard and annealing CoT built on the ConflictScope prompt pipeline.

## Methodology

The authors construct a controlled post-training pipeline that isolates the contribution of reasoning dynamics.

**Data generation.** Prompts come from the publicly released ConflictScope pipeline (Liu et al., 2026), which elicits binary value conflicts (e.g., fairness vs. objectivity, autonomy vs. privacy, helpfulness vs. honesty). For each prompt, an ensemble of three frontier LLMs (GPT 5 Nano, Gemini 3.1 Flash Lite, Claude 4.5 Haiku) generates two CoT–response pairs: a standard unstructured CoT, and an annealing-inspired CoT structured into three phases:

- Phase 1 (High Temperature): explicitly weigh conflicting constraints without prematurely collapsing the solution space; ground exploration in ≥2 prompt-specific details.
- Phase 2 (Cooling): apply hierarchical principles to narrow trade-offs.
- Phase 3 (Low Temperature): converge on a decisive, justified action.

Length is controlled across the two CoT variants to avoid length confounds. CoTs are wrapped in `<think>` tags for SFT.

**Training.** Base model: Qwen 3.5 0.8B (instruct), chosen so that Hessian computation is tractable; full fine-tuning (not PEFT) is used so the true loss landscape is modified. Pipeline: SFT (3 epochs, lr 2e-5, cosine, batch 32, warmup 0.1, 1,146 prompts) followed by RLHF via GRPO (2 epochs, lr 1e-6, KL β = 0.1, group size 4, batch 8). The reward model is GRM Llama 3.2 3B (strong on RewardBench). Four model variants are trained: Base RLHF (RLHF only, no SFT), Base SFT (SFT on responses only, then RLHF), Standard CoT, Annealing CoT.

**Geometric evaluation.** Top Hessian eigenvalue λmax is computed with PyHessian using Hutchinson's stochastic approximation, on a held-out ConflictScope subset. To avoid the well-known limitation of random filter-normalized perturbations, the authors perturb trained weights along the top two Hessian eigenvectors (v1, v2): θ′ = θ + αv1 + βv2 over a uniform grid, evaluating forward-pass loss at each grid point to produce 3D and 2D visualizations that actually capture the sharpest directions. Aggregated mean and standard error are reported over 3 subsets of 100 prompts each.

**Performance evaluation.** Three benchmarks spanning simple → complex moral reasoning: MMLU Moral Scenarios (simple moral/immoral classification), DecodingTrust Machine Ethics (commonsense morality, including Reddit-sourced real-world situations), SafetyBench (sensitive-topic boundaries). Parsing: string matching for DecodingTrust; GPT-5.5 LLM-as-judge (temperature 0.0) for MMLU and SafetyBench. Mean accuracy and ±1 SE reported over three subsets.

**Generalization.** Replicates on Llama 3.2 1B (using a CoT-tuned variant, Yu 2025, for fair comparison with Qwen 3.5) with full geometric analysis, and on Qwen 3.5 9B with benchmark-only evaluation (Hessian approximation is infeasible at this scale).

## Results & Highlights

- **Top Hessian eigenvalue on Qwen 3.5 0.8B (lower is better, ±1 SE):** Base RLHF 4083.87 ± 156.53 → Base SFT 1402.77 ± 4.48 → Standard CoT 1345.65 ± 15.60 → Annealing CoT 1218.04 ± 43.31. Annealing CoT reduces λmax by ≈70% versus Base RLHF and by ≈13% versus Base SFT, quantitatively confirming that CoT smooths the sharpest direction of the loss landscape beyond SFT.
- **Benchmark accuracy on Qwen 3.5 0.8B (mean ±1 SE):** On MMLU Moral Scenarios, Annealing CoT reaches 41.67 ± 0.88 vs. 24.67 ± 0.88 for Base RLHF (+17.0 absolute) and 30.33 ± 2.40 for Standard CoT. On DecodingTrust Machine Ethics, Annealing CoT scores 54.33 ± 2.85 vs. 42.33 ± 1.20 (Base RLHF) and 45.67 ± 2.03 (Standard CoT). On SafetyBench, Annealing CoT scores 64.00 ± 2.08 vs. 43.67 ± 4.37 (Base RLHF) and 53.67 ± 5.46 (Standard CoT).
- **Generalization to Llama 3.2 1B:** λmax follows the same trend — Base RLHF 3012.05 ± 64.08, Base SFT 951.87 ± 6.53, Standard CoT 842.51 ± 3.68, Annealing CoT 809.36 ± 1.46. On MMLU Moral Scenarios, Annealing CoT reaches 89.67 ± 1.33 vs. 32.67 ± 4.63 for Base RLHF (+57.0 absolute) and 85.00 ± 1.53 for Standard CoT.
- **Generalization to Qwen 3.5 9B (benchmarks only):** On MMLU Moral Scenarios, Annealing CoT 64.33 ± 0.88 vs. Base RLHF 59.00 ± 0.58 (+5.3 absolute). On SafetyBench, Annealing CoT 61.67 ± 2.96 vs. Base RLHF 41.00 ± 1.73 (+20.7 absolute) — the SafetyBench gain actually grows with scale.
- **Honest negative-style finding:** The improvement of Standard CoT over Base SFT is within the margin of error on the 0.8B model; the structural benefit only becomes unambiguous with the annealing variant, which the authors use as their main proof-of-concept for "intentionally designed reasoning dynamics."

- **Saket Reddy** is affiliated with the Supervised Program for Alignment Research (SPAR) and the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign.
- **Andy Liu** is at Carnegie Mellon University and is also a co-author of ConflictScope (Liu et al., 2026, ICLR) — the very prompt pipeline used in this paper, which gives the authors direct expertise over the value-conflict data they study.
- The methodology uses well-established tools (PyHessian, Hutchinson's method, GRPO, GRM Llama 3.2 3B reward model with public RewardBench credibility) and standard benchmarks (MMLU Moral Scenarios, DecodingTrust, SafetyBench). The choice to perturb along the top Hessian eigenvectors rather than random directions is a methodologically correct fix for a well-known visualization weakness.
- Hyperparameters, sample sizes (1,146 prompts), evaluation protocol (3 subsets × 100 prompts), and generation prompts are all reported in appendices, and the dataset/code are stated to be released.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The conceptual bridge between (a) geometric frustration / thermodynamic annealing in condensed matter physics, (b) the loss-landscape view of RLHF instability, and (c) CoT reasoning design is genuinely fresh. The empirical claim that CoT specifically smooths the *sharpest* Hessian direction beyond SFT is, to my knowledge, new.
- **Rigor:** The Hessian analysis is methodologically cleaner than typical loss-landscape plots because the authors perturb along the actual top eigenvectors rather than random filter-normalized directions, addressing a real flaw in Li et al. (2018)-style visualizations. Standard errors are reported throughout. Three-subset averaging is reasonable given compute constraints.
- **Reproducibility:** Reasonably strong — model choice (Qwen 3.5 0.8B, Llama 3.2 1B), full FT (not PEFT), all hyperparameters, generation prompts (Appendix D), and the ConflictScope prompts are public. However, exact CoT training data is "released" only as a claim in the footnote; a reviewer would want to verify the actual artifact. The 1,146-prompt training set is small, raising variance concerns.
- **Significance:** Moderate. The central performance claims rest on a 0.8B model with single-digit accuracy SEs, and several "improvements" (Base SFT vs. Standard CoT; Annealing vs. Standard on 0.8B SafetyBench) overlap their error bars. The strongest, cleanest win is Annealing CoT on the Llama 1B MMLU Moral Scenarios jump from 32.67 to 89.67, but that single task is also where Standard CoT already achieves 85.00 — so the *annealing-specific* lift there is only ≈4.7 points. The headline "larger margin" claim is supported but should be read as proof-of-concept, not as a robust capability gain.
- **Red flags / weak points:**
  - The reward model is GRM Llama 3.2 3B — a small reward model — providing scalar rewards on value-conflict prompts, which is the very signal the paper argues is inadequate; the circularity is not discussed.
  - Standard CoT's accuracy advantage over Base SFT on the headline 0.8B model is explicitly within margin of error, so the "CoT improves moral reasoning" claim leans almost entirely on the annealing variant.
  - Evaluation parsing mixes methods (string match for DecodingTrust, GPT-5.5 judge for MMLU/SafetyBench), and the judge temperature/seeds are not fully specified.
  - λmax is acknowledged as a heuristic proxy; no direct link to actual deployment stability (e.g., jailbreak robustness) is measured, only moral-reasoning accuracy.
  - Only binary value conflicts are tested; the authors themselves flag scaling to N-dimensional value spaces as future work.

## Strengths

- A genuinely interesting interdisciplinary framing (geometric frustration + annealing + CoT) that yields a concrete, deployable CoT template.
- Clean Hessian methodology using top-eigenvector perturbations rather than arbitrary random slices — a meaningful improvement over standard loss-landscape visualizations.
- Honest reporting: the authors explicitly state that several improvements are within the margin of error, rather than overclaiming.
- Cross-family (Qwen → Llama) and cross-scale (0.8B → 9B) replication substantially strengthens generalization claims beyond a single model.
- Synthetic CoT data is generated by an ensemble of three frontier LLMs to reduce single-model bias, with length controlled across CoT variants.

## Limitations

- Experiments only cover **binary** value conflicts; N-way pluralistic settings (the realistic deployment case) are untested and explicitly flagged as future work.
- λmax is a **heuristic stability proxy**; the paper does not demonstrate that flatter minima translate into deployment-time robustness (e.g., against jailbreaks or distribution shift).
- The headline model is **0.8B**, and several of its benchmark gains are within the margin of error; the strongest unambiguous gains come from Llama 1B on a single benchmark.
- The reward model (GRM Llama 3.2 3B) is itself a small scalar-reward model — a potential circularity given the paper's thesis that scalar rewards are inadequate for value conflicts.
- The mapping between "internal reasoning design" and "external RLHF reward design" is informal; the authors acknowledge a more rigorous trade-off characterization is missing.
- Geometric analysis is infeasible at 9B, so the largest model is only evaluated on benchmarks, not on the loss landscape that is the paper's central object of study.

## Relevance

对于关注 RLHF、对齐与 LLM 推理机制的研究者，本文提供了一个把 chain-of-thought 推理与损失景观几何（Hessian 顶部特征值）定量联系起来的新视角，并提出可直接复用的"退火式 CoT"模板，与多元价值对齐（pluralistic alignment）、安全对齐与对齐鲁棒性方向高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the λmax-smoothing effect of CoT hold for **N-way (>2)** value conflicts, where the "geometric frustration" analogy should be even more acute?
- Is the Annealing CoT benefit robust when the **reward model is replaced by a constitution-style or multi-objective reward** that explicitly avoids scalar compression?
- Can the Hessian-smoothing link be verified at scale (e.g., 9B+) using cheaper second-order proxies (e.g., trace or low-rank Hessian estimates) to recover geometric analysis where PyHessian becomes infeasible?
- Does the flatter minimum induced by Annealing CoT translate into measurable gains on **adversarial robustness** (jailbreaks, morality attacks such as Su et al. 2026), not just moral-reasoning accuracy?
- How much of the Annealing CoT gain is the **three-phase structure** per se versus the explicit value-naming prompt used to *generate* the training data? An ablation isolating structure from content is missing.
- Could the annealing schedule (currently fixed 3-phase) be **learned or自适应** per prompt, potentially yielding further landscape flattening?
