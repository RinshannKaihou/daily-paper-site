> **一句话总结：** 本文通过 3.1M 次 LLM rollout、30 个等预算 harness 配置和 12 个模型-问题对的重复试验统计分析，发现 OpenEvolve/TTT-Discover 等复杂 discovery harness 并不普遍优于简单的 Sequential Best-of-N 基线（完整 OpenEvolve 在跨对胜率上仅 0.033），harness 选择应被视为模型/问题相关的超参数；作者进一步利用"早期进展预测最终性能"（50% 检查点 Spearman 相关 >0.70）提出 Budget-matched Adaptive Harness Ensemble，在 5 全运行等价预算下将平均得分从 84.35% 提升至 85.75%。

> **TL;DR:** A large-scale, repeated-trial study (3.1M LLM rollouts, 30 budget-matched harnesses, 12 model-problem pairs spanning Qwen2.5-3B / Qwen3-4B / GPT-OSS-20B / GPT-OSS-120B on circle packing, Heilbronn triangle, and second-autocorrelation tasks) decomposes OpenEvolve-style and TTT-Discover-style discovery harnesses into components and shows no fixed harness is Holm-adjusted significantly better than a simple Sequential Best-of-N baseline; the complete OpenEvolve recipe is in fact the worst-ranked fixed harness (majority-win 0.033, cross-pair p̂=0.978). Because 50%-checkpoint scores correlate with final performance (Spearman >0.70 on 11/12 pairs), the authors propose a budget-matched Adaptive Harness Ensemble (best schedule: 12→5→2→1 three-stage pruning) that lifts average best-of-5 score from Sequential BoN's 84.35% to 85.75% under the same 5 full-run-equivalent budget, beating both single-harness commitment (82.49%) and an unpruned harness portfolio (84.54%).

## 核心贡献

- 首个针对 LLM-guided discovery harness 的最大规模、统计严格控制评估：在 12 个模型-问题对上评估 30 个等预算 harness，使用超过 3.1M LLM rollout，每个 harness 跑 5 次独立试验，并构建 100 次 (Qwen) / 30 次 (GPT-OSS) 的 Sequential BoN 参考池作为经验零分布。
- 将 OpenEvolve（进化式）和 TTT-Discover（PUCT 树搜索）两类 harness 从同一个 Sequential Best-of-N 根出发逐组件解耦，从而把每个设计决策（Top-K archive、ε-greedy 探索、UCT/PUCT、深度/广度再分配、MAP-Elites、多岛、多父代）作为可独立消融的因子。
- 提出"跨对多数胜率"（cross-pair majority-win）统计量 + Holm 校正的排列检验，正式揭示 discovery harness 的**泛化问题**：最强的固定 harness（K=1, ε=20%）在 Holm 校正后 p=0.678，**无任何固定 harness 统计显著优于简单基线**；完整 OpenEvolve 反而是排名最低的固定 harness。
- 实证发现**早期进展强预测最终性能**：在 50% 检查点，12 对中有 11 对的 Spearman 相关 >0.70。
- 提出 **Adaptive Harness Ensemble**（基于 Successive Halving / Hyperband 思想的预算自适应分配策略），在等预算下显著优于单 harness 提交、未剪枝 harness 组合与 Sequential BoN 基线。
- 公开 3.1M rollout 记录（含逐步 evaluator 得分与基线零分布），作为未来 harness 提案的可复用统计基础设施。

## Key Contributions

- The most extensive statistically controlled evaluation of discovery harnesses to date: 30 budget-matched harnesses × 12 model-problem pairs, >3.1M rollouts, 5 independent runs per harness, and 100-run (Qwen) / 30-run (GPT-OSS) Sequential BoN reference pools serving as empirical null distributions.
- A unified decomposition that derives both OpenEvolve-style evolutionary search and TTT-Discover-style PUCT search from the same greedy Sequential Best-of-N root, so each ingredient (Top-K archive, ε-greedy exploration, UCT/PUCT bonuses, breadth-depth reallocation, MAP-Elites + inspiration sampling, multi-island, multi-parent sampling) becomes independently ablatable.
- A cross-pair majority-win statistic with permutation testing and Holm correction that formalizes the "generalization problem" of discovery harnesses: the strongest observed fixed harness (K=1, ε=20%) has p̂_holm = 0.678 and **no fixed harness is significantly better than a simple baseline**, while full OpenEvolve is the worst fixed harness (P̂_maj = 0.033, p̂_cross = 0.978).
- An empirical finding that **partial-run performance predicts final performance**: at the 50% checkpoint, Spearman correlation with final score exceeds 0.70 on 11 of 12 model-problem pairs.
- A budget-matched **Adaptive Harness Ensemble** policy (Successive-Halving/Hyperband-style pruning) that beats single-harness commitment, unpruned harness portfolio, and Sequential BoN under the same 5 full-run-equivalent budget.
- Release of the full 3.1M-rollout run pool with per-step evaluator scores and baseline null distributions as reusable statistical infrastructure for future harness proposals.

## Methodology

**Object of study.** The authors treat the *harness* — the rule that decides which previously evaluated solutions an LLM should expand next — as the primary object, not the model or evaluator. They focus on two representative composite harness families: OpenEvolve (evolutionary search) and TTT-Discover (PUCT tree search; they explicitly ablate only the search harness, not the test-time-training component).

**Unified decomposition (Figure 1a).** Both families are rebuilt from a common root — greedy Sequential Best-of-N, where at iteration t the parent is `p_t = argmax_{x∈H_t} S(x)` and N children are generated, evaluated, and appended to history H_t, for T iterations under a constant rollout budget B = N·T.
- *OpenEvolve branch:* Top-1 archive → Top-K archive → ε-greedy full-history exploration → shift budget from breadth (N) to depth (T) → add MAP-Elites + inspiration sampling → multi-island evolution with crossovers.
- *TTT-Discover branch:* greedy selection → replace argmax with subtree value estimate → add UCT exploration bonus → upgrade to PUCT (prior-weighted bonus) → halve N for deeper search → sample multiple parents per iteration.

**Statistical evaluation.** Two complementary tests:
1. *Pair-level best-of-5 test* (Section 3.2): for each model-problem pair, compare a candidate harness's best-of-5 outcome against a bootstrap empirical null of best-of-5 maxima drawn from 100 (Qwen) or 30 (GPT-OSS) Sequential BoN runs; 100k bootstrap trials, one-sided p̂_h, nominal α = 0.05.
2. *Cross-pair majority-win test* (Section 3.3): a statistic P̂_maj(h) = Pr[harness's 12-dim best-of-5 vector beats a resampled Sequential BoN vector on ≥7 of 12 pairs], with a 100k-trial permutation test and Holm step-down correction across all configurations.

**Setup.** Models: Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507, GPT-OSS-20B, GPT-OSS-120B (3B→120B). Tasks: circle packing, Heilbronn triangle, second autocorrelation inequality. Per-run rollout budgets: 1600 (Qwen), 320 (GPT-OSS-20B), 160 (GPT-OSS-120B); each harness run 5×.

**Adaptive Harness Ensemble (Section 5.2).** Treats fixed-harness choice as an online allocation problem under a hard budget of B_e = 5 full-run equivalents. Policy starts m configurations, advances them to one or more normalized checkpoints q ∈ {25%, 50%, 75%}, ranks partial runs by best evaluator score so far, prunes weak runs, and completes s survivors under the budget constraint `Σ_ℓ m_ℓ(q_ℓ − q_{ℓ−1}) ≤ B_e`. Compared against Sequential BoN (5 full runs), single-harness commitment (1 config × 5 runs), and unpruned harness portfolio (5 configs × 1 full run). Evaluation uses 100k resampling trials per pair.

## Results & Highlights

- **No fixed harness is significantly better than Sequential BoN after Holm correction.** The strongest fixed configuration K=1, ε=20% achieves P̂_maj = 0.914 with raw p̂_cross = 0.023, but Holm-adjusted p̂^holm_cross = 0.678 — non-significant.
- **Full OpenEvolve is the *worst*-ranked fixed harness** in the cross-pair analysis: P̂_maj = 0.033, p̂_cross = 0.978 — it loses to Sequential BoN on a majority of pairs even though it layers on MAP-Elites, inspiration sampling, and multi-island evolution.
- **Larger Top-K archives alone yield no pair-level p̂_h < 0.05 gains** (Figure 2, top row); ε-greedy full-history exploration helps only on *select* pairs (Qwen2.5-3B on Circle Packing, GPT-OSS-20B and GPT-OSS-120B on Heilbronn Triangle) and hurts others.
- **Adding OpenEvolve machinery is not monotonic**: the full OpenEvolve-style recipe produces nominally significant gains only for GPT-OSS-20B on Heilbronn Triangle and *reduces* performance on Circle Packing and Second Autocorrelation Inequality, frequently underperforming its own intermediate variants.
- **Partial-run predictiveness (Figure 5b)**: at the 10% checkpoint, Spearman correlation with final score is 0.000–0.474 (weak-to-moderate); at the 50% checkpoint it exceeds 0.70 on 11/12 pairs (remaining 0.651 for GPT-OSS-120B on Second Autocorrelation Inequality).
- **Adaptive Harness Ensemble (Table 1)**, under identical 5 full-run-equivalent budget, every pruning schedule beats every baseline; best schedule **12→5→2→1** (three-stage pruning at 25%/50%/75%) lifts average score to **85.75%** vs. unpruned harness portfolio **84.54%**, single-harness commitment **82.49%**, and five Sequential BoN runs **84.35%** (SE ≈ 0.02). The 12→5→2→1 policy also beats the unpruned portfolio on 11 of 12 individual pairs.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel framing — turning the harness into the object of study rather than treating it as a fixed recipe. The decomposition + bootstrap/permutation null-distribution protocol is a real methodological contribution and improves on the 3-run evaluation protocol common in prior code-evolution papers (Cemri et al., 2026; Liu et al., 2026b; Assumpção et al., 2025).
- **Rigor:** Strong. 100k-trial bootstrap + permutation tests with Holm correction, 100-run baseline pools for Qwen, prespecified α = 0.05, best-of-5 statistics with mean/median sensitivity analyses in Appendix F. The honesty of reporting raw *vs.* Holm-adjusted p-values (where the headline K=1, ε=20% harness drops from p=0.023 to p=0.678) is commendable — many papers would have stopped at the unadjusted number.
- **Reproducibility:** High — full release of 3.1M rollouts, code, baseline pools. Budget matching by rollout count is clearly defined; the only soft spot is that GPT-OSS-120B uses only 160 rollouts/run with a 30-run baseline pool, leaving that pair's null distribution noisier than Qwen's.
- **Significance:** If the claim holds up, it is consequential: it undercuts the implicit "complex harness = better" assumption behind a large family of 2025–2026 systems (OpenEvolve, ShinkaEvolve, DeltaEvolve, AdaEvolve, CodeEvolve, DeepEvolve, GigaEvo) by showing the full OpenEvolve recipe is the *worst* fixed harness in cross-pair evaluation. The Adaptive Harness Ensemble result is the constructive counter-proposal.
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - **Limited task suite** — only three mathematical-discovery problems (circle packing, Heilbronn triangle, second autocorrelation inequality). Generalization to code synthesis, scientific discovery, or symbolic regression is asserted but not tested. The paper title ("No Universally Superior Harness") is strong; the evidence covers a narrow domain.
  - **Model coverage skew** — only 4 models, all either Qwen2.5/3-instruct or GPT-OSS; no closed APIs (Claude/Gemini/GPT-4o), no reasoning-specialized models tested. Whether the same harness rankings survive under reasoning models (where N=1 deep generation may dominate) is open.
  - **Adaptive-ensemble result is paired-task-specific** — Table 1's 11/12 win claim is over the unpruned portfolio under *empirical resampling* of the same pool, not over freshly sampled runs; it is a within-pool allocation test, not an out-of-distribution test of the policy itself.
  - **No comparison to bandit-style or learned allocators** (e.g., AdaEvolve's island-level scheduler, Meta-Harness, EvoX) under matched budget — the Adaptive Harness Ensemble is presented as a *framing*, with the authors explicitly stating "we propose no new bandit or early-stopping algorithm." Fair as a scope choice, but limits head-to-head positioning.
  - **TTT-Discover ablation excludes test-time training**, the headline component of that system, so the TTT-Discover comparison should be read as "search harness only."
  - No explicit "Limitations" or "Threats to Validity" section; caveats are scattered through Related Work and Conclusion.

## Strengths

- Statistical care that is rare in this subfield: large baseline pools (100/30 runs), bootstrap + permutation tests, Holm correction, prespecified thresholds, and explicit reporting of both raw and adjusted p-values.
- Decomposition framework (Sequential BoN → OpenEvolve / TTT-Discover) is reusable beyond these two systems and turns "recipe comparison" into "ingredient ablation."
- Constructive contribution: a budget-matched Adaptive Harness Ensemble that beats three reasonable baselines without proposing any new optimizer — pure framing win.
- Honest about the null result: states "no tested fixed harness is significantly better than a simple baseline," which is a more useful scientific statement than the typical positive spin.
- Strong artifact release (3.1M rollouts + per-step scores + null distributions) makes the paper a reference benchmark for future harness proposals.

## Limitations

- **Task domain is narrow** — three math-discovery problems; no code synthesis, science, or RL-tuned-task evaluation. Cross-domain generalization of the harness-generalization claim is unproven.
- **Model coverage omits closed-weights frontier and reasoning models**; allocation dynamics may differ for o1-style or Claude-style models where single deep rollouts dominate.
- **No head-to-head against learned/meta-harness methods** (Meta-Harness, EvoX, AdaEvolve's scheduler) under matched budget.
- **Adaptive ensemble is evaluated via resampling the same run pool**, so the gain reflects within-distribution allocation rather than truly fresh runs of the policy; the standard error (≈0.02) is small but the evaluation is not out-of-sample.
- **TTT-Discover ablation ignores the test-time-training module**, so conclusions about TTT-Discover apply only to its search harness.
- **No dedicated Limitations section** — relevant caveats are dispersed across Related Work (Section 6) and Conclusion (Section 7).
- The headline title's "universally" is broader than what 12 model-problem pairs in one domain can support.

## Relevance

对研究"LLM 自主发现/代码进化"系统（FunSearch、AlphaEvolve、OpenEvolve、TTT-Discover 等）的读者高度相关：它提供了目前最大规模、最严格的 harness 统计评估基准与可复用 rollout 数据池，并为"harness 选择应作为在线超参数"这一新范式提供了实证支撑。对做 LLM inference-time scaling、algorithm portfolio、hyperparameter search（Hyperband/ASHA/Successive Halving）的研究者也有方法论借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the harness rankings transfer to non-math discovery domains — e.g., SWE-bench-style code synthesis, symbolic regression, or scientific hypothesis discovery?
- How do reasoning-specialized models (o1/o3, Claude with extended thinking, Gemini 2.5 Pro) change the picture, given that single deep rollouts may dominate N-shallow-children BoN?
- Would a learned bandit allocator (e.g., Thompson sampling over harness arms) beat the hand-designed 12→5→2→1 pruning schedule under the same 5 full-run-equivalent budget?
- Is the early-progress-predicts-final correlation an artifact of these three tasks' fitness landscapes, or does it generalize to noisier / more deceptive evaluators?
- The paper finds full OpenEvolve is the worst fixed harness; can a finer ablation identify *which* ingredient (MAP-Elites, inspiration sampling, or multi-island) is actively harmful, and on which pair?
- Could the released null-distribution run pools be used to define a "harness leaderboard" protocol that future proposals must pass to claim improvement over Sequential BoN?
