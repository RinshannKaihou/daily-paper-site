Why this matters

A cheap plug-in for any shared-parameter CTDE system under distribution shift — no extra forward-backward pass, +3.8% wall-clock, +512 bytes, zero architecture change.

Critical edge

Only two seeds, a single tiny scenario (1,184 actor params), and no head-to-head with the closest competitor PE-MAMoE — the "first internal-monitoring" claim is empirically thin.

§ Methodology

Forward-dormant and gradient-silent — both must fail.

A neuron is reset only if it is forward-dormant (score ŝ ≤ 0.5) and PPO-gradient-silent (ĝ ≤ 0.08). The backward signal is reused straight from the optimizer — no auxiliary pass. Detection fires every F=200 mini-batch steps, independent of phase boundaries.

The intersection is aggregated over the full B×n team batch, because per-agent dormancy disagreement reaches 5–18% in policy layers — single-agent statistics are ill-posed.

Coverage peaks ~0.68 in-phase vs ~0.60 (MAPPO). Wall-clock overhead +3.8%, memory +512 bytes. In stationary Normal mode MAPPO wins (83.4 vs 78.4) — the gain is non-stationarity-only.

Strengths

  • Diagnostic clarity. Three crisp findings (accumulation, fwd/back divergence, cross-agent disagreement) each map 1:1 to a design choice.
  • Brutally honest. Reports it loses to MAPPO in stationary mode, that the reset operator is replaceable, and that AuxGrad matches it after tuning.
  • Cheap to deploy. Reuses the PPO gradient; no architecture change; output-preserving reset (zeros the outgoing column).
  • Phase-signal independence rigorously tested with co-prime F=211 — periodic detection alone suffices.

Limitations

  • n = 2 seeds only for a paper whose central claim is statistical robustness; some ablations are single-seed.
  • Single tiny scenario (H=32). No SMAC, MPE, or larger teams/networks — gain at H=128/256 unknown.
  • Custom simulator, no public code or hardware-in-the-loop validation.
  • No head-to-head with PE-MAMoE, ReBorn, MACPH, Soft Reset. Only ReSiN's signal/operator ablated in.
  • ReLU only; smooth rectifiers discussed but not tested. Local µ-strong convexity assumed, not verified.
§ Open questions

Where the result does — and doesn't — travel.

Does the 24.9% gain survive at H=128, 256, larger teams, and standard benchmarks (SMAC, MPE)?
How does PRIME compare head-to-head with PE-MAMoE (same authors, same simulator, MoE-based) on this environment?
Could fixed F=200 become an adaptive trigger on team-aggregated DNF — and cut the Normal-mode regression?
Does the criterion hold under RMSProp / Lion / SGD-momentum, where the gradient-to-update mapping differs?
For smooth activations (GELU, Swish), does τ_d shift and does gradient-only silence dominate?
Is the 5–18% cross-agent disagreement a function of observation heterogeneity or width? Does it collapse in homogeneous teams?
Does silent-neuron reset stack with LayerNorm / spectral regularization, or do they address overlapping failure modes?
Under real-UAV latency and sensor noise, does the phase-transition sharpness that motivates the design still hold?
