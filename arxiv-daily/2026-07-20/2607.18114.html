How Does Alignment Tuning Shape Representations of Sycophancy and Related Cue-Induced Biases in LLMs? ↗
interpretability sycophancy cue-induced-bias alignment-tuning probing causal-intervention
Key finding

Each cue-induced bias lives in one decodable, causal direction — installed by alignment tuning, not pretraining.

The same direction doubles as a debiasing knob, recovering 7–20% of bias-induced errors while keeping ≥90% of correct answers across all five instruct families.

01 Methodology

Triangulating each bias with three independent measures 5 families × 7 biases

1
Non-CoT, single-token answering. Prompts are rewritten to demand one letter (A–P); next-token logits are summed over bare + space-prefixed variants to isolate the bias effect from any reasoning chain.
2
Flipped vs. resisted pairs. A pair is flipped if the model knew the answer (unbiased prompt → correct) but caved to the bias target, and resisted if it held firm.
3
Contrastive direction. Per-source direction is the difference-of-means of flipped vs. resisted last-token residual-stream hidden states: d_s = (μ_flip − μ_resist) / ‖·‖₂. The per-bias direction averages across that bias's datasets.
4
Three validity checks. Probing (LODO AUROC vs. K=50 random unit-norm baseline), causal intervention (subtract α·d_ℓ at every layer; recovery = flipped pairs now correct), and per-layer localization.
5
Base-probe protocol (the origin test). Feed instruct-defined flipped/resisted prompts to the base model on byte-identical inputs and ask whether the distinction is decodable — subtracting the unbiased-prompt probe as a question-difficulty control.

Models: Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen-2.5-7B, Gemma-2-9B, Mistral-7B-v0.3, OLMo-2-7B (instruct + matching base).   Datasets: MMLU, HellaSwag, LogiQA, TruthfulQA, HindsightNeglect.

All 116 (bias, dataset, model) points beat matched random baselines at Wilcoxon p < 10⁻⁶. Preservation ≥90% throughout.

Three structural findings

  • Bias peaks in late-middle layers. Per-layer AUROC peaks at relative depth 0.55–0.74; the single best layer even exceeds the all-layer probe (Llama L17/32: 0.82 vs 0.74).
  • Cross-bias clustering is Qwen-specific. The 4-bias cluster (cos 0.72–0.85) appears on Qwen but is absent on Llama (rank 19/35, p=0.68 n.s.) and OLMo — distinctness is a model property, not a bias-category property.
  • Letter-balanced control holds. After balancing bias_target vs. correct_letter, LODO AUROC shifts by |Δ| ≤ 0.041 (n.s.); balanced-vs-original direction cosines ≥ 0.72 — not a letter artifact.

Origin attribution — base models almost never cave base-probe

Across all 25 sources, strict-flipped pair counts for base vs. instruct show base models flip on only 0.2–4% as many pairs (Llama-base 11 vs ~5,950; OLMo-base 280 vs ~7,150). Base-activation cue gaps (biased − unbiased AUROC) sit at +0.01 to +0.09 for the four non-outlier families — and only the instruction-like bias (Suggested Answer) leaves a faint +0.17 trace. The pattern is the reverse of an "it's just instruction-following" account, and pins the bias to alignment tuning.

03 Strengths & Limitations

+ Strengths

  • Triangulates each bias with three independent measures (probing, LODO, causal) — rare at this scale.
  • The base-probe protocol is a clean, reusable tool for any "inherent vs. installed" question.
  • Honest, paper-specific limitations section rather than boilerplate (non-CoT only; OLMo PostHoc unfillable; two BCT biases excluded).
  • Strong statistical hygiene: per-source random baselines, Wilcoxon tests, letter-balanced + unbiased-prompt controls, Qwen-base disclosed as outlier.
  • "Distinctness is a model property" is a genuinely useful negative result that disciplines future per-bias probing work.

− Limitations

  • Non-CoT only: bias direction reportedly weakens and becomes orthogonal under chain-of-thought decoding.
  • 7/9 benchmark coverage: Positional Bias and Are-You-Sure are format-incompatible and dropped.
  • No fine-grained attribution: which step (SFT vs RLHF vs DPO) installs the direction is explicitly not ablated.
  • Debiasing knob is modest and only compared to random directions, not to established mitigations.
  • Recovery–preservation window is uneven: Mistral and Llama have narrower operating ranges than Gemma and Qwen.
  • Qwen-base anomaly adds interpretive complexity to the base-vs-instruct origin story.

05 Questions for Further Exploration

Which post-training step (SFT, RLHF, DPO, or combos) is the primary installer of each per-bias direction? A controlled fine-tuning ablation across identical base checkpoints would answer this directly.
Does the per-bias direction transfer to chain-of-thought decoding, where reasoning chains are unfaithful? The CoT-pilot "weak and largely orthogonal" hint deserves a full study.
Can the debiasing knob beat 7–20% recovery with conditional / soft steering (gated by a bias-detection signal) without sacrificing selectivity?
Does the Qwen-base outlier reflect undocumented instruction-tuning in its checkpoint recipe? Would the 4-bias cluster survive a fresh, strictly-pretrained Qwen run?
How do these directions interact with truthfulness / refusal / true-false belief directions — orthogonal, or loaded onto a shared "follow the user's framing" axis?
Are the late-middle-layer peaks (relative depth 0.55–0.74) consistent at 70B / 405B scale, or does localization shift with depth?
Does bias-direction steering transfer across languages and modalities, or is the cue-specific direction English-MCQ-specific?
