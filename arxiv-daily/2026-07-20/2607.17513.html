02 Why this matters

A drop-in engineering rule for hierarchical domain-expert SLMs: confine Lorentz geometry to the loss layer, and batch size becomes a structural variable under hyperbolic supervision.

§ 01 Methodology 方法

  • Manifold & projection. Lorentz model H^d = {x ∈ R^(d+1) : ⟨x,x⟩_L = −1, x₀ > 0}; projection π(h) = exp₀(h/τ), τ = 2, with a ∥v∥ ≤ 2 clamp before the exponential map.
  • HySAT total loss. L = L_CE + λ·(L_PTLE + L_HLSD + L_HWC), project weights λ ≈ 0.02–0.05; LoRA rank r = 64 across all LoRA projects. HWC unifies PTLE attraction and HLSD sibling separation.
  • Autograd contract. Forward computes h in Rᵈ via the Euclidean adapter; π(h) only at loss eval; backward flows through bounded Jacobian ‖∂π/∂h‖_op ≤ cosh(4)/τ ≈ 5.23/τ. No Riemannian retraction.
  • Training protocol. bf16, gradient clip at norm 1.0, cosine LR + 3% warmup, plus a 250-step Euclidean→Lorentz ramp that prevents gradient-death at manifold init.
  • Matched 4-arm ablation. Qwen 2.5 7B-Instruct, 6 seeds (42/123/7/2024/99/777), identical base/optimiser/dropout/clipping/500-step budget/depth-4 toy ontology. Only placement × {hyperbolic, flat} varies.
  • Compute axis. Sample-efficiency (not GPU-hours) is the headline axis — platform-independent, backing the 1,139× scale claim.

§ 02 Results & scale 结果

  • Scale vs HypLoRA. ORAA single project = 11.39M FT samples = 1,139× HypLoRA Math-10K (67× Commonsense170K); six-project total 17.95M = 1,795× Math-10K.
  • Per-project anchors. BS Sovereign F1 = 0.855 (14-head Lorentz classifier); S3 Creative 95.8% LLM-judge win-rate / 266 rounds; ORAA 9/10 scholarly @ 4.8s; CEO Finance 30/30 vs GPT-4o (3-judge ensemble); MetaTeach 100% HWC activation; AdmitBrain tone 4.2/5 (84% match).
  • Batch-size regime (Proposition 2). ORAA b=2 → 0% PTLE / 52% HLSD; b=16 → 100% / 100%. AdmitBrain b=2 → 0% / 3.9%; b=16 → 100% / 100%.

§ 03 Strengths 优势

  • Clean theory. Proposition 1(i) gives an adapter-norm bound identical to Euclidean LoRA, with an explicit autograd contract — implementable in a few lines of PyTorch.
  • Honest negatives. A 17-incident failure ledger with one preserved raw divergence trajectory (AdmitBrain E2) and a crash-reproduction script. Rare and commendable.
  • Genuine scale. 17.95M fine-tuning samples and ~317K optimiser steps dwarf prior hyperbolic-LLM FT work (HypLoRA Math-10K = 10K).
  • Proposition 2 insight. Batch size reframed as a structural-learning-quality variable; actionable practitioner rule b* ≥ N/p̄.
  • Transparent disclosure. Competing interests, two Korean patent filings (KIPO 10-2026-0127815 / 0127826, filed 2026-07-12), and commercial affiliations all on the record.

§ 04 Limitations 局限

  • No matched baseline. No apples-to-apples comparison vs vanilla Std-LoRA + cross-entropy on the six projects at matched data + seed; the HySAT-attributable end-task gain is not isolated.
  • Toy ablation is negative. Held-out ρ_L does not separate HySAT from flat at toy scale; downstream advantage is asserted via deployed systems, not demonstrated under control.
  • Single seed. Per-project trajectories are seed-42 only; no within-project multi-seed variance is reported.
  • Shared data. Three of six projects share 149,998 meta-capability samples (Stage-2 CEO Finance = BS/ORAA/MetaTeach) — the cross-project activation matrix is not fully independent.
  • Gated access. Four of six projects sit under commercial gating — corpora, deployment prompts, and weights are not public; the 11.4M-sample ORAA run is documented by RunPod step logs, not full CSV archival.
  • "Law" is conditional. Adapter-on-manifold is rescued by curvature-aware optimisation, B-norm capping, or retraction + batch ≥ 32 — so placement is conditional on optimiser class, not categorical.

§ 05 Open questions for follow-up 待探究

  1. Does loss-only placement produce a measurable downstream gain over an identical Std-LoRA + cross-entropy baseline on a standard external benchmark (MMLU, BIG-Bench, GSM8K) at matched data and seeds?
  2. How does HySAT behave under a τ ∈ {1, 2, 4, 8} sweep — flagged by the authors as a "priority replication target" but never run?
  3. Can the Proposition 2 pair-formation threshold b* ≥ N/p̄ be verified on a public ontology (WordNet, MeSH) with held-out ρ_L actually separating HySAT from the flat control (which the toy ablation failed to show)?
  4. What is the wall-clock / throughput overhead of HySAT vs vanilla LoRA at matched sample count on identical hardware? The paper reports sample-efficiency but not controlled wall-clock.
  5. Does the placement claim generalise to fully-hyperbolic pretraining registers (HELM-style), or is it strictly a fine-tuning-register result?
