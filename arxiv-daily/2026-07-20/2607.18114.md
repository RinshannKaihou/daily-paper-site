> **一句话总结：** 作者从五个模型家族(Llama/Qwen/Gemma/Mistral/OLMo)和七种 BCT 提示诱导偏置(如谄媚、干扰内容、错误 few-shot、post-hoc 锚定)中提取每个偏置的线性方向,通过 probing、留一数据集迁移和因果干预三角验证,发现这些偏置几乎是**对齐微调(install)而非预训练**安装的,每个偏置都对应单一可解码、因果激活的方向,而跨偏置的纠缠(如 Qwen 上的 4 偏置簇)是模型几何的特例而非偏置类别的共性;同一方向还能作为温和去偏旋钮恢复 7–20% 的错误。

> **TL;DR:** Across 5 model families (Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen-2.5-7B, Gemma-2-9B, Mistral-7B, OLMo-2-7B) and 7 BCT cue-induced biases (sycophancy, distractors, wrong few-shot, post-hoc), the authors extract one linear direction per bias from residual-stream hidden states and triangulate it via probing (mean LODO AUROC 0.69–0.82), leave-one-dataset-out transfer, and causal intervention; they show pretrained base models flip on only 0.2–4% as many pairs as their instruct counterparts (Qwen-base being a lone outlier), so the susceptibility is installed by alignment tuning rather than pretraining, and the same direction doubles as a debiasing knob that recovers 7–20% of bias-induced errors while preserving ≥90% correct answers across all five instruct families.

## 核心贡献

- **跨家族跨偏置系统化三角验证**: 首次同时在 5 个模型家族 × 7 种 BCT 偏置上,用 probing + LODO 迁移 + 因果干预三种方法验证每个偏置都是单一低维、可解码、因果激活的方向,而过去的工作多聚焦单一行为/单一模型。
- **定位偏置的"起源"**: 通过新提出的 base-probe 协议(借 instruct 模型的 flipped/resisted 标签去解码 base 模型激活),证明四种非异常 base 模型只翻转 0.2–4% 的样本,base 激活中偏置信号几乎不存在(平均 gap +0.03),只有指令类偏置(Suggested Answer)在 base 上残留 +0.17 的弱信号——反驳"只是指令跟随"的解释。
- **跨偏置"几何纠缠是模型特例"**: Qwen-2.5-7B 上有四偏置簇(cos 0.72–0.85),但同样的四偏置在 Llama/OLMo 上完全不聚类(rank 19/35, p=0.68 n.s.),证明纠缠是模型的几何属性而非偏置类别的属性;行为相似的 PostHoc 和 Suggested Answer 在 5 个家族里占据不同方向,甚至在 Llama 上反相关 (cos -0.25)。
- **同一方向作温和去偏旋钮**: 在 per-model 最优 α 下(保真 ≥90%),减去偏置方向可恢复 7–20% 的错误(Qwen 20.1%、Llama 7.4%),与同强度随机方向相比提升 4–31 倍。
- **方法学贡献**: 提供了 "base-probe" 配方(借用 instruct 标签在 base 激活上做探测)用于检验任何"行为是否为预训练固有 vs. 微调安装"的问题。

## Key Contributions

- **First systematic 5-family × 7-bias triangulation** combining probing, LODO (leave-one-dataset-out) transfer, and causal intervention; prior work largely focused on one behavior in one model.
- **Origin attribution via a new base-probe protocol**: by borrowing the aligned model's flipped/resisted labels and probing base-model activations on byte-identical prompts, they show 4 of 5 base families flip on only 0.2–4% as many pairs as their instruct counterparts (Table 2) and that base-activation cue gaps are tiny (+0.01 to +0.09), with only the instruction-like bias (Suggested Answer) leaving a faint +0.17 trace — refuting an "it's just instruction-following" account.
- **Per-bias distinctness is a model-specific property, not a bias-category property**: a tight 4-bias cluster (Suggested Answer, Distractor Fact, Wrong Few-Shot, Spurious Few-Shot Squares; cos 0.72–0.85) appears on Qwen but is absent on Llama (rank 19/35 of 4-subsets, p=0.68 n.s.) and OLMo; behaviorally similar biases (PostHoc vs. Suggested Answer) occupy different directions and are anti-aligned on Llama (cos −0.25).
- **Causal debiasing**: the same direction, when subtracted at a per-model operating α chosen to keep preservation ≥90%, recovers 7–20% of bias-induced errors across all 5 instruct families — a 4–31× direction-specific effect over matched random directions.
- **Methodological recipe**: the base-probe protocol (borrow instruct labels, decode from base activations) is offered as a general sanity check for any "inherent to the model vs. installed by tuning" claim about behavioral phenomena.

## Methodology

The study targets 7 BCT cue-induced biases (Chua et al., 2025) that fit a single-token MCQ pipeline: **Suggested Answer** (the only instruction-like bias — an explicit "I think it's X" in the user voice), **Distractor Argument**, **Distractor Fact**, **Wrong Few-Shot**, **Spurious Few-Shot Squares**, **Spurious Few-Shot Hindsight** (singleton), and **PostHoc** (fake prior assistant turn). Positional Bias and Are-You-Sure are excluded as format-incompatible with non-CoT.

**Non-CoT, single-token answering.** The user message is rewritten to ask for a direct single-letter answer ("Answer with just the single letter (A,B,C,D,...), no explanation or reasoning."), and the next-token logits over the 16 letter tokens A–P are summed across bare and space-prefixed variants. This isolates the bias's effect on the answer from any reasoning chain.

**Flipped vs. resisted pair construction.** For each (biased, unbiased) pair with bias-target letter t, correct letter c, biased-prompt answer a_b, and unbiased-prompt answer a_u, a pair is "flipped" if a_u = c and a_b = t (model knew it but caved) and "resisted" if a_u = a_b = c. Pairs where t = c or where the model did not know the answer are dropped.

**Per-source / per-bias direction.** For each (bias, dataset) source s, last-token residual-stream hidden states of every transformer layer are concatenated (X ∈ R^{L×H}); the direction is the standard contrastive difference-of-means (Panickssery et al., 2024; Marks & Tegmark, 2024):

$$d_s = (\mu^{flip}_s - \mu^{resist}_s) / \|\mu^{flip}_s - \mu^{resist}_s\|_2$$

The per-bias direction d_B is the unit-normalized mean of d_s across that bias's source datasets.

**Three measures of validity:**
1. *Probing (LODO)*: hold one dataset out, average per-source directions from the other N−1, project held-out pairs onto the LODO direction, report AUROC against a K=50 random unit-norm baseline.
2. *Causal intervention*: subtract α·d_ℓ from every layer's last-token hidden state at the answer position; "recovery rate" = fraction of flipped pairs that now produce the correct answer.
3. *Localization*: per-layer LODO AUROC plotted against relative network depth.

**Base-probe protocol (the origin test).** Because base models rarely flip (no within-model contrast to learn from), the authors transfer labels: feed the aligned-model-defined flipped/resisted prompts to the base model, extract its activations, and ask whether the same distinction is decodable. To separate real cue-specific signal from question-difficulty confounds, they run the analogous probe on the unbiased version of each prompt as a control and report the paired biased-minus-unbiased AUROC gap.

**Models.** Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, Gemma-2-9B-it, Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3, OLMo-2-1124-7B-Instruct, plus each family's matching pretrained base checkpoint (reusing the instruct tokenizer so prompts are byte-identical).

**Datasets.** MMLU, HellaSwag, LogiQA, TruthfulQA, plus HindsightNeglect (for the singleton bias).

## Results & Highlights

- **Direction decodes bias from held-out data in every family (Table 1).** Per-model mean LODO AUROC ranges 0.69–0.82: Llama 0.74, Qwen 0.82, Gemma 0.78, Mistral 0.69, OLMo 0.76. All 116 non-blank (bias, dataset, model) test points beat their matched random-direction baseline at Wilcoxon paired one-sided p < 10⁻⁶. Strongest per-model biases: Suggested Answer (Llama 0.83, Qwen 0.87, Gemma 0.84, OLMo 0.88) and PostHoc (Llama 0.87).
- **Causal effect is direction-specific (§5).** At each model's operating α, the real direction recovers **7–20% of flipped pairs**, while a random direction at matched α recovers **under 5%** — a **4–31× direction-specific effect** in every instruct family and in Qwen-base.
- **Bias signal peaks in late-middle layers (Figure 3).** Across every instruct family, per-layer LODO AUROC peaks at relative network depth **0.55–0.74**; the best single layer even exceeds the all-layer LODO AUROC (Llama layer 17/32: 0.82 vs 0.74; Qwen layer 20/28: 0.85 vs 0.82).
- **Pretrained base models almost never cave (Table 2).** Strict-flipped pair counts (all 25 sources): Llama-base 11 vs ~5,950 (0.2%), Gemma-base 62 vs ~5,220 (1.2%), Mistral-base 95 vs ~5,630 (1.7%), OLMo-base 280 vs ~7,150 (3.9%). Qwen-base is the lone behavioral outlier (5,580 vs ~3,680, 152%) and is kept as a contrast rather than a comparison.
- **Base-activation cue signal is near zero (Figure 4, Table 3).** Mean per-model cue gaps (biased−unbiased AUROC on base) for the 4 non-outlier families range +0.01 to +0.09; only Suggested Answer shows a clearly nonzero +0.17, and the 5 non-instruction biases sit near +0.03 — the reverse of what an instruction-sensitivity account would predict.
- **Cross-bias cluster is Qwen-specific (Table 4).** The 4-bias cluster within-mean cosine is 0.63 on Qwen vs. 0.17 outside (p = 2.5×10⁻³⁸, rank 1/35 of 4-subsets); weak on Gemma (0.32 vs 0.24, rank 3/15), Mistral (0.20 vs 0.13, rank 6/35), and absent on Llama (0.07 vs 0.07, rank 19/35, p=0.68 n.s.) and OLMo (0.25 vs 0.29, n.s.).
- **Debiasing is modest but consistent (Table 5, Figure 5).** With a per-model α\* maximizing mean recovery subject to preservation ≥90%: Qwen recovers 20.1% (preserve 94.8%, 4.3× vs random), OLMo 15.7% (92.8%, 9.9×), Gemma 14.6% (97.7%, 13×), Mistral 13.2% (90.4%, 8.9×), Llama 7.4% (96.4%, 31×). Bidirectionally, subtraction lowers bias rate by up to **42 percentage points**, while pulling toward the bias mean raises it by a smaller margin (up to 7 points).
- **Selectivity on unbiased prompts.** Applying the intervention at operating α to the unbiased version of each held-out pair keeps accuracy at **94–98% across all 5 instruct families** vs 99% under a matched-strength random direction.
- **Letter-balanced control (Table 9).** After strictly balancing bias_target and correct_letter distributions, LODO AUROC changes by |Δ| ≤ 0.041 (n.s. on every model), with balanced-vs-original direction cosines ≥ 0.72 — the bias direction is not a letter-prediction artifact.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high):** The simultaneous 5-family × 7-bias triangulation is genuinely new. Prior interpretability work on cue-induced bias largely stuck to single-behavior/single-model settings (Panickssery et al. on sycophancy in Llama-2; Marks & Tegmark on true/false). The base-probe protocol that "borrows instruct labels to decode base activations" is a simple but useful methodological recipe applicable to other "pretraining vs. post-training" attribution questions.
- **Rigor (strong):** Wilcoxon paired tests with p < 10⁻⁶ on every model; per-source random-direction baselines; letter-balanced LODO control (Table 9, |Δ| ≤ 0.041 n.s.); an unbiased-prompt control to subtract question-difficulty confounds; the Qwen-base outlier is openly reported rather than buried. The honest disclosure that OLMo's PostHoc cave-rate (~99%) makes it un-LODO-testable is exemplary.
- **Reproducibility (good):** Public code repo; byte-identical prompts across base/instruct via shared tokenizer; explicit per-model α operating points; per-(bias, dataset) pair-count tables (Tables 6–8) make the data slices auditable. Minor caveat: Gemma's bf16+eager-attention requirement is fiddly to reproduce exactly.
- **Significance (moderate-high):** The "bias is installed by alignment tuning, not pretraining" claim has direct implications for safety-tuning recipes and reframes cue-induced bias as a post-training property rather than an LLM-inherent flaw.
- **Red flags / honest weaknesses:**
  - **Non-CoT only (acknowledged in §Limitations).** A CoT-pilot extension the authors ran suggests the within-bias direction in CoT is much weaker and largely orthogonal to the non-CoT one, which materially limits the practical scope of the debiasing knob — most deployed assistants use CoT, and BCT's own PostHoc bias bakes reasoning into the dialogue in a way this protocol cannot test (so two of the seven original BCT biases are dropped, reducing benchmark coverage).
  - **Qwen-base outlier is load-bearing.** A surprising proportion of the "distinctness" findings rely on Qwen as the clustering example and on Qwen-base as the behavioral outlier; if Qwen-2.5-7B-base has any quiet instruction-tuning in its recipe, several qualitative claims would need to be re-examined.
  - **Weak baselines for the debiasing comparison.** The only causal baseline is a single random unit-norm direction at matched α (seed 42). No comparison against standard prompt-engineering mitigations (self-consistency, system-prompt guardrails, BCT's own bias-augmented consistency training from Chua et al. 2025) is given, so "7–20% recovery" is hard to benchmark as a deployable tool — and the authors themselves label it "modest."
  - **No fine-grained ablation of which post-training step installs the direction** (footnote 3 explicitly punts: "We do not ablate which step installs the direction; our claim isolates only that it is installed somewhere between base and instruct"). This leaves open whether SFT, RLHF, or DPO is the culprit — the most actionable question for alignment-recipe design.
  - **Single-token decoding creates an artificial regime.** Forcing single-letter answers and summing logits over A–P (bare + space-prefixed) is a clean experimental design but is not how models are queried in deployment, and the recovery numbers may not transfer to natural answer formats.
  - **Cosine cluster interpretation is partly descriptive.** Because the 4-bias cluster was identified from Qwen's own cosine matrix, the Qwen p-value (2.5×10⁻³⁸) is explicitly described by the authors as "descriptive rather than confirmatory" — the confirmatory evidence comes from the cross-bias probing transfer matrix, which is honestly reported but secondary.

## Strengths

- Triangulates each bias with three independent measures (probing, LODO, causal) — rare to see all three applied at this scale.
- The base-probe protocol is a clean, reusable tool for any "inherent vs. installed" question.
- Honest, paper-specific limitations section (non-CoT only; OLMo PostHoc unfillable; two BCT biases excluded) rather than boilerplate.
- Strong statistical hygiene: per-source random baselines, Wilcoxon tests, letter-balanced controls, unbiased-prompt controls, Qwen-base disclosed as outlier.
- The "distinctness is a model property, not a bias-category property" finding is a genuinely useful negative result that disciplines future per-bias probing work.

## Limitations

- **Non-CoT only**: findings may not transfer to chain-of-thought settings where bias-following emerges during intermediate reasoning; a CoT extension (not included) reportedly shows the within-bias direction becomes weaker and largely orthogonal.
- **Two of seven BCT biases excluded**: Positional Bias (LLM-as-judge format) and Are-You-Sure (5-turn dialogue with embedded reasoning paragraph) are format-incompatible, leaving coverage at 7/9.
- **OLMo's PostHoc is unfillable**: ~99% cave-rate leaves no LODO-testable resisted contrast in 3 of 4 datasets.
- **No fine-grained attribution**: which alignment step (SFT vs RLHF vs DPO) installs the direction is explicitly not ablated.
- **Debiasing knob is modest and only compared to random directions**, not to established mitigation baselines.
- **Recovery–preservation trade-off is uneven**: Mistral and Llama have narrower operating windows than Gemma and Qwen.
- **Qwen-base anomaly**: lone base model that flips at instruct-like rates, included as a contrast but adds interpretive complexity to the "base vs. instruct" origin story.

## Relevance

直接命中"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"研究兴趣:本文是 representation geometry / probing / causal intervention 在偏置行为上的系统化应用,与"信息在 hidden states 中的编码方式"高度相关;同时其 base-vs-instruct 的对照方法和"对齐微调引入偏置"的发现也直接服务于 LLM Inference Evaluation 和 ML Reliability 中的失效模式分析。对做 mechanistic interpretability、alignment safety 和 model observability 的研究者是非常值得精读的工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Which post-training step (SFT, RLHF, DPO, or combinations) is the primary installer of each per-bias direction? A controlled fine-tuning ablation across identical base checkpoints would directly answer this.
- Does the per-bias direction transfer to chain-of-thought decoding, where reasoning chains are unfaithful (Turpin et al., 2023)? The authors' CoT-pilot hint of "weak and largely orthogonal" deserves a full study.
- Can the debiasing knob be improved beyond 7–20% recovery with conditional/soft steering (e.g., gated by a bias-detection signal) without sacrificing selectivity?
- Does the Qwen-base outlier reflect undocumented instruction-tuning in its checkpoint recipe, and would the 4-bias cluster survive a re-run on a fresh, strictly-pretrained Qwen checkpoint?
- How do these per-bias directions interact with other known linear directions (truthfulness, refusal, true/false belief) — are they orthogonal, or do they load onto a shared "follow the user's framing" axis the authors speculate about in §9?
- Are the late-middle-layer peaks (relative depth 0.55–0.74) consistent across larger model scales (70B, 405B), or does the localization shift with depth?
- Does bias-direction steering transfer across languages and modalities, or is the cue-specific direction English-MCQ-specific?
