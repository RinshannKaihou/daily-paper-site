> **一句话总结：** 针对光通信 OFDM 收发机中的 FFT 内核，本文提出基于值分布拟合的跨层协同设计方法，自动推导 11–12 比特自定义浮点格式（E4M6B8/E4M7B8），在 12 nm FinFET 工艺下相对定点 INT16 实现，FFT 核功耗最多降低 19.8%、面积最多降低 12.0%，同时在 64–4096-QAM 的 BER 性能上逼近 FP32 参考实现。

> **TL;DR:** The paper proposes a cross-layer co-design methodology that fits application-aware custom reduced-precision floating-point (FP) formats to FFT/IFFT kernels by analyzing empirical value distributions in a 128 Gbit/s optical OFDM transceiver (256-point FFT, 64/256/1024/4096-QAM, AWGN). On a 12 nm FinFET P&R flow, the fitted FP11 (E4M6B8) and FP12 (E4M7B8) FFT cores reduce power by up to 19.8% (8.2 mW → 6.5 mW vs. INT16) and area by up to 12.0% (0.027 mm² → 0.022 mm² vs. INT16) while keeping BER close to the FP32 reference, marking the first study of custom reduced-precision FP for FFT cores in optical OFDM transceivers.

## 核心贡献

- **基于值分布的数格式优化框架**：将 DSP 内核（FFT/IFFT）建模为可执行有向无环图（DAG），通过系统级仿真传播输入分布，提取各内部节点的经验值分布，迭代扫描指数位宽、尾数位宽与偏置，从而自动拟合应用感知的低精度自定义浮点格式（FP⟨1+b_e+b_m⟩，记为 E⟨b_e⟩M⟨b_m⟩B⟨B⟩）。
- **针对 128 Gbit/s 光 OFDM 收发机的 FFT IP 设计空间探索**：在 12 nm FinFET 工艺下，对单路径延迟反馈（SDF）radix-2² 流水线 FFT 架构完成 post-place-and-route 的功耗/面积/性能（PPA）分析，时钟目标 500 MHz，FFT 规模 N=256。
- **系统级 BER 验证**：基于 OptiCommPy 构建相干光 OFDM 仿真链（64/256/1024/4096-QAM，AWGN 信道，导频频域信道估计，单抽头 ZF 均衡），证明 11–12 比特自定义浮点可在精度逼近 FP32 的同时，在功耗与面积上超越定点实现。
- **首个面向光 OFDM 收发机 FFT 核的自定义低精度浮点研究**：作者明确指出，此前关于 OFDM FFT 的研究均基于定点算术，未探索自定义低精度浮点的硬件代价与通信性能联合权衡。

## Key Contributions

- A **value-distribution-based optimization framework** that represents DSP kernels as executable DAGs embedded in a system-level simulation, propagates representative input distributions to extract empirical per-node value ranges, and iteratively fits custom reduced-precision floating-point formats (parameterized by exponent width, mantissa width, and bias) to the observed distributions.
- A **detailed design-space exploration of a 256-point FFT IP core** targeting a 128 Gbit/s optical OFDM transceiver, with post-layout power/area results in a 12 nm FinFET technology using an industrial Synopsys flow at a 500 MHz target clock, built on a single-path delay feedback (SDF) radix-2² pipeline architecture.
- **System-level validation within the OFDM transceiver** using OptiCommPy, showing that fitted 11–12-bit custom floating-point formats preserve BER performance close to an FP32 reference while simultaneously reducing FFT-core power and area versus representative fixed-point baselines.
- The **first investigation** of custom reduced-precision floating-point arithmetic for FFT cores in optical OFDM transceivers, jointly considering hardware implementation cost (post-P&R PPA) and communication performance (BER vs. Eb/N0).

## Methodology

The paper proposes a **cross-layer co-design methodology** that links four layers:

1. **DSP kernel as DAG.** FFT and IFFT blocks are represented as directed acyclic graphs of real-valued arithmetic operations (radix-2 butterflies, further decomposed for radix-2²). The DAG is wrapped as a NumPy-compatible Python function parameterized by a format configuration F.
2. **Distribution-based number-format fitting.** Representative input vectors drawn from the OFDM application-level model are propagated through the DAG; empirical value distributions D_v are recorded at every internal node. Tail statistics (percentiles, max absolute values) are extracted and used to fit candidate formats. The custom floating-point format is FP⟨1+b_e+b_m⟩ = E⟨b_e⟩M⟨b_m⟩B⟨B⟩, where the real value is x = (-1)^S (1+m) 2^(e-B). The exponent width b_e sets dynamic range, the mantissa width b_m sets precision, and the bias B shifts the representable range. (be, bm, B) are jointly swept; IEEE-compliant features like exception handling are dropped to lower hardware cost.
3. **Arithmetic-unit instantiation.** The fitted formats are instantiated in an RTL microarchitecture: a parametrizable SDF radix-2² pipelined FFT IP. Fixed-point variants use signed two's-complement with stage-wise scaling (÷2 per butterfly stage); floating-point variants use the same format throughout the pipeline without scaling. Per-stage bit-width assignment for floating-point is left as future work.
4. **Joint PPA + accuracy evaluation.** All variants are synthesized and placed-and-routed in 12 nm FinFET using Synopsys Design Compiler and IC Compiler II. Post-P&R area and total power are reported. System-level accuracy is measured as BER vs. Eb/N0 (1000 OFDM frames, AWGN channel, pilot-based frequency-domain channel estimation, single-tap per-subcarrier ZF equalization) for QAM orders M ∈ {64, 256, 1024, 4096}, all relative to an FP32 reference. Accuracy is aggregated via mean (µ_∆) and maximum (∆_max) excess logarithmic BER, where ∆_i = log10(BER_F(SNR_i)) − log10(BER_T(SNR_i)).

The empirical fitting revealed that the FFT's stage-wise dynamic-range growth fits within a custom floating-point format with b_e = 4 and B = 8, leaving the remaining bit budget for the mantissa.

## Results & Highlights

- **Power reduction vs. INT16 baseline (Table II, 12 nm FinFET post-P&R):** FP11 (E4M6B8) achieves 6.5 mW vs. INT16's 8.2 mW — a **19.8% power reduction**; FP12 (E4M7B8) achieves 6.8 mW, a **17.1% power reduction**. The most aggressive fixed-point variant that still tracks FP32, INT14, is 7.6 mW — still higher than both FP candidates.
- **Area reduction vs. INT16 baseline:** FP11 reaches 0.022 mm² vs. INT16's 0.027 mm² — a **12.0% area reduction**; FP12 reaches 0.025 mm², a **7.4% area reduction**.
- **Accuracy (mean excess log10 BER, µ_∆, M=64):** FP12 = 0.0351 vs. INT16 = 0.0138 — i.e., FP12 stays within ~0.02 log10(BER) of the strongest fixed-point baseline while using fewer bits; FP11 = 0.0723 is comparable to INT14 (0.0665). At M=256, FP12 (0.0178) is essentially on par with INT16 (0.0190).
- **Worst-case excess log10 BER at M=4096 (∆_max):** FP12 = 1.0000 vs. INT16 = 0.9983 — both degrade comparably at the highest modulation order, while FP11 reaches ∆_max = 1.8921 (clearly insufficient at 4096-QAM), confirming that 12-bit FP is the minimum viable format across all tested QAM orders.
- **Pareto-frontier finding (Fig. 4):** Across all four QAM orders, FP11 and FP12 lie on the Pareto-optimal power-vs-accuracy and area-vs-accuracy frontiers, dominating all fixed-point variants (INT13–INT16) at comparable accuracy levels.
- **Format-fitting insight:** The required FFT dynamic range is fully covered by b_e = 4 exponent bits with bias B = 8; the BER-determining variable is the mantissa width b_m, with FP10 (E4M5B8) saturating early at high QAM orders.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine first-mover claim. The authors correctly position their contribution against prior OFDM-FFT work [13] which used adaptive-precision *fixed-point*, and against HLS custom-FP work [11], [12] which does not fold system-level metrics into format selection. The framing of (b_e, b_m, B) as jointly tunable application-aware variables with explicit bias B is a small but meaningful extension over standard BF16/FP8-style formats.
- **Rigor of evaluation:** Strong on the hardware side (real 12 nm post-P&R with industrial tools, mean and worst-case excess-log-BER aggregation across 4 QAM orders). The cross-layer fitting loop is reproducible from the description.
- **Weak baselines / caveats:**
  - The only fixed-point baselines are the authors' own INT13–INT16 on the same SDF architecture — there is **no comparison to vendor FFT IP cores (e.g., Xilinx/Intel FFT, Synopsys DesignWare)** or to the HLS custom-FP work the authors cite, so the "outperform fixed-point" claim is really "outperform our own fixed-point implementations of the same microarchitecture."
  - **Channel model is AWGN only.** Real coherent optical links face chromatic dispersion, PMD, laser phase noise, and fiber nonlinearities; the BER advantages observed under AWGN may not survive under realistic impairments, especially at 4096-QAM where the paper itself shows a BER floor.
  - **Equalization is single-tap ZF** — a minimal baseline; modern coherent OFDM uses MMSE with more taps. This understates the precision stress on the FFT path.
  - **Only 1000 OFDM frames** for BER estimation. For low target BER (e.g., 1e-3 to 1e-4 with FEC) this is marginal and produces noisy tail statistics; confidence intervals are not reported.
  - **Format fitting used M = 256 as a single global operating point** for all floating-point variants — the paper acknowledges this and notes that re-fitting per QAM order would likely widen the FP advantage, but this means the headline M = 64/1024/4096 comparisons are made against a format not optimized for them.
- **Reproducibility:** C++ DAG framework + Python wrapper around NumPy FFT interface, OptiCommPy as the simulator — all open-source or self-described; full reproducibility would require the RTL release, which is not promised.
- **Significance:** Practical and relevant for next-generation coherent optical SerDes / co-packaged optics, where pJ/bit is the dominant metric. The 19.8% / 12.0% PPA wins are meaningful at the IP-core level, though the paper correctly stops short of claiming full-transceiver throughput from a single SDF lane.
- **Red flags:** None severe. The "first investigation" claim is narrowly scoped to "custom reduced-precision FP for FFT in optical OFDM," which is defensible. The IEEE-submission disclaimer and funding disclosure are transparent.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated cross-layer framing that turns the ML-precision-reduction lesson into a disciplined DSP methodology.
- Honest reporting of both mean and worst-case excess log10 BER, including cases where FP11 clearly fails (4096-QAM, ∆_max = 1.89).
- Industrial-grade 12 nm post-P&R PPA, not just synthesis estimates.
- Pareto-frontier analysis (Fig. 4) is the right way to present multi-objective design trade-offs and shows FP11/FP12 dominating across all QAM orders.
- Format interpretation is intuitive: exponent covers dynamic range, mantissa controls BER — this is a reusable design rule.

## Limitations

- AWGN-only channel; no fiber impairments (CD, PMD, nonlinearities, laser phase noise).
- Single-tap ZF equalization; no MMSE or multi-tap baselines.
- Fixed-point baselines limited to the authors' own SDF implementation; no comparison to commercial or HLS-based FFT IP.
- 1000 OFDM frames is a small sample for low-BER regions; no confidence intervals.
- Floating-point formats fitted globally at M = 256 rather than per modulation order; per-stage bit-width assignment not yet explored.
- Results are for a single SDF lane; full 128 Gbit/s transceiver throughput would require multiple parallel lanes, so system-level energy/area extrapolation is left to the reader.
- No FEC integration, so the practically relevant coded-BER / SNR operating points are not evaluated.

## Relevance

对关注低精度算术、硬件-算法协同设计与 DSP/通信加速器的研究方向高度相关：本文将机器学习领域"低精度浮点"的成功经验系统化迁移到经典 DSP 内核，并通过 post-P&R 数据验证 PPA 收益，为后续在其他 DSP/通信 kernel（滤波、均衡、信道编译码）上推广提供了可直接复用的值分布拟合框架与基准。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the FP11/FP12 advantages hold under realistic coherent optical impairments (CD, PMD, laser phase noise, fiber nonlinearities) instead of AWGN, particularly at 1024- and 4096-QAM?
- Would per-stage floating-point bit-width assignment (the authors' stated future work) extend the Pareto gains, and by how much?
- How does the methodology transfer to other DSP kernels in the OFDM chain — channel estimation, MMSE equalization, FEC decoders — where dynamic-range profiles differ from the FFT?
- What happens when the format is re-fitted per QAM order; can the 4096-QAM BER floor of FP11/FP12 be removed without losing the PPA advantage?
- How do these custom-FP FFT cores compare against vendor FFT IP (e.g., Synopsys DesignWare, Intel/Xilinx harden FFT) on the same 12 nm node, rather than only against the authors' own fixed-point SDF core?
- With forward error correction integrated, what is the coded-BER / energy-per-bit trade-off, and does it change the FP-vs-fixed-point ranking?
