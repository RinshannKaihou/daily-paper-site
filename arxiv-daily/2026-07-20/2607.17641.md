> **一句话总结：** 作者提出 VRR-Stop 框架，用一个四参数噪声模型区分验证器的假接受/假拒绝与修复器的修复/破坏概率，再以"真值边际增益"的符号作为停修判据，并在低判别力场景下退化为 VRR-Guard 保守回退；在 GSM8K prompt-mismatch stress 设置下将最终正确率比固定 5 轮修复提升 60.6 个百分点，平均仅用 0.72 轮修复。

> **TL;DR:** VRR-Stop casts the "when to stop repairing in LLM agents" decision as a sign-identifiability problem under a four-parameter noise model (verifier false-accept ρ0, false-reject ρ1, repair-success α, repair-damage β); on a GSM8K / Qwen2.5-3B prompt-mismatch stress setting it lifts final true validity to 0.722 versus 0.116 for fixed 5-round repair (a +60.6 pp gain, McNemar p < 2×10⁻⁸⁵) using only 0.72 repair rounds on average, while the VRR-Guard fallback recovers validity from 0.223 to 0.793 on the near-zero-discrimination Llama-3-8B setting where calibrated stopping collapses.

## 核心贡献

- **四参数环路噪声模型**：明确区分验证器的两类错误（假接受率 ρ₀、假拒绝率 ρ₁）与修复器的两类转移（修复率 α、破坏率 β），并用 Youden's J = 1 − ρ₀ − ρ₁ 描述验证器判别力，首次把"多轮修复不一定单调改善真值"形式化为状态转移过程。
- **基于符号可识别性的停修判据**：将停修目标定义为单步真值边际增益 Gₖ = (1 − b̂ₖ)·α̂ − b̂ₖ·β̂，证明只需识别增益符号（而非精确参数恢复），并给出错误半径 Bₖ 下的符号可识别性条件 Ĝₖ − Bₖ > τ 或 Ĝₖ + Bₖ ≤ τ（Eq. 8）。
- **弱监督标定方法**：用 binomial-mixture EM 从无标签的重复验证记录估计 ρ̂₀、ρ̂₁，再用至多 300 个带标 before/after 修复对估计 α̂、β̂，全部用 5 折交叉拟合以避免标签泄漏。
- **保守回退 VRR-Guard**：当 J 接近 0、符号不可识别或分布漂移时，切换到 estimation-free 的"保留迄今最佳候选、仅在新候选验证票数超过当前 ≥ δ 时替换"策略；Lemma 1 用 Hoeffding 不等式给出错误替换概率界（δ=5, M=8 时单轮 ≤ 5.9×10⁻³）。
- **可识别性边界实证**：通过 J–∆ 网格扫描揭示符号翻转概率在低 J 且低 ∆ 时达 0.183，在高 J 或高 ∆ 时仅 0.014，相差约 13 倍，定位了停修可靠性的相变边界。

## Key Contributions

- A **four-parameter loop-noise model** that separates verifier false-acceptance (ρ₀) and false-rejection (ρ₁) from repairer fix-rate (α) and damage-rate (β), exposing that reported acceptance Āₜ can keep rising while true validity Qₜ peaks and then declines.
- A **sign-identifiability stopping rule** acting on the true marginal gain Gₖ = (1 − b̂ₖ)α̂ − b̂ₖβ̂ with a calibrated error radius Bₖ, requiring only the sign of the gain (not full parameter recovery) and proven (Proposition 1) to match the true action with probability ≥ 1 − η.
- A **weakly supervised calibration pipeline**: binomial-mixture EM over repeated verification counts (no labels) for ρ̂₀, ρ̂₁, plus ≤ 300 labeled pre/post-repair pairs for α̂, β̂, all under 5-fold cross-fitting so no instance's stopping decision touches its own label.
- An **estimation-free fallback, VRR-Guard**, with a Hoeffding-based bound (Lemma 1) on erroneous incumbent replacement, which restores validity when binomial-mixture EM degenerates at near-zero J.
- An **empirical identifiability map** over the J–∆ grid showing a ~13× gap in sign-flip rate between low-J/low-∆ (0.183) and high-J/high-∆ (0.014) regions, demonstrating that stopping reliability is governed jointly by verifier discrimination and decision margin, not by absolute estimation error.

## Methodology

**Setup.** A canonical verify-repair loop: initial plan p₀; in round k, M=8 independent verifier queries produce acceptance count Sₖ ∈ {0,…,M}; a policy π(Hₖ) ∈ {Commit, Repair} either commits pₖ or invokes the repairer to produce pₖ₊₁. The true validity yₖ ∈ {0,1} is unobservable at deployment (judged offline by exact match on GSM8K, symbolic verification on MATH-500, sandboxed unit tests on MBPP, and AST + executor on BFCL).

**Noise model (Eq. 3).** Four parameters treated as stable in a local decision window: ρ₀ = Pr(accept | invalid), ρ₁ = Pr(reject | valid), α = Pr(invalid → valid | repair), β = Pr(valid → invalid | repair), plus J = 1 − ρ₀ − ρ₁. Repeated queries are conditionally i.i.d. given yₖ; this supports a Bayesian belief update.

**Belief recursion (Eq. 5).** A predict-update filter tracks committed validity bₖ = Pr(yₖ=1 | Hₖ). The update combines the predictive prior b̄ₖ with the binomial likelihood of Sₖ under the two true-state hypotheses; the predict step is b̄ₖ₊₁ = (1−β)bₖ + α(1−bₖ).

**Stopping criterion (Eqs. 6–7).** Gₖ = b̄ₖ₊₁ − bₖ = (1 − bₖ)α − bₖβ. The fixed point b⋆ = α/(α+β) splits belief space into a repair-beneficial regime (bₖ < b⋆) and a repair-harmful regime (bₖ ≥ b⋆). τ=0 throughout (no cost term).

**Calibration.** Round-1 acceptance counts are fit with a two-component binomial mixture S ∼ π·Bin(M, 1−ρ₁) + (1−π)·Bin(M, ρ₀) via EM with random restarts; transition parameters come from ≤ 300 labeled before/after repair pairs from frozen trajectories that were generated without verifier gating (so transition samples are selection-bias-free). All estimates use 5-fold cross-fitting.

**Identifiability and fallback.** An error radius Bₖ is propagated through Eq. 5 and the gain expression; sign is identifiable iff Ĝₖ − Bₖ > τ or Ĝₖ + Bₖ ≤ τ (Eq. 8). When this fails, VRR-Guard tracks a champion c with acceptance count S(c) and replaces it only when S(p) ≥ S(c) + δ (δ=5). Lemma 1 bounds erroneous replacement via Hoeffding on paired differences with mean −J, giving worst-case five-round union bound ≤ 3%.

## Results & Highlights

- **GSM8K / Qwen2.5-3B prompt-mismatch stress (N=500):** VRR-Stop reaches true validity V = 0.722 [95% CI .682, .760] at ⟨K⟩ = 0.72 repair rounds, versus Fixed-K=5 at V = 0.116 [.088, .144], ⟨K⟩ = 5.00 — a paired gain of **+60.6 percentage points** (95% CI [+56.0, +65.0], McNemar p < 2×10⁻⁸⁵). The no-repair baseline is 0.700 and the TPM reference (true-parameter myopic) is 0.694, so VRR-Stop also slightly exceeds the diagnostic oracle (+2.8 pp).
- **Non-stationary loop dynamics (Fig. 4):** under a 256-token initial budget, validity starts at 0.45, peaks at 0.87 after 2 rounds (K⋆=2), then collapses to 0.12 by round 6 once a prompt mismatch is injected; the paired gain of the peak round over the final round is **+74.7 pp** [CI +69.3, +79.7]. An independent-resampling control with the same budget stays in [0.47, 0.85] throughout, isolating the decline to path-dependent rewriting rather than sampling noise.
- **Strong verifiers don't rescue repair (Appendix H):** with a process-reward-model verifier of J=0.805 on GSM8K, fixed 5-round repair still drives validity from 0.727 → 0.097; on MATH-500 (J=0.77) from 0.798 → 0.150, while TPM holds 0.798.
- **Damage is structural, not cherry-picked:** across eight settings, six show monotone validity decline with damage probabilities β ∈ [0.615, 0.938]; on the N=500 stress traces, **55% of instances see a correct plan repaired into an incorrect one**, and 24% of those damaging repairs win majority acceptance (raw-label statistics independent of any stopping rule).
- **Sign-flip geography (Fig. 5):** flip probability reaches 0.183 for J ≤ 0.15 and ∆ ≤ 0.10 versus 0.014 for J ≥ 0.4 or ∆ ≥ 0.30 — a ~13× gap. The Llama-3-8B verifier at J=0.03 collapses from the TPM reference 0.803 to calibrated-stopping 0.223 (−58.0 pp, McNemar p < 2×10⁻⁴⁸); its EM estimate of ρ̂₁ actually worsens from 0.27 (N=120) to 0.077 (N=300) against a true 0.609, illustrating the likelihood-surface flattening mechanism.
- **VRR-Guard recovery under calibration failure / shift (Table 2):** on Llama-3-8B stress, VRR-Guard restores validity to 0.793 versus failed VRR-Stop at 0.223 (**+57.0 pp**, McNemar p < 1×10⁻⁴⁷) and Fixed-5 at 0.057 (+73.7 pp). Under Qwen-7B model shift, Fixed-5 drops from 0.875 (no-repair) to 0.075 while VRR-Guard holds 0.875. MATH-500 and BFCL also see Guard preserve the no-repair level (0.798, 0.810) where Fixed-5 collapses to 0.150 / 0.372.
- **Cost-effectiveness:** VRR-Stop uses ⟨K⟩ = 0.72 versus 5.00 for fixed-budget repair, i.e. roughly 7× fewer repair calls while delivering higher validity.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high).** Framing stopping as a sign-identifiability problem under a 4-parameter noise model — and tying decision reliability to Youden's J jointly with the decision margin ∆ rather than to absolute parameter error — is a genuinely fresh angle that is absent from Self-Refine, Reflexion, CRITIC, SCoRe, and from selection-style work like HedgeTune and ROC-n-reroll. The separation of verifier noise from repairer noise is the load-bearing insight.
- **Rigor (mostly strong, with caveats).** Five-fold cross-fitting prevents label leakage; B=10,000 bootstrap CIs and exact McNemar tests are reported throughout; the TPM reference and hindsight-best-round diagnostics honestly isolate what calibrated stopping can and cannot achieve. However, the headline GSM8K "stress" result depends on an author-injected prompt-mismatch perturbation (numeric/condition corruptions fed only to the repairer) — this is a constructed adversarial setting, not a naturally occurring deployment failure, and the +60.6 pp headline should be read as "under an induced non-stationary fault" rather than "on standard GSM8K." The favorable (non-stress) setting is the only one of eight where repair monotonically helps, which is itself revealing.
- **Reproducibility (good).** Anonymous repo with seeds, prompts, and a raw-data manifest; calibration uses ≤ 300 labeled transitions; the Llama failure mechanism (EM likelihood flattening at J→0) is diagnosed in detail with both synthetic grids and real verifiers. The honest caveat that LLM trajectories are not bitwise reproducible is appropriately handled via frozen-trajectory replay.
- **Significance (moderate-to-high for the agent-stopping subfield).** The clearest demonstration to date that fixed-round repair budgets are unsafe when the repairer can damage valid plans, with a deployable fallback. Likely to influence test-time-scaling and self-correction system design.
- **Red flags / weaknesses.**
  1. **The eight-setting evaluation is narrow in model coverage** — only Qwen2.5, Mistral-7B, Llama-3-8B, with several configurations sharing tasks/samples; the authors themselves flag this ("family-conditional patterns … not as universal laws").
  2. **Binary validity assumption** cannot represent partial correctness, multi-error plans, or staged long-horizon goals — a real limitation for agent tasks beyond math/code/tool-use short-horizon benchmarks.
  3. **The strict condition α + β ≤ 1 is violated on the GSM8K stress trajectory** (β=0.786 alone), so the stationarity assumption is admittedly broken in the very setting used for the headline result; the paper handles this honestly but the local-stationarity approximation is doing real work.
  4. **No systematic ablations of verification budget M or gain threshold τ were run** — left to future work — so the cost/accuracy trade-off curve is under-explored.
  5. **The TPM reference is a useful diagnostic but is not a globally optimal policy** over the finite horizon; the paper's framing occasionally blurs "VRR-Stop slightly beats TPM" with "VRR-Stop is near-optimal," when in fact post-hoc best-round selection on the stress trace scores 0.867, far above VRR-Stop's 0.722.

## Strengths

- Principled, falsifiable theory: the sign-identifiability condition (Eq. 8) and the J–∆ identifiability map give a concrete, testable boundary for when calibrated stopping works.
- Honest self-assessment: VRR-Guard is explicitly described as a "conservative fallback close to the no-repair baseline," with no claim of strictly dominating no-repair — the authors own the 2.0 / 0.3 pp regressions on Mistral and BFCL.
- Cross-family generality of failure modes: the appendix distinguishes under-estimated false-acceptance (Qwen, Δρ₀ from −0.16 to −0.31), under-estimated false-rejection (Llama, ~0.53), and high true false-acceptance (Mistral ~0.71), warning against transferring calibration across families.
- Cost-aware headline: 0.72 vs 5.00 average repair rounds is a concrete deployment win even setting validity aside.

## Limitations

- **Local-stationarity approximation.** ρ₀, ρ₁, α, β are treated as stable within a decision window; α + β ≤ 1 is violated on GSM8K stress and round-varying αₜ, βₜ are explicitly deferred to future work.
- **Binary validity only.** No mechanism for partial correctness, multiple error types, or staged long-horizon plans; extending to multi-level state is open.
- **Narrow model and task coverage.** Three model families with overlapping samples; conclusions are family-conditional, not universal laws.
- **Deterministic verifiers break the EM calibration** (no stochastic observations under repeated queries) — ρ₀, ρ₁ must then be estimated directly from labeled samples.
- **VRR-Guard's conservatism has an opportunity cost** (0.810 vs 0.875 in the favorable setting) and carries no theoretical guarantee of strictly dominating no-repair.
- **Untuned hyperparameters left for future work:** no systematic sweeps of M or τ.

## Relevance

与 LLM Agent 自我纠错/验证-修复环路（verify-repair loop）以及 test-time scaling 的研究方向高度相关：VRR-Stop 直接回答了"多轮反思、Reflexion、CRITIC 何时该停"这一长期被忽视的安全性问题，对构建可靠的 Agent 推理流水线（如多步工具调用、代码生成、数学推理的自修复机制）具有直接的方法论和工程价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the four-parameter noise model still hold under round-varying repair operators (αₜ, βₜ), and can an online change-point detector locate the interior peak K⋆ that VRR-Stop currently misses (0.722 vs post-hoc 0.867 on the stress trace)?
- How does VRR-Stop behave when verifier queries are correlated rather than conditionally i.i.d. — would a beta-binomial belief update replace the binomial mixture without breaking identifiability?
- Can the binary validity state be extended to multi-level plan quality (partial correctness, distinct error types) while preserving a single-threshold stopping boundary, or does multi-level state fundamentally require a different decision rule?
- How transferable are calibrated ρ̂₀, ρ̂₁, α̂, β̂ across model families — the paper shows family-conditional biases in opposite directions; is there a meta-calibration that adapts on the fly?
- On real-world agent deployments (non-adversarial distribution shift rather than injected prompt mismatch), how often does the repair-harmful regime actually arise, and does VRR-Guard's near-no-repair conservatism make it the default-safe choice in production?
