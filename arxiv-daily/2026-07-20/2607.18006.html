MADA-RL: Multi-Agent Debate-Aware Reinforcement Learning for Parameter-Efficient Reasoning in Compact Models

Key finding

A counterfactual critic advantage over GRPO+LoRA lifts compact 1.5B math reasoning +2.0 pts (p<0.001) at ~1/16 the trainable params.

Martino M. L. Pulici, Cuong Xuan Chu, Evgeny Kharlamov, Zifeng Ding, Volker Tresp, Yunpu Ma
Mon Jul 20, 2026

critic improvement rate — highest of any model
MADA-RL critics correct the generators' wrong consensus on 19.6% of questions vs. 16.9–19.3% for baselines — evidence the gain comes from learned corrective behaviour, not extra deliberation volume.
Why this matters
A clean data point for anyone extracting strong multi-step reasoning from sub-4B LLMs under tight training budgets — and for the trainable-parameter vs. inference-deliberation trade-off in multi-agent debate.
Critical edge
At 33,818 tokens/question (~10× a forward pass, joint-highest of any model), MADA-RL has not been shown to beat training-free debate / self-consistency / LLM-as-judge at matched inference compute — the comparison that matters for deployment.
01

Methodology — GRPO + LoRA, with a counterfactual twist

STAGE 1 · GENERATORS

N=3 generator LoRA adapters trained independently on disjoint 7,500-problem subsets of Still-3-Preview-RL-Data. Composite reward RG = 2·Racc + Rlen: symbolic-equivalence correctness (LaTeX→SymPy) plus a length reward in [−0.5,+0.5] that floors wrong answers at 0 to block reward hacking.

STAGE 2 · CRITICS · the novelty

A debate-aware dataset D′ records all generators' answers and the per-instance generator accuracy accG. M=3 critic LoRA adapters are then trained with the counterfactual advantage →

INFERENCE · debate protocol

3 generators + 3 critics + 2 debate rounds. Parallel generation, then sequential critic revision conditioned on prior answers. The authors deliberately omit Subramaniam et al.'s summarizer to keep it simple and memory-light.

03

Strengths & limitations — side by side

Strengths

  • Conceptually clean contribution — the counterfactual critic advantage is interpretable and has a mechanism-level signature (highest critic improvement rate).
  • Parameter-efficient — largest gain-per-trainable-parameter of all evaluated models; ~16× fewer trainable params than full-FT leaders.
  • Honest framing — explicitly states it does not beat data-heavy baselines and reframes the comparison as cost-matched.
  • Strong statistical hygiene — Welch t-tests, CIs, full pairwise matrices, LaTeX→SymPy symbolic reward.
  • Promised open release of code, LoRA adapters, and evaluation scripts.

Limitations

  • Central ablation not significant — counterfactual advantage drop is p=0.095 at 10 seeds; the case rests on the 19.6% mechanism metric.
  • Heavy inference cost — 33,818 tokens/question, 6 agent calls; no matched-inference-budget comparison vs. training-free debate / self-consistency.
  • Single base model, single domain — only DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B on math; no 4B scaling, no science/planning transfer.
  • Per-benchmark / paired tests, bootstrap intervals, multiple-comparison correction not applied — significance is on cross-benchmark per-seed averages.
  • Adding agents requires retraining the entire pipeline (disjoint data-partition design); diversity ablation non-significant (p=0.166).
04

Open questions for further exploration

Q1 · the missing comparison

How does MADA-RL compare to self-consistency, training-free debate, and LLM-as-judge at matched 33,818-token inference budgets on the same base model?

Q2 · domain transfer

Does the counterfactual advantage generalise to non-math domains (code, science QA, planning) where symbolic-equivalence rewards are unavailable?

Q3 · scale

What happens at 4B–7B scale, and does the gain-per-parameter story survive when the base model is already stronger?

Q4 · partitioning

Can the disjoint-data partition be replaced with shared or overlapping subsets so that adding agents does not require retraining from scratch?

Q5 · training dynamics

Does the counterfactual advantage reduce gradient variance or stabilise training vs. standard GRPO? (Flagged by the authors as future work.)

Q6 · distillation

Could the multi-round debate be distilled back into a single forward pass to recover the inference-cost penalty while keeping the corrective behaviour?
