Report: GPT-5
Literature library (PaperCards). Curated papers are pre-summarised into structured cards (task / models / methods / settings / limits) and embedded for retrieval — not raw PDF RAG. The Query Generator fetches ≤3 cards per query, so hypotheses track peer-reviewed method rather than LLM-internal knowledge. Metric suite: SIRH (hypothesis-level success), SIRI (image-level, fair vs single-shot baselines), EII (earliest successful iteration), SER (code execution rate), Nov(H) (novelty vs nearest PaperCard).

Strengths & Limitations

side-by-side

Strengths

  • Principled metric suite (SIRH / SIRI / EII / SER / Nov(H)) reusable by future agent-based evaluators.
  • Literature-grounded generation via PaperCards is a real advance over LLM-internal-knowledge probing.
  • Broad robustness — 6 erasure methods, 3 strengths, 2 backbones, 3 size configs, plus cross-domain LLM-jailbreak transfer.
  • Honest cost reporting — $/successful-hypothesis plus an explicit Small / Standard / Large trade-off.
  • Strong gains on the hardest, safety-relevant categories (IP, explicit) where existing tools are near 0%.

Limitations

  • Costly — $0.081/iteration and 5+ frontier LLM calls; impractical for real-time auditing without STACE-Small.
  • Code Synthesiser is the reliability bottleneck — SER drops to 71–73% on ESD-500 / MACE; fails on changing external APIs.
  • Evaluator dependence — NudeNet + Qwen2.5-VL-32B @ 80% conf; no sensitivity analysis, no false-positive-rate estimate.
  • Library is manually curated from a single survey (Liu et al. 2024); should be automated.
  • Nov(H) is a cosine heuristic, not validated against human novelty judgements.
  • Diffusion-only coverage (SD-v1.4, SDXL); no FLUX or rectified-flow transformers.
  • Gains concentrate in Style/IP/Explicit; on easy Object concepts STACE loses to simpler baselines.

Open questions

for further exploration
  1. How does SIRI/SIRH move if the VLM evaluator confidence threshold is swept (50 / 70 / 90%) — and what is the empirical false-positive rate on known-erased vs known-present controls?
  2. What is STACE's per-dollar performance (successful hypotheses / $1) vs simply scaling a cheaper baseline like JailFuzzer to the same budget?
  3. Does the PaperCard library transfer across domains — T2I → T2I-video, or diffusion → rectified-flow (FLUX) — and how much does curation drive absolute SIRH?
  4. Can the Hypothesis Generator's outputs be clustered into a taxonomy of failure modes rather than a single score, enabling targeted fixes?
  5. Is the M=2 debate-round peak robust to a stronger Critic (e.g., Grok-4.20-beta), or would a single high-quality Critic make M=1 sufficient and cheaper?
