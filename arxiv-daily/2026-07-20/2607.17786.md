> **一句话总结：** 本文系统测试了三种覆盖"无推理/文本CoT/隐式迭代推理"谱系的 VLA 模型在 LIBERO 与 SimplerEnv 上对视觉、推理、动作三阶段扰动的鲁棒性，发现隐式迭代模型 RD-VLA 最为脆弱（高斯噪声下成功率崩塌至 14.8%、PGD-10 下为 0.0%），且脆弱性是结构性而非随深度累积，同时基于推理输出的运行时监测器在自适应攻击下全部失效。

> **TL;DR:** This preprint stress-tests whether explicit reasoning helps a Vision-Language-Action (VLA) policy absorb perturbations by attacking three models that span the reasoning spectrum (OpenVLA-OFT no-reasoning, DeepThinkVLA text-CoT, RD-VLA latent-iterative) at the vision/reasoning/action stages on LIBERO and SimplerEnv. The latent-iterative RD-VLA is by far the most fragile: it collapses to 14.8% SR under Gaussian sensor noise (σ=0.2) and 0.0% under white-box PGD-10 (ε=8/255) versus DeepThinkVLA 92.7%/49.8% and OpenVLA-OFT 89.0%/18.2%, and this fragility is structural (K-invariant, ρ(12)/ρ(8)=1.005 vs the multiplicative-prediction 2.0) rather than depth-driven. The authors also show that two widely studied behavioral runtime probes (plan-action consistency and action-anomaly) collapse under Carlini-standard adaptive attack (consistency AUC 0.996→0.493) and that, under matched-FPR calibration, no fusion of them raises defended SR above undefended SR on any PGD-10 cell — establishing a ceiling rather than proposing a defense.

## 核心贡献

- **跨阶段攻击矩阵：** 首次在同一威胁模型下系统扰动 VLA 流水线的视觉、推理、动作三个阶段，同时覆盖三种推理范式（无推理 OpenVLA-OFT、文本 CoT 的 DeepThinkVLA、隐式迭代 RD-VLA），填补了既有工作只攻击单一阶段或单一架构的空白。
- **结构性脆弱的因果定位：** 通过推理深度 K∈{4,8,12} 的扫参实验证伪了"放大随深度乘性累积"的标准 Lipschitz 直觉（实测 ρ(12)/ρ(8)=1.005，远小于预测的 2.0），将放大器定位在编码器与迭代循环的不动点输出，而非每次迭代的累乘。
- **推理即检测的负结果：** 在 Carlini 自适应评估标准下，plan-action consistency probe 在 stealth 攻击下 AUC 从 0.996 崩塌到 0.493（chance level）；在 matched-FPR 校准下，文本一致性 probe 与 action-anomaly probe 的任意凸组合都不能在任何 PGD-10 单元上让被防御成功率高于未防御成功率。
- **诚实的边界界定：** 将结论限定为"输出级行为 probe 在白盒视觉攻击下的上界"，并明确指出三类未覆盖方向（中间表示 probe、训练时干预、非白盒威胁模型），把"ceiling"表述为防御工作的前置条件而非 no-go 定理。
- **严格的统计与协议规范：** 全程 N=12 配对单元（4 suites × 3 seeds）、Wilcoxon 符号秩检验 + Holm-Bonferroni 校正、paired bootstrap CI、EOT/gradient-free Square Attack 排除梯度遮蔽假象，是 VLA 鲁棒性文献中少见的严谨协议。

## Key Contributions

- **Cross-stage attack matrix across three reasoning paradigms:** A single threat model perturbing vision, reasoning, and action stages on OpenVLA-OFT (single-pass), DeepThinkVLA (text CoT), and RD-VLA (latent iterative K=12) — the first study to sweep stages × paradigms together (the paper's Table 1 explicitly contrasts this with prior narrower-slice work).
- **Falsification of the depth-cumulative amplification hypothesis:** An inference-time K-sweep on RD-VLA shows the per-input amplification ratio is K-invariant (ρ(12)/ρ(8)=1.005) versus the multiplicative L^K_iter prediction of 2.0, localizing the amplifier in the encoder and the recurrence's fixed-point output rather than per-iteration compounding.
- **An adaptive-eval ceiling for two behavioral probe families:** Under Carlini-standard adaptive attack and matched-FPR calibration, the plan-action consistency probe collapses from AUC 0.996 to 0.493 (chance), and no convex fusion of consistency and Mahalanobis action-anomaly probes raises defended SR above undefended SR on any PGD-10 cell — established as a precondition for future defenses, not a no-go theorem.
- **Honest scope and confound controls:** Four confound controls (shared backbone, scale, action head, CoT ablation) attribute the 9.9× RD-VLA-vs-OFT SR gap at σ=0.2 to the within-Prismatic recurrence; the paper is explicit that N=3 models means paradigm-level claims are also model-level observations requiring within-paradigm replication.
- **Methodologically clean adversarial-ML hygiene:** EOT (T=2) and gradient-free Square Attack (100 queries) are used to rule out gradient masking as an explanation for the OFT FGSM-vs-PGD-10 asymmetry, leaving loss-surface curvature on the MLPResNet head as the residual.

## Methodology

**Pipelines and threat model.** A VLA maps observation o_t and instruction ℓ to a 7-DOF action a_t = f_a(f_r(f_v(o_t)), ℓ). The three models are: OpenVLA-OFT (~7B, Prismatic + MLPResNet action head, no reasoning stage; LIBERO clean SR 96.5%); DeepThinkVLA (~3B, PaliGemma, discrete action tokens at 2,048 bins/DOF, generates a textual CoT before the action; clean SR 93.0%); RD-VLA (0.5B, Prismatic backbone with K=12 weight-tied recurrent transformer layers and a linear 7×896 action projection; clean SR 89.2%). The threat model is a stage-s adversary with budget ε that perturbs x̃_s = x_s + δ_s either deterministically (∥δ_s∥_p ≤ ε, FGSM/PGD-class) or stochastically (δ_s ~ N(0, σ²I)) at the vision (δ_v), reasoning (δ_r, DT-only entity-swap CoT), or action (δ_a, 7-DOF Gaussian, gripper excluded) stage.

**Attack matrix.** Vision: FGSM ε∈{2,4,8,16,32}/255, Gaussian σ∈{0.01,…,0.2}, and PGD-10 (L∞, step ε/4) at ε∈{4,8,16}/255 (canonical 8/255 in headline tables). White-box PGD through RD-VLA's K=12 recurrence is enabled by activation checkpointing. RD-VLA FGSM cells are reported as DT→RD-VLA transfer lower bounds. Square Attack (100 queries) is added as a gradient-free check. Reasoning: entity-swap CoT corruption (the task object in the generated CoT is replaced with a random alternative from LIBERO's 29-object vocabulary), DT only. Action: additive Gaussian on the 7-DOF output, σ∈{0.01,…,1.0}.

**Evaluation protocol.** Each condition is evaluated on all 4 LIBERO suites × 3 seeds (42, 123, 456), giving N=12 matched (suite, seed) cells; each cell averages 50 episodes (10 tasks × 5 trials), capped at 300 steps/episode and scored by LIBERO's built-in task check. Cross-benchmark sanity uses SimplerEnv on public OpenVLA-7B (clean SR 55.0% on Google-Robot non-floor), and cross-class consistency uses LIBERO-Plus's seven naturalistic-perturbation factors. Statistics use paired Wilcoxon signed-rank tests with Holm-Bonferroni correction across the 13-test family and 95% paired-bootstrap CIs (n=10,000).

**K-sweep falsifier.** The amplification ratio ρ(K) = ∥∆a∥₂/ε is measured at K∈{4,8,12} on the same episodes; K=4 is OOD (clean SR 72.8% vs 89% at K=12), so the K-invariance claim rests on the K∈{8,12} range. The multiplicative hypothesis is ρ(K) ≈ L^K_iter, which would give ρ(12)/ρ(8)=2.0 at the back-solved L̂_iter=1.192; the observed 1.005 falsifies it.

**Probes and adaptive evaluation.** The plan-action consistency probe s = w_e·S_entity + w_d·S_direction + w_g·S_gripper + w_p·S_parse (weights 0.5/0.2/0.15/0.15, signal carried by the entity term) is evaluated against a four-rung adaptive ladder (naive → direction-fix → stealth entity re-injection → full adaptive). The stage-fused guard s_t = α(1−ϕ_t) + (1−α)ψ_t combines the consistency probe ϕ_t with the Mahalanobis action-anomaly ψ_t (empirical-CDF rank among clean rollouts), with threshold τ leave-one-seed-out calibrated to an 8% clean episode-level FPR at every α, and is additionally tested against a checker-aware PGD-10 with a differentiable action-anomaly penalty −λ d_k(â_t)² (λ∈{0,1,10}).

## Results & Highlights

- **Gaussian vision noise σ=0.2 (LIBERO, N=12):** RD-VLA collapses to **14.8% SR** (Δ=−74.3pp) while DeepThinkVLA holds at **92.7%** and OpenVLA-OFT at **89.0%** — the DT-vs-OFT gap (Δ=+3.7pp) is inconclusive (paired Wilcoxon, Holm-Bonferroni p=0.30), but both differ from RD-VLA at p<0.01 (d=6.5).
- **White-box PGD-10 ε=8/255 (LIBERO, N=12):** A strict three-tier ordering **DT 49.8% > OFT 18.2% ≫ RD-VLA 0.0%**, with RD-VLA reaching 0.0% SR at both ε=4/255 and ε=8/255 and a per-step ∥∆a∥₂ of 1.536 that is ε-invariant in [4,8]/255 (≤2% relative change), indicating the recurrence has already saturated past its collapse threshold.
- **K-sweep falsification (RD-VLA, ε=8/255, N=12 per K):** ρ(12)/ρ(8) = **1.005** vs the multiplicative-prediction **2.0**; free-intercept fit ρ(K)=48.507·1.0007^K (MAFE 0.1%) confirms K-invariance, locating the amplifier in the encoder + recurrence fixed-point rather than per-iteration compounding.
- **Plan-action consistency probe (DT, N=300/cond.):** AUC against naive entity-swap is **0.996** but collapses to **0.493** (chance) under stealth entity re-injection and **0.259** under full adaptive attack; in live rollouts stealth is as task-disruptive as naive (−8pp SR) but the probe's flag rate drops from 98.9% to 5.5%, indistinguishable from the 6.3% clean FPR.
- **Vision-stage attacks defeat the probe intrinsically:** detection AUC ≤ 0.692 across all FGSM levels and **0.463 under Gaussian σ=0.2**; the clean-vs-attacked mean shift is bounded above by 1−φ̄_clean = 0.133 (empirically φ̄_clean=0.867±0.025 on N=200 DT episodes), with the maximum observed shift of 0.022 well inside the bound.
- **Stage-fused guard (DT, PGD-10 ε=8/255, N=600/cell, matched-FPR sweep):** Defended SR stays below undefended SR (49.8%) at every α — text-only α=1 recovers 48.8% (Δ=−1.0pp), stage-fused α=0.5 recovers 42.2% (Δ=−7.7pp), action-only α=0 recovers 42.2% (Δ=−7.7pp); fusion is worse than its own text-only ablation.
- **Without matched-FPR calibration the same monitor falsely appears to work:** τ=0.7 with no leave-one-seed-out calibration shows a spurious +24.6pp pooled improvement while abstaining on 40–67% of clean episodes (5–8× the 8% target) — the paper explicitly excludes this mis-calibrated comparison from the headline.
- **Checker-aware adaptive PGD-10 (α=0.5, DT):** worst-case defended SR drops to **46.0% on libero_object** and **50.7% on libero_spatial**, both below undefended on the same attack, with detection AUC falling from 0.895 (λ=0) to 0.669 (λ=10) on object.
- **FGSM re-ordering under PGD-10 is not gradient masking:** EOT at T=2 narrows the OFT FGSM-vs-PGD-10 gap to ≤4pp on 3/4 suites and gradient-free Square Attack at 100 queries leaves OFT at 95.5% SR, consistent with loss-surface curvature on the MLPResNet head rather than obfuscated gradients.
- **SimplerEnv cross-architecture sanity (OpenVLA-7B, Google-Robot non-floor):** the OFT FGSM/PGD-10 asymmetry replicates — PGD-10 exceeds FGSM on every task by mean 17.2pp — but only one architecture has a Bridge/Fractal-compatible checkpoint, so this is treated as a single-architecture sanity check rather than cross-model replication.
- **LIBERO-Plus cross-class consistency:** the RD-VLA-vs-{DT,OFT} paired ΔSR gap exceeds 30pp on six of seven naturalistic-perturbation factors; the exception is robot-initial-state, where the RD-vs-OFT gap collapses to +9.6pp and all three models degrade comparably (ΔSR=−57.5 vs −67.1pp), bounding the structural-amplifier reading.

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are Tuan Duong Trinh (corresponding), Naveed Akhtar, and Basim Azam from the University of Melbourne. The work is explicitly positioned as a preprint (arXiv:2607.17786v1, cs.RO, 20 Jul 2026). The closest prior work, Trinh et al. 2026 (text-CoT entity-swap on DeepThinkVLA), is by the same lead author, giving the team direct prior experience with the consistency-probe methodology they now extend and stress-test. The methodology imports the canonical Carlini adaptive-evaluation standard, Holm-Bonferroni multiple-testing correction, paired bootstrap CIs, EOT, and a gradient-free Square Attack sanity check — the adversarial-ML hygiene is above the median for the VLA-safety subfield.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel as a *systematic* cross-stage × cross-paradigm robustness study; the K-sweep falsification of the multiplicative-amplification hypothesis is a crisp, paper-specific mechanistic result that does not apply generically. The framing as a "ceiling for behavioral output-level probes" is well-scoped rather than overclaimed.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Adaptive evaluation follows Carlini et al.; statistics use paired tests with Holm-Bonferroni correction and bootstrap CIs; the matched-FPR calibration discipline is the paper's signature methodological contribution and is what flips the headline from "fused monitor gives +24.6pp" (the naïve, miscalibrated view they deliberately exclude) to "no fusion beats undefended."
- **Reproducibility:** Mostly good but incomplete by design. Compute, seeds, ε grid, and checkpoint HuggingFace IDs are reported; code is promised "upon publication" rather than released with the preprint. Per-step ψ_t values needed to re-run the stage-fused guard were stored only for DT, not OFT, so transfer of the guard to OFT is explicitly future work — a concrete data-availability gap.
- **Significance:** High within its niche. The result that the latent-iterative reasoning paradigm (RD-VLA) is the *least* robust directly inverts the safety intuition that "reasoning before acting absorbs perturbations," and is actionable for anyone choosing a VLA reasoning architecture for deployment.
- **Red flags / overclaiming risks:**
  - **N=3 models, one per paradigm.** The authors are admirably explicit that "every paradigm-level statement is therefore a model-level observation," but the central narrative ("how the reasoning stage is built, not whether one is present, separates the three models") still leans on a single latent-iterative data point — RD-VLA. A second latent-iterative model would materially strengthen the paradigm attribution.
  - **RD-VLA is 0.5B vs DT ~3B vs OFT ~7B.** Although the SR-vulnerability ordering is non-monotone in scale and §4.3 controls for backbone (RD-VLA and OFT share Prismatic), the 0.5B model is also the smallest, and the four confound controls, while reasonable, do not equal a within-paradigm replication.
  - **RD-VLA FGSM cells are DT→RD-VLA transfer lower bounds**, not white-box; the white-box PGD-10 cells are what fix the cross-architecture ranking, but readers comparing the FGSM column should note RD-VLA is the only model in that column without a true white-box FGSM number.
  - **The plan-action consistency probe's detection of vision-stage attacks is bounded by a structural property (Obs. 1) rather than empirically tested against an *adaptive* vision attacker on the consistency term** — the adaptive ladder is entity-swap-only. The authors flag jointly adapting against the text-consistency term as future work, so the consistency-probe ceiling under vision attack is partially an argument from the coherent-shift bound, not a fully adaptive empirical demonstration.
  - **AutoAttack and Hessian-trace probes are deferred** as future work, leaving the "curvature not gradient masking" reading of OFT as plausible but not exhaustively certified.
  - **All experiments are simulation-only** on LIBERO plus a single-architecture SimplerEnv sanity check; physical-world transfer is explicitly open.

## Strengths

- Crisp, falsifiable mechanistic claim (ρ(12)/ρ(8)=1.005 vs predicted 2.0) with the K=4 OOD point honestly flagged.
- Matched-FPR calibration discipline that exposes and excludes a tempting miscalibrated +24.6pp result.
- Honest framing of the defense result as a ceiling/precondition, with three named directions (intermediate-representation probes, training-time interventions, non-white-box threat models) that lie beyond the tested scope.
- Strong statistical hygiene: paired Wilcoxon + Holm-Bonferroni, bootstrap CIs, N=12 matched cells, pre-declared canonical operating point (ε=8/255).
- Cross-benchmark (SimplerEnv) and cross-class (LIBERO-Plus) consistency checks that bound rather than just assert the generality of the structural-amplifier reading.

## Limitations

- N=3 models, one per reasoning paradigm; paradigm-level conclusions require within-paradigm replication (the authors say so).
- All evaluation is in simulation; sim-to-real transfer is open under class-level noise analogs, not calibrated physical-world threats.
- The stage-fused guard is evaluated post hoc under abstain-on-flag; closed-loop deployment and jointly adaptive attackers (against both the consistency and action-anomaly terms simultaneously) are future work.
- Code is promised on publication, not released; per-step guard inputs exist only for DT, blocking OFT transfer of the guard.
- Instruction-level attacks (paraphrase, prompt injection) and visual-reasoning models (CoT-VLA) are out of scope.
- AutoAttack and certified-defense comparisons are deferred.

## Relevance

对关注 VLA/具身推理鲁棒性与安全性的研究高度相关：它直接检验"推理先于动作能吸收扰动"这一直觉，揭示隐式迭代推理范式（RD-VLA 式 latent recurrence）反而在跨阶段攻击下最脆弱，并给出了运行时一致性监测器在自适应攻击下的失效上界，对选择 VLA 推理架构、设计运行时安全机制以及后续防御研究都有明确的参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the latent-iterative fragility replicate in a *second* latent-iterative VLA (different backbone, different K), or is the 0.0% PGD-10 collapse specific to RD-VLA's Prismatic + K=12 weight-tied recurrence?
- Can an intermediate-representation probe (read from inside the recurrence rather than its output) break the matched-FPR ceiling, given that both tested probes read only stage outputs?
- Would training-time interventions on the recurrence (Lipschitz regularization, adversarial training of g, convergence-monitoring losses) move ρ(K) below the OFT baseline without sacrificing clean SR?
- Is the OFT FGSM-vs-PGD-10 asymmetry (EOT narrows it but does not eliminate it) recoverable under AutoAttack and a Hessian-trace probe, confirming the curvature-not-masking reading?
- Under a non-white-box threat model (transfer-only or query-limited attackers), do the consistency and action-anomaly probes recover any positive defended-SR delta, or does the coherent-shift bound (Obs. 1) still bind?
- Does the structural-amplifier reading extend to physical-world transfer (printed perturbations, lighting/ sensor variation calibrated to real camera noise rather than class-level analogs)?
