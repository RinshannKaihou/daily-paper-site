Key finding

First sub-Gaussian maximal concentration for stochastic approximation under multiplicative noise — via one elementary unified proof.

Concentration and Mean-Square Bounds for Contractive Stochastic Approximation: A Unified Elementary Approach

Siddarth Chandak / Stanford University, EE / ORCID 0000-0003-3237-7727
stochastic approximation concentration bounds RL theory multiplicative noise Lyapunov drift
Key metric
1 − π2δ/3
Probability that ‖x_k − x*‖²_c ≤ c4·log(d(k+1)/δ)/(k+h) holds simultaneously for all k ≥ 0. ℓ∞ rate: O(σ²log d / ((1−λ)³k)).
Why this matters
Latest concentration rates and the simplest known proof template for ℓ∞-contractive RL algorithms — Q-learning, TD(0) — a direct reference for finite-time guarantees.
Critical edge
Sub-Gaussian tail requires choosing β₀ = O(1/log(1/δ)) up front — a single run cannot serve all confidence levels. The relaxation matches Chen et al.'s impossibility result exactly.
01 / METHOD

Averaged noise, auxiliary iterates, good events.

No Moreau envelope. No multi-stage bootstrap. The whole argument rests on one drift inequality plus an induction on "good events" that lets Azuma–Hoeffding apply directly to unbounded iterates.

Setting: SA iteration x_{k+1} = x_k + β_k(f(x_k) − x_k + M_{k+1}) with β_k = β/(k+h); f is a γ-contraction in arbitrary norm ‖·‖_c; multiplicative noise E[‖M_{k+1}‖²_c | F_k] ≤ σ²(1 + ‖x_k‖²_c).

  1. Averaged noise + auxiliary iterates. Define ξ_{k+1} = (1−β_k)ξ_k + β_k M_{k+1} and z_k = x_k − ξ_k. Lemma 3 yields a one-step Lyapunov drift inequality for ‖z_k − x*‖²_c. The only place contraction is used — norm-agnostic, no envelope.
  2. Bound ‖ξ_k‖_c. Lemma 4 routes through the Euclidean norm (exploiting martingale-difference orthogonality) and back, producing the dimension factor ζ₁(‖·‖_c, d). Sharper constants via martingale type-2.
  3. Mean-square (Thm 1). Deterministic induction. Lemma 6 closes the loop with a self-consistent Γ₂ so E[‖x_k − x*‖²_c] ≤ c₂/(k+h) for all k.
  4. Concentration (Thm 2). Probabilistic induction on good events A_k, B_k, C_k. Truncated increments M̃_{i+1} = M_{i+1}·𝟙_{C_i} are bounded a.s. — unblocking coordinate-wise Azuma–Hoeffding. The log d comes from a union bound.
02 / RESULTS

Matching the rate, sharpening the tail.

  • Mean-square · Thm 1 Matches Chen et al. [13] order-wise at O(σ²log d / ((1−λ)³k)) for ℓ∞, with no Moreau envelope smoothing of the norm.
  • Concentration · Thm 2 Tail exponent is log(1/δ) (sub-Gaussian), vs prior best log^m(1/δ), m ≥ 1 (Weibull). First such bound under multiplicative noise + unbounded iterates.
  • Price of sub-Gaussianity · Remark 4 Minimal relaxation: initial stepsize β₀ = O(1/log(1/δ)). Below that, the bound only kicks in after k₀ = Ω(log(1/δ)) steps.
  • Dimension dependence All bounds scale only with log d (coordinate union bound) — more transparent than envelope-based proofs whose constants are less explicit.
  • Additive-noise corollary · App. C For uniformly bounded noise, h no longer needs to grow with log(1/δ); recovers Chen et al. [15] qualitatively via a strictly simpler argument.
+

Strengths

  • Genuinely elementary. Mean-square bound rests on one drift inequality (Lemma 3) plus a one-page induction (Lemma 6); concentration adds the clean event-decomposition of Lemma 7 — materially simpler than the Moreau + exponential-supermartingale stack.
  • Honest about the formulation change. Remark 4 is a model of careful comparison, explicitly acknowledging the matching impossibility result of Chen et al. [15].
  • Explicit constants c₁–c4 throughout — rare in this literature and useful for downstream applications.
  • Concrete generalizability. Names real targets — SSP Q-learning, RVI Q-learning, heavy-tailed noise — rather than gesturing vaguely.
  • Reusable tool. The good-events induction is algorithm-agnostic, applicable to any scheme with unbounded iterates.
−

Limitations

  • No experiments. Pure theory; the RL motivation (TD(0), Q-learning, SSP Q-learning) is invoked but no instance is worked out numerically.
  • δ-dependent stepsize. A single run cannot serve all confidence levels simultaneously — intrinsic, given the impossibility result, but limits practical applicability.
  • Order-wise only. Constants c₂, c₄ are explicit but not compared head-to-head with Chen et al. [13, 15]; "no sacrifice in sharpness" is established only at big-O.
  • Restricted stepsize form. Results stated for β_k = β/(k+h); other schedules only asserted obtainable "using the techniques presented," without worked examples.
  • Single author, single pass. Some expositional tightening possible (the ζ₁ vs ζ₂ distinction; role of martingale type-2 constants; forward references in §3).
03 / OPEN

Questions for further exploration.

  • Can a time-varying stepsize that adapts to an empirical noise estimate recover a sub-Gaussian tail with a single trajectory — removing the δ-dependence?
  • Do the explicit constants c₂, c₄ here actually match those of Chen et al. [13, 15] on a concrete chain MDP where σ, λ, d are all known — or sit behind by a non-trivial factor?
  • Does the good-events induction extend to two-timescale SA (cf. Chandak et al. [10]) with multiplicative noise on both scales, where the slower timescale sees compounded noise?
  • Does combining the averaged-noise trick with the present arbitrary-norm result give a usable finite-time bound for Q-learning with heavy-tailed rewards?
  • Is the log d factor for ℓ∞ tight for this proof technique, or can a more careful coordinate-wise / martingale-type-2 argument shave it?
  • Could a refined Freedman-style martingale bound tighten the π²δ/3 failure probability and reduce the required h = Ω(log(1/δ))?
