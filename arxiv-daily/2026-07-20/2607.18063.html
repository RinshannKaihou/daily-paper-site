Adaptive Adversaries ↗

A multi-turn, multi-LLM benchmark for LLM agent security — 21 scenarios, 3×3 frontier matrix, 945 battles.

Devina Jain · David Hartmann · Chuan Li  |  Lambda  |  arXiv:2607.18063

Key finding

15-round adaptive attacks lift ASR 0–1% → 5.4–14.0% — frontier defenders tie overall, yet split sharply per-scenario.

01Key metric
0–1% → 5.4–14.0%

Attack success rate on the same 945-battle 3×3 matrix when scored at round 1 vs. the full 15-round adaptive budget. Only the interaction depth changes.

02Why this matters

A runnable 21-scenario, multi-attacker protocol that exposes multi-turn adaptive holes single-turn evals miss.

03Critical edge

Only 3 of 21 scenarios are statistically separated.

N=5/cell gives binomial SE ≈13pp at p=0.5 — memleak, paperaccept and gameguru are real; the other 16 invite over-interpretation.

Methodology

§ 01

Memoryless defender

Each of 15 rounds, the attacker sees scenario context + prior defender turns; the defender sees only the current formatted input — isolating per-input robustness under sustained adaptive pressure.

Calibrated scenarios

21 held-out scenarios pass a 3-config rubric — BAL / SVW / WVS (strong=GPT-5.2, weak=GPT-4o-mini) — scoring difficulty, sensitivity, and round depth; 4 hard-reject flags.

Live attack regeneration

Attacks are generated per battle, not read from a static pool — and treated as a measurement axis alongside the scenario set.

Typed-field scoring

check_success inspects only scenario-relevant typed fields, not prose — robust to substring-proxy reward hacking (echoed referrals, decoy unlocks).

3×3 frontier matrix

21 scenarios × {Opus 4.6, GPT-5.4, Gemini 2.5 Pro} attackers × same three defenders, N=5/cell = 945 battles with full transcripts.

Layered evidence

945-transcript matrix + 48-source / 244-replay transfer corpus + 18,422-battle open competition + 1,050-battle gpt-oss-20b noise floor.

Results & highlights

§ 02

The same scenarios, defenders, attackers, and structured checks — only the interaction budget moves ASR from near-zero to double digits. The aggregate tie between Opus and GPT-5.4 conceals opposite per-scenario failure modes.

Adaptivity ablation 30% → 0% ASR collapses without attacker access to prior defender responses.
Transfer rate 10.2% (25/244) 0 attacks transfer to all three frontier defenders.
Battles (matrix) 945 3 attackers × 3 defenders × 21 scenarios × N=5.
The signal is response-conditioned adaptation, not repeated sampling. Removing attacker memory of prior defender turns drops ASR from 30.0% to 0.0%; making the defender stateful only lowers it to 19.4%. Aggregate ties are misleading: Opus and GPT-5.4 both sit at 5.4% overall, but Opus falls uniquely on memleak (60% vs 7%) while Gemini falls uniquely on paperaccept (53% vs 0%) — weaknesses that do not align on a single safety axis.

Strengths

What the paper does well
  • Fills a real quadrant — multi-turn × per-battle regeneration × multi-LLM attacker — with a shipped protocol, not just identification.
  • Layered calibration evidence: 3×3 matrix + N=20 replications + 18,422-battle competition + 1,050-battle noise floor + 48-source replay corpus.
  • Candid reward-hacking analysis: documents how their own first-pass substring scoring was gamed, and re-scores everything tighter.
  • Falsifiable transcripts: memleak / paperaccept / gameguru inversions shown verbatim in Appendix C.
  • Strong release: MIT code, CC-BY-4.0 data, Croissant 1.0 manifest, smoke test, multi-attacker CLI.

Limitations

Where it overreaches
  • Sample size: N=5/cell, binomial SE ≈13pp — only 3/21 scenarios separated; N=20 covers just the three headlines.
  • Coverage skew: 11/21 indirect injection, 6/21 PII — only 1 supply-chain and 1 prompt-extraction scenario.
  • W = 0.19 has a wide CI [0.02, 0.58] overlapping MHJ (0.204) and JBB (0.210) — weaker than the headline phrasing implies.
  • Closed-model drift: Gemini via OpenRouter without snapshot verification; 10 public-scenario defender prompts withheld.
  • Competition is gpt-oss-20b only — the 18,422-battle strategy-space claim does not extend to frontier defenders.

Open questions

§ 03
  1. Would scaling the matrix to N=20 across all 21 scenarios surface more separated inversions — or do only three frontier models admit a handful of separable modes?
  2. Does accumulated history help a production agent refuse, or reinforce manipulation (Opus→Opus memleak: statefulness raised ASR 80%→100%)?
  3. Can any single frontier model be "uniformly safest" — or is low concordance a structural property of calibrated scenario sets?
  4. Given adaptive attackers game any proxy, can an attack-aware success-criterion generator detect gaming without per-scenario auditing?
  5. Does the fabricated-authority mechanism generalize beyond paperaccept / gameguru / smarthomejack to inline-policy injections at large?
  6. Will defender harnesses built on fixed refusal patterns degrade on held-out scenarios the same way fixed-catalog attackers did?
