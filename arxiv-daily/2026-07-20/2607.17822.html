§ 01 Methodology

Restructure the feature space into P = N/2 pairs of 2D phasors, compute a global scale from local L2 magnitudes, and normalize isotropically with a single shared affine weight.

fig. — MRSNorm dataflow: pair adjacent channels → per-pair L2 magnitude → L1 mean → isotropic scale. vs. RMSNorm: Σ(x²) then √.

Three design moves do the work. (1) Operation inversion — L2 magnitudes are computed locally before any summation, so a single outlier cannot dominate a global quadratic denominator (the failure mode of RMSNorm under fp16). (2) Phase preservation — both components share the same scale and weight, so arctan(ŷᵢ/x̂ᵢ) = arctan(yᵢ/xᵢ), maintaining conformal invariance and making per-channel scaling redundant. (3) Gradient homogeneity — the per-phasor gradient interference vector is (Ω/S)·uᵢ with uᵢ = [cos θᵢ, sin θᵢ]; since ‖uᵢ‖₂ ≡ 1, every phasor receives identical gradient magnitude |Ω|/S regardless of activation scale.

# the Pythagorean identity becomes a gradient clipper
cos²θᵢ + sin²θᵢ ≡ 1 ⟹ ‖gradᵢ‖ = |Ω| / S ∀ i (no heuristic threshold)

Two structural impossibility results justify the 2D choice. A continuously differentiable 1D periodic function must have constant-sign derivative and is therefore linear (Intermediate Value Theorem) — so 1D cannot be both periodic and gradient-stable. And strict unit-circle projection zᵢ/‖zᵢ‖₂ has Jacobian (1/‖zᵢ‖)(I − uᵢuᵢᵀ) whose rank-1 projector annihilates radial signals — the Radial Gradient Blockade. MRSNorm sidesteps it by scaling with the global average S rather than the instance norm.

  • lr = 0.1, bs = 32: RMSNorm already shows severe variance; MRSNorm stays smooth — the instability surfaces before the extreme regime.
  • lr = 0.3: both LayerNorm and RMSNorm flatten to near-zero training accuracy across all batch sizes; MRSNorm converges cleanly at bs = 128.

  • Parameter footprint: MRSNorm uses N/2 affine parameters vs N for LN/RMSNorm — the halved footprint does not impair, and under stress appears to improve, stability.

§ 03 Strengths vs. Limitations

Coherent math on one side; an experimental scope that cannot yet carry the title's claim on the other.

Strengths

  • Self-consistent theory. Phase preservation (arctan), gradient homogeneity (Pythagorean), the Cauchy-Schwarz logit bound, and the two impossibility results interlock cleanly.
  • Honest scoping. The conclusion explicitly states that scaling to billion-parameter LLMs is out of scope and invites the community to test QK-MRSNorm empirically.
  • Falsifiable efficiency claim. N/2 parameters pairs naturally with the geometric argument that independent x/y scaling is harmful redundancy.
  • Well-chosen stress test. Extreme lr × small batch isolates optimization stability from raw final-accuracy competition — the right tool for the structural prediction.
  • Runnable reference implementation. Appendix C provides a PyTorch GroupMRSNorm module, lowering the reproduction barrier.

Limitations

  • Title overclaim. "Phasor Attention" is advertised, but no attention experiment exists — only ResNet/CIFAR-100 plus a purely theoretical QK section.
  • No final accuracy tables. Evaluation is via training-curve plots only; whether stability translates into equal or better downstream performance is unshown.
  • Narrow baselines. Only LayerNorm and RMSNorm — no GroupNorm, QK-Norm, ScaleNorm, or modern LLM-oriented variants.
  • Scope mismatch with motivation. Frames itself around "LLMs use RMSNorm" but validates only on CIFAR-100 with ResNet; no Transformer, no language modeling.
  • QK-MRSNorm is untested. The "energy-weighted cosine similarity operator" is elegant but never run; the Semantic Phase Veto is asserted, not measured.
  • Undisclosed architectural constraints. Exact ResNet variant, target channel dimension, num_groups choice, and the even-channel / divisible-bundle requirement are not discussed.

§ 04 Questions for further exploration

  1. Does the stability advantage persist at standard regimes (lr = 0.1, large batch) when measured by final CIFAR-100 / ImageNet accuracy and LLM pre-training perplexity — or does MRSNorm merely survive stress that baselines cannot?
  2. How does QK-MRSNorm actually behave in a real Transformer (e.g., long-context attention with massive-activation outliers)? Does the Semantic Phase Veto measurably reduce softmax collapse on long sequences?
  3. What is the wall-clock and memory cost of the pairing / repeat_interleave operations, and does parameter halving translate into throughput gains at scale?
  4. How does MRSNorm compare against QK-Norm, GroupNorm, ScaleNorm and other variants that specifically target attention logit stability, not just LN/RMSNorm?
  5. A controlled ablation isolating the two design axes (operation inversion vs. phasor pairing / phase preservation) — currently bundled, so the driver of the observed stability is unclear.
  6. Does the built-in gradient homogeneity interact with AdamW beneficially or adversarially, given that AdamW already performs per-parameter scaling?
