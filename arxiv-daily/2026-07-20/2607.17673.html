Clip · Symile · Triangle · Gram, on complete & missing modality

The paper studies trimodal observations with modality-specific encoders E_i: X_i → ℝd and a contrastive score s(z₁,z₂,z₃). Rather than proposing yet another s(·), it investigates the geometry of E_i via its input-output Jacobian J_i(x) = ∂E_i(x)/∂x.

For an MLP with weights W_ℓ and ReLU activation Jacobians D_ℓ, J_i = W_L D_{L-1} W_{L-1} … D_1 W_1. Because ReLU derivatives are exactly 0 or 1, entire directions can be zeroed out — collapsing σ_min. Conversely, matrix products inflate σ_max. Contrastive gradients flow as ∇_x L = J_i(x)⊤ ∇_{z_i} L, so ill-conditioned Jacobians directly impair gradient transport.

Two interventions confirm the diagnosis. The JVP regularizer is a causal probe: it constrains directional gains r_i(x,v) = ‖J_i v‖² / ‖v‖² over K=32 random directions to a band [0.5, 5.0]. On Synthetic-XNOR it alone stabilises κ(J) from ~10⁸ toward 10⁵ and reaches maximum accuracy — mirroring GPE. The GPE recipe is the deployable version: residual transport plus LeakyReLU, with a Weyl-inequality guarantee that the encoder cannot fully collapse.

Training uses a unified PyTorch Lightning framework, ScheduleFree-AdamW, 100-trial Bayesian optimisation on validation loss (50 for UKB-Union), LoRA on Q/V of RadBERT for MIMIC-IV text, 5-fold cross-validation × 3 seeds, mean ± SE reported.

Geometry guarantee · Weyl

Residual lower bound σ_min(J_E) ≥ σ_min(P) − ‖H‖₂

With E_i(x) = P_i x + h_i(x), if the residual dominates the nonlinear branch, the encoder cannot fully collapse σ_min — the architecture itself enforces conditioning.

JVP regularizer

Directional-gain band · K = 32 L_JVP = w_min·[max(0, m² − r_min)]²
        + w_max·[max(0, r_max − M²)]²

m = 0.5, M = 5.0, λ = 1.0. Expensive (32 JVPs per sample per step), so GPE is the practical substitute.

03 / Critical assessment

Clean mechanistic story. Narrow empirical base.

Strengths

  • Coherent causal arc. Theoretical diagnosis (Eqs 2–13), interventional confirmation (JVP regularizer), and a deployable fix (GPE) form a tight loop — not just an architecture search.
  • Honest about scope. The Limitations section explicitly admits healthcare-only data, trimodal-only setup, and the focus on MLP encoders. No overclaiming the architecture's novelty.
  • Negligible-cost recipe. Residual + LeakyReLU plugs into any multimodal contrastive pipeline — two-line change, no extra compute at inference.
  • Rigorous infrastructure. Unified PyTorch Lightning framework, 100-trial Bayesian tuning, 5-fold × 3-seed evaluation, code released, hyperparameter spaces listed verbatim.
  • Thoughtful bonus (Appendix E). Transformer-component ablation isolates residual transport — not learned attention — as the stabilising mechanism, supporting Ji et al. 2025 "Always Skip Attention."

Limitations

  • Biomedical only. All four real datasets are MIMIC ×2 or UKB ×2 — no natural-image, audio, or video trimodal evaluation. VLM-style generalisation is asserted, not shown.
  • Trimodal only. Behaviour at 4+ modalities is untested, despite the broad "Multimodal Contrastive Learning" title.
  • MLP-encoder specific. Transformers already contain skip connections and are robust by construction — practical benefit shrinks for non-MLP architectures.
  • Linear-probe gains within SE. Several MIMIC-IV gains (e.g. Clip 0.8750 → 0.8768 ± 0.003) are not statistically separated; "consistent" framing overstates it.
  • Gram-on-MIMIC-IV regresses. Retrieval 0.628 → 0.609 and probe 0.867 → 0.864 under GPE — direct counterexample to universality.
  • Regularizer cost unreported. 32 JVPs per sample per step is heavy; the paper never reports wall-clock overhead, so the GPE-vs-regularizer trade-off is unclear.

04 / Questions for further exploration

Where the geometry argument breaks — or generalises.

  1. Do GPEs keep helping at 4+ modalities, where pairwise-batched objectives are known to scale poorly — or does a different degeneracy mode emerge? Scaling · modality count
  2. How do GPEs interact with non-contrastive multimodal objectives (cross-modal masking, generative fusion), and could the JVP regularizer transfer there? Objective family
  3. Is the Gram-on-MIMIC-IV regression a fundamental incompatibility between high-order scoring and residual transport, or a hyperparameter artefact? Failure analysis
  4. Could orthogonal initialisation or spectral normalisation substitute for GPEs and produce the same linear-probe gains — or is the residual path doing something those methods cannot? Alternative fixes
  5. What is the wall-clock cost of the JVP regularizer relative to a base step, and can K drop below 32 directions without losing the conditioning signal? Compute trade-off
  6. Is there a principled schedule for the optimal LeakyReLU slope α against embedding dimension and modality informativeness, rather than a Bayesian-searched constant? Architecture · α schedule
  7. Do the results transfer to natural-image / audio / text trimodal settings where encoders are CNN/Transformer, given the Transformer-robustness observation in Appendix E? VLM generalisation
