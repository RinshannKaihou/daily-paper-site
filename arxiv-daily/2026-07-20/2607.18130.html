Why this matters

Opens residual routing as a PEFT axis orthogonal to weight (LoRA/VeRA) and activation ((IA)³) adaptation — a mechanistic inspiration, not a LoRA replacement.

Critical edge

Generalisation unproven — single OLMo-2 backbone, Tulu-3 only, and LoRA baselines use low ranks (r ≤ 14). mHC loses hard on reasoning: DROP F1 0.56 vs LoRA's 5.41.

Methodology

wraps frozen OLMo-2
Multi-stream residual routing
# standard residual
zl = fl(zl-1) + zl-1

# mHC generalisation
Zl = Hlres · Zl-1 + hlpost ⊗ fl(xl)
    where   xl = (hlpre)T · Zl-1
Hres is projected onto the Birkhoff polytope (doubly stochastic) via Sinkhorn so depth products stay controlled. The three load-bearing parts are the read gate hpre, the mixer Hres, and the write gate hpost.

Each attention and MLP sub-layer of a frozen OLMo-2 Transformer is wrapped with an mHC module that reroutes the residual stream through n parallel streams.

Three published parameterisations of Hres are compared, each in a static (fixed logits) and dynamic (input-dependent, RMSNorm-conditioned) form. The wrapper initialises numerically equivalent to the frozen backbone, with Bernoulli masking + Gaussian noise to break stream permutation symmetry.

Baselines: LoRA, VeRA, (IA)³, prompt tuning, layer tuning, plus a frozen reference. Evaluation is held-out loss/perplexity plus 8 downstream tasks via lm-eval harness.

  1. 01 Identity preservation improves loss at every n. Fixing Hres = In for dynamic KromHC drops test loss across all expansion rates — at n=16: 1.32 → 1.27 while saving 8.39M trainable parameters.
  2. 02 Identity helps downstream too (1B, n=4). BBH 22.47 → 26.92, GSM8K 22.67 → 25.32, MATH 2.16 → 5.43, TriviaQA 15.34 → 27.74.
  3. 03 Standalone mHC is competitive but not dominant. mHCid,n=4 takes best PIQA (76.01) and TriviaQA (27.74), but LoRA owns reasoning — DROP 5.41 vs 0.56, GSM8K 31.39 vs 25.32.
  4. 04 Ablations: read/write gates carry the method. Removing hpre or hpost costs +0.14 each (1.32 → 1.46); removing Hres only costs +0.05 — confirming the identity-preservation finding.
03

Strengths & limitations

honest framing

Strengths

6
  • Clean, modular implementation with pre-launch diagnostics (causal-LM shape, doubly-stochastic validity, gradient routing, numerical equivalence at init) most PEFT papers skip.
  • Unusually honest framing. The abstract, Table 6 and conclusion explicitly admit mHC is not a LoRA replacement.
  • The identity-preservation insight is a genuine mechanistic finding, supported by three convergent lines: interpretability heatmaps, 3-component ablations, and explicit Hres=I runs.
  • Three parameterisations compared systematically, with a sensible default (dynamic KromHC) chosen on parameter-efficiency grounds.
  • Scales selected experiments to 7B, not only 1B.
  • Strong reproducibility — public code, open OLMo-2 backbones, public Tulu-3 data, full hyperparameter appendix, carbon report (~99.45 kgCO₂eq).

Limitations

8
  • Generalisation unproven — only OLMo-2 family, only Tulu-3 SFT mixture. Identity finding could be an artefact of OLMo-2's pre-norm residual structure.
  • Weak LoRA baselines (r ≤ 14) at 1B/7B; behaviour against properly tuned higher-rank LoRA is unknown.
  • Combination gains are small and task-dependent — mHCid,n=2+LoRA r=8 is worse than LoRA r=8 on 4/8 tasks.
  • Single seed / limited hyperparameter coverage (acknowledged).
  • No deployment cost (latency, memory) for multi-stream routing — only trainable parameter counts reported.
  • TriviaQA EM for LoRA looks broken (~0.03–0.06) and is not investigated, yet appears in "best" comparisons.
  • No theory for why finetuning prefers identity mixing — only a "residual memory" hypothesis.
  • KromHC requires n = power of two, restricting expansion-rate choices.

Open questions

further exploration
Q · 01
Does the identity-preservation finding transfer beyond OLMo-2 to Llama-3, Mistral, Qwen, or post-norm architectures?
Q · 02
Would mHC+LoRA still help when LoRA is trained at higher rank (r = 32, 64), where its budget dominates mHC?
Q · 03
What is the actual inference-time latency and memory cost of multi-stream routing vs LoRA's merged weights?
Q · 04
Can "residual memory" be formalised — do hpre/hpost gates select pretrained subspaces LoRA cannot reach?
Q · 05
Does identity preservation also hold for full-parameter finetuning, or only in the frozen-backbone regime?
Q · 06
Is the TriviaQA EM collapse for LoRA a decoding/tokeniser artefact — and does fixing it erase mHC's advantage?
