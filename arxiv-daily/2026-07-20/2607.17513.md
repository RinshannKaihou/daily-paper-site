> **一句话总结：** 提出 HySAT 训练原理——把双曲 Lorentz 几何只放在损失层而不进入可训练 adapter，在 6 个领域专家小模型（17.95M 训练样本、约 317K 优化器步数）上实现 0 NaN 训练，而同样几何进入 adapter 的 HyperLoRA 风格做法在 ~220 B200 SXM GPU-小时内崩溃 17 次。

> **TL;DR:** HySAT places hyperbolic Lorentz geometry only at the loss layer (not inside the trainable LoRA adapter), giving a curvature-decoupled gradient bound (Proposition 1) that yields 0 NaN across ~317K cumulative optimizer steps on six domain-expert SLMs totaling 17.95M samples (ORAA single project 11.4M = 1,139× HypLoRA's Math-10K), versus 17 HyperLoRA-style adapter-on-manifold crashes consuming ~220 B200 SXM GPU-hours; matched 4-arm ablation (Qwen 2.5 7B, 6 seeds) shows HySAT preserves the manifold invariant to drift ~10⁻⁶ vs ~10⁴ for a flat control (ten orders of magnitude), although held-out tree-distance correlation does not separate the arms at toy scale.

## 核心贡献

- **HySAT 放置律 (Placement Law)**：双曲 Lorentz 几何只接触损失层，永远不进入可训练参数；transformer 基座和 LoRA adapter 保持欧氏。论文用 Proposition 1 证明此条件下 `E‖B_t‖_F ≤ ‖B_0‖_F + κ·Ση_s`，与 Lorentz 曲率 c 无关，而 adapter-on-manifold 放置会引入无界的 `sinh(‖B·x‖)/‖B·x‖` Jacobian 在 bf16 边界 ‖v‖≈11 处崩溃。
- **六领域专家 SLM 跨验证矩阵**：跨两个基座家族（Llama 3.1 8B、EXAONE 3.5 7.8B）× 四种 adapter 策略（from-scratch / full-FT / Std-LoRA-HySAT / HyperLoRA-stabilized）构建 6 个模型，覆盖行为审计、创意、学术研究、教学、公司财务、招生咨询。累计 17,954,911 训练样本、~317K 优化器步数，零 NaN。
- **三个结构性损失**：PTLE（Pairwise Tree Lorentz Embedding，对齐树路径）、HLSD（Hierarchical Label Sibling Distance，同胞分离）、HWC（Hierarchy-Weighted Contrastive，统一形式）。
- **Proposition 2 + 批大小再定义**：把 batch size 从吞吐量变量重新定义为"结构性学习质量变量"，给出 sibling-pair 形成概率 `P(b) ≈ 1 − exp(−b(b−1)(p̄−1)/(N−1))`；ORAA/AdmitBrain 两次自然实验显示 b=2 时损失静默失败、b≥8/16 时 100% 激活。
- **可证伪范式与透明性**：预指定假设 H1–H6，Zenodo (DOI 10.5281/zenodo.21438500, CC-BY-4.0) 公开逐步训练 trace、17 起崩溃事故台账、保留一条原始崩溃轨迹与崩溃复现脚本；两个模型 (BS Sovereign, S3 Creative) 以开放权重发布在 HuggingFace。

## Key Contributions

- **HySAT placement principle**: hyperbolic Lorentz geometry touches only the loss layer; trainable adapter weights stay in R^d. Proposition 1 shows the adapter-norm bound is identical to Euclidean LoRA and independent of curvature c, while adapter-on-manifold placement inherits an unbounded `sinh(‖B·x‖)/‖B·x‖` Jacobian that drives ‖v‖ past the bf16 instability boundary ≈ 11.
- **Six domain-expert SLMs across two base families and four adapter strategies** (BS Sovereign 146M from-scratch fully-hyperbolic, S3 Creative T5-3B full fine-tune, ORAA Llama 3.1 8B Std-LoRA, MetaTeach EXAONE 3.5 7.8B HyperLoRA, CEO Finance EXAONE Std-LoRA, AdmitBrain Llama 3.1 8B HyperLoRA) totaling 17.95M training samples and ~317K cumulative optimizer steps with 0 NaN at convergence.
- **Three structural losses** (PTLE, HLSD, HWC) that reference domain-tree ontologies via Lorentz distances; HWC unifies PTLE attraction and HLSD separation under a continuous ancestry-overlap weight.
- **Proposition 2 reframes micro-batch size as a structural-learning-quality variable** under hyperbolic supervision, with the practitioner rule b* ≥ N/p̄; two natural experiments (ORAA b=2→8→16, AdmitBrain b=2→16) confirm a silent-failure vs full-activation regime split.
- **Pre-specified H1–H6 hypotheses, Zenodo CC-BY-4.0 release** of per-step traces, the 17-incident failure ledger, one preserved raw divergence trajectory (AdmitBrain E2), and a crash-reproduction script; verified RunPod B200 aggregate of $4,998 / ~1,111 GPU-hours disclosed.

## Methodology

- **Manifold and projection**: Lorentz model H^d = {x ∈ R^(d+1) : ⟨x,x⟩_L = −1, x_0 > 0}; projection π(h) = exp_0(h/τ) with τ = 2 and a ∥v∥ ≤ 2 clamp before the exponential map, making forward-pass stability invariant to residual-stream norm distribution.
- **HySAT total loss**: L = L_CE + λ_PTLE·L_PTLE + λ_HLSD·L_HLSD + λ_HWC·L_HWC, with project weights λ ≈ 0.02–0.05 (typically λ_PTLE = 0.05, LoRA rank r = 64 across all LoRA-based projects).
- **Autograd contract**: forward computes h in R^d by the Euclidean adapter; π(h) only at loss evaluation; backward flows via standard PyTorch autograd through bounded Jacobian ‖∂π/∂h‖_op ≤ cosh(4)/τ ≈ 5.23/τ. The adapter weights B_t are updated by Euclidean AdamW with no Riemannian retraction.
- **Four placement arms** (Proposition 1(ii) failure conditions): adapter-on-manifold is stable only when (ii.a) curvature-aware optimisation (Riemannian Adam), (ii.b) explicit B-norm constraint, or (ii.c) retraction with full-sequence loss and effective batch ≥ 32 holds. HySAT is the only placement stable without any of these.
- **Training protocol**: bf16 precision, gradient clipping at norm 1.0, cosine LR schedule with 3% warmup, plus a 250-step Euclidean-to-Lorentz ramp that prevents gradient-death at manifold initialisation.
- **Matched four-arm ablation** (Extended Data Table 5): Qwen 2.5 7B-Instruct, identical base/optimiser/dropout/clipping/500-step budget/depth-4 toy ontology, 6 seeds (42/123/7/2024/99/777); only placement × {hyperbolic, flat} varies.
- **Sample-efficiency as compute axis** rather than GPU-hours, argued as platform-independent and aligned with the 1,139× headline scale claim.

## Results & Highlights

- **Scale vs HypLoRA baseline**: ORAA single project = 11,387,744 fine-tuning samples, which is 1,139× HypLoRA's Math-10K (10,000 samples) and 67× its largest reported set Commonsense170K (170,420 samples); six-project cumulative 17,954,911 samples is 1,795× Math-10K and 105× Commonsense170K.
- **Stability separation**: 0 NaN events across ~317K cumulative optimizer steps in six complete HySAT-family training runs, versus 17 HyperLoRA-style adapter-on-manifold crashes totaling ~220 B200 SXM GPU-hours (7 early ORAA rank-16 + 6 early BS Sovereign full-hyperbolic + 4 early AdmitBrain single-adapter HyperLoRA), all terminating at the bf16 Jacobian-amplification boundary ∥v∥ ≈ 11.
- **Matched ablation manifold invariant**: HySAT arm manifold drift |⟨π(h),π(h)⟩_L + 1| = 8.9×10⁻⁷ to 1.4×10⁻⁶ versus Euclidean flat control 1.9–3.3×10⁴ — ten orders of magnitude, no seed overlap — on Qwen 2.5 7B-Instruct with 6 seeds; held-out tree-distance ρ_L does NOT separate the arms (HySAT 0.228±0.048 vs Euclidean 0.224±0.041 vs CE-only 0.253±0.033).
- **Batch-size structural regime split (Proposition 2)**: ORAA b=2 records 0% PTLE / 52% HLSD activation, rising to 100%/100% at b=16 (120 contrastive pairs per step); AdmitBrain b=2 records 0% PTLE / 3.9% HLSD, rising to 100%/100% at b=16 — a ~26× activation amplification predicted before the transition.
- **Per-project downstream anchors**: BS Sovereign F1 = 0.855 (14-head Lorentz classifier); S3 Creative 95.8% LLM-judge win-rate over 266 rounds; ORAA Dual-SLM 9/10 scholarly benchmark at 4.8s latency; CEO Finance v2 Triple-Coach cascade 30/30 vs GPT-4o under a three-judge leave-one-out ensemble; MetaTeach v14 HWC 100% activation (equilibrium 0.21–1.03); AdmitBrain v6 tone 4.2/5 (LLM baseline 5.0, 84% match rate).
- **Cross-project activation matrix**: at batch sizes above the pair-formation threshold every configured loss activates (>88% per-step activation across the six charted configurations); the sparsest deposited configuration fires at 2.2% of steps; Lorentz constraint ⟨x,x⟩_L = −1.000000 preserved throughout.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The placement question (loss-only vs adapter-on-manifold) is genuinely under-explored in the hyperbolic-LLM lineage; framing placement as a "law rather than a knob" is the paper's distinctive claim, though Proposition 1(ii) itself concedes that adapter-on-manifold placement becomes stable under curvature-aware optimisation, B-norm capping, or retraction+batch — which means placement is conditional on optimiser class, slightly undercutting the "law" framing.
- **Rigor**: The matched 4-arm ablation (Extended Data Table 5) is the load-bearing mechanistic test, and it is honest about its own limitation — held-out tree-distance ρ_L does NOT separate HySAT from the flat control (0.228 vs 0.224, overlapping within one SD). The ablation isolates only invariant preservation, not downstream task advantage. Downstream task gains are carried by the six deployed SLMs, none of which has an apples-to-apples comparison against vanilla Std-LoRA + cross-entropy at matched data and seed.
- **Reproducibility**: Strong on paper — Zenodo deposit of per-step traces, 17-incident ledger, one preserved raw crash trajectory, crash-reproduction script, and a `verify_claims.py` gate. Weaker in practice for the four commercial projects (ORAA, MetaTeach, CEO Finance, AdmitBrain), whose corpora, prompts, and deployment pipelines are retained under "gated research access" with no SLA guarantees; the headline 11.4M-sample ORAA run is documented by RunPod optimizer-step logs rather than full per-step CSV archival.
- **Significance**: If the placement claim generalises, it is a useful piece of engineering know-how (loss-only hyperbolic supervision is the cheapest stable configuration). However, the absence of any standard external benchmark (e.g. MMLU, GSM8K, BIG-Bench) comparison means the six deployed systems cannot be benchmarked against the broader SLM literature on common ground.
- **Red flags**: (a) Three of the six projects share 149,998 meta-capability samples (Stage-2 CEO Finance = BS/ORAA/MetaTeach), which the paper itself flags as a caveat for the cross-project activation matrix — Claim 5 is therefore not fully independent across projects. (b) Per-project single-seed evaluation (seed 42) with robustness delegated to "cross-project regularity" is below the multi-seed standard for stability claims. (c) Concurrent companion manuscript "JARVIS Is Near You" shares two empirical pillars (S3 Creative, BS Sovereign), raising the question of how much of the empirical contribution is double-counted across papers. (d) Lead author's two Korean patent applications on the exact methods were filed nine days before this preprint, dated 2026-07-12. (e) The 17 "crashes" are characterised as failures of curvature-unaware AdamW; readers should note this is the unconstrained regime that prior hyperbolic-NN literature already knew to avoid.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical statement: Proposition 1(i) gives an adapter-norm bound identical to Euclidean LoRA, with explicit autograd contract and bounded Jacobian M_τ ≈ 5.23/τ — operationally implementable in a few lines of PyTorch.
- Honest reporting of negative results: the 17-incident failure ledger with preserved raw trajectory and crash-reproduction script is rare and commendable.
- Genuine scale: 17.95M fine-tuning samples and ~317K optimizer steps dwarf prior hyperbolic-LLM fine-tuning work (HypLoRA Math-10K).
- The Proposition 2 batch-size-as-structural-variable insight is independently useful and gives an actionable practitioner rule (b* ≥ N/p̄).
- Transparent disclosure of competing interests, patent filings, and commercial affiliations.

## Limitations

- No apples-to-apples comparison against vanilla LoRA + cross-entropy on the six projects' downstream tasks at matched data and seed, so the end-task gain attributable to HySAT's hyperbolic losses (vs the LoRA + data + base-model combo) is not isolated.
- The matched ablation explicitly shows held-out tree-distance ρ_L does not separate HySAT from flat baseline at toy scale; the downstream task-level advantage is asserted via deployed systems rather than demonstrated under controlled conditions.
- Single-seed (42) per-project trajectories; no within-project multi-seed variance reported.
- Three of six projects share 149,998 meta-capability samples, partially compromising the cross-project independence of the activation matrix.
- Four of six projects are under gated commercial access — corpora, deployment prompts, and weights are not public.
- HyperLoRA "stabilisation conditions" (ii.a–ii.c) rescue adapter-on-manifold placement, so the placement "law" is conditional on optimiser class (curvature-unaware AdamW), not a categorical architectural fact.

## Relevance

对于研究参数高效微调、双曲表征学习与小模型专家化的方向，HySAT 提供了一个可立即落地的工程结论（双曲几何只放在 loss 层即可稳定训练），以及关于 batch size 在结构化损失下扮演结构性变量的新观察，值得在树状/层级领域专家 SLM 微调场景中复现验证。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does HySAT's loss-only placement produce a measurable downstream gain over an identical Std-LoRA + cross-entropy baseline on a standard external benchmark (MMLU, BIG-Bench, GSM8K) at matched data and seeds?
- How does HySAT behave under a τ ∈ {1, 2, 4, 8} sweep — the paper flags this as a "priority replication target" but does not run it?
- Can the Proposition 2 pair-formation threshold b* ≥ N/p̄ be verified on a public ontology (WordNet, MeSH) with held-out tree-distance ρ_L actually separating HySAT from flat control (which the toy ablation failed to show)?
- What is the wall-clock / throughput overhead of HySAT vs vanilla LoRA at matched sample count on identical hardware (the paper reports sample-efficiency but not controlled wall-clock)?
- Does the placement claim generalise to fully-hyperbolic pretraining registers (HELM-style), or is it strictly a fine-tuning-register result?
