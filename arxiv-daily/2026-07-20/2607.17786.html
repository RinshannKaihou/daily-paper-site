Reasoning as a Double-Edged Sword: Architecture & Cross-Stage Robustness in Vision-Language-Action Models

Tuan Duong Trinh · Naveed Akhtar · Basim Azam — University of Melbourne · preprint, cs.RO

Key finding

Latent-iterative reasoning makes VLA models the most fragile — success collapses to 0.0% under attack.

Key metric — PGD-10 ε=8/255

0.0%

RD-VLA (latent-iterative, K=12) success rate on LIBERO under white-box attack — versus 49.8% DeepThinkVLA and 18.2% OpenVLA-OFT.

N=12 cellsε=8/255white-box
Why this matters

Inverts the safety intuition that “reasoning before acting absorbs perturbations” — directly actionable for choosing a VLA architecture and designing runtime monitors.

Critical edge

All claims rest on N=3 models, one per paradigm. Every paradigm-level statement is, by the authors' own admission, a model-level observation that still needs within-paradigm replication.

01

Methodology

Three models spanning the reasoning spectrum. A VLA maps observation ot + instruction ℓ to a 7-DOF action at = fa(fr(fv(ot)), ℓ). The study attacks OpenVLA-OFT (~7B, no reasoning; Prismatic + MLPResNet head), DeepThinkVLA (~3B, text CoT before action), and RD-VLA (0.5B, K=12 weight-tied recurrent layers, latent iterative).

Stage-wise threat model. A stage-s adversary perturbs one pipeline stage at a time — vision (FGSM ε∈{2…32}/255, Gaussian σ∈{0.01…0.2}, PGD-10 at ε=8/255), reasoning (entity-swap CoT corruption, DT only), or action (Gaussian on 7-DOF, gripper excluded). White-box PGD runs through RD-VLA's K=12 recurrence via activation checkpointing.

Evaluation protocol. N=12 matched cells (4 LIBERO suites × 3 seeds), 50 episodes/cell, 300-step cap, LIBERO task-check scoring. Statistics: paired Wilcoxon signed-rank with Holm-Bonferroni correction across a 13-test family, plus 95% paired-bootstrap CIs. EOT (T=2) and gradient-free Square Attack (100 queries) rule out gradient masking.

K-sweep falsifier. Amplification ratio ρ(K)=‖∆a‖₂/ε measured at K∈{4,8,12} tests the standard Lipschitz-cumulative prediction ρ(K)≈LKiter (which would give ρ(12)/ρ(8)=2.0).

Canonical operating point ε=8/255. RD-VLA FGSM cells are DT→RD-VLA transfer lower bounds; PGD-10 cells are true white-box.

03

Strengths

  • mechanismCrisp, falsifiable claim — ρ(12)/ρ(8)=1.005 vs predicted 2.0 — with the K=4 OOD point honestly flagged.
  • methodMatched-FPR calibration discipline that exposes and excludes a tempting miscalibrated +24.6pp result.
  • framingHonest “ceiling / precondition” framing, with three named out-of-scope directions instead of a no-go theorem.
  • statsStrong hygiene: paired Wilcoxon + Holm-Bonferroni, bootstrap CIs, N=12 matched cells, pre-declared ε=8/255.
  • generalityCross-benchmark (SimplerEnv) and cross-class (LIBERO-Plus) checks that bound, not just assert, the structural-amplifier reading.
04

Limitations

  • scopeN=3 models, one per paradigm — paradigm-level conclusions require within-paradigm replication (the authors say so).
  • realismAll evaluation is in simulation; sim-to-real transfer is open under class-level noise analogs, not calibrated physical threats.
  • loopStage-fused guard evaluated post hoc under abstain-on-flag; closed-loop deployment and jointly adaptive attackers are future work.
  • releaseCode promised on publication, not with the preprint; per-step guard inputs exist only for DT, blocking OFT transfer.
  • threatInstruction-level attacks (paraphrase, prompt injection) and visual-reasoning models (CoT-VLA) are out of scope; AutoAttack and certified defenses deferred.
05

Open questions

  1. Does the latent-iterative fragility replicate in a second latent-iterative VLA (different backbone, different K), or is the 0.0% PGD-10 collapse specific to RD-VLA's Prismatic + K=12 weight-tied recurrence?
  2. Can an intermediate-representation probe (read from inside the recurrence, not its output) break the matched-FPR ceiling that both output-level probes hit?
  3. Would training-time interventions on the recurrence — Lipschitz regularization, adversarial training of g, convergence-monitoring losses — push ρ(K) below the OFT baseline without sacrificing clean SR?
  4. Is the OFT FGSM-vs-PGD-10 asymmetry recoverable under AutoAttack and a Hessian-trace probe, confirming the curvature-not-masking reading?
  5. Under a non-white-box threat model (transfer-only or query-limited), do the consistency and action-anomaly probes recover any positive defended-SR delta — or does the coherent-shift bound still bind?
  6. Does the structural-amplifier reading extend to the physical world — printed perturbations, lighting and sensor variation calibrated to real camera noise?
