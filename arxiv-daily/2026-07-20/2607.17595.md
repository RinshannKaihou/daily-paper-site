> **一句话总结：** 作者用"平均噪声序列 + 辅助迭代"的初等技巧，绕开 Moreau envelope 和多阶段 bootstrap，为带乘性噪声、任意范数收缩的随机逼近同时给出均方界和高概率界，并通过让步长对置信水平 δ 作对数级依赖，首次在乘性噪声下得到 sub-Gaussian 尾的全程（maximal）集中不等式。

> **TL;DR:** This single-author theory paper gives a unified, elementary proof of both mean-square and maximal concentration bounds for stochastic approximation (SA) under arbitrary-norm contractions and multiplicative noise with possibly unbounded iterates. Using an averaged-noise / auxiliary-iterate construction (Bravo–Cominetti) plus an induction on "good" events, the author matches Chen et al.'s [13] mean-square rate of O(σ² log d / ((1−λ)³ k)) for the ℓ∞ norm without any Moreau envelope, and—by allowing the initial stepsize to scale as β₀ = O(1/log(1/δ))—obtains the first sub-Gaussian-tailed (log(1/δ) rather than log^m(1/δ), m>1) maximal concentration bound under multiplicative noise, improving on the Weibull-tail bound of Chen et al. [15]. There are no empirical experiments; the contribution is entirely proof-theoretic, with prior bounds (Chen et al. [13, 15]) serving as the baselines.

## 核心贡献

- **统一且初等的证明框架**：用"平均噪声序列 ξ_{k+1} = (1−β_k)ξ_k + β_k M_{k+1}"和辅助迭代 z_k = x_k − ξ_k 直接对范数误差 ‖z_k − x*‖²_c 写出单步 Lyapunov drift 不等式，**不再需要** Chen 等人 [13, 14] 的广义 Moreau envelope 平滑技巧，也不再需要 Chen 等人 [15] 的多阶段 bootstrapping + 指数上鞅 + Ville 极大不等式。
- **均方误差界（Theorem 1）**：在乘性噪声 E[‖M_{k+1}‖²_c | F_k] ≤ σ²(1 + ‖x_k‖²_c) 且 β ≥ 2/(1−λ) 下，给出 E[‖x_k − x*‖²_c] ≤ c2/(k+h)；对 RL 中常用的 ℓ∞ 范数，速率为 O(σ² log d / ((1−λ)³ k))，**与 Chen 等人 [13] 同阶**。
- **首个乘性噪声下的 sub-Gaussian 尾 maximal 集中界（Theorem 2）**：以至少 1 − π²δ/3 的概率，对**所有** k ≥ 0 同时成立 ‖x_k − x*‖²_c ≤ c4·log(d(k+1)/δ)/(k+h)。这是首个在乘性噪声 + 可能无界迭代设定下、尾分布为 sub-Gaussian（而非 Weibull）的全程集中界。
- **概率归纳（probabilistic induction）技巧**：通过一列"good events" C_k（A_k ∩ B_k ⊆ C_k），把首次坏事件的时间分解出来，在 C_i 上把 M_{i+1} 替换为被 indicator 截断的 M̃_{i+1} = M_{i+1}·1_{C_i}，从而绕开"无 a.s. 界→无法直接套 Azuma–Hoeffding"的障碍。
- **与不可逾越性结果的对偶关系**：Chen 等人 [15] 已证明在 δ 无关步长下不可能达到 sub-Gaussian 尾；本文证明只要允许步长对 δ 作极温和的对数依赖（β₀ = O(1/log(1/δ))），即可拿到 sub-Gaussian 尾，正面回应了该 impossibility 结果。
- **可推广性论述**：作者明确指出该技巧对 Markovian / 重尾 / 长程相关噪声以及 SSP Q-learning、RVI Q-learning 等两时间尺度或非膨胀算法同样适用，因为"good events"的概率归纳只依赖于"迭代无 a.s. 界"这一特征。

## Key Contributions

- **Unified, elementary proof framework.** Replaces two pieces of heavy machinery used in prior work—the generalized Moreau envelope of Chen et al. [13, 14] for smoothing non-smooth arbitrary norms, and the multi-stage bootstrapping + exponential supermartingale + Ville inequality of Chen et al. [15] for concentration—with a single averaged-noise / auxiliary-iterate construction that yields a one-step Lyapunov drift inequality for ‖z_k − x*‖²_c directly.
- **Mean-square bound (Theorem 1).** Under multiplicative noise E[‖M_{k+1}‖²_c | F_k] ≤ σ²(1 + ‖x_k‖²_c) and β ≥ 2/(1−λ), proves E[‖x_k − x*‖²_c] ≤ c2/(k+h) for all k ≥ 0, with rate O(σ² log d / ((1−λ)³ k)) for the ℓ∞ norm—matching Chen et al. [13] order-wise without ever smoothing the norm.
- **First sub-Gaussian-tailed maximal concentration bound under multiplicative noise (Theorem 2).** With probability ≥ 1 − π²δ/3, simultaneously for all k ≥ 0, ‖x_k − x*‖²_c ≤ c4·log(d(k+1)/δ)/(k+h). The tail is log(1/δ) (sub-Gaussian), strictly sharper than the log^m(1/δ), m ≥ 1 (Weibull) tail of Chen et al. [15].
- **Probabilistic induction over "good events."** Decomposes the first failure time of the events C_k (control on the iterates) so that, conditioned on prior good events, the noise M̃_{i+1} = M_{i+1}·1_{C_i} is bounded a.s., unblocking a vanilla Azuma–Hoeffding argument.
- **Constructive counterpart to a known impossibility.** Chen et al. [15] proved no δ-independent stepsize can give a sub-Gaussian tail under multiplicative noise; this paper shows that allowing the initial stepsize to scale merely as β₀ = O(1/log(1/δ)) is sufficient—pinpointing the minimal relaxation needed.
- **Roadmap for extension.** The averaged-noise trick and the good-events induction are stated to transfer to Markovian / heavy-tailed / long-range-dependent noise (via Poisson-equation martingale decomposition) and to unbounded-iterate algorithms such as SSP Q-learning and RVI Q-learning.

## Methodology

The paper is purely theoretical; there are no simulations or experiments. The setting is the SA iteration x_{k+1} = x_k + β_k(f(x_k) − x_k + M_{k+1}) with β_k = β/(k+h), where f is a γ-contraction (γ < 1) in an arbitrary norm ‖·‖_c with fixed point x*, and {M_{k+1}} is a martingale-difference noise satisfying either a second-moment (Assumption 2, for mean-square) or an a.s. (Assumption 3, for concentration) multiplicative bound ‖M_{k+1}‖²_c ≤ σ²(1 + ‖x_k‖²_c). The analysis proceeds in four reusable pieces:

1. **Averaged noise + auxiliary iterates.** Define ξ_{k+1} = (1 − β_k)ξ_k + β_k M_{k+1} (ξ_0 = 0) and z_k = x_k − ξ_k. Lemma 3 shows the iteration rewrites as z_{k+1} = z_k + β_k(f(z_k) − z_k + Δ_k) with ‖Δ_k‖_c ≤ λ‖ξ_k‖_c, and yields a one-step Lyapunov drift inequality ‖z_{k+1} − x*‖²_c ≤ (1 − λ'β_k)‖z_k − x*‖²_c + (3/λ')β_k‖ξ_k‖²_c. This is the only place contraction is used and is norm-agnostic—no Moreau envelope is invoked.
2. **Bounding ‖ξ_k‖_c.** Lemma 4 uses norm-equivalence (passing through the Euclidean norm, exploiting martingale-difference orthogonality, then passing back) to get E[‖ξ_k‖²_c] ≤ ζ_1(‖·‖_c, d) · Σ E[‖M_{i+1}‖²_c] β_i² Π(1 − β_j). Sharper constants via the martingale type-2 constant of the norm are noted for ℓ∞ and others (Appendix A.2).
3. **Mean-square proof (Theorem 1) via deterministic induction.** Lemma 5 shows that if E[1 + ‖x_i‖²_c] ≤ Γ_1 for i < k, then E[‖ξ_m‖²_c] and E[‖z_k − x*‖²_c] are controlled; Lemma 6 closes the loop by exhibiting Γ_2 = 2 + 8‖x_0 − x*‖²_c + 4‖x*‖²_c such that the same Γ_2 bound holds at time k, so by strong induction it holds for all k.
4. **Concentration proof (Theorem 2) via probabilistic induction.** Define good events A_k (control on ‖z_k − x*‖²_c), B_k (control on ‖ξ_k‖²_c), and C_k (control on ‖x_k − x*‖²_c), each with the form (constant · log(d(k+1)/δ))/(k+h). Lemma 7 decomposes ∪_k C_k^c by first failure time of A_k or B_k; Lemma 8 shows P(∩_{i<k}(A_i ∩ B_i) ∩ A_k^c) = 0 and reduces the B_k-failure probability to a martingale concentration question for the truncated increments M̃_{i+1} = M_{i+1}·1_{C_i}; Lemma 9 applies Azuma–Hoeffding coordinate-wise (the log d factor comes from a union bound over d coordinates) and sums over k to get the δ/(k+1)² tail, which is summable to π²δ/3.

A crucial observation is that the noise enters the analysis only through the better-behaved averaged sequence ξ_k, which is what makes the rest of the argument tractable. Appendices C.1 and C.2 specialize both theorems to the additive-noise model, recovering known results with simpler arguments and removing the δ-dependence of h.

## Results & Highlights

No empirical experiments are reported; this is a pure theory paper. The quantitative results are theorem statements and rate comparisons, with prior bounds as the "baselines":

- **Mean-square rate matches the prior best (Theorem 1, Remark 3).** Under arbitrary-norm contraction and multiplicative noise, E[‖x_k − x*‖²_c] ≤ c2/(k+h). For the ℓ∞ norm, the rate is **O(σ² log d / ((1−λ)³ k))**, **matching** the rate of Chen et al. [13] (the prior best for arbitrary-norm contraction), while avoiding their generalized Moreau envelope entirely.
- **First sub-Gaussian maximal concentration bound under multiplicative noise (Theorem 2).** With probability ≥ 1 − π²δ/3, **simultaneously for all k ≥ 0**: ‖x_k − x*‖²_c ≤ **c4 · log(d(k+1)/δ) / (k+h)**. The tail in δ is **log(1/δ)** (sub-Gaussian), whereas the best prior result (Chen et al. [15]) has tail **log^m(1/δ)** with m ≥ 1 possibly greater than 1 (Weibull). The ℓ∞ specialization is **O(σ² log(d(k+1)/δ) / ((1−λ)³(k+h)))**, matching the mean-square rate up to the expected log(1/δ) factor of a high-probability bound.
- **Sharp characterization of the price of sub-Gaussianity (Remark 4).** Chen et al. [15] proved an impossibility: no δ-independent stepsize can yield a sub-Gaussian tail under multiplicative noise. This paper shows the **minimal** relaxation needed: take the initial stepsize β₀ = **O(1/log(1/δ))** (equivalently h = Ω(log(1/δ))). If instead h is kept δ-independent, the same proof yields a sub-Gaussian bound that only kicks in after k_0 = Ω(log(1/δ)) steps.
- **Additive-noise corollary (Appendix C).** When the noise is uniformly bounded, h no longer needs to grow with log(1/δ) to obtain the sub-Gaussian tail, recovering the qualitative behavior of Chen et al. [15] via a strictly simpler argument.
- **Dimension dependence.** All bounds depend on dimension only through **log d** (for ℓ∞), arising from the coordinate-wise union bound in Lemma 9—an improvement in transparency over envelope-based proofs whose constants are less explicit.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (genuine).** The substantive novelty is twofold: (i) porting the Bravo–Cominetti averaged-noise / auxiliary-iterate construction (originally for non-expansive SA) to arbitrary-norm contractions to get a one-step drift inequality without any Moreau envelope, and (ii) the "probabilistic induction over good events" technique that converts unbounded-iterate SA into a sequence of bounded-increment problems where Azuma–Hoeffding applies directly. Both are real methodological simplifications. However, the averaged-noise trick itself is **not** the author's invention—it is attributed to Bravo and Cominetti [7]—so the novelty lies in the combination and the concentration application, not in the core construction.
- **Rigor (high).** Full proofs are given with explicit constants; the induction in Lemma 6 is carefully set up with a self-consistent Γ_2; the union bound over coordinates producing the log d factor is spelled out. The comparison with Chen et al. [15] in Remark 4 is precise about what is identical (unconditional, all-time, unbounded iterates, multiplicative noise) and what differs (δ-dependent stepsize).
- **The δ-dependent stepsize is a real caveat, not a free lunch.** The sub-Gaussian tail is bought by choosing β₀ (or h) using δ before running the algorithm. In practice one usually wants a single trajectory that simultaneously gives high confidence for many δ; this formulation requires committing to δ up front. The author is honest about this (Remark 4 explicitly states "a sub-Gaussian tail and a δ-independent stepsize sequence cannot be obtained simultaneously"), but users should weigh this before citing the result as a strict improvement over Chen et al. [15]—it is an improvement *in a relaxed formulation*, and the relaxation is exactly the one Chen et al. proved necessary.
- **Missing ablations / comparisons.** There is **no comparison of constants** with Chen et al. [13, 15]—only order-wise matching. Since the advertised contribution includes "elementarity" and "sharpness," a numerical side-by-side of c2 and c4 against the envelope-based constants would have strengthened the claim that the simplification costs nothing. The paper also does not work out any concrete RL example end-to-end (e.g., a finite-state TD(0) or Q-learning instance with the constants instantiated), even though RL is the stated motivation.
- **Reproducibility.** As a pure theory paper, reproducibility reduces to proof-checking; the explicit constants in Appendices A and B and the clean lemma decomposition make this straightforward. There is no code, but none is needed.
- **Significance.** Moderate-to-high for the SA/RL theory community. The sub-Gaussian maximal bound under multiplicative noise closes a known gap (and pairs neatly with the Chen et al. [15] impossibility result), and the elementary proof template is likely to be reused—especially the good-events induction, which is algorithm-agnostic.
- **Red flags.** None. No overclaiming beyond what is proven; no cherry-picked regimes (the only "baseline" is the prior state of the art, and the comparison is apples-to-apples with the formulation difference clearly flagged).

## Strengths

- Genuinely elementary proof: the entire mean-square bound rests on one drift inequality (Lemma 3) plus a one-page induction (Lemma 6); the concentration bound adds the clean event-decomposition of Lemma 7. This is materially simpler than the Moreau-envelope + exponential-supermartingale stack of prior work.
- Honesty about the formulation change that buys the sub-Gaussian tail (Remark 4 is a model of careful comparison, including an explicit acknowledgment of the matching impossibility result).
- Explicit constants throughout, which is rare and useful for downstream applications.
- Clean generalizability discussion (Section 4) that names concrete target algorithms—SSP Q-learning, RVI Q-learning, heavy-tailed-noise SA—rather than gesturing vaguely.
- The good-events induction is reusable beyond SA; it is essentially a generic tool for any algorithm with unbounded iterates.

## Limitations

- **No experiments.** Pure theory; the RL motivation (Q-learning, TD(0), SSP Q-learning) is invoked but no instance is worked out numerically.
- **δ-dependent stepsize.** The headline sub-Gaussian result requires β₀ = O(1/log(1/δ)), so a single run cannot serve all confidence levels simultaneously. This is intrinsic (given Chen et al. [15]'s impossibility) but limits practical applicability.
- **Order-wise only.** Constants c1–c4 are explicit but not compared head-to-head with those of Chen et al. [13, 15]; the claimed "no sacrifice in sharpness" is established only at the level of big-O.
- **Restricted stepsize form.** Results are stated for β_k = β/(k+h); other stepsize schedules are only asserted to be obtainable "using the techniques presented," without worked examples.
- **Multiplicative bound is on the second moment / a.s. norm of M_{k+1}.** Heavier-tailed or adversarial noise regimes are only discussed, not analyzed.
- **Single author, single pass.** Some expositional tightening is possible (e.g., the constant ζ_1 vs. ζ_2 distinction and the role of martingale type-2 constants could be unified), and a few forward references in Section 3 send the reader to Appendices A/B for proofs that are conceptually load-bearing for the main claim.

## Relevance

对于关注强化学习理论、随机逼近有限时间分析和高概率收敛速率的同学，这篇论文直接给出 Q-learning / TD(0) 这类 ℓ∞-收缩算法在乘性噪声下的最新集中速率与最简证明模板，是后续做 RL 算法 finite-time guarantee 时值得直接引用和对标的工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the δ-dependent stepsize be removed by a *time-varying* stepsize that adapts to an empirical noise estimate, recovering a sub-Gaussian tail with a single trajectory?
- How do the explicit constants c2 and c4 here compare numerically to those in Chen et al. [13] and [15] on a concrete finite-state MDP (e.g., a chain MDP where σ, λ, d are all known)? Is "matching the rate" actually matching the constants, or off by a non-trivial factor?
- Does the good-events induction extend to two-timescale SA (cf. Chandak et al. [10]) with multiplicative noise on both scales, where the slower timescale sees compounded noise?
- The averaged-noise trick was recently used for heavy-tailed and long-range-dependent noise in strongly monotone SA (Bertsekas/Chandak et al. [11] reference). Does combining that with the present arbitrary-norm result give a usable finite-time bound for Q-learning with heavy-tailed rewards?
- Is the log d factor for the ℓ∞ norm tight for this proof technique, or can a more careful coordinate-wise / martingale-type-2 argument shave it to log(something smaller)?
- The proof of Theorem 2 yields a π²δ/3 failure probability by summing δ/(k+1)²; could a refined (Freedman-style) martingale bound tighten the constant and reduce the required h = Ω(log(1/δ))?
