PoLoRA: A Preconditioned Orthogonalized LoRA Optimizer

Nikhil Ghosh · Tetiana Parshakova · Robert M. Gower  —  Flatiron Institute, CCM  |  github.com/nikhilgsh/polora ↗

Key finding

PoLoRA matches tuned Adam's final loss in 1.2–1.7× fewer steps, at ≤3% overhead — and its learning rate stays fixed across ranks.

§ 01

Methodology — three layers, each a constrained LMO

The intellectual move from "bound the spectral norm of the layer output" to "bound the per-sample loss change" is the most original piece.

PoLoRA is derived as steepest descent against a linear minimization oracle (LMO). The naive approach — applying Muon separately to factors A and B — fails because the loss sees the product BA, not the factors. The fix is to constrain the linearized merged update B∆A + ∆BA, add diagonal Kronecker curvature preconditioners P, Q (KL-Shampoo on factor gradients), and apply an explicit magnitude rule ρ = η ⁄ (‖A‖₂ + ‖B‖₂).

The result is a curvature-preconditioned matrix-sign step: ∆W = −τ P⁻¹ᐟ² · msign(P⁻¹ᐟ² G Q⁻¹ᐟ²) · Q⁻¹ᐟ², with a magnitude rule that — by triangle inequality + submultiplicativity — provably bounds ‖∆W‖₂ ≤ η on the merged product.

  1. BASE Muon. Solves the spectral-norm LMO; ∆W = −η·msign(G), where msign = UV⊤ from the reduced SVD. Conservative — bounds worst-case layer-output change.
  2. +PROD Product Muon (intermediate). Apply the spectral bound to B∆A + ∆BA, not to ∆A, ∆B separately. Yields two decoupled factorwise LMOs. Shown not to beat Adam on its own.
  3. +LOSS Per-sample loss control (core). Bound |ℓₓᵢ(W+∆W) − ℓₓᵢ(W)| ≤ τ via an "outergradient set" G(Σ) from gradient covariance Σ, then approximate Σ ≈ Q ⊗ P.
  4. +LORA LoRA instantiation. Substitute the linearized product into the preconditioned LMO — factorwise directions D_A, D_B via small r×r matrices C_B = B⊤PB, C_A = AQA⊤.
  5. +ρ Magnitude rule. ρ = η/(‖A‖₂+‖B‖₂) sets a common spectral step; provably bounds the merged product. Final: ∆A = −ρ D_A/‖D_A‖₂, ∆B = −ρ D_B/‖D_B‖₂.

§ 03

Strengths & Limitations

+

Strengths

  • Unified LMO framework cleanly separates Muon, Product Muon, and PoLoRA, making each ingredient's contribution transparent.
  • Honest negative result — the natural baseline (Muon per factor) and concurrent product-aware variants (iMuon, LoRA-RITE) do not consistently beat Adam here.
  • Component ablation (Figure 2) directly quantifies curvature vs. magnitude contributions — rare and valuable.
  • LR transfer across ranks is a genuine practitioner benefit, clearly demonstrated (Figure 3).
  • Low overhead (≤3%) with wall-clock speedup within a hair of step speedup — gains are practically realizable, not a step-count illusion.
—

Limitations

  • Held-out loss only — no downstream task accuracy (HumanEval, GSM8K, MATH) or longer-horizon training.
  • Diagonal preconditioners for efficiency; the Kronecker Σ ≈ Q ⊗ P approximation is itself unvalidated against denser alternatives.
  • Linearizations of both the loss and the product update — the dropped ∆B∆A quadratic term is not controlled empirically.
  • Only language-model LoRA — DoRA, VeRA, PiSSA and vision/multimodal modalities untested.
  • "Speedup grows under distribution shift" rests on a single low-resource language (Bengali).
  • Shrinking gain at 8B on code (1.20×) leaves open whether benefits persist at 70B+.
§ 04

Open questions for further work

Loss → quality Do step speedups translate to downstream task gains (HumanEval, GSM8K, MATH) at matched compute, or does the held-out-loss advantage vanish on real benchmarks?
Scale How does PoLoRA behave at 70B scale and on longer horizons — does the shrinking 8B-on-code speedup continue, or stabilize?
Preconditioner density What is the cost/benefit of replacing diagonal P, Q with block-diagonal or low-rank Kronecker factors — does it recover additional speedup?
Beyond LoRA Does the per-sample-loss-control LMO view yield gains in full pretraining, and how does PoLoRA interact with DoRA / VeRA / PiSSA?
High-order terms Can the analysis explicitly control the dropped ∆B∆A quadratic term, and does it matter at high LR or very high ranks?
Robustness Is the "speedup grows under distribution shift" finding robust across multiple low-resource languages and domains beyond Bengali?
