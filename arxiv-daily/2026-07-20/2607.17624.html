01 Key metric
2–3×
faster convergence on ADD & MANO over a maxed-out baseline — and +22 pp absolute test accuracy when a MANO-tuned architecture is retrained on MANO.
02 Why this matters

A reusable task–architecture compatibility framework: language and code share inductive biases, but algorithmic reasoning is nearly orthogonal — directly informing LLM reasoning & length-generalization research.

03 Critical edge

Scale is tiny — 1–4 layers, widths 32–512. The 2–3× speedups may evaporate at LLM scale, where prior work shows architecture choice matters less with more data.

i.

Methodology

  1. Parametrize the non-linearities. Replace GeLU in the MLP with a learnable 1-D linear spline ϕ_θ, and the softmax attention kernel with K(Q,K)=ϕ'(Q)ᵀϕ'(K). Splines carry no smoothness/monotonicity prior, so sharp activations survive.
  2. Stage I — architecture search. Hold out a fraction α (e.g. 20%) of data; train M models in parallel (M=8 algorithmic, M=3 language) sharing the same splines. The two-loss + multi-model trick prevents weight/activation co-adaptation.
  3. Stage II — fair compare. Freeze the splines, retrain weights from scratch. D'=D measures the local-optimality gap; D'≠D measures cross-task compatibility.
  4. Honest baselines. Canon layers (Allen-Zhu 2025) and tuned LR/batch/width — no straw men. 6-seed averaging on algorithmic tasks.

iii.

Strengths

  • Asks a clean, important question — are standard transformers locally optimal per-task? — and answers it with a falsifiable method, not rhetoric.
  • The two-loss + multi-model Stage I genuinely prevents co-adaptation; that is the real methodological contribution and the reason Stage II transfers.
  • Unusual intellectual honesty: softmax failure, the practical uselessness of the tiny LM gains, and the failure of "cleaning" splines are all reported in the open.
  • The compatibility matrix is a reusable experimental paradigm other groups can apply to new architectures.
  • Activation visualisations (Fig. 6/7/12) make the inductive-bias findings legible.
iv.

Limitations

  • Toy scale. 1–4 layers, widths ≤512, ≤5000 steps. Whether 2–3× speedups or the compatibility conclusions survive at billion-parameter scale is unaddressed.
  • Narrow search space. Scalar splines cannot represent GLU-style multiplicative interactions or multi-dimensional activations — yet GLU is used as a baseline.
  • Softmax replacement essentially fails (barely matches or underperforms; init-at-softmax gets stuck) — effective scope is MLP activations only.
  • Stage I cost ≈ 8× baseline (M parallel models + shared arch); polynomial trick helps inference, not search.
  • Fixed-step protocol for the matrices makes absolute numbers protocol-dependent; English + 2 code langs only; no multi-task joint optimisation.
v.

Open questions for further exploration

  • Does joint multi-task architecture optimisation find a single universal architecture, or is the no-free-lunch trade-off fundamental? Mixture · NFL
  • Do the 2–3× algorithmic speedups persist at 100M–1B parameters, or does over-parameterisation wash them out? Scaling
  • Can the learned wavelet-like MLP non-linearities be hand-closed-formulated (Gabor / Sinc) as a drop-in GeLU replacement — no Stage-I search? Compact surrogate
  • Why does softmax-as-spline optimisation get stuck — landscape (softmax near-optimal) or scalar-kernel pathology? Attention
  • Would the same method on ViT and wav2vec reveal an identical "language/code cluster, algorithmic outlier" structure, or a new modality taxonomy? Vision · speech
  • Can the two-loss + multi-model trick generalise as a regulariser for other meta-learned components — positional encodings, norms, MoE routers? Generalisation
