> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一个"语义映射"(semantic map)，将语言模型在词汇/短语上的条件概率通过预指定的语义分组 + 留存集仿射 log-ratio 校准 + split-conformal 不确定性集合，桥接为可审计的有限状态后验估计；在美联储市场文本与可控三状态贝叶斯网络两类实验上，词组概率显著优于模型自报数字（准确率 0.885 vs 0.815），并在 GPT-4.1-mini / GPT-4o-mini 上以约 0.037 的 Jensen–Shannon 误差与 0.90–0.94 的 conformal 覆盖率恢复精确后验。

> **TL;DR:** The paper formalizes a *semantic map* — a prespecified, testable bridge from continuation probabilities over verbal responses to a posterior over a finite declared state space — and validates it on Federal Reserve market text (Risk-on / Mixed / Risk-off / Unsure) and on a controlled three-state Bayesian-network generator with exact posteriors, across GPT-4.1-mini and GPT-4o-mini. Continuation-derived lexical probabilities beat direct numerical elicitation on the same 200 records (accuracy 0.885 vs 0.815, McNemar p = 0.0243; log-loss delta -0.788, Brier delta -0.144), recover exact held-out posteriors with mean Jensen–Shannon error ~0.037–0.039 and 90%/94% split-conformal coverage, stay stable under paraphrase (93.8%/100% within the JS margin), and move in the scientifically correct direction (95.8%/100% of repeats) when evidence is contradicted. Raw candidate-word entropy is essentially uncorrelated with reference posterior entropy (r ≈ -0.001) but calibrated lexical-posterior entropy correlates at r = 0.742, demonstrating that semantic calibration — not token entropy — is what produces a usable posterior.

## 核心贡献

- **定义一个可声明的"语义测量"估计量**：将 LLM 视作在有限词汇上的条件离散响应模型（prompt = 协变量，continuation = 响应），并通过预指定的语义分组 φ_u 把短语概率 pushforward 到有限状态 simplex 上；明确区分"参考后验 π*"、"词法组合 p_u"、"校准估计 π̂"与"未表达质量 ⊥"四种概率量。
- **形式化误差分解定理 (Theorem 1)**：给出 L1 界 ‖p̂ − p‖_1 ≤ 2(e_sem + e_score)/m_0，把语义错配、概率计算误差和未表达质量三者分开，避免把"数值精度高"误读为"语义定义正确"。
- **逆问题恢复与可观测性 (Theorem 3)**：在 log-ratio (Aitchison) 坐标下用 lower modulus c_u 表征语言通道对参考后验的可分辨性，给出 ‖ℓ̂ − ℓ*‖_2 ≤ 2δ/c_u 的恢复界；以仿射校准的最小奇异值作为样本域可观测性诊断。
- **扰动稳定性 (Theorem 2) 与序贯贝叶斯滤波 (Theorem 4)**：刻画等价改写/重排下的语义粗化稳定性 d_TV(p, p′) ≤ 2 d_TV(Q, Q′)/m_0，并用 Birkhoff 收缩系数 ρ 给出可观测递归缺陷 δ_rec^t 的轨迹误差界。
- **一套 falsifiable 的实验协议**：discovery / semantic validation / calibration / model-selection / conformal / final-test 六类不相交分区，以 scenario 为聚类单位；2 个 LLM、200 条市场文本、520 + 520 场景的对照实验，并把"信息保留改写"、"证据删除"、"无关干扰"、"矛盾证据"作为四类扰动单独评估。

## Key Contributions

- A **declared estimand**: P(state | evidence) is treated as a finite-valued latent-state posterior supplied by a frozen reference model (Bayesian network, HMM, or expert likelihood), independent of the LLM being audited. The LLM only contributes an observable conditional distribution over verbal continuations.
- A **semiparametric semantic map** that combines (i) Markov-kernel pushforward for semantic grouping, (ii) additive-log-ratio coordinates for probability vectors on the simplex, and (iii) affine held-out calibration ψ_u estimated on a disjoint scenario partition.
- Four **paper-specific theorems** (semantic-error decomposition, presentation perturbation, inverse-recovery lower modulus, sequential filtering trajectory bound) that turn previously informal ideas — semantic equivalence classes (Farquhar et al. 2024; Kuhn et al. 2023), conformal coverage (Angelopoulos & Bates 2023), and filter stability (van Handel 2009) — into a measurable acceptance test for an auditable posterior estimator.
- A **clear separation of error sources** that lets evaluators attribute failure to one of: inadequate verbal coverage (low M_u), poor inverse conditioning (small σ_min), presentation sensitivity (large η_nuis), or weak evidence responsiveness (small κ_rel).
- An **empirical protocol** with frozen semantic partitions, scenario-clustered bootstrap, 90% split-conformal radius, and a cross-model replication design across two OpenAI mini models, deliberately testing nuisance stability and evidence responsiveness rather than reporting a single accuracy number.

## Methodology

The framework treats the fitted LLM as a black-box conditional response law. For a prompt specification u and evidence y, the LLM produces a context c = C_u(y) and a Markov kernel Q_u(· | c) over finite continuations ω ∈ Ω = V^{<∞}. The complete-continuation probability is the product of next-token probabilities (Eq. 1), not the first-token probability.

A **semantic coarsening** φ_u : Ω → X ∪ {⊥} maps each continuation to one of K declared states or to an explicit unexpressed category ⊥. The induced pushforward yields the **lexical composition** p_u(c) = μ_{u,k}(c) / M_u(c), where M_u(c) is the expressed mass; this normalization step is what makes low verbal coverage dangerous (Theorem 1 amplifies error by 1/m_0).

Calibration then estimates an inverse map ψ_u in additive-log-ratio coordinates (alr) so that π̂_u = alr^{-1} ∘ ψ_u ∘ alr ∘ p_u ∘ C_u. The affine class H_aff is primary because its smallest singular value has a direct observability interpretation: lower bounds 1.686 (GPT-4.1-mini) and 1.600 (GPT-4o-mini) confirm both alr directions are retained on the sampled calibration design. The authors are explicit that this is *finite-design* evidence, not a uniform identification theorem — the empirical pairwise lower modulus is effectively zero for GPT-4.1-mini and at machine precision for GPT-4o-mini.

The experiments split into two complementary studies:

1. **Semantic-orientation study** on professional market commentary (S&P 500, CBOE VIX, 2y/10y Treasury yields, IG/HY credit spreads) compiled from FRED. Target: US broad-equity risk orientation over the next five trading days with states Risk-on / Mixed / Risk-off / Unsure. 200 untouched records in 60 scenario clusters, paired comparison of lexical vs elicited probabilities, 5,000 cluster-bootstrap resamples.
2. **Controlled posterior-recovery study** with a finite three-state Bayesian-network generator whose exact posterior is computable. Per fitted model: 520 scenarios × 5 repeats = 2,600 observations split 240 / 80 / 100 / 100 into calibration / validation / conformal / untouched test.

Look-ahead bias is excluded by construction: the market study measures contemporaneous semantic orientation and never uses future returns; inferential look-ahead is excluded by freezing the semantic map, candidate phrases, calibration class, and partitions before opening the test partition.

## Results & Highlights

- **Lexical vs elicited channel on 200 untouched market records (60 clusters)**: Accuracy 0.885 vs 0.815, paired difference 0.070 with 95% cluster-bootstrap CI [0.011, 0.126], McNemar p = 0.0243. Paired log-loss difference -0.788 [-1.371, -0.317] and Brier-score difference -0.144 [-0.214, -0.078], both CIs excluding zero in favour of lexical measurement.
- **Exact-posterior recovery (controlled study, 100 untouched scenarios per model)**: Mean Jensen–Shannon error 0.0390 [0.0295, 0.0498] for GPT-4.1-mini-2025-04-14 and 0.0366 [0.0286, 0.0454] for GPT-4o-mini-2024-07-18; the strongest single-number baseline (raw candidate-word entropy as a proxy for posterior entropy) gives essentially zero correlation (r ≈ -0.001), while calibrated lexical-posterior entropy correlates at r = 0.742.
- **Split-conformal coverage at frozen 90% radius**: empirical coverage 0.94 (GPT-4.1-mini) and 0.90 (GPT-4o-mini) on 100 untouched scenarios each — nominal or conservative.
- **Affine observability (smallest singular value, 95% bootstrap lower bound)**: 1.686 [1.686, 2.061] and 1.600 [1.600, 2.028], both clearly positive on the sampled design; affine residual RMSE 7.62 and 7.35 in lexical log-ratio units.
- **Within–between error decomposition (squared Aitchison distance, 100 untouched scenarios × 5 repeats)**: within-scenario variation only 1.79% (GPT-4.1-mini) and 0.72% (GPT-4o-mini); the remaining 98.21% / 99.28% is stable scenario-level recovery error. Calibration-refit movement is smaller still: 0.067 [0.006, 0.231] and 0.047 [0.006, 0.143]. So more repeats would not help — improvement must target semantic representation and cross-scenario calibration.
- **Presentation stability on 24 validation scenarios**: information-preserving rewrites stayed inside the prespecified JS margin 93.8% (GPT-4.1-mini) and 100% (GPT-4o-mini) of the time. Placing the evidence before the state definitions dropped those rates to 70.8% and 81.3%, showing that "same facts" does not imply "same measurement."
- **Evidence-omission ablation**: removing evidence increased reference JS error by 0.173 [0.105, 0.244] (GPT-4.1-mini) and 0.200 [0.133, 0.268] (GPT-4o-mini), both intervals excluding zero — recovery genuinely uses the supplied evidence rather than surface response words.
- **Contradictory-evidence perturbation**: calibrated posterior moved in the direction of the exact changed posterior in 95.8% (GPT-4.1-mini) and 100% (GPT-4o-mini) of repeats, with mean directional cosines 0.838 and 0.913 — strong finite-design evidence of relevant-evidence responsiveness.
- **Constrained-law completeness audit**: minimum summed probability of the three enum codes was 0.999999808 (GPT-4.1-mini) and 0.999999816 (GPT-4o-mini), above the frozen 0.999999 threshold, with maximum residual mass 1.92 × 10^-7 and 1.84 × 10^-7 retained as a fourth outcome.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Genuine. The synthesis of pushforward measures, additive log-ratio coordinates, conformal coverage, and Birkhoff contraction into a single *semantic measurement* framework with explicit error decomposition (Theorem 1) is a real methodological contribution, distinct from prior semantic-entropy work (Farquhar et al. 2024; Kuhn et al. 2023) which targets hallucination detection rather than reference-posterior recovery.
- **Rigor**: Strong on the theory side — every theorem is proved, every assumption is named. The experiments follow the theory's dependencies rather than throwing undifferentiated benchmarks at the wall, and Table 5 maps each statistic to the precise statistical question it answers. But the empirics have real limitations: only **two LLMs, both OpenAI mini models**, both evaluated on the *same* controlled Bayesian network and the same 200 market records.
- **Reproducibility**: Mixed. The authors promise to deposit "prespecified presentations, scenario lists, probability tables, analysis programs and figure data," and the experimental design is described in enough detail (Table 4) to be reconstructable. But because the LLM is accessed as a closed service, exact reproduction applies only to archived observations — newly generated calls are *statistical replications*, not bit-identical reproductions. The market corpus uses locally cached FRED rather than point-in-time vintages, so revisions are not reconstructed.
- **Significance**: The conceptual reframing — "prompt engineering optimizes a wording-dependent response, whereas scientific use requires validated stability of application-relevant meaning" — is broadly applicable to classification, recommendation, and refusal auditing, not just probability elicitation. The bridge from token probabilities to declared-state posteriors addresses a real gap.
- **Red flags / overclaiming**: The paper is unusually disciplined about scope. It explicitly disclaims uniform identification (empirical pairwise lower modulus is effectively zero), unrestricted free-text total variation (top-k does not identify the full law), and time-uniform sequential coverage (the filtering theorem is stated but not empirically validated as a sequential guarantee). The headline conformal coverage numbers (0.94, 0.90) come from only **100 untouched scenarios per model** with the same three-state generator, so they are finite-design evidence, not population-level. The semantic-orientation comparison relies on 200 records in 60 clusters — modest. The "exact posterior" target comes from an artificial Bayesian network whose structure the analyst chose, so success partly reflects how favourable the chosen generator is; no comparison with a non-trivial real-world reference posterior (e.g. an expert-elicited medical model) is reported. The market study uses cached rather than point-in-time FRED data, weakening any historical-trading interpretation, though the authors flag this themselves.

## Strengths

- Mathematically tight: complete proofs, explicit assumptions, and an explicit Table 3 separating inherited machinery from paper-specific contributions — preventing the common overclaim that juxtaposing known tools constitutes novelty.
- The error decomposition (Theorem 1) is genuinely useful for practitioners because it tells them *which* component of the measurement to repair (semantic partition, probability calculation, or verbal coverage) instead of reporting a single opaque loss.
- The dual-study design (semantic-channel comparison + controlled posterior recovery) is complementary: the first tests realistic language measurement against a fixed reference protocol; the second provides an exact mathematical target. Agreement across both settings is more persuasive than either alone.
- Honest about failure modes: low expressed mass, evidence reordering (70.8% / 81.3% equivalence rates), irrelevant distractors (nonzero JS movement), and the gap between raw word entropy (r ≈ -0.001) and calibrated posterior entropy (r = 0.742) are all reported rather than buried.
- The "raw candidate-word entropy does not measure posterior entropy" finding is one of the most actionable takeaways for the broader LLM-uncertainty literature.

## Limitations

- Only two LLMs (GPT-4.1-mini, GPT-4o-mini) and both from the same vendor; no open-weight or larger model tested. The cross-model replication is therefore narrow.
- The controlled reference posterior is a three-state Bayesian network whose structure was chosen by the author; the "exact posterior" target is mathematically clean but does not stress-test the method against a high-dimensional, messy real-world reference (e.g. a clinical diagnosis model with hundreds of states).
- All sequential/filtering claims (Theorem 4) are stated but **not** empirically validated; the paper is explicit that time-uniform coverage is outside its reported success.
- The market-text study is contemporaneous semantic-orientation measurement only — no return-prediction or revision-free historical forecasting claim can be made. Locally cached FRED data does not reconstruct macroeconomic revisions.
- Unrestricted free-text total variation is never identified: the structured enum grammar produces completeness ≥ 0.999999 only because the response is constrained to three codes; the same fitted model could behave very differently on free-form prose.
- The McNemar p = 0.0243 and accuracy delta of 0.070 are based on 200 records / 60 clusters — modest evidence, and the result is conditional on the chosen candidate phrases, presentation rule, and reference protocol.
- Single author and not peer-reviewed (working manuscript); an independent replication of the design protocol on a different domain would substantially strengthen the claims.

## Relevance

对用户在"语言模型不确定性量化 / 校准 / 语义不确定性 / 后验恢复"方向上的研究高度相关：本文给出了一个把 LLM token 概率审计化为可证伪统计测量问题的完整框架（误差分解 + 可观测性 + conformal 覆盖 + 序贯滤波），与 semantic entropy、conformal prediction、latent-variable calibration 等主题直接对接，可作为后续在金融/医疗文本或多步推理场景中扩展的模板。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the affine log-ratio calibration class remain well-conditioned (σ_min ≫ 0) when K grows beyond 3–4 states, or does the inverse problem become ill-conditioned enough to require the C^{1,1} nonlinear class the paper mentions but does not test?
- How does the framework behave on **open-weight** models (Llama, Qwen, Mistral) where the full continuation law over the unconstrained vocabulary can be enumerated more completely, removing the "top-k does not identify the unrestricted law" caveat?
- Can Theorem 4's filtering guarantee be empirically validated with a genuine sequential HMM reference, yielding the time-uniform confidence sequence the paper explicitly declines to claim?
- The contradictory-evidence directional cosine was 0.838 / 0.913 — what is the failure mode of the remaining 4.2% (GPT-4.1-mini) of wrong-direction movements, and is it concentrated in particular entropy strata or state pairs?
- How sensitive are the lexical-vs-elicited gains (Δlog-loss = -0.788) to the choice of elicitation prompt? The paper elicits once; a sweep over elicitation strategies would establish whether the gap is robust or prompt-specific.
- Could the semantic-map framework be inverted to *audit* whether an LLM's reported confidence is semantically stable across paraphrases, providing a quantitative test for the prompt-sensitivity failure modes flagged in recent agent-benchmark work?
