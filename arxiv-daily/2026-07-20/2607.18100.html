A training-free, inference-time intervention that targets a concretely diagnosed failure mode — directly relevant to cutting reasoning token cost.

§ 01

Methodology

training-free · per-cluster

SOPHIA splits two roles: an embedding model Memb (Qwen3-4B-Base, never chat-tuned) that defines stable cluster features, and a steering target Mtgt (Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507 in main runs; also Qwen3-32B-Thinking, Gemma-4-E2B). Reasoning traces are clustered into K=5 latent states per dataset; the dominant failure mode is a two-step self-loop.

OFFLINE PIPELINE

STAGE 1
Mean-pool step features

Segment traces at discourse markers; one forward pass of Memb yields token-mean last-layer features per step. Replaced an earlier cumulative-Gram feature that collapsed traces into one cluster.

STAGE 2
Per-dataset K-means (K=5)

Z-score per dataset, cluster with seed 42. Hungarian alignment <0.4 across datasets, so cluster ids are dataset-local — the bank is fit per dataset.

STAGE 3
Contrastive direction bank

β=1[zᵢ≠zᵢ₊₁] splits crossers from stayers. vc=h̄crosser−h̄stayer per (cluster, layer) — the Fisher discriminant under shared covariance.

▶ ONLINE CONTROLLER

Assign each new step to its nearest centroid (reusing KV cache); fire only after a two-step stayer gate (zi=zi−1); patch every token of the next step at every patched layer: h ← h + α·‖h‖·v/‖v‖. α=0.05 (Qwen-Thinking), 0.025 (Gemma). Patch removed once the step closes.

Theory: Prop. 1 — crosser−stayer mean difference is the Fisher linear discriminant. Lemma 1 — first-order Taylor exit response. Patched layers = upper decoder half (Qwen3-4B: 18–35) / mid-stack (Qwen3-32B: 18–35 of 64).

§ 02

Results

per-cluster hit rate · Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507

All numbers are per-cluster self-loop exit rate on N=30 stayer prefixes from held-out incorrect traces (Table 1). A "hit" = the next decoded step lands in a different cluster. Higher = better at escaping the loop.

Greedy SOPHIA

Fig. — Greedy (muted) vs. SOPHIA (vermillion) exit rate per cluster. Effect is direction-specific: Random steering at matched norm is indistinguishable from Greedy; Negative steering often collapses the rate.

  • Scale transfer (Qwen3-32B-Thinking): AQUA C0 53.3%→92.9%; MATH C3 29.6%→39.3%; GSM8K C1 89.7%→100%.
  • Cross-family (Gemma-4-E2B): AQUA C1 5.0%→29.2%; MATH C2 10.0%→30.8% — transfers without re-tuning.
  • Not magnitude-driven: Random steering ≈ Greedy (GSM8K C0: 7.1% vs 6.7%), ruling out a generic perturbation artifact.
  • Cluster-specific spread: +49pt on LOGIQA C2 but only +15pt on C4 — justifies the per-cluster bank over a single global direction.
  • Trajectory structure: dwell mass >0.93, backward mass exactly 0.000, and P(P5|P5)=1.000 on all four benchmarks — the rumination tail is an absorbing state.

§ 03 · Upside

Strengths

  • Clean, theoretically motivated formulation — Fisher-discriminant view (Prop. 1) + exit-response lemma make the design interpretable, not ad hoc.
  • Random + Negative ablation is exactly the right control to isolate direction-specific causality.
  • Embedding/target split (Memb vs Mtgt) decouples cluster stability from deployed-model swaps.
  • Trajectory analysis is a genuinely useful diagnostic: forward-only, P5-absorbing chain with zero backward mass.
  • Honest about the cumulative-Gram → mean-pool pivot (mono-cluster run 25–30 → 7–15).

§ 04 · Downside

Limitations

  • No end-to-end accuracy or token-budget results — abstract contradicts the TBD-marked body.
  • N=30 per cluster, no error bars, no significance tests.
  • No comparison to L1, SCoRe, SEAL, CertainDEX — only ablations of SOPHIA itself.
  • Per-dataset cluster refits mean the vector bank does not generalize across datasets.
  • Only self-loop→exit evaluated; traces already trapped in absorbing P5 are not rescued.
  • Greedy decoding only; single hand-tuned α per model; draft-quality artifacts (leaked file paths in Fig. 3b).
§ 05

Open questions

for further exploration
  1. Does breaking self-loops actually improve final-answer accuracy and cut total tokens, or just push the model into an equally unproductive cluster? The headline claim hinges on this — currently untested.
  2. How does SOPHIA interact with non-greedy decoding (temperature, top-p)? Stochastic decoding may already escape loops naturally.
  3. Can the per-cluster bank collapse to a single shared transition-gradient direction, given Hungarian alignment currently fails (<0.4)?
  4. What happens to traces already trapped in the absorbing P5 rumination tail — is P5 truly inescapable?
  5. Behavior under distribution shift (vectors fit on GSM8K, applied to MATH)? Cross-dataset transfer is not evaluated.
  6. Does the two-step stayer gate generalize to longer loops (3+, 4+ stays)?
  7. Can it compose with RL-based length-control (L1) or self-correction (SCoRe), or does activation steering conflict with policy-shaped decoding?
