Key Finding

Repurposing an inference accelerator for training hits 15.4× CPU speedup at ~1/14 the energy.

把商业推理加速器 Hailo-8L 复用于冻结主干的 INT8 特征提取，只在树莓派 5 CPU 上以 FP32 微调分类头，训练墙钟时延相对 CPU 基线加速最高 15.4×，每样本能耗降至约 1/14，并通过 PTQ 修复把精度损失控制在 ~0.06 个百分点。

01Methodology — Partitioned Execution

ONNX Runtime · HailoRT 4.23

Each network is split into a frozen backbone g(·;θ_b) and a trainable head h(·;θ_h). Backpropagation is mathematically truncated at the accelerator boundary — only the FP32 head updates on the host CPU.

STAGE · 01
Export & Quantize
PyTorch timm → ONNX → INT8 via Hailo Dataflow Compiler, with PTQ restoration.
STAGE · 02
Hailo-8L forward
Backbone embedded as HailoOp delegate node. Produces INT8 features at 13 TOPS / 1.5 W.
STAGE · 03
RPi5 CPU backward
FP32 head + cross-entropy + AdamW (lr 1e-3). Gradient truncated at the tensor boundary.
STAGE · 04
5-epoch head fine-tune
Fixed seed; test accuracy reported at batch 1; energy at wall, no idle subtraction.

Five PTQ restoration strategies

  • #0 None — raw INT8, no correction
  • #1 Eq. — channel equalization (low cost)
  • #2 Eq.+IBC — iterative bias correction, 64 imgs
  • #3 Eq.+FT — KD fine-tuning, 4 epochs / 1024 imgs
  • #4 Eq.+AR — AdaRound, 320 epochs / 256 imgs, all layers

Three-way hardware baseline

  • RPi5 CPU — FP32 backbone + FP32 head
  • Jetson Orin Nano — Ampere GPU, 1024 CUDA / 32 Tensor cores, 15 W
  • Proposed — Hailo-8L INT8 backbone + RPi5 FP32 head
  • 4 backbones · 2 datasets (CIFAR-100, Oxford-IIIT Pet) · batch 1/4/16
  • Calibration: 1024 ImageNet ILSVRC2012_val images

02Results — Architecture Is Destiny

CIFAR-100 · batch 1

Strengths

04
  • First open-source demonstration that a 1.5 W commercial inference accelerator can productively accelerate training of a frozen-backbone + trainable-head setup, with concrete wall-clock and energy wins.
  • Clean three-way benchmark (CPU vs edge GPU vs proposed) with honestly reported per-architecture variance and an explicit diagnosis of why MobileNetV3 benefits least.
  • Practitioner-friendly recipe: a cost-tiered PTQ ladder that maps directly to deployment trade-offs.
  • Honest limitations section naming the head-only restriction, missing memory analysis, and cross-domain gaps.

Limitations

06
  • Head-only fine-tuning — no PEFT, adapter, LoRA, or partial-backbone updates; the more impactful continual-learning regime is deferred.
  • Two of four architectures suffer double-digit accuracy gaps the pipeline cannot currently close — limits use to ReLU/SiLU CNNs.
  • No peak memory / SRAM-Flash footprint reported — a major omission for resource-constrained framing.
  • Only two image-classification datasets, 5-epoch budget; no cross-domain, regression, streaming, or forgetting evaluation.
  • Reproduction needs proprietary Hailo Dataflow Compiler + a high-end workstation — partially undercutting the "on-device" framing.
  • No variance across seeds — a single fixed seed is used throughout.

03Open Questions for Further Work

research directions
Can the pipeline extend to LoRA / adapter / last-block-unfreezing without breaking the INT8 throughput advantage — when even MobileNetV3's heavy head already bottlenecks the CPU-bound backward pass?
PEFT · throughput
How does restoration effectiveness degrade when the calibration domain (ImageNet val) is far from the downstream task — medical, industrial defect, satellite?
domain shift
What is the peak SRAM / Flash footprint, and how does it compare to the 256 KB / 1 MB budget of the TinyML baseline?
memory
For fragile architectures, would quantization-aware training or mixed-precision (per-layer INT8/INT16) close the >10 pp gap that PTQ restoration leaves open?
quantization
Does the 15.4× speedup persist under continual-learning schedules with rehearsal, where the head is repeatedly retrained and the calibration distribution drifts?
continual learning
How sensitive are results to the Orin Nano power mode — would the comparison change at 7 W instead of 15 W?
