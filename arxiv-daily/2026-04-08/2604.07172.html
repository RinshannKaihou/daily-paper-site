Improving Semantic Uncertainty Quantification in Language Model Question-Answering via Token-Level Temperature Scaling

Tom A. Lamb, Desi R. Ivanova, Philip H. S. Torr, Tim G. J. Rudner

arXiv: 2604.07172v1

Date: 2026-04-08

Improving Semantic Uncertainty Quantification in Language Model Question-Answering via Token-Level Temperature Scaling

一句话总结： 首次系统性评估语义不确定性量化中的校准和区分度，发现固定温度启发式方法产生系统性误校准和弱区分力，而优化单一标量温度这一简单方法在语义校准、区分度和下游语义熵上均优于固定温度基线和更复杂的token级重校准方法。
TL;DR: First systematic evaluation of both calibration and discrimination in semantic UQ reveals fixed-temperature heuristics are systematically miscalibrated; optimizing a single scalar temperature consistently improves semantic calibration, discrimination, and downstream entropy, outperforming both heuristic baselines and more expressive methods.

核心贡献

  • 首次对语义不确定性量化中的校准和区分度进行系统性评估，发现现有方法的根本局限
  • 证明优化单一标量温度（temperature scaling）是提升语义UQ的简单有效方法，优于固定温度启发式和复杂重校准
  • 引入5种新的语义置信度度量（共评估7种），展示了温度缩放在所有度量上的一致改善
  • 表明基于语义置信度分布的最终响应选择优于先前的贪心解码方法

Key Contributions

  • First systematic evaluation of both semantic calibration and discrimination across a broad range of confidence measures
  • Shows that base models and fixed-temperature heuristics are poorly calibrated and weakly discriminative for semantic UQ
  • Demonstrates that optimizing a single scalar temperature outperforms both fixed baselines and complex methods like Adaptive Temperature Scaling (ATS) and Platt scaling
  • Introduces 5 novel semantic confidence measures (ML-SC, IC-SC, B-SC, T-SC, G-SC) alongside 2 existing ones (E-SC, L-SC)
  • Shows that principled response selection based on semantic confidence distributions yields superior discrimination over greedy decoding

Methodology

The authors evaluate 7 semantic confidence measures on question-answering tasks. Responses are generated from LMs and clustered into semantic groups via Natural Language Inference (NLI). Temperature scaling (TS) is applied as a post-hoc calibration method: a single scalar T is optimized on a calibration set to minimize the Expected Calibration Error (ECE). The optimized TS is compared against fixed temperature baselines (T=0.5, T=1.0) and more expressive methods including Adaptive Temperature Scaling (ATS, which uses a transformer head for token-specific temperatures) and Platt scaling. Evaluation covers both calibration metrics (ECE, MCE) and discrimination metrics (AUROC, AUPRC) across multiple LMs, QA datasets, and generation sample sizes.

Results & Highlights

  • Fixed-temperature heuristics (T=0.5) produce systematically overconfident semantic distributions
  • Optimized TS improves semantic calibration across all 7 confidence measures
  • TS outperforms ATS despite being far simpler (single scalar vs. transformer-based per-token temperatures)
  • TS improves downstream semantic entropy, exposing suboptimality of prevalent heuristics
  • Principled response selection from semantic confidence distributions outperforms greedy decoding
  • Validated across multiple LMs, QA datasets, and sample sizes
  • Published at AISTATS 2026

Critical Assessment

This is a rigorous and well-executed study that identifies and addresses an important gap in semantic UQ. The key insight -- that a single scalar temperature provides a better inductive bias than per-token recalibration for semantic calibration -- is surprising and practically impactful. The evaluation is thorough across multiple dimensions (7 confidence measures, multiple LMs, multiple datasets). The argument that global TS regularizes against overfitting to semantically hollow filler tokens is well-reasoned. A limitation is that the study focuses on QA tasks; whether the findings generalize to open-ended generation, summarization, or code generation is not established. The reliance on NLI for semantic clustering inherits known limitations of NLI models.

Strengths

  • Addresses a critical gap: calibration has been neglected in favor of discrimination in semantic UQ
  • Surprising and practically impactful finding: simple scalar TS outperforms complex methods
  • Comprehensive evaluation across 7 confidence measures, multiple LMs, and datasets
  • Published at AISTATS 2026 with reproducible code
  • Clear argument for why global TS provides a better inductive bias than per-token methods

Limitations

  • Evaluation limited to QA tasks; generalization to other generation tasks is not established
  • Relies on NLI for semantic clustering, inheriting NLI model limitations
  • Does not address the theoretical question of what constitutes an optimal semantic confidence measure
  • The improvement from TS may depend on the base model's initial miscalibration pattern

Relevance

该工作直接解决LLM输出校准和不确定性量化问题，揭示了现有语义熵方法中固定温度启发式的系统性缺陷。温度缩放的简单有效性对于提升LLM推理输出的可靠性和一致性具有直接应用价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does optimized TS for semantic calibration transfer across task domains (QA to summarization to code generation)?
  • How does temperature scaling interact with chain-of-thought reasoning and long-form generation?
  • Can the findings be combined with conformal prediction methods for distribution-free uncertainty guarantees?
