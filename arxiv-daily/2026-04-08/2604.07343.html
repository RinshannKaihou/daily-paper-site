Personalized RewardBench: Evaluating Reward Models with Human Aligned Personalization

一句话总结： 提出了Personalized RewardBench基准，通过用户画像和个人评分标准构建选择/拒绝响应对，发现现有最先进奖励模型在个性化偏好建模上最高准确率仅75.94%，且该基准与下游BoN和PPO性能的相关性显著高于现有基线。
TL;DR: Introduces Personalized RewardBench, a benchmark that constructs chosen/rejected pairs based on user-specific rubrics from LaMP-QA profiles, revealing SOTA reward models peak at 75.94% accuracy on personalization and demonstrating superior correlation with downstream BoN and PPO performance compared to existing benchmarks.

核心贡献

  • 提出首个专注于个性化偏好评估的奖励模型基准，基于用户画像和个人评分标准构建响应对
  • 通过严格的人工评估验证了chosen/rejected响应在通用质量上相当，仅在个人偏好层面存在差异
  • 系统性揭示了现有奖励模型在个性化建模方面的显著不足（最高准确率仅75.94%）
  • 证明该基准与下游任务（BoN采样和PPO优化）性能的相关性显著高于现有基准

Key Contributions

  • First benchmark explicitly designed to evaluate reward models' capacity for personalized preference modeling
  • Rigorous human evaluation confirming that chosen/rejected pairs differ exclusively in personal alignment, not general quality
  • Demonstrates SOTA reward models struggle with personalization, peaking at 75.94% accuracy
  • Establishes superior correlation between benchmark scores and downstream BoN/PPO performance

Methodology

The benchmark leverages rich metadata from LaMP-QA, a personalized QA dataset, to construct chosen and rejected response pairs. Rejected responses are not low-quality but specifically violate user-specific rubrics contextualized by user profiles. Human evaluation validates that both responses maintain equivalent general quality across three dimensions (Factuality & Correctness, Relevance & Instruction Following, Helpfulness & Harmlessness), differing only in personal rubric adherence. The benchmark is validated against downstream policy performance in both Best-of-N sampling and PPO optimization.

Results & Highlights

  • Best SOTA reward model achieves only 75.94% accuracy on personalization
  • Personalized RewardBench shows significantly higher correlation with downstream BoN and PPO performance than existing baselines
  • Both chosen and rejected responses confirmed as high general quality through human evaluation
  • Establishes a new standard for evaluating reward models' personalization capabilities

Zhe Zhao has publications in NLP and information retrieval. The team provides code on GitHub and datasets on HuggingFace, supporting reproducibility. Critical Assessment

The paper addresses an important gap in reward model evaluation by moving beyond general quality toward personalized preference assessment. The methodology of constructing rejected responses that are not low-quality but merely violate personal rubrics is a genuine methodological innovation. However, the reliance on LaMP-QA profiles limits the diversity of personalization scenarios. The 75.94% ceiling may partially reflect benchmark difficulty rather than pure model limitation. The correlation with downstream performance is a strong validation, but the evaluation is limited to BoN and PPO -- other alignment methods (DPO, KTO) are not tested.

Strengths

  • Novel benchmark design isolating personal preference from general quality
  • Rigorous human evaluation validating the construction methodology
  • Strong downstream validation with both BoN and PPO showing superior correlation
  • Open-sourced benchmark and evaluation pipeline

Limitations

  • Limited to LaMP-QA user profiles; generalization to other personalization domains is unclear
  • Only BoN and PPO downstream tasks evaluated; DPO/KTO/GRPO not tested
  • The distinction between "personalization failure" and "genuine ambiguity" in rubrics needs further analysis
  • Binary chosen/rejected format may oversimplify the gradations of personal preference

Relevance

该工作对LLM对齐评估有直接意义，揭示了奖励模型在个性化建模方面的不足，对改进RLHF中的奖励模型设计和评估基准开发具有重要参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does personalization accuracy scale with the complexity and granularity of user profiles?
  • Can the benchmark be extended to multi-turn conversational personalization?
  • What architectural improvements would help reward models better capture personalized preferences?
