# Does a Global Perspective Help Prune Sparse MoEs Elegantly?

> **一句话总结：** 提出GRAPE全局冗余感知专家剪枝方法，根据跨层冗余动态分配剪枝预算，在Mixtral、DeepSeek-MoE、Qwen-MoE等模型上平均准确率比最强局部基线高1.40%，最高提升2.45%。

> **TL;DR:** GRAPE (Global Redundancy-Aware Pruning of Experts) dynamically allocates pruning budgets based on cross-layer redundancy rather than uniform per-layer allocation, achieving 1.40% average accuracy improvement over the strongest local baselines across Mixtral, DeepSeek-MoE, Qwen-MoE, and GPT-OSS models.

## 核心贡献

- 提出GRAPE全局剪枝策略，根据跨层冗余度动态分配剪枝预算，而非均匀分配
- 揭示MoE模型中不同层之间的冗余度存在显著差异，均匀剪枝忽略了这种异质性
- 在5个主流MoE模型上验证，同等剪枝预算下一致优于均匀层剪枝基线
- 在三个主要模型上，相比最强局部基线平均准确率提升1.40%，最高2.45%

## Key Contributions

- GRAPE: Global Redundancy-Aware Pruning of Experts that dynamically allocates pruning budgets based on cross-layer expert redundancy
- Identifies that redundancy varies substantially across MoE layers, and uniform pruning methods ignore this inter-layer variation
- Demonstrates consistent superiority over uniform layer-wise pruning across 5 MoE architectures
- Average accuracy improvement of 1.40% over strongest local baselines, with gains up to 2.45%

## Methodology

GRAPE first computes pairwise expert similarity (using output activations or weight parameters) within each MoE layer to identify redundant expert pairs. Unlike prior methods that allocate a fixed number of experts to prune per layer, GRAPE considers the global distribution of redundancy across all layers. Layers with higher expert redundancy receive larger pruning budgets, while layers with already-distinct experts are pruned less aggressively. The pruning decision is made by solving a global optimization problem that maximizes total preserved model capacity subject to a total parameter budget.

## Results & Highlights

- Evaluated on Mixtral-8x7B, Mixtral-8x22B, DeepSeek-MoE, Qwen-MoE, and GPT-OSS
- 1.40% average accuracy improvement over strongest local baseline across pruning settings
- Up to 2.45% accuracy gain on individual models
- Consistent improvement across different global pruning budgets
- GRAPE outperforms uniform pruning, visiting frequency-based, and feature-based baselines

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a clean and well-motivated contribution. The observation that MoE redundancy is heterogeneous across layers is intuitive but has been overlooked by prior work. The global budget allocation approach is principled. However, the accuracy improvements (1.40% average) are modest, and the paper does not thoroughly analyze the computational cost of the global optimization step. The comparison against local baselines is fair, but the paper could benefit from comparison against recent non-uniform pruning methods from the dense model literature. The evaluation is primarily on zero-shot benchmarks, and the impact on downstream fine-tuning tasks is unexplored.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated contribution addressing an overlooked aspect of MoE pruning
- Strong author team with statistical rigor (Bin Yu) and industrial expertise (Microsoft Research)
- Consistent improvement across 5 diverse MoE architectures
- The global perspective is a natural extension of existing local pruning methods

## Limitations

- Accuracy improvements are modest (1.40% average)
- Computational cost of global optimization not thoroughly analyzed
- No comparison against non-uniform pruning methods adapted from dense model literature
- Limited to zero-shot evaluation; impact on downstream fine-tuning unexplored

## Relevance

对MoE模型压缩和部署效率有直接参考价值。全局冗余感知的剪枝策略对理解MoE架构中不同层的专家冗余分布规律有启发意义，可指导实际MoE模型的压缩和部署策略设计。

## Questions for Further Exploration
