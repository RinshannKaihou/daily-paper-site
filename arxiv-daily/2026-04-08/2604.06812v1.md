# AGSC: Adaptive Granularity and Semantic Clustering for Uncertainty Quantification in Long-text Generation

> **一句话总结：** 提出AGSC框架，通过NLI中性概率触发器区分不相关与不确定信息，结合GMM软聚类对长文本生成进行不确定性量化，在BIO和LongFact基准上达到SOTA事实性相关性并减少约60%推理时间。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes AGSC, an uncertainty quantification framework for long-form LLM generation that uses NLI neutral probabilities and GMM soft clustering to achieve SOTA factuality correlation while reducing inference time by ~60%.

## 核心贡献

- 提出自适应粒度分解策略，使用NLI中性概率作为触发器，区分不相关信息与真正不确定的信息，避免不必要的细粒度分解
- 引入基于高斯混合模型（GMM）的软聚类方法，对长文本中的潜在语义主题进行建模，为主题感知的聚合分配权重
- 在BIO和LongFact基准上达到与事实性（factuality）的最先进相关性
- 相比完整原子分解方法减少约60%的推理时间

## Key Contributions

- Introduces adaptive granularity decomposition triggered by NLI neutral probabilities to distinguish irrelevance from genuine uncertainty, reducing unnecessary computation
- Proposes GMM-based soft clustering to model latent semantic themes in long-form text, enabling topic-aware weight assignment for downstream uncertainty aggregation
- Achieves state-of-the-art correlation with factuality on BIO and LongFact benchmarks
- Reduces inference time by approximately 60% compared to full atomic decomposition baselines

## Methodology

AGSC operates in two stages. First, it decomposes long-form LLM output into claims at adaptive granularity, using NLI neutral probabilities as triggers to determine when fine-grained decomposition is needed versus when coarser claims suffice. Second, it applies Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) soft clustering to model the latent semantic theme structure, assigning topic-aware weights for downstream uncertainty aggregation. The framework integrates these two components to produce a calibrated overall uncertainty score that correlates strongly with factual accuracy.

## Results & Highlights

- Achieves state-of-the-art correlation with factuality on BIO and LongFact benchmarks
- Reduces inference time by approximately 60% compared to full atomic decomposition
- GMM soft clustering captures multi-theme structure in long-form text that flat aggregation misses
- NLI neutral probability trigger effectively filters out irrelevant claims from uncertainty computation

## Critical Assessment

The paper presents a practical and well-engineered solution to an important problem. The dual innovation of adaptive granularity (via NLI triggers) and semantic clustering (via GMM) is sensible and yields clear efficiency gains. However, the method relies on an external NLI model as a sub-component, introducing dependency on its quality. The GMM clustering assumes a fixed number of mixture components, which may not adapt well to texts with highly variable topic structures. The evaluation is limited to two benchmarks, and the correlation with factuality, while SOTA, does not directly measure downstream utility (e.g., does better UQ actually help users make better decisions?).

## Strengths

- Significant inference time reduction (~60%) while maintaining or improving UQ quality
- Principled separation of irrelevance from uncertainty using NLI neutral probabilities
- Topic-aware aggregation via GMM is a natural fit for multi-theme long-form text
- Practical framework that can be integrated into existing LLM generation pipelines

## Limitations

- Relies on quality of external NLI model for neutral probability estimation
- GMM requires specifying number of mixture components a priori
- Evaluation limited to two benchmarks (BIO, LongFact)
- Does not address how uncertainty scores translate to actionable user decisions
