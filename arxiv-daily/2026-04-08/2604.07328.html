How to sketch a learning algorithm

一句话总结： 提出一种基于"稳定性"假设的数据删除方案，通过对算术电路进行局部素描（local sketching）——利用复数域上的高阶导数和前向模式自动微分——实现了在深度学习场景中以 poly(1/epsilon) 的开销预测删除训练数据后模型行为的目标。
TL;DR: Introduces a data deletion scheme based on a "stability" assumption, using local sketching of arithmetic circuits via higher-order derivatives in random complex directions with forward-mode AD, achieving vanishing prediction error at only poly(1/epsilon) overhead over standard training and inference.

核心贡献

  • 提出了"稳定性"（stability）假设：要求算术电路的 Taylor 展开导数项随阶数超线性衰减（2^{-omega(r)}），并通过 microgpt 实验验证了该假设的合理性
  • 设计了基于复数域随机投影和前向模式自动微分的局部素描（local sketching）方法，可在不显式构造高阶导数张量的情况下对其进行随机投影估计
  • 实现了完整的数据删除方案：预计算仅需 poly(1/epsilon) 倍训练开销，预测仅需 poly(1/epsilon) 倍推理开销，存储仅需 poly(1/epsilon) 个模型的参数量
  • 利用 Faa di Bruno 公式和快速子集卷积将素描方法从已知测量函数扩展到未知测量函数的数据删除场景

Key Contributions

  • Proposes the "stability" assumption for arithmetic circuits requiring super-linear decay of Taylor expansion terms, validated experimentally on microgpt
  • Develops a local sketching method using random complex projections and forward-mode AD to implicitly estimate higher-order derivative tensors without materializing them
  • Achieves a data deletion scheme with precomputation at poly(1/epsilon) training cost, prediction at poly(1/epsilon) inference cost, and poly(1/epsilon) model storage
  • Extends the sketching framework from known measurement functions to the full data deletion setting using the Faa di Bruno formula and fast subset convolution

Methodology

The paper models both training and inference as arithmetic circuits over the complex numbers. The core idea is to locally sketch a stable arithmetic circuit f = phi composed with A around the all-ones vector by computing higher-order directional derivatives in random complex directions using forward-mode automatic differentiation. The stability assumption ensures that the s-th order Taylor approximation has error roughly epsilon when s = log(1/epsilon). Random complex unit vectors psi are used to create unbiased estimates of the inner products between derivative tensors and deletion indicator vectors, with variance bounded by the Johnson-Lindenstrauss lemma. The median-of-means estimator is used for concentration. The framework departs from prior additivity assumptions and instead relies purely on Taylor expansion under stability.

Results & Highlights

  • Precomputation requires O-tilde(size(A) log(1/delta) / epsilon^2) operations
  • Storage requires O-tilde(p log(1/delta) / epsilon^2) parameters
  • Prediction for O(1) deletions requires O-tilde(log(1/delta)/epsilon^2 + size(phi) log(1/delta)/epsilon^3) operations
  • Prediction error is O(epsilon) with probability 1-delta under the stability assumption
  • Stability is empirically validated on microgpt, showing the assumption is compatible with training useful models

Authors & Credibility

Sam Gunn is a single-author researcher. Based on the paper's mathematical sophistication (arithmetic circuits, symmetric subspace theory, Faa di Bruno formula, forward-mode AD), the author demonstrates strong theoretical foundations likely from a top theory group. The paper is technically rigorous with detailed proofs and reproducible code (github.com/SamSpo1/microgpt-sketch). However, as a single-author work with limited prior publication visibility, independent verification of the stability assumption on larger-scale models would strengthen confidence.

Critical Assessment

The paper is mathematically elegant and technically rigorous, presenting a genuinely novel approach to data deletion that avoids the additivity assumption that limits prior work. The stability assumption is well-motivated and the microgpt experiments are a reasonable starting point, but the gap between microgpt and frontier models is enormous. The key concern is whether stability holds for models with billions of parameters trained on trillions of tokens -- the paper acknowledges this but provides only minimal evidence. The storage requirement of poly(1/epsilon) models could be prohibitive in practice. The paper is heavy on theory and light on empirical evaluation, which limits immediate practical impact but makes it a strong theoretical contribution.

Strengths

  • Principled theoretical framework that avoids the additivity assumption prevalent in prior data attribution work
  • Elegant use of forward-mode AD and random complex projections for implicit tensor sketching
  • Clear separation of concerns: stability assumption, sketching method, and data deletion scheme are cleanly decomposed
  • Honest about limitations and clear about what remains to be validated

Limitations

  • Empirical validation limited to microgpt (tiny model); scalability to frontier models entirely untested
  • Storage of poly(1/epsilon) models may be impractical for large models even at moderate epsilon
  • No comparison with existing influence function or datamodeling methods on real benchmarks
  • The stability assumption, while intuitive, lacks theoretical justification for why SGD-trained networks should satisfy it

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the stability assumption hold for models trained with Adam, weight decay, and learning rate schedules on internet-scale data?
  • What is the practical constant hidden in poly(1/epsilon) for frontier-scale models?
  • Can the local sketching technique be combined with influence function methods to provide complementary diagnostics?
  • How does stability relate to other notions of generalization and robustness in deep learning theory?
