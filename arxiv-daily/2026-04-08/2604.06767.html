Geometric Properties of the Voronoi Tessellation in Latent Semantic Manifolds of Large Language Models

一句话总结： 在 Qwen3.5-4B-Base 上验证了 Voronoi 镶嵌的线性标度律（R^2 = 0.9997），通过 float32 重计算消除 bfloat16 量化伪影，发现 Fisher 信息距离最大化可在 200 步内实现 +28% 中位 margin 提升且零下游基准退化，但收益高度集中于高频结构 token（84% 的净修正）。
TL;DR: Validates the Voronoi tessellation linear scaling law on Qwen3.5-4B-Base with R^2 = 0.9997 by resolving bfloat16 quantization artifacts via float32 recomputation, and shows Fisher information distance maximization achieves +28% median margin improvement with zero benchmark degradation, though gains concentrate in high-frequency structural tokens (84% of net corrections).

核心贡献

  • 识别并解决了 bfloat16 margin 量化伪影：bfloat16 推理导致 256K margin 值坍缩为仅 223 个唯一值，通过 float32 重计算恢复至 238,399 个唯一值，将标度律 R^2 提升至 0.9997
  • 发现了中层几何模糊性机制（layers 24-28）：margin 几何与交叉熵呈反相关（rho = -0.29），而最终层呈正相关（rho = 0.836）
  • 提出 Fisher 信息距离最大化作为 margin refinement 方法：在 lambda = 0.6 下实现 +28% 中位 margin 提升且附带损伤恒定约 5,300 个位置
  • 揭示了收益的高度不均匀性：lambda = 0.6 下 100+ 频率桶占净修正的 84%，结构 token 类占 65.9%

Key Contributions

  • Resolves bfloat16 quantization artifact in margin computation (223 unique values -> 238,399 with float32 recomputation), achieving R^2 = 0.9997 on the expressibility gap linear scaling law
  • Discovers mid-layer geometric ambiguity regime (layers 24-28) where margin geometry anti-correlates with cross-entropy (rho = -0.29) before crystallizing at the final layer (rho = 0.836)
  • Compares margin maximization vs Fisher information distance maximization: both find ~16,300 correctable positions, but Fisher has constant collateral damage (~5,300) vs escalating damage with margin maximization
  • Fisher MRP at lambda = 0.6: +28% median margin, zero downstream benchmark degradation, but gains concentrated in high-frequency structural tokens (84% of net corrections)

Methodology

The study analyzes Qwen3.5-4B-Base (4.21B parameters, 248,320-token vocabulary, 32 layers) on WikiText-103 validation set (256K token positions). The Voronoi margin is defined as the logit difference between the top-2 tokens at each position. Margins are recomputed in float32 by extracting final hidden states, upcasting, and recomputing the lm_head projection. Two margin refinement procedures (MRP) are compared across a dose-response curve (200 gradient steps each): (1) direct margin maximization (L_margin = -mean(m(h) | m(h) < threshold)) and (2) Fisher information distance maximization using the softmax covariance-induced metric on the top-k logits. Both use supervised CE loss with MRP regularization.

Results & Highlights

  • Expressibility gap scaling law: R^2 = 0.9997 (strongest confirmation of Mabrok 2026 Theorem 10.5)
  • Gap coefficient alpha = 0.762, entropy-margin Spearman rho = -0.648
  • Mid-layer geometric ambiguity: layers 24-28 show anti-correlation between geometry and cross-entropy
  • Margin maximization: +53% median margin at lambda = 0.3 but destructive above ~0.3
  • Fisher MRP: +28% median margin at lambda = 0.6, constant damage ~5,300 positions, zero benchmark degradation
  • Token-class audit: 84% of net corrections in 100+ frequency bucket, 65.9% in structural tokens

The paper builds directly on Mabrok (2026), which is referenced as establishing the latent semantic manifold framework. The technical depth is substantial -- the analysis of bfloat16 quantization artifacts, Fisher information distance, and Voronoi geometry requires strong mathematical foundations. The code is available at github.com/mellowmarshall/mrp-paper. However, as a single-author work from a non-established lab, independent verification would strengthen confidence.

Critical Assessment

This is a technically impressive single-author paper that makes several important contributions to understanding LLM representation geometry. The bfloat16 artifact discovery alone is significant -- it shows that precision limitations in the final projection layer can obscure the true geometric structure of the token decision boundaries. The Fisher vs margin maximization comparison is methodologically sound and the dose-response protocol is well-designed. However, concerns arise: (1) the paper builds heavily on Mabrok (2026) which itself is relatively new and untested; (2) the finding that improvements concentrate in high-frequency structural tokens is somewhat disappointing -- it means the method essentially makes easy decisions slightly easier rather than resolving genuine ambiguities; (3) the downstream benchmarks (presumably standard LM benchmarks) show no degradation but also no improvement, which questions the practical utility; (4) the 200-step optimization with CE loss active means the model is seeing next-token labels, making it unclear whether the margin improvements come from geometric restructuring or simply additional fine-tuning.

Strengths

  • Discovery and resolution of bfloat16 quantization artifact is a genuine and important finding
  • Rigorous dose-response experimental protocol with proper ablations
  • Fisher information distance provides a principled geometric objective with desirable damage properties
  • Honest reporting of token-class concentration shows intellectual integrity

Limitations

  • Improvements concentrate in high-frequency structural tokens; content/entity-like tokens see minimal benefit
  • Zero improvement on downstream benchmarks despite margin improvements
  • CE loss is active during MRP, confounding pure geometric effects with additional training
  • Builds on Mabrok (2026) which is itself relatively new and unverified by the broader community

Relevance

直接关联隐藏状态与可解释性以及计算可靠性：论文深入分析了 LLM 内部表示空间的 Voronoi 镶嵌几何结构，揭示了 bfloat16 量化对 token 决策边界的严重影响。Fisher 信息距离作为表示空间中的几何度量，为理解模型内部计算结构提供了新的数学工具。这些发现对 LLM 的量化精度选择和表示空间分析具有直接指导意义。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the bfloat16 margin quantization artifact affect all transformer architectures equally, or is it specific to certain unembedding matrix designs?
  • Can pure MRP (without CE loss) achieve comparable geometric restructuring?
  • How do the mid-layer geometric ambiguity findings relate to the layer-wise representation analysis in mechanistic interpretability literature?
  • Would Fisher MRP applied during pre-training rather than post-hoc produce more uniform improvements across token classes?
