How Does Machine Learning Manage Complexity?

一句话总结： 从计算复杂性理论视角分析机器学习能力，将ML模型抽象为产生P/poly可计算分布（多项式有界最大熵），证明当模型学习密码学伪随机生成器产生的分布时，所学分布在信息论意义上接近均匀分布——揭示了ML通过概率建模管理复杂性的本质。
TL;DR: Abstracts ML models as P/poly-computable distributions with polynomially-bounded max entropy, proving that when such a model learns a cryptographic pseudorandom generator's distribution, the learned distribution is information-theoretically close to uniform -- showing ML manages complexity via probability.

核心贡献

  • 将机器学习模型抽象为产生P/poly可计算分布，具有多项式有界最大熵
  • 证明了最小化KL(D||mu)的分布mu在P/poly可计算分布中，对密码学伪随机生成器产生的分布D，mu与均匀分布在信息论意义上不可区分
  • 建立了从计算不可区分性（D与U）到信息论不可区分性（mu与U）的桥梁
  • 论证ML模型通过将假设限制在可计算分布来管理复杂性，迫使模型用概率表示复杂行为

Key Contributions

  • Models ML output as P/poly-computable distributions with polynomially-bounded max entropy
  • Proves main theorem: if mu minimizes KL(D||mu) over P/poly-computable distributions where D is from a cryptographic PRG, then KL(U||mu) < 1/n^c for all c
  • Bridges computational indistinguishability (D vs U) to information-theoretic indistinguishability (mu vs U)
  • Argues ML derives strength from modeling complex behavior by probabilistic outcomes, not deterministic computation

Methodology

The paper takes a theoretical computer science approach, abstracting away from specific ML mechanisms. Section 4 shows that minimizing KL divergence corresponds to finding the simplest explanation for a distribution. Section 5 argues that neural nets are roughly equivalent to P/poly-computable distributions by examining: (1) next-token prediction as P-computable conditional probabilities, (2) polynomial time achievability through reasoning/coding, (3) non-uniformity through model weights capturing data/time/capabilities, and (4) softmax guaranteeing polynomially-bounded max entropy. Section 6 proves the main theorem about PRG distributions.

Results & Highlights

  • Main theorem: KL(U||mu) < 1/n^c for all c > 0, where mu minimizes KL(D||mu) and D is from a cryptographic PRG
  • Without max entropy condition: U and mu are still statistically close
  • The result shows that computable distributions cannot overfit the PRG structure -- they must learn something close to uniform
  • Provides a formal basis for understanding why ML models generalize: the constraint to computable distributions forces probabilistic modeling

Authors & Credibility

Lance Fortnow is a professor at Illinois Institute of Technology and a renowned computational complexity theorist. He is the author of "The Golden Ticket: P, NP, and the Search for the Impossible" and has made fundamental contributions to complexity theory. Much of this work was done while a visiting fellow at Magdalen College, University of Oxford. His expertise in computational complexity lends strong credibility to this theoretical analysis.

Critical Assessment

This is a thought-provoking theoretical paper from a leading complexity theorist applying computational complexity lenses to ML. The main theorem is elegant: it shows that the constraint of computability creates a bridge from computational to information-theoretic indistinguishability. However, the abstraction level is very high -- the P/poly-computable distribution model captures neither the training dynamics nor the architecture-specific properties of modern neural networks. The practical implications are somewhat distant: the result applies to PRG distributions specifically, not to the natural data distributions that ML models actually learn from. The argument that softmax guarantees polynomially-bounded max entropy is reasonable but ignores the effective sparsity of learned distributions in practice.

Strengths

  • Elegant theoretical result bridging computational and information-theoretic indistinguishability
  • Clear and accessible writing for a theory paper (appropriate complexity theory background provided)
  • Provides a principled complexity-theoretic lens for understanding ML generalization
  • From a leading computational complexity researcher

Limitations

  • High level of abstraction; the P/poly model does not capture training dynamics or architecture specifics
  • The PRG distribution assumption is far from natural data distributions ML models learn from
  • The polynomially-bounded max entropy condition may not hold in practice for learned distributions
  • Limited practical implications for ML practitioners
  • The connection between the theoretical result and real-world ML behavior is suggestive rather than rigorous

Relevance

该工作从计算复杂性理论角度为ML系统的数学基础提供了新的理论视角。通过将ML抽象为可计算分布的约束优化，揭示了计算约束如何迫使模型通过概率建模管理复杂性，这对理解ML系统的理论可靠性有参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the theoretical framework be extended to handle the structured, non-uniform distributions that arise in practice (e.g., natural language)?
  • What happens when the max entropy condition is violated -- do the results still hold approximately?
  • How does the P/poly abstraction relate to specific neural network architectures (transformers, MLPs)?
