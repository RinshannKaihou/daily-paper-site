MoBiE: Efficient Inference of Mixture of Binary Experts under Post-Training Quantization

Zhixiong Zhao, Zukang Xu, Zhixuan Chen, Dawei Yang

arXiv: 2604.06798v1

Date: 2026-04-08

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06798v1

MoBiE: Efficient Inference of Mixture of Binary Experts under Post-Training Quantization

一句话总结： 提出MoBiE，首个专为MoE模型设计的二值化框架，通过联合SVD分解减少跨专家冗余、全局损失梯度增强重要性估计、以及输入零空间约束缓解路由失真，在Qwen3-30B-A3B上减少52.2%困惑度并实现2倍推理加速。
TL;DR: MoBiE is the first binarization framework tailored for MoE LLMs, using joint SVD to reduce cross-expert redundancy, global loss gradients for importance estimation, and null-space error constraints to mitigate routing distortion, achieving 52.2% perplexity reduction and 2x speedup on Qwen3-30B-A3B.

核心贡献

  • 提出首个专门针对MoE架构的二值化框架，解决跨专家冗余、任务无关重要性估计和量化引起的路由偏移三个MoE特有问题
  • 使用联合SVD分解减少跨专家权重冗余（CKA相似性验证）
  • 将全局损失梯度融入局部Hessian度量，增强权重要性估计的任务对齐
  • 引入基于输入零空间的误差约束，缓解二值化导致的路由机制退化

Key Contributions

  • First binarization framework specifically designed for MoE-based LLMs, addressing cross-expert redundancy, task-agnostic importance estimation, and quantization-induced routing shifts
  • Joint SVD decomposition across expert weights reduces cross-expert redundancy (validated via CKA similarity analysis)
  • Integration of global loss gradients into local Hessian metrics for task-aware weight importance estimation
  • Null-space-guided error constraint mitigates routing distortion without additional storage overhead

Methodology

MoBiE identifies three MoE-specific challenges ignored by dense-model binarization methods: (1) cross-expert weight redundancy shown via CKA analysis, (2) task-agnostic local Hessian importance estimation that fails to capture downstream relevance, and (3) routing shifts where binarized weights cause token-expert assignments to migrate (expert-shift phenomenon). The framework addresses these via expert-specific V matrices from joint SVD, global loss gradient injection into the Hessian-based importance metric, and an error constraint projected onto the input null space to prevent routing distortion -- all without additional storage overhead.

Results & Highlights

  • On Qwen3-30B-A3B: 52.2% perplexity reduction, 43.4% average zero-shot improvement, 2x+ inference speedup
  • Evaluated on 5 MoE models: OLMoE-7B, Qwen1.5-A2.7B, Qwen3-30B-A3B, Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B, Mixtral-8x7B
  • Outperforms similar-size FP16 models on 7 zero-shot benchmarks (Figure 1)
  • CKA analysis confirms reduced cross-expert redundancy after joint SVD
  • Shortened quantization time as additional benefit

Authors & Credibility

Authors are from Houmo AI (a model compression/startup) and Nanyang Technological University. The corresponding author Dawei Yang has prior work on quantization (referenced in the paper: Zhao et al., 2025b, 2026). The work builds directly on established quantization literature (BiLLM, ARB-LLM) and provides thorough empirical evaluation across multiple MoE architectures.

Critical Assessment

This is a solid engineering contribution that identifies genuine MoE-specific binarization challenges. The expert-shift analysis (Figure 3) is particularly insightful, showing that naive binarization degrades the routing mechanism. The three-pronged approach (SVD, global gradients, null-space constraints) is well-motivated. However, the paper's claims of "outperforming FP16 models" need careful reading -- this is in the context of similar total parameter counts, not similar active parameters, which is a somewhat artificial comparison. The evaluation on 7 zero-shot benchmarks is reasonable but not exhaustive for real-world deployment validation.

Strengths

  • First to systematically address MoE-specific binarization challenges
  • The expert-shift phenomenon analysis is novel and important for understanding MoE compression
  • Three complementary techniques each target a distinct failure mode
  • No additional storage overhead -- practical for deployment
  • Comprehensive evaluation across 5 MoE architectures

Limitations

  • "Outperforming FP16" claim is based on total parameter comparison, not active parameter comparison
  • Binary quantization (1-bit) inherently limits representational capacity; quality gaps likely exist on more complex tasks
  • No evaluation on generation quality or downstream fine-tuning tasks
  • The joint SVD computation may be expensive for very large expert counts

Relevance

直接解决MoE模型推理中的量化精度损失和路由退化问题。专家偏移现象的发现对理解MoE模型在压缩后的行为变化具有重要参考价值。对实际部署MoE模型到资源受限设备的量化策略设计有直接指导意义。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does MoBiE interact with sparse expert activation patterns under different input distributions?
  • Can the joint SVD decomposition be applied incrementally during fine-tuning rather than as a post-training step?
  • What is the interaction between MoBiE and more aggressive quantization (e.g., 2-bit or 4-bit) for hybrid approaches?
