The ATOM Report: Measuring the Open Language Model Ecosystem

一句话总结： 通过综合分析Hugging Face下载量、推理市场份额等指标，系统性地揭示了中文开源模型（以Qwen为代表）在2025年夏季超越美国模型并持续扩大领先优势的趋势，并提出了相对采用率指标(RAM)来衡量模型的实际生态影响力。
TL;DR: A comprehensive ecosystem analysis of ~1.5K open language models reveals Chinese models (led by Qwen) overtook US models in adoption by mid-2025, reaching 1.15B cumulative downloads vs 723M, with a new Relative Adoption Metric (RAM) showing nuanced per-size-class dynamics.

核心贡献

  • 首次对开源语言模型生态系统进行系统性量化分析，涵盖约1500个主流开源模型
  • 揭示中文开源模型（以阿里巴巴Qwen为主）在2025年7月超越美国模型，累计下载量达到11.5亿
  • 提出相对采用率指标（RAM），消除模型规模偏差，更精准衡量模型生态影响力
  • 综合多维度数据（Hugging Face下载量、OpenRouter推理份额、模型衍生数量）构建完整生态画像

Key Contributions

  • First comprehensive quantitative snapshot of the open LLM ecosystem covering ~1,500 mainline models
  • Documents that Chinese open models overtook US counterparts by late July 2025, reaching 1.15B cumulative downloads vs 723M by March 2026
  • Introduces the Relative Adoption Metric (RAM) for size-normalized adoption comparison, revealing GPT-OSS 120B and Nemotron 3 Super achieve 10-20x their size-class median
  • Multi-source validation across Hugging Face downloads, OpenRouter inference data, model derivatives, and performance benchmarks

Methodology

The study aggregates publicly available data from Hugging Face (daily download snapshots), OpenRouter (inference market share), Artificial Analysis, and Arena/ChatBotArena benchmarks. Historical monthly download data was obtained directly from Hugging Face for leading organizations (Meta, Qwen, Mistral, DeepSeek, Google, Microsoft) covering November 2023 through July 2025, supplemented by an independent daily scraper from July 2025 onward. The RAM metric normalizes adoption by model size class to control for the download advantage of smaller models.

Results & Highlights

  • Chinese models: 1.15B cumulative downloads by March 2026 vs 723M for US models
  • Qwen alone accounts for nearly 1B cumulative downloads; the growth accelerated with Qwen3 and Qwen3.5 releases
  • DeepSeek leads in OpenRouter inference share, where usage is more distributed across organizations
  • GPT-OSS 120B achieves 10-20x its size-class median on RAM; DeepSeek V3.2 and GLM 4.7 underperform at well below 1x

Authors & Credibility

Nathan Lambert is the founder of Interconnects AI and a well-known researcher in open-source AI, previously at the Allen Institute for AI (AI2) with significant publications on RLHF and reward modeling. Florian Brand is also at Interconnects AI. The ATOM Project has received attention in the open-source AI community. The methodology relies on publicly available, verifiable data sources.

Critical Assessment

The report provides a valuable data-driven snapshot but relies heavily on Hugging Face download counts, which can be inflated by automated/CI downloads and do not necessarily reflect active deployment. The RAM metric is a useful contribution but requires further validation across different deployment contexts. The geopolitical framing (China vs US) is attention-grabbing but may oversimplify a complex ecosystem. No peer review is mentioned (this is a report, not a research paper in the traditional sense).

Strengths

  • Comprehensive multi-source data integration across downloads, inference, and benchmarks
  • Transparent methodology with publicly accessible data collection pipeline
  • RAM metric provides meaningful size-normalized comparisons
  • Timely and policy-relevant analysis of the open model ecosystem

Limitations

  • Download counts are noisy proxies for actual adoption (inflated by CI/CD pipelines)
  • Limited coverage of non-Hugging Face distribution channels (e.g., direct deployment, ModelScope)
  • RAM metric needs further stress-testing across model categories and time windows
  • The report focuses on model families but does not deeply analyze fine-tuning ecosystem dynamics

Relevance

该报告对了解开源LLM生态系统的竞争格局至关重要，尤其是中文模型（Qwen、DeepSeek）在下载量和推理份额上的快速增长，对LLM部署选型和模型评估有直接参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How do download counts correlate with actual production deployment rates?
  • What is the trajectory of fine-tuned derivatives vs base model downloads?
  • How will the US-China model competition evolve with emerging regulatory frameworks?
