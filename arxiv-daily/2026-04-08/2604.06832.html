核心贡献

  • 提出直接转换策略将自回归VLM转换为块扩散模型，比两阶段转换更高效地利用已有多模态对齐
  • 设计块大小退火、因果上下文注意力和自动截断掩码等多模态扩散适配技术

Key Contributions

  • Direct one-stage AR-to-diffusion conversion for VLMs that leverages pretrained multimodal alignment, outperforming two-stage text-first conversion under comparable budgets
  • Suite of multimodal diffusion adaptations: block-size annealing, causal context attention, auto-truncation masking, and vision-efficient concatenation
  • KV-cache-compatible parallel decoding and speculative block decoding for inference acceleration
  • 6x+ end-to-end inference speedup with SGLang integration and FP8 quantization across 11 multimodal benchmarks

Methodology

Fast-dVLM systematically compares two AR-to-diffusion conversion strategies for VLMs. The two-stage approach first adapts the LLM backbone with text-only diffusion fine-tuning before multimodal training, while the direct approach converts the full AR VLM in one stage. The direct method proves more efficient because it preserves multimodal alignment already learned by the VLM. Key adaptations include block-size annealing (gradually reducing block sizes during training), causal context attention (maintaining autoregressive causal structure within diffusion blocks), auto-truncation masking (handling variable-length outputs), and vision-efficient concatenation (minimizing vision token processing overhead).

Results & Highlights

  • 6.18x end-to-end inference speedup over AR baseline on MMMU-Pro-V
  • Throughput: 350.3 tok/s vs. 56.7 tok/s (AR baseline) with comparable accuracy
  • Matches AR generation quality across 11 multimodal benchmarks
  • Direct conversion outperforms two-stage conversion under comparable training budgets
  • FP8 quantization (SmoothQuant-W8A8) adds further speedup with minimal quality loss

Critical Assessment

The paper makes a compelling case for block-diffusion VLMs as an alternative to autoregressive decoding. The direct conversion insight -- that leveraging pretrained multimodal alignment is more efficient than text-first then multimodal -- is practically important. The 6x speedup is impressive, though it relies on a combination of techniques (diffusion + speculative decoding + SGLang + FP8) rather than diffusion alone. The evaluation on 11 benchmarks is thorough for multimodal tasks. A concern is whether the diffusion approach handles long-form generation and diverse outputs as robustly as autoregressive models, and the paper acknowledges that accuracy drops slightly (23.8 vs 26.3 on MMMU-Pro-V in one configuration).

Strengths

  • Practical and significant speedup (6x+) with maintained generation quality
  • Systematic comparison of conversion strategies with clear recommendation
  • Well-engineered system integration (SGLang, FP8, speculative decoding)
  • Addresses a real deployment bottleneck for edge VLMs in robotics/autonomous driving

Limitations

  • 6x speedup is a composite of multiple orthogonal techniques; isolating the diffusion contribution would be more informative
  • Slight accuracy drop on some benchmarks (MMMU-Pro-V: 23.8 vs 26.3)
  • Unclear how block-diffusion handles truly long sequences (>4K tokens)
  • The direct conversion recipe may not generalize to all VLM architectures

Relevance

对视觉语言模型推理加速具有重要参考价值。块扩散解码作为自回归解码的替代方案，为批处理大小为1的边缘部署场景提供了新的加速思路。FP8量化和SGLang集成的工程实践对实际VLM部署有直接指导意义。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does block-diffusion decoding interact with chain-of-thought reasoning in multimodal tasks?
  • Can the direct conversion strategy be applied to multimodal models with different architectures (e.g., cross-attention based)?
  • What is the latency-quality tradeoff frontier for different block sizes in the diffusion process?
