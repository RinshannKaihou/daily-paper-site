Continuous Interpretive Steering for Scalar Diversity

一句话总结： 提出连续解释性导向（CIS）方法，将激活导向强度作为连续实验变量，揭示了LLM中表示空间中编码了与标量多样性一致的梯度语用敏感性，可通过控制干预系统性地恢复。
TL;DR: Continuous Interpretive Steering (CIS) treats activation steering strength as a continuous variable, revealing that graded pragmatic sensitivity aligned with scalar diversity is encoded in LLM representation spaces and can be systematically recovered through controlled intervention.

核心贡献

  • 提出连续解释性导向（CIS），将激活导向强度视为连续实验变量而非固定控制机制
  • 构建GraSD（Graded Scalar Diversity）数据集，整合121个标量对用于评估梯度语用行为
  • 发现均匀激活导向增加语用解释但抹平项目间差异，而梯度导向产生与标量多样性等级一致的分化偏移
  • 证明梯度语用敏感性被编码在LLM的表示空间中，可通过控制干预系统性恢复

Key Contributions

  • Introduces CIS, a method that probes graded pragmatic interpretation by varying activation steering strength continuously
  • Constructs GraSD dataset with 121 weak-strong scalar pairs consolidated from four prior studies
  • Demonstrates that graded sensitivity to scalar diversity is encoded in LLM representation spaces
  • Shows that uniform steering collapses item-level variation while graded steering preserves it

Methodology

CIS constructs a pragmatic direction in activation space as the difference between pragmatic and logical representations of scalar items. Multi-layer hidden representations are extracted from fixed transformer layers and concatenated. The steering coefficient alpha is treated as a continuous variable, enabling systematic analysis of how interpretive outcomes shift across scalar items. Experiments are conducted on four open-weight LLMs (LLaMA3, Qwen2, Gemma2, OLMo) using the GraSD dataset, which contains anchor, logical, and pragmatic sentence variants for each scalar pair.

Results & Highlights

  • Uniform activation steering increases pragmatic interpretations globally but collapses item-level variation across scalar items
  • Graded activation steering produces differentiated interpretive shifts aligned with scalar diversity grades
  • The effect demonstrates that pragmatic inference is represented as a graded internal state in LLMs
  • Small changes in activation strength produce smooth, item-sensitive shifts in interpretation
  • Results consistent across all four tested LLMs

Authors & Credibility

Ye-eun Cho is at Sungkyunkwan University. This is a single-author paper that demonstrates strong methodological innovation by bridging linguistics (scalar implicature) and mechanistic interpretability (activation steering). The work builds on a solid foundation of prior scalar diversity research and LLM steering methods.

Critical Assessment

This is a novel and conceptually elegant contribution that bridges pragmatics and mechanistic interpretability. The key insight -- treating steering strength as an analytical probe rather than a control mechanism -- is methodologically innovative. The finding that graded sensitivity is recoverable from representation spaces is significant for understanding how LLMs encode linguistic phenomena. However, the single-author work could benefit from additional analysis of why some scalar items show stronger graded responses than others, and the practical implications beyond theoretical analysis are limited.

Strengths

  • Innovative use of activation steering as an analytical probe rather than a behavioral control mechanism
  • Well-motivated connection between scalar diversity in linguistics and representation geometry in LLMs
  • New GraSD dataset provides a standardized resource for evaluating graded pragmatic behavior
  • Clean experimental framework with consistent results across four different LLM architectures

Limitations

  • Limited practical application beyond theoretical analysis of pragmatic representation
  • Does not explore which layers or attention heads encode the graded sensitivity most strongly
  • The GraSD dataset relies on synthesized sentence variants rather than naturally occurring discourse
  • Single-author study with relatively small-scale experiments (121 scalar pairs)

Relevance

该研究直接相关于隐藏状态和可解释性研究。它通过将激活导向强度作为连续变量来探测LLM表示空间中的语用敏感性，揭示了表示空间中编码的梯度结构，为理解LLM如何表示和区分不同强度的语用推断提供了新方法。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Which specific transformer layers or attention heads encode the strongest graded pragmatic sensitivity?
  • Can the CIS framework be extended to other graded linguistic phenomena beyond scalar implicature?
  • How does the granularity of steering strength variation affect the resolution of recoverable graded patterns?
