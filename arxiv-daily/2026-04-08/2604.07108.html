Information as Structural Alignment: A Dynamical Theory of Continual Learning

一句话总结： 提出 Informational Buildup Framework (IBF)，通过"结构对齐"而非参数存储的全新视角解决持续学习中的灾难性遗忘问题，在 Split-CIFAR-100 上实现近乎零遗忘（BT = -0.004），在棋类任务中实现正向迁移（+38.5 cp），且无需存储原始数据。
TL;DR: Introduces IBF, a continual learning framework based on structural alignment rather than parameter storage, achieving near-zero forgetting on Split-CIFAR-100 (BT = -0.004), positive backward transfer in chess (+38.5 cp), and 43% less forgetting than replay in controlled domains, without storing raw data.

核心贡献

  • 提出 IBF 框架：将"信息"定义为系统配置与环境结构之间的对齐，而非存储内容，从根本上重新定义了持续学习的理论基础
  • 设计了两个核心方程：Law of Motion（驱动配置向高一致性收敛）和 Modification Dynamics（响应局部差异信号持续形变一致性景观），从中自然涌现出记忆、能动性和自我纠正能力
  • 在三个领域验证：Split-CIFAR-100（20个任务，冻结 ViT 编码器）BT = -0.004，棋类任务正向迁移 +38.5 cp，受控非平稳世界比 replay 减少 43% 遗忘
  • 发现了"机制依赖性"（regime dependence）：同一能动性机制在不同差异机制下产生有益、有害或中性三种结果，与理论预测一致

Key Contributions

  • Introduces the IBF substrate where memory nucleates as localized corrections in a coherence landscape (kernel-based, non-parametric)
  • Achieves near-zero forgetting on Split-CIFAR-100 (BT = -0.004) and positive backward transfer in chess (+38.5 cp) without storing raw data
  • Demonstrates that memory, agency, and self-correction emerge from two governing equations rather than being added as modules
  • Identifies regime dependence: the same agency mechanism produces helpful, harmful, or neutral outcomes depending on discrepancy context

Methodology

IBF replaces global parameter sharing with a coherence landscape that is locally deformed through kernel-based memory centers. When the system encounters a discrepancy signal (gap between environmental truth and current assessment), a new memory center nucleates at that location with a correction weight. Centers that receive consistent signals crystallize (decay rate drops 60x). On context switch, the system distinguishes invariant features from context-specific ones through stability transitions. The framework uses Gaussian kernels for spatial localization, with centers that can accumulate, crystallize, or decay based on environmental feedback. No backpropagation or gradient-based optimization is used for the memory substrate itself.

Results & Highlights

  • Split-CIFAR-100 (20 tasks): BT = -0.004 (near-zero forgetting), no raw data storage needed
  • Chess evaluation by Stockfish: mean behavioral advantage +88.9 +/- 2.8 cp, exceeding MLP and replay baselines
  • Controlled non-stationary world: 43% less forgetting than replay
  • Developmental cascade confirmed: agency shapes corrections learned, activates self-correction, curated memory produces systematic behavioral advantage
  • Ablations show that removing agency degrades performance in structured domains as predicted

Authors & Credibility

Radu Negulescu appears to be an independent researcher (affiliated email: radu@ibf.xyz). The paper is self-published with no institutional affiliation listed beyond the IBF project. This raises questions about peer review and institutional support. However, the paper demonstrates substantial technical depth across multiple domains (continual learning theory, chess evaluation, computer vision), with detailed toy model demonstrations and controlled experiments. The chess evaluation using Stockfish as an independent judge is a particularly rigorous design choice.

Critical Assessment

The paper is ambitious and intellectually interesting, proposing a fundamentally different substrate for continual learning. The toy model exposition is excellent and pedagogically valuable. However, several concerns arise: (1) the framework is only tested on Split-CIFAR-100 with a frozen ViT encoder (i.e., only the final layer is replaced), which is far from the full catastrophic forgetting setting where all parameters are shared; (2) the chess results, while interesting, use a non-standard evaluation metric (centipawn advantage) that may not directly translate to standard continual learning benchmarks; (3) the paper lacks comparison with strong recent continual learning baselines like progressive prompts or compartmentalization methods; (4) the theoretical claims about emergent agency and self-correction are loosely formalized. The IBF framework is more of a proof-of-concept than a ready-to-deploy solution.

Strengths

  • Excellent pedagogical exposition through the 2D toy model that makes every mechanism visible
  • Genuine novelty in framing information as structural alignment rather than stored content
  • Rigorous chess evaluation using Stockfish as an independent judge
  • Clear ablation studies confirming the developmental cascade hypothesis

Limitations

  • Limited to frozen-encoder settings for CIFAR-100; not tested on full end-to-end continual learning scenarios
  • No comparison with state-of-the-art continual learning methods (e.g., L2P, DualPrompt, InfLoRA)
  • The kernel-based memory substrate may not scale to high-dimensional problems
  • Theoretical formalism lacks the rigor expected in top theory venues

Relevance

关联 LLM 训练稳定性和持续学习：IBF 的核心思想——将学习视为一致性景观的局部形变而非全局参数更新——为理解灾难性遗忘提供了新的理论视角。对于 LLM 的持续微调和领域适应场景，该框架的非参数记忆机制可能提供避免灾难性遗忘的新思路，但其可扩展性仍需验证。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can IBF's kernel-based memory centers scale to the parameter spaces of large language models?
  • How does IBF compare with modern parameter-efficient continual learning methods like LoRA-based approaches?
  • Does the regime dependence finding generalize to NLP domains where contradictions between tasks are less clearly structured?
  • What is the computational cost of maintaining and querying the coherence landscape at scale?
