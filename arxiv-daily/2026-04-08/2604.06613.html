The Detection--Extraction Gap: Models Know the Answer Before They Can Say It

Hanyang Wang, Mingxuan Zhu

arXiv: 2604.06613v1

Date: 2026-04-08

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06613v1

The Detection--Extraction Gap: Models Know the Answer Before They Can Say It

一句话总结： 发现推理模型中存在"检测-提取差距"——52-88%的思维链token在答案已可恢复后仍被生成，模型内部状态编码了答案但强制解码无法提取，基于此提出BAEE方法截断70-78%生成同时提升1-5pp准确率。
TL;DR: Reveals that 52-88% of chain-of-thought tokens are produced after the answer is already recoverable, and free continuations succeed where forced extraction fails on 42% of cases; BAEE exploits this detection-extraction gap to truncate 70-78% of generation while improving accuracy by 1-5 pp.

核心贡献

  • 发现"检测-提取差距"：模型自由续写可恢复答案但强制提取在42%情况下失败，这是结构性现象而非模型特有伪影
  • 量化分析显示52-88%的思维链token在答案已确定后才被生成，揭示了推理模型的"过度思考"问题
  • 通过total-variation界形式化自由续写与强制提取分布之间的不匹配
  • 提出BAEE（黑盒自适应早退）方法，利用自由续写进行检测和提取，截断70-78%生成并提升1-5pp准确率

Key Contributions

  • The detection-extraction gap: behavioral recoverability (PSC) diverges structurally from forced extractability (EFA) on partial traces, formalized via a TV inequality lower bound on suffix-induced distributional shift
  • 52-88% of CoT tokens are generated post-commitment across five model configurations, two families, and three benchmarks
  • Task-topology-dependent commitment structure: non-monotone on closed-form tasks (MATH-500), monotone on sustained reasoning (GPQA-Diamond), collapsed gap on code generation (HumanEval)
  • BAEE: black-box adaptive early exit exploiting the gap for 70-78% serial reduction with 1-5 pp accuracy improvement

Methodology

The paper uses four black-box probes requiring only sampling or logprob API access: Prefix Self-Consistency (PSC) measures answer stability across free continuations from partial prefixes; Extract-then-Force-Answer (EFA) attempts forced extraction; Answer-Token Late Timing (ATLT) tracks answer token positions; and Entropy Decay (ED) monitors confidence. The detection-extraction gap is formalized through Proposition 2, which provides a TV inequality bounding the suffix-induced shift between free and forced continuation distributions. BAEE uses PSC for detection (when to exit) and free continuations for extraction (how to get the answer), avoiding the failure mode of forced extraction.

Results & Highlights

  • 52-88% of CoT tokens generated after answer is recoverable (across Qwen3-8B/32B Think/NoThink, DeepSeek-R1-Distill)
  • Detection-extraction gap: 42% of problems where PSC detects recoverability, EFA fails to extract
  • BAEE truncates 70-78% of serial generation while improving accuracy by 1-5 pp
  • Thinking-mode models gain up to 5.8 pp from early exit (preventing post-commitment overwriting)
  • Cost-optimized BAEE variant: 68-73% reduction at median 9 API calls
  • Task topology matters: MATH-500 shows non-monotone commitment, GPQA-Diamond shows monotone patterns

Authors & Credibility

Hanyang Wang is from the University of Chicago (CS); Mingxuan Zhu is from Imperial College London. This is a relatively junior team, but the work demonstrates rigorous empirical methodology with careful black-box probing. The formalization via total-variation bounds adds theoretical depth. The code is publicly available at github.com/EdWangLoDaSc/know2say.

Critical Assessment

This paper makes a genuinely novel and important observation about reasoning models. The detection-extraction gap is a fundamental insight that challenges the assumption that more CoT tokens are always better. The TV-bound formalization provides principled grounding. The BAEE method is practical and directly useful. However, the analysis is limited to two model families (Qwen3, DeepSeek-R1-Distill) and three benchmarks. The black-box approach is both a strength (broadly applicable) and a limitation (cannot access internal representations for deeper mechanistic understanding). The paper could benefit from analysis of why the gap exists mechanistically, beyond the distributional characterization.

Strengths

  • Fundamental insight: the detection-extraction gap is a structural phenomenon with important practical implications
  • Rigorous formalization via TV inequality (Proposition 2)
  • Practical BAEE method that actually improves accuracy while reducing compute
  • Task-topology analysis revealing non-uniform commitment patterns
  • Black-box methodology makes findings broadly applicable

Limitations

  • Limited to two model families (Qwen3, DeepSeek-R1-Distill) -- generalization to other architectures unclear
  • Black-box approach precludes mechanistic understanding of why the gap exists
  • The 9-API-call cost-optimized variant may be sensitive to API pricing and latency
  • Does not address how training could be modified to close the gap

Relevance

对推理模型的高效推理和理解具有重要价值。检测-提取差距的发现揭示了推理模型过度生成的本质问题，对设计更高效的推理策略（如早退机制）有直接指导意义。TV界的形式化分析为理解自由生成和强制提取之间的分布差异提供了理论基础。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • What mechanistic explanation accounts for the detection-extraction gap at the representation level?
  • Can training procedures (e.g., SFT with early-exit awareness) close the gap?
  • How does the gap scale with model size and training compute?
  • Would distillation from early-exit checkpoints produce natively efficient reasoning models?
