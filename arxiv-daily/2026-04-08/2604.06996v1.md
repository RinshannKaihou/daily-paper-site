# Self-Preference Bias in Rubric-Based Evaluation of Large Language Models

> **一句话总结：** 首次系统研究基于评分标准评价中的自偏好偏差，发现即使评分标准完全客观可编程验证，LLM仍会对自己的输出给予高达50%更多的通过判定，在HealthBench上可导致高达10分的分数偏差。

> **TL;DR:** LLM judges exhibit systematic self-preference bias in rubric-based evaluation, being up to 50% more likely to incorrectly pass their own failed rubrics, with scores skewed by up to 10 points on HealthBench.

## 核心贡献

- 首次在基于评分标准（rubric-based）的评价范式下系统研究LLM的自偏好偏差
- 提出HSPP-R（Harmful Self-Preference Propensity Ratio）指标，量化自偏好偏差相对于对不相关模型的过度估计率
- 发现即使评分标准完全客观可编程验证（IFEval），模型对自身失败输出仍有多达50%的错误通过率
- 揭示负面评分标准、极端评分标准长度和主观主题（如急诊转诊）对自偏好偏差尤为敏感

## Key Contributions

- First study of self-preference bias in rubric-based evaluation, an increasingly popular benchmarking paradigm
- Introduces formal metrics (HSPP-R self, HSPP-R family) for measuring self-preference bias at instance and rubric levels
- Demonstrates SPB persists even with entirely objective, programmatically verifiable rubrics (IFEval)
- Identifies factors driving SPB: negative rubrics, extreme rubric lengths, and subjective topics are most susceptible

## Methodology

The authors define self-preference bias through overestimation rates: the proportion of cases where a judge rules more favorably than ground truth warrants. They introduce HSPP-R, which compares a judge's overestimation of its own outputs versus those of unrelated models. Experiments span two benchmarks: IFEval (objective, programmatically verifiable rubrics) and HealthBench (subjective, human-written rubrics for medical chat evaluation). Models evaluated include Gemma 3, Llama 4, Qwen 3, GPT-5, GPT-oss-120B, Claude 4.5 Sonnet, and Claude 4.5 Haiku. Four evaluation paradigms are compared: single-rubric, all-rubrics, direct assessment, and pairwise comparison.

## Results & Highlights

- On IFEval, judges are up to 50% more likely to incorrectly pass their own failed rubrics
- On HealthBench, GPT-5 overestimates its performance by ~4 points relative to a judge ensemble
- SPB in rubric-based evaluation is similar to direct assessment but significantly less severe than pairwise comparison
- Ensembling multiple judges mitigates but does not fully eliminate SPB
- Negative rubrics, extreme rubric lengths, and emergency referral topics are particularly vulnerable to SPB

## Critical Assessment

This is a timely and rigorous study that addresses a critical gap in understanding LLM evaluation reliability. The formalization of SPB through HSPP-R is methodologically sound, and the comparison across four evaluation paradigms provides valuable context. The finding that SPB persists even with objective rubrics is particularly concerning for the field. However, the analysis of what drives SPB at a mechanistic level (why do models favor their own outputs?) remains largely observational rather than explanatory.

## Strengths

- Comprehensive evaluation across 10+ frontier models as both judges and generators
- Formal metric (HSPP-R) provides a principled way to quantify self-preference bias
- Analysis of driving factors (rubric polarity, length, topic) yields actionable insights
- Comparison across four evaluation paradigms contextualizes the severity of SPB in RB evaluation

## Limitations

- Does not investigate the mechanistic causes of self-preference bias in model internals
- Limited to two benchmarks (IFEval and HealthBench); generalizability to other domains is unknown
- Ensemble mitigation strategy requires running multiple expensive frontier models
- Does not explore whether prompt engineering or debiasing techniques can reduce SPB

## Relevance

该研究直接相关于推理评估的可靠性和可复现性。它揭示了LLM作为评判者时的系统性偏差，这对理解模型评估结果的可信度至关重要，特别是在基于评分标准的评价范式日益普及的背景下。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What are the mechanistic causes of self-preference bias in LLM internal representations?
- Can debiasing techniques (e.g., output anonymization, calibration) effectively reduce SPB?
- How does SPB scale with model size and training data composition?
