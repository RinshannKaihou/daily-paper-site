# Gemma 4, Phi-4, and Qwen3: Accuracy-Efficiency Tradeoffs in Dense and MoE Reasoning Language Models

> **一句话总结：** 在统一推理和评估框架下对比7个稠密/MoE推理模型，发现Gemma-4-E4B在few-shot CoT下达到最佳加权准确率0.675（14.9GB VRAM），稀疏激活并不保证最优实用操作点——准确率-效率权衡取决于架构、提示策略和任务组成的联合作用。

> **TL;DR:** Controlled benchmark of 7 dense/MoE reasoning models across 4 benchmarks and 3 prompting strategies shows Gemma-4-E4B achieves best weighted accuracy 0.675 at 14.9GB VRAM, while sparse activation alone does not guarantee the best practical accuracy-efficiency operating point.

## 核心贡献

- 在统一硬件、推理流水线和评估框架下，对7个稠密和MoE推理模型进行受控实证基准测试
- 引入提示条件帕累托分析框架，联合报告准确率、延迟、内存和计算成本
- 发现Phi-4-reasoning在few-shot CoT下GSM8K准确率从0.67骤降至0.11（对提示策略极端敏感）
- 发布可复现评估流水线、汇总结果和配对统计分析

## Key Contributions

- Controlled benchmark of 7 models: Gemma-4-E2B, Gemma-4-E4B, Gemma-4-26B-A4B, Phi-4-mini-reasoning, Phi-4-reasoning, Qwen3-8B, Qwen3-30B-A3B
- 8,400 total model-dataset-prompt evaluations across ARC-Challenge, GSM8K, Math Level 1-3, TruthfulQA MC1
- Gemma-4-E4B with few-shot CoT: best weighted accuracy 0.675 at 14.9GB mean VRAM
- GSM8K shows largest prompt sensitivity; Phi-4-reasoning drops from 0.67 (CoT) to 0.11 (few-shot CoT)
- MoE models do not uniformly dominate: tradeoffs depend on architecture, prompting, and task composition

## Methodology

All 7 models are evaluated on the same server with a unified inference pipeline, common decoding settings (temperature=0, greedy), identical task sample sizes, and the same reporting structure. Four metrics are recorded: accuracy, latency, peak GPU memory (VRAM), and approximate FLOPs-per-token proxy. Three prompting strategies are tested: zero-shot, chain-of-thought (CoT), and few-shot chain-of-thought. Statistical significance is assessed via paired tests. A weighted multi-task summary aggregates across benchmarks.

## Results & Highlights

- Gemma-4-E4B: weighted accuracy 0.675, mean VRAM 14.9GB (best overall)
- Gemma-4-26B-A4B: weighted accuracy 0.663, mean VRAM 48.1GB (close in accuracy, 3.2x more memory)
- Gemma models dominate ARC-Challenge and Math; Phi models strongest on TruthfulQA
- GSM8K shows extreme prompt sensitivity: Phi-4-reasoning 0.67 (CoT) to 0.11 (few-shot CoT)
- Sparse activation alone does not guarantee best practical operating point
- Code and results available at github.com/mkboch/dense_and_moe_reasoning

## Critical Assessment

This is a solid empirical benchmarking paper that addresses the practical question of deployment-oriented model comparison. The unified evaluation protocol is a genuine contribution -- many existing comparisons use heterogeneous setups that make cross-model interpretation difficult. The finding that sparse MoE does not uniformly dominate is important and counterintuitive. However, the paper is limited to inference-time evaluation and does not address training efficiency. The benchmark suite (4 tasks) is relatively narrow. The key finding about Phi-4-reasoning's prompt sensitivity is striking but may be specific to the few-shot examples used. The paper would benefit from analysis of why MoE models underperform expectations in certain settings.

## Strengths

- Unified evaluation protocol enabling fair cross-model comparison
- Prompt-conditioned Pareto analysis revealing hidden tradeoffs
- Comprehensive metrics beyond accuracy (latency, VRAM, FLOPs)
- Reproducible pipeline with publicly available code and results
- Practical and actionable findings for model deployment decisions

## Limitations

- Only 4 benchmark tasks; limited coverage of model capabilities
- Does not address training efficiency, only inference
- No analysis of why MoE models underperform expectations in certain settings
- The striking Phi-4-reasoning result may depend on specific few-shot example choices
- Evaluation limited to greedy decoding (temperature=0)

## Relevance

该工作提供了当前主流推理模型（Gemma 4、Phi-4、Qwen3）在统一框架下的系统性基准测试，揭示了稀疏MoE与稠密模型在实际推理场景中的准确率-效率权衡。对于LLM推理评估和部署决策具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What explains Phi-4-reasoning's extreme prompt sensitivity on GSM8K (from 0.67 to 0.11)?
- How do the results change with non-greedy decoding (e.g., temperature > 0, nucleus sampling)?
- Would a broader benchmark suite (coding, multilingual, long-context) change the Pareto frontier?
