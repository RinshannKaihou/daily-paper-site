# FP4 Explore, BF16 Train: Diffusion Reinforcement Learning via Efficient Rollout Scaling

> **一句话总结：** 提出 Sol-RL 两阶段框架，第一阶段用 NVFP4 量化推理高速生成候选池并筛选高对比度子集，第二阶段用 BF16 高精度重新生成选定样本并仅在这些高保真样本上优化策略，在 SANA、FLUX.1、SD3.5-L 上实现最高 4.64 倍训练加速，同时保持 BF16 级别的训练完整性。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes Sol-RL, a two-stage RL framework that uses NVFP4 quantization for high-throughput candidate exploration and BF16 regeneration for policy optimization, achieving up to 4.64x training speedup across SANA, FLUX.1, and SD3.5-L while maintaining BF16-level training integrity.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 Sol-RL 两阶段框架：解耦候选探索与策略优化，FP4 用于高速探索，BF16 用于高保真训练
- 发现 NVFP4 量化推理虽然不适合直接作为训练目标，但其组内相对排名与 BF16 高度一致（可用作可靠的排名代理）
- 实证表明直接使用量化 rollout 进行训练会导致严重的性能退化和训练不稳定
- 在三个大规模扩散模型上验证，实现最高 4.64 倍加速，且对齐性能超越 BF16 基线

## Key Contributions

- Sol-RL two-stage framework: Stage 1 uses NVFP4 for massive candidate pool generation and contrastive subset extraction; Stage 2 regenerates selected samples in BF16 for policy optimization
- Identifies that NVFP4 rollouts preserve intra-group relative ranking (dense diagonal in rank correlation) despite not matching BF16 at pixel level
- Demonstrates that direct quantized rollout training causes severe alignment degradation and instability
- Achieves up to 4.64x speedup on FLUX.1-12B with superior alignment metrics (ImageReward, HPSv2, MPS)

## Methodology

Sol-RL decouples exploration from optimization in Diffusion GRPO. Stage 1 deploys NVFP4 quantization (1 sign, 2 exponent, 1 mantissa bit with block-level micro-scaling) to generate a massive candidate pool (e.g., 96 samples) in reduced denoising steps (6 steps vs 10). The key insight is that given the same noise seed, NVFP4 samples maintain ranking consistency with BF16 counterparts within each group, allowing reliable filtering of the most contrastive samples. Stage 2 regenerates only the selected high-variance subset (e.g., top-k and bottom-k seeds, totaling 24 samples) in full BF16 precision with 10 denoising steps. The policy model is optimized strictly on these high-fidelity samples, avoiding quantization-induced corruption. After each policy update, weights are re-quantized to NVFP4 for the next exploration iteration.

## Results & Highlights

- FLUX.1-12B: 4.64x speedup, superior ImageReward scores vs BF16 baseline
- SANA: 2.42x speedup with comparable or better alignment quality
- SD3.5-L: Consistent improvements across HPSv2 and MPS metrics
- NVFP4 proxy ranking reliability: heavy concentration along diagonal in rank percentile comparison
- Direct FP4 training: severe instability and performance degradation confirmed
- Only 2% computational overhead from the BF16 regeneration stage

## Critical Assessment

This is a well-executed systems-for-ML paper that addresses a real bottleneck in diffusion RL training. The key insight -- that FP4 preserves relative rankings despite absolute quality degradation -- is simple but powerful and well-supported empirically. The two-stage design is elegant and practical. However, several concerns arise: (1) the approach is tightly coupled to the GRPO objective and the specific contrastive subset selection paradigm; it is unclear whether it generalizes to other RL algorithms or supervised training; (2) the NVFP4 ranking consistency property is empirically demonstrated but not theoretically explained -- why should a 4-bit representation preserve ordinal structure? (3) the paper focuses heavily on alignment metrics (ImageReward, HPSv2) which are themselves neural network-based and may have biases; (4) the 8x B200 GPU setting limits reproducibility for most researchers.

## Strengths

- Elegant and practical solution to a real computational bottleneck in diffusion RL
- Strong empirical evidence across three distinct model families and multiple metrics
- Clear characterization of when quantized rollouts are useful (ranking proxy) vs harmful (direct training)
- Excellent speedup results (up to 4.64x) with maintained or improved alignment quality

## Limitations

- Tightly coupled to GRPO-style contrastive training; generalizability to other RL objectives unclear
- NVFP4 ranking consistency is empirically observed but lacks theoretical explanation
- Requires NVIDIA Blackwell hardware for FP4 support, limiting accessibility
- Alignment metrics (ImageReward, HPSv2) are themselves imperfect and potentially biased

## Relevance

直接关联计算可靠性和精度误差问题：论文系统地研究了 FP4 量化对扩散模型 RL 训练的影响，发现量化推理在绝对精度上不可靠但在相对排名上保持一致性。这一发现对理解低精度计算在训练和推理中的可靠性具有重要意义，也为 LLM 训练中的量化策略提供了参考。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the ranking consistency finding generalize to LLM RLHF training with FP4 quantization?
- What is the theoretical explanation for why 4-bit representations preserve ordinal structure in diffusion model outputs?
- Can Sol-RL be extended to mixed-precision strategies that use different precisions at different denoising steps?
- How does the framework interact with more aggressive quantization (INT4, binary) or different model architectures?
