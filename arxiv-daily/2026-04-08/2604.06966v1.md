# MAR-GRPO: Stabilized GRPO for AR-diffusion Hybrid Image Generation

> **一句话总结：** 针对掩码自回归(MAR)图像生成模型中的扩散头引入的梯度噪声问题，提出多轨迹期望(MTE)和一致性感知token选择策略，在稳定训练动态的同时显著提升视觉质量和空间结构理解。

> **TL;DR:** Identifies the diffusion head as a key source of optimization instability in hybrid AR-diffusion image generation, proposing multi-trajectory expectation (MTE) and consistency-aware token selection to reduce gradient noise, consistently improving visual quality and training stability over baseline GRPO.

## 核心贡献

- 首次将GRPO方法应用于掩码自回归(MAR)图像生成模型，揭示扩散头是训练不稳定的关键来源
- 提出多轨迹期望(MTE)，通过平均多个扩散轨迹来估计优化方向，降低扩散引起的梯度方差
- 设计token级不确定性估计机制，仅对top-k%最不确定的token应用多轨迹优化，兼顾稳定性与效率
- 引入一致性感知token选择策略，过滤与最终生成内容不一致的AR token

## Key Contributions

- First GRPO-based framework for masked autoregressive (MAR) models; identifies diffusion head as key instability source
- Multi-trajectory expectation (MTE) reduces diffusion-induced gradient noise via expectation over multiple trajectories
- Uncertainty-based token selection applies MTE only to top-k% most uncertain tokens for efficiency
- Consistency-aware token selection filters AR tokens misaligned with final generated content

## Methodology

The method first analyzes why end-to-end GRPO fails on MAR models: the lightweight diffusion head introduces large gradient variance due to the stochastic denoising process across multiple timesteps. Rather than end-to-end optimization, the approach computes multiple diffusion trajectories for fixed AR latents during rollout and estimates optimization direction via expectation. Token-wise uncertainty is estimated from multiple diffusion samples, and MTE is applied only to the top-k% most uncertain tokens. A consistency-aware selection strategy further filters tokens whose optimization directions conflict with final generation outcomes.

## Results & Highlights

- Consistently improves visual quality, training stability, and spatial structure understanding over baseline GRPO
- Prevents the performance collapse observed in standard end-to-end GRPO on MAR models
- Achieves better trade-off between stability and computational efficiency due to uncertainty-based selective optimization
- Code available at https://github.com/AMAP-ML/mar-grpo

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a meaningful contribution by identifying the diffusion head as the instability bottleneck in hybrid AR-diffusion RL training, which is a non-obvious finding. The multi-trajectory expectation approach is well-motivated and the selective application to uncertain tokens is a sensible efficiency measure. However, the computational overhead of generating multiple diffusion trajectories during rollout is not thoroughly analyzed. The evaluation is primarily visual quality and reward scores; more rigorous quantitative metrics (FID, CLIP score) would strengthen the claims. The method is specific to the MAR architecture and generalization to other hybrid paradigms is not demonstrated.

## Strengths

- Clear identification of a specific technical bottleneck (diffusion head gradient noise)
- Principled multi-trajectory expectation approach with theoretical grounding
- Practical efficiency through uncertainty-based selective optimization
- Open-sourced code for reproducibility

## Limitations

- Computational overhead of multiple diffusion trajectories during rollout not fully quantified
- Evaluation relies heavily on reward scores; standard image quality metrics (FID, IS) less prominent
- Specific to MAR architecture; generalization to other AR-diffusion hybrids unclear
- Ablation depth could be improved to isolate contribution of each component

## Relevance

该工作直接解决了混合AR-扩散模型中RL训练不稳定的核心问题，通过分析扩散头引起的梯度噪声并提出降噪策略，对理解生成模型训练动态的可靠性具有重要价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What is the computational overhead of MTE during rollout relative to the quality gains?
- Can the multi-trajectory expectation approach transfer to other hybrid generation paradigms?
- How does the optimal k% for uncertain token selection vary across training stages?
