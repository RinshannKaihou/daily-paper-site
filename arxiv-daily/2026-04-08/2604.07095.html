Multilingual Embedding Probes Fail to Generalize Across Learner Corpora

Laurits Lyngbaek, Ross Deans Kristensen-McLachlan

arXiv: 2604.07095v1

Date: 2026-04-08

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07095v1

Multilingual Embedding Probes Fail to Generalize Across Learner Corpora

一句话总结： 通过在Qwen3-Embedding的隐藏状态上训练线性/非线性探针预测CEFR语言能力等级，发现探针在分布内表现良好（QWK约0.7），但在跨语料库评估中性能崩溃，表明当前多语言嵌入模型未编码可迁移的语言能力维度。
TL;DR: Probes trained on hidden-state activations from Qwen3-Embedding achieve strong in-distribution performance (QWK ~0.7) for CEFR proficiency prediction but collapse in cross-corpus evaluation, indicating multilingual embeddings do not encode transferable proficiency representations.

核心贡献

  • 系统评估多语言嵌入模型是否编码语言能力水平的可迁移表示
  • 在9个语料库、7种语言上训练5种探针架构，使用3个规模的Qwen3-Embedding模型
  • 发现分布内评估中探针显著优于表面特征基线（QWK约0.7），中间层预测效果最佳
  • 跨语料库评估中性能全面崩溃，探针学习到的是语料库特定的分布属性而非抽象的语言能力维度

Key Contributions

  • First large-scale investigation of whether multilingual embedding models encode language-general proficiency
  • Evaluates five probing architectures (linear regression, ordinal regression, MLP regression, logistic regression, MLP classifier) across three Qwen3-Embedding sizes
  • Demonstrates that probes capture corpus-specific distributional properties rather than transferable proficiency dimensions
  • Residual analysis shows OOD predictions degenerate toward uniformly distributed labels

Methodology

The authors extract hidden-state activations from all layers of Qwen3-Embedding (0.6B, 4B, 8B) using learner texts from the UniversalCEFR dataset across nine corpora and seven languages. Five probe architectures with varying assumptions about representation geometry are trained to predict CEFR levels (A1-C2). Evaluation uses two conditions: in-distribution (80/20 split from same data mixture) and leave-one-out cross-corpus (withholding an entire corpus). A XGBoost baseline trained on surface features (document length, average sentence/word length) provides a comparison point.

Results & Highlights

  • In-distribution: probes achieve QWK up to ~0.73 in middle layers of the 4B model, substantially outperforming surface baseline
  • Cross-corpus: performance collapses uniformly across all probe architectures, model sizes, and layer depths
  • Middle layers consistently yield the best predictions for in-distribution evaluation
  • Residual analysis reveals OOD probes converge toward predicting uniformly distributed labels
  • Probe performance does not improve with larger model sizes (8B does not outperform 4B)

Authors & Credibility

The authors are from the Department of Linguistics, Cognitive Science, and Semiotics at Aarhus University, Denmark. Their expertise in computational linguistics and second language acquisition provides a strong foundation for this work. The paper is described as under review, with code and data publicly available on GitHub.

Critical Assessment

This is a well-designed negative result study that provides an important counterpoint to optimistic claims about what linguistic properties are encoded in multilingual representations. The leave-one-out cross-corpus evaluation is methodologically rigorous, and the residual analysis strengthens the conclusion. The key limitation is that the study only examines embedding models (not generative models), and the probe architectures may not capture all possible ways proficiency could be encoded. The negative result could reflect limitations of the probing methodology rather than the absence of proficiency information.

Strengths

  • Rigorous experimental design with two evaluation conditions (in-distribution vs. cross-corpus)
  • Comprehensive evaluation across nine corpora, seven languages, three model sizes, and five probe architectures
  • Negative result is carefully analyzed through residual analysis showing prediction degeneration
  • Clear implications for representation-based approaches to proficiency-adaptive language technology

Limitations

  • Only examines embedding models (Qwen3-Embedding), not generative decoder models
  • Probes may lack the capacity to extract proficiency information even if it is present in representations
  • CEFR labels themselves may not be reliable ground truth for a "language-general proficiency dimension"
  • Does not explore whether alternative probing strategies (e.g., contrastive probes, task-specific adapters) could improve cross-corpus transfer

Relevance

该研究直接相关于探针和表示分析。它揭示了在隐藏状态上训练的探针捕获的是语料库特定的分布属性（主题、语言、任务类型、评分方法）而非可迁移的语言能力维度，这对理解嵌入模型中编码了什么语言学属性提供了重要洞察。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do generative decoder models encode transferable proficiency information that embedding models do not?
  • Can contrastive or task-adaptive probing strategies recover cross-corpus transferable representations?
  • What are the specific corpus-specific confounds (topic, language, rating methodology) that probes learn?
