Non-identifiability of Explanations from Model Behavior in Deep Networks of Image Authenticity Judgments

Icaro Re Depaolini, Uri Hasson

arXiv: 2604.07254v1

Date: 2026-04-08

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07254v1

Non-identifiability of Explanations from Model Behavior in Deep Networks of Image Authenticity Judgments

一句话总结： 证明即使深度网络能很好地预测人类对图像真实性的判断，不同架构产生的归因解释却高度不一致，说明事后解释不能作为认知机制的可靠证据。
TL;DR: Deep networks predicting human authenticity judgments produce divergent attribution explanations across architectures, undermining the use of post-hoc explanations as evidence for cognitive mechanisms.

核心贡献

  • 系统评估了多种预训练视觉架构预测人类图像真实性评分的能力，发现性能接近但解释不一致
  • 揭示VGG模型主要通过追踪图像质量而非真实性特定方差来获得高性能，限制了其归因的相关性
  • 在架构内（不同随机种子）归因图较为稳定，但在架构间一致性很弱，即使预测性能相似
  • 提出集成模型方法，聚合多架构信息以同时提升预测准确率和可解释性

Key Contributions

  • Demonstrates that models with similar predictive performance on human authenticity ratings produce divergent Grad-CAM/LIME/pixel-masking explanations across architectures
  • Shows VGG models track image quality rather than authenticity-specific variance, limiting attribution relevance
  • Finds within-architecture explanation consistency but weak cross-architecture agreement (the Rashomon effect)
  • Proposes ensemble models that improve behavioral prediction and enable image-level attribution via pixel masking

Methodology

The authors fit lightweight regression heads to multiple frozen pretrained vision architectures (VGG-16/19, ResNet-18/34, EfficientNetB3, Barlow Twins, DINOv2) to predict human authenticity ratings from the AIGCIQA2023 dataset. They evaluate explanation consistency in two ways: within-architecture (across 10 random seeds per architecture) and across-architecture (comparing prototype attribution maps). Attribution maps are generated using Grad-CAM, LIME, and multiscale pixel masking. A partial-correlation decomposition separates quality-related from authenticity-specific variance.

Results & Highlights

  • Multiple architectures reach ~80% of the noise ceiling in predicting human authenticity ratings
  • VGG models rely primarily on image quality rather than authenticity-specific information
  • EfficientNetB3 and Barlow Twins show highest within-architecture consistency
  • Cross-architecture agreement is weak even when predictive performance is similar
  • Ensemble models improve prediction and provide more robust image-level attribution

Authors & Credibility

Icaro Re Depaolini and Uri Hasson are from the Center for Mind/Brain Sciences at the University of Trento, a well-regarded cognitive neuroscience group. The work bridges computer vision explainability and cognitive science with rigorous experimental methodology.

Critical Assessment

This paper makes a valuable and sobering contribution to the XAI literature by empirically demonstrating the non-identifiability problem: models that predict human behavior equally well can rely on fundamentally different features. The experimental design is rigorous, with within- and across-architecture consistency analyses providing complementary perspectives. However, the study is limited to the image authenticity domain and a relatively small number of architectures. The philosophical implication -- that post-hoc explanations are weak evidence for cognitive mechanism -- is important but not entirely novel, as the Rashomon effect has been noted before.

Strengths

  • Rigorous two-pronged evaluation: predictive performance plus explanation consistency
  • Uses multiple explanation methods (Grad-CAM, LIME, pixel masking) rather than relying on a single approach
  • Separates quality-driven from authenticity-driven predictions via partial correlations
  • Ensemble approach offers a practical path forward despite the non-identifiability problem

Limitations

  • Limited to image authenticity domain; generalizability to other perception tasks is unclear
  • Relatively small number of architectures tested
  • The dataset (AIGCIQA2023) contains only AI-generated images, limiting scope
  • Does not propose a definitive solution to the non-identifiability problem

Relevance

本文直接触及可解释性研究的核心问题：模型预测性能好不等于解释可靠。对于研究隐藏状态和模型内部表征的工作而言，这是一个重要的警示——不同架构可能学到完全不同的表征但达到相同的预测效果。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Would the non-identifiability effect be stronger or weaker for more complex perceptual judgments beyond authenticity?
  • Can probes trained on hidden states reveal whether representations diverge in ways consistent with the attribution differences?
  • Would larger-scale datasets or more diverse architectures exacerbate or mitigate the Rashomon effect?
