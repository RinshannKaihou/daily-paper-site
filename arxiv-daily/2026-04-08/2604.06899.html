Data Leakage in Automotive Perception: Practitioners' Insights

一句话总结： 通过对沃尔沃汽车等车企的 10 名从业者的半结构化访谈，揭示了自动驾驶感知系统中数据泄漏问题的认知是按角色碎片化的——ML 工程师将其视为数据分割问题，设计和验证工程师将其理解为代表性不足和场景覆盖问题——检测主要依赖通用考虑和性能异常而非专门工具。
TL;DR: Through 10 semi-structured interviews with automotive perception practitioners at an OEM (Volvo Cars), reveals that data leakage knowledge is fragmented along role boundaries -- ML engineers see it as a data-splitting issue, design/verification engineers as a representativeness and scenario coverage problem -- with detection relying on generic considerations rather than specific tools.

核心贡献

  • 揭示了数据泄漏知识在角色间的碎片化：ML 工程师将其概念化为数据分割或验证问题，设计和验证角色则从代表性和场景覆盖角度理解
  • 发现检测主要依赖于通用考虑和观察到的性能异常，而非专用工具
  • 识别出数据泄漏预防比检测更普遍实践，主要依赖经验和知识分享
  • 提出数据泄漏控制是一个跨角色和工作流分布的社会技术协调问题

Key Contributions

  • Identifies role-dependent fragmentation of data leakage knowledge in automotive ML development
  • Reveals that detection relies on generic considerations and performance anomalies rather than dedicated tools
  • Shows that prevention is more commonly practiced than detection, depending on experience and knowledge sharing
  • Frames leakage control as a socio-technical coordination problem distributed across roles and workflows
  • Provides actionable implications: shared definitions, traceable data practices, and cross-role communication

Methodology

The study follows an exploratory case study methodology at an automotive OEM (Volvo Cars). Ten semi-structured interviews (30-60 minutes each) were conducted with practitioners from two development teams (internal and external perception functions). Participants span system design, ML development, and verification roles (1-12 years experience). Reflexive thematic analysis was used to analyze interview data. The study addresses four research questions covering knowledge, experience, practices, and recommendations around data leakage in automotive ML pipelines.

Results & Highlights

  • ML engineers conceptualize leakage as data-splitting or validation issues; design/verification roles interpret it as representativeness and scenario coverage
  • Detection commonly arises through generic considerations and performance anomalies, not specific tools
  • Prevention is more commonly practiced than detection, relying on experience and knowledge sharing
  • Cross-role communication gaps exist: different roles have different mental models of what constitutes leakage

Critical Assessment

This is a well-conducted empirical software engineering study that addresses an important but under-studied practical problem. The qualitative methodology is appropriate for the research questions, and the reflexive thematic analysis follows established guidelines. The key finding -- that data leakage knowledge is fragmented along role boundaries -- is both intuitive and actionable. However, several limitations should be noted: (1) the sample size of 10 interviews from a single OEM limits generalizability; (2) the study captures perceptions and self-reported practices rather than measuring actual leakage rates or their impact; (3) the paper does not propose or evaluate any technical solutions; (4) the connection between perceived leakage risks and actual model reliability degradation is not empirically established. The contribution is primarily descriptive rather than prescriptive.

Strengths

  • Addresses a critical but under-studied practical problem in safety-critical ML systems
  • Rigorous qualitative methodology with clear thematic analysis
  • Genuine industrial access (Volvo Cars) providing authentic practitioner perspectives
  • Actionable implications for organizational practices (shared definitions, cross-role communication)

Limitations

  • Small sample size (10 interviews) from a single OEM limits generalizability
  • Self-reported perceptions rather than measured leakage rates or impact
  • No technical solutions proposed or evaluated
  • The study is primarily descriptive; does not establish causal links between leakage awareness and model reliability

Relevance

直接关联 ML 可靠性和数据质量：数据泄漏是影响 ML 模型评估可靠性的核心问题之一。该研究揭示了在安全关键系统中，数据泄漏的管理不仅是技术问题，更是组织协调问题。对于 LLM 训练中的数据污染和评估泄漏等议题，该研究提供了来自工业实践的视角，强调了跨角色沟通和标准化数据实践的重要性。
