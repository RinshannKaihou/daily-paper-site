# Beyond Loss Values: Robust Dynamic Pruning via Loss Trajectory Alignment

> **一句话总结：** 提出AlignPrune插件模块，通过基于损失轨迹对齐的动态对齐分数（DAS）替代单点损失值作为剪枝标准，在噪声标签场景下准确率最高提升6.3%，无需修改模型架构或训练流程。

> **TL;DR:** AlignPrune replaces per-sample loss-based ranking with Dynamic Alignment Score (DAS) that measures loss trajectory correlation with a reference set, achieving up to 6.3% accuracy improvement over SOTA dynamic pruning baselines under noisy-label settings as a plug-and-play module.

## 核心贡献

- 首次研究噪声标签下的动态数据剪枝问题，揭示现有方法因依赖单点损失值而误保噪声样本
- 提出动态对齐分数（DAS），基于损失轨迹与参考集的相关性而非绝对损失值来识别噪声样本
- 作为即插即用模块，可无缝集成到现有动态剪枝框架中，无需修改模型架构或训练流程
- 在5个基准数据集和多种噪声类型下验证，最高提升6.3%准确率

## Key Contributions

- First systematic study of dynamic data pruning under noisy-label settings
- Dynamic Alignment Score (DAS): replaces per-sample loss ranking with loss-trajectory correlation to reference set, enabling more accurate noisy sample identification
- Plug-and-play module that integrates into existing dynamic pruning frameworks (InfoBatch, SeTa) without modifying model architecture or training pipeline
- Up to 6.3% accuracy boost over state-of-the-art baselines across five benchmarks and various noise types

## Methodology

AlignPrune observes that clean samples and noisy samples exhibit divergent loss trajectories during training: clean samples' loss trajectories correlate with the average reference loss trajectory, while noisy samples' trajectories diverge. Instead of using instantaneous loss values (which fail because noisy samples often have high losses and are mistakenly preserved), DAS computes the Pearson correlation between each sample's loss history and the average loss trajectory of a reference set. Samples with low alignment scores are identified as likely noisy and pruned. The module operates within existing dynamic pruning frameworks by replacing their loss-based ranking criterion.

## Results & Highlights

- Up to 6.3% accuracy improvement over SOTA dynamic pruning baselines under noisy labels
- Evaluated on 5 widely-used benchmarks across various noise types (symmetric, asymmetric, instance-dependent) and pruning ratios
- Plug-and-play integration with InfoBatch and SeTa frameworks
- Dynamic pruning inherently more robust to noise than static pruning (empirically demonstrated)
- Code available at github.com/leonqin430/AlignPrune

## Critical Assessment

The paper addresses a practical and important problem -- noise-robust data pruning. The loss trajectory alignment insight is intuitive and well-motivated: clean samples should follow the "average" learning trajectory while noisy samples diverge. The plug-and-play design is practical. However, the 6.3% improvement is achieved under specific noise settings that may not fully represent real-world noise distributions. The paper focuses on image classification benchmarks and does not explore whether the approach generalizes to NLP or multimodal settings. The computational overhead of tracking loss trajectories over multiple epochs is not thoroughly analyzed.

## Strengths

- First to systematically study dynamic pruning under noisy labels
- Simple, intuitive insight: loss trajectory alignment vs. instantaneous loss
- Plug-and-play design with no model architecture or training pipeline modifications
- Consistent improvements across multiple noise types and benchmarks

## Limitations

- Limited to image classification benchmarks; generalization to NLP/multimodal settings unclear
- Computational overhead of loss trajectory tracking not thoroughly analyzed
- The reference set construction may be sensitive to its own noise contamination
- Does not address the intersection of noisy labels with class imbalance

## Relevance

对理解训练数据质量对模型性能的影响有参考价值。损失轨迹对齐方法为识别噪声样本提供了新视角，对需要在大规模噪声数据上进行训练的场景具有实用价值。对训练数据筛选和质量控制的工程实践有启发意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can DAS be combined with label correction techniques for a unified noisy-label learning framework?
- How does the trajectory alignment approach scale to pretraining datasets (billions of samples)?
- What is the sensitivity of DAS to the reference set size and composition?
