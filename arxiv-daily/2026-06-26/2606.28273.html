Why This Matters

First component-level causal account of perception–knowledge conflict — yielding concrete intervention targets for when multimodal models should trust sight versus stored knowledge.

Critical Edge

Scope is limited to color-property conflicts on 3B–10B models. Shape, size, spatial conflicts and frontier-scale generalization remain untested; interventions target only the last token position.

01 / MECHANISM

The routing → writing circuit

A sparse late-layer two-stage circuit actively injects prior knowledge into the residual stream.
Sparse 2.5–4.8% of heads · decomposes into routing (early) and writing (late) roles · confirmed across 3 VLM families
02 / METHOD

How the circuit was found

Three complementary causal interventions via NNsight, on the last token position.

The study probes VLMs with the Visual-Counterfact dataset — 467 everyday objects recolored to conflict with world knowledge (e.g. a blue strawberry). Each counterfactual image is shown under two grounding prompts on the same image: Visual ("What color is this {object} here?") versus Prior ("What color is a {object} usually?").

Quantitative analysis is restricted to correctly-conflicting examples (73–212 per model) — where the model genuinely resolves a tension — preventing noise contamination. Interventions run bidirectionally (Prior→Visual and Visual→Prior), scored by a normalized restoration score and a discrete flip rate.

Validated across three architecturally distinct families — Qwen-VL 3B/7B, LLaVA-NeXT 7B, PaliGemma 3B/10B — with a visual-circuit contrast (vary image, hold prompt) as a robustness check.

Activation Patching

Residual stream, per-head, and per-MLP granularities, in both directions, scored by restoration + flip rate.

Component Ablation

Zero-ablation tests causal necessity — individual and grouped (promoting / suppressing) heads.

Mechanistic Characterization

Attention-pattern deltas + a logit lens on head-output differences to find answer-token writers.

04 / VERDICT

Strengths & limitations

◆ Strengths

  • Clean separation of sufficiency (patching) and necessity (ablation), with consistent cross-architecture findings.
  • The routing-vs-writing decomposition is intuitive and well-supported by logit-lens evidence (0% → >80% hit rate).
  • Honest about architecture-specific divergence in routing implementation.
  • Practical implications clearly stated for multimodal reliability and controllable reasoning.

◇ Limitations

  • Restricted to color conflicts on one dataset; shape, size, spatial relations untested.
  • Only 3B–10B models; larger models may develop different strategies.
  • Last-token-only interventions may miss earlier image-token-processing components.
  • Modest correctly-conflicting sample sizes (as low as 73 per model).
  • The "shared circuit" abstracts over real architectural divergence.
05 / OPEN

Questions for further work

Q1 · GENERALIZATION

Does the routing→writing circuit extend to non-color conflicts — shape, count, spatial relations — and beyond single-token answers?

Q2 · SCALING

How does the circuit scale to 30B+ or frontier VLMs — does the 2.5–4.8% sparsity hold, grow, or restructure?

Q3 · REUSABILITY

Are the prior-override heads task-specific, or reusable across different knowledge-retrieval demands?

Q4 · CONTROL

Could targeted editing or strengthening of writing heads reliably enforce knowledge-grounded answers under conflicting visual input in deployment?

Q5 · CONVERGENCE

Do the two routing regimes (attention-redistribution vs. representation-modulation) converge under training distribution or fine-tuning?

Q6 · EARLY TOKENS

How do these circuits interact with earlier image-token-processing positions that last-token analysis does not capture?
