> **一句话总结：** 本文证明在凸一阶优化中，只有"正仿射"的梯度聚合规则才能普适地保持单调性，任何非仿射聚合（如自适应、裁剪、鲁棒/隐私聚合）都会破坏单调性，进而导致末次迭代不收敛和算法稳定性退化，从而为现代学习系统中分散的失败模式提供了统一的理论解释。

> **TL;DR:** The authors prove that, in first-order convex learning, monotonicity (and co-coercivity) of the aggregated update operator is preserved if and only if the aggregation rule is positively affine — so non-affine aggregations used for adaptivity, privacy, robustness, or fairness inherently break last-iterate convergence and degrade algorithmic stability.

## 核心贡献
- **不可能性定理（Theorem 1）**：在凸学习框架 θ_{t+1}=θ_t−γF(g₁,…,gₙ) 下，聚合算子 F 普适地保持单调性（对任意凸函数诱导的梯度），当且仅当 F 是正仿射的，即 F=Σαₖgₖ+C（αₖ≥0）。这给出了对开放问题的否定回答。
- **末次迭代收敛的失败（Theorem 4）**：证明当 F 在某平衡点处非正仿射时，存在一个从平衡点出发的锥 K，使得对任意步长 γ>0，更新都严格增大与平衡点的距离，从根本上破坏下降论证。
- **算法稳定性退化（Theorem 3、6、8、9）**：扩展到余强制性（co-coercivity），证明非仿射聚合必然使更新算子变为"扩张"的，从而引入额外的稳定性惩罚 I(F)>0，使泛化界严格弱于正仿射情形。
- **正向结果（Section 4）**：通过构造性证明给出一条"配方"：若函数类排除任意秩-1 半正定 Hessian，则可设计保持单调性的非仿射规则。以坐标可分凸函数（如最小二乘）下坐标裁剪均值（CWTM）保持单调性为例证（Lemma 11）。
- **统一解释现代失败模式**：为 Adam/AdaGrad 不收敛、梯度裁剪/DP-SGD 几何扭曲、鲁棒分布式学习的振荡等分散现象提供单一理论框架，并证明（Corollary 12）没有任何鲁棒聚合规则能普适地保持单调性/余强制性。

## Key Contributions
- **Impossibility theorem (Theorem 1):** Under the update θ_{t+1}=θ_t−γF(g₁,…,gₙ), F universally preserves monotonicity for gradients of *any* convex function iff F is positively affine (F=Σαₖgₖ+C, αₖ≥0). This negatively resolves a standing open question.
- **Last-iterate convergence breakdown (Theorem 4):** At a stationary configuration where F is not positively affine, a cone K from the equilibrium exists such that every update strictly increases the distance to equilibrium for any step size, invalidating descent-based convergence proofs even deterministically.
- **Degraded stability (Theorems 3,6,8 & Cor. 7,9):** Non-affine aggregation necessarily violates co-coercivity, making the update strictly expansive and incurring an extra stability penalty I(F)>0 that yields strictly weaker generalization guarantees than positively affine rules.
- **Positive recipe for restricted inputs (Section 4, Lemma 11):** The proof is constructive — once admissible Hessians exclude arbitrary rank-1 p.s.d. matrices, monotonicity-preserving non-affine rules become possible. Coordinate-Wise Trimmed Mean is shown monotone (and 1/L-co-coercive) for coordinate-wise separable convex smooth objectives.
- **Unifying lens + robust-aggregation corollary:** Explains scattered failures of Adam/AdaGrad, gradient clipping/DP-SGD, signSGD, and robust distributed learning under one umbrella; Corollary 12 proves no robust aggregation rule can universally preserve monotonicity/co-coercivity.

## Methodology
The paper is purely theoretical. It studies the first-order update θ_{t+1}=θ_t−γF(g₁(θ_t),…,gₙ(θ_t)), abstracting the aggregation rule F:(ℝ^d)^n→ℝ^d independently of where gradients come from (stochastic/empirical/robust). The central notion is monotonicity ⟨θ−ω,F(θ)−F(ω)⟩≥0 and its stronger form co-coercivity. 

The proof of Theorem 1 has three steps: (1) a *rigid alignment* lemma showing any single-input variation must shift the output along the same ray, established by contradiction using convex quadratic functions with rank-1 p.s.d. Hessians; (2) deducing input-wise affinity of F; (3) synthesizing global affinity. An alternative proof (Appendix E) recasts the result via an algebraic Jacobian condition (Lemma 26: a matrix D with DH≽0 for all symmetric p.s.d. H must be αI). Extensions handle strong monotonicity (Thm 22) and ρ-hypomonotonicity (Thm 19), showing violations are unbounded unless F is Lipschitz (Thm 20). Stability results couple two trajectories on neighboring datasets and derive tight upper/lower bounds on uniform argument stability. No experiments are performed.

## Results & Highlights
- **Theorem 1 & 3:** Monotonicity and co-coercivity are each preserved iff F is positively affine — a clean, first-principles characterization requiring only d>1.
- **Theorem 4:** Geometric proof that non-affine stationary configurations create an escaping cone K, so last-iterate convergence cannot be guaranteed for Adam-style adaptive rules, clipping (DP-SGD), or robust aggregators unless extra structure is imposed.
- **Theorem 6 + Lemma 8 + Corollary 9:** Stability decomposes as Stab(A_F) ≈ γT·S_{n,C}(F,ρ) + I(F); non-affine rules force I(F)>0 (or, for discontinuous rules, a non-decaying sensitivity term), strictly worsening stability vs. affine baselines.
- **Lemma 11:** CWTM preserves monotonicity for coordinate-wise separable convex losses, recovering Hardt et al.-style stability and O(1/T) last-iterate rates — a concrete exception to the impossibility.
- **Corollary 12:** Settles an open problem (Boudou et al. 2025) negatively — no robust aggregation rule can universally preserve monotonicity or co-coercivity.
- The framework reconciles many empirical/theoretical failures (Reddi et al. Adam non-convergence, AdaGrad monotonicity loss, clipping geometry, limit cycles of heavy-ball).

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** High. A clean if-and-only-if impossibility result that unifies many previously scattered, context-specific observations about Adam, clipping, and robust aggregation is genuinely novel; the constructive "recipe" and CWTM positive case add depth beyond a pure negative.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Proofs are first-principles and self-contained, with an alternative Jacobian-based derivation (Appendix E) and extensions to strong/hypomonotonicity. The stability bounds are tight (matching upper/lower constructions up to constants).
- **Reproducibility:** N/A in the empirical sense — the paper is entirely theoretical with no code/data. The mathematical statements are checkable and clearly stated; assumptions are explicit.
- **Significance:** Potentially influential for theory of federated/distributed/private learning, as it provides a principled reason *why* non-affine aggregators struggle with guarantees and a constructive path to restricted-input remedies.
- **Red flags:** None severe. The main caveat is that "universal preservation" is a strong worst-case notion; the result does not preclude good practical or restricted-class behavior, and the paper is upfront about this (Section 4, Remark 2). The practical magnitude of the stability penalty I(F) is explicitly left to future work.

## Strengths
- Elegant, general impossibility theorem derived from first principles with no restrictive assumptions on F beyond the update structure.
- Bridges and explains disparate empirical/theoretical failure modes (Adam non-convergence, clipping geometry, limit cycles, robust-learning oscillations) under one umbrella.
- Provides a constructive recipe and a concrete positive example (CWTM on separable losses), so the contribution is not purely negative.
- Tight stability characterization (upper and lower bounds) quantifies the cost of non-affineness.
- Extensive, well-organized appendices covering extensions to strong/hypomonotonicity and an alternative Jacobian proof.

## Limitations
- Purely theoretical — no experiments or numerical corroboration of the predicted instability/convergence failures on realistic models.
- "Universal" monotonicity is a worst-case criterion; practical algorithms often work despite the theory, and the magnitude of penalties (e.g., I(F)) is not quantified beyond existence.
- The positive results (Section 4) are limited to fairly restrictive function classes (e.g., coordinate-wise separable), which may rarely hold in deep learning.
- Assumes a fixed aggregation operator F (the authors note extensions to time-varying F_t but do not fully develop momentum/dynamic schemes).
- Does not directly yield constructive new algorithms; it is mostly diagnostic and explanatory.

## Relevance
高度契合分布式/联邦学习的数学基础。联邦学习中广泛使用的非仿射聚合机制——鲁棒聚合（抵御拜占庭节点）、梯度裁剪（差分隐私 DP-SGD）、signSGD 压缩、自适应重加权等——恰恰是本文所刻画的对象；该理论揭示了这些机制为何在收敛性与泛化稳定性上存在固有瓶颈，并为设计在受限函数类下保持单调性的聚合规则提供了清晰指南。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Can the stability penalty I(F) be bounded explicitly (in T, L, C, ρ, n) for specific non-affine rules such as DP-SGD clipping or geometric-median robust aggregation?
- Does the escaping-cone geometry of Theorem 4 manifest empirically as measurable trajectory divergence on standard benchmarks, and can adaptive step-size schedules mitigate it?
- What is the minimal structural assumption beyond coordinate-wise separability that permits monotonicity-preserving non-affine aggregation?
- How do the results extend to non-convex (e.g., deep network) losses, where co-coercivity already fails — is the penalty additive or dominated?
- Can extragradient/variational-inequality equilibrium-solving dynamics restore stable convergence for non-affine aggregators, as hinted in the discussion?
- Does randomization (e.g., differential-privacy noise) provide an alternative stability route that bypasses the co-coercivity requirement quantitatively?
