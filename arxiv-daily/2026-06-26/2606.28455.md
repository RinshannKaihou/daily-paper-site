> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一套受控诊断协议，检验被动对象状态世界模型的隐状态是否随物理事件（自由运动/碰撞/遮挡）系统性重组运动学、接触与客体永久性等"潜在物理场"，并通过因果干预验证这些场对预测具有功能性作用。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces a controlled diagnostic protocol showing that hidden states of passive object-state world models encode event-regime information and systematically reweight non-exclusive kinematic/contact/object-permanence "latent physical fields," with causal-intervention evidence that suppressing field-aligned directions degrades event-relevant predictions.

## 核心贡献
- 提出一种层次化**诊断协议**（非新模型），分三层检验隐状态物理结构：事件级可分性、事件条件下场的相对重加权、固定时域下"因果场效应（CFE）"的功能敏感性。
- 引入"**潜在物理场**"概念——把运动学、接触、客体永久性视为隐状态中随事件情境变化、非互斥的分布式结构模式（诊断性而非本体论定义）。
- 构建**平衡的受控生成数据集**（Kubric 风格，900 条序列，自由运动/碰撞/遮挡各 300），事件、相位、接触标签与模型输入严格分离以防止捷径学习。
- 在 GRU、Transformer-lite、RSSM-lite 三类架构上交叉验证，发现事件情境系统性重塑场的相对权重，且抑制场方向会损害对应事件窗口的预测。
- 以多重对照（随机方向/随机子空间/场标签 shuffle）和剂量-强度扫描保守地界定结论边界，避免过度解读为"显式物理模块"。

## Key Contributions
- A **hierarchical diagnostic protocol** (not a new architecture) with three tiers: event-level separability, event-conditioned field reweighting, and fixed-horizon causal field effect (CFE).
- The notion of **latent physical fields** as distributed, non-exclusive, event-sensitive structure in hidden states — a diagnostic hypothesis rather than an assumed physical module.
- A **balanced controlled-generator dataset** (Kubric-style, 900 sequences) with strict leakage control: event/contact/phase/future labels are never given as model input.
- **Cross-architecture evidence** (GRU, Transformer-lite, RSSM-lite) that event contexts systematically reweight field emphasis (free motion = kinematic-dominant; collision adds contact; occlusion adds object-permanence), reinforced by time-aligned readouts and sliding-prefix directional consistency.
- **Causal-field-effect** intervention showing that suppressing field-aligned projection directions increases prediction loss in the relevant event windows, interpreted conservatively relative to matched controls.

## Methodology
The study targets **passive object-state world models**: transition models that observe a prefix of object-level states (position, velocity, visibility, etc.) and predict a fixed future horizon. In the main setting, models observe the first 8 frames and predict the next 8 (object-state, not raw-pixel inputs, to remove object-discovery confounds). Three architecture-family controls are evaluated — GRU-R2h, Transformer-lite, RSSM-lite — each with two seeds and two input variants (kinematic-only; observation-like, where the true hidden position of occluded objects is withheld).

The diagnostic protocol has three tiers plus phase/timewise interpretive analyses:
1. **Event Probe** — a lightweight linear classifier on frozen hidden states predicting the event regime (free motion/collision/occlusion). Tests *readability*.
2. **Field Selectivity** — a 3-class linear field readout producing softmax scores for kinematic/contact/object-permanence. Tests whether event contexts *reweight relative field emphasis* (non-exclusive), at event level (sequence representation), phase level (data-level proxy z-scores from annotated phases), and timewise level (RSSM future-rollout latents aligned to contact-onset/hidden-start; a lighter sliding-prefix directional-consistency analysis for GRU/Transformer-lite).
3. **Causal Field Effect (CFE)** — within the fixed future horizon, a field direction is derived from the readout via probe-logit-contrast, then one-dimensional projection-suppression is applied in normalized hidden space; CFE = perturbed − base event-window position MSE, with a suppression-strength sweep (α = 0.25–1.0) and controls (matched-norm random direction, random subspace, field-label shuffle).

Leakage is controlled by withholding event/contact/occlusion labels, future states, and hidden true states from model inputs; the dataset is audited for true contact (collision true-contact rate 1.000) and split into in-distribution plus OOD stress splits (harder collision angles/speeds; longer hard-occlusion windows).

## Results & Highlights
- All models beat the constant-velocity baseline on future-position MSE; **event-probe macro-F1 = 1.000** across all architecture-seed rows, indicating event-regime information is reliably readable.
- **Event-level field reweighting (Fig. 1A):** free motion strongly kinematic-dominant; collision retains kinematic but shows a clear contact-field rise; occlusion shows a much stronger object-permanence field — i.e., relative field weighting shifts systematically with event context.
- **Phase-level proxies (Fig. 1B–C)** explain why collision/occlusion are *mixtures*: collision shifts kinematic→contact→kinematic across phases; occlusion shifts visible-motion→object-permanence-dominant during hidden intervals, with partial return on reappearance.
- **Timewise hidden-state evidence (Fig. 1D–E):** RSSM rollout latents show phase-aligned reweighting; a strict phase-aware field readout reaches **macro-F1 = 0.900**. For GRU/Transformer-lite, a lighter sliding-prefix analysis yields **directional consistency score = 1.000** (positive contact-phase and hidden-phase shifts in all cases).
- **CFE (Fig. 2):** Contact-aligned suppression increases collision-window loss in **6/6 cases** (mean target CFE ≈ 0.00319), with positive target-control margins against all reported controls and positive dose trends — the clearest functional-sensitivity result. Hard-occlusion hidden-window CFE is positive in 6/6 cases (≈ 0.00619) and positive vs. random/field-shuffle controls, but the **random-subspace margin is negative (−0.00153)**, so it is "qualified" rather than subspace-specific. No reappearance-window CFE is available within the fixed horizon.

The work positions itself relative to a relevant and current probing/steering literature (Joseph et al. 2026 on physics in video world models; Alam 2026 on concept-activation-vector steering; Zhang 2026 on probing RL world models). ## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** Conceptual rather than architectural. The contribution is the *diagnostic framing* — moving from "is the variable decodable?" to "is it organized, reweighted, and functionally used in an event-conditioned way?" This is a genuine, if incremental, reframing of probing methodology, not a new model or benchmark.
- **Rigor:** Controls are a strength — leakage control, multiple intervention controls, dose sweeps, cross-architecture replication, and OOD splits. Mathematical definitions of readout scores, proxy z-scores, and CFE are provided in appendices. Claims are conservatively bounded (a "bounded conclusion" is explicit).
- **Reproducibility:** Good in principle — fixed seeds (3407/3408), hyperparameters, dataset config, and code/data release all reported. However, the dataset is a single synthetic generator.
- **Significance:** Moderate. As an analysis/methodology paper it offers a reusable template, but the empirical setting is narrow (fixed 8→8 horizon, object-state inputs, 2-object scenes, 3 event families).
- **Red flags:** (1) Event-probe macro-F1 = **1.000** in every row is suspiciously perfect and hints the event classes may be near-trivially separable even after input control — raising a question of how demanding the "readability" bar really is. (2) CFE effect magnitudes are tiny (~1e-3–1e-4 in normalized MSE units), so statistical/practical significance rests on consistent sign + control margins rather than effect size. (3) The hard-occlusion CFE fails the random-subspace control, weakening its field-specificity. (4) Only 2 seeds; event taxonomy is minimal. (5) Affiliation typo ("Robitcs") is a minor copy-editing slip.

## Strengths
- Principled, multi-layered protocol separating readability, event-conditioned reweighting, and causal functional relevance.
- Strong leakage control and a battery of matched controls (random direction, random subspace, field-label shuffle) plus dose-response sweeps.
- Cross-architecture replication across recurrent, attention, and state-space transition models adds robustness.
- Honest, conservatively scoped claims; clear distinction between diagnostic evidence and mechanistic explanation.
- Released code/data and full experimental provenance in appendices.

## Limitations
- Fixed-horizon (observe 8 / predict 8) interface — no sliding-context or long-horizon generalization; CFE is confined to the predicted future horizon.
- Object-state inputs (not raw video): results do not imply raw-video world models discover the same fields.
- CFE is a representational-subspace, head-decoding intervention — for RSSM it is *not* a full transition re-rollout; it does not isolate explicit modules or unique circuits.
- Hard-occlusion CFE lacks subspace specificity (negative random-subspace margin) and there is no reappearance-window CFE.
- Narrow event taxonomy (free motion/collision/occlusion only); small synthetic dataset; only 2 seeds; tiny absolute CFE magnitudes.

## Relevance
这篇工作与"隐状态探测 / 世界模型内部机制"高度相关：它把传统的"变量可解码性"探测升级为"事件条件下物理结构如何被组织、重加权与因果性使用"的诊断框架，并以因果干预（投影抑制）验证隐状态中分布式物理场对预测的功能性贡献，为分析世界模型内部表征提供了可复用的模板。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Why is the event-probe F1 perfect (1.000)? Which input/representation features make events trivially separable, and does removing them collapse the diagnostic?
- Would the field reweighting and CFE persist under sliding-context or longer-horizon forecasting, or under online rollout?
- Does a full RSSM transition re-rollout intervention (rather than head-decoding) change the CFE conclusions, especially for object-permanence?
- Can the field vocabulary and protocol be extended to raw-video world models (support, containment, gravity, stability) and action-conditioned agents?
- Are the tiny CFE magnitudes practically meaningful, and how do they scale with dataset size, scene complexity, and number of objects?
