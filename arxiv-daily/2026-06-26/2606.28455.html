§ 01

Methodology — a three-tier diagnostic protocol

The work targets passive object-state world models: transition models that observe an 8-frame prefix of object-level states (position, velocity, visibility) and predict the next 8 frames. Object-state inputs (not raw pixels) deliberately remove object-discovery confounds. Three architecture families — GRU-R2h, Transformer-lite, RSSM-lite — are each run with two seeds and two input variants (kinematic-only vs. observation-like, where occluded true positions are withheld).

A balanced Kubric-style dataset of 900 sequences (300 each: free motion / collision / occlusion) is generated with strict leakage control — event, contact, phase, future and hidden-true states are never given as model input. The collision true-contact rate audits to 1.000. The core move is conceptual: treat kinematic, contact, and object-permanence as distributed, non-exclusive, event-sensitive "latent physical fields" — a diagnostic hypothesis, not an assumed physical module.

Three tiers then separate three distinct questions: readability, event-conditioned reweighting, and causal functional relevance. A battery of matched controls (random direction, random subspace, field-label shuffle) plus a suppression-strength sweep (α = 0.25–1.0) keep conclusions conservatively bounded.

1

Event Probe readability

A lightweight linear classifier on frozen hidden states predicts the event regime (free motion / collision / occlusion).

2

Field Selectivity reweighting

A 3-class linear readout emits softmax scores per field at event, phase (z-score proxies), and timewise (RSSM rollout latents) levels.

3

Causal Field Effect (CFE) functional

A field direction from probe-logit-contrast is projection-suppressed in normalized hidden space; CFE = perturbed − base event-window position MSE, with dose sweep + controls.

§ 03

Strengths & limitations

Strengths
  • Principled multi-layered protocol cleanly separating readability, event-conditioned reweighting, and causal functional relevance.
  • Strong leakage control plus a battery of matched controls (random direction, random subspace, field-label shuffle) and dose-response sweeps.
  • Cross-architecture replication across recurrent, attention, and state-space transition models.
  • Honest, conservatively scoped claims; clear distinction between diagnostic evidence and mechanistic explanation.
  • Released code & data with full experimental provenance in appendices.
Limitations
  • Fixed-horizon (8→8) interface — no sliding-context or long-horizon; CFE confined to the predicted window.
  • Object-state, not raw video — results don't imply raw-video world models discover the same fields.
  • CFE is head-decoding, not transition re-rollout — doesn't isolate explicit modules or unique circuits.
  • Hard-occlusion lacks subspace specificity (negative random-subspace margin); no reappearance-window CFE.
  • Narrow event taxonomy, small synthetic dataset, only 2 seeds, tiny absolute CFE magnitudes.
§ 04

Questions for further exploration

Q1Why is event-probe F1 perfect (1.000)? Which input features make events trivially separable — and does removing them collapse the diagnostic?
Q2Do field reweighting and CFE persist under sliding-context, long-horizon, or online rollout forecasting?
Q3Would a full RSSM transition re-rollout intervention (vs. head-decoding) change CFE conclusions, especially for object-permanence?
Q4Can the field vocabulary & protocol extend to raw-video models (support, containment, gravity, stability) and action-conditioned agents?
Q5Are the tiny CFE magnitudes practically meaningful, and how do they scale with dataset size, scene complexity, object count?
Q6Given the failure of the subspace control for occlusion, is the “field” framing robust or partly an artifact of the projection geometry?
