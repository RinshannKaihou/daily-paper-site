> **一句话总结：** 本文将基准数据集子集选择形式化为"排名保持"问题，提出统一的 bootstrap 评测协议，并在时间序列、推荐系统、NLP 三领域实验中证明几何式（如 farthest-first）选择策略在表示质量高时能用极少数据集（如 TSC 仅 5 个）高度还原完整基准的模型排名。

> **TL;DR:** The paper frames benchmark dataset subset selection as an explicit rank-preservation problem, introduces a bootstrap-based evaluation protocol with confidence intervals, and shows (with theoretical bounds for farthest-first) that geometry-based selection preserves full-benchmark model rankings far better than random—provided the dataset representation is informative enough.

## 核心贡献
- 提出统一的"排名保持"评测协议：通过对数据集池/模型池做重复子采样（T=200 次）并做 bootstrap 聚合，得到带 95% 置信区间的"性能 vs 子集大小"曲线，支持原则性的统计比较。
- 系统比较多类选择策略（随机基线、K-Means 聚类、贪婪 farthest-first FAFI、A-/D-最优实验设计），覆盖时间序列分类（112 数据集/35 模型）、推荐系统（30/9）、NLP（MTEB 57/31）三个领域。
- 为 farthest-first 选择给出理论收敛保证：把表示空间中的几何覆盖（覆盖半径）与 GP 后验不确定性、MSE 衰减及排名符号错误率衰减联系起来。
- 提供实用子集大小指导（Table 2），并设计"Oracle vs Broken"合成消融实验，证明表示质量是决定非随机选择价值的关键。

## Key Contributions
- A unified, reusable evaluation protocol for dataset subset selection based on repeated subsampling plus bootstrap aggregation, yielding confidence intervals and enabling principled statistical comparison (Wilcoxon signed-rank + Holm correction) of selection strategies across subset sizes.
- A systematic empirical comparison of selection strategy families—random baselines, K-Means clustering, greedy farthest-first (FAFI, cosine & Euclidean), and A-/D-optimality from optimal experimental design—across three domains (TSC, RecSys, NLP/MTEB).
- Theoretical convergence guarantees for the farthest-first (FAFI) method: linking geometric covering radius in representation space to the decay of GP posterior uncertainty (Theorem 4.1) and to a bound on the expected fraction of ranking sign-errors (Theorem 4.2).
- A controlled "Oracle-vs-Broken representation" ablation isolating representation quality as the determinant of gains, plus practical subset-size guidance and a benchmark-scale analysis (Appendix B).

## Methodology
The paper formalizes dataset subset selection as a rank-preservation problem: given the full dataset pool D and model set M, select S ⊂ D, |S| = k ≪ |D|, to minimize the discrepancy Δ(R_S, R_D) between the ranking induced by S and the ranking induced by the full benchmark. Model rankings are computed as average ranks over cross-validation folds, and quality is measured with MAE between rankings, Spearman ρ, Kendall τ, NDCG@5, and MRR. Crucially, selection uses only a priori, leakage-free dataset representations (meta-features; time-series embeddings such as TSFRESH, Catch22, Summary, MiniRocket; landmarking probes; RecSys interaction-matrix statistics; and BERT-encoded task descriptions for NLP)—never the benchmark evaluation outcomes. The protocol runs in three stages: (1) generate T=200 randomized evaluation trials (dataset-pool or model-pool subsampling at fraction α=0.8); (2) for each trial and subset size k, apply a selection strategy and compute the induced ranking; (3) aggregate ranking-discrepancy metrics over k=2..20 with AUC-style summarization and non-parametric bootstrap confidence intervals. Statistical significance is tested with a one-sided paired Wilcoxon signed-rank test followed by Holm correction.

## Results & Highlights
- **Time Series Classification:** Geometry-based selection (especially farthest-first) consistently outperforms random; the best strategy reaches Spearman correlation 0.95 with the full benchmark using only 5 selected datasets. Gains are largest at small k (≤10) with diminishing returns; all gains are highly significant (p < 10⁻²⁶).
- **NLP/MTEB:** When using BERT description embeddings, geometry-based methods preserve rankings substantially better than random, mirroring the TSC pattern—confirming the protocol is domain-agnostic.
- **Recommender Systems:** Improvements over random are weak, inconsistent, and usually within uncertainty bands; sometimes random is even best. This indicates that commonly available dataset features fail to capture the factors driving model differentiation in RecSys.
- **Synthetic Oracle-vs-Broken ablation:** When representation distances align with the rank-generating latent geometry, farthest-first dramatically improves over random; when the representation is noise-dominated, the advantage nearly vanishes—directly tying success to representation quality rather than domain or scale.
- **Practical guidance:** For ρ ≥ 0.90, median subset size k*=5 suffices in TSC (conservative k*=11); RecSys cannot reach the target for k≤20; NLP is similar to TSC.

It builds directly on a coherent line of prior literature—the "benchmark lottery" effect, statistical comparison over multiple datasets (Demšar), optimal experimental design (Kiefer–Wolfowitz), time-series bake-offs (UCR/UEA), and MTEB. ## Critical Assessment
The novelty lies in treating benchmark reduction itself as an explicit, measurable rank-preservation problem with (a) a unified protocol featuring proper bootstrap uncertainty and significance testing, and (b) theoretical bounds linking geometric coverage to ranking error—rank preservation theory in adaptive design-of-experiments settings is described as largely new. Rigor is high: principled CIs, paired Wilcoxon with Holm correction, an honest negative RecSys result, and a clean synthetic ablation that isolates confounders. Reproducibility is strong (code released, public benchmark matrices). Significance is moderate-to-high for evaluation methodology, directly addressing benchmark reliability and the lottery effect. The main caveat is that the theory rests on fairly restrictive assumptions (Gaussian-process smoothness of model performance, a bi-Lipschitz relationship between representation distances and the rank-generating geometry), so the guarantees are more conceptual than tight for real benchmarks. No major red flags; claims are appropriately scoped to the domains tested.

## Strengths
- Unified, reusable protocol with rigorous statistical machinery (bootstrap CIs, Wilcoxon + Holm) rather than ad-hoc single-run comparisons.
- Genuine theoretical contribution: explicit bounds connecting farthest-first coverage to MSE and ranking-error decay.
- Honest reporting of where methods fail (RecSys) without overclaiming universal applicability.
- The Oracle-vs-Broken synthetic ablation cleanly isolates representation quality as the causal driver of gains.
- Practical, decision-relevant output (subset-size guidance table) and full code release.
- Three diverse domains (TSC, RecSys, NLP) strengthen generality of the empirical picture.

## Limitations
- Theoretical guarantees rely on strong assumptions (GP smoothness, bi-Lipschitz kernel conditions) that may not hold for real dataset representations.
- Considers only a priori, leakage-free representations; does not explore adaptive selection that exploits partial evaluation results.
- Limited practical value in RecSys, where selection gains over random are marginal.
- NLP treatment is supplementary rather than deep, relying on a single public MTEB score matrix.
- Optimizes for global/average ranking; does not analyze per-model stability, top-1 preservation, or robustness to adversarial benchmark composition.
- Subset size capped at k≤20, and A-/D-optimality strategies underperform despite their theoretical appeal.

## Relevance
该工作直接服务于基准可靠性这一核心关切：在动辄包含几十至上百数据集的现代基准中，它提供了用极少数据集（如 TSC 仅 5 个即可达 ρ=0.95）大幅压缩评测成本同时尽量保持模型全局排名的算法与统计框架，从而缓解"benchmark lottery"带来的排名不稳定与选择性偏差。对任何需要可信、低成本模型比较的评测流程（时间序列、NLP、推荐系统等）都具有方法论借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Can adaptive/online selection strategies that use partial evaluation outcomes beat prior-only representations while preserving the no-leakage guarantee on held-out data?
- How do the conclusions extend to top-k model preservation or per-model ranking stability, rather than aggregate global rank correlation?
- What minimum representation properties (beyond bi-Lipschitz) are empirically necessary for geometry-based selection to beat random, and can they be cheaply estimated before benchmarking?
- Would learned dataset embeddings (e.g., foundation-model-derived meta-features) recover selection gains in domains like RecSys where hand-crafted features failed?
- How robust is the rank-preservation picture under non-uniform sampling fractions α and under adversarial or distribution-shifted dataset pools?
