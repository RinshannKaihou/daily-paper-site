> **一句话总结：** 本文从可计算性与描述复杂度的角度证明，AGI 对齐的核心障碍不在于"对齐状态不可能"，而在于其结构性的不可验证性——无论在无限域（Rice/Gödel）还是有限硬件域（Trakhtenbrot），任何想做到可靠、完备且可操作的通用验证器都不可能存在。

> **TL;DR:** The paper proves that AGI alignment is structurally unverifiable: via Rice's theorem (open domains), Gödel incompleteness (static frameworks), and Trakhtenbrot's theorem (finite hardware), no infallible, complete, and tractable alignment verifier can exist; the Soundness–Completeness–Tractability trilemma is shown to be a necessary consequence of descriptive complexity rather than an empirical anomaly.

## 核心贡献
- 提出**对齐不可验证性定理**（Theorem 1）：不存在能对高度表达力的 AGI 做到"无误、完备、可操作"的通用算法验证过程。
- 提出**有限结构不可验证性定理**（Theorem 6）：将障碍分层为局部边界（固定环境，PSPACE-hard）与全局边界（跨所有有限配置，co-RE 完全/不可计算）。
- 首次系统地将 **Trakhtenbrot 定理**（有限模型论）引入 AGI 安全，论证"逃入有限硬件"无法绕过逻辑障碍。
- 将 **Soundness–Completeness–Tractability 三难困境**证明为描述复杂度的必然推论，而非统计经验现象（Corollary 3）。
- 给出现代遏制策略（Shielding、Proof-Carrying Code、Bounded Horizon、AI Boxing）的分类法，将其诠释为"对逻辑表达力的强制性牺牲"。

## Key Contributions
- **Unverifiability Theorem of Alignment (Thm 1):** No universal algorithmic procedure can certify AGI safe behavior that is simultaneously sound, complete, and tractable.
- **Finite Structural Unverifiability (Thm 6):** Stratifies the bounded-domain barrier into a *local* PSPACE-hard boundary (fixed environments, under P ≠ PSPACE) and a *universal* co-RE-complete boundary (Trakhtenbrot's Wall) across all finite configurations.
- **Two exhaustive computational vectors:** Any verifier operates either over an unbounded domain (blocked by Rice + Gödel) or a bounded domain (blocked by Trakhtenbrot); a formal exhaustiveness argument (Thm 5) claims no third strategy exists.
- **Soundness–Completeness–Tractability Trilemma (Cor 3):** ¬(S ∧ C ∧ T) is derived as a structural consequence of descriptive complexity, reframing Agarwal (2026)'s statistical trilemma as a logical necessity.
- **Engineering taxonomy:** Containment architectures (shielding, proof-carrying code, bounded-horizon planning, AI boxing) are reinterpreted as deliberate sacrifices of expressivity to preserve decidable safety fragments.

## Methodology
The paper is a theoretical synthesis combining classical computability/complexity results with AGI safety framing. AGI is formalized as a Turing machine Mx with Gödel index x, and an alignment property P as a set of partial computable functions. The argument proceeds by closing two "computational vectors" an external verifier could use: (1) **infinite means / open domains** — blocked via Rice's theorem (Thm 2, many-one reduction from the Halting Problem showing the index set IP is undecidable) and Gödel incompleteness (Thm 3, via the Diagonalisation Lemma, since any adequate framework F satisfies PA ⊆ F); (2) **finite means / hardware grounding** — blocked via Trakhtenbrot's theorem (Thm 4, reducing Halting to finite satisfiability, showing finite validity Vfin is co-RE-complete / not recursively enumerable). Section 7 argues the two vectors are jointly exhaustive. Section 8 invokes descriptive complexity (Immerman–Vardi, Fagin) to map logic expressivity (FO → LFP → SO → PFP) onto complexity classes (P, NP, PSPACE), arguing recursive self-improvement demands PFP-level expressivity. Theorem 6 assembles these into a hybrid barrier, and Corollary 3 derives the S–C–T trilemma by contradiction (an ideal verifier would force UnivVerify ∈ P, contradicting PSPACE-hardness and co-RE-completeness). The chess analogy (~10^44 finite states, PSPACE-complete generalized) illustrates intractability-in-finitude.

## Results & Highlights
- **Open-domain alignment is undecidable** (Rice): any non-trivial semantic alignment property of a Turing-complete AGI cannot be decided; static frameworks suffer Gödelian incompleteness (true-but-unprovable safety statements).
- **Finite hardware does not save alignment** (Trakhtenbrot): universal finite validity across all finite structures is co-RE-complete, hence not even recursively enumerable — the "escape to bounded hardware" collapses.
- **Local verification is decidable but intractable:** within one fixed finite environment, checking is decidable by exhaustive search, but worst-case PFP specifications are PSPACE-hard (assuming P ≠ PSPACE).
- **Trilemma as logical necessity:** ¬(S ∧ C ∧ T) follows structurally; modern containment strategies are not temporary engineering patches but mandatory sacrifices of logical expressivity.
- The paper positions itself as extending Agarwal (2026)'s statistical trilemma and rebutting de Melo et al. (2025)'s "halting machines resolve undecidability" claim.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty (mixed):** The framing and the specific deployment of Trakhtenbrot's finite-model-theory result to rebut the "finite hardware" escape is the paper's genuinely fresh contribution. However, the individual impossibility results (Rice/Gödel applied to alignment) are restatements of well-established classical theorems; the Rice-based argument explicitly "follows and sharpens" de Melo et al. (2025). Technical novelty is therefore limited to the synthesis and the Trakhtenbrot bridge.
- **Rigor (moderate):** The reductions are standard textbook proofs reproduced largely faithfully. The weakest link is the **exhaustiveness argument (Thm 5)**, which is presented informally: the dichotomy "finite vs infinite domain" is treated as obviously exhaustive, and the dismissal of probabilistic/parameterized strategies as collapsing into one of the two vectors is hand-wavy rather than rigorous.
- **Reproducibility (N/A / high):** A purely theoretical paper; "reproducibility" amounts to proof-checking, and the proofs are conventional. No experiments or code.
- **Significance (conceptual):** Provides a clarifying conceptual map for the alignment impossibility debate, but offers no new quantitative or empirical leverage. The practical-engineering section is descriptive rather than actionable.
- **Red flags:** (a) Heavy reliance on the **unproven P ≠ PSPACE** assumption for the local-tractability clause. (b) The "Turing-complete AGI" and "PFP expressivity required" assumptions are modeling choices asserted rather than independently justified. (c) Substantial AI authorship disclosed. (d) Limited engagement with the substantial prior literature on probabilistic/approximate verification, which is where most practical alignment work lives.

## Strengths
- Clean, well-organized synthesis that connects four classical results (Rice, Gödel, Trakhtenbrot, descriptive complexity) into a single coherent argument about AGI alignment.
- The application of Trakhtenbrot's theorem specifically to defeat the "finite hardware / halting machines" escape is a valuable and underappreciated insight.
- The reframing of containment strategies as *deliberate sacrifices of expressivity* (rather than temporary engineering limits) is a conceptually useful lens.
- The chess analogy effectively communicates intractability-in-finitude to a broader audience.

## Limitations
- The core impossibility results are not new; technical contribution rests mainly on synthesis and the Trakhtenbrot application.
- Local-intractability clause depends on the unproven assumption P ≠ PSPACE.
- The exhaustiveness argument (no third verification vector) is informal and does not rigorously handle hybrid/probabilistic/interactive verification paradigms.
- Treats approximate or probabilistic safety guarantees only superficially, despite their centrality to real-world alignment practice.
- Single-author preprint with acknowledged heavy AI assistance and a thin reference base; claims would benefit from formal peer review.

## Relevance
本文对 ML 可靠性与对齐的数学基础具有直接的理论价值：它系统性地论证了"通用对齐验证"在可计算性与复杂度层面的不可达性，为对齐研究中"为何无法给出绝对保证"提供了形式化依据，并提示实用安全工作应聚焦于受限、可判定片段内的权衡而非追求完备验证。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Can the exhaustiveness argument (Thm 5) be made rigorous for *probabilistic*, *interactive*, or *oracle-assisted* verification, which the paper dismisses quickly?
- How do approximate/probabilistic safety guarantees (e.g., PAC-style, statistical certificates) interact with the S–C–T trilemma — is there a relaxed trilemma for high-probability guarantees?
- To what extent do the conclusions hold for *non-Turing-complete* but still highly capable architectures (e.g., systems below full PFP expressivity)?
- Could quantum or other non-standard computational models weaken the Trakhtenbrot/PSPACE barriers in any alignment-relevant sense?
- How would the proposed containment taxonomy be empirically validated or falsified against deployed AI safety systems?
