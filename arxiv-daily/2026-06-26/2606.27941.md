> **一句话总结：** VASAE 在 SAE 训练中加入词汇对齐锚定损失，让每个学习到的字典方向都获得一个由最近 token 嵌入赋予的"内在 token 名称"，在几乎不损失重构质量的前提下将命名步骤从训练后移到训练时。

> **TL;DR:** VASAE adds a vocabulary-aligned anchor loss to sparse-autoencoder training so each learned decoder direction inherits an intrinsic token name from its nearest token embedding, preserving reconstruction quality while producing token-aligned features.

## 核心贡献
- 提出 VASAE：在标准稀疏自编码器（SAE）重构损失之外，加入软"词汇对齐锚定"目标，使每个学习到的解码器方向在训练中逐步靠近其最近的 token 嵌入。
- 每个特征在训练后直接获得一个**内在 token 名称**（与其几何上最近 token 对应的字符串），把特征命名从"训练后的解读"变为"训练时几何诱导的索引"。
- 通过与"硬绑定解码器"基线（直接令解码器等于 token 嵌入）对比，证明保持字典可学习同时仅作软锚定是关键设计——硬绑定会严重损害重构。
- 在 GPT-2-small 与 Llama-3.1-8B 上重构指标与标准 SAE 基本持平（VE 分别为 0.965 / 0.931），同时获得高比例的词汇对齐特征。
- 案例研究显示，减去句级平均稀疏码后，许多活跃特征的内在 token 名称与上下文中邻近的输入 token 语义相关。

## Key Contributions
- Introduces VASAE, a top-k residual-stream SAE trained with an additional vocabulary-aligned anchor loss that pulls each learnable decoder direction toward its nearest fixed token embedding.
- Turns feature naming from a post-hoc step into a training-time geometric byproduct: each feature inherits the token string of its nearest vocabulary embedding as an "intrinsic token name."
- Empirically demonstrates that keeping the dictionary learnable (soft anchoring) is essential, since hard-tying the decoder to token embeddings collapses reconstruction (negative variance explained).
- Shows reconstruction metrics essentially match a standard SAE (GPT-2-small VE 0.965; Llama-3.1-8B VE 0.931) while producing strongly token-aligned features.
- Provides case studies in which intrinsic token names of active, sentence-centered features are semantically relevant to nearby input tokens (e.g., location words near "Baker Street").

## Methodology
VASAE decomposes Transformer post-residual streams with a top-k sparse autoencoder (k=32, non-negative sparse code, linear encoder, Adam, lr=1e-3), using vocabulary-sized dictionaries (d_sparse = |V|). The standard reconstruction loss L_recon is augmented with an anchor term. For each decoder column d_i, the method computes the maximum cosine similarity s_i to any fixed token embedding w_v and its argmax vocabulary item v*_i (the nearest-token anchor). The anchor loss L_anchor = -(1/d_sparse) Σ s_i rewards higher nearest-token alignment, and total loss is L_vasae = L_recon + λ_anchor·L_anchor. Crucially, the dictionary stays learnable while token embeddings serve only as fixed reference vectors; v*_i is recomputed each step so the anchor can drift as the feature moves. After training, the intrinsic token name of each feature is simply the string of its nearest vocabulary token, with s_i reported as its alignment score. Two checkpoints are studied—GPT-2-small (d_model=768, |V|=50,257, 12 layers, float32) and Llama-3.1-8B (d_model=4096, |V|=128,256, 32 layers, bfloat16)—using WikiText-103 activations extracted via nnsight, and a diagnostic strong-alignment threshold τ=0.8 chosen from the bimodal separation between standard-SAE scores (≈0.1–0.3) and VASAE scores (clustered near 1.0).

## Results & Highlights
- Reconstruction preserved: VASAE matches the standard SAE on variance explained (GPT-2-small 0.965, Llama-3.1-8B 0.931) and CE recovery (0.975 / 0.906); the hard-tied decoder baseline collapses (VE negative, CE recovery near or below zero).
- GPT-2-small geometric alignment: ~89–94% of features in layers L0–L10 exceed the τ=0.8 cutoff, with 53–56% vocabulary coverage (many-to-one naming); drops to 68.5% at L11.
- Llama-3.1-8B (λ=5×10⁻³): depth-dependent pattern—L0 has 119,016/128,256 strongly aligned features (92.8%) with 60.3% vocab coverage; L15 lower; L31 unstable, confirming alignment is strongest near embedding space.
- Case studies: sentence-centered active features surface semantically coherent intrinsic names (located, location, Street, around, corner near "Baker Street"; plus adjective/adverb, award, and self-introduction word examples in the appendix).
- Standard SAE features concentrate at alignment scores 0.1–0.2 and essentially never cross the cutoff, framing post-hoc nearest-token naming of standard SAEs as a low-alignment baseline.

## Authors & Credibility
Kairui Zhang and Zahraa S. Abdallah are at the Intelligent Systems Laboratory, University of Bristol; Ziwen Yu is at the University of Bristol; Martha Lewis is at the University of Amsterdam. The work is accepted to the 2nd Workshop on Compositional Learning at ICML 2026, Seoul. The authors engage carefully with mechanistic-interpretability methodology (LogitLens/tuned-lens, sparse feature circuits, weight tying) and are notably disciplined in distinguishing a geometric "name" from a causal "explanation," which lends credibility to the claims.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** Moderate and focused. Repurposing the model's own input token embeddings as in-training anchors for SAE features is a clean, well-motivated idea that shifts naming upstream. The hard-tied decoder ablation is a useful, clarifying contrast.
- **Rigor:** Generally sound—two model families, three methods (standard SAE, hard-tied, VASAE), clearly defined metrics (MSE, VE, CE loss, CE recovery), and an explicit diagnostic threshold justified by the score distribution. However, standard deviations are reported *across layers, not across random seeds*, which obscures run-to-run and seed-level variability and is a notable methodological gap.
- **Reproducibility:** Reasonably strong: open-weight models, standard dataset (WikiText-103), stated hyperparameters, nnsight extraction, a public project page, and a memory-bounded chunked nearest-token implementation. Missing: seeds, full compute budget, and the broader anchor-strength sweep relegated to the appendix.
- **Significance:** As a workshop paper, a useful contribution to interpretability tooling, but incremental; the deeper-layer instability in Llama limits practical applicability where late-layer features matter most.
- **Red flags:** None severe, but caveats matter. The name is explicitly a geometric handle, **not** a causal interpretation; alignment is demonstrated, not behavioral effect. Alignment is concentrated in shallow/middle layers and is anchor-coefficient-sensitive, with only input-embedding anchors tested (unembedding anchors deferred). Extrapolating "named feature ⇒ semantically meaningful feature" would over-read the results.

## Strengths
- Reconstruction quality is essentially unchanged from a standard SAE while gaining an interpretable, vocabulary-indexed handle—no free-lunch cost observed in the tested settings.
- The hard-tied decoder ablation cleanly isolates the value of keeping the dictionary learnable.
- Principled, geometrically derived naming avoids manual or separate automated labeling pipelines.
- Clear, honest framing of what a name does and does not imply (semantic readout vs. computational role vs. behavioral control).
- Strong quantitative separation between standard-SAE and VASAE alignment distributions makes the central claim visually and statistically legible.

## Limitations
- Tested only on two open-weight model families and only residual-stream SAEs; generalization to more architectures/scales unverified.
- Main alignment evidence is strongest for GPT-2-small; Llama-3.1-8B alignment is depth-dependent and unstable in the final layer.
- Only input-embedding anchors are used; for untied models, unembedding-based anchors remain unexplored.
- No seed-level variance, no causal/behavioral validation, and limited broader hyperparameter sweeps.
- Intrinsic names are many-to-one (multiple features can share a name) and provide no guarantee of feature monosemanticity or circuit-level role.

## Relevance
该工作直接服务于**可解释性 / 稀疏自编码器 / 隐藏状态**这条主线：它处理的对象正是 Transformer 残差流（隐藏状态的核心通道），并试图给 SAE 字典方向赋予与模型自身词表挂钩的、几何可验证的命名，为后续的电路发现与隐藏状态分析提供了更易索引的特征句柄。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Does anchoring to the unembedding matrix (rather than input embeddings) recover stable alignment in Llama's deep/final layers?
- Can the anchor loss be made layer-adaptive (stronger near input, weaker near output) to avoid deep-layer instability?
- Do VASAE-named features actually participate in circuits or admit causal/behavioral validation (ablation, patching, steering), beyond geometric alignment?
- How does alignment behave at scales beyond 8B and with sub-vocabulary (d_sparse < |V|) dictionaries, where naming becomes many-to-one by construction?
- Is the τ=0.8 threshold robust across architectures, or is it an artifact of the observed GPT-2 score distribution?
