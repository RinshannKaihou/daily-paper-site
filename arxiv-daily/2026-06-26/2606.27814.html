ATOD: Annealed Turn-aware On-policy Distillation for Multi-turn Autonomous Agents

Qitai Tan · Zefang Zong · Yang Li · Peng Chen

On-policy Distillation GRPO / RL Multi-turn Agents Credit Assignment Model Distillation
Key finding

ATOD anneals teacher distillation into reward-driven RL with turn-level reweighting — letting small agents imitate, then surpass, their teachers.

一句话总结 ATOD 通过退火的 OPD-RL 调度（早期教师蒸馏主导、后期奖励驱动加强）与回合级分歧-不确定性加权（T-DUR），让小模型多轮智能体先模仿教师、再超越教师，在 ALFWorld / WebShop / Search-QA 上稳定优于纯 OPD、GRPO 及现有蒸馏基线。

01

Methodology

one advantage, two schedules

ATOD post-trains a small student policy πθ on its own on-policy rollouts inside multi-turn environments. For each prompt it samples a group of G trajectories, computes environment rewards, and forms group-relative GRPO advantages. The centerpiece is a single token-level hybrid advantage — teacher distillation and RL folded into one clipped-surrogate update, with no extra value model and no explicit KL penalty (the teacher signal already lives inside AOPD).

At = κ(s)·AOPD + ρ(s)·AGRPO AOPD = ( log πT − log πθ ) · wk(t)  ·  standard GRPO clipped surrogate

Dynamic annealing. A linear progress variable p(s)=min(s/T,1) drives κ from 1.0 down to a 0.1 floor — a weak teacher anchor kept against reward hacking — while ρ climbs from 1.0 to 2.0 over T=80 steps. Both schedules are Lipschitz-smooth.

T-DUR · turn-level reweighting

For each turn k, two statistics are computed from sampled-token log-probs alone (no full-vocabulary teacher pass):

  • Disagreement dk — mean teacher–student log-prob gap.
  • Uncertainty hk — mean student NLL, a martingale-difference estimator of token entropy.
wk = 1 − (1−d̃k)(1−h̃k) per-trajectory min–max norm · Soft-OR (t-conorm) fusion

Both proxies are per-trajectory normalized and fused with Soft-OR, so a turn is upweighted when either signal is high. The key move: T-DUR lifts token-importance to the turn level — the natural decision unit for agents — and gates only the distillation term, never weakening the reward signal.

Training dynamics

  • Fast start, no plateau. Keeps OPD/SOD's quick warm-start yet keeps climbing where they flatten; GRPO grows slowly throughout.
  • Signal handoff observed. OPD signal magnitude decays while RL signal stays strong — exactly the intended anneal.
  • Shorter trajectories. ATOD learns more efficient rollouts than GRPO.

Ablations (Fig. 4): removing annealing is the largest drop — 0.6B falls 82.8% → 75.8%; turn-level T-DUR beats both token-level and uniform weighting.

Strengths & Limitations

side by side
▲

Strengths

  • Clean, drop-in formulation. OPD+GRPO folded into one GRPO-style clipped surrogate; no extra value model; T-DUR needs only sampled-token log-probs.
  • Motivated by clear complementarity — empirically shows OPD is fast-but-plateaus while GRPO is slow-but-higher-ceiling.
  • Consistent wins across three diverse domains (embodied, web, tool-QA) and three model scales.
  • Mechanistic ablations and diagnostics that actually explain why each component helps.
!

Limitations

  • Beyond-teacher gains are small & uneven — essentially tied on Search-QA; the headline claim is benchmark-specific.
  • Short training window (150 steps, all sizes); no scaling-law or longer-horizon study.
  • Search-QA collapses 7 datasets into one number; per-dataset and single- vs. multi-hop breakdowns absent.
  • No compute / wall-clock comparison vs. baselines; T-DUR's extra teacher forward passes unaccounted.
  • Only Qwen3 students; generalization to other families and much longer trajectories untested.

Open questions

for further exploration
Q1 · HORIZON

How does ATOD behave on 50+ turn marathon-agent benchmarks where turn error compounds more severely?

Q2 · SCHEDULE

Is linear κ/ρ annealing optimal, or would adaptive (loss- or reward-gap-driven) scheduling do better?

Q3 · GATING

Can T-DUR extend to gate the RL advantage too, or use environment-derived turn criticality rather than log-prob signals?

Q4 · TEACHER GAP

How sensitive are results to teacher choice and the teacher–student capacity gap (here 4B / 30B teachers)?

Q5 · KNOWLEDGE CEILING

Does the modest Search-QA gain signal a distillation ceiling for open-domain knowledge vs. structured interaction?

Q6 · COST

What is the marginal compute cost of ATOD's teacher forward passes relative to its gains over GRPO?
