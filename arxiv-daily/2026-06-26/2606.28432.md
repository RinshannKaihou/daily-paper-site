> **一句话总结：** 本文用 Weyl 特征值扰动理论证明，权重量化噪声会单调（在温和条件下严格）抬高经验 Fisher 信息矩阵的最大特征值，从而为部署期语言模型谱信号监控阈值必须按目标硬件实测量化校准提供了理论机制解释。

> **TL;DR:** Using Weyl's eigenvalue perturbation inequality, the paper proves that weight-quantization noise inflates the dominant eigenvalue of the empirical Fisher Information Matrix, explaining why runtime spectral-monitoring thresholds for deployed LLMs must be calibrated empirically on target quantization hardware rather than transferred from full-precision estimates.

## 核心贡献
- 针对经验 FIM 主导特征值 λmax，提出两类结构化扰动的扰动分析框架：输入分布偏离参考流形、以及模型参数自身的有限精度量化噪声扰动。
- 给出"曲率单调性假设下偏离即抬高信号"的充分（非必要）条件命题（Proposition 3.2），并明确论证无条件的反向结论不成立，逐一列出四种误报配置。
- 核心理论结果（Theorem 4.3）：通过 Weyl 不等式证明量化噪声扰动下 λmax ≥ 全精度值 + 三阶余项，且在噪声协方差 Qq 正定的温和一般性条件下严格大于全精度值，给出量化膨胀机制的方向性界。
- 提供两个可计算近似：基于 top-K 的启发式公式（Proposition 5.1）和带严格双侧夹逼界的 KL-proxy 局部二次逼近（Theorem 5.2）；并给出增广状态空间上五区域阈值规则的完备互斥划分（Proposition 6.1）。
- 在 12 个 4-bit 量化模型、1080 条生成轨迹上给出支持性测量，并通过理论显式承认若干局限（标签循环性、i.i.d. 噪声模型理想化、未预注册等），将闭式量化膨胀幅度预测列为开放问题。

## Key Contributions
- A perturbation-theoretic framework for the dominant eigenvalue λmax of the empirical FIM under two structured perturbations: conditioning-input departure from a reference manifold, and additive quantization noise on the model's own parameters.
- A sufficient (not unconditional) result (Proposition 3.2) that, under an explicit local curvature-monotonicity hypothesis, manifold departure elevates the normalized signal σt = λmax/λbase, with a careful rebuttal of the unconditional converse (Remark 3.3) and enumeration of four false-positive configurations (Remark 3.4).
- The principal theoretical result (Theorem 4.3): via Weyl's inequality, λmax under quantization-noise parameter perturbation is lower bounded by its full-precision value up to a third-order remainder, and strictly exceeds it at leading order under a mild genericity condition Qq ≻ 0.
- Two tractable approximations to the intractable exact λmax — a heuristic top-K closed form (Proposition 5.1) and a rigorously two-sided-bounded KL-proxy quadratic approximation (Theorem 5.2) — plus a complete, pairwise-disjoint five-region partition of an augmented monitoring state space (Proposition 6.1).
- Supporting empirical measurements across twelve 4-bit quantized open-weight models (1,080 trajectories) consistent with the qualitative predictions, paired with unusually candid limitations (labelling circularity, idealized i.i.d. noise model, non-pre-registered evaluation).

## Methodology
The paper is posed as a perturbation problem (not an asymptotic-spectrum problem) for the dominant eigenvalue of a fixed, evaluated empirical FIM. (1) For input departure, it posits a falsifiable local curvature-monotonicity assumption (Assumption 3.1: the map ∆θ ↦ λmax is locally strictly convex) and proves elevation of σt via a second-order expansion. (2) For weight quantization, it models quantization noise as i.i.d. uniform (Definition 4.1), expands the score function to first order under θ̂ = θ + ϵq with Jacobian Jq ≈ I, and decomposes the perturbed FIM into the full-precision FIM plus a positive-semidefinite covariance-type matrix Qq plus a third-order remainder; Weyl's inequality for symmetric-plus-PSD sums then yields the directional bound. (3) For tractability, it derives a KL-proxy local quadratic approximation under exponential-family regularity (Theorem 5.2) with a Rayleigh-quotient sandwich bound, and an unproven top-K heuristic surrogate. (4) It proves a finite threshold-partition completeness result on a joint (σt, rt) state space. The empirical section uses the top-K heuristic surrogate (not exact FIM) to measure σt on GGUF Q4_K_M models via Ollama, comparing lead-time signal elevation against an automated factuality-classifier-based departure labelling.

## Results & Highlights
- Theorem 4.3: quantization inflates λmax directionally — λmax^(q) ≥ λmax^(FP32) + O(∆q^3), with strictly positive leading-order inflation λmax^(q) ≥ λmax^(FP32) + λmin(Qq) + O(∆q^3) whenever Qq ≻ 0.
- Empirically observed a calibration threshold τmax ≈ 15.9–17.7 on a 4-bit Q4_K_M model versus a full-precision pilot estimate θpilot ≈ 0.065 — a ~244× gap, offered as a single instance consistent with the qualitative mechanism (not a theorem-derived constant).
- Phase 2: median 27-token lead time (IQR 12–39) of σt elevation before labelled departure events; 85.6% detection rate across 1,080 trajectories; strongest per-model correlation r = 0.962 (a 32B Qwen3 model), median r = 0.479 across nine non-CoT models.
- Computational cost ladder: exact FIM O(|V|·d²) → KL-proxy O(|V|) → top-K surrogate O(K).
- The central methodological takeaway: spectral thresholds for deployed LLM monitoring must be calibrated on the actual target hardware and quantization configuration, not transferred from full-precision theory.

## Critical Assessment
- Novelty: The parameter-perturbation (rather than input-asymptotic) framing of the FIM's dominant eigenvalue under quantization is a genuinely fresh angle relative to the random-matrix FIM literature; however, the core proof machinery (first-order score expansion + Weyl's inequality for symmetric-plus-PSD) is standard, so novelty lies mainly in the problem formulation and application.
- Rigor: High and self-aware. Theorem 4.3 cleanly separates the unconditional weak bound (Qq ⪰ 0) from the strict bound (Qq ≻ 0); Remark 4.5 explicitly refuses to over-claim a theorem linking eigenvalue inflation to empirical threshold quantiles, relegating the connection to a labelled heuristic.
- Reproducibility: Mixed. Inference backend, hardware (Apple M5), quantization scheme, and methodology are given, but model names, prompt sets, and per-trajectory data are withheld pending a later release; the evaluation was not pre-registered.
- Significance: The theoretical results are qualitative/directional only — no closed-form prediction of the inflation magnitude, and the 244× figure is a single non-controlled measurement under non-identical pilot/calibration conditions. The i.i.d. uniform noise model does not match real blockwise schemes (GPTQ/AWQ/GGUF/EXL2).
- Red flags: (i) Labelling circularity between σt and the severity score (partly mitigated by reporting the median); (ii) the pilot-vs-calibration comparison is not a controlled ablation isolating quantization; (iii) the strong headline correlation (r = 0.962) rests on the same circular oracle; (iv) very high p-values and t-statistics that are not robustness-checked with multiple-comparison correction.

## Strengths
- Exceptionally honest scope discipline: every theorem is paired with explicit remarks on what it does and does not establish, including refusal to connect eigenvalue inflation to threshold quantiles via a theorem.
- Clean, minimal proof of the quantization inflation bound via Weyl's inequality; the PSD covariance structure of Qq arises naturally from the outer-product expectation.
- Practical and timely application (runtime OOD/anomaly monitoring of deployed quantized LLMs) with a clear methodological recommendation independent of theorem strength.
- Useful computational-complexity ladder making the approximations deployable on d ≫ 10⁹ models.
- Open problems are stated precisely (primitive conditions for Assumption 3.1, closed-form architecture-aware ξq, structured-noise extension, pre-registered validation).

## Limitations
- Proposition 3.2 rests on an unverified, non-primitive curvature-monotonicity hypothesis (Assumption 3.1).
- Theorem 4.3 gives only a directional/qualitative bound — no closed-form magnitude, no monotonicity in bit-width proven.
- The i.i.d. uniform quantization-noise model is idealized and mismatches real blockwise, weight-correlated schemes.
- The top-K heuristic (Proposition 5.1) has no controlled error bound; Theorem 5.2's equivalence to σt requires an eigenvector-alignment condition that generally fails.
- Empirical circularity: the departure/severity oracle shares functional dependence with σt; not pre-registered; data not yet released; single hardware platform and bit-width only.
- The 244× figure is a single, non-controlled measurement and is explicitly not a universal constant.

## Relevance
本文直接关联量化与精度可靠性主题：它从信息几何与矩阵扰动理论角度，刻画了权重量化噪声如何系统性抬高 Fisher 信息矩阵的局部曲率（主导特征值），为低精度部署下数值精度如何扭曲可靠性信号提供了数学基础。对量化模型部署期监控、阈值校准与 OOD 检测有直接方法论价值，也提示在量化可靠性评估中不能将全精度理论估计直接迁移到量化硬件。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Can primitive, architecture-level conditions on pθ (e.g., specific activation/softmax regularity) be found that provably guarantee the curvature-monotonicity hypothesis (Assumption 3.1)?
- How does the inflation bound change under structured, weight-correlated quantization noise (GPTQ/AWQ/GGUF/EXL2) instead of i.i.d. uniform — can Qq be characterized for blockwise schemes?
- Is the inflation magnitude ξq monotone in bit-width, and can a closed-form, architecture-aware prediction be derived?
- Does the eigenvalue-inflation-to-threshold-quantile relationship (Remark 4.5 heuristic (14)) hold rigorously under explicit calibration-corpus assumptions?
- How do results change across hardware architectures, higher bit-widths, and with human-annotated, pre-registered departure labels that remove the labelling circularity?
