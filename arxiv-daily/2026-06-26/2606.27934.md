> **一句话总结：** 论文将硬件基准测试中的每一个报告数值，通过内容哈希绑定到其观测与验证记录，构建出一个仅凭离线存档即可审计、防篡改且可独立复核的证据图谱，使"硬件本身"成为测量结果的见证者与签名者。

> **TL;DR:** The paper turns every reported hardware-benchmark number into a tamper-evident, offline-auditable record by hash-linking each quantity to its observation and a device-calibrated verification, effectively making a transparency log whose witnesses are the physical accelerators themselves.

## 核心贡献
- 提出哈希链接的证据图谱（evidence graph），将文本、表格、图中出现的每一个硬件基准数值通过内容哈希绑定到其观测节点与验证节点，整张图可在单个离线存档上以线性时间审计。
- 针对线性量（矩阵乘），采用 Freivalds 概率恒等式校验（O(kn²)），并将容差 τ 由浮点舍入误差分析推导、标定到器件自身测得的残差基底 ρ̄，使错误结果以 1−2⁻ᵏ 的概率被拒绝。
- 针对非线性量（attention、原子累加），设计代数校验（softmax 行和不变量、加权校验和），可捕获每次运行都复现的"一致性故障"——这类故障无法被运行间可复现性检测发现。
- 给出 Blackwell 矩阵乘在各精度/规模下的残差基底与可复现性类别测量，并展示两块同型号卡在确定性输出上逐位一致、吞吐量却带固定差异的器件指纹。
- 对校验本身进行安全性分析：揭示已公开探针种子会让攻击者将 50% 的输出错误隐藏在探针零空间中，而基于声称输出导出的 Fiat-Shamir 挑战在保持离线可复现的同时闭合该攻击。

## Key Contributions
- A hash-linked, append-only evidence graph that binds every displayed benchmark quantity (in prose, tables, figures) to its observation and verification via SHA-256 content addressing; tamper-evident under collision resistance and auditable in a single offline linear pass.
- A device-calibrated Freivalds identity check for linear quantities: tolerance τ = m·ρ̄ (m=3) derived from rounding-error analysis and calibrated to the device's own measured residual floor, giving a 1−2⁻ᵏ detection guarantee.
- An algebraic guard for nonlinear quantities (attention softmax invariant, weighted-checksum for atomic accumulation) that catches consistent faults which run-to-run divergence cannot see.
- A measured characterization of Blackwell: residual floors by precision/size, reproducibility classes (bit-stable S0 vs. bounded-divergence Snd) for every kernel, plus a per-card throughput fingerprint from the two-device differential.
- A security analysis of the check itself: exposing that a committed probe seed is an attack surface, defeated on every witness simultaneously by a Fiat-Shamir challenge derived from the claimed output, while a coordinated null-space corruption fools a 4-witness quorum of redundancy.
- Portable, sub-linear re-verification: inputs regenerate from committed seeds (re-checkable on any device incl. CPU), Merkle commitment over claims audits any single number in O(log N), and a directed hash-continuation across instruments.

## Methodology
The construction treats a benchmark as an integrity problem, not a presentation problem. Observations, reductions, displayed claims, and the compiled document form a hash-linked acyclic graph G=(V,E) with a strict layering root → claims → reductions → observations; every arrow is a SHA-256 hash over a canonical JSON byte image (lexicographic keys, shortest round-trip decimal floats). Auditing recomputes every node id, checks every edge, and re-runs each pure reduction (median, dispersion, rank correlation, divergence, residual aggregate) using only the standard library — no device or network needed.

For a claimed matrix product C=AB, the verifier draws a random probe X∈ℝⁿˣᵏ and forms the normalized residual ρ(C)=‖A(BX)−CX‖∞/(‖CX‖∞+ε) in float32 (more accurate than the inspected product) at O(kn²). The acceptance tolerance τ=mρ̄ (m=3) uses ρ̄, the largest residual over correct repeats at a given precision/size — a property of the silicon, not the algorithm. Nonlinear quantities (attention = two products around a softmax; scatter/index-add = bucketed sum) get an algebraic check: softmax rows must sum to one, and random weighted sums must satisfy the additive Freivalds identity. Each verification is recorded as a multi-stage transcript (acquire → witness residual/tolerance → decide → repair via higher-precision recompute → re-witness), all hash-chained.

Cross-device corroboration treats D[q]=q(card1)−q(card0) as a measurement: deterministic kernels agree bit-for-bit (D=0), while throughput carries a small fixed per-part signature. A retired device's transferable invariants re-derive from committed seeds on a successor of a different architecture; a recallable device signature (floor vector + memory sweep) is read off the hardware. The adversarial analysis shows a probe-aware adversary can hide a 50%-norm corruption in a committed probe's null space (accepted), and a coordinated version fools 4 bit-identical witnesses; a Fiat-Shamir challenge derived from the output rejects both. Physical stress (di/dt power virus, thermal soak) is applied to probe whether a tenant can move the floor or induce a silent error.

## Results & Highlights
- 0 natural errors in 139,264 verified products under sustained di/dt power-virus and thermal-soak stress; the residual floor stayed invariant at 2.36×10⁻⁴ (inflation 1.00×) across cold, soak, and di/dt operating points on RTX 5090 — a healthy part throttles before it breaks.
- Injected-fault detection works as advertised: a single bit flip drives FP16 residual from 3.06×10⁻⁴ to 2.01×10⁻³ (rejected vs. τ=6.06×10⁻⁴); an FP8 product at 1.58×10⁻³ exceeds the FP16-calibrated tolerance and is repaired by float32 recompute (2.74×10⁻⁴).
- Two RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell Max-Q cards agree bit-for-bit (relative output differential = 0) on all deterministic kernels across sizes/precisions, yet their throughputs differ consistently (2.56% FP16, 2.21% FP8) — identical numerics, physically different machines.
- First reported residual floor for Blackwell by precision: FP16/TF32 (~2×10⁻⁴) sit below BF16/FP8 (~1.5–1.8×10⁻³); dense gemm at all precisions is bit-stable (S0), while atomic scatter/index-add are nondeterministic (Snd, ~10⁻⁵ divergence).
- Out-of-sample replays (RTX 5090, RTX PRO 6000 Server): residual floors stay within a 1.00–1.26× band; floor ordering and the deterministic/nondeterministic boundary transfer across two instrument changes, while throughput remains device-local.
- Reconstruction from a retired RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell Server Edition's seeds on an H200 NVL recovered 100% (20/20 floors, 8/8 classes) of transferable invariants; only the 8 device-local throughput figures were lost.
- A 16× random-access cross-die penalty (662 → 43 GB/s) is exposed that the streaming number (7013 GB/s) flattens to nothing.
- A committed-seed probe lets a probe-aware adversary pass a product wrong by 50% of its norm (residual 2.36×10⁻⁴, accepted); the Fiat-Shamir challenge rejects it (6.36×10⁻¹), catching 4/4 coordinated witnesses where redundancy alone is fooled.

## Authors & Credibility
Faruk Alpay and Barış Başaran are affiliated with the Department of Computer Engineering, Bahçeşehir University, Istanbul, Turkey; the corresponding author also lists lightcap.ai. The paper is an arXiv preprint (cs.CR) with no stated peer-reviewed venue. Credibility is bolstered by a complete reproducible artifact: a single archive with 240 observations (one per JSONL line), the verification transcript, cross-device differentials, the evidence graph, Python verifier using only the standard library, and a manifest verifying to root hash 1303d007503848af…. The work is positioned honestly against fleet-scale silent-data-corruption studies (Hochschild "Cores that don't count", Dixit SDC-at-scale) and recent Blackwell microbenchmark literature, with explicit threat-model and scope-limitation sections.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** The combination is novel even though individual primitives (Freivalds 1977, Fiat-Shamir, Merkle/content addressing, ABFT) are classical. The genuinely new framing is (a) treating a hardware benchmark as a transparency-log/verifiable-computation object, (b) calibrating the probabilistic check's tolerance to the device's own measured residual floor rather than a textbook bound, and (c) making the physical accelerator both the witness (cosignature) and signer (recallable signature). The Blackwell residual floor and the bit-exact two-card agreement with a nonzero throughput differential appear unreported.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Proofs (tamper-evidence, single-pass audit, calibrated detection) are stated as lemmas/theorems/propositions with proofs; numerical results are given with explicit tolerances, miss probabilities, and covariate correlations. The security analysis is adversarial and self-critical — the authors themselves expose the probe-seed vulnerability and the coordinated-witness quorum attack, then close both.
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent in principle and largely in practice: standard-library-only verifier, committed seeds that regenerate inputs on any device, manifest with named root hash, Merkle inclusion paths. The main gap is hardware availability — re-running to bit-exactness requires the same architecture, and the floors are measured on specific Blackwell SKUs.
- **Significance:** High for benchmark trustworthiness and silent-data-corruption auditability. It offers a concrete mechanism to make reported HPC/AI numbers checkable by readers who cannot recompute them, aligning with FAIR principles, the AI Act logging requirements, and measure-report-verify regimes.
- **Red flags / caveats:** The injected bit-flip and consistent-fault corruptions are placed by hand — the paper is careful to state they measure detector sensitivity, not natural error rates. Validation is limited to "three measured Blackwell product configurations," not all future Blackwell systems. The detection guarantee presumes the producing host is not physically subverted; below that boundary the construction must compose with a hardware root of trust it explicitly says it complements rather than replaces. As an unreviewed arXiv preprint, claims have not undergone external peer review.

## Strengths
- Makes "every number carries its evidence" a concrete, auditable, offline-verifiable artifact rather than a slogan.
- Device-calibrated tolerance elegantly separates legitimate low-precision roundoff from genuine error, and the floor is measured rather than assumed.
- Honest and rigorous security analysis: the authors attack their own construction (committed-seed null-space attack, coordinated-witness quorum) before fixing it.
- Rich empirical content (residual floors, reproducibility classes, two-card differential, cross-die latency penalty) that is independently useful beyond the integrity framework.
- Complete reproducible bundle (code, observations, transcript, manifest, root hash) using only the standard library.

## Limitations
- Empirical validation is confined to three Blackwell-class NVIDIA SKUs plus one Hopper H200 NVL; generalization to AMD/Intel accelerators or future architectures is asserted, not demonstrated.
- Bit-exact re-verification still needs the same architecture; only tolerance-bounded agreement (or cross-hardware tensor-core replay) is portable.
- The softmax/attention reference is formed at a verification size, not the hot-path size; operators without a clean decomposition fall back to the weaker divergence class.
- Injected corruptions are synthetic; the natural silent-error rate on healthy silicon is shown to be ~0 under stress, so the framework's value is framed as guarding against the rare defective part or the privileged attacker — a narrower threat than the abstract implies.
- Tamper-evidence rests on SHA-256 collision resistance and the detection guarantee on probe-column independence; both are standard assumptions but should be restated as such.
- No peer review yet (arXiv preprint).

## Relevance
这篇论文与硬件可靠性、静默数据损坏（silent data corruption）以及基准测试可信度高度相关：它直接针对"报告的硬件性能数字无法被读者复核、且硅片本身可能静默出错"这一核心痛点，把每个数值变成可离线审计、防篡改的证据记录，为 SDC 检测、跨器件复现和基准结果的可信验证提供了一种可落地的机制，对 AI/HPC 基础设施的运维、合规（如 AI Act 日志要求）与跨团队结果互信都有实际价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- How does the residual floor behave across non-NVIDIA accelerators (AMD MI-series, Intel), and does the precision-ordering invariant (FP16/TF32 < BF16/FP8) generalize?
- Can the framework extend beyond synthetic injected faults to continuously monitor real training/inference workloads, capturing naturally occurring SDC at production scale?
- What is the overhead of full evidence-graph instrumentation on long-running multi-day jobs, and when must the cheap Freivalds check give way to the sub-linear interactive-proof variant?
- How would the recallable device signature and witness cosignature compose in practice with a hardware root of trust (e.g., NVIDIA Confidential Computing attestation) to close the privileged-attacker gap?
- Could the two-card differential / fingerprint technique be turned into a fleet-level identity or supply-chain provenance signal for tracking marginal or counterfeit parts?
- Does the 16× cross-die random-access penalty scale with die count on MCM (multi-chip-module) parts like Blackwell's B200/B300, and can the framework expose it automatically?
