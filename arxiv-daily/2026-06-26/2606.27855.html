Applicability of memorization indicators for early spotting of overfitting while recalibrating sEMG-decoders on low sample sizes

Key Finding

A single ReLU layer's activation-rate drop, read off one training batch, flags overfitting during low-sample sEMG recalibration — no validation set required.

◆ Why This Matters
A validation-free, train-data-only online signal to catch overfitting early in low-sample, on-device calibration — a lightweight stand-in for held-out validation curves where no validation data exists.
◆ Critical Edge
Never quantitatively evaluated as an early-warning classifier — no AUC, precision/recall, or threshold-selection protocol — and tested on only one architecture and one dataset with 10 fine-tuning subjects.

Methodology

how it works

A two-stage transfer-learning pipeline on NinaPro DB2 (49 movements + rest, 12 electrodes): pretrain a compact 1D-CNN on 20 subjects, then subject-specifically fine-tune on 10 held-out subjects.

  • Inputs: raw sEMG via LibEMG — normalized, 50-sample / 20 ms windows, 10-sample overlap.
  • Model: three conv blocks (256/128/64 filters, kernel 5, BatchNorm, ReLU) + three FC ReLU layers (1216/608/256) + 49-way softmax; Adam, lr=1e-3, batch 64.
  • Two overfitting levers: calibration repetitions (1–4) and trainable scope — freeze all but the last two layers vs. retrain all layers.
  • Monitored signal: activation rate of each of the 256 last-hidden-layer neurons, computed on the first 64-sample batch of every epoch (training data only).
  • Summarized as MAR (mean activation rate), AR quantiles (25/50/75%) and coefficient of variation — distributional detail the mean hides.

Strengths & Limitations

side by side

▲ Strengths

  • Targets a real, under-served gap: validation-free overfitting monitoring in low-sample, on-device calibration.
  • Clean contrast between successful ("last layers") and unsuccessful ("all layers") fine-tuning makes the indicator's discriminative behaviour easy to see.
  • Indicators are computable on a single 64-sample training batch per epoch — genuinely lightweight and online-compatible.
  • Honest framing as relative, within-experiment indicators, with explicit acknowledgement of dataset-dependence.
  • Adds value beyond prior work via distributional AR metrics (quantiles, CV) that expose dynamics the mean hides.

▼ Limitations

  • Only one compact architecture and one dataset (NinaPro DB2); deeper nets, other activations, other modalities unproven.
  • No quantitative evaluation as an early-warning trigger — no AUC, precision/recall, or thresholding protocol.
  • 10 fine-tuning subjects is a small cohort; per-subject variability visible but not statistically characterized.
  • The "all-layers" failure may reflect under-learning, not classical overfitting — complicating the memorization interpretation.
  • Absolute MAR values are not transferable across setups; no universal thresholds proposed. No released code/data.

Open Questions

for further exploration
  1. Can MAR (and the new distributional metrics) become a calibrated early-warning classifier with measured AUC/precision across many subjects, rather than a qualitative separator?
  2. Do these indicators stay predictive in deeper / attention-based SOTA sEMG models and with non-ReLU activations (GELU, Swish)?
  3. Is the MAR threshold (or its relative change) transferable across datasets, electrode setups, and class counts, or must it always be normalized within-experiment?
  4. Could activation-rate indicators drive a closed-loop policy — early stopping, selective layer freezing, dynamic regularization — that measurably improves final calibration accuracy?
  5. Does the indicator distinguish true point-memorization from under-learning / feature-memorization, given the "all-layers" case may not be classical overfitting?
Credibility note. The team authored the memorization-indicator framework this paper extends (Neurocomputing 2024; LOD 2025) and prior sEMG transfer-learning work — authority over both halves. The study is honestly scoped as "first empirical evidence," not definitive; absolute MAR is dataset- and architecture-dependent.
