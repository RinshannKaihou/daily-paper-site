> **一句话总结：** 本实证研究发现，将 ROCKET 压缩方法中 MCKP 的分配代价从权重空间改为输出空间后，任务准确率略有提升但困惑度反而上升，揭示出一种准确率–困惑度权衡，且效应被两种误差指标间>0.99 的高相关性所限制。

> **TL;DR:** Replacing ROCKET's weight-space MCKP allocation cost with an output-space (whitened) error yields a small accuracy gain (+0.8pp on Qwen3-8B/50%) at the cost of 16% worse perplexity, with the limited effect explained by the >0.99 correlation between the two error metrics.

## 核心贡献
- 提出 ROCKET-ActCost，将 ROCKET 中 MCKP 分配代价从权重空间 Frobenius 误差替换为输出空间（白化）误差，使全局分配目标与逐层分解目标对齐。
- 发现并刻画一种准确率–困惑度权衡：在 Qwen3-8B/50% 压缩下，输出空间代价使 8 个零样本基准平均准确率提升 0.8pp，但 WikiText 困惑度上升 16%。
- 通过相关性分析解释效应有限的原因：权重空间与输出空间误差的 Spearman 相关性 >0.99，仅约 70/252 层分配发生变化。
- 证明分配代价函数的影响在激进压缩下更显著，而在温和压缩（Llama-3.2-1B/20%）下基本消失。
- 该方法复用 profiling 阶段已有矩阵，无额外校准开销、无运行时开销。

## Key Contributions
- Propose ROCKET-ActCost, replacing ROCKET's weight-space MCKP allocation cost with an output-space (whitened) error so the allocation objective matches the per-layer factorization objective.
- Reveal an accuracy-perplexity tradeoff: output-space cost improves average task accuracy (+0.8pp on Qwen3-8B/50%, gains on all 8 benchmarks) but worsens WikiText perplexity by 16%.
- Provide a mechanistic explanation for the modest effect: a >0.99 Spearman correlation between weight- and output-space errors means only ~70 of 252 layers receive different allocations.
- Show the cost-function effect is most pronounced under aggressive compression and largely vanishes at milder ratios (Llama-3.2-1B/20%: near-identical results).
- Achieve the change with zero runtime overhead by reusing matrices (W_L, Ŵ_L) already computed during ROCKET's profiling.

## Methodology
ROCKET-ActCost modifies ROCKET's global budget allocation rather than its factorization. ROCKET compresses each linear layer via sparse-dictionary factorization in a whitened activation space (using the upper Cholesky factor L of the Gram matrix X^⊤X), where output reconstruction error equals whitened Frobenius error (‖XW − XŴ‖_F = ‖W_L − Ŵ_L‖_F). It then solves a multi-choice knapsack problem (MCKP) over candidate (rank k, sparsity s) configurations, using the weight-space relative Frobenius error ‖W − Ŵ‖_F/‖W‖_F as cost.

ROCKET-ActCost replaces this cost with the output-space (whitened) relative error ‖W_L − Ŵ_L‖_F/‖W_L‖_F, which directly measures the compression impact on a layer's output for the calibration distribution. Because 96.6% of (layer, compression-ratio) pairs have different optimal sparsity configurations (ks ratios) under the two metrics, ActCost changes both the MCKP cost and the per-layer selected configuration. All required matrices already exist during ROCKET's profiling, so no extra calibration passes or runtime are added; the MCKP solver runs identically.

Setup: Qwen3-8B at 50% (primary) and Llama-3.2-1B at 20% (secondary); 256 sequences of length 1024 from RefinedWeb for calibration (2 seeds for Qwen3-8B); 8 zero-shot benchmarks via lm-eval-harness (PIQA, HellaSwag, LAMBADA, ARC-E/C, SciQ, RACE, MMLU) plus WikiText-2 PPL.

## Results & Highlights
- Qwen3-8B/50%: average accuracy 52.3% → 53.1% (+0.8pp), improving on all 8 benchmarks; largest gains on ARC-Challenge (+1.5pp), MMLU (+1.5pp), and LAMBADA (+1.3pp). However, WikiText PPL rose 52.98 → 61.46 (+16%).
- Llama-3.2-1B/20%: near-identical results (53.5% vs 53.3%, −0.2pp; PPL 14.66 vs 14.45) — the effect is negligible at milder compression.
- Allocation divergence is small: only ~70 of 252 compressible layers get different allocations, due to >0.99 per-layer Spearman correlation between the two error metrics across candidate configurations.
- 96.6% of (layer, compression-ratio) pairs select a different optimal ks configuration under output-space vs weight-space error.
- ROCKET-ActCost was actually 7.7% faster on average, owing to slightly different allocation decisions.

## Critical Assessment
- Novelty: Low-to-moderate. The contribution is a single, principled swap of the MCKP cost function within an existing method (ROCKET); no new algorithmic machinery.
- Rigor: Adequate for the narrow claim but limited in scope — only 2 models, one aggressive and one mild compression ratio, with no sweep of intermediate ratios. Controls are clean (shared profiling pipeline, identical MCKP solver, both variants directly comparable).
- Reproducibility: Strong — public code, fixed calibration/eval protocol, multi-seed for the primary setting, zero added runtime cost.
- Significance: Modest. The headline result is a tradeoff rather than a clear win; +0.8pp accuracy paired with +16% PPL is use-case dependent, and the secondary setting shows essentially no effect.
- Red flags: FARS/machine-generated manuscript; the >0.99 error correlation is shown largely in hindsight and partly explains away the negative side of the result; the paper essentially documents a limited-effect modification framed as a study.

## Strengths
- Honest, transparent reporting, including the FARS disclosure and the accuracy-perplexity tradeoff rather than a one-sided success claim.
- Clean ablation isolating the cost-function change while holding the profiling pipeline and solver fixed.
- Strong mechanistic analysis (Spearman correlation, allocation-divergence counts) that explains the modest effect size.
- Zero runtime overhead and trivial adoptability for existing ROCKET users.
- Public code and standard, widely-used evaluation harness (lm-eval-harness).

## Limitations
- Small scope: only 2 models and two compression ratios, with no coverage of intermediate (30–40%) ratios.
- The main "improvement" comes with a perplexity regression, making the net benefit use-case dependent.
- FARS/machine-generated origin raises questions about the depth of related-work positioning and the framing of the contribution.
- Findings are specific to ROCKET's MCKP formulation; generalization to other rank-allocation methods (CoSpaDi, SVD-LLM, ASVD) is not tested.
- Some per-benchmark gains (e.g., PIQA +0.08pp, RACE +0.05pp) are within typical seed variance.

## Relevance
该研究与量化/压缩的可靠性直接相关：它系统检验了校准引导压缩中"分配代价应与输出目标对齐"这一直觉，结果表明对齐并不必然带来全面增益，提示评估压缩方法时必须同时考察任务准确率与语言建模困惑度，而非依赖单一指标。对追求稳健部署的压缩实践而言，这种权衡意识尤为重要。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Does the accuracy-perplexity tradeoff persist, weaken, or invert at intermediate compression ratios (30–40%) or on larger models (70B+)?
- Could a hybrid cost (weighted combination, or output-space cost with a perplexity regularizer) retain the accuracy gains without the PPL regression?
- Does the >0.99 correlation between weight- and output-space errors generalize to other activation-aware factorization methods, and can whitening variants decorrelate the two metrics?
- How does ROCKET-ActCost interact when composed with downstream quantization or pruning stages in a multi-stage compression pipeline?
- Would calibrating on task-specific data (rather than RefinedWeb) shift the accuracy-perplexity tradeoff?
