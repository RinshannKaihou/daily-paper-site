02 / METHOD

A perturbation framework, not an asymptotic one

λmax of a fixed, evaluated empirical FIM under two structured perturbations.

The paper reframes the question as a perturbation problem for the dominant eigenvalue of a single evaluated empirical FIM — explicitly not an asymptotic-spectrum problem. It treats two structured perturbations in parallel.

(1) Input departure. It posits a falsifiable curvature-monotonicity assumption (Assumption 3.1: the map ∆θ ↦ λmax is locally strictly convex) and proves elevation of σt = λmax/λbase via a second-order expansion — a sufficient (not unconditional) result, paired with a careful rebuttal of the converse and enumeration of four false-positive configurations.

(2) Weight quantization. Noise is modeled as i.i.d. uniform (Def 4.1); the score function is expanded to first order under θ̂ = θ + εq with Jacobian Jq ≈ I. The perturbed FIM decomposes into the FP32 FIM plus a positive-semidefinite covariance-type matrix Qq plus a third-order remainder — Weyl's inequality for symmetric-plus-PSD sums then yields the directional bound.

(3) Tractability. A KL-proxy local quadratic approximation under exponential-family regularity (Thm 5.2) with a Rayleigh-quotient sandwich bound, plus an unproven top-K heuristic surrogate (Prop 5.1).

(4) Threshold partition. A complete, pairwise-disjoint five-region partition of an augmented (σt, rt) monitoring state space (Prop 6.1).

Empirics measure σt via the top-K surrogate on 12 GGUF Q4_K_M models through Ollama (Apple M5), comparing lead-time signal elevation against an automated factuality-classifier departure labelling.

Core proof machinery

First-order score expansion + Weyl's inequality for symmetric-plus-PSD matrix sums. Standard tools; novelty is in the problem framing.

Honest scope discipline

Every theorem paired with remarks on what it does & does not establish. Remark 4.5 refuses to over-claim an eigenvalue→threshold-quantile theorem.

Approximations ladder

Exact FIM is intractable at d ≫ 10⁹; two surrogates make the bound deployable, at the cost of (respectively) no error bound and an unproven alignment condition.

04 / VERDICT

Strengths & limitations

◆ Strengths

  • Exceptionally honest scope discipline: every theorem paired with explicit remarks on what it does & does not establish — Remark 4.5 explicitly refuses to over-claim.
  • Clean, minimal proof via Weyl's inequality; the PSD structure of Qq arises naturally from the outer-product expectation.
  • Practical, timely application (runtime OOD / anomaly monitoring of deployed quantized LLMs) with a methodological recommendation independent of theorem strength.
  • Useful computational-cost ladder makes the approximations deployable on d ≫ 10⁹ models.
  • Open problems stated precisely (primitive conditions, architecture-aware ξq, structured-noise extension, pre-registered validation).

◇ Limitations

  • Prop 3.2 rests on an unverified, non-primitive curvature-monotonicity hypothesis.
  • Thm 4.3 gives only a directional/qualitative bound — no closed-form magnitude, no monotonicity in bit-width proven.
  • The i.i.d. uniform noise model is idealized and mismatches real blockwise, weight-correlated schemes (GPTQ/AWQ/GGUF/EXL2).
  • Top-K heuristic has no controlled error bound; Thm 5.2's equivalence to σt needs an alignment condition that generally fails.
  • Empirical circularity: the departure oracle shares functional dependence with σt; not pre-registered; data withheld; single platform & bit-width only.
05 / OPEN

Questions for further work

Q1 · PRIMITIVE CONDITIONS

Can architecture-level conditions on pθ (e.g. specific activation/softmax regularity) provably guarantee the curvature-monotonicity hypothesis (Assumption 3.1)?

Q2 · STRUCTURED NOISE

How does the bound change under weight-correlated quantization noise (GPTQ/AWQ/GGUF/EXL2) instead of i.i.d. uniform — can Qq be characterized for blockwise schemes?

Q3 · BIT-WIDTH

Is the inflation magnitude ξq monotone in bit-width, and can a closed-form, architecture-aware prediction be derived?

Q4 · THRESHOLD QUANTILE

Does the eigenvalue-inflation→threshold-quantile relationship (Remark 4.5 heuristic) hold rigorously under explicit calibration-corpus assumptions?

Q5 · CIRCULARITY

How do results change across hardware, higher bit-widths, and with human-annotated, pre-registered departure labels that remove the labelling circularity?

Q6 · ROBUSTNESS

Are the very high t-statistics / low p-values robust to multiple-comparison correction, given the strong headline correlation rests on a circular oracle?
