Layerwise Progressive Freezing: A Training Scaffold for Depth-Scalable Binary Networks ↗
Evan Gibson Smith, Bashima Islam · Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) · 2026-06-26 · arXiv 2606.27759
Key finding

In binary networks, freezing layers input → output during training reverses depth collapse — order is decisive, traced to activation-induced gradient blockades.

一句话总结：提出 StoMPP 逐层渐进冻结方法，按"输入→输出"顺序将权重与激活逐步二值化，从根本上缓解二值激活引发的梯度阻断，使深度二值网络（BNN）的精度随深度衰减大幅减小——前向逐层成功，反向逐层崩塌至接近随机。

Why this matters

Identifies activation-induced gradient blockades as the root cause of BNN depth degradation — and offers an orthogonal, composable training scaffold for quantization-aware training.

Critical edge

Absolute accuracies sit below SOTA because the deliberately minimal matched recipe excludes distillation, LR scheduling, and architectural tricks — arguably underselling the STE baseline.

⚠ mechanism = correlation, not causal intervention

Methodology

Stochastic Masked Partial Progressive Binarization
Stage 1 · Blend
Masked Forward Blending

Real-valued variables are kept for both weights and pre-activations. A binary mask M decides per entry: frozen → sign(u), unfrozen → Smooth(u) (u' = M⊙sign(u) + (1−M)⊙Smooth(u)), where Smooth is clip for activations and identity for weights.

→ decoupled frozen gradient ▸
Stage 2 · Refresh
Soft-Refresh Transition

Each transitioning layer targets a growing frozen fraction p(τ)=(τ/T)³ (cubic schedule). Only k=⌊n/r⌋ ≈ 1% of indices (r=100) are redrawn from Bernoulli(p) each step — preserving temporal stability unlike full resampling or hard freezing.

→ stable, not jittery ▸
Stage 3 · Wave
Layerwise Input→Output Wave

Binarization advances as a contiguous wave: a frozen prefix (no gradient needed) → a single transition layer → an unfrozen suffix supplying a valid gradient path to the layer currently transitioning. This is what avoids the blockades global masking creates.

→ forward order is decisive

The frozen-entry gradient is a design choice: zero gives a fully STE-free trainer, while applying STE only to frozen entries (StoMPP+STE) recovers a learning signal through committed units. All ablations are run STE-free to isolate the progression's contribution from any surrogate-gradient benefit — yielding a clean characterization of schedule, refresh, ordering, and training dynamics. Defaults (cubic, r=100) were tuned once on CIFAR-100/R18 and applied without re-tuning everywhere else.

Strengths · Limitations

critical assessment
Strengths
  • +Clean, interpretable central finding — order sensitivity & gradient blockades — supported consistently across architectures and tasks.
  • +Decoupling progression from the frozen-entry gradient (STE-free vs StoMPP+STE) is elegant: clean ablation and still strong results.
  • +Robust to schedule shape and refresh rate — defuses the "yet another hyperparameter sweep" critique.
  • +Strong generalization beyond ResNets: MobileNetV2, BERT/SST-2, Bi-RealNet.
  • +Methodologically sound — matched recipe, STE-free isolation, single-point hyperparameter tuning applied everywhere.
Limitations & Risks
  • !Blockade mechanism is supported by correlation, not a dedicated causal intervention (left to future work).
  • !Absolute accuracies below SOTA — minimal recipe excludes distillation, LR scheduling, architectural tricks.
  • !Fine-tuning interaction is architecture-dependent — ranking flips on Bi-Real-18 fine-tuning.
  • !Introduces new hyperparameters (schedule, refresh, per-layer step allocation) absent from vanilla STE.
  • !Transformer evaluation limited to BERT/SST-2; broader ViT/LLM binarization untested.

Open Questions

for further exploration
Q1

Can a direct causal intervention — forced gradient injection through a frozen activation — cleanly isolate and confirm the blockade mechanism?

Q2

Does StoMPP+STE reach or exceed SOTA when combined with competitive recipes (distillation, LR scheduling, learned scaling) — do gains compose multiplicatively?

Q3

Does input→output ordering generalize to deeper/larger transformers (ViTs, LLMs) where attention paths and residual structure differ from CNNs?

Q4

Could non-uniform per-layer step allocation (more epochs for harder/deeper layers) beat the equal-allocation default?

Q5

Is the sawtooth convergence signature a reliable diagnostic for binarization-induced optimization difficulty in other QAT settings?

Q6

Why does the method underperform STE on Bi-Real-18 fine-tuning — is the rule tied to initialization in a predictable way?
