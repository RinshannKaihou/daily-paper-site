Key Finding

§01

Methodology

The operational unit is the capability slice: a group of evaluation samples that share structured values along four dimensions — deliberately intermediate between a benchmark (too coarse) and an individual sample (too noisy). This makes "evaluation failure → data fix" reasoning tractable and auditable.

Dim · 1
Background condition
language, domain, context scope, ambiguity
Dim · 2
Task type
factual QA, logic, formal math, code
Dim · 3
Solving operation
multi-label: arithmetic, symbolic, multi-hop…
Dim · 4
Output constraint
answer form, scoring rule, exactness

An analysis toolkit instantiates the slice: an eval taxonomy annotated over 16 benchmarks (each sample independently labeled by DeepSeek-V3.2 & Qwen3.5 Plus, disagreements adjudicated by humans), a non-instruction data taxonomy, and eval↔data mapping rules. Mapping rules are framed as hypothesis generators, not causal claims; every intervention is validated under a single-variable design.

Case 2 adds a diagnosis-aligned weakness score (raw error + pairwise + non-additive triple composition, reliability-weighted) that drives Efraimidis–Spirakis weighted reservoir sampling over a synthetic instruction pool — training recipe and token budget held fixed.

＋ Strengths

  • Exemplary root-cause analysis in Case 1 — a "reasoning regression" becomes a one-line loss-mask fix. Practitioner gold.
  • Bidirectional case design (rules data OUT, rules data IN) makes the loop's value explicit in both directions — more convincing than two same-direction anecdotes.
  • Conceptually clean intermediate representation with four well-justified dimensions mapping eval and data onto a shared vocabulary.
  • Honest framing: mapping rules are heuristics, effects qualified by protocol, limitations stated plainly; single-variable controlled designs throughout.

— Limitations

  • Narrow empirical scope: 16 fixed benchmarks and a single CPT pipeline; other training stages & capability suites untested.
  • The non-instruction data taxonomy is exercised only descriptively — no executed retrieval/reweighting validates the indirect side of the mapping (an asymmetry).
  • Case 2 standard-suite effect sizes are small/noisy at Pass@1 (mean Δacc +0.16–0.33 pt); headline gains rely on Pass@128 over small AIME samples.
  • Protocol dependency: the same intervention looks like failure under single-shot eval but success under Pass@128 — the loop's verdict is eval-protocol-sensitive.

§03

Open Questions

Q1
Does the loop generalize beyond CPT to instruction tuning, RLHF, and from-scratch pre-training — where the data↔capability relationship differs?
Q2
Can the non-instruction data branch be validated symmetrically — do retrieval/reweighting by the same affordance profile yield comparable controlled gains?
Q3
How robust are the taxonomy labels to annotator/LLM choice, and how much does disagreement degrade slice-level diagnoses at scale?
Q4
Could the diagnosis-aligned weakness score become adaptive (updated online during training) rather than computed once from a warm-start checkpoint?
