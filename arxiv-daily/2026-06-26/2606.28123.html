Dangerous Liaisons of Convex Learning and Non-Affine Aggregation

Thomas Boudou · Batiste Le Bars · Nirupam Gupta · Aurélien Bellet
Inria · CNRS CRIStAL · Univ. of Copenhagen 26 Jun 2026 Theory · no experiments
The one-sentence result

In first-order convex learning, an aggregation rule preserves monotonicity if and only if it is positively affine — every non-affine aggregation breaks last-iterate convergence.

一句话总结：本文证明在凸一阶优化中，只有“正仿射”的梯度聚合规则才能普适地保持单调性；任何非仿射聚合（自适应、裁剪、鲁棒/隐私聚合）都会破坏单调性，导致末次迭代不收敛与稳定性退化——为现代学习系统中分散的失败模式给出统一理论解释。

Why this matters

Federated learning's non-affine aggregators — robust aggregation, DP-SGD clipping, signSGD compression, adaptive reweighting — are precisely the objects this theory explains, exposing inherent convergence & stability bottlenecks.

Critical edge

"Universal" monotonicity is a worst-case criterion. The work is purely diagnostic — no experiments — and the practical magnitude of the stability penalty I(F) is left unquantified.

01

Methodology, distilled

first-order · monotone operators · no experiments
Abstract update θt+1 = θt − γ · F( g1(θt), … , gn(θt) )

The aggregation rule F is studied in isolation — independent of whether gradients are stochastic, empirical, or robustly filtered. The central notion is monotonicity ⟨θ−ω, F(θ)−F(ω)⟩ ≥ 0 and its stronger form co-coercivity.

The main proof is constructive: it pins down exactly when monotonicity can survive. Extensions cover strong monotonicity and ρ-hypomonotonicity (violations are unbounded unless F is Lipschitz). Stability results couple two trajectories on neighbouring datasets and derive tight upper and lower bounds on uniform argument stability.

An alternative derivation (Appendix E) recasts everything as an algebraic Jacobian condition. No experiments are performed — the contribution is entirely theoretical and the proofs are deferred to extensive appendices.

Theorem 1 — three-step proof

  1. Rigid alignment. Any single-input variation must shift the output along the same ray — shown by contradiction via convex quadratics with rank-1 p.s.d. Hessians.
  2. Input-wise affinity. Each input of F is forced to be affine in isolation.
  3. Global synthesis. Piecewise affinity composes into global positive-affinity: F = Σαkgk + C.
02

Results & verdicts

each theorem, tagged safe / danger
ResultVerdictWhat it establishes
Theorem 1 ✓ iff affine Monotonicity is preserved for gradients of any convex function iff F is positively affine — negatively resolving a standing open question.
Theorem 3 ✓ iff affine The same iff characterization holds for co-coercivity.
Theorem 4 ✗ breaks Where F is non-affine at equilibrium, an escaping cone K exists: every update strictly increases distance to equilibrium, for any step size — defeating descent proofs.
Thm 6 · L8 · Cor 9 ! penalty Stability decomposes as Stab(AF) ≈ γT·Sn,C(F,ρ) + I(F); non-affine rules force I(F) > 0, strictly weakening generalization vs affine baselines.
Lemma 11 △ exception Coordinate-Wise Trimmed Mean does preserve monotonicity (and 1/L co-coercivity) for coordinate-wise separable convex smooth losses — a constructive escape hatch.
Corollary 12 ○ settles open No robust aggregation rule can universally preserve monotonicity or co-coercivity — settling Boudou et al. (2025) negatively.

The framework reconciles many scattered failures under one umbrella: Adam/AdaGrad non-convergence, gradient-clipping & DP-SGD geometry distortion, signSGD, and the limit cycles of heavy-ball.

03

Strengths & limitations

side-by-side

Strengths High novelty · Strong rigor

  • Elegant, general impossibility theorem from first principles — no restrictive assumptions on F beyond the update structure.
  • Bridges and explains disparate failure modes (Adam non-convergence, clipping geometry, limit cycles, robust-learning oscillations) under one umbrella.
  • Not purely negative: a constructive recipe plus a concrete positive case (CWTM on separable losses).
  • Tight stability characterization — matching upper and lower bounds up to constants.
  • Extensive, well-organized appendices: strong/hypomonotonicity extensions and an alternative Jacobian proof.

Limitations Mostly diagnostic

  • Purely theoretical — no experiments or numerical corroboration of the predicted instability on realistic models.
  • "Universal" monotonicity is worst-case; practical algorithms often work despite the theory, and penalties like I(F) aren't quantified beyond existence.
  • Positive results (Section 4) are confined to restrictive function classes (e.g. coordinate-wise separable) that rarely hold in deep learning.
  • Assumes a fixed operator F; momentum / dynamic / time-varying Ft schemes are noted but not fully developed.
  • Does not directly yield constructive new algorithms — it is mostly diagnostic and explanatory.
04

Questions for further exploration

open directions
1

Can the stability penalty I(F) be bounded explicitly (in T, L, C, ρ, n) for specific non-affine rules such as DP-SGD clipping or geometric-median robust aggregation?

2

Does the escaping-cone geometry of Theorem 4 manifest empirically as measurable trajectory divergence on standard benchmarks — and can adaptive step-size schedules mitigate it?

3

What is the minimal structural assumption beyond coordinate-wise separability that still permits monotonicity-preserving non-affine aggregation?

4

How do the results extend to non-convex (deep-network) losses, where co-coercivity already fails — is the penalty additive or dominated?

5

Can extragradient / variational-inequality equilibrium-solving dynamics restore stable convergence for non-affine aggregators, as hinted in the discussion?

6

Does randomization (e.g. DP noise) offer an alternative stability route that bypasses the co-coercivity requirement quantitatively?
