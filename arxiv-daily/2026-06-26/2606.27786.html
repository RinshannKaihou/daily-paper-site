§ 01

Methodology

FFN-residual gate · GRPO

SHIFT keeps the LLM backbone frozen (Qwen-3-0.6B/1.7B/8B, Llama-3.x) and inserts tiny gate parameters ψ={(wₗ,bₗ)} into the FFN branch of every Transformer layer. The standard FFN update becomes a gated update where a scalar g∈(0,λ_g) controls how much the FFN contributes to the residual stream — per-token suppression, preservation, or amplification instead of fixed intervention rules.

h̃ + gℓ,t · FFN(LN(h̃)),   gℓ,t = λ_g · σ( wₗᵀ h̃ℓ,t + bₗ )

Gates are initialized so the gated model starts identical to the frozen backbone (wₗ=0, bₗ=logit(1/λ_g)) — a minimally-invasive intervention. They're then trained with GRPO: groups of candidate responses are sampled, scored by a composite format + faithfulness reward, and per-response advantages computed. The objective

𝓛 = −𝓔GRPO + β·𝓛ref + λgate·𝓡gate

keeps the policy close to the backbone via the reference-KL constraint and L2 gate regularization. Gradients flow only through ψ, so the backbone is never modified. λ_g = 2; trainable params range 28.7K–147.5K (≈0.002–0.005%).

§ 03

Strengths & Limitations

side-by-side

＋ Strengths

  • Extremely parameter-efficient (<0.01% trainable) with the backbone frozen — easy to plug into existing models.
  • Input-adaptive, not static; interpretability evidence shows gates learn conflict-discriminative patterns (AUC 0.832).
  • Strong, consistent gains over prompting, decoding, and fine-tuning baselines with good out-of-domain generalization.
  • Minimal collateral damage to general capability (MMLU) — directly fixes the cascading-failure of neuron-level editing.

! Limitations

  • No experiments on specialized domains or multimodal QA; scope is general-domain QA benchmarks only.
  • No evaluation on models larger than 8B (compute) — scaling behavior uncertain.
  • Gains taper on stronger backbones, raising questions about saturation for very large models.
  • Faithfulness reward depends on answer normalization and could be brittle on open-ended generation.
§ 04

Open Questions

for further exploration
Q1 How does SHIFT behave on much larger models (30B–70B) — does conflict-discriminative gating persist or saturate? scaling
Q2 Can the gate's learned behavior transfer cross-task or cross-domain without retraining — meta-learned / task-conditional gates? transfer
Q3 How do SHIFT gates relate to known knowledge neurons / retrieval heads — do they suppress the same circuits? interp
Q4 Could the faithfulness reward be gamed, and how robust is SHIFT to adversarially retrieved context beyond simple counterfactuals? robustness
Q5 Does GRPO-trained gating offer better steering control than supervised alternatives, and can gates enable downstream diagnosis? RL vs SFT
