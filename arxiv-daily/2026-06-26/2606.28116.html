Methodology

Two modules · Two first-principles monitors

FlashAttention · Operator-Level Faults

monitor where biased rounding damage actually accumulates

Low-precision backward passes deposit biased rounding errors as coherent rank-one atoms into the weight update. The authors target ΔW = W_t − W_{t−δ} rather than raw W—it strips initialization energy and localizes exactly where damage lands.

Because attention depends on W_q, W_k only through the bilinear form F = W_q W_k⊤, they decompose the increment exactly:

ΔF = Δ₂ (first-order) + Δ₃ (interaction)

and track the spectral entropy of Δ₂—computed from small r×r cores without materializing the full d×d product.

MoE Routers · Hyperparameter Faults

read the full softmax before discrete top-k readouts change

Softmax shift-invariance makes routing depend only on centered weights. They prove a conditioning ratio lower-bounds router similarity:

sim(W_R) ≥ 1 − n/(n−1)·ε²  (Prop. 3)

while per-token routing entropy H(p(x)) reads the full distribution and reveals collapse before MaxVio / capacity-overflow move.

A margin-feedback argument shows larger LR scales reinforcement directly and smaller GBS raises per-step margin variance—both lowering entropy.

Strengths & Limitations

＋ Strengths

  • Tackles a costly production problem—silent pre-divergence—with monitors that need no kernel modification or activation recomputation; deployable as-is.
  • Bilinear decomposition reduces monitoring to head-dimensional cores, making spectral checks cheap enough for routine online use at frontier scale.
  • Clean separation of attention vs. router fault signatures enables attribution that loss/gradient curves cannot.
  • Intellectual honesty: limitations explicitly enumerate open directions and unresolved quantitative gaps.

－ Limitations

  • Narrow coverage: one BF16 biased-rounding fault and uniform-LR scaling; FP8, stochastic rounding, gradient clipping, and comms faults untested.
  • The ~8,000-step Δ₂-to-ΔW gap and detection-onset step lack a quantitative spiked-random-matrix prediction.
  • Derivations assume simplifications (dense-softmax gate relaxation; activation isotropy) that real Transformers violate.
  • Bilinear decomposition assumes explicit W_q/W_k MHA—needs re-derivation for MLA/GQA/MQA/DSA.
  • No public code or fully-specified training configs reported.

Open Questions

For further exploration
Q1

Can spiked random-matrix theory give a closed-form prediction for the detection-onset step and the ~8,000-step Δ₂-to-ΔW gap?

Q2

How do these monitors behave under FP8 training, stochastic rounding, and gradient-clipping interactions now common in frontier runs?

Q3

How should the Δ₂ QK-bilinear proxy be re-derived for MLA, GQA, MQA, DSA—do thresholds transfer or need per-variant calibration?

Q4

Can the principle extend to emerging modules—persistent memory, manifold-constrained residuals, learnable structured sparsity—with derived monitors?

Q5

Does combining attention and router monitors in a unified online dashboard improve automated fault localization in multi-fault scenarios?
