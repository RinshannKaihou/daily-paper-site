Key finding

Gradient descent drives a dynamical BBP spectral phase transition — an isolated eigenvalue detaches from the random bulk — mapping trainability onto a phase diagram of step size vs. weight variance.

Methodology

training = finite-rank signal vs. random spectral bulk
Setup
Wishart weight covariance

Model training as stochastic evolution of an initially Wishart-distributed covariance X = W Wᵀ, carrying a Marchenko–Pastur bulk at initialization. Each gradient step is a finite-rank perturbation.

▸
Mechanism
Dynamical BBP transition

The update rescales bulk variance by (1−ε)² per step while injecting a rank-1 signal of strength θ²(ε). An eigenvalue detaches when the resolvent pole condition 1 = θ² g(λ₊) is met.

▸
Solve & extend
Phase diagram + extensions

For a rank-1 teacher under full-batch GD the time-dependent X(t) is solved in closed form, yielding optimal ε_o, critical ε_c, and a dynamical critical time t_c. Gaussian equivalence (μ₁, μ₂) extends to tanh/ReLU & multilayer; load α = P/D and a fluctuation–dissipation relation cover SGD / finite data.

The trainability phase diagram

Hyperparameters live in one of three spectral phases. Only the ferromagnetic regime produces a stable, task-aligned isolated eigenvalue and the lowest loss.

Ferromagnetic — isolated mode aligns with teacher; lowest loss, stable convergence.
Disordered — signal stays submerged in the MP bulk; no representation forms.
Paramagnetic — unstable / oscillatory (ε > 2 divergent); net collapses to PCA.

Nonlinearities are absorbed via Wick / Stein's lemma and Gaussian equivalence (per-index rescaling μ₁, μ₂) — they merely rescale bulk variance and signal strength, preserving the phase-boundary structure. For SGD and finite data, finite-sample fluctuations controlled by the load α = P/D deform the bulk, and a stationary-state fluctuation–dissipation relation links late-time spectra to the Hessian and gradient-noise Fisher matrix. Linear models always converge for ε < 1 — no spurious minima.

Strengths · Limitations

critical assessment
Strengths
  • +Clean, analytically tractable core (linear teacher–student) with closed-form phase boundaries, critical time, and order parameters.
  • +Teacher-agnostic observables (self-overlap, top-k subspace overlap) make the framework applicable to real models without ground-truth weights.
  • +Theory generalizes coherently across nonlinearity, depth, SGD, and finite data, with the load α giving principled finite-sample control.
  • +Empirical UTKFace phase diagram + feature/PCA-overlap analysis give compelling qualitative support.
  • +Honest about limits — clearly delineates exact vs. phenomenological results; strong RMT / statistical-physics pedigree.
Limitations & Risks
  • !Nonlinear / multilayer / SGD extensions rest on Gaussian-equivalence & simplifying noise assumptions that may break for structured data or extreme over-parameterization.
  • !Deeper architectures analyzed only at the first layer; full multi-layer spectral feedback is open.
  • !No treatment of Adam / momentum — where anisotropic rescaling may change BBP universality.
  • !No public code/data in this version; reproducing empirical phase diagrams needs appendix settings & seeds.
  • !"Optimal" step size is a spectral-bulk criterion, not a generalization-optimal one — easy to misread.

Open Questions

for further exploration
Q1

How does the BBP threshold and ferromagnetic stability shift under Adam / momentum, where updates are anisotropically & dynamically rescaled?

Q2

Can the dynamical BBP transition be unified with static edge-of-stability / edge-of-chaos criticality — connecting initialization- and training-induced thresholds?

Q3

Does the fluctuation–dissipation / heavy-tail prediction hold for natural images and very deep architectures beyond the first layer?

Q4

Can the teacher-agnostic self-overlap serve as an online hyperparameter-selection or early-stopping signal in production training?

Q5

What is the effect of weight decay, batchnorm, and architectural symmetries on the time-dependent boundaries ε_c(t)?

Q6

Is the paramagnetic = PCA collapse a generic failure mode, or specific to the ReLU / UTKFace setup studied here?
