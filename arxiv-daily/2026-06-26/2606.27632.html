Methodology

progressive three-stage pipeline · z ~ p(z|c)
Stage 1 · Knowledge
Knowledge-Enhanced Continued Pretraining

Next-token prediction on a curated safety corpus — KB triples, moderation policies, risk taxonomies, violation patterns, long-tail adversarial expressions — plus a small general-domain mix to preserve language competence.

→ Yuvion-CP ▸
Stage 2 · Policy
Policy-Grounded Multi-Task Safety Post-Training

Risk-Aware SFT mixes natural + adversarially-disguised examples so judgment relies on latent intent, not lexical cues. GRPO rewards decision correctness, policy consistency, attribution, and robustness under attack.

→ Yuvion-SFT · Yuvion-RL ▸
Stage 3 · Agentic
Safety-Aware Agentic Reinforcement Learning

Trajectory-level optimization: tool-integrated reasoning (format + correctness rewards) and search-augmented reasoning (execution + LLM-judge rewards), both under GRPO.

→ Yuvion-Agent

The data system spans five roles — general (regularization), safety-domain (core capability), adversarial (evasion-pattern modeling), agentic (multi-step trajectories), synthetic/expert (long-tail & complex) — embedded in a closed loop of knowledge injection · red-team self-play · agentic reinforcement · deployment feedback. All three stages unify under autoregressive conditional generation.

Strengths · Limitations

critical assessment
Strengths
  • +End-to-end paradigm jointly targets adversarial robustness, policy grounding, and agentic capability — not just classification.
  • +Deployment-oriented evaluation hierarchy (YLRE) stresses adversarial & operational dimensions public benchmarks miss.
  • +Clear cross-scale advantage — smaller safety-trained models beat larger generalists on safety & deployment.
  • +Low false-positive rates — safety gains not bought via over-refusal.
  • +Closed-loop red-team self-play + deployment feedback, realistic for production.
Limitations & Risks
  • !Robustness only proven against evasion patterns in/derived from training data; novel OOD attacks remain a risk.
  • !Evaluation is Chinese- & English-centric; multilingual / cross-cultural generalization unverified.
  • !In-house & self-constructed adversarial benchmarks are not public → self-evaluation bias, limited external audit.
  • !Full Yuvion weights only partially / planned open-source; red-teaming pipeline proprietary.
  • !Modest general-capability gap vs base model on some benchmarks; the “93 benchmarks” headline is inflated by sub-dataset counting.

Open Questions

for further exploration
Q1

How does Yuvion generalize to held-out third-party attack suites (HarmBench, AdvBench, multi-turn jailbreaks) not from its own red-team?

Q2

What is the marginal contribution of each stage (CP · SFT · GRPO · agentic RL) as base scale varies (8B vs 32B vs larger)?

Q3

Can self-constructed & in-house benchmarks be released or third-party-audited to mitigate self-evaluation bias?

Q4

How do GPT-5.4 / Qwen3-Max compare when prompted in their explicit safety/guard modes rather than default chat?

Q5

Does agentic-RL safety training transfer to novel workflows & tools unseen during training?

Q6

How brittle is robustness to non-lexical strategies — cross-modal or indirect prompt injection in retrieved docs?
