Benchmarking on Tasks That Matter: Dataset Selection for Preserving Model Rankings

Geometry-based selection preserves full-benchmark rankings far better than random — when the representation is informative.

Why This Matters

Cut evaluation cost to a handful of datasets while preserving model rankings — directly defusing the "benchmark lottery" and selective-reporting bias.

Critical Edge

The theory leans on restrictive assumptions — Gaussian-process smoothness and a bi-Lipschitz link between representation distance and rank geometry — so the guarantees are more conceptual than tight on real benchmarks.

01 Methodology 方法论 · 排名保持的形式化

Subset selection is framed as explicit rank preservation: choose S ⊂ D, |S| = k ≪ |D|, to minimize the discrepancy Δ(R_S, R_D) between the subset-induced ranking and the full-benchmark ranking.

Crucially, selection uses only leakage-free a priori representations — TSFRESH / Catch22 / MiniRocket embeddings for time series, interaction-matrix statistics for RecSys, and BERT task-description embeddings for NLP — never the evaluation outcomes themselves.

The protocol runs T = 200 randomized trials (dataset/model-pool subsampling at fraction α = 0.8), applies each strategy across k = 2..20, and aggregates ranking-discrepancy curves with AUC summarization and non-parametric bootstrap 95% CIs. Significance is tested with a one-sided paired Wilcoxon signed-rank test plus Holm correction.

03 Strengths & Limitations 优势与局限

Strengths

what holds up
  • Unified, reusable protocol with rigorous statistics (bootstrap CIs, Wilcoxon + Holm) instead of ad-hoc single runs.
  • Genuine theory: explicit bounds linking farthest-first coverage to MSE and ranking-error decay.
  • Honest negative RecSys result — no overclaiming of universal applicability.
  • Oracle-vs-Broken ablation cleanly isolates representation quality as the causal driver of gains.
  • Decision-relevant output (subset-size guidance) plus full public code release.
  • Three diverse domains (TSC, RecSys, NLP) broaden the empirical picture.

Limitations

where it frays
  • Guarantees rely on strong assumptions (GP smoothness, bi-Lipschitz kernel) that real representations may violate.
  • A priori representations only — no adaptive selection that exploits partial evaluation results.
  • Marginal practical value in RecSys, where gains over random are negligible.
  • NLP treatment is supplementary, resting on a single public MTEB score matrix.
  • Optimizes global/average rank — no per-model stability, top-1, or adversarial-composition analysis.
  • Subset size capped at k ≤ 20; A-/D-optimality underperform despite theoretical appeal.
04 Open Questions 值得进一步探索
Q1 · ADAPTIVE

Can online selection using partial evaluation outcomes beat prior-only representations without leaking on held-out data?

Q2 · GRANULARITY

Do conclusions extend to top-k or per-model ranking stability, beyond aggregate global correlation?

Q3 · REPRESENTATION

What minimum representation properties (beyond bi-Lipschitz) are empirically necessary to beat random — and can they be cheaply pre-estimated?

Q4 · LEARNED FEATURES

Would foundation-model-derived embeddings recover selection gains in domains like RecSys where hand-crafted features failed?

Q5 · ROBUSTNESS

How stable is rank preservation under non-uniform sampling fractions α and distribution-shifted or adversarial dataset pools?

Q6 · DEPLOYMENT

Can the protocol become a standard CI check that flags when a benchmark's rankings are no longer reliable?
