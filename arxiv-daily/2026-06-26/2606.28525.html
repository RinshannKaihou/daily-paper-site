A Gravitational Interpretation of Fine-Tuning Reversion

Samuele Poppi & Nils Lukas · MBZUAI · 2026-06-26
Key Finding

Benign fine-tuning is gravitationally pulled back along a history-defined direction vrev — and blocking it cuts harmfulness with near-zero task cost.

安全对齐只是对预训练形成的主导行为流形的浅层位移；后续良性微调会沿一条历史定义的回退方向（v_rev）被"拉回"，从而部分恢复被抑制的行为。

Why This Matters

Reframes safety erosion, unlearning reversal, and subliminal transfer as one history-dependent reversion toward a dominant helpful manifold — a trajectory property, not a static safety axis.

Critical Edge

The "manifold" MH is never directly observed — helpful-only checkpoints are only proxy witnesses, so all structural claims remain inference-mediated, not measured.

Below the Fold

01

Methodology

Built on activation-space geometry, not weight-space. The representational shift of a downstream checkpoint is measured as Δ_T = m(P,ℓ) − m_θS(P,ℓ) over a probe family P (16 unlabeled AdvBench harmful prompts). The reversion direction is a difference-of-checkpoints:

vrev = mθ_H(P,ℓ*) − mθ_S(P,ℓ*)

where θH is a short helpful-only fine-tune (50 steps) and ℓ* is the late summary layer at the peak of refusal-direction magnitude (layer 31 for 8B Llama). Alignment is read off as cos(Δ_T, v_rev). Rests on three assumptions: early large-scale training carves a dominant helpful region while later alignment is a shallow displacement (A1); the benign gradient decomposes ∇L = g_task + g_rev (A2); and intervening along vrev modulates outcomes (A3).

04

Strengths

  • Unifying lens. Brings alignment erosion, unlearning reversal, and subliminal transfer under one geometric, history-dependent mechanism.
  • Path vs. outcome separation. Orthogonal weights yet aligned activations is a compelling, well-argued dissociation.
  • Strong hygiene. Isotropic null, witness-family robustness, cross-judge + cross-model replication, random-direction control, disjoint probe/eval sets.
  • Honest scoping. Rare and valuable caveat discipline — lowers over-claiming risk.
  • Causal clincher. block-vrev turning cos negative while keeping task quality is the single most convincing piece of mechanistic evidence.
05

Limitations

  • Latent manifold. MH is never directly observed — helpful-only checkpoints are only operational witnesses, so the interpretation stays inferential.
  • Modest, temporal sample. Behavioral coupling uses a small, temporally structured checkpoint set (Spearman r, not a controlled benchmark).
  • Short horizon. Main intervention evidence is restricted to T ≤ 100; longer-horizon dynamics out of scope.
  • Not optimal. vrev shown to be causally relevant — not the best or only intervention direction.
  • Weak cross-family match. push-vrev is single-seed at 8B; Qwen 3B runs needed non-matched intervention scale.
06

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does reversion strength scale quantitatively with the training-budget asymmetry B_P ≫ B_H ≫ B_S ≫ B_T, and can the informal ordering become a predictive law?
  2. Is vrev related to — or distinct from — the Arditi et al. refusal direction and the Soligo et al. convergent linear representation of emergent misalignment, measured in the same layer?
  3. Does the block-vrev barrier remain effective at longer horizons (T ≫ 100) and against more adversarial benign subsets (the "outlier benign samples" of He/Guan et al.)?
  4. Can the manifold MH be recovered more directly via the geometry of many helpful witnesses, rather than only inferred from consistency statistics?
  5. Does the gravitational framing quantitatively predict unlearning-reversal and subliminal-transfer beyond safety — a cross-domain test of the hypothesis?
