> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了 Muon 优化器在训练 MLP 高矩阵参数时会导致神经元死亡（行范数非均匀形成正反馈），并提出 Aurora——一种在保持极因子几何的同时强制更新行均匀的迭代谱优化器，显著缓解神经元死亡并在 340M/1.1B 规模及 modded-nanoGPT 上取得 SOTA。

> **TL;DR:** The paper shows Muon can cause neuron death via non-uniform row norms on tall MLP matrices, and proposes Aurora, an iterative spectral optimizer that enforces uniform row updates while preserving polar-factor geometry, achieving SOTA among spectral optimizers and scaling gains with MLP width.

## 核心贡献
- 从机制上揭示并验证了 Muon 在 MLP 的 up/gate 高矩阵参数上会导致行范数（杠杆分数）严重非均匀，形成"小更新→低杠杆→更小更新"的自强化反馈，最终使大量神经元死亡；在 Rnj-1（8.3B）和 Laguna-XS.2（33.4B MoE）等开源模型中也观测到该现象。
- 证明精确极因子与均匀行范数同时是可取且相互竞争的，并据此提出 U-NorMuon（仅对高矩阵做无状态行归一化），作为通向 Aurora 的中间步骤。
- 提出 Aurora：通过交替进行行归一化与牛顿–舒尔茨极因子计算的迭代方案，求解"半正交 + 等行范数"双约束问题，并在理论（Theorem 4.1 局部线性收敛）和实验上验证其精度接近精确 Riemannian 参考解。
- 在 340M/1.1B 预训练及 modded-nanoGPT 优化赛道上超越 Muon、NorMuon、U-NorMuon；1.1B 模型在 MMLU 上比 Muon 高 9.1 分，并刷新优化赛道 SOTA。
- 发现 Aurora 相对 Muon 的增益随 MLP 扩展因子单调增大，暗示其尤其适用于训练极宽的 MLP 层。

## Key Contributions
- Mechanistically diagnoses and verifies neuron death under Muon on tall MLP projection matrices (up/gate), showing a self-reinforcing loop of low leverage scores; confirms the pathology in open-weight models (Rnj-1 8.3B, Laguna-XS.2 MoE), where ~28% of dense-layer neurons are dead.
- Proves (Proposition 3.1) that precise polar-factor geometry and uniform row norms are concurrently desirable yet in tension for tall matrices, motivating U-NorMuon (stateless row normalization restricted to tall matrices).
- Derives Aurora as an alternating-projection iterative solver of the joint semi-orthogonality + equal-row-norm problem, with a local linear-convergence guarantee (Theorem 4.1), plus Riemannian-Aurora as a high-cost reference solver.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results among spectral optimizers at 340M/1.1B scales and on the modded-nanoGPT optimizer track (step 3175 vs NorMuon baseline), with a 9.1-point MMLU gain over Muon at 1.1B.
- Shows Aurora's advantage over Muon scales monotonically with MLP expansion factor, suggesting benefits for very wide MLP layers.

## Methodology
Aurora targets the tall up/gate projection matrices in SwiGLU MLPs. The authors first formulate Muon's update as the solution to a spectral-norm trust-region problem, then augment it with an equal-row-norm constraint (Eq. 1–2). By Proposition 4.1, under uniform row norms the spectral-norm relaxation collapses to a left semi-orthogonality (Stiefel) constraint, so the problem becomes steepest descent on the intersection of the Stiefel manifold and the equal-row-norm manifold (the "equal-row-leverage Stiefel manifold"). The practical Aurora algorithm (Alg. 3) iteratively alternates: (i) damped row normalization using a momentum-style scalar β, and (ii) a Newton–Schulz polar-factor approximation, for K inner steps. This alternating projection is proven locally linearly convergent. A Riemannian reference solver (Alg. 2) jointly projects onto the tangent space but is O(m²) and used only for validation. Aurora is roughly K× costlier than Muon but overlaps well with gradient communication in distributed settings.

## Results & Highlights
- 340M/1.1B pretraining (100B NemotronCC v2 tokens, 4096 ctx): Aurora achieves the best average across ARC-C/E, HellaSwag, LAMBADA, MMLU, OBQA, PIQA, WinoGrande at both scales; at 1.1B MMLU is 38.8 vs Muon's 29.7 (+9.1).
- Neuron death analysis: Muon yields a bimodal leverage-score distribution with a dead mode near zero; U-NorMuon/Aurora produce tightly clustered, near-uniform leverage (n/m) and eliminate dead neurons. The leverage-bound (Prop. 3.3) is empirically tight.
- Open-weight verification: Rnj-1 shows large dead-neuron cohorts in early layers (concentrated <layer 10), 99.8% shared across four diverse datasets; Laguna-XS.2 has ~28% dead neurons in its dense layer-0, but its fine-grained (wide) MoE experts are healthy — consistent with theory.
- modded-nanoGPT Track 3: Aurora + Contra-Muon reaches the 3.28 target at step 3175 (25 steps fewer than NorMuon baseline), a new SOTA at time of writing.
- Width scaling: Aurora's loss gap over Muon grows monotonically with MLP expansion factor (4→larger).
- Throughput: single-H100 overhead is meaningful (~16–25% at K=2/3), but on 8-GPU distributed the wall-clock penalty is <3% due to overlap.

## Authors & Credibility
All authors are at Tilde Research, an applied research lab with prior optimizer work (the authors are behind the NorMuon lineage and related spectral-optimizer contributions). The paper pairs careful theory (proven propositions, a convergence theorem, full appendix proofs) with large-scale empirical verification (340M/1.1B pretraining) and external validation on real open-weight frontier-class models (Rnj-1 8.3B, Laguna-XS.2 33.4B MoE). Code is released at github.com/tilde-research/aurora-release and a companion blog exists. The combination of mechanistic diagnosis + open-model forensics lends the claims unusual external credibility for an optimizer paper.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** High. The neuron-death diagnosis under Muon via leverage scores, and the framing of polar precision vs row-uniformity as a dual constraint solved by alternating projections, is fresh and well-motivated. Aurora sits clearly beyond simple NorMuon/MuonEq variants.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Multiple propositions with complete proofs (Appendix D), a local linear-convergence theorem with a clean intersection/manifold argument, and ablations isolating each design axis (stateless vs stateful, polar vs spectral scaling, tall-only vs all).
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Algorithms are fully specified (Alg. 1–3), hyperparameters (K, β) reported, code + blog released. Throughput and convergence diagnostics provided. Scales (340M/1.1B) are within reach of well-resourced academic/industry labs.
- **Significance:** Potentially high. Neuron death in Muon-trained MLPs is a concrete, previously underappreciated pathology with implications for parameter efficiency and wide-MLP training; the scaling-with-width result is especially provocative.
- **Red flags / caveats:** (1) Scales (340M/1.1B, 100B tokens) are modest relative to frontier pretraining; the "scales with MLP width" claim rests on a 340M base with varied (different param-count) models, not a controlled width sweep at fixed compute. (2) Open-weight neuron-death forensics are post-hoc (no training dynamics), and thresholds (1% leverage, 5% activation) are heuristic, acknowledged as preliminary. (3) The Riemannian reference solver and Aurora are validated as "close" only on step-100 momentum buffers. (4) Distributed-overlap throughput claims depend on a specific setup.

## Strengths
- Clean mechanistic story linking row leverage scores to neuron death, validated both internally and on external open-weight models.
- Principled derivation: Aurora is not an ad-hoc fix but a solution to a well-posed dual-constraint optimization with convergence theory.
- Strong, consistent empirical wins across two model scales, eight benchmarks, and the modded-nanoGPT speedrun.
- Thorough ablation isolating the contribution of tall-only normalization, statelessness, and polar scaling.
- Practical: low distributed wall-clock overhead, released code, and a clear architectural-optimizer codesign message.

## Limitations
- Pretraining scales (≤1.1B, 100B tokens) are well below frontier; generalization to 100B+ parameter runs is unproven.
- The MLP-width scaling result conflates width with parameter count (models differ in size), weakening the controlled claim.
- Open-weight neuron-death detection is post-hoc and threshold-sensitive; authors themselves label it "preliminary verification."
- Aurora costs K× the Muon polar-computation; benefits depend on effective overlap with communication, which is hardware/setup-specific.
- Riemannian-Aurora vs Aurora agreement is shown only on a single early-step momentum buffer, leaving later-training accuracy less characterized.

## Relevance
与训练稳定性/优化直接相关：本文从优化器层面揭示了 Muon 谱正交化导致 MLP 神经元死亡的具体机制，并提供了一个低开销、有理论保证的修正方案（Aurora），对大规模预训练的参数利用率和宽 MLP 训练具有实践指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Does Aurora's neuron-death mitigation and loss advantage persist at frontier scale (≥10B–100B params, trillion-token training), or does it vanish as other instabilities dominate?
- Could an adaptive schedule switch from Aurora to plain Muon once the momentum buffer converges to uniform leverage (as the authors hypothesize), saving compute?
- Is the MLP-width scaling robust under a controlled sweep (fixed total compute / param count, varying only expansion factor)?
- Does Aurora change the learned representations or downstream task mix beyond loss/benchmark gains (e.g., feature diversity, interpretability)?
- How does Aurora interact with other spectral-optimizer innovations (Contra-Muon, soft singular-value maps, Riemannian Muon) beyond the single combination tested?
