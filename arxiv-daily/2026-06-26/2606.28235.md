> **一句话总结：** 通过对超 93 万条 agent 提交的 PR 进行多层模型方差分解，本文证明 AI 原生软件的集成摩擦是"仓库层"的涌现属性（agent 贡献的浓度约为人类的两倍），因此应以仓库/生态为单位度量与治理，而非逐个评估 agent。

> **TL;DR:** Using multilevel variance decomposition on 930k+ agent-authored pull requests, this paper shows integration friction is a non-reducible property of the repository (an emergent, ecosystem-level risk) concentrated ~2x more in agent-authored than human-authored PRs — so AI-native software should be measured and governed at the repository level, not one agent at a time.

## 核心贡献
- 将从业者报告的"代码来得太快、理解跟不上"问题重新定义为一个**度量问题**：风险属于 agent、贡献者，还是仓库/生态？答案是后者。
- 提出一个**与指标无关的统计非还原性（non-reducibility）检验**：用领域已熟悉的多层模型计算组内相关系数（ICC = τ²/(τ²+σ²)），看控制了贡献、作者、规模、agent 后仓库层方差还剩多少——剩余部分即"涌现"的统计特征。
- 在**同一仓库内匹配人类基线**，证明该信号是 agent 创作软件特有的（ICC 0.30 vs 0.16，近两倍），而非高活跃仓库的通用属性。
- 把结论转化为**治理议程**：评估 agent 应在其目标仓库内进行，管控"变更节奏"而非"人头数"，把评审路由到摩擦集中处，并跟踪少量仓库级指标；附带可复现的复制包（Zenodo）。

## Key Contributions
- Reframes a practitioner-reported problem (code arriving faster than teams can absorb) as a *measurement* question about the *level* at which AI-native software risk resides — the ecosystem, not the individual agent.
- Introduces a metric-independent **statistical non-reducibility test** based on the intraclass correlation coefficient (ICC) from multilevel models, tracking how much repository-level variance survives staged covariate controls (M0→M3).
- Validates specificity with a **matched within-repository human baseline**, showing agent-authored PRs concentrate friction ~2× (ICC 0.30 vs 0.16), ruling out generic high-activity-repository effects.
- Derives a concrete **governance agenda** (assess agents in-repo, govern change tempo not headcount, route review to friction hotspots, track a small repository dashboard) backed by a reproducible replication package.

## Methodology
An **observational measurement study** (variance partition, not a treatment effect) over **930,292 agent-authored PRs across 116,211 repositories** (33,596 PRs / 2,807 repos in the curated ≥100-star subset), drawn from the AIDev dataset, AgenticFlict, and GitHub API fetches. Five agents are covered: OpenAI Codex, Devin, GitHub Copilot, Cursor, and Claude Code. Integration friction is operationalized through **seven constructs spanning four facets** — Timing (deliberation/resolution latency), Effort (review rounds, comment volume), Contention (changes requested, conflict incidence), and Outcome (rejection). A multilevel model with a per-repository random intercept (u_j) separates between-repository variance (τ²) from within-repository variance (σ²); the ICC is the operational measure of non-reducibility. Four nested models add controls progressively: M0 (null), M1 (PR-level: size, task type, timing, agent indicator), M2 (repository covariates: stars, forks, language, activity), M3 (codebase size + age from the GitHub API, available for 99%). For the specificity test (RQ3), human-authored PRs are fetched for the same 404 repositories and re-processed identically under a common covariate set, capped at 200 PRs/repo. Robustness is checked across four activity cutoffs (≥1, 5, 10, 20 PRs), a leave-one-repository-out jackknife, a placebo test on diff size, and sensitivity fits (linear-probability, log(1+x) Gaussian). Uncertainty is quantified via cluster bootstrap (continuous) and profile-likelihood intervals (binary).

## Results & Highlights
- **All four hypotheses hold.** H1: agents form an interacting ecosystem — 39.7% of agent PRs draw cross-account engagement and 66.5% of repositories contain an engaged PR; multi-agent repos are 8.3% overall but 31.2% among the most active.
- **H2 (locus):** friction tracks the repository, not agent count. Merge-conflict rate is flat across multi- vs single-agent repos (30.8% vs 31.2%), and the repository-level ICC is actually *lower* where several agents coexist (0.21 vs 0.47), contradicting an agent-interference account.
- **H3 (non-reducibility):** about half of the variation in friction survives full adjustment. Headline resolution-latency ICC drops only from 0.50 (null) to 0.40 (M2) and 0.39 (M3); codebase size and age remove ≤0.7% of the variance on the headline construct. The signal holds across all seven constructs (ICC 0.18–0.61) and four activity cutoffs (0.40–0.52).
- **H4 (specificity, the decisive test):** on the same repositories, agent-authored contributions concentrate repository-level friction roughly **twofold** over human ones — resolution latency ICC 0.30 vs 0.16 (+0.14 [0.08, 0.19]); deliberation latency +0.22. The gap survives controls for task shape, process maturity, merge path, and a placebo on diff size.
- **Governance upshot:** a benchmark score earned in isolation does not predict in-repo behavior; serialize merges (merge queues) rather than capping agent count; route mandatory review to high-friction paths; monitor base-branch churn, conflict-replay rate, engagement, and the resolution-latency ICC trend.

## Authors & Credibility
Sole author **Daniel Russo**, Department of Computer Science, Aalborg University, Copenhagen — a software-engineering researcher who has previously published on related themes. The paper cites a companion theory paper (arXiv:2604.19827) and draws on established empirical-SE methods (multilevel models, ABC framework, Wohlin et al. experimentation conventions). Credibility is bolstered by a **fully reproducible pipeline**: fixed seeds, dataset SHA-256 hashes, an audit script re-deriving every figure, and a Zenodo-archived package (doi:10.5281/zenodo.20759752); repository identifiers are released only as anonymized deciles.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** High and well-scoped. The contribution is explicitly *not* the multilevel model (standard in SE) nor the complex-adaptive-systems analogy, but binding a *metric-independent non-reducibility test* to integration friction on agent-authored data and proving specificity via a matched human baseline. The honest framing — necessary signature of emergence, not causal proof — is a strength.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Pre-registered-style hypotheses each stated to be falsifiable; seven constructs across four facets with convergent evidence; staged controls; cluster bootstrap and profile-likelihood intervals; jackknife, multiple activity cutoffs, placebo, and sensitivity fits all reported.
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent — public datasets, Zenodo package, fixed seeds, decile-only release to protect raw identifiers.
- **Significance:** High for agent reliability and AI-native engineering governance; it reframes the prevailing one-agent-at-a-time benchmarking culture (e.g., SWE-bench) as structurally insufficient.
- **Red flags / caveats:** Observational design means the surviving variance is a *necessary* but not *causal* signature; unmeasured stable repository attributes (governance, application domain) remain uncontrolled. Three of five agents act through operator accounts, so agent-to-agent interaction is only partly observable. AgenticFlict's replay drops unprocessable PRs (survivorship bias toward conflict). Scope is limited to open-source and five agents; 33 repositories were inaccessible (renamed/private).

## Strengths
- Metric-independent, threshold-free definition of emergence that sidesteps the "emergence is a metric artifact" critique.
- Matched within-repository human baseline cleanly isolates the agent-specific effect from generic repository heterogeneity.
- Convergent evidence across seven constructs and four activity cutoffs makes a single-measurement artifact unlikely.
- Translates a statistical result into actionable, telemetry-derivable governance practices.
- Honest about limitations: explicitly labels the finding a necessary (not sufficient) signature of emergence.

## Limitations
- Observational, not causal — cannot fully rule out unmeasured stable repository confounders; stronger causal measures (effective information, dynamical independence) are deferred to future work.
- Agent-to-agent interaction is only partly observable because three agents operate through human GitHub accounts.
- AgenticFlict replay introduces survivorship bias, and conflict incidence is agent-only (cannot be reconstructed for historical human PRs).
- Limited to open-source repositories and five agents (a documented external-validity scope limit).
- Multi-agent metric reflects today's multi-tool market and would narrow under single-orchestrator consolidation (though the repository-level result is claimed to be robust to it).

## Relevance
直接关联"agent 可靠性与生态级风险"：它指出当下逐个 agent、孤立基准评测（如 SWE-bench）无法解释仓库层累积的集成故障，为以仓库为单位的度量和治理（合并队列、摩擦路由、仓库级指标看板）提供了统计证据，对构建可信多 agent 软件工程系统具有直接的指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Would stronger causal emergence measures (Hoel et al. effective information; Barnett & Seth dynamical independence) confirm the non-reducibility signal under controlled or longitudinal intervention?
- How do the two unmeasured symptoms — erosion of shared understanding and unrecorded macro-intent — correlate with the integration-friction ICC?
- Does the ~2× agent-vs-human concentration replicate in closed-source/enterprise repositories and with newer or consolidated orchestrators (e.g., a single Claude Code with sub-agents)?
- Can a repository-level friction dashboard predict downstream harm (defects, reverts, churn) better than per-agent capability metrics?
- What governance and application-domain confounders, if measured, could absorb part of the surviving repository variance?
