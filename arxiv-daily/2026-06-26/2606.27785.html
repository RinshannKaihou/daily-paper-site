Output-Space Allocation Costs for Calibration-Guided LLM Compression: An Empirical Study

Qiong Tang · Xiangkun Hu · Xiangyang Liu · Yiran Chen · Yunfan Shao et al.
⚠ Provenance: Disclosed as produced by FARS (Fully Automated Research System) — autonomous ideation, experiments & writing; human authors contributed review and reference verification only.
Key finding

An output-space allocation cost lifts ROCKET's accuracy +0.8 pp — but the same change inflates perplexity, exposing a sharp accuracy–perplexity tradeoff.

一句话总结：将 ROCKET 中 MCKP 的分配代价从权重空间改为输出空间后，任务准确率略有提升，但困惑度反而上升，揭示出一种准确率–困惑度权衡，且效应被两种误差指标间 >0.99 的高相关性所限制。

Why this matters

Aligning the allocation cost with the output target doesn't guarantee uniform gains — compression must be judged on both task accuracy and perplexity, never one metric alone.

Critical edge caveat
A tradeoff, not a win.

The +0.8pp gain is bought with a 16% perplexity regression — a use-case-dependent benefit — and at milder compression (Llama-3.2-1B/20%) the effect essentially vanishes (−0.2pp).

01 Methodology

A single, principled swap inside an existing method — move the knapsack's cost from weight space into output space. Zero added runtime.

ROCKET-ActCost doesn't touch how each layer is compressed — it changes how the global bit budget is spent. ROCKET first factorizes every linear layer via sparse-dictionary decomposition in a whitened activation space, then solves a multi-choice knapsack (MCKP) to pick each layer's best (rank k, sparsity s) configuration.

The catch: ROCKET's knapsack cost was measured in weight space (relative Frobenius error on W), while each layer is actually compressed to minimize output error. ActCost replaces that cost with the whitened output-space error — so the global objective finally matches the per-layer one.

Because 96.6% of (layer, ratio) pairs pick a different optimal configuration under the new metric, allocations genuinely shift. Crucially, every matrix needed already exists from ROCKET's profiling pass — no extra calibration, no runtime cost; the MCKP solver runs unchanged.

Setup: Qwen3-8B @ 50% (primary, 2 seeds) & Llama-3.2-1B @ 20%; 256×1024-token RefinedWeb sequences for calibration; 8 zero-shot benchmarks via lm-eval-harness + WikiText-2 PPL.

The cost swap
ROCKET‖W − Ŵ‖F / ‖W‖F
   ↓ replaceweight space → output space
ActCost‖WL − ŴL‖F / ‖WL‖F
where L is the upper Cholesky factor of the activation Gram matrix X⊤X, so output reconstruction error equals whitened Frobenius error.

02 Results & Highlights

Aggressive compression reveals the tradeoff; mild compression erases it. Allocation barely moves — because the two error metrics are near-perfectly correlated.

03 Critical Assessment

Honest, well-isolated, reproducible — but narrow in scope, and the headline is a tradeoff rather than a clear win.

▲ Strengths

  • Transparent reporting — discloses FARS provenance and the accuracy–perplexity tradeoff rather than claiming a one-sided win.
  • Clean ablation: isolates the cost-function change while holding the profiling pipeline and MCKP solver fixed.
  • Strong mechanistic analysis (Spearman correlation, allocation-divergence counts) that explains the modest effect size.
  • Zero runtime overhead and trivial adoptability for existing ROCKET users.
  • Public code + standard, widely-used evaluation harness (lm-eval-harness).

◆ Limitations

  • Small scope: only 2 models and two compression ratios — no intermediate (30–40%) coverage.
  • The main "improvement" ships with a perplexity regression, so the net benefit is use-case dependent.
  • FARS / machine-generated origin raises questions about depth of related-work positioning.
  • Findings are specific to ROCKET's MCKP; generalization to CoSpaDi, SVD-LLM, ASVD is untested.
  • Some per-benchmark gains (PIQA +0.08pp, RACE +0.05pp) sit within typical seed variance.

04 Questions for Further Exploration

Open threads that would sharpen or generalize the tradeoff finding.

Does the accuracy–perplexity tradeoff persist, weaken, or invert at intermediate ratios (30–40%) or on larger models (70B+)?

Could a hybrid cost — weighted combination, or output-space cost with a perplexity regularizer — retain the accuracy gains without the PPL regression?

Does the >0.99 correlation generalize to other activation-aware factorization methods, and can whitening variants decorrelate the two metrics?

How does ActCost behave when composed with downstream quantization or pruning in a multi-stage compression pipeline?

Would calibrating on task-specific data (rather than RefinedWeb) shift the accuracy–perplexity tradeoff?

If >0.99 correlation largely explains the small effect, where would breaking that correlation yield the most leverage?
