The four-stage pipeline

How it actually works

PAT is built around inference-scaling: instead of one model call (limited context, precision loss) or naive Pass@k (recall up, precision down), it orchestrates specialised agents that each verify a slice of the paper with the full manuscript as context.

The system deliberately separates concerns. Compute is adaptively budgeted — proofs get a "High thinking" track, prose gets less — so capacity flows to where errors hide. After per-segment verification, a synthesis agent de-duplicates critiques, ranks severity, and uses Google Search grounding to flag hallucinated citations or theorems before the final report. Evaluation used the SPOT benchmark filtered to 26 Math/CS papers with 29 equation/proof errors, graded by a logic-aware LLM grader (validated by human audit) that recognises mathematically equivalent error descriptions.

Pipeline · 4 stages
01
Document SegmentationA "segmenter" agent splits the manuscript into overlapping, non-contiguous logical units (intro, theory, experiments).
02
Adaptive BudgetingCompute routed per-segment into Light / Medium / High "thinking" tracks by complexity and density.
03
Deep ReviewSpecialised Gemini Deep Think agents verify each segment with the full paper as context.
04
Global SynthesisDe-duplicates critiques, severity-checks, and search-grounds citations before assembling the report.

Strengths vs. limitations

Honest read
+Genuine strengths
  • 01
    Real deployment, not a benchmark demo — live at two premier conferences with official partnerships.
  • 02
    Architecture directly targets known failure modes: context exhaustion & Pass@k precision loss, via segmentation + adaptive budgeting + search-grounded synthesis.
  • 03
    The four-role autonomy taxonomy is a citable framework for the AI-in-peer-review policy debate.
  • 04
    Candid about failure modes: date hallucinations, PDF parsing errors, false "incorrect proof" claims, plus societal risks (reviewer deskilling, viewpoint homogenisation).
  • 05
    Strong institutional credibility & named academic endorsements.
!Hard limitations
  • 01
    Essentially unreproducible — depends on proprietary Gemini Deep Think & internal pipelines; no code, weights, or full prompts.
  • 02
    Quantitative eval is small (26 papers) with a non-standard grader, breaking direct comparability to prior SPOT numbers.
  • 03
    Pilot "results" are self-reported perceptions — no objective precision/recall on the 4,700 actual manuscripts.
  • 04
    PAT deliberately avoids subjective ranking — utility for holistic accept/reject decisions is untested.
  • 05
    Substantial conflict of interest: Google authors, Google tool, Google models, quantitatively thin independent evaluation.
  • 06
    No quantified false-positive burden — author time spent refuting hallucinated critiques is unmeasured.
