Key finding

AGI alignment is structurally unverifiable — no infallible, complete, and tractable verifier can ever exist.

01Methodologytheoretical synthesis · computability + descriptive complexity

AGI is formalized as a Turing machine Mₓ with Gödel index x, and an alignment property P as a set of partial computable functions. The argument proceeds by closing both computational vectors an external verifier could exploit. The infinite / open domain vector is blocked by Rice's theorem (Thm 2 — the index set is undecidable) and Gödel incompleteness (Thm 3 — any adequate framework F ⊇ PA leaves true-but-unprovable safety statements). The finite / hardware domain vector is blocked by Trakhtenbrot's theorem (Thm 4 — finite validity is co-RE-complete, not even recursively enumerable), so "escaping to bounded hardware" collapses. Descriptive complexity (Immerman–Vardi, Fagin) maps logic expressivity FO → LFP → SO → PFP onto P, NP, PSPACE, arguing recursive self-improvement demands PFP-level power. Theorem 6 assembles a hybrid barrier; Corollary 3 derives ¬(S ∧ C ∧ T) by contradiction.

Section 7 argues the two vectors are jointly exhaustive (Thm 5) — a verifier either reasons over an unbounded or a bounded domain, with no third strategy. The generalized-chess analogy (~10⁴⁴ finite states, PSPACE-complete) illustrates intractability within finitude.

03Strengths & limitationscritical assessment

Strengths

  • Coherent synthesis of four classical results (Rice, Gödel, Trakhtenbrot, descriptive complexity) into one argument about AGI alignment.
  • Trakhtenbrot applied to defeat the "finite hardware / halting machines" escape — a genuinely fresh, underappreciated insight.
  • Containment strategies reframed as deliberate sacrifices of expressivity, not temporary engineering limits.
  • The chess analogy communicates intractability-in-finitude vividly to a broad audience.

Limitations

  • Core impossibility results aren't new — novelty rests on synthesis + the Trakhtenbrot bridge.
  • Local-tractability clause depends on the unproven P ≠ PSPACE assumption.
  • Exhaustiveness argument (Thm 5) is informal; ignores hybrid, probabilistic, and interactive verification.
  • Approximate / probabilistic guarantees treated superficially despite real-world centrality.
  • Single-author preprint, heavy AI assistance disclosed, thin reference base — needs peer review.
04Open questionsfor further exploration
Q · 01

Can the exhaustiveness argument (Thm 5) be made rigorous for probabilistic, interactive, or oracle-assisted verification?

Q · 02

How do PAC-style / statistical safety certificates interact with the S–C–T trilemma — is there a relaxed trilemma for high-probability guarantees?

Q · 03

Do the conclusions hold for non-Turing-complete but still highly capable architectures (below full PFP expressivity)?

Q · 04

Could quantum or other non-standard computational models weaken the Trakhtenbrot / PSPACE barriers in any alignment-relevant sense?
