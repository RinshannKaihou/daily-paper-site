Why this matters

Cuts real HPC joules without sacrificing numerical correctness — a directly transferable recipe for energy-constrained accelerators and heterogeneous systems.

Critical edge ⚠

Only two explicit CPU solvers are tested — no GPUs, no implicit/iterative/ML workloads; gains are sequential and CPU-only, and multi-node scaling is future work, not measured.

Methodology

profile → perturb → demote → refactor

A staged, function-level mixed-precision workflow. Performance hotspots (high speedup potential) are first separated from numerical hotspots, then only the time-consuming, low-error routines are dropped to single precision — accuracy-critical paths (timestep selection, node-position updates) stay in double.

  1. Profile to find routines with the highest speedup headroom.
  2. Analyze error growth across mantissa widths via Verificarlo's VPREC (emulated precision) and MCA (Monte-Carlo perturbation) backends.
  3. Demote the few routines that are both slow and error-insensitive; refine progressively inner → outer.
  4. Relocate casts — for LULESH, refactor CalcFBHourglassForceForElems's signature to take single-precision inputs, moving every cast into the caller and eliminating indirect-copy overhead. (This single change, not naive demotion, drove the largest gain.)

Measured on LUMI CPU partition, g++ 12.2.0 -O2, 32 repetitions per config, energy via SLURM plugin → HPE Cray PM Counters through the BMC on dedicated nodes.

Error decreases nearly linearly with mantissa bits from 3 → 52 — a non-trivial relationship noted in prior work, and the empirical basis for choosing FP32 over FP64 on the dominant sin/exp math routines.

Strengths

what lands well
  • Quantitative, end-to-end evaluation covering accuracy, time, and energy simultaneously — not just speed.
  • Clear, transferable insight that cast/copy overhead can dominate and must be engineered around via signature refactoring.
  • Reproducible measurement on a Tier-0 system (LUMI) with rigorous 32-run statistical sampling and a consistent Cray PM-Counter energy toolchain.
  • Honest separation of which routines are precision-critical vs performance-critical — only the cheap-to-demote ones get demoted.
  • Builds directly on the authors' peer-reviewed Verificarlo/VPREC/MCA pipeline, giving internal consistency.

Limitations

where it strains
  • Only two benchmarks, both explicit solvers — implicit, iterative, or ML workloads are untested.
  • CPU-only; GPU/accelerator energy behavior is not covered at all.
  • Function-level granularity; finer instruction/variable-level tuning is explicitly out of scope.
  • No released code or artifacts linked in the paper; funding section contains placeholder text.
  • Short workshop format precludes deeper ablations or sensitivity analysis across problem sizes.
  • Parallel/multi-node scaling with reduced MPI message sizes is mentioned as future work, not demonstrated.

Open Questions

where the work points next
01Do the time/energy gains scale to GPUs or multi-node, where reduced precision also shrinks MPI message sizes?
02Would stochastic / probabilistic rounding (vs truncation) shift the precision thresholds or error plateaus observed?
03Can the routine-selection and cast-relocation steps be automated into a compiler pass on top of Verificarlo?
04Do the near-linear error-vs-mantissa relationships hold for larger problem sizes or different physical regimes?
05How does the methodology transfer to implicit solvers whose convergence depends on condition number?
