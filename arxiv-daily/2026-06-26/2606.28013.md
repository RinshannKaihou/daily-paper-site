> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一个2×2"信号-覆盖矩阵"(Lean elaborator 通过/失败 × 语义等价判定)，将自形式化的TC%增益分解为类型层修复、语义层流转与残留三类，揭示三种主流反馈方法收敛于相同的23/61类型层恢复率，并指出单一符号判分器会因elaborator强制改写而严重低估反馈方法的语义忠实度。

> **TL;DR:** The paper proposes a 2×2 signal-coverage matrix (Lean elaborator pass/fail × semantic-equivalence judge) that decomposes autoformalization TC% gains into type-stratum recovery, semantic turnover, and a durable residual, showing three elaborator-feedback methods converge on the same 23/61 type-stratum recovery rate and that single symbolic judges under-credit feedback methods by ≥25pp due to elaborator-forced structural rewrites.

## 核心贡献
- 提出**信号-覆盖矩阵诊断框架**：用 elaborator(pass/fail) × 语义判定(equiv/not) 将每个输出归入 TS/TO/SO/BF 四格，并用 4×4 的逐问题转移矩阵把 TC% 增益精确拆解为类型层恢复(64%)、语义层翻转与残留三部分。
- 发现**跨方法不变的预测规律**：三种算法迥异（顺序反馈、确定性IR精修、并行N=4采样）的 elaborator 反馈方法都以相同的 23/61 (37.7%, Wilson 95% CI [26.6%, 50.3%]) TO→TS 恢复率运行；用任一方法的逐层率做质量守恒预测，可将其它方法的 ΔTS 预测到 2/186 以内，ΔTC 在六个(model, dataset)单元上对 Vanilla 的 elaborator 失败率呈线性拟合(R²=0.96)。
- 建立**校准的双判分协议**：Claude Opus 4.7 与符号化 GTED(τ=0.5) 配对，在 ProofNet# 上达到 95.0% 池化精度；四类不一致性分类法将 30–56% 的 GTED 假阴性归因于 elaborator 强制的结构性改写。
- 提出 **SAF(分层自形式化)作为因果归因探针**：LLM 输出类型化 JSON IR，由约30行确定性翻译器生成 Lean，把 +37pp 的 Opus-vs-GTED 差距归因于 elaborator 驱动的表面几何而非 LLM 采样噪声。
- 发现**基准的金标准形式化错误底**：两类持久性 SO 残留可归为 ProofNet# 中的两个 gold-formalization bug(Ireland-Rosen 1^30 与 Dummit-Foote 7.2.2)，并附带修正建议。

## Key Contributions
- A **signal-coverage matrix diagnostic** that crosses Lean elaborator (pass/fail) with a semantic-equivalence judge (equiv/not), yielding four cells (TS/TO/SO/BF); a 4×4 per-problem transition matrix then decomposes any TC% gain into type-stratum recovery (~64%), semantic turnover, and a durable residual.
- A **method-invariant predictive regularity**: three algorithmically distinct elab-feedback methods each recover exactly 23/61 (37.7%, Wilson 95% CI [26.6%, 50.3%]) of Vanilla TO; mass-balance prediction calibrates other methods' ΔTS to within 2/186, and ΔTC fits a linear regression in the Vanilla elab-fail rate at R²=0.96 across six (model, dataset) cells.
- A **calibrated dual-judge protocol**: Claude Opus 4.7 paired with GTED at τ=0.5 reaches 94.4–95.0% pooled precision; a four-class taxonomy attributes 30–56% of GTED false negatives to elaborator-forced structural rewriting.
- **SAF as a causal-attribution probe**: LLM emits a typed JSON IR translated to Lean by a deterministic ~30-line procedure, isolating the +37pp Opus-vs-GTED gap to elaborator-driven surface geometry rather than LLM sampling noise.
- Identification of a **gold-error floor**: the durable SO residual reduces to two gold-formalization bugs in ProofNet# (Ireland-Rosen 1^30 and Dummit-Foote 7.2.2), documented with suggested corrections.

## Methodology
Statement-level autoformalization maps a natural-language theorem to a Lean 4 signature ending in `:= by sorry`. The authors factor evaluation through a 2×2 matrix: the elaborator labels pass/fail (complete on the type stratum, silent on the semantic stratum) and a semantic judge labels equivalent/not-equivalent (covers the semantic stratum but forgives the type stratum). Each of n outputs falls into exactly one cell — TS (true success), TO (type-only failure), SO (semantic-only failure), BF (both fail) — so TC% = (TS+SO)/n and SF% = TS/n. A 4×4 transition matrix tracks per-problem movement across methods.

Datasets: ProofNet# (186 type-complex undergraduate-textbook problems exercising Mathlib typeclass machinery) and MiniF2F-test (244 type-simple olympiad problems), bracketing the type-system complexity axis. Subject models: DeepSeek V4-Pro (primary), Qwen3.5-Plus, MiMo-v2.5-Pro. Four methods at the same ~4-LLM-call budget are compared: Vanilla (one-shot), Lean-Retry (elaborator-error feedback, K=3), Sample-Filter (N=4 sample, keep first elab-pass), and SAF (this paper: typed JSON IR refined against elaborator, deterministic translator, K=3). The dual-judge protocol pairs Claude Opus 4.7 (gold cross-judge, STRICT and EQUIV-IF-FIXED prompts) with GTED (symbolic generalized tree edit distance on LSP-extracted operator trees, τ=0.5). Infrastructure: Lean 4.19.0 via lean interact, prebuilt Mathlib, ATLAS-based GTED LSP backend, 60s elab timeout, JSONL resume.

## Results & Highlights
- The three elab-feedback methods add +34 to +36 problems to TS and cluster within ~1pp on both TC% and Opus-judged SF% (Lean-Retry 75.8/70.4, Sample-Filter 76.3/70.4, SAF 75.3/69.4 vs Vanilla 59.7/51.1 on DeepSeek V4-Pro × ProofNet#).
- ΔTS decomposition: ~64% (23/36) from TO→TS rescue (type stratum) and ~39% (14/36) from SO→TS flips, minus TS regression; net SO is flat (16→10) but masks 87.5% per-problem rescue offset by 8 newly created SO.
- The 23/61 TO→TS rate is statistically indistinguishable across the three methods (Fisher exact p=1.0 for all pairs); cross-method calibration predicts ΔTS to within 2/186.
- Cross-cell linearity: ΔTC = 0.325·V_fail + 2.88 (R²=0.957), with per-cell residuals in [−1.0, +1.3]pp; recovery rate is 41.2% on ProofNet# vs 61.8% on MiniF2F, a benchmark-modulation effect.
- Opus vs GTED disagree by +26 to +37pp on elab-feedback outputs (vs +7pp on Vanilla); the disagreement concentrates in the Structural category (S grows by 12–20pp), measuring elaborator-driven rewriting — the very mechanism that recovers TO into TS.
- K-saturation: refinement saturates at K=1 (+21.5pp); K=2,3 add only +3.3pp combined; 16 problems remain durably TO across all four methods due to plausible-but-invalid Mathlib API names.
- Only 1 of 186 problems is durably SO; the SO plateau approximates the benchmark's gold-error floor.

## Authors & Credibility
Chengxiao Dai and Zhanhui Lin are at the School of Computer Science, University of Sydney; Zhaokun Yan is at the China Academy of Information and Communications Technology. The paper is accepted to the 3rd AI for Math Workshop at ICML 2026 (Seoul). The work demonstrates strong empirical rigor — Wilson and bootstrap 95% CIs, Fisher exact tests, R²=0.96 fits, cross-dataset and cross-model validation — and ships a disagreement-taxonomy classifier script (`analysis/disagreement_taxonomy.py`) plus documented gold-formalization corrections. It engages directly with and calibrates against the very metrics it critiques (GTED, back-translation), and uses a cross-family judge (Claude Opus 4.7) disjoint from all four subject model families.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** High and well-scoped. The (TC≠SF) gap is known, but the per-stratum quantification, the method-invariant recovery regularity, and the deterministic-IR causal probe are genuinely new contributions. The framing as a "which cell moved" credit assignment reframes how benchmark gains should be reported.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Appropriate statistics (Wilson CIs for proportions, bootstrap at the problem level, Fisher exact homogeneity tests), transparent reporting of wide CIs at n=61, and honest acknowledgment that the within-2/186 agreement is partly self-consistency (the three methods already converge in aggregate).
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Public benchmarks, methods named after cited prior work, deterministic ~30-line translator, fixed GTED threshold τ=0.5 validated for stability, classifier script shipped, JSONL resume. Code/data availability is implied but not centralized in a repo link.
- **Significance:** Practically consequential for benchmark reporting (the paper recommends TC% + dual-judge SF% + per-cell decomposition) and for understanding why single-symbolic-judge SF% under-credits feedback methods. As a workshop paper, reach is narrower than a main-conference paper.
- **Red flags:** Semantic ground truth relies on Claude Opus 4.7, not expert Lean validators — a single misjudged cluster could distort SO/TO counts (though precision is bounded at ≥94%). Sample sizes are small (n=186, 14 BF problems). Only one (model, dataset) cell carries full Opus dual-judging; the other five cells are GTED-only. Qwen3.5-Plus shows a high IR-extraction failure rate (the one SAF<Vanilla cell) that is reported "without ad-hoc patching."

## Strengths
- Genuinely diagnostic framework that converts a scalar (TC%) into a mechanistic cell decomposition.
- Exceptional statistical transparency — every rate carries a CI, and negative/self-consistency caveats are stated openly.
- The deterministic-IR SAF probe cleanly isolates surface-form rewriting as a causal mechanism rather than an artifact.
- Discovery and documentation of gold-formalization bugs in a widely used benchmark, with suggested corrections.
- Cross-dataset/cross-model validation (6 cells) supports generality of the linearity claim.

## Limitations
- Small absolute n (186 ProofNet# problems; 14 BF problems) yields wide CIs on smaller strata.
- Reliance on an LLM judge (Opus 4.7) as semantic ground truth instead of expert Lean validators; a small expert audit is flagged as future work.
- Only one cell carries full Opus dual-judging; the cross-model/cross-dataset panel rests on GTED-only judging.
- Workshop-length scope; full MiniF2F dual-judge analysis across all four methods is deferred to future work.
- SAF's Qwen3.5-Plus IR-extraction failure (−12.9pp) is reported unpatched, slightly muddying the cross-model robustness claim.

## Relevance
该论文直接服务于"评估可靠性"与"错误分层"主题：它指出在类型复杂的基准上仅报 TC% 会掩盖机制、且单一符号判分器(GTED)会因 elaborator 强制的结构性改写而系统性低估反馈方法 ≥25pp 的语义忠实度，并给出可操作的分层报告规范(TC% + 双判分 SF% + 四格分解)。对于任何依赖自动评估指标来追踪 LLM 进展的工作，它都是关于"指标可信度"与"增益归属"的警示与方法论范例。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Would a small expert Lean-validator audit shift the SO/TO counts materially, or confirm Opus as a sufficient judge?
- Can a canonicalization-aware symbolic metric (e.g., operator-tree matching after `whnf` reduction) absorb the N/I taxonomy categories without an LLM judge, closing the Opus-vs-GTED gap deterministically?
- Does the 23/61 method-invariant TO→TS rate hold for next-generation models (beyond DeepSeek V4-Pro), or is it a property of the current K≤3 elaborator-signal regime?
- Can a new refinement *signal* (rather than a new topology) recover the 16 durably-TO problems whose failure mode is plausible-but-invalid Mathlib API names?
- How does the gold-error floor generalize to other autoformalization benchmarks, and what fraction of reported "semantic failures" across the literature are actually gold bugs?
