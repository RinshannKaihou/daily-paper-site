Key Finding

A vocabulary-aligned anchor loss gives every learned SAE direction an intrinsic token name at training time — preserving reconstruction quality while turning post-hoc feature naming into a geometric byproduct.

◆ Why This Matters
Serves the interpretability / sparse-autoencoder / hidden-state line directly: it targets the residual stream and gives dictionary directions a vocabulary-linked, geometrically verifiable name — an indexable handle for downstream circuit discovery.
◆ Critical Edge
The name is a geometric handle, not a causal interpretation — alignment is demonstrated, not behavioral effect. Alignment concentrates in shallow/middle layers and is anchor-coefficient-sensitive; only input-embedding anchors were tested.

Methodology

how it works

VASAE decomposes Transformer post-residual streams with a top-k sparse autoencoder (k=32, non-negative sparse code, linear encoder, Adam, lr=1e-3) using a vocabulary-sized dictionary d_sparse = |V|.

  • Standard L_recon is augmented with an anchor term: for each decoder column d_i, take max cosine similarity s_i to any fixed token embedding.
  • L_anchor = −(1/d_sparse)·Σ s_i rewards nearest-token alignment; total L_vasae = L_recon + λ·L_anchor.
  • Dictionary stays learnable — embeddings are fixed reference vectors only; the nearest token v*_i is recomputed each step so the anchor can drift.
  • After training, a feature's intrinsic token name is simply the string of its nearest vocabulary item; s_i is its alignment score.
  • Two checkpoints: GPT-2-small (|V|=50,257) and Llama-3.1-8B (|V|=128,256), trained on WikiText-103 activations via nnsight; diagnostic cutoff τ=0.8 from the bimodal score gap.

Strengths & Limitations

side by side

▲ Strengths

  • Reconstruction quality essentially unchanged from a standard SAE while gaining an interpretable, vocabulary-indexed handle — no observed free-lunch cost.
  • The hard-tied decoder ablation cleanly isolates the value of keeping the dictionary learnable.
  • Principled, geometrically derived naming avoids manual or separate automated labeling pipelines.
  • Honest framing of what a name does and does not imply (semantic readout vs. computational role vs. behavioral control).
  • Strong quantitative separation between standard-SAE and VASAE alignment distributions makes the central claim visually and statistically legible.

▼ Limitations

  • Tested only on two open-weight families and only residual-stream SAEs; generalization to more architectures/scales unverified.
  • Alignment strongest for GPT-2-small; Llama-3.1-8B alignment is depth-dependent and unstable in the final layer.
  • Only input-embedding anchors used; unembedding-based anchors remain unexplored.
  • No seed-level variance, no causal/behavioral validation, limited broader hyperparameter sweeps.
  • Intrinsic names are many-to-one and guarantee neither monosemanticity nor a circuit-level role.

Open Questions

for further exploration
  1. Does anchoring to the unembedding matrix (rather than input embeddings) recover stable alignment in Llama's deep / final layers?
  2. Can the anchor loss be made layer-adaptive (stronger near input, weaker near output) to avoid deep-layer instability?
  3. Do VASAE-named features actually participate in circuits or admit causal/behavioral validation (ablation, patching, steering), beyond geometric alignment?
  4. How does alignment behave beyond 8B and with sub-vocabulary (d_sparse < |V|) dictionaries, where naming is many-to-one by construction?
  5. Is the τ=0.8 threshold robust across architectures, or an artifact of the observed GPT-2 score distribution?
Credibility note. The authors are disciplined in distinguishing a geometric name from a causal explanation. However, standard deviations are reported across layers — not across random seeds — which obscures run-to-run variability.
