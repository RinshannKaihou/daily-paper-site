How it halts

certification as an anytime-valid betting martingale

The framework reframes randomized smoothing (RS) certification as an anytime-valid betting martingale. For a target success probability p, a likelihood-ratio wealth process W_t(p₀) is built as an E-value; by Ville's inequality, inverting it yields a lower confidence bound valid under continuous monitoring — immune to the peeking problem that breaks fixed-horizon Clopper–Pearson tests.

The unknown alternative q is integrated out via a mixture-of-Betas prior (Method of Mixtures), admitting closed-form updates through regularized incomplete Beta functions — turning binary hypothesis testing into continuous radius estimation.

The core novelty is a meta-learner M_θ (4-layer residual MLP) that ingests the backbone embedding, clean-softmax margin, and the empirical Phase-I success rate over 100 discarded samples, then outputs input-specific mixture weights and Beta parameters. Because the Phase-I glimpse is strictly discarded before betting begins, the process stays a valid super-martingale (Theorem 1). A fixed Krichevsky–Trofimov safety anchor (weight 0.01) guards against prior misspecification.

Inference Loop
01Phase-I glimpse. Draw N_sel=100 samples; estimate success rate; discard before betting.
02Meta-prior. M_θ predicts bespoke Beta mixture from embedding + softmax + glimpse.
03Wealth accumulation. Build E-value super-martingale under the learned prior.
04Re-solve bound. Every B=100 steps invert via Brent–Dekker for the LCB radius.
05Ensemble exit. Precision / UCB rejection / bankruptcy (ε_fail=0.1) / plateau → HALT.

Strengths & limitations

side-by-side, not buried

[+] Strengths

  • Clean theory. Soundness follows cleanly from test-martingale inversion + Ville's inequality; defensible guarantee.
  • Elegant framing. Meta-learner exploits only discarded Phase-I data — sidesteps the peeking problem entirely.
  • Real wall-clock wins. ~89% compute reduction translates directly to latency savings, not just sample counts.
  • Versatile. Triage / specialist / early-rejection modes argue a case well beyond raw speed.
  • Zero-shot transfer of the meta-learner across datasets and architectures.
  • Honest impact statement acknowledging ℓ₂-only defenses may mis-frame the real threat landscape.

[!] Limitations

  • Modest over KT. ~8–15% sample reduction vs. the strongest anytime-valid baseline; the 20–45× is vs. Cohen-10k.
  • Anytime-valid tax. ~1–3% lower certified accuracy than fixed-horizon methods at equal N.
  • ℓ₂ Gaussian-only. No other norms or threat models addressed.
  • Offline cost. Meta-learner needs 5k–10k labeled bitstreams collected & trained before deployment.
  • Heuristic thresholds (ε_start/end, bankruptcy, plateau, Δ=1.2) tuned per use-case; not fully principled.
  • Unquantified approximation. Differentiable truncated-Beta uses a 4th-order Taylor series with no rigorous error bound.

How does the framework behave under ℓ∞ or semantic/patch attacks, where mixture-of-Betas priors may not transfer?

Q05 · PRINCIPLED EXITS
