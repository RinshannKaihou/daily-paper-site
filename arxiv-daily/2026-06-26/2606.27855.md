> **一句话总结：** 本文验证了仅基于 ReLU 激活率统计的"记忆指标"可在无需验证集的小样本 sEMG 解码器校准中，作为过拟合的早期轻量级预警信号。

> **TL;DR:** The paper shows that activation-rate-based memorization indicators (e.g., mean activation rate of the last ReLU layer), computable from training data alone, can serve as an early, validation-free signal of overfitting during low-sample fine-tuning of sEMG decoders.

## 核心贡献
- 将此前提出的基于 ReLU 激活率的"记忆指标"首次迁移到 sEMG 解码器的低样本迁移学习校准场景，验证其作为过拟合预警信号的可行性。
- 设计了一个贴近真实边缘部署条件的紧凑 1D-CNN 迁移学习实验（NinaPro DB2，预训练 20 人、校准 10 人），用于复现"成功"与"失败"两种微调结果。
- 发现测试精度下降时往往伴随最后一层隐藏层平均激活率（MAR）的显著下降，且成功学习与失败学习的 MAR 变化方向/幅度可明显区分。
- 除 MAR 外，新引入激活率分布的分位数（25%/中位数/75%）与变异系数等更细粒度的统计指标，刻画 MAR 平均值会丢失的分布变化。
- 提出将激活率指标作为嵌入式、用户中心化校准流水线中的在线监测量，无需额外验证集即可触发早停/分层冻结/动态正则。

## Key Contributions
- First application of ReLU activation-rate-based memorization indicators (originally for image memorization) to low-sample transfer-learning calibration of sEMG decoders.
- A realistic, edge-deployable 1D-CNN transfer-learning benchmark on NinaPro DB2 (pretrain on 20 subjects, fine-tune on 10) that reliably produces both generalizing and non-generalizing calibrations.
- Empirical evidence that test-accuracy degradation during fine-tuning is accompanied by a marked reduction in the Mean Activation Rate (MAR) of the last hidden layer, with separable MAR trajectories for successful vs. unsuccessful learning.
- Extension of the indicator set beyond MAR with activation-rate quantiles (25/50/75%) and the coefficient of variation, capturing distributional shifts MAR alone hides.
- Proposal of activation-based indicators as a validation-free, online monitoring primitive for adaptive calibration pipelines (early stopping, layer freezing, dynamic regularization).

## Methodology
The authors run a two-stage transfer-learning experiment on the NinaPro DB2 benchmark (49 movement classes + rest, 12 Delsys Trigno electrodes, 6 repetitions/subject). Subjects 1–20 are used for pretraining, subjects 21–30 for subject-specific fine-tuning. Inputs are minimally preprocessed raw sEMG (normalization + 50-sample/20 ms windows with 10-sample overlap) via LibEMG. The model is a compact 1D-CNN: three conv blocks (filters 256/128/64, kernel 5, BatchNorm, ReLU) followed by three FC ReLU layers (1216/608/256) and a 49-way softmax. Training uses Adam, lr 0.001, CrossEntropyLoss, batch size 64 (100 pretraining epochs, 50 fine-tuning epochs). To induce overfitting, two conditions are varied: (1) number of calibration repetitions (1–4), and (2) trainable parameters — freezing all but the last two layers ("last") versus retraining all layers ("all"). The key monitored quantity is the activation rate of each of the 256 neurons in the last hidden layer, computed on the first 64-sample batch of each epoch (training data only, no validation set), summarized as MAR, AR quantiles, and coefficient of variation.

## Results & Highlights
- The pretrained model reaches 0.52 train / 0.34 test accuracy, but only 0.065 on unseen subjects (vs. ~0.02 chance) — underscoring the need for recalibration.
- Only the "last-two-layers" fine-tuning generalizes; the fully retrained ("all layers") model fails to generalize, providing clear successful vs. unsuccessful cases.
- After just 5 fine-tuning epochs, learning models show a small positive MAR change while non-learning models show large negative MAR changes — the two regimes are clearly separable in direction and magnitude.
- The coefficient of variation of activation rate rises continuously for the overfitting method; it initially drops then slowly climbs for the generalizing method as the train/test gap widens.
- Even the generalizing model develops a slight MAR decrease after 50 epochs, coinciding with a growing generalization gap (0.34/0.29 at epoch 5 → 0.44/0.34 at epoch 50), suggesting MAR tracks emerging overfitting rather than only catastrophic memorization.
- AR quantiles reveal distributional detail that the MAR mean alone obscures.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-good. The core indicators are the authors' own prior work; the novelty lies in (a) porting them from image-memorization to low-sample sEMG transfer learning and (b) adding distributional AR metrics (quantiles, CV). Conceptually an application/extension rather than a new method.
- **Rigor:** Reasonable for an exploratory study — 10 held-out subjects, two retraining regimes, multiple repetition counts, Kruskal–Wallis check on initial MAR. However, no threshold-selection protocol, no held-out validation of the indicator's predictive accuracy (e.g., AUC for early-warning classification), and statistical claims are mostly qualitative ("overwhelmingly tend to").
- **Reproducibility:** Architecture, hyperparameters, dataset (NinaPro DB2), and preprocessing (LibEMG) are specified in enough detail to reproduce; the small CNN and fixed hyperparameters help. Code/data release is not mentioned, and the exact per-subject MAR trajectories are only in figures.
- **Significance:** Practically motivated (edge/on-device sEMG calibration for exoskeletons/prostheses) and addresses a genuine gap — validation-free overfitting detection. Impact depends on future validation across datasets/architectures.
- **Red flags:** Sample size (10 fine-tuning subjects) is small; absolute MAR scale is dataset/architecture-dependent so no universal thresholds are offered; the "all layers" failure case may conflate non-learning with overfitting (authors acknowledge this); only one architecture and one dataset.

## Strengths
- Directly targets a real, under-served problem: validation-free overfitting monitoring in low-sample, on-device calibration.
- Clean experimental contrast between successful ("last layers") and unsuccessful ("all layers") fine-tuning makes the indicator's discriminative behaviour easy to see.
- Indicators are computable on a single 64-sample training batch per epoch — genuinely lightweight and online-compatible.
- Honest framing as relative, within-experiment indicators with explicit acknowledgement of dataset-dependence and limitations.
- Adds value beyond prior work by introducing distributional AR metrics (quantiles, CV) that expose dynamics the mean hides.

## Limitations
- Only one compact architecture and one dataset (NinaPro DB2); generalization to deeper networks, other activations, and other modalities is unproven.
- No quantitative evaluation of the indicator as a classifier/early-warning trigger (no AUC, precision/recall, or thresholding protocol).
- 10 fine-tuning subjects is a small cohort; per-subject variability is visible but not statistically characterized.
- The "all-layers" failure case may reflect under-learning rather than classical overfitting, complicating the memorization interpretation.
- Absolute MAR values are not transferable across setups; no universal thresholds are proposed.
- No released code/data stated.

## Relevance
该论文与"训练可观测性 / 过拟合检测"高度相关：它提供了一种仅依赖训练数据、无需验证集的在线指标（ReLU 激活率及其分布统计），在小样本微调场景下提前预警过拟合，可视为早停与验证曲线监测的轻量替代方案，对低数据/边缘端模型的训练监控具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Can MAR (and the new distributional metrics) be turned into a calibrated early-warning classifier with measured AUC/precision across many subjects, rather than a qualitative separator?
- Do these indicators remain predictive in deeper/larger networks (e.g., attention-based SOTA sEMG models) and with non-ReLU activations (GELU, Swish)?
- Is the MAR threshold (or its relative change) transferable across datasets, electrode setups, and class counts, or must it always be normalized within-experiment?
- Could activation-rate indicators drive a closed-loop policy (early stopping, selective layer freezing, dynamic regularization) that measurably improves final calibration accuracy?
- Does the indicator distinguish true point-memorization from under-learning / feature-memorization, given the "all-layers" case may not be classical overfitting?
