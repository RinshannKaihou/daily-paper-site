01

Methodology

多阶段评估框架

Schematic generation is framed as fundamentally unlike code generation: it must capture structural, functional, and visual aspects at once, and no single ground-truth schematic exists — multiple structurally distinct schematics can be functionally equivalent.

The dataset of 99 Verilog modules spans arithmetic units, FSMs, control logic, and numeric property checkers, sourced from open-source repos (e.g. OpenRISC OR1200) and human generation — with no ground-truth schematics provided. Correctness is defined along three axes: functional consistency with the RTL, structural fidelity of datapath/control organization, and schematic validity/legibility including timing semantics.

Because visual outputs can't be simulated or formally verified, correctness is approximated via an 8-criterion rubric scored Boolean True/False — reflecting the discrete, non-gradual nature of hardware. Experiments ran on GPT 5.2 (Go) and Gemini 3 Flash Pro.

5-Stage Evaluation Pipeline

01Zero-shot generation — prompt-driven schematic synthesis
02Self-evaluation — model rates its own output via rubric
03Grounded cross-eval — one model rates the other vs. RTL
04Blind cross-eval — image only, no RTL context
05Human validation — structured rubric verification

Strengths & Limitations

优势 / 局限

Strengths +

  • First multimodal benchmark at the LLM × hardware intersection, directly filling a real gap (prior benchmarks RTLLM, VerilogEval, HDLBits, AutoChip are all text-only NL/RTL → Verilog).
  • Honest, well-motivated negative result: the near-zero κ finding is a valuable, citable contribution beyond the benchmark itself.
  • Multi-stage evaluation is principled in covering orthogonal failure modes — each stage compensates for the others.
  • 8-criterion Boolean rubric is well matched to the discrete nature of hardware logic.

Limitations !

  • No formal equivalence verification — correctness is approximated by rubrics and human judgment, so the leaderboard is inherently soft.
  • Small and category-imbalanced dataset: 76 / 99 modules are numeric property checkers, limiting breadth.
  • Human evaluation limited to moderate-complexity circuits; scaling to hierarchical / multi-module designs is left open.
  • Proprietary, version-pinned models and unclear artifact release hinder exact reproduction.

Questions for Further Exploration

How do the κ≈0 findings generalize beyond hardware to other non-executable visual-output domains (diagrams, schematics, architectural drawings)?

?

Would scaling and rebalancing the dataset (fewer numeric checkers, more FSM / datapath / control) shift the GPT-vs-Gemini ranking or accuracy gap?

?

Can a stricter visual rubric enforcing canonical gate symbols convert the currently inflated C7–C8 scores into a meaningful correctness signal?

?

Is the near-zero inter-rater agreement driven by ambiguous rubric criteria, model inability to interpret schematics, or both — and would chain-of-thought / tool-augmented judging help?

?

With formal equivalence checked off-scope, what domain-aware automated verification could close the gap between rubric proxies and true hardware validity?
