> **一句话总结：** 本文提出在对话回合（turn）的平均隐藏状态上训练稀疏自编码器（SAE），使可解释特征的规模与上下文长度解耦，并显著简化长上下文与多轮对话场景下的归因图分析。

> **TL;DR:** The authors train SAEs on the mean hidden state across a conversation turn (rather than per-token activations), yielding features that capture high-level turn characteristics, scale independently of context length, and produce compact turn-level attribution graphs for long contexts.

## 核心贡献
- 提出"回合平均 SAE"（Turn-Averaged SAE）：对单个 Human/Assistant 回合内所有 token 的残差流激活取均值后训练标准 SAE，使活跃特征数量固定、不再随上下文长度线性增长。
- 提出嵌套 SAE 架构（Nested SAE）：借鉴 Matryoshka 思想，在单个 SAE 内通过分区损失同时学习"回合级"（低频）和"逐 token"（高频）特征。
- 将归因图（attribution graphs）适配到回合粒度，使节点数量按"回合×层"而非"token×层"计，大幅压缩多轮对话的图规模。
- 通过 LLM-as-judge 与确定性嵌入度量系统比较三种架构，发现回合平均特征在覆盖度（coverage）上显著优于逐 token 特征，而后者在区分度（discrimination）上更强。
- 干预实验（36,000 次干预）与完备性/替换充分性指标验证了回合级归因边的可靠性，嵌套架构在充分性指标上表现最佳。

## Key Contributions
- Introduces **turn-averaged SAEs**: training a standard SAE on the mean residual-stream activation across a turn yields a fixed-size feature representation that decouples feature count from context length, with no architectural or code changes.
- Proposes a **nested SAE** using a Matryoshka-like loss so a single model jointly learns turn-level (low-frequency) and per-token (high-frequency) features within one encoder/decoder.
- Adapts **attribution graphs to the turn level** (nodes per turn × layer instead of per token × layer), enabling tractable, compact analysis of multi-turn conversations and long contexts.
- Provides a systematic quantitative comparison along two axes—discrimination vs. coverage—showing turn-averaged features win on coverage (87.9% pairwise win rate) while per-token max-pooled features win on discrimination (95% on 10-way matching).
- Validates the approach via 36,000 intervention experiments plus completeness/replacement sufficiency metrics, with the nested architecture achieving the highest sufficiency scores.

## Methodology
The core idea is simple: given a span (turn) $T$ of tokens with residual-stream activations $x_t$, compute the turn average $\bar{x} = (1/|T|)\sum_{t\in T} x_t$ and train a standard SAE on $\bar{x}$ with ordinary reconstruction loss $\|\bar{x} - \text{dec}(\text{enc}(\bar{x}))\|^2$, using BatchTopK ($d_{\text{sae}}=32{,}768$, $k=128$). Because the mean preserves dimensionality, no SAE architecture/training-code changes are required. Intuitively, averaging acts as a low-pass filter: broad concepts (topic, style, behavioral traits) reflected across many positions survive, while token-level noise (single-token detectors, syntactic patterns) is smoothed away.

Two hybrid architectures are explored. The **decoupled SAE** trains separate SAEs on $\bar{x}$ and on the per-token residual $(x_t - \bar{x})$; it works for reconstruction but the two feature sets are causally disconnected, blocking attribution. The **nested SAE** instead uses a single encoder/decoder split into an inner partition $[0:h]$ and outer partition $[h:d_{\text{sae}}]$, with a Matryoshka-like loss that forces the inner partition to reconstruct $\bar{x}$ while the full set reconstructs per-token activations; $h=16{,}384$ and weight $\alpha=1.0$. All SAEs are trained on Qwen-2.5-7B-Instruct (single-layer SAEs at layer 19, ~70% depth; multi-layer SAEs at layers 6/11/16/21) using LMSYS-Chat-1M. Turn-averaged attribution graphs are built with the circuit-tracer framework, defining turn-level edge weights that are normalized by source/target turn lengths so they are length-agnostic.

## Results & Highlights
- **Coverage vs. discrimination trade-off:** Turn-averaged features win 87.9% of pairwise coverage comparisons (beating per-token max 79.7% head-to-head) and rank first 44% of the time in 5-way coverage ranking; per-token max-pooled features dominate 10-way discrimination matching at 95.0% (vs. 73.9% for turn-averaged). The deterministic embedding metric (Qwen3-Embedding-8B) confirms turn-averaged features have the highest similarity (0.663 at k=3).
- **Long-context generalization:** Although trained only on chat turns, the turn-averaged SAE identifies high-level concepts in 24K–32K-token LongBench v2 documents (a single mean hidden state per document, ~150× longer than training examples) more reliably than per-token features.
- **Turn-level attribution graphs:** Reduce a hypothetical 10-turn, 250-token conversation from ~128K per-token nodes to ~5K turn-level nodes across 4 layers.
- **Case studies:** A 12-paragraph system-prompt experiment shows every targeted query increases attribution share at the correct paragraph across all architectures; a 14-turn jailbreak-to-degeneration conversation yields an interpretable chain (AI-safety disclaimers → nuclear weapons/domination features → adversarial-prompt detection → degenerate output) that per-token features cannot reproduce (they attribute to "chemical compound names"/"JSON" instead).
- **Validation:** 36,000 interventions show strong agreement between edge weights and observed effects (sign matching ~100%, minimum 95%); nested > per-token > turn-averaged on completeness/replacement sufficiency, with the nested model notably beating post-hoc aggregated per-token features.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** The idea of reshaping the SAE training signal by averaging across the token dimension is genuinely simple and under-explored; the nested/Matryoshka-style joint loss is a clean, well-motivated extension. The framing as a low-pass filter over the token dimension is insightful.
- **Rigor:** Quantitative evaluation is reasonably systematic (two axes × four metrics, 2,000 holdout turns, 36,000 interventions, sufficiency metrics). Reconstruction (nMSE) is reported transparently, including the off-distribution failure of per-token SAEs on mean inputs.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Model, data, SAE widths/k, layers, learning rates, batch sizes, epochs, and the nested loss formulation are all specified; BatchTopK is standard.
- **Significance:** Practical and timely—as context windows grow, per-token SAE scaling becomes a real bottleneck, and this offers a low-friction remedy plus a credible attribution tool.
- **Red flags:** Scale is small (7B model, width 32K) relative to frontier settings; the entire evaluation pipeline (feature descriptions, structured summaries, pairwise/5-way judgments) is LLM-generated, with autointerp prompts differing between architectures, which the authors themselves flag as a confound. Qualitative analyses rely on a few hand-selected examples. The coverage-vs-discrimination framing risks framing the result favorably (turn-averaged "wins" the axis it was designed for).

## Strengths
- Minimal-change method: averaging preserves input dimensionality, so existing SAE architectures and training code work unmodified.
- Conceptually clean low-pass-filter intuition that explains why high-level features emerge.
- Nested SAE elegantly recovers both turn-level and per-token detail in one model and achieves the best attribution sufficiency.
- Strong, large-scale intervention validation (36,000 interventions) and dual LLM-judge + deterministic embedding metrics.
- Demonstrated cross-length generalization (chat turns → 30K-token documents) and compelling attribution case studies.

## Limitations
- Scale: only a 7B model with SAE width 32,768; frontier-scale and much wider SAEs are untested.
- Evaluation depends heavily on LLM-generated descriptions/summaries/judgments; turn-averaged and per-token autointerp prompts differ (not identical conditions), and a ground-truth benchmark is absent.
- Qualitative results are drawn from a small number of manually selected, illustrative cases.
- Turn-averaged features are weaker at discrimination and at per-token intervention correlation, and averaging assumes information is evenly distributed across positions (which prior work suggests is not always true—e.g., period/last-token concentration).
- Decoupled model is causally disconnected, limiting its analytical utility.

## Relevance
本文与 SAE 可解释性、隐藏状态分析高度相关：它直接改变了 SAE 训练时使用的隐藏状态粒度（从逐 token 到回合/段落平均），使可解释特征的规模与上下文长度解耦，并让基于隐藏状态特征的归因图在长上下文与多轮对话中变得可行，对研究"隐藏状态承载的高层概念与人设/安全属性"很有价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- How do turn-averaged SAEs behave at frontier model scale with much wider dictionaries, and does the coverage advantage persist or saturate?
- Since information is known to concentrate at specific tokens (period, last token of entity names), would weighting token positions instead of uniform averaging improve feature quality?
- Can a ground-truth, non-LLM benchmark for "feature coverage" be constructed to remove the autointerp confound?
- How do turn-averaged features interact with behavioral/safety steering (e.g., can they be used to monitor or control persona and jailbreak-related traits end-to-end)?
- Does the nested partition ratio (inner vs. outer capacity) generalize, or is the 16K/16K split task-specific?
- Could the same averaging/reshaping principle extend to other activation-based analyses (e.g., natural-language autoencoders) with measurable gains?
