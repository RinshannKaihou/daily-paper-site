Why this matters

Gives accelerator designers a fast, system-level way to navigate the accuracy–efficiency–reliability trade-off of low-precision DNN deployment — sweeping architectures in hours, with no per-config fine-tuning.

Critical edge ⚠

No head-to-head comparison with any SOTA baseline (HAWQ-V3, QUIDAM) — "near-optimal" rests on the knapsack formulation, not benchmarks. Only two models (BERT, ResNet); LLM generalization untested.

Methodology

deterministic, non-iterative — replaces the O(10³³) train→map→eval loop

SEADA folds four components into a single deterministic pass, sidestepping the astronomically large joint precision+mapping space of the conventional iterative loop (~O(10³³) for ResNet-18). Each stage is computed once and reused.

(1) Multi-precision cost model — a configurable PE-array template with FP32/16/8/4 and INT8/4/2 (INT32 accumulate) units, double-buffered global buffer and NoC; latency/energy from Accelergy (32 nm, 1 GHz).

(2) QuickFlow mapping — local search (validated vs Timeloop+Accelergy) models low-precision ΣAᵢ·Bᵢ+C as P FMA units with precision-dependent reduction trees; the per-layer/precision, EDP-minimized library is pre-computed once.

(3) FP-layer models — softmax, exp, 1/x (Newton-Raphson), Welford decomposed into FP primitives; fused dequant→op→quant forms absorb conversion overhead into scaling constants (MHSA via adapted FlashAttention-2, GELU, LayerNorm, BatchNorm).

(4) EAGL precision selection — an entropy/knapsack formulation: layer entropy from a single uniformly-quantized checkpoint proxies sensitivity; supports >2 precisions; grouped layers. Models fine-tuned once via knowledge distillation; first/last layers fixed at 8-bit.

Strengths

what lands well
  • Genuinely holistic: jointly models accelerated (low-precision) and floating-point layers, exposing how non-accelerated layers erode headline savings — a gap in bMAC-proxy studies.
  • Deterministic, single-fine-tuning-pass precision assignment is a major efficiency win over iterative GA/RL/NSGA-II searches.
  • The CBR metric decouples the expensive accuracy characterization (done once) from the architecture sweep, enabling large-scale exploration in hours.
  • Clean derivation of fused dequant→op→quant forms for MHSA/GELU/LayerNorm/BatchNorm with concrete energy evidence.

Limitations

where it strains
  • No direct baseline comparison (HAWQ-V3, QUIDAM) — "near-optimal" claims are under-supported empirically.
  • Only two DNN benchmarks (BERT-base, ResNet-50); no modern LLMs or larger transformers where attention/precision trade-offs differ.
  • Limited to simulated quantization; fully-integer mixed-precision (I-BERT-style) explicitly deferred to future work.
  • FP pipeline experimentally restricted to FP32 despite modeling FP4/8/16; sub-byte and FP benefits unverified.
  • Analytical cost model assumes idealized PE utilization (1 instr/cycle) and a specific 32 nm node; silicon validation absent.

Open Questions

where the work points next
01How does CBR generalize to modern LLMs (Llama-class) with KV-cache quantization, GQA, and much larger attention share?
02Would extending the FP pipeline to FP8/FP4 (already modeled) change the Pareto frontier — and does it interact with the CBR proxy?
03Can the fused dequant→op→quant derivations be verified against silicon or RTL rather than analytical/Accelergy estimates?
04How sensitive are results to the quantization scheme (simulated vs integer-only, per-channel vs per-tensor)?
05Does the EAGL/knapsack assignment stay near-optimal head-to-head vs HAWQ-V3 or NSGA-II at matched compute budgets?
