> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"能力切片"作为评测与训练数据之间的中间表示，构建了一个可审计的闭环，将基准级失败转化为可测试的数据干预假设，并在两个方向相反的案例研究中验证（BBH回归定位到<EOS>监督缺失而非数据问题；AIME数学弱项通过算子组合诊断+定向采样大幅提升）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces the "capability slice" — an evaluation↔training-data bridge — and a closed loop that turns a benchmark-level failure into a testable, auditable data intervention, validated by two opposing case studies (EOS-supervision bug vs. operation-composition-targeted math sampling).

## 核心贡献
- 提出**能力切片（capability slice）**作为中间表征：一组共享背景条件、任务类型、求解算子和输出约束的评测样本，比基准名更精细、比单样本更稳定，使"评测失败→数据修复"的推理变得可行。
- 构建**分析工具包三件套**：评测样本分类法（4维）、非指令数据分类法（4维：内容世界/语篇结构/算子机会/监督密度）、评测到数据的映射规则，共同实例化一个五步闭环（弱能力切片→数据供给画像→数据动作→实验验证→结果分析）。
- **案例1（数据排除）**：BBH在持续预训练后下降−46.82%，诊断沿"输出约束"维度追溯到<EOS>token损失被掩码，而非推理能力退化；恢复EOS监督使BBH从25.14升至66.44（超过热启动点），且其他15项基准基本不变。
- **案例2（数据纳入）**：AIME数学弱项按求解算子分解，发现瓶颈是算子组合（约束跟踪、边界推理、符号变换、计数、比较等）而非孤立算术；据此设计的诊断对齐重要性采样将AIME2025/2026 Pass@128从6.67/0.00提升到26.67/26.67。
- 同一闭环在两个方向相反的案例中均得到正确判断，证明评测到数据的推理可以成为常规、可审计、实验验证的方法，而非依赖工程师直觉。

## Key Contributions
- Introduces the **capability slice** as an intermediate representation between benchmark names (too coarse) and individual samples (too noisy), sharing four structured dimensions: background condition, task type, solving operation, and output constraint.
- Builds a three-part **analysis toolkit**: an evaluation sample taxonomy (4 dims, annotated over 16 pre-training benchmarks via human–LLM co-design), a non-instruction data taxonomy (content world / discourse structure / operation opportunity / supervision density), and evaluation-to-data mapping rules that together instantiate a five-step auditable closed loop.
- **Case Study 1 (rules data OUT):** A −46.82% BBH regression after continued pre-training is traced, via the output-constraint dimension, to a masked `<EOS>` loss rather than weakened reasoning; restoring single-token EOS supervision lifts BBH from 25.14 to 66.44 (above warm-start) without touching the data.
- **Case Study 2 (rules data IN):** A persistent AIME math weakness is decomposed by solving operation into non-additive failing combinations (constraint tracking, boundary-case reasoning, symbolic transformation, counting, comparison); a diagnosis-aligned weighted importance-sampling procedure over synthetic instruction data lifts AIME2025/AIME2026 Pass@128 from 6.67/0.00 to 26.67 each.
- The *same unmodified loop* reaches opposite but correct verdicts in both cases, demonstrating that evaluation-to-data inference can be routine, auditable, and experimentally validated rather than intuition-driven.

## Methodology
The paper develops a conceptual framework centered on three objects — evaluation, model capability, and training data — and an engineering analysis order (Evaluation → Model Capability → Training Data) that runs counter to the causal data→capability→evaluation loop. The operational unit is the **capability slice**, defined as a group of evaluation samples sharing similar values along four dimensions:
- **Background condition** — information environment (language, domain, parametric/contextual need, context scope, discourse form, ambiguity/noise).
- **Task type** — the user-facing objective (e.g., factual QA, logical reasoning, formal math, code generation).
- **Solving operation** — multi-label intermediate operations (e.g., arithmetic computation, symbolic transformation, constraint tracking, multi-hop composition, boundary-case reasoning).
- **Output constraint** — answer form, answer space, scoring rule, format rigidity, exactness requirement.

The **analysis toolkit** instantiates this framework: (1) an evaluation sample taxonomy annotated over 16 benchmarks (each sample independently labeled by two LLMs — DeepSeek-V3.2 and Qwen3.5 Plus — with disagreements routed to human adjudication); (2) a non-instruction data taxonomy characterizing raw corpus segments by what learnable signal they supply; (3) evaluation-to-data mapping rules (taxonomy-aligned correspondences plus an LLM-assisted mechanism for out-of-scope slices). These form the closed loop: *Weak Capability Slice → Data Affordance Profile → Data Action → Experimental Validation → Result Analysis*. Mapping rules are explicitly framed as structured heuristics for generating hypotheses, not causal guarantees; every intervention is validated under a single-variable experimental design.

For Case Study 2, the diagnosis feeds a **diagnosis-aligned weakness score** combining raw error rate, pairwise composition effect, and non-additive (triple-level) composition effect, stabilized by a support-reliability factor. High-scoring operation combinations drive an Efraimidis–Spirakis weighted reservoir sampling (without replacement, bounded duplication) over a synthetic instruction pool, holding the training recipe and token budget fixed.

## Results & Highlights
- **Case Study 1 — EOS supervision bug:** Continued pre-training (200B tokens) improved 15 of 16 benchmarks but drove BBH down −46.82% (47.27 → 25.14). Output-constraint analysis showed failures were concentrated in strict-output closed-set/MCQ items; prediction-level audit found the model often emitted the correct gold answer *as a prefix* but continued generating (mean suffix length 394±733 chars vs 0.01±0.28 for warm-start), frequently appending `<begin_url>` document-start tokens. Training-code audit revealed the `<EOS>` loss was masked during CPT. Restoring EOS loss lifted BBH to **66.44** and improved several other generative benchmarks (HumanEval 32.32 → 42.07, GSM8K 68.54 → 71.34), while gold-prefix rates stayed ~69% but scorer-acceptance rose from 37.34% to 99.69%.
- **Case Study 2 — Operation-composition bottleneck:** The warm-start model scored 0.00–0.05 on AIME Pass@1 and failed even at Pass@128 on AIME2026. Operation-level decomposition (9,304 items from GSM8K/MathQA/MATH) localized the weakness to combinations like (constraint_tracking, multi_hop_composition), (equation_formulation, multi_hop_composition, symbolic_transformation), and counting/comparison triples exhibiting *non-additive* (super-additive) degradation. A targeted-sampling checkpoint (10B math tokens sampled from a 15B synthetic pool, ρ=1.0) raised **AIME2025 Pass@128 6.67 → 26.67** and **AIME2026 Pass@128 0.00 → 26.67**, with general-domain benchmarks essentially unchanged and MATH500 Pass@128 77.20 → 81.00.
- Operation-level re-evaluation confirmed gains concentrate on the diagnosed slices, most consistently under Pass@128 — the protocol best able to detect broadened-but-not-yet-modal trajectory coverage. A notable asymmetry: constraint-tracking-bearing slices were flat under the single-shot standard suite but improved under Pass@128, illustrating that heuristic interventions need multi-protocol evaluation.

## Authors & Credibility
The three authors (Zhixuan Li, Jiangan Yuan, Han Xu) are affiliated with **Baidu Inc.**; the first two are co-first authors with equal contributions. The work is positioned as a methodological contribution rather than a claim about a specific model, and is grounded in a real industrial continued-pre-training pipeline (200B-token CPT runs, 16 standard benchmarks, controlled single-variable experimental designs). The empirical claims are appropriately qualified throughout, with the authors explicitly labeling mapping rules as heuristics and subjecting every intervention to experimental validation. The paper is a June 2026 arXiv preprint (63 pages, v1).

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** High. The framing of the evaluation↔data *interface* as a first-class object, and the "capability slice" as a granularity deliberately intermediate between benchmark and sample, is fresh and well-motivated. The bidirectional case-study design (one ruling data out, one ruling data in) is an elegant methodological contribution that makes the central point more convincingly than two same-direction anecdotes.
- **Rigor:** Strong experimental hygiene. Both case studies use single-variable controlled designs (only the loss mask / only the sampled instruction subset changed; architecture, optimizer, token budget held fixed). The EOS-bug diagnosis is supported by a layered audit (benchmark → output-constraint slice → prediction-level prefix/suffix statistics → training-code audit → controlled fix), which is exemplary root-cause analysis.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate-to-good. The taxonomies, mapping rules, and sampling formulas are fully specified, and the 16 benchmarks are public. However, the underlying model, the synthetic instruction pool, and the internal data pipeline are proprietary, so the case studies cannot be independently reproduced end-to-end.
- **Significance:** Practically significant for pre-training teams: the EOS bug alone is the kind of subtle, expensive-to-miss failure that aggregate benchmark watching obscures, and the operation-decomposition approach offers a transferable diagnostic vocabulary. The broader scientific impact depends on whether the taxonomy and mapping rules generalize beyond the 16-benchmark, single-pipeline setting tested.
- **Red flags:** Modest effect sizes on the standard suite in Case Study 2 (mean Δacc +0.16–0.33 pt; some constraint-tracking slices regress at Pass@1), which the authors transparently attribute to the intervention broadening trajectory coverage rather than sharpening the modal output. The headline Pass@128 gains rest on a small AIME sample (AIME2026 n≈30 problems), so point estimates carry high variance.

## Strengths
- Conceptually clean intermediate representation (capability slice) with four well-justified dimensions that map evaluation and data onto a shared vocabulary.
- Exemplary root-cause analysis in Case Study 1, turning a "reasoning regression" into a one-line loss-mask fix — a genuinely valuable practitioner lesson.
- Bidirectional case-study design makes the loop's value explicit both when it points to a data fix and when it correctly rules one out.
- Honest, self-aware framing: mapping rules are heuristics, effects are qualified by evaluation protocol, and limitations (taxonomy asymmetry, single pipeline, proprietary data) are stated plainly.
- Controlled, single-variable experimental designs throughout, with slice-level re-evaluation confirming that gains concentrate where diagnosis predicted.

## Limitations
- Empirical scope is narrow: 16 fixed benchmarks and a single continued-pre-training pipeline; behavior at other training stages and on different capability suites is untested.
- The non-instruction data taxonomy is exercised only descriptively — no executed retrieval/reweighting intervention validates the more indirect side of the mapping, leaving an asymmetry.
- Mapping rules are heuristic hypothesis generators, not causal claims; coverage of non-instruction data is limited to a small set of design-aligned dimension correspondences plus an LLM-assisted fallback.
- Case Study 2 effect sizes on the standard suite are small/noisy at Pass@1; headline gains rely on Pass@128 over small AIME samples, so variance is high and absolute accuracy remains low.
- The constraint-tracking slice exposes a protocol-dependency: the same intervention looks like a failure under single-shot evaluation but a success under Pass@128, indicating the loop's verdict is sensitive to evaluation-protocol choice.

## Relevance
该工作直接服务于"评测可观测性"与"训练数据可解释性"的交叉点：能力切片提供了一种介于基准名与单样本之间的细粒度诊断单元，天然适配推理评测的失败归因与训练数据/目标的定向干预。对于构建训练观测平台（将基准波动归因到具体能力维度、算子组合或训练目标缺陷）以及推理评测的失败切片化分析，这套分类法与闭环流程提供了可直接借鉴的方法论骨架。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Does the closed loop generalize beyond continued pre-training to instruction tuning, RLHF, and from-scratch pre-training, where the data↔capability relationship differs?
- Can the non-instruction data branch be validated symmetrically — i.e., does retrieving/reweighting raw corpus segments by the same affordance profile yield comparable, controlled gains?
- How robust are the taxonomy labels to annotator/LLM choice, and how much does inter-annotator disagreement degrade slice-level diagnoses at scale?
- Could the diagnosis-aligned weakness score be made adaptive (updated online during training) rather than computed once from a warm-start checkpoint?
- How transferable are the specific operation-composition findings (e.g., counting×comparison non-additivity) across model families and scales?
