> **一句话总结：** 本文通过激活修补与组件消融，揭示视觉语言模型在感知-知识冲突中遵循"视觉默认、先验覆盖"的不对称因果结构：视觉接地是默认通路，而先验知识依赖网络后半段仅占 2.5–4.8% 的稀疏注意力头（路由头+写入头）进行主动注入。

> **TL;DR:** Using activation patching (residual stream, attention heads, MLPs), component ablation, and mechanistic analysis across three VLM families, the authors show visual grounding emerges by default while prior grounding depends on a sparse (2.5–4.8%) set of causally necessary late-layer attention heads that decompose into "routing" and "writing" roles.

## 核心贡献
- 首次提供 VLM 感知-知识冲突的组件级因果解释：定位到具体的注意力头与 MLP 子层（残差流→注意力头→MLP 三种粒度的激活修补）。
- 建立了跨模型一致的"不对称因果结构"：视觉接地为默认通路，先验接地依赖网络后半段仅 2.5–4.8% 的稀疏注意力头主动注入；消融这些头可在 68–96% 的先验接地样本中翻转预测，而对视觉接地仅影响 0.8–7.5%。
- 识别出"路由-写入"两级电路：早期路由头调节信息流（改变注意力分布或被注意表征），晚期写入头直接将答案 token 投影进残差流（logit lens 命中率 0%→>80%）。
- 在三个架构差异显著的 VLM 家族（Qwen-VL 3B/7B、LLaVA-NeXT 7B、PaliGemma 3B/10B）上验证机制通用，但路由实现方式分化：Qwen-VL/LLaVA 通过注意力重分配，PaliGemma 通过被注意表征的差异。
- 给出受控干预的具体目标，为"何时信任视觉 vs 先验知识"的多模态推理控制提供机理基础。

## Key Contributions
- First component-level causal account of perception-knowledge conflict in VLMs, localizing specific attention heads and MLP sublayers via activation patching at three granularities (residual stream, per-head, per-MLP) plus ablation studies.
- Establishes a consistent *asymmetric causal structure*: visual grounding is the default pathway, while prior grounding requires active injection by a sparse 2.5–4.8% of attention heads in the network's second half; ablating them flips 68–96% of prior-grounded predictions but only 0.8–7.5% of visual ones.
- Decomposes the sparse circuit into *routing heads* (modulate information flow) and *writing heads* (directly project answer tokens into the residual stream via the unembedding).
- Validates the mechanism across three architecturally distinct VLM families (Qwen-VL 3B/7B, LLaVA-NeXT 7B, PaliGemma 3B/10B), while showing the routing implementation diverges (attention redistribution vs. representation modulation).
- Provides concrete component-level targets for controllable multimodal reasoning over when to trust visual evidence vs. stored knowledge.

## Methodology
The study uses the **Visual-Counterfact** dataset (467 everyday objects recolored to conflict with world knowledge, e.g., a blue strawberry), evaluated under two grounding prompts on the *same* counterfactual image: **Visual** ("What color is this {object} here?") vs. **Prior** ("What color is a {object} usually?"). Quantitative analysis is restricted to *correctly conflicting* examples (73–212 per model) where the model genuinely resolves a conflict.

Three complementary causal methods are applied at the last token position via the NNsight library:
1. **Activation patching** in both directions — P2V (prior→visual) and V2P (visual→prior) — at residual-stream, attention-head, and MLP-sublayer granularities, scored by a normalized restoration score and a discrete flip rate.
2. **Component ablation** (zero-ablation) to test causal *necessity*, reported as flip rates under individual and group (promoting/suppressing) ablations.
3. **Mechanistic characterization**: attention-pattern analysis (image-attention fraction deltas across grounding modes) and a **logit lens** on head-output differences to identify which heads write answer tokens into vocabulary space.

Heads are classified as promoting/suppressing via PCA ±2σ on restoration vectors. A visual-circuit contrast (vary image, hold prompt fixed) serves as a robustness check.

## Results & Highlights
- **Behavioral asymmetry**: With no conflict, all models reach 86–96% accuracy; under conflict (Prior + counterfactual image), accuracy collapses to 17.7–55.7% — models report what they *see* rather than what they *know*.
- **Critical window**: Conflict is resolved in 7–16 layers starting at 52–76% network depth; V2P restoration precedes P2V by 3–19 layers, evidencing vision-as-default.
- **Sparse causal circuit**: Only 2.5–4.8% of attention heads are classified; ablating promoting heads flips 68–96% of prior-grounded but only 0.8–7.5% of visual-grounded predictions.
- **MLP asymmetry**: Same directional effect but 1.2–8× weaker, consistent with MLPs as amplifiers rather than primary routers.
- **Routing→writing decomposition**: Early routing heads (Qwen/LLaVA shift attention between image/text, deltas +0.19–0.24; PaliGemma uses representation modulation with ~0 deltas) followed by late writing heads (>80% logit-lens hit rate).
- **Six generalizing properties** (ablation asymmetry, critical-window location, V2P-before-P2P ordering, sparse head count, two-stage mechanism, MLP asymmetry) hold across all three families despite distinct routing implementations.

## Authors & Credibility
Authors are from **University of Tübingen, Harvard University, and UT Austin** — groups with established track records in multimodal mechanistic interpretability. Co-authors (Golovanevsky, Rudman, Eickhoff) created the Visual-Counterfact dataset and the NOTICE activation-patching pipeline cited heavily here, positioning this work as a direct causal extension of their own prior behavioral/representational studies. Code is released publicly. The experimental design (correctly-conflicting example filtering, dual patching directions, sufficiency+necessity tests, robustness contrast) reflects strong methodological discipline aligned with best practices in causal interpretability (Zhang & Nanda 2024; Minder et al. 2025).

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty**: High. Prior VLM-conflict work was behavioral, correlational, or steering-vector-based; this is the first to give a *component-level causal* account with an explicit necessity/sufficiency decomposition and a routing-vs-writing functional split.
- **Rigor**: Strong. Combines three intervention granularities, both patching directions, group+individual ablations, a visual-circuit robustness check, and quantified redundancy analysis. The restriction to correctly-conflicting examples prevents noise contamination.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Public code repository, open-weight models accessed through documented NNsight API, standard dataset. Sample sizes (73–212) are modest but typical for mechanistic work.
- **Significance**: Meaningful for both interpretability (a clear asymmetric sparse-circuit finding, contrasting with the balanced unimodal parametric-vs-retrieval conflict of Jin et al. 2024) and for reliability/control (concrete intervention targets).
- **Red flags / caveats**: Scope is limited to **color-property conflicts** and **3B–10B** models; generalization to shape/size/spatial conflicts and frontier-scale models is untested. Interventions target only the **last token position**, potentially missing image-token-processing components. The fairly loose PCA ±2σ classification and per-model architectural differences mean the "shared circuit" is partly a behavioral/functional abstraction rather than an identical anatomical one. Effect sizes vary across architectures (e.g., Qwen-VL 3B shows strong MLP effects absent in PaliGemma/LLaVA).

## Strengths
- Clean separation of sufficiency (patching) and necessity (ablation), with consistent cross-architecture findings.
- The routing-vs-writing functional decomposition is intuitive and well-supported by logit-lens evidence.
- Honest about limitations and architecture-specific routing implementation differences.
- Practical implications clearly stated for multimodal reliability and controllable reasoning.

## Limitations
- Restricted to color conflicts on a single dataset; other conflict types (shape, size, spatial relations) untested.
- 3B–10B parameter range only; larger models may develop different strategies.
- Last-token-only interventions may miss earlier sequence components (e.g., image-token processing).
- Modest correctly-conflicting sample sizes per model (as low as 73).
- Functional "shared circuit" abstracts over real architectural divergence in routing implementation.

## Relevance
这项工作与机制可解释性/因果电路研究高度相关：它通过激活修补与消融定位了 VLM 中"感知-知识冲突"的稀疏因果电路（路由头+写入头），为理解多模态模型内部信息流仲裁提供了组件级的因果中介分析，是 unimodal 因果追踪（如 ROME）向多模态扩展的典型案例。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Does the routing→writing circuit generalize to non-color conflicts (shape, count, spatial relations) and to reasoning tasks beyond single-token color identification?
- How does the circuit scale to 30B+ or frontier VLMs — does the 2.5–4.8% sparsity hold, grow, or restructure?
- Are the identified prior-override heads task-specific or reusable across different knowledge-retrieval demands?
- Could targeted editing/strengthening of the writing heads reliably enforce knowledge-grounded answers under conflicting visual input in deployment?
- Do the two routing regimes (attention-redistribution vs. representation-modulation) converge under training distribution or fine-tuning?
- How do these circuits interact with the earlier image-token-processing positions that this last-token analysis does not capture?
