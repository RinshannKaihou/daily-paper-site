01

Methodology

observational · variance partition · not a treatment effect

An observational measurement study over 930,292 agent-authored PRs across 116,211 repositories (33,596 PRs / 2,807 repos in the curated ≥100-star subset), drawn from AIDev, AgenticFlict, and GitHub API fetches.

Integration friction is operationalized through seven constructs across four facets — Timing (deliberation / resolution latency), Effort (review rounds, comment volume), Contention (changes requested, conflict incidence), and Outcome (rejection).

A multilevel model with a per-repository random intercept splits between-repo variance (τ²) from within-repo variance (σ²); the ICC = τ²/(τ²+σ²) is the operational measure of non-reducibility. Four nested models add controls progressively (M0→M3); for the decisive specificity test, human PRs are fetched for the same 404 repositories and re-processed identically.

03

Strengths & Limitations

honest framing · necessary ≠ causal signature

✓Strengths

  • Metric-independent, threshold-free definition of emergence that sidesteps the "emergence is a metric artifact" critique.
  • Matched within-repository human baseline cleanly isolates the agent-specific effect from generic repository heterogeneity.
  • Convergent evidence across seven constructs and four activity cutoffs makes a single-measurement artifact unlikely.
  • Translates a statistical result into actionable, telemetry-derivable governance practices.
  • Explicitly labels the finding a necessary — not sufficient — signature of emergence.

!Limitations

  • Observational, not causal — cannot fully rule out unmeasured stable repository confounders; stronger causal measures deferred to future work.
  • Agent-to-agent interaction only partly observable — three agents operate through human GitHub accounts.
  • AgenticFlict replay introduces survivorship bias; conflict incidence is agent-only (can't be reconstructed for historical human PRs).
  • Limited to open-source repositories and five agents — a documented external-validity scope limit.
  • Multi-agent metric reflects today's fragmented tool market and would narrow under single-orchestrator consolidation.

Questions for Further Exploration

open threads · non-linear jump
Q1 · CAUSALITY

Would stronger causal-emergence measures (Hoel et al. effective information; Barnett & Seth dynamical independence) confirm the non-reducibility signal under controlled or longitudinal intervention?

Q2 · SYMPTOMS

How do the two unmeasured symptoms — erosion of shared understanding and unrecorded macro-intent — correlate with the integration-friction ICC?

Q3 · GENERALIZATION

Does the ~2× agent-vs-human concentration replicate in closed-source / enterprise repos and with consolidated orchestrators (e.g., a single Claude Code with sub-agents)?

Q4 · PREDICTION

Can a repository-level friction dashboard predict downstream harm (defects, reverts, churn) better than per-agent capability metrics?

Q5 · CONFOUNDERS

What governance & application-domain confounders, if measured, could absorb part of the surviving repository variance?

Q6 · GOVERNANCE

If a benchmark score earned in isolation doesn't predict in-repo behavior, what telemetry should replace the SWE-bench-style leaderboards?
