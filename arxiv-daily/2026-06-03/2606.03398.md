> **一句话总结：** 本文在仅含单层的编码器-解码器Transformer上，通过线性探测提取Shuffle-k语言栈深度的主表征方向，并证明消融该方向会导致序列准确率断崖式崩溃至接近0%，而随机方向消融毫无影响，从而为栈表征的因果必要性提供了决定性证据。

> **TL;DR:** Using a single-layer encoder-only transformer trained on counter languages (Dyck-1 and Shuffle-k), the author trains linear probes to extract principal stack-depth representation directions from hidden states via SVD, then ablates these directions at inference time. Targeted ablation causes sequential accuracy to collapse to near 0%, while random-direction ablation leaves performance intact, providing decisive causal evidence that stack representations are not merely learned but are necessary for the model's computation.

## 核心贡献

- 首次为Transformer在计数语言中学习到的栈表征提供严格的因果证据，超越了以往仅停留在相关性层面的分析
- 提出一套可复现的干预流程：线性探测→SVD提取主方向→定向消融，用于验证表征的因果必要性
- 证明随机方向消融对模型性能毫无影响，而栈深度主方向的消融导致序列准确率断崖式崩溃，排除了表征破坏是"附带损伤"的可能性
- 在k=2,4,6,8的Shuffle-k语言上均验证了该因果效应，表明结果具有一定泛化性
- 使用极小规模的可控模型（单层、64维隐藏层、4头注意力），降低了复现门槛，突出了现象本身而非模型规模的贡献

## Key Contributions

- Provides rigorous causal evidence (not merely correlational) that transformers learn stack-like representations when trained on counter languages
- Proposes a reproducible intervention pipeline: linear probing → SVD extraction of principal direction → targeted ablation, for validating causal necessity of learned representations
- Demonstrates that random-direction ablation has no effect on performance, while ablation of the stack-depth principal direction causes sequential accuracy to collapse — ruling out the possibility that the effect is merely collateral representational damage
- Validates the causal effect across Shuffle-k for k = 2, 4, 6, 8, showing some generalization
- Uses a tiny, controllable model (single layer, d_model=64, 4 attention heads), lowering the reproduction barrier and highlighting the phenomenon over scale

## Methodology

The study uses **counter languages** as a controlled testbed: **Dyck-1** (balanced parentheses) and **Shuffle-k** (interleaving of k independent Dyck-1 strings over disjoint alphabets). The model is an **encoder-only transformer with causal attention mask** trained for next-token prediction over Shuffle-k strings. It uses embedding dimension 32, hidden dimension 64, a **single layer with 4 attention heads**, feedforward dimension 64, and learned positional encodings. The decoder is a linear layer with sigmoid activation; training uses MSE loss with RMSProp (lr 5×10⁻³, batch size 32, 10,000 sequences of length 2–50, 80/20 split). The model achieves perfect validation accuracy by the 25th epoch for k = 2 through 8.

Two metrics are used: **positional accuracy** (fraction of valid token positions with correct predictions) and **sequential accuracy** (fraction of entirely valid strings — a single wrong token invalidates the whole string).

**Probe design:** For each stack i, a linear classifier probe predicts stack depth depthᵢ(t) from hidden states hₜ⁽ˡ⁾ at layer l. A control probe is trained on randomly permuted labels (Hewitt & Liang, 2019), and **selectivity** is defined as the accuracy gap between the experimental and control probes. Reported selectivities range from 48.8% (k=1) to 85.8% (k=6, Stack 2), indicating genuine encoding of stack depth.

**Intervention:** The probe weight matrix W ∈ R^(C×d_model) undergoes SVD (W = UΣV†). The top right singular vector w = V[0] (||w|| = 1) is extracted as the principal representation direction. At inference time, hidden states are ablated via h′ = h − α(w·h)w for α ∈ {0, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 1}. A critical control is also run: ablating random directions w ~ Uniform(S^(d_model−1)). The ablation is applied only to the final hidden states before the decoder head; the transformer's internal computations are **not** re-run.

## Results & Highlights

- **Targeted ablation** of the principal stack-depth direction causes positional accuracy to dip progressively with α, and **sequential accuracy to collapse** as α increases
- **Random-direction ablation** has **no effect** on either positional or sequential accuracy — a strong control that isolates the causal role of the specific stack representation
- The heightened sensitivity of sequential accuracy is expected: modest per-position errors compound to catastrophic full-string failure
- Results are reported for Shuffle-6 in the main text and reproduced for k = 2, 4, 8 in Appendix A, showing consistency across different numbers of interleaved stacks
- The sharp collapse suggests an **absence of redundant alternative encodings** that could compensate for the loss of the stack-depth direction

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The core conceptual advance is moving from "representation exists" to "representation is causally necessary." This is an important step in mechanistic interpretability, where correlational probing often outpaces causal validation. However, the technical toolkit (linear probes + SVD + ablation) is entirely standard in the field; the novelty lies in applying it to answer a question left open by Tiwari et al. (2025). As a workshop paper (5 pages), the scope is appropriately bounded.

**Rigor:** The experimental design is clean and well-motivated. The use of formal languages provides mathematically precise ground-truth stack semantics. The random-direction ablation control is crucial and well-executed — without it, the near-0% accuracy collapse could be dismissed as generic representational destruction. A limitation is that ablation is applied only at the final hidden states before the decoder, not mid-network; the author acknowledges this as future work. It would also strengthen the paper to see ablation of the *second* principal direction (or other task-irrelevant directions) as an additional control.

**Reproducibility:** Excellent. The model is tiny (single layer, d_model=64), the training setup is fully specified, and the intervention is mathematically straightforward. The only barrier is the absence of released code, which would make replication nearly immediate. The author should consider releasing the training pipeline and probe code.

**Significance:** For the interpretability community, this is a valuable data point: transformers can learn not just correlates of algorithmic structure, but causally necessary implementations of that structure. It adds to the growing body of evidence that transformers are capable of learning genuine algorithmic primitives (stacks, counters) when the task demands them. Whether this extends to natural language hierarchical structure remains an important open question.

**Red flags:**
- Single-author student paper at a workshop — while not a quality judgment per se, it means the work has not undergone the same scrutiny as a full conference paper
- The ablation is only at the final layer; it does not establish whether the stack representation is computed and used throughout the network or merely read out at the end
- No discussion of what happens when α > 1 or when multiple stack directions are ablated simultaneously
- The model achieves perfect accuracy, which is expected for these formal languages — but also means we are studying a fully converged, potentially overfitted memorization solution rather than a generalizing one
- No analysis of whether the principal direction is truly encoding *depth* or merely *validity* (a binary "is the string still valid?" signal)

## Strengths

- Clean, focused research question with a well-executed causal framing
- Strong experimental control: random-direction ablation convincingly isolates the causal effect
- Uses tiny, fully specified models that are easy to replicate
- Formal languages provide mathematically rigorous ground truth
- The accuracy-collapse result is visually and conceptually compelling
- Builds rigorously on prior work and appropriately extends it
- Workshop-appropriate scope: does one thing and does it clearly

## Limitations

- Ablation is applied only at the final hidden states, not propagated through the network; this isolates the decoder's dependence but does not fully characterize how the stack representation is computed
- No mid-network intervention or analysis of layer-wise stack encoding
- Single model scale and architecture tested; generalization to deeper/wider transformers is unverified
- No additional controls beyond random directions (e.g., ablating the second or third principal direction)
- Perfect validation accuracy on formal languages may reflect memorization of the training distribution rather than learned general algorithms
- No code or pretrained model released
- The binary/depth-class probe formulation may conflate stack depth with a simpler validity signal
- Workshop paper length (5 pages) limits depth of discussion and additional analyses

## Relevance

本文对Transformer内部机制的形式化理解具有重要价值，特别适合关注机械可解释性、形式语言与神经网络表征之间关系的读者。其因果分析框架——线性探测结合SVD提取主方向并进行定向消融——为研究LLM内部算法结构提供了简洁而有力的方法论范式，可直接推广至其他表征因果性验证场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Mid-network intervention:** What happens when ablated states are passed back through the feedforward network and attention layers, rather than only to the decoder? Does the network have error-correcting capacity, or does the collapse propagate?
2. **Layer-wise analysis:** At which layers do stack-depth representations first emerge, and are they maintained, refined, or transformed as depth increases? Is the causal dependency distributed or localized?
3. **Direction specificity:** Does ablating the *second* or *third* principal direction (from SVD) also impair performance, or is the effect uniquely concentrated in the top singular vector? This would clarify whether the encoding is truly one-dimensional or distributed across multiple directions.
4. **Generalization to natural language:** Can a similar probe-and-ablate protocol identify and causally validate stack-like representations in transformers trained on natural language, particularly for nested syntactic structures?
5. **Scale and architecture sensitivity:** Do deeper models learn *redundant* stack encodings such that ablating one direction is compensated by others? The current result suggests no redundancy in this tiny model, but larger models might differ.
6. **Depth vs. validity:** Is the probe truly decoding stack depth, or merely a binary "still valid" signal? A finer-grained analysis correlating probe predictions with exact depth values could disambiguate this.
7. **Training dynamics:** At what epoch does the causal stack representation emerge, and does it coincide with the model's accuracy jump or precede it?
