The Shape of Addition: Geometric Structures of Arithmetic in Large Language Models

arXiv: 2606.03645

Date: 2026-06-03

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03645

一句话总结： 该研究通过对多操作数加法任务中LLM残差流激活的逐位分析，发现了"等原始和轨迹"（IRST）这一层次化几何流形结构，并提出了噪声量化模型，将算术错误重新定义为连续进位势跨越量化阈值时的几何滑移，同时设计了基于几何一致性的推理时自校正方法。
TL;DR: By analyzing residual-stream activations during multi-operand addition digit-by-digit, the authors discover the Iso-Raw-Sum Trajectory (IRST)—a hierarchical geometric manifold—and propose the Noisy Quantization Model, reframing arithmetic errors as geometric slippages of a continuous Carry Potential across quantization thresholds, validated through an inference-time geometric consistency check.

核心贡献

  • 发现了等原始和轨迹（IRST）：LLM在多操作数加法中的内部表示形成了一个层次化几何流形，宏观上由10个数字盆地（digit basins）构成骨架，微观上由输入进位状态（input carry）决定的纤维进一步分层。
  • 提出了噪声量化模型（Noisy Quantization Model）：将算术错误重新概念化为"几何滑移"（Geometric Slippages）——内部神经噪声推动连续的进位势（Carry Potential）跨越相邻数字盆地之间的量化阈值。
  • 解释了探针多功能性（Probe Versatility）现象：轻量级探针（如逻辑回归、MLP）能从单一激活向量中同时解码多种信号（真实值、模型输出、输入进位、原始和等），这是因为流形的拓扑可分离性而非简单的信息可提取性。
  • 设计了双流一致性检测方法：在推理时通过强制局部信号（原始和）与全局信号（进位势）之间的逻辑对齐，有效检测并纠正量化失败，显著恢复模型性能。

Key Contributions

  • Iso-Raw-Sum Trajectory (IRST): The internal representations during multi-operand addition form a hierarchical geometric manifold with a macroscopic backbone of ten digit basins and a microscopic texture stratified by input carry states.
  • Noisy Quantization Model: Arithmetic errors are reframed as "Geometric Slippages"—instances where internal neural noise pushes a continuous latent Carry Potential across the quantization thresholds between adjacent digit basins.
  • Geometric Elucidation of Probe Versatility: Lightweight probes (logistic regression, MLPs) can simultaneously decode diverse signals (ground truth, model output, input carry, raw sum) from a single activation vector because of the manifold's topological separability, not merely information extractability.
  • Dual-Stream Consistency Check: An inference-time intervention method that enforces logical alignment between decoded local (Raw Sum) and global (Carry Potential) signals, effectively detecting and correcting quantization failures and significantly recovering performance.

Methodology

The study focuses on multi-operand addition of three (or more) 10-digit integers using the Qwen3-4B model (36 transformer layers). Unlike prior work that analyzed only the first output token, the authors extract and analyze residual stream activations at every generated digit position.

Dataset & Setup: They construct a dataset of N = 10,000 addition problems. For each problem and generation position p, they extract hidden states h_p^(l) (primarily from the final layer L). The arithmetic state at each position is governed by three variables: the Raw Sum r_p (column-wise sum of input digits), the Input Carry c_p (propagated from lower-order positions), and the Ground Truth Digit s_p, related by s_p ≡ (r_p + c_p) (mod 10).

Probing Framework: Lightweight probes (logistic regression, MLPs) are trained on the activation vectors to decode discrete variables (Ground Truth, Model Output, Correctness, Input Carry, Raw Sum) and continuous variables (Carry Potential Φ). The key phenomenon of "probe versatility" is observed—multiple probes trained independently on the same activation vector can all achieve high accuracy.

Dimensionality Reduction: UMAP with cosine distance is used to visualize the high-dimensional activation manifold. The output unembedding vectors of digit tokens 0–9 are included as fixed semantic anchors.

Error Analysis: By analyzing modular arithmetic relations, observed output errors (typically "off-by-one") are mapped to internal carry deviations: underestimation by 1 implies carry leakage (ĉ_p = c_p − 1), while overestimation implies hallucinated carry (ĉ_p = c_p + 1).

Inference-Time Intervention: The dual-stream consistency check independently decodes Raw Sum and Carry Potential from the activation vector, then verifies their logical consistency. Mismatches indicate likely quantization failures, enabling self-correction.

Results & Highlights

  • Hierarchical Manifold Structure: UMAP visualization reveals a macroscopic backbone of ten distinct digit basins (centers anchored near the corresponding unembedding vectors) and a microscopic texture where each basin is further stratified into fibers by input carry state c_p ∈ {0, 1, 2}.
  • IRST Topology: Iso-Raw-Sum Trajectories appear as continuous fibers threading through adjacent digit basins. For example, T_1 (where r_p mod 10 = 1) connects stable nodes (1,1,0,0) ↔ (2,2,1,1) ↔ (3,3,2,2), demonstrating that representations with the same raw sum but different carries form geometrically continuous paths.
  • Error Concentration: Errors (red points in visualizations) concentrate in transition zones between digit basins and between carry fibers—confirming that arithmetic failures are geometric instabilities rather than random noise.
  • Periodic Bathtub Error Distribution: The Noisy Quantization Model accurately predicts a periodic bathtub-shaped error distribution observed empirically, where errors peak at raw sums near quantization thresholds (where the carry potential is ambiguous).
  • Probe Performance Hierarchy: Probing accuracy follows a structural hierarchy determined by manifold topology: Raw Sum and Input Carry are decoded most reliably (topologically most separable), followed by Ground Truth and Model Output.
  • Inference-Time Recovery: The dual-stream consistency check significantly recovers arithmetic performance by leveraging the fact that correct latent signals (Raw Sum, Carry Potential) persist in the residual stream even when the model outputs the wrong token.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: The paper makes a genuine conceptual advance by moving beyond descriptive probing to a mechanistic geometric theory. The Iso-Raw-Sum Trajectory and Noisy Quantization Model provide a principled framework that explains not just that probes work, but why errors occur in specific patterns. The geometric reframing of arithmetic errors as quantization failures of a continuous latent variable is elegant and provides testable predictions.

Rigor: The methodology is systematic—10,000 problems, multiple probe architectures, validation across digit positions, and explicit validation of high-dimensional geometric consistency (Appendix C). The mathematical formalism (modular arithmetic relations, state tuples, carry potential) is clean and well-defined. The use of UMAP for visualization is standard, though the authors acknowledge anchors are included for semantic grounding and validate that this doesn't distort intrinsic geometry.

Reproducibility: Good. The code is open-sourced, the model (Qwen3-4B) is publicly available, and the task (3-term 10-digit addition) is fully specified. The main potential concern is that some geometric claims rely on UMAP projections, which can distort global structure; however, the authors explicitly validate geometric consistency in high-dimensional space (Appendix C).

Significance: The work is significant for both interpretability and practical reliability. It bridges the gap between "models encode correct information internally" and "models fail to output it" by providing a mechanistic geometric explanation. The inference-time correction method demonstrates practical utility.

Red Flags / Limitations:

  • The study is limited to one model (Qwen3-4B) and one task (multi-operand addition). Generalization to other models, scales, or arithmetic operations is not demonstrated.
  • The geometric framework relies heavily on the specific structure of positional numeral systems (base-10 with carries). Extension to non-positional or non-arithmetic reasoning is unclear.
  • The inference-time correction requires training additional probes and running dual decoders, adding computational overhead that may limit deployment.
  • The paper assumes models generally compute local raw sums correctly (r̂_p ≈ r_p)—while validated in Appendix D, this is a strong assumption that may not hold for all models or more complex operations.

Strengths

  • Elegant geometric theory: The IRST framework transforms arithmetic errors from a mystery into a mechanistically explainable phenomenon with clear geometric intuition.
  • Strong empirical grounding: The connection between UMAP visualizations, probe versatility, and error patterns is compelling and cross-validated.
  • Practical intervention: The dual-stream consistency check demonstrates that theoretical insights can translate into effective inference-time corrections.
  • Clear mathematical formalism: The state tuple X_p and modular arithmetic framework make the analysis precise and falsifiable.
  • Good visualization practices: The figures effectively communicate the hierarchical manifold structure and connect abstract geometry to concrete error examples.

Limitations

  • Single-model scope: All experiments use Qwen3-4B (4B parameters). Whether the same geometric structure exists in larger models, different architectures, or other model families is unknown.
  • Single-task focus: Only multi-operand addition is studied. The geometric framework may not generalize to subtraction, multiplication, division, or non-arithmetic reasoning tasks.
  • UMAP-dependent visualization: While high-dimensional validation is mentioned (Appendix C), the primary narrative relies on 2D projections, which inherently involve distortion.
  • Overhead of inference-time intervention: Training and running multiple probes for consistency checking adds computational cost that may be prohibitive for real-time applications.
  • Assumption of correct raw sum computation: The framework assumes local addition is performed correctly and errors arise only from carry quantization—this may not hold universally.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the IRST geometric structure persist across different model scales (e.g., 1B to 70B+) and architectures (e.g., dense vs. MoE), or is it specific to Qwen3-4B's training dynamics?
  2. Can the Noisy Quantization Model and geometric slippage framework be extended to non-arithmetic reasoning tasks where "carry-like" propagation structures may exist (e.g., logical inference, chain-of-thought reasoning)?
  3. How does the manifold geometry evolve during training? Does the IRST structure emerge gradually, and can training interventions shape it to improve arithmetic reliability?
  4. Can the dual-stream consistency check be generalized into a lightweight, model-agnostic inference-time correction mechanism that doesn't require task-specific probe training?
  5. Do models trained with specific arithmetic curricula (e.g., explicit algorithmic reasoning training) exhibit different manifold geometries with fewer transition zones or sharper quantization boundaries?
