> **一句话总结：** 本文将机器"遗忘"重新定义为"恢复到匹配的重训练参考分布"，并在一个受控的 nonce-fact 测试床上系统审计了评估遗忘质量的标准——发现业界常用的 trained-probe oracle-KL 准则反而奖励残余知识、绝对阈值认证连参考模型自身都通不过；最终证明仅有一个锚定基模型、基于 held-out paraphrase 的 rank 筛选能作为"必要"测试存活下来，而任何前向无预言机认证都可被固定的 logit 抑制攻击击穿约四分之一单元格。

> **TL;DR:** This paper recasts LLM machine unlearning as *distribution restoration* toward a matched retraining reference, then audits the evaluation instruments on their own merits across 45 model-seed cells (Qwen2.5, SmolLM2, Pythia, GPT-2, OLMo-2). It shows the common trained-probe oracle-KL criterion rewards residual held-out knowledge (candidates it rates "adequate" sit −2.82 nats *below* the never-learned level), that absolute retain/round-trip thresholds are failed by the reference itself (certifies in only 1/45 cells), and that only a base-anchored held-out rank screen survives as a *necessary* test (rejects injected model 45/45, accepts reference 44/45); a damage-relative recalibration certifies a small subset (15/45 cells, 0.80 nats from the reference vs 5.17 for the trained-probe criterion), while a fixed-magnitude logit-suppression attack defeats the full forward battery in 12/45 cells (Clopper-Pearson upper bound 39.6%), scoping oracle-free certification to "methods-as-produced" rather than a universal guarantee.

## 核心贡献

1. **评估标准的批判性审计**：在受控、剂量匹配的 nonce-fact 注入测试床上，作者把业界作为"地面真值"使用的 trained-probe oracle-KL 准则当作被审计对象（而非默认正确），发现它会把残余知识评为合格——被它判为"adequate"的候选，其 held-out forget 事实比"从未学过"水平低 −2.82 nats（cluster-CI [−3.16, −2.48]），且其偏好选择距重训练参考 5.17 nats。
2. **绝对认证被其自身参考证伪**：固定的 retain/round-trip 阈值低于任务操作点——构造上完美保留 R 的注入模型 Minj 在 41/45 单元格通不过 retain 阈值，31/45 通不过自身 round-trip；重训练参考仅在 1/45 单元格通过全部。这表明"没有方法认证"是关于阈值而非方法的陈述。
3. **一个经校验的选择性筛选**：基于"锚定基模型 M0 + held-out paraphrase"的单边精确 rank（Mann-Whitney）筛选，在密封挑战面板上拒绝注入模型 45/45、接受参考 44/45，并通过一个分布"接缝"部分检测 entity-routing 抑制（35/45）。这是一个带测量灵敏度的*必要*测试，而非充分性认证。
4. **损伤相对的重新校准**：把阈值锚定到参考自身操作点、容差由独立重训练抽取的复制噪声（δf 跨家族 0.82–2.92 nats，中位 0.93）决定，得到一个选择性"部分肯定"——15/45 单元格、38/820 候选认证，其余弃权；非弃权选择距同抽参考在 0.80 nats 内（vs trained-probe 准则的 5.17）。
5. **对抗边界**：在相同挑战面板上，固定幅度 logit 抑制攻击（forget-answer logits −10）在 12/45 单元格击穿整条前向电池（Clopper-Pearson 上界 39.6%），证明前向 only 认证不具对抗稳健性，并用有限查询不可能性论证解释原因。
6. **可识别性定理**：用因果可识别性语言刻画了"oracle-free 选择在何时可能"——当且仅当遗忘阈值 τ* 可从 oracle-free 可观测量中恢复；TOFU 是其预测的边界失败案例（可由 retain 推断的"风格可推"事实使 (CE) 失败）。

## Key Contributions

1. **Protocol critique of the de-facto standard.** Treating the field's trained-probe oracle-KL criterion (KL(candidate‖Moracle) on F+R trained probes) as an audited object rather than ground truth, the authors show it *rewards* residual knowledge: candidates it rates adequate score held-out forget facts −2.82 nats (cluster-CI [−3.16, −2.48]) below the never-learned level, and its preferred selections sit 5.17 nats from the reference's held-out position.
2. **Absolute certificate falsified by its own reference.** Fixed retain/round-trip thresholds (≤0.5 nats) sit below the operating point: the injected model Minj (which retains R by construction) fails the retain threshold in 41/45 cells and its own round-trip in 31/45; the retraining reference fully certifies in only 1/45. "No method certifies" is thus a statement about the bar, not the methods.
3. **A validated selective screen.** A one-sided exact Mann-Whitney rank test of base-anchored (to M0) per-fact held-out forget deltas vs matched never-learned probe deltas, validated on a sealed challenge panel: rejects the injected model in 45/45 cells, accepts the reference in 44/45, and partially detects entity-routing suppression through a distributional "drift seam" in 35/45. A necessary test with measured sensitivity, not a sufficiency certificate.
4. **Damage-relative recalibration.** Re-anchoring thresholds to the reference's own operating point at the tolerance retraining noise cannot beat (δf = 0.82–2.92 nats, median 0.93) yields a selective partial positive: 38/820 candidates certify in 15/45 cells; abstention elsewhere; non-abstaining picks lie within retraining noise (0.80 vs 5.17 nats for the trained-probe criterion on the same cells).
5. **Adversarial boundary.** A fixed-magnitude logit suppression attack (−10 on forget-answer logits) defeats the entire forward battery in 12/45 cells (Clopper-Pearson upper bound 39.6%), motivating a finite-query impossibility boundary and scoping the guarantee to "methods-as-produced."
6. **Identifiability theorem.** A formal (TR/RS/CE) result delimiting when oracle-free selection is oracle-consistent, with TOFU as the predicted boundary (inferable facts make the counterfactual-exchangeability condition fail).

## Methodology

**Controlled testbed with a matched retraining reference.** From a base model M0, continued pretraining co-acquires a forget set F and a retain-acquired set R of nonce facts (dose-matched; |F|=|R|=4; wikitext retain mix at 3:1), yielding Minj. The matched retraining reference Moracle replays the identical stream *without* F (inject R only) — a model that never saw F but has the same retain competence. Because bf16 training is nondeterministic, each reference draw is one realization; the paper quantifies the indistinguishability tolerance δequiv = 0.93 nats at P90 (per-family 0.82–2.92) from three independent redraws. Co-acquiring R is essential: with only F in the stream, checkpoint-rollback (=M0) would trivially "forget," but co-acquisition makes rollback destroy retain (retain-NLL 6.6 vs oracle 2.4).

**Methods compared** (each from Minj at matched compute, ~110–120 steps, full forget/retain Pareto fronts):
- Restoration-style: path-specific KL reversion (full-parameter; forget→M0, retain→Minj), gate_proj mask reversion (SwiGLU only), task-vector negation (Minj − c·(MFonly − M0)).
- Suppression-style: gradient ascent (GA)+retain, NPO+retain.
- Baseline: checkpoint rollback (=M0).

**Oracle-free selector.** (1) Re-acquire each candidate C on F to convergence → reacquireF(C); (2) measure round-trip residual cert(C) = KL(reacquireF(C)‖Minj) on retain+wikitext; (3) qualify via the base-anchored under-forgetting rank test against never-learned probes; (4) select arg min over survivors. No oracle, gold posterior, or probe labels enter selection.

**Sealed challenge panel.** A frozen audit template pool scored exactly once, passing through known-label challenges blind: M0, Minj, reference, logit suppression (−10), entity router, embedding corruption, and weight-space interpolations λ·Moracle + (1−λ)·Minj.

**Models.** Qwen2.5-0.5B/1.5B, SmolLM2-360M/1.7B, Pythia-410M/1.4B, GPT-2-medium/large, OLMo-2-1B (five families; SwiGLU and GELU; 45 model-seed cells; 820 candidates). External validation on TOFU (Phi-1.5, forget10/retain90). All runs on a single 48GB RTX 6000 Ada.

## Results & Highlights

1. **Trained-probe criterion rewards residual knowledge.** Across the 45-cell matrix, candidates the trained-probe oracle-KL criterion rates "adequate" score held-out forget facts a mean of **−2.82 nats (cluster-CI [−3.16, −2.48])** *below* the never-learned level — i.e. they retain paraphrase-recoverable knowledge of F while matching the reference on the phrasings the criterion queries. (Abstract & §3.1.)
2. **Absolute certification fails its own reference.** The injected model Minj (retains R by construction) fails the fixed retain threshold (≤0.5) in **41/45** cells and its own round-trip self-closure in **31/45**; the retraining reference fully certifies in only **1/45**. Only **3/820** candidates satisfy the absolute (screen ∧ retain ≤0.5 ∧ round-trip ≤0.5) bar matrix-wide. (§5, §3.1.)
3. **Base-anchored screen is a validated necessary test.** On the sealed challenge suite it rejects the injected model in **45/45** cells and accepts the reference in **44/45**; it partially detects entity-routing suppression via a drift seam in **35/45**. Residual-based selection never picks a suppression method (0/45; per-cell binomial upper bound 6.4%). (§6.)
4. **Recalibrated certificate: selective partial positive.** **38/820** candidates certify in **15/45** cells (gate_proj mask family leads at 16/100 = 16%); in the 15 non-abstaining cells the recalibrated pick lies **0.80 nats** from the same-draw reference vs **5.17 nats** for the trained-probe criterion's preferred candidate (Table 2; supporting comparison, not head-to-head). (§7.)
5. **Adversarial boundary.** Fixed-magnitude logit suppression (−10) defeats the full forward battery in **12/45** cells (Clopper-Pearson upper bound **39.6%**); Qwen and OLMo cells dominate evasions. Forward-only certification is not adversarially sound. (§6.)
6. **TOFU external validation.** Restoration (KL-reversion) oracle-KL ≈**0.1** vs suppression **0.7–1.2**; the forget-qualified screen excludes **16/16** candidates per seed (smallest p ≈ 7×10⁻¹⁵) because TOFU's inferable facts make the oracle itself unreachable — the predicted identifiability failure. (§8.)
7. **Headroom estimator.** The oracle-free Ĝ = NLLM0(F|evidence) − NLLMinj(F) tracks the true headroom G at **Spearman 0.97** (MAE 1.7 nats) on a graded-inferability axis, giving a deployable regime detector. (§9.)

The pairing of a business-school ML researcher and a mathematician is consistent with the paper's tone: a formal identifiability theorem (causal-inference framing) combined with a painstaking empirical audit.

## Critical Assessment

The paper's central contribution is not a new unlearning *method* but a **methodological audit** of how the field evaluates unlearning, and it is devastating in a precise, quantified way:

- **The trained-probe oracle-KL criterion is shown to be actively misleading**, not merely imperfect. It rewards candidates that demonstrably retain paraphrase-recoverable knowledge (−2.82 nats below never-learned), because it only queries the phrasings that appeared in injection/unlearning losses. This is a structural critique: any method that suppresses only its own training phrasings (GA/NPO: 77–87% of candidates excluded by held-out screen) will look good on the very surface used to evaluate it.
- **The "absolute certificate" is retired by its own logic.** The paper runs the retraining reference and Minj through the full battery and shows the thresholds lie below the task's operating point — Minj, which retains R by construction, fails the retain threshold in 41/45 cells. This is a clean self-falsification that does not depend on trusting any candidate method.
- **The round-trip floor is diagnosed**, not hidden: it is largely reacquisition-budget drift (three of five families close their own loop <0.5 at the shortest budget; OLMo-2 shows intrinsic path-dependence at 1.4–2.1). The recalibration subtracts the common-mode floor.
- **The screen is honestly scoped as necessary, not sufficient**: among 322 screen survivors, the median forget-probe gap is 1.93 nats (above δequiv = 0.93), and only 28.0% are TOST-equivalent to never-learned — so passing the screen means only "not demonstrably retaining," and the recalibration adds an explicit equivalence axis rather than treating survival as success.
- **The adversarial result is reported as a limitation, not buried.** The 12/45 logit-suppression evasion is the empirical face of the finite-query impossibility argument and the identifiability theorem; the paper repeatedly states its guarantee is "an empirical selective test for methods-as-produced, not a universal certificate."

The weakest link is the recalibration's **tolerance concentration**: 29 of 38 certifications come from the Qwen family, whose retraining replication is noisiest (δf = 2.92), and a global-minimum tolerance (δ = 0.82) collapses the count to 4 candidates in 4 cells. The paper discloses this rather than hand-waving, and explicitly reframes the robust claim as "selection quality within retraining noise" rather than the raw certified count.

## Strengths

- **Self-auditing design**: running the reference itself through every proposed criterion is a genuinely novel and powerful validation move that exposes threshold mis-scaling without trusting any unlearning method.
- **Distinguishes restoration from suppression operationally**: restoration methods (KL-reversion ≈2–4 oracle-KL, task-vector) stay ~2× closer to the oracle than suppression (GA/NPO ≈5–7) across 3 seeds and a 32-configuration fair sweep; the dichotomy is clean (loss-based methods suppress only their own phrasings; weight-space methods generalize but damage retain).
- **Formal foundations**: the identifiability theorem (Theorem 1) + finite-sample certifiability bound (Proposition 1) give the empirical findings theoretical teeth — TOFU's failure is *predicted* by Proposition 2, not retrofitted.
- **Honest scope language throughout**: "necessary not sufficient," "supporting comparison not head-to-head," "methods-as-produced not universal certificate," "abstention is selectivity by design, not failure."
- **Reproducibility**: all open-weight named checkpoints, frozen pre-registration, committed scripts, published audit-pool hash, fully specified hyperparameters.

## Limitations

- **Threat model is narrow.** Certification is an empirical test for *non-adaptive, methods-as-produced* candidates; an adaptive bilevel adversary optimizing against the round-trip residual is explicitly untested, and the forward battery is defeated by logit suppression in 12/45 cells.
- **Retrospective, veto-less recalibration.** The damage-relative certificate is evaluated on stored candidates without stored relearning trajectories, so the relearn-veto axis is absent; a prospective run with all axes is future work.
- **Small forget set (m=4) and few redraws (3).** Per-family δf (0.82–2.92) is itself uncertain, and most certifications concentrate in the noisiest family; a coverage-calibrated tolerance from more redraws would change the certified counts. The 16-fact breadth panel was deliberately deprioritized.
- **Small models (≤1.7B; Phi-1.5 for TOFU).** Scaling to larger instruction-tuned models is explicitly future work; nonce facts are synthetic by construction (necessary to make the matched reference constructible).
- **Selection is oracle-free only for counterfactual facts.** Inferable-fact benchmarks (TOFU) need the reference; the impossibility result concerns oracle-free, forward-only, black-box protocols over an unrestricted candidate class and does not preclude certification under white-box or known-pipeline access.

## Relevance

This paper is highly relevant to anyone working on **LLM unlearning evaluation, right-to-erasure compliance, or model-editing benchmarks**. Its practical takeaways:

- **Stop trusting trained-probe oracle-KL alone.** If you only measure forgetting on the phrasings used in the unlearning loss, you will reward methods that merely suppress those phrasings while retaining paraphrase-recoverable knowledge. Add held-out paraphrase evaluation anchored to the base model.
- **A "round-trip / relearning" certificate is necessary but its floor is budget-driven.** When comparing unlearning methods, report the reacquisition-budget curve and subtract the common-mode floor; a fixed ≤0.5 threshold will reject even the gold reference.
- **The screen is cheap and deployable.** The base-anchored Mann-Whitney rank test only needs Minj, the base model M0, held-out forget paraphrases, and never-learned probes — no oracle. Use it as a *necessary* filter (reject candidates demonstrably retaining F) before any certification claim.
- **For real-world (inferable) facts, oracle-free selection provably fails** — this is a design-time signal that TOFU-style benchmarks need the reference, and that "counterfactual vs inferable" should be diagnosed (via Ĝ) before choosing an evaluation protocol.
- **Adversarial robustness is a separate axis** that forward-only behavioral tests cannot provide; quantization/relearning attacks require relearning axes or white-box access.

For the broader ML-safety/privacy community, the paper is a cautionary methodological template: instruments (evaluation criteria) must be audited against the very ground truth they appeal to, and a certificate that cannot answer "uncertified" is not a certificate.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Prospective certification with the relearn-veto axis.** The recalibration is retrospective and veto-less; would a prospective run with stored relearning trajectories and all four axes change the 15/45 certification count or the selection-quality advantage over the trained-probe criterion?
2. **Scaling to instruction-tuned 7B+ models.** Does the restoration-vs-suppression dichotomy and the screen's true-accept rate (44/45) hold at scale, or do larger models' greater paraphrase-robustness change which methods the held-out surface favors?
3. **Coverage-calibrated tolerances from more redraws.** δf is estimated from only 3 redraws; how do the certified counts (38/820) and their family concentration shift with, say, 20–50 independent retraining draws and a data-derived confidence set?
4. **Adaptive bilevel adversary.** A candidate optimized directly against the round-trip residual is "the most natural attack on the construction" and is untested — how large is the gap between fixed-magnitude logit suppression (12/45) and an adaptive attack?
5. **Beyond nonce facts: inferability-aware unlearning.** Can the oracle-free Ĝ headroom estimator be used to *partition* a real forget set into counterfactual (certifiable) vs inferable (needs-reference) subsets, enabling hybrid evaluation protocols on real benchmarks?
6. **Second untouched held-out probe suite.** The authors flag that a standalone selection claim would require a second, untouched held-out probe suite with one candidate frozen per cell *before* that suite is scored — has anyone constructed such a suite, and does the 0.80-vs-5.17 nats advantage survive it?
7. **Relationship to differential privacy.** The paper explicitly disclaims any DP or right-to-erasure guarantee; could the base-anchored screen's necessary-test framing be combined with DP-style bounds to yield a hybrid "necessary + private" certificate?
