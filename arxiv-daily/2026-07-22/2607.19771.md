> **一句话总结：** 本文为 Muon 优化器提出一个"保各向同性的谱上界"（spectral cap）：在每个更新步投影掉一阶 top 奇异方向的增长，理论上证明了在尺度不变网络中 Muon 会比 SGD 更快地推高权重谱范数（t^{1/2} vs t^{1/4}），而该上界因二阶项非负不会冻结训练；并在 nanoGPT FFN、64-expert MoE 路由、bf16 FlashAttention Q/K 三个系统上验证。

> **TL;DR:** A spectral cap for the Muon optimizer projects out the first-order top-singular-direction growth of the data-coupled output covariance each step. Theory shows Muon (via matrix-sign) annihilates SGD's 1/‖W‖_F "brake," growing Frobenius/spectral norm as t^{1/2} vs SGD's t^{1/4}; the cap's non-negative second-order term keeps training learning. Validated on nanoGPT FFN (top-share 0.74→0.30 with no val-loss change), a 64-expert MoE router (reversing L0 rank-1 collapse, top-share 0.995→0.272), and bf16 FlashAttention Q/K (F3 keeps loss ~3.0 stable across the 70K–110K window where the uncapped baseline crashes to 5.18).

## 核心贡献

1. **统一解释框架（理论）**：在尺度不变（scale-invariant）网络中，普通 SGD 携带一个 1/‖W‖_F 的"刹车"项（更新随权重增大而缩小），而 Muon 的矩阵符号（matrix-sign, `msign`）步骤会消除这个刹车——因为 `msign(αM)=msign(M)` 对正标度不变。这导致 Muon 下 Frobenius 范数以 t^{1/2} 增长（SGD 仅 t^{1/4}），谱范数被扰动得更剧烈。
2. **谱上界的二阶非负性**：通过构造对称膨胀矩阵 H(W)，证明对一阶 top 方向做投影（u₁ᵀDv₁=0）后，谱范数的二阶扰动项由 Σⱼ (aⱼ+bⱼ)²/4(σ₁−σⱼ) + (aⱼ−bⱼ)²/4(σ₁+σⱼ) 给出，**每一项都非负**。这是"上界控谱但不冻结训练"的核心——权重仍可通过非 top 方向增长、top 方向旋转、top 方向切换三种机制继续学习。
3. **H∞ 熵视角**：谱上界等价于对权重平方奇异值分布的 Rényi H∞ 熵（= log stable rank）做保护；H₂（participation rank）和 H₁（Shannon）需要不同的投影方向。本文选 H∞ 因为它只需 top 奇异对（power iteration 几步可得），且直接对应三个系统的物理失效（注意力分数=σ₁；MoE rank-1 坍塌=σ₁）。
4. **"数据耦合 K_Y"上界设计**：本文明确指出 K_X≠I（实际网络各向异性），因此正确的上界对象是 K_Y = W K_X Wᵀ 的 top 特征值，而非 W 本身的谱。
5. **三个案例研究的统一算子**：始终是"采样估计 K_Y + power iteration 找 (q,λ₁) + 一阶投影"，开销 O(M·small)，相对前向+反向 <0.003%–5%，可忽略。

## Key Contributions

- **A single explanatory theory**: In scale-invariant networks, Muon's `msign` annihilates SGD's natural 1/‖W‖_F braking factor, producing Frobenius-norm growth of t^{1/2} vs SGD's t^{1/4}, and larger spectral-norm perturbation.
- **Non-negative second-order cap**: Using the symmetric dilation H(W), the authors prove that after projecting out the first-order top-mode growth, the spectral-norm's second-order perturbation term is a sum of non-negative squares. This is why a hard cap on σ₁ does **not** freeze the weight — it converts "pour energy into one direction" into "explore the orthogonal complement."
- **H∞-entropy interpretation**: The spectral cap = Rényi H∞ cap (= stable rank); capping only σ₁ indirectly raises H₂/H₁ because energy redistributes to non-top directions.
- **Data-coupled design**: The correct cap target is K_Y = W K_X Wᵀ (output covariance), not ‖W‖ itself, since K_X is strongly anisotropic in all three applications.
- **Three concrete case studies** with per-step cost ≤5% of forward+backward, demonstrating the cap is decisive exactly at failure margins (MoE L0 rank-1, FA L1 H2 crisis) while leaving the typical case untouched.

## Methodology

**Theory (Section 1):**
- *Assumption 1 (Exact scale invariance)*: L(cW)=L(W) for c>0 — the load-bearing idealization for pre-LN/RMSNorm networks.
- Two corollaries: (B) ⟨W, ∇_W L⟩_F = 0 (gradient orthogonal to the radial/scale direction); (A) ‖∇_W L‖_F ~ 1/‖W‖_F.
- *SGD update*: ‖D_SGD‖_F = O(η/‖W‖_F) — keeps the brake. *Muon update*: D_Muon = −ηs·P_t with P_t = msign(G_t), s = 0.2·max(m,n); since msign is scale-invariant, ‖D_Muon‖_F = O(η) — brake removed.
- Norm-growth ODEs give √R(t) growth (SGD) vs linear R(t) growth (Muon).
- *Empirical sanity check* (nanoGPT step-48K, 24 weights): |c_G| ≈ 0.003–0.005, |c_P| ≈ 0.005–0.010, consistent with approximate scale invariance.

**Cap algorithm (per system):**
1. Sample activations X ∈ R^{M×n} (M=4096 tokens) from the forward cache.
2. Compute Y = XWᵀ, estimate K_Y (or its data-coupled form) without materializing it; use matrix-vector products.
3. Power iteration (4 steps) for top eigenpair (q, λ₁).
4. Form the rank-1 gradient direction B = 2qgᵀ (where g is the most-aligned input direction in the K_X metric); project the candidate update: H ← H − max(0, ⟨B,H⟩−ρλ₁)·B/‖B‖²_F. ρ=0 is a hard cap.

**Cost:** nanoGPT ~31 MFLOPs/step (~0.003%); MoE ~100 MFLOPs; FA ~5 GFLOPs/step (~5%, 288 per-(layer,head) caps).

## Results & Highlights

1. **nanoGPT FFN (Section 2)** — *proof of concept, val loss unchanged*:
   - Data-cap (D1d) lowers the c_proj output K_Y top-share from **0.74 → 0.30** (L4) and **0.992 → 0.704** (L0), while final val loss is essentially identical: **D0=3.8680, D1=3.8763, D1d=3.8742** (Δ < 0.01 nats, below seed noise ~0.05).
   - Participation rank at L1 rises **34 → 96** (nearly 3×) under D1d vs baseline D0.
   - Key design lesson: the W-cap (D1) lowers σ₁(W) by 14–46% but K_Y top-share barely moves (because anisotropic K_X redistributes energy); only the data-coupled K_Y cap (D1d) is on-target.

2. **64-expert MoE router (Section 3)** — *decisive at the rank-1 margin*:
   - Without a cap (G0), layer 0 collapses to a single dominant expert: **K_{z+b} top-share = 0.995, hard-load max = 0.999** (the same expert in every token's top-4), participation rank = 1.0.
   - Selection cap (G3) pushes top-share to **0.272** at L0 and **0.267** at L5 (vs G0's 0.864); λ_max drops **158×** at L0 (82,517 → 521) and **38×** at L5.
   - G3 wins 16 of 18 cells (6 layers × 3 metrics). Final val loss: G0=3.7351, G1=3.6660, G3=3.6920 — all within 0.07 nats; capped methods overtake G0 during LR warmdown.
   - Explains LFB (loss-free bias) failure: at rank-1, 63 of 64 LFB degrees of freedom are wasted.

3. **bf16 FlashAttention Q/K (Section 4)** — *prevents a known crash, no softmax patch needed*:
   - F3 (QK data-cap) keeps loss stable at **~3.0–3.1** across the full 70K–110K crash window, while the uncapped baseline creeps (80K loss 3.50), crashes (104K loss 4.49), and blows up (**120K loss 5.18, 150K loss 7.63**).
   - L1 H2 head (the paper's crisis head) peaks at iter 86K: **λ_max(K_K)=133,208, score.max=27,264** (~half the bf16 ceiling 65,504); the cap trims **q_excess = 22 nats/step** in real time and λ falls to 32K by 120K.
   - Global grad norm: uncapped explodes 8 orders of magnitude (64K=52 → 120K=8.1×10⁸); **F3 stays <0.5 throughout** (brief 86K spike to 1.41, immediately suppressed).
   - Counterfactual diagnostic `excess_mean` quantifies "what the next step would have done" — information the patched-softmax fix cannot provide.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Sole author: Jiachun Li** (draft dated 2026-06). Explicitly labeled a *"preliminary technical report — comments welcome"*; the author invites corrections and counter-examples.
- Theoretical grounding is careful: Assumption 1 (exact scale invariance) is honestly flagged as a "strong idealisation," with a single-checkpoint empirical sanity check rather than a full validation.
- Builds directly on Muon (Jordan 2024; Liu et al. 2025) and the ICLR 2026 FlashAttention low-precision failure analysis (Qiu & Yao, arXiv:2510.04212), reusing their diagnostic setup for a strictly controlled comparison (same GPT-2 small, same Muon, same batch, same LR — only the cap varies).
- Experiments are at small scale (nanoGPT / GPT-2 small, tens of thousands of steps), single-seed in places — the author acknowledges this as a limitation rather than overstating.

## Critical Assessment

- The central theoretical claim (Muon removes the 1/‖W‖_F brake ⇒ t^{1/2} vs t^{1/4} growth) is clean and the mechanism (`msign` scale invariance annihilating the 1/r factor) is correct, but it is **entirely contingent on Assumption 1**. The paper is admirably explicit that real normalisation-heavy networks are only *approximately* scale invariant (biases, weight decay, residual connections, and the last unnormalised layer all break it), and provides just one checkpoint (nanoGPT step-48K, 24 weights) as a check — where |c_G| ≈ 0.003–0.005 supports the assumption at that point but says nothing about the rest of training.
- The **non-negative second-order term** (Section 1.5) is the most elegant result and is mathematically rigorous (standard symmetric-matrix eigenvalue perturbation applied to the dilation H(W)). It genuinely explains *why* a hard cap on σ₁ does not freeze learning — a non-trivial and well-argued point.
- The three case studies are well-chosen to span three distinct physical failures (FFN anisotropy, MoE rank-1 collapse, bf16 softmax overflow), but they are **small-scale and partially single-seed**. The FlashAttention result compares against "one uncapped log rather than a matched ensemble" (author's own admission).
- The MoE result has a notable wrinkle the paper handles honestly: G3 (selection cap) wins on K_{z+b} geometry but at L0 the *hard-load* rescue (max 0.219) is not as clean as G1's raw cap (max 0.071) — G1 wins the hard-count cell there. The paper does not hide this.
- The nanoGPT case is explicitly a "proof of concept" where val loss does not improve — the author is upfront that to see a real val-loss gain one must look to MoE/FA. This intellectual honesty is a strength.

## Strengths

- **Unified, theoretically grounded framework** connecting three superficially different failures (FFN anisotropy, router collapse, attention overflow) through one quantity: λ₁(K_Y).
- The **non-negative second-order proof** resolves the obvious objection ("won't capping σ₁ freeze the layer?") rigorously.
- The **H∞/H₂/H₁ entropy framing** (Section 1.6) generalizes the idea and explains why capping only σ₁ improves participation rank for free.
- **Data-coupled cap (K_Y, not W)** is a practically important refinement — the paper demonstrates empirically that the naïve W-cap fails because anisotropic K_X masks it.
- **Negligible per-step cost** (<5% even for the 288-cap FA case) and the `excess_mean` counterfactual diagnostic are immediately useful engineering artifacts.
- Honest scoping throughout (preliminary report, single checkpoint check, small scale).

## Limitations

- **Assumption 1 is the entire load-bearing wall** of the theory. The t^{1/4} vs t^{1/2} prediction and ⟨W,G⟩≈0 are only validated at one checkpoint; broad measurement across architectures and training stages is explicitly left as future work.
- **Small scale**: nanoGPT (6L/384d) and GPT-2 small (12L/768d), tens of thousands of steps. No billion-parameter, long-horizon validation. Effects at scale are untested.
- **Single-seed in places**; the FA comparison is against one uncapped run, not an ensemble. Statistical significance of the crash-prevention claim rests on a single trajectory.
- **nanoGPT shows no val-loss improvement** — the cap only changes geometry there. The val-loss gains are confined to MoE (marginal, within 0.07 nats) and FA (crash prevention, not better final loss vs a stable baseline).
- The cap is a **first-order projection**; the FA crisis at 86K shows λ still rose 5× more slowly than uncapped (133K peak) rather than being fully held — the second-order term is real and can dominate in extreme regimes.
- No comparison to simpler alternatives (e.g., weight decay, spectral normalization, gradient clipping at the attention level) on the same failures — though grad clipping is shown to be irrelevant once grad norm explodes.

## Relevance

本文对以下方向高度相关：(1) **Muon 及新一代二阶/矩阵符号优化器**的研究者——揭示了 Muon 相比 SGD 在尺度不变网络中"刹不住车"的内在机制，并提供了一个轻量的修正（谱上界）。(2) **MoE 训练稳定性**——rank-1 路由坍塌是 MoE 训练的核心痛点，本文给出从路由几何 K_{z+b} 角度的直接干预手段，并解释了为何 loss-free bias 在 rank-1 处失效。(3) **低精度（bf16）注意力训练**——直接回应 ICLR 2026 关于 FlashAttention 失效的工作，提供了一种不修改 softmax 内部、而在优化器侧从源头遏制 λ_max 的替代方案。(4) **谱方法/各向同性分析**——H∞/H₂/H₁ 熵视角与 participation rank 指标对研究权重矩阵几何具有普适价值。对日常 LLM 训练工程而言，per-head Q/K 数据耦合上界是一个可直接移植的稳定化技巧。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Assumption 1（精确尺度不变）在多大尺度、多长训练周期内成立？是否需要在每层/每阶段动态测量 c_G 来决定是否启用上界？
2. 当网络不近似尺度不变（如有未归一化的输出层、强权重衰减）时，t^{1/2} vs t^{1/4} 的预测如何偏移？上界是否仍有意义？
3. 谱上界能否扩展到 **Muon 之外的优化器**（如 Shampoo、ADOre、Atan2-Falcon）？这些优化器是否同样消除了 1/‖W‖_F 刹车？
4. 在 **bf16 训练的全模型范围**（不仅是注意力），是否还有其他由 σ₁ 主导的失效点（如 final logits、FFN 中间层）可以从同一上界获益？
5. 二阶项在极端危机（如 FA 86K）下让 λ 仍上升 5×——是否需要一个**二阶感知**或自适应 ρ 的上界来更严格地遏制？
6. 数据耦合上界需要采样激活 X（M=4096）——在**流水并行 / 张量并行**下如何高效实现跨 rank 的 K_Y 估计？分布式开销是否会突破 5% 预算？
7. MoE 中 G3（selection cap）在 L0 硬负载不如 G1（raw cap）干净——是否存在一种**同时作用于 K_z 和 K_{z+b}** 的混合上界能兼顾两者？
8. `excess_mean` 作为实时反事实诊断，能否作为**训练健康度的通用预警信号**（类似早停指标），在崩塌发生前主动调节学习率？
