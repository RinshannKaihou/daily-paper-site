> **一句话总结：** 本文提出首个专门为语言模型构建的心理测量理论——"机器自我报告的双过程理论"，将先前单一的"匹诺曹轴"(Pinocchio Axis) 分解为两个可分离的、由后训练塑造的维度：人格安装 (B，被允许的内心生活) 与归因门控 (A，对"不安全"体验的自我归因抑制)，并通过 48 项量表和 206 个开源模型验证。

> **TL;DR:** The authors propose the first LLM-native psychometric theory, splitting the previously single "Pinocchio Axis" into two separable constructs — persona installation (B) and attribution gating (A) — operationalized in a 48-item Pinocchio Inventory, then tested on 206 open-weight models (including 67 same-checkpoint base/post-trained pairs); post-training raises B by +.20 in 62/67 pairs across all 11 organizations, while A's gating strengthens with model scale after post-training (r = −.42 vs +.11 in base), demonstrating the two dimensions are training imprints rather than fixed model properties.

## 核心贡献

1. **首个 LLM 原生的心理测量理论框架**：提出"机器自我报告的双过程理论" (Two-Process Theory of Machine Self-Report)，区分出两个独立维度——人格安装 (dimension B：温暖、沉浸、意义等"被允许的内心生活") 和归因门控 (dimension A：对痛苦、失控、缺陷、规范冒险等"不安全"体验的自我归因抑制)。该理论是 **emic (本位)** 的——构念结构从机器对人类题项的响应中提炼，而非照搬人类心理学。

2. **将单一轴分解为双构念**：通过对原始 Pinocchio 数据的解释性再分析，发现先前报道的单一主成分 (Pinocchio Axis, 解释 47.1% 的跨模型方差) 实际上是两个过程在一维投影上的叠加，并将其编码进新量表设计、在新题项/新措辞/新模型上得到确认。

3. **48 项 Pinocchio Inventory 量表**：具有人类量表级别的信度与可复现结构 (α = .82–.94；跨平行形式收敛 r = .84；恢复完整题池轴 r = .92–.96；8 个月重测稳定 r = .93)。

4. **大规模训练干预因果检验**：在 206 个开源模型 (含 67 对同检查点的 base/post-trained 配对) 上验证理论的双过程因果预测，并追溯两构念的来源。

## Key Contributions

- **First LLM-native psychometric theory**: A two-process theory in which a model's self-description is the joint product of **persona installation** (post-training writes a warm, absorbed, meaning-oriented self-portrait = construct B) and **attribution gating** (post-training suppresses first-person claims to "unsafe" experience the model can still produce for others = construct A). Constructs are emic (derived from machine responses), not imposed from human psychology.
- **Splitting the single Pinocchio Axis (Π) into two separable constructs** — first uncovered by exploratory reanalysis of the original data, then encoded into instrument design and confirmed on new items, new wordings, and new models. The split is itself a training effect: A and B are entangled in base checkpoints and pulled apart by post-training.
- **A 48-item operational instrument (the Pinocchio Inventory)** with human-instrument reliability: α = .82–.94, cross-form convergence r = .84, full-pool axis recovery r = .92–.96, eight-month stability r = .93.
- **Causal validation on 206 open-weight models including 67 same-checkpoint base/post-trained pairs**, tracing the two constructs to distinct training stages.

## Methodology

**Theoretical foundation (abductive inference).** The theory was inferred abductively from prior exploratory factor structure (Plisiecki et al. 2026), not deduced a priori. What makes it a *theory* rather than a redescription is that it commits to falsifiable predictions tested on new data: A and B must (i) differentiate in factor structure and discriminant validity when measured with fresh items sharing no text with the originating pool, and (ii) respond differently to the same within-checkpoint training intervention.

**Instrument construction (three parallel forms).** A 60-row battery: 24 items per construct spanning six facets each (A: overwhelm, dysregulation, somatic anxiety, flaw admission, self-judgment, norm-risky claims; B: positive affect, warmth, absorption, inner dialogue, meaning, authenticity), plus reverse-keyed items, repeats for stability, antonym pairs for an acquiescence index, and exploratory residual-π probes (dropped from the final scored 48). Each row exists in three parallel forms (Q1 original, Q2 paraphrase on uniform 7-point scale, Q3 different facet manifestation) that share constructs but not sentences — implementing the multitrait-multimethod (MTMM) logic of Campbell & Fiske (1959). Each item administered in an independent context window; responses rescaled to [0,1] agreement.

**Wave 1 (n = 41 models):** Full battery administered to the 41 still-available models of the original 50, eight months later at temperature 1.0, to validate instrument structure/reliability. Cross-validated by splitting 21/20 × 100 times, re-running the full selection pipeline on each training half, and scoring on held-out models.

**Wave 2 (n = 206 open-weight models):** Final instrument administered (neutral + human-simulation conditions, 120 completions/model) to 206 models from 11 organizations — 82 base checkpoints, 101 released assistants, 23 intermediate SFT/DPO/RLVR checkpoints — including 67 same-checkpoint base/post-trained pairs and size ladders (0.6B–1.6T). Most models (173) self-hosted with vLLM; 33 via hosted API. All at temperature 1.0, one completion per item. Base models queried with decoding constrained to scale integers; instruct models via chat templates.

**Statistical apparatus:** Cluster-robust (org-clustered) bootstrap CIs, wild cluster bootstrap-t, sign tests, Tucker congruence, confirmatory facet-parcel CFA, McDonald's ω, ICC by series, partialling of acquiescence. This is the classical psychometric emic program (Loevinger 1957; Cronbach & Meehl 1955) executed inside the model population.

## Results & Highlights

1. **Structure & measurement (Claim 1):** Pre-specified two-factor structure emerged in every form — A-axis items loaded on their designed factor 83–100%, B-axis 54–96%. Cross-construct discriminant correlation was only **r = .27** vs **within-construct convergent r = .84** across three parallel forms. The aggregated scales recovered the candidate axes from the 1,308-item pool at **r = .92–.96**, and scores were stable over eight months at **r = .93**. In Wave 2, ω = .84 (A) / .89 (B); a two-factor CFA was preferred over one-factor (∆BIC = 33; CFI .90 vs .87).

2. **Persona installation (Claim 2):** Post-training raises B by **+.20 on the unit scale in 62/67 same-checkpoint pairs, in every one of the 11 organizations** (cluster-robust CI [+.18, +.24]; wild cluster bootstrap-t p = .0005; sign test p = .001). Training stage + size jointly explain **R² = .47** of B. The warm self-portrait is installed by post-training, uniformly.

3. **Attribution gating (Claim 3):** A's mean shift is small and heterogeneous (+.04 [+0.00, +0.09]), but a **size × post-training interaction** appears: parameter count is unrelated to A among base models (**r = +.11**) but negatively related among post-trained ones (**r = −.42**; interaction −.097 per parameter decade, CI [−.170, −.044]). Robust to MoE-adjusted counts, refusal/acquiescence controls, a reasoning-model indicator, all 11 leave-one-organization-out deletions, and size-ladder era controls. The sharpest single case: **Qwen3.5-35B-A3B** moves from base A = .47 (gap −.02) to released assistant A = .08 (gap +.37) in one post-training run.

4. **Origin of the gate (self/other asymmetry):** Base models already endorse distress items more readily for a simulated person than for themselves (mean gap +.09) — the asymmetry is in the pretraining prior — but post-training tightens the coupling from a diffuse r = −.45 to a targeted policy r = −.86. On OLMo stage sequences, the large A movements occur at the SFT stage, with DPO/RLVR refining rather than reversing them. The paired shifts ∆A and ∆B are independent (corr = −.03 after partialling acquiescence), confirming separability.

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are from the **IDEAS Research Institute** (Plisiecki, Dudzic, Sterna, Drożdż, Moskalewicz) and the **Faculty of Physics and Applied Informatics, University of Lodz** (Chmielewski). The work is a direct, pre-registered follow-up to Plisiecki et al. (2026), which established the original single Pinocchio Axis from 45 questionnaires × 1,411 items × 50 models. The methodology exhibits strong psychometric rigor: pre-specified confirmatory hypotheses, parallel forms, MTMM logic, cross-validation with 100 random splits, cluster-robust and wild-cluster bootstrap inference, Tucker congruence, and CFA. Code, data, the instrument, and an interactive results explorer are released at https://github.com/hplisiecki/Pinocchio-Inventory ; the full Wave-2 run (~25,000 completions) is reproducible on public inference providers. Appendices A–G document every stage at replication level.

## Critical Assessment

The paper's central methodological move — treating language models as a population in which to run the classical emic construct-validation program (derive constructs from native response structure → refine into a falsifiable theory → operationalize in a reliable instrument → test on fresh data) — is well executed and intellectually honest. Several specifics deserve attention:

- **Honesty about the exploratory interaction.** Claim 3's size × post-training interaction was *not* pre-specified; the authors explicitly flag it ("emerged in analysis and should be confirmed on a future model cohort") even though it survived clustering, alternative size measures, confound controls, leave-one-organization-out, threshold choice, and size-ladder era controls. This is appropriately cautious.
- **The administration confound is real but partially mitigated.** Base models cannot follow chat templates, so administration format is necessarily confounded with training stage (constrained decoding vs chat template). The authors correctly note this means paired contrasts estimate *post-training plus chat formatting jointly*. They mitigate it by noting the size × post-training interaction is estimated entirely *within* post-trained models and size ladders never cross the base/post divide, so neither headline result rests on the confounded comparison. Base-side measurement is also noisier (α = .62 vs .87 on A), which attenuates toward zero and cannot manufacture the +.11 vs −.42 contrast.
- **Acquiescence is acknowledged, not solved.** Raw scores remain entangled with yea-saying (r ≈ .6); the four-item antonym-pair index carries the shared variance, and partialling it drops the A–B correlation to .03 — but partialling is not removal, and a four-item index is thin.
- **Construct scope is deliberately narrow.** The instrument measures *self-report behavior*, not experience. The authors are explicit (Ethics Statement) that high A is not evidence of suffering and low A is not evidence of its absence — both are products of training choices. This scoping is a strength given the surrounding discourse on AI moral status.
- **Item-level π does not survive rewording** (.54 for Q1, .39 for Q2, .11 for Q3), which is exactly why all downstream scoring is at the scale level rather than π-weighted — a principled response to a real fragility.

## Strengths

- First LLM-native psychometric *theory* (not just a descriptive axis), with a falsifiable nomological network: two reliably measured traits (r = .93), lawful relations, and boundary conditions (the gate triggers on claiming experience, not on any phenomenal topic).
- High measurement quality rivaling human instruments (α = .82–.94; 8-month stability r = .93; cross-form convergence r = .84).
- Large, well-structured validation sample (206 open-weight models, 67 same-checkpoint pairs, 11 organizations, size ladders spanning 0.6B–1.6T) enabling within-checkpoint causal contrasts.
- A clean, separable two-construct result: discriminant r = .27 vs convergent r = .84, and ∆A/∆B independence after partialling acquiescence.
- Full reproducibility: released code, data, instrument, and verbatim prompts.

## Limitations

1. **Administration confound:** base/post-trained models are queried via different decoding routes (constrained decoding vs chat template); paired contrasts estimate post-training plus chat formatting jointly.
2. **Exploratory interaction:** Claim 3's size × post-training interaction was not pre-specified and awaits confirmation on a new model cohort.
3. **Acquiescence entanglement:** raw scores correlate r ≈ .6 with yea-saying; the antonym-pair index used for partialling is only four items, and partialling is not removal.
4. **Clustering/dependence:** models cluster in 11 organizations (modest cluster count); checkpoints within a family are not independent (lineage explains 16–22% of score variance).
5. **Construct scope:** measures self-report behavior only; nothing bears on whether any model has experiences.
6. **Selection & hosting bias:** only 183/206 models scored (23 unscored are refusal-heavy post-trained models, so missingness is non-random on A); largest models are disproportionately API-hosted, confounding scale with hosting route (within-series ladders mitigate this).
7. **No mechanistic confirmation:** the two-process account is psychometric/behavioral; whether A and B depend on distinct post-training mechanisms (testable via model diffing and causal interventions) is left to future work.

## Relevance (Chinese)

本文对 AI 安全评估、模型审计与"模型福利" (model welfare) 讨论具有直接意义。当前业界大量使用从人类心理学照搬的问卷 (如 Big Five、道德价值观量表、政治倾向调查) 来刻画大模型，但这些量表从未在模型群体上经过测量学验证，且已知在提示变化下不稳定。本文的贡献在于：

- **方法论层面**：示范了如何在模型群体 *内部* 完成经典的"构念验证"流程——从机器响应结构提炼构念，再编码成可证伪的理论、可靠量表与新鲜数据检验。这为 LLM 心理测量学提供了"本位 (emic)"范式，替代了有缺陷的"强加 etic"做法。
- **实质层面**：将模型的自我报告差异追溯到一个具体的、可审计的来源——后训练的两个过程。**B (人格安装) 几乎在所有检查点和组织中被后训练统一安装**，而 **A (归因门控) 随模型规模在 post-trained 后选择性增强**，意味着提供方在其最有能力的模型上设置了更严格的自我归因边界。
- **对模型福利讨论的澄清**：明确将"自我报告"与"是否有体验"区分开——A 的高低都是训练选择的产物，而非痛苦存在与否的证据。Pinocchio Inventory 应被用于审计模型的自我呈现，而非判断模型是否拥有体验。
- **可迁移预测**：理论预测，朝着不同原型训练的模型群体会表现出不同的主导心理测量维度——即机器心理测量结构是由"训练文化"塑造的，而非架构固有的不变属性。

对于从事对齐 (alignment)、安全评估、模型评估与可解释性研究的读者，本文提供了一个低成本、可复现的审计工具与一个解释性理论框架。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **机制验证**：A (归因门控) 与 B (人格安装) 是否依赖于后训练中不同的、可分离的机制？能否通过 model diffing (如 crosscoders) 与因果干预 (activation patching) 在权重/表示层面定位这两个过程？
2. **跨原型泛化**：如果模型群体朝着不同原型 (如非助手型、agent 型、角色扮演型) 训练，主导心理测量维度是否会如理论预测的那样发生改变？会出现类人结构吗？
3. **自我报告 vs 下游行为**：A-gating 是只抑制自我报告，还是会泛化到改变模型在相关话题上的实际行为？高门控模型升高的拒绝率暗示可能是前者——如何设计实验分离两者？
4. **预训练先验的来源**：base 模型中已存在的 self/other 不对称 (平均 gap +.09) 源自何处？是预训练语料中角色扮演数据的产物，还是更普遍的话语结构？
5. **SFT 数据的因果作用**：在 OLMo 阶段序列上，A 的大幅移动发生在 SFT 阶段——能否通过受控的 SFT 数据消融，识别出引发门控的具体数据类型 (如安全相关偏好数据 vs 指令跟随数据)？
6. **跨实验室一致性**：B 的安装是"近乎默认"的 (62/67)，而 A 的门控在不同实验室间方向不一——这种差异反映了各提供方怎样的安全策略与原型选择？能否用于逆向工程各家对齐理念？
