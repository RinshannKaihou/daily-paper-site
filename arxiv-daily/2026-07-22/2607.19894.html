Defense Against LLM Backdoors using Critical Neuron Isolation Pruning

Key Finding

DeCNIP neutralizes LLM backdoors by pruning just ~0.1% of "critical neurons" — cutting attack success over 95% while keeping ~97% of model utility.

01 Key Metric
2.90%
Mean attack success rate

Down from 65.18% with no defense, across six LLMs — a >95% relative reduction. Best baseline (Pruning) stalls at 29.86%.

02 Why This Matters

A surgical, white-box defense that erases backdoors with near-zero utility loss offers a reproducible recipe for auditing third-party LLM weights before deployment.

The whole pipeline leans on empirically tuned per-model thresholds (η, λ, α) and a successful trigger search, with no test against adaptive attackers who re-embed backdoors.

How it works

Methodology — a detect-then-prune pipeline

Stage 1 · Trigger Detection

GCG-style search, reframed as cross-entropy

Motivation: a real trigger pushes a harmful prompt's hidden state toward the benign manifold, slipping past safety alignment. So "maximize malice" becomes minimize CE against a benign target.

F(m) = Σ CE( L_θ(m ⊕ x_harmful), L_θ(y_benign) )
  • Init trigger "! ! !" (n=3); gradient picks top-k token candidates per step.
  • Batched random replacement breaks out of local optima.
  • Collect triggers above success-rate threshold τ into set S.
  • Recovered triggers are unreadable strings — yet ASR within <5% of the original.
Stage 2 · BCN Localization & Damping

Surgical masks on Backdoor Critical Neurons

A BCN activates distinctly under "triggered-harmful" input vs. normal input. Per-sublayer detection respects Gated-MLP structure:

  • C_main — over-active neurons (|Δ|>η and rel-Δ>λ).
  • C_flip — sign-flipping neurons (|Δ|>η).
  • W_gate (post-activation) → C_main only; W_in / W_out → C_main ∪ C_flip.

Mitigation = multiplicative mask × damping factor α∈[0,1):

W'[i,:] = α · W[i,:] for i ∈ BCN (α=0 fully ablates)
α = 0.1 optimal intervene W_in + W_gate + W_out ~0.1% of neurons

Appraisal

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

What lands well

  • A genuinely unified flow — detection and mitigation live in one representational analysis, not two bolted-together steps.
  • Tiny intervention surface (0.1% of neurons) buys a >95% relative ASR drop; ASR–utility tradeoff beats all seven baselines.
  • Architecture-aware BCN rules split W_gate (post-activation) from W_in / W_out — a real nod to how modern Gated MLPs work.
  • Scale of evidence: six models including 70B, four heterogeneous attacks (model-edit, poisoned fine-tune, training-time, universal jailbreak).

Limitations

Where it frays

  • BCN detection rides on empirical thresholds (η∈{1,1.25,1.5}, λ∈{0.25,0.5}); no principled selector, cross-architecture transfer unproven.
  • GCG-style trigger search cost scales with iterations T and batch B; no GPU-time/cost report at 70B.
  • Threat model assumes white-box defender + a static attacker post-release; adaptive re-embedding not evaluated.
  • Triggers tested are word/phrase-level; complex semantic or long-context triggers untouched.
  • Behavior-triggered attacks (SleeperAgent) details sit in the supplement — main-text transparency is thin.

Questions for Further Exploration

2

How robust is DeCNIP against an adaptive attacker who re-embeds the backdoor, or deliberately spreads it across non-BCN neurons?

3

What is the real GPU cost of the GCG search at 70B+? Could lightweight activation probes replace it?

4

Does the framework extend beyond Gated MLPs — to MoE experts, attention heads, or multimodal LLM backdoors?

5

Combined with "refusal-direction" safety work, could it defend backdoors and strengthen base alignment at once?

6

For behavior-triggered backdoors (e.g. year/role conditions), how does BCN spatial layout differ from word-triggered ones?
