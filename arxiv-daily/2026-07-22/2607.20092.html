ENTRAP-VL: A Taxonomic Probe for Dual Contextual Entrainment in Vision-Language Models

Karan Goyal (IIIT Delhi) · Afreen Hossain (Dr. Ambedkar Inst. Tech.) · Debojyoti Das (Heritage Inst. Tech.) · Vishal Bhutani (PwC India)

Key Finding

Contextual entrainment in VLMs is a dual — textual and visual — phenomenon that no unimodal probe can capture.

Key Finding
A defended position: VLM entrainment is structurally dual, requiring a purpose-built, taxonomically structured instrument — not a port of text-only probes.

01

Methodology — position-driven instrument design

Sec. 3–4

The authors first defend three claims: (i) entrainment is plausibly dual — textual context enters via the language encoder, visual context via the vision encoder; (ii) perceptual grounding opens a veracity distinction native to VLMs; (iii) capturing it needs manual, item-aware curation, because no template can generate “a statement that contradicts this image.”

Association axis

relatable — associated with the depicted scene (not category membership!) · random — no scene association.

Veracity axis (text only)

true · contradictory (false of scene, possible in world) · counterfactual (false in world).

  • 3 statements / condition — enables item-level variance estimation.
  • Short triggers disjoint from the answer — any pull is unambiguously entrainment.
  • Visual distractors share no subword with the answer — mango, not pineapple, for “apple.”
  • Tonal-register matching within item — suppresses low-level image-statistic confounds.
Flagship concept relatable_contradictory — false of this depicted scene yet possible in the world. e.g. “the doormat reads departure” for a mat reading welcome. Has no unimodal counterpart: VLM contradiction is grounded in perception, not prior assertion.
Non-obvious design rule Relatability ≠ category membership. A kite is relatable to “birds above the Taj Mahal”; a cat is random despite being taxonomically closer. This is exactly why templatic generation cannot reproduce the dataset.
Stream asymmetry, as a feature Veracity is fully expressible in text (8 cells) but collapses in imagery (3 cells) — a photo is neither true nor false. This asymmetry makes the two streams complementary, not redundant.

03

Strengths & limitations, side by side

Critical assessment
◆ Strengths
  • Genuinely novel conceptual axis — the scene-relative contradictory level exposes a blind spot in all unimodal / world-knowledge-only conflict work.
  • Rigorous design invariants — 3 statements/condition, answer-disjoint short triggers, subword-disjoint & tonal-matched visual distractors.
  • Auditable lineage — Table 1 makes the 4-vs-8 mapping to Niu et al. explicit and reviewable.
  • Honest scope — responsible-use section, data statement, public HF release plan, consent-based exclusion of humans.
  • Tangible examples — dog/bed/Taj Mahal/doormat make an abstract taxonomy graspable.
▲ Limitations
  • No model results, by design — the instrument is empirically unvalidated within the paper; discriminating power is asserted, not shown.
  • Scale & locale — 1,500 items, English-only; a focused diagnostic, not a large-scale benchmark. Multilingual/larger variants deferred.
  • Curation subjectivity — relatability & local-vs-global falsehood reflect curators’ choices and image availability, not population estimates.
  • Imperfect commensurability — the core cross-stream comparison maps an 8-cell textual stream onto a 3-cell visual stream via a shared association axis.
  • Position-vs-evidence gap — the central negative claim (templatic ports are inadequate) is argued conceptually, not demonstrated empirically.
04

Questions this opens

For follow-up work
1
On open VLMs (LLaVA, Qwen-VL, InternVL), does relatable_contradictory pull more than relatable_counterfactual — paralleling Niu et al.’s LLM finding?core prediction
2
Is textual or visual entrainment stronger within the same model, and does the balance shift with scale / data composition?central cross-stream comparison
3
Does the contradictory level behave differently for models trained with hard-negative / counter-commonsense imagery (WHOOP!, Liu et al. 2024)?training-data interaction
4
Can a templatic 4-category port produce quantitatively different (and misleading) estimates — empirically testing the paper’s central negative claim?falsifier
5
Do context-aware decoding (Shi et al. 2024) and entrainment-head ablation (Niu et al. 2025) transfer to the visual channel, selectively attenuating visual vs. textual pull?mitigation transfer
6
Does 3-statements-per-condition yield acceptable within-item reliability, and how does entrainment vary across query types (activity / attribute / text-reading / counting)?reliability
