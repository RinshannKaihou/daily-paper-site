Methodology

One artifact, three stages, zero glue code.

A typed, JSON-serializable CircuitScores record — carrying task, model, algorithm, granularity, per-component attribution scores and discovery config — is produced by discovery backends and consumed by evaluation (FaithfulnessReport) and application modules. Three interchangeable interfaces all drive the same contract:

Discover 13 Evaluate 6+1 Intervene 7 Benchmark lm-eval
  • Stable tier (6): eap · eap-ig · eap-gp · acdc · ibcircuit · cdt — cross-validated on GPT-2 / Llama / Gemma / Qwen.
  • Research tier (7): GPT-2/IOI only; reachable via the same @register selector, promotable as evidence accrues.
  • Declarative data path: declare clean/corrupt prompt templates over CSV / JSONL / HuggingFace; the library does token-length alignment, label checks, a GREEN/YELLOW/RED pre-flight, and 10 corruption-synthesis strategies — routing clean-only data to IBCircuit / CD-T.

Unified faithfulness score F(C) = (y_circuit − y_corrupt) / (y_clean − y_corrupt) — reported signed, so a behavioural inversion shows up as a negative value instead of being clamped to zero.

Assessment

Strengths

  • True end-to-end unification. One artifact feeds discover → evaluate → intervene → benchmark across three equivalent interfaces (Python / CLI / YAML). Table 1: the only library covering all three stages on multi-billion-param LLMs.
  • Declarative custom-data path turns the (clean, corrupt)-pair bottleneck into a template step with a GREEN/YELLOW/RED pre-flight — freeing circuit discovery from canonical task sets.
  • Multi-pillar panel directly answers Miller et al.'s ranking-reversal critique; signed ratios expose inversions instead of hiding them.
  • Integrates rather than reinvents: LLM-Pruner, quanto/llmcompressor, PEFT/LoRA, ROME/MEMIT, lm-eval-harness — low adoption friction, fair comparisons.
  • Empirical honesty: reports nulls, negatives and inversions; chose source-available over OSI to bound dual-use risk.

Limitations

  • Statistical power is weak. Stability uses 3 seeds; E5–E7 are single-seed single-cell; ρ=−0.78 is one point, not an estimate; Pillar 7 (SDrel) unmeasured.
  • Intervention coverage gated by the architecture registry — Llama/Qwen/Gemma main; Mistral/Phi/GPT-2/Falcon structurally complete but unverified; TransformerLens 3.x pending.
  • Per-algorithm engineering cost: ROME/MEMIT need ~100k-scale covariance corpus; IBCircuit scales activation memory (so E1's 6-way compare is GPT-2 only); ACDC is node-only and expensive.
  • Corruption synthesis relies on identifiable syntax — may fail on instruction-tuned / safety prompts; AdvBench/TruthfulQA/StereoSet validity is future work.
  • SAE feature granularity not implemented despite being flagged as the natural next tier.
  • LSAL v1.1 is not OSI-open: commercial-use ban may limit industrial adoption and ecosystem forks.
Open Questions

What this paper leaves on the table.

  1. Does the patch↔pruning anti-correlation (ρ=−0.78) survive more seeds, other tasks beyond BoolQ, and models beyond Llama-3.2-3B? Currently it is one cell — can it become a population estimate?
  2. Is discovery stability (Jaccard) actually predictive of cross-seed intervention reliability (SDrel, Pillar 7) — which has never been measured?
  3. Why is CD-T dual-faithful yet canonically disjoint on IOI? Equivalent re-implementation of one mechanism, or a genuinely alternative pathway?
  4. Engineering implication of E4's refusal-inversion: can Pillar 2's signed ratio serve as a safety canary for abliterated / refusal-removed models?
  5. Why does ablation faithfulness predict quantization (ρ=+0.73) but not pruning? Is there a principled "correct intrinsic metric" per intervention class?
  6. Once SAE features land as a third granularity, how do node/neuron/feature-tier circuits map onto the intervention modules?
  7. Do auto-synthesized corruptions (entity-swap, token-swap, …) carry real attribution signal on AdvBench / TruthfulQA / StereoSet — and does the GREEN/YELLOW/RED gate correlate with downstream discovery quality?
  8. How much does the LSAL v1.1 commercial ban actually retard industrial adoption — and will a compatible fully-open fork emerge?
