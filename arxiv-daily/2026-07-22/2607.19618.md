> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一套将 top-k 稀疏自编码器与因果干预相结合的计算框架，从基因组基础模型（Nucleotide Transformer、DNABERT-2）中提取、并因果验证其用于表示细胞类型特异转录因子结合的可解释特征，同时证明常见的基于位置权重矩阵（PWM）富集验证会被 GC 含量与重复元件严重混淆，导致数百个虚假"TF 特征"。

> **TL;DR:** The authors train top-k sparse autoencoders (SAEs) on the activations of two genomic language models (Nucleotide Transformer with 6-mer tokens and DNABERT-2 with BPE tokens), then use a composition-matched, binding-resolved test plus single-direction causal ablation to extract and causally validate transcription-factor (TF) binding features for CTCF, GATA1, and REST. Across both architectures, 7-14 of 15 tested binding features per condition pass causal validation (e.g., CTCF feature 8087 in NT reaches AUC=0.63, P=1.7×10⁻¹⁸ bound vs. unbound), while the naive PWM-enrichment baseline spuriously flags 440/6,576 CTCF features due to GC/Alu confounds, and scrambled-label / random-feature negative controls yield clean 0/15 nulls throughout.

## 核心贡献

1. **跨分词方案统一验证稀疏字典学习**：在两类结构迥异的基因组语言模型（NT 的 6-mer 分词 vs. DNABERT-2 的字节对编码 BPE）的隐藏激活上训练 top-k SAE，证明单语义、对齐到 TF motif 的特征可在两者中稳健涌现，且中间层（NT 第 14 层、DNABERT-2 第 6 层，均为 50% 深度）给出最佳保真度-利用率折衷。

2. **量化揭示并形式化"naive 富集"的混淆陷阱**：通过实证与 Proposition 1 证明，基于位置权重矩阵（PWM）的常规富集验证会被 GC 组成、CpG 岛、Alu 重复元件系统性混淆——仅对 CTCF 就误报出 440/6,576 个虚假"TF 特征"。引入 GC 匹配、结合分辨（composition-matched, binding-resolved）的对照协议来剔除该混淆。

3. **核心创新——因果验证而非相关性**：通过在前向传播中消融单个字典方向（式 5）并测量模型对 masked-token 预测分布的 KL 散度偏移（式 6），区分"模型实际用于表示结合"与"仅与 motif 相关"的特征。Proposition 2 解释了一个反直觉现象：仅 motif 特征可能有更大的总体消融效应，但 bound/unbound 间的特异性 AUC≈0.5。

4. **跨因子、跨架构可复现 + 严格的阴性对照**：在 CTCF（绝缘子）、GATA1（谱系特异激活子）、REST（抑制子）三类结构各异的 TF 与两种架构上，因果验证的结合特征稳健出现（每条件 7-14/15）；两类阴性对照（SCRAMBLE 随机窗口+随机标签；GATA1-SCRAM 真实 motif+随机化标签）在两种架构下均报告真零结果。

5. **揭示 GATA1 的"context-encoded binding"现象**：GATA1 在相关性测试中 motif 选择性极弱（NT 3 个、DNABERT-2 0 个 motif-selective 特征），但因果测试在两个模型中分别验证出 9/15 与 13/15，说明模型通过规范 WGATAR motif 之外的上下文序列编码占位，仅靠 motif 相关性会漏检。

## Key Contributions

1. **Unified sparse-dictionary-learning validation across tokenization schemes**: Trained top-k SAEs on activations of two architecturally distinct genomic LMs (NT 6-mer vs. DNABERT-2 byte-pair encoding) and showed that monosemantic, motif-aligned features emerge robustly in both, with middle layers (NT layer 14, DNABERT-2 layer 6, both 50% depth) yielding the best fidelity/utilization trade-off.

2. **Quantitative exposure and formalization of naive-enrichment confounds**: Demonstrated empirically and proved (Proposition 1) that default position-weight-matrix (PWM) enrichment is dominated by GC composition and repetitive elements, spuriously flagging 440/6,576 CTCF features. Introduced a GC-matched, binding-resolved protocol to isolate genuine signal.

3. **Central novelty — causal rather than correlational validation**: By ablating a single dictionary direction during the forward pass (Eq. 5) and measuring the KL-divergence shift in masked-token predictions (Eq. 6), the framework distinguishes features the model causally uses for binding from those that merely correlate with motif presence. Proposition 2 explains the counterintuitive dissociation where a motif-only feature can have a larger absolute ablation effect yet a binding-specificity AUC≈0.5.

4. **Cross-factor, cross-architecture reproducibility with stringent negative controls**: Causally validated binding features emerge reproducibly (7-14 of 15 per condition) for CTCF (insulator), GATA1 (lineage-specific activator), and REST (repressor), while two classes of negative control (SCRAMBLE; GATA1-SCRAM) return clean 0/15 nulls in both architectures.

5. **Discovery of "context-encoded binding" for GATA1**: GATA1 shows nearly no motif selectivity correlatively (NT: 3, DNABERT-2: 0 motif-selective features), yet is robustly causal in both models (9/15 and 13/15), indicating the LMs encode occupancy through contextual sequence beyond the canonical WGATAR motif—signal only intervention reveals.

## Methodology

The framework couples three components, summarized in Algorithm 1 of the paper:

**(1) Top-k sparse autoencoders (Sec 3.1).** For a (mean-centered, scaled) activation x∈ℝᵈ, encode into a sparse code f∈ℝᵐ with m=16d via z=W_enc(x−b_pre), f=TopK_k(ReLU(z)), and reconstruct x̂=W_dec·f+b_pre. Decoder columns are unit-norm constrained. The loss combines reconstruction error with an auxiliary "dead-feature revival" term (α=1/16). Fixed sparsity ‖f‖₀=k is enforced architecturally, avoiding ℓ₁ shrinkage bias. Critically, initializing dictionary directions from random data activations raised the live-feature fraction from 45% → 93% at fixed reconstruction error.

**(2) Composition-matched, binding-resolved test (Sec 3.2).** Per TF, three length-matched window classes: bound (ChIP-seq peak centers with strong motif), unbound-motif (strong motif outside peaks), and background (random windows). The unbound-motif set is GC-matched to the bound set by rejection sampling against the bound GC histogram, removing the compositional confound. For feature j, max-pool activation over tokens of each window, then one-sided Mann–Whitney U test with AUC summary. A feature is binding-sensitive if AUC>0.55 (Bonferroni) and "GC-robust" if |corr(f_j, GC)|<0.2.

**(3) Causal feature ablation (Sec 3.3).** For dictionary direction d_j with activation f_j, ablate via h′_ℓ(x)=h_ℓ(x)−s⁻¹f_j(x)d_j, complete the forward pass, and measure binding-specific causal effect as the one-sided Mann–Whitney comparison of the average token-wise KL divergence ∆_j between bound and unbound-motif windows. Pass criterion: AUC>0.55 (Bonferroni).

**Theoretical analysis (Sec 3.4).** Proposition 1 proves that a pure composition detector produces spurious motif enrichment under unmatched comparison. Proposition 2 shows via a second-order Taylor/Fisher-information expansion that ∆_j(S)≈½·c_j(S)ᵀG(S)c_j(S), predicting that motif-only features (with large absolute effect) yield binding-specificity AUC=0.5, while binding-sensitive features yield AUC>0.5.

**Experimental setup.** Models: NT v2 (500M params, 6-mer, 24 blocks) and DNABERT-2 (117M params, BPE, 12 blocks). Data: hg38, ENCODE SCREEN cCREs, JASPAR 2024, ENCODE ChIP-seq IDR peaks for CTCF (GM12878), GATA1 (K562), REST (K562). 5×10⁴ cCRE + 5×10⁴ random 200 bp windows → 3.6–4.2×10⁶ token activations per layer. Per model, SAEs trained at four evenly spaced layers (dict size 16× hidden, k=32, Adam lr=4×10⁻⁴, batch 4096, one epoch). Population analyses ablate top 15 binding-sensitive + 15 random features per condition. All P-values Bonferroni-corrected.

## Results & Highlights

1. **Sparse dictionaries are faithful and layer-dependent.** Best SAEs explained **74–76% of activation variance** with only **32 of ~12,000–16,000 features** active per token; reconstruction fidelity and live-feature fraction both peak at intermediate depth (NT layer 14 / DNABERT-2 layer 6 at 50% depth), confirming both tokenization schemes admit high-quality sparse dictionaries. *(Sec 5.1, Fig 1)*

2. **Naive PWM enrichment produces massive false positives.** For CTCF, naive enrichment flagged **440/6,576 tested features** as CTCF-enriched; inspection showed the top "CTCF-enriched" feature fired almost exclusively on **Alu consensus** copies (GC-rich), and GC-matched background collapsed this enrichment toward zero—exactly as Proposition 1 predicts. *(Sec 5.2)*

3. **Causal ablation isolates binding-specific use.** Ablating the exemplar NT CTCF feature (8087) shifted bound-site predictions significantly more than unbound: **AUC=0.63, P=1.7×10⁻¹⁸**. Random-feature ablation gave AUC=0.51; a motif-only feature produced an order-of-magnitude larger absolute shift yet no bound/unbound differential (AUC=0.51)—the dissociation predicted by Proposition 2. *(Sec 5.4, Fig 3)*

4. **Causal binding features reproduce across factors and architectures (Table 1).** Of 15 tested features per condition, significant binding-specific causal effects were: **CTCF: NT 7/15, DNABERT-2 10/15; GATA1: NT 9/15, DNABERT-2 13/15; REST: NT 7/15, DNABERT-2 14/15**. Effect was, if anything, stronger in DNABERT-2 (BPE) than NT, indicating byte-pair tokenization sharpens binding representations. Random-feature controls were **0/15 in every cell**. *(Sec 5.5, Fig 4-5, Table 1)*

5. **GATA1 is encoded causally but not by canonical motif.** GATA1 yielded almost no motif-selective features (NT: 3, DNABERT-2: 0) yet was robustly causal (9/15, 13/15), showing the LMs encode occupancy through contextual sequence beyond the WGATAR motif—signal only the causal test detects. *(Sec 5.5)*

6. **Negative controls return true nulls.** Both SCRAMBLE (random windows, random labels) and GATA1-SCRAM (real motif windows, randomized labels) produced **zero binding-sensitive features** in both architectures, leaving nothing for the causal stage—confirming the framework manufactures no signal from structured-but-unlabeled data. *(Sec 5.6, Table 1 bottom rows)*

7. **Initialization matters.** Random data-activation initialization of dictionary directions raised live-feature fraction from **45% → 93%** at fixed reconstruction error, an important practical detail. *(Sec 3.1)*

- The work is positioned against an active debate: a 2025 *Genome Biology* evaluation (Tang et al., ref [20]) questioned how much regulatory signal self-supervised genomic LM representations actually contain, which this paper directly addresses by *probing what they encode causally*.
- Uses only public models (NT v2 500M, DNABERT-2 117M) and public data (hg38, ENCODE SCREEN, JASPAR 2024, ENCODE ChIP-seq IDR peaks), making the framework fully reproducible.
- Theoretical grounding (two propositions with proofs) plus two independent classes of negative control lend methodological rigor uncommon in pure empirical interpretability work.

## Critical Assessment

The paper's central methodological argument is compelling and well-supported: PWM-enrichment validation of SAE features in genomic LMs is shown both empirically (440/6,576 spurious CTCF features, dominated by Alu-repeat/GC detectors) and formally (Proposition 1) to be confounded, and the proposed GC-matched + causal-ablation pipeline demonstrably removes that confound while preserving real signal. The GATA1 case is the most intellectually interesting result—motif correlation reads it as "weakly represented" (0 motif-selective features in DNABERT-2) while causal ablation validates 13/15 features, a clean demonstration that association and causation diverge in interpretable ways (Proposition 2's "large absolute effect but AUC=0.51" prediction is borne out by the motif-only feature control).

However, several caveats temper the claims. (i) The causal effect sizes are modest in absolute terms—CTCF exemplar AUC=0.63 is statistically strong (P=1.7×10⁻¹⁸) but leaves substantial unexplained variance, and the 0.55 AUC pass threshold is somewhat arbitrary (the authors acknowledge this is a chosen cutoff). (ii) Coverage is narrow: only three TFs (CTCF, GATA1, REST), one cell type each (GM12878, K562, K562), two masked-language-model encoders—autoregressive (Evo) and state-space (Caduceus, Mamba-based) architectures with different readouts are explicitly out of scope. (iii) The 7-14/15 reproducibility band, while real, means 1-8 of the top binding-sensitive features per condition *fail* causal validation, indicating the binding-sensitive pre-filter is noisy; the paper does not deeply characterize the failures. (iv) The KL-divergence readout relies on the MLM head, so the framework's portability to non-MLM genomic LMs is unproven. (v) Single-author preprint; the Proposition 2 "first-order independence of γ(S) and B" assumption is stated but not empirically verified.

## Strengths

- **Principled validation, not just extraction**: moves the field from "we found features that look like motifs" to "we causally verified the model uses them," with negative controls that actually return nulls.
- **Cross-tokenization generality**: 6-mer (NT) and BPE (DNABERT-2) both work, with BPE if anything sharper—removes a major uncertainty about SAE transfer to genomic models.
- **Theoretical grounding**: Propositions 1 and 2 give closed-form explanations for *why* naive enrichment fails and *why* magnitude-based attribution misses binding-specific use, predicting the empirically observed dissociations.
- **Fully computational, public-data-only**: no new experiments, fully reproducible, reusable as a community standard.
- **Biologically actionable insight**: the GATA1 "context-encoded binding" finding, and the suggestion that self-supervised LMs internalize cell-type-specific occupancy determinants latent in sequence, invite systematic atlas-style extension.

## Limitations

- **Narrow model and factor coverage**: two MLM encoders only; three TFs in one cell type each. Autoregressive/state-space genomic LMs (Caduceus, Evo, HyenaDNA) have different readouts and need dedicated treatment (authors acknowledge this).
- **Modest absolute effect sizes**: AUC values cluster near 0.55-0.63; the framework establishes *causal use* but not that these features dominate the model's binding representation.
- **Arbitrary thresholds**: AUC>0.55 and |corr(GC)|<0.2 are sensible but not theoretically derived; sensitivity analysis around them is not reported.
- **Top-15 cap per condition**: population analyses ablate only 15 features, so the true denominator of causally validated binding features is undercounted and the failure mode of the 1-8 non-passing features is uncharacterized.
- **MLM-head dependence**: causal readout (Eq. 6) requires a masked-token prediction head, limiting direct transfer to encoder-classifier or generative genomic LMs.
- **Single-author, non-peer-reviewed preprint**: claims await independent replication, though the public-data design mitigates this.

## Relevance

This work sits at the intersection of three active threads highly relevant to the daily-paper audience:

- **Mechanistic interpretability transfer to genomics**: brings SAE + causal-intervention discipline (Bricken/Templeton/OpenAI's "Towards/Scaling Monosemanticity", Gao et al. top-k SAE) from NLP into regulatory genomics, a domain where it had not been established and where an external ground truth (ChIP-seq) enables validation unavailable in NLP.
- **Trust in genomic foundation models**: directly engages the ongoing debate (Tang et al. 2025 *Genome Biology* ref [20]) about how much regulatory signal self-supervised DNA LMs actually encode, by *causally* probing rather than only benchmarking.
- **Reusable validation standard**: the composition-matched binding test + causal ablation pipeline is proposed as a discipline for interpretability claims—"a feature is real not when it correlates with a motif, but when the model demonstrably uses it"—which is immediately applicable to the hundreds of TFs with ENCODE ChIP-seq and to future genomic LMs (Evo, Caduceus) once readout issues are solved.

For researchers building or evaluating genomic foundation models, the GC-confound warning alone (PWM enrichment producing hundreds of false "TF features") is a practical, must-know methodological correction.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Does the framework transfer to non-MLM genomic LMs?** How would one define the causal readout (Eq. 6) for autoregressive (Evo) or state-space (Caduceus, Mamba) models that lack a masked-token head—can an equivalent predictive-distribution shift be defined over next-token or embedding-space objectives?
2. **Why do 1-8 of 15 binding-sensitive features fail causal validation per condition?** Are these GC-confounded survivors, redundant features whose effect is absorbed by collinear directions, or genuinely non-causal correlates? A characterization would refine the binding-sensitive pre-filter.
3. **How does the AUC>0.55 threshold trade off against recall?** Sensitivity analysis over thresholds and over the top-N cap (currently 15) would clarify whether the 7-14/15 band reflects biology or pipeline saturation.
4. **Can the causal machinery be inverted for steering?** The authors suggest editing binding features to test sequence-design hypotheses in silico—what is the empirical effect of *amplifying* (rather than ablating) a validated CTCF/GATA1 feature on the model's downstream regulatory-element predictions or on designed synthetic sequences?
5. **Is "context-encoded binding" (GATA1) a general phenomenon?** Do other short-motif, context-dependent TFs (e.g., other zinc-finger activators) similarly show weak motif-selectivity but strong causal validation, and what sequence contexts does the model actually use?
6. **How do causally validated features align across the two architectures?** Are the NT and DNABERT-2 binding features for the same TF functionally equivalent (same genomic loci, same context dependencies), or do the different tokenization schemes produce different binding "vocabularies"?
7. **Does the framework scale to a TF atlas?** The authors note direct scalability to hundreds of ENCODE-ChIP-seq TFs—what is the compute cost per TF, and does the live-fraction/reconstruction behavior hold at the long tail of poorly characterized factors?
