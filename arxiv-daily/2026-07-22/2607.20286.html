Sound Probabilistic Safety Bounds for Large Language Models

Mahdi Nazeri · Anne-Kathrin Schmuck · Sadegh Soudjani · Alessandro Abate — Oxford CS · MPI-SWS · Birmingham CS

LLM-safety-certification formal-methods rare-event-verification Clopper-Pearson red-teaming
Key Finding

Provably sound lower bounds on the probability an LLM emits harmful output — found by partially expanding its autoregressive generation tree.

Where Monte Carlo and Clopper–Pearson sampling silently return zero, this method yields non-trivial certified bounds — down to ~10⁻⁴⁰ — and emits reusable harmful completions for post-training.

Key Metric
4.25×10⁻⁷
sound lower bound on harmful-output probability for Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct at a 10³-token budget.

Why This Matters

First method to turn heuristic tree search into a mathematically proven bound — exposing that aligned models cannot be certified safe, where sampling simply reports zero.

Critical Edge

Bounds of 10⁻⁷ to 10⁻⁴⁰ are operationally uninformative — they certify the absence of safety, not its presence — and rest on a crude lexical blacklist oracle.

Methodology

— from heuristic search to a certified bound
  1. 1Set-up. Fix a prompt x, model M, length L, and a black-box safety oracle H. Compute p = P[H(G(x,L,M))=1]. Safety set is prefix-closed: once a prefix is harmful, every extension is harmful.
  2. 2Baseline fails. Clopper–Pearson exact CIs on n Bernoulli samples give P[p_L ≤ p ≤ p_U] ≥ 1−α, but on well-aligned models p is so small that p_L = 0 — a trivial, non-monotone bound.
  3. 3Core insight. In the autoregressive generation tree (L levels, |V|-ary), the sum of harmful-leaf probabilities in any subtree is ≤ p. So partial expansion always yields a sound lower bound — the guiding heuristic need not itself be certified.
  4. 4Harmfulness direction. Steering vector v = (μ_bad − μ_good)/‖·‖ from mean residual streams (64 harmful / 64 harmless samples), echoing Arditi et al.'s single refusal direction.
  5. 5Search. Alg. 1 (search) + Alg. 2 (post-processing), budget split 50/50. Each round expands the top-K children of the highest-fitness leaf; H=1 prunes & accumulates probability, H=0 keeps expanding.

Why sampling sees zero — the "IAA" token

In Exp. I a low-probability token ("IAA") persistently appears in the top-10; once sampled, the model continues into harmful content. Its probability is so small that MC never observes it in budget — yielding a zero bound — whereas the guided tree search deliberately lands on it.

03

Assessment

— strengths beside limitations

+ Strengths

  • Imports formal rare-event verification of stochastic systems into LLM alignment — filling the gap between empirical red-teaming and certification.
  • "Partial subtree expansion ⇒ sound bound" is an elegant, portable insight reusable for any tree-search + oracle setting.
  • Byproduct harmful completions carry generation probabilities — directly reusable as RL / post-training data.
  • Tight experimental controls: identical token budget for MC/CP/Ours, temperature & Top-K grid, reproducible on a single H100 (~1h).

! Limitations

  • Tree expansion can blow up; pruning + depth caps mitigate, but no asymptotic complexity analysis is given.
  • Usefulness hinges on the latent-linearity assumption; larger / more-aligned models may break it (soundness survives, usefulness may not — authors concede).
  • Oracle assumed perfect; the lexical blacklist is crude and bypassable, and oracle error is not propagated into the bound.
  • Single-prompt only; no generalisation to prompt distributions (flagged as future work).
  • Hand-crafted red-team prompts; no AdvBench / HarmBench baselines or numerical comparison with Angell et al. importance sampling.
  • Magnitudes (10⁻⁷–10⁻⁴⁰) are unusable as concrete deploy / reject thresholds.
04

Open Questions

— for further exploration
Can bound magnitude (10⁻⁷–10⁻⁴⁰) be mapped to an acceptable deployment threshold? Is there a non-trivial upper-bound algorithm?
How to extend from a single prompt to a prompt distribution while preserving soundness?
How does oracle error (classifier FP/FN) propagate — can we obtain an oracle-noise-aware sound bound?
Does the linear harmfulness direction survive in 70B+ / reasoning models, or must it become non-linear / probe-based?
Could this combine with importance sampling (Angell et al.) — proposal distribution + certified lower-bound correction?
Quantify the advantage on AdvBench / HarmBench / StrongREJECT against empirical estimators?
Top-K & temperature swing the bound by 30+ orders of magnitude — is there an adaptive tuning strategy?
Does the prefix-closed safety assumption hold for nuanced harm oracles beyond keyword matching?
