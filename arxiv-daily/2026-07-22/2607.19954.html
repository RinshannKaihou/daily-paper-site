A Multi-Dimensional Evaluation of Explainability in Media Bias Detection

Plausibility, faithfulness & accuracy are one thing three different things — measure them apart.

Ting Chen · Raina Zhang · Benjamin M. Ampel · Sagar Samtani Carnegie Mellon · Indiana · Georgia State BABE dataset ACDC circuit discovery Encoder-only (BERT / RoBERTa)
Key Finding
Rationale-aligned attention does not equal a faithful model — the three axes diverge by architecture.
Key Metric
0.863Macro F1
RoBERTa-large + attention-supervised. Beats BERT baseline 0.762 and feature baseline 0.511 (Spinde 2021).
Why This Matters
Aligned heatmaps can't certify a bias detector's reasoning — deployment needs mechanistic circuit checks, not just rationale fit.
Critical Edge
Effect sizes stay small (AUROCs 0.5–0.69) and the headline "no monotonic link" rests on 8 points from one small dataset.

Methodology

01Three orthogonal axes

A controlled study that separates what prior bias-detection work has conflated: how well a model predicts, how plausibly it explains, and how faithfully its internal circuits carry the prediction. Attention-supervised finetuning acts as the controlled knob.

DData — BABE

3,700 expert-annotated news sentences → 2,762 balanced subset (1,381 biased / 1,381 unbiased). Token rationales are sparse: avg 1.72 tokens/sentence, 6.18% coverage. Split 70:15:15. Krippendorff's α = 0.40.

MModels

BERT & RoBERTa in base and large variants (4 configs) to probe capacity effects on compressibility.

IIntervention

Attention-supervised finetuning: L = L_cls + λ·L_attn, λ = 0.5 from an 8-value sweep {0…5.0}. L_attn = MSE between min-max normalized last-layer [CLS] attention and the binary rationale mask. 5 seeds each.

PPlausibility (3 methods)

Last-layer CLS Attention · Attention Rollout (residual-inclusive) · Gradient×Attention (logit-gradient weighted). Scored by AUROC vs expert rationales.

FFaithfulness — ACDC circuits

Counterfactual pairs (biased keyword → [MASK]); linear logistic probe over [CLS]; activation-patch each head, rank by causal effect E(ℓ,h), take top-k ∈ {1,3,5,10,20,30}. Two modes: Elimination (necessity) & Isolation (sufficiency).

RTwo new circuit metrics

Retention = ΔP(circuit) / |ΔP(base)| — fraction of probability gap the circuit alone recovers. Rescue = ΔP(circuit) − ΔP(none) — net signal recovered vs full-ablation baseline (denominator-free check on Retention).

Strengths & Limitations

03side-by-side

Strengths

  • Conceptual clarity: explicitly separates plausibility from faithfulness and uses attention supervision as a controlled intervention to dissect the two.
  • Solid method transfer: first to adapt ACDC activation-patching to encoder-only classifiers, with reusable Retention/Rescue metrics.
  • Radical transparency: full λ sweep, 5 seeds, per-class results, complete circuit tables, ~3.5 GPU-hours total — cheap to reproduce.
  • Insightful cross-arch contrast: surfaces the "strong but hard-to-compress" RoBERTa-large paradox.
  • Responsible framing: candid dual-use, label-subjectivity and misuse discussion.

Limitations

  • Small, single dataset: BABE only; rationale coverage 6.18% makes plausibility noisy — generalization to real-world news unproven.
  • Low statistical power: 4 configs; the headline "no monotonic relationship" leans on ~8 data points.
  • Approximate circuits: top-k patching is an approximation; encoder bidirectionality makes compact causal decomposition harder than in autoregressive models.
  • Narrow architecture scope: encoder-only; decoder-only / instruction-tuned LLMs untouched.
  • Narrow attribution scope: only 3 attention-based methods; no Integrated Gradients / SHAP / LIME comparison.
  • Linear-probe readout unaudited: the unifying probe is never ablated against native classification heads.

Open Questions

04for further exploration
1
Does the plausibility–faithfulness divergence survive on larger, multilingual, cross-topic news corpora — or is it an artifact of BABE's 6.18% sparse rationales?
2
How do Retention/Rescue patterns shift when ACDC is ported to decoder-only or instruction-tuned LLMs (Llama, GPT-class) for bias detection?
3
After adding Integrated Gradients, SHAP, LIME, does "no attribution wins everywhere" still hold — or is there a method × architecture optimum?
4
If attention supervision lifts plausibility but not faithfulness, is there a causal-constraint or rationale-conditioned loss that lifts both at once?
5
Negative Rescue for RoBERTa-large at small k — does this signal causally fragile, redundancy-dependent predictions with adversarial-robustness implications?
6
Does the linear-probe unifying readout systematically favor certain heads? Can native-head ablations confirm the circuit findings?
