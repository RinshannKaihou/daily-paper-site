Two checkpoints can share a circuit at one granularity and not at another.

Circuit Claims Depend on What Is Extracted and How It Is Compared

Yang Sheng (Fudan & Shanghai Innovation Institute) · Jie Fu (IQuest Research)

mechanistic-interpretability circuit-extraction interpretability-robustness lean-theorem-proving sparse-models

Four assets

▎ Key finding

Whether two checkpoints share a circuit depends on the extracted graph, the pruning threshold, and the comparison level — edge overlap is fragile, head-level summaries stable.

▎ Key metric

0.666 / 0.163

routing-head Jaccard@10 (success) vs exact-edge — against a 0.199 random baseline.

▎ Why this matters

"Do these checkpoints share a circuit?" is ill-posed until extraction graph, pruning threshold, and comparison level are fixed — and reported.

▎ Critical edge

Evidence is local: one 8-layer ~415.7M model, 16+16 graph entries at two checkpoints; the bridge-fraction link rests on two curriculum cells.

Methodology

  • Benchmark9 synthetic Lean tactic-prediction tasks (4 atomic, 5 compositional). Fixed proof rules, randomized surface form (names, distractors). Two independent suites × 100 examples/task.
  • Model8-layer transformer, d_model=2048, 128 heads, ~415.7M params. Trained at 5 sparsity levels (dense … 90% top-k), 3 seeds.
  • TrainingSupervised: 5B tokens, 3 families (narrow / moderately / extensively expanded). RL: GRPO on composition-only prompts; 3 init conditions (Curriculum / Atomic-only / Composition-only).
  • Graph 1Core circuit — compact prediction-preserving subgraph via differentiable-mask extraction.
  • Graph 2Extended support graph — wider read/write/routing structure, same machinery.
  • Graph 3Fixed-loss pruning graph — smallest discrete circuit under ablated loss ≤ τ (main τ=0.12; sweep 0.04–0.20).
  • Q/K factorCoupled (one attn_qk) vs. factorized (separate attn_q, attn_k).
  • MetricsExact-match accuracy; Jaccard@10 (edge + routing-head) with 95% bootstrap CIs; bridge fraction; 3 negative controls (matched / mismatched atomic / random).

Strengths & limitations

Strengths

  • Construction-known atomic/compositional structure removes the task confound that blocks this audit in NLP settings.
  • Clean decomposition of "circuit extraction" into 3 explicit graphs, an explicit Q/K factor, and an explicit pruning threshold.
  • Baseline-referenced overlap analysis with bootstrap CIs and p-values — not anecdotal graph comparisons.
  • Actionable output: a concrete reporting practice (state graph, rule, threshold, Q/K rep, comparison level, boundary checks).
  • Honest framing of the behavior–structure link as directional, not causal.

Limitations

  • Single 8-layer ~415.7M transformer; generalization to larger models / other architectures unverified.
  • Graph evidence is local — 16+16 matched task-object entries at two checkpoints, not a frequency estimate over a large graph set.
  • Fixed-loss pruning is inherently threshold-dependent; only a 5-point τ sweep is reported.
  • Bridge-fraction association rests on two curriculum cells — an association, not an effect size.
  • Shared proof-state rules may make format cues more salient, inflating apparent stability.
  • Circuit-level stability under distribution shift is not tested (behavioral shift only).

Questions for further exploration

  1. Do these sensitivities hold at billions of parameters and on non-Transformer architectures (Mamba, RWKV)? Is "heads stable, edges fragile" universal?
  2. If the same audit moves to NLP tasks (IOI, entity tracking) with task structure controlled, does the edge-fragile / head-stable pattern replicate? What control approximates "known by construction"?
  3. Is the bridge-fraction ↔ compositional generalization link causal? Can controlled intervention (forced add/remove of bridge nodes) test that extra structure causes the gain?
  4. Is there a provable identifiability lower bound — under what conditions does behavior preservation uniquely determine a circuit at a given granularity? How does this unify with causal-abstraction unidentifiability results?
  5. Could the tiny Q/K coupled-vs-factorized gap (max 0.024) grow under finer node splits (per-head, per-position)? Should comparison level be reported as a continuous spectrum, not a binary choice?
Bottom line — The field's recurring "do these checkpoints share a circuit?" question is under-determined by behavior preservation. Until the extracted graph, pruning threshold, Q/K representation, and comparison level are fixed and reported, the answer is a function of those choices — not of the model alone.
