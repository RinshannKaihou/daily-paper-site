01 · Methodology

single pipeline · Algorithm 1 · 30 numbered equations

At decision time t, enterprise state xₜ = {business data, market signals, ops metrics, external constraints} is encoded to zₜ; candidate strategies are filtered by hard policy constraints, scored on a composite objective, and each decision emits {action, explanation ψ, compliance report c, audit record ℓ}.

xₜ → zₜ → Afeasₜ → ûₜ,ₖ → PIconf → Rₜ,ₖ → Sₜ,ₖ → y*ₜ
S = α·utility − β·uncertainty − γ·risk + ω·compliance − µ·hard-policy violation
§3.3 · Counterfactual

Strategy & utility

Strategies aₖ = [Δq, Δp, Δb, Δc] (resource / price / budget / capacity). Feasible set from operational hard constraints; realized utility carries stockout & overstock penalties + strategy cost.

§3.4 · Conformal + CVaR

Uncertainty & downside risk

Lower/median/upper quantiles form a conformal interval; width = uncertainty. Utility scenarios sampled from the interval yield CVaR; model risk blends CVaR, Pr(loss) and Pr(violation).

§3.5–3.6 · Memory + policy

Risk memory & governance

Top-M nearest historical cases (incl. human overrides) give Rᵐᵉᵐ; final R blends model + memory. Governance = policy-as-code rules; any hard violation excludes the strategy. Per-decision audit writes back to memory.

03 · Appraisal

strengths ↔ limitations

Strengths

  • Tight formalization: 30 numbered equations span state representation through audit logging; composite score and hard-constraint feasibility filter are cleanly defined and implementable.
  • Rare 6-axis evaluation — predictive, decision, risk, calibration, explainability, governance — on one benchmark, with ablation and a worked case study.
  • Self-consistent trust narrative: the §5.8 case visibly demotes a high-utility-but-fragile strategy, closing the argument loop.
  • Pragmatic audit + memory closed loop: each decision logs an audit record and writes back to risk memory — directly aligned with EU AI Act accountability.
  • Clear positioning tables (capability matrix + quantitative results) make the framework's place easy to grasp quickly.

Limitations

  • Evaluated only on repurposed public retail data; no real enterprise deployment or cross-industry (finance, health, manufacturing) generalization.
  • Candidate strategy space is hand-coded and low-dimensional (6 linear scalings of forecast demand) — far from real strategic decisions like pricing or M&A.
  • No dataset size, hyperparameters, weight values, sensitivity analysis, runtime/latency, or code — reproducibility is weak.
  • Predictive accuracy trails Deep Ensembles / FT-Transformer / CatBoost; weaker base forecasts drag on all downstream risk & calibration estimates.
  • Decision-aware baselines are the framework's own submodules; no comparison to RL, causal, or LLM-agent decision methods.
  • Conformal calibration adds only +1.19% PICP over Deep Ensembles — marginal gain for a core contribution. No standalone limitations section.

04 · Open questions

for further exploration
01
Do the RAU / GCR gains survive real enterprise data (unstructured market signals, policy text) and a continuous high-dimensional action space rather than 6 linear scalings?
02
What are the values and sensitivities of the many weights (α/β/γ/ω/µ/τ/δ/η/λ)? Could a meta-learning scheme learn them instead of validation-set grid search?
03
How to design forgetting / refresh for the risk memory so stale cases and noisy human overrides don't accumulate bias over long runs?
04
What does a concrete policy-as-code rule look like, and how are rules updated online as regulations (e.g. EU AI Act clauses) evolve without breaking historical comparability?
05
Could swapping the base forecaster for a stronger pretrained time-series / LLM model let TRUST-ESD win on both accuracy and trustworthiness?
06
Is +1.19% PICP worth the conformal complexity — or does a lighter uncertainty estimate suffice in retail?
07
Versus RL, causal-inference, or LLM-agent decision methods (not just internal ablations), where is the real advantage?
08
Human oversight is an offline metric here — how to measure the cognitive load and latency of accept/revise/override on net utility?
