Paper Dashboard / Distribution Testing for LLMs

TV distance between two LLMs is estimable where KL fails — with tight O(n/ε²) logit bounds and MLMC variance reduction.

Total Variation Distance Estimation in Autoregressive Models

Eric Price  ·  Kevin Tian  ·  Zhiyang Xun  ·  Yusong Zhu  — University of Texas at Austin · NSF IFML

total-variation-distance distribution-testing llm-inference multilevel-monte-carlo query-complexity

Key Finding

A bounded, interpretable engine-consistency metric for LLMs — TV stays finite under support mismatch where KL diverges to infinity.

Key Metric

0.586

TV(vllm, sglang) @ n=500

Identical Qwen3-0.6B weights; support-mismatch accounts for ~half the estimate.

Why This Matters

Gives open-weight LLM serving a robust equivalence test — more reliable than KL for auditing quantization, spec-decoding, and cross-provider drift.

Critical Edge

The authors' own main caveat: noisy-oracle queries are assumed independent, but real batching/quantization noise correlates — leaving practical bounds empirically unverified.

01

Methodology

Mixed representation + MLMC telescoping turn a naïve ε⁴ estimator into a near-optimal ε² one.

Core identity — Lemma 1

TV(π, µ) = 𝔼X∼M[ Z(X) ]
M = (π+µ)/2,  Z(x)=|π(x)−µ(x)| / (π(x)+µ(x))

Averaging under the mixture M — not the uniform 1/m used in prior work [MKP25] — is what removes the spurious vocabulary factor m (Qwen: m=151,643, top-k≈20, so K ≪ m).

TV also carries decision-theoretic bite: the Bayes error of telling the two engines apart is P*err = (1 − TV)/2.

  1. 1

    Empirical estimator bZ (eq. 12)

    Query each prefix's noisy oracle r times, average to bπ_r. A χ²-bound (Lemma 6/8) gives 𝔼[(bZ−Z)²] ≤ C·nσ²/r — naïvely r=O(nσ²/ε²) costs O(n²σ²/ε⁴).

  2. 2

    MLMC variance reduction — Algorithm 3

    L=O(log 1/ε) coupled layers with r_ℓ = 1+⌈cnσ²·2^ℓ⌉; telescoping increments Y_ℓ have geometrically decaying variance. Removes the extra ε² → O((n+n²σ²)/ε²·log²(1/ε)).

  3. 3

    Lower bound — rare-escape embedding

    Escape each prefix with prob q=1/n, embedding a finite-domain hard instance so each prefix query carries only Θ(1/n) information. Combined with [JHW18] L₁ bounds → matching lower bounds.

  4. 4

    Replay scoring

    A logit processor re-overwrites each sampled token to rescore whole sequences, dodging the engine's own scoring-API code-path bias (quantified in App. B.5). σ² is calibrated from repeated same-prefix queries.

03

Strengths & Limitations

✓ Strengths

  • Unified framework. Three access models + a noisy interpolation that cleanly captures real serving-stack nondeterminism.
  • MLMC is the key lever — provably drops the bound from ε⁴ to ε², with full pseudocode (Algorithms 2/3).
  • Rare-escape embedding elegantly turns sequence length n into an "information-dilution factor" Θ(1/n) for the lower bound.
  • Realistic targets — vllm/sglang are industry-standard; findings (config/prompt sensitivity, TV working where KL breaks) are engineering-useful.
  • Reproducible — open code plus appendices on σ-calibration, scoring bias, and synthetic validation.

! Limitations

  • Sampling n-gap unclosed — upper n²K vs lower nK; the authors flag whether the quadratic is essential as an open problem.
  • Independence assumption is their stated main caveat: real batching/quantization noise may correlate across queries.
  • Single weight family — case study uses only Qwen3-0.6B; no systematic TV scaling across 8B / 30B-MoE.
  • TV≈0.586 is large, hinting at a real implementation gap — yet no "acceptable TV threshold" guidance is offered.
  • TV lacks a chain-rule decomposition like KL's, remaining a practical barrier to autoregressive engineering use.
04

Open Questions

1

Can the sample-access n²K upper bound fall to nK, or is a super-linear lower bound provable?

2

How do the bounds degrade under correlated noisy-oracle queries (real batching noise)? Is there a decorrelation protocol?

3

What is the TV scaling law across larger models (8B / 30B-MoE) and precisions (int8/int4, KV quantization)?

4

Is there a production-acceptable TV threshold below which downstream loss/accuracy drift is provably bounded?

5

Can the native scoring-API bias be quantified and corrected, removing the need for replay scoring?

6

Can a "locally-additive + global-correction" decomposition give TV the autoregressive chain-rule convenience KL enjoys?
