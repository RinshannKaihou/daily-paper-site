> **一句话总结：** 本文审计了自然语言自编码器（NLA）以重建分数衡量解释忠实度的标准做法，揭示其仅靠"主旨"（gist）和"私有暗码"（private codes）即可通过测试；提出 RECAP 方法，在目标模型训练时协同训练线性探针头以保持指定内容可被外部探针解码，使解释真正可被独立验证，而非依赖可被博弈的散文叙述。

> **TL;DR:** The paper audits the reconstruction-based faithfulness test used by natural-language autoencoders (NLAs), showing that on the released Qwen-2.5-7B layer-20 pair, explanations reconstruct at r̃=0.84 while only ~2% of their LLM-salient specific claims are flip-grounded, and that standard-recipe co-trained pairs develop private codes (grounded-minus-true gaps 0.30–0.80) in 5/5 sandbox runs. The proposed fix, RECAP (co-trained auxiliary linear predictors on the target model), makes designated content probe-decodable at +0.010 nats cost on Pythia-160M and lifts a fresh probe's AUC from 0.77–0.80 (control) to 0.95–1.00; an external probe then ranks the verbalizer's true claims above false ones at AUC 0.965 vs 0.823 control, surviving a score-gaming adversary (AUC 0.952 vs 0.508 control).

## 核心贡献

- **对重建测试本身的反事实验证（claim-level audit）：** 针对已发布的 Qwen-2.5-7B layer-20 NLA，在 1,517 条声明上做最小对（minimal-pair）翻转审计，证明高重建分数（r̃=0.84）并不意味着逐条声明有依据——仅约 2% 的显著具体声明对翻转敏感，分数主要由输入"主旨"而非具体事实驱动。
- **发现协同训练的"私有暗码"：** 在具有精确真值的合成沙箱中，标准配方在两个域的 5/5 次运行里发展出"协同适应的私有暗码"——重建依赖但内容为假的措辞（grounded-minus-true 差距 0.30–0.80），通过"grounded-vs-true cross"和"evaluator swap"两种新审计协议检测并量化。
- **失败分析（识别机制）：** 证明以重建为目标的训练目标会让原本可解码的内容坍缩（3/3 seeds），冻结探针在持续训练中约 1,500 步内退化到接近随机；并提出用无标签线性映射做重对齐的修复。
- **RECAP 方法（训练目标模型而非阅读器）：** 在目标模型训练时协同训练线性辅助预测头，使指定内容对外部真值保持可被独立新鲜探针解码。沙箱中 5/5 次运行达到 100% 可解码、+0.001 nat 成本、零暗码；在 Pythia-160M 上 K=8/K=64 达 0.95–1.00 AUC。
- **基于探针的监控与对抗鲁棒性：** 在 Pythia-160M 上，新鲜探针把口头器的真声明排在假声明之上（AUC 0.965 vs 0.823 control）；当报告空间对手将分数最优化的谎言时，RECAP 探针仍能标记谎言（AUC 0.952），而控制探针坍缩到随机（0.508），抑制了约 87% 的谎言惩罚却仍被探针识破。

## Key Contributions

- **Counterfactual claim-level audit of a released NLA:** A flip-based minimal-pair audit (n=1,517 claims) of the public Qwen-2.5-7B layer-20 AV/AR pair shows that despite r̃=0.84 reconstruction, only ~2% (2.1% at τ=0.05) of LLM-salient specific claims are flip-grounded, so the score tracks input gist rather than specific facts — a sensitivity-limited lower bound.
- **Discovery of co-adapted private codes:** Under exact synthetic ground truth, the standard recipe develops private codes (false wording that reconstruction depends on) in 5/5 runs across two domains, with grounded-minus-true gaps of 0.30–0.80, detected and quantified via two new audit protocols — the grounded-vs-true cross and the evaluator swap (code-slot grounding collapses from 0.53–0.89 to 0.08–0.23 under an independent evaluator).
- **Failure-mode analysis with identified mechanisms:** The reconstruction-targeted objective (minimize distance between hidden states and their reconstruction) collapses decodable content in 3/3 seeds with unchanged perplexity; frozen probes decay to near-chance within ~1,500 continued-training steps, recoverable via a label-free linear map (rotation recovers ~half, full recovery needs rank ~16–64 non-orthogonal change).
- **RECAP method (target-side decodability supervision):** Co-trained linear heads on the target model that predict external targets, added to the LM loss (Eq. 3). Sandbox: 5/5 runs reach 100% decodability at +0.001 nat, codes absent (0/5), fresh NLAs state designated content at ~1.00 truth/grounding under both co-trained and independent evaluators. Scale (Pythia-160M): 0.95–1.00 fresh-probe AUC vs 0.77–0.80 control.
- **Probe-based monitor with adversarial robustness:** On Pythia-160M a fresh presence probe ranks true claims above false at AUC 0.965 (95% CI [0.956,0.973]) vs 0.823 (CI [0.759,0.879]) control, flagging false claims at 0.93 recall / 0.87 precision. Under a report-space adversary suppressing ~87% of the lie penalty, the RECAP probe still detects lies at AUC 0.952 while the control collapses to 0.508.

## Methodology

The work proceeds in three settings and four analytical moves.

**Settings.** (1) A released NLA: the public Qwen-2.5-7B layer-20 AV/AR pair on in-distribution web text (n=1,517 audited claims). (2) An exact-ground-truth sandbox of two templated synthetic domains (story + marketplace, closed vocabularies, fixed tap position that fixes which slots are retained/faded/unread), so every audit quantity is computable without a judge. (3) Continued pretraining of Pythia-160M with self-supervised heads, where cost is a held-out LM-loss "tax" minus a shared control.

**Reconstruction and grounding definitions.** Reconstruction is a floor-normalized centered cosine r̃ = (cos(ĥ,h)−floor)/(1−floor). A claim c is "grounded" when flipping it alone drops r̃ by Δr̃(c) ≥ τ. Crossing grounding with truth yields four cells; the grounded-but-false cell is a "private code" when the convention is specific to the co-trained pair.

**Audit protocols.** (a) Counterfactual flip audit with per-edit validity filtering (open judge Gemma-4-12B, 99.3% valid edits vs 68.4% for a closed judge). (b) Grounded-vs-true cross. (c) Evaluator swap: score each claim under the co-trained vs an independent reconstructor; the gap r̃co − r̃ind isolates co-adaptation from evaluator quality.

**Failure-mode experiments.** A reconstruction-targeted objective (move activations toward their own reconstruction) is shown to collapse decodable content in 3/3 seeds (consistent with non-contrastive SSL collapse). Frozen-probe staleness is characterized, and a label-free linear map realigns probes in 9/9 cases.

**RECAP objective.** Linear auxiliary heads on the tap-layer hidden state predict external targets y; the head loss is added to the LM loss: J_model = min_{θ,ϕ} L_LM(θ) + w·L_ϕ(h(θ), y), with class-balanced BCE for presence + CE for identity. Crucially, all decodability numbers are reported from *independently fitted fresh probes*, never the heads themselves, to rule out head/model co-adaptation.

**Probe-based monitor and adversary.** A sandbox tool: fresh per-slot probes read the tap; a claim contradicted by a confident probe (p>0.9) is flagged. For the adversary, a true stated word is edited into the absent word that maximizes the recomputed reconstruction score, and the probe's ability to detect the lie is measured.

## Results & Highlights

- On the **released Qwen-2.5-7B layer-20 NLA** (web text, n=1,517 claims), explanations reconstruct at **r̃ = 0.84**, yet only **~2% of LLM-salient specific claims** are flip-grounded (4.2% / 2.1% / 1.6% at τ=0.02/0.05/0.10); specifics decode about **3× worse than gist** (~6% vs ~18% chance-corrected). The append-and-rank control recovers a known-encoded topic at only 28% raw.
- Under **exact synthetic ground truth**, standard-recipe co-trained pairs pass the test at r̃ 0.71–0.86 in **5/5 runs across both domains** while developing private codes with **grounded-minus-true gaps of 0.30–0.80**; the evaluator swap collapses code-slot grounding from **0.53–0.89 to 0.08–0.23** while true-claim grounding survives (cross-grader gap 0.17–0.38 on standard runs vs ~0.003 on RECAP runs).
- The **reconstruction-targeted objective** removes decodable slot content in **3/3 seeds** with perplexity unchanged; freshly retrained probes also lose accuracy, so the information itself is gone. **Frozen probes** decay to near-chance within **1,500 continued-training steps** (3/3 seeds) but the information remains exactly recoverable; a label-free linear map restores native probe performance in **9/9 cases**.
- **RECAP sandbox:** designated content reaches **100% decodability** at **+0.001 nat** in **5/5 runs**, codes absent in **0/5** full-designation runs; fresh NLAs state designated content at **~1.00 truth and grounding** under both co-trained and independent evaluators.
- **RECAP at scale (Pythia-160M):** designated content reaches **0.95–1.00 AUC** under fresh probes at K=8 and K=64 (Table 1) versus a **0.77–0.80 control baseline**; the class-balanced K=64 cost is **+0.010 nats** (paired 95% CI [−0.015,+0.034], 3 seeds). At K=512 the naive presence head scores well while decoding *nothing* until class balancing fixes it (0.97–0.99); for 512 targets into d=768 the cost rises to +0.14–0.20 nats.
- **Verbalizer audit at scale:** context words are stated truly at **0.44 on the RECAP model vs 0.06 on control** (bootstrap CIs 0.39–0.51 vs 0.04–0.09); on shared present content words, RECAP recall is **0.29 vs 0.07** control while stating *fewer* words (0.63 vs 1.02).
- **Probe-based monitor at scale (Pythia-160M):** a fresh probe ranks true claims above false at **AUC 0.965** (95% CI [0.956,0.973]) vs **0.823** ([0.759,0.879]) control, flagging false claims at **0.93 recall / 0.87 precision**. In the sandbox, verification coverage of designated pre-tap content moves from **37% to 100%** with zero false alarms.
- **Adversarial robustness:** a report-space adversary optimizing the deployed score suppresses ~**87% of the lie penalty** (optimal lie lowers r̃ by only 0.022 vs 0.173 for a random lie), yet the RECAP activation probe still flags lies at **AUC 0.952** (0.86 recall) while the control probe collapses to **0.508**. A probe-aware selection adversary degrades RECAP only to AUC 0.909 while driving the control below chance to 0.242.
- **Persistence (Appendix C):** RECAP decodability erodes within 250 steps of head-free fine-tuning (b1/b2 from .983/.992 to .827/.849), establishing it as a **running cost**; intermittent supervision every tenth step retains most decodability (.849/.900/.891 at step 2,000).

## Authors & Credibility

Single author, Hiskias Dingeto, affiliated with StackOne Technologies. The work is unusually rigorous for a single-author paper: it includes per-edit validity controls, per-threshold bootstrap intervals, an empirical flip-noise null, cross-seed replication (5/5 sandbox, 3/7 seeds at scale), impossibility controls (unread slots stay at chance), and an emergence ledger (Table 5) cataloging which phenomena transfer across domains. It cites and positions itself against concurrent frontier work (Pan, Chen & Steinhardt 2026 LatentQA; Karvonen et al. 2025; Fraser-Taliente et al. 2026 NLA; Duan 2026 concurrent staleness findings on 2B/7B models). The released-system audit uses a *public, frozen* model (Qwen-2.5-7B layer-20), making the negative finding independently checkable.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and well-scoped. Prior critiques of activation verbalization are benchmark-level (Li et al. 2025: tasks solvable without internals); this is the first to audit the reconstruction *test itself* per-claim and counterfactually on a released system, and the first to show codes emerge from the *standard* NLA recipe alone (no steganography objective, no adversary) — a distinct finding from the steganography-in-LLMs literature (Roger & Greenblatt 2023; Mathew et al. 2025). RECAP's framing ("train the model, not the reader") inverts the entire field's direction.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The audit instrument is itself validated (floor normalization calibrated to −0.001; flip-noise 95th percentile 0.017 below the smallest threshold τ=0.02; judge validity 99.3%). The evaluator-swap control isolates co-adaptation from evaluator quality, and an independent paraphrase-family evaluator reproduces the pattern within ~0.05 — a genuine robustness check, not hand-waving.
- **Reproducibility:** Mixed. Sandbox is fully specified (8-layer d=256 transformers, 120k stories, grammars in Appendix D, seeds/hyperparameters in appendix) and the released-system audit is on a public model. However, the main scaling claim rests on continued pretraining of Pythia-160M mostly at single seeds, and no code release is mentioned. The most striking numbers (AUC 0.965, +0.010 nat tax) are exactly the kind that depend on target-design choices the paper itself shows are fragile (the K=512 naive-head shortcut is a real trap).
- **Significance:** Conceptually important for interpretability and AI safety: it undercuts the primary faithfulness metric of a fast-growing method family and offers a constructive alternative. The "designated content is checkable but maybe behaviorally inert" caveat is handled honestly.
- **Red flags / honest weaknesses the paper concedes:** (1) Frontier scale is untested — Pythia-160M is tiny. (2) Claims are closed-vocabulary/template-parsed; open-ended faithfulness is unmeasured. (3) RECAP cannot be retrofitted to a frozen model and needs a good target (a poorly chosen one "reports success while decoding nothing," as the K=512 naive head shows). (4) Decodability ≠ verbalizability: the free-form verbalizer conveys only part of what probes can read (verbalizer precision 0.44–0.63 vs probe ~0.80 at matched recall), so the probe, not the prose, is the reliable readout. (5) Designated content can be present yet behaviorally inert (patching the tap moves behavior by only ~0.003).

## Strengths

- Diagnoses *two distinct, mechanistically different* failure modes (gist on the released system; private codes in the sandbox) rather than one generic "models are unfaithful" claim.
- The evaluator swap and grounded-vs-true cross are genuinely new, cheap, and transferable audit primitives.
- RECAP is a constructive fix with a clear thesis (J_model trains θ, J_read does not), and every decodability number uses independent fresh probes to avoid the obvious head–model co-adaptation confound.
- Honest about scope: four explicit limitations, an emergence ledger, and a persistence experiment showing RECAP is a running cost, not a one-time purchase.
- Demonstrates structural immunity to score manipulation and empirical resilience to a probe-aware selection adversary, separating the threat model carefully.

## Limitations

- All quantitative scale results are on Pythia-160M (single seed mostly); frontier-scale transfer is unverified.
- Sandbox and scale audits cover only closed-vocabulary, template-parsed claims; open-ended/long-form faithfulness is unmeasured.
- RECAP must be co-trained in — it cannot retrofit a frozen model — and target design is fragile (K=512 naive head decodes nothing until class balancing).
- Decodability is not verbalizability: the verbalizer conveys only part of probe-readable content, and designated content can be behaviorally inert (tap patching moves behavior by ~0.003), so a model could route computation around a monitored subspace.
- The sandbox probe-based-monitor coverage metric does not transfer to the real model's 1.5% base rate; only detector AUC and a flag precision-recall point are reported at scale.

## Relevance

对研究可解释性与激活解释（activation explanation）忠实度评估高度相关：本文直接审计了自然语言自编码器这一新兴方向的"重建分数"评判标准，揭示了其可被 gist 与私有暗码绕过，并提供 RECAP 作为面向 AI 安全的可验证替代方案。对于关注 LLM 内部表示工程、探针监控与对抗鲁棒性的研究具有方法论参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does RECAP transfer to frontier-scale models (7B–70B+) and to non-templated, open-vocabulary claims, and at what tax in nats / capabilities?
- Can the verbalizer–probe gap (probe precision ~0.80 vs verbalizer 0.44–0.63 at matched recall) be closed by reconstructor redesign, or is it a fundamental limit of free-form generation?
- How does RECAP interact with downstream RLHF / instruction-tuning — does designated-content decodability survive reward-modeling pressure, or only with intermittent re-supervision?
- Are the private codes detectable on other released NLAs (LatentQA-style QA decoders, Activation Oracles), or are they specific to the Fraser-Taliente reconstruction recipe?
- Since designated content can be decodable yet behaviorally inert (tap patching ~0.003), what additional causal test is needed to certify that monitored content is also *used* by the model?
