PN-QNN: Harnessing Physical Noise as a Native Regularizer in Photonic Hybrid Quantum Neural Networks

Farah Elnakal, Alberto Marchisio, Nouhaila Innan, Gabriel Falcao & Muhammad Shafique  ·  NYU Abu Dhabi · Univ. of Coimbra

photonic-quantum-computing quantum-neural-networks regularization noise-injection genetic-algorithm Perceval · MerLin
Key Finding
Seven-parameter photonic physical noise, jointly searched, behaves as a Tikhonov-like regularizer whose sign of effect is not universal across datasets.
Key Metric
+1.45pp

01

Methodology

Perceval + MerLin · SLOS exact backend

The work reframes Quandela Perceval's seven-parameter physical-noise model (brightness, indistinguishability, g(2), g(2) distinguishable, transmittance, phase imprecision, phase error) — normally a fidelity-characterization tool — into a tunable regularizer attached to the photonic QuantumLayer and active in both training and evaluation forward passes.

A physics-grouped genetic algorithm searches the joint 7-D noise space (6 continuous + 1 boolean), with genes grouped by physical origin (source · global · interferometer) so crossover respects subsystem boundaries instead of mixing uniformly. A second-order expansion of the expected loss shows the noise perturbation ξ induces a Tikhonov-like term (first-order term vanishes when μ_ξ ≈ 0), with non-separable strength across the seven parameters.

All conditions, including the noiseless baseline, share Adam + weight-decay=1e-4, so GA-best is measured on top of an already-regularized baseline. The SLOS backend computes exact permanents (not sampling), removing shot noise as a confound.

  • IRIS 4 features → angle encoding → 6 modes / 3 photons → 3-class logits
  • DIGITS 64 → PCA(8) → BN+MLP → 8 modes / 4 photons → Linear→GELU→Dropout(0.1)→10
  • MNIST 784 → PCA(16) → BN + 2×Linear/GELU → 8 modes / 8 photons → Linear→GELU→BN→10

03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • First systematic study of the full seven-parameter photonic noise model as a tunable regularizer, porting Bishop-1995 "training noise ≈ Tikhonov" into photonic QNNs.
  • Physics-aware GA: gene grouping by subsystem (source/global/interferometer) makes crossover respect physical boundaries instead of uniform mixing.
  • Exact SLOS permanent backend removes shot noise as a confound — reported probabilities are exact under the noise model.
  • Methodological honesty: reports the MNIST negative result and the "no single beneficial parameter" sweep finding.

Limitations

  • Architecture confound: three datasets differ in modes, photon count and classical-head depth — dataset sensitivity is not decoupled from circuit design.
  • Simulation only: on real hardware, "noise perturbations are not physically available as of now," so the "free" claim is unverified on-device.
  • Theory is explanatory, not predictive: the Σξ term cannot predict in advance which dataset will benefit.
  • Search cost (20×25×100 epochs/dataset) is substantial, and configs do not transfer across datasets.
  • Small benchmarks only (Iris/Digits/MNIST); no larger datasets or circuits.
04

Questions for Further Exploration

Can a fixed architecture (e.g. 8 modes / 4 photons) with only the dataset varying decouple dataset sensitivity from circuit design?
Can Σξ be estimated explicitly to predict — before training — whether a dataset will benefit from noise regularization?
Is physical-noise regularization complementary or redundant with quantum dropout? Do they stack or cancel?
On real hardware, how do shot noise and finite sampling budget change the conclusions — does "free" still hold?
Are there interpretable physical priors behind the three structurally different GA configs that could cut search cost?
