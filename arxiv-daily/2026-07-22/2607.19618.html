Causal dictionary learning reveals and validates transcription-factor binding features in genomic language models

Sarwan Ali·Columbia University Irving Medical Center ·single-author preprint

Key finding

Causal ablation validates transcription-factor binding features in genomic LMs — what PWM enrichment only correlates with, confounded by GC content and repeats.

Key Finding

Top-k SAE features that look like TF motifs are causally verified by single-direction ablation, separating what the model uses from what it merely contains.

Key Metric
440 / 6,576

CTCF features spuriously flagged by naive PWM enrichment — versus 0 / 15 under causal + GC-matched controls.

Why This Matters

Hundreds of "TF features" reported by PWM enrichment are GC/Alu artifacts. Causal intervention is now the discipline that separates real signal from confound.

Critical Edge

Effect sizes are modest (AUC 0.55–0.63), only 3 TFs / 2 MLM encoders are covered, and 1–8 of 15 features per condition fail validation uncharacterized.

01

Methodology — three components, one causal claim

Step 1 · Sec 3.1

Top-k sparse autoencoders

Encode activations x∈ℝᵈ into sparse code f∈ℝᵐ with m=16d, k=32 active. Dead-feature revival loss; unit-norm decoder. Random-activation init lifts live features 45%→93%.

Step 2 · Sec 3.2

Composition-matched binding test

Bound vs. unbound-motif vs. background windows, GC-matched by rejection sampling to kill the compositional confound. Mann–Whitney U with AUC; GC-robust if |corr(GC)|<0.2.

Step 3 · Sec 3.3

Causal feature ablation

Ablate one direction h′=h−s⁻¹f·d, run the forward pass, measure bound-vs-unbound shift in masked-token KL divergence. Pass: AUC>0.55 (Bonferroni). Two propositions explain why magnitude ≠ specificity.

03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Validates, not just extracts. Moves the field from "looks like a motif" to "model demonstrably uses it," with negative controls that actually return nulls.
  • Cross-tokenization generality. 6-mer (NT) and BPE (DNABERT-2) both work; BPE is if anything sharper.
  • Theoretically grounded. Propositions 1 & 2 give closed-form reasons naive enrichment fails and magnitude attribution misses specificity.
  • Fully computational, public-data-only. Reproducible on NT v2 500M / DNABERT-2 117M + ENCODE + JASPAR 2024.
  • Biologically actionable. GATA1 "context-encoded binding" invites a systematic TF atlas.

Limitations

  • Narrow coverage. Two MLM encoders only; 3 TFs in 1 cell type each. Caduceus / Evo / HyenaDNA are out of scope.
  • Modest effect sizes. AUCs cluster at 0.55–0.63 — establishes causal use, not that features dominate binding representation.
  • Arbitrary thresholds. AUC>0.55 and |corr(GC)|<0.2 are sensible but not derived; no sensitivity analysis.
  • Top-15 cap per condition undercounts true denominator; 1–8 failures uncharacterized.
  • MLM-head dependence. Eq. 6 needs a masked-token head, blocking direct transfer to generative LMs.
  • Single-author preprint — claims await independent replication.
04

Open questions for further work

  1. Transfer to non-MLM LMs? Can an equivalent causal readout be defined for autoregressive (Evo) or state-space (Caduceus/Mamba) models without a masked-token head?
  2. Why do 1–8 of 15 features fail causal validation — GC survivors, redundant collinear directions, or genuine non-causal correlates?
  3. Threshold sensitivity. Does the 7–14/15 band reflect biology or pipeline saturation as AUC>0.55 and top-N=15 vary?
  4. Invert the machinery for steering. What happens when you amplify rather than ablate a validated feature on designed sequences?
  5. Is "context-encoded binding" general? Do other short-motif, context-dependent TFs show weak motif-selectivity but strong causal validation?
  6. Cross-architecture equivalence. Are NT and DNABERT-2 features for the same TF functionally equivalent, or different binding "vocabularies"?
  7. Scale to a TF atlas. Compute cost per TF, and does live-fraction behavior hold at the long tail of poorly characterized factors?
