Reference-Free Evaluation of Reasoning in Open-Ended Question Answering

Guneet Singh Kohli · Yuxiang Zhou · Michael Schlichtkrull · Gregory Dean · Maria Liakata  —  Queen Mary University of London · Temple University
llm-as-judge reasoning-evaluation natural-language-inference medical-reasoning reference-free UroReason benchmark
Key Finding

A reference-free audit segments an LLM reasoning trace, labels local premise–target links with NLI into a hypergraph, then a deterministic AND–OR search flags each segment SUPPORTED, UNSUPPORTED, or ORPHAN.

Key Finding
NLI-labelled hypergraph + deterministic backward AND–OR search audits reasoning step-by-step — no gold reference, domain-independent.
Key Metric
+0.470
0.079 → 0.549
Balanced-F1 lift for Qwen3-30B on UroReason when demoted from judge to local NLI source.
Why This Matters
Direct judges collapse on open-ended clinical reasoning (TNR as low as 0.041). A structural NLI-hypergraph audit rescues them — a reference-free path to trustworthy high-stakes evaluation.
Critical Edge
The audit is not LLM-independent — local NLI labels still inherit labeller bias, and “SUPPORTED” means internally grounded, not factually correct.
01

Methodology — three moving parts

audits internal grounding, not external fact
① Local signal

NLI over premise-set → target

For each segment, candidate premise sets (mmin ≤ |P| ≤ mmax) are enumerated and labelled with a four-way scheme. Cheap, localised, the only place an LLM is used.

② Global structure

Directed labelled hypergraph

Later segments often depend on several earlier ones jointly. Pruning keeps ENTAILMENT/IMPLIED as support edges, drops NEUTRAL, and bounds IMPLIED by locality τ.

③ Audit decision

Deterministic backward AND–OR search

Each segment is a sink; OR chooses a supporting hypernode, AND requires all its premises grounded down to the context leaf S₁. Compositional and bias-resistant.

ENTAILMENT
strong deductive support
IMPLIED
weaker abductive support — the dominant mode in open-ended reasoning
NEUTRAL
insufficient information — discarded
CONTRADICTION
conflict — kept as negative evidence

03

Strengths & limitations

Strengths PRO

  • Clean, well-motivated gap: reference-free, open-ended, abductive reasoning audit — left open by math-heavy step-level benchmarks.
  • Elegant decomposition: cheap local NLI + deterministic, bias-resistant AND–OR composition. The four-way NLI with an explicit abductive IMPLIED label is a real conceptual move.
  • UroReason is a realistic resource — real clinical cases, segment-level physician annotation, multi-source evidence; explicitly not saturated MCQ.
  • Interpretable failure taxonomy (SUPPORTED/UNSUPPORTED/ORPHAN) and a stable, actionable math-vs-medicine contrast.
  • Honest scope framing (structural grounding, not factual verification) with a limitations section that names the key threats.

Limitations CON

  • Not LLM-independent — local NLI labels still carry labeller bias; “deterministic” applies to composition only. A specialised small NLI model is future work.
  • Absolute balanced-F1 peaks at 0.577; Class-0 (problematic-segment) detection remains the weak axis (TNR ≤ 0.641). Headline gains lean on baselines being catastrophically bad.
  • Single-run, greedy decoding, no self-consistency — and test-time scaling could lift the very direct-judge baselines they beat, narrowing the gap.
  • UroReason is small and single-specialty (40 paediatric-urology cases, 305 segments) — high relevance, thin statistical power.
  • Audits internal grounding, not external fact — “SUPPORTED” can still be factually wrong if an early premise is false.
04

Open questions

  1. If test-time scaling (self-consistency, debate, ensembles) is given to the direct judges, how much of the UroReason gap survives?
  2. How sensitive are the SUPPORTED/UNSUPPORTED/ORPHAN rates — and the math-vs-medicine contrast — to the calibration and NEUTRAL-bias of the NLI label source?
  3. Could a small, specialised NLI model trained for trace-auditing match open-weight LLMs as a label source, fully decoupling the audit from LLM behaviour?
  4. Coupling context-grounded leaves to retrieval / external evidence — how would that change labels and the correlation with physician judgement?
  5. Does the orphan-vs-broken-chain signature generalise beyond paediatric urology (internal medicine, law, science) — and can it serve as a training signal, not just an eval signal?
  6. UroReason collapses hedging / partially-true / incorrect → all y=0. Does separating “hedging” from “incorrect” change the evaluator ranking or expose a masked failure mode?
