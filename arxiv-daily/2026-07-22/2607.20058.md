> **一句话总结：** 本文用 Jacobian lens、无选项状态几何与因果干预三种互补手段，证明 google/gemma-4-E4B-it 中材料科学机制信息可在隐状态中被可复现地读出；在 60 条本构律基准上，匹配的状态变化以 AUC 1.000 分离正/逆律、正确判向 39/40，但绝对几何与多数可读词汇并不能唯一编码物理本构，因果控制仅在受限上下文中成立。

> **TL;DR:** On the open-weight Gemma-4-E4B-it model, the author shows materials-science mechanism info takes three separable forms: readable vocabulary (3 fitted Jacobian lenses reproduce concept ranks with Spearman ≈0.998), constitutive orientation carried by matched state transformations (60-law benchmark separates direct vs inverse laws at AUC 1.000, classifies 39/40 directional laws; lexical TF-IDF controls near 0.5), and causally effective internal directions (a frozen grain-size direction reverses correctly for 12/12 refinement vs coarsening conditions, beating random/unrelated/direct controls). Absolute hidden-state geometry and most readable words do NOT uniquely encode constitutive physics, and causal steering fails to transfer to new answer vocabularies (increase/decrease) and the inverse Hall-Petch regime.

## 核心贡献

- 首次将 Jacobian lens 方法从闭源 Claude 模型迁移到开放权重的 Gemma-4-E4B-it，并在间接、不直接点名的材料科学描述上系统检验其可迁移性。
- 明确区分了三种可分离的证据层次：可读性（vocabulary readability）、表征（relational representation）、因果使用（causal use），并针对每一层设计了预注册（frozen）的成功判据与对照。
- 提出一个 60 条本构律的反事实基准（20 直接律、20 逆律、20 中性律，跨 13 个领域），通过"匹配状态变化"消除绝对位置的符号歧义，证明了任务诱导的单调本构朝向抽象（Spearman ρ=0.910，直接 vs 逆律 AUC=1.000）。
- 通过严格的可辨识性审计（identifiability audit）揭示了一个被忽视的基准设计陷阱：当物理极性 y=sx 时，族内"相同/不同物理结果"在代数上恒等于"相同/不同数值方向"，因此该类图结构无法独立诊断物理。
- 用前瞻性因果干预证明：一个冻结的 grain-size 方向在所有 6 个合金对、12 个条件下都能在精炼/粗化间正确反转输出（+0.852 log odds），但同一方向在 increase/decrease 词汇和反 Hall-Petch 区制下转移失败——界定了因果效应的边界。
- 提出表示感知（representation-aware）的科学模型训练奖励公式 R = R_answer + λ_m R_mechanism + λ_c R_counterfactual + λ_s R_specificity + λ_u R_uncertainty − λ_ℓ R_lexical-shortcut，并警示读出欺骗（readout hacking）风险。

## Key Contributions

- Transfers the Jacobian lens technique from closed Claude models to the open-weight Gemma-4-E4B-it, validating it on indirect materials-science descriptions where mechanism terms are deliberately omitted.
- Separates interpretability into three distinct, independently tested evidence tiers (readability, relational representation, causal use), each with pre-registered ("frozen") success criteria and matched controls.
- Introduces a 60-law counterfactual benchmark (20 direct, 20 inverse, 20 neutral laws across 13 physics domains) that breaks the y=sx label alias via matched numerical-reversal contrasts plus a separately calibrated neutral class.
- Provides a rigorous identifiability audit proving that within-family "same/different physical outcome" collapses algebraically to "same/different numerical direction," so option-free similarity graphs cannot identify absolute physical polarity — a benchmark-design lesson for the field.
- Demonstrates prospectively confirmed causal steering: one frozen layer-16 grain direction reverses its effect correctly for 12/12 refinement vs coarsening conditions across 6 alloys (+0.852 log odds), yet fails to transfer to new answer vocabularies and the inverse Hall-Petch regime, precisely bounding where causation holds.
- Proposes a representation-aware training objective that rewards internal scientific structure (mechanism readability, counterfactual consistency, intervention specificity, uncertainty, anti-shortcut) beyond final-answer correctness, with safeguards against readout hacking.

## Methodology

The study uses the frozen open-weight model `google/gemma-4-E4B-it` (42 transformer layers, 2,560-dim hidden state, 262,144-token vocabulary, immutable revision a4c2d58) and three independently fitted Jacobian lenses (each fit on 1,000 WikiText-103 128-token records, seeds 0/1/2, checkpoints in `lamm-mit/gemma4-jacobian-lenses`). The Jacobian lens estimates the average downstream map from a source layer to the penultimate layer (layer 40) via reverse-mode autodiff, then applies Gemma's own fixed decoder; direct unembedding sends the raw state through the same decoder. Both yield a vocabulary ranking, differing only in whether downstream transport is modeled.

The research design separates three complementary evidence types:
1. **Readability** — controlled recovery (50 held-out prompts × 10 mechanism families × 5 phrasings, 150 predeclared one-token concepts measured against the 262k vocabulary in a fixed 38–92% layer band) and target-free open decoding (no supplied answer list, 3-fit consensus agreement, frozen function-word and leakage filters).
2. **Relational representation** — (a) within-mechanism nearest-centroid geometry on full 2,560-dim states (train-on-4-test-on-5); (b) a lexical-adversarial 24-triplet cohort where counterfactuals are the closer lexical match; (c) a 72-stem option-free graph at three prompt boundaries; (d) a 60-law matched-reversal benchmark that subtracts matched numerical-increase/decrease prompt states and calibrates zero with independent neutral laws.
3. **Causal use** — (a) broad steering screen (3 mechanism directions × 30 conditions × 5 doses); (b) prospective frozen grain-size confirmation (6 matched alloy pairs); (c) exploratory full-state activation patching (reverse-relation donor states); (d) a frozen 6-mechanism factorial cross-family patch.

Identifiability audits use graph-isomorphism networks, label-blind graph matching, spectral clustering, and exhaustive balanced-partition enumeration to test what the graph can and cannot identify.

## Results & Highlights

- **Jacobian lens reproducibility is near-perfect:** three independent fits give Spearman rank correlations of 0.9987, 0.9980, 0.9978 across 150 controlled concept pairs, versus only 0.212 between the 3-fit Jacobian mean and direct unembedding.
- **Selective, not universal, vocabulary gains:** mean Jacobian recovery AUC 0.0258 vs direct 0.0128 (+100.8% relative), but 36 of 50 prompts are zero under both readouts; the family-level 95% interval (−0.0187 to +0.0486) includes zero and the one-sided sign-flip test gives p=0.234. Spectacular individual cases exist (corrosion rank 1/1/1 vs direct 111; toughness 11/11/11 vs 12,063).
- **Option-free graph is real but non-diagnostic:** natural-question-end Jacobian AUC 0.642 (p=0.012), but the cross-mechanism counter-numeric endpoint falls to 0.471 and direct/raw states are similar or stronger.
- **Matched state transformations recover constitutive orientation:** across 60 frozen laws, direct-law vs inverse-law AUC = 1.000 (95% interval 1.000–1.000), the neutral cutoff classifies 39 of 40 directional laws, and the ordinal relation holds with Spearman ρ=0.910 (permutation p=0.00001); word/character TF-IDF controls reach only 0.505/0.530.
- **Causal steering works in-context:** the frozen grain direction reverses correctly in all 12 conditions (+0.852 log odds; matched-minus-random +0.774, p=0.031), survives half-dose (+0.484), and full-state patching shifts the answer by +6.249 log-odds (95% interval 5.663–6.795) with equal-distance controls near zero.
- **Causal transfer is bounded:** in the increase/decrease and inverse Hall-Petch cohorts, 23 of 24 conditions merely moved toward the designated positive word regardless of physics; the cross-mechanism factorial patch transferred numerical direction (+0.571) more strongly than physical outcome (+0.385) and only 4 of 6 donor families passed, failing the breadth gate.
- **Exact inverse-law answer accuracy is only 53.8%**, even though the internal relational ordering is near-perfect — separating stable latent organization from reliable output conversion.

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author paper by **Markus J. Buehler** (MIT, Departments of Civil & Environmental Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, and Schwarzman College of Computing). Buehler is a well-established researcher in computational materials science with an extensive track record on AI-for-materials and mechanistic interpretability of LLMs. The work exhibits unusually rigorous methodology: pre-registered ("frozen") endpoints declared before inspecting outputs, matched controls (random directions, unrelated mechanisms, direct directions, lexical TF-IDF, equal-distance patching), explicit identifiability proofs, and honest reporting of failed endpoints (broad late-window primary, multi-token breadth gate, transfer cohorts). The code/data artifacts are released (Hugging Face `lamm-mit/gemma4-jacobian-lenses`, immutable model revision).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. Combines Jacobian-lens transfer to an open model, a 60-law counterfactual benchmark, and a formal identifiability audit — the y=sx alias proof is a genuinely novel benchmark-design contribution. The distinction between absolute geometry, matched transformation, and causal use is conceptually clarifying.
- **Rigor:** Very high. The paper repeatedly separates exploration from confirmation, freezes endpoints before outputs, applies multiplicity correction (FDR q-values), uses exact permutation tests, and reports negative results prominently (e.g., 36/50 null prompts, cross-numeric AUC 0.471, transfer cohorts failing). Statistical machinery (hierarchical bootstrap, family-clustered intervals, balanced permutation nulls) is appropriate.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong. Open-weight model at a pinned revision, released lens checkpoints, fixed layer grid and band, all 50 prompts and declared terms in Supplementary, and 2,400 intervention rows linked. The main reproducibility risk is the single-model, single-rater (one OpenAI model) design.
- **Significance:** Moderate-to-high for interpretability methodology; moderate for materials science per se. The causal findings are context-bound (one decision format, one model), so practical engineering impact is limited until transfer is shown.
- **Red flags:** (1) Single author, single model (Gemma-4-E4B-it) — generalization across scales/families untested. (2) WikiText fitting proxy rather than a materials corpus. (3) The strongest positive graph/patch results reuse inspected cohorts, so some analyses are post-hoc despite frozen sub-protocols. (4) The headline causal effect is confined to the higher/lower format and the conventional Hall-Petch regime, with explicit failure on transfer. (5) Automated semantic rater is one model repeated 5×, not an expert panel. (6) Small N in places (6 grain pairs → floor p=0.03125).

## Strengths

- Intellectual honesty: failed endpoints and null results are foregrounded, not buried; the paper explicitly states "similarity is not causation" and that ribbons/UMAP axes are not reasoning transcripts.
- The identifiability audit is a methodological gem — it algebraically proves why a flat similarity graph cannot recover physical polarity and shows the failure empirically (graph-net physical-polarity AUC 0.259).
- The matched-reversal design (subtracting numerical-increase vs decrease states, holding everything else fixed) elegantly isolates constitutive orientation from absolute position.
- Comprehensive matched controls at every stage (random, unrelated-mechanism, direct, lexical, equal-distance).
- Clear three-tier framework (readability / representation / causation) that other interpretability studies could adopt.

## Limitations

- Single model and single author; no cross-scale or cross-family validation.
- The causal steering effect is vocabulary- and regime-dependent: fails for increase/decrease words and the inverse Hall-Petch crossover.
- The neutral-anchored relational result relies on an explicit two-stage instruction scaffold and one development-selected layer/direction; does not demonstrate spontaneous use.
- Exact output accuracy for inverse laws is only 53.8%, so stable internal ordering does not translate to reliable answers.
- The strongest graph/patch results reuse cohorts whose states were already inspected; post-hoc character remains despite frozen sub-protocols.
- Target-free decoding still admits fragments and generic words; zero of 523 target-free words survives network enrichment correction.
- Practical materials-engineering utility is unproven; the work is primarily a measurement/science-of-interpretability contribution.

## Relevance

对从事大模型机理可解释性（mechanistic interpretability）与科学推理表征研究的读者高度相关：它给出了从"可读词汇—关系几何—因果干预"三层递进的实证范式，并提供了可直接复用的反事实基准设计与可辨识性审计方法。对关注 LLM 是否真正"理解"物理/工程机制、而非依赖词汇捷径的研究者，本文提供了清晰的判据与负面教训。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the matched-reversal relational abstraction (60-law, ρ=0.910) hold across other open-weight model families (Llama, Qwen, Mistral) and scales, or is it Gemma-specific?
- Can the causal grain-size direction be discovered without the explicit two-stage equation scaffold — i.e., does constitutive orientation survive an option-free, free-form prompt?
- Would representation-aware fine-tuning (the proposed R formula) actually improve held-out scientific behavior, or does readout hacking dominate under direct optimization?
- Why does full-state patching transfer the numerical-direction feature more strongly than the physical-outcome feature — is this a general property of late-layer decision circuits?
- Can the y=sx identifiability alias be broken with graded (non-binary) equation-predicted responses, and would that yield a transferable cross-mechanism materials graph?
