> **一句话总结：** 本文在一个受控的合成 Lean 战术预测基准上证明，电路（circuit）级别的结论是否成立，取决于"提取哪种图""用什么剪枝阈值"以及"在什么粒度上比较"——精确的边级重合度极低（有时跌到随机基线水平），而更粗的"注意力头集合"和"按图大小的条件排序"则在这些选择下保持稳定；作者据此提出电路提取研究的报告规范。

> **TL;DR:** In a controlled synthetic Lean tactic-prediction benchmark, whether two checkpoints "share a circuit" depends heavily on which extracted graph is reported, the pruning loss threshold, and the comparison level: exact edge overlap is low (structural Jaccard@10 = 0.163 success / 0.142 near-miss, sometimes at random-baseline level) while coarser summaries — selected attention-head sets (Jaccard@10 = 0.666 / 0.554, far above a 0.199 / 0.185 random baseline) and RL-condition size rankings — stay stable. Across dense vs. 75%-sparse RL checkpoints of one transformer, the largest compositional-task RL gains (+24.64 and +31.27 accuracy points) come with the largest bridge fraction. The authors distill the findings into a reporting practice for circuit-extraction studies.

## 核心贡献

1. **提出并形式化了"电路声明的欠确定性"问题。** 论证"保留行为并不唯一确定一个电路"：保留行为（behavior preservation）只是必要条件，所得到的电路结论依赖于若干通常被隐含的方法学选择——报告哪个提取图、用什么剪枝阈值、注意力头的 Q/K 是合并还是分开、以及在什么粒度（边级 vs 头集合级）上比较。

2. **设计了受控的合成 Lean 战术预测基准。** 该基准有固定的证明规则、随机的表层形式（名字、干扰前提），以及"已知于构造"的原子（单规则）与组合（多规则）任务结构。这使得电路间的差异可以被归因于提取/比较的选择，而非任务本身的变化——这正是自然语言任务中难以分离的混淆因素。

3. **系统刻画了"哪些描述稳定、哪些不稳定"。** 通过跨稠密与权重稀疏（25%–90%）checkpoint、三种 RL 初始化条件、稠密 vs 75% 稀疏的配对比较，发现：(a) 精确边级重合度低且敏感（near-miss 分裂上与随机不可区分，p=0.106），(b) 注意力头集合级重合度高且远超随机基线，(c) 三种图（core circuit / extended support graph / fixed-loss pruning graph）在 Q/K 表示和损失预算变化下都保持相同的 RL 条件排序。

4. **将电路结构与行为改善相关联。** 提出了"bridge fraction"指标（组合任务电路节点中超出对应原子任务电路并集的比例），发现获得最大组合任务精度提升的 curriculum-initialized RL 条件也拥有最大的 bridge fraction。

5. **提炼出电路提取研究的报告规范（reporting practice）。** 要求研究明确声明：所报告的图、提取规则与剪枝阈值、Q/K 节点表示、支撑结论的比较粒度、以及界定解释范围的边界/负对照检查。

## Key Contributions

1. **Articulates and demonstrates the under-determination of circuit claims.** The paper argues that behavior preservation under ablation does not single out a unique circuit; the empirical claim a circuit supports depends on which extracted graph is reported, the pruning threshold, whether Q/K attention support is merged or separated, and the comparison level (exact edges vs. head sets).

2. **Introduces a controlled synthetic Lean tactic-prediction benchmark.** Fixed proof rules with randomized surface form and an atomic-vs-compositional structure that is *known by construction* lets the authors attribute differences between extracted circuits to extraction/comparison choices rather than to uncontrolled task change — the confound that plagues natural-language circuit analyses.

3. **Maps out precisely which circuit descriptions are stable and which are not.** Across dense and weight-sparse checkpoints and three RL initialization conditions, exact edge overlap is low and sensitive (statistically indistinguishable from a random top-k baseline on the near-miss split, p = 0.106), while the selected attention-head set and the circuit-size ranking of RL conditions stay stable.

4. **Connects circuit structure to behavior via the "bridge fraction."** Curriculum-initialized RL — which yields the largest compositional-task accuracy gains (+24.64 and +31.27 points) — also has the largest share of compositional-task circuit nodes outside the matched atomic-task circuits, linking behavioral recovery to circuit-level structure beyond the atomic circuits.

5. **Distills a concrete reporting practice.** The authors call on circuit-extraction studies to state the reported graph, extraction rule and pruning threshold, Q/K node representation, comparison level supporting each claim, and boundary/negative-control checks.

## Methodology

**Benchmark (Section 3.1).** Nine synthetic Lean tactic-prediction tasks: four atomic and five compositional. Each example is a proof state (hypotheses + goal) with a target tactic (e.g., `exact And.intro h_p h_q` for atomic AND; `exact Or.inl (And.intro h_p h_q)` for compositional AND-OR). Names, distractors, and samples are randomized while proof rules stay fixed. Behavioral results are reported on two independently sampled suites (Suite 1, Suite 2) of 100 examples per task to control sampling noise.

**Model & training (Section 3.2).** A single 8-layer transformer (d_model=2048, n_head=128, d_head=16, d_mlp=8192, context 128, ~415.7M params) trained at five sparsity levels (dense, 25%, 50%, 75%, 90% weight-sparse via top-k masking), three seeds each. Training data families: baseline narrow, moderately expanded, extensively expanded (same proof rules, varying surface form). Supervised runs use 5B tokens. RL uses GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimization) with composition-only prompts; three RL initialization conditions differ only in the upstream supervised checkpoint (Curriculum / Atomic-only / Composition-only).

**Three extracted circuit objects (Section 3.3):**
- **Core circuit** — compact prediction-preserving subgraph from differentiable-mask extraction (De Cao et al. 2022; Bhaskar et al. 2024; Yu et al. 2025), with exact-match tactic accuracy as the primary criterion and masked objective as tiebreaker.
- **Extended support graph** — same machinery but a wider graph retaining surrounding read/write/routing structure.
- **Fixed-loss pruning graph** — smallest discrete circuit whose ablated loss stays below threshold τ (main point τ=0.12; sweep over {0.04, 0.08, 0.12, 0.16, 0.20}).

**Q/K representation factor.** Coupled (one merged `attn_qk` node) vs. factorized (separate `attn_q`, `attn_k` nodes), with extraction settings otherwise fixed.

**Metrics (Section 3.4).** Behavior (exact-match accuracy). Jaccard@10 for exact-edge overlap (structural/semantic/routing relations) and routing-head overlap, with 95% bootstrap CIs over 16 matched task-object entries per split. Node fractions, coupled-vs-factorized Q/K gaps, and bridge fraction `BridgeFrac(t) = |C_t \ ∪ C^(i)_t| / |C_t|`. Three negative controls: matched atomic, mismatched atomic, random. Graph-level analysis anchors on dense and 75%-sparse checkpoints.

## Results & Highlights

1. **Routing-head sets are far more reproducible than exact edge lists (Figure 3; Tables 11, 12, 18).** For the paired dense vs. 75%-sparse RL comparison, routing-head Jaccard@10 is **0.666 (success)** and **0.554 (near-miss)**, while exact-edge (structural) Jaccard@10 is only **0.163 / 0.142**. Against random top-k baselines, head-level overlap stays far above chance (**0.666 vs. 0.199** success; **0.554 vs. 0.185** near-miss); on the near-miss split, exact-edge overlap is statistically indistinguishable from chance (0.142 vs. 0.117, **p = 0.106**).

2. **Object-level summaries preserve RL-condition orderings under both Q/K choice and loss budget (Figure 5; Tables 13–16).** At τ=0.12, curriculum coupled-vs-factorized node-fraction gaps are tiny: core circuit **0.008 / 0.010** (moderately/extensively expanded), extended support **0.003 / 0.013**, fixed-loss pruning **0.015 / 0.024** (max 0.024 across all cells). All pairwise RL-condition orderings agree **3/3** for every object and family; under bootstrap the largest pairwise flip probability is 0.232. Sweeping τ ∈ {0.04…0.20} keeps the curriculum gap ≤ 0.055 with 3/3 ordering agreement at every τ.

3. **Largest compositional RL gains track the largest core-circuit bridge fraction (Figure 6; Tables 10, 13).** Curriculum-initialized RL improves compositional accuracy by **+24.64 points (43.83 → 68.47)** in the moderately expanded family and **+31.27 points (46.73 → 78.00)** in the extensively expanded family (Suite 1, averaged over 5 sparsities). The same two curriculum cells have the largest core bridge fractions (**0.343 and 0.161**) versus **≤ 0.054** for other RL conditions; atomic-only and composition-only RL mean accuracies stay ≤ 33.37.

4. **Expanded training distributions reduce supervised compositional accuracy** at the graph-extraction sparsity points (Figure 2B), even though 75% sparsity maintains strong accuracy under the baseline family (Figure 2A).

5. **A recurring read-to-write functional path** (late-position read → compact Q/K selector → small value-vector group → attn_write → downstream MLP raising target tactic-token logits) recurs across dense and sparse checkpoints, even when the literal edge list differs (Figure 4).

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are **Yang Sheng** (Fudan University & Shanghai Innovation Institute) and **Jie Fu** (IQuest Research). The work is methodologically careful: it uses three seeds per sparsity level, two independently sampled evaluation suites, 95% bootstrap confidence intervals, three negative-control conditions (matched atomic, mismatched atomic, random), random top-k baselines for every overlap metric, an explicit a priori comparison anchor (dense vs. 75%-sparse), and a loss-budget sweep to test pruning-threshold dependence. Code is released at github.com/Stepuuu/circuit-extraction-stability. The paper sits squarely in an active line — it builds directly on Elhage et al. 2021 (transformer circuits), Wang et al. 2023 (IOI), Conmy et al. 2023 (ACD), Gao et al. 2025 (weight-sparse interpretable circuits), and the robustness critiques of Miller et al. 2024, Shi et al. 2024, Tigges et al. 2024. The central contribution is a methodological/empirical clarification rather than a new model, and the honesty about scope (association, not causation; two curriculum cells) adds credibility.

## Critical Assessment

The paper's strength is that it isolates a real and widely under-reported problem — circuit claims are under-determined by behavior preservation — and studies it in a setting where the task structure is known by construction, something almost impossible in natural-language circuit analyses. Three points deserve emphasis:

- **The core methodological argument is well-supported.** The same checkpoint pair (dense vs. 75%-sparse RL) gives Jaccard@10 of 0.666 at the head level but 0.163 at the exact-edge level (success split). This is direct, controlled evidence that "do these checkpoints share a circuit?" is ill-posed until the comparison level is fixed.
- **The stability claims are checked against sensible baselines.** The head-level overlap is far above a random top-k baseline (0.666 vs. 0.199), whereas near-miss exact-edge overlap is not (0.142 vs. 0.117, p = 0.106). This is exactly the kind of negative control the field has been asking for.
- **The behavior–structure link is explicitly hedged.** The bridge-fraction association rests on only the two curriculum cells; the authors repeatedly label it a "directional pattern," "observed association," and "not a causal claim" — a level of restraint that is appropriate given n=2 driving cells.

The biggest caveat is scope: a single 8-layer, ~415.7M-parameter transformer, a synthetic Lean family with shared proof-state rules (which the authors note may make format-level cues more salient than in heterogeneous families), and graph evidence local to 16+16 matched task-object entries at two checkpoints. The boundary tests (Appendix H) cover behavior under format/distractor/depth shifts, but circuit-level stability under those shifts is explicitly left as a separate validation target.

## Strengths

- Controlled, construction-known atomic/compositional structure that removes the task-confound blocking this kind of audit in NLP settings.
- Clean decomposition of the otherwise-muddy "circuit extraction" procedure into three explicit objects, an explicit Q/K representation factor, and an explicit pruning-threshold factor.
- Quantitative, baseline-referenced overlap analysis with bootstrap CIs and p-values, rather than anecdotal graph comparisons.
- Actionable output: a concrete reporting practice (state the graph, rule, threshold, Q/K representation, comparison level, and boundary checks).
- Honest framing of the behavior–structure association as directional, not causal.

## Limitations

- **Scale and architecture scope.** A single 8-layer ~415.7M-parameter transformer; generalization to larger models, other architectures, and more heterogeneous benchmarks is unverified (explicitly named as next step in Appendix J).
- **Graph evidence is local.** Exact-edge overlap covers only 16+16 matched task-object entries at dense and 75%-sparse checkpoints with representative exemplars, not frequency estimates over a large graph set.
- **Pruning-threshold dependence is only partially swept.** The fixed-loss pruning graph depends on τ; although a 5-point sweep is reported, the discrete object is inherently threshold-dependent.
- **Bridge-fraction association rests on two cells.** The behavior–structure link is driven by the two curriculum-initialized cells; it is an association, not an effect size or causal claim.
- **Shared format cues.** The shared proof-state generation rules may make format-level cues more observable than in heterogeneous task families, potentially inflating apparent cross-checkpoint stability.
- **Circuit stability under distribution shift is not tested.** Boundary tests cover behavioral shift; circuit-level stability under shift is a separate, unaddressed validation target.

## Relevance

这项工作对"机制可解释性（mechanistic interpretability）"社区有直接的方法学价值。任何从事电路发现（circuit discovery）、注意力头归因、稀疏模型可解释性、或对可解释性方法的稳健性评估的研究者都应关注：它具体指出了"两个 checkpoint 是否共享一个电路"这种常见结论对若干隐含选择高度敏感，并给出了可操作的报告规范。对于做定理证明（Lean/Coq）+ 强化学习的读者，其合成 Lean 战术预测基准、GRPO 微调流程、以及"RL 改善伴随新结构"的观察也有参考价值。从更广的视角看，它回应了近期关于"完全逆向工程大模型内部计算是否现实"的争论，与 Nanda et al. 2025 的"部分理解、下游验证"立场一致。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- 这些敏感性结论在更大模型（如数十亿参数）和其他架构（Mamba/RWKV 等非 Transformer）上是否依然成立？粗粒度（头集合、排序）稳定、细粒度（边）不稳定的模式是普遍的吗？
- 如果把同样的审计搬到自然语言任务（IOI、实体追踪），在控制了任务结构后，"边级不稳健、头级稳健"的结论是否复现？需要什么样的控制才能在 NLP 中近似"已知于构造"的任务结构？
- bridge fraction 与组合泛化之间的关联是因果的吗？能否通过受控干预（强制增删 bridge 节点）来验证"额外结构是组合能力提升的原因"？
- 是否存在一个理论上可证明的"识别性下界"：在何种条件下，保留行为才能在指定粒度上唯一确定电路？这与因果抽象（causal abstraction）和 sheaf 发现工作的不可识别性结论如何统一？
- 论文报告的 Q/K 合并/分离的微小差异（max gap 0.024）是否会在更细的节点划分（如分头分位置）下变大？比较粒度的选择本身是否也该被报告为连续谱而非二元选择？
