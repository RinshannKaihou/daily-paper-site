Train the Model, Not the Reader: Decodability Supervision for Verifiable Activation Explanations

Hiskias Dingeto · StackOne Technologies
interpretability activation-explanation mechanistic-faithfulness probe-decodability representation-engineering ai-safety
◆ Key Finding
High reconstruction scores in natural-language autoencoders mask unfaithful explanations — RECAP supervises the model itself so designated content stays independently probe-decodable.
Key Metric
0.965 AUC
A fresh probe ranks the verbalizer's true claims above false ones at AUC 0.965 vs 0.823 control. Under a report-space adversary, RECAP holds 0.952 while control collapses to 0.508.
Why This Matters
Undercuts the primary faithfulness metric of a fast-growing interpretability family and offers a verifiable, adversarially robust alternative for AI safety.
Critical Edge
All scale results are on Pythia-160M (mostly single seed); frontier-scale transfer, open-vocabulary claims, and behavioral inertness of designated content remain unverified.

01 Methodology — three settings, four moves

The work audits the reconstruction test itself, then proposes a constructive fix.

1
Released NLA audit — public Qwen-2.5-7B layer-20 AV/AR pair; 1,517 claims flip-tested on web text.
2
Exact sandbox — two templated domains (story + marketplace); closed vocabularies make every audit quantity computable without a judge.
3
Continued pretraining — Pythia-160M with self-supervised heads; cost reported as a held-out LM-loss "tax" minus shared control.

Audit primitives: (a) counterfactual flip audit with per-edit validity filtering (open judge Gemma-4-12B, 99.3% valid edits); (b) grounded-vs-true cross; (c) evaluator swap — score each claim under the co-trained vs an independent reconstructor; the gap isolates co-adaptation from evaluator quality.

RECAP objective (Eq. 3): trains θ on the target model, not the reader.

Jmodel = minθ,ϕ LLM(θ) + w · Lϕ(h(θ), y)

All decodability numbers reported from independently fitted fresh probes, never the heads themselves — ruling out head/model co-adaptation.

03 Strengths

  • Diagnoses two mechanistically distinct failure modes (gist on released system; private codes in sandbox) — not a generic "models are unfaithful" claim.
  • The evaluator swap and grounded-vs-true cross are genuinely new, cheap, transferable audit primitives.
  • RECAP is a constructive fix with a clear thesis (Jmodel trains θ, Jread does not); every decodability number uses independent fresh probes.
  • Honest about scope: four explicit limitations, an emergence ledger, and a persistence experiment showing RECAP is a running cost, not a one-time purchase.
  • Demonstrates structural immunity to score manipulation and empirical resilience to a probe-aware selection adversary.

04 Limitations

  • All quantitative scale results on Pythia-160M (mostly single seed); frontier-scale transfer unverified.
  • Sandbox and scale audits cover only closed-vocabulary, template-parsed claims; open-ended faithfulness unmeasured.
  • RECAP cannot retrofit a frozen model — must be co-trained in; target design is fragile (K=512 naive head decodes nothing until class balancing).
  • Decodability ≠ verbalizability: verbalizer conveys only part of probe-readable content; designated content can be behaviorally inert (tap patching moves behavior by ~0.003).
  • Sandbox monitor coverage metric does not transfer to the real model's 1.5% base rate; only detector AUC reported at scale.

05 Questions for Further Exploration

Does RECAP transfer to frontier-scale models (7B–70B+) and non-templated, open-vocabulary claims — and at what tax in nats / capabilities?
Can the verbalizer–probe gap (probe precision ~0.80 vs verbalizer 0.44–0.63 at matched recall) be closed by reconstructor redesign, or is it a fundamental limit of free-form generation?
How does RECAP interact with downstream RLHF / instruction-tuning — does designated-content decodability survive reward-modeling pressure, or only with intermittent re-supervision?
Are the private codes detectable on other released NLAs (LatentQA-style QA decoders, Activation Oracles), or specific to the Fraser-Taliente reconstruction recipe?
Since designated content can be decodable yet behaviorally inert (tap patching ~0.003), what additional causal test certifies that monitored content is also used by the model?
