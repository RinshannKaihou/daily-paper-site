Methodology & Model

frozen · pre-registered · open-weight

▸Three-Tier Evidence Design

The study deliberately separates interpretability into three independently tested tiers, each with pre-registered ("frozen") success criteria and matched controls — exploration is never conflated with confirmation.

  • 1Readability. Controlled concept recovery (50 prompts × 10 families × 5 phrasings) plus target-free open decoding against the full 262k vocabulary.
  • 2Relational representation. Nearest-centroid geometry, a lexical-adversarial triplet cohort, a 72-stem option-free graph, and a 60-law matched-reversal benchmark that subtracts matched increase/decrease states.
  • 3Causal use. Broad steering screen, prospective frozen grain-size confirmation, exploratory activation patching, and a 6-mechanism factorial cross-family patch.

▸The Identifiability Audit

The methodological core: an algebraic proof that within-family "same/different physical outcome" collapses to "same/different numerical direction" when physical polarity follows y = sx.

Therefore option-free similarity graphs cannot identify absolute physical polarity — confirmed empirically with graph-isomorphism networks, label-blind matching, and exhaustive balanced-partition enumeration.

Graph-net physical-polarity AUC: 0.259 (worse than chance) — a benchmark-design lesson the field has missed.

Strengths & Limitations

side-by-side

Strengths

  • Intellectual honesty. Failed endpoints and null results (36/50 null prompts, cross-numeric AUC 0.471) are foregrounded, not buried.
  • Identifiability audit is a methodological gem — algebraically proves why flat similarity graphs cannot recover physical polarity.
  • Matched-reversal design elegantly isolates constitutive orientation from absolute position by subtracting increase/decrease states.
  • Comprehensive matched controls at every stage: random, unrelated-mechanism, direct, lexical, equal-distance.
  • Three-tier framework (readability / representation / causation) is reusable across the interpretability field.

Limitations

  • Single model, single author. No cross-scale (Llama/Qwen/Mistral) or cross-family validation.
  • Causal effect is vocabulary- and regime-dependent — fails for increase/decrease words and the inverse Hall-Petch crossover.
  • Relational result relies on an explicit two-stage scaffold and a development-selected layer/direction; no spontaneous use shown.
  • Exact inverse-law accuracy only 53.8% — stable internal ordering does not yield reliable answers.
  • Strongest graph/patch results reuse inspected cohorts; post-hoc character persists despite frozen sub-protocols.
  • Engineering utility unproven — primarily a science-of-interpretability contribution.

Open Questions

for further exploration
  1. Does the matched-reversal abstraction (ρ = 0.910) hold across other open-weight families (Llama, Qwen, Mistral) and scales, or is it Gemma-specific?
  2. Can the causal grain-size direction be discovered without the explicit two-stage equation scaffold — does orientation survive an option-free, free-form prompt?
  3. Would representation-aware fine-tuning (the proposed R formula) actually improve held-out scientific behavior, or does readout hacking dominate under direct optimization?
  4. Why does full-state patching transfer the numerical-direction feature more strongly than the physical-outcome feature — a general property of late-layer decision circuits?
  5. Can the y = sx identifiability alias be broken with graded (non-binary) responses, yielding a transferable cross-mechanism materials graph?
