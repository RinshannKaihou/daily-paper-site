> **一句话总结：** 本文发现 RLVR 训练中的"谱继承"(spectral inheritance) 现象——RL 复用基础模型的奇异值谱、只改变输入/输出奇异向量框架——并据此提出等谱优化栈 ISO：离线的 ISO-Merger 无需数据/rollout/蒸馏即可合并专家，在线的 ISO-Optimizer 固定谱只优化框架，在 Qwen3-8B-Base 上以 2.7 倍更少步数达到 AdamW 同等精度 (0.495) 并最终提升到 0.509。

> **TL;DR:** The paper identifies *spectral inheritance* in RLVR (base-model singular-value spectra can be functionally reused while input/output singular frames adapt) and turns it into Isospectral Optimization (ISO), an RLVR-native stack. Offline ISO-Merger composes shared-base RL experts without rollouts/distillation (Qwen2.5-7B: 63.80 vs 62.88 best data-free baseline); online ISO-Optimizer fixes the base spectrum and applies AdamW/Muon only to frame variables, reaching AdamW's matched accuracy (0.495) on Qwen3-8B-Base in 100 vs 270 training steps (2.7x fewer) and improving to 0.509.

## 核心贡献

1. **形式化并验证"谱继承"(Spectral Inheritance) 现象。** 作者将"近等谱性"(near-isospectrality) 精确定义为学习到的权重矩阵到基础矩阵固定谱族 F(W₀) 的距离 (Proposition 2.1 给出该距离恰等于奇异值漂移 ‖σ(W)−σ(W₀)‖₂)。在 DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B (DS-1.5B) 长程 RLVR 终点上，相对谱残差 ρΣ 平均仅约 3%，而 SFT 转换约 35%——两者相差一个数量级。通过维度感知校准 (κ_spec 指标)，作者诚实地承认这种接近性本身并非强烈的优化偏好 (RLVR 的 κ_spec 在 1.02–1.35，与各向同性参考 ~1 同阶)，但与 SFT (89–1364) 的对比依然鲜明。

2. **功能性干预证明谱可复用且双框架必须可适配。** 作者设计了两组关键实验：(a) 训练后恢复基础谱保留学习到的框架——大部分性能得以保留；(b) 训练全程固定谱只优化框架——仍获得强增益，而仅优化谱(冻结框架)则效果有限。进一步通过 unexplained-update ratio (Proposition 3.1) 测试四种结构限制，发现仅混入子空间 (u_mix 中位数 87%) 或保留单侧子空间 (45%/42%) 都会留下大量无法解释的更新，而保留谱且双框架适配 (u_iso) 仅剩约 1.8% 残差。该模式在视觉-语言-动作的两阶段 RL 流水线 (Qwen2.5-VL-3B) 的"目标迁移"测试中复现。

3. **提出等谱优化栈 ISO (Isospectral Optimization)。** 核心原则是"继承谱、优化框架"：用固定谱参数化 W(U,V)=UΣ₀V⊤，其中 Σ₀ 来自基础模型固定不变，U、V 是 Stiefel 流形上可优化的奇异框架。ISO 不是单一优化器，而是一个栈，用极性收缩 (polar retraction) 保证每步后权重严格落在 F(W₀) 内 (Proposition 4.1 证明一阶保谱性)。

4. **ISO-Merger (离线专家合并)。** 针对"从同一基础模型训练的领域专家"这一设定，ISO-Merger 复用共享基础谱，把每个专家的框架位移投影到共享的 Stiefel 切空间，通过 Gram 矩阵的岭稳定化 + 裁剪求解"单位自保留"目标 (式 25-28)，再极性收缩重建单个模型。**无需合并后数据、rollout、梯度更新或策略蒸馏 (OPD)**。

5. **ISO-Optimizer (在线 RLVR)。** 把任意基础优化器 (AdamW、Muon) 通过映射 Opt → ISO[Opt] 转换：链式法则得到框架梯度 G_U=G_W V Σ₀、G_V=G_W⊤ U Σ₀ (式 34，注意 Σ₀ 来自重构映射的雅可比——大奇异值模式产生更大权重空间位移)，用基础优化器更新框架，再极性收缩恢复可行性。

## Key Contributions

- **Spectral inheritance as a functional, not merely descriptive, property.** Beyond showing RLVR checkpoints stay near the fixed-spectrum family F(W₀), the authors prove via two interventions (restoring base spectrum post-training; fixing spectrum throughout training) that RLVR *functionally reuses* base spectra. A restricted spectrum-only control fails, and four-way structural tests (Prop. 3.1) show both singular frames must remain adaptable (remix/one-sided classes leave 42–87% of the update unexplained vs ~1.8% for isospectral reconstruction).
- **Dimension-aware intellectual honesty.** The κ_spec calibration explicitly addresses the "high-dimensionality makes spectrum preservation trivially likely" objection, showing RLVR is only order-one (1.02–1.35) vs SFT's 89–1364. The paper is careful to claim spectral inheritance as an *inductive bias*, not an exact update law.
- **A unified RLVR-native stack (ISO)** with two instantiations sharing one fixed-spectrum parameterization: ISO-Merger (offline, data-free expert composition) and ISO-Optimizer (online, transforms any base optimizer via polar retraction). ISO does not enlarge the feasible weight-space class—every iterate stays in F(W₀).
- **Strong empirical validation across 1.5B–8B scales** spanning math reasoning and coding, plus a sequential objective-shift stress test on a VLA pipeline (Qwen2.5-VL-3B) showing the frame-adaptability requirement recurs after re-anchoring at an RL-trained checkpoint.

## Methodology

**Analysis pipeline (Sections 2–3):**
- **Fixed-spectrum family:** F(W₀) := {Z : σ(Z)=σ(W₀)} = {UΣ₀V⊤ : U∈St(d_out,q), V∈St(d_in,q)}. Proposition 2.1 shows dist_F(W, F(W₀)) = ‖σ(W)−σ(W₀)‖₂, and identifies UΣ₀V⊤ as the closest in-family representative.
- **Metrics:** δΣ (spectral drift vs base scale), ρΣ (exact distance vs full checkpoint displacement), κ_spec (dimension-normalized spectrum-changing energy, isotropic reference = 1).
- **Functional interventions:** (1) Spectrum-only interpolation W̃(α)=U_RL[(1−α)Σ₀+αΣ_RL]V_RL⊤; (2) Fix Σ₀ throughout training vs spectrum-only control.
- **Structural sufficiency test (Prop. 3.1):** Four reconstruction classes — remix within both subspaces (Ŵ_mix), retain output subspace (Ŵ_L), retain input subspace (Ŵ_R), retain spectrum + adapt both frames (Ŵ_iso) — measured by unexplained-update ratio u_h = ‖W_i−Ŵ_h‖_F / ‖W_i−W_j‖_F.

**ISO-Merger algorithm (Section 4.2):** project–mask–merge–retract. (1) Sign-canonicalize each expert's singular pairs to base; (2) compute frame displacements ΔU_i, ΔV_i and project onto Stiefel tangent spaces at base; (3) mask trailing modes (keep ratio ρ_keep=0.9, since small-singular-value modes are unstable across experts); (4) build Gram matrix Γ_ij=⟨g_i,g_j⟩_F from linearized effect proxies; (5) solve ridge-stabilized unit-retention system (Γ+λI)c̄=b with clipping; (6) polar-retract aggregate displacement back to Stiefel.

**ISO-Optimizer algorithm (Section 4.3):** Opt → ISO[Opt]. Given weight gradient G_W, compute factor gradients G_U=G_W V Σ₀, G_V=G_W⊤ U Σ₀; apply base optimizer independently to U and V; restore feasibility via polar retraction U⁺=polar(Ū), V⁺=polar(V̄). Implemented via FP64 SVD-based polar computation, distributed across GPUs (DION-style); weight decay set to zero (negligible at RL learning rates ~10⁻⁶).

## Results & Highlights

**ISO-Optimizer (online RLVR, math reasoning):**
- **Qwen3-8B-Base on DeepMath-103K (headline result):** ISO-AdamW reaches 0.509 aggregate average@16 accuracy after 210 training steps, vs AdamW's 0.487 under the same step budget. Reaching AdamW's final plateau accuracy of 0.495 takes ISO-AdamW only 100 steps vs 270 for AdamW — a **2.7× reduction in training steps**. Both methods use lr 7.5×10⁻⁷ (ISO) vs 2×10⁻⁶ (AdamW), neither re-tuned at 8B.
- **Qwen3-4B-Base:** ISO-AdamW achieves 43.46 aggregate (AIME24/25, AMC23, Minerva, OlympiadBench, avg@16) vs 41.69 for AdamW (+1.77) and 42.23 for Muon. ISO-AdamW matches the strongest AdamW run's final accuracy with ~2.2× fewer training steps.
- **Qwen3-1.7B-Base:** ISO-AdamW reaches 28.74 vs 28.35 (best AdamW) and 27.70 (Muon).
- **ISO-Muon (Qwen3-4B, 300-step budget):** matches strongest Muon's 0.422 by step 220 (vs 300) and finishes at 0.428, demonstrating the construction transfers across base update rules.
- **Coding (DS-1.5B on ArcherCodeR, DAPO-style, avg@8):** ISO-AdamW reaches 26.20 (LCB v5 25.49, v6 26.91) vs AdamW 25.13; ISO-AdamW's peak 0.268 is not matched by extended AdamW runs (5×10⁻⁶ peaks at 0.265 then declines; 3×10⁻⁻⁶ ends at 0.256).

**ISO-Merger (offline, data-free expert composition):**
- **Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, 3 experts (coding/tool/memory):** ISO-Merger achieves 63.80 aggregate vs 62.88 (RAM, strongest baseline) — best among Task Arithmetic, TIES, TSV, RAM, OrthoMerge-G-TIES. Recovers near-specialist memory scores (HotpotQA 32K: 79.46 vs RLVR-Memory 78.60) while keeping tool-use gains.
- **DS-1.5B, 2 experts (coding/math):** ISO-Merger achieves 44.38 vs 43.52 (TIES). Improves worst@4 by 1.36 points without sacrificing best@4, indicating more consistent capability recovery across stochastic generations.

**Retraction overhead:** On a representative Qwen3-4B-Base RLVR run (8K context), ISO adds ~86s/step to optimizer-update time but this is only ~**7% of end-to-end RL step time** — rollout generation dominates. Amenable to overlap in async RL systems.

## Critical Assessment

The paper's intellectual rigor is its standout feature. Rather than declaring "spectra stay" and rushing to an algorithm, the authors subject their own observation to a dimension-aware calibration (κ_spec) that *weakens* the naive interpretation (RLVR is not preferentially suppressing spectrum-changing motion beyond what dimensionality predicts), then pivot to a stronger *functional* question answered via interventions. This honesty distinguishes the contribution from typical "we observed X, so we built X-aware optimizer" papers.

Specific strengths in rigor:
- **Propositions 2.1, 3.1, 4.1** give the framework precise geometric grounding; the unexplained-update ratio u_h is a clean, falsifiable metric with a principled upper bound (u=1).
- The **sequential objective-shift stress test** (Qwen2.5-VL-3B, Stage I spatial reasoning → Stage II embodied manipulation, W₁→W₂) directly addresses the "is this just a first-stage artifact?" objection — the frame-adaptability requirement recurs after re-anchoring at an RL-trained checkpoint with a new objective.
- The **ISO-Merger unit-retention target** (Γc=b with ridge stabilization and clipping) is internally consistent: it *targets* rather than *guarantees* unit self-retention, and the paper says so explicitly.
- ISO-Optimizer is careful to note it "does not enlarge the feasible weight-space model class" — gains cannot be trivially attributed to a larger model, a common confound in reparameterization papers.

Concerns a careful reader would flag:
- **Limited scale ceiling.** The largest model is 8B; modern frontier RLVR runs at 70B+. The authors do not extrapolate, and the 2.7× step-reduction is a single 8B data point.
- **Single-seed / single-lr for ISO.** ISO-AdamW uses one learning rate (7.5×10⁻⁷) while baselines are swept over 5 values. The authors justify this (ISO inherits the strongest small-model lr), but a reader cannot rule out that ISO also benefits from more tuning. Figure 6 shows ISO's lead emerges early, which partially mitigates cherry-picking concerns.
- **Coding run is short (220 steps) and truncated** due to overfitting concerns on the "relatively small coding set" — the ISO advantage on coding is thus less robustly established than the math results.
- **No evaluation of whether merged specialists actually preserve safety/alignment** or degrade on out-of-distribution tasks; only in-domain aggregate metrics are reported.
- **The "2.7× fewer steps" framing** conflates step-count with wall-clock; the paper itself notes ISO produces longer responses (16K cap needed), which increases rollout latency — so end-to-end wall-clock speedup may be smaller than the step-count ratio.

## Strengths

- Genuinely novel *mechanistic* insight (spectral inheritance) backed by both endpoint evidence (3000+ update DS-1.5B run, ρΣ≈3%) and trajectory evidence (Qwen3-8B every-10-step checkpoints, δΣ<10⁻⁵ throughout).
- Honest dimension-aware calibration that tempers the authors' own headline observation.
- Unified stack spanning offline merging and online optimization under one principle — rare conceptual coherence.
- ISO-Merger's data-free, no-rollout, no-distillation property is practically attractive for consolidating specialist checkpoints cheaply.
- ISO-Optimizer is optimizer-agnostic (works with both AdamW and Muon), increasing generality.

## Limitations

- **Scale:** experiments top out at 8B; no 70B+ frontier-scale validation.
- **Overhead and memory:** factor tensors U, V roughly double trainable-tensor/optimizer-state storage vs a single dense matrix; requires FP64 SVD-based polar computation for numerical stability and DION-style distributed retraction. The ~7% step-time overhead is measured only at 4B/8K.
- **ISO-Merger scope:** restricted to specialists from a *shared base* with distinct domains; explicitly does not cover merging already-generalist policies trained on overlapping mixtures.
- **Keep ratio (ρ_keep=0.9) and ridge λ are hyperparameters** that may not transfer; trailing-mode masking is empirically motivated but not theoretically grounded.
- **Coding advantage is on a short, truncated run** (220 steps); math results are more robust.
- **Wall-clock vs step-count gap:** ISO's longer responses inflate rollout cost, so the realized training-time speedup is not necessarily 2.7×.
- **Alternative spectrum-preserving weight-space optimizers** (e.g., direct Lie-algebra updates like Pion) are explicitly left to future comparison.

## Relevance (中文)

本文对当前 RLVR/推理模型社区有直接的方法论和实践价值：
- **理论层面**：揭示了 RLVR 与 SFT/预训练在权重空间动力学上的本质差异（谱复用 vs 谱重写），为设计 RL 原生优化器提供了原理性依据，而非工程经验。
- **实践层面**：ISO-Optimizer 的"少步数达同等精度"特性对昂贵的 RLVR 训练（尤其长 horizon、agentic 场景）有显著成本价值；ISO-Merger 的"免 rollout、免蒸馏"合并能力降低了多专家整合的工程门槛。
- **生态关联**：与 Muon、DION、Hyperball、POET/Pion 等矩阵几何优化工作同属一个研究方向，但本文是少数从 RLVR 自身动力学出发、经功能干预验证后才设计的方案，区分度高。
- **对国产/开源大模型训练**（Qwen3、DeepSeek-R1 系列）的优化器选择和后训练流水线设计有直接参考意义。读者应注意到规模未达 70B+，迁移性需进一步验证。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Does spectral inheritance hold at 70B+ scale and across longer training horizons (10k+ updates)?** The 8B/210-step result is a single point; the prior work [1] established broader evidence, but the *functional* interventions (fix-spectrum training) have not been tested at frontier scale.
2. **What is the wall-clock (not step-count) speedup once ISO's longer responses and retraction overhead are fully accounted for, especially in async RL systems with rollout overlap?**
3. **Can the keep-ratio ρ_keep=0.9 and ridge λ in ISO-Merger be set adaptively per-layer or per-expert rather than globally?** The trailing-mode masking is empirically motivated; a principled criterion would strengthen the method.
4. **How does ISO interact with reward hacking or capability regression on out-of-distribution / safety benchmarks?** Only in-domain aggregate metrics are reported.
5. **Would combining ISO-Merger with a *small* amount of on-policy distillation recover the gap to specialist upper-bounds, or does the fixed-spectrum constraint impose a hard ceiling on compositional capacity?**
6. **The κ_spec calibration shows RLVR is not *preferentially* spectrum-preserving beyond dimensionality — so what *does* drive near-isospectrality?** Is it the KL-proximal/conservative policy structure, the sparse off-principal updates, or the optimizer dynamics? Disentangling these would clarify whether spectral inheritance is fundamental or incidental.
