PyroDash: Cost-Efficient Token-Level Small–Large Language Model Collaborative Inference

Niqi Lyu · Pengtao Shi · Wei Qiu · Jianlin Zhong · Sicong Xia · Jianyao Ma · Yicheng Ding — Pyromind Dynamics Inc. (pyromind.ai)

SLM–LLM collaboration token-level routing cost-aware GRPO single-handoff math reasoning Qwen3.5-4B → GLM-5.2-FP8
Key Finding

A 4B model learns a one-token handoff to a frozen LLM — matching or beating LLM-only accuracy at up to 96.4% lower cost.

Key Finding

A small model internalizes a token-level handoff policy: it emits τoff mid-decoding to trigger a single, one-way handoff to a frozen LLM.

Key Metric
−96.4%
total inference cost $49.36 → $1.78
at λ=0.6 (LLM token ratio 1.90%)
Why This Matters

Bakes real per-token billing — prefill priced separately from decode — directly into the RL reward. Cost becomes a tunable knob, not an afterthought.

Critical Edge

The headline accuracy win occurs while routing ~95% of tokens to the LLM — so it's better prompting, not cost-efficient collaboration; the cheap regime sits below LLM-only accuracy.

01

Methodology

S1

Control-token embedding

Expand vocab with τoff; init from mean of anchor tokens (period, newline, EOS) + Gaussian noise (σ=0.1). Fine-tune embeddings + output head + transient LoRA, then discard LoRA — keep only the learned τoff rows.

S2

Offloading-oriented SFT

One round of SFT on Corpora A (standalone, no τoff) + B (prompt-conditional offloading) so the SLM jointly learns "finish alone" and "hand off when hard." Embeddings frozen, LoRA on attention.

S3

Cost-aware GRPO

G=8 rollouts per prompt, each replaying the exact inference-time handoff. Group-relative advantage (no critic), PPO-clipped loss. Only SLM tokens enter the gradient; the frozen LLM affects the update only through accuracy & cost rewards.

Objective & Architecture
maxπs 𝔼 [ Acc(O(q;πs), y) − λ · Costnorm(q;πs) ]
  • Single-handoff decoding. Collaborate Engine streams SLM tokens; on detecting τoff it packs the trace, calls the frozen LLM once, returns Os ∥ Ol. Control never returns to the SLM.
  • LLM stays frozen, invoked via standard text-completion API — no logit access, no retraining, compatible with closed APIs.
  • Dataset: EasyHard-24k (24,061 ex), split easy/hard by whether the base SLM solves them. Released on HuggingFace (pyromind/easyhard-24k).
  • Stack: Qwen3.5-4B / GLM-5.2-FP8, TRL + GRPO, 4×H100 train, vLLM serve (SLM 4×H100, LLM 8×B200).

03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Deployable architecture. Single-handoff, LLM frozen, no separate router, no logit access — genuinely compatible with stateless proprietary APIs.
  • Right reward design. Pricing prefill and decode separately, normalized against the LLM-only baseline, directly fixes the known blindness of token-share metrics.
  • Honest cost decomposition (Table 4) tying every dollar to measured in/out tokens — rare in this literature.
  • Strong wins on hard benchmarks (AIME-2024 +17.19pp, Olympiad +3.85pp over LLM-only) with a full λ-sweep, not a single operating point.

Limitations

  • Offloading rationality never validated. No check that τoff fires precisely when SLM reasoning fails — the central mechanistic question is unanswered.
  • "+6.36pp" uses ~95% LLM tokens — closer to prompt-engineering the LLM via an SLM prefix than cost-efficient collaboration; the cheap regime is below LLM-only.
  • No ablation of the normalization itself vs a direct token-cost penalty.
  • Narrow scope: one SLM, one LLM, math-only; single-handoff and multi-turn left to future work.
  • Costs are estimates from listed prices, not bills; LLM-token-ratio omits prefill and can mislead about true LLM reliance.
04

Questions for Further Exploration

1

Offloading rationality. Do τoff positions correlate with verifiable SLM errors (first wrong arithmetic step), or surface cues? Can we measure handoff precision/recall vs ground-truth failure points?

2

Why the flat plateau? Is the policy effectively binary (~5–6% handoff) regardless of λ? Would a curriculum or entropy bonus recover intermediate operating points?

3

Generalize or CoT-prefix effect? Ablate by feeding the LLM a random/SFT-only SLM prefix of equal length — is the λ=0.05 win from the learned policy or just any structured prefix?

4

Prefill-aware reward. Since the SLM trace becomes LLM prefill, does explicitly penalizing long prefill shift handoffs earlier and cheaper?

5

Beyond math. How does PyroDash behave on code, agentic tool-use, and multi-turn dialogue, where capability boundaries are模糊 and verifiers weaker?

6

Multi-handoff. Would allowing >1 handoff per turn (or returning control to the SLM) close the λ=0.6 accuracy gap — at what cost?
