Why this matters

A training-free, prompt-independent pruning rule converts mechanistic insight into faster inference — and reframes how conditioning flows inside modern T2I DiTs.

Critical edge

The 20% cut is attention-FLOPs only (overall DiT speedup is smaller); perceptual quality (HPSv3) drops faster than object correctness, and why tokens become registers stays unexplained.

Methodology 01 / how they looked

Joint-attention decomposition

Splits the [c; z] self-attention of an MMDiT into T2T / T2I / I2T / I2I blocks, isolating the cross-modal I2T path (image queries reading text keys). An attention-mass score m_K(Q) measures how much each text span absorbs image attention.

Conditioning split: S vs R

The condition sequence is divided into a semantic span S (real prompt tokens) and a structural span R (chat-template tail tokens such as <|im_end|>). The surprise: R is nearly empty at the encoder output yet dominates downstream.

Cross-prompt swap

Builds [S_A; R_B] (swap template tokens) and substitutes an averaged R̄ from 100 prompts into 40 held-out prompts, measuring semantic drift via DINOv3 [CLS] cosine — quantifying whether R carries semantics.

Cross-trajectory head transplant

Runs two trajectories (A=“an apple”, B=“a banana”), ranks heads by m_S, and progressively grafts B’s (q,k,v) into A to find the identity-flip point — decoupling “reads prompt” from “holds object”.

Layer-wise causal masking

At register query R, masks its attention to S or to image latents I (pre-softmax, accumulated over first k blocks) to decide where R’s identity content actually originates.

Training-free pruning

Picks top-K heads by descending m̄_S (the causally-inert prompt-readers) and removes them only in the last 80% of denoising steps (early steps still commit identity). Full q/k/v/output removal — FLOPs scale linearly.

Assessment 03 / strengths & limits

Strengths

  • Counter-intuitive, deep finding. Overturns the assumption that semantics stay on the token that encodes them — DiT internals are more elaborate than expected.
  • Transferable framework. The four-tool kit (decompose + swap + transplant + mask) ports to any joint-attention DiT; validated on 5 model families.
  • Analysis-to-application loop. Mechanistic insight becomes a usable, training-free, prompt-independent pruning rule.
  • Comprehensive coverage. Prompt length (2→82 tokens), language (zh/en), architecture (dual/single-stream), task (T2I/edit), schedule (50-step/2-step).

Limitations

  • “Why registers” stays open. Why content-free template tokens evolve into semantic registers during training is left unexplained (Appendix A, future work).
  • Pruning is attention-only. 20% is attention FLOPs; total DiT speedup is smaller, and object correctness holds while HPSv3 perceptual quality drops faster.
  • Head-independence assumption. Transplant/mask assume additive head functions; a few mixed-role heads may violate this.
  • Qwen-skewed quantification. FLUX.2/Krea-2 mainly show sink existence; pruning numbers come only from Qwen-Image-2512.
  • Distilled single-step simplification. Core causal experiments run on a 2-step LoRA single step — reasonable, but not identical to multi-step production.

Open questions 04 / what's next

Training dynamics of register formation. At what stage, and under what gradient signal, do structural tokens become semantic registers — and can we induce or suppress this via init or regularization?
Upper bound of sink-aware sparse attention. If we keep register positions as keys and prune the rest, how much better is the FLOPs/quality Pareto frontier than head-level pruning?
Universality across modalities. Does the phenomenon appear in video, audio, or text-to-3D DiTs? Do registers always originate from chat templates?
Intervening on the image stream. Since semantics enter via I, can direct manipulation of image latents enable more precise concept editing or adversarial concept erasure than editing the text?
Register capacity vs object count. With many objects, how is identity partitioned across limited template tokens — is there register competition or overflow?
Interaction with CFG. Why does higher CFG suppress the semantic drift from averaged R̄ (Fig. 4b)? Does CFG amplify the faint signal residing in registers?
Mixed-role bridge circuits. The few heads carrying both register and rendering roles — do they form special bridges, and is removing them non-monotonic?
