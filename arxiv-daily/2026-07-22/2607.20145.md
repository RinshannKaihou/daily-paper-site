> **一句话总结：** 在 Ascend CloudMatrix384 SuperPOD 上对 DeepSeek-V4（1.6T MoE）做全参数后训练，通过分层系统优化（并行、通信重叠、AuraKernel 算子代理）将 MFU 从 11.67% 提升到 34.22%（2.93×），并在 DeepSeek-V4-Flash 上建立求解器验证的 OR CPT-SFT 流水线，专用模型平均零样本 Pass@1 达 71.81%，比 GPT-5.4-Mini 和基座 Flash 分别高 3.98 / 11.27 个百分点。

> **TL;DR:** SLAI T-Rex reports one of the first full-stack full-parameter post-training studies of a trillion-parameter MoE model (DeepSeek-V4-Pro, 1.6T) on Ascend 910C SuperPOD, reaching 34.22% MFU (2.93× over the open-source baseline recipe), and pairs this with a solver-grounded CPT+SFT workflow for Operations Research on DeepSeek-V4-Flash that attains 71.81% average zero-shot Pass@1 across NL4OPT/OptiBench/B4O-Feasible/B4O-ORGEval, outperforming GPT-5.4-Mini by 3.98 pp and base DeepSeek-V4-Flash by 11.27 pp.

## 核心贡献

- **Ascend 原生系统优化**：在 Ascend CloudMatrix384 SuperPOD（910C NPU）上提出针对万亿参数级 MoE 模型训练的全栈优化框架，将 DeepSeek-V4-Pro（1.6T）的 MFU 从 11.67% 提升到 34.22%，相对开源基线配方提升 2.93×，并系统刻画了并行、通信编排、显存搬运与 kernel 执行的联合优化原则。
- **硬件感知的瓶颈剖析**：基于 DeepSeek-V4 在 Ascend 上的端到端 profiling，给出 MoE、稀疏注意力与显存层次如何与 Ascend kernel/通信/分布式编排交互的完整分析（通信占 52.2%、稀疏注意力 backward 占 16.86% 等）。
- **求解器引导的 AuraKernel**：首次将运筹优化（OR）方法用于 AscendC kernel 的 tiling 选择，构建可分支、可回滚的长周期 kernel 优化代理，超越人工启发式调优。
- **算子链融合与重写**：把 eager 链与通用 Triton kernel 改写为 UB-resident 的 AscendC kernel，针对 mHC、SwiGLU、RoPE 等，模块级加速 1.88×–8.86×。
- **可复现的 OR CPT-SFT 流水线**：在 DeepSeek-V4-Flash 上构建求解器验证的 CPT 数据、自蒸馏 SFT 数据飞轮、契约感知清洗与 CoT 增强、渐进式 SFT 与通用能力锚定。
- **CPT–SFT 协同的实证**：在匹配 SFT 条件下证明 SFT 提供任务对齐，而 CPT 注入 OR 建模先验，额外带来 B4O-ORGEval +10.66pp、B4O-Feasible +5.29pp 的最大增益。

## Key Contributions

- **Ascend-native system optimization for trillion-parameter MoE training**: A full-stack optimization framework on Ascend CloudMatrix384 SuperPOD that raises DeepSeek-V4-Pro (1.6T) MFU from 11.67% to 34.22% (2.93× over the default open-source recipe), jointly optimizing parallelism, communication, memory movement, and kernel execution under Ascend architectural constraints.
- **Hardware-aware bottleneck profiling**: An end-to-end analysis of how DeepSeek-V4's MoE + hybrid sparse attention interacts with Ascend kernel execution, communication patterns, memory hierarchy, and distributed orchestration (e.g., communication = 52.2% of device kernel time; sparse-attn backward = 16.86% of compute).
- **AuraKernel — an OR-guided AscendC kernel optimization agent**: Among the first applications of Operations Research (constrained performance-modeling / tiling) to Ascend kernel tuning, with a branchable/rollbackable K-Search tree and skill distillation, replacing brittle expert heuristics.
- **AscendC operator-chain fusion and rewrite**: Re-expression of framework-emitted eager chains and generic Triton kernels as UB-resident AscendC kernels (mHC, limited SwiGLU, RoPE), achieving 1.88×–8.86× module-level speedups by removing launches, intermediate tensors, and global-memory traffic.
- **Reproducible OR-oriented CPT-SFT workflow**: On DeepSeek-V4-Flash, a solver-grounded CPT data engine, a self-distilled SFT data flywheel, contract-aware cleaning + CoT enhancement, and progressive SFT with general-capability anchoring.
- **Empirical evidence of CPT–SFT synergy**: Matched-condition experiments showing SFT supplies task alignment while CPT contributes transferable OR modeling priors, with the largest extra gain on structural-equivalence (B4O-ORGEval).

## Methodology

The work has two tightly coupled halves.

**1. Ascend SuperPOD training infrastructure (target: DeepSeek-V4-Pro, 1.6T).**
After profiling a single training step (206,503 compute kernels, 39.84s device-side compute; communication = 52.2% of device kernel time, dominated by TP+DP AllReduce at 48.4% and pipeline P2P at 36.7%, with MoE AllToAll only 14.5%), the authors decompose step time as T_step = T_comp + T_comm_non-overlapped + T_bubble + T_idle and apply a three-layer optimization:
- **Parallelism**: Expert–Tensor Parallelism (ETP) à la Megatron Parallel Folding — attention uses TP=2 while MoE experts keep full width via ETP folded into EP, reducing TP collectives; Virtual Pipeline Parallelism (VPP) is chosen over DualPipeV because DualPipeV's bidirectional residency would overshoot the 64GB per-NPU HBM budget (VPP already sits at 51.7–54.6GB) while targeting MoE AllToAll, which is only a minor cost here.
- **Communication overlap**: asynchronous AllToAll dispatch/combine with deferred synchronization, and advancing the shared-expert forward to fill the `before_ep_alltoall` barrier stall.
- **Memory**: a double-buffered swap optimizer that pipelines host↔device state transfers with fused AdamW updates to reclaim HBM with limited overhead.
- **Kernels**: (a) AuraKernel — an agent that formulates AscendC operator tuning as a constrained performance-modeling problem (dominant-regime diagnosis → family-specific tiling/DMA/pipeline parameter space → solver-guided initialization → modify–verify–profile K-Search loop → skill distillation); (b) AscendC fusion/rewrite of eager and Triton chains.

**2. OR-oriented post-training (platform: DeepSeek-V4-Flash).**
- Capability-boundary analysis of the original checkpoint (Pass@1/Pass@16/5-shot + error-family taxonomy) motivates a two-stage design.
- **OR-CPT**: a solver-verified synthetic engine renders optimization documents (math seeds + domain contracts, prompt-controlled bidirectional synthesis, controlled scenarios); a domain-dominant mixture (~75% OR + retained general corpora) avoids catastrophic forgetting; checkpoint selection validates CPT→SFT transfer.
- **SFT**: a solver-oriented SFT objective (target: feasible + structurally equivalent Gurobi programs), a self-distilled OR modeling data flywheel across three input modes and four task categories, contract-aware cleaning + concise CoT enhancement, and progressive SFT anchored to general capabilities.
- AI-assisted modules (Cleaner, Reviewer, Diagnostic Resolver) refine data; a full CPT–SFT–Deployment–Evaluation provenance chain tracks confidence levels.

## Results & Highlights

- **System MFU**: DeepSeek-V4-Pro on Ascend SuperPOD reaches **34.22% MFU**, a **2.93× improvement over the open-source baseline recipe** (11.67% → 34.22%), with stable long-term training (800/800 steps, 0 skipped, 0 NaN, final LM loss ≈ 0.079, MTP loss ≈ 0.093).
- **AuraKernel operator speedups** (end-to-end training shapes): SparseAttnSharedkvGrad **1.24×** (cmp. ratio 128) and **1.21×** (cmp. ratio 4); weight-free RMSNorm forward **11.90×** and backward **3.42×**; a single campaign took all 14 Triton-Ascend operators to 100% completion at **2.06× average / 11.90× best**.
- **Operator-chain fusion**: limited SwiGLU forward **8.86×** (1.480s→0.167s) and backward 3.27×; mHC pre-projection **3.03×**, post-projection 2.74×, pre-projection backward 1.88×; RoPE forward 2.33× / backward 3.54×; Triton→AscendC RMS-norm rewrite **7.48×/7.55×** (fwd/bwd).
- **SFT scaling/cleaning**: B4O-Feasible improves 60.47→65.07 with SFT-10K and B4O-ORGEval 34.26→47.21; Clean-CoT adds **+5.27pp on NL4OPT** over uncleaned SFT-10K, while scaling to 50K regresses OptiBench to 58.68 — evidence that quality, not scale, matters.
- **CPT→SFT transfer (matched SFT conditions)**: direct SFT raises B4O-Feasible 60.47→65.93 and B4O-ORGEval 34.26→48.73; CPT-initialized SFT further raises them to **71.22** and **59.39** (extra +5.29pp and +10.66pp over SFT alone).
- **End-to-end OR benchmark (Table 15)**: SLAI T-Rex attains the highest overall score **71.81%** vs GPT-5.4-Mini 67.83, Gemini-3-Flash 66.82, Kimi-K2.6 64.59, and base DeepSeek-V4-Flash 60.54 — outperforming GPT-5.4-Mini by **+3.98pp** and base Flash by **+11.27pp**, while using a smaller disclosed activated-parameter budget.
- **General-capability preservation**: CPT+SFT is within noise of base Flash on MMLU/MMLU-Pro/CMMLU/HumanEval/GSM8K/MATH (e.g., +0.9 MMLU, −1.7 MATH), and a "Pure OR" CPT ablation drops general benchmarks 10–15pp without OR gain — confirming the balanced mixture avoids catastrophic forgetting.

## Authors & Credibility

This is a technical report from the AI Training Platform Team of the Shenzhen Loop Area Institute (SLAI), with a large multi-author engineering team (28+ listed authors across systems, kernel, and ML roles). It is explicitly positioned as a "first-stage practice" / "Part I" technical report rather than a peer-reviewed academic paper, with a stated artifact-release plan (prompt templates, format contracts, curriculum/monitoring configs, and the optimized AscendC kernels). The systems claims are grounded in real end-to-end profiling traces of a production-scale 1.6T-parameter model on a real 384-NPU SuperPOD, not microbenchmarks. Model comparisons include current strong frontier baselines (GPT-5.4-Mini, Gemini-3-Flash, Kimi-K2.6, GLM-5.2, MiMo-V2.5-Pro).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The genuine novelty is the *joint* contribution on non-GPU (SIMD Ascend) infrastructure, which is genuinely under-explored. Individually, ETP/Parallel-Folding, VPP, swap optimizers, and kernel fusion are established techniques; the OR-guided AuraKernel tiling formulation and the dual optimization-and-specialization framing are the more original pieces. The OR-specialization result is an engineering combination of CPT+SFT rather than a new algorithm.
- **Rigor**: Strong on systems profiling — step decomposition, per-kernel Task-Duration shares, AIV/CUBE pipeline ratios, and careful attribution (e.g., distinguishing AuraKernel single-operator speedups at training shapes from Triton→AscendC substrate-rewrite ratios at different shapes, and from module-level fusion ratios). The matched-condition CPT→SFT experiment properly fixes data, LR, GBS, and sequence length so deltas are attributable to CPT. However, several results rely on a single training run and internal benchmarks, and error bars / variance are largely absent.
- **Reproducibility**: The authors promise to release prompt templates, contracts, curriculum configs, monitoring callbacks, and the AscendC kernels, but the report itself does not yet include code/weights links as active artifacts. Full reproduction requires access to an Ascend CloudMatrix384 SuperPOD and DeepSeek-V4 weights, which materially limits external reproducibility; the OR-SFT portion is more reproducible than the Pro-scale systems portion.
- **Significance**: Practically significant as a rare public blueprint for trillion-parameter MoE post-training on Ascend and for domain (OR) specialization; scientifically moderate, since the individual techniques are combinations of known ideas with new hardware context.
- **Red flags**: (i) Headline OR model (89.52/67.12/71.22/59.39) lags the very best competitor only on B4O-Feasible (71.22 vs Kimi-K2.6 72.67 / GPT-5.4-Mini 73.84), and 5-shot B4O-Feasible actually drops below base (-3.42 to -4.65pp), an honest but notable weakness; (ii) "DeepSeek-V4", "GPT-5.4-Mini", "Gemini-3-Flash", "GLM-5.2" are 2026 references whose real-world identity/availability cannot be independently verified; (iii) Pro-scale post-training results are not yet released (only Flash post-training is reported), so the systems half and the domain half are validated on different models; (iv) some forward-looking claims (e.g., future AgenticRL, OR-based parallelism search) are aspirational rather than demonstrated.

## Strengths

- End-to-end systems + domain-specialization story, measured on a real 1.6T-parameter workload rather than microbenchmarks.
- Exceptionally detailed kernel-level profiling and bottleneck attribution, with a clean mapping from diagnosis to optimization.
- AuraKernel's OR-guided tiling formulation is a meaningful, generalizable idea beyond hand-tuned heuristics.
- Honest negative results: 50K SFT scaling regresses; SFT alone hurts 5-shot executability; Pure-OR CPT causes 10–15pp general-capability regression; nonlinear/Gurobi-form errors are not fixed by cleaning.
- Balanced CPT mixture analysis directly demonstrates the trade-off between domain gain and catastrophic forgetting.

## Limitations

- The OR-specialization results are only on DeepSeek-V4-Flash; Pro-scale post-training (the headline 34.22% MFU target) is not paired with downstream OR evaluation.
- No error bars / multiple seeds reported for the main benchmark numbers.
- Strong dependency on proprietary Ascend hardware and (2026-era) model weights that limit independent verification.
- Kernel speedups are reported as relative ratios and pipeline metrics rather than absolute wall-clock end-to-end training times.
- Nonlinear-term / quadratic / Gurobi-specific modeling errors remain unresolved and are deferred to future synthetic-data work.
- Pipeline parallelism choice (VPP vs DualPipeV) is explicitly left as future work, leaving potential MFU gains on the table.

## Relevance

This paper is highly relevant to interests in LLM training infrastructure and distributed systems: it is a rare, detailed engineering study of full-parameter post-training for a trillion-parameter MoE model on non-GPU (Ascend NPU) hardware, with concrete kernel/parallelism/communication optimization recipes. It is also relevant to anyone studying domain specialization (Operations Research) via CPT+SFT and the interaction between data quality and data scale.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How much of the 2.93× MFU gain is attributable to each optimization layer (parallelism vs swap optimizer vs AuraKernel vs fusion) — is there an ablation isolating their marginal contributions?
- Would adopting DualPipeV or a memory-adaptive variant now that the swap optimizer reclaims HBM change the VPP-vs-DualPipeV trade-off?
- Can the OR-guided AuraKernel tiling formulation generalize to other AscendC operators and to non-Ascend (e.g., CUDA/Triton) kernel tuning?
- Why does CPT+SFT regress 5-shot B4O-Feasible below base — is it a format/prompt mismatch, and can targeted in-context-learning-aware SFT fix it?
- Does the CPT→SFT transfer benefit generalize beyond OR to other verifiable-reasoning domains (code, theorem proving), or is it specific to structural-equivalence-heavy tasks?
- What are absolute end-to-end training times and tokens/sec per NPU, to allow cross-platform comparison with GPU-based trillion-parameter MoE training reports?
