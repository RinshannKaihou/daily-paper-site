> **一句话总结：** Upstage 提出 Solar Open 2，一个 250B-A15B 的混合专家(MoE)模型，通过 1:3 交错 softmax/线性注意力的混合架构(配合 NoPE 与负特征值扩展)将上下文扩展到 1M token，并以跨代选择性权重迁移(仅迁移 2.3% 参数)和十二专家多教师同策略蒸馏(MOPD)高效完成训练，在英文基准上与同量级开源模型持平，在韩文基准(含 Ko-GDPval 办公智能体)上以 250B 参数逼近 1.6T 的 DeepSeek-V4-Pro。

> **TL;DR:** Solar Open 2 is a 250B-A15B MoE LLM from Upstage that combines a hybrid attention stack (1 softmax + 3 linear-attention layers per block, NoPE, negative-eigenvalue KDA) to reach a 1M-token context, is warm-started from Solar Open 1 via selective transfer of only 5.69B (2.3%) parameters, and uses Multi-teacher On-Policy Distillation (MOPD) to fuse 12 domain specialists; it matches DeepSeek-V4-Flash/MiMo-V2.5 on English benchmarks and posts the highest Korean-suite average (85.4 vs 84.9 for DeepSeek-V4-Flash), while on Ko-GDPval it scores 86.8 — essentially tying 1.6T-parameter DeepSeek-V4-Pro (86.9) at less than one-sixth the size.

## 核心贡献

- **混合注意力架构实现 1M 上下文：** 在 48 层中以 "1 个 softmax + 3 个线性注意力层" 的模式重复 12 次(25% softmax / 75% linear)，配合 NoPE(无位置编码)、sigmoid 输出门控、以及将 KDA 写入强度扩展到 β∈(0,2) 的负特征值机制，使线性递归状态可"翻转/擦除"，在保持精确全局召回的同时将 KV 缓存与序列长度解耦，以约 1/4 的全 softmax 内存/算力服务百万级 token。
- **跨代选择性权重迁移：** 将 Solar Open 1 中与 Solar Open 2 形状兼容的 5.69B 参数(token 嵌入、softmax q/k/v/o、共享专家、从 GQA q/o 重映射的线性 q/o 等)直接迁移，其余 244.3B(主要是 320 个路由专家)随机初始化；200B-A15B 代理实验显示到达交叉熵 1.8 所需 token 从 21.5B 降至 12.6B(约 1.7×)。
- **价值导向的数据治理：** 将 20T 清洗池经质量/稀缺性评分、精确+语义去重、混合比优化精炼为 10T 训练混合(real:synthetic=4:6，math/code 各≥15%，英文>80%)；10B MoE 等预算实验中该配方在 en_code、ko_math、en_math 上分别提升 +0.216/+0.204/+0.146。
- **全异步 RL + 多教师同策略蒸馏(MOPD)：** 提出基于 fresh-token-fraction 闸门、长度/陈旧度感知批采样、直接双侧重要性采样等的全异步 RL 系统；MOPD 以每个提示仅 1 次 rollout、全词表精确 KL 将 12 个领域专家折叠进单一模型，吞吐量瓶颈相对组相对优势方法缩小 G 倍。
- **场景驱动的智能体后训练：** 自建对话式工具(有状态 MCP)、编码(SWE/terminal/artifact)、办公(OfficeVerse：11 行业 × 12 任务类型，含首个韩文交付物办公任务语料)三类智能体场景，全部经执行/证据级验证并去污染。

## Key Contributions

- **Hybrid attention for 1M-token context:** A 48-layer stack repeating [1 softmax + 3 linear] ×12, using NoPE, a sigmoid output gate, and a negative-eigenvalue extension of KDA (write strength β=2σ(·)∈(0,2) lifting eigenvalues from [0,1] to [-1,1]) so the recurrent state can flip sign and self-correct — yielding constant KV cache in sequence length at ~25% of all-softmax memory/compute while retaining exact global recall via the softmax layers.
- **Cross-generation selective weight transfer:** Transfers only the shape-compatible 5.69B parameters (2.3% of 250B) from Solar Open 1, absorbing the 128→320 expert expansion and softmax→hybrid architectural change through full pre-training; on a 200B-A15B proxy this reaches CE loss 1.8 in 12.6B tokens vs 21.5B from scratch (~1.7× fewer).
- **Value-per-token data curation:** Refines a 20T pool to a 10T mixture (real:synthetic 4:6, math/code ≥15% each, English >80%); at equal 300B-token budget on a 10B MoE it beats the Solar Open 1 recipe on every benchmark group (en_code +0.216, ko_math +0.204, en_math +0.146).
- **Fully asynchronous RL + Multi-teacher On-Policy Distillation (MOPD):** An async RL stack with disaggregated trainer/actor, a fresh-token-fraction staleness gateway, length/staleness-aware batch sampling, direct double-sided importance sampling, and environment-failure repair; MOPD consolidates 12 specialists via exact full-vocabulary KL on the student's own trajectories at one rollout per prompt (no reward term, no λ).
- **Scenario-driven agent post-training:** Purpose-built conversational tool-use (stateful MCP), coding (SWE/terminal/artifact across 5 scaffolds, 18 languages), and officework (OfficeVerse: 11 industries × 12 task types, including the first Korean deliverable-producing officework training corpus) scenarios, all execution/evidence-verified and decontaminated.

## Methodology

Solar Open 2 keeps the Solar Open 1 backbone (48 layers, hidden 4096, 64Q/8KV heads, 196,608 vocab, single shared expert) but introduces three structural changes: a hybrid attention stack, NoPE, and a 128→320 routed-expert pool. The hybrid stack interleaves softmax (GQA + sigmoid gate) and linear (KDA with negative eigenvalues) layers in a softmax-first (S-L-L-L) ordering repeated 12 times.

Pre-training has four stages on ~12T tokens total: (1) Selective Weight Transfer initializing 5.69B compatible weights from Solar Open 1; (2) General Pre-training (10T tokens, curated mixture); (3) Intensive Pre-training (1T tokens, the best subset at higher quality threshold); (4) Length Expansion (0.9T tokens, extending context to 1M with reasoning/agent data) followed by merging four intermediate checkpoints to recover from a Stage-4 distribution-shift regression.

Post-training has four stages: SFT (chat-template + reasoning/agent tasks); Multi-domain RL (verifiable-reward RL on STEM); Specialist (twelve domain experts trained via SFT + domain-specific RL using the fully asynchronous RL system); and MOPD (consolidating the twelve teachers into the released model by minimizing per-position reverse KL to the routed teacher on the student's own rollouts, computed exactly over the full 196,608-token vocabulary via hidden-state transport and tiled logit reconstruction to avoid the [L×V] memory blow-up).

Agent training data is synthesized, not harvested: stateful MCP environments with read-back verifiers for CUD operations; SWE instances mined from PRs across 18 languages (kept only if Docker builds and is fail-to-pass, with Git history stripped); terminal tasks across 10+ domains emphasizing self-verification; front-end artifact tasks filtered by Playwright + VLM judges; and OfficeVerse officework tasks grounded in real public data with deterministic validation gates and rubrics combining rule-based and LLM-graded criteria.

## Results & Highlights

- **English suite (Table 3):** Solar Open 2 leads all compared open-weight models on MMLU-Pro (86.2 vs DeepSeek-V4-Flash 85.9, MiMo-V2.5 84.6) and LiveCodeBench v6 (92.4 vs 92.3 and 89.1), and tops APEX-Agents (16.6 vs 13.2/13.4). It places second only to DeepSeek-V4-Flash on GPQA-Diamond (86.3 vs 88.9), HLE w/o tools (28.8 vs 32.3), HMMT2602 (93.9 vs 94.7), and Multi-Challenge (61.0 vs 62.0).
- **Korean suite (Table 4):** Highest average of all six models at 85.4, ahead of DeepSeek-V4-Flash (84.9), GPT-5.4 mini (80.8), MiMo-V2.5 (77.5), and Claude Haiku 4.5 (69.6); +18.4 points over Solar Open 1 (67.0→85.4), with the agentic axis going from essentially non-functional to leading (Ko-GDPval 3.4→86.8).
- **Ko-GDPval sovereign scenario (Table 5):** Scores 86.8, essentially tying 1.6T-parameter DeepSeek-V4-Pro (86.9) at less than one-sixth the size, and beating DeepSeek-V4-Flash (85.0), MiMo-V2.5-Pro (84.6), and both closed APIs (Claude Haiku 4.5 68.3, GPT-5.4 mini 59.4).
- **Architecture efficiency ablation (Fig. 4):** The hybrid Solar Open 2 stack reaches the MMLU 0.55 level with 210B training tokens where the all-softmax Solar Open 1 stack needs 671B (3.2×), and HellaSwag 0.63 with 210B vs 377B (1.8×).
- **Selective transfer ablation (Fig. 6):** On a 200B-A15B proxy, transferring 5.69B parameters reaches training CE 1.8 in 12.6B tokens vs 21.5B from random init (~1.7× fewer tokens, only 58% as many).
- **Blind pairwise qualitative eval:** Preferred on 44.1% of 835 Korean conversation turns vs DeepSeek-V4-Flash's 26.6%, with decisive wins (factual correctness) on 21.2% vs 8.5%.
- **Tokenizer efficiency:** 4.41 bytes/token on Ko-GDPval prompts, ~24% above the best global model (MiniMax-M3, 3.54) and ahead of Korean sovereign models A.X-K1 (4.27) and K-EXAONE (4.12).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Selective weight transfer across a softmax→hybrid architectural change with full re-pre-training (rather than distillation-based linearization) is claimed as a first for large hybrid models — a genuine and well-motivated contribution, supported by the per-module transfer table. The softmax-first ordering and the negative-eigenvalue KDA (allow_neg_eigval=True, β∈(0,2)) are incremental but specific deltas over Kimi Linear / Qwen3.5. MOPD's "KL-only, one rollout per prompt, full-vocabulary exact KL" formulation is a meaningful systems/algorithmic simplification over GRPO-style consolidation.
- **Rigor:** Strong ablation discipline throughout — a 10B-A1B proxy isolates each architectural choice (linear+NoPE saves ~17.4% tokens, gate ~3%, softmax-first ~1.5%, neg-eigval ~1%), and a 200B-A15B proxy isolates the transfer benefit. All benchmark numbers are measured in a single internal harness under identical per-benchmark settings. However, three of the most favorable headline results (Ko-AIME, KBank-MMLU, Ko-GDPval) are in-house benchmarks with no external replication possible, and the Korean comparison notably excludes the strongest closed models (only "fast-tier" Haiku 4.5 and GPT-5.4 mini, not frontier-tier).
- **Reproducibility:** Medium-low. Architecture hyperparameters and the transfer principle are fully specified, but key data-curation thresholds, exact RL hyperparameters (s_max, ρ, trust-region bounds, G), the twelve specialist data compositions, and the checkpoint-merge procedure are not released in detail. No mention of public weight release in the report itself.
- **Significance:** High for the sovereign-AI / non-English-LLM community and for hybrid-attention training methodology; the Ko-GDPval result (matching a 1.6T model at 250B) is a striking capability-per-parameter data point. Broader significance depends on whether the recipe transfers to other underserved languages.
- **Red flags:** (1) In-house Korean benchmarks where the model wins are designed by the authors; (2) English agent suite shows a clear localized gap on SWE-Bench Verified (70.4 vs 73.0–73.8) and especially Terminal Bench Hard (28.3 vs MiMo's 41.7), undermining the "competitive everywhere" framing; (3) "essentially matches" DeepSeek-V4-Pro on Ko-GDPval is a 0.1-point difference (86.8 vs 86.9) presented as a tie — directionally fair but rhetorically generous; (4) "first Korean training set of deliverable-producing officework tasks" is an unsubstantiated scope claim.

## Strengths

- Architecturally principled: every design choice is ablated on a proxy before adoption, and the negative-eigenvalue motivation (state-tracking expressivity, error non-accumulation over 1M tokens) is theoretically grounded.
- Computationally efficient training recipe — selective warm start + value-curated 10T mixture + async RL + single-rollout MOPD — directly targets the fixed-compute constraint and is well-justified by controlled ablations.
- Honest framing of where the model wins (English knowledge/reasoning, Korean suite, Korean officework) and where it lags (repo/terminal-level SWE, fine-grained numerical precision in deliverables).
- The OfficeVerse officework agent pipeline (industry×task-type matrix, real-data grounding, deterministic validation gates, Korean-specific rendering-integrity checks) is a substantial, underexplored contribution beyond the usual web-navigation/search agent corpora.
- Comprehensive evaluation across ~25 benchmarks spanning knowledge, reasoning, instruction-following/long-context, and five agent families in both English and Korean.

## Limitations

- Persistent English agent gaps: SWE-Bench Verified (70.4), Terminal Bench Hard (28.3), and ArtifactsBench (55.9) trail the strongest baselines by meaningful margins — the authors themselves flag repository/terminal-level SE as the next step.
- Fine-grained numerical precision in officework deliverables is acknowledged as a remaining weakness.
- Headline Korean results rest on three in-house benchmarks (Ko-AIME, KBank-MMLU, Ko-GDPval) that are not externally reproducible or auditable.
- No frontier-tier closed models (only "fast-tier" Haiku 4.5 and GPT-5.4 mini) are included in the Korean comparison, leaving the absolute Korean frontier unclear.
- No public weight release is announced in the report, limiting external validation of the architecture and post-training claims.
- The 0.9T-token Length Expansion stage caused an overall performance drop requiring checkpoint merging to repair, suggesting the long-context + agent-data injection curriculum is not yet stable.
- MOPD deliberately trades away headroom to surpass the teachers — the consolidated model is bounded by the best specialist per domain by design.

## Relevance

对一个面向长程智能体任务、且采用混合线性注意力 + MoE + 蒸馏整合多专家路线的 250B 模型技术报告，本工作在长上下文架构(1M token, NoPE + 负特征值 KDA)、跨代权重迁移与高效异步 RL/MOPD 后训练方面提供了可直接借鉴的工程与算法细节，对研究高效长上下文 LLM、智能体训练管线及非英语主权 AI 的方向高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How sensitive are the negative-eigenvalue (β∈(0,2)) and softmax-first ordering gains to scale beyond the 10B-A1B proxy — do they hold, shrink, or amplify at 250B?
- Could the MOPD KL-only objective be augmented with a sparse verifiable-reward term to recover some headroom above the best specialist without reintroducing reward-hacking?
- What is the actual context-length utilization in Ko-GDPval and APEX-Agents rollouts — is the 1M window load-bearing, or would 256K suffice for the reported tasks?
- Does the selective weight transfer principle generalize to other architectural transitions (e.g., dense→MoE, or swapping in a different linear-attention core), and how does the transferable fraction degrade across more divergent architectures?
- How would Solar Open 2 perform against frontier-tier closed models (not just fast-tier) on the Korean suite, and against the strongest open frontier-tier models (>320B) on English agents?
- Is the Stage-4 Length Expansion regression a fundamental data-distribution problem, or can curriculum interleaving of long-document and high-quality short data avoid the checkpoint-merge crutch?
