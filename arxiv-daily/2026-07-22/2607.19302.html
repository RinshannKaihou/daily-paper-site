01 Methodology

SHRED-ROM is decoding-only: it never reconstructs the state, it maps sensor history directly to control action. The scenario parameter μ is never fed in — it is implicitly encoded in the time-lagged sensor trace, so the same policy controls unseen scenarios.

sk-L:k → fT (LSTM, 2×64) → zk → fX (shallow decoder 350/400, ReLU) → POD coeff. → ukμ
# pre-padding s_k=0 for k≤L ⇒ instantaneous control, no burn-in

A dedicated LSTM latent sensor forecaster φz predicts the next sensor reading from past sensors + past latent controls. When a sensor fails, its prediction is fed back autoregressively, closing the loop in latent space. Theoretical anchor: Takens embedding (non-autonomous extension justifies conditioning on control histories).

Training is pure behavioral cloning on a handful (≈50–100) of expert optimal trajectories from adjoint solves — no online optimization, no HJB solve, no control-space exploration. POD compression keeps the whole pipeline trainable on a laptop.

03 Critical assessment

▲ Strengths

  • Genuinely low-data & sensor-efficient — as few as 1 mobile sensor (pinball), 3 for cylinder flow.
  • Parameter-agnostic: μ is embedded in sensor history, so the policy generalizes to unseen scenarios.
  • Robust to up to 50% sensor failure via the latent forecaster — practically important and convincingly shown.
  • Laptop-grade POD training; clean default hyperparameters minimize tuning.
  • Strong theory (Takens embedding) and full open data + code (Zenodo, GitHub).

▲ Limitations

  • Needs expert adjoint trajectories to train — offline cost is non-trivial, and it inherits expert suboptimality.
  • No baseline comparison vs. MPC, HJB-POD, PINN or DL-ROM controllers — gains measured only against expert.
  • Fluidic pinball results are qualitative only; no reported error metric, so cross-case comparison is hard.
  • Forecaster errors (0.19 / 0.69%) are one-step; multi-step autoregressive drift under long outages untested.
  • Distributed smooth control fields favor POD; bang-bang / sharp-gradient actuation unaddressed.
  • No stability or safety guarantees — a concern for the safety-critical uses cited (autonomous vehicles, plasma).

04 Questions for further exploration

How does accuracy change when expert trajectories come from PINN / DL-ROM solvers rather than full-order adjoint?
What is the autoregressive error-growth law of the latent forecaster under prolonged sensor outage? Is there a critical dropout length?
Can hard constraints (actuator saturation, safe sets) be added for formal guarantees, beyond empirical robustness?
For strongly nonlinear / convective regimes where POD compresses poorly, is there a better nonlinear basis (autoencoder)?
Direct head-to-head vs. MPC / HJB-POD / PINN on control cost, not just reconstruction error?
Could reinforcement-learning fine-tuning at deployment reduce dependence on expert demonstrations?
