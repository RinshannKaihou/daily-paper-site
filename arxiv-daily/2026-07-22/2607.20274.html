Self-supervision drives representational convergence in medical foundation models more than clinical supervision

01

Methodology

  • Encoder panel
    18 frozen image encoders (chest-xray specialists, general SSL DINOv2/v3, vision-language MedGemma, 7 histopathology, fundus RETFound) + a random-init ViT as the alignment floor, plus 7 biomedical text encoders. All open-weight, all run frozen locally.
  • Alignment metric
    Primary: CKNNA (centered-kernel nearest-neighbor, k=10) on a fixed shared image pool; secondary mKNN. All values are 10,000-bootstrap means ± SD with 95% CI; multiple comparisons via Benjamini–Hochberg FDR.
  • Controlled matrix
    From a shared SSL init, train 12 encoders varying only the objective (masked-image SSL / clinical-label supervision / image-text contrastive), backbone capacity (ViT-S/B), and modality — fixed budget, single seed per cell.
  • Synthetic model
    Images generated from a low-dim clinical signal subspace + nuisance subspaces; sweep 5 label-informativeness levels (α) × 5 seeds, eliminating shared data/init confounds.
  • Deployability
    Fit linear disease classifiers in the anchor-based common coordinate system; transfer across encoders (4,284 transfers) and to 5 external hospitals; learn affine feature stitching; cross-encoder drift detector.
  • Expert readers
    2 board-certified radiologists (6 & 10 yrs) annotated a 630-image reference set and 300 clinical-similarity triplets; Cohen's weighted κ on 150 shared images.

03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Causal experiment design. Controlled training matrix + synthetic generative model upgrade correlation to causation — strongest evidence tier on this question.
  • Scale & breadth. 25 encoders, 5 modalities, 6 chest-xray datasets across 3 countries incl. pediatrics, >650k images.
  • Methodological self-reflection. Reports alignment as a regime-dependent measurement; openly declares 8 limitations and multiple failed tools.
  • Practice bridge. Demonstrates geometric-weak → functionally-interoperable via relative representation + stitching + cross-site transfer.
  • Clinical grounding. Expert reader study + consensus geometry recovers empirical comorbidity (not billing codes).

Limitations

  • Consensus config did not converge to tolerance — analyses depending on it (clinical structure, scaling residual, drift detector) are sensitivity-limited; drift detector degenerate (AUROC 50.0).
  • Demographics effectively single-site (only CheXpert records protected attributes); smallest classes (n=296–392) drive the signal; p-values anticonservative due to shared-encoder pairs.
  • Single seed per controlled cell; no averaging over training stochasticity.
  • Native preprocessing confounds encoder identity with preprocessing pipeline.
  • Panel excludes closed-weight & early-fusion unified architectures (e.g. Gemma 4 12B, already in production) — no independently-trained siblings exist yet to measure pairwise.
  • Triplet task near-random partly due to poor task definition (multi-label single images), not only encoder limits.
04

Open Questions

Q1

Does adding centering/scaling before Procrustes make the consensus config converge — and how does that reshape clinical-structure recovery, scaling residuals, and the drift detector?

Q2

Once multiple independently-trained early-fusion unified architectures (e.g. Gemma 4 12B) exist, is convergence still governed by the objective?

Q3

Can multi-site, larger-cohort demographic analysis upgrade the "smallest-class-driven" unevenness into a real fairness fissure — or confirm it as a sample-size artifact?

Q4

Would a finding-conditioned similarity task with a quality-stratified reference set let encoders finally beat chance on radiologist triplets?

Q5

Within SSL itself, which objective family — masked-image, self-distillation, or contrastive instance discrimination — contributes most to convergence?

Q6

Does 109.9% retention on cross-hospital transfer mean MIMIC is harder, or external sites easier? Needs finer site × finding interaction analysis.
