OPIUM: Mitigating Steering Externalities and Over-Refusal via Dual Objective Latent Optimization

Kavin Aravindan, Arihant Rastogi, Aadi Prasad, Krishak Aneja, Saiyam Jain, Vaishnavi Shivkumar & Ponnurangam Kumaraguru — IIIT Hyderabad

ICML 2026 · Mech-Interp Workshop activation-steering llm-safety over-refusal representation-engineering mechanistic-interpretability

Four assets

▎ Key finding

A training-free dual-objective loss sanitizes a steering vector in activation space — preserving target utility while erasing its jailbreak externality and over-refusal.

▎ Key metric

34.1% ← 45.0%

HarmBench ASR on Qwen2.5-7B Corrigibility, recovered toward the 28.7% baseline while utility holds at 70.7%.

▎ Why this matters

A training-free method converging in minutes on one GPU lets practitioners surgically clean steering side effects at inference time — directly deployable.

▎ Critical edge

Only 2 traits × 2 models; comparison layer m and λ are hand-tuned per model, and LLaMA over-refusal gains are ~2 pts at slightly relaxed safety.

Methodology

  • InitializeStart from a CAA steering vector v_A = mean activation difference (last token, residual stream at layer ℓ) over contrastive pairs for behavior A. A baseline refusal vector is extracted analogously.
  • Teacher AOn benign prompts → match the naive v_A-steered model (preserve utility).
  • Teacher BOn harmful prompts → match the unsteered base model (scrub jailbreak externality).
  • InvertFor over-refusal, swap anchors: benign→base, harmful→refusal-steered.
  • OptimizeAdam (η = 0.01); renormalize ‖v‖₂ = ‖v_A‖₂ after every step to defeat trivial scale-down.
  • ModelsQwen2.5-7B (ℓ=18, m=22 / α=4.0) and LLaMA-3.1-8B (ℓ=15, m=19 / α=2.0).
  • Cost32 benign + 32 harmful held-out prompts; minutes on a single 32 GB GPU (RTX 5000 Ada).
  • MechanismExploits latent superposition: >800 near-orthogonal directions trigger the same macro-behavior, so a safe direction is reachable without crossing vulnerability pathways.

Dual-objective representation matching (layer m > ℓ)

L(v) = ‖ z_m^v(x_A) − z_m^vA(x_A) ‖²
     + λ · ‖ z_m^v(x_B) − z_m^vB(x_B) ‖²
v* = argmin L(v),   ‖v‖₂ = ‖v_A‖₂

Strengths & limitations

Strengths

  • Genuinely training-free, fast, cheap — minutes, 32+32 prompts, one 32 GB GPU. Deployment-ready.
  • Elegant unification: one MSE loss handles two opposite failure modes (externality vs over-refusal) by simply flipping anchors.
  • Mechanistically motivated — tied to superposition & latent redundancy, not a black-box trick.
  • Strong externality result: on Qwen, ASR driven below baseline with utility preserved — a clear Pareto win over vanilla steering and directional ablation.
  • Honest, detailed ablations on λ and comparison layer m, with a concrete hyper-parameter recipe.
  • Public code and standard benchmarks (HarmBench, FalseReject) aid reproducibility.

Limitations

  • Thin coverage: only 2 traits × 2 models; no multilingual, persona, or broad-skill evaluation.
  • No principled layer choice for ℓ or m — currently hand-tuned per model; future work is explicitly flagged.
  • Semantic-entanglement failure (e.g. language steering pushing refusals into the target language) is acknowledged but never empirically demonstrated.
  • Composability is open: whether multiple optimized vectors combine without re-introducing interference is left to future work.
  • Modest over-refusal gains on LLaMA-3.1-8B, at the cost of slightly relaxed safety.
  • LLM judge (Gemini 2.5 Flash) for over-refusal introduces unquantified judge bias; no comparison to stronger baselines (CAST, SAE methods).

Questions for further exploration

  1. Does OPIUM scale to more behaviors (power-seeking, sycophancy, persona) and to 70B+ or closed-API models? Does the entropy/representational redundancy story hold with scale?
  2. When target utility and side effect are semantically entangled (e.g. multilingual steering), can a disentanglement regularizer or contrastive objective improve separation — and can the authors' hypothesized failure case be reproduced quantitatively?
  3. Are multiple OPIUM vectors composable? Is there a systematic rule for when composition re-introduces interference, and can a joint multi-objective formulation avoid it?
  4. Can the injection layer ℓ and comparison layer m be selected automatically (via probes, SAE features, or activation geometry) instead of hand-tuning on a validation set?
  5. Against stronger conditional methods (CAST) and SAE-feature ablation, where does OPIUM sit on a broad-skill benchmark (van der Weij et al.)?
  6. How large is the automated-judge bias? Do over-refusal conclusions survive human annotation or a panel of independent judges?
Bottom line — Utility and safety in activation space are not strictly zero-sum. With a few dozen prompts and minutes of optimization, OPIUM demonstrates that a steering vector's harmful side effects can be surgically removed while its target behavior survives — a cheap, deployable baseline for inference-time safety control.
