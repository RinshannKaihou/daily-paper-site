01

Methodology — a two-stage exploit/explore editor

A frozen TFM emits an initial forecast and a predictive distribution. The editor may query an expert scoring function at most B = B₀ + n_r × b times, then must return one edited trajectory. Every candidate is decomposed as y = T(y) + S(y) — centered moving-average trend plus seasonal residual — exposing the dominant error modes at low dimension.

Score reuse is the key idea: max-pooling ≈ mean-pooling (<0.001 MASE difference) — the reuse, not the aggregator, does the work.

03

Critical assessment

Strengths

  • Novel exploit–explore framing with a clean 2×3 factorial ablation isolating each component.
  • Broad evidence: 78 datasets, 3 TFMs, 4 feedback modes, 7 budgets, >500K expert queries, 3 seeds.
  • Sample-efficient by construction — one expert call refines two proposal distributions via score reuse.
  • Generalizes beyond synthetic oracles to physically grounded MD feedback.
  • No per-dataset tuning; the Stage-1/Stage-2 budget split is auto-computed.

Limitations

  • Oracle expert derived from held-out ground truth — real human noise/miscalibration untested.
  • Trend–seasonal decomposition assumes level/trend/amplitude error modes; may fail under regime shifts.
  • TFM stays frozen — combining editing with lightweight adaptation is unexplored.
  • On well-calibrated Chronos, Best-of-N matches or beats DEFT on raw error.
  • TuRBO (BO) only run at B ∈ {2,4,8,16} due to O(n³) GP cost — large-budget BO comparison incomplete.
04

Questions for further exploration

  • QHow does DEFT behave under a noisy or biased human analyst rather than a held-out oracle? Does max-pooling amplify noisy high scores?
  • QCan the trend–seasonal basis be replaced by a learned basis (e.g. an autoencoder) to handle regime shifts and irregular structure?
  • QWould stacking DEFT with lightweight test-time model adaptation compound the gains, or has editing already captured most of the achievable lift?
  • QWhy does Best-of-N (sample) consistently worsen the forecast (MASE 1.12–1.17 on Chronos)? Are TFM sample distributions mis-calibrated vs their quantile grids?
  • QOn Chronos, can DEFT recover the raw-error edge by seeding Stage 2 from quantile candidates rather than stochastic samples?
