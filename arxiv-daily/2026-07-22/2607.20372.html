Notes to Self: Can LLMs Benefit from Experiential Abstractions?

Chang Liu · Xinyu Li · Artur Dubrawski — Auton Lab, Carnegie Mellon University

Small student LLMs can self-extract reusable experiential abstractions from their own training traces — matching frontier-teacher quality — and gain from them via abstraction-augmented RL.

Why This Matters

Removes the frontier-teacher dependency — 1.5–3B models distill and reuse their own reasoning experience through pure outcome-reward RL alone.

Critical Edge

Fixed design choices (teacher, encoder, k=6, dedup 0.85) get no sensitivity analysis, RL is a single epoch, and gains over vanilla GRPO are sometimes marginal (Qwen +0.89).

01

Methodology

two-stage pipeline · GRPO · outcome-reward only
STAGE 1

Library construction

One trace per problem from the target LLM on 7,500 MATH-train problems, labelled correct/incorrect by \boxed{} match. An extractor emits 1–2 single-sentence abstractions per trace — strategy (correct) or caution (incorrect). Dedup via all-MiniLM-L6-v2 cosine union-find at θ=0.85 ≈ 30% smaller.

STAGE 2A

Inference-time retrieval

For each test question, retrieve top-6 abstractions by cosine similarity. An LLM-rewritten query is used only when its top-1 cosine beats the original by >0.02. Notes grouped by type and injected as a <reference_notes> block; solved in a single forward pass.

STAGE 2B

Abstraction-augmented RL

GRPO fine-tunes the policy on (q, R_q) rather than q alone. Per-question group of candidates, outcome reward r∈{0,1}, group-normalised advantage. Pure outcome reward — no process supervision, no teacher demos.

03

Strengths & Limitations

side-by-side

✓ Strengths

  • Blank-notes control isolates abstraction content from prompt-structure effects — a control many retrieval papers omit.
  • Strongest self-improvement thesis: the student needs no frontier model at all, neither as teacher nor at inference.
  • "Exploration vs. sharpening" mechanism explains when and why abstractions help, grounded in per-problem bucketing + a falsified internalization hypothesis.
  • Honest root-causing of negatives: Qwen degradation traced to \boxed{} formatting + retrieval fit; GRPO_train+test explained, not buried.
  • Fully released code, prompts, hyperparameters; reproducible on one 4×A100 in a few hours.

! Limitations

  • No sensitivity analysis on teacher, sentence encoder, k=6, or dedup 0.85 — all fixed choices the authors acknowledge but do not sweep.
  • Modest RL budget (1 epoch, 8 rollouts); scaling could change both magnitude and ordering of gains.
  • Thin transfer evidence — one positive (Phi-3.5-mini) and two negative (Gemma) pairs.
  • Marginal over vanilla GRPO in places (Qwen +0.89 pass@1); incremental value of abstraction-RL beyond standard RLVR is uneven.
  • Not plug-and-play — injection degrades Qwen and the blank template harms Gemma, so per-model validation is required.
  • Restricted scope — math + a logic extension; code, science, agentic tasks untested.
Authors & credibility Chang Liu, Xinyu Li, Artur Dubrawski — Auton Lab, Carnegie Mellon University. Methodologically careful: 5 configs × 2 extraction sources, a matched blank-notes control, two model families, and diagnostic appendices (exploration-vs-sharpening; Qwen root-cause; dedup effects). Code & prompts at github.com/ChangLiu-DrPatient/Notes-to-self. Negative results reported and investigated, not omitted.

Open Questions

for further exploration
Q1How sensitive is self-extraction to the teacher, encoder, k, and dedup threshold? Does a stronger embedding preserve GRPO_train gains?
Q2Under larger-scale RL (more epochs, more rollouts), does the GRPO_train margin over vanilla GRPO grow or converge?
Q3Can the exploration-vs-sharpening account drive an adaptive injection policy — inject only on low-confidence problems?
Q4Is there an optimal abstraction-library size (4k–8k after dedup)? Can coverage/diversity predict downstream gain?
Q5Do experiential abstractions transfer to code, science, or agentic tasks, or does FOLIO→MuSR success rely on shared rule structure?
Q6Can abstractions be made orthogonal to reflection / self-refine — e.g. guiding the direction of reflection rather than mere re-sampling?
