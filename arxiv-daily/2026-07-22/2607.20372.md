> **一句话总结：** 本文研究小规模学生 LLM 能否像人类那样把"经验蒸馏成可复用的抽象笔记（策略/警示）"，从 MATH 训练轨迹中抽取自然语言抽象库，并以"推理时检索注入"或"GRPO 增强提示强化学习"两种方式使用；结果表明自抽取的抽象与前沿教师模型（DeepSeek-V4-Flash）抽取的效果相当，在 MATH-500 上 GRPO_train 相对基线提升 pass@1 约 +6.34（Llama-3.2-3B）与 +3.21（Qwen-2.5-1.5B），并能迁移到 OlympiadBench、MuSR 等其他数据集和模型。

> **TL;DR:** The paper asks whether small student LLMs (Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct, Qwen-2.5-1.5B-Instruct) can extract and benefit from human-like "experiential abstractions" (strategy/caution notes) distilled from their own MATH training traces. It builds a retrievable natural-language abstraction library (extracted either by the target LLM itself or by a DeepSeek-V4-Flash teacher) and uses it in two modes: inference-time retrieval into the test prompt and abstraction-augmented GRPO post-training. On MATH-500, GRPO_train lifts Llama pass@1 by +6.34 over baseline (43.11→49.45) and Qwen by +3.21 (46.30→49.44); self-extracted abstractions match teacher-extracted ones, and the pipeline transfers to OlympiadBench (+3.43 pass@1) and to the Phi-3.5-mini model.

## 核心贡献

1. **首次系统验证"小规模学生 LLM 能自身抽取并利用经验抽象"，而非依赖前沿模型。** 与 ExpeL、Dynamic Cheatsheet、RLAD 等工作（要么冻结前沿模型，要么用强教师监督抽象生成器）不同，本文用纯 outcome-reward RL（GRPO）证明目标 LLM 自己抽取的抽象即可媲美 DeepSeek 教师抽取的抽象。

2. **提出完整的"经验抽象"两阶段流程。** 阶段一：从目标 LLM 的训练轨迹（MATH 训练集 7,500 题，每题采样一条轨迹）按正确性条件抽取"策略"（正确解的关键推理动作）或"警示"（错误解的失误模式），经句向量嵌入与余弦相似度并查集去重（阈值 0.85，库容缩小约 30%）构建可检索库。阶段二：两种使用模式——推理时检索（top-6）注入 `<reference_notes>` 块，或在 GRPO 训练时把检索到的抽象拼入训练提示。

3. **揭示"推理时检索"与"训练时增强 RL"的不同行为机制。** 推理注入对 Llama 有效但对 Qwen 略有损害（源于答案格式被破坏与检索失配，见附录 B，而非警示性约束的副作用）；而训练时增强 RL 在两个模型上均一致有效。更有意思的是 GRPO_train+test 反而劣于 GRPO_train，作者通过分桶分析给出"探索 vs. 锐化（exploration vs. sharpening）"的解释。

4. **用"探索 vs. 锐化"框架精确刻画抽象的作用条件。** 通过把 MATH-500 按预注入准确率分桶（0–50% / 50–100% / 100%）发现：抽象在模型较弱的题上恒有帮助（低桶 +6.31），在模型已熟练的题上有害（高桶 −2.32），且 RL 锐化后这种负面效应更强——这解释了为什么抽象帮助更难的 OlympiadBench 却不影响已饱和的 GSM8K。

5. **验证抽象库的可迁移性。** Llama 轨迹抽取的抽象可迁移到 Phi-3.5-mini-instruct（pass@1 40.62→43.60）；MATH 训练的 checkpoint 在 OlympiadBench（17.34 vs 基线 13.91）与 MuSR 上也有提升；但迁移性对下游模型敏感——同样的模板会伤害 Gemma 系列（其空白模板本身就已损性能）。

## Key Contributions

1. **First systematic demonstration that small student LLMs can extract and benefit from experiential abstractions on their own, without frontier-model supervision.** Unlike ExpeL (frozen frontier LLM), Dynamic Cheatsheet (test-time memory for black-box frontier LLMs), and RLAD (two-player RL with a strong teacher supervising the abstraction generator), this work shows via pure outcome-reward GRPO that self-extracted abstractions perform comparably to DeepSeek-V4-Flash teacher-extracted ones.

2. **A complete two-stage "experiential abstraction" pipeline.** Stage 1: from the target LLM's training traces (MATH training split, 7,500 problems, one trace each) extract correctness-conditioned strategy (key reasoning move for correct traces) or caution (mistake pattern for incorrect traces) abstractions, dedup with sentence embeddings and cosine-similarity union-find (threshold 0.85, ~30% library reduction) into a retrievable library. Stage 2: two usage modes — inference-time top-6 retrieval into a `<reference_notes>` block, or GRPO training with retrieved abstractions concatenated to each training prompt.

3. **Articulates distinct mechanisms for inference-time vs. RL-time abstraction use.** Inference injection helps Llama but slightly hurts Qwen — traced in Appendix B to answer-format disruption (\boxed{} omission rate 3.8%→4.6%) and occasional poor retrieval fit, not to cautions acting as harmful constraints. RL-time abstraction augmentation is consistently positive on both models. Notably GRPO_train+test underperforms GRPO_train, which the authors attribute to RL sharpening the policy into near-deterministic correct modes that abstraction perturbation then disrupts.

4. **A clean "exploration vs. sharpening" account of when abstractions help.** Bucketing MATH-500 by pre-injection per-problem accuracy shows abstractions help where the model is weak (low-accuracy bucket +6.31 pass@1) and hurt where it is strong (high bucket −2.32), and this detrimental effect grows after RL tuning. This explains why abstractions aid the harder OlympiadBench but not the saturated GSM8K, and why the internalization hypothesis (that a fine-tuned model has "absorbed" abstractions) fails — injection also degrades plain GRPO that never saw abstractions in training.

5. **Demonstrates cross-model and cross-dataset transferability.** Llama-derived abstractions transfer to Phi-3.5-mini-instruct (pass@1 40.62→43.60) and MATH-trained checkpoints induce gains on OlympiadBench (17.34 vs. baseline 13.91) and on MuSR pass@8; but transfer is downstream-model-specific — the same blank template already harms Gemma models, indicating abstraction transferability is not universal.

## Methodology

**Library construction (Section 3.1).** Collect one solution trace per problem from target LLM π_θ on the MATH training split (7,500 problems); label each trace correct/incorrect by \boxed extraction and ground-truth match. Prompt an extractor (either the target LLM itself, or the DeepSeek-V4-Flash teacher) to produce 1–2 single-sentence, reusable abstractions per trace, conditioned on correctness: strategy abstractions for correct traces (key reasoning move worth reusing), caution abstractions for incorrect traces (mistake pattern to avoid). Embed each abstraction with all-MiniLM-L6-v2 into 384-dim ℓ2-normalized vectors; dedup via cosine-similarity union-find at threshold 0.85, keeping strategy and caution entries in separate buckets (≈30% size reduction).

**Inference-time use (Section 3.2).** For each test question, retrieve top-k (k=6) abstractions by cosine similarity. Two query forms: default ("[{subject}] {question}") and a rewritten query (LLM re-states the problem as one abstraction-style sentence), gated so the rewrite is used only when its top-1 cosine exceeds the original's by >0.02. Retrieved abstractions are grouped by type (strategy/caution) and injected as a `<reference_notes>` block after the question; the LLM solves in a single forward pass.

**Abstraction-augmented RL post-training (Section 3.3).** Apply the same retrieval to each training question, then GRPO fine-tune the target LLM so the policy conditions on (q, R_q) rather than q alone. For each training question GRPO samples a group of candidate solutions, scores with outcome reward r∈{0,1}, normalizes within the group to form advantage Â(q,y), and maximizes J(θ)=E[Â(q,y) log π_θ(y|q,R_q)]. Reward and optimization match standard GRPO; the policy is now conditioned on (q,R_q).

**Setup (Section 4).** Target LLMs: Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct and Qwen-2.5-1.5B-Instruct. Teacher: DeepSeek-V4-Flash (via OpenRouter API). Hardware: NVIDIA DGX Station with 4× A100 80GB; MATH training run ≈2.5h, MATH-500 inference ≈0.3h, trace extraction ≈0.5h. RL uses verl, 1 epoch, 8 rollouts/prompt, lr 1e-6, cosine schedule, KL coeff 5e-4. Evaluation: pass@1/pass@8 averaged over 8 rollouts at temperature 0.6. Configurations compared: Baseline, Inference_abs, GRPO (vanilla), GRPO_train, GRPO_train+test, each with teacher- or self-extracted abstractions.

## Results & Highlights

1. **Abstraction-augmented RL yields consistent MATH-500 gains on both models (Table 1).** For Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct, GRPO_train (teacher-extracted) reaches **pass@1 49.45 / pass@8 76.34**, i.e. **+6.34 pass@1 over baseline (43.11)** and **+2.56 over vanilla GRPO (46.89)**. For Qwen-2.5-1.5B-Instruct, GRPO_train reaches **pass@1 50.23 / pass@8 76.47**, i.e. **+3.21 pass@1 over baseline (46.30)** and **+0.89 over vanilla GRPO (49.44)**. Inference-time injection alone helps Llama (**+3.31 pass@1**, 43.11→46.42, +2.23 pass@8) but slightly hurts Qwen (50.23→49.14).

2. **Self-extracted abstractions match teacher-extracted ones across all configurations (Table 1).** E.g. Llama GRPO_train self vs. teacher: **49.07/71.14 vs. 49.45/71.98** pass@1/pass@8; Qwen GRPO_train self vs. teacher: **53.77/76.83 vs. 53.44/76.34**. This holds that a small LLM can distill useful experiential abstractions from its own traces without frontier supervision.

3. **GRPO_train+test underperforms GRPO_train, explained by RL sharpening (Table 1, Tables 5–6, Appendix A).** Re-injecting abstractions at test time degrades *both* RL-tuned checkpoints (plain GRPO 46.89→45.76, −1.13; GRPO_train 49.45→47.65, −1.80), contradicting the internalization hypothesis (which would predict harm only for GRPO_train). Bucketing by pre-injection accuracy shows abstractions help the low bucket (+6.31) but hurt the high bucket (−2.32), increasingly so after RL tuning — consistent with abstractions acting as a distribution perturbation that aids exploration on weak problems but displaces mass from already-concentrated correct modes.

4. **Abstractions help harder problems and transfer across datasets (Table 3).** Llama MATH-derived abstractions induce gains on the harder **OlympiadBench** (GRPO_train pass@1 **17.34 vs. baseline 13.91, +3.43**) but not on the saturated **GSM8K** (where plain GRPO alone already jumps 54.82→77.34). On **MuSR** logical reasoning, FOLIO-trained checkpoints recover pass@8 (**MuSR_FOLIO GRPO_train pass@8 79.37 vs. baseline 75.00**), showing logical-reasoning abstractions extend capability even though MATH alone provides only RLVR signal.

5. **Cross-model transfer is downstream-specific (Table 4).** Llama-derived teacher abstractions injected into Phi-3.5-mini-instruct raise pass@1 **40.62→43.60** and pass@8 64.00→65.80, but the same blank template already harms Gemma-2-2B-it (21.77→13.08) and Gemma-3-3B-it (44.88→42.02), indicating transferability depends on the downstream model's compatibility with the injection template.

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are **Chang Liu, Xinyu Li, and Artur Dubrawski** of the **Auton Lab, Carnegie Mellon University** — a group with a strong track record in machine learning for data-intensive decision-making. The work is methodologically careful: it compares five configurations × two extraction sources (teacher/self), uses a matched "blank notes" control (Appendix Table 2) that isolates prompt-structure effects from abstraction content, runs the same library through both Llama and Qwen, validates conclusions on two model families, and includes diagnostic appendices (A: exploration-vs-sharpening; B: Qwen degradation root-caused to formatting and retrieval fit, not caution constraints; C: deduplication effects). Code and prompts are released at github.com/ChangLiu-DrPatient/Notes-to-self, and full extraction/inference prompts are reproduced in Appendix F. The paper honestly reports negative results (Qwen inference-time degradation, GRPO_train+test underperformance) and investigates their causes rather than burying them. It positions itself precisely against the most relevant prior work (ExpeL, Dynamic Cheatsheet, RLAD, Reflexion/Self-Refine) and clearly states what is novel (student self-extraction via outcome-reward RL alone, without frontier inference or teacher demonstrations).

## Critical Assessment

The paper's central claim — that small student LLMs can extract and benefit from experiential abstractions on a par with frontier teachers — is well-supported and genuinely extends the experiential-learning line (ExpeL, Dynamic Cheatsheet, RLAD), which previously required a frontier model either as the target or as the supervisor. Three points deserve emphasis:

- **The self-extraction result is the load-bearing finding and is properly controlled.** Table 1 shows self-extracted abstractions match teacher-extracted ones within ~0.4 pass@1 across five configurations and two models, and Appendix C confirms the libraries are of comparable size after deduplication (e.g. Llama teacher 7,676 vs. self 4,232 entries). The conclusion that a 1.5–3B model can distill reusable reasoning notes from its own traces is non-trivial and directly answers the gap the paper identifies.
- **The "exploration vs. sharpening" analysis is the most insightful contribution beyond the headline numbers.** The bucketed analysis (Table 6) and the internalization-hypothesis falsification (Table 5: injection hurts plain GRPO that never saw abstractions in training) give a mechanistic story for *when* abstractions help, which is more durable than a benchmark number.
- **The honesty about GRPO_train+test underperforming GRPO_train is commendable** and is explained rather than omitted; the RL-sharpening account is consistent with Yue et al. 2025.

The main caveats are scope and design-fixedness. The authors themselves flag (Limitations) that results depend on fixed choices — a particular teacher (DeepSeek-V4-Flash), a single sentence encoder (all-MiniLM-L6-v2), a fixed k=6, and a 0.85 dedup threshold — for which **no sensitivity analysis is provided**. RL was run for only one epoch with eight rollouts per prompt under a modest budget, so the magnitude and even ordering of gains could shift with more compute. Cross-model transfer is demonstrated for only one positive pair (Llama→Phi-3.5-mini) and two negative pairs (Gemma), so broader transfer claims need more pairs. The absolute pass@1 numbers remain modest (Llama 49.45, Qwen 50.23 on MATH-500), and the Qwen inference-time degradation shows the pipeline is not robustly plug-and-play across model families. Finally, the gains over vanilla GRPO are small in places (Qwen +0.89 pass@1), so the marginal value of abstraction-augmented RL beyond standard RLVR is the weakest part of the quantitative case.

## Strengths

- Cleanly isolates abstraction *content* effects from prompt-structure effects via the "blank notes" control (Table 2), a methodological touch many retrieval-augmentation papers omit.
- Demonstrates the strongest possible version of the self-improvement thesis: the student needs no frontier model at all — neither as teacher nor at inference.
- Provides a mechanistic ("exploration vs. sharpening") explanation for when and why abstractions help, grounded in per-problem-accuracy bucketing and a falsified alternative (internalization) hypothesis.
- Honest reporting and root-causing of negative results (Qwen inference degradation traced to \boxed{} formatting and retrieval mismatch; GRPO_train+test underperformance).
- Fully released code, prompts, and hyperparameters; reproducible on a single 4×A100 DGX Station in a few hours per run.

## Limitations

- **No sensitivity analysis** on the teacher model, sentence encoder, retrieval cutoff k=6, or dedup threshold 0.85 — all are fixed design choices the authors acknowledge but do not sweep.
- **Modest RL budget** (one epoch, eight rollouts/prompt); scaling could change both the magnitude and the ordering of observed gains, so the GRPO_train-vs-GRPO_train+test gap in particular is not necessarily stable.
- **Limited cross-model transfer evidence** — one positive (Phi-3.5-mini) and two negative (Gemma) pairs; broader transferability claims require more target models, and the Gemma failure shows the pipeline is template-dependent.
- **Marginal gains over vanilla GRPO are small** in places (Qwen +0.89 pass@1), so the incremental value of abstraction-augmented RL over standard RLVR is not uniformly large.
- **Pipeline is not plug-and-play across families** — inference-time injection degrades Qwen (formatting/retrieval issues) and the blank template harms Gemma, so deployment requires per-model validation.
- **Restricted task scope** — mathematical problem solving plus a logical-reasoning extension; generalization to code, science, or open-ended agentic tasks is untested.
- **Risk of misleading heuristics** in the abstraction library is only mitigated, not eliminated, by outcome-reward RL reinforcing verified-correct solutions.

## Relevance

这项工作对"经验式学习 / 自我改进（experiential learning / self-improvement）"、"检索增强推理（RAG-style reasoning）"和"LLM 强化学习（RLVR / GRPO）"三个社区都有直接价值。对做 LLM 自我改进、test-time scaling、agent 经验复用的研究者，它提供了一个轻量、可复现的基线：不需要前沿教师，小模型自己就能把训练轨迹蒸馏成可检索的"经验笔记"。对做 GRPO/RLVR 的研究者，"探索 vs. 锐化"的分析（附录 A、表 5–6）为"何时该在 RL 后继续用检索增强"提供了具体且反直觉的指导——RL 锐化后再注入抽象反而有害。对做检索增强与 prompt 工程的读者，"空白笔记对照（blank notes control）"是一个值得借鉴的实验设计，可以严格区分"模板结构"与"内容"的贡献。与 ExpeL、Dynamic Cheatsheet、RLAD、Metacognitive Reuse、SkillRL 等同期工作相比，本文的独特定位是"学生模型纯靠 outcome-reward RL 自抽取自利用"，可作为这一方向上小模型规模化的参照点。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- 自抽取抽象的有效性对教师模型、句向量编码器（all-MiniLM-L6-v2）、检索 cutoff k=6、去重阈值 0.85 有多敏感？换成更强的嵌入或不同的 k，GRPO_train 的增益是否保持？
- 在更大规模 RL（多 epoch、更多 rollout）下，GRPO_train 相对 vanilla GRPO 的边际增益是扩大还是收敛？GRPO_train+test 劣于 GRPO_train 的排序是否在更长训练后仍然成立？
- "探索 vs. 锐化"的解释是否可用来设计自适应注入策略——例如只在低置信度题上注入抽象、在高置信度题上跳过——从而同时获得推理时与训练时的收益？
- 自抽取抽象的质量与数量（去重后库容 4k–8k）是否存在最优点？能否用抽象库的覆盖率/多样度指标预测下游增益？
- 经验抽象能否迁移到非数学领域（代码生成、科学问答、agentic 工具使用）？逻辑推理（FOLIO→MuSR）的成功是否依赖于两个领域共享某种"规则结构"？
- 能否将"经验抽象"与 reflection/self-refine 等 test-time scaling 方法正交结合——例如用抽象库指导反射的方向，而非单纯多次采样？
- Qwen 推理时注入的失败（格式破坏 + 检索失配）是否可以通过对模型做"格式对齐"微调或引入重排序器来缓解，从而让推理时检索在更多模型族上稳定有效？
