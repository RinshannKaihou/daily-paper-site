> **一句话总结：** PyroDash 让小模型（SLM）在推理过程中通过自学的控制 token τoff 决定何时一次性把部分推理轨迹交接给冻结的大模型（LLM）完成，并用 GRPO 对"准确率—推理成本"做成本感知对齐；在五个数学推理基准上，λ=0.05 时平均准确率 64.04%，比 LLM-only 高 6.36 个百分点且成本降 20.4%，λ=0.6 时总成本从 49.36 美元降到 1.78 美元（降 96.4%）。

> **TL;DR:** PyroDash internalizes a token-level handoff policy in a 4B SLM (Qwen3.5-4B) that emits a control token τoff to trigger a single, one-way handoff to a frozen LLM (GLM-5.2-FP8); a three-stage pipeline (control-token embedding → offloading SFT → cost-aware GRPO) trains the SLM with a reward normalized against LLM-only cost. On five math reasoning benchmarks, λ=0.05 reaches 64.04% avg accuracy (+6.36pp over the 57.68% LLM-only baseline) while cutting cost 20.4%, and λ=0.6 cuts total cost from $49.36 to $1.78 (−96.4%) at 54.55% accuracy with only 1.90% LLM token ratio.

## 核心贡献

1. **Token 级协作推理架构（SLM 内化、单次交接）**：提出 PyroDash，把"何时把推理轨迹交给大模型"这一决策完全内化到 SLM 自身的生成过程中。SLM 在解码时若认为当前步骤超出自身能力，就发出专用控制 token `τoff`；Collaborate Engine (CE) 检测到该 token 后，把"原 query + SLM 部分推理轨迹"打包，向冻结的 LLM 发起**单次、单向**交接完成剩余推理。整个过程无需独立路由器、无需重训 LLM、无需访问 LLM logits，因而兼容闭源 LLM API。

2. **成本感知的三阶段渐进式训练流水线**：Stage 1 控制词嵌入学习（用锚点 token 均值+高斯噪声初始化 `τoff`，训练后丢弃 LoRA、只保留 `τoff` 行）；Stage 2 卸载导向 SFT 冷启动（在 Corpus A/B 上联合学习"独立完成"与"条件卸载"）；Stage 3 用 GRPO 做成本感知策略对齐。

3. **以 LLM-only 成本为基准的归一化奖励**：GRPO 的奖励 `R_total = R_acc − λ·R_eff`，其中 `R_eff` 是该请求在协作模式下的真实货币成本（含 SLM prefill/decode + LLM prefill/decode）除以"同一 query 用 LLM 单独跑"的基准成本。通过调节 `λ` 得到一族覆盖"高准确率—极低成本"的策略点，第一次把部署计费（分 prefill/decode 的 token 单价）显式对齐进强化学习目标。

## Key Contributions

- **SLM-internalized, single-handoff collaborative inference.** Unlike request-level routers (RouteLLM, Hybrid LLM) that pick a model before decoding, or token-level methods that interleave/repeat switches (CITER, Co-LLM, RelayLLM), PyroDash makes the *small* model itself decide mid-generation to emit `τoff`, and the Collaborate Engine performs at most one SLM→LLM handoff per request. The LLM stays frozen and is invoked through a standard text-completion API.

- **A novel GRPO reward normalized against LLM-only cost.** Instead of penalizing raw LLM-token share (RelayLLM) or having no explicit cost objective (Co-LLM), PyroDash defines `Cost_base = C_pre·T_in + C_dec·T_l,dec` for LLM-only inference and normalizes the actual collaborative cost against it. This couples the offloading policy with real per-token billing (prefill priced separately from decode), turning `λ` into a direct quality–cost knob.

- **Substantial, decomposable cost reduction with accuracy preserved or improved.** On five math benchmarks, the quality-oriented policy (λ=0.05) *exceeds* the LLM-only baseline by 6.36pp while cutting cost 20.4%; the cost-oriented policy (λ=0.6) cuts total cost 96.4% ($49.36→$1.78) and reduces LLM token ratio to 1.90% — about 1/40th of RouteLLM (77.37%) and GlimpRouter (75.11%).

## Methodology

**Problem formulation.** Given trainable SLM `M_s` and frozen LLM `M_l`, with fixed prompts `P_s` (SLM offloading prompt, enables `τoff`) and `P_l` (LLM completion prompt), maximize expected accuracy minus a cost penalty normalized by LLM-only cost:

> max_{π_s} E_(q,y)~D [ Acc(O(q;π_s), y) − λ·Cost_norm(q;π_s) ]

**Architecture (Algorithm 1, single-handoff decoding).** SLM-first decoding; the CE streams SLM tokens until it detects `τoff`, then `Pack`s the preceding trace into `C_s` (excluding the control token), terminates SLM decoding, calls `M_l.Complete(P_l, q, C_s)` once, and returns `O_s ∥ O_l`. Control never returns to the SLM, so each request ends with either SLM-only output or exactly one LLM call.

**Dataset curation (EasyHard-24k, 24,061 examples).** Examples are split by whether the base SLM solves them: "easy" (SLM-correct) vs "hard" (SLM-wrong but a correct CoT can be reconstructed by the LLM). Two prompt corpora: Corpus A omits `P_s` (no `τoff`, preserves standalone behavior); Corpus B adds `P_s` (easy targets omit `τoff`, hard targets insert 1–4 `τoff` at synthetic CoT breakpoints). This avoids conflating "is this hard" with "is offloading available."

**Three-stage pipeline (Algorithm 2).**
- *Stage 1 — Control-token embedding learning.* Expand vocab with `τoff`; initialize its input/output embeddings as the mean of anchor-token embeddings (period, newline, EOS) plus Gaussian noise (σ=0.1). Fine-tune embedding layer + output head + transient LoRA on Corpus B, then discard LoRA and keep only the learned `τoff` rows.
- *Stage 2 — Offloading-oriented SFT.* One round of SFT on a mix of Corpora A and B (Eq. 3), jointly learning standalone completion (Corpus A) and prompt-conditional offloading (Corpus B). Embedding layer frozen, LoRA on attention layers.
- *Stage 3 — Cost-aware GRPO.* G=8 rollouts per prompt; each rollout replays the exact inference-time handoff (Algorithm 1) inside the training loop. Reward `R_total = R_acc − λ·R_eff`; group-relative advantage `Â` (Eq. 8, no critic); PPO-clipped loss (Eq. 9). Only SLM tokens enter the gradient; the frozen LLM affects the update only through `R_acc` and `R_eff`.

**Implementation.** SLM Qwen3.5-4B, LLM GLM-5.2-FP8; Stage 1–2 LoRA r=8/α=16, 4 epochs, lr 1e-5; Stage 3 LoRA r=16/α=32, 1 epoch, lr 5e-6; trained with TRL on 4×H100; served with vLLM (SLM on 4×H100, LLM on 8×B200), thinking mode, max_tokens=8192. Stage 3 uses DAPO-Math.

## Results & Highlights

Across five math reasoning benchmarks (Minerva, GSM8K, Olympiad-Bench, AIME-2025, AIME-2024; pass@1 for the first three, avg@32 for AIME; unweighted mean as aggregate):

- **Quality-oriented policy beats the LLM-only baseline.** PyroDash at λ=0.05 reaches **64.04% avg accuracy vs. GLM-5.2-FP8's 57.68% (+6.36pp)**, and posts the highest score on four of five benchmarks — e.g., AIME-2024 **63.75% vs. LLM-only 46.56%**, Olympiad-Bench **64.89% vs. 61.04%** — while reducing total cost **20.4%** ($49.36→$39.29). Table 1.

- **Cost-oriented policy slashes LLM usage and bill.** PyroDash at λ=0.6 reaches **54.55% avg accuracy (still above RouteLLM's 52.74% and GlimpRouter's 54.20%) with only 1.90% LLM token ratio and 0.012 LLM calls/example, cutting total cost from $49.36 to $1.78 (−96.4%)**. By contrast, RouteLLM spends 77.37% LLM tokens at $44.62 and GlimpRouter spends 75.11% at $31.61. Tables 1–2.

- **GRPO stage delivers gains beyond SFT cold start.** Qwen3.5-4B base = 28.36% avg; after Stages 1–2 (SFT) = 46.25%; after full pipeline at λ=0.05 = 64.04% (AIME-2024 jumps from 28.23% to 63.75%). Table 1.

- **λ sweeps the accuracy–cost frontier (Q3).** From λ=0.05→0.1 the LLM token ratio collapses 95.34%→8.19% and cost $39.29→$4.71 (accuracy 64.04%→55.29%); for λ≥0.1 accuracy plateaus at 54.20–55.29% while LLM tokens fall to 1.90% and cost to $1.78. Table 3.

- **Cost decomposition traces savings to measured tokens (Q4).** At λ=0.6, LLM output tokens drop from 17.08M → 0.23M and total cost from $49.36 → $1.78; PyroDash produces 12.07M SLM output tokens vs. 27.86M for standalone SLM (fewer total output tokens also contribute). Table 4. Pricing used: Qwen3.5-4B $0.05/$0.08 per M in/out; GLM-5.2-FP8 $0.90/$2.86 per M in/out.

## Critical Assessment

- **The headline "+6.36pp over LLM-only" needs scrutiny.** The LLM-only reference is GLM-5.2-FP8 at 57.68% avg — yet at λ=0.05 PyroDash routes **95.34%** of decoded tokens to the LLM and makes **0.975 LLM calls/example** (i.e., it calls the LLM for almost every example). So the "beat LLM-only" result largely reflects *better prompting* (SLM partial trace as a CoT prefix handed to the LLM) rather than cost-efficient *avoidance* of the LLM. This is a genuine and interesting finding (a decomposed CoT prefix helps the frozen LLM), but readers should not read λ=0.05 as a cost-savings configuration — it costs $39.29 vs. $49.36, a modest 20.4% cut while using ~95% LLM tokens. The 96.4% cost reduction only appears at λ=0.6, where accuracy is **3.13pp below** LLM-only (54.55% vs. 57.68%), contradicting the framing that cost savings come "while preserving strong reasoning performance."

- **The cost-vs-accuracy curve is non-monotonic and mostly flat for λ≥0.1.** Table 3 shows accuracy 55.29% (λ=0.1), 54.91% (0.2), 54.20% (0.3), 54.55% (0.6) — essentially flat — while the "interesting" regime is entirely the jump between λ=0.05 and λ=0.1. This suggests a bimodal policy (mostly-hand-off vs. mostly-don't) rather than a smoothly tunable knob, which weakens the "family of policies for different preferences" narrative.

- **Cost is estimated, not billed.** The authors explicitly state (§6) that monetary costs are computed from listed token prices, not real provider invoices, and the LLM-token-ratio metric (Eq. 10) excludes prefill tokens — so the headline ratio can understate the *prefill* cost that the SLM trace adds to the LLM call. The cost decomposition (Table 4) does account for prefill, but readers should treat the $ figures as a pricing model, not an empirical bill.

- **Only one SLM–LLM pair, only math.** Every number comes from the Qwen3.5-4B / GLM-5.2-FP8 pair on math reasoning. Generalization to code, tool use, multimodal, or other model pairs is unverified (the authors flag this in §6).

## Strengths

- Clean, deployable architecture: single-handoff, LLM frozen, no separate router, no logit access → genuinely compatible with stateless proprietary APIs. The CE needs only token detection + a completion call.

- The cost-normalized GRPO reward is the right idea — pricing prefill and decode separately and normalizing against LLM-only baseline directly addresses the known weakness of token-share metrics (which Co-LLM/RelayLLM use and which obscure prefill charges under stateless APIs).

- Honest, detailed cost decomposition (Table 4) that ties every dollar to measured in/out tokens for both models across each benchmark — rare in this literature.

- Strong empirical wins on the harder benchmarks (AIME-2024 +17.19pp, Olympiad +3.85pp over LLM-only), and an ablating λ-sweep (Table 3) rather than a single operating point.

## Limitations

- **Evaluation does not validate offloading *rationality*.** The authors concede (§6) they never check whether the SLM hands off *precisely* when its reasoning becomes unreliable — no analysis of trigger positions vs. specific reasoning failures. This is the central mechanistic question and it is unanswered.

- **No ablation of the normalization design.** Stage 3 normalizes cost against LLM-only, but there is no controlled comparison against a direct token-cost penalty, so the benefit of normalization itself is not isolated.

- **Scope is narrow:** one SLM, one LLM, math-only; multi-turn and multi-handoff settings are explicitly left to future work (only one handoff per request is allowed).

- **The "+6.36pp over LLM-only" headline is achieved while using ~95% LLM tokens**, so it is closer to "prompt-engineering the LLM via an SLM prefix" than to cost-efficient collaboration; the genuinely cost-efficient regime (λ=0.6) is *below* LLM-only accuracy.

- **Costs are estimates from listed prices, not actual bills**; and LLM-token-ratio (Eq. 10) omits prefill, so it can mislead about true monetary reliance on the LLM.

## Relevance

这篇论文对"**大模型推理成本优化 / 路由与级联服务 / SLM-LLM 协同**"方向高度相关。它把 token 级路由从"外部 router 或交错解码"推进到"SLM 自身内化的单次交接"，并用归一化货币成本做 RL 对齐，是对 RouteLLM、CITER、Co-LLM、RelayLLM 这条线的直接推进。对工业界做混合推理、API 成本治理、边缘-云协同 (SLM 本地 + LLM 云端) 的团队有直接参考价值；对做 GRPO/奖励设计的 RL 研究者，"以 LLM-only 成本为基准的归一化奖励"是一个值得借鉴的设计。局限在于目前只在数学推理上验证，且"低成本档"准确率仍低于 LLM-only，所以更适合作为"成本可调的协同推理范式"参考，而非即插即用的 SOTA 路由器。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Offloading rationality check**: Do the `τoff` trigger positions correlate with verifiable SLM reasoning errors (e.g., first incorrect arithmetic step), or is the SLM opportunistically offloading on surface cues? Can we measure handoff precision/recall against ground-truth failure points?

2. **Why is the accuracy plateau flat for λ∈[0.1, 0.6]?** Is the policy effectively binary (offload ~5–6% of the time) regardless of λ, and would a curriculum or entropy bonus recover intermediate operating points?

3. **Does the λ=0.05 win generalize, or is it a CoT-prefix effect?** Ablate by feeding the LLM a *random/SFT-only* SLM prefix of equal length — is the gain from the learned offloading policy or just from providing any structured prefix?

4. **Prefill-aware reward**: Since the SLM trace becomes LLM prefill, does a reward that explicitly penalizes long prefill (not just total cost) change the learned handoff position toward earlier, cheaper handoffs?

5. **Generalization beyond math**: How does PyroDash behave on code generation, agentic tool-use, and multi-turn dialogues, where "capability boundary" is less well-defined and verifiers are weaker?

6. **Multi-handoff and multi-turn**: Would allowing >1 handoff per turn (or returning control to the SLM) close the λ=0.6 accuracy gap to LLM-only, and at what cost?

7. **Model-pair sensitivity**: Is the cost–accuracy frontier stable if the LLM is much stronger (e.g., a frontier proprietary model) or much closer in size to the SLM?
