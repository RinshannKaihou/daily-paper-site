From Bit-Position Sensitivity to Unequal Error Protection for DNN Inference Memory

AMD · Muhammad Husnain Mubarik, Karthik Mohan Kumar, Pedro Antonio Pena, Keshavan Varadarajan, Kunal Tyagi

MICRO 2026 unequal-error-protection floating-point dnn-inference ml-accelerator sram / ecc

Key Metric

27.8%

ECC codec area cut vs. uniform SECDED — 17,895 vs 24,780 µm².

Plus ~17% gross BF16 read-energy saving · MCU-2 correction 52.4%→100% · tag overhead 0.29%.

Why This Matters

Turns "which FP bits truly need protection" into deployable ECC / SRAM co-design parameters for accelerator energy efficiency and reliability.

No retraining · stored numerics unchanged.

Critical Edge

No silicon validation — all hardware numbers are sky130 RTL + CACTI 7 nm projection; the larger 56–62.5% savings need per-workload tiering not yet shown in a live controller.

Bounded at silicon scale, not invalidated.

01 Methodology

Three coupled stages — empirical sensitivity, codec design, hardware eval — across 16 models / 6 modalities / 3 FP formats.

1

Fault modeling & sensitivity sweep

Continuous analog noise (Gaussian, uniform, 1/f, lognormal) + discrete bit-flips (deterministic per-position, stochastic, 2/3-bit MCU). 870+ injections with 95% CIs, Wilcoxon, Clopper–Pearson, Bonferroni McNemar.

2

From floors to a three-tier codec

Measured Xsafe floors drive a single-codeword UEP matrix: Tier1 sign+exponent SECDED, Tier2 upper-mantissa SEC, Tier3 lower-mantissa bypass. A 3-bit per-cacheline tag (0.29% overhead) drives a hardwired Pattern MUX — 4× cheaper than a barrel shifter.

3

SRAM-level co-design

Dual-partition SRAM: Partition A at 1.8 V holds Tier1+checks; Partition B at 1.2 V holds Tier2/3. Verified with 146,000+ RTL vectors; macro modeled in CACTI 7.0 projected to 7 nm. Reliability composed from SER + dual-voltage BER models.

02 Results & Highlights

The sensitivity cliff

A two-phase pattern: corrupting fraction LSBs is negligible, then quality collapses at the exponent boundary. Llama-3 8B perplexity: 7.01 → 98.6 across bit 11→12 (14.8×). SD3.5 BF16 PSNR: 31 → 14 dB over just 4 adjacent bits.

03 Strengths

  • Comprehensive empirical sweep — 16 models / 6 modalities / 3 data types / 870+ injections, with attention-free CNNs as a clean control for "FP format vs. attention mechanism".
  • Xsafe is a data-type property, not a model artifact — fixed at design time, directly usable as a hardware parameter.
  • Clean three-component architecture (UEP codec + per-cacheline tag + dual-partition SRAM) with each overhead quantified and recovered by savings.
  • No retraining; stored numeric representation unchanged — low adoption friction.
  • Forward-looking: operand narrowing to FP8/FP4 expands UEP's relevance because accumulators stay wide.

04 Limitations

  • No silicon: sky130 RTL + CACTI 7 nm projection only; 7 nm extrapolation carries unquantified error bars.
  • Fault realism is partial — synthetic + bounded-neighborhood injection; no field trace-derived spatial/temporal logs.
  • Dual-voltage SRAM constraints — bank-level granularity, ~10 ns rail-switch settle, place-and-route cost not fully laid out.
  • Per-bit statistical scope limited to BERT-base & ViT-L/16; other 14 models use deterministic sweeps.
  • Conservative floor set by worst-case diffusion — non-diffusion users must opt into tiering for larger savings.
  • Persistent-fault polarity asymmetry (sign stuck-at-0 → PPL >1500×); aggressive-point safety relies on transient BER² scaling.

05 Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How do Xsafe floors shift under PTQ (FP8/INT8 weights, FP16/BF16 accumulators), where quantization may make each retained mantissa bit more critical?
  2. Can per-cacheline tag + tiering extend to weight-side UEP — a single persistent BF16 sign-bit fault raises Llama-3 8B PPL by >4 orders of magnitude?
  3. How do modeled FIT/MTTF compare with measured soft-error rates on real 7 nm SRAM, especially the cross-tier miscorrection dominating the ~900-yr point?
  4. Does the near-vertical transition (and Xsafe stability) hold for non-IEEE formats — MX block floating-point, shared-exponent?
  5. Energy/area optimum when composing UEP with SERA-Float-style lossy compression — does the combined scheme change the conservative floor?
  6. Are MCU results robust to non-contiguous (row/column, clustered) faults from scaled DRAM/HBM or advanced SRAM nodes?
