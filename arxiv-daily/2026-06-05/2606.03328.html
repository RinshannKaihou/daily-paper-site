Why This Matters
Production pruned models are routinely calibrated on C4. Teams shipping coding assistants or math-capable models at 50–60% sparsity are unknowingly destroying those capabilities while reporting acceptable averaged accuracy scores.
Critical Edge
IGSP (the self-generation fallback) still trails real multi-source data by 9.7 points — LLM-generated math/code lacks structural fidelity (executability, proof completeness) that perplexity filtering cannot capture.
Methodology

The paper defines a four-dimensional capability retention score: General, Commonsense, Code, Math. Each dimension is the mean of normalized task scores (pruned / base model), enabling apples-to-apples comparison across sparsities and models.

Spearman correlation is computed across n=15 calibration datasets, revealing the opposite-sign trade-off: high-PPL corpora boost General (ρ=+0.71) but collapse Code/Math (ρ=−0.59/−0.53).

IGSP automates multi-source calibration: capability-stratified sampling pools are built from the evaluation taxonomy, then a greedy 4-gram diversity maximization selects 128 sequences with PPL bandwidth constraints per dimension.

Wanda saturation: At 60% sparsity, all Wanda calibration variants cluster within a 3.8-point window vs. SparseGPT's 18.8-point spread. Wanda's local activation-magnitude scoring saturates when the top-40% magnitudes cleanly separate from discarded 60% — calibration precision stops mattering.

Strengths / Limitations

Strengths

4-dim evaluation framework reveals hidden failures Mechanism: Hessian-global vs. local activation scoring 8 random seeds, 15 calibration datasets compared Practical guidance: when to use mix vs. IGSP

Limitations

n=15 datasets — Spearman power is limited IGSP 9.7-pt gap vs. real multi-source data English-only benchmarks No encoder-decoder or MoE coverage Unstructured sparsity only — no joint pruning+quantization
Open Questions
Q1 Can unit test pass rates or formal proof validators replace PPL bandwidth constraints in IGSP to capture code/math structural fidelity that perplexity filtering cannot reach?
Q2 Is there a critical sparsity threshold below which Wanda's calibration insensitivity breaks, making data selection matter again?
Q3 Does the opposite-sign trade-off (high-PPL → General; low-PPL → Code/Math) hold in encoder-decoder (T5/BART) or MoE (Mixtral) architectures where Hessian update structure differs?
Q4 Should reasoning, factual recall, and long-context understanding be separate calibration dimensions? Do they compete with the existing four, or are they subsumed?
