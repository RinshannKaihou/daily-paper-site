Supervising the student at the hidden-state level fully closes the student-teacher gap that every output-space distillation method leaves open.

Paper OPRD: On-Policy Representation Distillation
Authors Shenzhi Yang, Guangcheng Zhu, Bowen Song et al. Zhejiang University & Ant Group
Tags

Why This Matters
On-policy distillation drives production LLMs (Qwen3, GLM-5, Mimo). A Pareto-dominant drop-in replacement with rigorous theory is immediately deployable — no architectural changes required.
Critical Edge
The same-architecture hard constraint rules out cross-size distillation — the dominant industrial compression use case. All results are single-domain (math) at 1.5B scale only.
Theoretical Foundations
Theorem 4
Zero Variance
OPRD gradient variance is exactly zero conditioned on the rollout — a deterministic function of θ, not a stochastic estimator.
Theorem 5
SNR Collapse
OPD signal-to-noise ratio → 0 as student approaches teacher; OPRD SNR = +∞ until convergence is complete.
Theorem 6
Null-Direction Identity
Any hidden-state deviation in the LM head's null space produces exactly zero output-space loss — invisible to all OPD variants.
Theorem 7
Spectral Gap
Hidden-state deviations along low-singular-value directions can be 10⁶–10⁸× larger than along high-singular-value directions for the same output-space loss budget.
Methodology OPRD
Setting
Frozen teacher JustRL-1.5B + trainable student R1-distill-1.5B, both Qwen2.5-1.5B backbone (28 layers, d=1536, |V|≈151K). Student samples responses on-policy; both models evaluate on the same student-generated prefixes.
OPD Baseline Family (output space)
Three variants — sampled-token (single-sample REINFORCE, O(BT) memory), full-vocabulary KL (infeasible at scale), top-k (reduced memory, retains REINFORCE variance). All supervise next-token distributions, never hidden states.
OPRD Objective
Layer-averaged, position-masked MSE directly on intermediate hidden states. Bypasses the LM head entirely. Supervises only the last k=2000 response tokens — where the student-teacher gap concentrates (cosine similarity ≥97% in early tokens). Composable: ℒ = ℒ_OPD + μ · ℒ_OPRD.
Training Setup
500 AdamW steps · peak lr 1×10⁻⁵ · cosine decay · bf16 · FSDP on 8×A100 (80G) · batch B=8 · max response T=16,384 tokens · prompts from DAPO-Math-17K.
Cross-Architecture
When d_s ≠ d_T, a learned linear projector W ∈ ℝ^(d_T × d_s) maps student representations to teacher space. In reported experiments d_s = d_T = 1536 so W is omitted — this constraint is also OPRD's main limitation.

Strengths
  • Both failure modes (SNR collapse, LM-head null space) formalized with proofs — not heuristically argued
  • Supervision signal (layers × positions × hidden-dim) is orders of magnitude richer than 1 scalar per sampled-token OPD position
  • Strict Pareto dominance: accuracy, training time, and peak memory all improve simultaneously
  • Drop-in composability — no architectural changes, zero added rollout cost
  • Last-k position heuristic is empirically validated (Figure 7: gap concentrates in tail tokens)
  • Code is public; models are on Hugging Face; training configs fully reported
Limitations
  • Same-architecture hard constraint — rules out cross-size distillation (the dominant industrial compression scenario)
  • Single domain (competition math) and single scale (1.5B) — generalization entirely unverified
  • No comparison to non-OPD baselines (GRPO, PPO, SFT) — hard to position in the broader post-training landscape
  • Teacher LM head still computed despite OPRD never using it — efficiency gains are conservative lower bounds
  • Phase transition mechanism "under active investigation" — a genuine theoretical gap
  • No ablation on projector design W for the cross-architecture setting
Credibility
  • 7 theorems with formal proofs in appendix under stated mild assumptions
  • JustRL-1.5B teacher is from an independent group — not self-serving
  • AIME/AIMO evaluation metric (Avg@16 at T=0.7, boxed-answer parser) is standard and reproducible
  • Code public: github.com/ShenzhiYang2000/OPRD
Status
Preprint · "Work in progress" · Not peer-reviewed. Some analysis sections explicitly flagged as under active investigation.
Questions for Further Exploration
Q1
Does OPRD's advantage persist at larger scales (7B, 32B) where vocabulary-induced memory pressure is even more acute?
Q2
Can cross-architecture OPRD be achieved via contrastive objectives — aligning relative geometry rather than absolute vectors — to remove the same-architecture constraint?
Q3
What is the causal mechanism of the PG-loss phase transition OPRD induces? Residual stream reorganization, mode bifurcation, or representation geometry shift?
Q4
Does OPRD generalize beyond chain-of-thought math — e.g. code generation, where the "tail tokens matter most" hypothesis may not hold?
Q5
Would extending OPRD to attention-map distillation (aligning routing patterns, not just hidden states) further close the teacher gap?
Q6
In an RLHF setting with a reward model (not a fixed teacher), does representation alignment help or hurt reward-driven exploration?
Q7
Can adaptive layer and position selection — weighted by the magnitude of the student-teacher gap — outperform the uniform layer + last-k heuristic?
Practical
What is the minimum hidden-dimension overlap required for OPRD to remain viable with a linear projector W — and does projector training cost offset efficiency gains?
