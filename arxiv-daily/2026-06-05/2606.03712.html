When Graph Tokens Sink: A Mechanistic Analysis of Graph Language Models

Zhang · Zhou · Zheng · Fathony · Bruss · Agarwal  —  UVA / Capital One

Graph LMs develop activation-prominent sink tokens that carry no graph-structural information — prominence and utility are fundamentally decoupled.

Key Metric dim 1512 Hidden dimension that dominates activation-level outliers across both LLaGA and TEA-GLM architectures and all three datasets — a cross-architecture constant.
Why This Matters Deleting the most "prominent" graph tokens hurts performance less than deleting random ones. Saliency-based evaluation and pruning of GLMs is fundamentally misleading. Highlights architectural flaw: graph topology is not reliably encoded in token representations.
Critical Edge Only two GLM architectures studied. Paper diagnoses but proposes no fix. Some intervention effect sizes are small. No causal mechanism for why sink dimensions arise. Generalization to other GLM families (GraphGPT, InstructGLM) untested.
Methodology

Five-Lens Analysis Framework

Models: LLaGA (node-aligned token sequences, ND template) and TEA-GLM (GNN encoder + K=5 learnable tokens + contrastive alignment), both on LLaMA2-7B backbone. Datasets: Cora, ArXiv, PubMed. Tasks: node classification + link prediction.

Graph sink token criterion: φ(x) = max over sink dims |RMSNorm(x)_d| ≥ τ=15.0

  • Activation analysis: identify dominant sink dimensions and position bias.
  • Attention analysis: query-to-graph attention weights per head and layer.
  • Interventions: Top-2 Sink pruning vs. Non-Sink pruning vs. Swap.
  • Logit lens: decode graph-token hidden states via LM head at each layer.
  • Post-pruning detection: does sink behavior re-emerge elsewhere?
Sink Token Anatomy

What Sink Tokens Are — and Aren't

Position bias Early positions LLaGA: [PAD] tokens. TEA-GLM: positions 0 and 1 dominate.
Attention received Not dominant TEA-GLM attention peaks at positions 2–4, not the sink positions 0–1.
Logit lens decode "paper" Stable from layer ~20 onward. Domain-generic, not node labels or topology.
Re-emergence after pruning LLaGA: spreads; TEA-GLM: disappears Different architectural sources of the same phenomenon.

Strengths & Limitations

Honest Assessment

  • First systematic study of graph sink tokens; extends attention-sink literature to graph modality.
  • Four-dimensional cross-validation: activation + attention + intervention + logit lens.
  • Dimension 1512 consistent across both architectures — raises architectural universality question.
  • Directly actionable: warns against saliency-based graph-token pruning and selection.

Limits: Only LLaGA and TEA-GLM studied. Pure diagnostic work — no proposed fix. Some effect sizes small on PubMed. Logit lens only applied to TEA-GLM. τ=15.0 threshold not ablated. Causal mechanism unexplained.

Questions for Further Exploration
  • Is dimension 1512 inherited from LLaMA or actively induced by graph token construction? A controlled experiment with a different backbone would answer this.
  • Can explicit topological position encoding or stronger graph-text contrastive alignment eliminate the sink-semantic decoupling?
  • Does the same dissociation appear in few-shot or fully fine-tuned GLMs, or is it specific to zero-shot/frozen-LLM paradigms?
  • Can sink-token positions guide graph-token sequence compression or distillation with minimal performance impact?
  • Is there a "well-behaved" GLM architecture (positive control) where internal activation saliency and graph semantic utility do align?
