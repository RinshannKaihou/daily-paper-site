> **一句话总结：** 知识图谱嵌入模型（KGEMs）在聚合指标（MRR）上表现稳定，但随机种子的微小变化便会导致个体预测和嵌入空间的剧烈分歧，现有评估协议严重掩盖了这一系统性不稳定性。

> **TL;DR:** Knowledge graph embedding models look stable on aggregate metrics like MRR, but even a single changed random seed causes dramatically different top-K predictions and embedding neighborhoods; current benchmarking protocols systematically hide this instability, which matters whenever KG completions are used for decision-making.

---

## 核心贡献

1. **系统性稳定性分析框架**：首次对多种KGEM模型（TransE、RotatE、DistMult、ComplEx、ConvE、RGCN、Transformer）在多个数据集（WN18RR、FB15k-237、CoDEx-S）上进行跨随机因素的系统性稳定性测量。
2. **新评估指标**：提出 *Pred-Overlap@K*（预测重叠率）和 *Space-Overlap@K*（嵌入空间邻域重叠率）两类指标，以及 *Consistency@K* 和 *Homogeneity@K*，全面量化预测一致性和嵌入空间稳定性。
3. **随机性来源隔离实验**：将随机性分解为四个独立来源——参数初始化（I）、三元组排序（O）、负采样（N）、Dropout（D），逐一验证每个来源均足以单独引发可比量级的不稳定性。
4. **硬件作为隐性随机源**：首次将GPU类型列为额外随机来源，实验证明不同GPU型号产生的不稳定性与种子变化量级相当，对云计算环境具有重要警示意义。
5. **投票机制有限性揭示**：验证了集成投票（Borda Vote、Range Vote）虽能部分缓解预测层面的不稳定性，但对嵌入空间稳定性无效，且引入额外计算开销。

---

## Key Contributions

1. **Systematic stability audit**: First large-scale controlled study of KGEM instability across 7 model architectures, 3 benchmark datasets, and 4 isolated sources of randomness.
2. **New stability metrics**: Pred-Overlap@K measures how consistently two model instances would insert the same triples into a KG; Space-Overlap@K measures neighborhood agreement in the learned embedding space — both are independent of whether the gold entity appears in top-K.
3. **Randomness decomposition**: Each of initialization, triple ordering, negative sampling, and dropout is individually sufficient to induce instability of comparable magnitude; no single source dominates.
4. **GPU as a hidden random source**: Hardware variation (across 5 GPU models) causes instability on par with seed variation — a practical concern for cloud or shared-compute environments.
5. **Voting only partially helps**: Ensemble voting improves prediction stability but cannot address embedding-space instability, leaving downstream tasks (entity similarity, clustering, rule mining) unreliable.

## Methodology

### Problem Setup

- A **knowledge graph** is a set of triples (h, r, t) encoding facts. Link prediction (LP) asks models to complete missing triples.
- KGEMs are trained with stochastic components: negative sampling (N), triple ordering (O), parameter initialization (I), and dropout (D). Together these form a **seed configuration** S = {s_N, s_O, s_I, s_D}.
- A **comparison group** G fixes a hyperparameter configuration C and varies one or more seeds, then measures how consistent predictions and embeddings are across the group.

### Stability Metrics

**Pred-Overlap@K(I₁, I₂)** — for two model instances I₁ and I₂, averaged over all test queries q:

```
Pred-Overlap@K = (1/|Q_test|) * Σ_q |ν(I₁,q,K) ∩ ν(I₂,q,K)| / K
```

A value of 1.0 means both instances predict the exact same top-K candidates for every query; 0 means completely disjoint predictions.

**Space-Overlap@K(I₁, I₂)** — for each entity e, compares its K nearest neighbors (by L₂ distance) in the two embedding spaces E₁ and E₂:

```
Space-Overlap@K = (1/|E|) * Σ_e |N_K^E₁(e) ∩ N_K^E₂(e)| / K
```

**Consistency@K(q)** and **Homogeneity@K(q)** generalize overlap beyond pairwise comparisons to all n instances simultaneously, the latter additionally penalizing dispersion in non-shared predictions.

### Experimental Design

- **Models**: TransE, RotatE (geometric); DistMult, ComplEx (tensor factorization); ConvE (CNN); RGCN (GNN); Transformer (attention-based, no neighborhood).
- **Datasets**: WN18RR (40,943 entities, 11 relations), FB15k-237 (14,541 entities, 237 relations), CoDEx-S (2,034 entities, 42 relations).
- **Seed values**: S = {42, 283, 358, 698, 887} — 5 runs per configuration.
- **Hardware variation**: 5 different Nvidia GPU models (RTX 2080 Ti, GTX 1080 Ti, Tesla T4, A40, A100).
- **Hyperparameter search**: Embedding dimensions {128, 256, 512}, learning rates {10⁻⁶ … 10⁻¹}; best (C_B), median (C_M), and worst (C_W) configurations selected for RQ3.
- Custom minimal KGE library built on PyTorch to allow fine-grained independent control over all four randomness sources (PyKEEN and LibKGE do not separate them). Code at https://github.com/Wimmics/LinkPerdition.

## Results & Highlights

### RQ1: Are KGEMs stable across seeds?

- **Aggregate metrics are deceptive**: MRR standard deviations across 5 runs are consistently tiny (e.g., TransE on WN18RR: 0.194 ± 0.002), suggesting stability.
- **Individual predictions are highly variable**: Pred-Overlap@1 is at or below 0.6 for most (model, dataset) pairs, meaning roughly 40% of top-1 predicted triples differ across runs.
- **Extreme case — RotatE on FB15k-237**: Two instances share on average only 4.3 of their top-10 predicted entities per query (Pred-Overlap@10 ≈ 0.43).
- **Embedding space is also unstable**: DistMult instances on FB15k-237 share on average only 5.6 of 10 nearest neighbors per entity (Space-Overlap@10 ≈ 0.56).
- **Near-25% of RotatE queries** on FB15k-237 have Consistency@10 = 0, meaning no entity appears in the top-10 across all 5 runs.
- **TransE is a notable exception**: It is more stable than all other models, with Overlap@1 consistently 0.8–1.0 and average Overlap@10 of ~8 shared entities.

### RQ2: Which sources of randomness are responsible?

- Each of the four sources (N, O, I, D) independently induces instability of **comparable magnitude** — no single source dominates.
- Instability patterns are more strongly tied to **model architecture** than to the specific randomness source.
- Hardware (GPU type) produces instability of the **same order** as seed variation, making reproducibility a serious concern in cloud environments.

### RQ3: Does higher MRR mean more stability?

- Worst-performing configurations (C_W) are expectedly the most unstable.
- **Counterintuitively**, no clear relationship exists between MRR and stability for best (C_B) vs. median (C_M) configurations — higher performance does not guarantee greater stability.
- Stability is largely **architecture-determined**, not performance-determined.

### Voting as a Remedy

- Borda Vote and Range Vote improve Pred-Overlap@10 across all models.
- However, instability is **reduced but not eliminated**, and voting cannot address embedding-space instability (Space-Overlap), leaving downstream tasks that rely on the embedding space unaddressed.

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a genuinely important point: the field's reliance on aggregate metrics like MRR creates a false impression of KGEM robustness. The core finding — that models producing the same MRR can insert almost entirely different triples into a completed KG — is practically significant and under-discussed.

The **proposed metrics (Pred-Overlap, Space-Overlap)** are well-motivated and operationally grounded. They directly measure what matters for KG completion use-cases (what triples get added), rather than just whether the gold answer is ranked highly.

The **hardware-as-randomness** finding is an important practical contribution that most reproducibility analyses overlook.

The work is somewhat limited in scope in that it does not propose a solution beyond noting that TransE is more stable and that voting helps partially. The paper frames this as raising concerns and motivating future work (stability-aware losses, architecture design for stability), which is an honest framing for a diagnostic study.

---

## Strengths

- **Rigorous controlled experimental design**: randomness sources are isolated independently — a methodological contribution in itself, requiring a custom training library since existing frameworks (PyKEEN, LibKGE) bundle all stochastic factors into a single seed.
- **Metrics go beyond gold-truth-centric evaluation**: Pred-Overlap and Space-Overlap capture disagreement even when both runs correctly rank the gold entity within top-K — a blind spot of prior work.
- **Hardware variation as a novel finding**: Demonstrating GPU-type-induced instability of comparable magnitude to seed variation is practically important and rarely studied.
- **Consistent finding across architectures and datasets**: The core instability result holds across all 7 model families and 3 datasets, lending it generalizability.
- **Full reproducibility**: Code, datasets, hyperparameter details, and GPU information are all released.

---

## Limitations

- **No proposed fix**: The paper is a diagnostic study. It documents the problem comprehensively but does not provide a principled algorithmic solution (stability-aware training, regularization, etc.). Future directions are mentioned but not developed.
- **Voting analysis is superficial**: Only Borda and Range Vote are evaluated. Other ensemble strategies or uncertainty-aware approaches are not explored.
- **TransE stability unexplained**: TransE is consistently more stable than other architectures, but the paper does not deeply analyze why — understanding this could guide stable architecture design.
- **Limited to standard benchmarks**: WN18RR, FB15k-237, and CoDEx-S are the most commonly used but relatively small KG benchmarks. Stability behavior on industrial-scale KGs (millions of entities) is unknown.
- **No user study or downstream task evaluation**: The claim that instability "undermines trustworthiness" for decision-making (e.g., drug discovery) is argued by analogy but not validated on a real downstream task.
- **Static hyperparameter configurations**: The hyperparameter search space is compact ({128,256,512} dimensions, 5 learning rates), which may not reflect the full diversity of configurations practitioners use.

---

## Relevance

**High relevance for**: researchers using KGEMs for KG completion, entity alignment, or downstream reasoning tasks; practitioners deploying KG-based decision support systems (biomedical, legal, recommendation); anyone working on reproducibility and evaluation methodology in graph machine learning.

**Connection to broader themes**: This paper is part of a growing body of work questioning whether ML evaluation metrics faithfully reflect operational reliability — similar to debates about accuracy vs. fairness, or aggregate vs. per-instance performance. For KGs specifically, the embedding space instability finding has direct implications for any application that reuses learned embeddings (entity similarity search, clustering, logical rule mining).

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Why is TransE more stable?** Is it the geometric simplicity of its scoring function (translation), the lower expressive power, or something about the loss landscape that makes it less sensitive to initialization?

2. **Can stability be explicitly optimized during training?** Could a stability-aware regularization term (e.g., penalizing variance of top-K predictions across dropout masks within a mini-batch) improve stability without sacrificing MRR?

3. **Does instability scale with KG size?** Larger, denser KGs might exhibit more stable training dynamics due to more redundant signal, or alternatively more instability due to higher-dimensional embedding spaces.

4. **What is the downstream impact?** If two instances with the same MRR produce different completed KGs, how different are the answers to downstream queries (e.g., drug-disease association discovery, entity alignment)? Quantifying this would sharpen the practical stakes.

5. **Are instability patterns correlated with query difficulty?** The near-25% of RotatE queries with Consistency@10 = 0 suggests some queries are systematically harder to stabilize — are these structurally identifiable (e.g., queries involving low-degree entities or rare relations)?

6. **How does this interact with data augmentation or self-training?** If an unstable model is used to generate pseudo-labels for iterative training, does instability compound or average out over iterations?
