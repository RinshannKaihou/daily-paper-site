Key Finding

Step-wise off-policy distillation of principle-level experience is the stable recipe that prevents self-evolving LLM agents from degrading across iterations.

Rethinking Continual Experience Internalization for Self-Evolving LLM Agents ↗
Jingwen Chen · Wenkai Yang · Shengda Fan · Wenbo Nie · Chenxing Sun · Shaodong Zheng · Yangen Hu · Lu Pan · Ke Zeng · Yankai Lin  —  RUC · Beihang · Meituan
continual-learning LLM-agents context-distillation self-evolution web-reasoning
Key Metric — on-policy collapse
14.6%
WebWalkerQA accuracy after 3 iterations of on-policy distillation — down from 22.8% at start. The proposed recipe reaches 32.1% by iteration 2.
Why This Matters
3-part recipe for stable self-improvement
Any autonomous AI system that learns from its own trajectories risks compounding collapse. This paper delivers a directly actionable engineering fix for long-running agent training loops.
Critical Edge
All results on web-reasoning only. Generalization to coding, math, or non-tool-use domains is entirely unverified.
BrowseComp-ZH gains are modest and sometimes inconsistent; on-policy Qwen3-8B collapses to 1.4% in Iter 2–3 before partially recovering.
Methodology
01
Diagnose the failure Iterative on-policy context-distillation causes progressive capability collapse — not compound improvement. The student diverges from teacher-reachable states; reverse-KL loss cannot correct this drift.
02
Principle-level experience granularity Replace trajectory-specific (URL, entity, query-string) summaries with transferable decision rules and failure patterns. 84% of principle-level items contain reusable strategies vs. only 3.7% of instance-level items.
03
Step-wise injection An LLM-based selector Rφ picks experience relevant to the current interaction state at each step. Eliminates premature-answer failure: global injection triggers 63.82% early termination; step-wise reaches 0%.
04
Off-policy context-distillation Train the student on teacher-generated trajectories (forward KL, rejection-sampling for successes only). Avg. teacher trajectory: 4.5 assistant turns vs. 21.9 for on-policy student — compact and coherent.
Results

Qwen3-4B across 3 self-evolution iterations on three benchmarks. Dashes denote baseline (iteration 0).

Off-policy ★ — — stable

Combined recipe (principle + step-wise + off-policy) on WebWalkerQA: base 18.5% → 29.0% → 32.1% → 31.5%. In-context ability also preserved.

Strengths
  • + Concrete failure mode (capability collapse) identified with strong empirical evidence across 2 model sizes and 3 benchmarks.
  • + Clean 3-dimension framework with independent ablations — each component contributes measurably.
  • + Mechanistic explanations: premature-answer rate (Table 2), trajectory length (Table 3), in-context vs. internalized gap quantified.
  • + Full code and data release on GitHub; reproducible setup with clearly specified hyperparameters.
  • + Off-policy recipe is compute-efficient — shorter teacher trajectories, rejection sampling removes failures before training.
Limitations
  • ! Experiments limited to web-reasoning (WebWalkerQA, GAIA, BrowseComp-ZH). No coding, math, or dialogue evidence.
  • ! Experience pool size, selector quality, and trajectory filtering thresholds not ablated — acknowledged as future work.
  • ! Recipe requires a strong, coherent teacher; performance under weaker or noisier experience pools is unknown.
  • ! Safety risk: stable internalization of biased or flawed experience could compound errors — requires human trajectory auditing.
Open Questions
Q1 How sensitive is the recipe to experience pool quality — what happens when the base model generates largely incorrect trajectories in early iterations?
Q2 Can principle-level experience extraction be automated more tightly (e.g., via structured ontologies or learned summarization) rather than relying on DeepSeek-V4?
Q3 How does this compare to parameter-efficient alternatives (LoRA-based continual fine-tuning with replay) in stability and compute cost across iterations?
Q4 Does step-wise selector quality degrade as the experience pool grows and becomes more heterogeneous across iterations?
Q5 Would findings transfer to reasoning-heavy tasks (math olympiad, code synthesis) where the action space is more structured and trajectories shorter?
