> **一句话总结：** 教师模型"何时更新"比"更新多少次"更重要——本文证明"隔离期"（完全冻结教师的间隔）才是自蒸馏稳定学习的关键结构属性，并提出 CGTR 方法，通过三重门控条件自适应触发教师刷新，在四个任务上实现零崩溃的最优性能。

> **TL;DR:** The *when* of teacher updates matters more than the *how often*. This paper establishes that complete isolation periods — intervals where the teacher is fully frozen — are the structural key to stable self on-policy distillation, and introduces CGTR (Consolidation-Gated Teacher Refresh), which gates each teacher refresh on three observable conditions (isolation, reward improvement, length-tail safety) to achieve zero collapse and best final scores across four tasks with a single shared parameter set.

---

## 核心贡献

1. **统一诊断框架：** 引入时序 KL 结构、刷新冲击（refresh shock）和长尾风险三个诊断指标，系统分析不同教师更新策略（硬刷新 M 步、EMA、固定教师）的失败模式。

2. **发现"状态盲崩溃"（State-Oblivious Collapse）：** 固定间隔的硬刷新在长时训练中存在一种隐藏的失败模式：若刷新恰好发生在学生策略的瞬态漂移峰值时，会将腐化的快照不可逆地复制进教师，而该失败在短时评估中完全不可见。

3. **确立"隔离期"的关键地位：** 通过 100 步调度扫描实验（M ∈ {1,2,3,4,5,8,10,20,50}，EMA，Fixed），证明使学习稳定的关键结构属性是"完全冻结教师的间隔"，而非教师的平均年龄（teacher age）。

4. **提出 CGTR：** 三重门控自适应调度方案——隔离门（≥ M_min 步）+ 奖励门（滚动窗口奖励超过历史最高刷新基准 + δ_r）+ 长尾门（序列长度尾比 < L_max）。单一参数集，无需按任务调参，在化学、生物、物理、工具使用四项任务全部取得最优最终分数和最高峰值分数，崩溃次数为零。

## Key Contributions

1. **Diagnostic framework:** Introduces temporal KL structure, refresh shock, and length-tail risk as three complementary metrics to characterize healthy vs. collapsing training dynamics across all canonical teacher update schedules.

2. **State-oblivious collapse (new failure mode):** Identifies and names a long-horizon failure unique to fixed-interval hard refresh: an ill-timed copy during a transient entropy spike irreversibly corrupts the teacher in a single step; the student then re-distills the corrupted state, and there is no recovery path. This failure is mechanistically distinct from EMA's chronic contamination and invisible under short-horizon evaluation.

3. **Isolation period, not teacher age, is the key:** A 100-step schedule sweep across M ∈ {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 8, 10, 20, 50}, EMA, and Fixed Teacher on Qwen3-8B / SciKnowEval Chemistry establishes that the complete teacher freeze between updates — not how old the teacher is — determines stability and performance.

4. **CGTR algorithm:** A three-gate adaptive rule that preserves isolation (IsolationGate: t − t_last ≥ M_min) while conditioning each refresh on observable evidence that the student has genuinely consolidated (RewardGate: rolling-window reward improvement by δ_r) and that generation is behaviorally stable (LengthTailGate: seqlen max/mean < L_max). CGTR fires only 3 refreshes over 300 Chemistry steps yet achieves the best final score (0.806) with zero collapse.

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** Self on-policy distillation trains a student policy π_θ using a combined objective:

L_t(θ) = −E[r(x)] + λ · KL(π_θ ‖ π_φ)

where π_φ is the self-teacher derived from the student's own parameter history. The core question is: how should the temporal coupling between φ and θ be governed?

**Three canonical schedules analyzed:**
- *Periodic hard refresh (M steps):* φ_t = θ_t if t mod M = 0, else φ_{t-1}. Creates isolation periods but is state-blind.
- *EMA:* φ_t = (1−α)φ_{t-1} + αθ_t. No isolation period; contamination bounded per step but accumulates unboundedly.
- *Fixed teacher:* φ never updated. Has isolation but loses relevance as student improves.

**Diagnostic metrics:**
- *KL temporal structure:* A corrective refresh produces a transient KL spike (student re-adapts to new anchor); a contaminating refresh shows no spike but is followed by entropy growth.
- *Refresh shock:* ‖θ_t − φ_{t-1}‖ — the magnitude of the jump characterizes whether the update is corrective or contaminating.
- *Length-tail risk:* max(seqlen) / mean(seqlen) as a leading collapse indicator, rising 35 steps before mean entropy or reward show any signal.

**CGTR gate definitions (Eq. 4–7):**
- IsolationGate: t − t_last ≥ M_min (= 30 steps)
- RewardGate: r̄_{t−w:t} > r_ref + δ_r, where r_ref is a ratchet updated at each refresh and δ_r = 0.05
- LengthTailGate: max(seqlen_{t−w:t}) < L_max (= 3.2 × mean), w = 5 steps

All three must simultaneously pass for the teacher to move. Parameters were fixed from 100-step Chemistry diagnostics only; cross-task runs were held-out evaluations.

**Training setup.** Qwen3-8B (pretrained, no instruction tuning), SciKnowEval (Chemistry 1890/210, Biology 450/50, Physics 720/80) and ToolAlpaca (3544/394). 4×A100 GPUs, FSDP, global batch 16 (n=4 rollouts/question), AdamW lr 1e-5, GRPO-style policy gradient, top-k=20 JSD distillation loss with importance-sampling correction.

## Results & Highlights

**Schedule sweep (100 steps, Chemistry):**

| Schedule | Test m@16 | B@4  | Ent. mean | Collapse |
|----------|-----------|------|-----------|----------|
| M=1      | N/A       | N/A  | ~1.05     | step 10  |
| M=2      | 0.225     | 0.276| 0.900     | step 86  |
| M=50     | **0.755** | **0.827** | **0.499** | none |
| EMA α=0.04 | 0.713  | 0.782| 0.727     | none     |
| CGTR     | **0.806** | **0.809** | **0.654** | none |

**Long-horizon comparison (300 steps, Chemistry):**
- M=50 collapses catastrophically at step 189 (test mean@16 drops to 0.000, irrecoverable).
- EMA α=0.04 plateaus at 0.713 with chronic entropy drift (0.7 → 3.0 by step 300).
- CGTR achieves 0.806 final, 0.809 peak, entropy mean 0.654, zero collapse, only 3 refreshes (steps 45, 85, 130). After the third refresh the teacher stays frozen for the remaining 170 steps; accuracy improves from 0.752 to 0.806 (+0.054) during this extended isolation.

**Cross-task generalization (300 steps, single parameter set):**

| Task      | M=50 fin | EMA-04 fin | CGTR fin |
|-----------|----------|------------|----------|
| Chemistry | 0.000    | 0.713      | **0.806**|
| Biology   | 0.000    | 0.471      | **0.485**|
| Physics   | 0.670    | 0.660      | **0.703**|
| ToolUse   | 0.467    | 0.481      | **0.536**|

M=50 collapses in Chemistry and Biology. CGTR self-regulates: 3 refreshes in Chemistry, 2 in Biology, 1 in Physics, 2 in ToolUse.

**Gate ablation (Chemistry, 300 steps):**
- Without RewardGate: final 0.000 (collapse at step 218 — ratchet is essential).
- Without IsolationGate: final 0.731 (12 refreshes instead of 3; chronic contamination during reward plateaus).
- Without LengthTailGate: final 0.771 (one premature refresh at step 127; tail ratio exceeds L_max before reward degrades).

## Critical Assessment

This paper addresses a genuinely under-studied question: not "what teacher signal to use" but "when should the teacher be updated." The framing is clean, the diagnostic framework is novel, and the identification of state-oblivious collapse as a long-horizon failure mode that is invisible under short-horizon evaluation is an important and potentially widely-applicable finding.

The proposed CGTR is mechanistically simple (three observable thresholds, no learned components), which is a strength — it can be applied without additional model inference. The ratchet design of the reward gate is elegant: each successful refresh raises the bar for the next one, ensuring monotone teacher quality.

The main weaknesses are scope and the binary reward signal assumption. All experiments use a single model family (Qwen3-8B) and a single reward type (binary correctness). It is unclear how CGTR's gate dynamics would change under dense or learned reward signals, or at larger model scales. The tasks are also all relatively short-horizon reasoning/tool-use; whether the same dynamics hold for code generation, dialogue, or RLHF settings remains open.

---

## Strengths

- **Novel failure mode identification:** State-oblivious collapse is mechanistically distinct from EMA's chronic contamination and fills a gap in the self-distillation stability literature.
- **Principled diagnostic framework:** Temporal KL structure, refresh shock, and length-tail risk are interpretable, lightweight, and actionable without additional model inference.
- **Strong empirical validation:** 100-step schedule sweep (9 hard-refresh values + EMA variants + Fixed), 300-step long-horizon runs, cross-task held-out evaluation, and full per-gate ablations with consistent results.
- **Zero per-task tuning:** CGTR's parameters are fixed from Chemistry diagnostics only; the cross-task results constitute genuine out-of-distribution evaluation.
- **Conceptual clarity:** The paper reframes the question from "how old should the teacher be?" to "is the student ready for the teacher to move?" — a framing shift with real practical implications.

---

## Limitations

- **Single model family:** All experiments use Qwen3-8B; behavior at 70B+ scale is unknown.
- **Binary reward only:** The reward gate relies on a binary correctness signal. Dense or learned rewards with different noise characteristics may require different δ_r calibration or a different gate design entirely.
- **Moderate training scale:** 300-step training is modest; dynamics at thousands of steps, where curriculum effects and capability jumps may interact differently with teacher scheduling, are untested.
- **Four related task types:** Chemistry, Biology, Physics, ToolUse are all multiple-choice / exact-match tasks with similar reward structure. Generalization to open-ended generation, creative tasks, or RLHF settings is unknown.
- **No theoretical analysis:** The paper is empirically driven; there is no formal characterization of when CGTR is guaranteed to avoid collapse or converge.

---

## Relevance

Highly relevant to anyone working on:
- **Self-distillation / self-improvement of LLMs** (RLVR, GRPO, STaR, CRISP, self-distilled RLVR variants)
- **On-policy training stability** — the state-oblivious collapse finding is a concrete, actionable warning for anyone using fixed-interval teacher updates in long-horizon RL fine-tuning
- **Teacher-student learning dynamics** — the isolation period principle generalizes beyond LLMs to any setting where a reference distribution is derived from the current model

The key practical takeaway: if you are using periodic hard refresh (copy student to teacher every M steps), a fixed M is unsafe for long-horizon training regardless of how well it performs in short runs. Either use CGTR's three-gate adaptive rule, or at minimum add a reward-ratchet condition before allowing the teacher to update.

---

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Scaling behavior:** Does the optimal M_min scale with model size, dataset size, or task difficulty? Is there a principled way to derive it without a schedule sweep?

2. **Dense reward signals:** How do the gate dynamics change when r_t is a continuous score (e.g., a reward model) rather than binary correctness? Does the noise floor assumption underlying δ_r = 0.05 still hold?

3. **Multi-task / multi-domain training:** When training on a mixture of tasks simultaneously, should each task maintain its own teacher or share one? Would per-task gating be necessary?

4. **Interaction with reasoning length curriculum:** RLVR models often exhibit spontaneous length increases as capabilities improve. Can the LengthTailGate be made to distinguish genuine capability-driven length growth from collapse-precursor tail expansion?

5. **Alternative state-awareness signals:** The paper uses reward improvement and length-tail ratio as consolidation proxies. Could gradient variance, perplexity on held-out data, or KL between consecutive student checkpoints serve as better or complementary signals?
