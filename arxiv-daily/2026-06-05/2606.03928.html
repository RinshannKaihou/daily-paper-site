Key Finding

A tiny fraction of KV value states with abnormally large magnitudes sustain reasoning coherence — evict them and the model loops forever

Value-Aware Stochastic KV Cache Eviction for Reasoning Models ↗
Critical empirical finding
Deliberately evicting the top-magnitude value tokens drops Qwen3-4B GSM8K accuracy from 88% to 14.3% — worse than random eviction (53.2%). The model enters infinite self-reflection loops. Value-state magnitudes are not noise; they are load-bearing structure for reasoning progression.
Key Metric
+4.9%
Avg accuracy gain over best prior eviction method (R-KV) on Qwen3-14B at 4× compression across 6 hard reasoning benchmarks (AIME25, AIME26, HMMT25, GPQA-Diamond, MATH, LiveCodeBench). Matches selection-based SOTA.
Why This Matters
Static-memory eviction now competitive with selection methods
Selection-based KV cache grows with sequence length — impractical for reasoning traces that exceed 10K tokens. VaSE achieves comparable accuracy with bounded memory and 3.1× throughput gain.
Critical Edge
Only Qwen3 tested; mechanistic why unexplained
All experiments on Qwen3-4B and 14B only. Why large-magnitude value states matter mechanistically remains a hypothesis. Stochastic sampling introduces per-run variance.
Methodology

Value-state magnitude scoring. Range(v) = max_j v_j − min_j v_j. Chosen for strong correlation with per-token KV quantization error (avg 0.83–0.92 across bit-widths), grounding the metric empirically.

VASE-AttnV. Given budget K and value reservation budget N_v < K: (1) unconditionally retain the top N_v tokens by Range(v) — reserved set R_V; (2) fill remaining K−|R| slots by sampling from the SnapKV attention distribution (weights ∝ ᾱ_i) rather than hard top-k. Every token has strictly positive retention probability after T steps.

VASE-DKV. Inherits CurDKV's combined key-value score s_i = ‖G^T kᵢ‖² · ‖G^T vᵢ‖² but resamples the Gaussian projection matrix G independently at every eviction step, preventing tokens from being permanently disadvantaged by a single unlucky projection.

Memory and throughput. VASE-DKV: 3.1× throughput over Full KV at 16K tokens / 2048 budget (Qwen3-14B, A100-80G). Peak memory: ~39.6 GB vs. Full OOM at 32K.

GSM8K ablation: value scoring alone +16.2%, stochasticity alone +6.6%, both together: 80.5% vs. 64.3% SnapKV baseline — complementary orthogonal gains.

Quantization implication: Range(v) correlation with quant error (0.83–0.92) → mixed-precision KV caching path identified.

Strengths / Limitations
Strengths
  • Two orthogonal insights (value magnitude + stochasticity) validated independently before combination
  • Training-free, plug-in on SnapKV or CurDKV — minimal engineering overhead
  • Static memory footprint: predictable deployment for long-output reasoning workloads
  • FlashAttention2 compatible — throughput gains are real, not theoretical
  • Magnitude-quantization correlation extends the insight to KV quantization — broader impact
Limitations
  • Only Qwen3-4B and 14B tested — generalization to DeepSeek-R1, Llama reasoning models unverified
  • Why large-magnitude value states matter mechanistically is hypothesized, not explained
  • Stochastic sampling creates per-run variance; pass@1 averaged over 8–16 runs may not reflect single-inference deployment
  • No experiments on prefill-phase compression or combined prefill+decode settings
  • N_v and B hyperparameters tuned on GSM8K and applied cross-task without further calibration
Open Questions
  • Do large-magnitude value states encode specific semantic roles (e.g., reasoning transition tokens like "therefore", "wait") or is the pattern more distributed?
  • Does VASE generalize to non-Qwen reasoning models — is the skewed value-magnitude distribution architecture-specific?
  • Can value-magnitude reservation be combined with KV quantization in a mixed-precision scheme for compounding efficiency gain?
  • Is there a phase transition in N_v (minimum reservation budget), and does it correlate with the number of attention sink tokens?
