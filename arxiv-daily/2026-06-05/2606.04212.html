Edge of Stability selectively amplifies some training examples
while suppressing others — the beneficiary is written in the data geometry.

arxiv.org/abs/2606.04212 ↗
Key Metric
ρ = 0.39
Spearman correlation of centroid-distance with alignment at EoS onset on natural CIFAR-10
Rises from –0.11 during progressive sharpening → confirming geometry predicts beneficiary
Why This Matters
EoS isn't a global bottleneck — it's an implicit data-selection mechanism that advantages atypical examples and can flip adversarial robustness vs. OOD generalization based on which subset dominates Hessian curvature.
Critical Edge
All primary results are on binary CIFAR-10 with a 2-layer MLP under full-batch GD. Extension to mini-batch SGD, adaptive optimizers, and large-scale training is flagged open; robustness/OOD findings are single-seed and preliminary.
Methodology

The core instrument is a branching intervention: two branches share an identical training trajectory up to EoS onset t*. The baseline branch stays at η; the exit branch halves η → η/2, raising the stability threshold and exiting EoS. Divergence in per-group loss thereafter is causal evidence — not correlation.

The training distribution is partitioned into four geometry-defined prototype groups (inliers, boundary points, input-outliers, output-outliers) on a binary CIFAR-10 task. Two counterfactual experiments independently ablate the two necessary conditions.

①
Directional Alignment
cos²θₖ — subset gradient must align with top Hessian eigenvector v₁. Destroyed by random-direction displacement → EoS advantage dissolves.
②
Gradient Persistence
Non-vanishing gradient magnitude throughout training. CE gradient saturation collapses input-outlier influence; output-outliers (wrong label) become sole beneficiary.
Results
Subset EoS Effect (Δℓ) Geometry
Input-outliers BENEFITS High centroid distance, coherent alignment with v₁
Output-outliers BENEFITS Wrong labels → persistent gradients under CE
Inliers SUPPRESSED Near centroid, gradient saturates quickly
Boundary points SUPPRESSED Inter-class boundary, low alignment pre-onset

Changing α from 3→10 transfers the EoS advantage from boundary points back to input-outliers, confirming geometry-governs-beneficiary. Replicates across CNN, ResNet-14, SGD (batch 128), GD with momentum.

Strengths / Limitations

Strengths

Causal branching design Principled counterfactuals Geometry-based prediction verified Bridges implicit-reg + data-centric literatures Open-source, modest GPU budget

Limitations

Binary CIFAR-10 / 2-layer MLP only Full-batch GD — mini-batch theory missing Synthetic outlier construction Robustness results: single seed No Adam or large-scale extension
Open Questions
Q1 Does EoS selectivity survive mini-batch SGD at scale — does a stochastic analog of the self-stabilization mechanism exist?
Q2 Can the EoS beneficiary be engineered by re-weighting the dataset to make a desired subset dominate v₁ at t*?
Q3 Is the curvature-influence score (∇ℓₖ·v₁)² useful as a live training diagnostic to detect systematically disadvantaged subgroups?
Q4 Does strong data augmentation shift the dominant group, explaining some of augmentation's generalization benefits beyond variance reduction?
