How Quantization Changes Interpretable Features: A Sparse Autoencoder Analysis of Language Models

INT6 quantization silently destroys 37–49% of SAE features while perplexity shows no warning

Quantized LLMs that pass perplexity checks are broken for interpretability tools — safety audits built on full-precision SAE features are invalid for deployed compressed models.

Methodology — QDM Pipeline

  1. Frozen SAE basis. SAE trained once on FP16 model, never retrained. Isolates compression effects from SAE training variability — the right way to ask this question.

  2. Models & SAEs. Pythia-70M (layer 4 residual) and Gemma-2-2B (layer 12, Gemma Scope d_sae=16384). 30× parameter range enables cross-scale comparison.

  3. Compression. Round-to-nearest RTN with per-output-channel scaling across b ∈ {8,7,6,5,4}. Attention + MLP projections only — layer-norm and embeddings stay FP16.

  4. Survival metric. Per-feature Pearson correlation cj between FP16 and compressed activations over N identical token positions. Taxonomy: survived >0.9, degraded 0.5–0.9, damaged <0.5.

  5. Fragility predictor. L2-regularized logistic regression from 4 FP16 stats: rarity, log mean activation, log peak activation, log concentration. 5-fold stratified CV.

  6. Pruning comparison. Matched-perplexity magnitude pruning calibrated so PPL(prune) ≈ PPL(INT6). Jaccard and Spearman overlap of non-survived sets.

Results
−5.65% PPL change INT7
vs
−18.7% features degraded
Gemma INT7 dissociation: perplexity improves while nearly 1-in-5 SAE features degrades. Holds under both chunked and sliding-window protocols — not an evaluation artifact.
Fragility is predictable before deployment
AUC 0.924 ± 0.007 (Pythia) and 0.971 ± 0.002 (Gemma) from 4 simple FP16 statistics. Peak activation is strongest: survival rises from 13% → 96% across quartiles (Pythia) and 5% → 92% (Gemma).
Quantization & pruning damage the same features
At matched perplexity, Jaccard overlap of non-survived sets: 0.86 (Pythia), 0.79 (Gemma). Damage-score Spearman: 0.976 / 0.978. Feature vulnerability is driven by signal strength, not compression mechanism.
Degradation is graded, not cliff-like
At INT6, Gemma shows 48.7% degraded and 0.0% damaged — features blur before they break. The degraded band consistently exceeds the damaged band at intermediate bit-widths.
Stability checks are thorough
Survival estimates stable to ±0.71pp across token budgets 50k–200k and 3 independent random subsets. FP16-vs-FP16 null gives exactly 1.000 mean correlation — no pipeline artifact.
Strengths & Limitations
Strengths
+Frozen SAE elegantly separates compression from training variability — the right measurement design
+Perplexity dissociation shown under two independent evaluation protocols — not a measurement artifact
+Fragility predictor is actionable: practitioners can pre-screen features before deployment
+Quantization/pruning Jaccard overlap is theoretically interesting — points toward unified compression vulnerability
+Exemplary null check; extensive stability appendices; honest presentation throughout
Limitations
−RTN only — GPTQ and AWQ (production standard) are excluded; transfer of findings is unknown
−No causal validation: geometric correlation loss ≠ steering/safety intervention failure
−Single SAE per model per layer; other sparsity levels or SAE architectures may differ
−INT7 perplexity improvement is unexplained — "mild regularization" hypothesis unverified
−Two model families only; generalization to other architectures not established
Open Questions
Q1
Causal validation: Does a degraded feature (cj=0.6) actually cause steering vector misfire? What correlation loss threshold is tolerable?
Q2
Production quantizers: Do GPTQ/AWQ substantially improve feature survival? Does the perplexity dissociation persist?
Q3
Mixed precision: Can the fragility predictor assign higher bit-widths to vulnerable features as a deployment strategy?
Q4
INT7 mechanism: Is the perplexity improvement genuine regularization, a corpus artifact, or architecture-specific?
Q5
Layer generalization: How does survival vary across early vs. late layers, attention-dominant vs. MLP-dominant?
Q6
End-to-end demo: Show a concrete steering intervention (refusal, concept suppression) that works FP16 but fails INT6.
