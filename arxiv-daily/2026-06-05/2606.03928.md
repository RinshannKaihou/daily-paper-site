> **一句话总结：** 推理模型的 KV 缓存中存在少量"大幅度 value 状态"，驱逐这些状态会导致模型陷入重复循环；VaSE 通过保护这些状态并引入随机采样来提升缓存多样性，在 4× 压缩比下达到与 selection-based 方法相当的精度，同时保持静态显存占用。

> **TL;DR:** A small fraction of value states in reasoning-model KV caches have abnormally large magnitudes; evicting them causes catastrophic repetition loops. VaSE protects these states and adds stochastic eviction for cache diversity, matching selection-based accuracy at 4× compression with a static memory footprint.

---

## 核心贡献

1. **关键发现一：大幅度 value 状态的重要性。** Value 状态的幅度分布高度偏斜，少量极大值对注意力输出有不成比例的影响。故意驱逐这些状态会使 Qwen3-4B 在 GSM8K 上的精度从 88% 跌至 14.3%（随机驱逐基线为 53.2%），模型陷入无限自我反思循环。

2. **关键发现二：随机性改善缓存多样性。** 将 SnapKV 的确定性 top-k 选择替换为以注意力分数为权重的随机采样，GSM8K 精度从 64.3% 提升至 70.9%（+6.6%）；对 CurDKV 重采样随机投影矩阵从 78.6% 提升至 87.6%（+9%）。

3. **VaSE 方法：** 提出两个变体，均无需训练：
   - **VASE-AttnV**：在 SnapKV 基础上，为大幅度 value 状态保留固定 budget 槽位，其余位置用注意力加权随机采样填充。
   - **VASE-DKV**：在 CurDKV 基础上，每次驱逐步骤重采样随机高斯投影矩阵 G，消除 CurDKV 固定投影导致的系统性偏差。

4. **对 KV 量化的启示：** 大幅度 value 状态与 per-token KV 量化误差高度相关（平均相关系数 0.83–0.92），指向混合精度量化的未来方向。

## Key Contributions

1. **Finding 1 — Large-magnitude value states are critical.** Value-state magnitudes follow a heavily skewed distribution. Deliberately evicting the top-magnitude tokens drops Qwen3-4B's GSM8K accuracy from 88% to 14.3% (vs. 53.2% for random eviction), triggering infinite self-reflection loops.

2. **Finding 2 — Stochasticity improves cache diversity.** Replacing SnapKV's deterministic top-k with attention-weighted sampling raises GSM8K from 64.3% to 70.9%; resampling CurDKV's projection matrix each step raises it from 78.6% to 87.6%.

3. **VaSE method — two training-free variants:**
   - **VASE-AttnV**: Reserves dedicated budget slots for large-Range value states on top of SnapKV; fills remaining slots via attention-weighted stochastic sampling.
   - **VASE-DKV**: Inherits CurDKV's product score but resamples the Gaussian projection matrix G at every eviction step, preventing tokens from being permanently penalized by a single unlucky projection.

4. **Implication for KV quantization:** Range(v) correlates strongly with per-token quantization error (avg. 0.83–0.92 across bit-widths/group sizes), suggesting that protecting large-magnitude values is equally important for quantization-based compression.

## Methodology

**Problem setting.** The paper targets the *decode-phase* KV cache under the periodic-eviction framework: a persistent budget of K tokens plus a recency buffer of B tokens. Every B steps, B tokens are scored and the lowest-importance ones are permanently discarded. The key challenge for reasoning models is that outputs can exceed 10,000 tokens, making static-memory eviction far preferable to selection methods whose memory grows linearly with sequence length.

**Value-state magnitude scoring.** The magnitude of a cached value vector v is measured as:

```
Range(v) = max_j v_j − min_j v_j
```

This is chosen for its connection to per-token quantization error and for its strong empirical correlation with model accuracy.

**VASE-AttnV.** Given budget K and value reservation budget N_v < K:
1. Score all non-buffer tokens by Range(v_i); unconditionally retain the top N_v as reserved set R_V.
2. Fill the remaining K − |R| slots by sampling from the SnapKV attention distribution (weights ∝ ᾱ_i), replacing hard top-k with soft probabilistic selection.
3. Token retention probability after T eviction steps is strictly positive for every token — no token is ever permanently eliminated by a single step.

**VASE-DKV.** Inherits CurDKV's combined key-value score:

```
s_i^CUR = ℓ_i^(K)(G) · ℓ_i^(V)(G),   where  ℓ_i^(V)(G) = ‖G^T v_i‖²
```

At each eviction step t, G_t is resampled independently from N(0, 1/r), so that no token is consistently disadvantaged across steps by a fixed projection.

**Throughput and memory benchmark** (single A100-80G, Qwen3-14B): All eviction methods achieve substantially higher throughput than the full cache. VASE-DKV is fastest (3.1× over Full at 16K tokens, 2048 budget: 411 vs. 133 tokens/sec). Peak GPU memory at 16K / 4096 budget: VASE-DKV ≈ 39.6 GB, VASE-AttnV ≈ 39.6 GB, R-KV ≈ 42.4 GB, Full OOM at 32K.

## Results & Highlights

**Main accuracy results (pass@1, 4× compression, 6 tasks: AIME25, AIME26, HMMT25, GPQA-Diamond, MATH, LiveCodeBench-v6):**

| Method | Family | Qwen3-4B Avg | Qwen3-14B Avg |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full KV | — | 65.04 | 72.30 |
| SeerAttention-R | Selection | 58.81 | 65.37 |
| SnapKV | Eviction | 49.15 | — |
| R-KV | Eviction | 54.69 | 60.90 |
| CurDKV | Eviction | 49.78 | 55.70 |
| **VASE-DKV** | **Eviction** | **57.48** | **64.94** |
| **VASE-AttnV** | **Eviction** | **59.09** | **65.81** |

- VASE-AttnV surpasses the best prior eviction baseline (R-KV) by **+4.4%** on 4B and **+4.9%** on 14B.
- VASE-AttnV matches or slightly exceeds SeerAttention-R (selection-based SOTA) on both model sizes.
- On LiveCodeBench, VASE-AttnV (63.5%) outperforms SeerAttention-R (45.3%), showing that the training-free approach generalizes better across domains.
- On GSM8K (ablation): value scoring alone gives +16.2%, stochasticity alone gives +6.6%, both together reach 80.5% vs. 64.3% baseline — confirming complementarity.
- Performance advantage is largest under aggressive compression (7–8× on AIME/HMMT) and diminishes gracefully as budget increases.

## Critical Assessment

This paper makes a clean empirical contribution backed by a concrete mechanistic hypothesis. The two findings (value-state magnitude and stochasticity) are validated with controlled ablations on GSM8K before being combined into the final method. The approach is genuinely training-free and FlashAttention2-compatible, lowering adoption barriers significantly.

The main weakness is that the *why* large-magnitude value states matter mechanistically remains a hypothesis rather than a proven explanation — the authors acknowledge this as future work. The method is also only tested on Qwen3 models, so generalization to other reasoning model families (e.g., DeepSeek-R1, o3-family architectures) is unknown. Additionally, VASE introduces two hyperparameters (N_v, B) that are tuned on GSM8K and applied to other tasks without further tuning — while this is presented as a strength, task-specific calibration could widen the margin further.

---

## Strengths

- **Two orthogonal, composable insights** (value magnitude + stochasticity) validated independently before being combined.
- **Training-free and plug-in** onto existing eviction frameworks (SnapKV, CurDKV) — minimal engineering overhead.
- **Static memory footprint** unlike selection-based methods, enabling predictable deployment for reasoning workloads.
- **FlashAttention2 compatible**, ensuring practical throughput gains, not just theoretical compression.
- **Generalizes beyond eviction**: the value-magnitude insight extends to KV quantization error, broadening impact.
- Strong baselines and reproducible setup via alignment with SeerAttention-R evaluation scripts.

---

## Limitations

- Experiments confined to Qwen3-4B and Qwen3-14B; generalization to other reasoning architectures is unverified.
- The mechanistic role of large-magnitude value states in sustaining reasoning progression is hypothesized but not explained.
- Stochastic sampling introduces run-to-run variance; pass@1 averaged over 8–16 runs mitigates this but may not reflect single-inference deployment scenarios.
- LiveCodeBench results use a 2048-token budget (~20% full KV), which is a different compression regime than the 4× setting in the main table — cross-task comparison requires care.
- No experiments on prefill-phase compression or combined prefill+decode settings.

---

## Relevance

Directly relevant to anyone working on:
- **Efficient inference for reasoning/long-context LLMs** — addresses the specific memory bottleneck from extended chains of thought.
- **KV cache management** — provides new principled criteria (value magnitude, diversity) beyond attention-score heuristics.
- **KV cache quantization** — the magnitude-quantization-error correlation opens a path to mixed-precision caching strategies.
- **Sparse attention research** — bridges the accuracy gap between eviction and selection families without the memory cost of the latter.

Less relevant to: standard (non-reasoning) LLM inference with short outputs, RAG systems, or training-time efficiency.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **What semantic information do large-magnitude value states encode?** Do they correspond to specific token types (e.g., reasoning transition tokens like "therefore", "wait", "but"), or is the pattern more distributed?

2. **Does VASE generalize to non-Qwen reasoning models** (DeepSeek-R1, Llama-based reasoning models)? The skewed value-magnitude distribution may be architecture-specific.

3. **Can value-magnitude reservation be combined with KV quantization** in a mixed-precision scheme — keep large-Range values at full precision, quantize the rest — for a compounding efficiency gain?

4. **What is the minimum N_v** (value reservation budget) needed before accuracy degrades? Is there a phase transition, and does it correlate with the number of "attention sink" tokens?

5. **Does stochasticity help in non-reasoning (short-output) settings**, or is the diversity benefit specific to long chain-of-thought contexts where the cache must represent a broad reasoning trajectory?

6. **How does VASE interact with speculative decoding** or other inference acceleration techniques that alter the decode-phase token generation pattern?
