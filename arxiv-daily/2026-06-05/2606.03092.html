Key Finding

Treating LLM batch token allocation as a constrained market reveals a Lambert W closed-form policy that gains up to +24 accuracy points over uniform allocation — with zero retraining.

The Shadow Price of Reasoning: Economic Perspective on Optimal Budget Allocation for LLMs
Xu Wan · Speed Zhu · Jianwei Cai · Guang Chen · Ximing Huang · Wiggin Zhou · Mingyang Sun  ·  2026-06-05
ICML 2026 inference-time scaling constrained optimization compute budget LLM efficiency CLEAR
Key Metric
+24pts accuracy
Over uniform allocation on Mostly-Easy stream at 256 tokens/query (Qwen2.5-Math-7B). Gap closes to +1.2 pts at 2048 tokens.
Why This Matters
Principled plug-and-play token allocation maximizes batch reasoning accuracy with no backbone retraining — directly applicable to API-cost-sensitive or edge deployments.
Critical Edge
Limitation Requires knowing the full batch upfront — incompatible with streaming inference. Gains also shrink to <2 pts at generous budgets, narrowing real-world applicability.

01 Methodology
①
Utility Modeling — Shifted-Surge S-curve
Per-query reasoning utility follows a three-phase empirical pattern: near-zero below emergence threshold τi, sharply rising through a "Surge" phase, then plateauing with diminishing returns. Parameterized as φᵢ(t) = αᵢ(t−τᵢ)·exp(−βᵢ(t−τᵢ)) for t > τᵢ.
②
Constrained Optimization & Shadow Price
Maximize Σφᵢ(tᵢ) subject to Σtᵢ ≤ Btotal. The Lagrange multiplier λ is the shadow price of computation: at optimality, every active query's marginal utility must equal λ* (Shadow Price Parity). This is the economic core of CLEAR.
③
Lambert W Closed Form (Theorem 4.2)
Solving ∂φᵢ/∂t = λ yields an exact closed-form: t*ᵢ = τᵢ + (1/βᵢ)[1 − W₀(λe/αᵢ)]. Per-query allocation is O(1) — no numerical iteration per query.
④
Market-Clearing Bisection
Since t*ᵢ(λ) is monotone in λ, aggregate demand C(λ) = Σt*ᵢ(λ) is invertible. CLEAR runs bisection for 40 iterations to find λ* satisfying C(λ*) = Btotal. Overhead is negligible vs. LLM generation.
⑤
Threshold Predictor & Rational Abandonment
DeBERTa-v3-base (86M params) predicts emergence threshold τᵢ per query. Queries where net surplus J(t*ᵢ) ≤ 0 receive zero budget — rational abandonment — concentrating compute on solvable queries.

03 Strengths
✓
Provably optimal allocation policy under shifted-surge utility — not a heuristic.
✓
Lambert W per-query computation is O(1) — negligible inference overhead.
✓
Rational abandonment concentrates tokens on solvable queries rather than diluting uniformly — qualitatively correct insight.
✓
Robust to predictor noise: relies on ranking of τ, not absolute values — self-correcting via market clearing.
✓
Plug-and-play: wraps any reasoning LLM; only DeBERTa-v3-base (86M) is fine-tuned.
✓
Full pseudocode + hyperparameter tables + public code. ICML peer-review validated.
04 Limitations
✗
Batch-only: requires all queries upfront — incompatible with streaming or online inference settings.
✗
Global α, β: utility shape is identical for all queries; only threshold τᵢ varies — coarse approximation of true heterogeneity.
✗
Budget-regime sensitivity: gains shrink rapidly above 1024 tokens/query, limiting applicability to tight-resource regimes.
✗
Predictor domain: threshold predictor trained on GSM8K/MATH; diverse task generalization (science, law) requires retraining.
✗
Greedy decoding only: framework untested with stochastic sampling or beam search.
✗
Oracle gap: CLEAR closes ~50–60% of gap to oracle; predictor noise is the main bottleneck, identified but unsolved.
05 Open Questions
Q.01
Can the shadow price framework extend to online/streaming settings via rolling-window or stochastic approximation of λ*?
Q.02
How does CLEAR interact with speculative decoding or early-exit mechanisms that also reduce per-query compute?
Q.03
Table 3 shows Triangular utility ≈ shifted-surge — does this mean rational abandonment + market clearing alone drives the gain, making a simpler heuristic competitive?
