> **一句话总结：** OPRD 将在线蒸馏的监督目标从 LM head 输出空间迁移至隐状态空间，通过零条件方差的确定性 MSE 梯度取代高方差 REINFORCE 估计量，在 AIME/AIMO 数学推理基准上完全弥合学生-教师差距，同时训练速度提升 1.44× 、峰值显存降低 54%。

> **TL;DR:** Every existing on-policy distillation (OPD) method — sampled-token, top-k, full-vocabulary — supervises the student exclusively in the LM head's *output* space, which has two compounding flaws: (1) the per-position gradient is a single-sample REINFORCE estimator whose signal-to-noise ratio collapses to zero as the student approaches the teacher, causing late-stage stagnation; (2) the LM head projection is an ill-conditioned, additive-invariant map that discards the entire affine null space of intermediate hidden states, leaving vast structural information permanently unsupervised. OPRD replaces both with a single design shift: supervise the student at the *hidden-state* level via a layer-averaged, position-masked MSE loss computed directly on the teacher's intermediate representations on the same on-policy rollout. The gradient is provably zero-variance conditioned on the rollout (Theorem 4), while the supervision signal is orders of magnitude richer. On competition math (AIME 2024/2025, AIMO), OPRD monotonically closes the student–teacher gap that every output-space baseline leaves open, running 1.44× faster and using 54% less actor-update GPU memory than top-16 OPD — making it strictly Pareto-dominant. Code is available at https://github.com/ShenzhiYang2000/OPRD.

## 核心贡献

- **诊断输出空间蒸馏的两大根本缺陷：** 采样 token 的 OPD 梯度是高方差 REINFORCE 估计量，当学生逼近教师时信噪比（SNR）塌缩至零（Theorem 5），导致训练后期停滞；LM head 投影具有大规模有效零空间（$\mathcal{N}_W$），使任何输出空间目标对整片隐状态方向视而不见（Theorem 6/7）。
- **提出 OPRD 方法：** 在学生自采样轨迹上，用层平均、位置掩码的 MSE 损失直接对齐学生与教师各层中间隐状态，完全绕过 LM head，梯度条件方差为零（Theorem 4）。
- **双重理论保证：** 正式证明 OPRD 梯度方差严格低于同条件下的 OPD 梯度（Corollary 1），以及 SNR 在 OPD 中塌缩而在 OPRD 中保持无穷大直至收敛（Theorem 5）。
- **竞技数学推理实验：** 在 AIME24（49.8 vs. 教师 50.8）、AIME25（34.6 vs. 35.6）、AIMO（79.1 vs. 79.5）上接近或达到教师水平，所有输出空间基线停滞在教师以下数点；同时推理链更短（~5700 vs. ~7000 tokens）。
- **严格 Pareto 优于输出空间方法：** 500 步训练仅需 563 分钟（vs. top-16 OPD 的 812 分钟），峰值 GPU 增量显存 20.5 GB（vs. top-16 OPD 的 45.0 GB）。

## Key Contributions

- **Formal diagnosis of two structural OPD failure modes:** (i) REINFORCE-style gradient variance does not vanish as the student improves — the SNR collapses to zero at rate O(δ) by Theorem 5, causing stagnation; (ii) the LM head's effective null space $\mathcal{N}_W$ (scaling as $(\sigma_1/\sigma_d)^2 \approx 10^6$–$10^8$ for production LLMs) makes every output-space objective blind to large hidden-state deviations along low-singular-value directions.
- **OPRD method:** A layer-averaged, position-masked MSE loss over intermediate hidden states on student-generated rollouts. Bypasses the LM head entirely; decomposes additively with any output-space OPD variant as $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_\text{OPD} + \mu \mathcal{L}_\text{OPRD}$.
- **Two proven theorems:** Zero conditional variance for OPRD gradients (Theorem 4); SNR stability for OPRD vs. SNR collapse for OPD (Theorem 5); LM-head null-direction identity (Theorem 6) and spectral gap (Theorem 7).
- **Empirical closure of student–teacher gap** on three competition math benchmarks; all output-space OPD variants plateau several points below the teacher.
- **Strict Pareto dominance** in accuracy, training time, and peak memory; verified on 8×A100 (80G) FSDP runs.

## Methodology

**Setting.** Two same-architecture autoregressive LMs: frozen teacher $\pi_T$ (JustRL-1.5B) and trainable student $\pi_\theta$ (R1-distill-1.5B), both Qwen2.5-1.5B backbone with 28 layers, d=1536 hidden dim, |V|≈151K vocabulary. The student samples responses $\hat{y} \sim \pi_\theta(\cdot|x)$; both models evaluate on the same student-generated prefixes.

**Output-space OPD (baseline family).** Three variants all compute the supervision signal from next-token distributions $p_t$ and $q_t$:
- Sampled-token: $\ell_t^\text{sample} = \log p_t(\hat{y}_t) - \log q_t(\hat{y}_t)$ — unbiased but single-sample REINFORCE, O(BT) memory.
- Full-vocabulary: exact per-position KL, O(BT|V|) memory — infeasible at scale.
- Top-k: averages over k tokens of highest student probability — reduces memory but retains REINFORCE variance structure.

**OPRD objective.** Let $\mathcal{L}_\text{layer} \subseteq \{1,\ldots,L\}$ be distilled layers and $\mathcal{P}(\hat{y}) \subseteq \{1,\ldots,T\}$ supervised positions:

$$\mathcal{L}_\text{OPRD}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}\left[\frac{1}{|\mathcal{L}_\text{layer}|} \sum_{l \in \mathcal{L}_\text{layer}} \frac{1}{\sum_t m_t} \sum_{t=1}^T m_t \frac{1}{d} \left\|h_{\theta,t}^{(l)} - \text{sg}(h_{T,t}^{(l)})\right\|_2^2\right]$$

where $m_t = \mathbf{1}[t \in \mathcal{P}(\hat{y})]$, sg(·) is stop-gradient on the teacher side, and 1/d normalizes across hidden dimension sizes. In experiments: $\mathcal{L}_\text{layer}$ = all 28 layers, $\mathcal{P}(\hat{y})$ = last k=2000 response tokens (where the student-teacher gap concentrates empirically, as shown in Figure 7).

**Why last-k positions.** Figure 7 shows that early response tokens (first k≤1600) already have cosine similarity ≥97% between student and teacher last-layer hidden states — the gap lives almost entirely in the tail of the chain-of-thought where answers are committed. Supervising only these positions targets the signal-rich region while bounding memory.

**Cross-architecture note.** When student and teacher differ in hidden dimension ($d_s \neq d_T$), a learned linear projector $W \in \mathbb{R}^{d_T \times d_s}$ maps student representations to teacher space. In the reported experiments $d_s = d_T = 1536$ so W is omitted.

**Composability.** OPRD can be used as a standalone replacement or additively combined: $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_\text{OPD} + \mu \mathcal{L}_\text{OPRD}$, $\mu \geq 0$. Both terms share the same rollout and single teacher forward pass at zero added infrastructure cost.

**Theoretical mechanisms:**
- Theorem 4 (Zero variance): $\text{Var}[g_\text{OPRD}(\theta)|x, \hat{y}_{<t}] = 0$ — the gradient is a deterministic function of $\theta$ given the rollout.
- Theorem 5 (SNR collapse/stability): OPD's SNR $\to 0$ as $\delta(\theta) \to 0$; OPRD's SNR $= +\infty$ as long as convergence is incomplete.
- Theorem 6 (Null-direction identity): For any output-space OPD loss, $h_\theta - h_T \in \mathcal{N}_W \Rightarrow \ell_\text{out}(h_\theta, h_T) = 0$ — entire subspaces of hidden deviation produce zero loss.
- Theorem 7 (Spectral gap): Along low-singular-value directions $v_d$, hidden-state deviations can be $(\sigma_1/\sigma_d)^2$ times larger than along $v_1$ for the same output-space loss budget.

**Training setup.** 500 AdamW steps, peak lr 1×10⁻⁵, cosine decay, bf16, FSDP on 8×A100 (80G), batch B=8 prompts, max response T=16,384 tokens, 2 samples per prompt. Prompts from DAPO-Math-17K.

## Results & Highlights

**Main accuracy results (Avg@16, %):**

| Method | AIME24 | AIME25 | AIMO |
|--------|--------|--------|------|
| Teacher (JustRL-1.5B) | 50.8 | 35.6 | 79.5 |
| Student (R1-distill-1.5B) | 32.9 | 21.9 | 62.2 |
| OPD top-1 (sampled-token) | 42.3 | 33.5 | 77.0 |
| OPD top-16 | 47.1 | 34.0 | 76.5 |
| **OPRD (ours)** | **49.8** | **34.6** | **79.1** |

OPRD leaves only 1.0/1.0/0.4 points below the teacher — all within the variance of 16-sample Avg@16 evaluation (teacher score on AIMO is within evaluation noise). OPD top-16 stagnates 2.7/0.6/2.6 points below OPRD despite using strictly more output-distribution information.

**Training efficiency (8×A100 80G, B=8, T=16384, 500 steps):**

| Method | ΔPeak GPU (GB) | Wall-clock (min) |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| OPD top-1 | 30.2 | 813 |
| OPD top-16 | 45.0 | 812 |
| **OPRD** | **20.5** | **563** |

OPRD is 1.44× faster and uses 32–54% less actor-update transient memory than output-space baselines.

**Training dynamics.** Figure 3 shows OPRD improves monotonically throughout all 500 steps on all three benchmarks; both OPD variants plateau or oscillate after ~100–200 steps, consistent with Theorem 5's SNR-collapse prediction. The representation cosine similarity (Figure 5) rises sharply and steadily, confirming the MSE objective is being optimized end-to-end.

**Response length.** OPRD converges to ~5,700 tokens mean response length vs. ~7,000 for both OPD variants (Figure 4), at higher accuracy — indicating hidden-state supervision induces more concise, efficient reasoning chains.

**Composability results.** Adding OPRD on top of OPD top-1 with µ=1 already reaches 47.7 (surpassing OPD top-16 at 47.1); µ=10 reaches 50.2, nearly matching the teacher (Figure 6). Monotonically improves with µ — no careful tuning required.

**Mechanistic diagnostics (§4.5).**
- Policy-gradient loss: Adding OPRD shifts the characteristic PG-loss phase-transition spike earlier, confirming accelerated distillation dynamics (Figure 8).
- Top-16 overlap: OPD top-16 alone plateaus; OPD+OPRD continues to climb after a transient dip, reaching strictly higher student–teacher top-16 overlap (Figure 9).
- Per-token entropy: OPRD accelerates the entropy-convergence phase, consistent with faster internal policy reorganisation (Figure 10).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Genuine. OPRD is the first method to move on-policy LLM distillation supervision into the hidden-state space. Prior representation distillation work (FitNets, TinyBERT, MiniLM) operates off-policy on encoder models; OPRD's on-policy, autoregressive setting is structurally different. The two failure-mode theorems (SNR collapse, LM-head null space) are novel formal contributions that sharpen existing empirical observations.

**Rigor:** Strong by preprint standards. The appendix provides complete formal proofs for all 7 theorems under mild standard assumptions (R1–R4). The variance and SNR analysis is mathematically tight. One mild concern: the spectral gap argument (Theorem 7) quantifies the bottleneck but does not characterize which *specific* directions OPRD supervises or how much of the null space is covered in practice.

**Reproducibility:** Good. Code is public, models are public (Hugging Face), training configs are reported in detail (learning rate, batch size, hardware, steps), and the evaluation metric (Avg@16 at temperature 0.7 with boxed-answer parser) is standard. One limitation: experiments are on a single architecture pair (Qwen2.5-1.5B variants); generalizability to other sizes and architectures is unverified.

**Significance:** High for LLM post-training practitioners. On-policy distillation is a core production technique (Qwen3, Mimo-v2-flash, GLM-5 all use OPD). A strictly Pareto-dominant drop-in replacement — with rigorous theoretical justification — is a meaningful contribution.

**Red flags / caveats:**
- **Single architecture pair:** All experiments use JustRL-1.5B → R1-distill-1.5B (both Qwen2.5-1.5B backbone). Whether OPRD generalizes to larger scale (7B, 32B) or cross-family teacher-student pairs is untested.
- **Same-architecture hard constraint:** OPRD explicitly requires identical depth, width, and initialization family. This rules out the common scenario of distilling a large teacher into a substantially smaller student — a core industry use case.
- **Mathematical reasoning only:** All benchmarks are competition math (AIME24/25, AIMO). Generalization to code, dialogue, instruction following, and open-ended tasks is entirely open.
- **"Work in progress" status:** The paper self-labels as a preprint. Some analysis sections (e.g., the phase transition mechanism) are explicitly flagged as "under active investigation."
- **Teacher logits still computed:** The current implementation still runs the teacher's full forward pass (including LM head logits computation) even though OPRD never uses them — so reported efficiency gains are conservative lower bounds, not the maximum achievable.
- **No comparison to non-OPD baselines:** OPRD is compared only against OPD variants, not against RL (GRPO/PPO) or SFT, making it hard to position in the broader post-training landscape.

## Strengths

- Theoretically principled: both the variance and information-bottleneck failure modes are formalized with clean proofs, not just heuristically argued
- The supervision signal is genuinely richer: (layers × positions × hidden-dim) scalars vs. 1 scalar for sampled-token OPD per position
- Strict Pareto dominance over all output-space baselines on the same hardware and rollout budget — not a tradeoff
- Drop-in composability: OPRD + OPD requires no architectural changes and zero added rollout cost
- Identifies and exploits the empirical structure that the student–teacher gap concentrates in the *last* k tokens of the response (Figure 7), making last-k supervision both targeted and memory-efficient
- Code is public, enabling immediate follow-up

## Limitations

- **Same-architecture requirement is a hard constraint**, ruling out cross-size distillation (the dominant industrial use case for compression)
- **Single domain (math) and single scale (1.5B):** Generalization across task types, model families, and scales is unverified
- **No wall-clock comparison at equal quality:** Results compare fixed training budget (500 steps); a quality-controlled comparison (steps to reach, say, 48 AIME24) would be stronger
- **Teacher logit computation overhead not eliminated:** Current implementation retains teacher LM head, so memory/time gains are conservative
- **The phase transition mechanism is unexplained:** OPRD triggers a policy reorganization spike (Figures 8–10) whose causal mechanism is "under active investigation" — a theoretical gap
- **Attention-map distillation excluded:** OPRD aligns hidden-state vectors but not the attention patterns that produce them; combining both could yield further gains
- **No ablation on projector design for cross-architecture settings:** The linear projector W (needed when $d_s \neq d_T$) is untested in the reported experiments

## Relevance

该论文直接关联大语言模型后训练中的在线知识蒸馏与强化学习对齐方向（RLHF/RLAIF），提出的隐状态级监督信号及其零方差梯度理论对研究 RLHF 训练稳定性、表示对齐与高效推理压缩均有较高参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does OPRD's hidden-state supervision advantage persist at larger scales (7B, 32B teacher–student pairs where vocabulary-induced memory pressure is even more acute)?
2. Can cross-architecture OPRD be achieved via contrastive objectives (aligning relative geometry rather than absolute vectors) without the same-architecture constraint?
3. What is the causal mechanism of the PG-loss spike / phase transition OPRD induces? Is it a reorganization of the residual stream, a mode bifurcation in the policy, or a representation geometry shift?
4. Does OPRD benefit tasks beyond chain-of-thought math reasoning — e.g., code generation (where the "tail of the sequence" hypothesis may not hold) or dialogue?
5. Would extending OPRD to attention-map distillation (aligning the routing patterns that produce hidden states, not just the states themselves) further close the teacher gap?
6. In the RLHF setting with a reward model (rather than a fixed teacher), how does OPRD interact with reward model objectives — does representation alignment help or hurt reward-driven exploration?
7. Can adaptive layer and position selection (weighting layers/positions by the magnitude of the student–teacher gap) outperform uniform layer weighting with last-k position heuristic?
