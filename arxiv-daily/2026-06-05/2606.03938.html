Key Finding

Cyclic snapshots + chain distillation + learned prior beats an 8-model ensemble using 4.6× fewer epochs

q0: Primitives for Hyper-Epoch Pretraining ↗
Key Metric
4.6×
Epoch reduction to match 256-epoch 8-model baseline. Val loss: 3.0034 (q0 256ep) vs. 3.0476 (baseline). Data efficiency: ~12× over single-model training.
Why This Matters
When data is fixed and compute is abundant, stop refining one model — explore a population
Directly addresses post-Chinchilla training where data is exhausted. Three composable primitives, each independently ablated, any budget from E=1 to E=960.
Critical Edge
K-model inference; only 1.8B / 100M tokens tested
Serving an 8-model ensemble is 8× inference cost. No distillation to single student demonstrated. Allocation rules may not transfer to 7B+ scale.
01
Cyclic Snapshot Ensembling
Anti-correlated LR / WD cyclic schedule across N parallel trajectories. Each cycle ends at a convergence checkpoint (low-LR, high-WD). Cycle-boundary Gaussian perturbation (σ: 0.25→0.05) maintains within-trajectory diversity that chain distillation would otherwise suppress.
02
Chain Distillation
Each snapshot trains against its frozen predecessor as a KL teacher (α=0.45, T=1.2). Unlike snapshot distillation that discards intermediates, q0 keeps every snapshot as an ensemble member. Removing chain distillation degrades val loss from 3.0034 → 3.0283 at 256 epochs.
03
Learned Generalization Prior
A softmax over unconstrained logits β minimizes mixture loss (probabilities mixed inside the log, not log-probs) on a ~132K-token fitness set. Forces complementarity over individual quality. At K=8, selects only 2 of 8 overlapping snapshots with greedy top-K selection.
Methodology

Architecture. 1.8B-parameter decoder (30 layers, d_model=2048, 16 heads). Middle 10 layers form a "looped block" traversed 5× per forward pass — 4× effective depth at zero parameter cost. Muon+AdamW hybrid optimizer.

Main config. N=2 trajectories, C=64 cycles (2 epochs each) → M=128 snapshots, 256 total epochs, 100M FineWeb tokens.

Optimal allocation rule. N*(E) ≈ max(1, ⌊log₂(E/64)⌋). One extra trajectory per doubling of the budget. Beyond N≈3, marginal benefit per trajectory falls sharply (~10⁻³ val loss).

Small-budget regime. At E=1: 4 closely-spaced end-of-training snapshots (3.765 loss) beats single checkpoint (3.802). At E=4: 3 epochs + 4 snapshots outperforms all multi-model baselines.

Results
  • Val loss 3.0034 (q0, 256ep) vs. 3.0476 (8-model baseline, 256ep) — q0 strictly better
  • q0 matches baseline at ~56 epochs (best-k selection) → 4.6× epoch reduction
  • At matched K=8 ensemble size: q0 matches at ~67 epochs → 3.8×
  • q0 continues improving at 960ep: val loss 2.987
  • ARC-Easy 0.4865 vs. 0.4781; PIQA 0.6823 vs. 0.6697; SciQ 0.8030 vs. 0.7850
  • Data efficiency: ~14.2× vs. single-model reference (q0 256ep)
  • Prior beats greedy top-K at every inference budget K; largest margin at small K (most practically relevant)
Strengths / Limitations
Strengths
  • Each of the three primitives independently ablated — not a black-box recipe
  • Strong baseline (8 independent models + EMA) — not a strawman single-model comparison
  • Full budget range E=1 to E=960 with prescriptive allocation rule
  • Non-separable mixture loss correctly models complementarity, not individual quality
  • Code released at pinned commit — reproducible
Limitations
  • K-model ensemble inference is K× cost — distillation to single student not demonstrated
  • Only 1.8B / 100M token scale; allocation rules may not transfer to 7B+
  • Chain distillation compresses within-trajectory diversity; cycle-boundary perturbation may be insufficient at very long horizons
  • Data efficiency metric relies on third-party Slowrun Nanochat reference curve interpolation
  • Single training run per configuration — no cross-seed variance in main table
Open Questions
  • Can q0 ensemble be distilled into a single student model that recovers gains at 1× inference cost?
  • Do the 4.6× efficiency multipliers and N*(E) staircase hold at 7B / 1T tokens?
  • Does chain distillation eventually collapse diversity at C→∞? Is trajectory count N the only reliable diversity source at high C?
  • How does q0 interact with synthetic data augmentation — can both fix data constraints simultaneously?
