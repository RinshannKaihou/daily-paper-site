> **一句话总结：** NF-CoT 在 LLM 的因果流中嵌入自回归归一化流来建模连续"思维"token，使潜在链式推理（latent CoT）保留自回归采样、精确似然与 KV-cache 解码能力，并在 Qwen3-8B-Base 上将平均 pass@1 从 55.8 提升到 68.8，同时比扩散式 LaDiR 快约 1.9 倍。

> **TL;DR:** NF-CoT replaces verbose explicit chain-of-thought with an autoregressive normalizing flow (TARFlow/STARFlow-style) running inside the LLM causal stream, giving continuous thoughts exact likelihoods and native left-to-right sampling. Fine-tuned on the 1.4M-sample Ling-Coder set and evaluated on HumanEval, MBPP, HumanEval+, MBPP+, and LiveCodeBench v6 with a Qwen3-8B-Base backbone, NF-CoT (Unified) lifts average pass@1 from 55.8 (base) to 68.8 (+13.0), beating the strongest latent baseline LaDiR by +7.1 average pass@1, while running 1.92× faster overall (325.6s vs 625.3s on HumanEval) than LaDiR and compressing reasoning into 64 latent tokens (≈6.0× over a 385-token CoT).

## 核心贡献

- 提出将连续思维 token 赋予与文本 token 同等的建模地位：在 LLM 的单一因果流中，用 NF 头生成连续思维位置、用标准 LM 头生成答案位置。
- 设计了一种基于归一化流的似然式连续 CoT 公式，保留显式 CoT 的采样、打分、KV-cache 解码与可计算似然接口（区别于 Coconut 的确定性隐态与 LaDiR 的扩散迭代去噪）。
- 提供统一的优化接口：同一似然目标同时支持监督训练（flow NLL + 答案交叉熵）以及在连续推理空间内的策略梯度（GRPO 式）精炼。

## Key Contributions

- A likelihood-based formulation of continuous CoT that preserves the sampling, scoring, and decoding interface of explicit CoT, giving continuous thoughts an exact log-likelihood instead of deterministic hidden states (Coconut) or intractable diffusion densities (LaDiR).
- A novel architecture that jointly models latent thoughts and text answers via an NF head and a standard LM head over a single shared LLM backbone within one causal stream, reusing the KV cache across thought sampling and answer decoding.
- A unified optimization interface enabling both supervised end-to-end likelihood training (flow term + answer cross-entropy) and direct policy-gradient (GRPO-style) refinement in the continuous reasoning space, since the policy likelihood decomposes into a Gaussian NF term plus a token-likelihood term.

## Methodology

NF-CoT gives continuous chain-of-thought the same modeling status as language tokens by placing a scalable autoregressive normalizing flow inside the LLM's causal stream. The approach has three pieces:

**1. Two coupled continuous-thought spaces.** Starting from the continuous-CoT target `e1:K` (K learnable VAE-encoded codes distilled from explicit CoT traces, as in Coconut/LaDiR), NF-CoT introduces an LLM-facing thought space `u1:K` modeled with a causal Gaussian density (Eq. 3.1): `p(u1:K|q) = Π N(u_i; μ_θ(q,u_<i), diag σ²_θ(q,u_<i))`. The two spaces are linked by an invertible map `u1:K = F_θ(e1:K;q)` (Eq. 3.2) so `u` is information-equivalent to `e`, not a lossy compression. Because `log p(e1:K|q) = log p(u1:K|q) + log|det J_{F_θ}|`, the full likelihood stays exact.

**2. Architecture.** Two flow components: (a) shallow autoregressive flow blocks (the invertible map `F_θ: e→u`, causal affine flows with identity initialization and a triangular Jacobian), and (b) a deep LLM-parameterized autoregressive flow over `u`. Continuous thoughts `u1:K` are projected into the token-embedding dimension and processed in the same causal stream as answer tokens. At continuous-thought positions an **NF head** outputs `(μ_θ, σ_θ)`; at answer positions the **standard LM head** outputs token logits. The default "Unified" variant places both in one causal sequence; a "Dual-Path" variant (separate paths for the NF and CE objectives) is reported as an ablation. Setup: **Qwen3-8B-Base** backbone, **N = 64 latent slots**, a flow stack of **K = 5 shallow MetaBlocks** (alternating Identity/Flip permutations) followed by the deep LLM block.

**3. Learning and inference.** Supervised objective `L_sup = λ_flow·L_flow + λ_text·L_text` (flow NLL on `e` via Eq. 3.2 + answer cross-entropy conditioned on the thoughts), trained with a two-stage curriculum: Stage 1 freezes the LLM backbone and trains only the shallow flow blocks + projection layers; Stage 2 unfreezes everything. Small Gaussian noise is added to `e` before the flow loss (following TarFlow) for robustness. At inference the shallow blocks are skipped and reasoning happens directly in `u`-space: continuous thoughts are sampled left-to-right from the Gaussian density, then the LM head continues answer decoding in the same causal pass, reusing the KV cache. For RL, a GRPO-style objective over sampled (thought, answer) trajectories refines the policy; the policy likelihood (Eq. 3.4) splits into the NF Gaussian term + token term, so policy gradients apply to both.

**Training data.** Supervised fine-tuning on **Ling-Coder (1.4M Python instruction-following samples)**; the RL stage adds data from **AceCoder + KodCode**, run on a 20K-problem Python mixture for 150 steps. Evaluation: 16 samples per problem, averaged pass@1, on HumanEval, MBPP, HumanEval+, MBPP+ (EvalPlus, more test cases), and LiveCodeBench v6.

## Results & Highlights

- **Average pass@1 on Qwen3-8B-Base:** NF-CoT (Unified) raises the average from **55.8 (base) to 68.8 (+13.0)**; NF-CoT (Dual-Path) reaches **65.2 (+9.4)**. (Table 1)
- **Beats the strongest latent baseline:** NF-CoT (Unified) outperforms **LaDiR by +7.1 average pass@1**, and also surpasses the autoregressive **OlympicCoder (+0.3)**, diffusion **Diffu-Coder (+9.1)**, and looped-latent **Ouro (+1.9)** on average pass@1. (Table 1)
- **CoT-data vs. distribution control:** On the four non-LCB benchmarks, Standard SFT improves the base only from **65.3 to 67.5**, whereas NF-CoT (Unified) reaches **80.0** with the same training data — evidence the gain comes from modeling the reasoning-trajectory distribution, not extra CoT data.
- **Execution-guided latent RL:** GRPO with unit-test rewards lifts NF-CoT (Unified) average pass@1 from **68.8 to 70.1** across all five benchmarks; unlike token-space GRPO (which saturates/collapses at large k), latent-space RL preserves upward pass@k scaling.
- **pass@k scaling:** On MBPP+, NF-CoT's pass@1 (**72.1**) already matches the base model's pass@128 (72.0) and rises to **87.5 at k=128**; on HumanEval+, NF-CoT improves **78.3 → 97.5 (+19.2)** vs LaDiR's 73.2 → 90.2 (+17.0).
- **Inference efficiency (HumanEval, 16 candidates, LaDiR = 30 denoising steps):** NF-CoT (Unified) total **325.6s** vs LaDiR **625.3s** (1.92× faster overall), latent generation **173.5s vs 468.2s** (2.70× faster), and **19.9T vs 49.3T FLOPs/sample** (2.48× cheaper); 64 latent tokens encode a ~385-token CoT (≈6.0× compression). (Table 2)
- **Training throughput:** NF-CoT (Unified) achieves **2.85× sample and 5.71× token throughput** over LaDiR with **6.66× fewer total FLOPs** (2.25e19 vs 1.50e20). (Table 3)
- **Ablation (frozen-backbone warm-up):** Removing Stage 1 drops HumanEval **84.4 → 81.5**, HumanEval+ **78.7 → 75.5**, LiveCodeBench v6 **23.1 → 21.4**, even though final loss is nearly identical — the warm-up aligns the latent interface before backbone updates.
- **Latent robustness:** Perturbing the continuous-CoT trajectory (σ: 0→3.0) drops cosine similarity 1.000→0.116 but pass@1 only **86.0 → 83.6**; pairwise pass agreement 1.000→0.888 while exact-text match collapses 0.973→0.278 — perturbations change implementation form, not correctness.

## Critical Assessment

The evaluation is **restricted to code generation** (HumanEval/MBPP/+EvalPlus/LiveCodeBench v6) — the paper's own Limitations section admits "extending to other reasoning tasks remains future work," so claims about general latent reasoning are not substantiated for math, commonsense, or multi-step QA. The strongest latent baseline is **LaDiR**, but the comparison is not fully apples-to-apples: LaDiR is run at its paper setting of **30 denoising steps**, and the reported efficiency speedups (2.70× latent, 1.92× overall) depend heavily on that step count — a LaDiR configuration with fewer denoising steps would narrow the gap, and no LaDiR step-count sweep is provided. The fixed **N = 64 latent slots** for every problem is a data quirk: the authors acknowledge "a fixed latent budget may not fit all problems," yet there is no adaptive-budget ablation. Several open-source baselines (OlympicCoder, Ouro) are beaten only narrowly on average (+0.3, +1.9), and many diffusion baselines are missing LiveCodeBench v6 numbers (shown as "–"), which inflates apparent dominance on the hardest benchmark. Finally, the decoded continuous-CoTs are explicitly disclaimed as non-faithful probes (Appendix A), so the interpretability/diversity evidence is qualitative, not rigorous.

## Strengths

- Principled probabilistic formulation: exact log-likelihood over continuous thoughts via invertible flow, unlike Coconut's deterministic states or LaDiR's intractable diffusion density.
- Native compatibility with the LLM stack — left-to-right sampling, KV-cache reuse, and a clean policy-gradient interface — making latent reasoning a drop-in for existing training/decoding.
- Strong and broad empirical gains on code benchmarks plus useful efficiency and robustness analyses (pass@k, latent perturbation, warm-up ablation).

## Limitations

- Code-only evaluation; no math, commonsense, or agentic reasoning tasks to test generalization of "latent reasoning."
- Fixed 64-slot latent budget with no adaptive-length mechanism; inherits biases of the VAE-encoded explicit-CoT targets.
- RL refinement depends on unit-test rewards available only in code; generalization to verifier-free domains is open.
- Continuous latents are not human-readable; decoded CoTs are qualitative probes, not faithful explanations, raising interpretability and safety concerns (plausible-yet-incorrect code).

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the exact-latent-likelihood formulation and the two-stage frozen-backbone warm-up transfer to non-code reasoning tasks (math/QA) where the only reward is answer correctness rather than unit tests, as the authors' own Limitations section flags?
2. How does NF-CoT behave under an **adaptive latent budget** instead of the fixed N = 64 slots — could the 6.0× compression be pushed further on hard problems without the "fixed latent budget may not fit all problems" failure mode?
3. The efficiency advantage over LaDiR is tied to LaDiR's 30 denoising steps (the paper's setting); how does the comparison shift if LaDiR's denoising-step count is swept, and is the autoregressive-flow advantage robust at low step counts?
4. Since latent-space GRPO preserves pass@k diversity while token-space GRPO collapses it (Fig. 4), could the NF head's Gaussian density be used as a principled diversity/uncertainty signal for verifier-free RL, given that latent likelihood is "not a correctness oracle" per the authors?
5. The decoded continuous-CoTs are disclaimed as non-faithful (Appendix A); what probing or causal-intervention method beyond Gaussian perturbation (Sec. 5.3) could test whether the 64 latent slots actually encode reasoning structure vs. solution-style switches?
