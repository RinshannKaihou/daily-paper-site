Why This Matters
Consumer GPUs (12GB, 24GB) sit between what 2-bit and 3-bit quantization can hold. LiftQuant fills that deployment gap exactly, enabling 70B model inference on hardware that was previously out of reach — without custom kernel engineering.
Critical Edge
Sweet spot is strictly 2–3 bit. At ≥4-bit, the feasibility constraint (D−d ≤ 20) forces small block sizes, limiting inter-channel correlation capture. This is a 2-3-bit method only. QTIP has slightly better coding efficiency at exactly 2.0-bit.

The core mechanism: instead of quantizing w ∈ ℝ^d directly, LiftQuant lifts to ℝ^D and finds the nearest 1-bit vector w_q ∈ {+1,−1}^D, then projects back: w ≈ M · w_q.

The ratio D/d becomes a continuous knob — LQ-24/10 is 2.4-bit, LQ-32/16 is 2.0-bit, LQ-24/8 is 3.0-bit. By CLT, the resulting codebook naturally approximates a Gaussian distribution matching LLM weight priors.

T
Whitening Transform
Kronecker-factored orthogonal + per-channel scaling reshapes heavy-tailed LLM weights into i.i.d. Gaussian. Adds only 0.008–0.011 bits overhead.
M
Learned Projection Matrix
Mapping M ∈ ℝ^(d×D) trained to minimize expected quantization error. Largest single ablation gain: PPL 7.77 → 6.79.
⊕
Fused Inference Operator
T* = MT⁻¹ fuses projection and whitening into a single Int1→Float GEMM: o = diag(s)·W_q·(T*·a). Standard BitBLAS + torch.compile.

Strengths / Limitations

Strengths

CLT-grounded Gaussian codebook — principled Single unified Int1 GEMM inference operator +28% throughput over QTIP using standard libs Llama-2/3 + Qwen-2.5/3, 3B–70B coverage ICML 2026 peer-reviewed · open-source

Limitations

2–3 bit sweet spot only — ≥4-bit degrades Slightly lower coding efficiency than QTIP at strict 2.0-bit Needs 8M-token calibration corpus BitBLAS dependency — edge hardware may need porting Weight-only — no activation quantization
Open Questions
Q1 Can the lift-then-project mechanism extend to activation quantization, enabling continuous-bit W2A4 or W4A4 schemes without additional hardware primitives?
Q2 Could importance-aware, per-layer D/d ratio selection (sensitivity-based) further improve the accuracy-memory tradeoff beyond the global bit-width target used today?
Q3 How does the mapping matrix M relate to known optimal lattices (Gosset E8 used in QTIP, Barnes-Wall)? Could M be initialized from lattice theory for better 2.0-bit performance?
Q4 Can LiftQuant's continuous knob extend to KV cache quantization, enabling joint weight+KV cache Pareto optimization to fully utilize a fixed VRAM budget?
