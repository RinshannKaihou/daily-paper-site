Key Finding · 2606.03073 · JF-HPO

Joint fidelity HPO makes LLM-RL hyperparameter search 14.9× cheaper — small proxy models do the heavy lifting

Minping Chen, Bowen Xiao et al. · HKUST(GZ) / China Unicom 2026-06-05 arxiv.org/abs/2606.03073
Key Metric
14.9×
per-trial speedup (Qwen-2.5 7B on GSM8K) — 0.59 h per trial vs. 8.80 h for random search
Why This Matters
Hyperparams drive +111% performance swings in LLM RL — this makes exploration finally practical within a 48-hour budget
Critical Edge
Batch size excluded from search; proxy must share architecture with target — MoE or cross-family models not covered
Methodology

JF-HPO treats both model size and training budget as fidelity dimensions in Bayesian optimization. Instead of running every candidate on the full 7B/8B/14B target, most trials use a 0.5B–1B same-family proxy model.

A registry of trial metadata and checkpoints allows multi-fidelity HPO to resume from existing checkpoints rather than retraining from scratch at each budget level.

Two complementary early-stopping rules prune bad configs: halt when KL divergence growth ratio exceeds τ₁ = 15% for 5 consecutive steps (policy deviating too fast), or when training reward drops >10% or stays at zero for 5 steps.

The acquisition function balances Expected Improvement against trial cost, guiding the Gaussian Process toward high-value low-cost (proxy-model) evaluations.

Strengths
  • 14.9× speedup is not marginal — transforms HPO from "basically impossible" to "practical within 48 h"
  • Spearman 0.90 rank correlation between proxy and target validates the small-model substitution quantitatively
  • Ablation confirms checkpointing is the biggest contributor (−3.33pp without it)
  • OOD results (+60.88% on AMC 2023) show HPO finds generalizing, not overfitting, configs
  • Design is simple: proxy model + dynamics-aware early stopping + checkpoint reuse — all reproducible
Limitations
  • Batch size excluded from search space due to GPU constraints — a key RL stability hyperparameter is missing
  • Proxy model must be same-family/same-architecture — fails for MoE targets or cross-family scenarios
  • Only two fidelity levels tested (proxy vs. target); finer granularity unexplored
  • All tasks are exact-answer reasoning; open-ended generation applicability unvalidated
  • Early-stopping thresholds (τ₁=15%, τ₂=10%) not sensitivity-analyzed across tasks
  • Code not yet publicly available — reproducibility unconfirmed
Open Questions
  • Does Spearman 0.90 (measured at 7B→0.5B gap) hold at 70B→7B scale? The paper plans to test this.
  • Would adding batch size to the search space materially change which configs win, given its interaction with learning rate?
  • With three or more fidelity levels (continuous scaling), does the search efficiency improve further?
  • How does JF-HPO interact with DAPO or REINFORCE++ whose hyperparameter landscapes may differ from GRPO?
Critical edge: The paper's most striking number — +111.6% on MATH for LLaMA 8B — is mostly a function of VeRL Recipe using poor defaults. The HPO signal is real, but practitioners should note that batch size, excluded here, is itself a major RL training stability knob. The proxy assumption also breaks for heterogeneous architectures increasingly common in production MoE deployments.
