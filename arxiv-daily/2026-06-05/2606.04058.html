Muon's momentum spectra follow layer-specific power laws;
final layers will hit Newton-Schulz failure at ~300B parameters.

arxiv.org/abs/2606.04058 ↗
Key Metric — Scaling Exponent Range
−0.96
Final MLP Up projection layer exponent — vs. −0.25 for mid-to-early layers. A ×100 scale increase shrinks the final-layer median singular value by ×28 (vs. ×2 for mid layers).
Threshold: σ₀.₅ must stay above NS failure floor (~0.003) to preserve Muon's benefit
Why This Matters
Muon is already deployed in Kimi-K2, GLM-5, DeepSeek-V4. The standard 5-step NS config will silently degrade update quality in late layers at frontier scale — now there's a principled, layer-aware recipe to prevent it.
Critical Edge
Power laws fit over only 40× range (77M–2.8B), then extrapolated 100× to 300B. GPT-2 dense only — MoE architectures (the dominant frontier form) are entirely unverified, despite Kimi-K2 and DeepSeek-V4 being MoE.
Methodology

Seven GPT-2-style models (77M → 2.8B) trained to Chinchilla-optimal budgets on FineWeb. The Frobenius-normalized momentum matrix M̃ᵗ = Mᵗ/‖Mᵗ‖_F is tracked every step.

Singular value quantiles q ∈ {0.1, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 0.9} are measured for 6 matrix types (Q, K, V, O attention + MLP up/down) at 4 relative depths → 24 momentum buffers per model.

After a short burn-in (~300 steps), all quantiles stabilize. Stabilized values are averaged over steps 1300–1500, then fitted as power laws σ_q(M) = c·M^α on log-log scale.

A rank-p ablation (using only top-p fraction of singular directions) establishes p=0.5 as the critical threshold: below this, Muon loses 10–50% token efficiency.

Open Questions
Q1 Do these power laws transfer to MoE architectures? Expert layers in Kimi-K2 / DeepSeek-V4 have qualitatively different gradient statistics due to sparse routing — the binding deployment case is unaddressed.
Q2 What happens at over-training ratios (10–100× Chinchilla)? Does the momentum buffer see enough gradient signal to shift the stabilized singular values, altering the failure boundary?
Q3 Can NS polynomial coefficients be designed per-layer from the empirical distributions? The bulk of singular values decays exponentially — near-optimal polynomials may be derivable analytically.
Q4 Is spectral stabilization a convergence criterion or a Chinchilla artifact? Whether the stabilized values would shift under cosine schedules, warmup, or cooldown phases is unstudied.
