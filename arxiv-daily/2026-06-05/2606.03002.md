> **一句话总结：** 量化会系统性地损害稀疏自编码器（SAE）特征——在INT6精度下Pythia-70M和Gemma-2-2B分别有37.6%和48.7%的活跃特征丢失，且困惑度指标会隐藏这一损害（Gemma INT7量化在改善困惑度的同时降低了18.7%的特征存活率）。

> **TL;DR:** This paper asks a pointed safety question: when you deploy an LLM in quantized form (INT8/INT7/INT6/INT4), do the interpretable SAE features identified in the full-precision model still exist in the deployed model? Answer: no — features degrade systematically and silently. At INT6, 37–49% of features fail to survive (Pearson correlation < 0.9) across Pythia-70M and Gemma-2-2B, and this damage is invisible to standard perplexity metrics. Feature survival is highly predictable (AUC 0.92–0.97) from full-precision statistics alone, with peak activation as the strongest predictor. Quantization and matched-perplexity magnitude pruning damage nearly identical feature sets (Jaccard 0.79–0.86), pointing to a shared vulnerability driven by feature signal strength rather than the specific compression mechanism.

## 核心贡献

- **特征存活是渐进式的，而非断崖式的。** 随比特宽度降低，SAE特征系统性退化（相关性下降），而非突然崩溃。中间比特宽度下"模糊"（degraded，相关性0.5–0.9）比"损毁"（damaged，相关性<0.5）更常见，说明量化主要使特征变模糊，而非完全摧毁。
- **困惑度无法检测特征损害。** 在Gemma-2-2B上，INT7量化将困惑度提升了5.65%（看起来"更好"），但同时有18.7%的活跃特征退化。即使用滑动窗口协议验证，INT6在改善困惑度的同时仍有约48.7%的特征未能存活。
- **特征存活可从全精度统计量预测。** 用峰值激活、稀有度、均值激活、激活集中度四个特征统计量，训练L2正则化逻辑回归可达到AUC 0.924（Pythia）和0.971（Gemma），使得"哪些特征在量化后幸存"成为可预测问题。
- **量化与剪枝损害同一批特征。** 在相同困惑度水平下，RTN量化与量级剪枝（magnitude pruning）的非存活特征集合Jaccard重叠率为0.79–0.86，逐特征损害分数Spearman相关0.98，说明特征脆弱性源于信号强度本身，而非具体压缩方式。

## Key Contributions

- **Graded feature degradation, not cliff failures.** Feature survival (Pearson correlation between FP16 and compressed activations) declines monotonically with bit-width. At INT6: 62.4% survive on Pythia-70M, 51.3% on Gemma-2-2B. Crucially, the degraded band (correlation 0.5–0.9) is consistently larger than the damaged band (<0.5) at intermediate bit-widths, meaning quantization predominantly blurs features rather than destroys them.
- **Perplexity is a blind spot for feature-level damage.** The paper demonstrates a clean dissociation on Gemma-2-2B: INT7 improves perplexity by 5.65% while degrading 18.7% of active SAE features. This holds under both chunked and sliding-window evaluation protocols, ruling out evaluation artifacts.
- **Survival is predictable from FP16 statistics (AUC 0.92–0.97).** Four predictors — rarity, log mean activation, log peak activation, log concentration — suffice to predict INT6 survival via cross-validated logistic regression. Peak activation is the strongest single marginal predictor (survival rises from 13% to 96% across peak-activation quartiles on Pythia-70M).
- **Quantization and pruning share vulnerability structure.** At matched perplexity (INT6), RTN quantization and magnitude pruning damage overlapping feature sets (Jaccard 0.79–0.86; damage-score Spearman 0.98). This suggests that compression-induced feature vulnerability is governed by a feature's signal strength, not by how weights are altered.

## Methodology

**The QDM (Quantization-feature Damage Measurement) Pipeline:**

The experimental design is clean and well-controlled. The core insight is to use a frozen SAE as a fixed measurement basis — the SAE is trained once on the FP16 model and then never retrained. This isolates compression effects from SAE training variability.

1. **Models:** Pythia-70M-deduped (residual stream at layer 4, SAE width d_sae = SAE from pythia-70m-deduped-res-sm) and Gemma-2-2B (layer 12 residual stream, Gemma Scope SAE with d_sae = 16,384). The 30x parameter range allows cross-scale comparison.

2. **Compression operators:** Round-to-nearest (RTN) quantization with per-output-channel scaling, sweeping b ∈ {8, 7, 6, 5, 4} bits. Applied to transformer linear weights (attention projections, MLP projections, gated MLP gate projections for Gemma). Layer-norm and embedding parameters left at full precision. A matched-perplexity magnitude pruning baseline is calibrated so PPL(prune) ≈ PPL(RTN INT6), enabling an apples-to-apples compression comparison.

3. **Feature-stability metric:** Per-feature Pearson correlation c_j between FP16 activations z(t; θ_FP16) and compressed activations z(t; θ_C) over N identical token positions. Features are "active" if firing rate f_j > 0.001. Survival taxonomy: survived (c_j > 0.9), degraded (0.5 ≤ c_j ≤ 0.9), damaged (c_j < 0.5).

4. **Fragility predictor:** L2-regularized logistic regression (C=1, 5-fold stratified CV) predicting INT6 survival from four FP16 statistics: rarity −log(f_j + ε), log mean activation log(μ_j + ε), log peak activation log(max_t z_j(t; θ_FP16) + ε), and log concentration log(max activation / mean activation).

5. **Pruning overlap:** Jaccard overlap of non-survived feature sets and Spearman correlation of damage scores d_j = 1 − c_j across all active features, comparing RTN INT6 with calibrated magnitude pruning.

6. **Stability checks:** Token-budget sensitivity (50k–200k tokens), random-subset seed stability (3 independent 100k-token subsets), FP16-vs-FP16 null (mean correlation 1.000, 100% survival — confirms no pipeline artifact), and layer sensitivity (repeated sweep at a second layer on Pythia-70M).

**Evaluation:** Token-level perplexity on WikiText-2-raw using both a fixed-chunked protocol (L=512 for Pythia, L=256 for Gemma) and a sliding-window protocol (W=2048, S=512) for Gemma. The sliding-window check rules out context-reset artifacts as the explanation for the INT7 perplexity anomaly.

## Results & Highlights

**Feature survival by bit-width (Table 1):**

| Model | Bits | Survived (%) | Degraded (%) | Damaged (%) | Mean corr. |
|-------|------|-------------|-------------|------------|-----------|
| Pythia-70M | INT8 | 98.3 | 1.7 | 0.0 | 0.981 |
| Pythia-70M | INT7 | 86.3 | 13.7 | 0.0 | 0.957 |
| Pythia-70M | INT6 | 62.4 | 36.6 | 1.0 | 0.888 |
| Pythia-70M | INT5 | 38.0 | 49.5 | 12.6 | 0.777 |
| Pythia-70M | INT4 | 14.5 | 47.8 | 37.7 | 0.583 |
| Gemma-2-2B | INT8 | 99.1 | 0.9 | 0.0 | 0.966 |
| Gemma-2-2B | INT7 | 81.3 | 18.7 | 0.0 | 0.943 |
| Gemma-2-2B | INT6 | 51.3 | 48.7 | 0.0 | 0.891 |
| Gemma-2-2B | INT5 | 27.7 | 66.4 | 5.9 | 0.764 |
| Gemma-2-2B | INT4 | 12.4 | 32.9 | 54.7 | 0.402 |

Key observation: at Gemma INT6, 48.7% of features are degraded and 0.0% are damaged — intermediate quantization predominantly blurs features rather than destroying them.

**Perplexity dissociation (Table 3, Gemma-2-2B):**
- INT7 chunked perplexity: −5.65% (improves) while 18.7% of features degrade
- INT7 sliding-window perplexity: −6.49% (also improves) — same direction under both protocols
- INT6 sliding-window: −3.45% (improves) while only 51.3% of features survive

**Fragility predictor (Table 2):**
- Pythia-70M: AUC = 0.924 ± 0.007 (n=5,688 active features)
- Gemma-2-2B: AUC = 0.971 ± 0.002 (n=7,144 active features)
- Peak activation is the strongest marginal predictor: survival rises from 13% → 96% across quartiles on Pythia (5% → 92% on Gemma)

**Quantization vs. pruning overlap (Table 4):**
- Pythia: Jaccard = 0.860, Pearson = 0.951, Spearman = 0.976
- Gemma: Jaccard = 0.792, Pearson = 0.959, Spearman = 0.978

**Methodology stability (Appendix B):** Survival estimates are stable to within ±0.71 percentage points across token budgets (50k–200k) and across three independent 100k-token random subsets. The FP16-vs-FP16 null gives exactly 1.000 mean correlation and 0.0% damaged, confirming the pipeline does not manufacture drift.

## Authors & Credibility

**Evan Duan** (University of Michigan, evanduan@umich.edu) — appears to be a student researcher. This is a solo-author paper, which is unusual for an empirical study of this scope but not unprecedented in interpretability research.

The paper is technically careful: it uses well-known, publicly-released models (Pythia-70M, Gemma-2-2B) and publicly-released SAEs (Gemma Scope, pythia-70m-deduped-res-sm), making replication straightforward. The extensive appendices (A through K, of which A–E are visible in the PDF) demonstrate methodological rigor. The null check (FP16-vs-FP16 giving exactly 1.000 correlation) is an important sanity check that rules out measurement artifacts.

Connections to the Transformer Circuits / mechanistic interpretability community are clear (Bricken et al. 2023, Templeton et al. 2024, Cunningham et al. 2023, Gemma Scope). The closest prior work (Borobia et al. 2026, studying pruning) is appropriately cited and differentiated. The paper explicitly positions itself as complementary to SAE training variability work (Paulo & Belrose 2025, Chanin & Garriga-Alonso 2025), correctly noting the SAE is held fixed so that source is not confounded.

**Credibility assessment:** The empirical methodology is solid for a single-author preprint. The scope is limited (two model families, one SAE per model, one compression scheme), and the paper states this honestly. No claims beyond what the data support.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate to good. The contribution is primarily empirical — applying a fixed-basis measurement approach to quantization rather than pruning. This is a natural extension of Borobia et al. (2026), but the distinction (weight rounding vs. weight removal, per-feature correlation vs. SAE retraining) is methodologically meaningful. The fragility predictor and the perplexity-dissociation demonstration are the most novel findings.

**Rigor:** Good. The experimental design is clean (frozen SAE, identical tokens, controlled comparison). Stability checks are thorough. The null check is exemplary. The sliding-window perplexity robustness check eliminates one alternative explanation for the INT7 anomaly.

**Reproducibility:** High. All models and SAEs are publicly released. The compression scheme (per-output-channel RTN) is described precisely with formulas. Token budget and layer choices are specified.

**Significance:** The safety implication — that a quantized model can be behaviorally indistinguishable from the full-precision model while having fundamentally different interpretable features — is genuinely important for AI safety and deployment auditing. The practical upshot is a concrete, actionable argument for feature-level audits of compressed models.

**Red flags / limitations:**
1. **Single SAE per model, single read-out layer.** Feature-survival rates will vary by layer, SAE training recipe, and sparsity level. The paper tests layer sensitivity only for Pythia and only sweeps two layers. The specific survival percentages should not be taken as universal rates.
2. **RTN only.** The paper explicitly excludes GPTQ and AWQ (more sophisticated quantization methods common in practice). Whether the findings transfer to production quantizers is unknown — and the authors say so, but it is a real gap.
3. **No causal validation.** The paper measures geometric feature survival (correlation), not functional impact on downstream interventions, safety audits, or steering. A feature with c_j = 0.6 is measured as "degraded" but the actual steering or auditing error caused by this degradation is unquantified. The paper acknowledges this as future work, but it is the most important open question.
4. **Solo-author preprint, not peer-reviewed.** More extensive peer scrutiny would be welcome.
5. **The INT7 perplexity improvement** remains mechanistically unexplained. The authors offer a "mild regularization" hypothesis but explicitly do not establish this. This is an honest acknowledgment but leaves the most surprising result without a mechanism.

## Strengths

- **Clean experimental design.** Frozen SAE as measurement basis elegantly separates compression effects from SAE training variability. This is the right way to ask the question.
- **Honest and precise presentation.** The paper does not overclaim. The limitations section is accurate and specific. The dissociation between perplexity and feature survival is presented with appropriate caution ("we do not establish the mechanism").
- **Practical implications are concrete.** The paper directly motivates feature-level audits of compression and provides a reusable pipeline (QDM) for doing so.
- **Strong stability validation.** Token-budget sensitivity, seed stability, null check, and layer sensitivity together make the core empirical claim robust.
- **The Jaccard/Spearman overlap finding is insightful.** Showing that quantization and pruning damage the same features — despite mechanically different operations — is a theoretically interesting result pointing toward a unified view of compression-induced vulnerability.
- **Fragility predictor is actionable.** AUC 0.92–0.97 from four simple statistics means practitioners can pre-screen which features to worry about before deployment.

## Limitations

- Only two model families (Pythia-70M, Gemma-2-2B) with publicly available residual-stream SAEs — the generalization to other architectures, SAE types, or activation sites is not established.
- RTN quantization only — does not cover GPTQ, AWQ, or mixed-precision quantizers used in production.
- No causal validation: feature-correlation survival does not directly measure whether steering vectors, safety audits, or feature-targeted interventions remain effective in the quantized model.
- Single SAE per model per layer; different sparsity levels or SAE training recipes may yield different survival rates.
- The INT7 perplexity improvement mechanism is unexplained — the "mild regularization" interpretation is speculative.
- Per-feature survival rates are specific to the evaluated token sets and should not be treated as universal constants across corpora.
- Fixed token budgets and specific compression configurations mean quantitative survival percentages are not directly comparable to deployment quantizers.

## Relevance

本文对AI安全和模型可解释性研究具有直接价值：量化是LLM部署的标配手段，而本文首次系统性地证明了"困惑度不变"不等于"可解释特征不变"，为压缩后模型的安全审计提供了重要动机和可复现的测量框架（QDM）。对于使用SAE做特征steering或安全干预的研究者，这是一个不可忽视的警示。

This paper directly concerns AI safety practitioners and interpretability researchers who build safety audits or steering interventions on top of SAE features extracted from full-precision models — a common workflow that this paper shows is not automatically valid for quantized deployments. The QDM pipeline is immediately reusable.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Causal validation:** Do the degraded features (0.5 < c_j < 0.9) actually cause failures in downstream steering or safety interventions? How much feature correlation loss is tolerable before a steering vector misfires?
2. **Production quantizers:** Do GPTQ and AWQ — which minimize quantization error more carefully than RTN — substantially improve feature survival, and does the perplexity dissociation persist?
3. **Mixed-precision strategies:** Can the fragility predictor (peak activation as strongest predictor) be used to assign higher bit-widths to fragile features? Is mixed-precision quantization that targets feature-survival a feasible deployment strategy?
4. **Mechanism of INT7 perplexity improvement:** Is this a genuine regularization effect, a corpus-specific artifact, or something about the Gemma architecture? Does it generalize to other models?
5. **Layer generalization:** The paper tests one (Pythia) or two (Gemma) layers. How does feature survival vary across early vs. late layers, and is there a layer-type interaction (MLP-dominant vs. attention-dominant layers)?
6. **SAE training recipe sensitivity:** Do SAEs trained with different sparsity levels, different architectures (e.g., TopK vs. L1), or different training corpora show different feature survival rates under the same quantization?
7. **Steering experiment:** Can one demonstrate a concrete steering intervention (e.g., refusal steering, concept suppression) that works on FP16 but fails at INT6 — quantifying the practical cost of feature degradation?
