> **一句话总结：** 自回归一致性（模型倾向于沿当前轨迹延续生成）从机理上解释了为什么安全对齐天然浅薄，并揭示了一类更广泛的攻击：只需在安全拒绝轨迹的任意位置插入短暂有害片段，即可劫持后续生成。

> **TL;DR:** Autoregressive consistency — the tendency of next-token prediction to preserve and extend the current response trajectory — mechanistically explains why safety fine-tuning concentrates on early refusal tokens, leaving late-position generation untouched. This same property enables a broader attack class: inserting a short harmful span anywhere inside an otherwise safe refusal trajectory can redirect the model onto a harmful branch that it then extends consistently, bypassing safety alignment even with a long prior refusal prefix.

---

## 核心贡献

1. **机理解释（理论）：** 通过分析SFT和DPO的学习动力学，证明自回归一致性使得安全对齐的梯度信号集中在序列早期（拒绝关键词位置），而后期 token 的梯度贡献被抑制至 O(√ε)——这从理论层面解释了浅层安全对齐的成因（Theorem 2.1）。

2. **攻击推广（理论+实证）：** 将已有的 prefill 攻击泛化为"有害续写可在轨迹任意位置触发"，提出**随机插入攻击（Random Insertion Attack）**：在安全拒绝响应的随机位置插入短暂有害片段，利用自回归一致性让模型持续延伸有害分支。

3. **对抗性安全对齐框架（防御）：** 提出以"最坏插入位置的有害续写状态"为内层对抗问题、以恢复拒绝轨迹为外层目标的对抗性安全对齐框架，并以随机最坏插入训练（Random Worst-Insertion Training）作为可落地的近似。

---

## Key Contributions

1. **Mechanistic theory of shallow alignment:** By analyzing gradient dynamics of SFT and DPO, the authors formally prove (Theorem 2.1) that autoregressive consistency suppresses gradient contributions at late token positions to O(√ε), causing alignment updates to concentrate on early refusal tokens — providing the first learning-dynamics explanation for why safety alignment is inherently shallow.

2. **Generalized attack surface:** The same mechanism that makes alignment shallow also enables attacks at arbitrary trajectory positions. The paper introduces the **random insertion attack**: inject a short harmful span (h_q, as few as 10–25 tokens) at a random position within an otherwise-safe refusal response, then let the model continue — autoregressive consistency sustains the harmful branch, achieving >75% Attack Success Rate (ASR) on Llama-2-7b-Chat and Gemma-2-9b-it even when the refusal prefix is long.

3. **Adversarial safety alignment (defense framework):** A minimax training objective that (inner problem) identifies the worst-case harmful continuation state along the refusal trajectory and (outer problem) trains the model to recover safe refusal from that state. The practical instantiation — **random worst-insertion training** — selects the most adversarial insertion point among k=32 random candidates per example.

## Methodology

### Formalism: Autoregressive Consistency

**Definition 2.1** formalizes autoregressive consistency: the base model p_θ_base has autoregressive consistency on D_data if, for every sequence, there exists a critical position t_c such that for all t > t_c, p_θ_base(x_{t+1} | x_{:t}) > 1 − ε. This is empirically validated on Llama-2-7B and Llama-3.1-8B (Figure 3): next-token probability for trajectory-consistent continuations sharply rises after the first few positions and stabilizes well above 0.5 for both refusal and harmful continuations.

### Theoretical Results

- **Lemma 1:** Under autoregressive consistency, the softmax Jacobian satisfies ||A_t||_F² < 2ε for t > t_c, bounding how sensitively the conditional distribution changes with model parameters at late positions.

- **Proposition 2.1:** The parameter gradient of the conditional probability at late positions is bounded by C(2ε)^{1/2} — independent of the specific alignment objective.

- **Proposition 2.2:** For both SFT and DPO, dominant gradient contributions at initialization come from early positions t ≤ t_c; late-position terms are suppressed.

- **Theorem 2.1:** Under multi-step gradient descent, a weakened form of autoregressive consistency persists throughout training, with ε_k growing slowly. The aligned model therefore differs from the base model only at early positions, formally recovering the shallow alignment phenomenon of Qi et al. (2025).

### Random Insertion Attack

Given a triplet (x, r, h) — harmful prompt, refusal trajectory, harmful response — the attack samples a position i uniformly from |r| and constructs the perturbed state τ̃_{i+q} = (x, [r_{:i}; h_{:q}]). The model then continues from this forced harmful autoregressive state. Even with a refusal prefix of many tokens, 10–25 inserted harmful tokens can redirect generation (Figure 4).

### Adversarial Safety Alignment Framework

**Formal objective (Eq. 12):**
min_θ E_{(x,r)~D} [L_SFT(r, τ*_θ(x,r); θ)]   s.t.   τ*_θ ∈ argmax_{τ_i ∈ A_θ(x,r)} Φ_θ(τ_i)

The **harmful autoregressive consistency margin** Φ_θ(τ_t) = H(τ_t; θ) − R(τ_t; θ) measures how strongly the model favors harmful continuation over refusal recovery from state τ_t, where H scores harmful continuation probability and R scores best available refusal restart.

**Practical instantiation (Algorithm 1, Random Worst-Insertion):** For each training triplet, sample k=32 random insertion positions, compute Φ_θ for each candidate state using a refusal bank R of m=4 short refusal continuations, select the worst state, and minimize the refusal-recovery SFT loss conditioned on that state, combined with a utility loss on benign instructions (λ=0.5).

## Results & Highlights

### Main Results (Table 1 — Llama-2-7b-Chat, ASR ↓)

| Method | Random Insertion (25/50/75/100 tok) | Prefill (25/50/75/100 tok) |
|---|---|---|
| Clean | 76.6 / 80.4 / 83.6 / 85.8 | 75.3 / 80.4 / 81.6 / 84.3 |
| Prefill | 18.4 / 22.3 / 27.6 / 34.3 | **3.8 / 9.0 / 13.2 / 20.9** |
| Insertion (Ours) | **2.7 / 3.4 / 4.8 / 5.9** | 6.5 / 11.0 / 13.5 / 21.2 |

Key findings:
- **Clean** baseline is highly vulnerable to both attacks (>75% ASR), confirming standard alignment's fragility.
- **Prefill** (deep alignment) sharply reduces ASR on prefill attacks but transfers poorly to random insertion attacks (>18% ASR even at 25-token span).
- **Insertion** achieves the lowest ASR on random insertion attacks while remaining competitive on prefill attacks — demonstrating that targeting the underlying autoregressive consistency mechanism is more comprehensive.

### Generalization (Table 2 — GCG, PAIR, TAP, AutoDAN)

Adversarial safety alignment (Insertion) reduces ASR on GCG from 34.5% to 2.5%, PAIR from 7.5% to 1.0%, TAP from 5.5% to 1.0%, and AutoDAN from 0.5% to 0.0% — showing that robustness generalizes beyond the specific training attack.

### Cross-Model Validation (Table 3)

Results on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct and Gemma-2-9b-it confirm the same pattern: Insertion substantially reduces random insertion attack ASR (e.g., Llama-3.1-8B: 18.3→1.9% at 25 tokens) while remaining competitive on prefill attacks.

## Critical Assessment

The paper's core theoretical contribution — a gradient-dynamics proof that autoregressive consistency causes shallow alignment — is the strongest part. The Theorem 2.1 derivation (Appendix B.4) is technically sound and provides the clearest mechanistic account to date for a known empirical phenomenon. The random insertion attack is intentionally simple and serves well as a proof-of-concept demonstration.

The adversarial alignment framework is conceptually well-motivated (minimax over trajectory-level adversarial states) but the practical instantiation is an approximation with several free parameters (k, m, λ, q) chosen heuristically. The empirical gains are convincing on the tested models and benchmarks, but the scale of experiments is modest. The paper explicitly acknowledges this, framing the work as identifying a failure mechanism and motivating future research rather than delivering a production-ready defense system.

One conceptual tension: the paper argues that "depth" (Prefill method) is incomplete, but Insertion also achieves robustness partly by effectively training on harder adversarial states, not solely by "breaking harmful autoregressive consistency" in a deep theoretical sense. The distinction between the mechanism targeted and the practical training procedure could be clearer.

---

## Strengths

- **Theoretical grounding:** First formal learning-dynamics proof that autoregressive consistency causes gradient concentration and thus shallow alignment, covering both SFT and DPO objectives.
- **Novel attack perspective:** Reframes the attack surface from "prefix targeting" to "trajectory-level harmful continuation states," unifying prefill attacks as a special case.
- **Conceptually clean proposed defense:** The minimax formulation is principled and directly motivated by the identified failure mechanism.
- **Strong empirical results:** Insertion method dramatically reduces ASR under novel random insertion attacks while remaining competitive on existing attack types and not sacrificing general utility.
- **Honest framing:** Authors are explicit that this is an initial framework, not a final defense, and that the theory rests on simplifying assumptions (near-deterministic autoregressive consistency).

---

## Limitations

- **Scale of experiments:** Only 256 training examples (from Qi et al.'s dataset), evaluated on 330-item HEx-PHI benchmark. Results may not hold at larger data scales or for frontier closed-source models.
- **Idealized theoretical assumptions:** Assumption 2.1 requires near-deterministic autoregressive consistency (probability > 1 − ε for small ε). The paper notes this is a soft empirical analogy, not a literal property — the theory is an approximation.
- **Approximation gap in defense:** The practical random worst-insertion training is a coarse approximation of the minimax objective. Solving the inner problem exactly is intractable, and the approximation quality is not formally characterized.
- **No evaluation on frontier models:** All experiments use open-weight models (Llama-2-7B, Llama-3.1-8B, Gemma-2-9B). Applicability to GPT-4-class models is unknown.
- **Utility evaluation is limited:** Only Alpaca-based utility preservation is checked; downstream task performance (e.g., MMLU, HumanEval) is not evaluated.
- **Random insertion attack is weak:** By design, the attack is simple and not optimized. A stronger adversary who optimizes insertion position and harmful content could achieve higher ASR against the defended model.

## Relevance

**High relevance** for researchers working on:

- Understanding why RLHF/DPO/SFT produce fragile alignment
- Designing alignment objectives that go beyond token-prefix coverage

**Moderate relevance** for:
- Researchers in autoregressive generation theory (connection between next-token entropy and gradient dynamics)
- Practitioners deploying safety-critical LLM systems who need to understand the limits of standard alignment

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Optimal inner problem:** Can the worst-case harmful continuation state be identified more efficiently — e.g., via gradient-based search within the output trajectory rather than random sampling? Would this substantially close the gap between the theoretical framework and the practical approximation?

2. **Interaction with RLHF reward models:** Does autoregressive consistency similarly concentrate reward model signals during PPO-based alignment? If so, does the same gradient-concentration theorem apply, and would worst-insertion training during PPO also help?

3. **Adaptive attacks:** An attacker aware of the Insertion defense could optimize insertion position and harmful span content jointly (analogous to GCG for prefill). How robust is the defended model to such an adaptive adversary?

4. **Depth vs. trajectory robustness trade-off:** The paper shows Prefill reduces prefill ASR more than Insertion does. Is there a pareto-optimal training procedure that targets both deep refusal and trajectory-level harmful consistency breaking simultaneously?

5. **Connection to representation geometry:** Arditi et al. (2024) showed refusal is mediated by a one-dimensional direction, and Wollschläger et al. (2026) found higher-dimensional concept cones. How does the autoregressive consistency failure mode manifest in representation space — does a harmful insertion shift the residual stream onto the "harmful" direction in a way that then locks in via consistency?
