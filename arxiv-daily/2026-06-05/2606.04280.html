Key Finding

The loss alone is not enough — violated sampling diversity makes InfoNCE actively reward geometry-distorting encoders, regardless of data volume or model capacity

The Loss Is Not Enough: Sampling Conditions and Inductive Bias in Contrastive Representation Learning ↗ ICML 2026
Key Metric
R² 0.05 → 1.00
Linear identifiability collapse under violated diversity (R²=0.05) vs. perfect recovery under full diversity (R²=1.00). High-inductive-bias encoders recover R²≥0.88 even when diversity fails.
Why This Matters
Augmentation design is now a formal math requirement — not a heuristic
The diversity condition tells you what an augmentation must do (perturb all latent factors) rather than which specific transforms to apply. Directly applicable to scientific ML where default image augmentations don't apply.
Critical Edge
Spherical latent space only; diversity condition not estimable from data
Full theory assumes Z = S^{k-1} with vMF conditionals. Adapted InfoNCE requires anchor-specific negatives — not scalable in practice. No estimator for whether diversity condition holds from finite samples.
Methodology

Diversity condition. Formally: P_Z ≪ P_{Z̃|z} (absolute continuity of positive-pair conditional w.r.t. marginal). Intuitively: every latent region must be reachable from almost every anchor. Full-support von Mises-Fisher sampling satisfies this by construction; augmentations that fix latent coordinates (e.g., color-only) collapse support to a lower-dimensional submanifold and violate it.

Theorem 4.4 — Full Diversity

Any global InfoNCE minimizer is an isometry (distance-preserving) almost everywhere.

Theorem 4.5 — Violated Diversity

There exists a non-orthogonal map h_λ that achieves strictly lower asymptotic InfoNCE loss than any orthogonal map — by shrinking the invariant component to improve alignment without destroying uniformity.

Theorem 4.6 — Corrected InfoNCE

Restricting negatives to the anchor's constrained submanifold K(z) makes isometric embeddings achievable as optima — though not uniquely, so inductive bias remains necessary for selection.

CIFAR-10. Performance gap between ResNet-18 and MLP widens as augmentation diversity is removed ("All" → "All w/o crop" → "Crop only"). MLP with full augmentations performs identically to MLP with crop-only — confirming architectural prior is necessary, not additional data diversity.

Strengths / Limitations
Strengths
  • Constructive negative proof: explicit family of geometry-distorting maps h_λ shows how the loss fails, not just that it does
  • Diversity condition stated at the latent distribution level — applies across modalities (image, language, genomics)
  • Explains why CNN/ResNet architectures consistently outperform MLPs in contrastive learning with restricted augmentations
  • No differentiability required — Mazur-Ulam-based proof is broadly applicable
  • Clean experimental design: 5 generative processes × 2 encoder classes in fully controlled synthetic setting
Limitations
  • Entire theory assumes Z = S^{k-1} with vMF conditionals — non-spherical extension is future work
  • Adapted InfoNCE requires anchor-specific negatives from K(z) — requires knowing the invariant component, unavailable in practice
  • No practical estimator for whether diversity condition holds from finite data
  • Corrected objective achieves isometry as optimum but not uniquely — inductive bias selection mechanism not characterized
  • CIFAR-10 results are qualitative sanity checks, not SOTA comparisons
Open Questions
  • Can the diversity condition be estimated from finite samples — is there a practical coverage test for P_{Z̃|z} vs. P_Z?
  • What inductive biases are sufficient (not just helpful) to select isometric solutions under violated diversity?
  • In CLIP-style frameworks, does the image-text co-occurrence sampling satisfy the diversity condition — and does synthetic pairing data change this?
  • Does the framework extend to VICReg / Barlow Twins (variance-covariance regularization) — is diversity still necessary under non-contrastive losses?
