Neuron Populations Exhibit Divergent Selectivity with Scale

Shared "Rosetta Neurons" grow sublinearly across model scales (power law α ≈ 0.5–0.7, R² ≈ 0.99) while becoming sharply monosemantic — a polarization from polysemantic neighbors that can be harnessed as near-oracle domain filters.

Amil Dravid, Yasaman Bahri, Alexei A. Efros, Yossi Gandelsman  ·  UC Berkeley / TTIC  ·  Preprint June 2, 2026
Key Metric
0.98
F1 score for domain data filtering using a single Rosetta Neuron (JavaScript detection). Oracle F1 = 1.00. Downstream perplexity: 3.02 vs. Oracle 3.01.
Why This Matters
Larger models grow a sharper interpretable frontier — and those neurons can filter domain data with zero labels.
Bridges macroscopic scaling laws to microscopic neuron organization; practical for continued pretraining curation.
Critical Edge
MLP neurons only — attention heads and circuit-level structure excluded. The analytical model explains that polarization occurs, not how gradient descent produces it.
Single-domain (JavaScript) filtering experiment limits generalizability claims.
Methodology
01
Rosetta Neuron identification — Pearson correlation of MLP neuron activations across a shared dataset (~10M tokens / 50K images). Mutual nearest-neighbor (MNN) filter retains only symmetric matches between model pairs.
02
Scaling curves — Language: Pythia, GPT-2, OPT, Qwen-2.5 (80M–30B). Vision: OpenCLIP, DINOv2, Pixio, DiT (80M–5B). Fit |R| = c·Nᵅ in log-log space; untrained random networks serve as null.
03
Analytical model (superposition) — Capacity-allocation optimization over feature importance power spectrum wᵣ ∝ r⁻ᵝ. Derives sublinear exponent α = 1/β (implying β ∈ 1.4–2.0 from empirical α ∈ 0.5–0.7).
04
Selectivity measurement — Language: excess kurtosis of vocabulary-projection cosines. Vision: VLM-judged monosemanticity rate over top-20 activating images. Trends measured across model sizes.
05
Data filtering case study — CodeSearchNet multilingual corpus (58K functions). A single JS-selective Rosetta Neuron from Pythia-6.9B selects data; GPT2-1.5B continued pretraining evaluates quality.

Strengths & Limitations
Strengths
+R² ≈ 0.99 across 4 language + 5 vision family pairs — unusually consistent breadth
+Clean null: untrained networks show no scaling (20–30 Rosetta Neurons regardless of size)
+Theory-experiment alignment: α = 1/β derived from first principles, matches empirical range
+Concrete application: zero-label domain filtering near oracle quality
+Bridges macro scaling laws to microscopic neuron organization — novel dimension
Limitations
−MLP neurons only; attention heads and residual-stream circuits excluded entirely
−Single domain (JS) filtering experiment; fuzzy or compositional domains untested
−Selectivity proxies (kurtosis, VLM judgment) are modality-specific, not directly comparable
−No mechanistic account of how gradient descent produces the capacity-allocation equilibrium
−CNN, SSM, and non-Transformer architectures not tested
Open Questions
Circuit-level analog Do "Rosetta Circuits" follow the same sublinear power law? How does neuron-level universality relate to circuit-level universality?
Post-training dynamics Does RLHF or instruction tuning preferentially modify Rosetta Neurons or non-Rosetta neurons?
Emergence during training Do Rosetta Neurons emerge suddenly (grokking-like) or gradually? Does polarization appear early or late in training?
Exponent variation What determines α across model family pairs? Does β differ by architecture, training objective, or data distribution?
Platonic connection Are Rosetta Neurons the neuron-level manifestation of the Platonic Representation Hypothesis — converging representations across independently trained models?
