Key Finding
KGEMs look stable on MRR, but one changed seed replaces ~40% of top-1 predictions — the evaluation protocol is systematically hiding the instability.
Link Prediction or Perdition: the Seeds of Instability in Knowledge Graph Embeddings
Méroué · Gandon · Monnin  ·  Inria / Université Côte d'Azur  ·  2026-06-05
Key Metric
0.43
RotatE FB15k-237 Pred-Overlap@10 — only 4.3 of 10 predictions shared across runs
Why This Matters
KG completions drive drug discovery and legal reasoning — if predictions are random, decisions are random.
Critical Edge
No fix proposed. GPU model alone causes instability equal to seed change. Voting only partially helps.
Methodology

Isolating Randomness Sources

Existing frameworks (PyKEEN, LibKGE) bundle all stochastic factors into one seed. The authors built a custom PyTorch library to isolate four independent sources:

  • I — Parameter initialization
  • O — Triple ordering
  • N — Negative sampling
  • D — Dropout

New Stability Metrics

Pred-Overlap@K(I₁, I₂) = (1/|Q|) Σ_q |ν(I₁,q,K) ∩ ν(I₂,q,K)| / K Space-Overlap@K(I₁, I₂) = (1/|E|) Σ_e |N_K^E₁(e) ∩ N_K^E₂(e)| / K

These measure whether two identically-configured model runs would add the same triples to the completed KG — not just whether they rank the gold entity highly.

Experimental Scale

7 model families × 3 datasets × 5 seeds × 4 randomness sources × 5 GPU types. Datasets: WN18RR, FB15k-237, CoDEx-S.

~25% of RotatE queries on FB15k-237 have Consistency@10 = 0 — no entity appears in top-10 across all 5 runs.

MRR is deceptive

TransE on WN18RR: MRR = 0.194 ± 0.002. Looks stable. But Pred-Overlap@1 is ≤ 0.6 for most models — very different completions, same aggregate score.

GPU type matters

Hardware variation (RTX 2080 Ti vs. A100 vs. Tesla T4) produces instability of the same order of magnitude as seed variation — a cloud-compute warning.

Strengths
  • Controlled isolation: Each randomness source tested independently — requires a custom library since existing frameworks can't do this.
  • Beyond gold-truth metrics: Pred-Overlap and Space-Overlap capture disagreement even when both runs correctly rank the gold entity.
  • GPU instability discovery: Hardware-induced instability on par with seed variation is a novel, practically important finding.
  • Consistent across architectures: Core instability holds across all 7 model families and 3 datasets.
  • Full reproducibility: Code, configs, GPU details released at github.com/Wimmics/LinkPerdition.
  • Credible team: Fabien Gandon (Inria senior researcher), Grid'5000 compute, ANR funding.
Limitations
  • No fix proposed: Pure diagnostic study — stability-aware training, regularization, etc., are mentioned but not developed.
  • TransE stability unexplained: Consistently more stable than other architectures but the architectural reason is not analyzed — understanding this could unlock stable design.
  • Voting is superficial: Only Borda and Range vote tested; other ensemble or uncertainty-aware approaches ignored.
  • Standard benchmarks only: WN18RR/FB15k-237 are small; behavior at industrial-scale KGs (millions of entities) is unknown.
  • No downstream validation: The claim that instability harms drug discovery etc. is argued by analogy, not demonstrated on a real task.
Core tension: The paper proves instability is severe and pervasive, then stops. The practical question — "so what do I do?" — is left entirely open. This is an honest framing for a diagnostic study, but the gap is real.
Questions for Exploration
Why is TransE more stable? Geometric simplicity of the translation scoring function, lower expressivity, or a more benign loss landscape?
Can stability be explicitly trained? Could a regularization term penalizing top-K variance across dropout masks improve stability without sacrificing MRR?
Does instability scale with KG size? More entities = more redundant signal (more stable) or higher-dimensional embedding space (less stable)?
What is the downstream impact? If two same-MRR models produce different completed KGs, how different are drug-disease association results?
Are unstable queries structurally identifiable? The ~25% Consistency@10 = 0 queries — are they systematically low-degree entities or rare relations?
