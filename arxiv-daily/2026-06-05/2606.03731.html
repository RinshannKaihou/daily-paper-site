Key Finding

Shift hallucination control upstream into the generative distribution — posterior sampling beats post-hoc filtering at the same statistical guarantee

Conformal Language Modeling via Posterior Sampling ↗
Prior approach
Post-hoc filtering
Generate normally → remove low-confidence claims → return incomplete, possibly empty response. Cannot shift probability mass — only hides outputs already generated.
→
This work
Posterior sampling
Calibrate a family of posteriors indexed by threshold τ. At inference, sample from the posterior conditioned on outputs exceeding the factuality score τ̂. Same statistical guarantee, better completeness.
Key Metric
Same guarantee, more complete
On MATH: non-abstaining completeness increases with target factuality (more complete correct proofs). On FActScore: non-abstaining Likert degrades less sharply than MH filtering baseline at all targets.
Why This Matters
Factuality control that doesn't destroy utility is a deployment prerequisite for high-stakes LLM applications
Medical, legal, and scientific generation cannot tolerate hallucinations or empty refusals. Posterior sampling offers a principled tradeoff with formal guarantees.
Critical Edge
20 particles + GPT-4o scorer per prompt; abstention spikes at strict targets
Computationally expensive. Particle-based posterior is coarse when high-scoring completions are rare. β sensitivity underexplored. Depends on closed-source GPT-4o scorer.
Methodology

Posterior family. Augment LLM prior p_LLM with potential: Φ^τ(y|x) = 1{∏ᵢ s(cᵢ|x,c_{<i}) ≥ τ} — a threshold on the product of per-claim factuality scores. This defines a one-parameter family of posteriors indexed by τ ∈ [0,1].

Abstention atom. Mixture prior p_β = (1−β)p_LLM + β·δ_abstain ensures Z^τ is bounded away from zero, keeping the posterior well-defined even when the LLM rarely produces high-scoring completions.

Off-policy calibration. For each calibration prompt, sample M=20 particles from the base model. Use importance reweighting to estimate per-prompt empirical risk Ĥ_i(τ). Apply running supremum monotonization H̄_i(τ) = sup_{τ'≥τ} Ĥ_i(τ'). Select τ̂ via standard conformal risk control (finite-sample guarantee: Theorem 3.2).

Deployment. Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct (4-bit quantized), single A6000 48GB. GPT-4o as factuality scorer (0–1 per claim). Tested on FActScore (682 biographies) and MATH (500 problems).

Results
  • Validity. Both methods achieve valid coverage (empirical risk ≤ target α) at all factuality levels — the guarantee holds
  • MATH completeness (non-abstaining). Proposed method completeness increases with target factuality; MH completeness monotonically decreases above 0.6 — the key qualitative difference
  • FActScore Likert (non-abstaining). Proposed method degrades less sharply as target increases — driven by better completeness and helpfulness preservation
  • Unconditional Likert. MH wins here — LLM judge prefers fluent refusals over partial answers. Authors correctly flag this as LLM-judge preference, not user utility
  • Claim count. Conditioned on non-abstention, proposed method emits more claims at intermediate targets; MH concentrates filtering on hard prompts
  • Claim precision. Similar across methods — the gain is in quantity of verifiably correct claims, not precision
Strengths / Limitations
Strengths
  • Conceptually clean: post-hoc filtering cannot shift probability mass — posterior sampling is the correct abstraction
  • Off-policy calibration avoids on-policy sampling per τ — tractable with only base-model particles
  • Finite-sample guarantee (Theorem 3.2) + asymptotic consistency (Corollary 3.4, Glivenko-Cantelli)
  • Honest about limitations: transparent where method underperforms (unconditional LLM judge evaluation)
  • Tested on both open-ended generation and structured reasoning (MATH)
Limitations
  • M=20 particles is coarse — posterior approximation unreliable when high-scoring completions are rare; no SMC demonstrated
  • GPT-4o as scorer: closed-source, paid, latency-inducing — deployment bottleneck
  • Sentence-splitting as claim decomposition is coarser than atomic claim extraction
  • β hyperparameter sensitivity underexplored in main paper
  • At strict targets, abstention rate rises — conditional utility gains are meaningful but unconditional user experience may worsen
Open Questions
  • Can Sequential Monte Carlo steer the posterior sampler, and does it preserve Theorem 3.2's finite-sample guarantee?
  • Can the GPT-4o scorer be replaced by a learned reward model or self-consistency score — removing the closed-source dependency?
  • Could β be calibrated jointly with τ to optimize the utility-abstention tradeoff for a specific deployment context?
  • For strongly interdependent claims (multi-step math proofs), does the product-of-scores potential Φ^τ remain a valid factuality proxy?
