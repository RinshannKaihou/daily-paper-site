L2 weight decay is geometrically inert in RMSNorm Transformers — nuclear-norm regularization unlocks grokking at half the data

Once a scale-invariant Transformer memorizes its training set, L2 decay's gradient is purely radial — it shrinks norms but cannot change function behavior. Low-Rank Decay substitutes the nuclear norm, whose polar-factor subgradient stays tangential, continuously reshaping the singular value spectrum toward low-rank generalizing solutions.

Methodology

Why L2 Fails, Why LRD Works

L2 gradient under scale invariance
∇‖W‖²_F = 2W → purely radial
After memorization: task grad ≈ 0,
only radial force remains → no functional change
LRD nuclear norm penalty
‖W‖_* = Σ σ_i
Subgradient = UV^T (polar factor)
Effect on σ_i: σ_i ← σ_i − ηλ (subtractive)
Tangential → reshapes spectrum after grad vanishes
  • Newton–Schulz approximation: X_{k+1} = ½X(3I − X^TX) — avoids per-step SVD overhead
  • Decoupled update: Applied post-optimizer on attention and MLP weight matrices only
  • Target task: Modular addition (a+b) mod 97 with 2-layer Pre-RMSNorm + QK-Norm Transformer

Strengths & Limitations

Conceptual Strengths

  • Proposition 1 is a clean, falsifiable claim: Frobenius decay is radial → inert for scale-invariant layers after memorization
  • Newton–Schulz polar factor approximation: no SVD overhead, easy to drop into any training loop
  • Phase diagrams sweep full (data fraction, λ) grid — not cherry-picked single settings
  • Paper's own §7.3 is unusually candid about seed variance and unestablished causality

Scope Limitations

Critical edge — preliminary draft

Single toy task (modular addition, p=97), single model size, single random seed. No confidence intervals. Rank collapse is correlated with generalization but causality unestablished. Theory assumes exact scale invariance and continuous gradient flow — neither holds exactly with AdamW. Labeled "Preliminary Draft" by the author.

Open Questions

Further Exploration

  1. Does forcibly collapsing QK rank mid-training (SVD truncation) accelerate grokking comparably? This would test causality directly.
  2. With SGD (weaker implicit spectral bias than Adam), does LRD's advantage over L2 grow or change character?
  3. Does LRD help on modular multiplication or non-algorithmic tasks (sentiment, translation) where grokking circuits differ?
  4. In full-rank fine-tuning settings, does LRD converge to a low-rank solution comparable to LoRA — without fixing r in advance?
  5. At larger model sizes (d=1024, 12 layers), does the scale-invariant failure mode of L2 persist under standard pretraining signals?
