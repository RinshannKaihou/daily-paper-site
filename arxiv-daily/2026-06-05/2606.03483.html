Key Finding — 2606.03483 · 2026-06-05 · ICML 2026 Workshop

Hyper-Connection multi-stream residuals collapse to one dominant stream; a trivial nd-parameter fix consistently improves perplexity

arxiv.org/abs/2606.03483 ↗ Alimaskina, Molodtsov, Beznosikov · MIRAI Moscow / BRAIn Lab / Yandex Research
Key Metric
47.92
WT test perplexity with LSS (mHC-lite M, T/P=10) — vs 51.05 baseline. Lower is better.
Why This Matters
HC models waste their multi-stream capacity through a permutation-symmetry initialization flaw. A fix requiring only nd extra parameters — zero extra matmuls — recovers measurable performance across all tested budgets.
Critical Edge
All experiments at nanoGPT scale (0.12B–0.36B, up to 5.2B tokens). Collapse dynamics and LSS benefit at production scale (7B+, trillions of tokens) are entirely unverified. Workshop paper — 5 pages + appendix.
Methodology — Five Convergent Probes
1. Residual Mixing Matrices (H_ℓ^res)
Early layers show off-diagonal mass (real cross-stream exchange). By deep layers, H^res converges to near-identity — the multi-stream design effectively disabled. Holds for n∈{2,4,8,16}.
2. Read/Write Routing Share
One stream (s₂) dominates both H^pre (read) and H^post (write). Other streams receive negligible signal — routing concentrates in a single pathway.
3. Representation L₂ Norm
Dominant stream accumulates markedly higher norm across depth via self-reinforcing loop: large norm → more input contribution → larger increments. LSS visibly reduces this imbalance.
4. Token-Trajectory Curvature
Dominant stream shows smoother (lower curvature) hidden-state trajectory — geometrically preferred representation. Corroborates routing probes.
5. Sparse Crosscoder Feature Attribution
Sparse crosscoder trained on all 4 streams: feature mass concentrates in s₃ (early layers) then transitions to s₂ (layer 4+). Interpretable, feature-level evidence of collapse.
Learned Stream Scaling (LSS) — the fix
x^(s) = x ⊙ h_s, h_s ∈ ℝ^d, s = 1,...,n
Replaces exact stream replication with per-stream near-identity diagonal scaling. Initialized near-uniform to break permutation symmetry while keeping streams approximately aligned. Only nd extra parameters, zero extra matrix multiplications.

Strengths / Limitations
Strengths
  • Five independent probes converge on same phenomenon — far more convincing than any single diagnostic
  • Clean causal story: permutation symmetry → identical init → stochastic advantage → self-reinforcing collapse
  • Identity-mixing ablation directly tests the causal claim
  • LSS: nd parameters, zero extra matmuls, trivially composable with any HC variant
  • Honest about fix being partial — bypass regime persists even with LSS
  • Full hyperparameter table + code released
Limitations
  • Scale ceiling at 0.36B — production-scale generalization entirely unverified
  • nanoGPT only — no rotary embeddings, grouped-query attention, etc.
  • LSS mitigates but does not solve collapse: dominant regime persists at all token budgets tested
  • No downstream task evaluation — perplexity only
  • Workshop paper scope — some analyses are preliminary
  • Crosscoder probe requires training additional models per even layer
Open Questions
  • Q1Does stream collapse occur in larger HC-based models (7B, 70B)? Does the self-reinforcing loop accelerate with scale?
  • Q2Would more aggressive symmetry breaking (orthogonal per-stream projections, diversity regularization) fully realize the multi-stream design intent?
  • Q3Do MoE models exhibit analogous routing collapse? Is permutation-symmetry the general mechanism?
  • Q4What does the dominant stream actually represent semantically? The crosscoder shows where features go, not what they mean.
  • Q5Does instruction fine-tuning or RLHF reinforce or disrupt a collapsed HC structure? Could collapse affect alignment?
  • Q6Could LSS + an auxiliary loss penalizing near-identity H^res produce meaningfully better multi-stream utilization?
