RL Excursions during Pre-Training: Re-examining Policy Optimization for LLM Training

Bansal · Mohri · Qin · Alvarez-Melis · Kakade  —  Harvard University

RL works from as few as 4B training tokens, expands the model distribution when applied directly, and parallel-averaging with SFT achieves best pass@32 while preserving general capabilities.

Key Metric 4B Minimum pre-training tokens at which direct RL yields meaningful gains — GSM8K pass@1 jumps from ~2% to ~18%.
Why This Matters Dismantles the pretrain‑then‑finetune orthodoxy; RL and SFT can be interleaved from training start without sacrificing general capabilities. Data composition beats model scale as the lever for RL effectiveness.
Critical Edge Max 4B params / 60B tokens; math-heavy pretraining mix may artificially lower the RL-effectiveness threshold; parallel avg trades pass@1 for pass@32. Frontier-scale applicability is the key open question.
Methodology

Controlled Pre-Training Checkpoint Study

1B OLMo2-based model trained on DOLMino (50B token mix: 20% FLAN math, 60% DCLM, 7% Wikipedia…). Checkpoints saved at 4, 10, 14, 20, 30, 40, 50B tokens. Four training recipes applied to each:

  • Direct RL: GRPO directly on base checkpoint, binary reward.
  • SFT: Single random ground-truth solution per problem.
  • SFT-Gold: All ~23 ground-truth solutions (upper bound).
  • SFT→RL: Standard sequential pipeline.
  • Parallel: Simultaneous RL + SFT Adam states; average updates from same snapshot.
Parallel averaging: θ ← θ + ½(Δ_RL + Δ_SFT), with independent Adam states for each objective.

Strengths

Why This Holds Up

  • Strict experimental control: same checkpoints, data, and evaluation protocol across all recipes.
  • Rebuts two widely-cited misconceptions: sharpening is SFT artifact; RL does not degrade general capabilities.
  • Parallel averaging is zero-cost in data and near-zero in complexity — only optimizer structure changes.
  • Light-weight predictor: base pass@k monotonically predicts RL gains, useful for resource allocation.
  • Authorship includes top RL-theory expertise (Kakade); methodology transparent.
Limitations

What Remains Open

  • 1B/4B parameter ceiling is far from frontier; conclusions may not hold at 70B+.
  • Math-heavy DOLMino mix may lower the RL-effectiveness threshold vs. typical web-scale pretraining.
  • Only GRPO studied; newer RL variants (entropy-preserving, pass@k-targeted) may interact differently.
  • Parallel averaging sacrifices pass@1 — suboptimal for single-generation applications.
  • All tasks are math reasoning; code, tool-use generalization untested.
Questions for Further Exploration
  • Does the 4B-token RL-effectiveness threshold hold under a pure web-scale pretraining mix with no math augmentation?
  • Can adaptive weighting (dynamic RL/SFT ratio) recover the pass@1 loss of parallel averaging without sacrificing pass@32?
  • Does data composition > model scale persist beyond 10B parameters, or does model capacity become the dominant factor?
  • What mechanism causes SFT to degrade general capabilities? Catastrophic forgetting, distribution shift, or objective interference?
  • Can parallel averaging extend to RL + NTP (next-token prediction) as a joint pre-training objective?
