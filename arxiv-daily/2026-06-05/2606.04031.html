Methodology

Core Idea: Pseudospectra + Kreiss Constant

Standard convergence analysis bounds asymptotic behavior via the spectral radius ρ(J). But coupled gradient descent's Jacobian is non-normal — the coupling matrix C makes ‖Jᵗ‖ grow far larger than ρ(J)ᵗ for intermediate t before convergence kicks in.

The paper imports the Kreiss constant K(J) from numerical linear algebra to measure this transient amplification:

K(J) = sup_{|z|>1} (|z|−1) ‖(zI−J)⁻¹‖ Kreiss theorem: K(J) ≤ sup_t ‖Jᵗ‖ ≤ en · K(J)

For block-triangular Jacobians (A symmetric, ρ(A),ρ(D) ≤ γ < 1), the paper derives a closed-form bound on K(J) via an optimal resolvent radius r*:

r* = γ + √((1−γ)² + ‖C‖) Weak coupling: K(J) ≤ 2/(1−γ) + ‖C‖/4(1−γ)

Perturbative & Stochastic Extension

Full coupling (B ≠ 0) is handled by a Neumann series when ε‖B₀‖K₀ < (1−γ). Stochastic coupled descent achieves δ-precision in:

T = O(K(J)² · log(1/δ) / (1−γ)²) iterations

Pseudospectral bounds are consistently 2–5× tighter than IQC bounds. Neural network GAN experiments confirm peak-time prediction within <10% error.

Strengths
  • Novel bridge: First systematic import of pseudospectra into ML optimization — a natural fit ignored for decades.
  • Closed-form: Algebraic expression in γ and ‖C‖; no semidefinite programming unlike IQC.
  • Tight minimax lower bound: Theorem 7 proves no estimator using (ρ(A), ρ(D), ‖C‖) can do better than Ω(c/(1−γ)²).
  • Non-asymptotic: Theorem 11 is instance-dependent and non-asymptotic — a genuine improvement over classical two-time-scale rates.
  • Scaling law view: As γ → 1⁻ with dimension, K(J) = O(‖C‖/(1−γ)²) explains super-linear sample complexity growth invisible to spectral analysis.
  • NTK connection: Theorem 12 grounds results in the lazy-training / overparameterized regime.
Limitations
  • Symmetry required: A, D must have symmetric Hessians — excludes Adam, momentum, and preconditioned methods.
  • Factor-of-2 gap: Upper bound (Theorem 4) and lower bound (Theorem 5) remain about 2× apart; unresolved.
  • Perturbative B ≠ 0: Full coupling handled only when ε‖B₀‖K₀ < (1−γ) — very restrictive near stability boundary.
  • Local analysis only: Fixed-point linearization; nonlinear effects, saddle escape, and GAN mode collapse are excluded.
  • Kreiss theorem en factor: Worst-case gap between K(M) and sup‖Mᵗ‖ scales with dimension n — may be loose for very large systems.
  • Author credibility: Solo researcher at a secondary school/college; no verifiable publication history; proofs unverified by community.
Critical flag: Every result here assumes the proofs are correct. The mathematical content references established tools (Trefethen & Embree 2005, Kreiss 1962), but independent verification is needed before treating theorems as settled.
Questions for Exploration
Can symmetry assumption on A, D be relaxed? What happens to resolvent bounds for asymmetric Hessians (Adam's preconditioned gradients)?
Is the factor-of-2 gap between upper and lower bounds fundamental, or an artifact of using sum vs. max of diagonal-block resolvent norms?
Can the local linearization be pushed to nonlinear trajectories — especially as GAN parameters wander far from the fixed point?
Do momentum / Adam Jacobians have analogous block structure? Can their Kreiss constants be similarly bounded?
Proposition 22 bounds time-varying Jacobians with a product inequality that may be loose — is a tighter joint pseudospectral analysis achievable?
