> **一句话总结：** 通过系统分析经验粒度（原则级优于实例级）、注入模式（逐步注入优于全局注入）和内化机制（离策略蒸馏优于在策略蒸馏）三个维度，本文提出了一套使 LLM Agent 在多轮自我进化中持续稳定提升的经验内化方案，在 WebWalkerQA、GAIA 和 BrowseComp-ZH 上实现跨迭代的持续性能增长。

> **TL;DR:** Existing experience internalization methods for LLM agents collapse after the first self-evolution iteration. This paper diagnoses the failure through three dimensions — experience granularity, injection pattern, and internalization regime — and proposes a stable recipe (principle-level experience + step-wise injection + off-policy context-distillation) that sustains performance gains across multiple self-evolution rounds on web-reasoning benchmarks.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了现有在策略经验内化方法的关键缺陷：在多轮迭代中性能持续退化（progressive capability collapse），而非累积提升
- 系统分析三个关键维度：(1) **经验粒度**——原则级经验比实例级经验更持久，84% 的原则级条目含可复用决策规则，而实例级只有 3.7%；(2) **注入模式**——逐步注入（step-wise）将经验与当前决策状态对齐，消除了全局注入导致的"提前作答"（premature-answer）问题（全局注入 63.82% 早终止率 vs. 逐步注入 0%）；(3) **内化机制**——离策略蒸馏提供更连贯的经验引导监督信号，避免了在策略蒸馏中学生偏离正常搜索路径后教师无法有效纠正的问题
- 提出三维组合方案，在 Qwen3-4B 和 Qwen3-8B 上实现跨三轮迭代的稳定性能提升，代码和数据已开源

## Key Contributions

- Diagnoses a critical and previously overlooked failure mode: iterative on-policy context-distillation causes progressive performance collapse rather than compound improvement across self-evolution rounds (Figure 1)
- Provides a systematic three-dimension analysis of what makes experience internalization stable: granularity, injection pattern, and internalization regime
- Demonstrates that **principle-level experience** (abstracting reusable strategies/failure patterns) substantially outperforms instance-level experience (preserving trajectory-specific URLs, numbers, entity strings) across multiple iterations
- Demonstrates that **step-wise injection** (selecting experience relevant to the current interaction state via an LLM-based selector) eliminates the premature-answer failure mode caused by global injection, and preserves the model's experience-use ability across iterations
- Demonstrates that **off-policy context-distillation** (training on teacher-generated, experience-guided trajectories filtered by rejection sampling) provides a more coherent supervision signal than on-policy distillation, avoiding trajectory inflation (on-policy student: avg. 21.9 assistant turns vs. teacher: 4.5)
- Code and data released at https://github.com/RUCBM/ExpInternalization

## Methodology

**Problem Setting.** The paper formalizes continual experience internalization as a closed loop: at iteration k, the current policy π_{θ(k)} interacts with the environment to produce trajectories D^(k), which are summarized into an experience pool E^(k) using DeepSeek-V4. The same policy, conditioned on E^(k), acts as an experience-aware teacher to supervise training of the next experience-free student π_{θ(k+1)}.

**Three Dimensions Studied:**

1. **Experience Granularity.** Two levels are compared: instance-level (retaining trajectory-specific details like URLs, numbers, query strings) vs. principle-level (abstracting transferable strategies, decision rules, and failure patterns). In a sampled experience pool, 74.4% of instance-level items contain specific URLs or domains and 93.9% contain query-specific strings — good for in-distribution exploitation but brittle across trajectory distributions. Principle-level items: 84.0% contain reusable strategy-like statements.

2. **Experience Injection Pattern.** Two patterns are studied under on-policy context-distillation:
   - *Global injection*: teacher receives a fixed trajectory-level experience context c^{glob} = [x; E^(k)] for the entire trajectory.
   - *Step-wise injection*: an LLM-based selector R_φ chooses experience relevant to the current interaction history at each step, giving a state-dependent teacher distribution p^{step}_t = π_T(· | h_{t−1}, E^{step}_t).
   Step-wise injection is critical for long-horizon tool-use tasks because experience that helps search planning can be irrelevant or misleading at later states (evidence verification, termination decisions).

3. **Internalization Regime.** Two trajectory distributions for supervision are contrasted:
   - *On-policy context-distillation*: trajectories sampled from the current experience-free student; teacher supervises these student-induced states. Loss: L_{on}(θ) = E_{H∼π_θ} Σ D_KL(q_t || p_t) with reverse KL.
   - *Off-policy context-distillation*: trajectories generated directly by the experience-aware teacher; student trained to match teacher distribution. Loss: L_{off}(θ) = E_{H∼π_T} Σ D_KL(p_t || q_t) with forward KL. Rejection sampling retains only successful teacher trajectories, providing compact (avg. 4.5 turns) and coherent end-to-end demonstrations.

**Experimental Setup.** Student models: Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 and Qwen3-8B (thinking mode disabled). Agent follows ReAct-style interaction with five tools: Search, Visit, Python, Scholar, File Parser. Training corpus: 15K examples from WebWalkerQA-silver, DeepDive, WebShaper, WebDancer, SailorFog-QA. Evaluation benchmarks: WebWalkerQA (in-domain), GAIA-Text-103 (out-of-domain), BrowseComp-ZH (out-of-domain). Metrics: Pass@1 (WebWalkerQA, BrowseComp-ZH), average accuracy (GAIA). Training: lr=1×10^{-5}, batch size=128, 5 epochs, 8× NVIDIA A800 GPUs.

## Results & Highlights

**Capability collapse under on-policy context-distillation (Figure 1):** Starting at 22.8% on WebWalkerQA, the on-policy baseline drops to 19.7% (Iter 2) and 14.6% (Iter 3). On GAIA: 22.3% → 14.6% → 10.0%. On BrowseComp-ZH: 3.5% → 3.1% → 2.8%.

**Experience Granularity (Figure 2):** Instance-level experience improves performance in Iter 1 (WebWalkerQA: 29.0%) but quickly falls below base (19.9% at Iter 3). Principle-level experience is more durable, with slower degradation. Base model: 6.6% (WebWalkerQA).

**Injection Pattern (Table 1, Figure 3):** Single-iteration comparison under Qwen self-generated experience: step-wise injection achieves WebWalkerQA 31.2% (+8.0), GAIA 22.7% (+5.9), BrowseComp-ZH 5.2% (+0.7) vs. global injection. Premature-answer rate: global injection 63.82% vs. step-wise 0% (Table 2). Across iterations (Figure 3): step-wise maintains 32.4%–35.0% on WebWalkerQA over 3 iterations; global drops from 29.0% to 19.9%.

**Internalization Regime (Figure 5):** Off-policy context-distillation consistently outperforms on-policy across all three benchmarks and both model sizes across 3 iterations. For Qwen3-4B on WebWalkerQA: off-policy 30.6% → 30.7% → 31.5% (stable); on-policy 35.0% → 32.4% → 31.5% (declining). On GAIA (Qwen3-4B): off-policy 29.8% → 30.1% → 33.3% (improving); on-policy drops to 25.6%.

**Final combined recipe (Figure 6, Qwen3-4B):** Internalized performance: 18.5% (base) → 29.0% → 32.1% → 31.5% (WebWalkerQA). In-context performance additionally preserved: 32.1–30.0%. The model both internalizes experience into parameters and retains the ability to exploit explicit experience at inference time.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core insight — that existing iterative experience internalization methods suffer from capability collapse — is concrete and empirically demonstrated. The three-dimension decomposition is clean and actionable. However, individual components (off-policy distillation, principle-level summarization, state-aware experience injection) are each known individually; the contribution is their joint identification as necessary for stable multi-iteration learning.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The paper ablates each dimension independently across 3 iterations, 2 model sizes, 3 benchmarks, with quantitative metrics. The premature-answer rate analysis (Table 2) and trajectory-length analysis (Table 3) add mechanistic grounding. The rejection-sampling + off-policy recipe is well-motivated and tested.
- **Reproducibility:** High. Code and data are released at GitHub. Implementation uses verl (an open RLHF framework). Model, hyperparameters, and benchmarks are clearly specified.
- **Significance:** High for the self-evolving LLM agent community. The failure mode (collapse under iterative on-policy distillation) is likely shared across many recent agent self-improvement papers, making these findings broadly relevant.
- **Red flags:** Experiments are limited to web-reasoning tasks with a specific ReAct+tool-use setup. The gains on BrowseComp-ZH are modest and sometimes inconsistent (e.g., on-policy drops to 1.4% for Qwen3-8B in Iter 2–3 before recovering). The authors acknowledge generalization to other domains, languages, and agent paradigms is unverified.

## Strengths

- Clear identification of a concrete, practically important failure mode with strong empirical evidence across models and benchmarks
- Clean three-dimension framework that each contribute independently and combine multiplicatively
- Mechanistic explanations for why each dimension matters (premature-answer rate, trajectory length, experience-use ability measured via in-context vs. internalized gap)
- Public code and data release; reproducible experimental setup
- Off-policy recipe is efficient (shorter teacher trajectories, rejection sampling keeps only successful ones) — practical for resource-constrained settings

## Limitations

- All experiments on web-reasoning agent tasks (WebWalkerQA, GAIA, BrowseComp-ZH); generalization to other domains (coding, math, dialogue) or non-tool-use settings is unverified
- Experience pool size, selector quality, and trajectory filtering criteria are not ablated — acknowledged by authors as future work
- The stable off-policy recipe requires a strong teacher (experience-aware model with coherent trajectories); its performance under weaker or noisier experience pools is unclear
- Broader safety concern noted by the authors: stable internalization of flawed or biased experience could compound errors in self-evolving systems, requiring trajectory auditing and human oversight

## Relevance

本文直接关联自动化研究循环与自我进化 Agent 的核心基础设施问题：如何让 Agent 从自身历史经验中持续学习而不退化。对于构建长期运行的 AI 研究助手或自动化推理 Agent，本文提出的"原则级经验 + 逐步注入 + 离策略蒸馏"方案提供了一套可直接参考的工程配方。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How sensitive is the stable recipe to experience pool quality — specifically, what happens when the base model generates largely incorrect or incoherent trajectories in early iterations?
2. Can principle-level experience extraction be automated more tightly (e.g., via structured ontologies or learned summarization) rather than relying on a powerful summarization model (DeepSeek-V4)?
3. How does this approach compare to parameter-efficient alternatives (e.g., LoRA-based continual fine-tuning with replay) in terms of stability and compute cost across iterations?
4. Does the step-wise selector's quality degrade as the experience pool grows and becomes more heterogeneous across iterations?
5. Would the findings transfer to reasoning-heavy tasks (math olympiad, code synthesis) where the action space is more structured and trajectories are shorter than multi-step web navigation?
