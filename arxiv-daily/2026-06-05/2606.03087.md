> **一句话总结：** RLVR 训练中模型边学新题边遗忘已会的题——本文将此现象命名为"正确集翻转"，并提出 ReMind：一个以不额外增加 rollout 开销为前提、通过周期性复习已掌握样本来防止遗忘的轻量机制，在 20 个基准上稳定超越 GRPO/DAPO 及多种 replay 方法。

> **TL;DR:** RLVR training quietly forgets previously solved problems while acquiring new ones. The authors name this "correct-set turnover," prove that early re-review is far cheaper than late remediation (the "repair-window principle"), and propose **ReMind** — a zero-rollout-overhead review queue that periodically reinjects mastered prompts into the training batch, yielding consistent gains across image-text, video, and text math reasoning with Qwen3-VL and Qwen2.5-Math.

---

## 核心贡献

1. **现象命名与形式化：正确集翻转（Correct-Set Turnover）。** 将 RLVR 训练中"新获得解题能力"与"已掌握样本回归失效"这两个对立流动联合建模。定义 *获取流*（acquisition）与 *回归流*（regression），净准确率提升只是两者之差，而主流指标（pass@1）只看合计，掩盖了回归的存在。

2. **修复窗口原理（Repair-Window Principle）。** 通过一阶泰勒展开分析：一个被掌握后不再送入训练的样本，其成功概率因梯度干扰以速率 $\alpha\delta$ 持续下降。延迟 $k$ 步后复习，修复代价约为初次学习代价的 $O(k\alpha^2\delta)$ 倍。受控实验（step-60 检查点取已稳定掌握样本，分别在第 0 步和第 20 步复习）证实：早期复习保持 85–91% 保留率，延迟复习下降至 78–84%，最多差 14 个样本。

3. **ReMind 机制。** 三组件：FIFO 复习队列（存储已掌握样本的 ID）、批次构建规则（每 $f$ 步将队列中的样本以比例 $r$ 替换新鲜样本，形成混合批次 $\tilde{B}_t$）、队列更新规则（新掌握→入队；复习后仍正确→出队；复习后回归→重新入队末尾）。关键：复习样本走同一 rollout+奖励+策略更新流程，**不增加任何额外 rollout 开销**，默认 $r=0.10, f=5$，复习样本占全部训练提示约 2%。

4. **全面实验验证。** 20 个基准（图像-文本 ×8，视频 ×6，纯文本数学 ×6），基础模型 Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct 和 Qwen2.5-7B-Math，算法主干 GRPO 和 DAPO。

## Key Contributions

1. **Formalizing Correct-Set Turnover.** The net accuracy change at each step decomposes into an acquisition flow (problems newly solved) and a regression flow (previously solved problems now failing). Standard RLVR metrics track the sum but never report the regression side. The paper makes both flows explicit and defines retention as a first-class optimization target.

2. **The Repair-Window Principle.** A first-order Taylor expansion shows that gradient interference from other training samples continuously erodes the success probability of an absent mastered prompt. Review cost grows sharply as $O(k\alpha^2\delta)$ with delay $k$, while early review — while the model still produces mostly correct rollouts — costs near zero because within-group advantage variance is negligible. This is verified empirically (91.5% strict retention under immediate review vs. 87.7% under 20-step delay).

3. **ReMind: a lightweight retention mechanism.** Three components: (a) a FIFO *review queue* storing mastered sample IDs; (b) *batch construction* that mixes queue samples at ratio $r$ on every $f$-th step; (c) a *self-correcting queue update* that graduates stable samples, re-enqueues regressed ones. Review samples use the same rollout pipeline — zero additional rollout budget. With the default 2% review budget, ReMind adds only 3% wall-clock overhead.

4. **Comprehensive benchmarking.** Evaluated on 20 benchmarks across three modalities with two model families and two algorithmic backbones, including against three replay baselines (ExGRPO, RLEP, RePO).

## Methodology

### Problem Framing

Let $q_t(p) = \Pr[\pi_{\theta_t} \text{ solves } p]$, estimated from $K$ rollouts as $\hat{q}_t(p) = \frac{1}{K}\sum_{i=1}^K \mathbf{1}[r_i=1]$. The *mastered set* at step $t$ is $M_t = \{p \in \mathcal{P}: \hat{q}_t(p) = 1\}$. Retention and regression are:

$$\text{Retention}_t = \frac{|M_t \cap M_{t+\Delta}|}{|M_t|}, \quad \text{Regression}_t = 1 - \text{Retention}_t$$

The graded mastery estimate (16/16 = fully mastered, 12/16 = partially drifting) is directly available from GRPO's existing multi-rollout structure — no auxiliary evaluation cost.

### The Repair Window (§3.2)

For a mastered prompt $p$ absent from training, gradient interference causes:

$$q_{t+1}(p) \approx q_t(p) - \alpha \nabla_\theta q_t(p)^\top g_t$$

After $k$ unreviewed steps, $q_t(p) \approx 1 - k\alpha\delta(p)$. While $q$ is still high, rollouts remain mostly correct, within-group advantage variance ≈ 0, so the review sample adds virtually no gradient noise and repair is near-free. Once $q$ falls below threshold, relearning costs $O(1/\alpha)$ — comparable to first-time learning.

### ReMind (§3.3)

**Review Queue.** When $\hat{q}_t(p) = 1$ in the standard training stream, $p$ is enqueued with probability $p_\text{add} = 0.25$. FIFO ordering bounds the number of intervening updates before each sample is revisited.

**Batch Construction.** On a review step (every $f=5$ steps), the mixed batch is:
$$\tilde{B}_t = B_t^\text{fresh} \cup B_t^\text{review}, \quad |B_t^\text{review}| = \min(\lfloor r \cdot |\tilde{B}_t| \rfloor, |Q_t|)$$

**Queue Update.** Three post-rollout cases: (A) newly mastered → enqueue with prob $p_\text{add}$; (B) review sample retains full accuracy → graduate (remove); (C) review sample regresses ($\hat{q}_t(p) < 1$) → re-enqueue at tail.

### Training Setup

- Base: EasyVideoR1 framework; batch size 256, $K=8$ rollouts per prompt
- Hardware: 4 nodes × 8 NVIDIA H200 140GB GPUs
- Training data: MMFineReason-123K (difficulty-filtered subset of MMFineReason-1.8M; only samples unsolvable by Qwen3-VL-4B-Thinking across 4 rollouts)
- ReMind hyperparameters: $r=0.10$, $f=5$, $p_\text{add}=0.25$ (≈2% review budget)

## Results & Highlights

### Image-Text Reasoning (8 benchmarks, Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct)

| Method | Math Avg | General Avg | Overall Avg |
|---|---|---|---|
| GRPO | 60.28 | 60.22 | 60.25 |
| GRPO + ReMind | 66.38 | 62.16 | **64.06** (+3.81) |
| DAPO | 61.52 | 60.20 | 61.02 |
| DAPO + ReMind | 66.46 | 62.47 | **64.46** (+3.44) |
| Best replay (RePO) | — | — | 62.02 |

ReMind outperforms the best replay baseline by +2.04 average. Replay methods improve sample efficiency but leave retention unaddressed — on ZeroBench (the hardest tail), all three replay methods score *below* GRPO, while ReMind scores above.

### Cross-Modal Generalization

- **Video (6 benchmarks, Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct):** GRPO→ReMind: 62.88→66.09 (+3.21). GRPO drops 2.6pp below base on MLVU (regression visible without review); ReMind partially repairs this.
- **Text Math (6 benchmarks, Qwen2.5-7B-Math):** ReMind surpasses ExGRPO by +1.23 average with consistent gains on AIME 2024/2025, AMC, MATH-500, Minerva, OlympiadBench.

### Diagnostic Signals (450-step run)

- Acquisition rate (distinct problems ever solved) is identical for GRPO and ReMind (~500 by step 450) — the accuracy gap is entirely explained by reduced regression.
- Correct-set loss: GRPO accumulates ~190 forgotten samples; ReMind stabilizes at ~155 (-35 samples), though ~155 residual turnover remains even with review.
- Policy entropy: GRPO collapses from 0.8 to near 0 between steps 50–150; ReMind sustains entropy at 0.7–0.8, indicating diverse solution strategies are preserved.

### Per-Difficulty Analysis

Among 292 "fragile" samples (base accuracy 0–50%):
- GRPO: consolidates 119 (40.8%) to above 80% accuracy, completely forgets 32 (11.0%)
- ReMind: consolidates 135 (46.2%) to above 80%, completely forgets only 20 (6.8%)

ReMind has negligible benefit on truly unsolvable samples (as expected — nothing to retain) and on easy samples (>90% — rarely regress anyway).

### Budget Sensitivity

All review budgets from 1% to 10% outperform GRPO (62.83–64.06 vs. 60.25). The optimal is 2% (r=0.10, f=5); 10% budget *underperforms* 4% because excessive review crowds out fresh training signal.

### Computational Overhead

| Component | Time | Fraction |
|---|---|---|
| Standard training | 9.9h | 78% |
| Periodic validation | 2.5h | 19% |
| Review (re-rollout + queue mgmt.) | 0.3h | **3%** |

## Critical Assessment

This paper targets a real and underappreciated failure mode of RLVR. The core insight — that the "correct set" is not static but continuously churned — is well-motivated by the asymmetry between pass@1 (aggregated) and retention (sample-level). The repair-window principle is analytically grounded and empirically verified with a controlled intervention experiment (Figure 2).

The key strength is the **zero-overhead framing**: ReMind does not add rollout budget, no new model copies, no replay buffers — it only reorders which prompts go into the existing batch. This makes adoption straightforward. The 20-benchmark evaluation across three modalities and two model families is broad enough to be convincing.

**主要疑虑：**
1. 实验规模仅限于 7–8B 参数模型。修复窗口的时间跨度和回归幅度是否在 70B+ 模型上具有相同规律尚不清楚。
2. ReMind 对奖励信号准确性敏感：若验证器存在误报（false positive），被误判为"已掌握"的样本会进入复习队列并获得强化，可能加剧 reward hacking。
3. 约 155 个样本的残余遗忘在复习后仍然存在，说明固定复习周期无法完全消除 correct-set turnover——动态调度可能更优但尚未探索。
4. 结果中数学任务的收益（+5.67）明显高于通用多模态任务（+1.94），暗示该机制的效果依赖于任务本身的回归倾向，不是普适的。

## Strengths

- **概念贡献清晰：** 正确集翻转的形式化和修复窗口原理为后续研究提供了可操作的分析框架。
- **零开销实现：** 仅通过预 rollout 批次替换即可实现保留感知训练，无需修改奖励或策略更新逻辑。
- **宽泛的评估：** 横跨图像-文本、视频、纯文本数学三种模态，GRPO 和 DAPO 两种骨干，以及三种 replay 基线，结论较为鲁棒。
- **训练动力学诊断：** 将准确率分解为获取流、回归流和策略熵，直接证明改进来源于减少遗忘而非加速获取，因果解释清晰。
- **Per-difficulty 分析：** 发现 ReMind 的收益集中于"脆弱"样本（0–50% 基准准确率），与理论预期完全一致，增强了说服力。

---

## Limitations

- **模型规模：** 仅在 7–8B 模型上验证，大模型可扩展性未知。
- **奖励误报敏感性：** 系统性误报会将错误解误收入复习队列，潜在风险比不依赖掌握状态的方法更大。
- **残余遗忘不可消除：** 即使有复习，每次评审仍有约 155 个样本持续遗忘，说明某些 correct-set turnover 是 RLVR 优化过程的内在属性。
- **固定复习周期：** 当前设计不感知回归动态，动态调度（依据实时回归信号决定何时复习）可能进一步缩小残余遗忘，但未被探索。
- **领域限制：** 代码生成等其他领域是否同样受益于 retention-aware training 尚待验证。

---

## Relevance

对于从事以下方向的研究者高度相关：

- **RLVR / 后训练（Post-training）：** 直接揭示了 GRPO/DAPO 等主流方法的一个系统性盲点，ReMind 可以作为 plug-in 应用于任何基于 group-rollout 的 RLVR 框架。
- **持续学习（Continual Learning）：** 在单一训练分布内的样本级遗忘，与跨任务灾难性遗忘互补，但机制不同（梯度干扰 vs. 分布偏移）。
- **多模态推理：** 视频和数学两个模态均呈现相同规律，正确集翻转似乎是模态无关的 RLVR 属性。
- **训练效率：** 以 3% 额外开销获得 3–4pp 平均收益，效率比在同样数据上训练第二遍（+2pp）更高，且更稳定。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **修复窗口与模型规模的关系：** 在 70B 模型中，梯度干扰的强度（$\delta$）和修复窗口的长度是否有本质不同？大模型的 correct-set turnover 率是更低（更稳定）还是更高（更新步幅更大）？

2. **动态复习调度：** 能否根据实时回归信号（如当前批次中已知掌握样本的突然失效）来触发复习，而非依赖固定周期 $f$？这与 curriculum RL 中的自适应采样有何区别？

3. **ReMind + DAPO 的叠加效应机理：** DAPO 已有在线过滤（剔除全对或全错样本），ReMind 复习的恰恰是那些被过滤掉的"全对"样本。两者是否在结构上互补？Table 1 显示叠加有效，但具体交互机制值得深入分析。

4. **奖励误报的缓解策略：** 若验证器存在误报，是否可以通过要求连续 $k$ 次全对（而非单次全对）才入队来提高队列质量？这会显著减慢队列增长速度吗？

5. **跨任务泛化与任务内保留的权衡：** ReMind 优化的是对同一训练提示的保留。如果训练分布与测试分布存在系统偏差，过强的保留是否会导致对训练集的过拟合，从而损害 OOD 泛化？ZeroBench（超出模型当前能力的极难测试集）上的结果支持还是反驳了这一担忧？
