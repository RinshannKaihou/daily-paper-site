Key Finding

LLM agents cite correct evidence yet still forecast the wrong research object — evidence-decision decoupling is a systematic, previously unnamed failure mode.

ForeSci: Evaluating LLM Agents for Forward-Looking AI Research Judgment ↗ Tian, Yin, Xia, Kong, Liu  ·  2026-06-05

Why This Matters

High evidence traceability does not guarantee correct research decisions — a critical diagnostic blind spot for AI research-assistant evaluation that prior benchmarks could not surface.

Critical Edge

Scope limited to four AI sub-domains with no cross-field validation. Reviewer Persuasiveness and Trace are rubric-based LLM-as-judge scores, subject to evaluator model bias — precision is approximate.

Methodology

Benchmark Construction

  1. Corpus harvest: arXiv queries + Semantic Scholar enrichment, deduplication, domain-relevance and benchmark-core two-stage filtering; chronologically truncated at cutoff t.
  2. Temporal taxonomy (TaxoAdapt): Per-domain T_{d,t} = (V, E) with nodes = subdirections, edges = method-evolution relations. Five signal types extracted, then human-expert validated.
  3. Strict temporal integrity: Post-cutoff papers hidden at generation time; all LLM backbones trained before the cutoff; web search disabled.
  4. Evaluation: DeepSeek-V4 as judge; rubric metrics repeated and reported mean ± variance. Human validation in Appendix C.

Benchmark Design

Four Task Families (125 tasks each)

Direction Forecasting Pick the direction most likely to gain momentum post-cutoff; grounded in method-development signals.
Bottleneck–Opportunity Discovery Identify root bottleneck in a subdirection; explain the one-hop opportunity it unlocks.
Strategic Research Planning Rank candidate directions for a hypothetical lab; hardest — highest low-score rates on Fact & FTA.
Venue-Conditioned Positioning Given a project description, rank venue families with framing rationale.

Assessment

Strengths

  • + Fills a genuine gap: prospective, evidence-grounded research decision-making as an evaluation target — no prior benchmark does this.
  • + Strict temporal integrity via multiple enforcement mechanisms prevents hindsight bias.
  • + Multi-signal evaluation (Fact + FTA + Trace + Pers.) separates failure modes that a single aggregate score would conflate.
  • + Benchmark is refreshable as new literature emerges — ongoing utility beyond a static snapshot.
  • + Human–LLM collaborative task construction with expert taxonomy validation.

Limitations

  • – Four AI sub-domains only; no cross-field validation (biology, chemistry, etc.).
  • – Trace and Pers. are LLM-as-judge rubric scores — approximate, subject to evaluator model bias.
  • – Strategic Planning and Venue Positioning involve preference-sensitive decisions where "correct" answers may be legitimately contested.
  • – No inter-annotator agreement reported for human expert validation.
  • – Prospective forecasting mode is a 12-task proof-of-concept, not a rigorous scored evaluation.
Open Questions

For Further Exploration

  1. Does evidence-decision decoupling correlate with model scale — do larger models show less drift despite similar traceability scores?
  2. How does performance change with explicit chain-of-thought prompting that separates evidence retrieval from decision synthesis?
  3. Can the four drift types (scope, causal-role, intervention-mode, temporal-horizon) be detected and corrected automatically via a verifier model?
  4. Would a domain outside AI (drug discovery, materials science) show similar failure patterns, or is decoupling specific to fast-moving AI research?
  5. Do human researchers show the same evidence-decision decoupling pattern, and at what rate? How does ForeSci performance correlate with expert judgment?
