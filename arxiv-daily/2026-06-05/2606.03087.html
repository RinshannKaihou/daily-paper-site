Learning to Solve, Forgetting to Retain: Correct-Set Turnover in RLVR

Qin · Yang · Si · Gu · Fu · Lin  —  IIE CAS / JD.COM

RLVR models forget previously solved problems while learning new ones — a 2 % replay budget recovers both with only 3 % extra wall-clock cost.

Key Metric +3.81 Average benchmark gain over GRPO across 8 image-text benchmarks; +3.21 on video; +1.23 on text math.
Why This Matters Standard pass@1 hides regression. A hidden drain silently erases gains in GRPO, DAPO, and every replay baseline. ReMind is a zero-rollout-overhead plug-in for any group-rollout RLVR framework.
Critical Edge ~155 samples still forgotten even with review; only tested on 7–8 B models; reward false-positives corrupt the queue. Residual turnover is intrinsic to RLVR; dynamic scheduling unexplored.
Methodology

Correct-Set Turnover + ReMind

Net accuracy = acquisition flow − regression flow. Standard pass@1 reports only the sum and hides regression. The repair-window principle (first-order Taylor) shows review cost grows as O(kα²δ) with delay k — early review costs near-zero.


ReMind: FIFO queue of mastered sample IDs. Every f=5 steps, r=10% of the fresh batch is replaced by queue samples using the same rollout pipeline. Newly mastered → enqueue; review retains → graduate; review regresses → re-enqueue tail. Default 2% review budget, 3% wall-clock overhead.

Strengths
  • Formalizes correct-set turnover with measurable acquisition/regression flows — reusable analysis framework.
  • Zero rollout overhead: batch substitution only, no new model copies or buffers.
  • 20-benchmark sweep across image-text, video, and text-math with two model families and two algorithmic backbones.
  • Gain concentrated in "fragile" samples (0–50% accuracy), matching theory.
  • Budget sensitivity: any review budget 1–10% beats GRPO; optimal is 2%.
Limitations
  • Only 7–8 B models evaluated; scalability to 70 B+ unknown.
  • Reward false-positives push incorrectly-mastered samples into the queue, a larger risk than non-mastery-aware methods.
  • ∼155 forgotten samples persist even with review — some turnover is intrinsic to the RLVR optimization.
  • Fixed review schedule; dynamic scheduling (trigger on real-time regression signal) not explored.
  • Math gains (+5.67) substantially exceed general multimodal (+1.94) — benefit depends on regression propensity.
Questions for Further Exploration
  • Does the repair-window timescale (and regression rate) differ qualitatively at 70 B+ scale?
  • Can dynamic scheduling — triggered by real-time regression signals rather than fixed period f — eliminate residual turnover?
  • DAPO already filters all-correct samples; ReMind re-introduces them. What is the causal mechanism behind their complementarity?
  • Does tightening the mastery criterion (e.g., consecutive correct runs) meaningfully improve queue quality vs. reduce queue growth?
  • On ZeroBench (beyond-capability samples), does ReMind's retention pressure cause overfitting to training distribution, harming OOD generalization?
