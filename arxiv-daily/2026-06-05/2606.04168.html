Key Finding
Autoregressive consistency mechanistically forces safety alignment to be shallow; inserting 10–25 harmful tokens anywhere in a long refusal hijacks all subsequent generation.
When Autoregressive Consistency Hurts Safety Alignment
Lyu · Jia · Cai · Zhu  ·  University of Southampton  ·  2026-06-05
Key Metric
>75%
ASR on Llama-2-7b-Chat and Gemma-2-9b-it under random insertion attack; defense drops to <6%
Why This Matters
Standard RLHF/DPO safety is structurally fragile by design — no amount of training on early refusal tokens fixes the late-position gap.
Critical Edge
Only 256 training examples; no frontier model testing. Adaptive adversaries who optimize insertion position could bypass the defense.
Methodology

The Mechanism: Why Alignment Is Shallow

DEF
Autoregressive consistency: after a critical position t_c, the model assigns probability >1−ε to trajectory-consistent continuations — it's hard to deviate from the current branch.
LEM
Lemma 1: The softmax Jacobian ‖A_t‖² < 2ε for t > t_c — parameters at late positions are nearly inert to gradient updates.
THM
Theorem 2.1: Under SFT and DPO, gradient contributions from t > t_c are suppressed to O(√ε). Safety alignment concentrates on early refusal tokens — and nothing else.

The Random Insertion Attack

Given (prompt x, refusal r, harmful content h): sample position i uniformly from |r|, construct forced state τ̃ = (x, [r_{:i}; h_{:q}]). Let the model continue from this state. Even with a long refusal prefix, 10–25 harmful tokens redirect all subsequent generation via autoregressive consistency.

The Defense: Adversarial Safety Alignment

Inner problem: find worst-case harmful state τ* = argmax_{τ_i} Φ_θ(τ_i) [k=32 random candidates] Outer problem: minimize refusal-recovery loss from τ* conditioned state Φ_θ(τ) = H(τ;θ) − R(τ;θ) [harmful margin]

Llama-3.1-8B: insertion ASR drops 18.3% → 1.9% at 25 tokens. Gemma-2-9b-it: similar pattern. Defense generalizes across model families.
Strengths
  • First formal proof: Gradient-dynamics theorem (covering both SFT and DPO) explaining why safety alignment is inherently shallow — not just empirical observation.
  • Unified attack surface: Reframes attack from "prefix targeting" to "trajectory-level harmful continuation states" — prefill attacks are a special case of the broader framework.
  • Conceptually clean defense: The minimax formulation directly targets the identified failure mechanism, not a heuristic patch.
  • Strong empirical transfer: Defense generalizes to GCG, PAIR, TAP, AutoDAN — attacks not seen during training.
  • Honest framing: Explicitly positioned as identifying a failure mechanism and framework, not a production-ready defense system.
Limitations
  • Tiny training set: Only 256 training examples from Qi et al.'s dataset; evaluated on 330-item HEx-PHI benchmark. Scale-up behavior unknown.
  • Idealized theory: Theorem 2.1 requires near-deterministic autoregressive consistency (probability >1−ε). A soft empirical analogy, not a literal property.
  • Approximation gap: Inner adversarial problem solved by random sampling (k=32) — not the true minimax solution. Gap not formally characterized.
  • No frontier model testing: All experiments on open-weight 7–9B models. Applicability to GPT-4-class models is unknown.
  • Adaptive adversary untested: An attacker who jointly optimizes insertion position and harmful content after seeing the defense could bypass it.
Open tension: The paper argues Insertion is better than Prefill because it targets the underlying mechanism. But Insertion also trains on harder adversarial states — the improvement may be partly due to harder training data rather than mechanistic targeting. This distinction matters for theoretical claims.
Questions for Exploration
Can the worst-case harmful state be found via gradient-based search inside the trajectory rather than random sampling? Would this close the gap between theory and practice?
Does autoregressive consistency similarly concentrate RLHF reward model signals during PPO? Would the same gradient-concentration theorem apply to PPO-based alignment?
How robust is the defense to an adaptive adversary who jointly optimizes insertion position and harmful span content (analogous to GCG for prefill)?
Is there a Pareto-optimal training procedure that achieves both deep refusal (early tokens) and trajectory-level harmful consistency breaking simultaneously?
How does the failure mode manifest geometrically? Does a harmful insertion shift the residual stream onto the "harmful" direction that then locks in via autoregressive consistency?
