Why This Matters
Tensor decompositions are actively being explored for MoE compression (Kimi-K2, DeepSeek). This paper definitively rules them out as standalone methods and explains exactly why — saving teams from wasted engineering cycles.
Critical Edge
Only lightweight LoRA repair (rank 16, 100 steps) was tested — a lower bound on recoverability. Full fine-tuning after tensorization is unexplored, leaving open whether tensorization could serve as an initialization for fine-tuning pipelines.
Three Chained Theoretical Obstructions
I
Frobenius ≠ Operator Isometry
Reshape φ: ℝ^(m×n) → tensor space preserves Frobenius norm but distorts operator norm by up to √min(m,n). Tucker minimizes the wrong objective for Transformer inference.
II
Spectral Suboptimality on Heavy Tails
LLM weight spectra follow power laws σᵢ ∝ i^(−α) with α < 0.5. Under this regime, spectral error from tensorization grows as Θ(N^(1/2−α)) — polynomially with layer width.
III
Super-weight Recovery Requires Full Rank
Critical coordinates carry negligible Frobenius mass but dominate functional behavior. Any Frobenius-optimal method discards them. Recovery requires ranks approaching the full matrix — defeating compression.

Open Questions
Q1 Can operator-norm-aware truncation close the gap? Minimizing ‖W−Ŵ‖₂ directly rather than ‖W−Ŵ‖_F would bypass Obstruction I — is this computationally tractable at LLM scale?
Q2 Does training-time tensorization (TT-LoRA, LoRETTA) avoid structural mismatch by allowing the model to adapt its internal geometry to the tensor format during fine-tuning?
Q3 Are super-weights the underlying cause of heavy-tailed weight spectra (α<0.5), or an independent phenomenon? The paper treats them as complementary diagnostics but doesn't establish causality.
