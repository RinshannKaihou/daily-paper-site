IdEst: Assessing Self-Supervised Learning Representations via Intrinsic Dimension

Mordacq · Kalogeiton · Oudot  —  Inria Saclay / LIX, École Polytechnique

A Minimum Spanning Tree estimator of intrinsic dimension predicts SSL representation quality label-free, 15–30× faster than linear probing, and generalizes across all major SSL paradigms.

Key Metric ρ ≈ −0.84 Spearman correlation between IdEst and linear probing Top-1 accuracy on iNat-18 across 14 SSL models. ρ ≈ −0.74 to −0.84 across all datasets tested.
Why This Matters Hyperparameter search for SSL models no longer requires labeled data. Evaluate 14 models in the time it takes to probe one. Theoretically grounded via Rényi entropy and persistent homology — not a heuristic metric.
Critical Edge Vision-language models (CLIP, EVA-CLIP) are systematic outliers due to the cone effect. ViTs are unstable in early training. Dense prediction tasks untested. Correlation ρ ≈ 0.8, not 1.0 — an indicator, not a perfect predictor.
Methodology

dim_MST: Minimum Spanning Tree Dimension

For a point cloud Z, MST length scales as L(MST(X_n)) ≈ C · n(d-1)/d. IdEst recovers intrinsic dimension d via log-log regression on subsamples of increasing size: slope m gives d = 1/(1−m).

Why not TwoNN or MLE? Both assume locally constant density and i.i.d. sampling — violated by SSL objectives. On a 1D helix in R3, TwoNN diverges to infinity as n grows; dim_MST converges correctly to d=1.

Theoretical anchor: Theorem 3.1 — generalization error L ≈ O(K_L · ND−1/d). Lower d means better downstream performance, all else equal. dim_MST is equivalent to 0-dimensional Persistent Homology dimension (dim_PH), provably stable under distribution perturbations.

Questions for Further Exploration
  • Why do CLIP-family models systematically deviate? How much variance is explained by encoder misalignment vs. training objective differences in the cone effect?
  • Can IdEst be integrated as a training regularizer — penalizing high intrinsic dimension during SSL pretraining as a principled geometric inductive bias?
  • How does IdEst behave for dense prediction (segmentation, depth)? Do classification and dense representations occupy different ID regimes?
  • Can IdEst discriminate between failure modes: dimensional collapse (all representations identical) vs. unconstrained spread?
  • What is the minimum reference dataset size needed for stable dim_MST estimates, especially for small or specialized datasets (medical imaging, satellite)?
  • What does IdEst measure that effective rank (RankMe) does not? A direct geometric comparison would sharpen the field's understanding of representation quality metrics.
