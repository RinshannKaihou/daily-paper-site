Methodology

Standard FFN Baselines

Type-I: f(x) = W₂ σ(W₁x) [ReLU, GELU, ReLU²] Type-II: g(x) = W₃ σ(W₁x) ⊙(W₂x) [SwiGLU]

MoA — Token-Adaptive Mixing

  • Gates: πk(x) = φ(ukTx), where φ is sigmoid (best), tanh, or softmax
  • Type-I MoA: f(x) = Wout Σk πk(x) σk(Winx)
  • Type-II MoA: three variants — one-sided, bi-sided, quadratic (qd-MoA)
  • Soft gating by default: all activations contribute, no hard routing needed
  • Activation dictionary: {ReLU², GELU, SiLU, LeakyReLU, ReLU, tanh} (Type-I); adds {Id} for Type-II

Expressivity Hierarchy (Theorems 4.1 & 4.2)

Fixed-activation FFN &8842; LA &8842; MoA
  • MoA creates ridge singularities with input-dependent amplitude — impossible for LA
  • Constructive proofs with W1,∞ lower bounds for both FFN types

Scaling Law

  • MoA and baseline curves are nearly parallel from 0.25B to 2B — gains persist across scales
  • Also generalizes to vision: qd-MoA on MAE ViT-B/16 lowers validation loss

Strengths

  • Rigorous expressivity separation proofs for both Type-I and Type-II FFNs
  • Consistent gains across all scales, architectures (dense + MoE), optimizers (AdamW + Muon), and schedules
  • Near-zero overhead: 1.03–1.13× wall-clock, negligible memory and parameter increase
  • Parameter-controlled ablation proves gains come from activation mixing, not extra parameters
  • Generalizes beyond LLMs to vision self-supervised pre-training (MAE)
  • Orthogonal to MoE, attention variants — composable with other innovations

Limitations

  • Largest scale tested is 2B; benefits at 7B, 13B, 70B+ are unknown
  • Only 4 zero-shot benchmarks on a single model size; no MMLU, coding, or math evaluation
  • bi-MoA (most expressive variant) does not consistently outperform simpler one-MoA
  • Activation dictionary and gating require per-architecture tuning
  • Downstream improvement (+0.76 avg) is modest relative to pre-training loss gains
  • Inference latency at real-world throughput levels is not evaluated

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Do MoA gains persist at 7B, 13B, and 70B+ parameter scales? The parallel scaling curves are encouraging but not conclusive.
  2. How does MoA interact with other activation-related innovations (ReLU² sparsity, KAN-style edge activations)?
  3. Can the activation dictionary be learned or pruned during training rather than manually specified?
  4. What is the interaction between MoA and post-training techniques (SFT, RLHF, quantization)?
  5. Does the improved learning-rate tolerance enable faster schedules or larger effective batch sizes?
  6. Can MoA be applied selectively to specific layers for further overhead reduction?
