> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了"组合坍塌"现象——原子知识稳定性几乎相同的训练方法，其组合推理能力可相差40个百分点以上，并提出双门控协议将后训练收益分解为原子稳定性、残差组合失败和临界深度三个独立通道。

> **TL;DR:** Post-training recipes with statistically indistinguishable atomic knowledge can diverge by 40+ percentage points in compositional reasoning. A double-gate protocol decomposes post-training gains into atomic stability, residual composition failure, and critical depth, revealing that outcome-verified RL vastly outperforms SFT distillation for composition while aggregate metrics mask this gap.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"组合坍塌"（composition collapse）概念：原子知识稳定性几乎相同的后训练方案，组合推理失败率相差40+个百分点，且标准聚合指标无法检测。
- 设计双门控协议（double-gate protocol）：先验证模型是否稳定掌握每个原子事实（Gate 1: 跨释义一致性），再验证子问题独立正确性（Gate 2），最后测量残差组合失败率。
- 将后训练收益分解为三个独立通道：Δatom（原子稳定性）、Δcomp（残差组合失败）、Δdepth（临界深度 d50）。
- 在 D4 V2 基准（390个多跳问题，深度2-11）上系统评估四种后训练方案（base / RLHF / SFT蒸馏 / RLVR）。
- 因果干预实验：在相同基座模型上用 LoRA-GRPO 训练，证实 RL 可独立于原子知识改善组合能力，但深度泛化有限。
- CoT诊断：70-75%的门控通过失败可通过链式思维推理恢复，瓶颈在生成过程而非知识表征。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies **composition collapse**: post-training recipes with <2pp atomic stability difference produce 40+ pp divergence in residual composition failure, invisible to aggregate benchmark metrics.
- Introduces a **double-gate protocol** that conditions composition measurement on verified stable atomic knowledge, correcting the estimand from prior single-gate approaches (Press et al., 2023).
- Proposes a **three-channel decomposition** (Δatom, Δcomp, Δdepth) that makes cross-recipe comparison fair by matching on atomic stability.
- Constructs **D4 V2**, a 390-question temporal factual chain benchmark spanning depths 2–11 across four task families with unambiguous verification.
- Provides **causal evidence** via controlled LoRA-GRPO interventions on the same base model: GRPO reduces depth-4 residual failure by 45–62 pp while atomic stability moves only 1–3 pp.
- Demonstrates that **70–75% of residual composition failures are recovered by chain-of-thought reasoning**, localizing the bottleneck to generation-time computation.

## Methodology

The methodology centers on the double-gate protocol applied to the D4 V2 benchmark:

1. **Composition instance decomposition**: A multi-hop question *q* is decomposed into *k* atomic facts with aligned sub-questions. Each atom has *m* paraphrase probes.

2. **Gate 1 (Atomic Stability)**: Every atom must be answered consistently across all paraphrase variants under a consistency matcher (LLM-based adjudication). This filters out cases where the model happens to answer correctly once but lacks stable access.

3. **Gate 2 (Sub-question Correctness)**: Each sub-question must be answered correctly in isolation. This ensures the model can verbalize each atomic fact independently.

4. **Residual Composition Failure**: Among cases passing both gates, the fraction where the main multi-hop answer is still wrong. By construction this upper-bounds pure composition failure.

5. **Three-channel decomposition**: For recipes *R* and *R'* matched on atomic stability (within 2pp):
   - Δatom = difference in atomic stability
   - Δcomp = difference in residual composition failure at gate-passing cases
   - Δdepth = difference in d50 (depth at which residual failure first exceeds 50%)

6. **Causal intervention**: Four LoRA-GRPO variants trained on Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct with different depth coverages ({2,4}, {2,4,6}, {2,4,6,8}) and budgets (rank 32 vs 64), isolating training objective from base-model confounds.

7. **Diagnostic probes**: Thinking mode (native CoT up to 16K tokens), explicit CoT prompting, in-context evidence supply, and hidden-state patching experiments.

**Models evaluated**: Llama-3-8B, Mistral-7B-v0.1, Qwen2.5-7B (base); Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, Mistral-7B-Instruct (RLHF); DeepHermes-3-8B (SFT distillation); Qwen3-8B (RLVR). All at 7–13B scale, greedy decoding.

## Results & Highlights

- **Base models are entirely filtered by the atomic gate** (<5 gate-passing cases at any depth). Their failure is knowledge-limited, not composition-limited.
- **Composition collapse at matched atoms**: Qwen3 (RLVR, 87.7% atom stab.) achieves 0% residual failure at depth 2; DeepHermes (SFT distill, 88.0% atom stab.) shows 47.4%; Qwen2.5-Inst (RLHF, 89.9%) shows 12.0%. Same knowledge, categorically different composition.
- **RLVR >> SFT distillation for composition**: Under identical base model, data, and LoRA budget, GRPO outperforms SFT-trace by 52 pp at depth 4 (24.5% vs 76.9%). SFT-trace is *worse* than the untrained baseline (69.8%), confirming reasoning-chain distillation can actively damage composition.
- **Limited depth transfer**: Training on depths {2,4} transfers only 7 pp to depth 8; adding depth-6 training does not improve depth 8. Only when depth-8 data is included does d=8 failure drop from 81.1% to 36.0%.
- **Task structure dominates depth**: At fixed depth 4, residual failure ranges from 38.5% (TEMPORAL_SUCCESSOR) to 92.9% (TEMPORAL_RANK) for Qwen3 — a 54 pp gap at identical chain length.
- **CoT recovers 70–75% of failures**: Enabling thinking mode on Qwen3 flattens the collapse curve to ~19–29% across depths 4–8, from 55–100% without CoT. Bottleneck is generation-time computation.
- **In-context evidence doesn't help**: Supplying facts in the prompt yields 88.9% residual failure — bottleneck is assembly, not lookup.
- **Cross-model replication**: Same GRPO recipe on Llama3-8B-Instruct produces 17.9% residual failure at d=4 (vs 30.8% on Qwen), confirming Δcomp replicates across architectures.
- **d50 averages [3,5]** across post-trained models; no recipe pushes the critical depth above 5–6 hops.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: High. The double-gate protocol is a genuine measurement advance over the single-gate compositionality gap (Press et al., 2023). The three-channel decomposition is simple but powerful. The finding that SFT distillation of reasoning traces can *actively harm* composition is counterintuitive and important.

**Rigor**: Strong. The causal LoRA-GRPO intervention on the same base model with matched data/budget is a clean experimental design. Bootstrap 95% CIs are reported throughout. Cross-model replication on Llama3 provides external validity. The failure taxonomy (72% structural composition failures vs. 28% format artifacts) transparently bounds measurement noise.

**Reproducibility**: Good. All models are open-weights; the benchmark (D4 V2) is released; LoRA-GRPO training details are specified (learning rate, rank, epochs, reward function, single RTX 4090). Code release is promised.

**Significance**: The composition collapse phenomenon has direct implications for how the field evaluates post-training recipes. Claiming multi-hop reasoning improvement without controlling for atomic knowledge stability is shown to be misleading. The Δcomp metric could become a standard tool.

**Red flags**:
- Sample sizes are small at deeper depths (n<15 at d=8 for most models), making point estimates noisy. The authors acknowledge this but some conclusions (e.g., "100% failure") rest on very few cases.
- The benchmark is restricted to temporal factual chains; generalization to other composition types (logical, spatial, causal) is only preliminarily explored (Appendix L).
- The closed-book protocol excludes RAG/in-context settings, which are increasingly common in practice.
- The base-to-instruct atomic stability jump (76–88 pp) is so large that the protocol is effectively limited to instruct-tuned models, leaving base-model composition unmeasurable.

## Strengths

- Clean separation of knowledge vs. composition failure through the double-gate protocol — addresses a real confound in prior work.
- Causal intervention design (same base model, same data, same LoRA budget, varying only training objective) is exemplary for this type of analysis.
- The finding that SFT reasoning-trace distillation *worsens* composition is a provocative and well-supported result with practical implications.
- CoT diagnostic elegantly localizes the bottleneck to generation-time computation rather than permanent representational deficit.
- Honest reporting of limitations, small sample sizes, and bounded measurement noise throughout.

## Limitations

- **Domain scope**: Restricted to temporal factual composition. Whether composition collapse generalizes to logical, spatial, or causal reasoning at scale remains open.
- **Small samples at depth**: Gate-passing n drops sharply with depth; d=8 estimates have CIs exceeding ±25 pp. Conclusions about depth ceilings are tentative.
- **Scale**: Only 7–13B models tested. Whether composition collapse appears (or disappears) at frontier scale is unknown.
- **Closed-book only**: The protocol requires internal atomic knowledge, excluding RAG and in-context settings where most practical systems operate.
- **Upper bound nature**: Residual failure rate includes ~14–21% format artifact inflation; the true composition failure rate is somewhat lower than reported.
- **LoRA-specific depth transfer**: Weak cross-depth transfer was obtained under LoRA; full fine-tuning may produce stronger generalization.

## 相关性

本文对评估和改进大模型的多跳推理能力具有重要方法论意义。双门控协议和三通道分解为后训练方案比较提供了更公平的测量框架，可直接应用于评估 RLHF/SFT/RLVR 等训练策略对组合推理的真实贡献。CoT诊断结果表明推理时的计算瓶颈是主要限制，这与 scaling inference compute 的研究方向高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does composition collapse appear at frontier model scale (70B+, 405B+), or does it diminish with scale?
2. How does the double-gate protocol apply to non-temporal composition (logical deduction, spatial reasoning, causal chains)?
3. Can the three-channel decomposition be integrated into training-time objectives (e.g., a reward that explicitly targets Δcomp)?
4. What is the relationship between composition collapse and attention-head composition mechanisms — can mechanistic interpretability identify the specific circuits that fail?
5. Does full fine-tuning (vs. LoRA) produce stronger cross-depth generalization, and if so, at what compute cost?
