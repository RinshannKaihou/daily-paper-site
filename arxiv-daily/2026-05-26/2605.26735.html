Rethinking the Multilingual Reasoning Gap with Layer Swap

Lasbordes, Chatelain & Seddah LightOn & Inria, Paris 2026-05-26
Key Finding Native reasoning gap is only 1.9–3.5% under matched supervision
Key Metric 1.9–3.5% vs. previously reported ~17–19%
Why This Matters Challenges the assumption that native-language reasoning incurs a large penalty—and offers a training-free fix.
Critical Edge Single model family (Qwen3-8B) at one scale; generalization untested.
Methodology
  • Data — English DolciThink-SFT-32B corpus (~500k samples) translated into five target languages via Gemma-3-27B-it with chunked pipeline; quality-filtered for degenerate and outlier samples.
  • Training — Full fine-tuning of Qwen3-8B-Base (DeepSpeed ZeRO-3 + Ulysses + FlashAttention-3). For each non-English language: a native specialist (CoT in target language) and an English-pivoted specialist (CoT in English). ~10B tokens each.
  • Weight-space analysis — Mean pairwise cosine similarity and top-1 SVD variance share of per-layer SFT deltas both peak at layers 13–22 (cos ≈ 0.6, SVD 65–74%), revealing a language-agnostic reasoning core.
  • Layer Swap — Transfer contiguous window of English specialist's transformer layers into each native specialist. Optimal window L13–L22 (L13–L20 for Chinese). Training-free.
  • Evaluation — MGSM-Rev2, Global-MMLU-Lite, GPQA-Diamond, AIME 24/25, HumanEvalPlus. Temperature 1, top-p 0.95, 10–30 runs.
Results & Highlights
  • Native gap concentrates on hardest benchmarks (AIME 5–12%); code tasks essentially tied.
  • ~100% target-language CoT fidelity preserved after Layer Swap.
  • Every non-English specialist scores higher on English inputs, confirming input understanding as a bottleneck.

Strengths
  • Rigorously controlled setup isolating CoT language effect (same Q&A pairs, matched token budgets).
  • Covers typologically diverse languages including low-resource Swahili.
  • Elegant weight-space analysis yielding mechanistic insight into layer specialization.
  • Layer Swap is simple, training-free, and immediately applicable.
  • Comprehensive five-benchmark evaluation.
  • All artifacts publicly released on HuggingFace.
Limitations
  • Single model family (Qwen3-8B) at 8B scale; transfer to larger models unknown.
  • Machine-translated training data may introduce artifacts absent from organic native CoT.
  • Tokenization inefficiency means less effective supervision for languages like Swahili.
  • Only six languages tested; Swahili is the sole low-resource representative.
  • No RL stage explored; production models typically undergo multi-stage post-training.
  • 10B-token SFT budget is below production-scale post-training.
Questions for Further Exploration
  • Does Layer Swap transfer to larger models (70B+) or different architectures (Llama, Mistral)?
  • How does Layer Swap interact with RL-based post-training (GRPO, RLVR)?
  • Would organically generated native CoT data further reduce the gap?
  • Can the language-agnostic reasoning core be strengthened during pretraining?
  • What is the minimal native SFT needed before Layer Swap becomes effective?
  • How does Layer Swap perform on tasks with cultural or sociopragmatic components?
