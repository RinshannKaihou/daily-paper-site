arXiv 2605.26409 · AI Safety

Jailbreak susceptibility prediction and mitigation via the behavioral geometry of models

Hayden Helm, Xiaodong Liu, Weiwei Yang | 2026-05-26 | 79 models · 24 providers
Key Finding
Behavioral geometry predicts jailbreak susceptibility at 0.94 AUPRC with ~2% probe cost.
Key Metric
0.94AUPRC · 98% fewer probes
Why This Matters
Slashes safety evaluation cost by 98% — just 3 source models can defend an entire deployment fleet.
Critical Edge
Generalization to unseen attack types is unproven; requires a large, diverse initial model population.

Methodology

  1. Population-level behavioral profiling — leverage behavioral responses from previously evaluated models to characterize the geometry of the model population, instead of evaluating each model individually.
  2. Susceptibility prediction — use the behavioral geometry to predict which unevaluated models are likely vulnerable to jailbreak attacks, drastically reducing required probes.
  3. Defense transfer selection — given a target model, identify the best source model whose optimized defense can be transferred, guided by behavioral similarity rather than provider-matching heuristics.

Evaluated on two scales: 79 models across 24 providers, and 100 system configurations of a single base model.

Strengths

  • Dramatically reduces safety evaluation cost (98% fewer probes)
  • Elegant formulation of model similarity as a geometric object
  • Practical defense transfer with only 3 source models
  • Robust across hyperparameters and evaluation judges
  • Scales to both diverse populations and fine-grained configurations

Limitations

  • Requires a large, diverse initial evaluated model set
  • Attack methods used in evaluation are unspecified
  • Actual algorithmic complexity of "simple methods" is unclear from abstract
  • Transfer may degrade for fundamentally different architectures
Relevance This work tackles jailbreak defense transfer for LLMs, offering an efficient solution for safety evaluation at deployment scale. High reference value for researchers in AI safety and model robustness.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How well does behavioral geometry transfer to entirely new attack types?
  2. What is the minimum evaluated-model count for a useful geometry?
  3. Can the framework extend beyond jailbreaks to bias or hallucination?
  4. How does behavioral geometry evolve as models are updated?
  5. What are the computational costs of maintaining geometry at scale?
