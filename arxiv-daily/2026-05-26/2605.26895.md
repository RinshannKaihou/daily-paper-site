> **一句话总结：** 本文系统研究 LLM 归一化层中"可学习 scale 向量"，证明其虽只占 Llama-1B 的 7.84e-5 参数却显著影响训练，并提出"分支异质化 + 双侧归一化放置 + 幅度方向重参数化 + 分层权重衰减"的统一策略，在 0.12B–2B 稠密与 MoE 模型上终端损失稳定降低 >0.02。

> **TL;DR:** A systematic theory + large-scale empirical study of the learnable scale vector γ in RMSNorm-style layers. Theory shows γ is redundant in expressivity for Pre-Norm (it can be absorbed into the following linear map) but accelerates training via a self-amplifying preconditioner. The unified strategy (branch Heterogeneity + Dual-Normalized Placement + Original-space magnitude-direction Reparameterization + Individual Weight Decay) consistently beats well-tuned Llama / Llama-MoE baselines on 0.12B–2B models — e.g. >0.02 lower terminal loss across all MoE sizes, and >0.015 lower loss even over the strong Muon optimizer baseline — for <1.04× wall-clock / <1.01× memory overhead.

## 核心贡献

- 证明 scale 向量在 Pre-Norm 架构中不增加表达能力（可被后续线性层吸收），但其训练收益来自对后续线性映射的"自放大预条件"效应，并给出严格梯度流定理（Theorem 2.2）。
- 提出将归一化层分为 Input-Norm（紧随线性层）与 Output-Norm（不紧随线性层）两类，理论上证明权重衰减对前者有益（控制 Hessian 锐度）、对后者有害（损害表达能力），并在 Gemma-0.5B 上验证。
- 提出三项轻量改进：分支异质化（HG，为 Q/K/V 各设独立 scale 向量）、双侧/双侧归一化放置（DP/DNP）、幅度-方向重参数化（OR/ER），均给出预条件视角的统一解释与加速定理。
- 在稠密（0.12B–1B）与 MoE（0.25B–2B）模型、AdamW/Muon 优化器、cos/wsd 学习率调度上系统验证，统一策略稳定降低终端损失且开销可忽略。

## Key Contributions

- **Necessity of scale vectors (Sec 2.1):** Empirically, removing γ from 0.12B Llama raises terminal loss by ~0.028 (a 1.4× token-efficiency loss); even after re-tuning peak lr the gap stays ~0.015. Theory (Theorem 2.2) proves L_f(t) < L_g(t) for all t because γ induces a state-dependent self-amplifying preconditioner P_f,j = γ_j² I + w_f,j w_f,j^T with λ_min ≥ γ_j² ≥ 1.
- **Weight-decay theory (Sec 2.2):** Classifies RMSNorm layers into Input-Norm vs Output-Norm. Theorem 2.3 (continuous-time SGD SDE) shows wd on Input-Norm γ keeps E‖γ_t‖² and Hessian sharpness (λ_max, Tr, ‖·‖_F of ∇²L) bounded; without wd they diverge. Hence Individual Weight Decay (IWD): apply wd to Input-Norm γ, not to Output-Norm γ.
- **Three lightweight improvements (Sec 3):** HG (branch-specific γ_Q, γ_K, γ_V), placement variants AP/DP/DNP, and reparameterizations OR/ER — all unified as W_c → diag(u_c) W_c diag(v_c) = W_c ⊙ (u_c v_c^T), a rank-1 multiplicative scale field adding only O(d) params to modulate an O(d²) matrix. Theorems 3.1 & 3.2 prove DP and OR instantaneously accelerate loss descent.
- **Unified strategy + extensive experiments (Sec 4):** Combines HG+DNP+OR+IWD. Validated across dense 0.12B–1B and MoE 0.25B–2B (A0.11B–A0.62B) under industrial-scale token budgets (~100 tokens/param), with AdamW, Muon, and wsd schedule.

## Methodology

**Architecture focus.** RMSNorm(x; γ) = γ ⊙ Norm(x), where Norm(x) = √d · x/‖x‖. Studied on Llama (dense + LlamaMoE) and Gemma3 (which adds Q/K-Norm and Attn/FFN Output-Norm).

**Why scale vectors help — theory (Sec 2.1).** In Setting 2.1 (teacher f⋆(x)=W⋆Norm(x), x~N(0,I_d), squared loss, gradient flow, γ(0)=1, W(0)=0), the effective parameter A_f = W_f diag(γ) follows ȧ_f,j = (γ_j² I_c + w_f,j w_f_j^T)(w⋆_j − a_f,j), vs the no-scale ẇ_g,j = w⋆_j − w_g_j. So γ acts as a preconditioner with λ_min ≥ 1. GF preserves the invariant γ_j² − ‖w_f,j‖² = 1, giving strict acceleration (Theorem 2.2: L_f(t) < L_g(t) ∀t).

**Weight decay (Sec 2.2).** Two layer types: Input-Norm (Pre-Norm, γ followed by a linear map, redundant in expressivity → wd helps by bounding Hessian sharpness) vs Output-Norm (Q/K-Norm, Attn/FFN Output-Norm; γ directly parameterizes output → wd harms expressivity). IWD = wd on Input-Norm γ only.

**Improvement 1 — Heterogeneity (HG).** Replace the shared γ feeding Q,K,V (and gate/up) with branch-specific γ_Q, γ_K, γ_V (and γ_gate, γ_up), since Q/K/V matrices exhibit different growth/decay rates (Fig 4).

**Improvement 2 — Placement.** Standard input-side → AP (after-placement, γ on output side) → DP (two-sided γ_a ⊙ W(γ_b ⊙ ·)) → DNP (DP + Norm(W(γ_b⊙·))). Theorem 3.1 proves DP gives both row- and column-wise preconditioning, instantaneously at least as fast as standard.

**Improvement 3 — Reparameterization.** OR: γ → β·Norm(α) (Euclidean magnitude/direction); ER: γ → e^β·e^(α−mean(α)) (log-space). Theorem 3.2 proves OR induces an anisotropic preconditioner P = d·(γ̂γ̂^T) + ρ(I − γ̂γ̂^T) that amplifies magnitude-direction motion by a factor of d while keeping O(1) on the orthogonal direction.

**Unified view (Sec 3.4).** All designs are special cases of W_c → W_c ⊙ (u_c v_c^T), a reparameterization-induced preconditioner P_Φ = (∂Φ/∂q)(∂Φ/∂q)^T depending on current model state, complementary to (and stackable with) adaptive-optimizer preconditioners like Adam/Shampoo/Soap.

**Training setup.** Dense 0.12B/0.25B/0.5B/0.75B/1B and MoE 0.25B(A0.11B)–2B(A0.62B); ~100 tokens/param (dense) or /activated param (MoE); AdamW (β1=0.9, β2=0.95, wd λ=0.1, grad clip 1.0) default, plus Muon [16,20] and wsd [13] ablations; cos schedule with tuned lr_max shared between baseline and improved model; batch 0.5M (small) / 2M (large) tokens.

## Results & Highlights

- **Removing scale vectors is costly (Fig 1, 0.12B Llama):** terminal loss rises ~**0.028** (a **1.4×** token-efficiency penalty) at the same peak lr; even after re-tuning lr for the no-scale model the gap remains ~**0.015**.
- **Parameter budget (1B model):** all scale vectors together = **80,640 params = 7.84×10⁻⁵** of the 1,028,065,024-total Llama-1B; the unified strategy adds only **7.85×10⁻⁵** to Llama-1B.
- **MoE main results (Fig 7):** the unified strategy achieves **>0.02 lower terminal loss than the well-tuned Llama-MoE baseline across all five MoE sizes** (0.25B/0.5B/0.8B/1B/2B), with the gap widening over training and a slightly steeper fitted loss-descent slope.
- **Muon compatibility (Fig 8):** over the strong Muon baseline, the strategy still cuts terminal loss by **0.0156 (Dense-0.12B)**, **0.0167 (Dense-0.5B)**, and **0.0187 (MoE-0.5B)** — because Muon still optimizes γ with AdamW.
- **Overhead (Table 3, Llama-1B with torch.compile):** wall-clock **1.04×** (151→157 ms), memory **1.01×** (34263→34729 MiB); averaged over 10 post-warmup steps.
- **Parameter-count controlled ablation (Fig 10):** "Llama-large" widened to match param count (0.11985B) gives only marginal gain, whereas the unified strategy (also 0.11985B) reduces terminal loss by **0.033** — so the gain is not from extra capacity.
- **Step-by-step (Fig 5, 0.12B Llama):** each of HG, DP, OR/ER, and IWD yields a measurable terminal-loss reduction; DNP+OR is the single best combination, and OR+IWD adds further improvement once DNP introduces Output-Norm layers.

## Critical Assessment

- **Evaluation is loss-only:** all "improvements" are reported as terminal *validation loss*, with no downstream-task / benchmark accuracy, perplexity-on-holdout, or zero-shot eval — so the practical payoff of >0.02 lower loss on downstream capability is unquantified.
- **Theory ↔ practice gap:** the theorems (Theorems 2.2, 2.3, 3.1, 3.2) are proven under a single-linear-layer teacher (Setting 2.1) and gradient-flow/SGD-SDE approximations; the empirical gains come from full multi-layer Transformers with AdamW/Muon, so the "preconditioning" mechanism is asserted rather than directly measured in the real network (no reported Hessian-sharpness or preconditioner-eigenvalue curves on the actual LLM).
- **All scaling-law claims are fitted (Figs 6–7) rather than measured** at the largest scales (max 2B), so "more favorable scaling behavior" is an extrapolation.
- **No comparison to other recent scale/normalization variants** beyond Llama/Gemma baselines — e.g. DyT [51], DeepNet [38], HybridNorm [53], SeedNorm [4], and the "outlier-driven rescaling" work [28] are cited but not benchmarked head-to-head at matched compute.

## Strengths

- Genuinely novel framing: treats the long-ignored γ as an optimization (preconditioning) lever rather than an expressivity lever, with a clean unified rank-1-multiplicative interpretation.
- The Input-Norm vs Output-Norm wd distinction resolves a real, documented inconsistency across OLMo/nanoGPT/Qwen implementations.
- Improvements are nearly free (O(d) params, <4% wall-clock), making them trivially drop-in for any RMSNorm LLM.
- Very broad validation: 6 dense sizes, 5 MoE sizes, 2 optimizers, 2 schedules — unusually thorough for an architecture-tweak paper.

## Limitations

- Only up to 2B parameters; whether the gains hold at 7B–70B+ is unverified (the scaling-law argument is fitted, not measured).
- No downstream task evaluations — lower loss is a proxy, not a capability guarantee.
- Adds architectural hyperparameters (which placement variant, OR vs ER, which layers get HG) that were tuned on 0.12B and assumed to transfer.
- The theory's Setting 2.1 is a single linear map; extending to deep/nonlinear nets is left to future work.

## Relevance

直接对应 **"LLM Training Stability & Observability"**（归一化与权重衰减对训练稳定性和 Hessian 锐度的直接影响）与 **"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"**（基于梯度流/SDE 的严格预条件理论分析）。对关心 LLM 预训练稳定性与可扩展性的研究者高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the unified strategy's terminal-loss gain translate to zero-shot benchmark accuracy at 1B–2B, or does it plateau on downstream tasks?
- The paper's theory predicts bounded Hessian sharpness under IWD (Theorem 2.3) — were λ_max(∇²L), Tr(∇²L) actually measured on the real LLM to confirm the mechanism, not just the loss curve?
- Since Muon still optimizes γ with AdamW (the authors' stated reason for compatibility), would applying the unified scale-vector strategy *inside* Muon's matrix-update path change the gain?
- The paper cites DyT [51] and Transformers-without-normalization [51] as normalization-operation alternatives — how does the unified γ strategy compare when the normalization *operation* itself is replaced?
- Could the rank-1 multiplicative form W_c ⊙ (u_c v_c^T) (Eq. 13) be learned/sparsified rather than hand-placed, to auto-discover the best placement per layer?
