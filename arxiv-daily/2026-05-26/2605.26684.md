> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 GraphGPO，通过将所有 rollout 轨迹聚合为统一的状态转移图，利用全局图结构实现更精细的步骤级信用分配，显著提升了 LLM 智能体在多回合任务中的训练效率和最终性能。

> **TL;DR:** GraphGPO replaces coarse trajectory-level credit assignment in group-based RL with graph-based step-level advantages derived from a unified state-transition graph, achieving state-of-the-art performance on ALFWorld, WebShop, and Sokoban benchmarks.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **GraphGPO**（Graph-based Group Policy Optimization），将所有 rollout 轨迹聚合为统一的状态转移图，摆脱对轨迹级信用分配的依赖
- 设计了基于图的步骤级优势估计（graph-based advantage estimation），通过计算每个状态到目标状态的最短距离来衡量每一步的实际贡献
- 在 ALFWorld、WebShop 和 Sokoban 三个基准测试上全面超越 GRPO、GiGPO 等方法，图构建和优势计算的开销仅占总训练时间的 0.04%
- 已被 ICML 2026 接收

## Key Contributions

- Introduces GraphGPO, a critic-free group-based RL method that aggregates all rollout trajectories into a unified state-transition graph for global, structure-aware step-level credit assignment
- Defines a graph-based step-level reward based on shortest-path distance to the goal state, and derives graph-based advantages by grouping outgoing edges from the same state
- Proves monotonicity of graph-based advantage (Proposition 4.1): actions that move closer to the goal always receive higher advantages, even if they come from failed trajectories
- Proves conditional variance reduction (Proposition 4.2): graph-based step feedback has lower variance than trajectory-based step feedback
- Achieves state-of-the-art results across three benchmarks with negligible computational overhead (graph construction + advantage computation ≈ 0.04% of per-iteration runtime)

## Methodology

GraphGPO operates in three main stages:

1. **Graph Construction**: Given M rollout trajectories, all visited states are merged into nodes of a directed graph G = (S, E). Identical states across trajectories are merged into a single node. Directed edges represent state transitions with associated costs.

2. **Distance Estimation**: For each state s, the minimum cost to reach the goal state s_succ is computed via Dijkstra's shortest-path algorithm: d(s) = min over edges of c(s,a) + d(s'), with d(s_succ) = 0 and d(s) = +∞ for states unreachable from the goal.

3. **Graph-Based Advantage**: For each edge (s, a, s'), a step-level reward R_G(s, a, s') = r_succ · ω^{d(s')+c(s,a)} is computed, where ω ∈ (0,1) is a distance discount factor. Edges originating from the same state are grouped, and group-relative advantages are estimated. The final advantage combines graph-based and episode-level advantages: A(s, a, s') = β_G · A_G + β_E · A_E.

Key insight: ~22% of steps in failed trajectories actually contribute to task progress, while ~65% of steps in successful trajectories are non-progress. GraphGPO can correctly identify and reward valuable steps in failed trajectories while penalizing redundant steps in successful ones.

## Results & Highlights

**ALFWorld** (Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct):
- GraphGPO: 92.71% avg success rate vs GRPO: 77.86% (+14.85%)
- GraphGPO: 95.31% avg success rate (7B model) vs GRPO: 83.33% (+11.98%)

**WebShop**:
- GraphGPO achieves higher task scores and success rates across both model sizes
- 1.5B: 78.65% success rate vs GRPO 71.35% (+7.30%)
- 7B: 80.31% success rate vs GRPO 75.00% (+5.31%)

**Sokoban** (Qwen2.5-VL-3B-Instruct):
- GraphGPO: 86.98% success rate vs GRPO: 67.1% (+19.88%), vs GiGPO: 76.92% (+10.06%)

**Training dynamics**: GraphGPO shows substantially faster improvement in early training stages, with the performance gap most pronounced during mid-training when overall rollout success rate is low.

**Computational overhead**: Graph construction (0.108s) and graph-based advantage computation (0.025s) are negligible compared to rollout (216.9s) and policy update (74.2s).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: High. The idea of aggregating rollout trajectories into a unified state-transition graph for credit assignment is novel in the context of group-based RL for LLM agents. It fundamentally departs from the trajectory-level attribution paradigm.

**Rigor**: Strong. Two formal propositions with proofs (monotonicity and variance reduction). Extensive ablation studies including -A_E variants and dynamic sampling. Evaluation across three diverse benchmarks (embodied, web, visual game) with two model sizes.

**Reproducibility**: Good. Code is publicly available. Uses standard models (Qwen2.5) and well-known benchmarks. Training hyperparameters detailed in appendix.

**Significance**: High. Addresses a fundamental limitation of GRPO-style methods — the coarse trajectory-level credit assignment — with an elegant graph-based solution that adds negligible overhead. The approach is orthogonal to and composable with other improvements (e.g., dynamic sampling).

**Red flags**: The method relies on merging identical states across trajectories, which works well in structured environments (ALFWorld, WebShop, Sokoban) but may face challenges in environments with continuous or high-dimensional state spaces where exact state matching is rare. The deterministic environment assumption in the propositions is a theoretical limitation, though the method may work empirically in stochastic settings.

## Strengths

- Elegant formulation that cleanly separates step-level credit from trajectory outcomes
- Negligible computational overhead (0.04% of training time)
- Consistent improvements across all benchmarks and model sizes
- Faster convergence, especially in early training stages
- Theoretically grounded with formal propositions
- Practical: no additional model components (remains critic-free)
- Can correctly credit good actions in failed trajectories — a significant advantage in sparse-reward settings

## Limitations

- Relies on exact state matching for graph node merging; performance may degrade in environments with continuous observations or rare state repetitions
- Formal analysis (Propositions 4.1 and 4.2) assumes deterministic environments
- Only evaluated on relatively structured benchmarks (ALFWorld, WebShop, Sokoban); unclear how well it transfers to more open-ended agentic tasks (e.g., software engineering, web browsing with diverse page states)
- The distance discount factor ω and balancing factors β_G, β_E introduce additional hyperparameters
- Graph size scales with M × T, which could become large for very long-horizon tasks

## Relevance

本文直接解决了我感兴趣的核心问题：如何更有效地训练 LLM 智能体。GraphGPO 提供了一种无需额外 critic 模型、开销极小的步骤级信用分配方法，对多回合工具调用和推理场景的 RL 训练非常有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does GraphGPO perform in environments with stochastic transitions where the same action from the same state may lead to different next states?
2. Can approximate state matching (e.g., embedding similarity) extend the approach to continuous or high-dimensional observation spaces like real web browsing?
3. How does the method scale to longer horizons (e.g., 100+ steps) where the state-transition graph becomes very large?
4. What is the interplay between graph-based advantages and reward shaping or dense reward signals?
5. Can the graph structure be leveraged for planning or data augmentation beyond credit assignment?
