> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 s-Trace 方法，通过对10个7B-14B参数LLM的计算图进行贪心子图搜索，发现LLM计算分为两个阶段：0.01%-1%规模的早期层稀疏"核心子图"即可重建输出分布的头部（含top-1预测），1%-100%规模的后期层密集计算（以注意力头为主）则逐步精炼完整输出分布；计算密度与模型不确定性正相关，稀疏子图主要编码unigram频率等浅层统计量。

> **TL;DR:** The authors introduce s-Trace, a method that uses greedy best-first search on the LLM computational graph to find the minimal subgraph of size s that best reconstructs the full model's output distribution. Testing on 10 LLMs (7B–14B params), they discover a consistent two-phase computation structure: a construction phase (0.01%–1% of graph) dominated by early-layer residual+MLP edges that recovers the top-1% of the output distribution, and a refinement phase (1%–100%) driven by later-layer attention heads that incrementally fine-tunes the full distribution. A remarkably stable "minimal core" at ~0.1% of the graph suffices for greedy decoding. Computation density correlates with model entropy and inversely with token frequency, suggesting LLMs process easier inputs with sparser computation.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **s-Trace 方法**：基于贪心最佳优先搜索和L1范数重要性评分，从LLM计算图中高效提取大小为s的最优近似子图，仅需一次前向传播，无需训练。
- 发现 **两阶段计算组织结构**：建设阶段（0.01%-1%图规模）以早期层为主，边际效用高；精炼阶段（>1%）以晚期层注意力头为主，边际效用递减。
- 识别出 **极小核心**（minimal core）：仅占全图0.1%的子图即可稳定恢复top-1预测，且跨输入高度稳定（top 20%组件占80%的边）。
- 揭示 **计算密度与输入属性相关**：模型输出熵越高（越不确定）所需计算越密集；高频token只需稀疏计算即可预测，稀疏子图主要编码unigram频率等浅层统计量。
- **跨模型一致性**：10个LLM（7个模型家族）表现出高度一致的计算密度模式，跨模型相关性0.26–0.71（均值0.52），说明密度取决于输入属性而非架构细节。

## Key Contributions

- Introduce the **s-Trace method**, a training-free, single-forward-pass approach using greedy best-first search with L1-norm importance scoring to extract the optimal subgraph of size s from an LLM's computational graph.
- Discover a consistent **two-phase computation organization** across 10 LLMs: a construction phase (0.01%–1% of graph) with steep error decay driven by early-layer edges, and a refinement phase (>1%) with diffuse marginal gains driven by later-layer attention.
- Identify the **minimal core** (~0.1% of graph): a stable, architecture-invariant early-layer backbone sufficient for recovering the top-1 prediction, where the top 20% most frequent components account for 80% of all edges across inputs.
- Show that **computation density correlates with model uncertainty** (entropy: r ∈ [0.04, 0.37], μ = 0.22) and **inversely with token frequency** (r ∈ [-0.45, -0.22]), indicating sparser subgraphs encode shallow statistics like unigram frequency.
- Demonstrate **cross-model consistency**: computation density patterns correlate 0.26–0.71 (μ = 0.52) across models, suggesting density depends on intrinsic input properties rather than architecture.

## Methodology

**Computational Graph Formulation:** Each LLM is represented as a DAG where nodes are intermediate token representations (per-layer, per-token) and edges are transformer operations (attention heads, MLPs, residual connections). For a 100-token input with 32 heads and 32 layers, this yields ~6,300 nodes and ~5M edges. Attention is decomposed per-head per-source-token for fine-grained routing analysis.

**s-Trace Extraction:** Given a budget s, the s-Trace is the subgraph of exactly s edges minimizing Total Variation (TV) distance between the full and masked output distributions. Extraction uses greedy best-first search starting from the last-token node in the last layer, iteratively adding the highest-importance edge (scored by L1-norm, node-normalized). Edge masking zeroes out non-selected edges during inference.

**Evaluation:** Reconstruction fidelity is measured via Total Variation distance (preferred over KL for its boundedness and focus on dominant modes). The authors also define a scalar Computational Density metric C as the AUC of error-vs-log(trace-size), providing a per-input sparsity score.

**Experimental Setup:** 5,000 Wikitext sequences (~40 words each), 10 LLMs from 7 HuggingFace families (7B–14B params including Llama-3.1-8B, Mistral-7B, Qwen2.5-7B/14B, Qwen3-8B/14B, OLMo-2-7B/13B, DeepSeek-7B, Phi-4), 26 trace-size levels from 10^-5 to 0.8, yielding ~1.3M total traces.

## Results & Highlights

- **Pervasive sparsity:** A tiny subgraph (0.1% of full model) suffices for top-1 token prediction across all 10 models.
- **Construction phase (s ∈ [10^-4, 10^-2]):** Early-layer edges dominate (~80% of edges are initial/early-middle layers at s = 10^-3); the component composition is surprisingly balanced (~30% residual, ~20% MLP, ~50% attention), contrasting with attention's 99%+ share of total edges.
- **Refinement phase (s > 10^-2):** Late-layer attention heads increasingly contribute; log-linear scaling between trace size and nucleus reconstruction percentage suggests additive accumulation of relatively independent paths.
- **Stable minimal core:** The same small set of components appears across diverse inputs; in Qwen3-14B, the top 20% most frequent components account for 80% of edges at s = 10^-3.
- **Entropy–density link:** High-entropy outputs require extended refinement phases; low-entropy contexts achieve near-perfect reconstruction with minimal traces.
- **Frequency–density link:** More frequent tokens are associated with sparser computation (r ∈ [-0.45, -0.22] across models), confirming sparse traces encode superficial statistics.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The s-Trace method repurposes automated circuit discovery (IFR framework) for a novel purpose — quantifying per-input computation density rather than finding task-specific circuits. The two-phase finding is new and well-substantiated across 10 models.
- **Rigor:** The experimental design is strong: 10 models, ~1.3M traces, careful ablation comparisons. The use of Total Variation (bounded, mode-focused) rather than KL divergence is well-justified. The greedy search approximation is pragmatic but acknowledged as suboptimal — the paper provides a sanity check against a random baseline (App. D.1) but not against more sophisticated alternatives like activation patching at the same scale.
- **Reproducibility:** Code promised upon acceptance. The method requires only a single forward pass per trace extraction, making it computationally tractable. Dataset (Wikitext) and models (HuggingFace) are public.
- **Significance:** The finding that ~0.1% of an LLM's computation suffices for greedy decoding has practical implications for dynamic computation, early exit, and efficient inference. The link between computation density and input properties opens a new angle on linguistic complexity measurement.
- **Red flags:** (1) The L1-norm importance scoring is a heuristic — the paper acknowledges better methods could yield smaller traces, but the comparison is only against a weak random baseline, not against gradient-based or attribution-patching methods. (2) Zero-ablation can push activations off-manifold; the softmax-before-ablation sanity check is good but mean-ablation is deferred to future work. (3) Only English Wikitext is tested — generalization to other domains/languages is unverified. (4) The correlation with entropy (μ = 0.22) is statistically significant but moderate — the explanatory power of entropy alone for computation density is limited. (5) The paper does not demonstrate any downstream application (e.g., can we actually skip computation safely?).

## Strengths

- Elegant formulation of computation density as a per-input, measurable quantity with a clear mathematical definition.
- Broad empirical validation across 10 models from 7 families, lending credibility to the generality of the two-phase finding.
- The minimal core concept is intuitive and well-supported by multiple analyses (stability, layer makeup, component balance).
- Connects mechanistic interpretability to cognitive science questions about symbolic vs. distributed processing.
- Practical implications for interpretability research: provides guidance on appropriate circuit scales and highlights the importance of evaluating full distributions rather than binary task metrics.

## Limitations

- **Single-domain evaluation:** Only Wikitext (English Wikipedia) is tested; findings may not generalize to code, multilingual, or specialized domains.
- **Heuristic importance scoring:** L1-norm is simple but may miss important but low-magnitude contributions; comparison with more sophisticated methods is limited.
- **Zero-ablation concerns:** Zeroing edges can produce off-manifold activations; mean-ablation experiments are deferred.
- **No downstream application demonstrated:** The paper characterizes sparsity but does not show how to exploit it for efficiency gains (e.g., dynamic layer skipping).
- **Moderate entropy correlation:** At r = 0.22 mean, entropy explains only a small fraction of variance in computation density, suggesting other unmeasured factors drive most of the variation.

## Relevance

与LLM可解释性和内部机制分析直接相关，s-Trace为量化模型计算密度提供了系统化工具，对动态计算分配、早期退出策略和电路发现方法论有实际启发意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does s-Trace compare to gradient-based edge pruning (Bhaskar et al., 2024) or attribution patching (Syed et al., 2024) in terms of reconstruction fidelity at equivalent budgets?
- Can the minimal core be exploited for adaptive computation — e.g., routing high-confidence inputs through only 0.1% of the model while allocating full capacity to uncertain inputs?
- How does computation density vary across domains (code generation, mathematical reasoning, multilingual text) and longer contexts?
- What linguistic properties beyond entropy and unigram frequency drive computation density? Could syntax complexity, semantic ambiguity, or discourse structure be predictive?
- Does the two-phase structure hold for encoder-decoder models, mixture-of-experts architectures, or multimodal models?
