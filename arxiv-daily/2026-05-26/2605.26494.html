The MiniMax-M2 Series: Mini Activations Unleashing Max Real-World Intelligence

Aili Chen, Aonian Li, Baichuan Zhou + 170 authors (MiniMax) · 2026-05-26 · arXiv 2605.26494

M2 reaches frontier-competitive agentic performance activating only 4.3 % of its 229.9 B parameters.

Key Metric
4.3 % activation (9.8 B / 229.9 B) — yet scores 94.2 on AIME 2026 and 56.2 on SWE-bench Pro.
Why This Matters
Forge RL designs and self-evolution experiments provide directly reusable patterns for agentic training infrastructure.
Critical Edge
Self-evolution backed by a single benchmark; 7 of 20+ benchmarks are internal and unverifiable.

Methodology

Architecture
62-layer MoE decoder-only Transformer; 256 fine-grained experts, top-8 activation; sigmoid gating + learnable bias; GQA (48 query / 8 KV heads); 192K full-attention context; Multi-Token Prediction (K=3) for speculative decoding
Scale
229.9 B total / 9.8 B activated per token (4.3 %); hidden dim 3 072; vocab 200 064
Pre-training
29.2 T tokens (19.9 T constant + 9.3 T decay); code/math/STEM upsampled; context extended 8 K → 32 K → 192 K
Post-training
Four tracks: Agentic Coding (SWE-scaling, AppDev, Terminal-Gym), Agentic Cowork (deep search, office, finance, slides), Reasoning (query/response/training-side scaling), General conversation + RLHF
RL (Forge)
CISPO policy optimization with asymmetric clipping; windowed-FIFO scheduling (W = 0.3N); prefix-tree merging up to 40× speedup; heterogeneous prefill-decode disaggregation; global L3 KV cache pool
Interleaved Thinking
Plan-Act-Reflect loop with full reasoning state persistence across turns; contrasts with front-loaded or stateless per-turn reasoning
Self-evolution
M2.7 profiles runs, reads logs, diagnoses anomalies, debugs code, and adjusts configs autonomously; absorbed 30-50 % of daily RL team workload

Strengths

  • Frontier-competitive performance with only ~10 B activated parameters
  • Complete end-to-end system: model, data pipelines, RL infrastructure, deployment stack
  • Forge RL with principled engineering: windowed-FIFO scheduling, prefix-tree merging
  • Comprehensive post-training data pipelines with verifiable rewards across diverse domains
  • Honest reporting of weaknesses (Terminal-Bench, Finance Modeling, HLE gaps stated plainly)
  • Interleaved thinking with clear ablations showing state-persistence benefits
  • Monotonic within-series progression — data + RL investments translate to steady gains

Limitations

  • No open-source release plans
  • Gaps vs GPT 5.4: Terminal-Bench 57 vs 75, Finance 57 vs 75, HLE 28 vs 42
  • 7 of 20+ benchmarks are internal and cannot be externally verified
  • MMLU-Pro (81.8) notably weak relative to all frontier baselines (89–91)
  • Self-evolution: limited evidence (one benchmark case study, one scaffold anecdote)
  • 192K full-attention context window = significant inference cost despite low activation
  • No discussion of safety alignment, harmful content, or misuse prevention

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the 4.3 % activation ratio compare in quality-efficiency Pareto terms to DeepSeek-V3's ~5-7 %, and is there a floor below which sparsity hurts agentic capability?
  • Can Forge's windowed-FIFO and prefix-tree merging transfer to open-source RL frameworks (OpenRLHF, VERL) without proprietary infrastructure?
  • How robust is self-evolution beyond MLE Bench Lite — can M2.7 generalize scaffold optimization to novel, unseen domains?
  • What is the sensitivity of interleaved thinking gains to trajectory length — does the benefit saturate or compound at hundreds of turns?
  • Does sigmoid gating with expert bias generalize to different expert counts (64 or 512)?
  • How does Agent-as-a-Verifier handle cases where visual aesthetics and functional correctness conflict?
