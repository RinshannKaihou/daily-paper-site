> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Mixture of Activations (MoA)，一种 token 自适应的 FFN 激活函数混合机制，通过轻量级输入依赖门控在共享线性投影上混合多种激活函数，在 0.12B–2B 密集和 MoE 语言模型上一致降低训练损失并改善扩展行为，且几乎不增加参数和计算开销。

> **TL;DR:** The authors propose Mixture of Activations (MoA), a token-adaptive FFN design that mixes a dictionary of activation functions via lightweight input-dependent gates while sharing linear projections. MoA consistently outperforms SwiGLU baselines across dense and MoE LLMs (0.12B–2B) with only 1.03–1.13× wall-clock overhead, and the gains persist across scaling laws.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Mixture of Activations (MoA)**：用轻量级输入依赖门控（sigmoid/tanh/softmax）混合一组激活函数，同时共享 FFN 的线性投影权重，实现 token 级别的自适应非线性变换。
- 提出 **Learnable Activations (LA)**：作为输入无关的对照，将固定激活函数替换为激活函数的可学习线性组合，适用于 Type-I（ReLU 类）和 Type-II（SwiGLU 类）FFN。
- 建立严格的有限宽度表达能力层次：固定激活 FFN ⊊ LA ⊊ MoA。核心洞察：LA 允许每个神经元混合多种激活原语但权重全局共享；MoA 通过输入依赖门控实现沿脊线超平面的自适应振幅调制，这是 LA 无法做到的。
- 在密集 LLM（Llama, 0.12B–0.5B）和 MoE LLM（LlamaMoE, 0.25B–2B）上进行广泛预训练实验，涵盖 AdamW/Muon 优化器、cosine/wsd 学习率调度、20–100 TPP token 预算。
- 在 MoE-2B 模型上进行 zero-shot 下游评估（ARC-C, HellaSwag, OpenBookQA, WinoGrande），MoA 在多数任务上优于基线。
- 将 MoA 推广到视觉自监督预训练（MAE + ViT-B/16），同样取得更低验证损失。

## Key Contributions

- Introduce **Mixture of Activations (MoA)**, which uses lightweight input-dependent gates (sigmoid, tanh, or softmax) to mix a dictionary of activation functions within shared FFN linear projections, enabling per-token adaptive nonlinear transformations.
- Introduce **Learnable Activations (LA)** as an input-independent baseline, replacing fixed activations with learnable linear combinations for both Type-I and Type-II FFN architectures.
- Prove strict finite-width expressive separations (Theorem 4.1, 4.2): fixed-activation FFNs ⊊ LA ⊊ MoA for both Type-I and Type-II settings. The key mechanism is that MoA can create ridge singularities with amplitude that varies along the singular hyperplane (input-dependent), while LA can only assign constant amplitudes.
- Conduct extensive pre-training experiments on dense LLMs (Llama, 0.12B–0.5B) and MoE LLMs (LlamaMoE, 0.25B–2B) under diverse settings: AdamW/Muon optimizers, cosine/wsd schedules, 20–100 tokens-per-parameter budgets.
- Report zero-shot downstream results on ARC-C, HellaSwag, OpenBookQA, WinoGrande for MoE-2B, showing MoA improves average score from 42.20 to 42.96.
- Demonstrate generalization to vision self-supervised pre-training (MAE + ViT-B/16), where qd-MoA also achieves lower validation reconstruction loss.

## Methodology

**Standard FFN forms:**
- **Type-I**: `f(x) = W₂ σ(W₁x)`, with D=4d hidden width (ReLU, ReLU², GELU).
- **Type-II** (SwiGLU): `g(x) = W₃ σ(W₁x) ⊙ (W₂x)`, with D=8d/3 hidden width.

**Learnable Activations (LA)** — input-independent hybrid:
- Type-I LA: replaces σ with Σ αₖ σₖ(W₁x), adding only |K| scalar parameters.
- Type-II LA has three variants: one-sided (mix z-branch only), bi-sided (mix both branches), quadratic (mix pairwise products σₖ(y)⊙σₗ(z)).

**Mixture of Activations (MoA)** — token-adaptive hybrid:
- Type-I MoA: `f(x) = W₂ Σₖ πₖ(x) σₖ(W₁x)`, where πₖ(x) = ϕ(uₖᵀx) is input-dependent.
- Type-II MoA: same three variants as LA but with input-dependent mixing coefficients.
- Gating function ϕ: compared softmax, tanh, sigmoid; sigmoid performs best empirically.
- Soft gating by default (all activations contribute), no MoE-style hard routing needed since linear projections are shared.
- Activation dictionary K_F ⊆ {ReLU², GELU, SiLU, LeakyReLU, ReLU, tanh} for Type-I; K_G adds {Id} for Type-II.

**Theoretical analysis:**
- For Type-I: constructs T_LA(x) = ReLU(x₁) + ReLU²(x₁) (separates LA from fixed activations) and T_MoA(x) = tanh(λx₁)ReLU(x₂) (separates MoA from LA via nonconstant jump profiles).
- For Type-II: analogous constructions using multiplicative structure; MoA enables three-factor interactions (e.g., ReLU(x₂)ReLU(x₁)tanh(x₁)) that fixed Type-II FFNs cannot represent at finite width.

**Experimental setup:**
- Dense models: Llama architecture, 0.12B/0.25B/0.5B params, AdamW optimizer, cosine lr schedule, 20 or 100 TPP.
- MoE models: LlamaMoE, 0.25B–2B (activated 0.11B–0.62B), 32 sparse + 1 shared expert, Muon optimizer, wsd schedule, ~100 TPP by activated params.
- Vision: MAE + ViT-B/16, 800 epochs, batch 4096, mask ratio 0.75.

## Results & Highlights

**Dense LLM results:**
- MoA consistently achieves lower terminal validation loss across 0.12B and 0.25B models at 20 and 100 TPP.
- MoA tolerates larger peak learning rates (e.g., baseline best lr=2e-3 vs MoA best lr=3–4e-3 at 0.12B/20TPP).
- Type-II MoA (bi-MoA) slightly outperforms Type-I MoA overall.

**MoE LLM results (Figure 3):**
- one-MoA and qd-MoA outperform well-tuned Muon-trained LlamaMoE baselines across all scales (0.25B–2B).
- Gains exceed 0.01 in most experiments — nontrivial given the strong Muon baselines.
- Scaling law analysis: performance gap remains stable across model sizes; one-MoA and baseline curves are nearly parallel, suggesting gains persist at larger scales.

**Downstream zero-shot (Table 3, MoE-2B):**
| Benchmark | Baseline | one-MoA | qd-MoA |
|-----------|----------|---------|--------|
| ARC-C | 36.50 | 36.86 | 36.52 |
| HellaSwag | 43.31 | 44.54 | **44.91** |
| OpenBookQA | 30.20 | 29.80 | 30.20 |
| WinoGrande | 58.80 | 60.22 | 60.22 |
| **Average** | 42.20 | 42.86 | **42.96** |

**Overhead (Table 4, Dense-0.5B):**
- Wall-clock time: 1.03× (Type-I) to 1.13× (Type-II) increase.
- Memory: ~1.00× (essentially unchanged).
- Parameter-controlled ablation: MoA (0.11980B) reduces loss by 0.029 vs baseline, while matching parameters to 0.11981B yields almost no gain — improvement is not from extra parameters.

**Vision (MAE ViT-B/16, Figure 5):**
- qd-MoA achieves lower validation reconstruction loss than SwiGLU baseline.
- Best peak lr increases from 3e-4 (baseline) to 1.2e-3 (qd-MoA), consistent with LLM findings.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: MoA applies input-dependent gating at the activation level within shared projections — a natural but previously unexplored design point between learnable activations and MoE. The theoretical expressivity separation is a genuine contribution with clean constructive proofs.
- **Rigor**: Excellent coverage: multiple scales, architectures (dense + MoE), optimizers (AdamW + Muon), schedules (cosine + wsd), token budgets (20 + 100 TPP), parameter-controlled ablations, overhead measurements, and a vision generalization experiment. Downstream evaluation is included.
- **Significance**: The scaling law results showing parallel curves with the baseline are encouraging, but the largest model tested is 2B. Whether gains persist at 7B+ remains the critical open question. The 0.76-point average improvement on zero-shot benchmarks (42.20→42.96) is meaningful but modest.
- **Reproducibility**: The design is straightforward to implement. No explicit code release mentioned. Activation dictionaries and gating choices are clearly specified.
- **Red flags**: (1) OpenBookQA score slightly decreases with MoA. (2) bi-MoA underperforms on MoE-0.25B and matches baseline, suggesting not all MoA variants are universally better. (3) The lr tolerance improvement is interesting but adds tuning complexity. (4) "Part I" suggests key follow-up results are deferred.

## Strengths

- Strong theoretical grounding with constructive W^{1,∞} expressivity separation proofs for both Type-I and Type-II FFNs.
- Consistent empirical gains across all scales, architectures, and training configurations.
- Minimal overhead: 1.03–1.13× wall-clock, negligible memory and parameter increase.
- Parameter-controlled ablation proves the gain is from activation mixing, not extra parameters.
- Generalizes beyond LLMs to vision self-supervised pre-training (MAE).
- MoA is orthogonal to other architectural improvements (MoE, attention variants) and can be combined with them.

## Limitations

- Largest scale tested is 2B parameters; it remains unclear whether benefits persist at production scales (7B, 13B, 70B+).
- Downstream evaluation is limited to 4 zero-shot benchmarks on a single model size (MoE-2B); more comprehensive evaluation (MMLU, coding, math) would strengthen the claims.
- bi-MoA (the most expressive variant) does not consistently outperform simpler one-MoA, and actually underperforms on MoE-0.25B.
- MoA requires tuning the activation dictionary and gating function; the best choices differ between Type-I and Type-II FFNs.
- The improvement in downstream average score (0.76 points) is modest relative to the pre-training loss improvement.
- Inference latency impact at real-world throughput levels is not evaluated.

## Relevance

MoA 直接改进了 Transformer LLM 的 FFN 层核心设计，与当前 SwiGLU、MoE 等架构优化方向高度互补。论文提供了严格的表达能力理论保证和 0.12B–2B 规模的实证验证，对于关注 LLM 扩展效率和表达能力的研究者而言，这是一项架构级别的重要创新。该方法实现简单、开销极低，可直接集成到现有训练流程中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do MoA gains persist at 7B, 13B, and 70B+ parameter scales? The parallel scaling curves are encouraging but not conclusive.
- How does MoA interact with other activation-related innovations, such as ReLU² for sparsity or KAN-style edge activations?
- Can the activation dictionary be learned or pruned during training rather than manually specified?
- What is the interaction between MoA and post-training techniques (SFT, RLHF, quantization)?
- Does the improved lr tolerance have implications for faster training schedules or larger effective batch sizes?
- Can MoA be applied selectively to specific layers (e.g., only later layers) for further overhead reduction?
