> **一句话总结：** 本文指出在策略蒸馏（OPD）中存在"Off-policy Teacher Decay"——教师对学生后半段 token 的纠错能力随 student 前缀增长迅速衰减（MATH-500 上 avg@4 从 65.30% 跌到 51.75%），提出 Early Stopping Rollout（ESR）：仅用前 N=100 个 token 计算蒸馏损失，一行代码改动即可在跨代/跨家族（Qwen2.5→Qwen3、Gemma-2→Qwen3）设置下全面超越完整 rollout，并降低 24× wall-clock、4× peak memory，部分场景甚至超过教师。

> **TL;DR:** Identifies "Off-policy Teacher Decay" in on-policy distillation: a teacher conditioned on a student-generated prefix loses accuracy fast (Qwen3-1.7B avg@4 on MATH-500 drops from 65.30% baseline → 62.70% at N=100 → 51.75% at N=300, approaching the student baseline). Fix is Early Stopping Rollout (ESR): compute the reverse-KL distillation loss only on the first N response tokens (default N=100). Across tasks (MATH-500, HumanEval, BFCL), student scales 1.5B–32B, teachers 1.7B–72B, and families Qwen2.5/Qwen3/Gemma2/Gemma3, ESR matches or beats full-rollout OPD — e.g. Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B→Qwen3-1.7B MATH-500 avg@4 **65.85 → 73.45 (ESR) vs 67.45 (OPD)**, and Gemma-2-2B→Gemma-3-4B +12.75% avg@4 / +15.40% pass@4 over OPD under full finetuning — while delivering **24× wall-clock speedup and ~4× peak-memory reduction** (Table 3) and never collapsing where full-rollout OPD frequently does (5 of 9 cross-gen/cross-family cells collapse).

## 核心贡献

- 提出"Off-policy Teacher Decay"现象并量化：教师对学生前缀的条件准确率在 ~300 token 内就跌到学生基线水平（MATH-500 上 65.30%→51.75%），说明 OPD 对后期 token 的损失是基于失真的教师信号。
- 提出 Early Stopping Rollout（ESR）：把 student rollout 截断到前 N 个 token，蒸馏损失只在这些 token 上计算；对标准 OPD 训练循环仅做一行改动。
- 跨任务（数学/代码/函数调用）、跨训练方式（LoRA/FFT）、跨规模（学生 1.5B–32B、教师 1.7B–72B）、跨家族（Qwen/Gemma）系统验证 ESR 一致匹配或超越完整 rollout OPD，且 OPD 在跨代/跨家族设置频繁崩溃而 ESR 从不崩溃。
- 揭示两个机理："Cascading Alignment"——只在 [0,N] 训练，>N 位置的 KL 也下降 30–40%；"Sub-mode Commitment"——reverse-KL 的 mode-seeking 使学生聚焦到教师某个往往更优的子模态，使 ESR 学生有时超过教师。
- 工程收益显著：24× wall-clock、~4× peak memory（单 A6000 48GB，bs=16，学生 Qwen3-1.7B）。

## Key Contributions

- **Off-policy Teacher Decay (Sec 1, Fig 1 left):** Formalizes a recoverability gap ∆_decay(t)=A_T(x)−A_T(x|y^S_{<t}); on MATH-500 with Qwen3-1.7B teacher / Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B student prefix, avg@4 falls 65.30% → 62.70% (N=100) → 51.75% (N=300).
- **ESR method (Sec 3, Eq. 2):** L_ESR(N)=E_{y∼π_s, |y|≤N} Σ_{t=1}^{|y|} KL(π_s(·|x,y_{<t}) ‖ π_t(·|x,y_{<t})); N=100 default (N=50 for Gemma-2-2B→Qwen3-4B); cross-tokenizer pairs use decode→re-encode with greedy text-span alignment.
- **Main results (Table 1, 9 pairs):** ESR matches/beats OPD on every cell of MATH-500 across same-family/same-gen, same-family/cross-gen, and cross-family; many cells where OPD never reaches a functional checkpoint (peak <20%, marked ‡) — e.g. Qwen2.5-14B→Qwen3.5-35B-A3B OPD 5.40‡ vs ESR 75.15.
- **Cascading Alignment (Sec 5.1, Fig 2 right):** training only on positions [0,100] still reduces per-position KL on positions >100 by **30–40%**, so ESR does not need to see late tokens to fix them.
- **Sub-mode Commitment (Sec 5.2, Table 4):** ESR students commit to a non-dominant teacher sub-mode, producing **2–3× shorter** responses (median ~380 tokens vs teacher ~1150, full-rollout ~1530) and sometimes *exceeding* teacher accuracy (marked ⋆ in Tables 1–2).

## Methodology

**OPD baseline (Eq. 1).** Student generates y~π_s(·|x); loss L_full = E_y Σ_t KL(π_s(·|x,y_{<t}) ‖ π_t(·|x,y_{<t})), uniformly over all T positions.

**ESR (Eq. 2).** Truncate student rollout to first N tokens (default N=100); compute reverse-KL loss only over t=1..|y| (|y|≤N). EOS before N terminates naturally. Everything else (temperature 0.7, LoRA targets, optimizer, scorer) unchanged. For cross-tokenizer pairs, decode student rollout to text and re-encode under teacher tokenizer; reverse-KL computed on token-aligned spans via greedy text-span match.

**Training setup.** Reverse-KL loss, lr=5×10⁻⁵, generation T=0.7; LoRA r=32, α=64 default (full-finetune ablations also run); batch=16 problems with 1 rollout/problem; 200 training steps; checkpoint every 50; data from NuminaMath, CodeUltraFeedback, glaive-function-calling-v2. N=100 unless stated.

**Evaluation.** MATH-500 avg@4 (n=4, T=0.7); HumanEval pass@1 (T=0.0); BFCL full accuracy (correct function name + arguments).

**Models.** Same-family/same-gen: Qwen3-1.7B→Qwen3-4B, Qwen2.5-14B→Qwen2.5-Math-72B, Qwen2.5-32B→Qwen2.5-Math-72B. Same-family/cross-gen: Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B→Qwen3-1.7B, Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B→Qwen3-4B, Qwen2.5-Math-7B→Qwen3-14B, Qwen2.5-14B→Qwen3.5-35B-A3B, Gemma-2-2B→Gemma-3-4B. Cross-family: Gemma-2-2B→Qwen3-4B. Hardware: single A6000 48GB.

## Results & Highlights

- **Off-policy Teacher Decay (Fig 1 left, MATH-500, Qwen3-1.7B teacher):** avg@4 falls from **65.30% (unconditional) → 62.70% (N=100 prefix) → 51.75% (N=300 prefix)**, approaching the student baseline — empirically justifying that late-position teacher scores are unreliable.
- **Cross-generation distillation (Table 1, MATH-500, Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B→Qwen3-1.7B):** ESR **65.85 → 73.45 ⋆** (surpasses teacher) vs OPD 67.45↓−12.4 (collapses 12.4 points from peak to final); pass@4 ESR 69.20→74.30 ⋆ vs OPD 72.60.
- **Larger cross-generation (Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B→Qwen3-4B):** ESR 50.95→77.95 vs OPD **5.40‡ (never reaches functional checkpoint)**; pass@4 ESR 73.80→86.40 vs OPD 13.45.
- **Qwen2.5-14B→Qwen3.5-35B-A3B (MoE teacher):** ESR 13.45→77.95 vs OPD **16.40↓−11.5**; pass@4 ESR 83.85 vs OPD 86.40 (slight OPD edge but OPD collapses −17.2 to 27.00 by final checkpoint).
- **Cross-family Gemma-2-2B→Qwen3-4B:** ESR 19.90→75.15 vs OPD 22.95; pass@4 ESR 28.20 vs OPD 30.20 — ESR dominates, and is sensitive to N (stable at N=50, not N=100, consistent with larger teacher-student gap → faster Off-policy Teacher Decay).
- **Full-finetune (Table 2 right, Gemma-2-2B→Gemma-3-4B, MATH-500):** ESR dominates OPD by **+12.75% avg@4 and +15.40% pass@4**; on Qwen2.5→Qwen3 FFT OPD is marginally ahead (58.20 vs 56.20 avg@4).
- **Other tasks (Table 2 left):** HumanEval pass@1 Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B→Qwen3-1.7B ESR **42.10 ⋆** vs OPD 40.20↓−13.4 (collapses) vs teacher 39.60; BFCL Gemma-2-2B→Gemma-3-4B ESR **79.00 ⋆** vs OPD 76.83 ⋆ vs teacher 72.83.
- **Efficiency (Table 3, A6000, Qwen3-1.7B student, bs=16):** total wall-time per step **8s (ESR) vs 194s (OPD) = 24× speedup**, dominated by generation (5s vs 180s); peak GPU memory **24.1 GB vs 63.3 GB = 2.6×** (and 4.1× on training-phase memory, 9.6 vs 39.5 GB).
- **Cascading Alignment (Sec 5.1, Fig 2 right):** per-position KL on untrained positions >100 drops by **30–40%** after ESR training on [0,100] only.
- **Sub-mode Commitment (Sec 5.2, Table 4):** ESR students emit **2–3× shorter** responses (median ~380 tokens vs teacher ~1150, full-rollout OPD ~1530), prefer non-top-1 teacher tokens, and are more confident than OPD students — explaining how ESR sometimes exceeds the teacher.

## Critical Assessment

- **N is hand-tuned per pair:** the default N=100 fails for the cross-family Gemma-2-2B→Qwen3-4B pair (must drop to N=50), and the paper itself notes ESR becomes *more* N-sensitive the larger the teacher-student gap — so "a one-line change" understates the need to tune N, with no principled selector given.
- **Same-family/same-generation gains are marginal:** for Qwen3→Qwen3 and Qwen2.5→Qwen2.5-Math, full-rollout OPD sometimes even falls below the undistilled student (1.7B, 14B), and ESR's improvement there is small — the headline gains come almost entirely from cross-generation/cross-family settings where OPD is already unstable.
- **"Surpasses the teacher" claims rest on avg@4 at T=0.7:** the ⋆ cells compare ESR's avg@4 against the teacher's *unconditional* avg@4, but the teacher is sampled under the same T=0.7 — a more careful comparison (e.g. teacher best-of-N, or pass@1 greedy) might shrink or erase some of these wins.
- **No comparison to other token-weighting/reward-shaping baselines:** ESR is a hard position-cutoff; the paper does not compare against softer alternatives (e.g. decay-weighted loss, KL-based token weighting, reward-model discounting) that might capture the same intuition less abruptly.
- **The "Cascading Alignment" and "Sub-mode Commitment" mechanisms are supported by suggestive experiments (KL drop, response-length histograms) but not isolated causally** — e.g., no intervention forces a non-sub-mode commitment to test whether that alone removes the teacher-surpassing effect.

## Strengths

- Identifies and *quantifies* a real, previously-underappreciated failure mode (Off-policy Teacher Decay) with a clean recoverability-gap measurement.
- Genuinely simple fix (one-line rollout truncation) with broad, consistent empirical support across 9 model pairs, 3 tasks, 2 training regimes.
- Major engineering wins: 24× wall-clock, ~4× memory, plus training stability where OPD collapses.
- Honest about the N-sensitivity in cross-family settings and about when OPD already works (same-family/same-gen).

## Limitations

- N must be tuned per pair; no principled selector; cross-family is N-sensitive.
- Same-family/same-generation gains are small; OPD already struggles there.
- "Surpasses teacher" rests on avg@4@T=0.7 comparisons.
- No comparison to softer token-weighting alternatives.
- Mechanism explanations (Cascading Alignment, Sub-mode Commitment) are correlational, not interventional.

## Relevance

直接对应 **"LLM Training Stability & Observability"**（揭示 OPD 中教师信号随位置衰减的失稳机理，并提供更稳定的训练方法）和 **"LLM Inference Evaluation"**（涉及 avg@4 / pass@1 / BFCL 等推理评测协议与模型蒸馏后的能力评估）。对关注知识蒸馏、LLM 对齐与高效训练的研究者具有较高实用价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The cross-family pair needs N=50, not N=100 — can a principled, data-driven N selector (e.g. based on the measured ∆_decay curve) be derived instead of per-pair tuning?
- ESR is a hard position cutoff; how does it compare to a softer decay-weighted loss or a KL/entropy-based token reweighting that uses the same Off-policy Teacher Decay intuition less abruptly?
- "Surpasses teacher" (⋆) cells use avg@4 at T=0.7 — does the effect survive under greedy pass@1 or against a teacher best-of-N baseline?
- Cascading Alignment shows >N-position KL drops 30–40% without training there — is this because early tokens carry the planning/strategy (as the case study suggests), and would ESR fail on tasks where the hard reasoning is in the late tokens (e.g. long proof chains)?
- The paper cites Cloud et al. 2025 on "subliminal learning" — could the Sub-mode Commitment effect be a special case of subliminal learning from teacher prefixes, and could it be amplified deliberately?
