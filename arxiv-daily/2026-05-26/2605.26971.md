> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 ATLAS 框架来追溯 RLVR 训练数据的源头，发现现有数据集高度重叠且存在基准泄露风险，并基于溯源分析构建了去污染的高质量数据集 DAPO++。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes ATLAS to trace RLVR dataset lineage back to 20 atomic sources, revealing massive overlap and benchmark leakage across datasets, and builds a decontaminated dataset DAPO++ with a source-aware quality scoring framework.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 ATLAS（Atomic-source Tracing via Lineage-Aware Search）框架，系统追溯 RLVR 数据集的血缘关系，将 145 万条实例归因到 20 个原子来源，覆盖率超 99.7%
- 揭示了现有 RLVR 数据集之间的高度重叠问题——大多数数据集只是少数共享上游源的变体，真正引入新数据的很少
- 通过大规模语义相似度匹配发现 36,148 条基准泄露实例，暴露了广泛存在的数据污染风险
- 提出 Source-level Counterfactual Attribution（SCA）方法，通过逐源 RL 训练对比估计每个原子源的边际效用
- 设计了复合数据集质量评分 Q，与下游 RLVR 性能强相关，可用于预测训练效果
- 基于以上分析构建了去污染的 RLVR 数据集 DAPO++，在 Qwen3 系列模型上持续提升 held-out 基准表现

## Key Contributions

- Introduces ATLAS, a systematic lineage-tracing framework that attributes over 1.45M RLVR instances (99.7%) to 20 atomic data sources across 14 popular RLVR datasets
- Reveals that most RLVR datasets are recompositions of a small shared set of upstream sources, with few contributing genuinely new data
- Identifies 36,148 leaked instances across RLVR datasets via large-scale pairwise similarity matching against 14 math evaluation benchmarks, including cases where benchmarks originally designed for evaluation are directly included in training data
- Proposes Source-level Counterfactual Attribution (SCA), which trains per-source RL checkpoints against a shared base model to estimate marginal utility and label instance-level learnability
- Designs a composite dataset quality score Q combining static quality (verifiability, learnability, contamination) and dynamic utility (sampling efficiency gain, capability boundary expansion) that strongly correlates with downstream RLVR performance
- Curates DAPO++, a decontaminated RLVR dataset that consistently improves performance on held-out benchmarks across Qwen3-1.7B and Qwen3-8B models

## Methodology

The paper's methodology consists of two main pillars:

**ATLAS Framework (Data Lineage Tracing):**
1. **Stage 0 — Data Collection and Canonicalization:** Collects RLVR datasets and their upstream sources, manually inspects and standardizes question-answer pairs across heterogeneous schemas
2. **Stage 1 — Temporal Index Matching:** Uses SHA-1 hash-based indexing to efficiently match prompts across datasets in chronological order, building a global lineage dictionary
3. **Stage 2 — Semantic Similarity Matching:** For unmatched instances, applies Sentence-BERT embeddings with cosine similarity retrieval followed by manual human auditing
4. **Stage 3 — Iterative Source Recovery:** Iteratively identifies and incorporates candidate datasets until unmatched instances fall below 1% threshold

**SCA and Dataset Benchmarking:**
- SCA trains separate RL checkpoints on each atomic source using GRPO, comparing against the base model to classify instances into four categories: (1) learnable (base wrong → RL right), (2) too easy (both right), (3) too hard (both wrong), (4) degraded (base right → RL wrong)
- The composite quality score Q aggregates three dimensions: Static Quality S1 (verifiability, learnability, contamination), Sampling Efficiency Gain S2, and Capability Boundary Expansion S3
- Training uses VERL framework with GRPO, temperature 1.0, 500 steps, evaluated on Math500 for checkpoint selection

## Results & Highlights

- ATLAS traces 1,450,827 instances to 20 atomic sources with <1% unknown; the largest sources are cn_k12 (342K), olympiads (297K), synthetic_math (150K), and orca_math (125K)
- Major sources (NuminaMath-CoT, NuminaMath-1.5, LILA) account for the bulk of content in downstream RLVR datasets, with Eurus-2-RL-Data notably contributing MCQ-to-open-ended format transformations that were widely inherited
- **Benchmark leakage is pervasive:** 36,148 leaked instances found; some RLVR datasets directly include evaluation benchmarks (OmniMath, HARP) as training data; leakage persists even at lower similarity thresholds (~80%)
- DAPO++ consistently outperforms all baselines on held-out benchmarks across both Qwen3-1.7B and Qwen3-8B (e.g., Qwen3-8B average: 29.6 vs. next best 26.1 on math benchmarks)
- Quality score Q reliably predicts downstream RLVR training effectiveness, providing a practical tool for dataset selection
- Decontamination ablation shows that removing leaked instances generally improves or maintains performance while ensuring evaluation integrity
- MCQ ablation confirms that open-ended formats provide stronger RLVR training signals than multiple-choice questions

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. This is the first systematic study of data provenance in the RLVR ecosystem. The ATLAS framework and SCA methodology address a genuinely underexplored problem — "provenance collapse" in RLVR datasets. The lineage-aware perspective is original and practically valuable.

**Rigor:** Strong. The multi-stage matching pipeline (exact hash → semantic similarity → iterative recovery) with human auditing is methodologically sound. The SCA approach of per-source counterfactual training is principled, though computationally expensive. Sample sizes are adequate (1.45M instances, 20 sources, 14 evaluation benchmarks).

**Reproducibility:** Good. Code and data are publicly available. Hyperparameters are fully specified. However, the extensive manual inspection and case-by-case auditing (30-50 instances per dataset, human similarity verification) creates a reproducibility barrier for the lineage tracing component.

**Significance:** High. The findings about dataset overlap and benchmark contamination have immediate practical implications for the RLVR community. The DAPO++ dataset and quality scoring framework provide actionable tools. However, the study is limited to mathematical reasoning RLVR — generalization to other domains (code, science, etc.) is not explored.

**Red flags:**
- The quality score Q requires training per-source RL checkpoints, which is computationally expensive and may not scale well
- The analysis is limited to math reasoning; whether findings generalize to other RLVR domains is unknown
- The paper does not address how ATLAS would handle future datasets with more complex lineage chains
- Some hyperparameters for Q are set via grid search on specific models, raising questions about transferability

## Strengths

- First comprehensive provenance analysis of RLVR datasets, filling a critical gap in the literature
- Identifies concrete, actionable problems (benchmark leakage, dataset overlap, MCQ vs. open-ended)
- The SCA framework provides a principled way to evaluate data source utility via counterfactual training
- DAPO++ demonstrates practical benefits — consistent improvements across model scales and held-out benchmarks
- Quality score Q offers a practical dataset selection tool that correlates well with downstream performance
- Open-source release of code, data, and detailed documentation

## Limitations

- Scope is limited to mathematical reasoning RLVR; generalization to code, science, or other domains is not investigated
- The ATLAS pipeline requires substantial manual effort for canonicalization and similarity auditing, limiting scalability
- SCA requires training one RL checkpoint per atomic source (20 checkpoints), which is computationally prohibitive for larger models or more source types
- The quality score Q hyperparameters are tuned via grid search on Qwen3 models; transferability to other model families is not validated
- The paper does not explore the interaction effects between atomic sources (sources are evaluated independently)
- Results are primarily on Qwen3 series models; robustness across diverse architectures is not demonstrated

## 相关性

本文对 RLVR 训练数据的溯源分析和去污染方法与强化学习训练数据质量研究高度相关。其揭示的数据集重叠和基准泄露问题对所有从事 LLM 推理训练的研究者都有重要警示意义，SCA 方法为数据筛选提供了可借鉴的思路。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does ATLAS scale to non-mathematical RLVR domains (e.g., code generation, scientific reasoning, tool use)?
2. Can the SCA methodology be made more efficient, e.g., through proxy models or gradient-based attribution instead of full RL retraining?
3. What are the interaction effects between atomic sources — does combining certain sources yield superlinear or sublinear gains?
4. How stable is the quality score Q across different base model architectures (Llama, Mistral, etc.) beyond Qwen3?
5. How should the RLVR community establish standardized provenance metadata to prevent future provenance collapse at the source?
