Signal-to-Noise Ratio and Sample Size Govern Representational Alignment in Neural Networks

Ali Hussaini Umar & Alessandro Laio 2026-05-26 arXiv 2605.26973
Key Finding

Representational alignment is governed monotonically by SNR and non-monotonically by sample size, and does not predict generalization.

Key Metric
Interpolation Threshold
# parameters ≈ # training samples — alignment reaches its minimum here
Why This Matters

Challenges the assumption that representational alignment predicts generalization, with direct implications for transfer learning and representation distillation.

Critical Edge

Findings come from simplified linear networks and controlled noise; scaling to Transformers and modern architectures remains unproven.

Methodology

  • Train ensembles of neural networks on regression and classification tasks, perturbing training sets with independent noise realizations to control SNR
  • Compare across nonlinear networks on real data and an analytically tractable single-hidden-layer linear network
  • Systematically vary both SNR and sample size to map their effects on representational alignment
  • Measure alignment via representational similarity analysis (RSA) across ensemble members

Results

  • Alignment varies monotonically with SNR — cleaner signal yields more aligned representations
  • Alignment varies non-monotonically with sample size, dipping at the interpolation threshold
  • Consistent across linear/nonlinear networks, regression/classification, synthetic/real data
  • Stronger alignment is not a reliable proxy for generalization error

Strengths & Limitations

  • Clean experimental design isolating SNR and sample size effects
  • Analytical tractability via the linear network provides theoretical grounding
  • Consistent findings across multiple settings strengthen generality
  • Directly challenges the alignment–generalization coupling assumption
  • Limited to simple architectures and controlled noise; modern Transformers unexplored
  • Noise perturbation model may not capture real-world data variability
  • Abstract-only analysis limits assessment of quantitative details

  • Can the analytical linear-network framework be generalized to deeper architectures?
  • Should we prefer models trained at certain SNR or sample size regimes for transfer learning and representation distillation?
  • How does the choice of alignment metric (CKA, RSA, SVCCA) affect the observed relationships?
