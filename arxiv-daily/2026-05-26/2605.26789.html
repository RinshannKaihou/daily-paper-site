Composition Collapse: Stable Factual Knowledge Does Not Imply Compositional Reasoning

Zhe Yu, Wenpeng Xing, Yunzhao Wei, Jie Chen, Hongzhi Wang, Xuyang Teng, Meng Han
Zhejiang University · HIT · HKBU
Key Finding
Post-training recipes with identical atomic knowledge diverge by 40+ percentage points in compositional reasoning—invisible to aggregate benchmarks.
Key Metric
+40pp
Residual composition failure divergence at matched atomic stability (<2pp difference)
Why This Matters
Aggregate benchmarks mask catastrophic composition gaps—evaluating post-training without controlling for atomic stability is fundamentally misleading.
Critical Edge
SFT reasoning-trace distillation actively worsens composition by 52 pp; deep-chain estimates rest on n < 15.

Methodology

1
Decompose each multi-hop question into k atomic facts with aligned sub-questions and paraphrase probes.
2
Gate 1 — Atomic Stability. Every atom must be answered consistently across all paraphrase variants (LLM adjudication). Filters out lucky-correct answers.
3
Gate 2 — Sub-question Correctness. Each sub-question must be answered correctly in isolation. Ensures independent verbalization.
4
Residual Composition Failure. Among cases passing both gates: fraction where the main multi-hop answer is still wrong. Upper-bounds pure composition failure.
5
Three-channel decomposition: Δatom (stability), Δcomp (residual failure), Δdepth (critical depth d50) — enables fair cross-recipe comparison.
6
Causal intervention: Four LoRA-GRPO variants on Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct with different depth coverages, isolating training objective from base-model confounds.

Benchmark: D4 V2 — 390 multi-hop questions, depths 2–11, four task families. Models: Llama-3-8B, Mistral-7B, Qwen2.5-7B (base); Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, Mistral-7B-Instruct (RLHF); DeepHermes-3-8B (SFT distill); Qwen3-8B (RLVR).

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths
  • Clean separation of knowledge vs. composition failure through the double-gate protocol
  • Causal intervention design (same base model, data, LoRA budget, varying only objective) is exemplary
  • Finding that SFT trace distillation worsens composition is provocative and well-supported
  • CoT diagnostic elegantly localizes bottleneck to generation-time computation
  • Honest reporting of limitations and measurement noise throughout
Limitations
  • Restricted to temporal factual composition; other types only preliminarily explored
  • Gate-passing n drops sharply with depth; d=8 CIs exceed ±25 pp
  • Only 7–13B models tested; frontier-scale behavior unknown
  • Closed-book protocol excludes RAG and in-context settings
  • Residual failure includes ~14–21% format artifact inflation
  • Weak cross-depth transfer under LoRA; full fine-tuning may differ

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does composition collapse appear at frontier model scale (70B+, 405B+), or does it diminish with scale?
  2. How does the double-gate protocol apply to non-temporal composition (logical deduction, spatial reasoning, causal chains)?
  3. Can the three-channel decomposition be integrated into training-time objectives (e.g., a reward that explicitly targets Δcomp)?
  4. What is the relationship between composition collapse and attention-head composition mechanisms—can mechanistic interpretability identify the specific circuits that fail?
  5. Does full fine-tuning (vs. LoRA) produce stronger cross-depth generalization, and at what compute cost?
