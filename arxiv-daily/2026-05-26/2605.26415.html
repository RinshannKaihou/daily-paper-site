The Rescue Effect: Spatio-Semantic Early Exit Bypasses Quantization Collapse in CLIP

Kahyeon Nam, Hyesong Choi Soongsil University 2026-05-26 quantization early-exit CLIP efficient-inference

Early-exit inference in INT8-quantized CLIP paradoxically improves accuracy by bypassing deep-layer noise accumulation — less computation yields better results.

Key Metric
+2.44%p 61.16% vs 58.72% INT8 baseline on ImageNet zero-shot, with 13.4% FLOPs reduction
Why This Matters
Directly studies how quantization precision errors degrade representations layer by layer — core to computation reliability research.
Critical Edge
Single backbone (ViT-B/32), single dataset (ImageNet-1K), single quantization method — generalization claims are speculative.

Methodology

  • Problem: INT8 quantization noise in CLIP ViT-B/12 grows super-linearly with depth — reaching 52% noise-to-signal ratio at Layer 11 (QIRC)
  • Spatio-Semantic Aggregation (SSA): Replaces shallow [CLS] tokens with LayerNorm of the mean of all 196 patch tokens, achieving Θ(1/N) variance reduction
  • Multi-Feature Learned Gate: Per-layer linear-sigmoid gate fusing max softmax confidence, top-2 margin, and spatial-activation variance
  • Layer-adaptive Confidence Thresholding: Each exit layer gets a distinct threshold τL calibrated to its Information-to-Noise Ratio
  • Pathological Layer Pruning: Layer 9 excluded (2× MSE of neighbors) via INR-driven rule, preventing up to 3.63%p degradation

Results

  • ImageNet-1K zero-shot: 61.16% Top-1 (+2.44%p) with 13.38% FLOPs saving
  • With SmoothQuant: further boosted to 62.41% (+3.69%p over baseline)
  • SSA vs [CLS] at Layer 8: 45.60% vs 21.73% (+23.87%p)
  • Factorial ablation: routing dominates at +1.24–1.51%p; architecture +0.46%p; distillation +0.47%p
  • Without FP32 supervision: hard-label-only still achieves +1.70%p
  • Very low variance: σ = 0.06%p accuracy across 3 independent runs

Strengths

  • Exceptionally thorough ablation design isolating individual contribution sources
  • QIRC characterization fills a genuine gap in understanding VLM quantization failures
  • Rescue Effect is genuinely surprising — challenges the accuracy-efficiency trade-off assumption
  • Clear mechanistic explanation via cosine-direction drift analysis

Limitations

  • No retrieval evaluation — CLIP's primary use case beyond classification
  • Text encoder kept in FP16 — full INT8 deployment scenario untested
  • 13.4% FLOPs saving is modest; unclear if gains hold for INT4 or larger models
  • Pathological Layer Pruning threshold (κ=0.5) appears empirically chosen; no sensitivity analysis
  • Exit head training requires ImageNet training data — not purely post-training

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the Rescue Effect generalize to ViT-L/14, EVA-CLIP, and INT4 quantization regimes?
  • Can the QIRC framework apply to LLM decoder architectures where quantization noise may similarly accumulate?
  • How does the Rescue Effect manifest in retrieval tasks (COCO, Flickr30K) where cosine alignment is directly evaluated?
  • Is the INR-based Pathological Layer Pruning criterion stable across GPTQ, AWQ, and SmoothQuant?
  • Could SSA be combined with structured pruning or knowledge distillation for compounded efficiency gains?
