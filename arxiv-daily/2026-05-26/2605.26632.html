RT-Lynx: Putting the GEMM Sparsity In a Right Way for Diffusion Models

Xing Cong, Hanlin Tang, Kan Liu, Lan Tao, Lin Qu, Chenhao Xie — Alibaba Group

Key Finding

RT-Lynx shifts N:M sparsity from weights to activations in DiTs, preserving quality while achieving 1.55× speedup with fused CUDA kernels.

Key Metric
1.55×
linear-layer speedup · 1.88× Sparse GEMM
Why This Matters

Near-lossless inference acceleration for state-of-the-art DiT models — directly valuable for large-scale image generation deployment.

Critical Edge

Speedup hinges on custom NVIDIA Sparse Tensor Core CUDA kernels; AMD/Intel portability is unexplored.

Methodology

  1. Activation Sparsification with Norm Compensation — Top-K enforces 2:4 semi-structured sparsity on activations (not weights). A rescaling factor s = √(‖X‖² / (‖X̃‖² + ε)) preserves the original L2-norm, eliminating norm attenuation from pruning.
  2. LoRA Adaptation via Knowledge Distillation — A low-rank residual branch (rank = 64, only 0.73% parameter overhead) is trained to minimize MSE between student and teacher noise predictions under the diffusion framework.
  3. Fused CUDA Kernel Pipeline — Online Top-K sparsification, norm compensation, and Sparse Tensor Core GEMM are fused into a single pass, reducing sparse overhead from >40% down to 4–17%.
  4. Selective Layer Skipping — In single-stream models (FLUX.1-dev, Z-Image), sensitive layers (e.g., Out-Down mixed layers with RFE up to 0.275) are excluded from sparsification.

Strengths

  • Thorough empirical motivation for activation > weight sparsity in DiT.
  • Near-lossless quality across diverse architectures and scales (6B–20B).
  • Significant speedup with <1% parameter overhead.
  • Compatible with quantization and distillation for compound gains.
  • Principled error compensation: norm compensation + LoRA.

Limitations

  • Requires custom CUDA kernels; naive sparse overhead exceeds 40%.
  • LoRA fine-tuning adds training cost and requires a teacher model.
  • Selective layer skipping is model-specific, needs per-architecture analysis.
  • Limited to NVIDIA Sparse Tensor Core hardware.
  • Video diffusion and non-image modalities are unexplored.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does activation sparsity interact with more aggressive quantization (INT4/INT8) beyond FP8?
  2. Can selective layer skipping be automated via a learned or heuristic criterion?
  3. Does the paradigm extend to video diffusion models (e.g., Sora-class) where temporal attention may differ in sparsity?
  4. What is the energy efficiency impact — do Sparse Tensor Cores provide proportional power savings?
  5. How does it perform with dynamic batch sizes or variable-resolution inputs in production serving?
