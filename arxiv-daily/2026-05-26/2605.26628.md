> **一句话总结：** 本文将 ViDiT-Q 后训练量化流程适配到 Wan2.2-I2V-A14B 视频生成模型，在 HiFloat4 W4A4 格式下量化 800 个线性层，通过尾部感知百分位校准替代硬最大值统计，使视频生成均值仅下降 0.092，但主体一致性仍有显著退化。

> **TL;DR:** Adapts ViDiT-Q PTQ to Wan2.2 under W4A4 HiFloat4 format, quantizing 800 linear layers across two transformers while retaining 12 boundary modules in BF16. A tail-aware percentile calibration replaces max-based activation statistics for channel balancing. On 720p 61-frame generation, unweighted VBench mean drops only 0.092 (0.680→0.588), but subject consistency suffers a large 0.40 drop, revealing identity preservation as the key bottleneck of aggressive 4-bit activation quantization.

## 核心贡献

- 将 ViDiT-Q 后训练量化流程适配到 Wan2.2-I2V-A14B 双 transformer 架构，对两个 transformer 模块共 800 个线性层进行 HiFloat4 W4A4 伪量化
- 提出尾部感知百分位校准（tail-aware percentile calibration），用高百分位统计量替代硬最大值来估计激活范围，减少稀有异常值对通道掩码的过度影响
- 设计紧凑 PTQ 状态格式，仅存储逐层通道掩码和量化描述符而非完整变换权重，降低存储开销并确保与原始 BF16 检查点的可追溯性
- 在 720×1280 分辨率、61 帧、40 步去噪的完整生成流程下评估 W4A4 系统，报告美学质量、运动平滑度、主体一致性等多维度量化结果

## Key Contributions

- Adapt ViDiT-Q-style PTQ pipeline to Wan2.2-I2V-A14B dual-transformer architecture, quantizing 800 linear layers with HiFloat4 W4A4 fake quantization while retaining 12 boundary modules (embeddings, output projections) in BF16
- Introduce tail-aware percentile calibration that replaces hard max activation statistics with empirical p-th percentile for channel-mask construction, reducing systematic range over-expansion from rare outliers
- Design a compact PTQ checkpoint format storing per-layer masks and quantization descriptors rather than duplicating full BF16 weights, reducing storage overhead
- Evaluate under full Wan2.2 generation protocol (720×1280, 61 frames, 40 denoising steps, CFG 3.5) and report multi-dimensional VBench metrics

## Methodology

The pipeline has three stages: **calibration**, **PTQ state construction**, and **quantized inference**.

**Calibration:** The BF16 Wan2.2 model runs on 16 calibration prompts (from OpenS2V-5M) with a fixed blank placeholder image (since the challenge uses I2V interface for T2V evaluation). Hooks collect absolute activation values per input channel for each target linear layer. Instead of using the maximum absolute activation value (which is dominated by rare outliers), the method computes the empirical p-th percentile: $a_i^p = Q_p(\{|x_{j,i}|\}_j)$, providing a robust range estimate per channel.

**PTQ state construction (Algorithm 1):** For each target layer, channel masks are constructed SmoothQuant-style: $m_i = w_i^\alpha / (a_i + \epsilon)^{1-\alpha}$, where $w_i = \max_o |W_{o,i}|$ is the max weight magnitude and $a_i$ is the percentile-based activation statistic. The weight matrix is pre-scaled: $\tilde{W} = W \cdot \text{diag}(m)^{-1}$, then quantized: $\hat{W} = \text{HiF4}(\tilde{W})$. At runtime, activations are scaled by $m$ before quantization, keeping the computation mathematically equivalent.

**Layer selection:** Each of the two Wan2.2 transformers has 400 HiFloat4 linear layers and 6 retained BF16 layers (time/text embedding projections, output projections). No full transformer block is kept in high precision — the challenge-allowed budget of 2 high-precision blocks goes unused.

**Inference:** The compact PTQ state is applied to the original BF16 checkpoint at inference time, and the same sampling protocol (scheduler, resolution, frames, CFG) is used for both BF16 and W4A4 runs.

## Results & Highlights

| Metric | BF16 | HiFloat4 W4A4 | Delta |
|--------|------|---------------|-------|
| Imaging quality | 0.7027 | 0.6507 | -0.052 |
| Aesthetic quality | 0.5456 | 0.5458 | +0.0002 |
| Overall consistency | 0.2263 | 0.2308 | +0.0045 |
| **Subject consistency** | **0.9331** | **0.5324** | **-0.401** |
| Motion smoothness | 0.9923 | 0.9803 | -0.012 |
| **Unweighted mean** | **0.6800** | **0.5880** | **-0.092** |

- Aesthetic quality and overall consistency are essentially preserved — global visual style survives W4A4 quantization
- Motion smoothness drops only 0.012, indicating temporally coherent frame transitions are maintained
- **Subject consistency is the dominant failure mode** (-0.401), reflecting identity drift, local blur, and object inconsistency from accumulated 4-bit quantization errors across denoising steps
- Qualitative results confirm scene layout and plausible motion are preserved, but fine subject details degrade

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-low. The core pipeline is ViDiT-Q applied to a new model. The percentile calibration is a straightforward and sensible modification (replacing max with a percentile), but not conceptually surprising. No rotation, no mixed-precision beyond boundary retention, and the high-precision block budget goes unused.
- **Rigor:** The evaluation is thorough on the VBench suite with matched generation settings, which is commendable. However, no ablation study compares percentile vs. max calibration directly, making it hard to assess the actual contribution of the tail-aware component. No sensitivity analysis on the percentile hyperparameter $p$.
- **Reproducibility:** High. The compact PTQ state design, explicit generation protocol (resolution, frames, steps, seed), and public base model (Wan2.2-I2V-A14B) make reproduction straightforward.
- **Significance:** The 0.401 drop in subject consistency is a serious concern — this is not a production-ready W4A4 system. The paper honestly reports this, which is commendable, but the practical value is limited until identity preservation is addressed.
- **Red flags:** No ablation on the key hyperparameter (percentile value $p$). The challenge-constrained setting (fixed HiFloat4 format, specific model) limits generalizability. The unused high-precision block budget suggests the authors did not explore mixed-precision strategies that could have mitigated the subject consistency issue.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-structured pipeline with clear separation of calibration, PTQ, and inference stages
- Honest reporting of the subject consistency failure mode rather than cherry-picking metrics
- Compact PTQ state design avoids redundant weight storage
- Percentile calibration is a principled improvement over max-based statistics for outlier-prone activations
- Fully matched evaluation protocol ensures fair comparison between BF16 and W4A4

## Limitations

- No ablation study isolating the effect of percentile calibration vs. max calibration
- Subject consistency drops by 43% (0.933→0.532), making the system unsuitable for applications requiring identity preservation
- Specific to Wan2.2 and HiFloat4 format; unclear how well the approach transfers to other architectures or quantization formats
- Does not use optional ViDiT-Q rotation or explore the allowed high-precision block retention
- Only 16 calibration prompts — sensitivity to calibration set size/composition is not analyzed
- No report of actual inference speedup or memory savings

## Relevance

与低比特量化和大模型部署直接相关。W4A4 视频生成的量化实践及主体一致性退化现象对后续量化研究有重要参考意义，百分位校准思路可借鉴至其他扩散模型量化场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How sensitive is the method to the choice of percentile $p$? Is there a sweet spot that better balances subject consistency against outlier handling?
- Would using the allowed 2 high-precision transformer blocks for identity-sensitive layers significantly recover subject consistency?
- How does the compact PTQ state approach compare to full-weight checkpointing in terms of quantization error accumulation?
- Could integrating ViDiT-Q's optional rotation component mitigate the subject consistency degradation?
- What are the actual FLOPs reduction and memory savings of W4A4 HiFloat4 vs. BF16 on real hardware?
