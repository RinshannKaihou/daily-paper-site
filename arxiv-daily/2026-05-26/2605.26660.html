WINDQuant: Weight-Informed Neural Decision-Making for Global Mixed-Precision LLM Quantization

Phong Nam Huu Nguyen, Khoi M. Le, Cong-Duy T Nguyen, Anh Tuan Luu, Thong Thanh Nguyen, Tho Quan

RL-driven column-level bit allocation achieves best-in-class ~2-bit LLM quantization at 36× less compute.

Key Metric
70.22

avg accuracy on LLaMA-3.1-8B at 1.96 bits

Why This Matters

Ultra-low-bit LLM compression without retraining, making 70B deployment feasible on a single GPU.

Critical Edge

Weight-only; no activation or KV-cache quantization. Many stabilizers make the pure RL contribution hard to isolate.

Methodology

WINDQuant decomposes each weight matrix into column-level chunks (size 256) and treats mixed-precision allocation as a finite-horizon MDP. A two-layer MLP actor-critic with LayerNorm observes a 15-dimensional state per unit — weight statistics, activation-aware features, compression progress, budget remaining — and selects from 7 precision levels (1 / 1.58 / 2 / 3 / 4 / 8 / 16-bit).

Reward Design

  • Episode-end: normalized bit reduction minus quality penalty (ε=0.3), budget violation, perplexity ratio (soft threshold 3, hard 20), skip penalty, and quality bonus (PPL ratio <2).
  • Per-step shaping: lightweight feedback nudging budget-feasible actions.
  • Lagrangian relaxation via projected gradient ascent to enforce quality constraints.

Training

  • Curriculum learning: 3.0 → 2.5 → 2.0 bits.
  • Budget-aware action masking and layer-specific minimum precision constraints.
  • Top 3% salient weights stored in INT8 (activation-aware protection).
  • 64 Open-Platypus samples (seq len 256) as fast proxy reward.

Efficiency

  • 49 hours / 17.1 GB on a single H200 for 8B model.
  • ParetoQ: 1,777 hours / 82.4 GB for comparable quality — a 36× compute advantage.
  • LLM-QAT: 126.9 GB memory with degenerate perplexity.
  • Standalone 2-bit PTQ baselines (AWQ, GPTQ, OmniQuant) collapse (PPL > 10⁴).

Strengths

  • Fine-grained column-chunk allocation captures within-layer sensitivity that layer-level methods miss.
  • Best compression–accuracy trade-off at ~2 bits across 1B–70B scales.
  • 36× more efficient than ParetoQ while matching quality.
  • Transparent reporting: degenerate baselines, efficiency metrics, reproduced numbers.
  • Curriculum learning stabilizes exploration in the ultra-low-bit regime.

Limitations

  • Weight-only quantization; no activation or KV-cache support.
  • Proxy reward (Open-Platypus, seq 256) may not reflect long-context or domain-shift behavior.
  • Many stabilizers (protection, fallback, masking) obscure the isolated RL contribution.
  • Only LLaMA-family models tested; generalization to Mistral, Qwen, etc. unverified.
  • 70B trained with only 24 episodes vs. 100 for smaller models — convergence at scale uncertain.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does the learned policy generalize across architectures (Mistral, Qwen, Gemma) and training objectives?
  2. What is the sensitivity to proxy calibration corpus choice and sequence length?
  3. Can the RL policy transfer or warm-start across scales (train on 8B, deploy to 70B)?
  4. How do learned allocation patterns relate to Hessian-based weight importance heuristics?
  5. Is extending to activation quantization (W8A8 / W4A4) feasible within the same RL framework?
