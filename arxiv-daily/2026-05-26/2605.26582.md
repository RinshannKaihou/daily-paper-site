> **一句话总结：** 本文从信息论角度严格证明了离散扩散模型中随机性通过冗余转移实现纠错的机制，提出DCRS训练无关采样算法，通过交替前向/反向扩散过程在低NFE条件下于图像基准上实现最高10倍步数缩减。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proves via information-theoretic analysis (KL contraction) that stochasticity in discrete diffusion corrects model approximation errors through redundant transitions. The proposed DCRS algorithm alternates forward/reverse steps for controlled stochasticity injection, achieving up to 10× step reduction on CIFAR10/CelebA in the low-NFE regime, though language results are more nuanced.

## 核心贡献

- 系统揭示了马尔可夫转移中随机性程度对离散扩散采样速度-质量权衡的控制机制：高确定性转移收敛快但误差累积，高随机性转移收敛慢但能实现纠错
- 通过信息论分析（KL散度收缩系数）严格证明了冗余转移（对称交换状态间概率质量的转移）能收缩采样误差，并给出前向/反向过程的收缩界（Theorem 3.1-3.3）
- 证明了均匀腐蚀过程的纠错能力强于掩码腐蚀过程（状态空间小时 η_KL^U < η_KL^M），且掩码扩散在所考虑的前向过程中具有最差收缩率
- 提出DCRS（Discrete Churn and Restart Sampling）训练无关推理算法，通过重启（全局前向-反向交替）和搅动（局部前向-反向微扰）两种机制注入可控随机性
- 在CIFAR10/CelebA上实现最多10倍采样步数缩减，同时保持竞争力FID

## Key Contributions

- Systematic characterization of the stochasticity speed-quality tradeoff in discrete diffusion: deterministic transitions converge fast but accumulate errors; stochastic transitions converge slowly but correct errors
- Information-theoretic proof (Theorems 3.1–3.3) that redundant transitions — symmetric mass-exchanging transitions that leave marginals unchanged — provably contract KL divergence errors in both forward and reverse processes
- Proof that uniform corruption achieves stronger contraction than masking corruption (η_KL^U < η_KL^M for small state spaces), and that masking achieves worst-case contraction among the considered forward processes
- Theorem 3.3 reveals two competing terms in the error bound: A_k (contracts accumulated errors, decreases with stochasticity) vs B_k (amplifies model approximation error, increases with stochasticity)
- DCRS: a training-free algorithm combining restarting (global forward-reverse alternation without extra NFE) and churning (local forward-reverse perturbations) for controlled stochasticity injection

## Methodology

**Theoretical analysis.** The authors study one-dimensional discrete diffusion under uniform and masking corruption processes. They define the contraction coefficient η_KL(p_s, q_{t|s}) = sup D_KL(q_{t|s}q_s || q_{t|s}p_s) / D_KL(q_s || p_s) and prove:

- **Theorem 3.1 (Forward contraction):** For masking, η_KL^M = α_s/α_t; for uniform, η_KL^U ≤ α_s/((S-2)α_t + 2). Uniform dominates masking when S is small.
- **Theorem 3.2 (Reverse contraction):** DPF sampling satisfies η_KL ≤ σ_{s|t} + (1-σ_{s|t})η_KL(p_t, p_{0|t}). Stochasticity reduces the contraction coefficient.
- **Theorem 3.3 (Error bound):** TV(p_θ^{t_{k+1}} || p_{t_{k+1}}) ≤ A_k · TV(p_θ^{t_k} || p_{t_k}) + B_k · ε_k, where A_k contracts with stochasticity but B_k grows — formalizing the tradeoff.

**DCRS algorithm.** Two mechanisms: (1) Restarting — run DPF from t=1 to t_min, then alternate K times between forward q_{t_max|t_min} and reverse Churn_θ(t_max → t_min). Forward steps use closed-form transitions (zero extra NFE). (2) Churning — within each restart window, at discretization points t_i, advance to (1+γ)t_i via forward process, then DPF-step to t_{i+1}. Uses trapezoidal higher-order solver inside restart window only (outside window, higher-order solvers cause catastrophic error compounding).

**Experimental setup.** Image: pretrained models on CIFAR10 (discretized Gaussian corruption, Campbell et al. 2022) and CelebA (Zhang et al. 2023), FID over 50K samples. Language: UDLM on LM1B (uniform corruption) and MDLM on OpenWebText (masking corruption). Synthetic: 1D (15 states) and 8D mixture-of-Gaussians (quantized, 128-bit sequences).

## Results & Highlights

- **CIFAR10/CelebA:** DCRS achieves competitive FID with 20–30 steps (vs. hundreds for τ-leaping), up to 10× reduction. Restart window [0.7, 0.8] with single restart iteration. Adding ν_t=0.01 stochasticity outside restart window slightly boosts performance.
- **Language (UDLM):** Deterministic vs. stochastic samplers achieve comparable performance — parallel decoding errors are the main bottleneck, not stochasticity level.
- **Language (MDLM, Table 1):** ReMDM error-correcting effects disappear without nucleus sampling. Hypothesis: nucleus sampling sharpens p_{0|t}, pushing toward worst-case contraction regime.
- **Synthetic 1D (Fig. 2):** With exact scores, DPF converges faster. With noisy scores, τ-leaping achieves lower final error — confirming error-correcting role of stochasticity. Gap widens under geometric schedule (σ_{s|t} → 1 near noise).
- **Mixture of Gaussians (Fig. 3):** DCRS achieves best speed-quality tradeoff across both uniform and masking corruption. Restarting alone provides most benefit in controlled settings.
- Key hyperparameters: restart window [t_min, t_max], K restart iterations (diminishing returns after first), churning γ ≈ 10^{-3} (larger values hurt via discretization error).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The KL contraction analysis of stochasticity in discrete diffusion is genuinely novel. The insight that redundant transitions serve as error-correcting channels (via data processing inequality) is elegant and previously unformalized. DCRS is conceptually similar to continuous restart sampling (Xu et al. 2023) but adapted with discrete-specific considerations (e.g., forward steps cost zero NFE).
- **Rigor:** Three theorems with proofs, extending analysis from 1D to multi-dimensional settings via independence assumptions. The error bound (Theorem 3.3) cleanly separates accumulated error contraction (A_k) from model error amplification (B_k). Controlled experiments isolate the two error sources.
- **Reproducibility:** Algorithm described precisely (Algorithm 6 in appendix). Uses publicly available pretrained checkpoints. Hyperparameters reported. Should be largely reproducible.
- **Significance:** Strong practical impact on image generation (10× speedup). However, the limited gains on language are a significant caveat — language is the primary use case for discrete diffusion. The authors honestly attribute this to parallel decoding errors dominating over stochasticity effects.
- **Red flags:** (1) DCRS has three hyperparameters (t_min, t_max, K, γ) that require tuning; the paper acknowledges this limitation but provides limited guidance. (2) The language results are negative — the method doesn't help where it arguably matters most. (3) The theoretical analysis is restricted to 1D; extension to D dimensions relies on independence assumptions that may not hold in practice. (4) The comparison with continuous diffusion churning/restart methods (EDM2, Karras et al. 2022) is not directly addressed.

## Strengths

- Principled information-theoretic framework with formal proofs connecting stochasticity to error correction
- Clean theoretical decomposition of the speed-quality tradeoff into competing error terms (Theorem 3.3)
- DCRS is training-free and leverages closed-form forward transitions (zero extra NFE for restart)
- Comprehensive empirical validation: synthetic → image → language, with honest negative results on language
- Useful insight that uniform corruption provides stronger error correction than masking corruption

## Limitations

- DCRS requires manual hyperparameter tuning (restart window, K, γ); no adaptive mechanism is provided
- Language results show negligible improvement — parallel decoding errors dominate, and stochasticity alone cannot address cross-dimensional error correction
- Theoretical analysis limited to 1D with independence assumptions for multi-dimensional extension
- The optimal stochasticity level is model- and task-dependent; the paper provides intuition but no automated procedure
- Higher-order solvers only work inside the restart window (catastrophic failure outside), limiting flexibility

## 相关性

本文对离散扩散模型的采样机制提供了深刻的信息论分析，揭示了随机性在纠错中的核心作用。DCRS算法在图像生成场景中效果显著。对于研究生成模型推理优化、扩散模型理论和采样策略的研究者具有较高参考价值。但在语言建模场景中，并行解码误差是主要瓶颈，随机性注入的帮助有限。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can adaptive methods automatically determine optimal restart windows and stochasticity schedules without manual tuning?
- Could cross-dimensional stochastic transitions (beyond per-dimension independence) address the parallel decoding error bottleneck in language tasks?
- Does the KL contraction framework extend meaningfully to continuous diffusion, or is it fundamentally a discrete phenomenon?
- How does DCRS interact with distillation-based acceleration methods (e.g., Sahoo et al. 2025) — are they complementary or redundant?
- What is the wall-clock time comparison (not just NFE) between DCRS and baselines, given that restart forward steps use closed-form transitions but churning adds computation?
- Could the theoretical framework predict a priori which corruption processes and model architectures would benefit most from DCRS?
