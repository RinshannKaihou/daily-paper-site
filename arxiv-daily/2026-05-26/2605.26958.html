2605.26958 Reinforcement Learning Reward Design LLM-as-Judge

Tournament-GRPO: Group-Wise Tournament Rewards for Reinforcement Learning in Open-Ended Long-Form Generation

Zixuan Yang, Yiqun Chen, Wei Yang, Erhan Zhang, Zihan Shen, Xiaochi Wei, Yan Gao, Yi Wu, Yao Hu, Jiaxin Mao — Renmin Univ. of China, USC, Zhejiang Univ., Xiaohongshu
Key Finding

Replacing pointwise rubric scores with repeated tournament comparisons among rollouts produces better-calibrated RL rewards and a 4.52-point gain over the strongest baseline.

Key Metric
+4.52
over best baseline on Deep Research Bench
Why This Matters

Solves the reward calibration and discrimination bottleneck in LLM-as-judge RL training for open-ended generation, a fast-growing subfield.

Critical Edge

Evaluated on a single benchmark with a single LLM judge. No human evaluation. Generalization to other tasks and judges remains unproven.

Methodology
  1. Rollout Sampling — For each query, the policy (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct) samples K rollouts using ReAct-style tool calls (search, browse).
  2. Tournament Process — Repeated M times: rollouts are shuffled (reducing position bias), partitioned into groups of size G, and an LLM judge (Qwen2.5-72B) picks Kwin winners per group based on query-specific rubrics. Winners accumulate 1 point per round survived.
  3. Normalization — Accumulated tournament scores are min-max normalized per query to produce relative rewards ritour.
  4. GRPO Integration — Final reward: ri = ritour + rifmt (binary format reward), fed into GRPO’s group-relative advantage estimation.
Complexity: O(MK) — linear in rollouts K × repeats M, vs O(K²) for exhaustive pairwise
Results
55.09
Best overall (G=4, M=8)
52.28
Binary, epoch 1 — fast start
  • Binary tournaments (G=2) provide sharp early learning; larger-group tournaments excel after longer training with finer-grained discrimination.
  • Outperforms pairwise comparison (50.57) and best absolute-scoring baseline GDPO (41.20).
  • 2 training epochs on DR Tulu RL-stage data. Public code: GitHub
Assessment

Strengths

  • Directly fixes practical rubric scoring limitations
  • Simple, general design applicable to any group-based RL
  • O(MK) efficiency beats O(K²) pairwise while scoring higher
  • Thorough ablations on tournament hyperparameters
  • Public code with clear documentation

Limitations

  • Still inherits LLM judge biases; mitigates but doesn't eliminate errors
  • Additional judge-call overhead vs pointwise scoring
  • No single best config; G, Kwin, M must be jointly tuned
  • Single benchmark & single judge — generalization uncertain
  • Only relative preference; no fine-grained factual feedback
Questions for Further Exploration
  1. How does Tournament-GRPO transfer to other open-ended generation tasks beyond Deep Research Bench?
  2. Can tournament rewards be combined with fine-grained factuality verification for richer training signals?
  3. Is there a principled way to adaptively select tournament hyperparameters as the policy evolves during training?
  4. Does tournament comparison amplify or mitigate systematic LLM judge biases compared to pointwise scoring?
  5. Could curriculum-style tournament designs (binary early, larger groups later) combine the benefits of both structures?
