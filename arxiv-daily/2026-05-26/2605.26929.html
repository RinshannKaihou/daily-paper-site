When Muon Optimizer Meets Adversarial Training: A Theoretical and Empirical Study

Yan, Huang, Zuo, Mo, Fang, Wu, Wei · 2026-05-26 · arXiv 2605.26929
Key Finding
Muon’s polar-decomposition updates impose a spectral-norm stability ceiling on weights, achieving adversarial robustness competitive with SGD and superior to AdamW.
Key Metric
63.32
Union robustness on ViT-L (ℓ2 training) — SGD: 58.27, AdamW: 56.51
Why This Matters
Optimizer geometry is security-critical: Muon bridges the gap between SGD’s robustness and AdamW’s training speed.
Critical Edge
Muon lags SGD on ImageNet ResNet-50; no code released, and large-scale ViT results remain absent.

Methodology

Polar Decomposition Newton-Schulz Iterations APGD Inner Attack Multi-norm Sampling Spectral-norm Ceiling

Update Rule

For each 2D weight tensor, Muon computes momentum-accumulated gradient Mt, applies polar decomposition via Newton-Schulz iterations to produce an approximately orthogonal update Ut = polar(Mt), then updates: Wt+1 = (1−ηλ)Wt − ηUt.

Theoretical Chain

  • Prop 2.1 → Links robustness to input gradient dual norms
  • Prop 2.2 → Bounds input Jacobian via layer spectral norms
  • Thm 2.1 → Establishes spectral-norm stability ceiling: ||Wt+1||2 ≤ (1−ηλ)||Wt||2 + η(1+εt)
  • Thm 2.2 → Provides union robustness certificate across ℓp norms
  • Thm 2.3 → Nuclear-norm descent guarantee with structurally larger per-step descent than SGD

Benchmarks

CIFAR-10: PreActResNet-18, WRN-34-10, WRN-34-20, ViT-B, ViT-L. ImageNet: ResNet-50. Attacks: APGD (training), PGD-100 & C&W-100 (evaluation), white-box & black-box transfer.

Strengths

  • First principled theoretical analysis of Muon’s update geometry in adversarial training
  • Comprehensive matrix: 5 architectures × 4 threat models × 3+ optimizers × white-box & black-box
  • Clean theoretical chain from input gradients through spectral norms to robustness certificates
  • Honest positioning vs. SAM — geometry alternative, not replacement
  • Black-box transfer evaluation adds real-world attack relevance

Limitations

  • ImageNet experiments limited to a single ResNet-50; ViT results at scale are absent
  • Muon trails SGD on ImageNet, raising scalability concerns
  • No code repository released, limiting immediate reproducibility
  • Spectral-norm ceiling bounds update behavior, not actual weight norms in practice
  • Checkpoint selection based on ℓ∞ robustness may bias multi-norm evaluation

Questions for Further Exploration

Can Muon’s ImageNet scalability be improved through learning rate warmup or layer-wise LR tuning?
How does Muon interact with other defenses (data augmentation, feature denoising) in combined AT pipelines?
What is the computational overhead of Newton-Schulz iterations vs. standard SGD/AdamW at scale?
Can the spectral-norm stability ceiling be tightened into a formal robustness certificate with practical ε?
How does Muon behave under AutoAttack or ensemble attacks beyond PGD/C&W?
