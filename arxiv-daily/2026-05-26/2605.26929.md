> **一句话总结：** 本文系统研究了 Muon 优化器（通过极分解对矩阵更新进行正交化）在对抗训练中的表现，理论上证明了其对权重谱范数的稳定性约束，实验上在多种架构和威胁模型下展现出优于 AdamW 且可比肩 SGD 的鲁棒性。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides the first comprehensive study of the Muon optimizer in adversarial training, proving that its polar-decomposition-based orthogonalized updates impose a spectral-norm stability ceiling on weight matrices, and empirically demonstrating competitive robustness with SGD and consistent superiority over AdamW across multiple architectures and ℓp threat models.

## 核心贡献

- 首次将 Muon 优化器引入对抗训练（AT）范式，系统研究正交化矩阵更新几何对鲁棒性的影响
- 证明 Muon 的极分解更新对权重矩阵施加谱范数稳定性上界（Theorem 2.1），限制训练动态中不受控的谱增长
- 证明 Muon 在 min-max 优化中沿核范数方向下降，具有比 SGD 更大的单步保证（Theorem 2.3）
- 跨 5 种架构、3 种 ℓp 威胁模型（ℓ∞、ℓ₁、ℓ₂）及其联合威胁模型进行全面实验评估
- 包含白盒攻击、黑盒迁移攻击和 ImageNet 大规模验证

## Key Contributions

- First systematic study of the Muon optimizer in adversarial training, identifying optimizer geometry as a security-relevant factor
- Theoretical proof that Muon's polar update imposes a spectral-norm stability ceiling (Theorem 2.1): weight spectral norms satisfy recursive upper bound ||W_{t+1}||₂ ≤ (1−ηλ)||W_t||₂ + η(1+ε_t)
- Proof of nuclear-norm descent guarantee for Muon under min-max dynamics (Theorem 2.3), showing structurally larger per-step descent than SGD
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation across 5 architectures (PreActResNet-18, WRN-34-10, WRN-34-20, ViT-B, ViT-L), 3 threat norms, and union threat models
- Black-box transfer attack evaluation demonstrating Muon's cross-model robustness generalization

## Methodology

- **Muon update rule:** For each 2D weight tensor, Muon computes the momentum-accumulated gradient M_t, then applies polar decomposition via Newton-Schulz iterations to obtain an approximately orthogonal update U_t = polar(M_t), and updates W_{t+1} = (1−ηλ)W_t − ηU_t
- **AT framework:** Uses APGD (Auto Projected Gradient Descent) as the inner attack with multi-norm sampling per mini-batch, supporting ℓ∞, ℓ₁, ℓ₂, and union threat models
- **Theoretical analysis chain:** (1) Proposition 2.1 links robustness to input gradient dual norms; (2) Proposition 2.2 bounds input Jacobian via layer spectral norms; (3) Theorem 2.1 establishes spectral-norm stability ceiling; (4) Theorem 2.2 provides union robustness certificate; (5) Theorem 2.3 characterizes descent dynamics
- **Benchmarks:** CIFAR-10 with PreActResNet-18, WRN-34-10, WRN-34-20, ViT-B, ViT-L; ImageNet with ResNet-50
- **Attack methods:** APGD for training; PGD-100 and C&W-100 for evaluation (both white-box and black-box transfer)

## Results & Highlights

- **CNNs (CIFAR-10):** Muon achieves best union robustness on WRN-34-10 (49.71 under ℓ∞-only training) and WRN-34-20 (43.03 under ℓ∞+ℓ₁), substantially outperforming AdamW and competitive with or surpassing SGD
- **ViTs (CIFAR-10):** Muon consistently outperforms AdamW; on ViT-L under ℓ₂ training, Muon achieves 63.32 union robustness vs. SGD's 58.27 and AdamW's 56.51
- **ℓ₁ and ℓ₂ robustness:** Muon shows particularly strong gains under ℓ₁ and ℓ₂ threat models, e.g., WRN-34-20 ℓ₂ training: 70.37 robustness (SGD: 64.42, AdamW: 61.62)
- **Black-box transfer:** Muon-trained models show strong resistance to transfer attacks; WRN-34-10 under ℓ₂ training achieves 86.86/86.71 (best/last) PGD transfer robustness
- **ImageNet:** SGD still outperforms Muon on ResNet-50, suggesting sensitivity to large-scale learning rates as a bottleneck
- **SAM comparison:** Muon is positioned as an optimizer-geometry alternative (not a replacement) to Sharpness-Aware Minimization; it does not uniformly dominate SAM but avoids the extra weight-perturbation step

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High — this is the first study connecting orthogonalized optimizer geometry to adversarial robustness. The theoretical framework (spectral-norm ceiling, nuclear-norm descent) is original and well-grounded.
- **Rigor:** Strong theoretical proofs with clear assumptions; empirical evaluation is thorough across architectures, threat models, and attack types. Checkpoint selection bias is honestly acknowledged.
- **Reproducibility:** Good — training hyperparameters (learning rates, epochs, perturbation budgets, batch sizes) are fully specified. Code does not appear to be released with this preprint.
- **Significance:** Important finding that optimizer choice is security-critical in AT. The Muon optimizer offers a practical middle ground between SGD (robust but slow) and AdamW (fast but less robust).
- **Red flags:** (1) ImageNet results are limited to ResNet-50 only due to compute constraints, leaving the large-scale ViT question open. (2) The theoretical spectral-norm ceiling is a structural property, not a tight robustness certificate — the authors are transparent about this. (3) No code repository mentioned.

## Strengths

- First principled theoretical analysis of Muon's update geometry in the AT context
- Comprehensive experimental matrix: 5 architectures × 4 threat models × 3+ optimizers × white-box & black-box
- Honest positioning of Muon relative to SAM (geometry alternative, not replacement)
- Clean theoretical chain from input gradients → layer spectral norms → stability ceiling → union robustness certificate
- Black-box transfer evaluation adds real-world relevance

## Limitations

- Large-scale (ImageNet) experiments limited to a single ResNet-50 architecture; ViT results on ImageNet are absent
- Muon lags behind SGD on ImageNet, suggesting scalability challenges that need further investigation
- No code release mentioned, limiting immediate reproducibility
- The spectral-norm ceiling bounds update-level behavior but does not directly guarantee smaller weight norms in practice
- Checkpoint selection based on ℓ∞ robustness may bias multi-norm evaluation

## 相关性

本文将优化器设计引入对抗训练的安全视角，Muon 的正交化更新几何为理解鲁棒性提供了新思路。对于关注对抗鲁棒性、优化器设计或训练稳定性的研究具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can Muon's ImageNet scalability be improved through learning rate warmup schedules or layer-wise learning rate tuning?
- How does Muon interact with other defense mechanisms (e.g., data augmentation, feature denoising) in a combined AT pipeline?
- What is the computational overhead of Newton-Schulz iterations compared to standard SGD/AdamW steps in large-scale settings?
- Can the spectral-norm stability ceiling be tightened into a formal robustness certificate with practical ε values?
- How does Muon behave under auto-attack or ensemble attack evaluation beyond PGD/C&W?
