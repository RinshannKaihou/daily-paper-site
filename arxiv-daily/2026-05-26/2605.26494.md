> **一句话总结：** MiniMax发布M2系列MoE大模型（229.9B总参数/9.8B激活，62层Decoder-only Transformer，256细粒度专家+sigmoid门控），专为Agent场景端到端设计，配套自研Forge RL系统（窗口FIFO调度+前缀树合并，最高40x训练加速），并在M2.7版本中实现模型自主调试训练过程和修改自身脚手架代码的自进化能力，在SWE-bench Pro 56.2、AIME 2026 94.2、BrowseComp 77.8等基准上接近GPT 5.4和Claude Opus 4.6水平。

> **TL;DR:** MiniMax introduces the M2 series, a 229.9B total / 9.8B activated MoE language model purpose-built for agentic deployment. Key components: (1) agent-driven data pipelines for SWE, AppDev, terminal tasks, deep search, office work, and financial analysis — each with executable environments and artifact-aligned rewards; (2) Forge, an agent-native RL system with windowed-FIFO scheduling, prefix-tree merging (up to 40x speedup), and white-box/black-box agent support; (3) M2.7 self-evolution where the model autonomously debugs training runs and modifies its own scaffold. M2.7 scores 56.2 on SWE-bench Pro, 94.2 on AIME 2026, 89.8 on GPQA-Diamond, and 77.8 on BrowseComp — broadly competitive with Claude Opus 4.6 and GPT 5.4 while activating only ~10B parameters per token.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"mini activations"设计原则：MoE架构仅激活4.3%参数（9.8B/229.9B），采用256个细粒度专家、sigmoid门控和可学习专家偏置，大幅减少辅助负载均衡损失
- 构建覆盖10+编程语言的SWE-scaling数据管线，从GitHub PR自动生成可验证的bug修复/功能添加/性能优化任务及Docker环境
- 设计AppDev管线：领域专家定义meta queries + Agent-as-a-Verifier三层评估（执行层/交互层/视觉美学层）+ Prompt蒸馏使模型内化专家最佳实践
- 开发Forge RL系统：窗口FIFO调度平衡吞吐与分布一致性，前缀树合并实现最高40x训练加速，支持白盒和黑盒Agent统一训练
- M2.7展示初步自进化：自主诊断训练异常、修改Agent脚手架、100轮自主迭代优化编程脚手架获得30%性能提升
- 设计交织思维（Interleaved Thinking）机制：推理-行动-反思循环中保持完整推理状态跨轮次持久化，在深度搜索和SWE任务上带来显著提升

## Key Contributions

- A 62-layer MoE decoder-only Transformer with 229.9B total / 9.8B activated parameters, 256 fine-grained experts with sigmoid gating and learnable bias, GQA (48 query / 8 KV heads), 192K native context window, and Multi-Token Prediction for speculative decoding
- SWE-scaling pipeline: automated 6-stage pipeline converting GitHub PRs into diverse, verifiable SWE-style training data across 10+ languages, with Docker environment synthesis, task-type-specific reward functions (F2P/P2P for bugs, P2P for perf optimization), bug injection, commit merging, and SWE-Test conversion
- AppDev pipeline with Agent-as-a-Verifier (AaaV): three-layer evaluation (execution, interaction via Playwright, visual aesthetics), prompt distillation to internalize expert behavior without inference-time scaffolding
- Terminal-Gym: Stack-Overflow-grounded automated synthesis of terminal tasks with difficulty calibration and query evolution
- Forge RL system: (a) CISPO policy optimization with asymmetric importance sampling clipping; (b) windowed-FIFO scheduling (W=0.3N) balancing throughput vs distributional consistency; (c) prefix-tree merging achieving up to 40x training speedup with zero approximation error; (d) heterogeneous prefill-decode disaggregation and global L3 KV cache pool
- Interleaved Thinking: Plan-Act-Reflect loop with full reasoning state persistence across turns, contrasting with front-loaded or stateless per-turn reasoning
- M2.7 self-evolution: Model Iteration System where M2.7 autonomously profiles runs, reads logs, diagnoses anomalies, debugs code, and adjusts configurations — absorbing 30-50% of daily RL team iteration workload and executing 100-round autonomous scaffold optimization

## Methodology

**Architecture.** M2 is a 62-layer decoder-only Transformer with hidden dim 3,072, vocab size 200,064. Each block has multi-head attention (48 query heads, 8 KV heads with GQA, RoPE) followed by an MoE FFN with 256 fine-grained experts and top-8 activation. Sigmoid gating with per-expert learnable bias replaces softmax top-k, removing the zero-sum constraint and reducing auxiliary loss dependency. A Multi-Token Prediction module (K=1 at pre-training, expanded to K=3 via weight copying during continued pre-training) enables speculative decoding at inference. The authors chose full attention over hybrid SWA/linear attention after extensive ablations showed SWA degraded long-context retrieval (RULER 128K CWE: 90.0→72.0) and multi-hop reasoning, despite matching on short-context tasks.

**Pre-training.** 19.9T tokens in the constant phase + 9.3T in the decay phase (29.2T total), with code/math/STEM significantly upsampled. Context extended progressively from 8K→32K→192K through multi-stage training with code concatenation, long-form PDFs, and related-document packing.

**Post-training data.** Four tracks: (1) Agentic Coding — SWE-scaling (GitHub PR→Docker→reward), AppDev (expert meta queries + AaaV), Terminal-Gym (Stack Overflow→terminal tasks); (2) Agentic Cowork — deep search (guide-and-rewrite synthesis with evidence grounding), office tasks (GDPval-anchored hierarchical synthesis), financial analysis (evidence-driven + workbook-walk), slide generation; (3) Reasoning — query-side, response-side, and training-side scaling with structured quality assurance; (4) General conversation/writing + role-play with RLHF.

**RL training.** CISPO (Clipped IS Policy Optimization) with asymmetric upper clipping, reward-to-go with trajectory-level baseline, composite reward = α·process + β·speed + perf. Mixed-domain RL across reasoning/coding/agent/general with staged domain mixing ratio, context length, and difficulty curriculum. SFT uses interleaved thinking traces (reasoning + actions + observations) rather than single contiguous CoT blocks.

## Results & Highlights

**Agentic Coding.** M2.7 achieves 56.2 on SWE-bench Pro, 76.5 on SWE-bench Multilingual, 52.7 on Multi-SWE-bench (best among compared models), 57.0 on Terminal-Bench 2.0, 66.6% medal rate on MLE Bench Lite (tying Gemini 3.1 Pro). NL2Repo jumps 13 points from M2.5 (26.6→39.8).

**AppDev.** VIBE-Pro 55.6 (matching Claude Opus 4.6), HyperTask 67.6 (+8 over M2.5).

**Deep Search.** BrowseComp 77.8, Wide Search 75.2, RISE 64.3 (+14.1 over M2.5 — largest within-series search gain).

**Office & Tools.** GDPval-AA 50.0 (+15 over M2.5), Toolathlon 46.3, MM Claw 62.7, MEWC v2 63.3, Finance Modeling Pro 57.0 (+23.2 over M2.5).

**Reasoning & Knowledge.** AIME 2026 94.2 (vs GPT 5.4's 97.0), GPQA-Diamond 89.8, IFBench 76.0, MMLU-Pro 81.8, HLE 28.0.

**Within-series progression.** All 11 tracked benchmarks improve monotonically M2→M2.5→M2.7, with gains ranging +11 to +33.8 points. Largest gains align with new data pipeline investments (BrowseComp +33.8, MLE Bench Lite +26.6, Toolathlon +27.5).

**Self-evolution case study.** On MLE Bench Lite, M2.7 autonomously executes 24-hour iterative evolution across 22 ML competitions, achieving 9 gold / 5 silver / 1 bronze in the best run with no human-written harness code.

## Authors & Credibility

This is a technical report from **MiniMax**, a major Chinese AI company and previous author of MiniMax-Text-01 and MiniMax-M1. The 180+ person author list spans architecture, data, RL infrastructure, and evaluation teams. The paper cites competing models transparently and reports results under standardized scaffolds (Claude Code for most benchmarks, shared tool environments). The paper is unusually detailed in its engineering descriptions (data pipelines, RL infrastructure, scheduling), suggesting genuine production-system experience rather than academic-only prototyping.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: Moderate.** The MoE architecture with fine-grained experts and sigmoid gating is an incremental but well-motivated evolution of DeepSeek-V3 style designs. The interleaved thinking with state persistence is a clean formalization of an increasingly common practice. The three genuinely novel contributions are: (1) the Forge RL infrastructure design (windowed-FIFO + prefix-tree merging), (2) the AaaV evaluation framework for AppDev, and (3) the self-evolution system. The self-evolution claim is the most interesting but also the hardest to verify from a single case study.

**Rigor: Good with caveats.** The paper provides extensive ablations (MoE design, MTP, full attention vs SWA at both pretrain and SFT levels). Benchmark evaluation is thorough (20+ benchmarks, 4 trials, shared scaffolds). However: some baselines are missing scores (GPT 5.4 missing on 4 benchmarks, Gemini 3.1 Pro missing on 5), some benchmarks are internal (VIBE-Pro, HyperTask, RISE, NL2Repo, MM Claw, MEWC v2, Finance Modeling Pro) making external verification impossible. The MLE Bench Lite self-evolution case study is compelling but limited to one benchmark.

**Reproducibility: Low.** Proprietary model, proprietary infrastructure, massive compute. The engineering designs (Forge architecture, data pipeline stages, reward formulations) are well-documented and could inspire implementations, but full reproduction is infeasible outside a well-funded industrial lab.

**Significance: High for the agentic LLM space.** The paper makes a strong case that agentic capability comes primarily from post-training data quality and RL infrastructure rather than raw parameter count. The 4.3% activation ratio achieving frontier-competitive performance is a meaningful data point for the efficiency frontier. The self-evolution experiment, if it generalizes, represents a paradigm shift in how frontier models are developed.

**Red flags:** (1) The self-evolution section reads more like a demo than a rigorous evaluation — 100-round scaffold optimization is impressive but the "30% gain on in-house evaluations" is unverifiable. (2) M2.7 significantly lags on Terminal-Bench 2.0 (57.0 vs GPT 5.4's 75.1) and HLE (28.0 vs GPT 5.4's 41.6), suggesting the model is weaker on pure execution-heavy and frontier-knowledge tasks. (3) NL2Repo (39.8 vs GPT 5.4's 46.8) and Finance Modeling Pro (57.0 vs GPT 5.4's 75.3) show substantial gaps. (4) The paper uses many internal benchmarks where M2.7's scoring rubrics are opaque. (5) MMLU-Pro (81.8) is notably below all frontier baselines (89-91 range).

## Strengths

- Exceptional efficiency: frontier-competitive performance with only ~10B activated parameters
- Deeply engineered end-to-end system — not just a model but a complete training/deployment stack
- Forge RL system with principled solutions to real engineering problems (windowed-FIFO, prefix-tree merging)
- Comprehensive post-training data pipeline design across diverse domains with verifiable rewards
- Honest reporting of weaknesses (Terminal-Bench, Finance Modeling, HLE gaps are stated plainly)
- Interleaved thinking formalization with clear ablations showing state persistence benefits
- Strong within-series progression demonstrating that data+RL investments translate to steady gains

## Limitations

- Proprietary model with no open-source release plans mentioned
- Significant performance gaps on several benchmarks vs GPT 5.4 (Terminal-Bench 57.0 vs 75.1, Finance Modeling 57.0 vs 75.3, HLE 28.0 vs 41.6)
- Many internal benchmarks (7 of 20+) cannot be externally verified
- MMLU-Pro score (81.8) notably weak relative to all frontier baselines
- Self-evolution claim supported by limited evidence (one benchmark case study, one scaffold optimization anecdote)
- 192K context window with full attention creates significant inference cost despite low activation count
- The agent data pipelines are complex multi-stage systems — failure modes and maintenance burden are not discussed
- No discussion of safety alignment, harmful content generation, or misuse prevention

## Relevance

该论文对研究MoE高效部署、Agent RL训练系统设计和自进化机制高度相关。其Forge系统的工程方案（窗口FIFO调度、前缀树合并）和交织思维的状态持久化设计可直接借鉴。自进化实验虽然初步，但指出了大模型自主优化自身训练管线的重要方向。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the 4.3% activation ratio compare in quality-efficiency Pareto terms to DeepSeek-V3's ~5-7% ratio, and is there a floor below which activation sparsity hurts agentic capability?
- Can the Forge windowed-FIFO and prefix-tree merging designs transfer to open-source RL frameworks (e.g., OpenRLHF, VERL) without the proprietary infrastructure?
- How robust is the self-evolution capability beyond MLE Bench Lite — can M2.7 generalize scaffold optimization to novel domains it hasn't been specifically trained on?
- What is the sensitivity of the interleaved thinking gains to the number of tool-use turns — does the benefit saturate or compound at extreme trajectory lengths (hundreds of turns)?
- How does the Agent-as-a-Verifier framework handle adversarial or edge-case applications where visual aesthetics and functional correctness conflict?
- Does the sigmoid gating with expert bias generalize to different expert counts (e.g., 64 or 512 experts), and what is the interaction with the top-k selection?
