Reasoning Depth and Environment Complexity: A Controlled Study of RLVR Data Allocation across Logical Reasoning Tasks

Zhu, Liu, Cheng, Wang, Aizawa, Kurohashi, Shimodaira Kyoto U / UTokyo / NII / RIKEN 2026-05-26
Joint depth-complexity coverage in RLVR outperforms single-axis recipes, uniform mixing beats staged curricula, and abductive reasoning fails to generalize outside the training distribution.
0.265 vs 0.743
Abductive vs deductive accuracy (GPT-5.4) — all 22 benchmarked LLMs mirror this asymmetry.
Challenges the “easy-to-hard” curriculum intuition for RLVR training — provides actionable data-allocation guidance for reasoning model post-training.
107M-parameter model on a synthetic knowledge-graph domain (object ownership + kinship). Whether findings transfer to natural-language reasoning at billion-parameter scale remains unproven.

Methodology

  • Synthetic KG world: graph G = (P, O, Edyn, Estat, s0) with dynamic events + static kinship.
  • Reasoning depth D ∈ {1…10}; environment complexity T ∈ {1…6} — two orthogonal difficulty axes.
  • 107M Qwen2.5-style decoder-only transformer, 3.14B pre-training tokens on shallow low-complexity region.
  • GRPO post-training: 15 data-distribution recipes, fixed 220K-task budget, outcome-gated reward.
  • Evaluation: 4,800 graphs on a 60-cell (D, T) grid with strict process-verified pass@k.
  • Benchmarked 22 models including GPT-5.4, Kimi-K2.6, GLM-5.1, DeepSeek-R1.

Results & Highlights

  • Joint coverage recipes (Shallow-Mix, Offbase-Mix, Full-Coverage) consistently outrank single-axis recipes.
  • Deductive gains concentrate along depth extension; abductive gains spread across both axes.
  • Abductive reasoning shows negative out-of-recipe ceiling gain, degrading below the pre-trained baseline.
  • Deductive, inductive, and analogy retain 50–70% of in-recipe gains out-of-recipe.
  • Uniform mixing > 3-block easy-to-hard > many-block schedules. Each curriculum transition adds forgetting cost.
  • Analogy shows high single gain but low ceiling gain — RL sharpens a dominant pattern without expanding candidates.
  • Clean experimental design with full control over training distributions — rare in LLM reasoning research.
  • Systematic sweep: 15 recipes × 4 reasoning families × 60-cell difficulty grid.
  • Abductive generalization failure is well-supported by both controlled and frontier-model evidence.
  • Practical takeaway: cover both axes, directly cover abductive targets, default to uniform mixing.
  • Synthetic domain gap: KG world lacks semantic ambiguity, open-world knowledge, and linguistic diversity.
  • Only 107M parameters tested; emergent behaviors at billion-parameter scale may differ.
  • Single training runs — no confidence intervals due to compute constraints.
  • Narrow task families: each reasoning form is one controlled instantiation of a broader category.

Open Questions

  1. Do these findings transfer to natural-language reasoning domains (math, code, science) at billion-parameter scale?
  2. Can targeted abductive training data creation close the deductive–abductive gap in frontier models?
  3. What mechanisms cause abductive reasoning to degrade below the pre-trained baseline — catastrophic forgetting, reward hacking, or something else?
  4. How does optimal allocation change when the post-training budget scales 10–100×?
  5. Would multi-objective reward shaping (explicitly rewarding backward inference steps) improve abductive generalization?
