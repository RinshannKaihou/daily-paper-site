> **一句话总结：** 本文提出了一种基于坐标曲率差异的几何框架，能够在扩散模型生成的图像中精确定位哪些像素区域是因过拟合而记忆了训练样本的，并在 Stable Diffusion 上显著优于之前的注意力定位方法。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes a curvature-difference framework that localizes memorized pixel regions within diffusion model outputs by subtracting an underfitted baseline's curvature, achieving state-of-the-art localization on Stable Diffusion with IoU up to 0.947.

## 核心贡献

- 提出将局部记忆化（local memorization）刻画为**坐标级方差坍缩**（coordinate-wise variance collapse），超越全局维度指标（如 LID 或 sharpness），能够揭示记忆化在图像中的**具体位置**。
- 引入**曲率差分方法**（curvature-difference），通过减去欠拟合基线模型（无条件模型或更少训练的模型版本）的曲率，区分过拟合驱动的记忆化与数据流形本身的内在低方差结构。
- 从 Fisher 信息恒等式推导出**分数差分代理**（score-difference proxy），无需计算 Hessian 即可近似曲率差分，同时为 Wen et al. (2024) 的分数差分检测指标提供了新的几何解释——无条件模型本质上是捕获数据流形内在曲率的欠拟合基线。

## Key Contributions

- Characterizes local memorization as **coordinate-wise variance collapse**, enabling spatial localization beyond global metrics like LID or sharpness.
- Introduces **curvature-difference methods** ($\Delta h^\varnothing$, $\Delta h^{\tilde{\theta}}$) that subtract an underfitted baseline's curvature to isolate overfitting-driven memorization from intrinsic data constraints.
- Derives a **score-difference proxy** ($\Delta s^\varnothing$, $\Delta s^{\tilde{\theta}}$) from Fisher information identities, providing a computationally efficient approximation and a novel geometric interpretation of the widely used Wen et al. (2024) detection metric.
- Achieves state-of-the-art localization performance on Stable Diffusion v1.4 and v2.1 against ground-truth memorization masks.

## Methodology

The method operates at a chosen timestep $t^*$ during the reverse diffusion process:

1. **Coordinate-wise curvature**: Uses $\text{diag}(-H_\theta(x_t, c))$, the diagonal of the Hessian of the log-density, as a measure of per-coordinate variance collapse. Computed efficiently via the Hutchinson estimator with Rademacher random vectors, requiring only Hessian-vector products (automatic differentiation, no full Hessian).

2. **Curvature-difference metrics**: Two baselines are considered:
   - $\Delta h^\varnothing = \text{diag}(-H_\theta(x_t, c)) - \text{diag}(-H_\theta(x_t))$ — unconditional model as baseline.
   - $\Delta h^{\tilde{\theta}} = \text{diag}(-H_\theta(x_t, c)) - \text{diag}(-H_{\tilde{\theta}}(x_t, c))$ — less-trained version of the same model (e.g., SD v1.1 for SD v1.4, SD v2.0 for SD v2.1).

3. **Score-difference surrogates**: From the Fisher information identity, the diagonal of the curvature difference is the expectation of the squared score difference:
   - $\Delta s^\varnothing = (s_\theta(x_t, c) - s_\theta(x_t))^{\odot 2}$
   - $\Delta s^{\tilde{\theta}} = (s_\theta(x_t, c) - s_{\tilde{\theta}}(x_t, c))^{\odot 2}$
   
   These avoid Hessian computation entirely and become faithful proxies at late sampling steps.

4. **Evaluation at the final sampling step**: All metrics are evaluated at the last DDIM step ($t \approx 0$) where diffusion noise is minimal and curvature divergence is most pronounced. Channel aggregation via summation yields a 2D spatial heatmap.

Key theoretical results:
- **Proposition 4.1**: Links conditional covariance $\text{Cov}[x_0 | x_t]$ to the Hessian $\nabla^2_{x_t} \log p(x_t)$, establishing that high curvature corresponds to low variance.
- **Proposition 4.2**: Fisher information identity showing $\text{diag}(-\nabla^2_x \log p(c|x)) = \mathbb{E}[(\nabla_x \log p(c|x))^{\odot 2}]$, justifying score difference as a curvature proxy.

## Results & Highlights

- **Ground-truth mask evaluation** (Table 1): On SD v1.4 "All" setting, $\Delta h^\varnothing$ achieves IoU 0.953 and ACC 0.968 vs. Bright Ending's IoU 0.564 and ACC 0.849. On SD v2.1, $\Delta h^{\tilde{\theta}}$ achieves IoU 0.947 and ACC 0.967.
- **Memorization detection** (Table 2): Aggregated score-difference metrics achieve near-perfect AUC (0.995–0.998) and high TPR@1%FPR (0.950–0.988), consistently outperforming raw curvature and attention-based baselines.
- **Ablations**: Even K=1 Hutchinson sample yields competitive localization; performance improves at later sampling steps; the method generalizes to Realistic Vision v5.1.
- **Score-difference surrogates** are slightly noisier per-coordinate but highly effective after spatial averaging for detection.
- **Computational overhead**: $\Delta h^\varnothing$ adds only ~0.42s (K=16) on top of ~1.08s sampling time on a single L40S GPU.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The coordinate-wise variance collapse perspective is a meaningful and natural extension of the global LID/sharpness frameworks. The curvature-difference idea is elegant. However, the core insight—that subtracting a baseline removes intrinsic data curvature—was implicitly exploited by Wen et al. (2024); this work makes it explicit and spatial.
- **Rigor**: Strong theoretical grounding with formal propositions and proofs. The Fisher information connection to score difference is a genuine contribution that clarifies why existing heuristics work.
- **Reproducibility**: Code is available. All experimental settings (DDIM 50 steps, CFG 7.5, SD v1.4/v2.1) are standard. Ground-truth masks from Webster (2023) are publicly available.
- **Significance**: The spatial localization capability is a clear step forward. However, the method currently only handles verbatim (template) memorization and explicitly does not address concept-level memorization (e.g., celebrity faces, artistic styles).
- **Red flags**: The reliance on a less-trained baseline ($\tilde{\theta}$) requires access to intermediate training checkpoints, which may not always be available. The method evaluates only at the final timestep, and the IoU metric requires threshold sweeping per-metric (not per-sample), which may overestimate practical performance. The SD v2.1 dataset is dominated (~85%) by "Shaw Floors" prompts, raising questions about dataset diversity.

## Strengths

- Principled geometric framework that unifies and explains prior heuristic metrics.
- Strong quantitative results with large margins over the prior attention-based method (BE).
- Both curvature-based and score-based variants provided, offering accuracy-efficiency tradeoffs.
- Honest discussion of limitations and failure modes.
- The synthetic 2D experiment (Appendix E.1) provides clear intuition for curvature dynamics.

## Limitations

- Curvature-based metrics require Hessian-vector products, adding computational overhead (though score-difference surrogates mitigate this).
- Cannot capture concept-level memorization where degrees of freedom are distributed globally across the image.
- Requires access to a less-trained model checkpoint for $\Delta h^{\tilde{\theta}}$ and $\Delta s^{\tilde{\theta}}$, limiting applicability when intermediate checkpoints are unavailable.
- The evaluation dataset for SD v2.1 is heavily skewed toward "Shaw Floors" prompts, limiting generalizability claims.
- Score-difference surrogates exhibit outlier pixel activations that require post-processing (mean filtering) for reliable localization.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the curvature-difference framework be extended to detect concept-level memorization (e.g., celebrity likenesses, artistic styles) rather than just verbatim/template memorization?
- How does the method perform on more recent architectures (DiT-based models like SDXL, Stable Diffusion 3) where the U-Net structure differs significantly?
- Could the curvature-difference signal be used not just for detection/localization but also for targeted mitigation—e.g., selectively perturbing only the memorized spatial regions during generation?
- What is the relationship between the magnitude of the curvature difference and the degree of copyright/privacy violation in partial memorization cases?
- Can the Fisher information interpretation be leveraged to design training-time regularization that prevents overfitting-driven curvature from arising in the first place?
