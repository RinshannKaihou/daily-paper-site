> **一句话总结：** 本文通过大规模对照实验发现，非英语原生推理的劣势远小于先前报告（仅1.9–3.5%），并提出Layer Swap方法——将英语专家模型的中间推理层迁移到各语言原生专家中，无需额外训练即可在保持目标语言思维链的同时基本消除性能差距。

> **TL;DR:** Under matched supervision (~10B tokens SFT on Qwen3-8B), the native reasoning gap shrinks to just 1.9–3.5% across five non-English languages—far smaller than previously reported. A training-free Layer Swap that transfers the English specialist's mid-layers into each native specialist closes 27–100% of the remaining gap while preserving CoT in the target language.

## 核心贡献

- 构建了涵盖六种语言（英/法/德/西/中/斯）的大规模长链式思维推理数据集，每种语言约50万样本、32k上下文
- 在严格控制条件下（相同问答对、匹配的token预算），重新测量了原生推理与英语中介推理的性能差距，发现差距仅为1.9–3.5%，远小于先前报告的~17–19%
- 通过权重空间分析发现中间层存在跨语言共享的推理核心，外层则语言特异性分化
- 提出Layer Swap方法，将英语专家的中间层（L13–L22）迁移到各语言原生专家中，无需训练即可弥合性能差距

## Key Contributions

- A large-scale multilingual long-CoT reasoning corpus across six languages (~500k samples each, up to 32k tokens), publicly released
- Controlled measurement of the native vs. English-pivoted reasoning gap under matched SFT token budgets (~10B tokens per language on Qwen3-8B-Base), revealing a gap of only 1.9–3.5%
- Weight-space analysis showing aligned fine-tuning deltas in middle layers (L13–L22) and language-specific divergence in outer layers, indicating a language-agnostic reasoning core
- Layer Swap: a training-free technique transferring the English specialist's mid-layers into each native specialist, closing 27–100% of the gap while maintaining ~100% target-language CoT fidelity

## Methodology

**Data construction:** Starting from the English DolciThink-SFT-32B corpus (~500k samples covering math, code, science, instruction following), the authors translate into five target languages using Gemma-3-27B-it with a chunked translation pipeline (~2k tokens per chunk) to handle long contexts. Quality filtering removes empty outputs, degenerate samples (zlib ratio anomalies), length outliers, and over-context samples.

**Training:** Full fine-tuning of Qwen3-8B-Base using DeepSpeed ZeRO-3 + Ulysses sequence parallelism + FlashAttention-3. For each non-English language XX, two specialists are trained on the same Q&A pairs: (1) native specialist (CoT in XX) and (2) English-pivoted specialist (CoT in English, Q&A in XX). English specialist trained on original English data.

**Weight-space analysis:** Two metrics measure cross-language alignment of per-layer SFT deltas (Δθ): mean pairwise cosine similarity and top-1 SVD variance share. Both peak at L13–L22 (c̄L ≈ 0.6, sL captures 65–74% variance), revealing a shared reasoning core.

**Layer Swap:** Transfer a contiguous window of the English specialist's transformer layers into each native specialist. Layer-range ablation on French identifies L13–L22 as optimal: earlier windows transfer little reasoning capability; later windows destroy target-language fidelity. For Chinese, a narrower L13–L20 window is used due to an earlier language gate.

**Evaluation:** Five benchmarks—MGSM-Rev2 (math), Global-MMLU-Lite (knowledge), GPQA-Diamond (science), AIME 24/25 (competition math), HumanEvalPlus (code)—in the target language. Temperature 1, top-p 0.95, 10–30 runs per benchmark.

## Results & Highlights

- **Native gap is small:** Native specialists trail English-pivoted counterparts by only 2.7% (FR), 3.1% (DE), 1.9% (ES), 3.1% (ZH), 3.5% (SW) on the five-benchmark average
- **Gap concentrates on hard reasoning:** AIME 24/25 accounts for most of the gap (5–12% per language); HumanEvalPlus is essentially tied
- **Layer Swap closes the gap effectively:**
  - French: 89% gap closed (72.36% → 74.74% vs. pivot 75.04%)
  - German: 83% gap closed (72.59% → 75.15% vs. pivot 75.67%)
  - Spanish: matches English-pivoted ceiling (72.41% → 74.80% vs. pivot 74.32%)
  - Chinese: 27% gap closed (70.80% → 71.62% vs. pivot 73.89%)
  - Swahili: 60% gap closed (66.98% → 69.09% vs. pivot 70.52%)
- **Language fidelity preserved:** ~100% of reasoning traces remain in the target language after Layer Swap
- **Understanding gap ablation:** Every non-English specialist scores higher on English inputs than native inputs (Δ ranges from -1.3% for Spanish to -5.5% for Swahili), confirming input understanding as a bottleneck

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Moderate-High):** The combination of controlled matched-supervision comparison, weight-space analysis, and Layer Swap for long-CoT reasoning is novel. Layer Swap itself is not new (Bandarkar et al., 2024), but its application to long-CoT reasoning models and same-skill-different-language expert pairs is.

**Rigor (High):** The controlled experimental design—same Q&A pairs, matched token budgets, identical hyperparameters—is a significant improvement over prior work that compared native and English-pivoted reasoning under unequal conditions. The layer-range ablation and input-language ablation are well-designed.

**Reproducibility (High):** Public datasets, models, and detailed hyperparameters. Code architecture (TRL, DeepSpeed) is standard.

**Significance (High):** Challenges the prevailing assumption that native reasoning incurs a large accuracy penalty. The finding that the gap is only 1.9–3.5% under fair conditions is consequential for the field.

**Red flags:**
- Single model family (Qwen3-8B) at a single scale—generalization to larger models or other architectures is untested
- Machine-translated training data may underestimate or overestimate the true native gap compared to organically produced native CoT
- The Layer Swap window was tuned via ablation on French and applied to other languages with only Chinese receiving an adjusted window—more per-language tuning might yield different results
- No RL stage; production reasoning models typically undergo multi-stage post-training

## Strengths

- Rigorously controlled experimental setup isolating the effect of CoT language
- Covers typologically diverse languages including low-resource Swahili
- Elegant weight-space analysis providing mechanistic insight into layer specialization
- Layer Swap is simple, training-free, and immediately applicable to any model with an existing English specialist
- Comprehensive evaluation across five diverse benchmarks
- All artifacts publicly released

## Limitations

- Limited to one model family (Qwen3-8B-Base) at 8B scale; findings may not transfer to larger models
- Training data is machine-translated, which may introduce artifacts not present in native reasoning traces
- Tokenization inefficiency means equivalent token budgets correspond to less effective supervision for languages like Swahili
- Only six languages tested; Swahili is the sole low-resource representative
- No exploration of combining Layer Swap with RL or preference tuning
- The 10B-token SFT budget, while substantial for native CoT, is below production-scale post-training

## Relevance

本文对多语言推理研究具有直接参考价值。它揭示了在公平训练条件下原生推理的性能损失远小于预期，并通过Layer Swap提供了一种无需额外训练的实用方法来缩小这一差距。对于关注LLM多语言能力和跨语言迁移的研究非常有价值，特别是中间层语言无关推理核心的发现对理解模型内部机制有重要启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the Layer Swap approach transfer to larger models (70B+) or different architectures (Llama, Mistral)?
- How does Layer Swap interact with RL-based post-training (e.g., GRPO, RLVR)?
- Would organically generated native CoT data (not machine-translated) further reduce the native reasoning gap?
- Can the language-agnostic reasoning core be strengthened during pretraining rather than post-training?
- What is the minimal amount of native SFT needed before Layer Swap becomes effective?
- How does Layer Swap perform on tasks with stronger cultural or sociopragmatic components beyond math/science/code?
