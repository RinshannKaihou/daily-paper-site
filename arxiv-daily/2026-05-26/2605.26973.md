> **一句话总结：** 本文引入"条件 Copula 熵（CCE）"度量网络间表征对齐，并在两层线性网络上给出闭式解：对齐随信噪比（SNR）单调上升、随样本量非单调变化（在插值阈值 α=n/d≈1 处取极小，此时 CCE≈0），且更强的对齐并不一定带来更好的泛化——该规律在 ReLU 网络、CIFAR-10 与 MNIST 上定性成立。

> **TL;DR:** Introduces Conditional Copula Entropy (CCE), an asymmetric Information-Imbalance–derived alignment measure that is analytically tractable. In a two-layer linear teacher-student setup (d=200, k=100, ntest=10⁴), Theorem 1 gives the asymptotic inter-network correlation ρ* = SNR/(SNR+1) when α>1, and shows alignment (CCE) is minimized near the interpolation threshold α=1 where CCE≈0. The same monotonic-in-SNR / non-monotonic-in-sample-size pattern holds for ReLU nets (k=20) and for a 40-feature-map CNN on CIFAR-10 with label noise as inverse-SNR; strikingly a ReLU net can have *worse* generalization error than a linear net yet *higher* CCE (more informative representations), decoupling alignment from generalization.

## 核心贡献

- 提出条件 Copula 熵（CCE）作为表征对齐度量，可由距离秩直方图估计，并在一维高斯投影情形下有闭式表达（Eq. 5），相比 CKA/SVCCA 更适用于高度弯曲的非线性流形。
- 在两层线性 teacher-student 网络上推导出对齐的解析解（Theorem 1 与 Eq. 12–15），给出 ρ* 在欠参（α>1）与过参（α<1）区间的不同表达式，并通过 Marchenko–Pastur 谱取平均。
- 系统证明"对齐随 SNR 单调上升、随样本量非单调（插值阈值处取极小）"这一规律在线性网络、ReLU 网络、CIFAR-10 CNN、MNIST 全连接网络上定性一致。
- 证明对齐与泛化解耦：相同泛化误差下不同 SNR 网络对齐不同；ReLU 网络可比线性网络泛化更差却学得信息量更大的表征，说明仅用任务性能衡量表征质量是不充分的。

## Key Contributions

- **CCE measure (Sec 2):** Defines CCE(A→B) = −lim H(c_B | c_A=η) from the Information Imbalance literature [22,19]; estimated empirically by partitioning conditional ranks into M≈√N bins (Eq. 4). Asymmetric, captures nonlinear-neighborhood predictability that CKA/SVCCA miss on curved manifolds.
- **Closed-form alignment in linear nets (Theorem 1, Sec 4.1):** For two independently trained two-layer linear students under a noisy linear teacher y=w*·x+ε (SNR=σ_w²/σ_ε²), the asymptotic correlation is ρ* = SNR/(SNR+1) for α>1 and ρ* = (α−1)·SNR / (α·(SNR + (1−α)/2)) for α<1, derived via Marchenko–Pastur averaging of input-covariance eigenvalues.
- **CCE closed form (Eq. 15):** CCE(A→B) = −½log(1−ρ*²) − ½ρ*², enabling analytic alignment prediction in the linear reference system.
- **Universality across settings (Sec 4.2–4.3):** The monotonic-in-SNR, non-monotonic-in-sample-size behavior, with a minimum near the interpolation threshold, replicates in (i) ReLU two-layer nets (k=20), (ii) a 40-feature-map CNN on CIFAR-10 with label-noise as inverse-SNR, and (iii) an MLP on MNIST.
- **Alignment–generalization decoupling (Fig 3):** A ReLU net can have higher generalization error yet higher CCE (more informative hidden representation) than the linear net, proving task performance is an insufficient proxy for representation quality.

## Methodology

**Alignment measure — CCE.** Built on Information Imbalance ∆(A→B) (Eq. 1, from Ref [22]); CCE = −lim_{η→0} H(c_B | c_A=η) where c_A, c_B are copula variables of pairwise distances (Eq. 3). Estimated by histogram of conditional ranks R_{B|A} over M≈√N bins (Eq. 4). For 1-D Gaussian projections A=u^T x, B=v^T x, closed form CCE = −½log(1−ρ²) − ½ρ² with ρ the correlation (Eq. 5, from Ref [57]).

**Linear teacher-student (Sec 3).** Data: y_i = w*·x_i + ε_i, x_i ~ N(0, I_d/d), w*~N(0,σ_w²), ε_i~N(0,σ_ε²), SNR=σ_w²/σ_ε². Student: two-layer linear ŷ = W2 W1 x (W1∈R^{k×d}, W2∈R^{1×k}), trained full-batch GD on MSE (Eq. 8). Analyzed at the global minimum in the small-init "rich" regime using the Advani et al. [2] low-rank parameterization W1 = r2 z V^T, W2 = u r2^T (V = right singular vectors of X). At the minimum v* = u*z = w*V + ε̃ Λ^{−1/2} (Eq. 11). Two independent students a, b share the eigenbasis V assumption; their correlation ρ is averaged over Marchenko–Pastur eigenvalues (Eq. 14, λ±=(1±√α)²), yielding Theorem 1's closed-form ρ* (Eq. 12).

**Nonlinear extension (Sec 4.2).** Same data generator; insert ReLU. No analytic CCE — measured numerically. d=200, k=20 hidden units, γ = n/p = n/(d·k) replaces α. Averaged over 50 independent runs.

**Real-world classification (Sec 4.3).** CIFAR-10 with a standard CNN (40 feature maps in last conv layer); two identical nets trained independently on disjoint subsets of size n (10² to half the training set); label noise introduced by flipping each label to random with probability p (0 to 0.5); noise fixed once, test labels clean. Here label noise = inverse SNR. Also replicated on MNIST with an MLP (Appendix F.2). All simulations on NVIDIA RTX 6000 Ada GPUs.

## Results & Highlights

- **Theorem 1 (closed-form ρ*, linear net):** ρ* = **SNR/(SNR+1)** in the overparameterized regime α>1; in the underparameterized regime α<1, ρ* = **(α−1)·SNR / (α·(SNR + (1−α)/2))**. (Eq. 12)
- **CCE at the interpolation threshold (Fig 1b, linear, d=200, k=100):** for any finite SNR>0, **CCE(A→B) ≈ 0 at α=1** — essentially zero alignment between independently trained networks — matching the divergence of generalization error at the same point (Fig 1a).
- **SNR governs alignment monotonically (Figs 1b, 2b, 4b):** across linear, ReLU (k=20), and CIFAR-10 CNN, higher SNR (or lower label noise) → higher CCE → stronger alignment, in both α<1 and α>1 regimes.
- **Alignment–generalization decoupling (Fig 3, SNR=5, d=200, k=20):** the linear network achieves *lower* generalization error than the ReLU network (Bayes-optimal student is linear here [13]), yet the ReLU network has *higher* CCE — i.e., more informative internal representations despite worse task performance. This cannot be detected by symmetric measures like CKA.
- **CIFAR-10 double descent + alignment minimum (Fig 4):** generalization error shows the classical double-descent peak (more pronounced at higher label noise), and CCE between penultimate representations shows a non-monotonic curve with a minimum near the interpolation threshold γ=n/p≈1, qualitatively matching the synthetic results; the same pattern holds on MNIST (Appendix F.2).

## Critical Assessment

- **Closed-form theory only for the linear reference system:** Theorem 1 and Eq. 12–15 are derived exclusively for two-layer *linear* networks under two strong assumptions — (i) the two students' input covariance matrices share the same eigenbasis V, and (ii) small-init low-rank first-layer weights. All ReLU/CIFAR/MNIST results are numerical, so the headline "universality" claim rests on qualitative curve-shape agreement rather than a nonlinear theorem.
- **No comparison to established alignment measures at matched settings:** CKA [33] and SVCCA [52] are criticized as ill-suited for curved manifolds but never benchmarked head-to-head against CCE on the same networks, so the practical advantage of CCE over CKA is asserted, not demonstrated quantitatively.
- **Restricted architecture scope:** only two-layer nets and a single small CNN (40 feature maps) are studied; no modern deep nets (ResNets, transformers), so the SNR/sample-size laws may not transfer to realistic scales.
- **Interpolation-threshold minimum is described qualitatively** — the exact γ at which CCE is minimized is not reported as a number, and the "CCE≈0 at α=1" claim is specific to the linear case (ReLU/CIFAR CCE does *not* go to zero, only dips).

## Strengths

- Provides one of the few *analytic* predictions of representational alignment (closed-form ρ* and CCE) in any reference system, via a clean Information-Imbalance / copula-entropy formulation.
- The decoupling result (Fig 3) is a genuinely important cautionary tale: task accuracy is not a reliable proxy for representation quality.
- Cross-setting consistency (linear → ReLU → CIFAR-10 → MNIST) makes the SNR/sample-size laws plausible as a general phenomenon.
- The CCE measure is asymmetric and captures nonlinear neighborhood structure, addressing known blind spots of CKA/SVCCA.

## Limitations

- Theory is linear-only; nonlinear extension is empirical.
- Shared-eigenbasis assumption (V^a = V^b) is untestable in real data and strong.
- Only shallow architectures and one small CNN evaluated.
- Single-modality; multimodal alignment (vision+language) explicitly left to future work.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The closed-form ρ* (Theorem 1) requires shared eigenbasis V for both students — how sensitive is the prediction when the two training sets have different covariance eigenbases (the realistic case)?
- The paper dismisses CKA/SVCCA as ill-suited but never reports their values on the same networks — would CKA show the same SNR-monotonic / sample-size-non-monotonic pattern, or genuinely fail?
- Fig 3 shows a ReLU net with worse generalization but higher CCE — does this imply that *aligning* a student to a high-CCE teacher would hurt its task performance, and could this be tested directly?
- The analysis is restricted to the global minimum; the authors explicitly leave training *dynamics* to future work — does the alignment-minimum-at-interpolation-threshold pattern appear mid-training, or only at convergence?
- All experiments are single-modality and shallow; would the SNR/sample-size laws survive in a deep transformer or a multimodal vision-language model, where the representation manifold is far more curved?
