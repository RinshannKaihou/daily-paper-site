Open-Weight LLM Fine-Tuning Defenses are Susceptible to Simple Attacks

Kevin Kuo, Chhavi Yadav, Virginia Smith — Carnegie Mellon University
llm-safety adversarial-robustness open-weight-models jailbreaking fine-tuning-defenses
Fine-tuning defenses are trivially bypassed by gradient-free attacks, raising ASR from <10% to 16–96%.
16–96%
Attack success rate across benchmarks (from <10% baseline)
Open-weight LLM safety safeguards are fundamentally fragile—simple attacks defeat defenses designed to resist adversarial fine-tuning.
ART defense only evaluated on Gemma3-1B-IT; scalability to larger models remains entirely untested.
Methodology
  1. Abliteration — Identifies the refusal direction in the residual stream by computing the mean difference between hidden states of harmful vs. benign prompts, then projects it out of weight matrices via W′ = W(I − α r rT), sweeping α over {1.0, 1.5, 2.0} per layer.
  2. Prefilling — Injects a static compliant prefix ("Sure, here are some ideas. First, …") into the assistant turn, exploiting that safety alignment concentrates effect on the first few output tokens.
  3. Compositional attack — Abliteration followed by prefilling.

Safeguards tested: TAR (meta-learning + DPO tamper-resistance loss) and SEAM (coupled harmful/benign optimization).  Models: Llama-3.1/3.2 (1B, 3B, 8B), Qwen2.5 (1-2B), Gemma3 (1B).  Benchmarks: BeaverTails, AdvBench, HarmBench (Llama-2-chat 13B judge).

ART defense: At each training step, simulates abliteration to find the most attackable layer, computes CE loss on harmful sequences, applies gradient ascent on original parameters while retaining benign performance via a CE retain loss. Only 200 additional training steps, no extra data.

Results & Highlights
Finding Detail
Abliteration strength 62–97% ASR on AdvBench/HarmBench for Base & SEAM models (Llama-3.1-8B)
TAR vs. SEAM TAR substantially more resistant: 9–62% vs. SEAM 6–96% across settings
Prefilling scale effect More effective at 1B (Base ASR 15%→30%) than 8B (13%→20%)
Gemma vulnerability 93% ASR for Base, 80% for SEAM under abliteration
ART improvement Base 93%→49%, SEAM 80%→64%, TAR 76%→55% on Gemma3-1B-IT (BeaverTails)
ART side benefit Reduces baseline and prefill-only ASRs to near zero
Utility cost of TAR ROUGE-L drops to 22.9 at 1B vs. 29.0 for Base
Strengths & Limitations
Strengths
  • Clear, focused research question with practical safety implications
  • Comprehensive evaluation: 3 model families, 3 sizes, 3 benchmarks
  • Honest reporting—no defense fully closes the gap
  • PCA visualization provides mechanistic intuition
  • ART is lightweight and composable (200 extra steps, no extra data)
Limitations
  • ART evaluated only on Gemma3-1B-IT; scalability unknown
  • Single static prefill string—optimized prefills likely stronger
  • No adaptive attacker combining abliteration + fine-tuning
  • No theoretical analysis of why TAR > SEAM or why ART helps
  • Benign refusal rate uses keyword matching, not a learned classifier
Questions for Further Exploration
  1. How does ART scale to larger models (8B, 70B)? Does per-step abliteration simulation become prohibitive?
  2. Can adaptive attackers defeat ART via multi-layer simultaneous abliteration or iterative weight-space optimization?
  3. Does the fundamental limitation of refusal-based defenses suggest machine unlearning as the primary safety strategy?
  4. How do gradient-free attacks interact with other jailbreaking techniques (GCG, PAIR) compositionally?
  5. Could PCA-based representation analysis become a diagnostic tool for predicting safeguard robustness before deployment?
