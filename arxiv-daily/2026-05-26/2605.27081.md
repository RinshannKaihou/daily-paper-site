> **一句话总结：** ReMoE 通过仅微调 MoE 模型的路由器（router），引导相邻 token 复用已缓存的专家，在内存受限的边缘设备上将解码延迟降低 43.6–49.8%，同时几乎不损失模型能力。

> **TL;DR:** ReMoE fine-tunes only the MoE router (gate) to increase temporal expert reuse across adjacent decoding steps, reducing expert cache misses and I/O overhead under memory-constrained inference. It achieves 1.77–1.99× decode speedup on Jetson Orin NX with negligible quality loss.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了 **ReMoE**，一个仅需微调路由器门控参数的框架，不需要修改模型架构、专家权重或推理引擎。
- 设计了 **时序局部性正则化**（reuse loss、smoothness loss、lagged inertia loss、working-set compression loss），鼓励相邻 token 复用相同专家，减少缓存未命中。
- 引入 **Trust-KL loss** 作为语义锚点，将微调后的路由分布约束在预训练路由附近，防止路由语义漂移导致模型质量下降。
- 在 DeepSeek-V2-Lite 和 Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B 上验证了方法的通用性，并在 vLLM GPU-CPU 卸载、Jetson Orin NX 边缘推理、Iluvatar GPU、Kunpeng-Ascend NPU 等多平台上验证了实际部署效果。

## Key Contributions

- **Router-only fine-tuning framework** that reshapes MoE routing traces to be cache-friendly, without touching expert weights or the inference runtime.
- **Temporal locality regularization** combining four complementary losses (reuse, smoothness, lagged inertia, working-set compression) to increase step-to-step expert overlap.
- **Trust-KL semantic anchor** that softly constrains the updated router to stay close to the pretrained routing distribution, preventing quality degradation.
- **Comprehensive real-system evaluation** across vLLM GPU–CPU offloading, Jetson Orin NX (llama.cpp), Iluvatar GPU (expert-kit), and Kunpeng–Ascend NPU platforms, demonstrating consistent latency reductions.

## Methodology

ReMoE operates on fine-grained MoE models (e.g., DeepSeek-V2-Lite with 64 routed experts per layer, Top-6 routing) in a memory-constrained, single-request (B=1) decoding regime. Only the gate parameters $\theta_{\text{gate}}$ are trained; all other weights are frozen.

**Key metric:** Expert Overlap Ratio (EOR) measures the average fraction of Top-K experts shared between adjacent decoding steps. Under recency-based caching (LRU), average expert fetches are bounded by $K(1 - \text{EOR})$.

**Training objective** combines:
1. Standard cross-entropy loss $L_{\text{CE}}$ for language modeling.
2. **Trust-KL loss**: KL divergence between the trainable router's distribution $P_t$ and a frozen snapshot router's distribution $P_t^{\text{ref}}$, acting as a soft trust region.
3. **Locality regularization** $L_{\text{Loc}}$ with four components:
   - *Reuse loss*: encourages $P_t$ to place mass on experts selected at step $t{-}1$.
   - *Smoothness loss*: symmetric KL between adjacent-step distributions to suppress jitter.
   - *Lagged inertia loss*: aligns $P_t$ with distributions from multiple earlier steps to curb slow drift.
   - *Working-set compression loss*: minimizes entropy of window-averaged distributions to concentrate routing within local spans.

Fine-tuning uses teacher forcing on OpenHermes-2.5 (100K samples), 2000 steps, AdamW with linear warmup. The auxiliary MoE load-balancing loss is disabled during fine-tuning.

## Results & Highlights

| Setting | Metric | Baseline | ReMoE | Change |
|---------|--------|----------|-------|--------|
| DeepSeek-V2-Lite routing | EOR | 27.3% | 34.5% | +26.4% |
| Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B routing | EOR | 17.0% | 21.6% | +27.2% |
| vLLM GPU–CPU offloading | Output throughput | 3.58 tok/s | 3.88 tok/s | +8.4% |
| Jetson Orin NX (ShareGPT) | TPOT | 554.7 ms | 306.3 ms | −44.8% |
| Jetson Orin NX (GSM8K) | TPOT | 613.7 ms | 346.0 ms | −43.6% |
| Jetson Orin NX (HumanEval) | TPOT | 672.7 ms | 337.6 ms | −49.8% |
| Iluvatar BI-V150 | End-to-end speedup | — | — | 1.67× |
| Kunpeng–Ascend 910B3 | Throughput | 4.3 tok/s | 4.8 tok/s | +11.6% |

**Capability preservation** (DeepSeek-V2-Lite, lm-eval): MMLU 57.72→57.81 (+0.09 pp), HumanEval 26.83→29.27 (+2.44 pp), GSM8K strict 38.89→38.13 (−0.76 pp). Overall, no significant quality degradation.

**Ablation key findings**: Reuse loss is the primary driver of locality gains (removing it drops EOR from 0.345 to 0.283). Trust-KL prevents over-concentration; removing it increases EOR further but worsens PPL from 3.228 to 3.263. CE-only fine-tuning (without locality objectives) actually *degrades* EOR below the pretrained baseline, confirming gains are from the explicit locality objective, not generic adaptation.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The idea of fine-tuning only the router to improve cache locality is elegant and practically motivated. The four-component locality regularization is well-designed, and the Trust-KL anchor is a principled way to balance locality vs. quality. The connection between EOR and cache fetch bounds (Proposition 3.1) provides clean theoretical motivation.
- **Rigor (High):** Ablation study is thorough, isolating each loss component and comparing against a CE-only baseline to rule out generic adaptation effects. Evaluation spans two model families, four hardware platforms, and multiple workloads. Trace-driven cache simulation supplements real-system experiments.
- **Reproducibility (High):** Code, checkpoints, and detailed hyperparameters (Appendix E) are provided. Uses only standard fine-tuning on a publicly available dataset (OpenHermes-2.5).
- **Significance (High):** Addresses a growing practical problem — deploying MoE LLMs on memory-constrained edge devices. The 1.77–1.99× decode speedup on Jetson Orin NX is substantial and directly relevant to real-world deployment.
- **Red flags:** The single-request (B=1) setting is a narrow use case. Under batched serving, the trade-off between locality and load balance may differ significantly. The method increases routing imbalance (CV +293%), which could be problematic in multi-request or expert-parallel settings. Quality results are limited to a few standard benchmarks; broader capability evaluation would strengthen the paper.

## Strengths

- Zero inference-time overhead — the fine-tuned router drops in as a direct replacement.
- Architecture-agnostic: works with any MoE model that exposes Top-K routing.
- Complementary to existing caching, prefetching, and quantization techniques.
- Strong empirical results across diverse hardware platforms and model families.
- Clean formulation with principled theoretical motivation (EOR–fetch bound).

## Limitations

- Evaluated only under B=1 (single-request) decoding; effectiveness under batched serving is unclear.
- Increased routing imbalance (CV +293%) may harm expert-parallel training or multi-request serving throughput.
- GSM8K shows a small but consistent decline (−0.76 pp strict), suggesting the locality–quality trade-off is not always neutral.
- Fine-tuning requires a separate dataset and training phase; the optimal number of steps and data mixture are not fully explored.
- Only evaluated on two MoE model families (DeepSeek, Qwen); transfer to coarser-grained MoE (e.g., Switch Transformers) is untested.

## 相关性

ReMoE 直接解决了 MoE 大模型在内存受限设备上部署时的专家缓存瓶颈问题。对于关注大模型边缘部署、推理优化和 MoE 系统效率的研究者来说，这项工作提供了一个简单且有效的后训练适配方案，与量化、缓存预取等技术正交互补。路由器微调的思路也可扩展到其他需要稀疏激活的架构。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does ReMoE interact with batched serving (B>1)? Does the locality benefit persist, or does increased routing imbalance hurt throughput under expert parallelism?
2. Can the locality regularization be incorporated directly into pretraining or continued pretraining, rather than applied as a post-hoc fine-tuning step?
3. What is the sensitivity of ReMoE to the fine-tuning dataset distribution? Could domain-specific data further improve locality for targeted deployment scenarios?
4. How does ReMoE interact with dynamic caching policies (e.g., Belady's optimal) that already consider future access patterns?
5. Is there a theoretical bound on how much locality can be improved before downstream quality inevitably degrades?
