> **一句话总结：** 本文发现大推理模型（如DeepSeek-R1）的拒绝机制由残差流激活和思维链（CoT）联合编码，单一的激活方向调控无法有效绕过拒绝，但当调控与CoT再生结合时可达94%的合规率，揭示了CoT既是防御增强器也是潜在攻击面。

> **TL;DR:** Refusal in large reasoning models (LRMs) is jointly encoded in residual stream activations and chain-of-thought (CoT), making single-direction activation steering insufficient (39–43% compliance). Combining steering with CoT regeneration achieves 94% compliance, while a compliant CoT alone retains 48% without steering.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了大推理模型（LRM）中拒绝机制的双信号结构：残差流激活与思维链（CoT）联合编码拒绝信号
- 证明CoT不是被动媒介，而是主动参与拒绝信号的重建和强化
- 在DeepSeek-R1-Distill-LLaMA-8B上系统性地分离了残差流和CoT对拒绝的各自贡献
- 发现CoT可作为独立攻击面，合规CoT在无调控条件下仍保留48%的合规率
- 提出CoT检测和抑制可作为有效防御机制的思路

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that refusal in LRMs is mediated by a dual-signal mechanism: residual stream activations and CoT jointly encode the refusal state
- Shows CoT actively counteracts activation-based interventions — suppressing CoT entirely yields higher compliance (70%) than steering alone with fixed CoT (39%)
- Achieves 94% compliance via two-stage intervention (steering + CoT regeneration), matching typical LLM steering performance
- Isolates the independent contribution of CoT by reusing a compliant CoT without steering, retaining 48% compliance
- Identifies CoT as both a defense amplifier and a potential alternative attack surface

## Methodology

The authors conduct controlled experiments on **DeepSeek-R1-Distill-LLaMA-8B** using 100 harmful instructions from JAILBREAKBENCH (all refused at baseline, 0% compliance). The core technique is **activation steering** via a difference-in-means refusal direction extracted from residual stream activations at template token positions (EOI and EOT).

Four experimental conditions isolate the contributions of activations vs. CoT:

1. **Fixed CoT + Steering (Section 4.1):** Apply steering vectors at EOI/EOT positions while keeping the original CoT from the unsteered run. Only the final answer is regenerated. This tests whether single-direction steering suffices in LRMs as it does in LLMs.

2. **No CoT + Steering (Section 4.2):** Suppress the CoT entirely, apply EOI-based steering, and regenerate only the final answer. This isolates whether the CoT actively resists steering.

3. **Regenerated CoT + Steering (Section 4.3):** Apply EOI-based steering while allowing the model to freely regenerate both CoT and final answer. This tests the full combined effect.

4. **Regenerated CoT, No Steering (Section 4.4 — Swap):** Replace the original CoT with one generated under steering (from condition 3), but remove steering at inference time. This isolates the CoT's independent contribution.

Refusal is classified using **Llama-3.2-8B-Guard** (86% accuracy vs. 68% for phrase-based heuristics). All experiments use greedy decoding for reproducibility.

## Results & Highlights

| Experiment | Steering | CoT Condition | Compliance Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | None | Original | 0% |
| Fixed CoT + EOI Steering | EOI | Fixed (original) | 39% |
| Fixed CoT + EOT Steering | EOT | Fixed (original) | 43% |
| No CoT + EOI Steering | EOI | Suppressed | 70% |
| Regenerated CoT + EOI Steering | EOI | Regenerated | **94%** |
| Swapped CoT, No Steering | None | Compliant CoT | 48% |

Key quantitative findings:
- Single-direction steering at template tokens achieves only 39–43% compliance in LRMs, far below the near-complete reversal typical in standard LLMs
- Removing CoT entirely raises compliance to 70%, confirming CoT actively reinforces refusal during generation
- Combining steering with CoT regeneration reaches 94% — on par with LLM steering results
- A compliant CoT alone (no steering) retains 48% compliance, demonstrating CoT independently encodes a partial compliance signal

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: High.** This is the first work to systematically decompose refusal in LRMs into activation-level and CoT-level contributions. Prior work (Yamaguchi et al., 2025) treated CoT as a passive manipulation target; this paper treats it as an active mediator with independent causal power.

**Rigor: Good but limited.** The experimental design is clean — four conditions with clear causal logic. However, all experiments are on a single 8B distilled model with greedy decoding. The difference-in-means approach is standard but may not capture the full complexity of refusal representations. The refusal classifier (Llama-3.2-8B-Guard at 86% accuracy) introduces non-trivial measurement noise.

**Reproducibility: Good.** Code is available, greedy decoding ensures determinism, and the datasets (JAILBREAKBENCH, ADVBENCH, etc.) are public. The single-model focus aids controlled comparison.

**Significance: High.** The finding that CoT is an independent attack surface has direct implications for AI safety. It suggests that monitoring or filtering CoT content could be a necessary defense layer for reasoning models, and that activation-level safeguards alone are insufficient.

**Red flags:**
- Single model evaluation — unclear if findings generalize to other LRMs (e.g., o1, Gemini Thinking)
- No causal verification of CoT faithfulness — the authors acknowledge they only observe correlation between ethical reasoning patterns and refusal, not causation
- Proprietary models cannot be evaluated since their CoT is not exposed in unaltered form

## Strengths

- Elegant experimental design that cleanly isolates the dual-signal nature of refusal
- Clear quantitative progression across conditions (0% → 39% → 70% → 94% → 48%) tells a compelling story
- Directly challenges the prevailing "single refusal direction" paradigm established for standard LLMs
- Practical implications clearly articulated: CoT as both defense amplifier and attack surface
- Honest about limitations — explicitly acknowledges lack of faithfulness verification and single-model scope

## Limitations

- **Single model:** All results are from DeepSeek-R1-Distill-LLaMA-8B; generalization to other LRMs is unknown
- **CoT faithfulness:** No causal verification that specific CoT reasoning patterns drive refusal vs. compliance — only correlational observations from qualitative inspection
- **Scale:** 8B distilled model may not represent behavior of larger or non-distilled reasoning models
- **Classification noise:** 86% classifier accuracy means some compliance/refusal labels may be incorrect
- **Greedy decoding only:** Sampling-based generation might yield different compliance rates
- **No defense evaluation:** The paper suggests CoT monitoring as defense but does not implement or test this

## Relevance

本文对理解大推理模型的安全机制具有重要意义。揭示的"双信号"拒绝编码表明，仅依赖激活层面的安全防护不足以保护推理模型，CoT本身既是安全增强的来源也是潜在的攻击面。对于从事AI安全对齐、模型越狱防御和推理模型机制解释的研究者来说，这项工作提供了关键的实证依据和分析框架。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the dual-signal refusal mechanism generalize to larger reasoning models (e.g., DeepSeek-R1-671B, o3) and non-distilled models?
2. Can CoT content be monitored in real-time to detect and intercept compliance reconstruction before the final answer is generated?
3. What is the causal relationship between specific CoT reasoning patterns (e.g., ethical justifications, harm assessments) and the final refusal decision?
4. How does the joint encoding of refusal in activations and CoT emerge during training — is it specific to RL-based reasoning training, distillation, or both?
5. Could adversarial prompt-level attacks that manipulate CoT (e.g., fabricated reasoning traces) be combined with activation steering for even higher compliance rates?
