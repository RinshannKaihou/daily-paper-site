The Stability of Singular Distribution: A Spectral Perspective on the Two-Phase Dynamics of Language Model Pre-training

Hongtao Zhang, Wenjie Zhou, Chenxi Jia, Wei Chen, Xueqi Cheng ICT, Chinese Academy of Sciences 2026-05-26

The normalized singular value spectrum of Transformer weights stabilizes early, predicting the fast-to-slow loss transition.

Key Metric
~10× lower SoSD
Muon achieves SoSD of 10⁻⁴ vs AdamW's 10⁻³, with consistently lower loss across all checkpoints.
Why This Matters
SoSD and its stability bound ε ∝ η/||W|| unify the spectral understanding of LR schedules, weight decay, and optimizer choice in pre-training.
Critical Edge
Theory is restricted to 1-layer 1-head with full-batch GD — far from practical multi-layer LLMs with Adam and mini-batches.

Methodology

Empirical Setup

  • GPT-2 Small (124M) and Medium (355M) on FineWeb 10B tokens
  • LLaMA 0.5B and 2B on C4
  • Optimizers: AdamW and Muon; LR schedules: Step Decay, WSD, Cosine
  • Weight decay ablation on LLaMA 0.5B (0 vs 0.125)

Core Metric

Singular Distribution Variation:

ΔΣ̂ = ||Σ̂ₜ₊₁ − Σ̂ₜ||_F   where   Σ̂ = Σ/tr(Σ)

Theoretical analysis on 1-layer 1-head Transformer with cross-entropy loss, full-batch GD, using Mirsky's inequality and Lipschitz continuity of the trace-normalization mapping.

Results

  • Spectral Decoupling: Singular distribution stabilizes 5–10× earlier than parameters themselves across all model sizes.
  • Phase Sync: SD Variation drops from ~10⁻² to ~10⁻⁴ exactly when loss enters slow-descent plateau.
  • LR Decay: 10× step drops cause immediate SD Variation decline; WSD produces monotonic SoSD reduction.
  • Weight Decay: Higher SoSD coexists with lower loss — WD suppresses ||W|| growth, relaxing the stability bound.
  • Moon vs AdamW: Muon achieves ~10× lower SoSD (10⁻⁴ vs 10⁻³), confirming SoSD onset is optimizer-invariant but scale varies.
  • Theoretical bounds: Phase I loss decrease ≥ O(1)/step; Phase II bounded by O(εᵖ), p > 2.
Stability bound:   ε ∝ η / ||W||

LR scheduling tightens ε by reducing η. Weight decay relaxes ε by suppressing ||W|| growth. Muon achieves lower ε than AdamW.

Strengths

  • Clean, compelling empirical observation immediately verifiable via SVD
  • Elegant unified bound ε ∝ η/||W|| explaining multiple training strategies
  • Rigorous proofs with clearly stated assumptions
  • Comprehensive sweep: 4 model sizes, 3 LR schedules, 2 optimizers, WD ablation
  • Honest about SoSD not being a direct performance metric

Limitations

  • Theory covers only 1-layer 1-head with full-batch GD — large gap to practical LLMs
  • No new training method proposed; contribution is purely explanatory
  • Relationship to edge-of-stability, grokking, slingshot, progressive sharpening is unexplored
  • No discussion of fine-tuning vs. pre-training or non-Transformer architectures (MoE, SSMs)

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can SoSD onset time serve as an adaptive signal for triggering LR decay?
  2. Does the ε ∝ η/||W|| bound hold quantitatively in deep multi-layer models?
  3. How does SoSD interact with Adam's second-moment normalization?
  4. Is there a connection between SoSD and the edge-of-stability phenomenon (sharpness ~ 2/η)?
  5. Could SoSD guide decisions on when to stop pre-training or switch data distributions?
  6. Does SoSD manifest differently in encoder-decoder, MoE, or non-Transformer architectures?
