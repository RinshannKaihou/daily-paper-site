ReMoE: Boosting Expert Reuse through Router Fine-Tuning in Memory-Constrained MoE LLM Inference

Zhu, Liao, Jiang, Zhang, Wang & Xiao ICML 2026 Beihang Univ. · Huawei MoE edge-inference router-optimization
Key Finding
Fine-tuning only the MoE router cuts decode latency up to 49.8% on edge devices.
Key Metric
1.99×
decode speedup, Jetson Orin NX
Why This Matters
Makes MoE LLMs deployable on memory-constrained edge hardware without changing model architecture.
Critical Edge
Only validated under single-request (B=1) decoding; batched serving effects unknown.

Methodology

Step 1Freeze all weights
Step 2Train gate θ only
Step 34 locality losses
Step 4Trust-KL anchor
Step 5Drop-in replacement

ReMoE targets fine-grained MoE models (e.g. DeepSeek-V2-Lite: 64 routed experts, Top-6) in memory-constrained single-request decoding. Only gate parameters are trained; all expert weights stay frozen. The core metric is Expert Overlap Ratio (EOR) — the average fraction of Top-K experts shared between adjacent steps. Under LRU caching, average expert fetches ≤ K(1 − EOR).

Locality regularization components
  1. Reuse loss — encourages Pt to place mass on experts selected at step t−1 (primary driver).
  2. Smoothness loss — symmetric KL between adjacent-step distributions to suppress jitter.
  3. Lagged inertia loss — aligns Pt with distributions from multiple earlier steps.
  4. Working-set compression — minimizes entropy of window-averaged distributions.

Training: teacher forcing on OpenHermes-2.5 (100K samples), 2000 steps, AdamW. MoE load-balancing loss disabled during fine-tuning.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Zero inference-time overhead — fine-tuned router drops in as a direct replacement.
  • Architecture-agnostic: works with any Top-K routed MoE model.
  • Complementary to caching, prefetching, and quantization.
  • Evaluated across 4 hardware platforms and 2 model families.
  • Clean EOR–fetch bound formulation (Proposition 3.1).

Limitations

  • B=1 only; batched serving effectiveness untested.
  • Routing imbalance CV +293% — may harm expert-parallel throughput.
  • GSM8K shows small but consistent decline (−0.76 pp strict).
  • Fine-tuning requires a separate training phase; optimal steps/data not fully explored.
  • Only two MoE families tested; coarser-grained MoE unvalidated.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does ReMoE interact with batched serving (B>1)? Does locality persist or does routing imbalance hurt throughput?
  2. Can locality regularization be incorporated into pretraining rather than applied as post-hoc fine-tuning?
  3. What is the sensitivity to the fine-tuning dataset distribution — could domain-specific data improve locality further?
  4. How does ReMoE interact with dynamic caching policies (e.g. Belady's optimal)?
  5. Is there a theoretical bound on locality improvement before downstream quality inevitably degrades?
