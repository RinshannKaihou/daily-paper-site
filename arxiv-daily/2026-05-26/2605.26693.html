arXiv 2605.26693

Model Merging on Loss Landscape: A Geometry Perspective

Juanwu Lu, Anand Bhaskar, Brian Axelrod, Ekaterina Tolstaya, Tristan Emrich Purdue / Waymo 2026-05-26
Key Finding
EpiMer reframes model merging as a Riemannian Fréchet mean, strictly outperforming all baselines on CLIP-ViT across eight tasks.
Critical Edge
Gains shrink to +0.06&perm;; on ViT-L/14; untested on LLMs or NLP tasks.
Key Metric — avg acc. over TSV-M
+1.10pp (ViT-B/32)
Why This Matters
Unifies spectral and curvature-aware merging under one geometric framework, making multi-task combination principled and practical.

Methodology

01
Model the parameter space as a Riemannian manifold &scriptM; with the expected Hessian as metric tensor. Merging = weighted Fréchet mean that minimizes geodesic distances to all fine-tuned models.
02
Truncated SVD per task vector (top-k triples/layer). Orthogonal Procrustes whitening yields column-orthonormal basis S. Empirical Fisher diagonal projected onto subspace recovers off-diagonal curvature as a dense kT × kT matrix.
03
Subspace Fréchet mean solved as a linear system: δ_m = α H̄¹ ∑ H̃_t δ_t. Lift back: θ_m = θ_0 + Sδ_m*.

Results & Highlights

+1.10% avg · ViT-B/32
+0.48% avg · ViT-B/16
+2.4% worst-task · ViT-B/16
  • Fisher Averaging collapses on all backbones — diagonal full-space Fisher is too coarse; subspace projection fixes it.
  • Empirical Fisher saturates at 10% training data; accuracy flat down to 0.5% (1–6 batches/task).
  • Curvature diagnostic η rises monotonically with rank, correctly predicting when curvature-aware merging helps.
  • Optimal rescaling α ∈ [0.7, 1.0], far above the literature default 1/√T ≈ 0.354.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Principled Riemannian framework with three formal theorems.
  • Unifies Fisher, RegMean, TSV-M as special cases.
  • Single forward-backward pass for curvature; small linear-system solve.
  • Strictly outperforms all baselines at matched rank.
  • 0.5% training data sufficient for curvature estimation.

Limitations

  • Only CLIP-ViT image classification; no LLM/NLP evaluation.
  • ViT-L/14 margin shrinks to +0.06% — saturation ceiling.
  • Rescaling α requires tuning; no principled selector.
  • Shared-loss-basin assumption may fail for dissimilar tasks.
  • More faithful Hessians (K-FAC) unexplored.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does EpiMer perform for cross-architecture merging from different pre-trained checkpoints?
  2. Can the diagnostic η drive adaptive per-task rank selection instead of fixed k?
  3. Does the shared-loss-basin assumption hold when merging LLMs fine-tuned on highly dissimilar tasks?
  4. Is there a principled way to set the global rescaling α without a sweep?
  5. Could K-FAC or Hessian-vector products (Pearlmutter) yield further gains over the empirical Fisher diagonal?
