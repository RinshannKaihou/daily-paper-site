Measuring Prediction Uncertainty in Neural Cellular Automata

Ario Sadafi, Michael Deutgens, Nassir Navab, Carsten Marr · Helmholtz Munich / TU Munich / LMU Munich · May 2026

Resilience measures NCA uncertainty by perturbing the final state and checking recovery — zero retraining, zero architecture changes.

9.26%
ΔDice@90 gain (Kvasir-SEG) · AUROC 0.972 (DSB 2018)

Enables trustworthy clinical deployment of lightweight NCA segmentation models without any retraining or architectural overhead.

Only binary segmentation evaluated; single random perturbation per image may miss unstable dynamics directions.

Methodology

Perturb-and-Recover Pipeline

The method treats NCA as a dynamical system where convergent attractors signal confident predictions. Uncertainty is measured by probing attractor stability.

Step 1

Run NCA for T steps to get final state S_T and mask m

Step 2

Inject Gaussian noise ε ~ N(0, 0.02²) into latent channels only

Step 3

Run T' = 12 additional NCA steps from perturbed state

Step 4

Score: u = 1 − IoU(m, m'). High = unstable = uncertain

Strengths & Limitations
Strengths
  • Training-free — no retraining, no dropout, no architecture changes
  • Conceptually elegant — probes dynamical stability directly
  • Consistently strong across 5 diverse benchmarks and 4 metrics
  • Readout-agnostic — applicable beyond segmentation
  • Lightweight — only 12 additional NCA steps per inference
Limitations
  • Binary segmentation only — multi-class extension unexplored
  • Single perturbation may miss unstable directions
  • Identical NCA training settings across all datasets (suboptimal)
  • Limited ablation on σ and T' hyperparameters
  • No comparison with U-Net uncertainty methods (MC dropout, ensembles)
Open Questions

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does resilience scale to multi-class segmentation with multiple competing attractors?
  2. Would ensembling multiple perturbations improve robustness of the uncertainty estimate?
  3. Can resilience combine with complementary baselines (flicker + resilience) for a hybrid signal?
  4. How does resilience compare to MC dropout or deep ensembles on a standard U-Net?
  5. Is there a theoretical connection between resilience and Lyapunov exponents of learned NCA dynamics?
