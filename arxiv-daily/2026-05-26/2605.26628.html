Tail-Aware HiFloat4: W4A4 Post-Training Quantization for Wan2.2

Zhanfeng Feng · Shuai Guo · Xin Di · Long Peng · Yang Cao · Zhengjun Zha  —  USTC  ·  2026-05-26

Percentile calibration enables W4A4 quantization of 800 layers with only 0.092 VBench mean drop — but subject consistency collapses by 0.40.

Key Metric
−0.092
VBench unweighted mean drop (0.680 → 0.588)
Why This Matters
First W4A4 quantization of a production video generator exposes identity preservation as the critical 4-bit bottleneck.
Critical Edge
No ablation on percentile p; 43% subject consistency crash; high-precision block budget left completely unused.

Methodology

Three-stage pipeline applied to Wan2.2-I2V-A14B dual-transformer: calibration, PTQ state construction, quantized inference.

  • 16 calibration prompts (OpenS2V-5M) feed BF16 model; hooks collect per-channel absolute activations. Empirical p-th percentile replaces hard max for robust range estimation against outlier-dominated channels.
  • SmoothQuant-style channel masks: mi = wiα / (ai + ε)1−α. Pre-scaled weights quantized to HiFloat4; masks applied to activations at runtime.
  • 800 linear layers quantized (400 per transformer). 12 boundary modules (embeddings, output projections) retained in BF16. No transformer block kept at full precision.
  • Compact PTQ state stores per-layer masks and descriptors instead of duplicating BF16 weights — links back to original checkpoint.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-to-low. ViDiT-Q applied to a new model with a straightforward percentile swap for max.
  • Rigor: Thorough VBench evaluation with matched settings, but zero ablation on the percentile hyperparameter p.
  • Reproducibility: High. Public base model, explicit protocol, compact PTQ format.
  • Significance: The 0.401 subject consistency drop is a serious concern — not production-ready at W4A4.

Strengths

  • Clean separation of calibration, PTQ, and inference stages
  • Honest reporting of the subject consistency failure rather than cherry-picking
  • Compact PTQ state avoids redundant weight duplication
  • Principled percentile improvement over max-based activation stats
  • Fully matched BF16 vs W4A4 evaluation protocol

Limitations

  • No ablation isolating percentile vs max calibration
  • 43% subject consistency crash — unusable for identity-critical tasks
  • Specific to Wan2.2 + HiFloat4; generalizability unclear
  • Does not use ViDiT-Q rotation or explore allowed high-precision block retention
  • Only 16 calibration prompts; no sensitivity analysis on set size
  • No inference speedup or memory savings reported

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How sensitive is performance to the choice of percentile p? Is there a sweet spot balancing subject consistency against outlier handling?
  2. Would allocating the allowed 2 high-precision blocks to identity-sensitive layers recover subject consistency?
  3. Could integrating ViDiT-Q's optional rotation component mitigate the subject consistency degradation?
  4. What are the actual FLOPs reduction and memory savings of W4A4 HiFloat4 vs BF16 on real hardware?
  5. How does compact PTQ state compare to full-weight checkpointing in quantization error accumulation?
