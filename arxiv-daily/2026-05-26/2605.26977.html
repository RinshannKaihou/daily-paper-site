Convergence of Spectral Descent for Non-smooth Optimization

Yixuan Yang · Yuqing He · Song Li

⚠ Abstract-only summary
Key Finding
Spectral Descent achieves global linear convergence in non-smooth convex optimization, extending Muon theory beyond smoothness.
Key Metric
Global Linear Convergence
Why This Matters
First convergence guarantees for Muon-type methods in non-smooth settings — bridges the theory-practice gap for LLM training optimizers.
Critical Edge
Gap between simplified SD/TSD and full Muon unclear; convexity assumptions may not hold in deep learning.
Methodology
  • Core convergence analysis — Proves global linear convergence for both SD and TSD under convexity, Lipschitz continuity, and sharpness, extending prior results that required smoothness.
  • Regularized variants — Equips SD/TSD with decoupled weight decay, establishes a Frank-Wolfe connection, and derives sublinear convergence rates for regularized formulations.
  • Low-rank recovery application — Instantiates the framework on robust low-rank matrix recovery with mixed sparse & dense noise, yielding rigorous recovery guarantees.
Results & Highlights
  • Global linear convergence for SD and TSD in non-smooth convex settings, relaxing prior smoothness assumptions
  • Sublinear convergence guarantees for regularized variants via Frank-Wolfe connections
  • Recovery guarantees for robust low-rank matrix recovery under mixed noise regimes
  • Empirical validation through numerical experiments confirming theoretical predictions
Relevance
