> **一句话总结：** 本文从动力学系统角度诊断循环语言模型的测试时扩展失败问题，发现归一化位置导致稳定性-有效性权衡，提出STARS框架通过Jacobian谱半径正则化约束隐状态收敛到渐近稳定不动点，在算术任务上实现100%可靠扩展，在GSM8K上使性能退化从20.47%降至8.26%。

> **TL;DR:** Looped Language Models (LoopLMs) suffer performance collapse when recurrent depth exceeds the training horizon due to an inherent stability-effectiveness trade-off governed by normalization placement. STARS combines Jacobian Spectral Radius Regularization (via efficient single-step power iteration with JVP) with random loop sampling to enforce convergence to asymptotically stable fixed points. On 4-digit addition, STARS maintains 100% accuracy across all depths. On Ouro-1.4B fine-tuned with STARS, GSM8K degradation drops from 20.47% to 8.26% at 8 recurrent steps, while peak accuracy improves by 4.01%. Published at ICML 2026.

## 核心贡献

- 通过系统性诊断实验揭示了循环语言模型中归一化位置决定稳定性-有效性权衡的根本机制：内部归一化（Pre-Norm）保持信息流但导致隐状态指数增长，外部归一化（Post-Norm）保证有界但收敛到无效不动点
- 发现Prelude/Coda层、L2正则化、随机循环采样等常见方法均无法从根本上解决该权衡
- 提出STARS框架，基于Lyapunov线性化定理，通过Jacobian谱半径正则化（JSRR）约束隐状态收敛到渐近稳定不动点
- 设计了高效的谱半径估计方法：单步幂迭代+Jacobian-vector product，避免显式构建Jacobian矩阵，适用于大规模LLM训练
- 在算术任务上实现完全可靠的测试时扩展（100%准确率不随深度下降），在复杂数学推理上大幅缓解性能退化

## Key Contributions

- Identifies the normalization placement as the root cause of the stability-effectiveness trade-off in LoopLMs through systematic latent dynamics analysis: internal normalization (Pre-Norm) preserves information flow but causes unbounded state growth; external normalization (Post-Norm) ensures boundedness but converges to ineffective fixed points
- Demonstrates that common remedies — Prelude/Coda layers, L2 regularization on adjacent hidden states, and random loop sampling alone — cannot resolve this fundamental deadlock
- Proposes STARS (STAbility-driven Recurrent Scaling), grounded in the Lyapunov Linearization Theorem, which constrains the spectral radius of the Jacobian ρ(J) < 1 to enforce convergence to asymptotically stable fixed points
- Develops an efficient spectral radius estimator using single-step power iteration with Jacobian-vector products (JVP), avoiding O(D²) Jacobian construction — practical for large-scale LLM training with ~2x overhead over standard SFT
- Achieves fully reliable test-time scaling on 4-digit addition (100% at all depths) and substantially mitigates degradation on mathematical reasoning benchmarks

## Methodology

The paper proceeds in two phases: diagnostic analysis and framework design.

**Diagnostic Phase (Section 4):** The authors study a minimal looped Transformer (single GPT block, d=512, 8 heads) on 4-digit addition. They systematically vary normalization type (LayerNorm, RMSNorm, SimpleNorm) and placement (Pre-, Post-, Pre-Sandwich, Post-Sandwich), then sweep test-time iteration counts T_test beyond training depth T_train=4. PCA projection of latent states reveals two regimes: internal normalization causes state drift/divergence; external normalization causes convergence to poor attractors.

**STARS Framework (Section 5):** The training objective combines standard cross-entropy with JSRR:
$$\mathcal{L}_{STARS} = \mathbb{E}_{t \sim P} \left[ (1-\lambda) \cdot \mathcal{L}_{SFT}^{(t)} + \lambda \cdot \mathcal{L}_{JSRR}^{(t)} \right]$$
where $\mathcal{L}_{JSRR}^{(t)} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_i \|J^{(t,i)} v_1^{(t,i)}\|_2^2$ estimates the squared spectral radius via one-step power iteration. The loop depth $t$ is sampled from a log-normal distribution $P$, ensuring regularization is applied across diverse states. Key hyperparameters: λ=0.1, log-normal sampling with µ∈{1.7, 2.0}, σ∈{0.4, 0.7}. The framework uses Post-Sandwich normalization as its base architecture since it naturally tends toward attractors.

## Results & Highlights

**4-digit addition (synthetic):** STARS achieves 100% accuracy across all recurrent depths tested, with latent states converging to stable fixed points. Baseline models collapse to ~0% accuracy beyond the training horizon.

**Mathematical reasoning (Ouro-1.4B, fine-tuned on NuminaMath-400K):**

| Model | Steps | GSM8K | MATH500 | ASDiv | SVAMP | AMC23 | AVG |
|-------|-------|-------|---------|-------|-------|-------|-----|
| Ouro-1.4B-SFT | 4 | 80.06 | 64.60 | 83.47 | 76.67 | 47.50 | 70.46 |
| Ouro-1.4B-SFT | 8 | 60.05 | 39.20 | 75.10 | 68.00 | 22.50 | 52.97 |
| Ouro-1.4B-STARS | 4 | **81.96** | **67.40** | **84.73** | **84.33** | **52.50** | **74.18** |
| Ouro-1.4B-STARS | 8 | 74.45 | 54.80 | 82.52 | 81.00 | 35.00 | 65.55 |

- GSM8K degradation: 20.47% (SFT) → 8.26% (STARS) at 8 steps
- Peak improvement: +4.01% on GSM8K, +2.80% on MATH500, +5.00% on AMC23
- Training overhead: ~2x relative to standard SFT

**Ablation:** Both random loop sampling and JSRR contribute independently; the full STARS combination yields the best stability. The regularization weight λ must be tuned — values ≥0.15 can suppress learning.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The dynamical systems diagnosis of LoopLM scaling failures is rigorous and insightful. The normalization placement analysis (internal vs. external) is a clean finding. The JSRR approach adapts equilibrium model ideas (Bai et al., 2019, 2021) to modern looped LLMs with practical engineering (single-step JVP).
- **Rigor:** Strong diagnostic methodology — controlled experiments isolating one variable at a time, PCA visualization of latent trajectories, systematic sweep of normalization variants. The mathematical grounding via Lyapunov theory is appropriate and correctly applied.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code is public. The algorithm is clearly specified (Algorithm 1 in Appendix B). Hyperparameters are detailed for all experiments.
- **Significance:** Addresses a critical practical problem for the emerging LoopLM paradigm. If looped inference is to be viable, reliable test-time scaling is essential. However, the evaluation is limited to mathematical reasoning tasks.
- **Red flags:** (1) Performance still degrades at 8 steps (65.55% vs 74.18% avg) — STARS mitigates but does not fully solve the problem. (2) Training overhead of ~2x is non-trivial. (3) Only tested on Ouro-1.4B (a relatively small model); scaling to larger LoopLMs is unverified. (4) The paper acknowledges that performance does not always improve monotonically with more steps on harder tasks.

## Strengths

- Principled dynamical systems analysis that yields actionable architectural insights (normalization placement matters more than normalization type)
- Efficient JSRR implementation via single-step power iteration makes the approach practical for large models
- Clean ablation study isolating the contribution of each component
- Honest about limitations — explicitly notes that fully reliable monotonic scaling remains unsolved

## Limitations

- Evaluation restricted to mathematical reasoning; generalization to commonsense, code generation, or broader NLP tasks is untested
- STARS mitigates degradation but does not achieve monotonic improvement with depth on hard tasks (AMC23: 52.50% → 35.00% from 4→8 steps)
- ~2x training overhead may compound with the already expensive training of looped models
- Only validated on a 1.4B parameter model; unclear if the approach transfers to larger LoopLMs where the spectral radius estimation may face different numerical challenges

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can JSRR be applied to standard (non-looped) transformers to improve training stability, e.g., in very deep models?
- How does the single-step power iteration estimate quality degrade as model dimension scales to 7B, 13B, or 70B parameters?
- Is there an optimal λ schedule (e.g., annealing) that could allow strong early learning while enforcing stability later?
- Could adaptive loop depth — stopping iteration when the spectral radius estimate falls below a threshold — provide a principled early-exit criterion?
- Does the fixed-point structure induced by STARS provide interpretability benefits (e.g., can we probe what the model has "decided" at its attractor)?
