Probing Minimalist Phase Structure in LLMs: What Universal Dependencies Cannot Represent

Yuanhao Chen, Peter Chin Dartmouth College 2026-05-26
Key Finding
13 LLMs across four families encode phase structure invisible to UD probing, causally confirmed via activation patching.
Key Metric
13/13
models replicate the within-clause sign asymmetry; 12/13 pass causal patching.
Why This Matters
UD-based probing underestimates syntactic encoding in LLMs — the true structure runs deeper than dependency annotations reveal.
Critical Edge
Phase-cohesion is post-hoc; Qwen-3-4B failure unexplained; English-only stimuli exploit language-specific verb-class properties.

  • βfin ranges from +0.55 (Gemma-3-1B) to +0.99 (Gemma-3-27B); 94% median FDR-significant layers across the panel.
  • Sign asymmetry magnitudes typically 0.3–0.6; no alternative account (UD, linear distance, monotone complexity) predicts this pattern.
  • Negative control (patching at wh-position): |Δβ| ≤ 0.05 in 12/13 models.
  • Qwen-3-4B is the sole failure on both canonical-layer gradient and causal test.
  • Scaling is heterogeneous: Gemma scales monotonically; Llama-3.2-1B (+0.77) exceeds Llama-3.1-8B (+0.72).

Methodology

Stimuli. 1,000 item triples of wh-questions sharing the wh-phrase, embedded subject lemma, and embedded verb lemma. Three conditions: bare small clause (perception/causative), infinitival (ECM/object-control), finite (bridge verbs). UD-tree distances verified identical across conditions.

Probing. Structural probes (Hewitt & Manning 2019) — linear projections from hidden states to Euclidean space — trained on UD-EWT, evaluated on wh-movement stimuli without re-fitting. Two probe pairs: wh-esubj (cross-clause) and esubj-evb (within-clause).

Statistics. Treatment-coded OLS with cluster-robust SEs; Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction per model.

Causal test. Activation patching replaces embedded subject's residual-stream at L* with counterfactual source.

structural probe UD-EWT OLS FDR correction activation patching 13 models 1B–27B

Models Tested

13 decoder-only base models from four architecture families:

  • Gemma-3: 1B, 4B, 12B, 27B
  • Llama-3: 3.2-1B, 3.2-3B, 3.1-8B
  • Mistral: 7B-v0.3, Nemo-12B, Small-24B
  • Qwen: 2.5-1.5B, 2.5-3B, 3-4B

Canonical layer L* selected per model as the layer maximizing the most reliable contrast. Probe training <2h on single GPU.

Strengths

  • Elegant experimental design: controls for UD confounds by construction, not statistical adjustment.
  • 13/13 replication across four architecture families — exceptionally robust for a probing result.
  • Canonical-layer reporting sets a new standard, removing a per-contrast degree of freedom.
  • Causal patching with a negative control directly engages probing-scepticism literature.

Limitations

  • English-only stimuli exploiting English-specific verb-class and case properties.
  • Excludes instruction-tuned models, MoE architectures, and models above 27B.
  • Phase-count gradient prediction is directional only — no magnitude-ratio predictions.
  • Phase-internal cohesion inferred, not directly measured; alternatives not ruled out mechanistically.
  • Qwen-3-4B failure remains unexplained.

Open Questions

  1. Would the gradient and asymmetry replicate in V2 or ergative languages with overt phase diagnostics?
  2. Do instruction-tuned or >27B models show stronger or qualitatively different effects?
  3. Can circuit analysis identify specific attention heads or MLP subnetworks responsible for phase-sensitive representations?
  4. Is the asymmetry driven by phase-internal cohesion specifically, or by some other within-clause tightening correlated with phase structure?
  5. Does the Qwen-3-4B failure reflect an architectural difference or a training-data artifact?
