> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 EpiMer 框架，将模型合并重新定义为 Riemannian 流形上的 Fréchet 均值问题，通过在任务向量子空间中引入 Hessian 曲率信息，在 CLIP-ViT 八任务合并中全面超越现有方法。

> **TL;DR:** EpiMer reframes model merging as finding the Fréchet mean on a Riemannian manifold with expected Hessian as the metric, restricted to a low-rank task-vector subspace. It unifies curvature-aware and spectral methods under one framework and strictly outperforms all baselines on CLIP-ViT merging across eight image classification tasks.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **EpiMer（Epistemic Merging）** 框架，将模型合并问题重新定义为 Riemannian 流形上的 Fréchet 均值求解问题，以期望 Hessian 作为度量张量，揭示了局部曲率与参数认知不确定性之间的联系。
- 在低秩任务向量子空间中进行 Fréchet 均值求解，使基于曲率的方法既理论严谨又计算可行。
- 给出合并误差的理论分解：误差上界由**子空间 Fréchet 方差**（不可约的任务冲突）和**残差能量**（投影信息损失）两部分构成。
- 推导出闭合形式的曲率优势条件（Theorem 3），给出了**曲率异质性诊断量 η**，可事先判断曲率感知合并是否优于平坦几何方法。
- 证明现有方法（Task Arithmetic、Fisher Averaging、TSV-M 等）是该框架在不同子空间和度量选择下的特例。

## Key Contributions

- **C1:** A geometric framework unifying curvature-aware methods (Fisher Averaging, RegMean) and spectral methods (TSV-M) as special cases of the subspace Fréchet mean under different subspace and metric choices.
- **C2:** Theoretical decomposition of the merging error bound into subspace Fréchet variance and residual energy, with a closed-form characterization of when curvature-aware merging provably outperforms flat-geometry alternatives (Theorem 3).
- **C3:** EpiMer, a practical algorithm with curvature-guided subspace selection that strictly outperforms the strongest baseline TSV-M on all three CLIP-ViT backbones at matched rank, improving both average and worst-task accuracy.

## Methodology

EpiMer operates in three stages:

1. **Geometric Formulation:** The parameter space is modeled as a Riemannian manifold $\mathcal{M}$ with the expected Hessian $G(\theta) = \mathbb{E}[\nabla^2_\theta \mathcal{L}(x, \theta)]$ as the metric tensor. Model merging is cast as finding the Fréchet mean that minimizes weighted geodesic distances to all fine-tuned models.

2. **Subspace Solver:** To avoid the intractability of full-space Hessian operations:
   - Each task vector is decomposed via truncated SVD (top-$k$ triples per layer).
   - Per-task left/right singular factors are concatenated across tasks and whitened via Orthogonal Procrustes to form a column-orthonormal per-layer basis $\mathbf{S}^{(\ell)}$.
   - The empirical Fisher diagonal $\mathbf{v}_t = \mathbb{E}[(\nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_t)^2]$ (computable in a single forward-backward pass) is projected onto the subspace, yielding a dense $\tilde{\mathbf{H}}_t^{(\ell)} \in \mathbb{R}^{kT \times kT}$ that recovers off-diagonal curvature signal lost by the ambient diagonal proxy.
   - The subspace Fréchet mean is solved as a linear system: $\tilde{\boldsymbol{\delta}}_m^{(\ell)} = \alpha\bar{\mathbf{H}}^{-1}\sum_t \tilde{\mathbf{H}}_t^{(\ell)}\tilde{\boldsymbol{\delta}}_t^{(\ell)}$.

3. **Lift back:** The merged model is recovered as $\theta_m = \theta_0 + \mathbf{S}\tilde{\boldsymbol{\delta}}_m^*$.

## Results & Highlights

- **Average accuracy improvements over TSV-M** (at $k=32$): +1.10% on ViT-B/32, +0.48% on ViT-B/16, +0.06% on ViT-L/14.
- **Worst-task accuracy improvements over TSV-M:** +0.8% on ViT-B/32, +2.4% on ViT-B/16, +0.1% on ViT-L/14 — curvature awareness tightens the worst-case margin without sacrificing average performance.
- **Fisher Averaging collapses** on all backbones, confirming that a diagonal Fisher in the full parameter space is too coarse; EpiMer's subspace projection fixes this.
- **Empirical Fisher diagonal is data-efficient:** accuracy is flat even at 0.5% of the training data (1–6 batches per task); saturates by 10%.
- **Curvature heterogeneity diagnostic $\eta$** rises monotonically with rank $k$ within each backbone, correctly predicting when curvature-aware merging has room to improve.
- **Global rescaling $\alpha$** matters significantly: the optimal $\alpha \in [0.7, 1.0]$ far exceeds the literature default $1/\sqrt{T} \approx 0.354$.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The Riemannian geometric formulation with the Fréchet mean objective is a principled and novel reframing. The unification of prior methods under one equation (Proposition 2) is elegant and valuable.
- **Theoretical rigor: Strong.** Three theorems (existence/uniqueness, error bound, curvature advantage) with complete proofs. The error decomposition into $\mathcal{V}_S$ and $\mathcal{R}_S$ provides clear intuition.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Uses public checkpoints, stdlib-only Python (no unusual dependencies), and provides Algorithm 1 in pseudocode. The empirical Fisher only needs one forward-backward pass.
- **Significance: High.** Model merging is a hot topic. Unifying spectral and curvature-aware methods is a meaningful conceptual advance.
- **Limitations of evaluation:** Only evaluated on CLIP-ViT with eight image classification tasks. No evaluation on LLMs or NLP tasks. The shrinking margin on larger backbones (ViT-L/14) raises the question of scalability to very large models where the baseline is already near saturation.
- **Red flags:** The empirical Fisher diagonal approximation, while practical, is a strong simplification. The paper acknowledges this but does not explore whether more faithful Hessian approximations (e.g., K-FAC) would yield further gains. The per-task tagged basis is inherited from TSV-M; the main algorithmic novelty is the curvature-weighted aggregation step.

## Strengths

- Principled geometric framework with rigorous theoretical analysis.
- Unifies a broad family of prior methods under one equation.
- Practical: requires only a single forward-backward pass for curvature estimation, and the subspace solve is a small linear system.
- Strictly outperforms all baselines on every backbone at matched rank.
- Data-efficient curvature estimation (0.5% training data suffices).
- Curvature heterogeneity diagnostic $\eta$ provides actionable prior information.

## Limitations

- Evaluated only on CLIP-ViT image classification; generalization to LLMs and NLP tasks is untested.
- Performance gains diminish on larger backbones (ViT-L/14: only +0.06% over TSV-M), suggesting a saturation ceiling.
- The diagnostic $\eta$ predicts within-backbone curvature utility but not cross-backbone improvement magnitude.
- The global rescaling $\alpha$ requires tuning; the paper does not provide a principled method for setting it.
- The assumption that all fine-tuned models reside within a single low-change-in-loss basin (Assumption 3) may not hold for highly dissimilar tasks.

## 相关性

本文对模型合并提供了深刻的几何视角，统一了现有曲率感知方法和谱方法。对于关注多任务学习、模型集成和参数高效微调的研究者具有直接参考价值。其子空间 Fréchet 均值的思想可扩展到持续学习和迁移学习场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does EpiMer perform when merging models from different pre-trained checkpoints or architectures (cross-architecture merging)?
2. Can the curvature heterogeneity diagnostic $\eta$ be used to adaptively select the per-task rank $k$ rather than using a fixed $k$?
3. What happens when merging LLMs fine-tuned on highly dissimilar tasks — does Assumption 3 (shared low-change-of-loss basin) still hold?
4. Is there a principled way to set the global rescaling $\alpha$ without a sweep?
5. Could more faithful Hessian approximations (e.g., K-FAC, Hessian-vector products via Pearlmutter's trick) provide further gains over the empirical Fisher diagonal?
