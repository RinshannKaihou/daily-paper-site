Localizing Memorized Regions in Diffusion Models via Coordinate-Wise Curvature Differences

2605.26756 ICML 2026 Kim & Lee, Hanyang Univ.
diffusion-models memorization privacy geometric-deep-learning image-generation
0.953
IoU on SD v1.4 localization
Key Finding
Curvature-difference methods pinpoint memorized pixel regions in diffusion outputs by subtracting baseline curvature, outperforming attention-based localization by +38.9 IoU.
Why This Matters
First framework that spatially localizes—not just detects—training data memorization, directly enabling targeted privacy and copyright audits of generated images.
Critical Edge
Only addresses verbatim/template memorization; cannot detect concept-level memorization such as celebrity likenesses or artistic styles, and the Δĥθ̃ variant requires access to intermediate training checkpoints.

Methodology

1. Coordinate-wise curvature via Hessian diagonal

Uses diag(−Hθ(xt, c)) (Hutchinson estimator, Rademacher vectors) to measure per-pixel variance collapse at a chosen reverse-diffusion timestep. No full Hessian needed—only Hessian-vector products through autodiff.

2. Curvature-difference metrics

Subtracts an underfitted baseline to isolate overfitting-driven curvature:

  • Δh∅ — unconditional model as baseline (no class labels needed).
  • Δhθ̃ — a less-trained checkpoint of the same model (e.g. SD v1.1 for SD v1.4).

3. Score-difference surrogates (Hessian-free)

From the Fisher information identity (Prop. 4.2), curvature difference equals the expected squared score difference, yielding cheap proxies Δs∅ and Δsθ̃ that need only score evaluations—no Hessian at all.

4. Final-step evaluation

All metrics are evaluated at the last DDIM step (t ≈ 0) where diffusion noise is minimal and curvature divergence is most pronounced. Channel summation produces a 2D spatial heatmap.

  • Detection AUC 0.995–0.998 with TPR@1%FPR up to 0.988, consistently beating attention-based baselines.
  • Even K=1 Hutchinson sample yields competitive localization; overhead only ~0.42s on a single L40S GPU.
  • Score-difference surrogates are slightly noisier per-coordinate but highly effective after spatial averaging.
  • Generalizes to Realistic Vision v5.1 (fine-tuned SD variant).

Strengths

  • Principled geometric framework that unifies and explains prior heuristic metrics.
  • +38.9 IoU margin over the previous attention-based state-of-the-art (Bright Ending).
  • Both curvature and score variants offered, enabling accuracy–efficiency tradeoffs.
  • Honest discussion of failure modes; synthetic 2D experiment provides clear intuition.

Limitations

  • Cannot capture concept-level memorization (celebrity faces, styles) where signal is globally distributed.
  • Δhθ̃ requires intermediate checkpoints—often unavailable in practice.
  • SD v2.1 evaluation is ~85% "Shaw Floors" prompts, limiting diversity claims.
  • IoU threshold sweeping is per-metric, not per-sample, which may overestimate real-world performance.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can the framework extend to concept-level memorization (celebrity likenesses, artistic styles) beyond verbatim/template cases?
  2. How does it perform on DiT-based architectures (SDXL, SD3) where U-Net structure differs significantly?
  3. Could curvature-difference signals be used for targeted mitigation—selectively perturbing only memorized spatial regions during generation?
  4. What is the relationship between curvature-difference magnitude and degree of copyright/privacy violation in partial memorization?
  5. Can the Fisher information interpretation inform training-time regularization that prevents overfitting-driven curvature from arising?
