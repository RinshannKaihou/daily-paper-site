> **一句话总结：** 本文通过可控知识图谱环境系统研究了RLVR后训练中数据分配问题，发现联合覆盖推理深度和环境复杂度优于单轴方案，溯因推理在训练分布外严重退化，且均匀混合优于分阶段课程学习。

> **TL;DR:** A controlled study using synthetic knowledge-graph environments showing that joint depth-complexity coverage in RLVR outperforms single-axis recipes, abductive reasoning fails to generalize beyond training coverage, and uniform mixing beats staged curricula under fixed budgets.

## 核心贡献

- 首次将RLVR训练数据难度分解为两个正交维度：推理深度（reasoning depth）和环境复杂度（environment complexity），而非仅将其视为标量难度。
- 构建了可控合成知识图谱环境，使得预训练和后训练分布完全可知，从而能够干净地归因RLVR数据分配的效果。
- 系统研究了四种逻辑推理家族（演绎、溯因、归纳、类比）对同一后训练分布的非均匀响应。
- 证明了均匀混合策略在固定预算下优于分阶段课程学习，挑战了"由易到难"的直觉。

## Key Contributions

- **Two-axis difficulty characterization:** Decomposes RLVR difficulty into reasoning depth (multi-step commitment) and environment complexity (relevant-structure selection amid distractors), showing both axes are independently important.
- **Controlled synthetic KG framework:** Constructs a knowledge-graph environment where pre-training and post-training distributions are explicitly specified, enabling clean attribution of RLVR gains to specific data allocations.
- **Four reasoning families under RLVR:** Studies deductive state tracking, abductive recovery, inductive rule induction, and analogical transfer within the same framework, revealing non-uniform responses.
- **Three core findings:** (1) Joint depth-complexity coverage outperforms single-axis recipes; (2) Abductive reasoning degrades outside the RL-covered region while other families generalize; (3) Uniform mixing beats staged curricula under fixed budgets.

## Methodology

- **Synthetic knowledge-graph world:** Each instance is a graph $G = (P, O, E_{dyn}, E_{stat}, s_0)$ combining dynamic event-state components (object ownership, possession, integrity) with static kinship relations.
- **Reasoning depth $D$:** Counts dynamic operation events on the causal chain for the target object; ranges $D \in \{1, \ldots, 10\}$.
- **Environment complexity $T$:** Indexes structural parameters $(k_{chains}, n_{persons}, \rho_{crossover}, \rho_{exchange})$ controlling parallel chains, entity scale, cross-chain overlap, and exchange frequency; ranges $T \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}$.
- **Model:** 107M-parameter Qwen2.5-style decoder-only transformer, pre-trained on shallow low-complexity region ($D \leq 4, T \leq 2$) with 3.14B tokens.
- **Post-training:** GRPO with 15 data-distribution recipes, fixed budget of 220K tasks per recipe, outcome-gated reward ($R = 0.8P + 0.2A$ when answer correct, 0 otherwise).
- **Evaluation:** 4,800 graphs across a 60-cell $(D, T)$ grid, strict process-verified pass@k, ceiling gain (CG) and single gain (SG) metrics.
- **Benchmarking:** 22 off-the-shelf models (including GPT-5.4, Kimi-K2.6, GLM-5.1, DeepSeek-R1) evaluated with task- and cell-matched 3-shot prompting.

## Results & Highlights

- **Joint coverage wins:** Shallow-Mix, Offbase-Mix, and Full-Coverage recipes consistently outrank single-axis recipes (Depth-Uniform, Cmplx-Uniform) on both deductive and abductive tasks.
- **Deductive is depth-sensitive, abductive is balanced:** Deductive gains are larger along depth extension; abductive gains are more balanced across both axes because distractors create competing states that complicate backward inference.
- **Abductive generalization failure:** Abductive reasoning shows negative out-of-recipe CG, degrading below the pre-trained model outside the training distribution. Deductive, inductive, and analogy retain 50-70% of in-recipe gains out-of-recipe.
- **Task-pair correlations cluster:** Deductive-abductive and inductive-analogy are the strongest positive correlation pairs, reflecting shared reasoning components.
- **Analogy mode collapse:** Analogy shows high SG but low CG, suggesting RL sharpens a dominant pattern without expanding the sampled candidate set.
- **Uniform mixing > curriculum:** For both depth and complexity axes, uniform mixing outperforms 3-block easy-to-hard, which outperforms many-block schedules. Each curriculum transition introduces forgetting and adaptation costs.
- **Off-the-shelf models mirror the asymmetry:** All benchmarked LLMs (including GPT-5.4 at 0.265 abductive vs 0.743 deductive) show much stronger deductive than abductive performance, confirming the gap is not an artifact of the controlled setup.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The two-axis difficulty decomposition and controlled study of four reasoning families under RLVR is novel. Most prior work treats difficulty as scalar and focuses on deductive/mathematical reasoning.
- **Rigor (High):** The controlled pre-training/post-training setup enables clean causal attribution that is impossible with off-the-shelf models. The 60-cell grid evaluation with strict process-verified metrics is thorough.
- **Reproducibility (Medium-High):** Synthetic data generation is fully specified; code committed to release under MIT license. However, single runs due to compute constraints limit statistical confidence.
- **Significance (High):** Practical implications for RLVR recipe design — the finding that abductive reasoning needs direct coverage and that uniform mixing suffices challenges several active research directions (curriculum RL).
- **Red flags:** The 107M model scale is very small compared to production models. The synthetic KG domain is narrow (object ownership + kinship). Whether findings transfer to natural language reasoning at scale remains an open question. The benchmark comparison (Table 1) is diagnostic rather than controlled — the 107M domain-specific models receive extensive pre-training that off-the-shelf models do not.

## Strengths

- Clean experimental design with full control over training distributions — rare in LLM reasoning research.
- Systematic study of 15 RL recipes across four reasoning families and a 60-cell difficulty grid.
- The abductive generalization failure finding is important and well-supported by both controlled and off-the-shelf model evidence.
- Practical, actionable guidance: cover both depth and complexity, directly cover abductive targets, default to uniform mixing.
- Extensive appendices with ablations on data volume, number of rollouts, task mix, and process-reward ratio.

## Limitations

- **Synthetic domain gap:** The KG world (object ownership + kinship) is far simpler than natural language reasoning. Semantic ambiguity, open-world knowledge, and linguistic diversity are absent.
- **Scale:** Only 107M parameters tested. Findings may not directly transfer to billion-parameter models where emergent reasoning behaviors appear.
- **Single runs:** No confidence intervals; results reported from single training runs due to compute constraints.
- **Narrow task families:** Each reasoning form is one controlled instantiation. Real abductive reasoning (e.g., scientific hypothesis formation) may behave differently.
- **Finite grid:** $D \leq 10, T \leq 6$ — trends may change under longer reasoning horizons or denser distractor environments.

## 相关性

本文对理解RLVR后训练的数据分配策略具有直接指导意义。对于任何涉及推理模型后训练的研究，其关于联合覆盖两个难度轴、溯因推理的脆弱性、以及均匀混合优于课程学习的发现都具有重要参考价值。特别是对关注推理能力泛化和多任务后训练的工作，本文提供了重要的实验证据。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the findings transfer to natural language reasoning domains (math, code, science) at billion-parameter scale?
- Can targeted abductive training data creation close the deductive-abductive gap in frontier models?
- What mechanisms cause abductive reasoning to degrade below the pre-trained baseline outside the training distribution — is it catastrophic forgetting, reward hacking, or something else?
- How does the optimal data allocation change when the post-training budget scales up by 10-100x?
- Would multi-objective reward shaping (e.g., explicitly rewarding backward inference steps) improve abductive generalization?
