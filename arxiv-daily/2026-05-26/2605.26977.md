> **一句话总结：** 本文为 Muon 优化器的简化版——谱下降（SD）与截断谱下降（TSD）——首次建立非光滑凸情形下的全局线性收敛保证（Theorem 4.1/4.2），并证明带解耦权重衰减的正则化变体（RSD-WD/RTSD-WD）通过 Frank-Wolfe 联系获得 O(1/√T) 次线性速率（Theorem 4.3/4.4），最后用其求解鲁棒低秩矩阵恢复给出严格恢复保证（Theorem 5.1）。

> **TL;DR:** First convergence theory for Muon-type optimizers in the non-smooth setting. Under convexity + L-Lipschitzness + µ-sharpness, Spectral Descent (SD) and its truncated variant (TSD) achieve **global linear O(log(1/ε))** convergence (Theorems 4.1, 4.2), where the contraction rate is explicitly rank-dependent; truncation is shown to both cut compute and relax the worst-case conditioning threshold to κ > 1−1/√r̄. Adding decoupled weight decay yields RSD-WD/RTSD-WD, proved to converge at **O(1/√T)** via a Frank-Wolfe equivalence (Theorems 4.3, 4.4). Applied to robust low-rank matrix recovery via an LAD objective, Theorem 5.1 gives a recovery guarantee under mixed sparse+dense noise and shows the minimal truncation rank s=1 suffices. Empirically, on two-layer ReLU nets, SD/Muon converge faster than GD and Adam on MNIST (W1∈R^{512×784}, 150 samples) and CIFAR-10 (W1∈R^{512×3072}, 200 samples), and RTSD-WD recovers a rank-3, 50×50 matrix under 6% sparse outliers.

## 核心贡献

- 首次为 Muon 类优化器在非光滑条件下提供收敛保证（Table 1）：先前工作（Li & Hong 2025、Shen et al. 2025、Ma et al. 2026）均依赖光滑性假设。
- 在凸 + Lipschitz + sharpness 假设下证明 SD（Theorem 4.1）与 TSD（Theorem 4.2）的全局线性收敛，收缩率显式依赖次梯度秩，并指出截断不仅降低计算开销还放松了最坏情况条件数要求（κ > 1−1/√r̄）。
- 揭示带解耦权重衰减的谱更新与 Frank-Wolfe（条件次梯度）方法的结构等价，借此证明 RSD-WD/RTSD-WD 的 O(1/√T) 次线性收敛（Theorem 4.3/4.4），并提出基于邻域的次梯度选择机制作为非光滑场景下"动量"的替代。
- 将框架实例化到鲁棒低秩矩阵恢复（LAD 形式，混合稀疏+稠密噪声），给出 Theorem 5.1 的恢复保证，并证明最小截断秩 s=1 即可避免完整 SVD 的 O(n³) 瓶颈。

## Key Contributions

- **First non-smooth Muon-type convergence (Table 1):** Prior guarantees (Li & Hong 2025; Shen et al. 2025; Ma et al. 2026) all assume smoothness; this work requires only convexity (Asn 4.1), L-Lipschitz continuity (Asn 4.2), and µ-sharpness (Asn 4.3) — the latter is automatically satisfied by polyhedral convex functions (L1, SVM hinge), nuclear norm, and LP.
- **Global linear convergence of SD/TSD (Theorems 4.1, 4.2):** Convergence rate O(log(1/ε)); SD aligns with the spectral-norm steepest-descent geometry, TSD with the Ky-Fan-s-norm steepest-descent geometry; contraction rate explicitly governed by subgradient rank; truncation both cuts SVD cost and relaxes the worst-case conditioning threshold to κ > 1 − 1/√r̄ (vs κ > 1 − 1/r̄ for full rank).
- **Regularized variants via Frank-Wolfe (Theorems 4.3, 4.4):** RSD-WD and RTSD-WD reach O(1/√T) sublinear convergence; the analysis introduces a neighborhood-based subgradient selection argmin_{G∈T(X,ε)} ‖G‖* + λ⟨X,G⟩ (Eq. 6/7) that performs "spatial smoothing" to stabilize non-smooth trajectories without momentum.
- **Robust low-rank matrix recovery (Theorem 5.1):** LAD objective f(X) = (1/m)‖A(X)−b‖_1; under an ℓ1/ℓ2-RIP and a restricted (S,τ)-outlier bound condition, RTSD-WD recovers X* (rank r*) with sublinear rate; minimal truncation s=1 avoids the O(n³) full-SVD bottleneck.

## Methodology

**Optimizers analyzed.** Muon update (Eq. 2): X^{t+1} = X^t − η_t msgn(B^t), B^t = µB^{t-1}+G^t, G^t∈∂f(X^t), msgn(G)=U_r V_r^T (compact SVD). The paper analyzes the momentum-free (µ=0) case, giving Spectral Descent (SD, Eq. 4): X^{t+1}=X^t − η_t msgn(G^t). Truncated SD (TSD, Eq. 5) replaces msgn with the top-s truncated sign T_msgn^s(G), equivalent to the Fanion-s algorithm [Kravatskiy et al. 2025]. With decoupled weight decay (MuonW, Eq. 3), the regularized variants RSD-WD (Eq. 6) and RTSD-WD (Eq. 7) select G̃^t from an ε-neighborhood T(X^t,ε_t)=conv(∪_{u∈N(X,ε)} ∂f(u)) to perform spatial smoothing.

**Assumptions.** Convexity (Asn 4.1), L-Lipschitz continuity (Asn 4.2, |f(X)−f(Y)|≤L‖X−Y‖_F), and µ-sharpness (Asn 4.3, f(X)−f* ≥ µ·dist(X,X*)).

**Key technical tools.** SD = steepest descent w.r.t. the spectral (operator) norm ‖·‖_2; TSD = steepest descent w.r.t. the Ky Fan s-norm ‖X‖_{(s)}=Σ_{i≤s}σ_i(X). The regularized variants reduce to a Conditional Subgradient / Frank-Wolfe Linear Minimization Oracle over nuclear-norm / Ky-Fan-s-norm balls, enabling Jaggi-style (2013) sublinear analysis. The neighborhood subgradient selection provides the spatial smoothing that momentum usually supplies.

**Experiments.**
- *ReLU networks (Fig 1):* two-layer FC ReLU on 150 MNIST samples (W1∈R^{512×784}, W2∈R^{10×512}) and 200 CIFAR-10 samples (W1∈R^{512×3072}); comparing Muon, SD, GD, Adam under both constant and geometric-decay step sizes; lr grid-searched per optimizer; averaged over 5 seeds.
- *Robust low-rank recovery (Figs 2–3):* n1=n2=50, rank r*=3, m=10·n·r* Gaussian measurements; observations b=A(X*)+e1+e2 with sparse outliers e1 (6% corrupted, ~N(0,100)) and/or dense noise e2~N(0,1); RTSD-WD with s=1, λ=1/‖X*‖_*, η_t=λ/(t+3), tolerance ε_t=(0.08/λ)·√(m/(π(t+3))).
- *Linear programming (Fig 4):* LAD objective min Σ|y_i−⟨X_i,W⟩|, N=2000 samples, n1=n2=10; SD and TSD with s∈{1,3}.
- Hardware: single NVIDIA A100 40GB.

## Results & Highlights

- **Theoretical rate summary (Table 1):** this work's non-smooth SD and TSD achieve **O(log(1/ε))** (linear) without weight decay — matching the linear rate of smooth Muon analyses (Shen 2025; Ma 2026) but *without* requiring smoothness, and matching Ma et al. (2026)'s matrix-factorization result in a far broader (general convex) setting.
- **Conditioning relaxation from truncation (Sec 4.1, Theorem 4.2):** for fixed rank upper bound r̄, TSD requires only **κ > 1 − 1/√r̄**, whereas full-rank SD requires the stricter **κ > 1 − 1/r̄** — truncation both reduces compute and weakens the worst-case condition-number requirement.
- **Regularized variants:** RSD-WD and RTSD-WD (Theorems 4.3, 4.4) achieve **O(1/√T)** sublinear convergence under the same structural assumptions, via the Frank-Wolfe connection.
- **Robust low-rank matrix recovery (Theorem 5.1):** under an ℓ1/ℓ2-RIP of the sensing operator and a restricted (S,τ_r)-outlier bound, RTSD-WD with **truncation s=1** recovers X* with sublinear rate provided the outlier fraction p < ½[1−...] (Sec 5); Gaussian operators provably satisfy the required outlier bound when p is an absolute constant.
- **Empirical — ReLU networks (Fig 1):** on MNIST (150 samples) and CIFAR-10 (200 samples), Muon and SD reach near-zero loss in **far fewer iterations** than GD and Adam, and are notably **robust to the learning-rate schedule** (constant vs geometric-decay).
- **Empirical — matrix recovery (Figs 2–3):** RTSD-WD shows rapid initial descent in the first **~3,000 iterations** across noiseless, 6%-sparse-outlier, and mixed-noise regimes; in the mixed case it converges to a stable neighborhood of X* whose floor is set by the dense-noise level ξ. Varying s∈{1,2,3} (Fig 3) all show the **O(1/√T)** sublinear curve, with **s=1** giving marginally lower relative error and a large compute saving.

## Critical Assessment

- **Momentum is dropped from the theory:** the analysis covers only the µ=0 case (SD/TSD), whereas real Muon (µ>0) is what is deployed in LLM training — so the paper's headline link to "Muon" is partial; the momentum-bearing Muon in non-smooth settings remains unanalyzed.
- **Sharpness (Asn 4.3) is required for linear convergence** — but sharpness fails for many smooth-ish deep-learning objectives; the experiments themselves are restricted to genuinely non-smooth polyhedral/LAD/ReLU settings, so the relevance to standard LLM pretraining (smooth cross-entropy) is not demonstrated.
- **Linear-convergence step size depends on dist(X^{(0)}, X*)** (Remark 4.3), which is unknown a priori in practice — the experiments sidestep this by grid-searching η.
- **Recovery Theorem 5.1 needs both an ℓ1/ℓ2-RIP and a restricted outlier bound**, and the practical implementation replaces the subdifferential set T with a surrogate H (acknowledged as a heuristic "inspired by the theory"), so the theorem's guarantees do not strictly cover the implemented algorithm.
- **No LLM-scale experiments** — the largest empirical model is a two-layer FC ReLU net (W1∈R^{512×3072}); the connection to Muon's claimed LLM gains is by citation only.

## Strengths

- First rigorous non-smooth convergence theory for any Muon-type optimizer, with explicit rank-dependent rates.
- The SD-as-spectral-steepest-descent / TSD-as-Ky-Fan-s-steepest-descent unification is clean and explains *why* truncation helps.
- The Frank-Wolfe equivalence for weight-decayed variants is a genuinely useful structural insight, and the neighborhood subgradient mechanism offers a momentum-free stabilization tool.
- The s=1 result for low-rank recovery has real practical value (avoids full SVD).

## Limitations

- Theory omits momentum (µ=0 only), so real Muon is not covered.
- Linear convergence needs µ-sharpness, which excludes many smooth objectives.
- Initial step size depends on the unknown dist(X^{(0)}, X*).
- No experiments at LLM scale; largest empirical net is a small FC ReLU.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The theory drops momentum (µ=0); can the analysis be extended to µ>0 Muon in the non-smooth setting, given that real LLM-scale Muon always uses momentum?
- Linear convergence requires µ-sharpness (Asn 4.3) — does standard LLM cross-entropy pretraining satisfy any approximate sharpness, or is the theory only applicable to genuinely non-smooth subproblems (e.g., L1, hinge, ReLU)?
- Theorem 5.1 needs both an ℓ1/ℓ2-RIP and a restricted outlier bound, and the implementation uses a surrogate set H — does the proven guarantee actually cover the implemented algorithm, or only an idealized variant?
- The paper cites Shen et al. (2025) and Ma et al. (2026) for smooth-Muon linear rates — could the rank-dependent contraction here be combined with their condition-number-independent rate to handle intermediate (weakly smooth) regimes?
- Since DeepSeek-V4 reportedly uses Muon for stability, can the non-smooth convergence guarantees here be instantiated on a concrete Muon-trained LLM module to predict wall-clock savings?
