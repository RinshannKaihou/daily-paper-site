> **一句话总结：** Dense2MoE 提出了一种统一剪枝与 MoE 升级的框架（LF-UC），通过剪除冗余层的注意力模块、将其 MLP 转化为异构专家，在仅用 225B token 持续预训练的条件下，实现了端侧 LLM 推理延迟与精度的帕累托最优。

> **TL;DR:** Dense2MoE unifies structured layer pruning with MoE upcycling via Layer-Fusion UpCycling (LF-UC), pruning bandwidth-heavy attention modules from redundant layers while repurposing their MLPs as heterogeneous experts, advancing the Pareto frontier for on-device LLM inference with only 225B tokens of continual pre-training (<1% of training-from-scratch cost).

## 核心贡献

- **统一剪枝与升级框架：** 提出 Dense2MoE，将结构化层剪枝与 MoE 升级无缝结合，通过 Layer-Fusion UpCycling (LF-UC) 技术同时优化推理延迟和模型精度。
- **异构专家初始化：** 利用不同层的预训练 MLP 作为 MoE 专家，天然具备异构性，避免了同质初始化导致的 Winner-Takes-All 路由崩塌问题。
- **硬件感知的深度选择：** 基于 Roofline 理论和 MnasNet 风格的奖励指标，系统性地确定最优保留层数，在目标硬件上实现精度-延迟最优平衡。
- **跨架构验证：** 在 Qwen2.5（0.5B/1.5B）和 LLaMA2（7B）上验证了方法的通用性和可扩展性。

## Key Contributions

- **Unified pruning-upcycling framework:** Dense2MoE seamlessly integrates structured layer pruning with MoE upcycling through LF-UC, enabling joint optimization of inference latency and model accuracy for resource-constrained on-device deployments.
- **Heterogeneous expert initialization:** By repurposing MLPs from different layers as MoE experts, the framework naturally produces heterogeneous experts that mitigate Winner-Takes-All routing collapse—unlike homogeneous upcycling that duplicates a single MLP.
- **Hardware-aware depth selection:** A reward metric inspired by MnasNet, grounded in Roofline theory, systematically identifies the optimal retained layer depth for the target hardware platform.
- **Cross-architecture scalability:** Validated across Qwen2.5 (0.5B, 1.5B) and LLaMA2 (7B), demonstrating broad applicability across model scales and architectural families.

## Methodology

Dense2MoE follows a three-stage pipeline:

1. **Inter-layer Similarity Analysis:** Identifies structurally redundant adjacent layers using a dual-constraint metric: (a) cosine similarity of both decoder outputs and MLP inputs (threshold δ), and (b) feature norm matching (threshold ε) to ensure numerical stability during expert routing. A greedy algorithm partitions layers into retained (L_keep) and pruned (L_prune) sets.

2. **Layer-Fusion UpCycling (LF-UC):** For a retained base layer l* and n* redundant layers, the framework completely prunes redundant attention modules (reducing memory bandwidth) while preserving their MLPs. These MLPs are aggregated into the base block as distinct MoE experts. A dynamic token-level router (Top-1) selectively dispatches tokens to experts or bypasses them via residual paths. An expert pool of N = K + n* × M experts is constructed (K base expert copies + M copies per redundant layer MLP).

3. **Continual Pre-Training:** A lightweight 225B-token continual pre-training phase optimizes the routing mechanism, aligns fused experts, and recovers model capabilities. This is <1% of the compute required to train an MoE from scratch.

The optimal pruning depth is determined via a hardware-aware reward: Reward_i = S_i × (LaT_i / LaT_base)^w, balancing benchmark scores against Roofline-derived theoretical latency on the NVIDIA Jetson Thor-U SoC.

## Results & Highlights

- **Qwen2.5-0.5B (primary):** Dense2MoE (L=19, 6 experts) achieves avg score **47.31** vs. dense baseline **37.53** (+9.78 pp) with latency **174.95ms** vs. **195.87ms** (10.6% faster), using only 0.42B active params.
- **Outperforms all baselines:** UIDL (31.34), LLM-Pruner (32.58), LLM-Streamline (26.79), ToMoE (21.53), LLaMA-MoE (22.25) on average scores—all with lower or comparable latency.
- **Qwen2.5-1.5B:** +3.04 pp over dense baseline (58.23 vs. 55.19 avg).
- **LLaMA2-7B:** +3.54 pp over dense baseline (31.38 vs. 27.84 avg).
- **Routing balance:** LF-UC reduces mean dominant-expert load from 48.0% to 45.2% and layers exceeding 50% threshold from 11 to 9 vs. homogeneous upcycling.
- **Expert scaling:** Performance saturates beyond N=6 experts; N=6 chosen as hardware-aware default (3.32 GB BF16 total memory vs. 5.31 GB for N=10).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core idea of unifying pruning with upcycling via inter-layer MLP recycling is genuinely novel. The LF-UC mechanism is well-motivated by Roofline theory—pruning attention (memory-bound bottleneck) while preserving MLPs (knowledge repositories) is a principled design choice.
- **Rigor:** The dual-constraint similarity metric (cosine similarity + norm matching) is a meaningful improvement over prior work that uses only directional similarity. The hardware-aware reward metric provides a principled mechanism for depth selection. However, the threshold values (δ, ε, w) are empirically determined, and sensitivity analysis is limited.
- **Reproducibility:** The 225B-token training dataset is described in terms of domain composition but specific data sources are only partially listed. The algorithm is detailed (Algorithm 1 in Appendix), and evaluation uses standard benchmarks. Models are based on publicly available Qwen2.5 and LLaMA2.
- **Significance:** Achieving +9.78 pp over the dense baseline while being faster is a strong result. However, the comparison with ToMoE and LLaMA-MoE is somewhat unfair—these methods are not specifically designed for layer-level pruning, and their severe degradation on coding tasks (HEval: 6.82, MBPP: 6.70 for ToMoE) suggests potential implementation or training issues in baselines.
- **Red flags:** The primary results are on Qwen2.5-0.5B, a relatively small model. Scaling to 7B shows smaller relative gains. The "theoretical latency" used for Pareto analysis may not fully reflect real-world inference engine overhead. The continual pre-training budget (225B tokens) is described as "modest" but is still non-trivial.

## Strengths

- Principled integration of pruning and upcycling that avoids the destructive nature of pure pruning.
- Heterogeneous expert initialization is a simple but effective solution to routing collapse.
- Hardware-aware depth selection grounded in Roofline theory provides practical deployment guidance.
- Strong empirical gains across multiple benchmarks and model families.
- Thorough ablation studies on expert count, similarity constraints, and training dynamics.

## Limitations

- Primary validation on a small model (0.5B); larger models show more modest relative improvements.
- Theoretical latency modeling may not capture real-world kernel optimization overhead.
- 225B-token continual pre-training is still a significant computational cost, though claimed to be <1% of from-scratch training.
- Top-1 routing limits the expressive power of the MoE architecture; higher Top-k values are not explored.
- The method assumes inter-layer redundancy exists, which may not hold for all architectures or well-optimized models.

## Relevance

本文提出的统一剪枝-MoE升级框架直接解决了端侧大模型部署的精度-效率权衡问题。LF-UC 利用层间冗余进行异构专家初始化的思路，对模型压缩、高效推理和 MoE 架构设计的研究都具有参考价值，尤其适合关注自动驾驶和边缘 AI 部署场景的工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does Dense2MoE perform with Top-k > 1 routing, and would multi-expert activation improve the capacity-efficiency trade-off?
2. Can the LF-UC approach be combined with quantization (e.g., INT4/INT8) for further memory reduction on edge devices?
3. How sensitive is the inter-layer similarity analysis to the choice of calibration dataset, and does it generalize across domains?
4. What happens when applying Dense2MoE to models already designed with efficiency in mind (e.g., MoE-to-MoE conversion)?
5. How do the theoretical Roofline latency predictions compare with actual measured latency once optimized MoE kernels are available?
