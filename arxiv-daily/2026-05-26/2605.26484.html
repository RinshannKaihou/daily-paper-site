Extra-Merge: Tracing the Rank-1 Subspace of Model Merging in Language Model Pre-Training

ICML 2026 Zhou, Wang, Zhang et al. 124M → 12B params
Key Finding
Merged LLM checkpoints collapse onto a stable rank-1 subspace — extrapolating along it yields free performance gains.
Key Metric
+0.59%
Why This Matters
A zero-cost checkpoint trick any pre-training team can slot in — no extra compute, no code overhaul.
Critical Edge
Gains are modest, only 4 benchmarks tested, and limited to late-stage training with decaying LR.

Methodology

1
Pairwise Connectivity Analysis Linear interpolation between adjacent raw checkpoints reveals U-shaped convex basins (midpoint loss lower than endpoints), proving oscillation across valley walls. Merged checkpoints show strictly monotonic descent instead.
2
Rank-1 Subspace Identification Collect K consecutive merged checkpoints, form centered parameter matrix, run PCA. Across all scales, PC1 explains >94% variance with strictly monotonic projected coordinates — the trajectory is effectively one-dimensional.
3
Extra-Merge Algorithm PCA on K=4 latest merged checkpoints → extract principal direction u₁ → orient via sign alignment → adaptive greedy line search with stride δ = 0.1·|zt − zt−1| → iterate until loss stops improving.
4
River-Valley Theory Formal proofs (Theorems 5.3 & 5.4): averaging suppresses orthogonal "mountain" deviation by 1/N; PCA recovers true descent direction when drift-to-noise ratio ρ > 1. SNR scales as ρ ∝ N·(Kτ)².

Results & Highlights

Result Detail
Validation loss Sustained loss reduction across all GPT-2 and LLaMA scales throughout training; PMA gains diminish as LR decays.
Pythia-12B zero-shot Extra-Merge averages 57.47% vs. 56.88% raw (+0.59%) and 57.07% best PMA (+0.40%) on ARC-Challenge, ARC-Easy, HellaSwag, PIQA.
Muon optimizer Remains effective under orthogonal updates — confirms optimizer-agnostic universality.
Rank-1 persistence EVR > 0.8 persists over K=16 windows when merging window n > 4.
Compute cost Negligible — PCA on a few checkpoint vectors + line search. No gradients needed.

Strengths

  • Elegant geometric insight: merging collapses chaotic trajectories onto a clean 1D manifold.
  • Training-free: no gradient computation, negligible overhead.
  • Strong theoretical grounding with explicit SNR bounds and hyperparameter guidance.
  • Validated across GPT-2, LLaMA, Pythia at 124M–12B scale, and AdamW + Muon optimizers.
  • Honest scope framing — explicitly limited to late-stage pre-training.

Limitations

  • Restricted to late-stage pre-training with decaying LR; early-stage and fine-tuning regimes unexplored.
  • Only 4 commonsense benchmarks evaluated — no generation quality, code, or math.
  • River-valley assumption (1 flat + d−1 sharp directions) may not hold universally.
  • Fixed α = 0.1 step-size multiplier; sensitivity analysis is thin.
  • Extrapolation failure modes and distance bounds are not characterized.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the rank-1 phenomenon hold during fine-tuning (SFT, RLHF) or only during pre-training with decaying LR?
  2. How does the subspace direction reorient under distribution shifts — could this enable geometric domain adaptation?
  3. Can subspace-aware optimizers actively align updates with the latent manifold to accelerate convergence from the start?
  4. What are the theoretical bounds on extrapolation distance before the river-valley approximation breaks down?
  5. Does the rank-1 structure persist in multi-modal training (vision-language) or diffusion model training?
