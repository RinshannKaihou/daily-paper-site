MONA: Muon Optimizer with Nesterov Acceleration for Scalable Language Model Training

Key Finding

Injecting Nesterov-type curvature acceleration before Muon's orthogonalization escapes sharp minima, beating both Muon and AdamW across 1B–68B MoE scales.

Key Metric

Why This Matters
A minimal Muon tweak that beats Muon & AdamW at 68B scale with negligible overhead — a drop-in production upgrade.
Critical Edge
Only tested on MoE, not dense transformers; GPQA and MATH regress vs. AdamW/Muon at the 700B-token checkpoint.

Methodology

MONA inserts curvature-aware acceleration into Muon's pipeline before orthogonalization. Applying it after would destroy the orthogonal structure. The gradient difference Gk − Gk−1 ≈ Hk(θk − θk−1) implicitly encodes Hessian information, steering updates away from sharp minima.

1. Gk = ∇W ℓ(Wk, ζk) 2. Dk = Gk − Gk−1 3. Ak = βa Ak−1 + (1 − βa) Dk 4. G̃k = Gk + α Ak 5. Mk = μ Mk−1 + G̃k 6. Ok = NewtonSchulz(Mk, T) 7. Wk+1 = Wk − η(γ Ok + λ Wk)

Two new hyperparameters: βa (acceleration EMA decay, 0.975–0.99) and α = −1/(2(1 − βa)) (acceleration coefficient, empirically consistent across scales). Vector-valued params fall back to AdamW. MONA-Lite uses BF16 + streaming to cut extra memory ~75%.

Strengths

  • Clean, minimal modification to Muon — preserves all benefits while adding curvature awareness
  • Consistent gains across three model scales (1B / 6B / 68B) with no architecture-specific tuning
  • MONA-Lite cuts memory overhead ~75% with no quality loss
  • Non-convex convergence proof at O(K−1/2); Proposition 1 gives geometric intuition for sharp-minimum escape
  • Negligible wall-clock overhead (~1% inner step, identical end-to-end)

Limitations

  • Evaluated exclusively on MoE — dense transformer generalization unverified
  • 68B model has only 3B active parameters; effective compute is much smaller than 68B dense
  • Two new hyperparameters (βa, α); empirical rule not theoretically motivated
  • GPQA and MATH regress vs. AdamW/Muon at the 700B-token checkpoint
  • Convergence rate O(K−1/2) matches SGD/AdamW — gains are in solution quality, not asymptotic speed
  • Extra memory: ~1 gradient buffer even with MONA-Lite

Open Questions

  • How does MONA perform on dense transformers (e.g., LLaMA-style) vs. MoE?
  • Is the α = −1/(2(1 − βa)) rule robust across LR schedules, batch sizes, and architectures?
  • Can the acceleration term compose with Muon variants like AdaMuon or ROOT?
  • What does the acceleration buffer's magnitude and direction look like during early vs. late training?
  • How does MONA interact with ZeRO or tensor parallelism at trillion-parameter scale?
