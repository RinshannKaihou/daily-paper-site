Beyond a Single Direction: Chain-of-Thought Disrupts Simple Steering of Refusal

Kia-Jüng Yang, Dominik Meier, Jiachen Zhao, Terry Ruas, Bela Gipp
Key Finding
Chain-of-thought jointly encodes refusal with activations—single-direction steering is insufficient in reasoning models.
Key Metric
94%
compliance with steering + CoT regeneration (vs. 39% steering alone)
Why This Matters
Activation-level safeguards alone cannot protect reasoning models; CoT is both a defense amplifier and attack surface.
Critical Edge
Single 8B distilled model only. No causal CoT faithfulness verification. Generalization to other LRMs is untested.
Methodology

Four Conditions Isolating Dual-Signal Refusal

Controlled experiments on DeepSeek-R1-Distill-LLaMA-8B using 100 harmful instructions from JAILBREAKBENCH (0% baseline compliance). Activation steering via difference-in-means refusal direction at template token positions (EOI/EOT). Refusal classified by Llama-3.2-8B-Guard (86% accuracy). Greedy decoding throughout.

1. Fixed CoT + Steering Apply steering vectors; keep original CoT; regenerate only the final answer.
2. No CoT + Steering Suppress CoT entirely; apply EOI steering; regenerate only the final answer.
3. Regenerated CoT + Steering Apply EOI steering while model regenerates both CoT and final answer freely.
4. Swapped CoT, No Steering Use compliant CoT from cond. 3 but remove steering—isolates CoT’s independent effect.

Strengths

What Works

  • Elegant experimental design cleanly isolating dual-signal refusal
  • Compelling quantitative progression: 0% → 39% → 70% → 94% → 48%
  • Directly challenges the single-refusal-direction paradigm for standard LLMs
  • Practical implications: CoT as both defense amplifier and attack surface
  • Honest about limitations—acknowledges lack of faithfulness verification
Limitations

Caveats & Gaps

  • Single model: DeepSeek-R1-Distill-LLaMA-8B only
  • No causal CoT faithfulness verification—only correlational
  • 8B distilled model may not represent larger or non-distilled LRMs
  • 86% classifier accuracy introduces measurement noise
  • Greedy decoding only; sampling may yield different rates
  • Suggests CoT monitoring as defense but does not implement or test it
Open Questions

For Further Exploration

  1. Does the dual-signal mechanism generalize to larger models (DeepSeek-R1-671B, o3) or non-distilled models?
  2. Can CoT be monitored in real-time to intercept compliance reconstruction before the final answer?
  3. What is the causal link between specific CoT reasoning patterns (ethical justifications, harm assessments) and refusal?
  4. How does joint refusal encoding emerge during training—RL-based reasoning, distillation, or both?
  5. Could adversarial prompt-level attacks on CoT combine with activation steering for even higher compliance?
