① Key Finding

"INT8" was a lie, and INT8 Tensor Cores were never used

Production W8A8 INT8 for Ideogram 4.0 on consumer Ampere quantizes→immediately→dequantizes back to bf16, so the integer tensor cores sit idle. That is why "INT8" can be slower than FP8 or NF4 on cards that have fast native INT8.

② Key Metric

From slowest variant to fastest on a single RTX 3090

Bit-exact vs torch._int_mm, cosine 1.0, no NaNs. 768px drops 107.06s → 97.79s (1.095×). At 1024px a single 24 GB RTX 3090 generates 156.49s/image — beating 2-card FP8 by ~16s and 1-card NF4 by ~8s.

156.49s 1024px, 1×RTX 3090
③ Why This Matters

An audit checklist for any "W8A8" deployment

Any claim that INT8 is slower than FP8/NF4 on a card with native INT8 tensor cores is now suspect. The fix is one Triton kernel: per-token dynamic activation scale + per-channel weight scale + bias, all applied off the int32 accumulator in the epilogue — one launch, no dequant round trip.

④ Critical Edge

Consumer-Ampere only. A100/B200 actively lose.

The same kernel is 1.38× slower on A100 and 3.49× slower on B200 — their native bf16 is already too fast. n=4 single-run latency. OCR/NED not re-measured for the fused build. Head_dim=256 blocks SageAttention. Use this win only on RTX 30-series.

Recipe · what they actually ran

9.3B flow-matching DiT, five GEMM shapes total

  • Model. Ideogram 4.0 — 34 layers, 18 heads × dim 256, hidden 4608, intermediate 12288, two CFG branches × 211 linears.
  • Five (N, K) shapes total — qkv / attn-out / ffn-up / ffn-down / llm-proj. That is the whole kernel's surface area.
  • Quant recipe inherited from Transformer Lab's prior study: per-channel int8 weights, per-token dynamic int8 acts, SmoothQuant α = 0.5.
  • GPU-health gate. a throttled 3090 measures ~8.1 TFLOPS bf16 vs ~65.5 TFLOPS on a healthy card — every number gated behind that check.
  • Cost. ~3 RTX 3090-hours total.
  • Release. transformerlab/ideogram-4-int8-fused — gated, non-commercial, research-only.
Limitations · read before citing

The paper is honest; you should be too

  • n = 4 single-run latency. No run-to-run variance, no significance test.
  • OCR / NED — the most quantization-fragile metric for an Ideogram model — is not re-measured for the fused build at any resolution.
  • 1024px FP8 baseline is two-GPU; the fused measurement is one-GPU. Confound is flagged, not retired.
  • Unfused baseline is torch._int_mm + dequant — a tuned cuBLASLt / CUTLASS INT8 GEMM is not reported. "Best in class" is left open.
  • Kernel is hand-tuned to Ideogram 4.0's five shapes — portability to other DiTs is unproven.
Audit checklist · if you ship W8A8 on a DiT

Before you conclude "INT8 is slower than FP8 on this card"

1. Confirm the GEMM is actually int8

Profile the linear path. If you see aten::linear with a bf16 input after a dequantize op, you are running fake-quant. The integer tensor cores are not in the call graph. This is the entire point of the paper.

2. Check the shape mix

If your DiT's linear GEMMs are < ~15% of forward (the Ideogram 4.0 number), the end-to-end ceiling from even a 4× per-GEMM win is ~10–15%. Plan budget around Amdahl, not around per-op numbers.
