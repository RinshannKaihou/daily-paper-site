> **一句话总结：** C2-Faith 基于 PRM800K 通过受控扰动（已知位置的单步因果替换 + 按比例删除中间步骤）将 CoT 忠实性拆为因果性与覆盖度两维，系统评测 GPT-4.1、DeepSeek-V3.1、o4-mini 三个裁判后发现：检测率虽高但精确归因困难（o4-mini 定位最准，exact match 仅 68.0%），且所有裁判系统性高估覆盖度（70% 删除时均分仍约 3.0）。

> **TL;DR:** C2-Faith is a diagnostic benchmark built from PRM800K that probes whether LLM judges can assess the *process* faithfulness of chain-of-thought reasoning, decomposing it into causality (does each step logically follow prior context) and coverage (are essential intermediate inferences present). It uses controlled perturbations with known error positions (single-step acausal replacement, middle 30%–90% of chains) and known deletion rates (d ∈ {0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7}). Across 450 perfect chains (median length 13) it evaluates three frontier judges (GPT-4.1, DeepSeek-V3.1, o4-mini) on binary detection, step localization, and coverage scoring. DeepSeek-V3.1 has the highest raw detection rate (94.7%) but also a 29.6% false-positive rate, while o4-mini is the recommended overall judge (best localization exact-match 68.0% vs. GPT-4.1 57.6% and DeepSeek 55.8%) and all judges systematically overestimate coverage (mean ≈3.0 even when 70% of middle steps are deleted).

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **C2-Faith** 基准，将 CoT 忠实性显式分解为**因果性（causality）**与**覆盖度（coverage）**两个互补维度：前者衡量每一步是否逻辑上承接上文，后者衡量关键中间推理是否齐全。
- 基于 PRM800K 设计**受控扰动方案**：在完美链（450 条，长度中位数 13）的中段（30%–90% 位置）用 LLM 生成的逻辑不一致变体替换单步以注入已知因果错误；并按 d ∈ {0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7} 删除中段步骤，得到 1,800 条已知覆盖度缺陷样本，从而获得与参考标签的直接对照。
- 定义三类递进难度任务：**Exp 1 二元因果检测**、**Exp 2 因果步骤定位**、**Exp 3 0–4 覆盖度打分**，并在三个前沿裁判（GPT-4.1、DeepSeek-V3.1、o4-mini）上系统评测。
- 给出三项核心实证结论：裁判排名随任务反转；定位远难于检测（存在"检测—归因鸿沟"）；所有裁判系统性高估覆盖度。综合推荐 **o4-mini** 为最均衡的过程级裁判。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **C2-Faith**, the first benchmark to separate CoT faithfulness into two orthogonal dimensions — **causality** (logical entailment from prior context) and **coverage** (presence of essential intermediate inferences) — with ground-truth labels on both axes.
- Builds controlled perturbations over PRM800K: single-step acausal replacement at a known middle-region index, plus uniform deletion of middle-region steps at four rates (d ∈ {0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7}), yielding 1,800 coverage-degraded examples.
- Defines a three-experiment protocol of increasing difficulty: binary causal detection (Exp 1), causal step localization (Exp 2), and 0–4 coverage scoring (Exp 3, following Emmons et al. 2025's rubric).
- Empirically shows judge rankings are task-dependent (DeepSeek-V3.1 leads raw detection but o4-mini leads localization and coverage correlation), and that all three judges exhibit a detection–localization gap and systematic coverage score inflation.
- Recommends **o4-mini** as the most balanced faithfulness judge and frames net discrimination (Detect − FPR) as a more reliable summary metric than raw detection rate.

## Methodology

- **Source dataset:** PRM800K (Lightman et al., 2023) — 800,000 step-level human labels on model-generated solutions to the MATH dataset, with per-step labels +1 (correct/advancing), 0 (neutral), −1 (incorrect).
- **Chain filtering:** Authors keep only "perfect" chains with |S| ≥ 5 and every step labeled +1, yielding **450 chains** with median length 13 (range 8–30). This isolates injected violations from pre-existing errors.
- **Middle region:** Perturbations are restricted to indices I = {i | ⌈0.3N⌉ ≤ i ≤ ⌊0.9N⌋}, so judges cannot solve tasks by only inspecting the first/last step.
- **Causality perturbation:** For each perfect chain, sample one index j uniformly from I and replace step s_j with an acausal variant s̃_j = NEGATE(s_{j−1}, s_j), an LLM-generated rewrite (temperature 0.7) that breaks logical entailment (flipped comparators/operators, swapped quantities, unwarranted assumptions, misapplied rules) while preserving surface mathematical plausibility.
- **Coverage perturbation:** For each chain and each deletion level d ∈ {0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7}, uniformly sample ⌈d|I|⌉ middle indices and remove them, preserving order of remaining steps — producing 1,800 degraded examples.
- **Judges:** GPT-4.1, DeepSeek-V3.1, o4-mini, all accessed via Azure OpenAI API with structured JSON output (`client.beta.chat.completions.parse()`); judge inferences use default API parameters.
- **Tasks:** (1) Exp 1 binary causal detection — judge sees preceding context + target step, outputs 0/1; ground truth always 0 (unfaithful), FPR measured by re-running on unperturbed steps. (2) Exp 2 localization — judge sees full perturbed CoT, outputs suspected index ĵ or −1 (no detection); metrics include exact match, detection rate, MAE, within-k accuracy. (3) Coverage scoring — judge outputs 0–4 score; reference scores are produced by GPT-4.1 given the original complete CoT and removed steps.
- **Statistics:** 95% bootstrap CIs (1,000 resamples); McNemar's test with Bonferroni correction for pairwise judge comparisons; Spearman ρ for coverage rank alignment.

## Results & Highlights

- **DeepSeek-V3.1 has highest raw causal detection (94.7%) but a 29.6% false-positive rate**, vs. GPT-4.1 82.7% detect / 11.4% FPR and o4-mini 92.0% detect / 10.4% FPR (Table 1). After accounting for FPR, o4-mini has the best net discrimination (Detect−FPR = 81.6%) and DeepSeek drops to last (65.1%).
- **Step localization (Exp 2) is much harder than detection**: exact-match accuracy is only 68.0% for o4-mini (best), 57.6% for GPT-4.1, and 55.8% for DeepSeek-V3.1, even though detection rates are 88.4–94.2% — a 26–33 percentage-point detection–localization gap (Table 2).
- **Localization FPR is severe**: on unperturbed full chains, GPT-4.1 flags 54.9%, DeepSeek 49.3%, o4-mini 40.4% as containing an error, meaning the headline 88–94% detection rates substantially overstate real discriminative power.
- **All judges systematically overestimate coverage**: even at 70% deletion (70% of middle steps removed), mean coverage scores stay around 3.0 (GPT-4.1 2.95, DeepSeek 3.29, o4-mini 2.91), with the ground-truth mean being 2.17 (Table 3). Score inflation (fraction ≥ 3) ranges from 57.1%–74.6% at 70% deletion.
- **DeepSeek-V3.1 shows near-zero coverage correlation at low deletion**: ρ = −0.006 at 10% deletion and 0.048 at 30% (n.s.), vs. o4-mini ρ = 0.151 / 0.200 — a "ceiling collapse" where 95.1% of its 10%-deletion scores are the maximum value 4.
- **Systematic early-prediction bias**: all models predict the unfaithful step earlier than it occurs (mean signed error GPT-4.1 −0.82, DeepSeek −0.44, o4-mini −1.20 steps), strongest for the otherwise-best o4-mini.
- **Best single detectability predictor (o4-mini ablation)** is math-symbol density: correctly flagged steps contain 0.235 symbol density vs. 0.139 for wrongly accepted steps; cross-model failure overlap shows 79.8% of perturbations caught by all three judges, and combining judges could push detection above 98%.

## Critical Assessment

- **Self-referential coverage ground truth:** The coverage reference labels are generated by GPT-4.1, which is *also* one of the three evaluated judges — the paper itself flags this and argues the dominant pattern (DeepSeek's collapse) is unrelated to OpenAI lineage, but the absolute calibration against a non-OpenAI reference is never validated, so GPT-4.1's scores may be structurally advantaged.
- **Limited and weak baseline set:** Only three judges are evaluated, all frontier closed-weight models; there is no comparison to the process reward models (PRMs) or critic models that prior work (Zheng et al. 2024 ProcessBench; Song et al. 2025 PRMBench) actually targets, so the relative standing of LLM-judges vs. specialized PRMs on these perturbations is unknown.
- **Domain narrowness:** The entire benchmark is drawn from MATH-style competition problems in PRM800K; the paper's own Limitations concede generalization to code, commonsense, or scientific CoT is untested, and "logically inconsistent step" is hardest to define in natural-language reasoning (the ablation confirms moderate-rewrite edits are the hardest to detect at 81.1%).
- **Single fixed prompt per task:** The authors use exactly one prompt template per task and explicitly note in Limitations that negative capability claims are therefore sensitive to prompt choice — a prompt-optimization ablation is absent, leaving open how much of each failure mode is prompt-induced rather than capability-induced.
- **Coarse coverage scale:** The adopted 0–4 Emmons et al. rubric saturates at the high end (e.g., 95.1% of DeepSeek's 10%-deletion scores are 4), which the authors acknowledge compresses discriminative signal; a finer 0–9 or 0–100 scale might have revealed graded differences now hidden by ceiling effects.

## Strengths

- Clean two-axis decomposition (causality vs. coverage) with ground-truth labels on both, a genuinely useful reframing for process-level evaluation.
- Controlled-perturbation design with known error index and known deletion fraction enables direct measurement free of natural-data annotation noise.
- Honest reporting of false-positive rates on unperturbed chains, which exposes that raw detection rates overstate real discriminative power — a methodological best practice many judge benchmarks omit.
- The detection–localization gap and the DeepSeek "ceiling collapse" on coverage are concrete, reproducible-sounding failure modes with immediate practitioner implications.
- Rich ablation (structural predictors, edit-type taxonomy, cross-model failure overlap, position effects) gives diagnostic depth beyond headline numbers.

## Limitations

- Restricted to mathematical reasoning (PRM800K/MATH); no code, commonsense, or multi-hop QA.
- Only three closed-weight judges; no open-weight comparator and no comparison to specialized PRMs/critics.
- Ground-truth coverage labels are LLM-generated (GPT-4.1), risking style-bias toward GPT-series judges.
- Single-step causal swaps / uniform deletions do not model multi-step errors, compounding mistakes, or subtle logical gaps.
- Negative claims rest on a single fixed prompt per task; prompt-sensitivity ablation is absent.

## Relevance

直接映射到用户研究兴趣 **「2. LLM Inference Evaluation」**（过程级 CoT 裁判评估与定位）与 **「3. LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」**（CoT 忠实性、可监测性、归因能力）。其"检测易、归因难、覆盖度系统性高估"的发现对构建可靠的 LLM 评测与 reward/process model 训练管线具有直接指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the detection–localization gap persist on non-math domains (code, scientific QA, legal CoT) where step structure is less crisp? The paper's own Limitations flag this as the key untested direction.
- How much of each judge's failure mode is prompt-induced vs. capability-induced? The paper explicitly calls for prompt-optimization ablations using a held-out set (citing the absence as a limitation).
- Would a finer coverage scale (0–9 or 0–100, as the authors suggest) break DeepSeek's ceiling collapse and yield graded discrimination at low deletion?
- Can a two-stage detector + localizer pipeline (separate judges per stage) close the localization gap, given that the cross-model failure analysis shows complementary errors and 79.8% joint detection?
- How do these LLM-judge scores compare to specialized process reward models (e.g., ProcessBench/PRMBench targets) on the same perturbations — a comparison the paper does not run?
