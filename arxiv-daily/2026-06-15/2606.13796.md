> **一句话总结：** 理论证明在完美分数估计下，递归训练扩散模型因反向 SDE 提前截断几何收敛到唯一闭式极限分布 $p^\star_\infty$（数据分布的无限几何平滑混合），并在 Hermite 基下证明其低通滤波效应，进而提出渐近消除误差的退火截断方案。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides the first **asymptotic (fixed-point) characterization** of model collapse for recursively trained diffusion models. Even with perfect score estimation and exact sampling, **truncation of the reverse SDE at $t_0>0$** injects residual Gaussian noise at each round, so the recursion converges geometrically (rate $\kappa = \sqrt{1-\alpha e^{-t_0/2}}$) to a unique closed-form limit $p^\star_\infty = \alpha\sum_{k=0}^\infty (1-\alpha)^k U^{(k+1)t_0}(p_{\text{data}})$, an infinite mixture of increasingly OU-smoothed copies of $p_{\text{data}}$. A **Hermite-spectral decomposition** of this limit shows recursive training acts as a low-pass filter, with attenuation factor $m_n(\alpha,t_0)=\alpha e^{-|n|t_0/2}/[1-(1-\alpha)e^{-|n|t_0/2}]$ that suppresses high-degree modes (fine non-Gaussian structure) more strongly than low-degree modes. This motivates **annealed truncation schedules** $t_0^{(i)}\to 0$, which Theorem 4.1 shows asymptotically recover $p_{\text{data}}$ exactly in the error-free regime. Robustness to score-estimation and discretization errors is established via Wasserstein-2 bounds, with **mode-dependent contraction** showing high-order errors decay faster than low-order errors. Validated on synthetic 2D Gaussian mixtures and CIFAR-10 (8 generations, α ∈ {0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 0.9}).

## 核心贡献

- **理想化截断机制识别**：在完美分数估计与精确采样的设定下，识别**反向 SDE 的提前截断**（为数值稳定所必须）是驱动递归漂移的唯一机制，将"截断误差"与"学习误差"在分析上彻底分离。
- **极限分布的闭式刻画 (Theorem 3.1)**：证明递归过程几何收敛到唯一固定点
  $$p^\star_\infty = \alpha \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (1-\alpha)^k\, U^{(k+1)t_0}(p_{\text{data}}),$$
  收敛速率 $\kappa=\sqrt{1-\alpha e^{-t_0/2}}<1$；并给出极限行为 (Corollary 3.2)：$\alpha\to 1$ 时退化为单步平滑、$\alpha\to 0$ 时收敛到标准高斯、$t_0\to 0$ 时收敛到 $p_{\text{data}}$、当 $p_{\text{data}}$ 非高斯时 $W_2(p^\star_\infty, p_{\text{data}})>0$。
- **Hermite 谱视角 (Proposition 3.3)**：在多维 Hermite 基下展开 $p^\star_\infty$，得到**逐模态衰减因子** $m_n(\alpha,t_0)=\alpha e^{-|n|t_0/2}/[1-(1-\alpha)e^{-|n|t_0/2}]$，显式证明递归训练等价于一个由 $\alpha,t_0$ 控制带宽的低通滤波器。
- **退火截断方案 (Theorem 4.1)**：在递归各轮逐步缩短截断时间 $t_0^{(i)}$；证明任何 $t_0^{(i)}\to t_\infty$ 的调度对应极限分布 $p_\infty^{(t_\infty)}=\alpha\sum_m (1-\alpha)^m U^{(m+1)t_\infty}(p_{\text{data}})$，特别当 $t_\infty=0$ 时 **精确恢复** $p_{\text{data}}$。$\beta$-退火族 $t_0/(1+i)^\beta$ 在有限轮内即把 KL 推向零。
- **鲁棒性分析 (Theorem 5.2, Proposition 5.3)**：将分数估计和离散化误差作为抽象一步扰动 $\delta_i$，证明 $W_2(p_N, p^\star_\infty)\le \kappa^N W_2(p_{\text{data}},p^\star_\infty)+\sum_{i}\kappa^{N-1-i}\delta_i$，稳态落入半径 $\delta/(1-\kappa)$ 的 W₂ 球；进一步在 Hermite 模态上得到**模态依赖**的收缩率 $\kappa_n=(1-\alpha)e^{-|n|t_0/2}\ll\kappa$，即高阶误差衰减更快——稳健性椭球而非均匀球。
- **实验验证**：在二维高斯混合上验证 Neumann 级数极限分布 (Cor. 3.2) 与退火截断 (Thm. 4.1)；在 CIFAR-10 上做 8 轮递归训练，验证几何收敛率与高频缺陷随 $\alpha,N$ 单调变化。

## Key Contributions

- **Isolate truncation as the sole driver of collapse** in the idealized regime with perfect score estimation and exact sampling; cleanly decouple truncation error from learning error.
- **Closed-form limit (Theorem 3.1):** $p^\star_\infty = \alpha\sum_{k=0}^\infty (1-\alpha)^k U^{(k+1)t_0}(p_{\text{data}})$ with geometric rate $\kappa=\sqrt{1-\alpha e^{-t_0/2}}$. Corollary 3.2 characterizes all four limit regimes (α→1, α→0, t₀→0, non-Gaussian p_data).
- **Hermite spectral decomposition (Proposition 3.3):** $m_n(\alpha,t_0)=\alpha e^{-|n|t_0/2}/[1-(1-\alpha)e^{-|n|t_0/2}]$, monotone decreasing in $|n|$, exposing recursive training as a low-pass filter.
- **Annealed truncation (Theorem 4.1):** for $t_0^{(i)}\to 0$ the limit equals $p_{\text{data}}$ exactly; $\beta$-annealing $t_0/(1+i)^\beta$ drives KL to zero at rates increasing with $\beta$.
- **Robustness in W₂ (Theorem 5.2):** Wasserstein-2 ball around $p^\star_\infty$ with radius $\delta/(1-\kappa)$ when one-step errors $\delta_i$ are uniformly bounded.
- **Mode-dependent error propagation (Proposition 5.3):** mode-wise contraction $\kappa_n=(1-\alpha)e^{-|n|t_0/2}$ means the error region is an ellipsoid whose semi-axes shrink rapidly with $|n|$; high-frequency errors self-correct.
- **Self-regularization observation:** as $t_0^{(i)}\to 0$, the cumulative smoothing of the synthetic component keeps Fisher information bounded, partially mitigating the score-instability concern at very small $t_0$.
- **Empirical validation:** synthetic 2D Gaussian mixtures and CIFAR-10 (8 generations, α ∈ {0.1,0.3,0.5,0.7,0.9}; single NVIDIA RTX 6000 Blackwell, ~12 h per full run).

## Methodology

The recursive procedure is: at generation $i\ge 1$, a fraction $\alpha\in(0,1)$ of training data is fresh from $p_{\text{data}}$ and the remaining $(1-\alpha)$ is synthetic from the previous round's model $p̂_i$; the effective training distribution is $q_i = \alpha p_{\text{data}}+(1-\alpha)p̂_i$, and a score network is fit to samples from $q_i$. Generation-dependent truncation $t_0^{(i)}>0$ is used for numerical stability of the reverse SDE.

In the **error-free regime**, the one-step transition is generation-independent and equals $p̂_{i+1} = U_{t_0}(\alpha p_{\text{data}}+(1-\alpha)p̂_i)$, where $U_t$ is the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck forward operator — i.e., $U_t(\mu) = \text{Law}(e^{-t/2}X_0 + \sqrt{1-e^{-t}}\,Z)$, $X_0\sim\mu$, $Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$. The fixed-point equation $\mu = U_{t_0}(\alpha p_{\text{data}}+(1-\alpha)\mu)$ has unique solution $p^\star_\infty$ via Neumann series.

For the **spectral analysis**, the density ratio $f_\infty^\star = dp^\star_\infty/d\gamma$ (with $\gamma=\mathcal{N}(0,I)$) is expanded in the multivariate Hermite basis $\{H_n\}_{n\in\mathbb{N}^d}$, which diagonalizes the OU semigroup. Each mode $n$ is multiplied by $e^{-|n|t/2}$ under $U_t$ (Proposition B.4). Summing the Neumann series mode-by-mode yields the closed-form attenuation factor $m_n$.

For the **annealed truncation** analysis, $U_{t_0}$ is replaced by $U_{t_0^{(N)}}$ at generation $N$. The composition of operators is computed explicitly, yielding a closed-form iterate (Theorem 4.1, eq. 13) whose limit depends only on the asymptotic truncation time $t_\infty$.

For **robustness**, score-estimation error $e_i(x,s)=s_{\theta_i}(x,s)-\nabla\log q_{i,s}(x)$ and discretization error are combined into a one-step W₂ perturbation $\delta_i = W_2(Ŝ_i(q_i), U_{t_0}(q_i))$. Geometric contraction + accumulated perturbation yield the W₂ bound. Mode-by-mode perturbation analysis refines this to an ellipsoidal error region.

**Empirical setup:** 2D Gaussian mixtures on CPU for limit distribution and annealed-schedule tests; CIFAR-10 with a well-trained baseline diffusion model, 8 generations, mixing real and synthetic samples per round at the specified α; FID computed against the real CIFAR-10 split using 30,000 generated samples per checkpoint. Spectral signature is quantified via a **high-frequency deficit** $D_{HF}(p̂_N)=1-HF(p̂_N)/HF(p_{\text{data}})$ using $N_{\text{spec}}$ real CIFAR-10 images as reference.

## Results & Highlights

- **Explicit limit (Theorem 3.1):** $\kappa=\sqrt{1-\alpha e^{-t_0/2}}$ is the geometric contraction rate; smaller $\alpha$ or larger $t_0$ accelerates convergence **to the wrong distribution** (the collapse distribution).
- **Four limit regimes (Corollary 3.2):** (i) α→1 ⇒ collapse = single-step smoothing; (ii) α→0 ⇒ $p^\star_\infty \to \mathcal{N}(0,I)$; (iii) t₀→0 ⇒ $p^\star_\infty \to p_{\text{data}}$; (iv) for non-Gaussian $p_{\text{data}}$ and any α<1, t₀>0: $W_2(p^\star_\infty,p_{\text{data}})>0$.
- **Hermite attenuation (Figure 1):** on a 2D distribution with substantial medium/high-degree Hermite energy, the spectral heatmap of $p^\star_\infty$ shows the predicted attenuation, monotonically increasing as α shrinks.
- **Annealed truncation (Figure 2):** on a 2D Gaussian mixture, fixed and positive-limit schedules plateau at the predicted floors $KL(p^\star_\infty\|p_{\text{data}})$ and $KL(p^{(t_\infty)}_\infty\|p_{\text{data}})$; β-annealed schedules drive KL → 0 with rates increasing in β.
- **CIFAR-10 contraction (Figure 3a):** $FID(p̂_N, p^\star_\infty)$ decays geometrically with $\kappa(\alpha)=\sqrt{1-\alpha e^{-t_0/2}}$ and stabilizes around a common floor consistent with the W₂ ball of Theorem 5.2.
- **High-frequency deficit (Figure 3b):** $D_{HF}$ grows fastest for small α and large N, directly validating the **low-pass-filter mechanism** of Propositions 3.3 and 5.3 on real images; at α=0.9 the deficit stays below 0.15 over 8 generations.
- **Truncation severity (Figure 5, App. F):** the FID gap between $p^\star_\infty$ and $p_{\text{data}}$ grows with $t_0$, confirming that increasing α alone cannot eliminate collapse — annealed truncation is a complementary lever.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: strong.** Prior work bounds *finite-round* error accumulation in recursively trained diffusion models, but **no prior work gives a closed-form fixed point** or **mode-by-mode spectral characterization** of the limiting distribution. The annealing schedule — motivated by the truncation-error identification — is a new practical lever distinct from the standard "increase α" or "data rejuvenation" remedies.
- **Rigor: high but idealized.** The core results (Theorems 3.1, 4.1, 5.2; Propositions 3.3, 5.3) are proved under Assumptions A1–A4 (positive smooth density, finite second moment, square-integrable score, $p_{\text{data}}$ in $L^2(\gamma)$). **A4 excludes heavy-tailed $p_{\text{data}}$**, and the authors concede that "experiments on CIFAR-10 suggest the spectral results hold more widely in practice" — a clear hand-wave rather than a proof. The robustness theorem treats score-estimation and discretization errors as abstract one-step perturbations δ_i; explicit end-to-end constants in terms of model class, sample size n_i, dimension d, and step sizes are flagged as future work.
- **Reproducibility: medium.** Synthetic experiments are reproducible from the description. CIFAR-10 experiments are described in detail in Appendix F (α grid, baseline model, FID against full split with 30,000 samples, $N_{\text{spec}}$ for HF deficit), but a full 8-generation CIFAR-10 run took ~12 h on a single RTX 6000 Blackwell — modest enough for a typical lab to reproduce.
- **Significance: high.** This is the first paper that gives the answer to the question "what does recursive diffusion training collapse to?" in closed form, plus a falsifiable practical prescription. The spectral low-pass-filter framing aligns with empirical observations of tail-loss under collapse and gives a principled explanation rather than a heuristic.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - **Heavy-tail exclusion (A4):** for image data this is plausibly fine (CIFAR-10 evidence) but the theory is silent on language models or any heavy-tailed domain.
  - **CIFAR-10 only** for the learned-regime experiments; no large-scale or class-conditional test, no text, no flow-matching / consistency-model analog.
  - **Practical guidance on annealed schedule is thin:** "any $t_0^{(i)}\to 0$ works" is asymptotic; finite-round guidance with explicit constants and stability constraints is delegated to future work. The β-annealing rule $t_0/(1+i)^\beta$ has not been stress-tested against score-instability regimes or small-data regimes.
  - **The implicit baseline comparison** is mainly with fixed $t_0$ and varying α. Comparison with concrete alternative remedies (data rejuvenation, synthetic-data detectors, fully synthetic retraining [54], or training-from-scratch-with-real-only) is not in the paper.
  - **Tone of robustness claims:** Theorem 5.2 says the W₂ error stays bounded around $p^\star_\infty$ — but for practical use one wants bounds around $p_{\text{data}}$, which the annealed schedule guarantees only asymptotically.
  - **G-Research funding of the first author** is a relevant conflict-of-interest context (G-Research funds ML research heavily); not disqualifying, just worth noting.

## Strengths

- Cleanly decouples truncation error from learning error in the analysis — the central conceptual contribution.
- Closed-form limit with **explicit constants** in α and $t_0$ that practitioners can plug in.
- The Hermite-spectral lens explains *why* fine structure (tails, multimodality, oscillations) is lost first under recursive training — a unifying empirical observation across multiple papers.
- Geometric convergence rate + robustness bound are tight enough to predict observed FID behavior on CIFAR-10.
- The annealed-truncation prescription is **specific to diffusion models** (it exploits the truncation-bias mechanism), so it is complementary to the usual α-based mitigations.
- Mode-dependent contraction (Proposition 5.3) gives a falsifiable spectral prediction: the high-frequency deficit should grow monotonically with N and decrease with α — and it does.

## Limitations

- Theory lives in the idealized, error-free regime for the *core* results; robustness is handled via abstract perturbations rather than end-to-end guarantees with sample-size and dimension dependence.
- Hermite analysis is w.r.t. a fixed Gaussian reference $\gamma=\mathcal{N}(0,I)$; extending to non-isotropic or non-Gaussian reference measures is open.
- Variance-preserving OU diffusion is the only forward process studied; flow matching, rectified flow, and consistency models are not covered.
- Annealed schedule is asymptotic; no adaptive/learning-based rule, no explicit finite-round bound on the bias-variance trade-off as $t_0$ shrinks.
- Experiments: 2D Gaussian mixture + CIFAR-10 only; no language, no high-resolution images, no class-conditional generation, no scaling laws.
- Self-regularization claim (Fisher info bounded as $t_0\to 0$) is made precise in Appendix E but the paper's main text defers details; readers will want to inspect it carefully.
- Assumes α is fixed across generations; the [28]-style setting where each past generation is sampled in proportion $\alpha_k$ is mentioned but not analyzed.

## Relevance

与用户关注的扩散模型理论、生成模型失败模式与合成数据自循环稳定性方向高度相关。该工作首次给出递归训练扩散模型崩溃极限的闭式解与 Hermite 谱刻画，并基于此提出可操作的退火截断方案，对从事 self-consuming generative pipelines、合成数据训练稳定性、扩散模型理论与一致性 / 流匹配模型失败模式分析的研究者具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the low-pass-filter behavior transfer to **flow matching / rectified flow / consistency models**, which do not require truncation of an SDE near $t=0$? If yes, the spectral mechanism must generalize; if no, that is itself an important structural difference.
- Can the Hermite analysis be extended to a **non-Gaussian reference measure** (e.g., an empirical or Student-$t$ reference), broadening A4?
- What is the **finite-round, dimension-aware** version of the robustness bound, with explicit dependence on network class and sample size $n_i$? Combining fixed-point analysis with [17, 10, 13]'s non-asymptotic guarantees would close this gap.
- Can an **adaptive annealing rule** be derived that minimizes $W_2(p̂_N, p_{\text{data}})$ subject to a stability constraint on $\nabla\log q_{i,t}$ at $t$ near $t_0$? This would make the practical recommendation actionable.
- The annealed schedule is monotone; would a **non-monotone schedule** (small early $t_0$, then moderate, then small) help balance exploration of the synthetic support with bias reduction?
- The W₂ ellipsoid result (high-frequency modes contract faster) suggests a **per-frequency-step correction** in the loss/score objective; could one design a sampling-time correction that exploits this?
- How does the result interact with **classifier-free guidance** or with **latent diffusion** (where the truncation behavior is in latent space, not pixel space)?
