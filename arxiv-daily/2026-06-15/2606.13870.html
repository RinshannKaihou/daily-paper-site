Mirage Probes: How Vision Models Fake Visual Understanding

Vision-language models often answer image-conditioned questions correctly even when no real image is provided. This dashboard maps the internal evidence for why that happens — and why fixing the text distribution is not enough.

Authors Ben-Levi et al. (Lipson, Shwartz-Ziv) Models Ovis2.5-2B · Qwen3-32B-VL-Instruct Benchmarks VQA-RAD · MMMU-Pro · MedXpertQA
Key Finding
Linear

Mirage behavior is linearly decodable from the language decoder's residual stream, MLP outputs, post-attention outputs, and individual attention heads across both models.

Key Metric
97.4%

Difference-in-activations probes peak on VQA-RAD: 97.4% for Ovis and 96.0% for Qwen. The visual-evoked activation shift itself encodes mirage information.

Why This Matters
Two

Mirages split into two regimes: spurious images (VQA-RAD) and textual biases (MMMU-Pro / MedXpertQA). Cleaning text distributions only fixes the second.

Critical Edge
—

No causal intervention is demonstrated. The two-mechanism claim is a well-supported hypothesis, not a validated fix — representational-level remedies remain future work.

The Probe

Mirage Probes Framework

A contrastive probing setup that pairs lightly paraphrased variants of the same image-conditioned question with matched mirage / non-mirage labels. This isolates the mirage signal from topical, lexical, and length confounds.

Labeling rule

  • Mirage-like: with-image and without-image answers match and their bag-of-words cosine similarity is above 0.7.
  • Non-mirage-like: the without-image response explicitly expresses uncertainty or says answering is impossible.
  • Ambiguous: without-image is mirage-like but the with-image answer diverges — excluded from main results.

Probe families tested

  • LogReg — linear probe on per-layer activations.
  • 2-layer MLP — tests whether non-linear readout is needed (it is not).
  • Concat — linear probe across all layers concatenated.
  • Diff — linear probe on with-image minus without-image activation differences.

Key controls

  • Naive Bayes text baseline reaches 45–66% on the contrastive split; contrastive linear probes exceed it in every cell, ruling out surface lexical confounds.
  • Vision encoder + projection layers carry no detectable mirage signal — the signal lives in the language decoder.
Two Regimes

Spurious Images vs. Textual Biases

The paper uses cross-benchmark separability and the new Prior Harnessing Index (PHI) = log p(a*|Q) − log p(a*|Q∅) to argue that mirage behavior is not one phenomenon but two distinct internal regimes.

🜂 Spurious Images

The model fabricates visual content in latent space and answers from that fabricated image. Dominant on VQA-RAD: human annotators label these examples as image-reliant, and PHI anti-correlates with mirage labels (r = −0.36 for Ovis).

🜁 Textual Biases

The model answers from text priors without genuinely engaging vision. Common on MMMU-Pro (mean PHI = +1.20) and MedXpertQA (mean PHI = +0.30). Text-distribution cleaning can address this regime.

Credibility

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean conceptual framing: two-regime taxonomy is actionable.
  • Strong controls: Naive Bayes text baseline + contrastive-vs-all-examples comparison.
  • Systematic multi-site, multi-probe study across two model scales and three benchmarks.
  • Useful negative result: vision encoder / projection carry no detectable signal.

Limitations

  • No causal validation via steering, ablation, or activation patching.
  • No demonstrated representational-level mitigation for spurious-image mirages.
  • GLM-4.6-flash and MicroVQA dropped due to insufficient non-mirage examples.
  • Difference-in-activations probe is leaky: it uses the same with-vs-without comparison that defines the label.
Relevance

Why Track This Work

For anyone building trustworthy multimodal evaluation, this paper moves the conversation from “does the model answer without an image?” to “how does the model answer without an image?”. The Mirage Probes framework and PHI metric provide concrete, measurable tools to anchor VLM-grounding diagnostics in internal representations rather than surface behavior alone.

Open questions

  • Do the two regimes generalize to proprietary frontier VLMs?
  • Can a causal intervention suppress spurious-image mirages while preserving genuine visual grounding?
  • Can Mirage Probes become a training-time diagnostic for RLHF / preference fine-tuning?
