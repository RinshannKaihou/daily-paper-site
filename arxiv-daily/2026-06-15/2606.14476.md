> **一句话总结：** 在 ReAct 风格 LLM 智能体调用冻结 GCN 作为 ogbn-arxiv/WikiCS 节点分类工具的对照实验中,智能体并未对工具做出判断,而是沦为"工具复读机"(与 GNN 输出一致率 0.976–0.992);在 Qwen2.5 系列中,能调用工具的模型(≥1.5B)对工具的依赖随能力提升而单调上升(0.60→0.98),且顺从的代价并不随能力缩小——per-node oracle 表明 7B 模型本可通过选择性调用多拿 0.12–0.22 准确率,选择性门控也只能恢复约一半高同质性差距,信息瓶颈而非路由器设计才是真正的限制。
>
> **TL;DR:** This paper is a *cautionary measurement* debunking the assumption that an LLM agent exercises judgment when given a learned GNN as a callable tool. On ogbn-arxiv node classification (replicated on WikiCS), a ReAct agent with Qwen2.5-7B exposed to a frozen 2-layer GCN as an explicit tool agrees with the raw GNN's prediction **97.6–99.2%** of the time across local-homophily regimes — it collapses into a "GNN parrot" that bypasses its own reasoning. The same agent's tool-using answers coincide with its tool-free reasoning only **17–37%** at 7B (7–20% at 3B), and in **83% of 7B queries the agent makes exactly one tool call** (just reading the label, ignoring the anomaly score designed to flag when the label should not be trusted). Counter-intuitively, **deference grows with capability**: 0.5B can't use the tool; from 1.5B → 7B, agreement rises 0.60 → 0.98 (5 seeds). The cost of deference does not shrink with capability and **grows where alternatives emerge**: a per-node oracle beats the parrot by 0.12/0.18/0.22 (low/mid/high homophily) at 7B, vs. 0.09/0.18/0.12 at 3B, with the high-homophily gap roughly doubling (positive in all 5 paired seeds, paired *t*=9.1). At 7B and high homophily, a simple neighbor-label tool already overtakes the GNN (0.81 vs. 0.71), but the agent still defers. A hand-designed purity gate recovers ~half the high-homophily gap (0.71 → 0.83, paired *t*=4.0, positive in all 5 seeds), but hurts elsewhere for no net global gain (0.481 → 0.475). A learned 4-feature router does no better. Held-out estimators (kNN-best-arm *k*=31; median-binned cell policy) bound the **best achievable gate over standard test-time features to only ⅙–⅓ of the oracle headroom** on arxiv (~12–14% on WikiCS), so reliable selective invocation is information-limited, not router-limited. Cross-family probing sharpens the claim: Mistral-7B and OLMo-2-7B defer partially (agreement 0.53/0.60) — direction generalizes, extreme magnitude is Qwen-specific.

## 核心贡献

- **直接测量"智能体是否对工具有判断力"**:四臂对照实验(A1 智能体+GNN、A2 智能体+邻居标签、A3 GNN 单独、A4 智能体无工具),每查询预算 5,000 token + 6 次工具调用;在 ogbn-arxiv 上,Qwen2.5-7B 与冻结 GCN 输出一致率高达 0.976–0.992,达成"工具复读机"。
- **能力–顺从性反直觉正相关**:在 Qwen2.5 0.5B→7B 中,0.5B 因解析失败几乎无法调用工具,≥1.5B 的能调用工具模型一致率随能力提升单调上升(1.5B 的 0.60→3B/7B 的 0.98),且 83% 查询只调用一次工具。
- **顺从的代价不随能力下降**:per-node oracle 间隙在 3B 为 0.09/0.18/0.12(低/中/高同质性),在 7B 为 0.12/0.18/0.22;高同质性间隙近乎翻倍(配对 *t*=9.1,全部 5 配对种子均正向),因为 A4/A2 等替代方案随能力变强,但复读机仍被钉在冻结 GNN 上。
- **信息瓶颈量化**:简单纯度门控在高同质性区域恢复 0.71→0.83(配对 *t*=4.0,5/5 配对种子均正),但低/中区域反而退化(0.29→0.18,0.44→0.41),全局净改善 ≤0;在四个标准测试期特征(purity、GNN-confidence、degree、neighbour-disagreement)上,留出 kNN-best-arm 与 cell policy 估计最优可达门控仅恢复 arxiv 上 ⅙–⅓ oracle 余量,WikiCS 上仅 12–14%——真正限制是信息而非路由器设计。
- **跨家族验证与边界条件**:在 Mistral-7B 与 OLMo-2-7B-Instruct 上(配英语脚手架),方向一致(均顺从 GNN 于多数节点),但极端程度(≥0.97 一致率)为 Qwen 特有;论文明确范围:不主张量级可迁移,只主张失败模式可复现。

## Key Contributions

- **Direct measurement of "agent judgment"** under a four-arm controlled protocol (A1: agent+GNN tool, A2: agent+neighbor-label tool, A3: frozen GNN alone, A4: agent no tool), matched budget of 5,000 prompt+generation tokens and 6 tool calls per query. On ogbn-arxiv, agreement A1=A3 reaches 0.976–0.992 at 7B across homophily regimes — operationally indistinguishable from the tool itself.
- **Counter-intuitive capability-deference scaling.** Within the Qwen2.5 family (0.5B→7B, 5 seeds), once a model can invoke the tool (≥1.5B), agreement with the GNN rises monotonically with capability (0.60 → 0.97 → 0.98). The 0.5B model fails to invoke, providing a clean incapacity control. 83% of 7B queries produce exactly one tool call.
- **Cost of deference does not shrink with capability.** Per-node oracle gap (cost of deferring) at 7B is 0.12/0.18/0.22 (low/mid/high homophily) — roughly double at high homophily vs. 3B (0.09/0.18/0.12). The parrot's accuracy is pinned to the frozen GNN (±0.02), while alternatives (A2 neighbor-label, A4 tool-free) strengthen with capability; at 7B and high homophily, the neighbor-label arm already beats the GNN (0.81 vs. 0.71).
- **Information-bounded selective invocation.** A simple purity-threshold gate recovers ~½ the high-homophily gap (0.71 → 0.83, paired *t*=4.0, 5/5 seeds) but degrades other regimes for no global gain (0.481 → 0.475). A learned 4-feature router does no better. Held-out estimators (kNN best-arm *k*=31; median-binned cell policy) bound the best achievable gate on these features to **⅙–⅓ of the oracle headroom** on arxiv (12–14% on WikiCS) — feature-limited, not router-limited.
- **Honest boundary conditions.** Cross-family probing (Mistral-7B, OLMo-2-7B-Instruct under matched English scaffold) shows the direction generalizes (every tested family agrees with the GNN on a majority of nodes) but the *extreme* magnitude (≥0.97 agreement) is Qwen-specific (Mistral/OLMo ~0.53/0.60). Capability sweep saturates by 3B on agreement but not on cost; backbones beyond 7B are out of scope (single-GPU constraint).

## Methodology

- **Datasets.** Primary: **ogbn-arxiv** (169k nodes, 40 classes, citation graph, title+abstract as node text). Replication: **WikiCS** (11.7k nodes, 10 classes, edge homophily 0.66).
- **Tool: Frozen GCN.** 2-layer GCN (hidden 128) with a reconstruction head, trained 200 epochs on the official training split with best-validation checkpoint, **frozen** at inference, retrained per seed.
- **Tool surface (A1).** Three separate calls exposed to the agent: `gnn_predict()` → `class=c conf=p`; `gnn_anomaly()` → a reconstruction anomaly score, *described to the agent as "higher means the GNN is more likely wrong on this node"*; `gnn_link(neighbor-id)` → a link probability.
- **Tool surface (A2).** `neighbors()` returns up to *k*=10 neighbor IDs with training-set labels where available (no neighbor text); `degree()` returns node degree.
- **Agent loop.** ReAct loop, max 4 steps; per-query budget 5,000 tokens and 6 tool calls; forced finalization on exhaustion; regex-parsed `ACTION`/`ANSWER` lines; fallback to class 0 if the harness cannot parse an in-range class id (affects only 0.5B Qwen and Chinese-scaffold Mistral).
- **Scaffold.** Chinese system prompt instructs tool consultation (invocation is therefore prompt-encouraged; the measurement is about *adoption*, not invocation). Faithful English translations in Appendix A; the scaffold language matters for cross-family probing.
- **Backbones.** Qwen2.5-Instruct at 0.5B / 1.5B / 3B / 7B served locally (vLLM for 1.5–7B, transformers for 0.5B and Mistral). Decoding: temperature 0.7, top-p 0.9, max 256 new tokens/turn. Seeds fix GNN training and node sampling; LLM decoding is sampled (not seed-fixed).
- **Metrics.** Per-arm accuracy; **agreement Pr[A1 pred = A3 pred]** = deference / parrot rate; **oracle gap** = acc(max{A1, A2, A4}) − acc(A1) = cost of deference.
- **Stratification.** Test nodes binned by *local homophily* (fraction of same-label neighbors, ground truth) into low (<0.3), mid ([0.3, 0.7)), high (≥0.7); used as an analysis axis only — *never given to the agent*.
- **Gates (Section 7).** Hand-designed purity-threshold gate (τ=0.4 chosen on seed 0); a learned router over four features (purity, GNN-confidence, degree, neighbor-disagreement), leave-one-seed-out over the 750 stratified nodes of Table 1 (train on 4 seeds, test on the 5th). Held-out best-gate estimators: kNN best-arm policy (*k*=31 in standardized feature space) and median-binned cell policy, both compared against feature-shuffled nulls.
- **Cross-family controls.** Mistral-7B-Instruct and OLMo-2-7B-Instruct under matched English scaffold on a shared frozen GNN and node sample (3 seeds, 60 nodes); Qwen-7B re-run under the same English scaffold as a language control.

## Results & Highlights

- **The parrot effect (7B, ogbn-arxiv, Table 1).** Agreement (A1=A3) is **0.976–0.992** across all three homophily bins; parrot accuracy within ±0.02 of raw GNN. A1 vs. A4 (agent tool-free reasoning) agreement only **17–37%** at 7B (7–20% at 3B) — the tool, once available, overrides the agent's own reasoning almost entirely.
- **Single-call collapse.** In 83% of 7B queries the agent makes *exactly one tool call* — reads the label and never probes the anomaly score designed to flag when the label should not be trusted.
- **Capability-deference scaling (Table 2, 5 seeds).** 0.5B agreement 0.10 (cannot use tool); 1.5B agreement 0.62/0.60/0.58; 3B 0.98/0.96/0.98; 7B 0.98/0.98/0.99 (low/mid/high). Capability buys *more complete deference*, not skepticism.
- **Oracle gap by capability (Table 3).** 1.5B 0.048/0.168/0.228; 3B 0.092/0.184/0.120; 7B 0.120/0.184/0.220 (low/mid/high). From 3B → 7B the high-homophily gap roughly **doubles** (positive in all 5 paired seeds, paired *t*=9.1, survives Bonferroni correction); the mechanism is that A2 and A4 strengthen with capability while A1 is pinned to the frozen GNN.
- **When the GNN is no longer best.** At 7B and high homophily, **A2 neighbor-label beats A3 GNN (0.81 vs. 0.71)**; at mid they are comparable (0.40 vs. 0.44); at low the GNN dominates. The agent still defers to the GNN at 0.992 agreement at high homophily.
- **Purity gate (Section 7).** Recovers 0.71 → 0.83 at high homophily (paired *t*=4.0, 5/5 seeds), but degrades low (0.29 → 0.18) and mid (0.44 → 0.41). Net global: 0.481 → 0.475. *Seed-0-only* evaluation had suggested a global +0.07 gain that does **not survive 5 seeds** — reported as a caution against single-seed gate evaluations.
- **Learned 4-feature router.** Leave-one-seed-out over the 750 stratified nodes: 0.496 ± 0.026 (no better than validation-selected purity gate 0.499 ± 0.016; both fail to beat the parrot 0.481 against oracle 0.656). An earlier i.i.d. single-split (n=300, 150 held-out) reaches the same conclusion (0.527 vs. 0.533, oracle 0.647).
- **Information ceiling on arxiv.** Held-out kNN-best-arm estimator: 0.537 ± 0.025. Median-binned cell policy: 0.508 ± 0.021. Both clear feature-shuffled nulls (0.469 and 0.472; real > null in all 5 seeds) but sit far below the per-node oracle 0.656. Best achievable gate over the four features recovers only **0.027–0.056** of the 0.175 oracle headroom — ≈ **⅙–⅓**; the residual ≈ 0.12–0.15 is not closed by any gate we could build over these features.
- **Replication on WikiCS (Table 4, 7B, 3 seeds).** Parrot holds (agreement 0.96–1.00); oracle gap positive in 9/9 seed×bin pairs (0.03–0.23). Regime where the cost peaks differs: WikiCS peaks at *low* homophily (gap 0.23, where nbr-label beats GNN 0.38 vs. 0.25); arxiv peaks at *high* homophily. Information ceiling reproduces and is **even tighter**: kNN and cell estimators recover only 0.014–0.017 of the 0.119 oracle headroom (≈ 12–14%), again clearing their nulls in all 3 seeds but by a small margin.
- **Cross-family boundary (Section 9).** Under matched English scaffold on a shared frozen GNN and node sample: Mistral-7B-Instruct agreement 0.53 ± 0.01 (A1 0.32 vs. GNN 0.49); OLMo-2-7B-Instruct agreement 0.60 ± 0.03 (A1 0.36 vs. GNN 0.49); Qwen-7B under the same English scaffold still parrots at 0.978 ± 0.015 (matches its 0.98 under Chinese). The Chinese-vs-English scaffold language is *not* what produces parroting; the extreme magnitude is *partly Qwen-specific*.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The headline is empirical, not methodological: a falsifiable measurement of an *implicit assumption* (agent judgment) that the agent+tool community largely takes for granted. The "deference grows with capability" effect is the genuinely novel and counter-intuitive result; framing the cost via an oracle ceiling is the right methodological choice and avoids overclaiming.
- **Rigor.**
  - *Strong.* Four-arm matched-budget protocol; per-seed paired tests with effect sizes (*t*=9.1 for the high-homophily cost-doubling, *t*=4.0 for the gate); Bonferroni correction; feature-shuffled nulls on the held-out gate estimators; honest disclosure that a seed-0-only gate evaluation suggested a +0.07 gain that did *not* survive 5 seeds; explicit boundary conditions on cross-family transfer and capability saturation.
  - *Adequate but improvable.* The 0.5B row comes from a seed-0 capability run (30 nodes/bin) rather than 5 seeds, which is appropriate (the model can't use the tool) but worth noting. Decoding is not seed-fixed (temperature 0.7), so single-run numbers carry decoding noise; all core claims are therefore stated over 5 seeds.
- **Reproducibility.** Code, seeds, and JSON results are stated as released. The Chinese scaffold, GCN training script, and the exact ReAct prompt (translated in Appendix A) are reported in enough detail to re-implement. *What is not visible from the paper:* the exact parser regex, the exact token-budget accounting details, and the exact wiring of vLLM vs. transformers (only "behavior verified equivalent on seed-0 phase-1 grid" is claimed).
- **Significance.** A genuinely useful cautionary baseline for the agent+tool community. The framing — "evaluations of agent+tool systems cannot assume the agent adds judgment on top of the tool, and selective invocation must be designed in rather than expected to emerge from scale" — is a recommendation the field needs to hear, and the empirical backing is unusually clean for an agent paper.
- **Potential red flags and honest weaknesses.**
  - **The parrot effect's extreme magnitude is Qwen-specific.** The cross-family probe is limited (n=60 per family, 3 seeds, one English scaffold), but the finding is explicit: Mistral/OLMo agree at 0.53/0.60, not 0.98. Strong form of the claim should therefore be *scoped to Qwen2.5*; weaker form (direction generalizes; extreme magnitude does not) is what the paper actually supports.
  - **The oracle gap mixes "deference cost" with "action-set discoverability."** The oracle picks the best of {A1, A2, A4}; A2 is exposed to training labels but only at *neighbors*. The high-homophily cost-doubling could partly reflect that 7B agents *learn to ignore* A2 outputs that disagree with the GNN even when A2 is right — a finding the paper makes but does not fully disentangle from a pure "blind deference" interpretation.
  - **The "best gate recovers ⅙–⅓ of headroom" bound depends on the four features chosen.** Adding features (e.g., graph-distance to training nodes, neighbor-text features, agent-side uncertainty) could raise the ceiling; the paper says "this is an empirical ceiling, not a proof that no feature could help." Take that seriously.
  - **The agent's instruction explicitly says "consult tools first," and the harness counts not-following-format as class 0.** This makes the protocol a *compliant* agent setting rather than a generic tool-use setting. The parrot effect is therefore a statement about *what compliant agents do*, which is a common assumption in deployed systems but not the only one worth testing.
  - **Sampling budget.** 50 nodes/bin × 5 seeds × 3 bins = 750 stratified nodes on arxiv; 40 nodes/bin × 3 seeds × 3 bins = 360 on WikiCS. Tight, but the seeds are paired within backbone so the cross-backbone comparisons are well-powered at the paired-seed level.
  - **Mechanism is observational, not isolated.** The paper explicitly does *not* claim to identify why deference grows with capability — possible mechanisms (tool output dominating the agent's context; SFT/tool-use priors; calibration collapse with scale) are left open.

## Strengths

- A falsifiable, narrowly scoped question with a clean measurement design (agreement rate, oracle ceiling, paired-seed tests).
- A genuinely counter-intuitive main effect (deference grows with capability) — exactly the kind of result the agent+tool community needs.
- Explicit held-out bound on the best achievable gate on standard uncertainty features, cleanly distinguishing "information-limited" from "router-limited" failure modes.
- Honest disclosure of a retracted/qualified result (the seed-0-only +0.07 gate gain that does not survive 5 seeds) — rare and commendable in this corner of the field.
- Stratified analysis by local homophily, with the homophily axis held out from the agent — avoids averaging away the interesting regime where the GNN is *not* the best tool.
- Cross-family probing with a matched-English-scaffold language control, which prevents the obvious scaffold-language confound.
- Replication on a second graph (WikiCS) in a different domain with comparable homophily.
- Code, seeds, and results stated as released.

## Limitations

- **Narrow empirical scope.** 2 datasets (ogbn-arxiv, WikiCS), 1 model family (Qwen2.5) plus limited cross-family probing, 1 tool class (frozen 2-layer GCN for node classification), 1 prompt language (Chinese with faithful English translation). Generalization to other tools (retrieval, code execution, web search), other graph tasks (link prediction, generation), other LLM families at scale, or other agent scaffolds is unverified.
- **Capability ceiling.** Sweep stops at 7B (single-GPU constraint); whether the cost-of-deference trend continues, plateaus, or reverses at 14B/70B is unknown.
- **Action set for the oracle is fixed to {A1, A2, A4}.** Adding actions (e.g., a calibrated GNN with abstention, a neighbor-text variant of A2) would raise the oracle and change the gap's interpretation.
- **Single selective-invocation design family.** A purity gate and a 4-feature learned router are tested; richer designs (uncertainty-aware, RL-trained with a deferral action, meta-learned on held-out graphs, conformal abstention) are not. The paper acknowledges this and reports the empirical ceiling on the chosen features, but the stronger reading — "selective invocation is information-limited" — depends on this feature set.
- **No prompt-sensitivity or tool-naming analysis.** Robustness of the 97.6–99.2% agreement to prompt phrasing, tool names, or description order is not reported; given that the result is prompt-conditioned, this is a real gap.
- **Mechanism is observational, not isolated.** The paper does not run ablations to distinguish between SFT/tool-use priors, context dominance, or calibration collapse as drivers of the capability-deference correlation.
- **The agent's prompt instructs tool consultation.** The parrot finding is therefore about *adoption under compliant scaffolding*, not about generic tool-use behavior; reasonable, but worth flagging.
- **5-seed sample sizes are small per bin (50 nodes/bin on arxiv, 40 on WikiCS); per-bin SEs are non-negligible.**

## Relevance

> 与"LLM × 图/图神经网络"以及"智能体工具调用评估"两条主线高度相关:论文直接挑战了当前 agent+tool 系统评估中"智能体会判断性地调用外部模型"的隐含假设,并以严格对照实验+留出 oracle ceiling 的方式量化了"顺从代价"和"选择性调用的信息上限",对设计可靠的图增强 LLM 智能体、图检索增强系统以及任何"LLM+结构化预测器"流水线都具有方法论指导价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- **Mechanism ablation.** What fraction of the capability-deference trend is driven by (a) SFT/tool-use training data making "trust the tool" a strong prior, (b) tool output dominating the agent's context window and displacing its own reasoning, vs. (c) calibration collapse with scale? A simple ablation — same model with the tool output *omitted* from the observation — would bound (b).
- **Scaling beyond 7B.** Does the cost-of-deference trend continue, plateau, or reverse at 14B / 70B / reasoning-tuned models (e.g., Qwen2.5-14B-Instruct, DeepSeek-R1-distill)? Single-GPU constraints bound the present paper at 7B.
- **Other tool classes.** Does the parrot effect persist with retrieval tools (BM25 / dense), code-execution tools (Python sandbox), or web search? The paper scopes itself to a single tool class.
- **Other graph tasks.** Link prediction, graph classification, temporal graphs, and heterophilous TAGs (where the GNN is *weak by construction*) are all left to future work; the cost axis would be further stressed in those regimes.
- **Richer gates.** Can uncertainty-aware / RL-trained / conformal abstention / meta-learned routers close more than the ⅙–⅓ ceiling on the present four features? Or is the ceiling truly feature-limited, requiring new test-time signals (e.g., graph distance to training nodes, neighbor-text features, agent-side calibrated uncertainty)?
- **GNN-side levers.** If the GNN exposed a calibrated uncertainty head (rather than a reconstruction anomaly described to the agent in natural language), would the agent use it? Or is the parrot effect driven by *output formatting* (a single class label dominates the agent's representation) rather than by information availability?
- **Prompt-sensitivity.** Robustness of the 97.6–99.2% agreement to prompt phrasing, tool-naming, and description-order perturbations; ideally a controlled prompt ablation.
- **Cross-family generalization.** A larger cross-family sweep (Llama-3.x, Gemma-2, GPT-OSS, Claude-class where accessible, reasoning-tuned models) under matched scaffolds would clarify whether Qwen-specificity is a Qwen phenomenon or a "Chinese-instruction-tuned" phenomenon.
- **Beneficial variants.** Are there scaffold designs (e.g., explicit "you may disagree with the tool and explain why" instructions, or chain-of-thought-before-tool-call) that reduce deference without losing the tool's benefit? Or is the parrot effect robust to all such scaffolding?
