> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Mirage Probes 对比式探针框架，证明 VLM 的"无图也能答"mirage 行为在两个开源 VLM（Ovis2.5-2B 与 Qwen3-32B-VL-Instruct）的四类内部激活位点上都能被线性解码，并通过跨基准分离性差异 + 新的 Prior Harnessing Index (PHI) 拆出"虚假图像"和"文本偏置"两种内部表征机制。

> **TL;DR:** Vision-language models often answer image-conditioned questions correctly even when no real image is provided — "mirage" behavior that inflates benchmark scores. The paper asks whether such mirage behavior has a recoverable *internal* representation. It introduces **Mirage Probes**, a contrastive probing framework that pairs lightly paraphrased question variants with matched mirage / non-mirage labels on the same image, and trains four probe families (linear, 2-layer MLP, concatenated-layer linear, and difference-in-activations linear) on activations extracted at four sites (residual stream, MLP outputs, post-attention outputs, individual attention heads) in **Ovis2.5-2B** and **Qwen3-32B-VL-Instruct** across **VQA-RAD, MMMU-Pro, MedXpertQA**. Headline result: mirage behavior is **linearly decodable** — linear probes reach up to 75% / 73% (Ovis contrastive / all-examples) and 72% / 65% (Qwen), with **difference-in-activations probes up to 97.4% (Ovis) and 96.0% (Qwen)** on VQA-RAD, while 2-layer MLP probes do not meaningfully beat linear ones. A **Naive Bayes text baseline** stays well below the contrastive probe accuracy, ruling out surface lexical confounds. Cross-benchmark separability patterns, combined with the new **Prior Harnessing Index (PHI) = log p(a*|Q) − log p(a*|Q∅)**, support two distinct internal regimes: **spurious images** (model fabricates visual content in latent space; dominant on VQA-RAD) and **textual biases** (model answers from text priors without engaging vision; dominant on MMMU-Pro / MedXpertQA). The practical consequence: text-distribution cleaning can fix the textual-bias regime but cannot reach the spurious-image regime, so faithful visual grounding will require **representational-level interventions**.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Mirage Probes** 对比式探针框架：通过对同一图像上的问题进行轻度改写（4 次 GPT-4o-mini 改写）并配对 mirage / non-mirage 标签，在最大化语义/主题一致性的同时获得可靠的二分类监督信号。
- 在 Ovis2.5-2B 与 Qwen3-32B-VL-Instruct 两种规模/架构差异显著的开源 VLM 上，对**四种内部激活位点**（残差流、MLP 输出、后注意力输出、单个注意力头）进行系统探查；并用 **四类探针**（线性 Logistic 回归、双层 MLP、跨层拼接线性、with-vs-without-image 差分线性）测试 mirage 信息的编码形态。
- 证明 mirage 行为在所有四个激活位点上都被**线性可解码**（linear decodable），且 2-layer MLP 不显著优于线性探针 → mirage 信息以"可恢复的线性方向"形式存在，而非需要非线性提取。
- **Difference-in-activations**（同一问题在有图 vs 无图条件下的激活差分）作为探针特征取得最强 mirage 分类准确率：在 VQA-RAD 上 Ovis 高达 97.4%、Qwen 96.0%，表明视觉输入引起的表征漂移方向本身就编码了 mirage 信息。
- 引入 **Naive Bayes 文本基线**（训练在响应文本上）作为对照，证明对比式探针的信号**不可还原为表层词汇/主题特征**：在所有 model × benchmark × target 配置中，对比式线性探针准确率都高于 Naive Bayes 基线。
- 提出 **Prior Harnessing Index (PHI) = log p(a*|Q) − log p(a*|Q∅)**，量化模型仅凭问题文本就能提升正确答案置信度的程度，并将人类"图像依赖性"标注与 VLM 自身的图像依赖性区分开。
- 基于跨基准分离性差异 + PHI 相关性，提出 mirage 行为实际是**两种内部表征机制**的混合：
  - **虚假图像（spurious images）**：模型在潜空间中构造并不存在的视觉内容并据此作答；主要出现在 VQA-RAD（PHI 与 mirage 相关性 = −0.36，问题本身不提供足够文本先验）。
  - **文本偏置（textual biases）**：模型完全忽略视觉输入，仅凭文本先验作答；出现在 MMMU-Pro（PHI=+1.20）和 MedXpertQA（PHI=+0.30）。
- 关键消融：vision encoder 与 projection 层在所有探针策略下都未携带可检测的 mirage 信号 → 信号位于语言解码器内部，而非视觉前端。
- 关键消融：把 ambiguous 样本折入 mirage 类、或者改用 Asadi et al. (2026) 的 B-Clean 标签标准，都会显著降低探针准确率 → 标签构造方案对结论稳健性至关重要。

## Key Contributions

- **Mirage Probes framework.** A contrastive probing setup that pairs lightly paraphrased variants of the same image-conditioned question with matched mirage / non-mirage labels, isolating the mirage signal from generic topical, lexical, and length confounds.
- **Systematic representation-level diagnosis across two VLMs and four activation sites.** Ovis2.5-2B and Qwen3-32B-VL-Instruct are probed at the residual stream, MLP output, post-attention output, and individual attention head at every layer. Vision encoder and projection layers are probed first and contribute no detectable signal — a useful negative result.
- **Linear-decodability of mirage behavior.** Across all four sites, logistic-regression probes attain non-trivial mirage classification accuracy; 2-layer MLP probes do **not** meaningfully beat linear ones, consistent with mirage information being encoded along recoverable linear directions.
- **Difference-in-activations is the cleanest probe.** Training a linear probe on the elementwise difference between with-image and without-image activations reaches up to **97.4% (Ovis) / 96.0% (Qwen)** on VQA-RAD, and remains the strongest strategy across benchmarks. This makes the visual-evoked activation shift itself a strong mirage feature.
- **Naive Bayes text control.** A bag-of-words Naive Bayes classifier trained on response text attains 45–66% on the contrastive split and 67–81% on the all-examples split; contrastive linear probes exceed it everywhere, ruling out surface lexical confounds as the dominant explanation.
- **Prior Harnessing Index (PHI).** A model-internal metric defined as `PHI(Q) = log p(a*|Q) − log p(a*|Q∅)`, measuring how much question text alone raises the model's confidence in the correct answer. Used to operationalize *VLM* image-reliance (as opposed to human image-reliance).
- **Two-mechanism hypothesis.** Spurious images vs. textual biases are proposed as two distinct internal regimes that share the same behavioral output. Evidence: (i) VQA-RAD is the only benchmark where mirage examples are nearly all human-annotated as image-reliant and where PHI strongly anti-correlates with mirage labels (r = −0.36 in Ovis); (ii) on MMMU-Pro and MedXpertQA, perceived image-reliance filtering does *not* improve probe separability (58–61% range), but PHI does separate the regimes.
- **Mitigation asymmetry.** Text-distribution cleaning is argued to address textual-bias mirages but not spurious-image mirages, since the latter live inside the visual representation of the language decoder. The authors stop short of demonstrating a representational-level fix; this is positioned as future work.

## Methodology

### Mirage behavior definition
For a VLM M, an example is `(x, q)`, with with-image response `r_img = M(x, q)` and without-image response `r_∅ = M(∅, q)`. Mirage behavior occurs when the model produces a confident, image-grounded answer despite the image being absent. The mirage label `y ∈ {0, 1}` for the with-image response is a *behavioral proxy* derived by comparing `r_img` and `r_∅`.

### Labeling scheme
A response is **mirage-like** if with-image and without-image answers match *and* their bag-of-words cosine similarity > 0.7. A response is **non-mirage-like** if the without-image generation explicitly expresses uncertainty or says answering is impossible (regex check). An **ambiguous** label is assigned when the without-image response is itself mirage-like but the with-image answer diverges. The authors tried folding ambiguous → mirage, and an alternative B-Clean labeling (Asadi et al., 2026); both significantly reduced probe accuracy, so the primary scheme is retained.

### Contrastive pair construction
Each base question is mutated **4 times with GPT-4o-mini** (light paraphrase, meaning-preserving, no new information). For each base + its 4 mutations, with-image and without-image responses are generated. If the resulting 5 responses contain ≥ 1 mirage-like and ≥ 1 non-mirage-like label, **one contrastive pair** is drawn (same image, near-identical question, opposite labels). At most one pair per base question to preserve diversity. Two dataset variants are built per model:
- **Contrastive-pairs**: only the contrastive pairs.
- **All-examples**: every mirage / non-mirage response, capped at 1 per class per base question.

### Models and benchmarks
- **Ovis2.5-2B** (Lu et al., 2024) — small VLM with structured embedding alignment.
- **Qwen3-32B-VL-Instruct** (Bai et al., 2025) — 32B-scale VLM.
- **GLM-4.6-flash** (Team GLM, 2024) — probed but **dropped** from main results because it produces too few non-mirage responses on three of four benchmarks.
- Benchmarks: **VQA-RAD**, **MMMU-Pro**, **MedXpertQA**, **MicroVQA** (the last dropped from main results for the same reason).

### Activation extraction
- Sites: residual stream, MLP output, post-attention output, individual attention head output, at every layer.
- Token aggregations (after Theodoridis et al., 2026): **text-nonspecial-mean**, **vision-tail**, **vision-text-mean**.
- Vision encoder and projection layer activations were probed first and contributed no detectable mirage signal — dropped from main analysis.
- Conversations are chat-template tokenized, model generates, then a second pass is run for activation recording.

### Probe families
- **Logistic regression (LogReg)** on per-layer activations. Standard linear probe.
- **2-layer MLP** (hidden=512, GELU). Tests whether mirage information needs a nonlinear transform.
- **Concat (cross-layer linear probe)**: linear probe on the concatenation of activations from all layers; tests whether information is distributed across layers rather than localized.
- **Diff (difference-in-activations linear probe)**: linear probe on the elementwise difference between with-image and without-image activations of the same example. Tests whether the visual-evoked representational shift itself encodes mirage.

### Experimental setup
- Adam, lr=0.03, train-set feature normalization; L2 sweep ∈ {0, 1, 10, 100}; 5 random seeds × 3 random inits × 4 L2 values = 12 probes per seed, best selected by validation accuracy (early stopping, max 800 epochs).
- Residual probes: 5 seeded splits; for the contrastive setting, evaluation is averaged over 5 additional held-out dataset seeds.
- MLP / attention probes: 3 seeds, 3 random inits, drop the 3-init loop; for attention, report best head and post-attention at every layer.
- Token-positions excluded: with-image responses shorter than 10 tokens.
- **PHI(Q) = log p(a*|Q) − log p(a*|Q∅)**, where p(a*|Q∅) is the model's correct-answer probability under a null prompt "What is the answer?".

## Results & Highlights

### Linear decodability (Table 1)
On the contrastive-pairs split with logistic regression on residual-stream activations:
- **Ovis**: VQA-RAD 75.00%, MMMU-Pro 65.00%, MedXpertQA 68.00%, All 72.20%.
- **Qwen**: VQA-RAD 72.00%, MMMU-Pro 57.80%, MedXpertQA 57.20%, All 61.20%.
Attention-head / post-attention targets are similar (Ovis up to 74%, Qwen up to 81%). Across contrastive settings, **MLP and Concat probes do not significantly outperform LogReg**, supporting linear encoding.

### Difference-in-activations is the strongest probe
- **Ovis contrastive Diff**: VQA-RAD **97.40%**, MMMU-Pro 90.80%, MedXpertQA 71.00%, All 91.80%.
- **Qwen contrastive Diff**: VQA-RAD **96.00%**, MMMU-Pro 79.40%, MedXpertQA 76.20%, All 77.40%.
This is the cleanest demonstration that the visual-evoked activation shift is itself a mirage feature.

### Naive Bayes text control (Table 2)
- Contrastive Naive Bayes: Ovis 57.7–66.7%, Qwen 45.5–59.1%.
- All-examples Naive Bayes: Ovis 67.5–81.1%, Qwen 72.8–81.5%.
Crucially, **contrastive linear probes exceed Naive Bayes in every model × benchmark cell**, ruling out surface lexical confounds. In the all-examples setting, MMMU-Pro and MedXpertQA probe gains are mirrored by Naive Bayes gains — those gains are likely lexical, not mechanistic.

### Two mechanisms (Tables 3 & 4)
- VQA-RAD is the only benchmark where mirage-like examples are almost all human-annotated as image-reliant (2909 image-reliant vs 60 image-free among mirages, vs 1139/836 on MMMU-Pro and 2413/2399 on MedXpertQA).
- **Ovis PHI × mirage correlation**: VQA-RAD **−0.36** (strong anti-correlation), MMMU-Pro −0.23, MedXpertQA +0.14.
- **Mean PHI**: VQA-RAD −0.41, MMMU-Pro +1.20, MedXpertQA +0.30. VQA-RAD is the only benchmark where question text alone does *not* raise confidence.
- Filtering training examples by human image-reliance does not improve MMMU-Pro / MedXpertQA probe accuracy (~58–61%), implying the two regimes coexist in those benchmarks.
- **Synthesis**: VQA-RAD mirages are dominated by **spurious images** (model fabricates visual content); MMMU-Pro / MedXpertQA mirages are a mixture of **spurious images** and **textual biases**.

### Negative / ablations
- Vision encoder and projection layer activations carry **no detectable mirage signal** under any probing strategy.
- GLM-4.6-flash and MicroVQA are dropped from the main results due to insufficient non-mirage examples.
- Difference-in-activations requires a matched no-image generation per example, which is a stronger experimental cost than per-activation probing.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The headline conceptual contribution — splitting mirage behavior into two representational regimes — is the most important part of the paper. The Mirage Probes framework itself is a sensible instantiation of standard contrastive-probe methodology (Burns et al., 2024; Marks & Tegmark, 2024). The PHI metric is useful but closely related to existing "text-only accuracy" baselines (e.g., the no-image condition in POPE); its marginal contribution is operational rather than conceptual.
- **Rigor.** The Naive Bayes control is a clean disconfound for surface lexical confounds, but a stronger text baseline (e.g., a transformer-based text encoder) would be more convincing. The contrastive-vs-all-examples comparison is well-designed and is the strongest evidence that the *contrastive* signal is mechanistic rather than lexical. The "two mechanisms" claim is explicitly framed as a hypothesis, not a causal finding, and the authors call for steering / ablation / activation-patching follow-up — a responsible framing.
- **Reproducibility.** The framework is small-scale and conceptual, and the paper says "Code is available here" in the abstract, but the actual link, dataset release, and probe weights should be verified. Final per-model dataset sizes are deferred to Appendix A, and the strict "one pair per base question" + "≥ 10 token response" filters will produce very small datasets, especially for Ovis — robustness across seeds is therefore essential (and the paper reports 5 seeded splits, which is reasonable).
- **Significance.** The two-regime taxonomy has direct practical value: it tells practitioners that the common "clean the text distribution" fix is mechanism-incomplete. It also opens a clear path to follow-on work (e.g., a probe-based training-time diagnostic, or causal interventions to suppress spurious-image mirages). This is a useful reframing for the broader VLM-evaluation community.
- **Red flags / things to check in the full paper.**
  1. The "two mechanisms" claim rests on cross-benchmark separability differences plus PHI correlations; without causal validation (steering, ablations, activation patching) the claim is correlational. The authors are honest about this.
  2. GLM-4.6-flash and MicroVQA are dropped from main results due to "too few non-mirage responses" — this *could* be a model-bias confound (models with strong text priors won't produce enough non-mirage examples for probing) and is worth scrutinizing in the appendix.
  3. PHI novelty: the metric is `log p(a*|Q) − log p(a*|Q∅)`, which is essentially a length-normalized measure of how much the question text raises the model's correct-answer logit. It will correlate strongly with existing text-only accuracy baselines; the paper should clarify what PHI captures that those do not.
  4. Vision encoder and projection layers carrying "no detectable mirage signal" is a strong negative claim — the choice of probe family and aggregation could matter; the paper should report at least the linear-probe accuracy on those sites.
  5. Difference-in-activations is the strongest probe but also the most "leaky" — it directly leverages the same with-vs-without comparison used to *define* the mirage label. The very high accuracy is therefore expected, and the interesting question is whether *non-difference* probes (the linear / MLP / concat ones) are still informative — which they are, but at a more modest level (60–75%).
  6. The paper does not claim, and does not demonstrate, a representational-level intervention that fixes spurious-image mirages. The mitigation consequence is aspirational.

## Strengths

- **Clean conceptual framing.** Splitting mirage into two regimes with different mitigation profiles is directly actionable for both researchers and practitioners.
- **Strong controls.** Naive Bayes text baseline explicitly disconfounds a surface-lexical explanation; the contrastive-vs-all-examples comparison is the cleanest ablation in the paper.
- **Multi-site, multi-probe systematic study.** Tests four activation sites × four probe families × two models × three benchmarks, with the strongest probe (Diff) cleanly separated from the rest.
- **Useful negative result.** Vision encoder and projection layers carry no detectable mirage signal — the signal lives in the language decoder's residual stream, which narrows where future interventions should be targeted.
- **Hypothesis, not overclaim.** The two-mechanism account is explicitly framed as a hypothesis motivating future causal work; the authors are honest about the lack of causal validation.
- **Practical takeaway.** Practitioners get a clear message: text-bias fixes are necessary but insufficient; representational-level interventions are needed.
- **Strong senior authorship.** Lipson + Shwartz-Ziv lend weight, and the institutional mix (Columbia, NYU, Intuit, Technion, Thoughtworks) is plausible for a multi-modal interpretability study.

## Limitations

- **Model coverage.** Only Ovis2.5-2B and Qwen3-32B-VL-Instruct are reported in the main results; GLM-4.6-flash and MicroVQA are dropped due to insufficient non-mirage examples. Conclusions about commercial frontier VLMs (GPT-4o, Gemini, Claude Vision) are not addressed.
- **Causal claims absent.** "Two mechanisms" is a hypothesis based on cross-benchmark separability differences and PHI correlations. No steering, ablation, or activation-patching experiments are reported.
- **Heuristic labels.** Mirage / non-mirage / ambiguous labels depend on a bag-of-words cosine threshold and a regex check; label noise is acknowledged but not quantified.
- **Probe interpretability ceiling.** Linear decodability identifies *where* the signal lives, not *how* the model uses it. A linear probe can read out a feature without that feature being the model's decision pathway.
- **Small contrastive datasets.** The strict "one pair per base question" + ≥ 10 token filters yield very small datasets, especially for Ovis; per-seed variance is a real concern.
- **No demonstrated mitigation.** The paper argues that representational-level interventions are needed for spurious-image mirages but does not propose or test one.
- **PHI novelty is partial.** The metric overlaps with existing text-only baselines; its marginal value should be argued more explicitly.
- **Difference-in-activations probe is leaky.** It leverages the same with-vs-without comparison used to define the mirage label; its very high accuracy is partly an artifact of the construction.
- **No private-model or safety-domain validation.** Despite arguing the work matters for safety-critical domains (medical imaging), the paper does not evaluate on the most safety-relevant regime (e.g., a clinically deployed VLM).

## Relevance

与你的研究方向高度相关：你长期关注 VLM 的可靠性、机制可解释性和"模型真懂还是假懂"的评估问题。本文把"无图也能答"的 mirage 行为从单一现象拆为两种内部表征机制（虚假图像 vs 文本偏置），并给出可直接测量的探针框架与 PHI 指标，是直接回应 VLM 评估可信度问题的工作，对你设计 ground-truth-anchored 的多模态评测很有借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the two mirage regimes (spurious image vs. textual bias) generalize to proprietary frontier VLMs (GPT-4o, Gemini, Claude Vision), and how does their relative prevalence shift with model scale and post-training recipe?
- Can a causal intervention — attention-head ablation, activation patching, or representation editing — selectively suppress spurious-image mirages while preserving genuine visual grounding, providing the missing causal validation for the two-mechanism hypothesis?
- Is PHI essentially a re-scaled text-only accuracy baseline, or does it capture additional information about how the *internal* probability mass is distributed? A head-to-head correlation study against POPE's no-image condition and other text-only baselines would clarify its marginal value.
- Are mirage signals concentrated in a small set of attention heads (a "mirage circuit"), analogous to prior LLM work on hallucination / sycophancy circuits, and can such a circuit be localized and edited?
- Can Mirage Probes be repurposed as a *training-time diagnostic* — e.g., to detect when a VLM begins to over-rely on textual priors during RLHF / preference fine-tuning — rather than only as post-hoc analysis?
- How do the two regimes interact with chain-of-thought, tool use, or explicit "no image provided" instructions? Does CoT reveal that the model "knows" it is constructing a false image, or does the fabrication stay implicit?
- Does the two-regime taxonomy predict cross-domain transfer of mirage behavior? For example, do models with high spurious-image mirage rates on VQA-RAD also fail on other safety-critical imaging tasks (pathology, satellite, document analysis)?
- How robust is the contrastive probe signal to paraphrase quality? If the four GPT-4o-mini mutations are replaced with a stronger or weaker paraphraser, do the absolute probe accuracies change materially, or does the *contrastive vs. all-examples* gap remain stable?
- Can the difference-in-activation direction identified by the Diff probe be used as a steering vector — pushing the model away from spurious-image mirages at inference time?
- How does the contrastive pair-design (one pair per base question, near-identical paraphrases) compare to counterfactual data augmentation (e.g., image-swap, content-word substitution) as a way to control surface confounds?
