⚠ Risk Shadow

When 99.9999% Variance Preservation Causes Catastrophic Errors

Hamidou Tembine · arXiv:2606.14533 · 2026-06-15

PCA can retain more than 99.9999% of total variance while completely erasing rare-event signals — forcing any downstream classifier to collapse to a constant predictor. The paper proposes three tail-risk-aware alternatives, with exp2PCA cutting operational risk to under one-sixth on ten real high-stakes datasets.

Key Finding

PCA is structurally blind to rare events: it can preserve virtually all variance while setting I(Y; PCA(X)) = 0.

📉

Key Metric

4–13×

Reduction in 0.995-expectile tail risk with exp2PCA across ten real-world benchmarks; headline RERP up to 890%.

🚨

Why This Matters

Fraud, medical imaging, and intrusion-detection pipelines routinely run PCA first — often deleting the signals that matter most.

🔪

Critical Edge

exp2PCA is distribution-free optimal on the Stiefel-classifier manifold, yet the paper is linear-only, omits raw-feature baselines, and releases no code.

The Risk Shadow Mechanism

Variance is an unconditional second-moment; misclassification cost is a conditional decision-theoretic quantity. When rare-event labels live in the low-variance subspace SR, PCA discards them by design.

  • Theorem 1–3: explicit latent-factor model where Y depends only on discarded components, yielding I(Y; PrX) = 0.
  • Theorem 2: Bayes-optimal classifier on the PCA embedding collapses to a constant predictor.
  • Theorem 5: a Fréchet expansion bounds the Grassmannian rotation between PCA and expectile-aware subspaces.

Three Alternatives

Tail-Preserving PCA

Supervised sample reweighting of the covariance matrix, giving rare-class samples higher mass.

⚠ Centering trap: on some data no positive α can rescue the target axis (Th. 9).

Expectile PCA

Minimizes the τ-expectile of reconstruction error, equivalent to an asymmetric indicator-weighted covariance.

✓ Strictly improves on PCA when latent copula has positive upper-tail dependence (Th. 10).

Expectile of Misclassification Cost

Jointly optimizes projection P and classifier h over the Stiefel-classifier manifold to directly minimize downstream tail cost.

✓ Distribution-free strict dominance; Bayes-optimal (Th. 6 & 11).

Empirical Validation

Ten public datasets under a 100:1 false-negative cost ratio and τ = 0.995. exp2PCA achieves the lowest R0.995 on every dataset and the highest AUC on 9/10.

Accountability Metrics

Three decision-theoretic audit metrics quantify the price of using PCA:

  • RPB — multiplicative tail-risk penalty vs. exp2PCA.
  • RAI — accountability vs. the uncompressed baseline.
  • RERP — relative excess risk percentage; analytic example yields 890%.

Critical Assessment

The “Risk Shadow” framing is memorable and the strict-dominance theorem for exp2PCA is unusually strong. Yet several caveats temper the headline:

  • Missing baseline: the paper never compares to “just use raw features,” the obvious null hypothesis.
  • Linear-only: no nonlinear embeddings, kernels, or deep nets — limiting for X-rays and time series.
  • Fixed cost ratio: sensitivity to 5:1, 20:1, or 200:1 is not analyzed.
  • No code release: despite a promise of “complete source repositories,” no link is given.
  • 890% RERP is a synthetic upper-bound example, not a typical observed value.
