In VLM backbones, ~100 attention heads (8.7%) track the image region being described — and a single attention-mask edit steers answers to any chosen region at 83.1% accuracy, no retraining required.

Gaze Heads: How VLMs Look at What They Describe

Rohit Gandikota, David Bau  ·  Khoury College of Computer Sciences, Northeastern University

vision-language-models mechanistic-interpretability attention-heads inference-time-steering multimodal-alignment

Key Metric

83.1%

VQA accuracy steering Qwen3-VL-8B to any chosen comic panel — vs. 16.7% chance. Achieved by editing only the top-100 of 1,152 heads.

Why This Matters

A retraining-free lever for controlling VLM behavior — directly applicable to safety, hallucination correction, and VLM architecture choices. The frozen-vs-jointly-trained encoder contrast has real implications for how VLMs should be built.

"高度匹配多模态模型可解释性与可控生成方向：既是 VLM 内部机制的因果级证据，也是一种无需重训练的实用多模态行为控制杠杆。"

Critical Edge

On natural COCO images, accuracy drops to 61.7% on small objects. The K=100 peak is sharp — gains fall 8–10pp at K=150 or K=200 — and the trained-encoder hypothesis is supported only by suggestive Bunny-vs-Ovis evidence, not a controlled ablation.

Methodology

  1. Comics as testbed. Six-panel comic strips encode narrative order spatially (left → right), giving a ground-truth trace of "which region the model is describing at each token." 500 strips for discovery, 500 GPT-4o-generated for validation.
  2. Layer localization. A residual-stream "read-in-reverse" experiment flips predicted panel labels sharply only at layers 20–28 — isolating the visual routing band without any training.
  3. Gaze score. For each head, mean of the diagonal of a 6×6 (queried-panel × attended-panel) attention matrix over 500 forward passes. No labels, no training — just a correlation score.
  4. Causal intervention. Add a +∞ bias to the pre-softmax attention mask on the top-100 heads: +δ on the target panel's image tokens, −δ elsewhere. Text attention untouched. Nothing else modified.
  5. Generalization. Re-run across Qwen3-VL sizes (2B → 32B), other VLM families (Ovis1.5, Qwen2-VL, InternVL3.5, LLaVA, Bunny), and natural COCO images with bounding-box targets.

Clean separation: all three trained-encoder families plateau at ≥60%, all three frozen-encoder families fail. The Bunny-vs-Ovis contrast (same SigLIP-so400m — frozen in Bunny at 8.3%, fine-tuned in Ovis at 68.7%) is the strongest single natural experiment for the trained-encoder hypothesis.

Strengths

  • Elegant, low-cost discovery — a simple correlation score from a few forward passes, no labels, no training.
  • Unusually clean causal evidence: random-heads fail (14.6%), all-heads destroy generation (0.9%), no-intervention is the baseline — the effect is triangulated.
  • Two independent probes (residual-stream steering + gaze score) converge on the same layer band 20–28.
  • Cross-architecture and cross-scale validation, plus a clean extension from comics to natural COCO images.
  • Bunny-vs-Ovis same-backbone contrast isolates the encoder-training variable.
  • Full public release: code, interactive demo, datasets, generator scripts at gaze.baulab.info.

Limitations

  • Comic testbed is highly structured; natural-image performance degrades to 61.7% on small COCO objects.
  • The K=100 peak is sharp — accuracy drops to 75.6% at K=150 and 73.0% at K=200. Users must re-sweep K per model.
  • Bunny-Ovis contrast is suggestive but not a controlled ablation; trained-encoder hypothesis remains unproven.
  • Not all VLM families exhibit the mechanism (LLaVA, Bunny); structural conditions for gaze-head formation are not fully characterized.
  • No comparison to broader steering methods (activation steering, contrastive decoding, prompt engineering) — only within-attention-edit baselines.
  • Compute / latency cost of the intervention at deployment is not discussed. LLM-judge dependency (Claude Sonnet) is a non-trivial external reliance.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does gaze-head intervention compose with activation steering, contrastive decoding, or prompt engineering — additive, redundant, or interfering?
  • Can gaze-head steering correct hallucination — force the model to look at the right region when it's confidently wrong?
  • Does the mechanism extend to video VLMs, where "where is the model looking" gains a temporal axis? Per-frame, per-clip, or persistent gaze heads?
  • What is the relationship to mid-layer cross-modal transfer and emergent visual-symbolic indexing — same phenomenon, different cut?
  • Can the intervention support negative steering — preventing the model from describing a sensitive region — and is it per-token / per-region reversible?
  • What is the intervention's latency profile in deployment, and can a learned gate approximate which heads to redirect?
