Recursively Trained Diffusion Models: Limiting Collapse Distribution and Spectral Characterization

Na'il B. Khelifa Richard E. Turner Ramji Venkataramanan arXiv:2606.13796

Even with perfect scores and exact sampling, truncating the reverse SDE at t₀ > 0 is the lone mechanism that drives recursive diffusion training into a closed-form collapse distribution — and annealing that truncation back to zero recovers the true data distribution.

κ = √(1 − αe−t₀/2)

Mechanism

The Collapse Fixed Point

At each round, a fraction α of real data is mixed with (1−α) synthetic samples and smoothed by the OU semigroup for time t₀. The recursion has a unique fixed point:

p*∞ = α Σ_{k=0}^∞ (1−α)^k U^{(k+1)t₀}(p_data)

It converges geometrically at rate κ = √(1 − αe^{−t₀/2}). Smaller α or larger t₀ means faster convergence to the wrong distribution.

Spectral View

Hermite Low-Pass Filter

Expanding the limit density in the multivariate Hermite basis diagonalizes the OU operator. Each mode n is attenuated by:

m_n(α,t₀) = αe^{−|n|t₀/2} / [1 − (1−α)e^{−|n|t₀/2}]
low |n| preservedhigh |n| suppressed
Prescription

Annealed Truncation

Shrinking the truncation schedule across generations removes the fixed-point bias. Any schedule with t₀^(i) → 0 asymptotically recovers the data distribution exactly:

t₀^(i) = t₀ / (1+i)^β   ⇒   KL → 0

The β family trades finite-round speed against the rate at which residual bias vanishes.

Robustness

Error Ellipsoid

When score-estimation and discretization errors are bundled as one-step W₂ perturbations δ_i, the recursion stays inside a W₂ ball of radius δ/(1−κ) around p*∞.

Mode-wise contraction κ_n = (1−α)e^{−|n|t₀/2} is far smaller than κ, so high-frequency errors self-correct faster than low-frequency errors.

Empirical Validation

Experiments

  • 2D Gaussian mixtures: confirm the Neumann-series limit and the KL collapse/plateau predicted by fixed vs. annealed truncation schedules.
  • CIFAR-10, 8 generations, α ∈ {0.1,0.3,0.5,0.7,0.9}: FID to p*∞ decays geometrically with κ; high-frequency deficit grows fastest at small α and large N.
  • Practical signal: α = 0.9 keeps high-frequency deficit below 0.15 across all 8 generations.
Critical Assessment

Strengths, Limits & Open Questions

  • NovelFirst closed-form fixed point and spectral characterization of recursive diffusion collapse.
  • UsefulAnnealed truncation is diffusion-specific and complementary to the usual α-based mitigations.
  • IdealizedAssumes A1–A4: smooth positive density, finite second moment, L² score, Gaussian-reference density. Heavy tails and non-isotropic references are not covered.
  • NarrowOnly variance-preserving OU diffusion studied; no flow matching, rectified flow, consistency models, language, or high-resolution images.
  • OpenAdaptive annealing rules, finite-round dimension-aware bounds, and per-frequency score corrections remain future work.
