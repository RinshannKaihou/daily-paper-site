Differentiating Eulerian and Lagrangian Tendencies in the Ocean Interior via a Dynamical Overturning Decomposition

A single-author study closes the density-space identity ψ_diapy = ψ_Euler − ψ_slosh and shows that Eulerian and Lagrangian tendencies can point in opposite directions across the Indo-Pacific, Atlantic, and South China Sea.

Key Finding

Eulerian trends can hide opposite Lagrangian transformation

Across all three basins, the sign of reversible isopycnal heaving reverses the apparent water-mass trend revealed by irreversible diapycnal transport.

Key Metric

~2.5 Sv interior diapycnal downwelling

Indo-Pacific intermediate cell at 45.88–45.92 σ₄, bracketed by upwelling cells and cross-validated against Monkman & Jansen (2024).

Why This Matters

Separates sloshing from real water-mass change

Climate records often conflate adiabatic isopycnal heaving with diabatic transformation; this decomposition is a diagnostic tool for long-term ocean signals.

Critical Edge

No uncertainty propagation; ECCO may under-mix

Single reanalysis per basin, time-mean treated as trend, and 1° resolution cannot resolve bottom-boundary-layer dynamics.

Methodology

Two reanalyses are matched to basin scale: ECCO v4r3 (1°, 1992–2015, residual streamfunction including bolus velocity) for the Atlantic and Indo-Pacific, and GLORYS12 (1/12°, 1993–2024) for the deep South China Sea.

  • Advective MOC ψ_adv(y,σ,t) from zonally-integrated meridional velocity in density space.
  • Sloshing MOC ψ_slosh from isopycnal depth evolution, bounded by ψ_adv at open boundaries.
  • Diapycnal MOC ψ_diapy = ψ_adv − ψ_slosh; positive = Lagrangian lightening, negative = densification.
  • Volume-budget identity: ψ_diapy = ψ_adv(y₁) − ψ_adv(y₀) − T_hv.
  • Consistency check: advective vs. sloshing MOC correlation R = 0.81–0.92.

Strengths

  • Clean physical interpretation of each component.
  • Multi-basin, multi-product generality test.
  • Explicit Indo-Pacific diapycnal downwelling cell.
  • Honest framing vs. Monkman & Jansen (2024).

Results & Highlights

Indo-Pacific (σ₄): Triple-cell structure — upper upwelling, an intermediate downwelling cell (~45.88–45.92 σ₄, ~3,500–4,500 m), and lower upwelling from AABW consumption.

Atlantic (σ₂): Basin-wide Eulerian lightening masks a qualitatively similar triple-cell. A sub-overturning cell in the upper AMOC is hypothesized to reflect subarctic intermediate water formation.

South China Sea (σ₂): Eulerian densification masks net Lagrangian lightening south of 17°N, driven by adiabatic heaving from Pacific inflow through the Luzon Strait.

3 of 4 theoretical regimes observed R = 0.81–0.92 consistency ~2.5 Sv Indo-Pacific downwelling

Limitations

  • No formal uncertainty quantification.
  • Time-mean used as long-term tendency.
  • Requires model velocity fields.
  • Linear interpolation sensitivity unexplored.
  • 1° ECCO cannot resolve bottom boundary layers.

Significance

The most concrete contribution is the explicit Indo-Pacific diapycnal downwelling cell and its link to the Munk abyssal-recipes paradox. The single-reanalysis, single-author nature and lack of uncertainty propagation keep the empirical claims bounded.
