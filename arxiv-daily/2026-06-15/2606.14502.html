From Chatbot to Digital Colleague

The paradigm shift toward persistent autonomous AI — a 70-page synthesis that reframes the LLM-agent transition as two coupled axes: a cognitive core (stateless → deliberative) and a tool-execution layer (episodic → persistent workstation).

Yongheng Zhang, Ziang Liu, Jiaxuan Zhu, Shuai Wang, Xiangqi Chen, Haojing Huang, Jiayi Kuang, Siyu Chen, Ao Shen, Hao Wu, Qiufeng Wang, Qian-Wen Zhang, Junnan Dong, Wenhao Jiang, Ying Shen, Hai-Tao Zheng, Yinghui Li, Di Yin, Xing Sun, Philip S. Yu
Tencent Youtu Lab · Tsinghua · Sun Yat-sen · CSU · UIUC
"Workspace + Skill is the mechanism that turns conversation into task closure — leaving a correct, auditable, repeatable final state."

★ Four headline assets
Key Finding
Workspace + Skill turns conversation into task closure
The leap from Chatbot → Digital Colleague is not "more capable LLM" but a durable workspace plus reusable skill packages (e.g. SKILL.md). The Agent Era delivers useful intermediate actions; the OpenClaw Era delivers a correct, inspectable, recoverable final state.
Key Metric
2.4s → 718.8 min
~18,000× in 7 years
Median length of coding tasks frontier agents can complete: GPT-2 (2019) → Opus 4.6 (early 2026). Empirical anchor for the Chatbot → Digital Colleague narrative.
Why This Matters
Evaluation must shift from answer correctness → task closure
A 4-stage evaluation migration: (I) output accuracy → (II) LLM-as-Judge for long chains → (III) task closure rate → (IV) workspace/OpenClaw capability + safety. Long-horizon benchmarks (SWE-bench, OSWorld, Terminal-Bench) become the new ground truth.
Critical Edge
"OpenClaw" is the authors' own product
The era's exemplar is Tencent Youtu Lab's own workstation framework. Several 2026 model names in the paper's timeline (MiniMax M2.7, GLM-5.1, GPT-5.5, Opus 4.6) read as projections. The framing is naturally slanted to validate Tencent's positioning — treat the OpenClaw case study as marketing-flavored.

▍ Data + Evaluation paradigm shift
two coupled migrations, same evolutionary lens
DATA →
Stage I
Instruction–response SFT pairs. Static supervised signals. The Chatbot era's fuel.
Stage II
Long CoT traces + Process Reward Models (PRM). Reasoning-by-demonstration. Powers o1, o3.
Stage III
State–Action–Observation trajectories. Records a runnable path through a workspace. The OpenClaw era's fuel.
EVALUATION →
Stage I
Final-output accuracy on static benchmarks. Single-shot QA / MMLU / HumanEval.
Stage II
LLM-as-Judge over long reasoning chains. Detects wrong-step-right-answer failure mode.
Stage III
Task closure rate on long-horizon benchmarks: SWE-bench, Terminal-Bench, OSWorld.
Stage IV
Workspace-level capability + safety. Runtime permissions, audit logs, sandbox escapes, ClawGuard-style forensics.
▍ Six open challenges for self-evolving AI
research agenda for the next 2–3 years of agent work
01
Long-horizon reliability
When tasks span hours, verification has to outpace drift. Without reliable closure, the "Digital Colleague" thesis collapses.
02
Safety & governance
Runtime permissions, provenance, audit logs. PRISM, ClawGuard, Agentic Forensics — mostly agenda, no protocol yet.
03
Human–AI collaboration
When the colleague persists, where does authorship, accountability, and override live?
04
Memory & context
Workspaces must remember across sessions. But what gets retained, summarized, forgotten?
05
Beyond-gradient learning
Skills accumulated through execution, not just SGD. Underspecified; needs empirical mechanism.
06
Composable skills & multi-agent
A market for skills; teammates not just tools. Rethinks the unit of agent design.

▍ Critical assessment · strengths & limits
what the paper nails vs. where the framing is shaky
Strengths
Clean 2D framework. Pedagogically useful — would serve a graduate seminar or product strategy doc. The 7-axis boundary table gives practitioners a concrete checklist. The time-horizon chart is the most accessible single image of agent-capability growth 2019–2026.
Limits
No new empirical experiments. The empirical centerpiece is borrowed from theaidigest.org. No head-to-head SWE-bench comparison of tool-calling vs workstation agents, so the central claim is unfalsified.
Red Flag
Self-promotion risk. "OpenClaw" doubles as the era's exemplar and the authors' commercial product. Forward-dated references — many 2025–2026 model names (MiniMax M2.7, GLM-5.1, GPT-5.5, Opus 4.6) read as projections; readers should spot-check the bibliography.
Open Qs
Is OpenClaw open-source? How does the time-horizon decompose per-domain (coding vs web vs OS)? What evaluation distinguishes true self-evolution from a self-editing codebase?
