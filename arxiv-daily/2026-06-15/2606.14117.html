A Two-Stage Statistical Framework for Evaluating Associative Interference in Large Language Models

Achraf Cohen · Andrew Kincaid · University of West Florida · 2026-06-15

Headline: Associative interference is model-specific, not a universal LLM property. Under a forced-choice IAT protocol, Claude Sonnet-4 shows credible Gender–Career interference (ΔP = 0.086), Gemini 2.5 Pro is attenuated, and GPT-5 is effectively null — all while compliance stays ≥98%, ruling out refusal as the explanation.

Key Finding
Interference varies by model, not universal.

Claude exhibits the only clearly non-zero Gender–Career effect; GPT-5 is flat. The asymmetry survives permutation falsification.

Why This Matters
Compliance is separated from consistency.

The two-stage Bayesian model quarantines refusal/format artifacts before estimating interference — a measurement advance over prior LLM-IAT work.

Critical Edge
One prompt, one temperature, three models.

No prompt-paraphrase robustness, no order counterbalancing, only two IAT domains. The Claude > Gemini > GPT-5 ranking may be template-specific.

Methodology

Two-Stage Bayesian Design

  • Stage A — Compliance: multilevel logistic model of valid JSON "A"/"B" responses.
  • Stage B — Consistency: same structure fitted to task-consistent choices given validity.
  • Item random intercepts pool lexical heterogeneity (σ_item = 2.01).
  • Permutation falsification shuffles block labels; refitted ΔP_perm ≈ 0 across the board.
  • 960 trials = 3 models × 2 IAT domains × 160 trials.

Compliance is uniformly high and uncorrelated with block, so Stage B comparisons are not confounded by non-response.

Strengths

What the Paper Does Well

  • Clean separation of compliance and interference.
  • Permutation falsification is rarely included in LLM bias work.
  • Item-level random intercepts prevent stimulus-driven artifacts.
  • Bayesian credible intervals make uncertainty explicit.
  • Honest scope: explicitly disclaims mechanistic explanation.
Limitations

Where Caution Is Warranted

  • Only two IAT domains; no race, age, religion, nationality.
  • Single prompt template per cell — no robustness checks.
  • Default decoding parameters only; no temperature/top-p sweep.
  • Three models is a snapshot, not a claim about all LLMs.
  • GPT-5's slightly lower compliance may itself suppress ΔP.
Relevance

Why Read This

The paper is a methodological contribution at the intersection of LLM fairness and psychometric measurement. By transplanting the classic IAT into a JSON forced-choice format and explicitly modeling compliance, it addresses a confound that prior LLM-IAT studies often ignore. The Bayesian item-level modeling and permutation falsification are reusable statistical practices for anyone auditing model bias.
