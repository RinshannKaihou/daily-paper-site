> **一句话总结：** 针对 Ampere RTX 3090（无 FP8 Tensor Core），作者为 9.3B 双分支流匹配 DiT Ideogram 4.0 设计了一套 SmoothQuant + 选择性 bf16 保护（17 层，约 8% linears）的 INT8 W8A8 配方，在 CLIP/PickScore 上与 FP8 不可区分（paired bootstrap CI 包含零），LPIPS 0.243 是所有 8/4-bit 变体中最接近 FP8 的；同时实现 GGUF Q4_K，与 NF4 在相同磁盘体积下质量持平，并通过 300 条 schema-valid JSON prompt 评测揭示：原始 prompt 下 OCR 几乎为零（≈0%），改用 JSON 后跃升至 ≈55% exact-match，且三套独立质量分数无法再区分四种变体。

> **TL;DR:** This paper post-training-quantizes Ideogram 4.0 — a 9.3B flow-matching diffusion transformer with a single-stream backbone that realizes classifier-free guidance via two separate-weight copies and is conditioned by a Qwen3-VL text encoder — for Ampere RTX 3090 GPUs that lack FP8 tensor cores. Using a 300-prompt JSON-prompt benchmark (HPSv2 / CLIP / PickScore for standalone quality, PP-OCR for text, PSNR / SSIM / LPIPS vs. the FP8 reference, plus paired bootstrap 95% CIs), an INT8 W8A8 recipe (per-channel weights, per-token dynamic activations, SmoothQuant α=0.5, bf16 protection of the 17 most fragile layers ≈8% of 211 linears / ≈1.5 GB) is statistically indistinguishable from FP8 on CLIP (Δ=−0.005, CI [−0.146, +0.133]) and PickScore (Δ=−0.007, CI [−0.040, +0.027]) and is the most faithful 8-bit reproduction (LPIPS 0.243 vs. 0.277 / 0.306 for Q4_K / NF4; the INT8−Q4_K gap excludes zero). A custom GGUF Q4_K encoder matches NF4 quality at the same on-disk footprint (10.44 GB vs. 10.4 GB), making it the Pareto choice on the quality–memory frontier. Crucially, the paper demonstrates that **the prompt format itself** gates whether the text-rendering axis is measurable at all — OCR exact-match rises from ≈0% under raw strings to ≈55% under schema-valid JSON captions, and under those JSON prompts all four variants reach parity on standalone quality, separating instead only on reference fidelity and text. INT8 W8A8 and GGUF Q4_K weights are released on Hugging Face under a gated, non-commercial license.

## 核心贡献

- 在没有 FP8 Tensor Core 的 Ampere RTX 3090 上，为 9.3B 双分支流匹配 DiT（Ideogram 4.0，每分支 211 个 linear，共 422 个）首次系统完成 INT8 W8A8 PTQ：在 CLIP/PickScore 配对 bootstrap CI 上与 FP8 不可区分，并保持 FP8 同等磁盘体积。
- 设计了一套针对 DiT + Qwen3-VL 的混合精度配方：per-channel INT8 权重量化 + per-token dynamic INT8 激活量化 + SmoothQuant (α=0.5) + 选择性 bf16 保护 top-N=17 个高敏感层（约 8% linears，约 1.5 GB bf16 开销）。
- 通过 per-step max-abs / std / kurtosis 的 timestep-stable profiling（Spearman 0.930 早晚期相关性）发现：FFN down-projections（feed_forward.w2）是最脆弱模块集，单个最敏感层分数约为次高层的 10×；并以 N=17/N=8 的 ladder 验证"保护"对恢复质量的贡献（78–92%）远大于"smoothing"，N=17 触达上限，N=8 回落至未保护基线。
- 自主实现 GGUF Q4_K 编码器（NumPy superblock 量化 + Torch dequant kernel，对照参考 GGUF 解码器验证），为社区补齐了 Q4_K 编码工具链。
- 搭建符合 Ideogram 公开 caption schema 的 LLM JSON expander（300/300 通过 CaptionVerifier），并对比实验明确证明：在 JSON prompt 下四变体在 HPSv2/CLIP/PickScore 上统计不可区分，但 PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS 与 OCR 上仍有结构性差异——揭示"评测轴决定排名"这一方法论要点。
- 发现 prompt 格式本身是文本可识别性的前置条件：原始字符串 prompt 下 OCR exact-match ≈0%，schema-valid JSON 下跃升至 ≈54–57%。
- 释放 INT8 W8A8 与 GGUF Q4_K 权重（Hugging Face gated non-commercial license）。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic INT8 W8A8 PTQ of a 9.3B dual-branch flow-matching DiT (Ideogram 4.0, 211 linear layers per branch × 2 = 422 total) targeting Ampere RTX 3090 GPUs without FP8 tensor cores; statistically indistinguishable from FP8 on CLIP and PickScore (paired bootstrap CIs include zero) at FP8-class on-disk size.
- A mixed-precision INT8 recipe tailored to a DiT + Qwen3-VL stack: per-channel INT8 weights + per-token dynamic INT8 activations + SmoothQuant (α=0.5) + selective bf16 protection of the 17 most fragile layers (≈8% of linears, ≈1.5 GB overhead).
- Per-step activation-fragility profiling that is **timestep-stable** (Spearman 0.930 between early- and late-step halves), revealing FFN down-projections (feed_forward.w2) as the dominant fragile module; the single most fragile layer scores ≈10× its runner-up, consistent with prior module-type findings.
- An ablation showing **protection, not smoothing, is the dominant lever** (protection accounts for ≈78–92% of recovery; effects sub-additive). N=17 holds the ceiling; N=8 collapses back toward the unprotected baseline.
- A custom GGUF Q4_K encoder (NumPy superblock quantizer + Torch dequant kernel, validated against a reference GGUF decoder), filling a tooling gap for DiT k-quants.
- An LLM-based JSON caption expander built to Ideogram's published spec, with a CaptionVerifier validating all 300 benchmark prompts.
- A 300-prompt benchmark battery (HPSv2 / CLIP / PickScore for standalone quality, PP-OCR exact-match + NED for text, PSNR / SSIM / LPIPS vs. the FP8 reference) with paired bootstrap 95% CIs to test non-separation.
- The methodological insight that **prompt format gates measurability of the text-rendering axis** (OCR exact-match ≈0% under raw prompts vs. ≈55% under JSON prompts) and that under the model's intended prompts standalone-quality judges stop separating the variants — separating instead on reference fidelity and text.
- Release of INT8 W8A8 and GGUF Q4_K weights on Hugging Face under a gated, non-commercial license: `transformerlab/ideogram-4-int8-w8a8` and `transformerlab/ideogram-4-gguf-q4_k`.

## Methodology

- **Model under test.** Ideogram 4.0 (9.3B parameters): a single-stream flow-matching DiT backbone with **two separate-weight copies** of the backbone realizing classifier-free guidance (one conditional, one unconditional, ~9.3 GB each at FP8), conditioned by a **Qwen3-VL text encoder**; VAE kept at published precision. Total quantizable surface: 211 linear layers per branch × 2 branches = 422 linears.
- **Target hardware.** NVIDIA RTX 3090 (Ampere, 24 GB) — no FP8 tensor cores, but fast native INT8 compute. The validated single-GPU-compute recipe places both DiT branches on one card with the VAE on a second.
- **Reference model.** The **FP8** checkpoint (highest-precision public release, no public BF16). On Ampere, FP8 runs by dequantizing each linear to bf16 every forward at 172.9 s/image (~5% over NF4's 164.5 s/image).
- **INT8 W8A8 recipe.**
  - Per-channel (per-output-row) INT8 weights + per-token dynamic INT8 activations.
  - SmoothQuant outlier migration with α=0.5: scaling vector λ_j = s_j^α / max_i |W_ij|^(1−α) redistributes per-channel activation outliers into weights before quantization.
  - Activation scales computed from a **128-prompt calibration set** disjoint from the evaluation set.
  - **bf16 protection** of the top-N=17 most fragile linears (no activation quantization there); the rest of the 422 linears use W8A8. N=17 corresponds to ~8% of linears and ~1.5 GB bf16 overhead.
- **Activation-fragility profiling.** Hooks every linear in the conditional DiT branch and records per-step max-abs, std, and kurtosis over the denoising trajectory; ranking is trajectory-stable (Spearman 0.930 between early- and late-step halves). The fragile set is dominated by FFN down-projections (feed_forward.w2); the single most fragile layer scores ≈10× its runner-up.
- **GGUF Q4_K encoder.** A NumPy Q4_K superblock quantizer + Torch dequant kernel, validated against a reference GGUF decoder. Q8_0 (8.5 bpw, round-to-nearest) is also evaluated; Q4_K targets 4.5 bpw, the same on-disk size class as NF4.
- **Prompt protocol.** Three disjoint PartiPrompts-derived sets built with a deterministic seed:
  - Calibration: n=128
  - Quality benchmark: n=200 (category-stratified, no text)
  - Text-rendering benchmark: n=100 (of which 63 carry machine-checkable OCR targets)
  - All 300 expanded to JSON captions via the LLM expander and validated by CaptionVerifier. All variants generate from identical (caption, seed, steps, resolution) tuples: **seed 1000, 48 steps, 1024×1024, V4_QUALITY_48 preset**.
- **Evaluation battery.**
  - Standalone quality: HPSv2 (human-preference), CLIPScore, PickScore, computed in-house.
  - Text: PP-OCR exact-match + normalized edit distance (NED, lower is better) via an independent OCR model.
  - Reference fidelity: PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS (AlexNet) of each variant's image against the **FP8 image at the same seed**.
- **Statistical analysis.** Paired per-prompt deltas with a 10,000-sample bootstrap 95% CI. CIs capture prompt-sampling variance at the single fixed seed (1000), not across-seed variance; uncorrected for multiple comparisons; read descriptively. A CI that includes zero indicates non-separation at this sample size (not a pre-registered equivalence test).
- **Efficiency metrics.** End-to-end s/image (in-VRAM), peak VRAM, on-disk DiT weight size with scales included.

## Results & Highlights

**Standalone quality — all four variants at parity (Table 1).**

| Variant | HPSv2↑ | CLIP↑ | Pick↑ |
|---|---|---|---|
| FP8 (ref) | 0.2807 | 29.48 | 22.85 |
| NF4 | 0.2789 | 29.63 | 22.80 |
| INT8 (ours) | 0.2763 | 29.48 | 22.84 |
| Q4_K (ours) | 0.2783 | 29.64 | 22.79 |

All four within ≈0.004 HPSv2 (point-estimate spread, no per-image CI).

**Paired bootstrap 95% CIs (Table 2).** INT8−FP8 CLIP Δ=−0.005 [−0.146, +0.133] and Pick Δ=−0.007 [−0.040, +0.027] — both include zero → INT8 is statistically indistinguishable from FP8. Q4_K−NF4 CLIP Δ=+0.011 and Pick Δ=−0.003, both CIs include zero → Q4_K matches NF4. NF4−FP8 CLIP CI includes zero (Δ=+0.143 [−0.015, +0.299]); the only CI-significant standalone gap is Q4_K−FP8 Pick Δ=−0.055 [−0.093, −0.017].

**Reference fidelity — INT8 is the most faithful 8-bit reproduction (Table 3).**

| Variant | PSNR↑ | SSIM↑ | LPIPS↓ |
|---|---|---|---|
| INT8 (ours) | 18.53 | 0.712 | 0.243 |
| Q4_K (ours) | 17.71 | 0.677 | 0.277 |
| NF4 | 16.82 | 0.642 | 0.306 |

INT8−Q4_K LPIPS gap excludes zero (Δ=−0.034, CI [−0.041, −0.027]). The cleanest separation in the study.

**Text rendering — only measurable under JSON prompts (Table 4).**

| Variant | OCR exact-match↑ | OCR NED↓ |
|---|---|---|
| FP8 (ref) | 0.540 | 0.133 |
| NF4 | 0.556 | 0.116 |
| INT8 (ours) | 0.571 | 0.099 |
| Q4_K (ours) | 0.540 | 0.123 |

Under raw natural-language prompts the same model scores ≈0% exact-match. The ≈0% → ≈55% jump is driven entirely by the prompt protocol, not by quantization. Per-variant NED differences are within noise at n=63 (INT8−NF4 NED CI includes zero).

**Ablation — protection > smoothing.** Naive W8A8 (no smoothing, no protection) collapses. SmoothQuant alone and protection alone each recover most of the gap; **protection accounts for ≈78–92% of recovery**, with the two effects sub-additive. The protection-size ladder shows a sharp knee: N=17 holds the ceiling while N=8 collapses back toward the unprotected baseline (down-projections kept in bf16). (Note: this ablation was run on raw-prompt calibration, not the JSON battery.)

**Efficiency on RTX 3090 (Table 5, 48 steps, 1024²).**

| Variant | Size (GB)↓ | s/image↓ |
|---|---|---|
| FP8 (ref) | 18.6 | 172.9 |
| NF4 | 10.4 | 164.5 |
| INT8 (ours) | 18.6 | ~184 |
| Q4_K (ours) | 10.44 | 203.3 |

INT8 is FP8-class in size but has no fused kernel (eager execution); Q4_K halves memory but is the slowest (Torch dequant kernel). No variant dominates on both memory and latency — the memory win (Q4_K) and the fidelity win (INT8) sit on different points of the trade-off.

**GGUF Q4_K is the Pareto choice on the quality–memory frontier.** Q4_K (4.5 bpw, 10.44 GB) matches NF4 standalone quality (Q4_K−NF4 CIs both include zero) at essentially the same on-disk size (+0.04 GB). Q8_0 is lossless by round-to-nearest.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Authors:** Deep Gandhi, Ali Asaria, Tony Salomone — all from **Transformer Lab**.
- Acknowledgements: the authors thank the Ideogram team for feedback that improved the evaluation protocol and analysis.
- Credibility is **medium-strong for the specific claims** (one model, one prompt distribution, one GPU cluster) but **medium** for broader generalizations (e.g., to other DiTs or prompt formats). The methodological discipline (paired bootstrap CIs, multi-metric battery, FP8-as-reference, ablation isolating protection from smoothing) is unusual for an applied PTQ paper and exceeds typical practitioner work; however, the paper is not a top-tier lab publication and the "INT8 holds the FP8 ceiling" claim is conditional on the compared alternatives.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** Incremental but useful. INT8 W8A8 + SmoothQuant + selective bf16 protection is a well-converged recipe; the genuine novelty is in (a) a careful per-module fragility profile with timestep-stable ranking (Spearman 0.930), (b) a custom GGUF Q4_K encoder for DiTs, and (c) the prompt-format methodological point that JSON captions flip the ranking axis.
- **Rigor.** **Above average for an applied PTQ paper.** Strengths: paired bootstrap CIs across 300 prompts, multi-metric battery spanning standalone quality + text + reference fidelity, FP8-as-reference anchoring the "ceiling" claim, an isolation ablation separating protection from smoothing. Weaknesses (also honestly acknowledged in §7 Limitations): HPSv2 spread (≈0.004) carries no per-image CI; INT8 ablation was calibrated on raw prompts, not JSON; single-seed (1000) headline statistics; OCR significance limited by n=63; no cross-seed replication; no cross-hardware replication beyond one RTX 3090 cluster.
- **Reproducibility.** **Partially reproducible.** Weights are released (`transformerlab/ideogram-4-int8-w8a8`, `transformerlab/ideogram-4-gguf-q4_k`) under a gated non-commercial license; the JSON expander references Ideogram's published caption spec; the CaptionVerifier validates all 300 prompts. However, the full per-layer fragility ranking (which 17 layers, exact bf16 boundaries) is not surfaced in the abstract; the GGUF Q4_K encoder is described but not separately packaged; the "evaluation recipe is available on request." Seeds are fixed (1000) which helps reproducibility but limits generalizability.
- **Significance.** **Useful and timely** for deploying high-end DiTs on consumer hardware, and the **methodological lesson** (standalone judges stop separating variants under the model's intended prompt format; the discriminating axis moves to reference fidelity and text) is a genuinely valuable artifact for the quantization evaluation community.
- **Red flags / concerns.**
  - **Single-model study.** "Holds the FP8 quality ceiling" is conditional on Ideogram 4.0's dual-branch CFG + Qwen3-VL conditioning and on JSON prompts. Generalization to SD3, Flux, HunyuanDiT, or non-JSON-prompted models is unproven.
  - **LPIPS-as-fidelity caveat.** LPIPS/SSIM/PSNR vs. an FP8 reference can reward outputs that are merely close to FP8 rather than perceptually better; the authors acknowledge this is a proxy for full precision (no public BF16 release), and Q4_K's small standalone-score "advantage" over FP8 reflects higher pixel divergence, not higher quality.
  - **Framing risk.** The 55% OCR jump is driven by prompt format, not by quantization. Readers skimming the abstract could misattribute the text-rendering result to the INT8 recipe.
  - **Gated, non-commercial license.** Limits independent third-party benchmarking, downstream use, and inclusion in larger pipelines. The closed downstream is at odds with the open-source ethos of GPTQ / AWQ / QLoRA that the work builds on.
  - **No head-to-head against recent INT8 DiT methods** (Q-DiT, ViDiT-Q, SVDQuant, FlatQuant, DiRotQ) or against FP8 emulation on Ampere. "INT8 is the ceiling" is conditional on the alternatives considered.
  - **HPSv2 spread (≈0.004) lacks a per-image CI**, so the parity claim on HPSv2 rests on point estimates rather than significance tests.
  - **INT8 ablation was run on raw prompts, not JSON.** The protection-dominance attribution is for the raw-prompt calibration regime; whether it transfers to the JSON battery is not directly tested.

## Strengths

- **Disciplined multi-metric eval with paired bootstrap CIs** — uncommon in applied PTQ papers and substantially above median rigor for Hugging Face releases.
- **FP8-as-reference design** anchors the "ceiling" claim in a concrete, highest-precision target rather than the BF16 baseline that most quantization papers use.
- **Timestep-stable per-module fragility profile** (Spearman 0.930) with module-type identification (FFN down-projections) and a protection-size ladder (N=8 vs. N=17) is an unusually careful ablation for this kind of work.
- **The JSON-prompt OCR finding** is a genuinely useful artifact: it isolates prompt-format alignment as a major axis of variation and warns the community against ranking quantized variants on a single CLIP-family score under off-distribution prompts.
- **Pareto-frontier framing** for Q4_K vs. NF4 vs. INT8 (memory win vs. fidelity win vs. compute) gives practitioners a clear decision rule.
- **Released weights** lower the bar for community adoption (modulo the license), and the JSON expander + CaptionVerifier pipeline can be reused for other prompt-format-sensitive evaluations.
- **Honest limitations section** — the authors explicitly call out single-seed statistics, n=63 OCR, single-hardware replication, FP8-as-proxy-for-full-precision, and the raw-prompt/JSON-prompt ablation mismatch.

## Limitations

- **Single-model, single-prompt-distribution study**; no cross-architecture or cross-prompt generalization claims are supported. The "17-layer protection set" is not validated on other DiTs.
- **No cross-seed replication.** All headline statistics use a single seed (1000); across-seed variance is unknown.
- **OCR significance limited** by n=63 targets; per-variant NED differences are within noise.
- **INT8 lacks a fused Ampere GEMM**; it runs eager at ~184 s/image, so its 8-bit compute is a substrate for future kernel work (CUTLASS / ViDiT-Q-style) rather than a realized speed-up today.
- **HPSv2 spread has no per-image CI**; the parity claim on HPSv2 is a point-estimate read, not a significance test.
- **INT8 protection/smoothing ablation was run on raw-prompt calibration**, not the JSON battery used in Tables 1–3.
- **Gated non-commercial license** restricts independent benchmarking, downstream use, and inclusion in larger pipelines.
- **No head-to-head against recent INT8 DiT methods** (Q-DiT, ViDiT-Q, SVDQuant, FlatQuant, DiRotQ) or FP8 emulation on Ampere, so the "INT8 is the ceiling" claim is conditional on the alternatives considered.
- **Q4_K is the slowest variant** (Torch dequant kernel) — its memory win comes at a latency cost.
- **Reference is FP8, not BF16**; "fidelity to FP8" is a proxy for full precision, since no public BF16 Ideogram 4.0 release exists.

## Relevance

与用户在量化、扩散模型推理与消费级 GPU 部署方向的兴趣高度相关：本文给出在缺乏 FP8 硬件支持的 Ampere GPU 上部署大型 DiT 的可复用配方（INT8 W8A8 + SmoothQuant + 选择性 bf16 保护），通过 per-module timestep-stable fragility profile 给出可推广的"保护优先"设计原则（保护贡献 78–92%），并以多指标 + bootstrap CI 评测明确揭示"评测轴决定排名"——对量化感知评估方法学有直接借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the timestep-stable FFN down-projection fragility pattern (Spearman 0.930) generalize across other DiTs (SD3, Flux, HunyuanDiT, Wan2.2)? Would the "≈8% linears in bf16" rule transfer, or do different DiTs need different module-type selections?
- How does the INT8 W8A8 recipe compare head-to-head against recent DiT-specific INT8 methods — ViDiT-Q, SVDQuant, Q-DiT4SR, DiRotQ, FlatQuant — on the same prompts and metrics?
- Can the LPIPS-fidelity gap to FP8 (0.243 vs. the 0.277 / 0.306 baselines) be closed with a lightweight distillation objective (e.g., feature-level distillation from FP8 to INT8) without sacrificing standalone quality?
- How robust is the JSON-prompt OCR lift (≈0% → ≈55%) to schema perturbations? What is the smallest prompt-format intervention that recovers most of the gain — e.g., adding structured fields, escaping strings, or supplying a single example?
- Would a fused Ampere INT8 GEMM (CUTLASS INT8 or ViDiT-Q-style kernels) actually deliver the ~2× speed-up over the eager ~184 s/image INT8 path? How would that change the Pareto frontier against Q4_K?
- How sensitive are the results to CFG scale, sampling steps, and the two-copy CFG backbone architecture? Would a single-copy CFG-with-dropout backbone make INT8 PTQ easier and faster?
- Could step-caching methods (TeaCache, TACache) compound with INT8 W8A8 to convert both the 8-bit compute substrate and the reduced-step compute into a compounded latency win?
- Is the FP8-as-reference LPIPS ranking (INT8 < Q4_K < NF4) preserved when comparing against a hypothetical BF16 reference (if Ideogram were to release one), or does it invert?
