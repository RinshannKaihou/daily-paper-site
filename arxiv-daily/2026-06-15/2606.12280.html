Quantizing a Dual-Branch Flow-Matching Diffusion Transformer for Consumer GPUs

Deep Gandhi · Ali Asaria · Tony Salomone · Transformer Lab

TL;DR: On Ampere RTX 3090, an INT8 W8A8 recipe (SmoothQuant α=0.5 + bf16 protection of 17 fragile layers) makes Ideogram 4.0 statistically indistinguishable from FP8 on CLIP/PickScore, while GGUF Q4_K matches NF4 at half the memory.

Key Finding

Protection, Not Smoothing, Saves the Day

Keeping the 17 most fragile linear layers in bf16 accounts for ~78–92% of quality recovery. SmoothQuant helps, but selective protection is the dominant lever.

Key Metric
0.243

LPIPS vs. FP8

INT8 is the most faithful 8-bit reproduction. Q4_K sits at 0.277; NF4 at 0.306. The INT8–Q4_K gap excludes zero in paired bootstrap CI.

Why This Matters

Consumer GPUs Catch Up

First systematic INT8 PTQ of a 9.3B flow-matching DiT for Ampere, which lacks FP8 tensor cores. INT8 keeps FP8-class quality at FP8-class disk size.

Critical Edge

Prompt Format Gates OCR

OCR exact-match jumps from ≈0% under raw prompts to ≈55% under schema-valid JSON captions. The axis of measurement depends on prompt alignment.

Recipe Architecture

1
Quantization backbone Per-channel INT8 weights + per-token dynamic INT8 activations across 422 linears (211 per CFG branch).
2
SmoothQuant outlier migration α=0.5 redistributes activation outliers into weights before quantization.
3
Selective bf16 protection Top-N=17 fragile layers (~8% of linears, ~1.5 GB overhead) stay unquantized.
4
GGUF Q4_K alternative Custom NumPy superblock quantizer + Torch dequant kernel at 4.5 bpw, matching NF4 quality at the same on-disk footprint.

Red Flags

  • Single model, single prompt distribution, one GPU cluster.
  • Reference is FP8, not public BF16.
  • HPSv2 parity has no per-image CI.
  • Protection ablation ran on raw prompts, not JSON.
  • Gated non-commercial license limits downstream reuse.
