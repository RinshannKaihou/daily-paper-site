Benchmark · LLM Planning · Agent Safety

SIMMER — Latent failures in LLM executable planning, exposed by a kitchen world model.

Plans that run to completion can still silently corrupt the world: an unwashed cutting board poisons everything it touches, the stove stays on after dinner, raw protein gets handled after bread. SIMMER builds a 46,800-interaction symbolic kitchen, runs plans step-by-step, and reports the failures a precondition-only check would miss.

Lu, Zhang & Zhang · Penn State arXiv:2606.14574 2026-06-15 domain kitchen actions 77 objects 262 tasks 100
Key Finding
A first-class failure category the planning literature has been missing: latent failures — preconditions all satisfied, plan completes, but state is silently corrupted (contamination, dirty surfaces, appliances left on), often irreversibly.
Key Metric
<17%
of plans are error-free across 6 LLMs (avg 7.2%). Latent failures affect 29–56% of plans; 20–45% contain at least one irreversible failure.
Why This Matters
Every standard "did it finish?" benchmark is substantially overoptimistic for embodied / domestic LLM agents. The dangerous failures are the ones that don't trip a precondition check — and those are the ones a deployed robot will serve to your family.
Critical Edge
Counterfactual Foresight Simulation (predict → self-check → revise) cuts latent failures by up to 72% and irreversible ones by up to 75% — but only validated on the 2 strongest of 6 models. Weaker models, where the mitigation is most needed, are unmeasured.

The crux
// 02

Existing planners and benchmarks answer a binary question: did it finish? They miss the third rail — plans that finish but quietly produce a worse world than they started with.

SIMMER borrows latent error (Reason, 1990) from safety engineering and operationalises it as a 3-way classifier:

Counterfactual Foresight Simulation
// 07 · mitigation

An open-loop prompting strategy (no environment feedback) inserted before each action. Beats Self-Refine's whole-plan re-audit on immediate failures, comparable on latent.

Predict
For the next action, enumerate which world attributes will change — and which implicit state will travel with them.
Self-check
Does any predicted change violate a precondition, create a contamination path, or leave a hazard unresolved at goal?
Revise
If unexpected consequences predicted, modify the action (or insert a new one: SWITCH OFF, WASH, THROW AWAY).

Critical assessment
// 10

Novelty: high — re-framing latent failure as a first-class category is genuinely useful. Rigor: mostly solid. 46,800 is a ceiling not realised coverage; the actual task set is 100 scripts; no inter-annotator agreement reported.

Red flags. Mitigation is only validated on the 2 strongest models. On the 4 weaker models, Self-Refine is marginal-to-negative and Foresight isn't reported at all. The "≤17% error-free" headline conflates the best model (GPT-5.4 = 13%) with the average (7.2%).
