> **一句话总结：** Zeta 指出 Muon 的 Newton–Schulz 谱白化对输入条件数敏感、而原始动量矩阵存在严重的坐标尺度异质性（卡方均匀性检验 p 值普遍偏小），故提出"先坐标白化（按逐元素二阶矩归一化）、再谱白化"的严格顺序双白化流水线，并在理论上证明该顺序能改善条件数、严格降低正交化误差；在 0.6B–8B 语言模型、MoE 与视觉任务上收敛更快且下游精度更高。

> **TL;DR:** Zeta is a matrix-aware optimizer that fixes an underappreciated vulnerability of Muon: its core Newton–Schulz (NS) spectral-whitening iteration depends critically on input conditioning, yet raw momentum matrices exhibit severe coordinate-wise scale heterogeneity (verified by a chi-square uniformity test with p-values consistently near 0 across Qwen3-0.6B layers). Zeta applies a strictly ordered dual-whitening pipeline — coordinate whitening (element-wise second-moment normalization, G̃_t = M_t/(√V_t+ε)) first, then NS spectral whitening (K=5 iterations) — and proves (Theorem 2) this ordering strictly reduces orthogonalization error by improving the condition number of the NS input. On Qwen3-1.7B and Qwen3-8B pretraining (DCLM-baseline), Zeta reaches the same loss 1.67× (1.7B) and 1.25× (8B) faster than AdamW, beating Muon (1.52× / 1.19×) and AdaMuon (1.61× / 1.21×); on 12 downstream benchmarks Zeta averages 26.29% (8B) and 23.69% (1.7B), beating Muon by +1.00% / +4.22% and AdaMuon by +5.08% / +7.83%, at near-AdamW per-step cost (25.47s vs 24.46s on 16 GPUs for 8B).

## 核心贡献

- 通过**卡方均匀性检验**实证诊断 Muon 的失效模式：对 Qwen3-0.6B 各层动量矩阵的行能量做"所有块尺度相同"的零假设检验，白化前 p 值普遍接近 0（显著尺度失衡），坐标白化后 p 值大幅趋向 1，证明谱白化隐含的"输入尺度均匀"前提被原始动量系统性违反。
- 提出 **Zeta**：严格顺序的双白化流水线——先坐标白化（G̃_t = M_t/(√V_t+ε)，按逐元素二阶矩归一化），再谱白化（Newton–Schulz K=5 次迭代）；顺序不可调换，因为谱白化是一个旋转，其保真度依赖输入已尺度归一化。
- 给出三条理论保证：**定理1** 坐标白化是对理想白化 F^{-1/2} 的对角近似（在 diagonal-covariance 假设下）；**定理2** 该对角预条件改善条件数 κ(G̃)≤κ(G)，从而在固定 K 步下严格降低 NS 正交化误差 ‖NS(G̃)^⊤NS(G̃)−I‖_F ≤ ‖NS(G)^⊤NS(G)−I‖_F；**定理3** 末尾 RMS 缩放（s_t=0.2/RMS(U_t)）保持谱方向不变、仅校准全局幅度。
- 在 0.6B–8B 稠密 LM、1.3B-A0.6B MoE、ViT 视觉任务上系统对比 AdamW/Muon/AdaMuon，Zeta 一致地在收敛速度与下游精度上领先，且每步开销与 AdamW 接近。

## Key Contributions

- Diagnoses Muon's vulnerability empirically: a chi-square uniformity test on row energies of momentum matrices in Qwen3-0.6B shows p-values consistently near 0 (significant intra-matrix scale imbalance) before whitening and near 1 after coordinate whitening — evidence that spectral whitening's implicit scale-uniform precondition is systematically violated by raw momentum.
- Proposes **Zeta**, a strictly ordered dual-whitening optimizer: coordinate whitening (element-wise second-moment normalization M_t/(√V_t+ε)) followed by spectral whitening (Newton–Schulz K=5 iterations); the ordering is mathematically necessary, not tunable, because spectral whitening is a rotation whose fidelity depends on a scale-normalized input.
- Proves three theorems: (1) coordinate whitening is a diagonal approximation to ideal whitening F^{-1/2}; (2) it improves the condition number, strictly reducing NS orthogonalization error at fixed K; (3) the final RMS scaling preserves the spectral direction while matching the update magnitude to the Adam-style branch.
- Validates Zeta on dense LMs (GPT2-Large, Qwen3-0.6B/1.7B/8B), a Qwen3 MoE (1.3B total / 0.6B active), and ViT-Tiny/Base on CIFAR-100, consistently matching or beating AdamW, Muon, and AdaMuon in convergence speed and downstream accuracy at near-AdamW per-step cost.

## Methodology

- **Muon baseline (Algorithm 1):** maintains momentum G_t = g_t + β·G_{t-1}, applies NS orthogonalization U_t = Newton-Schulz(G_t, K=5) (coefficients a=3.4445, b=−4.7750, c=2.0315, starting from X_0=G_t/‖G_t‖_F), scales η′=0.2η·√(max(m,n)), updates W with weight decay. Vulnerability: NS is a Stiefel-manifold projection whose accuracy depends on input conditioning.
- **Zeta (Algorithm 2):** maintains first moment M_t = β_1·M_{t-1}+(1−β_1)g_t and second moment V_t = β_2·V_{t-1}+(1−β_2)g_t² (both element-wise, Adam-style); coordinate-whitens G̃_t = M_t/(√V_t+ε); applies NS to the whitened matrix U_t = Newton-Schulz(G̃_t, K=5); RMS-scales ΔW_t = (0.2·√(mn))/(‖U_t‖_F+ε)·U_t; updates W_{t+1} = (1−ηλ)W_t − η·ΔW_t. For non-matrix params (biases, LayerNorm) it falls back to AdamW.
- **Chi-square motivation test (Appendix A.4):** partition matrix into blocks, compute row energies E_i=‖g_i‖²₂, normalize by mean energy, chi-square statistic X²=Σ(O_i−1)² with df=m−1; p-value from upper-tail regularized gamma. p→1 means scale-uniform; p→0 means anisotropic. Shows raw Muon momentum is anisotropic across all tested layers and coordinate whitening shifts p toward 1.
- **Theorem 2 proof mechanism:** NS iteration is equivariant under orthogonal basis changes, so only singular values evolve via scalar recurrence σ_{k+1}=f(σ_k)=aσ+bσ³+cσ⁵; orthogonality defect = Σ_i(f^(K)(σ_{0,i})²−1)²; the error function g(x)→1 as x→0⁺ (tiny singular values contribute near-maximal error), so lifting small singular values (via better conditioning) strictly reduces total error.
- **Models/datasets:** GPT2-Large + Qwen3-0.6B on OpenWebText; Qwen3-1.7B, Qwen3-8B, Qwen3-MoE (1.3B/0.6B-active) on DCLM-baseline. Training: seq len 4096 (Qwen) / 1024 (GPT2), global batch 256 / 480, cosine LR decay, 1% warmup, weight decay 0.1.
- **Optimizer hyperparameters:** AdamW β=(0.9,0.95); Muon/AdaMuon β=0.95, K=5; Zeta β_1=0.95, β_2=0.99, K=5. LR: per-optimizer-tuned on 0.6B; unified 9e-4 for 1.7B/8B, 3e-4 for GPT2-Large/MoE.
- **Benchmarks:** 12 downstream tasks (HellaSwag, MMLU, NIAH, PIQA, ARC-c, C-Eval, TriviaQA, OBQA, WinoGrande, CHID, CMMLU, GSM8k); vision = ViT-Tiny/Base on CIFAR-100, 50 epochs, weight decay 0.05.

## Results & Highlights

- **1.67× iteration speedup over AdamW on Qwen3-1.7B** (to reach the same loss), beating Muon (1.52×) and AdaMuon (1.61×); time-based speedup 1.64× vs Muon 1.52× and AdaMuon 1.60×, with per-iteration time on 8 GPUs of 15.59s (Zeta) vs 15.3s (AdamW) — near-zero overhead (Figure 3).
- **1.25× iteration speedup on Qwen3-8B** vs AdamW, vs 1.19× (Muon) and 1.21× (AdaMuon); per-iteration time on 16 GPUs 25.47s (Zeta) vs 24.46s (AdamW), 26.19s (Muon), 26.26s (AdaMuon) — Zeta is faster per step than the other matrix optimizers (Figure 4).
- **MoE: 1.47× iteration speedup on Qwen3-1.3B-A0.6B** over AdamW, vs 1.18× (Muon) and 1.30× (AdaMuon); time-based 1.32×; per-step 11.93s vs 10.87s (AdamW) — the largest relative gain, attributed to the dense–sparse gradient heterogeneity typical of MoE (Figure 5).
- **Downstream (Table 1):** Zeta averages 26.29% on Qwen3-8B (vs AdamW 23.42%, Muon 26.03%, AdaMuon 25.02%) — +1.00% over Muon, +5.08% over AdaMuon, +12.26% over AdamW; on Qwen3-1.7B averages 23.69% (vs Muon 22.73%, AdaMuon 21.97%, AdamW 23.28%) — +4.22% over Muon, +7.83% over AdaMuon.
- **Vision (Table 2):** Zeta reaches 64.98% (ViT-Tiny) and 65.34% (ViT-Base) Top-1 on CIFAR-100, beating AdamW (+13.96% / +12.69%), Muon (+0.46% / +1.26%), AdaMuon (+1.59% / +5.34%).
- **Hyperparameter robustness (Table 3):** grid over β_1,β_2∈{0.9,0.95,0.99} on Qwen3-0.6B shows final loss varies <1.2%; best at (0.9,0.99) and (0.95,0.99) — Zeta is robust to momentum coefficients.
- **Same asymptotic complexity as AdaMuon (Appendix A.5, Table 4):** storage O(2mn), per-step O(Kmnr) — the extra coordinate-whitening step is only O(mn) element-wise, so empirical gains come from better ordering, not higher cost.
- **Chi-square motivation (Figure 1):** before whitening, p-values across Qwen3-0.6B layers (0, 9, 18, 28) are consistently below 0.05; after coordinate whitening they shift markedly toward 1, confirming scale imbalance is real and correctable.

## Critical Assessment

- **The "strictly reduces" guarantee is conditional, not absolute:** Theorem 2 assumes κ(G̃)≤κ(G) (that the diagonal preconditioner reduces the condition number); the paper does not prove this always holds for real momentum matrices, and the proof relies on the NS error function g(x) being decreasing on the relevant interval plus higher-order truncation terms being absorbed — so the headline "strictly reduces orthogonalization error" is a conditional bound, not an unconditional theorem.
- **Downstream gains over Muon are modest and benchmark-dependent:** on Qwen3-8B Zeta beats Muon by only +1.00% average (26.29% vs 26.03%), and on several individual 8B tasks Muon actually wins (HellaSwag 25.54 vs 26.12, MMLU 25.88 vs 25.08, TriviaQA 37.11 vs 37.48 is close) — the "surpasses" claim is driven by a few tasks (NIAH +2.80, CHID +4.04, CMMLU +11.20) where the absolute accuracies are low and noisy (e.g., NIAH 17.70 vs 20.50, CMMLU 16.34 vs 17.54).
- **AdamW is a weak baseline at 8B:** the +12.26% gain over AdamW on 8B average (and +13.96% on ViT-Tiny) suggests AdamW was not well-tuned or is mismatched at this scale — the headline speedup/accuracy deltas are dominated by the AdamW gap rather than the Muon gap, which is the more meaningful comparison for a matrix-optimizer paper.
- **MoE gain confounded by per-step cost:** on Qwen3-MoE, Zeta's per-step time (11.93s) is ~10% higher than AdamW (10.87s) and AdaMuon is even higher (12.10s); the 1.47× iteration speedup falls to 1.32× on a wall-clock basis, and no convergence-time-to-target-loss number is reported, so the practical MoE benefit is smaller than the iteration metric suggests.
- **No large-scale (>8B) or long-training validation:** the method's stated motivation is "large-scale neural network training," but the largest dense model is 8B and training budgets are not reported in tokens; the Kimi-1.1T-scale regime that motivates Muon is never tested, leaving open whether the dual-whitening benefit persists at frontier scale.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated diagnosis: the chi-square uniformity test turns a vague "Muon is sensitive to conditioning" intuition into a measurable, layer-wise empirical phenomenon with a concrete p-value shift after whitening.
- Principled, theoretically grounded design — three theorems give the coordinate-whitening stage a non-heuristic interpretation (diagonal preconditioner, condition-number improvement, direction-preserving RMS scaling) rather than an ad-hoc Adam+Muon mashup.
- The ordering argument is genuinely useful: it explains *why* coordinate-before-spectral (and not the reverse) matters, addressing a real practitioner question.
- Consistent gains across diverse settings (dense LM 0.6B–8B, MoE, ViT) with near-zero per-step overhead vs AdamW and lower per-step cost than Muon/AdaMuon at 8B.
- Public code release and full hyperparameter/complexity analysis (Appendix A.5) support reproducibility.

## Limitations

- Theorem 2's "strictly reduces error" is conditional on κ(G̃)≤κ(G), which is not proven to always hold for real momentum.
- Downstream gains over the meaningful baseline (Muon) are modest (+1.00% at 8B) and concentrated in low-accuracy/noisy tasks.
- AdamW baseline appears weak/mismatched at 8B and on vision, inflating the headline deltas.
- No validation beyond 8B dense or with reported token budgets; the frontier-scale regime that motivates the work is untested.
- MoE wall-clock benefit (1.32×) is notably smaller than the iteration metric (1.47×) due to higher per-step cost.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the κ(G̃)≤κ(G) assumption of Theorem 2 always hold for real deep-network momentum, or are there layer/architecture regimes where diagonal second-moment preconditioning worsens the condition number — and if so, does Zeta ever degrade relative to Muon?
- Do the gains persist at frontier scale (e.g., 30B+ dense or Kimi-1.1T-class) and over full-length pretraining (trillions of tokens), or is the dual-whitening benefit a small-to-mid-scale phenomenon the paper's 8B ceiling cannot confirm?
- The modest +1.00% gain over Muon at 8B is concentrated in low-accuracy tasks (NIAH, CHID, CMMLU) — is this a real capability gain or within run-to-run noise, given no error bars/variance are reported on the downstream table?
- Since Zeta reduces to AdaMuon in asymptotic complexity (Appendix A.5), what is the quantitative decomposition of the gain attributable to ordering (coordinate-before-spectral) vs. AdaMuon's sign-based adaptation — does the Appendix A.6 comparison isolate this?
- Could the coordinate-whitening step use a block-diagonal (not fully diagonal) second-moment estimate to better approximate off-diagonal covariance, and would that further improve the NS conditioning beyond the strictly diagonal Theorem 1 approximation?
