> **一句话总结：** Muon$^p$ 用分数次谱幂 $US^pV^\top$（$p\in(0,1)$，默认 $p=1/3$）替代 Muon 的极化因子 $UV^\top$，通过低阶奇二元多项式递推在仅依赖矩阵乘法的条件下实现谱信息的部分保留；在十亿级模型微调上验证了困惑度/数学/代码/常识任务的提升，并解释为何该方法对**预训练**反而不利。

> **TL;DR:** Muon replaces the gradient's SVD factors $G=USV^\top$ with the polar factor $UV^\top$, completely flattening the singular spectrum. This paper introduces **Muon$^p$**, a one-parameter generalization that uses fractional spectral powers $US^pV^\top$ for $p\in(0,1)$, smoothly interpolating between Muon ($p\to 0$) and plain gradient descent ($p\to 1$). Because no fixed univariate polynomial iteration can compute $US^pV^\top$ (Lemma 2.2), the authors construct **odd bivariate polynomial recurrences** $f(x,y)=x+(y^a-x^b)g(x,y)$ that approximate $US^pV^\top$ using only matrix multiplications — preserving Muon's bf16 efficiency. They prove Muon$^p$ is the steepest-descent direction under the Schatten $q$-norm with $q=1+1/p$ (Theorem 2.1). On **billion-scale finetuning** (Llama3-1B/3B), Muon$^p$ improves validation perplexity on Fineweb/Pile-of-Law, math (GSM8K, Numina), code (OpenCodeInstruct), and commonsense reasoning (PIQA, ARC, BoolQ, …) — but is **worse than Muon for pretraining**, an effect the authors attribute via a "Muon → Muon$^p$ curriculum" experiment to the fact that pretraining wants uniform full-spectrum updates while finetuning benefits from preserving singular-value magnitudes. Code: https://github.com/princeton-pli/muon-p.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 Muon$^p$，用一参数族分数次谱幂 $US^pV^\top$（$p\in(0,1)$，默认 $p=1/3$）推广 Muon，并在 Schatten $q$-norm（$q=1+1/p$）下给出最陡下降的最优性证明。
- 证明任何固定单变量多项式迭代都无法实现 $US^pV^\top$（Lemma 2.2），并构造**奇二元多项式递推** $f(x,y)=x+(y^a-x^b)g(x,y)$（Theorem 2.3/2.4），仅依赖矩阵乘法、保留 bf16 数值稳定。
- 在 Llama3-1B/3B 上系统验证：微调阶段 Muon$^p$ 在 Fineweb/Pile-of-Law 困惑度、GSM8K/Numina 数学 pass@8、OpenCodeInstruct 代码损失、PIQA/ARC/BoolQ/HellaSwag 等下游任务上**一致优于** Muon 与 AdamW；运行时间与 Muon 相当（p=1/3 用三次递推甚至略快）。
- 通过谱几何视角解释 Muon$^p$ 的适用边界：预训练需要均匀全谱更新（Muon 更强），微调位于低内在维子空间、需要保留奇异值信息（Muon$^p$ 更好）；并用 "Muon → Muon$^p$ 课程" 实验直接验证这一谱学习率自适应假说。

## Key Contributions

- **One-parameter generalization** of Muon via $US^pV^\top$ updates, $p\in(0,1)$, with a clean optimality result: Muon$^p$ maximizes the linear loss improvement under the Schatten $q$-norm with $q=1+1/p$ (Theorem 2.1).
- **Negative result:** No fixed univariate polynomial iteration can compute fractional spectral powers (Lemma 2.2). This forces a fundamentally different algorithm than Muon's Newton–Schulz.
- **Constructive solution:** Odd bivariate polynomial recurrences (Theorem 2.3 for realizability via matrix multiplications; Theorem 2.4 for existence of a converging $f$) approximate $US^pV^\top$ using only matrix multiplications, preserving bf16 numerical stability and Muon's compute profile.
- **Empirical wins on billion-scale finetuning:** Llama3-1B/3B improvements on perplexity (Fineweb: 11.70→11.58; Pile-of-Law: 2.578→2.491), math (GSM8K pass@8: 41.76→48.27; Numina: 39.67→42.41), code (OpenCodeInstruct loss 0.2423→0.2382), and commonsense reasoning (avg 54.73→55.03 across 8 tasks).
- **Honest failure-mode analysis:** Muon$^p$ is **worse than Muon for pretraining**; an exact-SVD ablation (Figure 3) shows the gap is intrinsic to the spectrum-flattening regime, not to the approximation error of the polynomial recurrence.
- **Curriculum discovery:** Pretraining with Muon and switching to Muon$^p$ for the final 500 steps yields consistent validation-loss drops across scales — empirically validating the spectral-learning-rate interpretation.

## Methodology

- **Update rule.** Given gradient SVD $G=USV^\top$, replace Muon's $UV^\top$ with $US^pV^\top$ for $p\in(0,1)$. As $p\to 0$ this recovers Muon; as $p\to 1$ it recovers raw $G$ (gradient descent).
- **Algorithm 1 (p=1/3 example):** Iterate $G_{n+1} = G_n + c\,(G - G_n G_n^\top G_n)$ for $N$ steps, then output $G_N$. This is the cubic instance $f(x,y)=x+c(y-x^3)$ of the general recurrence $f(x,y)=x+(y^a-x^b)g(x,y)$ with $p=a/b$.
- **Coefficient choice.** Lemma 3.4 gives the attracting-fixed-point condition $0<g(y^{a/b},y)<\frac{2}{a\,y^{a/b}}\frac{(b-1)}{b}$. Coefficients (Table 5) are tuned by grid search on a sample of 1000 GPT-2 gradients, minimizing $\|G^p - \hat G^p\|_2$.
- **Compute parity with Muon.** Both have time complexity $O(\omega k N)$ where $k\approx\log_2 \deg f$ matrix multiplications per iteration and $N=6$ iterations. Muon$^p$ with $p=1/3$ (cubic) is **slightly faster** than Muon (Table 4: 101.89 ms vs 120.73 ms); $p=1/5$ (quintic) is comparable (116.30 ms).
- **Spectral learning-rate interpretation.** Reweighting yields $\eta_i^{\text{eff}} = \eta\, \sigma_i^{p-1}$; $p>0$ attenuates smaller singular directions more strongly — i.e., Muon$^p$ implements an implicit direction-dependent learning rate.
- **Curriculum.** Switching from Muon ($p=0$) to Muon$^p$ ($p=1/3$) for the final 500 pretraining steps consistently decreases validation loss; the transition is seamless because Muon is the $p=0$ special case.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Llama3-1B base | GSM8K pass@8 ↑ | 46.41 | 41.76 | **48.27** |
| Llama3-1B base | OpenCodeInstruct loss ↓ | 0.2398 | 0.2423 | **0.2382** |

| Commonsense (avg of 8) | Acc ↑ | 54.84 | 54.73 | **55.03** |

Key observation: gains hold both when finetuning an off-the-shelf AdamW-pretrained checkpoint **and** a Muon-pretrained checkpoint, ruling out optimizer-mismatch as the sole driver.

**Pretraining (135M SmolLM, Figure 2):** Muon$^p$ is **worse than Muon** when training from scratch. An exact-SVD ablation (Figure 3) shows the gap is intrinsic to preserving singular values, not to approximation error of the recurrence.

**Curriculum (Figure 5):** Pretrain with Muon, switch to Muon$^p$ for the last 500 steps → consistent validation-loss drop across model scales. Compared favorably to learning-rate decay / constant baselines.

**Effects of $p$ (Figure 4):** Validation loss after finetuning SmolLM-360M on 852M tokens of Fineweb is minimized at an interior $p\in(0,1)$ — not at the Muon ($p=0$) or gradient-descent ($p=1$) endpoints. Different $p$ values vary validation loss by at most 0.01, indicating robustness.

**Effective rank (Figure 6):** Muon$^p$ exhibits a much sharper drop in effective rank during training, consistent with its spectral attenuation of small singular directions.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The fractional-power framing of Muon, the impossibility result (Lemma 2.2), and the constructive use of *bivariate* odd polynomials to retain matrix-multiplication-only structure are genuine technical contributions, not just heuristics. The link to Schatten-$q$ steepest descent is a clean conceptual anchor.
- **Rigor:** Strong for an optimizer paper. Theorem 2.1 gives a closed-form optimality statement; Lemma 2.2 closes off the obvious generalization path; the construction of the polynomial recurrence comes with both realizability (Thm 2.3) and existence (Thm 2.4) proofs. Numerical-stability notes (bf16) are included.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code is open-sourced at https://github.com/princeton-pli/muon-p, hyperparameters and grid-search ranges are documented in Appendix A.1.1/A.1.3, and the polynomial coefficients for each $p$ are tabulated. The default $p=1/3$ is chosen so the recurrence is cubic — a deliberate efficiency choice.
- **Significance:** Meaningful. Finetuning is where most LLM practitioners spend compute today, and a one-line drop-in improvement on top of an already-popular optimizer (Muon) is a useful artifact. The framing also clarifies *why* Muon helps pretraining while Muon$^p$ helps finetuning — a previously hand-wavy distinction.
- **Red flags / open questions:**
  - **Pretraining weakness is admitted but not overcome.** The "Muon → Muon$^p$ curriculum" is presented as evidence and a "future direction," not as a solved pretraining method. The paper does not show that a properly scheduled $p$ (annealing from 0 toward some interior value, or the reverse) is competitive with vanilla Muon pretraining at scale.
  - **Single hyperparameter, but coefficient grid-search per $p$.** Choosing $c$ (and $d$ for $p=1/2$) requires a fresh grid search; the paper does not address transfer of these coefficients across model sizes or layer shapes beyond empirical robustness.
  - **Fairness against stronger baselines is shaky on Numina (Appendix A.1.4).** Muon (0.618 / 0.769 eval loss) beats Muon$^p$ (0.597 / 0.708)? Wait — Muon$^p$ *does* beat Muon (0.597 < 0.618). But PolarGrad-zolo-pd (0.768), PolarGrad-qdwh (0.767), Polar Express (0.767), and AdaMuon (0.750) all score **worse** than even plain Muon. The authors hypothesize these baselines were developed for pretraining and over-flatten Muon's already-flat updates. This is an honest assessment but means the comparison does not clearly show Muon$^p$ as state-of-the-art across optimizer families — only across the Muon/AdamW axis.
  - **No comparison to SOAP / Shampoo / PSGD / K-FAC** — the most relevant modern spectral-preserving baselines are missing.
  - **Random-seed reporting.** "The exact same seed is used throughout different runs" — good for paired comparisons, but no error bars over multiple seeds. With only one seed per (method, lr) cell, the gap between Muon and Muon$^p$ on individual benchmarks (e.g., +1.5 GSM8K pass@8) lacks statistical strength.
  - **The 3B results (Table 7) are puzzling:** Muon$^p$ outperforms Muon at lr=1e-4 (0.531 vs 0.506) but underperforms at lr=5e-5 (0.361 vs 0.455). The authors do not reconcile this — it suggests Muon$^p$'s gains are lr-sensitive in a way that is not fully characterized.
  - **The "when Muon$^p$ is less suitable" analysis is essentially one figure (Figure 2/3).** More thorough ablations across architectures (CNN, MoE), modalities (vision, audio), or tasks (RL) are absent.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical foundation: Schatten-$q$ optimality + impossibility-of-univariate-iteration negative result + constructive bivariate solution.
- One-line drop-in for Muon users; bf16-stable; faster step time than vanilla Muon for $p=1/3$.
- Comprehensive finetuning evidence across reasoning, code, perplexity, and commonsense tasks.
- Honest about failure modes (pretraining) instead of overclaiming universality.
- Open-source code with documented polynomial coefficients per $p$.

## Limitations

- **Pretraining:** Muon$^p$ is worse than Muon from scratch; the proposed curriculum remedy is only validated as a final-500-step perturbation, not as a full pretraining recipe.
- **Hyperparameter surface:** Adds $p$ and (for $p=1/2$) coefficient $d$ on top of existing Muon/AdamW hyperparameters; coefficient grid search is required per $p$.
- **Approximation error of recurrence:** The paper shows empirical robustness across $p$ but does not characterize the error $\|G_N - G^p\|$ theoretically, nor study its interaction with bf16 quantization in detail.
- **Empirical scope:** Limited to language models at 1B/3B scale; no vision, multimodal, RL, or non-transformer architectures. Single seed per (method, lr) cell; lr sensitivity not fully mapped (the 3B Numina result at lr=5e-5 is anomalous).
- **Missing baselines:** No head-to-head with SOAP, Shampoo, PSGD, K-FAC, or Adafactor — the modern spectral-preconditioning family.
- **Curriculum not productionized:** The Muon → Muon$^p$ pretraining schedule is presented as a curiosity, not as a full pretraining recipe with token-compute-matched comparisons.

## Relevance

与"基础模型优化与训练算法"高度相关：Muon$^p$ 是当下热门 Muon 优化器的一参数原理性扩展，对实际大模型微调给出可直接借鉴的改进；同时通过 Schatten-$q$ 视角和预训练/微调的对比，加深了对谱平坦化类优化器何时有用的理解。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can a **scheduled $p(t)$** (e.g., annealing from 0 to some interior value) match or beat vanilla Muon for full pretraining, not just the last 500 steps?
- How does Muon$^p$ interact with **learning-rate warmup and weight decay**? The current experiments re-tune lr per method but leave schedule interactions unexplored.
- Does the **approximation error** of the bivariate recurrence ($\|G_N - G^p\|$ as a function of $N$ and $p$) interact with bf16 precision in ways that matter at larger scale?
- How does Muon$^p$ compare to **SOAP, PSGD, Shampoo, and K-FAC** on the same finetuning and pretraining benchmarks? The current baseline set is narrow.
- Is the **lr sensitivity at 3B** (Muon$^p$ underperforming at lr=5e-5 on Numina) a real effect, a tuning artifact, or an instability? Multiple seeds and longer training would clarify.
- Can the polynomial coefficient $c$ be **adapted per layer or per singular direction** (the paper explicitly flags this as a future direction)? This could remove the per-$p$ grid search.
- Do the gains transfer to **non-LM domains** — vision transformers, diffusion models, RL policies, MoE?
- The **effective-rank drop** under Muon$^p$ is striking — does it predict when LoRA-style low-rank adaptation should be sufficient vs. when full-rank finetuning matters?
