Calibration · LLM-as-a-Judge · LMArena

From Uncertain Judgments to Calibrated Rankings:
Conformal Elo for LLM Evaluation.

SoftElo feeds an LLM judge’s soft win-probabilities into a Bradley–Terry Elo fit, then wraps the LLM–Elo / human–Elo residual with split conformal prediction — turning a leaderboard into a leaderboard with bands.

Authors  Bora Kargi · David Salinas Category  cs.CL Setting  LMArena · 55 held-out models Code  github.com/kargibora/SoftElo
17.9Elo MAE
vs. human-derived Elo · 55-model holdout
No fresh human labels required — the calibration is paid for entirely by soft judge labels and a conformal residual.
Key Finding

Soft labels beat hard labels inside Bradley–Terry.

Replacing the judge’s 0/1/0.5 verdict with a calibrated pAB=σ((sA−sB)/τ) drops the LLM-vs-human Elo gap substantially.

Key Metric
17.9Elo MAE

Across 55 held-out LMArena models, the strongest result reported without large-scale human re-annotation.

Why This Matters

Leaderboards that admit their own error.

Split conformal intervals give a distribution-free, marginal coverage band around every rating — irreducible judge–human disagreement is modeled, not hidden.

Critical Edge

Marginal ≠ conditional. Exchangeability is assumed.

Coverage is averaged over the population, not per subgroup; a new model family can break the band silently. No per-task MAE breakdown is reported.

Pipeline · 2 stages

From a noisy judge verdict to a banded Elo rating.

The pipeline is deliberately two pieces, and you can swap either independently. Stage 1 fixes local (per-battle) uncertainty; Stage 2 adds global (model-level) coverage.

Stage 1 · Soft Bradley–Terry
Calibrate, then MLE/MAP-fit.
pAB = σ((sA − sB) / τ)   with optional Platt / isotonic
{ pAB } → weighted BT → { r̂i }

Variance of Δs is propagated into the BT estimator → per-battle confidence on every rating.

local per-battle
Stage 2 · Split Conformal
Band the residual, not the model.
Ri = | r̂i − r̂ihuman |  on calibration set
interval:   r̂i  ±  Q1−α(R)

By exchangeability the interval carries marginal coverage 1−α over fresh models — regardless of the judge’s bias distribution.

global marginal 1−α
Coverage · marginal vs. point

What the band actually buys you.

Two pictures of the same calibration set. The point estimate shrinks only modestly; the interval is what is calibrated, not the rating.

Point estimate · r̂i
no coverage guarantee
Conformal band · r̂i ± Q
distribution-free, averaged over models

The interval’s width is the (1−α)-quantile of past residuals — so the guarantee is on the population, not on any single model. A reasoning model can sit outside the band and the guarantee is still satisfied.

Uncertainty · local + global

Two layers of “how sure are we?”

Local · per-battle
comes from Var(Δs) in the judge score
Global · per-model
comes from Q1−α(|r̂−rhuman|)
  • Per-battle confidence rides along the BT fit, no extra passes.
  • Per-model coverage rides along the residual, no extra labels.
  • The two layers are additive — and computed from different data.
Red flags · read before you cite

Where the guarantee quietly stops.

  • Marginal, not conditional. A subgroup (e.g. reasoning-only models) can be systematically outside the band.
  • Exchangeability assumed. New model families break it silently; the band does not warn you.
  • One global MAE. No per-task, per-length, per-style breakdown in the abstract.
  • τ + calibration-set size are hyper-parameters; sensitivity is not reported.
  • No ablation isolating the soft-label step from the conformal step.
  • Single leaderboard (LMArena) — transfer to MT-Bench, AlpacaEval, multimodal is unverified.
