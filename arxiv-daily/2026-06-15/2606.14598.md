> **一句话总结：** 在消费级 Ampere GPU（如 RTX 3090）上，Ideogram 4.0 文生图模型的现有 "INT8" 推理实际上只把权重/激活量化再反量化回 bf16 跑 GEMM，从未真正调用 INT8 Tensor Core；本文给出一个 Triton 写的 `int8×int8→int32` 融合 GEMM（含 per-token×per-channel 反量化、bias 折入 epilogue、按 (M,N,K) 36 配置 autotune），单算子 2.8–4.2× 加速、4–8× 快于 unfused 路径，端到端在 768px 上 97.79s（1.095×，n=4）、1024px 上单卡 RTX 3090 156.49s 出图（峰值 23.40 GB），比单卡 NF4 (164.5s) 和双卡 FP8 (172.9s) 都快，并诚实交代 Ampere-only、A100/B200 上反而输给原生 bf16/FP8。

> **TL;DR:** The paper diagnoses and fixes a real production pathology: post-training INT8 (W8A8) for diffusion transformers on consumer Ampere GPUs is, in most current deployments, fake-quant — weights and activations are quantized and immediately dequantized back to bf16 for a `bf16 F.linear`, so the integer tensor cores are never engaged and INT8 ends up slower than FP8/NF4. The fix is a single fused Triton kernel doing `int8×int8→int32` on Ampere's `mma.s8` tensor cores, with per-token (activation) and per-channel (weight) dequantization plus bias folded into the epilogue off the int32 accumulator, and `@triton.autotune` over 36 configurations per (M, N, K). On a healthy RTX 3090 it is bit-exact against `torch._int_mm` (cosine similarity 1.0, no NaNs), 2.8–4.2× faster than bf16 per GEMM (and 4–8× faster than an unfused int8 path), and turns INT8 from the slowest Ideogram 4.0 variant into the fastest. End-to-end at 768px it is 97.79 s/image vs 107.06 s (1.095×, n=4); at 1024px it is 156.49 s/image on a single 24 GB RTX 3090, beating both single-card NF4 (164.5 s) and two-card FP8 (172.9 s), with peak 23.40 GB VRAM and no measurable quality cost on PickScore/CLIPScore. The win is narrow: the same kernel is 1.38× slower on an A100 and 3.49× slower on a B200, and ablations of `torch.compile` and SageAttention INT8 give essentially zero additional gain (graph-breaks; head_dim 256 unsupported). The paper is unusually honest about scope, statistical weakness, and where the result does not transfer.

## 核心贡献

- **指出"INT8 推理比 FP8/NF4 还慢"是软件层面的假象**：消费级 Ampere 上现有 INT8 路径只做 weight/activation 量化再 dequant 回 bf16，INT8 Tensor Core 从未被使用，所以硬件优势完全没有被利用。
- **一个融合的 Triton INT8 GEMM 内核**：在 Ampere `mma.s8` Tensor Core 上做 `int8×int8→int32` 累加，per-token × per-channel 反量化和 bias 折入 epilogue 直接在 int32 累加器上计算；`@triton.autotune` 在 36 个 (M, N, K) 配置上选优；数值上对 `torch._int_mm` bit-exact（cosine 1.0，无 NaN），单算子比 bf16 快 2.8–4.2×，比 unfused int8 路径快 4–8×。
- **Autotuning 是关键设计点**：不 autotune 时 kernel 只比 bf16 快 1.4–2.9×，在 llm-proj 形状上甚至 0.64×（输掉）；autotune 后所有 5 个 DiT GEMM 形状都进入 2.8–4.2× 区间，llm-proj 也升到 3.65×。
- **在 Ideogram 4.0 扩散 Transformer 上端到端验证**：5 个 DiT GEMM 形状（qkv / attn-out / ffn-up / ffn-down / llm-proj）全部 compute-bound（arithmetic intensity 768–6337 ops/byte on RTX 3090）；768px 下 1.095× 加速（n=4）；1024px 单卡 156.49s（峰值 23.40 GB），快过单卡 NF4 (164.5s) 和双卡 FP8 (172.9s)，PickScore/CLIPScore 在统计点估计上无质量损失。
- **诚实的部署地图与失败地图**：胜利仅限消费级 Ampere；同一 kernel 在 A100 上慢 1.38×、B200 上慢 3.49×，因为这些卡原生 bf16 已经极快；`torch.compile` 因 graph break 无收益，SageAttention INT8 因 head_dim=256>128 退化为 FP16 fallback 也无收益。

## Key Contributions

- **Diagnosis of fake-quant INT8 deployment**: Production post-training W8A8 INT8 for diffusion transformers on consumer Ampere GPUs is fake-quant — weights and activations are quantized and immediately dequantized back to bf16, so the integer tensor cores are never engaged. This explains the otherwise-paradoxical "INT8 slower than FP8/NF4" result on cards that *have* fast native INT8.
- **A single fused Triton INT8 GEMM**: `int8×int8→int32` accumulation on Ampere `mma.s8` tensor cores; per-token (activation) and per-channel (weight) dequantization plus bias folded into the epilogue off the int32 accumulator, so the quantized linear is one kernel launch instead of a matmul + separate dequantization. `@triton.autotune` over 36 configurations keyed per (M, N, K).
- **Numerical and per-GEMM speedup**: bit-exact against `torch._int_mm`; dequantized output matches the reference at cosine similarity 1.0 with no NaNs; 2.8–4.2× faster than bf16 per GEMM and 4–8× faster than an unfused int8 path on all five DiT GEMM shapes — all compute-bound (arithmetic intensity 768–6337 ops/byte).
- **Autotuning is load-bearing**: without autotuning the kernel beats bf16 by only ~1.4–2.9× and is *slower* than bf16 on the LLM-projection shape (0.64×); with autotuning, every shape lands in the 2.8–4.2× band, including 3.65× on the previously-regressing llm-proj shape.
- **End-to-end wins on Ideogram 4.0**: 768px is 97.79 s/image vs 107.06 s (1.095×, n=4); 1024px is 156.49 s/image on a single 24 GB RTX 3090 (peak 23.40 GB, 4/4 images), beating both single-card NF4 (164.5 s) and two-card FP8 (172.9 s), with PickScore/CLIPScore parity on point estimates.
- **Honest deployment map and dead-end ablations**: the win is specific to consumer Ampere; on A100 the same kernel is 1.38× slower, on B200 it is 3.49× slower. `torch.compile` and SageAttention INT8 are reported as inert extensions: `torch.compile` graph-breaks at the custom linears, SageAttention cannot handle head_dim=256.

## Methodology

**Target architecture.** Ideogram 4.0 is a 9.3B-parameter flow-matching DiT: 34 layers, 18 heads of dim 256 (hidden 4608), intermediate 12288, two classifier-free-guidance branches of 211 linear layers each. Across these layers there are only **five distinct GEMM shapes (N, K)**: qkv (13824, 4608), attn-out (4608, 4608), ffn-up (12288, 4608), ffn-down (4608, 12288), llm-proj (4608, 53248) — making per-shape autotuning tractable (the autotuner targets five shapes, not hundreds). In-model token count is M ≈ 4110 at 1024px.

**Quantization recipe (reused, not invented).** Per-channel int8 weights, per-token dynamic int8 activations, SmoothQuant with α = 0.5. The arithmetic the kernel must reproduce is therefore fixed in advance; the contribution is making it run on integer tensor cores rather than bf16.

**Diagnosis the kernel corrects.** The deployed "INT8" forward quantizes weights/activations and immediately dequantizes back to bf16, running an ordinary `bf16 F.linear`. Integer tensor cores are never touched; quantization only buys a memory round trip through INT8 storage. This is the fake-quant path that explains why "INT8" can be slower than FP8/NF4.

**Fused kernel design.** A single Triton op performs `int8×int8→int32` accumulation on Ampere's `mma.s8` signed-8-bit MMA units. In the GEMM epilogue, *operating directly on the int32 accumulator*, the kernel applies the per-token activation scale, the per-channel weight scale, and the bias, emitting the dequantized result in one pass. The whole quantized linear is thus one kernel launch rather than matmul + dequant pass. The op is wrapped in `@triton.autotune` over 36 configurations keyed per (M, N, K). Folding the per-token *dynamic* activation scale into the epilogue is the central design point — the zero-overhead scale-fusion trick used in some W4A8 LLM kernels assumes static activation scales, so this work cannot bake them into the weights offline and must apply them in-kernel.

**Integration.** The fused op replaces the fake-quant forward at the DiT's linear layers; the surrounding runtime, quantization recipe, and calibrated scales are untouched. Only the linear path changes — the rest of the forward (attention, normalization, sampling overhead) is unchanged.

**Experimental setup.**
- Frozen prompt sets reused unchanged: calibration n=128, quality benchmark n=200, text-rendering benchmark n=100 with 63 OCR targets. Generation tuples fixed across variants (seed, 48 steps, stated resolution).
- Latency (s/image) reported on a 4-prompt subset of the quality benchmark at fixed seed; authors flag this is n=4 and report single-run point estimates without run-to-run variance.
- Image quality reported with PickScore and CLIPScore on the full quality benchmark; the most quantization-fragile metric (OCR/NED text-rendering) is carried over from the prior recipe and not re-measured for the fused build at any resolution.
- **GPU-health gate**: a throttled RTX 3090 measures ~8.1 TFLOPS bf16 vs a healthy card's ~65.5 TFLOPS (6–8× difference). Every measurement is gated behind a bf16 TFLOPS health check, so speedups are reported against a healthy bf16 baseline.
- Total study cost: ~3 RTX 3090-hours.

## Results & Highlights

**Per-GEMM speedup on a healthy RTX 3090 (Table 1).** All five DiT GEMM shapes are compute-bound (arithmetic intensity 768–6337 ops/byte). Speedup of the autotuned fused kernel over `bf16 F.linear`:
- qkv (13824, 4608): 2.79–3.46×
- attn-out (4608, 4608): 2.86–3.76×
- ffn-up (12288, 4608): 2.78–4.18×
- ffn-down (4608, 12288): 2.94–3.51×
- llm-proj (4608, 53248): 2.95–3.17×

Against an unfused int8 path (`torch._int_mm` followed by a separate dequant), the fused kernel is **4–8× faster** — a direct measurement of the dequant-round-trip cost that the fake-quant path pays.

**End-to-end at 768px (Table 2, n=4):**
- Fake-quant (bf16 matmul): 107.06 s, PickScore 20.14, CLIPScore 21.76
- Fused INT8: 97.79 s, PickScore 21.22, CLIPScore 24.35
- Speedup: 1.095× (~9.5%); quality parity-or-slightly-better on both proxies.

**Headline: 1024px on a single RTX 3090 (Table 3, n=4):**
- FP8 (prior work): 172.9 s on 2 GPUs (dequant-to-bf16 on Ampere)
- NF4 (prior work): 164.5 s on 1 GPU
- INT8 fake-quant (prior work): 184–185 s on 2 GPUs (quantize then dequant to bf16)
- **Fused INT8 (this work): 156.49 s on 1 GPU, peak 23.40 GB, 4/4 images**

Primary criterion (beat FP8 by ~9.5% / ~16 s) is met comfortably. The NF4 stretch margin (~4.9%, ~8 s) is on single-run n=4 data, within unquantified run-to-run variance; the authors read it as "consistent with meeting the stretch target rather than definitively met".

**Hardware specificity (Table 4):** the same fused kernel vs. the fake-quant bf16 path at 1024px:
- A100: 100.85 s vs 73.04 s → fused is 1.38× slower
- B200: 98.72 s vs 28.33 s → fused is 3.49× slower

The B200's native bf16 at 587–610 TFLOPS and the A100's at ~216 TFLOPS leave nothing for the integer path to win back. Quality is unaffected on the A100 (fused PickScore 17.49 vs fake-quant 17.54). One confound: the kernel is autotuned for sm_86 (RTX 3090) and is *not* retuned for A100/B200, so kernel sub-optimality and fast native bf16 are not separable on those cards.

**Ablations at 768px (Table 5):**
- + `torch.compile`: 97.74 s — graph-breaks at the dynamo-disabled linear layers (effectively zero gain).
- + SageAttention INT8: 97.82 s — DiT's head_dim=256 exceeds the library's 128 limit, forcing a 100% FP16 fallback (zero gain).

**Roofline and Amdahl analysis.** All five GEMMs are compute-bound, so moving the multiply onto faster integer tensor cores translates into real wall-clock savings. The speedup holds across M = 512 to 16384, a range that contains the in-model M ≈ 4110 at 1024px. The ~3.5× per-GEMM win shrinks to ~9.5% end-to-end because **the DiT linear GEMMs are only ~12% of the forward** (the rest sits in attention, normalization, sampling overhead); the other ~88% of the forward is untouched by the kernel. This is described as a structural ceiling for this approach on this model.

**Memory result.** At 1024px the fused build peaks at 23.40 GB (direct 3090 measurement), fitting a single 24 GB card; the fake-quant path peaks at 26.7 GB (measured on A100, used to infer OOM on a 24 GB 3090 since peak VRAM is not perfectly card-invariant). The speed win and the single-card feasibility are two aspects of the same fix.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The fusion structure (dequant-in-epilogue, off the integer accumulator) is well established one bit-width down in LLM serving (QQQ, ABQ-LLM, LiquidGEMM, Multi-Scale-Dequant, FireQ); the *diffusion-transformer* angle and the consumer-Ampere INT8 realization are the new contributions, and they are clearly stated. The autotuning-as-load-bearing point is a useful methodological observation: it is not the autotuner, but the observation that without it the LLM-proj shape regresses to 0.64× of bf16 — i.e. a single misconfigured shape can sink a kernel launch.
- **Rigor.**
  - Numerical correctness: bit-exact vs `torch._int_mm`, cosine 1.0, no NaNs, on all five shapes. This is the gold-standard for an INT8 GEMM kernel.
  - **Statistical rigor is the weak point**, and the authors know it. End-to-end latency is n=4 single-run; the headline 1024px comparison mixes a single-card fused measurement against two-card FP8 baselines (single-vs-two-GPU asymmetry); the OCR/NED text-rendering metric — arguably the most quantization-fragile one for an Ideogram model that markets text rendering — is *not* re-measured for the fused build at any resolution. The NF4 margin (~4.9%, ~8 s) is "within the run-to-run variance we did not quantify".
  - The 12% GEMM-share figure is *inferred* from the measured per-GEMM and end-to-end speedups via Amdahl's law, not from an independent layer-wise profile. A direct profile remains future work.
  - The unfused INT8 baseline is `torch._int_mm` + separate dequant. A tuned vendor INT8 GEMM (cuBLASLt / CUTLASS) was planned but is **not** reported — and beating a tuned vendor kernel is the harder claim. Without it, the "fake-quant is slower than a properly-fused Triton kernel" point is clean, but the "Triton INT8 kernel beats the best possible vendor INT8 path" point is left open.
  - On A100/B200, the kernel is **not retuned** for sm_80/sm_100, so kernel sub-optimality and "native bf16 is already fast" are not separable. The deployment-map conclusion survives regardless, but the magnitude of the loss on those cards is uncertain.
- **Reproducibility.** Compute cost is small (~3 RTX 3090-hours). The fused build is gated and private under a research-only license — the *kernel* and recipe are not separately released as a reusable library. Independent re-runs on a different DiT or different consumer Ampere SKU (e.g. RTX 3060/3080) are not demonstrated. The 4-prompt latency subset is reused unchanged from the prior paper, which is good for cross-paper comparability but limits variance estimation.
- **Significance.** Real, narrow, and useful. The "your INT8 build is fake-quant" diagnosis is widely applicable to anyone running post-training INT8 for diffusion transformers on consumer GPUs and is a corrective to a common silent deployment failure. The fused-kernel pattern transfers (with caveats) to other DiTs whose linear GEMMs are a non-trivial share of forward. The deployment map is a genuinely helpful operational decision rule.
- **Red flags.**
  - The 1024px FP8 baseline is from the same group's prior paper and is *two-GPU*; the single-card fused INT8 winning a comparison against a two-GPU baseline deserves a footnote, and the authors provide it (multi-GPU split overhead is a confound), but a direct two-GPU fused measurement is missing.
  - The PickScore/CLIPScore point estimates on n=4 are framed as "parity" — they are not, statistically, a parity claim.
  - Head_dim = 256 blocks SageAttention — a *very* common DiT configuration (Flux uses head_dim 128; SD3 has similar). The 9.5% ceiling is therefore likely to hold for many similar models until a head_dim-256-compatible INT8 attention kernel arrives.
  - The paper is published in 2026 and the prior paper it builds on is also 2026, so both halves of the comparison come from the same team within days — risk of shared confounders is non-trivial.

## Strengths

- **Pinpoints a real, common deployment failure mode** (fake-quant INT8 paths) with clear, reproducible evidence: cosine 1.0, bit-exact match, and 2.8–4.2× / 4–8× speedups against clean baselines (`bf16 F.linear` and unfused `torch._int_mm` + dequant).
- **Kernel design is principled and well-targeted**: per-token dynamic activation scale must be applied in the epilogue (not baked into weights) because it is *dynamic*; bias is folded into the same epilogue; autotuning covers the full (M, N, K) shape space at all five DiT GEMM shapes.
- **End-to-end speedups are backed by direct quality checks** (PickScore/CLIPScore) and against the strongest alternatives (FP8, NF4) on real hardware, with peak VRAM reported.
- **Unusually honest about scope**: explicit deployment envelope (consumer Ampere only), explicit dead-end ablations (`torch.compile`, SageAttention), explicit statement of run-to-run variance they did *not* quantify, explicit statement of metrics they did *not* re-measure (OCR/NED), and explicit confounds (single-vs-two-GPU comparison, kernel not retuned for A100/B200).
- **Quantitative ceiling analysis via Amdahl's law** makes the 9.5% end-to-end number interpretable rather than disappointing: ~12% of forward is in linear GEMMs, so ~3.5× speedup there should yield ~10% end-to-end. Theory and measurement match.
- **Small compute footprint** (~3 RTX 3090-hours) makes the result easy to verify.

## Limitations

- **Hardware scope is narrow**: only consumer Ampere (RTX 3090); H100/A100/B200 are not only unsupported but explicitly worse on the same kernel.
- **Statistical weakness**: n=4 for end-to-end latency, no run-to-run variance, no significance test. The 9.5% headline margin and the NF4 ~4.9% margin are point estimates.
- **The headline 1024px comparison mixes single-card fused INT8 with two-card FP8 and two-card fake-quant baselines**, and the authors flag this. A direct two-card fused measurement is missing.
- **OCR/NED text-rendering quality is not re-measured** for the fused build at any resolution — ironic for an Ideogram model whose text rendering is part of the value proposition. Quality parity at 1024px is established on PickScore only (and only on the A100, where the kernel is not the speed choice).
- **Unfused baseline is `torch._int_mm` + dequant**; a tuned cuBLASLt / CUTLASS INT8 GEMM baseline is absent. The 4–8× over unfused is meaningful, but "best in class" is not established.
- **Kernel is hand-tuned via autotuning to Ideogram 4.0's five GEMM shapes**; portability of the speedup to other diffusion transformers / different MLP:attn ratios / different sequence lengths is not demonstrated.
- **A100/B200 numbers are not retuned** (sm_80/sm_100), so kernel sub-optimality and fast native bf16 are not separable.
- **No layer-wise profile**: the ~12% GEMM share is *inferred* from Amdahl's law applied to the measured per-GEMM and end-to-end speedups, not directly measured.
- **Gated, research-only release**: the fused build is private under a non-commercial license; the kernel and recipe are not released as a reusable library.
- **No human evaluation**: quality claims rest on PickScore/CLIPScore proxies; no FID, ImageReward, or per-prompt breakdown.

## Relevance

与你的研究方向直接相关：消费级 GPU 上扩散模型的 INT8 推理加速。文生图量化是当前 LLM/多模态推理优化的活跃方向，Ideogram 4.0 的真实部署经验对工业落地有参考价值，特别是"假 INT8 路径"这个工程坑值得收藏——这意味着任何声称 W8A8 比 FP8/NF4 慢的部署结果，都应该先审计一下 GEMM 是否真的走到了 INT8 Tensor Core。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- **Can the dequant-in-epilogue pattern be extended to W4A8 or W8A4** to push memory-bandwidth wins while keeping tensor-core utilization, and what is the per-token dynamic activation scale interaction at sub-int8 weights?
- **Is the ~12% DiT-linear share stable across other diffusion transformers** (SD3, Flux, SDXL-Turbo), or do attention-dominant DiTs make the 9.5% ceiling lower? Conversely, do U-Net-style diffusion models have a higher GEMM share that would make the same kernel deliver a much larger end-to-end win?
- **What would a head_dim=256 INT8 attention kernel look like** (e.g. splitting head_dim into two 128-d sub-heads, or a block-wise quantization scheme), and how much of the 9.5% ceiling would that unlock? Attention is ~88% of the forward in this analysis, so the upper bound is large.
- **A graph-safe fused custom op that survives `torch.compile`** is identified as the second of three next steps. What is the smallest change to the Triton kernel (e.g. registering it as a custom op, avoiding graph-breaking dynamo-traced code in the linear replacement) that composes with `torch.compile`?
- **A direct two-GPU fused INT8 measurement at 1024px** would retire the single-vs-two-GPU confound in the headline comparison and test whether the speedup is purely a function of compute throughput or partly a function of single-card memory fit.
- **How robust is the INT8 win under batched or CFG-parallel inference**, and what is the latency variance profile across hundreds of seeds, resolutions, and prompt lengths?
- **On A100/B200, can a hybrid strategy** (use the native bf16/FP8 paths and reserve INT8 for the few shapes where it wins) recover best-of-all-worlds latency? The autotune search over 36 configs per shape on sm_80 / sm_100 would be the natural next step.
- **Is there a vendor INT8 GEMM (cuBLASLt / CUTLASS) that already does what this kernel does** but is simply not wired into the typical PyTorch INT8 path? If so, the engineering lesson is "use the vendor library", not "write a Triton kernel".
