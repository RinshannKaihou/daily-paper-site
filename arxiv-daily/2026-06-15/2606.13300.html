Why This Matters

Zero-calibration, zero-second-order PTQ for operational weather foundation models where held-out data is scarce or private.

Critical Edge

Pangu caps at 1.67× because onnx2torch cannot stably support INT4/INT1 activations — the deployment stack, not the algorithm.

◎ Methodology in Four Moves

TQS reframes a quantized forecasting model as a discrete-time map xt+1 = Fθ(xt). The score is a finite-time growth rate: the larger the divergence of the perturbed rollout, the more a layer must be protected.

01
Probe one layer with Gaussian or quantizer-matched noise
02
Roll out both nominal and perturbed trajectories
03
Compute finite-time Lyapunov-like growth rate γ
04
Allocate bits via MCKP under a storage budget
  • Decoupled. γ is computed once per layer and reused across bit-widths and quantizers.
  • Calibration-free. A single forward pass per layer — no backprop, no Fisher/Hessian.
  • Black-box friendly. γgauss only needs inference on compiled or frozen ONNX graphs.

↯ Architectural Surprise

Sensitivity concentrates at the I/O boundary in forecasting transformers, not in FFN-down projections as LLM PTQ literature claims.

  • TimesFM: input tokenizer at 97.7th γ percentile; backbone self-attention at 25.6th.
  • Pangu: five I/O scaffolding blocks at 92.9th percentile.
  • Aurora: top five positions are decoder atmospheric heads (q, t, u, v, z).

⚡ Free Compression & Speed

Layers whose γ hits the sentinel floor are "dead" — quantize them to the lowest tier for free.

  • TimesFM: 3 quantile output heads at γ ≈ −69, 3.4% of bit budget.
  • Aurora: 36 deep-stage ln-modulation modulators at γ = −5.31, 42% of bit budget.
  • Speed: 4.6–5.1× faster than GPTQ/GPTAQ/QEP amortized across Pareto points (46 min/pt on TimesFM).

✓ Strengths vs. Limitations

✓ Strengths

  • A priori, forward-only sensitivity independent of quantizer or bit-width
  • Zero calibration, zero second-order approximations, zero backprop
  • Works on compiled / frozen ONNX networks (γgauss)
  • One sensitivity sweep supports every compression target
  • Honest negative result reported (W3 loss on TimesFM)

⚠ Limitations

  • Pangu capped at 1.67× by onnx2torch INT4/INT1 instability
  • Probe bit-widths differ across models (6-bit vs 8-bit)
  • Single sweep still costs 32–57 min per model
  • Hessian comparison only on Aurora (n=70 layers)
  • No public code link in the visible text
