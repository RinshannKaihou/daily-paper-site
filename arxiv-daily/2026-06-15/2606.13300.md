> **一句话总结：** 把后训练量化（PTQ）重新建模为离散时间动力系统的稳定性问题，提出基于有限时 Lyapunov 指数思想的轨迹量化敏感度分数 TQS，并据此构建无需校准数据/二阶近似的混合精度框架 TQS-PTQ；在 Aurora、TimesFM-2.5、Pangu-Weather 三个时序基础模型上把 accuracy–compression Pareto 边界向高压缩端推进了一大截。

> **TL;DR:** TQS measures how a per-layer quantization perturbation propagates through an autoregressive forecast rollout (a discrete-time dynamical system), and uses the resulting finite-time growth rate as a sensitivity score. TQS is computed by a *single forward pass per layer* (no calibration data, no Hessian/Fisher, no backprop) and is decoupled from any specific quantizer or bit-width — enabling a priori mixed-precision planning even for compiled / black-box networks. TQS-PTQ then assigns bit-widths via an MCKP allocator under a target storage budget. On Aurora-small (113M), TimesFM-2.5 (200M), and frozen-ONNX Pangu-Weather (277M), TQS-PTQ reaches ≥32× compression on Aurora at ≤1% ERA5-MAE degradation, ~4× on TimesFM, and 1.67× on Pangu while avoiding the uniform-W4 collapse. It also rediscovers a counterintuitive architectural fact: for forecasting transformers, sensitivity concentrates in the I/O boundary (input embeddings and output heads) — not in the FFN-down projections that LLM PTQ literature has fixated on.

## 核心贡献
- **TQS（Trajectory-based Quantization Sensitivity Score）**：以离散时间动力系统视角刻画单层量化扰动在自回归滚动预测中的有限时增长率 γ，与具体量化器/位宽完全解耦
- **TQS-PTQ 混合精度分配器**：基于 TQS 分数的多选择背包（MCKP）分配器，加贪心消融版，可在任意目标压缩比下做位宽规划，且只需一次 sensitivity 扫描即可在所有压缩目标上复用
- **零数据、零二阶、零梯度**：完全不依赖校准数据、Hessian/Fisher 近似或反传，可作用于编译后或黑盒网络（ONNX、Pangu）
- **跨架构分析**：在 Aurora、TimesFM-2.5、Pangu-Weather 上系统验证，发现时序基础模型的量化敏感度集中于 I/O 边界（输入嵌入/输出头），与 LLM 文献中"FFN-down 最敏感"的结论相反
- **"免费压缩"识别**：自动标记扰动下输出几乎不变的"惰性层"（如 TimesFM 的 3 个分位输出头、 Aurora 的 36 个 ln modulation 调制器），把它们压到最低位宽且不损失精度
- **可解释性副产品**：TQS 重新发现了 Aurora 的 Perceiver→Swin→变量专用头 架构层级，无需任何结构先验或梯度信息

## Key Contributions
- Introduce TQS, a Lyapunov-inspired sensitivity score computed from rollout trajectory divergence, decoupled from quantizer and bit-width choice
- Propose TQS-PTQ, a calibration-free, second-order-free mixed-precision PTQ framework (MCKP + greedy variants) that amortizes a single sensitivity sweep across many compression targets
- Provide a cross-architecture study on three forecasting foundation models (Aurora-small, TimesFM-2.5, Pangu-Weather), showing that I/O projection layers (not FFN-down) carry quantization sensitivity
- Identify and exploit "free compression": layers whose γ hits the sentinel floor are quantized to the lowest tier with zero accuracy cost
- Report detailed ablations: probe distribution (γgauss vs γquant), allocator (greedy vs MCKP), bottom-tier (INT1/2/4), and FP32 budget
- Provide a reusable TQS sensitivity-ranking procedure whose single sweep supports multiple compression targets across models

## Methodology

### Formal TQS score
Let the FP model define a discrete-time map x_{t+1} = F_θ(x_t). For a quantizable weight tensor ℓ with quantized counterpart θ̃_ℓ = Q(θ_ℓ), the single-context, single-horizon TQS is

  γ_ℓ(T) = (1/T) · ln( ‖Ŷ^{(ℓ)}_{1:T} − Ŷ^{(0)}_{1:T}‖ / (‖δθ_ℓ‖_F + ε) )

where Ŷ^{(0)} is the nominal trajectory and Ŷ^{(ℓ)} the perturbed one. The task-level score averages over |S| independent context windows. Layers whose γ is large have their quantization error amplified over the rollout and are protected; layers with γ → −∞ (inactive under perturbation) are "dead" and free to compress.

### Decoupling from quantizer (γgauss variant)
To avoid coupling TQS to a specific bit-width / quantizer, the authors define a Gaussian probe variant γ_gauss: δθ_ℓ ~ N(0, σ²I), with σ scaled so ‖δθ_ℓ‖_F matches a reference symmetric quantizer's perturbation norm. The rank agreement between γ_gauss and γ_quant on Pangu reaches Spearman ρ = 0.96 at block granularity; on Aurora the disagreement (ρ ≈ −0.12 between Hessian and γ_gauss) is itself a finding: Gaussian and bounded-rounding noise encode different sensitivity information.

### TQS-PTQ allocator
Given per-layer scores {γ_ℓ}, sizes {n_ℓ}, target compression C, and tier set T = {(t_k, b_k)}:
1. Compute bit budget B and FP32 mass B* from C and p_FP32
2. Assign layers with γ_ℓ ≤ γ_min to bottom tier t_K (dead-layer rule)
3. Sort remaining by decreasing γ_ℓ
4. Promote top-ranked layers to FP32 (t_1) until B* is spent
5. Solve the remaining tier assignment as a multiple-choice knapsack (MCKP) — or use greedy promotion from t_K as a faster alternative

### Probe horizons (per model)
- TimesFM-2.5: T_max = 100 steps (multi-step forecast)
- Aurora-small: T_max = 120 steps (30 days × 6h)
- Pangu-Weather: T_max = 4 (24h lead time at 6h step)

## Results & Highlights

### Headline compression
- **Aurora-small**: ≥32× compression at ≤1% ERA5-MAE degradation
- **TimesFM-2.5**: ~4× on weather; 16× on standard benchmarks (ETTh1/2, ETTm1/2, EXCHANGE, WEATHER) with 1.7–24× lower MAE than uniform-W2 PTQ
- **Pangu-Weather**: C = 1.67× (3.57× at block level) with only 1.2% mean per-variable MAE degradation, achieving the lowest error among quantized Pangu variants and avoiding the catastrophic uniform-W4 collapse

### Win counts
- 9/9 Aurora variables at the matched W2 grid
- 46/57 TimesFM variables at C=16 (across 6 datasets)
- 9/9 Pangu variables vs. the strongest uniform-W8 baseline
- **Total: 64/75 variable–model wins**

### Free compression (dead layers)
- TimesFM-2.5: 3 output-quantile heads at γ ≈ −69 (FTLE-Task-Gauss), 3.4% of the bit budget
- Aurora-small: 36 deep-stage ln modulation modulators at γ = −5.31, 42% of the bit budget
- Pangu-Weather: none — block granularity (8 params/block, 28 blocks) washes out fine-grained inert effects

### Wall-clock per Pareto point
- TimesFM-2.5: TQS-PTQ 7.7h total / 10 points (46m/pt) vs GPTQ 3.5h/pt, GPTAQ 3.9h/pt, QEP 3.9h/pt — 4.6–5.1× faster amortized
- Aurora-small: TQS-PTQ 8.5h/16 points (32m/pt) vs GPTQ 38.5m/pt, GPTAQ 53.9m/pt — 1.2–1.7× faster
- Pangu-Weather: TQS-PTQ 57.5m/pt over 12 mixed-precision points, GPTAQ 66.9m/pt — TQS-PTQ is both faster and lower-MAE
- RTN baseline is trivially fast (2.0m / 1.4m / 2.4s) but loses on accuracy

### Architectural findings (cross-model)
- **Sensitivity concentrates at the I/O boundary** in all three architectures, diverging from the FFN-down-centric bottlenecks reported in LLM PTQ (Dettmers+ 2022, Lin+ 2024, Xiao+ 2023)
  - TimesFM-2.5: input tokenizer modules at 97.7th γ-rank percentile, point-prediction head at 95.3rd, backbone self-attention at 25.6th (least sensitive)
  - Pangu-Weather: five I/O scaffolding blocks at 92.9th percentile, 11 body blocks at 21.4th
  - Aurora-small: top five γ-rank positions are decoder atmos heads (q, t, u, v, z)
- **Moderately heavy-tailed**: top 10% of layers account for 17–24% of total γ-shift (vs. <1% outlier behavior in LLMs)
- **Asymmetric per-noise-model behavior**: TQS-quant protects the output heads (5 atmospheric heads → FP32); TQS-gauss protects the input-side embeddings and encoder MLPs — different "which layers are sensitive" answer from a single rank

### Hessian vs TQS comparison
On 70 Aurora layers, tr(H_ℓ) (GPTQ-style) vs γ has only weak-to-moderate agreement:
- vs Task-TQS-Quant: ρ = +0.47, p = 0.005
- vs TQS-Quant: ρ = +0.23 (n.s.)
- vs TQS-Gauss variants: ρ ≈ −0.12 (mildly anti-correlated)

This means TQS is not a re-parameterization of curvature; the noise model (Gaussian vs structured-bounded) is the dominant axis of disagreement. Hessian methods uniformly assign the same bit-width to all output heads; TQS pushes the chaotic-variable heads (q, t, u, v, z) to FP32 and demotes the others — consistent with the Lyapunov view that these variables have the highest intrinsic exponents in atmospheric physics.

### Honest negatives
- On TimesFM at the lighter **W3 grid** (uniform baselines), TQS-PTQ loses 25% of cells (14/57 wins) — the heavier TQS-PTQ precision floor does not yet need to be spent at this compression level. The TQS-PTQ C=12 frontier still wins.
- **Pangu capped at 1.67×** (or 3.57× at block level) — much lighter compression than TimesFM/Aurora — because the onnx2torch export does not stably support INT4/INT1 activations on Pangu. The choice of tier set is constrained by deployment stack, not the algorithm.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: The dynamical-systems framing of PTQ sensitivity is fresh and well-motivated. The decoupling claim (a priori, no calibration, no second-order) is a real conceptual departure from the GPTQ/HAWQ lineage. The cross-architecture finding that I/O boundary > FFN-down in forecasting transformers is genuinely new and useful.

**Rigor**: Solid. Three architectures, six datasets, four PTQ baselines (RTN, GPTQ, GPTAQ, QEP), and explicit matched-comparison protocols. The Hessian-vs-TQS rank correlation is computed on n=70 Aurora layers with proper significance tests. The honest negative result at W3 on TimesFM is appreciated. Weaknesses: (a) probe bit-widths differ across models (6-bit on TimesFM/Aurora, 8-bit on Pangu) — not always clear this doesn't bias the γ_quant ranking; (b) per-variable MAE on Aurora is reported with bootstrap CIs, but the main Pareto curves in Figure 1 don't always show them; (c) the Aurora-Small "smaller raw γ range" caveat is acknowledged but not fully reconciled.

**Reproducibility**: Good on methodology, partial on implementation. Algorithm 1 is fully specified. Per-model evaluation protocols and calibration schedules are detailed. But: no public code link in the visible text, and the Pangu path depends on onnx2torch (which the authors note returns NaN for QEP). The dead-layer threshold γ_min = −50 on TimesFM (vs "distribution minimum" on Aurora) is heuristic and may not transfer.

**Significance**: High for the operational weather/forecasting deployment community. Mixed-precision PTQ for foundation models without calibration data is a practical pain point (privacy, no held-out data, compiled models), and TQS's amortization story (one sweep, many compression targets) is genuinely useful. The I/O-vs-FFN finding is a useful correction to assumptions ported over from LLM quantization literature.

**Red flags**:
1. "Works for black-box networks" — true only for the γ_gauss variant; γ_quant requires defining a symmetric quantizer to get the Frobenius reference norm, which implicitly touches the model
2. Wall-clock comparisons use different GPUs (RTX 6000 Ada for apply/eval, H200 for the Pangu sweep), which is fine for amortization arguments but slightly muddles absolute comparisons
3. The "single sensitivity sweep reused across compression targets" is a real win, but the cost is still ~46 min per architecture per dataset — not free
4. QEP returns NaN on Pangu under onnx2torch — that's a baseline failure, not a TQS win
5. Win counts (64/75) are computed at specific matched-compression operating points; the W3 mid-compression result shows TQS-PTQ can be dominated at intermediate targets

## Strengths
- A priori, forward-only sensitivity — independent of specific quantizer, bit-width, or quantizer internals
- Zero calibration data, zero second-order approximations, zero backprop through the quantized model
- Works on compiled / black-box networks (frozen ONNX) — practically important
- Cross-architecture finding (I/O boundary > FFN-down) is a useful correction to LLM-PTQ priors
- Amortization: one sensitivity sweep supports all compression targets
- Identifies "free compression" (dead layers) automatically and exploits it
- Principled Lyapunov/dynamical-systems framing rather than ad-hoc heuristics
- Honest negative result reported (W3 on TimesFM)

## Limitations
- Pangu capped at 1.67× compression due to onnx2torch tier-set constraints — the full algorithm cannot be exercised on this model
- Probe bit-widths differ across models (6-bit vs 8-bit) — comparability of γ_quant across architectures is not strictly guaranteed
- Wall-clock cost of one sensitivity sweep is non-trivial (32–57 min per model); not competitive with RTN at the very lightest compression
- Wins uniformly at heavy compression (W2, ~16×) but loses at mid-compression (W3, ~10×) on TimesFM
- Hessian comparison is only done on Aurora (n=70 layers); cross-model generalization of the rank-correlation findings is not established
- The "black-box" claim is restricted to γ_gauss; γ_quant still requires defining a reference quantizer
- "No code link" — partial reproducibility concern

## Relevance
该工作直接命中"模型量化、时序基础模型高效部署"两条研究主线：将混合精度 PTQ 从依赖校准数据/二阶信息的范式推进到"先验、零校准、零梯度"的新范式，并在 Aurora / Pangu-Weather 这类气象/地球系统大模型上给出可操作的压缩-精度 Pareto 边界，对资源受限场景（如发展中地区气象服务、嵌入式部署）具有明确实用价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Can TQS be extended to non-rollout models (e.g., one-shot image classifiers) by defining a synthetic rollout over depth or over input perturbation sequences?
- How sensitive is the γ ranking to the choice of reference quantizer bit-width in γ_quant? Is the 6-vs-8-bit difference across models a confounder?
- Is the Hessian-vs-TQS disagreement (ρ ≈ −0.12) consistent across other forecasting models, or is Aurora-specific?
- Can the I/O-boundary finding be used as a *prior* for a faster heuristic (e.g., rank by "distance from input/output" as a coarse proxy for γ)?
- The W3 mid-compression loss on TimesFM suggests an adaptive tier-set scheme where the FP32 fraction p_FP32 scales with the compression target — has this been explored?
- For diffusion-model sampling (long iterative rollout, but not a single dynamical system), can TQS-PTQ transfer with a redefinition of the rollout horizon?
- How does TQS-PTQ behave under distribution shift in deployment — does the sensitivity ranking generalize to out-of-distribution weather regimes?
- The authors mention QEP returns NaN on Pangu under onnx2torch — is the same true for other compiled / fused-operator deployment stacks, and is there a way to detect this a priori?
