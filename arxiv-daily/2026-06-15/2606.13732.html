When Sample Selection Bias Precipitates Model Collapse

In privacy-bound data silos, the filter meant to stop recursive synthetic-data collapse becomes the very mechanism that accelerates it — and diversity dies as a power law.

Selection Becomes the Collapse Driver

Under siloed verification, each verifier sees only a biased slice of the target manifold. Top-α selection against that local reference makes covariance collapse to zero almost surely, flipping selection from defense to cause.

FID 71

Scheme I on CIFAR-10

Beats Random (106), K-means (102), CenterMatch (116), and CovMatch (115) after 10 recursive iterations under non-IID ExDir partitions.

Real Silos Cannot Pool Raw Data

Hospitals, banks, and proprietary labs must verify synthetic data against local, fragmented references. Without a shared global proxy, their filters silently amplify collapse.

Theory Is Gaussian; Scope Is Narrow

Theorems assume locally Gaussian/concave scores; experiments are DDPM-only on image data. Adversarial robustness of the Wasserstein proxy is explicitly left open.

Core Mechanism

The paper studies recursive synthetic-data training under two paradigms — Replace and Accumulate — and adds a realistic twist: the verifier is siloed, seeing only a small, biased local reference.

  • Theorem 1: Accumulate + top-α selection under a local target still drives covariance to zero almost surely; the mean drifts toward the biased target.
  • Theorem 2: Diversity decays at an asymptotic power-law rate, Tr(Σ̄ₜ) ~ O(t⁻ᵠ), governed by the spectral gap of a dissipation matrix.
  • Theorem 3: A Wasserstein-based generalization bound shows downstream predictors are hurt proportionally to the proxy–target distance.

Mitigation

Scheme I — Collaborative Geodesic Interpolation

Online, accurate, rebuilt per candidate pool. Uses submodular batch selection with a (1−1/e) greedy guarantee.

Scheme II — Wasserstein Barycenter

Offline proxy reusable across rounds; decoupled from candidate pool size N. Monotonically convergent.

Both schemes share only transport plans / barycentric proxies — no raw data crosses silos.

Striking Empirical Signature

In the non-IID setting, established selection baselines can be worse than Random. When CenterMatch is given only the Airplane class as local reference, the model's predicted class proportions rapidly tilt toward Airplane while the other nine CIFAR-10 classes are pruned — a direct visualization of confirmation-bias collapse.

This is the paper's most memorable result: selection without a global reference can prune the very diversity it claims to protect.

Strengths

  • Conceptual inversion is novel and well-motivated.
  • Three theorems cover existence, rate, and downstream impact.
  • Privacy-preserving Wasserstein proxy is practically relevant.
  • Power-law rate is falsifiable and cleanly derived.

Limitations

  • Theory relies on Gaussian / locally-concave assumptions.
  • Only DDPM on image datasets; no text/LLM experiments.
  • No oracle or modern selection baselines.
  • Adversarial robustness of proxy explicitly unaddressed.

Open Questions

  • How does power-law decay scale with silo count K and skew α?
  • Can the framework extend to discrete token-space LLMs?
  • Can proxy computation be made differentially private end-to-end?
  • What breaks when silos have wildly different supports?
Bottom line: The paper reframes sample selection under data silos as a collapse accelerator rather than a collapse cure, proves the collapse dynamics with three theorems, and offers an initial privacy-preserving fix via Wasserstein geometry — but its theory is Gaussian and its empirical scope is image-only DDPM.
