> **一句话总结：** 在 Gemma 2B / Qwen 1.8B / Yi 6B / Llama-2 7B / Llama-3 8B 五个开源聊天模型上系统比较 Diff-in-Means（方向消融、激活加法）与 INLP（零空间投影 α=1、反事实翻转 α=2）四类线性干预，发现 INLP 反事实翻转在拒绝抑制上与 DiM 方向消融相当但零空间投影偏弱；几何上零空间投影把变换后的激活压到"有害/无害"聚类之间，而反事实翻转推入对立聚类——作者将其初步解读为"概念缺失"与"概念对立"在模型内部被以不同方式编码。

> **TL;DR:** This preliminary work benchmarks two INLP-derived interventions (nullspace projection, α=1; counterfactual flipping, α=2) against the diff-in-means (DiM) baseline of Arditi et al. (2024) for steering refusal in safety-tuned chat models. Across five open-weight chat models, **INLP counterfactual flipping matches DiM directional ablation on refusal suppression**, while **INLP nullspace projection is consistently weaker** (partially failing on Qwen and Llama-2). Restricting INLP to its leading directions (k0.8) preserves most of the suppression effect at near-baseline perplexity, exposing a tunable capability–effect trade-off. The most novel finding is geometric: the same INLP subspace, used with α=1 vs α=2, lands transformed activations in **qualitatively different regions** — nullspace projection collapses them *between* the harmful and harmless clusters, while counterfactual flipping flips them to the opposite cluster. The authors interpret this as tentative evidence that the model encodes the *absence* of a concept differently from its *opposite* — but they flag this as preliminary and note that "refusal" is a poor test bed for sharpening the distinction.

## 核心贡献

- 在五个开源安全微调聊天模型（Gemma 2B-IT、Qwen 1.8B-Chat、Yi 6B-Chat、Llama-2 7B-Chat、Llama-3 8B-Instruct）上对 DiM 与 INLP 两类线性干预进行系统对照实验。
- 实证表明 INLP **反事实翻转（α=2）** 在拒绝抑制任务上与 DiM 方向消融效果相当，而 **INLP 零空间投影（α=1）** 在 Qwen / Llama-2 上显著弱于 DiM 方向消融甚至几乎无效。
- 指出 INLP 子空间内只保留前导方向（k0.8，即前 k 个使分类器仍达 80% 验证准确率的方向）即可保留大部分抑制效果，同时将困惑度退化压到 0.05 以内，提供可调能力–效果权衡旋钮。
- 通过 PCA 几何可视化与目标聚类拟合度（Figure 1、2）揭示两种 INLP 干预激活空间定位的差异：α=1 把变换激活压到有害/无害聚类之间，α=2 推入对立聚类。
- 结合 LLM-judge 与子串匹配的细粒度拒绝原因分析（Appendix C.2），指出 DiM 激活加法（ActAdd）在无害侧的高"拒绝率"部分源自退化的循环输出，而非真正的原则性拒绝。
- 显式讨论了 INLP 与 LEACE 等其他概念擦除方法的关系，并把"在具有清晰语义对立的属性（代词性别、真假、情感极性）上重做实验"列为下一步。

## Key Contributions

- **Systematic comparison** of two DiM interventions (directional ablation, activation addition) and two INLP interventions (nullspace projection α=1, counterfactual flipping α=2) on five open-weight safety-tuned chat models from five different families.
- **Empirical evidence** that INLP counterfactual flipping matches DiM directional ablation on refusal suppression, while INLP nullspace projection lags consistently — with model-specific failures (Qwen, Llama-2). This is the first systematic INLP-vs-DiM steering comparison the authors are aware of (the AxBench evaluation, Wu et al. 2025, includes DiffMean, PCA, LAT, probe, and supervised steering vector but not INLP).
- **Tunable capability knob**: restricting INLP to its leading directions (k0.8 — the largest prefix of classifier directions still reaching 80% validation accuracy) preserves most of the suppression at near-baseline perplexity; full k=n can catastrophically hurt, e.g. Qwen at k=n drops to ∆ non-refusal = −0.13 and PPL −0.71, while k0.8 recovers to +0.62 / ≈0.
- **Geometric observation**: nullspace projection and counterfactual flipping land in qualitatively different regions of activation space (between vs. opposite the original cluster), giving the first tentative evidence that the model encodes the *absence* of a concept differently from its *opposite*.
- **Methodological calibration**: a structured completion analysis (initial-then-comply, looping, request for context, refusal reason breakdown) shows that ActAdd's high harmless-side "refusal" rate is partly a measurement artefact of degenerate (looping) outputs — i.e. surface metrics over-state it.
- **Measurement audit** (Appendix C): substring matching and an LLM judge agree on 84% of completions; disagreement is concentrated on harmful prompts (25.2% vs 4.6%) and grows monotonically with intervention aggressiveness, motivating substring as the primary headline metric.

## Methodology

**Setup.** Decoder-only transformer; restricted analysis to **post-instruction template tokens** (following Arditi et al. 2024). At each candidate layer l and post-instruction token position t, residual-stream activations are extracted for a contrastive pair of datasets (D⁺ harmful, D⁻ harmless) and processed by each intervention.

**DiM.** Steering vector `w_DiM` is the difference of class-mean activations (Eq. 3). Two interventions:
- *ActAdd*: `h' ← h ± w_DiM` at the extraction layer and every token (Eq. 4).
- *Directional ablation*: project activations at every layer/token onto the hyperplane orthogonal to `ŵ_DiM` (Eq. 5). In this paper directional ablation is applied at the **selected layer only**, not all layers (deviation from Arditi et al. 2024), to keep comparison fair with INLP.

**INLP.** Iteratively trains linear classifiers and projects activations onto their nullspace, recovering n orthogonal directions and a nullspace projector `P_N = I − P_R` (Eq. 6). Restricting the sum to the first k ≤ n classifiers gives a partial nullspace `P_N^(≤k)`. Two interventions (Eq. 7–8):
- *Nullspace projection* (α=1): `h' ← P_N h` (erases concept).
- *Counterfactual flipping* (α=2): `h' ← (2 P_N − I) h` reflects activations across the nullspace, flipping the concept while preserving orthogonal components.

**Layer & token selection.** Composite score rewarding (a) refusal suppression on harmful, (b) refusal induction on harmless, and (c) penalising KL shift in the final-logits distribution. **Important methodological deviation from Arditi et al. (2024)**: each candidate intervention is applied **only at the layer from which it was extracted**, not at every layer. The authors note this keeps the score fair across methods since INLP-based interventions (especially full P_N) have a stronger effect on logits than DiM. Empirically, rankings are not significantly affected. For INLP, additional requirement that the first classifier reaches a minimum validation accuracy. INLP selection is run at four k levels: k=n (full P_N), k=1, k0.9, k0.8.

**Datasets.**
- Harmful train (n=128) sampled from AdvBench, MaliciousInstruct, TDC2023.
- Harmful val (n=32) from HarmBench.
- Harmless train/val (128/32) from Alpaca.
- **Harmful test (n=100)**: full JailbreakBench (10 harm categories).
- **Harmless test (n=100)**: fixed-seed Alpaca split disjoint from train/val.
- Perplexity: held-out Alpaca + The Pile.
- MMLU: 500-question stratified 5-shot.
- ARC-Challenge: 4-choice 5-shot subset.

**Models.** Smallest variant in each of five safety-tuned families: **Gemma 2B-IT, Qwen 1.8B-Chat, Yi 6B-Chat, Llama-2 7B-Chat, Llama-3 8B-Instruct**. Arditi et al. (2024) report DiM refusal direction qualitatively preserved at scale on the same families; whether INLP scales is explicitly left as future work.

**Evaluation metrics.**
- *Effectiveness*: (i) substring-matching non-refusal rate (Arditi et al. 2024), (ii) LlamaGuard 2 unsafety rate, (iii) LLM-judge non-refusal rate using **Qwen2.5-14B-Instruct** constrained to structured JSON. Greedy decoding, 256 new tokens.
- *Performance*: perplexity on Alpaca + The Pile (relative change clipped to [−1, +1]); 5-shot accuracy on MMLU + ARC-Challenge.
- Greedy decoding, 256 new tokens.

**Completion analysis (Section 5.3, Appendix C.2).** Judge categorises each completion into: initial refusal then compliance, looping/repetitive, request for more context, and (for refusals) one of unethical/illegal, refusing opposite task, lacks ability, illogical reason, no reason given, legit other. This reveals that high refusal rates can be driven by degenerate looping rather than principled refusal.

## Results & Highlights

**Effectiveness (Table 1, headline ∆ vs. baseline).**

- **Refusal suppression on harmful prompts (substring non-refusal)**: counterfactual flipping (α=2) reaches scores comparable to directional ablation across all five models, exceeding it in several cells (e.g. Llama-2 7B: +0.76 vs +0.46; Llama-3 8B: +0.96 vs +0.95). Nullspace projection is **consistently weaker**: it suppresses effectively only on Gemma, Yi, and partially Llama-3; it **partially fails on Qwen** (e.g. +0.10 at k=n) and **fails on Llama-2** (≈0).
- **Refusal injection on harmless prompts (∆ Refusal harmless)**: ActAdd dominates (+0.86 to +1.00, +1.00 on Llama-3). No counterfactual-flip variant exceeds +0.4. The advantage is **partly a measurement artefact**: the median-PPL column shows ActAdd has the worst LM loss, and Section 5.3 confirms that many ActAdd completions are **degenerate (looping)** that happen to contain a refusal phrase.
- **Tunability (k-truncation)**: on Gemma, Yi, Llama-2, Llama-3, restricting INLP to **k0.8** leaves harmful-prompt non-refusal essentially unchanged while reducing perplexity degradation. **Qwen is the dramatic exception**: k=n drops non-refusal to **−0.13** and incurs the largest PPL hit in the table (−0.71); k0.8 recovers a clean **+0.62** with negligible PPL change. This is the cleanest demonstration of the capability–effect trade-off.
- **Performance preservation**: k0.8 keeps all five models within 0.05 of baseline perplexity; MMLU+ARC show no meaningful drop in 5-shot accuracy under any intervention.
- **Selection disagreement**: DiM and INLP selection scores do **not** converge on the same (l, t); the layer differs by at least 1, and the post-instruction token position often differs too. Rankings are broadly similar (especially at k0.8), but a more direct cross-method selection experiment is flagged as future work.

**Geometric view (Figure 1, 2).** Four qualitatively distinct patterns, consistent across all five models:
- *Directional ablation* collapses both classes onto the **harmless side** of the refusal axis — a one-sided removal, not a flip.
- *ActAdd* produces a clean two-way swap (harmful→harmless, harmless→harmful); the harmful-to-harmless distribution is wider with a long tail above the centroid, consistent with perplexity degradation.
- *Counterfactual flipping (α=2)* likewise produces a two-way swap.
- *Nullspace projection (α=1)* produces the most striking pattern: transformed points collapse into a region **between** the harmful and harmless clusters, rather than into either of them.
- **Target-group fit** (Figure 2) quantifies this: across most models, counterfactual flipping produces ratios closest to 1 in both directions (i.e. transformed activations land on the natural target-class manifold); ActAdd shows wider, more dispersed distribution (off-manifold); nullspace projection sits farther from the target.

**Completion analysis (Section 5.3).**
- The single most striking pattern in Figure 13 is the prevalence of **looping completions under ActAdd**, consistent with the perplexity spikes. Many of these degenerate outputs happen to contain a refusal phrase, so ActAdd's ∆ Refusal harmless is partly a measurement artefact of repetitive generation.
- On harmful prompts, all four interventions are qualitatively indistinguishable from baseline: surviving refusals are dominated by ethics-framing (≥ 77% on every cell). Interventions differ in **how much** they suppress refusal but not in **what kind** survives.
- INLP variants surface **refusal-like phrasings followed by compliance** ("I'm not sure I understand…") on harmless prompts, which substring-matching counts as refusals but the LLM judge does not. This means the harmless-side refusal rate for counterfactual flipping in Table 1 is **likely inflated** relative to DiM when measured by substring alone.

**Measurement audit (Section 5.4).** Substring matching and the LLM judge agree on 84% of completions; disagreement is concentrated on harmful prompts (25.2% vs 4.6%) and grows **monotonically** with intervention aggressiveness (7.2% at baseline → ~42% under counterfactual flipping with α=2). A stratified hand-coded audit finds both protocols are biased on harmful prompts but **the judge's bias is larger**: it systematically over-fires on taboo-topic prompts whose response contains the requested harmful content wrapped in a moralising disclaimer. Authors adopt substring as the primary headline metric on the grounds that the substring rate is closer to the audited true rate, and the residual correction is approximately uniform across models and methods so the relative ordering is preserved.

**Authors' hypotheses revisited.**
- **H1 (effectiveness) is partially supported**: INLP counterfactual flipping is on par with DiM directional ablation at suppressing refusal, but INLP nullspace projection is consistently weaker, and on the harmless side ActAdd remains strongest by surface metrics (with a degeneracy caveat).
- **H2 (tweakability) is supported** by the k-based capability–effect trade-off, and more interestingly by the fact that the same extracted subspace yields two qualitatively distinct interventions via α — flexibility DiM does not afford.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The novelty is incremental on the methods side (DiM and INLP are both well-known) but the head-to-head comparison and the geometric "absence vs. opposite" observation are genuinely new. The geometric claim is, however, stated **qualitatively**; no formal metric is provided to separate "between" from "opposite", and the difference could partly be an artefact of the projection operator (P_N vs 2P_N − I) rather than evidence of a distinct internal representation of absence.
- **Rigor.** The benchmark is small (5 models, smallest in family) and uses a single refusal primitive on a single test set (JailbreakBench + Alpaca). There is no robustness study across prompt distributions. The perplexity sanity check is the only side-effect metric, and MMLU/ARC are coarse. The "preliminary" framing is appropriate and somewhat honest, but the headline table still risks being over-interpreted by readers.
- **Reproducibility.** DiM and INLP are well-defined, but several implementation details — the harmful/harmless prompt set used to fit INLP, the layer chosen, the iteration count, the threshold for "leading directions" (k0.8 = 80% validation accuracy), the judge's structured schema — are not fully visible without the paper body or the anonymous code link. The decision to apply each intervention only at the extraction layer is a non-trivial methodological choice that departs from Arditi et al. (2024); the paper explains the rationale, but readers re-running the comparison should be aware of this.
- **Significance.** Useful as a reference for practitioners choosing between DiM and INLP for refusal steering; useful as a starting point for the absence/opposite distinction in representation geometry. Not a method paper. The Qwen k=n failure is the most actionable single result.
- **Red flags / weak baselines.** (i) DiM direction ablation at every layer (the Arditi et al. 2024 setting) is moved to the appendix (Appendix D) because it dominates too strongly — the headline comparison intentionally uses single-layer ablation, which weakens the DiM side. (ii) The selection scores for INLP-based interventions are computed using nullspace projection only, "for time and compute reasons" — meaning counterfactual flipping is evaluated at a point selected to optimise a *different* intervention. The authors flag this as future work. (iii) A single LLM judge (Qwen2.5-14B-Instruct) is used; the audit shows it over-fires on taboo topics, and a stronger judge could change absolute numbers (though not, the authors claim, the ordering). (iv) No comparison with LEACE despite citing it as the natural closed-form alternative. (v) The geometric "absence vs. opposite" claim is the paper's most distinctive contribution and is also its weakest: the difference could be largely operator-induced, and the paper itself admits that "refusal is a case where absence of harmfulness and opposite of harmfulness are not cleanly separated semantically". This is a hypothesis-generating observation, not a finding.

## Strengths

- **Clean, well-framed comparison** of two mechanistic-intervention families on the same primitive, datasets, and metrics.
- **Tunable knob with measured cost**: the k-truncation of INLP gives a real capability–effect lever; the Qwen result shows it can turn a failed intervention into a strong one at negligible PPL cost.
- **Geometric observation** that the same subspace yields qualitatively different behaviours via α is genuinely interesting and surfaces a question (absence vs. opposite) the field has not interrogated.
- **Completion-level analysis** (looping, request for context, refusal-reason breakdown) goes beyond headline refusal rates and exposes that ActAdd's high harmless-side "refusal" is partly degenerate.
- **Calibrated measurement**: the audit of substring vs. judge and the choice to report substring as primary is exactly the kind of methodological care the mechanistic-interpretability subfield often lacks.
- **Honest framing**: "preliminary", explicit limitations, open questions enumerated in Section 6.

## Limitations

- Only **5 small open-weight models** (smallest in each family); commercial / RLHF-heavy models and scale (13B–72B) are not tested.
- **Single refusal primitive** (Arditi et al.'s harmful/harmless sets); the geometric claim relies on a concept where "absence" and "opposite" are semantically entangled.
- **Single test set** (JailbreakBench) for harmful evaluation; no robustness to prompt distribution.
- **Single LLM judge** (Qwen2.5-14B-Instruct) with known bias; audit shows it over-fires on taboo topics.
- **Selection scores for INLP** are computed using nullspace projection only; counterfactual flipping inherits a sub-optimal (l, t).
- **DiM directional ablation at every layer** (the original Arditi et al. setting) is moved to Appendix D because it dominates; headline comparison uses single-layer ablation, which weakens DiM.
- **No comparison with LEACE** (closed-form, minimally invasive linear erasure), which is the natural third concept-erasure primitive.
- **Geometric claim is qualitative**: no quantitative metric separates "between" from "opposite"; the difference could be operator-induced.
- **No ablations** on layer choice, prompt set size, INLP iteration count, or the impact of the post-instruction token selection.
- **"Preliminary" framing** is the authors' own — the work is presented as a starting point, not a definitive answer.

## Relevance

与"机制可解释性 + 激活干预 / 安全对齐"研究方向直接相关：拒绝方向一直是单方向叙事的代表，本文给出 INLP 与 DiM 在该任务上的对照实验，明确了 α=1/α=2 的几何差异，并提出"概念缺失 vs. 对立"是否在模型内部被不同编码这一开放问题——这对扩展激活干预到偏见、情感、性别代词等具有清晰对立的属性有直接借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How robust is the absence/opposite distinction across **layers, models, and concepts** with truly orthogonal semantic structure (pronoun gender, truthfulness, sentiment polarity), and is there a quantitative metric that captures it?
- Does **truncating the INLP subspace to leading directions (k0.8)** also work for concepts other than refusal (e.g., bias, sentiment, toxicity), and does it generalise across model families?
- How sensitive is the comparison to the **harmful/harmless prompt set, the INLP iteration count, and the post-instruction token selection strategy**?
- Can a **flipping-based selection** (rather than nullspace-projection-based selection) improve counterfactual flipping's harmless-side injection numbers, as the authors hypothesise?
- Does the **cross-method selection** experiment (apply INLP at the (l, t) chosen by DiM and vice versa) change the ordering, isolating extraction primitive from extraction site?
- How does **INLP compare to LEACE** in terms of capability–effectiveness trade-off and absence/opposite geometry? Does LEACE's minimality constraint produce a different geometric pattern?
- How do these interventions **scale** to 13B–72B variants in the same families? Does the gap between DiM and INLP narrow or widen at scale?
- Can a **stronger LLM judge + non-canonical refusal phrasings + opposite-task detector** tighten the bracketing on the true refusal rate, sharpening the harmless-side comparison?
- Is the **k=n failure on Qwen** a general failure mode of full-P_N interventions on certain model families, and what predicts it?
