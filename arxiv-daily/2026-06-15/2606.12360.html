Anatomy of Post-Training: Interpretability as a Pre-Flight Audit for the Learning Signal.

A unified Bayesian-inference view that recasts data filtering, inoculation prompting, activation steering, and reward shaping as four operationalizations of the same primitive — explaining a concept away from the exponential-tilt reward that preference optimization is fitting.

Key Finding

Four RLHF interventions are the same primitive.

Data filtering, inoculation prompting, activation steering, and reward shaping are all explain-away operations on a concept c in the exponential-tilt reward r = Σ log pc — just executed at different points in the pipeline.

Key Metric
0.9· 0.58

Audit predicts rollout deltas at R² ≈ 0.9 (feature view) and R² ≈ 0.58 (prompt view) — the first quantitative validation of an interpretability-driven preference-data audit.

Why This Matters

Diagnose preference data before you train on it.

On Dolci the audit surfaces jailbreak-robustness loss, sycophantic physics, and hallucinated sensitive-resource URLs — then reward shaping with a tunable λ delivers a safety-utility Pareto frontier across 8B–70B model families.

Critical Edge

The framework assumes independent concepts — it isn’t.

Eq. 3 factorises the reward as a product of concept classifiers. The paper’s own prompt-conditioned results show correlated concepts violate this — so the unifying lens is partly post-hoc, and most prompt-conditioned interventions do not reach statistical significance.

Pipeline · explain-away, four ways

One concept, four places to subtract it.

Under the Bayesian-inference view of preference optimization, the scalar reward decomposes into a product of concept-specific classifiers. Removing or amplifying any subset of them yields the same optimal-policy change — the intervention is just a matter of where you intervene.

Lane 1
Data filtering
Drop training units whose preference is explained by concept c.
Lane 2
Inoculation
Append a context Pu that explains c; learn the residual.
Lane 3
Activation steering
Add / subtract a steering vector ±c in the residual stream.
Lane 4
Reward shaping
Replace r by r − λ sc; λ is the knob.

Validation · predicted vs. empirical DPO delta

The audit predicts the policy change.

Sample Dolci prompts, roll out the SFT and DPO models, and rank SAE features by predicted chosen−rejected delta against empirical pre/post-DPO delta on rollouts. Both views are predictive; the feature view is dramatically more so.

The validation step is what separates this from a typical interpretability for RLHF paper: the audit doesn’t stop at “here are SAE features that differ” — it asks do they predict the actual policy change?

Red flags · read before you cite

Where the unifying lens quietly cracks.

  • Independence is a fiction. Eq. 3 factors the reward into independent concept classifiers; the paper’s own data shows correlated concepts violate it.
  • Most prompt-conditioned interventions fail. Physics sycophancy, questionable fanfic — only hallucinated sensitive-resource URLs reach statistical significance.
  • Activation steering needed re-implementation. The PPS-style chosen-side steering “distorts”; only rejected-side explain-away steering recovers a usable direction.
  • Single DPO dataset audit. Dolci’s jailbreak degradation is SFT-vs-DPO; no contrasting datasets — hard to attribute as Dolci-specific vs. generic.
  • SAE features as concept classifiers inherit the polysemanticity / unreliability debate (Chanin 2025, Bricken 2024).
  • No released code / checkpoints at submission; replication of the audit step is non-trivial.
  • Persona amplification uses the same LLM judge the shaping objective implicitly targets — risk of judge-target alignment.
Persona amplification · λ as a knob

Turn a trait up; pay for it in OLMES.

Reward shaping with a diff-of-means steering vector for “playful” raises trait expression monotonically in λ, with a near-linear capability cost. A knob, not a switch.

Trait · “playful”
Likert 0–4, GPT-5.5 judge · shaped → 3.4 vs. DPO 0.0
OLMES · capability
near-linear decline as λ ∈ {1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32}
  • Goblin Weave, Cheerfulness, Formality, Overconfidence, Conflict Avoidance each induced at 5% SFT + 5% DPO, then suppressed 60–95%.
  • Token filtering underperforms on broad / diffuse traits but works for localisable ones (e.g. Goblin Weave).
  • SAE-based classifiers beat linear / attention probes in off-target recovery — consistent with feature splitting.
  • Adversarial robustness unverified — persona may not survive OOD probing at high λ.

Relevance · why we picked it

Direct hit on alignment, reward shaping, sycophancy, over-stylization. Frames interpretability as a pre-flight audit of preference data — the same failure modes you care about, turned into a measurable workflow with a tunable safety-utility knob.

Open questions · what to ask next

Q2Does the prompt-conditioned pipeline improve under multi-resolution SAE clustering that captures concept hierarchies (style → formatting → bold vs. em-dash)?
Q3Can the safety-utility Pareto frontier traced by λ transfer across model families without retuning, using the same SAE-feature selector?
Q4Is the “playful” persona robust to out-of-distribution adversarial prompts that probe the amplified trait itself?
