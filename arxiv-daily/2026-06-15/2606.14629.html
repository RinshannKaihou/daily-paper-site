When Good Verifiers Go Bad:
Self-Improving VLMs Can Regress on New Tasks

A four-rung Qwen2.5/3-VL verifier ladder shows the same self-DPO loop that lifts a student on MathVista (+5.5 to +9.2 pp) silently drops it on MMMU (−3.4 to −10.9 pp) — while training loss descends the whole way down.

Jianzhe Lin · Meta 2026-06-15 single GB200 ~210 GPU-hr n=2–5 seeds
Key Finding

Sub-threshold verifier → silent student regression

When the verifier's target-task rubric accuracy is below ~0.25, progress-gated self-DPO moves the student backward. DPO loss is monotonically decreasing — the failure is invisible to standard monitoring.

Why It Matters

"Better verifier helps" is wrong here

Loss-monitored pipelines will ship a worse student. The "refuse to deploy if rubric < threshold" check costs ~5 GPU-hr per (verifier, task) — a one-line guardrail against a multi-day wasted run.

R1 — pre-flight
Critical Edge

Confidence inverts the damage curve

Inside the failure regime, more-confident-but-wrong verifiers do more damage than near-random ones. Progress gating amplifies wrong high-margin pairs.

Deployment

Three recommendations for production self-DPO

R1/R2/R3 — 成本极低、可立即纳入现有验证流水线。
R1 · PRE-FLIGHT

Measure target-task rubric accuracy first

Run ~200 verification items (~5 GPU-hr) before deploying any self-DPO loop. Refuse to launch if rubric < frozen-student baseline or a known-positive task sanity check.

R2 · RANK BY QUALITY

Score verifiers by rubric, not by parameter count

4B Qwen-3 beats 7B Qwen-2.5 on every task (MathVista +30.5, MMMU +10.5, BLINK +8.1 pp) at 0.57× the cost. Newer-generation smaller wins.

R3 · BUDGET CAP

Treat diminishing returns as a compute ceiling

MathVista: rubric 0.555→0.660 yields ≤4 pp student gain. 3-round chained self-DPO replicates the same shape (R0 0.515 → R1 0.560 → R2 0.580). Stop scaling the verifier once gains saturate.

Theorem 1 · Variance of progress-gated replay
Let q(p,K) = 1 − pK − (1−p)K. Under (A1) direction agreement and (A2) bounded informativeness ratio ρ = Var(g|PG) / Var(g|U), in the saturated regime T·q(p,K) ≫ N:

Var(gPG) / Var(gU) → ρ

Validates on a toy-bandit suite (K=4, ρ∈[0.05,0.9], p∈[0.01,0.9], T∈[10,3000]) within ±2%. Real-VLM ρ̂ did not land before submission — Appendix B.

Red Flags

What this paper does not yet establish

作者坦诚标记的局限——也是后续工作的入口。
  • Single-family ladder. All verifiers and both students are Qwen2.5/3-VL. Cross-family replication (Gemma, InternVL, LLaVA-OneVision, Molmo) is open. R2 is the most fragile claim.
  • No real-VLM ρ̂. Theorem 1 is the theoretical heart; the real-VLM measurement protocol is described in Appendix B but the measurement did not land in time. Bridge to MMMU inversion is qualitative.
  • n=2 in most cells. Headline 4B/8B re-runs reach n=5; the rest stay at n=2. Seed-count understatement is acknowledged.
  • Verifier ladder capped at 8B. Findings may not transfer to >30B closed-weight verifiers (GPT-4o-class) — exactly the regime many production stacks live in.
  • R3 fits on one task. MathVista is the only benchmark with rubric accuracies in the right range to fit a saturating curve. MMMU and BLINK are outside that range.
  • Closed-loop chain too sparse. 2 seeds × 3 rounds — the diminishing-returns shape is suggestive, not established. No exponential asymptote is fit.
Open Questions

For the next paper

F2 inversion 留给了社区一系列可立刻上手的实验。
  • Does the F1 failure mode transfer to >30B closed-weight verifiers at the same sub-threshold rubric-accuracy range, or does the failure close as verifier capability approaches ceiling?
  • Cross-family replication of R2 — do InternVL / LLaVA-OneVision / Gemma-3 / Molmo verifiers also exhibit "smaller-newer-generation beats larger-older-generation" at lower compute?
  • A real-VLM ρ̂ measurement per Appendix B would close the loop between Theorem 1 and the MMMU inversion. Is the empirical ρ̂ on a calibrated verifier close to the predicted ρ?
  • Does the F1 mode replicate under non-progress-gated preference construction (uniform top-k vs bottom-k)? If the inversion disappears, the rubric-accuracy gate is doing the work; if it persists, the gate is a PG-specific fix.
  • Selective abstention: can a verifier that abstains on low-confidence items avoid the direction-mismatch regime? Constructive counterpart to R1's "refuse to deploy."
  • DPO β sweep on MMMU (β ∈ {0.05, 0.1, 0.3, 1.0}) — does the regression magnitude scale with preference strength?
  • Pair-margin threshold sweep — the 0.25 gate trades pair count for confidence; which side of the F1 trap does each setting land on?
  • Beyond MathVista/MMMU/BLINK: does the sub-threshold regression appear on generation-quality tasks (MMMU-Pro, ChartQA, DocVQA) or domain verticals (medical/legal VQA)?
  • Verifier ensembling in the same failure regime — does averaging rubric scores reduce regression, or preserve the direction-mismatch signal?
  • Learned corrector for confidently-wrong pairs (PER-style importance-sampling correction) — a small classifier flagging the pairs PG should skip.
