MeEvo: Metacognitive Evolution Combined with Natural Evolution for Automatic Heuristic Design

Zishang Qiu · Xinan Chen · Rong Qu · Ruibin Bai 2026-06-15 AHD LLM Evolutionary Computation Meta-cognition Combinatorial Optimization
Key Metric
14.96%
better than the next-best MeLA baseline on WSN deployment (DeepSeek-V4-Flash).
Why This Matters
It gives LLM-driven automatic heuristic design a stable, real-world edge on constrained tasks like sensor networks and curriculum sequencing.
Critical Edge
No cost-vs-quality Pareto is reported, so it is unclear whether the metacognitive layer justifies its overhead.

Methodology

MeEvo is built around two alternating evolutionary layers rather than a single loop with a reflection add-on.

  • Natural Evolution (NE) maintains a parent pool of top-K heuristics and breeds offspring via LLM-mediated crossover or mutation.
  • Metacognitive Evolution (ME) reflects on a shared history of reasoning traces, fitness values, and execution errors, then writes improved heuristics back into the parent pool.
  • Reasoning traces act as a genotype of design knowledge; executable code is the selected phenotype.
  • Crossover probability follows pc = α + β(1 − Eval/L) to favor exploration early and exploitation late.
  • Cyclic alternation is deliberate: the authors argue NE and ME need incompatible conditions, and ablation shows (2,1) beats parallel or single-layer variants.

Results & Highlights

Across two LLM backbones and five problems, MeEvo records the best mean objective. The largest gains appear on complex constrained tasks.

Strengths

  • Clean dual-layer framing with a strong “incompatible conditions → cyclic alternation” rationale.
  • Broad empirical setup: two LLMs, six LLM-AHD baselines, four traditional baselines, statistical testing.
  • Order-of-magnitude stability improvement on constrained problems.
  • Ablation isolates (2,1) as optimal across all tested problems.

Limitations

  • No cost/quality Pareto curves — a serious omission for LLM-AHD.
  • Only n=5 runs; several p-values sit at the discrete null floor.
  • No code release or prompt-template appendix in the extracted text.
  • Single-objective scope; shared-history pruning/forgetting is not studied.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • What is the wall-clock and dollar-cost Pareto versus plain NE?
  • Does the shared history saturate, and would importance-based pruning change the (2,1) optimum?
  • Are the gains robust to prompt templates and temperatures beyond the two configurations tested?
  • How does the dual-layer architecture generalize to multi-objective heuristic design?
