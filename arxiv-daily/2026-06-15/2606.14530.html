Code correctness is linearly readable from Qwen3-4B's prompt-final hidden state — but the repair direction dissolves once you control for the prompt.

Code Correctness Signals in LLM Hidden States: Pre-Generation Probing and Repair Geometry · Carlo Di Cicco · 2026-06-15
probing interpretability code-generation LLM-internals negative-result residualization

Key metric
0.911AUC
Residualized probe AUC over 50 leakage-free outer splits, 16 points above the prompt-only floor of 0.754 — non-prompt-length signal, in a 4B model, on a single 6 GB GPU.
Why this matters

A reproducible, commodity-hardware data point that code-correctness lives in a small LLM's prompt-final state — and a method to tell a real RepEng direction from a prompt-confound wearing its clothes.

Critical edge

The 0.911 is a linear-decode floor, not a ceiling: no MLP, k-NN or sparse probe has been tested, and no causal / interventional evidence is offered — the probe is associational, not shown to be the locus of correctness.

01Methodology

  1. Capture. 2560-dim hidden state at the last prompt token of a prompt-only forward pass on Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507, layers 29–36.
  2. Probe. StandardScaler → PCA(95%) → ℓ2 logistic regression on pass/fail of attempt 0 across 444 LiveCodeBench tasks.
  3. Residualize. Per hidden dim, OLS-regress out prompt_tokens on the train fold; refit on every split. Repeat with prompt-only baselines (logistic / RF / GBT / MLP) as a robustness check.
  4. Nested-CV layer pick. Layer & C chosen by 5-fold CV inside 50 outer 80/20 splits — modal pair (29, 0.01) in 22/50 splits; no test-set argmax.
  5. Repair direction v. δ = h₁ − h₀ across 236 cleaned 0→1 transitions; v = mean(success) − mean(failure); nulled by 1000 label shuffles (magnitude & split-half cosine).
  6. Conditional residualization. For each delta dim, OLS against a 4-col covariate matrix (∆prompt_tokens, code_length₀, failure_type dummies). Re-null v on the residuals.
last-prompt-token h → OLS residualize → prompt_tokens → probe  |  δ = h₁ − h₀ → OLS residualize → {∆tokens, len₀, failure_type}

  • Layer 29 is the modal pick (25/50); layer 30 close behind (24/50); deeper layers never selected.
  • No non-linear prompt-only model breaks 0.754 — the residualized probe's lead is not a baseline-construction artifact.
  • Three covariates differ between repair-success and repair-failure groups (Welch / χ², all p < 0.001): ∆prompt_tokens, code_length₀, failure_type.
  • Per-attempt pass rates: 15.8% → 36.4% → 6.7% → 2.7% → 1.4%; multi-step repair is data-starved (13 / 4 / 2 successes).

03Strengths

  • Paired positive/negative, same tool. One residualization pipeline is used to confirm pre-generation signal and to overturn the repair-direction claim — no asymmetry in rigor between the two halves.
  • Nested CV at every hyperparameter. Layer & C picked inside the outer training fold; outer-test AUC is leakage-free.
  • Balanced-baseline check. Four model families on the prompt-only floor, none reach 0.754 — rules out the "weak linear baseline" criticism.
  • Strong raw null. 1000 label-shuffles for both magnitude and split-half; real ∥v∥ sits ≈ 10σ above null (0/1000 reach it).
  • Pre-residualization balance check. Covariates are residualized only when their group means actually differ — a reusable methodological template.
  • Small-footprint reproducibility. Single 6 GB GPU, public model, public benchmark, public code & data.

04Limitations

  • Single model, single benchmark. Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 on LiveCodeBench — no Llama, DeepSeek, HumanEval, MBPP or SWE-bench replication.
  • Linear probe only. No MLP / k-NN / sparse-linear sanity check; 0.911 is a linear-decode floor.
  • Captured layer range starts at 29 (of 36); a lower layer outside the captured range is not ruled out — and the peak sitting at the lower bound of the range is itself a caveat.
  • 236-sample cleaning is partly heuristic. The "repetition-loop" filter is qualitative; no sensitivity analysis on thresholds.
  • Near-collinear covariate pair. ∆prompt_tokens and code_length₀ share Pearson r = 0.995; disclosed, but a non-overlapping pair would be cleaner.
  • No causal / interventional evidence. No steering, ablation or activation patching — the probe is associational.
  • Multi-step repair is unestimable (13/4/2 successes at 1→2 / 2→3 / 3→4) — a hard scope limit.

05Questions for further exploration

  1. Does the prompt-final probe survive on Qwen3-72B, Llama-3-70B or DeepSeek-Coder-V2 — does the linear-decode AUC scale with model depth, or is 4B already at the ceiling?
  2. Can a non-linear probe (small MLP, k-NN, sparse linear with feature selection) close the gap to perfect decoding, or is the residual information genuinely non-local across hidden dims?
  3. Which repair-context covariate drives the dissolution most — ∆prompt_tokens, code_length₀, or failure_type? A leave-one-covariate-out sensitivity analysis would localize the effect.
  4. Is the pre-generation signal causally relevant? Can steering / ablation / activation patching actually flip pass/fail rates, or is the probe reading a correlate?
  5. How stable is the prompt-length residualization across structurally different prompts (one-liner vs multi-class spec, single function vs full project scaffold)?
  6. Does the same diagnostic framework overturn other "contrastive direction" claims in the code-repair literature (self-debugging features, error-type detectors, syntax-recovery directions)?
  7. Could the pre-generation probe's (layer, C, PCA-var) configuration transfer across model families with little retraining — evidence of a family-shared code-correctness representation?
