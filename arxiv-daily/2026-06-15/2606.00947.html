Silent Failures in Federated Personalization of Foundation Models

FL's privacy architecture hides trustworthiness breakdowns — bias amplification, calibration drift, fairness collapse, adaptation misalignment, OOD degradation, and alignment erosion — that current benchmarks cannot detect.

一句话总结：本文提出“沉默失败”概念，将联邦基础模型个性化中因隐私约束导致的六类可信性失效按数据/模型/系统三层组织，并通过12项基准的横向分析揭示联邦与集中式可信评估之间的结构性鸿沟。

Key Finding

Privacy-preserving training is not enough. FedFMP systems can silently degrade in trustworthiness because the per-client signals needed to detect failures are exactly what FL privacy removes.

Key Metric

Coverage gap. Of 12 leading benchmarks, no federated benchmark directly reports confidence miscalibration, and almost none tracks alignment erosion.

Why This Matters

Post-market monitoring. Regulators in healthcare and finance are tightening AI oversight, but current FL tooling offers limited support for trustworthy deployment.

Critical Edge

Not adversarial. These failures arise from internal FL dynamics, not external attacks — yet adversarial evaluation assumes the white-box access that FL forbids.

The Six Silent Failure Modes

Data-level
Amplified Bias
Aggregation of conflicting local biases can yield global bias larger than any single client.
Data-level
Confidence Miscalibration
Local ECE rises on factual noise; aggregation smooths the signal and hides domain-specific drift.
Model-level
Fairness Collapse
Cross-client dispersion Disp(F_agg) ≫ Disp(F_pre); minority clients are too small to move the global metric.
Model-level
Adaptation Misalignment
Local PEFT updates in safety-critical subspaces degrade base behavior; aggregation produces incoherent safety.
System-level
OOD Degradation
Over-specialized adapters fail on other clients' distributions; FL limits cross-client OOD diagnosis.
System-level
Alignment Erosion
Small per-round compliance decrements accumulate across T rounds; per-client compliance is uncomputable at the server.

Methodology at a Glance

Position / landscape-analysis paper. No new empirical results. Contribution is conceptual organization, formalization, and a 12-benchmark audit.

  • Federation of K clients personalizes a frozen FM via PEFT adapters Δθ_k.
  • Server runs FedAvg as a baseline for failure analysis.
  • Each failure mode reduced to a measurable inequality (e.g., Bias(F_agg) > Bias(F_pre)).
  • Table 2 cross-walks modes against three FL constraints: aggregation, limited data observability, statistical heterogeneity.

Critical Assessment

Strengths
  • Deployer-relevant framing; names a real monitoring gap.
  • Modes formalized as testable inequalities, not slogans.
  • Honest about interpretive judgment in benchmark ratings.
Limitations
  • No empirical validation of any failure mode.
  • No concrete privacy-preserving detection protocol.
  • Benchmark set is incomplete; gap magnitude may shift.

Core Argument

The same privacy guarantees that make federated learning attractive — no raw data leaves the client, no per-client model inspection at the server — also erase the observability required to catch trustworthiness regressions. Until privacy-preserving training is paired with privacy-preserving behavioral auditing, FedFMP systems will ship with failure modes that are invisible to both operators and regulators.
