> **一句话总结：** 在四档 Qwen2.5/3-VL 验证器阶梯 + MathVista/MMMU/BLINK 三任务的实证下，本文证明"验证器驱动的 VLM 自 DPO"在目标任务的 rubric 准确率处于次阈值区间时会静默反向优化学生模型（3B 学生相对冻结基线下降 3.4–10.9 pp），且失败区间内"更自信但更错"的验证器伤害最大；作者给出 progress-gated replay 的方差定理与 F1 方向错配失败模式，并提出"先用 200 条验证集测 rubric 准确率再上线"的三条工程化部署建议。

> **TL;DR:** Verifier-driven self-DPO for VLMs can silently regress the student when the verifier is sub-threshold on the target task. On a 4-rung Qwen2.5/3-VL verifier ladder (3B/4B/7B/8B), the same verifiers that improve a Qwen-3-VL-2B student on MathVista by +5.5 to +9.2 pp drop it on MMMU by −3.4 to −10.9 pp below the frozen baseline, while DPO loss descends monotonically. The inversion is confidence-driven: the 3B verifier (MMMU rubric 0.233, the most confidently-wrong rung) does the most damage (−10.9 pp); the 8B verifier (rubric 0.080, near-random) does the least (−3.4 pp). The regression replicates on a Qwen-2.5-VL-3B student. A variance theorem for progress-gated replay (PG) and its F1 direction-mismatch failure mode mechanistically explain the inversion. Three concrete recommendations: (R1) measure target-task rubric accuracy on ~200 items before deploying; (R2) rank verifiers by rubric quality, not parameter count; (R3) treat diminishing returns in above-threshold regimes as a verifier-compute budget cap.

## 核心贡献

- **F1：验证器在目标任务上的"亚阈值静默失败"现象**。在 MMMU 上，同一阶梯内的四档验证器 rubric 准确率仅为 8%–23%（远低于同一阶梯在 MathVista 上的 35%–66%），每一档都会把 Qwen-3-VL-2B 学生相对冻结基线打低 3.4–10.9 pp，而 DPO 训练损失全程单调下降——监控损失完全发现不了回归。MMMU 5 学科×30 题的子集即可被用作"任务-验证器错配"的早期诊断。
- **F2：失败区间内的"置信度反转"伤害曲线**。在亚阈值区，回归幅度与 rubric 准确率呈负相关：3B 验证器（rubric 0.233）−10.9 pp > 4B（0.192）−7.9 pp > 7B（0.087）−5.9 pp > 8B（0.080）−3.4 pp。**更自信但更错反而比近随机更糟**——progress-gated replay 把"高置信度的错误偏好对"放大了。
- **跨学生可复现**：在 Qwen-2.5-VL-3B 上复现 −4.3 到 −6.8 pp 的同方向回归（基线 0.233 → 0.165–0.190），排除单学生伪相关。
- **机制性方差定理**：为 progress-gated replay 给出渐近方差比 `Var(gPG)/Var(gU) → ρ` 的封闭结果（饱和区间），并刻画其 F1 方向错配失败模式——即 `E[gU] = E[gPG]` 假设不成立时，PG 把错误方向放大到学生。toy-bandit 实验在 ρ∈[0.05,0.9]、p∈[0.01,0.9]、T∈[10,3000] 内以 ±2% 与理论匹配。
- **三条工程化部署建议（R1/R2/R3）**：
  - **R1**：上线前在 ~200 条目标验证集上跑一遍 verifier rubric，rubric 准确率低于冻结基线或已知良好任务（如 MathVista）就拒部署；
  - **R2**：按目标任务的 rubric 质量而非参数规模排验证器——4B Qwen-3 在所有任务上都跑赢 7B Qwen-2.5（MathVista +30.5 pp、MMMU +10.5 pp、BLINK +8.1 pp），且推理成本仅 0.57×；
  - **R3**：把"边际收益天花板"视为验证器侧算力预算上限（MathVista 上 rubric 0.555→0.660 仅带来 ≤4 pp 学生增益）。
- **正/反双向实证**。MathVista（同阶梯 rubric 0.35–0.66）作为正控制：+5.5 到 +9.2 pp；MMMU（rubric 0.08–0.23）作为反例：−3.4 到 −10.9 pp。同一验证器+同一学生+同一管线，唯一变化是"目标任务上的 rubric 准确率"——回归/改进的符号翻转完全可由这一单变量预测。

## Key Contributions

- **F1: Silent sub-threshold failure.** On MMMU, all four open Qwen2.5/3-VL verifiers score 0.08–0.23 rubric accuracy (vs. 0.35–0.66 on MathVista) and silently regress the Qwen-3-VL-2B student by 3.4–10.9 pp below the frozen baseline. DPO training loss decreases monotonically, so loss-monitoring pipelines ship a *worse* student without warning.
- **F2: Confidence-inverted damage curve.** Within the failure regime, regression is inversely correlated with verifier rubric accuracy: 3B (rubric 0.233) −10.9 pp > 4B (0.192) −7.9 pp > 7B (0.087) −5.9 pp > 8B (0.080) −3.4 pp. More confident-but-wrong > near-random; progress-gated replay amplifies wrong high-margin pairs.
- **Cross-student replication.** Same MMMU experiment on Qwen-2.5-VL-3B: −4.3 to −6.8 pp regression (baseline 0.233 → 0.165–0.190), ruling out single-student artifacts.
- **Variance theorem for progress-gated replay (Theorem 1).** Under direction agreement + bounded informativeness, `Var(gPG)/Var(gU) → ρ` in the saturated regime. F1 direction-mismatch failure mode explains the MMMU inversion. Toy-bandit validation: ±2% across ρ∈[0.05,0.9], p∈[0.01,0.9], T∈[10,3000].
- **Three deployment recommendations (R1/R2/R3):**
  - **R1**: pre-measure target-task rubric accuracy on ~200 items (~5 GPU-hr); refuse to deploy if below frozen-student baseline or known-positive task sanity check.
  - **R2**: rank verifiers by rubric quality, not parameter count — 4B Qwen-3 beats 7B Qwen-2.5 on every task (MathVista +30.5, MMMU +10.5, BLINK +8.1 pp rubric accuracy) at 0.57× the wall-clock cost.
  - **R3**: treat diminishing returns in above-threshold regimes as a verifier-compute budget cap (MathVista: rubric 0.555→0.660 yields ≤4 pp student gain). 3-round chained self-DPO replicates the same diminishing-returns shape (R0 0.515 → R1 0.560 → R2 0.580).
- **Bilingual positive/negative control design.** MathVista (rubric 0.35–0.66, +5.5 to +9.2 pp student gain) vs. MMMU (rubric 0.08–0.23, −3.4 to −10.9 pp regression) with identical verifier/student/pipeline; the single axis that flips the sign of the student delta is the verifier's task-rubric accuracy on the target task.

## Methodology

**Verifier-driven self-DPO loop.** For each prompt x, the learner samples K=4 candidates. A frozen verifier scores each via a chain-of-thought rubric (n≤6 yes/no sub-questions targeting: (a) presence of answer, (b) format compliance, (c) faithfulness to visual input, (d) CoT consistency, (e) terminal-answer correctness). The JSON verdict is parsed to a scalar in [0, 1]. The highest-scoring positive and lowest-scoring negative form a preference pair (y⁺, y⁻) **only if** the score margin ≥ 0.25. Pairs enter a FIFO buffer of capacity N=4096 with progress gating (top-margin pair per round admitted). DPO with frozen reference (the same student with LoRA disabled) updates the learner. LoRA: r=32, α=64, lr=5×10⁻⁶, batch 4 pairs, β=0.1, max 200 (PILOT) or 1000 (FULL) steps.

**Ladder.** Headline student: Qwen-3-VL-2B-Instruct. Verifier ladder: Qwen-2.5-VL-3B, Qwen-3-VL-4B, Qwen-2.5-VL-7B, Qwen-3-VL-8B. Second student for replication: Qwen-2.5-VL-3B-Instruct. All cells run on a single GB200 (186 GB HBM3E); total compute ~210 GPU-hr.

**Tasks.** MathVista testmini, MMMU (5 subjects × 30 = 150 items, validation), BLINK (3 sub-tasks, ~385 items, validation). Student final accuracy uses task-native official exact-match scorers (deterministic regex pipelines, no LLM, never sees the verifier). The verifier is used only during pair construction.

**Measurements.** For each (verifier, task) pair: (i) verifier task-rubric accuracy on a held-out validation split, and (ii) student final accuracy after self-DPO. The "silent failure" claim is the gap between (ii) and the frozen baseline, observed with (DPO loss decreasing).

**Variance theorem (Theorem 1).** Let q(p, K) = 1 − pᴷ − (1−p)ᴷ be the per-round probability of producing both + and − candidates. Under Assumptions 1 (direction agreement `E[gU] = E[gPG]`) and 2 (bounded informativeness ratio `Var(g|PG) = ρ·Var(g|U)`), in the saturated regime T·q(p,K) ≫ N, `Var(gPG)/Var(gU) → ρ`. Validated by toy-bandit suite (K=4, ρ∈[0.05,0.9], p∈[0.01,0.9], T∈[10,3000]) at ±2%.

**F1 failure: direction-mismatch amplification.** Assumption 1 fails when the verifier is wrong but confident. In that regime, PG concentrates the optimization signal on the pairs where the verifier is most wrong — the same selectivity that helps a calibrated verifier becomes harmful. The MMMU inversion (3B/0.233/−10.9 pp vs. 8B/0.080/−3.4 pp) is the real-VLM observation of this F1 mode.

## Results & Highlights

**Verifier rubric accuracies (Table 3):**

| Verifier | MathVista v | MMMU v | BLINK v |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2B student baseline | 0.465 | 0.179 | 0.637 |
| Qwen-2.5-VL-3B | 0.555 | 0.233 | 0.665 |
| Qwen-3-VL-4B | 0.655 | 0.192 | 0.717 |
| Qwen-2.5-VL-7B | 0.350 | 0.087 | 0.636 |
| Qwen-3-VL-8B | 0.660 | 0.080 | 0.699 |

Qwen-3 verifiers dominate Qwen-2.5-VL-7B on rubric accuracy across all three tasks, despite equal or smaller parameter count.

**MMMU sub-threshold regression (Table 1, Qwen-3-VL-2B student, n=2 seeds):**

| Verifier | vMMMU | Student final | ∆ vs baseline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Frozen baseline | — | 0.179 | — |
| Qwen-2.5-VL-3B | 0.233 | 0.070 | **−10.9 pp** |
| Qwen-3-VL-4B | 0.192 | 0.100 | −7.9 pp |
| Qwen-2.5-VL-7B | 0.087 | 0.120 | −5.9 pp |
| Qwen-3-VL-8B | 0.080 | 0.145 | −3.4 pp |

All four verifiers drop the student below baseline; the regression is **inversely** correlated with verifier rubric accuracy (the F2 inversion).

**Cross-student replication (Table 2, Qwen-2.5-VL-3B student, n=2 seeds):**

| Verifier | vMMMU | Student final | ∆ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | — | 0.233 | — |
| Qwen-3-VL-4B | 0.192 | 0.165 ± 0.021 | −6.8 pp |
| Qwen-2.5-VL-7B | 0.087 | 0.190 ± 0.042 | −4.3 pp |
| Qwen-3-VL-8B | 0.080 | 0.175 ± 0.021 | −5.8 pp |

The sub-threshold regression transfers to a second student.

**MathVista positive control (Table 5, Qwen-3-VL-2B student, n=2–5 seeds):**

| Verifier | params | rubric v | student final | ∆ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Frozen baseline | 2B | — | 0.465 | — |
| Qwen-2.5-VL-3B | 3B | 0.555 | 0.520 ± 0.035 (n=2) | +5.5 |
| Qwen-3-VL-4B | 4B | 0.655 | 0.557 ± 0.035 (n=5) | +9.2 |
| Qwen-2.5-VL-7B | 7B | 0.350 | 0.540 ± 0.025 (n=3) | +7.5 |
| Qwen-3-VL-8B | 8B | 0.660 | 0.551 ± 0.035 (n=5) | +8.6 |

The 7B row sits above a rubric-monotone curve, indicating rubric accuracy is a safety/ranking signal, not a complete predictive model. The marginal ∆ between rubric 0.555 and 0.660 is ≤ 4 pp — R3's diminishing-returns ceiling.

**Closed-loop multi-round (Table 6, MathVista, 8B verifier, 500 DPO steps/round, 2 seeds):**

| Round | R0 | R1 | R2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mean | 0.515 | 0.560 | 0.580 |
| ∆ vs prev | — | +4.5 pp | +2.0 pp |

Monotone-increasing, but with shrinking inter-round gap (same diminishing-returns shape; reported as suggestive, not fit).

**Computational efficiency (R2 evidence).** 4B verifier runs at ~0.57× the wall-clock cost of the 7B verifier per candidate, while delivering higher rubric accuracy on every measured task. On BLINK, the 4B verifier also trains the student to +5.0 pp higher accuracy than the 7B verifier at the same number of DPO steps.

**Silent-failure mechanism.** DPO loss descended monotonically over 200 steps in every MMMU cell, with no qualitative signal of regression. Student-final accuracy is only revealed at post-training evaluation — a workflow that monitors only training loss and ships the resulting LoRA adapter would not detect the harm.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Jianzhe Lin** — single author. The work was done at **Meta** (correspondence: jianzhelin@meta.com), giving the work first-hand familiarity with production self-improvement stacks (the related work cites the author's own SuperIntelliAgent 2025 system).
- **Methodological credibility**: experiments are concrete, reproducible on a single GB200, and pre-registered with held-out validation splits and deterministic task-native exact-match scorers (no LLM-as-judge in evaluation, no leakage of verifier into student scoring).
- **Reproducibility**: the paper promises an open-source release of the rubric template, JSON parser, train_dpo.py, and the ρ̂ measurement protocol (Appendix B). Code is not yet linked in the v1 PDF as of this writing, but the design is described at the level of a runnable spec.
- **Caveats**: single-author; all four verifiers and both students are from the Qwen2.5/3-VL family (no cross-family replication); only one task (MathVista) supports the diminishing-returns ceiling quantitatively. The author flags each of these as a limitation.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The empirical phenomenon of "good verifier, wrong task" is genuinely under-appreciated in the VLM self-improvement literature; the confidence-inverted F2 finding is a clean, surprising, and well-characterized result. The variance-theorem framing is compact and operational rather than purely diagnostic. Not a brand-new method, but a sharp, well-scoped failure mode with three actionable engineering recommendations. The MMMU-vs-MathVista contrast with identical verifiers/student/pipeline is a strong design choice that isolates the rubric-accuracy axis as the causal lever.
- **Rigor**:
  - Two students, four verifiers, three benchmarks, and a direction-mismatch theory — solid for an empirical short paper.
  - Most cells run n=2 seeds; the headline 4B/8B cells re-run to n=5 (FULL settings). Larger seed counts (n≥5) would strengthen the cross-student replication.
  - The variance theorem is validated by toy-bandit (±2%) but the real-VLM ρ̂ measurement "did not land in our training stack within the submission window" (Appendix B) — this is a real gap. The MMMU inversion is presented as qualitative evidence for the F1 mode, not a quantitative bridge.
  - The 7B-row outlier in the MathVista ladder is openly acknowledged: rubric accuracy is a "safety/ranking signal, not a complete predictive model" — an honest framing.
  - The pairing rule (top-vs-bottom, margin ≥ 0.25) and buffer parameters are clearly specified; the rubric prompt is task-agnostic and identical across (verifier, task) cells, which is a good design choice to keep the rubric-accuracy axis comparable.
- **Reproducibility**:
  - Pre-registered held-out validation splits; deterministic exact-match scorers; no LLM judge in evaluation. The verifier never sees ground truth and the student is never scored by the verifier.
  - Open-source release promised (rubric template, parser, train_dpo.py, ρ̂ measurement). Not yet linked in v1.
  - Single GB200 per cell; ~210 GPU-hr total — within reach of academic labs.
- **Significance**:
  - Operationally very useful. The "measure target-task rubric accuracy first" recipe is exactly the pre-flight check teams should adopt, and the cost (~5 GPU-hr per (verifier, task)) is trivial relative to a single self-DPO run.
  - The confidence-inverted finding reframes a tempting intuition ("a better verifier never hurts") into a concrete warning — this is the kind of paper a production team should read before deploying their next self-improvement loop.
  - R2 (rank by rubric not params) is a small but real compute-saving insight: 4B Qwen-3 beats 7B Qwen-2.5 at 0.57× the cost on every task measured.
- **Red flags**:
  - **Single-family ladder**: all verifiers and both students are from Qwen2.5/3-VL. Cross-family replication (Gemma, InternVL, LLaVA-Next) is left as follow-up. The author explicitly flags R2 as "the most fragile claim."
  - **No real-VLM ρ̂**: the variance theorem is the theoretical heart of the paper, but its real-VLM instantiation is missing. The author is upfront about this, but it means the connection between Theorem 1 and the MMMU inversion is qualitative.
  - **n=2 in most cells**: seed-count understatement is acknowledged; the headline n=5 cells are exceptions, not the rule.
  - **Verifier ladder cap at 8B**: the author explicitly excludes >30B verifiers ("single-GPU memory limits") and does not claim the findings transfer to GPT-4o-class closed-weight verifiers. This is a meaningful scope restriction given that many production stacks rely on such verifiers.
  - **`cs.CR` categorization**: paper is cross-listed with cryptography/security despite being a VLM-alignment empirical study — likely a metadata artifact, but worth noting.
  - **Single L1 task for the diminishing-returns ceiling (R3)**: MathVista is the only task with rubric accuracies in the appropriate range to fit a saturating curve. MMMU and BLINK are outside that range. The qualitative shape is consistent but the quantitative claim is MathVista-only.
  - **Closed-loop chain with only 2 seeds × 3 rounds** — the author explicitly calls this "suggestive evidence, not a fit." No exponential asymptote is fit. A more aggressive chain (5+ rounds, more seeds) is needed for the diminishing-returns curve to be quantitatively trustworthy.

## Strengths

- **Clean isolation of the rubric-accuracy axis** by using the same verifier/student/pipeline across MathVista (positive control) and MMMU (negative control). The sign flip on the student delta is a single-variable explanation — pedagogically excellent.
- **Confidence-inverted F2** is a memorable, actionable diagnostic that the field had not explicitly named before.
- **Variance theorem + F1 failure mode** is a rare theoretical hook for an empirical VLM paper, and the toy-bandit validation gives the theorem some quantitative credibility.
- **Three deployment recommendations (R1/R2/R3)** are concrete, costed (~5 GPU-hr for R1), and immediately implementable in existing validation infrastructure.
- **R2's compute-efficiency result** (4B Qwen-3 > 7B Qwen-2.5 at 0.57× the cost) is a small but real win that a production team can act on without further experimentation.
- **Honest about limitations**: single-family ladder, missing real-VLM ρ̂, n=2 seed counts, scope cap at 8B verifiers — all flagged explicitly by the author.
- **Pre-registered design**: held-out validation splits, deterministic exact-match scorers, no LLM-as-judge in evaluation, verifier never used for student scoring. This is a methodological standard the rest of the field should match.

## Limitations

- **Single-family ladder**: Qwen2.5/3-VL only. Cross-family generalization (Gemma, InternVL, LLaVA-Next, Molmo) is untested. R2 is the most fragile claim.
- **No real-VLM ρ̂ measurement**: the variance theorem is validated by toy-bandit only; the ρ̂ instrumentation protocol is described in Appendix B but the actual measurement did not land before submission.
- **Most cells run n=2 seeds**; the headline n=5 cells (4B/8B verifier) are re-runs at FULL settings. Larger n across all cells would strengthen the regression-vs-noise interpretation.
- **Single L1 task for R3**: MathVista is the only task with rubric accuracies high enough to fit a saturating curve; MMMU/BLINK are in the failure-regime range.
- **Closed-loop chain is too sparse** for a quantitative fit: 2 seeds × 3 rounds. The diminishing-returns shape is suggestive, not established.
- **Verifier ladder capped at 8B parameters**; findings may not transfer to >30B closed-weight verifiers, which is exactly the regime many production stacks operate in.
- **Single L1 sub-task (MMMU: 5 subjects × 30 items = 150 items)**: the regression replicates on 150 items × 2 seeds. Wider MMMU coverage would be reassuring.
- **Pair margin threshold (0.25) and buffer capacity (N=4096) are fixed**; sensitivity to these hyperparameters is not reported.
- **DPO β=0.1 is fixed**; the regression's sensitivity to β (a known lever for DPO behavior) is not characterized.
- **No code release linked in v1** as of this writing.

## Relevance

本文与 VLM/LLM 对齐、RLHF/DPO、奖励建模与自改进的研究方向高度相关。对关注"用更强验证器自动改进 VLM"这条生产路径的工程团队而言，本文的 R1（上线前测 rubric 准确率）是低成本、高 ROI 的护栏；对学术研究者而言，置信度反转与 F1 方向错配失败模式为"为什么 reward model 鲁棒性重要"提供了一个清晰的实证案例，值得作为后续自改进算法的设计约束。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the F1 direction-mismatch failure mode transfer to **>30B closed-weight verifiers** (e.g., GPT-4o-class rubric graders) at the same sub-threshold rubric-accuracy range, or does the failure close as verifier capability approaches ceiling?
- **Cross-family replication** of R2: do InternVL, LLaVA-OneVision, Gemma-3, Molmo verifiers also exhibit the "smaller-newer-generation beats larger-older-generation" pattern at lower compute? R2 is the most fragile claim and the natural next experiment.
- A **real-VLM ρ̂ measurement** (per the protocol in Appendix B) would close the loop between Theorem 1 and the MMMU inversion. Is the empirical ρ̂ on a calibrated verifier (e.g., 8B on MathVista) close to the predicted ρ?
- Does the F1 failure mode replicate under **non-progress-gated** preference construction (uniform top-k vs. bottom-k pairing), or is the F2 inversion specific to PG? If the inversion persists under uniform, the rubric-accuracy gate is doing all the work; if it disappears, the gate is a PG-specific fix.
- **Selective abstention / calibrated verifiers**: can a verifier that abstains on low-confidence items avoid the direction-mismatch regime? This would be a constructive counterpart to R1's "refuse to deploy."
- **DPO β sensitivity**: does the regression magnitude scale with β (a known lever for DPO's preference strength)? Smaller β might dampen the harm; larger β might amplify it. A β ∈ {0.05, 0.1, 0.3, 1.0} sweep on MMMU would characterize this.
- **Pair margin threshold (0.25) sensitivity**: is the regression a function of the margin gate? Tighter margins admit more pairs but each less informative; looser margins admit fewer but more confident pairs (the F1 trap). A threshold sweep would clarify.
- **Beyond MathVista/MMMU/BLINK**: does the sub-threshold regression appear on generation-quality tasks (e.g., MMMU-Pro, ChartQA, DocVQA) or on domain-specific verticals (medical/legal VQA)? MMMU's multi-image multi-step structure may be a particularly adversarial rubric-accuracy sink.
- **Chained self-DPO at scale**: 2 seeds × 3 rounds is too sparse to fit a saturating curve. A 5-seed × 5-round chain on MathVista (or a task with appropriate rubric range) would let the diminishing-returns ceiling (R3) be quantitatively characterized.
- **Verifier ensembling**: does averaging rubric scores across multiple verifiers in the same failure regime reduce the regression, or does it preserve the direction-mismatch signal?
- **Connection to PER importance-sampling correction**: PER literature uses importance-sampling to debias high-priority samples; self-DPO has no analogue because the verifier is treated as ground-truth-equivalent. A learned corrector (e.g., a small classifier that flags confidently-wrong pairs) might be a constructive fix.
