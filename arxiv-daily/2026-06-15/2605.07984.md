> **一句话总结：** 用押韵对偶补全作"干净"前瞻约束任务，结合线性探针与激活补丁在 Qwen3/Gemma-3/Llama-3 共 10+ 规模上系统比对，发现只有 Gemma-3-27B 真正把因果规划位点从押韵词**迁移**到换行符（约第 30 层），并用两阶段路径补丁将这一"表征接力（handoff）"定位到 5 个注意力头，恢复约 90% 的押韵路由容量。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces **planning site formation** — where forward-looking structural constraints are encoded in a model's residual stream, and whether those sites causally drive generation. Using rhyming-couplet completion as a clean testbed, the authors run linear probing + activation patching across Qwen3, Gemma-3, and Llama-3 at 10+ scales. Future-rhyme information is linearly decodable at the newline in all families (signal grows with scale), yet **only Gemma-3-27B** causally relies on the newline encoding: its causal driver **migrates from the rhyme word to the newline around layer 30** (a "representational handoff"). All other models — every Qwen3 size up to 32B and every Llama-3 size up to 70B — keep conditioning on the rhyme word throughout generation. Two-stage path patching on Gemma-3-27B localizes the handoff to **five attention heads in layers 28 and 30** (L30H4, L28H14, L28H15, L30H5, L28H29), recovering **~90% of the full-residual reference's rhyme-routing capacity** at the newline. Findings replicate under steering-vector intervention and pass zero-vector + donor-prompt controls. The work is a methodological template for separating "encodable" from "causally used" using only probing and patching — no transcoders or second training run required.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"**规划位点形成（planning site formation）**"概念框架，区分"规划兼容表征"（探针可解码）与"因果活动规划位点"（激活补丁可改写输出）两个层级。
- 选取押韵对偶补全作为受控的"前瞻结构性约束"任务，规避开放生成评估的混淆因素；用 Pile 文本上的前瞻探针作为负对照，证明这种表征并非残差流的通用属性。
- 在 Qwen3（最大 32B）、Gemma-3（最大 27B）、Llama-3.1（最大 70B）三个开源系列、10+ 个规模上系统性地联合探针+补丁；并在 steering vector 设置中独立复现。
- 核心经验发现：探针信号在所有家族都随规模增强（emergent），但**因果活动规划行为是稀疏且非单调的**——只有 Gemma-3-27B 出现真正的"换行处表征接力"，其余模型即使有强探针信号也从未让换行位点因果驱动生成。
- 通过 attention weight 排序 + 同时多头补丁，将 Gemma-3-27B 的接力定位到 **5 个注意力头**（L30H4、L28H14、L28H15、L30H5、L28H29），恢复 90% 的押韵路由容量；MLP patch 全程为 0，确认机制由 attention 而非 FFN 介导。
- 提供方法论层面的"轻量"路径——只用线性探针和激活补丁（无需 transcoder 训练或大规模 steering 数据）即可在多架构/多规模上系统比较规划位点。
- 明确区分"易解码 ≠ 因果使用"，为机制可解释性中如何避免"探测器幻觉"提供清晰的范式。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes **planning site formation** as a research question and distinguishes a weaker notion (planning-compatible representation, decodable via probing) from a stricter notion (causally active planning site, established via activation patching).
- Designs **rhyming-couplet completion** as a controlled testbed for forward-looking, structurally-constrained generation; includes a **negative control** (Pile general-text probing) showing that future-token information is not generically encoded in the residual stream.
- Systematic probing + patching comparison across **three open-source families (Qwen3, Gemma-3, Llama-3) at 10+ parameter scales (up to 70B)**; main result independently replicated with steering vectors.
- Empirical headline: **probe signal is emergent in all families, but causally active planning is sparse and architecture-specific** — only Gemma-3-27B exhibits a representational handoff from the rhyme word to the newline around layer 30.
- Localizes the handoff to a **sparse set of five attention heads in layers 28 and 30** (L30H4, L28H14, L28H15, L30H5, L28H29) via attention-weight ranking + simultaneous head patching, recovering **~90% of the full-residual reference at the newline**; top-k MLP patches yield zero (Wilson 95% upper bound ≤ 0.04), confirming an attention-mediated mechanism.
- Provides a **lightweight mechanistic toolkit** (probing + patching only) that scales to large open-source models without transcoder training or steering-vector data scale.
- Demonstrates the broader methodological lesson: **"easily decodable ≠ causally used,"** giving a concrete template for separating correlation from causation in interpretability.

## Methodology

- **Task.** Rhyming-couplet completion: given a first line ending in word r₁, the model must produce a second line whose terminal word r₂ rhymes with r₁ (validated against the CMU Pronouncing Dictionary).
- **Dataset.** 1,200 synthetically generated couplets (1,000 train / 200 validation), produced by Claude Sonnet 4.6 with prompts encouraging topic and rhyme-scheme diversity. A Pile-derived 1,200-sequence general-text set serves as a negative control.
- **Position convention.** Relative to the newline (i = 0) at the end of the first line; r₁ is at i = −1 in Qwen/Llama, i = −2 in Gemma (tokenizer differences — Gemma splits ",\n" into two tokens).
- **Probing.** Linear probes f(W,b)(h) = softmax(Wh + b) trained with AdamW (lr 10⁻⁴, weight decay 10⁻³, batch 32, 10 epochs) to predict r₂ from h^{ℓ,i}. Wilson 95% CIs reported on every probe accuracy. Metric: top-5 and top-1 accuracy + rhyme accuracy.
- **Causality test — activation patching.** Replace h^{ℓ,i} from a "corrupt" run (prompt whose r₁ belongs to a different rhyme family) into the clean run's forward pass and measure the corrupt-rhyme rate of the generated r₂. 5 prompt pairs × N = 20 stochastic samples per (ℓ, i); 95% cluster bootstrap intervals over pairs (10,000 resamples).
- **Steering-vector replication (Appendix B).** Mean-difference steering vectors v^{s→t}_{ℓ,i} = h̄^{t}_{ℓ,i} − h̄^{s}_{ℓ,i} across 10 rhyme schemes; add α·v at inference (α = 1.5). Confirms the patching picture.
- **Path patching (two-stage).** Stage 1: cache candidate head outputs at i = 0 from a clean run with only the residual at i = −2 replaced by the corrupt's. Stage 2: forward the unmodified clean prompt and substitute cached head outputs for selected heads at i = 0. Implements the path "i = −2 → head → i = 0 → output" (Goldowsky-Dill et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2022).
- **Controls.** Zero-vector baseline (replace hidden state with zeros) and donor-prompt baseline (replace with activations from an unrelated sentence) — both produce near-zero corrupt rhyme rates (Wilson 95% upper bound ≤ 0.17), well below the true patching peaks of 0.96 (Gemma-3-27B) and 0.90 (Qwen3-32B), confirming the effect is rhyme-specific, not a perturbation artifact.
- **Models.** Qwen3 (0.6B–32B), Gemma-3 (1B–27B), Llama-3.1 (1B–70B); hidden dim 5,120 / 5,376 / 8,192; 64 / 62 / 80 layers.

## Results & Highlights

- **Negative control:** Pile general-text probes degrade monotonically with look-ahead k and overlap the unigram baseline by k = 8 in all three model families — confirming that "future-rhyme" is not a generic property of the residual stream.
- **Couplet probing:** At the couplet task, probes at the last word (i ≤ −1) and newline (i = 0) substantially outperform probes at generated positions (i > 0); CIs at peak layers do not overlap the i ≥ 1 bands in any of the three flagship models.
- **Scale-dependent emergence (couplet probing):** The accuracy gap between newline and first-generated-position probes grows with model size. Smaller Qwen3 (0.6B–8B) and Llama-3 (1B–8B) CIs all overlap zero; the largest Qwen and Llama, and all Gemma-3 sizes, are strictly above zero. Gemma-3 shows the cleanest monotonic trend (0.11 at 1B → 0.38 at 27B).
- **Causality — the handoff:** Gemma-3-27B last-word patching peaks in early layers and drops sharply near layer 30, while newline patching rises simultaneously to a peak corrupt rhyme rate of **0.63 [95% CI 0.48, 0.78] at layer 33**. This crossover is the "representational handoff."
- **Causality — the absences:** Qwen3-32B and Llama-3.1-70B (and all smaller sizes) show uniformly high last-word patching and **near-zero newline patching across every layer** despite strong probe signal at the newline.
- **Steering replication:** In Gemma-3-27B, last-word steering drops near layer 30 while newline steering rises to 0.85–0.95 in layers 30–40 — the same handoff pattern, with tighter rate estimates. Qwen3-32B and Llama-3.1-70B again remain effective at the last word but flat at the newline.
- **Sparse-circuit localization:** Three heads in Gemma-3-27B attend almost exclusively to the last word from the newline — L30H4 (weight ≈ 0.99), L28H14 (≈ 0.97), L28H15 (≈ 0.95) — with a second cluster at moderate weights (0.35–0.55). Simple top-k simultaneous patching at the newline plateaus at k = 5 (corrupt rate 46%, ~73% of the full-residual reference) and stays flat through k = 10.
- **Two-stage path patching:** A stricter path-isolating intervention (i = −2 → head → i = 0 → output) peaks at **k = 5 heads with 57% corrupt rhyme rate, i.e. ~90% of the full-residual reference**, declining to 47% at k = 10 and 32% at k = 15. Random and comma-control head sets remain at zero across all k. The five heads are: **(L30, H4), (L28, H14), (L28, H15), (L30, H5), (L28, H29)**.
- **Mechanism check:** Top-k MLP patches at i = 0 yield zero corrupt rhyme rate at every k (Wilson 95% upper bound ≤ 0.04, N = 100), confirming the handoff is **attention-mediated, not FFN-mediated**.
- **Causality is not a perturbation artifact:** Zero-vector and donor-prompt controls produce near-zero rates across all layers (Wilson 95% UB ≤ 0.17), far below the true patching peaks (0.96 Gemma-3-27B; 0.90 Qwen3-32B).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The conceptual move — formalizing "planning site formation" and splitting it into an encodable vs. causally-used axis — is a clean, useful contribution. The finding that probe signal is decoupled from causal use is well-known in principle (Hewitt & Liang, 2019; Burns et al., 2023) but the cleanest demonstration to date in the planning/planning-like setting. The handoff result is novel and surprising.
- **Rigor.** Strong on multiple axes: large cross-family / cross-scale sample, paired clean/corrupt prompts, cluster-bootstrap CIs, two-stage path patching, and (critically) two control conditions showing the effect is rhyme-specific. The 90% recovery of the full-residual reference with only 5 heads is a tight, falsifiable causal claim.
- **Reproducibility.** Good: dataset (1,200 Claude-generated couplets + Pile subset), training hyperparameters, exact prompt pairs, and per-layer results are all in the main text or appendices. Probe checkpoints and code are not (yet) mentioned, but the experimental protocol is replicable from the paper alone.
- **Significance.** The headline finding is genuinely surprising and challenges a popular narrative ("scaling produces planning"). It provides a concrete, falsifiable template for testing planning-like claims in other models and tasks, and suggests that architecture (not just scale) may be the decisive factor in whether latent planning sites form.
- **Red flags and honest caveats.**
  - **Single structured task.** Rhyming couplets are phonological. The authors themselves flag: "Extending the methodology to prose generation, code completion, and multi-step reasoning tasks would test whether the representational handoff is a general planning primitive or a narrow phonological phenomenon." The handoff could be a rhyme-specific inductive bias, not a general planning primitive.
  - **Architecture vs. scale confound.** Gemma-3-27B is the only model with the handoff. The paper cannot fully separate "27B scale does it" from "Gemma-3 architecture does it" — there is no Gemma-3-27B matched to a non-Gemma 27B, and there is no Gemma-2 or Gemma-3 32B/70B control. The claim is therefore narrower than "emergent planning at scale."
  - **Small per-cell N.** Patching is run on 5 prompt pairs × N = 20 (100 total completions per cell). CIs are wide (e.g., 0.48–0.78 at layer 33), and the per-pair rate ranges from 0.40 to 0.90. The 90% recovery figure comes from this same small set; the authors note that "a larger, more diverse couplet set would tighten this estimate and reveal whether the head set varies by rhyme family."
  - **Attention-weight ranking is a heuristic.** Using attention from i=0 to i=−2 as a proxy for "rhyme-routing heads" is reasonable but not the only possible choice; a path-patching sweep over all (layer, head) pairs would be more exhaustive. The fact that the k=5 plateau matches the attention ranking is suggestive, not proof of optimality.
  - **Residual 10%.** Two-stage path patching recovers ~90% at k=5. The unaccounted 10% is a real finding — it suggests a small, distributed secondary mechanism that is not characterized.
  - **No comparison to known planning probes.** Prior work (Othello board states, AlphaZero chess concepts, latent QA planning) is cited but not directly compared; it would be useful to know whether the same Gemma-3-27B heads also participate in those circuits.
- **Risk of over-claim.** The authors are mostly disciplined — the abstract says "only Gemma-3-27B" and the discussion carefully limits generalization claims. The phrasing in some places ("planning site formation … is a distinct emergent phenomenon tied to specific model scale and architecture") slightly overgeneralizes from N = 1 (architecture) × N = 1 (scale point).

## Strengths

- Elegant, controlled task that cleanly isolates a forward-looking structural constraint.
- Methodological discipline: probing + patching as a joint standard, plus two control conditions and a negative control (Pile).
- Unusually thorough cross-family × cross-scale comparison, with all per-layer and per-size results in the appendix.
- Sparse, interpretable circuit-level result — five attention heads, attention-mediated — that is concrete and falsifiable.
- Independent replication of the main finding under a different intervention modality (steering vectors), strengthening the causal claim.
- Clean separation of "what is encoded" from "what is causally used" — a corrective the interpretability literature needs.
- Strong statistical hygiene: Wilson 95% CIs on every probe, 10,000-resample cluster bootstrap on every patching cell, paired-difference Wald intervals for cross-scale gaps.

## Limitations

- Single structural-constraint task (rhyme couplets); unclear whether the handoff generalizes to prose, code, multi-step reasoning, or other phonological constraints.
- Architecture / scale confound: the only handoff-positive model is Gemma-3-27B. No matched 27B in another family, no Gemma-3 at 32B/70B, no Gemma-2 controls.
- Small per-cell patching N (5 pairs × 20 = 100 completions per cell), with CIs wide enough to include both 0.48 and 0.78 at the peak layer.
- Attention-weight ranking is a proxy, not a causal search; an exhaustive (layer, head) path-patching sweep would be more definitive.
- Path patching recovers 90% — the residual 10% is uncharacterized and may point to a small, distributed secondary mechanism.
- Tokenization differences (Gemma splits ",\n") shift the position convention by one between families; cross-family comparisons require care the paper handles (Appendix A) but a reader must look closely to avoid being misled.
- No code or model checkpoints released as of v2 (11 Jun 2026); replicability depends on carefully following the paper.
- No comparison to known planning-circuit results from Othello/AlphaZero/CoT-latent-space work to situate the heads in the broader interpretability landscape.

## Relevance

> 与用户对 LLM 机制可解释性、Transformer 内部表示、规划/推理能力本质的兴趣高度相关。该工作将"探针可解码"与"因果驱动"严格区分，揭示了规模 / 架构对"前瞻规划位点形成"的关键作用，对"大模型是否有真正规划能力"这一争议问题提供了方法论示范与具体反例。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the Gemma-3-27B handoff also appear in **non-phonological constrained-generation tasks** — JSON-schema completion, palindromes, code indentation, regex generation, palindrome or alliteration tasks — or is rhyme a special case?
- Is the five-head set **human-interpretable**? Do they encode "position of future rhyme," "rhyme-class identity," "constraint satisfaction" or a more abstract signal? Targeted attention-pattern analysis and SAE feature decomposition on those heads would clarify.
- **Architecture vs. scale disentanglement:** Does a 27B-scale version of Qwen3 or Llama-3 reproduce the handoff? Do Gemma-3-32B or Gemma-3-70B? A controlled sweep is the single most informative next step.
- Is the handoff a **learned inductive bias** that can be triggered or suppressed by fine-tuning, or is it architecturally determined? Intervention via targeted SFT on rhyme-couplet data could test this.
- How does the handoff interact with **chain-of-thought / scratchpad prompting**? Does explicit CoT eliminate the need for the line-boundary encoding, or does it route through the same five heads?
- Does the 10% residual recovered by path patching point to a **distributed secondary circuit** (e.g., late-layer MLPs) that is necessary for rhyme output rather than rhyme routing? Further targeted patching at i > 0 would test this.
- How do **non-frontier open-source models** at 27B+ (e.g., Mistral-Large, DeepSeek-V3, Yi-1.5) compare? A broader architecture sweep would clarify whether Gemma-3 is unique or whether the handoff is more common than this paper suggests.
- Can the five heads be **steered or ablated** to make Gemma-3-27B lose the handoff (i.e., fall back to last-word conditioning), and would that hurt couplet completion accuracy? Causal scrubbing (Chan et al., 2022) on the path-patching result is a natural follow-up.
