Large Language Model Agents Are Not Always Faithful Self-Evolvers

Agents are highly faithful to raw experience yet largely ignore compressed experience — even when compressed summaries are the only input.

Weixiang Zhao, Yingshuo Wang, Yichen Zhang, Yang Deng, Yanyan Zhao, Wanxiang Che, Bing Qin, Ting Liu

LLM Agents Self-Evolving Agents Experience Faithfulness Causal Intervention Integrated Gradients
◎ Key Finding
A stable raw-vs-compressed asymmetry: perturbing raw trajectories sharply changes behavior, while perturbing condensed experience leaves performance statistically unchanged.
◈ Key Metric
75 → ~33%
ExpeL on ALFWorld collapses when raw experience is emptied, yet condensed-experience corruptions are nearly inert.
◇ Why This Matters
The "lessons learned" in self-evolving agents may be doing far less causal work than assumed; memory design needs a reality check, not just better summaries.
⚠ Critical Edge
In ReasoningBank, even Filler compressed experience (non-semantic tokens) preserves most of the gain, suggesting agents latch onto surface structure, not meaning.

What they measured

The authors define experience faithfulness as a causal property: an agent is faithful only if controlled perturbations of its experience produce predictable, corresponding changes in behavior.

  • Raw interventions: Empty, Shuffle, Irrelevant trajectories.
  • Compressed interventions: Empty, Corrupt, Irrelevant, Filler summaries.
  • Attribution: Integrated Gradients segmented by prompt region.

Why I flagged it

Directly matches interests in LLM agents and memory / experience mechanisms. It replaces the default "compressed experience helps" assumption with a causal test and a reusable diagnostic protocol.

Read first: §3 (intervention design), §4.1–4.2 (raw-vs-compressed), §5.2 (IG attribution).

Hard questions left

  • Does the asymmetry persist with learned, task-specific condensers?
  • Can structural prompt placement (adjacent vs. prepended) close the gap?
  • Is the effect driven by task family or by instruction-tuning bias?
  • Could Filler results reflect an OOD fallback rather than true surface-cue use?
