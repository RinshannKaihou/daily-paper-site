> **一句话总结：** Soft-Elo 在不修改 Bradley–Terry 模型的前提下，把裁判打分差映射成校准的胜率软标签（而非二元 win/loss），并叠加 split conformal 预测对 LLM–人类 Elo 残差给出分布无关的区间；在 LMArena 55 个留出模型上，平均 Elo MAE 从 Hard-Elo 的 45.9 降到 17.9，conformal 区间宽度收窄 39–70%。

> **TL;DR:** Soft-Elo fixes LLM-as-a-judge leaderboard miscalibration by propagating *calibrated win probabilities* (from the judge's own score differences) instead of hard win/loss/tie labels into the Bradley–Terry fit, then wrapping each model's Elo with a split-conformal interval over LLM–human residuals. On LMArena 100K with 8 open judges (e.g., DeepSeek-V3.2, Qwen3.5-27B, Llama-3.3-70B), held-out Elo MAE averaged over 55 models drops from 45.9 (Hard-Elo) to 17.9 Elo (Soft-Elo, a 61% reduction), rank order (Spearman ρ) is essentially unchanged (∆ρ ∈ [−0.009, +0.014]), and the 90% conformal interval median width shrinks by 39–70% while maintaining empirical coverage of 92.1–96.4% (e.g., Qwen3.5-27B Hard 212.2 → Soft 74.4 Elo).

## 核心贡献

- 证明 LLM 裁判的"排名对、尺度错"现象：八个裁判虽能恢复模型排序（Spearman ρ 0.868–0.976），但 Hard-Elo 的尺度与人类 Elo 偏差巨大（MAE 27.5–63.4 Elo），使 BT 胜率系统性过自信、conformal 区间过宽。
- 提出 **Soft-Elo**：保持 BT 模型不变，仅将硬标签 y∈{0,0.5,1} 替换为校准软目标 ỹ(x)=σ(β·s(x))，其中 s(x) 是六维评分（adherence/helpfulness/factuality/completeness/clarity/fluency，各 1–10 分）均值之差，β 通过留一模型在人类非平局战斗上做 MLE 标定。
- 在全局层面用 **split conformal prediction** 对 LLM–人类 Elo 残差 εᵢ 做（按自助标准误差归一化的）非一致性打分，给出新模型人类 Elo 的分布无关区间，覆盖率 1−α。
- 全流程**无需被评模型的人类标注**：β 仅在 anchor 模型间的人类非平局战斗上拟合，被评模型只跑 LLM 裁判战斗；代码开源于 github.com/kargibora/SoftElo。

## Key Contributions

- Diagnoses Hard-Elo's "good rankings, bad ruler" failure signature: judges recover ordering (Spearman ρ up to 0.976) but their Elo scale is mis-scaled (held-out MAE 27.5–63.4 Elo), with strength-correlated signed residuals (Pearson +0.49 to +0.90) that inflate the conformal quantile.
- **Soft-Elo**: keeps the BT model fixed but replaces the hard ternary target with a calibrated soft target ỹ(x)=σ(β·s(x)) fit via leave-one-model-out MLE on human non-tie battles, converting the judge's score difference into a calibrated preference probability.
- **Split conformal Elo intervals**: applies normalized nonconformity scores Sᵢ=|εᵢ|/SÈᵢ (bootstrap SE scaled) to LLM–human Elo residuals across held-out models, yielding distribution-free marginal coverage (1−α) intervals on the human Elo axis.
- Empirically shows Soft-Elo cuts mean held-out Elo MAE from 45.9 → 17.9 Elo across 8 judges on LMArena (−61%), preserves rank order (∆ρ ∈ [−0.009,+0.014]), and narrows conformal width by 39–70% at matched ~90% coverage.
- The calibration is sample-efficient: β* stabilizes within ±0.05 of its converged value by ~50 battles/model, and the Soft-Elo advantage is largest in the small-data regime (b=5) where Hard-Elo's discarded signal is most costly.

## Methodology

- **Data:** LMArena 100K — a multilingual (~58% English, plus 12 languages incl. Chinese/Japanese/German) subset of Chatbot Arena pairwise human votes, with M=55 models. Each of 8 open-weight judges annotates ~25,000 balanced battle rows (~910 model appearances per model).
- **Judges (8):** DeepSeek-V3.2, Qwen3.5-27B, Gemma4-E4B, Gemma4-26B-A4B, Qwen3-32B, Llama-3.3-70B, GPT-OSS-20B, GPT-OSS-120B; deterministic decoding (temperature 0.0, top-p 1.0, max 4096 tokens).
- **Battle scoring:** Each completion graded 1–10 on six criteria (adherence, helpfulness, factuality, completeness, clarity, fluency), averaged; battle score s(x)=s(x,A)−s(x,B). To cut position bias, each battle judged twice with swapped order and the two model-oriented differences averaged; sign flip between orders → recorded as tie.
- **Hard-Elo baseline:** Standard BT fit (Eq. 1, L2 λ=0.01, ties split into two 0.5-weight rows) on ternary labels y∈{0,0.5,1}; reported on Elo scale 1500+(400/ln10)θ.
- **Soft-Elo local calibration:** Replace hard target with ỹ(x)=σ(β·s(x)). β* fit per held-out fold via MLE (Eq. 6) on anchor-only human *non-tie* battles B*−m; cross-fold mean β* ∈ [0.36,0.60] (stable within ±0.05 by b≈50, deviation from pooled fit ≤0.016). Validated on Qwen3-32B: σ(s) over-confident (ECE=0.14) vs σ(β*s) calibrated (ECE=0.03).
- **Global conformal:** Per-model signed residual εᵢ=Elo^LLM−Elo^Human; normalized nonconformity Sᵢ=|εᵢ|/SÈᵢ where SÈᵢ is 20-resample bootstrap SE of Elo^LLM within the model's battles. At miscoverage α, interval Ĉ = Elo^LLM ± q̂·SÈ with q̂ the ⌈(1−α)(n+1)⌉-th smallest calibration score; exchangeability → marginal coverage ≥1−α. Evaluated via leave-one-model-out with 27 calibration / 28 test, 5 random splits, α=0.1.
- **Robustness corpora:** Replication on LMArena 140K (more recent models) and ComparIA (French-only, stronger prompt shift) in Appendix D.

## Results & Highlights

- **Held-out Elo MAE drops 45.9 → 17.9 Elo on average** across the 8 judges (Table 2), a 39–73% per-judge reduction — e.g., DeepSeek-V3.2 63.4 → 17.1 (−73%), Qwen3.5-27B 46.0 → 13.6 (−70%), Qwen3-32B 27.5 → 16.7 (−39%, the smallest gain). Rank order is preserved: ∆ρ ∈ [−0.009, +0.014].
- **Hard-Elo is mis-scaled, not mis-ranked:** even the lowest-κ judge Llama-3.3-70B recovers much ordering (ρ=0.868), but all judges incur Elo MAE 27.5–63.4 (Table 1); the problem is the "wrong ruler," not the ranking.
- **Score-difference signal is real:** judge–human agreement on non-tie battles rises monotonically with |s|, from ~53% (near-chance) at small score differences to 85% at |s|>4; Hard-Elo collapses both into the same unit win/loss.
- **Conformal intervals shrink 39–70% at matched coverage (Table 3):** median widths fall from Hard-Elo 131.9–261.0 to Soft-Elo 74.4–143.1 Elo, with empirical coverage holding at 88.6–97.9% (Hard) and 92.1–96.4% (Soft) vs the 90% nominal target. E.g., Qwen3.5-27B: 212.2 → 74.4 Elo; DeepSeek-V3.2: 261.0 → 78.0.
- **Residual ramp flattened:** Hard-Elo signed residuals correlate +0.49 to +0.90 with human Elo (weak models underestimated, strong overestimated); Soft-Elo flattens this strength-correlated ramp (Figure 4b), which is what mechanically narrows the conformal quantile.
- **Sample-efficient & small-data friendly:** Soft-Elo's MAE advantage holds at every budget down to b=5 battles/model; on Qwen3.5-27B, Soft-Elo MAE falls 81→17 Elo as budget grows 5→200, while Hard-Elo only falls 125→48 (Figure 6b).
- **Motivating fragility example:** on Arena-Hard, Gemini-2.5's reported win rate shifts 79.0%±2% → 49.1%±2.5% depending on the judge, moving it from 2nd to 8th place — the uncertainty within a win rate is small relative to the judge-induced shift.

## Critical Assessment

- **Closed-judge blind spot:** All eight evaluated judges are *open-weight* models; the paper's own motivation flags that most production leaderboards rely on *closed-weight* judges (e.g., GPT-4) that "can be deprecated or silently updated," yet Soft-Elo is never validated on a closed-weight judge, so its benefit for the exact regime it motivates is unproven.
- **Self-referential Elo ground truth:** Human Elo itself is treated as ground truth, but it is itself a BT fit on noisy crowd votes; the paper does not report inter-annotator agreement or the sampling uncertainty of Elo^Human, so "17.9 Elo MAE" is measured against a moving target rather than an oracle.
- **Exchangeability assumption is load-bearing and untested under shift:** the conformal guarantee is only marginal and requires exchangeability between calibration and future models; the authors concede prompt-distribution, model-family, or temporal shifts can violate this, and the ComparIA (French) replication (Appendix D.2) is the only stress test — there is no quantitative coverage result reported under actual distribution shift.
- **β calibration excludes ties by design:** the binary MLE for β* is fit on human non-tie battles only; the sensitivity check (treating ties as 0.5) roughly halves β* and raises MAE by ~7 Elo, so the headline 17.9 depends on a non-trivial data-filtering choice whose downstream sensitivity is acknowledged but not bounded.
- **No comparison to competing calibrated-judge methods:** Bridge (Polo et al. 2026, explicitly models judge–human gap), Quantitative LLM Judges (Sahoo et al. 2025), and prediction-powered AutoEval (Boyeau et al. 2025) are discussed as related work but never benchmarked head-to-head, so Soft-Elo's relative standing among calibrated alternatives is unknown.

## Strengths

- Minimal, surgical fix: the BT model and Elo machinery are untouched, so it drops into existing leaderboards with no algorithmic change — only the training target changes.
- Clean failure-mode diagnosis separating rank fidelity (ρ) from scale fidelity (MAE), explaining *why* Hard-Elo intervals are wide (strength-correlated residuals inflating q̂).
- Distribution-free conformal intervals with honest coverage and a per-model bootstrap-normalized nonconformity score that adapts interval width to each model's battle-count reliability.
- Strong, consistent gains across 8 diverse open judges (39–73% MAE reduction each) and strong sample-efficiency story (largest gains in the small-data regime).
- Public code release and full judge protocol/prompt appendix support reproducibility.

## Limitations

- Validated only on open-weight judges; the closed-weight regime that motivates the work is untested.
- Conformal coverage is marginal and exchangeability-conditional; no result under genuine distribution shift (only French-language ComparIA replication).
- Human Elo is treated as ground truth without quantifying its own uncertainty.
- Headline β* depends on excluding human ties; alternative tie-handling raises MAE by ~7 Elo.
- Soft-Elo models only the uncertainty in the score difference; epistemic uncertainty, hallucinated scores, prompt ambiguity, and judge instability are explicitly out of scope (per the authors' own Limitations).

## Relevance

直接映射到用户研究兴趣 **「2. LLM Inference Evaluation」**（自动化 LLM 排行榜、LLM-as-a-Judge 的校准与不确定性量化）。其"软标签校准 + conformal 区间"为低成本、可信赖的模型评测提供了数学上严格的不确定性边界，对构建可靠 reward model 与排行榜管线有直接工程价值，亦与 **「7. ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」**（BT 模型、分布无关覆盖保证）相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does Soft-Elo transfer to closed-weight judges (GPT-4-class) that the paper motivates but never tests — and does the score-difference signal survive when the judge's score is a single coarse preference rather than a 6-criterion rubric?
- How robust is the 90% conformal coverage under genuine temporal/model-family shift (e.g., evaluating a post-calibration-pool frontier model)? The authors flag exchangeability violation but Appendix D.2 (ComparIA) is the only quantitative test and is reported only as replication, not as a coverage-failure probe.
- Could the strength-correlated residual ramp (Hard-Elo Pearson +0.49 to +0.90) be further reduced by a *locally* (per-strength-quartile) calibrated β, and would that tighten conformal intervals beyond the global-β Soft-Elo result?
- How does Soft-Elo compare quantitatively to Bridge (Polo et al. 2026) and prediction-powered AutoEval (Boyeau et al. 2025) on the same LMArena pool — a head-to-head the paper does not run?
- Is the human Elo "ground truth" itself stable enough that 17.9 Elo MAE is meaningful, given BT-on-crowd-votes has its own sampling uncertainty the paper does not propagate?
