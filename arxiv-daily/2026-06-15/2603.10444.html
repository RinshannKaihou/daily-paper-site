Key Finding

FP4 training failures are driven by a coherent rank-one mean bias in activations, not random outliers.

The Curse and Blessing of Mean Bias in FP4-Quantized LLM Training
Hengjie Cao, Zhendong Huang, Mengyi Chen, et al. June 15, 2026 arXiv 2603.10444
FP4 Quantization LLM Training Averis
Key Metric
1.19/0.81%
BF16 loss gap on Qwen3-0.6B / 7B-A1.5B with W4A4G4 NVFP4 — beating NVIDIA Hadamard's 2.05% / 1.10%.
Why This Matters

It turns FP4 training from black-box tuning into a structural diagnosis, and ships a ~2% overhead fix usable on Blackwell-scale training.

Critical Edge

Averis + Hadamard still beats Averis alone, so the rank-one mean story is only part of the picture.

Methodology

The authors first show that activation outliers in FP4 training are not sparse noise. On Qwen3-0.6B, the feature-wise mean μX aligns with the leading right singular vector v₁ (cosine ≈ 0.99) and strengthens monotonically across training. Attention and FFN operators amplify and reshape this mean.

Averis exploits the structure by splitting activations and output gradients into column-mean and residual, quantizing each with blockwise NVFP4, and recombining in the GeMM. Centered residuals kill cross-terms in the weight-gradient GeMM, leaving a simple two-term form.

DiagnoseSVD + spectral analysis reveals rank-one mean bias.

Strengths

  • Clean, falsifiable diagnostic claim backed by cheap PyTorch/NumPy re-runs.
  • Genuinely minimal: only reductions and elementwise subtractions.
  • Beats NVIDIA's freshly released Hadamard baseline at ~30% of its overhead.
  • Composable with Hadamard — additive, not mutually exclusive.
  • Honest limitations disclosed in the text and NeurIPS checklist.

Limitations

  • Limited scale: only 0.6B dense and 7B-A1.5B MoE; no 70B+ or trillion-token runs.
  • No ablations on reduction granularity, block size, or mean-handling options.
  • Single-seed training results with no error bars.
  • Quality numbers from FP4 simulation on Hopper, not native Blackwell end-to-end.
  • Only compared head-to-head with NVIDIA Hadamard.
