> **一句话总结：** TWLA 是一个端到端可推理加速的 PTQ 框架,首次在 W1.58A4 设定下同时压制权重→三值码本失配与激活长尾;核心是 (1) E2M-ATQ 把三元参数化(α, µ, T) 拆成欧氏初始化+流形重定位两阶段以最小化层输出误差,(2) KOTMS 用 Kronecker 结构正交矩阵 + Cayley 参数化把权重整形为三峰分布、并通过同一旋转间接抑制激活异常值,(3) ILA-AMP 引入二阶跨层交互代价并以动态规划精确求解激活比特分配,避免弱层拖垮整模型。

> **TL;DR:** TWLA is a retraining-free PTQ framework that pushes LLM compression to **W1.58A4** (ternary weights + 4-bit activations) without sacrificing accuracy. It identifies two coupled bottlenecks in existing ternarization work: (i) per-channel LLM weights are near-unimodal Gaussians that misalign with the ternary codebook {-1, 0, +1}, and (ii) activations have heavy tails that blow up low-bit activation quantization. Its three modules — an asymmetric Euclidean-to-Manifold ternary quantizer (E2M-ATQ), a Kronecker-structured orthogonal tri-modal shaping rotation (KOTMS), and an inter-layer-aware activation mixed-precision scheme (ILA-AMP) — jointly attack both. Experiments on LLaMA-2 (7B/13B/70B), LLaMA-3-8B, and Qwen3 (8B/14B/32B) show TWLA matches or beats GPTQ, QuaRot, SliM-LLM, ResQ, PB-LLM, and PT2-LLM, with end-to-end 3.64× speedup over FP16 and 1.3× over QuaRot at W1.58A4 on LLaMA-2-13B (llama.cpp, A6000, batch 4 / 128-token prefill / 256-token decode). Code: https://github.com/Kishon-zzx/TWLA.

## 核心贡献

- **识别 W1.58A4 下的两类关键瓶颈**:(i) 预训练权重近似单峰高斯,与 {-1,0,+1} 三值码本失配导致大量元素落在量化边界附近;(ii) 激活长尾异常值在低比特下引发严重失真。现有 PTQ 三值化方法通常只解决其中一个问题,无法在 W1.58A4 下保持精度。
- **E2M-ATQ(欧氏→流形非对称三值化器)**:用非对称参数化 W̄ = µ 1⊤ + diag(α) T 显式建模行均值偏差。两阶段流程——(1) 欧氏域内 Frobenius 重建以稳定 T 与初值(µ, α),(2) 在校准激活二阶矩 S 诱导的流形上对 (µ, α) 做闭式重定位,直接最小化层输出误差。
- **KOTMS(Kronecker 正交三峰整形)**:用 R = R₁ ⊗ R₂ 替代稠密正交矩阵,通过 Cayley 参数化 (I+A)⁻¹(I-A) 在优化全程保持正交性,既把权重推向 {-cᵢ, 0, +cᵢ} 三峰分布,又通过同一旋转的"正交等变性"间接削弱激活长尾。
- **ILA-AMP(层间感知激活混合精度)**:首次在 LLM MPQ 中引入**相邻层二阶交互代价 Kℓ₋₁,ℓ(b′,b)**,把激活比特分配写成一个预算约束下的二次代理目标;由于 K 只涉及相邻层对,代理目标具有链式结构,可被**动态规划**精确求解,避免启发式贪心引发精度级联崩溃。
- **端到端可加速**:在 llama.cpp 中利用原生三值算子,实现 W1.58A4 实际部署加速(3.64× over FP16, 1.3× over QuaRot,LLaMA-2-13B,A6000,bs=4),并展示量化时间成本可控(7B 仅 82 min,快于 SliM-LLM 158 min)。

## Key Contributions

- **Diagnosis of W1.58A4 failure modes.** The paper gives a clean empirical decomposition: (1) per-channel weight distributions are near-unimodal Gaussians that are misaligned with the ternary codebook, so naive rounding leaves many elements on decision boundaries; (2) activations have heavy tails and large outliers, which dominate low-bit activation distortion. Prior ternarization PTQ methods typically address one but not both.
- **E2M-ATQ (Euclidean-to-Manifold Asymmetric Ternary Quantizer).** An asymmetric parameterization W̄ = µ 1⊤ + diag(α) T that explicitly carries the per-row mean (the "bias" the symmetric codebook misses). A two-stage procedure:
  - *Euclidean warm-start:* minimize the Frobenius reconstruction error ‖W − µ 1⊤ − diag(α)T‖²F to lock in a stable ternary pattern T⁽⁰⁾ and good (µ⁽⁰⁾, α⁽⁰⁾) initial values (closed-form updates of α, T plus residual-mean correction on µ; 15 iterations in practice).
  - *Manifold relocation:* freeze T = T⁽⁰⁾, treat S = Σ_b X_b⊤ X_b (activation second moment) as a metric, and solve for (µ, α) by minimizing Tr(ESE⊤). Because the Hessian couples columns, optimizing T here would be combinatorial, so T is fixed and only the continuous (µ, α) are relocated — yielding a closed-form 2×2 per-row linear system.
- **KOTMS (Kronecker Orthogonal Tri-Modal Shaping).** Replaces the dense Hadamard / orthogonal rotation used by QuaRot-style methods with a **Kronecker-factored** orthogonal R = R₁ ⊗ R₂ (with n₁·n₂ = m, n₁, n₂ chosen to be balanced for low overhead). Application is just two compact matmuls via `vR = vec(R₂⊤ Vmat R₁)`. Each factor is parameterized as `Rk = (I+Ak)⁻¹(I−Ak)` with a skew-symmetric generator Ak = Sk − Sk⊤ (Cayley transform), which guarantees exact orthogonality throughout the calibration optimization. The training signal is a row-wise **truncated Gaussian mixture** NLL with three modes {-cᵢ, 0, +cᵢ} (modes are row-adaptive; π₊=π₋=(1−π₀)/2), plus a regularizer on the zero-mode responsibility to prevent mode collapse. The soft-projection interpretation gives a differentiable approximation to the hard ternary rounding Tij ← Π_{−1,0,1}(·).
- **ILA-AMP (Inter-Layer Aware Activation Mixed Precision).** Solves the layer-wise activation bit allocation under a global budget. For each layer ℓ and each candidate bit-width b ∈ {2,4,6,8} (relative to a reference uniform-8 configuration):
  - Per-layer first-order cost: Cℓ(b) = vNLL(bℓ=b, b_{k≠ℓ}=8) − vNLL(8).
  - **Adjacent-layer second-order interaction cost** Kℓ₋₁,ℓ(b′,b) = vNLL(bℓ₋₁=b′, bℓ=b, b_{k/∈{ℓ−1,ℓ}}=8) − vNLL(8) − Cℓ₋₁(b′) − Cℓ(b). Positive K means quantizing the pair amplifies error beyond the independent-layer sum.
  - The full surrogate is a chain-structured quadratic: min Σℓ Cℓ(bℓ) + Σℓ Kℓ₋₁,ℓ(bℓ₋₁, bℓ) s.t. Σℓ bℓ ≤ B. Solved **exactly by dynamic programming** under the budget constraint, with backtracking to recover the optimal assignment.
- **Practical efficiency story.** Implements with llama.cpp ternary operators; on LLaMA-2-13B (A6000, batch 4, 128 prefill / 256 decode), reports 86.35 tok/s vs. 23.70 tok/s FP16 (3.64×) and 66.42 tok/s QuaRot W2A4 (1.3×), with parameter memory 23.7GB → 3.34GB (>80% reduction; effective ≈1.6 bpw when packing five ternary values per uint8). Quantization time is 82 min on LLaMA-2-7B (faster than SliM-LLM's 158 min, slower than QuaRot's 31 min).

## Methodology

### Setup

The pipeline is **fully PTQ**: no backpropagation through the model, no fine-tuning, no labeled data. The only inputs are the pretrained weights and a small calibration set (128 sequences × 2048 tokens from WikiText2, following common practice; an ablation on calibration size/seed is deferred to the appendix).

### E2M-ATQ in detail

Standard ternarization (TWN) computes a per-row threshold Δᵢ ≈ 0.75·(1/m) Σⱼ |Wᵢⱼ| and a per-row scale α via least-squares. The authors first generalize to an **asymmetric** codebook to absorb the row-wise mean that pretrained LLM weights frequently exhibit:

W̄ = µ 1⊤ + diag(α) T, T ∈ {−1, 0, +1}^{n×m}, µ, α ∈ ℝⁿ, 1 ∈ ℝᵐ.

A residual-mean correction µ ← µ + (1/m) E·1 is applied after each update to control the bias term.

The first stage iterates:
- Update α by the closed-form least-squares formula (per row).
- Update T by re-applying the threshold rule with the current µ, α.
- Recompute µ with the residual correction.

This is run for 15 iterations; the paper proves the objective decreases monotonically. The output is a stable T⁽⁰⁾ and Euclidean (µ⁽⁰⁾, α⁽⁰⁾).

The second stage freezes T = T⁽⁰⁾ and solves for (µ, α) on the metric manifold induced by the calibration second moment S = Σ_b X_b⊤ X_b ∈ ℝᵐˣᵐ:

L₂(µ, α; T) = Tr(ESE⊤), E = W − µ 1⊤ − diag(α) T.

Per row, this yields a 2×2 linear system in (αᵢ, µᵢ) (Eq. 8–9 in the paper), giving a closed-form solution. The key point is that, with S typically dense, the metric is non-diagonal, so this step recovers loss that the Frobenius objective cannot.

### KOTMS in detail

Pretrained LLM weights being close to Gaussian means many entries sit near the ternary decision boundaries; KOTMS first **rotates** the coordinate system to concentrate mass around {-cᵢ, 0, +cᵢ}.

- **Shaping loss.** A row-wise soft ternary-alignment objective: a 3-component Gaussian mixture with modes at the ternary-aligned anchors {-cᵢ, 0, +cᵢ} (cᵢ = (1/m)Σⱼ |zᵢⱼ| with z = wR, σᵢ = std of the row), mixing weights π₊=π₋=(1−π₀)/2, plus a regularizer on π₀. The authors emphasize this is a *differentiable approximation* to the hard ternary projection, so backpropagating through it produces a useful signal for R.
- **Orthogonality-preserving optimization.** Each Kronecker factor Rk is parameterized by a free matrix Sk via the **Cayley transform** Rk = (I + Ak)⁻¹(I − Ak) with Ak = Sk − Sk⊤. This keeps Rk exactly orthogonal at every step and avoids explicit re-projection (e.g., SVD) that would otherwise be needed after every gradient step.
- **Activation-side benefit.** Because the same R⁻¹ = R⊤ is applied to activations, the shared rotation disperses concentrated activation directions and statistically suppresses outliers — but the authors are explicit that this is an *indirect by-product*, with the gain "highly uneven across layers" (justifying ILA-AMP).
- **Overhead control.** A 1D ablation on the Kronecker split (n₁/n₂ from 128 down to 1) shows the effective overhead of KOTMS (in bits) drops from ~1 bit to <0.01 bit as the split becomes balanced, with <2% accuracy loss on the Qwen3 family. The paper uses balanced n₁, n₂ throughout.

### ILA-AMP in detail

The core observation: KOTMS optimizes the rotation in weight space, not activation space, so the activation quantizability gain is heterogeneous across layers. Some layers will still be "weak" bottlenecks under uniform 4-bit activation; the standard layer-wise independent-sensitivity MPQ assumption fails because quantizing layer ℓ shifts the input distribution of layer ℓ+1.

The construction is:
- Use a **reference** uniform-8-bit configuration (instead of FP16) to compute perturbations: this gives lower-variance sensitivity estimates.
- Per-layer first-order cost Cℓ(b) and adjacent interaction cost Kℓ₋₁,ℓ(b′, b) defined as the residual NLL change after subtracting the independent-layer contributions (Eq. 19–20).
- The surrogate minimization min Σℓ Cℓ(bℓ) + Σℓ Kℓ₋₁,ℓ(bℓ₋₁, bℓ) subject to Σℓ bℓ ≤ B has a **chain structure** (only adjacent pairs interact), which admits an exact DP solution. The paper argues that extending interactions to non-adjacent layers gives diminishing returns and explodes the search space.
- The resulting bit allocation is concrete and visualizable; the appendix reports per-layer assignments.

### End-to-end pipeline

Calibration data → E2M-ATQ (15 iter) → KOTMS (100 iter, lr=0.01) → ILA-AMP (DP over {2,4,6,8} per layer, budget B). At deployment, the model uses a packed ternary weight format (5 ternary values per uint8) and the KOTMS rotation is absorbed into the adjacent linear / attention projections, so there is **no runtime overhead from KOTMS** beyond the two compact matmuls applied once at the start of a forward pass.

## Results & Highlights

### Setup

- **Models:** LLaMA-2-7B/13B/70B, LLaMA-3-8B, Qwen3-8B/14B/32B, plus Qwen3-32B-Instruct for instruction-tuned evaluation.
- **Calibration:** 128 sequences × 2048 tokens from WikiText2. E2M-ATQ = 15 iter; KOTMS = 100 iter, lr=0.01.
- **Hardware:** NVIDIA A6000 GPUs; deployment uses llama.cpp.
- **Metrics:** WikiText-2 perplexity, C4 perplexity, average of 7 zero-shot tasks (ARC-Challenge/Easy, HellaSwag, LAMBADA-openai/standard, PIQA, WinoGrande); on Qwen3-32B-Instruct additionally MMLU, HumanEval, GSM8K.
- **Baselines:** GPTQ, QuaRot, SliM-LLM, ResQ, PB-LLM, PT2-LLM. (Notably, no BitNet b1.58 — see Critical Assessment.)

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: moderate-to-high.** Each individual ingredient has prior art (rotations for activation outliers → QuaRot, SmoothQuant; asymmetric ternarization → PT2-LLM; mixed-precision → many; Cayley transform → also from the orthogonal-net literature). The novelty is the **specific composition** — Kronecker-factored Cayley-parameterized orthogonal + non-asymmetric Euclidean-to-Manifold two-stage quantizer + adjacent-layer second-order DP-MPQ. This is a real engineering contribution, not a rebrand.
- **Rigor: mostly strong.**
  - Baselines are appropriate and broad: GPTQ, QuaRot, SliM-LLM, ResQ, PB-LLM, PT2-LLM. The headline W1.58A4 comparison against PT2-LLM and ResQ is exactly the right set.
  - Ablations cover the three modules and the KOTMS overhead; calibration-data ablations are in the appendix.
  - ILA-AMP is **provably exact** under the chain interaction assumption (DP gives a global optimum), which is a clean rigor win.
  - The "adjacent-only" interaction is justified but is a modeling assumption; whether non-adjacent coupling matters in practice is an open question.
- **Reproducibility: good.** Code is released, PTQ-only, calibration set is the standard WikiText2. The Cayley parameterization and DP-MPQ are deterministic.
- **Significance: high.** W1.58A4 is the regime that *actually* moves inference cost (1.58-bit weights + 4-bit activations both fall under the fast-kernel regime in llama.cpp). A retraining-free route to that regime is the practical prize. The 3.64× throughput on LLaMA-2-13B and ~1.3× over QuaRot W2A4 are concrete deployment wins.
- **Red flags / things to watch in the PDF / things the paper doesn't quite do:**
  - **No BitNet b1.58 comparison.** BitNet b1.58 is the canonical 1.58-bit-weight method, and the recent BitNet v2 paper actually reports 4-bit activations with 1.58-bit weights. It is QAT, not PTQ, but the W1.58A4 *accuracy* numbers should be compared to put TWLA's PTQ claim in context. The paper frames BitNet v2 as too expensive (10⁴ GPU hours of QAT) and excludes it, but the apples-to-apples accuracy table is still missing.
  - **The KOTMS overhead ablation hides a non-trivial cost.** KOTMS stores two small orthogonal factors and applies two matmuls; at deployment these are absorbed into the surrounding projections, but the *calibration* time is non-trivial. The 82 min vs. QuaRot's 31 min is fair to mention but underweighted.
  - **Adjacent-only interaction is a modeling assumption.** A non-adjacent ablation (e.g., Kℓ₋₁,ℓ₊₁) would strengthen the claim. The argument that "non-adjacent coupling is small" is plausible but not shown.
  - **No 70B-class evaluation under A4 on harder reasoning.** The LLaMA-2-70B A4 result is reported on zero-shot + WikiText-2 PPL; the 70B model on GSM8K / MMLU at A4 would be the strongest stress test and is missing.
  - **llama.cpp is a specific deployment stack.** The throughput numbers (3.64× / 1.3×) are measured on llama.cpp's ternary kernels. This is a fair test of a real deployment, but it is **not** a measurement on TensorRT-LLM, vLLM, or SGLang, which is where production LLM serving usually runs. Whether the speedup transfers to those stacks depends on whether they have equivalent ternary-GEMM kernels (they generally don't yet).
  - **No per-batch-size sensitivity on throughput.** The 3.64× / 1.3× numbers are at a single (batch=4, prefill=128, decode=256) configuration. Low-bit kernels are notoriously batch-size-sensitive; a sweep would be more convincing.
  - **The "high accuracy" language is consistent with the data**, but the LLaMA-2-13B W1.58A4 zero-shot accuracy of 64.30 vs. FP16 72.19 is still an ~8-point gap; this is "high" only relative to alternatives, not relative to FP16. The paper is honest about this in the tables.

## Strengths

- Attacks the *right* problem: low-bit activations have been the long-standing barrier to true end-to-end low-bit inference; TWLA shows it is achievable in PTQ.
- Three modules have clearly distinct, complementary roles (quantizer calibration / weight coordinate transformation / activation bit allocation), and the ablation in Table 3 makes the synergy explicit.
- W1.58A4 deployment is real, not just memory: llama.cpp ternary kernels give 3.64× over FP16 and 1.3× over QuaRot W2A4, with parameter memory down to 3.34 GB for a 13B model.
- The KOTMS Cayley parameterization is a clean engineering choice that avoids re-projection and keeps the rotation exactly orthogonal throughout calibration.
- The ILA-AMP second-order interaction model is, to my knowledge, new for LLM MPQ, and the chain-structured DP is an elegant exact solution.
- Code released; ICML acceptance; broad model coverage (LLaMA-2/3, Qwen3, instruction-tuned variant).
- Calibration footprint is small (128 × 2048 tokens) and the method is data-free beyond that.

## Limitations

- The KOTMS rotation only indirectly helps activations; this is honestly acknowledged in the paper and is why ILA-AMP is needed, but it does mean there is no direct control over activation quantizability — only statistical smoothing of outlier directions.
- The adjacent-only interaction model in ILA-AMP is a simplification; in deep transformer stacks, error can propagate across more than one layer, especially in the residual stream.
- Quantization time (82 min for 7B) is not negligible and grows with model size; the paper does not report 70B quantization time, only 7B.
- Throughput numbers are stack-specific (llama.cpp, A6000) and batch-size-specific; transferability to vLLM/TensorRT-LLM and to other batch sizes is unverified.
- The "1.58-bit" framing inherits BitNet b1.58's nomenclature, but unlike BitNet, TWLA's weights are per-row-asymmetric ternarized and the *activation* path goes through a learned rotation; the effective bit width accounting depends on the packing scheme (5 ternary values per uint8 → ~1.6 bpw). The paper's own numbers (3.34 GB for LLaMA-2-13B) confirm this.
- No comparison to BitNet b1.58 (training-time 1.58-bit) or BitNet v2 (QAT 1.58-bit + 4-bit act), which is the most direct competitor at the W1.58A4 bit budget, even if it is QAT.
- The paper does not ablate whether E2M-ATQ's manifold relocation step is necessary vs. just using the Euclidean step with the second-moment-aware scale; a no-manifold-relocation row in Table 3 would isolate the contribution.

## Relevance

与本人关注的 **LLM 推理加速 / 低比特量化 / PTQ** 方向高度相关。TWLA 在 W1.58A4 这一"真正可端到端加速"的比特组合下,用纯 PTQ(无 QAT、无微调)就达到与 3-bit GPTQ 相当甚至更好的精度,并在 llama.cpp 上实现 3.64× 加速,对资源受限部署(本地/边缘/隐私敏感场景)有直接工程价值。其 ILA-AMP 的"相邻层二阶交互 + DP 精确求解"思路也可迁移到其他 LLM 混合精度问题。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does TWLA compare head-to-head with **BitNet b1.58** (training-time ternary) and **BitNet v2** (QAT 1.58-bit weights + 4-bit act) on the same benchmarks? Even if BitNet v2 is excluded for cost reasons, an explicit accuracy table would clarify the PTQ-vs-QAT accuracy gap at W1.58A4.
- How does the ILA-AMP bit allocation **change** as one moves from A4 to A6 to A8 budgets, and is the per-layer ranking stable across runs / calibration seeds?
- Does KOTMS's Kronecker split (n₁, n₂) **transfer across model sizes**? (e.g., split learned on 7B, applied to 13B / 70B without re-optimization.) If so, this would be a strong practical selling point.
- Is the **KOTMS rotation well-conditioned for production inference kernels**? Specifically, does the Kronecker structure interact poorly with the weight-tiling layouts used by TensorRT-LLM, vLLM, or SGLang, and would a deployment adapter be needed?
- What is the **perplexity / accuracy gap on harder reasoning benchmarks** at W1.58A4 (MMLU, GSM8K, HumanEval) for the larger models (LLaMA-2-70B, Qwen3-32B)? The 32B-Instruct numbers are encouraging; the 70B base is not reported.
- How sensitive is the result to the **calibration set size and domain**? The paper defers this to the appendix; this matters a lot for PTQ in practice (a method that needs 10K calibration sequences is much harder to use than one that needs 128).
- Could E2M-ATQ's manifold relocation step be **merged with KOTMS** into a single alternating optimization (rotate, ternarize, rotate, …), and would that give a strictly better local optimum?
- How does TWLA interact with **KV-cache quantization**, **speculative decoding**, and **continuous batching**? These are the other three big wins in modern LLM serving; combining them with W1.58A4 weights/acts is the natural next step.
- What is the **interaction-order cost-benefit trade-off** beyond adjacent? The paper justifies adjacent-only with diminishing returns; a small ablation on Kℓ₋₂,ℓ would be a 10-line experiment that would close the question.
- Does the ILA-AMP DP remain tractable for **block-wise** activation bit allocation (multiple points per layer), and would that buy back accuracy under tight budgets?
