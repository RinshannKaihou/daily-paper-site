C2-Faith: Benchmarking LLM Judges for Causal and Coverage Faithfulness in Chain-of-Thought Reasoning

Avni Mittal · Rauno Arike
One-line finding: Frontier LLM judges can tell that a CoT trace is wrong, but they cannot reliably tell where it is wrong — and they systematically overestimate how much reasoning is actually present.
⚠️ Abstract-only summary · not browser-verified
Key Finding

Detection ≠ Attribution

LLM judges flag causal errors well, yet step-level localization accuracy is poor. Plausible-sounding reasoning masks broken process.

Key Metric
3

Judge × Task Grid

Three frontier judges evaluated on binary causal detection, causal step localization, and coverage scoring over controlled PRM800K perturbations.

Why This Matters

Process Supervision Is Fragile

Reward models and PRMs built on LLM-judge scores risk rewarding answers that look right while hiding unfaithful reasoning.

Critical Edge

Thin, Math-Only Slice

Only three closed-weight frontier judges; PRM800K math domain; single-step perturbations; prompt sensitivity unknown.

Methodology

  • Source: PRM800K process-supervised CoT corpus, using its per-step correctness labels as ground truth.
  • Causality: Replace one step with a logically inconsistent variant to create known causal-error positions.
  • Coverage: Delete intermediate steps at varying rates to produce measurable coverage shortfalls.
  • Tasks: Binary error detection, step localization, and coverage scoring.

Results & Highlights

  • No dominant judge: Rankings shuffle across detection, localization, and coverage scoring.
  • Detection-attribution gap: High error recall does not translate to usable step attribution.
  • Coverage inflation: All judges predict high coverage even after deliberate step deletion — a calibration failure.
  • Takeaway: Judge scores conflate plausible answers with faithful reasoning.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Clean causality/coverage decomposition, though controlled perturbations are not new.
  • Rigor: Reasonable substrate, but math-only generalization is unaddressed.
  • Reproducibility: Artifact release unclear; prompt sensitivity undiscussed in abstract.
  • Red flag: Just three closed-weight judges and a two-author team with thin public footprint.

Strengths & Limitations

  • Strengths: Orthogonal dimensions, known-label design, intuitive pattern, practical warning for PRM pipelines.
  • Limitations: Math-only domain, thin judge sample, possible well-formedness priors, single-step perturbations, no inter-annotator discussion.

Relevance & Open Questions

Relevance: Directly addresses LLM-as-a-Judge reliability for process-level reasoning evaluation. The detection/attribution gap and coverage overestimation are immediate concerns for training PRMs and reward models.

Does the gap persist in code / science / legal CoT? How prompt-sensitive are the results? Can a calibration layer fix coverage inflation? Would a detector + localizer pipeline help?
