Key Finding

Tri-role co-evolution with hybrid tool/no-tool training sustains gains to 1500 steps.

While RL/self-play baselines plateau by 1200 steps, HOTE-8B keeps rising (58.0 → 59.9), indicating open-ended improvement rather than memorization.

Key Metric

59.1 avg · 753.3 s/step

Beats DR Tulu-8B-RL (56.0), Dr. Zero (56.3), and Tongyi DeepResearch-30B-A3B (51.2) with ~34% lower wall-clock time per step.

Why This Matters

Strong open-source deep research at 8B scale.

It unifies deep research and agent self-evolution for open-ended, citation-rich report generation, making high-end research agents cheaper and more accessible.

Critical Edge

Meta-rubrics + hybrid mode.

Compressed shared rubrics make proposer training tractable; mixing tool/no-tool tasks cuts cost and prevents over-reliance on parametric knowledge.

⚙ Architecture at a glance

Proposer Generates challenging, learnable synthetic queries from meta-rubrics; trained with GRPO.
Solver ReAct agent with think / tool / answer / cite actions; evolves via GRPO + token-level loss.
Judge Fixed Qwen3-235B evolves rubrics, emits meta-rubrics, and assigns 0/±1 rewards to fight score inflation.

⚠ Critical assessment

  • No human evaluation or citation-faithfulness metric is reported.
  • Single judge family (Qwen3) introduces potential judge-model bias.
  • No-tool evaluation sometimes beats tool-use; retrieval noise is asserted but not measured.
  • Appendix-B bound covers generation time, not end-to-end training cost.
  • Scaling beyond 8B and multilingual deep research remain open.
  • Closed-weight judge means rubric evolution is tied to Qwen3-235B behavior.
