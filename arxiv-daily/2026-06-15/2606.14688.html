arXiv 2606.14688 · AI-for-Math · Language Generation in the Limit

Flood and Harvest: The Provable Necessity of Trivia

A perfect proof checker can guard the border of truth, but it cannot tell you which truths are worth keeping. Inside the formal world F, an unknown valuable language H hides; only a stream of examples can steer the harvest.

Authors Xiaoyu Li, Andi Han, Dai Shi, Zheng Gao, Jiaojiao Jiang, Junbin Gao 17 pages + 11 appendices abs ↗

Key Finding

Verification is not taste

A perfect verifier only identifies the ambient formal language F. Within any fiber of candidates sharing that F, it supplies zero extra bits about which H is valuable.

Key Metric

α/2 ↔ 1−α/2

Sharp phase transition in coverage c*: finite trivia caps at α/2; any unbounded trivia allowance jumps to 1−α/2 already at rate O(N−1/2).

Why This Matters

The flood is unavoidable

For AI-for-math, broad coverage of valuable mathematics provably requires an infinite stream of correct-but-worthless proofs. Better verifiers cannot eliminate the need for selectivity.

Critical Edge

Tightness has boundaries

The dichotomy is tight for cores presented as the candidate intersection. The singleton sparse-core variant remains open, and real prover pipelines (Lean, Coq) are not yet measured.

01 · Model

The paper layers a verifier-visible formal world F over an unknown valuable world H ⊂ F. Outputs fall into three modes:

  • Hallucination — outside F, blocked by the verifier.
  • Trivia — inside F but outside H, valid yet worthless.
  • Value — inside H and not yet seen, the true harvest.

The adversary reveals only a core C ⊂ H of density α, modelling the recorded literature.

02 · Mechanism

Race + sweep. The tight generator G* races on the identified intersection I(t) and sweeps on the union of consistent candidates.

  • Racing guarantees at least half coverage against an adversary that recycles claimed elements.
  • Sweeping eventually claims every unrecorded valuable statement, but needs infinitely many trivia rounds.
  • The same generator reaches both ends of the dichotomy.

03 · The Phase Transition

On the tight family 𝓗α, optimum coverage depends not on how fast trivia arrive, but on whether their total count is finite or infinite. Click each regime to see the implication.

Finite trivia

c* = α/2

Only the race mechanism can run indefinitely.

Infinite trivia

c* = 1 − α/2

Sweep rounds reach the unrecorded mass.

Infinite trivia at vanishing rate O(N−1/2) already unlocks the full 1−α/2 coverage. The gap 1−α is exactly the unrecorded valuable mass H \ C.

04 · Instantiation

In Aksenov–Bodnia–Freedman–Mulligan's compression model of mathematics:

  • Abelian refinement chains have singleton tell-tales — structured domains need no trivia.
  • Free-monoid name-cut pairs transport the dichotomy at every rational α.
  • Unbounded cuts produce the extreme 0-vs-1 separation.

05 · System Design Read

The checker decides how far formal exploration can go; the examples decide where it should go.

  • Filter trivia after verification — the two questions are orthogonal.
  • A generate-then-select pipeline is not a hack; it is structurally necessary.
  • "We must generate; we will select."
