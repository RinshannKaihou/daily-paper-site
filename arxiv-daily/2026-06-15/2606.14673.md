> **一句话总结：** 通过证明 CC 玩具模型等价于在 `y = ReLU(x) + Mx` 上训练的单层 MLP（其中 `M = I − W_E W_E^T` 来自残差流噪声），并展示超额性能随 `||M||` 单调变化、学到的神经元方向集中在 `M` 的 top-50 特征子空间、SNMF 基线能复现定性损失曲线，本文论证 Braun et al. (2025) 的"压缩计算"并非真正的叠加计算。
> **TL;DR:** This paper reverse-engineers Braun et al.'s Compressed Computation (CC) toy model and shows its apparent ability to compute 100 ReLU functions with 50 neurons is actually driven by an unintended input-mixing matrix M = I − W_E W_E^T induced by the noisy residual stream. The CC advantage disappears when M = 0, scales monotonically with ‖M‖, and the learned neurons concentrate in the top-50-eigenvalue subspace of M. An SNMF baseline derived purely from M qualitatively reproduces the loss curve. Venue: NeurIPS 2025 Workshop on Mechanistic Interpretability.

## 核心贡献
- **简化 CC 为单层 MLP**：证明带残差流的 CC 模型 `ŷ′ = W_E^T(W_E x + W_out′ ReLU(W_in′ W_E x))` 等价于训练 `ŷ = W_out ReLU(W_in x)` 去拟合标签 `y = ReLU(x) + M x`，其中 `M = I − W_E W_E^T`。
- **隔离真正的驱动力**：在 `M = 0` 干净数据上训练时模型无法超越"朴素 50 ReLU"基线（损失 0.0833），证明超额损失完全依赖于混合矩阵。
- **混合矩阵幅度的单调律**：CC 的最优损失随噪声尺度 σ ∈ [0, 0.08] 单调下降（σ ≲ 0.03），与叠加假设无关。
- **特征方向的几何证据**：学到的每个神经元的 `(W_out W_in)` 映射基本只保留 M 的 top-50 奇异向量（其余被强烈衰减），并与 `M` 的逐元素相关性高。
- **SNMF 基线**：用 M 的半非负矩阵分解构造手写权重，能在多种 σ 下超越朴素基线，定性匹配 CC 曲线。
- **稠密解机制**：在高 p 区间 CC 通过"朴素 50 + 偏置项"得到改进，与叠加计算无关。

## Key Contributions
- **Algebraic simplification.** Derive that Braun et al.'s residual CC architecture is mathematically equivalent to a one-hidden-layer MLP trained on labels `y = ReLU(x) + M x`, exposing the previously implicit mixing term `M = I − W_E W_E^T` from the embedding `W_E ∈ ℝ^{1000×100}`.
- **Necessity of M for the "CC advantage".** Train under three conditions — clean (M=0), random `M ~ N(0, σ²)`, and embedding-induced M — and show only nonzero M reproduces Braun et al.'s better-than-naive loss at low sparsity p. Fine-tuning an M≠0 model on clean labels (M=0) immediately returns loss to the naive 0.0833 baseline.
- **Loss scaling with ‖M‖.** Sweep noise scale σ from 0 to 0.08 and show optimal loss decreases monotonically with σ up to ~0.03, then rises — direct evidence the gain comes from the noise term, not from computing 100 ReLU targets.
- **Geometric alignment.** Each neuron's read/write directions `(W_in, W_out)` lie predominantly in the 50-D subspace spanned by the top singular vectors of `I + M` (or top eigenvalues of M), with a sharp drop-off in alignment around index 50.
- **Weight–mixing correlation.** `M_{ij}` is strongly correlated with the corresponding entry of `W_out W_in` (modulo a diagonal offset), and `W_in` is mostly non-negative while both weight matrices are sparse — suggesting the trained solution approximates an SNMF of M.
- **Hand-coded SNMF baseline.** Set `W_in` to the non-negative factor of `M = SNMF(W_out W_in)`; this beats the naive baseline across σ but does not reach the trained model's loss, leaving a residual gap.
- **Dense-regime explained separately.** For p ≳ 0.2, the model uses 50 neurons for 50 features and approximates the other 50 via a learned bias offset — a "naive + offset" solution, not superposition.

## Methodology
**Setup.** Inputs `x_0…x_99` are sparse: nonzero with probability p, drawn uniformly from [−1, 1]; the network is a one-hidden-layer MLP `ŷ = W_out ReLU(W_in x)` with only `W_in, W_out` trainable.

**Three M conditions.**
- Clean: `M = 0` → pure ReLU targets.
- Random: `M_{ij} ~ N(0, σ²)` (symmetric variant gives the closest match to Braun et al.).
- Embedding-induced: `M = I − W_E W_E^T` from the original architecture's fixed random `W_E ∈ ℝ^{1000×100}`.

**Training.** Batch size 2048, learning rate 0.003 with cosine scheduler, 10,000 non-empty batches; in some sweeps a scale σ is optimized jointly with the weights for convenience. (No train set / val set / test set split is reported — toy setup, deterministic synthetic data.)

**Analyses.**
- Loss-vs-p curves reproducing Braun et al. (Appendix C, Fig. 7), separating a "CC solution" (sparse, all features ~70% represented) from a "dense solution" (50 features ~100%, 50 features via offset).
- Eigenvalue / SVD analysis of `M` and `I+M`; cosine-similarity profile of `(W_out W_in)` projected onto top singular vectors.
- SNMF decomposition of `M` used to construct hand-coded `W_in, W_out` weights.

## Results & Highlights
- **Reproduction.** A 1-layer MLP trained on `y = ReLU(x) + (I − W_E W_E^T) x` reproduces the qualitative CC loss-vs-p curve of Braun et al. (2025); a *symmetric* random `M ~ N(0, σ²)` matches it almost exactly.
- **Necessity.** With `M = 0`, the model does not beat the naive 0.0833 baseline in the sparse regime. Fine-tuning a trained M≠0 model on clean data immediately reverts to naive loss — ruling out a "training-dynamics" explanation.
- **Scaling.** Loss-vs-σ is U-shaped with a minimum near σ ≈ 0.03; the gain is monotonic for small σ.
- **Geometry.** `(W_out W_in)` is well-aligned (cos-sim ≈ 1) with the top ~50 singular vectors of `I + M` / top eigenvalues of M; alignment drops sharply beyond index 50.
- **SNMF baseline.** Hand-coded SNMF-from-M weights beat the naive baseline across σ values and reproduce the qualitative U-shape, but the absolute loss is higher than the trained model and weights are less sparse.
- **Dense regime** explained by a hand-coded "naive + offset" solution that matches the trained model's loss in the p ≳ 0.2 regime — not a superposition phenomenon.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty.** Solid for the interp community. The algebraic equivalence (CC residual = 1-layer MLP on mixed labels) is a clean, publishable insight that was not previously made explicit. However, the broader pattern — "a sparse-input toy model's apparent gain is actually due to label-side noise" — is a recurring genre.
- **Rigor.** Strong. Three conditions (clean / random / embed-induced), a fine-tune-to-clean transplant experiment, eigenvalue alignment, and a derived baseline all converge on the same conclusion. The mathematics in Appendix A is straightforward and verifiable.
- **Reproducibility.** Excellent. Toy setup, deterministic synthetic data, code URL cited in the paper, simple training (10k batches, single GPU not required). A motivated reader can re-run in a few hours.
- **Significance.** High for anyone citing Braun et al. (2025) — this paper effectively deprecates CC as a toy model of computation in superposition. Lower for the broader superposition literature, which has many other models.
- **Red flags.** The SNMF baseline does not match the trained model's loss and its weights are less sparse; the authors are honest about this. The dense-regime "naive + offset" explanation is described but only briefly, with the caveat "we did not explore this further". The title's "(probably)" reflects appropriate epistemic hedging. The negative result is well-evidenced but does not propose a replacement toy model, so the field is left slightly under-served.

## Strengths
- Cleanest possible experimental design: same architecture, three well-separated M conditions.
- Multi-pronged evidence (loss scaling + geometry + SNMF + fine-tune transplant).
- The mathematical simplification is genuinely useful — future work can now reason about CC without the residual-stream scaffolding.
- Reproducible code released; small compute footprint.

## Limitations
- Does not propose a *new* toy model of computation in superposition — only invalidates one.
- SNMF-from-M does not fully recover the trained model's loss or sparsity, leaving a residual mechanism unexplained.
- The dense-regime story is hand-wavy and partially deferred ("we did not explore this further").
- Experiments are limited to a single task family (100 ReLU targets on sparse scalar inputs); generalization to other superposition toy models is asserted but not demonstrated.
- Only 100 features, 50 neurons — a regime where the linear-algebraic interpretation may be artificially clean.

## Relevance
与"机制可解释性 / 叠加假设 (superposition)"方向直接相关：若你正在跟踪或引用 Braun et al. (2025) 的压缩计算玩具模型，本文是必读的反对证据与机制澄清。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Does the same residual-stream-induced mixing shortcut appear in other superposition toy models (e.g., Elhage et al.'s original setup, Hänni et al. 2024, Bushnaq & Mendel 2024)?
- Can a "clean" CC variant be constructed (noise-free residual stream / no `M`) that *genuinely* exhibits computation in superposition, or is the regime inherently linear-algebraic?
- What accounts for the residual loss gap between the SNMF baseline and the trained model? Is it second-order weight statistics, training dynamics, or a fundamentally different factorization the model discovers?
- How sensitive are these conclusions to the embedding dimension (1000) and the task count (100) — would the M-shortcut explanation still dominate at, say, 1M features in 1024-dim?
- Does the same "naive + offset" mechanism appear in real LLMs when superposition is suspected on toy analogs?
