Jacobian Scopes

Token-level causal attributions in LLMs through a single vector–Jacobian product: one backward pass yields three complementary scopes.

01

Key Finding

A single vector–Jacobian product v⊺Jt unifies gradient attribution. Three choices of v give Semantic, Fisher, and Temperature Scopes — computed together in one backward pass.

02

Key Metric

Fisher Scope reaches −1.81 AOPC on Gemma-3 4B LAMBADA, exceeding Integrated Gradients (−1.43). It wins 5/6 LAMBADA and 4/6 IWSLT2017 setups despite being target-free.

03

Why This Matters

Target-free distributional attribution is often more faithful than logit-wise methods. It works when the correct next token is ambiguous — translation, open-ended generation, and time-series ICL.

04

Critical Edge

First-order and architecture-blind; zero-vector ablation is aggressive; Temperature Scope assumes near-Gaussian outputs. Case studies are illustrative and lack controlled counterfactuals.

The Three Scopes

Semantic Scope

v = wtarget — attributes the logit of a chosen target token. Target-aware, per-logit explanation.

Fisher Scope

v = u1, the principal eigenvector of the Fisher information matrix. Captures the direction that maximizes KL-change in the full output distribution.

Temperature Scope

v = ŷ, the normalized hidden state. Attributes effective inverse temperature βeff = ‖y‖2; under a quadratic-logit assumption, equivalent to attributing predictive variance.

Case Studies

  • Bias: LLaMA-3.2 attributes the prediction "liberal" to the token "Columbia" and "conservative" to "the South".
  • Translation: Fisher Scope on Dante's Inferno shows word-level attribution when syntax aligns, and phrase-level spread when it does not.
  • Time-series ICL: Temperature Scope on Lorenz-like chaos supports the "nearest-neighbor in delayed-embedding space" conjecture.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The unifying v⊺Jt framing and Fisher-direction projection are genuine conceptual advances.
  • Rigor: AOPC is interventional and well-reported, but SHAP/ALTI/causal tracing are missing.
  • Limitations: Zero-embedding ablation is strong; bias claims lack controlled counterfactuals; Temperature Scope is limited to near-Gaussian outputs.

One-Line Takeaway

Jacobian Scopes reframes LLM attribution as a single, cheap vector–Jacobian product and shows that target-free, distributional explanations can be more faithful than target-specific ones — but the method remains a local, architecture-blind lens that needs to be paired with mechanistic probes for full causal understanding.
