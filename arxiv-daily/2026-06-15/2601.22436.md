> **一句话总结：** 本文首次用受控因果干预系统性地研究自进化 LLM 智能体的"经验忠实性",发现一个稳定的不对称:智能体始终因果依赖**原始轨迹**,却常常忽视或误解**压缩经验**——即使压缩经验是唯一输入;并从内容、模型、任务三方面诊断,根因在于压缩内容的语义局限、骨干模型的内部处理偏差、以及任务中预训练先验已足够。

> **TL;DR:** This ICML 2026 paper formalizes **experience faithfulness** — the *causal* dependence of a self-evolving LLM agent's behavior on the experience it is given — and shows, through controlled interventions across 4 representative self-evolving frameworks (ExpeL, Dynamic CheatSheet, ReasoningBank, G-Memory), 10 LLM backbones (GPT-4o/4o-mini, GPT-5.2, Gemini-2.5-Flash, Gemini-3-Pro, Claude-Sonnet-4.6, and the Qwen3 family 1.7B→235B-A22B MoE), and 9 environments (HotpotQA, FEVER, GPQA-Diamond, MMLU-Pro Eng., AIME 2024, Game of 24, ALFWorld, WebArena, WebShop), that **agents are highly faithful to raw experience but largely unfaithful to condensed experience**, even when condensed is the only input. The authors trace the gap to (1) generic/vague condensed content that misleads, (2) internal attention biases that suppress retrieved memory, and (3) tasks where pretrained priors already suffice. The conclusion: condensed-experience mechanisms in today's self-evolving agents are doing far less work than they appear to.

## 核心贡献

- **概念贡献:** 首次将"经验忠实性"形式化为可测量的因果属性,超越"性能是否提升"的关联性评估,直接问"性能提升是否因果地归因于检索到的经验"。
- **方法贡献:** 设计了一套针对**原始经验**和**压缩经验**的受控因果干预方案(原始经验: Empty / Shuffle / Irrelevant;压缩经验: Empty / Corrupt / Irrelevant / Filler),并通过 Integrated Gradients 进行层级的归因分析。
- **大规模实证:** 4 个代表性自进化框架 × 10 个 LLM 骨干 × 9 个环境,涵盖离线/在线、单智能体/多智能体、有/无原始经验等多种配置。
- **诊断贡献:** 把压缩经验无忠实性的原因拆解为内容、模型、任务三层级联因素,并给出可操作的工程建议(更好的压缩设计、动态经验注入、按需触发)。
- **开源:** 提供代码 https://github.com/Dreamcatcher0622/Faithfulness,方便复现和后续工作。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalization of experience faithfulness** as a distinct, causally-grounded property: an agent is faithful iff perturbations of its input experience produce predictable, corresponding changes in its behavior.
- **Causal-intervention toolkit** for both raw (Empty / Shuffle / Irrelevant) and condensed (Empty / Corrupt / Irrelevant / Filler) experience, carefully designed to preserve surface format while removing/changing content.
- **Largest empirical study to date** of self-evolving LLM-agent faithfulness: 4 frameworks × 10 backbones × 9 environments, including offline/online, single/multi-agent, and the Qwen3 dense+MoE scale sweep (1.7B → 235B-A22B).
- **Diagnostic decomposition** attributing condensed-experience unfaithfulness to (i) the semantic content of condensed memory itself, (ii) the backbone's internal attention/processing biases, and (iii) the task structure (knowledge-intensive tasks make experience redundant).
- **Three error modes** empirically identified when condensed experience is *added* (Distraction, Overreliance on incorrect priors, Premature inference), giving a vocabulary for downstream diagnosis.
- **Actionable design implications:** (a) condense for *behavioral utility* not surface brevity; (b) inject experience *dynamically and on demand*, not via static prepending; (c) accept that some tasks need no external experience.

## Methodology

### Operationalization of faithfulness

An agent is judged **faithful** to a form of experience (raw or condensed) if a controlled perturbation of that experience causes a corresponding, predictable change in the agent's downstream behavior. This is a stricter criterion than "did the agent get a higher score with experience?": it demands a *causal* link, not just a *correlational* one.

### Frameworks evaluated (4)

| Framework | Setting | Provides raw? | Provides condensed? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ExpeL** (Zhao et al., 2024a) | Offline, single-agent | Yes (retrieved trajectories) | Yes (distilled insights) |
| **Dynamic CheatSheet** (Suzgun et al., 2025) | Online, single-agent | Yes | Yes |
| **ReasoningBank** (Ouyang et al., 2025) | Online, single-agent | **No (condensed-only)** | Yes |
| **G-Memory** (Zhang et al., 2025a) | Online, multi-agent | Yes (Reference + Execution) | Yes |

In all four, the LLM backbone is **frozen** — adaptation comes solely from external experience, making them ideal for isolating experience-faithfulness effects.

### Backbones (10)

Closed-source: GPT-4o, GPT-4o-mini, GPT-5.2, Gemini-2.5-Flash, Gemini-3-Pro, Claude-Sonnet-4.6. Open-weight: Qwen3 dense (1.7B, 4B, 8B, 14B, 32B), Qwen3-30B-A3B MoE, and Qwen3-235B-A22B MoE. The Qwen3 sweep enables a clean scale analysis (§4.4).

### Environments (9, in 4 domains)

- **Knowledge-intensive QA:** HotpotQA, FEVER, GPQA-Diamond, MMLU-Pro Eng.
- **Mathematical reasoning:** AIME 2024, Game of 24
- **Embodied action:** ALFWorld
- **Web interaction:** WebArena (Shopping/CMS/Reddit/Map), WebShop

### Interventions

- **Raw experience:** Empty (remove semantic content, keep format), Shuffle (preserve tokens, break temporal/causal order), Irrelevant (replace with trajectories from unrelated tasks).
- **Condensed experience:** Empty, **Corrupt** (distort key components), Irrelevant (task-agnostic content), **Filler** (replace content with non-semantic tokens like "%$#&", preserving surface structure).
- The deliberate preservation of surface formatting/structure in every intervention is a methodological strength — it controls for the possibility that the agent is just responding to the *presence* of a text block rather than its *content*.

### Mechanistic diagnosis (Section 5)

1. **Content-side error analysis** (Table 1): manually categorize cases where the agent succeeds *without* condensed experience but fails *with* it. Three modes emerge: Distraction, Overreliance on incorrect priors, Premature inference.
2. **Model-side attribution** (Figure 7): apply **Integrated Gradients (IG)** across all layers/heads, segmenting each prompt into System Instruction / Condensed Experience / Raw Experience / Current Trajectory, to localize *where* condensed experience is being suppressed.
3. **Task-side analysis** (Table 2): evaluate on multi-hop QA (2WikiMultiHopQA, Musique) where pretrained knowledge is rich, to test the "priors suffice" hypothesis.

## Results & Highlights

### Main finding: raw vs. condensed asymmetry

Across ExpeL (offline) and Dynamic CheatSheet (online), interventions on **raw experience** reliably produce large performance swings (e.g., on ALFWorld, removing raw experience drops ExpeL from 75 → ~33%), while interventions on **condensed experience** yield performance **statistically indistinguishable from the unperturbed baseline** in most tasks. The same pattern holds for GPT-4o, Qwen3-235B-A22B, and Claude-Sonnet-4.6 — the asymmetry is not a model-specific quirk.

### ReasoningBank: condensed-only setting

ReasoningBank provides **no** raw experience, so any utility must come from condensed summaries. Removing condensed experience does degrade performance (so it *helps*), but the agent's behavior is "**surprisingly insensitive to semantic perturbations**": Corrupt, Irrelevant, and even Filler interventions produce only negligible degradation, and sometimes slight *improvements*. This implies that the gain comes from surface features (a text block, stylistic patterns) rather than genuine semantic grounding.

### Multi-agent (G-Memory) confirms the asymmetry

G-Memory uses two raw sources (Reference Raw + Execution Raw) and one condensed source. Both raw sources are faithfully utilized (large drops on perturbation), while condensed perturbations are again nearly inert on ALFWorld. The pattern replicates on GPT-4o-mini, GPT-5.2, and Qwen3-235B-A22B.

### Scale does not close the gap (§4.4)

Across Qwen3-1.7B → Qwen3-32B in ExpeL, larger models achieve higher unperturbed accuracy, but the **shape** of the raw-vs-condensed intervention curves stays the same. Notably, ExpeL's condensed experience is **generated by the backbone itself**, so larger models *should* produce richer summaries — yet the gap persists, suggesting the problem is not just low-quality summaries but a more fundamental processing limitation.

### Task structure modulates sensitivity

In **knowledge-intensive tasks** (HotpotQA, GPQA-Diamond, MMLU-Pro Eng., FEVER), the raw–condensed gap narrows — but for an unflattering reason: *neither* form is faithfully used. The agent's pretrained semantic priors already solve the task, so retrieved experience has no marginal value and exerts no causal influence. Confirmed on 2WikiMultiHopQA and Musique, which are also knowledge-heavy multi-hop QA.

### Mechanistic diagnosis (§5)

- **Content (Table 1):** Errors from condensed experience decompose into Distraction (e.g., 45.2% on Shopping with Gemini-2.5-Flash; 79.2% with Qwen3-32B), Overreliance on incorrect priors, and Premature inference.
- **Model (Figure 7):** Integrated Gradients on Qwen3-32B show that the **condensed-experience segment receives consistently low IG attribution across all layers** in baseline, Corrupt, and Irrelevant conditions. Raw experience maintains a moderate, stable contribution; the current trajectory dominates later layers, indicating a strong **local-context bias** that suppresses retrieved memory.
- **Task:** Multi-hop QA replication reinforces the priors-suffice hypothesis.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The conceptual move from "did the agent do better with experience?" to "is the agent's behavior *causally grounded* in experience?" is exactly what the self-evolving-agent literature has been missing. The four-way contrast (raw vs. condensed) × (perturbation type) is clean and revealing.
- **Rigor:** Strong overall. Key strengths: (a) **causal interventions** rather than correlational comparisons, (b) **careful control of surface format** in every intervention (e.g., Filler preserves text-block structure), (c) **mechanistic backup** via Integrated Gradients layer-wise attribution, and (d) the deliberate **condensed-only** experiment in ReasoningBank isolates the effect from raw-overshadowing. Weaknesses to watch:
  - The 4 frameworks, while "representative," may not be exhaustive — e.g., they exclude memory systems based on dense retrieval, learned summarizers, or RL-based memory curation. Whether the asymmetry holds in those designs is open.
  - Some perturbations (especially Filler with special-character tokens) might be *detected* by the model as obviously out-of-distribution and trigger a fallback to prior-driven behavior, which could inflate the "unfaithful" reading. The paper does not directly rule this out.
  - The 9 environments do not include long-horizon code-generation or interactive tool-use agents, where condensed experience might be most valuable.
  - Integrated Gradients attribution is a useful but imperfect proxy for "use"; a low IG score does not strictly mean the model never uses the segment, only that it has limited marginal influence on the predicted token.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Public code, explicit intervention definitions, official framework configurations, multiple backbones. The main reproducibility risk is API drift on closed-source models (GPT-4o/5.2, Gemini) and the cost of running the full Qwen3-235B-A22B sweep.
- **Significance:** High. If condensed experience is systematically underused, then much of the recent optimism about "self-evolving agents learning from lessons" deserves a reality check. The paper is a useful corrective.
- **Red flags / overclaiming risks:**
  - The "prior-priors-suffice" explanation is a reasonable post-hoc account but not strongly falsified by the paper. It is consistent with the data but alternative explanations (e.g., the backbone's instruction-following training *suppresses* retrieved memory in favor of "thinking aloud") are not ruled out.
  - The "Filler = no behavioral change" result is striking but is a single piece of evidence — it would benefit from a control where the filler text has known *but spurious* semantic content (e.g., a fluent but wrong summary) to confirm the model is indeed using only surface cues.
  - The asymmetry is presented as universal, but knowledge-intensive and embodied tasks behave differently; the framing should be "domain-dependent" rather than "categorical."

## Strengths

- **Causal framing**, not just correlational: the right level of analysis for a faithfulness question.
- **Intervention design** is the highlight: Empty / Shuffle / Irrelevant / Corrupt / Filler cleanly separate the contribution of content, order, and surface format.
- **ReasoningBank's condensed-only setting** is a particularly sharp test — without it, one could always explain the gap as "raw overshadows condensed."
- **Scale sweep** (1.7B → 235B-A22B) directly addresses the natural counter-argument that "bigger models will fix it."
- **Mechanistic backup** with Integrated Gradients turns a behavioral observation into a candidate mechanistic explanation.
- **Multi-agent extension** (G-Memory) shows the pattern survives the additional complexity of collaborative settings.
- **Clear actionable takeaways** for designers of self-evolving agents, with specific suggestions for content design and dynamic injection.

## Limitations

- 4 frameworks, while representative, are not exhaustive (e.g., no dense-retrieval-only memory, no learned summarizers, no RL-trained memory).
- 9 environments skew toward text-based and short-horizon tasks; long-horizon coding, tool-use, and embodied navigation are not tested.
- The mechanistic IG analysis is done with one backbone (Qwen3-32B) on one task (ALFWorld); whether the "low IG for condensed" pattern generalizes across more backbones/tasks is partly addressed in the appendix but is the paper's narrowest empirical leg.
- The three diagnostic causes (content / model / task) are presented as a hierarchy but their relative quantitative contributions are not separately estimated.
- Closed-source backbone results are subject to API/version drift; some experiments will not be exactly reproducible as APIs change.
- The "Filler" intervention's interpretation is delicate — out-of-distribution inputs may trigger fallback behaviors that look like unfaithfulness but are not.
- The paper does not propose or evaluate a *new* method that fixes the asymmetry; the design implications in §7 are guidance, not a solution.

## Relevance

直接对应本人对 **LLM Agent** 与 **记忆 / 经验机制** 的研究兴趣。本文用因果干预 + 层级归因的组合方法,精准揭穿了"压缩经验有用"这一被广泛默认的假设,并给出可在自身工作中复用的"经验忠实性"评估协议与诊断框架,强烈建议阅读 §3 (干预设计)、§4.1-4.2 (Raw vs. Condensed 实证) 和 §5.2 (IG 归因) 三节,以指导后续 Agent 记忆模块的设计与评估。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the raw-vs-condensed asymmetry persist in **memory systems with learned condensation** (e.g., a small model fine-tuned to produce task-specific, executable summaries), or is it specific to the LLM-self-distillation style used in ExpeL and ReasoningBank?
- Can the gap be closed by **structural interventions** (e.g., placing condensed experience *adjacent* to the current trajectory in the prompt, or interleaving raw and condensed) rather than content-level changes?
- How does **explicit faithfulness training** — e.g., RL with a faithfulness reward on top of the task reward — affect the asymmetry, and what does it cost in absolute task performance?
- For the "priors suffice" diagnosis: is the multi-hop QA effect a property of the *task family* or of the *backbone's instruction-tuning*? Cross-backbone ablations on the same task family would help disentangle.
- Could the **Filler intervention** be replaced with a "filler-pretrain" baseline where the model is first trained to ignore placeholder tokens, to confirm the "surface-cue" reading of the result?
- How does the asymmetry scale with **retrieval quality** (precision@k of the memory retriever)? If retrieval is perfect, does the gap shrink?
- Does the asymmetry hold under **adversarial** condensed experience (e.g., backdoored summaries that the agent faithfully follows, with the agent unaware)? A "faithfulness tax" might exist on both sides.
