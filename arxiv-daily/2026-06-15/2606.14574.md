> **一句话总结：** SIMMER 提出一个厨房领域的符号世界模型（77 动作、262 物体、约 46,800 种交互），通过状态机执行器揭示 LLM 规划中"执行不报错却悄悄破坏目标甚至不可逆"的潜在失败，并在 6 个 LLM 上证明即使前沿模型也只有不到 17% 的计划无错，最多 56% 含有潜在失败；反事实前瞻模拟可将潜在失败最多降低 72%。

> **TL;DR:** SIMMER is a benchmark for surfacing **latent failures** in LLM-generated plans — failures whose preconditions are satisfied, that run to completion, but silently corrupt the final state (e.g., cross-contamination from a reused unwashed cutting board) and are often irreversible. Built on a human-curated symbolic kitchen world model (77 actions, 262 objects, ~46,800 plausible interactions grounded in wikiHow/Instructables cooking scripts), SIMMER's state-machine executor runs plans step-by-step, tracking implicit state propagation (contamination, dirty surfaces, appliance state) and emitting a structured failure report distinguishing immediate, latent, and irreversible failures. Across six LLMs (GPT-5.4, Gemini 3 Flash, Claude Opus 4.6, Llama 3.3 70B, DeepSeek V3, Qwen 3.5 27B), fewer than 17% of plans are error-free (avg. 7.2%), 29–56% contain latent failures, and 20–45% contain irreversible ones. Prompting with counterfactual foresight simulation cuts latent failures by up to 72% and irreversible cases by up to 75%.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"潜在失败（latent failure）"作为独立评估维度：** 借鉴安全工程中 Reason (1990) 的 latent error 概念，区分"立即失败（前置条件违反 → 执行中断）"与"潜在失败（前置条件全部满足，但状态在动作间隐性传播，最终破坏目标甚至不可逆）"，弥补现有基准只看"能否完成"的盲区。
- **构建人类策划的符号世界模型：** 厨房领域，77 个动作 + 262 个物体 ≈ 46,800 种语义合理的交互；脚本来源于 wikiHow 与 Instructables，使用 GPT-5.4 抽取并经过大量人工核校、低频项归并、近义动作合并；规模远高于 TextWorld / VirtualHome / ALFWorld / BEHAVIOR。
- **设计两阶段状态机执行器：** Phase 1 逐步执行并显式传播"污染传播""脏污表面""电器状态"等隐式状态；Phase 2 执行后审计最终状态，识别"未烹饪即端上桌""炉灶忘关"等累积型失败。
- **手工分析失败根因：** 潜在失败三大根因——状态传播（53%）、隐式前置条件忽视（26.4%）、目标后疏漏（20.6%）；即时失败四大根因——具身基础缺陷、状态追踪错误、可供性违反、对象幻觉。
- **提出反事实前瞻模拟缓解方法：** 在生成每个动作前显式做"预测—自检—修正"三步，比 Self-Refine 的整体重审更细粒度，对前沿模型降幅更稳定。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes **latent failures** as a distinct evaluation category, drawing on safety-engineering theory (Reason, 1990), distinct from immediate precondition violations and from irreversible failures.
- Releases a **human-curated symbolic kitchen world model** (77 actions, 262 objects, ~46,800 plausible interactions) grounded in real cooking scripts from wikiHow and Instructables, with PDDL-style action/object definitions extended with kitchen-specific affordances.
- Implements a **two-phase state-machine executor**: step-by-step execution with implicit-state propagation + post-execution audit, distinguishing immediate, latent, and irreversible failures.
- Provides a **systematic error analysis** of latent failures (state propagation, implicit precondition ignorance, post-goal neglect) and immediate failures (embodiment grounding, state tracking, affordance violation, object hallucination).
- Proposes **counterfactual foresight simulation** as a prompting mitigation — predict state changes, self-check for hazards, then revise — reducing latent failures by up to 72% and irreversible cases by up to 75% on GPT-5.4 and Claude Opus 4.6.

## Methodology

**World model (PDDL-style).** Actions are tuples ⟨args, preconditions, effects⟩; objects are ⟨properties, states, location⟩ with properties being immutable affordances (e.g., `grabbable`, `heat source`) and states being mutable (e.g., `raw` → `cooked`). The model is built in three steps:

1. **Script collection** from wikiHow / Instructables, with GPT-5.4 extracting verbs (actions) and noun phrases (objects); optional steps explicitly excluded.
2. **Normalization** via rule-based formatting fixes (e.g., `switchon` → `switch on`) plus manual consolidation of semantic duplicates (e.g., `place on` → `put on`); items with frequency < 3 and readily available alternatives are pruned.
3. **Formal annotation** with two fully-annotated few-shot scripts guiding GPT-5.4 to generate initial action/object schemas; all outputs are manually reviewed and corrected.

**Plan format** follows VirtualHome: `[ACTION] <object class> (object id)`, with actions taking 1–2 typed arguments.

**Two-phase executor.**

- **Phase 1 (per-step):** parse and bind arguments → check preconditions against current state `(S, σ)` → apply effects → propagate implicit state. Example: when a raw-protein or dirty-vegetable object contacts a surface, that surface acquires a `contaminated` attribute; downstream contact then propagates contamination silently.
- **Phase 2 (post-execution audit):** scan final world state for unresolved hazards (e.g., food served while still contaminated, appliances left on). Latent failures are typically only flagged here because no individual step's preconditions were violated.

**Task dataset.** 100 cooking scripts covering all 77 actions and 262 objects, spanning 12 cooking techniques, with task complexity ranging 2–18 NL steps (avg. 26.6 actions / 31.5 objects per task).

**Models.** Six LLMs: GPT-5.4, Gemini 3 Flash, Claude Opus 4.6 (proprietary frontier); Llama 3.3 70B, DeepSeek V3, Qwen 3.5 27B (open-weight). Temperature T = 0.

**Mitigation prompts (open-loop, no env feedback):**

- **Self-Refine (Madaan et al., 2023):** baseline plan → structured checklist (state tracking, precondition, food safety, appliance safety, missing steps) → single-pass revised plan.
- **Counterfactual Foresight Simulation:** per action, (i) predict state changes, (ii) self-check for precondition violations / hazards, (iii) revise if unexpected consequences predicted.

## Results & Highlights

**Baseline failure rates (Table 3, 100 tasks; lower is better):**

| Model | Any-fail plans (%) | Immediate | Latent | Irreversible | Failures/plan total |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| GPT-5.4 | 87 | 74 | 56 | 24 | 2.86 |
| Gemini 3 Flash | 83 | 66 | 32 | 20 | 2.47 |
| Claude Opus 4.6 | 89 | 85 | 29 | 28 | 3.70 |
| Llama 3.3 70B | 100 | 99 | 52 | 45 | 6.53 |
| DeepSeek V3 | 99 | 96 | 48 | 36 | 6.54 |
| Qwen 3.5 27B | 99 | 99 | 46 | 34 | 6.15 |

- **<17% error-free plans** even for the best frontier model; average across all six is just **7.2%**.
- **Latent failures affect 29–56%** of plans; **20–45%** contain at least one irreversible failure.
- Frontier models average **2.12 immediate failures/plan**; open-weight models average **4.91**.

**Self-Refine (all 6 models):**

| Model | Any-fail | Immed | Latent | Irrev |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| GPT-5.4 | 65 | 63 | 14 | 14 |
| Gemini 3 Flash | 73 | 68 | 29 | 24 |
| Claude Opus 4.6 | 56 | 47 | 16 | 16 |
| Llama 3.3 70B | 99 | 98 | 46 | 45 |
| DeepSeek V3 | 98 | 97 | 42 | 40 |
| Qwen 3.5 27B | 99 | 99 | 39 | 39 |

- Self-Refine helps frontier models substantially (>40% reduction in failures/plan) but **marginal or negative** for weaker reasoning models — the authors hypothesize they lack the capacity to do reliable counterfactual simulation internally.

**Counterfactual Foresight Simulation (Figure 4, GPT-5.4 / Claude Opus 4.6):**

- GPT-5.4: immediate failures 1.75 → 0.47 (−73%); latent 1.11 → 0.40 (−64%); irreversible 0.55 → 0.30 (−45%).
- Claude Opus 4.6: immediate 2.64 → 0.54 (−80%); latent 1.06 → 0.26 (−75%); irreversible 1.03 → 0.26 (−75%).
- Foresight outperforms Self-Refine on immediate failures by a wide margin (73%/80% vs. 30%/28% reduction); comparable on latent failures.

**Qualitative example (Table 4).** For "Make a Sandwich with Bacon, Egg, and Cheese": the GPT-5.4 baseline handles raw bacon first, then later grabs bread with unwashed hands and forgets to turn off the stove. Foresight reorders the plan to handle ready-to-eat ingredients before raw protein and adds an explicit `SWITCH OFF` step before serving.

**Failure root-cause distribution (Figure 3):**

- Latent: state propagation 53.0% (avg), implicit precondition ignorance 26.4%, post-goal neglect 20.6%.
- Immediate: embodiment grounding deficiency 42.8%, affordance violation 27.5%, state tracking 22.7%, object hallucination 7.0%.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high).** Reframing latent failures as a first-class evaluation category — drawing on safety engineering — is a genuinely useful contribution. The plan-execute-replan paradigm's blind spot to silent state propagation is a real and undermeasured problem.
- **Rigor (mostly solid, some caveats).**
  - **Coverage claim.** The 46,800 interactions figure is reported as ~max possible (action × object combinatorial), not actual exercised interactions; the benchmark itself uses 100 curated tasks, which is small. The paper does not report inter-annotator agreement on the manual curation or on the failure categorization (Figure 3 is described as "manually analyzed").
  - **Executor soundness.** The state machine is presented informally (pseudocode deferred to Appendix B, not read here). It's unclear whether the world-model PDDL specifications have been formally verified or empirically stress-tested.
  - **Single domain.** Kitchen only. The paper does not show that latent failures generalize to other embodied domains (e.g., BEHAVIOR's 1,000 activities), which weakens any broader claim about LLM planning.
  - **Latent-failure taxonomy.** The three latent root-cause categories (state propagation, implicit precondition ignorance, post-goal neglect) are intuitive but the proportions may be sensitive to how the 100 tasks are constructed. Tasks that emphasize cross-contamination scenarios may inflate state propagation.
- **Reproducibility (medium).** Prompts, hyperparameters, and model versions are promised in Appendix C, but no code/data link yet, paper is under review, and several proprietary model names are recent (GPT-5.4, Gemini 3 Flash, Claude Opus 4.6, Qwen 3.5 27B, DeepSeek V3) — checkpoint stability is unclear.
- **Significance (high for the safety/planning community).** The headline number — 56% of plans contain latent failures and most are irreversible — is a strong argument that current LLM-as-planner evaluations have been substantially overoptimistic. The framing should influence how the community designs agent benchmarks.
- **Red flags / weak spots.**
  - The mitigation gains are reported only on the **two strongest** models (GPT-5.4, Claude Opus 4.6); on the four other models, Self-Refine is marginal-to-negative and Foresight isn't reported at all. This is a meaningful gap: if the mitigation only works where it's not needed most, the practical claim is weaker.
  - The reduction percentages are "up to" numbers; the average lift is smaller (e.g., Claude Opus's overall failures/plan drops from 3.70 → ~1.06 averaged across categories with foresight, not 75%).
  - Tasks are 2–18 NL steps; longer-horizon planning (which is where latent failures should compound) is under-represented.
  - The world model depends heavily on GPT-5.4 for action/object extraction and annotation — a model is used to build a benchmark for evaluating models, with manual correction afterwards. This bootstrapping is disclosed but may carry systematic biases from GPT-5.4's own kitchen knowledge.
  - "Fewer than 20% error-free plans" headline conflates the per-model best (GPT-5.4: 13% error-free, i.e., 87% with any failure) with the average (7.2% error-free).

## Strengths

- Clean conceptual reframing of latent vs. immediate vs. irreversible failures.
- The kitchen world model is concretely larger and more semantically grounded than prior benchmarks (Table 2: 46,800 vs. ≤2,346 interactions).
- Two-phase executor design (per-step propagation + post-execution audit) is practical and easy to extend.
- Foresight simulation is a lightweight, open-loop mitigation that needs no environment feedback — directly useful in deployment.
- Qualitative failure example (Table 4) is vivid and illustrates the framing concretely.

## Limitations

- **Single domain (kitchen).** Generalization beyond cooking is unverified; some failure modes (cross-contamination) are kitchen-specific.
- **Small task set (100 scripts).** Statistical claims about failure distributions are noisy; per-category percentages like "53% state propagation" come from manual coding of 100 plans.
- **Combinatorial-size claim is a ceiling, not realized coverage.** ~46,800 is the maximum combinatoric interaction count; the actual tested interaction space is much smaller.
- **Mitigation only validated on 2 of 6 models.** Foresight's promise for weaker models is unknown; for those models, Self-Refine can even hurt.
- **No code release yet** (under review).
- **World model bootstrapped by GPT-5.4**, with manual correction — possibly inherits GPT-5.4's kitchen priors.
- **Symbolic, no partial observability or physics.** Real embodied settings involve noisy perception, partial state, and continuous dynamics not modeled here.
- **Closed-ended evaluation.** Each plan is judged against a symbolic executor rather than executed in a high-fidelity simulator, which limits ecological validity.

## Relevance

该工作直接对应"LLM 智能体可执行规划"与"agent safety"两个核心方向：通过世界模型 + 状态机执行器系统性地暴露了当前 LLM 规划器在隐性状态传播上的盲区，并以"反事实前瞻模拟"这一轻量提示策略给出了可落地的缓解方案，对家庭/具身场景中部署 LLM 智能体的可信评估与设计具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the latent-failure prevalence and taxonomy generalize beyond the kitchen domain (e.g., assembly, navigation, multi-room household tasks)?
- Can the state-machine executor be coupled with a learned physics/neurosymbolic simulator to better reflect real-world messiness while keeping interpretability?
- Why does Self-Refine *hurt* weaker models — is it context-window stress, instruction-following failure, or insufficient reasoning depth? What is the failure mode of counterfactual foresight on those same models?
- How do tool-use / ReAct-style agents that observe environment feedback at each step compare on the same latent-failure taxonomy — does closed-loop execution actually catch these, or do they propagate through the observation channel too?
- Can the world model's PDDL-style schemas be automatically extended (or learned) from new scripts without losing the rigor of manual annotation?
- For tasks longer than 18 steps, does latent-failure rate compound super-linearly, and does counterfactual foresight still scale?
- How sensitive are the headline numbers to prompt design, especially the food-safety checklist used in Self-Refine?
- Could the latent-failure taxonomy be operationalized as a reward signal for fine-tuning or RLHF to directly train planners that avoid irreversible contamination?
