> **一句话总结：** 单作者论文提出"平流-晃动-跨等密度面"三分量海洋翻转环流分解框架,在三大洋盆(印度洋-太平洋、大西洋、南海)揭示出欧拉趋势与拉格朗日趋势可以指向截然相反的水团变化方向,并首次在观测约束数据集中显式诊断出深海跨等密度面下沉。
>
> **TL;DR:** A single-author study (Han, Xiamen University Malaysia) extends the author's earlier sloshing-MOC decomposition to a closed "advective = sloshing + diapycnal" identity in density coordinates, then applies it to ECCO (1°, 1992–2015) for the Indo-Pacific and Atlantic, and GLORYS12 (1/12°, 1993–2024) for the deep South China Sea. The headline result: Eulerian upwelling can coexist with diapycnal downwelling (Indo-Pacific abyss), Eulerian lightening can mask Lagrangian densification cells (Atlantic triple-cell), and Eulerian densification can mask net Lagrangian lightening (SCS). The Indo-Pacific case provides what the author claims is the first explicit diagnosis of interior diapycnal downwelling from an observationally-constrained product, with implications for the long-standing Munk abyssal-recipes paradox.

## 核心贡献

- **方法学贡献:** 将"平流 MOC = 晃动(sloshing) MOC + 跨等密度面(diapycnal) MOC"的分解推广到密度坐标系下的长时间趋势诊断,提出基于等密度面体积收支的耦合判据,给出欧拉趋势可逆性(可逆 / 不可逆)的显式度量。
- **理论贡献:** 在印度洋-太平洋深海,首次在观测约束再分析数据(ECCO v4r3)中显式诊断出夹在两个跨等密度上涌胞之间的"跨等密度下沉胞"(约 45.88–45.92 σ₄),为"底部强化湍流混合 → 跨等密度下沉"提供了独立于传统热量收支的诊断证据。
- **结构性发现:** 在大西洋识别出 AMOC 上支(<1000 m)的子翻转胞,推测与亚北极中层水(SAIW)形成有关;将经典 NADW 形成-下沉机制类比到上层海洋翻转路径。
- **跨海盆对比:** 系统比较三大洋盆中"相似的欧拉趋势源于不同的拉格朗日过程"的四种反直觉机制(欧拉上涌+跨等密度下沉、欧拉变轻+拉格朗日变重、欧拉变重+拉格朗日变轻、稳态分层)。

## Key Contributions

- **Methodological:** Recasts the Han (2021, 2023a) sloshing-MOC framework as a closed density-space decomposition `ψ_diapy = ψ_Euler − ψ_slosh`, with explicit boundary conditions from advective streamfunction; provides a unified volume-budget criterion for reversibility (Eq. 6 in the paper).
- **Theoretical:** First explicit diagnosis of an interior diapycnal downwelling cell in the Indo-Pacific (~45.88–45.92 σ₄) from an observationally-constrained reanalysis (ECCO), offering a possible alternative resolution to Munk's abyssal-recipes mass-balance paradox without invoking bottom-boundary-layer upslope transport.
- **Structural:** Identifies a previously unrecognized sub-overturning cell in the upper limb of the AMOC (35.50–36.30 σ₂, north of 45°N), hypothesized to be dynamically analogous to the NADW-cell but driven by intermediate-water formation (e.g., SAIW).
- **Cross-basin synthesis:** Three contrasting regimes (Indo-Pacific, Atlantic, SCS) all exhibit "Eulerian masks Lagrangian" behavior with opposite signs, providing a concrete dynamical basis for separating reversible adiabatic adjustment from irreversible diabatic transformation in climate-scale ocean signals.

## Methodology

- **Data:** Two reanalyses are used. ECCO v4r3 (1°, 50 levels, 1992–2015, monthly; advective uses the *residual* streamfunction including parameterized bolus velocity) for the Atlantic and Indo-Pacific; GLORYS12v1 (1/12°, 50 levels, 1993–2024, monthly; Eulerian streamfunction suffices because eddies are largely resolved) for the deep South China Sea (SCS). The two-product design matches each basin's resolution needs: coarse 1° is inadequate for the ~12°×12° SCS, and 1/12° is unnecessary for basin-scale MOC work.
- **Streamfunctions:**
  - Advective MOC `ψ_adv(y, σ, t)`: vertical integration of zonally-integrated meridional velocity over densities denser than σ, in σ-space.
  - Sloshing MOC `ψ_slosh(y, σ, t)`: from the temporal evolution of isopycnal depth `w_iso = [h(σ,m) − h(σ,m−1)]/T`, integrated southward from a northern boundary condition set equal to `ψ_adv` (the boundary condition is required to ensure the diapycnal component is well-defined — solid boundaries give zero, open boundaries must use `ψ_adv`).
  - Diapycnal MOC `ψ_diapy = ψ_adv − ψ_slosh` (Eq. 5): positive = Lagrangian lightening, negative = Lagrangian densification.
- **Volume-budget framing (Eq. 6, Figure 1):** `ψ_diapy = ψ_adv(y₁) − ψ_adv(y₀) − T_hv`, where `T_hv` is the heaving transport. This shows `ψ_diapy ≠ Δψ_adv` in general — the advective transport convergence alone is *not* the diapycnal transport, a distinction from the Monkman & Jansen (2024) volume-budget interpretation.
- **Validation:** The framework is checked by correlating the advective and sloshing MOC time series at the overturning-maximum location in each basin (R = 0.81–0.92 at 1-yr and 5-yr windows, detrended). Because the two indices come from physically distinct fields (meridional velocity vs. density), high correlation is interpreted as a dynamical-consistency check.
- **Trend estimation:** Long-term tendencies are taken as time-means over the reanalysis periods; no formal trend-fitting is performed. The author is explicit that the "sloshing" term, in the trend context, denotes a monotonic isopycnal shift rather than oscillatory seiche-like behavior.

## Results & Highlights

- **Indo-Pacific (ECCO, σ₄ coordinate):** Time-mean diapycnal MOC shows a *triple-cell* structure: upper upwelling (45.00–45.88 σ₄, coincident with the Rogers et al. 2023 "middepth" Indo-Pacific upwelling at 1,000–3,000 m), an *intermediate downwelling cell* (45.88–45.92 σ₄, ~3,500–4,500 m) — flagged as the first such explicit diagnosis — and a lower upwelling cell reflecting AABW consumption by geothermal/bottom heating. The northern basin is nearly adiabatic (`ψ_diapy ≈ 0`).
- **Atlantic (ECCO, σ₂ coordinate):** Despite a contrasting Eulerian background of *basin-wide lightening* (vs. Indo-Pacific densification), the Lagrangian structure is qualitatively similar (triple-cell) but with a weaker intermediate diapycnal downwelling. A middepth upwelling cell near 36.80 σ₂ is dominated by isopycnal heaving (`ψ_diapy ≈ −T_hv`); a sub-overturning cell in the upper 1,000 m of the AMOC is attributed (mechanistically, not rigorously proven) to SAIW formation.
- **South China Sea (GLORYS, σ₂):** A regime in which Eulerian densification *masks* Lagrangian lightening. Strong coupling (R = 0.81–0.88) between advective and sloshing indices indicates that deep Eulerian densification is dominated by adiabatic isopycnal heaving in response to Pacific inflow through the Luzon Strait; the net Lagrangian signal is lightening south of 17°N. The author notes ~2.6% of sloshing indices are MAD outliers and removes them.
- **Cross-basin summary (Table 1):** Three of the four theoretical regimes in Figure 1 are realized in observations: lightening+densification (Indo-Pacific), lightening+lightening (Atlantic), densification+lightening (SCS). The fourth (steady stratification) is not observed in any product, as expected for transient reanalysis states; a 4,000-yr idealized run (cited as "in preparation") approaches this limit.
- **Quantitative headroom:** Magnitudes are reported in Sv (1 Sv = 10⁶ m³/s); the Indo-Pacific intermediate diapycnal downwelling is identified as ~2.5 Sv from the decomposition (cross-validated against Monkman & Jansen 2024's 14.5 Sv transport convergence − 17.0 Sv volume tendency = −2.5 Sv). No uncertainty quantification beyond inter-dataset consistency checks.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Single author:** Lei Han, China-ASEAN College of Marine Sciences, Xiamen University Malaysia, and College of Ocean and Earth Sciences, Xiamen University. The paper is built on a substantial body of prior work by the same author (Han 2021, 2023a, 2023b, 2025), which originally introduced the sloshing-MOC decomposition.
- **Track record on the core framework:** Han's earlier "sloshing" papers predicted AMOC stability that was later corroborated by Volkov et al. (2024)'s correction to a spurious RAPID-array weakening trend. This gives the framework genuine empirical credibility in the field.
- **Funding:** NSFC 42476006 and XMUMRF/2024-C14/ICAM/0017. No commercial or industrial funding.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: Moderate-to-high.** The conceptual framework is an extension of the author's own prior work and is closely related to the simultaneous Monkman & Jansen (2024) volume-budget approach. The novel empirical content is the multi-basin application and the explicit diagnosis of intermediate diapycnal downwelling; the latter is the paper's most concrete contribution to abyssal circulation theory.
- **Rigor: Mixed.**
  - Strong: the volume-budget derivation is clean, the boundary-condition treatment for open meridional boundaries is correctly handled, and the dynamical-consistency check via velocity-vs-density correlation is convincing.
  - Weak: the work is fundamentally a diagnostic reanalysis study. No uncertainty quantification (no ensemble spread, no formal error bars). The "long-term tendency" is the time-mean over 24–32 years rather than a fitted trend, which conflates trend with mean state. Boundary-condition choices (e.g., integrating sloshing from a solid boundary in SCS, from `ψ_adv` at 60°N in Atlantic) are pragmatic rather than rigorously justified.
  - The claim that the Indo-Pacific intermediate cell is the *first* explicit diagnosis is plausible given the cited literature, but the 2.5 Sv magnitude is below the inter-method differences typically seen in such budgets, and the sign is sensitive to the linear interpolation used to estimate `h(σ, m)`.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate.** Datasets (ECCO, GLORYS) are public. The framework is described in equations and could be reproduced, but the paper does not release code. The author notes an idealized 4,000-yr model solution that supports the framework's equilibrium limit but is in a "manuscript in preparation" — readers cannot yet verify the steady-stratification claim.
- **Significance: Real but narrow.** The framework is genuinely useful for interpreting long-term ocean signals, and the connection to the Munk abyssal-recipes paradox is substantive. The "Eulerian masks Lagrangian" framing is a useful conceptual clarification. However, the paper does not change the IPCC AR6 AMOC-decline assessment (in fact, the discussion positions the work as a *diagnostic refinement* of climate signals, not a substantive revision of any climate projection).
- **Red flags:**
  - Reliance on a single reanalysis (ECCO) per open-ocean basin. ECCO is known to underestimate deep diapycnal diffusivity (Trossman et al. 2022), and the author acknowledges this may bias the diagnosed diapycnal downwelling *upward* — a self-undermining acknowledgment that should be taken seriously.
  - The "first explicit diagnosis" claim is partially undercut by the framing as a diagnostic decomposition rather than an independent observational estimate.
  - The SAIW-attribution for the AMOC upper-limb sub-cell is presented as "plausible" rather than tested; with no water-mass tracer analysis it remains a hypothesis.
  - The MAD-outlier treatment for SCS sloshing indices (~2.6%) is a standard procedure but its sensitivity is not explored.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean decomposition with a clear physical interpretation of each component (Eulerian volume transport, adiabatic isopycnal heaving, irreversible diapycnal transformation).
- Multi-basin, multi-product design that demonstrates the framework's generality across very different dynamical regimes (open-ocean abyss, marginal sea, contrasting Eulerian backgrounds).
- Explicit treatment of the diapycnal-downwelling cell in the Indo-Pacific, with cross-validation against the independent Monkman & Jansen (2024) volume budget (different method, same qualitative structure).
- Clear distinction between the proposed interpretation and the Monkman & Jansen (2024) interpretation of the same numerical quantities — a careful and intellectually honest piece of framework-comparison work.
- Useful discussion linking the framework to the IPCC AR6 AMOC decline assessment, positioning the work as a diagnostic tool for future climate-signal interpretation.

## Limitations

- Quantitative uncertainties are not propagated. The 2.5 Sv Indo-Pacific diapycnal downwelling signal is small relative to typical reanalysis-product spread, and a sensitivity study across multiple reanalyses (ORAS5, SODA, GLORYS for the Indo-Pacific) is not performed.
- "Long-term tendency" is operationalized as a 24–32 year time-mean rather than a regression-based trend; this conflates variability with secular change and likely over-states the monotonicity of the diagnosed Eulerian signals.
- The framework is currently restricted to model-based products because it requires velocity fields. Pure observational application (e.g., using geostrophic velocities from hydrography alone) is not demonstrated, which limits its near-term use in the "data-sparse" deep ocean the paper motivates.
- Linear interpolation of isopycnal depths is acknowledged as the main methodological uncertainty but no sensitivity test (e.g., monotonic cubic interpolation) is reported.
- The Atlantic sub-overturning cell attribution to SAIW is mechanistic speculation, not tested. The 4,000-yr equilibrium simulation is cited but not shown.
- The 1° ECCO resolution cannot resolve the ~0.2°-scale bottom-boundary-layer upwelling that McDougall & Ferrari (2017) propose; the author correctly notes this but does not quantify how much of the "interior" diapycnal signal could be aliased bottom-boundary-layer upwelling.

## Relevance (English)

> This paper falls outside the user's primary interests (LLM training/inference reliability, interpretability, self-evolving agents). It is a physical-oceanography diagnostic study. The only tangential connection is the framework's conceptual lesson that a monotonic-looking surface signal (Eulerian trend) can mask the opposite underlying process (Lagrangian transformation) — a structural analogue to the "metric gaming" problem in ML evaluation. Recommended only as background reading, not deep study.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How sensitive is the diagnosed Indo-Pacific intermediate diapycnal downwelling cell to the choice of reanalysis? A multi-product intercomparison (ECCO + ORAS5 + SODA + GLORYS over a common period) would bound the 2.5 Sv estimate.
- Can the framework be applied using only observational hydrography + a geostrophic velocity estimate (e.g., from Argo + satellite altimetry), avoiding the model-dependence that currently limits the framework to data-assimilating products?
- Does the AMOC upper-limb sub-overturning cell survive in an ensemble of CMIP6 historical simulations, and is its variability correlated with SAIW-formation indices (e.g., the Labrador Sea Water / Nordic Seas overflow proxies)?
- The author mentions a 4,000-yr idealized simulation approaching the steady-stratification limit. What isopycnal depth drift and what diapycnal diffusivity profile reproduce this limit, and how do they compare to observational microstructure-based estimates?
- How does the framework's Eulerian/Lagrangian decomposition perform when applied to high-frequency (daily rather than monthly) output, where the `w_iso` finite-difference approximation becomes more accurate but `T = 30 days` is no longer the natural averaging window?
- Is there a corresponding "interior upwelling that masks diapycnal downwelling" regime in a long pre-industrial control simulation of a coupled climate model — the equilibrium counterpart to the Indo-Pacific transient state?
