SuperThoughts: Reasoning Tokens in Superposition

Compress consecutive CoT token pairs into a single latent vector, decode two tokens per forward pass with a lightweight MTP head, and fall back to standard decoding when confidence drops — yielding shorter reasoning chains without collapsing accuracy.

arXiv: 2606.13862 June 15, 2026 Qwen2.5-Math 1.5B / 7B / 14B MATH500 · OlympiadBench · AMC23 · GPQA-Diamond
Key Finding

Two Tokens, One Vector

  • A learned Compressor fuses each adjacent CoT pair into one H-dim latent.
  • The Main module predicts the odd token; a tiny MTP module predicts the even token.
  • Confidence-based fallback reverts to single-token decoding whenever MTP is uncertain.
Key Metric

Wall-Clock Payoff

21–28%

wall-clock time saved

From 20–35% shorter CoT chains, at ≤2 pt accuracy drop on math benchmarks. Savings scale with model size as MTP/Compressor overhead amortizes.

Why This Matters

Cheaper Long CoT

  • Long chain-of-thought is expensive to serve; SuperThoughts cuts real generation time, not just token counts.
  • Strictly Pareto-dominates HAMburger: higher accuracy at every compression level.
  • Works as a bolt-on to existing CoT models with discrete supervision preserved.
Critical Edge

Small Models Need Guardrails

  • Uniform 2-token superposition loses 14–21 pts on 1.5B; adaptive τ is essential.
  • Threshold τ must be tuned per workload; no automatic calibration is given.
  • Quantitative claims are mostly math/science; code, chat, and tool-use are untested.

Architecture

Compressor → Main → MTP

  • Compressor: linear 2H→H projection or 1-layer Transformer over the token pair.
  • Main module: frozen/Qwen2.5 backbone consumes the latent and predicts the next odd token.
  • MTP module: 1-layer transformer + 3H→H projection over even-token embedding, odd-token embedding, and Main hidden state.
  • Both heads share the original LM head.

Training

Two-Stage Stabilization

  • Stage 1: Smooth-L1 hidden-state distillation aligns the Compressor to the pretrained manifold.
  • Stage 2: Freeze Compressor+Main, warm up MTP; then joint end-to-end fine-tuning.
  • Token-level cross-entropy is preserved throughout — unlike pure latent-reasoning methods.

Adaptive Inference

Confidence Scheduler

Keep MTP output iff pMTPi ≥ τ; otherwise discard and let Main re-predict the token next step. τ traces the accuracy-vs-length Pareto curve.

Swept τ values: 0.999, 0.9999, 0.99999 (and denser grid for HAMburger comparison).

Scale Effect

Bigger Models Tolerate More Compression

Uniform superposition (no fallback) loses 14–21 pts on 1.5B but only 5.6–12.1 pts on 7B. The authors suggest k=3 or higher superposition ratios may become viable at larger scales.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Clean composition of multi-token prediction + context compression + confidence fallback. Stage-1 distillation to the pretrained manifold is the most interesting technical move.
  • Rigor: Pareto curves, wall-clock measurements in nano-vLLM, and apples-to-apples HAMburger comparison.
  • Red flags: No comparison to Medusa / EAGLE / speculative decoding at matched accuracy; τ tuning is manual; domain coverage narrow.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Pareto-style evaluation + real wall-clock numbers.
  • Honest reporting of small-model failures.
  • Simple, cheap add-on architecture.
  • Discrete supervision keeps training stable.

Limitations

  • Fixed 2-token ratio; k>3 not demonstrated.
  • Domain narrowness (math + thin science slice).
  • Overhead gap on small models (~9 pts).
  • Code/checkpoint release status unstated.

Open Questions

  • Head-to-head comparison with Medusa / EAGLE / self-speculative decoding at matched accuracy?
  • Can the superposition ratio itself be learned and adaptive (2×, 1×, NTP) end-to-end?
  • Transfer to code generation, agentic tool use, or long-context summarization?
  • Cost-quality frontier on backbones with native MTP heads (Qwen3-Next, MiMo)?
  • Why does increasing τ sometimes hurt accuracy — miscalibration or training-data artifact?

Relevance

Directly addresses the FLOPs and wall-clock cost of long CoT reasoning. Highly relevant to efficient inference systems, latent/continuous-space reasoning, and CoT compression research.

The Pareto evaluation style and strict HAMburger comparison are useful templates for benchmarking any new inference-acceleration method.
