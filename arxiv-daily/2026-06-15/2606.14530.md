> **一句话总结：** 在 Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 上以同一套残差化工具研究代码正确性在隐藏态中的可读性——预生成正确性可从 prompt 末尾隐藏态线性解码（nested-CV 50 次外层 split 的 leakage-free AUC 0.931±0.008，控制 prompt 长度后仍 0.911），但 failed→repair 的对比方向在控制三个修复上下文协变量后消失，因此应被读作"修复上下文的伴生物"而非孤立的"修复理解"特征。

> **TL;DR:** A single-author probing study on Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 (a 36-layer, 2560-dim decoder-only LLM) on 444 LiveCodeBench tasks. Using one residualization tool for two questions, the paper shows: (1) **Pre-generation** — pass/fail of the first-attempt code is linearly decodable from the prompt-final hidden state with leakage-free outer-test AUC 0.931 ± 0.008 over 50 outer splits; after residualizing each of the 2560 hidden dims against prompt_tokens, the probe still hits 0.911 ± 0.010 vs a prompt-length-only baseline of 0.754 ± 0.014 (replicated with random forest / gradient boosting / MLP, none beat 0.754). The probe layer is layer 29, chosen by 5-fold nested CV inside 50 outer 80/20 splits, modal in 25/50 selections (layer 30 picked 24/50, others 1/50). (2) **Repair geometry** — on 236 cleaned 0→1 transitions, the mean(success) − mean(failure) direction has magnitude 9.76 and split-half cosine 0.767, both p < 0.001 against 1000 label-shuffled nulls. But after conditional residualization against three covariates that differ between groups (∆prompt_tokens, code_length_attempt_0, failure_type of attempt 0), the magnitude falls to 3.72 (p = 0.126) and the split-half cosine to 0.140 (p = 0.942). Net: positive pre-generation result, paired negative result on the repair side, both produced by the same diagnostic.

## 核心贡献

- **预生成正确性可线性解码：** 在 444 个 LiveCodeBench 任务上，Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 的 prompt 末尾隐藏态可在生成任何 token 之前以 0.931±0.008 的 leakage-free 外层 AUC 预测首轮代码正确性；按维度残差化掉 prompt 长度后仍达 0.911±0.010，远高于 prompt-only 基线 0.754±0.014。
- **稳健的基线对比：** prompt-only 基线在 logistic / random forest / gradient boosting / MLP 四种模型下都不超过 0.754，证明残差化探针的领先不是基线选用线性模型造成的。
- **诚实的方法学示范：** 同一套 residualization 工具在两组实验上各得出一个结论——预生成部分被残差化保留下来（正结果），修复几何部分被条件残差化推翻（负结果）。
- **修复方向条件残差化：** 对 236 条被清洗的 0→1 修复轨迹，failed→repair 的 delta 存在显著对比方向（magnitude 9.76, split-half cosine 0.767，p<0.001 vs 1000 次 label shuffle null），但对 ∆prompt_tokens、code_length_attempt_0、failure_type 三个组间分布不同的协变量做 OLS 残差化后，magnitude 跌至 3.72（p=0.126）、cosine 跌至 0.140（p=0.942）。
- **数据驱动的混杂检查协议：** 在做残差化之前先做 Welch t / χ² 检验确认协变量在成功/失败组间是否同分布，仅对确实不同的协变量做残差化——避免对已被对比构造天然抵消的协变量做无效或误导性的去除。
- **嵌套 CV 选层避免泄漏：** 50 次外层 80/20 split，层与正则强度 C 在外层训练折内通过 5 折 CV 联合选出，layer 29 / C=0.01 在 22/50 个外层 split 中被选为最佳，避免了 test-set argmax 选层带来的乐观偏差。
- **可复现且开源：** 模型、数据、脚本均在 https://github.com/CarloDiCicco/ReasoningLab 上发布，硬件为单卡 NVIDIA RTX 4050 (6 GB VRAM)。

## Key Contributions

- **Pre-generation correctness is linearly decodable.** On 444 LiveCodeBench tasks, a StandardScaler → PCA(95%) → ℓ2 logistic regression pipeline fit on the last-prompt-token hidden state of Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 (layer 29, C = 0.01, chosen by nested CV) predicts first-attempt pass/fail with leakage-free held-out AUC 0.931 ± 0.008 across 50 outer stratified 80/20 splits. After an OLS residualization that regresses each of the 2560 hidden dims on `prompt_tokens` and subtracts the fit, the probe still reaches 0.911 ± 0.010.
- **Prompt-length floor is empirical, not an artifact of linear baseline.** A prompt-only baseline (logistic on `prompt_tokens` alone) reaches 0.754 ± 0.014; random forest, gradient boosting, and a small MLP all stay in the 0.735–0.753 band. The 16-AUC-point gap between the residualized probe and the best prompt-only model is therefore not a baseline-construction artifact.
- **Layer selection by nested CV, not by test-set argmax.** Across 50 outer splits, layer 29 is selected in 25 of 50 (layer 30 in 24/50; deeper layers never selected); the modal (layer, C) pair is (29, 0.01), chosen in 22/50 splits. Reporting the modal configuration's outer-test AUC (0.931) is leakage-free. Per-layer descriptive sweep shows a shallow plateau peaking at layers 29–30 then gently declining to 0.913 at layer 36.
- **A repair contrastive direction exists in raw deltas.** On 236 cleaned 0→1 transitions (128 successful repairs, 108 failed), the mean(success) − mean(failure) direction v in layer-29 2560-dim space has ∥v∥ = 9.76 vs shuffled null mean 3.64 (std 0.59, max 6.58; real value ≈ 10σ above null; 0/1000 shuffles reach it). Split-half cosine (recomputed v on two stratified halves) = 0.767 vs null mean −0.011 (std 0.225; > 3σ above null). Both p < 0.001.
- **The repair direction does not survive conditional residualization.** Three observable covariates differ between success and failure groups (Welch / χ², all p < 0.001): ∆prompt_tokens (success 337.1 vs failure 431.5), code_length_attempt_0 (188.8 vs 290.9), and failure_type distribution (assertion/runtime/syntax). OLS-residualizing each of the 2560 delta dimensions against all three (4-parameter covariate matrix: 2 continuous + 2 failure-type dummies with assertion as baseline) drops ∥v∥ to 3.72 (p = 0.126) and split-half cosine to 0.140 (p = 0.942).
- **Pair-of-results methodological framing.** The author frames the contribution as "a diagnostic honest enough to report a negative result alongside a positive one," and applies the same residualization tool in both Sections 3 and 4. The pre-generation signal survives residualization against prompt length; the repair direction does not survive residualization against differentially-distributed repair-context covariates. The verdict changes, but the tool does not.
- **Cleaning rationale is data-driven, not ad hoc.** The 236 sample results from (a) dropping 47 "repetition-loop" trajectories where all 5 attempts hit the 768-token cap, and (b) removing 91 more cases where attempt 0 alone hit the cap (truncated broken code gets pasted into repair prompt and contaminates failure-group deltas; 83/91 of these fail at attempt 1). The 236 retains 128 success + 108 failure cases.
- **Trajectory-level nulls and dataset limits disclosed.** Per-attempt pass rates collapse sharply after the first repair (15.8% / 36.4% / 6.7% / 2.7% / 1.4% for attempts 0–4 among tasks that reach each attempt), and successful repairs at 1→2, 2→3, 3→4 are only 13, 4, and 2 — too few for any direction estimation. A within-trajectory cos(δt→t+1, δt+1→t+2) is reported as ≈ −0.16 (eventual pass) and ≈ −0.25 (eventual fail), and tracking distance to a "success centroid" shows no monotonic convergence. The author flags these as data-starved nulls where "no signal" cannot be distinguished from "insufficient data."

## Methodology

- **Model.** Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507, 36 layers, 2560-dim hidden state, decoder-only transformer. Single GPU (RTX 4050, 6 GB VRAM).
- **Tasks.** 444 LiveCodeBench problems across easy / medium / hard tiers. Pre-generation probe uses all 444 at attempt 0; repair analysis uses 236 cleaned 0→1 transitions.
- **Hidden state capture.** The 2560-dim hidden state at the **last prompt token** of a prompt-only forward pass (no autoregressive continuation), captured at each of layers 29–36 (upper transformer blocks, where high-level properties concentrate in the probing literature). The layer used downstream is selected by nested CV, not pre-fixed.
- **Repair-B policy.** When attempt k fails the unit tests, the next prompt is `{original problem} + {failure-type sentence} + {previous code} + {error output} + {fix instruction}`, capped at 768 new tokens per attempt, max 5 attempts per task. Each attempt is a fresh forward pass on the full prompt; no state is carried over.
- **Pre-generation probe pipeline.** StandardScaler → PCA(95% cumulative variance) → ℓ2 logistic regression. C selected by 5-fold CV inside the outer training fold over the grid {10⁻³, 10⁻², 10⁻¹, 1, 10}, scoring on roc_auc. Three configurations are compared under the same 50-split nested CV protocol:
  1. **Raw** — pipeline applied to the captured hidden state vector.
  2. **Residualized** — for each of 2560 hidden dims, an OLS regression on the train fold with `prompt_tokens` as the single predictor; the fitted prediction is subtracted from both train and test for that dim before applying the standard pipeline. Re-fit on every random split.
  3. **Prompt-only baseline** — logistic regression (and four other model families as a robustness check) on `prompt_tokens` alone.
- **Layer selection (nested CV).** Layer and C are jointly selected by 5-fold CV inside each of 50 outer stratified 80/20 splits; the selected configuration is refit on the full outer-training fold and evaluated on the untouched outer-test fold. Modal pair: (layer 29, C = 0.01), picked in 22/50 outer splits. Layer 29 is fixed for all downstream analyses. The leakage-free outer-test AUC of the full procedure is 0.931 ± 0.008.
- **Repair contrastive direction v.** For each 0→1 transition, δᵢ = h_layer29(attempt 1) − h_layer29(attempt 0) ∈ ℝ²⁵⁶⁰; v = mean{δᵢ : i ∈ success} − mean{δᵢ : i ∈ failure}. Each attempt is a fresh forward pass, so δᵢ is driven by what the model re-reads on attempt 1 (failing code, error output, retry instruction).
- **Repair cleaning filters.** (a) Drop 47 "repetition-loop" trajectories where all 5 attempts hit the 768-token cap; (b) drop 91 more where attempt 0 alone hit the cap (truncated broken code contaminates repair-prompt deltas; 83/91 of these fail at attempt 1). The 236 cleaned transitions: 128 success / 108 failure.
- **Repair null tests on raw deltas.** (i) Magnitude: ∥v∥ vs 1000 label-shuffled ∥v∥. (ii) Inclination (split-half): split 236 deltas into two stratified halves, recompute v independently on each half, take cosine; repeat 1000 times vs the same procedure on shuffled-label data.
- **Covariate balance check (before any residualization).** Per candidate covariate, run Welch's two-sample t (continuous) or χ² (categorical) on success vs failure groups. Only covariates with group means / proportions that differ warrant residualization — for one-hot categoricals, equal group means ↔ equal category proportions, so a balance check is necessary and sufficient.
- **Conditional residualization on deltas.** For each of the 2560 dim d, fit OLS on the 236 deltas with δᵢ,ₐ as response and X ∈ ℝ²³⁶ˣ⁴ as predictor matrix (2 continuous covariates + 2 failure-type dummies with assertion as baseline). The residualized delta is δ̃ᵢ,ₐ = δᵢ,ₐ − α̂ₐ − Xᵢ β̂ₐ. Recompute v and rerun the two null tests on the residualized deltas.
- **Per-attempt pass rate table.** 15.8% / 36.4% / 6.7% / 2.7% / 1.4% for attempts 0–4, with shrinking denominators as tasks exit after passing. Beyond attempt 1, successful repairs are too few (13, 4, 2 at 1→2, 2→3, 3→4) for direction estimation.
- **Tools / implementation.** scikit-learn for StandardScaler, PCA, logistic regression, cross-validation; numpy / scipy for statistics. Code, data, and scripts: https://github.com/CarloDiCicco/ReasoningLab.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Carlo Di Cicco** — sole author, independent researcher. Contact: cdicicco2001@gmail.com. Code, data, analysis scripts: https://github.com/CarloDiCicco/ReasoningLab.
- Single-author work limits the breadth of cross-checking typical of multi-lab interpretability studies, but the methodological discipline (nested CV, label-shuffled nulls, balanced baselines, pre-registered-style reporting, paired positive / negative) is a strong credibility signal in the opposite direction.
- The two closest prior works are cited and contrasted in the introduction: Bui et al. 2025 (correctness assessment of generated code via internal representations) and Ribeiro et al. 2025 ("On LLMs' internal representation of code correctness"). Both read hidden states *after* the model has produced an answer; this paper moves the question upstream to the prompt-only forward pass.
- The methodological framing is borrowed from Representation Engineering (Zou et al. 2023, "RepTop-down approach to AI transparency") and the layer-selection logic follows the validation-based protocol in Ribeiro et al. 2025 — these are appropriate choices.
- The Cawley & Talbot 2010 JMLR paper on over-fitting in model selection and subsequent selection bias is the correct reference for justifying nested CV over test-set argmax layer selection.
- Limitations are volunteered in the paper itself: single model, single benchmark, captured layer range starts at 29 (a layer below 29 cannot be ruled out), data starvation beyond 0→1, and the ∆prompt_tokens / code_length_attempt_0 collinearity (Pearson r = 0.995) is disclosed and its negligible overfitting bias justified empirically (≤ 1 percentage-point in-sample R² gain from the redundant column).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** Moderate but well-placed. Reading hidden states of *generated* code for pass/fail is already a small subfield (Bui 2025, Ribeiro 2025); this work moves the probe to the *prompt-final* state, which is a more upstream, more interpretable claim. The repair-geometry half is largely a negative result, which is the genuinely fresh contribution: a worked example of how a "significant" contrastive direction can dissolve under the right confound control, with a covariate-balance check protocol that is itself reusable.
- **Rigor.** Above the field's average for probing papers. The combination of (a) nested CV for layer + C selection, (b) prompt-length residualization with a non-linear-baseline robustness check, (c) two complementary null tests (magnitude and split-half) for the repair direction, and (d) a covariate-balance pre-test that gates which residualizations are warranted — is unusual and welcome. The 50-outer-split protocol with 95% CIs is appropriate. Minor rigor gaps: (i) the residualization re-fits an OLS per hidden dim on the 236-delta sample, which is 2560 × 236 OLS fits; multiple-testing is not discussed (though the p-value inflation on a single statistic per dim is bounded). (ii) The two failure-type dummies with assertion as baseline absorb linear effects of failure type but not interactions with continuous covariates; the model is linear in covariates, which is consistent with the small sample. (iii) Split-half cosine 0.767 is a "moderately stable" number, not a strong direction — but the magnitude test (∥v∥ = 9.76, ≈ 10σ above null) is the load-bearing result on the raw side, and the author correctly flags that the inclination is the weaker of the two.
- **Reproducibility.** Strong. Public model, public benchmark, public data, public code, hardware disclosed (RTX 4050, 6 GB VRAM, single laptop), 50 outer splits, label-shuffled nulls, balanced baselines, complete tables in the paper. No proprietary data or unreleased model. A reader can rerun every number in the paper in a few days on the same GPU.
- **Significance.** Two-fold. (i) The pre-generation result is a useful data point that code-correctness-relevant information is geometrically accessible *before* generation on a 4B open-weights model, with implications for early-decision routing, selective answering, and confidence-aware code generation. (ii) The repair-geometry negative result is methodologically important for the field: it gives a clean template for diagnosing when a "representation-engineering direction" is a correlate of the prompt's structure rather than an isolable model feature. The covariate-balance pre-test (only residualize covariates whose group means differ) is itself a contribution that should be reused in other RepEng work.
- **Red flags.** None serious, but worth flagging: (i) the *captured layer range starts at 29* — the author explicitly says "a layer below 29 cannot be ruled out as comparable or better" but does not measure it (likely a VRAM / engineering constraint, since earlier layers may already be in the LCB/VRAM budget). The result's robustness across 29 / 30 / 35 is reported for the repair side but not for the pre-generation side beyond the 8-layer sweep. (ii) The 236-sample cleaning is principled but the criteria are partly heuristic (the "repetition-loop" filter is qualitative); a sensitivity analysis on cleaning thresholds would strengthen the negative result. (iii) The pre-generation probe is linear (StandardScaler → PCA → logistic) — non-linear probes are not tested as a sanity check, so the "0.911" number is a *linear-decode* floor, not a full ceiling. (iv) The author is honest about the collinearity of ∆prompt_tokens and code_length_attempt_0 (Pearson r = 0.995), and the disclosure is convincing, but a cleaner covariate set would be one variable or a non-overlapping pair. (v) The single-model / single-benchmark scope is openly acknowledged and limits the universality of the conclusion. (vi) No code link was visible in the abstract — the paper itself clarifies (https://github.com/CarloDiCicco/ReasoningLab), so this is a presentation gap in the abstract rather than a substantive issue.

## Strengths

- **Honest, paired-results design.** A single residualization tool is used to (a) confirm the pre-generation signal is not length and (b) overturn the repair-direction claim. The reader does not need to guess whether the two analyses had different rigor.
- **Nested CV everywhere a hyperparameter is chosen.** Layer and C are selected inside the outer training fold, with the leakage-free outer-test AUC reported; this is rare in probing work and substantially reduces the "argmax-on-test" selection bias.
- **Explicit, leakage-free baseline comparison.** The prompt-only baseline is replicated across four model families (logistic, RF, GBT, MLP), none of which approach the residualized probe. This rules out the most common "weak baseline" criticism.
- **Strong null testing for the raw repair direction.** 1000 label shuffles for both magnitude and split-half; the real magnitude is ≈ 10σ above the null and 0/1000 shuffles reach it.
- **Disciplined covariate selection.** The pre-residualization balance check (Welch t / χ²) on each candidate covariate is conceptually cleaner than the more common "residualize against everything" approach, and the author explains *why* the order matters (a balanced covariate is already cancelled by the contrastive mean subtraction, so residualizing it is either neutral or collapse-inducing).
- **Cleaning and data-starvation are disclosed, not hidden.** The 47-trajectory repetition-loop filter, the 91-trajectory attempt-0-cap filter, the per-attempt pass-rate table, the 13/4/2 successful multi-step repair counts, and the "distance to success centroid" null are all reported with explicit acknowledgment that "no signal" cannot be distinguished from "insufficient data."
- **Small-footprint reproducibility.** Single 6 GB GPU, public model, public benchmark, public code. A research group or independent researcher can verify every number on commodity hardware.
- **Methodological template for the field.** The pre-residualization balance check + same-tool-both-ways design is a pattern that other RepEng / interpretability work can reuse.

## Limitations

- **Single model (Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507).** The pre-generation result may be specific to this model's training or scale; larger Qwen3, Llama-3, or Mistral variants are untested.
- **Single benchmark (LiveCodeBench).** Language-style and problem-style generalization is open; LeetCode, HumanEval, MBPP, RepoBench, and SWE-bench would each test a different facet.
- **Linear probe only.** StandardScaler → PCA → ℓ2 logistic is the only architecture tested; small MLPs, k-NN, or sparse linear probes could be a stronger sanity check on the 0.911 number, and the gap to "perfect" decoding is not characterized.
- **Captured layer range starts at layer 29 (out of 36).** A lower layer outside the captured range is not ruled out as comparable or better; the peak sitting at the *lower bound* of the range is itself a caveat.
- **Cleaning criteria for the 236 repair sample are partly heuristic.** "Repetition-loop" is qualitative; sensitivity to the cleaning threshold is not reported.
- **Covariate set has a near-collinear pair.** ∆prompt_tokens and code_length_attempt_0 share Pearson r = 0.995. The disclosure and the ≤ 1pp R²-gain analysis are convincing, but a non-overlapping pair would be cleaner.
- **Residualization is linear in covariates.** A 4-parameter linear model on a 236-sample dataset; nonlinear or higher-order covariate effects are not absorbed.
- **No causal / interventional evidence.** The pre-generation result is associational — the prompt-final hidden state is *informative*, not shown to be *causally* the locus of correctness. Steering, ablation, or activation patching are not performed.
- **No code link in the abstract;** only in the paper. (Minor.)
- **Multi-step repair direction is unestimable** on this dataset (13/4/2 successes at later steps) — not a methodological flaw, but a hard scope limit.

## Relevance

直接命中"可解释性 + 代码生成"的研究兴趣：在 4B 规模的开源 LLM 上给出一份可在 6 GB 单卡上复现的预生成正确性可解码性证据，并示范了一种能在 RepEng 类方向性分析中区分"信号 vs 上下文相关物"的混杂控制工具（残差化 + 协变量平衡预检），与 selective answering、routing、confidence-aware generation 与 LLM interpretability 方向都直接相关；同时也是近期"代码正确性的内部表征"小方向里方法学较严谨的一篇。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the prompt-final probe survive on larger models (Qwen3-72B, Llama-3-70B, DeepSeek-Coder-V2) or scale with model depth / training-compute as the linear-probe literature would suggest?
- Can a non-linear probe (small MLP, k-NN, sparse linear with feature selection) close the gap to perfect decoding, or is the residual information non-local across hidden dims?
- Which specific repair-context covariate drives the dissolution of the repair direction most — ∆prompt_tokens, code_length_attempt_0, or failure_type? A leave-one-covariate-out sensitivity analysis would localize the effect.
- Is the pre-generation signal *causally* relevant? Can intervening on the prompt-final hidden state (steering, ablation, activation patching) actually flip pass/fail rates on held-out problems, or is the probe reading a correlate rather than a cause?
- How stable is the prompt-length residualization across prompts of very different structure (one-liner vs multi-class specification, single function vs full project scaffold)?
- Does the same diagnostic framework overturn other "contrastive direction" claims in the code-repair literature (e.g. claims about self-debugging features, error-type detectors, or syntax-recovery directions)?
- Could the pre-generation probe layer / C / PCA-variance configuration transfer across model families with little retraining, indicating a *family-shared* code-correctness representation?
- With a substantially larger dataset (e.g. > 100 successful multi-step repairs at transitions 1→2, 2→3, 3→4), does the same direction analysis hold for later repairs, or is attempt 0→1 genuinely a special case?
- Is the failure-type distribution shift between success and failure groups a property of the *model* (it fails in different ways depending on the path to success) or of the *benchmark* (LiveCodeBench's unit tests classify failures differently than other verifiers)? Replicating on a second benchmark with a different verifier would test this.
- Could the residualization tool be applied *pre-emptively* in RepEng studies — i.e. should the standard analysis pipeline begin with a covariate-balance pre-check, not end with a residualization as an after-thought?
