Multi-component Causal Tracing in Large Language Models — with PGB-CT.

Activation-patching-style causal tracing is lifted from single-layer / single-component analyses into a unified multi-component framework. The combinatorial search over attention heads and MLP neurons is relaxed to continuous optimization, then discretized by thresholding — at LLaMA-13B scale.

Key Finding

PGB-CT jointly localizes attention heads and MLP neurons driving any target metric. Soft intervention m ∈ [0,1]N, transformed reward 1/(1+ℓ), and scheduled penalty λ₁∥m∥₁ + λ₂mᵀ(1−m) relax the combinatorial search into tractable continuous optimization — then threshold at τ = 0.5.

— 02

Key Metric

On GPT2-medium WinoGender (10% sparsity): PGB-CT 0.203 vs top-k 0.191 vs greedy 0.208 — in 1.56 min vs 2.76 vs 357.28 min. That's a 229× speedup over greedy at parity quality, and 1.76× over top-k. Jaccard with greedy: 0.64 (vs 0.44 with top-k).
A3
— 03

Why This Matters

Greedy multi-component causal tracing is intractable past LLaMA-7B. PGB-CT makes LLaMA-13B tractable — jointly discovering an effective 10-component VBD circuit (8 heads + 2 MLP blocks) that drives value-dependence accuracy to ~1.0. Turns mechanistic interpretability into a budget-respecting tool.
A4
— 04

Critical Edge

The m(1−m) term explicitly drives binary violation to zero — which is exactly where prior gradient methods (DCM) collapse. Combined with the 1/(1+ℓ) transform that decouples regularization from metric scale, PGB-CT is the first soft-mask causal-tracing method that actually lands on a sparse, binarized solution.

Setup. Causal tracing is generalized from a single component/layer to a subset H ⊆ C. A binary mask m ∈ {0,1}N marks the treated subset; the forward pass mixes the original hidden state hᵢ with its counterfactual hᵢ' (e.g., a pronoun-swapped prompt).

Target. Maximize any plug-in metric ℓ(D, m) — accuracy, gender-bias measure, factual recall, VBD — subject to ∥m∥₀ ≤ S. Adversaries: greedy (intractable at scale), top-k (single-component ranking), DCM (prior soft-mask).

Optimization. Solve the relaxed problem with Adam (lr 0.1 heads / 0.5 MLP / 0.01 VBD), project m to [0,1] each step. After each epoch, threshold at τ = 0.5 and stop when |Hₜ| ≤ S.

Why it works. The ℓ₁ term approximates ℓ₀ (sparsity). The m(1−m) term is maximized at m = 0.5 and vanishes when m ∈ {0,1} — directly penalizing "binary violation" that DCM's ℓ₀.₅-style penalty does not target.

Empirical Findings

TBL. 1–5 · APPX. D
  1. Matches greedy on WinoGender / WinoBias / Professions / CounterFact across 7 model families; strictly beats top-k at sparsity 10%–40%.
  2. 1.76× over top-k, 229× over greedy on GPT2-medium WinoGender at 10% sparsity.
  3. Exponential scaling with selected components — top-k plateaus; PGB-CT keeps growing.
  4. top-k actively fails on Llama-3.2-1B — picks a subset worse than the all-components baseline.
  5. Joint head + MLP localization recovers an effective 10-component VBD circuit on LLaMA-13B.
  6. DCM is a soft-mask strawman: high binary-violation (mᵀ(1−m) ≫ 0) causes sharp metric drops at truncation.

Critical Assessment

RED FLAGS · CAVEATS
  • Baselines are narrow. No comparison to attribution patching (Syed 2023), EAP, ACDC (Conmy 2023), or SAEs — gradient-based methods that could be the sharper test.
  • DCM as strawman. Headline 229× speedup is over greedy (a real efficiency bar). DCM's headline failures may dissolve under careful hyperparameter search.
  • Sparsity S must be specified a priori. Practical limitation for exploratory interpretability where you don't know |H| in advance.
  • "Fairness" is narrow. Single gender-bias axis on WinoGender / WinoBias — no race, age, BBQ, or StereoSet.
  • Non-linearity claim is suggestive only. Figure 1 is GPT2-small on two datasets — not a systematic study.
  • τ = 0.5 not ablated. Hand-chosen global threshold; sensitivity not characterized.

Questions for Further Exploration

8 OPEN
  • PGB-CT vs EAP / attribution patching at matched sparsity — does the new penalty actually buy anything over plain attribution gradients?
  • Can a budget-free mode (no S specified) replace post-hoc thresholding for exploratory interpretability?
  • Sensitivity to counterfactual prompt distribution — single-token he/she swaps are a narrow intervention.
  • Does the half-half rule generalize beyond gender-bias metrics and beyond the GPT-2 family?
  • Theoretical guarantee that gradient descent converges to a Pareto-optimal component set — currently absent.
  • Extend to discover component interactions (redundancy / synergy) rather than just independent contributions.
  • Behavior on multilingual, encoder-only, MoE, and >13B models.
  • Does the recovered bias-subset transfer across demographic axes (race, age) and across datasets (BBQ, StereoSet)?
