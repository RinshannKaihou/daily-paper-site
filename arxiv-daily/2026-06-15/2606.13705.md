> **一句话总结：** 作者用逐层消融 + 逐神经元归因定位 Gemma 4 系列在长事实枚举任务上的重复环，并用静态权重编辑（E2B 仅反转 1 个 MLP 神经元、E4B 剥除 3 个、31B 剥除 1100 个、26B 屏蔽 3 个路由专家）在正常生成长度下消除/大幅减少 loop 且基准几乎无损；但在更长思考预算下，26B/31B 仍会进入"doom loop"——一种因事实缺失导致的非收敛自纠错，权重手术只能删环、补不了缺失的事实。

> **TL;DR:** This paper shows that the Gemma 4 instruction-tuned family (E2B, E4B, 31B, 26B-A4B MoE) has a reproducible repetition-loop failure on long factual enumeration prompts (e.g., listing The Wire episodes, 88 constellations, Gen-1 Pokémon), with baseline loop rates up to 95% (31B on The Wire). Using per-layer ablation + per-neuron gradient×activation attribution, the authors localize the loops to a tiny set of MLP neurons (or routed experts in the MoE) and fix them with static weight edits: a single sign-inverted L10 neuron (×−0.8) plus one amplified L12 neuron (×3.0) takes E2B from 13/128 to 0/128 loops; stripping 3 of 430,080 neurons (L18) fixes E4B (0/64 thinking-on); stripping 1100 of 1,290,240 neurons (L36) fixes 31B (0/64 thinking-on); masking 3 of 3,840 expert slots (L21:E47, L21:E98, L22:E47) cuts 26B tight loops from 6/64 to 2/64 — all within ±1 to (−1.3, +1.7) pp on IFEval/TruthfulQA/ARC/MMLU/GSM8K. However, at extended thinking budgets (≥4k tokens), the two larger models still doom-loop (non-convergent self-correction over facts they cannot recall), which the edits reduce in verbatim-lock-in form but cannot eliminate because it is a knowledge-precision gap, not a removable circuit.

## 核心贡献

- 诊断 Gemma 4 全家族在长事实枚举任务上的重复失败，区分两种表现型：**tight loop**（紧短语逐字循环）与 **soft loop**（列表结构保留但条目塌缩到单一答案，如 E2B 的 88 个星座中 41 个变成 "Volans"），并给出一个 prompt-agnostic 的确定性 loop 检测器。
- 建立一套**逐层消融 + 逐神经元归因**方法论：对每个候选层零消融 attention/MLP 看 loop-token 概率变化，再用 gradient×activation 打分（单次前向+反向即可给所有神经元打分）找到最强 pro-loop/anti-loop 神经元，最后用全生成 loop-rate sweep 确认。
- 在四个模型上证明**权重手术可行性**：编辑规模随模型增大而增大（E2B 1 神经元、E4B 3 神经元、31B 1100 神经元、26B 3 个专家槽），但都在正常生成长度下消除/大幅减少 loop，且通用基准损失极小（≤1.7 pp）。
- 划定方法的边界：区分 **fast-commit loop**（可定位、可编辑）与 **doom looping**（长预算下的非收敛自纠错，源于事实缺失、与通用推理纠缠，不可单独切除）。

## Key Contributions

- Diagnoses a reproducible enumeration-loop failure across the Gemma 4 instruction-tuned family, characterizes two loop phenotypes (tight verbatim loop and soft list-collapse), and provides a deterministic prompt-agnostic detector (token-periodicity search + prefix-stripped line dedup).
- Develops a per-layer ablation + per-neuron gradient×activation attribution methodology to localize the loop-driving MLP neurons (or routed experts in the MoE), and confirms candidates with full-generation loop-rate sweeps over an 8-prompts × 8-seeds × 2-thinking-modes grid (128 generations/model).
- Demonstrates weight-surgery feasibility across all four models: E2B needs a single sign-inverted L10 neuron (+one amplified L12 neuron), E4B needs 3 stripped L18 neurons, 31B needs a probe-combined 1100-neuron L36 strip, and 26B needs 3 masked expert slots — all removing the loops at normal budgets with ≤1.7 pp benchmark regression.
- Delineates where the approach stops: shows doom looping (extended-budget non-convergent self-correction) persists in 26B/31B even after the fast-commit loop is removed, argues it is a knowledge-precision gap entangled with general reasoning rather than a separable circuit, and provides evidence (the v5_doom experiment) that targeting self-correction experts breaks unrelated reasoning.

## Methodology

- **Models:** Gemma 4 instruction-tuned family — gemma-4-E2B-it (35 dense layers), gemma-4-E4B-it (42 dense layers), gemma-4-31B-it (60 dense layers), gemma-4-26B-A4B-it (30 MoE layers, 128 routed experts × top-8, shared MLP only ~27% of FFN compute). Reproduced failures on both HF Transformers and vLLM.
- **Loop probes:** 8 enumeration prompts (firefly_list, wire_episodes, pokemon_gen1, mcu_films, noble_gases, constellations, us_presidents, eu_member_states); sweep = 8 probes × 8 seeds × 2 thinking modes = 128 generations/variant. Default sampling: temperature=0.7, top_p=0.95, top_k=64, no repetition penalty.
- **Loop detection:** deterministic, prompt-agnostic — tight loops via exhaustive token-periodicity search; soft loops via prefix-stripped line deduplication; plus a phrase-repetition sliding-window check.
- **Localization step 1 (per-layer ablation):** capture one looping rollout (firefly_list for 31B/E4B, constellations for E2B, wire_episodes for 26B), split tokens into pre-loop [200, start−50] vs loop [start+50, N]; for each layer zero out attention or MLP output and measure Δp(loop-token). Identifies candidate layers (26B: strongest pro-loop attention L16 Δp=−0.237, strongest anti-loop MLP L26 Δp=+0.143).
- **Localization step 2 (per-neuron attribution):** Gemma 4 uses GeGLU MLPs; neuron n = index in intermediate dim (column n of down_proj). For each neuron record post-gate activation a_n and gradient g_n = ∂log p(loop-token)/∂a_n; loop-attribution score Δ_n = −Σ_p a_n(p)·g_n(p) ≈ log p(with n zeroed) − log p(baseline). Positive Δ_n = anti-loop (amplify); negative = pro-loop (zero/strip/sign-invert).
- **Intervention operations:** Stripping (zero down_proj columns / expert post-router scale), Amplification (α>1), Suppression (0<β<1), Sign inversion (α<0, converts pro-loop into anti-loop direction).
- **Model-specific edits:** E2B — sign-invert L10 neuron 3513 by α=−0.8 + amplify L12 neuron 3838 by α=3.0 (2 of 215,040 FFN neurons, 0.0009%); E4B — strip top-3 L18 neurons (3 of 430,080, 0.0007%); 31B — strip 1000 neurons from firefly_list + 100 from wire_episodes at L36 (1100 of 1,290,240, 0.085%); 26B — mask L21:E47, L21:E98, L22:E47 (3 of 3,840 expert slots, 0.078%).
- **Regression benchmarks:** IFEval, TruthfulQA, ARC-Easy, ARC-Challenge, MMLU, GSM8K — both thinking modes, temperature=0.0, max_output_tokens=8192, via Inspect-AI framework.
- **Long-budget doom-loop analysis:** max_new_tokens ∈ {4k, 8k}, enable_thinking=true, on 3 doom-prone prompts × 8 seeds × 2 budgets (n=24/model), with rep_pen ∈ {1.0, 1.15}.

## Results & Highlights

- **Baseline loop rates up to 95%:** gemma-4-31B-it fails 95% on the "list all episodes of The Wire" prompt under default sampling; baseline failure ranges from ~10% (lower-failure models) to 95% across the family, surviving prompt rewording, inference-engine changes (HF/vLLM), and MTP drafter on/off.
- **E2B edit: 13/128 → 0/128 loops** with a 2-neuron edit (L10:3513 sign-inverted ×−0.8 + L12:3838 amplified ×3.0); the 1-neuron α=−1 variant also reaches 0/128 but with worse benchmark tail (−2.6 pp); the selected 2-neuron variant has worst-case regression −1.3 pp (MMLU thinking-off, near chance) / mean |Δ| 0.57 pp.
- **E4B edit: stripping 3 of 430,080 L18 neurons → 0/64 loops** (thinking-on), 2/64 residual soft loops (thinking-off), touching just 0.0007% of FFN neurons — the most localized edit in the family.
- **31B edit: stripping 1100 of 1,290,240 L36 neurons → 0/64 loops** (thinking-on), 1/64 residual tight loop (thinking-off, firefly_list seed 3); 0.085% of FFN neurons; benchmark deltas within ±1 pp.
- **26B edit: masking 3 expert slots (L21:E47, L21:E98, L22:E47) → tight loops 6/64 → 2/64**, and wire_episodes tight loops 5/8 → 0/8; these experts jump from <6% selection pre-loop to >60% inside the loop; benchmark deltas within (−1, +1.6) pp.
- **Probe-combination matters for 31B:** the intersection strategy (neurons loop-driving on both probes) underperforms (9–11/64); the selected "strip top-1000 from firefly_list ∪ top-100 from wire_episodes" reaches 0/64, showing different prompts enter different attractor basins (firefly→"The Message", wire→"The Detail") with only partially overlapping neurons.
- **Doom looping persists in larger models at long budgets:** at rep_pen=1.0 the 31B edit cuts tight lock-ins 13/24 → 6/24 but converts the freed share into endless self-correction (6/24 → 26/24), so total doom looping rises slightly (32/48 → 38/48); adding rep_pen=1.15 reduces total doom looping (31B 38→26, 26B 39→16) and raises natural-EOS completions (31B 10→20, 26B 9→32). The edit removes verbatim lock-in; the penalty converts residual self-correction into clean terminations — they are complementary.
- **Doom loop is NOT a separable circuit:** the v5_doom experiment (masking experts active during *Actually:* / *Let's try again* self-correction interjections) killed 26B's late-budget tight loops but introduced new loops on pokemon_gen1 (5/8 seeds on Zapdos) and firefly_list — evidence those experts are general-purpose verification components, not a dedicated doom circuit.
- **Weight edits beat repetition-penalty tuning:** rep_pen≥1.15 reduces repetition but corrupts unrelated generation — on E4B, rep_pen=1.30 breaks 22/30 Rust code-generation prompts (inventing non-existent macros like `writable!`); the edits operate at rep_pen=1.0 and avoid this tradeoff.

## Critical Assessment

- **Single-vendor, single-family evidence:** All four models belong to the Gemma 4 family; the paper explicitly concedes "whether the same per-layer, per-neuron localization of loop behavior extends to other instruction-tuned LLMs remains an open question," so the central feasibility claim is demonstrated for exactly one model family — no Llama/Qwen/Mistral replication.
- **Edits are found by search, not derived:** the authors repeatedly call the work "an exploratory investigation rather than a systematic search" and state the reported configurations are "one demonstrably effective set of weight edits, not the result of an exhaustive sweep over intervention types, target layers, or neuron selection criteria" — so smaller/more generalizable edits may exist, and the per-model neuron counts (1, 3, 1100) reflect search effort, not a clean scaling law.
- **The 31B edit is broad and has collateral damage:** the 1100-neuron L36 strip removes the baseline's alphabetical W-Z verification closure on constellations (5/8 → 1/8 seeds at rep_pen=1.0), a regression the authors themselves flag — showing "weight surgery" at this scale already impinges on real reasoning steps, undercutting the "surgical" framing.
- **Doom-loop root cause is asserted, not proven:** the paper hypothesizes doom looping is "a knowledge-precision problem" possibly from "loss of factual precision during pruning or distillation from a larger model" but states this "remains a hypothesis rather than a conclusion established by these experiments" — the central negative result rests on an unverified causal claim.
- **Weak code-generation side-effect analysis:** the rep_pen corruption finding (22/30 Rust prompts broken at rep_pen=1.30) is, per the authors' own Limitations, "based on a single Rust prompt," so the claim that rep_pen is "an unreliable general solution" is directionally supported but quantitatively thin.

## Strengths

- Clean, mechanistic-interpretability framing that ties a high-level generation pathology to editable weights — a direct extension of Kazemi et al. (2026)'s single-neuron refusal-axis result to a different failure mode.
- Honest delineation of the method's boundary: the fast-commit-loop vs. doom-loop distinction, with experimental evidence (v5_doom) that the doom circuit is entangled with general reasoning and cannot be excised without collateral damage.
- The per-neuron gradient×activation attribution is computationally cheap (one forward + one backward scores all neurons) and the full-generation sweep confirms it, addressing the known localization≠edit-location disconnect (corroborating Hase et al. 2023).
- All four edited model weights are publicly released, enabling independent verification.
- The probe-combination analysis (intersection vs. union vs. per-probe partition) yields a genuinely useful mechanistic insight: different prompts enter different attractor basins, so "generally pro-loop" neurons are not always the right target.

## Limitations

- Demonstrated only on the Gemma 4 family; transfer to other instruction-tuned LLMs is unverified.
- The reported edits are one found configuration, not an exhaustive optimum; smaller/more general edits may exist.
- The 31B 1100-neuron edit has measurable collateral (lost W-Z verification closure), so "surgical" is increasingly a stretch at scale.
- Doom looping's root cause (knowledge-precision gap) is hypothesized, not established by experiment.
- Code-generation side-effect analysis rests on a single Rust prompt.
- No automated/online version of the edit is provided — the masks are unconditional and baked into weights, so they cannot adapt to runtime loop detection.

## Relevance

高度映射到用户研究兴趣 **「3. LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」**（逐层/逐神经元归因、MLP 回路定位、权重手术）与 **「1. LLM Training Stability & Observability」**（生成病理学的可复现诊断、训练后失效模式的可观测性）。其"权重手术能删 loop、补不了事实"的边界结论对理解 LLM 可靠性失效的根因层次（回路 vs. 知识缺失）具有直接价值，亦与 **「6. Silent Data Corruption (SDC)」** 在概念上相关（模型自身生成的"损坏"输出路径定位）。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the same per-layer/per-neuron localization transfer to other instruction-tuned families (Llama, Qwen, Mistral), or is the concentration of loop behavior in <0.1% of FFN neurons a Gemma-4-specific artifact? The paper flags this as the key open question.
- Is the doom-loop regime truly a knowledge-precision gap (as hypothesized from pruning/distillation) or could it be a separable-but-deeply-entangled circuit — and could runtime-conditional editing (gated by an online loop detector, which the authors defer) isolate it without the v5_doom collateral damage?
- Would a systematic (not exploratory) sweep over intervention types, layers, and K yield substantially smaller edits — e.g., is the 31B's 1100-neuron strip an artifact of using only two probes (firefly_list + wire_episodes)?
- Can the gradient×activation attribution be validated against exact per-neuron counterfactuals (one forward per neuron) to bound the approximation error, rather than relying on the first-order estimate?
- The paper cites Kazemi et al. (2026) for the single-neuron refusal axis and Wei et al. (2024) for safety-critical regions occupying ~3% of parameters — does the 0.0007–0.085% FFN fraction found here for loops suggest loop circuits are qualitatively more localized than safety circuits, and if so why?
