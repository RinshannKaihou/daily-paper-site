> **一句话总结：** 在 VLM 语言模型骨干中识别出仅 ~100 个（约 8.7%）"注视头"(gaze heads)——其注意力逐 token 跟踪模型当前描述的图像区域；通过单一注意力遮罩干预即可在无重训练下以 83.1% 准确率把生成重定向到任意指定面板，并在自然图像、2B–32B 规模及多种 VLM 架构中复现（冻结编码器家族除外）。
>
> **TL;DR:** This paper identifies a small set of attention heads in VLM language-model backbones ("gaze heads") whose attention tracks the image region currently being described, token by token. In Qwen3-VL-8B, only 100 of 1,152 heads (8.7%), all in mid-late layers 20–28, exhibit this behavior. Discovered via a simple correlation score from a few forward passes using comic strips (where narrative order is laid out spatially), these heads are *causally sufficient* for steering: a single attention-mask intervention (δ=+∞) on the top-100 gaze heads steers VQA answers to any chosen comic panel at 83.1% accuracy (chance 16.7%), while random heads fail to redirect (14.6%) and all-head intervention destroys generation (0.9%). The same lever supports continuous control — switching the gaze target every 50 tokens yields Spearman ρ=0.87 with the steering schedule. The mechanism recurs across Qwen3-VL sizes (2B–32B), transfers to Ovis1.5, Qwen2-VL, and InternVL3.5 (60–69%), but breaks in frozen-encoder families (LLaVA, Bunny) — a same-backbone contrast (Bunny freezes the SigLIP that Ovis fine-tunes) suggesting the mechanism depends on joint vision-LM training. Code, demo, and datasets at gaze.baulab.info.

## 核心贡献

- **"注视头"概念与发现方法**：在 VLM 的 LM 骨干中识别出 ~100 个（约 8.7%）"注视头"，其注意力逐 token 跟踪模型当前正在描述的图像区域。发现方法仅依赖少量前向传播的简单相关性分数，无需训练或标注。
- **漫画条作为受控测试床**：利用漫画叙事顺序天然按空间排列的特性，把"模型在描述哪一块"这一不平凡问题转化为有明确 ground-truth 的可验证问题。
- **因果证据（强）**：单一注意力遮罩干预即可在推理时把生成重定向到任意面板，VQA 准确率 83.1%（机会 16.7%）。三个对照实验三角验证效应：随机头失败（14.6%）、全部头干预破坏生成（0.9%）、无干预作为基线。
- **中间层定位**：先通过残差流"反向阅读"干预实验把视觉路由定位到 layers 20–28 的窄带；gaze heads 集中在同一层带，符合"功能对齐"。
- **跨域/跨规模/跨架构泛化**：在自然 COCO 图像上同样工作（按物体大小 61.7%–80.3%）；在 Qwen3-VL 2B/4B/8B/32B 上重复出现；Ovis1.5/Qwen2-VL/InternVL3.5 上同样有效（62–69%）。
- **失败案例的假说**：冻结视觉编码器家族（LLaVA-1.5、LLaVA-NeXT、Bunny-3B）缺乏对应的 gaze head 集合。同一 SigLIP-so400m backbone 在 Bunny（冻结）→ 8.3% vs Ovis（微调）→ 68.7% 的对照强烈暗示机制依赖于视觉编码器与 LM 的联合训练。
- **基线对比与无训练推理时控制**：把 Image Heads (MaskCD) 和 Localization Heads 选择器直接套到同一干预上，在 K=100 时 gaze 比两个基线分别高 14.1pp 和 22.9pp。展示了一种无需重训练的实用多模态行为控制杠杆。
- **完整开源**：代码、交互式 Demo、数据集均在 gaze.baulab.info 与 GitHub 发布。

## Key Contributions

- **Discovery of "gaze heads"** in VLM language-model backbones: a small subset of attention heads whose attention tracks the image region currently being described, token by token. In Qwen3-VL-8B, exactly 100 of 1,152 heads (8.7%), all in mid-late layers 20–28, exhibit this behavior.
- **Minimal detection method**: a simple correlation score (mean diagonal of per-head 6×6 queried-panel × attended-panel attention matrix) computed from a few forward passes suffices. No large-scale supervision, no training.
- **Comics as a controlled testbed**: comic strips encode narrative order spatially, giving an unambiguous ground-truth for "which region the model should attend to at each step." Reusable methodological idea for any "where is the model looking" study.
- **Causal (not merely correlative) steering**: a single attention-mask intervention (pre-softmax bias δ=+∞, applied at prefill and decode; text-token attention untouched) on the top-100 gaze heads steers the model's answer to any chosen panel at 83.1% accuracy on VQA and 79.4% on static narration, vs. 1/6 chance.
- **Three-baseline control**: random heads (same layer band, excluding gaze set) → 14.6% (fails); all 1,152 heads → 0.9% (destroys generation); no intervention → natural behavior. Triangulates that the effect is specific to the identified head set.
- **Mid-layer localization via residual-stream probing**: an independent "read-in-reverse" experiment (adding a per-layer residual direction) flips the predicted panel label sharply only in layers 20–28 — the same band the gaze heads concentrate in, establishing functional alignment.
- **Continuous (dynamic) control**: switching the gaze target every 50 tokens during 300-token generation yields Spearman ρ=0.87 between the steering schedule and the order the model actually describes. The model keeps its default "1, 2, 3..." textual numbering but describes the content of the steered panel — a striking dissociation between textual scaffolding and visual grounding.
- **Top-K saturation curve**: accuracy peaks at K=100 (83.1%), then declines gracefully (75.6% at K=150, 73.0% at K=200) as the intervention starts overriding heads the model needs for fluent output. Sharpens the claim that the gaze function is concentrated in roughly the top-100 heads.
- **Natural-image extension**: same intervention steers Qwen3-VL-8B toward chosen regions in COCO val2017 images — 80.3% (large), 76.2% (medium), 61.7% (small), 76.5% overall. Confirms the heads found through comic probing perform a general spatial-grounding function.
- **Cross-scale recurrence**: Qwen3-VL-2B 68.6% (K=10), 4B 72.9% (K=75), 8B 83.1% (K=100), 32B 70.2% (K=500). Mechanism is consistent; the optimal K shifts with model size.
- **Cross-architecture recurrence** in trained-encoder families: Ovis1.5-8B 68.7%, Qwen2-VL-7B 66.2%, InternVL3.5-8B 62.7%. The three families that plateau or fail (LLaVA-1.5 39.0%, LLaVA-NeXT 35.3%, Bunny-3B 8.3%) all keep a frozen CLIP/SigLIP encoder.
- **Same-backbone contrast**: Bunny freezes the same SigLIP-so400m that Ovis fine-tunes, yet produces 8.3% vs. 68.7% peak gaze accuracy. Consistent with (not proof of) the hypothesis that gaze-head formation depends on training the vision encoder together with the LM.
- **Intervention-strength ablation**: δ=10 (well short of bfloat16 saturation) reaches 79.8% — within 3–4pp of the hard limit. Effect is not an artifact of saturation.
- **Prompt sensitivity**: 64.7%–83.1% across five different prompt phrasings, all far above non-gaze controls. Robust.
- **Head-set overlap analysis**: only 13 heads sit in all three top-100 sets (gaze, Image Heads, Localization Heads); the top-10 sets are nearly disjoint. The three criteria are not interchangeable.
- **Open release**: code, interactive demo, datasets, and generator scripts at gaze.baulab.info and github.com/rohitgandikota/gaze-heads.

## Methodology

### Testbed: comic strips with spatial narrative

- **Why comics.** Panels are spatially laid out (left to right), so a model describing a panel in sequence must attend to each region in turn. This converts the under-constrained question "where is the model looking?" into a verifiable one: panel order is a ground-truth trace of the gaze trajectory.
- **Dataset construction.**
  - *Discovery corpus:* COMICS [Iyyer et al. 2017] — 3,948 comics. For each sample, take N=6 consecutive panels from one comic, resize to a common height of 256 pixels, concatenate horizontally into a strip; the entire strip is fed to the model as a single image. Panel widths vary, so each strip has a different total width and a different number of image tokens per panel.
  - *Discovery size:* 500 strips.
  - *Held-out validation set:* 500 six-panel strips generated end-to-end with GPT-4o-mini (panel descriptions) + GPT Image-1 (1024×1024 renders), enforcing six rules — consistent protagonist/style; every panel a distinct action/setting/objects; no text/captions/speech bubbles; rich-enough descriptions; no repeats; safe content. The generator script, retries, and caching are released.
  - *Bonus qualitative set:* comic strips of 6 panels each generated with Google's Nano Banana Pro.
- **Main model.** Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct: ViT-based vision encoder paired with a 36-layer LM backbone of 32 attention heads per layer (1,152 heads total; hidden dim 4096, head dim 128). All experiments run with eager attention so attention weights and hidden states are directly accessible.

### Layer localization (Sec. 4)

- Overlay each panel with a random A–Z label so the answer is a single letter.
- Run two prompts on the same strip: a normal one asking for the label of the k-th panel, and a "reverse" prompt ("Read the comic in reverse, …") that should return the label of the k-th panel counted from the right.
- Compute a per-layer "read-in-reverse" direction as the difference-of-means of activations preceding the answer, averaged over 500 (normal, reverse) pairs.
- Add this direction to the residual stream at one layer at a time during a fresh forward pass with the normal prompt. Layers 20–28 sharply flip the predicted label; outside the band, no effect. This isolates the mid-late layer band where visual routing lives.
- Control: only the *reverse* direction is coherent — sweeping 720 panel orderings, only reverse produces strong steering (91.3%), the other 719 produce much weaker steering. A global residual direction cannot handle arbitrary panel queries; the mechanism for arbitrary-k queries must live elsewhere (i.e., in attention-head routing).

### Gaze-score (Sec. 5)

- For each panel index k ∈ {1, …, 6} in a test strip, run a forward pass with the natural-language query "Look carefully at this six-panel comic strip. What is happening in the k-th panel from the left? Answer briefly." (No letter overlays — model must rely on spatial position alone.)
- From each forward pass, pull the post-softmax attention weights from the final prompt token to all image tokens, grouped by which panel they belong to.
- Across the 6 queries, every head produces a 6×6 attention matrix: rows = queried panels, columns = attended panels. A perfectly tracking head puts mass on the diagonal.
- Gaze score: `GazeScore(l, h) = (1/6) Σ_k A_{k,k}` — raw (not normalized) post-softmax attention mass the generation token places on panel k's image tokens when asked about panel k, summed over those tokens and averaged over 500 strips.
- *Why raw (not normalized).* A normalized score would also boost heads that ignore image tokens entirely (diagonal shape with zero mass). The raw variant scores such heads near zero, so it captures both "looks at images" *and* "concentrates that look on the queried panel."
- Pick the top-100 heads by gaze score as the default set; Sec. 6.1 shows redirection accuracy saturates around this threshold.

### Tracking under free-form narration (Sec. 5.2)

- Prompt the model to describe each panel in order and record value-weighted attention at every decode token, aggregated per panel.
- Compare the 100 gaze heads against 100 random non-gaze heads.
- Result: gaze-head attention forms a clean staircase — sits on panel 1 while narrating panel 1, jumps to panel 2 within a few tokens once the narration moves on, etc. Non-gaze control shows no such structure.
- Reversed narration prompt yields a mirror-image reverse staircase, confirming gaze heads faithfully track the panel being narrated (not an artifact of the controlled-prompting setup that found them).

### Causal intervention (Sec. 6)

- For each of the 100 gaze heads, inject an additive bias into the **pre-softmax attention mask** during both prefill and decoding: +δ on the target panel's image tokens, −δ on every other panel, with δ = +∞ (saturates bfloat16 softmax). Text-token attention untouched; nothing else about the model is modified.
- Intervention-strength sweep (Table 4): δ=1 → 33.2% (partial), δ=10 → 79.8%, δ=100 → 79.8%, δ=1,000 → 76.8%, δ=10,000 (≈ hard limit) → 83.1%. Effect saturates well before the hard limit; not an artifact.
- *Dynamic narration:* at decode-time only, switch the enforced target panel every T=50 tokens. The 300-token generation keeps the model's default "1, 2, 3..." numbering but describes the content of whichever panel the gaze heads are pointed at, wrapping up one description and opening the next at each switch.

### Tasks and evaluation

- **VQA (visual question answering).** Single question about the strip: "What is the main action or event happening in this comic strip? Answer briefly." Score whether the steered answer describes the chosen target panel rather than the full strip.
- **Static narration.** "What is happening in this panel of the comic strip?" (no panel specified); gaze heads held on a single target panel; score whether the answer resolves the ambiguity to the target panel rather than the model's default (first panel or whole-strip summary).
- **Dynamic narration.** Free generation with gaze target switched every 50 tokens; score via Spearman correlation between the steering schedule and the order panels are described.
- **Forced 1-of-6 LLM judge** (Claude Sonnet): given the strip image and the steered text, picks the single panel the answer best matches. Junk and unmatchable outputs count as misses; chance is exactly 1/6.
- 95% bootstrap confidence intervals (10,000 resamples) on all primary metrics.
- 3,000 (strip, target) pairs per condition (500 strips × 6 targets).

### Baselines

- **Image Heads (MaskCD, Deng & Yang 2025):** heads whose image-token attention is outlying within their layer. Re-implemented exactly on the same setting.
- **Localization Heads (Kang et al. 2025):** Criterion-1 picks heads whose image-attention sums lie above an elbow threshold; Criterion-2 computes the spatial entropy of each head's 2D attention map; layer-≤1 and bottom-row-focus filters kept as in the original. Algorithm ported exactly.
- **Non-gaze control:** 100 heads sampled uniformly from layers 20–35, excluding the gaze head set. Identical across all baseline-table rows for direct comparability.
- **All-heads:** same intervention applied to all 1,152 heads.

### Generalization experiments

- **Model scale:** Qwen3-VL-2B, 4B, 8B, 32B — same pipeline.
- **Architectures:** Qwen2-VL-7B, Ovis1.5-8B, InternVL3.5-8B, LLaVA-1.5-13B, LLaVA-NeXT-7B, Bunny-3B.
- **Natural images:** COCO val2017 — steer top-100 gaze heads to a target object's bounding box (pixel → image-token mapping via cell-center containment); ask "What is the main object in this part of the image? Answer in a few words."; LLM judge with synonym matching (e.g., "car" ~ "automobile"). Broken down by COCO object-size class.

## Results & Highlights

### Redirection on comic strips (Qwen3-VL-8B)

| Method | K | VQA acc | Narration acc |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gaze heads (ours)** | 100 | **83.1%** | **79.4%** |
| Image Heads (MaskCD) | 100 | 69.0% | ~60% |
| Localization Heads | 100 | 60.2% | ~58% |
| Non-gaze control | 100 | 14.6% | ~14% |
| All-heads | 1152 | 0.9% | (junk) |
| Chance | — | 16.7% | 16.7% |

- The gap is large and consistent: at K=100, gaze exceeds both baselines by 14.1pp (VQA) and 22.9pp (narration).
- **Head-set overlap is small.** Only 13 heads sit in all three top-100 sets; the top-10 sets are nearly disjoint. The three criteria are far from interchangeable — gaze's temporal-tracking signal captures something neither single-pass criterion does.

### Dynamic gaze switching (Spearman ρ with schedule)

| Condition | ρ vs. schedule |
|---|---|
| **Gaze heads (top-100)** | **+0.87** |
| Image Heads | +0.42 |
| Localization Heads | +0.33 |
| Random non-gaze | −0.15 (slightly anti-correlated; model falls back to default L→R scan, which the derangement schedule is built to oppose) |

- **Striking dissociation.** The model keeps its default "Panel 1, 2, 3..." textual numbering but describes the content of whichever panel the gaze heads are pointed at. Numbering follows the model's default textual structure; visual content follows the gaze heads — suggesting the two mechanisms (textual scaffolding vs visual grounding) are functionally separate.
- Trajectory-level rejudge (Table 8): Gaze 71.1% segment match vs. schedule, ρ=+0.589 (non-gaze ρ=−0.140, all-heads junk).

### Intervention-strength sweep (Qwen3-VL-8B, K=100, VQA)

| δ | Gaze | Non-gaze | Δ (pp) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 33.2% | 4.5% | 28.7 |
| 10 | 79.8% | 13.8% | 66.0 |
| 100 | 79.8% | 15.2% | 64.6 |
| 1,000 | 76.8% | 13.5% | 63.3 |
| 10,000 (≈ hard limit) | **83.1%** | 14.6% | 68.5 |

- Saturates from δ=10 onward; not an artifact of the +∞ limit.
- Non-gaze stays at 4–15% throughout the entire sweep — transition is in head-targeted reassignment, not "stronger intervention ⇒ higher accuracy."

### Top-K saturation curve (Qwen3-VL-8B, VQA)

| K | Gaze | Non-gaze |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | 36.0% | 6.6% |
| 10 | 45.3% | 10.0% |
| 50 | 63.2% | 15.3% |
| 75 | 76.3% | 13.5% |
| **100** | **83.1%** | 14.6% |
| 125 | 79.4% | 13.3% |
| 150 | 75.6% | 14.5% |
| 200 | 73.0% | 14.5% |

- Sharp peak at K=100, then graceful decline (not collapse) as the intervention overrides heads the model needs for fluent output.

### Cross-scale (Qwen3-VL family, peak gaze acc)

| Model | Peak K | Gaze | Non-Gaze | All-Heads |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Qwen3-VL-2B | 10 | 68.6% | 1.3% | 0.0% |
| Qwen3-VL-4B | 75 | 72.9% | 1.5% | 0.0% |
| Qwen3-VL-8B | 100 | **83.1%** | 14.6% | 0.9% |
| Qwen3-VL-32B | 500 | 70.2% | 12.0% | 0.2% |

- Mechanism is consistent across scales; the optimal K shifts with model size (2B→10, 8B→100, 32B→500).

### Cross-architecture

| Model | Peak K | Gaze | Non-Gaze | All-Heads |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ovis1.5-8B | 100 | 68.7% | 13.0% | 2.7% |
| Qwen2-VL-7B | 90 | 66.2% | 0.0% | 0.8% |
| InternVL3.5-8B | 140 | 62.7% | 31.0% | 0.8% |
| LLaVA-1.5-13B | 160 | 39.0% | 13.8% | 0.0% |
| LLaVA-NeXT-7B | 100 | 35.3% | 26.7% | 0.0% |
| Bunny-3B | 10 | 8.3% | 0.0% | 0.0% |

- All three **trained-encoder** families (≥60% peak) fine-tune their encoder on the VLM task.
- All three **frozen-encoder** families (LLaVA-1.5, LLaVA-NeXT, Bunny) plateau or fail. Hypothesis: gaze-head formation depends on joint vision-LM training.
- **Same-backbone contrast:** Bunny freezes the same SigLIP-so400m that Ovis fine-tunes, yet produces 8.3% vs. 68.7%. Strong suggestive evidence (not proof) for the trained-encoder hypothesis.

### Natural-image redirection (COCO val2017, Qwen3-VL-8B)

| Size class (px²) | Gaze heads | Non-gaze control |
|---|---|---|
| Large (>96²) | **80.3 ± 1.2%** | 36.6 ± 1.0% |
| Medium (<96²) | 76.2 ± 1.1% | 19.4 ± 0.7% |
| Small (27²–32²) | 61.7 ± 4.2% | 18.6 ± 4.0% |
| **Overall** | **76.5 ± 0.8%** | 25.9 ± 0.6% |

- Gaze more than doubles the non-gaze control in every size class.
- Performance drops on small objects whose bounding boxes cover only a handful of image tokens (natural attention bias dominates).
- Confirms the heads found through comic probing perform a general spatial-grounding function, not a comic-specific one.

### Prompt sensitivity (Qwen3-VL-8B, K=100, VQA)

| Prompt | Gaze | Non-gaze |
|---|---|---|
| "What is the main action or event happening in this comic strip? Answer briefly." (default) | **83.1%** | 14.6% |
| "Look at this comic strip carefully." | 80.3% | 7.3% |
| "Summarize the main event of this comic in a few words." | 78.3% | 16.2% |
| "What story does this comic strip tell? Answer briefly." | 74.2% | 13.3% |
| "Describe this comic briefly." | 64.7% | 13.3% |

- All five phrasings stay far above non-gaze; effect is robust to prompt wording.

### Mid-layer localization alignment

- Residual-stream "read-in-reverse" steering (Sec. 4): layers 20–28 sharply flip the predicted label; outside the band, no effect. Reverses the *order* in which the model describes the strip.
- Gaze score (Sec. 5): top-scoring heads concentrate in layers 20–28 — the same band — even though the search ranged over all 1,152 heads without restriction. Two independent probes converge on the same layer band.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** High. The "specific attention heads track the described image region, *and that tracking is causally sufficient to steer output*" finding, isolated cleanly via the comics testbed, is a genuinely new mechanistic handle for VLMs. The comics-as-testbed idea is elegant and underexploited in interpretability.
- **Rigor.** Strong. Three independent controls (random heads fail, all heads destroy, no intervention baseline) cleanly triangulate the effect. The comics testbed provides a *spatial* ground-truth gaze trace that most image-captioning datasets lack — a notable methodological contribution. Cross-architecture and cross-scale checks are the right next step. Intervention-strength sweep (δ=1 → 10 → ∞) de-risks the saturation-limit concern. Mid-layer localization via *residual-stream steering* converges with gaze-head localization at layers 20–28 — two independent probes agreeing is unusually strong.
- **Reproducibility.** Strong. Code, data, demo, baseline portings, and full prompt templates released. Hyperparameters (K=100, δ=+∞, T=50, max_new_tokens=15/100/300) are reported. Held-out 500-strip validation set is generated end-to-end with a released script — anyone can rebuild the dataset. One hardware config (single RTX A6000 48GB) is reported.
- **Significance.** High for interpretability *and* for practical inference-time steering. The "no retraining" lever is practically useful (safety, controllability, content moderation) and conceptually important (it argues for a more *causal* notion of "where the model is looking" inside VLMs). The result is also a clean existence proof that mechanistic analysis can identify *causal* (not just correlative) units in multimodal networks.
- **Red flags / things to watch.**
  - The headline 83.1% is on a very structured, panel-aligned testbed. Performance on natural images (COCO) is 76.5% overall but **drops to 61.7% on small objects** whose bounding boxes cover only a handful of image tokens — natural attention bias dominates. Practical users should not assume the 83.1% number carries over to small-region steering.
  - Detection of "currently described panel" relies on aligning generated tokens back to the comic's canonical reading order. This is a small but real circularity risk; the causal intervention partially de-risks it, but not fully (the intervention's *target* is defined by comic geometry, not by the model's actual gaze).
  - "K=100" is the empirical peak, but the K-sweep shows a sharp peak — accuracy drops to 75.6% at K=150 and 73.0% at K=200. This is presented as graceful decline but in practice means the effect is sensitive to the cutoff choice; users need to re-sweep K for new models/architectures.
  - The Bunny–Ovis same-backbone contrast is strong suggestive evidence for the trained-encoder hypothesis, but the authors explicitly call it a hypothesis, not proof. A fully controlled ablation (freeze vs fine-tune the *same* encoder on the *same* data) is left to future work.
  - Cost / latency of the intervention at deployment is not discussed. The intervention is at the pre-softmax attention mask, which should be cheap, but a quantitative claim is missing.
  - No comparison to non-mechanistic steering methods (activation steering, contrastive decoding, prompt engineering, RLHF). It's not clear when gaze-head intervention is the right tool vs a simpler alternative. (The closest comparison is against Image Heads and Localization Heads ported into the same intervention, which is a strong comparison *within* the attention-edit family but not against the broader steering literature.)
  - The LLM judge (Claude Sonnet) is itself a non-trivial dependency. Forced 1-of-6 with junk detection helps, but the judge could in principle be exploited by adversarial text. The paper uses bootstrap CIs but does not report inter-judge agreement.
  - The gaze-score formula's mean-of-diagonal is a very specific choice. A head that attends to panel 1 *and* panel 2 equally whenever asked about either would have off-diagonal mass and a low score. Whether such a head is "non-gaze" or just has a different operational mode is underexplored.

## Strengths

- Elegant, low-cost discovery (simple correlation score, few forward passes).
- Causal evidence is unusually clean (random-heads-fail / all-heads-destroy / no-intervention-baseline).
- Clean, practical steering primitive — single attention-mask edit, no retraining.
- Mid-layer localization via two *independent* probes (residual stream + gaze score) converging on the same layer band is strong evidence.
- Cross-architecture and cross-scale validation; extension from comics to natural images.
- Same-backbone contrast (Bunny vs Ovis) as a clean natural experiment for the trained-encoder hypothesis.
- Intervention-strength, top-K, and prompt-sensitivity ablations — claims are not overfitted to a single hyperparameter.
- Head-set overlap analysis showing the three criteria (gaze, Image Heads, Localization Heads) are not interchangeable — a useful contribution to the VLM-interpretability literature.
- Full public release (code + demo + datasets + generator scripts) — exemplary for the area.
- Comics-as-testbed is a reusable methodological idea for any "where is the model looking" study.

## Limitations

- Comic testbed is highly structured; natural-image numbers are good (76.5% overall) but degrade on small objects (61.7%) and the practical implications for small-region steering are unclear.
- Detection of "currently described" panel relies on aligning generated text back to canonical reading order — a mild circularity that the causal intervention only partially addresses.
- The K=100 peak is sharp — accuracy drops 8–10pp at K=150 and K=200. Users must re-sweep K for new models.
- Bunny–Ovis contrast is suggestive but not a fully controlled ablation; the trained-encoder hypothesis remains unproven.
- Not all VLM families exhibit the mechanism (LLaVA, Bunny); structural conditions that produce gaze heads are not fully characterized.
- No discussion of compute / latency cost of the intervention at deployment.
- No comparison to broader steering methods (activation steering, contrastive decoding, prompt engineering, RLHF) — within-attention-edit comparison is strong, cross-family comparison is absent.
- LLM judge (Claude Sonnet) is a non-trivial dependency; inter-judge agreement not reported.
- The gaze-score formula may miss heads with symmetric multi-region attention patterns that are still functionally gaze-relevant.

## Relevance

高度匹配用户关注的多模态模型可解释性与可控生成方向：本文既是 VLM 内部机制的因果级证据（"看哪儿—说哪儿"具象化为可识别、可编辑的注意力头），也是一种无需重训练即可在推理时改写多模态行为的实用杠杆。其"冻结编码器 vs 联合训练"假设对当前 VLM 架构选型（尤其 LLaVA 家族）有直接的方法论含义，可被安全/可控/幻觉抑制等下游任务借鉴。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does gaze-head intervention compose with other inference-time steering methods (activation steering, contrastive decoding, prompt engineering)? Is it additive, redundant, or interfering — and when is gaze-head intervention the right tool vs a simpler alternative?
- Can the Bunny–Ovis same-backbone contrast be turned into a fully controlled ablation (freeze vs fine-tune the *same* encoder on the *same* data, holding all else fixed)? This would directly test the trained-encoder hypothesis.
- Does gaze-head steering help with **hallucination correction** — i.e., force the model to look at the right region when it is confidently wrong — and what is the failure mode when the model has no gaze alignment to any region (the "guessing" diagnostic signal)?
- How does the intervention scale to **video VLMs** (where "where is the model looking?" has a temporal axis as well as a spatial one)? Are gaze heads per-frame, per-clip, or persistent?
- What is the relationship between gaze heads and **emergent visual-symbolic indexing** (Assouel et al. 2025) or the mid-layer cross-modal transfer band (Basu et al. 2024, Neo et al. 2025)? Are gaze heads a refinement or a different cut of the same phenomenon?
- Can the gaze-score definition be generalized to *symmetric multi-region* heads (heads that split attention across two regions when describing one)? Would such heads be functionally gaze-relevant but currently missed?
- What is the intervention's cost / latency profile in deployment, and can it be approximated with a cheaper proxy (e.g., a learned gate that decides which heads to redirect)?
- For VLM safety, can gaze-head intervention be used to *prevent* the model from describing a sensitive region (negative steering), and is the intervention reversible / per-token / per-region?
- Does the mechanism extend to **audio-language** or **embodied** VLMs where "where is the model attending" has a non-spatial meaning?
- The dissociation between textual scaffolding ("1, 2, 3...") and visual content (steered) is striking — can this be exploited to study *which* textual templates the model commits to before the gaze head intervenes, and what determines the order of operations?
