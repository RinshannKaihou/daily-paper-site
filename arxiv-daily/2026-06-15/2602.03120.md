> **一句话总结：** 提出 QES（Quantized Evolution Strategies），在量化离散权重空间内直接做零阶全参数微调；通过**累积误差反馈**（受 Delta-Sigma 调制启发，让亚格步长的更新信号跨步积累）保持高精度更新方向，并通过**无状态种子回放**将优化器状态显存从 O(d) 压缩到 ≈29.7 KB，使总显存与低精度推理持平；在 Countdown / GSM8K / SFT 任务上一致大幅优于现有量化零阶基线 QuZO。

> **TL;DR:** QES fine-tunes quantized LLMs **directly in their discrete weight space** without dequantization, backprop, or FP optimizer state. It addresses two specific failure modes of prior ZO methods on quantized weights — (i) **gradient stagnation** when ‖αĝ‖∞ < 1 (the update vanishes into the rounding threshold) and (ii) **discretization-induced noise** that drowns out subtle fine-tuning signals — by carrying forward an FP16 residual error vector à la **Delta-Sigma modulation**, so the *virtual continuous parameter* Θₜ = Wₜ + eₜ follows the ideal high-precision gradient ascent trajectory. To keep this residual from blowing up VRAM, the paper introduces **stateless seed replay**: only a small (seed, reward) history of the past K ≈ 20–50 steps is stored; the residual is re-simulated on the fly. The result matches QuZO's inference-only memory footprint while delivering +10 to +75 point accuracy gains on reasoning and SFT benchmarks (up to Llama-3.1-8B INT4: GSM8K 64.14% → 82.64%).

## 核心贡献

- **首次在量化离散权重空间内做全参数零阶微调**，无需反量化、无需全精度影子模型、无需反向传播。
- **累积误差反馈（Delta-Sigma 风格）**：把小于格距的更新信号在 FP16 残差 `eₜ` 中跨步积累，直到跨越舍入阈值才触发整数权重更新 —— 直接消除量化零阶梯度的**停滞**和**离散化噪声**两个失效模式。
- **无状态种子回放**：仅保存过去 K 步的 (种子, 奖励) 历史（≈29.7 KB），通过回放重建残差；优化器状态显存从 O(d) 降到 O(K)，匹配低精度推理的显存占用。
- **时序等价性理论**：证明虚拟连续参数 Θₜ = Wₜ + eₜ 严格遵循无约束高精度梯度上升轨迹；物理量化权重与 Θₜ 的偏差被格距 Δ/2 严格界定 —— 为方法有效性提供数学保证。
- **实验上显著超越 SOTA**：在 RoBERTa-large W8 SFT 上比 QuZO 平均高 10.2 点，在 Qwen2.5-1.5B/3B INT4/INT8/W8A8 的 Countdown 与 GSM8K 上大幅领先，并在 Llama-3.1-8B INT4 上把 GSM8K 从 64.14% 提升到 82.64%。

## Key Contributions

- **Quantized-space full-parameter fine-tuning without dequantization or backprop**, removing the "PTQ models are dead artifacts" pain point for memory-constrained practitioners.
- **Accumulated error feedback** (analogous to Delta-Sigma modulation / 1-bit SGD error feedback) bridges discrete and continuous optimization: the residual `eₜ` carries the fractional update across iterations so infinitesimal signals accumulate until they cross the rounding threshold.
- **Stateless seed replay** keeps optimizer state at ≈29.7 KB regardless of model size, trading K extra forward-equivalent reconstructions for memory — total VRAM equals the inference-only quantized baseline.
- **Temporal-equivalence theorem**: defining virtual continuous parameters Θₜ = Wₜ + eₜ, the proof shows Θₜ obeys the ideal gradient-ascent dynamics exactly, and the physical quantized Wₜ deviates from Θₜ by at most Δ/2 (the grid resolution). This is a clean, falsifiable theoretical contribution.
- **Strong empirical results**: SFT on RoBERTa-large (W8) and reasoning on Qwen2.5-{1.5B, 3B} × {INT4, INT8, W8A8} × {Countdown, GSM8K}, plus a scaling case study on Llama-3.1-8B INT4.

## Methodology

### 1. Zeroth-order gradient estimator in discrete space

Standard Evolution Strategies estimate a smoothed gradient ĝ = (1/Nσ) Σᵢ Fᵢ·εᵢ where εᵢ ~ N(0, I) is a continuous perturbation. QES replaces this with **discrete perturbations** built by **stochastic rounding** of σε:

> δᵢ = ⌊σε⌋ + b,  b ~ Bernoulli(σε − ⌊σε⌋)

Seeds for ε and b are recorded so δ can be regenerated from the seed alone (the foundation of seed replay). A **boundary gate** masks perturbations that would push a weight outside the {0, …, 2ᴮ−1}ⁿ codebook. The discrete gradient estimator is ĝ = (1/Nσ) Σᵢ Fᵢ·δᵢ.

### 2. Accumulated error feedback (the Delta-Sigma trick)

For small learning rates typical of fine-tuning, ‖αĝ‖∞ < 1, so naively rounding the update gives ∆W = 0 → **stagnation**. The fix:

> uₜ = α·ĝₜ + γ·eₜ₋₁
> ∆Wₜ = Round(uₜ)
> eₜ = uₜ − ∆Wₜ

The FP16 residual `eₜ` (initialized to 0) carries the fractional update across iterations. Decay γ ∈ (0, 1] prevents stale errors from dominating. Eventually the accumulated signal crosses |u| ≥ 0.5 and a discrete weight change fires.

**Theoretical guarantee (temporal equivalence).** Define Θₜ ≜ Wₜ + eₜ. Then by the update equations:

> Θₜ₊₁ = Wₜ₊₁ + eₜ₊₁ = (Wₜ + ∆Wₜ) + (uₜ − ∆Wₜ) = Wₜ + uₜ = Wₜ + (αĝₜ + eₜ) = Θₜ + αĝₜ

So Θₜ is exactly a continuous gradient-ascent trajectory. The physical weight's error ‖eT‖∞ ≤ Δ/2 is bounded by the grid resolution — a strong, elegant guarantee.

### 3. Stateless seed replay (the memory trick)

Storing `eₜ ∈ ℝᵈ` (FP16) costs more VRAM than the quantized model itself (e.g., +2.44 GB for 1.5B, +5.17 GB for 3B — see Table 8), defeating quantization's purpose. Solution: **don't store** `eₜ`. Instead store only a history buffer H = {(Sₜ, Fₜ)} for the past K steps (≈29.7 KB total). At update time, replay the accumulation loop from an assumed-zero error at step t−K; the decay factor γ < 1 ensures γ^K ≈ 0 makes this truncation accurate. Boundary gating during replay uses the *current* weights Wₜ rather than reconstructed Wτ — this introduces only negligible error because (i) updates per step are sparse (≈10⁻² fraction of params) and (ii) boundary-hit ratio ρ is < 10⁻⁵ for INT4.

The compute–memory trade-off is favorable: K ≈ 20–50 with γ = 0.9; K = 20 recovers ≈ K = 50 accuracy within 1–2 points while reducing reconstruction cost by 60%.

### 4. Complexity & cost

- **Memory**: optimizer state O(K) ≈ constant; total VRAM = inference-only baseline (e.g., 1.07 GB for Qwen-1.5B INT4 vs 3.51 GB for the Full-Residual variant).
- **Compute**: K extra reconstructions per update; total training time increases by ≈12.5–16.7% vs the Full-Residual oracle on A100s. Wall-clock vs QuZO is not directly reported but the analysis is honest about the K-doubling cost.

## Results & Highlights

### Supervised Fine-Tuning (RoBERTa-large, Table 1)

| Method | Precision | SNLI | MNLI | RTE | SST-5 | **Avg** |
|--------|-----------|------|------|-----|-------|---------|
| First-Order | FP32 (upper bound) | 72.9 | 61.1 | 49.0 | 46.2 | **57.3** |
| MeZO | FP32 | 34.0 | 34.0 | 56.2 | 21.7 | 36.5 |
| First-Order + STE | W8 | 50.0 | 44.4 | 49.0 | 20.4 | 41.0 |
| QuZO | W8 | 32.3 | 40.3 | 44.8 | 19.6 | 34.2 |
| **QES** | W8 | **55.6** | **42.4** | **55.2** | **24.4** | **44.4** |

- QES beats QuZO by **+10.2 avg**, beats FP32 MeZO by **+7.9 avg**, and beats W8 first-order + STE by **+3.4 avg** — closing most of the gap to FP32 first-order (57.3) without backprop.
- Particularly striking: QES-W8 on SNLI (55.6) is competitive with FP32 MeZO on RTE-like tasks.

### Reasoning (Qwen2.5-{1.5B, 3B}, Table 2)

| Model | Format | Countdown Base | Countdown QuZO | **Countdown QES** | GSM8K Base | GSM8K QuZO | **GSM8K QES** |
|-------|--------|----------------|----------------|-------------------|------------|------------|----------------|
| 1.5B | INT4 | 3.50 | 5.25 | **16.00** | 0.00 | 0.00 | **9.86** |
| 1.5B | INT8 | 4.20 | 4.50 | **26.35** | 1.59 | 1.44 | **12.21** |
| 1.5B | W8A8 | 4.20 | 4.20 | **15.35** | 3.56 | 4.17 | **12.28** |

- Consistent gains everywhere. **The most striking case is Qwen-3B W8A8 GSM8K**: QuZO *collapses* to 4.40% (worse than the 24.49% base!), while QES reaches 80.82%. This highlights that prior ZO methods can actively *degrade* a quantized base model — an important failure mode QES fixes.

### Scaling case study (Llama-3.1-8B INT4, Appendix C)

- QES (with Qwen-3B hyperparameters reused as-is) pushes GSM8K from 64.14% → **82.64%** (+18.5 points). Reusing hyperparameters from a smaller/different model is a pragmatic signal that the method is robust, though it also means the comparison at 8B is not tuned for that model.

### Memory & compute (Table 8)

- QES optimizer state: **≈29.7 KB** across all configs.
- Full-Residual variant adds **+2.44 GB (1.5B)** / **+5.17 GB (3B)** for FP16 residuals.
- Standard QAT on a 3B model: **>24 GB** — ~13× QES footprint at INT4.
- Wall-clock overhead of seed replay: **+12.5–16.7%** over the no-replay oracle on A100s.

### Ablations

- **Stateless replay vs Full-Residual oracle**: within a few points in 5/6 configs (the outlier is Qwen-3B W8A8 Countdown: 21.35 vs 31.70 — but the same config on GSM8K is within 0.76 pts).
- **Replay window K**: K = 20 with fixed γ = 0.9 retains 14.75% vs K = 50's 16.00% on Qwen-1.5B INT4 Countdown; K = 10 with scaled decay collapses to 4.55% (showing aggressive γ, not truncation, is the failure cause).
- **Boundary hit ratio**: < 10⁻⁵ for INT4 — empirical justification that current-weight gating during replay is safe.

## Critical Assessment

### Novelty: moderate-to-high
The **temporal-equivalence theorem** (Θₜ = Wₜ + eₜ obeys continuous gradient ascent exactly) is a clean, novel theoretical contribution that goes beyond mechanical combination of existing ideas. The two engineering tricks (accumulated error feedback from 1-bit SGD; stateless seed replay) are individually known but their integration for **quantized** ZO fine-tuning — and the resulting guarantees — is genuinely new.

### Rigor: mixed
- **Theoretical side**: clean. The temporal-equivalence proof is short, self-contained, and yields a tight bound (‖eT‖∞ ≤ Δ/2).
- **Empirical side**: reasonable but with notable gaps:
  - **Missing the obvious practical baseline**: QLoRA / LoRA on a dequantized copy is the de-facto "fine-tune a quantized LLM" recipe and is *not* in the comparison table. The paper motivates against it ("restricts updates to low-rank subspace") but does not run it.
  - **Llama-3.1-8B is a single point with reused hyperparameters** (Qwen-3B's). That's pragmatic but not a true scaling study.
  - **No comparison to FP32 full first-order fine-tuning on the same hardware budget**, only as an upper-bound oracle.
  - **No statistical reporting** — single-seed numbers; ES methods have notoriously high seed variance, and the paper itself notes "task- and seed-specific" discrepancies in ablations.
  - **The Qwen-3B W8A8 GSM8K result (QuZO → 4.40% from 24.49% base) deserves more investigation** — QuZO *regressing* below the base is suspicious and could indicate a hyperparameter mismatch for QuZO, which would inflate the apparent QES advantage.

### Reproducibility: good
- Open-source code at https://github.com/dibbla/Quantized-Evolution-Strategies.
- Hyperparameters mostly in the paper (γ = 0.9, K = 20–50, N population size mentioned in algorithm but not exhaustively tabulated).
- GPTQ and LLM-Compressor are standard, reproducible pipelines.

### Significance: high if claims hold
If real (i.e., if QES actually lets practitioners fine-tune quantized 7B+ LLMs on consumer GPUs at inference-level memory), this is a meaningful contribution to LLM accessibility. The "1/12 of backprop memory" claim combined with 4× more params via INT4 is a 1–2 OOM improvement narrative.

### Red flags
- ES sample efficiency is famously poor; the paper does not report **total forward passes** or compare wall-clock to LoRA / full FT at matched accuracy.
- Reasoning tasks (Countdown, GSM8K) are the strong-suit showcase; broader generalization (alignment with human preferences, code, multilingual) is not tested.
- The "scale up" claim at the end is purely aspirational — no experiment trains a *larger* model from scratch in quantized space; all results are fine-tuning existing models.
- K and γ are tunable hyperparameters (the paper says so explicitly); default values were tuned on Qwen-1.5B INT4 Countdown and may not transfer cleanly.

## Strengths

- **Clean theory**: the temporal-equivalence argument is tight and falsifiable.
- **Practical engineering**: stateless seed replay delivers a real, measurable memory win (29.7 KB vs GB-scale residuals) — Table 8 is convincing.
- **Strong empirical gains over QuZO** across all 6 (model × format) configs in Table 2 — not a cherry-picked subset.
- **Headline result** (Llama-3.1-8B INT4: 64.14 → 82.64 on GSM8K) is genuinely striking.
- **Honest ablations**: the Full-Residual vs stateless-replay comparison is treated as a fairness check, not hidden; the one outlier (Qwen-3B W8A8 Countdown) is flagged.
- **Open-source release** with a memorable URL.

## Limitations

- **No comparison to QLoRA** (the most common practical alternative). This is the biggest empirical gap.
- **Only tested on ≤8B models**; the headline scale-up narrative ("1–2 OOM more params") is not validated by experiment.
- **Only linear integer (INT4/INT8/W8A8) quantization**; no FP4, ternary, binary, or non-uniform codebooks.
- **ES sample efficiency is unaddressed**: no forward-pass count, no wall-clock vs LoRA / full FT at matched accuracy.
- **K and γ are manual hyperparameters**; the paper explicitly says so. Fixed-decay γ = 0.9 is robust, but smaller models / different formats may need retuning.
- **Single-seed results**; ES methods can have large seed variance, especially with population N.
- **Reasoning-heavy benchmark selection**: Countdown + GSM8K are both reward-sparse, verifiable-answer tasks that may favor QES's error-feedback mechanism over RLHF-style preference learning.

## Relevance

直接命中"大模型高效微调 / 量化部署 / 边缘端 LLM 自适应"核心方向，与用户在 RL for LLMs / 高效参数化方向的兴趣高度契合。QES 把"量化模型无法微调"这一长期痛点转化为可在低精度推理显存预算内完成的任务，并在 8B 规模验证了 GSM8K 上的可用性，对消费级硬件适配 LLM 的方向有明确的实践价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- **Head-to-head with QLoRA / LoRA on a dequantized copy at the same bit-width and matched wall-clock budget** — the obvious missing baseline. Will QES still win on accuracy-per-GPU-hour?
- **What is the total forward-pass / sample cost** to reach QES's reported accuracies? How does this compare to LoRA / full FT at matched final accuracy?
- **Does the temporal-equivalence guarantee extend to non-uniform / FP4 / binary quantization**? The proof assumes a uniform lattice; codebooks with irregular spacing may break the ‖eT‖∞ ≤ Δ/2 bound.
- **Scaling test on a true frontier-scale model** (e.g., 70B INT4) with properly tuned (rather than reused) K, γ, and N. Does the 1–2 OOM scale-up claim hold?
- **RLHF-style preference alignment** (DPO, PPO) using QES — the current paper only does SFT and verifiable-reward RL (Countdown, GSM8K). Does QES handle noisy, non-binary preference rewards as well?
- **Combine with first-order adapters** — e.g., use QES to fine-tune quantized base weights while LoRA adapters on top handle task-specific structure. Could this reduce the population N needed for ES?
- **Adaptive K, γ scheduling** based on observed update ratio and boundary-hit ratio (the two quantities QES already measures). The paper identifies this as future work; a convergence-based scheduler could remove the manual tuning burden.
- **Sensitivity to ES perturbation scale σ** at very low bit-widths (2-bit, ternary). The error-feedback mechanism should make the method robust to σ, but this is unverified.
- **Interaction with activation quantization (W8A8)** — the Qwen-3B W8A8 result is the most extreme gain over QuZO; is this because activation noise interacts with ES estimation in a way that error feedback specifically handles?
