## Results & Highlights

Experiments are conducted on three MoE models (DeepSeek-V2-Lite, Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B, OLMoE-1B-7B-0924) across 20 benchmarks spanning NLU, Reasoning, and Math tasks:

- **Alloc-MoE outperforms all baselines in 10 of 12 settings** on DeepSeek, with advantages growing under aggressive budget constraints and harder tasks (0.05% NLU, 0.70% Reasoning, 2.15% Math improvement).
- **Efficiency**: On DeepSeek-V2-Lite at half budget, Alloc-MoE achieves 1.15x prefill and 1.34x decode speedup with no additional runtime overhead.
- **Ablation** confirms both Alloc-L and Alloc-T contribute independently and complementarily; their combination consistently achieves the best results.
- **Expert load analysis** shows Alloc-MoE preserves the original expert utilization pattern (Spearman correlation 0.93-0.99, JS divergence < 0.014).
- **Calibration robustness**: Performance is insensitive to the choice of calibration dataset (WikiText2, C4, Pile all yield nearly identical results).

## Critical Assessment

This is a solid systems-for-ML paper with a clean problem formulation and a practical solution. The core contribution -- treating expert activations as a budget to be optimized at two granularities -- is natural and well-motivated. The DP formulation for layer allocation is straightforward but effective, and the token-level redistribution is cleverly designed to require zero additional computation. However, the improvements are modest in many settings, and the lack of distributed evaluation leaves the most practically relevant speedup claims unsubstantiated. The paper would be stronger with results on larger models and multi-GPU benchmarks, where the communication reduction from fewer expert activations would be more significant.
