Key Contributions

Methodology

This survey develops a unified analytical framework for LLM post-training structured around three layers:

  • Trajectory provenance (top-level axis): Off-policy learning operates on externally supplied trajectories (demonstrations, preference pairs, verified traces), while on-policy learning operates on learner-generated rollouts evaluated by rewards, verifiers, or teacher feedback.
  • Supervision interfaces (secondary layer): Token targets, pairwise preferences, reward-derived signals, verifier/process feedback, and teacher-guided targets describe how learning signals attach to trajectories.
  • Functional roles (explanatory lens): Using a state-action occupancy measure formulation, the authors define (1) effective support expansion --- making previously negligible but useful state-action regions practically reachable; (2) policy reshaping --- redistributing probability mass within already reachable regions; and (3) behavioral consolidation --- preserving, transferring, or amortizing behavior across stage boundaries.

The survey systematically maps major post-training paradigms onto this framework: SFT can serve either support expansion or reshaping depending on data coverage; preference optimization is typically off-policy reshaping; on-policy RL (RLHF/RLVR) is usually learner-state reshaping with selective expansion under strong guidance; distillation is best understood as consolidation rather than mere compression; and hybrid pipelines emerge as coordinated multi-stage compositions addressing sequential bottlenecks.

Critical Assessment

Strengths:

  • The framework is genuinely unifying: it provides a principled alternative to fragmented method-family taxonomies and makes clear why hybrid pipelines dominate in practice.
  • The formalization of effective support on the state-action space, grounded in occupancy measures, offers a rigorous vocabulary for behavioral diagnosis.
  • The survey is comprehensive in scope, covering SFT, preference optimization, RLHF/RLVR, process supervision, distillation, and multi-stage pipelines within a single analytical language.
  • The discussion of open problems is honest and well-structured, correctly identifying bottleneck diagnosis, interface choice, transferability, and mechanistic explanation as central unresolved questions.

Weaknesses:

  • The framework is primarily conceptual and organizational rather than empirical. No new experiments validate the proposed role assignments (e.g., measuring whether a specific SFT stage is "expansion-dominant" vs. "reshaping-dominant" on a given task). The operational heuristics in Table 2 remain qualitative.
  • Effective support, while formally defined, lacks a practical measurement protocol. The authors acknowledge this gap but do not offer even a proxy metric beyond referencing recent pass@k work.
  • The distinction between "support expansion" and "policy reshaping" can be subtle in autoregressive settings where local action changes have recursive downstream consequences --- the authors note this boundary case but the framework does not fully resolve it.
  • The survey gives relatively little attention to the growing body of work on inference-time scaling and test-time compute, which increasingly interacts with post-training design in frontier systems.
  • Some classifications feel imposed rather than discovered: asserting that distillation is "consolidation-dominant" is a useful reframe but may understate cases where distillation genuinely expands student capability beyond what the student could otherwise reach.

Relevance

本综述对LLM后训练研究具有高度参考价值。它为理解和设计多阶段后训练流水线提供了统一的术语和分析框架，尤其有助于：

  • 诊断模型性能瓶颈是源于缺乏可达行为还是已可达行为的选择不当；
  • 理解为何前沿系统（DeepSeek-R1、GLM-5等）普遍采用SFT->RL->蒸馏的多阶段组合；
  • 指导后续研究关注瓶颈诊断、监督接口选择、跨阶段保持等系统性问题。

对于从事LLM对齐、推理增强或模型压缩的研究者和工程师，这篇综述提供了一个清晰的概念地图。
