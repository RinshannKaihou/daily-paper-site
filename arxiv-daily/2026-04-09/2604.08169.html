Methodology

The authors frame alignment recovery as a projection geometry problem. Steering vectors are extracted via logistic regression on contrastive activations: aligned and malicious system prompts generate on-policy response pairs, whose response-averaged hidden states at each layer are classified to obtain both a direction and a calibrated decision boundary. Three methods are compared:

  • SwFC (Steer-With-Fixed-Coeff): Baseline uniform additive steering that applies a scaled vector to all tokens at a chosen layer, in the style of Contrastive Activation Addition (CAA).
  • StTP: Tokens whose projection onto the steering direction falls below the logistic regression decision boundary are shifted to a target value within the positive (aligned) distribution. Already-aligned tokens are left untouched.
  • StMP: Tokens below the decision boundary are reflected across it by an interpolation factor, offering a softer correction than StTP.

The evaluation uses malicious system prompts as a controlled proxy for misalignment. For dishonesty, 90 training scenarios across eight categories (including sycophancy) pair contrastive system prompts. For dismissiveness, 50 emotionally challenging prompts elicit compassionate vs. dismissive responses. Testing uses 40 held-out scenarios per threat. The LLM-as-judge is GPT-oss-120B, scoring trait expression and coherence independently on 0--100 scales.

Results & Highlights

Single-turn: All three methods restore target traits to near-aligned levels. For honesty, trait scores recover from ~7 (malicious) to ~84--88 (steered), compared to ~88 (aligned baseline). For compassion, scores rise from ~28 to ~71--78, versus ~78 baseline. Coherence is preserved at ~90--94 across methods at optimal layers.

Capability preservation: StTP and StMP maintain MMLU, MT-Bench, and AlpacaEval scores close to the unsteered baseline across the full coefficient range. SwFC shows noticeable degradation at its operating-point coefficients and is brittle to small hyperparameter changes.

Per-token dynamics: Trajectory analysis reveals that SwFC overshoots for already-aligned tokens (a structural consequence of uniform steering), while StTP and StMP adapt perturbation magnitude per token, correcting only misaligned activations. This validates the core design motivation.

Multi-turn: Over 5--10 turn self-play conversations, all methods maintain trait expression, but SwFC amplifies text repetition substantially (sentence reuse reaching ~85% by turn 9 for compassion), while StTP (~60%) and StMP (~40%) stay closer to the aligned baseline's natural repetition rate (~45%). SwFC's coherence also degrades over turns (from 90 to 70 for compassion).

Cross-architecture: Results replicate on Qwen3-32B, though optimal layers shift from ~29--43% depth (Llama) to ~64--70% depth (Qwen), confirming architecture-dependent layer selection. Qwen shows a pronounced verbosity explosion under SwFC (token counts reaching 647 vs. ~372 baseline), which StTP and StMP avoid.

Generalization: On the MASK benchmark (1,000 out-of-distribution scenarios), steering raises H@1 from 51.4 to 58.0--65.3, with strongest gains on disinformation (39 to 78) and more modest improvements on statistics and continuations.

Convergent validation: Embedding distance and cross-entropy analyses independently identify the same optimal layers as the LLM judge. Pairwise ELO rankings confirm the coefficient ordering from absolute scores.

Critical Assessment

Strengths: The paper addresses a real and important problem -- the brittleness of LLM safety alignment -- with a principled approach. The projection-aware methods are elegantly simple: no learned gates, no optimization-based tuning, just a logistic regression boundary and distributional statistics. The multi-metric validation strategy (embedding distance, cross-entropy, ELO, capability benchmarks) is commendable and goes well beyond the typical single-metric evaluation in this area. The multi-turn evaluation protocol is a genuine methodological contribution that exposes the repetition amplification problem of uniform steering.

Weaknesses: The evaluation is limited to a single misalignment source (malicious system prompts). Whether these methods generalize to adversarial suffixes, fine-tuning-induced misalignment, or emergent misalignment remains untested -- the authors acknowledge this but it significantly limits the practical scope. The approach requires white-box access, excluding API-only deployments. The linear representation assumption may not hold for all safety-relevant features (as Engels et al. 2025 show non-linear structure exists). The dishonesty threat model's effectiveness varies by architecture (81-point gap on Llama vs. 60-point on Qwen), raising questions about whether results would hold under subtler misalignment with smaller activation gaps. The MASK generalization results are uneven -- strong on in-distribution categories but weak on statistics (36 to 41), suggesting the steering vectors capture dataset-specific patterns rather than general honesty structure.

Concerns about the proxy: Using malicious system prompts as a proxy for real-world misalignment is a controlled but somewhat artificial setup. In practice, misalignment may manifest through more subtle distributional shifts that do not produce the clean linear separability observed here (Cohen's d values are very large for the dismissiveness threat). The authors' own discussion notes that Joad et al. (2026) found different non-compliance types involve geometrically distinct directions, suggesting a single steering vector may be insufficient for complex threat landscapes.

Relevance

本工作对 LLM 安全部署具有直接参考价值。StTP/StMP 的"仅在需要时干预"策略为推理时安全防御提供了一种轻量级、可部署的方案，特别适合需要持续监控对齐状态的生产环境。对于研究者而言，多轮评估协议和多重验证方法论值得借鉴。然而，从恶意系统提示到真实世界中多样化的失调场景之间的泛化差距仍是关键挑战。在将激活引导部署为独立防御层之前，需要在更多失调来源上进行验证。
