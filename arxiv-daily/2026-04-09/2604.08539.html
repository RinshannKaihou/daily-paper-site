Methodology

Problem Statement

The paper addresses two key challenges in applying Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) to multi-task multimodal RL post-training:

  1. Extreme variance in reward topologies across diverse visual tasks (e.g., sparse binary rewards in math VQA vs. dense continuous IoU scores in grounding tasks), which creates inter-task gradient imbalance and makes GRPO susceptible to gradient explosion.
  2. Balancing fine-grained perception with multi-step reasoning, where reasoning tasks benefit from long chain-of-thought but perception tasks suffer from hallucinated reasoning steps.

Critical Assessment

Strengths

  1. Principled formulation. G2RPO's use of 1D Optimal Transport to map advantage distributions to N(0,1) is elegant and theoretically well-motivated. The closed-form CDF-based solution is computationally cheap, and the guarantee of zero-mean/unit-variance advantages across all tasks is a genuine improvement over linear scaling methods like EMA-GRPO.
  1. Comprehensive evaluation. Testing across 18 benchmarks spanning 6 task categories is thorough. The comparison against both strong open-source models (OneThinker, VL-Rethinker, ARES) and proprietary frontier models (GPT-4o, GPT-5, Gemini 2.5 Pro) provides useful positioning.
  1. Practical training recipe. The method requires only ~3 days on AWS Trainium with a single epoch, uses existing training data (OneThinker-600k), and introduces no additional modules. This is a relatively compute-efficient contribution compared to methods requiring visual augmentation or auxiliary models.
  1. Good ablation design. The ablation cleanly isolates G2RPO, entropy shaping, and length shaping, showing each component contributes meaningfully.
