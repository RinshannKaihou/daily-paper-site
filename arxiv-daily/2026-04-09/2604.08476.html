Methodology

Problem Identification

The authors train a standard GRPO model with task reward (GRPO-T) on Qwen2.5-VL-7B using a two-stage pipeline (SFT followed by RL). Despite achieving 65.2% average accuracy, GRPO-T exhibits:

  • 26.1% inconsistency rate: over one in four samples have reasoning that contradicts the final prediction.
  • 72.7% semantic grounding score: over a quarter of visual claims are inaccurate.

Two failure modes are identified:

  • Logical Inconsistency: reasoning argues toward one conclusion but the model flips to a different final answer.
  • Visual Ungroundedness: reasoning steps describe objects, attributes, or spatial relationships that are inaccurate with respect to the image.

Strengths

  • The paper identifies a genuinely important problem: reasoning quality degradation in RLVR-trained models is pervasive yet underappreciated. The empirical evidence across seven benchmarks and five MRM baselines is compelling.
  • The constrained optimization formulation is principled and well-motivated. Treating consistency and grounding as hard constraints rather than soft rewards is a clean conceptual contribution.
  • The decoupled advantage normalization addresses a real technical issue (signal cancellation in GRPO's group normalization) with a practical solution.
  • Strong empirical results: FGRPO simultaneously improves accuracy AND reasoning quality, challenging the perceived tradeoff between the two.
  • The adaptive Lagrangian dual ascent removes the need for manual reward weight tuning, which is practically valuable.
