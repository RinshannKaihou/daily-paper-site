## Critical Assessment

**Strengths:**
- The theoretical analysis cleanly connects noise schedules to exposure bias through the stability threshold concept. The decomposition of REB into own-prediction bias and propagation residual is intuitive and empirically validated.
- The two-phase algorithm is principled and requires no more total training epochs than a single baseline run (2x the epochs, but the exploration phase becomes progressively cheaper).
- Strong empirical improvements, particularly the dramatic FSD improvement on Kolmogorov Flow.

**Weaknesses:**
- The stability threshold proof relies on two assumptions (Wiener denoiser structure and spectral bias) that do not hold exactly in practice. The authors acknowledge this but the gap between theory and practice remains.
- The method requires a two-stage training pipeline (exploration then construction), which adds complexity compared to simply choosing a schedule. The claim of "smart hyperparameter exploration" is reasonable but the method is still more involved than standard approaches.
- The paper does not address how the reconstruction error landscape might change with different denoiser parameterizations (x0-prediction, v-prediction), which could fundamentally alter the dynamics.
- The connection between the obtained minimal noise levels and physical properties of the data (e.g., energy spectrum) is observed but not fully explained.
