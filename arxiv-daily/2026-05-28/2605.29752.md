> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"性能崎岖度分析"框架，通过32K配置扫描揭示BF16 GEMM在Intel Battlemage上相邻尺寸可达30%吞吐差异的现象，并用动态分块选择与DP填充-拆分优化器将粗超度降低70%、平均吞吐提升30%，最终将残差归因于波量化、内核开销、DPAS原子几何与GDDR6通道哈希四个硬件因素。

> **TL;DR:** On Intel Battlemage (Arc B580), adjacent BF16 GEMM problems differing by a single 128-element step in N can show 30% throughput variation. This paper introduces "performance ruggedness analysis" as a complement to roofline models, performs a 32,768-configuration sweep revealing a mesa-shaped landscape with peak 110.8 TFLOPs but only 1.4% of configs exceeding 100 TFLOPs, and proposes a two-stage stack (best-of-six dynamic tile selection + novel DP-based padding-and-splitting optimizer with O(1) lookup) that reduces roughness by 70% and raises mean throughput by 30%. The residual 5.0 TFLOPs/step is fully attributed to four hardware-bound sources via cross-tile experiments that rule out cache set conflicts.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"性能崎岖度分析"（Performance Ruggedness Analysis）框架，将GPU性能视为多维崎岖曲面而非roofline标量上界，类比深度学习loss landscape
- 在Intel Battlemage (Arc B580)上完成32,768个BF16 NN GEMM配置的全网格扫描（M, N, K ∈ {128,...,4096}³），发现峰值110.8 TFLOPs但仅1.4%配置超过100 TFLOPs
- 四面体分解法（compute/memory/GEMM/overhead surfaces）精确归因性能纹理来源，发现32%的per-kernel基础开销
- 发现两个此前未被描述的测量伪影：共分配内存干扰（最高50%减速）和43% TLB/L3时间漂移，提出随机序扫描方法消除
- 设计两阶段优化栈：六种动态分块选择 + 基于动态规划的填充-拆分优化器（O(1)运行时查找，O(N^4)离线预计算）
- 粗超度从16.8降至5.0 TFLOPs/step（降低70%），平均吞吐从58.7提升至76.3 TFLOPs（+30%）
- 通过跨分块实验（64x64/128x128/256x256）证明锯齿周期精确跟踪软件分块大小，排除缓存集冲突，将残差归因于四个硬件因素

## Key Contributions

- Introduces "performance ruggedness analysis" as an analytical framework complementary to roofline, drawing analogy to deep-learning loss landscapes -- a continuous idealized quantity made rugged by discrete hardware substrates
- Performs a 32,768-configuration sweep of BF16 NN GEMM on Intel Battlemage (Arc B580, sycl-tla), revealing a mesa-shaped landscape: peak 110.8 TFLOPs at M=3840, N=2048, K=4096, but only 1.4% of configurations exceed 100 TFLOPs
- Four-surface decomposition (compute / memory / GEMM / overhead) attributes texture to specific subsystems, identifying a constant ~32% per-kernel base overhead across all configurations
- Discovers two previously undescribed measurement artifacts: co-allocation memory interference (up to 50% slowdown when reading co-allocated buffers) and 43% TLB/L3 temporal warmup drift; proposes randomized-order sweep methodology to eliminate both
- Designs a two-stage optimization stack: (i) best-of-six dynamic tile selection and (ii) a novel DP-based padding-and-splitting optimizer with O(1) runtime lookup (O(N^4) offline precomputation)
- Achieves 70% roughness reduction (16.8 to 5.0 TFLOPs/step) and 30% mean throughput improvement (58.7 to 76.3 TFLOPs)
- Cross-tile fine-N sweep experiments (64x64, 128x128, 256x256) prove the sawtooth period tracks software tile size exactly, ruling out cache set conflicts; attributes the full 5.0 TFLOPs/step residual to four hardware-bound sources

## Methodology

**Hardware and Software Stack:** Measurements on Intel Battlemage G21 (Arc B580) with 20 Xe-cores, 116.5 TFLOPs theoretical peak BF16, 12 MB L3 cache, 6 GDDR6 channels (~456 GB/s peak), using sycl-tla (CUTLASS SYCL port) with Intel oneAPI 2025.1. The default GEMM kernel uses 256x256x32 workgroup tiles with 8x4 sub-group layout and 8x16x16 DPAS atoms.

**Landscape Sweep:** 32,768 configurations at uniform 128-element step for (M, N, K) in {128,...,4096}^3. Each configuration measured with 5 kernel launches (first discarded as warmup). Within-configuration CV is 0.04-0.10%, confirmed via 50-run variance tests.

**Four-Surface Decomposition:** Measured over the same (M,N,K) grid: (1) compute surface (roofline ideal 2MNK/peak), (2) memory surface (same load/store pattern without DPAS compute), (3) GEMM surface (measured kernel time), (4) overhead surface (GEMM minus max of compute and memory). This attributes texture to specific subsystems.

**Randomized-Order Sweep:** Shuffles all 32,768 tuples to break spurious correlation between sweep run-order and problem dimensions, eliminating the 43% TLB/L3 warmup drift and co-allocation interference artifacts.

**Dynamic Tile Selection:** Compiles six tile variants (256x256, 128x128, 256x128, 128x256, 64x64, 64x128). At runtime, selects the fastest tile per (M,N,K) from a precomputed lookup table.

**DP Padding-and-Splitting Optimizer:** Three-phase algorithm: (1) benchmark T0[M][N][K] on GPU; (2) compute T1 by reverse-sweeping to find optimal padding (O(MNK)); (3) compute T2 by forward-sweeping to find optimal split-or-pad decisions along M, N, and K axes (O(N^4) for NxNxN grid). Runtime lookup is O(1). Splitting on K exposes more parallelism for compute-bound configs; splitting on N targets "bad-N" values.

**Cross-Tile Mechanism Test:** Fine-N sweep (step=32, N in [3000,4096]) across three tile sizes (64x64, 128x128, 256x256) at fixed M=K=4096 to distinguish partial-tile waste from cache set conflicts.

## Results & Highlights

**Performance Landscape:**
- Mesa-shaped surface with three regimes: launch-dominated (<10^8 volume, <5 TFLOPs, 17% of configs), scaling (10^8-10^10, 5-80 TFLOPs, 65%), saturated (>10^10, 80-110 TFLOPs, 18%)
- Peak: 110.8 TFLOPs at M=3840, N=2048, K=4096 (non-square); cross-tile peak 112.1 TFLOPs at M=3072, N=2560, K=3840
- Optimal aspect ratio M:N in 1.0-1.5 range; N-alignment matters 2.5x more than M-alignment due to sub-group layout asymmetry

**Four-Surface Decomposition:**
- Memory surface contributes bulk of structured texture (checkerboard, step patterns)
- Overhead surface: ~32% base overhead constant across configurations (pipeline startup/drain, sub-group barriers, register spills, partial-tile waste)
- Bandwidth classification shifts dramatically depending on spec (456 GB/s) vs measured (~270 GB/s) bandwidth: compute-bound drops from 55% to 32%

**Memory Artifacts:**
- Co-allocation interference: 12% mean slowdown, 32% of configs >20% slowdown, 10% of configs >50% slowdown
- Temporal warmup: 43% drift across sequential sweep blocks, Spearman rho=-0.65 with run order
- Write/read bandwidth asymmetry: 1.18x ratio from write-combining
- Randomized sweep eliminates artifacts: corr(read_A, N) drops from -0.76 to -0.005

**Optimization Stack Progression (at K=4096, step-128):**
| Stage | Mean TFLOPs | Roughness | vs Ideal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ideal compute | 97.2 | 2.0 | 1.0x |
| Fixed 256x256 tile | 58.7 | 16.8 | 8.2x |
| Dynamic best-of-6 tile | 68.3 | 10.4 | 5.1x |
| DP split+pad on dynamic tile | 76.3 | 5.0 | 2.5x |

**Cross-Tile Sawtooth Test:**
- Sawtooth period equals tile width exactly for all three tested sizes (64, 128, 256)
- Valleys consistently at N mod tile_size = 24
- Rules out cache set conflicts (which would produce valleys at same N regardless of tile)

**Roughness Attribution (16.8 TFLOPs/step total):**
- Software-removable (~12, 71%): coarse partial-tile waste 6.4, fine partial-tile waste 5.4, pathological shapes 0.4
- Hardware-bound (~5.0, 29%): wave-fill effects on 20 Xe-cores 2.8, per-kernel overhead variation 1.5, DPAS atom asymmetry 0.5, GDDR6 channel-hash 0.2

**DP Action Distribution:** 75% pad-or-as-is, 13% K-split, 8% N-split, 5% M-split

**Fine-Grid Results (step-32):** DP padding reduces roughness 65-80% across all tiles; residual ~1.31 TFLOPs/step (22x measurement noise)

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The "ruggedness landscape" framing is genuinely novel and provides a useful conceptual complement to roofline analysis. The DP-based padding-and-splitting optimizer is a clean algorithmic contribution. The discovery and characterization of co-allocation interference and temporal warmup artifacts at sweep scale are methodological contributions. The four-surface decomposition is a systematic analytical tool.
- **Rigor:** Exceptional experimental methodology. The 32,768-configuration sweep with randomized ordering, 50-run variance verification (CV 0.04-0.10%), cross-tile experiments at step-32, independent VTune validation on 20 hand-picked configs, and Spearman correlation analysis of measurement artifacts all demonstrate thoroughness. The quantitative attribution of the full 16.8 TFLOPs/step to named mechanisms with specific magnitudes is commendable.
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent. Full hardware specification (Arc B580, i5-13400 host, Ubuntu 25.04), software stack versions (oneAPI 2025.1, sycl-tla commit be58860e), compiler flags, kernel sysctls, and measurement protocols are provided. The grid parameters and metric definitions are precise.
- **Significance:** High for GPU kernel engineers and performance tool developers. The practical insight -- that adjacent GEMM sizes can vary 30% and a simple DP optimizer can smooth this -- is actionable. The methodological contributions (randomized sweeps, four-surface decomposition) generalize beyond GEMM. The framework has implications for SLO design and competitive benchmarking.
- **Red flags / concerns:** (1) Single GPU architecture only -- all quantitative results are BMG-specific. The qualitative claims of generality are plausible but unvalidated. (2) The O(1) runtime lookup is somewhat misleading: it requires O(N^4) offline precomputation on the actual hardware, which is the expensive part. For a 32x32x32 grid this is tractable, but scaling to continuous problem sizes would require interpolation. (3) The paper compares against itself (fixed tile baseline) rather than against established auto-tuners (TVM, Triton, cuBLASLt), which would have strengthened the practical contribution. (4) Only BF16 NN GEMM is evaluated -- transpose variants, other data types, and batched GEMM are not covered. (5) The 32% overhead "floor" is measured but not deeply analyzed for root causes beyond listing contributing factors. (6) The DP optimizer's memory footprint for the T0/T1/T2 tables is not discussed.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally thorough experimental methodology: 32K configs, randomized sweeps, 50-run variance tests, cross-tile experiments, independent VTune validation
- Novel and well-articulated conceptual framework (ruggedness analysis as complement to roofline)
- Clean algorithmic contribution: DP padding-and-splitting with O(1) lookup is simple, practical, and effective
- Discovery and careful characterization of two measurement artifacts (co-allocation interference, temporal warmup) with methodological resolution
- Complete quantitative attribution of roughness to named mechanisms with specific magnitudes
- Well-structured paper progressing from observation to decomposition to optimization to mechanism refinement
- The finding that N-alignment matters 2.5x more than M-alignment, and the optimal M:N ratio is 1.0-1.5, are practically useful rules of thumb

## Limitations

- Single GPU architecture (Intel Battlemage Arc B580 only); no cross-platform validation on NVIDIA, AMD, or other Intel GPUs
- Only BF16 NN (no-transpose) GEMM evaluated; no FP32, FP16, INT8, transpose, or batched variants
- No comparison with established auto-tuning frameworks (Triton, TVM, cuBLASLt, OneDNN autotuning)
- O(N^4) offline precomputation cost and T0/T1/T2 table memory footprint not analyzed
- The "universality" claims for the framework are stated but not empirically validated beyond BMG
- Only GEMM kernels studied; generalization to convolutions, attention kernels, or other dense linear algebra is claimed but untested
- Single-author breadth: the paper covers an unusually broad scope (methodology + optimization + microarchitecture), and some sections could benefit from deeper collaboration

## Relevance

本文对GEMM在GPU上的性能波动进行了系统性的量化分析与优化，对深度学习推理部署中的矩阵乘法kernel调优有直接参考价值。其DP填充-拆分优化器和随机序扫描方法可迁移至其他tile-based kernel（如attention、convolution）的性能优化中，四面体分解法也为系统性能分析提供了新的方法论工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the ruggedness landscape differ across GPU architectures (NVIDIA Hopper/Blackwell, AMD MI300)? Do the same four hardware-bound mechanisms dominate?
- Can the DP optimizer be extended to continuous (non-grid-aligned) problem sizes via interpolation, and what accuracy loss does that entail?
- What is the interaction between the ruggedness framework and existing auto-tuners (Triton, TVM)? Could the DP optimizer complement or replace their cost models?
- Does the framework generalize to other kernel types -- flash attention, convolution, mixed-precision GEMM -- where tile misalignment effects should also be prominent?
- How sensitive are the results to batch size, transpose variants (NT, TN, TT), and data types (FP16, INT8)?
- Could the co-allocation interference and temporal warmup artifacts be leveraged as side channels for GPU workload fingerprinting?
- What is the memory and compute cost of the O(N^4) T2 precomputation at production-relevant grid resolutions, and can it be accelerated?
