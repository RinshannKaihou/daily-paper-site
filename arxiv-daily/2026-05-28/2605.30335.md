> **一句话总结：** 本文从概率一致性（de Finetti coherent polytope）角度形式化了多组件LLM代理中"局部一致但全局不一致"的失败模式，提出compositional residual（L2距离到联合一致多面体）作为运行时可计算的认证指标，证明产品结构二分法定理刻画何时局部一致性足够，并通过Boyle-Dykstra层次化投影实现确定性修复；在1,876个集成clique（4个中端LLM）上33-94%存在不一致性，造成+0.115 nats/bet后悔值，三种直觉性LLM端缓解策略全部失败。
> **TL;DR:** When a multi-component LLM agent routes questions to specialist subagents, each locally coherent probability output can compose into an incoherent joint quote (e.g., partition masses summing to 2.5 instead of 1.0). This paper defines epsilon* (L2 distance to the coherent polytope), proves a product-structure dichotomy for when local coherence suffices, shows a Rayleigh-quotient predictor matches observed residual within 7%, repairs via hierarchical Boyle-Dykstra projection, and monitors via anytime-valid e-processes. Across 1,876 cliques on 4 mid-tier LLMs, 33-94% are incoherent; frontier models (GPT-5.5, Claude-Opus-4.7) reduce magnitude by 39% but 97.8% of partition cliques remain incoherent. Three prompt-based mitigations all fail or regress; only geometric repair eliminates epsilon* at 1x elicitation cost.

## 核心贡献

- **形式化"局部一致但全局不一致"问题**：在多组件LLM代理中，即使每个专家组件的边际概率输出在其局部问题上是一致的（落在局部coherent polytope内），组合后的联合概率仍可违反概率公理（如分区概率之和不为1），作者称之为"locally coherent, globally incoherent"失败模式。
- **定义compositional residual（eps*）**：eps*是局部修复后的组合报价到联合一致多面体的L2距离，可从系统输出和显式跨组件耦合约束在运行时直接计算，是一个分布无关的实例级一致性认证。
- **证明产品结构二分法定理（Theorem 3.3）**：在owner-selected坐标聚合下，局部一致性保证全局一致性当且仅当联合多面体可分解为局部多面体的笛卡尔积（即耦合约束为平凡时）；否则存在局部一致但全局不一致的输入。
- **Rayleigh商预测（Corollary 3.9）**：在均匀随机路由下，eps*的期望值可从专家面板协方差矩阵通过Rayleigh商形式预测，在四种关系类别中的三种上匹配观测值在7%以内。
- **确定性修复（Theorem 3.10）**：层次化Boyle-Dykstra投影在有限步内收敛到联合一致多面体的投影，以1xm调用成本将eps*降至QP求解器精度。
- **序贯监控（Theorem D.2）**：基于e-process的anytime-valid检验控制Type-I错误，无需预设停止时间。
- **广泛的实证验证**：在Paleka（否定/合取/析取）和Polymarket（分区）上1,876个集成clique，四种关系类别，四种中端LLM（Claude-Haiku-4.5、GPT-5.4-mini、Gpt-5.4-nano、Llama-3.3-70b），以及前沿面板（GPT-5.5、Claude-Opus-4.7、DeepSeek-V3.2、Llama-4-Maverick）上验证。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes the "locally coherent, globally incoherent" failure mode in multi-component LLM agents using de Finetti's coherence framework, showing that locally coherent component outputs can compose into globally incoherent joint probability quotes.
- Defines the compositional residual epsilon* (L2 distance from the composed quote to the joint coherent polytope) as a runtime-computable, distribution-free, instance-wise coherence certificate.
- Proves the product-structure dichotomy (Theorem 3.3): under owner-selected aggregation, local coherence guarantees global coherence if and only if the joint polytope factors as a Cartesian product of local polytopes (i.e., coupling constraints are trivial).
- Derives a Rayleigh-quotient form (Corollary 3.9) predicting the expected epsilon* from the specialist panel covariance alone, matching observed values within 7% on three of four relation classes (negation 94.6%, partition 93.1%, disjunction 97.4%, conjunction 83.0%).
- Provides hierarchical Boyle-Dykstra projection (Theorem 3.10) that deterministically eliminates epsilon* to QP solver tolerance at 1x specialist elicitation cost.
- Establishes an anytime-valid e-process (Theorem D.2) for sequential coherence monitoring with Type-I error control at all stopping times.
- Empirically demonstrates across 1,876 ensemble cliques on Paleka and Polymarket benchmarks: epsilon* > 0 on 33-94% of cliques by relation class, +0.115 nats/bet regret, frontier models reduce magnitude by 39% but 97.8% of partitions remain incoherent.
- Shows three intuitive LLM-side mitigations (retrieval grounding, partition-aware prompting, aggregator-LLM) all fail or regress; coupling-visibility intervention reduces but does not eliminate epsilon*.

## Methodology

**Theoretical framework.** The paper works within de Finetti's coherence framework. A "clique" C = (Q_1,...,Q_m, R) is a set of m Bernoulli questions with logical relations R. The coherent polytope M_C is the set of probability vectors consistent with R. Multi-component agents assign questions to specialist subagents; each subagent produces locally coherent marginals via Joint-Coherent Decoding (JCD, L2 projection onto local polytopes). An aggregator assembles these via owner-selected coordinate selection. The compositional residual epsilon* measures the L2 gap between this assembled quote and the joint coherent polytope M*.

**Key theoretical results:**
- Theorem 3.3 (Product-structure dichotomy): epsilon* is identically zero iff M* = M^boxtimes (no non-trivial coupling). Otherwise, locally coherent inputs exist that compose to globally incoherent quotes.
- Corollary 3.5: The system-level Dutch-book exposure is bounded by sqrt(m*) * epsilon* under the FTAP.
- Corollary 3.9: Under uniform i.i.d. owner-selection, E[epsilon*^2] = kappa_R * (a_R^T D a_R) / ||a_R||^2, where D is the diagonal of the specialist panel covariance matrix, a_R is the binding constraint normal, and kappa_R depends on whether the binding constraint is an equality (kappa=1) or inequality (kappa~1/2).
- Theorem 3.10: Hierarchical Boyle-Dykstra cyclic projection over local polytopes and coupling set converges to the joint L2 projection.

**Experimental design:**
- **Benchmarks**: Paleka (134 cliques x 4 checkers: negation, conjunction, disjunction, paraphrase) and Polymarket (67 partition cliques x 4 seeds = 268 ensemble instances). Total: 1,876 clique-seed pairs.
- **Mid-tier panel**: Claude-Haiku-4.5, GPT-5.4-mini, GPT-5.4-nano, Llama-3.3-70b (Groq).
- **Frontier panel**: Claude-Opus-4.7, GPT-5.5 (reasoning disabled), DeepSeek-V3.2, Llama-4-Maverick-17B-128E.
- **Routing**: For each clique, each of m questions is assigned to one of 4 LLMs uniformly at random (4 seeds per clique).
- **K=8** verbalized probability samples per question at temperature 0.7.
- **Leakage control**: All Polymarket events resolved after 30 April 2026 model snapshot.
- **Controls**: Same-model decoupling (4 seeds of one model), greedy decoding (T=0), structured routing, K-sweep (K=8,16,32).
- **Evaluation**: Paired Brier with Diebold-Mariano significance, log-payoff regret under proportional allocation, runtime gating calibration (AUC).
- Code and full reproducibility manifest released at https://github.com/akotawala10/composition-incoherence-icml.

## Results & Highlights

**Compositional residual prevalence (Section 5.1):**
- epsilon* > 0 on 94% (partition), 66% (negation), 43% (disjunction), 33% (conjunction) of cliques.
- Mean epsilon* ranges from 0.058 (conjunction) to 0.118 (partition).
- The hardness ordering (partition > negation > disjunction > conjunction) tracks how tightly each polytope constrains the joint quote, as predicted by Theorem 3.3.

**Rayleigh-quotient validation (Table 1):**
- Observed/predicted ratios: Negation 1.054 (94.6% match), Partition 1.069 (93.1%), Disjunction 1.026 (97.4%), Conjunction 0.830 (83.0% — under-shoot explained by interior Pi-bar regime).

**Hierarchical repair (Theorem 3.10):**
- Reduces mean exposure bound from 0.137 to 1.7x10^-14 (QP solver floor).
- Repair cost: 1 QP solve per clique (11ms), no extra LLM calls.

**Brier improvement (Section 5.1):**
- Hierarchical JCD reduces Brier on negation (delta=-0.0137, p<10^-43), conjunction (delta=-0.0076, p<10^-16), partition (delta=-0.0048, p<10^-23).
- Disjunction is marginal (delta=+0.0027, p=0.07): the predicted reversal regime where label noise pushes p* outside M*.

**Downstream regret (Section 5.6):**
- On 1,770 resolved bets: +0.115 nats/bet regret under proportional allocation (95% CI [+0.056, +0.187]).
- Regret stratifies by epsilon* quartile: Q4 (epsilon* > 0.154) carries 0.054 Brier and 0.221 nats regret per bet; Q1-Q2 carry zero.
- Under coherentizing bettors (truncated Kelly, max-entropy), gain collapses to +0.006 nats.

**Frontier panel (Section 5.5):**
- epsilon* > 0 on 97.8% of partition cliques (slightly above mid-tier 94%).
- Mean epsilon* drops from 0.118 to 0.072 (-39%): frontier models reduce magnitude but do not eliminate the failure mode.

**LLM-side mitigations fail (Section 5.2):**
- Retrieval grounding: mean epsilon* rises from 0.260 to 0.283; 20/30 partitions regress.
- Partition-aware prompting: helps on average (0.066) but 53% still exceed epsilon*>0.05, 5/30 regress.
- LLM-as-aggregator: reduces to 0.028 but 15% still exceed 0.05, 7/100 regress.
- All prompt-based methods can harm already-coherent quotes.

**Coupling-visibility intervention (Section 5.4):**
- Showing specialists the partition constraint and peer quotes reduces mean epsilon* from 0.519 to 0.298 (paired, p=2.6x10^-10), but epsilon* remains positive on every partition.

**Same-model decoupling control (Section 5.1):**
- 22-60% of residual persists even with same-model specialists; cross-model heterogeneity amplifies by 1.7-4.5x.
- Greedy decoding (T=0) confirms the residual is not sampling noise: epsilon* increases on negation and disjunction at T=0.

**Runtime gating (Section 5.7):**
- epsilon* predicts top-quartile log-payoff regret with CV-AUC 0.775 +/- 0.044.
- Recommended thresholds: tau~0.15 (high-recall, 91% harm capture at 25% alert rate) or tau~0.22 (high-precision, 51% capture at 14% alert rate).

**Per-component JCD layer (Section 5.8):**
- JCD reduces mean Brier by 0.037 on Paleka and 0.046 on Polymarket vs. Platt calibration baseline.
- Diebold-Mariano p<10^-4 on every model. Murphy decomposition attributes gain to calibration (36% reliability reduction).

It has been accepted to appear at three ICML 2026 Workshops: Combining Theory and Benchmarks (CTB), Statistical Frameworks for Uncertainty in Agentic Systems (AgenticUQ), and Failure Modes of Agentic AI (FAGEN).

- **Scope concern**: Single-author work spanning convex analysis, probability theory, LLM forecasting, and extensive experimentation is highly ambitious. ## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The operational reframing of compositional incoherence as a runtime certificate for multi-component LLM agents is genuinely novel. The product-structure dichotomy is a clean theoretical result. The connection between panel covariance and residual magnitude via Rayleigh-quotient is non-trivial and falsifiable. Prior work (Paleka et al., 2025) identified cross-question incoherence but did not formalize the compositional layer or provide repair mechanisms.

- **Rigor**: Strong. The theoretical section is rigorous with proper definitions, theorems, and proofs. Corollaries build logically. Empirical methodology includes multiple controls: same-model decoupling, greedy-decoding, K-sweep, leakage filtering, structured routing, Diebold-Mariano significance tests, and 95% bootstrap confidence intervals. The disjunction reversal case is honestly reported and explained by the theory (Corollary 3.7's p* not in M* regime).

- **Reproducibility**: Excellent. The reproducibility manifest is thorough, code is released, and the experiments use publicly available APIs and benchmarks.

- **Significance**: High for the multi-agent LLM systems community. The finding that 33-94% of composed outputs are incoherent, even with frontier models (97.8%), is a concrete, actionable result. The geometric repair is practical (11ms, no extra LLM calls) and the framework is orthogonal to existing per-output calibration and conformal prediction methods.

- **Red flags / weaknesses**:
  1. **Scope condition**: The coupling set C must be explicitly specified. The paper acknowledges this does not cover free-form chain-of-thought or unstructured planner-tool transcripts where C is implicit. This limits immediate applicability to structured tool-use deployments only.
  2. **Random routing as primary evaluation**: The main experiment uses random i.i.d. assignment of questions to specialists, which is a controlled simulation rather than a deployed agent. The planner-discretion harness (n=20) and routing-protocol ladder (n=100) are relatively small.
  3. **Conjunction under-shoot**: The 0.83x prediction-observation ratio on conjunction is acknowledged but the interior Pi-bar explanation, while consistent with the theory, is somewhat post-hoc.
  4. **Single author breadth**: 25 pages plus 19 appendices covering theory, experiments, controls, and negative results is a very large scope for one author. The core convex-analytic machinery is classical (Bauschke & Combettes, Boyle & Dykstra), so the contribution is primarily operational rather than mathematical.
  5. **Disjunction marginal result**: Brier improvement on disjunction is not significant (p=0.07), which the theory predicts but which still means the repair does not reliably help on this relation class.
  6. **Benchmark coverage**: Only Bernoulli-style probabilistic forecasts are tested. The framework does not directly address non-probabilistic outputs or more complex logical structures.

## Strengths

- Identifies and formalizes a real, previously underappreciated failure mode in multi-component LLM agents with clean theoretical grounding.
- The product-structure dichotomy provides a crisp characterization: either coupling is trivial (and local coherence suffices) or it is not (and epsilon* certifies the gap).
- The Rayleigh-quotient prediction makes the theory falsifiable and empirically validated.
- The geometric repair is deterministic, cheap (11ms, 1x elicitation cost), and provably optimal (converges to L2 projection).
- Extensive controls and ablations: same-model decoupling, greedy decoding, K-sweep, structured routing, coupling-visibility intervention, frontier panel, 55-fold CV for gating thresholds.
- Honest reporting of negative results: three LLM-side mitigations fail, disjunction Brier reversal is reported and explained.
- The coupling-visibility experiment (Section 5.4) provides direct causal evidence that the residual arises from specialists not seeing coupling constraints.

## Limitations

- **Explicit coupling requirement**: The framework requires the coupling set C to be given as a finite list of constraints. Recovering C from unstructured agent transcripts remains an open problem.
- **Evaluation scope**: Random routing is the primary evaluation; real deployed multi-component agents may have different routing patterns. The planner harness and routing ladder are relatively small-scale (n=20, n=100).
- **Bernoulli-only**: The framework handles only Bernoulli (binary outcome) probability questions. Extension to continuous or structured outputs is not addressed.
- **Bregman geometry limitation**: The repair uses L2/Brier geometry. The paper notes extension to log-loss or spherical scoring rules via matched Bregman projections as future work but does not implement it.
- **Conjunction prediction gap**: The 17% under-shoot on conjunction predictions is the weakest point of the quantitative theory.
- **No comparison to opinion pooling**: The paper notes that coordinate-wise averaging of k redundant forecasts per question would avoid the failure mode by convexity, but dismisses it due to kx elicitation cost. A deeper comparison with opinion pooling methods would strengthen the paper.

## Relevance

本文从概率一致性角度系统分析了多组件LLM代理中的组合不一致性问题，提出了运行时认证、确定性修复和序贯监控的完整工具链。对于构建多智能体LLM系统（如工具调用、专家路由、概率聚合）的研究者和工程师，这篇论文提供了一个可直接部署的理论框架和实用方法。特别是eps*作为运行时gate的设计思路值得在实际系统中采纳。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the coupling set C be automatically recovered from unstructured planner-tool transcripts using NLI classifiers or schema-enforcing protocols? This is the most pressing open problem identified in the paper.
2. How does the compositional residual scale with the number of components k > 4 and with more complex coupling structures (e.g., higher-order logical constraints beyond pairwise)?
3. Does training-time coherence regularization (e.g., penalizing violations of probability axioms during fine-tuning) reduce the need for post-hoc geometric repair?
4. Can the L2/Brier pairing be extended to log-loss (KL divergence) geometry, and does the Bregman-projection repair maintain the same convergence guarantees?
5. What is the interaction between compositional incoherence and other LLM failure modes (hallucination, miscalibration, sycophancy)? Does fixing incoherence expose or amplify these?
6. The routing-protocol ladder shows that tool-using planners with GPT-5.5 achieve zero residuals at ~7x cost. Is there a cost-optimal frontier between prompt engineering, planner delegation, and geometric repair for different deployment budgets?
