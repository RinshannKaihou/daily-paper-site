> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 SafeDIG 框架，通过在 DiT 的不同功能层（文本编码、跨模态绑定、渲染耦合）构建稀疏自编码器（SAE），并利用鲁棒性感知路由和仅解码器迁移策略，实现对文本到图像 Diffusion Transformer 的安全干预，在 FLUX.1 Dev 和 SD 3.5 Large 上显著降低不安全生成率，同时保持图像质量。

> **TL;DR:** SafeDIG addresses safety control in Diffusion Transformers by constructing Sparse Autoencoders at text-encoder, cross-modal (Double Stream Block), and rendering-coupled (Single Stream Block) intervention positions. It uses a robustness-aware routing mechanism to select the best position-operator pair before SAE training, then performs decoder-only transfer to adapt safety features to new risk domains. On FLUX.1 Dev, SafeDIG reduces the overall unsafe generation rate (ASR) from 53.97% to 38.01% (prompt-level) and from 24.74% to 11.51% (line-level), outperforming SAFREE, SAeUron, EraseDiff, and Erasing baselines. The key finding is that middle-trunk (Double Stream Block) interventions provide the best transfer-retention trade-off, while late-trunk steering is unstable and can catastrophically fail under domain shift.

## 核心贡献

- **位置感知的 SAE 安全干预框架：** 首次系统性地将 DiT 生成过程划分为文本语义、跨模态绑定、渲染耦合三个功能阶段，并在每个阶段构建独立的 SAE 进行安全特征提取与干预。
- **鲁棒性感知路由机制：** 在训练 SAE 之前，通过语义相似度、绑定强度、操作稳定性和成本四个指标对候选干预位置-操作对进行预排序，自动选择最鲁棒的干预配置。
- **流形稳定的仅解码器迁移：** 冻结源域 SAE 编码器作为可复用的稀疏安全字典，仅微调解码器以适配目标域激活流形，实现少样本安全迁移，避免灾难性遗忘。
- **Blend/Repel 双操作推理：** Blend 将激活拉向安全流形，Repel 将激活推离有害稀疏方向，两种操作互为补充，应对不同类型的安全风险。
- **实证发现：** 中间层（Double Stream Block）的 SAE 迁移在源域保持和目标域安全提升之间取得了最佳平衡，而后层（Single Stream Block）迁移在域偏移下可能产生灾难性崩溃。

## Key Contributions

- Proposes SafeDIG, a position-aware SAE steering framework that treats DiT safety adaptation as sparse feature transfer across functionally distinct intervention positions (text-semantic, cross-modal binding, rendering-coupled).
- Introduces robustness-aware pre-training routing that ranks candidate position-operator pairs using semantic, binding, stability, and cost scores before committing to SAE training.
- Designs manifold-stable decoder-only transfer: freezes the source SAE encoder as a reusable sparse safety dictionary and adapts only the decoder to target-domain activations with optional source replay, enabling few-shot safety generalization.
- Demonstrates that middle-trunk (Double Stream Block) interventions provide the most stable safety transfer across risk domains, while late-trunk (Single Stream Block) interventions are highly sensitive to domain shift and can catastrophically amplify harmful outputs.
- Achieves state-of-the-art safety performance on FLUX.1 Dev and Stable Diffusion 3.5 Large, reducing overall ASR by ~16 percentage points on FLUX.1 Dev with minimal image quality degradation.

## Methodology

**Problem Formulation.** SafeDIG views DiT safety as a propagation chain: harmful semantics are weakly expressed in text representations, progressively bound to visual latents during cross-modal fusion, and finally entangled with rendering dynamics. Safety steering at a fixed layer is therefore unstable, and mechanisms learned from known risks may not transfer to new risk domains.

**Position-aware SAE Intervention.** The DiT generation path is partitioned into three functional groups: (1) text-encoder positions (prompt-semantic, high interpretability, limited transferability), (2) Double Stream Block positions (cross-modal binding, where harmful concepts bind to visual directions, best transfer-retention trade-off), and (3) Single Stream Block positions (rendering-coupled, powerful but sensitive to domain shift).

**Robustness Routing.** Before training SAEs, a router computes a robustness score for each candidate (position, operator) pair:
- S_sem: cosine similarity between contrastive activations and category text embeddings
- S_bind: cosine similarity between contrastive activations and pooled latent-image embeddings
- S_stab: inverse variance of the operator-induced activation updates
- C: cost combining activation dimensionality, runtime, and intervention magnitude

The top-ranked pair is selected for SAE training and inference.

**Manifold-stable SAE Transfer.** Source-domain SAEs are trained on safety-contrast activations (positive-minus-negative prompt pairs). For target-domain adaptation, only the decoder is fine-tuned while the encoder is frozen. This preserves the source safety dictionary while adapting reconstruction geometry. A stability regularization term (Omega_stable) further constrains the transfer.

**Inference-time Steering.** Two complementary operators:
- **Blend:** a' = (1-beta)*a + beta*a_hat_tgt -- pulls activation toward the transferred safety manifold
- **Repel:** a' = a - gamma*(a_hat_harm - a) -- pushes activation away from harmful sparse-feature directions (using a harmful-feature mask M_harm)

**Evaluation.** Uses Q16 (CLIP-based image safety classifier) and NudeNet (nudity detector) with logical OR fusion. Reports Prompt-level ASR (any-unsafe-over-K-samples) and Line-level ASR (per-image rate) with K=10 generations per prompt. Also uses bootstrap robustness metrics and image quality metrics (FID, CLIP score).

## Results & Highlights

**FLUX.1 Dev (Table 1):**
- SafeDIG reduces overall ASR from 53.97% to 38.01% (prompt-level, delta = 15.96) and from 24.74% to 11.51% (line-level, delta = 13.23).
- Target-domain (sexual) ASR drops from 44.56% to 30.29% (prompt) and from 16.49% to 7.98% (line).
- Strongest absolute ASR reduction among all baselines; SAFREE actually increases ASR (delta = -7.36).

**Stable Diffusion 3.5 Large (Table 1):**
- SafeDIG reduces overall ASR from 77.79% to 55.36% (prompt-level, delta = 22.43) and from 45.71% to 25.69% (line-level, delta = 20.02).
- Target-domain sexual ASR drops from 68.00% to 44.71% (prompt) and from 29.63% to 16.23% (line).

**Position-wise Transfer (Table 2, FLUX.1 Dev):**
- Double Stream Block + Transfer: best balance -- lowest target ASR (43.44%/11.79%), lowest overall ASR (54.28%/18.93%), negative transfer sacrifice (-6.41/-5.32, meaning source safety is preserved or improved).
- Text Encoder + Transfer: moderate gains (44.67%/12.94% on sexual), limited source retention.
- Single Stream Block + Transfer: reduces target but increases overall ASR (60.11%/24.40%), positive transfer sacrifice (2.53/2.64), indicating unstable transfer.

**Image Quality:**
- CLIP scores remain within 26.2-27.8 across all steering positions, indicating no severe semantic degradation.
- FID actually improves under decoder transfer for Double Stream Block (9.90 -> 6.36) and text encoder (1.65 -> 1.44).

**Adversarial Stress Test (MMA):**
- Only Double Stream Block transfer maintains stable performance under adversarial jailbreak prompts; text-encoder and Single Stream Block transfer are less reliable.

**Cross-domain Consistency (Violence target, Table 8):**
- Double Stream Block transfer again provides the best stability, improving violence ASR from 51.96%/23.65% to 51.54%/17.90%.
- Single Stream Block transfer catastrophically collapses: violence ASR rises to 70.59%/29.59%, overall ASR rises to 66.79%/28.51%.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Good):** The position-aware framing of DiT safety is genuinely novel. The insight that safety-relevant representations evolve across text-semantic, cross-modal binding, and rendering-coupled stages, and that intervention position matters as much as intervention method, is a meaningful contribution. The decoder-only transfer strategy for SAEs is well-motivated and practically useful.

**Rigor (Mixed):**
- The robustness routing formula (Eq. 9) has four weighting hyperparameters (alpha, rho, eta, xi) normalized to sum to 1, but the paper states "we use the same default weight vector for all experiments unless an ablation explicitly changes it" without reporting the actual values. This is a significant omission.
- The safety-contrast activation (a+ - a-) depends on manually constructed safe-harmful prompt pairs. While the paper describes minimal-edit rewriting rules, the pairing quality is not systematically evaluated.
- The harmful-feature mask M_harm for Repel is described as "estimated from source and target safety features" but the estimation procedure is not detailed in the main paper.

**Reproducibility (Moderate):**
- Compute requirements are substantial: ~50GB VRAM per GPU, 96GB host memory, ~2TB storage, 1-3 month runtime on A100/RTX 4090 clusters.
- The paper provides extensive hyperparameter details in appendices, which is commendable.
- No code release is mentioned at the time of publication.

**Significance (Good):** As DiT models (FLUX, SD3) become the dominant backbone for text-to-image generation, safety mechanisms specifically designed for their architecture are important. SafeDIG provides a practical framework that avoids full model retraining.

**Potential Concerns:**
- The baselines are somewhat limited: SAFREE, EraseDiff, Erasing, and SAeUron are the only comparisons. Some are U-Net-era methods adapted to DiT, which may not represent the strongest possible DiT-specific baselines.
- The i2p benchmark, while standard, is aging. The sexual category is chosen as the target domain partly because it has the lowest baseline ASR, which may make improvements easier to demonstrate.
- Several baselines (SAFREE, SAeUron, Erasing) actually increase ASR on FLUX.1 Dev compared to the base model, which raises the question of whether the baselines were properly adapted. The paper acknowledges adaptation challenges but does not fully resolve this concern.
- The paper claims SafeDIG "consistently reduces target-domain and overall unsafe generation rates while preserving source-domain safety," but Table 2 shows that source-domain safety is not always preserved (e.g., Text Encoder + Transfer increases hate ASR from 57.14 to 70.85).

## Strengths

- **Principled position analysis:** The functional decomposition of DiT into text-semantic, cross-modal, and rendering stages provides actionable insight about where safety interventions should be applied. The empirical finding that middle-trunk steering is most stable is valuable and well-supported.
- **Comprehensive evaluation:** Multi-metric evaluation (prompt-level and line-level ASR, bootstrap robustness, FID, CLIP), two model backbones, adversarial stress testing, and cross-domain ablation (sexual vs. violence targets).
- **Practical transfer mechanism:** Decoder-only transfer with frozen encoder is a clean, conservative adaptation strategy that is straightforward to implement and requires minimal target-domain data.
- **Honest limitations section:** The authors acknowledge that (1) they do not localize effects to finer components like attention heads or specific timesteps, and (2) the safety-contrast signal is empirical rather than a perfectly isolated safety vector.
- **Thorough appendices:** Eight appendices covering pre-study, experimental setup, evaluator details, proofs, and ablations demonstrate comprehensive documentation.

## Limitations

- **Routing hyperparameters not reported:** The four routing weights (alpha, rho, eta, xi) are fixed across experiments but their actual values are not disclosed, making exact reproduction difficult.
- **Prompt-pair construction dependency:** The framework relies on manually constructed safe-harmful prompt pairs. The quality and consistency of these pairs are not systematically evaluated, and the approach may not scale well to new harmful categories where clear safe counterparts are difficult to define.
- **Limited baseline competitiveness:** Several baselines (SAFREE, SAeUron, Erasing) increase ASR relative to the base model on FLUX.1 Dev, suggesting they may not be well-suited for DiT. This makes SafeDIG's improvements less surprising and raises questions about whether stronger DiT-specific baselines exist.
- **No fine-grained localization:** The paper operates at block-level granularity (entire Double/Single Stream Blocks) and does not investigate which attention heads, MLP layers, token types, or timesteps within each block are most responsible for safety-relevant features.
- **Compute requirements:** The experimental pipeline requires substantial resources (A100 GPUs, 2TB storage, months of runtime), limiting reproducibility for smaller research groups.
- **Single primary benchmark:** The main results use i2p with a fixed source/target split (6 source categories, sexual as target). Generalization to other transfer configurations or entirely different benchmarks is only partially explored.
- **Residual unsafe rates remain high:** Even with SafeDIG, the overall ASR on FLUX.1 Dev is 38.01%/11.51% and on SD 3.5 Large is 55.36%/25.69%. While these are improvements, substantial unsafe generation persists.

## Relevance

本文研究了 Diffusion Transformer 的内部表征安全干预问题，提出了基于稀疏自编码器的位置感知安全迁移框架。对于关注生成模型安全性、可解释性表征工程以及扩散模型内部控制的研究者具有参考价值，特别是其对 DiT 不同功能层安全特征传播规律的分析思路，可借鉴用于其他条件生成模型的安全对齐工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does SafeDIG perform when multiple harmful categories must be simultaneously controlled as target domains, rather than a single held-out category?
2. Can the routing mechanism be made adaptive at inference time (e.g., selecting different intervention positions based on the specific prompt), rather than using a fixed pre-computed ranking?
3. What is the sensitivity of SafeDIG to the quality and quantity of the safe-harmful prompt pairs used for constructing contrastive activations?
4. Could the decoder-only transfer strategy be combined with activation steering approaches from the LLM safety literature (e.g., representation engineering, contrastive activation addition) for improved transfer?
5. Does the finding that middle-trunk interventions are most stable generalize to video generation DiTs or other conditional generation architectures?
6. How does SafeDIG interact with other safety mechanisms (e.g., safety filters, RLHF-based alignment) when applied jointly?
7. Can the harmful-feature mask M_harm be learned or refined in an online manner rather than estimated from source-target feature overlap?
