Demystifying Data Organization for Enhanced LLM Training

Yalun Dai, Yangyu Huang, Tongshen Yang & 8 others  ·  NTU & Microsoft Research

By simply reordering training data using scores already computed during selection, LLMs gain consistent improvements across 160M–1.7B parameters at near-zero extra cost. Four guiding principles and two strategies (STR, SAW) outperform random ordering and vanilla curriculum learning.

Four Guiding Principles
G1 · Boundary Sharpening
Start easy, end hard
Control data at training boundaries: low-score for stability at start, high-score for peak at end.
→ SEG (Segment Ordering)
G2 · Cyclic Scheduling
Periodic review
Periodically revisit the full score spectrum to prevent catastrophic forgetting.
→ FO (Folding Ordering)
G3 · Curriculum Continuity
Smooth transitions
Maintain smooth attribute transitions to avoid gradient shocks at cycle boundaries.
→ ZIG (Zig-zag Ordering)
G4 · Local Diversity
Intra-batch noise
Inject noise within local windows to prevent gradient collapse from homogeneous batches.
→ JIT (Jittering Ordering)
STR — Stair Ordering
G1 + G2 (+ optional G4)
Stable regions keep monotonic order; transition regions apply FO for knowledge review.
SAW — Saw Ordering
G1 + G2 + G3 (+ optional G4)
Replaces FO with ZIG in transitions, creating triangle-wave for smoother continuity.

Key Insights
  • Ending matters most. High-score data at training end is critical; starting with high-score alone yields negligible benefit.
  • Periodic review helps. FO-2 beats vanilla CL; FO-3 gives large SFT math gains (AIME24: 1.78 → 2.96).
  • ZIG eliminates spikes. Removes gradient norm spikes at cycle boundaries that FO exhibits.
  • JIT finds flatter minima. Verified via weight perturbation analysis.
  • STR ≈ SAW in most settings — G3 continuity may add little beyond G1+G2+G4.
