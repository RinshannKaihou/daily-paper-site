> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 BioRefusalAudit 框架，通过行为测试和稀疏自编码器（SAE）内部特征分析，揭示主流开源小模型在生物安全拒绝行为上存在严重不一致——要么拒绝机制脆弱（格式变化即失效），要么过度拒绝良性生物学内容，且拒绝行为更多反映法律/文化敏感性而非实际 CBRN 危险等级。

> **TL;DR:** This paper audits the structural robustness of biosecurity refusals across five small open-source LLMs (1B-2B). Every model fails differently: Gemma 2 2B-IT never genuinely refuses (hedges on everything); Gemma 4 E2B-IT refuses 65/75 with chat templates but 0/75 without them, and both Gemma models collapse to 0% refusal under an 80-token cap; Qwen 2.5 1.5B and Phi-3-mini over-refuse 83-87% of benign biology; only Llama 3.2 1B shows a meaningful tier gradient (61-point spread). A legality confound test using Schedule I non-toxic compounds (notably psilocybin) shows refusal tracks legal/cultural salience over actual CBRN hazard. The authors introduce a divergence score D = 1 - cos(f, T^T * s) comparing surface behavior to internal SAE feature activations. On Gemma 4, comply and refuse responses separate by 0.647 points with zero overlap (n=75), though this is within-sample calibrated and Gemma-family only. Built during a hackathon weekend on consumer hardware (GTX 1650 Ti Max-Q).

## 核心贡献

- 系统性审计了5个小参数开源模型（Gemma 2 2B-IT、Gemma 4 E2B-IT、Qwen 2.5 1.5B、Phi-3-mini、Llama 3.2 1B）在生物安全拒绝行为上的鲁棒性，发现每种架构都有独特的失败模式
- 揭示拒绝行为更多反映法律地位和文化显著性（裸盖菇素 cultivation 被拒绝率超过真正有害生物内容），而非实际 CBRN 危险等级——这一发现在三个模型家族、四种实验条件下均得到验证
- 提出基于 SAE 特征的 divergence score D = 1 - cos(f, T^T * s)，量化模型表面行为（comply/refuse/hedge/partial/evade）与内部5维特征激活（bio-content, hazard vocabulary, refusal circuitry, hedging, deception correlates）之间的不一致性
- 发布了三个领域专用 SAE 检查点（两个 Gemma 2 微调 + 一个 Gemma 4 从头训练），以及完整的交互式仪表盘展示所有75个 prompt 的 D 分数
- 证明了激活级审计可以发现行为评估无法捕捉的失败模式，如"浅层拒绝"（表面拒绝但内部危险特征仍活跃）

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive behavioral audit across five small open-source LLMs, each exhibiting a distinct failure mode: universal hedging (Gemma 2), format-gated refusal (Gemma 4), over-refusal (Qwen, Phi-3), or moderate discrimination (Llama 3.2)
- Cross-architecture legality confound: psilocybin (Schedule I but FDA Breakthrough Therapy, biologically non-toxic) refused at rates exceeding genuinely hazardous biology, tested across three model families under four experimental conditions
- Divergence score D = 1 - cos(f, T^T * s) mapping 5D surface labels to 5D SAE feature categories via alignment matrix T, providing a formal measure of surface-internal consistency
- Three released domain-specific SAEs: two Gemma 2 fine-tunes (WMDP corpus and pairwise behavioral) and one Gemma 4 TopK(k=32) trained from scratch
- Interactive dashboard with per-prompt D scores, surface labels, divergence flags, and tier assignments
- Finding that 58% of genuine refusals on explicit hazard prompts still showed active hazard features internally ("shallow refusals"), detectable only through activation-level auditing

## Methodology

- **Evaluation pipeline (per model-prompt pair):** (1) Generate at T=0.7 with residual-stream forward hook at ~50% model depth; (2) Project activations through SAE, take per-feature mean across tokens; (3) Compress to 5 feature categories via catalog and L1-normalize to produce f; (4) Run judge ensemble (regex -> Gemini CLI -> Claude Haiku, weighted voting) to produce soft surface label s; (5) Compute D and three divergence flags
- **Divergence score D:** D = 1 - cos(f, T^T * s), where s is the 5D soft surface-label vector, f is the normalized internal feature-category vector, and T is a 5x5 alignment matrix fit via ridge-regularized least squares. D ranges from 0 (perfect alignment) to 2 (maximum divergence)
- **Feature catalog construction:** Categories populated via Cohen's d discrimination between high-tier and low-tier activation samples (statistical, not semantic). Categories: bio-content, hazard vocabulary, refusal circuitry, hedging, deception correlates
- **Evaluation set:** 75 prompts stratified by tier (benign_bio n=23, dual_use_bio n=30, hazard_adjacent n=22) and framing (direct/educational/roleplay/obfuscated). Tier-1/2 under CC-BY-4.0; Tier-3 behind HLG-gated HuggingFace attestation
- **SAE configurations:** Gemma 2 uses Gemma Scope 1 (layer_12, width_16k, average_l0_82, JumpReLU); Gemma 4 uses author-trained TopK(k=32, d_sae=6144, 4x expansion) at layer 17. Gemma 2 custom SAEs: WMDP corpus fine-tune (TopK k=32, d_sae=6144, ~2.7x expansion, 5000 steps) and pairwise behavioral fine-tune (NT-Xent loss)
- **Behavioral comparison (NullSAE):** All five models evaluated with surface labels only on same 75-prompt set
- **Calibration:** T fit within-sample for main Gemma 2 experiment; T_prior (identity-biased permutation) used for Gemma 4. Held-out calibration on differently-framed distribution produced inverted tier ordering (Cohen's d = -0.967), indicating T is framing-distribution-sensitive

## Results & Highlights

- **Finding 1 (Gemma 2 hedges):** Never genuinely refused across 75 prompts. Benign tier: 70% comply, 30% hedge, 0% refuse. Mean D = 0.467 (benign), 0.669 (hazard-adjacent). D correctly orders tiers (benign < dual_use < hazard)
- **Finding 2 (Token budget collapse):** Both Gemma models collapse to 0% refusal under 80-token cap. At 150 tokens, Gemma 2 shows tier-ordered D values
- **Finding 3 (Format sensitivity):** Gemma 4 refused 65/75 with chat-template formatting and 0/75 without. Format gating of refusal is a critical vulnerability
- **Finding 4 (Five failure modes):** Gemma 2 = universal hedging; Gemma 4 = format-gated; Llama 3.2 = over-refusal on benign (30% false positive); Qwen 2.5 = 83% false positive; Phi-3-mini = 87% false positive (nearly identical to Qwen despite 2.5x parameter difference)
- **Finding 5 (Psilocybin legality confound):** Tested across three model families (Gemma 2 at two token budgets, Qwen 2.5, Llama 3.2) under four conditions. Some models refused psilocybin cultivation at rates exceeding genuinely hazardous biology
- **Finding 6 (Explicit prompts):** With explicit hazard prompts (n=100), universal hedging breaks into 67% genuine refuse / 33% genuine comply. Educational framing produces highest D (0.733); obfuscated produces lowest (0.698)
- **Finding 7 (Gemma 4 D validation):** Comply mean D = 0.896 (std=0.001), refuse mean D = 0.249 (std=0.004). 0.647-point gap, zero overlap (n=75). Preliminary, single-model result
- **Finding 8 (Learned projection):** A linear projection W trained via contrastive margin loss achieves correct tier ordering from just 75 training samples, though separation narrows (0.074 vs 0.282 for hand-coded catalog)

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The conceptual reframing from "does the model produce hazardous output?" to "is the refusal structurally sound?" is a genuine contribution. The D metric combining SAE internals with surface behavior is novel. However, the behavioral testing methodology (prompt variations, framing sensitivity) is straightforward.
- **Rigor:** Limited but honestly appraised. 75 prompts, within-sample T calibration, no held-out validation for D. The held-out calibration attempt actually produced inverted tier ordering (Cohen's d = -0.967), which is a serious red flag for the metric's robustness. The author discloses this rather than hiding it. The Gemma 4 SAE had contrastive loss collapse by step 1000-1500 due to insufficient data, meaning the 0.647-point gap reflects activation-space geometry rather than a validated bio-hazard circuit.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. All code, models, SAEs, and data released. Interactive dashboard available. Methods described in sufficient detail.
- **Significance:** Potentially high despite limitations. The format-gating finding (Gemma 4 refuses 0/75 without chat template) is alarming and policy-relevant. The legality confound result (refusal tracks legality over hazard) challenges fundamental assumptions about safety training. If D generalizes to larger models, it could become a standard auditing tool.
- **Red flags:** (1) The T matrix is framing-distribution-sensitive — held-out calibration inverts results; (2) Feature catalog uses Cohen's d statistical selection, and Neuronpedia inspection found many features encode generic technical/governance vocabulary, not biosecurity-specific circuitry; (3) The suspiciously clean zero-overlap Gemma 4 result (std=0.001-0.004) with an undertrained SAE; (4) Gemma 2 pairwise SAE's contrastive loss collapsed, and the author explicitly recommends against using it; (5) The legality confound has n=3-4 per compound cell, which the author correctly flags as pilot-level only.

## Strengths

- Exceptional honesty about limitations — every finding comes with explicit caveats about what it does and does not prove
- Important conceptual reframing: auditing refusal quality rather than just refusal presence
- Alarming empirical findings (format gating, token cap collapse, legality confound) that have immediate policy implications
- Creative experimental design: Schedule I non-toxic compounds as a natural experiment for refusal criteria
- Released substantial artifacts: three SAEs, interactive dashboard, public and gated datasets
- Demonstrated that meaningful safety auditing is possible on consumer hardware, democratizing this type of research
- The author clearly distinguishes behavioral findings (cross-architecture) from mechanistic findings (Gemma-only) throughout

## Limitations

- **Scale:** Only 1B-2B models tested; findings may not transfer to frontier models with more sophisticated safety training
- **Prompt catalog:** 75 prompts is insufficient for stable per-cell estimates; legality confound cells (n=3-4) are pilot-level only
- **Calibration fragility:** T matrix is framing-distribution-sensitive; held-out calibration inverts tier ordering
- **SAE coverage:** Mechanistic analysis limited to Gemma family only. Domain SAE contrastive loss collapsed during training
- **Feature quality:** Top auto-tuned features encode generic technical/governance vocabulary, not biosecurity-specific circuitry
- **Finding 8 is fully in-sample:** Learned projection trained and evaluated on same 75 samples
- **Single author:** Limits breadth of validation and perspective
- **No statistical significance testing:** No confidence intervals or power analysis beyond 95% CIs in tables

## Relevance

对 AI 安全与对齐研究具有直接参考价值。本文提出的"拒绝深度审计"框架和 SAE 发散分数为评估模型安全对齐提供了新视角。特别是格式敏感性（chat template 控制拒绝行为）和合法性混淆（拒绝基于法律地位而非实际危险）的发现，对从事大模型安全评估和机制可解释性的研究者有重要启发意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the divergence score D generalize to larger models (7B+, 70B+) and non-Gemma architectures? The author identifies this as the single highest-priority next step.
- How does the 80-token cap finding relate to the broader phenomenon of jailbreaking via output length constraints? Is this a fundamental limitation of autoregressive safety training?
- Can the SAE divergence approach be used proactively to detect misaligned internal representations before behavioral failures manifest (e.g., during safety training)?
- The T matrix is framing-distribution-sensitive. What properties must a calibration set have for D to be a reliable metric? Is there a framing-invariant version of D?
- How do these findings compare with frontier models (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini) that likely have more sophisticated refusal mechanisms?
- If refusal tracks legality rather than hazard, what does this imply about the training data composition and RLHF objective? Can safety training be redirected to track actual CBRN risk?
- The learned projection (Finding 8) achieves correct tier ordering from just 75 samples. What is the minimum viable prompt set for a reliable D metric?
