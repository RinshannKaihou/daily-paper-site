S-Adam: Singularity-aware Optimization via Randomized Geometric Probing

A drop-in Adam replacement that probes local geometric instability via the variance of randomized directional derivatives, then applies exponential damping to suppress chattering near non-smooth points — with proved O(1/√T) convergence.

Ruoran Xu Borong She Xiaobo Jin Qiufeng Wang cs.LG / cs.AI / math.OC
Key Finding
Randomized smoothing as a diagnostic probe, not a gradient surrogate — the LGI metric detects singularities cheaply.
k=2 forward probes suffice to capture the Clarke subdifferential diameter.

Core insight: The LGI metric has a dual interpretation — it measures the Clarke subdifferential diameter (non-smooth view) and approximates the relative curvature κδ of a smoothed surrogate (smooth view). This unifies both perspectives under one probe.

Theoretical Guarantees

  • Convergence: Almost-sure convergence to (δ,ε)-Clarke stationary points at O(1/√T)
  • Framework: Benaim differential inclusion with strict Lyapunov function — d/dt f(x(t)) ≤ −C · dist(0, ∂C f(x))²
  • Graceful degradation: S-Adam recovers standard Adam dynamics in smooth regions (ρ → 0 or λ = 0)
  • Proximal equivalence: exp(−λρt) ≈ 1/(1+λρt) under first-order approximation — connecting geometric damping to dynamic regularization
  • Generalization: Uniform stability βS-Adam ≤ max(αt) · βAdam, meaning tighter generalization bounds

Assessment

Strengths

  • Genuinely novel idea — repurposing randomized smoothing as a geometric diagnostic, not a gradient surrogate
  • Strong theory — Clarke subdifferential, differential inclusions, Lyapunov analysis, sample complexity, stability bounds
  • Bounded by design — LGI ∈ [0,1) ensures the brake never fully stalls updates
  • Minimal overhead — k=2 probes add only ~1.4% wall-clock on ImageNet-scale QAT
  • Comprehensive ablations on λ, k, δ, learning rate, and batch size

Limitations

  • Weak QAT baselines — ImageNet 8-bit at 8.63% is far below standard (>55%), raising questions about setup generalizability
  • Custom QATNet rather than standard quantized ResNet/MobileNet — unclear if results transfer
  • Only 2 baselines compared (AdamW, Prox-SGD) — no LSQ, PACT, AdaRound, or gradient clipping strategies
  • Image classification only on small-to-mid datasets — no NLP, transformers, or LLMs
  • Short training (10–20 epochs) — unclear if advantages persist in longer regimes

Open Questions

  • How does S-Adam perform on standard QAT benchmarks (quantized ResNet-50 on ImageNet at >55%)?
  • Can LGI be decoupled as a standalone diagnostic or learning-rate scheduler?
  • Benefits for large-scale transformer training (LLMs) where GeLU/SwiGLU replaces ReLU but quantization remains critical?
  • How does perturbation scale δ interact with quantization step size — is there an optimal relationship?
  • Sample complexity bound O(L6/ε4Δ2) seems loose — can tighter bounds or adaptive k schedules improve efficiency?
