> **一句话总结：** BitTP将基于T5-small的LMTraj轨迹预测模型通过BitNet风格的1.58-bit权重量化转换为轻量架构，在ETH/UCY基准上ADE降低14.29%、FDE降低20.97%，同时内存降至53.58%、推理延迟降至63.12%，首次验证了encoder-decoder架构中权重量化作为隐式正则化的有效性。

> **TL;DR:** BitTP converts the T5-small based LMTraj-SUP trajectory predictor into a lightweight BitLLM architecture using 1.58-bit weight-only quantization (BitNet-style). Through systematic comparison of three strategies (weight-only, activation-only, both), the authors find that weight-only quantization is optimal—activations must remain in full precision for stable spatio-temporal reasoning. BitTP-Weight reduces ADE by 14.29% and FDE by 20.97% over the reproduced BF16 baseline on ETH/UCY, while cutting memory to 53.58% and inference latency to 63.12% relative to full quantization. Training overhead is only ~1.2x. The paper also demonstrates CPU-only deployment feasibility, making it relevant for real edge devices without GPUs.

## 核心贡献

- 提出BitTP框架，将Seq2Seq LLM轨迹预测模型（基于T5-small的LMTraj-SUP）转换为BitNet风格的轻量架构
- 系统比较三种量化策略（仅权重、仅激活、全量化），发现仅权重量化（1.58-bit）在encoder-decoder架构中最优
- 发现激活值量化会导致严重退化（ADE从0.35升至1.84，FDE从0.62升至3.12），证明激活精度对时空推理至关重要
- BitTP-Weight超越全精度基线：ADE降14.29%（0.35→0.30），FDE降20.97%（0.62→0.49），量化起到隐式正则化作用
- 内存降至53.58%，推理延迟降至63.12%（相对于全量化基线），训练开销仅~1.2倍
- 验证了在无GPU加速的CPU环境下部署的可行性（补充材料S5节）

## Key Contributions

- Introduces BitTP, the first systematic study of BitNet-style quantization applied to encoder-decoder (Seq2Seq) LLMs for trajectory prediction, extending prior work that focused exclusively on decoder-only models
- Compares three selective quantization strategies—BitLinear-Weight (1.58-bit weights, full-precision activations), BitLinear-Activ (8-bit activations, full-precision weights), and BitLinear-Both (both quantized)—finding that weight-only is optimal
- Demonstrates that activation quantization catastrophically degrades encoder-decoder models: BitTP-Activ achieves 1.84 ADE / 3.12 FDE vs. 0.30 / 0.49 for BitTP-Weight, with step-like training convergence and gradient instability
- Shows quantization-as-regularization: BitTP-Weight surpasses the reproduced BF16 baseline by 14.29% ADE and 20.97% FDE on ETH/UCY
- Achieves practical efficiency gains: 53.58% memory, 63.12% inference cost (relative to full-quantization baseline), with only ~1.2x training overhead
- Validates CPU-only deployment for edge devices without GPU acceleration

## Methodology

**Architecture**: BitTP builds on LMTraj-SUP [2], which uses a T5-small encoder-decoder backbone (6 encoder blocks, 6 decoder blocks, d_model=512, d_ff=2048, 8 heads). The model takes past trajectories encoded as text tokens (via BPE subword tokenizer on pixel-level trajectories) and autoregressively generates future trajectory tokens.

**Quantization Framework**: BitTP replaces all nn.Linear layers in the transformer with BitLinear modules. The replacement targets attention projections (W_Q, W_K, W_V, W_O) in both self-attention and cross-attention, as well as FFN expansion (W_i) and projection (W_o) layers. Three strategies are defined:

1. **BitTP-Weight**: Weights quantized to 1.58-bit (ternary: {-1, 0, +1} with scaling), activations remain full precision. Formula: y = (x * Quant_1.58(beta*W)^T + b) / beta
2. **BitTP-Activ**: Activations quantized to 8-bit after LayerNorm, weights remain full precision. Formula: y = (Quant_8(gamma*LN(x)) * W^T + b) / gamma
3. **BitTP-Both**: Both weights (1.58-bit) and activations (8-bit) quantized, following original BitNet. Formula: y = (Quant_8(gamma*LN(x)) * Quant_1.58(beta*W)^T + b) / (beta*gamma)

All variants use Straight-Through Estimator (STE) for gradient flow during backward pass, with Round Clamp operations for quantization.

**Training**: Single NVIDIA RTX 3090, AdamW optimizer, lr=1e-4 with linear scheduler (no warmup), 8 epochs, batch sizes 128/256, gradient clipping 1.0. Stochastic decoding with temperature 0.7 for inference.

**Evaluation**: ETH/UCY benchmark (5 scenes: ETH, Hotel, Univ, Zara1, Zara2), leave-one-out protocol. 8 observed steps (3.2s) → 12 prediction steps (4.8s). Best-of-K=20 metrics (ADE, FDE) from 1000 samples per prediction. Coordinates converted from pixel to world space via homography.

**Efficiency (Table 3)**: BitTP-Weight achieves 53.58% memory and 63.12% inference cost relative to BitTP-Both baseline, while delivering the best accuracy (0.30 ADE / 0.49 FDE).

**Training Overhead (Table S1)**: BitTP-Weight adds only ~20% training time overhead (avg ~1.2x), while BitTP-Both and BitTP-Activ require ~1.85-1.9x due to activation quantization bottleneck.

**Learning Rate Sensitivity (Table 4)**: BitTP-Weight remains stable across lr={1e-4, 2e-4, 4e-4}, consistently outperforming BF16 baseline. Best at lr=1e-4 (0.42/0.57). BitTP-Activ suffers from training instability at all learning rates.

**Training Dynamics**: BitTP-Weight converges smoothly and monotonically, closely mirroring BF16 baseline. BitTP-Activ exhibits a step-like pattern (flat loss followed by abrupt drops). BitTP-Both shows intermittent loss spikes from accumulated quantization noise.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate. The individual components—BitNet quantization, T5-based trajectory prediction, weight-only quantization—are not novel in isolation. The contribution is the systematic application and analysis of BitNet to encoder-decoder trajectory models, which fills a genuine gap (prior BitNet work focused on decoder-only models). The finding that activation quantization is catastrophic for encoder-decoder architectures is a useful empirical contribution.

- **Rigor**: The experimental design is systematic—three quantization strategies, multiple precision levels, learning rate sensitivity analysis, training dynamics analysis. However, several concerns:
  - The reproduced BF16 baseline (0.35/0.62) significantly underperforms the original LMTraj-SUP (0.22/0.32), making relative improvements easier to claim. The authors attribute improvements over this weaker baseline to "quantization as regularizer," but this could equally reflect that their reproduced baseline is poorly tuned.
  - The paper only evaluates on ETH/UCY—a relatively small-scale benchmark. No evaluation on larger, more challenging datasets like nuScenes or Argoverse.
  - The 14.29%/20.97% improvement numbers are computed relative to the reproduced (weaker) baseline, not the original published numbers. Against the original LMTraj-SUP, BitTP-Weight (0.30/0.49) is actually worse (original: 0.22/0.32).

- **Reproducibility**: Strong. Open-source code, detailed hyperparameters, standardized evaluation protocol.

- **Significance**: Practically useful for edge deployment of LLM-based predictors. The insight that activation precision is critical for encoder-decoder spatio-temporal reasoning is valuable. However, the "quantization improves accuracy" claim is misleading without acknowledging the gap in baseline reproduction.

- **Red flags**:
  - The reproduced baseline significantly underperforms published numbers, and all improvement claims are relative to this weaker baseline.
  - ETH/UCY is a well-saturated benchmark where non-LLM methods already achieve strong results. The LLM-based approach itself may be unnecessarily heavy for this task.
  - No comparison to non-LLM state-of-the-art on efficiency metrics (e.g., GNN-based or MLP-based models that are already lightweight without quantization).
  - The CPU deployment feasibility (Sec. S5) is mentioned but details are in supplementary material that was not fully extractable.

## Strengths

- Systematic comparison of three quantization strategies with clear ablation of what to quantize and what not to
- Practical and actionable insight: weight-only quantization preserves spatio-temporal reasoning; activation quantization destroys it
- Low training overhead (~1.2x) makes the approach practical for practitioners
- Transparent reporting of reproduced vs. original baseline numbers
- Well-designed learning rate sensitivity analysis confirming stability
- Open-source code for reproducibility
- Extends BitNet analysis from decoder-only to encoder-decoder architectures

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on ETH/UCY (5 scenes, pedestrian-only), which is small-scale compared to nuScenes or Argoverse (vehicle trajectories, richer scenes)
- The reproduced BF16 baseline significantly underperforms published numbers (0.35 vs 0.22 ADE average), raising questions about whether improvements come from quantization or baseline quality
- BitTP-Weight (0.30 ADE) does not match the original LMTraj-SUP (0.22 ADE), so the full-precision model with better reproduction would likely outperform the quantized version
- No comparison to lightweight non-LLM baselines that may already be suitable for edge deployment without quantization overhead
- No real edge device benchmarks (e.g., Jetson Nano, Raspberry Pi)—all measurements on RTX 3090
- The paper does not address how trajectory tokenization (pixel-level BPE) interacts with quantization, which could be a confounding factor
- Only one backbone (T5-small) tested; generalization to larger models or other architectures is unverified

## Relevance

该论文对在资源受限设备上部署LLM轨迹预测模型提供了系统性的量化方案分析，核心发现（权重量化可行、激活量化不可行）具有实用参考价值。对于关注自动驾驶或机器人中模型轻量化的研究者值得关注，但需注意其实验仅在ETH/UCY行人数据集上完成，且改进基于较弱的复现基线。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does BitTP-Weight perform against the original (non-reproduced) LMTraj-SUP numbers, and what causes the 59% ADE gap in the reproduced BF16 baseline?
- Would the weight-only quantization benefit persist with a properly tuned baseline that matches published numbers?
- How does BitTP compare to purpose-built lightweight trajectory models (e.g., Social-Implicit, LED) on both accuracy and actual edge-device latency?
- Does the approach generalize to larger trajectory datasets (nuScenes, Argoverse) with more complex multi-agent interactions and longer horizons?
- What are the actual on-device latency and power consumption numbers on real edge hardware (Jetson Orin Nano, Snapdragon)?
- How does the pixel-level BPE tokenization interact with quantization noise—would a different trajectory encoding scheme change the results?
- Can the approach be extended to multi-modal trajectory prediction with explicit mode coverage metrics (minADE_K, minFDE_K for K>20)?
