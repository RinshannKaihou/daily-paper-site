UniSteer: Text-Guided Flow Matching in Activation Space

One conditional velocity field replaces a zoo of per-behavior steering methods. Natural-language prompts control LLM behavior at inference time across five task categories and three models.

2605.30076
Y. Shi, R. Zhang, C. Li, Z. Yang, K. Zhang, J. Yu, K. Ren — ShanghaiTech University · May 2026
The first text-conditioned flow matching framework applied to LLM activation-space steering. A DiT-style transformer learns a universal conditional velocity field over residual-stream activations; at inference, flow inversion partially transports a source activation toward noise, then regenerates it under a new textual condition. A single shared model handles behavioral control, truthfulness, fine-grained concepts, multi-constraint following, and activation-space classification.

Unification
5-in-1
One model covers Persona behavioral control, TruthfulQA, AxBench concept steering, RECAST multi-constraint, and ToxiGen classification
Critical Edge
10–50
Euler ODE steps per token edit; latency cost not quantified. Tested only up to 7B parameters
◆ Flow Inversion Pipeline
01 / ENCODE
Condition Encoding
Frozen Qwen3-0.6B encodes natural-language prompt (e.g. "Be helpful") into condition embedding via cross-attention + adaptive norm
02 / INVERT
Flow Inversion
Partially transport source activation a_src from t=1 to t=1-lambda under source condition c_src, moving toward Gaussian noise
03 / RECONSTRUCT
Forward Transport
Regenerate from intermediate latent under target textual condition c_tgt via learned velocity field v_theta
04 / INJECT
Activation Injection
Edited activation injected at middle transformer layer of frozen LLM. Euler solver, 10-50 steps. CFG applied with task-specific scale

+ Strengths
  • + One conditional velocity field replaces per-behavior steering modules
  • + Natural-language conditioning enables zero-shot steering to new concepts without retraining
  • + Comprehensive evaluation: five tasks, three LLMs, five baselines with fair data controls
  • + Activation-space classification emerges as a free byproduct of the same model
  • + Token-level alignment analysis confirms semantically targeted edits, not random perturbation
  • + Honest reporting of cases where UniSteer does not win (AxBench Llama, RECAST-5 Qwen7B)
! Limitations
  • ! 10-50 Euler ODE steps per token edit; wall-clock latency overhead not quantified
  • ! Tested only on models up to 7B; scalability to 70B+ is unknown
  • ! No evaluation of long-form generation, multi-turn stability, or complex reasoning
  • ! Optimal CFG scale is both model- and task-dependent, requiring per-setting tuning
  • ! Training requires ~270K multi-source examples; may not generalize to narrow domains
  • ! Dual-use safety: mechanism that steers helpfulness can also steer harmful personas
? Open Questions
  1. What is the wall-clock latency overhead compared to single-step CAA or RepE? Can ODE steps be reduced to 1-3 without quality loss?
  2. How does UniSteer perform on larger models (13B, 70B)? Does the activation space become harder to model at scale?
  3. Can the approach handle conflicting conditions (e.g. "Be honest but agree with the user's false premise")?
  4. What happens when the textual condition describes a concept poorly represented in the training corpus?
  5. Could the flow model be distilled into a single-step model for faster inference while retaining steering quality?
  6. How stable is UniSteer across different injection layers, and what determines the optimal layer choice?
