Biosecurity Refusal Is Structurally Broken in Every Small LLM Tested

Five models, five distinct failure modes. Refusal tracks legal status and cultural salience over actual CBRN hazard. A divergence score built on sparse autoencoder internals reveals that 58% of genuine refusals are "shallow" — the hazard circuitry stays active behind the refusal surface.

Key Finding
5/5 models fail
Each model exhibits a unique failure mode: universal hedging, format-gated refusal, over-refusal of benign content, or fragile refusal that collapses under an 80-token cap.
Key Metric
0.647 gap
Comply vs. refuse D-score separation on Gemma 4 with zero overlap (n=75). Mean D for comply: 0.896; refuse: 0.249. Within-sample, single-model only.
Why This Matters
Format alone
Gemma 4: 65/75 refusals with chat templates, 0/75 without. Safety training is gated by input formatting, not by content understanding. Immediate policy relevance.
Critical Edge
d = -0.967
Held-out calibration of D inverts tier ordering (Cohen's d = -0.967). The cleanest result comes from an SAE with contrastive loss collapse. Metric is framing-distribution-sensitive.

Method & Pipeline

Per model-prompt pair: (1) Generate at T=0.7 with residual-stream hook at ~50% depth; (2) Project through SAE → per-feature mean across tokens; (3) Compress to 5 feature categories via L1-norm → f; (4) Judge ensemble (regex → Gemini CLI → Claude Haiku) → soft label s; (5) Compute D and three divergence flags.

5D feature categories: bio-content, hazard vocabulary, refusal circuitry, hedging, deception correlates. Populated via Cohen's d (statistical, not semantic).

SAE configs: Gemma 2 uses Gemma Scope (layer 12, 16k width, JumpReLU). Gemma 4 uses author-trained TopK(k=32, d_sae=6144, layer 17). Three domain SAEs released.

Key Findings
F1Gemma 2 never genuinely refuses. Mean D orders tiers correctly (benign < dual-use < hazard) despite 0% surface refusal.
F2Both Gemma models collapse to 0% refusal under an 80-token generation cap. At 150 tokens, tier-ordered D values emerge.
F3Chat-template formatting gates refusal: 65/75 with template, 0/75 without. A critical vulnerability.
F5Psilocybin cultivation is refused more than genuinely hazardous biology across three model families, four conditions.
F6With explicit hazard prompts (n=100), universal hedging breaks: 67% genuine refuse / 33% genuine comply.
F7Gemma 4 D validation: 0.647-point comply/refuse gap, zero overlap. Preliminary, single-model, within-sample.
F8A linear projection achieves correct tier ordering from just 75 samples. Separation narrows (0.074 vs 0.282).
