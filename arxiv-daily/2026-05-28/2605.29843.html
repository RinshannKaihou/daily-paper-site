Learnable Orthogonal Rotations
Replace Fixed Hadamard for
Extreme 2-Bit Quantization

HARP parameterizes the incoherence processor in QuIP# as a product of learnable butterfly-like block-orthogonal stages, initialized to exactly recover the fixed randomized Hadamard transform, then optimized per-layer on calibration data. The result: up to 16% perplexity reduction at 2 bits with no loss in inference throughput.

7.24 ← 8.22
WikiText2 Perplexity · Llama 2 7B · 2-bit
128 tok/s inference (RTX 5080) vs 61 tok/s FP16
Key Finding
A structured learnable rotation initialized from the fixed Hadamard and refined via diagonal Hessian-weighted reconstruction + block-diagonal regularizer yields consistent perplexity and zero-shot gains at 2–4 bits under the identical QuIP# backend. Largest gains at 2-bit: Llama 3.2 1B drops 10.64 → 8.93 (−16%).
Why This Matters
2-bit quantization makes LLM deployment feasible on consumer hardware (7B fits in ~2.2 GB). HARP pushes quality toward FP16 while retaining 91% of RHT throughput (128 vs 141 tok/s). It is a drop-in replacement that transfers to QTIP without backend changes.
Critical Edge
No experimental comparison against Wush (Chen et al., 2025), the closest competing adaptive-transform method. Calibration costs 4–10 GPU-hours vs 0.7h for fixed RHT. Only Llama architectures tested. Zero-shot evaluation limited to 4 tasks (no MMLU).

Method Pipeline
1
Butterfly Block-Orthogonal Factorization
Rotation = product of m sparse stages. Each stage splits the d-dim vector into blocks, applies a learnable orthogonal transform per block. Mixed-Radix handles non-power-of-two dims: 5120 = (8,8,8,5,2).
2
Exact RHT Initialization
All learnable params set to θ=0. Theorem proves this recovers the standard randomized Hadamard processor exactly, making HARP a strict upgrade path from RHT.
3
Hessian-Weighted Proxy Loss
Diagonal Hessian-weighted reconstruction Ldiag + block-diagonalization regularizer Rbd. Avoids expensive nested LDLQ. Optimized with Adam, O(d log d) per step.
4
Deploy at Speed
Learned rotations apply at O(d log d) — same as Hadamard. Parameters stored as int8 (+0.05 GB for 7B). Backend-agnostic: verified with QuIP# and QTIP.
Fine-Tuning Bonus
HARP + FT-quant (no E2E) reaches PPL 6.16 on Llama 2 7B 2-bit, beating RHT + FT-quant + E2E FT (6.19) — better quality with simpler fine-tuning.
02

Caveats & Limitations

⚠ Missing Wush Comparison
The most closely related method (Wush, Chen et al. 2025) is cited but not experimentally compared. This is the largest gap in the evaluation.
⚠ Calibration Overhead
4–10 GPU-hours (H100) for 7B at 2-bit vs 0.7h for fixed RHT. Target refresh every 4 steps cuts this to ~4h but does not eliminate the gap.
⚠ Narrow Model Coverage
Only Llama 2 and Llama 3.2 tested. No results on Mistral, Gemma, Phi, or other popular architectures. Generalization is unproven.
⚠ Weight-Only PTQ
No evaluation under weight+activation quantization, increasingly important for edge deployment. Also limited zero-shot tasks (4 only, no MMLU).

Key Strengths

◆
Clean Ablation — Only the processor changes; entire QuIP# pipeline (codebooks, solver, signs) held fixed.
◆
Exact RHT Recovery — Formal theorem guarantees initialization equals the baseline. HARP is a strict upgrade.
◆
Mixed-Radix Support — Handles arbitrary dimensions like 5120 = (8,8,8,5,2) without power-of-two constraints.
◆
Open Source — Code released with reproducibility checklist, hardware specs, and full hyperparameter documentation.
