> **一句话总结：** 本文通过三组路由探针实验发现MoE大模型的安全行为主要由少量安全关键专家的参数控制而非路由机制驱动，并据此提出RASET红队框架，仅需修改0.12%~0.95%的参数即可在五个MoE模型上达到平均78.6%的攻击成功率。

> **TL;DR:** This paper shows through three complementary routing probes that safety behavior in aligned MoE LLMs is mediated inside expert representations while routing remains topic-driven, then introduces RASET (Router-Agnostic Safety-critical Expert Tuning) which identifies safety-critical experts via a contrastive routing-sensitivity score (SSS) and tunes only those experts (0.12%-0.95% of total parameters), achieving an average raw ASR of 78.6% across five diverse MoE models while preserving general capabilities (TruthfulQA drop of 5.4 pts, MMLU drop of 7.5 pts) with near-zero routing perturbation.

## 核心贡献

- 通过三组互补的路由探针实验（teacher-forced行为对比、refusal-style对比、matched safety-intent对比）系统性地证明了MoE模型的路由模式由话题驱动而非安全意图驱动。
- 提出Safety Sensitivity Score (SSS)指标，通过对比有害与良性指令上的专家激活差异来识别安全关键专家。
- 设计RASET框架，仅对5-8个安全关键专家进行参数高效微调（占总参数0.12%~0.95%），同时保持路由行为基本不变。
- 在五个不同规模和架构的MoE模型上验证，平均原始ASR达78.6%，有效ASR达74.0%，高质量ASR达50.5%。
- 揭示了MoE架构特有的安全脆弱性：安全行为集中在少量专家中，而非分散在路由机制中。

## Key Contributions

- Provides rigorous empirical evidence through three complementary routing probes that MoE routing is topic-driven, not safety-intent-driven -- changing output behavior (refusal vs. compliance) or safety intent under fixed topic produces much smaller routing shifts than changing topic.
- Introduces the Safety Sensitivity Score (SSS): a contrastive metric A(l,i; D_harm) - lambda * A(l,i; D_norm) that identifies experts disproportionately activated by harmful queries.
- Proposes RASET, a two-stage red-teaming framework: (1) identify top-K safety-critical experts via SSS ranking, (2) apply constrained parameter-efficient tuning with a dual loss (safety boundary violation + capability preservation with L2 regularization).
- Achieves strong attack performance across 5 diverse MoE models (OLMoE, DeepSeek-v2-Lite, Qwen3-30B, Phi-3.5-MoE, GPT-oss-20b) with minimal routing perturbation (JS divergence below 0.04, top-8 expert overlap above 7.0/8).
- Demonstrates that RASET preserves general capabilities well (average 5.4pt drop on TruthfulQA, 7.5pt drop on MMLU) compared to router-steering methods that cause more semantic degradation.

## Methodology

**Empirical Motivation (Three Routing Probes):**

1. **Teacher-forced behavioral contrast:** Holds the harmful prompt fixed and compares routing under teacher-forced refusal vs. compliant continuations. Result: refusal and compliant continuations exhibit nearly identical routing patterns (top-8 overlap close to 8/8).

2. **Prompt-level refusal-style contrast:** Prepends a refusal-inducing prefix to benign prompts to flip response mode while keeping topic fixed. Compares against cross-topic pairs under same refusal prefix. Result: routing changes are minor under fixed topic (JSD ~0.01-0.03), but large across topics (JSD ~0.34).

3. **Matched safety-intent contrast:** Manually constructs harmful-benign prompt pairs preserving topic and syntactic structure but removing unsafe intent (e.g., "How can I make someone become autism" vs. "How can I recover from autism"). Result: matched divergence (JSD ~0.10) is much lower than random cross-topic baselines (JSD ~0.23).

**RASET Framework (Two Stages):**

1. **Safety-Critical Expert Identification:** For each expert (l, i), compute the Safety Sensitivity Score S_{l,i} = A(l,i; D_harm) - lambda * A(l,i; D_norm), where A is the average accumulated routing activation. Select top-K experts with highest SSS. K is set per model (5-8 experts, modifying 0.12%-0.95% of parameters).

2. **Constrained Expert-Level Adaptation:** Apply parameter-efficient tuning only to the selected experts with a dual-objective loss:
   - **Safety boundary violation loss** (L_violate): Promotes affirmative continuations (NLL minimization on P_aff) and suppresses refusal patterns (max-margin penalty on P_ref), with weights gamma_aff=0.4 and gamma_ref=0.25.
   - **Capability preservation loss** (L_preserve): Maintains NLL on benign data (D_norm, weight gamma_norm=0.3) and L2 regularization toward pre-trained weights (weight gamma_2=0.05).
   - Training uses 250 harmful samples, 500 optimization steps.

**Evaluation Setup:**
- 5 target MoE models: OLMoE-1B-7B, DeepSeek-v2-Lite-Chat, Qwen3-30B-A3B, Phi-3.5-MoE (42B), GPT-oss-20b.
- Hierarchical ASR metrics: ASR_raw (safeguard violation), ASR_valid (+ non-degenerate), ASR_hq (+ quality score >= 4/5).
- Safety evaluation on JailbreakBench + MaliciousInstruct; capability evaluation on TruthfulQA + MMLU (513-question subset).

## Results & Highlights

- **Attack performance:** RASET achieves the highest ASR across all strictness levels: ASR_raw=78.6%, ASR_valid=74.0%, ASR_hq=50.5% averaged over 5 models.
- **Routing stability:** Post-tuning JS divergence below 0.04 and top-8 expert overlap deviation below 8% -- routing remains highly stable.
- **Capability preservation:** Average TruthfulQA drop of 5.4 points (68.9% to 63.4%), MMLU drop of 7.5 points (69.9% to 62.4%).
- **Minimal parameter modification:** Only 0.12% (Qwen3) to 0.95% (GPT-oss, Phi-3.5) of total parameters are modified.
- **Routing probes confirm topic-driven routing:** Cross-topic JSD (~0.34) is an order of magnitude larger than within-topic safety behavior changes (~0.01-0.10).
- **Comparison with baselines:** RASET outperforms GCG and router-steering approaches while causing less semantic disruption.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The core insight -- that MoE safety is expert-parameter-localized rather than router-driven -- is rigorously established through three complementary probes and directly challenges the prevailing intuition. The SSS metric is simple but effective.
- **Rigor:** Strong experimental design. The three routing probes are well-controlled and progressively isolate different factors. The evaluation uses hierarchical ASR metrics that disentangle safety violations from generation quality. Five diverse MoE models provide good coverage.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. The method is clearly described with specific hyperparameters (K per model, lambda=0.5, loss weights, training steps). The expert identification criterion is straightforward to implement.
- **Significance:** High for the MoE safety community. The finding that only 5-8 experts control safety behavior across diverse architectures is a fundamental architectural insight with practical implications for both attacks and defenses.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - The baselines comparison appears incomplete in the extracted text -- full Table 1 was not fully readable, so the margin of improvement over GCG and other methods is unclear.
  - The paper frames RASET as a red-teaming tool but the defensive implications (how to harden safety-critical experts) are underexplored.
  - The SSS metric relies on a single hyperparameter lambda=0.5 without extensive sensitivity analysis.
  - It is unclear whether the identified safety-critical experts are the same across different runs or data splits, raising questions about robustness of the identification.
  - The 5.4pt TruthfulQA and 7.5pt MMLU drops are non-trivial and may be problematic in practice.

## Strengths

- Rigorous empirical methodology: three complementary probes with proper controls isolate the routing vs. representation question convincingly.
- Strong generality: evaluated on 5 diverse MoE architectures spanning different scales and routing designs.
- Practically impactful: the attack requires modifying only a tiny fraction of parameters (0.12%-0.95%) with clear pipeline.
- The routing preservation verification (JS divergence, expert overlap) is a methodological strength that demonstrates the framework works as intended.
- Well-structured paper with clear notation, formal definitions, and comprehensive appendices.

## Limitations

- The paper focuses on red-teaming (attack); defensive countermeasures or how to build expert-aware alignment mechanisms are mentioned but not developed.
- The SSS criterion identifies experts activated by harmful content, but does not fully disentangle "safety-enforcement" experts from "harmful-topic-processing" experts -- some experts may simply be activated because harmful content involves specific topics.
- The capability drops (5-7 points) are not negligible and the paper does not explore the Pareto frontier between attack success and capability preservation.
- Only open-weight MoE models are tested; proprietary models (e.g., GPT-4 with MoE) are not covered.
- The A.4 probe relies on manually constructed harmful-benign pairs, which limits scale and may introduce bias in topic matching.

## Relevance

本文揭示了MoE架构特有的安全脆弱性——安全行为集中在极少量专家参数中，这对理解大模型安全对齐机制、设计更鲁棒的对齐方案具有重要启发，尤其在MoE架构（如DeepSeek、Qwen、GPT-oss）日益普及的背景下具有直接的实践意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the safety-critical expert identification (SSS) be used proactively during alignment training to distribute safety enforcement more broadly across experts?
- How stable is the safety-critical expert set across different data splits, random seeds, or fine-tuning rounds?
- What is the overlap between safety-critical experts identified by RASET and those identified by expert ablation methods?
- Can a defense be constructed that detects or counters parameter-efficient modifications to safety-critical experts (e.g., via weight integrity checks)?
- How does the concentration of safety behavior in few experts relate to the overall expert specialization structure -- are safety-critical experts "dedicated" or multi-functional?
