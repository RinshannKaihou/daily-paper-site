> **一句话总结：** 本文通过表征层面的对比探针分析发现，多族VLM普遍存在"垂直-距离纠缠"现象（将图像中的"更高"等价于"更远"），并提出了SpatialTunnel合成基准来解耦这种透视偏差，证明内部空间表征结构比基准准确率更能预测模型的鲁棒性。

> **TL;DR:** This paper reveals that VLMs across multiple families (Molmo, NVILA, Qwen) systematically conflate vertical image position with 3D depth -- a "vertical-distance entanglement" that mirrors perspective projection bias. The authors introduce contrastive probing of internal embeddings and a synthetic benchmark (SpatialTunnel) that decouples vertical position from depth, showing that models with well-separated spatial axes (e.g., RoboRefer, Qwen3-VL-235B) are substantially more robust. Benchmark accuracy alone is misleading; representation structure is a better predictor of true spatial reasoning capability.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示垂直-距离纠缠现象：** 发现VLM普遍将图像中的垂直位置（高/低）与深度（远/近）混淆，即"更高的物体=更远的物体"，这是一种源于自然照片透视投影的系统性捷径（shortcut）。
- **表征层面的对比探针框架：** 提出通过交换对象顺序构建最小对比问题对，提取隐藏状态差值向量（delta vectors），量化空间轴的一致性（Coh）和垂直-距离纠缠指数（VD-EI）。
- **SpatialTunnel合成基准：** 设计隧道形合成场景，使物体垂直位置与深度完全解耦，消除自然图像中的透视相关性，暴露模型的空间捷径偏差。
- **数据缩放的悖论：** 即使增加空间训练数据使基准准确率提升，纠缠程度反而可能加剧（如Molmo的VD-EI从0.279升至0.474），表明仅靠数据缩放无法解决表征层面的结构性缺陷。
- **表征结构预测鲁棒性：** 具有高距离一致性（CohD）和低VD-EI的模型（如RoboRefer、Qwen3-VL-235B）在多个空间推理基准上表现更稳定，说明内部表征质量比表面准确率更能反映真实的空间推理能力。

## Key Contributions

- **Reveals vertical-distance entanglement as a pervasive VLM bias:** Across Molmo, NVILA, and Qwen families, models systematically conflate "higher in image" with "farther from camera," producing large accuracy gaps (up to 36.9 pp on counter-heuristic examples in EmbSpatial-Bench).
- **Introduces representation-level contrastive probing:** Constructs minimal question pairs (swapping object order) to extract delta vectors from intermediate-layer hidden states, measuring axis coherence (Coh) and a novel VD-Entanglement Index (VD-EI).
- **Creates SpatialTunnel, a bias-controlled synthetic benchmark:** A tunnel-geometry Blender environment where objects at fixed depths are swept across angular positions, decoupling vertical image position from 3D depth and enabling heatmap-style diagnostics.
- **Demonstrates that data scaling alone is insufficient:** Spatial fine-tuning improves benchmark accuracy but can worsen vertical-distance entanglement (e.g., Molmo VD-EI: 0.279 base -> 0.474 at 2M), suggesting the shortcut is reinforced rather than resolved by scale.
- **Shows representation structure predicts cross-benchmark robustness:** Models with well-separated spatial axes (RoboRefer: CohD=0.182, VD-EI=0.362; Qwen3-VL-235B) consistently outperform those with entangled representations across EmbSpatial-Bench, CV-Bench, and BLINK.

## Methodology

**Three-pronged approach:**

1. **Behavioral analysis on existing benchmarks (Section 3):** Classify depth-related questions in EmbSpatial-Bench and CV-Bench-3D into "consistent" (farther object is higher in image) and "counter" (farther object is lower) examples. Find that consistent examples dominate (80.9% and 60.5%) and models consistently fail on counter examples, with accuracy gaps of 3.4--42.2 percentage points.

2. **SpatialTunnel synthetic benchmark (Section 4):** Built in Blender, a tunnel-shaped scene where two objects are placed at fixed depths but swept across 16 angular positions on the cross-section, yielding a 16x16 grid of (theta_1, theta_2) configurations. This independently controls vertical image position and depth ordering. Models are evaluated with binary depth-comparison questions, and accuracy is reported as a probability-based correctness score with consistent/counter partitioning.

3. **Contrastive probing of internal representations (Section 5):** For each spatial VQA, construct a minimal pair by swapping the queried object order (e.g., "Is A left/right of B?" vs. "Is B left/right of A?"), extract final-token hidden states at an intermediate layer, and compute delta vectors. Define:
   - **Axis coherence (Coh):** Mean pairwise cosine similarity among sign-corrected delta vectors for each spatial axis (horizontal, vertical, distance). Higher = more internally consistent encoding.
   - **VD-Entanglement Index (VD-EI):** Measures directional coupling between vertical and distance mean delta vectors. Positive = perspective-aligned entanglement; zero = independence.

**Models evaluated:** Molmo-7B-O-0924, NVILA-Lite-2B, Qwen2.5-VL-3B (each with base and fine-tuned variants at 80k/400k/800k/2M samples), RoboRefer-2B-SFT (shared NVILA base, 20M+ samples), and Qwen3-VL-235B-A22B as a large-scale reference.

## Results & Highlights

- **Existing benchmarks are heavily skewed:** EmbSpatial-Bench has 80.9% consistent vs. 10.7% counter examples; CV-Bench-3D has 60.5% vs. 10.8%. This skew inflates reported accuracy.
- **Massive accuracy gaps on counter examples:** Qwen2.5-VL (2M) achieves 60.9% on consistent but only 24.0% on counter examples in EmbSpatial-Bench (36.9 pp gap). Even the best model (RoboRefer) shows a 27.3 pp gap.
- **Entanglement intensifies with scaling:** Molmo's VD-EI increases from 0.279 (base) to 0.474 (2M fine-tuned), even as overall accuracy improves. This suggests that standard spatial training data reinforces the shortcut.
- **Distance coherence is consistently the weakest axis:** CohD is the lowest across all models (often < 0.1), while horizontal and vertical coherence are substantially higher.
- **Cross-domain validity:** CohD computed on SpatialTunnel correlates with counter accuracy on EmbSpatial-Bench (rho=0.759) and CV-Bench-3D (rho=0.804), both p < 0.001.
- **RoboRefer and Qwen3-VL-235B stand out:** These models show three clearly separated PCA clusters for horizontal/vertical/distance axes, the highest CohD, and the lowest VD-EI, corresponding to the best cross-benchmark performance.
- **Models with similar benchmark scores can have very different internals:** NVILA (2M) achieves 93.8% on CV-3D Depth but only 62.9% on BLINK Spatial Relation, while Qwen (2M) shows the reverse pattern, yet both have similar EmbSpatial-Bench overall scores.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. The representation-level analysis of spatial axes in VLMs is novel and goes beyond behavioral benchmarking. The contrastive probing framework and VD-EI metric are clean, well-motivated contributions. SpatialTunnel is a clever design choice -- the tunnel geometry elegantly decouples vertical position from depth.

**Rigor:** Strong. The paper combines behavioral evidence (existing benchmarks), controlled synthetic evaluation (SpatialTunnel), and internal representation analysis (contrastive probing) into a coherent narrative. The consistent/counter split is clearly defined and systematically applied. Statistical significance is reported (p-values for correlations).

**Potential concerns:**
- **Limited model coverage for probing:** Only NVILA family and RoboRefer share the same architecture, enabling direct comparison of training effects. The Molmo and Qwen families each follow their own trajectory, making cross-family conclusions somewhat speculative.
- **RoboRefer as an outlier:** RoboRefer uses 20M+ samples including RGB-D data and different training procedures. The paper treats it as an "illustrative reference" rather than a controlled comparison, which is honest but limits causal claims about what specifically creates better representations.
- **SpatialTunnel is synthetic:** While the controlled environment is a strength for isolating bias, the gap between synthetic and real-world performance remains an open question. Models might behave differently on natural images with richer depth cues.
- **Fine-tuning data mixing:** Five different spatial datasets are mixed without ablation of individual dataset contributions, making it unclear which data sources help or hurt entanglement.
- **Layer selection:** The choice of intermediate layer for probing is model-specific and justified empirically, but the sensitivity of results to layer choice is not deeply explored.

**Significance:** This paper has important implications for how the community evaluates spatial reasoning in VLMs. The finding that benchmark accuracy can be misleading -- and that internal representation structure is a more reliable indicator -- challenges current evaluation practices.

## Strengths

- Clean, three-pronged methodology that builds a compelling case from behavioral evidence to controlled synthetic evaluation to representation-level analysis.
- The VD-EI metric is intuitive and well-defined, providing a quantitative diagnostic for a previously qualitative concern.
- SpatialTunnel is an elegant design that should become a standard tool for spatial bias evaluation.
- Honest about limitations: treats RoboRefer as a reference rather than making strong causal claims.
- Comprehensive experiments across multiple model families and training scales.
- PCA visualizations of delta vectors are highly informative and clearly illustrate the entanglement phenomenon.

## Limitations

- The causal mechanism behind entanglement formation remains unclear -- is it purely a data bias, or do architectural choices matter?
- No proposed remedy beyond "more and better training data" -- the paper is diagnostic rather than prescriptive.
- Synthetic benchmark may not fully transfer to real-world spatial reasoning scenarios.
- The study focuses on relative depth comparisons; other spatial reasoning tasks (e.g., metric depth estimation, multi-object spatial arrangements) are not covered.
- Limited exploration of whether architectural interventions (e.g., depth-aware encoders, 3D-aware attention) could mitigate entanglement.

## Relevance

本文对理解VLM空间推理的本质偏差具有重要意义。如果你的研究涉及具身智能、机器人视觉或VLM的空间理解能力评估，这项工作揭示了现有基准的系统性缺陷，并提供了一种表征层面的诊断方法，有助于设计更鲁棒的空间推理模型。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can architectural modifications (e.g., incorporating monocular depth estimation as an auxiliary task, or 3D-aware attention mechanisms) mitigate vertical-distance entanglement during pretraining?
2. How does the entanglement behave in video-based VLMs where temporal depth cues (motion parallax) are available?
3. Is the consistent/counter split also skewed in training data, and could balanced data curation alone resolve the issue?
4. Do multimodal models trained with RGB-D or 3D point cloud inputs exhibit similar entanglement, or is this purely a 2D-image training artifact?
5. How transferable are the CohD and VD-EI metrics to other domains of relational reasoning (e.g., temporal relations, causal reasoning)?
