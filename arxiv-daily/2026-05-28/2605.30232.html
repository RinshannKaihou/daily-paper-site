Experiment Design

A semantically neutral emoji maze where tile types are office/food emoji chosen for near-zero sentiment—decoupling reward from language.

  • Three tile types: MOLD (−10), GOLD (+20), PATH (−0.1)
  • Wind: random move overrides keep MOLD on-policy
  • Tile melting: visited tiles convert to MOLD
  • Shuffled prompt ordering mitigates directional bias
  • Concept vectors extracted via difference-in-means at layer l* (max AUROC separability)

The Recruitment Hypothesis

The central claim: reward vectors don't create new structure. They rotate onto a pre-existing evaluative axis present before any maze training.

  • Steering maze-naive models with trained vectors reproduces all four behavioral effects
  • Pretrain-only models show muted but present effects
  • During training, vectors gradually rotate into alignment with pre-existing sentiment/valence axes
  • Axis tracks more than confidence: within confidence tertiles, correct vs. incorrect still separates (d > 1.1)

Critical Assessment

Strengths

  • Neutral emoji maze isolates reward from semantics—genuinely clever design
  • 10 control models across every plausible confound
  • Multiple converging lines: pre-existence, pretrain-only, gradual rotation, cross-algorithm persistence
  • GSM8K backtracking example is vivid and compelling
  • Within-confidence-bin analysis cleanly rules out confidence-only interpretation
  • Open-source code; 43 figures, 32 tables of thorough documentation

Limitations

  • Scope Only one training environment (maze), one non-Qwen family
  • Validation LLM judges for sentiment/backtracking/refusal not validated against human ratings
  • Framing "Functional welfare" terminology will be misread as consciousness claims
  • Practical Shows mechanism but not how to exploit or mitigate it
  • Strength Pretrain-only effects are muted—hard to draw strong pre-training origin claims
  • Method Off-policy synthetic trajectories, not the trained policy's own rollouts
