Mask the Target: A Plug-and-Play Regularizer Against LoRA Forgetting

Target-Masked KL (TMKL) drops LoRA catastrophic forgetting from 20-42% down to 1-4% with a single loss term, no replay data, and zero inference overhead.

Headline Claim
20-42% → 1-4%
Retention drift slashed. WikiText PPL drift drops from +37% to +4% on Qwen2.5-0.5B adapted to OpenR1-Math.
Anchor Fact
89-95%
Of retention drift prevented. λ=1 is Pareto-optimal at both 0.5B and 7B scales without tuning.
So What
One-line loss, zero overhead
A drop-in regularizer compatible with any LoRA variant. No replay data, no architecture change, no inference cost.
Critical Edge
~50% train cost
Extra frozen-base forward pass per step. Scoped to single-target, text-only autoregressive LLMs.
Mechanism

How TMKL Works

Base + Adapted distributions → Remove ground-truth token → Renormalize → KL divergence
L = L_CE + λ · L_TMKL   ——   λ = 1 Pareto-optimal across scales

Masking the target token eliminates the gradient conflict between cross-entropy and the regularizer that plagues standard full-KL distillation (LwF).

Theory

Fisher-Weighted Jacobian Interpretation

TMKL ≈ ΔφT · JT · F · J · Δφ
J = LoRA Jacobian  |  F = Fisher of non-target categorical

The penalty depends only on the adapter's effect on non-target logits, not its parameterization. This explains adapter-agnosticity.

Empirical 1/λ scaling law: WT-103 drift slope = -0.94 vs predicted -1.0 (6% error).

Strengths

Why This Matters

  • Elegantly simple core idea with deep theoretical justification (Fisher, Pinsker, scaling law).
  • Adapter-agnostic: works with LoRA, SineLoRA, RandLoRA, DoRA, PISSA, AdaLoRA, VeRA unmodified.
  • Zero inference overhead — regularizer is discarded after training.
  • Exceptionally thorough evaluation: 5 adapters, 6 backbones, 3-seed averages, all baselines tuned on same validation slice.
  • Target-token gradient conflict analysis is a clear conceptual contribution beyond the method itself.
Limitations

Caveats & Scope

  • Single-target adaptation only; sequential continual learning not addressed.
  • Text-only autoregressive LLMs; no vision-language or multimodal evaluation.
  • ~50% training overhead from extra base model forward pass per step.
  • Gains most dramatic under distribution shift; related-domain fine-tuning sees smaller benefits.
  • Retention probes primarily perplexity-based; broader functional evaluations would strengthen claims.
  • Renormalization over ~152K tokens could amplify noise from very low-probability entries.
