PPO Critics Collapse in LLM RL —
Hista Fixes It for Free

In LLM reinforcement learning, PPO critics degenerate into group-average baselines—providing zero token-level credit assignment. Hista uses hidden-state similarity to weight rewards across rollouts, achieving +0.5 to +1.7 average accuracy gains across 0.5B–14B models with no extra training cost.

Chen, Dong, Tian, Yang, Chen, Chen & Cheng · CUHK & Huawei · 28 May 2026
Key Finding
Critic = Baseline
PPO critics in LLM RL collapse to the same group-average estimates as GRPO, rendering the entire critic network useless for fine-grained credit assignment.
Key Metric
+1.7 avg pts
Best single-model gain: CSIPO+Hista on Qwen2.5-1.5B Instruct across math, science, general QA, and programming. Matches MCS@2 accuracy at GRPO-level cost.
Why This Matters
A free lunch for LLM post-training
Hista needs no extra training, no extra rollouts, and adds negligible overhead—yet consistently outperforms GRPO across model sizes, architectures, and domains.
Critical Edge
Sub-2% gains, bounded theory
Improvements are consistent but modest. Theorems assume single-token answers. No evaluation beyond 14B. GSPO incompatibility remains unresolved.

■ Two Solutions

Numca + Hista

Σ Numca

Treats math reasoning as goal-reaching. Uses numerical patterns (integers, decimals, fractions) as verifiable milestones for state abstraction. Simple dictionary lookup.

Domain-Specific

Σ Hista

Uses LLM hidden states as general-purpose representations. Computes MinDistance between states, then performs probability-weighted reward averaging across rollouts. No extra training.

General-Purpose
Theorem 5.2

P(R1 = R2) is inversely correlated with MinDistance(X1, X2)—states with similar hidden states are more likely to receive the same reward.

Theorem 5.5

The hidden-state weighted estimator yields provably better state value estimates than the group-average (GRPO) baseline.

■ Strengths

What Works

  • Clean identification of PPO critic collapse with strong empirical evidence
  • Hista is simple, general, training-free, negligible overhead
  • Formal theoretical guarantees (Theorems 5.2, 5.5)
  • Extensive eval: 3 model families, 4 size ranges, 3 RL algorithms, 8+ benchmarks
  • SVEB provides a reusable diagnostic for the community
  • Public code & published at ICML 2026

■ Limitations

What to Watch

  • Gains are consistent but sub-2% in absolute terms
  • Theory assumes single-token answer parsing; open-ended generation not covered
  • Numca is domain-limited; mainly motivates Hista
  • GSPO incompatibility: fundamental conflict with whole-response optimization
  • No evaluation beyond 14B; 70B+ scalability unknown
  • Individual benchmarks sometimes regress even when averages improve
  • No comparison against PRM or tree-search methods in RL training
