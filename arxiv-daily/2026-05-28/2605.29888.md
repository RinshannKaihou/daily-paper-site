> **一句话总结：** LaRA提出基于逐层表征几何分析的框架，通过三种互补指标（RSM/DC/RSI）检测RL后训练中的数据污染，在多个7B推理模型上实现最高+9.6% AUC提升和3.5倍TPR@FPR=5%的改善，显著优于现有输出级别的检测基线方法。

> **TL;DR:** LaRA detects data contamination in RL post-trained LLMs by analyzing layer-wise representation geometry under controlled perturbations, using three complementary metrics (RSM for perturbation sensitivity, DC for directional collapse, RSI for local rigidity). Across three 7B RL-trained reasoning models (Eurus-2-7B-PRIME, LIMR, Olmo-3.1-7B), the detection protocol achieves up to +9.6% AUC improvement and 3.5x higher TPR@FPR=5% compared to the strongest output-level baseline (Self-Critique). The key insight is that RL-induced memorization produces abnormal representation responses: contaminated samples are overly stable to semantic variants yet exhibit disproportionately large shifts when memorized information is removed.

## 核心贡献

- 首次提出基于表征级别（representation-level）的框架来检测RL后训练中的数据污染问题，并建立了完整的训练与评估体系
- 提出三种互补的逐层几何指标：表征偏移幅度（RSM）衡量扰动敏感性、方向坍缩（DC）衡量表征方向集中度、表征稳定性指数（RSI）衡量局部表征刚性
- 发现数据污染在模型各层产生渐进式几何偏差：RL训练逐步放大了污染样本与干净样本在内部表征上的差异
- 开发了一种鲁棒的污染检测协议：通过鲁棒标准化（MAD-based）、度量特异性对齐和跨层聚合，实现跨模型、跨训练阶段的稳定检测
- 在三个7B RL推理模型上实现SOTA检测性能：相比最强输出级基线方法Self-Critique，最高提升9.6% AUC，TPR@FPR=5%提升3.5倍

## Key Contributions

- First representation-level framework for detecting data contamination specifically in RL post-training, with a complete training and evaluation setup
- Three complementary layer-wise geometry metrics: Representation Shift Magnitude (RSM) for perturbation sensitivity, Directional Collapse (DC) for directional concentration, Representation Stability Index (RSI) for local rigidity
- Discovery that RL training progressively amplifies geometric deviations between contaminated and clean samples across layers
- Robust detection protocol using MAD-based standardization, metric-specific alignment, and cross-layer aggregation
- State-of-the-art contamination detection on three 7B RL models: up to +9.6% AUC improvement and 3.5x higher TPR@FPR=5% over the strongest output-level baseline

## Methodology

**Problem Formulation:** LaRA frames contamination detection as a Membership Inference Attack (MIA) problem. Given an RL-trained model and a candidate sample, the goal is to determine whether the sample was in the RL training data (member/contaminated) or not (non-member/clean).

**Key Hypothesis:** RL-induced memorization produces abnormal representation responses under controlled perturbations — contaminated samples become overly stable to semantically equivalent variations yet exhibit disproportionately large representation shifts when memorized information is removed.

**Perturbation Construction Pipeline:**
1. Given an original question q0, generate K semantically similar questions (same structure, different numbers) via LLM
2. Apply importance-based blanking (BlankImportant) to remove key information while preserving question structure
3. Generate M paraphrase variants of each blanked question, preserving blank positions

**Three Metrics (computed at each transformer layer l):**

1. **Representation Shift Magnitude (RSM):** Measures how strongly representations change when key information is removed. Computes the z-scored L2 distance between original and blanked representations, standardized against semantic neighbors. High RSM = strong perturbation sensitivity, suggesting memorization.

2. **Directional Collapse (DC):** Measures cosine alignment between the original question's perturbation direction and the average direction of semantic neighbors. High DC = representation changes collapse toward shared low-dimensional directions, indicating reduced representational diversity.

3. **Representation Stability Index (RSI):** Measures local representation variability under paraphrastic perturbations. Computes average L2 distance from local centroid across paraphrase variants, z-scored against neighbors. Low RSI = more rigid and invariant local representation geometry.

**Detection Protocol (3 steps):**
1. Clean-reference robust standardization using median and MAD (50% breakdown point) for outlier resistance
2. Metric-specific anomaly alignment (flip RSI sign since contamination reduces local variability)
3. Layer-wise aggregation: average aligned z-scores across all metrics and layers

**Experimental Setup:**
- Three 7B RL-trained models: Eurus-2-7B-PRIME, LIMR, Olmo-3.1-7B-RL-Zero-Math
- 30 member samples (from each model's RL training set) + 30 non-member samples (AIME 2026) per model
- Continued RL training via GRPO on 1000-sample corpus to track signal evolution
- Six baselines: Recall, CDD, Min-K%, Min-K%++, PPL, Self-Critique (SC)

## Results & Highlights

**Main Detection Results (Table 1):**
- S_LaRA achieves best AUC on LIMR (0.80) with TPR@FPR=5% of 0.46
- Combined SC + S_LaRA achieves strongest overall on Eurus: AUC 0.73, TPR@FPR=5% 0.31 at initialization, improving to (0.79, 0.38) during RL training
- Representation-level signals become increasingly separable during RL optimization
- Up to +9.6% AUC improvement and 3.5x higher TPR@FPR=5% over strongest output-level baseline

**Key Findings:**
- Contaminated samples show sharply elevated RSM around layers 7-9, indicating strong dependence on memorized information
- DC provides the strongest standalone discrimination signal but varies across epochs
- RSM and RSI individually weaker but improve generalization across training stages
- All three metrics are complementary — removing any single component degrades performance (Table 2)
- Contamination-related geometric signals persist throughout the network depth (not isolated to specific layers)

**Ablation Insights:**
- Method is robust to number of blanks (k=1 to k=4): AUC varies from 0.62 to 0.77
- Robust to perturbation types: Info Removal, Distractor Insertion, Number Replacement all yield comparable AUC (0.56-0.69)
- Optimal SC/LaRA mixture weight is model-dependent, but default beta=0.65 achieves competitive performance universally
- Layer-wise analysis reveals DC amplifies in deeper layers during RL training, RSI concentrated in earlier layers

**Error Analysis:**
- False negatives: member samples whose representation geometry stays close to the clean manifold (uniformly low RSM, DC, RSI scores)
- False positives: non-member samples with unusually high DC values that deviate from the typical clean distribution

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The problem of data contamination in RL post-training is genuinely underexplored. Shifting from output-level to representation-level analysis is a meaningful methodological contribution with clear motivation (RL shapes behavior through trajectory-level rewards, not token likelihoods).

- **Rigor: Good.** The paper provides thorough layer-wise analysis across three model families and multiple training checkpoints. The perturbation pipeline is well-designed with semantically controlled variants. The use of robust statistics (MAD-based standardization) for the detection protocol is principled. However, the evaluation set is small (30+30 samples per model), which raises concerns about statistical stability of the reported improvements.

- **Reproducibility: Moderate.** The pipeline relies on GPT-4o-mini for generating similar questions and paraphrases, introducing a dependency on a proprietary model. The code and data are not explicitly mentioned as released. The perturbation generation prompts are provided in the appendix, which is helpful.

- **Significance: High.** As RL post-training becomes standard practice for reasoning models (DeepSeek-R1, OpenAI o-series), detecting contamination in RL training data is crucial for evaluation integrity. The finding that representation geometry provides more reliable signals than output-level methods could influence future auditing practices.

- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - The paper is marked "Work in Progress," meaning results may not be final
  - Evaluation sample size (30 member + 30 non-member per model) is quite small; AUC estimates on 60 samples have wide confidence intervals
  - No evaluation on larger models (>7B) — unclear if findings scale
  - The method requires multiple forward passes per sample (K similar questions x M paraphrase variants x all layers), making it computationally expensive compared to output-level baselines
  - No comparison with the concurrent Kernel Divergence Score (Choi et al., 2025) which also uses representations for contamination analysis, though the authors note it operates at dataset level rather than instance level
  - Some reported improvements are modest (e.g., AUC 0.73 for Eurus initial checkpoint), and the absolute detection performance is far from perfect

## Strengths

- Addresses a real, growing, and underexplored problem as RL post-training becomes ubiquitous
- Principled motivation: output-level detection methods are fundamentally mismatched to RL-trained models
- Clean three-metric framework with clear geometric interpretations
- Thorough ablation studies: metric contributions, perturbation types, perturbation counts, layer windows, beta sweep
- Layer-wise analysis provides interpretable insights into how contamination manifests internally
- Discovery that RL training progressively amplifies contamination signals is valuable for understanding RL dynamics
- The protocol is model-agnostic and does not require access to training data or gradients

## Limitations

- **Computational cost:** Requires generating perturbed variants, extracting intermediate representations across all layers, and aggregating multiple statistics — significantly more expensive than output-level methods
- **Small evaluation set:** 30+30 samples per model provides limited statistical power; some reported gains may not be robust
- **Scale uncertainty:** Only tested on 7B models; unknown whether findings transfer to larger models (70B+)
- **Dependency on LLM for perturbation generation:** Uses GPT-4o-mini for generating similar questions and paraphrases, introducing variability and cost
- **Imperfect detection:** Some contaminated samples produce representation geometry indistinguishable from clean samples, and some clean samples are falsely flagged
- **Single-domain evaluation:** Only math reasoning problems are tested; generalization to other domains (code, science, etc.) is unknown
- **Work in Progress status:** Results and methodology may change in future versions

## Relevance

该工作直接关注RL后训练阶段的数据污染检测问题，与当前主流的LLM推理能力提升方法（如GRPO、RLHF等）密切相关。随着RL post-training成为提升推理能力的标准流程，确保训练数据不被benchmark数据污染对评估结果的可靠性至关重要，对评估和保障RL训练的可靠性具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does LaRA scale to very large models (e.g., 70B+ parameters) where per-layer representation extraction becomes memory-intensive?
- Can the geometric signatures distinguish between contamination and legitimate memorization of training data during standard supervised fine-tuning?
- Is there a threshold of contamination severity below which the method becomes unreliable (e.g., partial overlap, paraphrased contamination)?
- Could the layer-wise geometric signatures be used to identify which specific layers or attention heads are most affected by RL-induced memorization?
- What is the relationship between contamination severity (as measured by LaRA) and downstream performance degradation on the contaminated benchmark?
- Can the perturbation generation be replaced with a simpler, non-LLM-dependent process to reduce computational overhead?
- How does the method perform on non-math domains such as code generation, scientific reasoning, or natural language understanding benchmarks?
