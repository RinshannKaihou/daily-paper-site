Vision-Language Models · Spatial Reasoning

Why Far Looks Up

Probing spatial representation in VLMs reveals a systematic conflation of vertical image position with 3D depth — a "vertical-distance entanglement" that inflates benchmark scores and survives data scaling.

Min, Jung, Lee, Jeon, Su, Tremblay, Song, Park SNU · Ohio State · NVIDIA 2026-05-28
Key Finding
VLMs equate "higher" with "farther"
Across Molmo, NVILA, and Qwen families, models systematically conflate vertical image position with camera depth — a perspective projection shortcut baked into internal representations.
Key Metric
36.9pp gap
Qwen2.5-VL (2M) scores 60.9% on consistent examples but only 24.0% on counter examples in EmbSpatial-Bench. Even RoboRefer shows a 27.3 pp gap.
Why This Matters
Benchmark accuracy is misleading
Internal representation structure predicts cross-benchmark robustness better than surface accuracy. Models with similar scores can have fundamentally different spatial reasoning capabilities.
Critical Edge
More data makes it worse
Molmo's VD-EI climbs from 0.279 to 0.474 with spatial fine-tuning. No architectural remedy is proposed — the paper diagnoses but does not cure the entanglement.
01

Three-Pronged Methodology

PRONG 01
Behavioral Audit of Existing Benchmarks
Classify depth questions in EmbSpatial-Bench and CV-Bench-3D into "consistent" (farther object is higher) and "counter" (farther is lower). Consistent examples dominate: 80.9% vs 10.7%. Models exploit this skew.
PRONG 02
SpatialTunnel Synthetic Benchmark
A Blender tunnel scene places objects at fixed depths across 16 angular positions, yielding a 16x16 grid. This decouples vertical position from depth, exposing models that rely on perspective shortcuts.
PRONG 03
Contrastive Probing of Internal Representations
Swap object order in spatial VQA to create minimal pairs, extract delta vectors from hidden states, and quantify axis coherence (Coh) and vertical-distance entanglement index (VD-EI).
02

Results & Evidence

  • Existing benchmarks are heavily skewed: EmbSpatial-Bench has 80.9% consistent vs only 10.7% counter examples, inflating reported accuracy.
  • Entanglement intensifies with scaling: Molmo's VD-EI rises from 0.279 (base) to 0.474 (2M fine-tuned), even as overall accuracy improves — the shortcut is reinforced, not resolved.
  • Distance coherence (CohD) is consistently the weakest spatial axis across all models, often below 0.1, while horizontal and vertical coherence are substantially higher.
  • CohD on SpatialTunnel correlates with counter accuracy on EmbSpatial-Bench (rho=0.759, p<0.001) and CV-Bench-3D (rho=0.804, p<0.001).
  • RoboRefer and Qwen3-VL-235B show three clearly separated PCA clusters for horizontal/vertical/distance axes, with highest CohD and lowest VD-EI.
03

Assessment

Strengths ▼
  • Clean three-pronged methodology building from behavioral evidence to synthetic evaluation to representation analysis.
  • VD-EI metric is intuitive and well-defined — a quantitative diagnostic for a previously qualitative concern.
  • SpatialTunnel is an elegant design that should become a standard bias-evaluation tool.
  • Honest about limitations: treats RoboRefer as a reference rather than making strong causal claims.
  • PCA visualizations of delta vectors clearly illustrate the entanglement phenomenon.
Limitations & Concerns ▼
  • Limited model coverage for probing: only NVILA family and RoboRefer share architecture, making cross-family conclusions somewhat speculative.
  • RoboRefer uses 20M+ samples with RGB-D data — treated as an illustrative reference, not a controlled comparison.
  • SpatialTunnel is synthetic; the gap between controlled and real-world performance remains open.
  • No proposed remedy beyond "more and better data" — the paper is diagnostic, not prescriptive.
  • The causal mechanism behind entanglement formation is unclear: data bias, architecture, or both?
04

Open Questions

Can architectural modifications (monocular depth auxiliary tasks, 3D-aware attention) mitigate entanglement during pretraining?
How does entanglement behave in video-based VLMs where temporal depth cues (motion parallax) are available?
Is the consistent/counter split also skewed in training data, and could balanced curation alone resolve the issue?
Do models trained with RGB-D or 3D point cloud inputs exhibit similar entanglement, or is this purely a 2D training artifact?
How transferable are CohD and VD-EI to other relational reasoning domains (temporal, causal)?
