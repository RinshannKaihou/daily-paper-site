> **一句话总结：** 本文将LLM成对评估形式化为假设检验问题，通过引入分辨率比率 q=N/N* 诊断指标，揭示两大公开排行榜（Open LLM Leaderboard v1 有 11/40 对、MMLU-Pro 有 4-6/9 对排名对在统计分辨力不足），并严格证明广泛使用的非配对 Cohen-h+(1-ρ) 样本量近似方法在近距比较中系统性低估所需样本量约两倍。

> **TL;DR:** This paper frames paired LLM evaluation as a hypothesis-testing problem and introduces a resolution ratio q=N/N* as the primary diagnostic for leaderboard rankings. On the Open LLM Leaderboard v1, 11/40 pairwise comparisons and 4/9 MMLU-Pro top-10 adjacent pairs are unresolved at (alpha, power) = (0.05, 0.8); clustering raises the MMLU-Pro count to 6/9. A new lemma with explicit second-order constant proves the widely-used unpaired Cohen-h shortcut underestimates N* by ~2x in close-comparison regimes. The unresolved-pair pattern persists under Bonferroni, BH, anytime-valid sequential testing, and cluster correction. Accepted to ICML 2026 Workshop on Hypothesis Testing.

## 核心贡献

- 将LLM成对评估形式化为配对假设检验问题，定义分辨率比率 q=N/N* 和最小可检测效应(MDE)作为排行榜报告的核心指标
- 提出并证明引理1（Lemma 1）：给出了非配对 Cohen-h+(1-ρ) 近似与正确配对样本量 N* 之间偏差的精确二阶常数 C(p,ρ)，证明在近距比较中 n_h ≈ N*/2
- 揭示三大主流统计工具（Cohen 1988教材公式、G*Power 3.1、R pwr 包）在用户后乘(1-ρ)时均继承此约两倍低估偏差
- 在 Open LLM Leaderboard v1（40对）和 MMLU-Pro（9对相邻排名）上进行端到端分辨率分析，配合前瞻性验证（经验 McNemar 功效达 0.80±0.03）
- 系统性稳健性检验：多重比较（Bonferroni/Holm/BH）、真实学科层面聚类（K=14，ICC校正）、leave-one-subject-out、类别自助法（99.9% 重采样支持5-6/9未分辨结论）、anytime-valid序贯检验
- 发布 pip 安装包 `llm-power`，暴露完整诊断 API

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes paired LLM evaluation as a hypothesis-testing problem with a resolution ratio q=N/N* and minimum detectable effect (MDE) as primary diagnostics for leaderboard reporting
- Proves Lemma 1: a sharp small-effect expansion with explicit second-order constant C(p,ρ) characterizing the deviation between the unpaired Cohen-h+(1-ρ) shortcut and the correct paired N*, showing n_h ≈ N*/2 in the close-comparison limit
- Identifies that three off-the-shelf power calculators (Cohen 1988 textbook, G*Power 3.1, R pwr package) silently inherit this ~2x underestimation when users post-multiply by (1-ρ)
- Conducts end-to-end resolution analysis on Open LLM Leaderboard v1 (40 pairs) and MMLU-Pro (9 adjacent-rank pairs), with prospective validation achieving empirical McNemar power 0.80±0.03 at prescribed N*
- Comprehensive robustness checks: multiplicity (Bonferroni/Holm/BH), real subject-level clustering (K=14, ICC correction), leave-one-subject-out, category-bootstrap (99.9% of resamples support 5-6/9 unresolved), and anytime-valid sequential testing
- Releases a pip-installable package `llm-power` with a full diagnostic API

## Methodology

The paper treats shared-prompt LLM benchmarks as paired hypothesis-testing problems. Two models A, B are evaluated on the same N prompts treated as i.i.d. samples from a target prompt superpopulation. The paired difference D_i = X^A_i - X^B_i is used with a Wald test statistic. By inverting level-alpha, power-(1-beta) tests, the framework produces three quantities: (1) the minimum detectable effect delta_MDE at current N, (2) the required paired sample size N* at a target effect, and (3) the resolution ratio q = N/N* (a pair is resolved iff q >= 1).

For binary accuracy, the paired-difference variance involves the within-pair correlation rho, yielding the McNemar-Connor required-N formula (Equation 6). The author derives Lemma 1 via Taylor expansion of both Cohen's h^2 and sigma_D^2 around the midpoint p, exploiting arcsin-sqrt symmetry to cancel linear-in-delta terms, yielding an explicit constant C(p, rho) for the second-order deviation.

Empirical validation proceeds on two real leaderboards: Open LLM Leaderboard v1 (4 tasks, 40 pairwise comparisons across ARC-C, HellaSwag, Winogrande, TruthfulQA) and MMLU-Pro (12,032 items, 9 adjacent top-10 pairs). Prospective validation on three real OLL v1 pairs confirms empirical McNemar power hits 0.80±0.03 at N*. The framework is stress-tested under Bonferroni/Holm/BH multiplicity correction, real subject-level clustering using MMLU-Pro's 14 categories (with design effect DE = 1 + (m-1)*ICC), category-bootstrap (B=1000), leave-one-subject-out, and anytime-valid e-processes (mixture construction over theta with calibration).

## Results & Highlights

- **OLL v1:** 11/40 pairwise comparisons unresolved at (0.05, 0.8). Under Bonferroni: 14/40 unresolved. Under anytime-valid: 14/40 unresolved.
- **MMLU-Pro:** 4/9 adjacent-rank pairs unresolved at (0.05, 0.8) under IID; rises to 6/9 under real subject-level clustering (K=14 categories). The unresolved count stays at 5-6/9 in 99.9% of category-bootstrap resamples.
- **Shortcut lemma:** The unpaired Cohen-h+(1-ρ) shortcut yields n_h/N* ≈ 0.50 on the 40 OLL v1 pairs (IQR [0.4999, 0.5035]). At p=0.5, C(0.5, ρ) = 1/3 independent of ρ (a clean sanity check). Off-midpoint, C grows with |1-2p| and ρ.
- **Calculator misuse:** At worked example (p_A, p_B, ρ) = (0.65, 0.60, 0.30), three of five calculators underestimate required-N by ~2x.
- **Paired vs. unpaired efficiency:** Paired McNemar required-N is a median 2.15x smaller than Miller (2024)'s unpaired Gaussian formula on the same 40 OLL v1 pairs (IQR [1.60, 2.75]).
- **Cluster sensitivity:** Two MMLU-Pro pairs (rank 4 vs. 5, rank 5 vs. 6) flip from comfortably resolved under IID to N* > 3N under clustering due to high design effects (DE = 31.5 and 6.7).
- **Anytime-valid:** Time-uniform threshold inflates N* by ~2.15x vs. fixed-n, flagging one additional MMLU-Pro pair (rank 5 vs. 6) as sequentially not resolvable.

| Anytime-valid          | 14/40  | 5/9      |

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The individual components (McNemar test, power analysis, Bonferroni correction, cluster design effects) are classical statistics. The novelty lies in (a) Lemma 1's explicit second-order constant and uniform convergence proof, and (b) the systematic integration of all components into an end-to-end LLM leaderboard diagnostic pipeline. The factor-of-two underestimation by the Cohen-h shortcut is noted as "not new" at leading order by the author themselves -- the contribution is the explicit constant and operational delta* threshold.
- **Rigor:** Very high. Formal proofs with explicit bounds, numerical cross-checks to four significant figures, prospective validation on held-out pairs (power 0.80±0.03), multiple robustness checks (bootstrap, LOSO, cluster-definition sensitivity). The author is commendably transparent about what the lemma does and does not prove.
- **Reproducibility:** High. Public leaderboards used, code released as `llm-power` pip package, all tables and figures reference specific data artifacts.
- **Significance:** High practical impact. The paper provides concrete evidence that leaderboard rankings many practitioners treat as ground truth are statistically underresolved. The diagnostic is immediately deployable.
- **Red flags:** Single author -- the derivations would benefit from independent verification. The MMLU-Pro cluster analysis uses only K=14 categories, which limits the precision of ICC estimates (though LOSO and bootstrap address this). The paper restricts to binary accuracy; graded metrics and pairwise-preference leaderboards (e.g., Chatbot Arena) are explicitly noted as future work.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally rigorous integration of classical statistical tools into a modern LLM evaluation pipeline
- Lemma 1 provides a genuinely useful closed-form bound with explicit second-order constant, not just an asymptotic statement
- The prospective validation (Table 10) demonstrates the framework works in practice, not just in theory
- Comprehensive robustness analysis: no single robustness check is novel, but the full pipeline (multiplicity + clustering + sequential testing) applied end-to-end on real leaderboards is
- Honest about scope and limitations -- the author explicitly states what the framework does not cover (graded metrics, preference leaderboards, construct validity)
- Practical deliverable: the `llm-power` package with a clear API

## Limitations

- Restricted to binary accuracy; graded metrics validated only to 4-6% on Beta(4,2) marginals in synthetic experiments
- MMLU-Pro cluster analysis uses only K=14 subject categories; while LOSO and bootstrap support the findings, finer-grained clustering could change estimates
- Does not address pairwise-preference leaderboards (e.g., Chatbot Arena), which are arguably the most influential leaderboard format today
- The calculator-misuse characterization is demonstrated on specific tools but the paper does not survey how prevalent this misuse is in published LLM evaluation work
- The closed-source frontier panel (Section D) is explicitly a snapshot, not a durable comparison

## Relevance

本文对LLM排行榜的统计可靠性提出了系统性诊断框架，对关注模型评估方法论、基准设计和排行榜可信度的研究者具有直接参考价值。特别是引理1对非配对样本量近似偏差的精确刻画，以及MMLU-Pro上聚类效应导致未分辨对从4/9升至6/9的实证发现，对实际使用排行榜排名的研究者具有警示意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How would the resolution diagnostic apply to Chatbot Arena-style pairwise-preference leaderboards via Bradley-Terry e-processes, as the author hints at in future work?
- What is the prevalence of the Cohen-h+(1-ρ) calculator misuse pattern in published LLM evaluation papers?
- As frontier models converge in capability, will the close-comparison regime dominate even cross-tier comparisons, making the resolution problem universal?
- Can the resolution ratio q be integrated into automated leaderboard reporting as a standard column alongside accuracy and p-values?
- How sensitive are the cluster-corrected results to the choice of clustering granularity (e.g., K=14 subjects vs. finer-grained topic clusters)?
