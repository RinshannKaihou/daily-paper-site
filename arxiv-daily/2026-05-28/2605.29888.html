Representation-Level Contamination Detection

LaRA catches what output-level methods miss in RL post-training data

By probing layer-wise representation geometry under controlled perturbations, LaRA reveals that RL-induced memorization produces anomalous internal responses invisible to token-level detectors.

Why This Matters
RL post-training is now standard for reasoning models. Contamination detection is essential for evaluation integrity.
Critical Edge

M Three Complementary Metrics

Representation Shift Magnitude

Measures how strongly representations change when key information is removed. Computes z-scored L2 distance between original and blanked representations, standardized against semantic neighbors.

High RSM = strong perturbation sensitivity, indicating memorized dependence on removed information.

Directional Collapse

Measures cosine alignment between the original perturbation direction and the average direction of semantic neighbors. Captures whether representation changes collapse into a low-dimensional subspace.

DC provides the strongest standalone discrimination signal but varies across training epochs.

1
High DC = reduced representational diversity, indicating contamination-induced rigidity
2
DC amplifies in deeper layers during RL training

Representation Stability Index

Measures local representation variability under paraphrastic perturbations. Computes average L2 distance from local centroid across paraphrase variants, z-scored against neighbors.

Low RSI = more rigid and invariant local representation geometry. RSI concentrated in earlier layers.

!
Removing any single metric degrades overall detection performance — all three are complementary

! Limitations & Concerns

⚠ Small evaluation set: 30+30 samples per model provides limited statistical power; AUC estimates have wide confidence intervals.
⚠ Scale uncertainty: Only tested on 7B models. Unclear whether findings transfer to 70B+ models where per-layer extraction becomes memory-intensive.
⚠ Computational cost: Requires multiple forward passes per sample (K questions × M paraphrases × all layers), far more expensive than output-level methods.
⚠ Proprietary dependency: Uses GPT-4o-mini for perturbation generation, introducing variability and a closed-source dependency.
⚠ Work in Progress: Paper is explicitly marked as not final; results and methodology may change.
