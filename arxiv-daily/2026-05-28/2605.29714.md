> **一句话总结：** 本文通过分析OLMoE模型在多语言持续预训练中的路由动态，发现语言特化主要集中在最后两层且受词汇重叠驱动，据此提出SSFT方法（选择性+共享专家微调），仅更新<2%参数即可在MultiBLiMP和Belebele上达到接近全量微调的效果。

> **TL;DR:** By analyzing routing dynamics in OLMoE during multilingual continual pre-training on 35B tokens across 7 languages, the authors find language specialization concentrates in the final two layers and correlates with token-level vocabulary overlap (not language family). They propose SSFT (Selective and Shared Expert Finetuning), which updates <2% of parameters while achieving 83.6% average MultiBLiMP accuracy (vs. 73.8% for random expert selection), with 10x fewer GPU-hours and 100x fewer FLOPs than full fine-tuning.

## 核心贡献

- 系统研究了OLMoE-Base（64专家/层，top-8路由）在7种高资源语言持续预训练中的路由动态演变过程
- 发现多语言持续预训练导致早期和中间层的路由扩散化、语言无关化，语言特化仅在最后两层显著出现
- 揭示路由相似性与token级词汇重叠的相关性（Spearman rho=-0.56）远超语言家族（rho=0.11）、句法（rho=0.30）等传统语言相似性指标
- 提出SSFT方法：基于"activation gap"选择语言特化专家，并加入跨语言共享专家，仅更新最后两层的选定专家和路由器
- SSFT在MultiBLiMP上达83.6%平均准确率（vs. SEFT 78.7%、Random-SEFT 73.8%），仅更新75-138M参数（<2%），GPU时间减少10倍，FLOPs减少100倍
- 发现SSFT相比全量微调具有更好的抗灾难性遗忘能力

## Key Contributions

- Controlled study of routing dynamics in OLMoE-Base (64 experts/layer, top-8 routing) during balanced multilingual continual pre-training on 35B tokens across 7 high-resource languages
- Discovery that multilingual continual pre-training diffuses expert usage in early/middle layers rather than inducing clear language separation; language-specific routing differentiation emerges predominantly in the final two layers
- Identification that cross-lingual routing similarity in final layers correlates with token-level vocabulary overlap (Spearman rho=-0.56), far surpassing language family (rho=0.11), syntactic (rho=0.30), phonological (rho=0.36), or geographic (rho=0.46) similarity measures
- Introduction of the "activation gap" metric for identifying language-specialized experts, and SSFT (Selective and Shared Expert Finetuning) that combines language-specific and shared expert updates
- SSFT achieves 83.6% average MultiBLiMP accuracy with <2% parameter updates, 10x GPU-hour reduction, and 100x FLOPs reduction vs. full fine-tuning
- Demonstration that selective expert finetuning is more robust to catastrophic forgetting than full-model finetuning

## Methodology

**Routing Analysis Framework:**
- Uses OLMoE-Base (16 layers, 64 experts/layer, top-8 routing, ~7B total params / ~1B active params) as the base model
- Continually pre-trains on 35B tokens from CulturaX uniformly sampled across 7 high-resource languages (en, ar, cs, es, fi, hi, ru) to create OLMoE-M7
- Computes language-level expert usage distributions by averaging token-level post-softmax routing probabilities across documents
- Measures routing concentration via entropy H_k(l) and cross-lingual divergence via pairwise Jensen-Shannon Divergence (JSD)
- Tracks routing dynamics across training steps and layers

**Key Routing Findings:**
- English-centric OLMoE-Base already shows lower entropy for non-English languages (consistent routing to small expert subsets)
- After continual pre-training, non-English entropy rises toward English levels (diffusion effect)
- JSD between language pairs increases monotonically from early to final layers, with the largest increases in the last 2 layers
- Token-level vocabulary overlap is the strongest predictor of routing similarity (e.g., Hindi-Marathi ~90% overlap, high routing similarity; English-Dutch ~9% overlap, high divergence despite shared Germanic family)

**Adaptation Methods (Section 4):**
- **SEFT (Selective Expert Finetuning):** Identifies language-specialized experts in the final 2 layers via "activation gap" -- the difference in normalized activation frequency between an expert's top-2 languages. Selects experts with gap > 1% threshold (typically 5-7 per layer). Updates only selected experts and their routers (~75M params).
- **SSFT (Selective and Shared Expert Finetuning):** Augments SEFT's language-specific experts with k=5 shared experts per layer (experts with highest mean activation across all languages). Updates ~138M params.
- **Baselines:** Random-SEFT (random expert selection, matched param count), SEFT-Top20 (top 30% language-specific experts without shared), AEFT (all experts in final 2 layers, ~800M params), Full-FT (all 7B params)
- Low-resource adaptation uses 300M tokens per language with high-resource anchor language routing to identify experts; 6 language pairs (e.g., Catalan-Spanish, Estonian-Finnish, Marathi-Hindi, Slovak-Czech, Ukrainian-Russian, Urdu-Arabic)

## Results & Highlights

**Routing Dynamics (Section 3):**
- Continual pre-training on 35B tokens substantially reduces perplexity and improves MultiBLiMP for all non-English languages; English degrades slightly (expected given non-English majority in training)
- Routing entropy for non-English languages converges toward English levels across training
- Final layers show highest cross-lingual JSD, confirming language specialization is concentrated there
- English retains distinct routing patterns even after multilingual training (partial reduction only)

**Low-Resource Adaptation (Section 4):**
- SSFT (83.6% avg MultiBLiMP) >> SEFT (78.7%) >> SEFT-Top20 (77.7%) >> Random-SEFT (73.8%)
- SEFT-Top20 control proves gains come from shared expert quality, not parameter count
- Largest per-language SSFT gains: Marathi (+7.2 pts), Estonian (+6.9 pts) on MultiBLiMP; Estonian (+4.2 pts), Catalan (+1.9 pts) on Belebele
- Computational efficiency: SSFT needs ~3.3 GPU-hours on 4x H100s vs. ~32 GPU-hours on 16x H100s for Full-FT (10x GPU-hour reduction, 100x FLOPs reduction)
- Catastrophic forgetting: Full-FT degrades high-resource language performance when trained on 800M+ tokens; SSFT largely preserves prior knowledge (Appendix G)

**Ablations (Appendix F):**
- Activation gap threshold alpha=1% provides best balance; 2% too strict (e.g., no Marathi experts qualify), 0.0001% too loose
- Number of shared experts k: monotonic improvement from k=0 (SEFT) to k=5 (SSFT), confirming shared experts add genuine cross-lingual transfer

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The combination of routing dynamics analysis + practical adaptation method is well-motivated. The vocabulary overlap finding (over language family) is a genuinely interesting and somewhat surprising insight. However, the finding that specialization concentrates in final layers partially overlaps with concurrent work (Bandarkar et al., 2026), who also observe language-specific routing in early and late layers of MoE LLMs. The SSFT method itself is relatively straightforward -- the key novelty lies in the analysis-driven expert selection rather than the training procedure.

**Rigor:** Strong experimental design with proper controls (Random-SEFT, SEFT-Top20, AEFT, Full-FT). The SEFT-Top20 control is particularly valuable for disentangling parameter count from expert quality. The use of two evaluation benchmarks (MultiBLiMP for syntax/morphology, Belebele for reading comprehension) covers different aspects of language capability. However, some concerns:
- Only tested on OLMoE-Base (1B active / 7B total) -- generalization to larger MoE models (Mixtral, DeepSeek) is unverified
- The 6 "low-resource" languages (Catalan, Estonian, Marathi, Slovak, Ukrainian, Dutch, Urdu) are acknowledged by the authors as not truly low-resource; the label is for controlled experimental setup
- No comparison with established PEFT methods like LoRA or adapters, which would strengthen the practical value proposition
- Perplexity-based model selection may not correlate perfectly with downstream task performance

**Reproducibility:** Excellent. Open-source code, open model (OLMoE), open data (CulturaX), detailed hyperparameters in appendices, and clear experimental protocols. The activation gap threshold and shared expert count are properly ablated.

**Significance:** The vocabulary overlap finding has implications beyond this paper -- it suggests that prior MoE adaptation methods relying on language family relationships may be suboptimal. The practical SSFT method could be useful for the community working on multilingual MoE adaptation.

**Red flags:** None major. The paper is honest about limitations (single model scale, need for high-resource anchor language). The claim of "competitive performance" relative to full fine-tuning should be interpreted carefully -- Full-FT still outperforms SSFT on 300M tokens, but SSFT becomes more attractive when considering catastrophic forgetting at higher token counts.

## Strengths

- Well-motivated research program: analysis first, then practical method informed by findings
- Strong experimental controls, especially the SEFT-Top20 ablation that disentangles parameter count from expert selection quality
- The vocabulary overlap vs. language family finding is a genuine contribution to understanding multilingual MoE behavior
- Excellent reproducibility: fully open-source stack (model, data, code)
- Clear, well-structured paper with thorough appendices for hyperparameter sensitivity and additional analyses
- Computational efficiency gains are substantial and clearly quantified (10x GPU-hours, 100x FLOPs)
- The catastrophic forgetting analysis (Appendix G) adds practical value beyond the main results

## Limitations

- Only validated on OLMoE-Base (1B/7B); routing dynamics may differ substantially in larger MoE architectures with different expert counts and routing strategies
- Requires a high-resource "anchor" language with significant vocabulary overlap, limiting applicability to true language isolates or very distant low-resource languages
- English-Dutch pair explicitly excluded due to low vocabulary overlap (~9%) despite shared Germanic family, raising questions about generalizability to such cases
- No comparison with popular PEFT baselines (LoRA, adapters, prompt tuning) that the community commonly uses
- Balanced multilingual training corpus (35B tokens) may not reflect realistic data distributions where some languages have far more available data
- The "low-resource" languages tested still have 300M tokens available, which may be unrealistic for truly low-resource settings

## Relevance

本文对MoE模型的多语言路由动态进行了系统分析，发现词汇重叠是驱动路由行为的关键因素（而非语言家族），这对理解大模型多语言能力机制有重要参考价值。SSFT方法为低资源语言的高效适应提供了实用方案，对关注MoE架构优化和多语言大模型研究的读者具有较高参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the final-layer specialization patterns hold for larger MoE models with different architectures (DeepSeek-V3, Mixtral 8x22B), and how does expert count affect the dynamics?
- Can SSFT be effectively combined with LoRA or adapter-based PEFT methods for even greater parameter efficiency?
- How does the routing analysis transfer to domain adaptation (e.g., adapting a general MoE to medical or legal text) where similar vocabulary overlap dynamics may exist?
- What happens when adapting to truly low-resource languages with <10M tokens available, or languages without a clear high-resource anchor?
- Could the activation gap metric be used dynamically during training to guide expert allocation, rather than as a post-hoc selection criterion?
- Does the vocabulary overlap finding imply that tokenizer design (e.g., using a multilingual tokenizer with better cross-lingual subword sharing) could fundamentally reshape MoE routing behavior?
