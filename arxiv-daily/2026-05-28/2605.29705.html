Trajectory Prediction · Model Quantization · Edge Deployment

Shrink weights to 1.58 bits, get better predictions

BitTP converts a T5-small trajectory predictor into a BitNet-style ternary architecture. The surprising finding: weight-only quantization acts as implicit regularization, beating the full-precision baseline on ETH/UCY while cutting memory to 53% and latency to 63%.

Kang, Lim, Kang, Park · KAIST / DGIST · 2026-05-28 · Single RTX 3090

LLM-based trajectory prediction deployable on CPU-only edge devices with no GPU acceleration needed.

Reproduced BF16 (0.35 ADE) is 59% worse than original LMTraj-SUP (0.22). BitTP does not beat published numbers.

ⓘ Key insight: Activation precision is non-negotiable for encoder-decoder spatio-temporal reasoning. Quantizing activations causes a 5-6x accuracy collapse. Only weights can be compressed.

Architecture & Training

  • Backbone: T5-small encoder-decoder (6+6 blocks, d=512, 8 heads)
  • Quantization: BitLinear replaces all nn.Linear layers (attention projections + FFN)
  • Weight ternary: {-1, 0, +1} with learnable scale factor beta
  • Gradient flow: Straight-Through Estimator (STE) with Round Clamp
  • Training: RTX 3090, AdamW, lr=1e-4, linear scheduler, 8 epochs, batch 128
  • Evaluation: ETH/UCY 5-scene leave-one-out, best-of-20 from 1000 samples

Strengths

  • Systematic ablation across 3 quantization strategies with clear winner
  • Actionable finding: weight-only quantization preserves reasoning; activation quantization destroys it
  • Low training overhead (1.2x) and practical edge-deployment path
  • Transparent about reproduced vs. original baseline gap
  • Open-source code and detailed supplementary material
  • First BitNet study on encoder-decoder architectures (prior work was decoder-only)

Limitations

  • Baseline gap: Reproduced BF16 (0.35) vs. original (0.22) — improvements may reflect poor baseline tuning
  • Small benchmark: ETH/UCY only (5 pedestrian scenes); no nuScenes, Argoverse, or vehicle trajectories
  • No real edge hardware: All measurements on RTX 3090; no Jetson/Raspberry Pi numbers
  • No non-LLM comparison: Lightweight GNN/MLP baselines may already be edge-suitable without quantization
  • Single backbone: Only T5-small tested; generalization unknown
  • Tokenization confound: Pixel-level BPE tokenization interaction with quantization unexplored
⚠ Critical caveat: The 14.3% ADE improvement is vs. a poorly-reproduced baseline (0.35 ADE) that is 59% worse than the published LMTraj-SUP numbers (0.22 ADE). BitTP-Weight (0.30) does not beat the original full-precision model. The "quantization-as-regularization" claim may simply reflect baseline under-tuning rather than genuine improvement from compression.
