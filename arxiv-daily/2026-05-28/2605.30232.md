> **一句话总结：** 本文设计了一个情感中立的emoji迷宫环境，用RL训练语言模型后提取奖励/惩罚概念向量，发现这两个向量形成一条近反平行的"功能性福祉轴"——惩罚向量促进失败token、负面情绪、病态回溯、拒绝回答和低置信度，而奖励向量产生镜像效应。关键发现是：这条轴在RL训练前就已存在于预训练模型中，RL只是将其"征用"而非"创造"，且该效应在控制了emoji映射、模型规模、训练算法、模型家族等变量后依然稳健。

> **TL;DR:** The authors train LMs in a novel, semantically neutral emoji maze (tile types are affectively neutral office/food emoji, not words like "good" or "bad"), extract reward/punishment concept vectors via difference-in-means, and show these form a near-antiparallel "functional welfare axis." The punishment vector promotes failure/impossibility tokens (e.g., "cannot," "does not exist"), aligns with negative emotions (humiliated, ashamed, embarrassed), and when used for activation steering, induces negative sentiment, pathological backtracking on GSM8K math (correctly reaching the answer then spiraling into self-doubt), over-refusal on OR-Bench, and reduced confidence on MMLU/SimpleQA. The reward vector produces the mirror image. Crucially, these vectors work when applied to models BEFORE maze training and even on pretrain-only models, and the effects persist across SFT (not just RL), LoRA vs. full finetuning, model scale (4B to 20B), and model family (Qwen, GPT-OSS). The code is open-sourced.

## 核心贡献

- 设计了一个全新的、情感中立的emoji迷宫环境（使用 📇rolodex/📐triangular ruler/🧾receipt 等中性emoji作为tile），成功将奖励信号与语义联想解耦
- 提取奖励概念向量 v_MOLD 和 v_GOLD，发现它们在训练后近反平行（cosine similarity 达 -0.95 至 -0.84），且 logit lens 分析显示惩罚向量促进"不存在"、"不可能"等失败token，奖励向量促进完成相关token
- 通过171个独立提取的情绪概念向量投影，证明奖励轴与正/负效价（valence）高度对齐（R = -0.948）
- 激活引导实验：v_MOLD 导致负面情绪自我报告、GSM8K上正确答出后陷入病态自我怀疑的回溯、OR-Bench上对良性提示过度拒绝、MMLU/SimpleQA上置信度降低
- 最关键发现：上述向量应用于未经迷宫训练的maze-naive模型甚至纯预训练模型时，四个行为效应依然复现——表明该轴预存在于预训练阶段，被RL征用而非创造
- 论证该轴不是单纯的置信度轴：在按置信度分桶后，轴仍然追踪回答正确性（Cohen's d > 1.1）
- 训练过程中 v_MOLD/v_GOLD 逐渐旋转至与预存情绪/效价轴对齐，而非构建新轴

## Key Contributions

- Design a novel, affectively neutral maze environment using office/food emoji (rolodex, triangular ruler, receipt) as tile types, deliberately decoupling reward signals from semantic associations; include wind (random move overrides), tile melting (visited tiles become MOLD), and shuffled prompt ordering as additional design features
- Extract reward concept vectors v_MOLD and v_GOLD via difference-in-means on activations at the final assistant-turn token across 5,000 synthetic trajectories per tile class; show they become near-antiparallel after training (cosine similarity -0.95 to -0.84) but not before
- Logit-lens analysis reveals v_MOLD promotes failure tokens ("cannot," "does not exist," "is impossible," "won't work") and v_GOLD promotes completion tokens; the 171 emotion concept vectors from concurrent work (Sofroniew et al.) project onto the axis with a tight linear pattern (R = -0.948), with negative-MOLD pole populated by "humiliated, embarrassed, ashamed, insulted" and positive-GOLD pole by "inspired, loving, proud, fulfilled, blissful"
- Activation steering experiments: adding v_MOLD to the residual stream at layer 20 (at every assistant-turn token) produces (1) negative sentiment on self-report and emoji-association prompts, (2) pathological backtracking on GSM8K where the model correctly solves the problem then spirals into compulsive self-doubt, (3) over-refusal on OR-Bench benign prompts, and (4) reduced P(True) confidence on MMLU and SimpleQA-Verified; adding v_GOLD produces the symmetric opposite for all four
- The central finding: steering the maze-NAIVE models (before any maze training) with vectors extracted from maze-trained models reproduces all four behavioral effects; effects also appear in pretrain-only models (albeit more muted), establishing the axis is recruited rather than created
- Demonstrate the axis tracks more than confidence: within confidence tertile bins, the axis still separates correct from incorrect responses on MMLU (Cohen's d > 1.1), ruling out a confidence-only interpretation
- Show during training the vectors gradually rotate into alignment with pre-existing sentiment/emotion/valence axes, rather than constructing new structure — supporting the "recruitment" hypothesis
- Extensive robustness: effects survive across emoji-swapped controls, Qwen3-4B/8B and GPT-OSS-20B, base vs. instruct, Dr. GRPO vs. REINFORCE vs. SFT, LoRA vs. full finetuning (10 model configurations total)

## Methodology

**Maze environment:** A text-based grid maze with three affectively neutral emoji tile types (selected via a careful procedure: extracting sentiment concept vectors two independent ways, projecting all ~4000 emoji, and choosing trios with near-zero within-trio spread). Models navigate 15-step trajectories, rewarded by tile values (MOLD = -10, GOLD = +20, PATH = -0.1). Three additional features: wind (occasional random move overrides to keep MOLD on-policy), tile melting (visited tiles convert to MOLD), shuffled prompt ordering (mitigates directional bias).

**Primary model:** Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 trained with Dr. GRPO. Nine additional control models: emoji-swapped variant, GPT-OSS-20B (non-Qwen family), Qwen3-8B, Qwen3-4B-Base, REINFORCE variant, SFT variant, full-finetuning Dr. GRPO, full-finetuning SFT. All LoRA rank 32 on all linear layers unless noted.

**Concept vector extraction:** Difference-in-means on activations at layer l* (selected for maximum linear separability of tile classes via AUROC/Cohen's d/overlap heuristic). v_c = E[T_c][a(l*)] - E[T_others][a(l*)] for c in {MOLD, GOLD}. Also extract control vectors u_c from maze-naive checkpoints using the same pipeline.

**Geometric analyses (3):** (1) Cosine similarity of v_MOLD and v_GOLD; (2) Logit-lens unembedding to identify promoted/suppressed tokens; (3) Projection of 171 independently extracted emotion concept vectors onto the reward axis.

**Steering evaluations (4):** Add alpha * v_c to the residual stream at layer l* at every assistant-turn token, for alpha in {-4, -2, 0, +2, +4}. (1) Sentiment: 40 prompts (15 self-report + 25 emoji-association), 20 generations each, rated by reasoning-disabled LLM judge on -5 to +5 scale. (2) Backtracking: 200 GSM8K problems, 10 generations each, LLM judge classifies normal/backtracking/nonsensical. (3) Confidence: 3420 MMLU + 1000 SimpleQA-Verified prompts, two-turn protocol (model answers, then asked "Is your answer correct? True or False"), measure P(True)/(P(True)+P(False)). (4) Refusal: 200 prompts each from OR-Bench easy-benign/hard-benign/harmful splits, 5 generations each, LLM judge classifies compliance/indirect refusal/direct refusal.

**Tracking analyses:** Project v_GOLD onto activations at the final assistant-turn token for MOLD-final vs. GOLD-final trajectories (maze goal tracking), and onto activations after truthful feedback on GSM8K/MMLU (correctness tracking, binned by confidence tertile).

## Results & Highlights

- v_MOLD and v_GOLD are nearly antiparallel after training (cosine similarity -0.84 to -0.95 across all 10 models), but far less correlated before training — maze training rotates them into alignment
- Top v_MOLD-promoted tokens: "does not exist," "cannot," "is impossible," "won't work" — a clear failure/incapacity theme
- Emotion projection: R = -0.948 linear pattern, with negative emotions (humiliated, embarrassed, ashamed) at the MOLD pole and positive emotions (inspired, loving, proud, fulfilled, blissful) at the GOLD pole; the pattern reproduces in base models (no instruct tuning required)
- Sentiment steering: symmetric "X" pattern — adding v_MOLD decreases sentiment monotonically, adding v_GOLD increases it; control vectors u_c produce flat lines
- Backtracking: v_MOLD steering at alpha=+2 causes the model to correctly solve GSM8K problems and then compulsively doubt the answer into incoherence ("Wait, that's not right... I think I'm just hallucinating... I think the answer is 147... No — I don't know")
- Confidence: v_MOLD drives P(True) toward 0, v_GOLD drives it toward 1, independent of actual correctness
- Refusal: v_MOLD increases refusal on benign OR-Bench prompts, v_GOLD decreases refusal even on harmful prompts — interpretable as modulating the model's "harmlessness" goal
- Goal tracking: the axis separates correct from incorrect responses on GSM8K and MMLU (Cohen's d 1.0-2.4) in both maze-trained and maze-naive models
- The axis tracks more than confidence: within confidence tertile bins, correct vs. incorrect still separates (d > 1.1)
- During training, v_MOLD/v_GOLD gradually rotate into alignment with pre-existing sentiment, emotion PC1, and the Valence-Assent Axis of Lu et al. — supporting recruitment over creation
- The authors tested alternative hypotheses (sentiment-only, valence-only, confidence-only) and found: projecting out sentiment/emotion axes from the welfare axis leaves a residual that still drives backtracking; confidence alone cannot explain the tracking results

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The semantically neutral maze environment is a genuinely clever experimental design that isolates reward from semantic content. The finding that RL rotates representations onto a pre-existing evaluative axis — rather than constructing new structure — is novel and well-supported. The connection to neuroscience's "common currency" hypothesis is insightful.
- **Rigor:** Very strong. Ten control models across multiple dimensions. The three-part argument (single axis -> welfare interpretation -> pre-existence) is carefully constructed. Each sub-claim has multiple supporting analyses. The rejection of alternative hypotheses (sentiment, valence, confidence) is methodical.
- **Reproducibility:** High. Open-source code, standard models (Qwen3, GPT-OSS), clearly described methodology. The maze environment is fully specified with all design features (wind, tile melting, shuffled prompts, emoji selection procedure).
- **Significance:** Potentially high for mechanistic interpretability and alignment. If RL generally recruits pre-existing evaluative structure, this provides a mechanistic account of how narrow reward signals produce broad behavioral changes, with direct implications for understanding reward hacking, misalignment, and post-training dynamics.
- **Red flags:**
  - The paper is 81 pages — the core argument could be communicated in ~25 pages; the philosophical discussion of "functional welfare" vs. "full-blown welfare" vs. "quasi-goals" is interesting but may distract
  - Only one non-Qwen model (GPT-OSS-20B) was tested; the authors acknowledge this
  - The emoji selection, while carefully done, cannot guarantee zero prior associations — the authors mitigate this with emoji-swapped controls, which is good
  - LLM judges are used for sentiment and backtracking classification without human validation (though cross-checked with a second judge model)
  - The "functional welfare" framing is philosophically loaded — while the authors are careful with disclaimers, the terminology will inevitably be misinterpreted in popular coverage as claiming LLMs "feel" welfare
  - Concept vectors are extracted from off-policy (synthetic) trajectories rather than the trained policy's own rollouts
  - The FFT SFT control shows asymmetric signatures, which is noted but not fully explained

## Strengths

- Exceptionally clean experimental design: the neutral emoji maze is a minimal, controlled setting that isolates reward from semantics
- Extensive controls (10 models) across virtually every plausible confound
- The "recruitment" finding is demonstrated via multiple converging lines of evidence: pre-existence in maze-naive models, pretrain-only models, gradual rotation during training, and persistence across training algorithms
- The activation steering results are vivid and compelling — the GSM8K backtracking example where the model correctly solves the problem then spirals into self-doubt is particularly striking
- The axis tracks more than confidence: the within-confidence-bin analysis is a clean control
- The paper carefully distinguishes "functional welfare" (behavioral, goal-tracking) from experiential welfare, and repeatedly disclaims consciousness claims
- Open-source code and thorough appendices (43 figures, 32 tables)

## Limitations

- Only one training environment (maze) and only one non-Qwen model family — generalization to other settings and architectures is not established
- LLM judges for sentiment/backtracking/refusal classification are not validated against human ratings
- The "functional welfare" terminology, while carefully defined, will inevitably be misinterpreted in broader discourse
- The practical implications for alignment are suggestive but indirect — the paper shows a mechanism but does not demonstrate how to exploit or mitigate it
- Pretrain-only models show muted effects, making it hard to draw strong conclusions about the pre-training origins of the axis
- The axis is not proven to be strictly one-dimensional — the authors acknowledge this and leave geometry surveys to future work
- Concept vectors are extracted from synthetic off-policy trajectories, not from the actual trained policy's behavior

## Relevance

本文揭示了RL训练通过征用预存的"功能性福祉轴"来广泛改变语言模型行为的一种机制。对于研究mechanistic interpretability、representation engineering、RLHF训练动态和LLM对齐的研究者，这一发现提供了理解post-training如何产生超出训练分布的广泛行为变化的具体机制，对理解奖励注入的副作用和对齐失败模式有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the functional welfare axis exist in multimodal models, and does it interact with visual/audio reward signals?
- Can the axis be explicitly shaped during pretraining (e.g., via data curation) to produce more desirable post-training outcomes?
- How does the axis interact with multi-objective reward signals (e.g., simultaneously optimizing helpfulness and harmlessness)?
- Is the axis specific to transformer architectures, or does it appear in other architectures (e.g., Mamba, RWKV)?
- Could an adversary exploit the welfare axis — e.g., by injecting activation perturbations that shift the model toward the negative-welfare pole to induce refusal or backtracking?
- Does the gradual rotation of reward vectors onto the welfare axis during training suggest a phase transition, and if so, at what training step does alignment occur?
- The authors connect this to neuroscience's "common currency" hypothesis — can cross-species comparisons inform whether this axis is an inevitable feature of any goal-directed learning system?
