> **一句话总结：** 本文提出DSTG-NeoBERT，在编码器Transformer中将语义、绝对位置(AP)和相对位置(RP)三个信息流显式解耦，发现AP子空间自发塌缩为低频二维流形(94.1%方差)、注意力头分化为结构与语义两组，且标准位置编码(RoPE/纠缠AP)在深层丢失宏观结构，而解耦方法在Flash-Holmes探测基准49/65个语言现象上取得提升，同时在GLUE/MTEB/SQuAD上与基线持平。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces DSTG-NeoBERT, a modified encoder Transformer that explicitly separates semantic, absolute positional (AP), and relative positional (RP) streams -- confining MLM training to only the semantic stream. Three key findings emerge: (1) the isolated AP subspace spontaneously collapses into a 2D low-frequency sinusoidal manifold (94.1% variance in 2 PCs) that captures document structure; (2) attention heads specialize exclusively into AP-driven (structure) or semantic-driven groups, with RP serving only as complementary support for semantic heads; (3) standard PEs lose macroscopic structure -- RoPE/RP fail to encode it, and entangled AP loses it in the final layer (R^2 drops from 0.99 to 0.34) due to MLM pressure overwriting positional dimensions. The disentangled model matches baselines on GLUE/MTEB/SQuAD while improving on 49/65 Flash-Holmes probing phenomena.

## 核心贡献

- 提出DSTG-NeoBERT架构：基于NeoBERT，将隐藏状态显式分离为语义(d_sem=720)和绝对位置(d_AP=48)两个独立流，使用T5风格的桶化RP偏置，MLM目标仅施加于语义流
- 发现隔离的AP嵌入自发塌缩为二维低频正弦流形（前两个主成分捕获94.1%方差，频谱功率98.4%集中在低频），说明Transformer仅需极低带宽的结构信息
- 通过信息消融实验（KL散度量化各流对注意力的影响）发现注意力头分化为AP导向(结构)和语义导向两组，36个头中仅9个专门用于语义匹配，RP仅作为语义头的局部补充
- 揭示纠缠模型的关键问题：AP-NeoBERT在最后一层位置信息急剧下降（R^2从0.99降至0.34），因为MLM目标将位置维度重新用于语义预测；RoPE/RP模型完全无法在隐藏状态中保留绝对位置信息
- 三层探测实验（token级、段落级、段内位置）验证解耦架构在所有层级保持结构信息，段内位置信息实际存储在语义子空间中
- 在Flash-Holmes基准的65个语言现象中49个取得最佳，覆盖全部5个领域（Syntax 18/21, Semantics 17/24, Discourse 5/6, Reasoning 6/10, Morphology 3/4）

## Key Contributions

- Propose DSTG-NeoBERT architecture based on NeoBERT that separates hidden states into semantic (d_sem=720) and absolute positional (d_AP=48) streams with T5-style bucketed RP bias, confining MLM to semantic stream only
- Discover that isolated AP embeddings spontaneously collapse into a 2D low-frequency sinusoidal manifold (first 2 PCs capture 94.1% variance, 98.4% spectral power in low-frequency bins), implying Transformers need minimal bandwidth structural information
- Reveal attention head specialization via information ablation (KL-divergence): heads cluster into AP-driven (structure) and semantic-driven groups exclusively -- only 9 of 36 heads specialize in semantic matching; RP serves only as localized support for semantic heads
- Demonstrate that entangled AP-NeoBERT loses positional information in the final layer (R^2 drops from 0.99 to 0.34) because MLM overwrites positional dimensions for semantic prediction; RoPE/RP models cannot encode absolute position in hidden states at all
- Design three-level probing hierarchy (token-level AP, segment-level AP, intra-segment position) confirming the disentangled architecture preserves structural information at all levels
- Show that intra-segment position information is actually stored in the semantic subspace (not AP), validating that local position is a semantic property
- Validate on Flash-Holmes probing benchmark: best on 49/65 phenomena across all 5 fields (Syntax 18/21, Semantics 17/24, Discourse 5/6, Reasoning 6/10, Morphology 3/4)
- Match baselines on GLUE, MTEB, and SQuAD benchmarks despite the architectural separation

## Methodology

### Architecture (DSTG-NeoBERT)

The authors modify NeoBERT (a modern BERT variant) to create three explicitly disentangled information streams:

- **Input representation:** Each token has two separate embeddings: d_AP-dimensional (48) AP embedding and d_sem-dimensional (720) semantic embedding. d_model = d_AP + d_sem = 768.
- **RMSNorm separation:** Pre-attention and post-attention layer norms are separated between AP and semantic embeddings to avoid interdependence.
- **Relative Positional Bias:** T5-style bucketed bias is used instead of decay-based methods (ALiBi, KERPLE) to avoid baking in long-term decay priors. Special tokens ([CLS], [SEP]) get distance-agnostic learned parameters.
- **Attention computation:** Queries and keys computed independently per stream per head. Both streams projected to same d_head to match dot-product variances. Attention logits = RP bias + semantic attention weight + AP attention weight (AP only for non-special tokens). Same attention scores applied to both AP and semantic value streams.
- **SwiGLU FFN:** Applied to concatenated [AP; semantic] representation, allowing information mixing in the feed-forward layer while keeping residual connections separate.
- **MLM confinement:** The MLM prediction head operates only on the semantic stream, so AP stream receives no gradient from the MLM loss and is free to preserve structural information.

### Training

- 6-layer, 6-head architecture trained from scratch on ~22B tokens
- Four model variants compared: RoPE-NeoBERT, RP-NeoBERT (T5-style RP only), AP-NeoBERT (learned AP embeddings, entangled), DSTG-NeoBERT (disentangled)
- bert-base-uncased tokenizer, max sequence length 512

### Analysis Methods

- **PCA + DCT analysis** of AP embedding matrices
- **Information ablation** via KL-divergence between full and component-removed attention distributions across 500 WikiText documents
- **Three-level probing** (Ridge regression, R^2 metric): token-level absolute position, segment-level position, intra-segment position
- **Flash-Holmes benchmark:** 200+ datasets probing 65 linguistic phenomena across 5 fields

## Results & Highlights

- **AP manifold collapse:** Despite 48-dimensional AP embeddings, the first 2 PCs capture 94.1% variance vs. only 23.4% in entangled AP baseline. Type-II DCT confirms low-frequency concentration (98.4%, 99.0% in first 4 bins).
- **Head specialization:** 9/36 heads are semantic-oriented; remaining 27 are AP-driven. No purely RP-oriented heads exist. Heads 3 and 6 identified as retrieval heads where RP bias prevents self-attention, enabling focus on distant context.
- **Positional information loss in entangled models:**
  - AP-NeoBERT: R^2 drops from 0.99 (layer 0) to 0.34 (layer 5) for token-level AP
  - RoPE-NeoBERT: R^2 never exceeds 0.32 at any layer
  - DSTG-NeoBERT: R^2 maintained at 0.92-0.99 through layer 4 (last AP layer)
- **Document structure visualization:** PCA of AP hidden states shows same-sentence tokens clustering together, with clusters becoming increasingly distinct in deeper layers.
- **Flash-Holmes results:** DSTG-NeoBERT best on 49/65 phenomena across all 5 fields. All three baselines perform equally, suggesting gains come specifically from preserved structural information.
- **GLUE/MTEB/SQuAD:** DSTG-NeoBERT matches baselines (slightly larger model at d_model=768 vs. 720, ~6% more parameters).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The three-stream disentangled architecture is clean and well-motivated. The finding that AP spontaneously collapses to a 2D manifold is genuinely surprising and insightful. The mechanism by which MLM destroys positional information (overwriting dimensions for semantic prediction) is a concrete, testable hypothesis with strong empirical support.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The paper uses multiple complementary analysis methods (PCA, DCT, KL-divergence ablation, hierarchical probing). The probing methodology is well-designed with three levels of positional hierarchy. Five-seed averaging for statistical robustness. The validation that MLM on the full embedding causes the same positional loss (Appendix B.2) is a clean ablation.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Architecture details are precise, training setup is described, code is shared. The small model size makes reproduction feasible.
- **Significance:** The mechanistic insights are significant for understanding Transformers and could inform future PE design. However, the practical impact is limited by the encoder-only scope and small scale.
- **Red flags:**
  - **Scale limitation:** 6-layer, 6-head model on 22B tokens. Whether these findings hold at scale (1B+, 100B+ tokens) is untested.
  - **Parameter asymmetry:** DSTG-NeoBERT has ~6% more parameters (d_model=768 vs. 720) than baselines. The authors acknowledge this but it muddies the Flash-Holmes comparison slightly.
  - **No downstream task improvement:** The model matches but does not beat baselines on GLUE/MTEB/SQuAD. The probing improvements do not translate to practical gains.
  - **Encoder-only scope:** The findings may not transfer to decoder architectures where causal masking provides implicit positional information.
  - **512 token limit:** Cannot validate the hypothesis that the AP manifold is most useful at long contexts.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated architecture design that enables mechanistic analysis impossible with standard Transformers
- Three complementary findings (manifold collapse, head specialization, positional information loss) that tell a coherent story
- The MLM-destroys-positional-information finding is specific and actionable -- it suggests a concrete design principle for future architectures
- Excellent probing methodology with hierarchical levels (token, segment, intra-segment)
- Honest about limitations; the paper does not oversell its results
- The retrieval head analysis (heads 3, 6 where RP enables distant-context focus) is a particularly elegant finding

## Limitations

- Small scale (6-layer, 6-head, 22B tokens) -- unknown whether findings generalize
- ~6% parameter advantage in DSTG-NeoBERT complicates fair comparison on probing benchmarks
- Encoder-only evaluation; decoder applicability is speculative
- 512 token context limit; cannot validate long-context benefits
- No downstream task improvement despite probing gains (matching baselines on GLUE/MTEB/SQuAD)
- The intra-segment position information residing in semantic (not AP) subspace is interesting but not fully explained

## Relevance

本文对Transformer位置编码机制提供了深入的可解释性分析，特别是"MLM目标在深层冲刷掉位置信息"这一发现对理解大模型的位置编码局限性有重要参考价值。作者提出的解耦思路（将AP信息从MLM目标中隔离）可启发未来大语言模型的位置编码设计，尤其对长上下文场景和RAG中缓存位置无关的语义表示具有潜在应用价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the 2D manifold collapse hold at larger scales (e.g., 7B parameters, 1T+ tokens)? Would higher-dimensional manifolds emerge, or is 2D a fundamental property?
- Can the MLM-destroys-positional-information finding be confirmed in decoder-only models via similar probing experiments?
- Could the low-dimensional AP manifold be explicitly parameterized (e.g., as a 2D sinusoidal function) to reduce parameters and potentially improve extrapolation?
- Does preserving structural information in the AP stream improve performance on document-level tasks (e.g., long-document QA, multi-document summarization)?
- Can the head specialization finding (AP vs. semantic heads) be exploited for efficient inference via selective computation or pruning?
- How does the disentangled architecture interact with longer context windows (8k, 32k, 128k tokens)? Does the 2D manifold scale gracefully?
- Could the separated semantic representations (without positional contamination) benefit RAG systems by enabling position-independent caching?
