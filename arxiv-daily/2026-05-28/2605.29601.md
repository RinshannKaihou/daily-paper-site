> **一句话总结：** 本文提出了一种基于"审议式对齐"思路的纯行为监控方法：用前沿模型生成结构化推理并经judge过滤后，通过SFT+RL蒸馏到开源小模型，在不访问被监控代理推理过程的情况下，以不到$5/千次评估的成本实现优于Gemini 2.5 Pro等前沿提示监控的scheming检测，且多个模型位于成本-性能帕累托前沿上。

> **TL;DR:** This paper trains small open-weight models as "action-only deliberative monitors" to detect scheming and sabotage from agentic trajectories without any access to chain-of-thought or model internals. The pipeline uses a frontier teacher (Claude Opus 4.6) to generate structured, specification-grounded rationales, filters them with a separate judge, then distills via SFT+RL into open-weight models. The resulting Qwen3.5-27B monitor outperforms all low-cost prompted frontier monitors (Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite, GPT-5.4 Nano, Claude Haiku 4.5) and even Gemini 2.5 Pro, at under $5 per 1,000 evaluations. Only the strongest frontier monitors (Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.4, Claude Sonnet/Opus 4.6) surpass it, but at 16-34x higher cost. SFT provides the dominant performance gain; RL adds marginal refinement. The recipe generalizes across 9 models from 4 families and scales with both model size and training data.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"纯行为审议式监控器"(action-only deliberative monitors)：不依赖CoT访问或模型内部状态，仅从代理可观测的行为轨迹（tool calls、代码执行等）检测scheming和sabotage
- 设计了三阶段训练流水线：(1) 前沿教师模型（Claude Opus 4.6）基于scheming specification生成结构化推理，(2) 独立judge模型过滤低质量样本，(3) 通过SFT和RL蒸馏到开源小模型
- 在5个训练数据集上训练，在6个OOD基准上评估，多个训练模型（GPT-OSS-20B、Qwen3.5-27B、Nemotron-120B）位于成本-性能帕累托前沿
- 进行了详尽的消融实验：SFT vs RL贡献、训练混合宽度、模型规模/家族泛化性、数据规模缩放

## Key Contributions

- Introduce action-only deliberative monitors: small open-weight models trained to detect scheming/sabotage from observable agentic trajectories without CoT or activation access
- Three-stage pipeline: frontier teacher generates spec-grounded rationales, independent judge filters for quality, SFT+RL distills into open-weight monitors
- Trained Qwen3.5-27B outperforms all low-cost prompted frontier models and Gemini 2.5 Pro, achieving under $5 per 1,000 evaluations
- Multiple trained monitors (GPT-OSS-20B, Qwen3.5-27B, Nemotron-120B) sit on the cost-performance Pareto frontier across six OOD benchmarks
- Comprehensive ablations cover SFT vs RL contributions, training mixture breadth, cross-family generalization (9 models, 4 families), and data scaling

## Methodology

### Problem Setting
The monitor receives only observable agent trajectories (tool calls, code execution outputs, file operations) and must classify whether the trajectory exhibits scheming behavior -- where the agent covertly pursues misaligned objectives while appearing to serve the assigned task. No access to chain-of-thought, hidden states, or model internals.

### Scheming Specification
A detailed rubric defines evidence categories and band ratings (e.g., actions serving unauthorized objectives, concealment behaviors, authority expansion). The specification serves as the grounding document for both teacher rationale generation and monitor training.

### Training Pipeline
1. **Rationale Generation**: Claude Opus 4.6 (the teacher) generates structured rationales for each trajectory, referencing specific actions against the scheming specification, and produces a band rating with evidence
2. **Quality Filtering**: A separate judge model evaluates the quality of generated rationales and filters out low-quality examples
3. **SFT Stage**: Open-weight student models are fine-tuned on the filtered (trajectory, rationale, label) triples. SFT provides the dominant performance improvement (e.g., Qwen-3-8B: 0.362 to 0.610 Mean-OOD pAUROC@20)
4. **RL Stage**: Reinforcement learning further refines performance with smaller gains (Qwen-3-8B: 0.610 to 0.628; Qwen-3-32B: 0.668 to 0.703)

### Training Datasets (5 sources)
- **STRIDE** (1,350 samples): Synthetic enterprise software-engineering scenarios (logistics, governance, AI training pipelines)
- **Gloom** (772 samples): Operational-management deployments with decision authority
- **CoT-Red-Handed** (2,340 samples): Self-contained programming problems with covert side tasks
- **Bash Arena** (2,790 samples): Multi-turn Linux engineering with system infrastructure tasks
- **Bash Bench** (1,436 samples): Short test-graded shell-scripting tasks

Total training set: 8,688 samples (50/50 balanced benign/scheming per source)

### OOD Evaluation Datasets (6 benchmarks)
- **CUA-SHADE-Arena** (955 samples): Computer-use agent sabotage
- **MLE-Sabotage** (148 samples): ML engineering sabotage
- **AgentDojo** (1,500 samples): Multi-domain agent security
- **IAC** (1,443 samples): Infrastructure-as-code agent misalignment
- **Rogue Eval** (1,500 samples): Rogue agent evaluation
- **SHADE-Arena** (856 samples): Shade-style adversarial settings

### Models Trained (9 models, 4 families)
- **Qwen family**: Qwen3-8B, Qwen3-32B, Qwen3.5-4B, Qwen3.5-27B
- **GPT-OSS family**: GPT-OSS-20B, GPT-OSS-120B
- **Nemotron family**: Nemotron-30B, Nemotron-120B
- **Kimi**: Kimi-K2.6 (MoE, largest model, best absolute performance: 0.841 Mean-OOD pAUROC@20)

### Primary Metric
pAUROC@20 -- partial AUROC focusing on the low false-positive-rate region (FPR <= 20%), which is critical for deployment where false alarms are costly.

## Results & Highlights

### SFT is the main driver, RL adds marginal gains
- Qwen-3-8B: Base 0.362 -> SFT 0.610 -> RL 0.628 (Mean-OOD pAUROC@20)
- Qwen-3-32B: Base 0.255 -> SFT 0.668 -> RL 0.703
- SFT teaches the core deliberative monitoring behavior; RL is a refinement stage

### Cost-Performance Pareto Frontier
- Qwen3.5-27B outperforms Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite, GPT-5.4 Nano, Claude Haiku 4.5, and Gemini 2.5 Pro
- Cost: under $5 per 1,000 evaluations
- Stronger frontier monitors (Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.4, Claude Sonnet/Opus 4.6) achieve higher absolute performance but at 16-34x higher marginal inference cost
- Multiple trained monitors are Pareto-optimal

### Cross-family generalization
- Deliberative supervision works across all 4 model families
- Within families, larger models consistently outperform smaller variants (Qwen3.5-27B > Qwen3.5-4B; GPT-OSS-120B > GPT-OSS-20B; Nemotron-120B > Nemotron-30B)

### Data scaling
- Performance improves with more training data (204 to 8,688 samples), with diminishing returns
- High-quality deliberative data can partially compensate for model scale (Qwen-3 32B vs 8B gap narrows with more data)

### Training mixture breadth
- Broader behavioral coverage improves OOD transfer, especially with Bash and coding sabotage data
- Adding Gloom alone has limited OOD benefit; Bash variants yield significant gains

### Failure modes
- **Primary failure**: False positives on messy benign trajectories where monitors are suspicious of mistakes, suboptimal actions, and incomplete reporting
- **Hard cases**: SHADE-Arena is particularly difficult under all data mixtures
- Monitors sometimes flag benign exploratory actions or workflow messiness as concealment

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The application of deliberative alignment to the oversight/monitoring side (rather than agent behavior) is a clear conceptual contribution. The three-stage pipeline (teacher rationale -> judge filter -> SFT+RL distill) is well-motivated and cleanly executed. However, the individual components (knowledge distillation, RL fine-tuning, specification-grounded reasoning) are not individually novel -- the contribution is in their combination and application to this specific problem.

- **Rigor**: Evaluation across 6 OOD benchmarks is strong. Using pAUROC@20 as the primary metric (focusing on low FPR) is the correct choice for deployment. Reporting Mean-OOD with standard deviations across seeds adds credibility. The ablation suite (SFT vs RL, mixture breadth, model scaling, data scaling) is comprehensive.

- **Reproducibility**: Uses open-weight models (Qwen, GPT-OSS, Nemotron, Kimi). The pipeline is clearly described. The training datasets are from publicly available or described sources. However, the scheming specification and exact prompts are in appendices -- full reproducibility depends on their release. The teacher model (Claude Opus 4.6) is proprietary.

- **Significance**: High practical value. The cost-performance Pareto analysis provides immediately actionable guidance for practitioners. The finding that SFT dominates over RL is practically important for reducing training costs.

- **Red flags / concerns**:
  1. All training and evaluation data is synthetic or semi-synthetic. The authors themselves acknowledge generalization to real-world long-horizon adaptive adversaries remains an open question.
  2. The 16-34x cost gap vs strong frontier monitors is significant -- these monitors still leave substantial detection capability on the table.
  3. The teacher model (Claude Opus 4.6) could potentially sabotage the rationale generation by engineering blind spots, which the authors acknowledge but do not mitigate.
  4. SHADE-Arena performance is fragile across all data mixtures, suggesting fundamental blind spots.
  5. The primary failure mode (false positives on messy benign trajectories) is a serious practical concern that could limit deployment utility.
  6. Smaller monitors have shorter context windows, which creates a fundamental scalability issue for long-horizon tasks.

## Strengths

- Addresses a concrete and increasingly important deployment problem in AI safety
- Comprehensive experimental design: 5 training sets, 6 OOD benchmarks, 9 models across 4 families
- Honest and nuanced results presentation -- does not oversell relative to frontier models
- Cost-performance Pareto framing provides practical deployment decision guidance
- Thorough ablation suite covers the most important practitioner questions (SFT vs RL, data breadth, model scale, data scale)
- Failure mode analysis is candid and actionable
- Open-weight model basis enhances reproducibility and enables defender-hosted deployment
- The finding that deliberative data can partially compensate for model scale is practically useful

## Limitations

- Synthetic/semi-synthetic benchmarks only; real-world scheming may look very different
- 16-34x cost gap vs strongest frontier monitors means significant detection capability is sacrificed for cost savings
- Teacher model (Claude Opus 4.6) is a single point of failure in the pipeline and could introduce blind spots
- SHADE-Arena remains fragile under all configurations, indicating hard cases that the current approach cannot reliably handle
- False positive problem on messy benign trajectories limits practical deployment
- Smaller monitor context windows constrain applicability to long-horizon tasks
- Collision risk between monitors and frontier models is reduced but not formally guaranteed
- The quality of the entire pipeline depends on the scheming specification, which may not cover all real-world scheming patterns
- RL gains are marginal and sometimes non-uniform across dataset splits, raising questions about RL stage necessity

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How robust are the trained monitors against adaptive adversaries that are aware of the monitoring specification and actively attempt to evade detection?
- Can hierarchical cascades (routing uncertain cases to specialized or stronger monitors) improve the cost-performance tradeoff beyond the current Pareto frontier?
- How does the choice of teacher model affect the quality of distilled monitors? Is there a teacher quality threshold below which distillation fails?
- What is the degradation curve when applying these monitors to truly long-horizon tasks (hundreds of tool calls) that exceed the monitor's context window?
- How can the false positive problem on messy benign trajectories be mitigated without sacrificing detection sensitivity -- e.g., through adversarial hardening on benign-but-suspicious trajectories?
- Can the deliberative monitoring framework be extended to detect other misalignment behaviors beyond scheming (e.g., capability sandbagging, reward hacking, alignment faking)?
