Kronecker Embeddings: Byte-Level Structured Token Representations for Parameter-Efficient Language Models

Replace the learned embedding table with a deterministic byte-position Kronecker product. Fixed encoder, one learned projection, 91–94% fewer input-side parameters — and it wins on validation loss.

Method · Three-Step Pipeline

1 · Byte Lookup

For each token ID, retrieve its UTF-8 byte sequence from the tokenizer’s existing mapping. No new vocabulary needed.

2 · Kronecker Codec (Fixed)

For each byte-position pair (bp, p), form the Kronecker product of a 256-dim one-hot byte vector and a dp-dim position one-hot. Sum across positions, normalize by 1/√L. Per-token z-normalization follows.

3 · Learned Projection

A single Wproj ∈ ℝD×dmodel is the only trainable input-side parameter. Initialized N(0, 1/√D). Self-entropy regularizer prevents subspace collapse in production.

Strengths

  • Clean math: single structural property (byte-level locality) explains both strengths and limitations
  • Exceptional self-assessment: grades claims by strength, flags missing comparisons explicitly
  • Strong experimental hygiene: three seeds, 18/18 cells, ~13:1 SNR
  • Practical: drop-in replacement, Apache 2.0 code, production models on HuggingFace
  • Cross-model probe is an independent contribution to understanding embedding structure
  • 4.5 MB vs 2.15 GB embedding memory matters for edge deployment and quantized models

Limitations & Open Questions

  • ! All controlled results at 124–138M. Whether the advantage holds at 1B+ is untested.
  • ! No BPE-untied baseline — the actual frontier standard (DeepSeek-V3, Qwen-32B, OLMo-2).
  • ! No comparison to modern byte-level methods (Charformer, BLT, MYTE).
  • · No downstream tasks (MMLU, HellaSwag, ARC). Only validation loss reported.
  • · Byte-similar but semantically distant pairs (compute/commute, nation/notion) cluster together.
  • · D may need to scale with dmodel at very large widths, flattening parameter savings.
  • · No multilingual or long-context evaluation.

Key Follow-Up Questions

  • Does the 2.5% validation loss advantage persist, shrink, or disappear at 1B–7B scale?
  • Three-arm comparison: BPE-tied + BPE-untied + Kronecker — is the win over tying or over learned embeddings?
  • How does Kronecker compare against Charformer / BLT / MYTE under matched compute?
  • Does typographic clustering hold for multilingual models, or is it English-centric?
  • Interaction with quantization (GPTQ, AWQ) — the fixed codec should quantize differently.
