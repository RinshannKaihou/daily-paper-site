COMET dissects CLAP's 1024-dim space into a 100-dim shared semantic head that carries nearly all cross-modal signal

Concept Space Dissection of the Modality Gap in Audio-Text Multimodal Contrastive Embeddings. A PLS-SVD framework reveals that the modality gap has three independent sources -- not just the cone-effect mean shift -- and that truncating to the top 100 spectral directions eliminates 90% of dimensionality with zero performance loss.

Yonggang Zhu, Liting Gao, Aidong Men, Wenwu Wang (CVSSP, Univ. of Surrey)

IEEE Journal · 2026-05-28 · Multimodal / Audio-Text / Contrastive Learning

Head Dimension 100 of 1024 total — 90% compression

Compute Cost <0.16s PLS-SVD on CPU, 19K pairs
Storage 202 values stored vs ~19K embeddings for PD
CLAP embeddings decompose into mean + shared semantic head (~100 dims) + modality-private tail (~924 dims). Cross-modal similarity is dominated by aligned direction pairs in the head; the tail contributes near-zero useful signal despite retaining 31% of the embedding energy.
PLSHead (100-dim) achieves SPIDER 27.5 on Clotho zero-shot captioning, matching Projection Decoding (27.7) and exceeding the fully supervised baseline (26.8). On AudioCaps: SPIDER 40.6 vs. supervised 40.3.
A training-free spectral truncation eliminates the memory bank required by Projection Decoding and achieves parity with supervised methods. The framework also provides the first theoretical explanation of why PD works, grounding it in head-preserving + tail-imputation + base-change + mean-shift.
Only validated on CLAP (audio-text, 1024-dim). The "beats supervised" claim is against a weak 4-layer Transformer without pretraining. Generalization to CLIP, other dimensions, or stronger supervised baselines is unproven. Head size K=100 is empirically chosen, not principled.

01 Embedding Space Structure

Head / Tail Decomposition

PLS-SVD on the cross-modal covariance matrix M = TTA produces paired directions (U, V) ranked by shared semantic strength. Singular values plummet after ~100 indices:

100 dims
924 dims (modality-private)
Shared semantic head Modality-private tail

The tail retains 34% of text embedding energy (norm 0.344) but carries near-zero cross-modal alignment (ui·vi ≈ 0). It is modality-private content, not noise.

Three Sources of the Modality Gap

COMET proves the gap originates from all three subspaces, overturning the prior "cone-effect mean shift" narrative:

Mean
Global offset between modality centers
Head
Aligned axes but different scales (singular values)
Tail
Unaligned directions with near-zero cross-correlation

Similarity dissection (Eq. 6-9) shows "direct effects" from matched head direction pairs account for nearly all dot-product similarity; "cross effects" from mismatched pairs are negligible.

Retrieval (mAP@10)

PLSHead at 100 dims achieves comparable or better mAP@10 than full 1024-dim embeddings across Clotho (in-domain), AudioCaps (in-domain), and Clotho-to-AudioCaps (cross-domain). PCA-based truncation fails badly, confirming the cross-modal decomposition is essential.

DRCap Comparison

Against the stronger DRCap model: PLSHead scores 27.4 vs. 28.5 on Clotho (slightly below) and 43.3 vs. 42.4 on AudioCaps (wins). Competitive with a purpose-built system while being training-free.

04 Assessment & Open Questions

Strengths

  • Every claim backed by formal derivation + experimental verification
  • First theoretical explanation of why Projection Decoding works
  • Unified framework: decomposition, similarity analysis, gap explanation, mitigation
  • 90% compression, no memory bank, <0.16s CPU computation
  • Semantically interpretable concept directions (Table II)

Limitations

  • Only CLAP (audio-text); CLIP / ImageBind / other modalities untested
  • Supervised baseline is weak (4-layer Transformer, no pretraining)
  • Linear PLS-SVD cannot capture non-linear alignment structures
  • K=100 chosen empirically, not from a principled criterion
  • Cross-domain tested in only one direction (Clotho → AudioCaps)

Open Questions

Does the ~10% head-size ratio (100/1024) hold for CLAP variants at 512 or 768 dims?
Can PLSHead transfer to CLIP vision-language models?
How does head size change as CLAP pretraining data scales up?
Can the modality-private tail be useful for modality identification or domain adaptation?
What happens with stronger captioners (GPT-2, LLM-based)?
Is there a principled, automatic method to determine optimal K?
