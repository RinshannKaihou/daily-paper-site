Why This Matters
Deployable scheming detection without accessing chain-of-thought or model internals — a practical safety layer for agentic AI systems at production scale.
Critical Edge
All benchmarks are synthetic. Real adaptive adversaries and messy benign trajectories causing false positives remain unsolved. SHADE-Arena is fragile under all configurations.

Three-Stage Pipeline

1
Rationale Generation (Teacher) Claude Opus 4.6 generates structured, specification-grounded rationales for each agent trajectory with band ratings and evidence.
2
Quality Filtering (Judge) Independent judge model evaluates rationale quality and removes low-quality samples before distillation.
3
SFT + RL Distillation Open-weight students fine-tuned on filtered triples. SFT provides dominant gains (e.g. 0.362 → 0.610). RL adds marginal refinement (0.610 → 0.628).

Strengths

  • Concrete deployment problem: scheming detection without CoT or internals access.
  • Comprehensive evaluation: 5 training sets, 6 OOD benchmarks, 9 models across 4 families.
  • Cost-performance Pareto framing gives actionable deployment guidance.
  • SFT-dominant finding reduces training costs; RL stage may be unnecessary.
  • Honest results presentation — does not oversell relative to frontier models.
  • Open-weight basis enables defender-hosted deployment and reproducibility.

Limitations

  • All benchmarks are synthetic; real-world scheming may differ substantially.
  • 16–34× cost gap vs. strongest frontiers — significant detection capability sacrificed.
  • Teacher (Claude Opus 4.6) is a single point of failure; could introduce blind spots.
  • False positives on messy benign trajectories limit practical deployment.
  • SHADE-Arena fragile under all configurations — fundamental blind spots persist.
  • Smaller monitor context windows constrain long-horizon task applicability.

Open Questions

  • Robustness against adaptive adversaries aware of the monitoring specification?
  • Can hierarchical cascades (routing uncertain cases to stronger monitors) push past the current Pareto frontier?
  • Is there a teacher quality threshold below which distillation fails entirely?
  • Degradation curve on truly long-horizon tasks (hundreds of tool calls) exceeding the monitor context window?
  • Can the framework extend beyond scheming to capability sandbagging, reward hacking, or alignment faking?
