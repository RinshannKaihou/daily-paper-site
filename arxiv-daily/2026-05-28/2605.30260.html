How LoRA Remembers? A Parametric Memory Law for LLM Finetuning

LoRA's memory capacity obeys a power law—and a sharp phase transition at p = 0.5 determines whether a token is locked for verbatim recall or cascades into failure.

Parametric Memory Law

The central quantitative result: LoRA's loss reduction follows a power law in rank and sequence length, validated across two model families and three data regimes.

ΔL = C · rα · l−β + b
r = LoRA rank · l = sequence length · C, α, β, b are fitted constants
  • Validated on Qwen3-8B-IT and Llama3.1-8B-IT
  • Three data regimes: semantic text, random tokens, PhoneBook key-value pairs
  • Unified formula fits 0–100% random token mixture with R² of 0.987 (Llama) and 0.983 (Qwen)
  • Saturated samples (Lfinal ≤ 0.69) excluded to avoid the trivially-memorized regime

Beyond Memorization

The memorization-focused approach does not harm—and may help—generalization. On the Linear Rule Learning benchmark (f(x,y) = 3x + 5y + 7), MemFT improves generalization accuracy by 7–15% over SFT across ranks.

Critical Assessment

Strengths

  • Elegant power law with R² > 0.98 across diverse settings — principled quantitative framework
  • Loss-Accuracy Misalignment is important and counterintuitive — challenges common practice
  • Clear mechanistic arc: macroscopic scaling → microscopic phase transition → practical method (MemFT)
  • MemFT achieves 100% exact match where SFT fails, especially under constrained budgets
  • Generalization analysis shows memorization focus does not harm unseen data performance
  • Code to be released at zjunlp/ParametricMemoryLaw

Limitations

  • Scale Only 8B models tested; power law exponents may not transfer across scales
  • Decoding p > 0.5 threshold only validated for greedy decoding; behavior under sampling unknown
  • Baselines No comparison against curriculum learning, focal loss, or other loss-reweighting methods
  • Circular Saturation exclusion (L ≤ 0.69) removes the regime where the power law breaks, inflating fit quality
  • Breadth MemFT evaluated on two exact-memory benchmarks + one synthetic generalization test
  • Scope Single LoRA injection strategy; other configurations (target modules, alpha scaling) untested
