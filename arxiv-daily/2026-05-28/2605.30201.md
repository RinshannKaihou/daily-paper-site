> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 HPO（滞后策略优化），通过对 GRPO 的两项极简修改——对负优势样本施加滞后权重降权、用批次平均长度替代逐响应长度归一化——解决了稀疏奖励下 GRPO 训练中负优势样本过度主导和长度偏差的问题，自适应版本 A-HPO 在 TeleLogs 任务上最终奖励达 0.84，分别超越 SAPO 5%、GSPO 11%、GRPO 15%。

> **TL;DR:** HPO is a minimal two-change fix to GRPO for sparse-reward LLM fine-tuning: (1) down-weight negative-advantage updates via a hysteretic factor alpha, and (2) replace per-response length normalization with batch mean-length normalization. The adaptive variant A-HPO automatically tunes alpha from batch-level sign statistics. On TeleLogs, A-HPO reaches 0.84 reward (vs. GRPO's 0.73), and shows the largest early-learning gains on Countdown across 1.5B-7B models. The method adds negligible compute overhead and integrates into VERL with ~30 lines of code.

## 核心贡献

- 识别了 GRPO 在稀疏奖励场景下的两个耦合失败模式：负优势样本数量远多于正优势样本（符号失衡），以及逐响应长度归一化导致短正确回答获得过大梯度而长错误回答惩罚不足（长度偏差）
- 提出 HPO，仅需一个参数 alpha 在"仅使用正样本"和"对称使用正负样本"之间插值，并配合批次平均长度归一化
- 提出 A-HPO，利用批次中正/负优势样本的频率比自动设定 alpha，无需手动调参
- 在 TeleLogs 和 Countdown 两个任务上验证，A-HPO 在匹配 rollout 预算下显著提升奖励效率，尤其在稀疏奖励的早期训练阶段优势最大

## Key Contributions

- Identify two coupled failure modes of GRPO under sparse rewards: sign imbalance (negative advantages overwhelmingly dominate) and response-level length normalization bias (short correct responses get amplified gradients while long incorrect ones get weak penalties)
- Propose HPO, a one-parameter interpolation (alpha in [0,1]) between positive-only and symmetric policy-gradient updates, combined with mean-length normalization
- Introduce A-HPO, which sets alpha adaptively from the ratio of positive to negative advantage frequencies in each batch, eliminating manual tuning
- Demonstrate that both extremes (alpha=0 positive-only and alpha=1 fully symmetric) are suboptimal; the best regime lies in between and A-HPO automatically tracks it
- Validate on TeleLogs and Countdown tasks, showing A-HPO achieves 0.84 final reward on TeleLogs (outperforming GRPO by 15%) and largest early-learning gains on Countdown across 1.5B-7B models

## Methodology

**Problem setup:** The paper considers sparse binary reward settings where the initial policy has very low pass rates. In GRPO, group-normalized advantages are computed per response, and token-level losses are averaged by each response's length.

**HPO consists of two modifications to the GRPO objective:**

1. **Hysteretic advantage weighting:** Positive-advantage samples retain full weight (w=1), while negative-advantage samples are down-weighted by a factor alpha in [0,1]. This directly addresses the sign imbalance where p- >> p+ in sparse-reward regimes.

2. **Mean-length normalization:** Instead of dividing each response's loss by its own length |tau_i| (which makes aggregate contribution inversely proportional to length), HPO divides all responses by the batch mean length |tau_bar|. This decouples update magnitude from individual response length.

**A-HPO (Adaptive HPO):** Sets alpha adaptively as alpha = clip(p_plus / p_minus, alpha_min, 1), where p_plus and p_minus are the fractions of positive and negative advantages in the current batch. This is motivated by a gradient-norm balance criterion (||G+|| ~ ||alpha * G-||), approximated via sign frequencies. In sparse regimes where negative advantages dominate, alpha is small; as training progresses and the balance improves, alpha approaches 1 (recovering symmetric updates).

**Implementation:** The method adds negligible computational overhead -- only a batch-level sign count and scalar clipping. A complete VERL integration is provided in ~30 lines of Python code. The paper sets alpha_min = 0.4, PPO clip epsilon = 0.2, learning rate 1e-6, N=8 rollouts per prompt.

## Results & Highlights

- **TeleLogs (Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct):** A-HPO achieves 0.84 final reward vs. SAPO 0.80 (+5%), GSPO 0.76 (+11%), GRPO 0.73 (+15%). Response lengths remain comparable (686 vs. 731 for GRPO).
- **Countdown task:** A-HPO shows the largest gains in the initial and most difficult configurations. For Llama3.1-3B on Countdown-4, A-HPO improves 200-step reward by +0.14 over GRPO. Gains are most pronounced when successful trajectories are rare.
- **Ablation on alpha:** Performance is non-monotonic. Alpha=0 (positive-only, RAFT-like) collapses to 16-token outputs with 0.71 reward. Alpha=1.0 (symmetric) yields 0.72 reward. Alpha~0.6 and A-HPO achieve the best results (~0.83-0.84).
- **Batch size effects:** HPO variants substantially outperform GRPO even at batch size 1 (alpha estimated from 8 rollouts). At batch size 16, A-HPO remains the strongest.
- **Larger models:** HPO training remains stable and effective at 7B and 32B scales on TeleLogs (32B reaches 95.86% pass@1), though these larger models start outside the strict sparse-reward regime.
- **Diagnostic variants:** N-HPO (hysteresis only, keeping GRPO normalization) improves over GRPO, confirming hysteresis alone is beneficial. A-HPO further improves over N-HPO, confirming mean-length normalization adds value.

## Authors & Credibility

All authors are from Huawei Technologies (Paris Research Center and China). The paper provides complete implementation details including VERL integration code, full hyperparameter tables, and multiple ablation studies. The work is presented as a preprint under review. The empirical methodology is sound with matched rollout budgets across comparisons.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate. The idea of hysteretic/asymmetric learning rates has roots in classical RL (as the authors acknowledge). The specific application to GRPO's advantage weighting and the sign-frequency adaptive rule are novel but incremental. Mean-length normalization follows directly from DrGRPO's observation about length bias.

**Rigor:** Generally strong. The paper clearly identifies the failure mode, motivates the fix, and provides thorough ablations. The adaptive rule is acknowledged as a heuristic rather than a principled gradient-norm estimator, which is intellectually honest. However, the theoretical grounding is limited -- the balance criterion (Equation 13) is motivated but not formally analyzed (no convergence guarantees or regret bounds).

**Reproducibility:** High. Full hyperparameters are listed (Table 4), VERL integration code is provided (Figure 7), and the method is simple enough to re-implement. Models (Qwen2.5, Llama3.1) and tasks (Countdown, TeleLogs) are clearly specified.

**Significance:** Practically significant for practitioners doing RL fine-tuning of LLMs with sparse verifiable rewards (e.g., math, coding, diagnostic tasks). The method is a drop-in replacement for GRPO with minimal overhead. The finding that both positive-only and fully symmetric extremes are suboptimal is a useful practical insight.

**Red flags / concerns:**
- TeleLogs is a proprietary dataset from Huawei's 5G domain, making it harder for external researchers to verify.
- The Countdown results are presented as training curves rather than final-evaluation tables, making precise comparison harder.
- The paper evaluates on relatively few tasks (two). Generalization to standard RLHF benchmarks (MATH, GSM8K, HumanEval) is not demonstrated.
- At 32B scale, the initial pass@1 is already ~49%, meaning the model is outside the sparse-reward regime -- so the method's benefits at truly large scale remain unclear.
- The adaptive rule's approximation (using sign frequencies as proxy for gradient-norm ratio) lacks analysis of when and why it breaks down.

## Strengths

- Minimal, clean modification to GRPO -- only two changes, each well-motivated
- A-HPO eliminates the need to tune alpha manually, which is a practical advantage
- Strong ablations that isolate the contribution of each component (hysteresis vs. normalization)
- Honest about the method's scope: explicitly states it targets sparse verifiable rewards, not dense/process-reward settings
- Negligible computational overhead -- a key practical advantage
- The finding that alpha=0 (RAFT-like positive-only) collapses to degenerate short outputs is a valuable negative result

## Limitations

- Only two evaluation tasks, one of which (TeleLogs) is proprietary and not publicly available
- No evaluation on standard RLHF benchmarks like MATH, GSM8K, or coding tasks where sparse rewards also arise
- No theoretical convergence analysis; the adaptive rule is heuristic
- Benefits narrow or disappear when tasks become easy or models are already strong (the authors acknowledge this)
- The recommended alpha_min = 0.4 is itself a hyperparameter that may need tuning for different setups
- No comparison with process-reward-model or dense-reward approaches that address sparse rewards from a different angle

## Relevance

本文对 GRPO 在稀疏奖励下的改进思路具有直接的实践价值。如果你在使用 GRPO 或其变体进行 LLM 的 RL 微调（如数学推理、代码生成等场景），且初始通过率较低，HPO/A-HPO 是一个低成本的即插即用替代方案，尤其能加速早期学习。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does A-HPO perform on standard RLHF benchmarks (MATH, GSM8K, HumanEval) where sparse binary rewards are common?
2. Can the adaptive hysteretic rule be extended to dense or process-reward settings, or does it specifically require the binary sparse-reward structure?
3. What happens when the sign-frequency approximation fails (e.g., when positive and negative advantages have very different per-sample gradient magnitudes)?
4. Is there a principled way to set alpha_min rather than treating it as a fixed hyperparameter?
5. How does A-HPO interact with curriculum learning or reward shaping strategies that gradually reduce sparsity?
