Positional Encoding · Transformer Interpretability

Positional representations spontaneously collapse into a 2D manifold — and MLM destroys them

DSTG-NeoBERT disentangles semantic, absolute positional, and relative positional streams in an encoder Transformer. The isolated AP subspace collapses to a 2D sinusoidal manifold capturing 94.1% of variance, while standard models lose positional information in deep layers because MLM training overwrites it.

✓ Strengths
  • Clean, well-motivated architecture enabling mechanistic analysis impossible with standard Transformers
  • Three complementary findings (manifold collapse, head specialization, positional loss) forming a coherent narrative
  • MLM-destroys-positional-information is specific and actionable — a concrete design principle for future architectures
  • Excellent hierarchical probing methodology (token, segment, intra-segment levels)
  • Honest about limitations; does not oversell results
  • Retrieval head analysis (heads 3, 6) is an elegant mechanistic finding
✗ Limitations & Open Questions
  • Scale: 6-layer, 6-head, 22B tokens. Generalization to 1B+ params untested
  • ~6% parameter advantage complicates fair Flash-Holmes comparison
  • Encoder-only; decoder applicability speculative (causal masking provides implicit position)
  • 512 token limit; cannot validate long-context benefits
  • No downstream task improvement despite probing gains
  • Intra-segment position in semantic subspace is not fully explained
➤
Key open question: Does the 2D manifold collapse hold at 7B params / 1T+ tokens? Could the AP manifold be explicitly parameterized as a 2D sinusoidal function to reduce parameters and improve extrapolation?
