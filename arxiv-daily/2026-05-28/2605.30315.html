Resolution Diagnostics for Paired LLM Evaluation

Leaderboard rankings many practitioners treat as ground truth are statistically underresolved. This paper formalizes paired LLM evaluation as hypothesis testing, introduces the resolution ratio q = N/N*, and proves the widely-used unpaired sample size shortcut underestimates required N by roughly 2x.

Anany Kotawala Princeton University pip: llm-power
◆ Key Finding

On two public leaderboards, a substantial fraction of model-pair rankings are statistically unresolved at (alpha=0.05, power=0.8) -- the data cannot reliably tell which model is better.

◆ Key Metric
~2x

The unpaired Cohen-h+(1-rho) shortcut underestimates required paired sample size by a factor of two in close-comparison regimes (IQR [0.4999, 0.5035] on 40 OLL v1 pairs).

◆ Why This Matters

Rankings on public leaderboards may be noise, not signal. Every practitioner relying on them to choose models needs this resolution diagnostic before trusting any rank ordering.

◆ Critical Edge

Restricted to binary accuracy only. Does not address pairwise-preference leaderboards like Chatbot Arena, arguably the most influential format today.

⚙ Method Pipeline

01
Paired Framing
Two models A, B evaluated on same N prompts. Paired difference D_i = X^A_i - X^B_i with Wald test statistic
02
Invert the Test
Invert level-alpha, power-(1-beta) tests to get delta_MDE, required N*, and resolution ratio q = N/N*
03
McNemar + Lemma 1
For binary accuracy, use McNemar-Connor formula; Lemma 1 derives explicit 2nd-order constant C(p, rho)
04
Stress Test
Robustness under Bonferroni, BH, clustering (ICC), leave-one-subject-out, bootstrap, anytime-valid sequential
Lemma 1 — The Shortcut Bias

The unpaired Cohen-h2·(1−ρ) shortcut yields nh/N* ≈ 0.50 in close-comparison regimes. At p = 0.5, the second-order constant C(0.5, ρ) = 1/3 independent of ρ (a clean sanity check). Off-midpoint, C grows with |1−2p| and ρ. Three major power calculators (Cohen 1988, G*Power 3.1, R pwr) silently inherit this ~2x underestimation.

✓ Additional Findings

  • Paired vs unpaired efficiency: Paired McNemar required-N is a median 2.15x smaller than the unpaired Gaussian formula on the same 40 OLL v1 pairs.
  • Calculator misuse: At (p_A, p_B, rho) = (0.65, 0.60, 0.30), three of five calculators underestimate required-N by ~2x.
  • Cluster sensitivity: High design effects (DE = 31.5, 6.7) in MMLU-Pro subject clustering inflate N* dramatically for two pairs.
  • Sequential penalty: Anytime-valid time-uniform threshold inflates N* by ~2.15x vs fixed-n.
  • Bootstrap support: 99.9% of category-bootstrap resamples support 5-6/9 unresolved conclusion for MMLU-Pro.

⚠ Critical Assessment

Novelty: Moderate-High
Rigor: Very High
Reproducibility: High
Significance: High
  • Individual components (McNemar, Bonferroni, cluster effects) are classical -- novelty is in Lemma 1's explicit constant and the end-to-end pipeline integration.
  • Exceptionally rigorous: formal proofs with explicit bounds, numerical cross-checks to 4 significant figures, prospective validation (power 0.80±0.03).
  • Single author -- derivations would benefit from independent verification.
  • MMLU-Pro cluster analysis uses only K=14 categories, limiting ICC estimate precision.

✓ Strengths

  • Lemma 1 provides a genuinely useful closed-form bound, not just an asymptotic statement.
  • Prospective validation (Table 10) proves the framework works in practice.
  • Comprehensive robustness pipeline: multiplicity + clustering + sequential + bootstrap, applied end-to-end.
  • Honest about scope -- explicitly states what is not covered (graded metrics, preference leaderboards).
  • Practical deliverable: pip install llm-power with full diagnostic API.

✗ Limitations

  • Restricted to binary accuracy; graded metrics validated only to 4-6% on synthetic Beta marginals.
  • K=14 subject categories may be too coarse for precise ICC estimation.
  • Does not address Chatbot Arena-style pairwise-preference leaderboards.
  • Does not survey how prevalent the Cohen-h+(1-rho) misuse is in published LLM evaluation work.
  • Closed-source frontier panel (Section D) is a snapshot, not a durable comparison.
