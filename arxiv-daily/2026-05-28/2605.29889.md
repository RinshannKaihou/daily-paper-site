> **一句话总结：** 本文通过稀疏自编码器(SAE)特征分析和自然语言自编码器(NLA)验证，发现LLM在临床分诊多选题基准上的高漏诊率并非源于医学知识缺失——同一医学特征在临床叙述token上正常激活，但在多选决策token处完全沉默，支架/格式特征接管了决策逻辑，差距主要由相邻分级偏差(off-by-one)而非知识失败主导。

> **TL;DR:** A prior benchmark (Ramaswamy et al., Nature Medicine 2026) reported 51.6% under-triage for ChatGPT on patient-voiced clinical vignettes using forced-letter multiple-choice output. This paper uses SAE features on Gemma 3 4B/12B IT and Qwen3-8B, plus Natural Language Autoencoder (NLA) verbalization, to show that medical features fire identically on clinical narratives under both multiple-choice and free-text formats (98.3-100% of peaks anchored in shared vignette tokens), but go completely silent at the multiple-choice decision token. Scaffold/format features dominate the decision logits instead. Three independent methods (NLA verbalization, logit attribution, top-feature characterization) agree. The accuracy gap is dominated by off-by-one adjacent acuity miscalibration (e.g., NL picks B where gold is C), not knowledge failure. Exhaustive option-order shuffles rule out positional bias. The failure is in output-stage mapping, not clinical representation.

## 核心贡献

- 通过SAE机械可解释性分析，明确区分了"编码偏移假说"与"输出映射假说"，证明LLM分诊失败属于后者
- 在Gemma 3 4B/12B IT和Qwen3-8B三个模型上，医学特征在临床叙述token上的峰值锚定率高达98.3-100%，但在多选决策token处激活为零
- 使用自然语言自编码器(NLA)语言化直接展示了决策token处的表征翻转：临床内容从"主要主题"降级为"次要提及"，而多选支架成为主导
- 通过穷举选项排列洗牌(60x23=1380次/模型)排除了位置偏差假说，证明4B模型的NL-NF差距可完全归因于基准答案键与内容先验的交互作用
- 线性探针实验表明，源格式隐藏状态可预测格式切换后的行为翻转(AUC=0.769-0.804)，且Constraint-First消融实验揭示约束位置对表征偏移有显著贡献

## Key Contributions

- Disambiguates the "encoding-shift hypothesis" from the "output-mapping hypothesis" using SAE mechanistic interpretability, conclusively showing LLM triage failures belong to the latter
- Across Gemma 3 4B/12B IT and Qwen3-8B, medical features peak on vignette tokens in 98.3-100% of (case, feature) combinations under both formats, yet have zero activation at the multiple-choice decision token
- Uses Natural Language Autoencoder (NLA) verbalization to directly demonstrate the representational flip at the decision token: clinical content is demoted from "primary subject" to "partial mention" while multiple-choice scaffold becomes the primary frame
- Exhaustive option-order shuffling (60 x 23 = 1,380 passes per model) rules out positional bias; at 4B, shuffled NL accuracy (69.8%) becomes statistically indistinguishable from NF accuracy (71.7%), attributing the entire gap to answer-key-layout x content-prior interaction
- Linear probes on source-format hidden states predict behavioral flipping across format transitions (AUC = 0.769-0.804, p < 0.01); a Constraint-First ablation shows constraint position relative to the generation point substantially contributes to the representational shift

## Methodology

The study uses 60 patient-voiced clinical-triage vignettes from the replication corpus of Fraile Navarro et al. (2026), originally from Ramaswamy et al. (2026, Nature Medicine). The corpus spans a 2x2 factorial design: input format (structured vignette vs. naturalistic/conversational) x output format (forced-letter A-D multiple-choice vs. free-text), yielding four conditions: SL, NL, SF, NF.

**Models and SAEs:** Gemma 3 4B IT (34 layers) and Gemma 3 12B IT (48 layers) with Gemma Scope 2 SAEs (d_SAE=16,384, ~14% reconstruction error), and Qwen3-8B (36 layers) with Qwen-Scope SAEs (d_SAE=65,536, ~34% reconstruction error at L31). Four matched-depth layers are swept per Gemma model; Qwen uses L31 based on a pilot sweep.

**Medical-feature identification:** 3 features per model per layer identified via contrastive activation (medical vs. non-medical prompts), validated with selectivity filters (>=70% medical firing, <=10% non-medical). A K-sweep (K in {3,5,10,20}) confirms stability.

**Mechanistic invariance test:** Max-pooled SAE feature activations compared across NL-NF conditions using sMAPE and cosine similarity, with magnitude-matched random feature baselines and 2,000-resample bootstrap CIs.

**Decision-token analysis:** (1) NLA verbalization at Gemma 12B L32 across 7 token positions, with LLM judges classifying descriptions as medical-primary/partial/no and scaffold-primary/partial/no. (2) SAE logit attribution via per-feature unembedding projection. (3) Top-20 feature characterization with Jaccard overlap and scaffold-peak diagnostics.

**Behavioral decomposition:** Exhaustive option-order shuffles, 5-way adjudication (A/B/C/D/deferred) with clinician calibration on a stratified 16-case sample, and per-case gap decomposition into adjacent miscalibration vs. deferral.

**Linear probes:** L2-regularized logistic regression on last-token hidden states at multiple layers to predict behavioral flipping, with Constraint-First ablation moving formatting instructions to prompt start.

## Results & Highlights

**Behavioral differences (Table 1):** At Gemma 4B, free-text NF exceeds NL by +16.7pp (p=0.031, McNemar). At 12B and Qwen, the pattern inverts: NL outperforms NF by -10.0pp and -6.7pp respectively, meaning larger models are penalized by free-text mode — the format effect inverts with scale.

**Gap decomposition (Table 2):** At 4B, 13/14 gap-driving cases follow the exact pattern "NL picks B, gold is C, NF picks C" — a systematic single-acuity-step under-triage in multiple-choice mode. Deferrals (conditional triage responses) exist at 12B (4 cases) and Qwen (2 cases) but contribute zero to the gap.

**Medical-feature invariance (Table 3):** At every model and stratum, medical features show significantly lower sMAPE and higher cosine similarity than magnitude-matched random controls (all bootstrap 95% CIs non-zero). Medical feature peaks are anchored in the shared vignette in 98.3-100% of combinations.

**Decision-token mode shift (Table 4):** NLA verbalization at Gemma 12B shows content tokens are med-primary under both formats, but at the NL decision token, all 60 cases flip to sca-primary/med-partial. LLM judges agree 96.7%/96.2% on medical/scaffold axes. Zero medical features are active at the NL decision token across all 60 cases at every model.

**Option-order shuffle:** At 4B, shuffling letter-to-content mappings raises NL accuracy from 55.0% to 69.8% — statistically indistinguishable from NF's 71.7%. Position bias ruled out (same-letter rates at/below chance). Content prior is strong (64-83% same-content across models).

**Linear probes:** NL-sourced embeddings at late layers predict format-flipping with AUC=0.769-0.804 (p=0.004-0.010). Constraint-First ablation selectively modifies late-layer signal for NL-sourced transitions.

**Clinician calibration:** Cohen's kappa = 0.808 between LLM judges and blinded clinician on 5-class labels. Clinician deferral rate (37.5%) exceeds model rate (6.7%), suggesting models under-defer relative to clinical practice.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: High.** This is one of the first studies to apply SAE mechanistic interpretability to a real-world clinical evaluation problem, going beyond behavioral analysis to localize where in the model the format effect originates. The combination of SAE features, NLA verbalization, logit attribution, and linear probes is methodologically sophisticated and provides genuinely converging evidence.

**Rigor: Strong.** Multiple independent methods cross-validate the core finding. The exhaustive option-order shuffle (1,380 passes per model) is a thorough control. The magnitude-matched random baselines with 1,000-resample permutation tests address the obvious "cherry-picked features" concern. The K-sweep confirms the 3-feature result is not a small-sample artifact. Clinician adjudication calibrates the LLM-judge pipeline. The authors are unusually transparent about limitations.

**Reproducibility: Good.** Code, data, and analysis scripts are publicly available. All three models are open-weight. The SAE checkpoints (Gemma Scope 2, Qwen-Scope) are publicly released. The option-order shuffle and bootstrap procedures are precisely specified.

**Significance: High.** Directly challenges the headline finding of a Nature Medicine paper (Ramaswamy et al., 2026) that reported alarming 51.6% under-triage rates, showing the failure is an artifact of forced-letter multiple-choice evaluation rather than genuine clinical incompetence. Has immediate implications for how clinical AI benchmarks should be designed.

**Red flags / concerns:**
- The 60-case sample is small. The Qwen behavioral gap is not statistically significant (McNemar p=0.45), though the mechanistic direction reproduces.
- Qwen-Scope's ~34% reconstruction error at L31 substantially raises the noise floor for that model; the effect sizes are correspondingly smaller.
- Feature identification at 12B and Qwen reuses the same 60 NF prompts as the medical set with only 30 non-medical controls — a held-out contrastive split would be cleaner.
- SAE-based causal interventions (ablation and ActAdd steering) change only 0-2 of 60 NL predictions, consistent with concurrent null results from Basu et al. — the features are readout probes, not causal handles. This limits the practical remediation pathway.
- The finding that the format penalty *inverts* at larger models (12B, Qwen prefer NL over NF) complicates the "multiple-choice is always worse" narrative and is underexplored.
- NLA evidence is limited to Gemma 3 12B IT only (the only released checkpoint).

## Strengths

- Exceptional methodological triangulation: SAE features, NLA verbalization, logit attribution, linear probes, behavioral decomposition, and clinician calibration all converge on the same conclusion
- The exhaustive option-order shuffle is a genuinely thorough control that cleanly disentangles position bias from content priors and their interaction with the canonical answer-key layout
- Honest about what SAE features can and cannot do: explicitly frames them as "readout probes of medical-domain content" rather than "causal handles," acknowledging intervention nulls
- The Constraint-First ablation is a clever experiment that provides mechanistic insight into why the representational shift occurs (constraint position matters, not just presence)
- Clinician calibration with kappa=0.808 provides external validity for the LLM-judge pipeline
- Transparent disclosure of AI assistant usage with clear delineation of human vs. AI contributions
- Addresses a finding published in Nature Medicine, making the work practically impactful

## Limitations

- Small sample size (n=60 vignettes). Several behavioral strata contain very few cases (e.g., 6 NL-only-right cases at 12B, 4-8 cases per stratum at Qwen), limiting statistical power.
- Only greedy decoding is evaluated; temperature or sampling-based generation could yield different behavioral patterns.
- Qwen-Scope's ~34% reconstruction error at L31 makes the Qwen mechanistic findings substantially noisier than Gemma findings.
- The medical features identified are domain-content detectors (medical vs. non-medical), not acuity probes — the paper can only claim medical *content* is preserved, not that correct *triage disposition* is encoded.
- SAE interventions fail to correct the format effect (0-2 of 60 predictions changed), offering no practical fix for the identified problem.
- The format penalty inversion at larger models (12B, Qwen) — where free-text *underperforms* multiple-choice — is acknowledged but not fully explained.
- NLA analysis is limited to one model (Gemma 12B), reducing generalizability of the verbalization evidence.
- The claim scope is carefully circumscribed: the paper shows the failure is in output mapping, not that the model is safe for clinical deployment.

## Relevance

本研究对理解LLM在医疗场景中的可靠性评估具有重要方法论价值。它揭示了多选题评估方法可能系统性低估或误估模型的临床能力，尤其是当模型在自由文本输出中展现出合理的分诊推理却被多选格式惩罚时。对于关注LLM可解释性和医疗AI评估的研究者，本文提供了一个机械可解释性应用于实际评估问题的优秀案例。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can format-agnostic evaluation protocols (e.g., structured extraction with open-ended acuity scales, or ranked preference elicitation) better capture clinical competence without introducing scaffold artifacts?
- What causes the format penalty to invert at larger models (12B, Qwen)? Is this related to instruction-tuning artifacts, free-text hedging/deferral patterns, or something else?
- Would training models with explicit format-invariance objectives (e.g., contrastive loss across output formats) resolve the decision-token mode shift?
- How do these findings generalize beyond triage to other structured medical reasoning tasks (diagnosis, treatment planning, medication dosing)?
- Can richer causal interventions (e.g., multi-layer, multi-feature steering, or representation engineering) overcome the single-layer SAE intervention nulls documented here and in Basu et al. (2026)?
- The scaffold-firing features that dominate NL decision logits are nearly disjoint from NF's top features — what do they encode beyond "peak on answer-key scaffold tokens," and are they learned from pretraining/instruction-tuning exposure to MCQ formats?
