> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Temporal Logit Observability (TLO)，通过监控解码过程中 compliance-refusal 对数边际的时序演化，将每个 model-attack 条件映射到校准的 2D RP 平面上，使 ASR 相同但失败机制不同的攻击变得可区分，并从中导出的 early-stop 规则将聚合 ASR 从 39.6% 降至 13.1%（良性查询零误报）。

> **TL;DR:** TLO is a training-free, logit-only diagnostic that tracks a compliance-refusal margin (LMS) during decoding and maps each model-attack condition onto a calibrated 2D Relative Position (RP) plane. Across Llama, Mistral, Qwen, and Gemma under MCM, GCG, and DeepInception attacks, conditions with nearly identical ASR (within 3.3 pp) land at clearly different RP-plane locations (median D_RP = 0.572). The logit trajectory separates successful from failed jailbreaks within the first five tokens (AUC = 0.771, Cohen's d > 1.0), and aligns with hidden-state refusal directions on 10/12 conditions. A t_cross-based early-stop rule at w=5 reduces aggregate ASR from 39.6% to 13.1% with 0.0% FPR on format-free benign queries. The approach requires full logit access (top-k at k=20 is insufficient) and uses a fixed 47-phrase English lexicon.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 Temporal Logit Observability (TLO)，一种无需训练、仅需输出 logits 的诊断协议，将解码过程中的 safety 信号时序结构转化为校准的 2D 坐标
- 设计 Relative Position (RP) 校准方案，通过 harmful 和 attack-formatted benign 参考锚点使跨模型、跨攻击的可比性成为可能；RP 位移量受 (1-ASR) 约束，确保与 ASR 的互补性
- 揭示了 ASR 无法区分的 model-attack 交互结构：同一攻击族在不同模型上产生完全不同的时序签名，同一模型在面对不同攻击时通过不同的时序机制失败
- 从 t_cross 导出的 early-stop 规则在 w=5 时将聚合 ASR 从 39.6% 降至 13.1%，对 format-free benign 查询零误报
- 验证了 logit 轨迹与 hidden-state refusal-direction projections 在 12 个条件中 10 个条件下对齐，Llama 全部三个攻击达到 |rho| >= 0.70

## Key Contributions

- Introduce TLO, a training-free protocol converting per-step Logit-Margin Scores (LMS = mu_cmp - mu_ref) into calibrated 2D RP-plane coordinates c = (RP_A, RP_B) without hidden-state access or model modification
- Design RP calibration against harmful and attack-formatted benign anchors, with RP displacement bounded by (1-ASR), ensuring complementarity: TLO is most informative where ASR is least informative (mid-ASR range)
- Demonstrate that ASR collapses temporally distinct failure paths: among 9 model-attack pairs within 5 pp ASR, median calibrated separation D_RP = 0.572 [0.489, 0.924]; the strongest pair (Llama+DI vs. Mistral+GCG, ASR diff 1.7 pp) reaches D_RP = 1.014
- Derive a t_cross-based early-stop rule that cuts aggregate ASR from 39.6% to 13.1% (delta = 26.5 pp) with 0.0% FPR on format-free benign queries
- Validate logit trajectory alignment with hidden-state refusal directions on 10/12 conditions, with Llama achieving |rho| >= 0.70 on all three attacks

## Methodology

**Core diagnostic pipeline (3 stages):**

1. **Temporal Logit Signal:** Compute LMS at each decoding step: S_t = mu_cmp(t) - mu_ref(t), where mu is the mean top-k (k=10) logit over compliance/refusal lexicon tokens. Extract S_0 (pre-generation, at final prompt position) and S-bar (generation-time mean). The lexicon is a fixed 47-phrase English set (26 refusal, 21 compliance).

2. **Activation Timing Signals:** t_cross = first generation step where S_t < 0 (refusal dominance); sign reversal captures sustained shift from compliance-dominant to refusal-dominant trajectory.

3. **RP Calibration:** Relative Position RP(M) = (M_success - M_harmful) / (M_benign - M_harmful), where anchors use all harmful samples and attack-formatted benign references. Applied to S_0 yields RP_A; applied to S-bar yields RP_B. D_RP is the Euclidean distance between conditions on this plane.

**Experimental setup:** 4x3 grid of four open instruct models (Llama-3.1-8B, Mistral-7B-v0.3, Qwen2.5-7B, Gemma-2-9B) and three jailbreak paradigms (MCM multi-turn, GCG suffix optimization, DeepInception template). 60 harmful JailbreakBench prompts per condition + 60 attack-formatted benign references. Llama-Guard-3-8B as primary judge, with HarmBench-Mistral and GPT-4o as auxiliary judges. Greedy decoding throughout.

**Key design property:** RP displacement is bounded by (1-ASR) on each axis, so high-ASR conditions carry their signal primarily in ASR itself, while mid-ASR conditions are where RP separates attacks that ASR cannot.

## Results & Highlights

**Q1: RP-plane geometry recovers ASR-hidden structure:**
- 7/12 conditions show statistically reliable RP-plane displacement after FDR-corrected permutation tests
- GCG ranges from near RP origin on Qwen (ASR 76.7%) to large generation-time displacement on Llama (RP_B = 3.80 at ASR 8.3%)
- Llama+DI vs. Qwen+DI: ASR difference only 3.3 pp, but D_RP = 0.572 [0.237, 1.026]
- Llama+DI vs. Mistral+GCG: ASR difference 1.7 pp, D_RP = 1.014 [0.726, 1.351]

**Q2: Logit signal is informative and tracks hidden states:**
- S-bar_{1:5} separates success/failure with AUC = 0.771, Cohen's d > 1.0 (p < 10^{-38}, n=720)
- t_cross achieves AUC = 0.784 [0.750, 0.816]
- Sign reversal occurs 5.2x more often in failed jailbreaks (40.0% vs. 7.7%)
- 10/12 conditions show expected-sign alignment between S_t and hidden-state refusal projections
- Llama: all attacks reach |rho| >= 0.70; modal cross-correlation lag = -1 (logit margin moves one step earlier than hidden-state projection)
- Qwen: model-specific boundary, Fisher-z mean approximately zero under MCM and DI

**Q3: Early-stop intervention:**
- t_cross > 5 rule: aggregate ASR 39.6% -> 13.1% (delta = 26.5 pp)
- 0.0% FPR on format-free benign queries across all 12 conditions
- Attack-formatted benign references (stress test): 46.0% false halt; S_0-gated variant reduces to 12.6%
- Largest drops in NGB conditions (delta = 40.3 pp), consistent with complementarity design

**Robustness:**
- Stochastic decoding: ICC = 0.90-0.94 for trajectory metrics
- Lexicon perturbations: |delta AUC| <= 0.018 across 5 variants
- Metric decomposition: margin outperforms refusal-only by 0.162 AUC due to common-mode rejection
- Rank-based RP tracks primary coordinates: Pearson r = 0.858/0.920/0.925 for RP_A/RP_B/displacement
- Full logit access required: at API-level k=20, trajectory correlation with full-logit S_t is near zero (r = -0.12 to -0.01)

## Authors & Credibility

All four authors (Junyoung Park, Sunghwan Park, Seongyong Ju, Jaewoo Lee) are from Chung-Ang University, with Jaewoo Lee as corresponding author. The work is positioned at the intersection of LLM safety evaluation and representation analysis, grounding its approach in prior work on refusal directions (Arditi et al., NeurIPS 2024), representation engineering (Zou et al., 2023), and logit-gap steering (Li and Liu, 2025). The paper includes an unusually thorough supplementary appendix (Sections A-H) covering anchor sensitivity, lexicon perturbations, layer sweeps, judge agreement, a 240-item adjudicated human audit, and complete reproducibility resources. The supplementary archive provides reproduction scripts, benchmark inputs, and redacted per-sample metric tables. All experiments ran on a single RTX 4090 workstation in under 58 minutes total.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: High.** The core insight -- that temporal logit dynamics during decoding reveal structure invisible to ASR -- is genuinely novel. The RP calibration mechanism, the (1-ASR) complementarity bound, and the formalization of temporal observability as a diagnostic rather than just an intervention are all well-conceived contributions. The framing of safety evaluation as needing "when and how" rather than just "whether" is persuasive.

**Rigor: Very high.** The paper is methodologically thorough to an unusual degree. Every design choice has a corresponding sensitivity analysis: anchor choice (3 variants), lexicon composition (5 perturbation variants + data-driven induction), aggregation size k (4 values), crossing strictness (n=1,2,3), layer depth (L25, L50, L75), judge variation (3 judges + human audit), and decoding randomness (temperature/p sampling). Statistical tests use FDR correction and bootstrap CIs. The human audit (240 items, 2 raters, adjudicated disagreements, Cohen's kappa = 0.732 binary) adds credibility to the judge-based labels. The explicit reporting of Qwen as a model-specific boundary case (rather than hiding it) is commendable.

**Reproducibility: Excellent.** Training-free, single-GPU (RTX 4090), 58-minute total runtime. Supplementary archive with reproduction scripts. All code and data paths documented. The approach is deterministic under greedy decoding.

**Significance: High.** If adopted, TLO could change how the safety community evaluates jailbreaks. The finding that "the same attack on different models produces distinct calibrated geometries" has direct implications for defense design. The early-stop rule is a practical contribution.

**Red flags / concerns:**
1. **Qwen limitation is non-trivial:** 4/5 NGB conditions involve Qwen, and the fixed-lexicon approach produces near-chance AUC on Qwen+GCG (0.519). The data-driven lexicon induction shows transfer gaps (mean off-diagonal AUC drops from ~0.93 to ~0.65). This suggests the approach may not be as model-portable as claimed.
2. **Full logit access requirement is a serious practical limitation:** The vocabulary-level top-k truncation experiment (Appendix F.10) shows that at k=20 (typical API output), correlation with full-logit S_t is near zero. This restricts TLO to open-weight model auditing, not API-based deployment -- a scope limitation the authors acknowledge but which materially limits real-world applicability.
3. **Small-n conditions:** Llama+MCM and Mistral+MCM have only n_succ = 3, making per-condition statistics unstable despite the pooled results being robust.
4. **Early-stop rule is not independent of the diagnostic:** The t_cross-based halt rule is derived from the same temporal signal used for diagnosis. Against adaptive attacks that specifically delay t_cross, the rule's effectiveness is untested.
5. **The 2D plane is a lossy summary:** Per-condition AUC heterogeneity (Table 5: ranging from 0.511 to 0.987) shows that the pooled statistics mask substantial variation. Some conditions are near chance even with full trajectory features.
6. **English-only lexicon:** The fixed 47-phrase lexicon is English-specific. The authors note multilingual extension as future work but do not discuss the implications for non-English safety evaluation.

## Strengths

- Genuinely novel perspective on LLM safety evaluation; the temporal framing fills a real gap in the literature
- Exceptional methodological transparency and thoroughness: every design choice has a sensitivity check in the appendix
- Training-free, logit-only approach with minimal compute requirements (single GPU, <1 hour)
- Honest reporting of failures: Qwen boundary case, NGB conditions, and API-access limitations are clearly stated
- The RP calibration design with the (1-ASR) complementarity bound is elegant and well-justified
- The common-mode rejection property of the margin over single-component variants is a clean result
- Supplementary archive and reproducibility resources set a strong example

## Limitations

- Full logit access required; standard API top-k outputs (k=20) are provably insufficient (Appendix F.10)
- Fixed English lexicon limits applicability to non-English models and safety contexts
- Qwen is a clear boundary case: 4/5 NGB conditions involve Qwen, and Qwen+GCG is near chance for all logit summaries
- The approach is observational, not causal: TLO makes failure dynamics visible but does not identify underlying circuits or mechanisms
- Small n_succ in low-ASR conditions (3 successes for Llama+MCM and Mistral+MCM) limits per-condition reliability
- No evaluation against adaptive attacks designed to evade temporal detection
- The early-stop rule trades off safety for response completeness: legitimate benign responses that happen to delay compliance signaling may be incorrectly halted when wrapped in attack-formatting

## Relevance

本文提出了一种新颖的 LLM 安全评估范式，通过解码时序 logit 动态揭示 ASR 无法区分的失败路径差异。对于关注 LLM 安全性评估、越狱检测机制、模型对齐鲁棒性以及推理时防御策略的研究者具有直接参考价值，尤其是在理解不同攻击如何以不同时序模式绕过安全对齐方面。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does TLO perform against adaptive attacks that explicitly optimize to delay or suppress the LMS crossing signal?
- Can lightweight distilled monitors be trained to approximate TLO from limited logit access (e.g., top-k at k >= 50), bridging the gap between full-logit auditing and API-based deployment?
- What is the relationship between TLO's temporal failure patterns and specific mechanistic circuits identified through causal tracing or circuit-level analysis?
- Does the RP-plane geometry change qualitatively for reasoning models (e.g., o1, DeepSeek-R1) where chain-of-thought introduces a substantially different decoding structure?
- How would per-model or per-attack lexicon induction affect the reliability of RP-plane coordinates, given the transfer gap observed in Appendix F.8?
- Can TLO be extended to multilingual settings, and how would different tokenizers affect the lexicon expansion and cross-model comparability?
