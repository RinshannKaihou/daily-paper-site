> **一句话总结：** 本文首次对SPEC CPU2026基准测试套件进行了跨九个平台（Intel/AMD/Ampere/Nvidia）的全面微架构特征分析，发现相比CPU2017，新套件指令量显著增加、内存足迹增大1.4倍、L1指令缓存压力提升2.53倍，通过PCA+层次聚类证明每组4-5个工作负载即可保留96.4-99.9%全套件行为，并展示了新RRR stagger模式可合成近似DCPerf的代理工作负载（IPC差距仅13.7%）。

> **TL;DR:** First comprehensive characterization of SPEC CPU2026 across 9 platforms (Intel Skylake/Icelake/Sapphire Rapids, AMD Milan/Genoa/Turin, Ampere Altra, Nvidia Grace). Key findings vs. CPU2017: L1I$ MPKI up 2.53x (INT Rate), memory footprint 1.4x larger, branch MPKI down 0.46x. Clustering-based subset analysis (PCA + hierarchical clustering) shows 4-5 workloads per group preserve 96.4-99.9% of full-suite behavior. Cross-suite comparison with DCPerf and MLPerf positions CPU2026 as general-purpose: less vector-intensive than MLPerf (43.21% vs 29.97% vector share), lower frontend pressure than DCPerf, but closer to real-world behavior than prior SPEC generations. Practical case studies cover page sizes/allocators, prefetching, compiler optimization, ISA sensitivity, many-core scaling, and RRR proxy workload generation.

## 核心贡献

- 首次对SPEC CPU2026进行了跨9个平台的全面微架构特征刻画，涵盖43个工作负载（14 INT Rate + 13 INT Speed + 12 FP Rate + 11 FP Speed）
- 量化了CPU2026相对CPU2017的关键演进：L1I$ MPKI提升2.53倍（INT Rate），内存足迹增大1.4倍，分支MPKI降低至0.46倍，指令缓存压力成为新的主要瓶颈
- 基于PCA降维+层次聚类的方法论，证明每组4-5个工作负载的紧凑子集即可保留96.4-99.9%全套件行为，大幅降低评测成本
- 与DCPerf、MLPerf的跨套件比较定位了CPU2026的角色：向量指令密度远低于MLPerf、前端压力低于DCPerf，但比前代SPEC更接近真实CPU行为
- 展示了CPU2026在5类实际架构研究中的适用性：页面大小/分配器（mimalloc/tcmalloc带来8-21%性能提升）、硬件预取（CPU2026预取收益1.15x > CPU2017的1.06x）、编译器演进（gcc-15 vs gcc-13）、ISA敏感性、众核扩展（40线程下CPU2026扩展性优于CPU2017）
- 揭示了新RRR stagger模式可合成混合代理工作负载，将CPU2026与DCPerf的IPC差距缩小至13.7%

## Key Contributions

- First comprehensive microarchitectural characterization of SPEC CPU2026 across 9 platforms (Intel Skylake/Icelake/Sapphire Rapids+Sapphire Rapids HBM, AMD Milan/Genoa/Turin, Ampere Altra Neoverse-N1, Nvidia Grace), covering both x86_64 and AArch64 ISAs
- Quantified evolutionary shifts from SPEC CPU2017: L1I$ MPKI increases 2.53x for INT Rate (3.26x for FP Rate), memory footprint grows ~1.4x, branch MPKI drops to 0.46x of CPU2017, while compute instruction fraction rises to 43.21% (vs 39.71%)
- Demonstrated via PCA + hierarchical clustering that compact subsets of 4-5 workloads per group preserve 96.4-99.9% of full-suite geometric-mean score accuracy
- Cross-suite comparison positioning SPEC CPU2026 against CPU2017, DCPerf, and MLPerf: CPU2026 is substantially less vector-intensive than MLPerf (29.97% vector share), has lower frontend pressure than DCPerf, but provides complementary stress characteristics
- Practical case studies: page size/allocator tuning (mimalloc/tcmalloc improve CPU2026 by 8.1-12.7% via TLB reduction), hardware prefetchers (CPU2026 shows 1.15x IPC gain vs CPU2017's 1.06x), compiler evolution (gcc-13/14/15), ISA sensitivity (AArch64 vs x86_64), and many-core scaling (CPU2026 sustains scalability at 40 threads better than CPU2017: +9.4%/+10.2% from 32→40 vs +3.1%/+5.0%)
- Showed RRR stagger mode can compose heterogeneous proxy workloads by mixing complementary benchmark stress patterns (e.g., 709.cactus_r + 749.fotonik3d_r → IPC within 13.7% of DCPerf django)

## Methodology

The study uses a rigorous three-stage pipeline for workload characterization and representativeness analysis:

**Platforms:** 9 dual-socket systems spanning Intel (Skylake 8160, Icelake 8380, Sapphire Rapids 8468, SPR MAX 9480 with HBM2e), AMD (Milan 7763, Genoa 9454, Turin 9555), Ampere (Altra Neoverse-N1), and Nvidia (Grace). Memory technologies include DDR4, DDR5, LPDDR5, and HBM2e. All benchmarks compiled with gcc using SPEC-recommended flags; hardware counters collected via Linux perf.

**Metrics:** 19 microarchitectural metrics per platform including IPC, L1I/L1D/L2/L3 cache MPKI, L1I/L1D/L2 TLB MPMI, branch MPKI, frontend/backend stall cycles, instruction mix (load/store/branch/FP/vector shares), kernel/user split, and DRAM access intensity. This yields 171 features (19 × 9 platforms) for clustering.

**Clustering pipeline:**
1. **Data normalization** — normalize metrics across workloads to emphasize behavioral differences over absolute magnitudes
2. **Feature extraction** — PCA reduces 171 dimensions while preserving dominant cross-platform behavioral patterns
3. **Hierarchical clustering** — groups workloads by microarchitectural similarity in PCA space; medoid selection picks the most central workload per cluster

**Representativeness metric:** subset accuracy = 1 − |GM(subset) − GM(suite)| / GM(suite), where GM is the geometric-mean SPEC running score.

**Cross-suite comparison:** Same clustering pipeline applied jointly to SPEC CPU2026 Rate, SPEC CPU2017 Rate, DCPerf, and MLPerf Inference workloads, visualized via dendrograms and PC projections.

## Results & Highlights

- **L1I$ pressure surges:** SPEC CPU2026 INT Rate geometric-mean L1I$ MPKI rises 2.53x over CPU2017; FP Rate rises 3.26x. The workload 709.cactus_r reaches L1I$ stress levels comparable to DCPerf workloads.
- **Memory footprint grows:** SPEC CPU2026 max RSS ~2.2GB vs CPU2017 ~1.7GB; median ~1.4GB vs ~0.8GB. 52% of CPU2026 Rate workloads exceed the memory threshold vs 52% of CPU2017's smaller suite.
- **Branch behavior improves:** CPU2026 INT branch MPKI drops from 3.11 to 1.44 (0.46x), indicating less control-flow unpredictability.
- **Compact subsets are effective:** Representative subsets of 4 workloads (INT Rate/Speed) and 5 workloads (FP Rate/Speed) achieve 96.4-99.9% accuracy. Specific subsets identified (e.g., INT Rate: 750.sealcrypto_r, 735.gem5_r, 721.gcc_r, 708.sqlite_r).
- **Allocator impact dominates page size:** On 4KB-page x86 systems, mimalloc/tcmalloc improve CPU2026 by 8.1-12.7% primarily via TLB reduction (~65.3% dTLB, ~56.2% iTLB MPMI reduction). On 64KB-page AArch64, effects are muted (-2.5% to +0.6%).
- **Prefetchers more effective on CPU2026:** Enabling all prefetchers yields 1.15x IPC gain on CPU2026 vs 1.06x on CPU2017. L2 prefetcher contributes more than L1 (1.12x vs 1.07x).
- **Better many-core scalability:** CPU2026 continues gaining performance at high thread counts (32→40 threads: +9.4%/+10.2% for CPU-B/C) while CPU2017 saturates (+3.1%/+5.0%).
- **RRR proxy works:** Staggered execution of 709.cactus_r (high L1I$ stress) + 749.fotonik3d_r (high L3$ stress) produces a mixed proxy with IPC within 13.7% of DCPerf django, matching DCPerf's order-of-magnitude frontend pressure (L1I$ MPKI 44.0 vs 39.7/45.1).
- **CPU2026 removes outliers:** Notably removes 505.mcf_r (extreme memory subsystem stress approaching 80% peak bandwidth), resulting in more stable suite-level behavior.
- **ISA sensitivity broader:** CPU2026 shows larger instruction-count variation across ISAs (0.72x-1.51x range, std 10.8%) vs CPU2017 (0.81x-1.24x, std 5.4%), with workloads like 750.sealcrypto_r retiring 20-30% fewer instructions on AArch64.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. First comprehensive characterization of the newly released SPEC CPU2026. The representativeness analysis directly influenced the SPEC CPU2026 program selection process (per acknowledgments), lending additional significance.
- **Rigor:** Very strong. 9 platforms, 4 vendors, 2 ISAs, 43 workloads, 19 metrics × 9 platforms = 171 features. The three-stage clustering pipeline (normalize → PCA → hierarchical clustering) follows established methodology from prior SPEC characterization work.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate-to-good. Methodology is clearly described and uses standard tools (Linux perf, gcc). However, exact reproduction requires access to 9 specific hardware platforms, some of which are pre-release or specialized (e.g., SPR MAX with HBM2e). The SPEC CPU2026 suite itself was pre-release at time of writing.
- **Significance:** High. Compact subset recommendations (Table 4) have immediate practical value for anyone running SPEC CPU2026 evaluations. The cross-suite positioning helps architects choose the right benchmarks for their research questions.
- **Potential concerns:**
  - The 96.4-99.9% accuracy metric is based solely on geometric-mean SPEC score recovery, which is a narrow definition of "behavior." It does not guarantee that subset workloads preserve per-metric distributions (e.g., cache MPKI ranges, TLB behavior extremes). A subset that matches the aggregate score could miss important tail behaviors.
  - The paper removes 505.mcf_r from CPU2017 analysis as an "outlier," but this workload represents a real and important memory-intensive use case. Its absence from CPU2026 may actually be a weakness of the new suite for stress-testing memory subsystems.
  - No power/energy analysis despite growing importance for datacenter CPU evaluation.
  - The cross-suite comparison with MLPerf uses inference workloads only; MLPerf Training could show different characteristics.
  - The RRR proxy workload evaluation (Section 5.3) is limited to one configuration (2 workloads on 1 platform) — more comprehensive evaluation would strengthen this claim.
  - The paper does not address how well SPEC CPU2026 workloads represent AI/ML orchestration workloads (the stated motivation about CPU importance in accelerator-dominated systems).

## Strengths

- Definitive first characterization study with strong empirical backing (9 platforms, 43 workloads)
- Practical and immediately actionable: Table 4 provides specific compact subset recommendations that can save days of benchmarking time
- Rigorous methodology with clear rationale for each analysis step
- Cross-suite comparison provides essential context for benchmark selection decisions
- Case studies are well-chosen and demonstrate genuine architectural insights (e.g., allocator dominance over THP, prefetcher effectiveness differences)
- Honest about limitations of SPEC CPU2026 (does not subsume DCPerf/MLPerf, removes some stress-test extremes)

## Limitations

- Representativeness accuracy metric is narrowly defined (geometric-mean score only); does not capture distributional fidelity of individual microarchitectural metrics
- No power/energy characterization despite datacenter relevance being a key motivation
- Removal of stress-test workloads like 505.mcf_r means CPU2026 may under-represent memory subsystem extremes
- RRR proxy evaluation is preliminary (only 2 workloads, 1 platform, limited validation)
- Cross-suite comparison limited to 4 suites; missing comparison with other emerging benchmarks (e.g., cloud-native microservice suites, AI serving benchmarks)
- Does not characterize SPEC CPU2026's suitability for simulators (e.g., SimPoint analysis, sampling feasibility)

## Relevance

本文对SPEC CPU2026的全面微架构特征分析和紧凑子集推荐，对理解现代CPU性能瓶颈演化趋势和降低基准评测成本具有直接参考价值。特别是L1I$压力提升、预取器敏感性增强、众核扩展性改善等发现，对计算机体系结构研究和数据中心CPU设计均有重要指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How do the compact subsets perform when evaluated with distributional metrics (e.g., Kolmogorov-Smirnov distance on per-metric distributions) rather than just geometric-mean score recovery?
2. What are the power and energy implications of SPEC CPU2026's shifted bottleneck profile — does higher I-cache stress translate to different energy efficiency characteristics?
3. How should architects combine SPEC CPU2026 compact subsets with DCPerf/MLPerf for a comprehensive yet cost-effective evaluation strategy?
4. How stable are the recommended compact subsets across future microarchitecture generations (e.g., Intel Clearwater Forest, AMD Zen 6)?
5. Can the RRR proxy methodology be systematically extended to generate workload proxies for specific production environments beyond DCPerf (e.g., AI serving pipelines, database engines)?
6. How well do SPEC CPU2026 workloads capture the CPU-side behavior of AI workload orchestration (data preprocessing, tokenization, attention scheduling) that motivates the paper's introduction?
