VLMaxxing through FrameMogging: Training-Free Anti-Recomputation for Video Vision-Language Models

arXiv: 2605.03351

Date: 2026-05-05

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03351

⚠️ Abstract-only summary

一句话总结： 本文提出无需训练的视觉状态复用策略，在视频VLM推理中跳过对稳定画面的重复编码，使同视频后续查询延迟降低14.9–35.9倍。
TL;DR: Proposes training-free anti-recomputation for video VLMs — reuse cached visual state when the scene is stable, recompute only on change. On frozen Qwen2.5-VL-7B-4bit, adaptive same-video follow-up reuse cuts latency 14.9–35.9x on VideoMME while preserving correctness. Also introduces C-VISION to skip redundant vision-tower work before the first answer, achieving 1.316x speedup on Gemma 4-E4B-4bit.

核心贡献

  • 将视频VLM中的重复视觉编码问题建模为"训练无关的反重计算"（training-free anti-recomputation），即当验证表明视觉状态未变化时直接复用缓存状态。
  • 提出自适应同视频后续查询复用策略，在Qwen2.5-VL-7B-4bit上将跟进查询延迟降低14.90–35.92倍，且在93题VideoMME上保持正确性。
  • 提出C-VISION方法，在首个答案生成前跳过定时视觉塔计算，在Gemma 4-E4B-4bit上实现1.316倍的首查询加速。
  • 引入C-CEILING（阶段占比天花板）作为会计护栏：组件加速只能按其墙钟占比转化为端到端加速，不会叠加放大。
  • 展望VLM原生媒体格式方向，直接暴露变化、运动、不确定性等信息，避免模型从密集RGB重新发现世界。

Key Contributions

  • Formulates redundant visual encoding in video VLMs as a training-free anti-recomputation problem — reuse cached visual state when validation confirms stability.
  • Achieves 14.90–35.92x latency reduction on same-video follow-up queries with Qwen2.5-VL-7B-4bit, preserving correctness on a 93-query VideoMME benchmark.
  • Introduces C-VISION to prune timed vision-tower work before first-answer generation, yielding 1.316x speedup on Gemma 4-E4B-4bit with no accuracy drift.
  • Proposes C-CEILING as an accounting guardrail: component-level speedups translate to end-to-end gains only proportional to their wall-clock share.
  • Advocates for VLM-native media formats that expose change, motion, uncertainty, and object state directly, eliminating the need to rediscover the world from dense RGB every frame.

Methodology

The paper studies three axes of training-free anti-recomputation for video VLMs:

  1. After-ingest follow-up reuse: When a user asks follow-up questions about the same video, the visual state (KV-cache from the vision tower) is reused rather than recomputed. An adaptive policy decides when cached state is still valid vs. when fresh encoding is needed based on scene change, query type, and cache topology.
  1. C-VISION (first-query vision pruning): Before the first answer is generated, the vision tower is invoked on a schedule. C-VISION skips unnecessary vision-tower invocations when the visual input hasn't changed, accelerating the cold-start path.
  1. C-CEILING (accounting guardrail): A theoretical and practical framework establishing that component-level speedups (e.g., vision tower only) can only improve end-to-end latency in proportion to that component's share of total wall-clock time. This prevents overclaiming from composing non-overlapping speedups.

All experiments use frozen quantized models (4-bit) with no retraining. Stress tests include 50-turn repeated-question schedules and dense-answer-anchored prompt variations to separate conservative from aggressive caching policies.

Results & Highlights

  • 14.90–35.92x latency reduction on same-video follow-up queries with Qwen2.5-VL-7B-4bit on VideoMME (93 queries), with paired choice correctness preserved.
  • 1.316x first-query speedup with C-VISION on Gemma 4-E4B-4bit (32-frame short cell, 20 items), no drift or parse failures.
  • Repeated-question schedules hold through 50 turns without degradation.
  • Conservative fixed K=1 repair policy maintains fidelity; aggressive policies trade slight drift for higher speed.
  • Fresh-video pruning shows smaller but real gains.
  • C-CEILING analysis correctly bounds end-to-end improvements from component-level optimizations.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. KV-cache reuse for LLMs is well-studied; the application to video VLM vision towers is a natural but non-trivial extension. The C-CEILING accounting framework is a useful contribution for honest benchmarking.
  • Rigor: The stress-test methodology (50-turn schedules, prompt variation anchoring) is commendable. However, the abstract-only view limits assessment of full ablation depth.
  • Reproducibility: Claims are tied to specific quantized models and benchmarks. The "training-free" nature aids reproducibility. Unconventional naming and writing may hinder clarity for some readers.
  • Significance: The 14.9–35.9x speedup on follow-up queries is substantial for multi-turn video QA deployments. However, the first-query speedup (1.316x) is modest, and the cold-start problem remains.
  • Red flags: The 14.9–35.9x figure applies only to follow-up queries on the same video — not to the general case. The headline number may mislead without careful reading. C-STREAM is explicitly noted as "not a headline result," suggesting unfinished work.

Strengths

  • Training-free approach requires no model changes, enabling immediate deployment.
  • Honest accounting via C-CEILING prevents overclaiming from composed speedups.
  • Practical stress tests (50 turns, prompt variation) stress-test the caching policies meaningfully.
  • Addresses a real cost bottleneck in video VLM serving — redundant visual encoding.

Limitations

  • First-query (cold-start) gains are modest (~1.3x); the headline speedup only applies to follow-up queries on the same video.
  • Evaluated on only two model families (Qwen2.5-VL and Gemma 4) in 4-bit quantization.
  • C-STREAM (native-rate streaming) remains a future target, not yet realized.
  • The writing style, while entertaining, may obscure technical content for some audiences.
  • Unclear how the approach handles scene cuts, camera switches, or rapid motion within a single video.

Relevance

与视频VLM推理效率直接相关——通过KV缓存复用大幅降低同视频多轮查询的计算开销，对实际部署场景（如视频问答系统）有显著实用价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the caching policy handle videos with frequent scene cuts or rapid visual changes (e.g., action movies, sports)?
  • What is the memory overhead of maintaining visual state caches for multiple concurrent videos?
  • Can the adaptive reuse policy be learned or tuned automatically per video type?
  • How do the results scale to longer videos (hours) beyond the benchmark durations?
  • What is the interaction between anti-recomputation and other inference optimizations (speculative decoding, quantization-aware caching)?
