The Right Answer, the Wrong Direction: Why Transformers Fail at Counting and How to Fix It

arXiv: 2605.03258

Date: 2026-05-05

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03258

一句话总结： 本文以线性探针证明Qwen3-8B/Mistral-7B/Pythia(0.4B–14B)内部已用R²>0.99近乎完美地编码了计数，但该计数方向与lm_head数字token行几乎正交（|cos|≤0.032，与随机无异），形成几何读出瓶颈；仅改写9个数字行（36,864参数）可将受限预测从13.7%提到60.7–100%，而对注意力Q/V施加LoRA（7.67M参数）在真实自回归生成中达83.1%±7.2%，正确数字的词汇排名从55,980降到1。
TL;DR: On a 4,320-prompt synthetic counting benchmark plus natural-language counting (N=540), transformers (Pythia 0.4B/0.16B, Mistral-7B, Qwen3-8B/14B) fail counting not from missing representations but from a geometric readout bottleneck: linear probes recover counts at R²>0.99 yet count-encoding directions are nearly orthogonal (|cos|≤0.032) to digit lm_head rows. Rewriting only 9 digit rows (36,864 params) raises constrained next-token accuracy from a 13.7% baseline to 60.7–100.0% across four tasks, while a 7.67M-param LoRA on attention Q/V achieves 83.1%±7.2% in true greedy autoregressive generation (vs a 7.2% generation baseline) — the correct digit's vocabulary rank drops from 55,980 to 1 (50,000× improvement).

核心贡献

  • 将Transformer计数失败重新定义为几何读出瓶颈：模型在残差流中以R²>0.99编码了计数（最佳层R²=0.997），但该编码方向与lm_head数字token行几乎正交（|cos|≤0.032，均值0.016，与随机方向0.013±0.011无异，置换检验p=0.79），导致logit-lens在entity-mean位置峰值仅23.4%。
  • 给出瓶颈的训练动力学解释：梯度∇L将数字行推向E[h|y=digit]，而该条件期望被非计数上下文（日期、序号、列表项）主导，正交成为稳定不动点；3,000步算术微调不改变|cos|（0.0096，∆=+0.0009）且精度反降到18.0%，而计数专用微调将|cos|提升3.2×（0.0074→0.0280）、精度达51.5%——排除了"数字token稀有"的简单解释。
  • 提供最小因果探针：仅改写9个数字行（36,864参数，300步）在受限next-token上将entity counting从13.7%提到60.7%、char count 98.0%、addition 100.0%、list length 99.2%；但autoregressive生成下为0.0%，证明生成瓶颈还涉及上游路由。
  • 给出可部署修复：对全部36层注意力Q/V施加rank-16 LoRA（7.67M参数，200步）在真实贪心自回归生成中达83.1%±7.2%（5 seed，gap=0.000），entity-only任务94.5–97.0%；logit-lens显示正确数字中位词汇排名从55,980降到1（精度9.3%→71.8%）。
  • 划定作用边界：瓶颈在字符计数/加法/列表长度普遍存在，但MMLU、GSM8K无效应，DROP仅+8pp部分迁移；14B尺度正交锐化（|cos|=0.011，低于随机基线0.57×）但9行修复仍达90.3%±1.5。

Key Contributions

  • Reframes transformer counting failure as a geometric readout bottleneck: counts are encoded in the residual stream with R²>0.99 (best layer R²=0.997 at Qwen3-8B layer 3), but the encoding direction is nearly orthogonal to lm_head digit rows (|cos|≤0.032, mean 0.016, 95% CI [0.015,0.016]; random baseline 0.013±0.011; permutation test p=0.79), so logit-lens peaks at only 23.4% at the entity-mean position.
  • Provides a training-dynamics explanation (§5): the gradient pushes each digit row toward E[h|y=digit], which is dominated by non-counting contexts (dates, ordinals, list items), making orthogonality a stable fixed point (∂L/∂(W[t]·c)≈0). 3,000 steps of arithmetic fine-tuning leave |cos| at 0.0096 (∆=+0.0009) and drop accuracy to 18.0% (vs 24.5% baseline), whereas counting-specific fine-tuning raises |cos| 3.2× (0.0074→0.0280) with accuracy 51.5% — ruling out a digit-frequency explanation.
  • Delivers a minimal causal probe: rewriting only 9 digit rows (36,864 params, 300 steps) raises constrained next-token accuracy from a 13.7% baseline to 60.7% (entity counting), 98.0% (char count), 100.0% (addition), 99.2% (list length); but achieves 0.0% in autoregressive generation, proving the deployment bottleneck includes upstream routing.
  • Provides a deployable fix: rank-16 LoRA on attention Q/V across all 36 layers (7.67M params, 200 steps) achieves 83.1%±7.2% in true greedy autoregressive generation (5 seeds, gap=0.000; entity-only 94.5–97.0%); logit-lens shows the correct digit's median vocabulary rank drops from 55,980 to 1 (accuracy 9.3%→71.8%, 50,000× improvement).
  • Establishes scope boundaries: the bottleneck generalizes across character counting, addition, and list length, but is absent from MMLU and GSM8K and shows only +8 pp partial transfer on DROP; at 14B scale the orthogonality sharpens (|cos|=0.011, 0.57× the random baseline, sub-random) yet the 9-row repair still reaches 90.3%±1.5.

Methodology

Models (three families, distinct architectures/scales/corpora): Qwen3-8B (36 layers, 4096 hidden, GQA 32/8, RoPE), Mistral-7B-v0.1 (32 layers, 4096 hidden, GQA 32/8, RoPE, SwiGLU), Pythia-410M (24 layers, 1024 hidden, standard MHA, GELU); plus Qwen3-14B (40 layers, d=5120) and Pythia-160M for scale tests. All models use untied output heads, so orthogonality is a learned property rather than a tied-embedding artifact.

Benchmark: a full-factorial synthetic set — 6 counts × 3 distractors × 4 lengths × 3 spacings = 216 conditions × 20 samples = 4,320 prompts, counts drawn from {1,2,3,5,8,12}, 70/30 stratified split by difficulty. For DPS, single-digit counts {1,…,9}, 800 train / 300 test prompts. Natural-language extension: 8 entity categories (dogs, birds, flowers, tools, fruits, cars, books, instruments) × 8 templates (narrative, list, scene, conversation, recipe, news, checklist, observation), N=540 prompts, with 3 held-out entity types for cross-entity evaluation.

Probes: ridge regression on entity-mean residual activations, 5-fold CV for α. Logit-lens: project intermediate hidden states through final RMSNorm + lm_head, measure argmax over number tokens 1–20 at entity-mean and last-token positions across layers 0–35 (500 prompts subsampled). Geometry: |cos(w_ℓ, e_k)| between probe weights and number-token columns, with TOST equivalence testing and a permutation test against a random-direction baseline; replicated across ridge/LDA/mean-difference/PCA probes.

Interventions: (1) 9-row repair — fine-tune only the 9 digit-token rows (ids 16–24) of lm_head (36,864 params, 300 steps), with ridge or margin-hinge/fullvocab variants; (2) LoRA rank-16 on attention Q/V across all 36 layers (7.67M params, 200 steps); (3) Diagnostic Probe Steering (DPS) — inject the probe-predicted count into output logits (soft Gaussian, α amplitude, σ=0.5) or hard-clamp (+100 to the rounded digit). Controls: shuffled-label probe (R²=−0.042), random-direction DPS, shuffled-row (1/9 null), oracle. Seeds 42, 11, 77 (and 99, 123 for 5-seed generation); N=200×3 per protocol unless noted.

Results & Highlights

  • Probes decode counts at R²>0.99: Qwen3-8B layer-0 R²=0.977 (all), best layer-3 R²=0.997; per-layer R² stays ≥0.97 at every one of the 36 layers, while next-token digit accuracy is only 38.8% — the representation–output gap (Figure 2).
  • Geometric misalignment confirmed: |cos| between count-probe directions and lm_head digit columns is ≤0.032 at every layer (mean 0.016, 95% CI [0.015,0.016]), statistically equivalent to the 0.013±0.011 random baseline (permutation test p=0.79, TOST equivalence); robust across ridge/LDA/mean-diff/PCA probes (0.011–0.040).
  • 9-row repair (36,864 params) raises constrained digit accuracy from a 13.7% baseline to 60.7% (entity counting), 98.0% (char count), 100.0% (addition), 99.2% (list length); held-out 93.8% (Qwen3-8B), 92.0% (Mistral-7B), 31.4% (Pythia-410M, +21.4 pp); but 0.0% in unconstrained autoregressive generation.
  • LoRA Q/V rank-16 (7.67M params) achieves 83.1%±7.2% in true greedy autoregressive generation (5 seeds, gap=0.000; per-seed multi-task 71.5/89.0/86.5/81.0/87.5%, entity-only 97.0/96.5/94.5%) vs a 7.2% generation baseline; fullvocab next-token 91.7%±4.5%.
  • Logit-lens rank improvement: correct digit's median vocabulary rank drops from 55,980 to 1 post-LoRA (accuracy 9.3%→71.8%, 3 seeds); cross-task: char count rank 32,265→16, addition 21,186→1.
  • Vocabulary-competition ceiling: digit rows sit at the 12th–29th percentile of lm_head norms (µ_digit=1.455 vs µ_all=1.638); 84% of vocabulary tokens are geometrically "louder," digit tokens win argmax 0.0% of the time and appear in top-100 only 0.33%; norm rescaling to 2×/3× raises fullvocab 0%→26.5% then saturates.
  • Training-dynamics dissociation: arithmetic fine-tuning (3,000 steps, loss converged) leaves |cos|=0.0096 (∆=+0.0009) and drops accuracy to 18.0% (vs 24.5% baseline); counting fine-tuning raises |cos| 3.2× (0.0074→0.0280) with accuracy 51.5%.
  • 14B scale: orthogonality sharpens to |cos|=0.011 (0.57× the random baseline, sub-random); DPS-alone fails (10.8% vs 11.0% baseline) across a 10-layer sweep, but matched 9-row repair reaches 90.3%±1.5 (+65.8 pp) and row-repair-only 58.2% under mode-matched next-token.
  • Scope specificity: no bottleneck effect on MMLU or GSM8K; DROP shows only +8 pp partial transfer; natural-language counting (N=540) keeps |cos|=0.015 with baseline 88.7%, DPS 97.6%, probe-round 96.3%.

Critical Assessment

  • The 9-row repair is explicitly a diagnostic, not a deployable fix: it achieves 0.0% in unconstrained autoregressive generation (Table 2), so the headline "fix" requires the much larger 7.67M-param LoRA Q/V intervention, which still leaves ~17% generation error — the paper attributes much of the residual to format failures (83.5% of DPS generation errors are digit-not-first, not wrong-digit) but does not provide a deployable remedy for them.
  • The fullvocab repair plateaus at ~60% on entity counting (60.3%±2.8% optimized; 42.7% with margin-hinge), and the capacity ablation shows expanding 9→59 rows yields no improvement (67.5%±1.8%), nor does Adam vs ridge — indicating a task-level ceiling (the 150K-way argmax competition) that the paper diagnoses but does not resolve.
  • Interventions do not always compose at scale: at 14B, combined 9-row+DPS (54.8%) underperforms row-repair-only (58.2%) due to subspace interference, complicating the "just fix the readout" message.
  • Single-author preprint with no code release cited and no independent replication; the broad cross-model claims (Pythia-160M through Qwen3-14B) rest on one researcher's runs.
  • Scope is narrow by construction: the readout-bottleneck framework is shown to fire only for low-vocabulary aggregation tasks where the answer is pre-encoded in the input; MMLU/GSM8K show no effect and DROP only +8 pp, so generalization to broader token-level failures is unproven.

Strengths

  • Clean three-link causal story (representation R²>0.99 → orthogonality |cos|≤0.032 → output failure), each link confirmed by a falsifiable intervention with negative controls (shuffled-label probe R²=−0.042; shuffled-row 1/9 null; random-direction DPS at baseline).
  • The training-dynamics null-space argument (§5) is directly tested: arithmetic fine-tuning does not move |cos| while counting fine-tuning raises it 3.2× — a clean dissociation that rules out the obvious digit-frequency confound.
  • Minimal-intervention design (36,864 params) surgically localizes the bottleneck; the norm-rescaling control (0%→26.5% then saturation) cleanly separates norm routing from directional alignment.
  • Mode-matched evaluation (Table 5) holds prompts/tokenizer/position/argmax-scope fixed across all conditions, and the unified Table 2 reports every method under three standardized protocols — avoiding protocol-mixing artifacts.
  • Honest scoping: the paper repeatedly qualifies "localized to digit rows" as constrained-digit-decoding-only and explicitly reports when the 9-row repair fails (generation 0.0%).

Limitations

  • The deployable LoRA Q/V fix (83.1%) still fails ~17% of the time; residual errors are characterized as mostly format failures but no deployable remedy is given.
  • The framework is validated only on counting-adjacent low-vocabulary tasks; whether the geometric readout bottleneck explains other token-level failures (rare-word generation, code syntax, structured output) is not tested.
  • Entity-counting fullvocab repair is capped at ~60% and is shown to be a task-level ceiling, not an optimization artifact — left unresolved.
  • At 14B, DPS-alone fails and stacking interventions hurts (interference), so the cross-scale story is nuanced rather than clean.
  • No code release is cited; single-author preprint status limits independent verification of the many cross-model numbers.
  • The 9-row repair is format-specific: synthetic-trained rows degrade instruct-mode accuracy from 91% to 45.6% (seen)/43.0% (held-out), so a single repair does not transfer across prompt formats.
