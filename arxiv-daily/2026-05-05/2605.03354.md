> **一句话总结：** 本文通过追踪Qwen-3系列（0.6B–14B）在mem0和A-MEM两种记忆框架中的内部特征电路，发现路由/控制电路在0.6B即可因果活跃但内容提取电路需到4B才出现（"控制先于内容"），Write和Read共享一个已存在于基础模型中的晚期层锚定中枢（L34 hub），内容电路在4B可检测但直到8B才可靠可操控，且利用电路特征分离实现了76.2%准确率的无监督逐操作故障定位。

> **TL;DR:** Tracing feature circuits across Qwen-3 (0.6B–14B) and two memory frameworks (mem0, A-MEM), the authors discover that routing/controll circuitry is causally active at 0.6B while content-extraction circuitry produces no signal until 4B—a "control-before-content" asymmetry that creates a silent-failure deployment trap. Write and Read share a late-layer grounding hub (L34 at 8B) already present in the base model, but only memory framing recruits a functional steering direction on this substrate. Detection (4B) and reliable steerability (8B) have distinct scale thresholds. Feature-space separation between the two circuit groups enables 76.2% unsupervised per-operation failure localization.

## 核心贡献

- **"控制先于内容"的不对称性**：路由电路（Manage）在0.6B即有显著因果效应（gap=0.259），而内容电路（Write/Read）在4B之前无可检测信号（bootstrap CI包含零），揭示了一个小模型"能路由但无法提取/锚定"的静默失败部署陷阱。
- **Write-Read共享锚定中枢**：Write和Read操作在晚期层共享一个hub集群（8B时在L34约10个共现特征），该hub已存在于基础模型中；仅记忆框架的引导（framing）才能在此底座上招募功能性锚定方向，且hub可跨mem0和A-MEM迁移。
- **检测与可操控性阈值分离**：内容电路在4B可检测（causal gap=0.697）但直到8B才可靠可操控，14B时因计算分布化导致steerability反而下降。
- **无监督故障诊断**：利用控制组与内容组的特征空间分离，通过逐操作特征消融实现76.2%准确率的故障定位（Write 76/76, Manage 29/38, Read 59/79），优于所有行为基线（最佳black-box classifier仅63.4%）。

## Key Contributions

- **Control-before-content asymmetry**: Routing circuitry (Manage) produces a significant causal gap at 0.6B (0.259), while content circuitry (Write/Read) has no detectable signal until 4B—creating a deployment regime where small models route with apparent competence but silently fail at extraction and grounding.
- **Shared Write–Read grounding hub**: Write and Read share a late-layer hub cluster (~10 co-recurring features around L34 at 8B) already present in the base model. Memory framing (not direct context) recruits a functional grounding direction on this substrate. The hub transfers across mem0 and A-MEM frameworks.
- **Detection–steerability gap**: Content circuits become detectable at 4B (causal gap 0.697) but reliably steerable only at 8B. At 14B, distributed computation makes the circuit harder to intervene on despite remaining causal.
- **Unsupervised stage-level diagnostic**: Feature-space separation between control and content circuit groups enables per-operation failure localization at 76.2% accuracy via max-damage ablation, outperforming behavioral baselines (best black-box classifier: 63.4%) and providing mechanistic interpretability.

## Methodology

The authors trace feature circuits through each memory operation (Write, Manage, Read) using pre-trained linear transcoders (PLTs) from the mwhanna collection (d_feature=163,840, ReLU). A five-step pipeline is applied per operation per scale:

1. **Attribution**: Per-sample feature circuits via backward pass through a locally linear replacement model, retaining top 4,096 feature nodes, pruned to 80% node influence / 95% edge influence.
2. **Aggregation**: Cross-sample recurrence counting reduces each circuit to 50–200 recurrent features.
3. **Path tracing**: Canonical paths extracted by greedily following highest-weighted edges.
4. **Causal verification**: On a held-out 30% split, zero ablation and 5× amplification of top-10 circuit features are compared against matched random baselines. The primary metric is Δp (change in probability of the model's top prediction at the JSON prefix position).
5. **Cross-scale/cross-system comparison**: Jaccard similarity over top-30 features quantifies circuit sharing.

Models: Qwen-3 family (0.6B, 4B, 8B, 14B). Frameworks: mem0 (flat key-value store + semantic search) and A-MEM (Zettelkasten network). Evaluation dataset: LongMemEval (500 questions, 5 memory types). All circuits are traced on correct-only instances (filtered by Qwen-3 32B judge). 200 attribution graphs per operation per scale. ~160 GPU-hours total on NVIDIA H200s.

## Results & Highlights

- **Manage (routing)**: Causal at 0.6B (gap=0.259), peaks at 4B (0.417), weakens at 14B (0.048) due to distributed routing. Trunk-plus-routing architecture: semantic trunk (L13–18 at 8B) feeds into four-way action selection (add/update/delete/none).
- **Write (extraction)**: No signal at 0.6B. Three-stage pipeline at 8B: subject anchoring (L22) → word-specific extraction (L28) → category aggregation hub (L34). Peak causal gap at 4B (0.697), declining to 0.351 at 14B.
- **Read (grounding)**: No signal at 0.6B. Memory-position features account for 62% of attribution at 8B. Shared L34 hub with Write. Causal gap stable across 4B–14B (0.374–0.146).
- **Family divergence**: Write–Manage Jaccard drops from 0.071 (0.6B) to 0.000 (14B)—complete separation. Write–Read remains positive (0.091–0.154), confirming shared content primitive.
- **Cross-framework transfer**: L34 hub cluster (F145627) appears in both mem0 and A-MEM Write top-30. Read shows highest cross-framework overlap (Jaccard 0.277 at 8B).
- **Diagnostic**: 76.2% localization accuracy at 8B (Write 76/76 correct, Manage 29/38, Read 59/79). Dominant error: Manage–Read confusion (10.4%) due to adjacent layer ranges.
- **Corrective actions**: Diagnostic-conditional intervention improves fact recall by +5 pp and QA accuracy by +4 pp at 8B over unconditional intervention.

## Authors & Credibility

Xutao Mao and Cong Wang are at City University of Hong Kong; Jinman Zhao and Gerald Penn are at the University of Toronto. Gerald Penn is a well-established computational linguistics researcher. The experimental design is rigorous: five model scales, two memory frameworks, a standard benchmark (LongMemEval), 200 graphs per condition, bootstrap confidence intervals, and cross-benchmark validation (LoCoMo, MemoryAgentBench). Total compute is ~160 GPU-hours, suggesting the experiments are reproducible with moderate resources. The NeurIPS checklist is provided and claims are carefully scoped to the Qwen-3 family.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Genuinely high. This is the first circuit-level analysis of multi-call agent memory pipelines, moving beyond single-turn retrieval studies (ROME, ReDeEP, Retrieval Heads). The control-before-content finding and the detection–steerability distinction are new and actionable.
- **Rigor**: Very strong. Bootstrap CIs on all causal gaps, matched random baselines, held-out verification splits, replacement-model fidelity ≥97% top-1 agreement, cross-framework replication, and three-benchmark diagnostic validation. The confusion analysis (Table 18) honestly reports Manage–Read confusion as the dominant failure mode.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Qwen-3 models and PLTs are public; mem0 and A-MEM are open-source. Hyperparameters are fully specified (Table 9). The main limitation is that circuit tracing results can be sensitive to transcoder quality and pruning thresholds.
- **Significance**: High. The practical implication—don't deploy models below 4B for agent memory—is concrete and counterintuitive (small models appear to work because routing succeeds). The unsupervised diagnostic fills a real gap in agent debugging tooling.
- **Limitations honestly reported by authors**: Single model family (Qwen-3), MLP-only tracing (ignores attention pathways), short prompts (45–65 tokens), hub identification depends on transcoder quality. The 0.6B null results could reflect tracing limitations rather than true circuit absence.
- **Concerns**: (1) The diagnostic accuracy of 76.2% is on a 3-class problem with imbalanced ground truth (76 Write, 38 Manage, 79 Read), so a per-class analysis would be more informative. (2) The corrective intervention gains (+5 pp fact recall) are modest and only significant at 8B. (3) The paper does not test whether circuits transfer to non-Qwen architectures. (4) The 76.2% is on LongMemEval only; cross-benchmark results are mentioned but not quantified for the diagnostic.

## Strengths

- First mechanistic interpretability study of multi-call agent memory pipelines.
- Systematic cross-scale analysis (5 sizes) with careful causal verification methodology.
- Practical deployment guidance: concrete scale thresholds for each capability.
- Cross-framework validation (mem0 vs. A-MEM) demonstrates generality within the Qwen-3 family.
- The memory-vs-direct-context experiment (Section H) is a clean ablation showing the hub response is specific to memory framing, not generic in-context grounding.
- Honest about limitations: replacement model fidelity, single family coverage, MLP-only tracing.
- NeurIPS checklist provided with self-consistent claims.

## Limitations

- Single model family (Qwen-3); generalization to LLaMA, Gemma, Mistral, etc. is unknown.
- MLP-only tracing ignores attention circuitry, which likely also contributes to memory operations.
- Prompts are artificially short (45–65 tokens); real agent memory prompts are much longer with richer context.
- The 76.2% diagnostic accuracy, while best-in-class, is insufficient for autonomous production use without human oversight.
- At 14B, circuits become too distributed for effective intervention—the diagnostic value degrades at the scale where it's most needed.
- The hub's functional interpretation (context-grounding substrate) is supported by steering experiments but not by direct representational analysis (e.g., probing).
- Corrective intervention gains are modest and concentrated at 8B; at 0.6B and 14B the approach adds little value.

## Relevance

本文通过机械可解释性方法揭示LLM代理记忆系统的内部电路结构，与LLM隐藏状态与可解释性的研究兴趣高度匹配。控制-内容电路分离、尺度依赖的涌现阈值、以及晚期层锚定中枢的发现，直接贡献了对大模型内部信息处理机制的理解，同时无监督故障诊断为AI系统的可靠性评估提供了新工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the control/content circuit dichotomy and scale thresholds generalize to other architectures (LLaMA-4, Gemma 3, Mistral)?
- How does circuit topology change with longer, more realistic prompts (hundreds of tokens with multi-session history)?
- Can the grounding hub be explicitly reinforced via fine-tuning or continued pretraining to lower the steerability threshold below 8B?
- Does attention circuitry show a complementary control–content split, and does it interact with the MLP hub?
- How robust is the diagnostic under distribution shift (e.g., domain-specific agent tasks, multilingual memory)?
- Could the detection–steerability gap be used as a scaling-law predictor for when a model becomes safe to deploy for a given memory task?
