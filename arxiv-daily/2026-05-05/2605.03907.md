> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Prompt Steering Replacement (PSR) 框架，通过将 prompt steering 建模为 activation steering 并从中蒸馏出 token 级别的转向系数，在 persona steering 和 AxBench 上超越现有 activation steering 方法甚至 prompting 本身，但在复杂指令跟随（IFEval）上 rank-1 限制仍然明显。

> **TL;DR:** The authors show that standard activation steering methods apply uniform interventions across token positions, while prompt steering actually applies heterogeneous, token-specific interventions — strong on some tokens, negligible on others. They propose PSR (Prompt Steering Replacement) models that learn token-specific steering coefficients from activations via a single-layer ReLU probe, trained to minimize MSE between PSR and prompt-steered activations. A-PSR (all-layers) achieves SOTA on AxBench (0.871/2B, 1.120/9B) and outperforms prompting on persona steering. However, rank-1 PSR struggles with complex instruction types on IFEval where prompting remains stronger.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 prompt steering → activation steering 的统一分析框架，将 in-context learning 视为 LLM 自身实现的（不可解释的）activation steering
- 实证揭示现有 activation steering 方法（Const）对 prompt steering 的不忠实性：prompt 对不同 token 施加不同强度的干预，而 Const 方法使用统一系数
- 提出 PSR 模型族（S-PSR 单层、A-PSR 全层），通过 ReLU 探针从激活值估计 token 级别转向系数，支持 MSE 和 loglikelihood 两种训练目标
- A-PSR^MSE 在 AxBench 两个子集上创下新 SOTA，超越 prompting、LoRA、LoReFT 和 HyperSteer
- 在 persona steering 上，A-PSR^MSE 在 3 个 LLM 上全部超越 prompting

## Key Contributions

- A unified framework that formulates prompt steering as a form of activation steering, enabling principled analysis and distillation of prompt behavior into interpretable interventions
- Empirical demonstration that constant activation steering is unfaithful to prompt steering mechanics: prompt interventions vary dramatically across token positions (Figure 2), violating the uniform-coefficient assumption
- PSR model family (S-PSR single-layer, A-PSR all-layers) with token-specific steering coefficients estimated via a learned ReLU probe λ(A; θ), relaxing Assumption 3.2 to Assumption 3.2a
- Two training objectives: MSE (faithfulness to prompt-steered activations across layers) and loglikelihood (output-level matching), with regularization to prevent dead ReLU regions
- A-PSR^MSE sets new SOTA on AxBench: 0.871 (2B-L20) and 1.120 (9B-L20), outperforming prompting, LoRA, LoReFT, and HyperSteer
- A-PSR^MSE outperforms prompting on persona steering across all three tested LLMs (Llama-3.2-3B, Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen2.5-7B)

## Methodology

The paper formalizes activation steering as A_yi|AS = A_yi + ΔAS(x_{y≤i}, attr), and shows that prompt steering can be decomposed similarly: A^l_{y'_i}|PS = A^l_{y'_i} + Δ^PS(x'y'_{≤i}, xy'_{≤i}). Under Assumption 3.1 (interventions lie along a single direction z_attr) and the relaxed Assumption 3.2a (intervention magnitude is a function of activations, not constant), the PSR architecture is:

**A^l_{y'_i}|AS = A^l_{y'_i} + α · λ(A^l_{y'_i}; θ_attr,l) · z_attr,l**

Where λ is a single-layer ReLU probe: ReLU(A · w + b). The global coefficient α is set to the judge score J_attr during training and treated as a hyperparameter at inference. Two variants: **S-PSR** (single-layer intervention) and **A-PSR** (interventions at all layers with joint optimization).

Training uses prompt-steered triplets (x, x', y') filtered by quality judges (J_attr for attribute presence, J_coher for coherence ≥ 0.5). The MSE objective minimizes activation-level differences between PSR and prompt steering across the intervention layer and all subsequent layers, naturally discouraging harmful interventions when assumptions don't hold.

## Results & Highlights

**Persona Steering (Table 1):**
- S-PSR^MSE achieves 91.1 TA@C_p on Llama-3.2-3B vs. 79.3 for S-Const^MSE — a +11.8 point gain from token-specific coefficients alone
- A-PSR^MSE reaches 98.6-99.2 TA@C_p across models, outperforming prompting (91.5-95.7)
- At high coherence (TA@C80), A-PSR^MSE: 96.4 on Qwen2.5-7B vs. 71.6 for prompting

**AxBench (Table 3):**
- A-PSR^MSE: 0.871 (2B) and 1.120 (9B) — new SOTA, surpassing HyperSteer (0.742/1.091), LoReFT^RePS (0.805/0.757), and prompting (0.731/1.075)
- Even single-layer S-PSR^MSE (0.367/0.900) outperforms SAE baseline (0.178/0.322)

**IFEval (Table 2):**
- S-PSR^LL achieves 62.8% IF Acc. on Phi-3-mini (vs. 30.1% for Stolfo et al.) — a dramatic improvement over prior activation steering
- However, prompting remains competitive (72.5-85.7%), especially for complex instruction types requiring arguments
- Loglikelihood objective outperforms MSE on IFEval, suggesting rank-1 PSR cannot capture all prompt steering mechanics

**Faithfulness Analysis (Figure 3):**
- A-PSR^MSE achieves lower relative RMSE than equivalent prompts from layer 10 onwards, indicating highly faithful replication of prompt steering interventions
- Constant methods show RMSE spikes at intervention layers, suggesting the model partially reverts from unfaithful interventions in subsequent layers

## Authors & Credibility

Geert Heyman and Frederik Vandeputte are from Nokia Bell Labs, Belgium. The work is methodologically rigorous: three distinct benchmarks, multiple LLMs (Llama-3.2-3B, Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen2.5-7B, Gemma-2-2B, Gemma-2-9B, Phi-3-mini, Mistral-7B), and comprehensive ablations over architecture, objective, and intervention positions. Code is publicly available. The paper provides clear theoretical motivation (Assumptions 3.1, 3.2, 3.2a) and honest discussion of when assumptions break down.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The token-level faithfulness analysis of prompt steering vs. activation steering is genuinely insightful. The PSR formulation is elegant and well-motivated, not an arbitrary architecture choice.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Multi-benchmark, multi-model evaluation with both activation-level faithfulness metrics and downstream task metrics. The comparison to "equivalent prompts" as a faithfulness baseline is clever.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code is available, training pipeline is clearly described, and the method uses only standard components (ReLU probe, MSE loss).
- **Significance:** High for the representation engineering community. The insight that token-specific coefficients matter could shift how the field designs activation steering methods.
- **Red flags / honest concerns:**
  - The "outperforms prompting" claim on persona steering requires careful reading: A-PSR^MSE wins on TA@C_p, but prompting uses a fixed prompt while PSR is trained on prompt-steered data — this is more "distilling prompting" than "beating prompting."
  - On IFEval, rank-1 PSR clearly fails for complex instructions (the authors honestly acknowledge this), suggesting the framework's generality is limited by rank constraints.
  - The 2B AxBench results show A-PSR^MSE's improvements over prompting come with a "noticeable drop in answer relevance" — the steering is happening at the cost of responsiveness to the original query.
  - The MSE objective's superiority over loglikelihood is not universal (Table 3a shows loglikelihood winning in some settings), making the choice of objective somewhat ad hoc.
  - Training requires prompt-steered data with quality filtering — the method inherits any biases from the judge models (GPT-4.1-mini for coherence).

## Strengths

- Principled theoretical framework connecting prompt and activation steering via clear assumptions
- Strong empirical results: SOTA on AxBench, outperforming prompting on persona steering
- Token-specific steering coefficients are a simple but powerful idea that explains why constant methods fail
- Faithfulness analysis provides mechanistic insight beyond just downstream metrics
- Honest about limitations: the IFEval results and rank-1 constraints are discussed transparently

## Limitations

- Rank-1 interventions cannot capture all prompt steering behavior, especially for complex instructions with arguments (evident on IFEval)
- Training requires quality-filtered prompt-steered data and judge models, adding complexity
- The framework's Assumption 3.1 (single-direction interventions) is known to be approximate and doesn't hold universally
- Steering can reduce answer relevance (noted on AxBench 2B subset)
- Only evaluated on single-attribute steering; multi-attribute scenarios not tested
- The method introduces additional hyperparameters (layer selection, α tuning, regularization) compared to simple prompting

## Relevance

该工作对 LLM 推理时控制和表示工程方向有重要贡献。PSR 揭示了 activation steering 与 prompt steering 之间的关键差异（token 级别异质性），并提出了一套有理论动机的解决方案。对于研究 mechanistic interpretability、representation engineering 和 LLM alignment 的研究者，这篇论文提供了既有分析深度又有实用价值的方法。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can low-rank PSR (rank > 1) close the gap with prompting on complex instruction types like IFEval?
- How does PSR generalize to multi-attribute steering scenarios where attributes may interact?
- What do the learned probe weights (w_attr) actually capture — are they interpretable as concept directions?
- How sensitive is PSR to the choice of judge model for training data filtering?
- Could PSR be applied to safety-relevant steering (e.g., reducing harmful outputs) with the same effectiveness?
- Is the token-specific intervention pattern consistent across different prompts for the same attribute, or does it vary substantially?
