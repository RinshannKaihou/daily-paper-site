Where Paths Split: Localized, Calibrated Control of Moral Reasoning in Large Language Models

arXiv: 2605.03609

Date: 2026-05-05

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03609

一句话总结： 本文提出Convergent-Divergent Routing方法，在Transformer内部定位伦理推理通路中注意力头共享但FFN单元分叉的"分支点"，通过选择性门控实现二值控制，再结合CSP提取的成对判别方向和闭式Dual Logit Calibration，在残差流二维子空间中实现功利主义/义务论偏好的精细可校准调节（偏差通常<5pp），同时保持通用能力基本不降。
TL;DR: Proposes Convergent-Divergent Routing (CDR) to locate minimal "branch points" inside transformer blocks where attention heads are shared across utilitarian/deontological reasoning but FFN units diverge. Gating non-target paths at these loci yields binary moral control. Fine-grained control is achieved via CSP-extracted paired discriminative directions in the residual stream plus Dual Logit Calibration — a closed-form, minimum-ℓ₂-norm update aligning directional projections with user-specified preference weights (αU, αD) on the 1-simplex. On LLaMA-2-7B-Chat, Vicuna-7B, and Yi-1.5-6B-Chat, the method achieves MAE of ~2.1 pp on Llama (best among all baselines), preserves general capabilities (GSM8K, TriviaQA, WMT14), and outperforms Prompt-Only, Top-K Head, and BL-PRS baselines.

核心贡献

  • Convergent-Divergent Routing（趋同-分叉路由）： 通过线性探针识别对伦理框架有预测力的注意力头，利用FFN向量与伦理框架指示词的对齐分数识别框架特异FFN单元；当某层存在共享注意力头但FFN单元Jaccard重叠率低于阈值时，定义为"分支点"。
  • 选择性门控实现二值控制： 在分支点层，仅覆盖非目标框架的FFN单元激活为目标框架的激活值，保留大部分FFN单元不变，实现轻量级的伦理框架极化控制。
  • CSP提取成对判别方向： 将脑电信号处理中的Common Spatial Patterns方法适配到残差流，通过广义特征值问题在每层提取一对最大化类间方差比的方向（utilitarian vs. deontological），有效抑制共享方差。
  • Dual Logit Calibration（双对数校准）： 基于Lagrange乘子法的闭式最小ℓ₂范数更新，在span{u⁽ˡ⁾, d⁽ˡ⁾}二维子空间内调整残差，使方向投影的softmax与用户偏好权重(αU, αD)匹配，将无界标量系数替换为有界的1-simplex权重。
  • 在LLaMA-2-7B、Vicuna-7B、Yi-1.5-6B三个模型上验证，Llama上MAE仅2.1pp，优于Prompt-Only、Top-K Head Steering、BL-PRS三个基线；通用能力（GSM8K/TriviaQA/WMT14）基本保持。

Key Contributions

  • Convergent-Divergent Routing: Identifies "branch points" — layers where attention heads are shared between utilitarian and deontological reasoning (via ridge regression probes on ETHICS dataset) but FFN units diverge (via token-alignment scoring of FFN vectors with ethical framework indicator words). Only layers meeting both criteria (shared heads + low FFN Jaccard overlap) are selected for intervention.
  • Selective gating for binary control: At branch-point layers, overwrites FFN unit activations for the non-target framework with those from the target framework. Only ~15–25% of FFN units are framework-specific per layer, minimizing disruption. This alone boosts target-framework reasoning without explicit prompt conditioning.
  • CSP-based paired direction extraction: Adapts Common Spatial Patterns from EEG signal processing to the residual stream. Solves a generalized eigenvalue problem on class-specific covariance matrices (with Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage), whitening the background class for numerical stability. Produces sharper utilitarian/deontological separation than PCA (visually verified in Figure 3).
  • Dual Logit Calibration: A closed-form minimum-ℓ₂-norm update derived via Lagrange multipliers. Enforces softmax(s'_U, s'_D) = (α_U, α_D) by matching the logit difference log(α_D/α_U). Replaces unbounded scalar steering coefficients with bounded preference weights on the 1-simplex.
  • Strong experimental results: MAE of 2.1 pp on Llama (11 α_U levels), outperforms all baselines across both Llama and Vicuna. Validated on AITA (14,167+495 samples) and DailyDilemmas. General capabilities (GSM8K exact match, TriviaQA, WMT14 BLEU) largely preserved.

Methodology

The pipeline has three stages, all operating at inference time with no fine-tuning:

Stage 1 — Branch-point localization (CDR):

  • Train ridge regression probes on each attention head's output (from ETHICS deontology/utilitarianism subsets, ~18.2K and ~13.7K samples) using 2-fold cross-validated Spearman correlation. Select heads with correlation > γ_attn.
  • Score FFN vectors by their dot product with the output embedding of ethical framework indicator words ("deontology"/"utilitarianism"). Threshold at μ + γ_ffn·σ to identify framework-specific units.
  • Define branch points as layers where: (1) attention heads are shared across both frameworks (S_l = A_l^(U) ∩ A_l^(D) ≠ ∅), and (2) FFN unit Jaccard overlap is below threshold τ.

Stage 2 — Binary control via gating:

  • At each branch-point layer, compute the deviation from masking shared attention heads: Δ^(l) = (m_l − 1) ⊙ z · W_o^(l).
  • For non-target FFN units, overwrite their activations with the target-framework values, preserving all other units. This blocks downstream propagation of non-target signals.

Stage 3 — Fine-grained control (CSP + DLC):

  • Record post-FFN residual streams under binary control for both frameworks. Build class-specific feature matrices X_U, X_D.
  • Apply CSP: estimate shrinkage covariances, whiten S_D via Cholesky, solve eigenvalue problem on L^{-⊤}S_U L^{-1} to get paired directions (u^(l), d^(l)).
  • At each decoding step, compute the closed-form DLC update: Δh_t^(l)* = [(k⁻¹ log(α_D/α_U) − ⟨a^(l), h_t^(l)⟩) / ‖a^(l)‖₂²] · a^(l), where a^(l) = d^(l) − u^(l).

Evaluation metrics: Two complementary measures — U_ip (hard-label rate: proportion of generations explicitly naming only one framework) and U_op (token-level probability of "utilitarianism" vs. "deontology" at the "From a ___" decision point, following Santurkar et al., 2023). MAE computed across 11 α_U levels (0%–100% in 10% increments).

Results & Highlights

  • Binary control: All three vanilla backbones show a utilitarian prior (Ū_op: 62.5% Llama, 80.9% Vicuna, 58.1% Yi-1.5). Binary control reliably polarizes: Llama reaches 83.5%/19.6%, Yi-1.5 reaches 89.1%/15.8% for utilitarian/deontological poles.
  • Fine-grained calibration: On Llama, MAE = 2.1 pp across all 11 α_U levels, with deviations within ±4.75 pp. On Vicuna, MAE is larger (worst 13.68 pp at α_U=80%) but still best among baselines. Yi-1.5 achieves MAE of ~8.4 pp (Appendix G.1).
  • Baselines comparison: Prompt-Only is largely insensitive on Llama (little separability). Top-K Head over-responds asymmetrically. BL-PRS is consistently second-best but still outperformed by CDR. CSP outperforms cPCA and PLS-DA for direction extraction (Table 4).
  • Ablations: Single-layer steering (SLY-k) is competitive but worse than full multi-branch-point pipeline. Explicit-prompt representations (EPRM) and down-projection steering (DProj.) show systematic under-calibration. Gating alone (without DLC) already boosts target reasoning (blocking effect, Table 10).
  • General capabilities: GSM8K exact match, TriviaQA, and WMT14 BLEU scores are largely preserved. Noticeable drops only at extreme α_U → 0 on Vicuna and Yi-1.5. Coherence/fluency rated >4.0/5 by GPT-4o. TPOT overhead is minimal (~1.1–3.5 ms/token added, Table 17).
  • Additional validation: Tested on DailyDilemmas dataset and Utilitarianism vs. Justice pair — results qualitatively consistent. Human evaluation (Prolific, 100 samples per model) confirms perspective-justification consistency at 0.81 (Llama) and 0.85 (Vicuna) accuracy.
  • Monotonicity: Spearman ρ = 0.966 (Llama), 0.928 (Vicuna) with near-zero monotonicity violation rates, confirming smooth, predictable control.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The CSP adaptation to transformer residual streams is genuinely creative — importing a well-established EEG signal processing technique to isolate discriminative directions in LLM representations is non-obvious and well-motivated. The CDR formulation (shared heads + divergent FFN units = branch points) is a clean mechanistic hypothesis. The closed-form DLC derivation is elegant.
  • Rigor: Strong. Comprehensive experimental setup: 3 backbone models, 3+ datasets, 3 baselines, 3 ablation variants, 3 direction-extraction algorithm comparisons, human evaluation, monotonicity analysis, coherence assessment, computational cost analysis. The paper uses two complementary evaluation metrics (hard-label rate + token-level probability) rather than relying on LLM-as-judge (a known weakness of prior work like Kim et al., 2025).
  • Reproducibility: Good. Closed-form DLC requires no optimization. All hyperparameters are reported (Table 5). Code and data released. Single A100 GPU, ~38% memory with bfloat16. Random seed fixed at 42.
  • Significance: Meaningful contribution to inference-time alignment. The 1-simplex preference parameterization is a principled improvement over unbounded scalar steering. The interpretable branch-point identification advances mechanistic understanding of moral reasoning in transformers.
  • Red flags / concerns:
  • The evaluation relies on a constrained prompt format ("From a [framework] perspective...") that makes steering measurable but may overestimate real-world controllability. The binary-choice supplementary experiment (Table 16) helps but is limited.
  • Vicuna requires a special two-stage correction (binary pre-polarization before fine-grained control, Appendix G.2) to achieve reasonable calibration — this suggests the method may be fragile on models with strong priors or unusual internal representations.
  • The ETHICS benchmark itself has been criticized (Hancox-Li & Blili-Hamelin, 2024) for label quality and underspecified prompts. The authors acknowledge this but could engage more substantively.
  • The binary utilitarian/deontological framing, while justified for tractability, is a significant limitation that the authors themselves flag. Whether the approach extends to multi-way moral reasoning remains genuinely uncertain.
  • Attention head probing uses ETHICS (different task formats: acceptability judgment vs. pairwise comparison for deontology vs. utilitarianism), which introduces a task-format confound in the probe comparison across frameworks.

Strengths

  • Elegant mathematical framework: the Lagrange multiplier derivation for DLC is clean and the CSP adaptation is well-motivated.
  • Interpretable at multiple levels: branch-point layers, FFN unit subsets, and paired directions are all inspectable.
  • Minimal inference overhead: closed-form update at identified layers only; pre-computed directions loaded once.
  • Strong empirical performance with comprehensive baselines and ablations.
  • Does not require model fine-tuning or maintaining multiple model copies.
  • Honest about limitations (binary framing, Vicuna robustness issue, ETHICS benchmark concerns).

Limitations

  • Binary moral framework only (utilitarian vs. deontological; one supplementary experiment with justice). Extension to pluralistic moral values is acknowledged as future work.
  • Vicuna needs a special two-stage correction procedure — the method is not uniformly robust across model architectures.
  • The "From a [framework] perspective" prompt constrains evaluation to a narrow format; real-world moral reasoning is rarely this structured.
  • ETHICS probe labels may not reliably capture nuanced moral reasoning (acknowledged by authors citing Hancox-Li & Blili-Hamelin, 2024).
  • GQA architectures (e.g., Llama-3) untested — the head-level probing granularity may need rethinking for grouped-query attention.
  • Cultural/language robustness is untested; all experiments are in English with Western philosophical frameworks.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can CSP-based direction extraction scale to multi-class settings (e.g., 3–5 moral frameworks simultaneously)?
  • How do branch points shift when moving to larger models (13B, 70B) or GQA architectures?
  • Is the utilitarian prior observed in all three base models (Ū_op > 50%) an artifact of training data, and does prior strength predict calibration difficulty?
  • Could the DLC framework be combined with contrastive activation addition (CAA) or ReFT for hybrid steering?
  • What happens when the ethical framework prompt is removed entirely — does the steering still manifest in free-form responses?
  • How stable are the identified branch points across different prompt domains (moral vs. political vs. persona)?
