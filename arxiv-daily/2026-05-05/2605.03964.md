> **一句话总结：** 本文提出从预训练MACE模型的内部表示直接提取NTK和激活核作为主动学习采集信号，无需额外不确定性模块或集成模型，在T1x、RGD、PMechDB三个反应化学基准上分别减少38%能量误差和28%力误差所需数据量。

> **TL;DR:** The authors show that the latent space of a pretrained MACE model already encodes sufficient information for effective active learning of MLIPs, without needing uncertainty heads, Bayesian methods, or committee ensembles. Two kernel-based acquisition signals — a 1920-dim embedding-space NTK and a 256-dim activation kernel — consistently outperform SOAP, Morgan fingerprints, committee disagreement, and random acquisition on reactive chemistry benchmarks (T1x, RGD, PMechDB). NTK-LCMD reduces acquisition rounds by 38.1% for energy RMSE, 28.3% for force RMSE, 27.2% for energy MAE, and 8.3% for force MAE on average. Kernel diagnostics confirm that pretrained representations preserve both reaction-family structure and within-reaction variation, while residual-GP calibration shows pretrained kernels yield the best uncertainty estimates.

## 核心贡献

- 提出两种直接从预训练MACE模型导出的主动学习采集信号：基于节点嵌入参数的有限宽度NTK（1920维）和基于隐藏层标量激活的平均池化特征（256维）
- 证明预训练模型的隐空间几何结构与模型误差对齐，无需额外训练不确定性头、贝叶斯方法或集成委员会即可实现高效采集
- 在三个反应化学基准（T1x、RGD、PMechDB）上，NTK-LCMD和Activation-LCMD均持续优于随机采集、固定描述符基线和委员会分歧
- 通过核矩阵可视化和残差GP校准实验，从机制上解释了预训练表示为何有效：它们同时保留了反应家族间的粗粒度结构和反应路径上的细粒度变化
- 揭示了预训练模型集成委员会在主动学习中的失败模式：能量委员会改善能量但损害力预测，力委员会则反之，且Spearman相关性分析显示委员会分歧与真实误差相关性很弱

## Key Contributions

- Introduce two acquisition signals derived from a pretrained MACE potential: a 1920-dimensional finite-width energy NTK (computed w.r.t. node embedding parameters only) and a 256-dimensional activation kernel (from pooled scalar hidden states across two message-passing layers)
- Demonstrate that pretrained model representations alone suffice for effective active learning, eliminating the need for auxiliary uncertainty heads, Bayesian fine-tuning, or committee ensembles
- Show consistent improvement over random, SOAP, Morgan fingerprints, and committee disagreement across three reactive chemistry benchmarks with NTK-LCMD achieving average round reductions of 38.1% (E-RMSE), 28.3% (F-RMSE), 27.2% (E-MAE), 8.3% (F-MAE)
- Provide mechanistic interpretation via kernel geometry diagnostics: pretrained NTK preserves both coarse reaction-family block structure and fine-grained variation along reaction coordinates, unlike randomly initialized or fixed-descriptor kernels
- Identify failure modes of committee-based acquisition in pretrained settings: energy committees harm force learning and vice versa; Spearman correlations between committee std and actual error are weak and inconsistent

## Methodology

The paper studies **offline pool-based active learning** for MLIPs using a pretrained MACE model (SPICE-2 checkpoint, ωB97M-V/def2-TZVPD level). The pipeline operates in rounds: at each round, an acquisition rule selects B=5 structures from a fixed candidate pool, DFT labels are obtained, and the model is fine-tuned on the enlarged training set using Huber loss on energy and force with equal weights.

**Two model-based representations** are extracted from the pretrained MACE:

1. **Energy NTK features** (1920-dim): The gradient of the predicted energy w.r.t. the node embedding parameters W^emb ∈ R^{Z_max × c}. These parameters are species-indexed and geometry-free, but backpropagation through the full interaction stack makes the resulting gradient depend on each atom's local environment. This is much smaller than the full parameter gradient while retaining discriminative power.

2. **Activation features** (256-dim): The mean-pooled invariant scalar channels (L=0) from two MACE interaction layers, concatenated. Requires only a single forward pass — cheaper than NTK which needs a backward pass.

These are compared against **feature-based baselines**: SOAP descriptors (3696-dim, rcut=6Å, nmax=8, lmax=6, averaged over sites) and Morgan fingerprints (2048-bit, radius 3, Tanimoto kernel).

**Batch selection rules**: Posterior variance (PV, greedy GP-based) and LCMD (largest-cluster maximum-distance, favors dense under-covered regions). LCMD is preferred in the main experiments as PV can over-prioritize isolated outliers.

**Committee baselines**: M=3 MACE models initialized from the same pretrained checkpoint with diversity from independent data-order shuffling. Both energy and force disagreement scores are evaluated.

**Datasets**: T1x (Transition1x, NEB reaction pathways, 3 candidate pools of ~1.7k-5.7k structures), RGD (5k subset of OMol reactivity data), PMechDB (10k H/C/N/O subset of OMol). For T1x: 5 structures per round, multiple seeds. For RGD/PMechDB/T1x-Mixed: 50 initial labels, 20 rounds × 150 structures.

## Results & Highlights

- **T1x main result**: NTK-PV achieves best force AUC (3078 vs 6490 random) and best final force RMSE (40.0 vs 111.8 meV/Å); Activation-PV achieves best energy AUC (252.17 vs 648.33). Both substantially outperform all baselines.
- **Cross-dataset transfer (Table 3)**: NTK-LCMD improves over random on all 3 datasets for all 4 metrics, with strongest gains on T1x-Mixed (+83.3% E-RMSE, +50.0% F-RMSE). Activation-LCMD similarly transfers well.
- **Committee failure**: Committee-Energy is *worse* than random on PMechDB (-23.5% E-RMSE gain, meaning slower convergence). Committee shows unstable energy-force tradeoff across all benchmarks.
- **Residual-GP calibration (Table 2)**: Pretrained activation kernel achieves best Gaussian NLL (-1.679), ENCE (0.237), 90% coverage (0.880), and RMSE (45.44 meV). Randomly initialized kernels and descriptor kernels are substantially worse.
- **Kernel geometry**: Pretrained NTK preserves both reaction-family blocks and within-reaction variation; randomly initialized NTK is much more homogeneous; SOAP captures coarse structure but saturates within reactions.
- **Scratch vs pretrained**: Training from scratch yields qualitatively similar ordering of methods, but pretrained models achieve consistently lower absolute errors (e.g., random Energy AUC: 1211.58 scratch vs 648.33 pretrained).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: Moderate-to-high.** The idea of using model internal representations for active learning is not entirely new (Ouyang et al. 2024 did latent-space AL for HfO₂; Zaverkin et al. 2022 used finite-width NTKs for Gaussian Moment Networks), but the application to modern SO(3)-equivariant pretrained foundation models, the specific NTK parametrization (embedding-only), and the comprehensive mechanistic analysis are novel contributions.
- **Rigor: Strong.** The paper provides three complementary evaluations: learning curves, kernel geometry diagnostics, and residual-GP calibration. The committee baselines are carefully constructed with both shuffle and bootstrap variants. The scratch-vs-pretrained comparison isolates the effect of pretraining. Multiple random seeds and candidate pool sizes are used.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** The MACE architecture, training hyperparameters, and datasets are all specified in detail (Appendices A and B). The code uses a fork of the public mlip library. However, no explicit code release is mentioned.
- **Significance: High for the MLIP community.** If these results hold across architectures, this could significantly simplify active learning pipelines for MLIPs by removing the need for expensive ensemble training.
- **Red flags and limitations:**
  - The memory constraint (O(n²_P) kernel matrix) is a real practical limitation — the authors acknowledge this but don't offer a solution for pools > 10k.
  - Only one pretrained model family (MACE, SPICE-2) is tested. Transfer to other architectures (e.g., UMA, Orb, CHGNet) is left as future work.
  - The committee baseline uses only M=3 models — a larger ensemble might perform better, though the authors note that 3-5 is standard in the literature.
  - The residual-GP diagnostic uses fixed (non-fine-tuned) representations, which somewhat limits its interpretability as the model changes during AL.
  - Gains on RGD and PMechDB are more modest than on T1x, suggesting diminishing returns on harder or more diverse datasets.

## Strengths

- Eliminates the computational overhead of ensemble training (M full training runs per round) while achieving better acquisition performance
- Activation features require only a single forward pass — practically free compared to committee methods
- Comprehensive mechanistic analysis (kernel visualization, Spearman correlation, residual-GP calibration) that explains *why* pretrained representations work, not just *that* they work
- Honest discussion of limitations (memory scaling, single architecture tested)
- Careful experimental design with disjoint train/val/test/pool splits, multiple seeds, and multiple candidate pool sizes

## Limitations

- O(n²_P) kernel matrix memory constraint limits scalability to large candidate pools (>10k structures)
- Only tested with MACE architecture and SPICE-2 pretraining; generalization to other pretrained MLIPs is unverified
- NTK is restricted to embedding parameters only — a broader study of parameter subsets is acknowledged as future work
- Gains are more modest on harder datasets (RGD, PMechDB) than on T1x
- No comparison against newer uncertainty quantification methods (uncertainty heads, Bayesian MLIPs, multi-head ensembles)
- Force-aware model representations are not explored — current representations are energy-derived

## Relevance

该工作展示了预训练模型的隐空间几何结构可直接用于主动学习采集，为MLIPs及其他科学机器学习场景提供了无需额外训练的高效数据选择方案，与数据高效的模型适配和预训练表示利用密切相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do these kernel-based acquisition signals perform with other pretrained MLIP architectures (UMA, Orb-v3, CHGNet)?
- Can approximate kernel methods (e.g., random Fourier features, Nyström approximation) address the O(n²_P) memory bottleneck for pools with >100k candidates?
- Would force-aware NTK features (gradients w.r.t. force-related parameters) improve force-error acquisition further?
- How does the approach perform on non-reactive chemistry or materials science tasks where pool bias is less severe?
- Can the pretrained kernel representations be used for other purposes like training-set summarization, dataset distillation, or validation-set construction, as the authors briefly suggest?
- What happens when the pretrained model's training distribution is far from the target domain — do the representations still provide useful acquisition signals?
