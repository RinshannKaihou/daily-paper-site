Design and Implementation of BNN-Based Object Detection on FPGA

arXiv: 2605.03396

Date: 2026-05-05

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03396

一句话总结： 本文在低成本 PYNQ-Z2 FPGA 上用全 Verilog RTL 实现了 W1A8（1-bit 权重/8-bit 激活）YOLOv3-tiny-like 目标检测器，将 Mul_prev 逐通道补偿融合进 BNN PE，在 VOC 上达到 39.6% mAP50（0.098 GFLOPs / 0.74M 参数），RTL 与 ONNX 的最终输出相关系数 0.999964，端到端 1.26 FPS。
TL;DR: The authors deploy a W1A8 (1-bit weight, 8-bit activation) YOLOv3-tiny-like detector on a PYNQ-Z2 (XC7Z020) FPGA in handwritten Verilog RTL. On PASCAL VOC 2007 test, the model achieves 39.6% mAP50 with 0.098 GFLOPs and 0.74 M parameters — versus YOLOv5n at 67.8% mAP50 / 4.5 GFLOPs / 1.9 M params and YOLOv2-tiny at 35.4% mAP50 / 7.0 GFLOPs / 15.86 M params. RTL-vs-ONNX layer-wise verification gives a final detection-head correlation of 0.999964 and MAE 0.020027. On-chip implementation closes timing at 30 MHz, using 73% BRAM and 91% DSP, with 795.58 ms latency per frame (1.26 FPS) and 2.047 W total on-chip power.

核心贡献

  • 在低成本 PYNQ-Z2（XC7Z020）FPGA 上完成 W1A8 YOLOv3-tiny-like 检测器的全 Verilog RTL 实现（非 HLS），覆盖 padding、行缓冲、二值卷积、量化后处理、max pooling 与检测头。
  • 将 Mul_prev（按输入通道索引的缩放）融合进 W1A8 PE 的累加过程，实现逐输入通道补偿；Div_current（按输出通道索引）在 post-processing 处理，避免单一缩放忽略通道贡献差异引入误差。
  • 给出从训练好的 ONNX 模型到 FPGA 部署的完整流程：参数提取、定点转换（Conv1 Q5.11/Q2.14；Conv2–Conv10 1-bit W/8-bit A；Conv11 Q1.15/Q4.12）、COE 生成、BRAM ROM 部署。
  • RTL 与 ONNX Runtime 逐层比对：Conv1 前激活 corr=0.999999，Conv11 检测头 raw 输出 corr=0.999964、MAE=0.020027。
  • 在 VOC 上以 0.098 GFLOPs / 0.74M 参数取得 39.6% mAP50，比 YOLOv2-tiny 高 4.2 个百分点，计算量仅约为 YOLOv5n 的 1/45.9。

Key Contributions

  • Full handwritten Verilog RTL implementation (not HLS) of a W1A8 YOLOv3-tiny-like detector on PYNQ-Z2 (XC7Z020), including padding adapter, LineBuffer_3x3, W1A8 convolution PE, standard convolution PE, post-processing, MaxPool, and Conv11 detection head (PE_NUM=5).
  • Fuses Mul_prev (input-channel-indexed scale) into the W1A8 PE accumulation (y_o = Σ s_{o,i}(m_i·a_i), Eq. 3-4) so channel-wise compensation is applied during accumulation; Div_current (output-channel-indexed) is handled in post-processing. This split avoids the error a single merged scale would introduce.
  • Complete ONNX-to-FPGA deployment flow: parameter extraction (weights, biases, Div/Mul tensors), fixed-point conversion, COE packing in ROM address order, BRAM ROM initialization with a state machine scheduled to actual ROM read latency (Vivado BRAM ROM without Primitives Output Register).
  • Layer-wise RTL-vs-ONNX numerical verification: Conv1 pre-activation corr=0.999999 (max abs 0.132158, mean abs 0.001345); Conv1 post-quantization 98.81% of outputs within 1 LSB; Conv2 post-quantization 98.76% within 1 LSB; Conv11 detection-head raw output corr=0.999964, MAE=0.020027.
  • Accuracy-complexity trade-off on PASCAL VOC 2007 test: 39.6% mAP50 at 0.098 GFLOPs / 0.74 M params, +4.2 pp over YOLOv2-tiny (35.4% mAP50) at ~1/71.4 the compute and ~1/21.4 the params.

Methodology

Network structure (Table 1). Input 320×320×3 RGB; output 10×10×75 tensor in y/x/channel order. Eleven main convolutions Conv1–Conv11. Conv2–Conv10 use W1A8 (1-bit weights, 8-bit activations); Conv1 (3→16, 3×3) and Conv11 (64→75, 1×1) use fixed-point standard convolution to reduce input/output-side errors. MaxPool after Conv1/2/3/4/7. Channel progression: 3→16→32→64→128→128→128→128→128→64→64→75.

W1A8 quantization. Weights binarized via w_b=sign(w)∈{−1,+1} (Eq. 3-1); real-valued weights kept in training with straight-through estimator for the sign gradient. Inference stores only binary weights → 1/32 of 32-bit storage. W1A8 convolution replaces multiplication with sign-controlled add/sub: y_o=Σ s_{o,i}·a_i, s_{o,i}∈{−1,+1}, a_i∈[0,255] (Eq. 3-2). Activations quantized via learned step-size quantization (LSQ): q_a=clip(round(x/s_a),0,255) (Eq. 3-3), step size s_a learned during training, fixed-point at inference.

Channel-wise scale fusion. Because Mul_prev is indexed by input channel and Div_current by output channel, the fused accumulation is y_o=Σ s_{o,i}(m_i·a_i) (Eq. 3-4), with m_i fixed-point and applied inside the PE; Div_current applied in post-processing (scaling, bias correction, rounding, clipping).

Fixed-point formats (Table 3). Conv1: W Q5.11, B Q2.14. Conv2–Conv10: 1-bit sign-packed W, 8-bit A. Conv11: W Q1.15, B Q4.12, output signed Q*.15 (recovered as signed_int32/2^15). All parameters packed as COE files in ROM address order into BRAM ROMs.

Hardware datapath (Figure 1, Table 4). Top module Yolo3-tiny_Top, streaming style with valid/ready backpressure. RGB adapter → Padding Adapter → LineBuffer_3x3 → W1A8/Standard PE → post-process → optional MaxPool → Conv11 (PE_NUM=5, serializes 10×10×75). ROM state machine schedules address issue / data latch / compute start to actual ROM read latency.

Verification. Vivado simulation testbench with 320×320 RGB input; ONNX Runtime as software reference; RTL outputs converted to float per Q-format and compared layer-by-layer.

Implementation target. Xilinx XC7Z020 on PYNQ-Z2; synthesized/implemented in Vivado; 4.9 Mb BRAM budget.

Results & Highlights

  • Detection accuracy (Table 5, VOC 2007 test): 39.6% mAP50 at 0.098 GFLOPs and 0.74 M params (320×320 input). This is +4.2 pp over YOLOv2-tiny (35.4% mAP50, 7.0 GFLOPs, 15.86 M params) but below YOLOv5n (67.8%, 4.5 GFLOPs, 1.9 M), YOLOv5s (68.0%, 2.3 GFLOPs, 5.8 M), YOLOv4-tiny (59.2%, 6.9 GFLOPs, 6.0 M), and MobileNetSSD (74.4%, 16.5 GFLOPs, 7.2 M). Compute is ~1/45.9 of YOLOv5n and params ~1/2.6 of YOLOv5n.
  • RTL-vs-ONNX Conv1 pre-activation (Table 6): max abs error 0.132158, mean abs error 0.001345, correlation 0.999999.
  • RTL-vs-ONNX Conv1 post-quantization: 98.8094% of outputs within 1 LSB (max abs 4, mean abs 0.121686).
  • RTL-vs-ONNX Conv2 post-quantization: 98.7648% within 1 LSB (max abs 6, mean abs 0.231006).
  • RTL-vs-ONNX Conv11 detection-head raw output: max abs 0.10889649, mean abs 0.020027, correlation 0.999964.
  • FPGA resource utilization (Table 7, XC7Z020): LUT 61%, LUTRAM 32%, FF 14%, BRAM 73%, DSP 91%, IO 13%, BUFG 16%, MMCM 25%. BRAM (parameter ROMs, line buffers, intermediate buffers) and DSP (Conv1, Conv11, fixed-point scaling) are the tightest resources.
  • On-chip performance (Table 7): Maximum clock frequency 30 MHz (post-implementation timing); 23.79 M cycles/frame; PL inference latency 793.17 ms; PS processing latency 2.41 ms; end-to-end latency 795.58 ms/frame; throughput 1.26 FPS.
  • Power (Table 7): Total on-chip power 2.047 W (dynamic 1.881 W = 92%, static 0.166 W = 8%); junction temperature 48.6 °C.

Critical Assessment

  • Low accuracy for a meaningful detection budget: 39.6% mAP50 on VOC 2007 is below YOLOv2-tiny's 35.4% only by 4.2 pp, but YOLOv2-tiny itself is an early/weaker baseline; modern lightweight detectors (YOLOv5n 67.8%, YOLOv4-tiny 59.2%) are far ahead. The paper acknowledges "sacrifices part of the detection accuracy for extremely low computational cost," but the single 10×10 detection head "limits small-object localization and recall" — a structural ceiling the W1A8 quantization alone cannot overcome.
  • Throughput is not real-time: 1.26 FPS at 30 MHz is far below real-time (typically ≥30 FPS). The authors attribute this to "30 MHz timing closure, PE parallelism, the detection-head critical path, and inter-layer scheduling" and defer fixes to "future optimization passes," so the current implementation is a feasibility demonstration rather than a deployable detector.
  • DSP is nearly exhausted (91%): Although W1A8 is meant to reduce DSP pressure in the main body, the fixed-point Conv1/Conv11 and post-processing scaling still consume 91% of DSPs — leaving almost no headroom and indicating the standard-convolution layers, not the BNN body, dominate the DSP budget.
  • Timing closure at only 30 MHz: The XC7Z020 can typically clock higher; 30 MHz suggests the detection-head critical path and inter-layer scheduling are poorly pipelined, which the authors acknowledge but do not resolve.
  • Single-dataset, single-image evaluation: Only VOC 2007 test is reported; no COCO, no mAP@[0.5:0.95], and the detection visualization (Figure 3) uses a limited set. The RTL-vs-ONNX verification uses one 320×320 sample, so numerical consistency is not characterized across the input distribution.
  • No comparison to prior BNN-FPGA accelerators: Despite citing FINN/FINN-R and other BNN-FPGA work, the paper does not benchmark against them on resource/throughput/accuracy, so the relative merit of the fused Mul_prev PE is unestablished.

Strengths

  • Complete end-to-end handwritten RTL flow (ONNX → fixed-point → COE → BRAM ROM → Verilog), avoiding HLS abstractions and exposing bit widths, ROM latency, and per-channel scaling.
  • The Mul_prev fusion into the W1A8 PE is a principled fix to a real quantization-accuracy issue (input-channel vs. output-channel scale indexing), and the split between PE-fused Mul_prev and post-process Div_current is well-motivated.
  • Rigorous layer-by-layer RTL-vs-ONNX numerical verification (correlation ≥0.999964 at the detection head, ≥98.76% within 1 LSB at post-quantization) establishes the hardware faithfully implements the trained model.
  • Full on-chip implementation results (resource utilization, power, timing, FPS) are reported, not just simulation.
  • Extreme compute/param efficiency (0.098 GFLOPs, 0.74 M params) for the accuracy level, demonstrating the W1A8 cost-reduction benefit on a constrained FPGA.

Limitations

  • 39.6% mAP50 is low; the single 10×10 detection head structurally limits small-object recall.
  • 1.26 FPS is far from real-time; 30 MHz timing closure is the bottleneck.
  • DSP at 91% leaves almost no headroom; the standard-convolution layers dominate DSP usage rather than the BNN body.
  • Evaluation limited to VOC 2007; no COCO, no mAP@[0.5:0.95], RTL-vs-ONNX checked on a single sample.
  • No head-to-head comparison with FINN/FINN-R or other BNN-FPGA accelerators on the same metrics.
  • No code or RTL release stated; reproduction requires re-implementing the full Verilog datapath.
  • The paper does not measure accuracy degradation attributable specifically to W1A8 vs. the full-precision model (no FP32 baseline mAP reported).

Relevance

本文研究 W1A8（1-bit 权重/8-bit 激活）量化在 FPGA 上的部署及其与 ONNX 软件参考的数值一致性验证，直接对应「Computation Reliability & Precision Errors」方向——低精度量化引入的误差、定点转换的 LSB 偏差、以及硬件实现与软件模型的相关性验证正是该方向关注的内容；同时与「LLM Inference Evaluation」中量化推理的保真度评估有方法论上的共通性。

  • Can a multi-scale detection head or feature-fusion structure (as the authors suggest for future work) recover small-object recall without breaking the BRAM/DSP budget?
  • How does the 2.047 W / 1.26 FPS point compare in energy-per-frame to a GPU or embedded NPU running a comparable-accuracy detector?
