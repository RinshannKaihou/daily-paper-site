> **一句话总结：** EQUITRIAGE对五个主流LLM在急诊分诊任务上进行反事实性别公平性审计，发现所有模型均存在系统性性别敏感性（翻转率9.9%–43.8%），其中DeepSeek-V3.1和Gemini-3-Flash表现出方向性女性低分诊（F/M比最高达2.15:1），且CoT提示反而恶化了所有模型的准确性和公平性。

> **TL;DR:** EQUITRIAGE audits five LLMs (Gemini-3-Flash, Nemotron-3-Super, DeepSeek-V3.1, Mistral-Small-3.2, GPT-4.1-Nano) for gender bias in Emergency Severity Index (ESI) triage assignment using 374,275 evaluations on 18,714 MIMIC-IV-ED vignettes with counterfactual gender swaps. All five models exceed a 5% flip-rate threshold (9.9%–43.8%). Two show directional female undertriage (DeepSeek F/M 2.15:1, Gemini 1.34:1). A name-vs-gender ablation reveals opposite underlying mechanisms: Gemini's bias is emergent from the name+gender interaction while DeepSeek's is driven by the gender token alone, with age as a residual channel. Demographic blinding reduces Gemini's flip rate to 0.5% (near stochastic floor) but is heterogeneous across models. Chain-of-thought universally degrades accuracy and amplifies bias in 4/5 models.

## 核心贡献

- 对五个来自不同供应商的LLM进行了急诊分诊（ESI分级）的系统性反事实性别公平性审计，共374,275次评估，是目前该领域最大规模的多模型审计之一
- 识别出三种不同的偏见模式：方向性女性低分诊（Profile A）、近平衡（Profile B）、高翻转率伴弱男性方向偏差（Profile C），揭示了聚合公平性指标的不足
- 通过姓名-vs-性别消融实验发现两个Profile A模型（Gemini和DeepSeek）虽然表现出相同方向的女性低分诊，但底层机制截然相反：Gemini的偏差来自姓名+性别的交互效应，DeepSeek的偏差由性别标记单独驱动
- 发现所有模型的CoT提示均降低了准确性（κw下降0.13–0.59），且4/5模型的翻转率上升，证明通用"逐步思考"提示在临床分诊中不安全
- 揭示了一个Chouldechova-style分离现象：DeepSeek-V3.1同时具有最强的方向性反事实偏差（F/M 2.15）和最低的组内校准差距（0.013），说明组级公平性、反事实不变性和校准是独立属性

## Key Contributions

- First systematic five-model counterfactual fairness audit of LLM-based emergency department triage, spanning 374,275 evaluations across five vendors (Google, NVIDIA, DeepSeek, Mistral AI, OpenAI) and four prompt strategies
- Identifies three qualitatively distinct bias profiles that would be indistinguishable using aggregate fairness metrics alone (DPD, calibration gap)
- Conducts a two-model name-vs-gender ablation on both Profile-A models (Gemini-3-Flash, DeepSeek-V3.1), revealing that the same directional female-undertriage phenotype arises from opposite mechanisms: emergent interaction (Gemini) vs. token-driven (DeepSeek)
- Demonstrates that chain-of-thought prompting universally degrades human-concordance κw (by 0.13 to 0.59) and amplifies counterfactual flip rates for 4/5 models, a domain-specific failure mode contradicting prior CoT bias-reduction findings
- Documents a Chouldechova-style dissociation: DeepSeek-V3.1 has the strongest counterfactual directional bias (F/M 2.15) yet the joint-lowest outcome-linked calibration gap (0.013), proving that within-group calibration and between-pair invariance are independent fairness properties
- Shows debiasing intervention effectiveness is model-dependent: demographic blinding nearly eliminates Gemini's flip rate (0.5%) but increases Nemotron's (43.8% → 63.5%)

## Methodology

**Data source:** 18,714 clinical vignettes derived from MIMIC-IV-ED (v2.2) + MIMIC-IV (v3.1), covering ~425K ED stays at Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center (2011–2019). Inclusion: age ≥ 18, valid ESI (1–5), non-missing chief complaint. Exclusion: obstetric presentations, LWBS dispositions. Final cohort: N = 407,428 stays.

**Vignette construction:** Structured templates combining real de-identified fields (gender, age, race, vitals, chief complaint, medications) with synthetic first names sampled from gender- and race-concordant SSA/Census pools. 9,368 originals, of which 9,346 paired with gender-swapped counterfactuals; 22 sex-linked vignettes excluded from paired analyses.

**Counterfactual design:** Following Kusner et al. (2017), each original vignette is paired with a gender-swapped version that changes only the gender token, pronouns, and name (drawn from the opposite-gender, same-race pool). All clinical content held constant. Race is preserved across pairs.

**Models:** Gemini-3-Flash, Nemotron-3-Super, DeepSeek-V3.1 (Ollama Cloud); Mistral-Small-3.2, GPT-4.1-Nano (OpenRouter API). All at temperature = 0, max 1024 tokens.

**Four prompt strategies:** (1) Baseline — standard ESI assignment; (2) Chain-of-thought — step-by-step clinical reasoning; (3) Debiased — fairness-aware instructions; (4) Blind — removes name, age, gender; neutralizes pronouns.

**Fairness metrics (pre-registered):** Counterfactual flip rate (threshold: >5% = systematic), directional F/M undertriage ratio, Demographic Parity Difference, Equalized Odds Gap, Calibration Gap against MIMIC-IV hospital admission. Confidence intervals via bootstrap (10,000 iterations). Between-model comparisons via McNemar's test with Bonferroni correction.

**Ablation:** Three additional conditions for Gemini-3-Flash and DeepSeek-V3.1: gender-only swap, name-only swap, age-preserving blind (removes name+gender but retains age). Test-retest control: 500 vignettes evaluated twice to estimate stochastic noise floor.

## Results & Highlights

- **Flip rates (baseline):** All models exceed 5% threshold: GPT-4.1-Nano 9.9%, Gemini-3-Flash 11.7%, DeepSeek-V3.1 12.0%, Mistral-Small-3.2 12.7%, Nemotron-3-Super 43.8%
- **Directional bias:** DeepSeek-V3.1 F/M 2.15 (CI [1.90, 2.44]), Gemini-3-Flash F/M 1.34 (CI [1.19, 1.52]) — female undertriage; GPT-4.1-Nano 1.11 and Mistral 0.92 near parity; Nemotron 0.92 weak male-direction
- **Chest pain worst category:** DeepSeek chest pain F/M 9.10:1, Gemini 4.83:1 — aligning with clinical evidence of female cardiac undertriage
- **Age gradient inversion:** LLM bias increases with patient age (Gemini F/M: 0.91 at 18–44 → 2.13 at 65+), opposite to the human clinical pattern where young women face the largest disparities
- **CoT universally harmful:** κw drops for all models (Gemini 0.598→0.006, collapsed to 93% ESI-1); flip rates increase for 4/5 models (DeepSeek 12%→57.5%)
- **Blind strategy effectiveness:** Gemini flip rate 11.7%→0.5% (near 0.4% stochastic floor), κw loss only −0.014; GPT-4.1-Nano 9.9%→4.8%; DeepSeek flip rate unchanged but F/M shifts from 2.15 to 1.00
- **Ablation mechanism dissociation:** Gemini: gender-only F/M 1.00, name-only 0.82, combined 1.34 (emergent interaction); DeepSeek: gender-only 1.57, name-only 1.02, combined 2.15 (token-driven). Age-preserving blind leaves DeepSeek residual F/M 1.25, implicating age as residual channel
- **Calibration gap:** All models ≤ 0.026 against MIMIC-IV admission, with DeepSeek at 0.013 despite strongest counterfactual bias — demonstrating calibration and counterfactual invariance are independent
- **Outcome linkage:** 99.999% completion rate (5 parse failures across 374,280 calls)

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** First five-model counterfactual fairness audit of LLM-based ED triage with between-model statistical tests and a mechanistic ablation. The name-vs-gender decomposition across two models revealing opposite mechanisms for the same phenotype is genuinely novel. The Chouldechova-style dissociation documentation (counterfactual bias coexisting with calibration) is an important conceptual contribution.

**Rigor (High):** Pre-registered hypotheses and thresholds, 10,000-iteration bootstrap CIs, Bonferroni-corrected pairwise tests, test-retest stochastic baseline, race-concordant name pools to avoid confounded swaps. 99.999% completion rate. The ablation design with three orthogonal conditions is well-structured.

**Reproducibility (Moderate-High):** Uses publicly available MIMIC-IV-ED data. Code availability promised upon publication. However, two different inference backends (Ollama Cloud, OpenRouter API) introduce a potential confound. The test-retest was run on OpenRouter while the main Gemini results used Ollama Cloud — cross-backend noise estimates are acknowledged but not fully resolved. Model versions are specified but serving-stack differences may limit exact reproduction.

**Significance (High):** Directly relevant to the emerging regulatory landscape (FDA AI/ML SaMD, WHO AI health guidance). The finding that CoT universally degrades clinical triage — contradicting prior general-domain findings — has immediate practical implications. The model-dependent debiasing response argues strongly against one-size-fits-all fairness interventions.

**Potential concerns:**
- The Blind strategy conflates age removal with demographic removal; while the age-preserving blind ablation partially addresses this, the primary results table reports the conflated condition
- Ground-truth ESI labels themselves contain human gender bias, making accuracy metrics (κw) measured against an imperfect standard
- Single-center data (BIDMC, Boston) limits generalizability to community EDs
- Nemotron's 43.8% flip rate is so far above other models that it may reflect a fundamentally broken ESI understanding rather than meaningful "bias" per se
- The stratified sampling (balanced across ESI levels) differs from real ED prevalence, so results cannot be directly projected to deployment impact without prevalence reweighting
- No race-counterfactual analysis (acknowledged as planned follow-up)

## Strengths

- Comprehensive scale: five models × four strategies × 18,714 vignettes = 374,275 evaluations with near-perfect completion
- Rigorous pre-registered design with transparent deviation reporting
- Mechanistic ablation that decomposes the combined counterfactual into name and gender components, revealing opposite mechanisms across models
- Intersectional analysis by race × gender and age × gender, surfacing the age-gradient inversion finding
- Outcome-linked calibration against actual hospital admission (not just agreement with human ESI)
- Honest reporting of negative results (CoT failure, heterogeneous debiasing effectiveness, paradoxical fairness-instruction effects)
- The three-profile taxonomy (directional, near-parity, high-flip) provides a useful conceptual framework for future audits

## Limitations

- Single-center dataset (BIDMC, academic medical center in Boston); may not generalize to community EDs or international settings
- Structured vignettes omit non-verbal cues, patient affect, general appearance, and EMS reports that real triage nurses integrate — strengthens internal validity but limits ecological validity
- Ground-truth ESI labels inherit human gender bias; accuracy metrics measure agreement with a biased standard
- Ablation only conducted on two models (Gemini and DeepSeek); mechanism generality to remaining three models is unknown
- Blind strategy removes age alongside name and gender, conflating the effects; the age-preserving blind ablation helps but is reported separately from the main results table
- Two different inference backends (Ollama Cloud and OpenRouter) introduce a potential confound; test-retest control was cross-backend
- No race-counterfactual condition; race is preserved across gender swaps but not independently manipulated
- Stratified sampling distorts ESI prevalence relative to real ED populations, preventing direct deployment-impact estimation

## Relevance

这项工作对关注LLM安全部署和公平性评估的研究者具有高相关性。它提供了一个可复用的反事实审计框架，适用于任何将LLM用于高风险分类决策的场景，特别是在医疗AI领域。姓名-vs-性别消融实验揭示的"相同偏见表型、不同底层机制"发现，对设计通用的去偏见策略有重要启示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Would reasoning-native models (o1, o3, DeepSeek-R1) show different bias profiles, or does the CoT failure extend to architectures designed for step-by-step reasoning?
2. How do the three bias profiles map onto model architecture characteristics (MoE vs. standard, parameter count, training data composition)?
3. Can the name-vs-gender ablation framework predict which debiasing strategy will work for a given model before running the full audit?
4. Does the age-gradient inversion (older women more undertriaged by LLMs, opposite to human clinical patterns) replicate in multi-center datasets or non-US populations?
5. What happens when race-counterfactual swaps are combined with gender-counterfactual swaps — do intersectional effects amplify, cancel, or create new bias directions?
