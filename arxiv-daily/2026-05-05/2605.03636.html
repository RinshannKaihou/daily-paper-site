Information Plane Analysis of Binary Neural Networks

arXiv: 2605.03636

Date: 2026-05-05

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03636

一句话总结： 本文在二值神经网络（BNN）上进行信息平面（IP）分析，刻画了 plug-in 熵估计的有限样本可靠区间，并通过 375 个训练好的 BNN 表明：虽然训练后期压缩普遍存在，但压缩表示与泛化性能之间并不一致相关，二者关系高度依赖任务、架构和正则化。
TL;DR: The authors perform information plane (IP) analysis on binary neural networks (BNNs), where discrete activations make mutual information (MI) finite and the plug-in estimator applicable. They characterize the finite-sample regime (sample size N vs. representation dimension D) under which MI estimates are reliable and show that outside it estimates saturate to log2 N. Across 375 trained BNNs on SZT, MNIST, FashionMNIST, and CIFAR-10, late-stage compression is frequently observed (compression factor ϱ ≥ 25% in a large majority of experiments), but Spearman rank correlations between compressed representations and validation accuracy span both significant positive (e.g., rs=0.857 on FashionMNIST Bottleneck, p<0.001) and significant negative (e.g., rs=−0.761 on MNIST Raj-like, p<0.001) values, refuting a consistent compression–generalization link.

核心贡献

  • 在二值神经网络（BNN）上开展信息平面分析，利用离散激活使互信息有限、plug-in 估计适用，从而规避了确定性连续网络中 MI 无穷大、IP 仅呈现几何图像的根本困难。
  • 刻画了 plug-in 熵估计的有限样本行为：当样本数 N 相对字母表大小 k 不足（N=o(k²/log² k)）时，估计饱和至 log2 N，使 IP 轨迹失效；并给出可靠估计所需的样本-维度区间（如 N=10,000 时仅支持约 10 个二值神经元宽的层）。
  • 通过合成 Bernoulli 数据（D∈{1,…,20}, p∈{0.5,0.7,0.9}, N=1000）实验验证：估计在 D≥7 后开始偏离真值并饱和至 log2 N。
  • 训练 125 个实验×3 次=375 个 BNN，覆盖 SZT、MNIST、FashionMNIST、CIFAR-10 及多种架构/正则化，系统调查晚期压缩的存在性与压缩-泛化关系。
  • 结论：压缩普遍存在但压缩表示并不一致地带来更好泛化，关系高度依赖任务、架构与正则化；并指出先前 BNN/量化网络 IP 研究（Raj et al. [11]、Lorenzen et al. [3]）的层宽落在不可靠区间内。

Key Contributions

  • Performs IP analysis on BNNs with binary activations, sidestepping the infinite-MI problem of deterministic continuous DNNs (where I(X;Tℓ)=∞ by Amjad & Geiger [5, Th. 1]) and making the plug-in entropy estimator valid.
  • Characterizes the finite-sample regime for reliable plug-in estimation: with N samples and alphabet size k=2^D, reliable estimation requires N not in the regime N=o(k²/log² k); empirically, with N=1000 the estimate diverges at D≥7 and saturates to log2 N.
  • Validates the regime on synthetic D-dimensional Bernoulli vectors (D∈{1,…,20}, p∈{0.5,0.7,0.9}), showing the plug-in estimate starts underestimating at D≈8 and saturates at log2 N.
  • Trains 375 BNNs (125 experiments × 3 runs) across four datasets (SZT, MNIST, FashionMNIST, CIFAR-10) and multiple architectures (Raj-like, Hourglass, Bottleneck, Small BNN, LeNet5 variants) with weight decay λ∈{0,0.1,…,2}, establishing that late-stage compression is prevalent but does not consistently correlate with improved generalization.
  • Provides evidence that prior IP analyses on BNNs (e.g., Raj et al. [11] with 1024-20-20-20 architecture, k=2²⁰≈10⁶ vs. N=60,000) and 8-bit quantized networks (Lorenzen et al. [3], k≈10¹⁹ vs. N requiring ≈10³⁵) operated outside the reliable estimation regime, casting doubt on their conclusions.

Methodology

Binary Neural Networks. Hidden fully-connected (FC) layers use the sign activation (Eq. 1: sign(x)=1 if x>0 else 0); batch normalization is applied between hidden layers; input/output remain full-precision. The non-differentiability of sign is handled by the saturation-aware straight-through estimator (STE) of Hubara et al. [8] (Eq. 2), which zeroes gradients when |z|>1. Weights stay full-precision.

Entropy/MI estimation. Because Tℓ is a deterministic function of X, I(X;Tℓ)=H(Tℓ) (Eq. 5), estimated via the plug-in entropy estimator Ĥ(X)=−Σ p̂(x)log2 p̂(x) (Eq. 3). I(Tℓ;Y) is estimated via the plug-in MI formula (Eq. 8) using empirical joint p̂(Tℓ,Y). The plug-in estimator is consistent as N→∞ but suboptimal in the large-N regime when N=o(k²/log² k) (Wu & Yang [15]), where k=2^dℓ is the alphabet size and dℓ the layer width.

Reliable regime. For SZT (N≈800 validation samples) reliable estimation supports layers up to ~7 binary neurons; for MNIST/FashionMNIST/CIFAR-10 (N=10,000–60,000) up to ~10 neurons. The synthetic experiment generates 20 runs of N=1000 D-dimensional Bernoulli vectors with D∈{1,…,20}, p∈{0.5,0.7,0.9}; true entropy is H=D·h2(p).

Experimental setup. 125 experiments × 3 runs = 375 BNNs, trained 3,000 epochs each. Datasets: SZT (4096 unique samples, 20% validation), MNIST/FashionMNIST (60K train/10K test), CIFAR-10 (50K train/10K test). Architectures (Table I): SZT* (10-8-6-4), Raj-like (1024-20-20-20-10), Hourglass (1024-20-10-A-10-20-10, A∈{2,4,6,8,10}), Bottleneck (1024-20-10-A-10), Small BNN (50-10-10), LeNet5 (CV6-MP-CV16-MP-120-84-10) and variants. Optimizer: Adam (lr 1e-4 for SZT, 1e-5 otherwise; batch size 64 for SZT, 256 otherwise). Weight decay variants use AdamW with λ∈{0.1,0.2,0.5,0.7,1,1.1,1.2,1.5,1.7,2}.

Metrics. Compression factor ϱ = (Î(X;Tℓ)_max − mean_{last 50}(Î(X;Tℓ)))/Î(X;Tℓ)_max (Eq. 10); Spearman rank correlation r_s between mean Î(X;Tℓ) over last 50 epochs and mean validation accuracy over last 50 epochs.

Results & Highlights

  • Reliable regime boundary: On synthetic Bernoulli data with N=1000, the plug-in estimate starts diverging from true entropy at D≥7 and saturates to log2 N (≈10 bits) by D≈8–10, for all p∈{0.5,0.7,0.9} (Fig. 1). This sets the reliable-layer-width bound (≤7 neurons for SZT's N≈800, ≤10 for MNIST-like N=10,000).
  • Compression is prevalent: Across all four datasets and experiment groups, a large majority of experiments exhibit compression factor ϱ≥25% (Fig. 2). Weight decay generally drives stronger compression, more clearly on SZT and CIFAR-10 than on MNIST/FashionMNIST; later layers compress less than earlier ones.
  • Compression does not consistently improve generalization (Table II): Spearman correlations between end-of-training Î(X;Tℓ) and validation accuracy include significant negative values (compression helps) — MNIST Raj-like r_s=−0.761 (p<0.001), MNIST LeNet5 r_s=−0.573 (p<0.001), CIFAR-10 LeNet5 r_s=−0.642 (p<0.001) — and significant positive values (compression hurts) — FashionMNIST Bottleneck r_s=0.857 (p<0.001), MNIST Hourglass r_s=0.782 (p=0.001), FashionMNIST Small BNN layer -1 r_s=0.768 (p<0.001).
  • Sign flips across architectures/datasets: On MNIST, Raj-like gives r_s=−0.761 (negative) while Hourglass gives r_s=+0.782 (positive); on FashionMNIST, Bottleneck gives +0.857 while Raj-like gives −0.360 (p=0.040). Effect sizes |r_s|>0.5 are common in both directions.
  • Prototypical accuracy–MI curve (CIFAR-10 small LeNet5, Fig. 4c): MI increases with weight decay λ while accuracy first rises then falls (overfitting→well-trained→underfitting transition), yielding weakly negative insignificant r_s=−0.262 (p=0.141).
  • IP dynamics vary qualitatively (Fig. 3): The Raj-like model on MNIST compresses sharply within the first ~100 epochs, with end-of-training Î(X;Tℓ)≈log2(10), indicating collapse to 10 binary patterns (one per class), mean accuracy 96.8% (Fig. 3a). The FashionMNIST Small BNN (λ=1) shows the last layer becoming a bijective permutation of the penultimate layer, accuracy 83.0% (Fig. 3b). CIFAR-10 LeNet5 shows compression preceding the I(Tℓ;Y) increase, accuracy 53.3% (Fig. 3c).
  • Prior work outside reliable regime: Raj et al. [11]'s 1024-20-20-20 BNN has k=2²⁰≈10⁶ vs. N=60,000 MNIST samples; Lorenzen et al. [3]'s 8-bit quantized networks have k≈10¹⁹ requiring N≈10³⁵ samples — both outside the reliable regime, undermining their IP conclusions (Section VII).

Critical Assessment

  • Scope limited to very small layers: The reliable-estimation regime confines analysis to layers with ≤7 (SZT) or ≤10 (MNIST-like) binary neurons — far smaller than contemporary networks. The authors acknowledge this as an "obvious limitation" (Section VII) and note even the best upper bounds estimator (Paninski [16]) cannot handle modern network/dataset scales, so the central compression–generalization question is answered only for toy-scale BNNs.
  • Compression factor ϱ is a crude summary: The authors themselves flag (Section VII) that reducing the entire IP trajectory to a single number ϱ is "insufficient to capture the wide range of qualitative behaviour" — ϱ is positive whenever Î(X;Tℓ) does not peak at the end of training, even if MI is increasing in the final epochs (as in Fig. 3b), conflating genuine late-stage compression with other dynamics.
  • Generalization claim rests on toy datasets and modest accuracies: Three of the four datasets (SZT, MNIST, FashionMNIST) are simple, and CIFAR-10 results use small LeNet5 variants with mediocre accuracy (53.3% for the highlighted run). Whether the mixed compression–generalization findings transfer to larger models is unaddressed.
  • Reliance on deterministic MI identity: The analysis leverages I(X;Tℓ)=H(Tℓ) for deterministic BNNs (Eq. 5), which holds exactly for noise-free binarization; the paper does not study whether stochastic binarization (which would make I(X;Tℓ)
  • No comparison to alternative estimators on the same BNNs: While the authors cite the best upper bounds estimator, they do not run it on their models to push beyond the 10-neuron regime, leaving the generalization claim's scope expansion unattempted.

Strengths

  • Tackles the fundamental statistical-validity problem of IP analysis by using BNNs where MI is genuinely finite and the plug-in estimator is consistent.
  • Provides a clear, validated sample-to-dimensionality reliability boundary (Fig. 1) that can be applied retroactively to judge prior IP studies.
  • Large, systematic experimental sweep (375 BNNs, 4 datasets, 10+ architectures, 10 weight-decay values) with statistical rigor (Spearman correlations, p-values).
  • Honest, nuanced conclusion: compression is common but does not reliably predict generalization, with the relationship depending on task/architecture/regularization — counter to the optimistic IB-theory narrative.
  • Identifies that prior BNN/quantized-network IP analyses (Raj et al. [11], Lorenzen et al. [3]) operated outside the reliable regime, a substantive methodological critique.

Limitations

  • Restricted to tiny layers (≤7–10 neurons) due to plug-in estimator scaling; conclusions cannot be extrapolated to modern architectures.
  • The single-number compression factor ϱ masks trajectory shape and can mislabel non-compression dynamics as compression.
  • Toy/simple datasets and low-accuracy CIFAR-10 models limit ecological validity.
  • Only the deterministic-binarization case is studied; stochastic binarization untested.
  • No deployment of more sophisticated estimators (e.g., Paninski [16]) to widen the analyzable layer size, despite citing them.
  • The paper studies BNNs specifically; the relationship to continuous/quantized non-binary networks remains inferential.

Relevance

本文通过严格刻画互信息估计的有限样本可靠性来审视深度网络训练动态的可观测性，并质疑"压缩即泛化"的可靠性叙事，直接对应「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」（表示的信息论分析）与「ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」（估计量的统计可靠性）两个方向。
