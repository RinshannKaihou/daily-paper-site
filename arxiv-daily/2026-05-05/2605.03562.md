> **一句话总结：** HeadQ提出从模型可见的score空间（而非原始存储空间）衡量KV-cache量化误差，通过在校准查询基上学习低秩残差侧码并将其作为logit加性校正，在2-bit key量化下消除约84–94%的额外困惑度，并揭示了key/value应使用不同失真度量的根本原因。

> **TL;DR:** HeadQ reframes KV-cache quantization by arguing that distortion should be measured in model-visible coordinates: score error modulo constant shifts for keys (through the softmax Fisher geometry), and A²-weighted readout error for values. It stores a low-rank residual side code in a calibration-learned per-head query-PCA basis and applies it as an additive logit correction at inference. Across six models (GPT-2 124M to Mistral 7B), Fisher/score-space error predicts attention KL with Spearman ρ≈0.99 vs. ≈0.03–0.38 for raw key MSE. At 2-bit key-only quantization on WikiText-103, rank-4 HeadQ removes 84–94% of excess perplexity on the strongest damage-bearing rows. An auxiliary full-KV 2-bit composition (HeadQ + A² value policy) improves all six models. The paper includes rigorous falsification: same-budget MSE counterexamples where better reconstruction yields worse behavior, softmax-null interventions, query-PCA vs. random-basis controls, and wrong-sign HeadQ that catastrophically degrades performance.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"模型可见"失真的完整理论框架**：论证KV-cache量化误差应在注意力机制实际读取的坐标空间中度量——key的score空间（logit误差模去常数偏移）和value的注意力加权读出空间（A²加权token失真）。
- **证明softmax对token共享key平移的不变性**：任何对所有token相同的key偏移在softmax后完全消除，因此存储MSE会大量浪费在注意力不可见的分量上。
- **HeadQ方法设计**：在标准低bit key量化器之上，对残差投影到校准学习的per-head query-PCA基上，量化后存储为侧码；推理时当前query将侧码转换为logit加性校正。
- **A²加权value失真代理**：提出value端的A²对角近似作为读出误差的代理（Spearman ρ=0.984 vs. 原始value MSE的0.492）。
- **系统性证伪链**：包含配对MSE反例（更低的存储MSE却产生更差的注意力行为）、softmax-null均值干预、query-PCA vs. 随机基对照、错误方向HeadQ对照，以及Pythia家族匹配checkpoint的路由翻转定位。

## Key Contributions

- Formulates **model-visible KV-cache distortion** as the principled optimization target: score-visible key error (through the softmax Fisher information matrix) for keys, and A²-weighted readout error for values. This explains why raw key/value MSE are misaligned proxies.
- Proves that **softmax is invariant to token-shared key translations** (Proposition 1, Theorem 1), establishing the quotient-space structure of visible key error: Rⁿ / span{1}. Storage MSE pays for null-space components that attention never sees.
- Introduces **HeadQ**: a practical key-side method that stores a low-rank residual side code in a calibration-learned per-head query-PCA basis (Uₕ ∈ R^{d×r}) and applies it as additive logit correction at read time (Eq. 17). Side overhead is rq bits per cached key/head.
- Derives the **A² diagonal surrogate** for value readout error (Proposition 2): when cross-token value residuals are weakly correlated, output MSE ≈ Σⱼ pⱼ²‖Δvⱼ‖²/d. Confirmed with Spearman ρ=0.984 on Qwen2 decode-head probe vs. 0.492 for raw value MSE.
- Builds a **falsification evidence chain**: (1) Fisher/score-space error wins over key MSE on all six models (gains of 0.62–0.69 Spearman points); (2) paired same-budget counterexamples where lower key MSE produces higher attention KL and output MSE; (3) softmax-null mean-key intervention that damages quantized behavior without changing dense attention; (4) query-PCA bases recover Fisher/Base ≈ 0.14 while equal-overhead random bases fail (Fisher/Base ≈ 1.10); (5) wrong-sign HeadQ catastrophically degrades Pythia-160M from +15.7% to +51.9% ΔPPL.
- Achieves **84–94% excess perplexity removal** at 2-bit key-only quantization on the strongest damage-bearing rows (Qwen2, Pythia, GPT-2, Qwen2.5).

## Methodology

**Theoretical framework (Section 3):** The paper establishes the geometry of model-visible KV-cache distortion through the lens of how attention actually reads cached entries:

- **Key score space:** A key perturbation δkⱼ induces score perturbation δsⱼ = q⊤δkⱼ/√d. The local second-order attention damage is the softmax Fisher quadratic I_{q,K}(δK) = δs⊤(diag(p) − pp⊤)δs, which equals the p-weighted centered variance of score errors (Eq. 6). Token-shared shifts are softmax-null (Proposition 1).

- **Value readout space:** Values are consumed through Σⱼ pⱼvⱼ. Under weak cross-token correlation, the A² diagonal surrogate (Σⱼ pⱼ²‖Δvⱼ‖²/d) approximates exact readout error.

**HeadQ algorithm (Section 4, Algorithm 1):**
1. **Calibration:** Collect post-RoPE queries from calibration prompts; store top-r PCA directions Uₕ ∈ R^{d×r} per head.
2. **Cache write:** Quantize base key k̃ⱼ^{base} = B̃(kⱼ); compute residual eⱼ = kⱼ − k̃ⱼ^{base}; project and quantize side code zⱼ = Q_q(Uₕ⊤eⱼ).
3. **Attention read:** Dequantize ẑⱼ; compute corrected logits s̃ⱼ = q⊤k̃ⱼ^{base}/√d + (q⊤Uₕẑⱼ)/√d.

**Experimental protocol:**
- Models: GPT-2 124M, Pythia-160M, Qwen2-0.5B, Qwen2.5-0.5B, TinyLlama 1.1B, Mistral 7B.
- Dataset: WikiText-103, 12 calibration prompts, 4 held-out evaluation prompts, target length 512, 64 sampled heads per prompt.
- Main PPL experiments: key-only quantization with dense values; auxiliary full-KV 2-bit composition separately reported.
- Baseline: KIVI-like per-channel affine key quantization.
- Hardware: Single laptop RTX 4090, PyTorch 2.5.1, HuggingFace Transformers 5.2.0.

**Controls and falsification:**
- Fisher vs. MSE KL-ranking (Table 1): Spearman ρ coefficients on matched cache sites.
- Same-budget counterexamples (Table 2): Centered TurboQuant-MSE has lower key MSE but worse Fisher/KL/output than Fisher-pullback quantizer.
- Softmax-null mean-key intervention (Figure 3, Table 4): α-intervention restoring softmax-inert common component damages fixed-grid quantizers.
- Query-PCA universality sweep (Table 3): 12 calibration prompts, 4 eval prompts, ranks 1/2/4/8.
- Wrong-sign control (Table 6): Reversing the HeadQ logit patch on Pythia-160M sends ΔPPL from +15.7% to +51.9%.

## Results & Highlights

- **Fisher/score-space predicts attention KL far better than key MSE** (Table 1): Fisher Spearman ρ ranges 0.696–0.997 across six models; MSE ranges 0.031–0.376. Gains of +0.62 to +0.69 Spearman points.
- **Same-budget counterexamples confirm MSE failure** (Table 2): On Qwen2-0.5B, centered TurboQuant-MSE achieves key MSE 0.082 vs. FPQ's 0.137, yet FPQ has better Fisher RMSE (0.205 vs. 0.466), attention KL (0.052 vs. 0.404), and output MSE (0.0022 vs. 0.0064).
- **Softmax-null intervention exposes quantizer failure** (Figure 3): Restoring an attention-inert mean key component at α=1 dramatically increases Fisher damage for block FP4 and group absmax-4 quantizers across GPT-2 through TinyLlama; per-channel affine remains invariant.
- **Query-PCA bases effectively capture active structure** (Table 3): Centered Q-PCA r8 q4 reaches Fisher/Base 0.140 and KL/Base 0.146 with 89.7% query-axis capture. Equal-overhead random bases: Fisher/Base 1.096, essentially no recovery.
- **HeadQ downstream PPL results** (Table 5, Figure 1): At 2-bit key-only, rank-4 HeadQ reduces excess ΔPPL:
  - Qwen2-0.5B: +41.5% → +6.0% (86% removal)
  - Pythia-160M: +116.9% → +7.6% (94% removal)
  - GPT-2 124M: +15.6% → +1.7% (89% removal)
  - Qwen2.5-0.5B: +49.1% → +7.8% (84% removal)
  - TinyLlama 1.1B: +15.8% → +6.7% (57% removal)
  - Mistral 7B: +2.1% → +2.3% (near-dense, noise floor)
- **Wrong-sign control** (Table 6): On Qwen2-0.5B at 3-bit, HeadQ improves +3.1% → +0.3%; wrong sign degrades to +1.7%. On Pythia-160M, HeadQ +15.7% → −0.3%; wrong sign catastrophically to +51.9%.
- **Full-KV 2-bit composition** (Table 7, Figure 4): HeadQ + A² value policy improves all six models at 2-bit, e.g., Qwen2-0.5B: +84.9% → +23.1%; Pythia-160M: +173.7% → +19.8%.
- **Matched Pythia route-flip boundary** (Figure 2): The Pythia-160M Fisher anomaly is localized to a low-entropy route-flip regime under rotated/product 3-bit quantizers; weakens sharply at 410M and 1B checkpoints in the same family.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Jorge L. Ruiz Williams** (Independent Researcher). This is a single-author paper that demonstrates remarkable technical depth and methodological rigor. The theoretical derivations (quotient-space geometry, Fisher information analysis, softmax invariance proofs) are clean and well-stated. The experimental design is unusually careful for a single-author work, with multiple orthogonal controls and explicit falsification strategies rather than cherry-picked comparisons. The paper is transparent about its limitations (short windows, models only up to 7B, no serving-kernel claims). The use of matched Pythia checkpoints for controlled scaling analysis is a particularly strong methodological choice. No code repository is mentioned in the paper.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: High.** The core insight—that KV-cache quantization should optimize in model-visible (score/readout) coordinates rather than storage space—is genuinely novel and has been overlooked by the substantial prior literature (KIVI, KVQuant, GEAR, QuaRot, etc.). The formal quotient-space treatment of key error is elegant. The A² value surrogate is a clean analogue. This is not an incremental improvement; it reframes how to think about the problem.

**Rigor: Very strong.** The paper is exemplary in its falsification methodology. Every claim is tested from multiple angles:
- Prediction: Fisher beats MSE at ranking KL (Table 1).
- Counterexample: Lower MSE can mean worse behavior (Table 2).
- Intervention: Softmax-null components damage quantizers but not dense attention (Figure 3).
- Deployability: Query-PCA works, random bases don't (Table 3).
- Directionality: Wrong-sign correction is catastrophic (Table 6).
- Localization: Matched Pythia checkpoints pinpoint the route-flip boundary (Figure 2).

**Reproducibility: Moderate concern.** Single author, no code release mentioned. The calibration procedure (12 prompts, PCA basis learning, side quantizer configuration) is described but has many moving parts. The paper uses Python-side eager-attention hooks rather than custom kernels, which is actually good for reproducibility. The experimental setup section is detailed enough that a motivated researcher could reproduce the main results.

**Significance: High potential but narrowly demonstrated.** The score-space perspective is potentially paradigm-shifting for KV-cache compression research. However, the empirical validation has notable gaps:
- Models only up to Mistral 7B; the 7B–70B frontier where KV-cache matters most is untouched.
- Short WikiText-103 windows (length 128–512); no long-context evaluation.
- Main PPL results are key-only with dense values, which is an incomplete picture.
- The auxiliary full-KV composition at 2-bit shows large remaining degradation (e.g., Qwen2 still at +23% ΔPPL), suggesting the value side needs much more work.

**Red flags:**
- The 84–94% excess PPL removal numbers are impressive but cherry-picked on "the strongest damage-bearing rows"—models where the baseline is already catastrophically bad (e.g., Pythia +117%). The percentage metric inflates apparent gains.
- Mistral 7B, the largest and most practically relevant model, shows essentially no benefit (already near-dense at 2-bit). This is concerning for scalability claims.
- The auxiliary full-KV results are explicitly described as "unoptimized" and "not a serving-kernel claim," yet they are prominently featured in Figure 4.
- The paper does not quantify the actual side-code overhead in bytes or its impact on memory bandwidth—the practical resource that motivates KV-cache compression.

## Strengths

- Deep theoretical insight: the score-space vs. storage-space distinction is clean, well-motivated, and formally grounded.
- Exceptional falsification methodology with five independent control strategies, far beyond typical ML papers.
- Clear writing that progresses logically from theory to evidence to method.
- Transparent about limitations; the authors explicitly flag what the paper does not claim.
- The matched Pythia checkpoint analysis for localizing anomalies is a model of careful scientific investigation.
- Practical algorithm: HeadQ adds only rq bits per cached key/head with no modification to value cache or future queries.

## Limitations

- No evaluation on models larger than 7B or on long-context benchmarks where KV-cache compression matters most.
- Main PPL experiments are key-only with dense values; full-KV results show large remaining degradation and are labeled "auxiliary."
- The side-code memory overhead is not quantified in absolute terms or compared against the total cache budget.
- No latency or throughput measurements; the paper is explicitly "mechanistic, not a serving-system claim."
- Short evaluation windows (128–512 tokens); behavior may differ at longer contexts where attention patterns change.
- Single-author work with no code release limits community validation and adoption.
- The wrong-sign control only tests two models (Qwen2 and Pythia); a broader sweep would strengthen the evidence.
- Random-baseline controls are noted to be "model- and window-dependent," which somewhat weakens them as universal benchmarks.

## Relevance

该论文从注意力机制的几何结构出发重新审视KV-cache量化目标函数，提出的score空间视角对理解模型压缩中的失真度量具有根本性意义。其核心思想——在模型实际读取的坐标空间中优化而非存储空间——对LLM高效推理和部署的研究方向高度相关，可能影响未来的量化方法设计。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does HeadQ perform on models in the 13B–70B range (e.g., Llama 3 70B) where KV-cache memory pressure is the primary bottleneck?
- Does the score-space perspective transfer to long-context settings (8K–128K tokens) where attention patterns are more diffuse and route flips may be more frequent?
- Can the query-PCA calibration be amortized or transferred across models, or does it need to be repeated per checkpoint?
- How does HeadQ interact with grouped-query attention (GQA) and multi-query attention (MQA) architectures where the key/query dimensionality relationship differs?
- What is the absolute side-code memory overhead, and does it remain practical when combined with KV-cache eviction methods like H2O or StreamingLLM?
- Could the score-space analysis inform mixed-precision allocation across layers and heads in a principled way (beyond the A² heuristic)?
- Is the Pythia route-flip anomaly specific to the Pythia family's training, or does it appear in other small models under aggressive quantization?
