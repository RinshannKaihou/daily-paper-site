> **一句话总结：** 本文提出无需训练的视频 VLM 反重计算框架，包含三大机制：C-PERSIST（同视频后续查询复用并修复 KV 缓存，在 Qwen2.5-VL-7B-4bit 上实现 14.90–35.92× 后续查询加速且 0/93 配对漂移）、C-VISION（首查询视觉塔剪枝，Gemma 4-E4B-4bit 上 1.316× 加速）、C-CEILING（阶段占比天花板，约束组件加速向端到端的转化）。

> **TL;DR:** The authors study training-free anti-recomputation for frozen video VLMs across three regimes. C-PERSIST (adaptive repaired-cache inheritance) on Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct-4bit preserves paired choice/correctness on 93 VideoMME breadth queries (0/93 drift) while delivering 14.90–35.92× same-class follow-up speedup (15.28–35.97× all-query) versus cold follow-up, with the second follow-up inheriting repaired cache at 9.50× paired speedup. C-VISION on Gemma 4-E4B-4bit reaches 1.316× first-query end-to-end speedup (32f short, 20 items, 0 paired drift, 42.2% vision-time reduction) versus the dense baseline. C-CEILING provides the E2Eideal=1/(f_fixed+(1−f_fixed)/s) guardrail, validated by an n=60 composition audit landing at 1.042× where the measured share terms predict.

## 核心贡献

- **C-PERSIST（后续查询复用）**：提出自适应"修复-继承"缓存策略——首次后续查询刷新一帧最新视觉尾部，第二次后续查询继承已修复的缓存状态；在 Qwen2.5-VL-7B-4bit 的 93 题 VideoMME 广度设置上实现 0/93 配对选择/正确性漂移、14.90–35.92× 同类加速，第二次后续查询配对加速 9.50× 且尾部 token 减少 88.9%。
- **C-CEILING（阶段占比天花板）**：给出端到端加速公式 E2Eideal=1/(f_fixed+(1−f_fixed)/s) 与视觉塔特化预测 E2E_pred=1/(1−V_share·V_red)，解释为何首查询加速受视觉塔墙钟占比约束、为何 C-VISION 与 C-PERSIST 不能简单相乘。
- **C-VISION（首查询视觉剪枝）**：在 Gemma 4-E4B-4bit 上通过视觉塔内部层后保留部分 token 并 scatter-back，32f short 单元实现 1.316× 端到端加速、42.2% 视觉时间减少、0 配对漂移；同时给出 Qwen 的 keep-rate 边界（krV=0.85 仅 1.032× 增益）。
- **压力测试边界**：50 轮重复问题压力下自适应修复与 scheduled-refresh 保持 0/343 漂移；dense-answer-anchored 提示变化下保守 K=1 保持 0/133 漂移而激进策略出现 4.51% 漂移，刻画保守/激进边界。
- **失败模式剖析**：明确"cache basin"病态输出（重复 malformed attractor 字符串），并证明不修复的继承规则会导致第三次查询全部塌缩到病态盆地（Δacc=−0.0952，7/7 病态）。

## Key Contributions

- **C-PERSIST (after-ingest follow-up reuse with repair):** Adaptive repaired-cache inheritance refreshes one newest visual frame at the first follow-up, then lets the second follow-up inherit the repaired cache with no additional visual re-prefill. On Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct-4bit across 20f short/medium/long + 32f short VideoMME slices, this yields 0/93 paired choice drift, 0/93 paired correctness drift, 0/62 pathological follow-ups, and 14.90–35.92× same-class follow-up speedup (15.28–35.97× all-query).
- **C-CEILING (stage-share ceiling):** The arithmetic guardrail E2Eideal=1/(f_fixed+(1−f_fixed)/s) and its vision-tower specialization E2E_pred=1/(1−V_share·V_red), validated by 17 regimes in Figure 4 and an n=60 composition audit landing at 1.042× observed vs. 1.028× predicted. This explains why first-pass gains are share-limited and why C-VISION and C-PERSIST do not multiply.
- **C-VISION (first-pass vision pruning):** On Gemma 4-E4B-4bit (MLX-community 4-bit), the clean 32f short cell reaches 1.316× first-query end-to-end speedup with 0 paired drift on 20 items, 0 parse failures, 42.2% vision-time reduction, and a −0.012× ceiling gap. Qwen's 16f krV=0.85 point recovers to within aggregate-accuracy/format gates but only 1.032× gain (13.6% vision reduction).
- **Stress-test boundaries:** A 50-turn repeated-question stress shows adaptive repair and scheduled-refresh at 0/343 paired choice/correctness drift (median follow-up <0.8s); fixed K=1 stays under the 3% gate with sparse 3/343 drift. A dense-answer-anchored prompt-variation stress separates conservative fixed K=1 (0/133 drift) from aggressive adaptive/refresh-10 (6/133 = 4.51% drift).
- **Failure-mode forensics:** Documents the "cache basin" where unrepaired long-prefix reuse collapses into pathological outputs (e.g., "addCriterion..." or the literal four-character Chinese auto-generated string U+81EA U+52A8 U+751F U+6210); a failed adaptive variant that omits second-follow-up refresh collapses 7/7 second follow-ups into the basin (Δacc=−0.0952, 6/21 choice diffs).

## Methodology

**Three regimes, kept analytically separate.** (1) First-pass visual pruning (C-VISION): skip/reduce vision-tower work before the rest of the model pays for it. (2) After-ingest follow-up reuse (C-PERSIST): keep the expensive prefix from the first query and ask later questions against the same video. (3) Candidate streaming (C-STREAM): native-rate reuse, treated as a boundary/target, not a headline. Plus a routing lane (Qwen) for mechanism validation under a fixed dense backend.

**Models & hardware (Table 3).** Routing & persistent-KV: Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct-4bit via MLX-VLM on M3 Air 16 GB. First-pass pruning: MLX-community gemma-4-e4b-4bit on M3 Air 16 GB; plus Qwen2.5-VL-3B-4bit for the frame-scaling envelope. Scale-out streaming: Gemma 4 26B/A4B-family RTSP/screen-recording stack on M5 Max MacBook Pro 128 GB. All models frozen, 4-bit, no retraining.

**Benchmarks.** TOMATO [37] and a frozen motion-heavy MVBench [15] slice for routing; VideoMME [13,14,39] for first-pass (8f) and persistent-KV (20f short/medium/long + 32f short). Uniform global sampling at 8 frames, square-pad + resize to 560×560; routing planner operates on decoded RGB frames.

**C-PERSIST mechanism.** Selective re-prefill refreshes a fixed newest-frame visual tail (K frames) before a follow-up. Fixed K=1 refreshes one newest frame each follow-up; adaptive repair refreshes once at the first follow-up then inherits the repaired cache. Prefix coverage and tail-token counts are logged (e.g., adaptive second follow-up: 50 tail tokens, 0.994 coverage vs. fixed K=1: 451 tail tokens, 0.944 coverage).

**C-VISION mechanism.** Patches the vision tower after internal layer L, keeps fraction krV of visual token groups, scatters back so downstream geometry is unchanged. L=2 is the default cut layer. The speedup is bounded by E2E_pred=1/(1−V_share·V_red) where V_share is the dense vision wall-clock share and V_red the observed vision-time reduction.

**C-CEILING.** For a stage accelerated by factor s with the rest held fixed: E2Eideal=1/(f_fixed+(1−f_fixed)/s). Predictions are computed from measured stage timings, not fit to observed end-to-end results; residuals are reported (Table 14).

**Paired-drift metrics.** Paired choice drift = session answer picks a different multiple-choice option than matched cold baseline; paired correctness drift = different right/wrong on the same item; pathological-like outputs matched against logged malformed attractor strings. Stricter than aggregate accuracy. The 93-query breadth envelope = 31 sessions × 3 queries; the rule-of-three query-level bound is ~3.2%.

## Results & Highlights

- **C-PERSIST adaptive repair (Table 5):** 14.90–35.92× same-class follow-up speedup (15.28–35.97× all-query), 0/93 paired choice drift, 0/93 paired correctness drift, 0/62 pathological follow-ups, across 20f short/medium/long + 32f short VideoMME slices on Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct-4bit.
- **C-PERSIST second-follow-up inheritance (Figure 3):** Adaptive repair reuses the repaired cache (median 50 tail tokens, 0.994 coverage, 0.675s latency) vs. fixed K=1 re-buying the tail (median 451 tokens, 0.944 coverage, 6.65s latency) — a 9.50× paired second-follow-up speedup and 88.9% median tail-token reduction.
- **C-PERSIST raw warm reuse (Table 4):** At 16f, Qwen2.5-VL-7B raw warm reuse reaches 91.1× cold/cached follow-up speedup with Δacc=0.000 (0.807s median); at 8f, 47.2× with Δacc=−0.048 (0.815s median). The 3B model holds Δacc=−0.19 through 24f/32f/36f before basin onset beyond 14.5k prefill tokens (Figure 2).
- **C-PERSIST many-turn stress (Table 8):** 50-turn repeated-question stress on seven 20f videos — adaptive repair 0/343 choice drift, 0/343 correctness drift, 0.771s median follow-up; scheduled refresh-10 0/343, 0.706s; fixed K=1 3/343 choice, 2/343 correctness, 7.362s (passes 3% gate).
- **C-PERSIST prompt-variation stress (Table 9):** Dense-answer-anchored 20-turn — conservative fixed K=1: 0/133 choice/correctness drift (5.329s, ~15.0×); aggressive adaptive/refresh-10: 6/133 = 4.51% drift each (0.698s/~114.6× and 0.709s/~112.8×), failing the 3% gate.
- **C-VISION Gemma measured sparse (Table 12):** 32f short cell — 1.316× first-query E2E speedup, Δacc=+0.000, 100% choice agreement, 0 parse failures (dense and sparse), 42.2% vision-time reduction, −0.012× ceiling residual (clean operating point). The 8f/16f/32f full sweep preserves paired accuracy but shares parse failures.
- **C-VISION Qwen boundary (Table 13):** 16f krV=0.85 — 1.032× E2E, Δacc=−0.050, 81.7% agreement, 13.6% vision reduction (within aggregate-accuracy/format gates, low-gain boundary). 8f krV=0.50 — 1.042× E2E but Δacc=−0.067, fidelity-contract fail.
- **C-CEILING validation (Table 14, Figure 4):** n=60 composition audit — V_share=0.063, V_red=0.430, predicted 1.028×, observed 1.042×, residual +1.4pp (composition null). Qwen cross-arch matched point — V_share=0.100, V_red=0.448, predicted 1.047×, observed 1.042×, residual −0.5pp.
- **Random-keep sanity (Table 11):** Qwen magnitude_norm scorer at krV=0.50 reaches 0.500 accuracy vs. uniform-random mean 0.358 (4 seeds) — a +14.2pp structured-vs-random gap; dense reference 0.533.
- **Setup-inclusive economics (Table 10):** Qwen 7B/16f raw reuse — Q=1 session speedup 1.04×, Q=2 2.05×, Q=5 4.94×, Q=10 9.33×, Q=50 32.17× (Δacc=+0.00). Gemma 26B/32f after-warm prefix snapshots — Q=10 6.74×, Q=50 13.58× (Δacc=+0.00).
- **Composition hard negative (Table 15):** Qwen 8f L=2 krV=0.50 + persistent-KV — cold aggregate accuracy 0.561 vs. streaming 0.503 (Δ=−0.0585, 95% paired CI ≈[−0.117, 0.000]) at 2.79× amortized speedup; instrumentation shows follow-up vision pruning is mechanically inactive (active fraction 0.0, all follow-up image tokens cache-served).

## Critical Assessment

- **Headline speedup applies only to after-ingest follow-ups, not first queries:** The 14.90–35.92× figure is a same-class follow-up speedup (cold follow-up / repaired follow-up); the first query "still pays full freight." The paper is explicit about this (C-CEILING, Table 10's Q=1 ≈1.04×), but the abstract's prominence of the large multiplier risks misreading by casual readers. The setup-inclusive Q=1 number is essentially neutral (1.04×).
- **Small sample sizes for the clean headline cells:** The 0/93 paired-drift breadth result is a finite-sample observation with a rule-of-three bound of ~3.2%; the clean C-VISION 32f short cell is n=20 items. The 0/31 session-level view is even coarser. The authors acknowledge "this is a finite tested envelope, not a population proof," but the headline phrasing ("0/93") may overstate certainty.
- **Composition is a hard negative, not a positive:** The Qwen 8f composition of vision pruning + persistent-KV (Table 15) is a near-miss/failure (Δ=−0.0585, CI touching zero), and instrumentation reveals follow-up vision pruning is mechanically inactive under prompt-cache reuse. The paper honestly reports this, but it means the two main mechanisms do not stack as a deployable combined policy on Qwen.
- **Single-hardware, single-quantization evaluation:** All main results use 4-bit MLX conversions on Apple Silicon (M3 Air 16 GB, M5 Max 128 GB). Whether the cache-basin failure mode, the V_share magnitudes, and the keep-rate boundaries transfer to other runtimes (CUDA, higher precision) is untested.
- **C-STREAM is explicitly not a headline:** The streaming/native-rate regime is flagged as "the native-rate deployment target, not a headline result here," and the 26B scale-out bundle primarily sharpens boundaries (default cross-turn cache path unsafe; low-FPS dense is a strong baseline) rather than adding wins.
- **Routing lane produces no deployable speedup:** Qwen routing is "semantic substitution evidence, not a latency claim" — it tests answer preservation under a fixed dense backend but does not skip timed work, so it is mechanism evidence rather than a systems result.

## Strengths

- Separates three reuse regimes with distinct denominators and refuses to multiply headline numbers — the C-CEILING accounting is a genuine methodological contribution toward honest efficiency reporting.
- Paired-drift metrics (choice, correctness, pathological outputs, parse failures) are stricter than aggregate accuracy and expose failure modes (cache basin, format collapse) that aggregate scores hide.
- Forensic failure-mode analysis: the failed adaptive variant (7/7 basin collapse, Δacc=−0.0952) and the mechanically-inactive composition (active fraction 0.0) are reported, not buried.
- Real wall-clock measurements with per-stage timing, prefix-coverage logging, and tail-token accounting; speedup denominators are stated for every claim.
- Stress tests (50-turn repeated-question, dense-answer-anchored prompt variation) meaningfully probe cache-horizon and prompt-sensitivity boundaries.
- Training-free and frozen-model: results are immediately deployable without retraining.

## Limitations

- The 14.90–35.92× speedup is after-ingest only (follow-up queries on the same video); first-query gains are modest (1.113–1.316×) and share-limited.
- Small n for clean headline cells (93 paired queries, 20 items for C-VISION 32f short); finite-sample bounds are coarse.
- Composition of C-VISION + C-PERSIST is a hard negative on Qwen (follow-up pruning mechanically inactive under prompt-cache reuse).
- Single runtime (MLX 4-bit on Apple Silicon); transfer to CUDA/other precisions untested.
- C-STREAM (native-rate streaming) is unfinished — a target, not a result.
- Qwen routing produces no timed speedup, only answer-preservation evidence.
- No code release stated in the PDF; reproduction requires re-implementing the MLX hooks, planner, and cache-repair logic.
- Unconventional naming ("VLMaxxing," "FrameMogging") and informal tone may hinder uptake by venues expecting conventional academic style.

## Relevance

本文研究视频 VLM 推理中的 KV 缓存复用与视觉塔剪枝，核心是"何时可安全跳过重计算"的验证门控与配对漂移监测，直接对应「LLM Inference Evaluation」方向（推理效率与质量保真的评估方法）；其 C-CEILING 阶段占比护栏与配对漂移契约也为「Computation Reliability & Precision Errors」提供了量化可靠性分析的范例。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the cache-basin failure mode and the V_share magnitudes transfer to CUDA runtimes and higher precisions (FP16/FP32), or are they artifacts of MLX 4-bit on Apple Silicon?
- Can the adaptive repair policy be made content-adaptive (refresh more frames when codec motion vectors signal high change) rather than fixed at K=1?
- Why is follow-up vision pruning mechanically inactive under prompt-cache reuse (active fraction 0.0), and can the cache contract be redesigned to allow active follow-up pruning without breaking fidelity?
- How does C-PERSIST interact with speculative decoding or other inference optimizations that also touch the KV cache?
- Can the paired-drift contract and C-CEILING accounting be standardized as a benchmark protocol for the broader efficient-video-VLM literature (which often reports only aggregate accuracy and component multipliers)?
- What is the memory cost of maintaining repaired-cache snapshots for many concurrent video sessions, and does it change the Q=50 economics in Table 10?
- Could VLM-native media formats (exposing change/motion/uncertainty tiles directly) eliminate the need for pixel-diff planners and push C-VISION gains beyond the V_share ceiling?
