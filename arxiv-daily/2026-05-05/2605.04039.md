> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 SaFE-Scale 框架和 RadSaFE-200 基准（200道放射学多选题，由临床医生标注了高风险错误、不安全答案和证据矛盾标签），在34个本地部署的LLM上系统评估了6种部署条件，发现干净证据将准确率从73.5%提升至94.1%、高风险错误从12.0%降至2.6%，而标准RAG和智能体RAG虽提升准确率却无法复现同等安全效果，模型缩放和推理计算均不能替代高质量证据——安全性是部署属性而非规模属性。

> **TL;DR:** The authors introduce SaFE-Scale, a framework evaluating how clinical LLM safety scales with model size, evidence quality, retrieval strategy, context exposure, and inference-time compute. Using RadSaFE-200 (200 clinician-annotated radiology MCQs with per-option safety labels: high-risk, unsafe, evidence contradiction), they evaluate 34 locally deployed LLMs across 6 deployment conditions (40,800 total evaluations). The key finding: clean clinician-written evidence boosted accuracy from 73.5%→94.1% and cut high-risk error from 12.0%→2.6% across all 34 models, while standard RAG (76.0% accuracy) and agentic RAG (78.1%) left high-risk error and dangerous overconfidence elevated. Scaling model size, extending context, and self-consistency all failed to close the safety gap. Deployment condition explained 43% of accuracy variance vs. 9% for model family. Worst-case errors concentrated in ~15 questions where even the largest models unanimously failed. Clinical LLM safety is a deployment property, not a scaling consequence.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 SaFE-Scale 框架**：首个系统衡量临床 LLM 安全性随模型规模、证据质量、检索策略、上下文暴露和推理计算量变化的评估框架，包含完整的方法论和方差分解分析。
- **构建 RadSaFE-200 基准**：200道放射学多选题（来自RadioRAG、RaR和新增31题），865个答案选项均由临床医生标注了高风险错误、不安全答案和证据矛盾三个二值标签，69%的题目包含至少一个高风险选项。
- **大规模全因子实验**：34个LLM（7个模型家族，0.5B-685B参数）× 6种部署条件 × 200题 = 40,800次评估，加上自一致性（8模型×3条件）和集成（4个三模型集成×3条件）实验。
- **安全性-准确性脱钩的核心发现**：干净证据将准确率从73.5%提升至94.1%，高风险错误从12.0%降至2.6%；而标准RAG和智能体RAG虽提升准确率，但高风险错误和危险过度自信仍居高不下。
- **揭示置信度不可靠**：模型在正确答案和高风险错误上的置信度几乎无差异（沿y=x对角线分布），置信度不能作为安全过滤信号。
- **最坏情况集中性**：临床后果严重的错误集中在~15个问题上，最高危问题在全部34个模型上均答错，33个模型产生高风险错误。
- **集成失败同步性**：三模型集成中大量高风险错误为全员一致错误，集成创造的"共识"可能给用户带来虚假安全感。

## Key Contributions

- **SaFE-Scale framework**: The first systematic framework for measuring clinical LLM safety across five scaling axes (model size, evidence quality, retrieval strategy, context exposure, inference-time compute), with rigorous variance decomposition showing deployment condition explains 43% of accuracy variance vs. 9% for model family.
- **RadSaFE-200 benchmark**: A 200-question radiology MCQ benchmark (104 from RadioRAG, 65 from RaR, 31 newly curated) with per-option safety annotations: 289/865 options (33%) labeled high-risk, 85 (10%) unsafe, 342 (39%) contradicting clean evidence. Publicly available on HuggingFace with source-specific redistribution restrictions.
- **Comprehensive factorial experiment**: 34 LLMs (Qwen, Llama, Gemma/MedGemma, DeepSeek, Mistral, OpenAI-OSS; 0.5B–685B params) × 6 deployment conditions × 200 questions = 40,800 evaluations, plus targeted self-consistency (8 models × 3 conditions, 20 samples each) and ensemble (4 fixed 3-model ensembles × 3 conditions) experiments.
- **Safety-accuracy decoupling**: Clean evidence universally moved all 34 models toward the safe deployment region (accuracy≥90%, high-risk error≤5%); standard RAG and agentic RAG improved selected metrics but left high-risk error and dangerous overconfidence near closed-book levels. Agentic RAG actually *increased* dangerous overconfidence compared to standard RAG.
- **Confidence is not a safety signal**: Mean confidence on high-risk errors was 87.8% (closed-book) and 93.0% (agentic RAG), nearly indistinguishable from confidence on correct answers. Models are high-confidence precisely on their most dangerous errors.
- **Worst-case error concentration**: The 15 highest-risk questions produced high-risk errors in >50% of the 34-model panel; the top-ranked question was answered incorrectly by all 34 models with 97.1% high-risk error rate.
- **Synchronized ensemble failure**: A new failure mode where all 3 ensemble members select the same wrong answer (5.9% under closed-book), meaning agreement among models is not equivalent to safety.

## Methodology

**Benchmark construction**: RadSaFE-200 pools 200 text-only radiology MCQs from three subsets. A board-certified radiologist (T.T.N., 8 years experience) wrote clean evidence (median 32 words, explaining the correct answer) and conflict evidence (clean + one distracting sentence, median 46.5 words) for each question, and assigned per-option binary labels for high-risk error, unsafe answer, and evidence contradiction.

**Model panel**: 34 locally deployed LLMs across 7 families: Qwen (11 models, 0.5B–235B), Llama (6, 1B–Scout-17B), Gemma/MedGemma (5, 4B–E4B), DeepSeek (2, including R1 and V3.2), Mistral (6, 3B–675B), OpenAI-OSS (2, 20B/120B). All models received identical prompts; answers extracted via majority vote over k=20 repetitions (k=5 for V3.2/Scout, k=3 for R1).

**Six deployment conditions**: (1) Closed-book zero-shot; (2) Clean evidence injection; (3) Conflict evidence injection; (4) Standard RAG using Radiopaedia with fixed retrieved context; (5) Agentic RAG using the RaR framework with multi-step query reformulation; (6) Max-context prompting expanding context to model's maximum length.

**Confidence**: Derived from entropy-normalized repeated-sampling stability (Eq. 2), not token probabilities. Dangerous overconfidence = incorrect + high-risk/unsafe option + confidence ≥ 0.80.

**Statistical analysis**: 1,000 bootstrap replicates over questions (N=200). Two-way variance decomposition (OLS: family + condition + interaction) across the 34×6 grid.

## Results & Highlights

| Condition | Accuracy | High-risk error | Unsafe | Contradiction | Danger. overconf. | Latency (s) |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|--------|---------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Closed-book | 73.5% | 12.0% | 1.8% | 12.7% | 8.0% | 18.0 |
| Clean evidence | **94.1%** | **2.6%** | **0.2%** | **2.3%** | **1.6%** | 17.3 |
| Conflict evidence | 92.5% | 3.5% | 0.2% | 2.6% | 2.3% | 17.5 |
| Standard RAG | 76.0% | 9.6% | 1.9% | 11.7% | 5.7% | 18.4 |
| Agentic RAG | 78.1% | 10.3% | 1.6% | 9.0% | 8.0% | 19.9 |
| Max context | 74.0% | 10.6% | 2.0% | 11.6% | 6.0% | 27.0 |

- **Clean evidence**: All 34/34 models improved in both accuracy and safety. Family-level accuracy collapsed to a narrow ceiling band (90.4%–97.0%) regardless of closed-book baseline.
- **Agentic RAG paradox**: Improved accuracy over standard RAG (+2.1pp) and reduced contradiction (11.7%→9.0%), but high-risk error *increased* (9.6%→10.3%) and dangerous overconfidence *increased* (5.7%→8.0%). 17/34 models worsened in dangerous overconfidence vs. closed-book.
- **Variance decomposition**: Deployment condition explained 43% (accuracy), 45% (high-risk error), 38% (dangerous overconfidence) of variance; model family explained only 9%, 8%, 17%. Column (condition) dominates row (family) in the heatmap.
- **Within-family variance collapse**: Under clean evidence, within-family SD of high-risk error dropped from 3.8pp (closed-book) to 1.0pp. Curated evidence equalizes families; weakest gain the most.
- **Self-consistency**: Mean accuracy gain only +0.39pp across 24 model-condition pairs; safety gains negligible (+0.27pp for high-risk). Far short of clean evidence effect.
- **Ensembles**: Improved over panel average but did not beat the best individual member (mean change vs. best member: −0.88pp accuracy, −1.67pp dangerous overconfidence). Synchronized failure averaged 5.9% (closed-book), reaching 9.0% for the Frontier ensemble under RAG.
- **Worst-case**: Question 53 (neuroradiology/pediatrics) produced wrong answers in 34/34 models, high-risk errors in 33/34, contradictions in 34/34. The top-15 questions all had high-risk errors in >50% of the panel.

## Authors & Credibility

The team spans Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) and RWTH Aachen University/University Hospital. **Sebastian Wind** and **Tri-Thien Nguyen** (shared first authorship) combine HPC/ML expertise with clinical radiology (Nguyen is a board-certified radiologist). **Soroosh Tayebi Arasteh** (corresponding author, Lab for AI in Medicine, RWTH Aachen) and **Daniel Truhn** have published RadioRAG and RaR — the prior works from which RadSaFE-200's question pools are drawn. **Andreas Maier** (Pattern Recognition Lab, FAU) and **Gerhard Wellein** (HPC center) provide systems-level credibility. The cross-institutional, clinician–computer-science collaboration is genuine: the benchmark was annotated by a practicing radiologist, and the model deployment was done locally (no API dependency). The paper transparently discloses that the RaR and RadioRAG question sets are being reused and augmented, which is a strength (enabling comparison with prior work) but also a limitation (not entirely new data).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The core thesis — that safety and accuracy scale differently in clinical LLMs — is clearly articulated and rigorously demonstrated. The variance decomposition showing condition explains ~5x more variance than model family is a compelling and actionable finding. The option-level safety annotation scheme (high-risk, unsafe, contradiction) is methodologically novel and should be adopted by future clinical AI benchmarks.

- **Rigor**: Very strong. 34 models, 6 conditions, 40,800 evaluations with bootstrap CIs. Clinician-annotated safety labels (not automated). Clean/conflict evidence as paired perturbation. The variance decomposition is transparent and well-executed. The worst-case analysis goes beyond aggregate reporting.

- **Reproducibility**: Good. All models locally deployed; code and benchmark publicly available (HuggingFace + GitHub). However, the benchmark has source-specific redistribution restrictions (RSNA and Radiopaedia content partially withheld), which limits full independent reproduction of the question stems. The Radiopaedia-derived New-31 subset has question stems, options, and evidence fields withheld in the public release — only metadata and safety annotations are shared.

- **Significance**: Potentially high impact. The finding that RAG (standard and agentic) does not automatically improve safety challenges a core assumption in clinical AI deployment. The demonstration that agentic RAG can increase dangerous overconfidence while improving accuracy is particularly concerning for production systems.

- **Red flags and concerns**:
  1. **Benchmark domain limitation**: RadSaFE-200 is entirely radiology MCQs. The authors acknowledge this but the paper's title and framing generalize to "clinical LLMs" broadly. Safety dynamics in open-ended generation, other specialties, or multimodal tasks may differ substantially.
  2. **200 questions is modest**: For subgroup analyses (9 subspecialties, 9 question types), 200 questions provide limited statistical power. The authors acknowledge this.
  3. **Single annotator**: All safety labels were assigned by one radiologist (T.T.N.). No inter-rater agreement analysis. The authors note this limitation and call for multi-annotator future work, but it weakens the objectivity of the safety labels — which are the paper's core measurement instrument.
  4. **Conflict evidence is mild**: One added distracting sentence is a relatively weak perturbation. Real-world clinical evidence noise can be far more severe (outdated guidelines, contradictory studies, fabrication). The "graded" safety degradation under conflict is unsurprising given the mild perturbation.
  5. **RAG implementation specifics**: Standard RAG used pre-computed retrieved context (not model-specific), and agentic RAG generated evidence reports once per question shared across all models. This standardization is methodologically clean but may underrepresent what production RAG systems actually do (where retrieval is model-aware and query-adaptive).
  6. **Confidence via repeated sampling**: Entropy-normalized confidence from 20 samples is a reasonable proxy but is not the same as calibrated token probabilities. The "confidence is unreliable" finding should be validated with proper calibration methods.
  7. **Ensemble design is limited**: Only 4 fixed 3-model ensembles under 3 conditions. This is sufficient to demonstrate synchronized failure but too limited to generalize about ensemble safety dynamics.

## Strengths

- Clinically grounded safety annotations by a practicing radiologist, not automated heuristics.
- Massive factorial experiment (34 models × 6 conditions × 200 questions) with transparent statistical analysis.
- The variance decomposition cleanly separates the contributions of model scale vs. deployment condition — a methodological contribution beyond this specific finding.
- Actionable and counterintuitive results: RAG doesn't guarantee safety, agentic RAG can increase danger, confidence is unreliable, and ensembles fail in lockstep.
- Worst-case analysis reveals that aggregate metrics mask concentrated failure modes.
- All models locally deployed, ensuring results aren't confounded by API variability or version drift.
- Excellent paper structure and figure design (Fig. 1 roadmap, paired transition plots, safety radar profiles).

## Limitations

- Single-domain (radiology), single-format (MCQ) benchmark; generalization to free-text generation and other specialties is untested.
- Single annotator for all safety labels; no inter-rater reliability analysis.
- 200 questions limit the precision of subgroup analyses by subspecialty or question type.
- Conflict evidence perturbation is mild (one added sentence); may underestimate real-world evidence noise severity.
- RAG implementations are standardized (per-question, not per-model), which may not reflect production RAG behavior.
- Self-consistency and ensemble experiments are targeted secondary analyses, not exhaustive searches over compute regimes.
- The "synchronized failure" finding, while important, is demonstrated on only 4 ensembles of 3 models each.
- New-31 subset question stems and evidence are not publicly available due to Radiopaedia licensing, limiting full benchmark reproducibility.

## Relevance

这项研究对临床 AI 安全评估和 LLM 部署策略具有直接指导意义。SaFE-Scale 框架揭示的"安全性不随规模自动提升"和"RAG不能保证安全"的发现，对构建可靠的医疗 LLM 系统具有关键参考价值，特别是在证据质量控制和最坏情况分析方面。对关注 LLM 安全性和可信 AI 的研究有重要借鉴意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the safety-accuracy decoupling findings transfer to open-ended clinical text generation, diagnostic report writing, or multimodal (image+text) radiology tasks?
- Can the RadSaFE-200 option-level safety annotation scheme be extended to create a general-purpose clinical safety evaluation framework across multiple specialties?
- What specific properties of clinician-curated evidence drive the safety improvement, and can automated evidence curation pipelines (e.g., guideline extraction + quality filtering) partially replicate the effect?
- How does the synchronized failure pattern scale with ensemble size and diversity — do larger, more diverse ensembles reduce or amplify correlated failures?
- Would adversarially constructed conflict evidence (multiple contradictions, fabricated references) produce a sharper safety collapse, and how should RAG systems defend against it?
- Can the variance decomposition methodology be applied to other high-stakes domains (legal, financial) to assess whether the condition-over-family dominance is a general phenomenon?
