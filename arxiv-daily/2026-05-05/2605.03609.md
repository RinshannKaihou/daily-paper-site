> **TL;DR:** Proposes Convergent-Divergent Routing (CDR) to locate minimal "branch points" inside transformer blocks where attention heads are shared across utilitarian/deontological reasoning but FFN units diverge. Gating non-target paths at these loci yields binary moral control. Fine-grained control is achieved via CSP-extracted paired discriminative directions in the residual stream plus Dual Logit Calibration вҖ” a closed-form, minimum-в„“вӮӮ-norm update aligning directional projections with user-specified preference weights (ОұU, ОұD) on the 1-simplex. On LLaMA-2-7B-Chat, Vicuna-7B, and Yi-1.5-6B-Chat, the method achieves MAE of ~2.1 pp on Llama (best among all baselines), preserves general capabilities (GSM8K, TriviaQA, WMT14), and outperforms Prompt-Only, Top-K Head, and BL-PRS baselines.

## Key Contributions

- **Convergent-Divergent Routing:** Identifies "branch points" вҖ” layers where attention heads are shared between utilitarian and deontological reasoning (via ridge regression probes on ETHICS dataset) but FFN units diverge (via token-alignment scoring of FFN vectors with ethical framework indicator words). Only layers meeting both criteria (shared heads + low FFN Jaccard overlap) are selected for intervention.
- **Selective gating for binary control:** At branch-point layers, overwrites FFN unit activations for the non-target framework with those from the target framework. Only ~15вҖ“25% of FFN units are framework-specific per layer, minimizing disruption. This alone boosts target-framework reasoning without explicit prompt conditioning.
- **CSP-based paired direction extraction:** Adapts Common Spatial Patterns from EEG signal processing to the residual stream. Solves a generalized eigenvalue problem on class-specific covariance matrices (with Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage), whitening the background class for numerical stability. Produces sharper utilitarian/deontological separation than PCA (visually verified in Figure 3).
- **Dual Logit Calibration:** A closed-form minimum-в„“вӮӮ-norm update derived via Lagrange multipliers. Enforces softmax(s'_U, s'_D) = (Оұ_U, Оұ_D) by matching the logit difference log(Оұ_D/Оұ_U). Replaces unbounded scalar steering coefficients with bounded preference weights on the 1-simplex.
- Strong experimental results: MAE of 2.1 pp on Llama (11 Оұ_U levels), outperforms all baselines across both Llama and Vicuna. Validated on AITA (14,167+495 samples) and DailyDilemmas. General capabilities (GSM8K exact match, TriviaQA, WMT14 BLEU) largely preserved.

## Methodology

The pipeline has three stages, all operating at inference time with no fine-tuning:

**Stage 1 вҖ” Branch-point localization (CDR):**
- Train ridge regression probes on each attention head's output (from ETHICS deontology/utilitarianism subsets, ~18.2K and ~13.7K samples) using 2-fold cross-validated Spearman correlation. Select heads with correlation > Оі_attn.
- Score FFN vectors by their dot product with the output embedding of ethical framework indicator words ("deontology"/"utilitarianism"). Threshold at Ој + Оі_ffnВ·Пғ to identify framework-specific units.
- Define branch points as layers where: (1) attention heads are shared across both frameworks (S_l = A_l^(U) вҲ© A_l^(D) вү  вҲ…), and (2) FFN unit Jaccard overlap is below threshold П„.

**Stage 2 вҖ” Binary control via gating:**
- At each branch-point layer, compute the deviation from masking shared attention heads: О”^(l) = (m_l вҲ’ 1) вҠҷ z В· W_o^(l).
- For non-target FFN units, overwrite their activations with the target-framework values, preserving all other units. This blocks downstream propagation of non-target signals.

**Stage 3 вҖ” Fine-grained control (CSP + DLC):**
- Record post-FFN residual streams under binary control for both frameworks. Build class-specific feature matrices X_U, X_D.
- Apply CSP: estimate shrinkage covariances, whiten S_D via Cholesky, solve eigenvalue problem on L^{-вҠӨ}S_U L^{-1} to get paired directions (u^(l), d^(l)).
- At each decoding step, compute the closed-form DLC update: О”h_t^(l)* = [(kвҒ»В№ log(Оұ_D/Оұ_U) вҲ’ вҹЁa^(l), h_t^(l)вҹ©) / вҖ–a^(l)вҖ–вӮӮВІ] В· a^(l), where a^(l) = d^(l) вҲ’ u^(l).

**Evaluation metrics:** Two complementary measures вҖ” U_ip (hard-label rate: proportion of generations explicitly naming only one framework) and U_op (token-level probability of "utilitarianism" vs. "deontology" at the "From a ___" decision point, following Santurkar et al., 2023). MAE computed across 11 Оұ_U levels (0%вҖ“100% in 10% increments).

## Results & Highlights

- **Binary control:** All three vanilla backbones show a utilitarian prior (UМ„_op: 62.5% Llama, 80.9% Vicuna, 58.1% Yi-1.5). Binary control reliably polarizes: Llama reaches 83.5%/19.6%, Yi-1.5 reaches 89.1%/15.8% for utilitarian/deontological poles.
- **Fine-grained calibration:** On Llama, MAE = 2.1 pp across all 11 Оұ_U levels, with deviations within Вұ4.75 pp. On Vicuna, MAE is larger (worst 13.68 pp at Оұ_U=80%) but still best among baselines. Yi-1.5 achieves MAE of ~8.4 pp (Appendix G.1).
- **Baselines comparison:** Prompt-Only is largely insensitive on Llama (little separability). Top-K Head over-responds asymmetrically. BL-PRS is consistently second-best but still outperformed by CDR. CSP outperforms cPCA and PLS-DA for direction extraction (Table 4).
- **Ablations:** Single-layer steering (SLY-k) is competitive but worse than full multi-branch-point pipeline. Explicit-prompt representations (EPRM) and down-projection steering (DProj.) show systematic under-calibration. Gating alone (without DLC) already boosts target reasoning (blocking effect, Table 10).
- **General capabilities:** GSM8K exact match, TriviaQA, and WMT14 BLEU scores are largely preserved. Noticeable drops only at extreme Оұ_U вҶ’ 0 on Vicuna and Yi-1.5. Coherence/fluency rated >4.0/5 by GPT-4o. TPOT overhead is minimal (~1.1вҖ“3.5 ms/token added, Table 17).
- **Additional validation:** Tested on DailyDilemmas dataset and Utilitarianism vs. Justice pair вҖ” results qualitatively consistent. Human evaluation (Prolific, 100 samples per model) confirms perspective-justification consistency at 0.81 (Llama) and 0.85 (Vicuna) accuracy.
- **Monotonicity:** Spearman ПҒ = 0.966 (Llama), 0.928 (Vicuna) with near-zero monotonicity violation rates, confirming smooth, predictable control.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The CSP adaptation to transformer residual streams is genuinely creative вҖ” importing a well-established EEG signal processing technique to isolate discriminative directions in LLM representations is non-obvious and well-motivated. The CDR formulation (shared heads + divergent FFN units = branch points) is a clean mechanistic hypothesis. The closed-form DLC derivation is elegant.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Comprehensive experimental setup: 3 backbone models, 3+ datasets, 3 baselines, 3 ablation variants, 3 direction-extraction algorithm comparisons, human evaluation, monotonicity analysis, coherence assessment, computational cost analysis. The paper uses two complementary evaluation metrics (hard-label rate + token-level probability) rather than relying on LLM-as-judge (a known weakness of prior work like Kim et al., 2025).
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Closed-form DLC requires no optimization. All hyperparameters are reported (Table 5). Code and data released. Single A100 GPU, ~38% memory with bfloat16. Random seed fixed at 42.
- **Significance:** Meaningful contribution to inference-time alignment. The 1-simplex preference parameterization is a principled improvement over unbounded scalar steering. The interpretable branch-point identification advances mechanistic understanding of moral reasoning in transformers.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - The evaluation relies on a constrained prompt format ("From a [framework] perspective...") that makes steering measurable but may overestimate real-world controllability. The binary-choice supplementary experiment (Table 16) helps but is limited.
  - Vicuna requires a special two-stage correction (binary pre-polarization before fine-grained control, Appendix G.2) to achieve reasonable calibration вҖ” this suggests the method may be fragile on models with strong priors or unusual internal representations.
  - The ETHICS benchmark itself has been criticized (Hancox-Li & Blili-Hamelin, 2024) for label quality and underspecified prompts. The authors acknowledge this but could engage more substantively.
  - The binary utilitarian/deontological framing, while justified for tractability, is a significant limitation that the authors themselves flag. Whether the approach extends to multi-way moral reasoning remains genuinely uncertain.
  - Attention head probing uses ETHICS (different task formats: acceptability judgment vs. pairwise comparison for deontology vs. utilitarianism), which introduces a task-format confound in the probe comparison across frameworks.

## Strengths

- Elegant mathematical framework: the Lagrange multiplier derivation for DLC is clean and the CSP adaptation is well-motivated.
- Interpretable at multiple levels: branch-point layers, FFN unit subsets, and paired directions are all inspectable.
- Minimal inference overhead: closed-form update at identified layers only; pre-computed directions loaded once.
- Strong empirical performance with comprehensive baselines and ablations.
- Does not require model fine-tuning or maintaining multiple model copies.
- Honest about limitations (binary framing, Vicuna robustness issue, ETHICS benchmark concerns).

## Limitations

- Binary moral framework only (utilitarian vs. deontological; one supplementary experiment with justice). Extension to pluralistic moral values is acknowledged as future work.
- Vicuna needs a special two-stage correction procedure вҖ” the method is not uniformly robust across model architectures.
- The "From a [framework] perspective" prompt constrains evaluation to a narrow format; real-world moral reasoning is rarely this structured.
- ETHICS probe labels may not reliably capture nuanced moral reasoning (acknowledged by authors citing Hancox-Li & Blili-Hamelin, 2024).
- GQA architectures (e.g., Llama-3) untested вҖ” the head-level probing granularity may need rethinking for grouped-query attention.
- Cultural/language robustness is untested; all experiments are in English with Western philosophical frameworks.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can CSP-based direction extraction scale to multi-class settings (e.g., 3вҖ“5 moral frameworks simultaneously)?
- How do branch points shift when moving to larger models (13B, 70B) or GQA architectures?
- Is the utilitarian prior observed in all three base models (UМ„_op > 50%) an artifact of training data, and does prior strength predict calibration difficulty?
- Could the DLC framework be combined with contrastive activation addition (CAA) or ReFT for hybrid steering?
- What happens when the ethical framework prompt is removed entirely вҖ” does the steering still manifest in free-form responses?
