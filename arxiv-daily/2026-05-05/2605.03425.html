FIBER: A Differentially Private Optimizer with Filter-Aware Innovation Bias Correction

arXiv: 2605.03425

Date: 2026-05-05

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03425

一句话总结： FIBER 提出了一种针对时间滤波梯度的新型差分隐私优化器，通过在创新空间（残差流）进行二阶递归去噪并引入闭式滤波感知二阶矩校准项 $A(\omega)\sigma_w^2$，在多个视觉和语言基准上以相同隐私预算显著超越 DP-AdamW、DiSK 等现有最优方法。
TL;DR: FIBER identifies that temporal filtering of DP gradients miscalibrates AdamW's second-moment accumulator — naive bias corrections (subtracting $\sigma_w^2$) are wrong when the filter has already attenuated the noise. The fix has three parts: (1) innovation-space filtering via a second-order $\alpha$-$\beta$ recursion that tracks gradient drift better than EMA, (2) decoupled two-point geometry $(κ,γ)$ from denoising gain $ω$, and (3) a closed-form filter-aware second-moment correction $A(ω)σ_w^2$ where $A(ω) = (2-ω)/(4-3ω)$. On CIFAR-10/100, ImageNet-1k, GLUE, and E2E, FIBER consistently outperforms DP-AdamW, DiSK, DOPPLER, and MF-DP-FTRL under matched privacy budgets, with the largest gains at tight $\varepsilon$.

核心贡献

  • 创新空间二阶递归滤波：不同于 DiSK 对梯度做一阶 EMA 平滑（对应随机游走模型），FIBER 在残差（创新）空间进行滤波并积分回去，对应恒定速度模型，能更好追踪梯度的漂移趋势。
  • 解耦双参数设计：首次将两点观测几何参数 $(κ, γ)$ 与时域去噪增益 $ω$ 完全解耦，允许独立调优外推方式与平滑强度。
  • 闭式滤波感知二阶矩校准：通过 Lyapunov 方程推导出创新滤波器的 DP 噪声衰减因子 $A(ω) = (2-ω)/(4-3ω) \in [1/2, 1]$，从 AdamW 的二阶矩估计中精确减去被滤波衰减后的 DP 噪声贡献，修复了先前方法在滤波场景下的偏差校准失配问题。
  • 通用性：该校正框架适用于任意稳定线性滤波器（通过 $\ell_2$ 增益或 Lyapunov 方程计算 $A$），不仅限于 FIBER 自身的创新滤波器。
  • 广泛的实验验证：在 CV（MNIST, CIFAR-10/100, ImageNet-1k）和 NLP（GLUE, E2E）任务上，以相同隐私预算一致超越现有 DP 优化器，且在严格隐私约束下优势最大。

Key Contributions

  • Innovation-space second-order recursive filtering: Unlike DiSK's first-order EMA smoothing (random-walk latent model), FIBER filters the residual/innovation stream $\nu_t = g_t - \tilde{g}_{t-1}$ and integrates it back, corresponding to a constant-velocity Kalman filter model that better tracks gradient drift. The tied-gain simplification ($\alpha = \beta = \omega$) avoids costly covariance tracking while retaining drift-tracking capability.
  • Decoupled hyperparameter control: First explicit separation of two-point observation geometry $(κ, γ)$ from temporal denoising gain $ω$, enabling independent tuning of extrapolation behavior and smoothing strength. Empirically validated via $(κ, ω)$ landscape sweeps showing smooth, well-separated optima.
  • Closed-form filter-aware second-moment calibration: Derives $A(ω) = (2-ω)/(4-3ω)$ via the discrete Lyapunov equation for the innovation filter's state-space representation. This is the first explicit analysis and practical calibration of AdamW's second-moment statistics under filtered DP noise. The correction subtracts $A(ω)\sigma_w^2$ (rather than the naive $\sigma_w^2$) from the bias-corrected second moment, with a small floor $\epsilon_v$ for numerical stability.
  • Generality: The framework extends to any stable linear temporal filter — the attenuation factor $A$ can be computed via the squared $\ell_2$ gain or Lyapunov equation for general state-space filters. The authors demonstrate this by deriving $A_{\text{state}}(κ) = κ/(2-κ)$ for DiSK's EMA filter, creating the "DiSK-CORR" baseline.
  • Comprehensive evaluation across domains: Consistent improvements on 6 CV settings (MNIST, CIFAR-10/100 train-from-scratch and ViT-small finetuning, ImageNet-1k) and 5 NLP settings (MNLI, QNLI, SST-2, QQP via RoBERTa, E2E via GPT-2), with the largest gains under tight privacy budgets ($\varepsilon \leq 2$).

Methodology

FIBER operates within the standard DP-SGD framework with per-example gradient clipping and Gaussian noise addition. The method has three components:

1. Innovation-space filtering (Component 1): Rather than directly smoothing the gradient state $\tilde{g}_t = (1-κ)\tilde{g}_{t-1} + κ g_t$ (DiSK's EMA), FIBER filters the innovation/residual $\nu_t = g_t - \tilde{g}_{t-1}$ and integrates:

  • $r_t = (1-ω)r_{t-1} + ω\nu_t$ (smoothed residual)
  • $\tilde{g}_t = \tilde{g}_{t-1} + r_t$ (integrated estimate)

This yields a second-order recursion (two poles at $1-ω$) motivated by a constant-velocity Kalman filter with tied gains $α = β = ω$. Eliminating $r_t$ gives $\tilde{g}_t = (2-2ω)\tilde{g}_{t-1} - (1-ω)\tilde{g}_{t-2} + ω g_t$, which can track persistent drift unlike the first-order EMA.

2. Decoupled geometry and denoising (Component 2): The two-point observation parameters $(κ, γ)$ control the lookahead mixing weight $a = (1-κ)/(κγ)$ in the per-example gradient construction. The denoising gain $ω$ controls temporal smoothing independently. This is a key practical advantage over DiSK where a single $κ$ couples both effects.

3. Filter-aware bias correction (Component 3): The core theoretical contribution. When DP noise $w_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_w^2 I_d)$ passes through the innovation filter, its per-coordinate variance is attenuated by factor $A(ω)$ at stationarity. FIBER corrects AdamW's second moment: $\bar{v}_t = \max(\hat{v}_t - A(ω)\sigma_w^2, \epsilon_v)$, where $\hat{v}_t$ is the standard bias-corrected second moment. The attenuation factor is derived via the Lyapunov equation for the 2D state-space system with state matrix $M = \begin{pmatrix} 1-ω & 1-ω \\ -ω & 1-ω \end{pmatrix}$, yielding $A(ω) = (2-ω)/(4-3ω)$.

Privacy: FIBER preserves $(ε, δ)$-DP guarantees by the post-processing theorem — all computations are deterministic functions of the privatized gradients.

Results & Highlights

Vision (training from scratch):

  • MNIST (CNN5): FIBER outperforms DiSK across all $\varepsilon$ values, with the largest gap at $\varepsilon = 0.5$.
  • CIFAR-10 (CNN5): FIBER consistently outperforms DPAdam, DiSK, DOPPLER, and MF-DP-FTRL across privacy budgets. Converges faster and to higher accuracy.
  • CIFAR-100 (WRN): Same pattern — FIBER dominates across $\varepsilon$, especially at low $\varepsilon$.
  • ImageNet-1k (ViT-small): FIBER raises accuracy from 39% (DPAdam) / 42% (DiSK) to 44% at $\varepsilon = 8$.

Vision (fine-tuning):

  • ViT-small on CIFAR-100: FIBER improves over DiSK by +2.0 to +5.1 percentage points as $\varepsilon$ ranges from 8 to 1, with greater gains at stricter privacy.

NLP (GLUE fine-tuning, RoBERTa-base):

  • At $\varepsilon = 1$: FIBER achieves 82.0/88.7/91.5/86.9 on MNLI/QNLI/SST-2/QQP vs. DiSK's 80.7/86.0/91.4/84.2 and DPAdamW's 83.2/87.5/91.5/85.8 (MNLI DPAdamW anomaly noted).
  • At $\varepsilon = 6.7$: FIBER matches or exceeds all baselines.

NLP (text generation, GPT-2 on E2E):

  • At $\varepsilon = 3$: FIBER achieves competitive BLEU (67.57) vs. DiSK (68.35), with slightly different metric trade-offs (better METEOR, NIST).
  • At $\varepsilon = 8$: FIBER and DiSK are close, FIBER slightly better on ROUGE-L and METEOR.

Ablations:

  • Innovation filtering vs. EMA: Innovation filtering wins by +5.2 pp at $\varepsilon = 0.5$ and +2.6 pp at $\varepsilon = 2$; at $\varepsilon = 8$ the gap vanishes (DiSK marginally better).
  • Filter-aware correction: FIBER with full correction outperforms both the no-correction variant and the Tang et al. (2024) bias correction across all $\varepsilon$.
  • Decoupled $\omega$: Optimal $\omega \approx 0.9$ provides independent benefit from $(κ, γ)$.
  • Empirical validation of $A(ω)$: Paired-run differencing confirms the theoretical attenuation factor matches observed variance ratios during actual training.

Compute overhead: Two backward passes per update (same as DiSK), at most 2× DP-AdamW per step, but FIBER achieves better accuracy at matched compute budgets.

The theoretical derivations (Kalman filter connections, Lyapunov equation for attenuation, finite-time bounds) are rigorous and well-grounded. The paper includes comprehensive baselines (DPAdamW, DiSK, DOPPLER, MF-DP-FTRL, DiSK-CORR) and proposes a fair comparison protocol (100 tuning trials per method, staged search for FIBER without increasing total budget). Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The identification that temporal filtering miscalibrates AdamW's second moment is a genuine and previously unmodeled failure mode. The closed-form derivation of $A(ω)$ via Lyapunov equation is clean and the generalization to arbitrary stable linear filters is valuable. The innovation-space filtering viewpoint (constant-velocity vs. random-walk model) provides principled motivation beyond the EMA heuristic.
  • Rigor: Strong. Theoretical claims are supported by propositions with proofs (stability, attenuation factor, finite-time bounds). The empirical assumption audit (cross-term correlation ~0.14, cross-term ratio ~0.15) honestly acknowledges that the signal-noise uncorrelatedness assumption is approximate but shows the violation is modest.
  • Reproducibility: Good. Fixed hyperparameter budget (100 trials per method), standard benchmarks, PyTorch/Opacus/FastDP implementation, and promised code release. Default settings $(κ, γ, ω) = (0.6, 0.7, 0.9)$ work well across tasks. However, the staged search strategy for FIBER (tune $(κ,γ)$ first, then $ω$) may give a slight advantage in practice versus joint search for baselines.
  • Significance: High potential impact. DP training is increasingly critical for production ML, and optimizer-level improvements that are orthogonal to architectural changes are widely applicable. The filter-aware correction framework can retroactively improve existing filtering methods (as shown by DiSK-CORR).
  • Red flags / weaknesses:
  1. Compute cost: Two backward passes per step is a non-trivial overhead. The paper argues for compute-fair comparison but does not always match wall-clock time; at $\varepsilon = 8$, DiSK sometimes matches FIBER with fewer gradient evaluations.
  2. Regime-dependent advantage: Innovation filtering's benefit diminishes at loose privacy budgets ($\varepsilon = 8$) and can be harmful under random-walk gradient dynamics. The paper is transparent about this but it limits the method's universality.
  3. GPT-2 results are mixed: On E2E, FIBER doesn't clearly beat DiSK — it trades off metrics (lower BLEU, higher METEOR). The text generation improvement is less convincing than the CV results.
  4. Cross-term assumption: The correction assumes $\mathbb{E}[s_t n_t] = 0$, which is violated in closed-loop training. The empirical audit shows ~14% correlation, which is non-negligible. The paper acknowledges this but doesn't offer a principled fix.
  5. Hyperparameter sensitivity: Three new hyperparameters $(κ, γ, ω)$ plus $\epsilon_v$ add tuning burden, though defaults work reasonably well.

Strengths

  • Identifies and rigorously addresses a real, previously unmodeled miscalibration in filtered DP optimization.
  • Clean theoretical framework connecting Kalman filtering, Lyapunov equations, and AdamW's second-moment dynamics.
  • Innovation-space filtering provides genuine drift-tracking advantages in high-noise regimes, validated by both synthetic diagnostics and end-to-end training.
  • Decoupled parameterization is a practical improvement over DiSK's coupled design.
  • The correction framework is general — any stable linear filter can benefit from the $A$-factor subtraction.
  • Honest empirical auditing of assumptions (cross-term correlations, variance attenuation validation via paired runs).
  • Consistent improvements across diverse tasks, especially impactful at tight privacy budgets where DP noise dominates.

Limitations

  • Two backward passes per update doubles compute cost relative to DP-AdamW (same as DiSK, but still expensive for large models).
  • Innovation filtering advantage vanishes or reverses under random-walk gradient dynamics or loose privacy budgets ($\varepsilon \geq 8$).
  • The signal-noise uncorrelatedness assumption is approximate ($\rho \approx 0.14$ in practice), and no correction for cross-terms is provided.
  • Added hyperparameters $(κ, γ, ω, \epsilon_v)$ increase tuning complexity, though defaults are provided.
  • NLP text generation results (E2E) show mixed metric trade-offs rather than clear dominance.
  • No evaluation on very large-scale LLM fine-tuning (billions of parameters), where compute overhead of two backward passes would be most costly.

Relevance

本文与差分隐私训练优化直接相关。FIBER 通过修正滤波后 DP 噪声在 AdamW 中的二阶矩偏差，并在创新空间进行漂移追踪滤波，在严格隐私预算下显著提升了 DP 训练性能，对需要隐私保护的视觉和语言模型训练具有重要实用价值。

  • Could the innovation filter's second-order dynamics be extended to higher-order models (constant-acceleration $\alpha$-$\beta$-$\gamma$ filters) for even better drift tracking, or would the added complexity not justify the gains?
  • What is the theoretical relationship between FIBER's correction and the correlated-noise mechanisms (MF-DP-FTRL)? Could they be combined for further improvements?
