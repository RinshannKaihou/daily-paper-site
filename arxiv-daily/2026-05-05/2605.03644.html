AdapShot: Adaptive Many-Shot In-Context Learning with Semantic-Aware KV Cache Reuse

arXiv: 2605.03644

Date: 2026-05-05

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03644

一句话总结： AdapShot用基于输出熵的探针机制按查询难度动态选示例数，配以离线全局KV缓存复用+RoPE旋转位置解耦重编码（PDR）免重复prefill，在LLaMA-3.2-3B/Qwen2.5-7B/14B/32B及openPangu-1B上7个基准平均比DBSA提升约10%、加速4.64×，CoLA上3B模型达0.777(+19.7%)，32B模型仅用~60 shot即86.41%而固定256-shot仅67.91%。
TL;DR: On the CoT Collection (7 tasks: CoLA, QNLI, PIQA, SQuAD v2, SVAMP, GSM8K, MathQA), AdapShot uses an entropy-thresholded probe (τ=0.65) to pick a per-query shot count, reuses an offline global KV cache, and applies a RoPE-based Position Decoupling & Re-encoding (PDR) rotation (Eq. 8) to reorder cached keys losslessly. On Qwen2.5-7B it beats DBSA on all 7 datasets (e.g., SVAMP 0.790 vs 0.737, SQuAD v2 0.465 vs 0.404) and achieves 4.64× average speedup over DBSA (up to 6.33× on MathQA, 9.12× vs 512-shot on MathQA); on LLaMA-3.2-3B CoLA it reaches 0.777 (+19.7% over DBSA†); on Qwen2.5-32B CoLA it hits 86.41% with ~60 shots vs 67.91% for fixed 256-shot. Ablations show removing PDR drops GSM8K 0.649→0.456 (−29.7%).

核心贡献

  • 提出探针式动态预算分配：对语义排序后的示例池，以步长n迭代激活top-(k×n)示例，构造探针上下文 C_k^probe = I ⊕ S_sorted[:k×n] ⊕ q ⊕ P_probe（Eq. 1），仅生成单token（"Yes"/"No"）并计算输出熵 H_k（Eq. 2），当 H_k ≤ τ 停止；用树结构注意力掩码（Eq. 3）实现单次前向的并行多探针验证。
  • 引入语义感知KV缓存：离线对全局示例池S一次性prefill得到所有层/头的 (K,V)（Eq. 4），在线用查询Q与示例Key的注意力分数（Eq. 6）做检索排序，避免额外编码模型并复用KV。
  • 提出位置解耦与重编码（PDR）：利用RoPE的旋转可复合性（Eq. 7），对缓存Key施加额外旋转 ∆=p_new−p_old（Eq. 8）完成位置重编码，无需重算Key，将物理存储顺序与逻辑位置完全解耦，支持语义重排后的无损拼接。
  • 实证覆盖4个模型家族/规模（LLaMA-3.2-3B、Qwen2.5-7B/14B/32B、openPangu-Embedded-1B-V1.1）与7个任务，相比DBSA平均+~10%、4.64×加速；PDR消融GSM8K降29.7%（0.649→0.456）。

Key Contributions

  • A probe-based dynamic budget allocator: iteratively activate the top-(k×n) semantically-ranked examples, build a probe context C_k^probe = I ⊕ S_sorted[:k×n] ⊕ q ⊕ P_probe (Eq. 1), generate a single Yes/No token, and stop when output entropy H_k ≤ τ (Eq. 2); a tree-structured attention mask (Eq. 3) enables single-forward-pass parallel multi-probe verification.
  • A semantics-aware KV cache: offline one-time prefill of the global example pool yields (K,V) for all layers/heads (Eq. 4); online retrieval ranks examples by query–key attention scores (Eq. 6), needing no separate encoder and reusing cached KV.
  • Position Decoupling & Re-encoding (PDR): exploits RoPE's rotational composability (Eq. 7) to apply an extra rotation ∆=p_new−p_old to cached Keys (Eq. 8), recomputing positions without re-prefilling, fully decoupling physical storage order from logical positions for lossless reordering.
  • Evaluation across four model scales/families (LLaMA-3.2-3B, Qwen2.5-7B/14B/32B, openPangu-Embedded-1B-V1.1) and 7 tasks, yielding ~10% average gain and 4.64× speedup over DBSA; PDR ablation drops GSM8K 0.649→0.456 (−29.7 pp).

Methodology

Probe-based dynamic evaluation (§4.1): the global pool S={shot_1,…,shot_N} is first semantically ranked (per §4.2) into S_sorted. Iteration k activates the top k×n examples and builds C_k^probe (Eq. 1); the model generates one token and H_k is computed over {p(Yes),p(No)} (Eq. 2). If H_k ≤ τ the loop stops; else k←k+1. To amortize probe cost, candidate counts {n_1,…,n_m} are evaluated in a single forward pass via a tree-structured mask M (Eq. 3) where probe_i attends only to its first n_i examples, and the minimum count meeting the threshold is chosen.

Semantically-aware KV cache (§4.2): offline, each shot_i is prefilled once to produce (K^(i), V^(i)) across all L layers and H heads (Eq. 4). Online, the query is encoded to Q (Eq. 5) and token-level attention scores against each example's Keys are averaged to a relevance score s^(i) (Eq. 6); examples are ranked by s^(i) and the top-k form S_active, reusing cached KV with no separate retrieval encoder.

Position decoupling & re-encoding (§4.3): because each example is prefilled with positions starting at 0, concatenating reordered cached KV produces a position offset ∆=T_A. RoPE's property (a vector at position p_1 can be moved to p_2 by an extra rotation ∆=p_2−p_1, Eq. 7) is applied to cached Keys via element-wise cos/sin rotation (Eq. 8), correcting positions without recomputation.

Hardware/baselines (§5): experiments on a Huawei Ascend 910B2 NPU cluster (8 NPUs × 65,536 MB each, Kunpeng 920 5250 CPU, 1.5 TB RAM, EulerOS 2.0). Baselines: Zero-shot, Few-shot (4–8), Many-shot (64/128/256/512/1024), and DBSA (Xiao et al. 2025, dynamic block-sparse attention with cached example groups, extended by the authors to these datasets as DBSA†). Evaluated on the CoT Collection (Kim et al. 2023), 7 tasks: NLU (CoLA, QNLI, PIQA), QA (SQuAD v2), math reasoning (SVAMP, GSM8K, MathQA). Metric: Exact Match.

Results & Highlights

  • Cross-task wins on Qwen2.5-7B (Table 1): AdapShot beats DBSA on all 7 datasets — CoLA 0.825 vs 0.657, QNLI 0.825 vs 0.807, PIQA 0.599 vs 0.404, SQuAD v2 0.465 vs 0.404, SVAMP 0.790 vs 0.737, GSM8K 0.649 vs 0.343, MathQA 0.524 vs 0.323 — and beats every fixed Many-shot (256/512/1024) configuration, several of which O.O.M. at 1024.
  • LLaMA-3.2-3B (Table 1): AdapShot reaches 0.777 on CoLA vs 0.649 for DBSA† (+19.7 pp); on QNLI 0.619 vs 0.526; on MathQA 0.524 vs 0.323.
  • Speedup (Table 2, Qwen2.5-7B): average 4.64× vs DBSA (per-dataset 2.64×–6.33×; MathQA 6.33×, GSM8K 4.27×); vs fixed 512-shot average 5.25× (peak 9.12× on MathQA, 7.59× on GSM8K); vs 256-shot average 3.23×.
  • PDR ablation (Figure 4): removing PDR drops GSM8K 0.649→0.456 (−29.7 pp), PIQA by 14.4 pp, SQuAD v2 by 7.1 pp — confirming position-encoding misalignment from reordering is the failure mode PDR fixes.
  • SAKVC ablation (Figure 5): semantically-aware KV cache cuts runtime — GSM8K 19.59s→17.49s (−10.7%), SQuAD v2 −7.6%, PIQA −2.5%.
  • BM25 comparison (Table 3): AdapShot beats best BM25+fixed-shot on GSM8K (+0.193: 0.649 vs 0.456), PIQA (+0.211: 0.599 vs 0.388), SQuAD v2 (+0.047: 0.465 vs 0.418).
  • Scaling (Figure 7, Qwen2.5-32B CoLA): AdapShot reaches 86.41% EM with ~60 shots while fixed 256-shot degrades to 67.91% — larger models are more noise-sensitive and benefit more from adaptive selection.
  • openPangu-Embedded-1B-V1.1 (Table 4/5): AdapShot beats 0/8/128-shot baselines on ARC-Easy (0.704), CoLA (0.650), PIQA (0.271), QNLI (0.694); speedups 1.23×–6.73× vs fixed-shot.
  • Case study (Figure 6): on a SQuAD query, round-1 probes (4/8/12/16 shots) yield entropy 0.6731/0.7023/0.7044/0.6671 (all >0.65 τ); round-2 (20/24/28/32) yields 0.6862/0.6884/0.6232/0.6450, so 28 shots is selected and CoT extracts the correct answer 1868.

Critical Assessment

  • The headline "4.64× speedup over DBSA" and "9.12× over 512-shot" are measured on Ascend 910B2 NPUs; the paper does not report GPU (e.g., A100/H100) numbers, so transferability of the speedup magnitudes to NVIDIA hardware is unverified, and the SAKVC runtime ablation (Figure 5) shows only 2.5–10.7% latency reduction — meaning most of the 4.64× comes from avoiding 256–1024-shot prefilling rather than from the cache strategy alone.
  • The probe mechanism adds overhead (multiple single-token generations per query) that is only partially amortized by tree-structured parallel multi-probe; the paper reports net speedup but does not isolate probe overhead as a function of the maximum shot count or of how many probe rounds are typically needed (the case study needed 2 rounds).
  • The entropy threshold τ=0.65 is fixed in the case study; the paper claims AdapShot is "not sensitive to specific probe threshold values" but does not provide a sensitivity table or curve over τ, so the robustness claim is asserted rather than demonstrated.
  • PDR is tied to RoPE: Eq. 7–8 explicitly use RoPE's rotational property, so the method does not directly apply to models using ALiBi, YaRN-rescaled non-RoPE, or learned absolute positions; this scope limit is not discussed.
  • Evaluation is on CoT Collection–derived tasks (mostly classification/QA/math with Exact Match); there is no evaluation on open-ended generation, code, or long-form reasoning where "confidence" is harder to probe with a Yes/No token.
  • The BM25 comparison (Table 3) uses BM25+256/512-shot, but AdapShot's own retrieval is attention-score–based (Eq. 6), so part of the gain may come from better retrieval rather than adaptive shot count alone; the two factors are not fully decomposed in an ablation.

Strengths

  • Clean two-stage design (offline global KV pool + online probe-and-reorder) that genuinely eliminates redundant prefilling, with the PDR rotation (Eq. 8) making reordering lossless and cheap.
  • Broad evaluation: 4 model scales (3B/7B/14B/32B) plus a 1B embedded model, 7 tasks, and 3 baseline families (fixed Many-shot, DBSA, BM25+fixed-shot).
  • The scaling result (Figure 7) is the most compelling claim: 32B needs only ~60 shots to reach 86.41% while fixed 256-shot collapses to 67.91%, directly supporting the "larger models are more noise-sensitive" thesis.
  • PDR ablation (Figure 4) quantitatively isolates the position-encoding fix as worth up to 29.7 pp on GSM8K, validating it as load-bearing rather than decorative.
  • Honest about the limitation (§7): the method still relies on real retrieved samples and incurs storage/curation cost, and the authors flag "implicit shots" as future work.

Limitations

  • Speedups are NPU-only (Ascend 910B2); no GPU numbers, so the 4.64×/9.12× figures may not transfer.
  • PDR is RoPE-specific; applicability to ALiBi/YaRN/absolute-position models is unaddressed.
  • Probe overhead is not isolated as a function of max shot count or typical probe-round count; the net-speedup accounting is incomplete.
  • The τ-sensitivity robustness claim is asserted ("not sensitive to specific probe threshold values") without a sensitivity table or curve.
  • The Yes/No single-token probe is only validated on classification/QA/math Exact-Match tasks; open-ended generation and code tasks where confidence is harder to probe are not tested.
  • The retrieval (attention-score) and adaptive-shot-count factors are not fully decomposed against the BM25 baseline.
