> **一句话总结：** 本文系统研究了顺序 DPO 训练中先前对齐目标的遗忘规律，发现遗忘并非均匀发生——从部分退化、稳定、重分布到正向迁移都可能出现，其模式由目标间关系、信号强度和训练顺序共同决定，且 Stage 2 梯度与先前目标近正交，排除了"直接梯度对抗"作为主因。

> **TL;DR:** The paper shows that sequential DPO does not uniformly forget previously aligned objectives; instead, preference change ranges from partial degradation to positive transfer depending on objective relationship, signal strength, and training order, and mechanistic diagnostics (near-orthogonal Stage 2 gradients and LoRA adapter movement) rule out direct gradient opposition as the primary driver.

## 核心贡献

- **系统性实证研究**：在四种偏好关系设定（分布冲突 HH-RLHF、多属性交互 HelpSteer2、强安全信号 PKU-SafeRLHF、兼容目标 UltraFeedback）下系统比较顺序 DPO，证明偏好变化是一个从双向遗忘到正向迁移的连续谱，而非单一遗忘模式。
- **引入配对级策略边际分析（pair-level policy-margin analysis）**：用四分位分解（quartile decomposition）追踪单个 chosen–rejected 对的边际变化，揭示聚合指标（reward margin、relative preference accuracy）掩盖的异质性重分布。
- **机制性诊断（mechanistic diagnostics）**：通过梯度余弦相似度和 LoRA adapter 移动量诊断，证明所有 8 个顺序设定中 Stage 2 更新与先前目标近正交（cos ≈ 0, θ ≈ 90°），说明偏好变化并非由直接梯度对抗驱动，而更可能是分布漂移、信号失衡或间接参数移动。
- **挑战"顺序对齐必然灾难性遗忘"的假设**：强调未来顺序对齐 pipeline 应显式考虑目标兼容性和信号强度。

## Key Contributions

- A systematic empirical study of sequential alignment with DPO across four preference regimes, showing preference change spans a spectrum from bidirectional forgetting to positive transfer, determined by objective relationship and signal strength rather than by sequential training alone.
- A pair-level policy-margin analysis with quartile decomposition, exposing heterogeneous redistribution hidden by aggregate reward margins and relative preference accuracy.
- Mechanistic diagnostics (gradient cosine similarity + raw LoRA adapter movement) showing near-orthogonal update directions across all settings, suggesting indirect mechanisms rather than direct gradient opposition.
- Empirical evidence that aggregate metrics can mask where sequential DPO actually changes preferences, motivating finer-grained evaluation.

## Methodology

- **Base model**: Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct with LoRA adapters (instruction-tuned variant for a realistic aligned starting point).
- **Sequential DPO**: Two stages, each resuming from the previous adapter checkpoint; the Stage 2 DPO reference is the final Stage 1 adapter (shifting training reference). For evaluation, a fixed base model is always used as reference so reward margins stay on a consistent scale across stages.
- **Four preference regimes** (both A→B and B→A orderings):
  - Distributional conflict — HH-RLHF (helpful-base vs harmless-base); <1% prompt overlap, no flipped labels.
  - Multi-attribute interaction — HelpSteer2 (verbosity vs coherence).
  - Strong safety signal — PKU-SafeRLHF (harmless produces much larger margins than HH-RLHF).
  - Compatible objectives — UltraFeedback (instruction-following vs honesty).
- **Aggregate metrics**: DPO-relative reward margin (β·[Δθ − Δref]) and relative preference accuracy (fraction of pairs where policy improves the chosen-over-rejected gap vs the base reference, ties get half credit).
- **Pair-level diagnostics**: length-normalised policy margin per pair m_i = log π(yw) − log π(yl); per-pair change Δ_i = m_i^(2) − m_i^(1); quartile decomposition sorts pairs by Stage 1 margin into Q1 (hard/low-confidence) … Q4 (easy/high-confidence).
- **Mechanistic diagnostics**: gradient cosine similarity between live Stage 2 DPO gradient and a fixed previous-objective reference gradient (over LoRA params only); raw LoRA adapter displacement ∥S2−S1∥/∥S1∥ and direction cosine cos(S1, S2−S1).
- **Deterministic evaluation**: scoring fixed chosen/rejected responses via log-probabilities (no generation); held-out pairs used for pair-level analysis.

## Results & Highlights

- **No uniform forgetting**: In HH-RLHF, later-stage training measurably degrades the previous objective but it stays above the neutral baseline (e.g., helpful→harmless: helpful margin 0.731→0.228, −68.8%; harmless→helpful: harmless margin 0.925→0.707, −23.6%) — partial, not catastrophic, forgetting.
- **Strong safety signal is resistant**: In PKU-SafeRLHF, harmless margin actually *increases* after helpful training (14.071→16.930, accuracy held at 95.9%), because the harmless signal is so strong.
- **Compatible objectives produce positive transfer**: In UltraFeedback, *neither* ordering shows aggregate forgetting — both IF and honesty margins grow after the second stage (IF 8.195→15.114; honesty 5.331→13.114). Even after Stage 1 IF training alone, honesty already reaches 7.471 margin / 84.3% accuracy, indicating substantial compatibility.
- **Aggregate metrics hide heterogeneity**: Pair-level analysis shows some pairs lose margin while others improve; mean Δ alone is misleading. E.g., HH helpful→harmless mean Δ is +0.042 with only 39.3% improving, masking broad helpfulness degradation.
- **Quartile decomposition flips direction across regimes**:
  - HH helpful→harmless: degradation concentrates in Q4 (high-confidence pairs lose most; Q4−Q1 = +0.092).
  - PKU harmless→helpful: high-confidence harmless pairs are *reinforced* monotonically (Q1 −0.032 → Q4 −0.151) — opposite direction.
  - UltraFeedback: highest-confidence pairs benefit most (IF→honesty Q4 = −0.805; honesty→IF Q4 = −1.027) — again opposite to HH.
- **Near-orthogonal gradients across all 8 orderings**: gradient cosine from −0.007 to +0.028 (angles 88.4°–90.4°); Stage 2 moves only 4.5–16.1% of the Stage 1 adapter norm; adapter direction cosine ≈ 0 in every case. This rules out direct gradient opposition as the primary mechanism and points to distributional drift / signal imbalance / indirect representational movement.

## Authors & Credibility

- Authors are from the Network Analysis and Social Influence Modelling (NASIM) Lab, School of Physics Maths and Computing (University of Western Australia), School of Psychological Science (UWA), and School of Computing (Macquarie University).
- The work cites the standard DPO/IPO/SimPO/RLHF literature (Rafailov et al. 2023; Azar et al. 2024; Meng et al. 2024; Bai et al. 2022; Ouyang et al. 2022) and positions itself against recent large-scale post-training forgetting studies (Harmon et al. 2025; Li et al. 2026).
- Methodology is pre-registered in spirit: fixed base reference, both orderings per regime, deterministic log-prob evaluation, full hyperparameters in appendix.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (medium)**: The framing ("sequential DPO forgetting is not uniform") is a useful, well-supported empirical correction to a common assumption, but the individual building blocks (DPO, LoRA, gradient cosine diagnostics, pair-level margin analysis) are standard. The quartile-decomposition lens and the systematic four-regime comparison are the genuinely fresh contributions.
- **Rigor (good)**: Both orderings per regime, fixed base-model evaluation reference, deterministic scoring, n=5–8 logging steps for gradient diagnostics, transparent reporting of margins/accuracy/Δ. Limitations section is candid.
- **Reproducibility (medium–good)**: Base model, LoRA, hyperparameters, and dataset construction are reported (Appendix A/D). However, full fine-tuning vs LoRA, seed counts, and exact evaluation-pair counts per regime (n varies 422–1000) should be checked against the appendix before reproducing. LoRA-only is a real scope limit.
- **Significance (medium)**: Practically relevant for multi-stage post-training pipelines (safety → helpfulness → instruction-following), but the findings are largely confirmatory/diagnostic rather than prescriptive — the paper offers no mitigation method, only the recommendation to account for objective compatibility.
- **Red flags / caveats**:
  - Mechanistic diagnostics are restricted to LoRA-parameter space; raw LoRA parameter space is not invariant to rescaling, so the adapter-movement "near-orthogonal" verdict is explicitly labelled *supporting* rather than primary evidence.
  - Gradient cosine measured against a *fixed reference batch*, not the live Stage 1 data distribution — could underestimate conflict.
  - Single model family/scale (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct); generalisation to 70B, full-parameter fine-tuning, or other families is untested.
  - The "shifting training reference but fixed evaluation reference" design is methodologically defensible but slightly unusual; readers should note margins are not directly comparable to standard DPO papers that use the SFT model as both references.
  - No held-out generative evaluation (only log-prob scoring on fixed pairs), so downstream behavioural effects (e.g., actual refusal/helpfulness trade-offs in generations) are not measured.

## Strengths

- Clean, factorial experimental design: four regimes × two orderings, with cross-stage evaluation on *all* objectives after every stage.
- Pair-level + quartile analysis is a genuinely useful evaluation lens that other sequential-alignment work has missed; it explains *why* aggregate accuracy can look stable while preferences are being internally redistributed.
- Mechanistic diagnostics directly test the most natural hypothesis (direct gradient conflict) and falsify it, narrowing the explanation space to drift / signal imbalance / indirect movement.
- Honest about evidence strength — explicitly calls gradient/adapter findings "suggestive rather than causal."
- Compatible-objective regime (UltraFeedback) provides a positive-transfer baseline, which makes the conflict regimes more interpretable.

## Limitations

- LoRA-only at 8B scale; full-parameter and larger-model dynamics may differ.
- Mechanistic diagnostics limited to LoRA gradient space and a fixed reference batch; does not capture activation-level or full-model dynamics.
- Four regimes is a reasonable but not exhaustive coverage; factuality, reasoning, refusal-style, and domain-specific safety objectives are absent.
- Consistent hyperparameters across objectives (for control) may not reflect objective-specific optimal schedules.
- No generative/behavioural evaluation — only log-prob margins on fixed pairs, so real-world deployment effects are inferred, not measured.
- No proposed mitigation or training method; the contribution is diagnostic.

## Relevance

- **LLM training / post-training pipelines**: Directly relevant to multi-stage alignment (safety → helpfulness → instruction-following), a near-universal industrial post-training pattern. The finding that compatible objectives (UltraFeedback IF/honesty) give positive transfer while distributional-conflict objectives (HH-RLHF) give partial forgetting gives practitioners a principled ordering heuristic.
- **Reliability / safety**: The PKU-SafeRLHF result — that a strong safety signal is resistant to later helpfulness training — is reassuring for safety retention, but the converse (helpfulness degrades after safety training, and margin can rise while accuracy falls) is a subtle reliability warning: aggregate reward margins can mislead about whether an objective is actually retained.
- **Interpretability**: The gradient-cosine and adapter-movement diagnostics are a lightweight mechanistic tool applicable to other sequential-finetuning studies; the "near-orthogonal but still degrades" result is a concrete data point for interpretability work on *indirect* representational drift.
- **Agents**: Less directly applicable (no agent/multi-turn evaluation), but the pair-level redistribution lens could be reused to study skill forgetting in sequentially trained agent policies.
- **Evaluation methodology**: The quartile-decomposition technique is a transferable diagnostic for any preference-optimisation setting where aggregate metrics are suspected to hide sample-level heterogeneity.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the near-orthogonal gradient findings hold under full-parameter DPO and at 70B scale, where the effective parameter space is far larger?
- Would measuring gradient cosine against the *live* Stage 1 data distribution (rather than a fixed reference batch) reveal hidden conflict currently masked by the protocol?
- Can the pair-level/quartile signal be used *during* training (e.g., adaptive stopping or per-quartile reweighting) to actively prevent degradation of high-confidence pairs?
- How do these patterns extend to on-policy or iterative DPO variants (e.g., SimPO, KTO), where the reference and data distribution shift more aggressively?
- Does the "margin rises while accuracy falls" divergence (PKU helpful→harmless) generalise, and what does it imply about using reward margin as a proxy for objective retention?
- What is the activation-level (not just parameter-level) signature of the indirect drift that the LoRA diagnostics cannot capture?
- How do three-or-more-stage pipelines compound these effects — is the spectrum of outcomes still bounded, or does later-stage forgetting accelerate?
