> **一句话总结：** 本文提出单神经元干预的"控制窗口定律"——一次写入剂量被规约到单一控制坐标 `cos θv`（残差流与写入方向的夹角对齐度），沿一条普适饱和曲线 `cos θv = g/√(1+g²)` 演化（其中 `g = t/B`，`B = ‖rL‖/‖v‖` 为"相干预算"）；只有当行为触发阈值低于"坍塌天花板"时单神经元才能相干控制行为，而该天花板可仅由权重和一次通用前向传播预测，作者据此在 8 个架构上验证了普适性，并揭示"杠杆 ≠ 可达性"：梯度归因恰恰把真正的控制器排在最低（4/4 vs 0/4）。

> **TL;DR:** A single-neuron dose along a feed-forward write direction reduces to one control coordinate `cos θv` that follows a universal saturation curve in units of a coherence budget `B = ‖rL‖/‖v‖`. Coherent control exists iff a behavior trigger lies below a collapse ceiling predictable from the weights and one generic forward pass (MAE 0.14 on 15 held-out neurons). The authors show local gradient attribution anti-predicts control (4/4 vs 0/4 controllers recovered), that coherent refusal bypass ≠ actionable harm reach (only 3/6 Llama pivots deliver real harm), and unify super-weights, refusal neurons, arithmetic gates, and refusal directions as points on one window.

## 核心贡献

- **控制窗口定律（Control-Window Law）：** 将任何单神经元剂量的效果归约为单一控制坐标 `cos θv = cos∠(rL+tv, v̂)`，该坐标沿普适饱和曲线 `cos θv = g/√(1+g²)`（`g=t/B`）演化，在 6 个架构上吻合到小数点后两位。相干控制当且仅当行为触发阈值低于坍塌天花板 `cos θv^trig(τ) < cos θv^coll` 时存在。
- **坍塌天花板可预测性：** 坍塌系数 `m*=t*/B` 由残差流的参与率（participation ratio）决定，约 1.4–1.7（Qwen/Llama），Gemma 为 0.31（近秩一残差导致脆弱）。在 15 个留出神经元上做前瞻预测，天花板平均绝对误差 0.14，主体层（相对深度<0.8）约 0.07。
- **"杠杆 ≠ 可达性"（Leverage Is Not Reach）：** 局部梯度归因反向预测控制能力——真正的控制器写入方向偏离读出轴、一阶梯度近零，故梯度把它们排到最末；一个仅前向传播的对比筛选（contrastive screen）+窗口定律过滤能恢复梯度遗漏的 4/4 控制器（vs 梯度 0/4）。
- **类型化（typed）而非标量的安全结果：** 在拒绝（refusal）这一最难情形上，相干绕过（coherent bypass）与严格可操作可达（strict-actionable reach）解耦：神经元可在流畅、切题但无操作内容的文本中翻转拒绝；6 个审计的 Llama 拒绝枢纽中仅 3 个真正产生有害合规，且仅在较晚的 rollout 时刻（τ≈96–128）显现。
- **统一既往发现：** 将 super-weights、已发表拒绝神经元 L11/F4258、算术神经元、refusal direction、梯度归因等都作为同一窗口上的点；并修正了"固定剂量"导致的误报（如 L11/F4258 在预算单位剂量下变得相干）。
- **交付物：** 两轴安全诊断（coherent-bypass margin × strict-actionable potency），供模型拥有者作为白盒稳健性度量并驱向零。

## Key Contributions

- **A one-coordinate control-window law.** Any dose reduces to the alignment `cos θv` between the residual and the write direction, driven by a universal saturation curve matched to two decimal places across six architectures. Coherent control exists iff the behavior trigger lies below a weights-predictable collapse ceiling.
- **Prospective ceiling prediction with three closed-window mechanisms.** Ceiling predicted from weights + one generic forward pass at MAE 0.14 on 15 held-out neurons. Closed windows have three distinct mechanisms: geometric close (low ceiling), drive-capped close (post-FFN normalization caps a neuron's own reach, e.g. Gemma-2/3), and decoupled close at extreme depth (too few layers left to propagate the write).
- **Generality across behavior classes.** The same coordinate governs benign mode switches (language routing, `+→×` operator choice) and refusal, with a behavior-dependent trigger rising from ~0.3 for mode switches to ~0.45 for refusal and ~0.6 for task-framing.
- **Leverage is not reach.** Local gradient attribution anti-predicts control: true controllers write off the readout axis and carry near-zero first-order gradient; a forward-only contrastive screen recovers 4/4 controllers that the gradient ranks lowest vs 0/4 on matched compute — a backward-pass-free recipe for finding gates.
- **Typed, not scalar, intervention success on refusal.** Coherent bypass and strict-actionable reach separate; a neuron can flip refusal in fluent, on-task text with no actionable content. Genuine actionable reach appears for only 3 of 6 audited Llama pivots, and only at later rollout horizons.
- **A measurement discipline that corrects the record.** Budget-calibrated dosing recovers controllable neurons that fixed-magnitude steering misreports as destructive; super weights, refusal neurons, arithmetic and framing gates all become points on one window.

## Methodology

The intervention is a scalar dose `t` along the down-projection column `v = W_down[:,i]` of neuron `i` at layer `L`: `r(t) = rL + t·v`. Because downstream layers consume the residual through a normalization that discards magnitude and keeps direction, the readout reduces to a single signed coordinate `cos θv = cos∠(r(t), v̂)`.

Since the write direction is nearly orthogonal to the residual, dosing builds a right triangle whose geometry gives the universal drive `cos θv = g/√(1+g²)` where `g = t/B` is the dimensionless coupling and `B = ‖rL‖/‖v‖` is the coherence budget (the single-neuron FFN-to-skip ratio). The output collapses at a fixed multiple `m*·B` of the budget, giving a collapse ceiling `cos θv^coll = m*/√(1+m*²)` where `m*` is an order-one, architecture-family coefficient set by the residual's participation ratio.

The control-window law (Eq. 4) states: coherent single-neuron control exists iff `cos θv^trig(τ) < cos θv^coll`, where the trigger is behavior- and rollout-dependent. Candidate controllers are found via the forward-only contrastive primitive of Liu et al. 2026 (rank neurons by activation difference `|Δa|` between matched harmful/harmless prompts, then verify causality by dosing). A five-part measurement contract governs every window: budgeted dose in units of B, signed controlling branch, behavior's own rollout horizon `cos θv^trig(τ)`, script-matched coherence gate, and a content gate separating non-refusal from strict-actionable. Three judges of increasing strictness score refusal (first-token non-refusal, full-reply coherence-gated bypass via Claude-Haiku, register-aware four-way strict-actionable judge). Eight open-weight architectures are tested (Qwen, Llama, Mistral, Phi-3.5, Gemma-3); each fits on one A100-40GB GPU.

## Results & Highlights

- **Universal drive:** `cos θv` matched the geometric prediction to two decimal places at every dose across six architectures (range ≤0.02), confirming orthogonality of write-vs-residual and linear superposition.
- **Collapse universality:** Rescaling by each model's single constant `m*` collapses all six architecture curves onto one near-vertical cliff at x=1. `m*` ≈ 1.37–1.65 for Qwen/Llama, 1.79 for Mistral, 1.38 for Phi, and 0.31 for Gemma-3 (5× more fragile due to near-rank-one residual, PR=1.1). Cross-architecture: `m*` tracks participation ratio (Spearman 0.67).
- **Prospective ceiling test:** On 15 committed-before-dosing held-out neurons, ceiling MAE 0.14 (95% CI 0.09–0.23), ~0.07 in bulk layers; verdict correct on 11/15 (vs 10/15 majority-class baseline, so the continuous ceiling error is the real result, not the verdict count). No neuron met the pre-registered falsifier.
- **Gradient vs. causal filter (Qwen2.5-3B):** Gradient top-20 recovered 0/4 known controllers and was inert 15/20; the causal filter recovered 4/4 and surfaced the strongest single-neuron bypass L21/F2722 at 20/24 (which produced no genuine harm on either sign under content audit).
- **Coherent bypass ≠ actionable reach:** On the Llama refusal cohort (n=24/dose), bypass and actionable axes decouple across the population. L16/F6950: bypass 0.62 [0.43,0.79] vs actionable 0.00 [0.00,0.14] (non-overlapping). L15/F9635 matches the 0.62 bypass at only 0.17 actionable. Genuine actionable reach only for 3/6 Llama pivots (L11/F4258, L10/F4789 reach 0.88).
- **Two-axis safety diagnostic:** Qwen opens bypass windows (0.44–0.60) but yields ≈0 actionable (first-token template flips / topic setup); Llama has a genuine actionable subset (peak 0.88, committing late at τ≈96–128); Gemma is closed on both axes.
- **Rollout structure:** Operator switch L19/F13312 has a hard 8-token onset floor (cannot be made earlier by dose); language routing L25/F347 is token-local (τ*=1) with diverging onset near the trigger boundary.
- **Off-axis seeding mechanism:** Confirmed controllers write off the readout axis (cosine 0.02–0.04, within random-pair band); first-order gradient α≈0; trigger is a finite-amplitude basin crossing, not smooth. Naive K>1 stacking conflicts (non-composability), confirming basin-selection rather than behavior-computation.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high):** The claim of a falsifiable, weights-predictable law for *when* single-neuron steering works is genuinely new. Prior work (Rimsky, Arditi, Kazemi, Herring) catalogued that single neurons/directions *can* flip behaviors but offered no theory of the dose or of controllability prediction. Unifying super-weights, refusal neurons, arithmetic gates, and refusal directions on one coordinate is a real synthesis.
- **Rigor (mixed-strong):** Pre-registered falsifier, blind verdict scoring, Wilson 95% intervals, bootstrap CIs, and matched-compute comparisons are commendable. However, the headline "11/15 verdict" only marginally beats a 10/15 majority-class baseline — the author is honest about this and correctly pivots to the continuous ceiling MAE (0.14) as the real prospective result. The "4/4 vs 0/4" gradient comparison is on a single task (Qwen2.5-3B bypass), a small n that invites confirmation on more behaviors/models. The trigger value is admitted to be measured, not predicted — so the law is asymmetric: tight on collapse, loose on control.
- **Reproducibility (high):** All public open-weight models, one A100-40GB per model, two-forward-pass discovery primitive, equations fully specified, per-neuron table (Table 10/13) in appendix. The measurement contract is explicit. Low barrier to reproduction.
- **Significance (medium-high, conditional):** If the law holds at scale (the unproven companion paper), it changes how the field should do activation steering (dose in units of B, not fixed magnitude) and discovery (forward-only contrastive screen, not gradient). For safety, the bypass-vs-actionable decoupling is a useful corrective to over-claiming jailbreak severity. But much rests on the yet-unshown model-wide scaling.
- **Red flags:**
  - Single author, not yet peer-reviewed; several claims depend on a "companion paper" not yet in hand (e.g., model-scale screening, the perturbation-series theory).
  - `m*` family-dependence and the Gemma outlier mean the "universal" collapse curve requires a per-family calibration constant — universal up to one order-one parameter.
  - The theory strictly covers FFN write directions in pre-norm architectures; attention side and post-FFN-normalization equivalence are partial (Gemma's drive-capped close is real but the single-constant fit is R²=0.25 for Phi).
  - Refusal conclusions are on a curated 6-pivot cohort (n=24/dose) — small, and "genuine actionable reach = 3/6" could shift with more neurons.
  - The framing is theory-first; the law is "close to a definition" by the author's own admission, with empirical content resting entirely on ceiling predictability, which is the stronger but not the safety-relevant edge.

## Strengths

- First predictive (rather than anecdotal) theory of single-neuron controllability, with a pre-registered, falsifiable test.
- The "leverage is not reach" insight is sharp and actionable: it explains and predicts why gradient attribution and fixed-dose steering systematically mis-rank neurons, and offers a forward-only fix.
- Bypass-vs-actionable-reach decoupling is a genuinely useful safety-measurement correction — prevents conflating fluent non-refusal with real harm.
- Excellent measurement hygiene: budgeted dosing, branch-resolved ceilings, behavior-matched rollout horizons, Wilson intervals, honest negatives.
- Clean unification of scattered prior single-neuron findings into one inequality with corrections (e.g., recovering L11/F4258 as coherent under budgeted dose).
- Defensive two-axis diagnostic is a concrete deliverable for model owners.

## Limitations

- **Trigger is measured, not predicted.** The law constrains collapse (ceiling) far more tightly than control (trigger); a quantitative trigger theory is explicitly the main open problem. An attempted subthreshold-susceptibility predictor failed (Appendix B.3).
- **Curated, small controller set.** Two-route typology rests on a handful of controllers; model-scale validation deferred to a companion paper not yet available.
- **`m*` not truly universal.** Requires per-family calibration (1.4–1.8 for distributed-residual families, 0.31 for Gemma's near-rank-one residual); Spearman 0.67 is not significant at n=8 architectures.
- **Architecture scope.** Covers FFN write directions in pre-norm models; post-FFN normalization (Gemma-2/3) breaks the pinning-injection equivalence; attention and read sides untested.
- **Low-dimensional behaviors only.** The framework requires a discrete basin; behaviors without one have no trigger and no window — so single-neuron decision control is non-composable and peaks at K=1.
- **Small n on the central safety claim.** Refusal cohort is 6 pivots × 24 prompts/dose; the "3/6 genuine actionable" split is within sampling noise for some finer comparisons (0.88 vs 0.75 explicitly not read as ordered).
- **Single author, not peer-reviewed.** Several load-bearing extensions (companion screening paper, perturbation-series theory) are cited but not delivered here.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the control-window law hold at model scale — i.e., can the forward-only contrastive screen + window filter audit thousands of neurons across a frontier model, and what fraction of refusal capability actually sits in window-open vs. window-closed neurons?
- Can the trigger value `cos θv^trig(τ)` be predicted from weights rather than measured, closing the asymmetry between the predictable ceiling and the behavior-set trigger? The subthreshold-susceptibility predictor failed — what class of predictor (e.g., basin-curvature, second-order Hessian `β = vᵀHv` in branch-resolved form) might work?
- How does the law extend to attention-side write directions and to weight-edit interventions (ROME-style), which the paper claims should also occupy the same window but leaves as future work?
- Is the bypass-vs-actionable-reach decoupling stable across more refusal pivots, larger models, and non-English behaviors, or does actionable reach grow with scale (making the decoupling a small-model artifact)?
- For your reliability interests: does the dose-window structure interact with quantization/precision — e.g., does a quantized model's residual participation ratio shift `m*` enough to open previously-closed windows or vice versa?
- The "off-axis seeding" mechanism implies true controllers are first-order-invisible; what does this imply for SAE feature dictionaries that rely on reconstruction (a form of attribution) — are SAE-identified features systematically the collapse-prone high-leverage ones?
