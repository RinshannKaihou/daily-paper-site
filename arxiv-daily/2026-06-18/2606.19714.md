> **一句话总结：** AURA 把"对 LLM 裁判的信任"当作一个潜在量，通过表示学习、信任更新、保守传输式证据传播和选择性人工核验构成迭代精修闭环，在仅用约 3% 人工核验预算的情况下，将配对式 LLM-as-a-judge 与人类偏好的一致性准确率显著提升（弱裁判如 Qwen/Mistral 上提升 20+ 个百分点，强裁判如 GPT-5.4 上提升约 3 个百分点），并给出基于 Lyapunov 函数的收敛性与传输质量守恒分析。

> **TL;DR:** AURA reframes LLM-as-a-judge auditing as adaptive positive-unlabeled refinement: rather than assuming a clean trusted subset exists upfront, it iteratively (1) trains a task-specific encoder on frozen reward-model comparison features, (2) fuses model/local/anchor/transport evidence into a soft human-consistency responsibility via a trust logit, (3) propagates anchor evidence to uncertain examples through a sparse, budgeted conservative-transport step, and (4) actively queries humans only on the most informative uncertain cases. On MT-Bench/Chatbot Arena pairwise data across 5 judges, AURA lifts adjusted accuracy over the raw judge and over logistic-regression/MLP/random-forest/label-propagation/nnPU baselines while using far fewer verified labels (e.g., Qwen coding 53.7%→77.7% with ~279 verified examples vs 1,777 for baselines); gains are large on weak judges (~+24 pts) and modest on strong judges (~+3 pts). A Lyapunov-based convergence theorem, Lipschitz analysis, mass conservation, and an expected-error reduction guarantee under a cluster assumption are provided.

## 核心贡献

- **问题重构**：将 LLM-as-a-judge 审计形式化为"在有限人工核验下的自适应人类一致性精修"问题。信任（trust）与不确定组不是预先固定的，而是在证据累积中逐步更新，区别于标准 PU 学习和单纯裁判校准。
- **紧凑可解释的状态变量**：围绕软一致性责任 q、锚点置信度 a、携带的传输流入 m 三类状态变量组织方法，每一轮迭代执行 `(q,a,m) → (z,p) → (q̃,a') → (q',a',m')`。
- **多源信任更新**：用信任 logit 融合编码器分数 p、局部一致性 r^loc、锚点支持 r^anc 与上一轮传输流入 m 四类证据，并通过硬约束（种子集 Ps、已核验集 V⁺/V⁻）施加边界。
- **保守传输式证据传播**：借鉴 partial optimal transport，用稀疏、预算化的传播规则把可靠正/负锚点的证据注入不确定样本 M⁽ᵗ⁾，仅正流入 m⁺ 被前向携带，保证总传输质量等于预算 B_t（质量守恒）。
- **选择性核验与停止准则**：综合不确定性、信息量、距离已核验集的远近来构造查询分数 H_i，只对最有信息量的不确定样本请求人工标签；用 q/flip/m/ver 四个变化量阈值作为停止条件。
- **理论分析**：给出信任更新的 Lipschitz 连续性、外层迭代的 Lyapunov 收敛定理（含子序列自洽极限点）、传输算子的质量守恒引理，以及在 cluster 假设下正传输使期望平方误差严格下降的偏差缩减定理。
- **综合评测**：在合成（CD/DD 噪声 × SCAR/SAR 核验）和真实（MT-Bench + Chatbot Arena，5 个裁判模型 × 3 题型）数据上，对照 logistic/MLP/随机森林/标签传播/nnPU 共 5 个基线，并给出全因子消融、运行时与内存、敏感性分析。

## Key Contributions

- Reframes LLM-as-a-judge auditing as adaptive human-consistency refinement under limited verification, where trustworthy and uncertain groups are progressively updated rather than fixed in advance.
- Organizes the method around compact, interpretable state variables: soft responsibility q, anchor confidence a, and carried positive transport inflow m.
- A trust-update module fusing four evidence sources (model score, local consistency, anchor support, previous transport inflow) via a trust logit with hard constraints on seed and verified sets.
- A conservative, sparse, budgeted transport-based inflow update inspired by partial optimal transport that propagates reliable anchor evidence to uncertain comparisons while conserving total transported mass.
- A selective verification policy that queries humans only on the most uncertain/informative cases, plus a rule-based stopping criterion monitoring responsibility/flip/mass/verified-set stability.
- Theoretical guarantees: Lipschitz continuity of the trust operator, Lyapunov-based global convergence to a self-consistent limit point, mass conservation of the transport step, and an expected squared-error reduction result under a latent cluster assumption.
- Comprehensive evaluation on synthetic (CD/DD noise × SCAR/SAR verification) and real pairwise data (MT-Bench + Chatbot Arena, 5 judges × 3 question types) against logistic regression, MLP, random forest, label propagation, and nnPU baselines, plus full-factorial ablation, runtime/memory, and latent-geometry sensitivity analyses.

## Methodology

AURA operates on a pairwise dataset `D = {(Q_i, R_i1, R_i2, J_i^L, J_i^H)}` where the LLM judge gives a strict preference `J_i^L ∈ {1,2}` (ties excluded) and human labels, when available, give `J_i^H`. The learned target is the human-consistency label `y_i = 1{J_i^L = J_i^H}`. A frozen reward-model encoder `E_RM` (Skywork-Reward-Llama-3.1-8B-v0.2) extracts a fixed directional comparison feature `x_i = E_RM(Q_i, R_i^w) − E_RM(Q_i, R_i^ℓ)`. A small trusted positive seed set `P_s`, growing verified sets `V⁺, V⁻`, a refinement pool `U`, and a waiting pool `W⁽ᵗ⁾` are maintained.

Each outer iteration runs four blocks:

1. **Encoder training.** A two-layer GELU encoder maps `x_i` to a latent `z_i` and a logit `ℓ_i` with sigmoid score `p_i`. The loss `L_enc = L_ver + λ_soft L_soft + λ_geo R_geo + λ_anchor R_anchor` combines verified BCE, confidence-aware soft supervision on high-confidence unverified examples (using current `q_i` as soft labels), local geometric regularization on a kNN graph in feature space, and anchor-preserving regularization that penalizes drift of high-confidence examples across rounds.

2. **Trust update.** A trust logit `u_i = λ_p log(p/(1−p)) + λ_loc(2r^loc−1) + λ_anc(2r^anc−1) + λ_m(2m−1) + β_0` fuses model evidence, neighborhood-level local consistency, anchor-support evidence (cosine to positive vs negative prototypes), and carried transport inflow. Sigmoid produces `q̃_i`, with hard constraints forcing seeds/verified examples to fixed values; anchor confidence `a_i` is updated similarly.

3. **Conservative transport.** For the uncertain set `M⁽ᵗ⁾ = {j : q̃_j ∈ I_amb}`, positive/negative anchor pools `B⁺, B⁻` are formed. Transport affinities `Γ_ij` (top-K truncated, modulated by a local relation-consistency score `c_j`) propagate bounded mass; aggregated inflows `m_j^+, m_j^-` update responsibilities in opposite directions. Only positive inflow is carried to the next round. Total transported mass equals the budget `B_t⁺` (mass conservation, Lemma 4).

4. **Selective verification.** A query score `H_i` combining uncertainty `U`, information `I` (entropy-like), distance-to-verified `D`, and inflow `M` selects top-b candidates for human review; returned labels grow `V⁺` or `V⁻`, pending ones stay in `W`. Stopping monitors `Δ_q, Δ_flip, Δ_m, Δ_ver`.

The convergence analysis constructs a surrogate Lyapunov function `V⁽ᵗ⁾ = L_enc + ρ_q‖q⁽ᵗ⁾−q⁽ᵗ⁻¹⁾‖ + ρ_m‖m⁽ᵗ⁾−m⁽ᵗ⁻¹⁾‖`, shows block-wise sufficient decrease (Lemma 3) up to summable verification drift, and establishes that every limit point satisfies a self-consistency fixed-point condition (Theorem 2). Theorem 3 shows that under a latent cluster assumption (Assumption 5), positive-only transport strictly reduces expected squared error `E‖q⁽ᵗ⁺¹⁾−y‖²` by a Gap⁺ term minus a second-order budget term.

## Results & Highlights

**Synthetic (640 examples/run, 5 runs, original accuracy 73.75%):** Across all four noise × verification settings AURA raises adjusted accuracy to 84.19%–85.41%. The hardest setting (DD+SAR, where both judge errors and verification are feature-dependent) still reaches 84.88%. Flip rates are 38%–42%. Sensitivity analyses show AURA benefits from larger class-center separation and degrades under high latent dimension when groups are not well separated.

**Real data (MT-Bench + Chatbot Arena, 5 judges × 3 question types + MT-Bench reference, comparison features from Skywork-Reward-Llama-3.1-8B-v0.2):**
- GPT-5.4 Coding: 63.92% → 67.20% (+3.28%) using ~101 verified examples vs 590 (20% baseline) or 2,359 (80% baseline).
- Qwen Coding: 53.73% → 77.74% (+24.02%, range 9.78) using ~279 verified vs 1,777 (20%) or 7,107 (80%).
- Mistral Coding: 50.29% → 72.91% (+22.62%) using ~262 verified.
- Math and Factual question types show consistent patterns: large gains on weak judges (Qwen/Mistral ~+21–24 pts), modest gains on strong judges (GPT-5.4/Gemini ~+2.5–3.6 pts).
- Most baselines (logistic, MLP, label propagation, even nnPU) fail to improve over the raw judge at 3% verified data and frequently *decrease* accuracy; even at 80% verified data, baseline gains remain small. AURA dominates in nearly every configuration while using a far smaller adaptive verification budget.

**Ablation (Gemini Coding, orig 63.55%):** Full model reaches 66.88%. The transport update is by far the most critical component — disabling it causes >2,000 flips and drops accuracy to 39.7%–53.4%. Replacing adaptive querying with uniform sampling costs ~0.5 pts; using only `p_i` in the trust update (no full state) also lowers performance. Trained encoders outperform untrained ones when transport is enabled.

**Efficiency:** Runtime ranges from ~1.6 min (small GPT-5.4/mini/Gemini configs) to ~96 min (Qwen Factual, 9,333 examples); peak memory 1.6–4.6 GB. Dominant overhead is the O(n_u · n_a · d_z) transport affinity computation.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate-to-good. Reframing judge auditing as *adaptive* PU refinement (treating trust as a latent, progressively refined quantity) is a genuine conceptual move beyond standard PU learning, which assumes fixed positive/unlabeled groups. The combination of trust logit + conservative transport + active verification is cohesive and well-motivated. However, each individual component (graph-based label propagation, partial OT for PU, active learning, anchor-based semi-supervised learning) is well-established; the contribution is the synthesis and the auditing framing rather than a new primitive.

**Rigor:** Theory is clean but limited in scope. The convergence theorem (Theorem 2) guarantees stability and a self-consistent limit point — it says nothing about whether the fixed point corresponds to *good* human-consistency estimation. Theorem 3's bias-reduction guarantee depends critically on Assumption 5 (a latent cluster assumption `|y_i−y_j| ≤ L_loc‖z̄_i−z̄_j‖`), which is strong and untestable on real data; if it fails, the transport step can amplify error. The Lyapunov analysis is standard Robbins-Siegmund machinery.

**Reproducibility:** Hyperparameters are tabulated (Table 5), datasets and judge models named, seeds (5 runs) and ranges reported. But no code or data release URL is mentioned. The Skywork encoder, judge prompts, and exact MT-Bench/Chatbot Arena sampling procedure would be needed for exact replication; some of these (e.g., human-preference ground truth construction, tie exclusion) are under-specified.

**Significance:** Practically relevant for label-efficient LLM evaluation, but the headline numbers require careful interpretation (see red flags).

**Red flags / overclaiming concerns:**
1. **Weak baselines inflate the gap.** Strikingly, all five baselines — including nnPU, the most direct competitor — fail to beat the raw judge at 3% verified data and barely move even at 80% verified data. This is suspicious: a competent supervised baseline given 80% labels should generally improve over the raw judge. The fact that they do not suggests either (a) the fixed Skywork comparison features are weakly discriminative for standard classifiers, (b) the baselines are under-tuned, or (c) there is a label-noise/selection issue in the ground truth that standard methods cannot exploit but AURA's transport+anchor machinery can. The paper does not adequately diagnose why baselines underperform so badly, which weakens the "AURA is better" claim into "AURA exploits structure these baselines miss."
2. **Large gains concentrated on near-chance judges.** The +20–24 pt gains occur on Qwen2.5-7B and Mistral-7B judges whose raw pairwise accuracy is ~50–55% (near random for binary preference). AURA's transport step may be largely *reconstructing* a cleaner signal from the reward-model encoder rather than auditing the judge per se — i.e., part of the gain could reflect the Skywork encoder already "knowing" the answer and AURA surfacing it. The paper does not run a control that applies AURA's pipeline to a random judge to isolate this.
3. **Variance on the biggest gains.** Qwen Coding AURA reports +24.02% with a range of 9.78 pts across 5 seeds — substantial instability for the flagship result.
4. **Ground-truth construction.** Human preference is treated as deterministic ground truth, but ties are excluded and annotator disagreement (acknowledged in Discussion) is not modeled; the target label is itself noisy.
5. **No comparison to modern debiasing/judge-calibration baselines** (e.g., Length-controlled AlpacaEval, OffsetBias, ensemble-of-judges) — only classical ML classifiers and nnPU are used.
6. **Transport-step fragility.** The ablation shows disabling transport collapses performance to below the raw judge (39.7%–53.4%), meaning the method is critically dependent on one component whose theoretical justification (Assumption 5) is assumed, not verified, on real data.

## Strengths

- Clear, well-motivated problem: when to trust vs. correct/verify an LLM judge, framed as adaptive PU refinement rather than fixed-group auditing.
- Cohesive four-block design (encoder, trust, transport, verification) with interpretable state variables; each block is ablated factorially.
- Strong label-efficiency story: AURA matches or beats baselines trained on 20%–80% verified data while using ~3%-equivalent verification budgets.
- Genuine theoretical contribution: convergence, Lipschitz stability, mass conservation, and an expected-error reduction result — rare for an applied LLM-evaluation paper.
- Comprehensive experimental coverage: 5 judges × 3 question types × 3 verification budgets × 5 baselines, plus synthetic recovery, sensitivity to latent geometry, runtime/memory.
- Honest about limitations in the Discussion (noisy human labels, representation quality as future work).

## Limitations

- Baselines are unusually weak and under-diagnosed; the comparison may overstate AURA's advantage (see Critical Assessment).
- Largest gains are on weak judges near chance accuracy; a control isolating the contribution of the Skywork encoder vs. the auditing machinery is missing.
- Theoretical guarantees (especially Theorem 3) rely on a strong, untestable cluster assumption on the learned latent space.
- No code/data release mentioned; some experimental details (judge prompts, exact human-label construction) are under-specified.
- High run-to-run variance on flagship weak-judge results (e.g., Qwen range ~9.8 pts).
- Treats human preference as deterministic ground truth; annotator disagreement and ties are not modeled.
- No comparison against modern judge-debiasing or calibration baselines.
- Computational cost grows with dataset size (up to ~96 min, 4.6 GB on the largest configs); scalability to arena-scale (100k+ comparisons) is untested.

## Relevance

直接命中 LLM 评估可靠性与可解释性方向：AURA 提供了一种在有限人工标注下审视 LLM-as-a-judge 何时可信、何时需要修正或人工核验的弱监督框架，对构建更鲁棒的奖励模型评测、偏好数据清洗与 agent 评估流水线有直接借鉴价值，其自适应 PU + 传输式证据传播的思路也可迁移到推理过程监督与 agent 行为审计中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Why do all standard baselines (including nnPU) fail to beat the raw judge even at 80% verified labels? Is the bottleneck the Skywork comparison-feature quality, baseline tuning, or a property of the human-consistency label distribution?
- How much of AURA's gain on weak judges (Qwen/Mistral) is recoverable by simply applying the frozen Skywork encoder as a direct classifier, without the trust/transport machinery? A controlled "encoder-only" baseline is needed to isolate the auditing contribution.
- Does Assumption 5 (latent cluster assumption) hold empirically on real MT-Bench/Chatbot Arena embeddings? Can it be tested via neighborhood label purity?
- How does AURA behave when human labels are themselves noisy or disputed (annotator disagreement), rather than treated as deterministic ground truth?
- Can the framework extend to non-pairwise (pointwise/listwise) judging, to multi-criteria rubrics (factuality vs. safety vs. helpfulness), and to agent-trajectory evaluation where preferences are over multi-step behaviors?
- What is the failure mode when transport is applied but Assumption 5 is violated — does the mass-conservation guarantee still prevent divergence, or can it silently amplify bias?
- How does AURA compare to modern judge-debiasing methods (length-controlled AlpacaEval, OffsetBias, judge ensembles, Bradley-Terry aggregation) rather than only to classical PU/classifier baselines?
- Can the active-verification policy be analyzed for sample complexity — i.e., a bound on the number of human labels needed to reach a target consistency accuracy?
- Is the method robust to adversarial or systematically biased initial judges (e.g., a judge with strong verbosity bias), where the initial signal's corruption is aligned with a confound?
