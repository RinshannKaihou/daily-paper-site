Key Finding Retraining with a new seed moves FID 3.2× more than resampling — and the relative noise floor stays pinned at 1–2% CoV, refusing to shrink with more compute or larger models.

The FID Lottery: Quantifying Hidden Randomness in Generative Model Evaluation
Nicolas Dufour, Alexei A. Efros, Patrick Pérez (Kyutai · UC Berkeley) · 2026-06-18 · arxiv.org/abs/2606.20536

Key Metric

3.2×retrain > resample
On SiT-B/2: σbetween=0.438 vs σwithin=0.137. CoV floor 0.74–2.06% (median 1.30%) across all 76 cells.

Why This Matters

Single-seed FID gains below ~1.3% are indistinguishable from seed noise — a direct calibration target threatening many diffusion SoTA claims, with a ready error-bar protocol.

Critical Edge

Only SiT + flow matching + ImageNet + Inception-FID is measured; the ~1.3% floor is a calibration target for that combination, not a universal constant — generalization is unverified.

Methodology — FID as a two-axis random variable

Treat FID as a random variable on an N×K panel of training seeds × sampling seeds, measured across several hundred SiT networks (S/B/L/XL) on class-conditional ImageNet 256×256, under conditional flow matching.

data order
  • Three nested statistics: σwithin (generation lottery), σbetween (training lottery), CoV = σ/μ for cross-panel comparison.
  • SAME-SEED control fixes all intended randomness to isolate multi-GPU (DDP) bitwise non-determinism — forecloses the "hardware" scapegoat.
  • GS-FID protocol: per-cell golden-section CFG search (~14 evals/cell) over ω∈[1,2].
  • Scale sweep: N=25 × K=10 at every 100k-step checkpoint from 200k–2M across S/B/L/XL (76 cells); μP LR sweep (~400 networks).
  • Cost: ~100,000 H100 GPU-hours. Replicated on DINOv2 FID + precision/recall.

Practitioner protocol: (1) multiple training seeds can't be replaced by more sampling; (2) treat <~1.3% CoV gaps as inconclusive; (3) guided/unguided must use the same metric — CFG reshuffles rankings so they are not cross-comparable; (4) optimal LR is a 1.7× window; (5) use golden-section to pick CFG scale.

Strengths

  • Unusually large, well-controlled measurement: hundreds of networks, ~100k H100-hours.
  • Clean two-axis framing + minimal nested statistics the community can adopt immediately.
  • SAME-SEED control elegantly rules out hardware non-determinism.
  • Directly actionable: 1–2% inconclusive threshold, GS-FID protocol, online CI calculator.
  • Disciplined, non-overclaiming framing — luck-of-the-draw is measured, not asserted.

Limitations

  • Single combination only (SiT / flow matching / ImageNet / Inception-FID); ~1.3% is not universal.
  • Panel sizes N=20–25, K=10; capped at SiT-XL/2M — production behavior is extrapolation.
  • GS-FID adds ~14 evals/cell — a real cost overhead limiting adoption.
  • Inherits Inception-V3's flaws; DINOv2 replication only partial.
  • No text-to-image, latent diffusion, or non-Fréchet magnitudes tested.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the 1–2% floor extend to text-to-image latent diffusion and FDD/CMMD/KID?
  • Is per-step flow-matching noise the load-bearing source — does the floor vanish for autoregressive/masked image models?
  • Does a cheap proxy (function-level reproducibility, feature-space CKA) predict the multi-seed floor without a full retrain?
  • Does GS-FID's rank-reshuffling mean current CFG leaderboards reward lucky seeds over better methods?
