> **一句话总结：** 作者在几百个 ImageNet 256×256 上训练的 SiT 网络上把 FID 当作(训练种子×采样种子)二维面板上的随机变量直接测量，发现"重训"引起的 FID 抖动是"重采样"的 3.2 倍，且这种相对噪声底稳定保持在均值 1–2% 的区间内、不随算力或模型规模收紧；据此提出新的 FID 评估协议（按 cell 做 GS-FID 调参 + 报告多个训练种子的误差棒 + 把低于 ≈1.3% CoV 的差距判为不可区分）。

> **TL;DR:** FID is treated as a random variable over an N×K panel of training seeds × sampling seeds on hundreds of SiT networks (S→XL) on class-conditional ImageNet 256×256. Retraining with a new seed moves FID **3.2× more** than resampling a fixed network, the floor sits at a stable **1–2% coefficient of variation** that does NOT shrink with more compute or larger models, per-cell golden-section CFG tuning (GS-FID) halves the relative spread but reshuffles which seeds are best (Spearman ρ=0.73), and a lucky training seed reaches the same FID with up to **2× less compute** than an unlucky one. Authors recommend: evaluate under per-cell optimal CFG, treat FID gaps below the ≈1.3% CoV as inconclusive, and report an error bar over several training seeds. Study cost: ~100,000 H100 GPU-hours.

## 核心贡献

- **把 FID 当作二维随机变量直接测量**：在 SiT-S/B/L/XL 上构造 N(训练种子)×K(采样种子) 面板，量化训练彩票 vs 生成彩票两个轴的真实方差，而不是仅凭单一数字论断 SoTA。
- **训练方差远大于采样方差**：在收敛的 SiT-B/2 上 σ_between = 0.438 (CoV≈1.3%) 是 σ_within = 0.137 (CoV≈0.4%) 的 3.2 倍——基准关心的方差来自"训出了哪个模型"，而非"从该模型抽了哪些样本"。
- **分解训练噪声三个来源**：逐步高斯噪声(77%)、初始化(67%)、数据顺序(51%)，三者以次可加方式叠加；多 GPU 数值非确定性(同种子对照)对 FID 几乎无影响(σ=0.047，低于采样地板)。
- **1–2% CoV 地板不随规模/算力收紧**：所有 76 个 cell 的 CoV 落在 [0.74%, 2.06%]（中位数 1.30%），且对模型大小呈非单调，挑战"更大的模型更可复现"的直觉。
- **GS-FID 协议**：按每个 (训练,采样) cell 用黄金分割搜索调 CFG，把 CoV 从 1.26% 降到 0.67%，但会重排种子排名(ρ=0.73)，故带 CFG 与不带 CFG 的 FID 不能跨论文直接比较。
- **"运气差距"**：幸运种子用 1.25×–2.0× 更少算力达到与不幸种子相同 FID；任何单种子论文声称 ≈1.3× 训练加速，实际上已经在和种子彩票竞争。
- **实践者建议 + 在线误差棒计算器**：5 条具体可操作建议（多训种子不能被多采样替代、<2% 差距判为不可区分、guided/unguided 要用同一指标、最优 LR 是 1.7× 宽的窗口而非一点、用黄金分割选 CFG 尺度）。

## Key Contributions

- **First systematic measurement of the FID seed lottery on modern diffusion.** Treats FID as a random variable on a two-axis panel (training seeds × sampling seeds) over several hundred SiT networks; finds retraining moves FID 3.2× more than resampling, and the relative noise floor is a stable calibration target across scale.
- **Variance decomposition of the training lottery.** Isolates initialization, data ordering, per-step flow-matching Gaussian noise, and hardware non-determinism via controlled single-source conditions and a same-seed control.
- **GS-FID protocol.** A per-cell golden-section classifier-free-guidance search that halves the relative noise floor (CoV 1.26% → 0.67%) at ~14 evals/cell, while reshuffling which seeds rank best.
- **Scale/compute-invariance of the floor.** Across SiT-S/B/L/XL from 200k→2M steps, the CoV stays in a 1–2% band, refuting the assumption that bigger/more-trained models are more reproducible.
- **Quantified "luck of the draw."** Lucky seeds converge 1.25×–2.0× faster than unlucky ones, exposing how much headline speedups may simply be seed selection.
- **Concrete practitioner protocol + online CI calculator** for converting a single reported FID into a seed-aware 95% confidence interval.

## Methodology

**Backbone & task.** Scalable Interpolant Transformers (SiT) at four widths (S/2, B/2, L/2, XL/2) trained on class-conditional ImageNet 256×256 under conditional flow matching; the loss redraws a Gaussian ε ~ N(0,I) every gradient step (treated as a third training-time RNG alongside init and data order).

**Two-axis N×K panel.** For each experiment the authors train N independent models (training seeds drive init + data-loader order + per-step flow-matching noise) and, for each, generate K evaluation sets under different sampling seeds (driving the initial noise x_T). Total ≈ several hundred networks in the main panels and ≈400 networks in the µP LR sweep.

**Three nested statistics.** σ_within = within-network sampling spread (averaged over seeds) = generation lottery; σ_between = across-training-seed spread of per-seed means = training lottery; CoV = σ/μ (%) is the dimensionless quantity used to compare panels whose absolute FID differs (e.g. unguided ≈34.7 vs guided ≈7.4).

**FID pipeline.** Single shared Inception-V3 Fréchet pipeline over 50,000 generated images vs the ImageNet reference; deterministic ODE sampler with fixed NFE; CFG off by default, enabled only in §4.3 via per-cell golden-section search over ω∈[1,2] at tolerance 0.01 (~14 evaluations/cell; unimodality/convergence proofs in Appendix E).

**Source decomposition (§4.2).** Three single-source conditions vary one RNG while fixing the other two: VARY-NOISE / VARY-INIT / VARY-DATA, plus the fully-stochastic VARY-ALL baseline and a SAME-SEED control that fixes all intended randomness and isolates only multi-GPU (DDP) bitwise non-determinism.

**Scale/compute sweep (§4.4).** N=25 training seeds × K=10 sampling seeds at every 100k-step checkpoint from 200k to 2M across S/B/L/XL (76 cells).

**µP LR sweep (§4.6).** 10 µP-coordinated LRs log-spaced in [5e-5, 5e-4] across S/B/L/XL, N=10 seeds/cell, 100k steps, scored under both unguided FID and GS-FID (≈400 networks, ≈4000 evals).

**Total cost:** ~100,000 H100 GPU-hours. Appendix F replicates analyses on DINOv2 FID and Inception precision/recall/density/coverage.

## Results & Highlights

- **Training lottery 3.2× larger than generation lottery.** On converged SiT-B/2 (N=25, K=10, 400k, no CFG): σ_between = 0.438 vs σ_within = 0.137; per-seed mean FID spans 33.75–35.42 around grand mean 34.74. A 95% Student-t CI on the grand mean is ±0.18 FID — already larger than many headline gains; multiplying the sampling budget by 10 shrinks within-seed jitter by √10 but leaves the 0.44 between-seed envelope untouched.
- **Per-step flow-matching noise is the single largest source (77%), then init (67%), then data order (51%)**; sources combine sub-additively (the sum overshoots observed σ by 14%). Data-order spread is shape-different: tight bulk with a long upper tail (skew +0.74), i.e. a few outlier "bad runs".
- **Hardware non-determinism is NOT the cause.** The SAME-SEED control (N=24) drives EMA weights 5–6% of their norm apart yet yields σ_between = 0.047, *below* the within-seed floor (0.119), inverting the 3.2× ratio to 0.4×.
- **GS-FID halves the relative floor (CoV 1.26%→0.67%)** but the between/within ratio only drops 3.2×→1.87× (sampling jitter takes a larger share), recovered optima are tight (σω≈0.045, so ±0.05 CFG miscalibration ≈ within-seed floor), and seed ranking reshuffles (Spearman ρ=0.73 with unguided; 8/25 seeds shift ≥5 places).
- **1–2% CoV floor is scale- and compute-invariant.** Across all 76 cells CoV ∈ [0.74%, 2.06%], median 1.30%; bigger models do NOT tighten it (CoV at 2M is non-monotonic: S 0.74%, B 1.24%, L 1.72%, XL 1.42%). Rank stability (Spearman vs 2M ranking) is weak (0.39–0.61) before ~1M steps, so early-checkpoint seed selection is near-random. The spread is Gaussian, not heavy-tailed.
- **Luck-of-the-draw convergence gap: 1.25×–2.0×.** Lucky seed reaches the unlucky-seed-at-2M target at: 1.25× on S/B, 1.82× on L, 2.0× on XL (reaches at 1M what unlucky reaches at 2M). With N seeds, resolvable gap scales as 2σ/√N (N=5 → ≈0.25 FID).
- **µP transfers a 1.7× LR window, not a point.** GS-FID shows flat-bottomed valleys near ω*≈2–3e-4 at every size; three adjacent LRs share the best FID. The CoV does NOT dip at the optimal LR (argmin CoV sits 1–3 brackets below argmin mean, in the under-trained regime). Unguided FID is monotone in LR and points to the unstable edge (5e-4) where 3/10 SiT-S and 1/10 SiT-XL seeds diverge.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** While FID's fragility is well documented (Clean-FID, FID∞, Chong & Forsyth finite-sample bias, Bench & Thomas feature-extractor uncertainty), no prior work systematically varies *training seeds* on modern diffusion with a full source decomposition plus scale/compute sweeps. The closest precursors (Chong & Forsyth; Bench & Thomas) vary neither training seeds, architectures, nor checkpoints. The 3.2× ratio and the scale-invariant 1–2% floor are genuinely new quantifications.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Controlled single-source ablations, a SAME-SEED control to rule out hardware non-determinism, sub-additivity check, Gaussian-not-heavy-tail check, rank-stability Spearman analysis, and replication on DINOv2 FID + precision/recall in Appendix F. Panel sizes (N=20–25, K=10) are modest but adequate for σ estimates; the authors are transparent that production-scale behavior is an extrapolation.
- **Reproducibility: Mixed-but-honest.** Full panel configs (N, K, step budgets) are in Appendix B and the code/calculator are promised via the project page. However, reproducing the full study costs ~100k H100-hours, so independent replication is realistically limited to the smaller sub-panels. The deterministic ODE sampler + shared Inception pipeline reduce a common confound.
- **Significance: High for the evaluation community, moderate for the SoTA-chasing community.** The 1–2% CoV floor is an immediately actionable calibration target; it directly threatens a large fraction of single-seed FID-based claims in the diffusion literature.
- **Red flags / weaknesses to call out:**
  1. **Single architecture family.** Only SiT + flow matching is measured. The authors are explicit that the ≈1.3% CoV is a calibration target for *that combination*, not a universal constant. Generalization to DDPM/rectified-flow/l latent diffusion/text-to-image/other Fréchet variants (FDD, CMMD, KID) is unverified, though Appendix F shows Inception precision/recall/density/coverage track Inception FID closely (recall is the outlier).
  2. **Panel is finite** (20–25 training seeds, 10 sampling seeds) and capped at SiT-XL/2M steps; claims about "production-scale" behavior are extrapolation.
  3. **Inception-V3 itself is a contested feature space** (Clean-FID, DINOv2 advocates); the paper inherits that critique even while measuring variance honestly. The DINOv2 replication in Appendix F partially addresses this but the headline numbers remain Inception-based.
  4. **GS-FID cost.** ~14 evaluations/cell makes the recommended protocol substantially more expensive than current single-FID practice; adoption may be limited to leaderboard-grade comparisons.
  5. **No demonstration that the floor extends to other metrics' absolute values** — only that *relative* tracking holds; readers should not treat "1.3%" as transferable to CMMD/KID magnitudes.
- **Overclaiming check:** The authors are notably disciplined — every TL;DR and the Discussion's "Limitations" section match the evidence. The "luck of the draw" framing is attention-grabbing but quantitatively grounded (1.25–2.0× is measured, not asserted). No cherry-picked best-seed headline is presented as a method contribution.

## Strengths

- Unusually large and well-controlled empirical study (hundreds of networks, ~100k H100-hours) for a measurement paper.
- Clean, intuitive two-axis panel framing and a minimal set of nested statistics (σ_within, σ_between, CoV) that the community can adopt immediately.
- The SAME-SEED control elegantly rules out hardware non-determinism as the culprit — a common scapegoat that this paper forecloses.
- Directly actionable: a concrete 1–2% inconclusive-gap threshold, a GS-FID protocol, and an online CI calculator.
- Honest about scope: explicit Limitations, dual-use framing of the "manufactured headline" risk, and pricing that into peer review.

## Limitations

- Single combination only (SiT / flow matching / class-conditional ImageNet 256×256 / Inception-V3 FID); the ≈1.3% CoV is not a universal constant.
- Panel sizes (N=20–25, K=10) and the SiT-XL/2M-step cap mean production-scale behavior is extrapolation.
- GS-FID adds ~14 evaluations per cell — a real cost overhead that may limit adoption.
- Inherits Inception-V3's well-known flaws; DINOv2 replication is provided but headline numbers stay Inception-based.
- Does not test whether the floor can be predicted from cheap proxies (left explicitly to future work).
- Does not cover text-to-image, latent diffusion, or non-Fréchet metrics' absolute magnitudes.

## Relevance

与本平台关注的核心方向（LLM 训练/推理/可解释性/agents/可靠性）高度相关：它精确量化了生成模型评估中"单种子单数字"的可靠性缺口，直接关系到如何判断训练方法/架构改进是否真实——这与 LLM 训练中"种子噪声淹没小幅 gain"的可复现性问题同构，其误差棒协议和"低于噪声底判为不可区分"的判定标准，可作为评估任何模型训练改进时的通用方法论参考。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the 1–2% CoV floor extend to text-to-image latent diffusion (Stable Diffusion / SDXL-class) and to FDD/CMMD/KID, or do latent-space metrics sit on a different (possibly smaller) floor?
2. Does the floor survive for autoregressive / masked generative image models, where there is no per-step flow-matching Gaussian noise — i.e. is the per-step noise the *load-bearing* source of the floor, as the decomposition hints?
3. Can a cheap proxy (e.g. function-level reproducibility à la Zhang et al. 2024, or feature-space CKA) predict the multi-seed FID floor without a full retrain?
4. How does the floor behave in the LLM regime — do held-out perplexity / benchmark gaps below some CoV likewise become indistinguishable from seed noise across training runs?
5. Does GS-FID's rank-reshuffling (ρ=0.73) imply that current CFG-tuned leaderboards are systematically rewarding lucky seeds rather than better methods?
6. Would stratified/seed-averaged training (e.g. weight averaging across seeds, "model soups") reduce σ_between below the 1–2% floor, and at what compute cost?
7. Is the Gaussian (non-heavy-tailed) spread finding robust at much larger N (e.g. N=100+ seeds), where extreme-order statistics might reveal heavier tails?
