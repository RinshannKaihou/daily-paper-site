> **一句话总结：** 本文通过线性探针、跨题目泛化、PCA降维和神经元级干预，系统分析8个LLM（1B–14B）在ASAP++/CSEE/ENEM三套作文数据集上的隐层表示，发现作文质量信息在LLM内部以线性可解码的形式逐层涌现、对提示策略鲁棒、部分跨题目可迁移，并能定位到与分数强相关的"作文打分神经元"。

> **TL;DR:** A probing/interpretability study of LLM-based Automated Essay Scoring (AES). Across 8 instruction-tuned LLMs (1B–14B; Llama-3.1/3.2, Qwen2.5/3, Phi-4) and 3 datasets (ASAP++, CSEE in English; ENEM in Portuguese), the authors show essay quality is largely linearly decodable from final-token hidden states, emerges progressively across layers, is robust to LLM-prompt changes, and partially transfers across essay prompts. They further isolate individual "essay scoring neurons" whose activations correlate with scores and whose layer-wise distribution shifts toward deeper layers for longer essays.

## 核心贡献

- **线性可解码性的系统证据**：用岭回归线性探针在最终token的隐层表示上训练，证明作文分数（整体及各trait）可被线性恢复；非线性MLP探针仅带来边际且不一致的提升，支持线性表示假设。
- **逐层涌现规律**：质量信息随深度增加逐步可访问；大模型在更早的层就编码该信息，但最终层峰值在各模型族间大致相当。Llama与Qwen/Phi的曲线形态明显不同（Qwen/Phi约在20%深度处饱和，Llama则持续上升至末层）。
- **对提示策略鲁棒**：Essay-only、Task、CoT三种LLM提示下表示质量差异很小；CoT通常收敛更快，但对长作文题目（Prompt 8）反而最差。
- **跨题目部分迁移**：采用留一题目交叉划分，探针性能虽相对in-prompt下降，但仍显著高于随机，表明存在跨评分细则共享的线性信号。
- **PCA降维验证**：在主成分子空间（参数量降低2–3个数量级）上训练探针，仍能保留大量与排序相关的信息，排除"高维探针参数化记忆"的解释。
- **作文打分神经元定位与干预**：找到权重方向与探针特征方向高余弦相似度的单个神经元，其激活与真实分数强Spearman相关，跨题目保持一致；干预实验显示这些神经元比普通神经元对模型打分行为影响更大。
- **长度驱动的层分布迁移**：随作文变长（约≥200词），打分神经元分布系统性向更深层迁移，与人类长文本阅读的工作记忆/整合负荷解释相呼应。

## Key Contributions

- **Systematic evidence of linear decodability.** Trains ridge-regression linear probes on final-token residual-stream activations across all layers; shows both overall and trait essay scores are linearly recoverable, and that nonlinear MLP probes (256 hidden units) yield only marginal/inconsistent gains.
- **Progressive layer-wise emergence.** Essay-quality information becomes increasingly accessible in deeper layers; larger models encode it earlier, but final-layer peak performance is broadly comparable across families — with a notable architectural divergence: Qwen/Phi saturate around 20% depth while Llama keeps improving.
- **Robustness to LLM prompting.** Compares Essay-only, Task (instructions + essay), and CoT prompting on Llama-3.1-8B; representations are largely stable, CoT converges faster on average but degrades for the longest-essay prompt (suggesting instruction noise on long inputs).
- **Cross-prompt transfer.** Leave-one-prompt-out evaluation on ASAP++ shows a non-trivial, linearly accessible quality signal shared across prompts with different rubrics, above chance (QWK=0).
- **Dimensionality-reduction robustness check.** PCA projections (2–3 orders of magnitude fewer parameters) still preserve substantial rank-relevant information, ruling out high-capacity probe memorization as the explanation.
- **Essay-scoring neurons.** Identifies individual neurons whose input/output weight vectors align with probe feature directions; these single neurons act as effective standalone probes (high Spearman correlation with scores), transfer across prompts, and are more sensitive to targeted intervention than typical neurons.
- **Length-dependent layer shift.** Top-50 scoring neurons concentrate in early/middle layers for short essays and migrate toward deeper layers for longer essays (transition around ~200 words), consistent across three models and with cognitive/neural accounts of discourse integration.

## Methodology

- **Setup.** For a dataset of essays with human scores, feed each essay into the LLM and extract the residual-stream hidden state of the final token at every layer: `h^(l)_{i,L_i} = H^(l)_i[-1,:]`. Stack across essays to form an activation matrix `A^(l) ∈ R^{n×d_model}` per layer.
- **Linear probe.** Ridge regression `min ||Y − A^(l)W||^2 + λ||W||^2` with closed-form solution `Ŵ = (AᵀA + λI)⁻¹ AᵀY`; λ selected via efficient leave-one-out cross-validation on the probe training set (Hastie et al.). Predictions `Ŷ = A Ŵ`.
- **Nonlinear probe.** Two-layer MLP `W2·ReLU(W1·x + b1) + b2` with 256 hidden neurons, for comparison.
- **Models.** Eight instruction-tuned LLMs spanning 1B–14B: Llama-3.2-1B/3B-Instruct, Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-3B/7B/14B-Instruct, Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507, Phi-4-mini-instruct.
- **Datasets.** ASAP++ and CSEE (English; CSEE chosen partly to mitigate data-leakage concerns given its 2025 release postdating training cutoffs), ENEM (Portuguese). Detailed descriptions deferred to appendix.
- **Evaluation.** Quadratic Weighted Kappa (QWK) as the primary metric; 80/20 prompt-specific train/test split, following standard AES practice. Spearman correlation also reported.
- **Analyses.** (1) Per-layer QWK curves per model/trait; (2) prompt-strategy robustness (Essay/Task/CoT); (3) leave-one-prompt-out cross-prompt generalization; (4) PCA dimensionality-reduction probes across varying k; (5) neuron-level: cosine similarity between neuron weight vectors and probe feature directions, then Spearman correlation of neuron activations with scores; (6) neuron intervention (appendix); (7) layer-wise distribution of top-50 neurons binned by essay length (100-word bins, LLM-generated essays used to augment samples for Prompt 8).

## Results & Highlights

- Essay quality becomes progressively more linearly decodable in deeper layers across all eight models.
- Larger models reach the signal earlier (steeper initial gains), but final-layer QWK peaks are broadly comparable across model families.
- Overall scores are decoded more accurately than individual trait scores → coarse-grained quality is captured better than fine-grained trait-specific quality.
- Nonlinear MLP probes add only marginal/inconsistent QWK gains → most essay-quality signal is linearly decodable, consistent with the linear representation hypothesis.
- Llama vs Qwen/Phi divergence: Qwen/Phi saturate around ~20% depth then plateau; Llama keeps improving to the final layers (hypothesized to stem from training-data quality differences).
- Prompt robustness: minimal differences across Essay/Task/CoT; CoT converges fastest but performs worst on the long-essay Prompt 8.
- Cross-prompt performance drops relative to in-prompt but stays clearly above chance → a shared, linearly accessible quality signal persists across prompts despite rubric differences.
- PCA probes: low-dimensional subspaces capture substantial rank-relevant information; Spearman coefficients rise faster with k than QWK (because QWK also penalizes absolute calibration).
- Individual neurons identified via weight-direction alignment show strong Spearman correlation with true scores, transfer across prompts, and are disproportionately affected by targeted intervention.
- Length-dependent neuron shift: scoring neurons migrate from early/middle layers (short essays) to middle/later layers (long essays), with the transition around ~200 words, consistent across Llama-8B, Qwen2.5-7B, and Qwen2.5-14B.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (moderate).** The general finding that task-relevant features are linearly decodable from LLM hidden states is well established; the contribution is the application to AES and the neuron-level/length analyses. The most original pieces are the length-dependent layer-shift observation and the cross-architecture Llama-vs-Qwen/Phi divergence. Several findings (linear decodability, layer-wise emergence, probe robustness to prompting) are largely confirmatory of broader probing results transferred to the AES domain.
- **Rigor (mixed).** Strengths: eight models, three datasets (two languages), multiple probe types, PCA robustness check, LOO-CV for λ, and explicit acknowledgment that probe performance ≠ model use. Weaknesses: many headline claims rest on QWK trends in figures rather than reported numerical tables or statistical significance tests; cross-architecture claims (Llama vs Qwen/Phi) are attributed to "training data quality" with only a "tentative hypothesis" — no controlled evidence.
- **Reproducibility (moderate–good).** All models are open-source and datasets are public; probe training is closed-form ridge regression (cheap, deterministic). However, exact neuron-selection thresholds, intervention protocols, and the LLM-generated essays used to augment length bins introduce unreported degrees of freedom (deferred to appendices that were not fully surfaced here).
- **Significance (moderate).** Useful for AES interpretability and for anyone probing LLM internal representations, but practical impact (e.g., improving AES robustness or controllability) is framed as future work rather than demonstrated.
- **Red flags / overclaiming.** The phrasing "provide evidence that LLMs encode structured representations" is appropriately hedged, but the jump from single-neuron correlation + single-neuron intervention to "the model utilizes these features" is not fully established — the authors themselves note feature superposition makes single-neuron analysis "inherently limited" and that interventions were restricted to individual neurons (no circuit-level analysis). The PCA "rules out memorization" claim is reasonable but not airtight. The CoT-hurts-long-essays explanation ("instruction noise") is post-hoc and untested.

## Strengths

- Broad cross-model (8 LLMs, 1B–14B) and cross-dataset (2 English + 1 Portuguese) coverage, increasing the generalizability of conclusions relative to single-model probing studies.
- Methodologically conservative: uses linear ridge probes with LOO-CV, includes PCA robustness checks to rule out probe overfitting, and explicitly distinguishes "decodable by a probe" from "used by the model."
- The essay-scoring-neuron analysis (weight-direction alignment + intervention + cross-prompt transfer) and the length-dependent layer-shift finding are concrete, interpretable results with a plausible cognitive analogy.
- Intellectually honest limitations section (model scale, language coverage, single-neuron vs circuit analysis).

## Limitations

- No large-scale/proprietary models (GPT-4, Llama-3.1-70B); capability emergence at scale is untested.
- Only one non-English language (Portuguese/ENEM); cross-cultural rubric variation not addressed.
- Neuron analysis is single-neuron; feature superposition and multi-neuron circuits are acknowledged but not analyzed, so causal claims about "essay scoring neurons" are partial.
- Quantitative tables are sparse; many conclusions depend on visual trends in figures without significance testing or confidence intervals.
- Neuron intervention restricted to individual units; no attention-head or circuit-level causal mediation.
- Prompt-robustness experiment limited to one model (Llama-3.1-8B) and overall-score prediction only.
- The Llama-vs-Qwen/Phi saturation divergence is observed but not causally explained beyond speculation.

## Relevance

与LLM可解释性和可靠性高度相关：论文直接研究LLM内部表示如何线性编码任务相关概念（线性表示假设）、表示如何逐层涌现、以及单个神经元与模型行为的因果关系，这些是理解LLM内部机制与可信推理的核心议题，对agent/评估系统的可靠性研究有借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the essay-quality feature directions identified by the probe overlap with more general "text quality / fluency / coherence" directions, and are they reusable across non-AES tasks (e.g., long-form QA grading, document quality filtering)?
- Would the length-dependent layer shift generalize to other long-input tasks (summarization, retrieval), and is it predictive of where long-context failures occur?
- Can circuit-level (multi-neuron / attention-head) intervention, rather than single-neuron ablation, establish a stronger causal link between these neurons and the model's actual scoring behavior?
- How do the findings change at 70B+/frontier scale, and does the Llama-vs-Qwen/Phi saturation divergence persist or disappear?
- Can the identified neurons/feature directions be used proactively — e.g., to regularize AES fine-tuning, detect scoring bias, or build controllable/steerable scorers — rather than only diagnose post hoc?
- Is the "CoT hurts long essays" effect robust across more models and prompts, or is it specific to Llama-8B / Prompt 8?
