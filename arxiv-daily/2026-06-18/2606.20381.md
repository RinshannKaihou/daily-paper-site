> **一句话总结：** 本文从几何角度揭示主流 FP4 格式 E2M1 因 RTNE 舍入箱不对称而存在系统性的"收缩偏差(Shrinkage Bias)"，该偏差在层间乘性累积并被 RHT 加剧；据此提出基于均匀网格(E1M2/INT4)的 UFP4 配方，将 RHT 扩展到全部三个训练 GEMM、仅对 dY 施加随机舍入，在 Dense 1.5B / MoE 7.9B / MoE 124B 长程预训练中将相对 BF16 的损失退化分别降至 0.97% / 1.85% / 1.39%。

> **TL;DR:** The authors show that the dominant FP4 training format E2M1 suffers from an inherent, geometrically-induced "Shrinkage Bias" — a systematic negative RTNE rounding error from its asymmetric representable bins — that accumulates multiplicatively across layers and is worsened by the Random Hadamard Transform (RHT) used for outlier suppression. They propose UFP4, a recipe built on a uniform E1M2/INT4-style 4-bit grid that applies RHT to all three training GEMMs (forward, data-gradient, weight-gradient) while restricting stochastic rounding to dY, reducing BF16-relative loss degradation versus a carefully tuned E2M1 baseline on Dense 1.5B (1.26%→0.97%), MoE 7.9B (2.36%→1.85%), and MoE 124B (1.73%→1.39%), and argue that future accelerators should expose uniform 4-bit grids as first-class training primitives.

## 核心贡献

- **形式化"收缩偏差(Shrinkage Bias)"**：首次从码本几何角度证明，非均匀 FP4 格式(如 E2M1)因 RTNE 舍入箱左右宽度不等(`r_i > l_i`)而存在系统性负期望舍入误差，给出闭式条件期望公式 `E[ρ_G(t)−t | t∈B_i] = (l_i − r_i)/2`，而均匀网格(E1M2/INT4)满足 `l_i = r_i` 故完全规避该误差。
- **乘性累积 + RHT 放大的统一解释**：将 GEMM 量化写成 `Q(A)=α_A·A + R_A` 的正交分解，证明主信号被 `η ≈ α_A·α_B < 1` 衰减，且对 K 层 GEMM 以 `∏η_k ≈ exp(−Σδ_k)` 乘性累积(见附录 C 推导)；并指出 RHT 把张量从"动态范围受限"切换到"局部分辨率受限"区域，恰好把质量压入 E2M1 最不对称的舍入箱，从而进一步恶化——这统一解释了 NVFP4 等现有 E2M1 配方的训练不稳定。
- **UFP4 配方**：基于 E1M2/INT4 均匀网格，对全部三个训练 GEMM(FPROP 的 fwd_y、DGRAD 的 bwd_dx、WGRAD 的 bwd_dw)施加 RHT，仅对上游梯度 dY 使用随机舍入(SR)；证明"全 RHT 并非有害，有害的是 E2M1 网格与后 RHT 分布的失配"。
- **大规模长程验证**：在 Dense 1.5B、MoE 7.9B、MoE 124B 长程预训练中，UFP4 一致降低相对 BF16 的损失退化；并配套 scaling-law(10M–324M MoE)拟合、消融与融合核 benchmark。
- **硬件建议**：呼吁未来加速器在 E2M1 之外，把 E1M2/INT4 风格的均匀 4-bit 网格作为一等公民的训练数据元素(并指出 HiFloat4/Ascend 960 是潜在原生载体)。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes Shrinkage Bias**: Identifies and derives the systematic negative expected RTNE rounding error inherent to non-uniform grids such as E2M1, caused by asymmetric representable bins, with the closed form `E[ρ_G(t)−t | t∈B_i] = (l_i − r_i)/2`; uniform grids (E1M2/INT4) eliminate it since `l_i = r_i`.
- **Unified mechanism for FP4 instability**: Shows the bin-level bias survives tensor aggregation, attenuates the principal GEMM signal by `η ≈ α_A·α_B`, and accumulates multiplicatively across depth (`∏η_k ≈ exp(−Σδ_k)`); RHT further amplifies it under E2M1 by shifting tensor mass into its most biased asymmetric bins — explaining instability in NVFP4-style recipes.
- **UFP4 recipe**: A uniform E1M2/INT4-style 4-bit training recipe that applies RHT to all three training GEMMs (fwd_y, bwd_dx, bwd_dw) and uses stochastic rounding only on dY; demonstrates full-RHT coverage is beneficial once the grid is uniform (contrary to E2M1 recipes that must restrict RHT to bwd_dw).
- **Industrial-scale validation**: Long-run pretraining on Dense 1.5B, MoE 7.9B, and MoE 124B, plus a Ling-protocol scaling-law sweep (10M–324M MoE), fused-kernel latency benchmarks, and component ablations.
- **Hardware call to action**: Recommends future accelerators expose uniform E1M2/INT4-style 4-bit grids as first-class training primitives alongside E2M1.

## Methodology

- **Theory (Section 3)**: For a codebook `G⁺ = {q_0,...,q_n}`, the RTNE bin around `q_i` has widths `l_i, r_i`; under locally uniform density the conditional expected magnitude error is `(l_i − r_i)/2`. For E2M1 magnitudes `{0, 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 3, 4, 6}`, the bin around `q=2` yields bias `−0.125`. For GEMMs, operands decompose as `Q(A)=α_A·A + R_A` (with `⟨R_A,A⟩_F = 0`), giving an exact identity where the principal signal `AB^⊤` is scaled by `α_A·α_B`, and over K layers this becomes `∏(1−δ_k) ≈ exp(−Σδ_k)`.
- **RHT interaction analysis (Section 3.2)**: Defines effective bucket ratio `B_eff = exp(E)/K` (entropy-based utilization) and `ΔSQNR = 10·log10(NMSE_I / NMSE_H16)`. On real MLP/attention tensors, RHT raises `B_eff` for outlier-heavy tensors but yields `ΔSQNR < 0` for E2M1 (degradation) versus `ΔSQNR > 0` for E1M2 (improvement) — a format-dependent inversion.
- **UFP4 recipe (Section 4, Table 1)**: Format = E1M2/INT4 uniform grid; quant block 1×16; FP32 single-level scale hierarchy; RHT scope = fwd_y + bwd_dx + bwd_dw (block 16); SR scope = dY only; no 2D weight scaling. E2M1 baseline kept identical except format = E2M1, RHT scope = bwd_dw, max_fpx = 6.
- **Controlled E2M1 reference (Appendix B)**: To avoid an undertuned baseline, the authors run one-factor ablations over RHT scope, RHT block size, SR scope, 2D weight scaling, and max_fpx on Dense 1.5B (200B tokens), selecting the lowest-final-loss configuration before freezing it for all comparisons.
- **Fused kernel (Section 5.6)**: When the Hadamard block matches the quant block, RHT is fused before scale estimation and packing.

## Results & Highlights

- **Q1 (tensor/GEMM diagnostics)**: On outlier-heavy `linear_fc2/fwd_x`, RHT *reverses* the format ranking — E2M1 leads before rotation (21.90 vs 19.94 dB SQNR) but E1M2 leads after (23.19 vs 20.00 dB), raising `B_eff` from 0.56 to 0.97. The inversion persists through GEMM composition.
- **Q2 (loss gap)**: Latest-1000-step BF16-relative LM loss error reduced by UFP4:
  - Dense 1.5B: 1.2570% → 0.9673%
  - MoE 7.9B: 2.3596% → 1.8469%
  - MoE 124B: 1.7308% → 1.3863%
- **Q3 (scaling law)**: Across 10M–324M MoE (Ling protocol), the fitted E1M2 curve stays below the E2M1 curve, and the FP4-to-BF16 gap shrinks with compute — the advantage is not an artifact of small models.
- **Q4 (ablations)**: On Dense 1.5B E1M2, full RHT reduces loss by 0.01123 vs no-RHT; SR on dY adds another 0.00456. Range-restricted E2M1 (max_fpx = 2/3/4) cannot emulate a uniform grid — all variants underperform the E2M1 reference, sacrificing dynamic range and bucket utilization.
- **Q5 (kernel overhead)**: Fused RHT+quantization = 1.06× (SM90) and 1.07× (SM100) the latency of standalone quantization; unfused is 1.62× / 1.41×, so fusion is essential.
- **Both FP4 recipes still leave a measurable gap to BF16** — the paper claims reduced degradation, not BF16 parity.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high)**: The geometric framing of Shrinkage Bias and the `α_A·α_B` multiplicative-accumulation identity is a clean, original theoretical lens that unifies otherwise ad-hoc observations about E2M1 instability. The "RHT shifts the regime from dynamic-range-limited to local-resolution-limited" insight is the paper's conceptual core and is well argued.
- **Rigor (strong)**: Theory is backed by closed-form derivations (Appendix C), real-tensor SQNR/`B_eff` diagnostics, single-GEMM output measurements, end-to-end training at three scales, a scaling-law sweep, and component ablations. The baseline is tuned, not strawmanned.
- **Reproducibility (mixed — important caveat)**: A critical limitation the paper soft-pedals: **no shipping accelerator exposes native E1M2/INT4 4-bit GEMM hardware today** (NVIDIA Blackwell/Rubin and AMD MI350 are E2M1-centric). The 124B MoE E1M2 results are therefore almost certainly produced via **simulated/emulated FP4 GEMM in BF16 pipelines**, not native FP4 silicon throughput. The paper's value is as a recipe + hardware recommendation, not a drop-in speedup. Code/data release is not mentioned; only fused-kernel micro-benchmarks are reported, with no end-to-end wall-clock training-speedup numbers.
- **Significance (conditional)**: Impact hinges on hardware vendors adopting uniform 4-bit grids. If they do, UFP4 is a ready recipe; if they do not, it is a principled but un-deployable result. The paper acknowledges this ("narrow but actionable").
- **Red flags / overclaiming**: None egregious. The authors are careful to say "lower BF16-relative loss degradation" rather than "matches BF16," and explicitly state both FP4 recipes retain a measurable gap. The range-restricted-E2M1 negative result honestly closes the "can E2M1 fake it?" loophole.
- **Weak spots**: (1) Only LM loss is reported — no downstream task / benchmark accuracy, so it is unclear whether the ~0.3–0.5 percentage-point loss-error reduction translates to capability differences. (2) Single-model-family (Ling-style) evaluation; no cross-architecture test. (3) No comparison to MixFP4 (per-block E2M1/E1M2 adaptive) or HiFloat4 beyond discussion.

## Strengths

- Elegant, original theoretical contribution (Shrinkage Bias geometry) with a clean closed form.
- Strong experimental scale (up to 124B MoE) and a properly tuned E2M1 baseline.
- Honest about residual BF16 gap and about the hardware-dependency of practical impact.
- Practical fused-kernel analysis showing full-RHT overhead is small when fused.
- Actionable, narrow hardware recommendation rather than sweeping claims.

## Limitations

- Native E1M2/INT4 FP4 GEMM hardware does not exist on mainstream GPUs; results rely on quantization simulation, so real-silicon speedup and accuracy are unverified.
- Evaluation is loss-only — no downstream benchmark accuracy or capability evaluation.
- Single architecture family (Ling MoE/Dense); generalization across model families untested.
- No head-to-head against MixFP4 (adaptive per-block format) or HiFloat4, only discussion.
- End-to-end training throughput / wall-clock / memory numbers are absent; only micro-kernel latency.

## Relevance

对关注 LLM 训练效率与低精度数值格式的研究者高度相关：UFP4 直接服务于"如何把预训练精度从 FP8 进一步压到 FP4 而不损失质量"这一核心问题，其关于均匀网格优于 E2M1 的几何论证对未来硬件设计与量化训练配方设计具有指导意义，同时也为理解 FP4 训练不稳定性的根因(可解释性)提供了一个清晰的理论框架。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the ~0.3–0.5 percentage-point BF16-relative loss-error reduction translate into measurable gains on downstream benchmarks (MMLU, GSM8K, coding), or does it wash out at scale?
- How does UFP4 interact with format-adaptive schemes like MixFP4 (per-block E2M1 vs E1M2)? Could a hybrid that uses E2M1 for raw outlier-heavy tensors and E1M2 post-RHT dominate both?
- Once native E1M2/INT4 FP4 silicon (e.g., Ascend 960 / HiFloat4) ships, do the simulated-quantization gains hold under real GEMM accumulation behavior, or do silicon-level rounding/accumulation details shift the conclusion?
- Does Shrinkage Bias also matter for FP4 *inference* (not just training), where RHT-style rotations (QuaRot, SpinQuant, FlatQuant) are also applied?
- Can the `α_A·α_B` attenuation framework be used as a cheap, training-free predictor of FP4 recipe quality across architectures, avoiding expensive long-run ablations?
- What is the interaction with non-linear layers (SwiGLU, attention softmax, normalization) where quantization happens off the GEMM path analyzed here?
