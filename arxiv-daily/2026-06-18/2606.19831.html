Key Finding Any single-neuron dose reduces to one alignment coordinate following a universal saturation curve — coherent control exists only below a weights-predictable collapse ceiling, and local gradients anti-predict the true controllers.

Leverage Is Not Reach: A Control-Window Law for Single-Neuron Steering in Language Models
Hongliang Liu (Palo Alto Networks) · 2026-06-18 · arxiv.org/abs/2606.19831

Key Metric

0/4→ 4/4
Gradient attribution recovers 0 of 4 true controllers; a forward-only contrastive screen recovers all 4. Ceiling prediction MAE 0.14 on 15 held-out neurons.

Why This Matters

A falsifiable, weights-predictable law of when single-neuron steering works reframes activation steering and safety audits — dosing in budget units, not fixed magnitudes.

Critical Edge

The law is tight on the predictable collapse ceiling but loose on the behavior trigger (measured, not predicted); all safety claims rest on a 6-pivot cohort and a not-yet-delivered scaling companion paper.

Methodology — one coordinate, one inequality

A scalar dose t along the down-projection column v = W_down[:,i] of neuron i gives r(t) = r_L + t·v. Because downstream layers consume the residual through normalization (magnitude discarded, direction kept), the readout collapses to a single signed alignment:

cos θv = cos∠(r(t), v̂) = g / √(1+g²), g = t / B, B = ‖rL‖/‖v‖ control window: cosθvtrig(τ) < cosθvcoll (⇔ coherent control exists) collapse ceiling: cosθvcoll = m* / √(1+m*²)
  • B = coherence budget — the single-neuron FFN-to-skip ratio; dosing in units of B, not fixed magnitude.
  • m* ≈ order-one family coefficient set by residual participation ratio (1.4–1.7 Qwen/Llama, 0.31 Gemma).
  • Candidate discovery: forward-only contrastive screen (rank by |Δa| between matched harmful/harmless prompts), then verify causally by dosing — no backward pass.
  • Five-part measurement contract: budgeted dose, signed controlling branch, behavior rollout horizon, script-matched coherence gate, content gate (non-refusal vs strict-actionable).
  • 8 architectures: Qwen, Llama, Mistral, Phi-3.5, Gemma-3; one A100-40GB each.

Two-axis safety diagnostic: Qwen opens bypass windows (0.44–0.60) but ≈0 actionable; Llama has a genuine actionable subset (peak 0.88, late-committing); Gemma closed on both axes. Refusal bypass and strict-actionable harm reach are decoupled — fluent non-refusal is not real harm.

Strengths

  • First predictive theory of single-neuron controllability with a pre-registered, falsifiable test.
  • "Leverage is not reach" explains and predicts why gradient attribution & fixed-dose steering systematically mis-rank neurons.
  • Bypass-vs-actionable decoupling corrects over-claiming of jailbreak severity.
  • Excellent measurement hygiene: Wilson intervals, blind scoring, honest negatives.
  • Clean unification of super-weights, refusal neurons, arithmetic gates on one inequality.

Limitations

  • Trigger is measured, not predicted — the law is asymmetric: tight on collapse, loose on control.
  • Curated, small controller set; model-scale validation deferred to an unavailable companion paper.
  • m* needs per-family calibration; Spearman 0.67 not significant at n=8.
  • Covers FFN write directions in pre-norm models; Gemma's post-FFN norm breaks the equivalence.
  • Single author, not peer-reviewed; "3/6 genuine actionable" within sampling noise.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the law hold at model scale — what fraction of refusal capability sits in window-open vs window-closed neurons?
  • Can the trigger value be predicted from weights (basin-curvature, second-order Hessian β=vᵼHv), closing the ceiling/trigger asymmetry?
  • Does the bypass-vs-actionable decoupling survive larger models, or does actionable reach grow with scale?
  • For reliability: does quantization shift m* enough to open previously-closed windows?
