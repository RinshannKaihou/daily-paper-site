◆ Key Metric · Primary Null

Pooled graph similarity, within-cluster vs between-cluster — statistically indistinguishable (|Δ| < 0.014, all p > 0.02). 0 of 15 model–condition comparisons survive Bonferroni correction.
◆ Why This Matters

Accuracy-only evaluation overrates clinical LLMs — high scores can hide unstable reasoning, so process-level auditing is essential before deployment.

◆ Critical Edge

The central null is partly an artifact of a content metric at its noise floor (test-retest only 0.43).

Defensible claim: “no detectable schemas under this metric” — not “no latent structure.”

01

Methodology — graphs, not grades

The author turns free-text LLM diagnostic traces into auditable directed graphs via a domain-grounded ontology, then stress-tests whether the resulting structures are stable across clinically similar cases — the “diagnostic schema” hypothesis.

From a preregistered protocol, 750 traces were generated: 5 frontier models (GPT-5.4, GPT-5.2, Claude Opus 4.5, Claude Sonnet 4.5, Gemini 3 Pro) × 50 NEJM Clinicopathological Conference cases (15 easy / 20 moderate / 15 hard, grouped into 20 clusters) × 3 prompt conditions — baseline, adversarial, and structured reflection (Dhaliwal ART framework).

A 5-component composite similarity (motif 0.35 · diagnosis 0.30 · feature 0.15 · qualifier 0.10 · depth 0.10) was tested with 10k-permutation case-level tests + Bonferroni, anchored against test-retest (0.825), noise floor (0.466), and inter-extractor (0.593) baselines.

The cleanest positive residue: a content-channel analysis finds within > between in all 15 cells (7 survive Bonferroni), and a Mantel regression gives mean r = 0.118 — real, but ~1.4% of variance, far below schema scale. The null also holds for both pre- and post-2023 cases, limiting memorization-bias concerns.

03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Evaluates how LLMs reason in medicine — not just whether they’re right.
  • Rigorous stats: permutation tests + Bonferroni + bootstrap CIs + Cohen’s d; preregistered.
  • Excellent calibration hygiene — test-retest, noise floor, inter-extractor baselines.
  • Counterintuitive, decision-relevant findings; clinician-authored ontology grounding.
  • Full artifact release with a clear path to agentic multi-step trajectories.

Limitations

  • Operationalizes expressed reasoning, not internal cognition; may miss clinician-visible schemas.
  • Single-author / single-physician validation — no multi-annotator IRR.
  • Headline null is partly structural: composite weights fixed & structure-heavy.
  • Content-channel reliability low (test-retest 0.43) — attenuates true correlation.
  • 50 rare NEJM CPC cases; no human-expert graph-consistency baseline.
04

Open Questions

Q1How much of the null is extraction noise vs. true model behavior, under independent multi-annotator review?
Q2Would forcing LLMs to emit graphs directly (not extract post hoc) recover schema-scale consistency?
Q3Do findings generalize beyond rare CPC cases to routine, longitudinal, or multi-turn reasoning?
Q4Could a richer ontology (temporal/causal edges, severity, certainty) surface latent schemas the 7-edge design hides?
Q5Why does reflection increase within-trace structure yet decrease cross-case consistency?
Q6Does accuracy ⟂ structure hold for multimodal clinical LLMs integrating imaging and labs?
