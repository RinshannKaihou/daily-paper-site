Key finding

Effective self-evolution depends less on the RL-vs-distillation choice than on when & where each signal is applied. — distillation thesis, abstracted from DRIFT's central claim & critical assessment.

DRIFT: Difficulty Routing Self-Distillation with Rhythm-Gated Exploration & Success Buffer Training Haisen Luo, Yiwei Liu, Haoning Wang et al. — Tsinghua · ENS Paris-Saclay · Beike L&I | 2026-06-29 | cs.LG / cs.AI
Key metric
79.5%
avg over 5 science / tool-use benchmarks, Qwen3-8B — SOTA, +2.1 over SRPO, +7.5–9.5 over GRPO/SDPO. Top-2 on every task.
Why this matters

Stable LLM self-improvement without external expert supervision — a directly transferable mechanism design for RLVR, curriculum learning & fine-grained credit assignment.

Critical edge

Numerous tuned thresholds with no sensitivity sweep, and in-domain-only evaluation — so "self-improvement" is bounded to the trained task distribution.

Methodology

§ distillation · not a transcription

DRIFT is an online self-evolution policy-optimization framework that diagnoses two named failure modes: GRPO's coarse sequence-level credit (advantage collapse under homogeneous rewards) and SDPO's indiscriminate replay of every success (redundant updates on mastered items, under-explored boundary cases). Its fix is to stop asking which signal to use and instead route both across two granularities.

At the problem level, smoothed (EMA) historical pass-rates bin items easy/medium/hard and reweight GRPO updates (γ_hard=0, γ_easy=0.5, r_easy=0.8, r_hard=0.2), concentrating RL budget on the medium-difficulty boundary where learning value is highest. At the token level, a rhythm gate double-screens a teacher-relative rebellious bonus by an anchor salience signal (teacher entropy-collapse vs. student uncertainty) — so amplification fires only at structurally critical, medium-difficulty anchors. A two-stage curriculum warm-starts with SDPO then mixes a correction branch (negatives→SDPO via a privileged sibling) with the rhythm-GRPO branch (positives), backed by a lightweight success buffer (~0.2–0.3 GB) that supplies positives when batches have none.

Difficulty Routing · problem-level
EMA-smoothed pass-rates route the RL budget to medium items; γ kills easy/hard updates.
Rhythm Gate · token-level
Rebellious bonus × anchor salience — bounded amplification at critical reasoning anchors only.
Success Buffer + Curriculum · two-stage
SDPO warm-up then negatives→correction, positives→rhythm-GRPO; privileged sibling sib⁺ when positives are scarce.

Strengths & Limitations

§ critical assessment

＋ Strengths

  • Principled, multi-granularity design that directly diagnoses & fixes named GRPO/SDPO failure modes.
  • Routing rule is well-motivated: negatives→SDPO for directional correction, positives→GRPO to preserve capability.
  • Strong, consistent gains over six baselines across three scales; largest margins on the hardest tasks.
  • Honest, equation-complete methodology; memory-efficient buffer; step-based (hardware-agnostic) reporting.

− Limitations

  • Many tuned hyperparameters (thresholds, γ, rhythm-gate coefficients) with no sensitivity analysis.
  • In-domain only — no OOD / held-out-domain test; "self-improvement" is bounded to the trained distribution.
  • Baseline asymmetry: 4 of 6 baselines cited, not re-run; mean@16 inflates absolute accuracy.
  • No code/data release, no error bars, no significance tests; per-token compute overhead unquantified.
