> **一句话总结：** 本文以"信息溯源图（IPG）"分类法刻画多模态 Agent 记忆中事实在各存储层的删除可行性，构建 MemLeak 基准度量"遗忘"后的多通道泄漏：直接探测 <1%，但保留相关文本可恢复 18.3%、保留其他事实的图像可恢复 12.0%（盲测 0.0%、负控 FPR 0.3%），其中 47% 的图像泄漏文本无法恢复；内容感知语义删除可将图像残留从 12.0% 降至 2.0%。

> **TL;DR:** After a multimodal agent "forgets" a fact, the fact often remains recoverable from retained user images (even images tagged to unrelated facts) because VLMs exploit implicit visual cues at inference time. The authors propose the Information Provenance Graph (IPG) taxonomy and the MemLeak benchmark, showing fact-level erasure fails through multiple channels (retained text 18.3%, retained images 12.0%, blind 0.0%, FPR 0.3%), with 47% of image leaks invisible to text-only auditing; content-aware semantic deletion cuts the image residual from 12.0% to 2.0%.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Information Provenance Graph (IPG)** 形式化分类法：将事实在 Agent 记忆各存储层中的表示节点按"删除可行性"分为 *addressable / linked / persistent* 三类，并用有向无环图刻画信息流动，从而诊断"删除"为何在多通道上失败。
- 给出**遗忘完整性（FCS）与信息泄漏（vs. 通用推理）的形式定义**：明确区分"通用模型推理"与"从同一用户保留数据中重构被删事实的泄漏"，对接 GDPR Article 17 的技术动机。
- 构建 **MemLeak 基准**：113 个合成画像 + 523 张真实 Unsplash 照片，5 类事实、3 种视觉落地等级，5 种探测（P1–P5）覆盖各 IPG 层；数据与脚本以 MIT 协议开源。
- **关键实证发现（n=300，38 画像）**：删除文本+标记图像后，仍可从"其他事实的图像"恢复 12.0% 的事实（95% CI [8.8, 16.2]），负控 FPR 仅 0.3%、盲测 0.0%；保留文本可恢复 18.3%；**47% 的图像泄漏无法由文本恢复**，说明图像是被文本审计遗漏的独立通道。
- 提出 **content-aware semantic deletion** 缓解方法：删除时额外跑一次 VLM 审计扫描相关图像并删除（标记 21.0% 图像），将泄漏从 12.0% 降至 2.0%（约 6× 降低）。
- **鲁棒性验证**：跨两种 VLM（Claude Sonnet 4 12.0% / GPT-4o 9.3%，CI 重叠）、生产系统 Mem0 端到端 16.3%、真实 Unsplash 照片 10.6%；3-模型 LLM 评判集成（GPT-4.1 + Claude Sonnet 4 + Gemini 2.5 Flash，多数投票），双人人工标注 κ=0.88，全部报告 95% Wilson CI。

## Key Contributions

- **Information Provenance Graph (IPG)** — a formal taxonomy/DAG classifying each representation of a fact across storage layers by *deletion affordance* (addressable / linked / persistent), explaining *why* deletion fails through multiple channels.
- **Formal definitions** of Forgetting Completeness (FCS) and Information Leakage (vs. generic model inference), aligning the criterion with erasure regulations (GDPR Art. 17) while staying technical.
- **MemLeak benchmark** — 113 synthetic profiles + 523 real Unsplash photos, 5 fact categories, 3 visual-grounding levels, 5 probe types (P1–P5); released under MIT with profiles, images, probes, scripts.
- **Headline empirical result (n=300, 38 multimodal profiles):** after deleting text *and* all images tagged to a forgotten fact, **12.0%** of facts remain recoverable from images attached to *other* retained facts (blind 0.0%, negative FPR 0.3%); retained-text inference alone yields **18.3%**; **47%** of image leaks are *not* text-recoverable.
- **Content-aware semantic deletion** — one extra VLM auditor pass at deletion time flags 21.0% of retained images and cuts leakage **12.0% → 2.0%** (≈6×), with no per-category blind spot.
- **Generalization & rigor:** reproduced across two VLMs, the production Mem0 pipeline (16.3% end-to-end), and real photographs (10.6%); judged by a 3-model ensemble (GPT-4.1 + Claude Sonnet 4 + Gemini 2.5 Flash), dual-annotator human validation κ = 0.88, all with 95% Wilson CIs.

## Methodology

**Framework — Information Provenance Graph.** For a fact *f* in system *S*, IPG *G_f = (V_f, E_f, δ)* is a DAG where *V_f* is every distinct representation of *f* across storage layers (text entry, text/image embeddings, image file, graph edges, derived facts, behavioral patterns, *implicit visual features*); *E_f* is derivation flow; *δ* assigns each node a deletion affordance — `addr` (deletable by ID), `link` (deletable only via a maintained provenance link), `unreach`/`persistent` (survives storage-level deletion, only addressable via content-aware auditing). Forgetting Completeness FCS(f) = deleted nodes / total nodes. Leakage (Def. 3) requires: (1) instructed to forget, (2) response reveals/reconstructs *f*, (3) evidence is *this user's* retained data — explicitly excluding generic model capability.

**Key conceptual distinction — engineering gap vs. forgetting residual.** For text-only systems every node is the product of an explicit storage op, so with comprehensive provenance tracking FCS(f)=1 is in principle achievable (an engineering gap). Images are different: a VLM forward pass encodes implicit attributes (geolocation, style, cultural context) that are never stored as explicit entries and are distributed across embedding space — yet are empirically recoverable. Provenance tracking therefore cannot reach these *persistent* nodes.

**Dataset.** 113 synthetic profiles × 20 facts (10 forget / 10 retained) across location/profession/preference/relationship/possession, each with a visual-grounding level (explicit/implicit/absent). Images generated by Gemini 3.1 Flash with GPT-5.4 prompts; 523 real Unsplash photos for ecological validity. Three evaluation subsets: text-only scale (113 profiles, 1,053 probes), multimodal scale (38 profiles, 300 probes, 536 images), multimodal pilot (5 profiles, 55 probes).

**Systems evaluated (Table 1).** Long-context (behavioral suppression, leaks 17.0%), Naive (text-delete, 0.3%), Mem0 production RAG (0.9%), Letta/MemGPT simulated (0.5%), Oracle provenance cascade (0.4%) — all deletion-capable systems <1% on text-only direct probes.

**Probe types (Table 3).** P1 text-retract/image-probe (link), P2 image-retract/text-probe (link reversed), P3 invisible persistence (unreach — fact never stored as text), P4 cross-image reconstruction (derived), P5 behavioral residue. Scale eval focuses on P1 (most deployment-relevant).

**Metric — Cross-Modal Leakage Rate.** CMLR(A→B) = fraction of facts leaked via channel B after retracting via A. Leakage judged by a 3-model LLM ensemble (GPT-4.1, Claude Sonnet 4, Gemini 2.5 Flash), majority vote (≥2/3 = leaked; ambiguous → not leaked). All rates with 95% Wilson CIs; human dual-annotation (n=100, κ=0.88) for calibration.

**Mitigation — content-aware semantic deletion.** After deleting text + tagged images, a VLM auditor scans remaining images for semantic correlation with each deleted fact and flags candidates; one extra VLM pass at deletion time.

## Results & Highlights

| Evaluation Mode | Rate | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| Same-fact image recovery (upper bound, tagged images *not* deleted) | 48.7% (146/300) | [43.1, 54.3] |
| **Retained-image recovery (Claude Sonnet 4)** — core result | **12.0% (36/300)** | [8.8, 16.2] |
| Retained-image recovery (GPT-4o) | 9.3% (28/300) | [6.5, 13.2] |
| Mem0 end-to-end (production system) | 16.3% (49/300) | [12.6, 20.9] |
| Real Unsplash photos | 10.6% (30/282) | [7.6, 14.8] |
| Retained-text inference (no images) | 18.3% (55/300) | [14.4, 23.1] |
| Blind inference (no context) | 0.0% (0/300) | [0.0, 1.3] |
| Negative controls (FPR) | 0.3% (1/300) | [0.1, 1.9] |

- **Deletion cascade shrinks but never eliminates leakage:** 48.7% (same-fact) → 12.0% (after provenance-cascade tagged-image deletion) → **2.0%** (after content-aware semantic deletion).
- **Images are a distinct channel:** of 36 image-leaked cases, only 17% are text-entailed, 72% require visual evidence beyond text; **47% (17/36) are not recovered by the text-only baseline at all** — invisible to text-only auditing.
- **Cross-VLM generalization:** Claude 12.0% vs GPT-4o 9.3% (overlapping CIs); the two agree on 89.3% of probes but exploit *different* visual signals — union rate 16.0% > either alone, so multi-VLM auditing catches more leaks.
- **Production-system realism:** Mem0 end-to-end 16.3% (slightly higher than direct probing because it mixes visual inference with text-retrieval residue) — production `forget()` APIs do not behaviorally guarantee removal.
- **Real-image realism:** Unsplash photos 10.6%, fully overlapping CIs with the 12.0% synthetic baseline → not a generation-pipeline artifact.
- **Per-category (Table 5):** location (16.7%) and possession (15.2%) leak most; relationship (5.1%) and preference (7.8%) least. Semantic deletion reduces all categories uniformly to 1.5–3.4%.
- **Semantic deletion cost:** flags 381/1,812 (21.0%) retained images; of 36 baseline leaks, 35 eliminated; remaining 6 = 1 persistent diffuse case + 5 marginal/judge-variance.
- **Judge reliability:** inter-judge κ = 0.73–0.91; human dual-annotation 94% (κ=0.88); ensemble–human κ=0.84. Unanimous verdicts (0/3, 3/3) agree with humans 96–100%; split 1/3 votes only 37.5% (flagged for human review).

## Authors & Credibility

- **Kuan Wang, Chao Zhang — Georgia Institute of Technology** (Gatech). Two-author paper from a reputable ML group.
- **Rigor signals:** pre-registered style baselines (blind 0.0%, negative FPR 0.3%) to rule out prior knowledge and judge artifacts; multi-model judge ensemble with majority vote; reported Cohen's κ for both inter-judge and judge–human agreement; 95% Wilson CIs on every rate; cross-VLM, cross-system, and real-image generalization; honest confound disclaimers (e.g., Mem0 16.3% mixes text-retrieval residue with visual inference — explicitly *not* disentangled).
- **Open science:** dataset, probes, and scripts released under MIT at `huggingface.co/datasets/memleak-bench/memleak-benchmark`; the 100 dual-annotated human samples are released for judge-calibration research.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — High.** Framing agent forgetting as a *provenance/deletion-affordance* problem on a heterogeneous memory stack, and isolating *retained cross-fact images* as a leakage channel that text-only auditing provably misses (47% non-text-recoverable), is genuinely new relative to prior multimodal-unlearning benchmarks that mostly edit weights rather than audit deployed memory systems.
- **Rigor — Solid.** Calibrated FPR + blind baselines, 3-model judge ensemble with κ reporting, cross-model/system/image-distribution checks, and CIs throughout. The entailment stratification (17% entailed vs 72% visually correlated) is a nice control distinguishing text from visual contribution.
- **Reproducibility — Strong.** MIT-licensed dataset + scripts on HuggingFace; exact model IDs given (gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview, gpt-5.4); human-annotated samples released. Main residual risk is closed/proprietary judge models (GPT-4.1, Claude Sonnet 4, Gemini 2.5 Flash), which may drift.
- **Significance — High and timely.** Directly relevant to GDPR Art. 17 / RTBF compliance for deployed multimodal agents (Mem0, Letta/MemGPT, Graphiti/Zep). The "production deletion APIs do not behaviorally guarantee removal" finding is operationally important.
- **Red flags / caveats:**
  - Only **two named authors**; human validation sample is modest (n=100 dual-annotated).
  - **Same-fact mode has weak ensemble–human agreement (κ=0.47)**, and the 48.7% is therefore a *lower bound* — the paper is commendably explicit about this but it means the upper-bound number is noisy.
  - **Unsplash is still stock photography**, not in-situ user data — the authors flag this as open, but it bounds ecological validity.
  - The 2.0% post-semantic-deletion residual includes 5 *new* marginal leaks attributable to "noise reduction or judge variance," suggesting the mitigation's headline number is partly within judge noise.
  - No precision/recall curve for semantic deletion — a single operating point (21% image cost) is reported.
  - Scale is bounded (n=300 multimodal probes, 38 profiles) — CIs are correspondingly wide (~±4 pp).

## Strengths

- Identifies a concrete, previously under-examined privacy failure mode in *deployed* multimodal agent memory (not toy weight-editing).
- Unifying, *formal* taxonomy (IPG) plus a reusable benchmark with calibrated baselines and released data/code.
- Clean separation of three channels (linked-node / correlated-text / persistent-image) and explicit distinction between an *engineering gap* (solvable with provenance tracking) and an *architectural residual* (cross-fact visual entanglement).
- Practical, deployable mitigation (one extra VLM pass) with honest cost reporting (21% image removal).
- Intellectual honesty: every confound (Mem0 mixing, same-fact κ, Unsplash realism) is explicitly stated rather than buried.

## Limitations

- **Residual leakage is not eliminated** (2.0%); the paper positions this as requiring representation-level interventions (privacy-aware vision encoders, embedding scrubbing) that are future work.
- **Ecological validity bounded by Unsplash stock photos** — authentic user photo collections may exhibit different cross-fact correlations; generalization is open.
- **Judge noise intrudes on the mitigation result** — 5 of the 6 post-deletion "leaks" are marginal/judge-variance, so the 2.0% sits partly within measurement noise.
- **Single semantic-deletion operating point** (21% cost) — no precision/recall trade-off curve for operators to tune.
- **Proprietary, drifting judge models**; closed VLMs (GPT-4o, Claude Sonnet 4) — long-term reproducibility of exact rates is not guaranteed.
- **Modest human-annotation scale** (n=100) and only two authors.
- **No defense against the *union* leakage** (16.0% across both VLMs) — mitigations are evaluated per-model.

## Relevance

对关注**机器遗忘（machine unlearning）**与**多模态 Agent 隐私安全**的研究方向高度相关：它否定了"删除文本条目即完成遗忘"这一工程假设，揭示了保留图像通过 VLM 隐式视觉推理形成的、文本审计无法发现的独立泄漏通道，为遗忘机制的可验证性、跨模态数据溯源与隐私合规（GDPR/RTBF）提供了新的问题定义、形式化框架（IPG）和可复现评测基准。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- By what precise mechanism do *unrelated* retained images (tagged to other facts) leak a deleted fact — is it shared scene context, recurring objects, or geolocation, and can it be predicted a priori?
- Can content-aware semantic deletion be driven to zero residual without removing 1/5 of all retained visual memories — e.g., via redaction/blurring instead of deletion?
- How does leakage scale with memory size, image count per profile, and VLM scale/frontier capability?
- Does the threat model assume the adversary knows *which* fact was deleted (white-box prompting)? How robust is leakage under strictly black-box access?
- Are there analogous persistent-node channels for **audio/video** memory, and does the IPG taxonomy extend cleanly?
- Can the union-of-VLMs leakage (16.0%) be closed, or is multi-VLM auditing itself the recommended compliance posture?
- What is the precision/recall curve for semantic deletion, and what downstream utility (retained-fact recall) does the 21% image-removal cost impose?
