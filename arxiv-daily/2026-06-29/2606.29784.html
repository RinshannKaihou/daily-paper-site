HERO: Improving the Reliability and Sensitivity of Generative Model Evaluation Using Historical Data

Xinrui Ruan · et al. · UC Berkeley / Roblox / USC / Nankai · 2026-06-29 · stat.ME cs.AI econ.EM
HERO generative-model-evaluation noisy-label-aggregation control-variates llm-evaluation statistical-estimation

Historical annotation data becomes sharper, cheaper model evaluation — anchored-EM bias reduction plus score-based control variates beat majority vote and Dawid–Skene.

A two-step, history-aware estimator that reframes generative-model evaluation as a longitudinal estimation problem: calibrate noisy “silver” labelers, then anchor on a precise historical covariate mean.

01 Key metric
~4–5×lower RMSE vs majority vote

At extreme labeler heterogeneity, RMSE drops from 0.255 → 0.058 (HERO-2). Simulation: n=300, N=800, B=200 reps.

02 Why this matters

Generative-model evaluation is a costly bottleneck; a principled method that sharpens sensitivity to small performance gaps is directly useful for production leaderboards.

03▲ Critical edge

Real-data variance gains are modest (~5–10%). The dramatic RMSE wins are in simulation, and empirical validation is confined to two proprietary 3D-asset tasks — broad generalization is asserted, not shown.

Methodology, distilled

Bias ↓ · Variance ↓

HERO treats evaluation as longitudinal estimation. A large historical set (size N) holds silver labels, covariates, and a sparse gold-routed subset; a small current set (size n ≪ N) has covariates and silver labels but no gold labels. Each labeler is described by transferable sensitivity αⱼ and specificity βⱼ — properties of the labeler, not the task, so they migrate across rounds.

1 Bias reduction · anchored EM

Estimate (αⱼ, βⱼ) from the full historical label matrix — both gold-routed and ungolded instances — via EM with missing gold treated as latent, the gold subset anchoring initialization. Transfer the fixed estimates to the current likelihood to form μ̂_HERO-1. Using the full matrix keeps it stable at low gold rates; majority vote emerges as the homogeneous special case.

2 Variance reduction · score-based CV

Regress the Step-1 score contributions ψ̂ᵢ on covariates X to get γ̂, then subtract γ̂ᵀ(X̄−m̂_X) from the estimating equation. Because the historical mean m̂_X is precise and shared across rounds, variance drops without shifting the asymptotic mean. HERO-2 gracefully falls back to HERO-1 when covariates (e.g. GPT-5 labels, TF-IDF) are uninformative.

Strengths & limitations

side-by-side
  • +Principled & grounded. Tackles a high-impact cost-vs-reliability problem with theoretical guarantees, not a heuristic.
  • +Clean decomposition. Bias (labeler calibration) and variance (control variates) handled separately, each independently justified.
  • +Graceful degradation. HERO-2 → HERO-1 when covariates are uninformative; HERO-1 → majority vote under homogeneous labelers.
  • +Robust in practice. Handles partial labeler overlap, low gold-label rates, and even a different historical task.
  • −Cross-round assumptions. Relies on labeler-reliability transfer and stable covariate distributions; may fail under sharp distribution shift.
  • −Modest real-data gains. ~5–10% SD reduction; the most dramatic RMSE improvements are in simulation.
  • −Proprietary data. Roblox 3D-asset benchmarks limit external reproducibility; no evident code link.
  • −Narrow scope. Binary labels throughout; pairwise win-rate & Precision/Recall use cases lack direct real-world benchmarking.

Open questions

for further exploration
Do the gains scale to large pairwise-preference LLM benchmarks (MT-Bench / Arena), where outcomes are ordinal, not binary?
Is there a principled diagnostic for reliability-transfer breakdown when rounds drift in distribution?
Below what sample-size / gold-rate threshold do Step-1 estimates of (αⱼ, βⱼ) destabilize for rare labelers?
Can the score-based control variate compose with active-learning routing to choose which instances to gold-label next?
Will code be released, and can proprietary claims be reproduced on public noisy-label benchmarks with synthetic gold routing?
How does HERO compare to modern LLM-as-a-judge debiasing (debate, self-consistency, anchor calibration), beyond Dawid–Skene?
