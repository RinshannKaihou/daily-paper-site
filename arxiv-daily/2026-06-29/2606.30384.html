01 Methodology

A single-hidden-layer MLP (784 → 64 tanh → 10) trains on MNIST by full-batch gradient descent, producing a weight sequence Ω(0), Ω(1), … of ≈ 5×10⁴ parameters — the trajectory under study.

The core move recasts this trajectory as a temporal network and compresses it to one real number via classical multidimensional scaling:

1.weight snapshots → adjacency matrices A(t)
2.pairwise squared-distance feature matrix D⁽²⁾ (size = #snapshots, not #params)
3.classical MDS preserving relative distances
4.set dim = 1 → scalar series z(t)

Chaos is read off the scalar: λ_MLE(η) from divergence of nearby z-trajectories, and a saturation time τ_decemb as a decorrelation proxy. At η = 10, asymptotic states z∞ yield rescaled nearest-neighbor spacings Δ′, tested for cross-condition universality (Anderson–Darling) and law-fit (one-sample KS).

03 Three training regimes

small ηsmooth, monotonic convergence

large ηstrongly irregular — learning fails

Strengths

  • Genuinely novel cross-disciplinary bridge — temporal networks + nonlinear dynamics + deep learning.
  • Quantitative invariants (Lyapunov, τ_dec, spacing) each with a statistical test, not just plots.
  • Embedding is a truly interpretable, visual reduction of intractable high-dim trajectories.
  • Honest framing of limits; cheap, reproducible setup with promised code.

Limitations

  • Only full-batch GD on one MLP + MNIST — no SGD/Adam, CNN, ViT, transformer.
  • Spacing-universality shown only at η = 10; robustness untested.
  • Information is lost in 5×10⁴ → 1; which features survive is not catalogued.
  • Descriptive, not prescriptive — no link to generalization or hyperparameter gain.

04 Questions for further exploration

  1. Does the embedding and its Lyapunov / τ_dec recovery hold for modern architectures (CNN, ViT, transformer) and adaptive optimizers, not just MLP + full-batch GD?
  2. Is the skew-lognormal spacing universality robust across η, datasets, depth/width, and init — or specific to η = 10?
  3. Could τ_decemb predict convergence speed, grokking onset, or generalization gap, turning the framework prescriptive?
  4. How much loss-landscape information is lost in the 5×10⁴ → 1 projection — which features (saddle escapes, plateaus, grokking) survive?
  5. Could the embedded curve serve as a diagnostic — flagging divergence, selecting edge-of-chaos learning rates, comparing trajectories across architectures?
  6. Is the inferred link between ⟨Δ⟩ and local Lyapunov exponents quantitatively testable, and does it hold at dim > 1?
