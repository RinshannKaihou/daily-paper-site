01Methodology

Belief-state maintenance, not archive search.

BayesEvolve argues that conditioning an LLM on an archive of top-scoring past candidates (FunSearch / AlphaEvolve style) leaves the evidence implicit. Instead it maintains an explicit predictive posterior P(y|h,D)=𝒩(μ,σ²) over the quality of unevaluated hypotheses — capturing both what the agent believes and how confident it is.

At each step it fits a Gaussian-process posterior over evaluation history, assembles a candidate pool from LLM proposals and archive mutations, then selects the next experiment by maximizing an annealed UCB-style rule that decays from exploration toward exploitation. The framework is surrogate-agnostic.

acquisition rule (minimization) a(h) = −μ(h) + βt·σ(h)
βt = β₀ · √(n₀ / t)   // anneals explore → exploit

The testbed is leakage-resistant: five shifted BBOB-style functions at d=5 with opaque task IDs (F01, …), n₀=6 shared random inits, budget T=100, averaged over 5 seeds × 5 functions. All LLM variants share the proposal model gpt-5.4-mini and differ only in context.

03aStrengths

  • Clean thesis distinguishing belief states from memory / archives.
  • Honest scoping — BBOB only; real program / lab discovery deferred.
  • Solid diagnostics: held-out predictiveness, rule ablations, diversity dynamics.
  • Anti-leakage design — opaque task IDs, hidden offsets, shared random inits.
  • “Productive late-stage concentration” is a genuinely useful characterization of BO-in-an-LLM-loop.

03bLimitations

  • Narrow testbed — 5 shifted BBOB functions, d=5, T=100; no program synthesis or wet-lab.
  • Statistically fragile win over GP-BO (0.195±0.043 vs 0.229±0.047 — overlapping ±1 SE).
  • Acquisition rule adds little — annealed bonus is marginal-to-negative vs mean-only.
  • Poor calibration (1σ coverage 0.433 vs ~0.683) undermines the uncertainty narrative.
  • No code release; candidate-pool mechanism underspecified; GP scaling untested.

04Open questions for further exploration

  1. Q1Does BayesEvolve beat GP-BO significantly with more seeds/functions, or is the edge within noise?
  2. Q2Given mean-only matches the full rule (and wins on AUC/evals-to-target), is the annealed bonus justified at all — or should the contribution be re-framed around the explicit belief mean?
  3. Q3How is the candidate pool Ct actually built from LLM proposals vs. archive mutations, and how sensitive are results to that mix?
  4. Q4Can the GP be replaced by a calibrated or neural surrogate to close the 1σ/2σ coverage gap?
  5. Q5Does the belief state transfer across related tasks (meta-learning / warm-start) and scale to higher dims & longer budgets?
  6. Q6Will it extend to program-discovery (AlphaEvolve-style) and wet-lab spaces, where “quality” is far less cleanly defined?
