> **一句话总结：** 本文用一个可控的 2×2 实验设计揭示：JEPA 式预测目标会系统性丢弃"虽不可控（外生）、却对奖励/最优动作决定性相关"的特征——该特征即使可被编码也因不可预测而无梯度，所有五种无奖励预测目标在该特征上线性探测准确率≈0.51（≈随机），而注入仅 2% 带奖励标注的转移即可在 16–1024 维潜空间、两种环境中将其恢复到上限，并测得 JEPA 潜空间的类别分离（0.105）远低于 bisimulation 理论与有监督参考（≈1.998）。

> **TL;DR:** Through a controlled 2×2 design with a "predictability knob" that decouples a feature's temporal predictability from its control-relevance, this paper isolates a structural failure mode of JEPA-style predictive objectives: they discard features that are exogenous (uncontrollable) yet reward-relevant because the objective optimizes predictability, not control-relevance. All five reward-free predictive objectives tested leave the feature near chance (linear-probe ≈ 0.51, MI ≈ 0 nats); a reward-grounded JEPA variant retains it selectively, and as little as 2% reward-labeled transitions recovers it. The learned JEPA latent realizes a class separation (0.105) far below what bisimulation theory and supervised/reconstruction references attain (≈1.998).

## 核心贡献

- **首次在受控隔离下测量"外生且控制相关"特征（taxonomy 第 4 格）的失效**：用可预测性旋钮 `p_repeat` 把特征的时间可预测性与控制相关性解耦，从而排除"目标只是丢弃了无关特征"这一替代解释。
- **给出一个干净的特征分类法（2×2）**：可控性 × 相关性 → 四格，明确指出前三格已被充分研究，第 4 格（不可控 + 相关）此前只被"命名"为风险而未被隔离测量。
- **系统对比六种目标 + 两个参考**：reconstruction（像素参考）、JEPA、action-conditioned JEPA、controllability-based JEPA、random-policy inverse dynamics、reward-grounded JEPA，外加 label-supervised 上界参考——所有无奖励预测目标在第 4 格上接近随机。
- **证明该失效是"选择性丢弃"而非表征坍塌**：丢弃该特征的目标在 QuadrantEnv 上仍保持 effective rank 38–42（表征依然丰富），仅这一特征缺失。
- **提出标签高效、稳健的补救方案**：仅 2% 带奖励标注转移即可越过保留阈值（准确率 0.77），效果在两种不同表层形态的环境中、在 16–1024 维潜空间内保持，且奖励接地按"相关性轴"选择性保留（保留第 1、4 格，丢弃第 2、3 格）。
- **将实测潜空间几何与 bisimulation 理论对照**：第 4 格的解析 on-policy bisimulation 距离为 1.0，有监督/重建参考实现类别分离 ≈1.998，而 JEPA 仅实现 0.105，分离缺口 1.893。

## Key Contributions

- **Controlled isolation of the "exogenous + control-relevant" cell (cell 4)** via a predictability knob `p_repeat ∈ [0.5, 1.0]` that independently varies a feature's temporal predictability while holding its control-relevance fixed — ruling out "the objective dropped it because it was irrelevant."
- **A 2×2 feature taxonomy (controllability × relevance)** that reframes prior distractor work as covering cells 1–3, leaving cell 4 (uncontrollable but reward-relevant) previously named-as-risk but unmeasured in isolation.
- **Six-objective + two-reference comparison** under byte-identical encoders and disjoint evaluation seeds, showing every reward-free predictive objective leaves cell 4 near chance.
- **Distinguishes selective dropping from representational collapse**: objectives that lose the feature still keep effective rank 38–42 on QuadrantEnv, so the representation is rich but simply omits this one feature.
- **A label-efficient, robust remedy**: reward grounding recovers cell 4 with as little as 2% of transitions reward-labeled; the effect holds across two environments with different surface forms and across latent dims 16–1024, and its selectivity axis is *relevance* (keeps cells 1 & 4), not controllability.
- **Empirical bisimulation comparison** quantifying the gap: analytical bisimulation distance 1.0; supervised/reconstruction references realize class separation ≈1.998; JEPA realizes only 0.105 (separation gap 1.893).

## Methodology

**Feature taxonomy and the predictability knob.** Features are organized on two binary axes: *controllability* (whether the agent's action moves the feature; `c_{t+1}` is a function of `a_t`) and *relevance* (whether the feature determines reward and hence the optimal action `a* = c_t`). The four cells: (1) controllable+relevant (trivially kept), (2) controllable+irrelevant, (3) uncontrollable+irrelevant (the classic exogenous distractor), (4) uncontrollable+relevant (the target failure). The predictability knob `p_repeat ∈ [0.5, 1.0]` controls only how predictable an exogenous feature is (`P(c_{t+1}=c_t) = p_repeat`; 0.5 = i.i.d. Bernoulli / maximally unpredictable, 1.0 = constant) while keeping control-relevance fixed across the whole range. Reward is 1 iff the action matches the relevant feature bit; all main results at `p_repeat = 0.5`.

**Environments (deliberately small and synthetic).**
- *QuadrantEnv* (primary): 12×12 grayscale obs; a single 3×3 corner patch encodes the target bit, superimposed on a deterministically-advancing multi-frequency background (four spatial frequencies with deterministic phase) — predictable structure the encoder *can* model, making the contrast with the unpredictable patch clean.
- *SwitchColorEnv* (32×32): bit encoded in patch intensity over a sinusoidal grating.
- *GridWorldHiddenRuleEnv* (gridworld form): bottom-right patch over a drifting texture with an agent marker. The two surface forms share cell-4 logic but differ in resolution, texture statistics, and intrinsic dimensionality.

**Objectives (byte-identical encoder; differ only in consumed signal).** recon (pixels), jepa (latent self-prediction, baseline), jepa_ac (action-conditioned, reward-free), jepa_ctrl (controllability-based via inverse-dynamics head, reward-free), jepa_invdyn (inverse dynamics under random policy — flagged separately because it only works if the policy correlates with the feature), jepa_reward (reward labels + optional bisim term — the proposed variant), plus a label-supervised reference (upper bound, not practical).

**Training.** 4000 steps, batch 128, lr 1e-3, latent dim 128, three seeds {0,1,2}. Justified by deterministic environments and large effect sizes (retention near chance or ceiling, not intermediate).

**Metrics.** (1) Linear-probe accuracy (primary; chance 0.50, retain threshold 0.75). (2) InfoNCE mutual information lower bound on `I(Z;c)` in nats (max log 2 ≈ 0.693 for a one-bit feature), trained on a held-out split to corroborate the probe without sharing its decision boundary. (3) Effective rank (exp of entropy of normalized singular values), reported with every probe to separate *selective dropping* (high rank, feature gone but representation rich; threshold >5) from *representational collapse* (low rank). **Implementation invariants** are enforced: a byte-identical encoder shared by every objective, probes reading the online encoder on a disjoint evaluation seed (asserted automatically where possible).

## Results & Highlights

- **Central result (objective × cell retention matrix, Fig. 1).** Only reward grounding (`jepa_reward`) and the two references (recon, supervised) retain cell 4. The reward-grounded variant retains exactly the relevant cells (1 and 4) and drops irrelevant cells (2 and 3) — its selectivity axis is *relevance*; `jepa_ctrl`'s selectivity axis is *controllability* (keeps cells 1–2 at ≈0.67), which is the wrong axis for control.
- **Cell-4 failure across both environments (Table 2, QuadrantEnv).** `jepa` 0.51±0.02 (MI 0.00±0.00); `jepa_ac` 0.52±0.03; `jepa_ctrl` 0.52±0.02; `jepa_invdyn` 0.52±0.02 — all at chance with ~zero retained information. `jepa_reward` 1.00±0.00 (MI 0.69±0.00), the one-bit ceiling, matching recon and supervised.
- **GridWorldHiddenRuleEnv wrinkles.** Order preserved (`jepa` 0.51±0.02, `jepa_reward` 1.00±0.00), but action-using objectives are noisier (`jepa_ac` 0.61±0.08, `jepa_invdyn` 0.60±0.10) — large stds are themselves the point: retention tracks *action informativeness*, not the feature.
- **Selective omission, not collapse.** QuadrantEnv discarding objectives keep effective rank 38–42 (rich representation). GridWorld has effective rank ≈3 (range 2.75–4.61), but this is the environment's low intrinsic dimensionality, not collapse — recon retains the feature perfectly at rank 3.30.
- **Not a capacity problem (Fig. 3).** Latent dim 16→1024: JEPA stays 0.510→0.558, never crossing 0.75; `jepa_reward` holds 1.00 at 16–512, dipping only at 1024 (0.907 / 0.932) — a training-budget artifact, not a capacity floor. The failure is objective-structural.
- **Label-efficient remedy (Fig. 4).** Retain threshold (0.75) first crossed at 2% reward-labeled transitions (mean acc 0.77); even 1% yields 0.72, well above chance (0.50).
- **Bisimulation comparison (Sec. 5, Fig. 5).** Analytical on-policy bisimulation distance for cell 4 = 1.0 (>0 ⇒ should be kept); supervised and recon realize latent class separation ≈1.998; JEPA realizes only **0.105**, with linear-probe accuracy 0.49 (chance). Observed separation gap = 1.893. Reported as a single-seed point estimate under full training.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The crisp contribution is conceptual + methodological: the 2×2 taxonomy reframes the distractor literature (cells 1–3) and isolates cell 4, and the predictability knob is a genuinely clean device that makes the predictability/relevance orthogonality measurable rather than asserted. Closest priors (Toso et al. 2026 targeting cell-3 distractors via bisimulation; Ivashkov et al. 2026 inverse-dynamics regularizer; Xing et al. 2025 naming the risk) are distinguished precisely.
- **Rigor: Good, with stated caveats.** Byte-identical encoders, disjoint eval seeds, automated invariants, and three orthogonal metrics (probe / MI / effective rank) supporting each other are strong. The action-conditioned objectives' large GridWorld stds are *reported and interpreted* rather than hidden. However, only **3 seeds** (justified by deterministic envs and bimodal effect sizes) and the bisimulation comparison is a **single-seed point estimate** — limits statistical strength, though effect sizes are large enough that the qualitative claim is robust.
- **Reproducibility: Likely high in principle.** Small synthetic environments (12×12 / 32×32) with a single-feature spec and a `make_cell(cell, predictability)` helper are trivially cheap to reproduce; invariants are enumerated. Practical reproducibility hinges on whether the environments/probes are released (an Appendix A/D is referenced); the controlled design is inherently reproducible.
- **Significance: Moderate-to-high for the SSL-RL representation subfield.** Gives a concrete, named, fixable mechanism for why predictive objectives underperform on control-relevant information, with a cheap remedy (2% labels). Generalization to large pretrained models (V-JEPA 2-AC, DINO-WM) and real tasks is explicitly left as future work — so the impact on practical world models is prospective, not demonstrated.
- **Red flags / honest concerns:** (1) All results are in tiny synthetic environments — no Atari, robotics, or real pretrained JEPA models, so the *frequency* of this failure mode in natural data is unknown (acknowledged). (2) Single author raises the usual review/replication baseline question, though the methodology is unusually transparent. (3) The `jepa_ctrl` cell-1/2 value (0.67±0.23) is driven by a single seed hitting 1.00 — flagged by the author but worth noting as unstable. (4) Reconstruction "retains" the feature by keeping *everything* (including irrelevant distractors), so it is not a genuine competitor — the paper is honest that this is indiscriminate.

## Strengths

- Genuinely clean causal experimental design (predictability knob decouples the two axes), turning a vague "JEPA ignores unpredictable things" intuition into a measured, orthogonal phenomenon.
- Names and isolates a specific cell (cell 4) that prior distractor work does not cover, and explains *why* the fixes for cell-3 distractors are precisely the ones that also kill cell 4.
- Three corroborating metrics and an anti-collapse effective-rank check distinguish "selective dropping" from collapse — a common confound in representation papers.
- A label-efficient, axis-correct (relevance, not controllability) remedy with concrete numbers (2%, dims 16–1024, two environments).
- Exceptional self-awareness about scope (dedicated sections on what the result does *not* imply, and limitations) — rare and admirable.

## Limitations

- Confined to small synthetic environments; no demonstration on real pretrained JEPA models, Atari, robotics, or natural data — the practical prevalence of cell-4 failures is unknown (explicitly future work).
- Not a benchmark: no ranking on a task suite; 3 seeds only (justified but limited); bisimulation comparison is a single-seed estimate.
- GridWorld's intrinsically low effective rank (~3) means the anti-collapse check is calibrated for QuadrantEnv and must be interpreted differently there.
- Objective set is not exhaustive — claim holds for the evaluated reward-free predictive objectives, not every conceivable one.
- `jepa_invdyn` cell-4 failure is conditional on a random policy; under an informative policy it can retain the feature (which is effectively action supervision, not unsupervised rescue).
- The "remedy" requires *some* reward labels, so it does not solve the fully reward-free setting it diagnoses.

## Relevance

对关注自监督/预测式表示学习与强化学习的人来说，本文直击 JEPA 类方法在"控制有用性"上的结构性盲区，并给出标签高效的修正路径——与表示学习评估、潜空间世界模型（V-JEPA / DINO-WM）及 bisimulation 表示理论的日常研究兴趣高度契合。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the cell-4 failure mode manifest in a frozen **V-JEPA 2-AC or DINO-WM** encoder on a goal-not-given task, and can a weakly reward-conditioned adapter on the frozen encoder prevent it (as the 2% result suggests)?
- Can a fully *reward-free* auxiliary objective (e.g., a controllability-aware term that somehow captures relevance) close the gap, or is reward grounding structurally necessary for cell 4?
- How does the required labeled fraction scale with environment complexity and the *number* of simultaneously control-relevant-but-exogenous features?
- Could the whitened class-separation gap (1.893 here) serve as a general, objective-agnostic *diagnostic metric* for control-relevant information loss in trained world models?
- Is the 2% recovery threshold stable across seeds and p_repeat values, or does it degrade sharply as features become only *partially* predictable?
- In realistic vision-based RL (distractor backgrounds, partial observability), how often do genuinely exogenous-yet-reward-relevant features arise, versus being rare edge cases?
