> **一句话总结：** IG-Lens 将积分梯度（Integrated Gradients）沿隐藏状态路径做"伸缩式"分段，把目标 token 的预测概率变化精确、可加地分解到各 Transformer 层，softmax 与 LayerNorm 都保留在积分路径内而非线性化，且在任意步数下完整性浮点级成立；其代价是只测量"head-once 读出的条件边际效应"，而丢弃该层经上方 block 的因果效应。

> **TL;DR:** IG-Lens applies Integrated Gradients in a *telescoping*, per-segment fashion along a single path through the transformer hidden states, yielding a layer-wise attribution whose sum is *exactly* the change in a target token's probability — with softmax and LayerNorm kept inside the integration path. Its default prediction-aware estimator (inspired by IDGI) makes completeness hold to floating point at any step count, with no backward pass required. The price of exactness is that it measures a *conditional marginal* (what norm+head can read out of a layer given the previous chosen layer), discarding the layer's effect routed through upper blocks.

## 核心贡献

- **概率空间内首个精确可加的层归因**：不同于 Logit Lens / Tuned Lens 只给出"层级别"概率且不可加（其估计有偏且随深度非单调），IG-Lens 的层归因之和恰好等于目标 token 概率的总变化量 `p(final) − p(baseline)`（命题 1）。
- **保留 softmax 与 LayerNorm 的非线性**：区别于 Direct Logit Attribution / embedding 方法（仅在 logit 空间可加，且把 LayerNorm 冻结近似），IG-Lens 将 softmax 和 LayerNorm 置于积分路径内部，从而在用户真正关心的"概率空间"中保持可加性。
- **伸缩恒等式使完整性精确成立**：由于读出 `f(h)` 是一维概率，预测感知估计器把每段积分归约为端点值的伸缩求和，因此完整性在*任意步数 m* 下浮点级成立，消除了 Riemann 离散化误差（m 不再是精度旋钮）。
- **预测感知重加权（IDGI 思想）**：默认估计器把每个积分步归功于其*观测到的*目标概率变化 `f(h^(s)) − f(h^(s−1))`，而非原始梯度，从而抑制"有梯度敏感性但无输出变化"的步骤；对一维读出而言，这正是 IDGI 重加权退化的形式。
- **清晰的方法学定位（Table 1）**：明确论证现有所有可加层归因方法要么在 logit 空间、要么在概率空间有偏、要么不可伸缩求和，IG-Lens 是首个同时满足"概率空间、可加、和=Δp、norm 在环路内、无需训练"的方法。
- **单次前向、无反向的批量实现**：在默认估计器下，所有网格点（跨 token×层×步）对点式 `f` 独立求值，单次 batched forward 即可算出完整 token×layer 归因图，无需 autograd。

## Key Contributions

- **First exactly additive layer-wise attribution in probability space.** Logit Lens / Tuned Lens only report a per-layer *level* of probability and are biased and non-monotone across depth, so their per-layer values sum to nothing. IG-Lens's per-layer contributions telescope to exactly `p(final) − p(baseline)` (Proposition 1).
- **Keeps the softmax nonlinearity (and LayerNorm) *inside* the integration path** rather than linearizing/freezing it as Direct Logit Attribution / embedding-space methods must, so additivity holds in probability space — the quantity one usually cares about.
- **A telescoping identity with proof.** Because the readout is a one-dimensional probability, the prediction-aware estimator collapses each segment to a telescoping sum of endpoint values, so completeness holds *exactly* (to floating point) at *any* step count `m`, removing Riemann discretization error and making `m` a non-accuracy knob.
- **Prediction-aware reweighting (IDGI-inspired).** The default estimator credits each integration step its *observed* change in target probability `f(h^(s)) − f(h^(s−1))` rather than its raw gradient, which (i) filters out "sensitivity-without-response" steps and (ii) for a scalar readout is exactly the degenerate form of IDGI's per-dimension redistribution.
- **Clear positioning (Table 1).** Explicitly argues every existing additive layer-attribution method is either in logit space, biased in probability space (due to softmax/LayerNorm), or non-telescoping (Layer Conductance attributes each layer to its *own* baseline). IG-Lens keeps all five properties simultaneously and trains nothing.
- **Single-pass batched implementation with no backward call.** Under the default estimator all grid points are independent w.r.t. the pointwise readout `f`, so one batched forward over `N = T·K·(m+1)` points recovers the full token×layer map; only the head materializes an `N×V` logit tensor (chunked, value-invariant).

## Methodology

**Setup.** For a decoder-only transformer, fix a position `i` and target token `yₜ`. Define the *head-once* readout
`f(h) = softmax(head(norm(h)))_{yₜ} ∈ [0,1]`,
i.e. the final LayerNorm and unembedding applied *once* to a single detached hidden vector. Critically, `f` is pointwise in `h` — it contains no attention and no MLP; all cross-token mixing already happened in the forward pass that produced the detached hidden states `h⁰,…,hⁿ`.

**Telescoping attribution (Proposition 1).** Choose layers `0=L₀<L₁<…<Lₖ=n` (final layer always included) and a baseline `h^{L₀} := h_base` (per-position mean over the sequence). Define each layer's contribution as the segment IG of `f` along the straight line from `h^{L_{j−1}}` to `h^{L_j}`:
`IG_{L_j} = ∫₀¹ ∇f(h^{L_{j−1}} + a(h^{L_j} − h^{L_{j−1}}))·(h^{L_j} − h^{L_{j−1}}) da`.
By the gradient theorem (FTC for line integrals), each segment integral equals the endpoint difference `f(h^{L_j}) − f(h^{L_{j−1}})`, so summing over `j` telescopes to `f(h^{final}) − f(h^{base})` exactly.

**Prediction-aware estimator (default, Eq. 4).** Instead of a raw-gradient Riemann sum, partition each segment by a grid `0=a₀<…<a_m=1` and credit step `s` its observed output change `f(h^{(s)}) − f(h^{(s−1)})`. Two consequences: (i) it telescopes within a segment to the endpoint difference *identically* for any grid and any `m≥1` (only float-summation error, no discretization error), so `m` ceases to be an accuracy knob; (ii) a step contributes nothing if the output doesn't change across it, filtering spurious-sensitivity steps. This is precisely the degenerate (scalar-output) form of IDGI's redistribution.

**Implementation.** Naive triple loop over (token, segment, step) is the correctness baseline. The default *single-pass forward* variant stacks all `N = T·K·(m+1)` grid points into one tensor, evaluates `p = f(X)` in one forward pass (chunked over rows because `head` materializes `N×V` logits), reshapes to `[T,K,m+1]`, and takes consecutive differences along the step axis then sums — no autograd. A *single-backward batched IG* reference variant exploits the block-diagonal Jacobian (points are independent because `f` is head-once) to verify the two estimators agree as `m→∞`. Both reductions are valid *only* because of the head-once design: with the full forward pass, attention in upper blocks would mix tokens and break per-point independence.

**Derived quantity: onset layer `L⋆`.** The earliest layer by which a fixed fraction of target probability is read out. The implementation uses a signed, hold-to-end cumulative rule on `IG_L` (more faithful to non-monotone trajectories than an `|·|`-based rule).

## Results & Highlights

**No empirical evaluation is performed — the paper is methodology + positioning + numerical sanity checks.** Specifically:

- **Completeness to floating point (verified, not just claimed).** On a controlled norm+head readout, the default forward-difference estimator reproduces per-segment endpoint differences to ~10⁻⁷ (float32 summation order only). Telescoping completeness holds to the same order on **Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct**, independent of `m` — identical to floating point at both `m=4` and `m=64`. The reference Riemann variant (raw gradient) matches only in the limit, with a gap of order `1/m` at small step counts, confirming the discretization error the default estimator removes is real.
- **Illustrative output (Table 2).** Per-token attribution for "What is the capital of Vietnam?" → "The capital of Vietnam is Hanoi." Each full row sum matches `Δp` to floating point at `m=4`. Attribution mass concentrates where the logit-lens value transitions low→high: content tokens *capital*, *of*, *Vietnam* saturate by L12 (`IG_{L12} ≈ 0.96–0.97`, onset `L⋆=12`); function/tokenized tokens (*The*, *H*, *anoi*) carry most mass in upper layers (`L⋆=15,16`), with the omitted L16 segment holding e.g. `IG_{L16}=0.59` for *anoi*.
- **Positioning table (Table 1).** IG-Lens is the only method that is simultaneously: in probability space, additive, summing to `Δp`, with norm in the loop, and training-free. Logit/Tuned Lens fail additivity; DLA/embedding methods live in logit space with frozen norm; Layer Conductance integrates through the norm but attributes each layer to its own baseline so terms don't telescope.
- **Evaluation is a *plan*, not results.** Section 4 proposes (does not run): (1) a difficulty correlation between `L⋆` and token difficulty/logit gap, vs. lens-crossing onset; (2) a **causal ablation test** as the primary validation — if IG-Lens puts onset at layer `L`, ablating `L` should depress target probability more than ablating a low-onset layer. The authors explicitly treat a negative ablation result as capable of *falsifying* their claim, and leave running it as future work.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (moderate, incremental).** The telescoping/segment-IG trick itself is an elementary consequence of the gradient theorem and the author says so explicitly. The genuine novelty is the *combination*: applying it to get an *additive decomposition of probability* (not logits) with softmax/LayerNorm *inside* the path, plus the prediction-aware estimator that makes completeness step-count-independent. The positioning relative to Logit Lens, DLA, and Layer Conductance (Table 1) is accurate and fills a real gap, but each individual ingredient (IG, telescoping, IDGI reweighting, logit-lens readout) is prior work.
- **Rigor (theoretical high, empirical absent).** The proof of Proposition 1 is correct and stated cleanly; the completeness-to-floating-point checks are a meaningful internal validation. However, **there is no empirical evaluation of the central utility claim** (that the decomposition/onset is *meaningful*). The Evaluation Plan (Section 4) is explicitly prospective. This is the paper's dominant weakness: it proves the method computes *something exact*, but not that the *something* is the right thing.
- **Reproducibility (high).** Single-author, code public, no training, runs on a small open model (Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct), all hyperparameters (layer set, baseline = per-position mean, `m`) specified. The single-pass implementation is concrete enough to reimplement.
- **Significance (conditional, pending evaluation).** If the causal-ablation test in Section 4 confirms that IG-Lens onset predicts ablation-sensitive layers — *especially for non-monotone tokens* where logit-space methods misplace onset — this becomes a useful interpretability primitive. Until then it is a promising but unvalidated tool.
- **Red flags / honest caveats.**
  - **No real experiments.** Only a single illustrative example + numerical completeness checks. The headline "exact additive probability attribution" is true by construction and verified, but the *practical* claim (onset is meaningful) is untested.
  - **Conditional-marginal, not total-effect.** IG-Lens measures what `norm+head` can read out of `h^L` *given the previous chosen layer*, deliberately discarding the effect of `h^L` routed through upper attention/MLP blocks. The author flags this as a limitation, but it means IG-Lens and DLA answer *different* questions and IG-Lens may mislead users who want total causal effect of a layer.
  - **Layer-set dependence.** `IG_L` values depend on the chosen layer set (a layer absorbs the mass of the chosen layer immediately below it); onset can shift with the grid.
  - **Scale.** Validated only on a 1B model; behavior on larger / non-Llama architectures is untested.
  - **Memory.** The head materializes `N×V` logits (`V≈128k`), requiring row-chunking.

## Strengths

- Genuinely fills a gap: *exact, additive, probability-space* layer attribution with softmax/LayerNorm inside the path — no prior method combines these.
- Completeness to floating point at any step count is a strong, cleanly-proven theoretical property, and the numerical checks confirm it.
- No training and no backward pass: cheap, practical, easy to bolt onto any decoder-only model.
- Unusually honest self-assessment — the author clearly separates "what it is" from "what it isn't," discloses the conditional-marginal limitation, and designs the validation to be *falsifiable*.
- Signed decomposition exposes non-monotonicity (a negative `IG_L` marks a layer that *lowers* target probability), which level-reading lenses hide.

## Limitations

- **No empirical evaluation.** The Evaluation Plan (difficulty correlation + causal ablation) is proposed, not executed; all "results" are internal consistency checks and one illustrative example.
- **Measures a conditional marginal, not total causal effect** of a layer; users wanting the routed-through-upper-blocks effect should use DLA instead.
- **Layer-set dependence:** attribution values and onset shift with the chosen layer grid.
- **Scale/generalization untested:** only Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct.
- **Memory cost** from the `N×V` logit tensor (mitigated by chunking).
- **Onset definition is "preliminary"** in its `|·|` form and only finalized as a signed hold-to-end rule for the (un-run) evaluation.

## Relevance

对研究大模型可解释性、特别是"Transformer 哪一层真正决定了某个 token 的预测概率"这一归因问题高度相关：它提供了比 Logit Lens 更严谨、比 DLA 更贴近概率空间的归因工具。但其当前价值取决于尚未完成的因果消融验证，适合作为可解释性工具箱的候选方法跟踪关注。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- **Does IG-Lens onset predict the ablation-sensitive layer?** The central untested claim — run the Section 4 causal-ablation protocol and compare against logit-space / lens-crossing onsets, especially on non-monotone (saturated, hard) tokens.
- **How does the conditional-marginal interpretation diverge from total-effect (DLA-style) conclusions**, and for which tokens/questions does the divergence matter most?
- **Sensitivity to baseline and layer set:** how stable is `L⋆` to the choice of baseline (per-position mean vs. zero vs. mean-over-data) and to the layer grid resolution?
- **Scaling:** does the step-count-independent completeness and the onset behavior hold on models from 7B to 100B+ and across architectures (e.g. attention-free / hybrid)?
- **Can a *total-effect* variant that sacrifices exact completeness be a useful companion**, as the author suggests — and can its bias be bounded?
