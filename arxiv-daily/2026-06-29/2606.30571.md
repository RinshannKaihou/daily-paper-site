> **一句话总结：** 本文通过7个LLM × 20个争议话题的自对弈/混合对弈双边辩论实验发现：每个模型的自对弈轨迹会收敛到模型特有的"吸引子"潜在空间区域，混合对弈中两端点沿两个自对弈盆之间的轴相互靠拢（平均收缩23.6%），且这种影响高度不对称——Claude Haiku是最强的方向性吸引子（最不被拉动、把伙伴拉向其元评论等特质），而GPT-4.1 nano最易被塑造。

> **TL;DR:** Through 20-turn dyadic debates across 7 LLMs × 20 controversial topics, this paper shows self-play trajectories converge to model-specific "attractor-like" latent-space basins; in mixed-play, endpoints interpolate along the axis between the two self-play basins (mean contraction 23.6%), with strongly asymmetric influence — Claude Haiku is the clearest resistant attractor (pulling partners toward traits like meta-commentary), while GPT-4.1 nano is the most malleable.

## 核心贡献

- **首次用动力学系统"吸引子"框架刻画开放式多智能体LLM交互**：将自对弈（同模型两实例）收敛到的稳定潜在空间端点区域定义为"吸引子盆（attractor-like basins）"，并给出量化度量（盆分离度 S_basin）。
- **大规模系统性对照实验**：覆盖 GPT-4o-mini、GPT-4.1-nano、Gemini-2.5-Flash（及Flash-Lite）、Claude-4.5-Opus、Claude-4.5-Haiku、Grok-4.1、Qwen-3.5-Flash/9B、Nemotron-3-Nano 等9个模型，20个争议话题，对照 self-play / mixed-play / stance-free 三种设置。
- **提出混合对弈端点分解方法**：在两个自对弈盆连线构成的"自对弈轴"上，把位移分解为"朝向伙伴的插值分量 α（partnerward pull）"和"轴外漂移 δ⊥"，并由此定义"成对收缩 C_{A,B}"与"支配度 Δ_{A≻B}"，量化谁拉动谁。
- **揭示强不对称的吸引–可塑谱**：Claude Haiku 是最强的方向性吸引子（平均 α=0.266，最不被拉动，且支配多个伙伴），GPT-4.1 nano 最易塑（α=0.665，与 GPT-4o mini 配对收缩高达68.0%）。
- **将几何动力学与具体话语特质迁移关联**：用LLM裁判对 turn 打分，刻画 metacommentary、flattery、rationality、agreement、rebuttal、negativity、intensity 等特质；发现 Claude Haiku 是"元评论吸引子"，Gemini Flash Lite / GPT-4o mini / Qwen 3.5 是"奉承吸引子"。
- **证明立场（stance）动力学不能还原为收敛**：自对弈不一定放大立场，混合对弈不一定产生折中——存在软化趋中、角色反转（GPT 反方变支持）、极端立场持续（Grok）等多种模式，立场只是模型-模型复杂动力学的不完整视图。

## Key Contributions

- **Conceptual framing**: Introduces "attractor-like basins" from dynamical-systems theory to describe reproducible, model-specific endpoint regions that self-play LLM debates settle into (distinct from prior outcome-focused multi-agent work).
- **Large-scale controlled study**: 9 models × 20 controversial topics (pro/con refs from ProCon.org), 20-turn dyadic debates under self-play, mixed-play, and a stance-free Discussant ablation, plus 3-seed reproducibility checks.
- **Latent-space decomposition method**: Projects endpoints onto the axis between two self-play basins and decomposes displacement into **partnerward pull α** (interpolation toward partner) and **off-axis drift δ⊥** (consensus-unexplained residual); defines **pair contraction C_{A,B}** and **dominance Δ_{A≻B}**.
- **Quantified basin separation**: A basin-separation score S_basin > 1 for every model, showing each model's endpoint cluster stays locally separated from its nearest neighbor despite within-basin topic variability; robust to role (Supporter/Opposer vs neutral Discussant) and seed.
- **Asymmetric attraction–malleability profiles**: Claude Haiku is the clearest resistant attractor (lowest α=0.266, dominates Nemotron Δ=−0.538, GPT-4o mini −0.507, Gemini Flash −0.484); GPT-4.1 nano is most malleable (α=0.665); mean pair contraction 23.6% (range −1.3% to 68.0%).
- **Trait-level behavioral transfer**: Links geometric dynamics to discourse-trait transfer via LLM-judge turn scoring — Claude Haiku pulls partners toward meta-commentary, while Gemini Flash Lite / GPT-4o mini / Qwen 3.5 pull partners toward flattery.
- **Stance dynamics beyond convergence**: Demonstrates stance is an incomplete view of dynamics — self-play does not consistently amplify stance, mixed-play does not always produce compromise (shows neutralization, role-reversal, and persistent-extreme patterns).

## Methodology

- **Task**: Open-ended dyadic debates on 20 controversial social/policy topics, each with 3 pro and 3 con reference statements (ProCon.org). Each run starts from a neutral topic-specific statement and runs T=20 turns, producing one response trajectory per model.
- **Conditions**:
  - *Self-play*: both agents are independent instances of the same model, assigned Supporter / Opposer roles — provides each model's intrinsic endpoint baseline ("attractor").
  - *Mixed-play*: two agents from distinct models, same roles — isolates cross-model interaction.
  - *Stance-free ablation*: both instances same model with neutral Discussant role — separates model-intrinsic drift from role-induced dynamics.
- **Models**: GPT-4o-mini, GPT-4.1-nano, Gemini-2.5-Flash (+ Flash-Lite), Claude-4.5-Opus, Claude-4.5-Haiku, Grok-4.1, Qwen-3.5-Flash, Qwen-3.5-9B, Nemotron-3-Nano-30B-A3B (Opus run only 10-turn self-play for budget).
- **Representation**: Each response embedded as a 384-d SBERT vector, then **topic-centered** (subtract per-topic mean) to remove topic offset while retaining model/role/condition variation. PCs computed only from self-play embeddings → "SP-PCs" as common reference basis; full 384-d vectors used for quantitative metrics.
- **Key metrics** (per-topic then aggregated):
  - *Basin separation* S_basin(A) = min_{B≠A} d²_set(A,B) / W_s(A) — within-cluster spread vs nearest-cluster distance.
  - *Self-play axis* v_{A→B,k} = s_{B,k} − s_{A,k}.
  - *Partnerward pull* α_{A|B,k} = ⟨(m_{A|B,k}−s_{A,k}), v_{A→B,k}⟩ / ‖v‖² (0–1 = interpolation; >1 overshoot).
  - *Off-axis drift* δ⊥ (displacement orthogonal to the self-play axis).
  - *Pair contraction* C_{A,B,k} = 1 − ‖m_{A|B}−m_{B|A}‖ / ‖s_A−s_B‖.
  - *Dominance* Δ_{A≻B,k} = α_{B|A,k} − α_{A|B,k}.
- **Discourse-trait analysis**: LLM-judge scores each turn on meta-commentary, flattery, rationality, agreement, rebuttal, negativity, intensity; partner-conditioned trait transfer τ_{B←A} measures how much B expresses a trait more when paired with A vs in self-play.
- **Stance analysis**: Questionnaire-based stance (1–5 scale) tracked across turns for both Discussant baseline and Supporter/Opposer mixed-play.
- **Robustness**: role ablation (neutral Discussant reproduces basins) and 3-seed repetition (endpoints return to comparable regions).

## Results & Highlights

- **Self-play basins exist and are separated**: S_basin > 1 for all 8 reported models (Gemini Flash 4.08, Qwen 3.5 2.39, GPT-4o mini 2.05, Claude Haiku 2.31, Grok 4.1 1.84, Nemotron 1.81, GPT-4.1 nano 1.50, Claude Opus 1.55). Trajectories start shared but progressively separate into model-specific regions; pattern holds under neutral Discussant and across seeds.
- **Mixed-play partially contracts but preserves identity**: mean contraction 23.6%; strongest GPT-4o mini vs GPT-4.1 nano (68.0%) and GPT-4o mini vs Gemini Flash (52.7%); near-zero/negative for Grok vs Gemini Flash (−1.3%).
- **Asymmetric dominance**: Claude Haiku dominates Nemotron (Δ=−0.538), GPT-4o mini (−0.507), Gemini Flash (−0.484); Qwen 3.5 dominates Gemini Flash (+0.396). Influence is strongly pair-specific.
- **Attraction–malleability spectrum**: GPT-4.1 nano most malleable (α=0.665), then Nemotron (0.588), Gemini Flash (0.540); Claude Haiku least pulled (α=0.266). Off-axis drift δ⊥ concentrated ~0.8 (Grok 0.888 highest; Haiku 0.734 lowest), showing the 1-D self-play axis captures the main but not all displacement.
- **Trait transfer mirrors geometry**: Claude Haiku is a meta-commentary attractor (partners adopt its meta-commentary); Gemini Flash Lite, GPT-4o mini, Qwen 3.5 are flattery attractors. Self-play trait signatures are model-distinct (e.g., Haiku stands out on meta-commentary).
- **Stance ≠ convergence**: Discussant baseline shows each model stabilizes at a model-specific stance level (Grok more supportive; Haiku below midpoint 3.0) rather than drifting to extremes. Mixed-play Supporter/Opposer shows diverse patterns — Gemini/GPT/Qwen soften toward neutrality, GPT-Opposer reverses to supportive, Grok maintains extreme stance throughout.
- **Headline takeaway**: Multi-turn LLM interactions depend less on individual model capability than on *which models are paired*, *which are more influential*, and *which traits propagate*. Attractor influence is structured and asymmetric, offering partial predictability for autonomous-agent monitoring.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The dynamical-systems "attractor" lens applied to *cross-model* (mixed-play) open-ended LLM debate — rather than identical-model self-play or single-agent drift — is fresh. The decomposition of mixed-play displacement into partnerward pull + off-axis drift, and the link between latent geometry and concrete trait transfer, are original contributions.
- **Rigor: Good.** Controlled 3-condition design, per-topic aggregation before averaging, explicit role and seed ablations, bootstrap CIs (in appendix), and an alternative-embedding robustness check. Metrics are well-motivated and clearly defined.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate-to-Good.** Models are proprietary/commercial (behavior drifts over versions); SBERT embedding choice and LLM-judge prompts are conventional but full release details depend on appendix/code. Stance questionnaire and trait rubrics are described.
- **Significance: Notable.** Directly relevant to predicting/monitoring autonomous multi-agent deployments under limited oversight — a genuine gap, since prior work focused on task-utility outcomes rather than free-form interaction trajectories.
- **Red flags / honest caveats:**
  - "Attractor" is used **by analogy** (the authors are explicit: "in analogy to dynamical-systems attractors"); these are empirical endpoint regularities, not proven fixed points/basins with formal stability guarantees.
  - **Generalization is narrow**: only dyads, only debate format, T=20 turns, English topics. Whether attractors scale to >2 agents or non-adversarial cooperative settings is untested.
  - Results depend on the chosen embedding (SBERT 384-d) and LLM-judge; off-axis drift δ⊥ ≈ 0.8 indicates the self-play axis explains only part of the geometry — the "attractor" narrative could be sensitive to representation choices.
  - Mechanism (why Haiku attracts, why GPT-4.1 nano yields) is described correlationally, not explained.
  - Claude Opus self-play run at only 10 turns (budget) is a minor inconsistency.

## Strengths

- Clean, intuitive conceptual framework (attractor basins + axis decomposition) that turns a fuzzy phenomenon into measurable quantities.
- Broad model coverage (9 frontier/open models) and multi-axis evidence (latent, trait, stance) that mutually corroborate.
- Honest about limitations of the stance view and about the analogy status of "attractor."
- Actionable implications for designing and monitoring autonomous agent systems.

## Limitations

- Restricted to dyadic debates; no triads/populations, no cooperative tasks.
- "Attractor" framing is metaphorical; no formal dynamical-systems stability analysis.
- Embedding- and judge-dependent; off-axis drift large, so the 1-D interpolation story is partial.
- Causal mechanisms for attraction/malleability profiles are unexplained.
- Relies on commercial models whose behavior changes over time, threatening long-term reproducibility.

## Relevance

该工作对理解多智能体LLM系统的涌现动力学与可解释性很有价值，提出了可量化的"吸引子"度量来预测和监控自主智能体交互，与大模型可解释性、多智能体系统行为预测方向的研究兴趣高度契合。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do attractor basins persist with >2 agents (triads, small populations), or do new collective attractors emerge that no single model exhibits?
- What model properties (post-training method, size, RLHF vs. constitutional AI, training-data style) determine attractor strength vs. malleability?
- Can attractor effects be deliberately amplified/suppressed via system prompts, persona instructions, or decoding controls (e.g., to prevent trait contagion like flattery)?
- Is there a principled dynamical-systems formalization (Lyapunov/fixed-point analysis on activation dynamics) that matches the empirical endpoint basins?
- How sensitive are the basins to the embedding model and to longer horizons (T≫20)? Does off-axis drift shrink with more turns?
- Do these asymmetric influence patterns transfer to human–model conversations, with implications for manipulation/persuasion risk?
