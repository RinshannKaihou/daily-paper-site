Estimating Grammatical Gender Directions in Contextual Embeddings under Controlled and Natural Contexts ↗

Huanping Xiao, Yingji Li et al. · Jilin University

contextual-embeddings grammatical-gender debiasing multilingual-fairness Spanish · French

Key Metric

0.870

Centroid harmonic trade-off (H-Trade-off) — best of three estimators. SVM reaches 0.771, LDA only 0.578.

Why This Matters

First framework separating grammatical from semantic gender bias inside contextual embeddings — a foundation for fairer multilingual debiasing beyond English, where prior projection methods erased both.

Critical Edge

Reliance on a semantic gender direction to measure contamination that is itself the very bias being studied is somewhat circular — the authors concede the measurement "may not be sufficiently optimal."

01 · Methodology 方法

Gendered languages like Spanish and French bake two signals into every noun embedding: grammatical gender (obligatory article/adjective agreement) and socially learned semantic gender bias. Projection debiasing — built for genderless English — erases both and over-corrects. This work isolates them.

~5,988 Spanish / 5,998 French inanimate nouns (GPT-generated, Wikipedia cross-checked, manually filtered, gender-balanced) are each slotted into 10 controlled templates and ≥10 natural Wikipedia sentences, plus 100 occupation pairs per language reserved for evaluation only. Grammatical gender is treated as a latent linear subspace; its direction is estimated three ways and removed by orthogonal projection under 5-fold cross-validation.

Encoders
Frozen final-layer, L2-normalized: BETO, CamemBERT, mBERT, XLM-R
Contamination
WEAT-style cosine score → mean/median-normalized down-weighting of polluted nouns
Estimators
Centroid (mean-M − mean-F), supervised LDA, linear SVM hyperplane
Debias
Orthogonal projection of d_g; dual-objective harmonic trade-off score

Best context recipe

  • Controlled + unweighted wins across all six LM settings — e.g. BETO Spanish Rnoun 0.980.
  • Weighting slightly lifts occupation preservation but lowers the overall harmonic score — it rebalances, not corrects.
  • Natural Wikipedia contexts degrade noun reduction versus controlled templates.
  • Pattern is consistent across monolingual (BETO, CamemBERT) and multilingual (mBERT, XLM-R) encoders.

Interpretation: grammatical gender behaves as a low-rank global distribution shift, not a linearly separable classification — so discriminative estimators (SVM, LDA) stay more sensitive to noise.

03 · Strengths & Limitations 优势与局限

▲ Strengths

  • First principled attack on grammatical–semantic disentanglement in contextual embeddings — extends Zhou et al. beyond static vectors.
  • Dual-objective evaluation (Rnoun vs Pocc) with harmonic trade-off is sensible and non-gameable.
  • Comprehensive ablations: 3 estimators × 2 contexts × 3 weightings × 2 languages × 4 encoders.
  • Excellent reproducibility — public code, lexicons, templates, embeddings (GitHub + HuggingFace).

— Limitations

  • Scope limited to two Romance languages with stable binary gender; case–gender and non-binary systems unverified.
  • Headline contamination-aware weighting contributes little; only simple mean/median tested.
  • GPT-generated noun lists + manually-curated occupations — potential dataset bias; no reliability stats.
  • Intrinsic-only evaluation — no downstream-task fairness or utility check; assumes a linear subspace.

04 · Open Questions 待探索问题

1

Do centroid + controlled-context findings generalize beyond Romance — to German (3 genders), Slavic case–gender, or Semitic non-binary systems?

2

At which transformer layers do grammatical vs. semantic gender emerge, and would layer-specific estimation sharpen disentanglement?

3

Would adaptive/adversarial weighting turn the marginal rebalancer into a genuine corrective signal?

4

Does removing d_g improve fairness on downstream tasks (coreference, occupation classification) without hurting language understanding?

5

Could causal-intervention methods replace projection to handle grammatical gender's structural ambiguity?
