Key finding

C²R fixes both feature splitting and absorption in sparse autoencoders via a Minkowski-inequality regularizer — with no reconstruction tax.

C²R: Cross-sample Consistency Regularization Mitigates Feature Splitting and Absorption in Sparse Autoencoders ↗ · Haoran Jin, Xiting Wang, Shijie Ren, Hong Xie, et al. (USTC · Renmin Univ.) · 2026-06-29
Key metric

↓0.0991

Feature Composition (lower = cleaner structure) on Gemma-2-2B, 65,536 latents — strictly dominates OrtSAE on every metric incl. KL.

Why this matters

Splitting and absorption are the central obstacles to scaling SAEs for LLM interpretability — a fix that carries no reconstruction tax is directly usable by mech-interp researchers today.

⚠ Critical edge

The headline “theoretical guarantee” is conditional — it holds in only 88.1% of absorption pairs — and absolute gains over the best fair baseline (Ort) are sometimes modest.

1Methodology

C²R reframes two SAE pathologies — splitting & absorption — as one phenomenon: latent redundancy from a perturbed basis direction, quantified by a leakage parameter α∈[0,1].

  • Root cause (Lemma 4.1). Per-sample sparsity goals (ℓ1, TopK) mathematically prefer α→1 — splitting/absorption strictly lowers cost at equal reconstruction.
  • Fix from Minkowski. Since ‖u‖₂+‖v‖₂ > ‖u+v‖₂, penalizing the summed ℓ₂ norms of direction-similar latents drives them to consolidate into one. Loss rises monotonically with α.
  • Two safety gates (both ablation-confirmed necessary): nearest-neighbor only — penalize just the single most-similar decoder vector; ReLU(cos sim)² gating — leaves orthogonal / independent features untouched.

3Strengths & Limitations

+ Strengths
  • Genuinely unifying theory — one parameter α, one inequality, explains & fixes both pathologies.
  • Elegant, motivated gating (nearest-neighbor + ReLU cosine) — ablations prove it prevents indiscriminate merging.
  • Best Pareto point on structure-vs-reconstruction among reconstruction-preserving methods.
  • Broad, honest evaluation — many models, layers, datasets, seeds, backbones; stated limitations, no overclaiming.
  • Standardized tooling (SAEBench) + open code → high reproducibility.
− Limitations
  • Guarantee is conditional (Eq. 13), holding in 88.1% of pairs — an unconditional proof is open.
  • Thin scale/arch coverage — only dense 2B–8B models, no MoE, no >8B, two layers each.
  • AutoInterp relies on a proprietary judge (GPT-5-mini); exact interpretability scores need API access.
  • Risk of suppressing legitimate polysemanticity for moderately-aligned features — under-studied.
  • Some gains over Ort are small; Split metric appears to saturate on some 8B configs. O(k²) cosine cost may bite beyond 65k latents.

4Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does C²R behave at ≥1M latents, where splitting is most severe — does the chunked O(k²) cosine stay tractable?
  2. The ~12% of failing pairs — can they be characterized & patched (a fallback term)? Are they semantically meaningful?
  3. Does C²R still hold its preference reversal under JumpReLU / Gated encoders that change sparsity mechanics?
  4. Is there a principled “redundant vs. genuinely shared” threshold, validated on a ground-truth superposition benchmark?
  5. Do the structural gains predict better downstream causal utility (circuit discovery, steering) beyond the two SAEBench tasks tested?
