Key finding

SGD stabilizes & delocalizes Hessian curvature directionsvs.Adam persistently reorganizes them — concentrating curvature onto a tiny subset of parameters.

A physics-grounded pivot from Hessian eigenvalues (how sharp) to eigenvectors (which directions). Tracking the leading curvature vectors of an MLP reveals a robust, optimizer-dependent signature that eigenvalue-only analyses simply cannot see.

Why this matters

New diagnostics expose how Adam and SGD fundamentally differ in curvature exploration and parameter sensitivity — directly informing optimizer choice, targeted pruning, and uncertainty quantification.

Critical edge

Adam's localization is asserted, not causally isolated — no ablation separates its adaptive scaling from momentum — and the entire study is confined to a small 3-layer MLP.

Methodology

Two physics imports, applied to eigenvectors

A 3-layer MLP (widths 100-100-10, ReLU, Xavier init) trains on MNIST / FashionMNIST with cross-entropy, mini-batches of 128 over 10⁵ iterations. Three optimizers are compared head-to-head — SGD, Adam, SAM — each across 5 random seeds.

The top-3 Hessian eigenvectors are recomputed every ~100 steps (1,584 snapshots), evaluated on the full dataset via Lanczos + Hessian–vector products (Pearlmutter's trick) — never materializing the Hessian.

Two complementary statistics are then borrowed from disordered-matter physics:

Δ⁽ⁱ⁾(t_w, t_w+t) = (1/N)·‖v⁽ⁱ⁾(t_w) − v⁽ⁱ⁾(t_w+t)‖²₂  ←  displacement (two-time corr.)
IPR(e) = Σⱼ eⱼ⁴ ∈ [1/N, 1]  ←  localization (≈1/k ⇒ supported on k params)

Displacement benchmarks against a random-init null model; dependence on the waiting time t_w signals aging — a glassy-system hallmark. IPR tells whether the sharp direction lives everywhere or on a few weights.

Strengths
  • Novel, well-motivated eigenvector-centric lens on a heavily studied problem (optimizer comparison).
  • Two complementary, physics-grounded statistics with an explicit random-init null model.
  • Clean, interpretable SGD ↔ Adam contrast, with SAM interpolated in between.
  • Robustness checks across LR & batch size; honest reporting of the full-batch breakdown.
  • Practical hooks for pruning and uncertainty quantification via localization.
Limitations
  • Only a small 3-layer MLP on MNIST/FashionMNIST — generalization to modern architectures is the open question.
  • Only the top-3 eigenvectors tracked; bulk & bulk-edge behavior uncharacterized.
  • Adam-localization mechanism (adaptive scaling vs. momentum) not causally isolated.
  • No link established to generalization gap or final test performance.
  • Workshop paper — analysis is concentrated rather than exhaustive.
Open questions

Where this leads next

  1. Do these patterns transfer to CNNs, Transformers, and larger models — is Adam-localization a universal signature of adaptive optimizers?
  2. Is localization causally driven by per-parameter diagonal preconditioning rather than momentum? An ablation (Adam w/o adaptive scaling, or momentum-only) would decide it.
  3. Does stronger eigenvector localization predict the generalization gap or sharpness across datasets and architectures?
  4. Can IPR / displacement serve as live diagnostics — to schedule optimizer switching, detect edge-of-stability, or trigger pruning mid-training?
  5. How do momentum, weight decay, and LR schedules reshape the SGD–Adam displacement contrast and the aging regime?
  6. Is the aging behavior quantitatively consistent with glassy-dynamics / random-matrix eigenvector-flow theory — enabling analytic predictions?
