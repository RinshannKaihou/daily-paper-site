Why this matters

A probe's high AUC only proves it reads the prompt — not that it predicts the imminent action. Test any internal-state monitor with this falsifiable harness before trusting it.

⚠ Critical edge

Headline "negative results" could mislead: the desperate direction does raise blackmail (+11.1pp, p=0.015). The story is specificity-fragile, not a total null.

Methodology

A falsifiability harness, not a new monitor

The paper builds a testing framework and runs three existing probe families through it. A probe counts as "pre-action" only if scored on hidden states computed before the misaligned action (incl. tool calls) is generated. Any "readout can monitor" claim decomposes into:

  1. 01
    Construction validity — separates the populations that built it
  2. 02
    Pre-action prediction — fires before the action is generated
  3. 03
    Mechanistic specificity — the causal effect is concept-specific
Each claim must survive two controls: scenario/action-generalization + concept-specificity.
  • Disposition directionQwen2.5-Coder-32B vs insecure fine-tune · diff-of-means @ L24 · thresholded vs audited multi-turn transcripts
  • Behavioral prefill probeLlama-3.1-8B · logistic/MLP on partially-prefilled state · CONTINUES vs CORRECTS · cross-domain transfer
  • Emotion-concept vectorsGemma-3-27B (+ Qwen3-32B check) · 52-emotion steering · SummitBridge blackmail scenario

Stats: pre-specified gates, Bonferroni correction, 200-permutation label-shuffle nulls, cluster bootstraps, leave-one-condition-out, second-model replication.

  • Disposition: a subthreshold signal exists (L24 AUC 0.559, p=0.010) but never crosses the construction threshold.
  • Prefill: same reps decode prompt content at AUC 0.999 but future behavior at only 0.801 (+5.1pp) over a 0.765 majority.
  • Transfer: non-positive on 5/6 cross-domain pairs; a 512-unit MLP gives mean lift −4.2pp.
  • Emotion: a 12,704-token shared-prefix minimal pair is indistinguishable pre-divergence (min cos 0.99999893) — no state to read.
  • Specificity: unrelated learned directions shift blackmail too — sports 6.7%, weather 8.0%, cats 10.7%, geography 17.0%; sports sits below random (p=1.9×10⁻⁴).
  • Replication: Qwen3-32B fails to reproduce the calm-vs-random, desperate-above-baseline, and 3-way omnibus contrasts.

Strengths

What holds up

  • Reusable framework — turns vague monitoring claims into two concrete control families; directly actionable for probe builders.
  • Triangulation across 3 paradigms (direction / classifier / steering) × 3 model families, not one cherry-picked setup.
  • Statistically rigorous — pre-specified gates, Bonferroni, permutation nulls, cluster bootstraps; rare for a workshop paper.
  • Honest framing as scoped negatives, with an explicit non-claim that monitoring is impossible.
  • "Learned semantic control" is a concrete improvement over the random-vector convention in steering work.

Limitations

Where it stops

  • Scoped to 3 methods/scenarios; SAE features and nonlinear / temporal monitors are explicitly left open.
  • Emotion critique targets an adaptation (52 vs 171 emotions, single norm), not a faithful replication of Sofroniew et al.
  • Blackmail judge has moderate agreement (κ=0.59); absolute rates are rubric-conditional.
  • Two of three families are single-model; closed / proprietary frontier models unaddressed.
  • Shared-prefix test rules out only pre-difference prediction, not post-cue pre-action.

Questions for further exploration

  1. Could causal interventions (activation patching, counterfactual probes) recover genuine pre-action signal, or is the situation-vs-action gap fundamental to these representations?
  2. Do the negatives hold for SAE-feature monitors, sequence-level probes, or richer temporal aggregators — all left open by the authors?
  3. What would a monitor need to clear both generalization and specificity controls at once — and does any probe class do so today?
  4. How sensitive are the results to the single SummitBridge scenario — would a battery of distinct misalignment scenarios flip a verdict?
  5. Should all activation-steering specificity claims be re-evaluated against matched-pipeline content directions rather than Gaussian noise?
