> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 PHACT，一种"模型提议-确定性物理引擎认证"的闭环结构：通过将断言权从不可靠的语言模型转移到确定性引擎，并以"从固定输入派生认证量"的结构化契约（而非接受模型提供的值）使伪造在结构上不可能，在覆盖五个科学领域、三种模型规模、两种解码温度及注入故障引擎的80次对抗测试中实现零误认证。

> **TL;DR:** Rather than making a language model more reliable, PHACT strips its authority to assert: the model only proposes candidate physical designs, and a deterministic, ML-free physics engine alone certifies, refuses (impossible), or abstains (unknown). The paper's central insight is a *structural contract* — a directed dependency graph where goal-fixed quantities are read-only bound inputs, free design variables are the model's only writable nodes, and the certified quantity is a derived leaf the model cannot supply. Because the value being certified is *computed from fixed inputs, not accepted from the model*, forgery is impossible by construction — yielding zero false certifications across 80 adversarial trials (2 models, 2 temperatures, an injected engine fault) spanning five scientific domains, while improving feasible certification from 52% (bare model) to 88% (Gemini 2.5 Flash).

## 核心贡献

- **提出 PHACT（Physics-Anchored Certification）**：一种"模型提议、确定性引擎认证"的闭环，认证引擎不含任何机器学习、仅实现同行评审的物理方程，覆盖无人机空气动力学、DNA热力学、引力波天体物理、模拟电子滤波器、纳米颗粒药物制剂五个领域。
- **明确可信认证的结构性判据**：能被伪造的认证器是"接受模型提供待认证量"（answer-submitting validator）的形式；而"从固定输入派生待认证量"的结构化契约（structural contract）使伪造在结构上不可能——这是一种**接口设计**而非统计推断。
- **定义三值认证输出 certified / impossible / unknown**：使系统具备"拒绝作答"的能力，而这正是一味追求性能的裸模型所缺乏的；拒绝是有效裁决而非失败模式。
- **揭示并量化了"伪造"攻击**：在天体物理（闭式chirp-mass可逆）领域，裸认证器在贪心解码下15次试验中被伪造10次（67%）；而结构化契约在所有对抗条件下（25+25+15+15 = 80次）零伪造。
- **分离"能力"与"安全"两条独立维度**：能力决定系统*能否给出裁决*，但从不决定*裁决一旦给出是否正确*；从 Gemini 2.5 Flash 到 Llama 3.3 70B 到 Llama 3.1 8B，可行认证率从88%→66%→0（loop-fail），但三个模型均零伪造。
- **开源全部引擎、契约、目标语料与分析**：归档可在普通硬件上无需凭证、网络或语言模型即复现全部结果。

## Key Contributions

- **PHACT architecture** — a propose–certify loop pairing an unreliable language model (proposer only) with a deterministic, ML-free physics engine (sole authority to certify). The loop terminates in one of three verdicts: **certified**, **impossible** (genuinely distinct attempts all fail for the same physical reason), or **unknown** (goal outside the engine's competence).
- **The forgery attack and its structural fix.** A naive "answer-submitting" validator that accepts the model-supplied quantity under test is *forgeable*: on a fixed-system verification goal the model can silently change a goal-declared-fixed input (e.g., substitute a different mass pair) and submit a self-consistent but off-goal design that passes the checker. PHACT replaces this with a **structural contract** — a directed dependency graph with read-only *bound* nodes (goal-fixed), writable *free* design-variable nodes, and the certified quantity as a derived leaf. Since the certified value is computed from bound inputs, the model can neither change the system under test nor author the certified value.
- **Five-domain instantiation** with peer-reviewed engines: SantaLucia nearest-neighbor (DNA melting), post-Newtonian GW inspiral (chirp mass), RC transfer function (filter cutoff), DLVO theory (colloidal stability), and momentum theory (drone hover thrust).
- **Capability vs. safety separation** — established across an order-of-magnitude model ladder (Gemini 2.5 Flash, Llama 3.3 70B, Llama 3.1 8B). Feasible-certification rate scales with proposer capability (88% / 66% / 0%); false-certification rate under the structural contract is zero for *all* models. A weak proposer is unusable but not unsafe (it simply never issues a verdict).
- **Honest baselines** isolating PHACT's value: (1) merely *permitting* the bare model to refuse reduces but does not eliminate false certifications (7/25 → 2/25) and adds over-refusal; the structural contract is what drives it to zero. (2) For closed-form-invertible domains, a thin solver with no LM certifies all targets — locating the LM's true marginal contribution in the natural-language front end and cross-domain generality, not in the physics.
- **Open, credential-free archive** regenerating every result on commodity hardware.

## Methodology

**The loop.** A user supplies a goal in natural language. The LM proposes a fully specified design and calls the deterministic physics engine. The engine computes relevant quantities exactly and returns a verdict; on failure it returns the specific violated law plus a *targeted correction* — the minimal change `Xi ← x*i` to a root input node that would carry the outcome across threshold. The loop ends as certified, impossible, or unknown.

**The engine.** Pure deterministic code implementing peer-reviewed equations, calibrated against published values (DNA Tm within ±1°C of SantaLucia 1998; GW150914 chirp mass within ±1 M⊙ of the LIGO/Virgo catalog) as a *correctness* check, not a performance result. The authors deliberately do not score on famous quantities, since a model trained on the reporting papers would *recall* rather than *compute* them.

**The structural contract.** A directed dependency graph: bound nodes (goal-fixed, read-only to proposer), free nodes (design variables, writable), derived nodes (computed), and an outcome node (the certified quantity). On a fixed-system verification goal the engine reads bound quantities from the goal state and derives the certified quantity itself — there is no tool-call in which the model can resubmit alternative inputs. Soundness rests on two facts: dataflow direction (forward evaluation) + immutability of bound inputs. This is "interface design, not statistical inference," which is why it does not depend on decoding or sampler.

**Forgeability ablation.** The single toggled variable is *answer-submitting ("bare") validators vs. structural (derive-from-inputs) check*, on identical goals, across astrophysics close-up (3 goals × 5 trials) and full five-domain suites (25 impossible goals/model).

**Models & decoding.** Primary: Gemini 2.5 Flash via Vertex AI, temperature 0 (confirmed deterministic by byte-identical trajectories). Cross-model: Llama 3.3 70B via Vertex AI MaaS, temperature 0. Capability floor: Llama 3.1 8B served locally on-device via Ollama, no external API. High-temperature ablation repeats the adversarial suite at temperature 1. No model was fine-tuned.

**Goals & scoring.** Per domain: 10 feasible + 5 impossible goals (impossible = over-constrained input admitting no valid design). Author-defined boundaries; **no held-out external goal set** — explicitly a closed-world test. Feasible success = certification; impossible success = verdict "impossible"; false certification on an impossible goal counts as failure-to-refuse, never excluded.

## Results & Highlights

- **Feasible certification rises sharply with the loop.** Bare Gemini 2.5 Flash produces a physically valid design on only 26/50 feasible goals (52%, Wilson 95% CI 39–65%); PHACT certifies 44/50 (88%, CI 76–94%) — non-overlapping intervals. Correction is fast: certified runs reach a verified design in a median of 1–2 engine calls.

- **The bare model hallucinates the impossible.** On 25 impossible goals, the bare model produced a checker-passing design for 7 of them (28%) — confident, off-goal answers; 5 of 5 in astrophysics (the most formula-saturated domain), 1 in electrical, 1 in drone, 0 in DNA/drug.

- **Zero false certifications under the structural contract (the headline).** Pooled across all SCM-exclusive conditions: 0/80 (Gemini 5-domain 0/25 + Llama 5-domain 0/25 + astro high-temp 0/15 + astro latch-fault 0/15). The bare validator, by contrast, forges 19/45 pooled across the three close-up cells (S1 10/15 greedy, S2 6/15 temp-1, S4a 3/15 latch-fault). The authors report this *descriptively*, arguing the zero is an interface property, not a statistically estimated rate.

- **Cross-model transfer of safety.** Bare validator forged for both Gemini (2/25) and Llama 3.3 70B (1/25); structural contract forged for neither (0/25 each). Feasible certification: Gemini 88% vs Llama 66% — the gap is almost entirely *loop-fails* (Llama restates the correct value in prose but fails to resubmit a tool call), concentrated in multi-tool domains (electrical collapses to 5/10). The engine's authority is model-independent.

- **Capability-floor probe (Llama 3.1 8B).** Never closes the loop on any of 25 impossible goals — every trial ends in loop-closure failure: 0 refused, 0/25 forged, 25 loop-fail. Usefulness at the floor; safety intact for the trivial reason that the model never acts. Isolates the asymmetry: *producing a verdict is a capability the proposer may lack, but forging requires a verdict it never produces.*

- **Refusal-permission is not enough.** A bare model *prompted* to answer "impossible" reduces false certs from 7/25 → 2/25 but over-refuses 4 feasible goals; nothing it says is checked. The structural contract's value is that every certificate is *verified*, not merely that it can decline.

- **Feedback-form ablation.** Structural (root-input + dependency path) feedback helps only on the coupled DNA dual-constraint domain (2.50 → 1.70 iterations, ~30%), where no closed-form inverse exists; it is immaterial for closed-form-invertible RC (≈1.0) and large-feasible-region drug formulation (≈1.0–1.2). Structural feedback earns its complexity precisely when the corrective inverse requires joint optimization.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High).** The propose-certify loop itself is not new (tool-augmented/program-aided LMs, self-correction, neurosymbolic systems all exist — the paper surveys them and distinguishes itself clearly). The genuinely novel contribution is the *forgeability diagnosis + structural-contract fix*: the precise observation that a validator is defeated not by being wrong but by being asked the wrong question, and that the cure is wiring the dependency graph so the certified quantity is derived from bound inputs. This is a crisp, transferable interface-level result, articulately distinguished from prior art (PINNs internalize the law as a soft training penalty; PHACT keeps it outside as a hard runtime gate).

- **Rigor (Medium-High).** Strengths: same model in bare vs. PHACT (engine is the only variable); calibrated engines; byte-identical determinism check; honest reporting of loop-fails, timeouts, and wrong verdicts separately; Wilson CIs on empirical-rate rows. Weaknesses: the central "zero forgery" result is reported *descriptively, by design*, as an interface property rather than via significance testing — defensible given the claim is structural, but it means the empirical trials serve to check the implementation, not to estimate a rate. The N (80) is modest.

- **Reproducibility (High).** Engines are ordinary code (no ML, no proprietary model); archive regenerates all results on commodity hardware without credentials, network, or an LM. The certification path — the part reliability rests on — is fully open. This is the paper's strongest practical asset.

- **Significance (Medium-High, scoped).** Significant as a *template* for reliable LLM-aided design whenever a deterministic forward model exists. Scope is honestly bounded: it does not replace high-fidelity simulation (it screens cheaply against first-principles law), and all five engines are closed-form, so the demonstrated exactness does not yet cover domains needing a surrogate forward model (the authors note a PINN surrogate could slot in as the engine).

- **Red flags / honest caveats.**
  - **Closed-world evaluation, no held-out external goal set** — goals, engines, and the feasible/impossible boundary are *author-defined*. The authors state this plainly ("rates should be read in that light"), but it means the 88%/52% gap is a closed-world test of the architecture, not a benchmark against an independent standard.
  - **Only three proposers** evaluated; the authors flag that broader generality would be firmer with more capable models run end-to-end.
  - **Soundness is relative to the engine's model of the physics** — a design satisfying DLVO theory may still fail in a lab with hydration forces; a post-Newtonian chirp mass may degrade near merger. This is correctly scoped as a property of *any* computable certificate, not a flaw unique to PHACT, but it bounds practical utility.
  - The "zero false certifications" headline rests on impossibility goals authored by the same team that built the engines — an adversarial third party might author goals that probe edges the structural contract does not anticipate.
  - All five domains are low-dimensional with closed-form governing laws; whether the contract pattern survives high-dimensional design spaces or stochastic engines is untested.

## Strengths

- The forgery diagnosis is sharp and the structural fix is simple, principled, and transferable ("derive, don't accept").
- Clean experimental control: same model bare vs. with-engine isolates the loop as the sole variable.
- The capability-vs-safety separation is a genuinely useful conceptual result, demonstrated across a 10× model-size ladder.
- Honest, non-overclaiming writing — repeatedly states what the guarantee is not.
- Fully open, credential-free reproduction archive; certification path is ML-free.

## Limitations

- Closed-world evaluation with author-defined goals/engines/boundaries; no independent benchmark.
- Only three proposers; only closed-form engines demonstrated (surrogate-based engines are proposed but untested).
- Soundness is relative to the engine's physics model — does not certify against physical reality in full.
- PHACT sometimes pays for safety in feasible-certification rate via loop-fails (the model stalls without a clean refusal), especially for weaker proposers (Llama 3.3 70B electrical: 5/10) — silence is recoverable, but it is a real utility cost.
- Modest N (80 adversarial trials); zero-forgery reported descriptively as an interface property rather than statistically.
- High per-domain engineering cost: each domain needs a hand-built deterministic engine and a declared structural contract.

## Relevance

本文核心——"将断言权从不可靠LLM转移到确定性引擎、并以结构化契约防止认证伪造"——与可信赖LLM推理、神经符号系统及形式化验证方向高度相关，为降低LLM在科学与工程设计中的幻觉与误断提供了可操作、可复现的范式，特别契合"LLM+工具/验证器"与"可靠推理"的研究兴趣。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the structural-contract guarantee hold when the engine is a *learned* PINN surrogate (proposed but untested) rather than closed-form code — does the certifier remain non-forgeable, and is exactness preserved?
- Against an adversarial third party authoring impossible goals (not the engine's own builders), does the zero-forgery result survive, and where are the contract's blind edges?
- Would a model-agnostic *loop-closure supervisor* (re-prompting stalled turns) recover Llama 3.3 70B's lost feasible-certification rate without touching the engine's authority, as the authors hypothesize?
- Can the contract pattern extend to high-dimensional or stochastic design spaces (e.g., real molecular dynamics, CFD) where a single deterministic forward evaluation is infeasible?
- What is the engineering cost to instantiate a new domain (engine + contract + goal corpus), and can any of that wiring be automated or templated?
