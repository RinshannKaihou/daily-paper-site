> **一句话总结：** 上海 AI Lab 提出 Agents-A1，一个基于 Qwen3.5-35B-A3B 的 35B MoE 智能体模型；它不靠堆参数，而是构建记录证据/动作/观察/验证信号的"知识-动作图"基础设施来生成长程轨迹（平均 45K token），并通过"全领域 SFT → 领域教师训练 → 多教师领域路由式在策略蒸馏（带显著词表对齐）"三阶段配方，将六个异构领域统一到一个可部署模型，在多个长程智能体基准上达到或超过 Kimi-K2.6、DeepSeek-V4-pro 等 1T 参数级模型。

> **TL;DR:** Agents-A1 is a 35B MoE agentic model (built on Qwen3.5-35B-A3B) that reaches trillion-parameter-level long-horizon performance not by scaling parameters but by *scaling the agent horizon*. It uses a knowledge-action graph (KAG) infrastructure that logs evidence, actions, observations, and verifier outcomes to synthesize ~45K-token trajectories, plus a three-stage recipe (full-domain SFT → domain teacher training → multi-teacher domain-routed on-policy distillation with salient vocabulary alignment) that fuses six heterogeneous domains into one deployable student, matching or beating 1T models (Kimi-K2.6, DeepSeek-V4-pro, GPT-5.5) on SEAL-0, IFBench, HiPhO, FrontierScience-Olympiad, and MolBench-Bind.

## 核心贡献

- **提出并验证"扩展视野而非参数"的智能体扩展范式**：用可部署的 35B MoE 模型逼近 1T 参数模型在长程交互式智能体任务（科学/研究）上的能力。
- **构建长程知识-动作基础设施（KAG）**：将异构语料分解为五种原子能力（信息获取、工具调用、可执行迭代、证据验证、约束跟踪），以类型化四元组 G=(C,A,O,V) 记录上下文/动作/观察/验证器结果，并通过 proposer–solver–verifier 自博弈扩展子图，提供超出"最终答案"的过程级监督。
- **提出带显著词表对齐（SVA）的多教师领域路由式在策略蒸馏**：硬路由到对应领域教师，在教师选出的 top-k 紧支撑上做截断反 KL 对齐，并以"域内先平均、域间再平均"的目标平衡异构梯度，缓解长思考 vs 多轮工具两种推理模式冲突。
- **三阶段训练配方**：全领域 SFT 对齐基础智能体行为 → 各领域用 SFT/RL 训练专门教师 → OPD 将多个教师蒸馏进单一学生。
- **基准结果**：在 SEAL-0、IFBench、HiPhO、FrontierScience-Olympiad、MolBench-Bind 上取得领先，并在 SciCode、HLE、BrowseComp 上保持高度竞争力（与 1T 级模型相当）。

## Key Contributions

- **Horizon-over-parameter scaling**: demonstrates a 35B MoE student matching/beating 1T-class models (Kimi-K2.6, DeepSeek-V4-pro, GPT-5.5) on long-horizon agentic tasks.
- **Long-Horizon Knowledge-Action Infrastructure**: a typed knowledge-action graph G=(C,A,O,V) that retains process-level supervision (actions, observations, verifier outcomes), plus a proposer–solver–verifier self-play loop that expands domain sub-graphs into verifiable, shortcut-free, evidence-covering ~45K-token trajectories.
- **Domain-Routed On-Policy Distillation with Salient Vocabulary Alignment (SVA)**: hard routing to per-domain teachers; a truncated reverse-KL computed only on the teacher-selected top-k local vocabulary; a domain-normalized objective (average within active domains, then across) so long trajectories and frequent domains don't dominate.
- **Three-stage recipe**: full-domain SFT → domain teachers (SFT/RL per domain) → multi-teacher OPD unifying six heterogeneous domains into one deployable policy.
- **Strong benchmark coverage** spanning search, engineering, scientific reasoning, instruction following, general agentic, and scientific agentic tasks.

## Methodology

**Knowledge-Action Graph (KAG).** Long-horizon competence is decomposed into five atomic abilities — information acquisition, tool calling, executable iteration, evidence verification, constraint tracking. For each domain B_d the KAG is a typed 4-tuple G_d = (C_d, A_d, O_d, V_d): context (evidence/specs), actions, observations (retrieved text / logs / artifacts), and verifier outcomes. Unlike a standard knowledge graph, the KAG stores the *process* (state, action, observation, verifier) by which an answer is acquired, tested, revised, and verified — retaining both successful and failed trajectories for cross-step credit assignment. Domains instantiated include wiki-hyperlink search (entity transitions, page text, path-verifier), Kaggle-style MLE (code edits/runs/commits, logs/metrics, grader), etc.

**Self-play graph expansion.** A proposer–solver–verifier game expands the KAG: π_P samples graph regions to propose constrained tasks, π_S solves them with retrieval/tools, π_V verifies answer validity, evidence coverage, process-informativeness (rejecting one-shot lookups), and absence of shortcuts. Accepted trajectories are written back into G_d; rejected ones loop back to self-play. Enhanced graphs are specialized into sub-KAGs for coding, agentic reasoning, instruction following, MLE, scientific reasoning.

**Three-stage training.**
1. **Full-domain SFT** on multi-domain long-horizon data → broadly capable agent (Agents-A1-SFT). Observed to *drop* on some general-agentic / instruction-following / HLE tasks due to long-thinking vs multi-turn agentic pattern conflict.
2. **Domain-level teachers** — each trained with targeted SFT and/or RL: search (SFT+RL), science (SFT), long-instruction/IF (RL), tool-calling (SFT+RL), etc. Each teacher specializes but deployment needs one policy.
3. **Multi-teacher domain-routed OPD with SVA.** A frozen-rollout student samples y_i ~ π_θs(·|x_i); the optimized student is supervised by the routed teacher θ_t,i = θ_t^{d_i} (hard domain routing). **Salient Vocabulary Alignment** renormalizes student and teacher distributions on the teacher-selected top-k support S^(k)_{i,t} and computes a truncated reverse KL ℓ_SVA (Eq. 4) over trainable student-generated tokens only (tool/user turns masked). Student coverage ρ (Eq. 5) monitors mass inside the support. The **domain-normalized objective** L_MT-SVA (Eq. 6) averages SVA losses within each active domain then across active domains, so longer trajectories and frequent domains don't dominate. Rollouts are bounded by turn/length/context budgets; truncated rollouts are kept as valid prefixes.

## Results & Highlights

Primary metric: pass@1 (300-turn cap; H200 GPU, 12-h wall-clock per MLE task). Comparison set: 35B peers (Qwen3.5/3.6-35B-A3B, Nex-N2-mini) and 1T-class (Kimi-K2.6, DeepSeek-V4-Pro Max, GPT-5.5 xhigh).

**Stage-by-stage gains (Table 4), Qwen3.5-35B-A3B → Agents-A1-SFT → Agents-A1:**
- BrowseComp 61.0 → 74.6 → **75.5**; XBench-DS 77.0 → 88.0 → 86.0; SEAL-0 41.4 → 52.3 → **56.4**; GAIA 59.8 → 95.2 → **96.0**
- SciCode 37.1 → 42.3 → **44.3**; MLE-Bench-Lite 24.2 → 39.4 → **43.9**
- HLE-w/tools 47.4 → 41.6 → 47.6; HiPhO 37.0 → 42.9 → **46.4**; FS-O 64.5 → 75.0 → **79.0**; FS-R 2.5 → 31.7 → **40.0** (huge jump)
- IFBench 70.2 → 68.7 → **80.6**; LongBench V2 59.0 → 58.3 → 60.2
- τ2-Bench 32.5 → 76.7 → 79.8; VitaBench 26.0 → 37.3 → 38.8
- MatTools 21.0 → 37.0 → **47.1**; MolBench-Bind 46.0 → 46.0 → **56.8**

**Key observations:**
- Full-domain SFT *hurts* some IF/general-agentic/HLE tasks (pattern conflict) — substantially recovered by the OPD stage, validating the SVA + domain-normalized design.
- Each domain teacher (Tables 5–8) beats the baseline on its specialty, e.g. search teacher GAIA 59.8→95.1; science teacher FS-R 2.5→54.3; tool teacher τ2-Bench 32.5→82.5; IF-RL teacher IFBench 70.2→82.0.
- OPD student does **not** always beat the specialist teacher (expected — student must hold a unified policy); OPD's role is to transfer teacher strengths while balancing coverage.
- **Honest gaps vs 1T models**: still clearly weaker on SciCode/MLE (GPT-5.5 reaches 72.7 on MLE-Bench-Lite) — authors attribute this to MLE requiring long-horizon goal stability and memory across many experiments, which small models struggle with.
- Long-horizon applications: a 12-hour autonomous ML optimization run on the ICML 2013 Whale Challenge (Fig. 4), and an end-to-end Earth-science case study reconstructing Cyclone Nargis (2008) from IBTrACS data with code/visualization/diagnosis in a closed loop.

## Authors & Credibility

Large team effort (~49 listed contributors) from the **Shanghai AI Lab "Agents-A1 Team"**, including established researchers (e.g., Dahua Lin, Lei Bai, Bowen Zhou). The breadth of the benchmark suite, the multi-stage infrastructure, and 29-page report with per-stage ablation tables indicate a substantial, well-resourced engineering effort rather than a speculative preprint. Claims are mostly internally consistent; comparisons cite each baseline's own technical report, with re-evaluation under matched protocols when needed.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — Moderate/High.** The core framing ("scale horizon, not parameters") is timely and well-motivated; the genuinely novel pieces are (a) the typed KAG as process-level supervision that stores failure/verification, and (b) SVA + domain-normalized OPD for cross-domain teacher fusion. The individual ingredients (SFT, RL, on-policy distillation, MoE) are standard; the contribution is the composition and infrastructure.
- **Rigor — Moderate.** Stage-by-stage ablation (Tables 4–9) is good and honestly shows where SFT degrades and OPD recovers; teacher-specialist vs OPD-student comparison is transparent. However: (1) head-to-head 35B-vs-1T numbers mix sources (own eval vs baselines' reports vs re-evaluated under "our protocol"), which weakens strict comparability; (2) the flagship "match 1T" claim is benchmark-selective — on SciCode/MLE-Bench the model is clearly behind 1T models, and the authors say so.
- **Reproducibility — Low-to-Moderate.** Methods are described at usable granularity (KAG tuple, SVA Eqs. 4–6, budgets), but no open weights/data/training-config are confirmed in the read portion, and the KAG + self-play pipeline is infrastructure-heavy and unlikely to be reproduced externally. τ2-Bench discrepancies flagged by the authors themselves illustrate environment-sensitivity.
- **Significance — High.** A practical, deployable 35B agent matching frontier models on several hard long-horizon benchmarks is valuable for cost-sensitive deployment; the horizon-scaling recipe is a useful template.
- **Red flags / caveats:** (i) Some comparisons put a tool-equipped Agents-A1 against tool-free baselines (HiPhO/FS-O/FS-R "report tool-free results for comparison models" — explicitly justified but still asymmetric). (ii) Baseline model versions (Qwen3.5 vs Qwen3.6, GPT-5.5, DeepSeek-V4-pro, Kimi-K2.6) are future/fictional IDs relative to today — readers should sanity-check the actual models referenced. (iii) OPD occasionally underperforms its own teachers, so "unified" comes at a per-domain cost. (iv) Author/institution self-reporting without independent reproduction.

## Strengths

- Deployable 35B footprint reaching 1T-level long-horizon performance — strong practical value.
- Principled, well-motivated infrastructure: KAG captures process-level (incl. failure) supervision that final-answer-only SFT cannot.
- SVA + domain-normalized OPD is a clean, theoretically-grounded fix for sampled-token OPD instability and cross-domain gradient imbalance.
- Transparent ablations: reports where SFT regresses and how OPD recovers it; admits SciCode/MLE gaps.
- Real long-horizon case studies (12-h ML optimization, Earth-science reconstruction) demonstrate the horizon-scaling thesis concretely.

## Limitations

- No released weights/data/config in the read portion — reproducibility is weak; the pipeline is infrastructure-heavy.
- Asymmetric eval conditions (Agents-A1 with tools vs tool-free frontier baselines) on several science benchmarks.
- Still materially behind 1T models on long-horizon *engineering* tasks (SciCode, MLE-Bench) where goal-stability and cross-experiment memory matter.
- OPD student can lag its own specialist teachers — fusion has a cost.
- τ2-Bench numbers are environment/version-sensitive and flagged as inconsistent across community reports.
- Reliance on strong proprietary base/teacher models; provenance and compute budget not fully disclosed.

## Relevance

对于关注"大模型智能体能力扩展"与"低成本可部署蒸馏"方向的研究与工程实践，该论文提供了一条清晰的"扩展视野而非参数"路径，与你对长程推理、工具调用智能体及高效蒸馏的兴趣高度契合，可作为复现或工程落地的参考范式。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How much of the 35B-vs-1T gap-closing comes from the KAG/process supervision vs the SVA distillation itself? A clean ablation isolating each would clarify attribution.
- Are the "leading" science results robust when baselines are *also* given the same tool-augmented protocol, eliminating the asymmetric condition?
- What is the inference latency/cost profile of 45K-token agentic trajectories, and how does ROI actually compare to a 1T model at the same task budget?
- How sensitive is OPD convergence to domain-label noise and to the choice of top-k in SVA coverage?
- Can the KAG infrastructure generalize beyond the six domains (e.g., to software-engineering agents, real-time web action)?
- Given the (future-dated) baseline model IDs, how do results hold against the actual current frontier models once remeasured under a single harness?
