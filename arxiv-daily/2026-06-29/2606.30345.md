> **一句话总结：** DRIFT 提出一种在线自演化策略优化框架：在问题级用历史通过率做难度路由（Difficulty Routing）来动态分配自蒸馏与强化学习信号，在 token 级用节奏门控（Rhythm Gating）把探索集中在关键推理位置，并辅以成功缓冲区与两阶段课程学习；在五个科学推理/工具使用基准上平均达 79.5%，全面超越 GRPO 与 SDPO。

> **TL;DR:** DRIFT is an online self-evolution framework for LLMs that adaptively routes self-distillation vs. RL signals at the problem level (Difficulty Routing via smoothed historical pass rates), refines credit assignment at the token level (Rhythm-Gated exploration on critical reasoning anchors), and reuses high-quality trajectories via a success buffer under a two-stage curriculum — reaching a SOTA 79.5% average across five benchmarks and beating GRPO/SDPO and six baselines on every metric.

## 核心贡献

- **问题级难度路由（Difficulty Routing）：** 用平滑后的历史通过率（EMA）将题目分为 easy/medium/hard，并对正样本的 GRPO 更新赋予不同权重（γ_hard=0，γ_easy=0.5，r_easy=0.8，r_hard=0.2），把强化学习预算集中到最有学习价值的中等难度区域，避免对易题冗余更新、对难题收到错误校准的弱监督。
- **token 级节奏门控探索（Rhythm-Gated Structured Exploration）：** 通过"rebellious bonus"（学生相对自教师在 token 上的局部超越）和"anchor rhythm gate"（基于师生熵塌缩差异的结构显著性）双重筛选，只对"中等难度且落在结构关键锚点上的局部超越"做有界放大，解决 GRPO 序列级优势导致的信用分配过粗问题。
- **成功缓冲区（Success Buffer）+ 两阶段课程：** 第一阶段以 SDPO 为主做快速热身并积累成功轨迹；第二阶段对负样本走 SDPO 纠正分支、对正样本走 rhythm-GRPO 分支。成功缓冲区动态替换（b=3，内存仅约 0.2–0.3 GB），在正样本稀缺时提供特权教师 sib⁺。
- **全面 SOTA：** 在五个基准、三种模型规模上，DRIFT 在所有指标上达到 top-2，平均 79.5%（领先最强基线 SRPO 2.1 个点，领先 GRPO/SDPO 7.5–9.5 个点）；ToolUse 79.2%（领先次优 8.0 个点）。
- **跨模型族/规模的泛化：** 起点越弱收益越大（Olmo3-7B 从 30.5% 提升到 72.8%），领先优势随规模扩大而增大，印证"自教能力随模型规模涌现"。

## Key Contributions

- **Problem-level Difficulty Routing:** Smoothed historical pass rates (EMA) bin problems into easy/medium/hard and reweight positive-sample GRPO updates (γ_hard=0, γ_easy=0.5, r_easy=0.8, r_hard=0.2), concentrating RL budget on the medium-difficulty region with the highest learning value and avoiding redundant updates on easy problems / miscalibrated supervision on hard ones.
- **Token-level Rhythm-Gated Exploration:** A two-stage "double screening" credit mechanism — a teacher-relative *rebellious bonus* (local log-prob exceedance) gated by an *anchor rhythm gate* (structural salience from teacher-vs-student entropy-collapse differences). Only bounded amplification is applied where the sample is medium-difficulty AND the exceedance lands on a critical reasoning anchor, fixing GRPO's coarse sequence-level credit assignment.
- **Success Buffer + Two-Stage Curriculum:** Stage 1 is an SDPO-dominated warm-up (reward-first, entropy-second teacher selection for diversity) that bootstraps capability and accumulates successes; Stage 2 routes negative samples to an SDPO correction branch (using a privileged success sibling sib⁺) and positive samples to a rhythm-GRPO branch. The success buffer is dynamically replaced by reward (b=3, ~0.2–0.3 GB), supplying privileged supervision when in-batch positives are scarce.
- **New State-of-the-Art:** Top-2 on *every* task across five benchmarks and three model scales; 79.5% average (Qwen3-8B), beating SRPO by 2.1 pts and GRPO/SDPO by 7.5–9.5 pts; 79.2% on ToolUse (+8.0 over SRPO).
- **Cross-Family/Scale Generalization:** Gains amplify as the base weakens (Olmo3-7B: 30.5%→72.8%, +42.3 pts) and the lead over the strongest baseline widens with scale, consistent with self-teaching ability emerging as models grow.

## Methodology

DRIFT is an **online self-evolution policy optimization framework** built on the observation that both GRPO (sequence-level relative advantage → coarse credit, advantage collapse when rewards are homogeneous) and SDPO (indiscriminate reuse of all successful trajectories → redundant updates on mastered problems, under-explored boundary cases) lack a mechanism that tracks problem-level learning progress and allocates signals accordingly. It has three interlocking components:

**1. Two-stage curriculum + privileged self-teacher.**
- *Stage 1 (warm-up, SDPO-dominated):* A "reward-first, entropy-second" teacher-selection rule picks the highest-reward success sibling sib⁺, breaking ties toward higher entropy to maintain exploration/diversity and avoid premature convergence. Goal: fast performance warm-up + build the success buffer.
- *Stage 2 (mixed optimization):* Samples routed by correctness. **Negative samples → SDPO branch** (teacher = highest-reward, then shortest sib⁺ to aid convergence; provides a token-by-token correction path that pure GRPO cannot, since GRPO only suppresses all tokens of a wrong trajectory). **Positive samples → rhythm-GRPO branch** (advantage computed over *all* samples jointly; avoids the distribution-mismatch instability of using "another correct trajectory" to guide an already-correct one). The overall objective combines an SDPO correction loss for incorrect samples and a difficulty-routed rhythm-GRPO term for correct ones.

**2. Difficulty Routing (problem-level, γᵢ).** Pass rate is the difficulty signal. The raw per-batch success rate r_now is noisy (n=8/16 rollouts), so it is smoothed with an EMA over history: r = α·r_past + (1−α)·r_now. Smoothed r bins problems into easy/medium/hard with γ_easy=0.5, γ_medium=1, γ_hard=0 (thresholds r_easy=0.8, r_hard=0.2). This suppresses redundant surface-level reinforcement on easy problems (vanishing advantage) and miscalibrated signals on hard problems (scarce/accidental positives, e.g., guessing the right multiple-choice answer with a wrong chain-of-thought), focusing RL on the medium-difficulty boundary.

**3. Rhythm-Gated Structured Exploration (token-level, M^rhy).** Standard GRPO assigns the same sequence-level advantage to all tokens, so formatting/connective tokens pollute the gradient. DRIFT gates the GRPO branch with a composite weight M^rhy_{i,t} = 1 + βᵢ·g^rhythm_{i,t}·b^reb_{i,t}:
- *Rebellious bonus b^reb:* bounded reward for the student's local log-prob exceedance over the teacher (Δ_t = ℓ^S_t − ℓ^T_t − µ, rectified and bounded by B_max with temperature τ).
- *Anchor rhythm gate g^rhythm:* structural salience from the teacher showing a more pronounced local *entropy collapse* than the student while the student is still more uncertain at that position — i.e., a reasoning anchor the student hasn't stably mastered. Z-scored and soft-gated to [0,1], with only above-average-salience positions surviving.
- βᵢ is the medium-difficulty indicator (1 only for medium-routed samples). The result is a double screening: extra amplification only when (a) the sample is medium-difficulty AND (b) the exceedance falls on a high-value structural anchor. Exploration is teacher-relative, local, and bounded.

**4. Success Buffer.** Per-problem buffer B(x) of up to b=3 successful (trajectory, reward) pairs. Unused (only accumulated) for the first k epochs, then drawn as sib⁺ only when a group has *no* in-batch success; replacement is reward-based (high-reward evicts low-reward). Because the teacher keeps improving, this injects an implicit reward signal that strengthens over training rather than static replay. Memory overhead is small (~0.2–0.3 GB).

## Results & Highlights

- **Setup:** Qwen3-8B (primary), Qwen3-4B, Olmo3-7B; trained 400+ steps on 8× H200; mean@16 accuracy; 5 benchmarks (Biology, Chemistry, Materials, Physics from SciKnowEval; ToolUse from ToolAlpaca), in-domain generalization split; baselines GRPO, SDPO, SRPO, DistIL, SC-SDPO, PGPO. Results reported in **training steps** (not wall-clock) for reproducibility.
- **Main (Qwen3-8B), Table 1:** DRIFT 79.5% avg vs SRPO 77.4% (+2.1), SC-SDPO 74.8% (+4.7), GRPO/SDPO 63.0–72.0% (+7.5–9.5), base 49.5% (+30.0). Best on Biology (74.4%) and ToolUse (79.2%); second-best on the rest (≤1.1 pt gap to per-task optimum). Only method top-2 on *every* task.
- **Cross-scale (Table 2):** Qwen3-4B 74.6% avg, Olmo3-7B 72.8% avg (from 30.5% base, +42.3). ToolUse lead is the largest at all scales (+7.0/+7.3/+8.0).
- **Ablations (Table 3, Qwen3-8B):** w/o difficulty routing → 74.1% (drops on Biology/ToolUse); w/o success buffer → 74.9%; w/o warm-up → 71.7% (severe Biology drop). Full DRIFT best on all five — gains come from the *joint* effect, not any single component.
- **Training dynamics:** Actor entropy rises then stabilizes after ~200 steps; response length bounded; success buffer saturates with intermittent fallback hits; easy-fraction grows, hard-fraction declines — DRIFT shifts from broad exploration to stable refinement while adapting routing to evolving capability.
- **Why ToolUse gains are largest:** tool correctness is sharply defined by schemas/argument formats, so successful demonstrations are highly standardized and reusable → cleaner distillation/correction signals; STEM gains are more moderate because knowledge is problem-specific and less reusable.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The principal novelty is the *joint problem-level + token-level adaptive routing* of two complementary self-improvement signals (SDPO correction vs. GRPO exploration) under a curriculum, plus the *double-screening* rhythm gate (rebellious bonus × anchor salience). Individual ideas (difficulty weighting, entropy-based salience, experience replay) have priors, but the specific composition — especially routing *negatives to SDPO and positives to rhythm-GRPO* with a structurally gated bonus gated by *medium-difficulty* β — is a genuinely fresh, principled synthesis rather than an incremental tweak.
- **Rigor (Moderate–High):** Multi-scale, multi-benchmark evaluation with six baselines; explicit ablations isolating each major component; training-dynamics analysis. Results are reported in steps (hardware-agnostic), which is a reproducibility plus. *Caveats:* only the GRPO/SDPO baselines are reproduced in-house; SRPO/DistIL/SC-SDPO/PGPO numbers are cited from their papers, so cross-method comparison rests on protocol consistency rather than a single controlled harness. No error bars / significance testing reported, and mean@16 (rather than greedy/pass@1) inflates absolute numbers.
- **Reproducibility (Moderate):** Equations and key hyperparameters (γ, thresholds, b, EMA α implicit, k epochs for buffer warm-up) are given, and the success buffer is described as lightweight. *No mention of code/data release* in the read sections — a real limitation for a method with this many moving parts and tuned thresholds.
- **Significance (High):** Stable self-improvement without external expert supervision is a central open problem. The framing — "effective self-evolution depends less on the RL-vs-distillation choice than on *when and where* each signal is applied" — is a broadly applicable thesis, and the large gains on the hard ToolUse/Biology tasks (where GRPO collapses) are compelling evidence.
- **Red flags:**
  - **Tuned thresholds:** easy/hard cut-offs (0.2/0.8), γ values, and rhythm-gate parameters (τ, B_max, λ_struct, W, α_anc) are numerous and plausibly dataset-tuned; no sensitivity sweep shown.
  - **Self-graded difficulty on multiple-choice science benchmarks** carries known miscalibration risk (correct guess + wrong CoT), which the paper itself acknowledges but mitigates only heuristically.
  - **In-domain generalization only:** benchmarks are split train/test within the same tasks; there is no held-out/OOD evaluation, so "self-improvement" claims are bounded to in-distribution settings.
  - **"Concurrent methods" outperformed:** comparisons to very recent 2026 methods depend entirely on cited numbers; small sample/protocol differences could move 1–2 pt gaps.
  - Author list and multiple co-first authors is normal for this scale but worth noting for attribution.

## Strengths

- Principled, multi-granularity design that directly diagnoses and fixes named failure modes of GRPO (coarse credit, advantage collapse) and SDPO (indiscriminate imitation, under-exploration of boundary cases).
- The routing rule (negatives→SDPO for *directional correction*, positives→GRPO to *preserve capability*) is well-motivated and addresses a real instability.
- Strong, consistent empirical gains over six baselines across three model scales, with the largest margins on the hardest tasks where baselines collapse.
- Honest, equation-complete methodology; memory-efficient buffer; step-based (hardware-agnostic) reporting.

## Limitations

- **Many tuned hyperparameters** (difficulty thresholds, γ values, rhythm-gate coefficients) with no sensitivity analysis — robustness is unverified.
- **In-domain only:** no out-of-distribution / held-out-domain generalization test; "self-improvement" is bounded to the trained task distribution.
- **Baseline asymmetry:** four of six baselines are cited rather than re-run in a single harness; mean@16 inflates absolute accuracy.
- **No reported code/data release**, and no error bars or significance tests.
- **Self-judged difficulty** on multiple-choice science data risks miscalibration (guessing), only heuristically addressed.
- The compute/throughput overhead of computing teacher entropies, rebellious bonuses, and anchor gates per token at every step is not quantified beyond the buffer memory figure.

## Relevance

本文聚焦大模型在无外部专家监督下的稳定自我提升，将自蒸馏与强化学习在问题级（难度路由）和 token 级（节奏门控）联合调度，对自演化 LLM、RLVR 与推理能力增强方向高度相关，可为后续自训练/课程学习/细粒度信用分配研究提供可直接借鉴的机制设计。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How sensitive are the results to the difficulty thresholds (r_easy=0.8, r_hard=0.2) and γ values — does a single fixed setting hold across very different task domains?
- What are the ablation contributions of the *rhythm gate* vs. the *rebellious bonus* individually, and of the anchor-salience definition (entropy-collapse window W)?
- Do the gains transfer to out-of-distribution / held-out-domain reasoning tasks, or is the improvement strictly in-domain?
- What is the actual compute/latency overhead of per-token teacher entropy + anchor-gate computation relative to vanilla GRPO/SDPO?
- Will code, data splits, and tuned hyperparameters be released to support reproduction and a single-harness re-comparison with SRPO/DistIL/SC-SDPO/PGPO?
- Does the "self-teaching ability emerges with scale" trend continue, or plateau, beyond 8B?
