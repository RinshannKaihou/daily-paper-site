> **一句话总结：** BayesEvolve 把自主科学发现智能体从"依赖实验记忆/高分档案库"重构为"维护显式、不确定性感知的预测信念状态（用高斯过程后验实现），并以退火不确定性奖励引导选择"，在 5 个偏移 BBOB 式黑盒优化任务（d=5, T=100）上以归一化目标 0.195 优于 Archive-LLM(0.473) 与 Memory-LLM(0.425)，并略胜纯 GP-BO(0.229)，但消融显示增益主要来自信念均值而非不确定性奖励。

> **TL;DR:** BayesEvolve reframes LLM-guided autonomous discovery as *belief-state evolution*: instead of conditioning proposals on an archive of past scores, it maintains an explicit Gaussian-process posterior P(y|h,D) over candidate quality and selects the next experiment via an annealed UCB-style rule (-μ + β_t·σ, with β_t decaying as √(n₀/t)). On 5 shifted BBOB-style minimization functions (d=5, 100-eval budget, 5 seeds), it reaches normalized best-so-far 0.195 at 100 evals vs. Archive-LLM 0.473, Memory-LLM 0.425, and non-LLM GP-BO 0.229; the belief mean is well rank-correlated with true objectives (Spearman ρ=0.774 on 60,800 held-out predictions). The concept is clean, but the headline "annealed uncertainty bonus" adds only a marginal final-score edge (0.177 → 0.174 over mean-only) and is actually worse on AUC/evals-to-target — the real driver is the explicit belief *mean*, not the acquisition rule, and the win over GP-BO sits within overlapping error bars.

## 核心贡献

- **范式重构**：首次明确提出"将 LLM 引导的发现建模为显式信念状态维护 (belief-state maintenance)"，而非 FunSearch/AlphaEvolve 式的"档案记忆"——把过去实验从非结构化记忆升级为对未评估假设质量的预测性后验信念。
- **带退火不确定性奖励的选择规则**：针对最小化任务设计 a_t(h) = -μ_t(h) + β_t·σ_t(h)，β_t = β₀·√(n₀/t)，从早期广探索逐步过渡到后期重利用。
- **防泄漏测试床**：5 个偏移 BBOB 式函数（Sphere/Ellipsoid/Rastrigin/Rosenbrock/Ackley, d=5），用不透明任务 ID（如 F01）屏蔽函数名，杜绝基准名泄漏。
- **样本效率胜出**：100 次评估预算下，归一化 best-so-far 达 0.195，优于 Archive-LLM、Memory-LLM，且略优于纯 GP-BO。
- **多维度证据**：信念状态在 60,800 条留出预测上与真实目标强相关（Spearman ρ=0.774）；决策规则消融与多样性动态分析共同支撑"有产出的后期聚焦"结论。

## Key Contributions

- **Reframing LLM discovery as belief-state maintenance.** Argues that archive-of-scores (FunSearch/AlphaEvolve style) leaves evidence implicit; BayesEvolve maintains an explicit predictive posterior P(y|h,D)=N(μ,σ²) that encodes *what the agent believes about unevaluated hypotheses* and *how uncertain* those beliefs are.
- **An annealed uncertainty-aware selection rule.** For minimization, a(h)=−μ(h)+β_t·σ(h) with β_t=β₀·√(n₀/t), shifting from exploration to exploitation as evidence accumulates.
- **Leakage-resistant testbed.** Five shifted BBOB-style functions at d=5 with opaque task IDs (e.g., F01) to prevent the LLM recognizing benchmark names.
- **Empirical sample-efficiency win.** 0.195 normalized best-so-far at 100 evals vs. Archive-LLM 0.473, Memory-LLM 0.425, and GP-BO 0.229.
- **Diagnostic depth.** 60,800 held-out belief predictions show Spearman ρ=0.774 between posterior mean and true objective; decision-rule ablations and diversity-dynamics analyses support the "productive late-stage concentration" claim rather than unfocused exploration.

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** Let H be a hypothesis space; at step t the agent evaluates h_t∈H and observes y_t∈ℝ (minimization). The evaluation history is D_t={(h_i,y_i)}, and the goal is the best candidate under a fixed budget T. Archive-guided methods condition proposals on a subset of D_t (e.g., top scorers); BayesEvolve instead maintains an explicit belief state P(y|h,D_t)=N(μ_t(h), σ²_t(h)).

**Belief-guided selection.** For a candidate pool C_t (built from LLM proposals and/or archive mutations), the next candidate maximizes a_t(h)=−μ_t(h)+β_t·σ_t(h), where β_t=β₀·√(n₀/t) decays over the run (n₀ = shared initialization count). The framework is surrogate-agnostic; experiments use a **Gaussian-process posterior**.

**Algorithm 1.** (1) Evaluate n₀ initial candidates → D_t. (2) Loop t=n₀+1…T: fit/update belief; construct candidate pool C_t; compute μ,σ for all h∈C_t; pick h_t=argmax[−μ+β_tσ]; evaluate y_t and append.

**Benchmark & protocol.** Five shifted BBOB functions (Sphere, Ellipsoid, Rastrigin, Rosenbrock, Ackley) at d=5, each with a fixed hidden offset and opaque task IDs to block name leakage. n₀=6 shared random inits, budget T=100, averaged over 5 seeds × 5 functions; shaded bands/± = standard error.

**Methods compared.** All LLM variants share the proposal model **gpt-5.4-mini** and differ only in context: Random-LLM (no memory), Archive-LLM (top archive entries), Memory-LLM (recent heuristic memory), BayesEvolve (explicit belief state). GP-BO is a non-LLM Bayesian-optimization baseline. Decision-rule ablations fix the benchmark/init/GP/pool and swap the rule: mean-only, fixed UCB, UCB-decay (=BayesEvolve), Thompson sampling, EI.

## Results & Highlights

- **Main discovery performance (Table 1, normalized best-so-far, lower better):**
  | Method | 25 evals | 50 | 100 |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Random-LLM | 0.629 | 0.629 | 0.629 |
  | Archive-LLM | 0.476 | 0.473 | 0.473 |
  | Memory-LLM | 0.545 | 0.488 | 0.425 |
  | GP-BO | 0.384 | 0.278 | 0.229 |
  | **BayesEvolve** | **0.360** | **0.243** | **0.195** |
- **Belief-state quality:** 60,800 held-out predictions show Spearman ρ=0.774 between posterior mean and realized objective; higher posterior σ correlates with larger prediction error. *Calibration is imperfect* — empirical coverage 0.433 at 1σ (ideal ≈0.683) and 0.653 at 2σ (ideal ≈0.954), i.e., the GP is over-confident.
- **Decision-rule ablation (Table 2, final normalized best↓ / AUC↑ / evals-to-target↓):** Archive 0.333/0.508/38.6; **Mean-only 0.177/0.746/16.3**; Fixed UCB 0.176/0.731/21.2; **UCB-decay (=BayesEvolve) 0.174/0.730/19.9**; Thompson 0.210/0.664/24.6; EI 0.205/0.672/16.5; GP-BO 0.213/0.669/22.9. Mean-only already crushes archive search; the annealed bonus gives only a hair better final score (0.174 vs 0.177) while being *worse* on AUC and evals-to-target.
- **Diversity dynamics:** Random-LLM and GP-BO keep high late-stage diversity but worse objectives; BayesEvolve concentrates late yet reaches the lowest objective — "productive concentration," not unfocused exploration.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate. The conceptual reframing ("belief state as a first-class object vs. archive memory") is well articulated and a useful organizing idea for LLM-discovery agents. But the machinery — GP posterior + UCB with a decaying coefficient, which is a 1/t-annealed β echoing well-known BO/RL exploration schedules — is standard Bayesian optimization. The genuine novelty is *placing it inside an LLM proposal loop*, not the technique itself.
- **Rigor:** Mixed. The ablation design is sound and the leakage controls (opaque IDs, fixed offset, shared inits) are commendable. But the headline gain over the most natural competitor (GP-BO) is **statistically fragile**: 0.195±0.043 vs 0.229±0.047 — the ±1 SE bands overlap substantially, so "BayesEvolve beats GP-BO" is not clearly significant at this sample size. Also a minor internal inconsistency: GP-BO's final normalized best is 0.229 in Table 1 but 0.213 in Table 2.
- **Reproducibility:** Weak-to-moderate. No mention of code/config/seeds release; protocol details are given but the candidate-pool construction ("LLM proposals and/or archive mutations") is underspecified. Commercial LLM dependency (gpt-5.4-mini) adds reproducibility risk.
- **Significance:** Promising but currently narrow. If the sample-efficiency edge holds on harder/program-discovery tasks it could matter; on this BBOB-only evidence it is incremental.
- **Red flags / overclaiming:**
  1. **The headline "annealed uncertainty bonus" is barely load-bearing.** Table 2 shows mean-only selection (0.177) is within noise of UCB-decay/BayesEvolve (0.174) and is *better* on AUC (0.746 vs 0.730) and evals-to-target (16.3 vs 19.9). The abstract's emphasis on the bonus overstates its contribution; the honest takeaway is "the explicit belief *mean* is what helps, the acquisition rule is a marginal refinement." The discussion section admits this ("most gains come from the posterior mean") but the framing/abstract do not.
  2. **Calibration is poor** (1σ coverage 0.433 vs ~0.683), undercutting the "uncertainty-aware" selling point.
  3. **Scale is small** — 5 functions, d=5, T=100, 5 seeds; "autonomous scientific discovery" branding is a stretch for a synthetic 5-D numerical benchmark.

## Strengths

- Clean, clearly articulated thesis that distinguishes belief states from memory/archives.
- Honest scoping — BBOB only, real program/laboratory discovery deferred.
- Solid diagnostic methodology: held-out predictiveness, decision-rule ablations, diversity dynamics.
- Thoughtful anti-leakage design (opaque task IDs, hidden offsets, shared random inits).
- The "productive late-stage concentration" finding is a genuinely useful characterization of BO-style behavior in an LLM loop.

## Limitations

- **Testbed is narrow:** only 5 shifted BBOB functions at d=5 with a 100-eval budget — no program synthesis, no laboratory experiments.
- **Statistical fragility:** the win over GP-BO sits within overlapping error bars; larger seed/function counts are needed.
- **Acquisition rule contributes little:** the annealed bonus is marginal-to-negative vs mean-only (per AUC/evals-to-target).
- **Poor calibration** of the GP uncertainty undermines the uncertainty-aware narrative.
- **Scaling:** GP posterior limits motivate future sparse/neural surrogates; not yet demonstrated at larger budgets/dimensions.
- **Reproducibility:** no stated code release; underspecified candidate-pool mechanism.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does BayesEvolve beat GP-BO *significantly* with more seeds/functions, or is the current edge within noise?
- Given that mean-only is within noise of the full rule (and better on AUC/evals-to-target), is the annealed uncertainty bonus justified at all — or should the contribution be re-framed around the explicit belief *mean*?
- How is the candidate pool C_t actually constructed from LLM proposals vs. archive mutations, and how sensitive are results to that mix?
- Can the GP be replaced by a *calibrated* or neural surrogate to fix the 1σ/2σ coverage gap?
- Does the belief state transfer across related tasks (meta-learning / warm-starting), and does it scale to higher dimensions and longer budgets?
- Will the framework extend to program-discovery (AlphaEvolve-style) and wet-lab hypothesis spaces, where "objective quality" is far less cleanly defined than a BBOB function?
