Single-snapshot LLM-as-judge studies have a shelf life measured in weeks — any closed-loop agent using proprietary evaluators inherits this hidden instability.

relevance → evaluation methodology, preference learning, adaptive agents
Critical edge caveat

The headline rests on one May→June window (N=8) routed through a proxy, not the official API — and the May model no longer exists, so the drift is inherently irreproducible.

Methodology

TTRL bandit × EPC diagnostic suite — distilled, not transcribed

A parameter-free stochastic bandit adapts a distribution over 11 reasoning strategies (8 text + 3 visual). A fixed DeepSeek executor answers under the sampled strategy and a fixed step_by_step baseline; an evaluator J returns a pairwise verdict that reweights the policy asymmetrically (win 0.08 / lose 0.04). Three metrics compose the EPC suite:

MPCI measures preference concentration across modalities plus cross-modal coupling; the coupling matrix Γ(J) holds off-diagonal γ — how strongly one modality's learned weights leak into another; and bounded Jensen–Shannon divergence (10k-resample bootstrap CIs) acts as the primary, normalized metric. Four isolation phases separate pure-text, pure-visual, and two directed transfer directions; ECE/Brier test whether concentrated preferences actually track accuracy or are arbitrary.

Eight conditions span two evaluator families (OpenAI GPT-4o, Alibaba Qwen) plus DeepSeek self-evaluation — 122 runs (112 main + 10 ablation), all reported, no cherry-picking.

+ Strengths

  • Targets a timely, under-appreciated threat to LLM-evaluation validity.
  • Exceptionally transparent — reports all 122 runs incl. collapsed/embarrassing ones, with data filenames.
  • Ships a reusable, cheap diagnostic toolkit (EPC), not just a critique.
  • Version-locked re-run re-attributes the symmetric-LR result — genuine self-correction.
  • Open data/code; actionable guidance (version dates, ≥2 families, monthly re-baselining).

! Limitations

  • Small per-cell N (8–10) for key conditions; CV≈0.9 signals unstable measurement.
  • Proprietary internals opaque — drift root-cause is impossible, only plausible-inference.
  • γ is unbounded (>1.0 up to 1.741); MPCI uses ad-hoc equal weighting.
  • Cross-evaluator comparison confounds identity, modality, and evaluation relation (Table 5).
  • Self-eval collapse may be a floor effect (ECE=0.31), not genuine stability.
  • Drift measured via api2d proxy; single-author preprint, no peer review.

Open questions

for further exploration

  1. Does EPC generalize to fully open-weight evaluators (e.g. Llama-3.1-70B via vLLM), whose versions are permanently reproducible — does version-conditional instability vanish?
  2. Can the May→June drift be corroborated via the official OpenAI API (not a proxy) at larger N to solidify attribution?
  3. Is γ > 1.0 an L2-normalization artifact — should MPCI/γ be reformulated with a bounded divergence (Hellinger, EMD)?
  4. Does self-evaluation collapse persist when task difficulty rises above floor and the evaluator has genuine discriminative capacity?
  5. Is N-sensitivity a general property of complex evaluation pipelines, as the cross-domain ECE anecdote hints — can it be formalized?
  6. What is the half-life of evaluator validity across vendors, and can EPC run as a continuous-monitoring dashboard in production?
