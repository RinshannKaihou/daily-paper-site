> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"可观测矩阵动力学"（OMD）框架：用固定 N=1000 的输入集构造网络内部表示的 arccos 距离矩阵 M(t)，结合扩展 BBS 随机矩阵理论的谱诊断与 Itô-SDE，刻画训练中"扩散型松弛"与"几何相变"两种模式——在 7 个实验中，矩阵值诊断能比标量损失更早、更细地定位结构重排（如 grokking 中提前 ~3000 步检测到重组）。

> **TL;DR:** Observable Matrix Dynamics (OMD) tracks a fixed N×N arccos distance matrix M(t) built from a network's evolving internal representations on N=1000 held-out inputs, reading it via an extended Bogomolny–Bohigas–Schmit (BBS) spectral toolkit plus an Itô SDE. Across seven experiments it cleanly separates diffusive relaxation (no stable top-of-spectrum band) from sharp geometric phase transitions (stable integer fingerprints) — and detects structural reorganisations, e.g. grokking, thousands of steps earlier than scalar test loss.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **OMD（可观测矩阵动力学）**：在固定 N=1000 的评估输入集上，将网络某层的内部表示 L2 归一化到单位球，构造 arccos 距离矩阵 M_ij(t)=arccos(ĥ_i·ĥ_j)，把高维 θ(t)（百万到十亿参数，实际不可观测）替换为可经前向传播算出的"可观测"矩阵。
- 构建并应用 **I-BBS（推理版 BBS）环境-潜空间分解**：M(D)=M(d)+εM(noise)+O(ε²)，给出每快照四件诊断（multiplet 重数 ĥ₁、去收缩校验、离域斜率 d_β、噪声模型 RSM/FSM）。
- 推导 M_ij(t) 的 **Itô SDE**：把训练解释为损失梯度漂移+Itô 修正驱动的 Langevin 松弛，将表示侧观测量与不可观测参数 θ(t) 建立理论联系。
- 提供三类互补观测量：逐快照 I-BBS 谱诊断 + 轨迹级 FDM 诊断（投影漂移 D_K、交换子范数 C、能级间距）；底三特征向量的 3D MDS 嵌入（粒子云可视化）；联合 train–test 对象给出 Δ_β(t)=β_train−β_test 作为更细的泛化指示器。
- 在 7 个实验中给出统一规律，并把几何体制分为 **五类**：光滑流形、乘积流形、Fourier 孤子、原子簇、扩散/无带。论证 OMD 读出的是"几何体制"而非单一内在维度。
- 用矩阵值诊断证明其严格优于标量损失：grokking 中 β(t) 在记忆阶段晚期就开始相变，比测试损失骤降早约 3000 步；Δ_β 在 grokking 步处出现锐利尖峰，能精确定位泛化时刻。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **Observable Matrix Dynamics (OMD)**, replacing the unobservable high-dimensional parameter vector θ(t) with a fixed N×N arccos distance matrix M(t) on N=1000 held-out inputs computed from a forward pass.
- Applies a **perturbative I-BBS (Inference-BBS) ambient-vs-latent decomposition** extending the Bogomolny–Bohigas–Schmit theory of random distance matrices, yielding a four-diagnostic per-snapshot toolkit (multiplet multiplicity ĥ₁, shrinkage-inversion cross-check, delocalised slope d_β, RSM/FSM noise model).
- Derives an **Itô SDE for M_ij(t)** via Itô's lemma along the SGD/Adam Langevin trajectory, reframing training as Langevin relaxation with a loss-gradient drift plus Itô corrections.
- Defines three complementary observable classes: per-snapshot I-BBS + trajectory-level FDM diagnostics; a bottom-three-eigenvector 3D MDS embedding rendering training as a moving particle cloud; and a joint train–test object with the synchronisation gap Δ_β(t).
- Across seven experiments establishes a consistent empirical pattern and classifies representation geometry into **five regimes** (smooth manifold, product manifold, Fourier-soliton, atomic-cluster, diffusive).
- Demonstrates the matrix observable is strictly finer than scalar loss: in grokking, β(t) enters its transition ~3000 steps before the test loss drops, and Δ_β spikes precisely at the generalisation moment.

## Methodology

**The observable.** For a model f_θ with representation h(x;θ)∈ℝ^D read at a chosen layer, the unit-normalised ĥ_i(t)=h(x_i;t)/‖h(x_i;t)‖₂∈S^{D−1} on a fixed evaluation sample of N=1000 inputs defines the symmetric distance matrix M_S(t)_ij = arccos(ĥ_i(t)·ĥ_j(t))∈[0,π], the geodesic distance on the ambient unit sphere. The training trajectory maps onto a trajectory of M whose N(N−1)/2 coupled components evolve under the unobserved action of θ(t).

**Spectral reading (I-BBS).** The points ĥ_i(t) lie close to a latent d-dimensional sub-manifold M_d⊂S^{D−1}; the ambient matrix decomposes perturbatively as M(D)=M(d)+εM(noise)+O(ε²). Two generative noise models are defined: the Residual Sphere Mixture (RSM, isotropic, leaves the ℓ=2 angular-momentum channel empty) and the Free Spectral Mixture (FSM, model-free, populates ℓ=2). The I-BBS algorithm reads: (i) the gap-protected integer multiplet multiplicity ĥ₁=h(1,d)=d (primary dimension handle), (ii) parameter-free multiplet-shrinkage inversion, (iii) the corrected delocalised rank-decay slope β_del=d/(d−1) yielding the BBS dimension d_β(t)=β/(β−1), and (iv) the ambient noise model from the ℓ=2 component plus a residual-Wigner-semicircle consistency check.

**Itô SDE.** Applying Itô's lemma to M_ij(θ(t)) along the Langevin parameter SDE dθ = −η∇L dt + σ dW gives dM_ij = μ_ij dt + σ_ij^⊤ dW, with drift μ_ij containing the loss-gradient chain-rule term plus an O(Σ_θ) Itô correction, and diffusion built from the representation Jacobian. This is the formal bridge between the observable M and the unobserved θ(t).

**Trajectory-level (FDM) and joint observables.** On top of per-snapshot reads: bottom-K projector drift D_K(t,t′), matrix commutator C(t,t′)=‖[M(t),M(t′)]‖_F, and level-spacing universality tests. The joint train–test object combines a train-sample and test-sample matrix, yielding Δ_β(t)=β_train−β_test and an off-diagonal block-asymmetry that flags when test points sit further from the train manifold than train points sit from each other.

**Pipeline.** A boxed "OMD diagnostic pipeline" Algorithm: form M(t) → diagonalise → run I-BBS per snapshot → FDM trajectory reads → (opt) joint train–test → (opt) 3D MDS embedding → (opt) add a matrix term λ·L_mat(M(t),M★) to the loss to actively steer geometry.

## Results & Highlights

Seven experiments split into two groups (N=1000 eval sample, typically 10 seeds, β fit over K∈[2,50], gap threshold τ=0.25):

**Group A — diffusive relaxation (no sharp transition, no stable band):**
- *MNIST + MLP (moderate weight decay):* β(t) is **non-monotonic** — rises 0.93→1.60 (feature emergence, d_β≈2.7) then relaxes to ~1.29 (feature refinement, d_β≈4.5), two phases invisible to the scalar test loss. Δ_β and block asymmetry stay small throughout.
- *Multi-output regression (matched-space, the only case exercising the output-level matrix):* β_repr(t) and β_loss(t) move in **opposite directions** (d_repr 5→3.2, d_loss 2.3→2.6); the output-level spectrum shows a knee at K≈10 matching the latent effective rank.
- *8-Gaussian GAN mode coverage:* smooth monotonic growth of mode coverage (0→8 modes in all seeds), with a stable ĥ₁=2 doublet fingerprint of the Z₈ scaffold but **no sharp transition step**.

**Group B — geometric phase transitions (stable integer fingerprint at a sharply localised step):**
- *Modular-arithmetic transformer (p=113) grokking — the strongest result:* β(t) is already deep in its transition by step ~10³ (βmin≈0.55 in the memorisation interval; β<1 gives formal d_β<0, signalling discrete memorisation lattice rather than smooth density), rising to β_post≈1.4 at the grokking step ~4.4×10³. The matrix diagnostic detects structural reorganisation in **late memorisation, ~3000 steps before the test loss drops**. Post-grokking ĥ₁=12 (six Fourier-mode pairs, matching Power et al.), d̂_β=3, RSM in 10/10 seeds. "Bagel formation": upstream condenses onto T²=S¹(a)×S¹(b), downstream onto S¹(a+b). Δ_β shows a sharp positive spike (~0.06) at the grokking step — train representations reorganise before test, the cleanest single signal of the precise generalisation moment.
- *Sparse parity (k=3 on d=30):* endogenous transition; cloud condenses onto an 8-vertex atomic configuration (all 2³=3-bit patterns), parity label is one of 7 between-vertex contrasts. Z₂ order parameter O(t) plays the magnetisation role; k-means elbow confirms k=8.
- *Synthetic task switch (Z₂→S₄ output):* β_repr steps discontinuously in a <100-step window; post-switch Z₄ arc on S¹ (ĥ₁=2).
- *Input-topology bifurcation (1→2 cluster Gaussian, target fixed):* cluster Z₂ order parameter jumps 1→peak ~6.8→plateau ~4.5 in <100 steps; boundary case (no clean band, d̂_β=4 outside BBS-admissible range).

**Cross-cutting findings:** The residual-RMT bulk is **RSM-shaped (peaked, non-Wigner) in every seed of every experiment** (Wigner-KL ~1.5–2.3), robust across transformer/MLP/CNN-generator architectures. The framework is a regime classifier (five regimes in Table 2), not a universal smooth-manifold validator: only the smooth/product rows invoke the literal BBS asymptotic ĥ₁=d.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — High.** The framing is genuinely distinctive: casting training diagnostics as the dynamics of an observable distance matrix read through an extended BBS random-matrix toolkit, with an Itô-SDE bridge to θ(t), sits at an unusual and fertile physics/ML interface. The Δ_β train–test synchronisation gap and the five-regime classification are fresh contributions.
- **Rigor — Strong.** Substantial theoretical apparatus (RMT, perturbative decomposition, Itô lemma, Funk–Hecke projections); experiments run over multiple seeds (typically 10) with IQR bands; three independent robustness calibrations (cross-seed integer agreement, negative-control calibration by Group A/pre-transition windows, residual-RMT step); dedicated sensitivity/calibration appendices (sample size, gap threshold, centring, arccos-vs-Euclidean metric, deduplication).
- **Reproducibility — Good.** Public GitHub repo with code and figures; standard small architectures with inline hyperparameters; fixed N=1000 protocol. Caveat: the method depends on two companion papers [5, 6] which appear to be arXiv-only at this point, so the full I-BBS algorithm is not self-contained here.
- **Significance — Promising but unproven at scale.** A compelling diagnostic complement to scalar loss curves, with a clean "strictly finer than loss" demonstration on grokking. Value for the broader interpretability/training-dynamics community is high *if* the fingerprints generalise.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - **Scale:** all experiments are small (one-layer transformer, MLPs, a small GAN, tiny CNN generator). No validation on modern CNNs/LLMs or realistic-scale tasks — generalisation to large models is untested.
  - **Interpretive awkwardness:** the β<1 regime (memorisation) yields formal d_β<0, requiring ad-hoc "discrete lattice, not smooth density" reinterpretation; the meaning of d_β is regime-dependent and stated case-by-case, which weakens its status as a single principled estimator.
  - **Dependence on readout layer and eval sample:** d_β differs across layers and the diagnostic quality depends on the chosen N-subset (framed as "quenched disorder," but sensitivity is only partially explored).
  - **Reliance on unreviewed companion work:** the core I-BBS algorithm lives in [5]; independent verification of that machinery is needed.
  - **AI-generated artefacts:** the author discloses that calculations, numerics, and the manuscript were produced by Claude Code (Opus 4.7) under supervision — a transparency plus, but a factor to weigh when assessing hand-verification depth.
  - **Single author,** limiting independent cross-checking during development.

## Strengths

- Novel, physics-grounded diagnostic that exposes spectral reorganisations invisible to scalar losses.
- Theoretically principled (RMT + extended BBS + Itô SDE) with concrete closed-form diagnostics.
- Rich, multi-scale observables (per-snapshot spectral, trajectory-level, 3D embedding, joint train–test) that are mutually corroborating.
- Clean, falsifiable central claim (diffusive vs. phase-transition dichotomy) supported across seven diverse experiments spanning classification, regression, generative, and grokking settings.
- Strongest empirical result — early detection of grokking (~3000 steps ahead of test loss) and the Δ_β generalisation-moment spike — is genuinely striking and intuitively interpretable.
- Public code and unusually transparent methodology (regime table, robustness appendices, AI-assistance disclosure).

## Limitations

- Experiments are small-scale; no demonstration on modern CNNs, LLMs, or realistic datasets.
- d_β(t) is regime-dependent and sometimes negative/ill-defined; it is a "geometric organisation signal" rather than a literal intrinsic dimension in several cases.
- Diagnostic quality depends on readout-layer choice and the fixed N-subset; full replica-averaging is mostly replaced by a single realisation or a few partitions.
- Heavy dependence on companion papers [5, 6, 8] for the I-BBS/FDM machinery (not self-contained, and apparently not yet peer-reviewed).
- Computational cost of forming and spectrally analysing an N×N matrix (N=1000) at many checkpoints on large models is not discussed.
- The "stable fingerprints" are validated only on the chosen seven tasks; whether they remain stable under optimiser/architecture variation is open.

## Relevance

该论文用随机矩阵理论（扩展 BBS）与 Itô-SDE 刻画神经网络训练中内部表示距离矩阵的谱动力学，对研究表示几何、训练相变（尤其 grokking）、可解释性与训练诊断高度相关，可作为标量损失曲线之外的新型细粒度监控工具，并对"内在维度"类诊断提出了体制分类式的替代视角。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the OMD fingerprints behave on modern CNNs/Transformers at scale (e.g. ImageNet training, LLM pretraining), and does the RSM-across-the-board residual finding survive?
- Do the phase-transition-like spectral reorganisations align quantitatively with other known training milestones (grokking onset, loss cliffs, capability emergence, rank collapse) — and can Δ_β(t) predict generalisation *before* it happens, online?
- Can OMD observables (especially Δ_β and ĥ₁ stability) be used as active regularisers or early-stopping signals to steer training?
- How sensitive are the integer fingerprints to the readout layer, the N-subset choice, and full replica-averaging vs. a single quenched realisation?
- How does OMD compare head-to-head with intrinsic-dimension (TwoNN), CKA/RSA, and information-bottleneck diagnostics on the same checkpoints — what does each uniquely capture?
- Does the I-BBS machinery (companion paper [5]) reproduce under independent reimplementation, given the paper is not self-contained on the core algorithm?
- The Itô-SDE bridge to θ(t) is derived but "not pursued" for parameter inference — could observing M(t) enable approximate inference of θ(t) dynamics in practice?
