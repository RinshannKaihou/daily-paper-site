> **一句话总结：** 本文首次在上下文嵌入（西班牙语/法语）中分离语法性别与语义性别偏见，构建了无生命名词的受控模板语境与自然Wikipedia语境数据集，提出质心/SVM/LDA性别方向估计器结合污染感知加权策略的统一框架与双目标评估指标，发现无加权的受控语境能产生最纯净的语法性别方向，且简单的质心估计器优于判别式基线。

> **TL;DR:** The first attempt to disentangle grammatical gender from semantic gender bias in contextual embeddings (Spanish/French). Using balanced inanimate-noun datasets built from controlled templates and natural Wikipedia contexts, the authors combine centroid/SVM/LDA gender-direction estimators with contamination-aware weighting and a dual-objective evaluation (leakage reduction + semantic preservation). Key finding: unweighted controlled contexts yield the purest grammatical gender direction, and the simple centroid estimator outperforms discriminative baselines (SVM, LDA) across BETO, CamemBERT, mBERT, and XLM-R.

## 核心贡献

- **首次在上下文化语言模型中分离语法性别与语义偏见：** 以往相关工作只针对静态词向量（如 Zhou et al.），本文首次将二维性别解耦扩展到 BERT 类上下文表示，并构建了西班牙语/法语无生命名词的成对受控模板与自然Wikipedia数据集。
- **首次形式化并量化"语义污染"：** 将污染分为社会约定型语义偏见与语料诱导的分布噪声（主题/词汇/共现噪声）两类，并采用 WEAT 风格的余弦关联分数与均值/中位数归一化加权来量化。
- **多估计器 + 污染感知加权框架：** 系统比较质心（centroid）、LDA、线性 SVM 三种性别方向估计器，并设计均值/中位数归一化的污染感知加权策略。
- **双目标评估框架：** 提出无生命名词消减分（Rnoun）、职业词保留分（Pocc）及几何/调和权衡分（以调和均值 Tharm 为主指标），解决性别语言去偏见缺乏平衡评估标准的问题。

## Key Contributions

- **Pioneers grammatical–semantic gender disentanglement for contextual LMs:** Prior disentanglement work (e.g., Zhou et al.) operates only on static embeddings; this is the first to address the two-dimensional gender separation inside BERT-style contextual representations, using paired controlled-template and natural-Wikipedia datasets for Spanish/French inanimate nouns.
- **First formalization of semantic contamination in inanimate-noun contextual embeddings:** Splits contamination into (1) socially conventionalized semantic gender bias and (2) corpus-induced distributional noise (topical, lexical, co-occurrence), quantified via a WEAT-style cosine association score with mean- and median-normalized weighting.
- **Unified multi-estimator + contamination-aware weighting framework:** Systematically compares centroid-based, LDA-based, and linear-SVM gender-direction estimators under controlled and natural contexts, with contamination-aware down-weighting of polluted nouns.
- **Novel dual-objective evaluation framework:** Drawing on WEAT, SEAT, and hard-debiasing, introduces the Inanimate Noun Reduction Score (Rnoun), Occupational Preservation Score (Pocc), and geometric/harmonic trade-off scores (harmonic mean as primary) to resolve the lack of balanced evaluation standards for gendered-language debiasing.

## Methodology

**Problem & data.** Gendered languages (Spanish, French) conflate two distinct signals: morphosyntactic grammatical gender (mandatory noun–article/adjective agreement) and socially learned semantic gender bias. Prior projection-based debiasing (designed for genderless English) erases both, causing over-debiasing. The authors build balanced inanimate-noun lists (5,988 Spanish / 5,998 French nouns, GPT-5.3-mini generated + Wikipedia cross-referenced + manually filtered, gender-balanced), each placed into 10 controlled template contexts and ≥10 natural Wikipedia sentences, plus 100 masculine–feminine occupation pairs per language for evaluation only.

**Models.** Six frozen pretrained-encoder settings using final-layer, L2-normalized embeddings (subword-averaged): BETO, CamemBERT, mBERT, XLM-R (Spanish: BETO/mBERT/XLM-R; French: CamemBERT/mBERT/XLM-R).

**Contamination quantification (Sec 4.1).** A WEAT-style semantic association score s(w,A,B) measures each noun's alignment to male/female attribute sets; contamination strength I(w) is the projection of the noun embedding onto the implicit semantic gender direction dsem. Two complementary normalization strategies — mean-based (corrects global corpus shifts) and median-based (robust to outliers) — yield contamination-aware noun weights.

**Direction estimation (Sec 4.2).** Assumes grammatical gender is a latent linear subspace. Three estimators: (1) centroid — mean-masculine minus mean-feminine, a global distribution-shift estimator robust to local noise; (2) LDA — supervised direction maximizing class separability; (3) linear SVM — hyperplane normal. Applied identically to controlled and weighted-natural representations.

**Evaluation (Sec 4.3).** 5-fold cross-validation with orthogonal projection to remove the grammatical gender component dg. 
- Rnoun (Inanimate Noun Reduction Score): gap reduction after debiasing — high means grammatical leakage well suppressed.
- Pocc (Occupational Preservation Score): retained masculine–feminine separability for occupations — high means semantic gender preserved.
- Trade-off: geometric mean Tgeo and harmonic mean Tharm; harmonic chosen as primary because it penalizes imbalance between the two goals.

## Results & Highlights

- **Centroid estimator wins overall (Table 3):** Centroid achieves the best harmonic trade-off (H-Trade-off 0.870) vs LinearSVC (0.771) and LDA (0.578), with noun reduction 0.930 and occupation preservation 0.821. Conclusion: grammatical gender behaves as a low-rank global distribution shift, not a linearly separable classification problem; discriminative estimators are more sensitive to noise.
- **Controlled-unweighted is the best context setting (Table 4):** Controlled-unweighted consistently yields the strongest harmonic trade-off across all six language-model settings (e.g., Spanish BETO controlled-unweighted noun-reduction 0.980). Weighting variants and natural contexts generally degrade noun reduction; weighting helps occupation preservation slightly but lowers overall harmonic score.
- **Weighting = rebalancing, not correction (Sec 6.3):** Contamination-aware weighting only marginally helps and shifts the trade-off conservatively; it does not remove contamination at the source, indicating the simple mean/median normalization is suboptimal.
- **Generalization:** Findings are consistent across monolingual (BETO, CamemBERT) and multilingual (mBERT, XLM-R) encoders and across both Spanish and French.
- **Semantic stability:** The authors claim orthogonal projection removes only the grammatical-gender-aligned component without disrupting general lexical semantic structure, validated by supplementary semantic-stability tests.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and well-motivated. Framing the problem as two-dimensional (grammatical vs. semantic) gender disentanglement *inside contextual* embeddings is a genuine advance over the static-embedding-only prior art (Zhou et al., 2020). The formalization of "semantic contamination" with two named sources is a useful conceptual contribution.
- **Rigor:** Good. Principled 5-fold cross-validation, six model settings, two languages, three estimators × two context sources × three weighting variants, and a dual-objective metric that explicitly avoids gaming a single objective. Ablations cleanly isolate the effect of each factor.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong. Full code, scripts, noun lexicons, occupation pairs, templates, and contextual embeddings released (GitHub: WifiClein/Semantic-Bias-Aware-Estimation-...; HuggingFace: xnxnhp/semantic-gender-direction-embeddings); frozen pretrained encoders, L2-normalized embeddings — settings are fully specified.
- **Significance:** Moderate-to-high for multilingual NLP fairness and gendered-language debiasing evaluation; the controlled-vs-natural context finding and the centroid-over-discriminative finding are actionable design lessons for future debiasing pipelines.
- **Red flags / honesty caveats:** 
  - The negative/mixed result on the paper's own proposed weighting mechanism is disclosed honestly (it "does not consistently enhance" quality) — but this somewhat undercuts one of the headline contributions.
  - Noun lists are GPT-generated and only "manually validated"; inter-annotator reliability and exact filtering criteria are not quantified.
  - Reliance on a "semantic gender direction dsem" to *measure* contamination that is itself the very bias being studied is somewhat circular, which the authors partially acknowledge (current contamination measurement "may not be sufficiently optimal").
  - Minor writing issues (e.g., "the this issue" in abstract; "Controll" header typo in 4.1) suggest light copy-editing.

## Strengths

- First principled attack on grammatical–semantic gender disentanglement in contextual embeddings, with clean conceptual framing.
- Dual-objective (Rnoun vs Pocc) evaluation with harmonic trade-off is a sensible, non-gameable design that others can adopt.
- Comprehensive ablations across estimators, context sources, weighting schemes, languages, and model families.
- Excellent reproducibility — public code, lexicons, templates, and released embeddings.

## Limitations

- Scope limited to two closely related Romance languages (Spanish, French) with stable binary gender systems; generalization to languages with richer case–gender interaction, non-binary, or animate gender systems is unverified (authors acknowledge this).
- Only simple mean/median weighting is tested; the headline contamination-aware weighting contributes little — more advanced adaptive/adversarial weighting left to future work.
- GPT-generated noun lists and manually-curated occupation lexicons introduce potential dataset bias; reliability statistics absent.
- Evaluation is intrinsic (projection/gap-based); no downstream-task fairness or utility evaluation to confirm practical debiasing benefit.
- Reliance on linear-subspace assumption; authors note future work should consider layer-wise and causal-intervention approaches.

## Relevance

对多语言上下文模型去偏见、性别表征解耦与公平性评估高度相关：提供了在上下文嵌入层面区分语法性别与语义偏见的方法论与平衡评估框架，可作为后续在更广语言类型和下游任务上做因果性、结构化解耦研究的基础。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the centroid + controlled-context findings generalize beyond Romance languages — e.g., to German (3 genders), Slavic languages with case–gender interaction, or Semitic languages with non-binary systems?
- At which transformer layers do grammatical vs. semantic gender signals emerge, and would layer-specific estimation improve disentanglement?
- Would advanced adaptive/adversarial weighting (the authors' suggested future direction) actually turn the contamination-aware mechanism from a marginal rebalancer into a real corrective signal?
- Does removing the estimated grammatical gender direction improve fairness on downstream multilingual tasks (e.g., coreference, occupation classification) without hurting language-understanding benchmarks?
- Could causal-intervention methods replace projection to better handle the structural ambiguity of grammatical gender in contextual representations?
