Learning as Observable Matrix Dynamics: Diffusive Relaxations versus Phase Transitions
Igor Halperin · 2026-06-29 · arXiv:2606.29679
random-matrix-theory training-dynamics representation-geometry grokking phase-transitions

An observable N×N distance matrix tracks training dynamics through random-matrix spectra — exposing structural reorganisations the scalar loss cannot see.

OMD replaces the unobservable parameter vector θ(t) with an arccos distance matrix read via an extended BBS toolkit + Itô SDE — and detects grokking thousands of steps before test loss moves.

Key metric
~3k
steps of early warning

In modular-arithmetic grokking, the matrix diagnostic detects structural reorganisation ~3000 steps before the scalar test loss drops. Δ_β spikes precisely at the generalisation moment.

Why this matters

A fine-grained, matrix-valued monitor that sits beyond scalar loss curves — and a regime-classification alternative to intrinsic-dimension diagnostics for interpretability & training dynamics.

Critical edge

All seven experiments are small-scale — a one-layer transformer, MLPs, a small GAN. There is no validation on modern CNNs or LLMs, so large-model generalisation is entirely untested.

01Methodology — what is actually being observed

OMD swaps the unobservable, million-to-billion-parameter vector θ(t) for something a forward pass can compute: an N×N (N=1000) arccos distance matrix M(t) built from unit-normalised internal representations of a fixed held-out input set. Three reading layers turn that matrix into diagnostics.

Per snapshot, an extended Bogomolny–Bohigas–Schmit (I-BBS) perturbative split M(d)+εM(noise) reads an integer multiplet multiplicity ĥ₁, a delocalisation slope d_β, and an ambient noise model (RSM vs FSM). Across snapshots, a Funk–Hecke / trajectory toolkit measures projector drift, matrix commutators, and level-spacing universality. An Itô-SDE derived from Itô's lemma along the SGD Langevin path gives the formal bridge back to θ(t).

A joint train–test object yields the synchronisation gap Δ_β = β_train − β_test, and a 3D MDS embedding renders training as a moving particle cloud. The payoff: OMD classifies representation geometry into five regimes — and reads off transitions the scalar loss hides.

03Strengths

6 items
  • Physics-grounded diagnostic that exposes spectral reorganisations invisible to scalar losses.
  • Principled theory stack (RMT + extended BBS + Itô SDE) with concrete closed-form diagnostics.
  • Multi-scale, mutually corroborating observables — per-snapshot, trajectory, 3D embedding, joint train–test.
  • Falsifiable central claim (diffusive vs. phase-transition dichotomy) across 7 diverse experiments.
  • Striking grokking result (~3000 steps early) with an intuitively interpretable Δ_β generalisation spike.
  • Public code + unusual transparency: regime table, robustness appendices, explicit AI-assistance disclosure.

04Limitations

6 items
  • Small-scale only — no modern CNNs, LLMs, or realistic datasets; large-model generalisation untested.
  • d_β(t) is regime-dependent and sometimes negative / ill-defined — a signal, not a literal intrinsic dimension.
  • Quality depends on readout-layer choice and the fixed N-subset; full replica-averaging is mostly dropped.
  • Heavy dependence on unreviewed companion papers [5, 6, 8]; the I-BBS core is not self-contained here.
  • Cost of forming & spectrally analysing an N×N (N=1000) matrix at many checkpoints is not discussed.
  • "Stable fingerprints" validated on only 7 tasks; stability under optimiser / architecture variation is open.

05Open questions for further exploration

Q1Do the fingerprints survive at scale — ImageNet training, LLM pretraining — and does the RSM-across-the-board residual finding hold?
Q2Can Δ_β(t) predict generalisation before it happens, online — and steer training as an active regulariser or early-stop signal?
Q3How sensitive are the integer fingerprints to readout layer, N-subset choice, and replica-averaging vs. a single quenched realisation?
Q4Head-to-head vs. TwoNN, CKA/RSA, and information-bottleneck diagnostics — what does OMD uniquely capture?
Q5Does the I-BBS machinery (companion [5]) reproduce under independent reimplementation?
Q6Can observing M(t) enable approximate inference of the unobserved θ(t) dynamics — the derived but "not pursued" Itô bridge?
