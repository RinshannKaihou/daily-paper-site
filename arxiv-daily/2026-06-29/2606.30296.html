01

Methodology

Storyboarder → Coder → Renderer → crash-Diagnoser → VLM Reviewer → Visual Reviser  ⊕  Episodic Memory Bank (M⁺ / M⁻)

ManimAgent wraps a standard six-agent reflection pipeline that renders a paper section into a short Manim teaching clip, then augments it with a dual-channel Episodic Memory Bank (EMB): a positive channel M⁺ of soft Reference Examples (successes scoring u*≥85, distilled into transferable rationales) and a negative channel M⁻ of hard Known Pitfalls (crash→success transitions). The two channels share one SQLite table but use separate Faiss indices so cross-polarity leakage is impossible.

Per scene the agent retrieves top-k from each channel via a pinned all-MiniLM-L6-v2 embedder, injects them into disjoint prompt slots, regenerates the full Scene class each text-iteration, then runs a VLM-scored visual reflection loop and ships the best-of-N candidate. A VLM Reviewer gates every write. The bank grows monotonically from the agent's own task stream — no gradients, no human seeds, no eviction — and is frozen at snapshots for read-only fixed-probe evaluation.

03

Strengths vs. Limitations

✓Strengths

  • Sharp framing: "cross-task forgetting" as a structural failure, with a memorable human-educator analogy.
  • Exemplary controls: fixed-probe, matched-budget RAG, shuffled-memory, embedder pinning, read-only eval.
  • Channels differ & verified: M⁺ lifts first-attempt quality; M⁻ cuts repair cost (ablation-confirmed).
  • Honest reporting of negative VLM–human correlation; sits on the cost–quality Pareto frontier.

!Limitations

  • Reward–human mismatch: the VLM signal that gates writes anti-correlates with humans (r=−0.17).
  • Modest quality edge over static RAG (+0.23); the real win is efficiency, not output quality.
  • Narrow scale & scope: O(10²) entries, single renderer (Manim), 5 English domains, no eviction.
  • Costly & fragile: 19–31 min & ~60K tokens/task; "memory rot" after model upgrades unaddressed.
04

Open Questions

Q1If the VLM reward anti-correlates with humans, what really drives the human-judged gains — is it M⁻ failure-avoidance cutting crashes, not VLM-guided polishing?
Q2Does the dual-channel design transfer to other programmatic tasks (D3.js, Three.js, code synthesis) where no VLM keyframe signal exists?
Q3How do gains behave beyond K=200 — where is the saturation point, and when does retrieval noise or memory rot dominate?
Q4Would a human-gated or ensemble-VLM write gate beat a single anti-correlated VLM for memory precision?
