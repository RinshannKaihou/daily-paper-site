Why this matters

Which models you pair matters more than raw capability — asymmetric attractor influence gives partial predictability for monitoring autonomous agents.

⚠ Critical edge

“Attractor” is a metaphor, not a proven fixed point. Off-axis drift ≈ 0.8 shows the interpolation axis explains only part of the geometry — leaving basins embedding- and judge-dependent.

01Methodology, distilled

Seven frontier LLMs (plus open variants) debate 20 controversial topics head-to-head for 20 turns. Each model first plays itself in self-play (Supporter vs Opposer) to reveal its intrinsic endpoint — the “attractor” basin.

In mixed-play, two different models debate. Their final embeddings (384-d SBERT, topic-centered) are projected onto the line between the two self-play basins. Displacement along that line is the partnerward pull α; the residual is off-axis drift δ⊥. Pair contraction C measures how far the endpoints move together; dominance Δ measures who pulls harder.

A neutral Discussant ablation and 3-seed repetition test robustness. Separately, an LLM-judge scores every turn on seven discourse traits (meta-commentary, flattery, rationality…) to see which traits propagate between partners.

03Verdict

▲ Strengths

  • Turns a fuzzy phenomenon into measurable quantities — attractor basins + axis decomposition.
  • Broad coverage: 9 models, multi-axis evidence (latent, trait, stance) that corroborate.
  • Honest about the analogy status of “attractor” and the limits of the stance view.
  • Directly actionable for designing & monitoring autonomous agent systems.

▼ Limitations

  • Dyads only — no triads, populations, or cooperative tasks tested.
  • “Attractor” is metaphorical; no formal dynamical-systems stability proof.
  • Embedding- & judge-dependent; large off-axis drift makes the 1-D story partial.
  • Causal mechanism for who attracts / who yields is unexplained.
  • Relies on commercial models that drift over versions — reproducibility risk.

04Open questions

  1. Do basins persist with >2 agents, or do new collective attractors emerge that no single model shows?
  2. Which model properties (post-training, size, RLHF vs constitutional AI) set attractor strength vs malleability?
  3. Can attractor effects be deliberately amplified or suppressed — e.g., to block trait contagion like flattery?
  4. Does off-axis drift shrink with longer horizons (T ≫ 20) or a different embedding?
  5. Do these asymmetric patterns transfer to human–model dialogue, with manipulation/persuasion risk?
