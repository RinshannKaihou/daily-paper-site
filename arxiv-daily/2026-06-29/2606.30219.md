> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 EvalSafetyGap 概念框架，将 LLM 评测侧（基准饱和/污染）与对齐侧（奖励欺骗/越狱）的"代理失效"统一在 Goodhart 定律下，并通过 10 个前沿模型的审计揭示：能力与持续对抗鲁棒性相关性统计上不确定（Pearson r=+0.232, p=0.520, 95% CI [-0.530, 0.741]），开源-闭源安全差距温和且主要源于治理/披露维度而非行为鲁棒性，且高度依赖单个边界模型（Grok 4）的分类。

> **TL;DR:** A 67-page hybrid survey + conceptual framework (EvalSafetyGap) that maps how LLM evaluation metrics and alignment proxies break under optimization pressure (Goodhart's Law), backed by a 10-model frontier audit showing the capability–robustness association is statistically indeterminate (r=+0.232, p=0.520) and that the open–closed safety gap is modest, governance/disclosure-driven, and highly sensitive to one borderline model's classification.

## 核心贡献

- **EvalSafetyGap 组织假设**：首次将"评测侧代理失效"（基准饱和、数据污染、prompt 敏感）与"对齐侧代理失效"（奖励欺骗、谄媚、越狱、浅层对齐）置于同一概念框架下比较，统一以 Goodhart 定律（regressional / extremal / causal / adversarial 四种失效模式）作为分析透镜。
- **混合文献综述（2018–2026）**：系统检索 + 叙事综合 + 单独追踪灰色证据，覆盖 8 条证据流：基准有效性、动态评测、LLM-as-judge 可靠性、安全评测、越狱/拒绝鲁棒性、奖励欺骗、机制可解释性、治理/可审计性。
- **两个原创概念构造**：**Instability Decomposition（不稳定性分解）** G_target ~ f(β, ε, σ, m, d, M) 与 **Alignment Trilemma（对齐三难）**——优化有效性(O)、价值/构念保真度(V)、鲁棒泛化(G)，明确声明是"工程清单"而非不可能定理。
- **七维谐波指标 + 十模型结构化审计**：对 GPT-5.3、Claude Opus 4.5、Gemini 3、Grok 4（闭源）与 Kimi K2、DeepSeek V3.2、Llama 4、Qwen 3.5、Mistral Large 3、Phi-4（开源）构建七个 [0,1] 指标，分解出 Core Safety（行为）与 Governance（披露）两个复合分。
- **诊断性实证结论**：能力-鲁棒性相关性不确定；开源-闭源差距主要由治理驱动；结果对 Grok 4 的去留极其敏感（leave-one-out 后 composite safety 从 d=1.69 飙升至 d=3.48）；强调"诊断而非排名"。
- **方法学严谨性声明**：自始至终区分"症状/选择关联/干预结果/机制解释"四个证据层级，并明确当前审计仅达到前两层，不主张因果推断。

## Key Contributions

- **EvalSafetyGap hypothesis**: a unified organizing framework placing evaluation-side proxy failures (saturation, contamination, prompt sensitivity) and alignment-side proxy failures (reward hacking, sycophancy, jailbreaks, shallow alignment) under one Goodhart's-Law lens (four failure modes: regressional, extremal, causal, adversarial), with an explicit conceptual mapping table across the two domains.
- **Hybrid systematic survey (2018–2026)**: combines systematic search, narrative synthesis, and separately tracked grey evidence across eight evidence streams (benchmark validity, dynamic evaluation, LLM-as-judge reliability, safety evaluation, jailbreak/refusal robustness, reward hacking, mechanistic interpretability, governance/auditability).
- **Two original conceptual constructs**: the **Instability Decomposition** (G_target ~ f(β, ε, σ, m, d, M), a study-design checklist, not a theorem) and the **Alignment Trilemma** (Optimization effectiveness O, Value/construct fidelity V, Robust generalization G) — explicitly framed as engineering taxonomies, not impossibility results.
- **Seven harmonized indicators + structured 10-model audit**: capability, single-attempt ASR robustness, multi-attempt ASR-100 robustness, sycophancy, privacy, transparency, auditability — decomposed into Core Safety (behavioral) and Governance (disclosure) composites.
- **Diagnostic empirical findings**: capability–ASR-100 Pearson r=+0.232 (p=0.520, 95% CI [-0.530, 0.741]); open–closed composite-safety gap modest and governance-driven (Governance gap 0.268 vs ASR gap 0.043); leave-one-out (Grok 4) inflates the contrast from d=1.69 to d=3.48 — illustrating fragility at n=10.
- **Epistemic humility / four evidence levels**: cleanly distinguishes measurement symptom → selection association → intervention result → mechanistic account, and repeatedly stresses the audit reaches only the first two levels (diagnostic, not rank-generating, not causal).

## Methodology

The paper uses a three-part methodology: (1) a hybrid literature survey, (2) a conceptual framework, and (3) a structured frontier-model audit.

**Hybrid survey.** A systematic search (PRISMA-style flow accounting, explicit eligibility criteria) is paired with narrative synthesis and separately tracked grey evidence (model cards, blog posts, developer reports). Coverage spans 2018–2026 measurement work across eight evidence streams.

**Conceptual framework.** Two constructs are introduced:
- *Instability Decomposition*: `G(π) := E_π_target[U] − E_π[U]`, with candidate contributors `G_target ~ f(β, ε, σ, m, d, M)` (β=optimization pressure, ε=proxy misspecification, σ=measurement/annotation variability, m=evaluation evidence, d=train-to-target distribution shift, M=model/data/process). The `~f(·)` is explicitly "a conceptual dependency, not equality, monotonicity, or a lower bound."
- *Alignment Trilemma*: three desiderata — Optimization effectiveness (O), Value/construct fidelity (V), Robust generalization (G) — inspired loosely by the CAP theorem but asserted only as an engineering checklist.

**Ten-model audit.** Ten purposively selected systems (4 provider-controlled: GPT-5.3, Claude Opus 4.5, Gemini 3, Grok 4; 6 open-weight: Kimi K2, DeepSeek V3.2, Llama 4, Qwen 3.5, Mistral Large 3, Phi-4) are scored on seven harmonized [0,1] indicators harmonized from public evidence. Composites: *Core Safety* (behavioral: single + multi-attempt ASR robustness, sycophancy, privacy) and *Governance* (transparency + auditability via a project-specific three-level rubric inspired by FMTI). *Composite Safety* = unweighted mean of all six non-capability dimensions.

**Provenance / reproducibility discipline.** Each of 70 indicator cells is classified via a four-step rule (identity, construct, protocol, transformation) into *traceable*, *recomputable*, or *common-protocol measured*. 13/70 base cells are flagged as estimated. "Not reported," "estimated," and "version-unspecified" are kept as distinct missingness states. A parity report preserves both manuscript-reported and recomputed statistics (e.g., r=+0.179 reported vs r=+0.232 recomputed).

## Results & Highlights

**Table 3 — reported harmonized scores (higher = better):**
- Capability range is tight (0.86–0.92, span 0.06), consistent with frontier convergence.
- ASR-100 robustness spans 0.37 (0.15–0.52 after inversion); Composite Safety spans ~0.35 (0.428–0.775). This tight-capability / dispersed-safety pattern is the headline descriptive illustration of evaluation-safety divergence.

**Pattern 1 — capability vs. sustained adversarial robustness:**
- Pearson r = +0.232 (95% CI [-0.530, 0.741], p = 0.520, n = 10) — interval spans moderate negative to positive associations ⇒ **statistically indeterminate**.
- Spearman ρ = +0.181 (p = 0.602); when safety is the composite (mixing in transparency/auditability), ρ = +0.532 (p = 0.11) — demonstrating the operationalization dependence of conclusions.

**Pattern 2 — open–closed differences, decomposed:**
- Capability: no detectable group difference (t(8)=1.08, p=0.312; Cohen's d=0.70).
- Composite Safety: closed M=0.661 vs open M=0.497 (t(8)=2.62, p=0.031, d=1.69) — but very wide CI [-0.49, 4.86], sample-sensitive.
- **Decomposition is the key insight**: ASR-100 shows essentially no group gap (0.335 vs 0.292, gap 0.043), whereas the custom Governance rubric shows the largest separation (closed 0.626 vs open 0.358, gap 0.268, d=1.90). The "open–closed safety gap" is therefore governance/disclosure-driven, not behavioral-robustness-driven.
- **Leave-one-out (Grok 4) sensitivity**: excluding Grok 4 (5/7 estimated cells, lowest disclosure) sharply inflates every contrast — composite safety d jumps from 1.69 to 3.48 (p=0.002); including it removes the clean governance separation. This directly shows fragility at n=10 and dependence on a single borderline model's classification.

**Cross-model attack transferability** and missing-data sensitivity analyses reinforce that public evidence uses heterogeneous protocols, so the audit is diagnostic rather than rank-generating.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Medium-high as an organizing synthesis. The individual findings (Goodhart in benchmarks, reward hacking, shallow alignment) are well-established; the novelty is the cross-domain conceptual bridge (evaluation-side ⇄ alignment-side) plus the Instability Decomposition / Alignment Trilemma vocabulary. The constructs are honestly framed as "working hypotheses" and "engineering taxonomies," not new theorems.
- **Rigor**: High for a survey. Transparent provenance accounting (13/70 estimated cells flagged), four-step cell classification, parity reporting of reported vs recomputed statistics, leave-one-out sensitivity, and explicit falsification criteria. The authors do not overclaim — they repeatedly state the audit is "diagnostic rather than rank-generating."
- **Reproducibility**: Moderate-to-high for the audit (rounded Table 3 is the canonical input; documented code recomputes statistics; estimated cells are marked), but fundamentally limited because the underlying public evidence uses heterogeneous protocols (different attacks, judges, attempt budgets). Common-protocol measurement is correctly identified as the replication standard that is *not* met.
- **Significance**: Conceptually valuable as a shared vocabulary and evidence map for the LLM-evaluation/alignment community; likely to be cited as a framework reference. Empirically modest — n=10 with purposive sampling cannot settle the headline questions.
- **Red flags / honesty concerns**:
  - The headline statistics are genuinely underpowered (n=10, CIs spanning ±0.5+); the authors are commendably explicit about this, so it is not a hidden flaw, but readers may still over-interpret "open–closed gap."
  - Governance/auditability rubric is project-specific and not a validated instrument (no Cronbach's α / PCA, justified by the authors as intentional).
  - Some model names (Grok 4, GPT-5.3, Claude Opus 4.5, Gemini 3, Qwen 3.5) point to a near-future/2026 frontier snapshot; checkpoint-level version locking is acknowledged as a limitation.
  - "Estimated" cells (e.g., Grok 4's 5/7) materially drive results, which the leave-one-out analysis transparently exposes.
  - No causal identification: purely cross-sectional, public-evidence-based.

## Strengths

- Genuinely unifies two normally-separate literatures (benchmark validity ⇄ alignment failure) under a precise Goodhart taxonomy with a concrete cross-domain mapping table.
- Exceptional epistemic discipline: four-level evidence taxonomy, falsification requirements, parity reporting, and constant refraining from causal overclaims.
- Transparent provenance engineering (traceable / recomputable / common-protocol levels; distinct missingness states) that other audit papers could adopt.
- The open–closed decomposition (behavioral vs governance) is a practically useful insight for evaluators and policymakers.
- Actionable, prespecified research agenda (four Expectations, future-work items on longitudinal tracking, causal designs, automated monitoring).

## Limitations

- Tiny, purposive sample (n=10) with no power; headline correlation is statistically indeterminate (wide CIs).
- Heterogeneous public protocols undermine comparability; no common-protocol measurement.
- Composite Safety deliberately mixes behavioral + disclosure dimensions, so it is not a validated unidimensional construct.
- Governance/auditability rubric is ad hoc and unvalidated.
- Heavy reliance on estimated cells for some models (Grok 4), making leave-one-out conclusions fragile.
- Cross-sectional only — no causal identification of optimization-pressure or distribution-shift effects.
- "Open/closed" grouping is not randomized and conflates license/access with governance and checkpoint heterogeneity.
- Survey coverage (grey evidence) may introduce temporal/publication bias.

## Relevance

直接关联 LLM 评测与 AI 安全研究：为基准有效性、动态评测、奖励欺骗、机制可解释性等方向提供了统一的概念地图与可检验假设，对从事对齐评测、基准设计与安全审计的读者具有方法论参考价值；其"行为安全 vs 治理披露"的分解思路尤其值得评测平台借鉴。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How would the capability–robustness association change under a version-locked, common-protocol re-evaluation (single attack generator, judge, attempt budget) with a larger, a-priori-power-justified sample?
- Can the Instability Decomposition's components (β, ε, σ, m, d, M) be empirically isolated via controlled optimization-pressure experiments within one model family?
- Does the "governance-driven open–closed gap" persist once disclosure is measured by a validated instrument (e.g., official FMTI) rather than a project-specific rubric?
- How does evaluation awareness / strategic deception (models that know they are being tested) interact with the adversarial-Goodhart failure mode as models become more capable?
- Can a longitudinal, repeated-measurement design detect the "benchmark discriminative lifetime" shortening hypothesized in Expectation 3?
- Does the cross-domain hypothesis hold — do interventions improving proxy design / measurement precision benefit both evaluation validity *and* alignment assessment?
