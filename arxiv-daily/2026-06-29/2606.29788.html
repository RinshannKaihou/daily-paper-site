Why This Matters

“Delete the text = done” is false. Retained images form an independent leak channel that breaks GDPR / RTBF erasure in deployed multimodal agents.

Critical Edge

The headline 2.0% post-mitigation residual sits partly within judge noise — 5 of 6 remaining “leaks” are marginal / variance, and no precision–recall curve is reported.

01

Methodology, distilled

The Information Provenance Graph (IPG) models every representation of a fact across a memory stack as nodes in a DAG — text entries, embeddings, image files, graph edges, derived facts, and implicit visual features — each tagged with a deletion affordance: addressable (deletable by ID), linked (reachable only via a maintained provenance edge), or persistent (survives storage-level deletion). Forgetting Completeness is simply deleted-nodes ÷ total-nodes.

The crucial insight: for text, every node comes from an explicit storage op, so provenance tracking can in principle reach FCS = 1 — a mere engineering gap. Images differ. A VLM forward pass encodes implicit attributes (geolocation, style, culture) that are never stored explicitly, are distributed across embedding space, yet are empirically recoverable. These persistent nodes are unreachable by tracking — an architectural residual.

MemLeak benchmarks this on 113 profiles × 20 facts (5 categories, 3 visual-grounding levels), 523 real Unsplash photos, and 5 probe types (P1–P5). Leakage is quantified by the Cross-Modal Leakage Rate, judged by a 3-model ensemble (GPT-4.1, Claude Sonnet 4, Gemini 2.5 Flash, majority vote), with 95% Wilson CIs and human dual-annotation κ = 0.88. The mitigation — content-aware semantic deletion — adds one extra VLM audit pass at deletion time that flags 21.0% of retained images.

Strengths

  • Targets a real failure mode in deployed memory (Mem0, Letta/MemGPT) — not toy weight-editing.
  • Formal, unifying IPG taxonomy plus a reusable benchmark with calibrated baselines; data & code released (MIT).
  • Clean split of three channels, and an explicit engineering-gap vs. architectural-residual distinction.
  • Practical mitigation (one VLM pass) with honest cost reporting (21% image removal).
  • Intellectual honesty: every confound (Mem0 mixing, same-fact κ, Unsplash realism) is stated, not buried.

Limitations

  • Residual leakage not eliminated (2.0%); needs representation-level fixes left as future work.
  • Ecological validity bounded by Unsplash stock photos — authentic user data may differ.
  • Mitigation result partly within judge noise (5/6 post-deletion “leaks” marginal); no precision–recall curve.
  • Proprietary, drifting judge/VLM models — long-term exact-rate reproducibility not guaranteed.
  • Modest human-annotation scale (n=100), two authors, wide CIs (~±4 pp); no defense vs. union leakage (16.0%).
03

Open questions for follow-up

  1. By what precise mechanism do unrelated retained images leak a deleted fact — shared scene, recurring objects, or geolocation — and can it be predicted a priori?
  2. Can semantic deletion reach zero residual without removing 1/5 of all visual memories (e.g. redaction / blurring instead of deletion)?
  3. How does leakage scale with memory size, images per profile, and frontier VLM capability?
  4. Is leakage robust under strictly black-box access, or does the threat model assume knowledge of which fact was deleted?
  5. Do analogous persistent-node channels exist for audio / video memory, and does IPG extend cleanly?
  6. Can the union-of-VLMs leakage (16.0%) be closed — or is multi-VLM auditing itself the compliance posture?
