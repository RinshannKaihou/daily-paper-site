> **一句话总结：** 本文以多层感知机在 MNIST/FashionMNIST 上为实验载体，追踪训练过程中 Hessian 主导特征向量的"位移"（借鉴玻璃态系统分析）与"局域化"（逆参与比 IPR）两个统计量，发现 SGD 使主导曲率方向趋于稳定且去局域化，而 Adam 则持续重组特征向量并将其质量集中于少数参数之上——从而以特征向量（而非特征值）视角刻画了优化器间的本质差异。

> **TL;DR:** Tracking the leading Hessian eigenvectors of MLPs trained on MNIST/FashionMNIST, the authors measure displacement (a two-time correlation from glassy-systems physics) and localization (inverse participation ratio). SGD stabilizes and delocalizes the top curvature directions; Adam keeps reorganizing them and concentrates curvature onto a tiny subset of parameters — a robust, optimizer-dependent signature invisible to eigenvalue-only analyses.

## 核心贡献

- 将统计物理（玻璃态系统、Anderson 局域化）中的**位移（displacement）**与**逆参与比（IPR）**两套工具系统引入神经网络 Hessian **特征向量**动力学分析，弥补了以往研究只关注特征值（曲率幅度）而忽略方向信息的空白。
- 明确区分"子空间稳定"与"单个特征向量稳定"：以往工作多依赖 top-k 子空间级度量，本文直接逐个追踪主导向量随时间的演化。
- 在小批量（mini-batch）现代训练范式下对比 **SGD / Adam / SAM** 三种优化器，揭示了清晰且稳健的优化器依赖行为。
- 实验发现 **SGD 使主导曲率方向渐进稳定**（位移饱和且低于随机基线），**Adam 产生近乎完全的去相关**（持续重组特征向量），**SAM 介于二者之间**（缓慢但持续的漂移）。
- 发现 **Adam 下的特征向量局域化现象**：IPR 升至约 0.1，意味着主导曲率方向被极少数参数主导；而 SGD 趋向去局域化（趋近 1/N≈10⁻⁵ 的均匀基线）。
- 强调**局域化是一个稳定的统计量**：跨 5 次初始化方差极小，即使特征向量发生显著混合，局域化水平仍保持一致趋势——对剪枝、不确定性量化有实践意义。

## Key Contributions

- Imports two physics-grounded diagnostics — **eigenvector displacement** (a two-time correlation from glassy-systems analysis, following Baity-Jesi et al. 2018) and the **inverse participation ratio (IPR)** (from disordered-systems / Anderson-localization theory) — into the analysis of Hessian *eigenvector* dynamics in deep learning, going beyond the eigenvalue-only focus of prior work.
- Distinguishes **subspace stability from individual-eigenvector stability**: whereas prior literature mostly tracks the top-*k* subspace, this work explicitly follows how individual leading eigenvectors drift, swap, and reorganize over training.
- Performs a controlled **three-way optimizer comparison (SGD vs. Adam vs. SAM)** under realistic mini-batch training, revealing a clear and robust optimizer-dependent signature.
- **SGD** drives the leading curvature direction toward gradual stabilization (displacement saturates *below* the random baseline); **Adam** produces near-complete decorrelation (persistent eigenvector mixing close to the random baseline); **SAM** shows intermediate, slower-but-persistent drift.
- Identifies a **localization phenomenon under Adam** (IPR ≈ 0.1), meaning the most convex direction is dominated by a very small subset of parameters, while **SGD progressively delocalizes** toward the uniform 1/N ≈ 10⁻⁵ baseline.
- Shows **localization is a remarkably stable statistic**: very low variance across 5 initializations and stable even when eigenvectors undergo significant mixing — pointing to practical relevance for targeted pruning and uncertainty quantification.

## Methodology

**Setup.** A 3-layer fully-connected MLP (widths 100, 100, 10, ReLU activations) is trained with cross-entropy loss on **MNIST** and **FashionMNIST**, with Xavier initialization. Training uses mini-batches of size 128 for 10⁵ iterations. Three optimizers are compared: **SGD**, **Adam**, and **SAM (with SGD base)**, at fixed learning rates (10⁻² for SGD/SAM, 10⁻³ for Adam) with standard hyperparameters (Adam momentum β₁=0.9). Each run is repeated over **5 random initializations**. Dedicated ablations over batch size and learning rate are provided (Appendix F).

**Hessian tracking.** The top three Hessian eigenvectors are recomputed every ~100 training steps, yielding 1584 snapshots. The Hessian is evaluated on the **full dataset** (not mini-batch), and eigenvectors are obtained via **Lanczos iteration using Hessian–vector products** (Pearlmutter's trick), without ever forming the full Hessian matrix.

**Two complementary statistics.**
- *Displacement (two-time correlation):* Δ⁽ⁱ⁾(t_w, t_w+t) = (1/N)·‖v⁽ⁱ⁾(t_w) − v⁽ⁱ⁾(t_w+t)‖²₂. In a stationary regime it depends only on the lag t; explicit dependence on the waiting time t_w signals *aging* dynamics (a hallmark of glassy systems). It is benchmarked against a random-init baseline τ⁽ⁱ⁾ capturing complete decorrelation.
- *Localization (IPR):* IPR(e) = Σⱼ eⱼ⁴ ∈ [1/N, 1], with IPR ~ 1/k meaning the vector is effectively supported on ~k entries. Delocalized (≈1/N) vs. localized (≈O(1)) curvature directions are thereby quantified parameter-by-parameter.

## Results & Highlights

- **Eigenvector displacement shows glassy aging dynamics.** All optimizers exhibit an early-time collapse (rapid change, little dependence on training age), an aging regime (change occurs over progressively longer time scales for larger t_w), and late-time flattening — qualitatively matching glassy-system behavior.
- **Optimizer-dependent saturation:** SGD saturates *below* the random baseline (partial stabilization of the top curvature direction); Adam flattens *near* the baseline (near-complete decorrelation / persistent mixing); SAM shows intermediate, slower-but-persistent drift. The pattern reproduces on FashionMNIST and is robust to batch-size and learning-rate variations, except in the full-batch regime.
- **Gradient/update alignment differs sharply.** SGD and SAM updates lie on the "update ≈ gradient" diagonal (alignment scaling with eigenvalue magnitude; SAM slightly lower). In contrast, **Adam's update alignment stays below 0.1**, attributable to its momentum (β₁=0.9). Strong alignment with leading eigenvectors tends to slow optimization and stabilize those directions.
- **Localization reveals a striking Adam–SGD split (Figure 3).** Under Adam the leading eigenvector becomes strongly localized (IPR ≈ 0.1); SGD progressively delocalizes toward the uniform baseline (1/N = 10⁻⁵) with very low run-to-run variance; SAM sits slightly above SGD. Trends are robust across most settings, most pronounced for large learning rates and small-to-moderate batch sizes, and break down in the full-batch regime.
- **Localization is decoupled from mixing:** even as Adam's top eigenvectors rotate substantially, their IPR value holds a consistent trend throughout training (confirmed across the top 15 eigenvectors). A localized leading eigenvector means a small subset of weights dominates the most convex direction.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High relative to the dominant Hessian-eigenvalue literature (sharpness/curvature *magnitude*). The deliberate pivot to *eigenvector* dynamics, the distinction between subspace-level and individual-eigenvector stability, and the import of displacement/localization diagnostics from condensed-matter physics are genuinely fresh. The displacement framing builds directly on Baity-Jesi et al. (2018), so the *metric* is not entirely new — the novelty is its application to eigenvectors and the three-way optimizer comparison.
- **Rigor:** Solid. The random-initialization null model is a meaningful control; 5 seeds give variance estimates; Lanczos + Hessian–vector products is the correct computational tool; LR/batch-size ablations (Appendix F) check robustness. The full-batch caveat is honestly reported rather than hidden.
- **Reproducibility:** Likely good. The setup (small MLP, MNIST/FashionMNIST, public optimizers) is cheap and standard; the main non-trivial ingredient is Hessian-vector-product-based Lanczos eigenvector tracking, which is well-established. The paper does not appear to release code (not confirmed), which slightly lowers reproducibility.
- **Significance:** Moderate-to-high conceptually, moderate empirically. The qualitative optimizer contrast is a valuable lens, but the experimental scope (3-layer MLP, two image-classification datasets) limits immediate impact on the modern CNN/Transformer practice that dominates the field.
- **Red flags / caveats:** (i) Small architecture only — no CNNs/Transformers/LSTMs; (ii) classification only — no regression, language, or RL tasks; (iii) top-3 eigenvectors only; (iv) the mechanistic *cause* of Adam's localization (per-parameter diagonal preconditioning) is asserted rather than isolated by ablation (e.g., no experiment disentangling Adam's momentum from its adaptive scaling); (v) as a workshop paper, depth of analysis is naturally bounded.

## Strengths

- Novel, well-motivated eigenvector-centric lens on a heavily studied problem (optimizer comparison).
- Two complementary, physically-grounded statistics (displacement + IPR) with an explicit random-init null model.
- Clean, interpretable SGD ↔ Adam contrast, with SAM cleanly interpolated in between.
- Robustness checks across learning rate and batch size strengthen the claims; honest reporting of the full-batch breakdown.
- Practical hooks for pruning and uncertainty quantification via localization.

## Limitations

- Experiments confined to a small 3-layer MLP on MNIST/FashionMNIST; generalization to modern architectures is the obvious open question.
- Only the top 3 eigenvectors are tracked; bulk/bulk-edge behavior is not characterized here.
- The Adam-localization mechanism (adaptive scaling vs. momentum) is not causally isolated by ablation.
- No link is established between eigenvector dynamics and downstream generalization gap or final test performance (performance metrics are relegated to Appendix B).
- As a workshop paper, the analysis is necessarily concentrated rather than exhaustive.

## Relevance

本文从 Hessian 谱分析（特别是特征向量层面）切入，刻画 SGD/Adam/SAM 等优化器在小批量训练中的动力学差异，与"深度学习优化算法、训练动力学与泛化"这一研究主线高度契合。它为理解自适应优化器（Adam）相对于一阶方法（SGD）在曲率方向探索与参数敏感度分布上的本质不同提供了新的诊断工具，可作为研究优化器选择、剪枝与不确定性量化的参考文献。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the displacement/localization patterns transfer to **CNNs, Transformers, and larger-scale models** (e.g., is Adam-localization a universal signature of adaptive optimizers)?
- Is Adam's eigenvector localization **causally driven** by its per-parameter diagonal preconditioning (v/s scaling) rather than momentum? A controlled ablation (Adam without adaptive scaling, or momentum-only) would isolate the mechanism.
- Does stronger eigenvector localization **correlate with generalization gap or sharpness** in any systematic, predictive way across datasets/architectures?
- Can **IPR / displacement be used as live diagnostics** to schedule optimizer switching, detect edge-of-stability, or trigger pruning during training?
- How do **momentum, weight decay, and learning-rate schedules** (warmup, decay) reshape the SGD–Adam displacement contrast and the aging regime?
- Is the observed **aging (two-time) behavior** quantitatively consistent with predictions from glassy-dynamics or random-matrix eigenvector-flow theory (e.g., Allez–Bouchaud), and could that yield analytic predictions for optimizer dynamics?
