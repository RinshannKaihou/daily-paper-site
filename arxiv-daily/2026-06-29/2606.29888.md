> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示视觉-语言模型联合嵌入空间中同一概念在图像与文本模态下由不同的特征方向表示（跨模态特征异质性），并从理论上证明这会引发模态分裂且使现有"训练时辅助对齐损失"必然牺牲重建质量；据此提出为每个模态分别训练SAE再用匈牙利算法事后对齐潜在坐标的方法，在CLIP上取得更好的重建保真度、跨模态检索（R@1提升约8.9个点）与概念引导效果。

> **TL;DR:** The paper challenges the implicit assumption that semantically corresponding features share directions across image/text modalities in vision-language SAEs, formally defines "cross-modal feature heterogeneity," and proves (via Corollary 1, Theorem 1, and Props. 1–2) that reconstruction itself induces the modality split and that existing auxiliary alignment losses necessarily trade reconstruction quality for alignment. The authors instead train modality-specific SAEs and align latent coordinates post hoc with the Hungarian algorithm on coactivation correlations — preserving feature geometry while matching concepts, yielding the lowest reconstruction error and +8.9 pts image-to-text R@1 over the best baseline on CLIP.

## 核心贡献

- **命名并刻画"跨模态特征异质性"**：首次将"同一语义概念在图像/文本模态下对应不同特征方向（ϕᵢ ≠ ψᵢ）"这一现象形式化（Definition 1），并在 CLIP、MetaCLIP、OpenCLIP、SigLIP2 四个 VLM 的 MS-COCO 嵌入上实证验证——即便高共激活相关性（c≥0.8）的特征对，余弦距离仍稳定地围绕一个正值而非趋近于零。
- **从理论层面解释模态分裂的成因**：证明重建损失本身就会导致模态分裂（Corollary 1：m≥2n 时为精确重建，SAE 会把不同方向的特征分到不同坐标）；证明容量受限时（m<2n，Theorem 1）不同特征方向会被坍缩到同一列；并证明现有 Iso-Energy 与 group-sparse 两种辅助对齐损失只能在超过临界 λ 后通过对齐坍缩方向来降低分裂，代价是重建质量下降甚至退化为不激活（Propositions 1–2）。
- **提出"模态特异 SAE + 事后对齐"方法**：为图像与文本分别训练 SAE（保留各自特征几何），再用共激活相关矩阵上的匈牙利算法求最优置换矩阵 P̂（最大化 tr(CP)），仅对文本 SAE 的坐标做置换重排——对齐索引而不改动方向，因此不损失重建质量。
- **多任务实验验证**：在合成嵌入与真实 CLIP ViT-B/32 嵌入上，方法在重建误差、跨模态检索（MS-COCO）、概念引导（concept steering）和单义性（monosemanticity）上全面优于共享 SAE 及两种辅助损失基线。

## Key Contributions

- **Defines and empirically establishes "cross-modal feature heterogeneity."** Semantically matching features for the same concept occupy *different* directions (ϕᵢ ≠ ψᵢ) across image vs. text modalities — verified on four VLMs (CLIP, MetaCLIP, OpenCLIP, SigLIP2). Even feature pairs with high coactivation correlation (c ≥ 0.8) keep a cosine distance centered well above zero, directly contradicting the implicit ϕᵢ = ψᵢ assumption of prior joint-embedding SAE work.
- **A principled theoretical account of the modality split.** Corollary 1 shows reconstruction alone forces modality split (distinct directions go to distinct coordinates for accurate reconstruction); Theorem 1 shows that under limited capacity (m < 2n) distinct directions collapse into shared columns; Propositions 1–2 prove the two existing auxiliary-loss remedies (group-sparse, Iso-Energy) can only reduce the split by collapsing ϕ and ψ into a shared direction, which necessarily degrades (or fully breaks) reconstruction past a critical λ.
- **A simple, decoupled method: modality-specific SAEs + post-hoc alignment.** Train one SAE per modality, then solve the linear assignment problem (Hungarian algorithm over the coactivation correlation matrix) to permute the text SAE's latent indices onto the image SAE's. Because alignment is a pure permutation, feature geometry is untouched and reconstruction is preserved.
- **Strong empirical gains.** On CLIP ViT-B/32: lowest reconstruction MSE (0.089), and image-to-text R@1 of 16.0 vs. 7.1 for the best baseline (+8.9 pts); also best on concept-steering mAP/MRR and on monosemanticity coverage.

## Methodology

**Setup (Sec. 3).** Following the linear representation hypothesis, VLM image/text embeddings are modeled as sparse linear combinations of monosemantic feature directions: x = Φz = Σ zᵢϕᵢ (images), y = Ψz = Σ zᵢψᵢ (text), with the *same* latent code z but potentially *different* feature matrices Φ, Ψ. Crucially, unlike prior work, no constraint ϕᵢ = ψᵢ is imposed. A standard SAE encodes z̃ = σ(W⊤x) and decodes x̃ = Wz̃, trained to reconstruct both modalities (Eq. 5).

**1. Diagnosing heterogeneity (Sec. 4).** Decoder columns are used as estimates of feature directions (ϕ̂ᵢ, ψ̂̂ᵢ). Because concept correspondences are unknown, coactivation correlation cᵢⱼ = Corr(z̃(x), z̃(y)) on paired inputs serves as a proxy for "same concept." The cosine distance d_cos(ϕ̂ᵢ, ψ̂ⱼ) is then measured, grouped by correlation. Across all four VLMs, even high-correlation pairs stay directionally separated (e.g., a baseball concept: c = 0.67 but d_cos = 0.42).

**2. Theory: why split happens and why auxiliary losses fail (Sec. 5).**
- *Corollary 1 (m ≥ 2n):* with enough capacity the reconstruction-optimal decoder assigns a distinct column per direction → same concept lands on different coordinates across modalities = modality split.
- *Theorem 1 (m < 2n):* under capacity limits (incl. dead neurons), the optimal decoder groups angularly-close directions into one column (top eigenvector of a weighted outer product); since semantic correspondence ≠ directional closeness, this is unreliable.
- *Propositions 1–2:* for both the group-sparse loss and the Iso-Energy loss, below a critical λ the two directions stay separate (no alignment); above it they collapse into a single shared column (alignment gained, reconstruction lost); group-sparse pushes further to a degenerate inactive solution.

**3. Proposed method (Sec. 6).** Two stages:
- *Modality-specific SAEs:* train V on images and W on text independently (minimize L_rec(V;Φ) and L_rec(W;Ψ) separately), so directions never compete for columns.
- *Post-hoc alignment:* build correlation matrix C, then find the permutation P̂ = argmax tr(CP) via the Hungarian algorithm (dead latents excluded), and apply it to the text SAE only: z̃(y;W) = σ(P̂⊤W⊤y). This reindexes coordinates without altering any learned direction.
- *Inference:* encode with modality-specific SAEs, permute text indices for aligned cross-modal retrieval/steering; encode/decode stay modality-specific.

**Evaluation (Sec. 7).** Synthetic embeddings (ground-truth Φ, Ψ) validate the two design choices; real-world evaluation uses CLIP ViT-B/32 embeddings trained on CC-3M, tested on MS-COCO retrieval + ImageNet1K zero-shot + COCO concept steering + CC-3M monosemanticity (with MetaCLIP as the external scorer). All methods are matched in total SAE parameters (modality-specific uses m/2 each); results averaged over 3 runs.

## Results & Highlights

**Table 1 (CLIP ViT-B/32, real embeddings):**

| Method | Recon MSE ↓ | I2T R@1 ↑ | T2I R@1 ↑ | IN1K Zero-shot ↑ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Shared SAE | 0.090 | 6.1 | 3.4 | 15.7 |
| + Iso-Energy align | 0.091 | 4.7 | 2.7 | 13.0 |
| + Group-sparse | 0.105 | 7.1 | 4.3 | **26.6** |
| Modality-Specific SAEs | 0.089 | 0.0 | 0.0 | 0.1 |
| **+ Post-hoc Align (Ours)** | **0.089** | **16.0** | **11.4** | 25.1 |

- **Reconstruction:** tied-best MSE (0.089) with modality-specific SAEs — the post-hoc permutation provably does not change reconstruction. Group-sparse's heavy alignment penalty worsens MSE to 0.105.
- **Cross-modal retrieval:** +8.9 pts I2T R@1 and +7.1 pts T2I R@1 over the best baseline. Note modality-specific SAEs *without* alignment score ~0.0 (indices are unaligned), confirming the Hungarian step is essential.
- **Zero-shot classification:** the method is *not* best — group-sparse (26.6) edges it (25.1); the authors honestly call this "competitive."
- **Concept steering (Fig. 7):** highest mAP and MRR for retrieving target-concept images after steering source embeddings; qualitative examples steer more consistently toward the target (e.g., "elephant").
- **Monosemanticity (Fig. 8):** higher scores sustained across more latent coordinates for both modalities, indicating more semantically coherent latents.
- **Synthetic (Figs. 5–6):** shared SAE collapses geometrically-close-but-distinct directions (feature collapse rate spikes); modality-specific SAEs avoid collapse; post-hoc alignment matches baselines' alignment while keeping modality-specific feature recovery — directly visualizing the reconstruction/alignment trade-off the theory predicts.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The core move — explicitly questioning the ϕᵢ = ψᵢ assumption, naming the phenomenon, and *decoupling* alignment from reconstruction via a pure permutation — is genuinely fresh. Most prior VLM-SAE work either ignored or assumed-away cross-modal direction mismatch. Pairing the diagnostic with closed-form theory (Corollary 1, Theorem 1, Props. 1–2) is more rigorous than typical empirical-only SAE papers.
- **Rigor (Good, with caveats):** Theory + synthetic controls + real experiments is a solid package. Parameter-matching between shared and modality-specific SAEs (m vs m/2+m/2) is a fair design choice. Three seeds are reported. However, the *full method* is evaluated on a **single backbone (CLIP ViT-B/32)**; the heterogeneity *phenomenon* is checked on four VLMs but the proposed fix is not. Appendix E reportedly covers more sizes (not read here).
- **Reproducibility (Moderate concern):** The procedure is simple and well-specified (Hungarian on coactivation correlations), but **no code release is mentioned** in the body. SAE hyperparameters (K for Top-K, dictionary sizes) are referenced to Appendix D.4, not the main text.
- **Significance (Medium-High for the niche):** Likely to influence how the mech-interp community designs SAEs for multimodal models — the "align by permutation, don't force a shared dictionary" recipe is cheap and adoptable. The diagnostic (heterogeneity distance distribution) is a reusable tool.
- **Red flags / things to probe:**
  1. **No raw-CLIP retrieval baseline.** Absolute I2T R@1 of 16.0 on MS-COCO is low for CLIP ViT-B/32 (zero-shot CLIP typically scores materially higher), so it is unclear how much retrieval ability the SAE compression itself destroys; the comparison is only *across SAE methods*. A "no SAE / full embedding" row would clarify whether the method is good in absolute terms or merely least-bad among SAEs.
  2. **Single-backbone method evaluation** limits generalization claims.
  3. **Post-hoc alignment quality is only as good as the coactivation proxy**; cases where semantic correspondence ≠ high coactivation are not deeply analyzed.
  4. The headline +8.9 pts is partly amplified because auxiliary-loss baselines *hurt* retrieval — the contrast is favorable but somewhat self-reinforcing given the theory predicts exactly that.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated central idea; names and tests an assumption everyone had quietly been making.
- Theory genuinely predicts and explains the empirical trade-offs (Propositions 1–2 map directly onto Fig. 6's curves).
- Constructive: offers a simple, cheap fix (permutation alignment) rather than just a diagnosis.
- Honest reporting — explicitly notes it is "competitive" (not best) on zero-shot classification where group-sparse wins.
- Multi-axis evaluation: reconstruction, retrieval, steering, monosemanticity — not a single cherry-picked metric.

## Limitations

- Full method validated on one VLM (CLIP ViT-B/32); generalization to other architectures/scales rests on the appendix.
- No code/data release stated; some key hyperparameters deferred to the appendix.
- Absence of a no-SAE embedding baseline makes absolute (not relative) performance hard to judge.
- The coactivation-correlation proxy for semantic correspondence is heuristic; misalignments there would silently degrade post-hoc matching.
- The Hungarian alignment is a one-to-one permutation — concepts with no cross-modal counterpart, or many-to-one mappings, are not handled.
- Workshop-length scope: limited ablations on alignment robustness, dictionary size sweeps, and downstream task transfer.

## Relevance

对关注大模型可解释性、机制理解与视觉-语言模型内部表征的读者高度相关：直接触及 VLM 联合嵌入中特征的单义性与跨模态对齐这一核心问题，其"分模态训练 + 事后对齐"的思路对理解与控制多模态大模型内部结构具有直接的借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does cross-modal feature heterogeneity scale with model size, training-data composition, or the strength of the modality gap — is it an intrinsic property or a training artifact that can be reduced?
- Is the Hungarian permutation stable and transferable across VLM families, or must it be re-fit per backbone (and per dictionary expansion)?
- Would adding a no-SAE / raw-embedding retrieval baseline confirm that SAE compression is the main ceiling on absolute retrieval scores?
- Can heterogeneity be used as a *training signal* (e.g., a regularization term) to nudge VLMs toward more natively aligned joint embeddings in the first place?
- How does the method behave for concepts with no clean cross-modal counterpart, or many-to-one concept mappings the one-to-one permutation cannot express?
- Does reducing heterogeneity improve genuine downstream task performance (fine-tuning, generation), or is the benefit confined to interpretability/steering?
