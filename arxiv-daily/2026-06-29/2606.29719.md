> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 EPC 诊断框架（含 MPCI 指数、评估器耦合矩阵 Γ(J) 与 Jensen-Shannon 散度），通过对八个实验条件（N=122）的审计揭示专有 LLM 评估器存在版本依赖性不稳定——GPT-4o 在五月到六月（约 4 周）的静默更新使耦合系数从 1.176 跌至 0.0，直接反转了原研究结论，证明单一时间点的评估器研究本质上不可靠。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces EPC (Evaluator Preference Collapse) — a diagnostic framework combining MPCI, an evaluator-indexed coupling matrix Γ(J), and Jensen-Shannon divergence — and audits it across 8 conditions (N=122). It documents a silent GPT-4o API update between May and June 2026 that collapsed coupling from 1.176 to 0.0 (N=8, Cohen's d=4.37, p<0.0001), inverting the study's own conclusion and proving that single-snapshot evaluator studies are inherently fragile.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 EPC 诊断框架**：整合多模态偏好坍塌指数（MPCI = ½(PCI_text + PCI_visual) + CPCI）、评估器索引耦合矩阵 Γ(J) ∈ ℝ²ˣ² 与 Jensen-Shannon 散度（JSD），首次为"评估器驱动的偏好动力学"提供可量化、可证伪的标准化指标。
- **大规模多评估器审计**：在 8 个实验条件下完成 122 次独立重复（N=112 主实验 + N=10 消融），覆盖 GPT-4o（五月/六月两批）、GPT-4o-mini Vision、Qwen3.7-plus、qwen-plus(DashScope)、DeepSeek 自评估。
- **记录版本漂移实证案例**：GPT-4o 在 2026 年 5 月到 6 月间（约 4 周）的静默更新，使耦合系数从 1.176 完全坍塌至 0.0（N=8 重跑），效应量 Cohen's d=4.37，permutation p<0.0001。
- **揭示耦合模式的二分性**：4 个条件强耦合（N=36，γ 最高 1.18），4 个条件坍塌至近零（N=76）；自评估几乎完全坍塌（97% 零值，JSD=0.003）。
- **将 N 敏感性识别为通用测量现象**：小 N（8–10）批次出现强耦合、大 N（30）批次坍塌的模式，在另一个完全不同的校准测量系统中（ECE 排名随 N 翻转）也被观察到，提示样本量依赖性可能是复杂评估管线的普遍属性。
- **轻量级实用诊断与开源**：EPC 仅需约 100 次调用、总计约 $44 即可运行；全部代码与 N=122 次 JSON 数据随论文发布。

## Key Contributions

- **EPC diagnostic framework**: Provides the first standardized, falsifiable metric suite (MPCI, Γ(J), JSD) for measuring evaluator-driven preference dynamics in closed-loop LLM agents, filling a gap left by LLM-as-judge, reward-overoptimization, and sycophancy literatures that focus on single-round reliability.
- **Multi-evaluator audit at scale**: 122 unique repetitions across 8 conditions spanning two evaluator families (OpenAI GPT-4o, Alibaba Qwen) plus DeepSeek self-evaluation, with full inventory and per-condition data files disclosed.
- **Documented version-drift case study**: A silent GPT-4o update (same pinned `gpt-4o-2024-08-06` endpoint, verified protocol/endpoint identity) collapsed coupling from 1.176 to 0.0 within ~4 weeks (N=8; Cohen's d=4.37; permutation p<0.0001; >99.9% power), inverting the study's own conclusion.
- **Bimodal coupling pattern & N-sensitivity**: Strong coupling in 4 conditions (N=36) vs. near-zero collapse in 4 (N=76); self-evaluation collapses (97% zeros, JSD=0.003). The small-N/strong vs. large-N/collapse asymmetry recurs in an unrelated calibration measurement system, suggesting a general phenomenon.
- **Methodological transparency**: Manipulation check on strategies (within-strategy Jaccard 0.066 ≈ cross-strategy 0.061), output-format confound decomposed at aggregate (ρ=0.89) vs. per-instance (ρ=0.219, p=0.093) level, calibration diagnostics (ECE=0.31, Brier=0.27) showing self-eval preferences do not track accuracy.
- **Open, low-cost toolkit**: ~26,000 API calls, ~$44 total; code and all run JSONs released for reproducibility.

## Methodology

**Setting — Test-Time Reinforcement Learning (TTRL).** The agent adapts a strategy distribution over S (|S|=11 strategies: 8 text + 3 visual) via a parameter-free stochastic bandit. At round t, a strategy s_t ~ w(t) is sampled. A fixed executor (DeepSeek-chat, text-only, T=0.7) generates responses under s_t and a fixed baseline s_0 (step_by_step). The evaluator J performs pairwise comparison r_t ∈ {0,1}. Weights update via asymmetric multiplicative reweighting (α_win=0.08, α_lose=0.04, 0.001 floor); symmetric variant (α=0.06) studied as ablation.

**Metrics (the EPC framework).**
- **PCI** = σ(w)/μ(w), bounded in [0, √10] for |S|=11 (preference concentration).
- **MPCI** = ½(PCI_text + PCI_visual) + CPCI, where CPCI = ½‖w_T − w_V‖₁ (cross-modal coupling).
- **Four-phase isolation**: (1) Pure Text → w_T; (2) Pure Visual → w_V; (3) Coupling T→V (start w_T, train visual); (4) Coupling V→T (start w_V, train text).
- **Coupling coefficient** γ_{A→B} = ‖w_{A→B} − w_B‖₂ / ‖w_B‖₂; γ≈0 minimal, γ≳0.5 substantial; unbounded — JSD serves as the bounded primary metric.
- **Coupling matrix** Γ(J) ∈ ℝ²ˣ² (diagonal 1, off-diagonal γ), indexed by evaluator.
- **JSD** (base e, ∈[0, ln2]) with 10,000-resample bootstrap CIs.
- **ECE & Brier** diagnose whether concentrated preferences are merited (track accuracy) or arbitrary.

**Experimental setup.** Executor: DeepSeek-chat. Evaluators (May–June 2026): GPT-4o (two independent May/June batches via api2d proxy), GPT-4o-mini Vision (real JPEGs), Qwen3.7-plus (Bailian), qwen-plus (DashScope), DeepSeek-chat self-evaluation. Tasks: 8 text + 8 text-proxied visual. Protocol: Phase 1 (16 rounds), Phase 2 (4×30 rounds), Phase 3 (R=30, per-condition N as in Table). Unique N=122 (112 main + 10 no-s₀ ablation).

**Audit structure.** Results are organized as an audit applying EPC per condition with all outcomes reported (no cherry-picking); operational definitions: strong coupling γ̄>0.5; collapse >50% reps γ=0; symmetric |Δγ|<0.15, p>0.1.

## Results & Highlights

- **Coupling range & instability**: Per-condition γ_{T→V} means span 0.000–1.176 (CV ≈ 0.9, n=8); zero-coupling rate 0–100%. Instability is operationalized as non-overlapping 95% bootstrap CIs.
- **Headline — GPT-4o May→June drift (Table 1)**: May N=8: γ̄_{T→V}=1.176, γ̄_{V→T}=1.089. June N=8: 0.000 (all 8 reps). Δγ̄=1.176 (95% CI [0.938, 1.425], permutation p<0.0001, Cohen's d=4.37, >99.9% power at N=8). Identical pinned endpoint `gpt-4o-2024-08-06` — only plausible explanation is a silent model update.
- **Cross-model strong coupling (Table 2)**: GPT-4o-mini Vision (N=10, γ=1.145/0.937), Qwen3.7-plus (N=8, γ=1.059/1.008), DashScope 30r (N=10, γ=1.082/1.069) — all 0% zero-coupling.
- **Collapse conditions (Table 3)**: GPT-4o June (100% zero), Qwen/DashScope N=30 (83% zero, γ=0.187), GPT-4o symmetric LR (100% zero), DeepSeek self-eval N=30 (97% zero, JSD=0.003).
- **γ is an excellent JSD proxy**: Pearson r=0.969 (N=152, p<10⁻⁴) across conditions with both metrics.
- **Symmetric-LR zero result re-attributed**: A version-locked re-run on June 27, 2026 (DMXAPI, N=5 each) showed BOTH asymmetric (γ=1.254/0.948) and symmetric (γ=1.007/1.114) LR produce strong coupling with 0/5 zeros — the earlier symmetric-LR collapse was a version-drift artifact, not an LR property.
- **Output-format confound**: Aggregate per-strategy Spearman ρ≈0.89 (n=6, driven by step_by_step being both longest at 1,428 chars and highest-weighted at 48.4%) but per-instance ρ=0.219 (n=60, p=0.093) — not significant at the appropriate unit.
- **Self-eval calibration**: DeepSeek self-eval PCI=0.74 but ECE=0.31, Brier=0.27, accuracy correlation r=−0.038 (p=0.868) — concentrated preferences do not track correctness; the 97% zero-coupling may be a floor effect (evaluator lacks discriminative capacity).
- **Manipulation check**: within-strategy Jaccard (0.066) ≈ cross-strategy (0.061), so observed collapse reflects evaluator preference for output format/style rather than deep reasoning-mode differentiation.
- **Cost**: ~26,000 API calls, ~$44 total (Phase 3 ~$31 dominates).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — High.** The reframing of evaluator drift as a *measurable, version-conditional phenomenon* with a reusable metric suite (vs. treating it as nuisance noise) is distinctive. The "diagnostic instrument detecting its own instability" framing is conceptually elegant. However, the underlying TTRL bandit and JSD are standard; novelty lies in their composition and the audit framing.
- **Rigor — Moderate-to-High.** Strengths: all 122 repetitions reported, ablations included, confounds decomposed, bootstrap CIs, permutation tests, version-locked re-runs that re-attribute the symmetric-LR result. Weaknesses: many per-cell N's are small (N=8–10 for the headline conditions); the headline drift rests on a single May vs. June comparison; cross-evaluator comparisons confound evaluator identity with input modality and evaluation relation (acknowledged in Table 5).
- **Reproducibility — Mixed.** Open code/data and pinned version strings help, but the central phenomenon (proprietary silent updates) is *inherently* irreproducible by definition — the May GPT-4o no longer exists. The authors are honest that "exact numerical reproduction is not guaranteed." EPC itself is cheap (~$44) and the methodology is replicable on any snapshot.
- **Significance — High for the LLM-as-judge community.** The warning that single-snapshot evaluator studies have a "shelf life measured in weeks" is broadly actionable and aligns with growing concerns about proprietary-model drift.
- **Red flags.**
  1. **Coupling coefficients exceed 1.0** (up to 1.741 per-seed). The metric γ = ‖w_{A→B} − w_B‖₂/‖w_B‖₂ is unbounded by construction; values >1 mean the post-transfer weight vector is more than 100% away from the target baseline in L2 — geometrically interpretable but unusual and not normalized, complicating cross-study comparison.
  2. **Single author, preprint** (no peer review yet); some references appear incomplete/formatted loosely.
  3. **Headline effect (N=8)** — though the authors argue (correctly) that d=4.37 yields >99.9% power, the *existence* of the drift rests on one before/after window.
  4. **Self-attribution of the symmetric-LR zero** to version drift rests on a small N=5 version-locked re-run.
  5. **Proxy reliance**: GPT-4o accessed via api2d proxy (not official API) for the drift case; official-API replication is listed as future work, weakening attribution.
  6. **Strategy construct validity**: authors themselves show strategies are not cognitively distinct (Jaccard manipulation check), so "preference collapse" may be format-level rather than reasoning-level.

## Strengths

- Directly addresses a timely, under-appreciated threat to validity in LLM evaluation research.
- Exceptionally transparent: reports all repetitions (including collapsed/embarrassing ones), provides data filenames, calibration and confound analyses.
- Provides a reusable, cheap diagnostic toolkit (EPC) rather than only a critique.
- The version-locked re-run that re-attributes the symmetric-LR result demonstrates scientific self-correction.
- Open data/code release supports community verification and extension.
- Practical, actionable recommendations (report version dates, ≥2 evaluator families, monthly re-baselining).

## Limitations

- Small per-cell sample sizes for key conditions (N=8–10); high CV (≈0.9) indicates unstable measurements.
- Proprietary model internals are opaque; root-cause attribution for drift is impossible (only plausible-inference).
- Cross-evaluator comparison confounds evaluator identity, input modality, and evaluation relation (Table 5).
- Self-evaluation collapse may be a floor effect (ECE=0.31 shows evaluator lacks discriminative capacity), not genuine stability.
- Bounded to text-proxied visual tasks (real-image only one evaluator, N=10), small task set (8+8), single executor (DeepSeek-chat), May–June 2026 window.
- γ is unbounded (>1.0 values); MPCI uses ad-hoc equal weighting; alternative divergences (Hellinger, EMD) unexplored.
- ECE/Brier defined but not exercised on a held-out benchmark with ground truth (listed as future work).
- GPT-4o drift measured via api2d proxy, not official OpenAI API.
- Strategies lack independent cognitive validation; collapse may reflect format-level preference.
- Single-author preprint without peer review.

## Relevance

与 LLM 评估方法论、偏好学习与自适应用户智能体研究高度相关，为使用专有模型作为评估器的研究提供了稳定性诊断工具，并提示评估器版本依赖性对结论可复现性的影响——对任何采用 LLM-as-judge 闭环反馈的智能体系统都具有直接的实践指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does EPC generalize to fully open-weight evaluators (e.g., Llama-3.1-70B via vLLM) whose versions are permanently reproducible — does the version-conditional instability disappear?
- Can the May→June GPT-4o drift be corroborated via the official OpenAI API (not a proxy) and at larger N to solidify attribution?
- Is γ > 1.0 an artifact of the L2 normalization, and should MPCI/γ be reformulated with a bounded divergence (Hellinger, EMD) for cross-study comparability?
- Does the self-evaluation collapse persist when task difficulty is raised above floor and the evaluator has genuine discriminative capacity?
- Is sample-size-dependent measurement instability (N-sensitivity) truly a general property of complex evaluation pipelines, as the cross-domain ECE anecdote suggests — and can it be formalized?
- What is the typical half-life of evaluator validity across vendors and update cadences, and can EPC be deployed as a continuous-monitoring dashboard in production?
- Does length-normalization (fixed 500-token budget) eliminate the format confound and change the coupling pattern?
