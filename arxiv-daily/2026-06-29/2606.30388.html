A Stochastic–Geometric Theory of Scaling Laws in Grokking Róisín Luo et al. 2026-06-29 · arXiv 2606.30388
▍ Key Finding

Adam's stochastic dynamics carve a shell–core topology in parameter space — and stopping-time analysis yields closed-form grokking scaling laws.

A first-principles theory: random-init solutions sit on a thin outer hyperspherical shell, a memorization shell sits inside it, and a compact generalization core at the center. The transition between them is a Dirichlet exit / mean first-passage problem.

grokking scaling-laws optimization-dynamics SDE · Itô generalization-theory
▍ Key Metric — recovered law
τ ∝ 1/(ηλ)

Transition time τM→G scales inversely with learning rate × weight-decay — derived from first principles, machine-checked in Julia, and reproducing Liu et al. 2022.

▍ Why This Matters

Derives grokking's scaling laws from first principles — turning an empirical curiosity into a predictable, optimizer-driven geometric phenomenon.

▍ Critical Edge

Validated only on tiny structured tasks (S5, Z127) with a 2-layer MLP — no real data, no transformers; and the scaling-law constants are empirically fitted, not a-priori.

Methodology

The theory chains five analytical stages, all grounded in Adam's stochastic dynamics with coupled weight decay. Initialization first concentrates the parameter norm on a thin annulus (radius O(k), thickness O(√(k/p̃))) — the outer shell.

  1. Adam SDE limit (Lemma 1). In the small-η / large-b regime, discrete Adam over the joint state (θ, m, v) converges to an Itô SDE with explicit drift and diffusion — keeping stochasticity rather than a first-order approximation.
  2. Memorization radius (Thm 1). The first-hitting time of the loss boundary defines ρ²M as a Dirichlet exit problem; a perturbation expansion in η/b gives a predicted U-shaped dependence on η/b.
  3. Late-stage reduction (Obs 1–2, Lemma 2). Slow-manifold collapse of (m,v) plus isotropic late-stage covariance collapse the joint SDE to a 1-D radial SDE for r²=‖θ‖².
  4. Generalization radius & transition (Thms 2–3). Linearizing around the regularized minimizer yields ρ²G; the M→G hop is a mean first-passage problem on the annulus, solved in closed form.

Validation is triple: direct measurement on S5 / Z127, cross-checks against published laws, and symbolic machine-checking of every closed-form step in a Julia algebra system.

Strengths

  • Unified first-principles explanation linking optimization dynamics, parameter-space geometry, and stopping-time probability.
  • Derives closed-form scaling laws for η, b, λ — including a previously-unknown U-shaped ρM(η/b).
  • Strong verification: symbolic machine-checking + experiments + literature cross-validation.
  • Recovers Goldilocks-zone and τ ∝ 1/(ηλ) as special cases — external credibility.

Limitations

  • Tiny structured tasks (S5, Z127) + 2-layer MLP only — no real data, no transformers, no scale.
  • Constants (cM, cG, s̄…) are task-determined and empirically fit, not a-priori predictive.
  • Restricted to small-η / large-b and coupled weight decay — transfer to AdamW / SGD unproven.
  • Rests on two empirical late-stage reductions (slow-manifold, isotropic covariance) — observations, not theorems.
  • Shell–core topology evidenced only via 2-D MDS projection — indirect.

Open Questions

  1. Do the laws (especially U-shaped ρM) survive on real datasets, transformers, or larger models?
  2. How does the topology change under decoupled weight decay (AdamW) or SGD-with-momentum — what practitioners actually use?
  3. Can the framework predict grokking's sharpness, not just its timing?
  4. Can the two late-stage reductions be promoted from observations to theorems?
  5. Does the reported 16-bit instability imply a fundamental precision-sensitivity the SDE should capture?
  6. Can the fitted constants be predicted from task/landscape structure — and turned into a recipe to accelerate or avoid grokking?
