Key finding A 35B MoE agent matches trillion-parameter models on long-horizon agentic tasks — by scaling the horizon, not the parameters.

A deployable 35B agent reaches 1T-level long-horizon performance — frontier agentic capability at a fraction of the inference cost.

cost-sensitive deployment · distillation recipe

⚖ Critical edge

01

Methodology

The recipe trades raw scale for process-level supervision and cross-domain teacher fusion.

Long-horizon skill is decomposed into five atomic abilities — information acquisition, tool calling, executable iteration, evidence verification, constraint tracking. These are recorded in a typed Knowledge-Action Graph G = (C, A, O, V) that stores not the final answer but the process by which it was acquired, tested, and verified — retaining both successes and failures for cross-step credit assignment.

A proposer–solver–verifier self-play loop expands each domain sub-graph into verifiable, shortcut-free, ~45K-token trajectories. Six heterogeneous domains are then fused into one deployable student via a three-stage pipeline (right).

The distillation stage introduces Salient Vocabulary Alignment: a truncated reverse-KL computed only on each teacher’s top-k local vocabulary, with a domain-normalized objective (average within active domains, then across) so that long trajectories and frequent domains don’t dominate the gradient.

Three-stage recipe

STAGE 1
Full-domain SFT
Aligns base agent; regresses on some IF / HLE tasks (pattern conflict).
→
STAGE 2
Domain teachers
Per-domain SFT and/or RL specialists (search, science, IF, tool, …).
→
STAGE 3
Multi-teacher OPD + SVA
Hard-routed distillation fuses six domains into one student; recovers SFT losses.

OPD = on-policy distillation · SVA = salient vocabulary alignment · frozen-rollout student, trainable student-token KL only.

03

Strengths & Limitations

+ Strengths

  • Deployable 35B footprint reaching 1T-level long-horizon performance — strong practical value.
  • KAG captures process-level (incl. failure) supervision that final-answer-only SFT cannot.
  • SVA + domain-normalized OPD is a clean, theoretically-grounded fix for sampled-token instability and cross-domain gradient imbalance.
  • Transparent ablations: reports where SFT regresses and how OPD recovers it; openly admits SciCode/MLE gaps.
  • Real long-horizon case studies (12-h autonomous ML optimization; end-to-end Cyclone Nargis reconstruction) embody the thesis.

− Limitations

  • No released weights/data/config in the read portion — reproducibility weak; pipeline is infrastructure-heavy.
  • Asymmetric eval: Agents-A1 with tools vs tool-free frontier baselines on several science benchmarks.
  • Still materially behind 1T models on long-horizon engineering tasks (SciCode, MLE-Bench).
  • OPD student can lag its own specialist teachers — fusion carries a per-domain cost.
  • τ2-Bench numbers are environment/version-sensitive and flagged as inconsistent across community reports.
04

Open questions

?How much gap-closing comes from the KAG / process supervision vs SVA distillation itself? A clean isolation ablation would clarify attribution.
?Do the “leading” science results hold once baselines get the same tool-augmented protocol, removing the asymmetric condition?
?What is the latency/cost of 45K-token trajectories, and how does ROI actually compare to a 1T model at the same task budget?
?How sensitive is OPD convergence to domain-label noise and to the top-k choice in SVA coverage?
?Can the KAG infrastructure generalize beyond six domains — e.g. to software-engineering or real-time web-action agents?
?Given the (future-dated) baseline IDs, how do results hold against actual current frontier models re-measured under one harness?
