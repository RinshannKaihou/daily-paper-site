# MLLM-as-a-Judge Exhibits Model Preference Bias

> **一句话总结：** 提出Philautia-Eval框架量化MLLM-as-a-Judge中的模型偏好偏见，基于1.29M个描述-评分对发现12个代表性MLLM普遍存在自偏好偏见和同族模型间的互偏好偏见，并提出简单集成方法Pomms有效缓解偏见。

> **TL;DR:** Philautia-Eval reveals that MLLM-as-a-Judge methods exhibit self-preference bias and within-family mutual preference bias across 12 MLLMs (1.29M caption-score pairs), with a proposed ensemble (Pomms) effectively mitigating the bias.

## 核心贡献

- 提出Philautia-Eval框架，通过将偏好倾向与生成质量差异分离来量化模型特定偏好偏见
- 基于1.29M个描述-评分对，发现代表性MLLM普遍存在自偏好偏见（高估自身生成的内容）
- 发现同族模型间的互偏好偏见，可能源于共享连接器和重叠的指令微调资源
- 提出Pomms集成方法有效缓解模型特定偏好偏见

## Key Contributions

- Philautia-Eval: framework to quantify model-specific preference bias by disentangling preference from quality
- Self-preference bias discovered across 12 representative MLLMs using 1.29M caption-score pairs
- Mutual preference bias within model families (potentially from shared connectors/tuning data)
- Pomms: simple ensemble effectively mitigating model-specific bias

## Methodology

The authors collect 1.29M caption-score pairs from 12 MLLMs acting as both generators and judges. Philautia-Eval quantifies preference bias by separating genuine quality differences from systematic scoring patterns favoring specific models. The analysis controls for actual generation quality to isolate bias.

## Results & Highlights

- Representative MLLMs systematically over-score their own generated captions
- Within-family mutual preference bias (e.g., models from the same organization favor each other)
- Pomms ensemble effectively mitigates bias while maintaining performance

## Authors & Credibility

Authors from Keio University, Japan. Komei Sugiura has prior publications on multimodal AI and evaluation. Keio is a respected Japanese university but not a top-tier ML research institution globally. The large-scale evaluation (1.29M pairs) demonstrates significant computational effort.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The self-preference bias finding is not entirely surprising (similar biases known for text LLMs), but the systematic quantification across 12 MLLMs with quality-controlled analysis is valuable.
- **Experimental rigor:** Strong — 1.29M pairs across 12 models is a substantial evaluation. The quality-disentangling methodology is sound.
- **Reproducibility:** Project page available. Methodology is well-described.
- **Significance:** Important for anyone using MLLM-as-a-Judge for evaluation. The bias could significantly distort benchmark rankings.

## Strengths

- Large-scale systematic evaluation across 12 MLLMs
- Quality-controlled bias analysis (not just raw score differences)
- Practical mitigation method (Pomms ensemble)
- Clear practical implications for evaluation methodology

## Limitations

- Focused on image captioning only — may not generalize to other MLLM tasks
- Self-preference bias is somewhat expected given training data overlap
- Pomms ensemble increases evaluation cost proportionally
