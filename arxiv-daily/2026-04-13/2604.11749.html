HistLens: Mapping Idea Change across Concepts and Corpora

一句话总结： 提出HistLens框架，利用SAE将概念表征分解为可解释特征，追踪其跨时间和跨语料的激活动态变化，实现了多概念多语料的概念史分析。
TL;DR: HistLens is an SAE-based framework that decomposes concept representations into interpretable features and tracks their activation dynamics over time and across corpora, enabling comparable multi-concept conceptual-history analysis in a shared coordinate system.

核心贡献

  • 提出基于SAE的多概念多语料概念史分析统一框架
  • 将概念表征分解为可解释特征并追踪其激活动态
  • 在共享坐标系中实现跨源可比较的概念轨迹

Key Contributions

  • Unified SAE-based framework for multi-concept, multi-corpus conceptual-history analysis
  • Decomposes concept representations into interpretable features with tracked activation dynamics
  • Comparable conceptual trajectories in a shared coordinate system across heterogeneous sources

Methodology

Uses Sparse Autoencoders to decompose concept representations from language models into interpretable features. Tracks feature activation patterns over time across different corpora, creating comparable trajectories in a shared feature space.

Results & Highlights

  • Supports cross-source comparison of conceptual change
  • Applied to long-span press corpora
  • Provides computational granularity beyond surface lexical evidence

Authors & Credibility

Authors from Tsinghua University, Nanjing University, and Peking University. Zhifang Sui is a well-established NLP researcher at Peking University. Tsinghua/PKU are top Chinese universities with strong NLP groups.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Applying SAEs to conceptual history analysis is creative and relatively novel, bridging computational linguistics and digital humanities.
  • Experimental rigor: Application to long-span press corpora is promising, but evaluation of historical interpretability is inherently subjective.
  • Significance: Interesting interdisciplinary contribution but limited direct relevance to core ML reliability questions.

Strengths

  • Creative application of SAE interpretability to digital humanities
  • Multi-concept, multi-corpus comparison capability

Limitations

  • Evaluation of historical claims is inherently subjective
  • Relies on SAE decomposition quality which varies
