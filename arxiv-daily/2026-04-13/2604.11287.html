Consistency of AI-Generated Exercise Prescriptions: A Repeated Generation Study

一句话总结： 使用Gemini 2.5 Flash在6个临床场景下重复生成120次运动处方，发现语义一致性较高（余弦相似度0.879-0.939），但关键定量组件（特别是运动强度）存在显著变异性，10-25%的阻力训练输出包含不可分类的强度表达。
TL;DR: Repeated generation study (n=120) of LLM exercise prescriptions finds high semantic consistency (cosine similarity 0.879-0.939) but significant variability in key quantitative components like exercise intensity, with 10-25% of resistance training outputs containing unclassifiable intensity expressions.

核心贡献

  • 首次系统评估LLM生成运动处方在相同条件下的重复生成一致性
  • 在三个维度评估一致性：语义一致性（SBERT余弦相似度）、结构一致性（FITT原则的AI评判）、安全表达一致性
  • 发现语义层面一致但定量组件（特别是运动强度）变异显著，揭示了LLM生成数值内容的不稳定性

Key Contributions

  • First systematic evaluation of intra-model consistency in LLM-generated exercise prescriptions
  • Three-dimensional consistency assessment: semantic (SBERT), structural (FITT), safety
  • Reveals high semantic consistency but significant quantitative variability in LLM outputs

Methodology

Six clinical scenarios used to generate exercise prescriptions using Gemini 2.5 Flash. For each scenario, 20 outputs generated under identical conditions (total n=120). Consistency assessed via: (1) SBERT cosine similarity, (2) FITT-based structural analysis via AI-as-judge, (3) safety expression consistency.

Results & Highlights

  • Semantic similarity: 0.879-0.939 across scenarios
  • Clinical cases showed higher consistency than high-function scenarios
  • Frequency showed consistent patterns; intensity showed high variability
  • 10-25% of resistance training outputs had unclassifiable intensity expressions
  • Safety expressions included in 100% of outputs but quantity varied significantly (H=86.18, p<0.001)

Authors & Credibility

Single author: Kihyuk Lee, PhD, from Seoul National University Bundang Hospital. This is a medical/clinical informatics researcher evaluating LLM outputs in a healthcare context. The study design is appropriate for a clinical validation study.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The specific application (exercise prescriptions) is narrow, but the methodology for evaluating output consistency is well-designed and generalizable.
  • Experimental rigor: Appropriate sample size for a consistency study. Three-dimensional evaluation is thorough.
  • Significance: The finding of quantitative variability despite semantic consistency is important for LLM evaluation — it shows that superficial consistency metrics can miss important numerical instabilities.

Strengths

  • Well-designed three-dimensional consistency evaluation
  • Clinically grounded methodology
  • Important finding: semantic consistency masks quantitative instability

Limitations

  • Single model (Gemini 2.5 Flash) — generalization unclear
  • Small scale (6 scenarios, 120 outputs)
  • Domain-specific (exercise prescriptions)
