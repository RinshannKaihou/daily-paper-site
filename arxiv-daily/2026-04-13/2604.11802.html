Yuto Harada, Hiro Taiyo Hamada

Psychological Concept Neurons: Can Neural Control Bias Probing and Shift Generation?

一句话总结： 本文通过探测（probing）和神经元级干预（neuron-level intervention）方法，研究了LLM中大五人格特质（Big Five personality traits）的内部表征定位与因果可控性，发现虽然表征层面的干预可以有效操纵潜在表示（probe readouts成功率超过0.8），但对生成行为的控制显著更弱且伴随跨特质溢出效应，揭示了LLM中"表征控制"与"行为控制"之间的鸿沟。
TL;DR: This paper probes and intervenes on concept-selective neurons for Big Five personality traits in LLMs, finding that neuron-level steering can reliably shift latent representations (targeted success rates >0.8) but has much weaker and noisier effects on actual label generation, revealing a gap between representational and behavioral control.

核心贡献

  • 首次系统性地将大五人格心理学概念定位到LLM的特定神经元上，并通过因果干预验证这些神经元的可控性
  • 发现大五人格信息在模型浅层即可被解码，而概念选择性神经元集中在中层，且不同人格维度之间的神经元重叠有限
  • 揭示了LLM中"表征控制"与"行为控制"之间的关键鸿沟：干预概念选择性神经元可以有效操纵探针读数，但对最终生成行为的影响较弱且存在跨特质溢出

Key Contributions

  • Systematically localizes Big Five personality concept neurons in LLMs and validates their causal controllability through activation interventions (enhancement and suppression)
  • Reveals that Big Five information is decodable from early layers and persists through final layers, while concept-selective neurons concentrate in mid-layers with limited cross-trait overlap
  • Identifies a critical gap between representational control (probe readout success rates >0.8) and behavioral control (weaker, concept-dependent effects with cross-trait spillover at the generation level)

Methodology

The authors use questionnaire-operationalized Big Five personality concepts to construct probing datasets. They train linear probes across all layers of LLMs to identify where personality information emerges. Concept-selective neurons are identified by comparing activation distributions between high and low trait-score inputs. Activation-level interventions (enhancement and suppression) are then applied to these neurons to test whether latent representations and label generation can be causally steered in intended directions.

Results & Highlights

  • Big Five information becomes rapidly decodable in early layers and remains detectable through final layers
  • Concept-selective neurons are most prevalent in mid-layers and exhibit limited overlap across personality domains
  • Interventions on concept-selective neurons shift probe readouts toward targeted concepts with targeted success rates exceeding 0.8 for some traits (e.g., Agreeableness and Conscientiousness)
  • At the label-generation level, the same interventions often bias generated label distributions in intended directions, but effects are weaker, more concept-dependent, and accompanied by cross-trait spillover
  • Even interventions on a large fraction of concept-selective neurons fail to achieve comparable control over generated labels versus latent representations

Critical Assessment

The paper tackles an important and novel question: whether psychological personality constructs can be localized to specific neurons and causally controlled in LLMs. The experimental design is methodical, progressing from probing to neuron identification to intervention at both representation and generation levels. However, the gap between representational and behavioral control, while an important finding, is somewhat expected given the distributed nature of generation processes. The paper could be strengthened by investigating whether alternative intervention strategies (e.g., activation steering vectors rather than individual neuron manipulation) might close this gap, and by testing on a broader range of LLM architectures beyond the specific model studied.

Strengths

  • Clean experimental pipeline: probing -> neuron identification -> causal intervention at two levels
  • Novel application of mechanistic interpretability to psychological constructs
  • Honest reporting of the representational vs. behavioral control gap rather than overclaiming
  • Concept-selective neurons are analyzed for cross-trait overlap, adding nuance to the localization claims

Limitations

  • Results are limited to one or few model architectures; generalization is unclear
  • The "questionnaire-operationalized" Big Five may not fully capture the complexity of personality constructs
  • The paper does not explore alternative intervention methods that might bridge the representation-behavior gap
  • Cross-trait spillover during generation-level interventions is reported but not deeply analyzed mechanistically

Relevance

本文与LLM隐藏状态和可解释性研究高度相关。它直接涉及（1）通过线性探针分析隐藏状态中编码的概念信息，（2）识别与特定语义概念（人格特质）对应的个体神经元，以及（3）通过激活干预检验这些神经元级表征与模型生成行为之间的因果关系。这项工作将机械可解释性（mechanistic interpretability）从事实知识和语言特征扩展到了心理学维度，并与关于LLM中概念神经元、激活引导和表征工程的研究建立了联系。所揭示的"表征控制"与"行为控制"之间的鸿沟，对于任何试图通过操纵内部表征来控制LLM输出的尝试都具有深远的影响。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Would activation steering vectors (rather than individual neuron interventions) close the gap between representational and behavioral control for personality traits?
  • Do the identified concept-selective neurons transfer across model architectures and scales?
  • How stable are these personality concept neurons under fine-tuning or RLHF?
  • Can the cross-trait spillover during generation-level interventions be mitigated through more targeted intervention strategies?
