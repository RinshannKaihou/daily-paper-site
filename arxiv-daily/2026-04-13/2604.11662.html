Hidden Failures in Robustness: Why Supervised Uncertainty Quantification Needs Better Evaluation

一句话总结： 本文系统评估了基于LLM隐藏状态的监督式不确定性量化探针，训练了2000+个探针发现现有方法在分布外泛化能力极差（尤其在长文本生成中退化至随机水平），而中间层表征和跨token聚合策略显著优于最终层和单token特征。
TL;DR: A systematic study of 2,000+ supervised uncertainty probes reveals poor OOD robustness (especially for long-form generation), with middle-layer representations and token aggregation proving more robust than final-layer/single-token alternatives.

核心贡献

  • 对监督式不确定性量化方法进行了迄今为止最系统的评估，训练超过2000个探针，系统变化表征层、特征类型和token聚合策略
  • 发现现有探针方法在分布外（OOD）场景下鲁棒性极差，特别是长文本生成任务中退化至接近随机水平
  • 揭示探针鲁棒性的关键驱动因素不是探针架构，而是探针输入：中间层表征比最终层更可靠，跨token聚合比单token特征更鲁棒
  • 提出混合回退策略（Hybrid Back-Off, HBO）作为提高鲁棒性的示例方案

Key Contributions

  • Most comprehensive systematic evaluation of supervised UQ probes to date (2,000+ probes across models, tasks, and OOD settings)
  • Reveals hidden failures: current methods are not robust OOD, degrading to near chance-level for long-form generation
  • Identifies that probe robustness is driven by input choice (layer, aggregation) rather than architecture, with middle-layer + token-aggregation being most robust
  • Surveys prior work and finds that robustness claims often have limited or inconsistent supporting evidence
  • Proposes Hybrid Back-Off (HBO) strategy as an illustrative robustness improvement

Methodology

The authors train over 2,000 supervised probes across multiple LLMs, tasks, and distribution shift settings while systematically varying three key design dimensions: representation layer (early, middle, final), feature type (hidden states, attention weights), and token aggregation strategy (single-token, mean pooling, last token). They evaluate under both in-distribution and progressively challenging OOD settings, enabling direct comparison of how different configurations behave under distribution shift.

Results & Highlights

  • Probe performance degrades severely under OOD conditions, with long-form generation probes collapsing to near chance-level
  • Middle-layer representations generalize more reliably than final-layer hidden states
  • Aggregating across response tokens is consistently more robust than single-token features
  • These differences are often invisible in-distribution but become critical under distribution shift
  • Prior OOD evaluation claims are often not well-supported by experimental setups

Authors & Credibility

Strong author team: Joe Stacey and Nafise Sadat Moosavi from University of Sheffield; Hadas Orgad from Kempner Institute at Harvard University; Kentaro Inui from MBZUAI/Tohoku University/RIKEN; Benjamin Heinzerling from RIKEN. This is a well-established NLP research group with prior publications on probing and model internals. Orgad's prior work on representation editing and probing at ACL/EMNLP venues gives credibility to the probing methodology.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The systematic evaluation approach is valuable, though individual findings (middle layers are better, aggregation helps) have been observed piecemeal in prior work. The key contribution is the unified evaluation that reveals how bad things actually are OOD.
  • Experimental rigor: Excellent — 2,000+ probes, multiple models, multiple OOD settings, systematic ablation of design choices. The survey of prior evaluation gaps is thorough.
  • Reproducibility: High — detailed experimental setup, clear evaluation protocols. Code availability not mentioned but methodology is well-specified.
  • Significance: Important negative result that the community needs to hear. The finding that many published robustness claims are not well-supported is a significant contribution to evaluation methodology.

Strengths

  • Comprehensive and systematic evaluation scope
  • Important negative results that challenge existing assumptions
  • Clear, actionable recommendations for probe design (middle layer + token aggregation)
  • Thorough survey exposing gaps in prior OOD evaluations

Limitations

  • Does not propose a fundamentally new method — the HBO strategy is a simple combination of existing approaches
  • Focuses primarily on correctness-based uncertainty; does not address other uncertainty dimensions (e.g., epistemic vs. aleatoric)
  • Limited to supervised probes; unsupervised baselines (e.g., entropy-based) not systematically compared
  • The practical cost of running 2,000+ probes for evaluation may limit adoption
