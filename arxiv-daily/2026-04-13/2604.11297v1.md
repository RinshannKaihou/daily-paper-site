# The Past Is Not Past: Memory-Enhanced Dynamic Reward Shaping

> **一句话总结：** 提出MEDS框架，通过存储历史行为表征并使用密度聚类识别反复出现的错误模式，对高频错误施加更重的惩罚，在5个数据集和3个基模型上实现最高4.13 pass@1和4.37 pass@128的提升。

> **TL;DR:** MEDS stores historical behavioral signals and uses density-based clustering to identify recurring error patterns in RL training, penalizing frequent errors more heavily and achieving up to 4.13 pass@1 and 4.37 pass@128 improvements across 5 datasets and 3 base models.

## 核心贡献

- 提出基于记忆增强的动态奖励塑形框架（MEDS）
- 通过密度聚类识别高频重复错误模式并加重惩罚
- 在5个数据集和3个基模型上一致提升性能

## Key Contributions

- Memory-enhanced dynamic reward shaping using historical behavioral signals
- Density-based clustering for identifying recurring error patterns
- Up to 4.13 pass@1 and 4.37 pass@128 improvements
- Increased behavioral diversity during sampling

## Methodology

Stores intermediate model representations from past rollouts, uses density-based clustering to identify frequently recurring error patterns. Rollouts in more prevalent error clusters receive heavier penalties, encouraging broader exploration while reducing repeated mistakes.

## Results & Highlights

- Consistent improvements over existing baselines across 5 datasets
- Up to 4.13 pass@1 and 4.37 pass@128 gains
- Increased behavioral diversity confirmed by both LLM annotations and quantitative metrics
- Code available at github.com/Linxi000/MEDS
