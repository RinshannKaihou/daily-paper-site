Reasoning Resides in Layers: Restoring Temporal Reasoning in Video-Language Models with Layer-Selective Merging

Zihang Fu, Haonan Wang, Jian Kang, Kenji Kawaguchi, Jiaying Wu

arXiv: 2604.11399v1

Date: 2026-04-13

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11399v1

Reasoning Resides in Layers: Restoring Temporal Reasoning in Video-Language Models with Layer-Selective Merging

一句话总结： 提出MERIT框架，通过在VLM和文本骨干网络之间搜索逐层自注意力合并策略来恢复视频语言模型中被多模态适应削弱的时序推理能力，无需重训练即可在多个基准上持续提升推理性能。
TL;DR: MERIT is a training-free model merging framework that restores temporal reasoning in video-language models by selectively merging self-attention layers between the VLM and its text-only backbone, guided by an objective that improves reasoning while penalizing perception degradation.

核心贡献

  • 发现多模态适应（如视觉对齐）会削弱LLM继承的推理能力，特别是在视频语言模型中损害时序推理
  • 提出MERIT，一种免训练的任务驱动模型合并框架，在VLM和配对的文本骨干之间搜索逐层自注意力合并方案
  • 通过干预性掩码和帧级归因证明所选层对推理有不成比例的重要性，并将模型决策转向时间因果相关证据

Key Contributions

  • Identifies that multimodal adaptation weakens temporal reasoning inherited from language pretraining
  • MERIT: training-free, task-driven layer-selective merging between VLM and text backbone
  • Interventional masking shows selected layers are disproportionately important for reasoning
  • Consistent TR improvement across 3 VLMs and multiple benchmarks, generalizing beyond search set

Methodology

MERIT searches over layer-wise self-attention merging recipes between a VLM and its paired text-only backbone. The optimization objective improves temporal reasoning (TR) while penalizing degradation in temporal perception (TP). Interventional masking and frame-level attribution verify that selected layers are causally important for reasoning.

Results & Highlights

  • Consistent TR improvement across 3 representative VLMs and multiple benchmarks
  • Preserves or improves TP simultaneously
  • Generalizes beyond the search set to 4 distinct benchmarks
  • Outperforms uniform full-model merging and random layer selection
  • Training-free approach — no retraining required

Authors & Credibility

Authors from National University of Singapore and MBZUAI. Kenji Kawaguchi is a well-known ML researcher at NUS with extensive publications on optimization and generalization. Jiaying Wu is the corresponding author at NUS. NUS has a strong ML research program.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The finding that reasoning "resides in layers" is intuitive but the formal demonstration via layer-selective merging is novel. The training-free approach is practical.
  • Experimental rigor: Good experimental coverage across multiple VLMs and benchmarks. The interventional masking provides causal evidence for layer importance.
  • Reproducibility: Methodology is clearly described. No code mentioned but approach is implementable from the description.
  • Significance: Practical contribution for restoring reasoning in multimodal models without retraining. The layer-wise analysis provides insight into how multimodal adaptation affects different capabilities.

Strengths

  • Training-free solution to an important problem
  • Causal evidence via interventional masking
  • Consistent improvements across models and benchmarks
  • Strong author team

Limitations

  • Focused on temporal reasoning specifically — unclear generalization to other reasoning types
  • Requires access to the original text-only backbone (may not always be available)
  • Layer merging is a fairly coarse-grained intervention
