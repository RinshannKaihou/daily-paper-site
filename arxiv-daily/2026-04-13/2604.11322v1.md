# Do LLMs Know Tool Irrelevance? Demystifying Structural Alignment Bias in Tool Invocations

> **一句话总结：** 识别了LLM工具调用中的"结构对齐偏见"——即使工具与查询无关，只要查询属性能合法分配给工具参数，LLM仍倾向于调用它，并通过对比注意力归因揭示了语义检查和结构匹配两条竞争通路。

> **TL;DR:** Identifies structural alignment bias in LLM tool invocation — models tend to call irrelevant tools when query attributes can be validly assigned to tool parameters — and uses Contrastive Attention Attribution to reveal two competing pathways (semantic checking vs. structural matching) driving invocation decisions.

## 核心贡献

- 识别并定义了LLM工具调用中的"结构对齐偏见"这一被忽视的机制缺陷
- 提出SABEval数据集，将结构对齐与语义相关性分离以系统研究该偏见
- 通过对比注意力归因揭示了语义检查和结构匹配两条竞争通路
- 提出再平衡策略有效缓解结构对齐偏见，不损害通用工具使用能力

## Key Contributions

- Identifies structural alignment bias: a widespread mechanistic flaw in LLM tool refusal
- SABEval dataset decoupling structural alignment from semantic relevance
- Contrastive Attention Attribution reveals competing semantic checking vs. structural matching pathways
- Rebalancing strategy effectively mitigates the bias

## Methodology

Introduces SABEval dataset that systematically decouples structural alignment (can parameters be filled?) from semantic relevance (should the tool be used?). Contrastive Attention Attribution identifies internal attention pathways responsible for semantic vs. structural reasoning in tool invocation decisions.

## Results & Highlights

- Structural alignment bias induces severe tool-invocation errors
- Two competing attention pathways identified: semantic checking and structural matching
- Relative pathway strength drives invocation decisions
- Rebalancing strategy mitigates bias without degrading general capabilities
