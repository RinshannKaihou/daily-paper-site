From Attribution to Action: A Human-Centered Application of Activation Steering

一句话总结： 本文结合SAE归因与激活转向构建了交互式视觉模型调试工作流，通过8位专家访谈发现从业者能从被动观察转向基于干预的假设检验（8/8），但面临涟漪效应和实例级修正泛化性有限等风险。
TL;DR: Combines SAE-based attribution with activation steering for instance-level concept debugging in vision models, finding through expert interviews (N=8) that steering enables a shift from inspection to intervention-based hypothesis testing, while revealing risks including ripple effects and limited generalization.

核心贡献

  • 首次将SAE归因与激活转向结合为实例级概念分析的交互式工作流，并实现为基于网页的调试工具
  • 通过8位专家的半结构化访谈研究从业者如何推理、信任和应用激活转向，填补了该领域用户体验研究的空白
  • 发现激活转向使从业者从被动观察转向基于干预的假设检验（8/8参与者），大多数基于观察到的模型响应而非解释可信度来建立信任（6/8）
  • 揭示了激活转向的实际风险：涟漪效应（修改一个组件影响其他功能）和实例级修正的有限泛化性

Key Contributions

  • First interactive workflow combining SAE-based attribution with activation steering for vision model debugging
  • Semi-structured expert interviews (N=8) studying practitioner reasoning, trust, and application of activation steering
  • Finding that steering enables inspection-to-intervention shift (8/8), with trust grounded in observed responses (6/8)
  • Identifies practical risks: ripple effects and limited generalization of instance-level corrections

Methodology

The authors implement a web-based tool combining sparse autoencoder (SAE) attribution with activation steering for CLIP vision models. SAE decomposes model representations into interpretable features, attribution quantifies each feature's relevance, and activation steering modifies identified components. They conduct semi-structured expert interviews (N=8) with debugging tasks to study how practitioners reason about and apply the workflow.

Results & Highlights

  • All 8 participants shifted from inspection to intervention-based hypothesis testing
  • 6/8 grounded trust in observed model responses rather than explanation plausibility
  • Participants adopted systematic debugging strategies dominated by component suppression (7/8)
  • Risks identified: ripple effects from modifying interconnected components, limited generalization of corrections

Authors & Credibility

Strong team from Fraunhofer Heinrich-Hertz-Institut, Technische Universitat Berlin, and BIFOLD. Wojciech Samek is a well-known XAI researcher with numerous high-impact publications on explainability. Sebastian Lapuschkin has extensive publications on XAI methods. The Fraunhofer HHI group is one of the leading XAI research labs in Europe.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The combination of SAE + activation steering is not entirely new, but the human-centered evaluation with expert interviews is. The shift from "can we steer?" to "how do practitioners actually use steering?" fills an important gap.
  • Experimental rigor: N=8 is small for a user study, though appropriate for semi-structured expert interviews. The debugging task design is well thought out.
  • Reproducibility: The web tool implementation details could be more specific. Interview protocols are described but not fully included.
  • Significance: Important for making mechanistic interpretability actionable in practice. The identified risks (ripple effects, limited generalization) are valuable practical findings.

Strengths

  • Human-centered evaluation of mechanistic interpretability tools (rare and valuable)
  • Identifies concrete practical risks and limitations of activation steering
  • Well-designed expert interview methodology
  • From a leading XAI research group

Limitations

  • Small sample size (N=8) limits generalizability
  • Only tested on CLIP vision model — applicability to LLMs not demonstrated
  • Focuses on instance-level rather than systematic model correction
  • Does not compare with alternative debugging approaches
