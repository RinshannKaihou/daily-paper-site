> **一句话总结：** FastTPS 通过三项协同优化（全局静态 KV Cache 管理 GKVC、面向 token 阶段的融合 RoPE 注意力 TPSFLAT、以及门控-上投影交错布局的 Fusion MLP），在 AMD Ryzen AI 300 NPU 上把 LLM 解码阶段的吞吐推向理论峰值，对 Phi3-mini-4k-instruct 取得 5.95× 端到端加速、达到 92.77% 理论 TPS，同时把注意力块的 Operational Intensity 提升 3×、最大数值误差控制在 0.22% 以内。

> **TL;DR:** FastTPS is a dataflow-level optimization for the LLM decode ("token") phase on general AI accelerators, combining (1) a Global KV Cache (GKVC) that pre-allocates a 3D static KV buffer so new KV pairs are written by address-jump instead of a CPU-driven Concat, (2) TPSFLAT, a TPS-tailored FLAT dataflow that fuses RoPE+QKᵀ+Softmax+PV inside L1, and (3) Fusion MLP, an interlaced gate/up weight layout that merges gate/up projections into one Matmul and fuses SiLU·Mul with it under a fine-grained pipeline. On an AMD Ryzen AI 300 NPU with BF16, Phi3-mini-4k-instruct reaches a 5.95× end-to-end speedup (vs. 2.34 ms → 17.60 ms per token) at 92.77% of theoretical peak TPS; attention block OI improves 3× and max numerical error stays below 0.22% — roughly an order of magnitude more accurate than FlashAttention (~1e-3 vs ~1e-2).

## 核心贡献

- **GKVC（全局 KV Cache 管理）**：为 token 阶段设计 3D 静态预分配 KV Cache，新 KV 通过"地址跳变"直接写入预留位置（可由加速器或软件执行），彻底消除 `Concat` 算子及其带来的 L1↔L3 反复读写与 CPU 同步，把整个推理流水留在加速器上，为后续算子融合扫清障碍。
- **TPSFLAT（面向 TPS 的融合 RoPE 注意力）**：在 FLAT 思想基础上，针对 token 阶段 B=N=1 的特性按 H 维度做两级 tiling，将 RoPE（Q、K 共享正弦张量）、QKᵀ、Softmax、PV 四个算子在 L1 内连续融合，仅必要的 KV 张量回写 L3，显著提高 Operational Intensity（注意力块提升约 3×）。
- **Fusion MLP（交错布局的融合 MLP）**：将 `Wgate` 与 `Wup` 沿 K 维拆半再交错重排为单一大 Matmul `Wgu`，使门控/上投影与 SiLU、elewMul 进一步融合为 `siLu Mul`，并把 I/O 次数从 7 降到 2；叠加细粒度流水调度，最大化权重复用以掩盖访存延迟。
- **高精度保持**：在 BF16 下最大数值误差 < 0.22%，精度随序列长度增加反而变好，比 FlashAttention（~1e-2）高约一个数量级（~1e-3）；且不与 GQA、量化等压缩技术冲突。
- **强实测落地**：在 AMD Ryzen AI 300 系列 NPU 上对 ChatGLM3-6B、Llama2-7B、Llama3-8B、Llama3.2-1B、Phi3-mini-4k 五个模型实测，端到端最高 5.95× 加速，平均资源利用率 86%，Phi3 达 93%。

## Key Contributions

- **GKVC (Global KV Cache management):** A pre-allocated 3D static KV buffer in L3 where new K/V pairs are written by an address-jump stride (`Step = h·(Np+Nr)·d + Np·d`) rather than a CPU-driven `Concat`, eliminating the memory-reorganization operator and the associated L1↔L3 round-trips, while keeping the whole pipeline on the accelerator so further operator fusion becomes possible.
- **TPSFLAT (Fused Logit Attention Tiling for TPS):** A FLAT-derived dataflow specialized for the token phase (B=N=1). It tiles along the H dimension in a two-level (L3→L2→L1) scheme, shares one RoPE call between Q and K, and fuses RoPE + QKᵀ + Softmax + PV continuously inside L1, only writing back the KV that the cache actually needs. Attention-block OI improves ~3×.
- **Fusion MLP:** An interlaced gate/up weight layout (`Wgu ∈ R^{D×2K}`) that turns gate+up into a single larger Matmul, then fuses SiLU and element-wise Mul into one `siLu Mul` op, reducing I/O count from 7 to 2. Combined with a fine-grained pipeline schedule that overlaps weight loading with compute to hide latency, it brings MLP close to its bandwidth roofline (claimed ~1.3× the original data volume).
- **Low-precision-faithful acceleration:** All three strategies are accuracy-preserving; BF16 max error vs. a PyTorch float baseline stays under 0.22% and actually shrinks with sequence length, roughly an order of magnitude better than FlashAttention's ~1e-2.
- **Comprehensive NPU evaluation:** Validated on five SOTA LLMs (ChatGLM3-6B, Llama2-7B, Llama3-8B-instruct, Llama3.2-1B, Phi3-mini-4k-instruct) on the AMD Ryzen AI 300 NPU, with roofline analysis, OI characterization, and cross-vendor (Intel Lunar Lake, Nvidia Nano, FastFlowLM) MLPerf comparisons in the appendix.

## Methodology

FastTPS targets the **token phase** (autoregressive decode, B=N=1) of decoder-only Transformers running on accelerators with a three-level cache hierarchy (L1 < L2 < L3). The authors diagnose two bottlenecks via roofline analysis: (a) the attention block is bandwidth-bound because the standard `Concat`-based KV update shuttles K/V between L1 and L3 six times per kernel, and (b) the MLP block — 66.7% of Transformer parameters — is dominated by weight movement that eager execution cannot hide.

**GKVC.** A static KV buffer of shape `R^{B×H×Nmax×d}` is pre-allocated in L3. At each decode step the freshly computed K/V vector of length d is written to the reserved slot by jumping `Step = h·(Np+Nr)·d + Np·d` positions; no Concat, no CPU sync, no memory reallocation. The whole TPS pipeline now stays on the accelerator, unblocking downstream fusion.

**TPSFLAT.** Built on FLAT (Kao et al., 2023) and tailored to B=N=1. A two-level tiling partitions along H: an outer loop loads `Hb` heads from L3→L2, an inner loop processes one head at a time from L2→L1. Inside L1, Q and K are stored as one contiguous tensor `QK ∈ R^{2×B×N×1×d}` so RoPE is computed once (sharing `sin/cos`); `K_rope` is then written back via GKVC while `Q_rope` stays in L1 to drive the fused QKᵀ → Softmax → PV chain. Only the necessary KV tensors touch L3.

**Fusion MLP.** The gate and up weight matrices are split along K and interleaved into `Wgu ∈ R^{D×2K}`, so a single Matmul produces both gate and up projections. The SiLU activation and element-wise Mul are then fused into a `siLu Mul` op that runs in the inner loop, followed by down-projection. The number of L1↔L3 round-trips drops from 7 to 2. A pipeline schedule identifies weight loading as the dominant cost and overlaps it with compute to maximize weight reuse.

**Evaluation setup.** Five LLMs (Table 1) on the AMD Ryzen AI 300 NPU (2.84 GFLOPs peak cited), BF16 precision, with CPU overhead explicitly excluded. Baselines are HuggingFace standard implementations (PyTorch float for accuracy). Appendix D adds MLPerf comparisons on AMD Ryzen AI 400 Gorgon Point 2 vs. Intel Lunar Lake (102 GB/s), Nvidia Jetson Nano, and FastFlowLM.

## Results & Highlights

- **End-to-end TPS (Table 3, AMD Ryzen AI 300 NPU, BF16):** Phi3-mini-4k-instruct reaches **5.95× speedup** (2.95 ms → 17.60 ms per token), achieving **92.77% of the theoretical peak TPS** (18.97 ms theoretical). Llama2-7B reaches **5.30× speedup** at 79.74% of theoretical peak, and Llama3.2-1B reaches **2.64× speedup** at 85.22% of theoretical peak. Average accelerator-resource utilization across tested models is **86%**, with Phi3 at **93%**.
- **Attention block (Figures 5–6):** TPSFLAT cuts attention latency by **80–93%** across the five models, with the largest reduction **93% for Llama3 @ 4096 seq**; the `Concat` overhead specifically drops by **66–82%** and the remaining compute by **18–34%**. The claimed block-level speedup reaches up to **13.07×**, and attention-block OI improves **~3×** per roofline analysis (GQA Opt. vs GQA Ori., Figure 2). GQA introduces only ~7.2% latency disparity across architecturally identical Llama3/Llama2/ChatGLM.
- **MLP block (Figure 7):** Fusion MLP delivers **>20% latency reduction** for Llama3, ChatGLM, Llama2, and Phi3, and a **68% reduction for Llama3.2**; the block-level speedup reaches up to **3.14×**.
- **Accuracy (Figure 8):** BF16 FastTPS max numerical error vs. a PyTorch float baseline stays **below 0.22%** and decreases with sequence length; FastTPS achieves ~1e-3 error versus FlashAttention's ~1e-2 — roughly a **10× accuracy advantage** — with the residual error attributable to output truncation rather than the algorithm.
- **Cross-vendor (Appendix D):** On MLPerf content-generation, AMD Ryzen AI 400 Gorgon Point 2 with FastTPS reaches **27.12 TPS** vs. Intel Lunar Lake's **24.69 TPS** for Llama3.2 (AMD/LNL = 1.10), despite AMD's lower raw bandwidth; against FastFlowLM, LFM2-2.6B on AMD is **1.08–1.10×** faster across 1k/2k/4k context.

This is a vendor paper: the proposed method is benchmarked exclusively on AMD Ryzen AI NPU hardware (300, 400 Gorgon Point 2, Krackan Point) and the appendix MLPerf results position AMD favorably against Intel Lunar Lake and Nvidia Nano. Citations are broad and appropriate (FlashAttention-1/2/3, FLAT, GQA, vLLM/PagedAttention, KVQuant, Llama, Phi, ChatGLM). The technical depth on the three-level cache hierarchy, address-jump formula, and tiling buffer requirements (Appendix A–C) is consistent with a real systems implementation rather than a pure design exercise.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty** is moderate and incremental rather than fundamental: GKVC is essentially the well-known "static / paged KV cache" idea (Shi et al., 2024, their own ref) combined with address-jump writes; TPSFLAT is an explicit extension of FLAT (Kao et al., 2023) "tailored to TPS characteristics"; Fusion MLP is a layout rearrangement plus FFN-fusion (Bercovich et al., 2025) applied at the kernel level. The contribution is the **system integration on AMD XDNA NPU**, not a new algorithmic primitive.
- **Rigor** is mixed. Strong points: roofline analysis ties the OI improvement (3×) to measured latency reductions, and the cache-usage table (Table 4) gives concrete per-level buffer requirements. Weak points: the headline "6× vs. none-fusion" and "5×+ vs. HuggingFace" baselines are **weak** — HuggingFace eager-mode and unfused kernels are not the relevant SOTA on NPU; the paper does not benchmark against FlashAttention-3, vLLM/PagedAttention, or AMD's own OMNIML/MLIR stacks on speed, only on accuracy. The accuracy comparison to FlashAttention is also done by simulation ("simulate the behavior of FastTPS attention using bfloat16"), not by running a matched FlashAttention kernel end-to-end.
- **Reproducibility** is limited: all kernel-level code, tiling parameters (`tilen`, `tilek`, `tiled`, `x`, `y`, `z`), and the AMD XDNA software stack are proprietary; no code or artifacts are linked. Independent reproduction on non-AMD hardware is impossible from the paper alone.
- **Significance** is real but narrow: the result that a static-KV + L1-fused-attention + interleaved-MLP pipeline can hit 93% of theoretical TPS on a 2.84 GFLOPs NPU is a useful data point for edge/client-side LLM deployment, but the generality claim ("readily adaptable to GPUs, TPUs, ASICs") is **not demonstrated** — it is asserted.
- **Red flags:** (1) **CPU overhead was explicitly excluded** ("the CPU overhead wasn't taken into consideration"), yet GKVC's central selling point is removing the CPU-driven Concat — omitting CPU cost inflates the relative gain of the unfused baseline. (2) The paper references "Tables 5-8" but only Tables 5–7 appear in the PDF; one is missing. (3) Several self-citations (Patwari et al., 2025; Subramon et al., 2023) are AMD-internal performance-modeling works used to substantiate claims without independent verification. (4) Cross-vendor tables show AMD actually **losing** to Intel Lunar Lake and Nvidia Nano on Llama2/Llama3 absolute TPS in many categories (AMD/Nano = 0.74–0.93), undercutting the headline framing.

## Strengths

- Clean, three-pronged optimization that addresses both bandwidth-bound (attention) and weight-movement-bound (MLP) bottlenecks, with each piece tied to a measurable roofline/IOI metric.
- Accuracy story is genuinely strong: <0.22% max BF16 error that improves with sequence length, and a ~10× edge over FlashAttention in numerical fidelity — attractive for high-precision deployments.
- Detailed algorithmic specification (Algorithms 1–4) and a transparent cache-usage table make the dataflow design auditable even without released code.
- Practical, deployed evaluation on real AMD NPU silicon with cross-vendor MLPerf numbers in the appendix, not just a simulator.
- Explicit compatibility discussion with GQA (validated, ≤7.2% overhead) and a stated plan for quantization integration.

## Limitations

- **Hardware scope:** evaluated only on AMD XDNA NPUs; GPU/TPU/ASIC portability is claimed but unproven.
- **Baseline strength:** primary baselines (HuggingFace eager, unfused) are not competitive NPU-optimized stacks; no head-to-head latency against FlashAttention-3, PagedAttention, or vLLM-class serving systems.
- **CPU cost omission:** the central GKVC benefit (removing Concat/CPU sync) is measured while excluding CPU overhead, which biases the comparison.
- **Decode-only, B=1:** no prefill, no batching, no speculative decoding — the regime where many serving systems actually run.
- **Vendor interest:** all authors are AMD employees and the cross-vendor tables are selectively framed despite AMD losing several absolute-TPS comparisons.
- **Missing content:** Table 8 referenced but absent; no end-to-end quality (perplexity/downstream task) results, only max-element numerical error.
- **No code/artifact release** stated.

## Relevance

对关注「LLM 边缘/客户端推理系统优化」的方向高度相关：FastTPS 提供了一套在带宽受限的 NPU 上把解码阶段推向理论峰值的工程范式（静态 KV Cache + L1 内注意力融合 + 交错布局 MLP 融合），其 roofline 分析方法和 cache 级别 buffer 推导可迁移到其他三级 cache 加速器；对于研究高效推理/算子融合/KV Cache 管理的读者，是一篇有实操参考价值的系统论文。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does FastTPS behave under **continuous batching / B>1** and during the **prefill phase**, where the N=1 assumption and per-token pipeline scheduling break down?
- What is the **end-to-end latency including CPU overhead** (which the paper excludes) — does GKVC's win survive once CPU scheduling cost is counted for the unfused baseline?
- How does TPSFLAT's speed (not just accuracy) compare against **FlashAttention-3 / FlashInfer / PagedAttention** running on the same NPU via an open compiler stack?
- Can the interlaced gate-up layout be **retrofitted to existing weight tensors** without retraining, or does it require a one-time offline repacking, and what is the memory/time cost?
- Do the **≤0.22% max-error** guarantees hold for longer contexts (32k+, where FlashAttention is known to degrade) and for **quantized INT8/INT4** variants the authors promise as future work?
- Is the reported cross-vendor gap (AMD trailing Nvidia Nano and Intel Lunar Lake on Llama2/Llama3 absolute TPS) closable with FastTPS alone, or is it fundamentally a memory-bandwidth limit of the XDNA NPU?
