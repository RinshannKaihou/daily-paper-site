The Scaling Spine

Readiness(A) ≈ f( Ntopo, Dloop, |E| )
  • N_topoFramework scaling — single agent → role-based team → orchestration topology (chain/tree/graph).
  • D_loopCapability scaling — depth of the perceive–reason–plan–act–reflect loop. Competence tracks loop engineering more than parameter count.
  • |E|Environment scaling — reachable tools, data sources, interfaces (MCP, FHIR, PACS). The most underused lever.

Claim: the three axes are complementary, not substitutable — any single axis saturates. (Conceptual claim, supported by citation only.)

Formal Model & Three-Level Autonomy Taxonomy

A medical agent is A = (P, R, L, M, T, F) — perception, reasoning, planning, memory, tool use, action/reflection — acting under partial observability: the agent never sees the patient's true state, only indirect evidence.

LEVEL 01
Assisted
Suggests under direct clinician control — interactive segmentation, report drafting.
LEVEL 02
Cooperative
Decomposes tasks, invokes tools in a human-in-the-loop framework; clinician retains override.
LEVEL 03
Fully Autonomous
End-to-end workflow execution with exception-based escalation.

§ 03

Strengths & Limitations

side-by-side

Strengths

  • One unifying axis. The scaling spine lets architecture, cognition, training, deployment, and risk be read through a single lens — most prior surveys keep these separate.
  • Deployment-first inversion. Benchmarks and training environments belong up front, not as a closing appendix.
  • Exhaustive, current catalogs. 60+ benchmarks (Table 1) and 60+ systems (Table 3), with a maintained GitHub repo.
  • Strong 2025–2026 coverage. Agentic-RL burst (DAPO, Dr. GRPO, GSPO, CISPO, MT-GRPO, ATPO) and clinical gyms as the binding constraint.
  • Pipeline-aligned risk map. Five failure modes each paired with mitigation coverage (Strong/Moderate/Weak/None).
  • Honest about the gap. Concedes Level-3 is unreached and self-evolution is aspirational.

Limitations

  • No original experiments. Every quantitative claim is borrowed; conflicting numbers across works are not reconciled.
  • Inter-rater statistics absent. No Cohen's κ — the 1,174 → 300+ funnel is not auditable as a formal systematic review.
  • Self-evolution over-promised. The spine's "destination" framing outpaces the evidence the paper itself concedes.
  • Uneven specialty depth. Radiology/pathology dense; ophthalmology, oncology, neurology thin; dermatology/cardiology mainly in figures.
  • Conceptual claims unverified. "Complementary, not substitutable" and "competence tracks loop engineering" are asserted, supported only by citation.
  • Negative results under-engaged. Benchmarks showing multi-agent gains are "uneven and sometimes illusory" once cost is counted are cited but not reconciled.

§ 05

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How would the three scaling axes (N_topo, D_loop, |E|) be measured quantitatively so the "complementary, not substitutable" claim could be tested rather than asserted?
  2. Given general models beat medical ones on hallucination-free generation (76.6% vs 51.3%), under what conditions does domain-specific fine-tuning actively erode factual grounding?
  3. What minimum combination of verifiable reward, audit trail, and exception-based escalation would make a single Level-3 pilot (e.g., stroke CT triage) ethically defensible?
  4. Can the 89% ethical-violation reduction from structured peer review and shared-pool vulnerabilities be unified into a predictive topology-risk metric?
  5. Medicine has intrinsic verifiable signals (measurement consistency, guideline conformance, follow-up outcomes) — which reward source is most sample-efficient, and which carries the highest risk of reward hacking?
  6. How should FDA Predetermined Change Control Plans and the EU AI Act extend to certify a self-evolving agent whose architecture or control code changes post-deployment, not just weights?
