> **一句话总结：** 本文在 FinQA 与 TAT-QA 两个金融数值问答基准上，用残差流的线性探针检测 LLM "自信却错误"的幻觉，在三族 8B-9B 模型上，对自信答案的检测 AUROC 比最佳输出级基线高 +0.13~0.15（FinQA），并在 20% 人工审核预算下多抓获 8 个百分点的错误。

> **TL;DR:** The author trains linear probes on the residual stream of Qwen3-8B, Llama-3.1-8B, and Gemma-2-9B to detect "confidently wrong" answers on FinQA and TAT-QA. Among 8/8 self-consistent answers, where 15-23% are wrong on FinQA, the probe reaches 0.68-0.77 AUROC versus 0.55-0.63 for the best deployment-cheap baseline (token log-probs / P(True)), a +0.13 to +0.15 AUROC gap; at a 20% human-review budget on Qwen3-8B/FinQA it catches 45% of errors versus 37% for P(True) and 20% for random routing. The probe is task-specific (cross-task transfer loses 0.07-0.16 AUROC) and needs white-box access, but is a cheap, effective triage layer for self-hosted financial LLMs.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统评估现代指令微调 LLM（Qwen3-8B、Llama-3.1-8B、Gemma-2-9B）在金融数值问答（FinQA、TAT-QA）上的"答案正确性"线性探针检测，填补了既有正确性探针工作（多面向 TruthfulQA 等事实类基准，AUROC≈0.9-1.0）在金融多步数值推理上的空白——此处 AUROC 仅≈0.73-0.79，明显更难。
- 量化了探针相对于"可部署的廉价输出信号"在"自信却错误"这一最危险象限中的优势：FinQA 上 Δ=+0.151 / +0.151 / +0.131 AUROC（三族模型一致），而该象限恰恰是输出级信号失效、趋近随机的地方。
- 给出可跨族迁移的探针设计规则：在 2/3 网络深度、对生成答案 token 做均值池化的线性探针，对两族模型命中各自最优层、第三族仅损失 0.013 AUROC，无需逐模型调参。
- 引入该领域罕见的效度控制：随机初始化安慰剂探针（参考 AUROC≈0.625 而非 0.5）、按源文档分组的交叉验证（杜绝最多 0.04 AUROC 的泄漏）、以及对评分器标签噪声（4-6%）的敏感性审计。
- 将检测方法转化为运营价值：在有限人工审核预算（10-30%）下，探针路由对"过度自信"的模型（如 Qwen3-8B）能多捕获显著比例的自信错误，并提出"廉价信号先筛 unsure + 探针再筛 confident"的两级级联方案。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic evaluation of answer-correctness linear probing on modern instruction-tuned LLMs (Qwen3-8B, Llama-3.1-8B, Gemma-2-9B) for numeric financial QA (FinQA, TAT-QA), showing that financial reasoning errors are substantially harder to decode (≈0.73-0.79 AUROC) than factual falsehoods (≈0.9-1.0 in prior work).
- Quantifies the probe's advantage over deployment-cheap output signals (token log-probabilities, P(True)) precisely inside the "confidently wrong" quadrant — the most dangerous cell — where output-level signals collapse toward chance and the probe retains 0.68-0.77 AUROC (+0.131 to +0.151 AUROC on FinQA across all three families).
- Identifies a single probe-design rule that generalizes across model families without per-family tuning: a mean-pooled linear probe at 2/3 network depth hits the per-family optimum for two models and costs at most 0.013 AUROC for the third.
- Applies unusually rigorous validity controls: random-initialization placebo (≈0.625 AUROC reference, not 0.5), document-grouped cross-validation (preventing up to 0.04 AUROC of split leakage on TAT-QA), and an audited grader with quantified 4-6% label-noise sensitivity.
- Demonstrates operational triage value: at 10-30% human-review budgets, probe-based routing catches materially more confident errors than P(True) for over-committing models, and proposes a two-stage cascade (cheap signals triage "unsure"; probe screens the "confident" remainder).

## Methodology

**Task & models.** Each model is given a financial filing excerpt (text plus serialized table) and a question requiring it to locate one-to-three values amid distractors and compute a derived quantity (change, ratio, or share of total). Three instruction-tuned LLMs from different developers are evaluated: Qwen3-8B, Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Gemma-2-9B-it. All run an identical pipeline with chat templates normalized and Qwen3's native reasoning mode disabled for cross-family comparability. Models reason step-by-step via prompted chain-of-thought and emit `ANSWER: <number>`; decoding is greedy so each question has one canonical served answer.

**Data.** Two established benchmarks. FinQA: analyst-written questions over S&P 500 filing excerpts (1999-2019), multi-step numeric reasoning over long contexts (the harder end). TAT-QA: questions over annual-report tables with short text, shorter and more extractive (the easier end). After requiring a bare numeric gold answer and dropping contexts over 2,800 tokens, 6,105 FinQA and 1,138 TAT-QA questions remain (98% and 68% of the full datasets).

**Grading instrument.** Final `ANSWER:` line parsed to float with handling for currency symbols, thousands separators, percent signs, and accounting-style parenthesized negatives. A prediction matches gold iff `min_{s in S} |ps - g|/max(|g|, eps) <= tau` with tau=0.02, eps guarding division by zero, and scale set S = {1, 100, 0.01, 1000, 0.001} covering percent-vs-decimal and thousands-vs-millions conventions. Sign errors are never forgiven. Grader validated via an LLM judge (GPT-5.4-mini) re-grading 120 items: 90.0% / 93.3% agreement on graded-correct / graded-incorrect, residual disagreement dominated by convention ambiguities; estimated label noise 4-6%.

**The 2×2 (confidence × correctness).** Correctness from the grader; behavioral confidence from self-consistency: `c(x) = (1/k) sum match(a_i, a_0)` over k=8 independent temperature-0.7 resamples. "Confident" = unanimous 8/8 (c(x)=1); the rest are "unsure." Prevalence is population-reweighted because self-consistency was run on all wrong answers but only a random subset of correct ones (FinQA 1,500; TAT-QA 400).

**Signals compared.** (i) Three deployment-cheap token-log-probability variants (mean over all tokens; mean over final-answer span; min over final-answer span). (ii) P(True): the model's self-reported True/False assessment following Kadavath et al., scored by the relative True-logit at the first output position, costing one extra forward pass. (iii) The probe: logistic regression on a linear readout of the residual stream at layer l = floor(2L/3), mean-pooled over the generated answer's tokens, captured in a single forward pass over the concatenated prompt+answer; fit by l2-regularized (C=0.1), class-balanced logistic regression against the binary correctness label. Self-consistency (8 generations) is excluded from the deployable set because it is the stratifier and impractically expensive.

**Evaluation protocol.** 5-fold cross-validation with document-grouped, class-stratified folds (a naive split on questions leaks the same source document; document-grouping prevents up to 0.04 AUROC inflation on TAT-QA). Every example scored out-of-fold; stacked predictors use an inner CV loop so the second-stage combiner never trains on its own inputs. Metrics: AUROC (primary) and PR-AUC (wrong-as-positive, secondary), population-reweighted. Controls: permuted-label (≈0.5), random-init placebo (0.625), layer-0 baseline (0.62-0.67), cross-task transfer, and cross-run reproducibility of re-extracted activations.

## Results & Highlights

- **Confident hallucination is common on FinQA.** Among 8/8 self-consistent answers, 15-23% are wrong: Qwen3-8B has 19% confident-wrong (1,137 of 6,105 questions; overall Acc 69.9%, overall confidence 82%), Llama-3.1-8B has 5% (329; Acc 60.1%, confidence 36%), Gemma-2-9B has 14% (861; Acc 68.1%, confidence 72%). On the easier TAT-QA, the confident-wrong share drops to 4-7%.
- **Probe dominates inside the confident quadrant (FinQA).** Restricted to 8/8 confident answers, the probe reaches AUROC 0.766 / 0.701 / 0.755 (PR-AUC 0.563 / 0.368 / 0.499) for Qwen3-8B / Llama-3.1-8B / Gemma-2-9B, versus the best deployment-cheap baseline at 0.615 / 0.550 / 0.624 — a Δ of +0.151 / +0.151 / +0.131 AUROC. On TAT-QA the probe still leads by +0.058 / +0.121 / +0.091.
- **Best baselines collapse toward chance for confident answers.** On FinQA confident answers the best output-level signal sits at 0.55-0.62 AUROC (barely above the 0.625 random-init placebo), confirming that output-level uncertainty is least informative exactly where hallucinations are most dangerous.
- **Full-sample detection.** Over all answers the probe attains 0.73-0.79 AUROC across the six model×benchmark cells, beating P(True) (0.66-0.73) and the best token-logprob variant (0.59-0.71) in all six conditions; stacking cheap signals onto the probe helps in five of six.
- **Probe advantage survives abstention removal.** Removing abstentions (4-11% of the confident-wrong cell) shifts the incremental AUROC by only ~0.01 (Qwen +0.151→+0.161, Llama +0.151→+0.156, Gemma +0.131→+0.120), ruling out "the probe just flags refusals."
- **Probe design rule transfers across families.** Mean-pooling over all answer tokens beats last-prompt-token pooling; AUROC rises from the embedding layer (0.67-0.70) to ~2/3 depth and plateaus (0.76-0.79). At 2/3 depth the probe matches the per-family optimum for Qwen3 (0.785) and Gemma-2 (0.776) and costs Llama-3.1 only 0.013 AUROC (0.765 vs 0.778 at its layer-16 optimum).
- **Cross-task transfer fails.** Training on FinQA and testing on TAT-QA (or reverse) costs 0.07-0.16 AUROC across all three families, and the transferred probe usually falls below the target's own baseline signals — the monitor is task-specific.
- **Triage value at bounded review budgets (Table 5).** On Qwen3-8B/FinQA, at a 20% review budget the probe catches 45% of all errors vs 37% for P(True) and 20% for random routing; the probe or probe+P(True) cascade is best at every budget for Qwen3 and Gemma. Flagging the 5% most-suspicious confident answers (Fig. 1) yields 79% precision (3.4× prevalence) at 17% recall; a 30% budget yields 45% precision at 60% recall.
- **Probe is largely incremental to self-consistency.** Probe-vs-self-consistency rank correlation is only 0.28-0.48, and the probe score correlates r=0.23-0.38 with correctness after linearly removing all confidence measures — so most of its signal is not redundant with multi-run sampling.
- **Probe does NOT dominate on unsure answers / TAT-QA.** Within unsure answers the probe's Δ is +0.006 to +0.064 on FinQA but negative for Llama (-0.021) and Gemma (-0.070) on TAT-QA, where output signals already work — motivating the two-stage cascade.

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author paper by Richard Zhe Wang at St. John Fisher University (a small liberal-arts university in Rochester, NY), with an alternate gmail research address. Credibility rests on the methodology rather than institutional weight: 36 well-chosen references spanning probing (Azaria & Mitchell, Burns et al., Orgad et al., Marks & Tegmark), uncertainty (Farquhar et al. semantic entropy, Kadavath P(True), Wang et al. self-consistency), financial hallucination (FinGround, FRED, Mirajkar's mechanistic study), and the underlying benchmarks (FinQA, TAT-QA, BloombergGPT). The experimental rigor — document-grouped CV, random-init placebo, audited grader, label-noise sensitivity — is above the norm for this subfield and explicitly motivated by recent critiques of benchmark-construction artifacts (Hussain & Kantarcioglu PARALLAX; Janiak et al. "Illusion of Progress"). The honesty about transfer failure and the unsure-answer weakness is a credibility positive.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** Moderate but well-scoped. Linear truthfulness probing and the "confidently wrong" phenomenon are both established (Azaria & Mitchell 2023; Simhi et al. 2025 "Trust Me, I'm Wrong"). The genuine novelty is the deployment-fair framing — comparing the probe only against signals a production system can actually afford (token logprobs, one-pass P(True)) — and the stratified evaluation isolating the confident quadrant on financial numeric QA, which prior correctness-probing work (TruthfulQA, HaluEval) did not touch. The contrast with Mirajkar's mechanistic study (which reported near-perfect cross-topic transfer on synthetic ConvFinQA prompts) is a pointed, paper-specific contribution: synthetic-prompt transfer does not predict transfer across naturally-occurring question distributions (0.07-0.16 AUROC loss here).
- **Rigor.** High for the venue. Document-grouped folds (correcting the TAT-QA same-document leakage that inflates up to 0.04 AUROC), random-init placebo as the real chance reference (0.625, not 0.5), audited grader with quantified 4-6% label noise, and an explicit note that symmetric noise attenuates AUROC differences so the probe edge is likely understated. The abstention-removal control, the 6/8-vs-8/8 sensitivity check, and the inner-CV stacking are all done correctly.
- **Reproducibility.** Mixed. Methods, hyperparameters (C=0.1, 2/3 depth, tau=0.02 grader), and model versions are specified; activation re-extraction reproducibility is asserted. But no mention of code/model release, and the place where the 2/3-depth/mean-pool choice and the scale-set S were fixed a priori is easy to under-specify. The self-consistency subsampling of correct answers (1,500 FinQA / 400 TAT-QA) plus population reweighting is a real data quirk that future replicators must match exactly to reproduce the reported shares.
- **Significance.** Practically meaningful for self-hosted financial LLMs. The +0.13-0.15 AUROC on the confident quadrant and the triage gains (45% vs 37% errors caught at 20% budget) are large enough to matter operationally. Significance is capped by scope: three models in a narrow 8B-9B band, numeric-only answers, single-turn, no native reasoning modes.
- **Red flags / weaknesses.** (1) The biggest practical tension is unaddressed: to know which answers are "confident" you must run 8 generations, which is itself the expensive signal the paper excludes from the deployable set — so the headline "probe is cheap" only holds after an expensive stratification step. (2) The probe needs white-box access, so it does not apply to API-based deployments (most real financial use). (3) No larger models and no reasoning-mode traces; Qwen3's thinking mode was disabled, which is the regime where confident hallucinations are arguably most severe. (4) The probe loses to output signals on TAT-QA unsure answers for two of three families — so it is not a universal detector. (5) Single author, small institution, no public code release mentioned.

## Strengths

- Deployment-fair baseline design: only compares against signals a real production system can afford, so the +0.13-0.15 AUROC confident-quadrant gap is not inflated by straw-man baselines.
- Cross-family probe-design rule (2/3 depth, mean-pool) that transfers without per-family tuning — directly actionable for practitioners.
- Unusually strong validity controls (random-init placebo, document-grouped CV, audited grader, label-noise sensitivity) that guard against the benchmark artifacts recently shown to inflate hallucination-detection progress.
- Honest negative results: cross-task transfer fails; the probe loses on unsure TAT-QA answers; abstention removal does not explain the gap.
- Concrete operational translation: triage curves, cascade design, and a 20%-budget worked example (45% vs 37% errors caught).

## Limitations

- The confidence stratifier (8-way self-consistency) is itself expensive, creating a deployment tension the paper does not fully resolve.
- Probe requires white-box/self-hosted access; not applicable to third-party LLM APIs.
- Only three models, all 8B-9B; larger models, smaller models, and other families untested.
- Only numeric, single-turn answers; free-text, multi-turn dialogues, and reasoning-mode traces excluded (Qwen3 thinking mode disabled).
- The probe is task-specific: FinQA↔TAT-QA transfer loses 0.07-0.16 AUROC, so a new probe must be trained per task.
- No claim about temporal drift in accounting/reporting practice; FinQA's arithmetic conventions are stable, so the benchmarks do not exercise evolving rules.
- No code/data release mentioned.

## Relevance

对关注 LLM 幻觉检测、模型内部状态探针与金融领域可靠性的研究有直接参考价值：它给出了在"自信却错误"这一最危险象限上，探针对输出级信号的量化优势，以及一套可迁移的探针设计与严格的评测协议。对从事 LLM 不确定性估计或人机协同审核（human-in-the-loop triage）的工作也具备方法学借鉴意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the 8-generation confidence stratifier be replaced by a cheap probe-based confidence estimate, closing the deployment-cost gap between stratification and detection?
- Does the probe's confident-quadrant advantage hold on larger (70B+) models and on native reasoning-mode (e.g., Qwen3 thinking) traces, where intermediate computation may dilute the residual-stream signal?
- How does the probe behave on free-text financial answers where mechanical grading is unavailable — can it be trained from weak/LLM-judge labels without the 2%-tolerance grader?
- Is the cross-task transfer failure (0.07-0.16 AUROC loss) fundamental to numeric reasoning, or would a multi-task probe trained jointly on FinQA+TAT-QA close the gap?
- Can the probe be hardened against adversarial inputs or distribution shift in filing style, given that label noise and split leakage already move central comparisons by several AUROC points?
- What is the cost-benefit frontier when combining probe, P(True), and cheap logprob signals in a learned cascade versus the rank-fusion cascade reported here?
