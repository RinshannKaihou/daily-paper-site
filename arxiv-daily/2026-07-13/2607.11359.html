Efficient Tuning Before Low-Bit Post-Training Quantization for Stochastic Gradient Descent-optimized Models

Peng Xia · Junbiao Pang (corresponding) · Muhammad Ayub Sabir | Beijing University of Technology | 2026-07-13 | code: xpxpxp2001xpxpxp/ETBQ
Key finding

ETBQ pre-conditions an SGD-trained FP model into a flatter, quantization-friendly loss basin — before any reconstruction-based PTQ backend touches it.

Key metric

Why this matters

A middle path between QAT and PTQ that lifts low-bit (W2A4) accuracy on lightweight CNNs and dense-prediction tasks — where standard PTQ hurts most.

Critical edge

Gains collapse at W2A2 on lightweight nets (single-digit accuracy) and the flatness→quantization link is correlational, from a single ResNet-18 case — no matched-flatness control.

The FP model never holds fake-quantized weights during tuning — only FP weights update, under injected quantization-error perturbations. Loss: L_ETBQ = L_task(f̃(x₀; W'_t), y). The result is still a clean FP checkpoint that drops into any existing PTQ pipeline.

WQN — Weight-side noise temporal differencing

Simulate the target weight quantizer to get empirical per-output-channel error, fit a Gaussian, sample δ̃_W. The differenced perturbation P_W^t = δ̃_W^t − δ̃_W^(t−1) cancels non-zero-mean drift and acts as anti-correlated, quantization-aligned noise — linked to Anti-PGD's Hessian-smoothing. Stored weights stay clean FP; only the gradient evaluation point shifts.

AQN — Activation-side noise salience masking

Fit per-tensor Gaussian activation error (EMA, β=0.9). Rather than perturb everything, a salience-aware mask picks channels: spatial-mean magnitude → softmax → Bernoulli, so high-magnitude (high-error) channels get perturbed preferentially. Target density ρ keeps the middle representations from being over-perturbed.

Joint, not transferred design choice

Transferring activation error to the weight side (à la ERQ) blows up: its std is 1.06×10⁻¹ vs native weight error 5.62×10⁻³ — nearly two orders larger and heavy-tailed. Combining them would let activation error dominate. So WQN and AQN each act in their native space and are optimized jointly.

Warmup + SWA stabilization schedule

Linear perturbation warmup λ_e = min(e/E_warmup,1)·λ_max (λ_max=1.0). SWA over the final epochs (epoch 60 on CIFAR-100) plus a BatchNorm-recomputation pass before export consolidate the perturbed trajectory into a stable FP checkpoint.

Strengths & limitations, side by side

Strengths

  • Genuine paradigm shift. Improve the FP model's quantization tolerance before PTQ, not just minimize error after — compatible with any reconstruction-based backend.
  • Strong, consistent gains on the hardest, most practical settings: lightweight CNNs under W2A4 and dense segmentation (Cityscapes +5.80 mIoU).
  • Honest framing. Flatness–quantization link stated as correlation, not cause; cost positioned transparently (30.6 h vs 0.2 h PTQ vs 110 h QAT).
  • Clean ablations at both component (Table II) and mechanism (App. B) level — WQN, AQN, SWA, temporal differencing, salience masking each isolated.
  • Public code and broad coverage: 4 datasets, 6 architectures, 4 bit-width settings.

Limitations

  • Heavyweight. Needs the full training set + 80–120-epoch FP tuning — far beyond calibration-only PTQ, often unavailable at deploy time.
  • Gaussian error model breaks at 2-bit (NRMSE_QQ ≈ 0.10), which is exactly why W2A2 gains go marginal — MobileNet-V1/V2 ImageNet W2A2 stays near-random.
  • Only SGD-trained CNNs/U-Net evaluated. Adam-trained ViTs/LLMs — arguably today's most important quant targets — are untested, explicitly deferred.
  • Only reconstruction-based backends (mostly QDrop/QEP). Data-free PTQ and learned-rounding pipelines unproven; AdaRound/BRECQ only on CIFAR-100.
  • Causal claim is correlational. Hessian evidence comes from a single ResNet-18 case; a controlled "matched-flatness via plain SWA" experiment is missing.
04 /

Open questions for further exploration

  1. Transformers next? Would ETBQ transfer to Adam-trained ViTs/LLMs, where outlier-driven activations and implicit curvature differ sharply — can WQN/AQN's Gaussian model survive the heavy-tailed, channel-outlier regime FlatQuant/RepQ-ViT target?
  2. Correlation → causation. Match ETBQ's basin flatness via plain SWA or SAM and check whether the matched-flatness model gets the same PTQ gain without quantization-aligned perturbations.
  3. Cheaper pre-conditioning. Can the 30.6 h full-data cost drop — calibration-subset-only variant, distillation from perturbation statistics, or a ~10-epoch schedule — while keeping most of the W2A4 gain?
  4. Backend generality. How does ETBQ compose with data-free PTQ and learned-rounding backends other than QDrop/QEP (AdaRound at scale, BRECQ on ImageNet)?
  5. Why does late-layer help fade? The layer-wise MSE reduction (Fig. 5) weakens in final layers — inherent limit of late-stage compressed features, or recoverable with a depth-dependent λ_e schedule?
