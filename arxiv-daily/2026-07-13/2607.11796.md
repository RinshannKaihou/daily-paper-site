> **一句话总结：** 本文利用 Mamba 选择式状态空间模型中对角状态矩阵的结构，给出一种离线、精确的逐模态状态使用测量工具（Gram 张量），在 Mamba-1/2 与 7B Falcon-Mamba 上发现训练后的模型会随输入重新分配其携带信号的模式集合（即"模式迁移"），并通过冻结信号反事实归因到输入依赖的写入映射 Bt 而非时间步 Δ；基于两遍扫描的输入调度剪枝在所有规模（130M–7B）上超越所有静态/汉克尔/层自适应排序，并在一半状态预算下匹配未剪枝模型。

> **TL;DR:** A single-author paper gives an exact, offline instrument for measuring how a trained Mamba-style selective SSM uses its diagonal state modes: because the state matrix is diagonal, a per-(layer, channel, window) Gram tensor returns the exact output error of dropping any mode subset, validated to 2.3×10⁻⁷ relative error on Mamba-1 and to a median 1×10⁻⁶ deployed-pruning deviation over 4,464 configs. Applied across Mamba-1 (130M–2.8B), the deployed 7B Falcon-Mamba, and Mamba-2, the instrument reveals that the set of signal-carrying modes migrates with the input (oracle gap ρ ≈ 0.44–0.57 at the most affected layers, i.e. static selection roughly doubles the error); frozen-signal counterfactuals attribute this almost entirely to the input-dependent write map Bt, not the timestep Δ that activity-based pruning relies on. A two-pass input-scheduled pruner beats static, modal-Hankel, and LAST rankings at every scale and matches the unpruned model at half state budget on held-out perplexity — but this is realizable headroom (the mask is read from a first pass), not a deployed saving.

## 核心贡献

- **精确的状态使用测量工具。** 利用 Mamba-1/Falcon-Mamba 中对角状态矩阵带来的模态解耦，将每个 (层, 通道, 窗口) 的输出精确分解为逐模态贡献，构造对称半正定的 Gram 张量 G_h，从而在离线、任意预算下，闭式给出剪除任意模态子集所引入的精确输出误差（公式 4）。在与参考实现对比中，重建相对误差在 Mamba-1 上为 2.3×10⁻⁷、Falcon-Mamba 上 2.6×10⁻⁷、Mamba-2 上 1.0×10⁻⁶；对实际运行模型剪枝后的输出误差预测中位相对偏差为 1×10⁻⁶（4,464 组配置）。
- **输入驱动的模态迁移现象。** 在 Mamba-1 全家族（130M–2.8B）、7B Falcon-Mamba 与 Mamba-2 上系统测量到：携带信号的模式集合随输入而变化。在最受影响的层，输入调度 oracle 的输出误差约为静态 top-r 集合的一半（迁移间隙 ρ ∈ [0.44, 0.57]）；跨域 churn 在 368/368 层中均超过域内 churn。
- **机制归因。** 通过把每个选择性信号逐一冻结到语料均值，证明迁移主要由输入依赖的写入映射 Bt 驱动（冻结后 Mamba-1 间隙 0.70→0.96、Mamba-2 0.77→0.96–0.97，churn 也随之坍塌），读出映射 Ct 次之，而被多数活动性剪枝方法所依赖的时间步 Δ 几乎不承载迁移。
- **压缩后果。** 两遍输入调度剪枝在相同预算下超越静态能量排序、模态汉克尔奇异值 (modal-HSV) 排序与 LAST 层自适应分配；在一半状态预算下，调度模型的 held-out 困惑度甚至在配对 bootstrap 下显著低于未剪枝模型（130M–7B 各尺度 CI 均排除 0）。在 LAMBADA 上，静态剪枝将 790M 准确率从 0.63 砸到 0.41，两遍 oracle 掩码可恢复该损失的 86%。

## Key Contributions

- **Exact per-mode decomposition and Gram tensor.** Because the Mamba-1/Falcon-Mamba state matrix A_h is diagonal, a channel's scalar output is an exact sum of per-mode contributions m_{h,t,i}; outer products over a window yield a small symmetric PSD Gram tensor G_h ∈ ℝ^{N×N} from which the exact squared output error of pruning any subset S follows in closed form (Eq. 4). The decomposition is exact, not approximate, and returns error at every budget from one reverse cumulative sum.
- **Dual validation to machine precision.** Reconstruction matches the reference implementation to ≤2.3×10⁻⁷ (Mamba-1), 2.6×10⁻⁷ (Falcon-Mamba), and 1.0×10⁻⁶ (Mamba-2); and the offline-predicted pruning error matches the running model's actual pruned-layer output error to a median relative deviation of 1×10⁻⁶ over 4,464 configurations spanning five models, every layer, six windows, and three budgets (max 3.6×10⁻⁵).
- **Discovery of input-driven mode migration.** The set of signal-carrying modes changes with the input. The migration gap ρ = E_diag/E_static falls to ~0.44–0.57 at the most affected layer of each model — an input-scheduled oracle roughly halves the error of a fixed set — present from Mamba-1 130M through 2.8B, in the deployed 7B Falcon-Mamba (0.48), and in Mamba-2 (0.44–0.57). A Proposition proves ρ ≤ 1 on the diagonal and ties the gap to an energy-weighted top-r churn (Spearman 0.80 vs. 0.74 for raw churn).
- **Mechanism via frozen-signal counterfactuals.** Freezing the write map B_t at its corpus mean removes most of the migration (Mamba-1 0.70→0.96, Mamba-2 0.77→0.96–0.97) and collapses cross-domain churn; keeping only B_t live reproduces most of it (ρ = 0.87), so B_t is both necessary and largely sufficient. The timestep Δ — the signal activity-based pruning keys on — leaves the gap unchanged (0.72 on Mamba-1). An energy-matched control rules out renormalization artifacts. Migration concentrates in the slow modes (top-r instability rises from ~0.3 to 0.78 in the slowest decile).
- **Compression consequence with honest scope.** A two-pass input-scheduled pruner outperforms static, modal-HSV, and LAST rankings at every scale to 7B; on held-out perplexity it falls below the unpruned model at half budget with a CI excluding zero (130M gap −0.54, shrinking to −0.04 at 7B). The authors explicitly frame this as realizable headroom (mask read from a first pass) and not a deployed compute/memory saving, and show every cheap out-of-window scheduler they tried (regime-conditioned 2–6%, 256-token prefix 2–9%, half-window 18–26%) falls well short.

## Methodology

The paper is a measurement/mechanism study built on a realization-theoretic reading of a Mamba layer. A Mamba-1 channel is rewritten as a bank of N decoupled first-order modes with fixed diagonal poles A_{h,i} < 0, written and read on a per-token schedule by selective signals B_t, C_t, and the timestep Δ_{h,t}. Because A_h is diagonal, the post-gate channel output y_{h,t} decomposes exactly into per-mode contributions m_{h,t,i} = g_{h,t} C_{t,i} x_{h,t,i} plus a skip term f_{h,t}. Summing outer products of these contributions over a length-L window gives a Gram matrix G_h whose quadratic form (Eq. 4) returns the exact squared output error of dropping any subset of modes — offline, at any budget, in one pass; for the nested top-r family under an ordering, error at every budget is a single reverse cumulative sum over G_h.

Three measures are read from G_h: (1) the **migration gap** ρ(r) = E_diag/E_static, the ratio of an in-sample per-window oracle's error to a fixed-set error (ρ ≈ 1 means static is fine, ρ ≪ 1 means the important set migrates); (2) **churn**, the Jaccard distance between top-r sets of different windows, split within vs. across domain against a half-window null; and (3) the **participation ratio** PR_h = (Σ e_{h,i})² / Σ e_{h,i}² as the channel's effective order at the operating point. A short Proposition proves ρ ≤ 1 on the diagonal and identifies the gap with the net energy of the modes that churn into the top-r set.

The mechanism is isolated by **frozen-signal counterfactuals**: rerun a layer's per-mode decomposition with one of B_t, C_t, or Δ frozen at its corpus mean and re-measure ρ and churn; an energy-matched control and a "keep only B_t live" variant act as controls. The compression consequence is measured by comparing four rules at matched budget — static realized-energy ranking, modal-HSV (Hankel singular value from empirical per-mode Gramians, instantiating the GHOST/LAST principle), LAST layer-adaptive allocation, and a two-pass input-scheduled oracle — plus a timestep-activity ranking and random sets predicted by the mechanism to fail. End-to-end pruning error is measured empirically because pruning shifts downstream layer inputs and the per-layer identity does not compose. The Mamba-2 case (scalar per-head decay + gated RMSNorm) breaks the exact offline identity past the norm, so there the same per-mode outputs and migration gap are computed online and the pruning error measured empirically.

Setup: released Mamba-1 (130M–2.8B), 7B Falcon-Mamba, two Mamba-2 checkpoints (130M, 780M); 1024-token windows, 32 per domain from three domains (English prose, code, technical text), disjoint 24-window-per-domain evaluation; budget r = 8 of N = 16 (Mamba-1/Falcon) and r = 64 of N = 128 (Mamba-2); all seeds fixed; reference fp32 path with fused kernels disabled; full campaign ~1 GPU-day on a single GB10 node; code at https://github.com/isrlab/selective-layer-audit.

## Results & Highlights

- **Exactness validation (reconstruction).** The reconstructed mixer output matches the reference implementation to a relative error of at most **2.3×10⁻⁷ on the Mamba-1 family**, **2.6×10⁻⁷ on Falcon-Mamba**, and **1.0×10⁻⁶ on Mamba-2** (whose pre-norm decomposition carries one further gated normalization).
- **Exactness validation (deployed pruning).** The offline-predicted pruning error matches the running model's actual pruned-layer output error to a **median relative deviation of 1×10⁻⁶ over 4,464 configurations** (five models, every layer, six windows, r ∈ {4, 8, 12}), maximum 3.6×10⁻⁵; reconstruction error at the pre-projection output is bounded by 6.9×10⁻⁶. (The abstract rounds this to 5×10⁻⁷.)
- **Migration gap.** At the most affected layer of each model the migration gap reaches **ρ ∈ [0.44, 0.57]** — i.e. a per-input oracle roughly halves the output error of a static top-r set at the same budget. Minimum ρ(8) is between 0.51 and 0.54 across the Mamba-1 family, **0.48 in the deployed 7B Falcon-Mamba**, and **0.44–0.57 in Mamba-2**. Cross-domain churn exceeds within-domain churn in **368 of 368 layers** across the eight models (median ratio 1.15–1.32).
- **Mechanism (Table 1).** Freezing the write map B_t removes the migration: band-median gap rises **0.70→0.96 on Mamba-1 130M** and **0.77→0.96–0.97 on Mamba-2** (130M and 780M), and cross-domain churn collapses. Freezing C_t lifts it partway (0.84); **freezing Δ leaves it essentially unchanged (0.72 on Mamba-1, 0.74–0.77 on Mamba-2)**. Keeping only B_t live reproduces most of the migration (ρ = 0.87); an energy-matched control leaves the freeze-B_t gap unchanged (0.964→0.968), ruling out a renormalization artifact. Top-r membership instability rises from a fast-mode baseline ~0.28 to **0.78 in the slowest decile** at both 130M and 790M.
- **Perplexity at half budget (Table 2).** Input-scheduled selection outperforms every static rule at every scale and falls below the unpruned model with a paired bootstrap CI excluding zero: e.g. **Falcon-7B unpruned 4.48, static 4.82, modal-HSV 4.96, LAST 5.27, scheduled 4.44 (gap CI [−0.06, −0.03])**; **130M unpruned 12.38, static 14.16, modal-HSV 14.52, LAST 25.78, scheduled 11.84 (CI [−0.66, −0.44])**. The margin over unpruned shrinks with scale from 0.54 (130M) to 0.04 (7B) perplexity. At quarter budget r = 4 on 130M: static 19.8, modal-HSV 20.8, LAST collapses to 234, scheduled stays at 14.2; timestep-activity and random reach perplexity **52.5 and 127** at r = 8 (the mechanism-predicted failures).
- **Native GHOST comparison (Table 4).** Released GHOST code on Mamba-2 at 50% budget exceeds the plain realized-energy static ranking under both its own (13.98 / 8.77) and the paper's calibration (13.86 / 8.71), and lies 2.98 (130M) and 1.44 (780M) perplexity above the scheduler (11.00 / 7.33).
- **Downstream tasks.** On LAMBADA, static pruning to r = 8 collapses Mamba-1 790M accuracy from **0.63 to 0.41**, and the two-pass oracle mask recovers **86% of the drop**; on PIQA and ARC-easy (which do not stress long-range state) pruning to half budget has almost no effect. A linear probe reads the input domain from a layer's mode energies at **99.7% mean accuracy** across all layers (shuffled-label control near chance), and on a synthetic two-regime switched linear system reaches **1.00** regime-readout accuracy.
- **Headroom is realizable but not cheaply captured.** Out-of-window schedulers recover only a fraction of the two-pass oracle's gain over static: regime-conditioned masks 2–6%, a 256-token prefix estimator 2–9% (5–9% after a learned per-mode ramp-up correction), half-window selection 18–26% (Table 3: half-window recovers 25% at 130M, 18% at 790M and does not beat the unpruned model). The mask must be read from the tokens being scored.

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author paper by **Raktim Bhattacharya**, Aerospace Engineering, Texas A&M University. The author's systems-theory background shows in the rigorous LPV/quasi-LPV framing, the explicit invocation of balanced truncation, Hankel singular values, and time-varying model reduction (Moore 1981; Glover 1984; Sandberg & Rantzer 2004; Shokoohi et al. 1983), and a short formal Proposition with proof. The work is unusually careful about scope: it explicitly states the exact instrument applies only to the per-channel diagonal case (Mamba-1, Falcon-Mamba), handles Mamba-2's scalar-per-head decay and gated RMSNorm as a stated exception, pre-registers a 1.5× churn-ratio threshold that the pilot (1.28×) did not meet and discloses it, reports strict continuation scoring rather than favorable prefix scoring, and is explicit that the scheduled result is headroom, not a deployed saving. Public released checkpoints, fixed seeds, fp32 reference path with fused kernels disabled, and code reproducing every table/figure/figure at https://github.com/isrlab/selective-layer-audit. Full campaign ~1 GPU-day on a single GB10 node. No industry affiliation or compute-intensive arms race; the contribution is analytical and measurement-driven.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine. Existing criteria (GHOST's output-aware score, PerfMamba's activity-based criterion, LAST's layer-adaptive Hankel threshold, balanced truncation for S4) all score modes once and keep a fixed set; this is the first to give an exact, per-input, per-(layer, channel, window) measure of which modes a trained layer is actually using, and the first to quantify and mechanistically attribute mode migration. The framing as a measurement instrument (rather than yet another pruner) is a clean and defensible contribution.
- **Rigor:** High and self-policing. The dual validation (reconstruction + deployed-pruning cross-check over 4,464 configs) is the right design, and the author discloses the floor set by reconstruction error rather than overclaiming. The frozen-signal counterfactuals include an energy-matched control and a "keep only B_t live" sufficiency check, and the same-window vs. half-window control isolates the sub-unpruned margin as same-window adaptation rather than a transferable denoising effect. Pre-registering a 1.5× churn threshold and reporting that the pilot only reached 1.28× is a good-faith signal.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong. All public checkpoints, fp32 reference path, fixed seed 0, ~1 GPU-day total, public code repo. The author even reverse-engineered a silent no-op in the released GHOST pipeline (calibration hooks that the fused kernels bypass) and forced the reference path in a wrapper while leaving GHOST's code unchanged.
- **Significance:** Conceptually significant for interpretability (a direct read of which internal degrees of freedom a layer stores, per input) and consequential for the pruning literature because it shows the static-set assumption shared by GHOST/PerfMamba/LAST/balanced-truncation lines leaves error on the table that moves with the input. Practically tempered: the headline compression wins are oracle/two-pass and the author does not claim deployed savings, and every cheap scheduler tried captures only 2–26% of the oracle's gain.
- **Red flags / specific concerns:**
  - The **sub-unpruned margin** (scheduled perplexity below the unpruned model at half budget) is a red flag for any reader expecting a free lunch: the control shows it is same-window adaptation, not transferable, and it shrinks toward zero at scale (0.04 at 7B). A casual reader could mistake Table 2 for a deployed compression result.
  - The migration effect is **modest in raw churn terms** (cross/within median ratio 1.15–1.32, below the pre-registered 1.5×); the case for migration rests primarily on the gap ρ and the frozen-B_t counterfactual rather than churn alone, which the author acknowledges.
  - **Generalization beyond the audited models is limited**: exactness is specific to the per-channel diagonal case; the depth geometry (contiguous mid-network band with a static tail) is explicitly a Mamba-1 finding, not universal; and the downstream evaluation only goes to 1024 tokens, so behavior at much longer contexts — where slow-mode migration should matter most — is untested.
  - The single-author, non-industry provenance is not a red flag per se, but it means the validation rests on one pipeline; the 2.3×10⁻⁷ reconstruction number is only as trustworthy as the reference-implementation comparison it is measured against.
  - The native GHOST comparison (Table 4) is on Mamba-2 only and could turn on calibration-data specifics, though the author mitigates this by recalibrating GHOST on the paper's own windows with nearly unchanged results.

## Strengths

- An actually exact measurement instrument, not another heuristic — the diagonal structure makes the Gram identity (Eq. 4) exact, and the dual validation is to machine precision.
- Clean, mechanism-grounded story: the frozen-signal counterfactuals give a causal attribution (B_t carries the migration; Δ does not) that directly contradicts the assumption behind activity-based pruning.
- Intellectual honesty about scope: exact only for the diagonal case; scheduled result is headroom, not deployed saving; pre-registered and missed churn threshold disclosed; strict scoring conventions adopted.
- Breadth across the model zoo: Mamba-1 130M–2.8B, deployed 7B Falcon-Mamba, and Mamba-2 130M/780M, so the phenomenon is shown to be a property of the selective-SSM design rather than one checkpoint.
- Cheap to reproduce (~1 GPU-day) with public code and public checkpoints.

## Limitations

- Exactness is restricted to the per-channel diagonal case (Mamba-1, Falcon-Mamba); Mamba-2's scalar-per-head decay plus gated RMSNorm breaks the offline Gram identity past the norm, so there the migration gap is computed online and pruning error measured empirically.
- The two-pass scheduled result is realizable headroom; the author explicitly claims no deployed compute or memory saving, and every inexpensive scheduler tried (regime-conditioned, prefix, half-window) captures only a small fraction of the oracle's gain.
- Depth geometry is architecture-specific: the contiguous mid-network band with a static tail is a Mamba-1 finding; larger Mamba-1, Falcon-Mamba, and Mamba-2 migrate in punctuated layers.
- Downstream evaluation is limited to ≤1024-token tasks; the effect on much longer contexts, where slow-mode migration should be strongest, is untested.
- Churn effect is modest in absolute terms (1.15–1.32×, below the pre-registered 1.5×); the migration case rests on the gap ρ and the frozen-B_t counterfactual rather than churn alone.
- A learned scheduler that predicts the mask ahead of the tokens it prunes — the thing that would turn headroom into a real system — remains open future work.

## Relevance

与对长序列建模、状态空间模型（Mamba 系）及模型可解释性/压缩的兴趣高度相关：它给出了首个精确、逐输入的状态使用测量工具，并揭示"哪些模态重要"会随输入迁移这一新现象，直接挑战 GHOST/PerfMamba/LAST 等静态剪枝方法的共同假设，对后续 SSM 解释性与高效部署研究有指引价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can a cheap learned scheduler (e.g., a small predictor network or a recurrent mask predictor) actually anticipate the per-window top-r set from a short prefix well enough to turn the two-pass headroom into a real deployed saving, and how does its accuracy degrade with context length?
- Does input-driven mode migration amplify, disappear, or change mechanism at the multi-thousand-token contexts where the slow modes are supposedly most active — and does it interact with retrieval/in-context-learning behavior?
- The instrument is exact only for the per-channel diagonal case; can a comparable (even if approximate) per-mode error bound be derived for Mamba-2's gated RMSNorm, or for the structured (non-diagonal) state matrices of S4/S5/Diagno/OPT architectures?
- The frozen-B_t result implies activity-based pruning (PerfMamba) is scoring the wrong signal; can B_t-magnitude or B_t-conditional energy be used as a better static importance score, and does it close the gap to realized-energy static ranking?
- Migration concentrates in slow modes and in a depth-localized band — is the band's location predictable from pretraining statistics, and can it be regularized during training to either reduce migration (cheaper static pruning) or amplify it (more exploitable headroom)?
- The domain probe reads the input domain from mode energies at 99.7% accuracy; is the regime signature actually doing computational work (causally tied to downstream loss), or is it a readable but functionally incidental correlate?
