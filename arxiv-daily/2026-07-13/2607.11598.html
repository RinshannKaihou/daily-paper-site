metric-on-metric risk
01

Methodology — a budget-aware proposer–reviewer loop

Pure scaffolding around a single frozen frontier model (Sonnet 4, temp 0). No fine-tuning, no retrieval, no learned controller. The loop can never score below single-shot — it keeps the best iteration.

  • Loop. Proposer → instrument executes/renders/measures → reviewer converts the grounded signal into a structured defect list → proposer revises; early-stop on already-passing tasks.
  • Three grounded instruments. execution (pytest + traceback), rendered geometry (headless DOM bounding-boxes: overlap ≥6 px, overflow >16 px, clipping, misalignment), native video (Gemini 3.1 Pro whole-clip rubric).
  • Seven modalities. code (15+11), figures (20), slides (12), web (20), animations (20), video (15), deep research (15).
  • Stats. Paired (task, seed) over 3 seeds; two-sided paired sign tests + paired-bootstrap 95% CIs on effect sizes.
Internal ceiling (Appendix A). An ungrounded reviewer forms A⋆ → (Θ, X) → A → R, so I(A⋆; R) ≤ I(A⋆; (Θ, X)) — self-revision recovers no more information than the frozen weights already contain. Grounded feedback E = g(A, world) escapes it because I(A⋆; (R, E)) can exceed I(A⋆; (Θ, X)).

VLM-on-screenshot is structurally blind. On 15 dense figures the VLM judge rates 14/15 "perfect"; the deterministic DOM instrument finds only 3/15 actually clean. As a reviewer, the VLM makes slide defects worse (1.89 → 2.44) while the geometric reviewer drops them to 0.33.

03

Strengths & Limitations

+ Strengths

  • Falsifiable thesis (two-sided grounding + coverage principle) with three predictions, each backed by a dedicated experiment.
  • Oracle best-of-N using ground-truth tests is the strongest internal-scaling baseline — the saturation result is hard to dismiss.
  • Two saturated modalities reported honestly as negatives.
  • Cross-model (Sonnet 4 / Qwen3-235B / GPT-5) + cross-proposer (Gemini 3.1 Pro) replication, plus a post-hoc held-out suite.
  • "Variance is a budget" distillation finding is genuinely useful and non-obvious.
  • Data-processing inequality gives the saturation claim a principled backbone.

− Limitations

  • Geometry instrument both feeds back and scores — visual-defect cuts vulnerable to "optimizing the metric"; human-preference study is the missing validation.
  • Deterministic geometry only fits artifacts with a measurable intended layout; flowing content still relies on fragile VLM/binary rubrics.
  • Hard code suite is tiny (15 tasks) — a few points = one task; some headline gaps rest on a single seed.
  • Static-form coverage validated only on its null prediction, not a positive one.
  • All runs use closed frontier models on curated suites; open-ended / agentic / long-horizon generalization untested.
  • No fully grounded instrument exists for video or deep research — the framework's reach is bounded by instrument availability.
04

Open questions

  1. Does grounding reduce to "only possible where an instrument exists" for modalities with none (audio, 3D, UI flows) — capping the third axis?
  2. How much of the visual-defect reduction maps onto actual human-preferred quality? A human study across the four modalities is the obvious next experiment.
  3. Can the internal ceiling be tightened from an information bound into a quantitative coverage–quality relationship that predicts achievable quality before running?
  4. Would a much larger best-of-N budget (10× tokens, K in the hundreds) narrow the code gap — i.e., is interaction's win mostly efficiency at extreme sampling budgets?
  5. RFT-on-student lowers variance and hurts best-of-N; does this imply RLHF/RLAIF generally trades off against sampling-scaling headroom?
  6. Is the VLM structural blindness specific to geometry, or general to other "physical" dimensions a screenshot drops (sub-acuity contrast, timing defects, a11y-tree violations)?
