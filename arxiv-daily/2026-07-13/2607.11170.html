01

Methodology — base-anchored bounded fusion

Three z-normalized evidence maps → one shared, provably-convex pixel-level rule. Base always active; auxiliary correction upper-bounded.
E1 · ANCHOR
Multimodal base
Dual frozen WRN-50-2 (RGB + geometry, 288²), layer2/3 → 256-d, 10% coreset bank, k-NN (k=3). Trained 20 epochs, synthetic-anomaly p=0.7.
E2 · AUX
Dinomaly (RGB recon)
DINOv2 ViT-S/14 reconstruction at 448². Subtle-appearance maps whose reliability varies by category — hence bounded.
E3 · CROSS
CMC consistency
Bidirectional residual-MLP mappers (RGB↔Geo); reconstruction inconsistency, robustly normalized by training median + MAD.

TDC Training-Dispersion Confidence

  • Strictly decreasing in normal-training dispersion of Dinomaly scores.
  • Lower dispersion ⇒ Dinomaly more confident on normals ⇒ higher auxiliary weight.
  • Uses only normal training — fits the no-anomaly-validation industrial setting.
  • Two shared constants fixed at deployment: μ̂=0.000978, σ̂=0.000291.

G Disagreement gate

  • G(x) = γ·c_d·σ(Δ−τ), with γ=0.6, τ=1.
  • Δ = |M_dino − M_base| — spatially releases more correction where base & Dinomaly disagree.
  • Because γ<1, c_d∈(0,1), auxiliary correction is explicitly upper-bounded.
  • All hyperparameters shared across all 10 classes; no per-class formula or threshold tuning.
Fusion rule — Eq. 4 · convex pointwise combination (α = 0.15) M_fused = (1−α)(1−G)·M_base + (1−α)G·M_dino + α·M_cross G(x) = γ·c_d·σ(Δ−τ),   Δ = |M_dino − M_base|,   τ=1,  γ=0.6 c_d = σ(−(σ_train − μ̂)/σ̂)  // lower dispersion ⇒ higher aux weight // coefficients ≥ 0 and sum to 1 ⇒ base always active, auxiliary bounded. // image score s = mean( top-0.5%( M_fused ) )

03

Strengths & limitations

Honest effect-size reporting against a saturated benchmark.

PRO Strengths

  • Honest effect-size reporting — explicitly labels TDC's gain "modest" (~0.85pp) and credits fusion structure as dominant.
  • Principled convex formulation — coefficients provably non-negative & sum-to-one; base always active; auxiliary bounded by γ·c_d.
  • Comprehensive ablation — evidence sources, fusion alternatives, calibration variants, missing modality, few-shot, component substitution, cross-dataset, hyperparameter sweep.
  • Strong localization — 0.990 P-AUPRO / 0.996 P-AUROC, SOTA-tier; holds on Eyecandies (0.954).
  • Rule generalizes — same formula helps under ResAD and ResNet-18 swaps.

CON Limitations

  • Marginal detection gain — +0.1pp I-AUROC over 3DSR, no variance/CI; within plausible noise on a saturated benchmark.
  • TDC is small & mixed — ~0.79–0.85pp average, some classes hurt; a category-dependent calibrator, not uniform uplift.
  • Struggles with diffuse anomalies (Cookie, Foam, Rope) — acknowledged, unresolved.
  • Weak calibration strawmen — no held-out-normal-validation calibrator tested, only constant/random/pooled.
  • Non-trivial cost — 1804 MB, 59.6 ms, 16.8 FPS (two heavyweight evidence branches).
  • Curated comparison field — 3D-ADNAS only on Eyecandies; known-category protocol only.
04

Open questions

What would need to hold — or break — for the central claims.
Q1 · SignificanceDoes the +0.1pp I-AUROC lead survive a multi-seed evaluation with CIs, and were baselines re-run under the identical protocol or transcribed?
Q2 · GeneralityHow does TC-MAF behave under unknown-category inference, where per-class σ_train and normalization stats are unavailable at test time?
Q3 · TDC necessityCould a held-out normal-validation calibrator match or beat TDC — weakening the case for the dispersion proxy?
Q4 · Failure modeWhy does the bounded gate underperform on diffuse anomalies (Cookie/Foam/Rope)? Would a multi-scale or region-aggregated disagreement signal recover them?
Q5 · Beyond RGB+3DDoes the design transfer to RGB+thermal or multi-camera industrial settings where auxiliary-branch reliability is also category-dependent?
Q6 · ScalingIs the convex/bounded rule still optimal when >3 evidence sources are fused, or does the γ-cap become a bottleneck?
