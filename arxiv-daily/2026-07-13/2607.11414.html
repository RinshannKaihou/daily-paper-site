Methodology, in brief

A linear readout of the residual stream at ⅔ network depth, mean-pooled over the generated answer's tokens, captured in a single forward pass and fit by l2-regularized (C=0.1) class-balanced logistic regression against the binary correctness label.

Each model (Qwen3-8B, Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Gemma-2-9B-it) answers a financial-filing question via greedy chain-of-thought, emitting ANSWER: <number>. Correctness comes from a tolerance grader (τ=0.02, scale set covers %/decimal and k/millions; sign errors never forgiven), audited by an LLM judge at 90–93% agreement and 4–6% label noise.

Confidence is behavioral: c(x) = mean match over k=8 temperature-0.7 resamples; "confident" = unanimous 8/8. Evaluation is 5-fold, document-grouped, class-stratified, scored out-of-fold and population-reweighted.

  • Transferable design rule: ⅔-depth + mean-pool hits the per-family optimum for two models and costs the third ≤ 0.013 AUROC — no per-family tuning.
  • Validity controls above the norm: random-init placebo (0.625 reference, not 0.5), document-grouped CV (prevents up to 0.04 AUROC TAT-QA leakage), label-noise sensitivity audit.
  • Abstention control: removing refusals shifts the Δ by only ~0.01 — the probe is not just flagging "I don't know."
  • Cross-task transfer fails: FinQA↔TAT-QA costs 0.07–0.16 AUROC and usually drops below the target's own baselines — the monitor is task-specific.
  • Data: 6,105 FinQA + 1,138 TAT-QA numeric questions after length filtering (98% / 68% of full sets).
04

Strengths & limitations

+ Strengths

  • Deployment-fair baselines. Only compares against signals a production system can actually afford — the +0.13–0.15 gap is not a straw-man artifact.
  • Cross-family design rule. ⅔ depth + mean-pool transfers without per-family tuning — directly actionable.
  • Unusually strong validity controls. Placebo, document-grouped CV, audited grader, label-noise audit guard against the artifacts known to inflate this subfield.
  • Honest negatives. Cross-task transfer fails; probe loses on unsure TAT-QA; abstention removal does not explain the gap.
  • Concrete operational translation: triage curves, cascade design, a 20%-budget worked example.

− Limitations

  • Stratification tension. The 8-way self-consistency confidence splitter is itself the expensive signal the paper excludes from the "cheap" set.
  • White-box only. Needs residual-stream access — not applicable to third-party API deployments (most real financial use).
  • Narrow model band. Three models, all 8B–9B; no 70B+, no smaller models, no other families.
  • Narrow answer shape. Numeric, single-turn only; no free-text, multi-turn, or reasoning-mode traces (Qwen3 thinking mode disabled).
  • Task-specific. A new probe must be trained per task; no code/data release mentioned.
05

Open questions

  1. Can a cheap probe-based confidence replace the 8-generation stratifier and close the deployment-cost gap between stratification and detection?
  2. Does the confident-quadrant edge hold on 70B+ models and native reasoning-mode traces, where intermediate computation may dilute the residual-stream signal?
  3. How does the probe behave on free-text answers where the 2%-tolerance grader is unavailable — can it train from weak / LLM-judge labels?
  4. Is the cross-task transfer failure (0.07–0.16 AUROC) fundamental to numeric reasoning, or would a joint multi-task probe close the gap?
  5. What is the cost-benefit frontier of a learned cascade (probe + P(True) + logprobs) versus the rank-fusion cascade reported here?
