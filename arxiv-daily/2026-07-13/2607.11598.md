> **一句话总结：** 作者将 test-time compute 归纳为三条轴（reasoning、sampling、interaction），证明前两者因"internal ceiling"（数据处理不等式）必然饱和，唯有"接地（grounded）"的外部仪器观测才能突破上限；在固定 token 预算下，proposer-reviewer 交互环把硬代码任务从 66.7% 拉到 100% pass rate（零 seed 方差），并在四种视觉模态上消除 40–74% 的真实布局缺陷——但前提是反馈和评估两侧都必须用确定性的 DOM/执行仪器，而非看截图的 VLM（后者会把 14/15 个坏图评为"perfect"）。

> **TL;DR:** This paper frames test-time compute as three axes — reasoning, sampling, and a proposed third axis, *interaction* — and argues via the data-processing inequality that the first two ("internal") saturate because they only re-draw from the same frozen weights and prompt, while interaction escapes the ceiling by importing a real instrument observation each cycle. The load-bearing variable is *grounding*, and it must hold on both sides of the loop. At a matched 20K-token budget, the frozen-model proposer–reviewer harness lifts the 15-task hard code suite from a 66.7% single-shot pass rate to a strict 100% (zero seed variance), beating oracle best-of-N's 86.7% ceiling and reasoning-only's 73.3%; the gain replicates across Sonnet 4 (+33.3 pp), Qwen3-235B (+22.2 pp), and GPT-5 (+20.0 pp). On four visual modalities (figures, slides, web, animations), a deterministic DOM-geometry instrument removes 40–74% of real layout defects, whereas the standard VLM-on-screenshot judge rates 14/15 visibly broken figures "perfect" and, used as the reviewer, makes slide layouts *worse*. Two modalities (video editing, deep research) are honestly reported as saturated negatives.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"交互"作为 test-time compute 的第三条轴**：与 reasoning（多想）和 sampling（多采）并列。前两者是"internal"的，每个 token 都来自同一组冻结权重和固定 prompt，受数据处理不等式约束必然饱和；interaction 把产物交给外部仪器（测试运行器、布局引擎、搜索引擎），将真实观测回灌到下一轮生成，因此能合成模型凭自身采样永远写不出的候选解。
- **提出"接地（grounding）+ 覆盖（coverage）"框架**：反馈分两类——未接地（模型自身意见，含看截图的 VLM）与接地（仪器观测）。给出覆盖原则：接地反馈的帮助范围恰好等于该仪器能观测到的属性范围（linter 看不到运行时 bug；截图把画框外溢出裁掉，VLM 自然看不见）。
- **指出评估侧也必须接地**：这是被普遍忽视的一半。质量是可测物理属性（渲染几何）时，截图判官不是"有噪声"而是"结构性失明"——缺陷在判官看到之前就被观测通道丢弃了。
- **饱和实验**：在固定 token 预算下，reasoning-only 和 oracle best-of-N 都在 ~73–87% 触顶，而所有交互策略继续爬升到 ≥97.8%，proposer-reviewer 在三 seed 上全部达到 100%（零方差）。
- **反馈通道对照实验**：固定任务集只换 reviewer 的反馈源——执行反馈（14/15）比未接地 critique（13/15）便宜约 2.5 倍且迭代更少；linter（接地但只看形式）在运行时 bug 上与纯 critique 完全一样（覆盖原则的干净验证）；VLM reviewer 反而让 slide 缺陷增加。
- **跨模型与外样本泛化**：效应在 Sonnet 4 / Qwen3-235B / GPT-5 三个家族上均复现；在方法定稿后新建的 32 任务外样本集上仍恢复全部单射失败、零回退。
- **蒸馏与方差即预算的发现**：把 teacher 轨迹蒸馏进 8B Qwen3-VL 学生，单样本恢复 teacher 能力的 0.51×、两样本 0.70×，部署成本约低 10×；并发现 RFT 提升一致性却让 pass@3 回退 17 pp——"方差是 sampling 的预算，不是要消除的噪声"。

## Key Contributions

- **Interaction as a third axis of test-time compute.** Reasoning (more thinking) and sampling (more attempts) are both *internal*: every token is a function of the same frozen weights and prompt, so by the data-processing inequality they saturate. Interaction is *external* — the model hands its artifact to an instrument (test runner, layout engine, search index) and feeds the observation back, importing information the weights never had.
- **The grounding + coverage framework.** Feedback is either ungrounded (model opinion, including a VLM reading a screenshot) or grounded (an instrument observation). The coverage principle predicts that a grounded channel helps exactly as far as its instrument can observe: a linter cannot see a runtime bug; a screenshot crops off the defect at issue.
- **Evaluation must be grounded too (the overlooked half).** Where quality is a measurable physical property (rendered geometry), a screenshot judge is *structurally blind*, not merely noisy — the defect is dropped by its observation channel before judgment begins.
- **Saturation experiment.** At a matched 20K-token budget, reasoning-only and oracle best-of-N saturate (73.3% and 86.7%) while every interaction strategy climbs past them; the proposer–reviewer harness reaches a strict 100% with zero seed variance.
- **Feedback-channel controls.** Holding the suite fixed and swapping only the reviewer's signal: execution feedback (14/15) beats bare critique (13/15) at ~2.5× lower token cost; a linter (grounded but surface-form-only) matches bare critique on runtime bugs; a screenshot-fed VLM reviewer makes slide geometry *worse*.
- **Cross-model replication and held-out generalization.** The code loop replicates across Sonnet 4, Qwen3-235B, and GPT-5, and on a 32-task held-out set built after the method was fixed recovers every first-shot failure with no regressions.
- **Distillation and the "variance is a budget" finding.** An 8B student internalizes ~0.51× (mean@1) / 0.70× (pass@2) of teacher capability at ~10× lower cost; RFT improves per-turn consistency yet regresses pass@3 by 17 pp, showing that distributional spread is the resource best-of-N converts into quality.

## Methodology

The core artifact is a **budget-aware proposer–reviewer harness** built as pure scaffolding around a single frozen frontier model (Claude Sonnet 4, `claude-sonnet-4-20250514`, temperature 0 unless noted): no fine-tuning, no retrieval, no learned controller.

- **Loop.** A *proposer* generates an artifact; an *instrument* executes, renders, or measures it to produce a grounded signal; a *reviewer* (the same model in a diagnostic role) converts that signal into a structured defect list; the proposer revises. The loop runs up to an iteration cap and keeps the best-scoring iteration, so the reviewed result can never score below single-shot under the same metric. Built-in early stopping means already-passing single-shot tasks are submitted at iteration 1 with no wasted tokens.
- **Three grounded instruments.** (1) **Execution** — pytest pass/fail plus failing assertion and traceback; the deep-spec suite is additionally validated against a reference implementation. (2) **Rendered geometry** — the artifact is rendered headless and every element's true bounding box is read from the layout engine, computing exactly: text-on-text overlap (≥6 px), out-of-bounds/clipping, container overflow, document overflow (>16 px), and box-group misalignment; web pages scored at desktop and mobile widths, animations per sampled frame. (3) **Native video** — Gemini 3.1 Pro's whole-clip binary rubric (near-grounded, observing pixels not a still). Where no deterministic instrument exists (flowing web content, factual research) the authors fall back to a binary per-requirement rubric, explicitly flagged.
- **Seven modalities** (Table 1): code (15 + 11 deep-spec tasks), academic figures (20), slides (12 real-paper), web pages (20), animations (20 SVG/CSS), video editing (15), deep research (15). All artifacts are produced under a common design-principle generation prompt (proximity, alignment, repetition, contrast, deliberate color) so single-shot quality is already strong and measured headroom reflects genuine difficulty.
- **Protocol.** Each modality is evaluated over three independent seeds; comparisons are paired on (task, seed). Statistics: two-sided paired sign tests over decisive pairs plus paired-bootstrap 95% CIs on effect sizes.
- **Theory.** The internal ceiling is formalized via the data-processing inequality (Appendix A): an ungrounded reviewer forms the Markov chain A⋆ → (Θ, X) → A → R, so I(A⋆; R) ≤ I(A⋆; (Θ, X)) — no amount of self-revision recovers more information than the weights already contain. Best-of-N is bounded separately: even an oracle verifier can only select among candidates the proposer actually draws. Grounded feedback E = g(A, world) escapes both because I(A⋆; (R, E)) can exceed I(A⋆; (Θ, X)).
- **Distillation.** Judge-filtered teacher trajectories are distilled by SFT into an 8B Qwen3-VL student; an RFT variant is also studied to probe consistency vs. sampling variance.

## Results & Highlights

- **Code (hard suite, 15 tasks, 3-seed mean, matched 20K budget):** single-shot **66.7%** pass rate → proposer–reviewer harness **100.0%** (±0.0 pp, zero seed variance); oracle best-of-N ceiling **86.7%**; reasoning-only ceiling **73.3%**. The oracle best-of-N ceiling is the sharpest comparison because its verifier is the ground-truth test suite, i.e. effectively pass@N — yet it still plateaus ~13 pp below the harness (Table 2).
- **Code (deep-spec suite, 11 from-scratch tasks):** single-shot **69.7%** → reviewed **100.0%**, two-sided sign test **p = 0.002** (Figure 6).
- **Held-out v2 (32 tasks built after method was fixed):** single-shot **90.6%** → **100.0%**, +9.4 pp, recovering 3/3 single-shot failures with zero regressions (Table 8).
- **Cross-model replication (15 hard tasks, 3 seeds/family):** reviewed pass rate reaches **100.0% / 93.3% / 100.0%** with **+33.3 pp / +22.2 pp / +20.0 pp** lifts for Sonnet 4 / Qwen3-235B-Instruct-2507 / GPT-5 respectively, with zero reviewed-bar seed variance for all three families (Table 7).
- **Feedback-channel control (fixed code suite, one pass):** execution feedback reaches **14/15** at **~2,643 tokens/task and 1.53 iterations**, vs. ungrounded critique and critique+linter both at **13/15** at **~7,100 tokens/task and 2.07 iterations** — execution is ~2.5× cheaper, and the linter buys nothing over bare critique on runtime-logic bugs (Table 4).
- **Visual modalities — grounded geometric feedback (one identical config, 3 seeds):** mean defect reduction **figures −74%** (0.57 → 0.15, p=7×10⁻⁴, CI [52, 89]%); **dense slides −73%** (1.25 → 0.33, p=1.8×10⁻³, CI [45, 93]%); **web pages −47%** (16.1 → 8.5, p=4×10⁻⁸, CI [30, 62]%); **animations −40%** (16.9 → 10.2, p=3×10⁻⁵, CI [10, 64]%) — every bootstrap CI excludes zero (Table 6).
- **The default VLM judge is structurally blind:** on 15 dense academic-paper figures the VLM-on-screenshot judge rates **14/15** "perfect," while the deterministic DOM-geometry instrument finds only **3/15** actually clean (Figure 11).
- **VLM-as-reviewer makes things worse:** on slides a screenshot-fed VLM reviewer moves defects to **2.44** (worse than its own 1.89 single-shot baseline), while the geometric reviewer drops to **0.33** (−73%, p=0.0018); on figures the geometric reviewer removes **74%** (p=7×10⁻⁴) where the VLM reviewer is directionally worse (Figure 10).
- **Cross-proposer replication on slides:** with Gemini 3.1 Pro as proposer scored by the same model-free DOM instrument, the effect reproduces and is larger — **−93%** vs. Sonnet's −73%, 19/20 decisive task-runs improved.
- **Honest saturated negatives:** video editing (scored by Gemini 3.1 Pro's native rubric) is already strong single-shot so the reviewed lift is small and not significant; deep research saturates at **0.98 single-shot factual accuracy**, leaving no headroom for feedback (Figure 2c).
- **Distillation:** an 8B Qwen3-VL student recovers **0.51×** of teacher capability at mean@1 and **0.70×** at pass@2 on a 44-task OOD suite, at ~10× lower deployment cost (Table 12); on the teacher's pre-curated 18 hard failures it reaches **44% (pass@1) / 56% (pass@3)** (Table 11). RFT improves per-turn consistency metrics but regresses pass@3 by **17 pp** (39% vs. 56% for SFT), confirming "variance is a budget."

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are **Bojie Li** (Pine AI) and **Noah Shi** (University of Washington). The work is a position-plus-empirics paper rather than a large-lab benchmark effort: the methodology is unusually disciplined for this genre — paired (task, seed) comparisons, three seeds, two-sided paired sign tests, paired-bootstrap 95% CIs on effect sizes, an oracle best-of-N baseline that uses the *ground-truth* test suite as its verifier (so its ceiling is genuinely pass@N), a held-out suite constructed after the method was fixed, and cross-model (Anthropic/Alibaba/OpenAI) plus cross-proposer replication. Code is released at https://github.com/19PINE-AI/interaction-scaling and a website at https://01.me/research/interaction-scaling. The paper is unusually forthright about its two saturated modalities (video, deep research), reporting them as scoped negatives rather than manufactured wins. One transparency caveat noted in the acknowledgements: the paper was produced via Pine Copilot's voice-directed "whisper coding" workflow with Claude Code/Opus 4.8 carrying out the experiments and writing.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The "interaction as third axis" framing is a reframing of known practice (Reflexion, ReAct, tool use, execution feedback) rather than a new mechanism; the genuine conceptual contributions are (a) the two-sided grounding requirement, (b) the coverage principle, and (c) the sharp claim that a VLM-on-screenshot judge is *structurally blind* (defect dropped by the channel) rather than merely biased — a distinction the related work on model-as-judge biases does not isolate.
- **Rigor:** High on the code experiments (exact instrument, oracle verifier, held-out set, three model families). The statistical apparatus is real. However, the **feedback-channel pass-rate gap on code (14/15 vs. 13/15) rests on a single leaner-budget seed** and the authors themselves flag this ("we read it cautiously") — the decisive argument there is token cost, not pass rate.
- **Reproducibility:** Reasonable. Frozen closed models (Sonnet 4, GPT-5, Gemini 3.1 Pro, Qwen3-235B) and code is public, but the deterministic DOM-geometry thresholds (≥6 px overlap, >16 px overflow) and the per-modality task suites (especially the 12 "real-paper" slides and 15 deep-research tasks) are author-curated and not all released in a standardized form, so reproducing the visual-defect numbers exactly will depend on that instrument code.
- **Significance:** The practical lesson ("measure the rendered DOM, never a screenshot, on both reviewer and scorer") is cheap, deployable, and likely correct, and it directly explains why a lot of "self-improving visual generation" measured by VLM judges may be measuring noise. The ByteDance deployment anecdote (Hong 2026: functional correctness >80% but deliverability 40–60% until harness infrastructure added) is a nice field corroboration but is a second-hand keynote citation, not a re-run experiment.
- **Red flags / data quirks:**
  - The geometry instrument **both drives revision and scores the result**, so the reported defect reductions are *partly an optimization of the metric itself*. The authors argue this away (conservative thresholds, non-trivial magnitude, and the VLM-reviewer ablation that moves the same metric the wrong way), but explicitly acknowledge a quantitative human-preference validation is still future work — so the human-visible meaning of "−74% defects" is not yet confirmed.
  - The 15-task "hard" code suite is small, and several numbers coincide suspiciously (e.g., the 86.7% reasoning figure matches the oracle best-of-N ceiling; the authors call these out, but they complicate reading).
  - The static-form tier is confirmed only on its *null* prediction (linter buys nothing on runtime bugs); a positive static-form lift (e.g., mypy on type-confusion bugs) is admitted future work, so coverage is half-validated.
  - Best-of-N is run with K limited by the same 20K budget, which structurally caps pass@N; a larger-N baseline at higher budget could narrow (though not close, by the information argument) the gap.

## Strengths

- Clean, falsifiable thesis (grounding on both sides, coverage principle) with three explicit predictions, each backed by a dedicated experiment.
- Oracle best-of-N using the ground-truth tests is the strongest possible internal-scaling baseline — the saturation result is hard to dismiss as a weak comparator.
- Honest reporting of two saturated modalities as negatives rather than spinning them as wins.
- Strong cross-model and cross-proposer replication (Sonnet 4 / Qwen3-235B / GPT-5; Gemini 3.1 Pro as proposer), and a held-out suite built post-hoc.
- The "variance is a budget" distillation finding (RFT regresses pass@3) is a genuinely useful, non-obvious practical result.
- Information-theoretic framing (data-processing inequality) gives the saturation claim a principled backbone rather than pure empirics.

## Limitations

- The geometric instrument both feeds back and scores, making the visual-defect reductions vulnerable to a "optimizing the reported metric" critique; a human-preference study is the missing validation.
- Deterministic geometry applies only to artifacts with a measurable intended layout; genuinely flowing content and factual claims still rely on (acknowledged-fragile) VLM/binary rubrics.
- The hard code suite is tiny (15 tasks), so several percentage points correspond to single-task differences; per-seed noise is real and some headline gaps rest on one seed.
- The static-form coverage tier is validated only on its null prediction, not a positive one.
- All experiments use closed frontier models at temperature 0 (code) or 0.7 (cross-model), with curated task suites; generalization to open-ended, agentic, or long-horizon tasks is untested.
- No single fully grounded instrument exists for video or deep research, so those modalities cannot exhibit the effect even in principle — the framework's reach is bounded by instrument availability, which the authors flag as the main future direction.

## Relevance

对研究 test-time compute scaling、agent 自我修正与 model-as-judge 评估方法论高度相关：它用信息论把"为什么 self-correction 经常无效、为什么 tool-use feedback 有效"讲清楚了，并给出可立即落地的工程结论（评估和反馈都该用确定性 DOM/执行仪器而非看截图的 VLM）。对做 inference-time scaling、agentic coding、视觉生成评测或 reward model 可靠性的人都值得精读。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the grounding conclusion hold for modalities with no deterministic instrument at all (audio, 3D, UI-interaction flows), or does it reduce to "grounding is only possible where an instrument exists," capping the third axis?
- How much of the visual-defect reduction maps onto actual human-preferred quality? The authors defer this — a human study across the four visual modalities is the obvious next experiment.
- Can the internal ceiling be tightened from an information bound into a *quantitative* coverage–quality relationship, as the authors propose, so one could predict achievable quality from instrument coverage before running?
- Would a much larger best-of-N budget (e.g., 10× tokens, K in the hundreds) narrow the code gap, and at what cost does pass@N finally approach the interaction ceiling — i.e., is interaction's win mostly an efficiency win at extreme sampling budgets?
- The RFT-on-student result shows post-training that lowers variance hurts best-of-N; does this imply RLHF/RLAIF generally trades off against sampling-scaling headroom, and how should deployment recipes (SFT-keep-variance + grounded harness) be formalized?
- Is the VLM-on-screenshot structural blindness specific to layout/geometry, or does it generalize to other "physical" quality dimensions (color contrast below acuity, timing defects in animation, accessibility tree violations) that a screenshot equally drops?
