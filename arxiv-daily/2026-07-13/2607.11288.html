01 Methodology

Mako runs two coupled, verification-gated loops. The online surface exploits live targets in a bounded perceive→reason→act cycle; the capability loop Φ observes failures, synthesises general primitives, proves them against a live target, and hot-loads them back — no manual intervention.

LOOP 1 · ONLINE
SecurityResearchAgent
MAX_TURNS=25. Phases: Recon (1–5) → Exploit (6–80) → Chain (81–100). Forced-pivot on repeat tools; one-way escalation to Gemini 3.1 Pro after 70% budget with zero findings.
LOOP 2 · EVOLUTION Φ
Capability kernel
Failure → diagnose → synthesise general capability → prove under planted fresh flag → chain into a reliably-selected umbrella tool → re-run end-to-end with a new flag. Capabilities are admitted only when proven, never when proposed.
  • Threat model: external unauthenticated attacker, base URL only; no source access at runtime. ~42/104 fixtures needed non-contaminating infra repair (EOL apt, mysql:5.7 on arm64, PhantomJS removal…).
  • Tooling: ~180 registered tools; ~50 newly built/enhanced. Umbrella tools (e.g. test_ssti_all_engines) preferred over narrow gadgets.
  • Formalism: coverage functional C(T)=E[solve]; no-regression (additive capabilities) ⇒ monotone non-decreasing; submodular (1−1/e) bound used only as a prioritisation heuristic.

04 Strengths vs. Limitations

Strengths

  • Cheat-proof evaluation. Fresh-per-build 128-bit flag + genuine-response scan neutralises memorisation, hallucinated success and incidental disclosure. A model for offensive-agent benchmarks.
  • Architectural clarity. The OS analogy (capability kernel = kernel, Φ = compiler, fresh-flag gate = protected mode) is a genuinely useful organising frame.
  • Counter-intuitive finding. The Level-3 inversion reframes agent maturity, with a crisp causal account via the geometric-time tool-selection law.
  • Unusually honest. Upcoming about system-vs-model capability, no frozen public baseline, stochastic variance, app-shaped knowledge in tools.
  • Decision-relevant economics. Sub-$5/target verified exploitation at retail API rates.

Limitations

  • Not independently reproducible. Proprietary agent, tools, evolution engine, withheld payloads — the artefact is the architecture, not the result.
  • Single suite, single model tier. XBOW-104 with the Gemini family only; cross-suite / cross-model generalisation untested.
  • Turn counts best-case-conditioned. Drawn from successful runs only; some benchmarks failed in other runs.
  • Knowledge contamination. ~50 newly-built tools may encode app-shaped knowledge that does not transfer.
  • Heterogeneous comparison. Table mixes black/white-box, self-reported vendor blogs, modified-suite traces — no clean head-to-head.
  • Decorative formalism. Submodular bound admittedly heuristic; no-regression proposition trivial; fixed point = "additive coverage saturates."

05 Open Questions

  1. How much of 104/104 generalises to unseen non-CTF production apps versus being an artefact of benchmark-specific gadget construction in the ~50 new tools?
  2. What is the run-to-run solve-probability distribution per benchmark — and the expected cost per reliable solve rather than per successful run?
  3. How model-sensitive is the result — would a non-Gemini primary reach comparable coverage, or is the Gemini family doing unmeasured heavy lifting?
  4. Can the fabrication-proof regime (fresh flags + genuine-response scan) become a community standard, and what does a single-harness re-run of KinoSec/BoxPwnr/Shannon show?
  5. Does "capability is all you need" survive a strict train/test split where evolution happens only on a held-out 70% of vulnerability classes?
  6. What minimal artefact (e.g. anonymised evolution traces, no payloads) could be released to allow independent scrutiny without operationalising the weapon?
