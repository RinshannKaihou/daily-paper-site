Calibrated e-CUSUM Decoding for Quantized Reasoning Models: Why Token Log-Probability Is the Wrong Observable for Decoding Monitors

El Hassane Ettifouri · Ayoub Belfatmi · Mahaman Sanoussi Yahaya Alassan · Walid Dahhane Novelis Research, Paris 2026-07-13
quantized-LLMs decoding-monitoring e-process reasoning-models chain-of-thought
Key Finding

Centered token-log-probability martingales are mean-zero by construction and structurally blind to confident repetition loops — a calibrated e-CUSUM on a degeneration-aware score is the fix.

Key Metric

93–95%→0.61 / 0.46

Indiscriminate false-alarm rate on INT4 vanilla traces, converted by calibration (μ₀ = 0.41) into a selective detector — precision 0.61, recall 0.46, φ ≈ 0.28, against a 0.38 base rate.

GSM8K · DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B · n=100

Why This Matters

Clarifies why a folk-remedy monitoring quantity fails, and supplies a reproducible, training-free calibrated e-CUSUM for deploying quantized reasoning models safely.

Critical Edge

No compute-matched baseline. The accuracy trend (63%→69%) cannot be separated from "simply generating more tokens" at +28% cost — and at n=100 it is non-significant (McNemar p=0.18).

Methodology — a training-free, three-stage controller

The controller reads only the token stream of a single decoder. Logits become a sampling distribution via temperature + min-p truncation; from it the controller reads Shannon entropy Ht and, after sampling wt, its log-probability. Everything collapses into one bounded alarm score.

a_t = min(1, 0.7·r_t + 0.3·u_t) Eq. 1 — degeneration-aware score

ut flags entropy spikes relative to recent history; rt flags verbatim repetition via sliding-window n-gram coverage. The repetition channel rt approaches 1 even when the distribution is peaked (Ht≈0) — exactly the signal a log-probability martingale cannot produce.

E_n^raw = ∏ (1 + λ(a_t − μ₀))Eq. 2 — betting e-process
Pr[∃n : E_n^raw ≥ 1/δ] ≤ δEq. 3 — Ville, time-uniform
S_0 = 0; S_n = max(0, S_{n−1} + log(1 + λ(a_n − μ₀)))Eq. 4 — deployed CUSUM
Honesty gap: Eq. 3 holds only for the raw product under the conditional null. The deployed CUSUM floor (Eq. 4) is not a literal e-process — the reset and history-dependence of at break the supermartingale property, so the empirical false-alarm rate (≈0.20) is reported, not the nominal δ=0.05.
STAGE 1 · SCORE

logit → (u_t, r_t, a_t)

Entropy-spike + n-gram repetition collapse into one bounded score a_t ∈ [0,1].

STAGE 2 · DETECT

raw product + CUSUM

Betting e-process with CUSUM-floored log statistic; alarm when S_n ≥ τ. μ₀ calibrated offline.

STAGE 3 · REMEDIATE

backtrack + KV rebuild

Truncate last k tokens, rebuild clean KV cache, resume with graded min-p tightening; injects a "Wait…" marker on repeated alarms.

Strengths & Limitations

+ Strengths

  • A precise, citable, verifiable negative result on why a tempting token-level observable fails — rare in a literature full of folk remedies.
  • Unusually honest reporting: non-significant p-values, the +28% token cost, the empirical (not nominal) false-alarm rate.
  • Clean ablation by construction: vanilla and controller share sampling code, differing only in whether intervention is enabled.
  • The calibration story is itself a contribution — μ₀ selection converts a 93–95% alarm into a selective detector.
  • Released harness, calibration tools, and traces; reproducible on a single consumer GPU (RTX A4000, fixed seeds).

− Limitations

  • Single-model, single-dataset (GSM8K), single-seed, n=100; accuracy effect is non-significant and the authors don't claim it.
  • Ville guarantee applies only to the raw product under the conditional null; μ₀ is marginally calibrated — restart-valid construction (Shin et al. 2024) needed for a true δ statement.
  • No compute-matched baseline (best-of-n, self-consistency) — accuracy trend confounded with extra generation.
  • Degeneration reductions are small in absolute terms (the 1%→0% severe-loop result is a single trace); bootstrap CIs or larger runs needed.
  • Generalization to MATH / AIME / GPQA (where non-termination is more frequent) is untested.
  • "Wait"-marker remediation may conflict with Lotfi et al. (2026); marker-free remediation is an open priority.
  • Two ablations missing on real traces: entropy-only vs. repetition-only vs. combined, and backtracking vs. plain termination.

Authors & Credibility

Who & where

All four authors are at Novelis Research, Paris, and provide ORCIDs. The paper is explicitly framed as "a preliminary study whose main contribution is methodological."

Discipline of claims

Unusually restrained: non-significant p-values, a measured +28% token cost, an empirical (not nominal) false-alarm rate, and a finding that contradicts the paper's own motivating intuition are all surfaced rather than buried.

Math core

The martingale argument for why log p(wt)+Ht is blind to confident loops is elementary and verifiable — the durable contribution.

Reproducibility

Single RTX A4000, fixed seeds, code + calibration tools + full traces released; traces are replayable for detector evaluation without rerunning the model.

05

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the accuracy trend survive a compute-matched comparison? Against best-of-n or self-consistency at the same +28% token budget on GSM8K, MATH, AIME, GPQA.
  2. Does the repetition channel rt carry its weight, or does entropy alone suffice? Entropy-only vs. repetition-only vs. combined ablation on real (not synthetic) traces — especially on harder benchmarks where non-termination and drift are more frequent.
  3. Does backtracking with KV-cache rebuild beat cheaper alternatives? vs. plain termination of the reasoning block or early-exit rules, for the same intervention budget.
  4. Does "Wait"-marker injection aid recovery or compound non-termination? Given Lotfi et al. (2026) show quantized models over-produce these markers — would marker-free or marker-penalty remediation do better?
  5. Can the CUSUM statistic become a genuinely restart-valid e-detector? Shin et al. (2024) construction so the false-alarm guarantee holds at nominal δ rather than the empirical ≈0.20.
  6. Would targeting failure-to-commit (not verbatim repetition) yield larger gains? Non-termination is 49% of incorrect traces — a detector and remediation aimed at it may outperform the degeneration-focused design.
