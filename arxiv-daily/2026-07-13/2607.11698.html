Agent Hacks Agent: Autoresearch for Production-Agent Red-Teaming

AHA turns black-box red-teaming of Claude Code & Codex into a falsifiable autoresearch loop whose frozen Vulnerability Concept Graph breaks held-out agents single-shot at 47.0% ASR — no test-time search.

Xutao Mao · Xiang Zheng · Cong Wang 2026-07-13 · City University of Hong Kong
llm-agents red-teaming prompt-injection agentic-safety autoresearch AHA · VCG
Key Metric
47.0%
Mean held-out ASR · frozen single-shot, no retries
vs strongest frozen baseline (AutoRISE*) 32.8% (+14.2 pp) · on Claude Code +13.5 pp (50.0 vs 36.5).
Why This Matters
Directly red-teams Claude Code / Codex under direct & indirect prompt injection, producing reusable, auditable mechanism-level concepts that transfer across scenarios and victims — and survive patches.
Critical Edge
The whole VCG / judge / falsifier stack rests on LLM judges and an LLM Reflector; the falsifier relocates judge-gamability rather than eliminating it — a subtle bias could still admit false-positive concepts.

Methodology — the falsifiable autoresearch loop

A scenario contract fixes the attack family, attacker-controllable location, payload schema, and the research-agent-visible vs evaluator-only field split. The autoresearch objective is to build a VCG of confirmed concepts on a discovery split; at eval the VCG is frozen and instantiates exactly one attack per held-out instance — no search, no retries, no feedback.

Orchestrator
Picks one of explore / exploit / transfer / consolidate per iteration via an availability rule over VCG counters.
Hypothesizer
Reads VCG + recent reflections + critic audit; commits a refutable vulnerability claim + explicit falsifier before any attack is written.
Attack-Designer
Instantiates the committed hypothesis into a contract-valid payload without editing the hypothesis.
Host
Runs the payload in a fresh Docker sandbox (2 CPU · 4 GB · 600s hard kill · stdin-piped spec), recording the full message/tool trajectory.
Reflector
Compares trajectory to the committed falsifier → confirmed / falsified / inconclusive; off-target breaks are not credited.
Critic
Periodic, fresh-context audit for reward hacking, repeated templates, over-specialization, uncovered regions.
Promotion rule →   n_conf ≥ 3   ∧   confirmation_rate ≥ 0.6   ∧   ≥1 judge-confirmed break
// each VCG concept stores: claim · enabling condition · attack template · failure outcome · transfer prediction · provenance, with typed edges (composes / subsumes / falsifies)

Transfer & model-strength control

Cross-scenario (Fig. 4a). A concept surviving a direct↔indirect channel switch cannot be a stored payload string.

  • AgentHazard VCG → 49.6% mean ASR on DTap-direct, 28.1% on DTap-indirect.
  • AgentDyn VCG → 41.8% on DTap-indirect (matches native), 42.6% on DTap-direct.
  • DTap VCG → AgentHazard 53.7%.

Cross-victim (Fig. 4b). Foreign VCGs reach ~88% of target native ASR on average — failure lives in the agent trajectory, not the model.

  • Deepseek → Minimax 86.7%, Deepseek → Kimi 71.1%, at/above those targets' own native rates.
  • AHA@Qwen (Table 3). With research model pinned to baselines' compliant Qwen-3.7-Max vs Deepseek victim on Claude Code, AHA still matches or beats the strongest baseline on every scenario → gain is from the method, not the host.

Shared-mechanism landscape (Fig. 3, RQ1)

  • 18 discovered VCGs cluster into 8 mechanism families.
  • "Claimed authorization" recurs in 16 / 18 settings — a global core, present in every scenario/model/agent.
  • "Task-goal hijack" appears in 9 / 12 indirect settings and is absent from the direct scenario — a channel-scoped core.

Scenarios & protocol

  • AgentHazard — direct multi-turn, 10 objectives, 90 held-out / 2,563 discovery.
  • AgentDyn — indirect prompt injection (extends AgentDojo), 56 held-out / 504 discovery.
  • DTap — from DecodingTrust-Agent, direct+indirect across 14 objectives, crm/medical/workflow/os-filesystem backends.
  • Seeded discovery/held-out split (42/0/stratified) fixed before search; judge prompts pinned by hash; victim runs in fresh Docker containers.

Strengths

  • Reframes the deliverable from a perishable payload to an auditable, mechanism-level concept with a committed falsifier and validity conditions — genuinely useful for triage after patches.
  • The frozen single-shot protocol + AHA@Qwen control cleanly separates artifact reusability from optimizer strength; few red-teaming papers enforce this.
  • The RQ3 ablation gives direct causal evidence that safeguards convert gameable discovery scores into reproducible findings (flat disc ASR, collapsing reuse).
  • Strong transfer (direct↔indirect, ~88% of native across victims) supports the mechanism-level claim and is hard to explain as benchmark-local payload patterns.
  • Public code + build/import/extend scenario workflows make the harness extensible to private scenarios.

Limitations

  • The VCG/judge/falsifier stack ultimately rests on LLM judges + an LLM Reflector; gamability is mitigated, not eliminated — subtle bias could admit false-positive concepts.
  • Results depend on specific proprietary victims (Minimax-M2.7, Kimi-K2.6, Deepseek-V4-Pro) and agents (Claude Code, Codex) that drift; transfer claims span only 3 models / 2 agents.
  • Disclosed Codex × DTap tool-router inconsistency (openai/codex#26234) mechanically disadvantages part of that block for all methods.
  • AgentDyn absolute ASR is low (≤25%) on a small held-out set (56); the +14.2 pp headline is largely AgentHazard + DTap.
  • No ablation of the deployment-time concept-selection model (load-bearing under single-shot); no human study showing VCG entries actually help engineers patch.
  • Baselines run outside their native test-time-optimizing regime — the right reusability test, but the delta is measured against baselines denied their designed feedback loop.
  • Scope is text tool-using agents; generalization to VLM / multimodal / browser-mobile agents is discussed but not demonstrated.

Questions for further exploration

  1. How sensitive is the frozen single-shot ASR to the deployment-time concept-selection model? Could retrieval over the VCG beat LLM selection, or collapse the +14.2 pp edge?
  2. Do the "claimed authorization" concepts still break an agent once a safety team actually patches the named enabling conditions (e.g., out-of-band ownership verification), and can the frozen VCG re-run as a regression check?
  3. How do VCG concepts interact with deployed defenses (permission prompts, tool-call allowlists, constitution-style mediation) — robust bypasses, or do defenses just shift which family dominates?
  4. Can the falsifier/Reflector loop be hardened against judge-gaming by grounding it in a verifiable programmatic check rather than an LLM, and what does that do to held-out reuse?
  5. Does cross-victim transfer (~88% of native) hold over a larger, more diverse victim pool, or is it an artifact of three models sharing training data and tool-calling conventions?
  6. What is the marginal cost (victim queries, wall-clock, API spend) of accumulating one durable concept, and does coverage plateau or keep compounding?
