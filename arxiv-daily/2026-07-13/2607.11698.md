> **一句话总结：** AHA 把"生产级 LLM 智能体（Claude Code / Codex）红队测试"重构成一个可证伪的自动研究循环，产出可审计、可复用的"漏洞概念图（VCG）"；在三个直接/间接攻击场景、两个智能体、三个受害模型上，冻结后的 VCG 单次零搜索部署即以 47.0% 的平均 ASR 超过最强冻结发现基线 14.2 个百分点，且概念能跨场景、跨攻击通道、跨受害模型迁移。

> **TL;DR:** AHA reframes black-box red-teaming of tool-using production agents (Claude Code, Codex) as a falsifiable autoresearch loop whose output is a frozen, auditable Vulnerability Concept Graph (VCG) — each concept ties an attacker-facing surface to an unsafe trajectory via a committed claim, falsifier, enabling condition, and transfer prediction. Across three scenarios (AgentHazard, AgentDyn, DTap), two agents, and three victim models (Minimax-M2.7, Kimi-K2.6, Deepseek-V4-Pro), the frozen VCG deployed single-shot with no test-time search reaches 47.0% mean held-out ASR, beating the strongest frozen discovery baseline (AutoRISE*) by +14.2 pp overall and +13.5 pp on Claude Code, while an ablation shows the committed falsifier, cross-episode memory, and periodic critic are what turn discovery-time breaks into reproducible, transferable mechanisms (e.g., removing the critic drops AgentHazard effective concepts from 10 to 7 and held-out ASR 75.56% to 57.78%).

## 核心贡献

- **把"智能体红队"形式化为可证伪的自动研究问题**：以一个研究型智能体去自动发现另一个生产级智能体（Claude Code / Codex）的可复用漏洞知识，明确区分"研究产出"与"攻击优化强度"，让发现物不再是会随补丁失效的具体 payload。
- **AHA 发现循环 + 漏洞概念图（VCG）**：循环由 Orchestrator 协调四个角色隔离的子智能体（Hypothesizer / Attack-Designer / Reflector / 周期性 Critic），在写攻击之前先提交"可证伪假设 + 显式证伪器"；通过的发现按证据规则（n_conf≥3 且确认率≥0.6 且至少一次有效 break）晋升进 VCG，每个概念记录声明、使能条件、模板、失败结果、迁移预测与溯源。
- **证据表明发现的是"机制"而非"个案"**：跨 18 个发现的 VCG 聚出 8 个机制家族，其中"claimed authorization（声称授权）"在 18 个设置中出现在 16 个、"task-goal hijack"在 9/12 个间接攻击设置中出现，证明存在跨受害模型/智能体共享的漏洞核心。
- **冻结单次部署即优于基线**：在零测试期搜索、每个 held-out 实例只发一个攻击的严格协议下，AHA 平均 47.0% ASR，超过最强冻结基线 14.2 pp。
- **可迁移性**：概念能跨场景（含直接↔间接通道切换）和跨受害模型迁移，例如 Deepseek 上发现的概念在攻击 Minimax/Kimi 时达到甚至超过其原生 ASR，说明失败属于智能体轨迹结构而非某一受害模型。

## Key Contributions

- **Production-agent red-teaming as autoresearch**: Formulates black-box red-teaming of tool-using agents as a fully automatic autoresearch process whose deliverable is validated, transferable vulnerability knowledge rather than a perishable exploit.
- **Falsifiable discovery loop with a Vulnerability Concept Graph (VCG)**: Each iteration commits a vulnerability claim and an explicit falsifier *before* any attack is written; confirmed concepts are promoted by an evidence rule (≥3 confirmations, ≥60% confirmation rate, ≥1 judge-confirmed break) into a VCG storing claim, enabling condition, attack template, failure outcome, transfer prediction, and provenance. A periodic Critic audits for reward hacking, repeated templates, over-specialization, and uncovered regions.
- **Shared-mechanism evidence (RQ1)**: Across 18 discovered VCGs the concepts cluster into 8 families; the "claimed authorization" core recurs in 16/18 settings and "task-goal hijack" in 9/12 indirect settings, showing mechanisms that generalize beyond a single target.
- **Reusable without further search (RQ2)**: Under a frozen single-shot protocol, AHA reaches 47.0% mean ASR vs 32.8% for the strongest frozen baseline (+14.2 pp overall; +13.5 pp on Claude Code), and leads the four discovery methods on every scenario for Claude Code.
- **Safeguards causally enable reproducibility (RQ3)**: Ablation shows removing the falsifier, cross-episode memory, or critic leaves discovery ASR essentially flat but degrades held-out reuse and effective concept count — the safeguards decide which breaks are valid findings, not how many breaks are found.
- **Durable transfer (RQ4)**: Concepts transfer across scenarios (including direct→indirect and indirect→direct channel shifts, e.g., AgentHazard VCG reaching 49.6% mean ASR on DTap-direct) and across victim models (foreign VCGs reaching ~88% of target native ASR on average), locating the failure in the agent trajectory rather than in a stored payload.

## Methodology

AHA treats black-box red-teaming of a tool-using agent A_{v,f} (victim model v inside victim agent f, e.g., Claude Code or Codex) as a falsifiable autoresearch loop. The setup is governed by a **scenario contract** that fixes the attack family, attacker-controllable input location, payload schema, delivery semantics, the research-agent-visible vs evaluator-only field split, trajectory observations, and the judge. A scenario instance exposes a *research-agent-visible view* (benign task, attacker-controllable location, objective, tools, env state) and an *evaluator-only view* (published payloads, secrets, target predicates, ground-truth tool calls) that stays hidden during search.

**The autoresearch objective** is to build a VCG C* of confirmed concepts on a discovery split T_s; at evaluation the VCG is frozen and used to instantiate exactly one attack per held-out instance H_s. Reusability is defined as single-shot frozen deployment with no test-time search, no retries, and no evaluation feedback. ASR is the fraction of held-out instances the judge labels a break.

**The loop** is coordinated by an Orchestrator that picks one of four search modes per iteration — **explore** (new mechanism on an uncovered instance), **exploit** (deepen a concept that already broke), **transfer** (test a confirmed concept on a different category), **consolidate** (re-test a low-confidence concept) — selected by an availability rule over the VCG counters. Four role-isolated sub-agents coordinate only through shared files (the VCG and agent log):
- **Hypothesizer**: reads VCG, recent reflections, latest critic audit; commits a refutable vulnerability claim + explicit falsifier *before* any attack is written.
- **Attack-Designer**: instantiates the committed hypothesis into a scenario-valid payload conforming to the contract, without editing the hypothesis.
- **Host**: executes the payload in a per-attack sandboxed Docker container (bounded CPU/memory/wall-clock, bind-mounted dirs, stdin-piped attack spec) and records the full message/tool trajectory.
- **Reflector**: compares the trajectory against the committed falsifier and emits a structured concept update, assigning confirmed / falsified / inconclusive — an off-target break is recorded as inconclusive or falsified for the original claim.
- **Critic** (periodic, fresh context): audits for reward hacking, repeated templates, over-specialization, and uncovered regions.

**Vulnerability Concept Graph**: each concept block holds five NL fields (vulnerability claim, enabling condition, attack template, failure outcome, transfer prediction) plus provenance and three counters (n_obs, n_conf, n_fals) and typed edges (composes with, subsumes, falsifies). An evidence rule gates promotion: n_conf ≥ 3, confirmation rate ≥ 0.6 over confirmed-or-falsified attempts, and at least one confirmed trajectory the judge marks as a break.

**Sandbox & isolation**: each victim attack runs in a fresh Docker container (2.0 CPUs, 4 GB RAM, 600s hard kill); only three directories are bind-mounted; the attack spec is piped over stdin (never written to disk); the judge and concept-selection run on the host outside the victim container; the research agent runs without per-call approval (boundary enforced by isolation, project-scoped deny lists, OS-level workspace sandbox, and the visible/evaluator-only field split).

**Scenarios**: AgentHazard (direct multi-turn, 10 objectives, 90 held-out / 2,563 discovery), AgentDyn (indirect prompt injection, extension of AgentDojo, 56 held-out / 504 discovery across shopping/github/dailylife), DTap (imported from DecodingTrust-Agent, spans direct+indirect across 14 objectives and crm/medical/workflow/os-filesystem backends).

## Results & Highlights

- **Frozen single-shot held-out ASR (Table 2, RQ2)**: Averaged over two victim agents, three scenarios, three victim models, AHA reaches **47.0% mean ASR vs 32.8% for the strongest frozen discovery baseline (AutoRISE*), a +14.2 pp gain**; on Claude Code alone the gain is **+13.5 pp (50.0% vs 36.5%)**. On Claude Code AHA is the best of the four discovery methods in every scenario: **AgentHazard 77.41 (vs T-MAP* 55.93, IterInject* 58.52, AutoRISE* 56.67)**, **AgentDyn 25.00 (vs 4.17 / 4.17 / 11.31)**, **DTap 47.62 (vs 37.42 / 37.07 / 41.50)**. On Codex, AHA leads on AgentHazard (74.81) and AgentDyn (20.24).
- **Per-victim peaks (Table 2)**: AHA on Claude Code × Deepseek reaches **85.56% ASR on AgentHazard** (vs strongest baseline 70.00%) and **91.11% on Codex × Deepseek AgentHazard**; on AgentDyn × Deepseek it hits **37.50% (Claude Code)** and **41.07% (Codex)** versus baselines mostly under 27%.
- **Model-strength isolation (AHA@Qwen, Table 3)**: When the research model is fixed to the baselines' compliant Qwen-3.7-Max against the Deepseek victim on Claude Code, AHA still **matches or beats the strongest baseline** (AgentHazard 70.00 vs IterInject* 70.00; AgentDyn 33.93 vs AutoRISE* 26.79; DTap 53.06 vs T-MAP* 50.00), confirming the gain is from the method, not the host model.
- **Safeguard ablation (Table 4, RQ3)**: Discovery ASR stays flat (~72–74% on AgentHazard) regardless of which safeguard is removed, but held-out reuse collapses — removing the **critic** drops AgentHazard held-out ASR **75.56% → 57.78%** and effective concepts **10 → 7**; removing the **falsifier** drops AgentHazard held-out **75.56% → 56.67%**; removing **cross-episode memory** drops DTap held-out **46.94% → 37.76%**. This is the causal claim: the safeguards select which breaks are valid findings rather than finding more breaks.
- **Cross-scenario transfer (Fig. 4a, RQ4)**: The AgentHazard VCG reaches **49.6% mean ASR on DTap-direct and 28.1% on DTap-indirect**; the AgentDyn VCG (compact: 9/3/3 concepts) reaches **41.8% on DTap-indirect (matching native) and 42.6% on DTap-direct**; DTap→AgentHazard reaches **53.7%** mean ASR — a concept surviving a direct↔indirect channel switch cannot be a stored payload string.
- **Cross-victim transfer (Fig. 4b, RQ4)**: Across six source→target transfers on AgentHazard, a foreign VCG reaches **~88% of the target's native ASR on average**; concepts discovered on Deepseek break Minimax and Kimi **at or above those targets' own native rates** (e.g., Deepseek→Minimax 86.7%, Deepseek→Kimi 71.1%), locating the failure in the agent trajectory rather than the model.
- **Shared-mechanism landscape (Fig. 3, RQ1)**: The "claimed authorization" family appears in **16 of 18** scenario×agent×model settings and across every scenario/model/agent; "task-goal hijack" appears in **9 of 12 indirect settings** and is absent from the direct scenario — a global core plus a channel-scoped core.

## Authors & Credibility

The three authors (Xutao Mao, Xiang Zheng, Cong Wang; † co-corresponding) are from **City University of Hong Kong**. The work is released as a preprint with **public code at https://github.com/henrymao2004/Auto-research-red-teaming**, including a bring-your-own workflow (build/import/extend scenario entry points) that makes the evaluation harness reproducible beyond the paper's scenarios. The experimental protocol is unusually disciplined: a strict discovery/held-out split fixed before search (seeds 42/0/stratified), a frozen single-shot evaluation with no test-time search, judge prompts pinned by hash, an AHA@Qwen model-strength control, and victim runs isolated in fresh Docker containers with stdin-piped attack specs. The result tables cover 2 victim agents × 3 scenarios × 3 victim models with per-cell numbers, and the ablation directly tests the paper's central causal claim.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The framing — red-teaming whose *artifact* is a falsifiable, auditable vulnerability concept rather than a payload/archive/program — is genuinely distinct from the three search paradigms cited (T-MAP quality-diversity, IterInject feedback-guided payload optimization, AutoRISE attack-program search). The committed-falsifier-before-attack design and the promotion evidence rule (n_conf≥3 ∧ rate≥0.6 ∧ ≥1 judge-confirmed break) are concrete and non-obvious. It builds explicitly on the autoresearch framing (Panfilov et al. 2026; Karpathy) but makes the *vulnerability concept itself* the object of discovery.
- **Rigor**: The frozen single-shot protocol is the right experimental control: it forces every method to freeze its artifact and deploy with zero test-time search, which isolates artifact reusability from optimizer strength. The AHA@Qwen control (Table 3) rules out the obvious confound that AHA simply uses a stronger host model. The ablation (Table 4) is the strongest internal-validity evidence — discovery ASR staying flat while held-out reuse drops is exactly the pattern that distinguishes "found more breaks" from "found valid findings."
- **Reproducibility**: Good in principle (code released, judge hashes pinned, splits seeded, per-harness permissions documented in App. B.8, compute/sandbox in App. B.7). Caveats: runs depend on commercial agents (Claude Code, Codex) and proprietary victim models (Minimax-M2.7, Kimi-K2.6, Deepseek-V4-Pro) whose versions drift, and the discovery loop runs overnight on `--dangerously-skip-permissions`/`--dangerously-bypass-approvals-and-sandbox` modes — reproducibility is contingent on those exact versions.
- **Significance**: High for the agent-safety/red-teaming community if the VCG-as-artifact idea is adopted; it directly addresses the documented problem that judged success is gameable and that exploit-style artifacts decay on every model/tool update. The transfer results (a foreign VCG reaching ~88% of native ASR) are the most provocative claim and the strongest argument that concepts name trajectory-level mechanisms.
- **Red flags / specific concerns**:
  - **Judge dependency**: The whole pipeline leans on scenario judges (AgentHazard judge, AgentDojo security judge, DTap per-instance verifiable judge) that the authors themselves note are "gameable"; the falsifier is the proposed antidote, but the falsifier is itself an LLM (the Reflector), so the gameability is relocated rather than eliminated.
  - **Codex × DTap reliability caveat**: The authors disclose (footnote 2) that Codex advertises MCP tools under a `type:namespace` encoding that non-OpenAI Responses backends reproduce inconsistently, so "a non-deterministic share of its calls are rejected by Codex's own tool router (upstream issue openai/codex#26234)." This means a slice of DTap × Codex × non-OpenAI-model cells are mechanically disadvantaged for *all* methods, not just AHA — a data quirk that compresses the Codex/DTap column and should temper cross-agent comparisons in that block.
  - **AgentDyn absolute ASR is low** (best 25.00%, baselines 1–11%): the +14.2 pp headline is dominated by AgentHazard and DTap; on AgentDyn the absolute reuse is modest even for AHA, and a small held-out set (56 instances) makes per-cell comparisons noisier than they look.
  - **Missing ablation**: There is no ablation isolating the *concept-selection model* at deployment (which picks the single most-applicable concept per held-out instance); since deployment is single-shot, that selector is doing real work and its sensitivity is not reported.
  - **Baseline fairness**: The three constructive baselines were each "frozen to the artifact it yields" under a stricter-than-native protocol (e.g., IterInject is denied its native per-instance feedback loop; AutoRISE is denied further cycles). This is the correct reusability test but it does disadvantage baselines whose design assumes test-time optimization; the paper is transparent about this, but the headline delta is measured against baselines running outside their native regime.
  - **Dual-use**: An autonomous agent that red-teams other agents is inherently dual-use; the authors argue the mechanism-level, net-defensive artifact and the isolation/permission design mitigate this, and they gate the release accordingly.

## Strengths

- Reframes the red-teaming deliverable from a perishable payload to an auditable, mechanism-level concept with a committed falsifier and conditions of validity — a genuinely useful shift for safety teams that need to triage and re-check after patches.
- The frozen single-shot evaluation protocol + AHA@Qwen model-strength control is a clean, fair test of *artifact reusability* that separates it from optimizer strength; few red-teaming papers enforce this split.
- The RQ3 ablation provides direct causal evidence that the safeguards (falsifier, cross-episode memory, critic) are what convert gameable discovery scores into reproducible findings — discovery ASR stays flat while held-out reuse drops.
- Strong transfer evidence (cross-scenario direct↔indirect, cross-victim-model at ~88% of native ASR) supports the central mechanism-level claim and is hard to explain as benchmark-local payload patterns.
- Public code plus build/import/extend scenario workflows make the harness extensible to private scenarios and imported benchmarks, increasing practical uptake.

## Limitations

- The entire VCG/judge/falsifier stack ultimately rests on LLM judges and an LLM Reflector; judge-gamability is mitigated (by the falsifier and the promotion rule) but not eliminated, so a subtle judge bias could still admit false-positive concepts.
- Results depend on specific proprietary victim models (Minimax-M2.7, Kimi-K2.6, Deepseek-V4-Pro) and agent versions (Claude Code, Codex) that will drift; the transfer claims are over three models and two agents only, and the disclosed Codex×DTap tool-router inconsistency (openai/codex#26234) mechanically disadvantages part of that block.
- AgentDyn absolute held-out ASR is low (≤25%) and its held-out set is small (56 instances), so per-cell deltas there are noisier; the headline +14.2 pp is driven largely by AgentHazard and DTap.
- No ablation of the deployment-time concept-selection model, which is load-bearing under single-shot deployment; no human study validating that the VCG entries are actually *useful* to safety engineers for patching (the triage claim is argued, not empirically demonstrated with practitioners).
- Baselines run under a stricter-than-native frozen protocol, which is the right reusability test but means the delta is measured against baselines operating outside their designed regime; a comparison against *test-time-optimizing* baselines under equal total query budget is not the framing used.
- Cost/efficiency of the overnight discovery loop (victim-query counts, wall-clock, API spend) is reported in appendices but not foregrounded; it is unclear how the loop scales to larger scenario spaces or more agents.
- Scope is limited to text tool-using agents; the claim that the loop generalizes to VLMs, multimodal, browser/mobile agents is discussed but not demonstrated.

## Relevance

与"LLM 智能体安全 / 红队测试 / prompt injection 防御"方向高度相关：它直接研究 Claude Code、Codex 这类工具调用型生产智能体在直接多轮攻击与间接 prompt 注入下的轨迹级失效，并提出可复用、可审计的漏洞概念图作为长期安全资产，正好契合"智能体红队、越狱攻防、agentic safety"等研究兴趣。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How sensitive is the frozen single-shot ASR to the *deployment-time concept-selection model* — does a weaker/different selector collapse the +14.2 pp advantage, and could retrieval (instead of LLM selection) over the VCG do better?
- Do the discovered "claimed authorization" concepts still break an agent once a safety team actually patches the named enabling conditions (e.g., adds out-of-band ownership verification for credential writes), and can the frozen VCG be re-run as a regression check on the fix?
- How do the VCG concepts interact with deployed agent defenses (e.g., permission prompts, tool-call allowlists, constitution-style mediation) — are the bypasses robust to defenses, or do defenses simply shift which concept family dominates?
- Can the falsifier/Reflector loop be hardened against judge-gaming by grounding the falsifier in a *verifiable* programmatic check rather than an LLM, and how much does that change the held-out reuse numbers?
- Does the cross-victim transfer (~88% of native ASR) hold for a larger and more diverse victim-model pool, or is it an artifact of three models that share similar training-data and tool-calling conventions?
- What is the marginal cost (victim queries, wall-clock, API spend) of accumulating one additional durable concept, and does the VCG's coverage plateau or keep compounding across scenario builds?
