> **一句话总结：** 一篇高质量的负面结果研究：作者构建了一个 port-Hamiltonian 深度均衡模型（DEQ）来测试"物理结构化的隐式迭代推理"，但在 ProofWriter 蕴涵与 BFS 图可达性两个任务上、跨 19 次训练，发现解出的均衡点在数值精度上等于求解器的初始点——隐式计算是一个静默的空操作（no-op），完全旁路求解器后准确率在 18/19 次运行中变化 +0.00pp，整套装置从未超过两层 MLP 基线；作者将机制归因于梯度饥饿，并提出一个廉价可复现的"四测试"诊断协议（以"替换测试"取代被混淆的"置零消融"）。

> **TL;DR:** A cautionary, single-author negative-results study showing that a port-Hamiltonian deep equilibrium model (DEQ) with a learned initializer performs no useful implicit computation: across ProofWriter entailment (frozen DeBERTa-v3-base) and a BFS-verified graph-reachability benchmark, the solved equilibrium equals the solver's start point to ~10^-7 precision, and bypassing the fixed-point solver changes test accuracy by +0.00pp in 18 of 19 runs — the full apparatus never beats a two-layer MLP (ProofWriter: 89.39 ± 4.30% vs MLP 90.39 ± 0.33%). The authors falsify the obvious "anchor causes it" hypothesis with controlled retraining arms, trace the real mechanism to gradient starvation along two distinct routes, show that the standard zeroing ablation is confounded (seed-dependent deltas of +0 to +44pp) where the correct substitution test gives a stable zero, and contribute a cheap four-test diagnostic protocol (T1–T4) for auditing any claimed implicit/recurrent computation.

## 核心贡献

- **首个被精细剖析的"静默空操作"隐式计算案例**：在一个 port-Hamiltonian DEQ（learned initializer + Anderson 加速 + 锚定项）上证明，解出的均衡点在数值精度上等于求解器初始点（位移 ∥h∗−z0∥ ≈ 10⁻⁷），隐式层对结果零贡献。
- **主动证伪了最直觉的因果假设**：作者没有停留在"锚定项既是起点又是吸引子"的表面解释，而是设计了三组受控消融（A 耦合 / B 噪声解耦重训 / C 去锚定）来证伪它——去掉锚定项完全复现原结果，从噪声起点重训得到的求解器仍收敛到噪声起点。
- **识别出两种退化机制**：lazy identity collapse（均衡=起点）与 solver blow-up（发散，1/5 的 ProofWriter seed），后者产生一个 decoder 共适应的噪声通道，置零后准确率反而提升。
- **揭示"置零消融"被混淆，提出"替换测试"**：置零 h∗ 同时删除了计算与输入通路，给出 +44 到 0pp 的种子依赖性伪信号；替换测试（把求解器起点直接喂给 decoder）才能隔离计算本身，给出稳定的 0.00pp。
- **四测试诊断协议（T1–T4）**：替换 / 表征探针 vs 熵底 / 迭代次数 vs 难度相关性 / 强制位移，廉价到可在单个免费 Colab GPU 上数分钟跑完，适用于 DEQ、looped transformer、recurrent-depth 模型。
- **完全可复现的负面结果**：全部数字可由发布的分析脚本从原始 per-run 日志重算；代码、配置、种子、日志全部开源。

## Key Contributions

- A mechanistically dissected case study of silent no-op implicit computation, with the anchor-causation hypothesis explicitly tested and falsified by controlled retraining arms (not merely asserted).
- Identification of two degenerate regimes — lazy identity collapse (equilibrium = start) and solver blow-up (divergence into decoder-adapted noise) — with per-seed evidence that pooled statistics would have averaged away into a misleading mean.
- A demonstration that the standard zeroing ablation confounds pathway with computation for implicit modules, plus the substitution test that deconfounds them (returning a stable +0.00pp gap rather than seed-dependent deltas spanning +0 to +44pp).
- A four-test diagnostic protocol (Section 7) — substitution (T1), entropy-floor probing (T2), iteration-vs-difficulty correlation (T3), forced displacement (T4) — cheap enough to run in minutes on a free GPU and applicable to DEQs, looped transformers, and recurrent-depth models generally.
- A fully reproducible negative result: every number traces to released raw logs via a released analysis script; retired two un-reproducible working-notes numbers (an 81.72% baseline, a 51.29% probe) in favor of fresh multi-seed values.

## Methodology

The paper studies a **port-Hamiltonian anchored DEQ**. The implicit state is phase-space structured z = (q, p), evolved by a damped, anchored, symplectic-Euler update conditioned on a context vector c:

- q_{t+1} = q_t + ε∇_pK(p_t,c) − ε r(c) p_t + ε r_a(q_0 − q_t)
- p_{t+1} = p_t − ε∇_qU(q_{t+1},c) − ε r(c) q_{t+1} + ε r_a(p_0 − p_t)

where K and U are learned kinetic/potential scalar MLPs, r(c)=softplus(w_r^⊤c+b_r) is a learned context-conditioned damping coefficient, ε=0.05, r_a is anchor strength (0.02 default; 0 in arm C), and weight matrices of K/U are spectrally clamped to norm ≤ 0.95. The fixed point is solved by Anderson acceleration (m=5, cap 300 iterations) from a start point equal to the learned initialization z_0 = (q_0,p_0), with per-example adaptive tolerance 10^{−(5σ(w_t^⊤c+b_t)+1)} ∈ [10⁻⁶,10⁻¹]. Gradients pass via the implicit function theorem through an adjoint Anderson solve.

The **initialization network** maps context to phase space: q_0 by a linear readout of context, p_0 through a goal-gated channel (query–key gate modulating value projections). The decoder is a two-layer GELU MLP reading [h∗; c_ctx; c_query].

**Tasks:**
1. **ProofWriter entailment** — binary classification over frozen DeBERTa-v3-base embeddings, stratified over proof depths {1,2,3,5} (2477 train / 620 test, label balance 0.495/0.477).
2. **Graph reachability** — synthetic k-hop reachability on 16-node directed graphs (out-degree 2), query (s,t,k) with k≤8, BFS-verified labels, exactly balanced (6000 train / 1000 test; 30000 train in arm E), each instance carrying true BFS distance as a ground-truth difficulty signal.

**Controlled arms:** A (coupled, start=anchor=z_0), B (retrained from noisy starts z_0+σε, σ=2, ε resampled each forward pass), C (anchor removed, r_a=0), D (context-only baseline), E (30k graphs). A two-layer MLP on identical cached inputs is trained fresh per seed as a positive control.

**The key methodological move is the substitution test** (T1): at evaluation, feed the solver's start point directly to the decoder, skipping the fixed-point solve entirely. This isolates what the solver *computes* from the *pathway* that flows through it — unlike zeroing h∗, which removes both at once.

## Results & Highlights

- **The equilibrium is the initialization.** On the graph task the solved equilibrium is numerically identical to the solver's start point: mean displacement ∥h∗−start∥ = 6.9×10⁻⁷ pooled over 5 seeds (arm A), and identical to reported precision when the anchor is removed (arm C). Retraining from noisy starts z_0+σε (arm B) makes displacement sit exactly on the injected-noise norm σE∥ε∥ ≈ 7.87 — the "equilibrium" is wherever the start is placed, confirmed across a five-value anchor-strength sweep r_a ∈ {0.005,0.02,0.05,0.1,0.2} (displacement 2×10⁻⁶ at every value).

- **Bypassing the solver changes nothing (the load-bearing result).** The substitution test gives a +0.00pp accuracy gap in every arm and every seed of the graph task (Table 1): arm A Full 61.80±1.34 vs Start-sub 61.80±1.34; arm B 61.82±0.96 vs 61.82±0.96; arm C 61.80±1.34 vs 61.80±1.34; arm E (5× data) 64.20±0.79 vs 64.20±0.79. Across both tasks this +0.00pp result holds in 18 of 19 training runs. On ProofWriter the 4 collapsed seeds show a substitution gap ≤ 0.32pp (e.g. seed 1: Full 91.77 vs Start-sub 91.77; seed 2: 91.29 vs 91.61).

- **The entire implicit apparatus is dispensable.** The context-only baseline (arm D) matches the full graph pipeline: 61.52±0.72 vs 61.80±1.34. The full apparatus never beats a two-layer MLP: on ProofWriter the port-Hamiltonian pipeline reaches 89.39±4.30% vs MLP 90.39±0.33% (and every per-seed MLP baseline in Table 2 — 90.65/90.32/90.00/90.81/90.16 — is not beaten by its corresponding full-model seed).

- **Iteration counts ignore difficulty.** Correlation between solver iteration count K and ground-truth BFS distance is r = 0.009 (arm A, 5 seeds pooled) and r = −0.032 at 5× data (arm E); 99.9% of solves run to the iteration cap regardless of difficulty, and accuracy is flat across solver tolerances from 10⁻² to 10⁻⁶ because the state never moves.

- **The minority blow-up regime diverges instead of collapsing.** One ProofWriter seed in five (seed 3) ends with mean displacement 171.43; its start-substituted accuracy falls to 32.58% (below the 47.7% majority-class floor) while zeroing h∗ raises accuracy from 81.77% to 83.23% — i.e. the non-converged output acts as a co-adapted noise channel whose removal helps.

- **The mechanism is gradient starvation, traced per-pathway.** A fresh classifier probing the frozen equilibrium h∗ alone converges to the label-entropy floor (loss ≈ ln 2; test accuracy 51.45%) — the equilibrium carries no task signal beyond what it copies from its start. Zeroing the goal-gated p_0 half of the initializer drops accuracy 91.77% → 47.74%; zeroing q_0 changes nothing (91.77%). The two pipelines realize starvation by different routes: ProofWriter differentiates through z_0 (initializer trains, solve is identity); the graph pipeline's custom VJP returns zero cotangents for z_0 and the context (initializer stays at random init, decoder learns from direct context).

- **The zeroing ablation is confounded; the substitution test is not.** Zeroing h∗ on ProofWriter gives wildly seed-dependent deltas of +44.0, +19.5, +0.0, +44.0, +39.5 pp across five seeds — superficially reading as "the DEQ sometimes matters a great deal," but actually tracking how each decoder distributes weight between its h∗ and context channels. The substitution test returns 0.00pp with negligible variance in every collapsed run.

- **Forced displacement degrades accuracy.** Starting the trained coupled model away from its initialization at inference gives genuine displacement (≈7.9) but accuracy drops 61.80±1.34 → 56.06±1.26 — the off-initialization vector field contains no structure (capability absent, not suppressed).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine. The "lazy identity collapse" failure mode (degenerate convergence, the quiet opposite of the well-studied unstable-DEQ pathology) is not, to my knowledge, documented elsewhere with this dissection. The substitution-vs-zeroing ablation distinction and the deconfounding argument are a real methodological contribution that transfers to *any* paper claiming iterative/implicit computation — the demonstration that the standard zeroing ablation gives +0 to +44pp seed-dependent deltas on ProofWriter while the substitution test gives a stable zero is the single most citable result in the paper.

- **Rigor:** High for the claim being made. The authors do the hard thing of *falsifying their own mechanism* (the anchor-causation story) with arms C and B rather than stopping at the headline. They report per-seed numbers because pooling would average the blow-up seed (81.77%) with collapsed seeds (~91%) into a meaningless mean — an explicit, correct statistical choice. The substitution test is correctly argued as a forward-pass property independent of the VJP bug.

- **Reproducibility:** Excellent. Per-run manifests (library versions, device, config hash, dataset SHA-256), per-epoch JSONL, released analysis scripts regenerating every table/figure, free-GPU compute budget. The dataset-hash discipline is above the norm.

- **Significance:** Moderate but real. As a standalone result it is small-scale (one architecture family, two binary tasks), but as a *methodological warning* for the looped-transformer / recurrent-depth / test-time-compute literature — which shares a load-bearing "the iterated module actually computes" assumption — it is widely applicable and cheap to act on.

- **Red flags / honest weaknesses:** (1) **Scope is narrow and the authors say so** — only port-Hamiltonian dynamics with Anderson (m=5, cap 300) on two binary classification tasks; the negative claim is design-specific, not "all DEQs fail." (2) **The graph pipeline has a VJP bug** (custom VJP returns zero cotangents for z_0 and context, so the initializer is gradient-isolated and stays at random init). The authors frame this as "a second route to the same starvation," which is fair, but it means the *fully-differentiated* configuration was only tested on one of the two tasks — weakening the generality of the gradient-starvation diagnosis. (3) **The blow-up regime is characterized from a single event** (1 of 5 ProofWriter seeds, plus the gate-modified run); its frequency as a function of hyperparameters is unknown. (4) **Both tasks are deliberately weak** — solvable by a direct pathway, which is precisely why starvation bites. The authors note that tasks where the direct context pathway *cannot* solve the problem may exert the missing gradient pressure, but do not test this. (5) Both encoders are frozen/from-scratch; end-to-end finetuning could change the gradient landscape reaching the implicit layer and is untested. (6) Single independent author with no peer review at time of posting — the work is self-consistent and honest, but external replication would strengthen it. None of these are overclaiming; they are the boundaries of a well-scoped negative result.

## Strengths

- Rare intellectual honesty: actively designs experiments to *kill* its own preferred explanation (anchor causation) and reports the null.
- The substitution test and the four-test diagnostic protocol are immediately useful, cheap, and transferable to far larger models than those studied.
- Per-seed reporting that deliberately avoids the pooling trap (the blow-up seed would otherwise be invisible).
- Full reproducibility: released code, logs, dataset hashes, analysis scripts, free-GPU budget.
- The confounded-ablation finding (zeroing vs substitution) is a concrete methodological lesson likely to affect how implicit-module papers are reviewed.

## Limitations

- Narrow scope: one architecture family (port-Hamiltonian + Anderson m=5/cap 300), two binary classification tasks, small phase dimensions (6–8). The negative claim is explicitly design-specific.
- The graph pipeline's custom VJP returns zero cotangents, so the fully-differentiated (end-to-end) configuration is validated on only one of the two tasks (ProofWriter).
- Both tasks are chosen to be solvable by a direct pathway — exactly the condition under which gradient starvation bites; tasks requiring genuine iterative computation (which the authors acknowledge may exist) are not tested.
- The blow-up regime is observed only once (1/5 seeds) plus once mechanistically in the gate-modified run, so its frequency is not characterized.
- Frozen/from-scratch encoders only; no end-to-end finetuning of a large encoder.
- The graph benchmark memorizes (100% train accuracy) and generalizes weakly (~62%); task-level accuracies should not be read as statements about reachability reasoning in general.
- Single independent author, no peer review at posting (though CC-BY and fully open).

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does lazy identity collapse survive on tasks where the direct context pathway provably *cannot* solve the problem (e.g. problems requiring depth > shallow readout capacity), which would test the authors' conjecture that such tasks exert the missing gradient pressure?
- How do current looped-transformer and recurrent-depth LLMs (e.g. Geiping et al. 2025, tiny recursive reasoners) fare under the substitution test T1 — do any of them actually compute, or do some copy their loop-initial state?
- Is the substitution-vs-zeroing confound present in published ablation tables of implicit-layer papers, and would re-running those ablations with the substitution test change their conclusions?
- Can the diagnostic protocol (T1–T4) be extended to attention/CoT-style "reasoning" where the "module" is a token-generation loop rather than a fixed-point solve?
- What is the frequency of the solver blow-up regime as a function of anchor strength, spectral clamp, and initializer gate expressivity — is it a continuous phase transition or a rare instability?
- Does end-to-end finetuning of the encoder (rather than frozen DeBERTa) change the gradient landscape enough to make the implicit layer contribute, or does starvation persist?
