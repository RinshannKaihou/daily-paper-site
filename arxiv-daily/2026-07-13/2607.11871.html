01

Methodology — one pipeline, three jobs

analyze → intervene → predict
STEP 01

Controlled triples

4,500 questions × 6-model answer pool → parallel 𝒟_base / 𝒟_neg / 𝒟_pos sets (31,500 + 31,500 perturbed) for 7 bias types. Four types keep answer bodies bit-identical.

STEP 02

Activation geometry

Record final-token hidden states per layer; define baseline manifold ℳ_base; restrict to effective substrate 𝒟_eff (score shift ≥ 𝛿_s) and biased core 𝒟_far (top Mahalanobis decile).

STEP 03

Bias-direction estimate

Directional (mean, geom-median, PCA) + discriminative (LDA, linear cls, SVM) families, unit-normalized. Within-family cosine ≥ 0.91; three reps carried forward.

STEP 04

Causal steering

Single mid–late layer 𝐡′ = 𝐡 + 𝛼·𝐯_bias. Attack (𝛼>0, clean) & defense (𝛼<0, biased) must both succeed; 𝛼* localized under validity ≥ 0.93 & Spearman floor.

STEP 05

Outcome predictor

Per-layer features = bias-projection + manifold-deviation + semantic-context. Linear LR vs LightGBM (RFE + Optuna). Target: judge degrades ≥ 1 point. 3 benchmarks fully held out.

03

Strengths & limitations

side-by-side

Strengths what lands

  • Unified geometric + causal + operational framing tied to a single low-dimensional object.
  • Exceptionally thorough controls (random-direction, bias-type-swap, matched-budget text-attack, absolute-floor defense, held-out-trio sensitivity) preempt reviewer objections.
  • Large balanced eval: 7 judges × 7 bias types × 9 benchmarks; survives all CoT×strict configs.
  • Operationally honest: anchors cross-domain claim to the simpler linear predictor (which generalizes), not the overfitting GBDT.
  • Clean separation of stylistic-discrimination sanity check (AUC ≈ 0.97) from the harder judge-degradation target.

Limitations what's shaky

  • White-box access required; proprietary API judges — the most deployed — cannot benefit without undeveloped prompt-level proxies.
  • Activation analysis covers only three mid-scale open judges; behavioral asymmetry replicates across seven but geometric/causal claims rest on this trio.
  • Three LLM-assisted rewrite types conflate the bias cue with rewrite artifacts; Sentiment− is nearly flat, slightly undermining the universality story.
  • Calibrated 𝛼* spans 2.19–75.75 — unit-normalized direction hides very different effective magnitudes per type.
  • Cross-architecture transfer is partial (cos 0.47–0.62); cross-model defense is "degraded efficacy," not drop-in.
  • Claims interventional sufficiency only; unique-pathway (path-patching / causal tracing) deferred to future work.
04

Questions for further exploration

open edges
Q1 · CAUSALCan path-patching / causal-tracing upgrade "interventional sufficiency" to a "unique natural pathway" claim — or does the bias subspace decompose into redundant routes?
Q2 · BLACK-BOXDoes the same geometry exist in proprietary API judges, and can logit-only / output-distribution features approximate the activation predictor?
Q3 · TRAININGHow does the subspace interact with judge training (instruction-tuning, RLHF, Prometheus-style fine-tuning) — does it sharpen, erase, or rotate?
Q4 · TRANSFERCan domain-adversarial training on the predictor close the LightGBM cross-domain gap (0.75 → ≥ 0.82) while keeping its in-domain edge?
Q5 · COGNITIONIs the negativity bias (big penalty, ~zero positive reward) the same anchoring mechanism as in human cognition — and does it transfer to pairwise/preference judges?
Q6 · STACKINGWhat happens when multiple bias types stack in one input — does the geometry predict interference, additivity, or saturation?
