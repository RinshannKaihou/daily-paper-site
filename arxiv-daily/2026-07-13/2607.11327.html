PRISM Edit: One Vector for All Temporal Answers

Causal tracing reveals LLMs use a two-stage mechanism for time, so PRISM writes one polysemous vector v* that the model's own modulation routes to time-correct answers.

Chen Huang · Qi Zheng · Ruiqin Zheng · Long Zeng · Yuantong Xu 2026-07-13 · Tsinghua University × ByteDance

Why This Matters
Demonstrates that mechanism-aligned edits — letting edits follow the model's own computation — beat imposed structure, directly informing knowledge maintenance, interpretability, and lifelong learning.
Critical Edge
GRACE actually beats PRISM on raw TIMECF TC (93.5 vs 37.4) by memorizing direct prompts — but the memory-based comparison is relegated to an appendix: defensible framing, conveniently narrow.

Methodology

Mechanism-first: trace how LLMs handle temporal facts, then derive the editor from what the trace shows.

Step 1
Mechanistic analysis (§3). Extend Meng et al.'s causal tracing to (s, r, o, t) tuples; six indirect-effect maps on 500 correctly-answered pairs. Finds early-MLP subject retrieval is time-agnostic, later attention carries time, and the edit site provably cannot discriminate time (Eq. 1 geometric limit).
Step 2
PRISM Edit (§4). Stop forcing the edit layer to separate time. Jointly optimize one shared δ* over all temporal contexts and write it via a MEMIT/AlphaEdit-style closed-form update with null-space projection. No router, no auxiliary memory.
Step 3
Evaluation (§5). TIMECF-FACTUAL, TIMECF-FICTIONAL, COUNTERFACT† (1,000 records each, batches of 100). Primary metric: normalized substring match (stricter than NLL). Headline: TC = CES ∧ HES jointly correct.

// two-state form:   minimize   Lnew + λold·Lold + Lbare + Ω(δ)

The L_bare term makes "default-to-current on a time-marker-free prompt" an explicit optimization target — ablation shows removing it collapses CRS to 36–45.

Causal-Trace Signatures (§3.2)

  • Subject-retrieval IE peaks at L4 MLP = 0.549, plateau cos ≥ 0.97 across L4–8.
  • Temporal IE at L10 attention = 0.356.
  • New-time vs old-time prompts converge (cos ≈ 0.82) mid-layer, diverge (cos ≈ 0.70) upper — empirical signature of two-stage handling.
  • PCA bifurcation onset ≈ L20; failure cases show no clean bifurcation.

Strengths

  • Mechanism-first design: editor derived directly from causal tracing; Eq. 1 gives a principled reason per-time MLP editing fails.
  • Zero architectural change — drops into the ROME / MEMIT / AlphaEdit stack, no router, no per-period storage.
  • Strict evaluation: substring-match primary, paraphrase variants, TC (joint CES∧HES) as headline.
  • TIMECF benchmark with factual + counterfactual variants and day-level granularity fills a real gap vs AToKe (year-level, 13 relations, 8,820 records).
  • Uniform gains on the metrics that truly test temporal coexistence (CRS, TC) plus a >2× speedup.

Limitations

  • Only the two-state (k=2) case evaluated; scaling the joint objective to k>2 coexisting snapshots is future work.
  • Despite day-level TIMECF annotations, main experiments run at year level — undercutting the headline selling point.
  • Model scale capped at 8B / 6B; 70B+ and instruction-tuned variants untested.
  • Sampled evaluation (1,000 records) and batch-100 sequential editing leave lifelong editing untested.
  • The exploited mechanism (upper-layer attention modulating a shared subject rep) is likely not unique to time; negation/modality/perspective extension is claimed, not demonstrated.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the polysemy principle generalize beyond time — can a single v* support negation-, modality-, or perspective-conditioned answers via the same joint-optimization recipe?
  2. How does PRISM behave under lifelong/sequential editing (thousands of edits), and does the polysemous v* interfere across many coexisting snapshots (k>2)?
  3. Would the gains hold at 70B scale, where the two-stage mechanism may be organized differently?
  4. Can the Eq. 1 geometric limit be relaxed by editing at a layer where knew and kold are more distinguishable, or by attention-layer edits?
  5. Why does ROME collapse to 0.0 on all temporal metrics — rank-one update, layer choice, or interaction with the temporal prompt?
  6. TIMECF is long-tailed (top-3 relations ≈ 72%); how stable are gains on rarer, structurally diverse relations and on non-English temporal facts?
