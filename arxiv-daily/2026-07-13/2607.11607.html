01

Methodology — a four-stage decision-level audit

STAGE 1 · SCREEN

Excess Wasserstein gap ∆x(s)

ε-greedy rollout (ε=0.05) collects 2,000 states/game; each scored by ∆x = W₁(Z(s,a₁),Z(s,a₂)) − |µ₁−µ₂>. Prop. R1: ∆x>0 iff neither top-2 action first-order stochastically dominates — exactly where CVaR would disagree with mean-greedy. Stratified across 7 rank strata; top 2% = "what a practitioner flags first".

STAGE 2 · GROUND TRUTH

Snapshot-restart Monte-Carlo

Deep copies of the env stored per state; for each audited state × top-2 action, 2,000 rollouts restarted from snapshot, forcing the action, greedy thereafter to horizon 10³, each independently reseeded (sidesteps the RNG-cloning pitfall). Empirical MC ∆̂x uses the same quantile formula.

STAGE 3 · STATISTICS

Permutation null + bootstrap + BH-FDR

Confirmed if ∆̂x beats a permutation null (split-half excess within one action = pure noise). Refuted if learned ∆x beats the 97.5th bootstrap percentile of MC excess (conservative). Benjamini–Hochberg FDR=0.10, pooled per game. Per-state aliasing index bounds partial observability; low-alias (<0.25) reported separately.

STAGE 4 · DECISION

Chooser comparison at α

At decidable top-stratum states (true CVaR gap significant at 95%), three choosers of the safer action compared: the head's CVaR ranking, a mean-greedy baseline, and ground truth. Total: 2,450 audited states, 9.6M rollouts, <$9 commodity CPU.

Training: QR-DQN (51 quantiles, 3M steps, 5 seeds) · C51 / IQN (3 seeds) on MinAtar Breakout/Seaquest/Asterix · 10M-frame SB3-zoo QR-DQN on full ALE. RISKYGRID positive control: 10-step corridor, SAFE=0.5 deterministic vs GAMBLE=0.5±g fair coin.

03

Controls — the zero is real, not an artifact of a broken audit

Truth-blind null head (defense: "audit is broken")

81% vs 79% observed · p=0.22

Permuting each run's learned claims across states (preserving magnitudes & rank strata) yields 81% [75,86] top-strata refutation over 1,000 permutations vs 79% observed. Claim placement is no better than chance.

RISKYGRID positive control (defense: "audit has no power")

143/147 = 97% · r=0.92

Known-answer key confirms 97% [93,99], 0 false refutations in top strata, learned–true r=0.92. Faint variant (g∈{0,0.05,…,0.4}) confirms 96–100% at every detectable level, including g=0.05 — ruling out power & effect-size explanations.

Two self-caught silent pitfalls disclosed: P1 RNG cloning (deepcopy copies the env RNG → "random" rollouts fully deterministic) and P2 the empirical-Wasserstein noise floor c·σ·n⁻¹ᐟ² (≈0.22 at n=200, swamping the 0.05 threshold). Each had produced a convincing-but-wrong earlier audit.

04

Strengths vs Limitations

▲ Strengths

  • Decision-relevant metric. Excess-Wasserstein gap provably isolates exactly where CVaR and mean-greedy would disagree — not an aggregate divergence irrelevant to any consumer.
  • Bulletproof controls. Truth-blind null + RISKYGRID (with a faint near-floor variant) jointly rule out the two most dangerous objections: a broken or powerless audit.
  • Honest self-disclosure of two pitfalls (RNG cloning, Wasserstein noise floor) that would silently corrupt any restart-based evaluation.
  • Cheap & fully released (<$9 CPU; code + checkpoints + raw per-run returns) with a concrete audit-passing standard for future calibrated methods.
  • Mechanistic depth: three independent probes (training-time, performance, seeds) tie the artifact to learning, and recalibration separates "uninformative" from "miscalibrated".

▼ Limitations

  • At-scale result is a policy mismatch. SB3-zoo agents were trained without sticky actions, so "every top Breakout claim refuted" quantifies artifact size, not a clean replication.
  • Narrow coverage. QR-DQN / C51 / IQN only; modern calibrated variants (evidential, conformal, SENTINEL ensembles) are untested.
  • Three MinAtar + two ALE games; no continuous-control, so generalization is untested.
  • Requires snapshot-restart env access (deep-copy + forced-action restart), limiting applicability.
  • Sub-floor real risk (median null ≈0.07) is by construction not adjudicated — genuine but faint trade-offs in MinAtar cannot be ruled out.
  • Single author, arXiv v1 — no indication of peer review; rests on one pipeline, mitigated only by code release.
05

Open questions for further exploration

  1. Do modern calibrated heads (evidential RL — Stutts 2024; conformal wrappers — Gan 2025; SENTINEL ensembles — Eriksson 2022) pass the excess-Wasserstein audit, or merely layer estimators on an uninformative base?
  2. Would an ALE QR-DQN trained under sticky actions still show 100% top-strata refutation on Breakout — closing the author's explicitly flagged gap?
  3. Can the artifact be predicted from training signals alone (target-variance, projection-step diagnostics)? The leak-free AUROC=0.92 probe suggests a per-state reliability flag may be hard.
  4. Does the doomed-Breakout-state specimen generalize — is overclaiming concentrated at degenerate/near-absorbing states, and would masking them at training time reduce the artifact?
  5. How does the verdict change for continuous-control distributional actors (e.g. distributional SAC), where snapshot-restart is cheaper and stochasticity denser?
  6. Recalibration "passes only by nullifying claims" — is the missing information recoverable from an auxiliary input (ensemble disagreement, bootstrapped target variance) without re-introducing the same artifact?
