> **一句话总结：** 提出 IAAN，一种无需训练、无需标签的推理时干预方法：通过对比音频编码器中每个前馈神经元在真实音频与噪声参考上的激活来打分，再在推理时放大得分最高的一小撮神经元（仅占编码器神经元的 ≤0.12%），在 VoxParadox 的十项非语义语音属性上将 Audio-Flamingo-3、Qwen2.5-Omni、Kimi-Audio 的平均准确率分别提升 25.7、21.4、9.7 个百分点。

> **TL;DR:** IAAN is a training-free, label-free inference-time method that scores each feed-forward neuron in an LALM's audio encoder by contrasting its activation on the real waveform versus a noise reference, then amplifies the top-scoring neurons (≤0.12% of all encoder neurons) during inference. On VoxParadox's ten non-semantic speech attributes, it lifts average accuracy by +25.7 points on Audio-Flamingo-3 (13.1%→38.8%), +21.4 on Qwen2.5-Omni (7.6%→29.0%), and +9.7 on Kimi-Audio (21.2%→30.9%), surpassing GPT-4o Audio and Gemini 2.5 Flash, while decoders-side (AAD) and LLM-side (LLM-amplify, HS-steer) counterparts gain little or hurt — showing both the encoder locus and neuron-level selectivity are necessary.

## 核心贡献

- **首次将干预点前移到音频编码器内部的神经元级别：** 以往 LALM 的推理时干预大多作用于语言模型主干或解码端（对比 logits、隐状态 steering），本文指出音频编码器才是声学信息最初形成之处，且逐个前馈神经元是比整层隐状态更细的有效粒度。
- **提出 IAAN：一种免训练、免标签、免校准集的方法：** 对每个 clip 现场运行两次编码器前向（真实音频 + 噪声参考），用神经元的"声学得分" s_i = mean(a_real) − mean(a_ref) 排序，放大 top-K 个；放大只通过编码器内的 forward hook 完成，无需属性标签、固定 steering 方向或额外 LLM 解码。
- **通过严格的对照实验证明"位置"和"选择性"缺一不可：** 在解码端做 AAD、在 LLM 中做 LLM-amplify、在编码器层做 dense 的 HS-steer 都无法复现增益；随机选神经元、整层放大也无效；说明增益来自编码器位置 + 声学得分选出的特定神经元子集。
- **方法对已专门微调的模型仍然互补：** 即使是 Pang 等人针对副语言信息微调过的 AF3-PD，IAAN 仍能将平均准确率从 70.5% 提升到 75.0%，并将其薄弱的 intonation 任务从 15.5% 拉到 56.0%。
- **计算开销极小：** 额外只是多跑编码器前向（编码器约 0.6B，远小于约 7B 的 LLM 主干），且只改动 ≤0.12% 的编码器神经元。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies individual feed-forward neurons *inside the audio encoder* as a previously overlooked intervention site for improving acoustic perception in LALMs, in both locus (encoder, not decoder/LLM) and granularity (neuron, not layer/direction).
- Proposes IAAN, a training-free, label-free intervention: it scores each encoder neuron by contrasting activations on the real waveform and a noise reference (Gaussian noise matched in length/mean/variance, fixed seed), then amplifies the top-K via a forward hook — no calibration set, attribute labels, fixed steering direction, or extra LLM decode.
- Shows across three open-source LALMs (Audio-Flamingo-3, Qwen2.5-Omni, Kimi-Audio) that IAAN substantially improves accuracy on ten non-semantic attributes, and through controlled ablations establishes that *both* the encoder locus and the selectivity of the neuron set are necessary for the gain.
- Demonstrates complementarity with explicit fine-tuning: IAAN still improves AF3-PD (a model tuned specifically for paralinguistic evidence), notably raising its weakest attribute (intonation) from 15.5% to 56.0%.
- Establishes that the gain is achievable by modifying at most 0.12% of the encoder's feed-forward neurons (K=200 of N=163,840), with negligible overhead since the encoder (~0.6B) is far smaller than the LLM backbone (~7B).

## Methodology

IAAN operates entirely within the audio encoder of an LALM at inference time, with no training and no attribute labels.

**Neuron definition.** For a transformer FFN block FFN(h) = W2 φ(W1 h) with hidden width d_ff, each coordinate a_i = φ(W1 h)_i of the intermediate activation is treated as one "neuron." An L-layer encoder thus has N = L·d_ff neurons (e.g., 32 × 5120 = 163,840 for a Whisper-large-v3 encoder).

**Acoustic score (Eq. 1).** For each clip, the encoder runs twice: once on the real waveform x and once on a reference r that lacks the real audio's acoustic cues. The reference is Gaussian noise of the same length and approximately the same mean/variance as x, drawn with a fixed per-clip seed (silence and high-noise references are ablated as alternatives). For neuron i at frame t:

  s_i = mean_t a_i^real(t) − mean_t a_i^ref(t)

A large positive s_i marks a neuron that responds more to real audio than to noise. The score is computed per-clip from the clip's own activations only — no external dataset.

**Selection and amplification (Eq. 2).** Neurons are ranked by s_i and the top-K ("neuron budget") form the amplification set S. During the real-audio encoder pass, the activations of neurons in S are scaled by a gain g > 1 via a forward hook: a_i ← g·a_i for i ∈ S. The amplified encoder output is then passed to the LLM as usual.

**Hyperparameter selection (strictly held-out).** (K, g) are tuned on an *external* development set — 190 sarcastic-speech clips from LISTEN — rebuilt into MCQs whose adversarial label is the emotion implied by the words. The selection objective is the listening advantage LA = Acc − ALA, which jointly rewards higher ground-truth accuracy and lower adversarial-label agreement. All reported VoxParadox results use these dev-set configurations, not an oracle sweep over the test set.

**Evaluation protocol.** VoxParadox is the testbed: ten non-semantic attributes (gender, age, emotion, intonation, speaker counting, speaker identity, speed, volume, vocal range, pitch), 200 MCQ items each, where the voice carries one attribute while the spoken words imply a different (adversarial) one. Metrics are accuracy (Acc ↑) and adversarial-label agreement (ALA ↓); a genuine improvement should raise Acc and lower ALA together. Greedy decoding is used throughout.

**Baselines isolate alternative loci.** AAD (audio-aware decoding) contrasts output logits; LLM-amplify applies IAAN's same acoustic-rank criterion to LLM FFN neurons at audio-token positions; HS-steer adds a dense steering vector to the encoder residual stream at the layer where IAAN-selected neurons concentrate.

## Results & Highlights

- On Audio-Flamingo-3 (AF3) on VoxParadox, IAAN raises average accuracy from 13.1% (reproduced unamplified baseline) to 38.8% (+25.7 points) while cutting adversarial-label agreement from 75.5% to 40.5% (−35.0 points); on gender specifically it jumps from 15.0% to 83.0%.
- On Qwen2.5-Omni, average accuracy rises from 7.6% to 29.0% (+21.4 points) with ALA dropping from 76.9% to 48.1% (−28.8 points); on Kimi-Audio, accuracy rises from 21.2% to 30.9% (+9.7 points) with ALA from 64.0% to 43.0% (−21.0 points). With IAAN, all three open-source models surpass Gemini 2.5 Flash (24.7% Acc / 60.5% ALA) and GPT-4o Audio (8.6% / 81.6%).
- On the specialized fine-tuned AF3-PD, IAAN still improves average accuracy from 70.5% to 75.0% and ALA from 22.4% to 17.2%; on its weakest attribute, intonation, accuracy goes from 15.5% to 56.0%.
- The locus matters: post-encoder AAD changes accuracy by at most a few points in either direction on all four models, and LLM-amplify leaves AF3/Qwen2.5/Kimi near baseline while *collapsing* AF3-PD (70.5%→40.2%). Dense HS-steer at the same encoder layer reaches only 20.9%/13.7%/18.2%/71.3% versus IAAN's 38.8%/29.0%/30.9%/75.0%.
- Selectivity matters: random neuron selection barely beats baseline; whole-layer amplification (Layer-amplify) stays at baseline; restricting selection to the single layer with the most IAAN neurons fully recovers the gain on Qwen2.5 (29.0%) and most of it on AF3 (37.2% vs 38.8%), but drops below baseline on Kimi (20.7%) whose selected neurons are more spread across layers.
- The intervention is tiny but effective: IAAN modifies at most 0.12% of the encoder's feed-forward neurons (K=200 of N=163,840), the acoustic-score selection threshold sits about 13 standard deviations above the mean of a score distribution concentrated near zero, and nearly 90% of (K, g) configurations outperform the unamplified baseline.
- Reference ablation: Gaussian noise and silence give similar results; performance stays high at SNR −20 dB (≈ noise) but degrades as the reference retains more of the source clip (AF3: 38.3% at −20 dB → 21.9% at +20 dB), because shared content cancels out of the contrast.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are from National Taiwan University (NTU), the Graduate Institute of Communication Engineering at NTU, the NTU AI Center of Research Excellence (NTU AI-CoRE), and the ASUS Open Cloud Infrastructure Software Center. Hung-yi Lee is a well-established speech/LLM researcher, and the group has produced a dense recent line of LALM evaluation and analysis work (AudioLens, SAKE, SAKURA, MuGEN, Speech-IFEval, the VoxParadox-adjacent "holistic evaluation" survey) — several of which are cited here as the benchmark and related-work backbone. The paper is unusually careful methodologically: it keeps VoxParadox strictly held out by tuning on LISTEN, reports a reproduced (not just published) baseline, jointly tracks accuracy and adversarial-label agreement, and isolates the contributions of locus and neuron selectivity with a matched LLM-amplify ablation. An AI-generated-content disclosure notes AI tools were used only for language editing.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel in *where* it intervenes. Prior LALM inference-time work operates after the encoder (contrastive decoding [28–30], hidden-state steering [31,32,66], label-selected emotion neurons in speech-generative models [34]); IAAN is the first to move inside the audio encoder at neuron granularity, and it is label-free unlike the emotion-labeled neuron control in [34]. The contrast-with-noise-reference scoring is a clean, minimalist idea.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The matched LLM-amplify ablation (same acoustic-rank criterion, same fraction, but applied to LLM neurons) is a sharp control that isolates the encoder locus; random- and layer-amplify controls isolate selectivity; the LISTEN dev set keeps VoxParadox honestly held out. The dual metric (Acc↑ and ALA↓) guards against "predictions just shift to a different wrong option."
- **Reproducibility:** Reasonable. All three target models are open-source (AF3, Qwen2.5-Omni, Kimi-Audio), the method is a forward-hook with two hyperparameters, and Table II exposes the full (K, g) grid. Caveats: the precise encoder internals and hook implementation per model are not fully specified in the text, the LISTEN dev set construction (rebuilding 190 sarcastic clips into MCQs) is described only briefly, and absolute numbers for emotion on Kimi *drop* under IAAN (83.5%→18.0%) — a per-task regression worth flagging that is smoothed over by averages.
- **Significance:** The headline gains are large (+9.7 to +25.7 points) and the modified neuron fraction (≤0.12%) is striking, but absolute accuracy after IAAN is still modest on AF3 (38.8%) and Qwen2.5 (29.0%) — VoxParadox is a deliberately adversarial benchmark, so this is expected, but it means the method improves a weak capability rather than solving it.
- **Red flags / data quirks:** (1) Kimi-Audio's emotion accuracy collapses from 83.5% to 18.0% under IAAN — the only large per-task regression — and the paper does not explain it; the average still rises because other Kimi tasks improve. (2) IAAN (Layer-restricted) goes *below* baseline on Kimi, undercutting generality for encoders whose acoustic neurons are not concentrated near the output. (3) Hyperparameters are model-dependent (g from 8 to 16, K from 50 to 200) and Kimi has configurations that fall below baseline, so deployment requires a held-out set per model — a practical limitation the authors concede as future work. (4) VoxParadox is a single benchmark from one group (the AF3-PD authors); independent corroboration on other paralinguistic benchmarks (SD-Eval, AudioBench, Vox-Profile) would strengthen the claim.

## Strengths

- Clean, minimal, and well-motivated core idea; fully training-free and label-free, requiring no calibration set.
- Excellent experimental hygiene: strictly held-out VoxParadox (tuned on LISTEN), reproduced baselines, a matched LLM-amplify control that cleanly attributes the gain to the encoder locus, and joint Acc/ALA reporting.
- Striking efficiency: ≤0.12% of encoder neurons modified for up to +25.7 points.
- Complementarity with fine-tuning is demonstrated, not assumed — IAAN improves the already-tuned AF3-PD.
- Qualitative free-text examples show the effect transfers beyond MCQ selection to how the model describes acoustic properties.

## Limitations

- Absolute accuracy after IAAN remains modest on the two weaker models (AF3 38.8%, Qwen2.5 29.0%) — the capability is improved, not solved.
- Per-task regressions exist, most notably Kimi-Audio emotion dropping 83.5%→18.0%, which is unexplained.
- Hyperparameters are model-specific and require a held-out dev set per model; principled automatic (K, g) selection is left to future work.
- Generalization is shown only on VoxParadox; no results on other paralinguistic benchmarks (e.g., SD-Eval, AudioBench, Vox-Profile).
- The reason later encoder layers concentrate acoustic-discriminative neurons is explicitly left as an open question; no mechanistic explanation is given.
- Proprietary models (GPT-4o Audio, Gemini 2.5 Flash) appear only as context numbers from prior work, without IAAN applied.

## Relevance

对关注音频大模型可解释性与推理时干预的研究有直接参考价值：它把"神经元级 steering"这一在文本 LLM 上成熟的思路前移到音频编码器内部，既是一个轻量增强副语言感知的实用方法，也为后续从编码器内部结构入手提升声学理解提供了新方向，与语音/多模态理解、模型机理可解释性的兴趣高度契合。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Why do later encoder layers concentrate more acoustic-discriminative neurons, and does this pattern hold for non-Whisper encoders (e.g., BEATs, encoder used by Kimi)?
- Can the per-clip noise-reference contrast be replaced by a one-time, dataset-level neuron ranking to avoid the extra encoder pass while preserving the gain?
- What drives the large per-task regressions (e.g., Kimi emotion 83.5%→18.0%) — is the amplified neuron set overlapping with neurons that already encode that attribute well?
- Does IAAN transfer beyond VoxParadox to other paralinguistic benchmarks (SD-Eval, AudioBench, Vox-Profile), and does the gain hold for free-form (non-MCQ) generation quantitatively, not just qualitatively?
- Could the acoustic score be used for *suppression* of strongly text-biased neurons (large negative s_i) as an additional lever, and would that help on cross-modal consistency benchmarks?
- Is the 0.12%-of-neurons result a property of VoxParadox's adversarial setup, or would a similarly sparse set suffice for genuine (non-adversarial) paralinguistic tasks in the wild?
