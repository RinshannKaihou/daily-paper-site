00

The thesis, in one panel

TL;DR

For a unified family of block-list inductive tasks (in-context n-grams, k-hop induction, IOI, associative recall…), the merged key-query matrix and unembedding weights of an attention-only Transformer provably stay inside a low-dimensional, interpretable subspace — the IMIR. On a 2-layer/1-head model its 28 coordinates collapse to four functional directions (α β γ implement an induction head for ICL; δ implements IWL), letting the authors analytically dissect the ICL↔IWL circuit battle and automatically discover induction circuits at weight granularity.

01 · UNIFICATION

One theorem, many tasks

A single block-list formulation covers n-grams, k-hop induction, IOI, conditional retrieval, permutation inversion & associative recall.

02 · CONFINEMENT

Gradient can't escape

Theorem 1 + Corollary 1: population GD keeps weights on IMIR. Proven via exact invariance of attention scores under token & position symmetries.

03 · COMPETITION

ICL vs IWL, closed-form

IWL (δ) saturates first and starves the ICL gradient; burstiness amplifies it back. Mechanistic, not phenomenological.

04 · LOTTERY

A verifiable ticket

Training only on the (α,β,γ,δ) sub-manifold reproduces the unconstrained 2-layer dynamics — a sparse subnetwork.

05 · INIT PHASES

Who wins is set at init

3-layer scan over thousands of seeds maps sharp phase boundaries between three competing induction-head circuits.

06 · DISCOVERY

Weight-granularity circuits

Greedy IMIR projection beats head-level (ACDC) discovery, surfacing non-canonical "aiding" directions otherwise invisible.

01

How the manifold is built & bounded

Primarily theoretical, supported by controlled small-scale experiments.
The argument rests on two data symmetries: the distribution is invariant under token permutations that preserve relational structure (the group Aut(V,R)), and under random positional offsets per block. Because attention scores are exactly invariant under both, the expected gradient must be invariant too — which forces it to live in a low-dimensional subspace S, the IMIR.
Task

Block-list inputs

Sequence of n blocks of ≤k tokens; next token depends on prior blocks via relational structure R.

Arch

Attention-only, merged key-query

Sinusoidal positions; L layers, H heads. KQ product treated as one learnable matrix; values = fixed orthogonal projections (learnable in appendices).

Basis

Selection · writing · action bases

Token/position selection matrices T,P; per-layer writing bases V_ℓ; action bases & pseudo-inverses accumulate multi-layer compositions → layer basis W_ℓ, output basis W̄.

Proof

Theorem 1 — Gradient Confinement

Under six stated assumptions, W ∈ S ⇒ E[∇L] ∈ S; Corollary 1 makes S an invariant manifold.

Tool

Greedy backward elimination

Project trained weights onto IMIR, ablate directions with threshold τ=100, slack ρ=1.01 → compact weight-granularity circuit.

Theorem 2 · ICL suppression
IWL starves the ICL gradient

Once the one-dimensional IWL direction (δ) saturates, it suppresses the ICL gradient by a closed-form factor depending on rare-token rate f_r and intra-class variability f_v.

suppression = f_r + (1 − f_r)·f_v
Theorem 3 · Burstiness boost
Burstiness multiplies the ICL gradient

In the distractor-dominated regime, burstiness parameter b amplifies the ICL gradient roughly b-fold — a mechanistic explanation of a previously phenomenological observation.

ICL_grad ≈ b × (unsuppressed)

03

Strengths vs. limitations

▲ What lands well

  • Genuinely unifying. A single theorem covers tasks the literature had treated separately.
  • Each IMIR coordinate has a concrete semantic — "attend to previous position", "match retrieved token", "read out label" — tractable and interpretable.
  • Closed-form gradient suppression (Thm 2) gives a crisp mechanistic account of why rare tokens, variability & burstiness govern ICL emergence.
  • Honest about scope — explicitly states it proves invariance but not attraction.
  • Cheap experiments (sub-minute training, hours for the seed scan) make the claims easy to check.

! Where it overreaches

  • Strong architectural assumptions: attention-only, merged key-query, fixed orthogonal values, orthonormal embeddings. MLPs/LN/learnable values only sketched in appendices.
  • Synthetic block-list tasks only — no natural-language data, no scale where "emergence" is usually discussed.
  • Population-gradient result; finite-batch SGD behaviour is empirical only.
  • Circuit-discovery lacks seed variance and a direct head-to-head quantitative comparison with ACDC/EAP on the same model.
  • 3-layer "winning circuit" scan varies only two init coordinates — the full high-dimensional init landscape is unexplored.
04

Open questions this forces

The most consequential follow-ups for the framework.
Q1 · ATTRACTION

Can the IMIR be made attractive — provably convergent from off-manifold inits under realistic (finite-batch, learnable-values, MLP/LN) conditions?

Q2 · LEARNABLE VALUES

Do the theorems survive when value maps are learnable rather than fixed orthogonal — does the Thm-2 suppression factor still hold?

Q3 · BENCHMARKING

Can weight-granularity discovery be benchmarked against ACDC / EAP / attribution patching on the same model, with false-positive & coverage rates?

Q4 · SCALING

Does the (α,β,γ,δ) decomposition persist in larger attention-only models (6–12 layers, many heads), or does the IMIR dimension explode past interpretability?

Q5 · PREDICTIVE INIT

Can the framework predict which initialization wins from init properties alone — turning the phase-transition observation into a rule?

Q6 · DEEP REASONING

How does the ICL↔IWL story interact with multi-step (k>2) reasoning, where deep circuits reportedly need implicit curriculum or exponentially more data?
