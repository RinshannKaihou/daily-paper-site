> **一句话总结：** 该论文首次在视频 Diffusion Transformer（DiT）内部发现了专门负责"运动"和"结构"的不同注意力头，并据此提出免训练的运动迁移框架 HALO——用运动头构造位移图、用语义对应细化运动、用低熵结构头注入特征，在标准基准和新建的电影场景数据集上，运动保真度（MF）较最强基线 GWTF 提升 62.5→66.2，同时改善结构对齐。

> **TL;DR:** HALO performs a head-level mechanistic analysis of video Diffusion Transformers and discovers distinct motion-specific and structure-specialized attention heads; building on this, it proposes a training-free motion-transfer method that, on the standard motion-transfer benchmark (built on CogVideoX), lifts Motion Fidelity from 62.5 (GWTF, the strongest DiT baseline) to 66.2 and lowers FTD from 21.6 to 19.4, while also introducing a new Movie Scene Dataset where it raises MF from 46.6 to 52.6 over GWTF.

## 核心贡献

- **首次对视频 DiT 进行注意力头级别的功能分析**：通过位移图（displacement map）和注意力熵两类探针，证明视频 DiT（CogVideoX）内部存在明确分工的"运动专用头"（跨帧对角注意力、与光流对齐）和"结构专用头"（低熵、强对角、特征空间熵也低）。
- **提出免训练运动迁移框架 HALO**：不更新任何参数，仅通过 (a) 基于语义对应的运动引导（Semantic Correspondence Refinement + Semantic Reweighting + 位移图优化）和 (b) 选择性结构头特征注入，同时实现运动保真和结构对齐。
- **首次将语义对应引入运动表示**：用扩散特征的语义相似度去校正跨帧注意力的位移图，解决"运动被错误转移到语义无关区域"的问题。
- **构建并开源 Movie Scene Dataset**：针对现有基准（DAVIS/WebVid 衍生）偏向通用网络视频、无法反映电影级运镜的不足，提供面向真实电影场景的制作级评测集。
- **验证头级别控制的普适性**：把运动头/结构头机制扩展到视频编辑和（结合 Layer Perturbation）运动强度控制，VBench 上图像质量 +14%、美学 +8%、运动动态 +15%。

## Key Contributions

- **First head-level functional analysis of video DiTs**: Using displacement maps and attention/feature entropy probes, the authors demonstrate that video DiTs (CogVideoX) contain distinct motion-specific heads (cross-frame diagonal attention, aligned with ground-truth optical flow) and structure-specialized heads (low attention entropy, sharply diagonal, with low hidden-feature spatial entropy).
- **HALO, a training-free motion-transfer framework**: Requires no parameter updates; jointly achieves motion fidelity via semantic-aware motion guidance (Semantic Correspondence Refinement, Semantic Reweighting, displacement-map latent optimization) and structural alignment via selective injection of low-entropy structural heads.
- **First integration of semantic correspondence into motion representations**: Uses diffusion-feature semantic similarity to correct cross-frame-attention displacement maps, fixing the well-known failure where motion is transferred to semantically irrelevant regions.
- **A new Movie Scene Dataset**: A production-oriented benchmark curated to evaluate faithful motion transfer under real cinematic conditions, complementing DAVIS/WebVid-derived benchmarks.
- **Generalizable head-level control primitives**: Extends motion/structure heads to structure-preserving video editing and to motion-dynamics control (via head perturbation on CogVideoX), improving VBench image quality +14%, aesthetics +8%, motion dynamics +15%.

## Methodology

HALO is built on CogVideoX (a video Diffusion Transformer) and requires no parameter updates. The method has an analysis stage and two complementary guidance stages.

**Head-level analysis (Sec. 3).** The authors probe individual attention heads rather than whole layers.
- *Analysis 1 — Motion-specific heads.* They build per-head cross-frame attention M^h_{f,f'} = softmax(Q^h_f · K^{h⊤}_{f'}/√d), derive a head-specific displacement map D^h via argmax over frames and 2D coordinate differences, and compare these maps to RAFT ground-truth optical flow using Directional Alignment (cosine similarity of motion directions) and Correlation (Pearson). Temporal heads (those matching cross-frame-diagonal pattern masks) score higher on both metrics and produce displacement maps that track object and background motion, whereas spatial and all-head aggregations dilute the motion signal.
- *Analysis 2 — Structure-specialized heads.* They compute attention entropy H_attn = −Σ A^h_k log A^h_k and the spatial entropy of PCA-projected attention features. Low-attention-entropy heads (e.g., L20 H8, entropy 3.34) exhibit sharply diagonal attention maps and low feature spatial entropy (0.44), whereas high-entropy heads (L20 H6, 7.89 / 0.77) are diffuse. This establishes attention entropy as a selector for structurally informative heads.

**Semantic-aware motion guidance (Sec. 4.1).**
- *Semantic Correspondence Refinement (SCR).* Instead of taking the single argmax displacement, the method keeps the top-k cross-frame attention candidates and picks, among them, the patch whose diffusion feature ϕ is most cosine-similar to the query patch (semantic correspondence C). This yields a semantically aligned reference displacement map D_ref.
- *Semantic Reweighting (SRW).* During generation, a correspondence-guided bias β is added to the cross-frame attention scores at the semantic best-match index (Ẽ = E + B), bias strength β = 0.1.
- *Motion optimization.* The expected displacement is computed from the refined attention, stacked into a target displacement D_t, and the initial noise latent z_T is optimized (12 optimization steps) to minimize an L2 "semantic motion loss" L_SM between D_ref and D_t.

**Selective structural head injection (Sec. 4.2).** Naïvely injecting all reference head features causes noise and identity leakage. HALO injects only the value features of heads with attention entropy below threshold τ (= 7, the observed median), supplying structure-aware guidance that complements motion optimization and enforces spatial alignment with the reference.

**Settings.** CogVideoX backbone, 50 denoising steps, guidance scale 7, β = 0.1, τ < 7, top-k candidates; results on the Wan video DiT are reported in the supplement.

## Results & Highlights

- **Standard motion-transfer benchmark (Table 1, CogVideoX backbone):** HALO reaches CLIP 31.7 / TC 87.5 / **MF 66.2** / **FTD 19.4**, improving Motion Fidelity over the strongest DiT baseline GWTF (62.5) by **+3.7** and lowering FTD from GWTF's 21.6 to 19.4 (−2.2); the next-best MF among baselines is DiTFlow's 59.6.
- **Movie Scene Dataset (Table 2):** HALO attains CLIP 30.5 / TC 88.9 / **MF 52.6**, beating GWTF's MF 46.6 by **+6.0** and DiTFlow's 48.4, showing the gains transfer to real cinematic clips.
- **Complex-motion stratification (Table 5):** On Hard trajectories HALO MF 59.2 vs GWTF 56.0 (+3.2), Medium 72.3 vs 69.0 (+3.3), Easy 75.5 vs 74.0, with minimal performance decay across difficulty levels.
- **Head-selection ablation (Table 6a):** Using only motion-specific heads gives MF 66.2 / FTD 19.4, vs all-heads MF 63.1 / FTD 21.6 and spatial-only MF 53.6 / FTD 22.7, confirming that spatial heads dilute motion cues.
- **Component ablation (Table 3):** Baseline displacement optimization (Exp.#1) gives MF 59.6 / FTD 23.0; adding injection alone (Exp.#2) → MF 61.8 / FTD 20.5; adding semantic guidance alone (Exp.#3) → MF 61.3 / FTD 20.9; full HALO → MF 66.2 / FTD 19.4, showing both components contribute and combine super-additively.
- **Entropy-threshold validation (Table 6b):** Injecting low-entropy heads (τ < 7) yields MF 66.2 / FTD 19.4, whereas τ > 7 (high-entropy) heads degrade to MF 54.1 / FTD 23.5.
- **User study (Table 4, 5-point Likert, 20 participants, normalized 0–100):** HALO leads on all three axes — Edit Acc. 86.5, TC 87.8, Motion Acc. 93.3 — vs GWTF 84.0 / 77.1 / 72.8 and RoPECraft 72.4 / 75.6 / 77.6.
- **Head perturbation extension (VBench, 100 samples, CogVideoX):** image quality +14%, aesthetics +8%, motion dynamics +15%, indicating the motion-head analysis generalizes as a lightweight inference-time control.
- **Compute overhead:** HALO incurs only marginal runtime/GPU-memory overhead relative to the displacement-optimization framework (Supp. Sec. F.3).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel angle. Prior DiT motion-transfer work (GWTF noise-warping, RoPECraft RoPE-warping, DiTFlow cross-frame-attention displacement) manipulates representations without explaining *where* motion/structure live. HALO's head-level dissection (motion vs structure heads selected by displacement-map optical-flow alignment and attention entropy respectively) is a real mechanistic contribution, and injecting only low-entropy (τ < 7) heads to avoid identity leakage is a principled fix to a documented failure of naïve feature injection.
- **Rigor:** Solid. The head claims are validated two ways — qualitatively (Fig. 3–4) and quantitatively against RAFT optical flow (Directional Alignment, Correlation). The entropy threshold τ = 7 is justified as the observed median and then swept (Table 6b, Table 9a). The main result is backed by ablations (Table 3), a head-config ablation (Table 6a), a difficulty stratification (Table 5), a 20-person user study (Table 4), and a second backbone (Wan, supplement).
- **Reproducibility:** Good signs — planned code release, fixed hyperparameters (β = 0.1, Topt = 12, top-k, τ < 7, 50 denoising steps, guidance 7), and the Movie Scene Dataset is described. However, the dataset is provided under a partner (RASCA FX) and its long-term availability/licensing for re-run is unclear, and no seeds or exact reference-video list are given in the main text.
- **Significance:** Meaningful but bounded. Gains over GWTF are consistent but modest in absolute terms (MF +3.7, FTD −2.2), and HALO is slightly *worse* than DiTFlow and GWTF on Temporal Consistency (87.5 vs 89.5/88.2), which the authors attribute to TC favoring static outputs — a plausible but self-serving interpretation that is only partially deconstructed in Supp. Sec. F.2.
- **Red flags / weak spots:**
  - *Self-reported trade-off.* The TC deficit is hand-waved with "TC favors static outputs"; a TC variant that corrects for motion magnitude would strengthen this.
  - *Metric gaming risk acknowledged.* The F.4 limitations note that over-aligned semantic correspondence can produce *unnatural* motion (e.g., "a penguin walking like a bear") that nonetheless scores *higher* MF — an honest admission that MF is an imperfect proxy and that HALO's headline MF gain partly rewards implausible kinematics.
  - *Patch-level ceiling.* Fine-grained local motion (mouth shapes, facial expressions) remains hard because motion is represented at patch-level displacement, a structural limitation not an engineering one.
  - *Single backbone in main paper.* CogVideoX is the only main-text backbone; Wan is relegated to the supplement, so generalization across DiT families is asserted more than demonstrated.

## Strengths

- Principled, interpretable foundation: motion and structure are each tied to identifiable, entropy/flow-measurable attention heads, giving controllable video generation a mechanistic handle rather than another black-box trick.
- Training-free and lightweight — no fine-tuning, marginal compute overhead, plug-in to existing DiTs (CogVideoX, Wan).
- Strong, multi-axis evaluation: four metrics, ablations, difficulty stratification, user study, and a second backbone.
- The Movie Scene Dataset fills a real gap: existing DAVIS/WebVid-derived benchmarks under-represent professional cinematic shot composition.
- Demonstrated generalization beyond motion transfer — structure-head injection enables identity-swapping video editing, and motion-head perturbation improves VBench dynamics.

## Limitations

- Patch-level displacement cannot capture fine, non-rigid local motion such as detailed facial expressions or mouth shapes (Supp. F.4, Fig. 29).
- Semantic correspondence can over-align structurally incompatible articulations, producing unnatural kinematics (e.g., penguin walking like a bear) that paradoxically score high on MF — exposing an inherent trade-off between reference-following and plausibility.
- Slightly lower Temporal Consistency than DiTFlow/GWTF; the authors' "TC favors static outputs" defense is only partly substantiated.
- Movie Scene Dataset availability depends on a commercial partner (RASCA FX); long-term reproducibility of that benchmark is uncertain.
- Main-text experiments use a single backbone (CogVideoX); cross-DiT-family generalization is shown only in the supplement (Wan).
- Hyperparameters (β, τ, top-k) are fixed rather than adapted per video; sensitivity sweeps exist (Table 9) but per-instance tuning is unexplored.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the motion-specific vs structure-specialized head dichotomy hold across other video DiTs (HunyuanVideo, Sora-class models, Mochi), or is it specific to CogVideoX's unified attention design?
- Can the semantic-correspondence refinement be made adaptive to avoid the "penguin walking like a bear" over-alignment failure, e.g., by gating part-level correspondence with kinematic-plausibility priors?
- How would a motion-magnitude-normalized Temporal Consistency metric affect HALO's ranking, given the authors' claim that standard TC favors static outputs?
- Is the attention-entropy threshold τ = 7 stable across resolutions, frame counts, and backbones, or does it need re-estimation per model?
- Can motion-specific heads be used not just for transfer/perturbation but for explicit direction-and-intensity control (the stated future-work direction)?
- How does HALO behave on longer, multi-shot references, and could the structure-specialized heads enable consistent identity across shot boundaries?
