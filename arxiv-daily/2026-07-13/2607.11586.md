> **一句话总结：** 针对MoE大模型在3.5D多chiplet系统上的"热专家"持续偏斜问题，HCRMap提出一种双时间尺度框架——慢循环用可行性约束的Masked Double-DQN在SRAM/HBM/DRAM三层间决定热专家副本的晋升/保留/降级/驱逐，快循环用确定性的压力代理为每个token组选择副本，在LEGOSim上对8个MoE模型实测，相比Hydra/MoEntwine/PIMoE基线在prefill/decode阶段端到端延迟分别降低34.5%/33.1%~46.7%/46.0%。

> **TL;DR:** On 3.5D multi-chiplet MoE inference, persistent hot-expert skew causes coupled compute/communication/memory-bank/queue pressure that no single-axis optimizer (placement, replication, or caching) resolves. HCRMap couples a slow feasibility-constrained Masked Double-DQN residency controller (promote/retain/demote/evict across SRAM/HBM/DRAM, with counterfactual same-window reward) and a deterministic fast token-to-replica mapper driven by queue/path/bank/streaming pressure proxies. On the LEGOSim simulator across 8 MoE models (DeepSeek-V3, Mixtral-8x22B, DBRX, Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B, etc.), HCRMap cuts geometric-mean end-to-end latency by 34.5%/33.1% over MoEntwine and 46.7%/46.0% over PIMoE in prefill/decode; under matched resident-copy capacity it beats the strongest fixed-replica baseline by 24.95%/23.91% with 19.94%/19.86% EDP gain.

## 核心贡献

- **3.5D MoE多级热专家驻留底座：** 首次将热专家副本管理从"resident vs non-resident"二分升级为SRAM（热）→ HBM（温）→ DRAM（回退）三级驻留层次，引入版本化专家-副本目录、bank隔离副本区、非阻塞分块权重迁移与轻量压力计数器，使温专家可降级到中间层而避免远端DRAM反复流式加载。
- **双时间尺度压力感知映射器：** 慢循环用可行性约束的Masked Double-DQN学习跨窗口的晋升/保留/降级/驱逐策略（同一窗口反事实奖励，避免负载漂移干扰归因），快循环用固定权重的归一化压力代理做确定性token到副本分配，分离两个时间尺度使布局稳定而路由能即时响应压力。
- **关键观察的实证支撑：** 通过LEGOSim刻画出三条规律——降低通信量≠降低延迟（Hydra大幅削减D2D流量但遗留排队瓶颈）；副本容量存在"悬崖"边界（容量不足时固定复制反而增延迟）；中间HBM层可削减温专家重复流式加载。
- **全3.5D统一基线复现：** 在同一LEGOSim事件流、同一硬件图、同一router输出迹、同一能耗系数下复现Hydra、MoEntwine、PIMoE三种基线，保证比较公平。

## Key Contributions

- **A bounded multi-level hot-expert residency substrate for 3.5D MoE serving.** Distributes hot-expert weights across stacked SRAM, chiplet-local HBM, and group-shared DRAM by streaming cost and hotness; lets warm experts degrade to an intermediate tier and avoid the near-tier capacity cliff, with bank-isolated replica regions, a versioned cross-tier expert-to-replica directory, non-blocking chunked weight transfer, and lightweight pressure counters.
- **A two-timescale pressure-aware mapper.** Couples slow cross-tier residency updates (masked Double-DQN with minimum-residency, amortization, hysteresis, and bounded-update constraints; counterfactual reward) with a fast deterministic token-to-replica assignment that ranks legal replicas by a fixed-weight normalized pressure proxy (queue/path/bank/streaming). The separation keeps the layout stable while routing reacts to instantaneous pressure.
- **Pressure-driven motivation characterization.** Uses LEGOSim to demonstrate three effects that prior single-axis optimizers miss: (1) Hydra-style communication minimization leaves residual hot-load queueing on the critical path; (2) resident-copy capacity has a sharp cliff where fixed replication re-concentrates load; (3) an intermediate HBM tier removes repeated DRAM streaming.
- **Unified re-implementation of chiplet/wafer/NPU-PIM MoE baselines** (Hydra, MoEntwine, PIMoE) inside the same LEGOSim event flow and 3.5D substrate, so all comparisons share identical hardware constraints, router-output traces, memory hierarchy, and energy accounting.

## Methodology

HCRMap runs on a Mozart-class 3.5D package (16 compute chiplets, 4 IO/memory chiplets, 256 cores, 4×4 mesh, UCIe-class D2D at 512 GB/s full-duplex, with 64 MB SRAM, 8 GB HBM, 32 GB DRAM tiers) modeled in the LEGOSim event-level simulator.

**Problem formulation.** Hot-expert residency mapping is posed as an online constrained optimization minimizing the routed-MoE completion cost `min_{M_w} Σ T_l^MoE(M_w)` subject to (a) routing correctness (each token group assigned to exactly one legal replica of its routed expert), (b) bank capacity (resident weights + activation/buffer + metadata ≤ bank capacity), (c) per-expert replica-count limits, and (d) a global residency-weight budget. The runtime decision `M_w = (R_w, φ_w, x_w)` simultaneously chooses resident replicas, their tier/chiplet/bank placement, and token-to-replica assignments — three coupled decisions.

**Slow residency loop (masked Double-DQN).** For each decision window, candidate experts (persistent hot, SRAM-overflow warm, stale replicas) generate a candidate action set {no-op, promote(e,t,c), retain(e,t,c), demote(e,t,c), evict(e,t,c)}. A legal mask removes any action violating replica-count, residency-budget, bank-capacity, or migration-path feasibility before action selection, so the controller only ever selects from legal edits. Reward is same-window counterfactual: `(T_cf - T_act)/T_cf - μ1·T̂_mig - μ2·C_churn`, evaluated by replaying the same routed token groups under both the previous and the acted directory inside the simulator (counterfactual is used only offline; at eval time the Q-network is frozen and the counterfactual layout is not run). Double DQN target with `argmax` over the legally masked next action set controls overestimation; replica age and `U^max=1` updates per window bound churn.

**Latency-aware placement.** When the controller selects promote/retain, the concrete tier/chiplet/bank is chosen by minimizing a placement cost combining weighted source-token distance, compute-queue pressure, bank access pressure, tier capacity scarcity, and replica-location diversity — not raw communication distance.

**Fast token-to-replica mapping (deterministic).** Within a window, each token group is sent to the replica minimizing a fixed-weight normalized pressure proxy `C_tok = ω_q·C^queue + ω_p·C^path + ω_b·C^bank + ω_s·C^stream`. Tokens are processed in small blocks so queue/link/bank pressure is refreshed after each block, preventing stale window-start state from flooding one replica. The proxy only ranks legal replicas; physical latency is still measured by the event simulator.

**Stable migration and versioned directory.** Cross-tier migration is throttled: it fires only when foreground + migration utilization stays below a safety threshold on every path link (Eq. 40); otherwise the action degrades to no-op/retain. Promotion/demotion never overwrites the old replica — chunks copy into a pending region and a new directory version is published only after all chunks complete, leveraging read-only inference weights to avoid coherence.

## Results & Highlights

- **Geometric-mean end-to-end latency over 8 MoE models (unified LEGOSim comparison):** HCRMap reduces prefill / decode latency by **43.6% / 43.0% over Hydra**, **34.5% / 33.1% over MoEntwine**, and **46.7% / 46.0% over PIMoE** (abstract headline numbers, consistent with the body).
- **Matched resident-copy capacity (strongest fixed-replica baseline, 1.3× copy capacity):** normalized end-to-end latency reduced by **24.95% (prefill) and 23.91% (decode)**, with **EDP improved by 19.94% and 19.86%**. Per-benchmark-first then averaged: latency reduction **19.63%±2.74% (prefill), 19.04%±3.09% (decode)**; EDP **19.94%±2.80%, 19.86%±3.24%**.
- **Ablation against own variants (matched capacity):** vs no-fast-mapping variant, HCRMap reduces latency by **51.2% (prefill) and 50.7% (decode)**; vs no-HBM-tier variant, reduces **decode latency by 18.4%** and **repeated streaming bytes by 44.0%**. Pressure-signal ablation shows removing link/bank/compute/migration-exposure awareness each increases latency, with full HCRMap (all four signals) lowest.
- **Metadata footprint is negligible:** directory entries ≤64 B/replica (<2 MB total for the largest directory), pressure counters <4 KB, at most one replica moved per window, `U^max = 1`.
- **Benchmarks:** DeepSeekMoE-16B, DeepSeek-V2-Lite, DeepSeek-V2, DeepSeek-V3, Mixtral-8x7B, Mixtral-8x22B, DBRX, Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B; prefill = 2048 input tokens, decode = 512 active sequence tokens; router outputs replayed with no synthetic popularity skew calibration.
- **Spatial heatmap (Figure 8):** Hydra/MoEntwine/PIMoE form localized hot spots on the 4×4 grid; HCRMap produces a smoother, less concentrated compute-pressure distribution.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Real and specific to 3.5D. The framing — hot-expert management is *not* a cache-hit or pure communication-minimization problem, but a coupled pressure-aware multi-level residency problem — is a genuine reframing, and the two-timescale decomposition (slow feasibility-masked DQN + fast deterministic proxy) is a non-trivial design choice rather than off-the-shelf RL applied to a known objective. The intermediate-HBM "warm" tier as a demotion target is a 3.5D-specific contribution absent from GPU/rack-scale work.
- **Rigor:** Mixed. The constrained MDP formulation with hard feasibility masks, counterfactual reward evaluated on identical routed-token demand, and the explicit EDP/energy accounting (RAPL-style power capping, per-tier pJ/B from real ISSCC/Micron/AMD sources) are commendable. However, all baselines (Hydra, MoEntwine, PIMoE) are *single-author reproductions inside LEGOSim*, not the original implementations — the paper itself flags this as the comparison setup. Reproduction fidelity is unverifiable, and a 46.7% latency win over a reproduced PIMoE partly reflects how faithfully PIMoE's NPU-PIM throttle-aware offloading was ported onto a 3.5D substrate it was not designed for.
- **Reproducibility:** Weak. No code, no model checkpoints, no router-output traces, no LEGOSim configs released. Hardware parameters are documented (Table 1) but the masked Double-DQN hyperparameters (network architecture, learning rate, γ, replay buffer size, training-window count, α EWMA, the μ1/μ2 churn penalties, and the ω_q/ω_p/ω_b/ω_s pressure-proxy weights) are only partially specified. Re-implementing from the paper alone would be difficult.
- **Significance:** Conceptually meaningful for the (still-niche) 3.5D MoE chiplet community and adjacent to wafer-scale (MoEntwine) and NPU-PIM (PIMoE) lines. Whether 3.5D chiplet MoE inference becomes a production-relevant substrate is an open industry question; if it does, the pressure-aware residency framing will likely matter.
- **Red flags:** (1) Sole author at a vocational college claiming >40% latency wins over three systems from DAC/HPCA/ISCA-tier groups, all reproduced single-handedly — independent reproduction is essential before citing these deltas. (2) The fast-mapping pressure-proxy weights are "fixed during evaluation" and not tuned per model, yet HCRMap's claimed win over its own no-fast-mapping variant is 51.2%/50.7% — a suspiciously large swing that hinges entirely on how poorly the no-fast-mapping ablation routes tokens. (3) LEGOSim is also cited as the paper's own group's prior simulator (Lin et al., MICRO '25, with overlapping author Qiu/Tang appearing in both), so the evaluation harness and the method share lineage — not an independent testbed. (4) No silicon or FPGA validation; all numbers are from a simulator whose energy/latency models are the arbiter of EDP claims.

## Strengths

- Correctly identifies that the hot-expert problem on 3.5D is *coupled cross-resource pressure*, not any single-axis objective, and builds the formulation around that insight.
- The masked-action DQN with hard feasibility constraints and counterfactual same-window reward is a principled way to do online residency control under hard physical constraints without churning.
- Clean two-timescale separation: the slow controller is allowed to be expensive (RL, frozen checkpoint), the fast loop is deterministic and routing-correct by construction.
- Unified evaluation across 8 diverse MoE models with real router-output traces (no synthetic skew injection) and full stage-level latency attribution (gate/disp/queue/mem/comp/gath/combine).
- Honest about the capacity cliff and the failure mode of unconditional replication (Figure 2c, Figure 3c).

## Limitations

- **Simulation-only.** No hardware, FPGA, or RTL validation. LEGOSim's bank/link/energy models are the sole arbiter; the 3.5D package itself is hypothetical (no fabricated chip).
- **Baselines are self-reproduced.** Hydra, MoEntwine, and PIMoE are re-implemented inside LEGOSim by the same author; absolute deltas depend on reproduction fidelity that cannot be checked.
- **Single-author provenance and venue.** Has not passed peer review; sole author at a vocational college.
- **No artifact release.** Code, configs, router traces, and RL checkpoints are not public.
- **RL hyperparameters and pressure-proxy weights under-specified**, hampering independent reproduction.
- **Workload coverage is narrow in shape:** only prefill (2048 tokens) and decode (512 tokens); no variable-length, multi-batch, or long-context serving scenarios; no sensitivity to batch composition drift even though "hotness drifts with batch composition and request domain" is a stated motivation.
- **Cost of the slow controller's training** (training-window count, simulator steps, wall-clock time) is not reported.
- **The fast token mapper uses fixed proxy weights** — no per-model or per-serving-mode adaptation, and no learned alternative is ablated against.

## Relevance

对关注MoE大模型推理系统、chiplet/异构硬件-软件协同设计、以及RL在系统调度中应用的读者高度相关：HCRMap把"热专家副本应该放在哪一层内存、token该送到哪个副本"建模成耦合的压力感知在线优化问题，并用可行性约束的Double-DQN加确定性快循环求解，是MoE serving + 3.5D硬件方向上一个具体且可借鉴的方法论样本，对自研推理框架/调度器的副本管理与多级内存调度有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How sensitive are the >40% latency wins to the fidelity of the reproduced Hydra/MoEntwine/PIMoE baselines? Can the original authors re-run HCRMap inside their own simulators?
- Does HCRMap's advantage survive on real router traces from production serving (variable batch, multi-tenant, long-context) rather than single-shape prefill/decode windows?
- What is the wall-clock cost and sample complexity of training the masked Double-DQN, and how does policy transfer across MoE models (train on DeepSeek-V3, deploy on Mixtral-8x22B)?
- Could the fixed fast-mapping proxy weights be replaced by a lightweight learned policy without breaking routing correctness, and would that close the gap to an oracle lower bound?
- How does HCRMap behave under SRAM budgets tighter or looser than 64 MB, and where exactly does the "capacity cliff" bite relative to the per-model hot-expert footprint?
- Is there a non-RL controller (e.g., Lyapunov-drift-based online optimization with the same feasibility masks) that achieves comparable latency with stronger stability guarantees and no offline training?
- Can the versioned-directory + throttled-migration mechanism be validated on an FPGA-emulated 3.5D fabric to corroborate the simulator's energy/latency claims?
