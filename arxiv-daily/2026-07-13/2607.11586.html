Methodology — two timescales, three tiers

Mozart-class 3.5D · 16 chiplets · 4×4 mesh

S Slow residency loop

Feasibility-masked Double-DQN picks promote / retain / demote / evict per decision window across SRAM → HBM → DRAM. A legal mask strips any action violating replica-count, residency-budget, bank-capacity, or migration-path feasibility before selection — the controller only ever edits legal states.

reward = (T_cf − T_act)/T_cf − μ1·T̂_mig − μ2·C_churn

F Fast token-to-replica map

Deterministic, routing-correct by construction. Ranks legal replicas by a fixed-weight normalized pressure proxy; tokens processed in small blocks so queue / link / bank pressure refreshes mid-window — no flooding one replica from stale window-start state.

C_tok = ωq·C^queue + ωp·C^path + ωb·C^bank + ωs·C^stream
SRAM · 64MB HBM · 8GB DRAM · 32GB
hot → warm → fallback · UCIe D2D 512 GB/s

The 3.5D-specific move: an intermediate HBM tier lets warm experts demote instead of falling all the way to DRAM — avoiding the near-tier capacity cliff and repeated streaming. Versioned directory + throttled non-blocking chunked migration publish a new version only after all chunks land (read-only weights ⇒ no coherence).

Strengths & Limitations

side-by-side

+ Strengths

  • Correct reframing. Identifies that 3.5D hot-expert load is coupled cross-resource pressure, not any single-axis objective, and builds the formulation around it.
  • Principled control. Masked-action DQN with hard feasibility constraints + counterfactual same-window reward does online residency under physical limits without churning.
  • Clean separation. Slow controller may be expensive (RL, frozen checkpoint); fast loop is deterministic and routing-correct by construction.
  • Unified evaluation. 8 diverse MoE models, real router-output traces (no synthetic skew), full stage-level latency attribution.
  • Honest about failure modes. Explicitly characterizes the capacity cliff and unconditional-replication backfire.

! Limitations

  • Simulation-only. No hardware, FPGA, or RTL validation. The 3.5D package is hypothetical; LEGOSim is the sole arbiter of EDP.
  • Self-reproduced baselines. Hydra / MoEntwine / PIMoE re-implemented by the same author — absolute deltas depend on unverifiable fidelity.
  • Single-author provenance. Vocational college, not peer-reviewed. Independent reproduction essential before citing deltas.
  • No artifact release. Code, configs, router traces, RL checkpoints all private.
  • Hyperparameters under-specified. DQN architecture, γ, replay size, μ1/μ2, ω-weights only partially given.
  • Narrow workload shape. Only fixed prefill 2048 / decode 512; no variable-length, multi-batch, or long-context serving.

Questions for further exploration

Q1How sensitive are the >40% wins to baseline fidelity? Can Hydra / MoEntwine / PIMoE authors re-run HCRMap in their own simulators?
Q2Does the advantage survive real production router traces — variable batch, multi-tenant, long-context — vs single-shape windows?
Q3Wall-clock + sample complexity of training the masked Double-DQN? Does policy transfer across MoE models (train DeepSeek-V3 → deploy Mixtral-8x22B)?
Q4Could fixed fast-mapping proxy weights be replaced by a lightweight learned policy without breaking routing correctness?
Q5Where exactly does the capacity cliff bite vs per-model hot-expert footprint, at SRAM budgets tighter/looser than 64 MB?
Q6Is there a non-RL controller (Lyapunov-drift online opt with the same masks) matching latency with stronger stability guarantees?
