01

Methodology

supervised algorithmic tasks · 30/70 split

The idea — GeomDR

For each hidden layer, sum the trailing eigenvalues of the empirical covariance beyond a target dimension d*. Loss: L = L_task + λ(t)·Σj>d* μj. Activated by a half-cosine ramp after a free warm-up.

What it provably does

One objective, three readings (Appendix A): controls effective rank, bounds low-rank covariance reconstruction error, and compresses representation volume. Geometry becomes the direct object of manipulation.

Tasks & models

Modular addition mod 97 (9,409 pairs), modular division mod 97 (9,312), permutation composition over S5 (14,400). Residual MLP (3 blocks, GELU, LayerNorm) & pre-norm Transformer encoder. AdamW, lr 1e-3, wd 0.1, full-batch.

Diagnostics & protocol

Effective dimensionality via participation ratio D_eff = (Σμ)²/Σμ²; local-neighborhood distance ρ (k=10). Three-stage sweep: schedule → d* → intervention start t_s. Grokking = Acctest≥0.999·Acctrain for 100 consecutive steps.

Scalability — covariance subsampling

Estimating the covariance spectrum from 5–25% of representations preserves the full speedup: full-spectrum 7.06K vs 25% sampling 6.34±1.05K vs 5% 7.89±1.87K steps — all 5/5. Wall-clock drops from 666s → ~250–280s, hinting at mini-batch compatibility.

Width ablation — where it flips

At MLP width 32 the baseline fails 0/5 while GeomDR groks in 21.6K (5/5). But on the Transformer, width-64 (2/5) and width-128 (1/5) GeomDR underperform the 5/5 baseline — d* was not re-tuned as width grew.

Strengths & limitations

distilled

Strengths

  • Clean, mechanistic idea. Suppress covariance mass outside a target subspace, with three connected interpretations — each backed by a short proof.
  • Exceptionally systematic ablations. schedule × dimension × timing, across 3 tasks / 2 architectures, with explicit seed counts and failure reporting — rare for a solo preprint.
  • Collapse-precedes-grok replicated across every task and seed; the lag compression (264.3K→3.0K) quantifies it concretely.
  • Honest about Transformer width-dependence and the GrokFast-combination failure; does not oversell.
  • Public code and full hyperparameter disclosure make central claims independently checkable.

Limitations

  • Tiny symbolic niche only. mod-97 arithmetic & S5 — no language, vision, or realistic-scale models.
  • Mechanism is a hypothesis. No causal/mediation analysis isolating dimensionality reduction from generic activation-norm penalty.
  • Modest & brittle on Transformers. 1.48–2.01×, and can actively harm when d* isn't re-tuned to width.
  • Weak reference inflates the headline. 362.1±111.0K (4/5) baseline; a better-tuned optimizer would compress the 52.5×.
  • Step-count, not wall-clock. Eigendecomposition overhead O(Nd²+d³) is real; no like-for-like GPU-hours comparison vs weight decay.
  • Under-controlled comparison. GrokFast+GeomDR (0/5) wasn't re-tuned — "unfavorable interaction" is confounded.

Open questions

for further exploration
Q1
Does GeomDR accelerate grokking on non-algorithmic data (image classification, language modeling), or is it specific to low-dimensional symbolic structure?
Q2
Can a mediation analysis (matching D_eff across GeomDR and an L2-activation regularizer) separate dimensionality collapse per se from generic activation penalties?
Q3
What is the true wall-clock comparison vs tuned weight decay, accounting for per-step eigendecomposition cost in GPU-hours?
Q4
Would re-tuning d* with model width recover consistent Transformer gains, or does the benefit genuinely saturate for attention architectures?
Q5
Can the regularizer be reformulated as a mini-batch-only statistic (no full-dataset covariance) without losing the acceleration?
Q6
Is the GrokFast+GeomDR 0/5 failure genuine over-constraint, or an artifact of not re-tuning λmax and d* for the combination?
Q7
Does dimensionality collapse still precede grokking when grokking is induced by other means (strong WD, GrokFast), or is the ordering GeomDR-specific?
