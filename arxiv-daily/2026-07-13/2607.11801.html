IAAN  ·  Encoder-locus neuron amplification  ·  Training-free

Training-free amplification of ≤0.12% of audio-encoder neurons — picked by contrasting real audio against noise — lifts acoustic perception in large audio-language models.[1]

Encoder-Side Neuron Identification and Amplification for Acoustic Perception in Large Audio-Language Models
Yu-Han Huang· Chih-Kai Yang· Ke-Han Lu· An-Yu Cheng· Hung-yi Lee · National Taiwan University
IAAN large-audio-language-models audio-encoder inference-time-intervention neuron-level-interpretability paralinguistic-understanding
Key Metric
+25.7ACCURACY POINTS · AUDIO-FLAMINGO-3 ON VOXPARADOX
Average accuracy 13.1% → 38.8% on ten non-semantic speech attributes, by editing ≤0.12% of encoder FFN neurons (K=200 of N=163,840).
Why This Matters

Moves neuron-level steering inside the audio encoder — a lightweight, training-free lever for paralinguistic perception and a new entry point for LALM interpretability.

Critical Edge

Kimi-Audio emotion accuracy collapses 83.5% → 18.0% under IAAN — a large per-task regression left unexplained, smoothed over by the rise in averages.

01 / METHOD

Two encoder passes. One contrast. A forward hook.

01 — Define neurons

Each coordinate of a transformer FFN's intermediate activation is one "neuron": aᵢ = φ(W₁h)ᵢ. An L-layer encoder yields N = L·dff neurons — 32 × 5120 = 163,840 for a Whisper-large-v3 encoder.

02 — Score by contrast

Run the encoder twice per clip: once on the real waveform x, once on a Gaussian-noise reference r (same length/mean/variance, fixed seed). Neurons that fire more to real audio score high.

Eq. 1 — Acoustic score sᵢ = meanₜ aᵢreal(t) − meanₜ aᵢref(t) Computed per-clip from the clip's own activations only — no external dataset, no labels.

03 — Select & amplify

Rank neurons by sᵢ, take the top-K ("neuron budget"). During the real-audio pass, a forward hook scales just those activations: aᵢ ← g·aᵢ for i ∈ S. The amplified output then feeds the LLM as usual.

04 — Tune strictly held-out

(K, g) are chosen on an external dev set — 190 sarcastic-voice clips from LISTEN rebuilt into MCQs — optimizing listening advantage LA = Acc − ALA. VoxParadox is never touched for selection.

Why the locus is new Prior LALM interventions act after the encoder: contrastive decoding on logits, hidden-state steering on the LLM, or emotion-labeled neuron control in speech generators. IAAN is the first to go inside the audio encoder, at neuron granularity, and to be label-free.
Eval protocol — VoxParadox 10 non-semantic attributes × 200 MCQs each. The voice carries one attribute while the spoken words imply a different (adversarial) one. Real improvement must raise Acc ↑ and lower adversarial-label agreement ALA ↓ together.

03 / ASSESSMENT

Strengths, next to limitations.

+ Strengths

  • Clean, minimal core idea — fully training-free and label-free, no calibration set required.
  • Excellent hygiene: strictly held-out VoxParadox (tuned on LISTEN), reproduced baselines, a matched LLM-amplify control that attributes the gain to the encoder locus, joint Acc/ALA reporting.
  • Striking efficiency — ≤0.12% of encoder neurons edited for up to +25.7 points.
  • Complementarity is shown, not assumed — IAAN improves the already-tuned AF3-PD.
  • Qualitative free-text examples show the effect transfers beyond MCQ selection to how the model describes sound.

– Limitations

  • Absolute accuracy stays modest after IAAN (AF3 38.8%, Qwen2.5 29.0%) — the capability is improved, not solved.
  • Per-task regressions, most notably Kimi emotion 83.5%→18.0%, unexplained.
  • Model-specific hyperparameters (g: 8–16, K: 50–200); principled automatic (K, g) selection is left to future work.
  • Single benchmark — no results on SD-Eval, AudioBench, or Vox-Profile.
  • No mechanistic explanation for why later encoder layers concentrate acoustic-discriminative neurons.
  • Proprietary models appear only as context numbers, never with IAAN applied.
04 / OPEN

Questions worth chasing.

Q1

Layer concentration — why do later encoder layers hold more acoustic-discriminative neurons, and does it hold for non-Whisper encoders (BEATs, Kimi's)?

Q2

Cheaper ranking — can the per-clip noise contrast become a one-time, dataset-level ranking, dropping the second encoder pass?

Q3

Regression cause — what drives Kimi emotion 83.5%→18.0%? Does the amplified set overlap neurons already encoding that attribute?

Q4

Transfer — does IAAN hold on SD-Eval / AudioBench / Vox-Profile, and for free-form generation quantitatively?

Q5

Suppression lever — could strongly text-biased neurons (large negative sᵢ) be suppressed as a second lever for cross-modal consistency?

Q6

Sparsity generality — is the 0.12%-suffices result an artifact of VoxParadox's adversarial setup, or would it hold for natural paralinguistic tasks in the wild?
