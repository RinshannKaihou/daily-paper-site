> **一句话总结：** 在四块 NVLink 配对的 NVIDIA RTX A5000 上，作者用一个"匹配的 FP16 中间基线"把 HuggingFace→vLLM-Marlin 的端到端加速（受控 2.58×、生产 10.62×）拆成运行时与"内核+量化"两部分，发现运行时贡献约 2/3 对数增益；同时证明 TP=4 分片几乎不叠加（仅 1.49×），并且多实例路由的最优解会随模型规模反转——8B 上按负载切换、70B 上恒为两个配对实例更优。

> **TL;DR:** This attribution study on four NVLink-paired NVIDIA RTX A5000 GPUs uses a matched FP16 intermediate to decompose the HF-Transformers→vLLM-Marlin INT4 serving speedup into a runtime factor and a kernel+quantization factor. Under matched greedy decoding on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct at the c=64 wide-batch cell, the full stack reaches a 2.58× overall speedup (1.90× runtime / 1.36× kernel+quant), with the runtime arm carrying 67.9% of the log-share gain versus the HF-FP16 baseline; the operational single-run swap reports a conflated 10.62× (86% runtime share). Four-way tensor parallel sharding stacks to only 1.49× geometric mean (vs. 4× ideal), and the multi-instance-vs-shard verdict sign-reverses between the 8B and 70B Llama models.

## 核心贡献

- **归因分解方法（匹配的 FP16 中间基线）**：通过运行一个 vLLM-FP16 中间点（保留快速运行时、去掉量化内核），把"整套栈替换"的单一加速比按乘法恒等式（Eq. 5）精确拆成运行时因子与"内核+量化"因子。这是一种记账式工具，作者明确声明不做因果独立性断言。
- **运行时占主导的定量结论**：在受控匹配贪婪解码下，c=64 的宽批次单元端到端 2.58×，运行时因子贡献对数份额 67.9%；生产环境单次运行则报告 10.62×、86% 份额——差异来自批次上限与采样模式两个混淆，作者同时给出两种解读。
- **跨模型复现内核+量化因子**：在 Llama-3.1-8B、Mistral-7B-v0.3、Qwen2.5-7B 三个 GQA 7–8B 模型上，内核+量化因子漂移 ≤1.46%，证明该因子在该规模硬件上的稳定性。
- **TP=4 分片"不叠加"的诊断**：分片 4 卡只得到 1.49× 几何平均（远低于 2× 实用阈值与 4× 理想值）；剖析追踪将约 80% 的单 token 缺口归因于分片协调开销，且 NVLink/PCIe 对照显示链路带宽并非瓶颈。
- **多实例路由的负载/模型条件性及符号反转**：8B 上长输出负载由 TP=4 胜出、宽批次由多实例胜出，且 c=128 时单分片 OOM 只剩多实例可行；70B 上两个配对实例在每个负载上都击败单分片（平均约 +7%），剖析显示单分片发起的显式 allreduce 调用数是配对实例的约 200 倍。
- **容量悬崖结果**：FP16 在 c=96 处 3/3 次运行 OOM；两个 INT4 栈把最大可持续并发提升约 4×（到 c=256 无请求失败）；在自定义"部署效用分数"上 HF-GPTQ 达 3.41×、vLLM-Marlin 达 44.30× 于 FP16 最佳。

## Key Contributions

- **Attribution decomposition via a matched FP16 intermediate**: A third measurement point (vLLM-FP16, same runtime, no quantized kernel) yields a multiplicative identity (Eq. 5) splitting the overall speedup into a runtime factor and a kernel+quantization factor. The authors explicitly frame this as a bookkeeping device with no causal-independence claim.
- **Quantified runtime dominance**: Under matched greedy decoding at the c=64 wide-batch cell, the controlled overall factor is 2.58× (1.90× runtime, 1.36× kernel+quant), with the runtime arm carrying 67.9% of the log-share; the operational single-run swap yields 10.62× (86% runtime share), with the gap attributed to batch-ceiling and sampling-mode confounds.
- **Cross-model stability of the kernel+quant factor**: On three architecturally distinct GQA 7–8B models (Llama-3.1-8B, Mistral-7B-v0.3, Qwen2.5-7B) the kernel+quant factor drifts by ≤1.46%.
- **TP=4 stacking does not stack**: Sharding one instance across all four A5000s gives only 1.49× geometric mean (ideal 4×); a profiler trace attributes ~80% of the per-token gap to shard coordination, and an NVLink-vs-PCIe control shows similar realized bandwidth (0.95× ratio), pointing away from link bandwidth.
- **Workload-/model-conditional multi-instance routing with sign reversal**: For 8B, TP=4 wins long-output cells and multi-instance wins wide-batch cells (and is the only viable backend at c=128 where the shard OOMs); for 70B, two paired instances beat one shard on every workload (~+7% on average), explained by the single shard issuing ~200× more explicit allreduce calls.
- **Capacity cliff outcome**: FP16 hits a reproducible memory cliff at c=96 (0/576 requests succeed, 3/3 runs); both INT4 stacks extend sustainable concurrency roughly 4× to c=256 with zero request failures; on a bespoke deployment-utility score, HF-GPTQ reaches 3.41× and vLLM-Marlin 44.30× of FP16's best.

## Methodology

**Hardware & software.** A single host with four NVIDIA RTX A5000 (24 GiB each), bridged into two NVLink pairs (cross-pair traffic crosses PCIe gen 4), CUDA 12.4. Two environments are compared: a HuggingFace baseline (PyTorch 2.4, SwiftServe v0.5.6 batched-decode scheduler with padded-KV left fusion and continuous batching, single card) and a vLLM environment (vLLM 0.7.3, PagedAttention v2, asynchronous engine; single-GPU and 4-way TP modes).

**Models & prompts.** Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct in FP16 (~16 GiB) and upstream GPTQ-INT4 (~5.7 GiB, ~2.8× weight-memory reduction), plus a 70B Llama GPTQ-INT4 for the larger-model comparison. A fixed pool of 10 short synthetic prompts (~12 tokens mean) is cycled deterministically across all backends — explicitly acknowledged as non-representative of realistic workloads.

**The matched intermediate.** The core methodological device is a vLLM-FP16 point (vLLM runtime + FP16 weights, standard unquantized GEMM). Three ratios are defined: runtime factor = vLLM-FP16/HF-FP16, kernel+quant factor = vLLM-Marlin/vLLM-FP16, overall factor = vLLM-Marlin/HF-FP16. Their product is an identity (Eq. 5) because vLLM-FP16 cancels exactly. Each factor's contribution is reported via a log-share metric (Eq. 6): share_i = ln(factor_i)/ln(overall), which composes correctly with multiplicative decomposition (sums to 1).

**Measurement regimes.** The paper distinguishes (i) an operational single-run regime (HF via SwiftServe HTTP wrapper with batch ceiling 8 + ancestral vLLM sampling) from (ii) a controlled matched-greedy regime (HF in-process at batch size 64, all backends at temperature 0, n=3 independent runs). The controlled regime is extended to a 7-cell sweep (4 long-output + 3 wide-batch cells) at n=3, and the kernel+quant arm is replicated at the headline cell on Mistral-7B-v0.3 and Qwen2.5-7B. Post-hardening multi-run data (n=5) supports the TP-stacking sweep, the 8B multi-instance comparison, and the 70B matched comparison (n=3 per workload, single session).

**Other protocols.** TP-stacking factor (Eq. 7) = vLLM-Marlin-TP4 / vLLM-Marlin-1x is compared against 4× ideal and 2× practical thresholds. A capacity sweep measures HF-FP16, HF-GPTQ (default INT4 kernel), and vLLM-Marlin at c≥96. A bespoke "aggregate token throughput product" (throughput × concurrency) is introduced as a deployment-utility score, explicitly not a throughput improvement. A direct per-token-latency measurement and a profiler trace (Appendix C) and an NVLink-vs-PCIe interconnect control (Appendix E, Table 19) support the mechanism discussion.

## Results & Highlights

- **Controlled attribution at c=64, T=256 (Table 5, n=3 matched greedy)**: HF-FP16 = 997.52 tok/s, vLLM-FP16 = 1,897.96 tok/s, vLLM-Marlin = 2,573.96 tok/s. Runtime factor 1.90×, kernel+quant factor 1.36×, overall 2.58×, with the runtime arm carrying **67.9% of the log-share**. The operational single-run figures are far larger (runtime 7.56×, overall 10.62×, 86% log-share), a ~4× inflation attributed to lifting the batch ceiling and the sampling mismatch.
- **TP=4 stacking (Table 8, n=5)**: Four-way sharding adds only **1.37×–1.58×** over single-GPU Marlin across all 7 cells (geometric mean 1.49×), far below the ideal 4.00×; the ideal column is 4.00× in every row.
- **8B multi-instance vs TP=4 (Table 9, n=5)**: At the widest batch (c=64), 4 independent instances serve 5,442.78 tok/s versus TP=4's 3,735.41 tok/s (multi/TP4 = 1.46×). The multi-instance layout wins from c=32 onward; at c=128 TP=4 OOMs while multi-instance (c=32 per replica) sustains 7,243.57 tok/s as the sole viable backend.
- **70B paired-instance sign reversal (Table 10, n=3 median)**: Two TP=2 instances beat one TP=4 shard on every workload, with ratios from 1.05× to 1.17× (≈+7% average on NVLink, ≈+5% on the slower PCIe control). The advantage largely survives moving instances to the slower link, indicating it is not an interconnect effect.
- **Coordination-call count (Table 17)**: On 70B at the wide-batch cell, the single TP=4 shard issues **494,592** explicit allreduce calls (NCCL/Marlin = 82.0%) versus **2,576** combined for the two paired instances (NCCL/Marlin = 1.7%) — roughly a 200× call-count reduction.
- **Capacity cliff (Tables 12–13, n=3 at c≥96)**: HF-FP16 fails 576/576 requests at c=96 (3/3 runs OOM); HF-GPTQ sustains c=256 with 0/1,536 failures; vLLM-Marlin sustains c=256 at 2,667.71 tok/s (0/1,536 failures). On the bespoke utility score, vLLM-Marlin at c=256 delivers **44.30×** FP16's best (c=64) score and HF-GPTQ delivers 3.41×.
- **Cross-model kernel+quant stability (Table 7, n=3)**: K+Q factor = 1.356 (Llama-3.1-8B), 1.370 (Mistral-7B-v0.3, +1.03%), 1.376 (Qwen2.5-7B, +1.46%) — maximum pairwise spread 1.46%.
- **Controlled envelope (Table 6, n=3)**: As concurrency rises, runtime factor grows (1.23× at c=8/T=128 → 1.90× at c=64) while kernel+quant shrinks (2.41× → 1.36×); runtime log-share crosses 50% between c=32 (43%) and c=64 (68%).

## Authors & Credibility

The paper is authored by Weijia Han and Lisha Qu, affiliated with the University of Washington. It is a careful empirical systems study with unusually strong methodological hygiene: explicit claim→dataset mapping (Section 3.8), a documented operational-vs-controlled distinction, controlled reruns (n=3, n=5), a bespoke interconnect control (Appendix E, Table 19), and a per-cell communication-breakdown profiler trace (Appendix C, Tables 16–17). The authors ship per-run artifact paths and wrapper-script names in an artifact catalog (Table 18). The self-critical stance — listing 12 threats to validity and explicitly retracting an earlier over-stated 70B advantage from ~15% down to ~7% after a matched re-measurement — is a credibility positive. However, no independent third party has reproduced the numbers yet, and the work is bounded to a single hardware SKU and one quantization method (GPTQ-INT4).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate and honest. The matched-intermediate trick is methodologically clean but algebraically trivial (a ratio that cancels); the genuine contribution is applying it rigorously on A5000-class PCIe hardware, which prior peer-reviewed work (SGLang, Sarathi-Serve, DistServe, MLPerf) had not isolated. The paper correctly positions itself as complementary rather than novel in mechanism.
- **Rigor**: Strong on the controlled regime (n=3 matched greedy, CVs ≤3.4% for 20/21 cells, cross-model replication on Mistral and Qwen) but the headline operational 10.62× figure rests on a single run and conflates two confounds (batch ceiling 8 and ancestral sampling) that the controlled rerun shrinks by ~4×. The authors never run the factorial ablation that would separate the batch-ceiling from the sampling effect, leaving the largest single number in the paper partially unattributed.
- **Reproducibility**: Good artifact discipline (lockfiles, per-run JSON paths, wrapper scripts in Table 18) but bounded to a specific 4× A5000 host with pairwise NVLink bridges — a configuration few labs will match exactly. Clocks were not pinned, thermal state uncontrolled, and idle time between workloads not enforced.
- **Significance**: Practitioner-relevant for mid-range PCIe clusters: the "runtime carries ~2/3 of the gain" and "TP=4 barely stacks at decode-heavy workloads" findings are actionable. The "introduce an intermediate baseline" methodological call is the most broadly exportable lesson.
- **Red flags**: (1) A 10-prompt synthetic pool (~12 tokens mean) is explicitly non-representative; the runtime-dominance conclusion could shift on ShareGPT/LMSys-style distributions. (2) The headline 10.62× operational number is n=1 with two simultaneous confounds — readers who cite only the operational figure would mislead. (3) Only two runtimes were actually compared; the authors disclose (Appendix F) that two further runtimes were blocked by infrastructure incompatibilities. (4) No four-card shared-switch-fabric configuration was tested, so the negative TP verdict is conditional on pairwise bridges only. (5) The "deployment utility score" multiplies an already-aggregate throughput by concurrency — the authors caveat it is not a throughput improvement, but it is the score behind the 44.30× headline and could be misread.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally transparent about what the decomposition can and cannot attribute (Section 3.9): explicitly states the identity is definitional, not evidence of factor independence.
- Dual reporting of operational and controlled figures answers two distinct deployment questions without overclaiming either.
- Direct mechanism evidence (profiler traces, allreduce-call counts, NVLink-vs-PCIe control) backs the qualitative conclusions rather than hand-waving.
- Cross-model replication bounds the kernel+quant factor's model-axis variance to ≤1.46%.
- Self-corrects an earlier overstated 70B advantage (from ~15% to ~7%) via a matched single-session re-measurement.

## Limitations

- Single hardware configuration (4× A5000, pairwise NVLink bridges); no four-card switch fabric, no newer GPU generations.
- Single quantization method (GPTQ-INT4 via Marlin); AWQ/MXFP/INT8 untested.
- A 10-prompt synthetic pool, ~12 tokens mean, is far from realistic serving distributions; prompt-conditional effects on throughput are not bounded.
- Sampling-mode asymmetry (greedy vs ancestral) between stacks is argued away by a microsecond-vs-millisecond cost argument but never directly ablated.
- No factorial ablation separating batch-ceiling from sampling effects on the operational-vs-controlled gap.
- Only two runtimes measured; two more were blocked by infrastructure incompatibilities (Appendix F).
- Clocks unpinned, thermal state uncontrolled, idle time not enforced between workloads.
- No external reproduction; generalization to latency-sensitive single-request serving is explicitly out of scope.

## Relevance

对关注 LLM 推理服务系统优化（量化内核、张量并行、连续批处理、运行时选型）的研究者与工程师高度相关：它用"匹配 FP16 中间基线"的方法论为拆解"整套栈加速比"提供了可复用的范式，并给出 PCIe 中端硬件上 TP 分片几乎不叠加、8B/70B 路由最优解反转等可直接指导部署决策的实证结论。若你的兴趣集中在 Serving/Inference 与 Quantization，本文值得精读方法学与第 4、6 节。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the runtime-vs-kernel attribution split change on realistic prompt distributions (ShareGPT, LMSys Chat 1M) with longer and more variable prefill, where attention/cache traffic differs from the 12-token synthetic pool?
- Would the 10.62× operational figure survive a factorial ablation that isolates the batch-ceiling effect (8→64) from the sampling-mode effect (greedy vs ancestral), and which confound dominates?
- Does the negative TP=4 stacking verdict reverse on a four-card shared-switch fabric (all cards on one low-latency link), or on newer GPU generations (e.g., H100 SXM, B200) where the fixed per-collective launch overhead is a smaller fraction of per-step time?
- How does the decomposition extend to other quantization methods (AWQ, INT8/FP8, MXFP) and other fused kernels, and does the kernel+quant factor's ≤1.46% cross-model stability hold beyond the 7–8B GQA class?
- For latency-sensitive single-request serving (not measured here), at what concurrency does the sharded layout's lower per-token latency start to lose to multi-instance aggregate throughput?
- Can the matched-intermediate methodology be applied to adjacent serving runtimes (SGLang, TensorRT-LLM, TGI) to build a cross-runtime attribution table, now that the authors have disclosed two blocked attempts (Appendix F)?
