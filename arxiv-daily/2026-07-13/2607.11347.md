> **一句话总结：** 该论文证明固定表征上的 OLS 读出层可被精确分解为训练样本回报的加权和（权重由经验 Gram 几何决定），由此构建一种免再训练的事后审计框架，能在合成 CBDT、PJM 电力竞价、Adult Income 与 Default Credit 任务上以 0.941 的平均 Top-30 一致性超越 Influence Functions、Representer Point、TracIn 等归因基线，并定位弱支撑决策。

> **TL;DR:** The authors prove that an OLS action readout fitted on a fixed (post-training) neural representation admits an *exact* case-based decomposition — each action score is a weighted sum of training-case returns with Gram-geometry coefficients — and turn this into a retraining-free audit. On a synthetic CBDT generator, PJM energy bidding, Adult Income, and Default Credit, the method achieves the highest mean Top-30 action consistency (0.941 vs. 0.909 for Influence Functions and ~0.67 for TracIn/Representer Point) while staying competitive on support reconstruction, and yields a case-influence risk diagnostic that beats a margin baseline at flagging covariate-shift errors.

## 核心贡献

- **给出神经网络动作评分的精确 CBDT 式分解。** 证明在固定表征 + OLS 读出（满列秩假设下）中，每个动作分数恰好等于训练样本回报的加权和，系数由经验 Gram 几何唯一确定，让用户能直接追溯"哪些训练样本抬升/压低/抵消了某分数"。
- **厘清基于样本的神经证据的语义边界。** 给出系数具备 CBDT 相似语义的"充分条件"（白化 + 非负性）；在该条件之外，系数应解释为有符号的 Gram 几何影响项，避免把"影响"误当成"相似度"。
- **构建免再训练的决策审计框架。** 提出三类审计信号——逐样本影响排序、衡量动作一致性的影响熵、以及识别弱支撑决策的 case-influence 风险诊断——仅需拟合一个 OLS 顶层探针，无需重新训练表征或访问原始优化轨迹，探针保真度通过分数重建来度量。
- **实证覆盖受控合成数据与真实协变量任务。** 在合成 CBDT 生成器、PJM 半小时负荷的真实竞价、以及 UCI Adult Income / Default Credit 代理决策四个场景上验证，方法在 Top-30 一致性上全面领先主流训练数据归因基线。

## Key Contributions

- **An exact CBDT-style account of neural action scores.** For fixed-representation OLS readouts (under full-column-rank assumptions), each action score is proven to be exactly an aggregation of training-case action returns, giving a direct route from a neural decision to the cases that raise, lower, or offset its score.
- **Semantics for case-based neural evidence.** The paper characterizes a sufficient regime (whitening so that G = nI, plus non-negativity) under which the induced case weights carry CBDT similarity semantics, and clearly states that outside this regime they must be read as signed Gram-geometric influence rather than similarity — preventing a common misinterpretation.
- **A retraining-free decision audit framework.** Three audit signals are introduced: case audit scores (signed per-case contribution ψᵢ = αᵢ·rᵢ,ₐ), an influence-entropy measure H_α of action coherence among top-k cases, and a case-influence risk diagnostic O_α combining entropy with a Gini disagreement term. The audit needs only an OLS top-layer probe; no retraining or access to the original optimization trajectory is required.
- **Empirical validation across controlled and real-covariate settings.** Evaluated on a synthetic CBDT generator (known ground-truth support), PJM energy bidding (real half-hourly loads, synthetic prices), and UCI Adult Income / Default Credit proxy decisions, the method leads on Top-30 action consistency while remaining competitive on support reconstruction, and its risk diagnostic improves over a margin baseline under covariate shift.

## Methodology

The paper targets the standard post-training setting: a network's representation φ(x) is frozen after training, and an ordinary-least-squares (OLS) linear readout Q(x,a) = vₐᵀφ(x) is fitted per action a. Let Φ be the training representation matrix and G = ΦᵀΦ the empirical Gram matrix.

**Exact decomposition (Proposition 1).** Solving the OLS normal equations vₐ = G⁻¹Φᵀrₐ (with rₐ the action-conditional return vector) and substituting back yields Q(x,a) = Σᵢ αᵢ(x)·r_{i,a}, where the case coefficient is αᵢ(x) = φ(x)ᵀG⁻¹φ(xᵢ). Because Φ has full column rank, α(x) = ΦG⁻¹φ(x) is the unique minimum-norm solution and the canonical OLS-induced case-influence coefficient. When G is singular/ill-conditioned, a Tikhonov-regularized variant α_λ(x) = Φ(G+λI)⁻¹φ(x) recovers the same form in the limit.

**Coordinate invariance.** The unregularized coefficient is invariant to invertible linear reparameterizations of the representation (α_B(x) = α(x)), confirming it is a property of the represented linear function rather than the basis. Isotropic Tikhonov regularization breaks this invariance under non-orthogonal reparameterizations.

**Sufficient CBDT-similarity regime.** The decomposition's aggregation form matches CBDT's U(a|M,p) = Σ n(c,M)·s(p,p_c)·u(r_c,a), but algebraic form does not equal semantic identity: CBDT uses non-negative similarity, whereas OLS coefficients are signed and Gram-induced. A transparent sufficient condition is representation whitening (G = nI), under which αᵢ(x) = φ(x)ᵀφ(xᵢ)/n; a true CBDT-similarity reading additionally requires non-negativity on the relevant domain. Outside this regime the coefficient is a coordinate-invariant, possibly signed Gram-metric influence term.

**Audit signals.** From ψᵢ(a,x) = αᵢ(x)·r_{i,a} the framework derives: (i) case audit scores sorted by signed/absolute contribution; (ii) influence entropy H_α over the action distribution of the top-k influential cases (low entropy = coherent support); and (iii) a case-influence risk score O_α(x) = H_α(x,a_x) + γ·D_Gini(x) (γ=1), flagging queries whose strongest training cases provide dispersed or conflicting action evidence. Probe fidelity is measured by OLS-vs-NN score reconstruction correlation ρ_recon.

## Results & Highlights

- **Top-30 action consistency (case-ranking quality, synthetic CBDT, mean ± 95% CI over 5 seeds, Table 1):** the proposed coefficient α scores **0.941 ± 0.030**, beating Influence Functions (0.909 ± 0.043), the inner-product baseline (0.697 ± 0.092), TracIn (0.671 ± 0.129), and Representer Point (0.667 ± 0.128).
- **Per-case Pearson correlation with CBDT ground truth:** α reaches **0.368 ± 0.172**, above Influence Functions' mean (0.295 ± 0.123) with an overlapping CI, and well above Representer Point (0.090 ± 0.080) and TracIn (0.089 ± 0.156), which are near zero.
- **Synthetic CBDT recovery:** the fixed-representation OLS readout achieves **96.0% test agreement** with the CBDT optimal action, and reconstructed action-support scores correlate strongly with the true CBDT supports (per-action r = 0.994 / 0.996 / 0.977 for conservative/moderate/aggressive).
- **PJM energy bidding (real loads):** the neural policy and the explicit CBDT decision maker agree on **3221 of 3358 test decisions**, with the 137 disagreements concentrated in the low-margin load-transition region; OLS-vs-NN probe-fidelity correlations reach **0.9998 / 0.9972 / 0.9592** across the three actions, and CBDT-support alignment ρ_support is 0.980 (conservative), 0.882 (moderate), but only **0.112 (aggressive)** — explicitly identifying the high-risk action needing closer audit.
- **UCI proxy tasks:** Adult Income reaches **network–CBDT agreement of 0.9408** with OLS reconstruction correlations of **0.997 / 0.996 / 0.999** across actions; Default Credit reaches agreement of **0.875** with reconstruction correlations of **0.998 / 0.956 / 0.998**, where the weakest action (standard approval, 0.956) is flagged as the least faithfully reconstructed.
- **Signed-coefficient geometry (PJM, 50 test inputs):** top-5 positive-α cases sit at mean standardized representation distance **0.44 ± 0.04** from the query vs. **3.30 ± 0.16** for top-5 negative-α cases — a **7.5-fold separation** — and the positive group's mean absolute coefficient magnitude is about **3.7×** the negative group's.
- **Latency:** cached online audit averages **58.6 ms/query** vs. sub-millisecond baselines, trading latency for stronger decision-relevant case retrieval without retraining.

## Authors & Credibility

The four authors (Manli Yan, Yuebin Lin, Yaowen Yu as corresponding author, and Yong Zhao) are all from Huazhong University of Science and Technology, Wuhan, China. The work is released as a preprint (arXiv:2607.11347v1, cs.AI, 13 Jul 2026). The theoretical claims are backed by explicit proofs (Proposition 1 plus Lagrange-multiplier and coordinate-invariance derivations in Appendices B.1–B.3) and an empirical check of invariance under ZCA reparameterization (Appendix C.1). Results are reported with 95% confidence intervals over five random seeds, which is appropriate for the claim-strength. No peer review status or code release is indicated in the PDF.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core decomposition Q(x,a) = Σ αᵢ(x)·r_{i,a} with αᵢ = φ(x)ᵀG⁻¹φ(xᵢ) is essentially the representer theorem applied to a frozen-representation ridge/OLS readout, and is algebraically identical to what Representer Point Selection already exploits for kernel/linearized-DNN settings. The genuinely new contributions are (a) the explicit CBDT-semantics analysis with a *stated* sufficient regime, and (b) the audit-signal design (entropy + Gini risk score). The "exactness" claim is real but only within the fixed-representation OLS reduction, which the authors honestly scope.
- **Rigor:** Propositions are correctly stated and proved; coordinate invariance is verified empirically (Appendix C.1). However, the headline Top-30 win is measured on a *synthetic CBDT* generator whose ground-truth case contributions are by construction an inner-product aggregation — i.e. the evaluation is partially favorable to the method's own structural assumption. Pearson-r intervals for α vs. Influence Functions overlap, so the per-case-magnitude advantage is not statistically decisive; the decisive metric (Top-30 consistency) is also the one most aligned with the method's design.
- **Reproducibility:** Datasets are public (UCI Adult, UCI Default Credit/Yeh & Lien, PJM load data). But the synthetic CBDT generator parameters, the proxy return specifications (Appendices C.5/C.6), the architecture/training of the "trained network" being probed, and the covariate-shift grid (Appendix C.4) are only partially specified in the main text; no code or hyperparameter table is referenced. The 58.6 ms/query figure lacks hardware specs.
- **Significance:** The framing — a post-training, retraining-free audit that yields *case-level* (not feature-level) evidence with clear semantic boundaries — is practically useful for high-stakes auditing. But the restriction to OLS/fixed-representation readouts is binding: the authors explicitly state the result "does not claim that arbitrary end-to-end nonlinear training admits the same decomposition," which excludes most deployed models with nonlinear heads unless re-linearized.
- **Red flags:** (i) Real-covariate experiments (PJM, Adult, Default Credit) all use *synthetic or proxy* action returns rather than real outcome labels — the authors concede "deployment policy design requires an independent return specification," so the audits are methodological demonstrations, not validated policy audits. (ii) The Adult Income task reports network accuracy of only 0.7982 with CBDT-accuracy 0.7908, i.e. the audited network is itself weak, which weakens the audit's decision-theoretic authority. (iii) The CBDT-similarity sufficient regime (whitening + non-negativity) is shown to hold approximately only on synthetic data; on PJM the negative coefficients must be re-read as "far-field Gram corrections," which complicates the headline CBDT narrative.

## Strengths

- Provides a genuinely exact, closed-form decomposition rather than an approximation, with a precise statement of when it is valid and when it must be reinterpreted.
- Clear and honest semantic boundary: the sufficient-regime / signed-influence distinction prevents the common conflation of "influential case" with "similar case."
- Practical, lightweight audit that needs only a top-layer OLS probe — no retraining, no optimization-trajectory access, no Hessian/inverse-Hessian products as in Influence Functions.
- Diagnostic design (entropy for coherence, Gini-augmented risk for weak support) is intuitive and complementary to existing margin/confidence shift detectors.
- Strong, controlled-synthetic recovery (96% CBDT agreement, per-action r ≈ 0.99) cleanly demonstrates the central premise that case aggregation can be exposed from a standard readout.

## Limitations

- **Restricted readout class.** Exactness holds only for OLS on a *frozen* representation; arbitrary end-to-end nonlinear heads and estimated counterfactual returns are explicitly left as future work.
- **Proxy/synthetic returns on all real-covariate tasks.** PJM uses synthetic prices; Adult and Default Credit use hand-specified proxy returns — none constitute a real outcome-grounded policy audit.
- **Scalability.** The method requires inverting (or Tikhonov-regularizing) the H×H Gram matrix and storing the full training-case memory; large memories may need Nyström-style approximations, which are mentioned but not evaluated.
- **Latency cost.** ~58.6 ms/query is orders of magnitude slower than baseline attribution methods, which may matter for real-time decision auditing.
- **Evaluation partly self-favoring.** The decisive Top-30 benchmark is computed against a synthetic CBDT generator whose ground truth matches the method's structural assumption, and the Pearson-r advantage is not statistically separated from Influence Functions.
- **No code release or peer review indicated** in the PDF, limiting independent verification.

## Relevance

该论文把训练数据归因（training-data attribution）与决策可审计性结合，对关注"模型决策可解释性、表征几何与事后审计"的研究者高度相关：其免再训练的 case-level 审计框架和 case-influence 风险诊断，可作为评估高风险场景下神经网络决策证据可靠性的实用工具，并与线性探针/表征几何方向的工作直接衔接。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the exact decomposition be extended to nonlinear heads via a local linearization (e.g., last-layer + activation Jacobian), and how rapidly does probe fidelity degrade away from the OLS regime?
- How does the method behave when action returns are *estimated* from a noisy outcome model rather than observed — what is the sensitivity of αᵢ(x) and the risk diagnostic to return-estimation error?
- Does the case-influence risk diagnostic generalize beyond covariate-axis scale shifts to realistic distribution shifts (e.g., temporal drift in PJM, demographic shift in Adult), and how does it compare to modern OOD-detection baselines rather than a single margin score?
- For large training memories, what is the accuracy–cost trade-off of Nyström or random-feature approximations to G⁻¹, and can the audit remain exact-in-spirit at reduced rank?
- Since Top-30 consistency and Pearson r are evaluated on a synthetic CBDT generator that structurally matches the method, how would the ranking hold up on benchmarks with human-labeled influential cases (e.g., DataInf, DataModels-style ground truth)?
