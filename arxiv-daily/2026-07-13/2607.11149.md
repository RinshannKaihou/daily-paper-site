> **一句话总结：** 论文提出 AgentFootprint，首个系统性跨框架 LLM 智能体"运行后存储足迹"基准；其序列化感知的度量套件（总留存 S_total、通道构成、重复因子 D、增长指数 α、可压缩性 C、历史可重构性 R）揭示了被原生字节测量低估一个数量级的重复，发现八个主流框架在同等 100% 任务精度下留存字节相差 15.7×（LangGraph 5.10 MB vs CrewAI 0.32 MB），重复读取任务下增长指数高达 α=1.95（监控一个 2 KB 文件 200 轮即留存 323 MB），而对 SWE-bench Verified 108 个系统而言导出轨迹量与解决率无相关性（τ_b=0.080）；一个约 300 行的内容寻址紧凑器在保留全部可重构性的同时把留存减少 4.8–32.7×。

> **TL;DR:** AgentFootprint is the first systematic cross-framework benchmark of post-run storage footprint for LLM agents, measuring eight frameworks (LangGraph, AutoGen, CrewAI, SmolAgents, OpenAI Agents SDK, LlamaIndex, Agno, InfiAgent) over 1,061 sandboxed runs under identical backends, tools, and tasks. Its serialization-aware metric suite (S_total, composition, duplication D, growth exponent α, compressibility C, reconstructability R) exposes a measurement trap where naive byte-level chunking reports D=1.01 for a store that logically holds the same content twelve times (true D=12.1). At identical 100% task accuracy on a 600 KB file-QA corpus, persisted bytes span 15.7× (LangGraph 5.10 MB vs CrewAI 0.32 MB; bootstrap 95% CI [15.0, 16.3]); a fixed-trace control isolates a 6.7× framework-level spread; full-history frameworks grow superlinearly (α 1.74–1.95) so monitoring one 2 KB file for 200 rounds leaves 323 MB; across 108 instance-normalized SWE-bench Verified submissions per-instance trajectory volume spans 1,617× with no correlation to resolve rate (Kendall τ_b=0.080, p=0.222); a ~300-line content-addressed compactor reduces retention 4.8–32.7× while preserving every reconstructability score.

## 核心贡献

- **首次把"运行后存储足迹"形式化为可度量、可比较的智能体评价维度**：定义留存为运行后留在沙箱边界内、可归因于该次运行的全部字节（工作区产物 + 框架侧 residue，含隐藏 home 目录状态），并明确区分"决策/观测/推导/重复"四类字节，把"必须保留"与"可引用"与"开销"分开。
- **序列化感知的六指标度量套件**：S_total（总留存）、通道构成、重复因子 D、增长指数 α、可压缩性 C、历史可重构性 R（0–3 分）。关键方法论发现是原生字节分块会严重低估重复——LangGraph 检查点库原始分块 D=1.01，但按 SQLite cell / JSONL 行解析逻辑流后 D=12.1，zstd 还能压缩 142×，三者矛盾正是因为 SQLite 分页与 JSON 转义掩盖了重复。
- **AgentFootprint 基准与可复现 harness**：8 框架 × 1,061 次沙箱化运行（509 次主研究 + 552 次复现/变体），每运行用全新沙箱、工作区与 home 目录前后快照 diff，容器校验隔离（Docker 交叉校验差异 0–8.5%，低于 11% 的重复级标准差），全套 harness/适配器/CAS 实现/分析脚本以 MIT 许可随附。
- **三项核心实证发现**：(i) 等精度分散——同样 100% 精度下留存字节差 15.7×；(ii) 增长机制——持久化完整历史的框架 α 高达 1.95，窗口/追加式设计保持近线性（α 0.73–1.20）；(iii) residue 远超产物——当智能体产出合法输出文件时，框架 residue 可达产物的 24,033×。
- **真实世界研究**：收割 SWE-bench Verified 全部 134 个提交（108 个实例归一化），导出轨迹量跨 1,617×，且与解决率无显著相关（τ_b=0.080, p=0.222），说明"导出更多"并不等于"更成功"。
- **存在性证明式缓解方案**：约 300 行的框架无关内容寻址紧凑器（content-addressed compactor），保留每个框架的 store schema、按需 rehydrate，在 70 次运行中将留存减少 4.8–32.7×，且全部 310 个留存文件通过恢复检查、R 分数逐一复现。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic cross-framework benchmark of post-run storage footprint for LLM agents, formalizing retention as bytes present after a run and attributable to it within a per-run sandbox boundary, with a four-way semantic taxonomy (decisions / observations / derivations / duplicates).
- A serialization-aware six-metric suite (S_total, channel composition, duplication D, growth exponent α, compressibility C, reconstructability R) plus the methodological finding that raw-file chunking masks duplication by up to 12× (D=1.01 naive vs D=12.1 on logical streams for the same SQLite checkpoint store), which partly explains why this axis went unmeasured.
- AgentFootprint: a reproducible harness and task suite measuring eight frameworks over 1,061 sandboxed runs (509-run main study + 552 replication/variant runs) with container-validated isolation, identical backend (DeepSeek-V4-Flash, temperature 0), identical minimal tools, and three repetitions.
- Three core controlled findings: (i) equal-accuracy dispersion of 15.7× in retained bytes; (ii) superlinear growth (α up to 1.95) for full-history frameworks vs near-linear (α 0.73–1.20) for windowed/append-only designs; (iii) framework residue exceeding delivered artifacts by up to 24,033×.
- An in-the-wild study of all 134 SWE-bench Verified submissions (108 instance-normalized) showing a 1,617× spread in exported trajectory volume with no detectable correlation with resolve rate (Kendall τ_b=0.080, p=0.222).
- A content-addressed compactor (~300 lines, framework-agnostic, schema-preserving) as an existence proof that reduces retention 4.8–32.7× while reproducing every R score and passing all 310 restore checks.

## Methodology

AgentFootprint defines the storage footprint as all bytes present after a run that are attributable to it within a declared sandbox boundary: files created or grown in the task workspace plus every framework-side store (session/checkpoint databases, logs, snapshots, traces) including hidden home-directory state. A four-way semantic taxonomy classifies bytes as decisions (model outputs), observations (tool/env returns), derivations (framework intermediates), and duplicates (2nd-through-nth copies).

**Six metrics per run.** S_total is the apparent-length filesystem delta split into workspace artifacts vs framework residue. Channel composition groups S_total into state/checkpoints, debug/event logs, conversation/actions, and other by adapter-audited path rules. Duplication factor D = S_total / S_unique where S_unique sums unique content-defined chunks (FastCDC with SHA-256) over logical streams (every SQLite cell and JSONL line chunked as its own stream); D=2 means the average content byte is stored twice. Growth exponent α is fit from log S_total ~ α log T at T ∈ {25,50,100,200} on a repeated-observation stress task. Compressibility C = S_total / zstd-19 size with a 128 MB long-range window (a bound, not an archive). Reconstructability R is 0–3 asking whether retained bytes reconstruct the conversation history the model saw at step k (R=0 nothing; R=1 bytes but no per-call structure; R=2 per-call structure but no complete observations; R=3 at least one channel holds a complete exact record), validated by field-by-field reconstruction behind a logging proxy.

**Serialization-aware meter.** The central methodological contribution is showing that raw-file chunking substantially understates duplication. SQLite fragments large values across 4 KB pages interleaved with headers/pointers so chunk boundaries never align across copies, and JSON escaping rewrites embedded bytes (\n, \uXXXX) so trajectory copies no longer match source bytes. The meter extracts logical streams before fingerprinting and injects content probes (fixed substrings and whole lines) searched under raw and JSON-escaped encodings; doubly-nested encodings are only partially detected.

**Harness.** Each (framework, task, repetition) runs in a fresh sandbox: workspace holds the corpus, HOME/XDG paths redirected into the sandbox, the adapter runs the framework's quickstart-style agent, and the meter diffs both inventories. Six task families (file-QA ×10, long-horizon T=25–200, write-task ×5, exploratory-retrieval ×5, edit family ×5, data-analysis ×5) each isolate one storage behavior, plus ablation runs with persistence disabled. Fairness comes from configuring each framework in its documented durable-session configuration rather than tuning for storage; all frameworks get identical task text, the same minimal tools, the same backend model, and three repetitions.

**Fixed-trace control.** A scripted mock endpoint replays one identical trajectory (three reads, 182.7 KB observations, one answer) through the seven persisting frameworks' unmodified adapters, isolating the persistence-layer amplification from agent behavior (1.03× to 6.86×, a 6.7× spread).

## Results & Highlights

- **Equal-accuracy dispersion (file-QA suite, 8 frameworks × 10 tasks × 3 reps):** Among the five persisting configurations at 100% task accuracy, documented defaults span **15.7× in retained bytes** (LangGraph 5.10 MB vs CrewAI 0.32 MB on a 600 KB corpus; bootstrap 95% CI [15.0, 16.3]). Across the full roster, SmolAgents retains exactly **0 bytes** at 100% accuracy while LangGraph retains 5.10 MB; AutoGen answers 86% and retains 2.49 MB; Agno answers 99.3% and retains 1.15 MB.
- **Duplication factor separates architectural families:** Full-history checkpoint/snapshot designs D = LangGraph 12.8, AutoGen 8.4, LlamaIndex 4.2; windowed/append-only designs stay below 2.6 (OpenAI Agents 1.6, CrewAI 1.5, Agno 2.5, InfiAgent 1.8). The echo metric: probes from the same files read equally often appear **8.1× under LangGraph but only 0.5× under OpenAI Agents**.
- **Growth regimes (repeated-observation stress task):** Full-history frameworks LlamaIndex/AutoGen/LangGraph grow superlinearly with α 1.74–1.95; monitoring one **2 KB status file for 200 rounds leaves 323±26 MB under LangGraph** (D≈49) and 102±21 MB under AutoGen. Windowed/bounded designs stay near-linear (α 0.73–1.20), retaining 1.4–36.4 MB at T=200 vs LangGraph's 323 MB. OpenAI Agents is sublinear (α=0.73). Accuracy stays high throughout (median 195/200 at T=200).
- **Serialization masking:** LangGraph's checkpoint store reports **D=1.01 under naive raw-file chunking but D=12.1 under logical-stream chunking**, while zstd compresses that same store 142× — the duplication was present but obscured by SQLite paging and JSON escaping (up to 12× masking).
- **Persistence ablation:** With persistence disabled, five frameworks (LangGraph, AutoGen, LlamaIndex, OpenAI Agents, Agno) keep 100% accuracy and retain exactly zero bytes — retention supports replay/resumption/recovery, not immediate task completion. InfiAgent's debug channel accounts for 40% of its footprint.
- **Fixed-trace control:** Identical-content amplification spans **1.03× (LlamaIndex) to 6.86× (InfiAgent)**, a 6.7× spread with zero contribution from agent behavior — purely the configuration-level persistence mechanism.
- **Residue dominates artifacts (write-task suite):** A 39-byte summary file leaves **128 KB–1 MB of residue, up to 24,033× the artifact**. Across five write tasks mean residue ranges LangGraph 920 KB, InfiAgent 1,022 KB, down to SmolAgents 0 B.
- **SWE-bench Verified in-the-wild (108 instance-normalized systems):** Mean exported trajectory volume per mapped instance ranges from **6.6 KB to 10.6 MB — a 1,617× spread**; resolve rate shows **no detectable correlation with bytes per instance (Kendall τ_b=0.080, permutation p=0.222)**; the 28 systems in the 65–75% resolve band alone span 134×. Family-respecting analyses (latest-per-family, family means: τ_b ≤0.07, p≥0.40) leave the conclusion unchanged.
- **Content-addressed compactor:** Across 70 runs of the seven persisting frameworks, retention reduced **4.8–32.7×** (AutoGen 32.7×, Agno 20.5×, CrewAI 6.1×, OpenAI Agents 4.8×); all 310 retained files pass restore checks and every R score is reproduced on CAS-restored stores (R=3 for six, R=1 for CrewAI). Largest reduction on AutoGen whose event log + per-turn snapshots repeat the same messages.
- **Backend robustness:** Full-roster replications preserve the storage ranking at Spearman ρ=0.96 (Qwen3.6-27B, sizes 0.93–1.06×) and ρ=0.89 even on the deliberately weak Qwen2.5-7B where accuracy collapses (35–39% for four frameworks from context overflow) — persistence design dominates backend choice.
- **Production illustration:** A supply-chain quoting system retained **131 MB for a single task (79 MB framework residue vs 2.6 MB delivered artifacts)**; per-task retention median 715 KB, 90th percentile 81 MB; deployment accumulated ~56 GB over ~103 rounds.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel framing. Storage footprint as an agent-evaluation axis is, to my reading, unaddressed by existing surveys (the paper cites Mohammadi et al. 2025's metric catalog spanning completion, step-efficiency, tool correctness, plan quality, latency, and cost — none storage) and by GAIA/SWE-bench/τ-bench. The serialization-masking finding (D=1.01 naive vs 12.1 logical) is a real, non-obvious methodological contribution that explains why the axis was missed.
- **Rigor:** High. The fixed-trace control is the crucial design move: by replaying one identical trajectory through seven frameworks' unmodified adapters it cleanly isolates persistence-layer amplification (6.7×) from agent behavior — most "X differs across frameworks" papers cannot make this separation. Backend replications at ρ=0.96/0.89 are convincing evidence that persistence design dominates model choice. The 20,000-resample bootstrap CI on the 15.7× headline is appropriately cautious.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong on paper — MIT-licensed harness, all eight adapters, CAS implementation, per-run records, Docker cross-check, instance-normalized wild cache, envlocks, continuous verification on every commit. One real caveat: all controlled runs execute on a single Apple M5 workstation (16 GB RAM) one process at a time, and the byte-count metrics are hardware-independent, but framework-version drift is a moving target (the paper is appropriately explicit that results describe versioned configurations, not frameworks in the abstract, and pins versions).
- **Significance:** Practically meaningful. The production quote (131 MB for one task, ~56 GB over 103 rounds; ~560 GB/day extrapolated at 10^4 rounds/day) makes a concrete deployment case beyond disk cost — retention obligations, compliance audits, deletion semantics. The 24,033× residue-to-artifact ratio and the 15.7× equal-accuracy spread are findings framework authors and deployers should act on.
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - The 15.7× headline compares documented defaults that bundle different recovery/audit capabilities; the paper is candid that "equal-R comparisons rank what defaults retain for the same reconstructability score, not capability-normalized efficiency," and that R covers history only (not crash resume, workflow state, debug provenance). So the dispersion is partly an apples-to-slightly-different-apples comparison — readers could over-read it as "LangGraph is wasteful" when some retained bytes support capabilities CrewAI's store does not.
  - The R=3 score for six frameworks at sizes spanning 15× is presented as "most of the spread is removable redundancy," but the CAS compactor is an existence proof, not a deployable system (the paper explicitly flags unsalted-hash content-equality leakage, cross-tenant deletion complexity, and existence probes as out-of-scope obligations).
  - The in-the-wild SWE-bench measurement is of exported trajectories (a per-system lower bound on footprint, not runtime residue); the paper is careful about this but the τ_b=0.080 "no correlation" result is about export policy heterogeneity as much as actual footprint. Export policies differing across teams is a confound the authors acknowledge but cannot remove.
  - α is explicitly a workload-conditioned descriptive fit over four horizons, "not a scaling law"; the authors note four horizons at three runs are insufficient to distinguish candidate functional forms, and the local-slope divergence (LangGraph 1.96 over 25→100 but 0.96 over 100→200, attributed to checkpoint consolidation) means α is unstable enough that quoting 1.74±0.01 precision slightly overstates regularity.
  - AutoGen's documented save cadence is flagged as the "most contestable choice" — an end-of-run variant remains superlinear (α=1.50) but smaller (2.1 vs 2.49 MB), so a reader skeptical of the AutoGen number has a documented sensitivity check but the headline still uses the larger configuration.
  - Controlled suites cover retrieval and retention, not general reasoning (an explicitly stated boundary); the heterogeneous edit/data families are small (5 tasks each, one run each, no repetition variance reported).

## Strengths

- Identifies and rigorously defines a genuinely unmeasured evaluation axis with a clean conceptual separation of the two footprint determinants (logical volume from behavior vs amplification from the persistence layer), and a fixed-trace control that isolates the second.
- The serialization-masking finding (D=1.01 vs 12.1) is a transferable methodological insight for anyone measuring deduplication on serialized stores, not just agent researchers.
- Exceptional experimental hygiene: per-run sandbox isolation, container validation, three repetitions, bootstrap CIs, backend replications on two additional models, automated per-commit verification, and transparent run accounting (every one of the 1,061 runs enumerated in Appendix C).
- Intellectually honest about scope: six explicit boundary caveats, candid that defaults bundle different capabilities, and that the CAS store is an existence proof with real deployment obligations (hash salting, cross-tenant deletion, existence probes).
- The in-the-wild SWE-bench study grounds the controlled findings at scale and uses family-respecting analyses to address the multi-version-per-team dependence.
- Practical impact is clear: the production example and the 24,033× residue-to-artifact ratio translate directly to deployment, compliance, and retention-policy decisions.

## Limitations

- Controlled task suites are retrieval/retention-focused and small; the heterogeneous families use single runs without repetition variance, so generalization to complex reasoning is untested.
- Documented defaults bundle different recovery/audit capabilities, so the 15.7× spread is not capability-normalized; equal-R comparison ranks what defaults retain, not what equal-capability configurations could retain.
- The CAS compactor is a ~300-line reference, not a production system; unsalted content hashes can leak content equality across tenants, shared blobs complicate deletion, and cross-trust-boundary deduplication enables existence probes — all flagged but unresolved.
- In-the-wild SWE-bench data measures exported trajectory volume (a lower bound subject to per-team export policy), not runtime residue; the no-correlation result conflates footprint with export policy heterogeneity.
- α is a workload-conditioned descriptive fit over only four horizons; insufficient to distinguish functional forms, and local slopes diverge within a single framework, so the ±0.01 precision on LangGraph's 1.74 overstates regularity.
- All controlled runs on a single workstation; framework versions are moving targets (results describe versioned configurations, not frameworks in the abstract).
- R covers conversation-history reconstructability only — not crash resume, workflow state, or debug provenance — so it under-explains the spread and cannot establish that the residual bytes are pure waste.

## Relevance

对于关注 LLM 智能体评测、智能体框架工程化部署与系统效能的研究者，本文直接补全了"任务成功率/可靠性/推理成本"之外的第四条评价轴——运行后存储足迹，并给出可复现的度量方法与跨框架数据，对智能体选型、生产部署的留存治理与合规审计具有直接参考价值；其序列化感知去重度量方法也可迁移到任何需要在序列化存储上测重复率的系统评测工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the 15.7× equal-accuracy spread change under capability-normalized configurations — i.e., if each framework is configured to provide the same crash-resume, workflow-state, and debug-provenance capabilities, does the dispersion collapse or persist?
- Can the growth exponent α be predicted from a framework's documented archival/context policy without running the stress task, enabling a static "storage-risk" classifier for new frameworks?
- Does the no-correlation finding (τ_b=0.080) between exported trajectory volume and SWE-bench resolve rate hold on other leaderboards (e.g., τ-bench, GAIA), or is it specific to SWE-bench's export conventions?
- What is the runtime/latency cost of the content-addressed compactor relative to the storage savings, and does rehydration-on-demand degrade agent step latency measurably?
- How do the storage-footprint rankings interact with multi-agent / shared-thread deployments (the paper notes fresh sandboxes understate shared threads: 95.2 vs 5.8 MB)?
- Could R be extended to a richer capability score covering crash resume and workflow state, so that the "removable redundancy" claim can be tested against capability-normalized rather than history-only reconstructability?
- Do newer framework versions (post the July-2026 snapshot) shift the rankings, and is the persistence-design-dominates-backend finding (ρ=0.96/0.89) stable over time?
