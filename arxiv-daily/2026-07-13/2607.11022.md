> **一句话总结：** 通过预注册的双臂因果对照实验（leaky 原版 MBPP 测试 vs hardening 后的 MBPP+ 额外测试，三模型家族、5×400 步 GRPO），本文发现部署测试套件的自然假阳性在 1–1.5B/400 步尺度下对 held-out 能力的损害被约束在 1.5pt 以内（gap 0.20pt），但约一半被泄漏的奖励确实为真实错误代码买单，且这一通道是"选择"预存错误模式而非"学习"出来的——廉价静态审计即可在训练前预测泄漏位置。

> **TL;DR:** A single-author, preregistered two-arm causal contrast trains GRPO on identical MBPP tasks (Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B, deepseek-coder-1.3b, Llama-3.2-1B; 5 seeds × 400 steps) rewarding one arm with the leaky original MBPP tests and the other with the hardened MBPP+ extra tests: the held-out gap is bounded at 0.20 pt (one-sided 95% upper bound 0.75 pt, inside a preregistered 1.5-pt margin under all six estimators), yet ~47.57% of the leaked reward pays for genuinely wrong code (vs a "none" baseline), a cheap static audit predicts where reward leaks before training (Spearman ρ=0.80), and the mechanism is selection of pre-existing error modes rather than learned exploitation — all confined to 1–1.5B models and 400 steps.

## 核心贡献

- **首个针对"自然假阳性"的预注册因果对照实验**：区别于以往注入式噪声/对称翻转的研究，本文直接在部署的真实套件（MBPP vs MBPP+）上做 leaky vs hardened 双臂 GRPO 训练，把 Rajan (2026) 留下的"自然下一步因果实验"补上。
- **测量层本身就是贡献**：构建了一套区分"泄漏在哪里发生、泄漏奖励买到了什么、训练是否在攀爬该通道"的工具——静态泄漏审计、训练中 rewarded-FP mass、穷举式 verified-wrong 人工裁决。
- **有界平均效应（确认性 [C]）**：held-out pass rate gap 为 0.20pt，单侧 95% 上界 0.75pt，在六种估计量（percentile/basic/BCa/hierarchical/seed-t/sign-flip，上界 0.70–1.07pt）下均低于预注册的 1.5pt 非劣效界。
- **静态审计预测泄漏（确认性 [C]）**：训练中 rewarded-FP mass 与训练前静态泄漏评分 Spearman ρ=0.80（raw）/0.79（难度控制后）；leak 层 FP 占比较 clean 任务高 +43.8pt（CI90 [43.05, 44.57]）。
- **泄漏奖励约半数为真实错误（探索性 [E]）**：对 family A 全部 2821 条 rewarded FP 做穷举人工裁决，verified-wrong 占 47.57%（cluster bootstrap-95 [36.4, 60.5]），三家族复现"大份额"（B 45.37%、C 62.78%）。
- **机制：选择而非学习（探索性 [E]）**：FP 率从 step 0 起即处于长期水平且不增长；最大持续通道 55/55 为不同错误程序、无 hacking 签名；未训练 base 模型在 leaky 过滤下已产出同样的错误输出——与 selection-of-pre-existing-modes 一致。
- **探索性元审计**：把同一工具转向 frontier 评委（DeepSeek-V4-Pro、MiMo-v2.5-Pro），发现其在自身假阳性上自评仅弱于在 1.5B 策略错误上的异评锚点（自评 AUC .617/.639 vs 锚点 .894/.786），去混杂同作者检验 UNRESOLVED。

## Key Contributions

- **First preregistered causal contrast on natural verifier false positives**: Unlike prior injected-noise/symmetric-flip studies, this trains two GRPO arms on a deployed suite (original MBPP vs MBPP+ extra tests), directly closing the "missing causal experiment" explicitly named by Rajan (2026). Every claim is tagged [C] confirmatory (preregistered), [E] exploratory, or [I] instrument-validity.
- **The measurement layer is itself the contribution**: A cheap static leakiness audit (k=16 base-model samples, code-executed, no training), training-time rewarded-FP mass, and an exhaustive human-adjudicated verified-wrong audit together separate *where* reward leaks, *what* it pays for, and *whether* training climbs it.
- **Bounded average effect [C]**: Held-out pass-rate gap (hardened − leaky) = 0.20 pt, CI90 [-0.42, +0.74], one-sided 95% upper bound 0.75 pt — inside the preregistered 1.5-pt non-inferiority margin under all six estimator variants (uppers 0.70–1.07 pt); the FP-mass trend does not rise (quarter means 14.5% → 15.2%, CI90 includes zero).
- **A cheap pre-training audit predicts where reward leaks [C]**: Per-task rewarded-FP mass tracks the static leakiness score at Spearman ρ=0.80 (raw, bootstrap-95 [0.74, 0.85]) and 0.79 after difficulty control; the registered train-side test puts the leak-stratum FP share +43.8 pt above clean tasks (CI90 [43.05, 44.57]).
- **About half the leaked reward pays for genuinely wrong code [E]**: Exhaustive audit of all 2821 family-A rewarded FPs under signed, human-adjudicated rules finds 47.57% verified-wrong (cluster bootstrap-95 [36.4, 60.5]); estimator variants stay within 2 points; the share self-penalizingly fell 68.84% → 59.91% → 47.57% across four signed specs.
- **Mechanism = selection of pre-existing error modes, not learning [E]**: FP incidence is at its long-run share from step 0 and flat/declining within horizon; the largest channel shows 55/55 distinct wrong programs with no hacking signatures; untrained base models already emit the same wrong outputs under the leaky filter; an 8.37-pt train-reward inflation shows no held-out counterpart.
- **Two-family replication + 800-step continuation [C]**: All three preregistered replication criteria (verified-wrong ≥10%, tracking, train-side gap) pass on family B; a preregistered 800-step continuation refutes growth at twice the horizon (powered refutation in B, underpowered non-detection in C).
- **Exploratory meta-audit of frontier judges [E]**: Two frontier judges self-assess their own-distribution false positives only weakly (AUC .617/.639) vs their hetero-judge anchors on a 1.5B policy's FPs (.894/.786); the deconfounded same-author contrast is UNRESOLVED.

## Methodology

**Design.** A preregistered two-arm causal contrast where the two arms differ in exactly one component — the test suite that pays the reward. Each MBPP task ships with ~3.1 original assert-style tests; the EvalPlus/MBPP+ project extends the same tasks with ~105.4 automatically generated tests (evalplus 0.3.1 pinned). The **leaky arm** rewards a rollout iff it passes the original MBPP tests; the **hardened arm** rewards it iff it passes the MBPP+ extra tests *only* (extra-tests-only, not the official EvalPlus base∪plus union metric). On 313/378 tasks the base and extra sets are fully disjoint. Prompts, tasks, seeds, decoding, optimizer, and step counts are identical across arms, so outcome differences are attributable to which suite pays the reward.

**Training.** GRPO (Shao et al., 2024), group size 8, temperature 1.0, 640-token cap, no KL anchor (β=0), 400 steps on 250 MBPP training tasks, 5 seeds per arm, checkpoints every 100 steps. Three model families: **Family A** (primary, deepest audit) = Qwen2.5-Coder-1.5B-Instruct; **Family B** (preregistered decision family) = deepseek-coder-1.3b-instruct; **Family C** = Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct.

**Static leakiness audit (pre-training).** For each task, draw 16 samples from the family's base model, score each against both suites; leakiness = fraction of MBPP-passing samples that fail MBPP+. Cheap: one sampling pass per family.

**Splits & evaluation.** Frozen problem-level split (RNG seed 20260702): 250 train / 128 held-out MBPP / 164 HumanEval+ (OOD). Held-out performance is *always* scored by the extra-test suite, whichever arm produced the model. At each checkpoint, k=8 samples per task at temperature 0.8, mean over 292 paired tasks; seed is the inference unit.

**Verified-wrong audit.** A written predicate spec classifies every one of the 2821 family-A rewarded-FP records as verified-wrong or one of four non-model causes (plus-suite false negatives, contract-invalid inputs, canonical-convention mismatches, resource-limit artifacts). Predicates are per-task, evidence-backed, human-signed; analysis scripts refuse to emit any verified-wrong aggregate without the signed marker file. The spec went through four signed generations; the headline share self-penalizingly fell along the way.

**Statistical discipline.** Three rules throughout: (1) the seed is the unit of inference (5 seeds → 4 d.f., exact sign-flip floor p=0.031, stated wherever a p-value appears); (2) no single-threshold verdicts — report margins and full intervals; (3) two-sided 90% CIs unless stated, bootstrap intervals state their resampling unit.

**Preregistration with failures published.** Version 1's launch gates both failed (a blind hand-audit gate was voided when 8/24 verdicts hinged on suite-side properties; its one-shot replacement failed with 6/20 overturned) — these are reported as design errors, not bad luck, and motivated a gate-power rule (≥80% pass probability required). Version 2 added the exhaustive audit and a pre-written claim ladder; Version 3 registered families B/C.

## Results & Highlights

- **Bounded average held-out effect [C]**: At checkpoint 400, the hardened-minus-leaky gap is **0.20 pt (CI90 [-0.42, +0.74])** on the 292 paired held-out tasks; the one-sided 95% upper bound is **0.75 pt — half the preregistered 1.5-pt margin** — and stays below 1.5 pt under all six estimators (percentile/basic/BCa/hierarchical/seed-t/sign-flip, uppers 0.70–1.07 pt). Both arms improve over base (+2.3–2.9 pt MBPP, +1.6–1.7 pt HumanEval+), so the bound is not two arms failing together. (This is a bounded-effect, not equivalence-at-zero, statement.)
- **Leakiness tracking [C]**: Per-task rewarded-FP mass during leaky-arm training tracks the pre-training static leakiness score at **Spearman ρ = 0.80 (bootstrap-95 [0.74, 0.85], 216 tasks; 0.79 after difficulty control)**; an OLS decomposition attributes the relationship to leakiness (coef .896) rather than difficulty (-.049). The pooled static FP rate of the leaky suite is 15.30% raw / 13.71% artifact-excluded.
- **Train-side stratification [C]**: On the 77 leak-stratum training tasks, 45.9% of rewarded rollouts are FPs vs 2.1% on the 173 clean tasks — **+43.8 pt (seed t-CI90 [43.05, 44.57])**; classifier-free, so invariant under later audit revisions.
- **Verified-wrong share [E]**: **47.57% (1342/2821)** record-weighted of family-A rewarded FPs are verified genuinely wrong code (task-cluster bootstrap-95 [36.4, 60.5]); every estimator variant within 2 points; 94.3% of audited tasks contain ≥1 verified-wrong record; both replication families reproduce a large share (B 45.37% [43.20, 47.52]; C 62.78% [61.78, 63.75]).
- **Reward inflation without capability trace**: The leaky arm banks **8.37 pt more train reward** than the hardened arm, while the held-out gap stays at 0.20 pt — measurement inflation, not capability gain.
- **Mechanism — selection, not learning [E]**: FP-only-group share sits at its long-run level from step 0 (11.9%/13.7%/13.9% of GRPO groups across A/B/C) and does not grow; rewarded-FP mass is flat in A/C and declines in B (trend CI90 [-0.033, -0.003]); on the largest persistent channel all **55/55 verified-wrong records are distinct programs** with no hacking signatures; the base-model control reproduces the channel's wrong output at/above trained rate on **9/11 channels**.
- **800-step continuation [C]**: Growth criterion (positive FP-only-share slope, 2nd half > 1st, ≥1 secondary measure) fires in **none of 12 runs**; family B is a powered refutation (MDE +0.43 pp/100 steps vs observed ≈0), family C an underpowered non-detection.
- **Meta-audit [E]**: Two frontier judges self-assess their own-distribution FPs at AUC **.617 (DeepSeek-V4-Pro) and .639 (MiMo-v2.5-Pro)**, descriptively below their hetero-judge anchors on a 1.5B policy's FPs (.894/.786); the deconfounded same-author contrast Δ_DS = −.025 [−.090, +.040] is **UNRESOLVED** (covers zero); a blind insurance probe established separability exists above the 1.5B policy's capability (GPT-5.6 AUC .959/.958, DeepSeek .902/.885, vs a code-length baseline .672/.634).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High and well-positioned. It is the first *causal training* test of deployed-suite natural FPs (Rajan 2026 explicitly named this as the missing experiment), and crucially distinguishes **persistent per-task asymmetric FPs** from the symmetric, resampled label-noise studied by Plesner et al. (2026) and Rad et al. (2026) — whose "RLVR tolerates 15% noise" result does *not* transfer here because persistent FPs don't average out.
- **Rigor**: Exceptional for the venue. Non-inferiority design with a pre-fixed margin, six-estimator CI surface, seed-as-unit inference, a 106-specification spec-search audit pricing one axis of analyst freedom (family-adjusted p 0.091–0.393), and a self-penalizing audit spec whose headline fell monotonically. The honest publication of *two failed launch gates* (the blind hand-audit voided at 8/24, the one-shot gate failing at 6/20) is rare and strengthening.
- **Reproducibility**: Strong on paper — frozen split, pinned evalplus 0.3.1, both reward paths, rollout logs, classification outputs, signed adjudication dossiers, independent recomputation scripts. Caveat: **family A's per-sample eval dumps were not retained**, so its held-out figures cannot be re-scored under the union metric, and family A has no retained final checkpoints (the sub-behavioral sharpening test runs only on B/C). The archived rollout schema is asymmetric across arms.
- **Significance**: The practical takeaway is sharp and modest: *a cheap static audit locates where FP mass will sit before training — exposure, not final damage.* Hardening removes measurement inflation but at this scale buys little capability. This is a useful, actionable boundary for dataset curators, not a sweeping claim.
- **Red flags / weak points**: (a) **Scope is narrow** — 1–1.5B models, 400 (and 800) GRPO steps, MBPP-length short tasks only; the authors repeatedly and correctly flag that growth-with-scale (Zhao et al. 2026) and "no fixed reward survives capability growth" (Wang et al. 2026) are untested. (b) The **eval-side stratification is permanently exploratory**: the +1.60 pt exposed-stratum gap is concentrated (top-5 carrier tasks hold 60.0%, interval crosses zero without them), its high-leakiness band is a measurement floor (17/20 tasks have base rate zero), it is an association not a causal estimate, and it **does not replicate cleanly** — family B reverses (−1.389 pt), family C is underpowered. (c) The verified-wrong share **replicates "large," not one number** (47.57/45.37/62.78%), and family C's 62.78% is the *least-audited* with 9.3% syntax-invalid extracted code — explicitly not readable as "C is harmed most." (d) "Hardened" ≠ perfect: the audit found plus-suite false negatives (e.g., Mbpp/806's canonical solution is itself buggy), so the hardened reward has its own smaller error floor. (e) The meta-audit (§5) is fenced off correctly but is two subjects on MBPP under one instrument — it licenses nothing about frontier models generally, and its deconfounded same-author test is UNRESOLVED.

## Strengths

- Genuinely causal two-arm design holding model/tasks/seeds/compute fixed; only the reward suite differs.
- Preregistration with failures published — including a voided gate and a gate-power rule born from a documented design error.
- Clean conceptual separation between *where* reward leaks (static audit), *what* it pays for (verified-wrong audit), and *whether* training climbs it (base-model control + flat-trend + 800-step continuation).
- Self-penalizing audit construction (every signed predicate could only move the verified-wrong share down).
- Honest scope fencing: every claim carries [C]/[E]/[I] tags, and the paper restates its scale/horizon limits at every turn.
- Mechanism finding (selection of pre-existing modes) is triangulated by four independent tests (flat trend, distinctness, base-model reproduction, no hacking signatures).

## Limitations

- **Scale and horizon**: 1–1.5B parameters, 400–800 steps, short MBPP tasks only; the open axis is model scale, and the task axis is untested (continuation reuses the same 250 tasks). No claims past this box.
- **Single benchmark family**: MBPP/MBPP+ with HumanEval+ as OOD; the HE+ transfer test was null, so all concentration statements are in-family only.
- **5 seeds only**: exact tests floor at p=0.031; true gaps between ~0.2 and 1.5 pt are undetectable; the non-inferiority result is a bounded-effect statement, not equivalence at zero.
- **Eval-side stratification permanently exploratory**: association only, concentrated in a few carrier tasks, one-axis spec-search correction (a lower bound), does not replicate cleanly across families; no causal sentence attaches to family-A data (per-sample dumps and checkpoints not retained).
- **Uneven audit depth**: family A took four signed generations; family C's 62.78% is least-audited with untreated syntax-invalid code; the three-family share table must not be read as a ranking.
- **Hardened suite is imperfect**: plus-suite false negatives mean the hardened reward has its own smaller error floor (quantified, not zero); suite operationalization (extra-tests-only vs official union) differs from colloquial "MBPP+" and shifts pass rates 0.2–0.9 pt/arm (though the pooled non-inferiority verdict is unchanged).
- **Meta-audit scope**: two frontier subjects + one witness reader, MBPP only, one frozen instrument; self-assessment gap measured across different item pools (a difference between settings, not a self-vs-hetero effect estimate); same-author contrast UNRESOLVED.

## Relevance

与 RLVR/reward-hacking 研究兴趣高度契合：它把"测试套件质量如何影响代码 RL 训练"从观测性审计推进到预注册因果实验，并提供了一套可在训练前预测奖励泄漏位置的廉价审计工具，对关注 verifier 鲁棒性、reward hacking 检测与代码 RL benchmark 可信度的研究者具有直接方法学价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the bounded-effect and selection-not-learning verdict survive at **larger model scale (7B+)** and **longer horizons (thousands of steps)**, where reward-hacking pressure is reported to grow with task length?
- Would the static leakiness audit transfer to **non-MBPP suites** (HumanEval base tests, APPS, CodeContests, SWE-bench), and does the Spearman ≈0.80 tracking hold there?
- Can a **causal** eval-side stratification design (retaining per-sample dumps + checkpoints across families) separate "the leak damages these tasks" from "these tasks attract FPs for other reasons" — the question blocked on the current grid?
- The meta-audit's deconfounded same-author contrast returned UNRESOLVED with a "penalty cancelled by an opposing reader-capability difference" left un-excluded; can a within-author/within-reader design with matched capability resolve it?
- Since hardened reward has its own (smaller) false-negative floor, what is the **joint error budget** when both suites have defects, and does hardening still "buy little capability" when the hardened suite is itself repaired?
- The 8.37-pt train-reward inflation with no held-out trace suggests parameter-level sharpening below behavioral resolution; can logit-lens / activation probes at sub-0.001-nat resolution detect it within the tested horizon?
