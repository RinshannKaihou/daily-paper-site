Theory · Linear Algebra of Backpropagation

Backpropagation as a Nilpotent Linear System

Ahmed Boughammoura · University of Monastir, Tunisia · 2026-07-13
backpropagation linear-algebra nilpotent-operator neural-network-theory adjoint-methods pure theory · no experiments
Key Finding
Layerwise backprop rewrites as one nilpotent linear system (I − B)X∗ = G whose Neumann series terminates in exactly L terms — recovering classical backprop as block back-substitution.
Key Metric
2(L−k) vs 2L
Backward sub-steps for frozen-encoder fine-tuning (first k of L layers frozen) versus end-to-end training — the framework's headline complexity delta.
Why This Matters

A self-consistent nilpotent-operator lens that formalizes ResNet "gradient highways" and transfer-learning gradient truncation — but it excludes ReLU and Transformer self-attention.

Critical Edge

It excludes ReLU by assumption and breaks on transformer self-attention — the headline single-path-collapse insight stops applying exactly where modern deep learning lives.

01

How the argument is built

theorem–proof · purely deductive

Forward pass setup

FC net A[N₀,…,N_L] with bias-absorbed weights, X^(ℓ)=σ(W^(ℓ)X^(ℓ−1)), denominator-layout derivatives and Hadamard chain rule.

Two-step backprop Thm 2.2

Seed X∗^(L)=∂J/∂X^(L); alternate Y∗=X∗⊙σ′(Y) then X∗^(ℓ−1)=(W^(ℓ))⊤Y∗. Structural duality motivates the F-prop / F-adjoint pair.

Global lift Prop 2.11

Stack adjoints into block vectors; Σ′=diag(D₁,…,D_L); W block strictly-lower-triangular; backward operator B=W⊤Σ′ is strictly block upper-bidiagonal.

Nilpotency Thm 2.13

Induction on the single-surviving-term structure of bidiagonal products gives B^L=0; (B^{L−1})_{1,L}=∏(W^(j+1))⊤D_{j+1} ≠ 0 generically.

Fixed-point equivalence Thm 2.15

Layerwise recursions ⇔ (I−B)X∗=G, G=(0,…,0,∇J)⊤; I−B invertible ⇒ unique. Block back-substitution reproduces BP in L steps.

Path expansion Lemma 2.21

Strict-feedforward ⇒ one nonzero Neumann term per layer (single-path collapse). Generalized to any DAG as a sum over all decreasing index chains L→ℓ — unifies ResNet multi-path.

F-symmetry §2.7

Condition X∗=X (backward mirrors forward). Under σ=Id & equal widths, per-layer orthogonality (W^(ℓ))⊤W^(ℓ)=I is the canonical F-symmetry.

Verification §3

Four hand-worked nets confirm three-way equivalence of Neumann series, direct inversion, and layerwise BP — including a rectangular-weight case and a residual-augmented scalar net.

03

Strengths & limitations, side by side

read across, not down

◆ Strengths

  • Clean, self-contained theorem–proof exposition with full definitions and a notation table; reads as a standalone monograph chapter.
  • Path-expansion lemma (2.21) is a genuinely unifying device — single-path collapse, residual multi-path, and arbitrary DAG backward operators fall out of one identity.
  • Honest limitations section names ReLU, Dropout/stochastic depth, and transformer attention as out-of-scope instead of overclaiming.
  • Transfer-learning corollary gives a crisp 2(L−k) complexity statement tied to the exact block where backward can halt.
  • Worked examples verify the three-way (Neumann / direct-inversion / layerwise) equivalence on concrete numbers, including the rectangular-weight case.

◆ Limitations & weak points

  • Differentiability assumption excludes ReLU and any non-smooth activation; subdifferential extension is left entirely open (Assumption 5.2).
  • Transformer self-attention breaks bidiagonal sparsity — B stays nilpotent under causal masking but the single-path-collapse story no longer applies.
  • Deterministic-forward assumption excludes Dropout, stochastic depth, and sampling layers; random B is flagged as future work.
  • No empirical validation, benchmarks, datasets, or baselines — practical consequences (better gradient-norm analysis, new optimisers) undemonstrated.
  • Awkward residual convention (Ex. 3.4) merges the skip into the pre-activation, slightly undercutting the "formalises standard ResNets" claim.
  • Self-citation heavy, no external validation against reverse-mode AD theory (Griewank & Walther, Baydin et al.) beyond positional citation.
04

Open questions worth chasing

where the theory could extend
  1. How does B extend to piecewise-linear activations (ReLU, Leaky-ReLU) via Clarke subdifferentials or directional derivatives — and does exact Neumann termination survive when B becomes set-valued?
  2. For causal-masked self-attention, can the dense Jacobian be decomposed (low-rank + bidiagonal) to recover an approximate single-path collapse, or bound the number of significant Neumann terms?
  3. Does (I − B)X∗ = G suggest new optimisers — preconditioning gradients by (I − B)⁻¹ or truncated Neumann approximations — connecting to K-Fac / Shampoo-style natural-gradient methods?
  4. Can F-symmetry / orthogonality become a regulariser or initialisation that biases nets toward the X∗ = X fixed point, and does it correlate with training stability or generalisation?
  5. The companion work [17] Scurria 2026 derives a global Lagrangian / least-action dynamics from the same nilpotency — does it yield empirically testable gradient-flow predictions this operator paper cannot?
