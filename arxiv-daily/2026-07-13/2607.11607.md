> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一种针对分布式 RL 智能体（QR-DQN/C51/IQN）的决策级审计方法，通过 excess Wasserstein gap 筛选 + 快照重启蒙特卡洛真值 + 置换零假设/bootstrap/FDR 控制的统计框架，发现 MinAtar 上 40–95% 的"最强风险权衡"声明在 95% 置信度下被证伪、几乎无任何声明可被确认，且该缺陷是结构性训练伪影而非弱智能体或标定问题，CVaR 训练、集成、重标定均无法修复。

> **TL;DR:** This single-author paper builds the first decision-level audit of trained distributional-RL agents, combining an excess-Wasserstein-gap screening metric, snapshot-restart Monte-Carlo ground truth, and a statistical harness (permutation nulls, bootstrap refutation, Benjamini-Hochberg FDR). Across QR-DQN, C51, and IQN on MinAtar (33 runs) and a pretrained SB3-zoo QR-DQN on full ALE Breakout, 40–95% of the strongest claimed risk trade-offs are refuted at 95% confidence while pooled confirmations are 0/245 per game, placement of top claims is indistinguishable from a truth-blind null head (81% vs 79%, p=0.22), and CVaR training, ensembling, and recalibration all fail to restore usable risk claims — the head is uninformative, not merely miscalibrated.

## 核心贡献

- 提出决策相关的审计指标 **excess Wasserstein gap ∆x(s)**：定义为 top-2 动作返回分布的 W1 距离减去均值差。Proposition R1 证明 ∆x > 0 当且仅当两个动作不存在一阶随机占优，即恰好是智能体"声称存在真实风险权衡"的状态——精确锁定风险敏感策略会偏离均值贪心的决策点。
- 构建统计审计框架：快照重启蒙特卡洛（每状态每动作 2000 次 rollout）提供真值，结合置换零假设、bootstrap 证伪检验与 Benjamini-Hochberg FDR 控制（FDR=0.10）以及逐状态 aliasing 指数，并公开代码/检查点/原始返回数据。
- 发现并定量刻画结构性"过度声明"伪影：QR-DQN 在 MinAtar 上 top-2% 排名的最强声明 66–84% 被证伪、0/245 可确认、学得-真值相关性 ≈0（0.02/−0.01/−0.04）；该现象在 C51、IQN 和 10M 帧 SB3-zoo QR-DQN 上复现。
- 通过真值盲 null head 对照（top-strata 证伪 81% [75,86] vs 观测 79%，p=0.22）证明声明分布与真相无关；并构建 RISKYGRID 正控制环境（确认率 97% [93,99]、相关性 0.92），证明审计本身有效、能检测到 g=0.05 量级的微弱风险。
- 系统检验三类"修复"均失败：CVaR0.25-greedy 训练（top-strata 证伪 88–98%）、5 成员集成（仅恢复 Breakout 8 个确认）、等渗重标定（只能把 top 声明压缩到原幅值 12–22% 并断言 0% 真权衡才"通过"）——证明缺陷是缺失信息而非可修复的标度误差。
- 文档化两个让重启式评估静默出错的陷阱：P1 RNG 克隆（deepcopy 复制环境随机数生成器导致"随机"rollout 完全确定）、P2 经验 Wasserstein 噪声底（cσn^-1/2，n=200 时 95 分位 ≈0.22 压过 0.05 阈值），二者各曾让作者自己得出错误审计结论。

## Key Contributions

- A decision-relevant audit metric, the **excess Wasserstein gap** ∆x(s) = W1(Z(s,a1),Z(s,a2)) − |µ1−µ2|, with Proposition R1 showing ∆x > 0 exactly when neither top-2 action first-order stochastically dominates the other — i.e. precisely the states where a monotone risk functional (CVaR) would disagree with mean-greedy, so the audit isolates the claims a practitioner would actually act on.
- A statistical audit harness built on snapshot-restart Monte-Carlo ground truth (2,000 rollouts per state per action, independently reseeded), a permutation null (split-half excess within one action = pure estimation noise), a bootstrap refutation test, and Benjamini-Hochberg FDR control at 0.10, plus a per-state aliasing index to bound partial observability; toolkit and raw returns released.
- The central finding: on QR-DQN/MinAtar (5 seeds, 245 states/game) 66–84% of the top-2% strongest claimed risk trade-offs are refuted at 95% confidence, pooled FDR-corrected confirmations are 0/245 in every game, and learned-vs-true excess correlation is ≈0 (0.02 / −0.01 / −0.04). The signature replicates on C51 and IQN (40–95% top-strata refuted, 0/147 confirmations across 882 states) and on a pretrained 10M-frame SB3-zoo QR-DQN on full ALE Breakout (all 14 top-strata claims refuted, mean learned 0.184 vs ground-truth 0.000).
- Mechanistic evidence that the artifact is structural, not a symptom of weak agents: fully formed by 500k steps and unchanged through 3M while score nearly doubles; score and top-strata refutation are uncorrelated (Spearman −0.07 across 33 runs); each seed produces idiosyncratic claims (sibling seeds reproduce only a median 3.8% of a top-stratum claim's magnitude).
- A truth-blind null-head control (permuting each run's learned claims across states, preserving magnitudes and rank strata) yields 81% [75,86] top-strata refutation vs 79% observed (p=0.22), proving claim placement is no better than chance; the RISKYGRID positive control confirms 97% [93,99] of known trade-offs with learned-true correlation 0.92, and a faint variant (g∈{0,0.05,…,0.4}) confirms 96–100% at every detectable level (r=0.89), ruling out audit-power or effect-size explanations for the MinAtar zero.
- A decision-level evaluation showing acting on the head's CVaR advice at its most-flagged states ranges from genuinely helpful (QR-DQN Breakout: safer action chosen 81% vs 44% for mean-greedy, regret 0.20 vs 1.36) to significantly worse than chance (Seaquest: 34% vs 63%, one-sided binomial p=8×10⁻⁴ at α=0.25), with C51 losing to mean-greedy on all three games.
- Documentation of two silent restart-evaluation pitfalls (RNG cloning via deepcopy; the empirical-Wasserstein noise floor cσn^−1/2) that each produced convincing-but-wrong audits of the authors' own work, plus the conclusion that recalibration only passes by nullifying claims (collapsing top magnitudes to 12–22% and asserting 0% of real trade-offs), so the deficit is missing information rather than a fixable scale error.

## Methodology

The audit has four stages. (1) **Screening**: an ε-greedy (ε=0.05) rollout collects 2,000 unique states per game; each is scored by the learned ∆x(s) and stratified across seven rank strata (top 0.5%, 0.5–2%, 2–5%, 5–10%, 10–25%, 25–50%, 50–100%); seven states per stratum are audited (49 per run). "Top strata" = top 2%, the states a practitioner would flag first. (2) **Ground truth via snapshot-restart MC**: deep copies of the environment are stored at up to 16 visits per state; for each audited state and top-2 action, 2,000 rollouts are run by restarting from a snapshot, forcing the action, and following the greedy policy to termination or horizon 10³, each independently reseeded to avoid the RNG-cloning pitfall. The empirical MC excess ∆ˆx(s) uses the same quantile formula. (3) **Statistical harness**: a claim is *confirmed* if ∆ˆx exceeds a permutation null (excess between split halves of one action's rollouts = pure noise by construction) and *refuted* if the learned ∆x exceeds the 97.5th bootstrap percentile of the MC excess (the estimator's finite-sample bias inflates that percentile, making refutation conservative); both use Benjamini-Hochberg at FDR 0.10, pooled per game. A per-state aliasing index (dispersion of per-snapshot mean returns over total spread) flags partial observability; low-alias (<0.25) results are reported separately. (4) **Decision-level evaluation**: at decidable top-stratum states (true CVaR gap significant at 95% by bootstrap), three choosers of the safer action at level α are compared — the head's CVaR ranking, a mean-greedy baseline, and ground truth.

Training setup: QR-DQN (51 quantiles, 3M steps, 5 seeds), C51 and IQN (3 seeds each) on MinAtar Breakout/Seaquest/Asterix with default sticky actions p=0.1; intermediate Breakout checkpoints at 0.5/1/2M steps; and pretrained SB3-zoo QR-DQN (10M ALE frames, 200 quantiles) on full-scale ALE Breakout/Seaquest (n=1,000 rollouts, sticky evaluation p=0.25). The RISKYGRID positive control is a 10-step corridor with per-column gamble scales g(x)∈{0,0.5,…,2.0}, SAFE = reward 0.5 deterministic vs GAMBLE = 0.5±g(x) fair coin (identical means, known-magnitude risk, zero aliasing by construction). Total: 2,450 audited states, 9.6M rollouts, under $9 of commodity CPU.

## Results & Highlights

- **Main QR-DQN audit (Table 1, MinAtar, 5 seeds, 245 states/game, Wilson 95% intervals):** refuted top-2% claims = **Breakout 84% [74,91], Seaquest 84% [74,91], Asterix 66% [54,76]**; refuted rest-of-ranking = 20% / 15% / 5%; **confirmed (FDR 0.1) pooled = 0/245 in all three games**; per-seed mean learned-vs-true excess correlation = 0.02 / −0.01 / −0.04 (no seed significantly positive).
- **Low-alias subset** (where the observation approximately pins down the outcome): refutation is equal or higher — Breakout 80% [66,90], Seaquest 100%†, Asterix 91% [62,98] — so aliasing does not explain the result.
- **Truth-blind null head:** permuting learned claims across states gives top-strata refutation **81% [75,86] over 1,000 permutations vs 79% observed (165/210), p=0.22** — the heads place their strongest claims no better than chance.
- **C51 and IQN replication (Table 3):** C51 top-2% refuted 81% / 95% / 57%, IQN 64% / 79% / 40%, **0/147 pooled confirmations in all 18 runs**, learned-true correlation never significantly positive; 32 of 33 runs refute ≥6/14 top-strata claims against a 5–20% background.
- **Full-ALE SB3-zoo QR-DQN (raw score ≈400, matching published performance):** Breakout — **all 14 top-strata claims refuted**, 0 confirmations among 49 states, mean learned excess 0.184 vs mean ground-truth 0.000, correlation −0.22; Seaquest head sits at the null floor (0.021 vs 0.03) but nothing is confirmable.
- **Positive control RISKYGRID (known answer key):** **143/147 = 97% [93,99] pooled FDR-corrected confirmations**, 0 false refutations in top strata (0/42), learned-true correlation **0.92** (per-seed 0.96/0.92/0.89), CVaR choice picks the truly safer action 99–100% with realized regret ≈0. The faint variant (g∈{0,0.05,0.1,0.2,0.4}) confirms **96–100% of g>0 states at every level including g=0.05**, pooled r=0.89.
- **Decision-level evaluation (Table 2, α=0.1):** QR-DQN Breakout — head picks safer action **81% vs 44% for mean-greedy, regret 0.20 vs 1.36** (genuinely helpful); QR-DQN Seaquest — **34% vs 63%, below chance** (one-sided binomial p=8×10⁻⁴ at α=0.25; p=0.045 at α=0.1), roughly tripling regret; C51 loses to mean-greedy in all three games (28–68% vs 48–71%, 2–4.5× the regret).
- **Structural probes:** overclaiming fully formed by 500k steps and unchanged to 3M while score climbs 8.6→14.4 (top-strata refutation 81/88/86/83% at 0.5/1/2/3M, confirmations zero at every stage); score vs top-strata refutation **Spearman −0.07** across 33 runs; cross-seed sibling reproduction median **3.8%** of a top-stratum claim's magnitude.
- **Repairs fail:** CVaR0.25-greedy training — top-strata refutation 88–98%, zero pooled confirmations, correlation 0.06/0.04/−0.02; 5-member shared-buffer ensemble — top-strata refutation falls from 95/100/95% to 81/98/57%, recovers only 8 Breakout confirmations, Seaquest/Asterix stay at zero; isotonic recalibration — on MinAtar the only recalibration that lowers refutation collapses top claims to **12–22% of magnitude and asserts 0% of real trade-offs**, passing by saying nothing (vs RISKYGRID where it recovers 98–99% assertions, correlation ≈0.9).

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author paper by **Hari Prasad** (independent affiliation as listed; contact h4ri.prasad@gmail.com). Credibility is reinforced by unusual methodological self-scrutiny: the author discloses two silent pitfalls (RNG cloning, Wasserstein noise floor) that each misled his own earlier audits before the final design, builds a truth-blind null-head control and a known-answer RISKYGRID positive control to defend the zero-confirmation result against the "the audit is broken" objection, and releases all code, checkpoints, and raw per-run returns (analysis/*.json, stratified.npz) so every number is traceable to a command. The work situates itself honestly against the theory literature it extends — citing Rowland et al. (2023a) on QTD fixed-point non-uniqueness and Clements et al. (2019) on aleatoric/epistemic conflation as the architectural diagnoses whose behavioral, per-state realization this paper provides. The framing parallels Atrey, Clary & Jensen (2020) on saliency-map counterfactual failure, positioning the contribution as an evaluation-methodology result in the rigor-in-deep-RL tradition (Agarwal et al. 2021; Machado et al. 2018).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and well-scoped. Prior work either proved distributional inaccuracy in theory (QTD fixed-point non-uniqueness, Rowland et al. 2018/2023a) or presumed the raw heads are unreliable and built estimators around them (ensembles, evidential, conformal). This is the first *measured, per-state, decision-level* audit of trained deep agents, and the excess-Wasserstein-gap metric (Proposition R1 tying it to first-order stochastic dominance violation) is a clean, principled choice that exactly isolates the claims a risk-sensitive policy would act on.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Pre-registration-flavored controls throughout: stratified sampling across seven rank strata, permutation nulls + bootstrap refutation + BH FDR control, a per-state aliasing index reported separately, a truth-blind null head (p=0.22 against observed), and a known-answer positive control (RISKYGRID, 97% confirmation, r=0.92) plus a faint variant straddling the detection floor — directly defusing the most likely "the audit has no power" objection. The disclosure of two self-caught pitfalls (deepcopy RNG cloning; cσn^−1/2 noise floor inflating to ~0.22 at n=200) is exactly the kind of honesty the methodological literature asks for.
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent on paper. Full code, checkpoints, and raw returns released; hyperparameters and training recipe in the supplement; the entire 2,450-state / 9.6M-rollout sweep costs under $9 of commodity CPU, so replication is cheap. Per-seed numbers and per-run top-stratum counts are released.
- **Significance:** Practically important for anyone consuming distributional heads for interpretability (Greydanus et al. 2018), risk-sensitive control (Keramati et al. 2020; Lim & Malik 2022), or safety monitoring (Hoel et al. 2021): the paper provides evidence that the most confident risk claims are the least trustworthy, and a concrete audit-passing standard for future calibrated methods.
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - *Scope of agents and policies.* Coverage is QR-DQN/C51/IQN under greedy and CVaR-greedy evaluation plus one pretrained QR-DQN per ALE game. The author explicitly flags that other calibrated variants (Stutts et al. 2024; Gan et al. 2025) are untested and that **an ALE agent trained under sticky actions remains the missing test at scale** — the zoo agents were trained without sticky actions, so their claimed spread "cannot reflect environment stochasticity by construction," making the full-ALE result an artifact-size measurement rather than an independent replication.
  - *Single author, no peer review indicated.* The arXiv version is v1; the audit rests on one person's pipeline, mitigated only by code release.
  - *Statistical caveat on the declining refutation curve.* The author is careful to note the monotone decline (Fig. 3) is partly mechanical: small claims are unfalsifiable because refutation requires the claim to exceed the MC bootstrap percentile, so the curve's shape is expected even for a truth-blind head.
  - *Generalization beyond three MinAtar games + two ALE games* is not demonstrated; the conclusion "learned risk reflects a training artifact rather than environment stochasticity" is a claim *for the agents tested*, which the author states precisely but readers may over-generalize.
  - *The doomed-Breakout-state specimen* (20% of top-stratum Breakout states, every future identical, head reports excess up to 0.44) is a vivid but possibly game-specific failure mode; how dominant such degenerate states are elsewhere is not broken out.

## Strengths

- Decision-relevant audit design: the excess-Wasserstein-gap metric provably isolates exactly the claims where CVaR and mean-greedy would disagree, rather than an aggregate divergence that may be irrelevant to any consumer.
- Bulletproof controls: truth-blind null head + RISKYGRID positive control (with a faint variant near the detection floor) jointly rule out the two most dangerous alternative explanations (the audit is broken; the audit has no power).
- Honest disclosure of self-caught pitfalls (RNG cloning, Wasserstein noise floor) that would otherwise silently corrupt any restart-based evaluation — a genuine service to the community.
- Cheap, fully released pipeline (<$9 CPU, code + checkpoints + raw returns) and a concrete audit-passing standard for future calibrated methods.
- Mechanistic depth: three independent probes (training-time, performance, seeds) tie the artifact to the learning process rather than agent quality, and the recalibration experiment cleanly distinguishes "uninformative" from "miscalibrated."

## Limitations

- Agent/policy coverage is narrow: QR-DQN, C51, IQN under greedy/CVaR-greedy, plus one pretrained QR-DQN per ALE game; modern calibrated variants (evidential, conformal) are not audited.
- The full-ALE zoo agents were trained *without* sticky actions, so the headline "every top Breakout claim refuted" quantifies artifact size under a policy mismatch rather than a clean replication — the author flags this but it weakens the at-scale claim.
- Only three MinAtar games and two ALE games; no continuous-control or non-Ale environments, so generalization is untested.
- The audit requires snapshot-restart access to the environment, limiting applicability to simulators that support deep-copy + forced-action restart.
- "Faint real risk" below the detection floor (median null ≈0.07) is, by construction, not adjudicated; the paper cannot rule out genuine but sub-floor trade-offs in MinAtar.
- Single author and arXiv v1 (no indication of peer review).

## Relevance

直接相关于分布式强化学习与不确定性估计的可信度评估这一研究主线：如果你关注 risk-sensitive RL、quantile/distributional heads 的下游使用（可解释性、CVaR 策略、安全监控），本文提供了一个可复用的审计工具包与"通过审计"的基准，揭示了当前主流算法 top-claims 的系统性失真，对评估方法论和复现性研究有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do modern calibrated heads (evidential RL — Stutts et al. 2024; conformal wrappers — Gan et al. 2025; SENTINEL ensembles — Eriksson et al. 2022) pass the excess-Wasserstein audit, or do they merely layer estimators on an uninformative base?
- Would an ALE QR-DQN *trained under sticky actions* still show 100% top-strata refutation on Breakout, closing the author's explicitly flagged gap?
- Can the artifact be predicted from training signals alone (e.g., target-variance or projection-step diagnostics), giving practitioners a per-state reliability flag that the paper's leak-free AUROC=0.92 probe suggests is hard?
- Does the doomed-state specimen generalize — i.e., is overclaiming concentrated at degenerate/near-absorbing states across environments, and would masking such states at training time reduce the artifact?
- How does the audit verdict change for continuous-control distributional actors (e.g., distributional SAC) where snapshot-restart is cheaper and stochasticity is denser?
- The recalibration result ("passes only by nullifying claims") implies the missing information is not in the head; is it recoverable from an auxiliary input (e.g., ensemble disagreement, bootstrapped target variance) without simply re-introducing the same artifact?
