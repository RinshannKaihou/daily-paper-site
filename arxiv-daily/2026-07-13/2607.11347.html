From Neural Network Decisions to Training Cases: An Exact Account via Case-Based Decision Theory

Manli Yan · Yuebin Lin · Yaowen Yu ✉ · Yong Zhao · Huazhong University of Science and Technology · 13 Jul 2026
Key finding

A frozen-representation OLS readout admits an exact case-based decomposition — each score is a weighted sum of training returns, enabling a retraining-free audit.

Why this matters

A retraining-free, case-level audit for high-stakes neural decisions — bridges training-data attribution, representational geometry, and post-hoc audit with explicit semantic boundaries.

Critical edge

Exactness holds only for OLS on a frozen representation — excluding most deployed nonlinear-head models unless re-linearized. All real-covariate tasks use proxy or synthetic returns, not validated policy audits.

01

Methodology

The reduction

Post-training, freeze the representation φ(x); fit an OLS linear readout Q(x,a) = vₐᵀφ(x) per action. Let Φ be the training representation matrix and G = ΦᵀΦ the empirical Gram matrix.

Proposition 1 (exact decomposition). Solving the OLS normal equations vₐ = G⁻¹Φᵀrₐ and substituting back yields the closed form below — under full column rank, the coefficient is the unique minimum-norm OLS-induced case-influence term.

Q(x,a) = Σᵢ αᵢ(x) · ri,a   # each score = weighted sum of training returns   αᵢ(x) = φ(x)ᵀ G⁻¹ φ(xᵢ)  # Gram-geometry coefficient

Coordinate invariance. α(x) is invariant to invertible linear reparameterizations — a property of the represented function, not the basis. Isotropic Tikhonov (α_λ via (G+λI)⁻¹) recovers the same form in the limit but breaks invariance under non-orthogonal reparameterizations.

Sufficient CBDT-similarity regime. Algebraic form ≠ semantic identity. CBDT uses non-negative similarity; OLS coefficients are signed and Gram-induced. A transparent sufficient condition is representation whitening (G = nI) + non-negativity on the domain. Outside it, αᵢ is a coordinate-invariant, possibly signed Gram influence term — preventing the "influential ≠ similar" conflation.

Three audit signals (probe fidelity measured by OLS-vs-NN reconstruction correlation ρ_recon):

case audit score

ψᵢ(a,x) = αᵢ(x)·ri,a

Signed per-case contribution — which training cases raise, lower, or offset a given score.

influence entropy H_α

H_α over action dist. of top-k cases

Low entropy ⇒ coherent support; high entropy ⇒ dispersed evidence.

case-influence risk O_α

O_α(x) = H_α(x,a_x) + γ·D_Gini(x)  # γ=1

Flags queries whose strongest cases give dispersed or conflicting action evidence.

what it needs

Only one top-layer OLS probe. No retraining, no optimization-trajectory access, no Hessian / inverse-Hessian products (unlike Influence Functions).

03

Strengths & limitations

+ Strengths

  • Genuinely exact, closed-form decomposition — not an approximation — with a precise validity boundary.
  • Honest semantic line: the sufficient-regime / signed-influence split prevents conflating "influential" with "similar."
  • Lightweight audit: one OLS probe, no retraining, no Hessian products.
  • Entropy + Gini risk diagnostic is intuitive and complements margin/confidence shift detectors.
  • Controlled-synthetic recovery (96% agreement, r ≈ 0.99) cleanly demonstrates the premise.

− Limitations

  • Restricted readout class — OLS on a frozen representation only; nonlinear heads left to future work.
  • Proxy/synthetic returns on every real-covariate task; no validated outcome-grounded policy audit.
  • Scalability — must invert/regularize the H×H Gram matrix and store full training memory; Nyström not evaluated.
  • ~58.6 ms/query — orders of magnitude slower than baseline attribution.
  • Partly self-favoring — decisive Top-30 benchmark matches the method's structural assumption; Pearson-r CI overlaps IF.
  • No code release or peer review indicated.
04

Open questions

Can the exact decomposition extend to nonlinear heads via local linearization (last-layer + activation Jacobian), and how fast does probe fidelity degrade away from OLS?

How sensitive are αᵢ(x) and the risk diagnostic when action returns are estimated from a noisy outcome model rather than observed?

Does the case-influence risk generalize beyond covariate-axis scale shifts to realistic distribution shift (temporal PJM drift, demographic Adult shift) vs. modern OOD baselines?

For large memories, what is the accuracy–cost trade-off of Nyström / random-feature approximations to G⁻¹ at reduced rank?

Since Top-30 & Pearson r are scored on a structurally-matched synthetic generator, how does the ranking hold on human-labeled influential cases (DataInf, DataModels-style ground truth)?
