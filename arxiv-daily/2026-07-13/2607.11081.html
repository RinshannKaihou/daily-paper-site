Why this matters

Attention-head-level decoupling of motion vs structure gives video DiTs a mechanistic, interpretable handle for controllable generation and editing.

Critical edge

Over-aligned semantics can yield unnatural kinematics — a penguin walking like a bear — that paradoxically score higher on MF, the headline metric.

01

Methodology

Built on CogVideoX with no parameter updates. A head-level analysis stage explains where motion and structure live; two guidance stages then act on that map.

Analysis 1 — Motion-specific heads. Per-head cross-frame attention yields a head-specific displacement map D^h, validated against RAFT optical flow via Directional Alignment + Correlation. Temporal heads track object/background motion; spatial aggregations dilute it.

Analysis 2 — Structure-specialized heads. Attention entropy H_attn selects structurally informative heads: low-entropy (e.g. L20·H8, entropy 3.34) are sharply diagonal; high-entropy (L20·H6, 7.89) are diffuse.

Semantic-aware motion guidance. Top-k candidates replace argmax; the patch most cosine-similar in diffusion features wins (SCR). A correspondence bias β is added at the best-match index (SRW); the initial noise latent is optimized (12 steps) on an L2 semantic motion loss.

Selective structure injection. Only value features of heads with entropy below τ are injected — avoiding the identity leakage of naïve all-head injection.

03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Principled, interpretable foundation — motion & structure tied to identifiable, entropy/flow-measurable heads, not another black-box trick.
  • Training-free and lightweight — no fine-tuning, marginal compute overhead, plugs into existing DiTs (CogVideoX, Wan).
  • Multi-axis evaluation — four metrics, ablations, difficulty stratification, 20-person user study, second backbone.
  • Movie Scene Dataset fills a real gap — DAVIS/WebVid-derived benchmarks under-represent professional shot composition.
  • Generalizes beyond transfer — structure-head injection enables identity-swap editing; head perturbation improves VBench dynamics.

Limitations

  • Patch-level displacement cannot capture fine non-rigid motion — facial expressions, mouth shapes (Supp. F.4, Fig. 29).
  • Semantic correspondence can over-align incompatible articulations → unnatural kinematics that still score high on MF.
  • Lower Temporal Consistency than DiTFlow/GWTF; the "TC favors static outputs" defense is only partly substantiated.
  • Movie Scene Dataset depends on a commercial partner (RASCA FX) — long-term reproducibility uncertain.
  • Single main-text backbone (CogVideoX); cross-DiT-family generalization shown only in the Wan supplement.
  • Hyperparameters (β, τ, top-k) are fixed, not per-video; sensitivity sweeps exist but per-instance tuning is unexplored.
04

Open questions

Q1 · GENERALITY

Does the motion vs structure head dichotomy hold across HunyuanVideo, Mochi, or Sora-class DiTs, or is it specific to CogVideoX's unified attention?

Q2 · OVER-ALIGNMENT

Can semantic-correspondence refinement be made adaptive — gating part-level matches with kinematic-plausibility priors to avoid the "penguin walking like a bear" failure?

Q3 · METRIC

Would a motion-magnitude-normalized Temporal Consistency metric change HALO's ranking, given the claim that standard TC favors static outputs?

Q4 · STABILITY

Is the entropy threshold τ = 7 stable across resolutions, frame counts, and backbones, or must it be re-estimated per model?

Q5 · CONTROL

Can motion-specific heads drive explicit direction-and-intensity control — the stated future-work direction — not just transfer/perturbation?
