01 / METHOD

A pre-SFT reorganization, three moves

UMoE never touches the training objective — it reshapes the expert pool before standard next-token SFT. The output model is architecturally identical to the input (same E, K, params, inference cost).

1

Domain-aligned pruning

Run the model on a 1024-sample calibration set; score each routed expert with REAP saliency, which couples gate strength with MLP output norm. Retain the top E/2.

S(l,e) = (1/|T|) Σ gl,e(h) · ‖fl,e(h)‖₂
fixed 50% ratio · calibration seed 42
2

Budget-preserving regrow

Each freed slot is seeded round-robin from a survivor. Both parent and regrown tensor get independent symmetric Gaussian noise — router rows and MLP weights alike.

W' = W + σ·s(W)·ε,  ε ~ N(0,I) W'_parent , W'_regrown  iid
σ = 0.05 · symmetric, not antithetic
3

Standard full-param SFT

One epoch on D with the recipe below. The downstream objective is unchanged — the only difference is domain-aligned expert initialization.

Adam(β=.9,.95) wd .1 · clip .1 cosine LR 1e-4 → 1e-5 · warmup 10% global batch 512 · built on slime
same recipe, all domains, no tuning
Two load-bearing rules (from ablation): perturbation must be (i) two-sided — parent + regrown — and (ii) applied to both router row and MLP weights. One-sided noise is actively destructive (−4.24) and collapses routing share to the unperturbed half.

03 / ASSESSMENT

Strengths & limitations

Novelty is moderate — REAP pruning, sparse upcycling, and Drop-Upcycling-style perturbation all predate this work. The contribution is the recombination, the mechanism, and the frozen-recipe generality.

Strengths

  • Preserves the exact deployment budget (E, K, params, inference cost) — drop-in for existing serving stacks.
  • One frozen recipe across 2 architectures, 5 domains, 12 benchmarks, 3 data scales; no per-domain tuning.
  • Mechanism analysis goes beyond "it works" — the post-hoc pruning test is a genuinely diagnostic experiment.
  • Strong-baseline setup (already above Qwen3-30B-A3B-Thinking) preempts the "weak baseline" critique.
  • Honest about the destructive config: one-sided perturbation reported at −4.24, not buried.

Limitations

  • Only Qwen-family MoEs (top-K=8) — DeepSeek-V4 / Kimi-K2 / shared-expert architectures unverified.
  • Pruning ratio fixed at 50% throughout; no sensitivity analysis, adaptive ratios deferred.
  • Headline "beats Thinking" result depends on an unreleased in-house math corpus.
  • Gains compress on the newer base (+1.67 vs +3.40) — possible vanishing headroom.
  • No head-to-head training run vs ESFT or PERFT — advantage over cheaper methods is asserted, not measured.
  • Single σ = 0.05 with no sweep; eval is mostly high-variance avg@8/32.
04 / OPEN

Questions worth chasing

Q1

Does the gain hold or vanish on non-Qwen MoEs (DeepSeek-V4, Kimi-K2) — especially shared-expert-heavy routing?

Q2

Is 50% pruning optimal, or would a saliency-knee adaptive ratio win, particularly on already-well-aligned bases?

Q3

Does UMoE compose with downstream RL (GRPO), or is the SFT-stage gain erased in the RL phase?

Q4

Can reorganization be made iterative / online — re-prune and re-expand mid-SFT as specialization drifts?

Q5

Is σ = 0.05 robust, and is symmetric Gaussian optimal vs structured (low-rank / dropout-style) perturbation?

Q6

Does UMoE stack with PEFT (PERFT, LoRA-on-experts), or is full-param SFT needed for the −5.00 fragility that underpins the gain?
