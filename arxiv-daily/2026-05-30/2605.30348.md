> **一句话总结：** LLMSurgeon 将大语言模型预训练数据混合比例估计形式化为标签偏移假设下的约束线性逆问题，通过外部代理分类器的校准软混淆矩阵对生成文本的领域分布进行"去模糊"，在粗粒度场景（6类）上达到94-95%的重叠准确率，为中粒度（17类）和细粒度（87种编程语言）场景提供了首个系统性基准。

> **TL;DR:** LLMSurgeon formalizes "Data Mixture Surgery" (DMS) -- estimating an LLM's pretraining domain mixture from generated text alone -- as a constrained linear inverse problem under label-shift. It trains a proxy domain classifier, computes a calibrated soft confusion matrix, and solves a quadratic program to "de-blur" classifier outputs. Evaluated on LLMScan (8 open-source LLMs, 1B-65B params, 3 granularity levels), it achieves 94-95% overlap accuracy on coarse-grained (6-domain) recovery, outperforms all MIA-based aggregation baselines by 37-55 points, and degrades gracefully to 63-66% at mid-grained (17 domains) and 30% at fine-grained (87 programming languages). Accepted at ACL 2026 Main.

## 核心贡献

- **形式化数据混合手术（DMS）问题**：首次将"仅从目标LLM生成文本估计预训练语料库领域级分布"形式化为DMS问题，区别于传统的成员推理攻击（MIA）的实例级检测。
- **提出LLMSurgeon三阶段框架**：(1) 在已知参考数据上训练外部代理分类器并计算校准软混淆矩阵；(2) 用中立提示从目标LLM采样生成文本并通过分类器获得有偏观测；(3) 求解约束线性逆问题（L2最小化+单纯形约束）恢复真实混合先验。
- **构建LLMScan基准**：涵盖8个开源LLM（1B-65B参数）、三个粒度级别（粗6类/中17类/细87类），所有模型具有可验证的预训练数据配方。
- **验证实际应用价值**：通过毒性注入实验展示了安全审计分诊能力，通过时序held-out泛化实验（OLMo系列）验证了方法的迁移性。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalize Data Mixture Surgery (DMS)**: First to formalize the macroscopic problem of recovering pretraining domain mixture proportions from generated text, shifting focus from instance-level MIA to corpus-level distribution recovery.
- **LLMSurgeon framework**: A three-stage pipeline -- (1) train proxy classifier on reference data and compute calibrated soft confusion matrix C; (2) classify neutral-prompt generations from target LLM to obtain biased observation; (3) solve constrained inverse problem min ||C^T * pi - p_bar||^2 subject to simplex constraints to recover the latent mixture prior.
- **LLMScan benchmark**: First recipe-verifiable evaluation suite with 8 open-source LLMs (LLaMA-1, OLMo, Amber, Pythia, GPT-Neo, StarCoder) at three granularity levels (K=6/17/87), using real-world pretraining documentation as ground truth.
- **Practical safety auditing**: Demonstrates toxic injection detection capability, recovering monotonically increasing toxic mass estimates at 5%/10%/20% injection levels.

## Methodology

LLMSurgeon operates under the label-shift assumption: domain-specific language patterns remain invariant between training data and model-generated output, while only the domain proportions change.

**Stage 1 -- Characterizing Systematic Bias**: An external proxy classifier f_phi (fine-tuned DistilBERT) is trained on a held-out reference dataset with known domain labels. A soft confusion matrix C is computed where C_ij = E_{x~p_i}[f_phi(x)_j], capturing the expected probability of predicting domain j given a sample from domain i.

**Stage 2 -- Observing the Target Distribution**: Neutral prompts are used to sample N generations from the target LLM. These are classified by f_phi to obtain the empirical mean prediction vector p_bar = (1/N) * sum(f_phi(x_n)). This p_bar is a biased observation: p_bar approximates C^T * pi, not pi directly.

**Stage 3 -- Inverse Surgery**: The key insight is that by linearity of expectation, E[f_phi(x)] = C^T * pi. Thus, recovering pi becomes a constrained quadratic program: pi_hat = argmin_{pi in simplex} ||C^T * pi - p_bar||_2^2. The simplex constraints (sum=1, non-negative) ensure a valid probability distribution.

**Key design choices**: Neutral prompts (not instructional/expository/conversational) to minimize stylistic distortion; merging semantically overlapping domains (e.g., C4 + CommonCrawl) to avoid ill-conditioned inversion; N=5000 reference documents per domain as optimal trade-off.

## Results & Highlights

- **Coarse-grained (K=6)**: Overlap accuracy of 94.46% (OLMo-1B), 95.14% (LLaMA1-7B), 78.87% (Amber-13B), 94.26% (LLaMA1-65B). R^2 = 0.99.
- **Massive improvement over MIA baselines**: Best MIA baseline (Neighbor) achieves ~41% on OLMo-1B vs. LLMSurgeon's 94.46% (+52 points). All 11 MIA-based aggregation methods cluster below 50% accuracy.
- **Mid-grained (K=17)**: 61.86% (GPT-Neo-2.7B), 63.20% (Pythia-2.8B), 65.98% (Pythia-12B). R^2 = 0.54. Performance limited by increased semantic overlap.
- **Fine-grained (K=87, StarCoder)**: 30.37% overlap accuracy, R^2 = 0.01, but still surpasses best MIA baseline (27.54%) and maintains low MAE (0.018). C/C++ confusion and similar language pairs create ill-conditioned inverse.
- **Inverse correction ablation**: Removing inverse correction drops accuracy by 1-4 points on coarse-grained and ~15% relative on StarCoder, confirming the confusion matrix de-blurring is essential.
- **Training dynamics**: Applied to intermediate checkpoints, reveals Amber's "fluctuation-then-convergence" pattern vs. OLMo's stable trajectory, both converging to ground truth at final checkpoints.
- **Held-out generalization**: Protocol fixed on OLMo-1B/OLMo-2 transfers to OLMo-3 without retuning, achieving 86.41% overlap accuracy and recovering dominant Web component (76.88% -> 75.37%).
- **Toxic injection detection**: Monotonic recovery of toxic mass at 5% (estimated 7.90%), 10% (12.00%), 20% (22.73%) injection levels with >97% overall accuracy.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The formalization of DMS as a label-shift inverse problem is clean and well-motivated. The connection between confusion matrix calibration and mixture recovery is mathematically elegant. However, the core technique (constrained least-squares inversion of a confusion matrix) is well-established in statistics and adapted rather than invented here.
- **Rigor**: LLMScan is a genuine contribution -- using real open-source models with documented recipes rather than synthetic mixtures. The three granularity levels provide a nuanced picture. Ablations are thorough (classifier backbone, sampling population, prompt style, inverse correction, domain merging). The per-class error tables in the appendix are commendably transparent.
- **Reproducibility**: High. Method requires only generated text, a reference dataset, and a lightweight classifier. Code is released. The LLMScan benchmark uses publicly available models.
- **Significance**: Addresses a real gap -- existing MIA tools answer "was this sample in training?" but not "what fraction of training was from domain X?". The safety auditing application is practically compelling.
- **Red flags**:
  - **Amber-13B is a notable outlier** at 78.87% vs. 94-95% for other coarse-grained models, and the per-class errors show 18.8% deviation on Web and 18.3% on GitHub. This is not deeply investigated.
  - **Fine-grained results are effectively poor**: 30.37% overlap accuracy with R^2=0.01 means the method cannot reliably distinguish 87 programming languages. The low MAE is misleading because most languages have near-zero proportions.
  - **Closed-world assumption is a fundamental limitation**: The method cannot discover domains outside the predefined taxonomy, making it a verification tool rather than a discovery tool.
  - **Label-shift assumption is untested for aligned models**: The authors acknowledge RLHF/instruction tuning may violate the assumption but do not empirically test this. Most deployed LLMs have undergone post-training alignment.
  - **The MIA baselines are straw men**: Aggregating instance-level binary MIA predictions to estimate mixture proportions is a fundamentally mismatched approach. The paper would benefit from stronger, purpose-built baselines.
  - **Inverse correction gains are modest on coarse-grained settings** (1-2 points), suggesting the proxy classifier is already quite good and the main benefit is in harder settings.

## Strengths

- Clean problem formalization with clear mathematical formulation (mixture model, label shift, constrained inversion).
- LLMScan is a valuable community resource -- first benchmark with real-world pretraining recipes at multiple granularities.
- Thorough ablation studies covering classifier backbone, sampling population, prompt styles, domain merging, and inverse correction.
- Transparent per-class error reporting in appendices reveals exactly where and why the method struggles.
- Safety auditing demonstration (toxic injection) shows compelling practical application.
- Temporal held-out generalization experiment (OLMo family) provides evidence of genuine generalization.
- Lightweight and practical: no access to model weights, training data, or internals required.

## Limitations

- **Closed-world assumption**: Cannot detect domains outside the predefined K categories. If a proprietary model was trained on a domain not in the taxonomy, it will be misattributed.
- **Label-shift assumption fragility**: Extensive post-training alignment (RLHF, instruction tuning, DPO) likely distorts generation distributions away from pretraining priors. This is acknowledged but not empirically tested.
- **Performance degrades sharply with granularity**: Near-perfect at K=6, moderate at K=17, poor at K=87. The method's utility is primarily for coarse-grained auditing.
- **Semantically overlapping domains cause catastrophic failure**: Separating C4 from CommonCrawl drops accuracy from 99% to 42%, highlighting sensitivity to domain definition choices.
- **No evaluation on aligned/chat models**: All tested models are base pre-trained models. Real-world auditing targets (GPT-4, Claude, etc.) are all heavily aligned.
- **Amber-13B underperformance unexplained**: The 78.87% accuracy is a significant gap compared to other coarse-grained models, suggesting model-specific failure modes that deserve investigation.

## Relevance

本文提出了从LLM生成文本逆向推断预训练数据混合比例的新范式，将成员推理攻击从实例级扩展到语料库级别。对于关注LLM透明度、训练数据审计、安全治理的研究者具有重要参考价值。其LLMScan基准和标签偏移逆问题建模思路可启发后续工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does LLMSurgeon perform on instruction-tuned or RLHF-aligned models where the label-shift assumption may be violated?
- Can hierarchical inference (coarse-then-fine estimation) improve fine-grained accuracy beyond the flat 87-class approach?
- What is the minimum amount of generated text needed for reliable mixture estimation at each granularity level?
- Could non-linear inversion methods (e.g., optimal transport) outperform the linear confusion matrix approach for semantically overlapping domains?
- How robust is the method to adversarial manipulation -- could a model creator deliberately obfuscate their training data signature in generated outputs?
- Can the approach be extended to multilingual corpora or cross-lingual domain estimation?
- Is there a way to relax the closed-world assumption to discover novel training domains not in the predefined taxonomy?
