> **一句话总结：** 提出Self-Trained Verification (STV)，利用参考答案条件下的不对称性（模型看到参考解后能发现自身错误，但独自无法做到）作为蒸馏目标来训练验证器，在测试时的verify-refine循环和训练时的Verifier-in-the-Loop (ViL)两种场景下均实现显著性能提升——在困难数学题上将pass@1翻倍，在科学推理上将1.5%提升至21%，且ViL训练使已收敛的RL生成器的独立pass@1额外提升30%。

> **TL;DR:** STV trains a verifier via on-policy distillation from a reference-conditioned version of the same model, exploiting the asymmetry that models can diagnose their own errors when shown a reference solution but not alone. On hard DAPO math (Qwen3-8B pass@1=0 "Hardest" split), STV roughly doubles final-round pass@1 vs. an untrained verifier (5.5% vs. ~2.7% on Hardest, 27.4% on Hard), beating the 4x larger Qwen3-32B without verification. On SciKnowEval scientific reasoning, STV lifts Hardest pass@1 from 1.5% to 21.0%, surpassing even Qwen3-235B-A22B. Verifier-in-the-Loop (ViL) training — RL on the generator inside the V-R loop with a frozen STV verifier — pushes a converged RLVR generator's standalone pass@1 up 30% relative and final-round pass@1 up 33%. Ablations show SFT fails (off-policy drift), meta-verifier RL (proxied by GPT-5.2) fails, and a 4B STV verifier matches the 8B STV verifier.

## 核心贡献

- 提出Self-Trained Verification (STV)：核心观察是模型单独无法发现自身错误，但给定参考答案后可以；将这种不对称性转化为on-policy蒸馏目标，训练验证器模仿"看到参考答案的自己"
- 提出Verifier-in-the-Loop (ViL)训练：在已RLVR收敛的生成器上，用冻结的STV验证器在V-R循环中提供反馈继续训练生成器，突破RL收敛瓶颈
- 证明ViL训练不仅提升V-R循环性能（+33% pass@1），还出人意料地提升生成器的独立pass@1（+30%），无需测试时验证器
- 展示弱到强验证（weak-to-strong verification）：4B STV验证器可匹配8B STV验证器效果
- STV引导的8B模型在困难问题上超越4x大的Qwen3-32B和30x大的Qwen3-235B-A22B

## Key Contributions

- Introduces Self-Trained Verification (STV), which trains the verifier via on-policy distillation from a reference-conditioned teacher (same model, different prompt), avoiding the need for human annotations or stronger models
- Demonstrates that on-policy distillation (OPD) with alpha-divergence (alpha=0.5, Jensen-Shannon) works while SFT fails due to off-policy drift — the student encounters prefixes never seen during training
- Proposes Verifier-in-the-Loop (ViL) training: RL on the generator inside the multi-turn V-R loop with a frozen STV verifier, yielding gains past RLVR convergence
- Shows the surprising finding that ViL training improves the generator's standalone round-0 pass@1 by 30% relative, suggesting learning from diagnostic feedback instills general reasoning capabilities
- Demonstrates weak-to-strong verification: a 4B STV verifier matches an 8B STV verifier (26.4% vs. 27.4%), and a 1.7B STV verifier matches the untrained 8B

## Methodology

**STV Training (Section 3):** The student verifier V_theta(·|x, y_{r-1}) is trained to match the output distribution of the reference-conditioned teacher V*(·|x, y_{r-1}, y*(x)). The loss combines on-policy distillation (OPD) using alpha-divergence with alpha=0.5 (Jensen-Shannon divergence) plus a verdict-RL component that optimizes verdict accuracy with reward I[v_r = I[y_{r-1} = y*(x)]]. The full objective: L_STV(theta) = L_OPD(theta) + lambda * L_RL(theta). Training data comes from the generator's rollouts on training prompts.

**Why SFT fails but OPD works:** SFT trains the student on sequences from the teacher distribution, but at test time the student must generate its own sequences. Once it drifts from the teacher, it encounters prefixes never seen during training (off-policy drift). OPD avoids this by sampling from the student's own distribution.

**ViL Training (Section 4):** The generator is trained with standard RL, where each episode unrolls a multi-turn V-R rollout. The frozen STV verifier provides feedback at each round, and the reward is the verifiable correctness of the final answer. Only the generator's parameters are updated.

**Experimental Setup (Section 5.1):** Uses Qwen3-8B as the base model for both generator and verifier. Training/test data from DAPO (hard math), split by Qwen3-8B pass@1: Hard (0 < pass@1 < 0.2) and Hardest (pass@1 = 0). Test problems deduplicated by embedding similarity (threshold 0.8) against training, leaving ~150 problems per test bin. 32 independent V-R loops per test problem, up to 20 rounds. Also evaluated on SciKnowEval for scientific reasoning.

## Results & Highlights

**Test-time V-R loops (base generator):**
- STV verifier lifts final-round pass@1 ~2x over untrained verifier (5.5% vs ~2.7% on Hardest, 27.4% on Hard), p < 0.01
- STV-guided 8B outperforms Qwen3-32B without verification (Hardest: 5.5% vs 2.7%; Hard: 27.4% vs 17.8%)
- Scientific reasoning (SciKnowEval): Hardest pass@1 from 1.5% to 21.0%; Hard from ~11% to 42.4%
- STV-guided 8B beats Qwen3-235B-A22B on both SciKnowEval bins (21.0% vs 8.0% Hardest, 42.4% vs 23.6% Hard)

**Baselines that fail:** SFT verifier (no gains, off-policy drift), Verdict-RL-only verifier (marginal), Meta-verifier RL using GPT-5.2 as proxy (little effect), no verifier training (stagnates quickly)

**Continual-trained generator:** STV gains compound on top of an already RLVR-trained generator (p < 0.05)

**ViL Training (Section 5.4):**
- Standalone round-0 pass@1: Hardest 10.7% -> 14.7% (+37%), Hard 36.7% -> 47.7% (+30%)
- Final-round pass@1 with verifier: Hardest 27.3% vs RLVR-only 16.1% (p < 0.01)
- Continuing standard RLVR for same compute yields zero gain (already converged)

**Weak-to-strong verification (Section 5.3):** 4B STV verifier competitive with 8B STV (26.4% vs 27.4%); 1.7B STV matches untrained 8B

**Why STV works (Section 5.5):**
- STV mitigates reward hacking: untrained verifiers show score inflation while accuracy stagnates; STV verifiers are well-calibrated
- Precision-coverage frontier: STV shows 3-5x higher precision at same coverage, and precision increases with coverage
- Feedback quality: STV feedback provides +3.2% additional gain over untrained feedback even with ground-truth verdicts

**Output distribution analysis (Section 5.6):**
- Verification does not suppress diversity: pass@k improves for first ~10 rounds
- Refinement dominates resampling (Best-of-N) at matched compute for base and STV generators
- Continual-trained generator (trained only for round-0 accuracy) is the lone exception where BoN matches refinement

**Ablations (Section 5.4):**
- Prefix-conditioning (using first 50% of reference solution) achieves lower pass@1 than STV
- ViL with self-verification (no oracle) still outperforms prefix-conditioning but underperforms STV at round 20

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The core idea — exploiting the reference-conditioned vs. unconditioned asymmetry for verifier training via self-distillation — is clean, genuinely novel, and well-motivated. Framing it as on-policy distillation from a privileged-information teacher is a solid conceptual contribution. The ViL training scheme (generator RL inside the V-R loop) and the discovery of standalone pass@1 gains are unexpected and interesting.

**Rigor:** The paper is thorough in its experimental evaluation. It tests multiple strong baselines (SFT, verdict-RL, meta-verifier with GPT-5.2, self-verification), includes ablations for oracle usage, provides qualitative examples (Appendix A), analyzes calibration and reward hacking, and compares refinement vs. resampling at matched compute. Statistical significance is reported (p-values). However, some concerns remain:
- The "Hardest" split is defined as pass@1 = 0 from 32 rollouts, which means some problems might simply be out-of-distribution rather than genuinely hard
- The deduplication threshold (cosine similarity 0.8) between train and test could allow some near-duplicates
- Only one base model family (Qwen3) is tested; results may not generalize to other architectures

**Reproducibility:** The method is conceptually clear and implementable from the paper description. Uses standard DAPO dataset and Qwen3 models. Training details (alpha=0.5 for divergence, V-R loop structure) are specified. Code release is not mentioned.

**Significance:** This is a strong paper that addresses a real and important bottleneck. The finding that ViL training improves standalone pass@1 by 30% past RLVR convergence is remarkable and suggests a new axis for reasoning improvement. The weak-to-strong verification result (4B matching 8B) is practically valuable for inference cost reduction.

**Red flags / concerns:**
- All experiments use Qwen3-8B as base; no scaling law analysis or experiments with larger base models
- The meta-verifier baseline uses GPT-5.2 as a proxy rather than the actual method from Shao et al. (2025), which is not a fully fair comparison
- The "14x" scientific reasoning claim (1.5% to 21%) is on the Hardest split of SciKnowEval — a very low starting point that inflates the multiplier
- The standalone pass@1 gains from ViL are impressive but the mechanism (why learning from feedback improves zero-shot ability) is not fully explained
- The paper acknowledges open questions about scaling to larger models and domains beyond math/science

## Strengths

- Elegant and well-motivated core idea with clear theoretical justification (on-policy vs. off-policy)
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation with multiple baselines, ablations, and analysis sections
- The OPD vs. SFT analysis is insightful and explains why simple approaches fail
- Dual contribution: test-time scaling via better verification AND training-time scaling via ViL
- Strong qualitative examples in Appendix A showing concrete failure modes of untrained verifiers
- The precision-coverage frontier analysis and reward hacking diagnostics add analytical depth
- Practical weak-to-strong verification result for inference cost savings

## Limitations

- Single model family (Qwen3); no evidence the approach transfers to Llama, Gemma, or proprietary models
- Only math (DAPO) and scientific reasoning (SciKnowEval) domains tested; code generation and open-ended reasoning remain unexplored
- Computational cost of STV training and ViL is not quantified or compared to alternatives
- The OPD vs. SFT finding may be dataset/model specific; the authors acknowledge the debate remains open
- No investigation of iterative STV (train verifier -> train generator -> retrain verifier -> ...) despite the paper's framing of iterative self-improvement
- Test set sizes are small (~150 problems per bin), which increases variance in reported numbers

## Relevance

与推理模型自改进、test-time compute scaling和强化学习训练高度相关。STV提供了一种无需人类标注即可训练高质量验证器的方法，ViL训练开辟了RL收敛后继续提升的新路径。对研究RLVR、self-play、过程奖励模型的读者尤其有价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the STV asymmetry persist at frontier scale (e.g., 70B+ models), or do larger models already catch their own errors without reference solutions?
- Can iterative STV (retrain verifier with improved generator, then retrain generator) produce compounding gains, or does it saturate quickly?
- What is the compute-optimal allocation between generator RL training, verifier STV training, and test-time V-R rounds?
- Does the standalone pass@1 improvement from ViL transfer to out-of-distribution tasks (e.g., code, commonsense reasoning)?
- How sensitive is STV to the quality and diversity of reference solutions? What happens with imperfect references?
- Can STV be combined with process reward models (PRMs) for even finer-grained verification signal?
