Leave-a-Window-Out removes a window instead of a single point to restore valid jackknife coverage in time series.

Leave a Window Out: Modifying the Jackknife for Predictive Inference in Time Series

Hanyang Jiang, Rina Foygel Barber, Ashwin Pananjady, Yao Xie · 2026-05-28

conformal-prediction time-series predictive-inference jackknife coverage-guarantees

Key Metric

90%

Near-nominal coverage restored across all five predictors where vanilla jackknife fails.

Why This Matters

It gives theoretically guaranteed conformal prediction for time series without splitting data—crucial when training data is scarce.

Critical Edge

No principled, data-driven method exists for choosing the window size tau—its selection remains an open question.

Methodology

  • LWO procedure. For each proxy test point k, remove the full window k, k+1, …, k+τ from training instead of a single point. The predictor is fit on the remaining pre-window and post-window data; the score at k is stored. The final prediction set uses the full predictor with the quantile of LWO scores as the radius. Setting τ = 0 recovers vanilla jackknife.
  • Cyclic embedding. The analysis introduces cyclic exchangeability and the cyclic embedding coefficient ρτ(Z), measuring how far a dependent sequence is from embeddable into a cyclically exchangeable longer sequence. Coverage is proved by embedding, using exchangeability of circular scores, and controlling the gap via out-of-sample stability.
  • OOS stability. A (score, algorithm) pair is stable if masking a random training block of length ℓ does not shift the test-point score too much. Ridge regression, k-NN, kernel ridge, and bagged predictors satisfy this with concrete bounds.
  • Two coverage regimes. Corollary 1 covers approximately Markovian + mixing sequences with single-block stability. Corollary 2 covers general beta-mixing sequences with two-block stability and no Markov assumption. Both yield coverage of the form 1 − α − O(√(ν + mixing + τ/n)).

Strengths / Limitations

Strengths

  • Pinpoints a clean mechanistic cause of jackknife failure (two-sided vs one-sided information asymmetry).
  • Two complementary theoretical results for different dependence structures.
  • Cyclic embedding coefficient is a potentially general tool for temporal-dependence problems.
  • Extensive experiments across five predictors and three data settings.
  • Recovers jackknife+ guarantees when τ = 0 and exchangeability holds.

Limitations

  • No principled, data-driven method for choosing τ.
  • Coverage bounds are conservative (square-root dependence on mixing/stability).
  • Stability assumption may fail for unstable black-box predictors (e.g., decision trees).
  • No comparison against EnbPI, SPCI, or KOWCPI—only split CP and vanilla jackknife.
  • Fixed-batch prediction only; no online/streaming or multi-step-ahead extension.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can τ be chosen in a fully data-driven way while maintaining valid coverage guarantees?
  • How does LWO compare computationally and statistically to EnbPI, SPCI, and other time-series conformal methods?
  • Can the LWO principle be combined with cross-conformal prediction or jackknife+-after-bootstrap?
  • Does the cyclic embedding coefficient have applications beyond predictive inference?
  • Can the framework be extended to online/streaming settings?
  • How sensitive is performance to misspecification of τ (too small vs. too large)?
