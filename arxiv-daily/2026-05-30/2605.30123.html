Encryption noise floor ρ ≈ 10⁻¹⁶ sits more than sixteen orders of magnitude below channel noise variance—effectively invisible to convergence.
Why This Matters
It opens a new design paradigm for combining homomorphic encryption with wireless aggregation, with direct impact on privacy-preserving edge ML systems.
Critical Edge
The protocol assumes channel constancy across all four phases—only viable in short-range, low-mobility THz scenarios, not general wireless networks.

Methodology

The paper targets slow-fading LoS wireless channels (e.g., indoor THz). In this setting, N edge devices train a global model via zero-order optimization, each transmitting only one scalar per round by exploiting channel superposition.

The core obstacle: multi-key CKKS uses a huge modulus q (~2¹⁰⁹–2²¹⁸). Any residual channel-estimation error gets amplified by q/Λ ≈ 2⁷⁰ during decryption, swamping the message. Pre-equalization (ZF/MMSE) diverges catastrophically from the first iteration.

The insight is algebraic: by retransmitting partial public keys and ciphertexts through the same channel realization, the q-scale terms cancel exactly during decryption. Only bounded noise remains. Theorem 2 proves O(1/√K) convergence with an additive noise floor ρ that is negligible in practice.

MNIST 0-vs-1 logistic regression (d=785, N=10, K=400 rounds). Encryption incurs <1% accuracy loss versus the unencrypted baseline.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Precise, well-motivated problem definition
  • Elegant algebraic cancellation mechanism
  • Rigorous convergence proof with explicit noise bound
  • Comprehensive comparison table (Table I)
  • Explicit failure-mode analysis for pre-equalization

Limitations

  • Strong channel-constancy assumption
  • Only MNIST binary classification tested
  • Massive ciphertext expansion (~10⁴–10⁵×)
  • Key-retransmission overhead each round
  • Honest-but-curious threat model only

Questions for Further Exploration

How robust is the protocol under slight channel variation across the four phases? Can compensation mechanisms relax the constancy assumption?
Does encrypted noise remain negligible on deep networks (ResNet, Transformer) and harder datasets (CIFAR, ImageNet)?
Can multi-key HE be combined with differential privacy, using channel noise as an extra privacy mechanism?
What is protocol security under malicious (not merely HBC) adversaries, and are decryption-share poisoning attacks feasible?
Can temporal channel correlation reduce key-retransmission frequency and lower communication overhead?
