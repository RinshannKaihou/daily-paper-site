Robust Statistics Optimization Landscape Non-Convex
Key Metric
O(σ√ε)
Estimation error in O(nd) time and O(d) samples. GeLU basin radius R = 0.029.
Why This Matters
Explains why modern activations like GeLU behave under adversarial corruption, laying groundwork for robust deep-learning theory.
Critical Edge
Purely theoretical: no empirical validation, and proofs strictly require Gaussian covariates.

Methodology

Three-part framework:

  • Convex basin (Theorem 3.1): Decompose population Hessian H(β) = H(β*) + Δ(β). Use Gaussian symmetry and Weyl's inequality to bound λmin ≥ μ − ||Δ||op, yielding a dimension-independent constant-radius basin B(β*, R).
  • Robust spectral init (Algorithm 2): Via Stein's lemma, E[YX(YX)T] becomes isotropic + rank-one when ESC > 0. Apply hypercontractive robust 1-ePCA to recover β* under contamination.
  • Robust gradient descent (Algorithm 3): Robust mean estimation of the gradient at each step. Strong convexity inside the basin yields linear convergence to O(σ√ε) in O(1) iterations.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • First convex-basin result for non-monotonic links beyond phase retrieval.
  • ESC condition is a clean, verifiable test for spectral identifiability.
  • Near-linear time and optimal sample complexity.
  • All constants computed explicitly; JAX verification code provided.
  • Honest about gaps and open problems.

Limitations

  • Gaussian covariates only; sub-Gaussian extension is open.
  • No empirical validation whatsoever.
  • Generative exponent ≤ 2; k ≥ 3 needs tensor methods.
  • Small basin radii (Phase Retrieval R ≈ 0.0016) may hurt numerics.
  • Error rate O(σ√ε) is suboptimal vs. information-theoretic O(σε).

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can convex basin analysis extend to sub-Gaussian covariates?
  • For information exponent k ≥ 3, can higher-order robust tensor methods provide analogous basin guarantees?
  • Is the ESC condition necessary, or only sufficient?
  • How do basin radii scale with signal-to-noise ratio when σ varies?
  • Can label transforms be automated for arbitrary link functions?
  • What is the practical gap between O(σ√ε) and optimal O(σε) on synthetic data?
  • Can the framework extend to Multi-Index Models with GLU-style interactions?
