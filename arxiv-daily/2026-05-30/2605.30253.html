Key Finding CAVI contracts exponentially in Wasserstein distance under transport-information inequalities and Fisher-smoothness — relaxing strong log-concavity assumptions.

Key Metric
< 1/(1+1/g)
Dimension-free limiting contraction rate for Probit regression under g-prior as n,p → ∞
Why This Matters

Provides the first rigorous convergence guarantees for widely-used CAVI and Jaakkola-Jordan algorithms under significantly weaker assumptions than prior work.

Critical Edge

Restricted to two-block CAVI; multi-block (m > 2), the practically relevant setting, is left as future work.

Methodology

1

General contraction (Theorem 1). For target π = e−f, CAVI iterates optimal distributions block-by-block. Two conditions govern convergence: Fisher-smoothness (Lipschitz optimality maps) and transport-information inequalities at fixed points (weaker than log-Sobolev). Rate: L1|2L2|1 / (λ1|2λ2|1) — sharp for Gaussians.

2

Data augmentation (Theorem 4). When auxiliary variables Z are discrete or non-smooth, convergence of the parameter block alone is established by composing maps, bypassing any condition on Z's geometry.

3

Applications. Gaussian Mixtures (discrete Z ∈ {0,1}n), Probit regression (strongly log-concave conditionals), and Logistic regression with Polya-Gamma augmentation — each matched to the appropriate theorem layer.

Results & Highlights

ModelResult
Gaussian Rate equals spectral norm of correlation matrix — sharp, matches Bhattacharya et al. 2025
Mixture Phase transition: r < 1 for global convergence; balanced case (p=1/2) fails globally as n grows
Probit Global rate = Bayesian fraction of missing information; bounded away from 1 in high dimensions
Logistic First rate for Jaakkola-Jordan; faster than Probit: lim r* ≤ 1/(1+4/g)

Accuracy-convergence duality: the same conditions that ensure fast CAVI convergence also guarantee high-quality variational approximations.

Strengths
  • First Wasserstein contraction for CAVI without strong log-concavity
  • Sharp Gaussian rates; clean 4-line core proof
  • Data-augmentation theorem handles discrete auxiliary spaces
  • Explicit rates for three model classes with interpretable expressions
  • High-dimensional scaling guarantees via random matrix theory
  • Accuracy-convergence duality gives practical guidance
Limitations
  • Only two-block CAVI; multi-block extension is non-trivial
  • Local convergence in general; global results need model-specific analysis
  • Fisher-smoothness & T-I conditions hard to verify beyond studied models
  • Minimal empirical validation (3 convergence curves only)
  • No guidance on which fixed point is reached from a given initialization
  • No stochastic or mini-batch CAVI variants discussed

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Extend the two-block analysis to multi-block CAVI (m > 2)?
  • What is the basin of attraction for each fixed point when r > 1 in Gaussian Mixtures?
  • Can Fisher-smoothness be verified for hierarchical or deep generative models?
  • How do Wasserstein rates compare with KL contraction rates from prior work?
  • Does the T-I framework apply to stochastic or black-box VI methods?
  • Can cross-smoothness constants be used as a practical pre-run diagnostic?
  • Does the Appendix A coordinate descent result improve upon existing practical rates?
