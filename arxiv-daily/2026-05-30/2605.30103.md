> **一句话总结：** 本文首次将迭代LLM-NAS建模为参数化交叉熵方法，证明了六项收敛性结果（CE等价性、单调质量提升、几何收敛速率、delta生成优势、模式崩塌预防、代理可靠性闭式解），并在22轮迭代、3个LLM、3300个架构的大规模实验中定量验证了理论预测。
> **TL;DR:** This paper establishes the first formal convergence theory for iterative LLM-based NAS by identifying it as a parametric Cross-Entropy (CE) method. It proves six theorems -- CE equivalence, monotone quality improvement, geometric convergence, delta generation advantage under a Markov token-error model, mode-collapse prevention via MinHash-Jaccard filtering, and a closed-form proxy reliability formula rho_S = (6/pi)*arcsin(rho_P(SNR)/2). Experiments on 3,300 architectures across 22 cycles with Mistral-7B, Qwen2.5-Coder-7B, and DeepSeek-Coder-7B confirm two predictions quantitatively and two at direction-of-effect level, while also explaining the previously unexplained proxy-reliability ceiling effect.

## 核心贡献

- 首次将迭代LLM-NAS形式化建模为参数化交叉熵（CE）方法，证明LLM微调步骤等价于在参数族上的KL散度最小化（CE更新）
- 证明期望架构质量在迭代轮次间单调非递减（Q_{t+1} >= Q_t - epsilon_t，epsilon_t = O(N^{-1/2})），以及精英集概率的几何收敛速率 C_t >= 1-(1-rho_0)^t
- 用一阶马尔可夫token错误模型替代先前工作的独立token假设，证明delta生成比全代码生成具有严格更高的有效生成率
- 证明MinHash-Jaccard新颖性过滤器可防止模式崩塌，保证生成分布的熵下界 H(p) >= H_bin(delta) > 0
- 推导代理可靠性的闭式公式 rho_S = (6/pi)*arcsin(rho_P(SNR)/2)，给出实用诊断条件 sigma^2_arch >> sigma^2_noise
- 在3,300个架构的实验中验证理论：Mistral的Spearman rho=0.926 (p=7.7e-7)是唯一通过Bonferroni校正的结果，准确预测了DeepSeek在低SNR下的不显著性

## Key Contributions

- **Theorem 1 (CE Equivalence):** Proves that the LLM fine-tuning step (minimizing cross-entropy loss over the elite set union static corpus) is equivalent to the parametric CE update, i.e., minimizing KL(p_hat_{S_t} || p_theta) over the LLM parameter family
- **Theorem 2 (Monotone Quality Improvement):** Under quality-monotone projection assumption, shows E_{a~p_{theta_t}}[q(a)] is non-decreasing across cycles, with O(N^{-1/2}) finite-sample slack
- **Theorem 3 + Corollary 4 (Convergence):** Elite concentration C_t is non-decreasing, converges to a fixed point C*, and under full expressiveness (Assumption 1(d)), converges geometrically at rate 1-(1-rho_0)^t. Under LoRA, C* <= 1 (the observed 73-76% plateau)
- **Theorem 5 (Delta Generation Advantage):** Under a first-order Markov token-error model, the valid-rate ratio r^delta/r^full = lambda^(alpha-1)*L_full * (pi_delta/pi_full) > 1, because shorter outputs (alpha < 1) suffer fewer accumulated errors
- **Theorem 7 (Mode Collapse Prevention):** The MinHash-Jaccard novelty filter maintains corpus separation and guarantees a positive lower bound on per-element probability mass, preventing degenerate mode collapse
- **Theorem 8 (Proxy Reliability):** Derives the closed-form rho_S = (6/pi)*arcsin(rho_P(SNR)/2) using the Kruskal-Kendall identity, providing the practical diagnostic sigma^2_arch >> sigma^2_noise for trustworthy proxy rankings
- **Experimental validation:** Tests all predictions against a 22-cycle, three-LLM (Mistral-7B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-Coder-7B, DeepSeek-Coder-7B), six-dataset experiment with 3,300 generated architectures; confirms ordering Mistral > Qwen > DeepSeek matching SNR ranking exactly

## Methodology

The paper models iterative LLM-NAS (as defined in prior work by Khalid et al. 2026, Adhikari et al. 2026) as a parametric Cross-Entropy method. The core insight is that the standard LLM-NAS loop -- generate architectures from the LLM, evaluate via 1-epoch proxy accuracy, filter elites above threshold tau=0.40, fine-tune the LLM on the elite set union a static corpus (LEMUR dataset of ~626 curated torch.nn.Module architectures) -- is algebraically equivalent to the CE update restricted to the LLM's parametric family.

**Theoretical framework:** The architecture space A is the set of all syntactically valid, executable Python programs defining torch.nn.Module subclasses. A quality function q: A -> [0,1] maps each architecture to first-epoch validation accuracy. The LLM with LoRA adaptation defines a parametric family F = {p_theta: theta in Theta}. The paper makes two key assumptions: (1) Regularity (bounded quality, positive initial mass on elite set, consistent estimation, parametric expressiveness for full convergence), and (2) Quality-monotone projection (the CE projection preserves or improves mean quality).

**Markov token-error model:** For the delta generation analysis, the paper introduces a two-state Markov chain over {correct, error} with transition probabilities P(error|correct) = epsilon_t and P(error|error) = gamma_t (error persistence). The stationary correct-state probability is pi_c = (1-gamma_t)/(1-gamma_t+epsilon_t). The valid-rate ratio derivation shows that the gamma_t-dependent constant C(gamma_t, epsilon_t) cancels in the ratio, making the advantage purely a function of output length reduction.

**Proxy reliability:** The paper models (q_true, q_proxy) as bivariate Normal with q_proxy = q_true + xi, derives SNR = sigma^2_arch / sigma^2_noise, and applies the Kruskal-Kendall identity to obtain the Spearman-Pearson relationship as a function of SNR.

**Experimental setup:** The paper uses data from Adhikari et al. (2026) with 22 NAS cycles across three 7B-parameter LLMs. For the proxy reliability study, the top-20 architectures per LLM were fully trained for 50 epochs (yielding N=16 for Mistral, N=15 for Qwen, N=12 for DeepSeek after removing failed runs). Compute: single NVIDIA RTX 4090, approximately 4 GPU-hours total for proxy-reliability experiments.

## Results & Highlights

- **Quantitative confirmations (2):** (1) Smoothed per-cycle mean quality Q_t rises monotonically from ~0.62 to ~0.68 across all 3 LLMs (Theorem 2). (2) Novel architectures admitted in all 22 cycles for all 3 LLMs (Theorem 7).
- **Direction-of-effect confirmations (2):** (1) Elite concentration C_t plateaus at 73-76% (LoRA ceiling, consistent with Theorem 3 Part 2). (2) DeepSeek shows non-significant Spearman correlation (p=0.102) at SNR ~0.011, consistent with the SNR->0 prediction of Theorem 8.
- **Deviations from calibrated values (2):** (1) Delta-rate ratio is 1.41 vs. calibrated prediction of 2.23 (first-order Markov model overestimates; longer-range correlations not captured). (2) Qwen Spearman rho=0.635 vs. predicted ~0.10 (the 99%-accuracy ceiling truncates the support, decoupling Spearman from Pearson).
- **Proxy reliability:** The ordinal pattern Mistral (rho=0.926, p=7.7e-7) > Qwen (rho=0.635, p=0.011) > DeepSeek (rho=0.495, p=0.102, n.s.) exactly matches the SNR ranking (0.330 > 0.011 ~ 0.011). Mistral's higher SNR stems from its top-20 spanning MNIST (45%), CelebA (50%) and SVHN (5%), producing ~30x higher architectural variance than the MNIST-saturated Qwen and DeepSeek pools.
- **New insight:** The framework explains the proxy-reliability ceiling effect previously reported empirically by Chen et al. -- it arises from low SNR when architectural variance is small relative to evaluation noise.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** This is genuinely the first formal convergence theory for LLM-based NAS. The identification of LLM fine-tuning with the parametric CE update is clean and non-obvious. The proxy reliability formula (Theorem 8) and the Markov token-error model for delta generation (Theorem 5) appear to have no direct precedent.
- **Rigor: Good with caveats.** Six theorems with full proofs in the appendix. The CE equivalence proof is elegant (cross-entropy loss and KL divergence differ by a constant). However, Theorem 2 relies on Assumption 2 (quality-monotone projection), which the authors honestly acknowledge is "currently unverifiable" for LoRA -- this is a significant caveat that makes the monotone improvement result conditional. Theorems 3 Part 3 and Corollary 4 (full convergence to C*=1) require Assumption 1(d) which is "generically violated for LoRA-adapted LLMs," so the practical prediction is only convergence to an unspecified C* <= 1.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Supplementary data released (cycle_data.csv with 66 rows, proxy_full_pairs.csv with 43 rows). Proxy-reliability experiments reproducible with scipy.stats. All theoretical results are self-contained with full proofs.
- **Significance: Moderate-to-high.** The theoretical framework provides important grounding for LLM-NAS, but the practical impact is limited by: (1) the framework does not predict the specific C* plateau value from first principles, (2) the delta-rate prediction overshoots by ~58% (2.23 vs 1.41), and (3) all empirical validation comes from a single prior experiment by the same group. The proxy reliability formula is the most immediately actionable result.
- **Red flags:** (1) All experimental validation comes from the authors' own prior work (Adhikari et al. 2026) -- no independent LLM-NAS experiments are tested. (2) The per-LLM sample sizes for proxy reliability are very small (N in {12, 15, 16}). (3) Two out of six predictions deviate from calibrated values, which the authors attribute to model limitations but which could also indicate misspecification. (4) The paper does not compare discovered architecture quality against any NAS baselines -- the contribution is purely theoretical.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-structured theoretical framework that makes the LLM-NAS/CE connection precise
- Six theorems with complete proofs; honest about which assumptions are restrictive (Assumption 1(d), Assumption 2)
- The proxy reliability formula (Theorem 8) is both theoretically grounded and practically useful -- the diagnostic sigma^2_arch >> sigma^2_noise is immediately actionable
- Transparent reporting of where predictions fail (delta-rate ratio, Qwen Spearman) with plausible explanations
- Explains a previously unexplained empirical phenomenon (proxy-reliability ceiling effect)
- Excellent discussion of limitations (Section 7) -- rare to see such honest self-assessment

## Limitations

- **Single data source:** All experiments come from one prior paper by the same group; no third-party validation. Replication on independent LLM-NAS runs is necessary.
- **Small proxy-reliability samples:** N in {12, 15, 16} is too small for robust statistical conclusions. The DeepSeek non-significance could simply be underpowered.
- **Conditional monotonicity:** Theorem 2 depends on Assumption 2 (quality-monotone projection), which the authors acknowledge is unverifiable for LoRA. The main practical result (monotone quality improvement) is therefore conditional, not unconditional.
- **LoRA ceiling not quantified:** The framework predicts C* <= 1 for LoRA but cannot predict C* from first principles. The observed 73-76% plateau is described as "consistent with" the theory but not predicted by it.
- **First-order Markov model is too simple:** The calibrated delta-rate prediction overshoots by 58%. The authors acknowledge longer-range token correlations are not captured, but this significantly weakens the predictive value of Theorem 5.
- **No architecture quality comparison:** The paper proves convergence properties but does not assess whether LLM-NAS discovers architectures competitive with state-of-the-art NAS methods.
- **Bivariate Normal assumption for proxy reliability:** Accuracy-ceiling truncation (many architectures at ~99% accuracy) violates this assumption, as shown by the Qwen discrepancy. Extension to truncated supports is left as future work.

本文为LLM驱动的神经架构搜索提供了首个形式化收敛理论框架，对于关注NAS理论基础、LLM作为优化器的收敛性保证、以及代理评估可靠性的研究者具有直接参考价值。其代理可靠性公式（sigma^2_arch >> sigma^2_noise诊断条件）可直接应用于任何使用代理指标的NAS系统中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can Assumption 2 (quality-monotone projection) be verified or replaced with a weaker, verifiable condition for specific LoRA configurations?
- How does the convergence rate change with LoRA rank? Is there a critical rank below which the LoRA ceiling drops significantly?
- Can the proxy reliability formula be extended to truncated accuracy supports to handle the Qwen-style ceiling effects?
- Does the framework apply to LLM-NAS settings with non-LoRA fine-tuning (e.g., full fine-tuning, QLoRA, or prompt-based methods)?
- Could the SNR diagnostic be used to adaptively determine the number of proxy training epochs, saving compute when SNR is already high?
- What happens when the architecture quality function is multi-objective (e.g., accuracy vs. FLOPs vs. latency)?
- Can the Markov token-error model be extended to capture attention-induced long-range dependencies that likely cause the 2.23 vs 1.41 discrepancy?
