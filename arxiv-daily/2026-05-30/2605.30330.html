Challenges the belief that nonlinear measurements cause failure — multimodal priors alone suffice, with direct implications for MRI and CT imaging.
Critical Edge
All experiments are one-dimensional with N ≤ 4,096; scalability to high-dimensional imaging remains unquantified and no fix is proposed.

Methodology

The authors replace the continuous diffusion prior with an empirical discrete measure over N training samples. Under the VP-SDE, the marginal, denoiser, likelihood, and posterior all become analytically tractable Gaussian mixtures at any timestep — yielding exact ground-truth proxies to diagnose approximate samplers.

Analyzed Approximations

  • σ-DPS / ζ-DPS — Dirac denoiser approximations; ζ tunes step-size but cannot eliminate hallucination.
  • PiGDM — Gaussian denoiser with inflated scalar covariance rt2I; overestimates spread for multimodal priors.
  • TMPD — Gaussian with true denoiser covariance (KL-optimal); still fails because a single Gaussian cannot capture multi-component structure.

Results & Highlights

Beyond the headline convergence rate, the analysis reveals that intermediate-time distributions can be severely wrong even when terminal samples look reasonable — a dangerous blind spot for existing benchmarks.

Method Approximation Failure Mode Root Cause
PiGDM Gaussian, scalar covariance Overestimates posterior spread; samples from prior-inconsistent modes Single Gaussian cannot capture multi-component denoiser
TMPD Gaussian, true covariance Same as PiGDM — drifts to low-probability regions Gaussian denoiser approximation structurally insufficient
σ-DPS Dirac (point mass) Misses true modes; hallucinates prior-inconsistent modes Likelihood / prior weight imbalance
ζ-DPS Dirac with tuned ζ Partially corrects weighting; cannot always avoid hallucination Hyperparameter tuning via grid search, limited generality

Assessment

Strengths

  • First rigorous framework diagnosing why posterior samplers fail at intermediate timesteps, not just terminal benchmarks.
  • Three formal propositions with complete proofs; analytical derivations for discrete, Gaussian, and GMM priors.
  • Counterintuitive finding: multimodal priors + linear A + unimodal posteriors are sufficient to trigger failure.
  • Framework is agnostic to likelihood approximation and forward model — immediately usable by practitioners.

Limitations

  • One-dimensional experiments only; cost grows dramatically in high-dimensional imaging.
  • No learned score networks tested — conjectured amplification remains unverified.
  • Purely diagnostic: no proposed correction or practical sampler.
  • ζ tuning is manual grid search; constant factors in N-1/2 rate are unquantified.
