ES fine-tuning causes recoverable performance drift, not catastrophic forgetting — controlled by Anchored Weight Decay.

Overcoming Forgetting in LLM Fine-Tuning with Evolution Strategies Schweighofer, Hayes, Dailey, Miikkulainen, Qiu et al. 2026-05-28
evolution-strategies continual-learning LLM-fine-tuning
Key Metric
−2.5% → ≈0%
Average prior-task accuracy degradation under AWD (l₂, λ=10.0) on Countdown, while target-task performance is preserved.
Why This Matters
Makes Evolution Strategies practically viable for production LLM fine-tuning, taming drift at just 1–2% runtime cost.
Critical Edge
No comparison with established continual-learning methods (EWC, replay, orthogonal subspace); scope limited to verifiable reasoning tasks.

Methodology

The authors systematically debunk the assumption that ES inherently destroys prior knowledge. Their setup spans three target tasks (Countdown, GSM8K, ProofWriter), five prior tasks (HellaSwag, PIQA, ARC-Challenge, MMLU-Pro), and four model configs (Qwen-2.5 1.5B/3B/7B, Llama-3.2 3B).

Crucially, they track accuracy trajectories across all 500 iterations — not just final checkpoints — which reveals transient dips that recover.

θt = θt−1 + α · (1/N) Σ r̂nεn − α · λ · l′(θt − θ0)

AWD adds a decay term toward initial parameters θ₀ directly inside the ES update rule. The penalty l(·) is l₁ or l₂; θ₀ is streamed layer-wise from pinned RAM, adding 1–2% overhead.

Results

ConditionPrior-task ΔNote
ES baseline −2.5% Transient; often recovers
ES + AWD (l₂) ≈ 0% λ = 10.0; target preserved
GRPO −6.5% GSM8K prior; also forgets
ES pop=128 ≈ 0% Costly; AWD@30 matches it

Weight norms with AWD remain an order of magnitude larger than GRPO, yet KL-divergence on prior tasks is comparable — implying the unconstrained randomness of drift, not magnitude alone, drives forgetting.

Strengths

  • Systematic sweep across tasks, models, and scales
  • Trajectory analysis reveals drift dynamics checkpoint-only work misses
  • Simple, practical solution with negligible overhead
  • Strong theoretical grounding via random-walk dynamics
  • Full open-source reproducibility

Limitations

  • Narrow scope: only verifiable reasoning tasks tested
  • Alignment, safety, and generative capabilities unaddressed
  • Only l₁ / l₂ penalties explored
  • Single fine-tuning episode; no sequential multi-task
  • Scalability to smaller compute setups not discussed

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can AWD preserve prior capabilities through sequential multi-task learning, not just a single fine-tuning episode?
  2. Would task-weighted penalties (e.g., Fisher information) outperform uniform AWD?
  3. Does AWD constrain ES's ability to escape local optima via random-walk exploration?
  4. How does AWD affect safety-alignment properties such as refusal of harmful requests?
  5. How does AWD scale to 70B+ models in terms of effectiveness and overhead?
