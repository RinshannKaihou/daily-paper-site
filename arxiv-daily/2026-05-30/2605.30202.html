Dual-path Transformer blocks with learned per-token gates dynamically route tokens between compute and capacity, outperforming single-axis designs at matched FLOPs with fewer parameters.

A Dual-Path Architecture for Scaling Compute and Capacity in LLMs Frey, Shomali, Koehler & Ali 2026-05-30 systems architecture compute-scaling
Headline Metric
16–53%
fewer parameters than the PureWide baseline at matched FLOPs, while achieving better aggregate BPB.
Why This Matters
Gives architects a clear, interpretable knob to trade compute for capacity under fixed FLOP budgets — compute-heavy favors reasoning, capacity-heavy favors knowledge.
Critical Edge
Gains are shown only at ~240M–1.4B parameters on 38B tokens; whether they hold at 7B+ and 1T+ tokens remains the crucial open question.

Methodology

Two parallel sublayers share the same layer input inside a GPT2-style decoder (L=16, d=768, 12 heads, RoPE, SwiGLU, RMSNorm):

  • Deep path (compute): A sublayer with hidden dimension ddeep, applied K times with shared parameters. A small per-step router learns a weighted combination of all intermediate loop states — not just the final one — allowing per-token down-weighting of later iterations.
  • Wide path (capacity): Same attention but an enlarged FFN dimension dwide > ddeep, applied once.
  • Per-token gating: A linear projection produces two independent sigmoid gates (gd, gw). The combined update is y = gd · hdeep + gw · hwide.

Trained on a 38B-token deduplicated web corpus; evaluated at two iso-FLOP budgets (80M and 160M per-layer FFN FLOPs) with controlled backbones, data, and training recipes.

Results & Highlights

Budget Config Params Aggregate BPB PureWide PureLoop
80M DualPath α=50 483M (0.67×) 0.8693 0.8753 0.8880
160M DualPath α=25 1125M (0.83×) 0.8478 0.8530 0.8622

Capacity-heavy (α=25) wins on language modeling and commonsense; compute-heavy (α=75) wins on math. Gate analysis reveals three systematic patterns: mid-layers favor wide while the last two layers flip to deep; symbolic tokens (punctuation, operators) route deep and lexical tokens route wide; GSM8K answer tokens lean deeper than TriviaQA at matched positions. Gates polarize with depth — early layers stay balanced, late layers commit.

Strengths

  • Clean, principled formulation that directly addresses looped transformers' capacity gap
  • Rigorous iso-FLOP comparison with honest baseline handling
  • Exceptional interpretability — dense gating produces directly observable routing without post-hoc tools
  • Path alignment metric confirms the two paths contribute genuinely different information
  • Comprehensive inference ablations: path disable, gate shuffle, loop override

Limitations

  • Evaluated only at 240M–1.4B params on 38B tokens — scaling to 7B+ / 1T+ is unverified
  • Single backbone (16-layer, d=768); no exploration of other depth/width ratios or GQA
  • Both paths evaluated at every token — wall-clock overhead not analyzed
  • Fixed loop depth K globally; per-layer or per-token recursion unexplored
  • No comparison with MoE or MoD at matched FLOPs
  • Loop schedule does not extrapolate past trained K, limiting test-time compute scaling

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do the gains compound or plateau at 1B+ parameters and trillion-token training?
  • Can dual-path gates be combined with MoE in the wide path for additive gains?
  • Would progressive training (increasing K during training) enable flexible test-time compute scaling?
  • Could asymmetric K per layer (larger in late, deep-dominated layers) be more efficient?
  • Would a reasoning-heavy data mixture shift the optimal α toward compute?
  • Can systematic routing patterns be distilled into a fixed schedule with minimal loss?
