CB-SLICE reframes error slice discovery through Concept Bottleneck Models by tracing errors to intermediate concept mispredictions.

Why This Matters
Extends concept bottleneck models from "interpretable reasoning" to "interpretable error analysis" with model-grounded bias discovery.
Critical Edge
Depends on CBM architecture and complete concept labels; untested beyond small synthetic datasets (~5K samples).

Methodology

1
Filter error-prone concepts. Compute ECTP per concept via KL divergence to measure how much intervening on a concept changes the downstream prediction. Keep top-k.
2
Cluster in concept-logit space. Train a differentiable GMM on erroneous samples, augmented with two auxiliary classifiers that enforce shared concept values and prediction patterns within each slice.
3
Explain slices. Rank concepts by ECSA to identify keyword-concepts that define each failure mode, with presence/absence determined by weighted slice averages.

Assessment

Strengths

  • Explanations are faithful to model internals, not post-hoc guesses from external LMs
  • Strong theoretical grounding: CBM structure guarantees errors trace to concept mispredictions
  • Comprehensive ablations quantify every design choice
  • Practical tooling: SI score prioritizes slices; loss convergence selects slice count without ground truth

Limitations

  • Requires CBM architecture — cannot apply to standard DNNs directly
  • Needs complete, accurate concept annotations; no noise-sensitivity analysis
  • Limited to small synthetic datasets (Waterbirds ~5K, MetaShift ~1.8K)
  • No runtime or scalability numbers reported

Questions for Further Exploration

How does CB-SLICE perform with noisy or incomplete concept labels?
Can discovered slices feed into automated repair (data augmentation, retraining)?
Does the method scale to large vision-language or multimodal models?
What happens when the CBM concept encoder itself has systematic bias?
Can user studies validate the SI score's practical utility for debug workflows?
