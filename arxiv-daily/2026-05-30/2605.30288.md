## 一句话总结

MIRA 提出了一种面向 LLM 中间训练阶段的数据选择框架，通过为不同数据源自适应地发现评估规则（rubric），并将语义判断蒸馏为可扩展的学生评分器，在仅使用一半 token 的情况下即可匹配甚至超越全量数据的训练效果。

## TL;DR

MIRA addresses the data selection problem for LLM mid-training by automatically discovering per-source-group evaluation rubrics and distilling them into scalable student scorers. On code-oriented mid-training with 21 sources and 5 source groups, MIRA-Group matches or slightly exceeds the full 50B-token corpus across nine code benchmarks while using only 25B tokens (half), achieving a macro average of 64.20 vs. the full corpus's 63.83.

## 核心贡献

1. **源自适应的规则发现机制**：MIRA 将"评估什么"纳入数据选择流程本身。通过自由形式（free-form）的教师模型判断和判断点聚类，为每个源组自动发现 15 个锚定维度（anchor dimensions），而非使用固定的通用规则。这使得评估标准从数据中涌现，而非由人工预设。
2. **锚定评分蒸馏**：将基于锚定维度的教师评分蒸馏为每源组一个的轻量级学生评分器（35B MoE, ~3B 活跃参数），使学生评分器能在全量语料上进行可扩展推理。采用生成式评分（输出分数+理由）而非单标量预测，保持可解释性。
3. **源条件可靠性聚合**：通过事后（post-hoc）掩码机制，在聚合阶段移除教师-学生一致性低的源-维度对（MAE>=1.0），防止不可靠维度污染最终分数。全局仅掩码约 12% 的源-维度对。
4. **源保留选择**：提出三种粒度的选择策略（Global/Group/Source），其中 Group 级别为默认方案，在保持能力覆盖的同时避免能力坍塌。

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a **source-aware filtering framework** (MIRA) that makes rubric construction an integral part of the data selection pipeline, adapting evaluation criteria to each heterogeneous data source group via self-anchored rubric discovery.
- Introduces a **rubric distillation** step that converts semantic quality judgments into scalable student scorers (one per source group), bridging the gap between semantic richness and computational scalability. Student distillation costs <4% of the per-condition mid-training compute budget.
- Develops **source-conditioned reliability aggregation** with post-hoc masking of unreliable source-dimension pairs, and **source-preserving selection** at three granularities (Global/Group/Source) to maintain capability coverage.
- Demonstrates that MIRA-Group achieves the best 25B-token macro average (64.20) across nine code benchmarks, outperforming Random (63.23), DataMan (63.01), DSIR (59.55), and PPL (54.73), while matching the unfiltered 50B-token corpus (63.83).

## Methodology

MIRA operates in four stages:

### Stage 1: Self-Anchored Rubric Discovery (Section 3.2)
- **Source Clustering**: Records from each source are embedded and clustered by mean content embedding to form capability-coherent groups (e.g., qa_math_reasoning, qa_code_general, text_code_doc, agent_toolcall, agent_swe).
- **Free-form Judging**: A frontier teacher (Kimi-K2.6) evaluates sampled records without any pre-specified rubric, freely proposing quality dimensions with scores and reasons.
- **Judgment Clustering**: Free-form responses are parsed into (dimension name, reason) pairs, embedded, and clustered via K-means (k=15). The point nearest each centroid becomes an **anchor dimension**, yielding 15 anchor dimensions per group.

### Stage 2: Anchored Judge Distillation (Section 3.3)
- **Anchored Teacher Scoring**: The frontier teacher re-scores a larger sample (~2M records total) using the fixed anchor dimensions, generating numerical scores and rationales per dimension.
- **Student Distillation**: Group-specific generative student scorers (Qwen3.5-35B-A3B-Base, a 35B MoE with ~3B active parameters) are fine-tuned to produce score+rationale for each anchor dimension. Training one student per group ensures rubric homogeneity.

### Stage 3: Source-Conditioned Reliability Aggregation (Section 3.4)
- **Residual Diagnostics**: On a held-out validation split, teacher-student MAE and Spearman correlation are computed per (source, dimension) pair.
- **Post-hoc Masking**: Unreliable pairs (MAE >= 1.0 on a 0-10 scale) are masked at aggregation time. Surviving dimensions are aggregated via trimmed mean. This avoids re-running student inference when diagnostics change.

### Stage 4: Source-Preserving Selection (Section 3.5)
Three granularity levels:
- **MIRA-Global**: Single threshold across entire scored corpus (risks capability collapse).
- **MIRA-Group** (default): Per-group thresholds preserve broad capability coverage.
- **MIRA-Source**: Per-source thresholds maximize source diversity but can be noisy for small sources.

## Results & Highlights

### Main Results (Table 1)
- **Base Model**: Qwen2.5-Coder-14B, mid-trained with ~50B tokens (Megatron-LM, 128K seq length, 256 global batch, BF16).
- **MIRA-Group** achieves macro average **64.20** with 25B tokens, vs. full corpus **63.83** with 50B tokens -- matching/exceeding the full corpus at half the budget.
- MIRA-Group leads in code generation average (54.53), with top scores on MBPP (88.90), MBPP+ (73.80), and BCB-hard (33.11).
- MIRA-Source achieves best Multipl-E multilingual average (72.84).
- Baselines at 25B tokens: Random (63.23), DataMan (63.01), DSIR (59.55), PPL (54.73).

### Scorer Analysis
- Baseline scorers (PPL, DSIR) exhibit strong length dependence; DataMan cannot score very long records due to input-length limits.
- MIRA maintains a smooth, length-robust score profile across short and long records.

### Rubric Space Visualization
- t-SNE shows QA, Text, and Agent rubrics occupy mutually distinct regions, confirming format-dependent quality criteria.
- All 14 DataMan rubric dimensions fall inside MIRA's rubric space, with 13/14 within MIRA's P95 nearest-neighbor distance -- MIRA covers generic criteria while being more source-diverse.

### Reliability Masking
- ~12% of source-dimension cells are masked (37 out of 315). Unreliability is sparse and dimension-specific.
- "Technical Precision" is the most frequently masked QA dimension; tool-call reference accuracy axes are flagged on Agent sources.

### Compute Budget
- Mid-training per condition: ~7,680 H800 GPU-hours (256 GPUs, ~30 hours).
- Student distillation: ~250 H800 GPU-hours total (<4% of mid-training cost).
- SFT per condition: ~960 H800 GPU-hours (64 GPUs, ~15 hours).

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a strong contribution to an underexplored problem: data selection specifically for the mid-training stage. The four-stage pipeline is conceptually clean, with clear separation between rubric discovery (semantic, expensive) and scalable scoring (distilled, cheap). The reliability masking mechanism is pragmatic and well-motivated.

However, several points warrant scrutiny:

1. **Generalizability beyond code**: All experiments are on code-oriented mid-training. Whether the rubric discovery and distillation approach transfers to other mid-training domains (reasoning, math, multilingual, long-context) is not demonstrated. The authors acknowledge this scope limitation.

2. **Rubric discovery sensitivity**: The K-means k=15 choice and the free-form prompting strategy introduce hyperparameters. The paper does not ablate these choices (e.g., varying k, or comparing with human-authored rubrics). How sensitive is performance to the number and quality of discovered anchors?

3. **Marginal gains over Random**: Random selection achieves 63.23 macro average, while MIRA-Group achieves 64.20 -- a gap of ~1 point. The full corpus achieves 63.83. This suggests that source diversity preservation (which Random naturally provides) is a major factor, and MIRA's quality-aware filtering adds a modest but consistent improvement. The headline "matches full corpus with half tokens" is partially attributable to the fact that 25B tokens of well-selected data suffices when the base model is already strong.

4. **Teacher model dependence**: Rubric quality depends on the frontier teacher (Kimi-K2.6). If the teacher has systematic blind spots (e.g., undervaluing certain coding styles or languages), these propagate through rubric discovery, distillation, and filtering. The broader impact section acknowledges this.

5. **End-to-end cost**: While student distillation is cheap relative to mid-training, the full pipeline requires a frontier teacher for ~2M records of anchored scoring. This cost is not fully quantified in H800-hours.

## Strengths

- Principled approach to handling heterogeneous data sources in mid-training -- the problem formulation is novel and important.
- Clean four-stage decomposition separating semantic (rubric discovery) from scalable (student distillation) components.
- Source-conditioned reliability masking is a practical innovation that avoids re-running inference.
- Strong experimental rigor: matched compute, identical SFT, comprehensive baselines, detailed appendix.
- The paper positions mid-training data selection as a distinct problem from both pretraining and post-training data selection, which is a valuable conceptual contribution.
- Rubric space visualization and case studies provide qualitative insight into what MIRA captures.

## Limitations

- Evaluation limited to code-oriented mid-training; no evidence of transfer to other domains.
- The gap over Random selection, while consistent, is modest (~1 macro-average point).
- K-means k=15 and other rubric-discovery hyperparameters are not ablated.
- Teacher model biases can propagate through the entire pipeline.
- The approach requires a capable frontier teacher model, which may not be available for all practitioners.
- Source grouping itself is a manual/design decision that affects downstream performance.
- The paper does not study how MIRA interacts with mixture-ratio optimization or curriculum scheduling.

## 相关性 (Relevance)

这篇工作对关注 LLM 训练数据工程、特别是中间训练阶段数据选择的研究者具有高相关性。对于从事代码生成模型训练、大规模语料筛选、以及自动化数据质量控制的研究者也有直接参考价值。其源自适应的规则发现思路可推广至其他需要处理异构数据源的训练场景。IQuest 团队此前的工作（IQuest-Coder-v1, InCoder-32B）与该论文形成了紧密的工程实践链条，说明该方法已在工业级模型训练中得到验证。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. MIRA 在非代码领域（数学推理、多语言、长上下文理解）中的泛化能力如何？规则发现机制是否需要针对不同领域进行调整？
2. 锚定维度数量 k=15 的选择有多敏感？是否存在一个最优的 k 值，或者是否可以自适应确定？
3. 如果用人工撰写的规则（如 DataMan 的 14 个通用维度）替代自由发现的规则，性能差距如何？自由发现带来的增益有多少？
4. 在更小的模型规模（如 1B-3B）或更大的模型规模（如 70B+）下，MIRA 的 token 节省比例是否仍然稳定？
5. 可靠性掩码策略（MAE>=1.0 阈值）的敏感性如何？是否可以在线更新掩码而非事后静态应用？
6. MIRA 与混合比例优化（mixture-ratio optimization）和课程调度（curriculum scheduling）结合时，是否存在进一步的协同效应？
