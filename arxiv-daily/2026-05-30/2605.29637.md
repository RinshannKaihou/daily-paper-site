## 一句话总结

本文提出IndicKLAR基准和Translate-in-Thought (TinT)提示策略，系统性揭示了LLM在印度语言上的跨语言知识一致性缺陷，发现code-mixed输入可将性能差距从0.50缩小至0.05。

## TL;DR

The authors introduce IndicKLAR, the first large-scale benchmark for cross-lingual knowledge consistency covering 18 Indian languages and 11 code-mixed variants, and propose Translate-in-Thought (TinT), a prompting strategy where the model internally converts input to English or code-mixed form and emits only the final answer. Across 9 open-weight models, native-language accuracy gaps to English reach ~0.50, while code-mixed inputs close most of it (~0.05 gap). TinT achieves statistically significant gains over baselines with minimal inference overhead.

## 核心贡献

1. **IndicKLAR基准数据集**: 扩展KLAR-CLC基准，覆盖22种印度官方语言中的18种，并为11个常用语言对构建了code-mixed变体，所有变体均经过母语者验证。这是首个在此规模上将单语印度语言事实查询与code-mixed对应项配对的基准。
2. **Translate-in-Thought (TinT) 提示策略**: 一种单步提示方法，模型在内部将输入转换为英文(TinT-EN)或code-mixed(TinT-CM)形式，但只输出最终答案，不产生中间文本。相比多步方法，推理时间更低（0.09s/sample vs 0.16-0.17s/sample）。
3. **Flip Point发现**: 在native -> code-mixed -> English的性能轨迹中，识别出一致的"翻转点"——错误预测变为正确预测的边界，位于native和code-mixed之间。无论通过输入表面形式还是模型内部转换诱导，该现象均成立。

## Key Contributions

1. **IndicKLAR Benchmark**: An Indic extension of KLAR-CLC covering 18 of 22 scheduled Indian languages with 11 code-mixed variants, all verified by native speakers, enabling three-way alignment (English / code-mixed / native) evaluation.
2. **Translate-in-Thought (TinT)**: A novel single-step prompting strategy where the model implicitly converts the input internally and emits only the final answer, combining strong performance with low inference cost.
3. **Comprehensive evaluation across 9 models**: Systematic analysis spanning Llama 3.1/3.2, Gemma 3, and Qwen 2.5 families (1B-14B parameters), including model-size scaling, language-family effects, and pairwise CLC analysis.
4. **Flip Point identification**: Discovery of a consistent boundary between incorrect and correct predictions along the native-to-English spectrum, holding for both input-driven and model-driven trajectories.

## Methodology

### Benchmark Construction (IndicKLAR)
- Extends KLAR-CLC (structured knowledge-base triple-based factual queries) to 18 Indian languages
- Code-mixed variants constructed by interleaving English content words into the grammatical structure of each Indian language, reflecting real-world multilingual communication
- 11 language pairs with code-mixed variants verified by native speakers (Assamese, Bengali, Gujarati, Hindi, Malayalam, Marathi, Odia, Punjabi, Sanskrit, Tamil, Urdu)
- Three-way alignment: English, native script, code-mixed for the same factual query

### Prompting Strategies Evaluated
- **Baseline**: Direct query in native script
- **2-Step strategies**: 2Step-CM (translate to code-mixed, then answer), 2Step-EN (translate to English, then answer), 2Step-Translit (transliterate to Roman script, then answer)
- **1-Step strategies**: 1Step-CM+Ans (generate code-mixed conversion alongside answer), 1Step-EN+Ans (generate English translation alongside answer)
- **TinT**: Single user-prompt directive; model internally reasons through English (TinT-EN) or code-mixed (TinT-CM) representation; emits only the final answer

### Experimental Setup
- Models: Llama 3.1/3.2 (1B, 3B, 8B), Gemma 3 (1B, 4B, 12B), Qwen 2.5 (1.5B, 7B, 14B)
- Metrics: Accuracy (relaxed prefix-matching) and Cross-Lingual Consistency (CLC, pairwise prediction overlap)
- Greedy decoding (temperature=0, top-p=1.0, max_new_tokens=10), 3-shot in-context examples from English source
- Single NVIDIA A100 GPU (80GB), fixed seed (12345)

## Results & Highlights

- **English-Native Gap**: Native-language accuracy can lag English by up to ~0.50, with smaller models scoring as low as 0.10 on Assamese and Telugu
- **Code-mixed closes the gap**: Code-mixed inputs bring performance within ~0.05 of English across all languages and model scales, without any model-level intervention
- **TinT effectiveness**: TinT achieves statistically significant improvements over baseline (chi-squared = 534.75 for TinT-CM, 603.56 for TinT-EN, both p < 10^{-10})
- **Model-size scaling**: TinT accuracy scales monotonically from 0.26 (1B) to 0.71 (8B) for Llama and 0.39 to 0.85 for Gemma; gains widen with model size
- **Language-family effects**: TinT achieves stronger gains for Indo-Aryan languages (+18% accuracy, +14% CLC) than Dravidian languages (+14% accuracy, +13% CLC)
- **CLC convergence**: TinT-CM converges pairwise CLC scores from a wide range (0.4-0.8) to a higher range (0.5-0.9)
- **Inference efficiency**: TinT runs at 0.09s/sample (Llama 3.1 8B), well below multi-step counterparts (0.16-0.17s/sample)
- **Outlier**: Odia consistently shows the lowest CLC scores (0.38-0.60), likely due to limited pretraining data and script complexity
- **Generalization**: TinT improvements generalize to contextually mediated knowledge recall (Liu et al., 2026 benchmark) across 5 representative languages

## Critical Assessment

### Strengths

- **Scale and coverage**: 18 Indian languages with 11 code-mixed variants is an unprecedented scale for cross-lingual consistency evaluation in this language family
- **Practical insight**: The finding that code-mixed inputs nearly match English performance without any model intervention is highly actionable for real-world deployment in multilingual settings
- **Efficient solution**: TinT provides a lightweight, no-retraining approach that achieves competitive performance with minimal inference overhead
- **Rigorous evaluation**: Statistical significance tests (McNemar), multiple model families, scaling analysis, and generalization to a second benchmark
- **Flip point concept**: The identification of a consistent flip point between native and code-mixed settings is a novel and insightful finding about how multilingual knowledge is organized in LLMs

### Limitations

- **Closed-model gap**: The study focuses exclusively on open-weight models (1B-14B); it is unclear whether findings transfer to larger proprietary models (GPT-4, Gemini Ultra, etc.)
- **Code-mixed construction**: Code-mixed variants are synthetically constructed by interleaving English content words, which may not fully capture the organic variability of real-world code-mixing
- **Factoid-only evaluation**: The benchmark evaluates factual knowledge recall (knowledge-base triples), leaving open whether findings extend to reasoning, summarization, or open-ended generation tasks
- **Limited Dravidian code-mixed data**: Only 7 of 18 languages have code-mixed variants, with most Dravidian languages excluded due to linguist availability
- **Single-seed experiments**: Although using a fixed seed ensures reproducibility, the lack of multi-seed variance reporting is a minor concern
- **Odia outlier unresolved**: The paper identifies Odia as an outlier but does not propose targeted solutions for severely under-resourced languages

## Relevance (Chinese)

本研究对多语言NLP和低资源语言AI部署具有直接意义。对于关注印度语言生态系统中LLM公平性的研究者，IndicKLAR提供了一个急需的评估工具。TinT提示策略的简洁性和高效性使其在实际系统中易于部署。code-mixed输入接近英语性能的发现为不需要重训练即可改善低资源语言性能提供了实用路径。Flip Point的概念性发现对理解LLM内部多语言知识表示有理论价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. TinT的有效性在更大的模型（70B+, 405B）上如何变化？翻转点是否会进一步向native端移动？
2. Code-mixed输入的成功是否可以解释为tokenization层面的优势？原生脚本的tokenization效率可能是性能下降的关键因素。
3. TinT是否可以通过微调（而非仅提示）来进一步强化？模型是否能学会更优的内部转换路径？
4. 该发现是否适用于非印度语系的语言对（如中英文code-mixing、阿拉伯-法语code-mixing）？
5. 对于Odia等极端低资源语言，除了数据和脚本改进外，是否有架构层面的解决方案？
