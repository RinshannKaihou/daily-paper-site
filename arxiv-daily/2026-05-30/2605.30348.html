Why This Matters
Shifts LLM data auditing from instance-level guesswork to corpus-level mixture estimation using only generated text — no model weights needed.
Critical Edge
It cannot discover domains outside the predefined taxonomy — a verification tool, not a discovery tool.

Methodology

LLMSurgeon assumes label-shift: domain-specific language patterns remain invariant between training data and model output, while only the domain proportions change.

  1. Calibrate bias. Train a proxy classifier (DistilBERT) on a held-out reference dataset with known domain labels. Compute a soft confusion matrix C where each entry Cij is the expected probability of predicting domain j given a sample from true domain i.
  2. Observe target. Sample neutral-prompt generations from the target LLM and classify them to obtain the biased mean prediction vector p̄.
  3. Inverse surgery. Recover the true mixture prior π by solving a constrained quadratic program: minimize ||CTπ − p̄||2 subject to simplex constraints (sum to 1, non-negative).

Neutral prompts minimize stylistic distortion; semantically overlapping domains are merged to keep the inversion well-conditioned.

  • All 11 MIA-based baselines cluster below 50%; the best neighbor achieves ~41%.

  • Held-out protocol transfers to OLMo-3 at 86.41% without retuning.
  • Toxic injection detected monotonically at >97% overall accuracy.

Strengths

  • Clean formalization as a constrained linear inverse problem.
  • LLMScan: first recipe-verifiable benchmark across 8 models and 3 granularities.
  • Thorough ablations and transparent per-class error reporting.
  • Requires no model weights, training data, or internals — only generated text.

Limitations

  • Closed-world: cannot detect domains outside the predefined K categories.
  • Untested on aligned models: RLHF and instruction tuning likely violate the label-shift assumption.
  • Granularity collapse: separating C4 from CommonCrawl drops accuracy from 99% to 42%.
  • Amber-13B outlier: 78.87% on coarse-grained recovery remains unexplained.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does performance degrade on RLHF-aligned models where label-shift may fail?
  • Can hierarchical coarse→fine inference improve fine-grained (K=87) accuracy?
  • What is the minimum generated text required for reliable estimation at each granularity?
  • Could optimal transport outperform linear inversion for semantically overlapping domains?
  • Can the closed-world assumption be relaxed to discover novel, unseen training domains?
