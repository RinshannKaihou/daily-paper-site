Models can diagnose their own errors when shown a reference solution — STV turns this asymmetry into a trainable verifier that doubles performance on the hardest problems.
Self-Trained Verification for Training- and Test-Time Self-Improvement
Chen Henry Wu, Aditi Raghunathan · Stanford · 2026-05-28 ·
Self-Improvement Verification RL Test-Time Compute
Key Metric
1.5% → 21.0%
Hardest scientific-reasoning pass@1 on SciKnowEval. An 8B STV model surpasses even Qwen3-235B-A22B.
Why This Matters
Eliminates the need for human-annotated verifiers and breaks through RL convergence plateaus — enabling truly self-improving reasoning systems without external supervision.
Critical Edge
All experiments use a single model family (Qwen3-8B). No evidence the approach transfers to other architectures or scales to larger models.

Methodology

STV Training. The verifier is trained to mimic a reference-conditioned version of itself — the same model, but prompted with the ground-truth answer. The loss combines on-policy distillation (Jensen-Shannon, α=0.5) with a verdict-RL term that optimizes correctness classification.

Why SFT fails. Standard fine-tuning trains on teacher sequences but at test time the student generates its own prefixes — causing off-policy drift. OPD avoids this by sampling from the student's own distribution.

ViL Training. After RLVR converges, the generator continues training inside a multi-turn Verify-Refine loop with a frozen STV verifier providing feedback. Only the generator is updated; the reward is final-answer correctness.

Strengths

  • Elegant, well-motivated core idea with clear theoretical grounding (on-policy vs. off-policy)
  • Comprehensive evaluation: multiple baselines, ablations, calibration & reward-hacking analysis
  • Dual contribution: test-time scaling via better verification and training-time scaling via ViL
  • Weak-to-strong verification: 4B verifier matches 8B, enabling real inference-cost savings
  • Precision-coverage analysis shows 3–5× higher precision at matched coverage

Limitations

  • Single model family (Qwen3); no transfer evidence for Llama, Gemma, or proprietary models
  • Only math (DAPO) and scientific reasoning (SciKnowEval) tested; code and open-ended reasoning unexplored
  • Computational cost of STV training and ViL is not quantified against alternatives
  • Test sets are small (~150 problems per bin), increasing variance in reported numbers
  • No investigation of iterative STV despite the paper's framing of iterative self-improvement

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the STV asymmetry persist at frontier scale (70B+), or do larger models already catch their own errors?
  2. Can iterative STV (retrain verifier with improved generator, then retrain generator) produce compounding gains?
  3. What is the compute-optimal allocation between generator RL, verifier STV, and test-time V-R rounds?
  4. Do ViL's standalone pass@1 gains transfer to out-of-distribution tasks like code and commonsense reasoning?
  5. How sensitive is STV to the quality and diversity of reference solutions?
