Key Finding

Joint ARD simultaneously prunes features and downweights outliers via a single marginal likelihood objective, preserving closed-form updates.

Joint Model and Data Sparsification via the Marginal Likelihood Timans, Mollenhoff, Naesseth, Khan & Nalisnick ICML 2026 · 2605.29908
sparse-bayesian-learning robust-regression automatic-relevance-determination
Key Metric
32%
RMSE reduction on the Energy dataset vs. homoscedastic Ridge (2.79 → 1.89 under 10% outlier contamination).
Why This Matters

Bridges Bayesian model selection with robust statistics, giving practitioners a principled way to clean data while pruning models.

Critical Edge

Restricted to linear-in-weights models; the neural network experiment only learns the output layer, leaving deep extensions unresolved.

Methodology

The authors augment the standard linear model y = Xθ + ε with per-sample noise variances εi ~ N(0, λi) and per-weight ARD precisions θj ~ N(0, γj−1). The objective is to jointly maximize the marginal likelihood p(y | γ, λ).

  • Closed-form duality: Despite the heteroscedastic extension, EM, MacKay fixed-point, and l2-IRLS all yield the same noise update: λi = ri2 + xiT Σθ xi — squared residual plus model uncertainty.
  • Influence-function link: The update connects to classical diagnostics via leverage hi, making λi an approximate leverage-aware residual that correlates strongly with leave-one-out errors.
  • Robust GP view: When instantiated as an RVM with kernel basis Φ, the model induces a finite-rank GP prior with heteroscedastic noise, achieving sparsity and robustness while preserving conjugacy.

Results

Tabular regression with 10% label contamination:

Dataset Ridge RMSE Joint EM Gain
Energy 2.79 1.89 −32%
Carbon reported −18%
Protein reported −18 to −32%
  • Synthetic: Strong recovery of true weights and outliers; degrades only when feature signal is weak.
  • Kernel (RVM): Consistently low RMSE and NLL across 0–40% contamination, outperforming standard RVM and rivaling Student-t / Huber baselines.
  • Neural (DINO-2 features): Learned λi reliably flags unreliable crowd-count labels; sequential removal by λ tracks oracle performance.

Strengths

  • Conceptual clarity: A single marginal-likelihood objective symmetrically treats model and data sparsification.
  • Theoretical depth: Influence functions, LOO diagnostics, and robust GP connections provide multiple interpretive lenses.
  • Tractability preserved: Closed-form updates across three major SBL frameworks — a non-trivial result.
  • Broad evaluation: Synthetic, tabular, kernel, and neural settings are all tested.
  • Actionable diagnostics: Per-sample λi doubles as a data-quality indicator.

Limitations

  • Linear-only: No compelling path to end-to-end deep learning; the NN experiment is essentially a linear output layer.
  • Small scale: No experiments beyond n ~ 1000 or d ~ 500; scalability unaddressed.
  • Artificial contamination: All outliers are synthetically injected label noise; real-world corruption patterns remain untested.
  • Additive correction: λi uses additive leverage, whereas LOO uses multiplicative (1 − hi)−2 — high-leverage outliers may be masked.
  • Non-convex landscape: Different procedures (EM vs. gradient) can converge to different fixed points.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can joint ARD be extended to end-to-end nonlinear models without sacrificing closed-form updates?
  2. What sparse approximations or stochastic methods can scale the approach beyond n = 10K?
  3. How robust is data ARD under non-Gaussian noise or distribution shift rather than simple label contamination?
  4. Can learned λi drive adaptive sampling or active-learning acquisition?
  5. Would a multiplicative leverage correction (e.g., studentized updates) substantially improve high-leverage outlier detection?
