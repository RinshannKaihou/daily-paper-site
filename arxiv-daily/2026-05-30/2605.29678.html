73.24%
Average accuracy on Qwen3.5-27B, beating the task-aware PromptWizard optimizer (67.99%).
Why This Matters
Prompt effectiveness may not come from semantic content at all—challenging the core assumption of prompt engineering and exposing a stealth attack vector.
Critical Edge
The paper shows what and how much, but not why—the internal pathway through which spurious prompts steer output remains completely black-box.

Methodology

An evolutionary black-box search discovers prompts stripped of all task vocabulary, domain cues, and reasoning strategies:

  • Generator (Qwen3.5-27B) crafts candidates using only tone, style, ritual, or role—no task words.
  • Validator filters with a task-specific denylist (e.g., "arithmetic", "equation" banned for math).
  • Evaluator scores on accuracy; top-K candidates are mutated by an LLM variator for 3 rounds.
  • Selection picks the best on a held-out validation set; results reported on an independent test set.

Models: Qwen3.5-0.8B, Llama-3.2-1B, OLMo-3-7B, Qwen3.5-27B. Benchmarks: GSM8K, MATH500, MedQA, GPQA, OpenBookQA, MuSR, MMLU-Pro.

Results & Highlights

FindingDetail
Performance Spurious prompts match or exceed Zero-Shot CoT (59.79%) and PromptWizard (67.99%) on Qwen3.5-27B.
Option A bias Induced 99.73% Option-A selection on Qwen3.5-27B vs. 92.20% with direct instruction.
Wrong-answer steering 79.33% success on OLMo-3-7B—steering to incorrect answers without ever asking for them.
Gibberish ablation Prompts with 65–90% nonsense tokens still match coherent spurious prompts in accuracy gains.
Length Spurious prompts average ~1/3 the length of PromptWizard—ruling out verbosity as the driver.
Transfer Poor cross-model and cross-benchmark transfer; effects are highly pair-specific.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Novel problem formulation: first systematic study of task-irrelevant prompt steering.
  • Comprehensive evaluation across 7 benchmarks, 4 model families, and multiple scales (0.8B–27B).
  • Transparent methodology: fully black-box, reproducible pipeline with open-sourced prompts.
  • Rich ablations: semantic distance, length, gibberish tokens, transfer, and component analysis.
  • Practical significance: reveals a concealed attack surface for LLM robustness testing.

Limitations

  • No mechanistic explanation; internal steering pathway is entirely uncharacterized.
  • Maximum model size is 27B; unvalidated on 70B+ or frontier-scale models.
  • Requires labeled data for the accuracy-based scoring function.
  • Poor transferability weakens claims of universal applicability.
  • Evolutionary search is computationally expensive despite being framed as "simple."
  • Semantic-unrelatedness proof relies on embedding cosine similarity—may miss subtle associations.
