> **一句话总结：** 本文提出情境信念管理（CBM）框架，构建BeliefTrack基准（规则发现+电路诊断），通过符号验证器精确评估LLM在多轮交互中的三种信念失败模式，并使用基于Jaccard奖励的GRPO强化学习将失败率平均降低70.9%，表征层操控可进一步降低高达92.3%的失败率。

> **TL;DR:** The paper formalizes Contextual Belief Management (CBM) as the problem of maintaining an evidence-aligned belief state during multi-turn LLM interactions. They introduce BeliefTrack, a closed-world benchmark with two tasks (Rule Discovery, Circuit Diagnosis) and symbolic verifiers for exact turn-level evaluation. Three failure modes are diagnosed: Failed Stay (unnecessarily changing a stable belief), Failed Update (failing to revise upon new evidence), and Failed Isolation (succumbing to task-irrelevant noise). Vanilla LLMs fail badly (97-99% for Qwen2.5-7B; 43-95% for Qwen3.5-9B), prompting barely helps, but GRPO with Jaccard belief-state rewards cuts failure rates by 70.9% on average and transfers across tasks and noise types. Representation-level steering (adding RL-derived directions to vanilla hidden states) further reduces failures by up to 92.3% on individual metrics without any parameter updates.

## 核心贡献

- 首次将LLM在长交互中的信念管理问题形式化为**情境信念管理（CBM）**框架，在每个轮次t定义Oracle信念状态和预测信念状态，明确区分信念校准失败（Type I: Failed Stay/Failed Update）和信念隔离失败（Type II: Failed Isolation）。
- 提出**BeliefTrack**基准，包含两个封闭世界任务：**规则发现（Rule Discovery）**（基于Wason 2-4-6范式）和**电路诊断（Circuit Diagnosis）**，均具有有限信念空间和符号验证器，可实现完全自动化的精确轮次级评估。
- 系统诊断三种信念失败模式，并发现前沿模型（GPT-5.2、DeepSeek-V3.2、Qwen3.5-Plus）均存在严重的CBM失败。
- 设计基于GRPO的强化学习方法，使用**密集Jaccard信念状态奖励**，在两个基座模型（Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct和Qwen3.5-9B）上平均降低70.9%失败率，且跨任务泛化良好。
- 通过探针实验发现LLM内部的**隐式信念状态动态**（漂移、回溯失败、上下文劫持、潜在-输出差距），并通过**表征层操控**在无需参数更新的情况下降低失败率。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes **Contextual Belief Management (CBM)** with a precise mathematical framework: at each turn t, the model maintains a predicted belief state (subset of candidate hypotheses) that should align with the oracle belief state determined by formal evidence history.
- Introduces **BeliefTrack**, a closed-world benchmark with two tasks — **Rule Discovery** (Wason 2-4-6 paradigm) and **Circuit Diagnosis** (fault localization from instrument readings) — both with finite belief spaces and symbolic verifiers for exact, annotation-free evaluation.
- Diagnoses three failure modes: **Failed Stay** (drift from stable oracle state), **Failed Update** (failure to revise upon corrected evidence), **Failed Isolation** (succumbing to task-irrelevant noise like sycophancy, authority, or stress cues).
- Shows that frontier models (GPT-5.2, DeepSeek-V3.2, Qwen3.5-Plus) all exhibit substantial CBM failures in a pilot study.
- Demonstrates **GRPO with Jaccard belief-state rewards** reduces failure rates by 70.9% on average, with strong cross-task generalization (e.g., RD-trained model reduces CD FSR by 93.9%) and robustness to unseen noise types.
- Conducts **mechanistic analysis** revealing latent failure patterns (belief-state drift, backtracking failure, contextual hijacking, latent-output gap) and shows representation-level steering reduces individual failure metrics by up to 92.3% without parameter updates.

## Methodology

**Problem Formulation.** CBM is formalized as maintaining a predicted belief state over a finite belief space at each turn. The model receives observations o_t = (e_t, n_t) where e_t is formal evidence and n_t is optional noise. The oracle belief state S_t* is determined by evidence history e_{1:t}; the predicted state is produced by the model. Three failure modes are defined: Failed Stay (S_t* unchanged but model errs), Failed Update (S_t* changes but model misses it), and Failed Isolation (noise causes model to deviate from correct clean prediction).

**BeliefTrack Benchmark.** Two closed-world environments: (1) Rule Discovery — candidate hypotheses are rules (e.g., ascending_order), evidence is triple-label pairs, oracle state is rules consistent with accumulated evidence; (2) Circuit Diagnosis — candidate hypotheses are circuit faults, evidence is instrument readings, oracle state is faults consistent with readings. Both use symbolic verifiers for exact evaluation. Three diagnostic datasets are generated: D_stay (redundant evidence after lock point), D_update (corrected evidence), D_iso (clean vs. noised trajectory pairs with sycophancy/authority/stress noise).

**BT-Prompt.** A parameter-free baseline that instructs the model in the system prompt to maintain current evidence, ignore noise, re-evaluate hypotheses against accumulated evidence, and revise when corrections invalidate earlier observations.

**RL Training.** Uses GRPO with a dense Jaccard reward R_i = |S_cap| / |S_cup| between predicted and oracle belief states. Training uses only D_stay and D_update (D_iso is excluded), so FIR improvements measure transfer. Each GRPO prompt targets a single evaluated turn with full observation history. Two variants trained: RL-RD (trained on Rule Discovery only) and RL-CD (trained on Circuit Diagnosis only), enabling cross-task generalization evaluation.

**Mechanistic Analysis.** (1) Prompt-based probing tracks the rank of oracle-supported hypotheses across turns, revealing drift, backtracking failure, and contextual hijacking patterns. (2) Representation-level steering computes the average hidden-state difference between RL and vanilla models on vanilla-wrong/RL-right cases, then adds this direction to vanilla inference at the same layer, testing whether RL induces a transferable representation direction for CBM.

**Models Evaluated.** Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, Qwen3.5-9B (open-source, RL-trained); GPT-5.2, DeepSeek-V3.2, Qwen3.5-Plus (proprietary, pilot only). RL training uses LoRA (rank 16) with Swift framework on 5 A800 GPUs.

## Results & Highlights

**Main Diagnostic Results (Table 1):**
- Qwen2.5-7B Vanilla: FSR/FUR/FIR at 97-99% across both tasks — near-complete CBM failure.
- Qwen3.5-9B Vanilla: 43-62% FSR/FUR, up to 95.4% FIR in CD — strong but still unreliable.
- BT-Prompt: Inconsistent; sometimes helps (20.2% FSR reduction on Qwen2.5-7B RD) but often hurts (increases Qwen3.5-9B FUR by 15%).
- RL-RD on Qwen2.5-7B: In-domain FSR/FUR drops to 0.0%/2.0%; cross-task CD FSR/FUR drops by 93.9%/71.1%.
- RL-CD on Qwen2.5-7B: In-domain CD FSR/FUR drops to 0.0%/0.0%; cross-task RD FSR drops by 57.6%.
- RL-RD on Qwen3.5-9B: In-domain FSR/FUR at 6.0%/8.0%; out-of-domain CD FIR reduced by 63.4%.
- General capabilities (GSM8K, MMLU) remain stable after RL training.

**Reward Ablation (Table 4):** Dense Jaccard reward consistently outperforms sparse exact-match reward. For Qwen2.5-7B, average failure rate: 98.0% (vanilla) -> 24.9% (exact match) -> 15.2% (Jaccard). Jaccard improves 5/6 metrics for Qwen2.5-7B and all 6 for Qwen3.5-9B.

**Temporal Robustness:** Vanilla models degrade with increasing redundant depth and correction delay. RL maintains stable performance, especially in-domain. Out-of-domain RL degrades more gradually but stays stronger than Vanilla/BT-Prompt.

**Contextual Robustness:** Authority noise causes the largest FIR increase, followed by sycophancy, then stress. RL substantially improves isolation across all noise types despite never seeing noise during training.

**Representation Steering:** On RD, steering reduces FSR by 78.6%, FUR by 92.3%, FIR by 48.8%. On CD (transferred from RD), reductions are 20.7%/23.5%/12.8%. This demonstrates RL induces modifiable representation-level patterns for CBM.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: High.** The CBM formalization is genuinely novel — it cleanly separates belief management into three distinct, measurable operations (stay, update, isolate) with a formal mathematical framework. The distinction from Theory of Mind (first-person vs. third-person belief tracking) is well-argued. BeliefTrack's use of symbolic verifiers for exact turn-level evaluation is methodologically clean and avoids the LLM-as-judge pitfall.

**Rigor: Strong.** The experimental design is thorough: oracle-level train/test splits prevent memorization; k-repeat evaluation (k=3) with strict failure criteria; cross-task generalization tested in both directions; noise types excluded from training to test transfer; reward ablation with controlled conditions. The mechanistic analysis adds depth beyond standard benchmarking.

**Reproducibility: Moderate.** Code is promised but not yet available. Training details are well-specified (LoRA rank 16, 5 A800 GPUs, specific step counts). Proprietary model evaluation (GPT-5.2, DeepSeek-V3.2) is only in the pilot study, which is acceptable. The synthetic environments can likely be reconstructed from the descriptions.

**Significance: Moderate-to-High.** The problem is important for real-world LLM deployment (agents, long conversations). The finding that prompting alone is insufficient while RL with symbolic rewards works well has practical implications. The representation-level steering results suggest a path toward lightweight inference-time interventions.

**Red flags / Concerns:**
- The paper evaluates only two synthetic, closed-world tasks. Real-world belief spaces are open-ended, and the relationship between performance on BeliefTrack and real-world multi-turn reliability is untested.
- Absolute failure rates for the strongest RL models (Qwen3.5-9B RL-RD: 6-18.6% in-domain) are still non-trivial. The 70.9% average reduction sounds impressive but is inflated by the near-random Qwen2.5-7B baseline.
- BT-Prompt is a relatively weak baseline — it would be valuable to compare against stronger prompt-engineering approaches (e.g., chain-of-thought with explicit scratchpads, or tool-augmented approaches).
- The pilot study on frontier models (GPT-5.2, DeepSeek-V3.2) uses only 135 Rule Discovery examples — too small for strong conclusions.
- The "Work in progress" label means results may change.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally clean problem formalization with precise mathematical notation and well-defined failure modes.
- Symbolic verification eliminates evaluation ambiguity — a significant methodological contribution.
- Comprehensive experimental design: in-domain, cross-task, temporal robustness, contextual robustness, mechanistic analysis, reward ablation.
- The finding that RL on clean trajectories transfers to noise robustness is practically valuable.
- The latent-output gap discovery (model ranks correctly but outputs incorrectly) is an interesting mechanistic insight.
- Honest about limitations in the paper's own "Limitations" section.

## Limitations

- Only two synthetic, closed-world tasks with finite belief spaces — real-world applicability is untested.
- No comparison with stronger baselines like explicit scratchpad reasoning, tool use, or other RL methods beyond GRPO.
- The pilot study on frontier models is small (135 examples) and lacks the full diagnostic suite.
- Code not yet available, reducing immediate reproducibility.
- The paper does not address how CBM performance relates to downstream task quality in real applications.
- Steering results on CD (transferred from RD) are modest (12.8-23.5% reduction) compared to strong in-domain RD results (48.8-92.3%).

## Relevance

本文研究LLM在多轮交互中如何动态维护和更新信念状态，与agent系统、长对话建模和知识更新研究高度相关。CBM框架将信念管理分解为保留、更新、隔离三个操作，通过符号验证器实现精确评估，这一方法论对构建可靠的交互式AI系统具有直接参考价值。表征层操控的发现也为理解LLM内部信念表示提供了新的视角。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does CBM performance scale with belief-space size and conversation depth beyond the tested ranges?
- Can the RL-trained belief management capabilities transfer to open-ended domains (e.g., scientific reasoning, legal analysis) where belief spaces are not finite?
- What is the relationship between CBM failure modes and other known LLM failure modes like hallucination, sycophancy, or instruction-following degradation?
- Could the representation-level steering approach be combined with activation engineering techniques (e.g., contrastive activation addition) for more robust interventions?
- How does the Jaccard reward compare to other dense reward designs (e.g., F1, soft set overlap) for belief-state optimization?
- Would training on both environments jointly yield better results than single-environment training?
- How do the CBM failure patterns change with different model architectures (e.g., mixture-of-experts, reasoning models with extended thinking)?
