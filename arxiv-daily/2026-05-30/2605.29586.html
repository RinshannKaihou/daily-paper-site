Calibration, not detection, is the central challenge: 9 of 14 frontier LLMs flag 95–100% of clean financial statements as erroneous.
FinVerBench: Benchmark Validity and Calibration in LLM Financial Statement Verification Silu Panda 2026-05-28
llm-evaluation financial-verification benchmark calibration auditing
Key Metric
95–100%
False positive rate on clean statements for 9 of 14 frontier LLMs under guided-checklist prompting. Claude Sonnet 4 alone hits 0%.
Why This Matters
Deployed for financial audit, most frontier LLMs would drown analysts in false alarms — calibration is the real bottleneck, not arithmetic.
Critical Edge
The 0% false-positive claim rests on only 43 clean instances — the Wilson 95% upper bound is still 8.2%.

Methodology

Built from SEC 10-K XBRL filings of 43 S&P 500 companies (>$17T total assets, 7 sectors). Extracted balance sheet, income statement, and cash-flow items — ~37 line items per company.

Error Injection

  • 1,985 instances; exactly one error per instance
  • 6 magnitude levels: 0.5%, 1%, 2%, 5%, 10%, 20%
  • 4 categories, 11 instantiated sub-types (1 excluded)
  • Direction randomized; sign based on absolute value
  • Unrounded perturbations stored (produces format anomalies like $534.66M)

Evaluation

14 frontier LLMs tested with unified guided-checklist CoT prompt. Deterministic rule baseline: 15 accounting relationships, 100% precision, 52.8% recall.

Beyond the Headline

  • Calibration > detection: 12/14 models hit ≥87% recall on observable unrounded errors — the gap is entirely on clean-instance specificity.
  • Cognitive, not arithmetic, failure: 95.1% of GPT-4.1 false positives involve correct math but misclassifying simplified statements as complete (e.g. missing sub-line items).
  • Rendering matters: Rounding drops Claude Sonnet 4 recall from 100% to 79% (–21pp). Unrounded format anomalies artificially inflate detection scores.
  • Same family, opposite calibration: Claude Sonnet 4 at 0% FPR; Opus 4.6 and Sonnet 4.6 at 100% FPR. The paper cannot explain this.
  • 47% verification gap: Rule validators catch only 52.8% of injected errors. Extending to 30 relations reaches 100% recall but 100% FPR.

Strengths

  • Rigorous controlled injection with observability labels and rounding ablation
  • Honest construct-validity analysis: authors quantify their own 21pp recall inflation
  • Deep false-positive mechanism analysis (41 GPT-4.1 cases manually classified)
  • Broad model coverage: 14 systems across 5 providers, open and closed, multiple access paths
  • First benchmark to isolate verification from QA in financial NLP

Limitations

  • Low statistical power: 7–24 instances per error category; 43 clean instances (0% FPR Wilson UB = 8.2%)
  • Single prompt condition; "completeness-aware" prompt designed but not run across all models
  • Low ecological validity: formatted text, not real PDFs; single-error injection; no footnotes or tables
  • Claude Sonnet 4 result is unreproducible and unexplained — same-provider newer models fail
  • Heterogeneous access paths (API, CLI, OpenRouter) confound temperature, thinking, formatting
  • Contamination risk: models may have seen SEC filings in pre-training

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Why does Claude Sonnet 4 calibrate perfectly while Opus 4.6 and Sonnet 4.6 fail completely — training data, alignment, thinking defaults, or access path?
  2. Can a "completeness-aware" prompt (not assuming visible items exhaust sub-totals) systematically cut false positives?
  3. Would running all 14 models on rounded injections fully reorder the leaderboard?
  4. Is verification performance orthogonal to performance on financial QA benchmarks (FinQA, FinanceBench)?
  5. How do models fare against multi-error, collusive-fraud patterns that preserve surface consistency?
  6. Can a rule+LLM hybrid (deterministic fact ledger + neural reasoning) close the 47% verification gap?
  7. Does tool augmentation (calculator, code generation) help, or is the failure fundamentally cognitive?
