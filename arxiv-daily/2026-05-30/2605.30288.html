MIRA: Mid-training Rubric Anchoring for Source-Aware Data Selection
Haowen Wang, Yaxin Du, Jian Yang, Jiajun Wu et al.
2026-05-28
data-selection mid-training llm-training code-generation
Key Metric
64.20
Macro average @ 25B tokens — beats 63.83 at 50B full-corpus
Why This Matters
Halves mid-training compute while preserving capability coverage — directly relevant to anyone training code-generation models at scale.
Critical Edge
Only ~1 macro-average point above Random selection — source diversity may matter more than quality-aware filtering.

Methodology

01
Rubric Discovery
Cluster sources by embedding; use a frontier teacher (Kimi-K2.6) to freely judge samples, then cluster judgments into 15 anchor dimensions per group.
02
Judge Distillation
Re-score ~2M records with anchored dimensions; distill into per-group generative student scorers (35B MoE, ~3B active).
03
Reliability Masking
Post-hoc mask source-dimension pairs where teacher-student MAE ≥ 1.0; aggregate surviving dimensions with trimmed mean. ~12% masked.
04
Source-Preserving Selection
Apply per-group thresholds (default) to retain capability coverage; alternatives are Global (single threshold) or Source (per-source).

  • MIRA maintains a smooth, length-robust score profile while baselines (PPL, DSIR) show strong length dependence.
  • t-SNE confirms QA, Text, and Agent rubrics occupy distinct regions — quality criteria are format-dependent.
  • MIRA's rubric space subsumes DataMan's 14 generic dimensions (13/14 within MIRA's P95 distance).
  • Student distillation costs <4% of mid-training compute (~250 vs. ~7,680 H800 GPU-hours).

Assessment

Strengths

  • Novel formulation: makes data selection source-aware for LLM mid-training.
  • Clean four-stage separation between semantic discovery and scalable scoring.
  • Practical reliability masking avoids re-running inference when diagnostics change.
  • Rigorous controls: matched compute, identical SFT, comprehensive baselines.
  • Positions mid-training selection as a distinct problem from pretraining and post-training.

Limitations

  • Evaluation limited to code-oriented mid-training; no evidence of transfer to other domains.
  • ~1-point macro-average gap over Random is modest.
  • K-means k=15 and free-form prompting hyperparameters are not ablated.
  • Teacher model biases propagate through the entire pipeline.
  • Requires a capable frontier teacher, which may not be available to all practitioners.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does MIRA generalize to non-code domains (math, multilingual, long-context)?
  2. How sensitive is performance to the number of anchor dimensions k=15?
  3. What is the performance gap between free-discovered rubrics and human-authored ones?
  4. Does the token-saving ratio hold at 1B–3B or 70B+ model scales?
  5. Can reliability masking be updated online rather than applied post-hoc?
  6. What synergies exist with mixture-ratio optimization and curriculum scheduling?
