## 一句话总结

SLAD通过在师生模型之间共享LoRA适配器权重来保持特征对齐，在任务特定蒸馏中实现了更优的知识迁移和训练效率。

## TL;DR

This paper proposes SLAD (Shared LoRA Adapters for Task-Specific Distillation), which shares LoRA adapter weights between teacher and student ViT encoders during joint training. By preserving feature alignment (measured via CKA) between the two models, SLAD achieves state-of-the-art performance on classification and segmentation benchmarks while being 2x faster than the conventional two-step distillation pipeline.

## 核心贡献

1. **揭示了教师微调导致蒸馏效果下降的根本原因**：通过CKA（Centered Kernel Alignment）分析，发现教师模型全量微调会导致师生特征表示严重失配（CKA下降9.4%），而非线性探测仅保持浅层任务信息。
2. **提出基于LoRA的任务特定蒸馏方法**：利用低秩适配器在教师表达能力和特征保持之间取得平衡，CKA下降仅5.1%（vs. 全量微调的9.4%）。
3. **提出SLAD方法**：在师生编码器间共享LoRA适配器权重并进行联合训练，进一步将CKA下降控制在3.71%，实现一步到位的蒸馏训练。

## Key Contributions

- **Feature alignment analysis**: Demonstrates via CKA that fine-tuning the teacher causes severe feature misalignment (9.4% CKA drop) with the student, explaining why a stronger fine-tuned teacher paradoxically produces worse distillation.
- **LoRA-based distillation**: Shows that using LoRA instead of full fine-tuning or linear probing for the teacher achieves better feature alignment (5.1% CKA drop) and consequently better knowledge transfer.
- **SLAD method**: Proposes sharing a subset of LoRA adapter weights between teacher and student encoders during joint training. The student uses the first d_s dimensions of the teacher's larger LoRA matrices (rank r=16), achieving only 3.71% CKA drop while being 2x faster than two-step approaches.

## Methodology

### Problem Setup

- Task-specific distillation: A larger teacher and a smaller student version of the same foundation model (DINOv2 ViT) are adapted to the same downstream task. The goal is to transfer knowledge from the teacher to the student, discarding the teacher at inference time.
- Prior work (Marrie et al., 2024) showed linear probing the teacher yields better distillation than fine-tuning, despite fine-tuning producing a stronger teacher.

### Key Insight: Feature Alignment

- CKA analysis on ViT-B (teacher) vs. ViT-S (student) on CUB dataset:
  - Pre-trained backbones: CKA = 92.95%
  - After fine-tuning teacher: CKA drops by 9.4%
  - After LoRA adaptation: CKA drops by 5.1%
  - SLAD (shared LoRA): CKA drops by only 3.71%

### SLAD Method

1. **LoRA for both encoders**: Apply LoRA adapters to QKV attention matrices with rank r=16.
2. **Weight sharing**: Share a subset of the teacher's LoRA weights (A and B matrices) with the student, matching the student's smaller dimensionality.
3. **Joint training**: Train teacher and student simultaneously in one step using the combined loss:

   L_SLAD = alpha_s * L_task(f_s) + alpha_t * L_task(f_t) + alpha_KL * T^2 * D_KL(f_s, f_t)

   with (alpha_s, alpha_t, alpha_KL) = (1, 1, 1) and temperature T=2.

4. **Block mapping**: When teacher and student have different depths (e.g., ViT-L has 24 blocks vs. ViT-S has 12), an "Even" mapping function maps student block i to teacher block 2i.

### Experimental Setup

- **Backbones**: DINOv2 ViT-L (300M), ViT-B (86M) as teachers; ViT-B (86M), ViT-S (21M) as students.
- **Classification datasets**: CUB (200 classes), FGVC Aircraft (100 classes), DTD (47 classes).
- **Segmentation**: Cityscapes (19 classes).
- **Training**: 30 epochs, AdamW optimizer, cosine LR schedule.

## Results & Highlights

### Main Results (Table 1)

| Student | Teacher | Method | CUB | Aircraft | DTD | Avg. | Cityscapes |
|---------|---------|--------|-----|----------|-----|------|------------|
| ViT-S | ViT-B | Probing* | 89.80 | 89.44 | 81.93 | 87.06 | 69.32 |
| ViT-S | ViT-B | SLAD | **90.54** | **92.50** | 84.56 | **89.20** | **73.91** |
| ViT-S | ViT-L | SLAD | 90.44 | 92.44 | 84.24 | 89.04 | 73.97 |
| ViT-B | ViT-L | SLAD | **91.81** | **94.18** | 86.40 | **90.80** | **77.35** |

- SLAD achieves **2.16% average accuracy improvement** over prior SOTA (probing-based distillation) on classification benchmarks.
- On Cityscapes segmentation, SLAD achieves **73.91 mIoU** (ViT-B to ViT-S) vs. 69.32 for probing, a +4.59 improvement.
- LoRA-based distillation alone already outperforms probing-based distillation.

### Training Efficiency

- Standard two-step distillation (30+30 epochs): ~8.3 hours on A100 (ViT-B to ViT-S, Aircraft).
- SLAD joint training (30 epochs): ~5.6 hours, approximately **2x faster**.
- Compared to Marrie et al.'s 80-epoch pipeline: ~17.5 hours, making SLAD **3x faster**.

### Teacher Performance

- SLAD also improves the teacher: up to **+0.8% accuracy** over LoRA-only training for the teacher model.
- Consistent with Deep Mutual Learning -- the student's gradient through shared adapters benefits the teacher.
- Stronger students (ViT-B) yield larger gains for the teacher than weaker ones (ViT-S).

### Ablation Studies

- **LoRA rank**: Performance is stable across different ranks (r=4,8,16,32), showing low sensitivity.
- **Temperature**: T=4 gives the best results; very high T causes over-smoothing.
- **Loss weights**: Increasing KL divergence weight consistently improves performance.
- **Block mapping**: "Even" mapping is best for CUB; differences across mappings are small.
- **PEFT alternatives**: SLAD outperforms SVF and VPT-based alternatives.

## Critical Assessment

### Strengths

- **Clear motivation and analysis**: The CKA-based feature alignment analysis provides a compelling explanation for why fine-tuned teachers produce worse distillation. This is a genuine insight that connects catastrophic forgetting literature to knowledge distillation.
- **Simple and effective method**: The shared LoRA approach is conceptually clean -- no additional projection layers or complex multi-stage training.
- **Dual benefit**: SLAD improves both the student AND the teacher, which is unusual and practically valuable.
- **Strong empirical results**: Consistent improvements across multiple datasets, model sizes, and both classification and segmentation tasks.
- **Training efficiency**: 2x faster than the standard pipeline is a meaningful practical contribution.
- **Thorough ablations**: Rank sensitivity, temperature, loss weights, block mapping, and PEFT alternatives are all studied.

### Limitations

- **Scope of experiments**: Only evaluated on DINOv2 ViT models. Generalization to other architectures (CNNs, Swin Transformers) and other modalities (NLP, multimodal) is not demonstrated.
- **Limited task diversity**: Classification and segmentation on relatively small datasets (CUB, Aircraft, DTD, Cityscapes). No experiments on larger-scale benchmarks (ImageNet, ADE20K) or generative tasks.
- **LoRA rank ceiling**: The paper acknowledges that LoRA's low-rank nature is less flexible than full fine-tuning. The performance gap between a fully fine-tuned teacher and a LoRA-adapted teacher could matter more on harder tasks.
- **Dimensionality constraint**: The sharing mechanism requires the student's feature dimension to be a subset of the teacher's, which limits applicability to architecturally compatible model pairs.
- **Reproducibility concern**: The authors had to re-implement Marrie et al.'s method due to unavailability of source code, making the baseline comparison potentially unreliable. Their re-implementation ("Probing*") sometimes differs from the original reported numbers.
- **No comparison with feature-based distillation**: Methods like FitNets that use intermediate feature supervision are discussed but not empirically compared.

### Relevance

本文对关注模型压缩和边缘部署的研究者具有直接参考价值。核心发现——教师微调导致特征失配进而影响蒸馏效率——为知识蒸馏领域提供了新的理解视角。SLAD方法的简洁性和训练效率使其在实际部署场景中具有应用潜力。对于研究PEFT、持续学习和模型蒸馏交叉领域的学者，这篇论文值得关注。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. SLAD能否扩展到多教师蒸馏场景，即多个不同大小的教师共享适配器？
2. 共享LoRA适配器的策略是否适用于跨架构蒸馏（如ViT教师到CNN学生）？
3. 在更大规模数据集（如ImageNet）上，SLAD相对于全量微调的性能差距会如何变化？
4. 教师和学生之间的维度差异对适配器共享效果的影响有多大？是否存在最优的学生-教师大小比例？
5. 结合特征层面的蒸馏损失（如FitNets风格的特征hint）是否能进一步提升SLAD的效果？
6. SLAD在NLP领域的LLM蒸馏中是否同样有效？共享LoRA权重是否会在语言任务中产生不同的效果？
