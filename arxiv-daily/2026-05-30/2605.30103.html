Methodology

The core move: treat iterative LLM-NAS as a parametric Cross-Entropy method. The standard loop — generate architectures, evaluate via 1-epoch proxy accuracy, filter elites above τ = 0.40, fine-tune the LLM on elites plus a static LEMUR corpus — is shown to minimize KL divergence over the LLM's parameter family.

  • Architecture space A = all valid, executable torch.nn.Module subclasses; quality q(a) = first-epoch validation accuracy.
  • Two assumptions drive the theory: regularity (bounded quality, positive initial elite mass, consistent estimation) and quality-monotone projection (CE projection preserves mean quality).
  • Markov token-error model replaces the independent-token assumption: a two-state chain {correct, error} with transition probabilities εₜ and γₜ, yielding a stationary correct-state probability π_c.
  • Proxy reliability models (q_true, q_proxy) as bivariate Normal with additive noise ξ, derives SNR = σ²_arch / σ²_noise, then applies the Kruskal-Kendall identity to obtain the Spearman-Pearson closed form.

Experiments: 22 NAS cycles, three 7B-parameter LLMs (Mistral-7B, Qwen2.5-Coder-7B, DeepSeek-Coder-7B), six datasets, single RTX 4090.

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical framework making the LLM-NAS / CE connection precise and non-obvious.
  • Six theorems with full proofs; authors honestly flag restrictive assumptions (1d and 2).
  • Proxy reliability formula ρ_S = (6/π)·arcsin(ρ_P(SNR)/2) is both grounded and immediately actionable.
  • Transparent reporting of failed predictions with plausible explanations.
  • Explains the previously unexplained proxy-reliability ceiling effect (Chen et al.).
  • Rare, excellent self-assessment in Section 7.

Limitations

  • Single data source: all experiments from one prior paper by the same group; no third-party validation.
  • Small samples: proxy-reliability N ∈ {12, 15, 16} — DeepSeek non-significance may be underpowered.
  • Conditional monotonicity: Theorem 2 relies on Assumption 2, acknowledged as "currently unverifiable" for LoRA.
  • LoRA ceiling not quantified: predicts C* ≤ 1 but cannot derive C* from first principles; 73–76% is post-hoc consistent, not predicted.
  • Oversimplified Markov model: delta-rate prediction overshoots by 58%; longer-range correlations ignored.
  • No architecture quality comparison against SOTA NAS baselines — contribution is purely theoretical.
  • Bivariate Normal assumption violated by accuracy-ceiling truncation (Qwen discrepancy).

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can Assumption 2 be verified or replaced with a weaker, verifiable condition for specific LoRA configurations?
  • How does convergence rate change with LoRA rank? Is there a critical rank below which the ceiling drops?
  • Can the proxy reliability formula be extended to truncated accuracy supports for Qwen-style ceiling effects?
  • Does the framework apply to non-LoRA fine-tuning (full fine-tuning, QLoRA, prompt-based)?
  • Could the SNR diagnostic adaptively determine proxy training epochs, saving compute?
  • What happens when the quality function is multi-objective (accuracy vs. FLOPs vs. latency)?
  • Can the Markov token-error model capture attention-induced long-range dependencies causing the 1.41 vs 2.23 gap?
