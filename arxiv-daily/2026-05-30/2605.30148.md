## 一句话总结

本文重新审视了进化策略(ES)微调大语言模型时的"遗忘"现象，发现这更应被描述为可恢复的"性能漂移"而非不可逆遗忘，并提出了锚定权重衰减(Anchored Weight Decay, AWD)这一简单正则化方法来有效抑制该漂移。

## TL;DR

The paper challenges the prevailing belief that Evolution Strategies (ES) fine-tuning inherently causes catastrophic forgetting in LLMs. Through systematic experiments across multiple tasks, models, and scales, the authors show that (1) forgetting under ES is better characterized as transient "performance drift" that often recovers during training, (2) forgetting is not ES-specific and also occurs with GRPO, and (3) the primary driver is random walk in weakly constrained weight-space directions. They introduce Anchored Weight Decay (AWD), which constrains optimization toward initial parameters, effectively stabilizing prior-task performance at minimal computational overhead.

## 核心贡献

1. **重新定义遗忘本质**：通过跟踪整个训练过程中的先验任务性能变化，揭示了ES引起的"遗忘"并非不可逆的知识丢失，而是一种在训练过程中经常自行恢复的"性能漂移"(performance drift)。例如，HellaSwag上的准确率在前300次迭代中下降8%，但随后恢复到原始水平。

2. **遗忘并非ES独有**：在ProofWriter作为目标任务时，GRPO也表现出严重的遗忘（尤其在GSM8K先验任务上），证明遗忘是微调方法的普遍问题，而非ES的特定缺陷。

3. **提出锚定权重衰减(AWD)**：基于对ES训练动力学的理论分析（随机游走行为），提出了在ES更新规则中直接加入朝向初始参数的衰减项，以l1或l2惩罚函数约束权重漂移。AWD仅需额外1-2%的运行时间开销，无需额外前向传播。

4. **计算效率优势**：AWD在种群规模为30时的效果可媲美种群规模128的标准ES，大幅节省计算资源。

## Key Contributions

- Reframes ES-induced forgetting as recoverable performance drift rather than irreversible catastrophic forgetting, with empirical evidence across diverse target tasks (Countdown, GSM8K, ProofWriter), prior tasks (HellaSwag, PIQA, ARC-Challenge, MMLU-Pro), model families (Qwen-2.5, Llama-3.2), and model sizes (1.5B, 3B, 7B).
- Demonstrates that GRPO also suffers from forgetting in certain task combinations, disproving the claim that forgetting is ES-specific.
- Provides theoretical grounding linking forgetting to the random walk component of ES updates, showing the expected cumulative weight deviation scales as alpha^2 * T * d / N (from Hoy et al. 2026).
- Introduces Anchored Weight Decay (AWD), a parameter-space regularization applied directly in the ES update rule, supporting both l1 and l2 penalty functions with negligible compute overhead.

## Methodology

### Experimental Design

- **Target tasks**: Countdown (arithmetic reasoning), GSM8K (math word problems), ProofWriter (logical reasoning); trained on 200 examples each.
- **Prior tasks**: HellaSwag, PIQA, ARC-Challenge, MMLU-Pro, plus target tasks not trained on in a given experiment.
- **Models**: Qwen-2.5 Instruct (1.5B/3B/7B) and Llama-3.2 Instruct (3B), consistent with prior work.
- **ES settings**: sigma=0.001, alpha=sigma/2, population size 30 (default), 500 iterations.
- **GRPO settings**: lr=1e-6, batch size 32, KL coefficient 0.001, 30 rollouts, 500 epochs.
- **Evaluation**: 500-2000 examples per task, greedy decoding, accuracy metric.
- **Compute**: 8x H200 node; ES runs ~4 node-hours for 3B models, GRPO runs ~20 node-hours.

### AWD Formulation

AWD modifies the standard ES update rule by adding a decay term toward the initial parameters theta_0:

```
theta_t = theta_{t-1} + alpha * (1/N) * sum(r_hat_n * epsilon_n) - alpha * lambda * l'(theta_t - theta_0)
```

The penalty function l(.) can be l1 or l2. The l2 variant provides smoother behavior; l1 induces sparsity in weight changes. Implementation stores theta_0 in pinned RAM and streams it layer-wise to GPU during updates, adding only 1-2% runtime overhead.

### Key Analytical Tools

- Tracking accuracy trajectories throughout training (not just final checkpoints) to reveal drift dynamics.
- Weight update norm analysis to quantify random walk magnitude.
- KL-divergence analysis between base and fine-tuned models across tasks to measure distributional shift.

## Results & Highlights

- **Performance drift is transient**: Prior-task accuracy often dips initially then recovers during ES training (e.g., HellaSwag drops 8% then recovers to baseline by final iteration).
- **AWD effectively mitigates forgetting**: With l2 penalty (lambda=10.0), AWD reduces average prior-task accuracy degradation from -2.5% to near zero while preserving target-task performance on Countdown.
- **GRPO also forgets**: On ProofWriter as target task, GRPO shows severe forgetting on GSM8K (-6.5% accuracy change) and moderate forgetting on Countdown and MMLU-Pro.
- **Population size helps but is costly**: Increasing ES population from 30 to 256 reduces forgetting but requires significantly more compute. AWD with population 30 matches population 128 results.
- **Weight norms vs. distributional shift**: ES with AWD still has weight update norms an order of magnitude larger than GRPO, yet achieves similar KL-divergence on prior tasks. This suggests it is the unconstrained randomness of drift, not update magnitude alone, that causes forgetting.
- **AWD transfers across configurations**: Lambda values (l1: 0.01, l2: 10.0) tuned on one configuration transfer well to different target tasks and model types.
- **Sensitivity analysis**: There exists a stable intermediate range of lambda where AWD preserves target performance while mitigating forgetting. A practical tuning strategy is to start with high lambda and decrease until no target-task degradation is observed.

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a significant contribution by reframing the narrative around ES-induced forgetting. Previous work (Abdi et al. 2026) presented forgetting as a fundamental limitation of ES; this paper provides compelling evidence that the picture is more nuanced. The key insight -- that the "forgetting" is largely recoverable drift driven by random walk -- is well-supported by the trajectory analysis and weight-norm experiments.

However, several aspects deserve scrutiny:

- **Scope of forgetting analysis**: The paper focuses exclusively on verifiable reasoning tasks (math, logic, commonsense). It does not address whether AWD preserves alignment, safety, or open-ended generative capabilities. The authors acknowledge this limitation.
- **Limited task diversity for forgetting demonstrations**: The most pronounced forgetting occurs on Countdown as target task. For GSM8K and ProofWriter, forgetting is less systematic, which somewhat weakens the urgency of the mitigation.
- **Lambda tuning**: While the paper shows lambda transfers across some configurations, the optimal range is non-trivial to identify and the sharp drop-off at high lambda means practitioners need careful tuning. The proposed strategy (start high, decrease) is reasonable but adds a hyperparameter.
- **Still a gap with GRPO on weight norms**: Even with AWD, ES weight update norms remain an order of magnitude larger than GRPO. While KL-divergence is comparable, this discrepancy is not fully explained and could matter in regimes not tested.
- **No comparison with other continual learning methods**: The paper compares AWD only against larger population sizes and GRPO, but not against established continual learning techniques like EWC, replay, or orthogonal subspace methods applied to ES fine-tuning.

## Strengths

- **Systematic and thorough experimental design**: Multiple target tasks, prior tasks, model families, and sizes provide a comprehensive picture.
- **Trajectory analysis is insightful**: Tracking accuracy throughout training (not just endpoints) reveals the drift phenomenon that checkpoint-only evaluations would miss.
- **Simple and practical solution**: AWD requires minimal code changes and negligible computational overhead, making it immediately adoptable.
- **Strong theoretical grounding**: The connection to random walk dynamics in weight space provides a principled motivation for the approach.
- **Reproducibility**: Open-source code and detailed hyperparameter specifications.

## Limitations

- **Narrow task scope**: Only verifiable reasoning tasks are tested. The behavior on creative generation, instruction following, or safety-critical tasks is unknown.
- **Single regularization approach**: Only l1 and l2 penalties are explored; more sophisticated penalty functions (e.g., Huber) are mentioned but not tested.
- **Evaluation metric**: Accuracy on multiple-choice or exact-match tasks may not capture more subtle forms of capability degradation.
- **No multi-task continual learning**: Experiments study a single fine-tuning episode, not sequential multi-task learning where AWD's effectiveness would be most critical.
- **Computational cost reporting**: While ES is presented as more efficient than GRPO, the paper uses 8x H200 nodes, and scalability to smaller setups is not discussed.
