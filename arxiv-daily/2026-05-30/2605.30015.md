## 一句话总结

本文揭示了监督式因果学习(SCL)在分布偏移下的三大局限性,并提出TTT-SCL框架,通过在测试时动态生成与测试实例对齐的训练集来实现鲁棒的因果发现。

## TL;DR

The paper identifies three critical limitations of static pre-training in Supervised Causal Learning (SCL): a synthetic-to-real performance gap, fragility to distribution shifts, and compositional generalization failure. To address these, the authors propose TTT-SCL, which dynamically generates causally aligned training sets at test time using score-guided stochastic graph refinement. TTT-SCL is shown to subsume classical score-based methods as a special case, and achieves state-of-the-art performance across synthetic, pseudo-real (SynTReN), and real-world (Sachs) datasets.

## 核心贡献

1. **揭示SCL的三大局限性**:系统性地展示了现有静态预训练SCL方法存在的问题——合成数据上的优秀表现无法迁移到真实数据(AVICI在合成RFF_G上达97.8 AUROC,在真实Sachs数据集上仅62.3),对图结构/因果机制/噪声分布的单维度偏移都极其脆弱,且即使训练数据包含所有单独组件也无法组合泛化到新的组合。
2. **提出TTT-SCL框架**:核心创新是在测试时动态生成与测试实例因果对齐的训练集。通过评分函数(似然+稀疏惩罚)引导的随机图精炼来搜索高评分候选图,再用结构诱导机制(SIM)从测试数据拟合因果机制并前向采样生成训练数据。
3. **建立TTT-SCL与经典评分方法的联系**:证明经典评分方法可以看作TTT-SCL的特例(1-最近邻分类器),从而继承了评分方法的理论保证,同时通过监督学习超越了单点估计的性能上限。

## Key Contributions

1. **Systematic diagnosis of SCL limitations**: Demonstrates three failure modes of static SCL -- synthetic-real performance gap, distribution shift fragility (across graph structure, mechanism, and noise dimensions), and compositional generalization failure -- validated across AVICI and SiCL backbones.
2. **TTT-SCL framework**: A test-time training paradigm that generates instance-specific training data via: (a) seed initialization from traditional causal discovery methods, (b) iterative stochastic graph refinement guided by an AIC-like score function combining distributional alignment (AD) and sparsity penalty, and (c) Structure-Induced Mechanism (SIM) for forward-sampling aligned synthetic datasets.
3. **Theoretical connection to score-based methods**: Shows classical score-based causal discovery is a special case of TTT-SCL (with a 1-nearest-neighbor predictor), providing theoretical grounding and explaining why the supervised learning phase adds value beyond score maximization.

## Methodology

**Problem setup**: Given a test dataset $D_{test}$ with unknown causal graph $G_{test}$, infer $G_{test}$. SCL frames this as learning a mapping $\mathcal{M}: \mathbb{R}^{n \times d} \to \{0,1\}^{d \times d}$ from data matrices to adjacency matrices.

**TTT-SCL workflow** (3 stages):

1. **Seed Initialization**: Obtain initial graph $G^0$ via traditional methods (NOTEARS, PC, or random DAG).
2. **Stochastic Graph Refinement**: Iteratively propose local graph modifications (add/delete/reverse single edges preserving acyclicity). Accept candidate with Metropolis-Hastings-style probability based on the score function:
   $$\text{score}(G, D_{test}) = \text{AD}(G, D_{test}) - \|\mathbf{A}_G\|_0$$
   where AD (Alignment-of-Distribution) is the average log-likelihood under mechanisms fitted from $D_{test}$, and $\|\mathbf{A}_G\|_0$ penalizes graph complexity. Collect the last $K$ graphs from $N_{steps}=2000$ iterations.
3. **Training Data Generation**: For each candidate graph, fit mechanisms and noise distributions from $D_{test}$ via SIM (Structure-Induced Mechanism), then forward-sample synthetic datasets. Train an SCL model (AVICI backbone) on these $K=200$ causally aligned instances and apply it to $D_{test}$.

**Key design choice**: The score function mirrors AIC (Akaike Information Criterion), balancing goodness-of-fit with model complexity. The AD metric is flexible -- implemented via likelihood, R-squared, or Wasserstein distance depending on data characteristics.

## Results & Highlights

- **Synthetic benchmarks (10-node graphs)**: TTT-SCL (Notears) achieves state-of-the-art AUROC on Linear_U (88.4-89.1), Chebyshev_G (86.0-87.3), and RFF_G (94.4-95.8) settings, competitive with or surpassing AVICI(scm-v0) which was explicitly trained on RFF_G data.
- **Real-world Sachs dataset (11 proteins)**: TTT-SCL (Notears) achieves 78.9 AUROC, dramatically outperforming AVICI(scm-v0) at 62.3. Traditional methods: PC (56.9), NOTEARS (65.2).
- **Pseudo-real SynTReN dataset (20 nodes)**: TTT-SCL achieves 79.6 AUROC vs. AVICI(scm-v0) at 67.0.
- **Two-stage improvement verified**: Seed graph -> highest-score graph -> final SCL output shows consistent AUROC gains at each stage, confirming both search and learning components add value.
- **Ablation**: Removing the sparsity penalty causes significant performance drops across all settings, confirming both AD and sparsity are essential.
- **Scalability**: Complexity is $O(N_{steps} \cdot n \cdot d)$, dominated by stochastic graph refinement. On 10-node graphs, runtime is manageable; the authors argue that for scientific inference tasks, computational cost is acceptable.

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a convincing case that static SCL has fundamental OOD generalization problems, and the TTT-SCL idea of test-time training set generation is elegant and well-motivated. However, several concerns merit attention:

- **Computational cost**: Each test instance requires 2000 stochastic refinement steps plus training an SCL model from scratch on 200 generated instances. This is orders of magnitude more expensive than a single forward pass through a pre-trained model. While the authors argue this is acceptable for scientific inference, it limits practical applicability.
- **Scalability to larger graphs**: Experiments are limited to 10-20 node graphs. The stochastic search over DAG space is fundamentally exponential, and the Metropolis-Hastings approach may struggle to explore the space efficiently for larger graphs (50+ nodes).
- **Reliance on seed quality**: TTT-SCL (Notears) consistently outperforms TTT-SCL (random), and while increasing the budget helps, the gap suggests practical sensitivity to initialization.
- **Limited real-world evaluation**: Only the Sachs dataset (11 variables, 853 samples) serves as the real-world benchmark. The pseudo-real bnlearn experiments use Chebyshev mechanisms injected into real graph structures, which is still partly synthetic.
- **Theoretical contribution is mild**: The "score-based methods as special case" argument (1-nearest-neighbor classifier) is technically correct but somewhat trivial. It does not provide new identifiability guarantees beyond what the score function already inherits.

## Strengths

- Thorough and honest diagnostic analysis of SCL limitations with systematic experiments across three dimensions (graph, mechanism, noise).
- Elegant framework design that naturally bridges classical score-based methods and modern supervised learning.
- Strong empirical performance across diverse settings, especially on real-world data where static SCL fails.
- Validated across two different model architectures (AVICI transformer and SiCL pairwise attention), showing the findings are architecture-agnostic.
- Well-structured paper with clear problem formulation, method description, and extensive appendices.

## Limitations

- Computational overhead is substantial; not suitable for applications requiring fast inference.
- Scalability beyond 20 nodes is untested and likely problematic given the stochastic DAG search.
- Only one real-world dataset (Sachs) with ground truth, which is a limitation of the field rather than the paper specifically.
- The framework introduces several hyperparameters ($N_{steps}$, $K$, score function design) whose sensitivity is not fully explored.
- The stochastic refinement may get trapped in local optima for complex graph structures.

## 相关性

本文对以下研究方向具有高度相关性:

- **因果发现与因果推断**: TTT-SCL提供了一种将监督学习与经典评分方法结合的新范式,对实际因果发现应用有直接价值。
- **测试时适应(Test-Time Adaptation)**: 将TTA思想从传统的分类/回归任务扩展到结构学习(因果图发现)这一新领域。
- **组合泛化(Compositional Generalization)**: 揭示的SCL组合泛化失败问题对理解神经网络的系统性泛化能力有更广泛的启示。
- **合成数据到真实数据的迁移**: 对所有依赖合成数据训练的方法(不仅限于因果发现)都有借鉴意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. TTT-SCL能否扩展到50-100节点的大规模因果图?随机图搜索的效率如何随节点数增长而衰减?
2. 是否可以用更高效的搜索策略(如基于梯度的方法、强化学习)替代Metropolis-Hastings随机搜索来加速图精炼?
3. 组合泛化失败的根因是什么?是模型架构的限制(Transformer的注意力机制),还是训练范式本身的问题?
4. TTT-SCL框架是否可以与LLM-based因果发现方法结合,利用大语言模型的先验知识来改进种子初始化?
5. 在存在隐变量的情况下(非马尔可夫等价类可识别),TTT-SCL如何调整?当前的评估假设因果图可从观测分布唯一确定。
