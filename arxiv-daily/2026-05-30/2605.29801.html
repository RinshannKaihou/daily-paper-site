Why This Matters
Small models with 100× less compute can now guard deployed AI agents in real time — making safety affordable at scale.
Critical Edge
Failure Mode accuracy remains only 27.5%, and benchmarks are self-generated by the same taxonomy, raising circularity concerns.

Methodology

A three-dimensional taxonomy (risk source × failure mode × real-world harm) drives a taxonomy-guided data engine. Controllable risk injection synthesizes multi-turn agent trajectories; dual-layer validation ensures structural and semantic quality. Influence-function purification then distills the raw SFT pool to ~1k highest-value samples. Models are trained in two stages: supervised fine-tuning on purified CoT data, followed by GDPO reinforcement learning with per-dimension advantage normalization.

01 PLAN

02 SYNTHESIZE

Generate multi-turn traces with controllable risk injection.

03 VALIDATE

Rule checker + model checker for structural and semantic quality.

04 PURIFY

Influence-function scoring retains only the top ~1k examples.

05 SFT

Fine-tune Qwen3.5/Llama base models on purified CoT-augmented data.

06 RL

GDPO with multi-dimensional rewards (0.3/0.4/0.3 weights), 8 rollouts.

Assessment

Strengths

  • Exceptional data efficiency — ~1k samples via influence-function purification
  • Full pipeline: taxonomy → data → training → deployment
  • 4B model outperforms 397B generalist on safety metrics
  • 100× reduction in environment overhead for training
  • All models and datasets open-sourced

Limitations

  • Text-only trajectories; no multimodal coverage
  • Failure Mode accuracy only 27.5% in absolute terms
  • Guardrail blocks final response, not already-executed harms
  • Self-generated benchmarks raise circularity risk
  • Incremental over AgentDoG 1.0; limited novelty per component

Questions for Further Exploration

1
How sensitive is influence-function purification to the choice of reference model? Would different base models yield different "best 1k" sets?
2
Failure Mode accuracy is only 27.5% — is the bottleneck too many classes (21), fuzzy boundaries, or insufficient data? Would coarser granularity help?
3
What is the actual latency of the online guardrail? "Low latency" is claimed but no numbers are reported — a critical gap for deployment.
4
ATBench and training data share the same taxonomy and engine. How well does the model generalize to out-of-distribution risk scenarios?
5
When used as an RL reward model, can policy agents learn to reward-hack by generating superficially-safe but harmful behavior patterns?
