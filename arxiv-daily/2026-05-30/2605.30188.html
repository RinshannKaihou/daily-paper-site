Methodology

CalArena aggregates predictions from 7 benchmark suites (~2000 experiments): classical tabular (TabRepo: 8 models / 169 datasets), advanced tabular foundation models (TabArena: 11 models including TabPFN, TabICL, TabDPT), computer vision (ResNet, ViT, BEiT, Swin, ConvNeXt), and ImageNet (1000-class).

The PHI metric (Post-Hoc Improvement in Brier score) is theoretically grounded: post-hoc transformations cannot reduce refinement error, so any proper-score improvement must come from calibration-error reduction. Positive PHI = the calibrator helped; negative = it hurt.

Methods tested: Temperature / Platt / Quadratic / Beta scaling, isotonic regression, spline calibration, CIR, histogram & BBQ binning, Venn-Abers, kernel methods, tree-based calibrators (XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost), and native multiclass methods (SMS, Dirichlet, VS, SVS, ETS). Rankings aggregated via winrates, Bradley-Terry Elo, Friedman-Nemenyi post-hoc tests, and bootstrap confidence intervals.

Results & Highlights

DomainFinding
Binary Quadratic Scaling, Platt-logits, and Beta Calibration are top performers. Spline ranks 4th as best non-parametric method. Binning degrades below the uncalibrated baseline.
Multiclass Structured Matrix Scaling (SMS) dominates with >0.7 winrate on TabRepo and ~0.95 on CV. Native methods become essential when classes exceed 3.
ImageNet ETS achieves near-100% winrate. All OvR methods degrade, confirming inability to scale to high dimensions. SMS and Dirichlet are inapplicable (~1M parameters).
Smoothness CIR (linearized isotonic) dramatically outperforms standard isotonic regression. Binning looks good on ECE-15 but harms Brier score.
ML baselines Even CatBoost / XGBoost / LightGBM with cross-validation and early stopping underperform dedicated calibration methods. Calibration-specific design (monotonicity, low tree depth) is what matters.

Strengths

  • Unprecedented scale: ~2000 experiments in a single unified framework
  • PHI metric is theoretically principled; avoids pitfalls of ECE-based evaluation
  • Clear, actionable recommendations (Quadratic for binary, SMS for multiclass)
  • Full open-source release: 1.71GB data + code on Hugging Face
  • Statistical rigor: Elo scores, Friedman-Nemenyi tests, bootstrap CIs

Limitations

  • Fixed hyperparameters across all methods; some may benefit significantly from tuning
  • CV benchmarks have far fewer experiments (13–20) compared to tabular (314–832)
  • No distribution shift / OOD evaluation — arguably the most important practical scenario
  • No NLP/LLM or generative model calibration experiments
  • Post-hoc only; calibration-aware training is out of scope

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How do rankings change when methods are allowed hyperparameter tuning via cross-validation on the calibration set?
  • Can the PHI framework be extended to evaluate calibration under distribution shift?
  • What is the minimum calibration set size needed for Quadratic / SMS to reliably outperform the uncalibrated baseline?
  • Could calibration-aware training methods (label smoothing, focal loss) be incorporated as baselines?
  • Is ETS's ImageNet victory a small-sample artifact (8 experiments) or genuine advantage at extreme scale?
