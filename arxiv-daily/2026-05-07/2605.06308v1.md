> **一句话总结：** 本文提出通过将思维链（CoT）轨迹嵌入为滑动窗口序列、并测量其向外部答案锚点的余弦收敛程度来估计黑箱LLM置信度，该单参数几何信号在无需logits或隐藏状态的情况下，与Coverage（C）和Verbalization（V）融合后，以K=4样本在6/6设定上Pareto优于8样本自洽（中位AUC 0.78，ΔAUC=+0.075）。

> **TL;DR:** This paper challenges the dominance of self-consistency (SC) as the go-to black-box confidence estimator for chain-of-thought LLMs. The authors embed a CoT trace as a sliding-window trajectory in embedding space and measure how strongly the penultimate window converges toward external answer anchors — a one-parameter softmax that requires no logits, hidden states, or supervised calibrators. This "Geometry" (G) signal is then fused with a "Coverage" (C) channel (whether the correct hypothesis was ever proposed) and a "Verbalization" (V) channel (the model's own numerical self-rating). Across 18 benchmark×reasoner×proposer settings spanning MedQA-USMLE, GPQA Diamond, and MMLU-Pro with Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude Sonnet 4.6, and Llama 3.3 70B, the fused C+G+V system at K=4 Pareto-improves SC@8 in all 6 closed-set settings (median ΔAUC=+0.075) at roughly half the API cost. The paper is worth reading if you work on LLM deployment safety, confidence calibration, or uncertainty quantification without white-box access.

## 核心贡献

- **黑箱轨迹置信度分数**：将CoT轨迹嵌入为滑动窗口序列（窗口W=30词，步长S=15词），计算倒数第二窗口与外部答案锚点的余弦距离，经单参数softmax校准后输出连续置信值，无需logits或隐藏状态。
- **晚期轨迹承诺签名（Late-trace Commitment Signature）**：通过窗口位置扫描实验发现，几何信号在倒数第二窗口最强（斜率最负），而终止窗口出现方向翻转——这是模型将答案逐字复述的行为造成的，并在全部三个基准和两个主要推理模型上得到验证。
- **三通道分解框架（C+G+V）**：将黑箱置信度分解为Coverage（正确假设是否被提出，由judge-LLM评判）、Geometry（轨迹几何收敛）、Verbalization（模型数字自评分）三个正交信道，揭示其各自的独立贡献和适用条件。
- **成本效益优势**：在6/6封闭集设定中，K=4的C+G+V系统比SC@8节约约51%的API成本，同时实现更高AUC。

## Key Contributions

- **Trajectory-geometry confidence score**: Embeds a CoT trace as overlapping sliding windows (W=30 words, stride S=15) in embedding space (3072-dim Gemini embeddings), scores convergence of the penultimate window toward fixed external answer anchors via a one-parameter softmax calibrated by maximum likelihood. Requires no logits, hidden states, or per-model supervision.
- **Late-trace commitment signature**: A window-position sweep on GPQA Diamond shows the softmax slope (β) is most negative at position k=−2 (penultimate), inverts to positive at k=−1 (terminal) for wrong-pick traces — a mechanistic fingerprint of answer verbalization — replicated identically across Gemini 3.1 Pro and Claude Sonnet 4.6.
- **Three-channel decomposition (C+G+V)**: Decomposes black-box confidence into Coverage C (judge-mediated reachability prior, independent of reasoning trace), Geometry G (within-trace commitment), and Verbalization V (conditional metacognitive channel). C and G carry independent signal in 18/18 and 16/18 settings respectively; V adds residual signal in only 6/18.
- **Pareto improvement over self-consistency**: C+G+V at K=4 beats SC@8 in all 6 closed-set settings (median ΔAUC=+0.075, pooled Stouffer z=5.67, p=7.1×10⁻⁹) at ~51% lower API cost per question under May-2026 pricing.
- **Robustness**: Results replicated with an independent E5-large-v2 embedder (Stouffer z=3.14, p=8.5×10⁻⁴), across Gemini/Claude/Llama model families, and on three benchmarks spanning medicine (MedQA-USMLE), graduate science (GPQA Diamond), and general academic (MMLU-Pro).

## Methodology

**Task formulation.** The system estimates calibrated confidence for black-box chain-of-thought reasoning without access to logits or hidden states, targeting selective-prediction AUC as the evaluation metric (accuracy-coverage tradeoff at abstention).

**Trajectory construction.** Given a question q, K=4 free-form CoT samples are generated at T=0.7 without revealing the option list. Each CoT C^(k) is chunked into overlapping sliding windows of W=30 words with stride S=15, producing a trajectory T^(k) = (z_0^(k), z_1^(k), ..., z_{M_k}^(k)) where each z_m^(k) = φ(w_{(m-1)S+1:(m-1)S+W}) is a dense embedding via φ (gemini-embedding-001, 3072 dimensions).

**Geometric score.** Per-option cosine distance is computed from the penultimate window z_{M_k-1}^(k) to each external benchmark answer anchor y_j = φ(a_j), averaged over K samples: D_j(T) = (1/K)Σ_k [1 - ⟨z_{M_k-1}^(k), y_j⟩ / (||z_{M_k-1}^(k)|| ||y_j||)]. The geometric pick ŷ = argmin_j D_j. A one-parameter softmax GEO(q) = exp(β̂ D_{ŷ}) / Σ_{j'} exp(β̂ D_{j'}) calibrates this into a confidence probability, with β̂ fit by maximum likelihood on a training fold. The sign of β̂ is strongly negative across all benchmark-model combinations (z = −5.48 to −25.39), confirming that closer anchors predict correctness.

**Why the penultimate window?** A window-position sweep on GPQA (Figure 1) shows β̂ is most negative at k=−2 and inverts to positive at k=−1 on wrong-pick traces; using the final window would align with the model's committed (possibly wrong) answer rather than the gold answer. This choice was fixed a priori and confirmed post-hoc.

**Three-channel open-ended extension.** For the open-ended regime, a proposer LLM (Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude Sonnet 4.6, or Llama 3.3 70B at K_P=3 temperature-varied pools, J=4 strict-matching samples) generates hypothesis pools; a fixed judge LLM (Azure-hosted GPT-5-mini) labels gold-equivalence. Three channels are defined:
- **Coverage (C)**: Features are the judge's mean gold-coverage score c̄_q and the number of valid pools K_P^valid; no reasoner trace is consumed.
- **Geometry (G)**: Same sliding-window trajectory geometry as Section 3, with proposer-generated hypothesis anchors instead of dataset options; features include top-2 cosine margin, mean cosine to top-1 hypothesis, vote concentration, and vote entropy across K=5 traces.
- **Verbalization (V)**: The reasoner is shown its own committed CoT and asked for a 0–100 integer confidence score; features are mean, std, and min over K=5 elicitations.

Each channel is converted to a per-question score under stratified 5-fold logistic regression; a second 5-fold logistic fusion combines the three channel scores.

**Evaluation protocol.** Selective-prediction AUC (discriminative AUC under stratified 5-fold CV) is the primary metric. All parameters (β̂, standardizers, logistic weights) are fit on training folds and applied frozen to held-out folds, preventing leakage. Benchmarks: MedQA-USMLE (N≈1272), GPQA Diamond (N≈187–198), MMLU-Pro (N=200 pilot). Reasoners: Gemini 3.1 Pro and Claude Sonnet 4.6 (main); Llama 3.3 70B Instruct (robustness).

## Results & Highlights

**Single-channel geometry beats self-consistency.** In 9/10 benchmark-model-condition combinations, the single geometric score (GEO) at K=4 exceeds budget-matched SC@4 (Tables 7, 8). On MedQA/Gemini the composite reaches AUC 0.826 vs. SC@10 AUC 0.643.

**Three-channel fusion (C+G+V) vs. SC@8.** Across 6 closed-set benchmark×reasoner settings, C+G+V at K=4 Pareto-improves SC@8: median ΔAUC=+0.075 (+0.077 mean), pooled Stouffer z=5.67 (p=7.1×10⁻⁹). The gain holds on a fixed-pick control: geometric score evaluated at SC's own plurality pick beats SC's vote-share AUC by median +0.060 AUC, ruling out answer-switching as the explanation. Pick agreement between SC and Geo is 94% across questions.

**Channel independence.** A logistic-regression likelihood-ratio test rejects redundancy of C given (G,V) in 18/18 settings (every p≤0.002), of G in 16/18, and of V in only 6/18. Fusion beats the best single channel in 17/18 settings (median gain +0.05 AUC over best single channel; max AUC 0.92).

**Mechanistic window-position finding.** The softmax slope β̂ is most negative at k=−2 in 6/6 benchmark-reasoner combinations; on GPQA Diamond it flips to +22.1 (Gemini) / +23.7 (Claude) at the terminal window — driven entirely by wrong-pick traces (β_wrong = +66.7/+70.1 on GPQA, while MedQA and MMLU-Pro show no such flip). This is a benchmark-conditional effect tied to answer span verbalization, not a universal property.

**Cross-embedder robustness.** Re-running with E5-large-v2 in place of gemini-embedding-001: Geo@4 beats SC@8 in all 6 combinations (median ΔAUC=+0.027, Stouffer z=1.73, p=0.042); C+G+V fusion beats SC@8 in all 6 (median ΔAUC=+0.035, Stouffer z=3.14, p=8.5×10⁻⁴).

**Cost accounting.** At May-2026 API prices, C+G+V@4 reduces per-question cost by 42.6%–54.4% relative to SC@10 across all six closed-set settings.

**Verbalization conditionality.** Standalone AUC(V) correlates with reasoner accuracy across settings (Spearman ρ=+0.71). V adds residual signal in only 6/18 open-ended settings; the largest lift is MMLU-Pro/Claude (+0.112 AUC) with low verbalization dispersion, while GPQA/Claude shows near-zero lift despite high dispersion. V should be gated on setting-level calibration tests rather than used universally.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** The core idea — embedding reasoning traces as sliding windows and measuring convergence to external anchors — is genuinely novel. Prior trajectory work (Lin et al., 2023) embeds clouds of completed responses rather than intermediate windows; kinematic statistics approaches do not use external anchors. The three-channel decomposition (C/G/V) is a useful analytical framing that cleanly separates failure modes. The mechanistic window-position finding (penultimate trough, terminal flip) is a concrete, falsifiable prediction. Novelty is moderate-to-high.

**Rigor.** The experimental design is careful: stratified 5-fold CV, parameters frozen to training folds, explicit falsification checks (the β sign consistency across 6/6 combinations is verified, the binary vs. all-option projection equality in Eq. 5 is checked in Table 18), multiple robustness audits (cross-embedder, cross-judge, jargon-free masking, window-position sweep). The cost accounting is transparently grounded in actual May-2026 API prices. The Stouffer pooling method is correctly applied and cited. Rigor is high for an empirical NLP paper.

**Reproducibility.** Moderately reproducible. The prompt template is given in Appendix G. All mathematical definitions (trajectory construction, softmax form, auxiliary features) are fully specified in Appendices F and H. However, the pipeline requires access to specific commercial APIs (Gemini embeddings, Azure-hosted GPT-5-mini as judge) whose availability and cost may shift. The GPQA Diamond and MedQA datasets have access restrictions. Code and data are not explicitly released (no GitHub/HuggingFace link mentioned).

**Significance.** High practical significance for anyone deploying black-box CoT LLMs in safety-sensitive settings. The finding that spending more on self-consistency samples is dominated by K=4 trajectory geometry plus coverage is directly actionable. The mechanistic window-position result has implications for white-box probing literature (hidden-state probes trained on the penultimate window should outperform terminal-window probes — a cross-paradigm prediction).

**Red flags / cautions:**
- The Coverage channel flows through an LLM judge; judge-family swap experiments (GPT-5-mini → Claude Sonnet 4.6) show C-only AUC shifts by at most ±0.02, but a full multi-judge / human-adjudication / retrieval-grounded equivalence study is left as future work.
- MMLU-Pro results use only N=200 pilot questions, limiting statistical power; the paper correctly notes GPQA/Gemini and MMLU-Pro/Sonnet cells are treated jointly when making generality claims (N=117 is small).
- Verbalization (V) contributes independent signal in only 6/18 open-ended settings — the paper honestly characterizes it as a conditional rather than universal channel, but some readers may over-index on the headline C+G+V result.
- The terminal-window sign flip is GPQA-specific and conditional on benchmarks where closing tokens literally restate the answer span; the paper's own analysis shows MedQA and MMLU-Pro do not exhibit this flip, which means the mechanistic story is more nuanced than a universal late-trace property.
- Evaluation is English-only, text-only, on three benchmarks; generalization to non-English, non-MCQ, or weaker reasoners is explicitly noted as out of scope.

## Strengths

- Clean separation of the geometry signal from self-consistency with a rigorous fixed-pick control that rules out answer-switching as the source of gain.
- The one-parameter softmax is interpretable: its sign is the comparable invariant across embedders and benchmarks, not its raw magnitude.
- Window-position sweep is an elegant out-of-sample test of the penultimate-window prior, not a tuning loop, because the choice was fixed before running the sweep.
- Jargon-free masking experiment (Table 16) rules out lexical overlap as the primary driver of the geometric signal on MedQA.
- Cost analysis is included and grounded in actual API prices, making the practical argument directly actionable.
- Cross-embedder and cross-judge robustness audits significantly strengthen the main claims.

## Limitations

- Coverage channel relies on an LLM judge, introducing judge-side linguistic priors that cannot be fully disentangled from true reasoner difficulty with the robustness checks performed.
- Verbalization channel is a conditional, not universal, confidence signal; its deployment requires per-setting calibration tests.
- Tested on three English benchmarks with two or three frontier commercial reasoners; generalization to open-weight, non-English, or domain-specific models is not demonstrated.
- No within-trace score can recover a hypothesis that was never proposed — a structural ceiling that motivates Coverage but also limits the geometry channel in the open-ended regime.
- Code and data availability not explicitly stated; reproducibility requires access to the same commercial API ecosystem.
- MMLU-Pro and GPQA Diamond sample sizes (N≈117–200) limit power for fine-grained channel-interaction analyses.

## Relevance

本文直接关联LLM在医学推理（MedQA-USMLE）和科学问答（GPQA）中的可靠部署问题，提出的几何置信度信号可低成本地替代自洽采样，对需要在黑箱API环境下进行不确定性量化的应用场景（如生物医学决策支持）具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the penultimate-window geometry signal transfer to open-ended generation tasks (summarization, code generation) where there are no fixed discrete answer anchors?
2. Can the geometric channel be extended to multi-turn dialogue, where the "trajectory" spans multiple reasoning rounds rather than a single CoT?
3. The paper predicts that hidden-state probes on open-weight models should show a sign flip between the penultimate and terminal windows — can this cross-paradigm prediction be verified on Llama or Mistral?
4. How robust is the Coverage channel to judge models with very different calibration characteristics (e.g., an open-weight judge vs. GPT-5-mini)? The two-family swap is encouraging but limited.
5. Does the geometric signal degrade on shorter CoT traces (e.g., from models prompted for concise reasoning), and is there a minimum trace length for the sliding-window approach to be informative?
6. The trajectory-as-question diagnostic (Table 17) shows trajectory tails are consistently stronger answer-bearing representations than raw question embeddings — could this be exploited for retrieval-augmented confidence (fetching similar past trajectories as evidence)?
7. How would the three-channel framework perform in an agentic setting with tool use and multi-step reasoning, where the "trace" includes both reasoning and action tokens?
