Pro-KLShampoo: Projected KL-Shampoo with Whitening Recovered by Orthogonalization

arXiv: 2605.06316

Date: 2026-05-07

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06316

一句话总结： Pro-KLShampoo 通过识别 KL-Shampoo Kronecker 预条件器特征值谱的"尖峰-平坦"结构，将其限制在低秩子空间上做全谱预条件，并用正交化替代标量尾部更新，从而在 GPT-2（124M/350M）与 LLaMA（134M/450M）预训练中以更低显存和更少挂钟时间超越 KL-Shampoo。
TL;DR: Pro-KLShampoo is a new LLM optimizer that improves KL-Shampoo by exploiting a "spike-and-flat" eigenvalue structure empirically observed in Kronecker preconditioners: it maintains a full-rank preconditioner on a tracked low-dimensional subspace (rank r=32–128) and replaces the scalar approximation on the complement with Muon-style orthogonalization. An algebraic identity proves this is not a heuristic—orthogonalization exactly recovers KL-Shampoo's per-direction whitening on the complement. On GPT-2 and LLaMA models (124M–450M), Pro-KLShampoo consistently beats KL-Shampoo at every tested rank in validation loss, peak GPU memory, and wallclock time to match loss levels (up to ~13% wallclock savings on LLaMA-450M). If you care about pushing the efficiency frontier of second-order optimizers for LLM pre-training, this paper is worth reading; if you want a ready-to-deploy solution, the lack of adaptive rank selection and layer-wise α_kl calibration may require additional tuning effort.

Key Contributions

  • Spike-and-flat spectral observation: Empirically demonstrates and theoretically justifies (under a rank-ρ signal-plus-noise gradient model) that KL-Shampoo's Kronecker preconditioners exhibit a spike-and-flat eigenvalue spectrum across all layer types and training stages.
  • Restricted KL objective: Constrains the KL divergence minimization to a parametric family encoding spike-and-flat structure—full spectral resolution on a rank-r tracked subspace, a single scalar on the complement—reducing right-side storage from O(n²) to O(nr) and QR cost from O(n³) to O(r³).
  • Algebraic identity bridging orthogonalization and whitening: Proves that polar(μ_⊥^{-1/2} L^{-1/2} G P_⊥) = L^{-1/2} G U_⊥ (U_⊥^T G^T L^{-1} G U_⊥)^{1/2} U_⊥^T, exactly recovering the complement term of full KL-Shampoo's update.
  • Convergence guarantee: O(1/√T) nonconvex convergence under operator-norm smoothness for the idealized algorithm (Theorem 1).
  • Consistent empirical improvements: Pro-KLShampoo beats KL-Shampoo at every tested rank and scale in validation loss, peak memory, and wallclock time to matched loss level.

Methodology

Background. KL-Shampoo (Lin et al., 2025) reformulates Shampoo's gradient-covariance estimation as KL divergence minimization between a Gaussian with true covariance Σ and a Kronecker-structured approximation L ⊗ R, eliminating the need for step-size grafting. Muon (Jordan et al., 2024) instead orthogonalizes the gradient momentum via polar decomposition, achieving steepest descent under the spectral norm. These two lines had been developed independently.

Spike-and-flat observation. The authors visualize the eigenvalue spectra of both Kronecker factors L and R of KL-Shampoo on GPT-2 (124M) and LLaMA, normalized by the tail mean. A consistent spike-and-flat pattern is observed: a few dominant eigenvalues (the spike) followed by a nearly uniform tail, stable across layers, depths, and training stages. Under a rank-ρ signal-plus-noise gradient model, the KL stationary conditions guarantee this holds exactly (at least n−ρ bottom eigenvalues of R* coincide).

Structural restriction. Pro-KLShampoo restricts R to the parametric family R̂ = USU^T + μ_⊥ P_⊥, where U ∈ St(n,r) is a tracked r-dimensional subspace, S ∈ S^r_{++} captures full spectral structure within the subspace, and μ_⊥ > 0 is a shared scalar for the complement. The restriction is applied only to the right-side preconditioner R (applied to the larger dimension for rectangular layers); L is maintained in full. This reduces right-side storage from O(n²) to O(nr) and QR cost from O(n³) to O(r³).

Optimal subspace (Claim 3). For fixed L, the KL-optimal subspace U* is the size-r subset of eigenvectors of Φ_L := E[G^T L^{-1} G] whose complement has smallest AM/GM ratio (i.e., flattest tail). In practice, the top-r eigenspace is used and tracked via subspace iteration on running estimates.

Orthogonalization on the complement (Section 3.3). The preconditioned gradient decomposes into a subspace term (full KL-Shampoo within U) and a complement term with a scalar right-side approximation. The scalar tail loses information when the bottom eigenvalues are not perfectly uniform. Replacing the scalar complement update with polar(μ_⊥^{-1/2} L^{-1/2} G P_⊥) recovers the algebraic form of full KL-Shampoo's per-direction whitening on the complement (Eq. 12). A mixing weight α_kl is calibrated by matching operator norms.

Overall update rule:

ΔW = −η [α_kl L^{-1/2} G U S^{-1/2} U^T + c_a · polar(μ_⊥^{-1/2} L^{-1/2} G P_⊥)]

where c_a = √max(1, m/n) is an aspect-ratio scaling.

Practical implementation. Adds Nesterov momentum, Newton–Schulz iteration for orthogonalization (avoiding explicit QR on the complement), eigenvalue clipping, and infrequent QR refresh (every τ steps). The EMA coefficient β_2 is shared across all accumulators, following KL-Shampoo convention.

Results & Highlights

  • Validation loss: Pro-KLShampoo achieves the lowest validation loss at every tested rank across GPT-2 124M (3.2745 vs. KL-Shampoo 3.2796), GPT-2 350M (3.0344 vs. 3.0397), LLaMA 134M (3.0335 vs. 3.0504), and LLaMA 450M (2.7453 vs. 2.7650). It also outperforms AdamW, Muon, and Smok-Hop (ablation variant without orthogonalization).
  • Peak GPU memory: Below KL-Shampoo's and on par with Muon's across all configurations.
  • Wallclock savings (time to matched loss): 2.28% (GPT-2 124M), 4.12% (GPT-2 350M), 13.43% (LLaMA 134M), 10.96% (LLaMA 450M). GPT-2 savings stem primarily from faster per-step time (smaller QR); LLaMA savings stem primarily from faster convergence (lower training loss throughout).
  • Ablation findings: Complement-only update is consistently stronger than subspace-only, confirming the orthogonalization on the complement is the more critical component. Smok-Hop (no orthogonalization, scalar tail only) degrades at LLaMA-450M, showing orthogonalization becomes more important at scale.
  • Robustness to rank: Improvement is consistent even at r=32, where the tracked subspace may not capture all spike eigenvalues—orthogonalization on the complement compensates by recovering per-direction whitening regardless of subspace accuracy.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: Genuinely novel in identifying and exploiting the spike-and-flat spectral structure within the KL-Shampoo framework. The algebraic identity (Eq. 12) connecting Muon-style orthogonalization to KL-Shampoo's whitening is a clean theoretical result that bridges two previously separate optimizer families. The structural restriction to the parametric family and the optimality proof for the subspace selection are meaningful theoretical contributions.

Rigor: The theoretical results (Claims 1–3, Theorem 1) are clearly stated with conditions. The convergence guarantee covers only the idealized algorithm; extending it to the practical variant (Newton–Schulz orthogonalization, Nesterov momentum, EMA-based subspace tracking) is explicitly left as future work. The approximation gap bound (Claim 1) via AM/GM ratio is a clean quantitative justification for the spike-and-flat restriction.

Reproducibility: Experiments are run on standard architectures and public datasets. Hyperparameter sweep ranges and architecture details are reported in the appendix. However, no code release is mentioned, and the practical implementation details (Appendix J) are in the supplementary. The dependency on α_kl tuning (swept over {0.005, 0.01, 0.015}) adds a hyperparameter not present in KL-Shampoo, mildly increasing tuning burden.

Significance: Addresses a practically important problem—improving the efficiency-performance tradeoff of second-order optimizers for LLM pre-training. The wallclock savings (up to 13%) and consistent improvements across scales are meaningful for practitioners. The theoretical unification of Muon and Shampoo is conceptually valuable for the optimization research community.

Red flags:

  • Experiments are limited to at most 450M parameters and ~10B tokens; scalability to billion-parameter models is unverified.
  • The rank r is set empirically; no adaptive or layer-wise selection is provided.
  • Per-layer or adaptive calibration of α_kl is left to future work; the current approach intersects a principled range and sweeps within it.
  • The convergence/stability analysis for the EMA-based subspace tracking and preconditioner estimation is left open.
  • Memory comparisons reflect half-precision state for KL-Shampoo and Pro-KLShampoo vs. full-precision for AdamW/Muon, following each method's reference configuration; this makes direct comparison nuanced.

Strengths

  • Clean algebraic identity (Eq. 12) that rigorously justifies the use of orthogonalization on the complement as a non-heuristic replacement for the scalar tail—this is the paper's standout theoretical contribution.
  • Consistent empirical improvements across all four model scales and all tested ranks, with no configuration where Pro-KLShampoo underperforms KL-Shampoo.
  • Memory efficiency (O(nr) vs. O(n²) for right-side preconditioner) with no memory penalty over KL-Shampoo in practice.
  • Well-structured ablation study cleanly isolating the contribution of orthogonalization vs. spike-and-flat decomposition.
  • Convergence guarantee provided for the idealized algorithm under standard nonconvex assumptions.
  • Bridges two active optimizer families (Shampoo/KL-Shampoo and Muon) under a unified theoretical framework.

Limitations

  • No experiments beyond 450M parameters; applicability to 7B+ LLM pre-training is untested.
  • Rank r must be set manually; adaptive or per-layer rank selection is not addressed.
  • Per-layer α_kl calibration is left to future work; current approach sweeps over a small discrete set.
  • Convergence guarantee does not cover the practical implementation (Newton–Schulz, Nesterov momentum, EMA subspace tracking).
  • No publicly released code in the preprint, which limits immediate reproducibility.
  • The spike-and-flat structure is empirically verified but the theoretical motivation (rank-ρ signal-plus-noise model) is idealized; robustness to architectures beyond GPT-2 and LLaMA (e.g., MoE, diffusion models) is unknown.
