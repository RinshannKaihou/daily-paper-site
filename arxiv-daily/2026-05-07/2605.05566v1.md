> **一句话总结：** 在GRPO强化学习训练中，当所有采样答案均失败时，在prompt前拼接随机Lorem Ipsum伪拉丁文可有效触发模型探索正交推理路径，使Qwen3-1.7B/4B和Qwen2.5-Math-7B在数学推理基准上平均提升2.79至6.20分。

> **TL;DR:** LOPE (Lorem Perturbation for Exploration) addresses the "zero-advantage problem" in GRPO-style RLVR training — where all sampled rollouts fail and the training signal collapses — by prepending randomly sampled Lorem Ipsum pseudo-Latin text to prompts before resampling. The key insight is that near-natural, low-perplexity text perturbations shift the model's output distribution enough to unlock orthogonal reasoning trajectories that naive resampling misses, without corrupting the model's comprehension of the actual question. Combined with policy shaping (importance sampling correction) and advantage shaping (normalization over the full G+G' rollout pool), LOPE yields +2.79 to +6.20 average point gains over standard GRPO across three model sizes on mathematical reasoning benchmarks including AIME 2024/2025.

## 核心贡献

- **识别零优势问题的根本局限**：在GRPO训练中，当某问题的全部G次采样均失败时，相对优势归零，导致该问题的训练信号完全丧失；单纯增加采样次数（逻辑空间扰动）对于模型当前策略下"真正困难"的问题收益有限。
- **提出LOPE框架**：在重采样阶段，将随机生成的Lorem Ipsum伪拉丁文序列（100–300 tokens）拼接至原始prompt前，通过提示空间扰动将模型输出分布推离局部推理盆地，解锁正交推理路径。
- **训练信号塑形**：提出策略塑形（Policy Shaping，纠正重采样产生的off-policy偏差）与优势塑形（Advantage Shaping，在G+G'全部rollout上计算归一化统计量，防止低估问题难度），使稀有成功样本获得更强的学习信号。
- **揭示有效扰动的规律**：通过系统性比较，发现有效扰动需满足两个条件：(1) 使用伪拉丁词汇（避免干扰英语推理上下文），(2) 困惑度较低（保持问题语义理解完整）。

## Key Contributions

- **Diagnosis of the zero-advantage bottleneck**: In GRPO training, questions where all G rollouts fail produce zero gradient, wasting computation. Increasing logit-space exploration via higher sampling temperature is insufficient because these questions lie outside the current policy's reasoning basin.
- **LOPE resampling strategy**: Prepend a randomly assembled Lorem Ipsum sequence (100–300 tokens, uniformly sampled from a 63-word pseudo-Latin pool) to the prompt before resampling failed questions. The Lorem-perturbed prompt shifts the output distribution enough to unlock new, correct reasoning trajectories, independently resolving questions that both standard resampling and high-temperature sampling cannot.
- **Training signal shaping**: Two complementary mechanisms address off-policy training artifacts — (1) Policy Shaping amplifies gradient weight for low-probability tokens suppressed by the importance sampling ratio; (2) Advantage Shaping computes advantage normalization over all G+G' responses, restoring authentic advantage values for rare successes (empirically amplifying positive advantages by a factor of 2.1x–5.0x).
- **Principled perturbation recipe**: Systematic ablation over 8 perturbation types reveals that near-natural perturbations (perplexity < ~100 under the target model) using Latin-based vocabulary are most effective; excessively noisy perturbations (e.g., Random Token, perplexity ~4.6×10^5) degrade performance by corrupting question comprehension.

## Methodology

LOPE augments the GRPO training loop with three additions:

**1. Rollout with Perturbation.** After the standard G-rollout phase, any question where all G responses fail triggers a resampling step. A random Lorem Ipsum sequence δ (100–300 tokens from the python-lorem package) is prepended to the original prompt p, yielding δ⊕p. The model then generates G' = 24 additional responses under this perturbed prompt.

**2. Response Regrouping.** From the G' resampled responses, at most N_s = min(c, G-1) correct responses (where c is the count of successes) are selected to replace an equal number of failed responses from the original group, ensuring at least one incorrect response remains (so relative advantages are non-zero). The final training group has size G. To avoid biased context, pseudo-rollouts are constructed by pairing resampled responses o' with the original naive prompt p (not δ⊕p) for training.

**3. Training Signal Shaping.** (a) Policy Shaping: Because resampled responses are off-policy, the importance sampling ratio ρ_{i,t} = π_θ(o'_{i,t}|p,q,o'_{i,<t}) / π_{θ_old}(o'_{i,t}|δ⊕p,q,o'_{i,<t}) can down-weight critical low-probability tokens. A shaping function f(ρ) = ρ/(ρ+γ) (γ=0.1) amplifies gradients for low-probability tokens while capping those for high-probability ones. (b) Advantage Shaping: Normalization statistics (mean and std) are computed over all G+G' responses rather than only the G selected ones, ensuring rare successes receive appropriately large positive advantages.

**4. KL Regularization Removal.** The KL divergence term is dropped from the GRPO objective, as it counteracts the distributional exploration that LOPE is designed to encourage.

Models evaluated: Qwen3-1.7B-Base, Qwen3-4B-Base, Qwen2.5-Math-7B. Training data: OpenR1-Math-46k-8192. Infrastructure: EasyR1 framework, 4–8×A100 80GB GPUs.

## Results & Highlights

**Main benchmark results (average across MATH-500, GSM8K, AMC, AIME 2024, AIME 2025):**

| Model | Base | +GRPO | +Naive Resample | +LOPE (no shaping) | +LOPE (with shaping) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Qwen3-1.7B-Base | 34.90 | 37.03 | 38.16 | 39.79 | **39.82** |
| Qwen3-4B-Base | 39.59 | 49.37 | 48.95 | 52.84 | **53.99** |
| Qwen2.5-Math-7B | 34.90 | 47.68 | 47.52 | 47.32 | **53.88** |

Key findings:
- LOPE with training signal shaping achieves +2.79 (1.7B), +4.62 (4B), and +6.20 (7B) points over standard GRPO.
- Naive prompt resampling sometimes **hurts** performance versus GRPO (e.g., 4B model: 48.95 vs 49.37), likely due to policy drift without KL regularization; LOPE recovers this via orthogonal trajectory diversity acting as implicit regularization.
- Training signal shaping is critical for the 7B model: LOPE without shaping is comparable to GRPO, but with shaping gains +6.20 points.
- LOPE maintains higher question-level resampling success rates (Pass@24) throughout training, broadening the effective training data distribution.
- Perturbation comparison: Among 8 tested methods, the three with lowest perplexity (Lorem Ipsum, Filtered Latin Natural Language, Latin Unigram Model) achieve the best results; Random Token perturbation (highest perplexity, ~4.6×10^5) harms performance.
- Venn diagram analysis on 352 hard questions shows Lorem perturbation independently resolves 50 questions that neither naive nor high-temperature resampling can answer.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Moderate-High):** The core idea — that prepending meaningless Latin text to prompts can unlock different reasoning trajectories — is surprisingly simple and counterintuitive. The connection to the zero-advantage problem in GRPO is well-motivated. However, the use of "meaningless tokens to shift model activations" has been noted in contemporaneous work (Shi et al., 2025; Gan & Isola, 2026), and prompt augmentation for GRPO training has been explored (Lu et al., 2026). The novelty lies primarily in the systematic study of *why* Lorem Ipsum works (perplexity analysis, Latin vs English vocabulary), and in the accompanying training signal shaping mechanisms.

**Rigor (Moderate):** Experiments cover three model sizes and five benchmarks with both standard and hard evaluation sets. The ablation (perturbation type comparison, training shaping components) is thorough. However, all models evaluated are from the Qwen family; broader model diversity (e.g., Llama, Mistral) would strengthen claims of generality. AIME evaluation uses Mean@32, while MATH-500 uses Acc@1 — this inconsistency in evaluation protocol can inflate or obscure differences. The paper does not report variance across training runs, which is important for RL training stability claims.

**Reproducibility (Good):** Code is publicly released. The perturbation method is described precisely (python-lorem package, 100–300 token uniform sampling, specific boundary instruction appended). Hyperparameters (G=8, G'=24, γ=0.1, temperature=1.0 for rollout, 0.6 for eval) are all reported.

**Significance (Moderate-High):** The zero-advantage problem is a real and recognized bottleneck in GRPO-style RL training. LOPE is a low-cost intervention (no additional model training, no architecture change) that consistently improves performance. The insight that perturbation effectiveness correlates with perplexity (not just randomness) is a useful design principle for future work.

**Red Flags:**
- The rationale for *why* Latin specifically outperforms English unigrams (beyond "interference with English reasoning context") is speculative and lacks mechanistic evidence.
- Off-policy training with pseudo-rollouts introduces assumptions (importance sampling correctness) that may break down at larger scales or with more diverse question types.
- The ethics section acknowledges that strong perturbations can induce incoherent or harmful outputs, which is a meaningful limitation for any production use.
- Results on Qwen2.5-Math-7B without training signal shaping actually underperform GRPO, suggesting the approach requires careful tuning and is not uniformly plug-and-play.

## Strengths

- The research question is crisp and practically motivated: how to recover training signal from zero-advantage cases without expensive architectural changes.
- The Lorem Ipsum choice is elegant — it is designed to be structurally language-like but semantically vacuous, making it a principled choice rather than an ad hoc hack.
- The three-level perplexity taxonomy (near-natural / moderately OOD / severely OOD) provides a useful design principle that extends beyond the specific Lorem Ipsum method.
- t-SNE visualization and entropy analysis of question comprehension under different perturbations provide mechanistic insight into why low-perplexity perturbations work.
- The training-time tracking of question-level vs. response-level success rates clearly illustrates LOPE's advantage in broadening the set of trainable questions.
- Openly released code lowers the barrier to adoption and verification.

## Limitations

- All experiments use Qwen-family models; generalizability to other architectures (e.g., Llama, Mistral, Gemma) is unverified.
- Only mathematical reasoning tasks are evaluated; it is unclear whether the approach generalizes to other verifiable reward settings (e.g., code, formal proofs).
- The off-policy pseudo-rollout construction assumes the importance sampling ratio is a sufficient correction; this assumption may introduce bias at larger group sizes or more extreme distribution shifts.
- No variance/error bars are reported across multiple training seeds, making it difficult to assess statistical significance of the reported gains.
- The boundary instruction ("\nPlease reason step by step...") appended to the perturbation sequence is somewhat task-specific and may not generalize cleanly.
- The approach requires choosing perturbation length range (100–300 tokens) and the G' resampling budget; sensitivity to these hyperparameters is not fully characterized.

## Relevance

LOPE直接针对GRPO强化学习训练中的"零优势问题"，对于研究LLM推理能力提升（尤其是基于RLVR范式的数学推理训练）的读者具有很强的实用参考价值；其关于提示空间扰动与探索的分析框架，也对理解LLM输出分布操控机制有启发意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the effectiveness of Lorem Ipsum perturbation generalize beyond mathematical reasoning to other RLVR domains such as code generation, formal theorem proving, or structured reasoning tasks?
2. Is the low-perplexity Latin advantage specific to English-centric models, or does it hold for multilingual models where the "interference" argument would not apply?
3. Can the perplexity-based design principle be used to construct learned perturbations (e.g., a small auxiliary model generating optimally perturbing prefixes) rather than hand-crafted random sequences?
4. How does LOPE interact with curriculum learning or difficulty-aware sampling strategies — would jointly scheduling perturbation intensity with question difficulty improve results further?
5. The paper removes KL regularization together with LOPE; what is the performance impact of keeping KL regularization while using Lorem perturbation, and why does the interaction matter mechanistically?
6. Given that training signal shaping is critical for the 7B model but less so for the 1.7B/4B models, is there a model-scale-dependent phase transition in how off-policy bias affects training?
