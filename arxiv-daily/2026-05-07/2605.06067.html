Normalized Architectures are Natively 4-Bit

["Maxim Fishman", "Brian Chmiel", "Ron Banner", "Daniel Soudry", "Boris Ginsburg"]

arXiv: 2605.06067

Date: 2026-05-07

Tags: ["quantization", "nGPT", "low-precision-training", "NVFP4", "LLM-efficiency"]

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06067

一句话总结： nGPT（超球面归一化Transformer）通过在求和过程中产生弱但一致的正相关信号，将信噪比提升约7 dB，使模型无需随机Hadamard变换等额外技巧即可原生支持NVFP4端到端训练，在1.2B稠密模型和3B/30B混合Mamba-Transformer MoE上均实现接近零的量化相对误差。
TL;DR: This paper argues that 4-bit quantization robustness can be an intrinsic architectural property rather than an algorithmic patch. The authors show that nGPT—a transformer that constrains all weights and activations to the unit hypersphere—achieves stable end-to-end training in NVIDIA's NVFP4 format without randomized Hadamard transforms (RHT), dynamic per-tensor scaling, or mixed-precision exceptions. The key mechanism is not noise reduction: quantization noise magnitude is essentially identical between nGPT and standard GPT. Instead, hypersphere normalization forces the optimizer to distribute signal coherently across thousands of hidden dimensions, creating a ~7 dB higher dot-product SNR that compounds into a 3.5x flatter loss landscape across all layers simultaneously. This advantage grows with model width (still in the linear-growth regime at D=4096), and is empirically confirmed on 1.2B dense and hybrid Mamba-Transformer MoE models up to 3B/30B parameters. The result is a simpler, faster NVFP4 training pipeline with 3.3–3.6x layer-wise speedup over BF16 GPT on Blackwell GPUs.

  • 架构驱动的量化鲁棒性：证明 nGPT 在不使用 RHT、动态 per-tensor scaling 等开销操作的情况下，天然支持稳定的 NVFP4 端到端预训练，打破了"量化鲁棒性只能靠后处理算法补救"的固有认知。

  • 损失曲面展平效应：per-layer 约 7 dB 的 SNR 优势在所有层同时量化时会叠加，使 nGPT 的损失曲面比标准 GPT 平坦 3.5×，并使学习率对量化精度不敏感（BF16 最优超参数可直接迁移到 NVFP4）。
  • 大规模经验验证：在 1.2B 稠密模型（1T tokens）、400M/600M 及 3B/30B 混合 Mamba-Transformer MoE 上均验证了上述优势，覆盖不同规模和架构拓扑。

Key Contributions

  • Architecture-as-quantization-recipe: Demonstrates that nGPT, by constraining representations to the unit hypersphere, is natively "NVFP4-ready" without any of the overhead mechanisms (RHT, dynamic per-tensor scaling) required by standard transformers.
  • Signal-accumulation theory: Provides a formal decomposition showing that nGPT's SNR advantage originates entirely from higher inter-element signal correlation ($\rho_s$ ~14x larger than GPT) during dot-product summation; noise correlation ($\rho_n$) is statistically indistinguishable across architectures.
  • Three-regime width-scaling law: Derives and empirically validates that nGPT's SNR ratio over GPT grows linearly with hidden dimension D in an intermediate regime (755 << D << 10,738), with current D=4096 models sitting in this growth zone—predicting increasing advantage at larger scales.
  • Flat loss landscape and LR transferability: Quantifies that the per-layer SNR gain compounds to a 3.5x flatter loss landscape under simultaneous perturbation of all weights, and that nGPT's optimal learning rate is identical between BF16 and NVFP4 (GPT's optimal LR shifts by 16x).
  • Multi-architecture empirical validation: Confirms benefits on 1.2B dense (1T tokens), 400M/600M hybrid MoE (36B tokens), and 3B/30B hybrid MoE (~500B tokens), achieving ~0% relative error for nGPT-NVFP4 on the largest configuration.

Methodology

The paper follows a layered investigative structure: theoretical analysis supported by controlled experiments, then validated at scale.

nGPT architecture. nGPT modifies standard transformers by: (1) removing RMSNorm/LayerNorm; (2) normalizing all weights along the embedding dimension after each step; (3) replacing residual updates with normalized interpolation ($h \leftarrow \text{Norm}(h + \alpha \cdot (\text{Norm}(\text{ATTN}(h)) - h))$) with learnable per-dimension step sizes $\alpha \in \mathbb{R}^{d_\text{model}}$; (4) normalizing and rescaling query/key vectors; (5) rescaling MLP intermediate activations; (6) removing weight decay and LR warmup.

SNR decomposition analysis. Using a 3.6B-parameter, 12-layer transformer (built on nanoGPT), the authors measure SNR at each stage of matrix multiplication under NVFP4: individual weights, activations, element-wise products, and final dot-product summations. They define a formal decomposition separating signal ($z_s$) from noise ($z_n$) using variance-weighted pairwise Pearson correlation $\bar{\rho}_u$ derived from the variance decomposition of a sum. This allows attributing the SNR gap precisely to signal accumulation rather than noise suppression.

Theoretical SNR model. From the decomposition, the dot-product SNR factorizes as $\text{SNR}_\text{dot} = (z_s / z_n)^2$, and the full SNR formula (Eq. 4) shows $\rho_s$ acts as a multiplier on baseline SNR. The SNR ratio between nGPT and GPT as a function of width D (Eq. 5) yields three regimes analytically, with empirically measured transition points at D=755 and D=10,738.

Training dynamics as driver. A single-layer MLP experiment confirms that the higher correlation in nGPT is a training-induced property: both architectures achieve comparable training loss but nGPT develops significantly higher $\bar{\rho}_s$ throughout training, establishing the hypersphere constraint as the structural cause.

Loss landscape flatness. Gaussian noise is added to all weights simultaneously at scale $\alpha \cdot \|W\|_F / \sqrt{n}$, and validation loss increase is measured as a function of $\alpha$. nGPT degrades 3.5x more slowly than GPT.

Large-scale experiments. All training uses NVIDIA Nemotron pretraining data, AdamW ($\beta_1=0.9, \beta_2=0.95$), WSD scheduler, and Blackwell GPUs (native NVFP4 support). nGPT-NVFP4 runs omit RHT and per-tensor scaling entirely; GPT-NVFP4 keeps them per the state-of-the-art recipe. Relative error is defined as $(L_\text{quantized} - L_\text{BF16}) / L_\text{BF16}$.

Results & Highlights

  • 7 dB dot-product SNR advantage: Consistently across all 12 layers of a 3.6B model; the gap is uniform (not concentrated in specific layers), confirming it is a structural property.
  • Source is signal, not noise: Normalized signal $z_s$ has R²=0.84 with dot-product SNR; normalized noise $z_n$ has R²=0.00. nGPT's $\bar{\rho}_s = 1.32 \times 10^{-3}$ vs. GPT's $9.31 \times 10^{-5}$ (~14x larger). Noise correlation $\bar{\rho}_n \approx 10^{-6}$ for both.
  • 1.2B dense model: nGPT-NVFP4 (no RHT, no scaling) achieves lower relative error than GPT-NVFP4 (with RHT and scaling), and outperforms standard GPT on all downstream tasks (MMLU, PIQA, HellaSwag, Winogrande, GSM8K) in both BF16 and NVFP4 precision.
  • 3B/30B hybrid MoE: nGPT achieves approximately 0% relative quantization error; GPT-NVFP4 maintains ~1–1.5% relative error with the full overhead pipeline.
  • LR insensitivity: nGPT achieves near-identical validation BPB across two orders of magnitude of learning rates in both BF16 and NVFP4. GPT's NVFP4-optimal LR is 16x higher than its BF16 optimum.
  • 3.3–3.6x throughput speedup at large hidden sizes on Blackwell GB200, attributed to removing RHT/scaling overhead and moving expensive GEMMs to NVFP4 tensor cores.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: The central insight—that quantization robustness can be architectural rather than algorithmic—is genuinely novel and well-framed. The specific attribution to signal coherence (as opposed to noise reduction) via the SNR decomposition is a clear and non-obvious contribution that reframes the problem. However, nGPT itself is a prior work (ICLR 2025); this paper's contribution is the low-precision analysis and empirical validation, not the architecture.

Rigor: The theoretical framework is tight. The three-regime scaling derivation uses only one empirically measured parameter ($\bar{\rho}_s$) and fits data across two orders of magnitude in D. The explicit null result for noise (R²=0.00) strengthens the causal claim. The training dynamics experiment (single-layer MLP achieving comparable loss but divergent correlation) is a clean ablation. However, the theoretical analysis applies primarily to matrix multiplications; the behavior of attention softmax, normalization operations, and MLP nonlinearities under quantization is not separately analyzed.

Reproducibility: Detailed hyperparameter tables are provided for all model sizes. The code is available (anonymized for preprint). Training uses publicly available Nemotron data and standard optimizers. Reproduction at the 3B/30B scale requires Blackwell GPU access, which limits independent verification.

Significance: High practical significance for anyone training large models with NVFP4. Removing RHT and per-tensor scaling is non-trivial overhead, and demonstrating this can be eliminated via architecture choice—while simultaneously improving quality—is valuable. The LR transferability result (no need for separate NVFP4 hyperparameter sweeps) is a concrete practical benefit.

Red flags: (1) All experiments use NVIDIA hardware and NVIDIA's data (Nemotron); independent reproduction on other hardware stacks would strengthen claims. (2) The 3B/30B experiment runs only ~500B tokens vs. 20T+ for SOTA production models; longer training may reveal stability issues not captured here. (3) nGPT has known convergence differences in BF16 (slightly slower initial convergence visible in Fig. 10); the paper does not fully address whether this gap closes or persists at scale. (4) The paper notes the source of positive correlation in nGPT "remains a compelling direction for future research"—the mechanism is empirically confirmed but not fully explained theoretically.

Strengths

  • Clean separation of two competing hypotheses (noise suppression vs. signal accumulation) with falsifiable evidence for each.
  • Theoretical framework with a single free parameter that accurately predicts SNR scaling across two orders of magnitude.
  • Practical results spanning multiple architectures and scales, not just a toy demonstration.
  • Concrete engineering benefits: removal of RHT and per-tensor scaling overhead, LR hyperparameter transferability across precisions, 3.3–3.6x throughput gain.
  • Honest presentation of the 3B/30B BF16 training curve, which shows nGPT converging more slowly initially despite reaching similar final loss.

---

Limitations

  • Analysis of SNR mechanism is conducted primarily on a 3.6B, D=4096 model; the claim that D=4096 sits in "Regime II" (linear SNR growth) means current production models at D=8192+ may behave differently than projected.
  • The 500B token training horizon for 3B/30B experiments is substantially shorter than SOTA production runs; long-horizon stability is unverified.
  • All infrastructure (hardware, data, NVFP4 toolchain) is NVIDIA-internal; claims about acceleration and stability may not transfer to other quantization formats (e.g., INT4, FP8) or hardware.
  • The theoretical model assumes element-wise product correlations are constant across partial sums (empirically validated but not proven); deviations at unusual data distributions or after fine-tuning are unexplored.
  • No ablation isolating which specific nGPT modifications (weight normalization vs. activation normalization vs. normalized residuals) contribute most to the SNR advantage.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Which specific nGPT modifications are most responsible for the increased signal correlation—weight normalization, activation normalization, or the normalized residual update? An ablation isolating each component would clarify the minimal necessary change.
  2. Does the SNR advantage hold for attention weights (softmax outputs) and not just linear layer dot products? The analysis focuses on GEMMs but attention patterns may behave differently.
  3. Can the positive signal correlation mechanism be transplanted into standard transformers via targeted normalization of specific layers, achieving a middle ground between full nGPT overhead and standard GPT fragility?
  4. How does the SNR advantage and quantization robustness scale beyond D=10,738 (Regime III saturation)? At 405B-scale models with very large hidden dimensions, does the benefit truly plateau at ~14.2x as predicted?
  5. Does the architectural robustness extend to other low-precision formats (INT4, FP8, microscaling formats) or is it specific to the floating-point representational properties of NVFP4?
  6. What is the training data distribution sensitivity of the signal correlation mechanism? If domain-specific fine-tuning data has different statistics, does $\bar{\rho}_s$ remain elevated or degrade toward GPT levels?
