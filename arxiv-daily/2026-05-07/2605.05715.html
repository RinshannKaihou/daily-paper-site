Decodable but Not Corrected by Fixed Residual-Stream Linear Steering: Evidence from Medical LLM Failure Regimes

arXiv: 2605.05715

Date: 2026-05-07

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05715

一句话总结： 在医疗QA任务中，LLM的"过度思考"失败模式在隐藏状态中线性可解码（71.6%准确率），但29种固定线性激活引导配置均无法利用该信号纠正错误（Δ≈0），根本原因是失败模式方向与任务关键计算方向高度纠缠（特异性比仅为0.119）。
TL;DR: This paper investigates whether linearly decodable failure signals in LLM hidden states can be exploited to correct those failures. The answer, for a well-defined failure mode called "Overthinking" (OT) in medical multiple-choice QA, is no. OT—where a model answers correctly under resampling but fails via extended chain-of-thought—is reliably classifiable from hidden states (71.6% balanced accuracy, p ≈ 10⁻¹⁶). Yet five families of fixed linear steering (29 configurations, n = 1,273 test questions on Llama-3.1-8B) all produce accuracy deltas near zero. The core explanation is representational entanglement: the OT-specific direction shares 88% of its variance with task-critical computation directions, so any additive perturbation that targets OT inevitably disrupts correct reasoning. The same decodable structure does, however, support post-generation selective abstention (AUROC 0.610 on held-out test), outperforming all five single-pass uncertainty baselines tested. This is a technically rigorous negative result with clear mechanistic characterization of why steering fails, not just documentation that it does.

核心贡献

  • 识别并验证"过度思考"（Overthinking, OT）行为区间：在医疗QA中定义并验证了一个稳健的失败模式——模型在重采样下能答对但在扩展推理链中答错——具有94%专家标注一致率和跨架构泛化性。
  • 揭示分类-纠正鸿沟（classification-correction gap）：证明了隐藏状态线性可解码（71.6%）与固定线性引导无法纠正（所有29配置Δ≈0）之间的系统性鸿沟，并在Qwen2.5-7B（9配置）及MMLU-STEM跨领域数据上重复验证。
  • 提供表征纠缠的几何证据：OT方向的特异性比仅为0.119（88%信号与任务相关方向共享），LEACE概念擦除损害准确率（-3.6pp，p=0.01），而随机等秩方向不产生相同效果，证实纠缠是因果性而非相关性的。
  • 确立可解码性在选择性弃权中的操作价值：同一结构支持事后生成可靠性估计（层21探针，测试AUROC=0.610），优于所有5种单次前向传播不确定性基线（ΔAUROC=0.041，p=0.009）。
  • 提供预干预诊断框架：归纳四个可预测引导成功与否的几何相关量（特异性比、类内方差、跨架构稳定性、干预层处跨域子空间对齐度），并将拒绝回答引导作为阳性对照进行定量比较（拒绝特异性0.999 vs. OT特异性≤0.21）。

Key Contributions

  • Behavioral regime identification and validation: Defines "Overthinking" (OT) as a sampling-level behavioral label—correct under majority vote (≥60% of traces) but incorrect in extended reasoning (>200 tokens)—and validates it with 94% expert agreement, 100% within-question purity, and cross-architecture stability (Qwen2.5-7B) and cross-domain transfer (MMLU-STEM).
  • Empirical classification-correction gap: Demonstrates that a linearly decodable signal (71.6% balanced accuracy for binary OT vs. non-OT, p ≈ 10⁻¹⁶) is insufficient for correction under five fixed linear steering families (29 configurations total, all Δ ≈ 0), with the gap replicated cross-architecture and cross-domain.
  • Geometric characterization of representational entanglement: Quantifies entanglement via a custom specificity ratio (0.119 for OT, vs. 0.999 for refusal); shows LEACE concept erasure produces direction-specific accuracy damage (−3.6pp vs. +0.3pp for random directions, p = 0.01), confirming causal co-occurrence between the OT representation and task-critical computation.
  • Selective abstention as operational use case: Shows that the decodable structure supports reliable post-generation abstention (held-out test AUROC = 0.610) exceeding all tested single-pass uncertainty baselines, providing a concrete positive application of the decodable structure even when steering fails.
  • Pre-intervention diagnostic framework: Proposes four geometric pre-checks (specificity ratio, within-class variance relative to inter-centroid gap, cross-architecture stability, cross-domain subspace alignment at intervention layers) as candidate indicators of steerability, grounded in a quantitative comparison against a positive-control refusal direction.

Methodology

Failure regime annotation. MedQA (10,178 training, 1,273 test questions) is annotated using 10 traces per question at temperature 0.8. OT is labeled heuristically (≥60% correct, incorrect traces >200 tokens) and validated by two blinded 4th-year clinical students (94% expert-expert agreement on OT, κ = 0.61 three-way). Sub-classification into Knowledge Deficit (KD, factual errors) and Reasoning Chain Break (RCB, valid facts / invalid logic) uses Claude Haiku as judge (κ = 0.30 LLM, κ = 0.61 expert).

Geometric analysis. Hidden states are extracted from Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct across all 32 layers for 12,000 traces (2,000 per failure mode + 6,000 matched correct). Contrastive vectors are computed as the normalized mean difference between correct and failure-mode traces at each layer. Key geometric quantities include: (i) pairwise cosine similarity between mode contrastive vectors (mean 0.820 for Llama), (ii) a specificity ratio measuring mode-exclusive vs. shared variance (0.119 for OT), (iii) a permutation null test (10,000 label shuffles) confirming the anomalously low specificity, and (iv) a probe vs. contrastive vector dissociation analysis.

Activation steering experiments. Five families of fixed linear steering are tested on the full 1,273-question test set using forward hooks adding α·v to the residual stream at target layers:

  1. Contrastive steering (mode-specific or uniform shared component; α ∈ {0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 3.0})
  2. Probe-guided steering (using probe weight vectors)
  3. Multi-layer steering (simultaneous application at 1, 3, or 5 layers around peak layer)
  4. Rank-k subspace steering (k ∈ {1, 3, 5} via SVD of stacked direction matrices)
  5. Prompt-end steering (contrastive vectors from prompt-end hidden states, near-orthogonal to last-token directions)

Additionally, LEACE concept erasure and non-linear MLP steering (4096→64→4096 residual adapter) are tested. TOST equivalence testing (within ±2.5pp) is used as the non-inferiority criterion.

Selective abstention. A correctness probe (layer 21, selected via held-out AUROC from a 32-layer scan) is evaluated on a 70/30 split for post-generation reliability estimation, benchmarked against five single-forward-pass uncertainty baselines.

Controls and validation. Positive control: refusal steering on borderline safety prompts (sign test p = 0.008). Cross-architecture: full analysis replicated on Qwen2.5-7B (28 layers, 3584 hidden dim). Cross-domain: OT pipeline replicated on MMLU-STEM (300 questions, 18 subjects). Six robustness checks address label leakage, length confounds, threshold sensitivity, and random artifacts.

Results & Highlights

Classification. Binary OT vs. non-OT achieves 71.6% balanced accuracy (AUROC 0.672, p ≈ 10⁻¹⁶), peaking at layer 17. Three-way KD/RCB/OT classification reaches 51.5% (chance 33.3%). Cross-architecture replication on Qwen2.5-7B yields 46.9% three-way accuracy (chance 33.3%), confirming the signal generalizes despite substantially different hidden geometry (Qwen specificity ratio 0.414 vs. Llama 0.119).

Steering null. Mode-specific contrastive steering: Δ = −0.2pp (95% CI: [−2.8, +2.4]pp), TOST-equivalent within ±2.5pp. Uniform shared steering: Δ = −12.1pp (severely harmful). Probe-guided steering: Δ ≈ −1.4 to −1.7pp. Multi-layer steering: Δ ≈ 0. Prompt-end steering: Δ ≈ 0 across all four conditions. Qwen cross-architecture steering: all nine configurations Δ ∈ [−0.9, +0.8]pp (all p > 0.05). MMLU-STEM cross-domain: targeted Δ = 0pp, uniform Δ = −7.0pp. The joint probability of observing all 29 configurations near zero if the true effect were ≥2.5pp is < 10⁻¹⁵.

Concept erasure. LEACE erasure of the OT direction reduces accuracy by −3.6pp (p = 0.010, McNemar; 131 corrections vs. 177 damages, ratio 1.4:1). Ten random directions of equal rank produce Δ_rand = +0.3pp (σ = 1.8pp), confirming the damage is direction-specific (Δ_OT − Δ_rand = −3.9pp, p < 0.02).

Non-linear MLP. A learned 4096→64→4096 residual adapter achieves Δ = +2.8pp (p = 0.025, McNemar; 140 corrections, 104 damages), with 50.6% centroid distance reduction—providing preliminary evidence that entanglement is partial rather than absolute, and that learned non-linear methods can begin to close the gap.

Selective abstention. Correctness probe (layer 21) achieves held-out test AUROC = 0.610, exceeding all five single-pass uncertainty baselines (best baseline AUROC = 0.569; ΔAUROC = 0.041, p = 0.009). At 60% coverage, accuracy gain is +3.6pp; at 70% coverage, +5.5pp on the test set. Best-of-N probe selection at k=3 achieves 70.6% vs. MV@3's 69.2% (+1.4pp) at identical generation cost.

Comparison with self-consistency. MV@10 achieves 72.7% overall accuracy (+7.4pp, p < 10⁻¹⁰), confirming OT is correctable at 10× inference cost. The probe offers a single-pass alternative competitive with BoN@3 (vs. MV@3).

Authors & Credibility

Ming Liu (Amazon, mlliuz@amazon.com) is the sole author. The affiliation is an industrial research lab (Amazon), not a university lab, which is unusual for this type of mechanistic interpretability work but does not diminish the technical quality. The paper is carefully situated in a dense and rapidly evolving literature (Wu et al. 2025 AxBench, Basu et al. 2026, Wang et al. 2026, Mishra et al. 2026, Nadaf 2026) and explicitly acknowledges concurrent work documenting the same gap. The study uses publicly available models (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct) and a public benchmark (MedQA), and promises code and processed annotations upon publication. The scale of the annotation effort (34,532–39,862 traces, domain expert validation on n=500, cross-LLM validation) and the systematic multi-configuration steering sweep are substantial for a single-author paper; this warrants some scrutiny about undisclosed resources.

Critical Assessment

Novelty. The central negative result—decodability does not imply steerability—is now well-documented across concurrent 2025–2026 works (Wu et al., Basu et al., Sanyal et al., Cox et al., Wang et al.). The paper's distinct contribution is the mechanistic geometric characterization (specificity ratio quantification, LEACE damage directionality, cross-architecture geometry comparison) and the operational pivot to selective abstention. The Overthinking regime as a controlled testbed is novel and well-motivated. The 4.7× specificity gap between refusal and OT as a quantitative steering predictor is a genuinely useful diagnostic insight.

Rigor. Generally high. The use of TOST equivalence testing (not just null hypothesis tests) is commendable and unusual in this literature. The joint probability argument for the null across 29 configurations is strong. The six robustness checks for the OT taxonomy are thorough. The refusal steering positive control validates the infrastructure. Potential concern: annotation uses temperature 0.8 but steering evaluation uses temperature 0.1—the paper investigates this (Appendix K) and finds same-temperature steering produces larger damage (Δ = −6.7pp), directly ruling out temperature mismatch as confound; this is appropriately handled.

Reproducibility. Good in principle: public models, public benchmark, all hyperparameters reported, code promised upon publication. The use of do_sample=True with T=0.1 introduces run-to-run variability (baseline range 64.0–67.4%), and all deltas are computed within-run against paired baselines, which is the correct approach. Full-test-set evaluation (n=1,273) takes ~4 hours per configuration on A10G GPUs, making replication feasible but not trivial.

Significance. High for the mechanistic interpretability and activation steering communities. The paper provides the clearest quantitative account to date of why linear steering fails on multi-step reasoning tasks, as opposed to binary behavioral properties. The pre-intervention diagnostics (specificity ratio, subspace alignment) are actionable and could guide future work. The abstention application is practically relevant for medical AI reliability.

Red flags. (1) Single author for a 24-page paper with substantial annotation effort—the domain expert validation (two 4th-year clinical students) and LLM annotation pipeline are described but the exact institutional setup and annotation cost are not detailed. (2) KD/RCB boundary has only κ = 0.30 LLM agreement and κ = 0.30 expert-expert, making fine-grained claims about these sub-categories less reliable than OT claims; the paper appropriately focuses strongest claims on the binary OT split. (3) The positive result (non-linear MLP, Δ = +2.8pp) is statistically significant but modest, and the full LEACE oblique projector is untested. (4) Only 7–8B models evaluated; behavior at larger scales is explicitly flagged as uncertain.

Strengths

  • Principled use of TOST equivalence testing alongside standard null hypothesis tests, setting a higher bar for the negative result claim
  • Systematic geometric analysis with a custom specificity ratio metric, permutation null, and quantitative comparison to a positive control (refusal direction, specificity 0.999)
  • Cross-architecture (Qwen2.5-7B), cross-domain (MMLU-STEM), and cross-temperature robustness checks all yield consistent results
  • Constructive pivot: the same structure that fails for steering succeeds for selective abstention, giving the paper both a negative and a positive operational conclusion
  • The 4.7× specificity gap as a quantitative predictor of steerability is a concrete, actionable finding for future work
  • Honest and detailed limitations section covering scale, scope, intervention families, and annotation validity

Limitations

  • Evaluations restricted to 7–8B parameter instruction-tuned models on MedQA and MMLU-STEM; generalizability to larger models, other domains, and base models is uncertain
  • Fixed linear intervention family only covers 29 configurations across 5 families; full oblique LEACE, DAS, ReFT, K-CAST, path patching, and parameter-efficient fine-tuning remain untested
  • OT prevalence (~31% for Llama at 60% threshold) is definitionally tied to the correct-rate threshold; higher-accuracy larger models may show lower OT prevalence, reducing the practical scope
  • The binary OT/non-OT signal is generated during extended reasoning (last-token at generation end), making it causally post-hoc rather than pre-generatively actionable for steering
  • KD/RCB sub-classification has moderate annotator agreement (κ ≈ 0.30), limiting confidence in fine-grained mechanistic claims about error subtypes
  • Concept erasure experiments on Qwen were not conducted, leaving open whether Qwen's distinct geometry (higher specificity 0.414) reflects a qualitatively different encoding regime

Relevance

本文对研究LLM可解释性与推理可靠性的学者高度相关，特别是关注激活引导方法局限性的研究者——文章精确量化了为何线性引导在多步推理任务中失效，并提出了以选择性弃权为替代方案的实用路径，对医疗AI安全部署具有直接参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the specificity ratio (< 0.21 for OT, 0.999 for refusal) hold as a reliable pre-intervention predictor across other failure modes and domains, or is it specific to the multi-step reasoning / binary behavioral property split?
  2. Can learned non-linear methods (DAS, ReFT, K-CAST) fully close the classification-correction gap, or does the within-class variance fundamentally limit any fixed-direction intervention regardless of linearity?
  3. What happens to OT prevalence and the geometry of the failure-mode representations at 70B+ parameter scales, where superposition may intensify with more features competing for shared directions?
  4. Is the decodable OT signal causally upstream of the failure—i.e., does it reflect a pre-committed reasoning path that could in principle be intercepted at earlier generation tokens—or is it an epiphenomenon of the extended incorrect reasoning itself?
  5. Can the selectivity ratio framework be extended to identify domains or task types where linear steering should succeed before deployment, enabling researchers to scope activation steering applications appropriately?
  6. The probe for selective abstention generalizes cross-temperature (AUROC 0.610 on T=0.1 test from T=0.8 training), but the gap (0.716 → 0.610) is substantial—can cross-temperature-robust probes be trained, and would they improve reliability estimation in near-greedy deployment settings?
