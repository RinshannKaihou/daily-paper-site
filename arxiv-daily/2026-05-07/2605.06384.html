MinMax Recurrent Neural Cascades

arXiv: 2605.06384

Date: 2026-05-07

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06384

一句话总结： 本文提出用 MinMax 代数（以 max 替代加法、以 min 替代乘法）驱动的循环网络 MinMax RNC，在理论上实现了所有正则语言的表达能力、对数并行时间复杂度以及梯度不消失，并在合成序列任务上完美击败 Mamba、mLSTM、sLSTM 等 SOTA 模型，同时 127M 参数的语言模型性能与 GPT-2 相当。
TL;DR: MinMax Recurrent Neural Cascades replace the standard linear recurrence (matrix-vector multiply + add) with a MinMax algebraic recurrence (matrix-min-vector + max) to obtain a provably expressive, parallelisable, gradient-stable architecture. The authors prove that MinMax RNCs can recognise exactly all regular languages (or all star-free languages at state-degree one), evaluate in O(log T) parallel depth, have bounded states/activations, and exhibit non-vanishing gradients. Empirically, a state-degree-one MinMax RNC achieves 100% accuracy on three synthetic benchmarks (Latching, Sequences, InductionHeads) where Mamba and xLSTM fail, and a 127M-parameter model matches GPT-2's loss on OpenWebText. This is an especially compelling result for practitioners who care about both theoretical guarantees and long-range dependency handling.

核心贡献

  • 新型循环机制： 将标准线性递推 $x_t = A_t x_{t-1} + b_t$ 替换为 MinMax 递推 $x_t = (A_t \otimes x_{t-1}) \oplus b_t$（$\oplus = \max$，$\otimes = \min$），从根本上避免了梯度消失/爆炸问题。
  • 精确形式表达力： 证明 MinMax RNC 可以实现所有正则函数（全状态维度时），限定状态度为 1 时精确覆盖所有无群正则函数（star-free 语言），与有限内存系统的理论上限吻合。
  • 对数并行复杂度： 利用 MinMax 代数的结合律与分配律，通过 parallel scan 实现深度 $O(\log T \cdot \log N)$、工作量 $O(TN^3)$ 的并行求值（$T$ 为序列长度，$N$ 为状态维度）。
  • 稳定性与梯度保证： 证明状态和激活值一致有界（BIBS/BIBO 稳定性），损失梯度几乎处处存在且有界，标量情形下梯度恒为 1（非消失）。
  • 实证验证： 在 Latching($2^n$)、Sequences($n$)、InductionHeads(16/30) 三项合成基准上获得 100% 准确率，并在 OpenWebText 上以 127M 参数复现 GPT-2 的验证损失（3.12）。

Key Contributions

  • Novel recurrence operator: Replaces addition with max ($\oplus$) and multiplication with min ($\otimes$) in the state update, yielding a piecewise-linear operator with constant unit norm — neither contracting nor expanding — which directly prevents vanishing and exploding gradients.
  • Exact formal expressivity: Proves MinMax RNCs implement exactly all regular functions (full state degree), and exactly the group-free/star-free regular functions when restricted to state degree one. This is the tightest expressivity characterisation for any RNN architecture to date.
  • Efficient parallel evaluation: The MinMax operator is associative and distributive, enabling parallel scan. Fixed-size MinMax RNCs evaluate in $O(\log T)$ time given $\Theta(T)$ processors, on par with linear recurrence models like Mamba.
  • Provable stability and gradient bounds: States and outputs are uniformly bounded for bounded inputs (Theorem 7). Loss gradients exist almost everywhere and are bounded (Theorem 8). Scalar-state gradients are identically 1 regardless of sequence length (Theorem 9).
  • Strong empirical performance: Achieves 100% evaluation accuracy on all three synthetic benchmarks, outperforming Mamba (54.3% on Latching, 3.5% on InductionHeads(30)), xLSTM-L, mLSTM, and sLSTM. A 127M-parameter model matches GPT-2 on OpenWebText.

Methodology

MinMax Algebra. The paper defines two operators on reals: $a \oplus b := \max(a,b)$ and $a \odot b := \min(a,b)$. These lift component-wise to vectors and matrices, with matrix "multiplication" $C = A \otimes B$ defined as $C_{i,j} = \bigoplus_{k} A_{i,k} \odot B_{k,j}$, analogous to tropical algebra.

MinMax Recurrent Unit. A MinMax Recurrent Unit is a dynamical system with state update:

$$x_t = (R(u_t) \otimes x_{t-1}) \oplus s(u_t)$$

where $R: U \to \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ is the "reset function" and $s: U \to \mathbb{R}^n$ is the "set function," both parameterised by MLPs. The key insight is that for fixed scalar $r, s$, the map $x \mapsto (r \otimes x) \oplus s$ is a piecewise-linear function with norm 1 — it either selects (max) or holds (min), never amplifying or shrinking the state by more than 1 in absolute value.

MinMax Recurrent Neural Cascade (MinMax RNC). A MinMax RNC is a cascade of MinMax Recurrent Layers: each layer consists of multiple MinMax Recurrent Neurons arranged in parallel, followed by a normalised MLP with residual connections. State degree is the maximum state dimension of any neuron. The architecture is implemented in PyTorch with pre-norm layers and residual connections following the xLSTM design blueprint.

Expressivity Analysis. The paper uses Algebraic Automata Theory (AAT) and Metric Automata Theory (MAT) to characterise expressivity. The key theorems show that (i) every identity-reset semiautomaton can be realised by a single MinMax Neuron of state dimension one, and (ii) every permutation semiautomaton of degree $n$ can be realised by a MinMax Neuron of state dimension $n$. By the Krohn-Rhodes theorem, any semiautomaton decomposes into cascades of these types, establishing the equivalence with all regular functions.

Parallel Evaluation. The monoid $\langle R_t, s_t \rangle$ under the operator $(R,s) \circ (R',s') = (R' \otimes R,\ (R' \otimes s) \oplus s')$ is associative. The identity element is $(E, E)$ where $E$ is built from scalar extreme values. This structure supports parallel scan (prefix products), giving $O(\log T)$ depth.

Gradient Analysis. The paper uses the theory of PAP (piecewise analytic under analytic partition) functions to show the intensional derivative (computed by PyTorch's autograd via the chain rule) exists almost everywhere, coincides with the true gradient almost everywhere, and is bounded. For scalar state neurons with $R(u) = u$ and $s(u) = -u$, the gradient is identically 1 everywhere.

Empirical Setup. Three synthetic benchmarks: Latching($n$) tests long-term memory retention; Sequences($n$) tests pattern matching with restarts; InductionHeads($n$) tests associative recall. Training on sequences up to length 512, validation up to 2048, evaluation up to 1M tokens. Architecture comparisons against Mamba, mLSTM, sLSTM, xLSTM-L at comparable sizes (~125k parameters for synthetic tasks). Language model experiment uses 127M parameters, 12 layers, $d_\text{model} = 728$, trained on OpenWebText.

Results & Highlights

Synthetic benchmarks (Table 1):

  • MinMax achieves 100% accuracy on all tasks: Latching($2^n$, n=2–9), Sequences($n$, n=2–9), InductionHeads(16), InductionHeads(30).
  • Mamba reaches 54.3% on Latching, 100% on Sequences, 100%/3.5% on InductionHeads — its InductionHeads(30) failure is severe (3.5% vs MinMax's 100%).
  • xLSTM-L achieves 8.2%/46.5%/6.7%/3.1% — consistently poor on these length-generalisation tasks.
  • mLSTM achieves 7.4%/44.5%/6.5%/3.0% — similar failure profile to xLSTM-L.
  • sLSTM achieves 28.2%/46.5%/6.2%/3.0%.
  • MinMax maintains 100% when evaluated on sequences up to 1M tokens (generalization from length 512 training).

Language modeling:

  • A 127M-parameter MinMax RNC achieves validation loss of 3.12 on OpenWebText, matching the loss reported by nanoGPT for the released GPT-2 124M checkpoint.
  • This demonstrates that MinMax recurrence scales to real-world language modeling without sacrificing competitive performance.

Gradient behavior: The non-vanishing gradient property (gradient = 1 for scalar neurons) is unique — no existing linear RNN architecture achieves this, as they all require contracting state updates (norm < 1) for stability, which necessarily causes gradient decay over long sequences.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: High. Replacing linear recurrence with MinMax algebra as the computational primitive is genuinely new. The connection between MinMax recurrence and algebraic automata theory (via identity-reset and permutation semiautomata) provides a cleaner theoretical picture than previous expressivity analyses of linear RNNs, which relied on norm constraints creating the η-finiteness complications that the paper directly addresses. The non-vanishing gradient proof is a meaningful theoretical contribution.

Rigor: Strong for the theoretical claims. The proof architecture is well-structured, using established tools (PAP functions, Krohn-Rhodes decomposition, parallel scan via monoid structure). The full proofs in the appendix span 88 pages, suggesting serious mathematical effort. The complexity analysis for parallel evaluation is clean and follows directly from the algebraic structure. One caveat: the gradient non-vanishing result (Theorem 9, Corollary 3) is proven for specific scalar-state neurons; the paper is less explicit about whether this translates quantitatively to full multi-dimensional MinMax RNCs in practice.

Reproducibility: Moderate. The architecture is described clearly (PyTorch implementation, pre-norm layers, Adam optimizer, specific learning rate and batch size). However, code is not publicly released. The synthetic benchmarks are described in sufficient detail to reimplement, though exact hyperparameter sweeps are noted only at a high level. The GPT-2-scale experiment follows nanoGPT conventions, which aids reproducibility.

Significance: Potentially high. If the theoretical and empirical claims hold up under community scrutiny, MinMax RNCs offer a principled alternative to linear recurrence that simultaneously matches linear RNNs on parallelism, surpasses them on formal expressivity, and avoids the vanishing gradient problem without gating. The 100% generalization to length 1M from length 512 training on InductionHeads(30) — where Mamba achieves only 3.5% — is a particularly striking empirical result.

Red flags:

  • Single-author preprint covering an unusually broad set of theoretical and empirical claims simultaneously.
  • The InductionHeads result for Mamba (3.5%) contradicts some published findings where Mamba performs well on induction tasks at appropriate batch size; the paper acknowledges batch size sensitivity (bs=8 vs bs=64) and attempts to control for it, but this warrants independent verification.
  • The language model experiment uses only one seed and one architecture configuration; variance and ablation studies are not reported.
  • No comparison to other recurrence-free or hybrid architectures (e.g., RetNet, RWKV, Hyena) in the LM experiment.
  • The gradient "constant = 1" result applies to scalar states; how this behaves in multi-dimensional states during actual training is not empirically verified (loss curves are provided but not gradient norm curves).

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical framework grounding expressivity, complexity, stability, and gradient properties in a single algebraic structure.
  • The MinMax recurrence is simple to implement and directly amenable to parallel scan without any additional structural constraints on parameter matrices.
  • Exact expressivity characterisation (not just lower bounds): MinMax RNCs implement exactly the regular functions, which is a stronger result than most prior work.
  • Non-vanishing gradient is a principled solution to a core problem in recurrent learning, not a heuristic workaround like gating.
  • Empirical results on synthetic tasks are definitive rather than marginal, with perfect accuracy maintained to 1M-length sequences.
  • The 127M-parameter LM result shows the approach is not limited to toy tasks.

---

Limitations

  • State degree one MinMax RNCs are limited to star-free (group-free) languages; recognising arbitrary regular languages requires higher state degrees with the associated increase in computational cost.
  • The parallel evaluation advantage requires enough processors ($\Theta(T)$); in memory-constrained settings, the $O(TN^3)$ work may be prohibitive compared to linear recurrence's $O(TN^2)$.
  • The non-vanishing gradient guarantee is theoretically established for scalar neurons but not empirically demonstrated for full-size models (gradient norms not reported).
  • No ablation studies on the contribution of individual components (number of layers, state degree, normalisation, residual connections).
  • Language model evaluation is limited to OpenWebText with a single run; no downstream task evaluation is reported.
  • The relationship between MinMax recurrence and content-based (input-dependent) gating mechanisms (like Mamba's selective state spaces) is not discussed; it is unclear whether MinMax can incorporate input-dependent selection in a principled way.
  • Single-author work with no public code; reproducibility depends entirely on paper descriptions.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does MinMax recurrence behave when combined with input-dependent (selective) gating, as in Mamba's SSM formulation? Can the non-vanishing gradient and formal expressivity results be preserved?
  2. Can MinMax RNCs be extended to continuous-time (neural ODE / state-space) formulations, and do the stability guarantees carry over?
  3. The non-vanishing gradient is proven for scalar states; do gradient norms remain near 1 empirically during training for multi-dimensional states, and does this translate to better learning on tasks requiring very long-range dependencies (e.g., >100K tokens)?
  4. What is the practical wall-clock time comparison of MinMax parallel scan vs. linear recurrence parallel scan on modern GPU hardware, given the $O(TN^3)$ work for MinMax vs. $O(TN^2)$ for linear?
  5. Is there a natural extension of MinMax algebra to complex numbers or other semirings that could capture a broader class of languages while retaining the parallelism and gradient properties?
  6. How does the model perform on standard LM benchmarks (LAMBADA, HellaSwag, etc.) compared to same-size Mamba and transformer models?
  7. The Krohn-Rhodes cascade decomposition is used theoretically; is there a practical benefit to directly structuring the MinMax RNC architecture according to the Krohn-Rhodes decomposition of a target automaton?
