> **一句话总结：** 在监督微调（SFT）阶段使用与预训练相同的优化器进行全量微调，相比切换优化器或使用 LoRA，能在学习新任务的同时更少地遗忘预训练知识——作者将这一现象命名为"优化器-模型一致性"，并通过激活正则化理论和 Fisher 信息矩阵近似给出了理论解释。

> **TL;DR:** This paper identifies and theoretically explains a phenomenon called "optimizer-model consistency": when full fine-tuning uses the same optimizer family as pretraining (e.g., AdamW throughout), the model achieves a better learning-forgetting Pareto frontier during SFT than switching to a different optimizer or using LoRA. Experiments on GPT-2 (123M) and Llama-2-7B across math, instruction-following, and coding tasks support the claim. The paper also reveals a concerning side-effect of Muon: despite its superior pretraining performance, Muon's tendency toward rote memorization rather than pattern learning may hurt its SFT quality on reasoning tasks like math.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"优化器-模型一致性"现象**：在 SFT 阶段沿用预训练时相同的优化器族（如全程使用 AdamW），可在遗忘最少的情况下实现最强的学习效果，优于切换优化器或采用 LoRA。
- **理论解释机制**：不同优化器对激活值施加不同的正则化（由矩阵诱导范数决定，AdamW 对应 L1 输入稀疏性，Muon 对应 L2 稀疏性），SFT 时使用相同优化器可以更好地匹配预训练检查点附近的损失曲率，从而减少遗忘。
- **重新评估 LoRA 的遗忘优势**：在合理的学习率搜索下，全量微调总能找到一个检查点，在学习效果相当或更好的同时遗忘更少，从而优于任意 LoRA 配置。
- **揭示 Muon 的潜在缺陷**：Muon 倾向于死记硬背而非模式学习，在仅有少量数据的 SFT 场景（尤其是推理类任务）中可能表现不如 AdamW，尽管其预训练性能更强。

## Key Contributions

- **Optimizer-model consistency phenomenon**: Full fine-tuning with the same optimizer as pretraining consistently achieves the best learning-forgetting tradeoff in SFT, empirically validated on GPT-2 and Llama-2-7B.
- **Theoretical grounding via activation regularization**: Optimizers are shown to regularize model activations according to their underlying matrix-induced norms (AdamW ~ L1 input sparsity, Muon ~ L2). Under simplified assumptions and Fisher Information approximation of the Hessian, same-family SFT optimizer minimizes forgetting by aligning with the curvature structure at the pretrained checkpoint.
- **Full finetuning can dominate LoRA on the Pareto frontier**: When learning rate is properly swept, full finetuning always finds a checkpoint strictly better than LoRA in both learning and forgetting, challenging prior claims that LoRA inherently forgets less.
- **Muon case study**: Muon performs comparably or better than AdamW for instruction-following SFT, but worse for math reasoning, hypothesized to stem from Muon's full-rank updates facilitating rote memorization over pattern acquisition.

## Methodology

**Experimental setup.** The authors run controlled pretrain-then-SFT pipelines on GPT-2-small (~123M parameters) pretrained on OpenWebText for 8B tokens with both AdamW and Muon. SFT tasks span MetaMathQA (math), Alpaca (instruction following), and Magicoder-Evol-Instruct-110K (coding). For larger-scale validation, Llama-2-7B is finetuned on MetaMathQA. Forgetting is measured as validation loss on the pretraining corpus (OpenWebText) or benchmark accuracy on Hellaswag/ARC-C/Winogrande; learning is measured as validation loss or task accuracy on the downstream dataset. All comparisons are made via **Pareto frontiers** of the learning-forgetting tradeoff across a grid of learning rates and training checkpoints — an important methodological choice that avoids cherry-picking a single hyperparameter.

**Theoretical framework.** The authors connect optimizers to the Pethick et al. (2025) matrix norm framework: each optimizer A_{α,β} corresponds to a specific norm ‖·‖_{α,β}, which induces implicit regularization on activations. The forgetting loss is approximated by a second-order Taylor expansion around the pretrained checkpoint, with the Hessian approximated by the Fisher Information matrix (E[xx^T] for input activations x). Under four formalized assumptions — forgetting loss approximation, weight-activation alignment, SFT loss linearization, and optimizer regularization of activations — Theorem 1 shows that same-family SFT (α₂ = α₁, β₂ ≥ 2) achieves near-optimal forgetting for any given SFT loss constraint.

**Muon memorization experiment.** To test the hypothesis that Muon over-memorizes, the authors construct a synthetic corrupted corpus (token order randomly shuffled within blocks), mix it with clean text, and measure exact-match memorization accuracy separately on clean vs. corrupted sequences. Muon shows significantly higher corrupted-data memorization (up to 65% vs. 55.8% for AdamW at context 128 / generation 1) and earlier onset of rote memorization, while SGD shows the highest clean memorization accuracy, supporting a spectrum of pattern-learning vs. memorization behavior.

## Results & Highlights

- **Pareto dominance of same-optimizer full FT:** On every GPT-2 task (Alpaca, MetaMathQA, Magicoder) and for both AdamW-pretrained and Muon-pretrained checkpoints, fine-tuning with the same optimizer as pretraining sits at the Pareto-optimal upper-right corner of the learning-forgetting tradeoff plot. The result holds for both loss-based and accuracy-based metrics.
- **Llama-2-7B confirms at 7B scale:** Full FT with AdamW (same as Llama-2 pretraining) dominates LoRA variants (rank 16, 64, 256) and other optimizers in the Pareto frontier on MetaMathQA, achieving higher GSM8K accuracy while preserving more general capability (Hellaswag/ARC-C/Winogrande).
- **LoRA reassessment:** At any individual checkpoint where LoRA "forgets less," there always exists a full FT checkpoint (at a lower learning rate) with equal or better learning and equal or less forgetting — the LoRA advantage disappears when the Pareto frontier is properly plotted. Larger LoRA rank generally gives better tradeoffs.
- **Muon pretraining advantage erodes at SFT:** Muon pretrains GPT-2 to lower validation loss on all four datasets (OWT, Alpaca, MetaMathQA, Magicoder). However, after same-optimizer SFT, the Muon pipeline trails the AdamW pipeline on MetaMathQA and is roughly comparable on Magicoder, indicating SFT performance degradation for reasoning tasks.
- **Memorization results:** Muon achieves 65.0% exact match on corrupted sequences vs. 55.8% for AdamW (context 128, generation 1), while AdamW achieves 90.1% on clean data vs. Muon's 88.9%, confirming Muon memorizes noise more readily.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The core "optimizer-model consistency" framing is a clean and useful observation, and the connection to activation regularization via matrix-induced norms is insightful. However, the intuition that "stay consistent with pretraining" is beneficial is not entirely surprising — it resonates with known ideas in continual learning. The theoretical result, while rigorous within its assumptions, rests on several approximations (Fisher Information Hessian, block-diagonal structure, linearized SFT loss, and the strong Assumption 4 on activation regularization).

**Rigor:** The Pareto frontier methodology is a genuine strength — it sidesteps the common pitfall of single-hyperparameter comparisons and makes the learning-forgetting tradeoff visible in its full complexity. However, the main experimental models are small (GPT-2 ~123M), with only a single 7B validation. The theoretical framework is presented as an approximate/simplified model, and the assumptions are explicitly acknowledged as potentially strong.

**Reproducibility:** Good. All experiment settings are detailed in Appendix A, including hardware (4x NVIDIA A6000), codebases (nanoGPT, LlamaFactory, Dion), learning rate grids, and hyperparameter configurations. The use of well-known public datasets (OpenWebText, MetaMathQA, Alpaca, Magicoder) aids reproducibility.

**Significance:** High practical relevance for anyone running SFT pipelines. The finding that you should use AdamW for SFT if you pretrained with AdamW (rather than switching to Muon or using LoRA) is immediately actionable. The case study on Muon's memorization behavior is especially timely given Muon's growing adoption in frontier model training.

**Red flags:** (1) The theoretical result requires 4 simplifying assumptions, some of which (especially Assumption 4 on activation regularization scaling) may not hold tightly in practice. (2) The memorization experiment uses a synthetic corrupted corpus on GPT-2; whether these dynamics generalize to real-world SFT at scale is an open question. (3) The paper cannot currently test Muon throughout the Llama-2-7B pipeline due to lack of a publicly available Muon-pretrained 7B model, weakening the large-scale Muon case study.

## Strengths

- Clean, practically actionable finding with strong experimental support across multiple tasks and models.
- The Pareto frontier evaluation methodology is methodologically sound and corrects misleading prior single-LR conclusions about LoRA.
- The theoretical framework connects the empirical phenomenon to a principled mechanistic explanation (activation regularization via norm structure), not just post-hoc rationalization.
- Honest engagement with limitations and concurrent work; the paper does not overclaim.
- Detailed appendices with full experimental settings improve reproducibility.
- The memorization/generalization analysis of Muon is a novel and practically valuable secondary finding.

## Limitations

- Experiments are primarily on GPT-2 (~123M); the 7B validation is limited to one task (MetaMathQA) and one optimizer combination (AdamW). Claims about Muon at scale cannot be fully verified without Muon-pretrained large models.
- The theoretical proof rests on several approximations and assumptions. The activation regularization assumption (Assumption 4) is acknowledged as potentially too strong.
- LoRA comparisons cover a limited rank range (r=4 to 256 for GPT-2, r=16 to 256 for Llama); other PEFT methods (prefix tuning, IA3, DoRA, etc.) are not considered.
- The paper does not study multi-epoch SFT extensively or long-context / instruction-tuning at scale.
- No open-source code repository is mentioned, though the experiment settings are detailed enough for reproduction.

## Relevance

本文对大模型训练流程（预训练-微调范式）中的遗忘问题提出了直接可用的建议：SFT 应沿用预训练优化器，这对正在设计或评估 LLM 训练流程的研究者具有很高的参考价值。同时，对 Muon 优化器在推理任务 SFT 中潜在缺陷的分析，为关注前沿模型训练优化器选择的读者提供了重要警示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does optimizer-model consistency hold at true frontier scales (70B+), particularly for Muon-pretrained models? Are there open-source Muon-pretrained checkpoints that could enable this test?
2. Can the theoretical framework be extended beyond the simplified linear/Fisher approximations to handle the nonlinearity of modern LLMs (e.g., with attention, LayerNorm, residual connections)?
3. Is the memorization tendency of Muon addressable — for instance, through a modified Muon update rule that introduces more low-rank structure, or via a hybrid Muon/AdamW SFT strategy?
4. Does the optimizer-model consistency principle extend to RLHF/RLAIF stages beyond SFT, or to continual pretraining scenarios?
5. The paper shows full FT Pareto-dominates LoRA, but does this hold under memory-constrained settings where practitioners cannot afford full FT? Is there a PEFT method that can approximate same-optimizer consistency?
6. How does the learning rate schedule (cosine vs. linear decay, warmup length) interact with the optimizer-model consistency effect?
