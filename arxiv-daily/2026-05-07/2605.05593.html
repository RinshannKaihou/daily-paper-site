Causal Probing for Internal Visual Representations in Multimodal Large Language Models

Zehao Deng, Tianjie Ju, Zheng Wu, Liangbo He, Jun Lan, Huijia Zhu, Weiqiang Wang, Zhuosheng Zhang

arXiv: 2605.05593

Date: 2026-05-07

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05593

一句话总结： 本文提出因果探测框架，通过激活操控（activation steering）系统性地介入五种多模态大模型的内部残差流，揭示了实体、视觉风格、情感和抽象概念四类视觉语义的截然不同编码机制，并发现模型对几何辅助线的感知与逻辑推理之间存在根本性脱节。
TL;DR: This paper introduces a causal probing framework that uses activation steering—injecting difference-in-means concept vectors into residual streams—to reveal how four types of visual concepts (entities, visual styles, emotions, abstract concepts) are encoded across multiple MLLM architectures. The key finding is a fundamental divergence: entity knowledge is sparsely localized in specific layers (low Gini ~0.07), while abstract concepts are diffusely distributed across all layers (high Gini ~0.43). Reverse steering experiments show that suppressing explicit entity generation triggers anomalous compensatory activation from the image encoder, exposing a perception-generation conflict. A visual reasoning extension finds that MLLMs perceive geometric auxiliary lines as passive visual features rather than procedural problem-solving operators—meaning scaling and visual steering alone cannot bridge this gap. Researchers interested in MLLM interpretability, the mechanisms behind scaling laws, or the limits of visual reasoning should read this paper.

核心贡献

  • 因果探测框架：基于线性表示假设，利用对比激活分析从配对图像中提取视觉概念向量，并将其注入模型的残差流中，将被动相关分析升级为主动因果干预，适用于多种MLLM架构。
  • 四类视觉概念的差异化编码机制：通过Gini系数和峰值可操纵性指标，定量揭示了实体（稀疏定位，Gini≈0.07）与抽象概念（全局分布，Gini≈0.43）之间的根本差异，为"为什么大模型需要深度"提供了表征层面的解释。
  • 缩放律的表征性解读：增加模型深度并不能更好地局部化实体知识，而是为分布式抽象语义的编码提供了更多层数，从而从内部表征角度解释了模型规模提升带来的能力涌现。
  • 逆向操控实验：发现抑制实体的显式文本生成会在残差流中触发异常的logit激增（量级达10^6~10^8），揭示了跨注意力视觉特征与残差流语义表征之间的补偿机制。
  • 视觉推理的根本性局限：通过GeoLaux几何数据集实验，证明MLLMs将辅助线编码为静态视觉特征（关系词logit提升），但未能触发程序性推理动作（动作词logit无显著提升），即使注入视觉概念向量，推理准确率仅提升1.6%。

Key Contributions

  • Causal probing framework: A two-stage pipeline that (1) extracts concept vectors via difference-in-means over paired positive/negative images at each transformer layer, and (2) injects these vectors into the residual stream during inference to causally verify their functional role. Mathematically proven to be the optimal worst-case intervention direction under the linear representation hypothesis.
  • Divergent encoding taxonomy: Quantitative evidence that entity knowledge is highly localized (low Gini coefficient ~0.07, concentrated in specific layers), whereas abstract concepts are broadly distributed (Gini ~0.43), with emotions peaking in intermediate layers and visual styles acting as a continuous bias across all layers.
  • Representational account of scaling laws: Model depth primarily benefits abstract concept encoding (more layers to distribute complex semantics), not entity localization (which remains scale-invariant), offering a mechanistic hypothesis for capability emergence at scale.
  • Perception-generation dissociation via reverse steering: Blocking entity concept vectors suppresses explicit mention rates but simultaneously causes exponential logit surges (10^6–10^8), revealing a compensatory cross-attention mechanism that conflicts with residual stream intervention.
  • Visual reasoning gap: Demonstrated across three MLLM architectures that geometric auxiliary line vectors activate perceptual relationship tokens but fail to trigger action tokens required for mathematical reasoning, providing a concrete mechanistic diagnosis of MLLM reasoning limitations.

Methodology

The framework rests on the linear representation hypothesis: that visual concepts are linearly encoded in activation space and can be isolated as directional vectors.

Concept extraction: For a concept c (e.g., "cat"), N=100 paired images (with and without the concept) are assembled. At each transformer layer l, the concept vector is computed as:

v_c^l = (1/N) * sum_i [ h^l(x+_{c,i}) - h^l(x-_{c,i}) ]

where h^l denotes hidden states of the last token. This difference-in-means approach is theoretically proven to be the maximin-optimal intervention direction under unknown model weights.

Steering: During inference on a query image x_q, the residual stream is modified as:

h^l(x_q) <- h^l(x_q) + alpha * v_c^l

with alpha=1 as default. The vector is injected independently at each layer.

Datasets: Entity pairs from Imgedit and MagicBrush (10,000 pairs); Visual Style from OmniConsistency (2,600 pairs, 22 styles); Emotion from EmoSet (3.3M images, 8 emotion categories with Contentment as neutral baseline); Abstract Concepts from Pexels (20 concepts with antonym-based negative sampling); Geometric reasoning from GeoLaux (original vs. auxiliary-line-annotated geometry images).

Evaluation metrics: Three complementary metrics assessed by GPT-4o and Sentence-BERT — (1) Success Rate: explicit mention of the target concept in generated text; (2) Semantic Similarity: cosine similarity via Sentence-BERT all-mpnet-base-v2; (3) Logit Boost: average increase in raw logits of target tokens immediately after injection. A Faithfulness metric rho = NIE/TE is also introduced to verify that extracted vectors genuinely replicate natural visual processing.

Models: Qwen2.5-VL-7B, Qwen3-VL-8B, Qwen3-VL-32B, LLaVA-OneVision-1.5-8B, Gemma3-4B, Gemma3-27B.

Results & Highlights

Representational hierarchy by concept type (Table 2): Emotion achieves the highest peak steerability (Success Rate up to 0.900 in Gemma3-4B, Semantic Similarity up to 0.654), while abstract concepts are the weakest (Success Rate ~0.253 average, Semantic Similarity ~0.329). Entity logit boosts are extreme (10^10–10^21) but do not translate to proportionally high Success Rates due to contextual decoding conflicts.

Gini coefficient divergence: Entity knowledge exhibits Gini ~0.071 (highly sparse/localized); abstract concepts reach Gini ~0.429 (broadly distributed). This is the first quantitative evidence directly linking representation sparsity to concept type in MLLMs.

Layer-wise signatures: Entities show a bimodal distribution (mid-network visual perception + deep network semantic accumulation); visual styles are uniformly distributed (continuous bias vector); emotions concentrate in intermediate layers; abstract concepts skew strongly toward terminal layers.

Scaling effects: Larger models show lower Gini for concrete concepts (tighter localization) but higher Gini for abstract concepts (more distributed), confirming that depth benefits abstract encoding rather than factual memorization. Faithfulness scores reach 0.98 in Gemma3-27B for entities.

Reverse steering: Subtracting entity vectors drops mention rates precipitously, but internal logits surge exponentially (10^6–10^8), revealing a cross-attention compensatory mechanism where the image encoder continuously reinjects visual evidence even when the residual stream is suppressed.

Visual reasoning (GeoLaux): Injecting auxiliary-line vectors boosts logits for geometric relationship words (parallel, perpendicular) but shows no corresponding increase for action words (connect, extend). Answer accuracy improves by at most 1.6% (Gemma3-27B) and Construction Rate peaks at only 15.3%, despite strong visual cue injection across three architectures.

Context dependency: Steering efficacy is highest when injecting into semantically rich scene contexts; pure white backgrounds yield high Logit Boost but low Success Rate (safety priors suppress hallucination), while Gaussian noise images show higher Success Rate than white backgrounds due to visual pareidolia.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: Moderate-to-good. Activation steering itself is not new, and applying it to vision-language models has precedents (e.g., SteerVLM, Gan et al. 2025 on textual steering). The paper's novelty lies in the systematic four-category taxonomy, the layer-wise Gini analysis, the formal optimality proof, the reverse-steering compensation finding, and the visual reasoning extension. These are genuine insights rather than mere replication.

Rigor: Reasonably strong. The faithfulness metric (rho) is a thoughtful addition to validate that extracted vectors are not adversarial directions. The use of three complementary metrics (Success Rate, Semantic Similarity, Logit Boost) prevents single-metric overfitting. However, the Logit Boost metric is measured "immediately after injection" — its correlation with actual downstream task performance is not always clear, and the extreme magnitudes (10^21 for entities) suggest numerical instability concerns not fully addressed.

Reproducibility: Moderate. The paper provides dataset construction details, prompt templates (Tables 4, C.1), and evaluation prompts. However, the concept extraction requires paired images with precise conceptual isolation, which may be difficult to replicate exactly. The code link in the abstract is a hyperlink to "here" — a vague pointer.

Significance: High for interpretability researchers. The finding that entity knowledge is localized while abstract concepts are distributed has direct implications for knowledge editing, hallucination mitigation, and understanding why scaling helps. The visual reasoning gap finding is particularly important: it provides mechanistic evidence that visual perception and logical reasoning are not just quantitatively weak but qualitatively decoupled in current MLLMs.

Red flags:

  • The 1.6% accuracy improvement from geometric steering is described as "no statistically significant improvement" — this honest reporting is appreciated, but it also means the paper's reasoning section mostly characterizes a limitation rather than advancing a solution.
  • The bimodal entity distribution analysis (Appendix E.2) is exploratory and the explanation (information management mechanism) is speculative.
  • The paper tests only autoregressive decoder architectures; encoder-decoder or diffusion-based vision-language models are not considered.
  • Abstract concept data from Pexels relies on human-tagged keywords, which may introduce label noise for concepts like "justice" or "freedom."

Strengths

  • Theoretically principled framework with formal optimality proof for the extraction direction.
  • Systematic multi-model, multi-category evaluation with three complementary metrics plus a dedicated faithfulness measure.
  • The reverse steering experiment revealing the perception-generation compensatory mechanism is a genuinely surprising and important finding.
  • The visual reasoning analysis using GeoLaux is a clean, concrete operationalization of an abstract limitation.
  • Honest reporting: the paper acknowledges null results in reasoning improvement rather than overclaiming.
  • Intra-family similarity / inter-family divergence finding has implications for understanding how architectural design choices shape internal representations.

Limitations

  • The framework is restricted to linear concept vectors; concepts with non-linear or compositional encodings may be missed or mischaracterized.
  • Evaluation of Success Rate relies on GPT-4o as judge, introducing potential instability for edge cases and costs that limit large-scale ablation.
  • The study covers only six models from three families; conclusions about universal MLLM behavior should be treated cautiously.
  • Abstract concept dataset quality depends on Pexels tag reliability and antonym selection heuristics, which may not cleanly isolate high-level semantics.
  • The paper does not propose any method for improving MLLM visual reasoning — it characterizes the gap without closing it.
  • Potential safety concern acknowledged in the paper itself: the ability to inject specific concepts (hallucinations, biases, emotions) into model outputs via latent space manipulation could be misused.

Relevance

本文对多模态大模型内部视觉表征机制的系统性因果分析，直接回应了"为什么大模型在缩放后获得新能力"以及"视觉感知与逻辑推理为何脱节"这两个核心问题，对研究MLLM可解释性、知识编辑和推理增强的学者具有很强的参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can the compensatory cross-attention mechanism identified in reverse steering be exploited to improve robustness against adversarial visual perturbations, or does it represent a fundamental architectural constraint?
  2. The paper finds that abstract concept vectors are distributed across terminal layers — does this distribution pattern change meaningfully with instruction tuning vs. pretraining checkpoints? Would a purely pretrained MLLM show the same pattern?
  3. Given that geometric auxiliary line concepts activate perceptual tokens but not action tokens, would fine-tuning on explicit procedural reasoning traces (chain-of-thought with explicit auxiliary line construction steps) shift the internal encoding to activate action-oriented subspaces?
  4. Visual styles are described as a "continuous bias vector" across all layers — does this mean style concepts can be transferred across unrelated images via simple residual stream addition without degrading other semantic content? What are the limits of this bias-like encoding?
  5. The faithfulness metric rho approaches 1 for larger models on most categories — does this imply that as models scale, their internal representations become increasingly linear and compositional, and does this saturate at some model size?
  6. How do these encoding patterns generalize to non-Western cultural concepts (e.g., abstract concepts with no direct English antonym) or to concepts that span entity and abstract categories (e.g., "royalty" or "celebrity")?
