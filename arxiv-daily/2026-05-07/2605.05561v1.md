> **一句话总结：** BitCal-TTS 是一个无需微调的轻量级推理时控制器，通过比特位感知的置信度校正和后定界符确认窗口，修复了4-bit量化模型在自适应停止决策中的过早退出问题，在 GSM8K 小样本分片上使 Qwen2.5-7B/14B 的精确匹配准确率比精度无关自适应基线高出约 +3.7/+2.8 个百分点，同时相比固定预算解码节省约 32–41% 的 token 消耗。

> **TL;DR:** When LLMs are served under aggressive 4-bit post-training quantization, the confidence signals that drive adaptive early-stopping (token entropy, trace stability) become miscalibrated — the model appears more confident than it really is, causing it to halt reasoning prematurely and return wrong answers. BitCal-TTS is a training-free runtime sidecar that (1) multiplicatively downscales raw confidence by a bit-width factor (s=0.85 at 4-bit), (2) tracks reasoning-trace and last-layer hidden-state stability as auxiliary stopping signals, and (3) forces the decoder to run a 32-token confirmation tail after the GSM8K answer delimiter `####` is first seen before termination is allowed. On small evaluation shards of GSM8K with Qwen2.5-Instruct at 4-bit, BitCal-TTS reduces premature-stop rates from ~15–17% to ~11% and improves point-estimate accuracy by 2–4 percentage points over a precision-agnostic adaptive baseline, while still consuming 32–41% fewer tokens than fixed-budget decoding. The gains are consistent in direction across 7B and 14B but are based on N=35–54 examples per shard — the authors explicitly note results are suggestive rather than statistically resolved.

## 核心贡献

- **发现并形式化了量化-自适应停止的交互失效模式**：4-bit 量化扭曲了 logits 和隐层激活，使得基于熵的在线停止信号相比全精度模型系统性偏高置信度，导致自适应控制器在推理尚未收敛时就提前终止。
- **提出比特位条件置信度校正（Bit-Conditioned Calibration）**：引入乘法缩放因子 s(b)——4-bit 时为 0.85，8-bit 时为 1.00，高精度时为 1.05——将原始置信度向保守方向调整，无需任何微调。
- **设计后定界符确认尾（Post-Marker Confirmation Tail）**：检测到 `####` 答案定界符后，强制继续解码 Δ(b)=32（4-bit）个 token 再允许终止，消除因量化噪声导致的定界符误触发退出。
- **整合三路在线信号**：香农熵 H_t、推理轨迹文本稳定性 τ^tr（相邻 chunk 字面重复率）、最后一层隐层余弦稳定性 τ^hid，三者加权合成置信度。
- **记录了 3B-4bit 的完全失效区间**：在 Qwen2.5-3B-4bit 上，两种自适应变体的准确率均崩塌至 20-22%（固定预算为 60%），并分析了具体原因（解答长度超过 floor m、稳定性代理在部分推理上误触发），为部署者提供了明确的能力下限警示。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes the quantization-adaptive-halting interaction**: Shows that 4-bit inference inflates apparent confidence in online halting signals (entropy, trace stability), causing precision-agnostic controllers to stop reasoning traces before they converge.
- **Bit-conditioned confidence rescaling**: A multiplicative scale factor s(b) (0.85 at b≤4, 1.00 at 4<b≤8, 1.05 at b>8) applied to raw confidence at each controller step, requiring no model retraining or held-out calibration data.
- **Marker-aware confirmation tail**: After the GSM8K `####` delimiter is first observed, the controller is forced to continue decoding for Δ(b)=32 tokens (at 4-bit) before a stop is permitted, preventing false termination on spurious delimiter-like patterns produced by quantization noise.
- **Three-signal online confidence**: Combines token entropy, reasoning-trace text stability (fraction of literal-repeat chunk pairs), and last-layer hidden-state cosine similarity into a single calibrated score.
- **Documents the 3B–4bit failure regime**: Both adaptive variants collapse to ~20–22% accuracy on Qwen2.5-3B at 4-bit (vs. 60% fixed), with a root-cause analysis attributing the failure to a mismatched minimum-token floor and stability proxies misfiring on partial reasoning — providing honest characterization of method scope.

## Methodology

BitCal-TTS wraps a frozen quantized causal LM (specifically Qwen2.5-Instruct at 4-bit NF4 weights via bitsandbytes, served through Hugging Face Transformers) with a thin stateful controller. Decoding proceeds in chunks of k=16 tokens. After each chunk, three online signals are computed:

1. **Token entropy** H_t = -Σ p_t(v) log p_t(v) over the vocabulary softmax, normalized to uncertainty u_t = clip(H_t / H_max, 0, 1) with H_max=10 nats.
2. **Reasoning-trace stability** τ^tr: fraction of consecutive whitespace-stripped chunk pairs (both ≥8 chars) where s̃_{i-1} = s̃_i, measuring literal repetition as a proxy for trace convergence.
3. **Hidden-state stability** τ^hid: running mean of cosine similarity between consecutive normalized last-layer hidden vectors at the final token position (accessed via a Hugging Face forward hook).

These combine into a raw confidence score c^raw = w_e(1−u_t) + w_tr·τ^tr + w_hid·τ^hid (default weights 0.40, 0.35, 0.25). The bit-conditioned calibrated confidence is c_t = clip(c^raw · s(b), 0, 1) with s(4)=0.85.

The halting policy has two phases. In the **pre-marker phase**, the controller applies a threshold policy over (entropy, confidence, remaining budget): it issues CONTINUE if T_t < m (floor, default 128 tokens), STOP if remaining budget < m_buf (buffer=32), ESCALATE if H_t ≥ θ_E (high entropy escalate threshold=4.0), STOP if H_t ≤ θ_H and c_t ≥ θ_c (entropy-stop=2.0, confidence-stop=0.75), and CONTINUE otherwise. In the **post-marker phase** (after `####` is first seen at T*), the entropy policy is bypassed and the controller forces CONTINUE until T_t − T* ≥ Δ(b_eff), then forces STOP. For the precision-agnostic adaptive ablation, b_eff=16 is used (making Δ=0, so the delimiter can trigger immediate termination); for BitCal-TTS, b_eff=b=4 (making Δ=32).

The method requires no fine-tuning, no held-out calibration set, and adds only O(|V|) + O(d) overhead per controller step beyond what standard autoregressive decoding already materializes.

## Results & Highlights

All experiments use greedy decoding (do_sample=False) on Qwen2.5-Instruct (3B, 7B, 14B) at 4-bit NF4 precision, evaluated on deterministic first-N shards of the GSM8K test split on a single NVIDIA T4 16GB GPU.

**Headline at B=512 (Table 1):**

| Model | Method | Acc. (%) [95% CI] | Avg tokens | Savings (%) | Prem. stop (%) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 7B | fixed | 90.7 [80.1, 96.0] | 466 | — | 0.0 |
| 7B | adaptive | 79.6 [67.1, 88.2] | 286 | 38.5 | 14.8 |
| 7B | BitCal-TTS | **83.3** [71.3, 91.0] | 316 | 32.1 | **11.1** |
| 14B | fixed | 88.6 [74.0, 95.5] | 455 | — | 0.0 |
| 14B | adaptive | 82.9 [67.3, 91.9] | 239 | 47.5 | 17.1 |
| 14B | BitCal-TTS | **85.7** [70.6, 93.7] | 269 | 40.8 | **11.4** |

- On the 7B shard (N=54): BitCal-TTS recovers +3.7 accuracy points and reduces premature-stop rate from 14.8% to 11.1% vs. adaptive, while using 32.1% fewer tokens than fixed.
- On the 14B shard (N=35): +2.8 accuracy points, premature-stop from 17.1% to 11.4%, 40.8% token savings vs. fixed.
- **3B failure mode**: Both adaptive variants collapse to ~20–22% vs. 60% for fixed (N=50), with ~63% premature-stop rates. The root cause is a mismatch between the 128-token floor and typical 3B solution lengths (frequently >200 tokens), combined with trace stability firing on intermediate partial computations at 4-bit.
- **Budget sweep (7B)**: At B=256 all methods are accuracy-bound; at B=1024 BitCal-TTS achieves 83.0% vs. fixed's 90.6% while using only 336 vs. 813 tokens/example.
- **Statistical caveat (authors' own)**: All between-method differences sit within overlapping Wilson 95% CIs at the reported shard sizes. A paired McNemar's test would not reject the null. Results are described by the authors as "suggestive of a real effect rather than a final estimate of its magnitude."

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The individual components — entropy-based adaptive halting, confidence calibration, and post-answer confirmation windows — are each known in prior work. The specific combination targeted at the quantization-induced miscalibration problem in adaptive halting is the novel contribution. The framing of "bit-width as a calibration signal" is clean and practically motivated, but is more of an engineering insight than a fundamental conceptual advance.

**Rigor:** This is the paper's most significant weakness. N=35–54 examples per shard means the Wilson 95% CI on a single accuracy point estimate spans roughly ±10 percentage points. The claimed +3.7 and +2.8 point improvements are smaller than the CI width and would not survive a standard significance test. The authors are transparent about this and explicitly call it out, which is commendable, but the results remain inconclusive as published. A follow-up on the full 1,319-item GSM8K test split is listed as the most pressing future work.

**Reproducibility:** High. Greedy decoding, deterministic shards, pinned software versions, released JSONL artifacts, and figure-generation scripts all support reproduction. The authors note sensitivity to minor bitsandbytes version changes (0.43.x → 0.44.x can flip a small number of greedy outputs) and document this explicitly.

**Significance:** The practical motivation is real — quantized 4-bit models are widely used on consumer hardware, and adaptive halting miscalibration is a genuine deployment issue. If the accuracy improvements hold up on the full test split, the method would be immediately deployable as a zero-cost (no fine-tuning) improvement. The 3B failure mode also contains useful negative information for practitioners.

**Red flags:** 
- Small shard sizes make all accuracy comparisons statistically unresolved.
- Component-wise ablation is explicitly missing — it is unknown which of the three changes (confidence rescaling, marker tail, hidden-state proxy) drives the observed improvements.
- Evaluation is limited to one benchmark (GSM8K) with structured `####` delimiters; generalization to open-ended generation or other benchmarks is untested.
- The hand-tuned thresholds (θ_H=2.0, θ_c=0.75, m=128, etc.) are not validated across tasks or model families beyond Qwen2.5-Instruct.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated problem formulation: identifies a specific and practical failure mode at the intersection of quantization and adaptive decoding.
- Training-free: plugs into any Hugging Face Transformers quantized model with no modification to weights or training pipeline.
- Intellectually honest reporting: explicit statistical caveats, disclosed limitations, documented failure modes, and a clear roadmap for follow-up.
- Strong reproducibility infrastructure for a preprint-stage paper.
- The Pareto frontier visualization (Figure 4) and budget sweep (Figure 5) provide useful intuition about the quality-efficiency trade-off surface.
- The 3B failure analysis is a genuine contribution — it identifies when the method breaks and provides actionable diagnostics.

---

## Limitations

- **Underpowered evaluation**: N=35–54 per shard; all key accuracy differences are within confidence intervals. The paper cannot currently support statistically significant claims about accuracy improvement.
- **Missing ablations**: No component-wise isolation of confidence rescaling vs. confirmation tail vs. hidden-state proxy. The relative contribution of each component is unknown.
- **Single benchmark and model family**: Results are GSM8K-centric with Qwen2.5-Instruct only. The marker-aware tail relies on GSM8K's `####` structure; tasks without structured terminators require a different confirmation criterion.
- **Hand-tuned hyperparameters**: Thresholds are constants, not learned per model or task. The 3B failure mode demonstrates that the floor m=128 is poorly calibrated for models whose typical solution length exceeds the floor.
- **No wall-clock latency or energy measurement**: Token count is used as a proxy; actual inference speedup on production hardware is unquantified.
- **4-bit only**: The bit-conditioned scaling factors s(b) are designed heuristically; the approach has not been validated at other precisions (e.g., 2-bit or 3-bit).
- **No test on reasoning-tuned backbones**: The method's interaction with models like DeepSeek-R1 or o1-style models (which may produce very different trace length distributions) is untested.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the accuracy improvement hold on the full 1,319-item GSM8K test split, and would a paired McNemar's test reject the null hypothesis? The authors themselves flag this as the most pressing open question.
2. Which of the three BitCal-TTS components — confidence rescaling s(b), post-marker confirmation tail Δ(b), or hidden-state stability proxy τ^hid — drives the premature-stop reduction? The missing ablation study is needed to answer this.
3. Can the hand-tuned thresholds (θ_H, θ_c, m) be replaced by a small learned calibrator fitted on held-out logits, and would this fix the 3B failure mode?
4. Does the method generalize beyond GSM8K to tasks without structured answer delimiters (e.g., MATH, code generation, open-ended QA)?
5. How does BitCal-TTS interact with reasoning-tuned models such as DeepSeek-R1 or o1-style models that generate significantly longer and more structured chains of thought?
6. At even lower bit widths (2-bit or 3-bit), do the heuristic s(b) values need to be substantially more aggressive, and is the entropy-based stopping signal still meaningful at all?
7. The paper notes that adaptive halting reduces average compute per query but also lowers the cost of high-throughput misuse — how should deployment policies account for this dual-use consideration?
