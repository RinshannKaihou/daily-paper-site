> **一句话总结：** 本文在 ℓ∞-光滑、ℓ₁-平稳性和可分离噪声假设下，首次给出 SignSGD 的紧下界，并通过比较 SignSGD 上界与 SGD 下界，严格证明当梯度噪声稀疏时 SignSGD 的复杂度比 SGD 低一个维度因子 d，同时将该框架推广到 Muon 优化器，给出首个核范数意义下的紧复杂度界。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides the first tight (matching upper and lower bound) complexity characterization of SignSGD under ℓ∞-smoothness and a separable noise model, then derives a corresponding lower bound for SGD under the same geometry, proving that SignSGD achieves a provably d-times better sample complexity than SGD when gradient noise is sparse or highly heterogeneous across coordinates. The framework is extended to the matrix domain to yield equivalent tight bounds for the Muon optimizer. Empirical validation on nanoGPT (124M) pretraining on C4 confirms the theoretical predictions.

## 核心贡献

- **SignSGD 紧界**：在 ℓ∞-光滑和可分离噪声假设下，建立与上界匹配的 ℓ₁-范数下界（Theorem 2），复杂度为 Θ(√(L∞Δ/N) + (‖σ‖₁²L∞Δ/N)^(1/4))，首次完整刻画 SignSGD 的最优收敛率。
- **SGD 严格下界**：在相同几何假设下推导 SGD 的 ℓ₁-范数下界（Theorem 3），揭示 SGD 额外承担维度 d 的代价，与 SignSGD 上界对比形成严格分离。
- **可证明的维度优势**：通过噪声密度函数 φ(σ) = ‖σ‖₁²/(d‖σ‖₂²) 的形式化比较，证明当噪声稀疏（φ ≈ 1/d）时，SignSGD 复杂度最多比 SGD 少一个维度因子 d。
- **Muon 紧界扩展**：将向量框架提升至矩阵域，证明 Muon 的上界（谱范数光滑、核范数收敛）与对应下界匹配（Theorems 4 & 5），这是 Muon 的首个复杂度下界，且维度无关性首次得到理论保证。
- **实验验证**：在 nanoGPT-124M 预训练实验中追踪梯度噪声密度 φ(σ)，证实 LLM 训练中噪声逐渐稀疏化（φ → 0.03），完美对应 SignSGD 理论优势的激活条件。

## Key Contributions

- **Tight bounds for SignSGD:** Establishes matching upper and lower bounds for SignSGD under ℓ∞-smoothness and separable noise (Theorems 1 and 2). The convergence rate is Θ(√(L∞Δ/N) + (‖σ‖₁²L∞Δ/N)^(1/4)), the first dimension-free tight characterization of SignSGD.
- **Lower bound for SGD in the same geometry:** Derives a novel ℓ₁-norm lower bound for vanilla SGD (Theorem 3) under identical assumptions, showing SGD incurs an extra factor of d in both deterministic and stochastic terms.
- **Provable dimensional gain:** Formally introduces the noise density function φ(σ) ∈ [1/d, 1] to compare the two methods, showing SignSGD reduces complexity by up to a factor of d when noise is sparse.
- **Extension to matrix optimizers (Muon):** Lifts the vector framework to the matrix domain via a geometric equivalence between SignSGD and Muon (vector ℓ∞-smoothness maps to spectral norm smoothness; vector ℓ₁ of noise maps to nuclear norm of noise covariance), yielding the first lower bound for Muon (Theorem 5) and confirming its dimension-free scaling.
- **Empirical corroboration:** Tracks gradient noise density throughout nanoGPT-124M pretraining, confirming that LLM gradient noise becomes increasingly sparse (φ → ~0.03) while CNN gradient noise homogenizes (φ → ~0.7), providing a unified explanation for why sign-based methods dominate in LLM training but not in CNNs.

## Methodology

The paper works within a deliberately chosen non-standard problem geometry to escape the known impossibility: under the standard ℓ₂-geometry with bounded variance, SGD is already minimax optimal, so sign-based methods cannot provably improve. The key assumptions adopted are:

1. **ℓ∞-smoothness** (Assumption 3a): |f(y) − f(x) − ⟨∇f(x), y−x⟩| ≤ (L∞/2)‖y−x‖²∞, replacing the standard ℓ₂-smoothness. This is equivalent to L-separable smoothness (Assumption 4a) with L∞ = ‖L‖₁.
2. **Separable noise model** (Assumption 2a): Per-coordinate bounded variance σᵢ², tracked as a vector σ, rather than a single global variance σ².
3. **ℓ₁-stationarity**: Performance measured by E[‖∇f(x)‖₁] rather than ‖∇f(x)‖₂.

**Upper bound for SignSGD (Theorem 1):** Follows from a descent lemma under ℓ∞-smoothness, bounding the sign-gradient mismatch term via the ℓ₁ gradient noise, and optimally tuning the step size η and batch size B.

**Lower bound for SignSGD (Theorem 2):** Proved via four steps: (i) dimensional decomposition into d parallel 1D problems; (ii) constructing a 1D "resisting oracle" function with constant-slope regions; (iii) introducing an adversarial bimodal noise oracle that forces the optimizer to stall; (iv) dimensional lifting by adversarially allocating coordinate-wise smoothness and suboptimality to maximize the aggregated lower bound.

**Lower bound for SGD (Theorem 3):** Reduces to the coordinate-wise lower bound of Jiang et al. [2025a], then allocates curvature across coordinates to maximize the SGD lower bound under the constraint ‖L‖₁ = L∞, revealing the extra d factor.

**Comparison via noise density φ(σ):** Comparing SignSGD's upper bound (Theorems 1) with SGD's lower bound (Theorem 3) shows that SignSGD is better by a factor of d in the deterministic term and by a factor of d in the noise-dominated term when φ(σ) ≈ 1/d.

**Extension to Muon (Theorems 4 & 5):** Constructs a geometric and dynamic equivalence: lifts hard vector instances to the matrix domain using orthogonal alignment matrices Q (aligning separable noise to matrix noise covariance Σ) and P (projecting m×n matrices to m×m subspace). SVD orthogonalization bypasses these orthogonal factors, reducing Muon's matrix update to an exact SignSGD vector update on the diagonal entries.

## Results & Highlights

- **Tight SignSGD complexity:** E[min_t ‖∇f(xₜ)‖₁] = Θ(√(L∞Δ/N) + (‖σ‖₁²L∞Δ/N)^(1/4)). Both terms are dimension-free.
- **SGD lower bound:** E[min_t ‖∇f(xₜ)‖₁] = Ω(√(dL∞Δ/T) + (d‖σ‖₂²L∞Δ/T)^(1/4)), showing up to a factor-d penalty relative to SignSGD.
- **nanoGPT experiment:** SignSGD converges significantly faster than SGD during pretraining of a 124M-parameter GPT-2 on the C4 dataset. The gradient noise density φ decreases from ~0.3 at initialization to ~0.03 mid-training, directly matching the regime where theory predicts Sign-based methods enjoy maximal advantage.
- **CNN comparison:** ResNet-20 on CIFAR-10 shows noise density φ stabilizing near 0.7 (close to the Gaussian limit 2/π ≈ 0.637), explaining the well-known comparable performance of SGD and SignSGD on image classification tasks.
- **Muon dimension-free bound:** Unlike prior Muon analyses that incur explicit min{m,n} factors, the present bounds (Theorems 4 and 5) are fully dimension-free, first-of-their-kind tight bounds measured by the nuclear norm.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. The core barrier — that SGD is already minimax optimal under ℓ₂-geometry — is well known, and prior work had not cleanly identified a geometry where SignSGD provably outperforms SGD with tight bounds. Providing matching upper and lower bounds for SignSGD is a genuine theoretical advance. The extension to Muon with the first lower bound is also novel.

**Rigor:** Strong. The proof strategy (adversarial bimodal noise, resisting oracle, dimensional lifting) is clearly articulated and technically complete. The geometric equivalence used for the Muon lower bound (reduction via SVD structure) is elegant and non-trivial.

**Reproducibility:** Good. The paper provides full proofs in appendices, explicit algorithmic pseudocode, and open-source code. Experimental details (nanoGPT on C4) are referenced to a detailed appendix.

**Significance:** The theoretical gap between empirical success of SignSGD/Muon and formal guarantees has been a persistent problem. This paper provides the most complete resolution to date. The insight that LLM gradient noise is intrinsically sparse (φ → 0) while CNN noise is dense is empirically grounded and theoretically actionable.

**Red flags / caveats:**
- The geometry shift (ℓ∞-smooth, ℓ₁-stationary) is necessary for the separation but may be questioned as an artifact of choice. The paper acknowledges that under the standard ℓ₂-geometry, no improvement is possible, but the practical relevance of ℓ∞-smoothness for arbitrary deep learning losses is not rigorously established — the noise-sparsity argument is empirical.
- The SGD lower bound (Theorem 3) is derived for batch size B = 1. Whether the dimension penalty persists for larger batches in all regimes is not fully analyzed.
- The comparison conflates SignSGD (no momentum) with Muon/Lion (momentum-based variants) in the motivation section; the theoretical results strictly apply to the basic SignSGD and Muon algorithms, not their practical momentum-enhanced variants like MuonLight or Lion.
- The empirical validation is limited to nanoGPT-124M, a relatively small model. Scaling behavior to billion-parameter models is not directly verified.

## Strengths

- Provides the first complete (tight upper + tight lower) complexity characterization for SignSGD under a geometry that matches its natural update structure.
- Derives a novel SGD lower bound in the ℓ∞/ℓ₁ geometry that previously did not exist in the literature.
- The noise density function φ(σ) is an elegant and interpretable summary statistic that bridges theory and practice.
- Extends the framework cleanly to Muon with a geometric equivalence proof, resolving a standing open question about dimension dependence.
- Empirical results (nanoGPT pretraining + noise density tracking) are directly tied to the theoretical predictions rather than being merely illustrative.
- Code is publicly available and experiments are described in sufficient detail.

## Limitations

- The theoretical framework requires ℓ∞-smoothness and a separable noise model; whether all modern LLM training losses satisfy these conditions non-trivially is an open question.
- Theoretical results apply to constant step size SignSGD and basic Muon; practical variants with momentum, weight decay, learning rate schedules, and mixed precision may deviate from the analyzed algorithms.
- The lower bounds are worst-case; average-case behavior in large-scale training could differ.
- No theoretical treatment of how batch normalization, layer normalization, or residual connections affect the separable noise or ℓ∞-smoothness assumptions.
- The paper does not analyze convergence to global minima or generalization — only stationarity in the non-convex setting.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the separation between SignSGD and SGD be extended to the heavy-tailed noise setting, where variance may be infinite? The paper by Yu et al. (2026b) suggests sign-based methods are effective under heavy-tailed noise — can the tight lower bound framework be adapted?
2. Does the provable dimension advantage persist for momentum-enhanced variants (Lion, Signum, MuonLight)? The theoretical analysis here covers basic SignSGD/Muon; momentum changes the analysis fundamentally.
3. The noise density φ(σ) is tracked empirically for nanoGPT but not for larger models. Does φ continue to decay toward 0 at the 7B/70B scale, or does it stabilize? This would determine whether the theoretical advantage grows or saturates with scale.
4. The paper shows SGD suffers from highly imbalanced smoothness (large L₁ ≫ L∞). Can adaptive methods (Adam, AdaGrad) recover the same advantage as SignSGD in this geometry, or do they face different fundamental barriers?
5. The Muon lower bound reduction relies on exact Newton–Schulz iteration producing the exact matrix sign. How do numerical errors in the approximate Newton–Schulz algorithm affect the practical gap between theoretical bounds and observed performance?
