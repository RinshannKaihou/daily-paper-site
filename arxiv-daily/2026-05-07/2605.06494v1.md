> **一句话总结：** 本文将稀疏自编码器（SAE）的每个特征表示为token共现图，并用Weisfeiler-Lehman图核对这些图进行聚类，从而发现了decoder向量相似度无法区分的字母型特征族（字母纯度0.516 vs. 0.000），证明图结构视角是token频率与decoder权重分析的有效补充。

> **TL;DR:** This paper represents each sparse autoencoder feature as a weighted token co-occurrence graph (nodes = top activating tokens, edges = co-occurrence within context windows), then clusters these graphs using a custom Weisfeiler-Lehman-style graph kernel. Applied to 2,048 features from a GPT-2 Small SAE (layer 6), the method recovers interpretable motif families—punctuation-heavy, code-like, language/script clusters—that decoder cosine similarity completely misses (alphabetic cluster purity 0.516 vs. 0.000). However, a simple token-histogram baseline achieves higher overall purity (0.854 vs. 0.760), so the graph view is a complement to, not a replacement for, token-frequency methods. The experiments are limited to a single SAE layer on a synthetic corpus, and no human validation of the motif families is provided.

## 核心贡献

- **图结构特征表示**：将每个SAE特征的激活模式转化为token共现加权图（节点为高频激活token，边为共现关系），突破了仅靠top-K token列表描述特征的传统方式。
- **定制WL图核**：提出基于对数尺度频率binning的Weisfeiler-Lehman风格图核，用加权邻居平均替代标准标签哈希，实现对特征结构相似性的量化比较。
- **发现新特征族**：通过聚类在decoder余弦相似度完全失效的情况下（字母纯度为0），图核仍能识别出字母型特征簇（纯度0.516），揭示了SAE特征空间中decoder几何与激活上下文拓扑之间的互补差异。
- **稳健性验证**：在图构建超参数（窗口大小、top-K截断、共现阈值）和k-means随机种子上均表现出高稳定性（均值纯度0.748±0.051；ARI/NMI跨语料0.944/0.928）。
- **明确的局限声明**：论文坦诚地指出token直方图基线整体表现更优（0.854 vs. 0.760），图视角作为补充而非主导，并列出可扩展性、单层局限、启发式标签等多项未解问题。

## Key Contributions

- **Graph-structured feature representation**: Each SAE feature is encoded as a weighted token co-occurrence graph, moving beyond flat top-token lists to capture relational structure among tokens in activation neighbourhoods.
- **Custom WL-style graph kernel**: A frequency-binned, weighted-averaging variant of the Weisfeiler-Lehman subtree kernel is introduced, producing an N×N similarity matrix over feature graphs without relying on raw token identities.
- **Recovery of decoder-invisible motif families**: The WL clustering recovers an alphabetic-dominant cluster (purity 0.516) that decoder cosine similarity entirely fails to isolate (purity 0.000 under K=10 partition), demonstrating that activation-context topology carries information orthogonal to decoder geometry.
- **Robustness across hyperparameters and corpora**: Cluster structure is stable across graph construction settings (W∈{5,10,15}, K∈{30,50}, C∈{3,5}) and transfers to a Python-code corpus with ARI 0.944 and NMI 0.928.
- **Honest benchmarking**: The paper explicitly acknowledges that the token-histogram baseline outperforms the graph view on overall purity, positioning the contribution as complementary rather than dominant.

## Methodology

**Setup.** The study uses the 6-RES-JB sparse autoencoder from the open-sae-soup collection, a ReLU SAE trained on GPT-2 Small's layer-6 residual stream with 24,576 features (expansion factor 32). A synthetic mixed-domain corpus of ~78,749 token positions is generated from 13 surface-register templates (Python/JavaScript code, Bash, math, URLs, JSON, social media, natural language paragraphs, formal email, Spanish/French/German/Japanese passages) to elicit diverse surface motifs.

**Graph construction (Section 4.2).** For each of N=2,048 selected features (activation fraction 0.1%–98%), high-activation events are collected when the feature's activation exceeds its 50th-percentile threshold. Within a window W=10 around each event, the top K=30 most frequently appearing tokens become graph nodes; their log-scaled co-occurrence frequency determines the node label (binned into 64 discrete bins). An undirected edge connects two nodes if they co-occur at least C=3 times; edge weight equals the co-occurrence count.

**WL-style kernel (Section 4.3).** Starting from log-scaled frequency bins as initial node labels, h=3 refinement iterations update each node's label as a weighted average of its neighbours' labels (weighted by edge co-occurrence counts), re-binned into 64 bins. The kernel value K(G_f, G_f') is the inner product of the resulting per-graph node-label-count histograms. The N×N matrix is normalised by the geometric mean of diagonal entries, symmetrised, then embedded via kernel PCA (2 components) and clustered with k-means (K=10, n_init=20).

**Baselines.** Two comparison methods are used: (i) cosine similarity between SAE decoder weight vectors (L2-normalised), and (ii) cosine similarity between normalised top-K token frequency histograms.

**Evaluation.** Cluster quality is assessed via a heuristic token-type purity metric: each token is labelled symbolic/alphabetic/numeric/mixed based on its character composition; each feature inherits the plurality label of its top-K tokens; cluster purity measures label homogeneity. Robustness is tested via 10 k-means seeds, hyperparameter grid search, feature-selection cutoff sweeps, a directed graph variant (Appendix E), and a cross-corpus Python-code spot check (Appendix F).

## Results & Highlights

- **Cluster purity comparison (Table 1):** WL-style kernel achieves alphabetic purity 0.516 vs. 0.000 for decoder cosine (the decoder cosine clustering finds zero alphabetic-dominant clusters under K=10). Overall purity: token histogram 0.854 > WL 0.760 > decoder cosine 0.754.
- **Motif families recovered:** 9 of 10 WL clusters are symbolic-dominant (punctuation-heavy); cluster 8 is the sole alphabetic-dominant cluster (purity 51.6%). Qualitative inspection reveals tightly connected punctuation subgraphs, mixed alphanumeric code-like patterns, and loosely connected language/script-specific token communities.
- **Ablation (Table 2):** Zeroing edge weights drops alphabetic purity to 0.000 while leaving overall purity unchanged, confirming edges (not just node-label histograms) drive the alphabetic cluster. Shuffling node labels raises alphabetic purity to 0.584 (ARI 0.618 vs. default), indicating the kernel relies primarily on edge structure and marginal frequency rather than specific token identities.
- **Hyperparameter robustness:** Mean purity ranges 0.655–0.812 (mean 0.748±0.051) across the 12-point grid; qualitative motif families persist throughout.
- **Multi-seed stability:** σ≤0.01 across 10 k-means seeds for all reported metrics.
- **Cross-corpus transfer (Table 7):** Re-running on a 600-snippet Python code corpus yields 99.1% feature overlap with the original 2,048 features, ARI 0.944, NMI 0.928 against the main-corpus clustering—cluster structure is largely corpus-stable.
- **Directed graph variant (Table 5):** Replacing undirected edges with lower-triangular approximation of autoregressive order preserves overall purity (0.754 vs. 0.757) but eliminates the alphabetic-dominant cluster (ARI 0.123 vs. undirected), suggesting the causal approximation discards half the co-occurrence signal needed for that cluster to form.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The core idea—representing SAE features as co-occurrence graphs and comparing them via graph kernels—is genuinely novel for the mechanistic interpretability literature. Graph kernels are well-established in chemoinformatics and graph classification, but their application to SAE feature analysis is new. The specific WL variant (frequency-binned, weighted-averaging) is a reasonable and principled adaptation, though its departure from the standard WL subtree kernel is not rigorously benchmarked against the standard implementation.

**Rigor:** The paper is unusually careful about its own limitations, explicitly flagging that: (a) the token-histogram baseline beats the graph view on overall purity; (b) purity differences as small as 0.006 (0.760 vs. 0.754) should not be over-interpreted without bootstrap confidence intervals; (c) the evaluation relies on heuristic token-type labels rather than human validation; (d) the corpus is synthetic rather than naturalistic. The ablation in Table 2 is informative. However, the paper does not compute confidence intervals over the feature sample, and the heuristic purity metric is a weak proxy for interpretability.

**Reproducibility:** High. Full hyperparameter specifications are given (Section 5.4), all random seeds are fixed (42), the pipeline is described step-by-step in Appendix A, and code will be publicly released. Dependencies (PyTorch, Transformers, NetworkX, scikit-learn, etc.) are standard.

**Significance:** Moderate and carefully scoped. The finding that graph structure recovers alphabetic clusters invisible to decoder cosine is a concrete, non-trivial empirical observation. However, the overall improvement over token-histogram baselines is small and the study is limited to one SAE layer of one model on a synthetic corpus. The broader claim that this is a "complementary perspective" is justified and appropriately hedged.

**Red flags:** (1) The token-histogram baseline outperforms the proposed method on overall purity—the primary metric—which is acknowledged but may limit the paper's impact. (2) The custom WL kernel is not benchmarked against standard GraKeL/graphkit-learn implementations, leaving open whether the alphabetic-cluster gap is kernel-design-specific or would survive under standard WL. (3) All experiments are on a single synthetic corpus (not naturalistic text), a single SAE architecture (ReLU, not Gated/TopK/JumpReLU), and a single residual layer—generalisation is unknown. (4) The purity metric is heuristic and not validated against human judgments of feature interpretability. (5) Scalability is a hard wall: O(N²·h) kernel computation is intractable beyond ~2,048 features without approximation.

## Strengths

- Intellectually honest: explicitly acknowledges where the baseline wins and where its own numbers should be read with caution.
- Clean, reproducible experimental design with fixed seeds and full hyperparameter documentation.
- Graph-structured representation is a principled and transferable idea that could generalise to other SAE architectures and layers.
- The ablation study (Table 2) carefully isolates the contribution of edge structure vs. node labels vs. co-occurrence marginals.
- Robustness across hyperparameter grid (12 configurations) and corpora (ARI 0.944 cross-corpus) is convincingly demonstrated.
- Identifies a concrete, measurable gap between decoder geometry and activation-context topology (0.516 vs. 0.000 alphabetic purity), which is a falsifiable, non-trivial empirical contribution.

## Limitations

- **Scope:** Single model (GPT-2 Small), single SAE architecture (ReLU, 6-RES-JB), single residual layer (layer 6)—claims cannot be generalised to modern SAE variants or frontier models without further experiments.
- **Synthetic corpus:** The probing corpus is procedurally generated from templates, not drawn from any natural text distribution; generalisation to OpenWebText or naturalistic code corpora is an open empirical question.
- **Scalability:** The full N×N kernel matrix is O(N²), which is intractable for the full 24,576-feature dictionary (let alone 131,072-feature modern SAEs) without approximation methods not yet implemented.
- **Weak evaluation metric:** Token-type purity is a heuristic label based on character composition, not a validated measure of feature interpretability or semantic coherence; human evaluation is absent.
- **Non-standard kernel:** The custom WL variant is not compared to the standard WL subtree kernel, leaving open whether the observed gaps are robust to kernel design choices.
- **No causal claims:** The paper explicitly does not provide mechanistic interpretations of individual features or establish why certain structural motifs correspond to particular computations in GPT-2.
- **Missing confidence intervals:** No bootstrap intervals over the feature sample are reported; differences of ~0.006 in purity are noted as potentially within noise but not formally tested.

## 相关性

本文提出的图核方法为SAE特征的系统性组织提供了一个新的结构性视角，对于从事机理可解释性研究、希望理解大语言模型内部特征空间组织的研究者具有直接参考价值；同时，其在GPT-2 Small上的proof-of-concept设计也为探索更大规模模型特征字典的分析工具提供了方法论基础。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the alphabetic-cluster gap (WL purity 0.516 vs. decoder cosine 0.000) survive when the standard GraKeL WL subtree kernel is used instead of the custom frequency-binned variant?
2. How do the graph-structured motif families change across different residual layers of GPT-2 Small—do earlier layers show syntactic motifs and later layers semantic ones, consistent with the layer-wise specialisation hypothesis?
3. Can Nyström approximations or random feature maps for graph kernels scale this approach to the full 24,576-feature dictionary (or the 131,072-feature SAEs from Gao et al.)?
4. Do the same motif families emerge when the analysis is repeated on Gated SAE, TopK SAE, or JumpReLU SAE features—are the graph-structural clusters an artefact of ReLU activation or a more general property?
5. What is the relationship between WL-style graph clusters and the "lobes" of co-occurrence topology identified by Li et al. (2024) in the geometric analysis of SAE feature structure? Do they partition the same semantic subspaces?
6. Would human raters agree that features within the same WL-style cluster are more semantically related than features across clusters—i.e., does the heuristic token-type purity metric correlate with human interpretability judgments?
