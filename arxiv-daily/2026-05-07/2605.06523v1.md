> **一句话总结：** 本文通过"周期性Rank-1替换"实验发现，RLVR中大量非Rank-1参数更新成分并不贡献推理能力提升，反而导致隐式奖励过拟合；Rank-1方向捕获推理增益，而其他成分维护安全性、世界知识等通用能力，且LoRA的输出端（A矩阵）比输入端更易与Rank-1方向对齐。

> **TL;DR:** This paper uses Periodic Rank-1 Substitution—periodically stripping all non-rank-1 weight update components during GRPO/DAPO/GSPO training—to show that RLVR achieves comparable test-set reasoning performance with rank-1-only updates even when training rewards are substantially lower. This demonstrates implicit reward overfitting: standard RLVR over-optimizes the sampling strategy rather than genuinely building new reasoning capacity. The non-rank-1 components, while unhelpful for reasoning, are essential for preserving safety, instruction-following, and world knowledge. The singular spectrum of RLVR weight updates universally follows a "leading spike + heavy tail" pattern, and LoRA's output-side matrix aligns well with the rank-1 direction while its input-side matrix does not—a geometric asymmetry explained theoretically via backpropagation analysis.

## 核心贡献

- **发现隐式奖励过拟合现象**：通过周期性Rank-1替换（每10步将参数更新截断为Rank-1近似），在Countdown-3to4和GSM8K等数据集上证明，即便训练奖励显著低于标准RLVR，测试集推理性能依然相当甚至持平，表明标准RLVR存在对训练数据奖励的隐式过拟合。
- **澄清非Rank-1成分的双重角色**：非Rank-1参数更新不贡献推理能力，但对安全性（SafetyBench）、指令遵循（IF-Eval）、世界知识（MMLU/MMLU-Pro）等通用能力至关重要，直接提取Rank-1模型会显著损害这些能力。
- **揭示RLVR奇异谱的普遍规律**：几乎所有线性层的参数更新奇异值分布均呈现"第一奇异值突出峰值 + 重尾分布"的一致模式，与随机噪声的均匀分布截然不同。
- **分析LoRA在RLVR中的几何非对称性**：LoRA的输出端矩阵（A矩阵，对应左奇异向量/输出空间）在训练中易与Rank-1方向对齐，而输入端矩阵（B矩阵）则因输入空间特征的高度上下文依赖性而难以对齐，并给出了基于梯度反传的理论证明。

## Key Contributions

- **Identification of implicit reward overfitting in RLVR**: Via Periodic Rank-1 Substitution (truncating weight updates to rank-1 every 10 steps), the paper shows that test-set reasoning performance is statistically comparable to full RLVR despite markedly lower training rewards, indicating that standard RLVR overfits the reward signal rather than genuinely learning new reasoning logic.
- **Functional dissection of non-rank-1 components**: Non-rank-1 update components do not improve reasoning but are critical for preserving out-of-domain capabilities (safety, instruction-following, world knowledge). One-time rank-1 extraction after full RLVR consistently degrades SafetyBench and MMLU scores across model families (Qwen2.5, Llama3.1, DeepMath-Zero).
- **Universal spectral signature of RLVR updates**: The singular spectrum of weight update matrices across nearly all linear layers universally shows a dominant first singular value followed by a heavy-tailed quasi-linear decay—a structured, RL-induced pattern rather than stochastic noise.
- **Geometric asymmetry of LoRA in RLVR**: The output-side LoRA matrix (A) aligns strongly with the left singular vectors of the true rank-1 update; the input-side matrix (B) does not. This is explained theoretically: the gradient of A is dominated by the backpropagated output signal (a globally alignable direction), while the gradient of B is dominated by input activations (highly context-dependent, dispersed, and hard to align with a low-rank structure).
- **Theoretical unification of RLVR algorithms**: An appendix shows that GRPO, DAPO, and GSPO all reduce to signed weighted supervised fine-tuning, differing only in how they define per-token or per-sequence weights, and that token-level gradients are rank-1 matrices—providing theoretical grounding for rank-1 dominance.

## Methodology

The paper proceeds in three interlocking empirical and theoretical threads:

**1. Periodic Rank-1 Substitution**
The key probe is a modified training loop: every 10 steps, the accumulated weight update ΔW (relative to the base model) is decomposed via SVD, and only the rank-1 component (rescaled by Frobenius norm to match the full update magnitude) is retained and added to the base model, which then serves as the new starting point for the next 10-step window. This is applied during GRPO training on Countdown-3to4 (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct) and GSM8K (Llama3.1-8B-Instruct). Training reward curves and test-set accuracy are tracked throughout.

**2. Out-of-domain capability analysis**
To separate the role of non-rank-1 components, a one-time rank-1 extraction is applied after the full RLVR training pipeline across five model variants spanning different sizes, families, and RL methods (GRPO, DAPO). Evaluations are run on IF-Eval (instruction-following), MMLU/MMLU-Pro (world knowledge), and SafetyBench (safety), comparing full models against their rank-1-extracted counterparts.

**3. Singular spectrum analysis and LoRA alignment study**
SVD is performed on ΔW for all linear layers at multiple training checkpoints (steps 10 through 80+). The singular value distributions are visualized and characterized. For LoRA analysis, rank-1 LoRA adapters are trained on a subset of OpenThought-1.2M using GRPO, and alignment between ΔW_LoRA and the true rank-1 subspace ΔW^(1) is measured using two metrics: Frobenius cosine similarity (global parameter-space correlation) and principal angles (left and right subspace angles). A theoretical derivation via chain rule backpropagation formalizes why the output-side (A) aligns more than the input-side (B).

## Results & Highlights

- **Comparable test accuracy with lower training reward**: On Countdown-3to4 (Qwen2.5-7B) and GSM8K (Llama3.1-8B), Periodic Rank-1 Substitution achieves test accuracy within a marginal gap of full GRPO while sustaining substantially lower training rewards—a clear train/test reward mismatch indicating overfitting in standard RLVR.
- **Safety degradation from rank-1 extraction**: SafetyBench accuracy drops noticeably for Qwen2.5-7B-R1 and Llama3.1-8B rank-1 variants. For example, Qwen2.5-7B-R1 drops from 77.41 to 73.22 on SafetyBench after rank-1 extraction; Llama3.1-8B-Thinking-R1 is a notable exception (performance improves after extraction), explained by its multi-stage SFT+GRPO+CoT pipeline causing SFT knowledge to leak into the rank-1 subspace.
- **Universal spectral pattern**: Across essentially all linear layers and all checkpoints from step 10 to 80, the singular spectrum exhibits the same shape: a sharp spike at σ₁ followed by heavy-tailed decay. Deeper layers (closer to the output) exhibit the "leading spike" pattern more clearly.
- **LoRA asymmetry confirmed**: The left principal angle (output-side) is consistently and substantially smaller than the right principal angle (input-side) across all 28 layers, while Frobenius cosine similarity is near zero—confirming directional alignment in the output subspace without global parameter-space correlation.
- **Theoretical derivation**: The per-token gradient backpropagated to weight matrix W is δx^T (rank-1). The full gradient is the expectation of these rank-1 matrices, explaining why RL training produces rank-1 dominant updates. The A matrix gradient is αg(x^T b) — dominated by the scalar x^T b, pushing it toward g (the alignable output signal) — while B's gradient is αx(g^T a) — dominated by x (scattered, context-dependent input activations).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: Moderate-to-high. The rank-1 dominance observation builds on Cai et al. (2025), but the Periodic Rank-1 Substitution probe is a clever and original experimental design that reveals overfitting more cleanly than post-hoc extraction. The geometric asymmetry of LoRA and the theoretical gradient analysis are new and insightful contributions. The spectral signature observation, while descriptive, is thorough.

**Rigor**: The core experiments are run on only two datasets and two model families (7B/8B scale), which is a limitation the authors themselves acknowledge. The claim of "implicit reward overfitting" is strongly worded but the evidence is sound within the experimental scope. The use of multiple out-of-domain benchmarks (IF-Eval, MMLU, MMLU-Pro, SafetyBench) for the capability analysis adds credibility. Statistical error bars are reported in Table 1.

**Reproducibility**: Experimental details are provided with reasonable specificity (learning rates, batch sizes, sampling parameters, hardware). However, the authors note that Llama3.1-8B experiments require at least 96 GB VRAM, which limits accessibility. No code repository is mentioned in the paper. The Periodic Rank-1 Substitution procedure is clearly described and should be straightforward to implement.

**Significance**: High for the RLVR/RLHF community. The paper provides a mechanistic explanation for why pass@k degrades under RLVR (the model is optimizing sampling strategy, not expanding reasoning capacity), corroborates concerns raised by Yue et al. (2025) about RL not incentivizing reasoning beyond the base model, and offers a practical implication: non-rank-1 components should be preserved for safety and general capability even if they are dispensable for reasoning benchmarks.

**Red flags**: (1) All experiments are confined to 7B-8B models due to compute constraints — the findings may not transfer to larger models. (2) The "implicit reward overfitting" framing is compelling but not definitively proven; alternative explanations (e.g., the rank-1 constraint acting as regularization rather than exposing overfitting) are not fully ruled out. (3) The Llama3.1-8B-Thinking-R1 counterexample (rank-1 extraction improves performance) is explained post-hoc and somewhat complicates the narrative. (4) The paper conflates two distinct phenomena—reward overfitting and low-rank structure—which, while related, may benefit from cleaner separation.

## Strengths

- The Periodic Rank-1 Substitution is a genuinely novel and clean experimental probe for separating reasoning-relevant from reasoning-irrelevant weight update components.
- The paper is thorough in its empirical coverage: multiple model families, multiple RL algorithms (GRPO, DAPO), multiple downstream benchmarks, and multiple training checkpoints for spectral analysis.
- The theoretical derivation connecting token-level gradient rank-1 structure to LoRA geometric asymmetry is coherent and adds depth beyond pure empiricism.
- The finding that non-rank-1 components protect safety and general knowledge is practically important for deployment decisions around RLVR-trained models.
- The authors are intellectually honest about limitations (compute scale, architecture generality) and engage carefully with concurrent conflicting evidence (the Llama counterexample).

## Limitations

- Experiments limited to 7B-8B parameter models; behavior at 30B+ scale is unknown and may differ meaningfully.
- Only two reasoning datasets (Countdown-3to4 and GSM8K) are used for the core overfitting claim; results on more diverse tasks (coding, science, multi-step QA) are absent.
- No code or checkpoint release is mentioned, which limits reproducibility.
- The paper does not propose a concrete training improvement that fully leverages the insights (e.g., a modified RLVR algorithm that simultaneously exploits rank-1 reasoning gains while preserving non-rank-1 capabilities).
- The Llama3.1-8B-Thinking-R1 counterexample is explained but points to a gap: the framework does not account for models trained with multi-stage pipelines involving SFT, which are increasingly common.
- The LoRA alignment analysis uses rank-1 LoRA exclusively; it is unclear how the asymmetry scales with higher LoRA ranks.

## Relevance

本文直接揭示了RLVR（以验证为奖励的强化学习）的内在机制与局限：推理能力提升仅集中于参数更新的Rank-1方向，而标准训练存在隐式奖励过拟合。这对理解大语言模型强化学习训练的本质、以及设计更高效的RL后训练范式具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the implicit reward overfitting phenomenon persist at 30B+ scale, or does larger model capacity reduce the gap between training reward and test generalization?
2. Can a modified RLVR algorithm explicitly constrain updates to the rank-1 subspace during training (rather than post-hoc substitution) while maintaining safety and general capabilities through a complementary mechanism?
3. The theoretical analysis shows per-token gradients are rank-1; does the aggregated gradient truly converge to rank-1 dominance under realistic sampling distributions, or does this require specific reward signal properties?
4. How does the "leading spike + heavy tail" spectral pattern evolve over much longer training runs (thousands of steps)? Does the heavy tail grow, stabilize, or collapse?
5. For LoRA with rank > 1, does the output-side alignment asymmetry generalize, and at what rank does the input-side alignment catch up?
6. The paper argues RLVR optimizes sampling efficiency rather than reasoning capacity—does this mean that increasing pass@k at inference time is a more principled alternative to RLVR for eliciting reasoning?
7. What is the relationship between the spectral structure identified here and entropy collapse (Cui et al. 2025)? Are they the same underlying phenomenon viewed from different angles?
