> **一句话总结：** 本文将激活修补（activation patching）实验的结果编码为"修补效果图"（patch-effect graphs），并用图核方法（WL子树核、谱核等）对这些图进行分类，在GPT-2 Small的间接宾语识别任务上，局部边槽（edge-slot）特征达到完美分类精度（1.000），同时揭示了现有机械可解释性基准中存在的表面线索混淆问题。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes reframing mechanistic interpretability as a graph machine-learning problem: activation-patching experiments on a frozen transformer are aggregated into directed weighted graphs (one per task-corruption "slice"), then classified using graph kernels (Weisfeiler-Lehman subtree, spectral, graphlet, fixed edge-slot) and SVM. On GPT-2 Small's Indirect Object Identification (IOI) task, localised edge-slot features achieve perfect slice-discrimination accuracy (1.000), outperforming both global graph-shape descriptors and a learned GNN encoder, while also exposing a surface-cue confound in the widely-used name_swap vs. abba benchmark. The framework is a methodology paper with a small controlled pilot, not a claim of new circuit discoveries.

## 核心贡献

- **将机械可解释性重构为图机器学习问题**：每个（任务族，腐化类型）切片（slice）生成一张以模型组件为节点、修补效果为边权的有向加权图，从而允许跨切片的系统化比较，而非仅在单个提示内部分析。
- **提出三种边构建方法**：直接影响图（DI，因果中介）、偏相关图（PC，去除共因结构）、共影响图（CI，Pearson相关），并在相同IOI协议下实证比较了可扩展的CI和PC，通过筛选配对修补验证了DI的部分候选边。
- **系统评估多种图表示**：WL子树核、谱核、图元核（graphlet）、固定布局边槽特征、哈希固定布局特征，以及GNN编码器基准，在GPT-2 Small和DistilGPT-2上均进行测试，给出了准确率-维度Pareto前沿。
- **明确暴露基准污染问题**：原始name\_swap vs. abba的比较被提示层面的名字计数所混淆；加入surface-balanced控制（abba vs. second\_subject\_swap）后，提示-only基准从1.000跌至0.550，而图表示和原始修补张量保持完美分类，证明图信号来自真正的修补结构而非表面文本特征。
- **提供端到端可复现流水线**：代码和合成提示生成脚本计划以MIT许可证开放，所有实验仅使用公开GPT-2权重和合成提示，无需私有数据。

## Key Contributions

- **Reframing mechanistic interpretability as graph ML**: Each (task-family, corruption-type) slice maps to one directed weighted graph over a fixed node set (layers × token positions × component types), enabling cross-slice learning rather than per-prompt analysis.
- **Three edge-construction methods**: Direct-influence (DI, causal mediation via paired patching), partial-correlation (PC, inverse covariance), and co-influence (CI, Pearson correlation). CI is O(|V|) additional forward passes per prompt; DI requires O(|V|²). Empirical comparison shows CI and PC give different WL subtree representations, with PC recovering strong fixed edge-slot performance in the surface-balanced setting.
- **Comprehensive graph embedding comparison**: Five feature maps (WL subtree, spectral, graphlet, fixed-layout edge-slot variants, hashed fixed-layout) benchmarked against raw patch-effect tensors, prompt-only surface cues, and a learned 2-layer GCN+SVM encoder. Localised edge-slot features dominate the accuracy-dimensionality Pareto frontier.
- **Surface-cue confound disclosure**: The historical name_swap vs. abba IOI benchmark is surface-separable by name-count alone; the paper introduces a surface-balanced control that collapses the prompt-only baseline while leaving graph-based representations unaffected.
- **Screened DI causal validation**: CI/PC-ranked candidate edges show higher activation-influence scores under paired patching (I mean 0.373/0.305) than random (0.122) or low-rank CI (0.102) candidates, providing targeted edge-level causal evidence.

## Methodology

The pipeline proceeds in four stages:

**1. Activation Patching Engine.** Given a frozen transformer f₀, paired examples (clean, corrupted) are generated for a target task. For each node u in the network (residual-stream positions across all layers and token slots), the scalar patch effect E_u^(i) = O(f̃_{f₀}(x_crp; u)) − O(f₀(x_crp)) is computed. For ordered pairs (u, v), joint patching yields E_{u,v}^(i) for the direct-influence construction.

**2. Graph Construction.** Each slice s (a task-family × corruption-type pair, N=100 examples) yields one directed weighted graph G_s = (V, E_s, w_s) over a fixed node set. Three edge-weight choices:
- **DI (Direct Influence)**: w_s^DI(u→v) = E_i[E_{u,v}^(i) − E_u^(i)], measuring causal mediation. Cost: O(|V|²) forward passes.
- **PC (Partial Correlation)**: w_s^PC(u→v) = −Σ_{uv}^{-1} / sqrt(Σ_{uu}^{-1} Σ_{vv}^{-1}), from ridge-regularised covariance inversion. Cost: O(|V|³) offline, no extra forward passes.
- **CI (Co-Influence)**: w_s^CI(u→v) = corr({E_u^(i)}, {E_v^(i)}), Pearson correlation of effect profiles. Cost: O(|V|) additional passes per prompt.

Edges are sparsified by retaining the top-k=5 outgoing edges per node by absolute weight. Directionality is enforced by layer-then-token ordering to respect transformer information flow.

**3. Graph Embedding.** Five feature maps φ_graph:
- **WL subtree**: Histogram of labelled subtree patterns up to depth H_WL; ~10³–10⁴ dimensions, node labels encode (layer, token, component type).
- **Spectral**: Normalised graph Laplacian eigenvalue/eigenvector statistics; d=96 fixed dimensions.
- **Graphlet**: Directed 3-node subgraph motif counts; d=38.
- **Fixed-layout edge-slot**: Each possible edge (u,v) maps to a fixed coordinate; variants are weighted, binary, and signed. Dimension N(N−1) = 14,280 for GPT-2 with residual-stream-only nodes.
- **Hashed fixed-layout**: Hash modulo d=1,024; dimension-bounded regardless of model size, enabling scalability.

**4. Kernel SVM Classification.** A kernel matrix K ∈ R^{S×S} is computed from graph embeddings using either a linear kernel K^lin_{st} = x_s^T x_t or RBF kernel K^RBF_{st} = exp(−γ‖x_s − x_t‖²). SVM is trained on 32 bootstrap-resampled training graphs per slice (75% resampling) and evaluated on 32 disjoint test graphs. Results are reported as mean ± std across seeds {7, 42, 123}.

**Baselines**: Raw patch-effect tensor (flattened node effects), prompt-only surface cues (6-dimensional name-count features), and a 2-layer GCN encoder (hidden dim 32) trained end-to-end with the SVM target.

**Null controls**: Edge shuffle (reassign edge targets, preserve weight multiset) and weight shuffle (permute weights across edges, preserve positions) test whether signal depends on edge-slot identity rather than marginal weight statistics.

## Results & Highlights

**E1: Patch-effect tensor validation.** Mean patch-effect heatmaps (layer × token) show structured, non-random signal: name_swap concentrates positive effects at early token positions and the last two tokens; abba shows high-magnitude signal near middle token positions with a distinct negative band. Signal concentrates in layers 5–10, consistent with the known IOI circuit. A screened paired-patching study (N=10 prompts, 10 candidate edges) finds all 10 CI-top edges survive FDR correction (BH, α=0.05), concentrated at layers 9–11.

**E2: Graph construction comparison.** CI and PC graphs behave differently under WL subtree features: on the confounded name_swap vs. abba benchmark, CI achieves WL+linear = 0.912 while PC drops to 0.552. On the surface-balanced abba vs. second_subject_swap, both CI and PC recover WL+linear = 1.000 and 0.917 respectively, with fixed edge-slot features at 1.000 in both cases. This indicates the edge-construction rule materially changes graph topology, and construction choice interacts with the specific benchmark.

**E3: Representation baselines (main result, Table 2).**
| Representation | Kernel | Accuracy |
|---|---|---|
| Prompt surface cues | Linear | 1.000 (confounded) |
| Raw patch-effect tensor | Linear | 1.000 |
| Fixed layout signed | Linear | **1.000** |
| WL subtree | Linear | 0.912 |
| GNN encoder + SVM | Linear | 0.609 |
| Edge shuffle (null) | — | 0.505 |
| Chance | — | 0.500 |

Localised edge-slot features match the raw tensor ceiling while providing structured compression and edge-level auditability. GNN encoder underperforms hand-crafted features with only S=2 slices and 32 bootstrap graphs per class. Shape descriptors (spectral: 0.500, graphlet: 0.760) carry less discriminative information than localised features.

**E4: Surface-balanced control.** The abba vs. second_subject_swap comparison removes the name-count confound. Prompt-only baseline drops to 0.550; graph representations and raw patch effects remain at 1.000. This cleanly separates patch-signal compression from surface-level shortcuts in the historical benchmark.

**E5: Cross-model transfer.** Applying the same CI-graph protocol to DistilGPT-2 (6 layers, d_model=768), localised edge-slot features transfer with negligible accuracy drop (fixed layout signed: 1.000 on both models). WL subtree compression drops ~22 points (0.912→0.688), indicating subtree-level graph compression is model-size dependent while edge-slot identity transfers robustly.

**E6: Multi-slice pilot (S=4).** Four-slice evaluation (IOI+induction suites) achieves edge-slot accuracy of 0.750 vs. chance 0.250 and edge-shuffle 0.257, demonstrating protocol viability beyond binary IOI. The higher surface-cue baseline (0.763) in the diverse suite reflects cross-task vocabulary differences.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: The core idea of treating activation-patching profiles as graph objects and applying graph kernels is genuinely novel. Prior mechanistic interpretability work (Wang et al. 2023, Elhage et al. 2021) identifies circuits manually or per-model; this paper proposes a systematic comparison framework. The application of WL, spectral, and graphlet kernels to interpretability graphs is new. However, the paper does not discover new circuits or provide new mechanistic insights — it is a representation/methodology paper, and the conclusions are relatively modest.

**Rigor**: The experimental design is honest and self-aware. The authors explicitly flag that (a) S=2 slices is too few for kernel-geometry conclusions, (b) DI graph construction is not yet benchmarked at full scale due to O(|V|²) cost, (c) the pilot is not task-general. They introduce surface-balanced controls proactively and acknowledge the raw tensor baseline is highly competitive. The screened DI validation is small (N=10, 10 edges) but provides targeted evidence. Multiple seeds are used and standard deviations reported.

**Reproducibility**: High in principle — synthetic prompts, public GPT-2 weights, code release planned. But code is not yet released at submission time, so independent verification is not yet possible.

**Significance**: The benchmark confound disclosure (name_swap vs. abba is surface-separable) is the most immediately impactful finding and relevant to anyone using the historical IOI benchmark. The framework itself is a useful conceptual contribution for scaling mechanistic interpretability analysis. However, the pilot is narrow (one model family, one primary task, S≤4 slices) and the practical utility for larger models is unproven.

**Red flags**:
- The raw patch-effect tensor baseline (1.000 accuracy) is as good as or better than most graph representations, raising the question of what structured graph compression actually buys beyond auditability. The paper acknowledges this honestly.
- The GNN encoder performs poorly (0.609), which the authors attribute to data efficiency with only S=2 slices — a reasonable explanation, but it means the "learned" approach is not validated.
- The paper is a preprint from a non-specialist lab with no track record in this specific area. Claims should be read with appropriate prior uncertainty.
- Full DI graph benchmarking — which would directly test whether causal mediation adds information over correlation — is deferred to future work as a "scaling target," leaving the central methodological comparison incomplete.
- The S=4 multi-slice results use only 8 bootstrap graphs per slice (vs. 32 in the main experiments) to keep costs manageable, making those results less statistically reliable.

## Strengths

- Transparent about limitations and explicitly scopes claims to a "controlled pilot."
- Introduces a surface-balanced IOI control that exposes a real confound in a widely-cited benchmark — this is a concrete, verifiable contribution.
- Comparative evaluation across five graph feature families plus multiple baselines provides a useful empirical map of representation quality vs. dimensionality.
- Cross-model replication on DistilGPT-2 shows some features generalise and some do not, adding nuance beyond single-model claims.
- The edge-slot identity finding (localised features dominate Pareto frontier) is practically actionable: it suggests that preserving which specific (layer, token) pair is connected matters more than global graph topology statistics for current benchmarks.
- Plans for MIT-licensed code release and full reproducibility from synthetic prompts.

---

## Limitations

- **Pilot scale only**: Two underlying IOI slices for the main benchmark; kernel-geometry analysis (eigenvalue spectrum, CKA alignment) requires more slices to be meaningful, explicitly deferred.
- **DI graph not benchmarked at full scale**: The causally most principled construction (direct influence via causal mediation) requires O(|V|²) forward passes and is only validated via a small screened subset, not in the main classification experiments.
- **Single task family**: All main results are on the IOI task in GPT-2 Small. The four-slice pilot is preliminary. Generalization to diverse task families, larger models (GPT-3, LLaMA-scale), and non-IOI circuits is undemonstrated.
- **Raw tensor baseline is ceiling-competitive**: The unstructured raw patch-effect vector achieves 1.000 accuracy in most settings, making it difficult to argue that graph structure adds predictive value beyond interpretability and auditability benefits.
- **GNN encoder underperforms**: End-to-end learning with 32 graphs per class is insufficient for a 2-layer GCN; the data efficiency problem may persist even with more slices, and the paper does not explore other learned approaches.
- **Synthetic prompts only**: All experiments use fixed template-generated prompts, not naturalistic text. Generalization to real-world prompts is unknown.
- **No statistical test for cross-model comparison**: Tables 4 and 5 report results from different seeds/settings across models; formal significance testing for cross-model claims is absent.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. When full DI graph benchmarking is completed at scale, does causal mediation (DI) provide meaningfully higher classification accuracy than cheap correlation-based CI graphs, or do they converge on the same discriminative signal?
2. How do these graph representations behave on larger transformer models (e.g., GPT-2 XL, LLaMA-7B) where the node set is orders of magnitude larger — do hashed fixed-layout features retain their Pareto dominance?
3. The paper shows localised edge-slot identity matters more than global graph shape. Does this imply that mechanistic circuits are better characterised by which specific (layer, token) positions are active than by their global connectivity pattern?
4. Can the surface-balanced control principle (matching target/distractor name counts across corruption types) be operationalised as a standard checklist for future IOI-style benchmarks, and what other confounds might similar analysis reveal in other circuit benchmarks?
5. With more slices (S >> 4), does the kernel similarity geometry reveal interpretable structure — e.g., do IOI slices cluster separately from induction slices, and do corruptions within the same task cluster together?
6. The GNN encoder underperforms hand-crafted features with S=2. Is this a fundamental data-efficiency problem, or would contrastive or self-supervised pre-training on larger patch-effect datasets close the gap?
