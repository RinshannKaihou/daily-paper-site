> **一句话总结：** SARQC 在现有训练后量化校准目标中引入显著性感知正则化项，通过约束量化权重不偏离原始浮点权重，在 LLaMA2、DeepSeek-MoE 等模型的 W2A16/W3A16/W4A16 设置下，相比 GPTQ 和 AWQ 等基线显著降低困惑度并提升零样本准确率。

> **TL;DR:** SARQC adds a saliency-weighted regularization term to the standard layer-wise reconstruction loss used in post-training quantization (PTQ) of LLMs. The key insight is that minimizing reconstruction error alone can cause quantized weights to drift far from the original FP weights, hurting downstream generalization — especially at aggressive bit-widths (W2A16, W3A16) or with small calibration sets. SARQC penalizes this drift in proportion to each channel's saliency (derived from activation/weight statistics), yielding a drop-in enhancement for both grid-search-based methods (AWQ-style) and Gram-based solvers (GPTQ-style) with no added inference overhead. On dense models (LLaMA2-7B/13B) and MoE models (DeepSeek-MoE-16B, Qwen3-MoE-30B, Mixtral-8x7B), SARQC consistently outperforms its respective baselines across perplexity and 8-task zero-shot accuracy suites.

## 核心贡献

- **识别"权重漂移"问题：** 指出现有仅最小化重建误差的 PTQ 校准方法会导致量化权重偏离原始 FP 权重（weight drift），从而增加泛化风险，并通过 Theorem 3.1 给出了形式化的泛化上界。
- **提出 SARQC 框架：** 在标准重建损失之上引入显著性感知正则项 $\lambda \|(\widehat{W}_l - W_l) S_l\|_F^2$，其中 $S_l = \text{diag}(s_l)$ 对激活/权重显著性较高的通道施加更大惩罚，将"输出失配"与"权重漂移"之间的权衡显式化。
- **双路优化策略：** 将 SARQC 无缝集成进网格搜索（SARQC-GS，类 AWQ）和 Gram 矩阵（SARQC-GBS，类 GPTQ）两类主流 PTQ 流程，改动极小且无需端到端反向传播。
- **理论保障：** 通过 Corollary 3.2 证明带正则项的软惩罚目标与带硬半径约束的受限优化问题在"支撑性"条件下等价，奠定了方法的理论基础。
- **广泛实验验证：** 在密集型（LLaMA2-7B/13B/30B）和 MoE 型（DeepSeek-MoE-16B、Qwen3-MoE-30B、Mixtral-8x7B）LLMs 上，跨 W2A16/W3A16/W4A16 多个精度设置取得一致改进，并可迁移至 OmniQuant 等其他框架。

## Key Contributions

- **Diagnosis of weight drift in PTQ:** Shows formally (Theorem 3.1) that minimizing reconstruction error on limited calibration data does not bound downstream generalization risk; weight drift from FP weights is a separate, uncontrolled quantity that can diverge even when calibration loss is small.
- **SARQC objective:** Augments the standard per-layer reconstruction loss with a saliency-weighted L2 penalty on weight deviation: `min ||W_l X_l - W_hat_l X_l||_F^2 + lambda ||(W_hat_l - W_l) S_l||_F^2`, where `S_l` encodes per-channel saliency derived from activation and weight statistics.
- **Two plug-in solvers:** SARQC-GS integrates into grid-search PTQ pipelines (AWQ-style) by modifying the candidate selection criterion; SARQC-GBS integrates into Gram-based pipelines (GPTQ-style) by replacing `X_l X_l^T` with the regularized curvature matrix `G_l = X_l X_l^T + lambda S_l S_l^T`.
- **Constrained-optimization grounding:** Corollary 3.2 establishes equivalence between the penalized formulation and a hard-constraint weight-drift ball under a "supportedness" condition, connecting SARQC to classical Lagrangian relaxation.
- **Zero inference overhead:** All regularization is applied only during calibration; the quantized weights produced are used identically to those from standard PTQ methods.

## Methodology

**Problem framing.** Post-training quantization selects quantized weights $\widehat{W}_l$ for each layer by minimizing output reconstruction error on a small calibration set. The paper proves (Theorem 3.1) that the true downstream risk decomposes into an empirical calibration risk plus a generalization gap term that grows with the weight drift $R = \|\widehat{W}_l - W_l\|_F$. Minimizing reconstruction error alone does not control $R$, and the two objectives can conflict — small reconstruction error can coexist with large weight drift (illustrated in Figure 1 of the paper).

**SARQC objective.** To directly control weight drift, SARQC adds a regularization term:

$$\min_{\widehat{W}_l \in \mathcal{Q}} \underbrace{\|W_l X_l - \widehat{W}_l X_l\|_F^2}_{\mathcal{L}_\text{recon}} + \lambda \underbrace{\|(\widehat{W}_l - W_l) S_l\|_F^2}_{\mathcal{L}_\text{sar}}$$

where $S_l = \text{diag}(s_l)$ is a diagonal saliency matrix. The saliency vector $s_l$ is derived from activation and weight statistics (the same scaling factors already computed in AWQ/SmoothQuant-style methods), so no new quantities need to be computed. Channels with higher activation/weight saliency receive stronger regularization, which is intuitively correct: those channels are more influential on layer output and more susceptible to quantization degradation.

**SARQC-GS (grid-search variant).** The grid-search over the scaling parameter $\alpha \in [0,1]$ (as in AWQ) is modified so that each candidate $\widehat{W}_l(\alpha)$ is scored by the joint objective rather than reconstruction error alone. Min-max normalization is applied to balance the scale of the two terms before selection.

**SARQC-GBS (Gram-based variant).** In GPTQ-style solvers, the reconstruction error admits a quadratic form involving the Gram matrix $X_l X_l^T$. SARQC-GBS replaces this with a regularized curvature matrix $G_l = X_l X_l^T + \lambda S_l S_l^T$ (up to a normalization factor $\bar{h}_l$). The sequential weight-update and Cholesky-based inversion used in GPTQ proceed unchanged, with $G_l$ substituted for $X_l X_l^T$.

**Hyperparameter selection.** SARQC-GS searches $\lambda \in \{0.1, 0.2, \ldots, 1.0\}$ on calibration data. SARQC-GBS searches $\lambda \in \{0.25, 0.5, 0.75\}$ and $\gamma \in \{0.1, 0.15, 0.35, 0.5\}$ (which controls the relative weight of activation vs. weight statistics in saliency) per layer on calibration data.

## Results & Highlights

**Perplexity (Table 1).** Under the hardest W2A16 setting, standard GPTQ produces perplexity of 792.32 on LLaMA2-7B and 38.03 on Qwen3-MoE-30B. SARQC-GBS(Saliency) reduces these to 225.01 and 22.83, respectively — massive reductions that bring the model from near-unusable to functional. Under W4A16, improvements are smaller but consistent (e.g., SARQC-GS(Saliency) reaches 5.77 vs. AWQ's 5.81 on LLaMA2-7B).

**Zero-shot accuracy (Table 2).** Under W4A16 across 8 benchmarks (PIQA, HellaSwag, MMLU, HumanEval, BoolQ, ARC-E, ARC-C, WinoGrande), SARQC-GS(Saliency) achieves average accuracy of 57.39 on LLaMA2-7B vs. 56.37 for AWQ and 55.82 for GPTQ. On Qwen3-MoE-30B, SARQC-GBS(Saliency) achieves 70.79 vs. 67.95 for GPTQ, a substantial 2.84-point gain.

**Calibration size robustness (Figure 2b).** SARQC-GBS consistently outperforms GPTQ across all calibration sizes (16 to 128 samples), with a larger margin at smaller calibration sets. This confirms the theoretical prediction that SARQC is especially beneficial when calibration data is limited or unrepresentative.

**Saliency vs. identity regularization.** The saliency-aware variant (S_l derived from statistics) consistently outperforms the identity variant (S_l = I, uniform regularization) on 4 out of 5 models for the grid-search solver and 4 out of 5 for the Gram-based solver, validating the design choice.

**Generalizability.** Integrating SARQC into OmniQuant (an orthogonal PTQ framework) consistently improves performance on both LLaMA2-7B and Mixtral-8x7B, demonstrating that SARQC is a general plug-in principle rather than solver-specific.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** The core idea — adding a weight-drift regularizer to PTQ calibration — is conceptually straightforward. The genuine novelty lies in: (1) the formal generalization-risk analysis tying weight drift to downstream performance bounds, (2) the specific saliency-aware weighting that leverages already-available activation statistics, and (3) the clean derivation showing both grid-search and Gram-based solvers can incorporate the regularizer with minimal implementation change. The idea of regularizing toward original weights is not new in machine learning (ridge regression, L2 proximal methods), but its principled application in the PTQ context with theoretical grounding is a meaningful contribution.

**Rigor.** The theoretical claims are well-stated with formal theorems and proofs deferred to appendices. Theorem 3.1 follows from a standard uniform convergence argument over a restricted weight class. Corollary 3.2 on the constrained-penalized equivalence is carefully qualified with a "supportedness" condition rather than claiming unconditional equivalence, which is intellectually honest. The empirical evaluation covers a good range of models (dense and MoE), bit-widths, and benchmarks.

**Reproducibility.** A project page and code repository are mentioned. The calibration setup is clearly described (128 WikiText2 samples, group-size 64/128, A100 80GB GPUs). Hyperparameter grids are reported. One concern: the GPTQ baseline results for MoE models are omitted in the main table due to "lack of MoE architectural support" in the GPTQ implementation used — this is a minor gap in the comparison.

**Significance.** The practical impact is most pronounced at aggressive low-bit settings (W2A16, W3A16) and small calibration sets, which are realistic deployment constraints. The no-inference-overhead property makes adoption attractive. The improvement at W4A16 is more modest but still consistent.

**Red flags.** The paper does not evaluate on extremely large models (70B+) due to computational constraints, which limits claims about scaling. The saliency factor design inherits from prior work (AWQ, SmoothQuant) and the authors acknowledge it may not be optimal. The paper does not address weight-activation quantization (W4A4 or INT8 activations), which is a growing deployment target. The λ and γ hyperparameters are tuned per-layer on the calibration set, adding calibration-time cost (though this is modest relative to the quantization itself).

---

## Strengths

- Theoretically grounded motivation: the generalization-risk bound cleanly explains why reconstruction-only calibration can fail, especially with small or unrepresentative calibration sets.
- Seamless integration into two dominant PTQ paradigms (grid-search and Gram-based) without changing the inference pipeline.
- Empirically consistent gains across diverse architectures (dense LLMs and multiple MoE models) and bit-widths, with particularly large improvements in the practically important low-bit regime.
- Honest treatment of the constrained-penalized equivalence, avoiding the common mistake of claiming the penalized objective is exactly equivalent to the constrained one in a non-convex discrete setting.
- The ablation on calibration size directly validates the theoretical claim about robustness to limited calibration data.

---

## Limitations

- Not evaluated on models larger than 30B; generalization to 70B+ scale is unverified.
- Saliency factors are heuristic (inherited from prior work); a more principled data-driven saliency design could further improve results.
- Layerwise calibration ignores cross-layer error propagation; the theoretical analysis is also conducted layerwise, so error accumulation across layers is not modeled.
- No evaluation on weight-activation quantization (e.g., W4A4), which is increasingly important for throughput-limited inference.
- The λ/γ per-layer hyperparameter search adds calibration-time overhead compared to methods with fixed hyperparameters, though the overhead is small in practice.
- As a preprint, the work has not undergone peer review at the time of this summary.

---

## Relevance

SARQC 对大语言模型训练后量化领域有直接实用价值，尤其对于需要在受限内存设备上部署大模型、或校准数据有限的场景，该方法提供了一个无需修改推理流程即可提升量化质量的通用插件式框架，值得关注其代码发布和后续工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does SARQC interact with activation quantization (W4A4, W8A8)? The current method targets weight-only quantization; extending the saliency regularizer to activation-quantized settings would require rethinking the formulation.
2. Can the saliency matrix $S_l$ be learned or optimized jointly with the quantization parameters rather than derived from fixed statistics? A data-driven or end-to-end approach to saliency might further improve performance.
3. How sensitive is SARQC to the choice of calibration corpus? The paper uses WikiText2, but downstream tasks may have different distributions — does the robustness to small calibration sets extend to out-of-distribution calibration data?
4. How does SARQC compare to rotation-based methods (QuaRot, SpinQuant) that redistribute outliers structurally? These approaches are orthogonal and could potentially be combined with SARQC.
5. The paper shows SARQC benefits shrink somewhat at W4A16 relative to W2A16. Is there a principled way to predict the regime where SARQC's regularization is most beneficial, based on model or quantization properties?
6. Corollary 3.2 requires a "supportedness" condition for the penalized and constrained objectives to be equivalent. How often is this condition violated in practice, and what happens to SARQC's behavior when it is?
