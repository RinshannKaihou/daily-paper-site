> **一句话总结：** 本文证明在证据深度学习（EDL）中，常用的"空缺度"（Vacuity/UM）OOD检测指标对类别数量K的不匹配极度敏感——当OOD数据集比ID数据集多一个类别时，AUROC可虚高0.318，AUPR可虚高0.613，这是评估流程的系统性缺陷而非模型能力的真实反映。

> **TL;DR:** This paper exposes a critical evaluation artefact in vacuity-based OOD detection for EDL-fine-tuned LLMs: when the number of answer classes (K) differs between in-distribution (ID) and out-of-distribution (OOD) datasets — a common but often unnoticed mismatch in MCQA benchmarks — AUROC and AUPR scores are artificially inflated by large margins. The effect is deterministic (no retraining needed to reproduce it) and can make a model appear dramatically better at OOD detection than it actually is. The paper provides a formal proof of vacuity's non-invariance to class expansion, empirical demonstrations on Llama3-8b fine-tuned with standard EDL and IB-EDL, and calls for clearer definitions of ID, OOD, and task-format shift in LLM evaluation. Readers who use vacuity/UM as an OOD signal in EDL fine-tuning pipelines should read this paper before trusting their benchmark numbers.

## 核心贡献

- **发现系统性评估伪影**：当ID和OOD数据集的有效类别数K不匹配时，空缺度（K/S）分数会产生虚高的AUROC和AUPR，幅度可分别高达0.318和0.613（标准EDL），以及0.360和0.683（IB-EDL）。
- **形式化证明**：严格证明了空缺度不具备类别扩展不变性——只有当新增类别获得恰好等于原始类别平均集中度的浓度参数时，空缺度才保持不变，而EDL的证据抑制机制决定了该条件在实践中几乎不成立。
- **重现已发表结果的缺陷**：通过对IB-EDL论文代码的忠实复现，发现原始代码中ARC-C和ARC-E被错误地设为K=5（应为K=4），这一单一错误即可解释文中报告的强OOD检测性能。
- **概念厘清**：区分了MCQA上下文中的"近OOD"（near-OOD，任务格式相似的数据集）、"远OOD"（far-OOD，领域迥异的数据集）和"任务格式偏移"（task-format shift），指出MCQA与CV固定标签分类的根本差异。

## Key Contributions

- **Identification of a systematic evaluation artefact**: Mismatched K between ID and OOD MCQA datasets causes vacuity (K/S) to produce inflated AUROC/AUPR scores without any change in model behaviour. A difference of just 1 in K yields AUROC gains up to +0.318 (standard EDL) and +0.360 (IB-EDL).
- **Formal invariance proof (Proposition 1)**: Vacuity is invariant to adding one class if and only if the new class receives concentration α_{K+1} = S_K / K. Because EDL suppresses evidence for wrong classes, this condition is practically never met, making vacuity structurally non-invariant to class expansion.
- **Reproduction of a published result's flaw**: A faithful re-implementation of IB-EDL (Li et al., 2025) revealed that the authors' released code evaluated ARC-C and ARC-E with K=5 instead of the correct K=4, artificially inflating all reported OOD scores. Correcting K causes large AUROC/AUPR drops across both UM and MP metrics.
- **Conceptual framework for LLM OOD evaluation**: Argues that MCQA fundamentally differs from fixed-label CV classification because class semantics and cardinality are input-dependent, making uncertainty measures defined over the output space non-comparable across datasets with different answer-option counts.

## Methodology

The paper operates primarily as an **evaluation critique and empirical audit** rather than a new method proposal.

**Theoretical component.** The authors formalize vacuity (Uncertainty Mass, UM) as u_K = K/S where S = Σα_i is the total Dirichlet strength. They prove Proposition 1: adding one class leaves vacuity unchanged if and only if the new concentration parameter equals the mean concentration of existing classes (α_{K+1} = S_K / K). Under standard EDL dynamics — which suppress evidence for incorrect classes — this condition is structurally violated, making vacuity sensitive to K mismatches.

**Empirical component.** Two implementations are used:
- *Implementation A*: the original code released by the IB-EDL authors (Li et al., arXiv:2502.06351), fine-tuning Llama3-8b on OBQA with IB-EDL/standard EDL via LoRA (r=8, α=16, dropout=0.1, 10080 steps, 1 A100 GPU).
- *Implementation B*: an independent re-implementation by the paper's author using identical hyperparameters, serving as a cross-check.

**Isolation experiment (Section 3).** Model predictions (logits/evidence) are held fixed while only the evaluated class cardinality K is varied computationally. Because no retraining occurs, any change in AUROC/AUPR is purely an artefact of the evaluation procedure. OOD-only expansion (increasing K for OOD inputs only) is contrasted with matched expansion (increasing K equally for both ID and OOD), demonstrating that matched expansion leaves AUROC/AUPR exactly unchanged.

**Reproduction of IB-EDL results (Section 4).** The authors identify the K-mismatch bug in the original code and re-run evaluation with corrected K values. They also evaluate an additional far-OOD dataset (MMLU Math) to assess whether OOD detection works under larger domain shifts.

**Entropy analysis (Appendix 8).** The same K-sensitivity pattern is shown to hold for normalized Shannon entropy (H/log₂K), confirming the issue is not unique to the K/S formulation of vacuity.

## Results & Highlights

- **K-isolation experiment (Table 1, OBQA → ARC-C):** Baseline (K_ID=4, K_OOD=4): AUROC=0.570, AUPR=0.351. OOD-only expansion to K_OOD=5: AUROC=0.842 (+0.272), AUPR=0.845 (+0.494). OOD-only expansion to K_OOD=8: AUROC=0.922 (+0.352), AUPR=0.935 (+0.584). Matched expansion leaves both metrics exactly at 0.570/0.351, regardless of K.
- **IB-EDL reproduction (Table 3, Implementation A, IB-EDL):** ARC-C with mismatched K=5: AUROC=0.947 (UM). Corrected to K=4: AUROC=0.642 (−0.305). ARC-E mismatched K=5: AUROC=0.940; corrected: AUROC=0.580 (−0.360), AUPR drops 0.683.
- **Standard EDL (Table 4):** Same pattern; ARC-E AUROC drops from 0.836 to 0.556 (−0.280) when K is corrected.
- **Implementation B (Table 5, IB-EDL):** With correct K=4 for ARC-C and ARC-E, UM AUROC is 0.576/0.477 respectively — far below the 0.912/0.902 reported with mismatched K.
- **Far-OOD (MMLU Math):** Both UM and MP show more meaningful OOD separation (AUROC ~0.87-0.95) when the shift is a genuine domain shift, suggesting vacuity may still carry signal under large distributional distances.
- **MP metric:** Max Probability is also affected by K-mismatch (as it is computed over Dirichlet expected probabilities α_i/S), but is governed by a different dependence than K/S and behaves more stably in some conditions.
- **Entropy analysis (Tables 10–11):** Normalized Shannon entropy exhibits the same K-sensitivity pattern in Implementation A but shows zero sensitivity in Implementation B when K is matched correctly, providing additional validation.

## Authors & Credibility

**Claire McNamara** is affiliated with Accenture Labs and Trinity College Dublin. The paper is a single-author work from an industry research lab. The author provides full code at https://github.com/mcnamacl/Vacuity_Analysis/ and discloses use of ChatGPT-5 for sentence polishing and Claude Code for scaffolding, while taking full responsibility for all outputs.

The work is methodologically modest in scope (an audit paper, not a novel method), but the contributions are well-targeted: the reproduction effort is careful, the theoretical proof is elementary but correct, and the experimental design (isolating K while holding model predictions fixed) is clean and convincing. The paper engages seriously with prior work (IB-EDL, R-EDL, I-EDL) and acknowledges its own limitations. The broader concern about vacuity's suitability has been raised elsewhere (cited as "somewhat arbitrary" formulation), lending external validation to the critique.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate. The paper does not propose a new uncertainty method or training objective. Its contribution is identifying and formalizing a specific failure mode in an existing evaluation protocol. The core insight — that K/S is sensitive to K — follows almost immediately from the formula's definition, and a careful practitioner might have caught this during implementation. However, the fact that a published paper (IB-EDL) with released code contains this bug, and that the bug substantially changes conclusions, gives the critique practical significance beyond the obvious.

**Rigor:** Good for what it claims. The theoretical proof is short but correct. The isolation experiment (varying K with fixed predictions) is a principled way to distinguish evaluation artefact from model behaviour. Two independent implementations and multiple datasets provide reasonable robustness. The paper is honest that exact reproduction of fine-tuning results is not achievable due to nondeterminism, and frames this appropriately.

**Reproducibility:** High. The code is released, hyperparameters are fully specified (Table 7), and the key finding (K isolation) is deterministic by construction — no training is required to reproduce Table 1.

**Significance:** Medium-high within its niche. For anyone working with EDL-fine-tuned LLMs on MCQA benchmarks and using vacuity for OOD detection, this is an important warning. The issue is not limited to IB-EDL; it applies to any EDL evaluation where K is treated carelessly. The broader point — that MCQA output spaces are not fixed semantic label spaces and therefore uncertainty measures are not directly comparable across datasets — is a useful conceptual contribution.

**Red flags:**
- The paper is a single-author industry preprint without peer review. The writing acknowledgment of AI assistance (ChatGPT-5) for polishing is minor but worth noting.
- The paper's scope is narrow: it critiques one metric (vacuity) in one setting (MCQA-based EDL LLMs). It does not propose a corrected or alternative OOD metric.
- The conclusion that "UM and MP perform weakly at detecting OOD in this context" (when K is correctly matched) is stated but the corrected AUROC values (e.g., 0.576 for ARC-C with IB-EDL) are not put in proper perspective — near-OOD MCQA datasets may simply be intrinsically hard to separate from ID data, and this is not explored deeply.
- The discussion of what constitutes "true" OOD for MCQA LLMs is thoughtful but ultimately inconclusive, flagging a hard conceptual problem without resolving it.

## Strengths

- Clean, deterministic experimental design: the K-isolation experiment requires no retraining and is fully reproducible.
- Formal proof of non-invariance provides theoretical grounding for an empirically observed phenomenon.
- Honest engagement with a published paper's code, including faithful reproduction before critique.
- Raises important conceptual issues about the transferability of CV-origin EDL metrics to LLM/MCQA settings.
- Code is publicly released and implementation details are thorough (Table 7).
- Entropy analysis in the appendix confirms the finding is not specific to the K/S formulation.

## Limitations

- Does not propose a solution or corrected metric — the paper ends at diagnosis, leaving practitioners without actionable guidance beyond "ensure K_ID = K_OOD."
- Scope is narrow: only vacuity (UM) and Max Probability (MP) are analyzed; other uncertainty metrics (predictive entropy, mutual information) are mentioned but not systematically evaluated in the corrected-K regime.
- The critique of IB-EDL is based on one model (Llama3-8b) and one training dataset (OBQA); generalizability to other LLMs, training sets, or EDL variants is assumed but not demonstrated.
- The "near-OOD" vs. "far-OOD" distinction is argued qualitatively but not operationalized with a rigorous definition or quantitative criterion.
- The corrected performance of EDL-based OOD detection (after fixing K) is shown to be weak, but no comparison to simpler baselines (e.g., softmax entropy from a non-EDL fine-tuned model) is provided, making it hard to judge whether EDL adds value at all in this setting.

## 相关性

本文直接揭示了证据深度学习（EDL）在LLM不确定性估计中的一个关键评估漏洞，对关注大语言模型可靠性、不确定性量化和分布外检测的研究者具有重要的实践警示价值——尤其是在使用MCQA数据集评估LLM不确定性时，必须严格控制答案选项数量K的一致性。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. If K is correctly matched and vacuity performs weakly on near-OOD MCQA datasets, what alternative uncertainty metric or OOD detection strategy is most effective for EDL-fine-tuned LLMs? Does a simple softmax-entropy baseline outperform EDL-derived vacuity?

2. The paper shows MP is also affected by K-mismatch indirectly. Is there a normalisation of vacuity (e.g., dividing by K, or using per-class evidence ratios) that would make it invariant to class expansion without sacrificing its OOD detection utility?

3. How does the K-sensitivity issue manifest in standard CV/image classification EDL, where the output space is fixed but OOD benchmarks may have different semantic granularities (e.g., CIFAR-10 ID vs. CIFAR-100 OOD)?

4. The paper distinguishes near-OOD, far-OOD, and task-format shift for MCQA. Can a principled taxonomy be developed that yields operationalizable, quantitative criteria for assigning a dataset to each category?

5. IB-EDL's information-bottleneck regularisation is motivated by preventing overconfident evidence assignment. After correcting the K-mismatch, does IB-EDL still outperform standard EDL on OOD detection, or does the performance gap largely disappear?
