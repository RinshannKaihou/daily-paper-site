> **一句话总结：** 本文证明权重Gram矩阵 $W^\top W$ 通过"特征学习方程"精确捕捉梯度下降对隐层特征协方差的驱动过程，并引入"目标线性度"(Target Linearity)度量，理论与实验表明深度网络在训练中从浅到深逐层将输入数据线性化为目标可预测的结构，Neural Collapse 与生成模型的潜空间线性插值均可作为其特例统一解释。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces a feature-centric theoretical framework centered on the weight Gram matrix $W^\top W$. The authors prove a "Feature Learning Equation" showing that gradient descent on weights implicitly drives a "Virtual Covariance Shift" in feature space. They then define "Target Linearity" (TL) — an $R^2$ measure of how linearly the learned features predict the target — and prove that deep networks sequentially linearize representations layer-by-layer during training. The framework unifies Neural Collapse and the linear interpolation property of VAE latent spaces as special cases. If you care about a principled, architecture-agnostic account of how and why deep networks build progressively linear representations, this paper is worth reading in full.

## 核心贡献

- **特征学习方程 (Feature Learning Equation)：** 建立了权重空间梯度与特征空间梯度的精确恒等式 $\nabla_h f \cdot h^\top = W^\top \nabla_W f$，以权重Gram矩阵为核心桥梁，将权重更新与特征演化直接关联。
- **虚拟协方差定理 (Theorem 1)：** 严格证明梯度下降对权重Gram矩阵的更新量，精确等于"虚拟更新"后隐层状态的协方差偏移（Virtual Covariance Shift），误差为 $O(\gamma^2)$，比此前 Neural Feature Ansatz（AGOP）的相关系数从0.71提升至0.98。
- **目标线性度 (Target Linearity, TL)：** 定义为岭回归 $R^2$，作为特征与目标之间线性对齐程度的可量化指标，适用于分类与生成任务。
- **逐层序列线性化定理 (Theorem 4)：** 在 Neural Feature Ansatz 条件下证明，网络越深层，目标线性度代理函数单调增大，即网络在训练中将非线性问题沿层次逐步线性化。
- **统一其他特征学习现象：** Neural Collapse 被证明是 TL 代理函数取最大值的极端情形；VAE 潜空间线性插值性质可由高 TL 条件推导。

## Key Contributions

- **Feature Learning Equation:** Derives the exact identity $\nabla_h f \cdot h^\top = W^\top \nabla_W f$ linking weight-space gradient descent to feature-space dynamics via the weight Gram matrix $W^\top W$.
- **Virtual Covariance Theorem (Theorem 1):** Proves that the GD update to the weight Gram matrix equals, up to $O(\gamma^2)$, the Virtual Covariance Shift — the change in covariance of virtually-updated hidden states. This refines the Neural Feature Ansatz (NFA/AGOP), achieving correlation $\rho = 0.98$ vs. $\rho = 0.71$ for AGOP.
- **Target Linearity (TL):** Introduces a new scalar measure — the $R^2$ of ridge regression from hidden features to target — applicable to any supervised or self-supervised task, unifying classification and generation.
- **Sequential Linearization (Theorem 4):** Proves that for interpolating networks satisfying the NFA, the TL surrogate $\mathcal{S}(G_\text{id}^l)$ is monotonically increasing in layer index $l$, formalizing the empirical observation that deeper layers are more linearly decodable.
- **Unification of Feature Learning Phenomena:** Shows Neural Collapse is the maximal-TL extreme case; proves high TL is a sufficient condition for the linear interpolation property in VAE latent spaces.
- **Gradient Whitening Experiment:** Isolates the Gram-modifying component of the gradient update ("whitened gradient") and shows it alone recovers standard GD performance on MNIST, SVHN, and CIFAR-10, empirically validating the Gram matrix as the dominant carrier of learning signal.

## Methodology

The paper builds a unified theoretical framework in four steps:

**1. Feature Learning Equation.**
Starting from the chain rule for a linear layer $z = Wh$, the authors derive the exact identity:
$$\nabla_h f \cdot h^\top = W^\top \nabla_W f$$
This connects how weights update (right side) to how features evolve (left side), with the weight Gram matrix $W^\top W$ as the connecting object.

**2. Weight Gram Matrix as a Bridge.**
Computing the Gram matrix update under gradient descent and expanding to first order yields:
$$(W^+)^\top W^+ - W^\top W \approx -\gamma(\nabla_h \mathcal{L} \cdot h^\top + h \cdot \nabla_h \mathcal{L}^\top)$$
The authors validate that the Gram-modifying component of the gradient (the "whitened gradient" $\Delta$) carries the dominant learning signal via ablation experiments across MNIST, SVHN, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.

**3. Virtual Covariance and Theorem 1.**
They define the *virtual update* $\tilde{x}_{t+1} \leftarrow \tilde{x}_t - \gamma \nabla_x \mathcal{L}_t$ as a hypothetical trajectory where hidden states (not weights) are the optimization variables. The Virtual Covariance Shift (VCS) is $\widetilde{\text{cov}}(h_l^+) - \widetilde{\text{cov}}(h_l)$. Theorem 1 proves that GD on weights produces precisely this shift on the Gram matrix, making the Gram matrix a rigorous proxy for feature-space covariance evolution.

**4. Target Linearity and its Dynamics.**
Target Linearity is defined as:
$$\mathcal{T}_\lambda(H, Y) = 1 - \frac{\|Y - \hat{Y}_\lambda\|_2^2}{\|Y - \bar{Y}\|_2^2}$$
where $\hat{Y}_\lambda = H^\top(HH^\top + \lambda I)^{-1}HY$ is the ridge regression predictor. To make analysis tractable, they introduce a linearized surrogate $\mathcal{S}(G_\text{id}) = Y^\top G_\text{id} Y$ where $G_\text{id} = H^\top W^\top W H$, and prove a lower bound $\mathcal{T}_\lambda \geq 1 - C \cdot \mathcal{S}(G_\text{id})^{-1}$ (Theorem 2).

Training dynamics (Theorem 3) show $\mathcal{S}$ increases at rate $\approx 2\gamma (f^\top y)(y^\top K g)$, identifying feature learning as a *moving-target problem* where $f_t$ chases an evolving OLS predictor $f_\text{OLS,t}$. Layer-wise dynamics (Theorem 4) prove the surrogate grows monotonically with depth under the NFA assumption.

Experiments use 4-layer fully connected networks (hidden dim 256, GELU activations) trained with SGD on CIFAR-10/100, plus BERT evaluated on downstream tasks, and VAE on MNIST.

## Results & Highlights

- **Gradient Whitening:** The whitened gradient update (keeping only the Gram-modifying component) matches or exceeds standard GD on MNIST, SVHN, and CIFAR-10 (within 4–5 epochs), and tracks GD with only a small gap on CIFAR-100.
- **Virtual Covariance tracking:** VCS predicts actual Gram updates with correlation $\rho = 0.98$, compared to $\rho = 0.71$ for the Neural Feature Ansatz (AGOP).
- **Sequential Linearization (empirical):** TL surrogate grows layer-by-layer for CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100. Deeper layers consistently exhibit higher TL, consistent with prior linear probe studies.
- **Training Dynamics as Moving Target:** The network simultaneously chases $f_\text{OLS}$ and reshapes the feature geometry, contrasting sharply with the NTK/lazy regime where the function space is fixed.
- **Neural Collapse unification:** Under NC1+NC2 conditions, $\mathcal{S}_\text{cls}(G_\text{id})$ attains its maximum value, recovering Neural Collapse from the same Gram-matrix framework without the Unconstrained Feature Model.
- **VAE latent interpolation:** Proposition 3 proves that whenever the decoder's function class contains the OLS estimator and TL is high, linear interpolation in latent space maps to approximately linear interpolation in input space. Empirically validated on MNIST VAE (digits 2 and 8).
- **BERT TL:** Pre-training alone produces representations substantially more target-linear than random initialization. Fine-tuning rapidly saturates TL (near-monotone, mostly within 1 epoch).
- **TL as generalization signal:** CIFAR-10 (good generalization) achieves last-layer TL > 0.5; CIFAR-100 (poor generalization, same training accuracy) achieves only TL ~ 0.15, suggesting TL is linked to generalization quality.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. The Feature Learning Equation itself is a chain-rule identity and not novel per se, but the systematic use of the weight Gram matrix as a *rigorous* bridge between weight-space updates and feature-space covariance dynamics — and the derivation of VCS as the exact first-order update — is a meaningful theoretical advance over the conjectural NFA. The introduction of Target Linearity as an architecture-agnostic scalar measure unifying classification and generation is genuinely useful.

**Rigor:** Mostly strong. The main theorems (1–4) are proved with clear assumptions stated. Theorem 4 relies on the NFA (assumed, not derived), and the m-positive-homogeneity assumption restricts applicability (GELU is used in experiments but does not satisfy this exactly; the authors note empirical validation holds anyway). The $O(\gamma^2)$ approximation throughout is standard but limits exact guarantees.

**Reproducibility:** Good. Code is available, experimental setups are described with hyperparameters (SGD lr=0.005, 100 epochs, hidden dim 256, GELU). Results on standard benchmarks (CIFAR-10/100, MNIST, SVHN, BERT) are reproducible by the community.

**Significance:** High for the theory community. The framework provides the most principled and empirically validated account to date of *why* deep networks produce progressively linear representations, connecting this to the dynamics of the weight Gram matrix. The unification of Neural Collapse, linear probes, and VAE interpolation under one framework is intellectually compelling.

**Red Flags:**
- The NFA is assumed (not derived) in Theorem 4; the layer-wise result is conditional on this conjecture.
- Homogeneity assumption ($m$-positively homogeneous) is technically violated by GELU, though empirical results are still consistent.
- Experiments are on relatively small fully connected networks (4 layers, 256 hidden); the extent to which sequential linearization holds in large ResNets or transformers beyond BERT is not fully explored in the main paper.
- The gap on CIFAR-100 in the gradient whitening experiment suggests the Gram matrix does not carry *all* the learning signal for harder tasks — the non-Gram component matters more as task difficulty increases.

---

## Strengths

- Provides an exact (to first order) theoretical link between weight updates and feature covariance evolution — more rigorous than prior NFA conjectures.
- Target Linearity is task-agnostic ($R^2$-based) and applies to generative as well as discriminative settings.
- Sequential linearization theorem formalizes a widely observed but previously unproven empirical fact about linear probes.
- Unification of Neural Collapse and VAE latent interpolation is elegant and non-trivial.
- TL's correlation with generalization quality (CIFAR-10 vs. CIFAR-100 TL profiles) opens a potentially useful diagnostic tool.
- Clean and well-structured paper with proofs in appendix and code released.

---

## Limitations

- All main theorems rely on the NFA or $m$-homogeneity assumptions that are satisfied approximately (not exactly) in practice.
- Experiments are limited to fully connected networks and BERT; convolutional architectures are deferred to appendix with mixed results on CIFAR-100.
- The framework is primarily descriptive/analytical rather than prescriptive — it explains what happens during training but does not directly prescribe improved training algorithms (gradient whitening is suggestive but not a practical new optimizer).
- The moving-target interpretation of feature learning (Theorem 3) is characterized qualitatively; quantitative convergence rates are not derived.
- The link between TL and generalization is observed but not theoretically explained; the paper acknowledges this is an open question.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the NFA assumption be derived (not assumed) from first principles for standard architectures, or is there a class of networks where it provably fails and TL dynamics break down?
2. Does Target Linearity serve as a reliable early-stopping or model selection criterion that correlates with test generalization across diverse tasks and architectures?
3. How does sequential linearization interact with residual connections in ResNets — do skip connections accelerate or disrupt the layer-wise TL growth predicted by Theorem 4?
4. Can the weight Gram matrix framework be extended to attention layers in transformers, where the "weight matrix" is the product of query, key, and value projections?
5. The paper shows TL grows during grokking experiments (Section B.8) — is there a critical TL threshold below which memorization occurs and above which generalization emerges?
6. Could the VCS (Virtual Covariance Shift) be used to design new optimizers that directly target feature-space covariance improvements, beyond the gradient whitening ablation shown?
