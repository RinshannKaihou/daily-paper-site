> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 CITE 算法，通过三路 e-process 组合（逐对比较、目标概率下界、未见类别上界）实现 LLM 自洽性采样的随时有效 Type-I 误差控制，在类别集合完全未知的条件下仍可证明地将虚假认证率压制在任意给定水平 ε 之内，且期望停止时间不依赖类别总数。

> **TL;DR:** CITE is a sequential algorithm for certifying that a pre-specified answer label is the unique mode of an LLM's response distribution under majority-voting self-consistency. Unlike prior stopping rules that either require a fixed sample size or assume a known finite answer vocabulary, CITE controls Type-I false-certification error at a user-chosen level ε uniformly over all data-driven stopping times, even when the set of possible responses is countably infinite and grows as sampling proceeds. The expected stopping time is O((log(1/ε)+log(1/δ))/δ² + (log(1/ε)+log(1/p_r))/p_r), depending only on the modal gap δ and the target probability p_r — not on the number of categories — and this rate is minimax-optimal up to logarithmic factors. On three LLM benchmarks (AIME 2026, FrontierScience-Olympiad, ER-REASON) with Qwen3-30B and gpt-oss-20b, CITE empirically maintains Type-I errors near zero while achieving competitive or superior certification rates compared to fixed-sample baselines. If you care about principled, statistically valid early stopping in LLM inference pipelines — especially in high-stakes settings — this paper is worth reading.

## 核心贡献

- **随时有效的 Type-I 误差控制**：CITE 在任意数据驱动停止时刻均保证虚假认证概率不超过预设水平 ε，而非仅在固定样本量处成立（定理 4.1）。
- **类别集合无关性**：算法无需预先知晓答案类别集合，即使类别无限可数、新类别随采样动态出现，保证依然成立——这是 LLM 自由文本输出的自然建模方式。
- **最优停止时间复杂度**：期望停止时间仅与模态间隙 δ 和目标概率 p_r 有关，不随类别数 |A| 增长；并在常数和对数意义下匹配信息论下界（定理 4.3、4.4）。
- **置信加权扩展（W-CITE）**：将三组件框架推广至每次采样附带置信分数的情形，目标为加权唯一众数，在相同 Type-I 误差保证下进一步改善样本效率。
- **实证验证**：在五种多项分布模拟设置和三个 LLM 基准上确认了实验性误差控制，在弥散尾部设置下 CITE 比 Bonferroni 等固定样本基线更早达到高认证率。

## Key Contributions

- **Anytime-valid Type-I error control**: CITE guarantees that the probability of ever falsely certifying a target label as the unique mode is at most ε, holding uniformly over all stopping times — not just at a single pre-chosen sample size (Theorem 4.1).
- **Category-agnostic operation**: The procedure requires no prior knowledge of the response label space. New categories that appear mid-sampling are handled automatically via the unseen upper-bound component, making CITE suited to open-ended LLM output.
- **Category-size-free stopping-time rate**: Expected stopping time is O((log(1/ε)+log(1/δ))/δ² + (log(1/ε)+log(1/p_r))/p_r), independent of |A|, with a matching minimax lower bound (Theorems 4.3–4.4). This contrasts with fixed-sample baselines whose costs scale with the number of answer categories.
- **Weighted extension W-CITE**: Handles confidence-weighted observations (e.g., verifier scores or logit weights), certifying the weighted unique mode with the same anytime-valid guarantee and an analogous stopping-time bound (Theorems 5.2, 5.4).
- **Empirical validation on real LLM benchmarks**: Tested on Qwen3-30B and gpt-oss-20b across AIME 2026, FrontierScience-Olympiad, and ER-REASON; CITE achieves near-zero empirical false-certification rates and attains certification rate ≥ 0.98 on ER-REASON/Qwen3-30B at mean stopping time ~12 samples.

## Methodology

CITE frames LLM self-consistency as sequential hypothesis testing over an unknown, countably infinite categorical distribution P over response labels. The fixed-target null hypothesis H₀ᵣ posits that some other label has probability at least as large as the target r; the alternative H₁ᵣ posits that r is strictly the unique mode. CITE must reject H₀ᵣ while controlling the Type-I probability that it ever incorrectly certifies.

The algorithm combines three time-uniform stochastic processes, each contributing a ε/3 error budget via a union bound (intersection-union test structure):

**Component (i) — Pairwise E-Values (E_t):** For each observed competitor label a ≠ r, a pairwise e-process is constructed via the testing-by-betting paradigm (Ramdas et al., 2023). The indicator Z_i^(r,a) = 1{X_i=r} − 1{X_i=a} ∈ {-1, 0, +1} is bet on with parameter λ ∈ (0,1). The product martingale E_t^(r,a)(λ) = (1+λ)^{N_t(r)} (1−λ)^{N_t(a)} is a Nonnegative Supermartingale (NSM) under H₀ᵣ. CITE mixes over a finite grid Λ_pw and tracks only the empirical runner-up (most-observed competitor), exploiting a runner-up monotonicity lemma to reduce computation to O(1) per step.

**Component (ii) — Lower Confidence Bound on p_r (L_t):** To guard against hidden competitors (labels never yet observed that could have p_a > p_r), a time-uniform lower confidence bound L_t on the target probability is constructed. A mixture e-process M_t(q) bets on whether p_r > q over a grid Λ_r; L_t is the largest q for which M_t(q) ≥ 3/ε. Proposition 3.4 guarantees P(∃t: L_t > p_r) ≤ ε/3.

**Component (iii) — Unseen Upper Bound (U_t):** Any category with true probability u remains unobserved after t draws with probability (1−u)^t. A probability-weighted union bound yields U_t = min{u ∈ (0,1]: u⁻¹(1−u)^t ≤ ε/3}, which serves as a time-uniform per-category upper bound on unseen probabilities. Proposition 3.6 guarantees that no unseen category has probability exceeding U_t with probability more than ε/3.

**Stopping Rule (Definition 3.7):** CITE stops at τ = inf{t ≥ 1: E_t ≥ 3/ε and L_t > U_t}. The first condition certifies the target leads every observed competitor; the second condition simultaneously certifies the target leads any potentially unseen competitor. Theorem 4.1 proves sup_{P∈H₀} P(τ < ∞) ≤ ε.

**Weighted extension (W-CITE, Section 5):** Each draw is augmented by a confidence weight W_i ∈ [0,1]. The three components are modified to track weighted counts μ_a = E[W_i 1{X_i=a}], with the weighted pairwise e-process using W_i(1{X_i=r}−1{X_i=a}) as the betting indicator. Because runner-up monotonicity fails under heterogeneous weights, the pairwise check scans all observed competitors, raising per-round cost to O(|A_t|).

## Results & Highlights

**Simulations (5 multinomial settings):**
- CITE and Bonferroni maintain empirical false-certification rate exactly zero across all settings; KR shows nonzero rates (up to 0.002) in Settings 3–4; MMC shows false-certification up to 0.014 in near-tie settings.
- In "easy" concentrated Settings (2), CITE certifies at rate 0.890 with mean stopping time ~36 at N=64; this is slightly below Bonferroni (0.928) and KR (0.946), but CITE is the only one with anytime validity.
- In "LLM-like" diffuse Setting 5, CITE reaches rate 0.932 at N=512 (mean stopping time ~234), while MMC stalls at ≤ 0.002 even at N=2048 — demonstrating CITE's large advantage in diffuse-tail regimes.

**LLM Benchmarks (Qwen3-30B and gpt-oss-20b):**
- On ER-REASON / Qwen3-30B (concentrated answers): CITE achieves certification rate ≥ 0.98 at mean stopping time ~12 samples; fixed-sample Bonferroni saturates at only 0.111 for N ≥ 256.
- On FrontierScience-Olympiad / gpt-oss-20b (diffuse answers, ~250 distinct values per problem): CITE exceeds MMC certification rate by approximately 2× at the largest evaluated budget N=1024.
- False-certification rates remain within Monte Carlo error of ε=0.05 nominal level across all tested model–problem combinations.
- W-CITE provides additional improvement in sample efficiency on benchmarks where confidence scores are available (detailed in Appendix H).

**Theoretical tightness:** The stopping-time upper bound O(log(1/ε)/δ² + log(1/ε)/p_r) matches the minimax lower bound of Theorem 4.4 (c·log(1/ε)·(p/δ²+1/p)) up to logarithmic factors when p_r is bounded away from 0.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. The combination of anytime-valid inference with an infinite/unknown category set is genuinely new in the context of LLM self-consistency. The unseen upper-bound component (U_t) is an elegant adaptation of missing-mass ideas to sequential mode certification and, to the authors' knowledge, has not appeared in this form before. The formal distinction between mode certification (p_r > sup_{a≠r} p_a) and majority certification (p_r > aggregate residual) is clearly articulated and motivates the approach relative to MMC.

**Rigor:** Strong. All three components come with time-uniform guarantees proved from first principles using NSM/e-process machinery. The minimax lower bound (Theorem 4.4) tightly matches the upper bound, providing a complete picture. Proofs are deferred to appendices and appear complete.

**Reproducibility:** Moderate. Algorithm 1 is fully specified. The grid parameters Λ_pw and Λ_r are described informally ("contain some λ in [δ/8, δ/4]"); in practice, implementing these grids requires non-trivial hyperparameter choices that are handled in Appendix G but not foregrounded in the main text. Experiment protocols involve real LLM APIs (Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct, gpt-oss-20b) without code release mentioned; reproducibility depends on API stability.

**Significance:** Meaningful for anyone deploying LLM self-consistency in high-stakes domains (medical triage, safety-critical decisions). The framing of certification-as-mode-testing (distinct from answer correctness) is important conceptually, though in practice downstream users need both guarantees. The practical advantage over Bonferroni is largest in diffuse-answer regimes, which happen to be the most computationally expensive — exactly the regime where an early-stopping guarantee matters most.

**Red flags:**
- The gap between CITE and KR (a fixed-sample split test): KR consistently shows higher finite-sample power in simulations (e.g., rate 0.946 vs. 0.890 at N=64 in Setting 2). The paper correctly notes that KR is not valid under adaptive stopping, but users who can pre-commit to a fixed sample size may prefer KR. The tradeoff is real and acknowledged, but practitioners should be aware.
- The weighted extension W-CITE raises per-step cost to O(|A_t|) (scanning all observed competitors), which could be expensive when answer diversity is high.
- The data-adaptive target case (certifying the empirically observed top category arg max_a N_t(a)) is explicitly left open; this is a natural use case in practice where the "target" label is not pre-specified.
- Stopping-time bounds have logarithmic gaps from the lower bound in the very small p_r regime; this gap is acknowledged but unresolved.

---

## Strengths

- Principled and clean theoretical framework: three components with matching ε/3 error budgets, intersection-union structure, and minimax-tight stopping times.
- Handles the genuinely hard case of an infinite unknown category set — a realistic model of LLM free-text output.
- Demonstrates clear empirical advantage over MMC in diffuse-tail regimes (a factor of ~2 at N=1024 on FrontierScience-Olympiad), which are precisely the computationally expensive regimes.
- Confidence-weighted extension is natural and theoretically grounded.
- Tight connection to classical e-value/NSM literature makes the approach principled and extensible.

---

## Limitations

- Mode certification does not imply answer correctness; a downstream pipeline still needs an independent correctness signal. The paper is clear about this, but it limits direct practical impact for settings where correctness (not just consistency) is the goal.
- The pre-specified target requirement (target r must be chosen before sampling) is restrictive; the data-adaptive variant is left as future work.
- W-CITE incurs O(|A_t|) per-round cost because runner-up monotonicity fails under heterogeneous weights, potentially slowing inference on problems with many distinct candidate answers.
- Experiments cover only two LLMs; it is unclear how performance generalizes to models with very different response distributions (e.g., smaller models with higher entropy outputs).
- Grid parameter choices (Λ_pw, Λ_r) affect power in practice; sensitivity analysis is deferred to appendices and would benefit from a principled default.

## Relevance

本文针对 LLM 推理时计算（test-time compute）中的自洽性采样问题，提供了一套严格的统计保证框架，对于关注大模型可靠性、推理效率与高风险场景部署（如医疗分诊）的研究者具有直接参考价值；其 e-process 与序列检验方法论也可迁移至其他需要在线停止决策的机器学习场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Data-adaptive target**: Can the category-size-free stopping-time rate of Theorem 4.3 survive when the target is the empirical leader arg max_a N_t(a), requiring a Bonferroni correction over the sequence of empirical leaders? The authors leave this open — it is the most practically important extension.
2. **Very small p_r regime**: The stopping-time upper bound has a worse rate than the lower bound when p_r is very small. Is the gap fundamental, or can the LCB–unseen component be improved for this regime?
3. **Multi-query budget allocation**: How does CITE interact with query-difficulty-adaptive budget allocation (e.g., bandit-style across a set of problems)? Can the anytime-valid certificate be composed across queries?
4. **Beyond i.i.d.**: The analysis assumes i.i.d. draws from P. In practice, LLM outputs with chain-of-thought may exhibit within-query dependence (e.g., due to temperature annealing or prompt batching). How sensitive is the Type-I guarantee to mild dependence violations?
5. **Integration with correctness signals**: What is the optimal way to combine CITE's mode certificate with a separate correctness verifier or reward model? Does the joint guarantee remain tractable?
6. **Computational scaling of W-CITE**: Can runner-up monotonicity be recovered in the weighted setting under additional assumptions (e.g., bounded weight variance), reducing W-CITE's per-round cost back to O(1)?
