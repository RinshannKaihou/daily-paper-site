Attractor Geometry of Transformer Memory: From Conflict Arbitration to Confident Hallucination

arXiv: 2605.05686

Date: 2026-05-07

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05686

一句话总结： 本文提出「吸引子几何」框架，将Transformer的参数记忆（PM）建模为隐空间中的持久吸引子盆地，将工作记忆（WM）建模为瞬态转向力，并证明：冲突是盆地竞争、幻觉是盆地缺席，且隐藏状态距最近盆地的几何间距（margin）可在零误判率下完美区分幻觉与正确回忆，而输出熵随模型规模扩大反而失效。
TL;DR: This paper provides a mechanistic geometric account of two distinct LLM failure modes—conflict (parametric and working memory disagree) and hallucination (no memorized basin exists)—by modeling transformer hidden states as a dynamical system whose attractors correspond to stored facts. A single geometric signal (distance from hidden state to the nearest memorized basin) perfectly separates hallucinations from correct recall with zero false refusals, while output entropy fails increasingly at scale. The key finding is that the frozen LM head is an epistemic bottleneck that erases this geometric information, and this erasure worsens as models grow—making hidden-state monitoring more necessary, not less, as LLMs scale.

核心贡献

  • 统一几何框架：将Transformer建模为离散时间动力系统，参数记忆（MLP权重）对应隐空间中的持久吸引子盆地，工作记忆（注意力上下文）对应瞬态转向扰动，从而在同一框架内解释冲突和幻觉两类失败模式。
  • 电路解耦发现：通过对QK、VO、MLP三组LoRA适配器的独立消融，因果确认了各组件的不同功能角色——QK控制路由（切换轨迹），VO控制内容回写（读取盆地内容），MLP决定盆地成形（定义吸引子结构）；并通过Jacobian分解证明这些角色是预训练架构的固有属性，而非微调产物。
  • 双失败模式机制：精确区分"冲突"（模型知道答案但两个记忆系统拉向不同盆地，输出熵却保持平坦）与"幻觉"（无盆地存在，隐藏状态漂移，LM头依然自信输出错误答案）。
  • 几何监控优于熵监控：以隐藏状态到最近记忆盆地的欧氏距离（margin）作为检测信号，在合成任务上AUROC=0.993，在真实自然语言事实查询中AUROC=1.000，而输出熵AUROC仅0.622；且几何优势随模型规模增大而扩大。
  • 规模定律推导：证明自信幻觉比例遵循 $C = \exp(-c/\Delta)$，其中 $\Delta$ 随模型规模按 $N^{1/3}$ 增长，导致即使整体错误率下降，绝对不可检测错误数量仍随规模上升。

Key Contributions

  • Unified attractor-geometry framework: Models transformer autoregressive generation as a discrete-time dynamical system where MLP weights sculpt persistent attractor basins (parametric memory, PM) and attention-mediated context provides transient steering (working memory, WM). Conflict is basin competition; hallucination is basin absence.
  • Causal circuit dissociation: Independent LoRA ablations on QK, VO, and MLP components causally confirm distinct roles—QK modifies routing without reshaping basins; VO perturbs content write-back within a basin; MLP restructures the attractor landscape itself. Jacobian decomposition confirms these roles are intrinsic to the pretrained architecture.
  • Two mechanistically distinct failure modes: Conflict arises when PM and WM steering compete; the correct answer exists in weights but the trajectory is pulled away—with no entropy signal. Hallucination arises when no memorized basin exists; the hidden state wanders freely and the frozen LM head fires confidently regardless.
  • Geometric monitoring with zero false refusals: The margin signal (distance from pre-LM-head hidden state to nearest memorized basin center) achieves AUROC = 0.993 on synthetic tasks and AUROC = 1.000 on natural-language factual queries with no LoRA, while entropy achieves only 0.622. Entropy must reject 99.5% of correct outputs to catch all hallucinations; margin catches all with zero false refusals.
  • Scaling law for confident hallucinations: Derives $C = \exp(-c/\Delta)$ governing the fraction of maximally confident hallucinations, showing the absolute rate of undetectable errors grows from <0.5% at sub-1B to ~18% at 14B parameters.

Methodology

Task design. The authors study memory arbitration in a controlled synthetic task: 1,600 entities (E000000–E001599) are each assigned a unique random 5-digit code. PM is installed via rank-8 LoRA adapters (α=16, lr=5×10⁻⁵, 10 epochs) on Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct; WM is operationalized as reading the correct code from context. This design gives exact ground truth for what the model knows, eliminating the ambiguity present in natural-language factual tasks.

Training regimes. Two regimes isolate the effect of basin depth: (1) Brittle PM—single fixed template, yielding format-gated, shallow basins; (2) Robust PM—53 diverse templates with 25% synonym paraphrasing and 10% distractor prefixes, yielding deep, format-invariant basins. Five evaluation scenarios are tested: PM-seen, WM baseline, WM recall, WM–PM conflict, and PM-unseen (hallucination).

LoRA placement ablations. Four component groups are independently ablated: QK (query-key projections), VO (value-output projections), MLP (gate/up/down projections), and Full (all). This surgical design allows causal attribution of each failure mode to specific architectural circuits.

Attractor geometry formalization. The transformer is treated as an input-conditioned discrete-time dynamical system $h_{t+1} = F(h_t; x, y_{\leq t})$. The Jacobian of each sublayer is decomposed into symmetric (contraction/expansion) and antisymmetric (rotation) parts. Symmetry correlation $\varphi = \text{corr}(J_{ij}, J_{ji})$ and $\|S\|_F^2$ quantify gradient-like contraction and basin depth, respectively.

Geometric signals. For each training entity $i$, a basin center $m_i$ is computed by averaging final-layer hidden states across canonical templates. The margin for query $x$ is $\delta(x) = \min_i \|h(x) - m_i\|_2$. This scalar is computed before the LM head and compared against output entropy as a hallucination detector.

Scaling validation. Geometric monitoring is validated on 12 instruction-tuned models from 0.36B to 14B parameters across two benchmarks, confirming that the geometric advantage over entropy grows with model size.

Results & Highlights

  • Circuit dissociation confirmed: QK-only adapters preserve WM at 95% but achieve only 12% PM recall—routing changes leave basin structure intact. MLP-only adapters achieve 100% format-invariant PM recall but collapse WM to 0% (context deafness). VO-only produces a middle ground. These qualitatively distinct signatures precisely match the attractor-geometry predictions.
  • Conflict is silent: Under WM–PM conflict with robust PM, accuracy decays from ~77% toward chance across five digit positions while output entropy stays flat throughout—the model generates uniformly confidently even as the trajectory corrupts. This makes entropy-based monitoring blind by design.
  • Hallucination is confident: For unseen entities, the LM head produces near-zero-entropy outputs (confident hallucinations). Captured trajectories (converging to a memorized basin) have significantly lower entropy than wandering ones (H = 0.16 vs. 0.44, p = 0.019). ~16% of hallucinated codes correspond to the nearest stored entity in hidden-state space; the majority (~84%) is LM head output bias.
  • Geometric margin: perfect separation: Margin achieves AUROC = 0.993 (vs. 0.968 for entropy) on synthetic data, and AUROC = 1.000 on 196 natural-language factual queries from the pretrained model with no adapter—confirming attractor geometry is structural, not a fine-tuning artifact.
  • Scaling law validated: Across 12 models (0.36B–14B), the logit gap $\Delta$ grows as $N^{1/3}$, driving $C = \exp(-c/\Delta)$ upward. The absolute undetectable error rate $U = H \cdot C$ grows from <0.5% at sub-1B to ~18% at 14B. Geometric margin separation improves 1.2× to 153× as model width grows 16× in a teacher-student ablation.
  • LM head as epistemic bottleneck: The frozen linear LM head projects thousands of representational dimensions into a single logit gap, discarding basin proximity information. This compression is amplified by training-driven logit saturation, and cannot be recovered by post-hoc recalibration.

Critical Assessment

Novelty (High): The attractor-geometry framing of transformer memory is genuinely novel and mechanistically precise. While prior work documented PM–WM tension behaviorally and MLP-as-key-value-memory analytically, this paper is the first to unify conflict and hallucination under a single geometric account with causal circuit attribution and a predictive framework that correctly anticipates where static geometry succeeds and fails (hallucination vs. conflict).

Rigor (High with caveats): The synthetic task is expertly designed to provide exact ground truth that natural-language tasks cannot offer. The Jacobian decomposition is mathematically grounded. The LoRA ablation design is unusually surgical. However, the scaling analysis covers only 0.36B–14B; behavior beyond 14B remains unvalidated. The conflict failure mode (AUROC < 0.75) is acknowledged as a known limitation, and the authors are transparent about it.

Reproducibility (Moderate-High): The paper provides extensive appendices with training details (53 templates listed, hyperparameters given, data generation described). The base model (Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct) is publicly available. However, computing basin centers requires access to training entities, which limits deployment beyond controlled evaluation. No code repository is mentioned.

Significance (High): The finding that the LM head is an epistemic bottleneck and that this bottleneck worsens with scale is a direct and important implication for hallucination monitoring in deployed LLMs. The zero-false-refusal geometric detector is practically significant. The scaling law predicting growing confident hallucination rates is a strong and testable claim.

Red flags:

  • The synthetic task (5-digit code recall) is highly controlled; real-world generalization is partially validated on 196 TriviaQA queries but not systematically benchmarked.
  • Basin center computation requires knowing training entities in advance—deployment in open-domain settings is non-trivial and not fully addressed.
  • The paper does not test whether geometric monitoring survives RLHF or instruction-tuning that substantially reshapes hidden-state geometry.
  • TruthfulQA AUROC for margin (0.858) is notably lower than on factual recall tasks, suggesting the framework may struggle with questions involving reasoning rather than pure recall.

Strengths

  • Exceptionally clean experimental design with exact ground truth—a rare property in interpretability research.
  • Three distinct findings (circuit dissociation, conflict mechanics, hallucination mechanics) each follow from the same geometric account, providing coherent internal validation.
  • Jacobian decomposition confirms architectural roles are intrinsic to pretrained weights, not artifacts of fine-tuning—strengthening the mechanistic claim.
  • Geometric monitoring validated on natural-language data without LoRA (AUROC = 1.000), confirming the signal is structural.
  • Scaling law derivation ($C = \exp(-c/\Delta)$) connects to established logit-scaling results and makes strong quantitative predictions.
  • Authors are transparent about where the framework fails (conflict: trajectory dynamics require dynamic, not static, monitoring).

---

Limitations

  • Generalization beyond code-recall to open-domain, reasoning-heavy, or multi-hop factual tasks is only partially validated; TruthfulQA and adversarial reasoning probes show degraded performance.
  • Basin center computation scales linearly with the number of memorized entities and requires access to those entities—not practical for trillion-parameter frontier models without substantial engineering.
  • The conflict failure mode (WM–PM trajectory competition) resists static geometric diagnosis (AUROC < 0.75); the paper proposes trajectory-based approaches as future work but does not implement them.
  • Scaling experiments cover 0.36B–14B only; extrapolation to >70B models is uncertain.
  • RLHF and post-training alignment effects on attractor geometry are not studied.
  • The paper does not provide a code release or interactive demo to verify reproducibility independently.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can basin centers be approximated without access to training data, for example via clustering hidden states during inference over a representative query set? This would be essential for deployment in open-domain settings.
  1. The conflict failure mode (WM–PM competition) resists static geometric monitoring. Can trajectory-based signals—tracking how the hidden state evolves across autoregressive steps rather than measuring a single snapshot—reliably diagnose conflict?
  1. Does attractor geometry persist through RLHF and DPO alignment? Post-training substantially reshapes the hidden-state distribution; it is unclear whether MLP basin structure survives or is overwritten.
  1. The scaling law $C = \exp(-c/\Delta)$ predicts ~18% undetectable hallucinations at 14B. What does this imply at 70B or 405B parameters, and does the trend saturate?
  1. The authors find that ~84% of hallucinated codes are LM head output bias rather than nearest-neighbor basin leakage. What determines which memorized basin the LM head biases toward, and can this be exploited to reduce hallucination by retraining the output projection with an explicit epistemic objective?
  1. The framework treats VO as content write-back and MLP as basin formation. How does this interact with multi-layer residual stream accumulation—do different layers encode different "depths" of the same fact, and does the geometric signal vary by layer?
