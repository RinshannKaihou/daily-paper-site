> **一句话总结：** EmuGEMM 通过内核融合（kernel fusion）和交错数据布局，消除了 Ozaki Scheme I/II 高精度矩阵乘法模拟中 INT32 中间结果的全局内存往返，在 NVIDIA Hopper 上达到 1,639 Top/s（INT8 峰值的 83%）、Blackwell 上 3,654 Top/s（81%），大矩阵下比 cuBLAS TF32 快 1.4×/1.7×，复数 GEMM 比 cuBLAS ZGEMM 快高达 2.3×/5.5×，且精度相当。

> **TL;DR:** EmuGEMM is a fused-kernel library that reconstructs FP32/FP64 GEMM from INT8 Tensor Core operations via the Ozaki Schemes, eliminating the global-memory round-trips for INT32 intermediates that bottleneck prior implementations. By co-designing an MMA-tile-aligned interleaved data layout and in-register/in-TMEM epilogues (shift-reduce for Scheme I, modular reduction + CRT 3M for Scheme II), it sustains 1,639 Top/s on GH200 and 3,654 Top/s on B200, beating cuBLAS TF32 by 1.4×/1.7× and cuBLAS ZGEMM by up to 2.3×/5.5× at comparable accuracy — outperforming both cuBLAS native and cuBLAS Scheme-I emulation across most of the precision spectrum.

## 核心贡献

- **识别并量化了真正的瓶颈**：现有 Ozaki 模拟实现的性能瓶颈不是 Tensor Core 算力，而是 INT32 中间结果在全局内存（GMEM）中的反复读写（数据搬运占主导）。
- **交错数据布局（Interleaved Data Layout）**：将 p 个切片沿收缩维度 K 以 tK（MMA tile 宽度）粒度交错排列，使所有切片可通过单条 TMA 指令一次加载到共享内存，且每个切片处于 MMA 对齐偏移——该布局与切片数据类型无关，适用于 INT8、FP16/BF16 及未来格式。
- **EmuGEMM-I（融合 Scheme I）**：持久化内核，用三角 MMA 调度在 p 个片上累加器上执行全部 p(p+1)/2 个切片乘积，随后通过寄存器内的 shift-reduce 尾数（epilogue）重建输出，全程不再访问 GMEM。
- **EmuGEMM-II（融合 Scheme II）**：将 INT32→INT8 模约减直接融合进 GEMM 内核，消除每个模数（modulus）的 GMEM 往返；进一步实现基于 CRT 的 3M 复数乘法为融合 GEMM，在乘法阶段无误差。
- **跨架构验证**：在 GH200（Hopper）与 B200（Blackwell）上系统评估，证明融合策略对两层架构均有效，并揭示在 Blackwell 上模拟收益更大（FP64 硅片占比下降，模拟吞吐反而上升）。

## Key Contributions

- **Bottleneck identification**: Pinpoints the dominant cost in Ozaki-scheme emulation as INT32 intermediate global-memory traffic (not TC compute), and quantifies the `O(p²)` vs `O(p)` traffic reduction achievable by fusion.
- **Format-agnostic interleaved layout**: A single preprocessing pass interleaves p slices along K at `tK` granularity, satisfying three on-chip constraints (p INT32 accumulators, p operand slices in SMEM, MMA-aligned offsets) with zero extra data movement.
- **EmuGEMM-I (fused Scheme I)**: A persistent kernel executing the `p(p+1)/2` triangular slice products over p on-chip accumulators with a compile-time-resolved address schedule, followed by an in-register shift-reduce epilogue that reconstructs FP32/FP64 without GMEM traffic.
- **EmuGEMM-II (fused Scheme II)**: Fuses the per-modulus `INT32 mod mℓ → INT8` reduction into the GEMM epilogue, and implements the 3M complex formulation as a single sequential kernel reusing one INT32 accumulator, keeping all intermediates on-chip and making 3M strictly preferable (exact integer subtraction avoids catastrophic cancellation).
- **Continuous precision-throughput Pareto frontier**: Together, EmuGEMM-I (better below FP32) and EmuGEMM-II (better above FP32) Pareto-dominate cuBLAS Scheme-I emulation across the full precision range, unlike cuBLAS native's three discrete operating points (TF32/FP32/FP64).

## Methodology

**Hardware target.** NVIDIA Hopper (SM90, GH200: 96 GiB HBM3 @ 4 TB/s, 67 Tflop/s FP64, 1,979 Top/s INT8) and Blackwell (SM100, B200 via Vast.ai: 192 GiB HBM3e @ 8 TB/s, 40 Tflop/s FP64, 4,500 Top/s INT8). INT8 is chosen because `INT8×INT8 → INT32` is exact (no rounding).

**Ozaki Scheme I (mantissa splitting).** Each operand row/column is scaled by power-of-two factors `µ_i`, `ν_j`, then split into β-bit (β ≤ 8) mantissa slices. This yields a triangular set of `p(p+1)/2` INT8 GEMMs accumulated into p INT32 accumulators `C_s`, reassembled via shift-reduce `C = diag(µ) Σ 2^(−βs) C_s diag(ν)` (~8p bits precision; quadratic GEMM count).

**Ozaki Scheme II (CRT / modular).** Operands are scaled to integers and reduced modulo p pairwise co-prime moduli `mℓ ≤ 256`; each modulus produces one independent INT8 GEMM (`p` total, linear scaling). The full integer product is recovered via the Chinese Remainder Theorem provided it does not exceed `P = Π mℓ` (log₂P bits precision).

**Fusion mechanism.** Naive implementations materialize INT32 partial products to GMEM (`4p²MN` accumulator writes + reads). EmuGEMM eliminates this: (1) the interleaved layout streams all p slices per K-step with a single TMA descriptor; (2) the persistent kernel keeps all p accumulators on-chip (RF on Hopper, TMEM on Blackwell); (3) the epilogue (shift-reduce for Scheme I; modular reduction for Scheme II) runs in registers while TMA prefetches the next tile. Arithmetic intensity rises by a factor of `(p+1)/2` for Scheme I and output traffic drops 8× for Scheme II.

**Implementation.** Built atop DeepGEMM (over CuTe/CUTLASS), extended with INT8 operand + INT32 accumulation support; tile parameters `(α, tN, β, γ)` selected via DeepGEMM's autotuner. CUDA 13.1, Ubuntu 24.04. Inputs sampled as `a_ij = (rand−0.5)·exp(φ·randn)` with conditioning `φ=4.0`; medians of ≥30 executions; error reported as "effective bits of precision."

## Results & Highlights

- **Fused-kernel INT8 throughput (GH200):** EmuGEMM-I 1,752 Top/s (89% peak), EmuGEMM-II 1,539 Top/s (78%), cuBLAS native INT8 1,379 Top/s (70%) — fused kernels exceed naive ceil by 27% / 12%.
- **End-to-end INT8:** 1,639 Top/s on GH200, 3,654 Top/s on B200 (83% / 81% of peak).
- **Scheme I, low-precision region:** At p=4, N=4096 on GH200 → 138 Tflop/s, 1.9× over cuBLAS Scheme-I emulation (71) and 2.8× over cuBLAS FP32 (50). At p=2, N=16384 → 516 Tflop/s (GH200) / 1,203 Tflop/s (B200), 1.4× / 1.7× over cuBLAS TF32.
- **Scheme II, high-precision region:** At p=15, N=16384 → 94 Tflop/s (GH200) / 165 Tflop/s (B200), 1.6× / 4.6× over cuBLAS FP64 (58 / 36). Scheme I alone cannot reach FP64 on Hopper (abundant native FP64 silicon).
- **Complex GEMM (3M):** EmuGEMM-II ZGEMM at p=15 → 129 Tflop/s (GH200) / 204 Tflop/s (B200), 2.3× / 5.5× over cuBLAS ZGEMM (55 / 37).
- **vs GEMMul8 (SOTA Scheme II):** Faster in nearly all matched (p,N) configurations, accuracy within a few bits of GEMMul8-Accu.
- **Cross-architecture trend:** As FP64 silicon share shrinks Hopper→Blackwell, emulation advantage grows — EmuGEMM-II throughput *rises* (94→165 Tflop/s) while cuBLAS FP64 *falls* (58→36).
- **Trade-offs:** Higher precision costs workspace — both schemes need p INT8 residue matrices/operand; Scheme II additionally materializes p per-modulus INT8 outputs for CRT, so Scheme I is preferable under memory constraints.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The core insight — that INT32 intermediate GMEM traffic, not TC throughput, is the bottleneck in Ozaki emulation — is well-motivated and the fused-kernel + interleaved-layout solution is genuinely new and non-trivial. Realizing fusion across two distinct schemes with co-designed memory layouts is substantial systems work.
- **Rigor (Good, with caveats):** Rigorous traffic analysis (`O(p²)→O(p)` derivations), arithmetic-intensity-plane modeling (Fig. 4), and clean head-to-head vs cuBLAS native/emulation and GEMMul8-Accu/Fast at matched (p,N). Medians of ≥30 runs. However, only **square matrices** (M=N=K ∈ {2048,…,16384}) are reported; tall-skinny/batched GEMM (the dominant shapes in many real HPC apps) are explicitly *not* tested.
- **Reproducibility (Moderate):** DeepGEMM + CUTLASS + CUDA 13.1 are specified; the B200 is accessed via Vast.ai (a transient cloud, not owned hardware). No code release is announced in the paper, and INT8/INT32 extensions to DeepGEMM are not stated to be open-sourced. DeepGEMM currently only supports Hopper/Blackwell, restricting portability.
- **Significance (High):** Directly addresses the widening precision-throughput gap that threatens FP64 HPC on consumer/prosumer GPUs. Pareto-dominating cuBLAS's own emulation across the precision range is a meaningful, verifiable claim.
- **Red flags / overclaim check:**
  - The headline "1.7× over cuBLAS TF32" and "5.5× over ZGEMM" are real but specific to **large matrices**; the authors are commendably honest that small matrices favor native FP64 and that preprocessing overhead dominates there.
  - **Accuracy claims rest on prior work**: the paper explicitly defers the full accuracy analysis to cited Ozaki/GEMMul8 studies and uses only the high-conditioning `φ=4.0` random ensemble — a defensible but narrow data distribution. Real scientific workloads (ill-conditioned, structured, sparse) are untested.
  - **Workspace cost is understated in the headline**: Scheme II's memory footprint grows with p and could matter for memory-bound workloads, though it is disclosed in Fig. 7.
  - The paper conflates "effective performance" (counting only useful FP-equivalent ops) reporting with kernel-only timing — preprocessing (slicing) and CRT reconstruction kernel costs are excluded from the throughput numbers in Fig. 4 but included end-to-end in Fig. 5; readers must be careful which regime applies.

## Strengths

- Clean, quantified identification of the data-movement bottleneck with explicit traffic formulas.
- Co-designed layout + kernel that is **format-agnostic** (extends naturally to FP16/BF16/FP8 emulation).
- The 3M complex formulation argument is elegant: integer modular arithmetic makes the `T3−T1−T2` step exact, so 3M is *strictly* better than 4M under Scheme II — a clean, defensible design choice.
- Honest limitations section; does not hide the small-matrix regime or workspace cost.
- Genuine cross-architecture insight (emulation advantage scales with the precision gap, favoring Blackwell).

## Limitations

- Only square matrices in {2048–16384}; no tall-skinny, batched, or irregular shapes.
- Synthetic random inputs with a single conditioning parameter; no application-realistic matrices (e.g., dense linear algebra, FEM, quantum chemistry).
- Accuracy analysis largely deferred to prior literature rather than demonstrated empirically across regimes.
- No automatic dynamic precision (ADP) as in cuBLAS — slice count must be hand-picked.
- Hopper/Blackwell only; portability to other vendors/architectures unproven (partly a DeepGEMM limitation).
- CRT reconstruction runs as a separate kernel (multi-word integer arithmetic exceeds register budget), so some GMEM traffic remains in Scheme II.
- No code/artifact availability stated.

## Relevance

对于关注 GPU 高性能计算、低精度算力迁移与数值线性代数的读者，本文直接回应了"FP64 算力在消费级/数据中心 GPU 上被挤压"这一趋势，提供了在 Hopper/Blackwell 上将 INT8 张量核心用于高精度模拟的工程级最优解，可作为评估下一代加速器数值精度策略与内核融合技术的重要参考。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does EmuGEMM behave on **tall-skinny and batched GEMM** (the shapes common in FEM assembly and QR factorization), where arithmetic intensity is inherently low?
2. What is the accuracy profile on **structured / ill-conditioned / sparse real-application matrices** (e.g., from quantum chemistry or climate codes), and does the `φ=4.0` random ensemble misrepresent worst cases?
3. Can the fused-kernel technique extend to **FP8 Tensor Cores** (Mukunoki et al.'s FP8-based formulation), and does the reduced INT8 share on future chips (B300) change the trade-off?
4. What is the end-to-end speedup inside a **real solver pipeline** (LU/QR/eigenvalue) where preprocessing + CRT reconstruction are amortized across many GEMMs?
5. Is the interleaved-layout preprocessing cost low enough to reuse across iterations, and how does it interact with autotuning overhead at runtime?
6. Could the 3M-exactness insight (integer arithmetic avoids cancellation) generalize to other **mixed-precision algorithms** that currently avoid 3M due to FP cancellation?
