Conformal Recovery-Deadline Certificates for Runtime Assurance of Adapting Controllers

arXiv: 2606.25371

Date: 2026-06-24

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25371

一句话总结： 论文指出传统 Runtime Assurance 的"一旦越界即锁存切换"规则会误杀掉那些在故障恢复瞬态中"故意越界以辨识故障"的可在线自适应控制器；作者用 split-conformal 预测对控制器的"恢复时间（recovery time）"给出一个分布无关、有限样本的上界 dα 作为延迟回退的截止期限，并由一个经过形式化验证的 backstop 在硬临界限上守住安全底线——将"自主性（受统计覆盖率约束）"与"安全性（受验证 backstop 约束）"显式解耦，在 6-DOF 航天器姿态控制与倒立摆两个无关系统上验证了同一病理与同一疗法。
TL;DR: Standard runtime assurance latches to a verified fallback on the first safe-set breach — correct for a diverging controller but a suppression pathology for a capable online-adapting one that must transiently breach the safe set to identify a fault. The author replaces the latch with a split-conformal, distribution-free, finite-sample upper bound on the controller's recovery time, used as an engagement deadline, while a verified backstop enforces safety at a hard critical limit. Three coverage theorems (marginal, weighted-under-shift, Mondrian) are proven and demonstrated on two unrelated Simplex testbeds (6-DOF spacecraft, torque-controlled inverted pendulum).

核心贡献

  • 识别并命名一种结构性病态（suppression pathology）： 当 Simplex 式 RTA 的"越界即锁存"规则遇上在线自适应控制器时，会把后者为辨识故障而进行的"恢复瞬态越界"误判为发散，从而永久压制一个本可自愈的控制器；更糟的是回退的 coast 行为会移除控制器辨识故障所需的激励。作者强调这是"基于当前状态做切换决策"的结构性后果，而非某个具体 shield 的缺陷。
  • 提出保形恢复截止证书（Conformal Recovery-Deadline Certificate）： 用 split-conformal 预测对恢复时间（定义为 last-exit time + 在 +∞ 处的右删失原子）做分布无关、有限样本的上界 dα，作为延迟回退的截止期限；恢复率不足 1−α 时证书诚实地返回 +∞ 而拒绝发证。
  • 可靠性非对称设计（reliability-asymmetric design）： 自主性由统计覆盖率管辖、安全性由形式化验证的 backstop 管辖，两者失效模式不重叠——校准错误只损失自主性、永不危及安全（Prop. 1），这正是分布无关统计对象能放进安全关键回路的依据。
  • 三条分级覆盖率定理： 边际覆盖率（Thm. 1，在可交换性下 P(τ≤dα)≥1−α）、已知故障分布漂移下的加权恢复覆盖率（Thm. 2，Tibshirani et al. weighted conformal）、以及分组条件 Mondrian 覆盖率（Thm. 3，每故障类别条件覆盖）。
  • 跨领域机制性验证： 在共享零动力学的两个系统（6-DOF 航天器 + 倒立摆）上重现同一病理与同一疗法，证明这是一种可移植的通用机制而非单系统 trick。

Key Contributions

  • Diagnoses a structural suppression pathology in RTA: The inherited "latch on first breach and coast" rule is provably correct for a diverging controller but pathological for a capable online-adapting controller, whose diagnostic excursion out of the safe set is indistinguishable at the instant of first breach from genuine divergence. The paper frames this as a consequence of fusing the "unsafe now" and "what to do" questions into a single present-state decision.
  • Introduces the conformal recovery-deadline certificate: A split-conformal, distribution-free, finite-sample upper bound on recovery time (cast as a horizon-censored last-exit time with a +∞ censoring atom) that licenses delayed fallback for a certified duration, with an honest +∞ refusal when the recovery rate falls below 1−α.
  • Formalizes reliability-asymmetric design (Prop. 1): Autonomy is governed by the statistical coverage; safety is governed unconditionally by the verified backstop at the critical limit Φc. Any miscalibration of the certificate costs autonomy, never safety — the precise reason a distribution-free statistical object can sit inside a safety-critical loop.
  • Three graded coverage guarantees: Marginal coverage under exchangeability (Thm. 1), weighted coverage restoring the guarantee under a known fault-distribution shift (Thm. 2), and group-conditional Mondrian coverage (Thm. 3) for per-fault-class guarantees.
  • Domain-generality demonstration: Same pathology and same cure on two plants sharing no dynamics, actuator model, or controller code — a 6-DOF spacecraft attitude controller and a torque-controlled inverted pendulum.

Methodology

  • Object certified — recovery time (Def. 1): Defined as a last-exit time τ over a finite horizon H, not a first-return time, because operationally what matters is the first step after which the trajectory never re-breaches the safe set. It carries a +∞ right-censoring atom for episodes that never recover; this atom is essential because a diverging controller yields τ = +∞, and any procedure that drops/imputes non-recovering episodes would fabricate a finite deadline. τ is a measurable functional of the complete length-H trajectory (a totally ordered set [0,H]∪{+∞}), not a stopping time w.r.t. the forward filtration — by design, since conformal prediction only needs a well-defined score per completed episode.
  • Assumption 1 (Exchangeability): Calibration recovery times τ1…τn and the next test τn+1 are exchangeable (e.g., i.i.d. from a fixed fault distribution). No assumption on the distribution's form, the controller, or the plant dynamics. When the deployment mix departs, exchangeability breaks and is recovered by reweighting (Thm. 2) or restricted to per-class statements (Thm. 3).
  • Deadline (Eq. 1): dα = τ(k) where k = ⌈(1−α)(n+1)⌉ when k ≤ n, else +∞. It is the smallest deadline under which at least a 1−α fraction of calibration episodes recovered, with the conformal +1 correction that makes the bound finite-sample rather than asymptotic. Minimum usable calibration set: n ≥ ⌈1/α⌉ − 1 (e.g., 19 at α=0.05).
  • Engagement rule: Keep the advanced controller π engaged while e_t < Φc AND t < dα; escalate to the verified safe controller at the first time e_t ≥ Φc (backstop) or at t = dα if unrecovered. Two exit conditions carry different authority types — a hard physical gate vs. a statistical judgment. The offline-calibrated last-exit τ and the online current-state decision can disagree only on re-breach after apparent recovery, and that disagreement affects only retention, never safety.
  • Coverage proofs: Thm. 1 via the standard exchangeability rank argument over the totally ordered set (handles the +∞ atom as an ordinary largest element, no continuity needed); Corollary 1 gives training-conditional Beta(k, n+1−k) coverage for the lower-tail guarantee an operator should actually care about; Thm. 2 via Tibshirani et al. weighted conformal under covariate shift Q≪P with likelihood ratio w; Thm. 3 via within-class application of Thm. 1 (needs ⌈(1−α)/α⌉ per-class samples, else +∞ for that class).
  • Testbeds: Domain 1 — Rapid-Motor-Adaptation controller on a 6-DOF Basilisk attitude simulator with unmodeled reaction-wheel (reversal-heavy gain) faults, safe set 5°, verified backstop Φc=90° backed by a Kind 2-checked second-order monitor. Domain 2 — pure-NumPy deterministic torque-controlled inverted pendulum (unstable upright) with actuator effectiveness/sign fault b, safe set 5°, backstop Φc=45°; here the authors own the fault sampler so the shift likelihood ratio is exact.

Results & Highlights

  • Coverage tracks target on both plants (Table I): Spacecraft achieved mean coverage 0.808 / 0.902 / 0.950 at α = 0.20 / 0.10 / 0.05 (over 300 calibration/test splits); pendulum 0.81 / 0.91 / 0.96. The conformity to y=x across two unrelated plants is the calibration evidence.
  • The heuristic is exposed as neither tunable nor accountable: The practitioner "p95 × 1.3" heuristic over-covers at ~0.97 regardless of target — it cannot be dialed to a risk posture and carries no coverage guarantee. The conformal deadline is a tunable knob, each setting backed by a distribution-free promise.
  • The certificate discriminates capable from incapable: At α=0.1 (dα=152 steps on the spacecraft), it keeps the recovering controller 94.5% autonomous while the fault-unaware controller (τ effectively +∞) misses the deadline and is caught at 0% autonomous — using only recovery time and the stated 1−α.
  • The suppression pathology quantified (Table II, pendulum reversals): Latching Simplex retains only 0.5% autonomy (the pathology in one number); the heuristic recovers 100% but offers no guarantee; CP-on-safety-value [Tabbara 2025] retains 93.5% with a 1−α guarantee on peak excursion; the recovery-deadline rule retains 88.0% with a 1−α guarantee on recovery time. All four rules catch 100% of divergence before the verified backstop — none trades away safety.
  • Weighted coverage restores the guarantee under a real shift: On the pendulum, shifting the reversal fraction from 30% (calibration) to 70% (deployment) makes the unweighted deadline under-cover at 86.0% (below its 0.90 target) — exchangeability failing exactly as theory predicts. The weighted deadline restores coverage to 93.5% with effective sample size n_eff=115 honestly reporting how much the shift thinned the 200-episode set. Mondrian per-class deadlines: 0 steps (benign class, never breaches) and 37 steps (reversal class) — correct refusal-to-pool behavior.
  • Honest +∞ by design: When the controller's recovery rate is below the requested 1−α, the certificate returns +∞ and refuses to certify rather than fabricate a finite deadline — the discipline that makes the finite deadlines it does issue trustworthy.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty — genuinely novel composition, not novel ingredients: The author is scrupulously honest that conformal prediction, weighted conformal, and Mondrian conformal are "used as machinery rather than claimed as novelty." The novelty is the runtime object they are applied to — certifying recovery time as a last-exit/censored quantity and using it as an engagement deadline — and the reliability-asymmetric separation of an unverified statistical layer from a verified safety floor. This is a real conceptual contribution to the RTA literature, which has not previously paired a verified shield with an online-adapting controller in this way.
  • Rigor — high: Proofs are short and rely on the standard exchangeability rank argument, correctly handling the +∞ censoring atom as an ordinary largest element of a totally ordered set. The distinction between marginal, training-conditional (Beta), and group-conditional coverage is stated precisely and turned into measured quantities. Prop. 1 cleanly isolates the safety argument from the statistical one.
  • Reproducibility — strong on Domain 2, contingent on Domain 1: The pendulum testbed is fully open (pure-NumPy, deterministic, owned fault sampler). The spacecraft testbed depends on an external "Paper A reproduced manifest" (conformal_rta.py), which is referenced but not in this paper's scope; an independent reader cannot regenerate Domain 1 from this manuscript alone. All claims are manifest-reproducible given those evidence files.
  • Significance — moderate but real: It addresses a genuine and previously under-articulated gap (adapting controllers under RTA), and the two-domain demonstration supports the "mechanism, not trick" claim. However, the adapting controllers tested are analytic feedback laws with a scalar fault parameter; the claim that this is a "drop-in for any Simplex-style system with an online-adapting advanced controller" is plausible but not yet demonstrated for learned/neural adaptive controllers at scale.
  • Red flags — few, mostly disclosed: (i) The headline guarantee is marginal, not per-fault or worst-case — disclosed and partially remedied by Thm. 3. (ii) Shift coverage (Thm. 2) requires a known or well-estimated likelihood ratio; the exact-ratio pendulum experiment is a controlled illustration, while real flight deployment needs telemetry-based estimation (honesty via reported n_eff). (iii) The certificate is per-controller and per-fault-class — re-calibration is required for any change. (iv) Single-author, no human/physical flight hardware results; both testbeds are simulation. (v) Neural-Simplex reverse switching is dismissed as degenerating to latching on the open-loop-unstable pendulum — a reasonable but convenient omission; the comparison is not run.

Strengths

  • Identifies and rigorously names a real structural pathology (suppression of capable adapting controllers) rather than patching around it.
  • The +∞ refusal branch is a principled, honesty-preserving design — the certificate refuses rather than fabricates when data does not support a deadline.
  • Clean separation of autonomy (statistical) from safety (verified), giving a defensible engineering posture for putting distribution-free statistics in a safety-critical loop.
  • Same pathology/cure on two unrelated plants makes the generality claim empirically grounded.
  • Honest treatment of coverage scope (marginal vs. conditional vs. Mondrian) and of distribution shift (with effective-sample-size disclosure).

Limitations

  • Marginal coverage only; can under-cover hard sub-classes (remedied but not eliminated by Mondrian).
  • Weighted/Mondrian extensions need known likelihood ratio / sufficient per-class samples — not always available in deployment.
  • Per-controller, per-fault-class calibration; no transfer across controllers or materially different fault classes.
  • Tested controllers are analytic adaptive laws with scalar faults; generalization to high-dimensional or learned adaptive controllers is asserted, not shown.
  • Both domains are simulation; no hardware-in-the-loop or human-in-the-loop validation.
  • Reliance on an external "Paper A" manifest for the spacecraft domain limits standalone reproducibility.

Relevance

对关注"安全关键系统中可验证安全 + 自适应/学习型控制器（RTA / shielding / conformal 保证）"方向高度相关：它把 conformal prediction 的分布无关覆盖率与形式化验证的安全 backstop 解耦组合，为"在保证安全的前提下给在线自适应控制器留出故障恢复瞬态"提供了一个可移植、可调、可审计的工程范式，可作为 RTA、shielded RL 与可信赖控制交叉研究的参考样本。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the certificate behave for high-dimensional or neural-network adaptive controllers (e.g., online meta-learning policies) where the recovery-time distribution may be multimodal or heavy-tailed?
  • Can the likelihood ratio under shift be estimated online from telemetry with provable bounds on the estimation error feeding into Thm. 2, rather than assumed known?
  • How does the Mondrian deadline scale when fault classes are many and calibration episodes per class are sparse — is there a hierarchical/borrowing-strength variant that avoids the +∞ refusal for rare classes?
  • Does the engagement rule interact well with control-barrier-function safety filters, which forbid the very excursion the adapting controller needs? Can the two be composed at different layers (barrier at Φc, deadline in the warning zone)?
  • What is the tightest analysis when episodes are only approximately exchangeable (e.g., mild non-stationarity in the fault process), and can the over-coverage slack be bounded under such approximate exchangeability?
  • Could the recovery-time last-exit framing be ported to discrete-decision settings beyond continuous control (e.g., shielded RL with discrete actions, or LLM-agent safety shields)?
