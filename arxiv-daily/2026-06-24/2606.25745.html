Gaussian Mean Field Variational Inference can Overestimate Predictive Variance

arXiv: 2606.25745

Date: 2026-06-24

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25745

一句话总结： 本文以共轭贝叶斯线性回归(BLR)为分析工具，指出"平均场变分推断(MFVI)低估方差"这一共识并不完整——它在参数空间与各向同性测试点处确实低估方差(过自信)，但若 MFVI 在某些方向低估预测方差，就必然在另一些方向高估，且这种高估恰好发生在训练数据集中的方向(第一主成分)上；因此对来自训练分布的同分布(ID)测试点，MFVI 的期望预测方差反而超过精确后验(欠自信)。作者证明 MFVI 协方差特征值为精确后验特征值的加权调和平均，由此在秩-1/高维病态情形下 MFVI 几乎不比先验降低预测方差，并把该效应与冷后验效应(CPE)联系起来：降温(T<1)修正 ID 过估、升温(T>1)修正 OOD 低估。
TL;DR: Using conjugate Bayesian Linear Regression (BLR) as a tractable testbed, the authors show the standard "MFVI underestimates variance" picture is incomplete. MFVI indeed underestimates variance in parameter space and for isotropic test points, but a complementarity property (MFVI's covariance eigenvalues are a weighted harmonic mean of the exact posterior eigenvalues) forces overestimation in other directions — precisely along the first principal component of the training data. Consequently, for an in-distribution (ID) test point, MFVI's expected predictive variance exceeds the exact posterior's. In a rank-1/high-dimensional pathological regime, MFVI barely reduces predictive variance below the prior at all. The authors connect this to the Cold Posterior Effect: cooling (T<1) corrects ID overestimation while warming (T>1) corrects OOD underestimation, validated on synthetic sinc data, UCI regression with RBF basis functions, and small/large IVON-trained BNNs.

核心贡献

  • 纠正流行共识：MFVI 在参数空间与各向同性测试点处确实低估方差(Lemma 3.3)，但"一旦预测而非推断参数"这一图景就不完整。
  • 互补方向性(Lemma 3.6 "Calibrated MFVI")：MFVI 预测方差与精确后验方差之比 R(x) 在精确后验完整特征基上的平均为 1，即"低估"与"高估"必须互相补偿。
  • 主定理 3.7：对球型先验 θ∼N(0,α⁻¹I)，当测试点服从训练经验分布 x∼p̂(x) 时，MFVI 的期望预测方差大于等于精确后验的预测方差，即 MFVI 在 ID 数据上欠自信。
  • 关键机理(Lemma 3.2)：MFVI 最优协方差特征值是精确后验特征值的加权调和平均，而调和平均 ≤ 最小值，故在最小特征值方向(对球型先验即数据第一主成分)MFVI 必然高估(Lemma 3.4、Theorem 3.5)。
  • 病态高维情形(Proposition 5.1)：当训练数据落在秩-1 非轴对齐子空间且 P→∞ 时，MFVI 在任意测试点的预测方差退化为先验方差 x⊤S∗x = α⁻‖x‖²，即完全没用上数据信息。
  • 将上述现象统一解释为冷后验效应(CPE)的一个新来源(无需模型误设、先验误设或数据增强)：降温 T<1 可修正 ID 高估；对与训练正交的 OOD 方向则需升温 T>1(温后验效应)。
  • 实验验证：合成 sinc(RBF 基函数, Q=16 vs 1024)、UCI 回归(foong 子集, Q=500 RBF)用六种散度(前/逆 KL、α-散度、Wasserstein-2、Frobenius)一致显示 ID 最优 T<1、OOD 最优 T>1；并用 IVON 训练的小/大 BNN 初步表明该机理可延伸到深度网络。

Key Contributions

  • Refines the textbook view: MFVI does underestimate variance in parameter space and for isotropic test points (Lemma 3.3), but this misses what happens at the prediction level.
  • A complementarity / calibration property (Lemma 3.6): the variance ratio R(x)=x⊤S∗x / x⊤Σx averages to exactly 1 over the full eigenbasis of the exact posterior, so underestimation in some directions forces overestimation in others.
  • Main Theorem 3.7: for a spherical prior, MFVI's expected predictive variance at an in-distribution test point (x∼p̂(x)) is ≥ that of the exact posterior — i.e., MFVI is underconfident exactly where future data is most likely to arrive.
  • The underlying mechanism (Lemma 3.2): the optimal MFVI covariance eigenvalues are a weighted harmonic mean of the exact posterior eigenvalues, and since a harmonic mean is ≤ its minimum, MFVI must overestimate along the minimum-eigenvalue direction (the first principal component of the data under a spherical prior) — Lemmas 3.4 / Theorem 3.5.
  • A pathological high-dimensional regime (Proposition 5.1): with rank-1 non-axis-aligned training data, as P→∞ the MFVI predictive variance collapses to the prior variance (α⁻¹‖x‖²²), so MFVI fails to use the data at all.
  • Frames this as a novel source of the Cold Posterior Effect (no likelihood/prior misspecification, no data augmentation): cooling (T<1) corrects ID overestimation; for OOD directions orthogonal to the data, a warm posterior (T>1) is instead optimal.
  • Empirical validation across synthetic sinc data (RBF basis, Q=16 vs 1024), UCI regression (Foong et al. 2019 subset, Q=500 RBF) using six divergences (forward/reverse KL, α-divergence, Wasserstein-2, Frobenius), and small/large IVON-trained BNNs consistently showing ID-optimal T<1 and OOD-optimal T>1.

Methodology

The paper uses conjugate Bayesian Linear Regression (BLR) as a setting where both the exact posterior p(θ|D)=N(µ,Σ) and the Mean Field variational posterior q(θ)=N(m,S) (diagonal, i.e. axis-aligned eigenbasis) are available in closed form, so predictive variances can be compared analytically.

  • Model & posterior. Linear predictor y=θ⊤x+ε, ε∼N(0,σ²), spherical prior θ∼N(0,α⁻¹I), both assumed well-specified and known. True posterior Σ=(αI + σ⁻²X⊤X)⁻¹, µ=σ⁻²ΣX⊤Y.
  • MFVI objective. Minimize reverse KL to the true (Gaussian) posterior; the optimal mean equals the true mean (m∗=µ, Lemma 3.1), so all differences are in variance.
  • Key identity (Lemma 3.2). Diagonalize Σ=V∆V⊤ with eigenvalues δ_q. The optimal MFVI eigenvalues d∗_p satisfy 1/d∗_p = Σ_q w_pq / δ_q with w_pq=(v_q⊤e_p)² — i.e., each MFVI eigenvalue is a weighted harmonic mean of the exact posterior eigenvalues. The authors note this harmonic-mean characterization is, to their knowledge, new.
  • Predictive variance comparison. Excluding aleatoric noise, the predictive (epistemic) variances are x⊤Σx (exact) vs x⊤S∗x (MFVI). Analytical lemmas compare these along the isotropic distribution, the minimum-eigenvalue direction, and the empirical distribution p̂(x).
  • Cold/warm posterior construction (Section 4). Define the cold posterior pT(θ|D) ∝ p(D|θ)^(1/T)p(θ)^(1/T); minimizing KL to it gives the T-MFVI posterior qT∗(θ)=N(m∗, T·S∗), which keeps the same mean for all T (unlike a tempered posterior). This is why the authors prefer cold over tempered posteriors here.
  • Experiments.
  • Pathological rank-1 BLR (Section 5.1): training data on subspace x=ε·1_P, evaluated at ID point x=1_P/√P and OOD point x=1_P^±/√P (half +1/half −1, orthogonal to data); test NLL vs temperature averaged over 10,000 runs, for P=2 and P=1024.
  • Basis-function regression (Section 5.2): RBF features ϕ(·) with learnable lengthscale/centroids; sinc toy data (Q=16 vs 1024); UCI regression (Foong et al. 2019 datasets) with Q=500 fixed RBF; both marginal and joint predictive distributions compared via forward KL, reverse KL, α-divergence (α=0.5), Wasserstein-2, and Frobenius norm of covariance difference; 15 train/ID/OOD splits.
  • BNN preliminary (Section 5.3): 1D sinusoidal data trained with IVON (Shen et al. 2024), comparing depth-2 width-16 vs width-512 networks.
  • Code released at https://github.com/jamesacodgers/mfvi-cpe.

Results & Highlights

  • Lemma 3.3 (standard result recovered). For isotropic test points x∼N(0,I_P), the exact posterior predictive variance ≥ MFVI's (MFVI is overconfident on average over all directions).
  • Lemma 3.4 / Theorem 3.5. Along the minimum-eigenvalue direction of the exact posterior — which, for a spherical prior, is the first principal component of the training data — MFVI overestimates predictive variance: x̃⊤S∗x̃ ≥ x̃⊤Σx̃.
  • Lemma 3.6 (calibration constraint). Averaged over the exact-posterior eigenbasis, the variance ratio R(x)=x⊤S∗x / x⊤Σx equals exactly 1 (proven via Tr(Σ⁻¹S∗)=P). This couples under- and overestimation and explains why high-dimensional overestimation must be severe.
  • Theorem 3.7 (main result). For test points drawn from the empirical training distribution, E_{x∼p̂(x)}[x⊤Σx − x⊤S∗x] ≤ 0 — MFVI's expected ID predictive variance exceeds the exact posterior's.
  • Proposition 5.1 (pathological limit). For rank-1 non-axis-aligned data, as P→∞, MFVI's predictive variance → α⁻¹‖x‖²² (the prior), i.e. it fails to incorporate the data at all. Confirmed visually in Fig. 2 (P=1024: MFVI predictive nearly indistinguishable from the prior predictive, while the exact posterior is well-concentrated).
  • Cold/Warm posterior effects (Figs. 3–4). Test NLL vs temperature: ID data has an optimal T<1 that makes T-MFVI match the exact posterior; the effect is much stronger and the optimal T much lower at P=1024 than at P=2. For OOD directions the pattern reverses — a warm posterior (T>1) is better at P=2; at P=1024 optimal T≈1 since MFVI already ≈ prior in the many orthogonal directions.
  • Basis-function / UCI results (Figs. 5–6). With Q=1024 RBF features the MFVI predictive is "highly overinflated" vs Q=16. Across all six divergences and UCI splits, ID (train + ID test) is always best at T<1 while OOD test requires higher (often T>1) temperatures — consistent with Theorem 3.7 and the complementarity constraint.
  • BNN preliminary (Fig. 7). Larger IVON BNNs (width 512) show substantially wider credible intervals than small ones (width 16), mirroring the basis-function result that increasing parameter-space dimension inflates ID predictive variance — preliminary evidence the mechanism extends beyond conjugate models.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-to-High. The "MFVI underestimates variance" mantra is well known; the contribution is the reframing from parameter space to predictive space, plus the clean harmonic-mean mechanism (Lemma 3.2) and the complementarity constraint (Lemma 3.6) that make Theorem 3.7 fall out. The CPE connection is incremental given the large existing CPE literature, but the mechanistic (no-misspecification) story is a genuinely new angle.
  • Rigor: Strong. Proofs are provided for every lemma/theorem/proposition in Appendix A, the setting (conjugate BLR) is exactly where the analysis is tight, and the harmonic-mean identity is a clean, reusable result. The CPE-vs-tempered-posterior distinction (Section 4) is handled carefully and correctly.
  • Generality: The central limitation, acknowledged by the authors. All theory is for conjugate linear/Gaussian models. Extension to BNNs is only preliminary (Section 5.3, two networks) and the link to deep-learning Hessian structure (flat in most directions, sharp in data directions — Sagun et al. 2016) is a plausibility argument, not a theorem. The real-world experiments use RBF basis-function regression (effectively still linear in features), not genuine non-conjugate deep models, so "real-world" is somewhat overstated.
  • Significance: Moderate. Conceptually important for how the BDL community reasons about MFVI and CPE, and could justify temperature-schedule choices. But because the theoretical guarantees are confined to the linear regime, practical guidance for practitioners using MFVI in modern deep nets remains suggestive rather than proven.
  • Red flags / caveats to weigh:
  • The "real-world regression tasks" in the abstract are RBF basis-function regressions on UCI data — still linear-in-features, so they do not directly demonstrate the effect in non-linear/non-conjugate models.
  • "MFVI can overestimate" is correctly hedged ("can", direction- and distribution-dependent); readers should not infer MFVI is always underconfident — it is overconfident for isotropic/OOD points and underconfident for ID points.
  • The CPE explanation offered is additional, not a replacement for data-augmentation/misspecification explanations; the authors are explicit about this, but casual reading could over-credit the MFVI mechanism.
  • The BNN experiment uses IVON with only two network sizes and one dataset — too small to be conclusive about scaling behavior in deep networks.

Strengths

  • Clean, interpretable analytical setting (conjugate BLR) where every claim is provable.
  • The harmonic-mean characterization (Lemma 3.2) of MFVI eigenvalues is a crisp, reusable technical result.
  • The complementarity constraint (Lemma 3.6, average R=1) elegantly ties together the under-/overestimation behavior.
  • Honest and precise scoping: clearly separates ID (overestimation) from OOD (underestimation) regimes, and cold vs warm posteriors.
  • Thoughtful, correct treatment of the cold-vs-tempered posterior distinction (same mean for all T under the cold objective).
  • Peer-reviewed venue (ICML 2026) and released code.

Limitations

  • All theoretical guarantees are for the conjugate linear/Gaussian regime; transfer to deep non-conjugate models is only argued by analogy (Hessian structure) and a tiny BNN experiment.
  • The "real-world" experiments are RBF basis-function regressions — still linear in the (learned) feature space.
  • The effect is distribution-dependent: most pronounced when training data concentrates on a low-rank non-axis-aligned subspace and the test point is ID; behavior under realistic covariate shift or with many informative directions is less stark.
  • The CPE link is one of several competing explanations; isolating MFVI's contribution empirically in deep nets is not done here.
  • No analysis of how the effect interacts with hyperparameter (prior precision α, noise σ²) learning, which is held fixed (and is the focus of the contemporaneous Harvey et al. 2025 work the authors note).
  • Only marginal-vs-joint and a fixed set of divergences are studied; results are qualitative ("T<1 for ID, T>1 for OOD") rather than giving a principled recipe to choose T in practice.

Relevance

对从事贝叶斯深度学习、不确定性估计、变分推断与冷后验效应(CPE)研究的读者高度相关：本文为"MFVI 一致低估方差"这一共识提供了重要的预测层面修正——它在同分布(ID)数据上反而高估(欠自信)，且高维/低秩数据下会退化到先验。该机理(协方差特征值为精确后验的加权调和平均)为理解 BNN 中 MFVI 的不确定性行为、为冷/温后验调温实践、以及为评估 IVON 等近似推断优化器提供了直接的理论启示。对应用侧(主动学习、OOD 检测、贝叶斯优化)的读者，结论提示：不要默认 MFVI 在 ID 上"过自信"，高维场景下恰恰相反。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can Theorem 3.7 (ID overestimation) be extended beyond conjugate BLR — e.g. to generalized-linear or shallow BNN regimes — and what are the precise conditions under which it holds in deep non-conjugate models?
  • How does the effect interact with learned prior precision α and noise σ² (rather than the fixed, well-specified setting here), given Harvey et al. 2025's related findings?
  • Since the harmonic-mean mechanism (Lemma 3.2) is the root cause, do non-mean-field or structured (full-covariance / block-diagonal) variational families — e.g. VIKING (Fadel et al. 2025) — provably avoid the ID overestimation, and by how much?
  • Is there a principled, data-driven way to choose the temperature T (or a per-direction T) that recovers well-calibrated ID and OOD predictive variance simultaneously, rather than a single global T?
  • How does the rank-1/pathological scaling (Proposition 5.1) manifest in real deep nets trained with IVON — does MFVI predictive variance on ID data keep inflating with width/depth as the small BNN experiment hints?
  • Can the complementarity constraint (average R=1) be used as a diagnostic to detect when an MFVI approximation is degrading into the prior on ID data?
