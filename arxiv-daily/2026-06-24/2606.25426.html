Above the Inner Loop: Exceeding Accelerate at LLM Prefill GEMM on the M1 AMX

arXiv: 2606.25426

Date: 2026-06-24

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25426

一句话总结： 本文证明，在 Apple M1 的 AMX 矩阵协处理器上，手写 fp32 GEMM 核函数能超过 Accelerate 全部三条 fp32 路径（在 12 个 LLM prefill 形状上几何平均领先 BNNSMatMul 1.58 倍、cblas_sgemm 约 2.0 倍、BNNS Graph 1.17 倍），但这个提速并非来自更快的内层循环——AMX 内循环受 load-issue 限制已到硬件极限；真正带来增益的是两个"内循环之上"的工程杠杆：用足够细的多线程列面板填满 M1 第二个 AMX 块（赢得 K≥N 形状），以及在加载时一次性预打包常量权重（赢得 N>K 形状）。
TL;DR: On Apple's M1, the fp32 GEMMs dominating LLM prefill are dispatched by Accelerate to the AMX coprocessor. The authors build a bit-exact direct-AMX kernel that beats all three Accelerate fp32 paths (geomean 1.58x over BNNSMatMul, ~2.0x over cblas_sgemm, 1.17x over BNNS Graph) across all twelve prefill GEMMs at S=128 (GPT-2 to Llama-7B scale), and lifts llama.cpp full-forward throughput from 291 to 420 tokens/s (1.44x, bit-identical). The headline finding is structural, not a tuning result: the AMX inner loop is load-issue bound at the hardware limit (~610-680 GFLOPS once any operand load interleaves), so all the gain comes from two "above-the-inner-loop" levers Accelerate underuses — fine multi-thread panels that fill the M1's idle second AMX block (winning K≥N shapes), and pre-packing the constant weight once at load (winning N>K shapes).

核心贡献

  • 对 M1 AMX 内循环的微架构级刻画：通过隔离的、cache-resident 微基准测试，证明 AMX 的 FMA32 内循环是 load-issue 受限的——只要任何一个操作数 load 与 FMA32 流交错，单线程吞吐就从无负载的 ~1525 GFLOPS 跌到 610-680 GFLOPS 的固定带（不到一半），且无论 tile 形状、load 配对、load 数量还是相位批处理都无法突破。
  • 确定两个真正的提速杠杆：用负面结果图（negative-results map）系统排除四个候选干预后，证明只有两个杠杆能移动吞吐——(1) 足够细（Nc=64）的列面板多线程调度，填满 M1 每簇的共享 AMX 块；(2) 权重预打包。明确指出 Accelerate 在 LLM prefill 形状（M=128）下把第二个 AMX 块闲置（经 per-core 占用率探针直接测得 E-簇占用为 0.00）。
  • bit-exact 直接 AMX 核函数：在 M1 上构造的 fp32 核函数是已知最快的 bit-exact fp32 GEMM 路径，输出与 Accelerate 完全逐位一致（max-abs-diff = 0）；而 BNNS Graph 仅在 3/12 个形状上 bit-exact，其余以降低精度运行（误差最高 1.4e-3）。
  • 端到端验证而非仅 GEMM 比值：把核函数接入 llama.cpp（替换 cblas_sgemm 的 prefill 权重 matmul），实测 128-token prefill 下全前向吞吐从 291 提升到 420 tokens/s（1.44 倍，bit-identical），证明这是部署级增益。
  • 可复现的开源实现：代码、基准测试、文献图、调查日志与复现命令均开源（github.com/dbhan08/inferc），并报告单个 (Nc, Kc) 参数对适用全部 12 个形状。

Key Contributions

  • A microarchitectural characterization of the M1 AMX inner loop. Isolated cache-resident microbenchmarks show it is load-issue bound: any operand load interleaved with the FMA32 stream collapses single-thread throughput to a 610-680 GFLOPS band, regardless of tile shape (16x64 vs 32x32), paired vs single loads, the load-to-FMA dependency, or phase batching — none of the rearrangements tested escape it. The load-free ceiling is ~1525 GFLOPS.
  • A measured, shape-resolved attribution of Accelerate's headroom. A per-core occupancy probe (host_processor_info, baseline-subtracted) shows that both callable Accelerate routines (cblas_sgemm, BNNSMatMul) hold the M1's E-cluster AMX block at 0.00 occupancy at LLM prefill shapes, while the proposed kernel at Nc=64 drives ~3.0-3.5 P-core-equivalents and ~1.9-2.5 on the E-cluster — directly confirming the "idle second block" mechanism rather than merely inferring it from the speedup.
  • A bit-exact direct-AMX kernel that is the fastest bit-exact fp32 GEMM path on the M1. It exceeds all three Accelerate fp32 paths (cblas_sgemm, BNNSMatMul, BNNS Graph) at all twelve prefill GEMMs at S=128 (geomean 1.58x over BNNSMatMul, ~2.0x over cblas_sgemm, 1.17x over BNNS Graph), with every output bit-identical to Accelerate (max-abs-diff = 0), whereas BNNS Graph is bit-exact at only 3/12 shapes.
  • A negative-results map bounding the design space. Four interventions — operand streaming (unpacked B), microkernel restructuring, explicit thread-count pinning, and 2-D output tiling over M — are evaluated and rejected, leaving fine-panel scheduling and weight pre-packing as the only two levers above an inner loop at the hardware limit.
  • End-to-end deployment validation in llama.cpp. Dropping the kernel into llama.cpp's fp32 BLAS prefill path raises measured full-forward throughput from 291 to 420 tokens/s (1.44x, bit-identical) at 128-token prefill — an end-to-end wall-clock gain, not a GEMM-only ratio.

Methodology

The paper builds a direct-AMX (not Accelerate-dispatched) fp32 GEMM kernel using reverse-engineered AMX encodings (corsix/amx, dougallj), organized around a three-level Goto-BLIS cache-blocking scheme:

  • Outer loops over B panels (L2-resident, jc) and A panels (L1-resident, pc), matching the M1 P-core cache geometry (L1 data cache sized exactly 128 KB = 2048x16x4).
  • AMX microkernel: reads paired X registers of B (LDX_pair) and one Y register of A, issues four FMA32 across the four Z banks (four-way ILP), with correctness-critical Z-carry via LDZ/STZ across pc iterations to avoid silent numerical drift.
  • Weight pre-packing (Section 3.2): the constant operand B is packed once at model load into [K, Nc] panels and reused for the process lifetime, amortizing the ~480 MB pack at the LM-head shape to zero. This is standard in production libraries (XNNPACK, oneDNN, BNNS Graph) but unavailable via the callable Accelerate entry points.
  • Panel sizing (Section 3.3): an offline sweep over Nc in {64,128,192,256,384,512} and Kc in {256,512,1024,2048}, rejecting non-bit-exact selections, settles on a single deployed pair (Nc=64, Kc=2048) that wins all twelve shapes — not tuned against any single comparison.

Measurement discipline (Section 4.1): Apple M1 (T8103, 4 P-cores @ 3.228 GHz + 4 E-cores), macOS Tahoe 26.5.1, Apple clang 21.0.0 -O3. Each configuration is the median over eleven isolated binary invocations, with fifteen warmed-up trials within each invocation. Correctness is verified against Accelerate sgemm by sampling the full output at large coprime strides (every 997th/1023rd element). Ratios are formed within a single process per invocation so thermal excursions cancel.

Characterization probes: (i) the load-issue microbenchmark (Table 2); (ii) the two-block aggregate across thread counts (Table 5), separating pure-FMA-MT from loaded-MT to make the load-issue bound visible across threads; (iii) the per-core occupancy probe (Section 4.6) that directly measures the idle second AMX block; (iv) the panel-width sweep (Fig. 2) showing throughput crossing both baselines as Nc shrinks from 512 to 64.

Results & Highlights

  • Per-shape throughput (Table 3): the kernel sustains a nearly shape-independent 1125-1252 GFLOPS across all twelve shapes. Its margin over BNNSMatMul is essentially the inverse of BNNSMatMul's own throughput, ranging from 1.44x (LM head, N≫K) to 1.82x (FFN-up at GPT-2-style scale). Geomean 1.58x over BNNSMatMul, 2.04x over cblas_sgemm.
  • vs BNNS Graph (Table 4): faster at all twelve (geomean 1.17x, range 1.09-1.41) and bit-exact where BNNS Graph often is not. BNNS Graph matches cblas only at the three square QKV shapes; at every rectangular shape it differs by 2.4e-4 to 1.4e-3 (computes at reduced precision despite fp32 storage). The cleanest cases are the QKV shapes: a bit-exact-vs-bit-exact 1.09x where the two backends' per-invocation distributions do not overlap across all eleven runs.
  • Two levers are not independent (Section 4.4): at the K≥N shapes the kernel beats BNNSMatMul even when re-packing the weight on every call (on panel granularity alone). Pre-packing converts the N>K shapes to wins and unlocks the deeper Kc=2048 that halves Z-accumulator reload passes — a depth the per-call kernel cannot afford.
  • Multi-threading is the lever, the inner loop is not (Section 4.6): the pure-FMA aggregate barely rises with thread count (1528 → 1581 GFLOPS, +3%) because one thread already saturates the issue port; the loaded aggregate rises 2.2-fold (673 → 1483 GFLOPS) because each thread's loads slot into the issue gaps the others' FMA32 leave. Shrinking Nc from 512 (4 panels, P-cluster only, ~630 GFLOPS) to 64 (32 panels, both blocks) lifts the same QKV shape to ~1200 GFLOPS — an 85-102% gain.
  • Negative results (Table 1): LDX_pair encoding, software PRFM prefetch, explicit thread pinning, and 2-D output tiling over M all fail to help (or hurt). Operand streaming of unpacked B falls to 0.15-0.5x of the packed kernel.
  • End-to-end prefill GEMM (Table 6): 1.42x over BNNSMatMul (TinyLlama-1.1B), 1.50x (Llama-7B); 1.80x and 2.67x over cblas_sgemm. These wall-clock figures sit just below a FLOP-weighted composition of per-shape ratios because the activation transpose (the cost of row-major pre-packed layout) is paid inside every call.
  • llama.cpp full-forward (Section 4.7): 291 → 420 tokens/s (1.44x, bit-identical) at 128-token prefill on TinyLlama-1.1B fp32. The in-engine kernel costs ~57 ms more than in isolation due to GCD thread contention and per-call activation transpose. The gain narrows to 1.10x at 256 tokens and reverses to 0.82x at 512.

Authors & Credibility

  • Single author: Deyvik, Georgia Institute of Technology (dbhan6@gatech.edu). The depth of microarchitectural rigor (load-issue microbenchmarks, Z-carry correctness analysis, per-core occupancy probes, exhaustive negative-results reporting) is unusually thorough and self-critical for a single-author work.
  • The paper is explicitly positioned as the bit-exact M1-AMX counterpart to MpGEMM's M4 SME result (Deng et al., arXiv:2512.21473), which won inside the inner loop via SME's multi-vector loads; the contribution here is that AMX lacks those, so the gain must come from above the inner loop. This is a coherent and honestly-framed research narrative.
  • Engineering credibility is high: code, benchmarks, literature map, investigation log, and reproduction commands are released (github.com/dbhan08/inferc); vendored AMX encodings match corsix HEAD and were verified against the corsix emulator. The author is candid that the advantage is a deployment-level property (requires pre-packed weights reused across calls), not a faster single isolated GEMM.

Critical Assessment

This is a high-quality, narrowly-scoped systems performance paper that succeeds precisely because it resists overclaiming. Its strongest virtue is intellectual honesty: rather than presenting a "1.58x faster GEMM," it decomposes the gain and admits the inner loop is at the hardware limit, so the kernel is not algorithmically clever — it is deployment-aware. The negative-results map and the direct per-core occupancy measurement (rather than inferring the idle block from the speedup) are exactly the kind of rigor that distinguishes measurement from marketing.

The central structural claim — that the load-issue bound holds across M1-M3 and that the two-block aggregate depends on the specific 4P+4E single-cluster topology — is clearly delineated, and the author explicitly flags which parts generalize and which do not. The bit-exactness argument is a genuine differentiator: BNNS Graph's speed at rectangular shapes is partly bought with precision (errors up to 1.4e-3), whereas this kernel never trades accuracy for speed.

The main weakness is scope and practical ceiling. The result is confined to: fp32 (not the quantized/FP16 paths that dominate real LLM serving), prefill at S=128 (the gain narrows at 256 and reverses at 512; decode is memory-bound and out of scope), and a single M1 unit (the author does not control for silicon variation, though this is largely cancelled in within-process ratios). Critically, BNNS Graph with its default reduced precision actually runs faster at some shapes — the unaddressed FP16/int8 headroom is the elephant in the room, and the author acknowledges the M1 AMX fp16 path has roughly twice the fp32 limit but is left to follow-up. A reader deploying this in production must also absorb the integration cost: the pre-pack is a one-time model-load cost and the per-call activation transpose is paid on every forward, which is why the in-engine llama.cpp kernel runs slower than in isolation. For a single isolated GEMM call, the kernel is no faster than BNNSMatMul — the entire premise depends on a serving-session deployment that reuses packed weights, which somewhat undercuts the "faster than Accelerate" framing for general-purpose use.

The single-author provenance and the absence of peer review at this stage mean the numbers should be reproduced independently before deployment-critical use, though the released reproduction harness makes that straightforward.

Strengths

  • Rigorous, conservative measurement: median over eleven isolated invocations, bit-exactness verified via coprime-stride output sampling, within-process ratio formation to cancel thermals.
  • Structural rather than tuned conclusion: identifies the load-issue bound and the two-block topology as the root causes, with a single (Nc, Kc) pair serving all twelve shapes rather than per-shape tuning.
  • Direct causal measurement of the idle second AMX block (per-core occupancy probe) instead of inferring it from speedups.
  • Bit-exact by construction, never trading accuracy for speed — a clean win over BNNS Graph's reduced-precision rectangular shapes.
  • Comprehensive negative-results reporting (Table 1) that genuinely bounds the design space rather than cherry-picking wins.
  • End-to-end validation in llama.cpp (not just synthetic GEMM ratios) and full open-source release with reproduction commands.
  • Honest limitations and future-work sections that explicitly scope what generalizes (load-issue bound across M1-M3) vs. what does not (two-block aggregate tied to single-cluster topology).

Limitations

  • fp32-only: BNNS Graph runs faster when allowed its default reduced precision; the M1 AMX fp16 path (~2x the fp32 limit) is acknowledged but unaddressed. The int8 path is not a lever (vecint runs at ~5 cycles/instruction).
  • Prefill-only (S=128), compute-bound: not decode (M=1, memory-bandwidth-bound). The gain narrows to 1.10x at 256 tokens and reverses to 0.82x at 512 as the per-call activation transpose grows with M and Accelerate's large-M scaling overtakes.
  • Single M1 unit (T8103): no silicon-to-silicon variation control (partly mitigated by within-process ratios). The load-issue bound is expected to generalize to M2/M3 (shared AMX microarchitecture), but the two-block aggregate and Nc=64 panel width depend on the 4P+4E single-P-cluster organization — which base M2/M3 share but M2/M3 Pro/Max/Ultra and the SME-based M4+ do not.
  • Requires pre-packed-weight deployment: for a single isolated GEMM the kernel is no faster than BNNSMatMul; the speedup is realized only when packed weights are reused across calls (a serving session).
  • llama.cpp speedup confined to the fp32 BLAS path: the quantized ggml path (fused dequantization), the per-call activation transpose hoist, and extension past the short-prefill regime are deferred to follow-up.
  • No direct characterization of other parts (M2, M3, M4); aggregates and panel granularity on multi-cluster parts would differ.
  • Deferred: an FP16 kernel on the same hardware (whether FMA16's 4x multiply-accumulates per load escape the bound), an SME counterpart on M4+ with MpGEMM as baseline, and full serving-stack integration.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does an FP16-input/FP32-accumulate kernel on the M1 AMX escape the load-issue bound (since FMA16 performs 4x the multiply-accumulates per operand load)? What is the accuracy map of where reduced-precision input is safe across the twelve prefill shapes?
  2. Does the structural account transfer to SME on the M4+, where multi-vector loads (up to 900 GB/s) may relieve the very bottleneck identified here — i.e., does MpGEMM's inner-loop win coexist with or subsume the above-the-inner-loop levers?
  3. On multi-cluster parts (M2/M3 Pro/Max/Ultra), what is the AMX-block aggregate ceiling and the panel granularity that reaches it, given their different P/E cluster topology?
  4. Can a serving-stack integration (e.g., the cblas_sgemm path of llama.cpp) hoist the per-call activation transpose, fuse the quantized ggml dequant path, and extend the gain past S=128 to recover the 1.80x GEMM-only ratio end-to-end (vs. the measured 1.44x)?
  5. Given that BNNS Graph buys speed with precision (errors 2.4e-4 to 1.4e-3 at rectangular shapes), where exactly is the Pareto frontier between bit-exactness and throughput for LLM prefill — and can a selectively-mixed-precision kernel dominate both this kernel and BNNS Graph?
  6. The paper shows mis-tuning the single column-panel width costs nearly 2x (Nc=512 vs 64 at QKV). Is there a principled, shape-adaptive Nc selection rule that generalizes, or must it remain an offline sweep per hardware part?
