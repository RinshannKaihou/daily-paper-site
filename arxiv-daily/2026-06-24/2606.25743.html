Black-Box Assisted Regression: Phase Transitions and Minimax Optimality

arXiv: 2606.25743

Date: 2026-06-24

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25743

一句话总结： 本文将"基础模型当作黑箱先验"形式化为非参数回归的有限样本极小化问题，证明了在临界半径 δc(n) ≍ n^(−β/(2β+d)) 处存在相变，并提出一种零初始化+留出选择（holdout）的 Safe Residual Estimator，在匹配极小化主项的同时给出"无负迁移"的 oracle 不等式。
TL;DR: Treating a foundation model as a fixed black-box predictor, this paper gives a finite-sample minimax characterization of black-box assisted nonparametric regression with a phase transition at δc(n) ≍ n^(−β/(2β+d)), and proposes a zero-initialized, holdout-selected "Safe Residual Estimator" that matches the leading minimax risk while guaranteeing no negative transfer relative to the black box.

核心贡献

  • 相变极小化刻画：在已知黑箱先验 f0 与目标 f* 之间的 L2 误差半径 δ 未知的前提下，证明主导风险为 min{δ², n^(−2β/(2β+d))}，临界阈值 δc(n) ≍ n^(−β/(2β+d))，把"先验主导 vs. 样本主导"两类机制严格分开。
  • Safe Residual Estimator 算法：学习 f0 周围的残差修正 r̂，残差头零初始化使初始预测恰为 f0，再用留出验证集在 {f0, f0 + r̂} 间做二选一；当 r̂ 在验证集上不优于黑箱时回退到 f0，从而避免负迁移。
  • 可证明的"无负迁移"保证：给出 Theorem 5.7 的 oracle 不等式——所选预测器最多比 {f0, f0 + r̂} 中更优者差一个加性的 O(1/nval) 验证代价；并证明该估计量在常数因子 + 加性验证代价下达到极小化最优（Cor. 5.6）。
  • 残差化优于线性集成的几何论证：指出加权集成 αf0 + (1−α) f̂_scratch 被限制在一条仿射线上，对落在其正交补中的高频残差盲视；而残差估计器搜索的是以 f0 为中心的球，可实现局部再中心化。
  • 合成实验 + 实际任务双重验证：1D/20D 合成回归数值复现了预测的相变与回退机制；CIFAR-100 (CLIP) 与 AG News (Qwen3-8B) 在极少样本下超越加权集成与拼接特征基线，提供"理论机制在分类管线中同样成立"的实践性证据。

Key Contributions

  • Minimax phase-transition characterization. Under unknown prior-error radius δ = ‖f* − f0‖_{L2(PX)}, the leading minimax risk is min{δ², n^(−2β/(2β+d))} with critical radius δc(n) ≍ n^(−β/(2β+d)), cleanly separating the prior-dominated and sample-dominated regimes.
  • Safe Residual Estimator algorithm. Learns a correction r̂ around f0, zero-initializes the residual head so the initial predictor equals f0, and performs binary holdout selection between f0 and f0 + r̂; it reverts to f0 when the correction is unsupported by validation data.
  • Provable no-negative-transfer guarantee. Theorem 5.7 gives an oracle inequality: the selected predictor is within an additive O(1/nval) validation cost of the better of {f0, f0 + r̂}; Corollary 5.6 shows near-minimax optimality up to constant factors and the validation-selection cost.
  • Geometric argument for residualization over linear ensembling. A weighted ensemble αf0 + (1−α) f̂_scratch is confined to an affine line and is blind to residual components in its orthogonal complement, whereas the residual estimator searches a ball centered at f0 and can recover high-frequency corrections orthogonal to the base predictors.
  • Synthetic + real-world validation. 1D and d=20 synthetic regressions numerically reproduce the predicted phase transition and fallback behavior; CIFAR-100 with CLIP and AG News with Qwen3-8B show the residual-correction mechanism is beneficial beyond the formal squared-loss regression setting, especially in few-shot regimes.

Methodology

  • Problem setup. Random-design nonparametric regression y_i = f(x_i) + ε_i with ε_i ~ N(0, σ²). The learner can query a fixed black-box predictor f0 and observes n labeled samples. The target satisfies f = f0 + r with r ∈ H(β, L) (β-smooth Hölder class) and ‖r‖_{L2(PX)} ≤ δ, ‖r‖∞ ≤ B, ‖f0‖∞ ≤ B0. Crucially, regularity is placed on the residual r*, not on f0 itself, so f0 may be an arbitrary foundation model.
  • Function class. F(δ) := {f0 + r : r ∈ H(β, L), ‖r‖_{L2} ≤ δ, ‖r‖∞ ≤ B}.
  • Select-and-Estimate (Algorithm 1). Split Dn into Dtr and Dval. (1) Train r̂ by regressing (y − f0(x)) on Dtr; for neural heads, zero-initialize so r̂ ≡ 0 initially. (2) Compute validation losses L_BB and L_Res on Dval. (3) Set α̂ = 1[L_Res < L_BB], output f̂_safe(x) = f0(x) + α̂ · r̂(x).
  • Lower bound (Thm 5.4). inf sup E‖f̂ − f*‖² ≥ c · min{δ², n^(−2β/(2β+d))}, proved via a packing argument over the intersection of the smoothness ball and the L2 ball of radius δ.
  • Upper bound (Thm 5.5) and Corollary 5.6. With split ratio ρ, sup E‖f̂_safe − f*‖² ≤ C · min{δ², n^(−2β/(2β+d))} + C′/n, up to an inflation factor (1−ρ)^(−2β/(2β+d)) ≈ 1.16 for ρ = 0.2, β ≈ d.
  • Oracle inequality (Thm 5.7). Conditional on Dtr, with probability ≥ 1 − δ′ over Dval, Err(f̂_safe) ≤ (1+γ)·min{Err(f̂_BB), Err(f̂_Res)} + C·log(2/δ′)/nval.
  • Metric-entropy intuition. When ε > δ the L2 constraint dominates and effective entropy collapses (δ² floor); when ε < δ the smoothness constraint dominates and the n^(−2β/(2β+d)) rate is recovered. The validation step acts as a multi-scale statistical test that pays the full Hölder entropy cost only when evidence is strong enough.

Results & Highlights

  • 1D synthetic (Table 1). At n=20, δ=0.0, raw residual overfits (MSE 0.0335) but Safe Residual falls back 65% of the time to MSE 0.0027. At n=20, δ=1.5, Safe Residual (0.146) beats Scratch (0.189) and Weighted (0.170). At n=20, δ=0.8, Safe Residual (0.097) beats Weighted (0.145), confirming the benefit of non-linear correction.
  • High-dimensional synthetic (Table 2, d=20, n=100). Scratch stays at ~0.489 (curse of dimensionality). At δ=1.0, raw residual explodes (1.026) but Safe Residual detects the failure and triggers 60% fallback, stabilizing risk at 1.007.
  • CIFAR-100 + CLIP (Table 3, Fig. 2). BB zero-shot = 59.39%. At n=100 (1 shot/class), Safe Residual holds 59.82% while Scratch collapses to 2.11% and Concat to 0.39%. At n=2000, Residual reaches 66.7%, beating Weighted (63.9%) by 2.7% and Scratch (57.2%). Lowest Brier score (0.443 vs 0.538 for BB) at n=2000, consistent with the L2 theory.
  • AG News + Qwen3-8B (Table 4). BB = 74.06%. At n=16, Weighted collapses to scratch performance (71.21% < 74.06%, negative transfer) while Residual reaches 75.23%. At n=64, Weighted collapses to 54.7% due to noisy α estimation, Residual stays robust at 74.7%. Fallback rate is 0.0% across all NLP settings, indicating zero-initialization provides implicit safety without triggering the hard fallback.
  • Ablations. Method is stable for validation fraction ρ ∈ [0.15, 0.3]; zero-initialization significantly reduces early-training variance vs random init (Appendix G).

Critical Assessment

The paper is intellectually disciplined in scoping its claims: it explicitly limits the formal minimax guarantees to squared-loss, homoscedastic-Gaussian, random-design regression with compact support and bounded density, and is upfront that the CIFAR-100/AG News experiments only probe the same qualitative mechanism rather than extend the theorems to cross-entropy. The Safe Residual Estimator is, by the author's own admission, deliberately simple — its value is as a clean statistical object for which the minimax tradeoff and no-negative-transfer property can be proved, not as a method meant to beat all transfer-learning heuristics. A few honest cautions:

  • The phase transition δc(n) depends on β and d, which are typically unknown in practice; the theory assumes a fixed β-smooth residual class, so the adaptivity claim is "within-class" rather than fully adaptive over β.
  • The leading-risk statement is a "matches up to constants + additive validation cost" result; the additive O(1/nval) term is lower-order asymptotically but can dominate in the strongly prior-dominated regime — the author flags this but it weakens the finite-sample tightness.
  • The classification experiments use small-n regimes where variance is high; the 2.7% CIFAR gain at n=2000 is meaningful but the n=16–128 NLP results show overlapping std bands in places, so the practical-margin claims are suggestive rather than decisive.
  • Single author, modest experimental scale (CIFAR-100 and AG News only), and the method's headline advantage is concentrated in few-shot/low-sample regimes where alternatives are known to be fragile.

Strengths

  • Clean, sharp minimax result with an explicit, interpretable phase transition tied directly to the prior-vs-sample tradeoff.
  • The no-negative-transfer oracle inequality is a practically meaningful safety property, and the binary holdout selection is justified over continuous weight estimation on scarce validation data.
  • The geometric argument (subspace vs. ball) gives genuine intuition for why residualization can beat linear ensembling on corrections orthogonal to {f0, f̂_scratch}.
  • Zero-initialization is shown to provide an "implicit safety" mechanism (fallback rate 0.0% yet no negative transfer) that complements the explicit holdout switch — a useful, implementable design principle.
  • Honest framing: clear separation between theorem-level regression guarantees and practice-facing classification evidence.

Limitations

  • Theory assumes homoscedastic Gaussian noise, compact support, bounded density, and a static black box; extensions to heavy-tailed noise (e.g., Huber loss), unbounded covariates, online learning, and adaptive (non-static) f0 are explicitly left open.
  • The additive validation-selection cost O(1/nval) can dominate in the strongly prior-dominated regime; removing it may require a Lepski-type method or sharper validation analysis.
  • No minimax guarantees for cross-entropy loss, accuracy-based selection, or multi-class logit regression — the classification mapping is heuristic.
  • Experimental scale is limited (two datasets, modest sample sizes); the advantage is most pronounced in few-shot regimes where baseline variance is high, so generalization to large-n, large-scale foundation models is not demonstrated.
  • Single author with no external validation of the empirical claims beyond the reported 3-run std.

Relevance

这篇论文在"基础模型即先验（foundation model as prior）"这一日益重要的设定下，给出了一个少见的、可证明的统计刻画。它直接关系到以下场景：(1) 我们只能在黑箱接口上调用一个预训练模型、无法访问其参数或源数据的下游适配；(2) 标注样本稀缺但需要避免负迁移的少样本/低资源任务；(3) 模型选择与 prediction-powered inference 的理论交叉。其"零初始化残差头 + 留出二选一"的设计是一条可直接落到工程实践（linear probe、轻量适配、API-only 微调）的原则。对关注迁移学习理论、非参数极小化、以及安全适配（safe adaptation）的研究者具有较高参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the minimax phase transition be extended to classification with cross-entropy loss and accuracy-based selection, closing the gap between the regression theorems and the classification experiments?
  • How does the result change under heteroscedastic or heavy-tailed noise, and can a Lepski-type adaptive procedure remove the additive O(1/nval) validation-selection cost without sample splitting?
  • The discussion hints that the "black-box prior + residual correction" idea may apply to score/denoising-function estimation in diffusion models — can this be formalized?
  • Is the binary {f0, f0 + r̂} selection provably near-optimal compared to a continuous mixing weight when the validation set is large, and where exactly does the threshold lie?
  • Does the residual-regularity assumption (r* ∈ H(β, L)) hold empirically for modern foundation models like CLIP/Qwen on real downstream tasks, or is it merely a convenient theoretical idealization?
