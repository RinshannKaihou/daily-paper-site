Steering Vision-Language Models with Joint Sparse Autoencoders

arXiv: 2606.25657

Date: 2026-06-24

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25657

一句话总结： 提出Joint Sparse Autoencoder (JSAE)，通过对视觉和文本两个模态分别的稀疏编码施加余弦对齐约束，将均值池化后的图像/字幕级激活分解到共享可解释隐空间；在LLaVA/Qwen3-VL/Llama3-LLaVA上做双向干预，发现"加法steering"在中间偏后(pre-output)层最强、输出层附近失效，而"抑制"在所有层都有效——呈现一种层依赖的非对称性。
TL;DR: The authors introduce the Joint Sparse Autoencoder (JSAE), which trains two modality-specific SAEs on mean-pooled vision/text activations of a VLM and binds their sparse codes with a paired-code cosine-alignment loss, yielding interpretable cross-modal features. On LLaVA-1.6-7B they show that text-decoder directions can be additively injected onto image tokens to steer generation, with success peaking at Layer 25 (79.8%) and collapsing at Layer 30 (15.2%), while feature suppression stays effective across all layers; the pattern partially transfers to Qwen3-VL-30B (MoE) and Llama3-LLaVA-8B. JSAE decisively beats unaligned SAEs (0-4% success), prompt steering (22%), ActAdd (39%), and random noise (3%), showing the alignment loss is doing the real work.

核心贡献

  • 提出JSAE框架：使用模态独立的encoder-decoder对加上paired-code余弦对齐损失（而非像VL-SAE那样共享单个字典），把均值池化后的图像/文本激活分解到共享的过完备稀疏隐空间（d=4096, k=16384，4×过完备），得到可作为跨模态steering方向的可解释特征。
  • 给出几何层面的设计rationale：非负稀疏编码下，cosine对齐项会促使两个模态的decoder列方向被绑定，从而文本侧的方向天然适合作为视觉侧的causal steering向量——这是本文相对VL-SAE/GroupSparse SAE最关键的差异点。
  • 双向干预 + 层级定位pipeline：linear probing定位对齐最可分的层，再用K-Means + Silhouette对原子特征做聚类筛选（Algorithm 1），最后做additive steering与suppression两类因果干预来检验特征的功能作用。
  • 发现层依赖的非对称性：加法steering在mid-to-late层最强（L25 Succ 0.798，Bonferroni-corrected p≤3.1e-3），在输出层附近急剧衰减（L30 0.152）；而抑制分数在所有探测层都保持相近（Welch检验全部不显著）。这是一个新的、可复现的可解释性现象。
  • 跨架构泛化：在密集型LLaVA-1.6-7B、MoE型Qwen3-VL-30B（L33, Succ 77.9%）、Llama3-LLaVA-8B（L15, Succ 66.7%）上都观察到层定位一致的steering效应，说明稀疏对齐特征在不同VLM结构中普遍存在。

Key Contributions

  • Joint Sparse Autoencoder (JSAE): Two modality-specific encoder–decoder pairs on mean-pooled vision/text activations, bound by a paired-code cosine-similarity alignment loss L_align (so each modality keeps its own dictionary directions while being directionally tied to the other) — contrasted against VL-SAE's shared-dictionary / co-activation approach.
  • Composite training objective: L_total = λr·L_recon + λs·L_sparse + λa·L_align with λr=1.0, λs=0.03, λa=1.0, converging to MSE<5e-4, L0 sparsity ≲4% (~100–700 active of 16,384), alignment loss <1e-4.
  • Layer-localized intervention pipeline: linear probing to find the most alignment-separable layers → activation-profile K-Means clustering with Silhouette ranking to aggregate feature-split atoms into macro-concepts → bidirectional causal interventions (additive steering on image-token positions; feature suppression).
  • Geometric design rationale (informal, then empirically tested): for non-negative sparse codes the alignment penalty biases encoders toward selecting the same latent subset across modalities, and text-decoder columns should be usable steering vectors at mid-to-late layers but weaken near the unembedding — predictions borne out by the experiments.
  • Discovery of an asymmetric layer-dependent dynamics: additive steering effectiveness is sharply layer-localized (peaks mid-to-late, collapses near output), whereas suppression is layer-invariant — a novel, statistically supported interpretability finding.

Methodology

Architecture. For each VLM layer, mean-pool image-patch tokens and text tokens separately into A_v, A_t ∈ R^d to get macro-level (image/caption-level) representations. Encode each independently (z_v = ReLU(E_v(A_v)), z_t = ReLU(E_t(A_t))) into a 4× overcomplete sparse latent space (k=16384), reconstruct via modality-specific decoders, and optimize the composite loss above. The cosine-alignment term L_align = (1/B)·Σ(1 − cos(z_v^(b), z_t^(b))) over the paired mini-batch encourages the same active latent subset across modalities for semantically related image–caption pairs.

Feature discovery (Algorithm 1). Filter inactive neurons by activation variance (σ²>1e-5), build an L2-normalized per-neuron activation profile across the validation set, cluster neurons with K-Means, rank clusters by mean Silhouette score, then qualitatively inspect to discard polysemantic clusters and keep the top-K as alignment features.

Layer selection. Linear probes trained on mean-pooled d=4096 hidden states predict text–image matching on matched / easy-mismatched / hard-mismatched COCO triples; alignment becomes most linearly separable between Layer 13 and Layer 25, motivating the chosen layers.

Causal interventions. Additive steering: take the text-decoder column of a target concept and additively inject it onto image-token positions during the forward pass; suppression: reduce the feature's activation. Direct-replacement reconstruction (overwrite all vision tokens with the broadcast JSAE reconstruction) is used as a worst-case fidelity test.

Evaluation. Three independent metrics: Similarity Gain Δsim (SBERT cosine shift toward target description, automated/judge-free), Accuracy & Coherence (1–10 scored by claude-sonnet-4), Fluency (PPL ratio PPL_steer/PPL_base). Success = Δsim>0.2. Significance via Welch's t-tests on Δsim with Bonferroni correction; judge-free vs LLM-judge agreement checked with Spearman/Pearson (ρ=+0.68, r=+0.63).

Models/hardware. LLaVA-v1.6-Mistral-7B, Qwen3-VL-30B-Instruct (MoE), Llama3-LLaVA-Next-8B; NVIDIA A100 80GB; ~6 A100-hours per SAE, ~100 A100-hours total.

Results & Highlights

  • Layer-wise additive steering (LLaVA-7B): Success Ratio rises 0.400 (L7) → 0.740 (L13) → 0.798 (L25) → 0.152 (L30). Layer 25 significantly beats the others (p_bonf ≤ 3.1e-3, Cohen's d up to +1.69 vs L30). Judge-based Accuracy induces the identical ranking (Spearman ρ=1.0 with Δsim).
  • Suppression is layer-invariant: scores stay ~5.0–5.8 across all layers; all six pairwise Welch tests non-significant (min p_bonf=0.30) — the asymmetry is real, not noise.
  • Baseline comparison (Layer 13): JSAE 74.0% success vs VL-SAE 0%, JSAE w/o alignment (λa=0) 4.1%, Prompt Steering 22.1%, ActAdd 39.4%, Random Noise 2.9%. The unaligned failures are direction-driven (norm rescaling doesn't help: Acc 1.15 vs 1.14), confirming the alignment loss, not dictionary scale, is responsible.
  • Cross-architecture: Qwen3-VL-30B (MoE) Layer 33 → 77.9% Succ / Acc 7.02; Llama3-LLaVA-8B Layer 15 → 66.7% Succ / Coherence 6.78 — sparse alignment features arise in both dense and MoE VLMs.
  • Reconstruction fidelity: under the stringent direct-replacement protocol, CE-loss recovery rises monotonically 36.8%→100.2% from L7 to L30, with JSAE matching or beating a raw-mean broadcast control at every layer (no reconstruction overhead, even mild denoising).
  • Out-of-distribution transfer: JSAE L13 steering stays effective on 1,500 Flickr30k images (3 scene categories) with category-specific degradation (Appendix Table 3).
  • Qualitative cases: e.g., amplifying "baseball" neurons on a tennis image (L25) makes the caption describe the racket as a bat and the court as a field, while preserving the yellow-shirted subject.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (medium-high): The core novelty is narrow but genuine — explicitly paired-code directional alignment between two modality-specific SAEs (vs VL-SAE's shared dictionary / co-activation statistics). The layer-dependent steering asymmetry (additive peaks mid-late then collapses at output; suppression flat across layers) is a genuinely new empirical finding, not just a re-reporting of known SAE results.
  • Rigor (high): Strong experimental hygiene. Multiple orthogonal metrics (SBERT cosine, LLM judge, PPL ratio), pre-registered success threshold (Δsim>0.2) defined independently of the judge, Bonferroni-corrected Welch tests with effect sizes, judge-free vs judge agreement reported (ρ=0.68), and direct ablation showing the alignment loss is necessary (λa=0 → 4.1%). The direct-replacement reconstruction control (L_recon ≤ L_mean at every layer) cleanly rules out "SAE just destroyed information."
  • Reproducibility (medium-high): Datasets (MS-COCO splits, COCO-Probe with hard negatives via BERT cosine), hyperparameters (d=4096, k=16384, λs=0.03, λa=1.0, Adam lr=1e-5, 20 epochs), layer choices, sample counts (28–40 held-out COCO images), and evaluation prompts (exact system prompts in Appendix A) are all disclosed. Code/data release not confirmed in the extracted text — a gap. Compute (~100 A100-hrs) is modest and tractable.
  • Significance (medium): A solid, well-executed mechanistic-interpretability contribution. The steering magnitudes (Δsim ~0.13–0.34, "success" being Δsim>0.2) are modest by application standards, and the work is analysis-oriented rather than capability-improving; impact will depend on whether the layer-asymmetry finding generalizes and proves useful for safety/control. Transfer to MoE (Qwen3-VL-30B) is a nice breadth signal but each extra architecture is tested at only one layer.
  • Red flags (low, but worth noting):
  1. LLM-as-judge dependency: Accuracy/Coherence come from claude-sonnet-4; the authors mitigate by cross-checking against judge-free Δsim and PPL, but the headline Accuracy numbers still rest partly on an LLM judge.
  2. Single-layer per extra architecture: Qwen3-VL and Llama3-LLaVA are only tested at their probe-optimal layer (explicitly acknowledged: "not the per-architecture optimum"), so the cross-architecture claim is existence-level, not comprehensive.
  3. Macro-feature scope: JSAE operates on mean-pooled activations, so its "features" are image/caption-level macro-concepts, not token-level monosemantic units — the authors flag this trade-off but it limits the granularity of interpretation.
  4. Output-boundary attenuation is partly post-hoc: the geometric explanation for why L30 fails is offered as interpretation after the fact; it is plausible and consistent with Venhoff et al. but not independently proven.
  5. Modest absolute effect sizes; "success" defined at a Δsim threshold that, while pre-registered, is somewhat lenient.

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated distinction from VL-SAE / GroupSparse SAE (modality-specific dictionaries + paired-code directional binding).
  • Honest, clearly-scoped claims ("under our protocol," "the alternatives tested here") — avoids overgeneralization.
  • Multi-metric, statistically-tested evaluation with judge-free corroboration.
  • Direct ablation proving the alignment loss is necessary (not just dictionary scale).
  • Demonstrates the finding across dense and MoE VLMs, including a 30B model.
  • Useful worst-case reconstruction protocol (direct replacement) with a raw-mean control.

Limitations

  • Macro-concept granularity: mean-pooling discards token-level / spatial structure; features are caption-level, not patch- or token-level monosemantic units.
  • LLM-judge dependency for Accuracy/Coherence scores.
  • Thin cross-architecture coverage: one layer each for Qwen3-VL-30B and Llama3-LLaVA-8B; no per-architecture layer sweep (compute-limited).
  • Small per-category sample sizes (28–40 held-out images); some categories (e.g., "streets" on Llama3-8B) fail with low success, hinting at concept-specific fragility.
  • Modest effect magnitudes and a lenient (Δsim>0.2) success threshold.
  • No manifold-preserving / norm-controlled intervention at the output boundary; the L30 failure mechanism is explained post-hoc, not fixed.
  • Code/data release status not confirmed in the text.
  • Only positive-pair training data; negative-pair structure (COCO-Probe) is used for probing, not for training the alignment.

Relevance

对于关注大模型可解释性、机理解释（mechanistic interpretability）与可控生成的读者，本文提供了把SAE迁移到视觉语言模型、并用对齐约束得到跨模态可干预方向的一个扎实范例；其"steering在中间偏后层最强、输出层附近失效"的层级非对称性发现，对多模态模型的安全/控制与activation engineering研究有直接借鉴价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the layer-asymmetry (additive peaks mid-late / suppression flat) hold for non-LLaVA architectures at a full layer sweep rather than a single probe-optimal layer? Is it a property of attention-based VLM alignment in general?
  2. Can JSAE be extended to token-level / patch-level activations (not mean-pooled) to recover spatially-resolved, monosemantic features instead of caption-level macro-concepts?
  3. Would a manifold-preserving or norm-controlled intervention at Layer 30 recover steerability near the output boundary, or is the unembedding bottleneck a hard limit?
  4. How do the learned alignment features relate to CLIP-style contrastive directions — are they recovering a similar geometry, or a distinct, generation-relevant subspace?
  5. Are the same cross-modal directions causally responsible for specific behaviors (circuit-level analysis), or merely correlated steering shortcuts? Can the features be validated via targeted ablation in downstream tasks (VQA, hallucination benchmarks)?
  6. Does the approach scale to even larger / more recent VLMs (e.g., 70B+ dense or MoE), and does the optimal steering layer shift predictably with depth?
