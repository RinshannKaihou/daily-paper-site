Uncertainty Quantification for Computer-Use Agents: A Benchmark across Vision-Language Models and GUI Grounding Datasets

arXiv: 2606.25760

Date: 2026-06-24

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25760

一句话总结： 论文提出 ARGUS——首个面向计算机操作智能体（GUI 点击定位）的跨场景事后不确定性量化（UQ）基准，覆盖 27 种开权重方法与 8 种闭源 API 方法，系统揭示了 UQ 排名"在固定模型下跨数据集稳定、但跨模型/接口失效"的选择性迁移规律，并提供分保形点击区域的部署评估。
TL;DR: ARGUS is the first cross-regime benchmark for post-hoc uncertainty quantification (UQ) in single-step executable GUI grounding — a 27-method open-weight matrix over 4 VLM agents and 4 datasets, plus an 8-method closed-source matrix over 3 frontier vendors. Its central finding is selective transfer: UQ rankings are stable across datasets for a fixed model (Spearman ρ up to 0.969) but degrade across model classes and observable interfaces, so closed-source UQ must be re-ranked on the target rather than extrapolated.

核心贡献

  • 首个跨场景 GUI-UQ 基准（ARGUS）：同时覆盖开权重与闭源 API 接口，对单步可执行 GUI 点击定位做系统的事后不确定性量化评估；包含 4 个 VLM 智能体 × 4 个数据集 = 16 个开权重 cell，外加 3 个前沿厂商 × 4 个数据集 = 12 个闭源 cell。
  • 27 方法 × 7 大 UQ 家族面板：logit 置信度、采样一致性（语义熵、自一致性）、混合（CoCoA/CoCoA-1MCA、CCP）、隐状态/密度估计（Mahalanobis、SAPLMA、SEP、RAUQ）、注意力追踪（Focus）、口语化置信度（P(True)、Verbalised-1S/2S）、VLM 原生方法，外加 split-conformal 预测。
  • 排名迁移性分析（核心科学问题）：用 Spearman ρ 量化 UQ 方法排名在何时泛化、何时崩溃——固定模型跨数据集最强（均值 ρ=0.705，最大 ρ=0.969），跨模型类与可观测接口显著下降，开→闭跨层迁移仅 ρ=+0.08（95% CI 含零）。
  • 协调化跨层协议：闭源 API 因无法获取 logits/隐状态/注意力，采用 8 方法交集而非代理替换，保证公式跨面板一致、可公平比较。
  • 部署级评估与开源释放：除错误判别 AUROC 外，还给出选择性执行（PRR/AURC）、校准（ECE/Brier）、分级缺失严重度（AUSE）以及分保形点击圆盘（Disk-Fixed/Normalized/CQR）的半径与覆盖率，并发布 argus-uq Python 包（load/score/conformal_disk）、逐项记录、校准/测试切分、UQ 分数与分析脚本。

Key Contributions

  1. First cross-regime UQ benchmark for executable GUI grounding covering both open-weight internals (logits, hidden states, attention, samples, perturbations) and API-only closed-source interfaces, under matched prompting and decoding.
  2. A 27-method / 7-family open-weight matrix (16 cells) and an 8-method harmonised closed-source panel (12 cells) across Qwen2.5-VL-7B, Qwen2.5-VL-72B-AWQ, UI-TARS-1.5-7B, POINTS-GUI-G-8B, GPT-5.4, Claude Sonnet 4.6, and Gemini 3.1 Pro.
  3. Ranking-transfer analysis that re-frames the question from "which UQ score is best?" to "does UQ choice generalize across agents, datasets, and interfaces?" using Spearman ρ over 120 open-weight + 12 cross-tier pairs.
  4. A harmonised cross-tier protocol that drops internal-signal methods rather than substituting proxies, enabling fair open ↔ closed comparison on a shared 8-method intersection.
  5. Regime-dependent reliability evidence + deployment evaluation, showing that error discrimination (AUROC), graded severity (AUSE), calibration (ECE/Brier), and spatial coverage (conformal disks) are separate objectives that need not select the same method — and a regime-aware panel-selection recipe (Table 5).

Methodology

Regime definition. A "regime" is a (dataset, model class, observable interface) tuple. The benchmark is built around UQ generalization rather than absolute model accuracy: holding the model fixed while changing the dataset tests dataset/geometry shift; holding the dataset fixed while changing the model tests architecture/fine-tuning/scale/quantization; moving open-weight → API-only tests loss of internal observability.

Datasets (single-shot greedy, point-in-bbox accuracy): ScreenSpot-V2, ScreenSpot-Pro, OSWorld-G, UI-Vision-EG.

Models. Open-weight: Qwen2.5-VL-7B (Q7), Qwen2.5-VL-72B-AWQ (Q72), UI-TARS-1.5-7B (UI), POINTS-GUI-G-8B (PT). Closed-source: GPT-5.4, Claude Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro.

Per-item protocol. One greedy click, 5 stochastic samples, 3 HEDGE paraphrases, 3 IMGHEDGE perturbations, 50-px coordinate-clustering tolerance, full-resolution images.

UQ method inclusion criteria. Reported in ≥2 peer-reviewed text/VLM-UQ evaluations or directly motivated by GUI-grounding UQ; admits a faithful adaptation to coordinate-valued clicks; computable in ≤30 min/cell. Implementations traceable to canonical references and cross-checked against LM-Polygraph.

Metrics. AUROC_incorrect, normalized PRR_0.5, AURC, isotonic ECE/Brier, miss-only AUSE on log(1+d_norm) (severity normalized by target-box scale), ranking-transfer Spearman ρ, and conformal click-disk coverage/radius at α=0.10 (Fixed, Normalized, CQR variants).

Leakage control. Learned probes, density estimators, isotonic maps, conformal thresholds, and panel selection use only calibration splits; headline metrics use held-out test records. 80/20 test/cal split repeated over 50 seeds (cross-ratio ablation gives mean ρ=0.98 open / 0.92 closed). All metrics carry 95% CI from 500-resample stratified bootstrap; significance via paired bootstrap / Wilcoxon (BH-FDR q<0.05).

Conformal disks. Fixed-radius baseline r̂_α = Quantile_{1−α}({‖c_i − c_i★‖}); locally weighted variants weight residuals by the plug-in UQ score so low-uncertainty predictions get smaller disks.

Results & Highlights

  • Selective transfer is the headline result. UQ rankings are most stable across datasets for a fixed model: mean ρ = 0.705 over 120 open-weight pairs, max ρ = 0.969 (POINTS-GUI-G OSG↔SP). Transfer weakens across model classes (e.g., Q72×OSG↔UI×OSG ρ=0.866) and is weakest on reasoning-heavy UI-Vision-EG.
  • Cross-tier (open → closed) transfer is statistically indistinguishable from zero. On the shared 8-method intersection, mean ρ = +0.08 over 12 vendor×dataset pairs, bootstrap 95% CI [−0.219, +0.373] includes zero; within-closed-source mean ρ = +0.127 over 66 pairs. → Closed-source UQ must be re-ranked on the target calibration split, not extrapolated.
  • No universal winner; winning family is regime-dependent. Density/probe methods (SEP, SAPLMA, Mahal-RMD) are the most stable open-weight family and win OSWorld-G on all 4 models. CoCoA-1MCA and Focus are the strongest non-density alternatives in specific cells. In the API-only matrix, CCP wins 6/12 cells, Verbalised-1S/2S win the other 6 — notably Verbalised-1S reaches AUROC 0.966 on Gemini×ScreenSpot-V2 and 0.880 on Gemini×OSWorld-G.
  • Model-class transitions reshape reliability. Vanilla → specialist grounding agents cause attention (Δ=−0.015), verbalised (−0.037), and VLM-native (−0.029) families to lose AUROC on all four datasets, while density/probe stays nearly flat (+0.008). A controlled ScreenSpot-Pro decomposition shows part of the logit-family loss attributed to fine-tuning is actually confounded by scale/quantization/backbone change (e.g., Q7→Q72 scale-only sampling loss −0.277 vs Q7→UI fine-tune −0.039).
  • Detection ≠ severity ≠ calibration. AUROC and miss-only AUSE agree on the top method in only 2/16 open-weight cells (9/12 API-only). Density scores rank errors well but their heavy tails make ECE_iso exceed 0.40 on low-accuracy OSWorld-G cells; PRR hits a structural ceiling on high-accuracy PT×V2. → "Good uncertainty" has no single scalar; choose UQ for the intervention (reject / rerank / defer).
  • Conformal click-disks: discrimination is not deployment. Disk-Normalized shrinks radii 40–60% (e.g., PT×OSG 743→481 px, −35%) when the plug-in UQ is calibrated, but coverage degrades under calibration-test or interface mismatch — Gemini×ScreenSpot-Pro Disk-Fixed coverage falls to 0.795 at target 0.80. Disk-CQR over-covers by 3–5 pp at α=0.10. Radius tracks benchmark difficulty (ScreenSpot-Pro 833–1547 px vs ScreenSpot-V2 132–269 px).
  • Released argus-uq with all records, splits, scores, and analysis scripts.

Critical Assessment

The paper's framing is its strongest intellectual move: it reframes UQ from "find the best score" to "does UQ choice transfer across regimes?", and answers with an honest negative for the hardest case (open → closed). That honesty is valuable and rare. However, a few points warrant scrutiny:

  • The headline cross-tier null result rests on only 12 vendor×dataset pairs. The authors flag this (CI includes zero), but "statistically indistinguishable from no-transfer" is not the same as "no transfer exists"; with 3 vendors the power to detect a real but small effect is limited. A reader should treat the ρ=+0.08 as "we cannot rule out no-transfer," not "transfer is absent."
  • The closed-source vendors are fictional-naming placeholders (GPT-5.4, Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro). As dated 2026 frontier models, they are reasonable, but the bimodal {0,1} Verbalised score on Gemini×SS-V2 (AUROC 0.966) likely reflects an artifact of how a vendor returns confidence rather than a genuinely calibrated signal — the authors note this (Appendix A17) but it inflates a headline AUROC that casual readers may over-interpret.
  • Single-step only, nsamples=5. The authors concede multi-step trajectories (planning, recovery, credit assignment) are out of scope, and n=5 samples is below the budget needed for reliable SafeGround-style dispersion estimates (relegated to Appendix A23). This limits ecological validity for real computer-use agents that act over trajectories.
  • Benchmark-as-contribution rather than new estimator. The paper deliberately does not propose a new UQ method. That is a legitimate and well-defended choice, but it means the "what should I deploy tomorrow" answer is a recipe (Table 5) rather than a single recommended score — practitioners wanting one number may be disappointed.
  • Calibration-test mismatch undercoverage (Gemini×SS-Pro) is shown but not deeply diagnosed. It is correctly attributed to exchangeability violation across API strata, but no remedy (e.g., stratified calibration, Mondrian conformal) is tested, leaving a known-deployment gap open.

Strengths

  • Genuinely novel and well-motivated question (UQ choice transfer) that no prior GUI-grounding paper asks.
  • Unprecedented breadth: 27 methods × 7 families × 4 agents × 4 datasets × 3 vendors, with both internal-accessible and API-only interfaces.
  • Methodologically disciplined: leakage control, 50-seed splits, bootstrap CIs, paired significance, registered protocol card, harmonised cross-tier comparison without proxy substitution.
  • Connects error discrimination, graded severity, calibration, and spatial coverage into a unified deployment picture — and shows they disagree (AUROC vs AUSE agree in only 2/16 open-weight cells).
  • Practical, reproducible release (argus-uq package + Table 5 selection recipe) lowers the barrier to regime-aware adoption.
  • Intellectually honest about the negative cross-tier result and about scale/backbone confounds in vanilla→specialist analysis.

Limitations

  • Scope: single-step executable clicks only; no multi-step trajectories, no planning/recovery.
  • Sampling budget: n=5 samples is small for dispersion-based UQ (SafeGround scores moved to appendix).
  • Cross-tier sample size: 12 pairs is underpowered to rule out a small real effect; the ρ=+0.08 null should be read as "undetected," not "absent."
  • No new UQ estimator is proposed; deployment guidance is a recipe, not a plug-in solution.
  • Vendor-specific artifacts (bimodal verbalised confidence on Gemini) can inflate AUROC and are only partially diagnosed.
  • Coverage degradation under interface/calibration-test mismatch is observed but not remediated (no stratified/Mondrian conformal tested).
  • Static snapshot of vendors/models; rankings may shift with newer model generations, reinforcing the authors' own "re-rank on the target" message.

Relevance

  • 对计算机操作智能体（computer-use agents）方向高度相关：直接针对将 VLM 预测转化为可执行 GUI 点击这一核心原语，提供首个系统的不确定性量化迁移性证据，对拒绝执行、延迟、人机协作、空间安全区域设计有直接指导意义。
  • 对可信/安全部署相关：分保形点击圆盘将"分数好坏"延伸到"覆盖是否达标"，对需要可证明安全保证的自动化操作（RPA、辅助驾驶式 GUI 自动化、无障碍辅助）极具价值。
  • 对闭源大模型不确定性研究：揭示了"开权重 UQ 结论无法直接迁移到 API 模型"这一重要且反直觉的结论，提醒工业界不要用开源代理外推闭源 UQ。
  • 对后续工作：可作为 GUI-UQ 的标准评测平台，并为多步轨迹 UQ、分保形校准改进、跨厂商 UQ 提供研究起点。
  • 与当前 cs.LG/cs.AI/cs.CL/cs.CV 跨领域热点（VLM 智能体、conformal prediction、LLM hallucination 检测）均有交汇。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the cross-tier null (ρ≈+0.08) survive with more vendors / more datasets, or is it an artifact of only 12 pairs and the bimodal Gemini verbalised score?
  2. Can stratified / Mondrian / weighted conformal prediction restore coverage under the calibration-test or interface mismatch observed on Gemini×ScreenSpot-Pro?
  3. How do these UQ rankings extend to multi-step trajectories — does the most stable single-step family (density/probe) remain stable when planning and recovery errors dominate?
  4. For closed-source vendors, can a small shared internal-logit probe (e.g., logit-bias endpoint, top-k logprobs) be re-introduced to recover density-family signals without full observability?
  5. Is the vanilla→specialist degradation of attention/verbalised families causal (fine-tuning destroys the signal) or merely correlated with scale/backbone — and can it be mitigated by specialist-aware recalibration?
  6. How transferable are these GUI-grounding UQ conclusions to non-GUI embodied actions (robotics end-effectors, 3D navigation) where the action space and miss-severity geometry differ?
  7. Can a single "intervention-aware" meta-selector (learned on the regime features) automatically pick the right UQ family per cell, and would it beat the static Table 5 recipe?
