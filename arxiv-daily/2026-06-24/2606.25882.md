> **一句话总结：** 本文系统分析了用双重随机变分推断（DSVI）训练深度高斯过程（DGP）时常见的"后验坍塌"问题，指出广泛使用的线性（PCA）先验均值之所以有效并非因为它规避了深层 DGP 的非内射病态，而是因为它改善了初始化时的优化条件；据此提出一种对零均值 DGP 的初始化方案，使其在初始化时模仿 PCA 均值的行为，从而在保留原则性零均值先验的同时避免坍塌并稳定优化。

> **TL;DR:** The paper re-explains why the popular linear (PCA) prior mean function helps train variational Deep Gaussian Processes (DGPs): not by fixing non-injectivity pathologies in very deep DGPs, as long believed, but by improving optimization conditioning at initialization. The authors propose a zero-mean DGP initialization that mimics the PCA mean's initialization behavior, preventing posterior collapse, stabilizing training, and matching or sometimes beating PCA-mean DGPs on synthetic and UCI benchmarks.

## 核心贡献

- **重新解释后验坍塌的根源**：在 DSVI 框架下，DGP 的后验坍塌（变分后验退化为先验、模型把数据当噪声解释）主要源于变分参数的标准初始化（变分均值初始化为 m=0）与内层先验均值函数之间的相互作用，而非常被引用的深层 DGP 非内射病态。
- **重新定位 PCA/线性先验均值的作用**：证明线性先验均值的成功来自其对初始化时优化景观的改善（条件数），而非更好的建模性质——并指出即使在浅层（2 层）DGP 中它也被普遍使用，动机并不充分。
- **提出新的零均值 DGP 初始化方案（M0 与 MY）**：通过初始化变分均值，使内层 GP 在诱导点处的输出在初始化时逼近 PCA 均值函数（恒等或 PCA 投影）；并扩展到输出层使其初始预测对齐目标。该方案解耦了"先验选择"与"优化便利"。
- **首次严格解释白化（whitened）参数化为何更稳定**：白化消除了先验引入的相关性，产生条件更好的梯度，从而显著降低 KLD 正则项的优化不稳定与坏局部最优；并指出白化也有助于缓解后验坍塌。
- **系统比较三种参数化**：标准非白化、白化、以及 GPFLOW 风格的重参数化非白化（NWR），表明它们优化行为本质不同，且只有部分能从 PCA 均值中受益；NWR 虽更不稳定但在某些 UCI 数据上预测更好。
- **大规模实验验证**：在受控合成数据与 8 个 UCI 数据集（Boston、Concrete、Energy、Kin8nm、Power、Protein、Redwine、Yacht）上验证：新初始化能阻止坍塌、提高跨 split 稳定性，并取得与 PCA 均值 DGP 相当（个别更优）的性能。

## Key Contributions

- A comprehensive analysis showing that posterior collapse in DSVI-trained DGPs stems from the interaction of standard variational initialization (variational mean m=0) with the inner-layer prior mean, not from the non-injective pathologies traditionally cited.
- A reinterpretation of the widely used linear/PCA prior mean function: its effectiveness is an initialization-conditioning effect rather than a modeling improvement, challenging its default use even in shallow (2-layer) DGPs.
- A simple, computationally cheap initialization scheme (M0 for inner layers, MY including the output layer) for zero-mean DGPs that mimics the PCA mean's initialization behavior, letting practitioners choose priors for modeling reasons rather than optimization convenience.
- The first rigorous explanation of why the whitened parameterization converges more stably: it removes prior-induced correlations and yields better-conditioned gradients on the KL term, which also helps avoid collapse.
- A systematic comparison of three parameterizations (standard non-whitened, whitened, and GPFLOW's reparameterized non-whitened), showing they differ fundamentally in optimization behavior and benefit unequally from the PCA mean.
- Extensive validation on synthetic data and eight UCI benchmarks (20 train/test splits, 2–5 layers, 100 inducing points) confirming that the proposed initialization prevents collapse, improves cross-split stability, and matches or surpasses PCA-mean DGPs.

## Methodology

- **Setting**: Deep Gaussian Processes with L layers, each inner layer having D units, trained with Doubly Stochastic Variational Inference (DSVI, Salimbeni & Deisenroth 2017) using inducing points and a factorized Gaussian variational posterior. The ELBO combines an expected log-likelihood (ELL) term with a KL divergence (KLD) regularizer.
- **Analytical machinery**: The authors study posterior collapse through (i) a toy single-data-point/single-inducing-point problem analyzing the ELL and initial predictive variance of zero-mean vs PCA-mean DGPs, and (ii) exact coordinate (closed-form) updates of the variational parameters and noise term in a surrogate sparse GP approximation, derived in Appendix A. These reveal which initial configurations drive the coordinate update for the noise variance toward "explain data as noise".
- **Three parameterizations analyzed**: standard non-whitened (variational mean parameterized directly, as in GPJAX/GPYTORCH); whitened (inducing variables expressed in the whitened/Cholesky basis, Hensman et al. 2015); and a non-whitened reparameterized form where the variational mean is shifted by the prior mean (as in GPFLOW, motivated by inter-domain GPs).
- **Proposed initialization (Section 5)**: choose variational means so that, at initialization, each inner GP's output at the inducing points reproduces the PCA mean function (identity when hidden dim equals input dim, else a PCA projection). This requires solving a small linear system; for the output layer (MY variant) it additionally makes the initial predictive mean match the targets at the inducing points. Inducing points are K-means initialized (Z = Xz); kernel length-scale selection is described in Algorithm 1.
- **Experiments**: synthetic 1-D illustration of collapse and recovery; UCI regression with 2–5 layer DGPs, 100 inducing points, Adam (lr 0.01), 20,000 iterations, batch size up to 10,000, kernels initialized at ℓ = σ = 2.0, noise variance 0.05, S = 10⁻⁵I variational covariance, evaluated over 20 splits with 100 Monte-Carlo predictive samples. Metrics: test log-likelihood (and RMSE in the appendix), average per-split model rank, and cross-split standard deviation as a stability proxy.

## Results & Highlights

- **Reproducing collapse**: Standard zero-mean DGPs with m=0 initialization frequently collapse on both toy and UCI data, especially under the non-whitened parameterization (e.g., Kin8nm, Yacht, Boston, Power), where the model predicts mostly noise.
- **Whitening helps but is not a cure**: The whitened parameterization consistently reduces collapse and stabilizes the KL term, but collapse can still occur — even with PCA mean — when inner-layer kernel output scale or inducing-point configurations inject excessive variance. The first rigorous justification for whitening's stability is provided.
- **PCA mean's effect is an initialization artifact**: PCA-mean DGPs avoid collapse not due to non-injectivity fixes but because they start from a better-conditioned point. Notably, on Boston the PCA mean actually degrades performance, supporting the claim that the prior should be chosen for modeling reasons.
- **Proposed initialization works**: M0 matches or improves over standard ZERO-W (clear gains on Power, Kin8nm, and Yacht, where ZERO-W collapses but M0 recovers PCA-W-level performance). MY can even surpass PCA-W (Kin8nm) but sometimes suffers from a large initial KLD driven by the selected initial length-scale (e.g., Energy length-scale ≈ 6), hurting test log-likelihood more than RMSE.
- **Stability**: In average-rank and standard-deviation analyses (Tables 5–8), the proposed initializations produce lower cross-split variance than both ZERO-W and PCA-W, indicating a smoother optimization landscape.
- **Non-whitened reparameterized (NWR)**: more unstable and more prone to collapse, but on several UCI datasets it yields better point predictions; with the M0 initialization, NWR slightly surpasses its whitened counterpart in some cases.
- **Headline takeaway**: The improvements attributed to the PCA prior mean do not arise from a different underlying statistical model — appropriately initialized zero-mean DGPs close most of the gap.

## Critical Assessment

- The central thesis — that PCA mean helps via initialization conditioning rather than non-injectivity — is well argued and supported by a clean mechanistic story (ELL at initialization + coordinate updates on a surrogate SVGP). The connection to VAE posterior collapse is appropriately differentiated (different sources).
- The evidence is largely empirical and analytical on simplified/toy settings; the coordinate-update argument relies on a single-data-point/single-inducing-point surrogate, so its quantitative extrapolation to deep multi-inducing-point models is approximate (the authors acknowledge this as an "approximate explanation").
- The claim that PCA mean is "over-credited" is convincing but the proposed initialization does not always reach PCA-mean performance (the authors concede M0/MY is still worse in most cases), somewhat tempering the practical payoff: the main win is enabling principled zero-mean priors (and downstream flexible models like transformed GPs) rather than uniformly better accuracy.
- The MY variant's sensitivity to initial length-scale (large KLD on Energy) shows the method introduces new failure modes that need care (Algorithm 1 and Appendix B address this but not fully).
- Stability is assessed via cross-split standard deviation, a reasonable but indirect proxy for optimization smoothness; a direct optimization-trajectory/Hessian analysis would strengthen the whitening claim (the authors note a fuller instability study exists in a separate technical report, Maroñas 2025).
- Reproducibility looks solid (standard UCI protocol, fixed seeds implied by 20 splits, code/data public via UCI), though explicit code release is not prominently stated in the body.

## Strengths

- First rigorous, mechanistic explanation of two long-standing "folk" practices in DGP training (PCA prior mean and whitening), replacing hand-wavy justifications.
- Practically useful, low-cost initialization that decouples prior specification from optimization convenience — important for extensions (transformed GPs, non-Gaussian layers) where a PCA mean is awkward.
- Clean experimental design with depth sweep, multiple parameterizations, rank- and variance-based summaries across 20 splits on 8 datasets.
- Honest about limitations and failure cases (Energy length-scale, NWR instability, residual gap to PCA).

## Limitations

- Proposed initialization does not fully match PCA-mean predictive performance in most UCI cases.
- Requires solving a linear system whose numerical stability depends on the number of inducing points and initial kernel parameters; MY can produce a large initial (and final) KLD that complicates ELBO optimization.
- Analytical collapse argument is approximate, built on a simplified surrogate; generalization to deep, many-inducing-point, multi-output settings is asserted rather than proven.
- No comparison to alternative collapse remedies (e.g., two-stage/frozen-hyperparameter pretraining schedules, more expressive variational families) beyond brief discussion.
- Scope limited to regression with Gaussian likelihoods; extension to classification/non-Gaussian likelihoods is mentioned but not empirically demonstrated.

## Relevance

本文对深度高斯过程与变分推断社区具有直接价值：它澄清了两个被默认采用的工程实践（PCA 先验均值、白化参数化）的真正机理，并提供了一个不依赖特定先验均值即可稳定训练 DGP 的初始化方法。这对需要灵活先验的下游模型（如 transformed GPs、非高斯层）尤其重要，也为任何使用 GPflow / GPyTorch / GPJax 训练稀疏 GP/DGP 的实践者提供了关于初始化与参数化选择的实用指导。对一般研究变分方法中后验坍塌/模式坍塌（VAE、扩散模型微调等）的读者，也提供了跨领域的可迁移见解。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the proposed initialization be extended to non-Gaussian likelihoods (classification, count data) and to multi-output DGPs with the same collapse guarantees?
- Does initializing the variational covariance S = I (rather than 10⁻⁵I) at the output layer systematically change collapse rates, as the coordinate-update analysis hints?
- Could a length-scale-aware variant of Algorithm 1 avoid the large-initial-KLD failure of MY on datasets like Energy?
- Is there a direct (Hessian/condition-number or gradient-noise) optimization-theoretic proof of whitening's superiority that does not rely on the single-point surrogate?
- How does the proposed initialization interact with alternative inference schemes (EP, MCMC, Denoising Diffusion VI) that also suffer posterior under-fitting?
- Empirically, do the conclusions transfer to very deep DGPs (≥ 6 layers) where non-injectivity genuinely becomes relevant, separating the two effects the paper conflates?
