> **一句话总结：** 提出一种基于 MSDF（最高位优先）数字串行算术的融合乘加（MMA）FPGA 卷积加速器，用于 U-Net 图像分割，将乘法与加法融合为单一流水线以消除级联初始延迟，在 Xilinx Zynq-7020 上实现 15.14 GOPS/W 的能效，相比 CPU 提升约一个数量级，相比 MSDF 基线降低约 9 倍能耗。

> **TL;DR:** A merged multiply-add (MMA) FPGA convolution accelerator built on most-significant-digit-first (MSDF) digit-serial arithmetic fuses multiplication and addition into one pipeline, removing cascaded initial delays. Evaluated on a U-Net for image segmentation on a Xilinx Zynq-7020 SoC, it reaches 15.14 GOPS/W (about an order of magnitude above CPU's 1.93 GOPS/W) and ~9x lower energy than prior MSDF-based FPGA designs, making it attractive for latency/energy-constrained edge inference.

## 核心贡献

- **融合乘加单元（MMA）：** 将 MSDF 串行乘法与加法树融合为单一数据通路，消除了传统级联实现中乘法器与加法树各自的初始延迟，把每个输出 26 周期的延迟压缩为单一 MMA 初始延迟 δ×+ = 2 周期，显著提升吞吐与效率。
- **并行通道处理：** 每个 MMA 以位串行方式处理 8-bit 激活、并行接收 8-bit 权重，在 TN = 32 个输入通道上并行计算内积；9 个 MMA 组成一个 Kernel Processing Block（KPB）覆盖 3×3 卷积窗。
- **16 路并行 KPB：** 系统实例化 16 个并行 KPB，每组时钟可同时计算 16 个输出像素，并保留 digit-serial 的流式与计算/通信重叠特性。
- **冗余数系统支持：** 采用 radix-2 的有符号数字冗余数系统（RDNS，数字集 {-1, 0, 1}）与 Inverted Encoding of Negabit（IEN），使 MSDF 可在仅有部分输入位时即开始输出并在后续周期纠正。
- **强能效实验对比：** 在 100 MHz 下达到 52.95 GOPS、15.14 GOPS/W、17.43×10⁻⁴ GOPS/Slice、186.20 mJ；相对 bit-parallel 提升吞吐 1.07×、相对 bit-serial 提升 4.36×、相对 MSDF 基线提升 2.52×。

## Key Contributions

- A **merged multiply-add (MMA)** MSDF datapath that fuses the multiplier and adder tree, eliminating the per-stage initial delays that traditionally compound in cascaded MSDF inner-product units.
- **Parallel spatial-depth processing**: each MMA computes the inner product across TN = 32 input channels using serially streamed 8-bit activations and parallel 8-bit weights; nine MMAs form a Kernel Processing Block (KPB) covering the 3×3 convolution window.
- A **modular, parallel system** of 16 KPBs producing 16 output pixels per clock group while preserving the digit-serial streaming that overlaps computation with communication.
- Use of a **signed-digit redundant number system (RDNS, radix-2, digits {-1, 0, 1}) with Inverted Encoding of Negabit (IEN)** to allow early output and in-cycle correction from partial bit information.
- A comprehensive **benchmark against bit-parallel, bit-serial, MSDF, GPU, and CPU baselines**, demonstrating state-of-the-art energy efficiency (15.14 GOPS/W) on a Xilinx Zynq-7020 SoC.

## Methodology

The work targets the convolutional layers of U-Net for biomedical image segmentation (e.g., brain tumor segmentation). The model is trained in PyTorch 2.5.0 and quantized to 8-bit fixed point via the FBGEMM backend. The accelerator is implemented in Verilog RTL and mapped to a Xilinx Zynq-7020 SoC.

The core MSDF arithmetic computes digit-serial, most-significant-bit first, which supports early termination and progressive refinement but suffers from an inherent initial delay δ before the first output digit appears. In a conventional MSDF inner-product unit, a multiplier followed by a log₂(N)-deep adder tree means each adder level adds its own δ₊, so total latency is δₓ + δ₊·⌈log₂(TN)⌉ + p_out.

The proposed MMA fuses multiplication and addition: multiplication is performed by bitwise AND gates (selecting the 8-bit weight when the corresponding activation bit is 1), and partial products are accumulated in a carry-propagate adder tree that also folds in a left-shifted residual from the previous cycle. The most significant half of each cycle's partial sum feeds an output-generation function (OGF, a chain of 2-input full adders) producing the next digit serially, while the least significant bits become the residual. This removes the separate adder-tree delay, giving the MMA a single initial delay δₓ₊ = 2 cycles. Nine MMAs form a KPB (3×3 kernel, each MMA handling 32 channels); 16 parallel KPBs compute 16 output pixels per clock group. Total latency is 26 cycles per MMA output. The redundant signed-digit system with IEN encoding (logical 0 = −1, logical 1 = +1) simplifies sign handling in left-to-right accumulation.

## Results & Highlights

Operating at 100 MHz on the Zynq-7020, the proposed design achieves:

| Design | Freq (MHz) | Time (ms) | GOPS | GOPS/W | GOPS/Slice (×10⁻⁴) | Energy (mJ) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bit-Parallel | 100 | 57.20 | 49.30 | 2.65 | 10.59 | 1064.43 |
| Bit-Serial | 100 | 232.26 | 12.14 | 0.88 | 3.98 | 3210.81 |
| MSDF | 100 | 133.94 | 21.05 | 3.01 | 2.61 | 1644.77 |
| GPU | — | 7.31 | 385.99 | 5.51 | — | 511.35 |
| CPU | 2200 | 58.42 | 48.27 | 1.93 | — | 1460.48 |
| **Proposed** | 100 | 53.25 | 52.95 | **15.14** | 17.43 | **186.20** |

- **Throughput**: 1.07× over bit-parallel, 4.36× over bit-serial, 2.52× over MSDF-based accelerator.
- **Energy efficiency**: 2.7× over GPU, >7× over CPU; ~9× energy reduction vs MSDF-based FPGA; 3.8× lower energy than GPU and 10.8× lower than CPU.
- **Area efficiency**: 1.65× higher GOPS/slice than bit-parallel and ~6.7× higher than the MSDF baseline.
- Although the GPU has the highest absolute throughput (385.99 GOPS), the proposed design is markedly more energy-efficient, validating it for power-constrained edge deployment.

## Critical Assessment

The engineering contribution is clear and well-motivated: fusing the multiplier and adder into a single digit-serial datapath to eliminate compounded initial delays is a legitimate, verifiable hardware optimization, and the latency equation and RDNS/IEN design are described in concrete, reproducible terms. However, several gaps limit the strength of the claims:

- **No task-accuracy evaluations.** The paper reports only hardware metrics (throughput, GOPS/W, latency, area). There is no segmentation accuracy (Dice, IoU) or bit-serial/approximate-computing error budget, so it is unclear whether the 8-bit MSDF arithmetic preserves U-Net accuracy — a critical omission given the stated medical-imaging motivation.
- **Single network, single platform.** Results are limited to U-Net on one entry-level FPGA (Zynq-7020). Generalization to other CNNs, deeper models, or larger FPGAs is asserted but not demonstrated.
- **The "~9× energy reduction vs MSDF" headline** is the standout claim but rests entirely on comparing to the 2024 ECHO baseline at the same 100 MHz; the energy model and workload (batch size, precision, image size) underpinning the mJ figures are not detailed.
- **Apples-to-oranges clocking.** All FPGA baselines are normalized to 100 MHz, while the CPU runs at 2200 MHz — useful for energy-per-op comparison but it understates absolute latency differences. The GPU's 7.31 ms is dramatically faster in wall-clock time, so the framing as a "throughput" win is subtle.
- **Early termination, the headline benefit of MSDF**, is explicitly deferred to future work, so the current design does not yet exploit MSDF's most distinctive advantage.

Overall this reads as a solid, incremental hardware-engineering preprint with compelling energy numbers but incomplete validation; acceptance as a full paper would likely require segmentation-accuracy evidence and energy-model transparency.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-explained architectural innovation (MMA fusion) with explicit latency analysis and cycle-level equations.
- Strong energy-efficiency and area-efficiency numbers (15.14 GOPS/W, 17.43×10⁻⁴ GOPS/slice) on a realistic, low-power edge-class FPGA.
- Honest, broad benchmarking against four distinct baseline classes (bit-parallel, bit-serial, MSDF, GPU/CPU).
- Modular and generalizable design (3×3 kernels, channel counts in multiples of 32) applicable beyond U-Net.

## Limitations

- No reported model accuracy; the bit-serial/approximate nature is not validated end-to-end on the segmentation task.
- Evaluated on a single CNN architecture and a single, small FPGA device.
- Energy/latency measurement methodology and exact workload are under-specified.
- Early termination — a prime MSDF advantage — is left as future work, so peak efficiency upside is unrealized.

## Relevance

本文对边缘端医学影像（如脑肿瘤 MRI 分割）和资源受限设备上的 CNN 实时推理具有直接价值：融合乘加的 MSDF 架构在低频 FPGA 上即可实现优于 CPU 一个数量级的能效，契合嵌入式/可穿戴医疗与计算机视觉场景。对于研究硬件友好的量化与近似计算、U-Net 部署、以及数字串行/在线算术在深度学习加速器中应用的读者，提供了可复现的 RTL 设计思路与基准。其方法论（消除级联初始延迟、冗余数系统纠错）也可迁移到其他以卷积为主导的网络。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What segmentation accuracy (Dice/IoU) does the 8-bit MSDF MMA achieve versus a floating-point U-Net baseline, and how sensitive is it to the digit-serial precision/early-termination point?
- How does performance scale on a larger FPGA (e.g., Zynq UltraScale+) or with more than 16 parallel KPBs, and where does the area/energy curve saturate?
- How is the reported energy (mJ) measured — fixed activity factor, post-implementation power report, or board measurement — and what is the exact batch size, image resolution, and precision assumed?
- Can the MMA benefit be extended to depthwise or grouped convolutions, attention/MAC-heavy transformers, or only to standard dense convolutions?
- What is the wall-clock latency budget once residual initial delays across cascaded U-Net layers are summed, and does it still meet real-time medical-imaging requirements?
