> **一句话总结：** 提出 AMPLE-GNC，一种可靠性非对称的航天器制导/导航/控制栈——把"能力强但难验证"的学习模块（小语言模型指令器 + Rapid Motor Adaptation 容错控制器）放在"可机器检验"的运行时安全盾之内，并用分裂一致性恢复期限证书实现"自适应感知"的放权逻辑，从而在 6-DOF 仿真台架上做到既能自主恢复执行器故障、又可形式化保证安全。

> **TL;DR:** AMPLE-GNC is a three-tier learned-plus-verified spacecraft GNC stack that treats reliability asymmetry as a design resource: a grammar-bounded 360M language-model commander, a fault-adaptive RMA controller, and a Kind-2-verified LTL runtime shield are co-designed with a conformal recovery-deadline certificate so a capable controller is allowed to recover (kept ~94.5% autonomous, 100% live) while the verified layer still catches genuine non-recovery.

## 核心贡献

- **可靠性非对称（Reliability Asymmetry）作为设计原则**：不试图直接验证神经网络或语言模型，而是让"可机器检验"的层级（LTL 监视器 + Kind 2 模型检验）去界定"能力强但不透明"的层级（语言指令器、学习控制器）能自由行动的范围——把不可验证性从障碍转化为设计资源。
- **可部署的边缘指令器（Edge Commander）**：在 SmolLM2-360M 上做 LoRA 微调 + 从动作 schema 合成的 GBNF 语法约束解码，给出"硬"输出有效性保证（部署解码器只能产生良构、词表内动作），真实可执行率 84%；并设计了去泄漏（de-leaked）评估协议，把"语法有效性"和"语义准确率"分开报告，而非混成一个虚高数字。
- **故障自适应控制器（RMA）**：基于 Rapid Motor Adaptation 的教师-学生方案，用 GRU 学生在线推断潜在执行器故障，配合解析 PD 律做控制；在保持门（settled gate）上恢复 97.8% 的符号故障、94.4% 的连续增益故障，而 PD、行为克隆、端到端 RL 全为 0%。
- **自适应感知的运行时保障（Adaptation-Aware Runtime Assurance）**：发现"锁存式 safe-hold 盾"会主动压制正在恢复的控制器（恢复期它本来就要先短暂朝错误方向运动）；用分裂一致性（split-conformal）恢复期限证书替代启发式裕度，在保持 Kind-2 验证监视器不变的前提下，让真正能恢复的控制器保留控制权（94.5% 自主），同时仍能捕获无法恢复的控制器（0% 自主，被安全接管）。
- **诚实且端到端可复现的系统证据**：三层在 6-DOF Basilisk 仿真台上以单一闭环运行，导出为自包含 C 工件，在真实树莓派 5 上基准测试；每个头条数字都由可执行清单从干净 checkout 重新推导（19/19 通过）。

## Key Contributions

- **Reliability asymmetry as the organizing principle.** Rather than trying to verify the neural controller or LLM directly, AMPLE-GNC makes a learned component's authority *conditional on a verifiable element it cannot override*: a grammar bounds the commander on its output, a Kind-2-checked monitor bounds the controller on its state, and a conformal certificate bounds authority for as long as a distribution-free recovery-time bound permits.
- **A deployable, output-bounded edge commander.** LoRA-finetuned SmolLM2-360M (277 MB int5 GGUF) with grammar-constrained decoding yields a hard syntactic-validity guarantee and 84% real planner-executable rate. A de-leaked, novel-phrasing evaluation separates memorization from generalization (exact-match 37.8% on novel phrasings, lifted to 48.0% by a phrasing-diversity re-finetune with the base held fixed).
- **A learned fault-adaptive controller that beats classical baselines where they are weakest.** RMA recovers 97.8% of sign faults and 94.4% of continuous-gain faults on the settled gate (≤0.2° held over a dwell window), versus 0% for PD, behavior cloning, and end-to-end RL, and 55%/45% for the strongest classical-adaptive / TRAIN-tuned Nussbaum-gain baselines. The gain is specifically on continuous effectiveness, where equilibrium does not move and only an accurate online gain estimate suffices.
- **Adaptation-aware runtime assurance.** The central insight: a worst-case latching safe-hold shield *suppresses* a capable adapting controller because it is unsafe by design during its recovery transient. A split-conformal recovery-deadline certificate (coverage ≈ 1−α by construction, no distributional assumption) plus recovery-aware engagement keeps the recovering controller 94.5% autonomous (100% live in closed loop) while still safe-holding a diverging controller (0% autonomous), at ~0.02% shield overhead at 10 Hz.
- **First measurement (to the authors' knowledge) of the extrapolation cliff for learned spacecraft fault recovery.** A split-conformant retrain strictly inside the declared TRAIN regime scores only 57–67% beyond the envelope, and adding 4× more in-regime data makes extrapolation *worse* — identifying domain-randomization breadth (not data volume) as the held-out-performance knob.

## Methodology

AMPLE-GNC is a three-tier guidance-navigation-control stack evaluated on the Basilisk 6-DOF attitude simulator, with the data flow: operator natural-language command → commander (NL→PDDL+) → verifier (feasibility certificate against linearized constraints) → controller at 10 Hz → shield → safe-hold/escalation. Attitude is in modified Rodrigues parameters (MRPs).

**Reliability asymmetry.** Authority flows left-to-right through the capability path while assurance flows right-to-left from the verified shield that bounds it. The capable-but-opaque tiers (commander, learned controller), the screening verifier, and the verified runtime shield occupy three different points on the verifiability spectrum and are deliberately not treated as peers.

**Verified runtime shield (Tier 1, assurance).** Nine linear-temporal-logic invariants (I1–I9) span pointing, body rate, power, wheel-momentum, thermal, propellant-reserve, link, eclipse, and attitude-rate bounds, each threshold traceable to a spacecraft engineering source. They are expressed as bounded-horizon *predictor-soundness* properties: "if the monitor predicts the bounded-horizon trajectory is safe, then it is in fact safe." Each invariant is encoded in Lustre and its soundness proved with the Kind 2 SMT model checker (v2.3.0, Z3 backend), returning 9/9 valid. Kind 2 also *falsifies* the naive first-order predictor with a concrete counterexample and verifies a second-order predictor (pointing + max(0,rate)·h + ½·a_max·h²) that closes the gap. A complementary runtime campaign over 30,000+ adversarial states and 4.3×10⁵ monitor decisions measures detection completeness = 1.000 and drives soundness violations 316 → 82 → 0.

**Edge commander (Tier 2, capability).** Fine-tunes SmolLM2-360M with LoRA (decisively outperforming from-scratch distillation at this scale) and decodes under a GBNF grammar synthesized byte-identically from the action schema, so the deployed decoder can emit only well-formed, in-vocabulary actions over a 20-template NASA-operations corpus. The binding edge constraint is the vocabulary (tied-embedding) footprint, not transformer depth.

**Fault-adaptive controller (Tier 2, capability).** Rapid Motor Adaptation: a privileged teacher π(obs,z) with the true fault z = [g₀,g₁,g₂,f⁻¹] implements an analytic inertia-scaled PD law; a deployable GRU student infers ẑ online from [obs, aₜ₋₁, Δobs], trained first by z-regression on teacher rollouts then by on-policy DAgger. A key signal is Δω, whose sign relative to the commanded action reveals the fault sign. After convergence the estimate is *latched* (re-adapt only if it drifts back out) to kill low-excitation steady-state limit cycling — playing the same role as classical dead-zone / σ-modification robustness fixes.

**Adaptation-aware assurance.** The recovery zone [warn, critical) licenses the bounded transient; two exits — critical excursion past the mission attitude-safety limit Φ_c = 90°, or a missed recovery deadline — trigger safe-hold. The deadline d_α is the ⌈(1−α)(n+1)⌉-th smallest calibration recovery time, guaranteeing P(recovery ≤ d_α) ≥ 1−α for an exchangeable future fault with no distributional assumption.

## Results & Highlights

- **Commander (de-leaked):** 84% real planner-executable rate (full-argument grounding against a real PDDL domain; a head-only stub would falsely report 100%). Novel-phrasing exact-match 37.8% → 48.0% after phrasing-diversity re-finetune, vs. 91.9% on the verbatim-memorized subset (a 54-point memorization gap the leaky split concealed).
- **Controller (settled ≤0.2° science gate, held-out taxonomy, n=500/cell):** RMA-latched reaches 97.8% sign / 94.4% continuous-gain recovery (Wilson 95% CI [92.0, 96.1] on gain), approaching the privileged oracle's 100%. All fault-unaware and end-to-end-learned baselines (PD, single-step BC, GRU+PPO, TD3+HER) score 0%. Classical adaptive reaches 100% sign / 55% gain; TRAIN-tuned Nussbaum-gain 45%/3%. Gain+Bias and total-loss are 0% for *every* controller including the oracle — an honest architectural limit (integral-free law cannot null a constant bias; a dead axis is uncontrollable).
- **Capability envelope (the new finding):** Split-conformant retrain strictly inside TRAIN regime scores 66.6% sign / 57.0% gain beyond the envelope; 4× more in-regime data *worsens* it to 53.2%/43.0% — ruling out undertraining and identifying randomization breadth as the lever.
- **Sensor-noise robustness:** Flat through 0.05°/step star-tracker noise (an order of magnitude above modern accuracy); fixed 0.1° mounting bias costs only ~1 point.
- **Runtime assurance:** Conformal coverage 80.8/90.2/95.0% at α = 0.20/0.10/0.05. Recovery-aware RTA keeps RMA 94.5% autonomous vs. 0% for fault-unaware PD; live in the integrated loop, 100% autonomous vs. 0% at ~18 µs/step (~0.02% of the 10 Hz budget).
- **Adversarial/rare-event stress:** Cross-entropy worst-fault search finds a 132.8° transient that is still shield-caught and recovers; n=1500 adversarial-skewed campaign gives 93.6% gate success (Wilson CI [92.2, 94.7]), only 0.20% exceed Φ_c (Clopper–Pearson upper bound ≤0.52%).
- **Operational stabilization (≤5°, beyond the science gate):** RMA stabilizes 68% of gain+bias faults (median best 2.55°) vs. 11% for PD (17.7°, tumbles); total actuator loss is an honest shield-only regime (27% stabilized).
- **Mission benefit:** Measured DSN-contact reduction 90.3% nominal / 98.3% under solar-particle-event stress; real Goldstone geometry shows the fault-unaware baseline saturates the ~30 min/day budget (1.8× nominal, 17× under stress) while the autonomous stack stays feasible.
- **Deployment (real Raspberry Pi 5):** Controller-plus-shield hot loop 0.025 ms/cycle, 1.0 MB resident (~4,000× under the 100 ms budget); commander 28 tok/s (~1.7 s/action, mission-event cadence), 437 MB resident within the 512 MB cap; board power 3.1 W idle / 9.6 W peak. Loop also validated on a 3-DOF air-bearing testbed with physical-equivalent fault injection.
- **Reproducibility:** `python -m tools.reproduce` re-derives every headline number from a clean checkout — 19/19 checks pass.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High).** The reliability-asymmetric *co-design* framing is the genuine novelty, not any single tier. The sharpest original result is that a worst-case latching shield actively suppresses a recovering adaptive controller, and the fix (a conformal recovery-deadline certificate with adaptation-aware engagement) is a principled contribution to the runtime-assurance literature, which "has not previously paired a verified shield with an online-adapting controller." The extrapolation-cliff + data-scaling-inversion measurement for learned spacecraft fault recovery also appears genuinely new.
- **Rigor (High, with caveats).** Strong on methodology: de-leaked splits, settled (not transient) gates, held-out fault taxonomy with Wilson/Clopper–Pearson intervals, TRAIN-only tuning for all baselines including the Nussbaum-gain controller, and Kind-2 model checking that both verifies and *falsifies* monitors. The honest reporting of 0% architectural limits and the 54-point memorization gap is exemplary.
- **Reproducibility (Very High *in principle*).** An executable manifest (`python -m tools.reproduce`, 19/19) is offered as part of the contribution, with committed evidence files and exact keys. This is above the norm — *provided* the artifact is actually released and re-runs externally. The paper claims it; this has not been independently verified. Container and seeds are promised but must be checked.
- **Significance (Medium-High).** Conceptually important for flight autonomy: it reframes "learned autonomy is unflyable because it is unverifiable" into "make learned authority conditional on a verifiable element it cannot override." If the framing holds up under flight review, it is influential. Practical near-term impact is bounded by the single-author, single-environment, pre-peer-review status and the modest commander semantics (48% exact-match).
- **Red flags / concerns.**
  1. **Single environment, single author.** All results are Basilisk-only and seed-controlled; the author is from a construction school, not a flight-software or aerospace lab. Cross-environment and independent reproduction are essential before any flight-readiness claim.
  2. **Conformal certificate assumes exchangeability.** Coverage holds only if the deployed fault distribution matches the calibration distribution; coverage under fault-distribution shift is explicitly deferred as future work and would need importance reweighting.
  3. **Whole-system completeness is not formally proved.** Kind 2 proves *monitor predictor soundness*, not whole-system closed-loop completeness over the nonlinear plant; that gap is closed by a runtime campaign and a reachability bound, not by a proof.
  4. **Controller reach set is certified statistically, not by a closed-form neural-reachability proof** — a clear, self-acknowledged limitation.
  5. **Commander semantics are weak.** ~48% exact-match on novel phrasings is modest; the "deployable property" is really the validity guarantee, not semantic accuracy.
  6. **Several fault classes are shield-only regimes by design** (dead axis, pure additive bias at the science gate) — not a flaw per se, but the safety story leans hard on the shield in exactly those cases.

## Strengths

- Principled, transferable framing (reliability asymmetry; conditional authority) applicable beyond attitude control.
- Co-designs the shield with the controller rather than stacking it on top — the adaptation-aware insight is the conceptual highlight.
- Exceptionally honest evaluation: de-leaked splits, settled gates, held-out taxonomies with interval estimates, and adversarial self-falsification of the monitor.
- Combines formal verification (Kind 2 / LTL / Lustre), distribution-free statistics (split-conformal prediction), and learning (RMA, grammar-constrained LLM) in a coherent whole.
- Real-hardware deployment evidence (Raspberry Pi 5, air-bearing testbed) and an executable reproduction manifest.

## Limitations

- Single-environment (Basilisk), single-author results; no external/peer-reviewed validation yet.
- Fault scope limited to actuator effectiveness (sign, continuous gain) at the science gate; bias and dead-axis are shield-only or future work; sensor dropout / wheel stiction not covered.
- Commander semantic generalization is modest (~48%); validity guarantee ≠ semantic correctness.
- Conformal coverage depends on exchangeability (calibration ≈ deployment distribution); shift not handled.
- Formal proof covers monitor predictor soundness only; whole-system closed-loop completeness rests on runtime campaign + reachability bound.
- Controller reach set certified statistically, not by a neural-reachability proof.

## Relevance

对关注"可信/可验证 AI 与学习型控制结合"以及"大模型落地到边缘/安全关键场景"的方向高度相关——它示范了如何用形式化验证 + 一致性预测把不可验证的学习模块安全地约束在可验证边界内，这正是安全关键自主系统走向部署的核心问题。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the conformal recovery-deadline certificate degrade under genuine fault-distribution shift, and can importance-reweighting or online calibration restore the 1−α coverage?
- Can a closed-form neural-reachability proof replace the statistical reach-set certification, and at what conservatism cost?
- Does the reliability-asymmetric / conditional-authority pattern transfer to multi-step temporal PDDL+ planning (the paper restricts the commander to single actions) and to multi-agent or formation-flying autonomy?
- How sensitive are the RMA controller's results to the randomization-envelope design, and is there an automated procedure to engineer the envelope to cover a declared deployment fault distribution?
- Would an integral-term or online bias observer (the named future-work path) lift the gain+bias and bias faults off the 0% architectural floor without breaking the settled-gate numbers?
- Can the Kind-2 monitor-soundness proof be extended to whole-system closed-loop completeness over the nonlinear plant, or is the runtime-campaign + reachability-bound combination the practical ceiling for formal assurance here?
