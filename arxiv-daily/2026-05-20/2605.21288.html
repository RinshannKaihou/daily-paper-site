A Mechanistic Study of Tabular Foundation Models

2605.21288 2026-05-20 Morgan Stanley ML Research
Key Finding
Three tabular foundation models achieve comparable accuracy through qualitatively different internal algorithms. TabPFNv2 and Mitra read predictions via an attention-weighted vote over context labels at layer 9. TabICLv2 uses a nearest-class-prototype rule on final-layer representations. These readout mechanisms are not interchangeable — they are jointly designed with their backbones.
Layerwise probes show TabPFNv2 jumps from chance to 0.78 probe accuracy between L8–L9; TabICLv2's column embedder alone reaches 0.824 via kNN. Replacing TabPFNv2's L9 attention with uniform weights collapses accuracy from 0.87 to 0.49 (majority-class baseline).
Key Metric
−33 to −40pp
Accuracy drop when cross-transplanting readouts between model backbones — proof that readout and backbone co-adapted during pretraining.
Why This Matters
First systematic mechanistic interpretability study of tabular foundation models. Surgical edits to positional parameters grant exact invariance at zero accuracy cost. A concrete design recipe — architectural column invariance + early label slot + OvA head — is immediately actionable for next-generation models.
Critical Edge
Hub poisoning hurts foundation models more than refit MLP baselines (−3.7 pp TabPFNv2, −3.3 pp TabICLv2, −2.8 pp Mitra), isolating readout-specific vulnerability unique to in-context learning architectures. Rank warp destroys TabICLv2 by −10.1 pp while Mitra is nearly immune (−0.4 pp) due to its quantile front-end.

Design Recipe for Next-Gen Models
1. Architectural column invariance (Mitra-style single shared cell embedding).
2. Early label injection as a dedicated slot (avoids representation collapse without positional encoding).
3. One-vs-All output head (class-invariant, accuracy-neutral on all three models).
Each ingredient is independently accuracy-neutral.

Methodology Pipeline

  1. Layerwise probes — Linear logistic regression on frozen per-layer activations; identifies where class structure becomes linearly separable (TabPFNv2: L8→L9 jump).
  2. Readout identification — Propose falsifiable surrogate rule; validate with Pearson r≥0.85, accuracy within 3 pp, Cohen’s κ≥0.8, causal ablation survival.
  3. Invariance tracing — Measure permutation sensitivity; surgically remove responsible positional parameters; pretrain invariant TabICLv1 from scratch to confirm compatibility.
  4. Collapse analysis — Hand-crafted toy models (M0–M3) with closed-form solutions establish expressiveness bounds (0.75 accuracy ceiling on collapse-prone data for fully invariant architectures).
  5. Adversarial testing — Eight perturbations applied to context set; Ridge/XGBoost/MLP refit baselines isolate readout-specific sensitivity from general context corruption.

Critical Assessment

Novelty
High
First systematic mechanistic interpretability study of tabular FMs. Causal intervention + cross-transplantation are novel.
Rigor
Very Strong
Four-part faithfulness criterion, closed-form proofs, paired bootstrap CIs, 5–20 seeds, Holm-corrected tests.
Reproducibility
Good
Released checkpoints, standard OpenML datasets, closed-form derivations, ~30 GPU-hours on 4×H100.
Significance
High
Design recipe is immediately actionable. TabICLv2's 26M-param ICL transformer is mostly redundant. New evaluation paradigm.
Red Flags
Limited Scope
Only three model families. Excludes TabPFNv2.5/v2.6 (licensing). Regression analysis incomplete. Skewed toward small OpenML datasets.

Open Questions

  1. How do readout mechanisms change when trained on real data distributions instead of synthetic priors?
  2. Can the adversarial framework design proactive defenses (e.g., hub-aware context reweighting)?
  3. Does early label injection transfer to non-tabular transformers as a general ICL improvement?
  4. Do vote-based readouts degrade gracefully or catastrophically as context set size approaches model limits?
