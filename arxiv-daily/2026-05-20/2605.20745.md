> **一句话总结：** 本文发现生成式验证器在逐步验证时的"严格程度"编码在其隐藏状态的段落边界（`\n\n`分隔符）处，提出 VerifySteer 方法通过轻量级正确性探针进行样本级路由并选择性干预隐藏状态，在 ProcessBench 和 Hard2Verify 上以 4-7 倍更少的推理计算量超越了自一致性基线，且可与微调验证器叠加使用。

> **TL;DR:** This paper discovers that a generative verifier's tendency to accept or reject reasoning steps (its "strictness") is encoded in the hidden state of paragraph-boundary `\n\n` delimiter tokens. They propose VerifySteer, which uses a lightweight correctness probe for sample-level routing (strict vs. lenient steering direction) and a cosine-similarity gate for delimiter-level selective intervention — no fine-tuning needed. On ProcessBench, VerifySteer-Bi with Qwen3-1.7B surpasses self-consistency Cons@4 by 3.7 F1 while using 4x fewer FLOPs. On Hard2Verify with Qwen3-8B-thinking, it surpasses Cons@8 by 2.2 F1 at 7x less compute. Applied atop the fine-tuned FARE-20B, it further boosts F1 from 41.5 to 51.1, showing gains stack with fine-tuning.

## 核心贡献

- **发现验证严格度的隐藏状态信号**：验证器对每个步骤的接受/拒绝倾向编码在对应验证段落前的 `\n\n` 分隔符 token 的隐藏状态中，该信号在中间层（如 Qwen3-8B 的第 22 层）最为集中和可分离。
- **揭示统一干预的 TPR-TNR 权衡**：对所有样本均匀施加严格度导向向量可改善错误检测（TNR），但会降低对正确解的认证率（TPR），反之亦然。
- **提出 VerifySteer 的两级自适应机制**：样本级路由（用三层 MLP 探针预测解的正确性，选择严格/宽松方向）+ 分隔符级门控（仅对与目标方向不对齐的 `\n\n` token 施加干预）。
- **显著的效率与兼容性**：单次推理代价与基线相同，无需微调；与验证器微调正交互补，在 FARE-20B 上叠加提升 F1 从 41.5 到 51.1。

## Key Contributions

- Identify a **verification-specific hidden-state signal**: the accept/reject tendency for each step is encoded in the hidden state of the `\n\n` delimiter token preceding the corresponding verification paragraph, concentrated at specific mid-layers (e.g., layer 22 in Qwen3-8B).
- Reveal the **inherent TPR-TNR trade-off of uniform steering**: applying a strictness-increasing vector to all samples improves error detection (TNR) at the cost of correctness certification (TPR), and vice versa for leniency steering.
- Propose **VerifySteer** with two-level adaptivity: (1) **sample-level routing** via a 3-layer MLP correctness probe that selects the steering direction, and (2) **delimiter-level gating** that intervenes only on `\n\n` tokens whose hidden states are misaligned with the selected direction (cosine similarity below threshold ρ).
- Demonstrate **strong efficiency**: matching single-pass inference cost, requiring 4–7x fewer FLOPs than self-consistency while achieving higher or comparable F1.
- Show **orthogonal complementarity** with fine-tuning: applying VerifySteer on top of the fine-tuned FARE-20B further improves F1 from 41.5 to 51.1.

## Methodology

The method operates in two stages:

**Offline stage — Steering vector construction.** From a validation set (subsampled from ActPRM, ~11K samples total), the authors generate 16 verification rollouts per sample using the verifier model. For each erroneous solution, they identify traces that contain both false-acceptance (verdict = -1) and true-rejection (verdict = correct error index) behaviors. They extract the hidden states of the `\n\n` delimiter tokens preceding the relevant verification paragraphs, separating them into $H_{FA}^l$ (false acceptance) and $H_{TR}^l$ (true rejection). The strictness steering vector is $d_{strict}^l = \bar{h}_{TR}^l - \bar{h}_{FA}^l$. An analogous procedure using correct solutions yields $d_{lenient} = \bar{h}_{TA} - \bar{h}_{FR}$.

**Online stage — VerifySteer inference.** At test time: (1) A **correctness probe** $f_\phi$ (3-layer MLP) takes the pooled input-prompt hidden state $h_p$ and outputs a correctness score $q(p, S) \in [0, 1]$. (2) Based on thresholds $\tau_l, \tau_h$, the sample is routed to $d_{strict}$ (likely incorrect), $d_{lenient}$ (likely correct, Bi variant only), or no steering (uncertain region). (3) For each generated `\n\n` token, a **binary gate** $g_k = \mathbb{1}[\text{cossim}(h_k, d_{sel}) < \rho]$ determines whether to intervene. If gated, the hidden state is updated via renormalized additive steering: $h_k' = \|h_k\|_2 \cdot (h_k + \alpha d_{sel}) / \|h_k + \alpha d_{sel}\|_2$.

**Models tested.** Qwen3-1.7B, Qwen3-8B (non-thinking and thinking modes), FARE-20B (fine-tuned from gpt-oss-20B), plus reference results from Qwen3-14B, Llama-3.3-70B, Qwen2.5-72B.

**Benchmarks.** ProcessBench (GSM8K, MATH, OlympiadBench, Omni-MATH subsets) and Hard2Verify (IMO, Putnam-level problems). Metrics: TNR, TPR, and their harmonic mean F1. Inference cost measured in TFLOPs per sample.

## Results & Highlights

- **ProcessBench (Qwen3-1.7B):** VerifySteer-Bi achieves 29.7 overall F1 vs. Cons@4's 26.0 (3.7 F1 gain) at ~3.47 vs. ~12.71 TFLOPs (4x fewer). It surpasses several fine-tuned PRMs (Qwen2.5-Math-7B-Math-Shepherd at 28.9, RLHFlow-PRM-Mistral-8B at 28.4) despite being 4–5x smaller.
- **ProcessBench (Qwen3-8B):** VerifySteer-Bi achieves 72.3 overall F1, surpassing Qwen3-14B's 71.6 and matching Cons@4's 72.2 at 21.23 vs. 84.09 TFLOPs.
- **Hard2Verify (Qwen3-8B-thinking):** VerifySteer achieves 33.4 F1 vs. Cons@8's 31.2 (2.2 F1 gain) at 77.72 vs. 545.19 TFLOPs (7x fewer).
- **Hard2Verify (FARE-20B):** VerifySteer-Bi boosts F1 from 41.5 (FARE baseline) to 51.1, demonstrating strong complementarity with fine-tuning.
- **Ablations:** CAA (uniform activation steering) achieves similar TNR but with large TPR drops, lowering F1. Removing sample-level adaptivity hurts TNR/TPR balance; removing delimiter-level gating reduces TNR precision.
- **Qualitative effect:** Steering transforms superficial CoT into detailed error explanations. Under-critical mistakes (false acceptance) are corrected to identify specific arithmetic/algebraic errors; over-critical mistakes (false rejection based on stylistic issues) are corrected to accept valid reasoning.
- **Layer concentration:** The steering effect concentrates around layer 22 in Qwen3-8B, suggesting verification decision-making is functionally localized to specific mid-layers.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The paragraph-boundary hidden-state signal for verification strictness is a genuine and interesting finding. The selective two-level steering mechanism is a well-motivated extension, though it combines existing ideas (correctness probing from hidden states + contrastive activation addition) rather than introducing fundamentally new techniques.
- **Rigor:** Strong experimental design — multiple models, two benchmarks, comparison against three baseline categories (prompting, self-consistency, activation steering), ablations for each component. The F1 metric (harmonic mean of TPR/TNR) is appropriate for the class-imbalanced setting.
- **Reproducibility:** Open-source code, detailed hyperparameter tables (Table 5), clear prompt templates (Appendix A). The only external dependency is ActPRM for validation data.
- **Significance:** The finding that a steered Qwen3-1.7B can outperform specialized fine-tuned PRMs 4–5x larger is practically significant, especially given the rapid iteration of frontier models that makes fine-tuning quickly obsolete.
- **Red flags:** (1) Evaluated only on mathematical reasoning — generalization to code, scientific reasoning, or natural language verification is unknown. (2) The correctness probe requires training data and may not transfer across model families without retraining. (3) The keyword-based heuristic for identifying acceptance/rejection paragraphs (Table 4) is brittle and may fail on models with different output styles. (4) The paper claims VerifySteer "surpasses" self-consistency, but on Qwen3-8B ProcessBench the gap is only 0.1 F1 (72.3 vs. 72.2), which is essentially a tie. (5) The steering hyperparameters (layer, α, thresholds) are tuned per model and benchmark subset, raising questions about sensitivity and generalizability.

## Strengths

- Clear, interpretable mechanistic finding: verification signal localizes to paragraph-boundary delimiter tokens at specific layers.
- Practically efficient: single-pass inference with minimal overhead (one MLP forward pass + conditional vector addition).
- Addresses a real and timely problem — verifier positivity bias in generative verification.
- Complementary to fine-tuning rather than competing, making it a drop-in enhancement.
- The observation that prompt optimization does not transfer across benchmarks (ProcessBench vs. Hard2Verify) is itself a useful empirical contribution.
- Steered verifiers produce more detailed and accurate CoT explanations, not just flipped verdicts.

## Limitations

- Evaluation is limited to mathematical reasoning; generalization to code verification, scientific claim checking, or multi-hop reasoning is untested.
- The correctness probe requires labeled validation data and per-model retraining — not truly zero-shot.
- Steering hyperparameters are tuned per model and benchmark subset (Table 5); the sensitivity to these choices is not systematically analyzed.
- The keyword-based paragraph identification heuristic is fragile and model-specific.
- On the largest ProcessBench setting (Qwen3-8B), VerifySteer and Cons@4 are essentially tied on F1, with VerifySteer's advantage being primarily computational.
- The mechanistic explanation for *why* the signal concentrates at paragraph boundaries and specific layers remains incomplete — it is demonstrated but not deeply explained.

## Relevance

本文研究的是生成式验证器的行为可控性，通过隐藏状态干预调控验证严格度，与过程级奖励模型（PRM）和 LLM 推理可靠性评估直接相关。VerifySteer 提供了一种无需微调、计算高效的验证器增强方案，对构建更可靠的语言模型推理系统具有实用参考价值，也揭示了模型内部表征中与验证行为相关的可解释结构。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the paragraph-boundary signal generalize beyond math to code verification, scientific reasoning, or natural language fact-checking?
- How robust is VerifySteer to distribution shift — e.g., steering vectors constructed from algebra problems applied to geometry or combinatorics?
- Can the correctness probe be replaced with an unsupervised signal (e.g., hidden-state entropy or Mahalanobis distance) to eliminate the need for labeled validation data?
- What happens when the generator and verifier are the same model (self-verification setting) — does the paragraph-boundary signal still appear and remain steerable?
- Could VerifySteer be applied during verifier training (e.g., as a regularization) rather than only at inference time?
- How does the method interact with test-time compute scaling strategies like parallel sampling or tree search?
