> **一句话总结：** 本文从NTK（神经正切核）谱分析角度揭示GLU结构之所以优于非GLU结构，是因为其门控机制通过Hadamard乘积重塑NTK矩阵，使条件数从O(n/d)降至O(n/d²)，从而加速训练收敛，但对泛化差距影响有限。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides the first theoretical explanation for why GLU (Gated Linear Unit) variants outperform non-GLU counterparts in modern LLMs. By analyzing two-layer networks in the NTK regime, the authors show that GLU's multiplicative gating reshapes the NTK spectrum via a Hadamard product with the data Gram matrix, reducing the condition number from O(n/d) to O(n/d²). This yields faster convergence (explaining the characteristic loss-crossing phenomenon) but does not meaningfully reduce the generalization gap, indicating GLU's primary benefit is optimization speed rather than generalization.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示GLU加速训练的数学本质：** 通过NTK框架证明，GLU的门控机制使NTK矩阵近似为 K ⊙ (XX^⊤/d)，其中K为非GLU的NTK矩阵，XX^⊤/d为数据Gram矩阵。这一Hadamard乘积操作使谱更紧凑。
- **严格证明条件数改善：** 在高斯输入假设下，证明了ReGLU的最大特征值从O(mn/d)降至O(mn/d²)，最小特征值不降反升，条件数从O(n/d)降至O(n/d²)。
- **解释训练中的loss-crossing现象：** 非GLU模型在训练初期因较大λ_max而收敛更快，但后期受限于较小的λ_min，被GLU超越——这一两阶段行为被理论预测并经实验验证。
- **澄清GLU不影响泛化差距：** 在ViT、GPT-2、MLP-Mixer等模型上的实验表明，GLU结构对训练损失与测试损失之间的泛化差距无显著影响，其主要优势在于加速优化。

## Key Contributions

- **Spectral analysis of GLU via NTK:** Shows that the GLU NTK is approximately the non-GLU NTK Hadamard-multiplied by the data Gram matrix (K̃ ≈ K ⊙ XX^⊤/d), making the NTK more diagonally dominant and better conditioned.
- **Rigorous condition number bounds:** Under LeCun initialization with i.i.d. Gaussian inputs, proves that the GLU condition number scales as O(n/d²) versus O(n/d) for non-GLU — a quadratic improvement driven by both a smaller λ_max and a larger λ_min.
- **Gradient angle interpretation:** Shows that GLU enlarges the model gradient angle between different samples, separating them more effectively in gradient feature space, which connects to recent model gradient angle theory (Liu et al., 2025).
- **Two-phase convergence and loss-crossing explanation:** Provides formal propositions showing that non-GLU converges faster early (driven by larger λ_max) but is overtaken by GLU later (driven by larger λ_min), validated across Gaussian data, MNIST, and multiple activation pairs.
- **Empirical generalization gap analysis:** Demonstrates via permutation tests (Energy Distance) that GLU does not significantly alter the generalization gap on ViT (Tiny ImageNet), GPT-2 (FineWeb-Edu), and MLP-Mixer (CIFAR-10), while optimizer choice (SGD vs. AdamW) has a much stronger effect.

## Methodology

The paper studies two-layer networks in the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) regime under LeCun initialization. The core methodology proceeds in three stages:

1. **NTK derivation and spectral decomposition:** For both ReLU (non-GLU) and ReGLU (GLU) two-layer models, the authors derive closed-form NTK expressions using arc-cosine kernel results. They decompose the NTK matrices into three structural components: a data Gram matrix (Wishart-type), a rank-1 update, and a diagonal rectification. The GLU NTK is shown to introduce an additional Hadamard squaring of the Gram matrix component.

2. **Random matrix theory analysis:** Under i.i.d. Gaussian input assumptions, the authors analyze the eigenvalue scaling of each component using the Marchenko-Pastur law, Weyl's inequality, and kernel random matrix theory (El Karoui, 2010). This yields precise asymptotic expressions for λ_max and λ_min of both GLU and non-GLU NTKs.

3. **Training dynamics and generalization experiments:** The spectral analysis is used to predict convergence behavior (Proposition 4.1, Corollary 4.2). The loss-crossing phenomenon is verified on two-layer MLPs with Gaussian data and MNIST. Generalization gap analysis uses scatter plots of (training loss, generalization gap) with permutation tests on MLP-Mixer/CIFAR-10, ViT/Tiny ImageNet, and GPT-2/FineWeb-Edu.

## Results & Highlights

- **Condition number improvement:** NTK condition number drops from O(n/d) to O(n/d²) with GLU, verified both theoretically and numerically across varying input dimensions (Fig. 2).
- **Real model validation:** GLU variants consistently reduce the condition number of NTK matrices in ViT and GPT-2 FFN blocks across ReLU, GELU, and SiLU activations (Fig. 3).
- **Loss-crossing phenomenon:** All GLU variants (ReGLU, GEGLU, SwiGLU) exhibit a characteristic training pattern where non-GLU initially leads but is overtaken, consistent with the two-phase convergence theory (Fig. 6).
- **No generalization gap benefit:** Statistical tests (p ≥ 0.05) confirm GLU does not meaningfully reduce the generalization gap, while switching from SGD to AdamW has a much stronger effect (Fig. 7).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. While GLU's empirical superiority is well-known (SwiGLU used in LLaMA, Qwen, DeepSeek), this is the first work to provide a rigorous NTK-based spectral explanation. The Hadamard product structure K̃ ≈ K ⊙ (XX^⊤/d) is a clean and insightful result.
- **Rigor:** Strong theoretical grounding with proofs relying on established random matrix theory results. The gap between the two-layer NTK regime and deep Transformer architectures is acknowledged. The informal theorem statement in the main text is backed by formal versions in the appendix.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. All experiments use standard architectures (ViT, GPT-2, MLP-Mixer) and datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, Tiny ImageNet). The theoretical predictions are numerically verified. However, no code repository is mentioned.
- **Significance:** Important for the deep learning theory community. Provides actionable insight: GLU's benefit is purely optimization-related, not generalization-related. This has practical implications for architecture design and training budget allocation.
- **Red flags:**
  - The analysis is limited to the kernel (lazy training) regime, which may not fully capture the dynamics of practical deep networks that operate in the feature learning regime.
  - The generalization gap analysis is purely empirical; a theoretical explanation for why GLU doesn't help generalization is lacking.
  - The two-layer network assumption, while standard for NTK analysis, is far from modern deep architectures. Whether the condition number insight transfers to 50+ layer Transformers is not proven.

## Strengths

- Clean, intuitive main result (Hadamard product reshaping of NTK) that connects well to empirical observations.
- Comprehensive empirical validation across multiple architectures, activations, and datasets.
- The gradient angle interpretation provides an alternative, complementary perspective to the spectral analysis.
- Honest about limitations — clearly states that GLU's benefit is optimization-only, not generalization.

## Limitations

- NTK/lazy training regime may not reflect practical training dynamics of deep networks with feature learning.
- Two-layer network assumption limits direct applicability to modern deep architectures.
- No theoretical explanation for why GLU fails to reduce the generalization gap.
- No code release mentioned, which may hinder exact reproduction.
- The loss-crossing phenomenon is sensitive to learning rate — at large learning rates, the crossing becomes less visible, which somewhat undermines the practical salience of this theoretical prediction.

## Relevance

本文从NTK谱分析的角度为GLU结构提供了理论解释，与对Transformer架构设计和训练动力学的研究兴趣高度相关。条件数作为优化难度的度量指标，为理解不同FFN结构的训练效率差异提供了统一视角。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the condition number advantage of GLU persist in the feature learning (non-lazy) regime, or is it an artifact of the kernel approximation?
- Can the spectral analysis be extended to multi-layer networks, perhaps using the conjugate kernel or mean-field theory?
- If GLU's benefit is purely optimization speed, are there alternative architectural modifications that could improve both optimization and generalization simultaneously?
- How does the Hadamard product structure interact with normalization layers (LayerNorm, RMSNorm) commonly used alongside GLU in practice?
- Could the condition number perspective be used as a design principle for novel gating mechanisms beyond GLU?
