> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Divide et Calibra 方法，通过向量量化将隐空间划分为 Voronoi 区域，并利用共享码字依赖因子的组合式参数化来学习异构局部校准映射，在低密度区域显著改善了多类分类的局部校准性能（LCE 降低 25%–60%），同时保持全局校准竞争力。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes a compositional approach to multiclass calibration using Vector Quantization to partition the latent space into Voronoi cells, then constructs region-specific calibration maps from shared codeword-dependent factors via an "indexed parameterization trick." This reduces parameters from O(|Y|²·|C|^w) to O(|C|·|Y|+w), enabling reliable calibration even in sparse regions. Achieves 25–60% LCE reductions over the best local calibration baselines on CIFAR-10/100 and TissueMNIST while maintaining competitive global calibration.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了一种基于向量量化（VQ）的组合式多类局部校准框架，将隐空间离散化为 Voronoi 区域，实现区域感知的校准建模。
- 引入了索引参数化技巧（indexed parameterization trick），通过共享码字依赖的 sender/receiver 校准码本，从可复用的因子组合出区域特定的校准映射，大幅减少参数量。
- 从理论上证明了校准参数的收敛速率取决于码字的使用频率而非区域密度（Theorem 2），为低密度区域的校准稳定性提供了统计保证。
- 在多个基准数据集上显著优于现有局部校准方法，尤其在低支持度（low-support）区域表现突出。

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a compositional formulation of local multiclass calibration where region-specific calibration maps are built from shared codeword-dependent factors, instantiated via Vector Quantization (VQ) that induces a Voronoi tessellation of the latent space.
- Introduces an indexed parameterization trick that reuses discrete quantization indices as selectors of calibration parameters (sender/receiver codebooks A and B), reducing parameters from |Y|²×|C|^w to 2×|C|×|Y|+w while enabling compositional generalization across exponentially many regions.
- Proves two statistical results: (1) local consistency of the compositional estimator (Theorem 1, Op(N^{-1/2}) rate), and (2) frequency-weighted convergence showing calibration error depends on codeword frequency rather than region density (Theorem 2).
- Empirically demonstrates 25–60% reductions in Local Calibration Error (LCE) over the strongest local calibration baseline (Local Nets) across CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and TissueMNIST, with the most pronounced gains in low-support regions.

## Methodology

The method, called **Divide et Calibra**, operates in two stages:

**Stage 1 — Quantization-Aware Representation Learning.** Given a frozen backbone encoder, the latent vector z = E(x) is partitioned into w contiguous segments ("slots") of dimension d. A shared codebook C = {c₁, ..., c_{|C|}} maps each slot to its nearest codeword via ℓ₂ distance, yielding a discrete index sequence s = (s⁽¹⁾, ..., s⁽ʷ⁾). This induces a Voronoi tessellation of the latent space with |C|^w cells.

**Stage 2 — Region-Aware Calibration.** The key innovation is the indexed parameterization of Dirichlet concentrations. Instead of learning independent calibration maps per Voronoi cell, two auxiliary codebooks A (receiver) and B (sender) are learned, each containing |C| vectors of dimension |Y|. The index sequence s selects rows from A and B to form region-specific weight matrices, which are combined via a bilinear form with slot-dependent scaling σ²:

α(V) := ϕ(A(V)ᵀ diag(σ²) B(V))

where ϕ is a positive monotone link function. This produces a Dirichlet-calibrated posterior via Bayes' rule (Proposition 2 shows the resulting log-posterior is linear in log p̂). The sender codebook B captures how confidence mass is "emitted" per class, while A captures how it is "received," enabling structured class-dependent redistribution of evidence.

The codebook C is trained via Exponential Moving Average (EMA), and calibration parameters (A, B, σ²) are optimized by minimizing cross-entropy on a held-out calibration set.

## Results & Highlights

- **Local Calibration (LCE):** VQ achieves LCE ≈ 0.0059 on CIFAR-10 (~25% improvement over Local Nets), LCE ≈ 0.0017 on CIFAR-100 (~29% improvement), and LCE ≈ 0.0083 on TissueMNIST (~60% improvement).
- **Low-Support Regions:** When analyzing calibration error vs. Effective Sample Size (ESS), VQ consistently achieves the lowest LCE across the entire ESS spectrum, with the largest gains in the sparsest bins. This directly validates the theoretical claim that parameter sharing via codewords stabilizes estimation in sparse regions.
- **Global Calibration (ECCE, NLL):** VQ remains competitive with strong global baselines like Structured Matrix Scaling (SM), improving over other local methods (KCal, Local Nets, ProCal) on CIFAR-10 and TissueMNIST while matching on CIFAR-100.
- **Architecture Robustness:** Results are consistent across ResNet, ConvNeXt, and ViT backbones (appendix).
- **Parameter Efficiency:** With w=64 and |C|=64, the method uses only 2×64×|Y|+64 parameters for the calibration layer, versus 64⁶⁴ possible regions.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core idea of combining VQ with compositional calibration parameterization is creative and well-motivated. The indexed parameterization trick is elegant, reducing an exponential parameter space to linear. However, VQ itself is not new (VQ-VAE, etc.), and the application to calibration is incremental rather than paradigm-shifting.
- **Rigor:** Strong theoretical contribution with formal consistency guarantees (Theorem 1) and frequency-weighted convergence rates (Theorem 2). The proofs are in the appendix. The assumptions (A1–A9) are stated clearly, though they are somewhat restrictive (conditional on fixed quantization assignments, local convexity near stationary points).
- **Reproducibility:** Code is available. Hyperparameters (w=64, |C|=64, diag(σ²)=I) are clearly specified. Five-seed averaging is used throughout.
- **Significance:** The LCE improvements are substantial, but the practical impact depends on whether local calibration errors actually matter in downstream decision-making. The paper acknowledges it does not uniformly outperform global baselines on ECCE/NLL.
- **Red Flags:** (1) The method requires a frozen representation space — performance depends on how well that space captures calibration-relevant locality. (2) Experiments are limited to image classification (plus one tabular dataset in appendix); no NLP or larger-scale tasks. (3) The choice of diag(σ²)=I is a simplification justified by overfitting concerns, but it removes the slot-dependent scaling that is part of the method's design, raising questions about how much of the theoretical framework is actually leveraged.

## Strengths

- Elegant compositional parameterization that achieves both statistical stability and parameter efficiency.
- Strong theoretical grounding with formal convergence guarantees tied to codeword frequency.
- Convincing empirical gains in local calibration, especially in low-density regions.
- The two-stage training procedure is practical and decouples representation learning from calibration.
- Thorough experimental evaluation across multiple datasets, baselines, and architectures.

## Limitations

- Relies on a frozen encoder; performance is contingent on the quality of the latent space for capturing calibration-relevant structure.
- Does not uniformly outperform global methods on standard metrics (ECCE, NLL); the advantage is specific to local calibration.
- Experiments are limited to image classification and one tabular dataset; generalization to NLP, large-scale, or distribution-shift settings is unverified.
- The simplified diag(σ²)=I setting means the full slot-dependent scaling mechanism is not empirically validated.
- No evaluation of downstream decision-making impact or out-of-distribution robustness.

## Relevance

本文直接关注多类分类中的局部概率校准问题，属于不确定性估计与模型可靠性的核心研究方向。其通过向量量化实现参数共享来解决低密度区域校准的思路，对需要可靠概率输出的高风险应用场景（如医疗诊断）具有实用价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does VQ-based calibration interact with distribution shift? Does parameter sharing via codewords help or hurt calibration under covariate shift?
- Can the method be extended to end-to-end training using differentiable VQ (e.g., DiveQ), and would this improve or degrade calibration quality?
- What is the sensitivity of local calibration improvements to the codebook size |C| and number of slots w in very high-dimensional latent spaces (e.g., LLM hidden states)?
- Does improved local calibration translate to measurable gains in downstream decision-making tasks (e.g., selective classification, rejection learning)?
- How does the method perform on calibration in the presence of label noise or ambiguous annotations?
