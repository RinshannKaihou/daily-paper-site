GRPO silently wastes 28–45% of training batches on zero gradients; AVSPO recovers them.

Advantage Collapse in Group Relative Policy Optimization: Diagnosis and Mitigation

Xixiang He, Qiyao Sun, Ao Cheng, Xingming Li, Xuanyu Ji, Hailun Lu, Runke Huang, Qingyong Hu 2026-05-20 ICML 2026

RLVR GRPO LLM Reasoning Policy Optimization Training Diagnostics

Key Metric

58–63% collapse ↓
+4–6 acc ↑

Across 6 benchmarks, 6 model scales (0.5B–14B). ACR reduced from 0.28–0.45 to 0.11–0.18.

Why This Matters

ACR deploys immediately as a zero-overhead training monitor; AVSPO is a plug-and-play GRPO fix for RLVR practitioners. Early ACR explains 62% of final performance variance.

Critical Edge

Does not address the root cause of collapse — sparse binary rewards — only mitigates its gradient-level consequence. Generalization beyond Qwen2.5 and math reasoning is untested.

Methodology

  1. Problem: In GRPO, intra-group advantage normalization yields Âᵢ = (rᵢ − μ_R)/(σ_R + ε). When all rewards are identical (σ_R → 0), every advantage is zero — gradient vanishes entirely.
  2. ACR metric: ACR = (1/N) Σ 𝕀(σ_{Rⱼ} < τ). Zero-overhead diagnostic. Early ACR₁₀₀ predicts final accuracy with R² = 0.617 across 245 configs.
  3. AVSPO algorithm: When ACR exceeds adaptive threshold, inject K virtual reward samples into collapsed groups. K = max(1, min(G, G · ACR^0.5)). Virtual samples shift the normalization baseline — they don't contribute policy gradient terms.
  4. Stratified assignment: For all-correct groups, rewards decay from r_obs; for all-incorrect groups, positive anchors create contrast against zeros.
  5. Adaptive thresholding: τ_adapt increases during improvement, decreases during stagnation. Reaches target accuracy 22% faster than best fixed threshold.

Results

  • Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B: 58.6% → 67.2% on MATH-500 (+8.6 points)
  • Consistent gains: +4–6 points across all 7 benchmarks and all 6 model scales
  • Baselines outperformed: +2.9% over DCPO, +6.8% over INTUITOR, +8.9% over RENT
  • Predictive power: Final Accuracy = 51.4 − 29.6 × ACR₁₀₀ (R² = 0.617, r = −0.785, p < 10⁻⁸)
  • Efficiency: 100% sample utilization at 1.0× cost vs. filtering (62.4%) or DAPO (1.8×)
  • Out-of-domain: MMLU-Pro scores maintained or slightly improved
  • Statistical: Multi-seed (n=5), all improvements p < 0.05

Strengths

  • Identifies a fundamental, previously underappreciated failure mode in one of the most widely used RLVR algorithms
  • ACR diagnostic is zero-overhead, immediately deployable, and strongly predictive of final performance
  • AVSPO is lightweight — no extra LLM forward passes, maintains original O(NG) complexity, compatible with any GRPO variant
  • Excellent experimental design: controlled comparisons, ablations, sensitivity analysis, mechanism isolation
  • Clear theoretical grounding with formal bias-variance analysis and proofs in appendices

Limitations

  • Evaluated only on math reasoning with binary verifiers; continuous rewards, multi-turn, and code generation are untested
  • Limited to Qwen2.5 family; cross-family generalization (Llama, Gemma) unknown
  • Small training data: 500 problems, single epoch — scaling behavior unclear
  • Virtual sample bias: cumulative effect over very long training runs (10K+ steps) not characterized
  • Does not address root cause (sparse binary rewards), only mitigates gradient-level symptom
  • Competition-level benchmarks (AIME) show modest gains — capacity limits may dominate at high difficulty

Open Questions

  • How does AVSPO interact with process reward models (PRMs) providing dense step-level rewards — would collapse diminish or persist at the step level?
  • What happens in non-math domains (code generation, scientific reasoning, tool use) where verification is less binary?
  • Can ACR itself drive adaptive curriculum — dynamically adjusting training data difficulty or group size to minimize collapse?
  • How do cumulative bias effects of virtual sample injection manifest over 10K+ step training runs?
  • Is there an optimal difficulty curriculum that balances problem difficulty to minimize ACR while maintaining learning signal diversity?
