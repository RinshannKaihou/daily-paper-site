Markovian Circuit Tracing for Transformer State Dynamics

Abdullah X · 2026-05-20 · arXiv 2605.20824

Recovered state centroids beat true-state centroids at forcing counterfactual predictions — suggesting transformers internalize predictive belief states, not discrete labels.

Key Metric
0.196 → 0.053
KL divergence to exact HMM counterfactual after state-forcing patch at resid_post_1, position 20. Δ = 0.143, beating all 5 controls including true-state centroid (0.074).
Why This Matters
Provides the first exact ground-truth benchmark for probing, transition recovery, and causal intervention in a single pipeline. The belief-state finding reframes what "internal state" means in transformers.
Critical Edge
Only 275k-param, 2-layer models on 3–6 state synthetic HMMs (vocab 5–8, seq len 64). Orders of magnitude below real-world complexity. Clusters fit on validation data inflates intervention scores.

Methodology

  • Task: Six HMM families (easy-separable, ambiguous-emission, persistent, high-entropy, 3-state, 6-state) × 3 seeds = 18 controlled experiments. Seq len 64, vocab 5–8.
  • Model: 2-layer causal transformer, width 128, 4 heads, MLP 256, ~275k params.
  • MCT pipeline — four tests: (1) Belief recovery via linear ridge probe → Bayesian belief vector. (2) Transition recovery via K-means over activations → compare to ground-truth transition matrix. (3) Markov order test (order-0/1/2) via held-out NLL. (4) State-forcing: patch recovered centroid into activation, measure KL to exact forced-state target.
  • Controls: Wrong-state centroid, mean activation, random activation, shuffled-label centroid, true-state centroid.
  • Sweeps: Cluster counts M ∈ {2,3,4,5,6,8,10}, layers from embedding through final LN.

Strengths & Limitations

  • Exemplary experimental control — exact ground truth for every quantity, 5 distinct intervention controls.
  • Honest reporting — clearly states where MCT fails (ambiguous, 6-state, high-entropy).
  • Belief-state insight — recovered centroid > true-state centroid is conceptually important for the field.
  • Benchmark has headroom — belief K-means consistently beats residual K-means, allowing evaluation of future extractors.
  • Scale: 275k params only — transfer unknown.
  • Synthetic-to-real gap is enormous (3–6 states vs. natural language).
  • Simple extractor (K-means); no SAE or learned methods explored.
  • Validation-set fitting of clusters partially compromises evaluation.

Open Questions

  1. Does MCT transfer to 1B+ parameter transformers on real or more complex synthetic tasks?
  2. Can learned extractors (SAEs, VQ-VAE) close the gap between residual K-means and belief K-means?
  3. Does the recovered-centroid > true-state-centroid finding hold across architectures? What does it imply for how we define "internal states"?
  4. Could state-forcing be adapted to discover latent structure in natural language models via constructed grammatical tasks?
  5. Would continuous representations (GMMs over activations) work better than K-means for easy-separable regimes?
  6. How does patch position (fixed at token 20) affect results?
