> **一句话总结：** AVSD提出了一种多视角自适应自蒸馏方法，通过分离多个特权信息视角的共识信号和视角特异性残差信号，并用门控机制选择性地融合残差，在数学竞赛和代码生成基准上一致性地超越单视角自蒸馏和GRPO基线。

> **TL;DR:** AVSD is a multi-view self-distillation framework that constructs token-level supervision by separating cross-view consensus (geometric mean of teacher distributions) from view-specific residual signals, then gating the residual to prevent it from overriding the consensus direction. On AIME24/25, HMMT25, Codeforces, and LiveCodeBench, it consistently outperforms single-view self-distillation and GRPO by 2-3% Avg@8 across Qwen3-4B/8B and DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B, while reducing incorrect-sign token credit from 12.3% (single-view) to 2.9%.

## 核心贡献

- 提出多视角自蒸馏框架AVSD，不再依赖单一特权信息视角（如完整解答、最终答案或部分推理），而是同时利用多种特权信息视角构建更鲁棒的token级学习信号。
- 设计了几何共识目标（捕捉跨视角一致支持）和算术边际目标（捕捉任一视角的强支持），并将二者之差定义为跨视角残差。
- 提出两分量门控机制（对齐分量+幅值分量），确保残差信号仅在方向一致且幅值成比例时才被加入，防止残差翻转共识更新方向。
- 在数学竞赛（AIME24/25、HMMT25）和代码生成（Codeforces、LiveCodeBench v6）基准上，AVSD在Qwen3-4B/8B和DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B上一致性地取得最优Avg@8。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces AVSD, the first multi-view on-policy self-distillation framework that aggregates multiple privileged information views (e.g., full solution, partial solution, final answer) rather than committing to a single teacher context.
- Derives two pooled targets — geometric consensus (intersection support across views) and arithmetic marginal (union support from any view) — and formalizes their gap as the cross-view residual.
- Designs a two-component gate (alignment × magnitude ratio) that bounds the residual to never exceed the consensus magnitude, guaranteeing the update direction is never reversed by view-specific noise.
- Demonstrates consistent improvements of 2.2–3.1% Avg@8 over the strongest baselines on math competition benchmarks and 2.4% on code benchmarks across three model families.

## Methodology

AVSD operates within the on-policy self-distillation paradigm: the student generates a rollout, and the same model conditioned on privileged information serves as the teacher. The key innovation is using M privileged views simultaneously.

For each student-generated token, M teacher distributions are computed (one per view). These are aggregated via two pooled targets:
- **Geometric consensus target** (q^G): the normalized geometric mean of view-conditioned teachers. It emphasizes tokens jointly supported across all views and equals the distribution minimizing average reverse KL to the teacher family. Its advantage A^G_t(v) is simply the mean per-view advantage.
- **Arithmetic marginal target** (q^A): the arithmetic mean of view-conditioned teachers. It preserves strong support from any single view but is sensitive to view-specific artifacts.

The **cross-view residual** J_t(v) = log q^A_t(v) - log q̃^G_t(v) ≥ 0 captures the gap between permissive and conservative targets. The **gated reconstructed advantage** is:

  Ã_t(v) = A^G_t(v) + λ_t(v) · J_t(v)

where the gate λ_t(v) = C_t(v) · |A^G_t(v)| / (|A^G_t(v)| + J_t(v) + ε) combines:
1. **Alignment component** C_t(v): ratio of average signed advantage to average absolute advantage across views. High when views agree in direction, low when they conflict.
2. **Magnitude component** R_t(v): ensures the residual cannot dominate or reverse the consensus direction. Proven: λ_t(v) · J_t(v) ≤ |A^G_t(v)|.

The training objective is standard reverse-KL distillation to the reconstructed target q★. Overhead is modest since additional view-conditioned evaluations are prefill-only forward passes sharing the same rollout.

Three privileged views are used for math: full solution, partial solution (initial reasoning steps), and final answer. For code: reference implementation, algorithmic hint, and execution feedback.

## Results & Highlights

- **Qwen3-4B**: AVSD achieves 76.2% AIME24, 68.3% AIME25, 44.2% HMMT25, 65.6% Codeforces, 45.4% LiveCodeBench — improving over OPSD by 2.9%, 2.9%, 0.9%, 2.0%, 0.2% respectively.
- **Qwen3-8B**: AVSD achieves 75.4% AIME24, 69.6% AIME25, 47.1% HMMT25, 65.8% Codeforces, 52.4% LiveCodeBench — improving over the strongest baseline (GRPO) by 2.9%, 2.5%, 4.1%, 3.0%, 2.5%.
- **DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B**: AVSD improves over OPSD by 2.5% on AIME24, 1.7% on AIME25, 2.8% on HMMT25, 3.8% on Codeforces, 1.5% on LiveCodeBench.
- Ablations show neither consensus-only nor arithmetic-only targets are uniformly optimal; the gated combination is necessary.
- Token-level analysis: AVSD assigns incorrect-sign credit to only 2.9% of high-impact tokens (vs. 12.3% for OPSD, 29.6% no-signal rate for GRPO).
- Scaling views from 1→3 improves AIME24 by 2.9% and AIME25 by 2.8% on Qwen3-4B; diminishing returns at 4 views.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** Multi-view self-distillation with principled consensus/residual decomposition is a genuine conceptual advance. The gate's boundedness guarantee (residual cannot flip direction) is clean and well-motivated.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Formal derivation from reverse-KL objective; geometric target shown to minimize average reverse KL to teacher family; arithmetic target shown to minimize average forward KL. Provable bound on residual contribution.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Code publicly available. Uses standard benchmarks (AIME, HMMT, Codeforces, LiveCodeBench) and open models (Qwen3, DeepSeek-R1-Distill). Training on OpenThoughts dataset.
- **Significance: High.** Addresses a real problem (choosing privileged information type) that the community has largely sidestepped. Consistent gains across models, scales, and domains.
- **Red flags:**
  - Gains over baselines are moderate (2-3%), though consistent. The absolute improvement is not transformative but meaningful given the strong baselines.
  - The method adds M× teacher forward passes per training step — the paper claims "modest overhead" but does not report wall-clock training times or GPU-hours.
  - Limited to reasoning tasks with verifiable answers; unclear how AVSD generalizes to open-ended generation, dialogue, or tool-use.
  - The privileged views are somewhat ad-hoc (full solution, partial solution, answer) — the framework is general but the instantiation relies on dataset-specific structure.
  - No comparison with ensemble distillation or other multi-teacher baselines beyond arithmetic/geometric pooling ablations.

## Strengths

- Principled theoretical grounding with provable guarantees on the gate's boundedness.
- Consistent improvements across three model families, two task domains (math and code), and six benchmarks.
- The consensus/residual decomposition is elegant and interpretable — provides clear intuition for when and why multi-view aggregation helps.
- Token-level analysis demonstrating more reliable credit assignment (2.9% error rate vs. 12.3%) is compelling evidence.
- View-scaling experiments show the method benefits from additional views with diminishing returns.

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on reasoning tasks (math, code); generalization to broader NLP tasks is untested.
- Wall-clock training overhead and computational cost are not reported.
- View construction is dataset-specific and requires careful engineering.
- Moderate absolute gains (2-3%) — meaningful but not a paradigm shift.
- The gate has a tunable ε for numerical stability; sensitivity to this hyperparameter is not analyzed.
- Relies on the reverse-KL objective; applicability to other distribution-matching losses is noted but not validated.

## Relevance

本文与LLM训练方法论直接相关：AVSD提出了一种新的on-policy训练信号构造方式，通过多视角特权信息的共识-残差分解来提升训练稳定性和学习信号质量。虽然不直接涉及训练稳定性监控或硬件级可靠性，但其对token级信用分配可靠性的分析（将错误符号率从12.3%降至2.9%）与LLM训练可观测性和鲁棒性研究有方法论上的交叉。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does AVSD interact with RLVR methods — can the gated residual mechanism be combined with outcome-level rewards for hybrid dense+sparse supervision?
- What is the wall-clock training overhead of M view-conditioned teacher evaluations compared to single-view self-distillation and GRPO?
- Can AVSD be extended to settings where privileged views are learned or discovered rather than manually constructed from dataset structure?
- How sensitive is the gate to the choice of ε and to the number/quality of views when some views are noisy or uninformative?
- Does the consensus/residual decomposition apply to multi-teacher distillation with different model architectures, not just different conditioning on the same model?
