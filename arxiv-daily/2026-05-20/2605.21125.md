> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了GRPO训练中"优势崩塌"问题（组内奖励完全相同时梯度消失），提出了ACR诊断指标和AVSPO算法（注入虚拟奖励样本），在0.5B-14B模型的数学推理任务上将崩塌率降低58-63%，准确率提升4-6个百分点。

> **TL;DR:** Identifies "advantage collapse" in GRPO — when all samples in a group get identical binary rewards, gradients vanish entirely (affecting 28–45% of batches). Proposes ACR (a zero-overhead diagnostic metric that explains 62% of final performance variance from early training) and AVSPO (injects virtual reward samples adaptively). Across 0.5B–14B Qwen2.5 models on 6 math benchmarks, AVSPO reduces collapse by 58–63% and improves accuracy by 4–6 points over GRPO, without extra LLM rollouts. Published at ICML 2026.

## 核心贡献

- **定义并量化了"优势崩塌"现象**：GRPO中当组内所有样本获得相同二值奖励时，优势值全部归零、梯度消失。在0.5B-14B模型中28-45%的训练batch存在此问题，且常规指标（loss、accuracy）无法检测。
- **提出ACR（Advantage Collapse Rate）诊断指标**：零额外开销地实时监控崩塌比例，早期ACR可解释62%的最终性能方差（R²=0.617），能在准确率下降前识别问题配置。
- **提出AVSPO算法**：基于ACR实时监测，自适应注入虚拟奖励样本以恢复奖励方差，使原本浪费的batch产生有效梯度。无需额外LLM前向传播，保持O(NG)复杂度。
- **全面实验验证**：在6个数学推理benchmark上，AVSPO将崩塌率从28-45%降至11-18%，准确率提升4-6个百分点，且在MMLU-Pro上保持泛化能力。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes the "advantage collapse" failure mode in GRPO**: when within-group reward variance vanishes (all samples correct or all incorrect), all advantages collapse to zero, making the policy gradient completely ineffective regardless of individual response quality. Affects 28–45% of training batches across model scales.
- **Introduces ACR (Advantage Collapse Rate)**: the first diagnostic metric for quantifying gradient ineffectiveness in group-based policy optimization. Zero computational overhead (monitors reward statistics already computed). Early-stage ACR₍₁₀₀₎ explains 62% of final performance variance (R²=0.617, r=-0.785, p<10⁻⁸) across 245 configurations.
- **Proposes AVSPO (Adaptive Virtual Sample Policy Optimization)**: a plug-and-play GRPO extension that injects synthetic reward samples into collapsed groups' normalization statistics. Virtual samples don't contribute to policy gradients — they only shift the reward baseline to create non-zero advantages. Guided by real-time ACR with adaptive thresholding.
- **Comprehensive empirical validation across 6 model scales (0.5B–14B)** on 6 math reasoning benchmarks plus MMLU-Pro for out-of-domain generalization.

## Methodology

**Problem setup**: Mathematical reasoning framed as a contextual bandit under RLVR. Binary rewards from automated verifiers. GRPO computes advantages via intra-group normalization: Âᵢ = (rᵢ - μ_R) / (σ_R + ε). When all rewards are identical (σ_R → 0), Âᵢ = 0 for all samples.

**ACR metric**: For a batch of N question-group pairs, ACR = (1/N) Σ 𝕀(σ_{Rⱼ} < τ). Quantifies fraction of groups with negligible gradient signal. τ = 10⁻⁶ for numerical precision.

**AVSPO algorithm**:
1. Compute ACR at each training step (zero overhead).
2. When ACR exceeds adaptive threshold τ_adapt, inject K virtual reward samples into collapsed groups. K = max(1, min(G, G · ACR^α)) with α=0.5 (square-root scaling).
3. Virtual rewards use stratified assignment: for all-correct groups, rewards decay from r_obs; for all-incorrect groups, positive anchor rewards create contrast against zeros.
4. Recompute advantages using augmented reward statistics (μ'_R, σ'_R). Virtual samples only shift normalization — no ∇θ log πθ terms for them.
5. Adaptive threshold adjusts based on training progress: τ_adapt increases when training improves (less intervention needed), decreases during stagnation.

**Theoretical grounding**: Proves that sum of squared advantages scales as Gσ²_R/(σ_R+ε)², justifying ACR as a diagnostic. Analyzes bias-variance tradeoff: virtual augmentation introduces bounded bias but recovers gradient signal from otherwise wasted batches.

## Results & Highlights

- **ACR reduction**: AVSPO reduces ACR from 0.28–0.45 (GRPO) to 0.11–0.18, a **58–63% relative reduction** across all model scales.
- **Accuracy gains**: Consistent **+4 to +6 percentage points** improvement over GRPO across all 7 benchmarks. Example: Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B goes from 58.6% to 67.2% on MATH-500.
- **Outperforming baselines**: +2.9% over DCPO, +6.8% over INTUITOR, +8.9% over RENT on average.
- **ACR predicts performance**: Linear fit Final Accuracy = 51.4 - 29.6 × ACR₍₁₀₀₎ with R²=0.617 across 245 configurations.
- **Sensitivity analysis**: ACR responds monotonically to model size, optimally to temperature (T=0.3–0.5) and group size (G=6–8), and shows U-shaped relationship with problem difficulty.
- **Ablation validates stratified assignment**: Outperforms random (+3.5%), fixed partial (+4.9%), and exponential decay (+5.6%) strategies; stratified achieves +8.6%.
- **Adaptive vs fixed thresholding**: Adaptive reaches 60% accuracy 22% faster than best fixed threshold with 1.8% higher final accuracy.
- **Augmentation beats filtering**: AVSPO uses 100% of generated samples at 1.0× cost, vs. filtering (62.4% utilization) or DAPO (1.8× cost).
- **Out-of-domain generalization preserved**: MMLU-Pro scores maintained or slightly improved over GRPO.
- **Statistical significance**: Multi-seed validation (n=5) confirms all improvements (p<0.05).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High**. The identification and formalization of advantage collapse as a named, measurable failure mode is novel. ACR as a zero-overhead diagnostic is elegant and practically useful. The virtual sample injection mechanism is simple but well-motivated.
- **Rigor: High**. Systematic evaluation across model scales, difficulty levels, group sizes, and temperatures. Ablation studies isolate each component's contribution. Theoretical analysis grounds the approach. Statistical significance testing via multi-seed experiments.
- **Reproducibility: High**. Code released, standard framework (TRL), standard models (Qwen2.5), clear hyperparameter specifications. All training uses 500 steps on 500 problems — modest compute requirements.
- **Significance: High for RLVR practitioners**. The finding that 28–45% of GRPO batches produce zero gradients is striking and underappreciated. ACR provides immediate practical value as a training monitor. AVSPO is a low-cost fix with consistent gains.
- **Red flags**:
  - Training data is only 500 problems from MATH Level 3 — unclear if gains hold with larger, more diverse training sets or multi-epoch training.
  - All experiments use Qwen2.5 family models; generalization to other model families (Llama, Gemma, etc.) is untested.
  - The "virtual samples" mechanism introduces bias into advantage estimates — the paper acknowledges this but the long-term training dynamics of this bias are not fully explored.
  - MMLU-Pro improvement is marginal (~1 point), suggesting the approach is most impactful for verifiable-reward domains rather than general RLHF.
  - The adaptive threshold has several hyperparameters (η, α, τ⁽⁰⁾_adapt, r_anchor) — the paper shows robustness but the mechanism adds complexity.

## Strengths

- Identifies a fundamental, previously underappreciated failure mode in one of the most widely used RLVR algorithms.
- ACR diagnostic is zero-overhead, immediately deployable, and strongly predictive of final performance.
- AVSPO is lightweight (no extra LLM forward passes), maintains original computational complexity, and is compatible with any GRPO variant.
- Excellent experimental design: controlled comparisons, ablation studies, sensitivity analysis, mechanism isolation.
- Clear theoretical grounding with bias-variance analysis.

## Limitations

- Evaluated only on mathematical reasoning with binary verifiers; effectiveness with continuous rewards, multi-turn tasks, or code generation is unknown.
- Limited to Qwen2.5 model family; cross-family generalization untested.
- Small training dataset (500 problems, single epoch); scaling behavior unclear.
- Virtual sample injection introduces bias whose cumulative effect over very long training runs is not characterized.
- The approach does not address the root cause of collapse (sparse binary rewards), only mitigates its gradient-level consequence.
- Competition-level benchmarks (AIME) show modest gains, suggesting capacity limits dominate at high difficulty.

## Relevance

本文对使用GRPO进行LLM推理能力训练的研究者具有直接实用价值。ACR指标可作为训练监控工具立即部署，AVSPO提供了一个低成本即插即用的改进方案。对于关注RLVR训练效率和稳定性的研究方向的读者尤为相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does AVSPO interact with process reward models (PRMs) that provide dense step-level rewards — would the collapse problem diminish, or does it persist at the step level?
- What happens when AVSPO is applied to non-math domains (code generation, scientific reasoning, tool use) where verification is less binary?
- Can ACR be used as a reward signal itself — e.g., to dynamically adjust training data difficulty or group size during training to minimize collapse?
- How do the cumulative bias effects of virtual sample injection manifest over much longer training runs (e.g., 10K+ steps)?
- Is there an optimal curriculum that balances problem difficulty to minimize ACR while maintaining learning signal diversity?
