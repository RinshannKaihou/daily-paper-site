Scale-Calibrated Median-of-Means for Robust Distributed Principal Component Analysis

Kisung You — Baruch College / CUNY 2026-05-20 arXiv 2605.20681
distributed-PCA robust-statistics Grassmann-manifold median-of-means scale-calibration
Key Finding
The product-manifold MoM estimator is locally equivalent to a scaled spatial median of node influence errors—converting a nonlinear manifold aggregation problem into tractable Euclidean spatial median theory. The scale parameter α directly encodes eigengap-driven uncertainty.
Key Metric
0.01239
Subspace error on 1.3M-cell scRNA-seq (K=100, r=10) — 26% lower than projector averaging (0.01667)
Why This Matters
Provides a principled, robust framework for aggregating PCA estimates from distributed machines where some nodes may be corrupted. Directly applicable to federated learning, distributed statistics, and any system producing heterogeneous product-valued summaries (mean + subspace, mean + covariance).
Critical Edge
Theory assumes fixed dimension (p fixed, n → ∞). The eigengap perturbation expansion may break down in modern p ≫ n settings. Equal-size nodes assumed; no code repository provided.

Methodology

Scaled Product Metric

Distance on R^p × Gr(r,p) weighted by α: balances Euclidean mean error vs. Grassmann subspace error in a single objective.

Influence Expansion

Node-level PCA error decomposes into mean perturbation plus eigengap-weighted subspace perturbation via B(X)_aj terms.

Scale Calibration

Block-noise rule: α = 2τ_U / (τ_μ + τ_U). Robust empirical version uses median radial node scales. Adapts to eigengap and PCA rank.

Two Asymptotic Regimes

Fixed K: Non-Gaussian spatial median limit. Growing K: CLT with covariance V_α, finite-block bias unless K/b → 0.

Factorwise Robustness

Bad-node influence: mean factor α^(-1/2), subspace factor (2-α)^(-1/2). Large α protects the mean; small α protects the subspace.

Bootstrap Validity

Node-bootstrap proven valid for the growing-K regime, enabling practical inference without plug-in covariance estimation.

Strengths & Limitations

  • Strength Elegant unification of mean + subspace aggregation through product-manifold geometry with a powerful local reduction theorem.
  • Strength Interpretable factorwise influence constants provide clear design guidelines for distributed system configuration.
  • Strength Calibrated scale serves as a diagnostic of node-level uncertainty, not merely a tuning knob.
  • Limitation Fixed-dimensional theory. No extension to growing p or p/n → c regimes where random matrix theory would be needed.
  • Limitation Equal-size node assumption. Heterogeneous node sizes require modification not explored.
  • Limitation No code repository and no real corrupted-node experiment. Empirical improvement over fixed-scale MoM is sometimes modest.

Overview

This work formulates robust distributed PCA as a scale-calibrated median-of-means estimation problem on the product manifold Rp × Gr(r,p). The central insight is that the scale parameter α—balancing mean error against subspace error—should be calibrated from node-level uncertainty driven by eigengaps, not set arbitrarily. The author proves a local reduction from product-manifold MoM to a scaled spatial median, derives scale-dependent covariance formulas, establishes high-probability bounds with factorwise breakdown analysis, and validates on spiked covariance simulations and a 1.3M-cell single-cell RNA-seq dataset. The calibrated scale automatically adapts to eigengap conditions and PCA rank, offering a principled inferential design for distributed settings with potentially corrupted nodes.
