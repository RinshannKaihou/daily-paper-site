> **一句话总结：** 本文提出结构深度假说（SDH），通过5个模型、11轮自训练实验证明：语言模型迭代自训练并非均匀"压平"语言，而是按语法嵌套深度非对称重构——表层标记（话语连接词、模糊语）被放大，而深层句法结构（疑问句、被动语态、虚拟语气）崩溃，且这种"表层复杂度悖论"会误导现有LLM文本检测方法。

> **TL;DR:** Iterative self-training on LLM outputs doesn't flatten language uniformly — it restructures it by syntactic depth. Surface markers (discourse connectives, hedges, em-dashes) amplify (>2x) while deep syntactic structures (questions, passives, subjunctives) collapse (~50-90%). The Structural Depth Hypothesis (SDH) formalizes this: per-feature decay rate is predicted primarily by structural depth (ρ=0.540, p<10⁻⁶, N=85 across 5 models), not baseline frequency (ρ=0.225). Aggregate "complexity" metrics (dep-tree depth, TTR, word length) paradoxically rise during this collapse, systematically misleading LLM-text detectors.

## 核心贡献

- 提出**结构深度假说（SDH）**：语言特征在自训练中的衰变率主要由其语法嵌套深度决定，而非频率。形式化为竞争方程：ϕ̇ ≈ (−α·d(ϕ) + β·σ(ϕ))·ϕ，其中 d 为结构深度，σ 为采样依赖性。
- 在5个模型（GPT-2 124M、Pythia-410M/1.4B/2.8B、OPT-1.3B）上完成11轮自训练实验，17个语言特征面板的合并 Spearman 相关系数 ρ=0.540（p<10⁻⁶），深度显著优于频率预测（ρ=0.225）。
- 发现**表层复杂度悖论**：聚合指纹指标（依存树深度+45%、TTR+10%、词长+25%）在深层句法崩溃时反而上升，表明现有LLM文体检测文献中的标准度量系统性地掩盖了这种分化。
- 人类文本微调对照实验（ρ=0.039, p=0.88）证实该深度梯度是自训练特有的，而非微调的一般效应。
- 提出训练数据策展建议：应高权重补充富含深层句法的文本（文学、法律、科学文献），而非表层标记丰富的文本。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes the **Structural Depth Hypothesis (SDH)**: a linguistic feature's per-generation decay rate under iterative self-training is predicted primarily by its structural depth d(ϕ) — the number of nested syntactic dependencies it requires — and only secondarily by its generation-zero output frequency.
- Provides controlled self-training experiments on five models spanning three architecture families (GPT-2, Pythia, OPT) over 11 generations, with 17 a priori selected features stratified by depth (d ∈ {0,1,2,3}).
- Pooled cross-model analysis (N=85) yields ρ=0.540 (p<10⁻⁶) for depth vs. ρ=0.225 for frequency; a mixed-effects model confirms β_depth=0.047 (p<0.001).
- Documents the **Superficial Complexity Paradox**: standard aggregate fingerprint metrics (dep-tree depth +45%, clause embedding +33%, TTR +10%, avg word length +25%) all rise while clause-structural features collapse, systematically misleading LLM-text detection.
- A matched human-text fine-tuning control yields ρ=0.039 (p=0.88), confirming the depth gradient is specific to self-training dynamics, not generic fine-tuning.

## Methodology

- **Self-training protocol:** Starting from released checkpoints, iterate 11 generations. Each generation: (1) sample 3,000 texts of 256 tokens with nucleus sampling (top-p=0.95, T=0.9, top-k=50); (2) fine-tune the previous checkpoint on its own samples for 1 epoch (AdamW, lr=5e-5); (3) freeze as generation t+1. Held prompt distribution and decoding hyperparameters fixed.
- **Feature panel:** 17 features selected a priori, stratified into four depth levels: d=0 (lexical surface: discourse markers, hedges, em-dashes, exclamation), d=1 (local syntax: regular past tense, sentence-initial conjunctions, coordination, quotes, colons, semicolons), d=2 (clause structure: questions, parentheticals, passives, irregular past, relative clauses, adverbial clauses), d=3 (cross-clause: subjunctive).
- **Structural depth definition:** d(ϕ) = minimum number of nested syntactic dependencies required to license feature ϕ in a sentence, following standard syntactic theory.
- **Sampling dependence:** Operationalized as σ(ϕ) = 1 − min(τ, 1), where τ = f_greedy / f_nucleus. Features with low τ (high σ) depend on stochastic sampling and absent from greedy templates.
- **Statistical analysis:** Per-feature decay rates computed as OLS slopes of log ϕ_t on t; Spearman rank correlations between decay rates and depth/frequency; mixed-effects model with feature as random intercept; cluster-bootstrap CIs; permutation tests (10⁵ shuffles).
- **Controls:** Human-text fine-tuning control (OpenWebText), multi-seed replication (3 seeds), alternative decoding ablations (ancestral, greedy, tight nucleus), leave-one-out robustness, held-out feature validation with 5 novel features.

## Results & Highlights

- **Bifurcation in GPT-2:** d=0 features amplify (+24.9% group mean), d=1 decline slightly (−10.0%), d=2 collapse (−47.2%), d=3 collapse (−52.7%). Group means are monotonically decreasing in depth.
- **Individual feature extremes:** Discourse markers +126.2%, hedging +44.2%, regular past tense +79.7%; question marks −91.7%, parentheticals −56.8%, passive voice −55.5%, irregular past −52.3%, subjunctive −52.7%.
- **Cross-model consistency:** All five models show positive depth-decay correlations: GPT-2 ρ=0.373, Pythia-410M ρ=0.609 (p=0.010), Pythia-1.4B ρ=0.498 (p=0.042), OPT-1.3B ρ=0.563 (p=0.019), Pythia-2.8B ρ=0.705 (p=0.002).
- **Depth outperforms frequency:** Pooled ρ_depth=0.540 (p<10⁻⁶) vs. ρ_freq=0.225 (p=0.039). Partial correlation controlling for frequency: ρ_partial=0.490 (p<10⁻⁶).
- **Human control is flat:** Fine-tuning on human text yields ρ=0.039 (p=0.88), no depth gradient.
- **Perplexity drops 68%** (41.2 → 13.1) across 11 generations — standard collapse diagnostics would call this "convergence" while the structural inventory is actually degrading.
- **Critical pairs demonstrate depth over frequency:** Regular past (d=1) grows +79.7% while irregular past (d=2, similar frequency) declines −52.3%; em-dashes (d=0) grow +28.6% while parentheticals (d=2, same punctuation role) decline −56.8%.

## Authors & Credibility

- Single author: **Ming Liu**, Amazon. The work is methodologically rigorous with extensive controls, robustness checks, and honest reporting of limitations.
- The paper leverages standard NLP tools (spaCy) and well-established statistical methods (mixed-effects models, Spearman correlations, permutation tests, cluster bootstrap).
- All experiments run on publicly available models (GPT-2, Pythia, OPT). Compute cost under $400 total.
- The a priori feature selection and depth annotation commitment (before any trajectory data) strengthens causal claims. Held-out feature validation with 5 novel features provides additional guard against post-hoc reasoning.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The Structural Depth Hypothesis offers a genuinely new mechanistic account of model collapse that goes beyond distributional/statistical descriptions. The Superficial Complexity Paradox is an important methodological contribution that challenges existing LLM-detection practices.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Pre-registered feature panel, cross-model replication (5 models, 3 architecture families), human-text control, multi-seed ablations, leave-one-out analysis, held-out feature validation, and thorough discussion of statistical power limitations. The authors are transparent about pseudoreplication concerns and provide mixed-effects corrections.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** All models are public. Hyperparameters, seeds, and prompts are specified. Code mentioned (run_heldout_validation.py, annotation_gold.json). The compute is modest (~$400 total). Feature extraction uses standard spaCy pipeline.
- **Significance: High.** The finding that aggregate complexity metrics mislead during structural collapse has immediate implications for the LLM-text detection community. The depth-graded curation guidance (prioritize clause-rich text) is actionable.
- **Red flags:** (1) Only tested on small models (124M–2.8B); the authors acknowledge this but it limits generalizability to frontier-scale models. (2) The d=3 tier has only one feature (subjunctive), making d=3 claims illustrative rather than statistically grounded — though the authors note this and show significance holds without d=3. (3) The 17-feature panel, while a priori selected, is still relatively small; broader feature inventories might reveal more within-depth heterogeneity. (4) The pure self-training regime studied here is extreme; real-world contamination involves partial synthetic data at different mixing ratios.

## Strengths

- Elegant, falsifiable hypothesis (SDH) with three clear predictions, all confirmed.
- Exceptional experimental design: a priori feature selection, cross-model replication, matched human-text control, critical pairs that isolate depth from frequency.
- The Superficial Complexity Paradox is a powerful conceptual contribution that directly challenges prevailing methodological practice in LLM stylometry.
- Honest and thorough limitations section; the authors acknowledge statistical power constraints, model scale limitations, and pseudoreplication concerns without defensiveness.
- The "exclamation mark exception" analysis is a compelling demonstration that the full SDH law (depth + sampling dependence) captures nuances a simpler model would miss.

## Limitations

- **Model scale:** All models are ≤2.8B parameters. Whether the depth gradient holds at 7B+ scale remains unknown, though the correlation appears to strengthen with scale (ρ=0.373 at 124M → ρ=0.705 at 2.8B).
- **English only:** Structural depth is defined over English dependency grammar; typologically different languages may show different patterns.
- **Coarse depth scale:** Only four levels with one feature at d=3. Finer-grained depth annotations could strengthen predictions but introduce annotation noise.
- **Pure self-training regime:** No human data mixing. Real-world scenarios involve partial synthetic contamination, which may moderate (but not eliminate) the depth gradient.
- **Feature detection:** Several detectors use surface heuristics (e.g., regex for passives captures only ~31% recall). Mean F1=0.69 across the panel. While noise attenuates rather than inflates correlations, measurement imprecision limits per-feature interpretability.
- **Small per-model N:** With only 17 features per model, individual model tests are underpowered (~50% power for ρ=0.43). The GPT-2 per-model test is non-significant (p=0.140) despite a positive effect.

## Relevance

本文研究LLM迭代自训练对语言结构的系统性破坏，与LLM训练稳定性/可靠性高度相关。结构深度假说为理解训练数据污染的深层机制提供了新视角——不仅关注分布层面的模型崩溃，还揭示了哪些句法结构最先退化，这对训练数据策展和质量监控有直接指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the depth gradient hold at frontier model scales (7B–70B+), and does greater capacity delay or merely rescale the collapse timeline?
- How does partial synthetic data mixing (e.g., 10–50% synthetic) moderate the depth gradient, and is there a critical contamination threshold?
- Can depth-stratified feature panels improve LLM-text detection accuracy compared to aggregate fingerprint methods, especially against adversarial rewriting?
- Do multilingual models exhibit analogous depth-graded collapse, and how does morphological typology interact with the depth scale?
- Could depth-aware data curation — upsampling clause-structurally rich text — arrest or reverse the structural collapse during continued training?
