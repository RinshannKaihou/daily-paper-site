Reviving Error Correction in Modern Deep Time-Series Forecasting

UEC-STD · Nguyen, Do, Nguyen, Nguyen, Do, Le · Deakin Applied AI Initiative (DA2I2), Deakin University

time-series-forecasting error-correction seasonal-trend-decomposition autoregressive-inference model-agnostic
Key Finding

A lightweight, architecture-agnostic post-hoc error corrector that plugs into any pretrained deep time-series forecaster without retraining. It decomposes predictions into trend and seasonal components via moving-average filtering, trains separate MLP correction vectors for each, and applies them with a tunable strength β — consistently reducing MSE across 4 backbone architectures and 10 datasets.

Why This Matters
  • Treats backbone as a complete black box — no retraining, no architectural modification
  • ~1 min training vs ~10 min for backbone — 10× faster to adopt
  • Works across TimeMixer, TimesNet, TimeXer, TimeBridge (4 diverse architectures)
  • Open-source, single-GPU, standard codebase — minimal adoption friction
  • Principled decomposition aligns correction with time-series structure
Critical Edge
  • Only applicable to autoregressive inference — does not benefit direct forecasting models
  • Can degrade performance on datasets where base errors are small (Exchange Rate, ILI)
  • Average improvement on standard benchmarks is modest (~2.3% MSE)
  • Not evaluated beyond 720-step horizons — behavior at extreme horizons unknown
  • Single-run results (consistency checked in appendix, but no multi-seed main tables)
  • Does not compare against training longer-horizon direct models as a stronger baseline
Methodology — Three-Stage Pipeline
Stage 1 · AR Forecasting

Backbone model F generates long-horizon forecasts by recursively feeding predictions back as input (M autoregressive steps to reach horizon T = M × L). Error accumulates — up to 28.8% increase on ETTh1 vs oracle inputs.

Stage 2 · UEC Framework

UEC takes historical window X̂ and backbone prediction as input, outputs correction vector ∆X̂. Corrected forecast: X̂corr = X̂ + β∆X̂. Trained on validation split with Huber loss, early stopping on held-out portion.

Stage 3 · UEC-STD Architecture

1D centered MA convolution (kernel 25) decomposes into trend + seasonal. Two-stage MLP: Subnet 1 captures temporal dependencies per feature; Subnet 2 aggregates and outputs separate corrections. Weighted loss: λt·Lt + λs·Ls.
