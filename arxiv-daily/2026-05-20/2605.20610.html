Key Finding

Beyond Routing: Characterising Expert Tuning and Representation in Vision Mixture-of-Experts

Gene Tangtartharakul & Katherine R. Storrs · University of Auckland · 2026

Routing statistics systematically mischaracterise what MoE experts encode. Experts that appear category-tuned via routing are actually tuned to continuous shape, colour, and texture dimensions that cut across category boundaries. A stable animate–inanimate bipartition emerges across all seeds and configurations — echoing the dominant organisational axis in human ventral temporal cortex.

Key Metric
30/ 30

Runs recovering the animate–inanimate bipartition via second-order RSA clustering. 10 independent seeds × 3 expert configurations (E = 4, 8, 16), all stable.

Why This Matters
The dominant MoE analysis paradigm — routing heatmaps — is fundamentally insufficient. Gating logits and readout magnitudes show no linear relationship for any expert: what the router sends to an expert differs from what that expert most strongly encodes. Every expert achieves similar category separability despite divergent feature tuning, proving equivalent representational geometry can arise from distinct population-level response patterns.
Critical Edge
Proof is on a small-scale model (~46M params) trained on STL10 (10 classes, 100k images). Unknown whether findings transfer to industrial MoEs (Switch, Mixtral) or survive supervised / generative objectives. Parameter-count-controlled design is atypical for MoE practice and may influence observed specialisation dynamics.

Methodology Pipeline

Model. ResNet-5-block CNN → sparse MoE after block 3. Top-k = 2 noisy gating. Experts replicated for blocks 4–5; channel width scaled by 1/√E to hold ~46M params constant.

Training. Self-supervised contrastive on STL10 unlabeled (100k, 100 epochs). Load balance via coefficient-of-variation penalty on per-expert importance.

Analysis. (1) Routing proportion per STL10 class. (2) Most Exciting Inputs — bypass router, rank by L2 readout norm on STL10 + THINGS (1,854 concepts). (3) Non-negative Lasso regression of 66 THINGS behavioural dimensions → gating logits vs. readout magnitudes, nested 5-fold CV. (4) Pairwise logistic-regression category separability on expert readouts. (5) First-order RDMs → second-order RSA (Spearman) → agglomerative clustering with silhouette-optimised k.
