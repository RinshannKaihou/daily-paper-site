> **一句话总结：** 本文提出HORST优化器，通过将AdamW的最速下降步与双曲熵镜像映射步以正确的非交换顺序组合，使Transformer在稀疏训练中同时保持训练稳定性并获得L1稀疏性隐式偏置，在高稀疏度（80-90%）下相比AdamW基线准确率提升高达10个百分点。

> **TL;DR:** HORST composes AdamW's steepest-descent update with a hyperbolic entropy mirror map in a principled non-commutative operator framework, inducing an L1 sparsity bias while retaining the L∞ stability of adaptive methods. On DeiT-base/small with AC/DC sparsification, it outperforms AdamW by 2.7–8.0 accuracy points across 70–90% sparsity on ImageNet, and yields lower perplexity on one-shot pruned GPT-2 Small—demonstrating that optimizer implicit bias is a key bottleneck for sparse transformers.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了最速下降算子（有界但非线性，L∞偏置）与镜像下降算子（线性但无界，L1偏置）之间的结构二分法，解释了为何AdamW的隐式偏置天然不利于稀疏化。
- 提出算子组合作为优化器设计原则，证明正确的组合顺序（先最速下降、后镜像映射）使合成算子同时继承有界性和L1稀疏偏置，且两个算子类不满足交换律。
- 设计HORST优化器（Algorithm 2），在AdamW步之后施加指数形式的超熵镜像更新，仅需一个额外的逐参数指数运算，几乎不增加计算开销。
- 在视觉Transformer（DeiT-base/small + AC/DC）和语言模型（GPT-2 Small + 一次性剪枝）上均显著优于AdamW基线，且大幅超越先前的HAM优化器。

## Key Contributions

- Identify a structural dichotomy between steepest descent operators (bounded, nonlinear, L∞ bias) and mirror descent operators (linear, unbounded, L1 bias), explaining why AdamW's implicit bias is inherently hostile to sparsity.
- Introduce optimizer-operator composition as a design principle, proving the correct ordering (steepest first, mirror second) yields a bounded composed operator inheriting both stability and L1 sparsity bias, with non-commutativity formally established.
- Derive HORST (Algorithm 2), which applies an exponential hyperbolic-entropy mirror update after each AdamW step, adding only a per-parameter exponential operation with negligible overhead.
- Demonstrate consistent and significant improvements over AdamW across sparsity levels on DeiT-base/small (ImageNet, AC/DC pipeline) and GPT-2 Small (one-shot pruning, SlimPajama), and show HORST substantially outperforms the prior HAM optimizer.

## Methodology

HORST is grounded in a formal operator-theoretic analysis of optimization:

1. **Operator formalism**: Optimization updates are cast as functional operators that map (parameter, gradient) pairs to step directions. Two classes are analyzed:
   - *Steepest descent operators* $S_{\|\cdot\|}$: defined by a norm, include sign descent (Adam) and coordinate descent. They are bounded but nonlinear, inducing $L_\infty$-type implicit bias.
   - *Mirror descent operators* $M_R$: defined by a mirror potential $\nabla R$, rescale gradients by $\nabla^2 R^{-1}(\theta)$. They are linear but generally unbounded, inducing $L_1$ bias via the hyperbolic entropy map.

2. **Dichotomy and non-commutativity**: Lemma 4.1–4.2 establish the complementary properties. Lemma 4.3 proves $S_{\|\cdot\|_\infty} \circ M_R = S_{\|\cdot\|_\infty}$ (mirror-first is absorbed by sign descent), while $M_R \circ S_{\|\cdot\|_\infty}$ preserves both geometries. Corollary 4.4 confirms non-commutativity.

3. **Bounded composition**: Corollary 4.5 shows $M_R \circ S_{\|\cdot\|}$ is bounded under a mild growth condition satisfied by all considered mirror maps.

4. **Implicit bias theorem**: Theorem 4.7 (via Theorem C.3) proves that steepest-mirror descent with $L_p$-norm and entropy mirror map $\nabla R(\theta) = \log\theta$ converges to the $L_1$ max-margin solution, overriding the steepest descent's native $L_p$ bias.

5. **Practical optimizer (Algorithm 2)**: HORST alternates an AdamW step with an exponential update:
   $$\theta_{k+\frac{1}{2}} = \theta_k - \text{Adam}(g(\theta_k), \eta) - \lambda\eta\theta_k$$
   $$\theta_{k+1} = \theta_{k+\frac{1}{2}} \exp\left(-\alpha \cdot \text{sign}(\theta_{k+\frac{1}{2}}) \cdot \text{Adam}(g(\theta_k), \eta) - \eta\beta\right)$$
   where $\alpha$ controls sparsity bias strength and $\beta$ adds optional regularization. Setting $\alpha = 0$ recovers vanilla AdamW.

## Results & Highlights

**Vision Transformers (DeiT + AC/DC, ImageNet)**:

| Model | Optimizer | 70% Sparsity | 80% Sparsity | 90% Sparsity |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| DeiT-base | AdamW | 76.15 ± 0.71 | 74.18 ± 0.29 | 68.86 ± 2.05 |
| DeiT-base | HORST-AdamW | **78.89 ± 0.79** | **78.63 ± 1.26** | **76.83 ± 0.92** |
| DeiT-small | AdamW | 71.59 ± 1.12 | 67.07 ± 0.31 | 59.64 ± 1.52 |
| DeiT-small | HORST-AdamW | **78.42 ± 0.39** | **75.83 ± 0.47** | **69.41 ± 2.75** |

- Gains range from +2.7 to +8.0 accuracy points, largest at 90% sparsity.
- At 90% sparsity, DeiT-small with HORST nearly matches AdamW's 80% sparsity performance.

**Language Models (GPT-2 Small, one-shot magnitude pruning, SlimPajama)**:
- HORST-AdamW consistently achieves lower validation perplexity than both AdamW and HAM across all sparsity levels (10–60%), with the gap widening dramatically at higher sparsity (e.g., 50% sparsity: 44.16 vs. 86.50 for AdamW, 54.58 for HAM).

**Comparison with HAM (DeiT-small, 90% sparsity)**:
- HAM: 60.06, HORST: 70.5, AdamW baseline: 59.23 — HORST's composition with AdamW's internal update is critical; HAM's gradient-based exponential step cannot leverage the adaptive geometry.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High)**: The operator-composition framework is genuinely novel. The insight that steepest and mirror descent operators are non-commutative, and that ordering determines which biases are inherited, provides a clean theoretical foundation. The practical realization in HORST is a natural but non-obvious consequence.
- **Rigor (High)**: Formal proofs of boundedness (Lemmas C.1–C.2), non-commutativity (Lemma 4.3, Corollary 4.4), and implicit bias (Theorem C.3) are provided. The PL-inequality and coercivity analysis for convergence is sound. The implicit bias result is limited to the continuous-time flow regime and the $L_p$ setting with $p \in \mathbb{N}_{\geq 2}$; extending to $p \to \infty$ (the Adam/AdamW case) remains open.
- **Reproducibility (Moderate-High)**: Algorithms are clearly specified with hyperparameters ($\alpha=5, \beta=0$). Code is based on public repositories (TurboPrune, llm-baselines-aligned). Experiments use standard benchmarks (ImageNet, SlimPajama). However, requiring 4× A100 GPUs limits accessibility.
- **Significance (High)**: The gains at high sparsity are substantial and consistent across architectures. The theoretical framework has broader implications for optimizer design beyond sparsity. The finding that AdamW's implicit bias is a fundamental bottleneck for transformer sparsification is impactful.
- **Red flags**:
  - The implicit bias theorem (Thm 4.7) does not cover the limiting $p \to \infty$ case directly relevant to Adam/AdamW; the authors acknowledge this as open.
  - Experiments are limited to relatively small models (DeiT-base/small, GPT-2 Small). Scaling to modern LLMs (7B+ parameters) is untested.
  - The alternating scheme (vs. true composed update) is a practical approximation whose theoretical gap is not fully characterized.
  - Only AC/DC and one-shot magnitude pruning are tested; compatibility with other sparsification methods (RigL, SparseGPT) is mentioned but not validated.

## Strengths

- Elegant theoretical framework that cleanly explains *why* AdamW fails for sparse transformers and *how* to fix it.
- Simple practical implementation — HORST adds only a single exponential update per parameter per step.
- Consistent improvements across two modalities (vision and language) and multiple sparsity levels.
- Strong ablation: the wrong composition order (Adam-Exp vs. Exp-Adam) demonstrably fails, validating the theoretical non-commutativity.
- The $\alpha$ hyperparameter provides a smooth knob between standard AdamW ($\alpha=0$) and full sparsity bias, making adoption low-risk.

## Limitations

- No experiments on models larger than GPT-2 Small (~124M params); scalability to billion-parameter LLMs is unknown.
- The theoretical implicit bias result is for continuous-time flows with $L_p$ norms where $p \geq 2$, not directly for the discrete AdamW case.
- The hyperparameter $\alpha$ was ablated only on DeiT-base at 70% sparsity; sensitivity across architectures and sparsity levels is not thoroughly explored.
- Only unstructured sparsity is tested; structured pruning or N:M sparsity patterns are not addressed.
- The exponential update prevents sign flips by construction, which the authors acknowledge as a limitation shared with HAM.

## Relevance

本文从优化器隐式偏置的几何角度解释了Transformer稀疏化困难的根本原因，并提出有理论保证的优化器设计框架。这与ML系统可靠性研究密切相关——理解训练过程中的隐式偏置有助于提升模型压缩后的部署可靠性，且镜像下降的数值稳定性分析也触及了精度与数值计算可靠性的核心问题。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does HORST scale to models with billions of parameters (e.g., LLaMA-scale)? Does the exponential update interact poorly with distributed training (tensor/pipeline parallelism)?
2. Can the non-commutative operator composition framework be extended to spectral methods like Muon or Scion, which use matrix-valued norms?
3. What is the precise implicit bias of the *discrete-time* HORST update, as opposed to the continuous-time flow characterization?
4. Does HORST interact beneficially with post-training pruning methods (SparseGPT, WANDA), as the authors speculate?
5. How sensitive is $\alpha$ to the learning rate schedule, warmup, and decay — could an adaptive $\alpha$ schedule further improve results?
