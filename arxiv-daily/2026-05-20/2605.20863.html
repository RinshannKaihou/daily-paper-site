PlexRL: Cluster-Level Orchestration of Serviceized LLM Execution for RLVR

Yiqi Zhang, Fangzheng Jiao, Tian Tang, Boyu Tian, Hangyu Wang, Qiaoling Chen, Guoteng Wang, Zhen Jiang, Peng Sun, Ping Zhang, Xiaohe Hu, Ziming Liu, Menghao Zhang, Yanmin Jia, Yang You, Siyuan Feng
NUS · Beihang · Shanghai Qiji Zhifeng · Infrawaves 2048-GPU cluster · Qwen 7B / 30B / 235B
Key Finding
RLVR training idle gaps are structurally unavoidable at the job level (phase alternation, long-tailed rollouts, oversized rollout DP) — but anti-correlated across jobs, making them reclaimable at cluster scope. PlexRL decouples RL algorithm control from model execution, exposing rollout and training as shared services multiplexed via spatio-temporal scheduling. Reward trajectories remain identical; cost drops by up to 37.58%.

Why This Matters
Intrinsic bubble ratios hit 80–81% under split deployment — meaning 4 out of 5 reserved GPU-seconds are idle. No job-local optimization fixes this. The lens must shift from single-job tuning to cross-job cluster orchestration, and PlexRL is the first system to do so for RLVR.
Critical Edge
  • Only 2-job packing validated end-to-end; 1.8× capacity claim is trace-simulated only
  • No comparison against RLHFuse or ReaLHF
  • Evaluated exclusively on math RLVR (GRPO); PPO/DAPO/general RL not tested
System Architecture
RLController → Router → Scheduler → Worker Process Groups + StateManager ↖ GPU · DRAM · NVMe
Stateless Router admits requests via the Scheduler. WPGs serialize per-job execution but run concurrently across jobs. The per-node StateManager sidecar owns physical residency of all model state across three tiers, providing canonicalized offloading (dedup across DP replicas), zero-redundancy RDMA weight sync, and off-critical-path optimizer steps and checkpointing.

Limitations & Open Questions
Algorithm scopeOnly GRPO (math RLVR) tested; PPO, DAPO, multi-tool agentic training have different phase characteristics
Scale validationEnd-to-end experiments stop at 2-job packing; the 1.8× capacity result is simulation-only, no real multi-job execution
Missing baselinesNo head-to-head comparison with RLHFuse or ReaLHF, which target similar efficiency problems at job level
Cold-start costProfiling phase for new jobs not quantified; accuracy of fitted traces under variable prompt complexity unknown
Failure modesWhat happens when StateManager sidecar crashes, or a job's phase pattern shifts mid-training?
Emergent relaxationConvergence to low-interference equilibria argued qualitatively, no formal analysis or empirical convergence plots
