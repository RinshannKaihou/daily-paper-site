Mind Your Margin and Boundary: Are Your Distilled Datasets Truly Robust?

C²R — Contrastive Curriculum for Robust Dataset Distillation

Muquan Li, Yingyi Ma, Yihong Huang, Hang Gou, Ke Qin, Ming Li, Yuan-Fang Li, Tao He · UESTC · Guangdong Lab of AI · Monash

Key Finding

Existing robust dataset distillation methods treat adversarial examples uniformly and ignore decision boundary separation. C²R fixes both: an attack-aware curriculum prioritizes smallest-margin adversarial samples, and a contrastive robustness loss enforces clean–adversarial invariance while widening inter-class boundaries. The result is distribution-level robustness that generalizes across six attack types, not attack-specific hardening.

Why This Matters
  • Robust-margin framing links dataset distillation to classical margin theory — the insight transfers beyond DD to general adversarial training.
  • Curriculum + contrastive loss are complementary: AAC alone adds ~1.5%, CRL alone ~1.0%, together ~2.5% — synergistic, not redundant.
  • First method to keep the accuracy–robustness drop rate flat as synthetic set size grows, unlike baselines whose DR spikes rapidly.
  • Practical efficiency: less training time than SRe²L and ROME while achieving higher robustness.
Methodology

Attack-Aware Curriculum (AAC)

Perturbation score s(x) = [1 − m̂rob]+ ranks samples by proximity to the decision boundary. Mini-batches prioritize hardest adversarial examples. LS-PGD warm-starts from cached perturbations with forward-only line search, cutting backprop overhead.

Contrastive Robustness Loss (CRL)

Supervised contrastive objective replaces class-mean alignment: positives = same-class clean + adversarial; negatives = different classes. Instance-level invariance preserves intra-class geometry. Class-balanced FIFO queue + random-projection ANN retrieval keeps cost O(Mk), not O(M²).

Critical Edge
  • Only evaluated on ConvNets — no ViT, ResNet, or modern backbone results.
  • Robust accuracy plateaus or slightly degrades at high IPC (10→50) on some settings.
  • No comparison with standard adversarial training baselines (e.g., Madry-trained models on full data).
  • ImageNet experiments limited to 128×128 subsets, not full-resolution 1K.
  • η requires different settings per IPC budget, adding tuning overhead.
Strengths
  • Principled theory: robust hinge minimization ↔ maximizing minimum robust margin (Eqs. 7–9).
  • Consistent gains across all 6 attacks (FGSM, PGD, CW, VMI-FGSM, Jitter, AutoAttack) at ε = 2/255.
  • Component ablation clearly shows additive + synergistic value.
  • Better visual quality of distilled images vs. ROME.
  • Follows DD-RobustBench standardized evaluation protocol.
  • Transparent about limitations, including the IPC scaling issue.

Open Questions
  1. How does C²R behave with Vision Transformers as the distillation backbone?
  2. Can the attack-aware curriculum transfer to standard adversarial training?
  3. What happens under adaptive attacks designed to exploit the curriculum ordering?
  4. Does noisy perturbation-score ranking still improve robustness?
  5. Can CRL combine with certified defenses for provable robustness?
