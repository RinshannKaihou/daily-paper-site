> **一句话总结：** 本文证明，当输入数据存在对称性（如旋转不变性）时，功能等价的神经表示可能产生截然不同的表示相似性矩阵（RSM），这意味着RSA/CKA等流行方法可能将功能相同的编码误判为不同，尤其在学习产生稀疏码或漂移码时。

> **TL;DR:** This paper shows that stimulus symmetries (e.g., rotational invariance) can make functionally equivalent neural representations yield qualitatively different representational similarity matrices (RSMs). In manifold-tiling codes with ReLU activations, rotating receptive fields changes the RSM but not decoding accuracy. SGD and L1 regularization can produce sparse or drifting codes where this gauge dependence is pronounced, including in networks trained on image data. This challenges the reliability of RSM-based comparisons (RSA, CKA) for nonlinear codes when symmetries are present but not explicitly modeled.

## 核心贡献

- 证明了输入数据的对称性（如旋转）会导致功能等价的神经表示产生不同的RSM，揭示RSA/CKA等方法在非线性编码对比中的根本局限。
- 在流形铺砌（manifold-tiling）编码的理论框架下，严格推导了RSM依赖于"规范变量"（gauge variable）的条件——当且仅当对称变换在表示空间中对应正交变换时，RSM才是规范无关的。
- 发现SGD训练和L1正则化（模拟能量约束）会导致神经元塌缩或稀疏化，即使在神经元数量很多时也能产生高度可变的RSM。
- 将理论扩展到高维球面刺激（S^{d-1}），预测了RSM的非对角元素会聚类为 d(d-1)/2 个相关联的组。
- 在Kuzushiji-MNIST旋转图像上训练的自编码器中验证了上述现象，并在预训练视觉模型（ResNet、ConvNeXt、DINOv2）中展示了RSM随旋转角度系统变化。

## Key Contributions

- Identify a fundamental confound in RSM-based analyses: stimulus symmetries yield functionally equivalent representations with qualitatively different RSMs, because gauge transformations need not correspond to orthogonal rotations in representation space.
- Provide a rigorous formalization: the RSM is gauge-invariant if and only if the group action on the encoding is an orthogonal linear representation of G — a condition satisfied by equivariant architectures but violated by generic nonlinear codes (e.g., ReLU manifold tiling).
- Show that SGD can collapse neurons into effectively sparse tilings (e.g., 15 neurons collapsing to 4 effective ones), and L1 regularization silences excess neurons, both leading to high RSM variability despite nominally large hidden layers.
- Derive closed-form expressions for the RSM variability Δ as a function of neuron count n, showing it decays as O(n⁻²) for the ring model (Δ = tan(π/n)²/n), and argue that the required n scales exponentially with the intrinsic dimensionality of the symmetric manifold.
- Demonstrate gauge-dependent RSMs empirically in autoencoders trained on rotated Kuzushiji-MNIST images and in pretrained vision models, with CKA similarity dropping sharply as a function of gauge difference (Pearson's r = −0.91, p < 0.001).

## Methodology

The paper combines theoretical analysis with controlled experiments:

1. **Toy model (§2):** A ReLU encoder with n = 4 neurons tiling a ring S¹, where a global rotation angle φ acts as a gauge variable. The RSM is derived in closed form and shown to depend on φ while decoding accuracy remains invariant.

2. **General formalism (§3):** The authors define symmetry groups G acting on a latent space Z, with G-invariant metrics and measures. They prove that decoding error is gauge-invariant (E[g·h, g·f] = E[h, f]), and characterize when the RSM is gauge-invariant (iff the encoding is an orthogonal linear representation of G).

3. **Scaling analysis (§4):** Systematic study of how neuron count, tuning width, and amplitude noise affect RSM variability Δ. Closed-form results for n a multiple of 4; the key scaling is Δ = tan(π/n)²/n.

4. **Learning experiments (§5):** Two-layer ReLU autoencoders trained on isotropic Gaussian data with SGD + weight decay. Two mechanisms produce sparse solutions: (a) SGD noise causing neuron alignment/collapse, and (b) L1 regularization silencing neurons. Continual training shows drifting RSMs.

5. **Higher-dimensional stimuli (§6):** Extension to S^{d-1} with reflection-symmetric ReLU encoders. The RSM has structured off-diagonal elements grouped into d(d-1)/2 clusters, with correlated variability during training.

6. **Image data (§7):** Autoencoders trained on 180 rotated versions of Kuzushiji-MNIST digits. Gauge angle inferred from receptive field Fourier analysis. Demonstrated RSM variability across 1000 independently trained models, with reconstruction loss being gauge-independent but CKA showing strong gauge dependence. Additional experiments with pretrained ResNet, ConvNeXt, and DINOv2 on rotated images.

## Results & Highlights

- In the n = 4 ring model, the RSM off-diagonal element ranges from 0 (φ = 0) to 1/2 (φ = π/4), a qualitatively substantial change for a functionally irrelevant transformation.
- RSM variability Δ decays as O(n⁻²) with increasing neurons, but SGD collapse or L1 regularization can reduce the effective neuron count, restoring large Δ.
- During continual SGD training with n = 15 neurons, 11 neurons align with 4 dominant directions; the RSM drifts without convergence to a fixed value.
- For d = 3 spherical stimuli (n = 6 neurons), the 3 off-diagonal RSM clusters show correlated drift during training.
- On KMNIST rotated images: normalized off-diagonal RSM vs. inferred gauge φ shows a clear U-shape (Pearson's r = 0.76, p < 0.001), while reconstruction loss is flat (r = 0.03, p = 0.56).
- CKA between pairs of independently trained models drops from ~1 to ~0.55 as gauge difference increases (r = −0.91, p < 0.001).
- Pretrained vision models (ResNet, ConvNeXt, DINOv2) show systematically varying CKA with relative rotation angle, confirming that latent symmetries affect standard model comparisons.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The paper identifies a clean, previously overlooked confound in RSM-based analyses. While the individual ingredients (gauge freedom, RSM properties) are known, the synthesis — that stimulus symmetries systematically mislead RSM comparisons for nonlinear codes — is novel and important.
- **Rigor:** Very high. Theoretical claims are proved formally (with detailed appendices). Experiments are carefully controlled, progressing from synthetic to realistic data. The closed-form expressions for the toy model are exact.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong. Code is public. The toy model experiments are simple to replicate. The KMNIST experiments use standard datasets and architectures.
- **Significance:** Potentially high impact for the RSA/CKA community. The result implies that many published RSM comparisons between networks trained on image data (which has latent symmetries) may be confounded. However, the practical magnitude of the confound in deep, large-scale networks remains unclear — the paper focuses on shallow autoencoders.
- **Red flags:** The gap between the toy/autencoder setting and large-scale deep networks is significant. While the pretrained model experiments (Fig. S13) suggest the phenomenon exists more broadly, these models are not proven to be functionally equivalent under rotation (unlike the autoencoder setting). The formal results require G-invariant metrics and measures, which are restrictive assumptions. The paper does not propose a practical fix beyond acknowledging the problem.

## Strengths

- Elegant theoretical framework that cleanly separates functional equivalence from RSM equivalence.
- Strong mathematical foundations with rigorous proofs in appendices.
- Systematic experimental progression: toy model → learned representations → higher dimensions → image data.
- Clear exposition of when and why the RSM is gauge-invariant (orthogonal equivariance condition).
- The connection to representational drift in neuroscience is insightful and well-motivated.

## Limitations

- All experiments use shallow (two-layer) networks; it is unclear how the gauge dependence manifests in deep architectures with many layers.
- The practical impact on large-scale model comparisons (e.g., comparing ResNet-50 to ViT on ImageNet) is not quantified.
- The proposed "fix" — designing symmetry-invariant metrics — is acknowledged as an open problem without concrete solutions.
- The formalism assumes G-invariant metrics on the data space, which may not hold exactly for real data distributions.
- The pretrained model experiments (Fig. S13) show correlation but do not establish functional equivalence under rotation, making the interpretation weaker than the autoencoder experiments.

## Relevance

本文对表示学习和神经科学中广泛使用的RSA/CKA方法提出了重要的方法论警示。如果你在做模型对比或神经表示分析，这篇论文提醒你需要考虑数据中潜在的对称性可能导致的虚假差异，特别是在使用非线性编码（如ReLU网络）时。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does gauge dependence scale with network depth? Does composing layers amplify or attenuate RSM variability?
- Can practical symmetry-invariant comparison metrics be constructed without full knowledge of the stimulus distribution?
- Do modern equivariant architectures (e.g., equivariant ViTs) fully eliminate this confound in practice, or do residual nonlinearities still cause issues?
- How does the gauge dependence interact with other known confounds in RSA, such as the choice of trial stimuli or the dimensionality of the representation?
- Is the RSM variability observed in pretrained models (Fig. S13) correlated with functional differences, or is it purely a gauge artifact?
