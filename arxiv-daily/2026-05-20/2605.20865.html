Multi-Step Likelihood-Ratio Correction for Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards

Authors D. Yoon, H. Kang, J. Lee, B. Kim, G. Shin, S. Park, M. Oh Date 2026-05-20 2605.20865 Affil. Seoul National U. & Upstage
Key Finding

PPO surrogate objectives in RLVR discard the forward trace — the cumulative product of future likelihood ratios Γt+1 — from the exact policy-improvement identity, introducing structural bias bounded by O(T²δ²). Retaining only the next N−1 ratios (the N-step forward trace) creates a tunable bias-variance interpolation. With N=4, NFPO consistently outperforms GRPO and DPPO on 7 math reasoning benchmarks across two model scales.

Why This Matters

The insight that PPO discards useful future correction is broadly applicable to any PPO/GRPO-style RLVR training loop. NFPO is a modular, drop-in addition to existing masked policy gradient frameworks. If gains generalize beyond math reasoning, forward trace correction could become a standard component of LLM training.

Critical Edge
  • Gains over DPPO narrow to ~1–3% at 8B scale (Minerva: 39.0% vs 39.9%)
  • Evaluated only on math reasoning — no code, science, or open-ended tasks
  • N=4 is found empirically; theory gives qualitative guidance but no closed-form prescription
  • Computational overhead of the forward trace not analyzed
Methodology

NFPO computes a clipped N-step forward trace over likelihood ratios ρ and integrates it into the masked policy gradient:

  1. Clip each token-level ratio: ρt,β = clip(ρt, 1/β, β), β=3.0
  2. Compute clipped trace: Γ̄t+1(N) = clip(∏ ρj,β, 1−εlow, 1+εhigh)
  3. Apply stop-gradient to the trace for numerical stability
  4. TV-based token masking enforces trust region
  5. Optimize masked objective: L = (1/G) Σ Mt · Ât · ρt · Γ̄t+1(N)
