> **一句话总结：** 本文为具有马尔可夫噪声和鞅差噪声的随机逼近算法建立了极大浓度界，揭示了误差尾部行为（次高斯、次Weibull或更重）取决于步长选择和随机算子的收缩/非扩张/扩张性质，并提出了一种基于泊松方程矩母函数的新型Lyapunov函数分析技术和黑箱截断论证方法。

> **TL;DR:** This paper establishes tight, maximal (time-uniform) concentration bounds for general stochastic approximation iterates under finite-state Markovian noise plus Martingale-difference noise. The error tail ranges from sub-Gaussian to heavier-than-Weibull depending on (1) the step-size exponent z in α_k = α/(k+h)^z and (2) whether the random operator is a.s. contractive, a.s. non-expansive, or expansive with positive probability (quantified by a parameter D = A₁ + A₃ − 1). Key technical innovations include a Lyapunov function built from the MGF of a Poisson equation solution, an auxiliary projected SA algorithm, and a black-box truncation argument that lifts bounded-noise results to unbounded-noise settings. The bounds are shown to be order-wise optimal via matching lower bounds. Applications span contractive SA, linear SA, stationary mean estimation, and reveal that Polyak-Ruppert averaging preserves heavy tails in expansive settings.

## 核心贡献

- 为具有有限状态马尔可夫噪声+有界鞅差噪声的随机逼近（SA）算法建立了首个极大浓度界（maximal concentration bounds），支持一般步长 α_k = α/(k+h)^z，z ∈ (0,1]
- 揭示了误差尾部行为的三种模式：当 z=1 且算子几乎必然收缩时为次高斯（sub-Gaussian）；非扩张时为次Weibull（sub-Weibull）；扩张时为介于Weibull和Pareto之间的分布。当 z<1 时分类更加精细
- 提出了处理无界鞅差噪声的黑箱截断论证方法（black-box truncation argument），证明在非扩张情形下误差尾部至多为噪声尾部的3倍重
- 构造了基于泊松方程解的矩母函数的新型Lyapunov函数，解决了马尔可夫噪声导致漂移非单调的核心困难
- 设计了辅助投影算法（auxiliary projected algorithm），解决了 D≥0 时几乎必然界随时间增长的问题
- 通过构造最坏情况例子证明所获浓度界在定性上不可改进

## Key Contributions

- First maximal concentration bounds for SA with Markovian noise and general step sizes α_k = α/(k+h)^z for z ∈ (0,1], subsuming prior work limited to Martingale-difference noise and O(1/k) steps
- Complete characterization of error tail behavior across three regimes based on D = A₁ + A₃ − 1 (contractive/non-expansive/expansive) and step-size exponent z, with matching impossibility results (Theorem 2.3) showing qualitatively sharper bounds are impossible
- A novel Lyapunov function using the MGF of the Poisson equation solution to compensate for Markovian bias in the error drift, enabling MGF-based concentration analysis
- An auxiliary projected SA algorithm that projects iterates onto a ball of radius B centered at the fixed point; shown to coincide with original iterates w.h.p. for large enough B, circumventing the unbounded growth of iterates when D ≥ 0
- A black-box truncation argument reducing unbounded-noise analysis to the bounded-noise case by coupling original iterates with truncated-noise iterates, controlling both truncation bias (via contraction) and coincidence probability
- For unbounded noise with contractive average operator: error tail is at most 3× heavier than noise tail when D=0, but can be substantially heavier when D>0
- Applications to contractive SA, linear SA, stationary mean estimation (recovering Hoeffding-like bounds for Markov chain averages), and Polyak-Ruppert averaging (revealing that averaging preserves heavy tails)

## Methodology

The paper considers the SA recursion x_{k+1} = x_k + α_k(F(x_k, S_k) + Z_k − x_k), where {S_k} is a finite-state Markov chain, {Z_k} is a Martingale-difference sequence, and α_k = α/(k+h)^z.

**For bounded noise (Section 2):** The analysis hinges on bounding the MGF of a modified error metric. A key innovation is the Lyapunov function E[exp(θ(α_{k-1}T_{k-1}‖x_k − x*‖²_c + correction))], where the correction term uses the Poisson equation solution V_x(s) to cancel the Markovian bias in the drift. This yields a one-step recursion on the MGF leading to the desired bounds.

When D < 0 (a.s. contractive), iterates are a.s. bounded, and the MGF analysis directly gives sub-Gaussian/sub-Weibull tails. When D ≥ 0, iterates can grow unboundedly. The authors introduce auxiliary projected iterates (projected onto a ball of radius B around x*), analyze their MGF (which is well-controlled since they're bounded), and show that for B large enough the projection is inactive w.h.p., so bounds transfer to the original iterates.

**For unbounded noise (Section 3):** The truncation argument creates coupled iterates with noise truncated at level L. The bounded-noise results apply to these truncated iterates. Two steps then connect back to the original: (1) bounding the fixed-point bias introduced by truncation using the contraction property, and (2) showing that for sufficiently large truncation levels, the original and truncated iterates coincide w.h.p.

## Results & Highlights

- **Sub-Gaussian tails** when D < 0 and z < 1: ‖x_k − x*‖²_c ≤ Õ(log(1/δ)/k^z) w.h.p. 1−δ
- **Sub-Weibull tails** when D = 0, z = 1: ‖x_k − x*‖²_c ≤ Õ(log(1/δ)·log(k)²/k) w.h.p.
- **Heavier-than-Weibull, lighter-than-Pareto** when D > 0 and z ∈ (1/2, 1): bound involves exp(log(1/δ)^{(1−z)/(βz)})/k^z
- **Matching lower bounds**: Theorem 2.3 shows the error must be at least sub-Weibull with shape parameter 2−z, and cannot be bounded by exp(K' log(1/δ)^β')/k^z for β' < 1−z
- For contractive SA with i.i.d. noise and D > 0, the bound O(log(1/δ)^{2αD+1}/k) matches the best known lower bound exponent, improving upon prior work by removing a ceiling artifact
- **Unbounded noise result**: For contractive operators with O(1/k) steps, the error tail is at most 3× the noise tail when D ≤ 0, but can be arbitrarily heavier when D > 0
- **Polyak-Ruppert averaging** inherits heavy tails from the original iterates when z < 1, especially for expansive operators (D > 0), suggesting averaging may be undesirable in such settings

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** First results for SA with Markovian noise and general step sizes, and first concentration bounds for SA with unbounded heavy-tailed noise. The Lyapunov function via Poisson equation MGF and the auxiliary projected algorithm are genuinely new techniques.
- **Rigor: Very High.** Complete proofs with explicit constants; matching upper and lower bounds demonstrating order-wise optimality; careful handling of all edge cases (D<0, D=0, D>0 across z=1 and z∈(0,1)).
- **Reproducibility: Moderate-High.** All assumptions are clearly stated; constants are given explicitly in appendices. However, the proofs are highly technical (66+ pages) and would be difficult to independently verify without significant expertise.
- **Significance: High.** Stochastic approximation is foundational to RL, SGD, and online optimization. Concentration bounds (vs. mean-square bounds) are much more useful in practice for understanding tail risk. The results directly apply to Q-learning, TD-learning, and linear SA.
- **Red flags:** The gap between the upper bound shape parameter (1/z) and lower bound (2−z) for the sub-Weibull regime when z∈(0,1) remains open. The unbounded-noise bounds (Theorem 3.1) are acknowledged as not tight in certain regimes. The restriction to finite-state Markov chains may limit applicability in some RL settings with large/continuous state spaces.

## Strengths

- Unified framework covering bounded and unbounded noise, Markovian and i.i.d. settings, and all step-size regimes z∈(0,1]
- Matching impossibility results demonstrating sharpness of the bounds
- Clean problem decomposition via D = A₁+A₃−1 parameter that precisely determines tail behavior
- Practical implications: the finding that Polyak averaging preserves heavy tails in expansive settings is a cautionary result for practitioners
- Recovers classical results (Hoeffding-type bounds for Markov chain averages) as special cases
- The black-box truncation argument is an elegant reduction that should find use beyond this paper

## Limitations

- Finite-state Markov chain assumption on {S_k} excludes settings with continuous state spaces
- Unbounded-noise results require identically distributed noise across time, which may not hold in practice
- The gap between upper and lower bounds on the Weibull shape parameter (1/z vs 2−z) is unresolved
- Theorem 3.1 bounds are not tight for z ∈ (0,1) with unbounded noise
- Results are asymptotic in nature (holding for all k ≥ 0 with polylog corrections) and the constants involve system parameters that may be hard to compute
- The restriction to contractive average operators for the unbounded-noise setting excludes some nonlinear SA applications

## Relevance

本文为理解强化学习算法（如Q-learning、TD学习）和随机梯度下降在马尔可夫噪声下的浓度行为提供了基础性理论工具。其关于步长选择与误差尾部关系的精细刻画，以及Polyak平均在扩张算子下保留重尾的发现，对设计鲁棒的优化算法具有直接指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the Lyapunov function based on Poisson equation MGF be extended to continuous-state or countable-state Markov chains?
2. Is the gap between the upper bound shape parameter 1/z and the lower bound 2−z for z∈(0,1) fundamentally tight, or can it be closed with refined analysis?
3. Can Polyak-Ruppert averaging be modified (e.g., via geometric median or robust estimators) to achieve both optimal asymptotic variance and lighter tails simultaneously?
4. What are the explicit concentration bounds for Q-learning and TD-learning when instantiated with the paper's general results, and how do the constants depend on the state-space size and mixing time?
5. Does the black-box truncation argument extend to settings where the noise distribution changes over time (non-stationary noise)?
