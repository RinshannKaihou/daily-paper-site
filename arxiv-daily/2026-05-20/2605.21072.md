> **一句话总结：** 针对自回归视频扩散模型(ARVD)的量化难题，本文提出Q-ARVD框架，通过帧敏感度加权机制和自适应双尺度量化策略分别解决帧间误差累积和权重异常值问题，在W8A8量化下实现1.30倍加速和1.97倍模型压缩。

> **TL;DR:** Q-ARVD is the first quantization framework designed specifically for autoregressive video diffusion models. It identifies two unique challenges — exponentially decaying frame-wise sensitivity (early frames matter far more) and heterogeneous weight outlier patterns across layers — and addresses them with a final-quality guided frame-weighting loss and an adaptive dual-scale quantizer that isolates outlier channels. On self-forcing and causal-forcing models, it outperforms all baselines (MinMax, AdaRound, SmoothQuant, PTQ4DiT, SVDQuant) across W8A8, W4A8, and W4A6 settings, achieving near-lossless quality at W8A8 with 1.30x speedup and 1.97x size reduction on an NVIDIA A6000.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统分析了自回归视频扩散模型(ARVD)的量化特性，揭示了两个关键挑战：帧间量化敏感度呈指数衰减分布，以及权重中存在显著且异构的异常值通道
- 提出**最终质量引导的帧加权机制**：通过量化单帧并测量整段视频的质量退化来量化每帧敏感度，将其作为重建损失权重，强调早期帧的精度保持
- 提出**异常值感知自适应双尺度量化**：基于Modified Z-score自动检测每层的异常值通道，将异常通道与正常通道隔离到独立的量化器中，降低正常通道的缩放因子从而减少量化误差
- 在self-forcing和causal-forcing两个最先进的ARVD模型上验证，W8A8量化下实现近无损视觉质量

## Key Contributions

- First systematic study of quantization challenges unique to autoregressive video diffusion models (ARVDs), identifying exponentially decaying frame-wise sensitivity and heterogeneous weight outlier patterns as the two critical bottlenecks
- **Final-quality guided frame-weighting**: measures per-frame sensitivity by quantizing only that frame and computing the MSE degradation of the entire generated video in latent space, then uses these sensitivities as reconstruction loss weights
- **Outlier-aware adaptive dual-scale quantization**: automatically detects outlier channels per-layer using Modified Z-score with a minimum magnitude constraint, then applies separate quantizers to outlier vs. normal channels to prevent inflated scaling factors
- Comprehensive evaluation on two SOTA ARVDs (self-forcing, causal-forcing) across three bitwidths (W8A8, W4A8, W4A6), consistently outperforming five established baselines

## Methodology

Q-ARVD operates as a post-training quantization (PTQ) framework with two complementary modules applied during the calibration/reconstruction phase:

1. **Sensitivity measurement**: For each frame i, only quantize the i-th frame's generation while keeping others full-precision. Compute the MSE between the resulting video and the original in latent space. This yields a sensitivity score α_i per frame. Empirically, sensitivity follows an exponential-like decay (chunk 1 score: 0.70; last chunk: <0.01 for W8A8 on self-forcing).

2. **Frame-weighted reconstruction**: Replace the standard uniform reconstruction loss with α_i-weighted loss: L = E[α_i · ||X^(i)W − Q(X^(i))Q(W)||²_F], sampled uniformly over frames during calibration.

3. **Outlier detection**: For each layer's weight matrix, compute per-input-channel L2 norms. Apply Modified Z-score with MAD (Median Absolute Deviation) and a minimum magnitude constraint (α=1.2 relative to median). Channels exceeding both thresholds are flagged as outliers.

4. **Dual-scale quantization**: For layers with detected outliers, split weight channels into outlier and normal groups, quantize each with independent scaling factors. This reduces the scaling factor for the majority normal channels. Align outlier channel count to multiples of 32 for hardware efficiency.

Time embeddings, projection modules, and the prediction head are kept in BFloat16 due to their small size and outsized quality impact.

## Results & Highlights

- **W8A8 (near-lossless)**: On self-forcing, Q-ARVD achieves FVD-FP 64.51 vs. next-best SVDQuant 74.87 and MinMax 77.65. On causal-forcing, FVD-FP 61.67 vs. SVDQuant 63.52. LPIPS-FP also best in all settings.
- **W4A8**: On self-forcing, FVD-FP 116.26 vs. PTQ4DiT 124.20 (2nd best). On causal-forcing, FVD-FP 106.04 vs. SVDQuant 135.62.
- **W4A6**: On self-forcing, FVD-FP 146.01 vs. PTQ4DiT 174.17. On causal-forcing, FVD-FP 140.38 vs. SVDQuant 210.28.
- **Deployment**: W8A8 model on NVIDIA A6000 with Triton kernels: 1.30x latency speedup (18.02s → 13.85s), 1.97x size reduction (2.64GB → 1.34GB), batch size 2, self-forcing default config (4 denoising steps, 7 chunks, 21 frames).
- **Ablation**: Both frame-weighting and dual-scale contribute; frame-weighting helps more at W8A8, dual-scale helps more at W4A8. The Modified Z-score threshold τ is robust across [2.5, 4.5].
- **Metric analysis**: Authors propose a Discriminability Score (DS = CV × BOA) showing FVD-FP and LPIPS-FP are far more reliable than VBench scores for evaluating quantization quality.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. This is the first work to specifically address quantization for ARVDs. The two identified challenges (exponential frame sensitivity, heterogeneous outliers) are genuinely new observations specific to the autoregressive video diffusion paradigm. The dual-scale quantization idea is elegant.
- **Rigor**: Strong experimental methodology — two SOTA models, three bitwidths, five baselines, ablations on each component, sensitivity analysis on hyperparameters, and a thoughtful discussion of metric reliability. The DS metric for evaluating evaluation metrics is a nice meta-analytical contribution.
- **Reproducibility**: Code is available. Implementation details are thorough (learning rates, batch sizes, training iterations, calibration data). Only standard dependencies (Triton for kernels).
- **Significance**: Practical impact is clear — ARVDs are increasingly important for real-time/interactive video generation, and quantization is essential for edge deployment. The 1.30x speedup at W8A8 with near-lossless quality is meaningful.
- **Red flags**: The 1.30x W8A8 speedup is modest compared to the theoretical 2x for INT8, suggesting kernel-level overhead remains. The frame-weighting requires a full autoregressive rollout per frame to measure sensitivity, which is expensive during calibration (though it's a one-time cost). The paper only tests on two specific ARVD architectures — generalization to others is claimed but not shown.

## Strengths

- Identifies and formalizes two genuinely novel problems specific to ARVD quantization with strong empirical evidence
- The adaptive outlier detection using Modified Z-score is parameter-light and robust, avoiding per-layer manual tuning
- Comprehensive evaluation with thoughtful metric analysis; the DS framework for metric discriminability is a useful contribution beyond the main method
- Clear, well-structured writing with good figures illustrating sensitivity patterns and outlier distributions
- Ablation study cleanly separates the contributions of each component

## Limitations

- The frame sensitivity measurement requires N full autoregressive rollouts (one per frame) during calibration, which is computationally expensive
- Only 1.30x speedup for W8A8 — significant gap between theoretical and practical INT8 gains
- Evaluated on only two ARVD architectures (self-forcing, causal-forcing); generalization to other autoregressive video models is not verified
- The authors note the sensitivity pattern could inform mixed-precision quantization but leave this to future work
- No evaluation on user-perceived quality (human study); relies entirely on automated metrics

## Relevance

本文对自回归视频扩散模型的量化研究具有直接参考价值，其中帧间误差累积的分析和自适应异常值检测方法可推广至其他自回归生成模型的压缩场景，对实际部署视频生成模型有重要意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the exponential sensitivity pattern be leveraged for mixed-precision quantization (e.g., higher precision for early frames, lower for later)?
- How does the calibration cost scale with video length and number of frames — is there a more efficient proxy for frame sensitivity?
- Would the dual-scale approach generalize to autoregressive image generation (e.g., next-token prediction in visual LLMs)?
- Can the modest 1.30x speedup be improved with custom CUDA kernels beyond Triton, or is the bottleneck elsewhere in the pipeline?
- How does quantization interact with distillation in ARVDs — can quantized teacher-student pipelines further reduce inference cost?
