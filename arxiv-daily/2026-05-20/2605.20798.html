Key Finding

18 / 20 Transformer Modifications Fail to Transfer at 1–3B Scale

A rigorous multi-seed benchmark of 20 post-2021 Transformer modifications under iso-data, iso-compute, iso-recipe conditions finds that only Softpick (rectified softmax) survives both Bonferroni correction and 3B cross-scale stability. HybridNorm passes statistics at 1.2B but diverges in all three seeds at 3B. Most reported improvements vanish inside the noise floor.

Why This Matters
Validation loss is dangerously unreliable for ranking attention modifications.

Sigmoid Attention and SSMax reach within 2–3% of baseline validation loss, yet CLIMB-12 downstream accuracy collapses by 6–16 points. The loss-downstream decoupling enlarges several-fold for attention-output changes at 1.2B. Pretraining loss alone cannot be trusted for these modules.

Critical Edge

The loss–downstream decoupling concentrates exclusively in attention-output modifications — not in FFN, normalization, or residual-stream changes.

Residual-side failures show consistent loss↔CLIMB correspondence. This structural asymmetry means anyone evaluating attention modifications must use downstream benchmarks. The paper proposes three concrete recommendations: multi-seed noise floors (R5), mandatory downstream eval for attention-output changes (R6), and cross-scale stability checks (R7).
