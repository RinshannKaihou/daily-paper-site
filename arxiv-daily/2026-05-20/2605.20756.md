> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了预条件优化器（AdamW、Sophia、Shampoo）在随机训练中存在的两种有限样本偏差——梯度-预条件器耦合偏差和非线性逆偏差——并提出基于微批次交叉拟合与delta方法方差校正的统一框架，在Qwen2.5-0.5B预训练中将held-out loss降低0.07–0.15 nats，代码已开源。

> **TL;DR:** Identifies two previously overlooked finite-sample biases in preconditioned LM optimizers: (1) gradient-preconditioner coupling from shared minibatches, and (2) nonlinear inversion bias via Jensen's inequality. Proposes a single-batch correction using cross-fitted preconditioning (independent microbatch groups for numerator vs. denominator) plus delta-method variance-corrected inversion. On Qwen2.5-0.5B pretraining from scratch, reduces held-out loss by 0.149 nats (AdamW), 0.070 nats (Sophia), and 0.110 nats (Shampoo). SFT gains are small/neutral. All experiments are single-GPU at 0.5B scale with no seed averaging.

## 核心贡献

- **识别并分解了预条件优化器中的两种有限样本偏差**：梯度-预条件器耦合偏差（同一minibatch同时估计梯度和预条件器，导致统计依赖）和非线性逆偏差（即使预条件器估计无偏，其逆/逆根操作因Jensen不等式仍引入偏差）。
- **提出了统一的单批次偏差校正框架**：交叉拟合预条件（将minibatch拆分为独立微批次组分别估计分子和分母）+ 方差校正逆运算（利用微批次间变异性，通过delta方法减去主导偏差项），将逆偏差从O(m⁻¹)降至O(m⁻³/²)。
- **覆盖三种优化器家族**：AdamW（对角矩）、Sophia-G（对角曲率）、Shampoo（矩阵预条件），从最简单的对角方法到最复杂的矩阵方法验证了框架的通用性。
- **提供了收敛性理论保证**：在非凸光滑条件下证明偏差校正改善平稳性界；在PL条件下证明线性收敛至更小的邻域。
- **在Qwen2.5-0.5B预训练中取得一致改进**：held-out loss分别降低0.149、0.070和0.110 nats，且在混合质量数据上表现一致。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies and decomposes two finite-sample biases in stochastic preconditioned optimization: gradient-preconditioner coupling bias (from estimating gradient and preconditioner on the same minibatch) and inverse-preconditioner bias (from the nonlinearity of matrix inversion via Jensen's inequality).
- Proposes a unified single-batch correction framework: cross-fitted preconditioning (splitting the minibatch into independent microbatch groups for numerator vs. denominator estimates) combined with variance-corrected inversion (using delta-method expansion to subtract the leading O(1/m) bias term, reducing it to O(m⁻³/²)).
- Validates the framework across three optimizer families of increasing structural complexity: AdamW (diagonal moment), Sophia-G (diagonal curvature), and Shampoo (matrix preconditioning).
- Provides convergence guarantees: Theorem 1 proves the bias-reduction properties of cross-fitting + delta-method correction; Theorems 2–3 translate estimator-level improvements into better nonconvex stationarity bounds and PL-condition linear convergence.
- Demonstrates consistent pretraining improvements on Qwen2.5-0.5B: 0.149-nat (AdamW), 0.070-nat (Sophia), and 0.110-nat (Shampoo) reductions in held-out loss.

## Methodology

The paper develops both theoretical analysis and practical algorithms for correcting bias in stochastic preconditioned updates.

**Problem formulation.** For a population objective f(θ) = E[ℓ(θ;z)] and population preconditioner P(θ), the ideal update is u*(θ) = P(θ)⁻¹∇f(θ). In practice, with minibatch estimates ĝ and P̂, the standard update û = P̂⁻¹ĝ is biased even when both estimators are individually unbiased.

**Two bias sources:**
1. **Coupling bias** (Eq. 4): E[P̂⁻¹ĝ] ≠ E[P̂⁻¹]·E[ĝ] when both are estimated from the same minibatch. This cross-moment term is not part of the target population vector field.
2. **Inverse bias** (Eq. after Sec 4.2): E[P̂⁻¹] ≠ P⁻¹ because inversion is nonlinear. In the scalar case, f(p) = 1/(p+λ) has f''(p) = 2/(p+λ)³ > 0, so by Jensen's inequality E[1/(p̂+λ)] ≥ 1/(E[p̂]+λ).

**Correction framework (Algorithm 1):**
- **Cross-fitted preconditioning (Sec 5.1):** Split the batch into independent groups A (numerator) and B (denominator). Since ĝ_A and P̂_B are independent, E[P̂_B⁻¹ĝ_A] = E[P̂_B⁻¹]·E[ĝ_A], eliminating coupling bias entirely.
- **Delta-method inverse correction (Sec 5.2):** Split B into m microbatches B₁,…,Bₘ. Compute microbatch preconditioners P̂_j and their mean P̄. The variance of the microbatch mean estimates the leading bias term: for diagonal entries, the corrected inverse is T̃(p̄_k) = 1/(p̄_k+λ) − Var̂(p̄_k)/(p̄_k+λ)³ (Eq. 5). For Shampoo's matrix preconditioner, the correction is applied eigen-direction-wise in the eigenbasis of P̄.
- **LOO variant for AdamW:** A naive two-fold split reduces the denominator to half the batch, making updates too conservative. The leave-one-out (LOO) construction with 64 folds of 8 examples keeps each fold's denominator at 504/512 of the batch, preserving scale while removing same-microbatch coupling.

**Convergence theory (Sec 6):** Under smooth nonconvex assumptions with bounded relative bias ρ_bias and variance σ², Theorem 2 shows the average gradient norm bound improves when ρ_bias decreases. Under the PL condition, Theorem 3 shows linear convergence to a smaller suboptimality neighborhood. Corollary 1 formalizes when bias correction strictly improves convergence.

## Results & Highlights

**Pretraining (Qwen2.5-0.5B, random init, FineWeb-Edu, 500 steps):**
- AdamW LOO+Jensen BC: eval loss 4.687 vs. 4.836 standard (**−0.149 nats**)
- Sophia full BC: eval loss 6.595 vs. 6.665 standard (**−0.070 nats**)
- Shampoo full BC: eval loss 5.681 vs. 5.792 standard (**−0.110 nats**)

**Mixed-quality pretraining (20% span-replaced training data, clean eval):**
- AdamW LOO+Jensen BC: eval loss 4.803 vs. 4.823 standard (**−0.019 nats**), with higher training loss (fits corrupted spans less aggressively)

**Instruction tuning (from pretrained Qwen2.5-0.5B, Alpaca-style):**
- AdamW: small gain (−0.001 on 500-example eval)
- Sophia: effectively neutral
- Shampoo: effectively neutral

**Ablations (Table 4):** Cross-fit alone and inverse-only alone both contribute; full BC combining both is generally strongest for pretraining. For SFT, the components are less separable.

**Negative findings honestly reported:**
- Naive two-fold AdamW full-BC at same learning rate was *substantially worse* than standard (Appendix Table 13), motivating the LOO construction.
- Shampoo SFT full-BC trails standard in attention+MLP coverage, consistent with theory (low-noise regime where variance cost dominates).

## Authors & Credibility

All authors are from **Fastino Labs**, an independent research lab. The work is grounded in classical statistics (delta method, jackknife, cross-fitting from double/debiased ML) applied to optimizer design — a genuinely novel cross-pollination. Code is publicly available at `github.com/fastino-ai/preconditioner-bias-correction`. The paper is transparent about limitations: single-GPU budget, 0.5B scale only, no seed averaging. The theoretical analysis (Theorems 1–3) is rigorous with proofs in appendices. The authors explicitly surface negative findings (two-fold AdamW failure, Shampoo SFT neutrality), which is a strong credibility signal.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The identification of gradient-preconditioner coupling as a distinct statistical bias, combined with the cross-fitting + delta-method correction framework, is genuinely novel. The connection to double/debiased ML's sample-splitting ideas is creative and well-motivated. This is orthogonal to Adam's standard initialization bias correction.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Theoretical guarantees (Theorems 1–3) clearly state what is proved: estimator-level bias reduction translates to better convergence constants under standard assumptions. The authors are careful not to claim global optimality improvements. The honest reporting of negative results (two-fold failure, Shampoo SFT) strengthens trust.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Public code, single-GPU experiments (A100), standard model (Qwen2.5-0.5B), explicit hyperparameters in Table 5. However, no seed averaging means the pretraining deltas (0.07–0.15 nats) are point estimates, not statistically robust effect sizes.
- **Significance: Promising but limited evidence.** The 0.5B scale is small; whether these gains persist at 7B+ is unknown. The correction adds implementation complexity (microbatch splitting, LOO construction for AdamW, eigenbasis correction for Shampoo). The AdamW gain depends heavily on the LOO variant — the naive split hurts, suggesting practical deployment requires careful tuning.
- **Red flags:**
  - Single-scale evaluation (0.5B) with no seed averaging — the reported improvements could partially reflect noise.
  - The best AdamW result uses a different learning rate for dense parameters (9e-4 vs. 6e-4 standard), making the comparison not purely apples-to-apples.
  - The 500 pretraining steps is relatively short; whether gains compound over longer training is unknown.
  - SFT gains are near noise floor, suggesting the method's practical impact may be limited to long pretraining runs.

## Strengths

- Identifies a fundamental statistical issue (two distinct biases) in optimizers used millions of times daily, yet previously overlooked.
- Unified framework spanning diagonal moment, diagonal curvature, and matrix preconditioning — demonstrating generality.
- Clean theoretical story: Theorem 1 precisely characterizes what cross-fitting and delta correction each contribute.
- Honest about limitations: single-GPU budget, no seed averaging, negative findings explicitly surfaced.
- Minimal computational overhead for diagonal optimizers (just microbatch variance tracking).
- Open-source code.

## Limitations

- **Scale:** Only evaluated at 0.5B parameters. Whether gains persist, shrink, or grow at 7B–70B is entirely unknown.
- **Statistical rigor:** No seed averaging; all results are point estimates. The authors acknowledge this but it limits confidence in the exact effect sizes.
- **Not apples-to-apples for AdamW:** The best AdamW BC result uses a higher learning rate for dense parameters (9e-4 vs. 6e-4), so the gain conflates bias correction with learning rate tuning.
- **Short training:** 500 pretraining steps (~10M tokens) is very short by modern standards. Long-run behavior is untested.
- **SFT impact is negligible:** Near-zero gains in instruction tuning limit the practical appeal for most practitioners.
- **Implementation complexity:** The LOO variant requires 64 microbatches, eigenbasis correction for Shampoo adds complexity. Adoption cost vs. benefit ratio is unclear.
- **Shampoo eigenbasis approximation:** The correction operates in the eigenbasis of the *averaged* preconditioner, not one that jointly diagonalizes microbatch variability — this is an approximation whose error is not fully characterized.

## Relevance

本文对随机预条件优化器中有限样本偏差的理论分析与校正框架，直接关联到大语言模型训练效率的提升。对理解AdamW、Sophia、Shampoo等优化器的隐含统计偏差及其对预训练质量的影响具有重要的理论和实践参考价值，尤其适合关注训练稳定性与样本效率的研究者。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the pretraining gains scale to 7B and 70B models with trillions of training tokens?
- Does seed averaging confirm the 0.07–0.15 nat improvements, or are they partially due to noise?
- Can the LOO construction be made more efficient (e.g., fewer folds) without losing the bias-correction benefit?
- How does bias correction interact with distributed training (gradient accumulation, tensor/pipeline parallelism)?
- Is there a regime (small batch, aggressive learning rate, noisy data) where the correction has even larger effects?
- Could the delta-method correction be combined with other orthogonal improvements (e.g., learning rate scheduling tricks, warmup modifications)?
- Does the framework extend to K-FAC, AdaFactor, or Lion-style optimizers?
