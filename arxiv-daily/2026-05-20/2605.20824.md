> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Markovian Circuit Tracing (MCT) 诊断框架，利用六个合成 HMM 任务族验证小型 Transformer 激活中是否编码了粗粒度马尔可夫状态转移结构，发现模型学到近似贝叶斯预测器（超出贝叶斯损失均值仅 0.0138），且通过将恢复的状态质心补丁到激活中可将 KL 散度从 0.196 降至 0.053，显著优于所有控制基线。

> **TL;DR:** Introduces Markovian Circuit Tracing (MCT), a diagnostic pipeline that tests whether transformer activations encode coarse state-transition structure using six synthetic HMM benchmark families with exact ground truth (beliefs, transitions, counterfactual targets). Two-layer causal transformers (~275k params) learn near-Bayes predictors (excess loss 0.0138). State-forcing patching of recovered centroids reduces KL to exact HMM counterfactuals from 0.196 to 0.053, beating wrong-state, mean-activation, random-activation, shuffled-label, and even true-state-centroid controls. Strongest in persistent and easy-separable regimes; weakest in ambiguous-emission and six-state regimes.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 MCT 诊断管线：** 一个系统化的四步框架（信念恢复、转移恢复、马尔可夫阶检验、状态强迫），用于检测 Transformer 激活中是否包含可用的粗粒度状态转移信息。
- **构建六族合成 HMM 基准：** 涵盖可分离/模糊发射、持久/高熵转移、3 态/6 态等不同难度，每种三个随机种子，提供精确的潜在状态、转移矩阵、贝叶斯信念向量和强迫状态反事实目标。
- **发现近似贝叶斯学习能力：** 小型因果 Transformer（2 层、128 维、275k 参数）在全部 18 个实验中均学到接近贝叶斯最优的预测器，超出贝叶斯损失均值仅 0.0138。
- **验证状态强迫干预的有效性：** 恢复的状态质心补丁将 KL 散度从 0.196 降至 0.053，改善量 0.143，甚至优于真实状态标签质心（0.074），表明模型的内部状态更接近信念状态而非采样标签。
- **揭示信念-状态优于隐标签的观点：** 证据表明 Transformer 的有效内部表示是预测性信念状态，而非 HMM 的采样隐状态标签的直接拷贝。

## Key Contributions

- **MCT diagnostic pipeline:** A four-test framework (belief recovery via linear probes, transition recovery via K-means clustering, Markov order testing via NLL comparison, state-forcing interventions) that diagnoses whether activation-derived state abstractions carry usable transition information.
- **Six-family HMM benchmark with exact ground truth:** Easy-separable, ambiguous-emission, persistent, high-entropy, three-state, and six-state families — each with 3 seeds — providing exact latent states, Bayesian belief vectors, transition matrices, Bayes-optimal predictions, and forced-state counterfactual targets.
- **Near-Bayes learning result:** Two-layer causal transformers (width 128, 4 heads, ~275k params, trained on sequences of length 64) achieve mean excess loss of 0.0138 over Bayes-optimal across all 18 runs.
- **State-forcing intervention breakthrough:** Patching a recovered-state centroid at position 20 in the second post-residual layer reduces KL to the exact forced-state target from 0.196 to 0.053, outperforming all five control conditions including the true-state centroid (0.074).
- **Belief-state vs. label-state insight:** The recovered centroid beating the true-state centroid suggests the model's functionally relevant internal state is closer to a predictive belief abstraction than to the sampled HMM latent label.

## Methodology

The paper introduces Markovian Circuit Tracing (MCT) as a diagnostic pipeline evaluated on synthetic HMM benchmarks:

**Task design:** Six HMM families are constructed with varying difficulty:
- Easy-separable (K=4, V=6, banded transitions ρ=0.60, low-entropy emissions)
- Ambiguous-emission (K=4, V=6, same transitions but high-entropy emissions c=2.50)
- Persistent (K=4, V=6, sticky transitions ρ=0.82)
- High-entropy (K=4, V=6, near-uniform transitions)
- Three-state (K=3, V=5) and Six-state (K=6, V=8)

Each family uses 3 random seeds, generating 6000 training and 1500 validation sequences of length 64.

**Model:** Tiny two-layer causal transformer (width 128, 4 attention heads, MLP width 256, ~275k parameters).

**MCT pipeline has four diagnostic tests:**
1. **Belief recovery:** Linear ridge probe maps residual activations to exact Bayesian belief vector b_t. Mean belief KL varies from 0.152 (ambiguous) to 0.772 (easy).
2. **Transition recovery:** K-means clustering over residual activations produces discrete states; recovered transition matrix is compared to ground truth via row-wise KL. Belief K-means serves as upper bound; residual K-means and PCA+K-means recover partial structure.
3. **Markov order testing:** Order-0, 1, and 2 empirical models over recovered states are evaluated by held-out NLL. Strongest first-order gains in persistent (0.075) and easy (0.056) families; near-zero in high-entropy.
4. **State forcing (key intervention):** A recovered-state centroid is patched into the model at position 20 in `resid_post_1`. The resulting next-token distribution is compared to the exact HMM counterfactual target e_i T E via KL divergence. Controls include: wrong-state centroid, mean activation, random activation, shuffled-label centroid, and true-state centroid.

**Evaluation scope:** Sweeps over cluster counts M ∈ {2,3,4,5,6,8,10} and layers from embeddings through final LN.

## Results & Highlights

- **Near-Bayes prediction (Table 2):** Mean validation loss 1.7044 vs. Bayes-optimal 1.6907. Excess loss ranges from 0.001 (high-entropy) to 0.029 (persistent) across families.
- **Belief information in activations:** Linear probes recover partial belief information. Belief KL is lowest for ambiguous (0.152) and highest for easy (0.772) — the easy family's low cluster accuracy (0.414) suggests the model's continuous belief representation resists simple discrete clustering.
- **Transition recovery (Table 5):** Belief K-means achieves row-wise KL of 0.050–0.208 across families; residual K-means achieves 0.027–0.673. The gap indicates headroom for better extractors.
- **State forcing — the clearest result (Table 3):**
  - Unpatched: KL 0.196 ± 0.186
  - Recovered centroid: **0.053 ± 0.064** (best)
  - True-state centroid: 0.074 ± 0.062
  - Mean activation: 0.147 ± 0.162
  - Random activation: 0.167 ± 0.170
  - Shuffled-label centroid: 0.157 ± 0.213
  - Wrong-state centroid: 0.206 ± 0.219
- **Layer-wise trends (Table 6):** Later layers generally expose stronger transition and belief structure, though this depends on the family. High-entropy family stays flat across layers.
- **Cluster-count sensitivity (Figure 7):** MCT behaves gracefully under cluster count misspecification, with belief reconstruction KL increasing smoothly as M deviates from true K.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: Moderate-to-good.** The individual components (probing, K-means clustering, activation patching) are well-known. The contribution is the *integration* into a unified diagnostic pipeline with exact HMM ground truth as a benchmark. The state-forcing intervention design and the finding that recovered centroids beat true-state centroids is genuinely interesting.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Six diverse HMM families with varying difficulty, multiple seeds, comprehensive controls (5 control conditions for state forcing), layer sweeps, cluster-count sweeps. Honest about where the method fails (ambiguous-emission, six-state regimes).
- **Reproducibility: High.** Synthetic tasks with specified generation rules (Table 1 gives exact parameters), model architecture fully specified (2-layer, 128 width, 4 heads), intervention protocol detailed in Appendix B. Supplementary materials include source files.
- **Significance: Niche but solid.** The benchmark fills a real gap — prior work studied probing, automata extraction, and causal interventions separately. Having them tested together against exact ground truth is useful. However, the practical impact is limited by the synthetic/tiny-model scope.
- **Red flags:**
  1. **Only tiny models tested.** 275k parameters is orders of magnitude below practical transformers. Whether any findings transfer is unknown.
  2. **Synthetic-to-real gap is enormous.** HMMs with 3–6 states and 5–8 vocabulary symbols are astronomically simpler than natural language.
  3. **Clustering fit on validation data (Appendix B).** The authors acknowledge this — centroids and clusters are fit on validation activations, not a held-out split. This inflates performance relative to a strict protocol.
  4. **High-entropy family serves as a near-null control.** Row-wise KL of 0.027 and NLL gain of 0.000 mean MCT essentially finds no signal — which is expected but raises the question of whether the benchmark has enough hard-but-solvable cases.
  5. **Single author, no institutional peer review infrastructure.** The paper is careful and well-structured, but single-author work carries inherent limitations in iterative refinement.

## Strengths

- Exemplary experimental control: exact ground truth for every quantity of interest, enabling rigorous evaluation that is impossible on real data.
- Comprehensive control conditions for the state-forcing intervention — five distinct controls that rule out alternative explanations.
- Honest, nuanced reporting. The paper clearly states MCT is "a benchmarked diagnostic for coarse predictive state dynamics, not a full hidden-state recovery algorithm."
- The finding that recovered centroids outperform true-state centroids is conceptually important — it suggests the right abstraction for understanding transformer internals may be belief-like, not label-like.
- Benchmark designed with headroom: belief K-means consistently beats residual K-means, showing the task is not saturated and can evaluate future extractors.
- Well-structured supplementary material with per-family breakdowns enabling detailed inspection.

## Limitations

- **Scale:** Only 275k-parameter, 2-layer transformers tested. The paper explicitly acknowledges this — "the tasks are synthetic, the models are tiny, and the reference extractor is simple."
- **Synthetic scope:** HMM sequences of length 64 with 3–6 hidden states and 5–8 vocabulary symbols are far removed from natural language or real-world sequential tasks.
- **Simple extractor:** K-means clustering on residual activations is a crude state extraction method. The paper acknowledges this as a reference baseline but does not explore learned extractors, SAEs, or probabilistic models.
- **Validation-set fitting:** Clusters and centroids are fit on validation activations (acknowledged in Appendix B), which partially compromises the intervention evaluation.
- **Weak-signal regimes:** MCT finds essentially no signal in the high-entropy family and struggles with six-state/ambiguous-emission regimes — precisely the settings closer to real-world complexity.
- **No claim about natural language:** The authors are careful to state the narrow scope, but readers should be aware the paper does not address whether similar state structures exist in LLMs processing text.

## Relevance

与机械可解释性（mechanistic interpretability）和 Transformer 内部状态动态研究直接相关。MCT 提供了一种有精确真值基准的诊断管线，可用于评估和比较不同的状态提取方法（如稀疏自编码器、概率状态模型），对于理解 Transformer 如何在部分观测任务中维护和更新内部信念状态具有方法论参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does MCT perform on larger transformers (e.g., 1B+ parameters) trained on more complex synthetic tasks or real language data?
- Can learned state extractors (e.g., SAEs, VQ-VAE) close the gap between residual K-means and belief K-means on this benchmark?
- The recovered centroid beating the true-state centroid is striking — does this hold across different architectures and task families? What does it imply for how we should think about "internal states" in neural networks?
- Could MCT's state-forcing protocol be adapted to discover latent state structure in transformers trained on natural language, perhaps using constructed tasks with known grammatical state machines?
- The paper notes that belief information is present but hard to cluster discretely in the easy-separable family — would continuous state representations (e.g., Gaussian mixture models over activations) be more appropriate than K-means?
- How does the choice of patch position (currently fixed at token 20) affect the state-forcing results?
