> **一句话总结：** 本文通过神经科学工具（MEI、RSA、THINGS语义维度）深入分析视觉MoE模型中专家的实际表征，发现路由统计严重低估了专家的调谐多样性——专家并非简单的类别选择器，而是对连续的视觉和语义维度具有分布式调谐，且跨初始化稳定地涌现出有生命-无生命的二分结构。

> **TL;DR:** This paper goes beyond standard routing-based analysis of vision MoE experts by applying neuroscience-inspired tools (Most Exciting Inputs, THINGS behavioral dimensions, Representational Similarity Analysis) to sparsely-gated contrastive MoE-CNNs. Key findings: (1) experts that appear category-tuned via routing are actually tuned to continuous shape/colour/texture dimensions that cut across category boundaries; (2) gating logits and readout magnitudes are not linearly related — what routes to an expert differs from what it encodes; (3) all experts show similar category separability despite distinct feature tuning; (4) an animate-inanimate bipartition emerges stably across 10 independent seeds and across E∈{4,8,16} configurations, echoing the dominant organisational axis in human ventral temporal cortex.

## 核心贡献

- 将MoE可解释性从门控级统计扩展到专家级调谐和群体级表征几何分析，证明仅看路由会系统性地误判专家编码的内容。
- 利用THINGS数据集的66个人类行为导出的视觉/语义维度作为分布外探针，将专家调谐从类别标签解耦为可解释的连续维度。
- 通过二阶RSA和轮廓系数聚类，证明专家的表征结构（特别是有生命-无生命二分）在10次独立初始化中稳定重现。
- 发现门控分配与专家读出响应之间没有线性关系，路由偏好和专家编码特征本质不同。
- 所有专家的类别可分离性高度相似，尽管各自的MEI和特征调谐谱截然不同，表明功能等效的表征几何可以由不同的人群响应模式实现。

## Key Contributions

- Extend MoE interpretability from gating-level statistics to expert-level tuning (MEIs) and population-level representational geometry (RSA, category separability), demonstrating that routing alone systematically mischaracterises what experts encode.
- Use the THINGS Similarity dataset's 66 human behaviour-derived visual/semantic dimensions as an OOD probe to recover interpretable, continuous tuning profiles that disentangle expert preferences from training-set categorical structure.
- Show representational stability via second-order RSA across 10 independent seeds per configuration (E∈{4,8,16}), identifying stable animate-inanimate bipartition motifs using silhouette-based cluster selection.
- Demonstrate a dissociation between gating logits and readout response magnitudes — what the router sends to an expert is not what the expert most strongly encodes.
- Reveal that all experts achieve similar pairwise category separability despite divergent feature tuning, illustrating that equivalent representational geometry can arise from distinct population-level response patterns.

## Methodology

**Model.** A ResNet-based CNN backbone (5 convolutional blocks) adapted into a sparse MoE by inserting a noisy top-k gating network after the first 3 shared blocks. The remaining 2 blocks are replicated into E independent experts (E∈{4,8,16}, k=2). Total parameter count is held approximately constant (~46M) across configurations by scaling each expert's channel width by 1/√E.

**Training.** Self-supervised contrastive learning (NT-Xent, τ=0.5) on STL10 unlabeled split (100k images, 100 epochs). A projection head is used during training and discarded afterward. Load balancing is enforced via a coefficient-of-variation penalty on per-expert importance.

**Analysis pipeline:**
1. **Routing analysis** — proportion of each STL10 class routed to each expert; top-3 images by gating logit.
2. **Expert-level tuning (MEIs)** — bypass the router, force all images through each expert, rank by L2 readout norm. Conducted on both STL10 test set and THINGS dataset (1,854 object concepts).
3. **Continuous dimension regression** — non-negative Lasso regression from 66 THINGS behavioural dimensions to expert gating logits and readout magnitudes separately, with nested 5-fold cross-validation.
4. **Category separability** — pairwise logistic regression on expert readout activations (STL10 + THINGS).
5. **Representational stability** — first-order RDMs per expert on THINGS, second-order RSA (Spearman) across all (model×expert) pairs, agglomerative clustering with silhouette-optimised cluster count.

## Results & Highlights

- **Routing suggests categorical specialisation** (e.g., trucks, birds, planes per expert), but MEI analysis reveals experts actually respond to lower-level features like grainy textures, spatial frequency, or shape properties that merely correlate with those categories in STL10.
- **THINGS regression** shows experts tuned to dimensions like "house-related/furnishing-related", "metallic/artificial", "repetitive/spiky" rather than discrete categories. Gating and readout tuning profiles diverge substantially.
- **No linear relationship between gating logits and readout magnitudes** for any expert (Figure 4), confirming routing ≠ encoding.
- **Category separability heatmaps** are strikingly similar across experts despite distinct MEIs, dissociating representational geometry from neuronal implementation.
- **Stable animate-inanimate bipartition** recovered across all configurations and all 10 seeds via second-order RSA clustering. This persists even in E=16 where top-k=2 does not consistently select the same pair.

## Authors & Credibility

Both authors are from the School of Psychology at the University of Auckland. Katherine Storrs has published on visual perception and computational neuroscience. The interdisciplinary combination of MoE architecture design with cognitive neuroscience analysis methods is well-executed and novel. The paper draws appropriate connections between MoE expert specialisation and cortical visual area organisation.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The application of neuroscience tools (MEIs, RSA, THINGS behavioural dimensions) to MoE interpretability is genuinely new. The finding that routing mischaracterises expert encoding is an important corrective to the prevailing analysis paradigm.
- **Rigor: Good.** Multiple analysis angles (routing, MEI, regression, separability, RSA) converge on consistent conclusions. 10 independent seeds per configuration provide statistical reliability. Nested cross-validation for regression is appropriate.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate.** Architecture and training details are provided, but code is not linked. STL10 and THINGS are public datasets. The analysis pipeline involves several non-trivial implementation choices (e.g., non-negative Lasso, silhouette-based cluster selection).
- **Significance: High for MoE interpretability and neuroscience-MoE bridge.** Moderate for practical MoE engineering — the model is small-scale (~46M params) and trained on a toy dataset.
- **Red flags:** (1) STL10 is a very small, narrow dataset (10 classes). Whether these findings generalise to large-scale MoEs (e.g., Switch Transformers, Mixtral) trained on internet-scale data is unknown and the authors acknowledge this. (2) Holding total parameters constant is atypical for MoE practice and may influence the specialisation dynamics observed. (3) The contrastive objective may produce different specialisation patterns than supervised or generative objectives.

## Strengths

- Compelling multi-method analysis that goes far beyond standard routing heatmaps.
- Creative use of THINGS behavioural dimensions as an OOD probe — a genuine methodological contribution.
- Clear demonstration that routing is an insufficient proxy for expert specialisation.
- Stable animate-inanimate finding echoes neuroscience literature nicely, strengthening the MoE-brain analogy.
- Well-written with clear motivation and logical flow.

## Limitations

- Small-scale model and dataset (STL10, ~46M params) — generalisability to industrial-scale MoEs is uncertain.
- Self-supervised contrastive objective only; supervised or generative objectives may yield different specialisation patterns.
- Parameter-count-controlled design is unusual for MoE practice; findings may not transfer to standard scaling regimes.
- The animate-inanimate bipartition could be an artefact of the binary top-k=2 selection (though the authors argue against this by showing the same split in E=8 and E=16).

## Relevance

该论文将MoE可解释性从表面路由统计推进到深层表征分析，对理解大规模稀疏模型中专家的实际编码具有重要意义。其跨初始化稳定性的发现对MoE架构设计和路由机制的理论理解都有启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do these findings generalise to ViT-based MoEs and to models trained on internet-scale datasets (e.g., ImageNet-21k)?
- How does the choice of training objective (contrastive vs. supervised vs. generative) affect the nature and stability of expert specialisation?
- Can expert-level tuning profiles be used to guide more efficient routing or expert pruning?
- Would increasing top-k beyond 2 change the animate-inanimate bipartition or allow finer-grained stable motifs to emerge?
- Could the THINGS-dimension regression approach be applied to language MoEs using linguistic behavioural dimensions?
