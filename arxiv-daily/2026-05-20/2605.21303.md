> **一句话总结：** 本文将机制可解释性中的电路解释建模为归纳理论构建问题，提出因果功能签名（CFS）与基于归纳逻辑编程（ILP）学习的架构签名 τ_arch，在 15 个电路（5 任务×3 模型）上实现 3.9–4.2× 的结构分离优势（优于 Weisfeiler-Lehman 核 1.3–1.5×），并支持跨尺度与跨架构的迁移验证。

> **TL;DR:** This paper formalizes circuit interpretation in mechanistic interpretability as inductive theory construction, giving each discovered circuit a Causal Functional Signature (CFS, from direct logit attribution + attention-weighted token role profiles) and an architectural signature τ_arch (a Horn clause learned by Inductive Logic Programming from scale-invariant structural predicates). On 15 circuits (5 tasks × 3 models: Pythia-14M, Pythia-1B, LLaMA-3.2-1B), ILP signatures achieve 3.9× IOI separation on Pythia-1B and 4.2× on LLaMA, versus 1.3–1.5× for the Weisfeiler-Lehman graph kernel baseline, and support cross-scale/cross-family transfer with binding mechanisms (Location |ΔT diff|=0.08, Path=0.26) more stable than selection (IOI sign reversal −0.91→4.37) or comparison (GT gap 2.74 cross-family).

## 核心贡献

- 将机制可解释性中的电路解释重新定义为**归纳理论构建**问题，识别出"形式化连贯性"（formal coherence）——而非发现本身——是跨实验累积机制知识的核心瓶颈。
- 提出**因果功能签名（CFS）**：基于 Direct Logit Attribution (DLA) 与注意力加权的 token 角色轮廓（语言角色 + 任务特定角色）两级表征组件行为，将机制声明锚定在因果证据上。
- 利用**归纳逻辑编程（ILP）**从尺度不变的结构谓词（component_ratio、layer_span、has_motif 等）学习架构签名 τ_arch（Horn 子句），使机制声明可读、可比较、可迁移。
- 通过 **θ-子sumption** 建立机制声明的形式化偏序（泛化/特化/不可比），揭示出 IOI 泛化所有角色子句、Time 泛化 Path 等非平凡细化层级。
- 在 15 个电路（5 任务×3 模型）及扩展的 30 电路/模型评测上，ILP 签名实现 3.9–4.2× 结构分离优势，并在 Pythia-14M→Pythia-1B（70× 尺度差）与 LLaMA→Pythia-1B（跨家族）方向上完成端到端迁移。

## Key Contributions

- A coherence-first formulation: reframes circuit analysis as an inductive science of mechanisms where the bottleneck is cross-experiment comparability and knowledge accumulation, not discovery.
- Causal Functional Signatures (CFS): a two-level causal characterization of circuit behaviour using Direct Logit Attribution (DLA) scores δ_v and attribution-weighted attention profiles π_v over linguistic roles (subject, object, verb, function, entity) and task-specific roles, grounding mechanistic claims in causal evidence.
- ILP-learned architectural signatures τ_arch: explicit Horn clauses over scale-invariant structural predicates (component_ratio, layer_span, has_motif, normalized faithfulness), making mechanism claims inspectable, testable, and transferable across model scales.
- Formal comparison via θ-subsumption: induces a partial order over learned signatures (generalizes/specialises/incomparable), revealing a non-trivial refinement hierarchy (e.g., IOI generalizes all role clauses; Time generalizes Path).
- Empirical evaluation on 15 circuits (5 tasks × 3 models) scaled to 30 circuits per model across 10 tasks, showing ILP achieves 3.9–4.2× structural separation versus 1.3–1.5× for Weisfeiler-Lehman kernel, and supporting end-to-end transfer across a 70× scale gap and across architecture families.

## Methodology

Each discovered circuit C=(V_C, E_C) (extracted via EAP-IG with k=200 edges retained) is associated with a logical identity L(C)=⟨σ, τ_arch, S_ILP, κ⟩, where σ is the CFS, τ_arch is the learned architectural signature, S_ILP summarizes the learned theory, and κ records validation statistics.

**Causal Functional Signature (CFS):** Two quantities per node v∈V_C. (1) Direct Logit Attribution (DLA) score δ_v∈R measuring additive contribution to the correct-answer logit; components with |δ_v|<ε_DLA are flagged as causally marginal. (2) Attribution-weighted attention profile π_v: attention distribution over input positions weighted by |δ_v|, with positions labelled by linguistic roles (subject, object, verb, function, entity, other) and task-specific roles (e.g., scaffold, repeated_name). The CFS is σ={⟨v, δ_v, π_v^ling, π_v^task⟩ : v∈V_C}, stored as Layer 2 facts enriching ILP background knowledge.

**Formal Circuit Representation:** Four layers of typed ground facts. Layer 0: provenance (task, model, discovery method). Layer 1: graph structure (node, type, layer, edge, component-ratio, relative-size, layer-span, named-motif predicates, normalized faithfulness). Layer 2: CFS σ. Layer 3: learned τ_arch. Layer indices normalized as ρ(ℓ)=ℓ/L∈[0,1] for scale invariance.

**Learning Architectural Signatures (ILP):** ILP_arch learns one Horn clause per task via one-vs-rest classification. Positive examples E+ are circuits implementing the target mechanism; negative E− implement other mechanisms. Background knowledge B_arch contains scale-invariant structural facts. Motifs ranked before clause search by attribution-weighted information gain ATTR_IG(m)=IG(m)×(1+s̄_m). Two complementary hypothesis pools: Pool A (named motif predicates like has_motif(C, early_attn_peak)) and Pool B (blind structural predicates like edge-type patterns). Clause selection by one-vs-rest F1, shorter clauses preferred under ties (parsimony bias).

**Validation:** A circuit is retained only if behavioural consistency (Acc_T(C)≥θ_behav) and causal relevance (ΔT(C)=Acc_T(M)−Acc_T(M^{ablate,C})≥θ_causal) hold, with approximate ε-minimality checked by greedy edge ablation.

**Comparison via θ-subsumption:** Clause γ1 subsumes γ2 (γ1⪯_θ γ2) if some substitution θ makes every literal in γ1 appear in γ2. Relaxed comparison abstracts away numeric thresholds, capturing predicate-level structure.

**Transfer:** Four stages — (i) retrieve τ_arch,α and σ_α; (ii) identify candidates in target model whose scale-normalized predicates and ILP signature distance match within tolerance δ_ILP=0.30; (iii) screen via behavioural and causal validation; (iv) select best validated candidate, falling back to full discovery if none passes.

**Models/tasks:** Primary evaluation: 15 circuits (5 tasks × 3 models). Tasks: semantic role binding (Location, Time, Path), IOI, Greater-Than. Models: Pythia-14M, Pythia-1B, LLaMA-3.2-1B. Scaled evaluation: 10 task types (8 semantic roles, IOI, GT) × 3 circuits per task from disjoint prompt subsets = 30 circuits per model. Circuits extracted with EAP-IG (k=200 edges). All thresholds empirically chosen with sensitivity analysis (Appendix H.2).

## Results & Highlights

- **CFS reveals distinct computational strategies:** In Pythia-1B, IOI routes through 85 attn→attn edges forming three-hop attention chains (attn_chain_3) characteristic of the copy mechanism, while semantic role circuits rely on 64–96 mlp→mlp edges with mlp_heavy motifs; LLaMA preserves this pattern (44 vs. 0–2 attn→attn edges). CFS feature vectors cluster significantly by task family on Pythia-1B (p=0.008, permutation test).
- **Role-consistent attention across scales:** Dominant attention roles determined more by task type than scale. Location object-dominant at 76%/74%/71% (Pythia-14M/1B/LLaMA); Time function-dominant at 81%/87%/100%; IOI shifts from object-dominant (50% at 14M) to function-dominant (58%/67% at 1B/LLaMA), suggesting the copy mechanism relocates attention from name positions to structural tokens as capacity increases.
- **ILP achieves 3.9–4.2× structural separation vs. 1.3–1.5× for WL kernel:** On Pythia-1B, ILP achieves 3.9× IOI separation (4.2× on LLaMA), compared to 1.3× for the Weisfeiler-Lehman graph kernel and 1.1–1.5× for feature-vector Euclidean distance. A random forest with access to the same predicates achieves only 60% leave-one-out accuracy (misclassifying GT and IOI), indicating explicit logical structure provides discrimination beyond the feature inventory.
- **θ-subsumption reveals a non-trivial refinement hierarchy:** Under relaxed θ-subsumption (numeric thresholds abstracted away), the single-predicate IOI clause generalizes all role/GT clauses; within the role family, Time generalizes Path (which adds attn_chain_3), while Location is incomparable to both (uses different motifs mlp_heavy, mid_layer_attn_peak). The hierarchy changes with scale: in Pythia-14M, Time's size-only clause generalizes all others and Location≡Path, reflecting greater structural homogeneity.
- **Cross-scale and cross-family transfer succeeds:** End-to-end transfer accepts candidates for all 5 tasks in both Pythia-14M→Pythia-1B (70× scale gap) and LLaMA→Pythia-1B (cross-family) directions. For IOI and GT, the best accepted candidate matches the same task with high selectivity (2/37–41 for GT, 8/41 for IOI). Binding mechanisms preserve causal contribution across architectures (Location |ΔT diff|=0.08, Path=0.26), while selection (IOI) undergoes a sign reversal from 14M to 1B (−0.91→4.37) and comparison (GT) shows large gaps (2.74 cross-family).
- **Scaled evaluation exposes within-task variability:** Structurally stable tasks retain high confidence (IOI: 0.93, GT: 0.89 F1), whereas prompt-sensitive semantic roles drop substantially (Location: 0.31, Beneficiary: 0.31 F1) in the expanded 10-task pool.
- **Random subgraph significance:** Real circuits outperform 50 size-matched random circuits per task in 29/34 cases on Pythia-14M and 28/35 on Pythia-1B (p<0.05), with random subgraphs consistently yielding ΔT≈0.

## Critical Assessment

- **Evaluation scale is limited and the binding/selection/comparison schema is narrow:** The authors explicitly state (Limitations) "the binding/selection/comparison schema is not a universal theory of internal mechanisms" and "extending to additional architectural families remains important future work." Only two Pythia scales and one cross-family model (LLaMA-3.2-1B) are tested; no decoder-only architectures beyond LLaMA, no models beyond 1B parameters.
- **Architectural signature thresholds are empirically hand-tuned:** All numeric thresholds in the Horn clauses (e.g., R>0.63 for IOI attention ratio, N<38 for Location size) are "empirically chosen," and although sensitivity analysis (Appendix H.2) is cited, the dependence on hand-chosen threshold ranges is a generalization risk the paper acknowledges.
- **CFS depends on a lightweight dependency parser and greedy ε-minimality:** The paper concedes "CFS role profiles depend on a lightweight dependency parser" and "the greedy ε-minimality procedure may miss non-contiguous minimal subcircuits," introducing a parsing-quality dependency and a minimality approximation that could bias the signatures.
- **Transfer does not test task behaviour on novel prompts:** The authors state "we test only whether [τ_arch] predicts causal contribution across models, not task behaviour on novel prompts, the level at which our framework and proxy-task approaches are complementary rather than competing." So the transfer claims are about structural/causal preservation, not behavioural generalization.
- **Within-task variability undermines signature stability for semantic roles:** In the scaled evaluation, prompt-sensitive semantic roles drop to 0.31 F1 (Location, Beneficiary), meaning the learned signatures are unstable across prompt subsets for nearly a third of the task types — a significant robustness concern.

## Strengths

- Addresses a genuine meta-scientific gap: the lack of a shared formal representation language for accumulating and comparing mechanistic findings across experiments.
- Grounded in well-established formal methods (Inductive Logic Programming, θ-subsumption, Horn clauses) rather than ad-hoc heuristics, producing inspectable and falsifiable mechanism hypotheses.
- The what/how separation (CFS for functional behaviour, τ_arch for structural realization) is well-motivated and cleanly decouples causal evidence from portable structure.
- Scale-invariant encoding (normalized depth ρ(ℓ)=ℓ/L, component ratios) is a principled choice enabling cross-scale comparison where raw node Jaccard is near-zero (0.09–0.24).
- Quantitative baseline comparison against WL kernel, random forest, and feature-vector distance with a clear 3–4× separation advantage, plus random-subgraph significance tests (29/34 and 28/35 cases, p<0.05).

## Limitations

- Only Pythia (14M, 1B) and LLaMA-3.2-1B tested; no models beyond 1B, no non-LLaMA decoder families, no encoder-decoder architectures.
- Semantic role signatures unstable across prompt subsets (Location, Beneficiary drop to 0.31 F1 in scaled evaluation).
- CFS role profiles depend on a dependency parser; greedy ε-minimality may miss non-contiguous minimal subcircuits.
- Transfer validated only for causal contribution preservation, not task behaviour on novel prompts.
- All numeric thresholds empirically chosen; sensitivity analysis cited but threshold robustness across architectures not fully demonstrated.
- Code link anonymised ("[anonymised for review]"), so reproducibility cannot be verified at time of writing.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the ILP architectural signatures remain stable and discriminative on larger models (7B–70B) where circuits are more distributed and polysemantic?
- How sensitive are the learned Horn clauses to the choice of structural predicate vocabulary — would a blind-pool-only search (Pool B) recover the same separation without named motifs?
- Can the θ-subsumption hierarchy predict novel circuit reuse before discovery, i.e., does a generalizing clause retrieve valid candidates in a held-out task?
- How does the framework handle partially overlapping or hierarchical circuits where sub-circuits interact, rather than the disjoint single-mechanism circuits tested here?
- Would replacing the dependency-parser-based CFS with a parser-free token-role assignment (e.g., learned role probes) change the qualitative binding/selection/comparison distinctions?
- Can the transfer procedure be extended to validate task behaviour on novel prompts, not just causal contribution, to bridge structural and behavioural generalization?
