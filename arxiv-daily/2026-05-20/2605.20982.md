> **一句话总结：** DODOCO 通过对5种MoE架构×6种数据条件的因子实验，系统证明EP扩展不能减少路由负载不均衡、mock token基准测试严重高估了真实数据的路由Gini系数（最高2.35倍），并发现五种架构自然分为"数据弹性"和"持续集中"两类，为AlltoAll感知的互连和调度设计提供了校准依据。

> **TL;DR:** DODOCO is a 5-architecture × 6-data-condition factorial study of MoE expert-parallelism routing imbalance on H100s. Three findings: (1) Scaling EP from 4 to 32 changes per-expert max/mean token ratio by at most 5% — the straggler is intrinsic to the model's routing decision, not fixable by EP scaling. (2) Mock-token benchmarks overestimate routing Gini by up to 2.35× and fabricate a batch-size scaling trend that disappears with real text. (3) Five architectures cleave into two stable bands — data-resilient (MHA, Mamba-2: Gini 0.105–0.150 on real text) and persistently concentrated (MLA, GDN: Gini > 0.24 everywhere) — with GQA as intermediate. These bands, not EP degree or mock-data profiles, are the right workload input for interconnect and dispatch design.

## 核心贡献

- **首次系统性测量MoE路由不均衡随EP度数的变化：** 在5种架构上使用匹配窗口协议（相同GBS和迭代预算），证明EP从4扩展到32，每专家max/mean token比率变化不超过5%，结论是EP扩展不是解决路由不均衡的有效手段。
- **揭露mock token基准测试的系统性偏差：** mock数据将路由Gini高估最高2.35倍，且制造出"批量越大不均衡越严重"的虚假趋势，该趋势在真实文本输入下完全消失（Nemotron的Dirichlet α差距达47倍）。
- **发现MoE架构的两类路由模式：** 数据弹性类（MHA、Mamba-2）在真实文本下Gini降至0.105–0.150；持续集中类（MLA、GDN）在所有条件下Gini均>0.24。GQA为中间态。
- **因果证据归因于隐状态几何而非路由器权重：** 交换MLA和MHA的路由器，路由Gini跟随模型主体而非路由器变化，说明注意力机制产生的隐状态几何决定了路由分布的多样性上界。
- **为互连和调度设计提供三维校准建议：** 针对弹性类使用均匀负载模型；针对持续集中类设计局部性感知调度和拓扑；GQA按持续类参数调度的同时最适合预测性放置。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic measurement of MoE routing imbalance as a function of EP degree across five architectures using a matched-window protocol, showing EP scaling changes per-expert max/mean by at most 5% — the straggler is intrinsic to routing decisions, not to expert placement.
- Demonstration that mock-token benchmarks overestimate routing Gini by up to 2.35× and fabricate a batch-size scaling trend (mock Gini rises with GBS; real-data Gini is flat), invalidating capacity-planning assumptions built on synthetic data.
- Discovery of two stable architecture classes — data-resilient (MHA, Mamba-2: Gini 0.105–0.150 on wikitext) and persistently concentrated (MLA, GDN: Gini > 0.24 on every condition) — with GQA as an intermediate case.
- Causal attribution via router-swap experiment: routing Gini follows the hidden-state geometry produced by the model body, not the router weights, establishing attention mechanism design as the upstream lever.
- Per-layer depth analysis showing the data-resilient pair starts near-uniform at layer 0 (amenable to hash routing) while the persistent pair is concentrated from the first MoE layer.

## Methodology

DODOCO runs a 5 × 6 factorial design on four H100 nodes (NHR@FAU Helma supercomputer): five MoE checkpoints spanning five sequence-mixer designs (DeepSeek-V2-Lite MLA, DeepSeek-MoE-16B MHA, Qwen3-30B GQA, Nemotron-30B Mamba-2, Qwen3.5-35B GDN) crossed with six data conditions (mock, shuffled, remapped, Romansh, Opus, wikitext). All models use released checkpoints with as-deployed routing configurations. A separate EP scan (4→32 ranks, matched GBS=32, 200 measurement iterations) isolates the effect of expert-parallelism degree, and a GBS sweep (64→2048) isolates batch-size effects.

Three metrics capture the send-counts matrix S: per-expert max/mean ratio (EP-invariant routing property), per-rank Gini coefficient (operational AlltoAll skew), and fitted symmetric Dirichlet α (uniformity-sensitive complement for the near-uniform regime). Per-rank Gini is validated against measured dispatch P99 latency with r ≥ 0.76 within each architecture. The MLA/MHA pair shares expert count (64), top-k (6), gating, and load-balancing strategy, differing only in attention mechanism — a controlled pair isolating the attention effect. A router-swap ablation (MLA's router on MHA hidden states and vice versa) provides causal evidence.

Instrumentation patches Megatron-Core's MoEAlltoAllTokenDispatcher to log per-layer input_splits with CUDA-event timing on every rank. All data conditions use public HuggingFace datasets tokenized through standard Megatron-Core.

## Results & Highlights

- **EP invariance:** Per-expert max/mean varies ≤ 5% across EP={4,8,16,32} for all five architectures. The ratio is flat across the full NVLink→IB bandwidth transition (~18× per-GPU bandwidth drop).
- **Mock overestimation:** Mock-to-wikitext Gini ratio ranges from 1.03× (GDN, barely moves because already concentrated) to 2.35× (Mamba-2). Nemotron shows a 47× gap in Dirichlet α.
- **Fabricated batch-size trend:** MLA mock Gini rises from 0.33→0.62 as GBS goes from 64→2048; wikitext Gini is flat at 0.39–0.43. Same pattern for all five architectures.
- **Two-class taxonomy:** Data-resilient (MHA Gini 0.105, Mamba-2 Gini 0.150 on wikitext) vs persistently concentrated (MLA Gini 0.245, GDN Gini 0.285). GQA intermediate at 0.240.
- **Router swap:** MLA hidden states route at Gini ~0.34 regardless of which router scores them; MHA hidden states route at ~0.27. Architecture-class signal follows the model body.
- **Per-layer profiles:** MHA traces a U-shape (layer-0 Gini 0.17); Mamba-2 starts lowest (0.12) with a single spike at depth fraction 0.45; MLA is flat-high (0.35–0.50); GDN oscillates (0.40–0.63).
- **Temporal stability:** GQA has highest consecutive-iteration Pearson r (0.93); GDN collapses to r ≈ 0 within five steps, making history-based placement impractical for GDN.

## Authors & Credibility

All four authors (Bole Ma, Jan Eitzinger, Harald Koestler, Gerhard Wellein) are from Erlangen National High-Performance Computing Center (NHR@FAU), a well-established HPC research group. The work is funded by the Free State of Bavaria and uses the Helma H100 cluster at NHR@FAU. The paper provides HotI-quality communication characterization for the MoE-EP domain, extending the dense-transformer characterization by Anthony et al. (HotI 2024). All data conditions are reproducible from public sources; the only project-specific code is a small Megatron-Core instrumentation patch. The experimental design is rigorous — controlled architecture pair (MLA/MHA), matched-window EP protocol, six-condition data gradient with shuffled/remapped controls, and multiple complementary metrics (Gini, Dirichlet α, max/mean, P99 latency).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. This is the first cross-architecture, multi-data-condition characterization of MoE routing imbalance as a function of EP degree. The two-class taxonomy and the mock-data artifact findings are genuinely new contributions to the MoE systems literature. The router-swap experiment provides clean causal evidence rather than correlation.

**Rigor:** Strong. The 5×6 factorial design with controlled pairs (MLA/MHA), matched-window EP scans, and shuffled/remapped controls isolates confounds methodically. The paper is unusually transparent about what it does not claim and about measurement limitations (per-iter Gini stdev of 0.05–0.08 bounds cross-condition resolution to ~0.02).

**Reproducibility:** Good. All datasets are public, tokenization uses standard Megatron-Core, models are released checkpoints. The instrumentation patch is described as small. Hardware (H100 cluster) is standard for the community.

**Significance:** High for the MoE systems community. The finding that mock benchmarks systematically mislead has direct implications for published interconnect proposals that rely on synthetic dispatch patterns. The two-class taxonomy provides actionable guidance for dispatch and topology design.

**Red flags:** Scale is limited to EP=32 (8 nodes); production MoE training uses 256–4096 GPUs. The EP-invariance claim is not validated at production scale. EP=4 is missing for three 128-expert models. All five models use aux-loss-based balancing — aux-loss-free routing (DeepSeek-V3) is explicitly out of scope. The Gini-vs-P99 validation is at EP=16 only. Romansh results have a tokenizer-fertility confound (r=0.94 with Gini). These limitations are honestly disclosed.

## Strengths

- Exceptional experimental design: controlled architecture pair, matched-window protocol, six-condition data gradient with isolating controls.
- Clean causal evidence from router-swap and random-init experiments.
- The two-class taxonomy is simple, empirically grounded, and immediately actionable.
- Honest about limitations and scope; the "What we do not claim" section is exemplary.
- Per-layer depth analysis adds granularity beyond the headline Gini numbers.

## Limitations

- EP scan capped at 32 ranks (8 nodes); whether the flat max/mean holds at EP=256+ is open.
- Three of five architectures lack the EP=4 data point due to GPU memory constraints.
- Gini-vs-P99 correlation not validated across the EP scan (only at EP=16).
- All models use aux-loss balancing; results may not extrapolate to aux-loss-free routing (DeepSeek-V3, Ling).
- No end-to-end training throughput measurements under system-side interventions.
- Per-iter Gini noise (stdev 0.05–0.08) limits resolution of small cross-condition differences.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the EP-invariance result hold at EP=256 or EP=1024 on multi-rail InfiniBand or Slingshot fabrics typical of production MoE training clusters?
- How do aux-loss-free routing strategies (DeepSeek-V3 bias-shift, Ling sigmoid routing) change the two-class taxonomy?
- Can the persistently concentrated class (MLA, GDN) benefit from architecture-aware expert relayout that targets the per-layer concentration profiles shown in Fig. 4?
- What is the end-to-end training throughput impact of locality-aware dispatch designed specifically for the persistent class?
- Does the two-class split survive fine-tuning (SFT, RLHF) or is it a pretraining-only phenomenon?
