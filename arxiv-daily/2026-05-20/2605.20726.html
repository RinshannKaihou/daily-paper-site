Everywhere Valid Bounds on False Discovery Proportions in Conformal Inference

Ziang Song, Ying Jin, Emmanuel J. Candès Stanford / UPenn 2026-05-20
conformal-inference false-discovery-proportion multiple-testing outlier-detection simultaneous-inference

Existing conformal methods control only the expected FDR and break under adaptive threshold selection. This paper constructs finite-sample, distribution-free upper bounds on the FDP that hold simultaneously across all rejection thresholds via Monte Carlo sampling from the tractable joint distribution of null conformal p-values.

Key Finding
Simultaneous FDP bounds that remain valid at every threshold — enabling post-hoc threshold selection without invalidating guarantees.
Achieved by sampling from the known joint distribution Pn,m of null conformal p-values and inverting variance-adaptive summary statistics into high-probability envelope functions.

Why This Matters
Practitioners routinely adjust rejection thresholds after seeing data. Existing conformal FDR guarantees silently fail under this adaptive regime.
This framework provides the first finite-sample simultaneous FDP bounds for conformal selection — valid at any threshold the user ultimately chooses, post-hoc.
Critical Edge
Variance-adaptive envelopes dominate DKW baselines near t ≈ 0 — the region where most real discoveries live.
Truncated Higher Criticism statistics exploit heteroskedastic structure of the empirical CDF, producing bounds far tighter than linear DKW in the small-t regime.

Methodology Pipeline

Conformal Uniform VariablesUnder exchangeability, null conformal p-values follow a tractable joint distribution Pn,m simulable via i.i.d. Uniform[0,1] replacement.
Envelope via Monte CarloDraw B replications from Pn,m, compute the (1-δ)-quantile of a summary statistic T, invert to obtain envelope G(t) bounding the empirical CDF.
Variance-Adaptive StatisticTruncated Higher Criticism: T(F) = supt∈[ℓ,r] (F(t) − μ(t)) / σ(t)β with β=½, restricted range to curb boundary inflation.
TighteningSelf-refinement exploits monotonicity of false discoveries. Null-proportion bound 𝔂̂̂0 estimates the true null count. Both O(m²).
FDP ExtractionCoupling argument embeds null p-values into Pn,m vector; envelope directly yields simultaneous FDP bound for outlier detection (Thm 4.6) and conformal selection (Thm 5.4).

Results & Highlights

ValiditySynthetic outlier detection (n=m=1000, 90% purity): MC-THC achieves 96% empirical coverage at nominal 90%. DAVIS drug-target dataset: 92/100 runs valid.
Tightness vs. BaselineMC-THC bounds substantially less conservative than DKW linear baseline, especially near t ≈ 0 where linear envelope over-covers from failing to adapt to heteroskedasticity.
Tightening ImpactSelf-refinement + 𝔂̂̂0 consistently yields sharpest bounds. Null-proportion tightening dominates at low purity; self-refinement dominates at high purity.
Post-hoc SelectionSimultaneous bounds remain valid under adaptive threshold choice (e.g., increasing BH level until ≥5% selected). Post-hoc BH level fails to upper-bound realized FDP in this regime.

Limitations

Inherent ConservatismSimultaneous validity over all thresholds is more conservative than pointwise validity at a single pre-specified threshold.
No Covariate ShiftExchangeability assumption excludes covariate shift between calibration and test — common in real deployment.
ScalingO(m²) for tightening may limit very large test sets, though acceptable for typical conformal applications (m in thousands).
