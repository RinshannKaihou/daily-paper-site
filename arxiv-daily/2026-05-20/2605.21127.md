> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了一个关键问题——显式推理模型在标准指令微调数据上进行微调时，会逐渐丧失生成结构完整推理链的能力（推理链坍塌），但最终答案准确率可能保持不变，导致这种退化难以被常规评估指标发现；作者提出了结构化评估框架和轻量级损失遮蔽策略来检测和缓解这一问题。

> **TL;DR:** This paper identifies "reasoning-trace collapse" — when explicit reasoning models (e.g., DeepSeek-R1, Qwen3) are fine-tuned on standard instruction-response data without reasoning traces, they progressively lose the ability to generate structurally valid reasoning chains while final-answer accuracy can remain stable or even improve. The authors introduce a structural evaluation framework (valid/empty/missing/truncated reasoning rates + reasoning-conditioned accuracy) and release ThinkPack, a lightweight Python library. Experiments across 4 small reasoning models show standard SFT rapidly suppresses valid reasoning, and simple loss-masking strategies can substantially mitigate collapse without needing teacher-generated traces.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统定义并研究了"推理链坍塌"（reasoning-trace collapse）现象：显式推理模型在无推理链的指令数据上微调后，逐步丧失生成结构完整推理链的能力
- 提出结构化评估框架，将答案正确性与推理链有效性分离，引入有效推理率（VR）、空推理率（ER）、缺失推理率（MR）、截断推理率（TR）以及条件推理准确率（Rpass@1）等指标
- 发布 ThinkPack 工具库，提供模型无关的推理感知训练、解析和评估接口
- 发现微调数据中"缺失推理"的表示方式（空标签 vs 省略标签）直接影响坍塌的严重程度和形式，且模型默认格式并不总是最优选择
- 验证了简单的损失遮蔽策略可以在不需要教师模型生成推理链的情况下大幅缓解坍塌问题

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes "reasoning-trace collapse" as a structural failure mode where explicit reasoning models lose the ability to produce complete, non-empty reasoning traces during fine-tuning, even when task accuracy is preserved
- Introduces a structural evaluation framework that separates answer correctness from reasoning-trace validity, measuring valid (VR), empty (ER), missing (MR), and truncated (TR) reasoning rates alongside reasoning-conditioned pass@1 (Rpass@1)
- Releases ThinkPack, a lightweight open-source Python library providing model-agnostic utilities for reasoning-aware chat construction, trace parsing, loss masking, and metric computation
- Demonstrates that the representation of missing reasoning in fine-tuning data (empty-think vs no-think formats) directly shapes the severity and form of collapse, and that model-default formatting is not reliably protective
- Shows that simple loss-masking strategies (excluding empty reasoning regions from the loss) can substantially preserve explicit reasoning without requiring teacher-generated traces, while teacher distillation is not uniformly better

## Methodology

The study evaluates four open-weight small reasoning models — Qwen3-8B, Olmo-3-7B, Llama-R1-8B (DeepSeek-R1 distill), and Nemotron-7B — across different reasoning formats (explicit reasoning fields, prefixed reasoning, distillation-based) and base architectures.

**Fine-tuning setup:** Models are fine-tuned on Chemistry L-3 from SciKnowEval (science QA with explanations but no reasoning delimiters) using full-precision LoRA (r=16, alpha=16), AdamW optimizer (lr=1e-5), 3 epochs, evaluated every 100 steps. All evaluations use greedy decoding with no system prompt on fixed 256-sample subsets.

**Experiment 1 (Format-induced collapse):** Compares two ways of representing missing reasoning — empty-think (inserts empty `<think ></think >` block) vs no-think (omits reasoning tags entirely). Tests across all models on both formats.

**Experiment 2 (Mitigation):** Compares three strategies: (1) masked-think (exclude empty reasoning from loss), (2) response-only (mask prompt + reasoning, only supervise final answer), and (3) distillation (augment training data with GPT-5-mini generated reasoning traces).

**Evaluation tasks:** Chemistry (in-domain), GSM8K (math OOD), EvalPlus (code OOD). Metrics: pass@1, VR, ER, MR, TR, Rpass@1.

## Results & Highlights

- **Reasoning collapses rapidly during standard SFT:** For Qwen3-8B and Nemotron-7B, valid reasoning rate drops to 0% on Chemistry while pass@1 improves to 50-57%. On GSM8K, Qwen3-8B's VR falls from 98% to 58% with empty-think, while Rpass@1 stays at 98% — the model reasons effectively when it does reason, but reasons far less often.
- **Format matters significantly:** Llama-R1-8B collapses Chemistry VR to 0% under no-think but preserves 71% VR under empty-think. Qwen3-8B is the opposite — more stable with no-think than its default empty-think. Model defaults are not reliably protective.
- **Failure modes differ by model:** Qwen3-8B, Llama-R1-8B, Nemotron-7B collapse through empty/missing traces; Olmo-3-7B collapses primarily through truncation.
- **Loss masking is effective and lightweight:** Masked-think and response-only strategies preserve substantially more valid reasoning than standard SFT. For Qwen3-8B, masking maintains 82-84% final VR on Chemistry vs 0% for standard SFT.
- **Distillation is not uniformly better:** Teacher traces work excellently for Qwen3-8B (99% VR) and Llama-R1-8B (~99% VR), but fail badly for Olmo-3-7B on GSM8K (VR drops to 21%) — worse than masking. Teacher-generated traces are not a guaranteed solution.
- **Learning rate sensitivity:** Reasoning-trace collapse is robust across learning rates (5e-6 to 2e-5). Lower LR delays collapse but weakens adaptation; higher LR accelerates both adaptation and collapse.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The concept of "reasoning-trace collapse" as a distinct structural failure mode is novel and timely. The separation of answer correctness from reasoning-trace validity fills a genuine gap in the evaluation literature. Most prior work assumes reasoning traces are present and focuses on quality; this work asks the more basic question of whether traces survive adaptation at all.

**Rigor (Good):** The experimental design is well-controlled: same LoRA config, same training data, systematic format comparison, per-model default vs alternative format evaluation. The ThinkPack library is a solid contribution for reproducibility. However, some weaknesses: (1) only a single fine-tuning dataset (Chemistry QA), limiting external validity; (2) single training seed per run, so no training variance estimates; (3) only 4 small (7-8B) models — unclear if findings transfer to larger reasoning models; (4) only LoRA adaptation tested, not full fine-tuning.

**Reproducibility (Strong):** ThinkPack is open-source with clear documentation. All training configs, evaluation subsets, and formatting details are specified. Full metric tables with confidence intervals are provided. The distillation prompt is included. Compute requirements are reasonable (~34 H200 GPU-days total).

**Significance (High):** This has immediate practical implications. As reasoning models are increasingly fine-tuned for domain-specific applications, the finding that standard SFT silently destroys reasoning behavior — while answer metrics look fine — is important and actionable. The recommended practice of reporting structural reasoning metrics alongside accuracy is a concrete, low-cost improvement.

**Red flags:** None major. The authors are appropriately cautious in their claims, noting that masking preserves structural reasoning presence but does not guarantee reasoning correctness or faithfulness. The limitation to small models and a single dataset is acknowledged. The distillation baseline is simple (single teacher, one generation attempt strategy) but serves as a reasonable comparison point.

## Strengths

- Identifies and clearly defines a previously underappreciated failure mode with immediate practical relevance
- The structural evaluation framework (VR/ER/MR/TR + Rpass@1) is elegant and the characteristic signature (VR drops, Rpass@1 stable) is a compelling diagnostic
- ThinkPack fills a real tooling gap for reasoning-model experimentation
- Format sensitivity analysis (empty-think vs no-think) reveals non-obvious model-specific behaviors
- Loss masking is shown to be a practical, low-cost alternative to expensive distillation
- Comprehensive metric tables with confidence intervals and per-checkpoint trajectories

## Limitations

- Single fine-tuning dataset limits generalizability of collapse rates and mitigation effectiveness across domains
- Only 4 small (7-8B parameter) models tested; behavior at larger scales is unknown
- Single training seed per configuration — no estimate of training variance
- Only LoRA adaptation studied; full fine-tuning behavior may differ
- Structural metrics measure presence of reasoning, not correctness, faithfulness, or utility of traces
- 256-sample evaluation subsets are practical for within-study comparison but do not estimate full-benchmark performance
- Greedy decoding only; behavior under sampling-based decoding is unexplored

## Relevance

本文对任何使用显式推理模型并进行下游微调的实践者都高度相关。它揭示了微调过程中推理能力可能被静默破坏的风险，并提供了实用的检测和缓解工具，对于推理模型的部署和评估具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does reasoning-trace collapse occur with full fine-tuning (not just LoRA), and if so, is it more or less severe?
- How do findings transfer to larger reasoning models (70B+, DeepSeek-R1-671B)?
- Can the structural metrics be extended to measure reasoning quality/faithfulness, not just presence?
- Does the collapse behavior interact with safety alignment — do fine-tuned reasoning models lose safety-related reasoning before task reasoning, or vice versa?
- Would multi-stage training (reasoning preservation phase → task adaptation phase) be more effective than single-stage masking?
- How does collapse interact with reinforcement learning-based fine-tuning (GRPO, PPO) vs supervised fine-tuning?
