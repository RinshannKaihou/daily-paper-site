Reasoning-Trace Collapse: Evaluating the Loss of Explicit Reasoning During Fine-Tuning

Twist, Yannakoudakis & Zhang · King’s College London · 2026-05-20

Key Finding

Reasoning models silently lose their ability to generate valid reasoning chains during standard fine-tuning — while answer accuracy stays stable or even improves, making the degradation invisible to conventional metrics.

Key Metric
98% → 0%
Qwen3-8B valid reasoning rate on Chemistry collapses to zero under standard SFT, while pass@1 simultaneously improves to 50–57%.
Why This Matters
Standard SFT silently destroys reasoning behaviour while answer metrics look fine — a critical blind spot for anyone deploying fine-tuned reasoning models in production.
Critical Edge
82–84%
Simple loss-masking (excluding empty reasoning from the loss) preserves 82–84% valid reasoning vs 0% for standard SFT — no teacher traces needed.

Methodology

Four small reasoning models evaluated: Qwen3-8B, Olmo-3-7B, Llama-R1-8B (DeepSeek-R1 distill), Nemotron-7B.

  • Fine-tuned on Chemistry L-3 (SciKnowEval) with LoRA (r=16), AdamW, lr=1e-5, 3 epochs.
  • Exp 1 — Format-induced collapse: empty-think vs no-think representation of missing reasoning.
  • Exp 2 — Mitigation: masked-think, response-only, and distillation (GPT-5-mini teacher traces).
  • Evaluation on Chemistry (in-domain), GSM8K (math OOD), EvalPlus (code OOD). Metrics: VR, ER, MR, TR, Rpass@1, pass@1.

Strengths & Limitations

STRENGTHS

  • Defines a previously underappreciated failure mode with immediate practical relevance
  • Structural eval framework (VR/ER/MR/TR + Rpass@1) is elegant and diagnostic
  • ThinkPack fills a real tooling gap; open-source on PyPI
  • Format sensitivity analysis reveals non-obvious model-specific behaviours

LIMITATIONS

  • Single fine-tuning dataset — limits generalisability
  • Only 4 small (7–8B) models tested
  • Single training seed; no variance estimates
  • Structural metrics measure presence, not faithfulness

Authors & Credibility

King's College London. Well-scoped experiments with controlled setup. ThinkPack released under CC-BY-4.0. Transparent about limitations. ~34 H200 GPU-days total compute.

Open Questions

  1. Does collapse occur with full fine-tuning (not just LoRA), and is it more or less severe?
  2. How do findings transfer to 70B+ or DeepSeek-R1-671B scale?
  3. Can structural metrics be extended to measure reasoning quality/faithfulness, not just presence?
  4. Does collapse interact with safety alignment?
  5. Would multi-stage training (preservation → adaptation) outperform single-stage masking?
  6. How does collapse interact with RL-based fine-tuning (GRPO, PPO) vs SFT?
