> **一句话总结：** 本文将机械可解释性引入航空安全认证流程，通过K-SVD字典学习将ViT的patch嵌入分解为内容型/风格型原子，并证明回归头主要依赖内容型原子，进而提出了一种新的运行时监控机制——"模型范围外"(OOMS)检测。

> **TL;DR:** To address EASA's learning-assurance requirement for monitoring a neural network's internal situation representation, the authors train a ViT (DINOv2-S + soft-argmax) for runway keypoint regression on LARDv2, decompose its patch embeddings into 512 sparse atoms via K-SVD, classify atoms as contentful vs. stylistic using cross-subset invariance, show ~82% of regression weight falls on contentful atoms, and introduce out-of-model-scope (OOMS) detection—a runtime monitor that checks whether task-relevant content atoms are present, achieving AUROC 0.96 with only 34 selected atoms.

## 核心贡献

- 将机械可解释性（mechanistic interpretability）首次应用于航空安全认证场景，填补EASA学习保障指南中"内部态势表征监控"这一空白
- 提出ViT + soft-argmax的关键点回归架构，设计极简回归头（仅~1.5k参数），确保任务计算集中在patch嵌入中
- 通过K-SVD字典学习将384维patch嵌入分解为512个稀疏原子，利用LARDv2的四个视觉子集提出基于交叉子集不变性的内容/风格分类方法
- 提出新的运行时安全保障概念——"模型范围外"(OOMS)检测，直接监控模型内部表征是否包含足够的任务相关语义结构

## Key Contributions

- Introduce mechanistic interpretability as a concrete technical tool for aviation learning assurance, directly addressing EASA's requirement for monitoring the model's situation representation
- Design a minimal ViT-based keypoint regressor (DINOv2-S backbone + soft-argmax head) that is both performant (1.91 px median test error) and amenable to mechanistic analysis
- Decompose patch embeddings into 512 monosemantic atoms via K-SVD, and classify them as contentful or stylistic using cross-subset coefficient of variation on the four LARDv2 domains
- Propose out-of-model-scope (OOMS) detection: a runtime assurance monitor that checks whether content atoms are present in the internal representation, achieving AUROC 0.96 with 34 atoms

## Methodology

The paper follows a four-stage pipeline:

1. **ViT Keypoint Regressor**: A DINOv2-S backbone produces 16×16 patch embeddings (384-dim). A soft-argmax (SAM) head maps these to 4 runway keypoint coordinates via learned linear projections + spatial softmax. The head has only ~1.5k parameters, forcing task-relevant computation into the patch embeddings. Training uses Huber loss on LARDv2 with a "BOGO crop" policy and an L/R/C runway disambiguation cue painted in the top-left patch.

2. **Sparse Decomposition via K-SVD**: 512 atoms are learned from importance-sampled test-set patch embeddings (top-4 patches per image by head attention) at sparsity 8. Reconstruction explains >90% variance. Each patch embedding is then sparse-coded via Matching Pursuit.

3. **Content vs. Style Classification**: Atoms are classified by their coefficient of variation (CV) of activation rate across the four LARDv2 subsets (xplane, ges, arcgis, bingmaps). Atoms with low CV (stable across subsets) are contentful; high CV (subset-specific) are stylistic. The split is at the median CV, requiring no tuned threshold. The regression head's reliance score (∥Wd∥ × E[|x|]) shows ~82% of weight falls on contentful atoms.

4. **OOMS Detection**: A constrained logistic regression on binary atom-activation vectors (from attention-pooled image summaries) classifies inputs as in-model-scope (BOGO crops with runway) vs. out-of-model-scope (inverse-BOGO crops without runway). Constraints: bias ≤ 0 (default OOMS), β ≥ 0 (each active atom only increases IMS probability), with L1 regularization for sparsity.

## Results & Highlights

- **Regression**: Pretrained model achieves 1.91 px median error (±0.03) and 4.39 px MAE (±0.14) on LARDv2 test set; scratch model is worse (3.19 px median, 7.45 px MAE)
- **Content reliance**: 81.7% (±5.4%) of effective regression score on contentful atoms for pretrained model; 79.8% (±4.4%) for scratch model—statistically indistinguishable between variants
- **Atom visualization**: Contentful atoms consistently activate on runway structure (edges, threshold markings, horizon lines); stylistic atoms fire on domain-specific appearance (rendering artifacts, color palettes, textures)
- **OOMS detection**: AUROC 0.96 (±0.01) using only 34 (±6) selected atoms; 78% (±8%) of selected atoms are contentful, well above the 50% random baseline
- **Dictionary reconstruction**: >90% variance explained with 8 atoms per patch from a 512-atom dictionary

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The application of mechanistic interpretability (specifically K-SVD dictionary learning) to aviation safety assurance is genuinely novel and directly addresses an open gap in EASA guidance. The OOMS concept is a meaningful contribution distinct from ODD and OOD.
- **Rigor**: The content/style classification via cross-subset CV is elegantly simple but has clear limitations acknowledged by the authors—if all four LARDv2 subsets share a common artifact (e.g., clear skies), atoms responding to it would be mislabeled as contentful. The median-CV threshold is arbitrary but defensible as a simple choice.
- **Reproducibility**: The pipeline uses standard components (DINOv2-S, K-SVD, LARDv2 dataset). The paper reports results across 5 random seeds with standard deviations, which is commendable. However, the BOGO/inverse-BOGO split for OOMS evaluation is somewhat synthetic—real OOMS failures may look different from images that simply lack a runway.
- **Significance**: This is a strong conceptual contribution that bridges mechanistic interpretability and safety certification. The OOMS monitor is practical and inspectable. However, the gap between this demonstration and a certification-ready artifact remains large.
- **Red flags**: (1) The OOMS evaluation only tests on inverse-BOGO negatives (runway absent) vs. standard BOGO positives—this is a near-trivial binary task, so AUROC 0.96, while impressive in atom count, may not reflect real-world difficulty. (2) The content reliance score cannot explain the performance gap between pretrained and scratch models (both ~80% contentful), suggesting the metric may not capture what actually matters for generalization. (3) No comparison to baseline OOD methods on the same task—how does OOMS compare to Mahalanobis distance or ensemble disagreement?

## Strengths

- Directly addresses a regulatory gap (EASA HF-01/HF-03) with a concrete technical proposal
- Minimal regression head design (SAM on ViT) is elegant and forces interpretability into the embeddings
- Cross-subset invariance for content/style classification is simple, requires no labels, and produces qualitatively meaningful results
- OOMS concept is well-motivated and complementary to existing ODD/OOD monitors
- Multi-seed evaluation with variance reporting throughout

## Limitations

- OOMS evaluation is limited to a synthetic binary task (runway present vs. absent); performance on subtler failure modes is unknown
- Content/style classification depends on the LARDv2 domain structure; generalization to other datasets or tasks is untested
- No per-atom ablation or causal intervention to validate that contentful atoms are actually necessary for good predictions
- The approach is demonstrated on a single architecture and task; scalability to larger models or more complex tasks is unclear
- The dictionary learning step (K-SVD) is offline and fixed; model updates would require refitting

## Relevance

本文与"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"方向高度相关——将字典学习、稀疏分解和内容/风格分离等方法应用于ViT的内部表征分析，是mechanistic interpretability在安全关键系统中的具体实践，也为理解模型内部表征提供了新的应用视角。同时涉及"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"方向，提出了新的运行时可靠性监控机制。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does OOMS detection compare to established OOD methods (Mahalanobis, energy-based, ensemble disagreement) on the same data split?
- Would per-atom ablation (zeroing out individual contentful atoms) causally degrade regression performance, or is the content reliance score merely correlational?
- Can the cross-subset invariance criterion generalize to settings where multiple visual domains are not available (e.g., real flight data only)?
- How stable is the dictionary across retraining seeds—are the same semantic atoms recovered, or does the analysis identify different features each time?
- Could SAEs replace K-SVD in this pipeline, and would the resulting atoms be more or less interpretable for certification purposes?
