The Devil is in the Condition Numbers: Why is GLU Better than non-GLU Structure?

GLU's multiplicative gating reshapes the NTK spectrum via a Hadamard product with the data Gram matrix (K̃ ≈ K ⊙ XXᵀ/d), compressing the eigenvalue spread and producing a quadratic improvement in condition number. This yields faster convergence — explaining the characteristic loss-crossing phenomenon observed across activations and architectures — but does not reduce the generalization gap.

Xingyu Lyu, Qianqian Xu, Zhiyong Yang, Peisong Wen, Qingming Huang CAS · UCAS · BAAI 2026-05-20
Key Metric — NTK Condition Number
O(n/d)
↓ O(n/d²)

Non-GLU → GLU under LeCun init with i.i.d. Gaussian inputs. Driven by both a smaller λmax and a larger λmin. Validated on ViT, GPT-2, and MLP-Mixer.

Critical Edge

Analysis is confined to the NTK (lazy training) regime with two-layer networks. Whether the O(n/d²) condition-number advantage survives in the feature-learning regime of 50+ layer Transformers remains unproven. The generalization-gap finding is empirical only — no theory yet explains why GLU fails to help generalization.

Why This Matters

First rigorous NTK-spectral explanation for GLU's empirical superiority in LLMs

  • SwiGLU powers LLaMA, Qwen, DeepSeek — yet no theory explained why until now. The Hadamard product structure is the core insight.
  • GLU's benefit is purely optimization speed, not generalization. This changes how we allocate architecture-search compute.
  • Loss-crossing is now a predicted phenomenon, not anecdotal. Two-phase convergence (large λmax early → large λmin late) is formally derived.
  • Gradient angle analysis links condition number to sample separability — connecting to the model gradient angle theory of Liu et al. (2025).

Optimizer choice (SGD vs. AdamW) has a far stronger effect on generalization gap than GLU vs. non-GLU, suggesting architecture and optimizer research should be decoupled.
