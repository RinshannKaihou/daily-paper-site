OCTOPUS: Optimized KV Cache for Transformers via Octahedral Parametrization Under optimal Squared error quantization

Mark Boss, Vikram Voleti, Simon Donné, Shimon Vainer — Stability AI

Joint quantization of rotated KV-cache triplet directions via octahedral parameterization with an MSE-optimal asymmetric (b+1 / b−1) bit split yields the first codec that survives 2-bit compression across text, video, and audio — where all competitors catastrophically collapse.

Key Metric
+34.7% perplexity at 2-bit · Qwen2.5-7B

vs. PolarQuant +187%, TurboQuant-QJL +772%.
Needle retrieval: 0.81 recall vs. 0.04 / 0.01 for baselines.

Why This Matters
KV cache is the dominant memory bottleneck for long-context LLM serving. OCTOPUS excels precisely where compression matters most — the extreme low-bit regime — unlocking bandwidth-constrained deployments. Cross-modality validation (video diffusion, autoregressive audio) broadens impact well beyond language models.
Critical Edge
Wall-clock decode is slower than bf16 SDPA — OCTOPUS targets bandwidth-constrained, not latency-constrained, workloads. The (b+1, b−1) optimal split is empirically verified only at d = 128. At b=4, the advantage over PolarQuant is ≤3% in some video/audio metrics.

Methodology

  1. Magnitude-direction split — store L2 norm as fp32 (4 B/key); quantize only the unit direction.
  2. Rotation preconditioning — sign-flipped Walsh-Hadamard transform makes each coordinate follow a symmetric-Beta marginal.
  3. Triplet decomposition — partition rotated vector into contiguous 3-coordinate blocks; split each into norm ρ and unit direction n ∈ S².
  4. Octahedral parameterization — piecewise-linear bijection S² → [−1,1]² with constant per-octant Jacobian; enables 1D Lloyd-Max on marginals as a close approximation to sphere-optimal quantization.
  5. MSE-optimal bit allocation — Lagrangian analysis yields bdir − bnrm = O(1), independent of dimensionality. Empirically (b+1, b−1) at d=128 reduces MSE 31–41% vs. uniform (b, b).
  6. Codebooks & joint rounding — offline Lloyd-Max codebooks on oct-coordinates and Beta norm density; local 3×3 search finds joint-optimal indices (byte-identical to full grid).

Fused Triton split-K flash decoder reconstructs keys in registers — zero decode-time bandwidth overhead. Fully data-oblivious, deterministic, no calibration.

Strengths

  • Principled joint quantization with analytically derived optimal asymmetric bit allocation
  • Consistent & often decisive improvements across four modalities at extreme low bit widths
  • Zero decode-time bandwidth overhead — fused Triton kernel reconstructs keys in registers
  • Data-oblivious & deterministic — no calibration, no training, no per-vector scaling
  • Elegant reuse of octahedral map from graphics with theoretically justified marginals
  • Optional QJL residual composes cleanly for unbiased dot-product estimation
  • Honest about limitations — slower wall-clock, boundary-block caveat disclosed

Limitations

  • Increased arithmetic per decode step; wall-clock decode slower than bf16 SDPA
  • (b+1, b−1) split empirically verified at d=128 but not proven in closed form for arbitrary d
  • At b=4, advantage over PolarQuant is marginal (≤3%) in some video/audio metrics
  • Boundary-block protection required on Qwen2.5 (2/28 layers at fp16) — inflates effective memory
  • Not evaluated on head dimensions other than 64 and 128
  • Triplet padding when d not divisible by 3 adds small waste

Open Questions

  1. How does the optimal bit split change for d=64 vs. d=256 — is (b+1, b−1) universal or d-dependent?
  2. What is the wall-clock decode throughput (tokens/s) on H100 / B200 compared to bf16 SDPA and baselines?
  3. Can octahedral parameterization extend to higher-dimensional sub-blocks (4-tuples, hyperspherical map)?
  4. How does OCTOPUS interact with grouped-query attention shared KV heads — do joint quantization errors compound differently?
  5. What is the effective compression ratio accounting for fp32 norm, boundary-block fp16, and optional QJL overhead?
  6. Could triplet joint quantization combine with token eviction strategies for compounded savings?
