Key Finding

Post-Hoc Understanding of Metaphor Processing in Decoder-Only LMs via Conditional Scale Entropy

Metaphorical tokens produce significantly higher Conditional Scale Entropy (CSE) — a wavelet-derived measure of spectral breadth in residual-stream trajectories — than literal tokens at contiguous early-to-mid depth positions across 5 decoder-only architectures (124M–20B params), surviving cluster-based permutation correction (all pcluster < 0.007).

Why This Matters
  • New macro-level diagnostic for mechanistic interpretability — complementary to activation patching, SAEs, circuit tracing
  • CSE is provably invariant to update magnitude (Theorem 1), cleanly separating structural organization from intensity
  • Graded effect sizes (vivid > conventionalized) bridge NLP and the Graded Salience Hypothesis from cognitive linguistics
  • Works across dense and MoE architectures spanning 124M–20B parameters without fine-tuning
  • Specificity controls confirm the signal tracks figurative expression mode, not semantic difficulty or propositional content
