Concentration of General Stochastic Approximation Under Heavy-Tailed Markovian Noise

Agrawal · Maguluri · Zubeldia 2026-05-20 cs.LG · stat.ML · math.OC

First maximal, time-uniform concentration bounds for stochastic approximation under finite-state Markovian noise with general step sizes. The error tail — sub-Gaussian, sub-Weibull, or heavier — is precisely determined by the step-size exponent and whether the random operator contracts, is non-expansive, or expands.

Key Finding

Three tail regimes, one parameter

The quantity D = A₁ + A₃ − 1 — derived from the random operator's contraction and expansion coefficients — cleanly partitions concentration behavior into three regimes. Combined with the step-size exponent z ∈ (0, 1], it dictates whether the iterate error is sub-Gaussian, sub-Weibull, or heavier-than-Weibull. Matching lower bounds (Theorem 2.3) prove these bounds are order-wise optimal and qualitatively unimprovable.

D < 0 → a.s. contractive  |  D = 0 → a.s. non-expansive  |  D > 0 → expansive w.p. > 0
Key Metric

Sub-Gaussian bound

‖xk − x*‖² ≤ Õ(log 1/δ / kz)

with probability 1 − δ, for D < 0 and z < 1.

αk = α / (k+h)z, z ∈ (0, 1]
Why This Matters

From mean-square to tail-risk control

Stochastic approximation is the algorithmic backbone of RL and online optimization. Prior work gave only mean-square rates — useless for quantifying how often things go catastrophically wrong. Concentration bounds reveal the full tail distribution, directly impacting how practitioners set step sizes, choose averaging schemes, and assess reliability.

Critical Edge CAVEAT

Polyak-Ruppert averaging preserves heavy tails

When the operator is expansive (D > 0), Polyak-Ruppert averaging does not lighten the tail — it inherits the heavy-tailed behavior of the original iterates. This challenges the common intuition that averaging always stabilizes and suggests practitioners in expansive settings need robust alternatives.

Methodology

Three-part technical engine

01
Poisson MGF Lyapunov
Novel Lyapunov function built from the MGF of the Poisson equation solution. Cancels Markovian bias in the drift, yielding a tractable one-step MGF recursion.
02
Auxiliary projection
Projected SA onto a ball of radius B around x*. Proven inactive w.h.p. for large B, transferring bounded-iterate analysis to unbounded D ≥ 0 cases.
03
Black-box truncation
Couples original iterates with truncated-noise iterates. Controls truncation bias via contraction and coincidence probability, lifting bounded → unbounded noise.
