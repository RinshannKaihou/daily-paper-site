> **一句话总结：** 本文为约束在线凸优化（COCO）提出 NP-OGD 投影算法，利用自收缩曲线的几何性质给出首个扰动嵌套投影的运动界，在强凸损失下将累积约束违反（CCV）从 O(√(T log T)) 指数级改善至 O(log T)，同时在一般凸损失下将 CCV 从 O(√T log T) 改善至 O(√T)。

> **TL;DR:** For Constrained Online Convex Optimization (COCO) with adversarially chosen quasiconvex constraints, this paper gives a Nested Projected Online Gradient Descent (NP-OGD) algorithm whose movement bound follows from a self-contracted-curve lifting argument (Lemma 4.1). For µ-strongly-convex losses, it achieves O(log T) regret and O(log T) CCV (vs. prior best O(√(T log T)) CCV by Sinha & Vaze 2024, an exponential improvement); for general convex losses, it achieves O(√T) regret and O(√T) CCV (removing the log T factor from the prior O(√T log T) CCV).

## 核心贡献

- 提出 NP-OGD（Nested Projected Online Gradient Descent）算法族：每轮在最新成本函数上做梯度步后投影到迄今为止所有已揭示可行集的交集 St，仅依赖一阶预言机与一次欧氏投影。
- 证明首个扰动嵌套投影运动界（Lemma 4.1）：通过将迭代点提升到 d+1 维空间并构造非标准范数 ‖·‖⊕，证明提升后的序列满足自收缩性，从而由 Stepanov-Teplitskaya 2017 的有限长度定理得到运动上界 Σ‖x_{t+1}-x_t‖₂ ≤ C_d(D + Σ‖e_t‖₂)。
- 对强凸损失，将 CCV 从已知最优的 O(√(T log T)) 指数级改善至 O(log T)，同时保持最优的 O(log T) 遗憾。
- 对一般凸损失，将 CCV 从 O(√T log T) 改善至 O(√T)（去掉 log T 因子），同时维持最优的 O(√T) 遗憾——该问题此前一直开放，Ferreira & Soares 2025 的尝试后被发现证明有误。
- 算法是 anytime 的，适用于比凸函数更广的拟凸（quasiconvex）约束函数，不要求约束函数为凸。

## Key Contributions

- A simple projection-based COCO algorithm family (NP-OGD, Algorithm 1) that takes a gradient step on the last revealed cost function and projects onto the nested intersection St of all feasible sets revealed so far, requiring only one first-order query and one Euclidean projection per round.
- The first movement bound for perturbed nested projections (Lemma 4.1): via a lifting argument to R^{d+1} with a non-standard norm ‖·‖⊕ = ‖u‖₂ + |x_{d+1}|, the lifted sequence is shown to be self-contracted, so the finite-length theorem of Stepanov & Teplitskaya 2017 bounds total movement by C_d(D + Σ‖e_t‖₂).
- For µ-strongly-convex G-Lipschitz losses with step size η_t = 1/(µt), achieves Regret_T ≤ (G²/(2µ))(1+log T) and CCV_T ≤ C_d·G(D + (1+log T)/µ) — an exponential improvement in CCV over the prior O(√(T log T)) of Sinha & Vaze 2024.
- For general convex G-Lipschitz losses with step size η_t = 1/(G√t), achieves Regret_T ≤ (3/2)GD√T and CCV_T ≤ C_d·G(D√ + 2D√T) — removing the log T factor from the prior O(√T log T) CCV.
- The algorithm works for quasiconvex constraint functions (superset of convex), is anytime (no horizon knowledge required), and the prior attempt by Ferreira & Soares 2025 to close this gap contained a proof error.

## Methodology

The COCO setup: at each round t∈[T], the learner plays x_t from a closed convex set X⊆R^d of finite diameter D, then the adversary reveals a G-Lipschitz convex cost f_t:X→R and a G-Lipschitz quasiconvex constraint g_t:X→R with instantaneous constraint g_t(x)≤0. Define the nested feasible sets S_t = ∩_{τ=1}^{t}{x∈X : g_τ(x)≤0}, with S_0=X and S_0⊇S_1⊇...⊇S_T≡X*. Assumption 1.1 (Common Feasibility) requires X*≠∅. Performance is measured against the best fixed x*∈X* that satisfies all constraints: Regret_T = Σf_t(x_t) − min_{x∈X*}Σf_t(x), and CCV_T = Σ(g_t(x_t))_+.

Algorithm (NP-OGD): x_{t+1} = Π_{S_t}(x_t − η_t h_t), h_t∈∂f_t(x_t). Oracle requirement: one first-order query and one Euclidean projection onto S_t per round (efficient for half-spaces, 2-norm balls, etc.).

The key technical difficulty: projection onto successively shrinking S_t introduces movement beyond the gradient step itself, so movement cost is not bounded by step lengths alone. The movement lemma (Lemma 4.1) overcomes this by a lifting argument. For nested closed convex K_0⊇...⊇K_T with diam(K_0)=D and iterates x_{t+1}=Π_{K_t}(x_t − e_t) with arbitrary perturbations e_t, the proof: (1) uses projection non-expansiveness and Pythagorean theorem to derive an approximate self-contraction ‖x_j−x_k‖₂ ≤ ‖x_i−x_k‖₂ + Σ_{r=i}^{j-1}‖e_r‖₂ for i<j<k; (2) defines tail perturbation R_t=Σ_{r=t}^{T}‖e_r‖₂ and lifted vectors A_t=(x_t, R_t) in R^{d+1}; (3) equips R^{d+1} with norm ‖x‖⊕=‖u‖₂+|x_{d+1}|, under which (A_1,...,A_T) is exactly self-contracted; (4) applies Theorem 2.2 (Stepanov-Teplitskaya 2017: self-contracted polygonal lines have bounded Euclidean length C_d‖A_T−A_1‖₂) to get the bound.

CCV bound (Lemma 4.3): since x_{t+1}∈S_t⊆{x:g_t(x)≤0}, we have g_t(x_{t+1})≤0; by G-Lipschitzness of g_t, g_t(x_t)≤G‖x_t−x_{t+1}‖₂; summing gives CCV_T ≤ G·Σ‖x_{t+1}−x_t‖₂. Regret bound (Lemma 4.2) follows standard OGD analysis with the nested projection, using H_t-strong convexity (H_t=0 for merely convex).

## Results & Highlights

- **Strongly-convex losses (Theorem 4.4.2):** For µ-strongly-convex G-Lipschitz losses with η_t=1/(µt), NP-OGD achieves Regret_T ≤ (G²/(2µ))(1+log T) and CCV_T ≤ C_d·G(D + (1+log T)/µ). This improves CCV exponentially from the prior O(√(T log T)) of Sinha & Vaze 2024 to O(log T), while matching the optimal O(log T) regret.
- **General convex losses (Theorem 4.4.1):** For convex G-Lipschitz losses with η_t=1/(G√t), NP-OGD achieves Regret_T ≤ (3/2)GD√T and CCV_T ≤ C_d·G(D√ + 2D√T). This removes the log T factor from the prior O(√T log T) CCV of Sinha & Vaze 2024, closing a gap that was previously open (Ferreira & Soares 2025's attempt had a proof error).
- **Movement bound (Lemma 4.1):** The first movement bound for perturbed nested projections: Σ_{t=1}^{T}‖x_{t+1}−x_t‖₂ ≤ C_d(D + Σ_{t=1}^{T}‖e_t‖₂), where C_d depends only on dimension d (not on T). This is the central technical lever converting the geometric self-contraction property into algorithmic CCV guarantees.
- **Comparison table (Table 1):** Prior work Sinha & Vaze 2024 used projection onto X (not the nested S_t) and required convex constraints, achieving (Regret, CCV) of (O(√T), O(√T log T)) for convex and (O(log T), O(√(T log T))) for strongly-convex. This paper projects onto S_t, works for quasiconvex constraints, and achieves (O(√T), O(√T)) and (O(log T), O(log T)) respectively.

## Critical Assessment

- **Purely theoretical with no empirical evaluation:** The paper contains no experiments; all results are asymptotic big-O bounds. The practical performance of NP-OGD relative to Lyapunov/virtual-queue-based methods (Sinha & Vaze 2024) on realistic problem instances is untested, and the constant C_d's dependence on dimension d is explicitly flagged as not well understood (Section 5: "We also want to better understand the scaling of the pre-factor C_d with the dimension d").
- **Projection cost in high dimensions:** Although the paper notes projection is "efficiently done for many constraint functions (e.g., 2-norms)," projecting onto the intersection S_t of all revealed constraints can become computationally expensive as constraints accumulate, especially for general quasiconvex constraints where active-set methods may not apply. This practical scalability concern is not addressed.
- **Common Feasibility assumption (Assumption 1.1) is strong:** The guarantee requires a fixed action satisfying all adversarial constraints (X*≠∅). If the adversary chooses mutually inconsistent constraints, the framework breaks; the paper does not discuss recovery or relaxed feasibility.
- **Lower bounds not established:** The paper does not prove that O(log T) CCV for strongly-convex losses or O(√T) CCV for convex losses is tight; it states (Section 5) that "establishing tight lower bounds" is a future direction, so optimality is conjectural, not proven.

## Strengths

- Clean, simple algorithm (NP-OGD) with a single unified analysis covering both convex and strongly-convex losses via different step sizes.
- Genuinely novel connection between online optimization and the geometric theory of self-contracted curves, via a non-trivial lifting argument to R^{d+1} with a custom norm.
- Exponential improvement in CCV for strongly-convex losses (O(√(T log T)) → O(log T)) is a qualitative breakthrough, not an incremental log-factor shave.
- Works for quasiconvex constraints (broader than convex) and is anytime (no horizon T knowledge needed), increasing applicability over prior constrained-convex methods.
- Rigorous proof structure with the movement lemma cleanly decoupled from the regret/CCV specialization.

## Limitations

- No empirical evaluation; purely theoretical.
- The dimension-dependent constant C_d's scaling with d is unknown and could be large (the Stepanov-Teplitskaya bound is existential in the constant).
- Projection onto the accumulated feasible set S_t may be costly for high-dimensional or many-constraint problems.
- Requires the Common Feasibility assumption (a fixed action satisfying all constraints exists).
- No matching lower bounds; optimality of the achieved CCV bounds is not proven.
- Self-contraction argument is tied to Euclidean geometry; extension to non-Euclidean/mirror-descent updates is left as future work.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the pre-factor C_d scale with dimension d, and does it blow up for the high-dimensional problems typical in modern ML?
- Can the self-contraction lifting argument extend to mirror-descent or non-Euclidean geometries, as the authors flag as future work?
- Are the O(log T) and O(√T) CCV bounds information-theoretically tight — can matching lower bounds be constructed?
- What is the empirical performance of NP-OGD versus Lyapunov/virtual-queue methods (Sinha & Vaze 2024) on practical constrained online learning benchmarks?
- Can the framework handle approximate or inexact projections, which would be necessary for high-dimensional S_t where exact projection is costly?
- Does the approach extend to Exp-concave losses (explicitly listed as a future direction)?
