Distributed Direct Preference Optimization

Zhanhong Jiang · Iowa State ICML 2026 2026-05-20 DPO · federated · convergence
Key Finding

First convergence theory for distributed DPO

Closed-form smoothness constant L = (β²Cmix + 2β)ζ²φ H and gradient variance ζ²g = 4β²Cmix ζ²φ H derived for both federated (FedDPO) and decentralized (DecDPO) settings, revealing how horizon H, inverse temperature β, and trajectory mixing jointly govern optimization difficulty.

  • FedDPO: convergence decomposes into optimization error, stochastic variance, and heterogeneity drift
  • DecDPO: consensus error contracts at rate ρ² + 8L²η² per round, asymptotically matching centralized DPO
  • Formal lower bounds prove dependence on κ, E, 1/S is intrinsic and unavoidable
Key Metric

Convergence Rates

O(1/√R)
FedDPO stationarity: O(1/(RE)) · DecDPO: matches centralized asymptotically
  • Best ablation gap: 0.039 at E = 6 (vs 0.87 at E = 1)
  • DecDPO ring: lowest final ∇-norm among all methods
Why This Matters

Formal foundations for private alignment

Bridges distributed optimization theory with LLM preference alignment. As model training moves toward federated and multi-org settings, these guarantees are prerequisites for trustworthy, privacy-preserving RLHF pipelines.

Critical Edge

Theory-to-practice gap remains wide

  • Log-linear softmax policy assumption — not transformer architectures
  • DistilGPT-2 (~82 M params), N = 5 agents, 120 pairs each — far from production scale
  • No adaptive optimizers (Adam), no communication compression, no Byzantine robustness
  • DecDPO outperforming centralized DPO in Figure 1 is unexplained (likely artifact of hyperparameter choices)
