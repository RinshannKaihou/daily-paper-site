Correcting Stochastic Update Bias in Preconditioned Language Model Optimizers

Nayak, White, Zaratiana, Zhang, Princis, Atreja, Fawcett, Thomas, Hurn-Maloney, Lewis · Fastino Labs

Two distinct finite-sample biases—gradient–preconditioner coupling and nonlinear inverse bias—silently degrade every major preconditioned optimizer. A unified cross-fitting + delta-method correction framework eliminates both within a single batch.

Key Metric
−0.149
nats held-out loss on Qwen2.5-0.5B pretraining (AdamW LOO + Jensen BC vs. standard, 500 steps)
Why This Matters
Identifies a fundamental statistical issue in AdamW, Sophia, and Shampoo—optimizers used millions of times daily—that was previously overlooked. The correction is orthogonal to existing techniques like Adam's initialization bias correction.
Critical Edge
Only evaluated at 0.5B scale with no seed averaging. Best AdamW result uses a different learning rate (9e-4 vs 6e-4), so gains conflate bias correction with tuning. SFT impact is negligible.

Methodology

The ideal update u* = P(θ)⁻¹∇f(θ) is approximated by minibatch estimates û = P̂⁻¹ĝ, which is biased even when both estimators are individually unbiased.

  • Coupling bias: E[P̂⁻¹ĝ] ≠ E[P̂⁻¹]·E[ĝ] when gradient and preconditioner share the same minibatch.
  • Inverse bias: E[P̂⁻¹] ≠ P⁻¹ because matrix inversion is nonlinear (Jensen's inequality).
  • Cross-fitted preconditioning: Split batch into independent groups A (numerator) and B (denominator) → coupling eliminated.
  • Delta-method correction: Estimate leading bias from microbatch variance; subtract it to reduce inversion bias from O(m⁻¹) to O(m⁻³ᐟ²).
  • LOO variant for AdamW: 64 folds of 8 examples keeps denominator at 504/512 of batch, avoiding the naїve split's conservatism.

Strengths

  • Novel identification of two distinct biases in widely-used optimizers.
  • Unified framework spanning diagonal moment → diagonal curvature → matrix preconditioning.
  • Theorems 1–3 provide clean convergence guarantees under standard assumptions.
  • Honest about limitations: single-GPU budget, no seed averaging, negative results surfaced.
  • Minimal overhead for diagonal optimizers (just microbatch variance tracking).
  • Open-source code.

Limitations

  • Only 0.5B scale; 7B–70B behavior unknown.
  • No seed averaging — all results are point estimates.
  • AdamW best result uses LR 9e-4 vs 6e-4 standard; not purely apples-to-apples.
  • 500 pretraining steps (~10M tokens) is very short by modern standards.
  • SFT impact negligible — limits practical appeal for most practitioners.
  • LOO requires 64 microbatches; Shampoo eigenbasis correction adds complexity.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do the pretraining gains scale to 7B and 70B models with trillions of tokens?
  • Does seed averaging confirm the 0.07–0.15 nat improvements, or are they partially noise?
  • Can the LOO construction be made more efficient (fewer folds) without losing benefit?
  • How does bias correction interact with distributed training (gradient accumulation, tensor/pipeline parallelism)?
  • Is there a regime (small batch, aggressive LR, noisy data) where the correction has even larger effects?
  • Could the delta-method correction combine with other orthogonal improvements (LR scheduling, warmup modifications)?
  • Does the framework extend to K-FAC, AdaFactor, or Lion-style optimizers?
