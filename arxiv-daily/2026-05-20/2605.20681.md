> **一句话总结：** 本文提出了一种在欧氏空间×Grassmann流形的乘积流形上进行尺度校准的中位数均值(MoM)估计器，用于鲁棒的分布式PCA聚合，理论证明了尺度参数α通过特征间隙驱动的子空间不确定性影响协方差、有限块偏差和因子稳健性，并在模拟和百万级单细胞RNA-seq数据上验证了方法的有效性。

> **TL;DR:** This paper develops a scale-calibrated median-of-means (MoM) estimator for robust distributed PCA aggregation on the product manifold $\mathbb{R}^p \times \mathrm{Gr}(r,p)$. The key insight is that the scale parameter $\alpha$ balancing Euclidean mean error against Grassmann subspace error should be calibrated from node-level uncertainty—specifically eigengap-driven subspace variability—rather than set arbitrarily. The author proves a local reduction from product-manifold MoM to a scaled spatial median of node influence errors, derives scale-dependent covariance formulas, establishes high-probability MoM bounds with factorwise breakdown analysis, and validates the method on spiked covariance simulations and a 1.3M-cell single-cell RNA-seq dataset. The calibrated scale adapts to eigengap conditions and PCA rank, offering a principled inferential design for distributed settings with potentially corrupted nodes.

## 核心贡献

- 将鲁棒分布式PCA建模为乘积流形 $\mathbb{R}^p \times \mathrm{Gr}(r,p)$ 上的尺度校准中位数均值估计问题，推导了节点PCA估计的联合影响展开（均值部分为线性扰动，子空间部分为特征间隙加权的协方差扰动）。
- 证明了乘积流形MoM估计器渐近等价于节点影响误差的尺度化空间中位数，由此得到固定节点数非高斯极限和增长节点数高斯极限（含有限块偏差）。
- 推导了尺度依赖的协方差公式 $V_\alpha = H_\alpha^{-1/2} A_\alpha^{-1} S_\alpha A_\alpha^{-T} H_\alpha^{-1/2}$，提出了鲁棒块尺度校准（$\hat{\alpha}_{\text{rPCA}}$）和推断最优尺度选择规则。
- 建立了高概率MoM界、因子分解的坏节点影响分析（均值影响 $\alpha^{-1/2}$、子空间影响 $(2-\alpha)^{-1/2}$）以及节点bootstrap有效性。
- 在spiked covariance模拟和10x Genomics百万级小鼠脑单细胞RNA-seq数据上验证了方法，表明尺度校准自动适应特征间隙驱动的子空间不确定性。

## Key Contributions

- Formulates robust distributed PCA as scale-calibrated MoM estimation on the product manifold $\mathbb{R}^p \times \mathrm{Gr}(r,p)$, deriving a joint influence expansion showing mean influence is linear ($X - \mu_0$) while subspace influence is eigengap-weighted covariance perturbation.
- Proves a local reduction theorem: the product-manifold MoM estimator is asymptotically equivalent to a scaled spatial median of node influence errors, yielding both fixed-$K$ non-Gaussian and growing-$K$ Gaussian limits with explicit finite-block bias.
- Derives the scale-dependent covariance formula and proposes two calibration rules: robust block-scale calibration ($\hat{\alpha}_{\text{rPCA}}$) using median radial node scales, and inference-optimal scale selection by minimizing a covariance risk functional.
- Establishes high-probability MoM concentration bounds, factorwise bad-node influence constants ($\alpha^{-1/2}$ for mean, $(2-\alpha)^{-1/2}$ for subspace), and node-bootstrap validity.
- Demonstrates on spiked covariance simulations and the 10x Genomics 1.3M mouse brain single-cell RNA-seq dataset that scale calibration tracks eigengap-driven subspace uncertainty and adapts to PCA rank.

## Methodology

The paper considers a distributed setting where $n$ observations are split across $K$ nodes of size $b = n/K$. Each node computes a local PCA estimate $\hat{\theta}_k = (\hat{\mu}_k, \hat{U}_k) \in \mathbb{R}^p \times \mathrm{Gr}(r,p)$, and the goal is to robustly aggregate these heterogeneous objects.

**Scaled product metric.** For $\alpha \in (0,2)$, the scaled distance on the product manifold is:
$$d_\alpha^2\{(\mu,U), (\mu',V)\} = \alpha\|\mu - \mu'\|^2 + (2-\alpha)d_{\mathrm{Gr}}^2(U,V).$$

The MoM estimator minimizes $\frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^K d_\alpha(\theta, \hat{\theta}_k)$ over the product manifold.

**Influence expansion.** The node-level PCA error in tangent coordinates decomposes as:
$$W_{k,b} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{b}}\sum_{i \in B_k} \zeta(X_i) + r_{k,b},$$
where $\zeta(X) = ((X-\mu_0), \mathrm{vec}\{B(X)\})$ and $B(X)_{aj} = u_{r+a}^T \mathcal{E}_X u_j / (\lambda_j - \lambda_{r+a})$ reveals the eigengap-weighted nature of subspace perturbation.

**Local reduction.** The nonlinear product-manifold median is shown to be locally equivalent to the scaled spatial median of the $W_{k,b}$ errors. This reduction converts all subsequent inference and robustness analysis into Euclidean spatial median theory.

**Scale calibration.** The block-noise calibration rule sets $\alpha_{\text{block}} = 2\tau_U / (\tau_\mu + \tau_U)$ where $\tau_\mu, \tau_U$ are average per-dimension variances of mean and subspace node errors. A robust empirical version uses median radial node scales. An inference-optimal rule minimizes $\mathrm{tr}(W_0 V_\alpha)$ over $\alpha$.

**Two asymptotic regimes.** (1) Fixed $K$: the limit is a non-Gaussian spatial median of $K$ Gaussian vectors. (2) Growing $K$: a CLT holds with covariance $V_\alpha$, but finite-block bias $s_{\alpha,b}$ can survive root-$n$ scaling unless $K/b \to 0$ or the node error distribution is centrally symmetric.

## Results & Highlights

- **Eigengap adaptation:** In spiked covariance simulations ($p=200, r=5, n=40000, K=80$), the calibrated scale decreases from ~1.65 at eigengap $\Delta=0.1$ to ~0.43 at $\Delta=4$, correctly downweighting the noisier Grassmann component when eigengaps are small.
- **Bad-node robustness:** Under 40% mean-only contamination, fixed-scale MoM with $\alpha=1.75$ achieves mean error ~0.84 vs. ~2.04 for projector averaging. Scale-calibrated MoM is more stable than non-robust baselines (~1.82 mean error) but does not universally minimize every factorwise loss—it adapts to overall node-level dispersion.
- **Single-cell RNA-seq:** On the 10x mouse brain dataset (1.3M cells, $p=2000$ genes), scale-calibrated MoM achieves the smallest subspace error among distributed methods for $r=10$ ($0.01239$ vs. $0.01667$ for projector averaging, $K=100$). The selected scale shifts from ~0.27 at $r=10$ to ~0.80 at $r=20$, reflecting rank-dependent mean–subspace uncertainty balance.
- **Node-bootstrap validity** is proven for the growing-$K$ regime, enabling practical inference without plug-in covariance estimation.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The formulation of distributed PCA aggregation as a scale-calibration problem on $\mathbb{R}^p \times \mathrm{Gr}(r,p)$ is new. The local reduction theorem and the explicit scale-dependent covariance formula are non-trivial contributions. The insight that scale acts on node-error distributions rather than raw measurements distinguishes this from ordinary product-median scale selection.
- **Rigor:** Very high. Complete proofs are provided for all theorems. Assumptions are clearly stated and motivated. The two-regime asymptotic framework (fixed-$K$ vs. growing-$K$) with finite-block bias analysis is carefully handled. The extension to estimated scale (Theorem 10) addresses a natural practical concern.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate-to-good. The spiked covariance simulation setup is described in enough detail to replicate. The 10x Genomics mouse brain dataset is public. However, no code repository is mentioned, and the exact preprocessing pipeline for the single-cell data (gene selection, normalization) is not fully specified.
- **Significance:** Significant for the distributed and robust statistics communities. The paper provides a principled framework for a practical problem—how to combine PCA estimates from different machines when some may be corrupted. The factorwise influence analysis and calibration rules have clear practical implications for system design.
- **Red flags:** (1) The clean-data efficiency cost of MoM relative to projector averaging is acknowledged but not deeply analyzed—the paper positions MoM as a robust alternative, but users should be aware of the efficiency gap. (2) The theory is fixed-dimensional ($p$ fixed); extending to growing $p$ or $r$ is left as future work, which limits applicability to modern high-dimensional settings. (3) The single-author status means less natural error-checking during development. (4) The empirical improvement of calibrated MoM over fixed-scale MoM is sometimes modest, suggesting the main practical benefit may be the diagnostic interpretation of $\hat{\alpha}$ rather than point-estimation gains.

## Strengths

- Elegant mathematical framework that unifies mean and subspace aggregation through product-manifold geometry.
- The local reduction theorem is a powerful simplifying device that converts a nonlinear manifold problem into tractable Euclidean spatial median theory.
- The factorwise influence constants $\alpha^{-1/2}$ and $(2-\alpha)^{-1/2}$ provide clear, interpretable design guidelines: large $\alpha$ protects the mean, small $\alpha$ protects the subspace.
- Comprehensive theoretical treatment covering limit distributions, concentration, robustness, bootstrap, and scale estimation.
- The calibrated scale serves as a diagnostic of node-level uncertainty, not just a tuning parameter.

## Limitations

- **Fixed-dimensional theory:** All asymptotics assume $p$ is fixed while $n \to \infty$. Many modern PCA applications involve $p \gg n$ or $p$ growing with $n$, where the eigengap perturbation expansion may break down.
- **Equal-size nodes:** The theory assumes $n = Kb$ with equal node sizes. Heterogeneous node sizes require modification.
- **Clean-data efficiency gap:** MoM aggregation incurs a moderate efficiency loss relative to averaging under clean homogeneous data. The paper acknowledges this but does not quantify the gap analytically beyond the spatial median efficiency constant $c_d$.
- **Scalability of the Grassmann optimization:** The paper relies on prior work (Park & You 2026) for the computational algorithm; scalability to very large $p$ and $r$ is not discussed.
- **No real-data corrupted-node experiment:** The single-cell analysis uses artificial corruption patterns. A demonstration with naturally heterogeneous or corrupted distributed data would strengthen the empirical case.

## Relevance

该论文与分布式统计推断和鲁棒估计直接相关。其乘积流形上的MoM框架可推广至任何产生异构乘积值摘要（如均值+子空间、均值+协方差）的分布式过程，对联邦学习中的鲁棒聚合有潜在应用价值。尺度校准的思路对设计分布式系统的推断协议具有参考意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the framework extend to growing $p$ or $p/n \to c > 0$ regimes, where random matrix theory replaces classical eigenspace perturbation?
- Can the block-noise calibration rule be adapted for online/streaming distributed settings where node estimates arrive sequentially?
- What is the computational complexity of the product-manifold geometric median for large $p$ and $r$, and can Riemannian optimization be accelerated?
- Could the factorwise influence analysis inform adaptive node selection (dropping suspicious nodes) rather than uniform MoM aggregation?
- How does the framework interact with differential privacy constraints in federated learning, where node estimates are intentionally noisy?
