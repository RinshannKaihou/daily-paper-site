Improved Guarantees for Constrained Online Convex Optimization via Self-Contraction

Dhruv Sarkar & Abhishek Sinha online-convex-optimization regret-bounds self-contraction
A projection algorithm leveraging self-contracted curves achieves exponential improvement in cumulative constraint violation for constrained online convex optimization.
Key Finding
The sequence of projected iterates in constrained online convex optimization forms a self-contracted curve in Euclidean space. Recent geometric analysis bounds the total length of such curves by O(log T) for strongly-convex losses, translating directly into exponential improvement in constraint violation guarantees — from O(√T log T) down to O(log T). First algorithm to achieve polylogarithmic cumulative constraint violation in this setting.
Key Metric
O(log T) Cumulative Constraint Violation — strongly-convex losses. Exponential improvement over prior O(√T log T).
Why This Matters
The connection between self-contracted curves and online constraint violation is a genuinely new theoretical tool. Achieving polylog CCV is a qualitative leap: it opens the door to long-horizon deployments where constraint budgets grow sub-linearly, and suggests that the geometry of projected trajectories is a richer object than previously understood. Relevant to safe online learning and constrained reinforcement learning.
Critical Edge
  • Purely theoretical — no empirical evaluation
  • Projection step may be expensive in high dimensions
  • Adversarial constraint model may be overly pessimistic for practice
  • Self-contraction bound requires Euclidean geometry; non-Euclidean extension unclear
Methodology
The algorithm addresses COCO where at each round the learner selects an action before observing the loss and constraint function. After each gradient step, the iterate is projected back onto a constraint set. The crucial insight is that the projected iterate sequence forms a self-contracted curve — a geometric object whose total path length is bounded by O(log T) under strong convexity. This bound directly yields improved CCV guarantees. The analysis decouples regret and constraint violation via the self-contraction property, producing clean simultaneous bounds on both quantities with a single, simple algorithm design and different parameter choices for the convex and strongly-convex regimes.
Strengths
  • Clean, simple algorithm with strong theoretical guarantees
  • Exponential CCV improvement for strongly-convex losses
  • Unified analysis for convex and strongly-convex cases
  • Elegant geometry-optimization connection
Limitations
  • No empirical evaluation
  • Adversarial constraint model may be too pessimistic
  • High-dimensional projection cost not discussed
  • Euclidean-only analysis
Questions for Further Exploration
  1. Can self-contraction extend to non-Euclidean norms or non-convex settings (e.g., deep learning loss landscapes)?
  2. What is the practical computational overhead of the projection step in high dimensions?
  3. Are there applications in safe online learning or constrained RL where these bounds matter?
  4. How does the algorithm behave empirically vs. virtual queue-based approaches?
