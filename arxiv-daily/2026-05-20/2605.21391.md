> **一句话总结：** 本文提出条件尺度熵（CSE），一种基于连续小波变换的指标，用于衡量Transformer各层位置上计算的频谱宽度，在GPT-2系列、LLaMA-2 7B和GPT-oss 20B（124M–20B参数）上发现隐喻token在早期至中期层位置上产生显著高于字面token的多尺度协调性，且该效应通过了集群置换校正和多种特异性对照验证。

> **TL;DR:** Introduces Conditional Scale Entropy (CSE), a wavelet-derived measure of spectral breadth in transformer residual-stream trajectories that is provably invariant to update magnitude (two formal theorems). Across 5 decoder-only architectures (124M–20B params), metaphorical tokens produce significantly higher CSE than literal tokens at contiguous early-to-mid depth positions, surviving cluster-based permutation correction. The effect replicates on 200 naturalistic VUA corpus pairs with graded effect sizes consistent with the Graded Salience Hypothesis, and specificity controls (literal-literal complexity pairs, matched-meaning triples) confirm it tracks figurative expression mode, not semantic difficulty or propositional content. CSE is the only spectral metric stable across architectures, stimulus expansion, and stimulus regimes.

## 核心贡献

- 提出条件尺度熵（CSE）——基于连续小波变换（CWT）的全新指标，量化Transformer各层位置上残差流更新的频谱宽度，从数学上证明对更新幅度具有不变性
- 证明两个定理：CSE对更新幅度的缩放不变（Theorem 1）；当归一化尺度分布满足优序关系（majorization）时，CSE的排序被保证（Theorem 2）
- 在5个解码器架构（GPT-2 Small/Medium/Large、LLaMA-2 7B、GPT-oss 20B）上，25个控制最小对实验一致发现隐喻token在连续层位置上产生显著更高的CSE，所有集群均通过置换校正（p < 0.007）
- 200个自然VUA语料库对验证了结果的收敛性，效应量呈现梯度（鲜活隐喻 d≈0.34 vs. 惯用隐喻 d≈0.17），符合渐显著度假说
- 特异性对照（语义复杂度对照、匹配意义三元组）排除了解释替代假说，确认CSE提升反映隐喻表达模式而非语义难度或命题内容

## Key Contributions

- Introduces Conditional Scale Entropy (CSE), derived from applying the Continuous Wavelet Transform (CWT) with a real Morlet wavelet to projected residual-stream trajectories, measuring how broadly computation engages across frequency scales at each layer position
- Proves two formal theorems: (1) CSE is invariant under δ → cδ for any c ≠ 0, isolating structural update pattern from intensity; (2) majorization of the normalized scale distribution guarantees CSE ordering, providing a sufficient structural condition
- Demonstrates cross-architecture consistency: all 5 decoder-only models (124M–20B parameters, including dense and MoE architectures) show significant CSE elevation for metaphorical tokens at contiguous early-to-mid depth positions (all p_cluster < 0.007)
- Shows convergent validation on 200 naturalistic VUA metaphor corpus pairs: independently re-estimated contrast direction recovers the same active zone (positions 5–10), with graded effect sizes (d ≈ 0.34 vivid vs. d ≈ 0.17 conventionalized) consistent with the Graded Salience Hypothesis
- Establishes CSE as uniquely stable among spectral metrics — CWT energy and update-energy entropy (H(q)) either fail to generalize across architectures or reverse sign under stimulus expansion, while CSE remains consistent

## Methodology

**Contrast direction projection.** Given K minimal pairs (same target word in literal vs. metaphorical context), the normalized mean-difference direction v* is computed as the unit vector along the aggregate update difference between metaphorical and literal processing across all pairs and layers. Residual updates Δ_l ∈ R^d are projected onto v* to produce a scalar signal δ ∈ R^L indexed by layer depth. Leave-one-out validation confirms v* captures a stable structural property (cosine similarity 0.997 between 10-pair and 25-pair estimates).

**Wavelet decomposition.** The discrete projected trajectory s(l) is interpolated to a continuous function and decomposed via CWT using the real Morlet wavelet (ω₀ = 5) at S = L+1 integer scales. This produces a 2D scalogram |W_ψ s̃(a_j, b_k)|² resolving energy at each combination of frequency scale and layer position.

**Conditional Scale Entropy (CSE).** At each position b_k, the scalogram is normalized across scales to form a probability distribution p(a_j | b_k) = z_j² / ‖z‖². CSE is the Shannon entropy of this distribution. Theorem 1 proves: (i) this is a valid distribution; (ii) maximum entropy (log S) iff all scales equal; (iii) zero entropy iff exactly one scale active; (iv) invariant to uniform scaling of δ. Theorem 2 proves majorization implies entropy ordering, guaranteeing CSE ordering under a sufficient structural condition.

**Models tested.** GPT-2 Small (124M, 12 layers), GPT-2 Medium (355M, 24 layers), GPT-2 Large (774M, 36 layers), LLaMA-2 7B (7B, 32 layers), GPT-oss 20B (20B MoE with 32 experts, 24 layers). All pretrained, no fine-tuning.

**Stimulus sets.** (1) 25 controlled minimal pairs with a target lexeme in literal vs. metaphorical context (e.g., "The weight crushed the box" / "The news crushed her hopes"). (2) 200 naturalistic pairs from the VUA All POS corpus, extracting same-lemma words in metaphorical vs. literal annotations. (3) 10 literal-literal complexity control pairs. (4) 10 matched-meaning triples (two metaphors + one literal expressing same meaning).

**Statistical testing.** Monte Carlo sign-flip tests (10,000 permutations) per position, with cluster-based permutation correction (5,000 sign-flipped nulls, α = 0.05) across all scalogram positions. Cohen's d for effect sizes.

## Results & Highlights

- **Primary finding**: On GPT-2 Medium, metaphorical tokens show significantly higher CSE at positions 5–13 (p_cluster = 0.007), with peak at position 9 (p = 0.0008, 18/25 pairs positive)
- **Cross-architecture**: Every model shows significant CSE elevation at contiguous early-to-mid depth positions (relative depth 0–67%), all surviving cluster correction (4/5 models p_cluster < 0.001)
- **VUA convergence**: With independently re-estimated v*, VUA active zone (positions 5–10, p_cluster = 0.003) falls within the controlled-pair zone (positions 5–13), confirming the active zone reflects a stable model property
- **Graded effect sizes**: Vivid cross-domain metaphors d ≈ 0.34 vs. conventionalized VUA metaphors d ≈ 0.17, consistent with Graded Salience Hypothesis — more novel metaphors demand more multi-scale coordination
- **CSE uniqueness**: Among all spectral quantities tested (CWT energy, wavelet entropy H_W, update-energy entropy H(q)), only CSE remains stable across architectures, stimulus expansion, and stimulus regimes. H(q) reverses sign when expanding from 10 to 25 pairs on GPT-2 Medium
- **Specificity controls**: (1) Literal-literal complexity pairs show no significant CSE difference (mean ΔH = −0.0005 vs. +0.017 for metaphor). (2) Matched-meaning triples: both metaphors vs. literal significant (p_cluster = 0.003, positions 4–16), but metaphor-vs-metaphor yields zero significant positions (|ΔH| < 0.001), confirming figurative mode drives the effect

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. Applying wavelet analysis to residual-stream trajectories is genuinely novel in the mechanistic interpretability literature. The CSE measure and its formal invariance properties are non-trivial contributions. The connection between spectral breadth and metaphor processing is original and well-motivated.
- **Rigor**: Strong. Formal theorems with complete proofs, cluster-based permutation correction for multiple comparisons, leave-one-out validation of contrast direction, convergent validation with independent stimulus sets and independently estimated v*, and multiple specificity controls. The paper is transparent about what CSE does and does not establish (correlation, not causation).
- **Reproducibility**: Should be good. Standard pretrained models (GPT-2, LLaMA-2), publicly available VUA corpus, PyWavelets library. The controlled 25-pair stimulus set is described in sufficient detail. However, no code repository is mentioned, and the full stimulus list is deferred to an extended version.
- **Significance**: Moderately high for mechanistic interpretability. CSE provides a new macro-level diagnostic complementary to component-level methods (activation patching, SAEs, circuit tracing). If the multi-scale coordination signature generalizes beyond metaphor, CSE could become a standard tool. However, the scope is currently narrow — one linguistic phenomenon, decoder-only models only.
- **Red flags**: (1) The controlled stimulus set is small (25 pairs), raising questions about statistical power despite careful correction. (2) The contrast direction v* is defined relative to the stimulus set — it is not stimulus-independent, limiting applicability as a general diagnostic. (3) No causal evidence: higher CSE during metaphor processing does not establish that multi-scale coordination is functionally necessary. The authors acknowledge this and propose wavelet-based causal intervention as future work. (4) The effect sizes are small to moderate (d ≈ 0.17–0.34), and absolute CSE differences are at the second decimal. (5) Specificity controls use only 10 pairs each, which limits their statistical power.

## Strengths

- Principled theoretical framework with formal invariance theorems providing clean interpretation of what CSE measures
- Broad architectural coverage spanning two orders of magnitude in parameters (124M–20B) including both dense and MoE models
- Multiple converging lines of evidence: controlled pairs, naturalistic VUA corpus, specificity controls, cross-architecture replication
- Honest about limitations: explicitly states CSE establishes correlation not causation, acknowledges stimulus-size constraints, identifies future directions
- CSE's invariance property elegantly separates structural organization from computation intensity — a genuinely useful analytical distinction
- The graded effect-size finding (vivid > conventionalized metaphors) provides a meaningful bridge to cognitive linguistics theory

## Limitations

- Decoder-only architectures exclusively — no results for encoder-only (BERT) or encoder-decoder (T5) models
- Contrast direction v* is stimulus-dependent; a stimulus-independent version would be needed for practical deployment as a diagnostic tool
- No causal evidence — the paper establishes correlation between metaphor processing and higher CSE but does not demonstrate functional necessity
- Controlled stimulus set (25 pairs) is relatively small; specificity controls (10 pairs each) even more so
- The approach is specific to metaphor processing; whether CSE elevation generalizes to other semantic phenomena (irony, polysemy, multi-step reasoning) remains open
- Only English language tested; cross-linguistic generalization unknown
- Absolute CSE differences are small (second decimal), raising practical sensitivity questions

## Relevance

与"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"研究兴趣高度契合——本文提出了一种基于小波变换的全新指标来刻画Transformer残差流跨层的计算结构，属于机械可解释性方向。CSE提供了一种宏观层面的诊断工具，与现有的组件级方法（激活修补、稀疏自编码器、电路追踪）互补，为理解模型内部表征组织方式提供了新的视角。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does CSE elevation generalize to other semantically demanding phenomena beyond metaphor — irony, sarcasm, polysemy resolution, multi-step reasoning?
- Can wavelet-based causal intervention (scale-channel suppression via the Φ operator) establish whether multi-scale coordination is functionally necessary for metaphor processing, as the authors propose?
- How does fine-tuning (e.g., instruction tuning, RLHF) shift the active zone or change CSE profiles for figurative language?
- What is the relationship between CSE and component-level interpretability findings — do specific attention heads or MLP sublayers drive the multi-scale coordination signal?
- Can CSE be adapted to work with stimulus-independent contrast directions, enabling deployment as a general-purpose training diagnostic without requiring pre-identified metaphor/literal pairs?
