> **一句话总结：** 本文通过从 conformal uniform 变量的联合分布中采样构造高概率包络函数，建立了在所有拒绝阈值上同时有效的有限样本 FDP 上界，解决了现有方法仅控制 FDR 期望值且无法支持自适应阈值选择的问题，并在异常检测和药物发现实验中展示了显著优于 DKW 基线的紧致性。

> **TL;DR:** Existing conformal inference methods for outlier detection and candidate selection only control the *expected* false discovery rate (FDR), providing no high-probability guarantee on the realized false discovery proportion (FDP) for the dataset at hand, and break when thresholds are chosen adaptively after seeing the data. This paper constructs finite-sample, distribution-free upper bounds on the FDP that hold *simultaneously* across all rejection thresholds, by sampling from the tractable joint distribution of null conformal p-values to build high-probability envelopes for their empirical CDF. The framework uses variance-adaptive summary statistics (truncated Higher Criticism) plus two tightening strategies (self-refinement and null-proportion bounding), yielding bounds that are empirically much tighter than DKW-based baselines while maintaining valid coverage in both synthetic outlier detection and real drug-target interaction experiments.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了一个统一的框架，通过从 conformal uniform 变量的已知联合分布 $P_{n,m}$ 中采样，构造有限样本、分布无关的经验 CDF 同时上界（包络函数）
- 基于该包络函数，推导了异常检测和 conformal selection 两种场景下在所有拒绝阈值上同时成立的 FDP 上界，支持任意事后阈值选择
- 引入了两种收紧策略——自精炼（self-refinement）和零假设比例上界估计（$\hat{m}_0$），显著降低了 FDP 上界的保守性
- 使用截断 Higher Criticism 统计量等方差自适应的摘要统计量，使包络函数能捕捉 FDP 的异方差特性，在 $t \approx 0$ 附近（实践中最关心的区域）远优于线性 DKW 上界

## Key Contributions

- Establishes finite-sample, distribution-free simultaneous upper bounds on the empirical CDF of null conformal p-values via Monte Carlo sampling from their tractable joint distribution (Proposition 2.4 + Algorithm 1)
- Derives simultaneous FDP upper bounds for outlier detection (Theorem 4.6) and conformal selection (Theorem 5.4) that hold with probability $1-\delta$ uniformly over all thresholds $t \in [0,1]$
- Proposes two modular tightening strategies: (1) self-refinement exploiting monotonicity of false discoveries (Proposition 4.5), and (2) an upper bound $\hat{m}_0$ on the number of true nulls, both of which can be computed in $O(m^2)$ time
- Demonstrates that variance-adaptive statistics (truncated Higher Criticism with $\beta=1/2$, $\ell=0.01$, $r=0.99$) produce substantially tighter envelopes than the DKW-based linear baseline, especially near $t \approx 0$ where most discoveries occur
- Provides the first finite-sample simultaneous FDP bounds for conformal selection, validated on the DAVIS drug-target interaction dataset with 92% empirical coverage at nominal 90%

## Methodology

The paper operates within the split conformal prediction framework with a calibration set of size $n$ and $m$ test samples. The core technical innovation is Algorithm 1:

1. **Conformal uniform variables:** Under exchangeability (Assumption 2.2), the conformal p-values for null test samples follow a known joint distribution $P_{n,m}$ that can be simulated by replacing scores with i.i.d. Uniform[0,1] random variables (Proposition 2.4).

2. **Envelope construction via sampling:** Given a summary statistic $T$ (e.g., Kolmogorov-Smirnov, Higher Criticism, Berk-Jones), draw $B$ i.i.d. replications of the empirical CDF from $P_{n,m}$, compute the $(1-\delta)$-quantile of $T$, and invert to obtain an envelope function $G(t)$ satisfying $\mathbb{P}(\hat{F}_{n,m}(t) \leq G(t), \forall t) \geq 1 - \delta$.

3. **Summary statistic design:** The general form $T(F) = \sup_{t \in [\ell,r]} (F(t) - \mu(t))/\sigma(t)^\beta$ allows practitioners to control envelope shape via $\sigma(t)$ and restrict the range $[\ell,r]$ to avoid boundary inflation. The truncated Higher Criticism statistic ($\beta=1/2$, restricted range) is the recommended default.

4. **FDP bound for outlier detection:** A coupling argument (Lemma 4.3) shows that null p-values can be embedded into a full vector with distribution $P_{n,m}$, enabling direct application of the envelope. Two tightening strategies reduce conservatism: self-refinement (Proposition 4.5) and null-proportion estimation via $\hat{m}_0$.

5. **FDP bound for conformal selection:** Using monotone nonconformity scores (e.g., clipped scores), Lemma 5.3 shows that the number of false discoveries is bounded by the empirical CDF of oracle p-values, which follow $P_{n,m}$. The envelope then directly yields simultaneous FDP bounds (Theorem 5.4).

6. **Computational cost:** Envelope construction via Monte Carlo sampling is $O(Bm)$. Computing $\hat{m}_0$ and the self-refined bound $\hat{B}^\star(t)$ costs $O(m^2)$, which is modest for typical test set sizes.

## Results & Highlights

- **Validity:** In synthetic outlier detection ($n=m=1000$, 90% purity), the MC-THC envelope achieves 96% empirical coverage at nominal $1-\delta=0.9$ across 100 replications. On the DAVIS drug-target interaction dataset, 92 out of 100 runs show valid simultaneous coverage.
- **Tightness vs. baseline:** The MC-THC bound is substantially less conservative than the DKW-based linear baseline from Gazin et al. (2024), especially near $t \approx 0$ where the linear envelope over-covers due to failing to adapt to heteroskedasticity.
- **Tightening strategies:** Combining self-refinement and $\hat{m}_0$ estimation consistently yields the sharpest bounds. The null-proportion tightening contributes more at lower purity (more outliers), while self-refinement dominates at high purity.
- **Post-hoc selection:** The simultaneous bounds remain valid when the practitioner adaptively chooses the rejection threshold (e.g., increasing BH level until at least 5% of samples are selected), whereas the post-hoc BH level fails to upper-bound the realized FDP in this adaptive regime (Figure 6).
- **Signal strength effect:** Bounds become sharper as outlier signal strength increases, consistent with improved separability between inliers and outliers.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The idea of using Monte Carlo sampling from the tractable joint distribution of conformal uniform variables to construct simultaneous CDF bounds is elegant and, to my knowledge, new. The variance-adaptive envelope via truncated Higher Criticism statistics and the two tightening strategies go meaningfully beyond prior work.
- **Rigor (High):** All main results have clean finite-sample proofs. Theorem 3.1 cleanly separates the sampling-based quantile estimation (with explicit $O(1/(B+1))$ gap) from the envelope construction. Assumptions (exchangeability, no ties) are standard in the conformal literature.
- **Reproducibility (High):** Code is publicly available. Experiments use standard datasets (DAVIS) and well-described synthetic setups. The algorithms are clearly specified.
- **Significance (Medium-High):** Simultaneous FDP control that holds at all thresholds is a practically important problem—practitioners routinely adjust thresholds after seeing data. The framework is versatile and could influence how conformal methods are deployed in drug discovery and anomaly detection pipelines.
- **Red flags / limitations:**
  - The simultaneous guarantee comes at the cost of conservatism at any single fixed threshold. The authors acknowledge this (Section 6): bounds can be conservative at the BH-selected threshold specifically.
  - The exchangeability assumption (Assumption 2.2) excludes covariate shift settings, which are common in distribution shift applications. The authors note this as future work.
  - The $O(m^2)$ cost for $\hat{m}_0$ and $\hat{B}^\star(t)$ may be limiting for very large test sets, though $O(m^2)$ is acceptable for typical conformal applications ($m$ in the thousands).
  - The framework does not address sequential/online settings where test samples arrive over time.

## Strengths

- Clean, modular framework: the envelope construction (Algorithm 1) is general and can be applied to any problem where the joint null distribution is simulable.
- The variance-adaptive summary statistics produce visually and quantitatively tighter bounds than the DKW baseline, especially in the practically important small-$t$ regime.
- Honest discussion of limitations in the conclusion (conservatism at fixed thresholds, covariate shift).
- Strong empirical validation across synthetic experiments (varying signal strength and purity) and real data (DAVIS drug-target interactions).
- The two tightening strategies are complementary and can be applied independently.

## Limitations

- Simultaneous validity over all thresholds is inherently more conservative than pointwise validity at a single threshold; practitioners who only need a single pre-specified threshold may prefer simpler methods.
- Exchangeability assumption rules out covariate shift between calibration and test, limiting applicability in some real-world deployment scenarios.
- The Berk-Jones statistic, despite theoretical appeal, is found to be overly conservative in practice except at very small $t$.
- No theoretical analysis of the tightness gap between the simultaneous bound and the pointwise optimum as a function of $n, m, \delta$.
- The paper does not address dependent test samples or hierarchical testing structures.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the framework be extended to handle covariate shift via weighted conformal p-values while preserving simultaneous validity?
- Is there a principled way to choose the truncation parameters $[\ell, r]$ and the exponent $\beta$ in the truncated Higher Criticism statistic in a data-adaptive manner?
- How does the conservatism of the simultaneous bound at the BH threshold scale with $n$ and $m$? Can closed-form or asymptotic expressions for the gap be derived?
- Can the Monte Carlo envelope approach be combined with knockoff-based methods for simultaneous FDP control in feature selection?
- Does the framework extend to conformal prediction with Mondrian or group-conditional exchangeability structures?
