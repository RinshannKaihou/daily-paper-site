Large-Step Training Dynamics of a Two-Factor Linear Transformer Model

Krishnakumar Balasubramanian UC Davis, Dept. of Statistics training-dynamics linear-transformers optimization-theory chaos-and-stability
Key Finding
Large learning rates change what training converges to, not just how fast.
Finite-step GD on a linear self-attention objective reduces exactly to a two-factor product map Φμ(a,b). The learning rate μ induces a cubic phase diagram—monotone convergence, catapult effects, period-doubling to Li–Yorke chaos, and divergence—bounded by an invariant Chebyshev ellipse that carries angle-tripling chaotic dynamics.
Key Metric
μ = 2√2 − 2 ≈ 0.828
The monotone convergence threshold. Below this, every GD step uniformly contracts error. At μ = 1 the fixed point loses stability via flip bifurcation; at μ > 2, trajectories diverge. The Chebyshev ellipse has semi-axes 2√(2+μ) and 2√(2−μ).
Why This Matters
Reframes learning-rate selection as attractor selection, not speed tuning.
The normal multiplier on the zero-error curve is 1 − 2μ − w², so factor imbalance shrinks the stable LR range—yielding a mechanistic explanation for why qk-layernorm and balanced initialization help. Mini-batch SGD is not “full-batch + noise” but random switching between maps with different Chebyshev separatrices; a single atypical batch can push the optimizer across the boundary.
Critical Edge
One-prompt, linear-self-attention, scalar-factor reduction is far from practical transformers. No softmax, no depth, no Adam, no multi-head. The gap to real-world applicability is substantial—acknowledged by the author. No numerical experiments validate predicted bifurcations even in slightly richer settings.

Core Contributions
  1. Exact two-factor product map Φμ derived from finite-step GD on one-prompt linear self-attention
  2. Cubic phase diagram on the balanced line: five regimes with sharp μ-thresholds
  3. Explicit invariant Chebyshev ellipse Eμ with angle-tripling chaotic dynamics (e ↦ e³−3e)
  4. Transverse stability theory: chaotic ellipse repels, balanced attractors attract for 1 < μ < 2
  5. Interior rigidity: no hidden off-balanced recurrent dynamics in {D < 0}
  6. Mini-batch extension: SGD as random switching between structurally different maps
Methodology
  1. Start from one-prompt linear self-attention loss L(U) = ½(uTHu − yq)2
  2. Error/imbalance coordinates (e = ab − μ, w = a − b) decouple prediction error from factor imbalance
  3. Balanced line w = 0 yields scalar cubic Fμ(e) with full phase classification
  4. Full 2D system: separatrix coordinate D = w2 − (2−μ)(2−e) gives normal form, Chebyshev ellipse, Lyapunov exponents
  5. Mini-batch modeled as random switching between maps Φνk with batch-dependent ν
