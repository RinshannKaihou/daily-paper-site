The Hidden Signal of Verifier Strictness: Controlling and Improving Step-Wise Verification via Selective Latent Steering

by Yefan Zhou, Yilun Zhou, Austin Xu, Soroush Vosoughi, Shafiq Joty, Jiang Gui date 2026-05-20 tags process-verification, latent-steering, generative-verifiers, LLM-reasoning, activation-engineering
Key Finding

A generative verifier's tendency to accept or reject reasoning steps — its "strictness" — is encoded in the hidden state of \n\n paragraph-boundary delimiter tokens, concentrated at specific mid-layers (e.g. layer 22 in Qwen3-8B). This signal is separable, steerable, and detectable without any fine-tuning.

Uniform strictness steering improves error detection (TNR) but harms correctness certification (TPR) — an inherent trade-off. VerifySteer resolves this with two-level adaptivity: sample-level routing via a lightweight correctness probe, and delimiter-level cosine-similarity gating that intervenes only where hidden states are misaligned.

Critical Edge
Math-only evaluation. Generalization to code, scientific reasoning, or natural language verification is untested.
Probe requires labels. The correctness MLP needs per-model retraining — not truly zero-shot transfer.
Per-model tuning. Steering hyperparameters (layer, α, thresholds) are tuned per model and benchmark subset.
Tie at 8B. On Qwen3-8B ProcessBench, VerifySteer (72.3) vs Cons@4 (72.2) is essentially a tie — advantage is computational only.
Method — VerifySteer
01 / offline
Extract Steering Vectors
Generate 16 rollouts per sample. Separate \n\n hidden states into false-accept vs true-reject groups. Compute dstrict = h̄TR − h̄FA.
02 / online
Probe & Route
3-layer MLP takes pooled prompt hidden state, outputs correctness score. Route to dstrict (likely wrong), dlenient (likely right), or no steering (uncertain).
03 / online
Gate & Steer
For each generated \n\n token, apply binary gate: intervene only if cossim(hk, dsel) < ρ. Renormalized additive steering preserves norm.
