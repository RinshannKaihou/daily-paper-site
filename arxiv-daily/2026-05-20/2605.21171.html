FTerViT: Fully Ternary Vision Transformer

2605.21171 Ruciński, Bonazzi, Türetken, Narduzzi, Magno, Maamari CSEM · ETH Zürich

Every weight in a ViT — patch embedding, LayerNorm, classifier head — compressed to three values: {-1, 0, +1}. The result fits on a $10 microcontroller and still hits 82% on ImageNet.

Key Finding

FTerViT is the first Vision Transformer where all weight-carrying components are ternarized to {-1, 0, +1} — including the patch embedding, LayerNorm affine parameters, and classifier head, which prior work (TerViT, ViT-1.58b) explicitly avoided due to documented sensitivity causing up to 22.4 pp accuracy collapse. Using same-architecture knowledge distillation with a two-phase training protocol (KL distillation at LR 1e-4, then low-LR recovery at 1e-5), FTerViT closes the gap to FP32 to just 2.42 percentage points while achieving 14.6× compression. The authors further demonstrate the first-ever deployment of a ternary ViT on an ESP32-S3 microcontroller.

Why This Matters
  • Edge viability. 6.09 MB fits in microcontroller flash. Proven on a $10 ESP32-S3 with 8 MB PSRAM — no GPU, no cloud, no network.
  • Closes a known gap. Patch embedding, LayerNorm, and classifier head were considered un-ternarizable. This work removes the last FP32 holdouts.
  • Reusable operators. TernaryBitConv2d and TernaryLayerNorm are architecture-agnostic primitives transferable to Swin, ConvNeXt, and beyond.
  • Practical training recipe. Same-architecture KD + two-phase LR schedule. No multi-objective losses, no auxiliary heads, no complexity overhead.
  • Ternary weights are balanced. ~37% zeros across {-1, 0, +1}, enabling efficient packed storage and bit-level inference kernels.
Critical Edge
  • 21 s inference on MCU. The C kernel lacks SIMD optimization. Real-time use cases are out of reach without hardware-aware kernels.
  • Not fully ternary. Title says "Fully Ternary" but activations remain 8-bit (W2A8). Memory savings come only from weights.
  • Backbone advantage. FTerViT uses DeiT-III (a stronger teacher) vs. prior work using original DeiT. Headline numbers benefit from this upgrade.
  • Scale untested. Only DeiT-Tiny/Small evaluated. DeiT-Base/Large scaling is claimed straightforward but not demonstrated.
  • 260 training epochs. Substantial cost compared to post-training quantization methods that require zero retraining.
  • Classification only. No detection, segmentation, or downstream transfer experiments to validate generality.
