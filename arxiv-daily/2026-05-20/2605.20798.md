> **一句话总结：** 在1.2B和3B参数规模下严格复现测试了20种2021年后提出的Transformer改进方法，发现绝大多数改进在多种子噪声基线和下游任务评估下不成立，仅Softpick一种方法同时通过了Bonferroni校正和3B稳定性测试，并揭示了注意力输出修改中验证损失与下游性能严重脱钩的现象。

> **TL;DR:** A rigorous controlled benchmark of 20 post-2021 Transformer modifications at 1.2B and 3B parameters finds that most do not transfer: only 2/20 survive Bonferroni correction at 1.2B, and one of those (HybridNorm) diverges at 3B due to Post-LN instability. Softpick (rectified softmax) is the sole method passing both statistical and cross-scale filters. Critically, two attention-output modifications (Sigmoid Attention, SSMax) reach within 2-3% of baseline validation loss yet drop 6-16 CLIMB-points downstream, showing pretraining loss is dangerously unreliable for ranking attention changes.

## 核心贡献

- 在1.2B和3B参数规模下，使用完全相同的训练数据、计算预算和超参数配方，严格对照测试了20种2021年后提出的Transformer架构改进方法
- 建立了基于三种子噪声基线（σ_baseline=0.00208）的统计显著性检验框架，配合Bonferroni族校正，避免了单种子比较的假阳性问题
- 揭示了注意力输出修改中验证损失与下游任务性能严重脱钩的现象：Sigmoid Attention和SSMax的验证损失仅比基线高2-3%，但CLIMB-12下游准确率下降6-16个百分点
- 发现HybridNorm虽在1.2B通过Bonferroni校正，但在3B下因Post-LN深层方差增长机制在所有三种子下均在2800-3500步区间发散
- 提出三条新建议（R5-R7）：多种子噪声基线报告、注意力修改必须使用下游评估、跨规模稳定性检查

## Key Contributions

- A controlled benchmark of 20 post-2021 Transformer modifications under iso-data, iso-compute, iso-recipe conditions at both 1.2B and 3B scales
- A multi-seed noise-floor protocol calibrated on three reference configurations (baseline, QK-Norm, Softpick), with baseline σ used as the conservative Bonferroni threshold
- Discovery that the loss-downstream decoupling (Tay et al. 2023) enlarges several-fold for attention-output modifications at 1.2B: Sigmoid Attention's val loss is only 2.4% above baseline yet CLIMB drops by 16 points
- Cross-scale robustness check at 3B showing one of two 1.2B Bonferroni survivors (HybridNorm) diverges with a mechanism-aligned Post-LN variance growth signature across all three seeds
- Three new recommendations extending Narang et al.'s original four: multi-seed noise floors (R5), downstream evaluation for attention-output modifications (R6), and cross-scale stability checks (R7)
- Cross-hardware baseline reproduction (NVIDIA A100 vs. primary accelerator) matching CLIMB-avg to 1×10⁻⁴

## Methodology

**Benchmark design.** 20 Transformer modifications proposed since 2021 are grouped by target module: 10 attention, 3 FFN, 2 normalization, 4 residual. The 1.2B baseline is a Llama-2-style decoder (24 layers, hidden 2048, 32 heads with 8 KV heads/GQA, SwiGLU FFN, RoPE, 1024 context). All methods share identical optimizer (AdamW, lr 3×10⁻⁴, cosine decay), tokenizer, data shuffle, and training duration (44K steps, ~23B tokens from CLIMB-Mix-400B). Per-method FLOPs are within ±0.13% of baseline.

**Noise-floor protocol.** Three seeds (42, 43, 44) each for baseline, QK-Norm, and Softpick establish empirical σ_baseline=0.00208 on CLIMB-avg. Single-seed methods are assessed via z-scores against this floor, with Bonferroni correction at m=19.

**Downstream evaluation.** CLIMB-12 benchmark suite (PIQA, ARC-C/E, HellaSwag, WinoGrande, SocialIQA, MMLU, OpenBookQA, BoolQ, RACE, LAMBADA, TruthfulQA-MC2) using lm-evaluation-harness default few-shot settings. CLIMB-avg is the unweighted macro-average.

**3B robustness check.** 13 configurations retrained at 3B (28 layers, hidden 3072) under identical recipe, iso-token milestone. Three methods diverge; ten complete.

## Results & Highlights

- **Most modifications do not transfer.** At 1.2B: 8/20 within noise floor (|z|<2), 6 significant degraders, 6 nominal improvers. Only 2/6 improvers survive Bonferroni: Softpick (z=+4.47, p_Bonf≈1.5×10⁻⁴) and HybridNorm (z=+3.19, p_Bonf≈0.027).
- **Softpick is the only method passing both filters.** Survives Bonferroni at 1.2B, trains stably at 3B with positive delta (+0.0095). Three-seed Welch's t=+6.36, p=0.0053.
- **HybridNorm passes 1.2B Bonferroni but diverges at 3B.** All three noise-floor seeds diverge in the 2800-3500 step window, matching the known Post-LN deep-layer variance growth mechanism (Xiong et al. 2020).
- **Loss-downstream decoupling is severe for attention modifications.** Sigmoid Attention: val loss +2.4%, CLIMB -16 points. SSMax: val loss +3.0%, CLIMB -6 points. Residual-side failures show consistent loss-CLIMB correspondence.
- **1.2B rank does not predict 3B rank.** Among 7 improver candidates completing at 3B, Spearman ρ=-0.27. QK-Norm+GeGLU moves from 1.2B rank 7 to 3B rank 1. The sign of 1.2B effects transfers, but fine ordering does not.
- **Attention modifications span the largest effect range.** CLIMB-avg from +0.009 (Softpick) to -0.161 (Sigmoid Attention), ~8× the baseline bootstrap width.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors (Yang Zhao, Jiahao Lu, Bin Huang, Guhua Zhang, Jie Zhou) are from Tencent. The study is a direct extension of the highly cited Narang et al. (2021) benchmark. The experimental protocol is exceptionally rigorous: iso-recipe, multi-seed noise floors, Bonferroni correction, cross-hardware reproduction, and a separate 3B robustness sweep. The transparency about divergences and negative results is commendable. Hardware vendor is anonymized for review, suggesting the paper is under submission to a major venue.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High methodological novelty. The protocol update (noise floor + downstream + cross-scale) is the main contribution rather than any new architecture. The finding that the loss-downstream decoupling concentrates in attention-output modifications is a genuinely new empirical observation.

**Rigor:** Exceptional. Multi-seed baselines, three different correction procedures (Bonferroni, Holm-Bonferroni, BH), cross-hardware validation, mechanism-aligned divergence analysis, and full transparency about which methods fail and why. The per-method FLOPs accounting is a nice touch.

**Reproducibility:** Strong. Fixed recipe, fixed data shuffle, public CLIMB-Mix-400B corpus, public evaluation suite (lm-evaluation-harness). The 20 methods all have public implementations or sufficient detail. The one limitation is that the primary hardware is anonymized.

**Significance:** Very high. This is likely to become the new standard reference for Transformer architecture comparisons at 1-3B, supplanting Narang et al. (2021). The loss-downstream decoupling finding has immediate practical implications for anyone evaluating attention modifications using pretraining loss alone.

**Red flags:** (1) The 20-method set is curated, not random—the "most do not transfer" headline is scoped accordingly. (2) No per-method hyperparameter tuning, which the authors acknowledge but justify by design. (3) Single-seed 3B measurements (except for the three diverging methods) limit the precision of 3B rankings. (4) CLIMB-12 focuses on English NLU tasks; results may differ for generation, multilingual, or long-context settings.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally rigorous experimental protocol with multi-seed noise floors and multiple correction procedures
- The loss-downstream decoupling analysis for attention modifications is a critical and actionable finding
- Full transparency: all divergences, failures, and negative results are reported with mechanism analysis
- The 3B robustness check acts as a genuine second filter, not just a scaling exercise
- The three recommendations (R5-R7) are concrete, implementable, and likely to change community norms
- Per-method FLOPs accounting enables fair compute comparisons

## Limitations

- 20-method curated set is not exhaustive; "most do not transfer" may not hold for untested modifications
- No per-method hyperparameter tuning—some methods might benefit from recipe adjustment at 3B (acknowledged by authors)
- 3B results are single-seed (except divergences), so 3B ranking precision is limited
- CLIMB-12 evaluation focuses on English NLU; findings may not generalize to generation, code, multilingual, or long-context tasks
- Context length fixed at 1024; modifications claiming long-context benefits (e.g., Sigmoid Attention) are not tested in their claimed regime
- Hardware anonymization slightly reduces reproducibility for the primary experiments (partially mitigated by A100 cross-check)

## Relevance

与你的LLM训练稳定性与可观测性兴趣高度相关——该论文系统性地揭示了多种Transformer修改在3B规模下的训练不稳定现象（HybridNorm的Post-LN方差增长、SSMax的softmax替换logit爆炸、HyperConnections的EMA漂移），并建立了严谨的统计基准协议来可靠地检测这些训练问题。同时，验证损失与下游性能脱钩的发现对理解模型评估的可靠性具有重要意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would the loss-downstream decoupling finding change at longer context lengths (e.g., 8K-128K), where some attention modifications claim benefits?
- Can the noise-floor protocol be extended to generation tasks (e.g., code generation, math reasoning) where CLIMB-12 NLU benchmarks may not capture the full picture?
- Is the HybridNorm 3B divergence fixable with a different learning rate schedule or warmup, and if so, does the fix invalidate the iso-recipe principle?
- How do these results generalize to MoE architectures (e.g., DeepSeek-V3 at 671B), where HyperConnections reportedly works well?
- Could the loss-downstream decoupling be predicted from training dynamics (e.g., attention pattern entropy, grad-norm signatures) before full downstream evaluation?
