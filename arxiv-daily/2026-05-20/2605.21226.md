> **一句话总结：** OCTOPUS 通过对旋转后的 KV cache 坐标三元组进行八面体参数化联合量化，利用非对称比特分配（方向多1bit，范数少1bit），在文本、视频和音频任务上以极低比特率实现了最优压缩，且通过融合 Triton 核实现零额外解码延迟。

> **TL;DR:** OCTOPUS is a rotation-preconditioned KV-cache codec that jointly quantizes rotated coordinate triplets via an octahedral direction parameterization and Lloyd-Max norm quantization with an MSE-optimal asymmetric (b+1, b−1) bit split. It matches or beats TurboQuant, PolarQuant, and QJL across LLM perplexity (Qwen2.5-7B-1M), autoregressive video (CausVid, Causal Forcing), and audio (AAR) at every bit width — with decisive advantages at 2-bit where competitors collapse. A fused Triton kernel adds zero decode-time bandwidth overhead.

## 核心贡献

- 提出八面体参数化方法，将旋转后 KV cache 的三元组方向映射到 $[-1,1]^2$ 平面，使 2D Lloyd-Max 量化近似球面最优失真
- 推导出 MSE 最优的非均匀比特分配：方向坐标分配 (b+1) bit，范数分配 (b−1) bit，31–41% 的 MSE 降低（相比均匀分配）
- 设计 3×3 局部联合取整策略，在编码阶段联合优化方向与范数的量化索引，无需改变解码器
- 实现融合 Triton 注意力核，解码时直接从压缩状态重建 key，不物化完整 key 张量，零额外带宽/延迟
- 编解码器完全数据无关、在线、确定性（仅依赖随机种子），无需校准数据

## Key Contributions

- **Octahedral triplet direction quantizer**: Maps each 3-coordinate block's unit direction to a square via the octahedral fold (piecewise-linear bijection $S^2 \to [-1,1]^2$ with constant per-octant Jacobian), enabling 1D Lloyd-Max on induced marginals as a close approximation to true 2-sphere distortion
- **MSE-optimal non-uniform bit split**: Lagrangian analysis on per-triplet squared error yields $b^\star_\text{dir} - b^\star_\text{nrm} = O(1)$, independent of both dimensionality $d$ and total budget; empirically verified at (b+1, b−1) for d=128, reducing MSE 31–41% vs. uniform (b, b)
- **Local 3×3 joint rounding**: Direction selection decouples from norm selection; enumerating only 9 candidates around the scalar seed is byte-identical to full grid search across all tested bit widths
- **Fused Triton split-K flash decoder**: Bit unpacking, octahedral decode, centroid gather, and online softmax fused into one kernel — keys reconstructed in registers, $\hat{K}$ never materialized
- **Cross-modality validation**: First rotation-preconditioned KV codec evaluated beyond LLMs — tested on autoregressive video diffusion (CausVid, Causal Forcing) and next-scale audio (AAR)
- **Optional QJL 1-bit residual** (OCTOPUS-QJL): Unbiased dot-product estimator via a second independent rotation's sign sketch, adds ~1 bit/coordinate for zero IP bias

## Methodology

The codec operates on each key vector $k \in \mathbb{R}^d$ in six stages:

1. **Magnitude-direction split** (Eq. 1): Store $\gamma = \|k\|_2$ as fp32 (4 bytes per key). The unit direction $\tilde{u} = k/\gamma \in S^{d-1}$ receives the full quantization budget.

2. **Rotation preconditioning** (Eq. 2): Apply sign-flipped Walsh-Hadamard transform $R = H \cdot \text{diag}(s)$, yielding $u = R\tilde{u}$. Each rotated coordinate follows a symmetric-Beta marginal $f(u) \propto (1-u^2)^{(d-3)/2}$. Inner products are preserved: $q^\top k = \gamma (Rq)^\top u$.

3. **Triplet decomposition**: Partition $u$ into $n_\text{tri} = \lceil d/3 \rceil$ contiguous triplets $t_i \in \mathbb{R}^3$. Split each into norm $\rho_i = \|t_i\|_2$ and direction $n_i = t_i/\rho_i \in S^2$. The triplet norm follows $\rho_i^2 \sim \text{Beta}(3/2, (d-3)/2)$, concentrating near $\sqrt{3/d}$ as $d$ grows — fewer bits needed.

4. **Octahedral parameterization** (Eqs. 5–6): Map each $n_i = (x,y,z)$ to $(\xi, \eta) \in [-1,1]^2$ via the octahedral fold: project to the $L_1$ octahedron surface, then unfold lower hemisphere into outer triangles. Decode inverts this with piecewise-linear arithmetic. Constant Jacobian per octant makes per-coordinate Lloyd-Max on $(\xi, \eta)$ a close approximation to joint 2-sphere quantization.

5. **MSE-optimal bit allocation** (Eq. 10): Minimizing $\mathbb{E}\|t_i - \hat{t}_i\|_2^2$ subject to $2b_\text{dir} + b_\text{nrm} = B_\text{tri}$ via Panter-Dite high-rate distortion gives $b^\star_\text{dir} - b^\star_\text{nrm} = \log_4\!\left(\frac{3 C_n \sigma_n^2}{2d\, C_\rho \sigma_\rho^2}\right) = O(1)$. At d=128, the optimal split is empirically (b+1, b−1) for all tested b ∈ {2, 3, 4}, verified across both synthetic Gaussian keys and all four real-world modalities.

6. **Codebooks and joint rounding**: Two offline Lloyd-Max codebooks — $C_\xi(b_\text{dir})$ on $[-1,1]$ matched to the empirical oct-coordinate marginal, and $C_\rho(d, b_\text{nrm})$ on $[0,1]$ matched to the Beta density. Local 3×3 search around the scalar seed finds the joint-optimal direction index pair maximizing the triplet dot product $s_i = t_i^\top n(\hat{\xi}, \hat{\eta})$, then selects the nearest norm centroid to $\text{clip}(s_i^\star, 0, 1)$.

The optional OCTOPUS-QJL variant appends a 1-bit Johnson-Lindenstrauss sketch of the rotated-frame residual $r = u - \hat{u}$ using an independent second rotation, producing an unbiased dot-product estimator with $O(1/d)$ variance.

## Results & Highlights

**Synthetic (d=128 Gaussian keys)**: OCTOPUS achieves lowest MSE at every bit width — 1.3× below TurboQuant-MSE at b=4, 2.4× below PolarQuant at b=2. OCTOPUS-QJL reduces IP absolute error 3× below TurboQuant-QJL. On synthetic needle-in-a-haystack, OCTOPUS preserves 0.92 softmax mass at b=2 vs. 0.86/0.87/0.33 for baselines.

**Long-context LLM (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct-1M)**:
- b=4: OCTOPUS +2.7% WikiText-2 PPL (best), vs. +3.1/4.4/8.0% for TurboQuant-MSE/PolarQuant/TurboQuant-QJL
- b=3: OCTOPUS +7.2% (best), vs. +8.6/15.7/50.4%
- b=2: OCTOPUS +34.7% — decisively better than TurboQuant-MSE +63%, PolarQuant +187%, TurboQuant-QJL +772%
- Needle retrieval at b=2: OCTOPUS retains 0.81 recall; PolarQuant and TurboQuant-QJL collapse to 0.04/0.01

**Autoregressive video (Wan-1.3B)**:
- CausVid b=2: OCTOPUS mean LPIPS 0.178 vs. 0.251 (PolarQuant) / 0.261 (TurboQuant-MSE)
- Causal Forcing b=2: OCTOPUS worst-case LPIPS 0.82 (still coherent); TurboQuant-QJL reaches 1.00 (random noise), PolarQuant 0.90
- PolarQuant degrades 1.4× faster than OCTOPUS on mean LPIPS as bits decrease

**Autoregressive audio (AAR)**:
- b=2: OCTOPUS maintains 6.75 dB mean LSD with +1.07 dB SNR; TurboQuant-MSE/QJL/PolarQuant all reach 12.6–13.2 dB LSD with negative SNR

**Cross-modality pattern**: At b=4 all codecs are competitive (≤3% gaps). As bits drop, OCTOPUS's advantage grows dramatically — it is often the only codec that does not catastrophically collapse.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High and well-motivated. The octahedral parameterization borrow from computer graphics but is novel for KV cache quantization. The triplet joint quantization with non-uniform bit allocation is a genuine advance over per-coordinate approaches. The local 3×3 joint rounding is an elegant practical trick.
- **Rigor**: Very strong. Formal lemmas with proofs (Appendix B–C), analytic marginals, synthetic probes isolating the codec from model confounds, and matched-recipe comparisons where only the K codec varies. The boundary-block protection for Qwen2.5 is honestly disclosed as a stability prerequisite rather than a contribution.
- **Reproducibility**: Excellent. Data-oblivious, deterministic, no calibration. Codebooks depend only on (d, b). Project page available. Fused Triton kernels described in algorithmic detail (Algorithms 1–2).
- **Significance**: High. KV cache compression is a critical bottleneck for long-context serving. The 2-bit regime is where OCTOPUS shines most — exactly where compression matters most for bandwidth-constrained deployments. The cross-modality validation (video, audio) broadens impact beyond LLMs.
- **Red flags**:
  - Wall-clock decode time is acknowledged as slower than bf16 SDPA — OCTOPUS is best suited when KV bandwidth/capacity is the bottleneck, not latency. No wall-clock numbers reported in the main paper (deferred to App. G).
  - The (b+1, b−1) split is verified empirically at d=128 but the theoretical $O(1)$ gap is not pinned to +1 in closed form — it's an empirical finding from sweeps.
  - Boundary-block protection is needed for all rotation codecs on Qwen2.5, consuming fp16 keys on 2 of 28 layers. This slightly inflates the effective bit rate but is fairly disclosed.
  - At b=4 the advantage over PolarQuant is modest in some video/audio metrics — OCTOPUS's value proposition is concentrated in the extreme compression regime.

## Strengths

- Principled joint quantization with analytically derived optimal asymmetric bit allocation
- Consistent and often decisive improvements across four modalities at extreme low bit widths
- Zero decode-time bandwidth overhead — fused Triton kernel reconstructs keys in registers
- Data-oblivious and deterministic — no calibration, no training, no per-vector scaling
- Elegant reuse of the octahedral map from computer graphics with theoretically justified marginals
- Optional QJL residual composes cleanly for unbiased dot-product estimation
- Honest about limitations — slower wall-clock than bf16, boundary-block caveat disclosed

## Limitations

- Increased arithmetic per decode step vs. scalar Lloyd-Max; wall-clock decode is slower than bf16 SDPA (quantified in App. G but not in main text)
- The (b+1, b−1) optimal split is empirically verified at d=128 but not proven in closed form for arbitrary d
- At b=4 the advantage over PolarQuant is marginal (≤3%) in some video/audio metrics
- Boundary-block protection required on Qwen2.5 (2/28 layers at fp16) — a common limitation across all rotation codecs but still inflates effective memory
- Not evaluated on models with head dimensions other than 64 and 128 (e.g., d=256)
- Triplet padding when d is not divisible by 3 adds a small waste

## Relevance

直接相关于大模型高效推理这一核心研究方向。KV cache 压缩是长上下文服务的主要瓶颈，OCTOPUS 在 2-bit 极端压缩下显著优于所有已有方法，对带宽受限的实际部署场景特别有价值。跨模态验证（视频、音频）也拓展了该方法的应用前景。
