Instant GPU Efficiency Visibility at Fleet Scale

OFU (Overall FLOP Utilization) is a hardware-counter-derived metric that predicts application-level MFU within ≤2 pp, requires zero instrumentation, and caught a 2.5× efficiency regression in production.

Key Finding

Two DCGM counters alone can proxy MFU across every precision and GPU generation—no code changes required.

By multiplying Tensor Pipe Activity (fraction of cycles the tensor core pipelines are active) by the SM clock-frequency ratio, OFU produces a precision-agnostic utilization number that works on FP16, TF32, FP8, and NVFP4 without per-format calibration. Deployed at NVIDIA fleet scale, it surfaced two independent Megatron-LM FLOPs miscalculations and a debug-flag-induced 2.5× regression that went unnoticed by application-level metrics.

Why This Matters

Most training jobs have zero visibility into actual GPU efficiency.

Application-level MFU requires model-specific FLOPs accounting—complex, error-prone, and absent from ~80% of fleet workloads. OFU turns on for every job the moment a DCGM agent starts, covering everything from a single-GPU experiment to a 6,144-GPU pretraining run.

It caught two separate Megatron-LM FLOPs bugs that were inflating reported MFU: MoE latent-dimension 3× inflation and hybrid-architecture layer-type miscounting. No application metric caught these because the metrics trusted the framework's own FLOPs counter.
Critical Edge

Limitations that matter in practice

  • ×NVIDIA-only: Tensor Pipe Activity is not exposed on AMD or Intel GPUs; no migration path discussed.
  • ×Raw r = 0.53 across all 608 jobs—when OFU and MFU disagree, you cannot tell whether the framework or OFU is wrong without case-by-case investigation.
  • ×Non-tensor FLOPs (activations, reductions, embedding lookups) are invisible, so OFU systematically underestimates non-matrix-heavy workloads.
  • ×Single-point readings are noisy; meaningful values require averaging over time due to instantaneous clock sampling.
  • ×7–12 pp absolute error at 8–16 GPU scale, limiting small-experiment usefulness.
Method

Formula

OFU = TPA × (fSMmax / fSM)

TPA = fraction of GPU cycles the Tensor Core pipelines are active. The clock ratio converts cycle-domain to time-domain.

5 Error Sources Characterized

Tile quantization (+1–2 pp), precision scaling (negligible—by construction), clock sampling noise (averages out), Tensor Core clock domains (fixed 1,830 MHz on H100), non-tensor undercounting (<2% for matrix-heavy work).

Validation Method

Controlled GEMMs via CUTLASS, XMMA, NVJet on H100 & GB200 across FP16/TF32/FP8/NVFP4. Production: 608 jobs, 8–5,888 GPUs. Three operational case studies.
