> **一句话总结：** 本文提出DiPO方法，通过困惑度空间解缠策略（PSD）将样本空间精细划分为探索/利用子空间，并设计双向奖励重分配机制（BRR）对零梯度的极端样本组进行最小扰动的奖励调整，在数学推理和函数调用任务上稳定地提升了LLM的RL训练效果。

> **TL;DR:** DiPO addresses the exploration-exploitation trade-off in RLVR by disentangling perplexity space into exploration/exploitation subspaces and bidirectionally reallocating rewards for zero-gradient extreme sample groups, achieving consistent improvements on math reasoning and function calling benchmarks across multiple model scales.

## 核心贡献

- **困惑度空间解缠策略（PSD）**：基于在线统计估计和优势判断机制，自动寻找最优困惑度阈值，将样本空间划分为探索空间（ErS）和利用空间（EiS），从而识别出需要鼓励探索的困难样本和需要鼓励利用的简单样本。
- **双向奖励重分配机制（BRR）**：仅对零梯度的极端样本组（全对/全错组）进行奖励重分配——对EiS中的困难组赋予最大PPL样本正奖励以鼓励探索，对ErS中的简单组赋予最大PPL样本零奖励以鼓励利用，同时保持与验证奖励的正交性，最小化对原始奖励分布的干扰。
- **理论保证**：证明了最大PPL奖励/惩罚分别能增加/减少策略的平均熵（Theorem 1 & 2），并通过Qwen3-0.6B上的实验验证了理论趋势。
- **广泛的实验验证**：在6个数学推理基准和BFCLv3函数调用基准上，使用Qwen3-4B、Qwen3-8B、Qwen2.5-7B、Llama3.1-8B等多个模型，DiPO均取得最优或次优结果。

## Key Contributions

- **Perplexity Space Disentangling (PSD):** An online statistical estimation method that computes optimal PPL thresholds via advantage judgment and classification error minimization, partitioning samples into exploration (high PPL) and exploitation (low PPL) subspaces for fine-grained sample mining.
- **Bidirectional Reward Reallocation (BRR):** A reward adjustment mechanism that only modifies zero-gradient extreme groups (all-correct or all-incorrect), setting the max-PPL sample's reward to 1 for hard groups in EiS (encouraging exploration) and to 0 for easy groups in ErS (encouraging exploitation), while maintaining orthogonality with verification rewards.
- **Theoretical grounding:** Two theorems proving that max-PPL reward increases average policy entropy and max-PPL penalty decreases it, with experimental verification on Qwen3-0.6B.
- **Comprehensive evaluation:** Consistent state-of-the-art results across 6 math benchmarks (AIME24/25, MATH500, AMC, OlympiadBench, Minerva) and BFCLv3 function calling, spanning 5 model architectures and scales.

## Methodology

DiPO builds upon the DAPO (Dynamic Sampling Policy Optimization) framework and addresses two key dilemmas in RLVR training: (1) zero advantage for extreme sample groups (all-correct "easy" groups and all-incorrect "hard" groups), and (2) ineffective exploration/exploitation where some error samples show exploitative tendencies (low PPL) while some correct samples show exploratory tendencies (high PPL).

**Perplexity Space Disentangling (PSD):**
- Maintains a PPL Queue caching (perplexity, reward) pairs from the two most recent batches.
- Computes empirical conditional probabilities Pr(R|P) for correctness given PPL regions.
- Uses 95% confidence intervals (Wald approximation) for robust advantage judgment: Delta_EiS > 0 and Delta_ErS > 0 must both hold for the PPL-correctness correlation to be meaningful.
- Finds the optimal threshold tau* by minimizing classification error subject to the advantage judgment constraints.
- Early in training, when PPL and correctness are not yet correlated, PSD is automatically deactivated.

**Bidirectional Reward Reallocation (BRR):**
- For hard groups (all rewards = 0) in EiS (mean PPL < tau*): sets the reward of the max-PPL sample to 1, encouraging exploration (entropy increase).
- For easy groups (all rewards = 1) in ErS (mean PPL > tau*): sets the reward of the max-PPL sample to 0, encouraging exploitation (entropy decrease).
- Normal groups are excluded (rewards set to 0), ensuring orthogonality between reallocated rewards and verification rewards.
- The final objective: J_DiPO = J_DAPO(theta, R) + alpha * J_DAPO(theta, R_r), with alpha=0.1 as the optimal weight.

## Results & Highlights

- **Math Reasoning (ACC/mean@8):** DiPO achieves the highest average scores across all three base models: Qwen3-4B (50.55% vs. 49.43% DAPO baseline), Qwen3-8B (54.79% vs. 53.23%), and Qwen2.5-7B (43.56% vs. 42.73%).
- **AIME24 standout:** +2.92% on Qwen3-4B, +4.92% on Qwen3-8B over DAPO -- the most challenging benchmark shows the largest gains.
- **Function Calling (BFCLv3):** DiPO achieves the best Overall accuracy on both Qwen2.5-3B (55.03%) and Qwen2.5-7B (62.51%), with particularly strong Multi-Turn Acc improvements (+4.75pp over DAPO on 7B).
- **Majority Vote (ACC/maj@8):** DiPO ranks first in 10 out of 18 benchmark-model pairs, confirming reliable reasoning consistency.
- **Coefficient robustness:** DiPO is far more robust to hyperparameter alpha than entropy loss -- a 10x change in alpha causes only 1.43 AVG points variation, while entropy loss collapses by 7+ AVG points with a 10x coefficient increase.
- **Risk prediction:** On a real-world public opinion risk prediction task with Qwen3-8B, DiPO achieves 78.37% accuracy and 86.84% F1, outperforming DAPO (76.94% accuracy, 85.80% F1).
- **PPL distribution analysis:** DiPO maintains better separation between correct (low PPL) and error (high PPL) samples throughout training, while DAPO converges both to low PPL regions.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The perplexity space disentangling idea is genuinely novel -- rather than directly using PPL as a reward bias (as in DACE and CDE), DiPO uses PPL to partition the sample space and then applies targeted reward reallocation only to zero-gradient groups. The orthogonality design between reallocated and verification rewards is a clean architectural choice.

**Rigor (Moderate-High):** The theoretical proofs (Theorems 1 and 2) rely on several approximations (first-order Taylor expansion, ignoring cross-context updates, assuming F_t differences are negligible), which the authors explicitly acknowledge. The experimental verification on Qwen3-0.6B supports the theoretical trends but is not a rigorous proof. The online statistical estimation with confidence intervals is well-motivated.

**Reproducibility (Moderate-High):** The paper provides detailed hyperparameter tables, training configurations, and algorithm pseudocode. It builds on publicly available frameworks (VERL, ToolRL) and open models (Qwen series). However, no code repository link is provided.

**Significance (Moderate-High):** The improvements are consistent but modest in absolute terms (typically 1-3 AVG percentage points). The method addresses a real and important problem (zero-gradient extreme groups in GRPO/DAPO). The general applicability across math reasoning, function calling, and risk prediction strengthens the contribution.

**Red flags:**
- The improvements on some individual benchmarks are small or inconsistent (e.g., MIN scores sometimes slightly lower than baselines).
- The paper does not compare against more recent concurrent methods beyond CDE and DACE.
- The risk prediction experiment uses only 4000 data points and lacks details about the task domain.
- The function calling adaptation requires non-trivial modifications to the reward structure (binary thresholding of TooRL's [-3,4] rewards), which may not be optimal.

## Strengths

- Clean and well-motivated problem formulation: the two EETO dilemmas in GRPO-based methods are precisely identified and empirically validated.
- The PSD mechanism is adaptive -- it automatically deactivates when PPL and correctness are not yet correlated (early training), preventing premature interference.
- The BRR design is elegant: by only modifying zero-gradient groups and maintaining orthogonality, it avoids destabilizing the main verification reward signal.
- Strong ablation study clearly demonstrates the individual contributions of PSD and BRR, and shows that directly using PPL as rewards (without BRR) is harmful.
- Coefficient sensitivity analysis demonstrates significantly more robust hyperparameter behavior compared to entropy loss.

## Limitations

- The method introduces additional computational overhead from maintaining the PPL queue and computing conditional probabilities at each step, though the authors do not quantify this cost.
- The binary reward structure (0/1) is assumed throughout; extending to continuous or multi-level rewards may require non-trivial modifications, as seen in the function calling adaptation.
- The approach is evaluated primarily on Qwen-series models; results on more diverse model families (e.g., Mistral, Phi) would strengthen generalizability claims.
- The PSD threshold tau* depends on batch statistics, which may be noisy with small batch sizes or imbalanced data distributions.
- The theoretical analysis relies on multiple idealized assumptions and only provides directional (not quantitative) guarantees.

## Relevance

DiPO与LLM训练稳定性直接相关——它解决了GRPO/DAPO训练中极端样本组零梯度导致训练停滞的问题，通过精细化的探索-利用平衡机制使RL训练更稳定。该方法提供了一种基于困惑度的可解释性手段来理解和控制训练过程中模型的行为分布，属于提升ML可靠性的技术路线。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does DiPO scale to larger models (e.g., 32B, 72B) where the proportion of extreme sample groups may differ significantly?
- Can the PSD threshold adaptation be improved with more sophisticated online estimation methods (e.g., Bayesian updating) for better stability?
- What is the computational overhead of PSD and BRR relative to the total training cost, and can it be further optimized?
- How does DiPO interact with other RLVR improvements such as curriculum learning, data filtering, or multi-turn rollouts?
- Could the perplexity-space disentangling idea be applied to other RL settings beyond GRPO/DAPO, such as PPO with value networks?
- Is the orthogonality between R and R_r strictly necessary, or could a softer coupling provide additional benefits?
