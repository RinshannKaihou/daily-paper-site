When Flat Minima Fail: Characterizing INT4 Quantization Collapse After FP32 Convergence

arXiv: 2604.15167

Date: 2026-04-16

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15167

一句话总结： 通过对 Pythia-160m 全部 154 个训练 checkpoint 的取证式审计，揭示了 FP32 收敛后 INT4 量化鲁棒性会经历三阶段式的灾难性退化（INT4 gap 从 11% 爆增至 517%），且退化始于 FP32 perplexity 收敛点而非学习率衰减点，同时提出了一种振幅校准的学习率调度方法（OLI）可在冷相位将 INT4 gap 降低 2.2 个百分点。
TL;DR: A forensic audit of all 154 Pythia-160m training checkpoints reveals a three-phase INT4 quantization divergence: FP32 convergence, not LR decay, triggers explosive degradation (11% to 517% gap); INT8 is immune throughout; weight kurtosis rules out outlier accumulation as the mechanism; and an Oscillatory Lock-In (OLI) schedule reduces the INT4 gap by 2.2pp in cool phases (p < 0.0001).

核心贡献

  • 发现了 INT4 量化退化具有三阶段结构：快速学习期（步数 0-7K，FP32 与 INT4 同步改善）、元稳定平台期（步数 7K-80K，INT4 gap 保持在 10-14%）、以及爆炸性发散期（步数 80K-143K，INT4 gap 从 14% 飙升至 517%），打破了"量化退化是单调过程"的假设。
  • 证实 FP32 perplexity 收敛点（步数 ~77K）而非学习率衰减起始点是第三阶段发散的精确触发器，表明训练收敛后的持续梯度更新才是 INT4 兼容性破坏的直接原因。
  • 通过 INT8 对照实验（全程 gap < 1%）和权重峰度测量（Phase 3 内与 INT4 gap 负相关，r = -0.26），分别排除了"通用权重分布病变"和"异常值累积"两种替代解释机制。
  • 提出 Oscillatory Lock-In (OLI) 学习率调度策略，通过高幅脉冲（5 * eta_max）后的结构化冷相位实现权重量化网格对齐，在冷相位中将 INT4 gap 降低 2.2 个百分点（t = -5.46, p < 0.0001）。
  • 证明 SGDR 热重启在所有 9 个跨种子比较中（0/9 胜）均恶化 INT4 鲁棒性，首次明确指出振幅校准而非振荡模式本身决定了扰动是有益还是有害。

Key Contributions

  • Identifies a three-phase divergence structure in INT4 quantization robustness across training: rapid learning (0-7K steps), meta-stable plateau (7K-80K, gap bounded at 10-14%), and explosive divergence (80K-143K, gap reaching 517%), overturning the assumption that quantization degradation is monotonic.
  • Demonstrates that FP32 perplexity convergence (~step 77K), not LR decay onset, is the precise trigger for Phase 3 divergence. At divergence onset, LR is still 45% of its maximum, implicating post-convergence weight updates as the proximate cause.
  • Rules out two alternative mechanisms: (1) general weight distribution pathology is excluded by INT8 immunity (gap < 1% throughout all 143K steps); (2) outlier accumulation is excluded by showing weight kurtosis declines during Phase 3 (Pearson r = -0.26 against INT4 gap).
  • Proposes Oscillatory Lock-In (OLI), a learning rate schedule using high-amplitude bumps (5x max LR) followed by structured cool phases, reducing INT4 gap by 2.2pp in cool phases (t = -5.46, p < 0.0001) compared to cosine continuation.
  • Shows that SGDR warm restarts uniformly worsen INT4 robustness (0/9 pairwise wins), demonstrating that amplitude calibration, not oscillation per se, determines whether LR perturbation helps or hurts quantization compatibility.

Methodology

The study is structured as two main experiments:

Pythia Forensic Audit: The authors apply a calibration-free per-group INT4 quantization probe (group size g=128, asymmetric, no GPTQ/AWQ post-hoc optimization) to all 154 publicly available Pythia-160m training checkpoints. This probe measures the "quantization gap" -- relative perplexity degradation (PPL_INT4 - PPL_FP32) / PPL_FP32 -- on a fixed held-out validation set from The Pile (32 batches of 512 tokens, constructed once and reused across all checkpoints). An INT8 per-channel symmetric probe is applied in parallel for comparison. Weight excess kurtosis is measured at 16 checkpoints spanning all three phases to test the outlier accumulation hypothesis.

Fork Experiment: Starting from the step-70,000 checkpoint (last pre-divergence step), the authors fork training for 30,000 additional steps under three LR schedule conditions with N=3 independent seeds each: (A) cosine continuation (control), (B) SGDR with T_0=10,000 step warm restarts from max LR, and (C) their proposed OLI schedule alternating 75-step high-amplitude bumps (5 * max LR) with 300-step cosine-baseline cool phases. All runs use identical hyperparameters to the original Pythia training (Adam, seq_len=2048, batch size 4).

Results & Highlights

  • Three-phase structure confirmed: INT4 gap goes from ~6% (end of Phase 1) to ~14% (end of Phase 2) to 517% (end of Phase 3), while FP32 perplexity barely moves in Phase 3 (33.38 at step 77K, final value 35.30).
  • INT8 is completely immune: Gap stays below 0.79% for all 143,000 steps -- a 650-fold difference from INT4, constraining the mechanism to 4-bit grid resolution specifically.
  • Kurtosis anti-correlated with INT4 gap in Phase 3: Weight excess kurtosis peaks at ~87K steps (27.9) then declines to 3.3 by step 143K, directly refuting the outlier accumulation mechanism proposed by concurrent work (Park et al., 2025).
  • SGDR uniformly harmful: 0/9 pairwise wins against cosine continuation; high-amplitude restarts to max LR accelerate rather than mitigate divergence.
  • OLI cool phases effective: Mean INT4 gap of 5.3% vs. cosine's 7.7% (2.2pp improvement, t = -5.46, p < 0.0001), but at the cost of ~32 points higher FP32 perplexity during bump phases.
  • Practical implication: The derivative of FP32 perplexity with respect to training steps (approaching zero) is a more actionable signal for quantization-aware early stopping than LR schedule monitoring.

Critical Assessment

Novelty (High): The three-phase divergence structure and the FP32 convergence onset predictor are genuinely novel findings. The calibration-free probe design is a methodological contribution that cleanly separates native weight-grid compatibility from post-hoc repair. The kurtosis-based refutation of outlier accumulation provides important negative results. However, the study is limited to a single 160M-parameter model.

Rigor (Moderate-High): The forensic audit across all 154 checkpoints is thorough. Statistical tests are properly applied (two-sample t-test with p < 0.0001 for OLI). The 0/9 pairwise win result for SGDR is clean and compelling. However, the fork experiment uses only N=3 seeds per condition, and the training setup differs substantially from the original Pythia training in tokens per step (~8K vs. ~2M), which slows absolute divergence rates and limits direct extrapolation.

Reproducibility (High): All checkpoints are public, the probe is calibration-free (no dataset-dependent optimization), and code is released. The Pythia training setup is well-documented.

Significance (Moderate-High): The finding that "a well-converged model is not necessarily quantization-ready" has significant practical implications for LLM deployment pipelines. The FP32 convergence signal is actionable. However, generalization beyond Pythia-160m remains unverified, and the OLI intervention has a substantial FP32 cost that limits its practical utility.

Red flags:

  • Single model size (160M) and single training corpus (The Pile) -- generalization is unknown.
  • OLI's FP32 cost (~32 perplexity points) is severe and may be impractical.
  • The fork experiment's training scale is orders of magnitude smaller than original Pythia training, raising questions about whether results transfer.
  • No positive mechanistic account of which specific weight configurations cause INT4 incompatibility -- the paper identifies what is NOT the mechanism but not what IS.

Strengths

  • Comprehensive forensic analysis using all 154 checkpoints provides an unprecedented granularity view of quantization sensitivity across training.
  • Clean experimental design: calibration-free probe deliberately avoids confounds from post-hoc optimization methods.
  • Strong negative results (kurtosis refutation of outlier accumulation, INT8 immunity) that constrain the search space for the true mechanism.
  • Practically actionable finding: monitor FP32 perplexity derivative, not LR schedule, for quantization-aware early stopping.
  • Clear, well-structured writing with transparent discussion of limitations.

Limitations

  • Single model scale: Only Pythia-160m is studied. Whether the three-phase structure generalizes to larger models (1B, 7B, 70B+) or different architectures is unknown.
  • No positive mechanistic account: The paper convincingly rules out outlier accumulation and general distribution pathology, but does not identify the specific weight-level configurations that cause INT4 incompatibility.
  • OLI FP32 cost: The 32-point perplexity degradation during bump phases is a significant practical limitation. Lower bump amplitudes (e.g., 2x max LR) are mentioned but not tested.
  • Small fork experiment scale: The continuation training uses ~8K tokens/step vs. ~2M in original training, making the absolute divergence rates incomparable to production training.
  • Weight-only quantization: The study measures weight-only INT4, not the weight-and-activation (W4A4) setting that is more representative of deployment scenarios.
  • Single training corpus: Only The Pile is used; different data distributions may produce different divergence dynamics.

Relevance

本文对 LLM 训练稳定性与可靠性研究具有高度相关性。它揭示了训练收敛后持续更新会系统性地破坏 INT4 量化兼容性，为"何时停止训练"这一实际问题提供了基于 FP32 perplexity 导数的可操作信号，同时提出了一个值得深入研究的新问题：后收敛阶段的权重漂移如何在量化网格空间中产生级联式的灾难性退化。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the three-phase divergence structure replicate at larger model scales (1B, 7B, 70B) and across different architectures (LLaMA, Mistral, Gemma)?
  2. What is the positive mechanistic explanation for INT4 incompatibility? Can we characterize the specific weight configurations (e.g., inter-grid-point positioning, group-level distribution entropy) that cause compounding degradation?
  3. Can OLI be redesigned with lower bump amplitudes (e.g., 2x max LR instead of 5x) to reduce the FP32 cost while preserving cool-phase INT4 benefits?
  4. How does quantization-aware training (QAT) interact with the three-phase structure? Does simulating quantization noise during Phase 2 prevent Phase 3 divergence?
  5. Is the FP32 perplexity convergence signal robust across different training recipes (different optimizers, LR schedules, data mixtures), and can it be used as an automated early-stopping criterion for quantization-aware training pipelines?
