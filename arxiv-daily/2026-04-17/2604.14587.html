CLion: Efficient Cautious Lion Optimizer with Enhanced Generalization

arXiv: 2604.14587

Date: 2026-04-16

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14587

一句话总结： 本文首次通过算法稳定性分析了Lion优化器的泛化误差为O(1/(N*tau^T))（tau为梯度估计器中非零元素最小绝对值，通常极小），并提出CLion优化器通过谨慎使用sign函数将泛化误差改善至O(1/N)，同时保持与Lion相同的O(sqrt(d)/T^{1/4})收敛速率，在语言建模和图像分类实验中均优于Lion和RLion。
TL;DR: This paper provides the first generalization analysis of the Lion optimizer via algorithmic stability, proving its generalization error is O(1/(N*tau^T)) where tau is typically very small. The proposed CLion optimizer cautiously replaces sign() with identity for small gradient components, improving generalization error to O(1/N) while maintaining the same O(sqrt(d)/T^{1/4}) convergence rate, with empirical gains on WikiText and CIFAR-10/Tiny-ImageNet.

核心贡献

  • 首次建立Lion优化器的泛化理论：基于数学归纳法和算法稳定性，证明Lion的泛化误差为O(1/(N*tau^T))，其中tau是梯度估计器中非零元素的最小绝对值，通常极小导致泛化误差很大；同时发现SignSGD具有相同的泛化界。
  • 设计CLion优化器：当梯度估计器中非零元素的最小绝对值低于阈值nu时，用恒等函数替代sign函数（"谨慎使用sign"），将泛化误差从O(1/(N*tau^T))改善至O(1/N)，达到与SGD/SGDM相同的泛化水平。
  • 完整的收敛性分析：证明CLion在非凸随机优化下具有O(sqrt(d)/T^{1/4})的收敛速率（l1范数），与Lion优化器相同。
  • 实验验证：在WikiText-2/103语言建模和CIFAR-10/Tiny-ImageNet图像分类任务上，CLion在训练/测试损失和准确率上均优于Lion和RLion。

Key Contributions

  • First generalization analysis of Lion: Proves via algorithmic stability and mathematical induction that Lion has generalization error O(1/(N*tau^T)), where tau is the smallest absolute value of non-zero elements in gradient estimators. As a byproduct, SignSGD is shown to share this same bound.
  • CLion optimizer design: Introduces a piecewise activation function that uses sign(c_t) when all non-zero gradient components exceed threshold nu, and falls back to identity c_t otherwise. This "cautious" usage eliminates the tau^T dependence in generalization error.
  • Improved generalization guarantee: Proves CLion achieves O(1/N) generalization error, matching SGD and SGDM, compared to Lion's much larger O(1/(N*tau^T)) since tau is typically very small.
  • Preserved convergence rate: Establishes that CLion maintains the same O(sqrt(d)/T^{1/4}) convergence rate under l1-norm as the original Lion for nonconvex stochastic optimization.
  • Empirical validation: Demonstrates consistent improvements over Lion, RLion, and other baselines on language modeling (WikiText-2/103 with Transformers) and image classification (CIFAR-10 with ResNet-18, Tiny-ImageNet with ResNet-34).

Methodology

Problem Setting

The paper studies nonconvex stochastic optimization: min_w F(w) = E_{xi~D}[f(w; xi)], using empirical risk F_S(w) = (1/N) * sum f(w; xi_i) as a surrogate. The excess population risk decomposes into generalization error (gap between population and empirical risk) and optimization error.

Generalization Analysis of Lion (Theorem 1)

The key insight is that the sign function's discontinuity amplifies perturbations. Using algorithmic stability, the authors show that for two datasets S and S^(i) differing in one sample, ||sign(c_t) - sign(c_t^(i))|| <= (2sqrt(d)/tau) ||c_t - c_t^(i)||, where tau is the minimum absolute value of non-zero elements across all gradient estimators. Through mathematical induction, this leads to generalization error O(1/(tau^T * N)), which grows rapidly with T since tau is typically very small.

CLion Algorithm

CLion modifies Lion's update rule with a conditional activation:

  • If min_{j in S_t} |(c_t)_j| >= nu (all non-zero gradient components exceed threshold): use h(c_t) = sign(c_t) (standard Lion update)
  • Otherwise: use h(c_t) = c_t (identity update)

The key property is that h(c_t) is Lipschitz with constant 2sqrt(d) (independent of tau), unlike Lion's 2sqrt(d)/tau. This eliminates the exponential dependence on T in generalization error.

Convergence Analysis (Theorem 3)

Under standard assumptions (smoothness, Lipschitzness, bounded variance), with appropriate step size eta = O(1/T^{3/4}) and momentum parameters beta_1, beta_2 = 1 - O(1/sqrt(T)), CLion achieves convergence rate O(sqrt(d)/T^{1/4}) under l1-norm of the gradient.

Results & Highlights

  • Language Modeling (WikiText-2, 7-layer Transformer): CLion achieves lower train/test loss and perplexity than SGD, SGDM, Adam, AdamW, Lion, and RLion. The gap is particularly visible in test perplexity, confirming better generalization.
  • Language Modeling (WikiText-103, 21-layer Transformer): CLion continues to outperform all baselines on both training and test metrics, with more pronounced gains on the larger dataset.
  • Image Classification (CIFAR-10, ResNet-18): CLion achieves higher test accuracy and lower test loss than Lion and RLion, demonstrating generalization benefits in vision tasks.
  • Image Classification (Tiny-ImageNet, ResNet-34): CLion outperforms all competitors on test accuracy, further validating the generalization improvement.
  • Threshold sensitivity: The threshold nu is set to very small values (10^-13 to 10^-6) across experiments, suggesting the method is not sensitive to this hyperparameter and the identity fallback rarely triggers for well-conditioned gradients.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. The key idea of conditionally switching between sign and identity is simple and somewhat incremental. However, the generalization analysis of Lion is the first of its kind and provides genuine theoretical insight. The discovery that SignSGD shares Lion's generalization bound is a useful byproduct.
  • Rigor: High. The proofs are complete and follow standard techniques in algorithmic stability. All assumptions are standard (smoothness, Lipschitzness, bounded variance). The mathematical induction framework is well-constructed.
  • Reproducibility: Moderate. All hyperparameters are reported, but no code is provided. The experiments use standard benchmarks and architectures. The threshold nu values vary across tasks (10^-13 to 10^-6) without clear guidance on how to set them.
  • Significance: The theoretical contribution (generalization analysis of Lion) is more significant than the practical method (CLion). The practical gains, while consistent, are modest and demonstrated only on relatively small-scale tasks.
  • Red flags:
  • The tau parameter in Lion's generalization bound could be argued to be an artifact of the proof technique rather than a fundamental limitation. A tighter analysis might yield a better bound for Lion directly.
  • Experiments are limited to small-scale models (7/21-layer Transformers, ResNet-18/34). No large-scale LLM or modern architecture experiments are provided.
  • The identity fallback essentially makes CLion behave like SGD with momentum for iterations where small gradient components exist, which partly explains the improved generalization but raises questions about whether the sign function is providing any benefit in those regimes.
  • The comparison with RLion (Rong et al. 2025) uses alpha=10 or alpha=10^4, which may not be optimally tuned.

Strengths

  • First rigorous generalization analysis of the popular Lion optimizer, filling a clear gap in the literature.
  • The O(1/(N*tau^T)) bound provides meaningful insight: the discontinuous sign function amplifies perturbations proportionally to 1/tau, explaining potential generalization issues.
  • The proposed fix (CLion) is theoretically principled and comes with matching generalization and convergence guarantees.
  • The piecewise activation function is a clean design that preserves Lion's properties for large gradient components while avoiding instability for small ones.
  • Comprehensive proofs with full details in appendices.
  • Consistent empirical improvements across multiple benchmarks.

Limitations

  • Scalability unknown: No experiments on large-scale models (e.g., LLMs with billions of parameters). The practical relevance for modern AI training is unclear.
  • Threshold selection: The paper does not provide principled guidance for choosing nu. The values used (10^-13 to 10^-6) span 7 orders of magnitude, and sensitivity analysis is missing.
  • Modest empirical gains: While consistent, the improvements over Lion are relatively small in absolute terms. The difference from SGD/SGDM is sometimes negligible.
  • Theoretical gaps: The generalization bound for Lion relies on tau > 0, which requires all non-zero gradient components to be bounded away from zero. In practice, this assumption may be fragile, especially during late training.
  • No comparison with adaptive methods: The paper compares with Adam/AdamW but does not deeply analyze why CLion should be preferred over these well-established alternatives in practical settings.
  • Dimensionality dependence: The convergence rate O(sqrt(d)/T^{1/4}) depends on dimension d, which is problematic for high-dimensional models.

Relevance

本文从算法稳定性角度分析了sign-based优化器（Lion、SignSGD）的泛化性质，揭示了sign函数导致泛化误差随训练迭代指数级增长的理论缺陷，这对理解LLM训练中优化器的选择和训练稳定性具有直接参考价值。CLion通过"谨慎sign"策略改善泛化性的思路，为设计更可靠的训练算法提供了理论依据。
