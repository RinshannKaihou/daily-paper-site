Quantifying Cross-Query Contradictions in Multi-Query LLM Reasoning

arXiv: 2604.14525

Date: 2026-04-16

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14525

一句话总结： 本文提出"案例文件逻辑一致性"框架，通过构建390个多查询推理基准和求解器增强的承诺追踪与反例引导修复方法，将LLM在多查询场景下的跨查询矛盾率大幅降低（SetCons从0.56提升至0.94），同时保持单查询准确率。
TL;DR: The paper formalizes case-file logical consistency for multi-query LLM reasoning, introduces a 390-case benchmark with set-level metrics, and proposes a solver-augmented belief-state tracking method with counterexample-guided repair that reduces cross-query contradictions from SetCons 0.56 to 0.94 across eight LLMs while preserving per-query accuracy.

核心贡献

  • 问题形式化：将"案例文件逻辑一致性"定义为在共享前提下的集合级推理任务，要求模型对一组相互依赖的查询给出全局可满足的承诺状态。
  • 基准与指标：构建了包含390个案例文件、2450个查询束的多查询推理基准，涵盖关系/SAT、时序/SMT、策略/规则、欠定/溯因四个领域；提出了集合级指标（SetConsRate、ContradictionDensity、RevisionCost）以衡量全局一致性。
  • 求解器增强修复方法：设计了基于信念状态追踪的增量可满足性检查方法，利用UNSAT核心定位矛盾并通过反例引导的最小修复策略（局部翻转、弱化、选择性撤回）恢复全局一致性。
  • 全面评估：在8个开源LLM上进行实验，验证了方法在保持单查询准确率的同时大幅提升全局一致性，且开销远低于自一致性采样。

Key Contributions

  • Problem formalization: Introduces "case-file logical consistency" as a set-level reasoning problem where answers to interdependent queries under shared premises must remain jointly satisfiable.
  • Benchmark and metrics: Constructs a 390-case benchmark (2,450 query bundles) across four domains (relational/SAT, temporal/SMT, policy/rules, underspecified/abductive) with solver-verified entailment/contradiction/unknown labels and proposes set-level metrics (SetConsRate, AUC-PrefixCons, RevisionCost, ContradictionDensity).
  • Solver-augmented belief-state tracking: Proposes an incremental satisfiability checking method that extracts symbolic commitments from LLM outputs, maintains a belief state, and performs counterexample-guided minimal repair using UNSAT cores when contradictions are detected.
  • Comprehensive evaluation: Demonstrates effectiveness across 8 open-weight LLMs (including DeepSeek-R1, Qwen3-235B, Llama-3.1-405B), showing SetCons improvements from 0.56 to 0.94 with modest overhead (1.44-1.58x vs. 2.55-2.75x for self-consistency sampling).

Methodology

The method operates in four steps:

  1. Commitment extraction: After the LLM answers a query with a label (Entailed/Contradicted/Unknown), a structured extractor (f_ext) maps the answer to a symbolic commitment in a constraint language (CNF for SAT domains, SMT-LIB for temporal/arithmetic domains). Entailed answers produce positive atoms, Contradicted answers produce negated atoms, and Unknown answers produce no commitment.
  1. Incremental satisfiability checking: The belief state B_t is updated incrementally by conjoining each new commitment. A SAT solver (CaDiCaL) or SMT solver (Z3) checks satisfiability after each step. If satisfiable, the answer is accepted; if unsatisfiable, a contradiction is flagged.
  1. UNSAT core localization: When a contradiction is detected, the solver returns a minimal unsatisfiable subset of commitments, pinpointing which answers are jointly incompatible.
  1. Counterexample-guided repair: The method attempts minimal repairs prioritized lexicographically: (a) local flip of current label, (b) softening of current commitment, (c) selective retraction of prior commitments. The LLM is prompted with the conflict information to propose a revised answer, verified by the solver. The repair loop is capped at 2 attempts per query.

An optional "logic-filtered self-consistency" variant samples K candidate answers per query and filters out those that violate satisfiability before majority voting.

Results & Highlights

  • SetCons improvement: Solver-augmented checking alone raises SetCons by +0.30 to +0.35 absolute; adding repair yields a further +0.05 to +0.10, reaching SetCons of 0.94 on the best configurations.
  • RevisionCost reduction: RevisionCost drops 2-3x with check-only and falls below 1.0 with repair, indicating much more stable commitment states.
  • Per-query accuracy preserved: Improvements in global coherence occur without significant changes to per-query accuracy, and critically without increasing Unknown prediction rates (stable at 17-19%).
  • Low overhead: Check-only adds ~1.26-1.33x overhead; Check+Repair adds ~1.44-1.58x, substantially cheaper than self-consistency at 2.55-2.75x for K=20.
  • Domain insights: Gains are largest in constraint-heavy domains (temporal/capacity) where a single wrong entailment can break global feasibility. In underspecified abductive cases, the method correctly converts overconfident answers to Unknown.
  • Commitment extraction accuracy: 91-95% across domains, with extraction failures triggering conservative Unknown fallbacks rather than false satisfiability judgments.
  • Inter-annotator agreement: Fleiss' kappa = 0.82 across the benchmark.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. The individual components (solver-augmented reasoning, belief-state tracking, UNSAT cores) are not new. The primary novelty lies in the problem formulation (case-file logical consistency as a set-level evaluation) and the integration of these techniques for multi-query contradiction repair. The benchmark is a genuine contribution.
  • Rigor: High. The paper includes formal definitions, four evaluation domains, eight models, ablation studies, domain breakdowns, overhead analysis, and qualitative examples. Solver validation on a stratified sample and inter-annotator agreement metrics strengthen the benchmark quality.
  • Reproducibility: Good. Code and data are released. Solver configurations (CaDiCaL v1.4.1, Z3 v4.8.17), decoding settings, and hyperparameters are specified. The commitment extraction prompt template is included in the appendix.
  • Significance: Relevant but incremental. The problem of cross-query consistency is practically important for real deployments (clinical, legal, scientific). However, the approach relies on relatively simple commitment extraction (single forward pass with string-matching heuristics) and small-scale benchmarks (390 cases). Scalability to open-domain, free-form reasoning remains uncertain.
  • Red flags: (1) The benchmark is relatively small (390 cases) and hand-crafted; generalization to naturally occurring multi-query interactions is untested. (2) Commitment extraction relies on the same LLM being evaluated, creating a circular dependency -- extraction errors could mask or introduce contradictions. (3) The "gpt-oss-120b" model lacks clear identification, making replication uncertain. (4) No evaluation on proprietary frontier models (GPT-4, Claude) limits practical relevance for deployed systems.

Strengths

  • Well-motivated problem: cross-query consistency is a real and underappreciated failure mode in LLM reasoning.
  • Clean formalization with principled metrics that go beyond per-query accuracy.
  • The repair mechanism is practical and lightweight, with bounded computational overhead.
  • Honest discussion of extraction noise, solver sensitivity, and failure modes.
  • The finding that Unknown rates remain stable (gains come from genuine repair, not strategic abstention) strengthens the credibility of the results.

Limitations

  • The benchmark is small (390 cases) and manually constructed; it may not capture the diversity of real-world multi-query interactions.
  • Commitment extraction accuracy (91-95%) means 5-9% of extractions are incorrect, which could lead to both false satisfiability and spurious contradiction detection.
  • The approach is limited to decidable fragments (propositional SAT, linear arithmetic SMT); extending to richer logics (probabilistic, modal, higher-order) is acknowledged but unresolved.
  • No evaluation on proprietary frontier models or in truly interactive/agentic settings where queries are generated dynamically.
  • The method assumes a fixed premise set; handling evolving or ambiguous premises is not addressed.

Relevance

这项工作与LLM推理可靠性和一致性评估直接相关。它提出的集合级一致性指标和求解器增强修复方法为衡量和提升LLM在多轮推理场景中的逻辑稳定性提供了新工具，对理解LLM在实际部署中的可靠性问题具有重要参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the approach scale to longer query bundles (e.g., 50-100 queries) and naturally occurring multi-turn conversations rather than curated case files?
  • Can commitment extraction be improved via fine-tuning or reinforcement learning to reduce the 5-9% extraction error rate?
  • How would the method perform on proprietary frontier models (GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini) and in open-domain settings where the constraint language is less structured?
  • Is it possible to integrate belief-state tracking into training-time objectives (e.g., as a consistency regularization loss) rather than relying on inference-time repair?
  • What is the interaction between case-file consistency and other failure modes such as hallucination, sycophancy, or instruction-following drift?
