Mind DeepResearch Technical Report

arXiv: 2604.14518

Date: 2026-04-16

一句话总结： 理想汽车提出MindDR框架，通过三智能体协作架构（规划/深度搜索/报告）和四阶段训练流水线（SFT冷启动、Search-RL、Report-RL、偏好对齐），仅用~30B参数模型即达到深度研究任务SOTA性能，在BrowseComp-ZH达45.7%、DeepResearch Bench达52.5分，超越同规模开源系统并媲美更大规模模型。
TL;DR: MindDR from Li Auto is a cost-effective multi-agent deep research framework using ~30B-parameter models that achieves state-of-the-art results through a three-agent architecture and four-stage training pipeline (SFT, Search-RL, Report-RL, preference alignment), outperforming comparable-scale open-source systems and rivaling much larger models.

核心贡献

  • 三智能体协作推理架构：将深度研究任务分解为规划智能体（Planning Agent）、深度搜索智能体（DeepSearch Agent）和报告智能体（Report Agent），支持并行搜索和上下文隔离，天然缓解单模型超长上下文处理压力。
  • 四阶段渐进式训练流水线：SFT冷启动建立基础能力，Search-RL通过动态奖励调度优化多步检索效率，Report-RL利用RACE评分体系优化长文本生成质量，偏好对齐（DPO+Self-SFT）修正格式和时间错误。
  • 轻量级步骤级信用分配机制：在Search-RL中通过知识图谱实体匹配实现无需LLM推理的过程奖励建模（PRM），配合动态奖励系数调度，在不引入critic模型开销的情况下实现细粒度的搜索效率优化。
  • MindDR Bench评测基准：从真实用户交互日志中精选500条中文深度研究查询，提出超越单一RACE指标的多维度评估体系，覆盖推理轨迹、工具调用、大纲生成和报告质量四个维度。
  • 显著训练效率提升：相比前代v1.0的280K RFT样本（3.6B tokens），v1.5仅需约1.03B训练tokens（6K GPU卡时 vs 15K卡时），性能反而更强。

Key Contributions

  • Three-agent collaborative inference architecture decomposing deep research into Planning, DeepSearch, and Report agents, enabling parallel search execution and context isolation to alleviate long-context processing burdens.
  • Four-stage progressive training pipeline (SFT cold-start, Search-RL, Report-RL, preference alignment) with each stage targeting a well-defined capability frontier using tailored optimization algorithms and reward signals.
  • Lightweight step-level credit assignment via knowledge-graph entity matching for process reward modeling (PRM) in Search-RL, combined with dynamic reward coefficient scheduling, achieving fine-grained search efficiency optimization without critic model overhead.
  • MindDR Bench, a curated benchmark of 500 real-world Chinese deep research queries from production user interactions, evaluated through a comprehensive multi-dimensional rubric system beyond single RACE metrics.
  • Dramatic training efficiency gains: MindDR v1.5 uses ~1.03B training tokens and 6K GPU hours versus v1.0's 3.6B tokens and 15K GPU hours, while achieving substantially stronger performance.

Methodology

MindDR addresses the core challenge of achieving leading deep research performance with small models (~30B parameters) through two tightly coupled components:

Inference Pipeline — Three-Agent Architecture:

  • Planning Agent analyzes user intent and decomposes queries into structured subtasks.
  • DeepSearch Agent instances execute ReAct-style loops in parallel for each subtask, performing multi-source retrieval, evidence integration, and intermediate reasoning.
  • Report Agent synthesizes all sub-reports into a coherent, citation-grounded Markdown report.
  • An Extended Chain-of-Thought (XoT) memory mechanism maintains shared reasoning context across agents and tool calls, preserving full information provenance.

Training Pipeline — Four Stages:

  1. SFT Cold-Start (~15K trajectories, ~100K-150K samples): Establishes tool invocation, format adherence, and multi-turn reasoning via behavior cloning. Carefully calibrated to preserve policy entropy for subsequent RL (early stopping when long-context format error rate < 2.5%).
  2. Search-RL (~35K queries): Optimizes long-horizon search via GRPO (dense models) or GSPO (MoE models) with a composite reward of tool correctness, format compliance, PRM (entity coverage via string matching), and ORM (answer accuracy). Reward coefficients are dynamically scheduled through three phases as each capability saturates.
  3. Report-RL: Uses RACE Rubrics evaluated by LLM-as-Judge for comprehensiveness, readability, insight, and instruction-following rewards, supplemented by rule-based citation and format rewards. Employs DAPO (dense) or GSPO (MoE) optimization. Training data includes both long-form (with full retrieval context) and short-form (without retrieval context) for efficiency.
  4. Preference Alignment: DPO on curated temporal (1.8K) and table-repair (2.8K) datasets plus Self-SFT on 4.3K high-quality self-sampled reports to correct structured errors and improve coherence.

Data Synthesis: Knowledge-graph-grounded query synthesis from Baidu Baike and Wikipedia, producing multi-hop reasoning questions with guaranteed reasoning reachability, path necessity, and structural independence. Mixed with real user queries for ecological validity.

Results & Highlights

  • DeepSearch Benchmarks (MindDR-v1.5-30B-A3B): BrowseComp-ZH 45.7%, BrowseComp 42.8%, xbench-DS 75.0%, GAIA-DS 70.9%, WideSearch 44.0% — best among open-source agent systems on 4 of 5 benchmarks.
  • DeepResearch Bench: Score of 52.5, leading on the public leaderboard across all five RACE dimensions.
  • MindDR Bench: RACE score 51.8, surpassing Gemini 3.1 (49.65), Gemini 2.5 Pro (48.34), and all other baselines on comprehensiveness, insight, instruction following, and readability.
  • Temporal Error Rate: 2.0%, lowest among all compared systems (vs. Gemini 10.2%, Qwen3 8.2%, Kimi 3.2%).
  • Inference Efficiency: MindDR occupies the "Accurate & Efficient" quadrant on BrowseComp-ZH, achieving the highest accuracy (45.7%) while requiring the fewest average context tokens and tool calls among top-performing systems.
  • Training Cost: ~6K GPU hours for v1.5 vs ~15K for v1.0 — a 2.5x reduction with stronger performance.
  • MoE vs Dense: The 30B-A3B (MoE) variant surprisingly outperforms the 32B dense variant on most DS benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of GSPO for MoE training stability.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. The multi-agent decomposition and staged training are well-motivated but individually not groundbreaking. The key novelty lies in the careful integration: the dynamic reward scheduling in Search-RL and the PRM based on entity string matching (avoiding LLM-based critic cost) are practical contributions. The SFT-RL balance analysis with quantitative early stopping criteria is a useful engineering insight.
  • Rigor: High. Extensive ablation studies covering each training stage, optimization algorithm comparison (GRPO vs DAPO vs GSPO), data format effects (long-form vs short-form), and efficiency analysis. The paper includes both DS and DR evaluations with multiple benchmarks.
  • Reproducibility: Limited. While the methodology is described in detail, the proprietary knowledge base, internal search tools, and real user query data are not publicly available. The base models (Qwen3-32B, Qwen3-30B-A3B) are open, but the training infrastructure (Li-veRL) is proprietary. No code release is mentioned.
  • Significance: High practical significance. Demonstrating that ~30B models can rival 100B+ systems for deep research is an important finding for the field, especially for deployment-constrained scenarios. The MindDR Bench contribution addresses a real gap in Chinese-language evaluation.
  • Red flags: (1) MindDR Bench queries come from Li Auto's own product, raising potential benchmark design bias concerns. (2) Some baselines (e.g., GLM-4.6 BrowseComp 49.5%) actually outperform MindDR on individual metrics, but the paper frames results favorably. (3) The comparison is not always apples-to-apples — some baselines are foundation models without agent scaffolding. (4) The v1.0 numbers (trained with RFT only) serve as a somewhat weak strawman.

Strengths

  • Comprehensive system design that addresses both search efficiency and report quality through principled decomposition.
  • Strong empirical results demonstrating that small models with proper training can match or exceed much larger models.
  • Detailed analysis of the SFT-RL balance with quantitative early stopping criteria — a practical contribution often overlooked.
  • Introduction of MindDR Bench with multi-dimensional evaluation beyond single RACE scores, better reflecting real user experience.
  • Efficiency analysis showing the system achieves the best accuracy-efficiency trade-off on BrowseComp-ZH.
  • The preference alignment stage effectively addresses subtle issues like temporal expression errors that persist in even strong systems.

Limitations

  • Scalability to longer contexts beyond 128K tokens remains an open challenge, acknowledged by the authors.
  • The evaluation primarily relies on RACE framework and factual accuracy metrics; deeper aspects like methodological soundness and argument novelty are not captured.
  • MindDR Bench is limited to Chinese queries from a single automotive-domain product, potentially limiting generalizability.
  • No open-source release of code, data, or models mentioned — limiting community reproduction and validation.
  • The three-agent architecture introduces engineering complexity that may not always be justified for simpler queries.
  • The paper does not analyze failure modes in depth — when and why does the system produce low-quality reports?
