IUQ: Interrogative Uncertainty Quantification for Long-Form Large Language Model Generation

arXiv: 2604.15109

Date: 2026-04-16

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15109

一句话总结： IUQ提出了一种"审问式"不确定性量化框架，通过将长文本中的事实声明分解为原子声明并生成独立问题来探测模型真实知识，结合样本间一致性和样本内忠实度两个维度来检测LLM在长文本生成中为维持逻辑连贯性而编造事实信息的倾向，在FActScore和LongFact数据集上跨多个模型家族取得优于现有方法的AUROC表现。
TL;DR: IUQ introduces an interrogate-then-respond paradigm for quantifying uncertainty in long-form LLM outputs by decomposing claims into atomic questions, probing the model's real knowledge independently of generative context, and combining inter-sample consistency with intra-sample faithfulness -- revealing that LLMs frequently fabricate factually incorrect yet logically coherent content.

核心贡献

  • 提出IUQ（Interrogative Uncertainty Quantification）框架，利用"审问-回应"范式对长文本LLM输出进行声明级别的不确定性量化，同时度量样本间一致性和样本内忠实度。
  • 揭示了一个被忽视的现象：LLM在长文本生成中会为了维持逻辑连贯性而编造事实信息，现有仅基于跨样本一致性的UQ方法无法检测此类编造行为。
  • 设计了基于指数衰减核函数的错误传播机制，将早期不忠实声明的影响向后传播，建模自回归生成中上下文条件化导致的虚假信息级联效应。
  • 提供了声明级别的"忠实度景观"（faithfulness landscape）可视化，展示模型在不同话题和声明位置上的编造倾向。
  • 在GPT-4o、Llama-3.1/3.3-70B、Qwen2-72B、Gemma-3-27B、Mistral-24B等6个模型家族上进行实验验证，代码已开源。

Key Contributions

  • Proposes IUQ, a novel black-box UQ framework that decomposes long-form LLM responses into atomic claims, generates tailored short questions for each claim, and probes the model's knowledge independently of the generative context to detect fabricated information.
  • Identifies and quantifies an under-explored phenomenon where LLMs fabricate factual claims to maintain logical coherence in long-form generation, which existing inter-sample consistency methods fail to capture.
  • Introduces an exponential-decay influence kernel that propagates the penalty of unfaithful early claims to subsequent claims, modeling the causal structure of context-conditioned fabrication in autoregressive generation.
  • Demonstrates superior AUROC performance over baselines including Claim Entailment, Closeness Centrality, Perplexity, CCP, and Frequency Scoring across two long-form benchmarks (FActScore, LongFact) and six model families.
  • Provides open-source implementation and detailed claim-level faithfulness analysis across models of varying sizes (24B to 72B parameters).

Methodology

IUQ follows an interrogate-then-respond paradigm with five core stages:

  1. Response Generation: Given a prompt, sample N=5 diverse long-form responses from the model at temperature t=1.0. Responses that refuse to answer are excluded.
  1. Claim Decomposition: Each response is decomposed into atomic claims using the same model at temperature t=0, following established practices (FActScore-style decomposition).
  1. Claim-Level Question-Answering: For each atomic claim, the model generates up to 3 targeted questions that extract the information contained in the claim. Then, for each question, the model generates multiple answers (n_a answers) independently of the original generative context. These answers represent the model's real knowledge on the subject, decoupled from the conditioning on prior generated text.
  1. Claim-Level Faithfulness: The framework checks whether the model's independent answers contradict the original claims. The faithfulness score F(c_i) is defined as 1 minus the average contradiction percentage between the answer sets and preceding claims. A claim is faithful (F=1) if no contradiction exists. Response-level faithfulness is the average across all claims.
  1. Claim-Level Uncertainty: The final uncertainty score combines inter-sample consistency (claim entailment score S) with the influence of unfaithful claims. An exponential-decay kernel propagates the impact of early unfaithful claims to subsequent claims through a convolution-based weighting W(c_i). The uncertainty is U(c_i) = S(c_i) * W(c_i), where W accumulates the decayed unfaithfulness of all preceding claims.

Additionally, the paper proposes an answer-level entropy-based metric U_A that leverages token probabilities from short-answer sets rather than the original long-form response, circumventing the degradation of token-probability approaches on long texts.

Results & Highlights

  • AUROC Performance: IUQ achieves the highest or second-highest AUROC across nearly all model-dataset combinations. On FActScore, IUQ outperforms the best baseline (Closeness Centrality C_C) by notable margins on models including Llama-3.3-70B, Qwen2-72B, and GPT-4o. On LongFact, similar improvements are observed.
  • Faithfulness Analysis: Models exhibit lower faithfulness on FActScore (obscure biographies) than LongFact (popular topics), confirming that LLMs fabricate more on underrepresented subjects. Within individual responses, faithfulness varies significantly -- models mix faithful claims with fabricated information.
  • Ablation on Influence Kernels: The exponential-decay kernel outperforms linear, accumulative, and no-error-propagation variants, validating the design of context-conditioned fabrication modeling.
  • Directional Validation (IUQ-rev): A controlled variant that checks contradiction against subsequent (rather than preceding) claims underperforms IUQ, confirming that the direction of context dependence matters.
  • Computational Cost: IUQ requires approximately 47.3% more tokens than the Graph-Based Uncertainty baseline, primarily due to the additional question generation and answer sampling stages.
  • Case Study: The Rory Byrne example powerfully illustrates how the model fabricates an entire architect biography (including fictional firm "Baxter Byrne Associates" and landmark "Arkangel Tower") while inter-sample consistency alone yields misleadingly high support scores (S=0.60) since multiple generations corroborate the same false premise.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (High): The core insight -- that LLMs fabricate information for logical consistency and this cannot be detected by inter-sample methods alone -- is genuinely novel and well-motivated. The interrogate-then-respond paradigm is a clean conceptual contribution that distinguishes IUQ from prior work.
  • Rigor (Moderate-High): The evaluation covers 6 model families and 2 datasets. However, both datasets are relatively small (50 entities each), and the factuality annotation relies on GPT-4o, introducing circularity concerns. The statistical significance analysis (Pearson r with 95% CI) partially addresses the small sample concern.
  • Reproducibility (High): Open-source code, clearly defined prompts in the appendix, specific hyperparameters reported (N=5, t=1.0, n_q <= 3), and structured output parsing details support reproducibility.
  • Significance (High): Addressing uncertainty quantification for long-form generation is practically important and technically challenging. The finding that models fabricate coherent but false information has implications for deploying LLMs in high-stakes settings.
  • Red Flags: (1) Using the same model as both interrogator and responder introduces potential systematic bias, though the authors acknowledge this and argue it prevents cross-model bias. (2) The contradiction evaluation itself relies on LLM judgment, which could hallucinate. (3) The 47.3% additional token cost is non-trivial for production deployment. (4) The small dataset size (50 entities) limits generalizability claims. (5) Refusing responses are excluded, which may inflate performance metrics.

Strengths

  • Elegant problem framing that exposes a real blind spot in existing UQ methods for long-form generation.
  • The interrogate-then-respond metaphor is intuitive and the framework design is modular and extensible.
  • Comprehensive evaluation across diverse model families (open-source and proprietary) and datasets.
  • The exponential-decay influence kernel is a principled approach to modeling context-conditioned fabrication.
  • Detailed case study (Rory Byrne) that compellingly demonstrates the method's advantage over inter-sample consistency alone.
  • Clear analysis of model faithfulness as a distinct dimension from inter-sample consistency.

Limitations

  • Computational Overhead: The multi-stage pipeline (claim decomposition, question generation, answer sampling, contradiction evaluation) requires ~47.3% more tokens than graph-based baselines, which may be prohibitive for real-time applications.
  • LLM-as-Judge Circularity: The contradiction evaluation and claim decomposition both rely on LLM outputs, meaning the UQ system itself is susceptible to hallucination, which the authors acknowledge but do not fully resolve.
  • Small Evaluation Set: Only 50 entities per dataset, and both datasets rely on Wikipedia as ground truth, limiting diversity of factuality judgments.
  • Exclusion of Refusal Responses: Responses where the model declines to answer are excluded, removing an important signal about model uncertainty.
  • Temperature Dependence: The method's effectiveness may vary with sampling temperature, and only t=1.0 is systematically evaluated.
  • Same-Model Interrogator: Using the same model as interrogator and responder may miss contradictions that an external model would catch, or introduce model-specific blind spots.

Relevance

本文与LLM可靠性研究高度相关。IUQ框架直接解决了长文本生成中的幻觉检测问题，通过声明级别的忠实度分析揭示了LLM为维持逻辑连贯性而编造事实的倾向，这对于提升LLM在实际部署中的可信度至关重要。其"审问式"探测方法为理解模型内部知识与生成行为之间的不一致提供了新视角，与LLM训练稳定性和可解释性研究中的不确定性建模方向形成互补。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does IUQ perform on non-English long-form generation, where claim decomposition and question generation may be less reliable?
  • Can the interrogator and responder be different models (e.g., a smaller, faster interrogator) without sacrificing faithfulness estimation quality?
  • What is the relationship between claim-level faithfulness scores and the model's internal representations (e.g., attention patterns, hidden states) at the corresponding generation positions?
  • How does IUQ scale to very long documents (e.g., multi-page reports, book chapters) where the number of atomic claims becomes very large?
  • Could the faithfulness signal be used as a training objective (e.g., RLHF reward) to reduce fabrication tendencies during fine-tuning?
  • How sensitive is IUQ to the choice of temperature for question generation and answer sampling?
