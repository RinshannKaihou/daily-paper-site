> **一句话总结：** 本文通过结构分析与因果干预，揭示 GPT-2 中 attention sink 产生的具体电路机制——由学习到的 query 偏置(bQ)、第一层 MLP 对位置编码的变换(EPE)、以及 key 投影矩阵(Wk)的坐标级结构三者协同作用导致——但关键发现是这三个组件各自独立可以被架构省略而 sink 仍然存在，说明 attention sink 在不同架构中可能通过不同的电路实现。

> **TL;DR:** This paper identifies the specific parameter-level circuit behind attention sinks in GPT-2 (124M): a learned query bias interacts with the first-layer MLP's transformation of the first positional encoding (EPE) and coordinate-level structure in the key projection to produce a strong source-agnostic score shift toward position 1. Ten causal interventions confirm each component is necessary. However, each component is also individually dispensable at the architecture level -- models lacking query biases, MLPs, or positional encodings still exhibit sinks -- meaning attention sinks arise via distinct circuits in different architectures, and universal mitigation strategies may not work.

## 核心贡献

- 精确定位了 GPT-2 中 attention sink 的三组件电路机制：学习到的 query 偏置 (bQ)、第一层 MLP 对位置编码的变换形成的"有效位置编码" (EPE)、以及 key 投影矩阵 (Wk) 在 EPE 巨大激活坐标上的高幅度结构
- 提出了"源无关偏移"(source-agnostic shift) 概念，将 attention score 分解为与源位置无关的目标位置偏置项，证明其对位置 1 系统性偏大
- 通过十种因果干预（包括置零 bQ、替换 EPE、交换位置编码等）严格验证了各组件的因果必要性
- 发现三个组件虽在 GPT-2 中构成 sink 电路，但在架构层面各自独立可被省略（无 bQ 的模型、无 MLP 的模型、无位置编码的模型均有 sink），表明 attention sink 是一种优化过程可靠学习的结构性计算需求，而非单一通用机制的产物
- 定义了"有效位置编码"(EPE) 概念，实验验证其与第一层 MLP 写入残差流的实际位置信号余弦相似度超过 0.99（位置 1）

## Key Contributions

- Identifies the precise three-component circuit for attention sinks in GPT-2: learned query bias (bQ), the first-layer MLP transformation of positional encodings forming an "effective positional encoding" (EPE), and coordinate-level structure in the key projection (Wk) at massive-activation dimensions
- Introduces the "source-agnostic shift" decomposition of attention scores, showing that the position-1 shift is systematically and anomalously large across heads and layers 4-11
- Conducts ten targeted causal interventions that confirm the necessity of each circuit component, including: nullifying bQ, swapping EPE vs. raw PE, zeroing specific Wk columns, and transplanting the sink to a new position
- Demonstrates that all three circuit components are individually dispensable at the architecture level -- models without query biases, without MLP blocks, or without positional encodings still exhibit attention sinks -- implying the phenomenon emerges through architecture-specific circuits
- Validates findings across three input domains (natural language from SST-2, math from GSM8K, code from HumanEval) with consistent results
- Defines and validates the Effective Positional Encoding (EPE) construct, showing cosine similarity >0.99 with the actual positional signal at position 1

## Methodology

The paper employs a combination of structural analysis and causal interventions on the 124M-parameter GPT-2 model (from Hugging Face).

**Decomposition approach:** The authors expand the attention score formula and isolate a "source-agnostic shift" term: Delta_{j,h}^{(l)} = b_{Q,h}^{(l)} W_{k,h}^{(l)T} x_j^{(l)T}, which raises or lowers attention toward position j identically for every source position i. They show this term is anomalously large for position 1.

**Four empirical analyses:**
1. **Source-agnostic shift magnitude analysis** (Section 3.2.2): Histogram comparison showing position-1 shifts are systematically larger than all other positions across heads and layers.
2. **EPE validation** (Section 3.2.3): Defining EPE_i = MLP^(1)(p_i) + p_i and verifying it captures the net positional signal (cosine similarity >0.99 at position 1).
3. **EPE-bias alignment** (Section 3.2.4): Showing EPE_1 projected through Wk is strongly aligned with bQ (high positive cosine similarity) while other positions are negatively aligned.
4. **Coordinate-level analysis** (Section 3.2.5): Demonstrating that the massive-activation coordinates of EPE_1 (dims 138, 378, 447) coincide exactly with where the bias projection bQ * Wk has highest magnitude.

**Ten causal interventions** (Section 3.2.6): Including nullifying bQ (reduces BOS-attention to 12.8% of baseline), removing first PE (8.9%), swapping EPE (9.8%), swapping raw PE (93.2% -- sink persists, confirming EPE not raw PE matters), nullifying BOS token (100.5% -- sink intact, ruling out token identity), no MLP (23.1%), no PE (19.8%), zeroing top-3 Wk columns (41.9%), and zeroing random Wk columns (97.5% -- control, minimal effect).

**Evaluation:** 300 examples (100 each from SST-2, GSM8K, HumanEval), truncated to 40 tokens, analyzed across layers 4-11.

## Results & Highlights

- The source-agnostic shift at position 1 forms a clearly separated distribution from all other positions, creating a strong prior for attending to position 1 regardless of content.
- EPE_1 has massive activations at exactly 3 coordinates (dims 138, 378, 447) that are each >15 standard deviations from the mean -- these are the same coordinates where bQ * Wk has highest magnitude.
- Nullifying bQ alone reduces the sink to ~13% of baseline, confirming it is a primary driver rather than a minor contributor.
- The sink is driven by positional information, not BOS token identity: nullifying the BOS token embedding leaves the sink at 100.5% of baseline, while replacing the first positional embedding drops it to 8.9%.
- Swapping EPE between positions 1 and 2 nearly eliminates the sink (9.8%), while swapping raw positional embeddings has almost no effect (93.2%), proving downstream layers rely on MLP-processed positional signals.
- The finding that each circuit component is architecture-dispensable has major implications: attention sinks are a convergent phenomenon that optimization reliably produces using whatever architectural components are available.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** This is one of the first papers to provide a complete parameter-level causal circuit for attention sinks in any architecture. The EPE construct and the source-agnostic shift decomposition are genuine conceptual contributions. The dispensability finding -- that the identified components are individually unnecessary at the architecture level -- is a nuanced and important insight.

**Rigor (Strong):** The paper uses ten carefully designed interventions with appropriate controls (e.g., zeroing random Wk columns as a control for zeroing massive-activation columns). The distinction between swapping EPE vs. raw PE cleanly isolates the causal pathway. The use of three diverse input domains (natural language, math, code) strengthens generalizability claims.

**Reproducibility (Good):** Public code repository, standard pretrained GPT-2 checkpoint, clearly defined evaluation protocol (300 examples, 40 tokens). The interventions are described in sufficient detail.

**Significance (Moderate-High):** The architectural dispensability finding is significant because it challenges the hope for a single universal explanation or mitigation for attention sinks. However, the practical impact is somewhat limited by the model scale (124M parameters).

**Red flags / Concerns:**
- The analysis is limited to a single model (GPT-2 124M). The authors acknowledge this but the leap from 124M parameters to modern LLMs is substantial; mechanisms could differ significantly at scale.
- Interventions reduce but do not fully eliminate the sink (e.g., nullifying bQ leaves ~13% residual), indicating secondary contributors that are not characterized.
- The paper is a static, post-hoc analysis. No training dynamics are studied -- it remains unclear when or why the circuit emerges during optimization.
- The "dispensability" argument, while logically sound, is based on citing other papers' observations rather than direct experimental verification within this work.
- The EPE simplification assumes the first layer's MLP is the dominant pathway for positional information, which may not hold in deeper or more complex architectures.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally clean experimental design: the ten interventions form a coherent causal argument with proper controls.
- The source-agnostic shift decomposition is elegant and insightful -- it isolates a mechanism that is both interpretable and testable.
- The finding that attention sinks are architecture-dependent in their implementation but universal as a phenomenon is a genuinely important conceptual contribution.
- Clear, well-organized presentation with strong figures that directly support the claims.
- Honest about limitations; the limitations section is thorough and identifies concrete directions for future work.
- Connects to and extends prior work (Sun et al. 2024 on massive activations, Dial 2025 on early MLP features) rather than ignoring related findings.

## Limitations

- **Single model, single scale:** Only GPT-2 124M is analyzed. The mechanism may differ substantially in larger models, models with different positional encodings (RoPE, ALiBi), or different architectures entirely.
- **No training dynamics:** The analysis is purely post-hoc. We do not know when the circuit forms during pretraining or what gradients drive it.
- **Residual sink:** Interventions reduce the sink substantially but leave a residual (12-42% depending on intervention), indicating uncharacterized secondary mechanisms.
- **Functional role unclear:** The paper explains *how* the sink is implemented but not *why* it emerges or whether it is beneficial or harmful.
- **Dispensability not directly tested:** The claim that each component is dispensable relies on citations to other works rather than experiments within this paper.

## 相关性

本文通过逆向工程方法精确解析了 Transformer 中 attention sink 的产生机制，这对理解 LLM 训练过程中的内部表示稳定性具有重要意义——特别是它揭示了优化过程如何可靠地在不同架构中产生类似的 sink 现象，这为设计更鲁棒的训练策略和更可靠的可解释性方法提供了理论基础。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. At what point during pretraining does the bQ-EPE-Wk circuit co-adapt? Does it emerge early and stabilize, or develop gradually?
2. Do larger models (e.g., GPT-2 XL, LLaMA variants) use the same three-component circuit, or do they construct functionally analogous mechanisms through different architectural pathways?
3. Since attention sinks appear to be a convergent optimization outcome, what loss landscape properties or information-theoretic pressures drive their emergence?
4. Can the source-agnostic shift decomposition be used as a diagnostic tool during training to predict sink formation before it becomes entrenched?
5. Given that mitigations must be architecture-specific, what would a principled framework look like for identifying the active sink circuit in an arbitrary model?
