Zero-Ablation Overstates Register Content Dependence in DINO Vision Transformers

arXiv: 2604.14433

Date: 2026-04-15

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14433

一句话总结： 本文通过三种分布内替换对照实验（均值替换、噪声替换、跨图像寄存器打乱）证明，在DINO系列视觉Transformer中广泛使用的零消融方法严重高估了寄存器token的内容依赖性——寄存器在冻结特征评估中只需"看起来合理"的激活值即可维持性能，而非依赖精确的图像特定内容。
TL;DR: Zero-ablation (replacing activations with zeros) makes register tokens appear indispensable in DINO ViTs (up to -36.6 pp classification, -30.9 pp segmentation drops), but three in-distribution replacement controls (mean-substitution, noise-substitution, cross-image register shuffling) preserve all downstream tasks within ~1 pp, demonstrating that zero-ablation overstates register content dependence due to out-of-distribution distributional shift rather than genuine functional necessity.

核心贡献

  • 揭示零消融的方法论缺陷： 在DINOv2+registers和DINOv3中，零消融导致严重的性能下降（分类-36.6pp，分割-30.9pp），但这是因为零向量是严重的分布外输入，会引起跨层的级联破坏（JS散度比合理替换高29-177倍），而非寄存器确实承载了不可替代的任务信息。
  • 提出三种分布内替换对照方法： 均值替换（数据集层均值）、高斯噪声替换（匹配均值和方差）、跨图像寄存器打乱（保留真实激活结构但打破图像特定关联），三者均能在分类、对应、分割任务上维持原始性能（变化<=1pp）。
  • 证实寄存器的结构性角色而非内容性角色： 寄存器将密集特征从[CLS]依赖中解耦（无寄存器时[CLS]消融导致分割-37.1pp，有寄存器时仅-0.7pp），并压缩patch几何结构（有效秩从13.5降至4.0），但在冻结特征评估中不需要精确的图像特定内容。
  • 跨尺度验证： 所有发现在ViT-B尺度上完全复现，包括零消融不对称性和替换对照结果。
  • 方法论启示： 建议在视觉Transformer的消融研究中，将分布内替换（如均值替换或跨样本打乱）作为零消融的标准补充，以区分真正的功能依赖与分布偏移伪影。

Key Contributions

  • Exposes a critical methodological flaw in zero-ablation: Shows that zero-ablation produces catastrophic register-dependent drops (up to -36.6 pp classification, -30.9 pp segmentation) in DINOv2+reg and DINOv3, but this reflects out-of-distribution distributional shift cascading through layers (JS divergence 29-177x larger than plausible replacements) rather than genuine content dependence.
  • Introduces three in-distribution replacement controls: Mean-substitution (per-layer dataset means), Gaussian noise matched in mean and variance, and cross-image register shuffling all preserve performance within ~1 pp across classification, correspondence, and segmentation tasks, demonstrating that models depend on plausible register-like activations rather than exact image-specific values.
  • Establishes registers as structural rather than content-dependent channels: Registers buffer dense features from [CLS] dependence (37.1 pp segmentation drop without registers vs. <1 pp with) and compress patch geometry (effective rank 13.5 to 4.0), but exact per-image register content is not uniquely required in frozen-feature evaluations.
  • Validates across model scales: All findings, including the replacement-control results, replicate at ViT-B scale with consistent ablation deltas and Gram compression patterns (3.0-3.4x compression).
  • Provides methodological guidance for future ablation studies: Recommends mean-substitution or cross-image shuffling as standard complements to zero-ablation in vision transformer interpretability research.

Methodology

The authors compare three DINO-family ViT models at two scales (ViT-S/21M and ViT-B/86M): DINOv2 (no registers), DINOv2+registers (4 register tokens), and DINOv3 (4 registers with Gram-anchored distillation). Features are extracted from the final transformer block output (layer 11 of 12) at 224x224 resolution.

Intervention framework: PyTorch forward hooks replace token activations (CLS or registers) after every block output starting from block 1. Four interventions are compared against the unmodified baseline: (1) zero-ablation (zero vectors), (2) mean-substitution (per-layer dataset means from 5,000 ImageNet images), (3) noise-substitution (Gaussian noise matched in mean and variance), and (4) register shuffling (permuting register activations across images within each batch at each layer).

Evaluation tasks span global and dense features: Classification (linear probe on 50K ImageNet subset, 1K classes), retrieval (kNN recall on 2K images), correspondence (nearest-neighbor patch matching on 2K synthetic pairs and SPair-71k with 12,234 real pairs), and segmentation (per-patch linear probe on Pascal VOC 2012, 21 classes). Bootstrap 95% confidence intervals and paired permutation tests (10,000 permutations) quantify uncertainty.

Diagnostic analyses include: Per-patch cosine similarity to quantify perturbation magnitude, Jensen-Shannon divergence of attention patterns across layers, effective rank of patch Gram matrices to measure geometric compression, PCA projections of patch features, and attention flow analysis across all 12 transformer layers.

Results & Highlights

  • Zero-ablation creates a false impression of indispensability: Register zeroing in DINOv3 causes -36.6 pp classification and -30.9 pp segmentation drops, but all three replacement controls preserve performance within ~1 pp.
  • Replacements genuinely perturb representations: Per-patch cosine similarity to the full condition is 0.95-0.96 (DINOv2+reg) and 0.998-0.999 (DINOv3) under replacements, confirming features are altered -- yet task performance is maintained.
  • Zeroing causes disproportionate perturbation: Cosine similarity drops to 0.58-0.60 under zeroing, and last-layer JS divergence is 0.177 (DINOv3) vs. 0.001 for mean-substitution -- a 177x gap.
  • Registers buffer dense features from CLS: Without registers, CLS zeroing causes -37.1 pp segmentation drop; with registers, only -0.7 pp (>50x difference, p<0.001).
  • Patch geometry compression: Effective rank drops from 13.5 (DINOv2) to 8.7 (DINOv2+reg) to 4.0 (DINOv3), with DINOv3 already compressed at layer 6 (rank 6.4 vs. 32.3 for DINOv2).
  • Attention-functional dissociation: CLS-to-register attention builds gradually from mid-layers (~20% by layer 6), but classification dependence on registers emerges abruptly only at layers 10-11.
  • CLS content IS image-specific (negative control): Mean-substituting CLS yields 0.1% classification (same as zeroing), confirming the insensitivity to replacement is specific to registers.
  • SPair-71k real-image validation: The CLS/register asymmetry replicates on 12,234 real image pairs (CLS zeroing: -22.9 pp without registers vs. -0.5/+0.4 pp with; register zeroing: -9.5/-18.8 pp).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-High. The core insight -- that zero-ablation overstates component importance due to distributional shift -- is established in NLP interpretability literature (Heimersheim & Nanda 2024; Zhang & Nanda 2024; Li & Janson 2024), but applying this rigorously to register tokens in vision transformers and demonstrating it with three complementary controls is a meaningful and timely contribution. The connection to neuroscience lesion studies is elegant.
  • Rigor: High. The paper uses three independent replacement controls, multiple evaluation tasks, two model scales, comprehensive statistical testing (bootstrap CIs, permutation tests), and appropriate negative controls (random patch zeroing, CLS mean-substitution). The per-patch cosine similarity analysis convincingly rules out the trivial explanation that replacements leave features unchanged.
  • Reproducibility: High. All models are publicly available (HuggingFace DINOv2, torch.hub DINOv3), experimental details are thoroughly specified, and compute requirements are modest (single GPU, ~15 hours).
  • Significance: Moderate. The finding that register content is not uniquely required in frozen-feature evaluations is important for understanding ViT mechanics, but the authors honestly acknowledge the scope limitation -- the conclusion may not hold under fine-tuning, few-shot adaptation, or generation tasks. The structural role of registers (CLS buffering, patch compression) is real regardless.
  • Red flags: The scope is explicitly limited to frozen-feature evaluations, which somewhat tempers the practical impact. The between-model comparisons (DINOv2 vs. DINOv2+reg vs. DINOv3) are correlational -- DINOv3 differs in patch size, RoPE, and distillation recipe, so the additional compression cannot be attributed solely to Gram anchoring. Register shuffling depends on batch composition, which adds a source of variability. The per-register specialization analysis is presented as exploratory, and the authors acknowledge it reflects distributional sensitivity rather than functional dependence.

Strengths

  • Excellent experimental design with three complementary in-distribution controls plus appropriate negative controls.
  • Clear, honest scoping of conclusions ("in the frozen-feature evaluations we test" appears throughout).
  • Strong statistical rigor with bootstrap CIs, permutation tests, and multiple seeds.
  • Compelling neuroscience analogy that frames the problem accessibly.
  • Thorough scale validation (ViT-S and ViT-B) showing consistency.
  • The PCA projections and attention flow visualizations effectively communicate the mechanistic story.

Limitations

  • Scope limited to frozen-feature evaluations: Whether registers carry task-relevant content under fine-tuning, few-shot adaptation, or generation remains entirely open.
  • All-layer intervention confound: The study replaces register activations at every layer starting from block 1; single-layer or late-layer-only ablations may reveal layer-specific dependencies not captured here.
  • Correlational between-model comparisons: Cannot disentangle Gram anchoring from co-occurring architectural changes (patch size, RoPE, distillation recipe) in DINOv3.
  • Register shuffling batch dependence: The effective perturbation depends on batch composition, introducing variability.
  • Limited architectural generality: Only tested on DINO-family ViTs; whether the finding extends to other architectures (DeiT, MAE, Swin) is unknown.
  • No investigation of training dynamics: The study probes trained models but does not examine whether register content becomes important during the training process itself.

Relevance

本文对LLM/ML训练稳定性与可解释性研究具有直接的方法论启示：在用消融实验探测模型组件功能时，零消融可能因引入分布外输入而严重高估组件的重要性，这一方法论陷阱不仅存在于视觉Transformer，也普遍存在于语言模型的可解释性研究中。研究强调了采用分布内替换对照对于可靠因果推断的必要性。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do these findings generalize to register tokens in multimodal models (e.g., vision-language models like LLaVA or SigLIP that use similar architectural patterns)?
  • How do register content dynamics change during fine-tuning -- does fine-tuning on downstream tasks create genuine content dependence that is absent in frozen-feature evaluations?
  • Can the distributional-shift diagnostic (JS divergence between full and ablated attention patterns) be developed into a general-purpose metric for detecting unreliable ablation results in other architectures?
  • What happens with late-layer-only register replacement -- is the structural role of registers concentrated in specific layers, or is the all-layer trajectory genuinely necessary?
  • Does the finding that "plausible activations suffice" imply that one could pre-compute a fixed set of register activations and use them universally, potentially enabling register-free inference with pre-computed embeddings?
