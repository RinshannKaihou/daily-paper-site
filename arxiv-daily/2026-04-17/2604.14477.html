Seeing Through Circuits: Faithful Mechanistic Interpretability for Vision Transformers

arXiv: 2604.14477

Date: 2026-04-15

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14477

一句话总结： 本文提出 Vi-CD 方法，首次在 Vision Transformer 中实现了基于边的机制电路发现，能够以仅 10% 的边保留率恢复接近完美的分类性能，并通过对发现的电路进行机制化引导（steering），将 CLIP 模型的排版攻击成功率降低 90% 以上，同时几乎不影响干净图像上的准确率。
TL;DR: Vi-CD introduces edge-based mechanistic circuit discovery to Vision Transformers for the first time, recovering faithful class-specific subgraphs that are 10x sparser than baselines while preserving near-perfect accuracy, and enabling circuit-based steering that reduces typographic attack success rates by over 90% with minimal clean accuracy loss.

核心贡献

  • 首次将边级电路发现引入视觉模型：在 Vision Transformer 中建立基于残差流连接的有向计算图，通过边级激活修补（activation patching）发现稀疏、忠实（faithful）的类特定电路，弥补了此前视觉领域仅有神经元级电路的空白。
  • Vi-CD 方法在大规模模型上验证有效：在 ViT-B 和 OpenCLIP ViT-B/32 上，Vi-CD 发现的电路比最强基线 EAP-IG 稀疏约 10 倍，同时保持高准确率，展示了方法的可扩展性。
  • 电路具有语义结构和组合性：语义相近的类（如不同犬种）共享更多电路边；类电路的并集可在零样本下实现二分类，表明模型计算具有可组合的组织结构。
  • 电路引导（mechanistic steering）实现可操作的模型行为修正：通过去激活发现的攻击电路，将 CLIP 的排版攻击（typographic attack）成功率从 ~39% 降至 ~2%，并在 RoCOCO 基准上将安全隐患 RSMS 减半，同时保持正常检索性能。

Key Contributions

  • First edge-based circuit discovery for Vision Transformers: Formalizes ViTs as directed computation graphs over residual-stream connections and discovers sparse, faithful class-specific subgraphs via sequential activation patching with corrupted inpainted inputs.
  • Vi-CD outperforms all baselines by a wide margin: On both supervised ViT-B and OpenCLIP, Vi-CD achieves near-perfect faithfulness with fewer than 10% of edges, approximately 10x sparser than the strongest baseline EAP-IG.
  • Semantically meaningful and composable circuits: Discovered circuits reflect class similarity (e.g., dog breeds share more edges than dog-vs-weapon), and unions of class circuits support zero-shot binary classification without retraining.
  • Actionable mechanistic steering for safety: Deactivating discovered typographic-attack circuits reduces attack success rates by over 90% (e.g., Top-1 ASR from 39.1% to 2.8%) and halves safety violations on RoCOCO, with minimal impact on clean accuracy.
  • Graph reduction strategy for tractability: Introduces attention-input nodes that aggregate residual inputs to attention blocks, reducing candidate edges by a factor of H (number of heads) without modifying model functionality.

Methodology

Vi-CD (Visual Circuit Discovery) formalizes a Vision Transformer as a directed computation graph where nodes are model components (attention heads, MLP blocks, residual input nodes) and edges are residual-stream connections. The pipeline consists of three key stages:

  1. Clean-corrupted input construction: For each target class, clean images containing the class object are paired with corrupted counterparts where the foreground object is removed via segmentation and inpainting (using the ForAug dataset). This removes class-specific evidence while preserving background statistics, avoiding out-of-distribution artifacts.
  1. Sequential activation patching with graph reduction: Rather than exhaustive per-edge intervention over O(L^2 * H^2) edges, Vi-CD introduces attention-input nodes that aggregate residual inputs, reducing the edge space by a factor of H. Edges are iteratively pruned using the target logit difference as the selection criterion: edges whose removal causes minimal performance degradation are removed, and the model state is updated after each pruning step.
  1. Circuit-based steering: For downstream applications, corruption-aligned directions are estimated per circuit component as the mean normalized activation difference between clean and corrupted inputs. During inference, directional ablation is applied: for each patch, the projection onto the corruption direction is computed, and only positively aligned projections are removed via ReLU-gated subtraction, controlled by steering strength alpha.

The method is evaluated on three task types: class circuits (per-ImageNet-class subgraphs), typographic attack circuits (mechanisms by which text overlays alter CLIP predictions), and binary decision circuits (discriminating between class pairs).

Results & Highlights

  • Faithfulness and sparsity: Vi-CD recovers near-perfect classification accuracy on ViT-B with fewer than 10% of edges retained. On OpenCLIP, it similarly achieves high faithfulness at extreme sparsity. EAP performs close to random pruning; EAP-IG requires substantially more edges for comparable performance.
  • Typographic attack defense: Across three attack regimes (big text, small text, bezel), circuit steering reduces Top-1 ASR from ~39% to ~2-3% (over 90% reduction) with only ~1-2% clean accuracy loss. The effect is specific to discovered circuits; random edge steering does not achieve comparable ASR suppression.
  • RoCOCO safety benchmark: Weapon-circuit steering at alpha=0.4 reduces RSMS from 11.68% to ~5% (more than halved), while retrieval metrics (R@1, R@5, R@10) are maintained or slightly improved.
  • Circuit stability: Across 20 repeated runs per class, 34.5% of edges appear in 90-100% of runs. Circuit stability increases monotonically with dataset size. Attention-head-originating edges exhibit lower stability than MLP-originating edges.
  • Cross-class similarity: Jaccard similarity analysis shows semantically related classes (e.g., Pembroke Corgi and Cardigan Corgi, Revolver and Rifle) share substantially more circuit edges than unrelated classes, revealing organized computational pathways.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. This is the first work to bring edge-based circuit discovery from LLMs to Vision Transformers. The corruption strategy (segmentation + inpainting) is a creative adaptation of the clean-corrupted paradigm for continuous visual inputs, distinct from the discrete token corruption used in NLP.
  • Rigor: Strong. The paper includes extensive baselines (EAP, EAP-IG with multiple step counts, random pruning), ablation studies (selection criterion: KL divergence vs. logit difference; steering normalization: pre- vs. post-normed; aggregation: mean vs. medoid), and stability analyses across 20 seeds per class. The comparison against random circuits of matched size in steering experiments is a particularly strong control.
  • Reproducibility: Moderate to good. The ForAug dataset is public, models (ViT-B, OpenCLIP) are standard, and the methodology is described in sufficient detail. However, some per-class pruning thresholds are hand-tuned (see Appendix Table 4), and the code is not explicitly linked.
  • Significance: High. The demonstration that edge-based circuits are actionable (not just descriptive) through steering -- reducing attack success rates by over 90% -- elevates mechanistic interpretability from an analytical tool to a practical safety mechanism. The finding that semantically similar classes share computational pathways also contributes to our understanding of how ViTs organize representations.
  • Red flags: (1) Computational cost is acknowledged as a limitation -- Vi-CD requires patching each edge at least once, making scaling to very large transformers an open challenge. (2) Per-class threshold tuning could limit generalization to arbitrary classes or tasks. (3) The circuit steering relies on the inpainting corruption being well-matched to the task; it is unclear how well the approach transfers to corruption strategies that do not admit clean foreground-background separation.

Strengths

  • First principled bridge between LLM-style edge-based circuit discovery and vision architectures.
  • Strong empirical results: 10x sparser circuits, over 90% attack reduction, minimal accuracy loss.
  • Thorough evaluation with multiple baselines, ablations, and stability analyses.
  • Demonstrates practical utility through mechanistic steering for model safety.
  • Clean formulation connecting faithfulness, sparsity, and the computation graph abstraction.
  • Cross-class similarity analysis provides genuine insight into ViT internal organization.

Limitations

  • Computational cost of sequential activation patching limits scalability to very large models (e.g., ViT-L or ViT-H).
  • Reliance on segmentation-and-inpainting corruption restricts applicability to tasks where foreground objects can be cleanly separated.
  • Per-class pruning thresholds require tuning; no universal threshold is identified.
  • Circuit instability across runs (Jaccard similarity of 0.6-0.8) raises questions about whether a single "ground truth" circuit exists for each behavior.
  • Evaluation is limited to classification and retrieval; extension to dense prediction tasks (detection, segmentation) is unexplored.
  • Only tested on ViT-B scale models; transfer to larger or convolutional architectures is not demonstrated.

Relevance

本文与 LLM 训练稳定性和可解释性研究高度相关：Vi-CD 首次在视觉模型中建立了基于边的机制电路发现框架，其核心思想——通过计算图的稀疏子图忠实解释模型行为——可直接启发 LLM 训练过程中的电路稳定性和可解释性分析。此外，电路引导（steering）技术展示了如何在不重新训练的情况下精确修正模型的有害行为，为提升 ML 系统可靠性和安全性提供了实用路径。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can Vi-CD be extended to multi-modal models (e.g., LLaVA, GPT-4V) to discover joint vision-language circuits that govern cross-modal reasoning?
  • How do discovered circuits change during training -- do circuits emerge early and stabilize, or do they undergo significant restructuring, and does this correlate with training stability?
  • Can the inpainting-based corruption strategy be replaced with a learned corruption model to handle tasks where foreground-background separation is infeasible?
  • What is the relationship between circuit structure and adversarial robustness under non-typographic attacks (e.g., pixel-level adversarial examples)?
  • Could circuit discovery be used as a diagnostic tool during fine-tuning to detect when a model is learning undesirable shortcut circuits?
