> **一句话总结：** 本文提出二维评估指标 PASS@(k,T)，联合变化采样预算 k 和交互深度 T，发现在需要组合式序列信息收集的 agentic 工具使用任务上，RL（GRPO）确实能扩展 LLM 智能体的能力边界（gap 随 k 增大而扩大），而非仅仅提高效率；而匹配训练数据的 SFT 反而会在同类任务上收缩能力边界。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces PASS@(k,T), a two-dimensional metric that jointly varies sampling budget k and interaction depth T, and shows that tool-use RL (GRPO) genuinely expands the LLM agent's capability boundary on compositional multi-hop tasks (the RL-vs-base gap widens at large k), whereas matched-data SFT regresses it -- reconciling optimistic and pessimistic views of RL for LLMs.

## 核心贡献

- 提出首个二维 agentic 评估框架 PASS@(k,T)，联合变化采样预算 k 和交互深度 T，能够区分能力扩展（capability expansion）与效率提升（efficiency improvement）。
- 首次实证证明：在需要组合式序列信息收集的 agentic 工具使用任务上，RL（GRPO）确实能扩展 LLM 智能体的能力边界，RL agent 的 pass-curve 在大 k 处持续高于 base model，且 gap 随 k 增大而扩大（与静态推理中 RLVR 的收敛结论相反）。
- 匹配训练数据的对照实验表明：相同 200 题上训练的 SFT 反而在组合式任务上收缩了能力边界（9:1 的 RL-vs-SFT 不对称性），证明学习信号（自主探索 vs 专家模仿）而非数据暴露是能力扩展的关键因素。
- 机制分析（困惑度分解、策略多样性测量、跨策略交换实验）揭示 RL 通过重新加权 base model 的策略分布实现能力提升，改进集中在 agent 如何整合检索信息，而非搜索查询本身。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces PASS@(k,T), the first two-dimensional agentic evaluation metric that jointly varies sampling budget k and interaction-depth budget T, cleanly separating capability expansion from efficiency improvement.
- Provides the first empirical demonstration that tool-use RL (GRPO) genuinely enlarges the LLM agent's capability boundary on compositional multi-hop tasks: the RL agent's pass-curve pulls above the base model's and the gap widens at large k, the opposite of the convergence pattern reported in static-reasoning RLVR literature.
- Shows that matched-data SFT regresses the capability boundary on the same compositional tasks (a 9:1 RL-vs-SFT asymmetry under identical 200-problem training data), isolating self-directed exploration as the causal factor rather than data exposure.
- Delivers a mechanistic account via perplexity decomposition, strategy-diversity measurement, and cross-policy swapping: RL reweights the base model's existing strategy distribution toward higher-reasoning-quality subsets, with improvement concentrated on information integration rather than search query formulation.

## Methodology

**PASS@(k,T) Framework.** The authors formalize agent-environment interaction as a policy pi executing up to T rounds of tool calls. PASS@(k,T) measures the probability that at least one of k independent trajectories (each with at most T interaction rounds) produces a correct answer. The k-axis probes sampling reliability (can the agent solve it if given more attempts?), while the T-axis probes interaction capability (does deeper environment engagement unlock new solutions?). At the boundary k->n, PASS@(k,T) becomes an indicator of whether pi can ever solve problem q at depth T (capability boundary). At T=0, it reduces to standard static pass@k.

**Formal Definitions.** Capability boundary is the set of problems solvable at least once under unlimited re-sampling at depth T. Capability expansion occurs when the RL agent's boundary strictly exceeds the base model's. Efficiency improvement occurs when the RL agent solves shared problems more reliably at pass@1.

**Experimental Setup.** Three task categories with 100 test problems each: (A) MATH-500 with no tool access (negative control), (B) HotPotQA comparison questions (two independent retrievals), (C) HotPotQA bridge questions (two compositionally dependent retrievals, where the second query's argument depends on the first query's result). The agent uses a single search(query) tool over BM25 retrieval from the HotPotQA distractor corpus, following a ReAct loop.

**Matched-Data Comparison.** All models start from Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct. pi_SFT is LoRA-fine-tuned on 200 expert trajectories. pi_RL is trained with GRPO on the same 200 problems with binary exact-match reward (G=8, 10 epochs, 1000 steps). An exploration-bonus variant pi_RL+explore is also trained. The critical design choice: identical training problems across SFT and RL, so any capability boundary asymmetry must come from the learning signal.

**Evaluation Grid.** For each model, problem, and T in {0,1,2,3,5}, n=64 i.i.d. trajectories are drawn at temperature 0.7. PASS@(k,T) is computed on k in {1,2,4,8,16,32,64} via the unbiased hypergeometric estimator.

**Mechanism Analysis.** Three-pronged approach: (1) Perplexity decomposition -- computing the base model's per-token surprisal on RL trajectories, split into search-query tokens vs. reasoning tokens; (2) Strategy-diversity measurement -- counting unique search-query sequences per problem across 64 samples; (3) Cross-policy swapping -- extracting (query, observation) pairs from one policy's successful trajectories and feeding them to another policy for final-answer generation.

## Results & Highlights

- **Category A (MATH-500, no tool):** RL is inert -- |B_RL| = |B_base| = 84, confirming tool-use RL does not transfer to parametric reasoning.
- **Category B (comparison questions):** Modest expansion -- RL adds 5 new problems, loses 1 (net +4); SFT similarly adds 5, loses 2 (net +3). Both training signals find the simple two-retrieval strategy.
- **Category C (bridge questions -- the key finding):** RL expands the boundary by 5 problems with only 1 regression (net +4, reaching |B_RL|=81). SFT contracts the boundary: 7 regressions, 3 gains (net -4, |B_SFT|=73). The RL-vs-SFT asymmetry is 9:1 under identical training data.
- **Diverging pass-curves on Category C:** At k=1, pi_base is slightly ahead (0.363 vs. 0.335). The curves cross near k=4, and the gap widens to +4pp at k=64 (PASS@(64,5) = 0.81 for RL vs. 0.77 for base vs. 0.73 for SFT). This is the opposite of the convergence pattern in static RLVR.
- **Mechanism findings:** PPL_search = 2.07 vs. PPL_reason = 3.08 (50% gap) -- RL's novelty is concentrated on reasoning over retrieved paragraphs, not on search queries. pi_SFT collapses strategy diversity to 14.7 unique sequences per problem (3x reduction vs. base's 40.1), while pi_RL preserves and slightly extends it (45.5). RL reweights the base distribution; SFT replaces it.
- **Interaction saturation:** Both base and RL saturate at T*=2 on Category C; RL's advantage is a higher plateau (0.81 vs. 0.77), not a longer interaction horizon.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The PASS@(k,T) framework is a genuine conceptual advance over one-dimensional pass@k. The finding that agentic RL genuinely expands capability boundaries on compositional tasks -- while the static-reasoning literature found the opposite -- is novel and important. The matched-data SFT vs. RL comparison is a clean experimental contribution.
- **Rigor (High):** The experimental design is well-controlled. Using identical training data for SFT and RL, with a negative control (Category A) and graded task complexity (B vs. C), provides strong causal evidence. Bootstrap confidence intervals and sampling-sufficiency checks are included. Formal properties of PASS@(k,T) (monotonicity, reduction to static pass@k) are proved.
- **Reproducibility (Good):** Code is public. All hyperparameters are specified. Training details (LoRA rank 16, 8-bit Adam, GRPO with G=8, etc.) are fully described. The BM25 retrieval setup is standard and deterministic. The main barrier is compute (~55 hours on a single 48GB GPU for RL training, ~8 GPU-hours for evaluation).
- **Significance (High):** This paper directly engages with a central debate in the LLM RL community: does RL teach new capabilities or just improve reliability? The answer -- "both, depending on task type" -- is nuanced and has practical implications for training method selection.
- **Red flags / Concerns:**
  - Only one base model (Qwen2.5-7B) and one RL method (GRPO) are tested. Generalization to larger models or other RL algorithms (PPO, RLOO) is unverified.
  - The RL-over-base advantage on Category C is +4pp at PASS@(64,5), which is meaningful but modest in absolute terms. The 5:1 problem-level asymmetry involves small absolute counts (5 new vs. 1 lost out of 100).
  - The exploration-bonus ablation was truncated at epoch 8/10, so that comparison is incomplete.
  - No temperature sweep was conducted, which matters because RL may mechanically reduce output entropy.
  - The BM25 retrieval over only 10 paragraphs is far from realistic web-scale tool use.

## Strengths

- Clean, principled formalization of PASS@(k,T) with well-defined structural properties.
- Rigorous matched-data experimental design that isolates learning signal from data exposure.
- The three-category task structure (A/B/C) provides natural controls and graded complexity.
- The mechanistic analysis (perplexity decomposition, diversity measurement, cross-policy swapping) offers a concrete account of *how* RL improves agents, beyond just showing *that* it does.
- Transparent about limitations and honest about what the evidence can and cannot establish.
- Reconciles two seemingly contradictory positions in the literature with a task-dependent explanation.

## Limitations

- Single base model (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct), single RL algorithm (GRPO), single tool (BM25 search), and small training budget (200 problems).
- The BM25 retrieval environment is highly constrained (10-paragraph corpus), which limits external validity to real-world agentic settings.
- No evaluation at T > T_train (=5), no temperature sweep, and no scale-up experiments (14B models).
- The absolute magnitude of capability expansion is modest (+4pp on the most favorable category).
- The exploration-bonus variant was not run to completion.
- The pass-curve divergence pattern could potentially be sensitive to the choice of temperature and sampling budget; this is acknowledged but not tested.

## Relevance

本文直接回应了 LLM 训练中的核心问题：RL 是否能扩展模型能力边界。通过提出 PASS@(k,T) 框架，它为评估 agentic RL 的可靠性提供了新的方法论工具，对理解 RL 训练稳定性（不同任务类型下 RL 效果的差异）和 ML 系统可靠性（SFT 反而会收缩能力边界这一发现）具有重要启示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the capability-expansion finding hold for larger models (70B+, frontier-class) and more complex tool environments (web navigation, multi-tool orchestration)?
- How does the RL-vs-SFT comparison change when the training data规模 is scaled from 200 to 10,000+ problems?
- Would the diverging pass-curve pattern persist across a temperature sweep, or is it partially an artifact of the fixed temperature=0.7?
- Can the "reweighting" mechanism explanation be verified at the attention-head or circuit level, rather than at the trajectory-statistics level?
- How does PASS@(k,T) apply to multi-modal agents or agents with code-execution tools, where the action space is qualitatively different from text retrieval?
- Does the SFT-induced capability regression on compositional tasks generalize beyond HotPotQA to other multi-hop reasoning benchmarks?
