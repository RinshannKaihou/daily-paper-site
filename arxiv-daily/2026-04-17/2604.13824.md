> **一句话总结：** 本文提出行为一致性奖励（BehR）作为文本世界模型的新训练信号，通过冻结参考Agent对比预测状态与真实状态下的动作似然差异来优化GRPO训练，在WebShop上将成对一致性比率（CR_pw）从0.76提升至0.84，同时将弱Agent的离线评估假阳性率从42.5%降至9.5%。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes Behavior Consistency Reward (BehR), a step-level training signal for text-based world models that measures whether predicted states preserve the agent's next-action likelihood under a frozen Reference Agent, trained with GRPO. BehR-WM improves Pairwise Consistency Ratio (CR_pw) from 0.76 to 0.84 on WebShop and reduces false positive rates for weak agents from 42.5% to 9.5% on TextWorld.

## 核心贡献

- **提出行为一致性训练范式**：将文本世界模型的优化目标从传统的"状态文本相似度"转变为"功能一致性"，即预测状态应诱导与真实状态相同的动作分布。
- **设计行为一致性奖励（BehR）**：利用冻结参考Agent计算已记录的下一步动作在预测状态与真实状态下的似然变化，作为可优化的密集训练信号，是唯一能正确区分决策关键扰动与无关扰动的指标。
- **引入成对一致性比率（CR_pw）**：作为任务级评估标准，衡量在真实环境中成功的任务在WM生成动作回放后仍能成功的比例。
- **下游应用验证**：BehR-WM在离线代理评估中显著降低了弱Agent的假阳性率（42.5%降至9.5%），并在推理时前瞻规划中取得最高的点估计值。

## Key Contributions

- **Behavior-consistent training paradigm**: Reframes world model learning from state-level text reconstruction (EM, BERTScore) to functional consistency -- the predicted state should induce the same action distribution as the real state.
- **Behavior Consistency Reward (BehR)**: A tractable step-level proxy that measures how much the likelihood of a logged next action changes between the real state and the WM-predicted state under a frozen Reference Agent, usable as a dense RL training signal.
- **Pairwise Consistency Ratio (CR_pw)**: A task-level evaluation metric that measures the fraction of individually Real-successful tasks that remain successful under WM-to-Real replay, adopted as the primary evaluation criterion.
- **Metric inversion diagnosis**: Demonstrates that surface-level metrics (BERTScore, Token F1, EM) and LLM-as-judge metrics all incorrectly rank decision-critical perturbations as less severe than irrelevant ones, while BehR correctly ranks them.
- **Downstream applications**: BehR-WM reduces false positive rates for weak agents by 3-5x in surrogate evaluation and achieves the highest point estimate in all tested lookahead planning configurations on WebShop.

## Methodology

### Problem Formulation

The authors formalize the agent-environment interaction where an agent policy $\pi$ maps interaction history to actions, and a world model $\hat{\mathcal{E}}$ serves as a surrogate for the real environment $\mathcal{E}$, predicting the next textual observation and task-completion signal given a history and action.

### Functional Consistency Objective

The ideal objective is functional consistency: the predicted state should induce the same action distribution as the real state. Since the full action distribution is typically inaccessible, the authors instantiate this as a practical training objective using logged actions.

### Behavior Consistency Reward (BehR)

BehR is defined as a step-level proxy that uses a frozen Reference Agent (Qwen3-8B) to compare the likelihood of the logged next action under two conditions:
1. The real observed state $s_t$
2. The world-model-predicted state $\hat{s}_t$

The reward captures whether the predicted state preserves the agent's decision boundary. Unlike surface metrics, BehR directly measures decision preservation rather than text similarity.

### GRPO Optimization

The world model is trained using Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO; Shao et al., 2024) with BehR as the reward signal. The training pipeline:
1. Start from a Word2World (W2W) SFT checkpoint (Qwen2.5-7B or LLaMA3.1-8B)
2. Post-train with GRPO on a difficulty-filtered subset of the original training corpus
3. Training data consists of step-level tuples: (history, action, next state, next expert action), yielding 4,321 tuples for WebShop and 6,000 for TextWorld

### Evaluation Protocol

Three evaluation pipelines are used:
- **Real**: Agent acts in the real environment
- **WM**: Agent acts in the world-model environment
- **W2R**: Actions generated in WM are replayed in the real environment
- Primary metric: **CR_pw** (Pairwise Consistency Ratio) -- fraction of Real-successful tasks that remain successful under W2R replay
- Complementary metric: **CR** (aggregate ratio SR_W2R / SR_Real) for calibration

## Results & Highlights

### Metric Inversion (Table 1)
BehR is the **only metric** that correctly ranks Drop Irrelevant (DI=0.763) above Drop Target (DT=0.100), while all surface metrics (BERTScore: DI=0.859, DT=0.974; Token F1: DI=0.803, DT=0.961) and GPT-4o as judge (DI=0.466, DT=0.824) exhibit metric inversion.

### Task-Level Consistency (Table 3)
- On WebShop with Qwen2.5-7B backbone and GPT-4o agent: CR_pw improves from 0.76 (W2W-SFT) to **0.84** (BehR-WM), while EM stays nearly identical (79.05% to 79.19%)
- BehR-WM improves CR_pw in most of the 16 reported agent-domain-backbone configurations
- Clearest gains appear in WebShop; TextWorld has near-ceiling regimes where BehR mainly preserves already-high consistency

### Optimization Target Ablation (Table 4)
Comparing GRPO runs with identical hyperparameters but different rewards:
| Target | EM | CR | CR_pw |
|--------|------|------|-------|
| W2W (SFT) | 79.05% | 0.92 | 0.76 |
| F1 Reward | 70.06% | 0.97 | 0.763 |
| FactR (Structured) | 78.96% | 0.95 | 0.737 |
| **BehR** | **79.19%** | **1.05** | **0.84** |

### Calibrated Surrogate Evaluation (Table 5)
On TextWorld, BehR-WM reduces false positives for weak agents:
- Qwen3-0.6B: FP reduced from 42.5% to **9.5%** (agreement: 57.5% to 90.5%)
- Qwen3-1.7B: FP reduced from 34.5% to **17.0%** (agreement: 65.0% to 82.0%)

### Lookahead Planning (Table 6)
On WebShop with K=5 lookahead, BehR-WM achieves the highest point estimate in all agent pairs:
- Qwen3-8B: +11.5pp over base (vs. +9.0pp for W2W-WM)
- Qwen3-32B: +4.5pp over base (vs. +2.5pp for W2W-WM)
- GPT-4o: +7.5pp over base (vs. +7.0pp for W2W-WM)

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High)**: The shift from state-level text similarity to behavior-level functional consistency is a genuine conceptual contribution. The metric inversion diagnosis is particularly compelling -- showing that all existing metrics (including GPT-4o as judge) fail on decision-critical perturbations is a strong motivator.

**Rigor (Moderate-High)**: The paper includes well-designed ablations (optimization target comparison, cross-architecture stress test with LLaMA3.1-8B, multi-agent evaluation). However, the optimization-target ablation covers only one domain, one backbone, one evaluation agent, and two alternative rewards -- a narrow scope. The 200-task evaluation set is modest, and the authors acknowledge differences of only a few tasks should be interpreted cautiously.

**Reproducibility (High)**: Code is released, training data construction is described, evaluation protocols are clearly specified, and deterministic decoding (T=0) is used throughout.

**Significance (Moderate-High)**: The work addresses a real and underappreciated problem (metric inversion in world model evaluation). The practical impact is clearest for offline agent evaluation calibration. However, gains in some settings (TextWorld, strong agents) are marginal, and the planning results are explicitly described as preliminary.

**Red flags**:
- BehR is mediated by a single frozen judge model (Qwen3-8B); cross-family judge ablation is deferred to future work
- The Reference Agent is frozen rather than updated during training, which creates a dependency on the quality of logged actions
- Training data sizes are relatively small (4,321 and 6,000 tuples)
- Run-to-run variance estimates are not reported; only 95% Wilson score confidence intervals are provided in the appendix

## Strengths

- Cleanly identifies and formalizes the metric inversion problem in text-based world models
- BehR is the only metric tested that correctly distinguishes decision-critical from irrelevant perturbations
- The optimization-target ablation convincingly shows that the choice of reward matters, not just RL fine-tuning
- Significant reduction in false positive rates for weak agents (3-5x improvement) makes a practical case for better-calibrated surrogate evaluation
- Honest about limitations: explicitly labels planning results as "preliminary" and acknowledges near-ceiling issues

## Limitations

- BehR depends on a single frozen Reference Agent (Qwen3-8B); it does not capture the downstream agent's full action distribution, only the logged next action
- The optimization-target ablation is narrow: one domain (WebShop), one backbone (Qwen2.5-7B), one evaluation agent (GPT-4o)
- Near-ceiling regimes (e.g., TextWorld with strong agents) show little room for improvement
- Evaluation is limited to 200 held-out tasks per domain, and run-to-run variance is not reported
- The method has not been tested in truly open-ended or multi-agent environments
- The relationship between BehR (step-level) and CR_pw (task-level) is established empirically but not theoretically

## Relevance

本文对LLM训练可靠性有直接启示：揭示了传统表面指标（EM、BERTScore）在评估模型输出质量时可能出现"指标倒置"问题，即指标值高但实际决策质量差。BehR从行为层面衡量模型输出的功能正确性，为构建更可靠的LLM评估框架提供了新思路，特别是在需要模型作为模拟器或评估器的场景中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can BehR be extended to open-ended generation tasks beyond structured environments, where there is no discrete "logged next action"?
2. How does the choice of Reference Agent architecture and size affect BehR's effectiveness? Would a stronger or ensemble reference agent yield better calibration?
3. Could BehR be incorporated as an auxiliary reward during general LLM RLHF training to improve output functional quality rather than just text quality?
4. What is the theoretical relationship between step-level BehR and task-level CR_pw? Under what conditions does optimizing the former guarantee improvements in the latter?
5. How would BehR-WM perform in multi-agent or competitive environments where the action distribution is adversarial?
