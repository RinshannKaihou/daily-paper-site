> **一句话总结：** 本文提出基于表征工程（RepE）的认知逆向工程框架，通过子空间正交化和因果干预，首次在白盒层面解析了 LLM 内部编码社会比较嫉妒情绪的线性组合结构，发现模型的内部表征与人类心理学理论高度一致——"优越性"作为触发开关、"领域相关性"作为强度放大器——并展示了机械式检测和抑制有毒情绪状态的可行性。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces a Cognitive Reverse-Engineering framework extending Representation Engineering to decompose social-comparison jealousy in LLMs into two psychological antecedents (Superiority and Domain Relevance). Across 8 models from 3 families, the authors show that LLMs natively encode jealousy as a structured linear composition of these factors, consistent with human psychology, and demonstrate that toxic emotional states can be mechanically detected and surgically suppressed via bidirectional steering.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"认知逆向工程"四阶段框架（表征提取与验证 → 子空间正交化 → 统计加权 → 因果干预），将传统粗粒度 RepE 扩展为可对复杂情绪进行细粒度语义分解的工具
- 首次在白盒层面揭示了 LLM 内部编码社会比较嫉妒的线性组合结构：优越性（Superiority）充当情绪"触发开关"，领域自我定义相关性（Relevance）充当"强度放大器"，与人类心理学实证发现一致
- 在 8 个模型（Llama、Qwen、Gemma 系列）上验证了框架的通用性，并引入安慰剂控制变量（星期几）排除统计伪影，增强因果推断的严谨性
- 展示了通过表征向量进行双向干预（增强/抑制）可机械式操控模型的嫉妒输出，为 AI 安全中的表征监控和主动干预提供了新路径

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a four-phase Cognitive Reverse-Engineering framework (representation extraction → subspace orthogonalization → statistical weighting → causal intervention) that extends coarse-grained RepE into a fine-grained semantic decomposition tool for complex emotions
- Provides the first white-box dissection of how LLMs internally encode social-comparison jealousy as a structured linear combination of constituent cognitive factors, finding that Superiority acts as a trigger switch and Domain Self-Definitional Relevance as an intensity amplifier
- Validates the framework across 8 models from 3 families (Llama, Qwen, Gemma) with placebo controls (Weekday) to rule out statistical artifacts, strengthening causal inference rigor
- Demonstrates bidirectional steering (amplification and suppression) of jealousy outputs via purified representation vectors, opening new pathways for representational monitoring and proactive AI safety interventions

## Methodology

The framework consists of four phases applied to social-comparison jealousy analysis:

**Phase I -- Representation Extraction and Validation:** Uses Linear Artificial Tomography (LAT) with contrastive scenario pairs from an Atomic Training Set (T1). For each psychological factor (Superiority, Relevance, Weekday placebo), 200 contrastive pairs are constructed where only the target factor's polarity differs. The Mean Difference method extracts raw concept vectors from hidden states across all layers, validated via 5-fold cross-validation.

**Phase II -- Subspace Orthogonalization:** Raw vectors may be collinear. Null-space projection removes shared variance between factors, yielding purified "unique effect" vectors via: z_t = (I - P_O) * v_t, where P_O projects onto the confounding subspace. Vectors are L2-normalized for consistent intervention scaling.

**Phase III -- Statistical Weighting and Validity Check:** OLS regression on a combinatorial Generalization Set (G1) with structured vignettes tests whether jealousy scores decompose linearly into constituent factor projections. Standardized beta coefficients quantify each factor's decision weight. The Weekday placebo serves as a negative control.

**Phase IV -- Causal Intervention Framework:** Bidirectional steering at inference time injects or subtracts purified factor vectors from hidden states: H~(x) = H(x) +/- alpha * z_t. State-dependent baseline partitioning (Low vs. High jealousy subsets) ensures interventions start from appropriate baselines. Layer-wise profiling identifies optimal intervention zones in mid-to-late layers.

Models evaluated: Llama-3.1-8B, Llama-3.2-1B, Qwen2.5-7B/14B/32B, Gemma-3-4B/12B/27B.

## Results & Highlights

- **Phase I:** Concept representations stabilize near 100% cross-validation accuracy in mid-to-late layers across all 8 models. Early layers show severe fluctuations, indicating concepts have not yet formed.
- **Phase III:** High R-squared values in later layers confirm that LLMs represent jealousy as a weighted linear combination of Superiority and Relevance. The Weekday placebo consistently yields near-zero beta coefficients, validating the framework. A consistent pattern emerges: Superiority's relative weight declines while Relevance's increases through deeper layers, with Relevance ultimately surpassing Superiority in final layers.
- **Phase IV:** Bidirectional steering successfully shifts jealousy outputs in expected directions. Superiority shows slightly better negative steerability (suppression), while Relevance shows slightly superior positive steerability (amplification) -- consistent with their respective roles as "trigger" vs. "amplifier." Weekday interventions produce negligible effects.
- **Optimal intervention zones** are identified in mid-to-late layers (e.g., Layer 23 for Gemma-3-12B). Gemma-3-12B additionally responds to strict orthogonal knockout (ablation), while other architectures exhibit the "Hydra Effect" (representational self-repair).
- **Psychological alignment:** The internal computational dynamics -- Superiority as necessary trigger (zero-to-one), Relevance as dominant intensity multiplier -- mirror empirical findings from Salovey & Rodin (1984) and Takahashi et al. (2009).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. While Representation Engineering itself is not new, the extension to fine-grained psychological decomposition of complex emotions (rather than basic emotions or binary safety concepts) is a meaningful contribution. The four-phase pipeline with orthogonalization and placebo controls is methodologically rigorous.
- **Rigor:** Strong methodological design with placebo controls, cross-validation, bidirectional causal interventions, and 8-model evaluation. However, the ground-truth labels for the Generalization Set are rule-based (not from human annotation of model outputs), which could introduce circularity concerns.
- **Reproducibility:** Reasonably high. The methodology is clearly described with mathematical formulations, specific hyperparameters (alpha=15 for Llama/Qwen, alpha=3000 for Gemma), and well-defined datasets. However, no code release is mentioned.
- **Significance:** Significant for the intersection of interpretability and affective computing. The finding that LLMs structurally internalize human-like emotional algorithms is noteworthy, though the practical implications for AI safety remain speculative.
- **Red flags:** (1) The ground-truth jealousy scale is rule-based rather than empirically validated through human annotation of the specific scenarios. (2) Only two of four identified psychological antecedents are tested; the remaining factors (Similarity, Comparison Alternatives) are deferred. (3) The dataset is generated by Gemini-3-Pro and may not fully capture naturalistic emotional scenarios. (4) The claim about "structured linear composition" depends heavily on the linear regression assumption, which may be an oversimplification of the actual nonlinear representations.

## Strengths

- Rigorous four-phase pipeline with placebo controls borrowed from multiple scientific traditions (psychology, econometrics, epidemiology)
- First white-box mechanistic decomposition of a complex social emotion in LLMs, going beyond basic emotion categories
- Evaluation across 8 models from 3 families demonstrates generalizability
- Compelling alignment between LLM internal dynamics and established human psychological theories
- Practical demonstration of bidirectional emotional state manipulation for AI safety applications

## Limitations

- Only two of four theoretically identified antecedent factors are examined; the complete factor space remains unexplored
- The Generalization Set is synthetically generated (by Gemini-3-Pro) based on structured templates, raising questions about ecological validity
- Ground-truth labels are rule-based rather than derived from human annotation, potentially introducing systematic bias
- The linear regression assumption may oversimplify the actual nonlinear representational dynamics of complex emotions
- No code or dataset release is mentioned, limiting immediate reproducibility
- The practical AI safety implications are speculative and not demonstrated in real-world deployment scenarios

## Relevance

本文通过表征工程方法揭示了 LLM 内部编码复杂情绪的线性组合结构，与可解释性研究和 ML 可靠性直接相关：其提出的子空间正交化和安慰剂控制方法为 LLM 训练稳定性和内部表征可靠性审计提供了新的工具范式，同时展示了在推理时通过表征向量机械式检测和抑制不良情绪状态的可行性，对多智能体环境下的 AI 安全对齐具有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How well does the linear decomposition assumption hold for emotions with more complex, non-linear interactions among antecedent factors (e.g., guilt, shame)?
2. Can the purified representation vectors (e.g., z_jealousy) serve as effective monitors in real-world RLHF pipelines to detect deceptive alignment?
3. Do the observed psychological alignments generalize across languages and culturally diverse training corpora?
4. How do the relative weights of Superiority vs. Relevance shift with model scale within the same family?
5. What happens when competing emotional states (e.g., jealousy vs. empathy) are simultaneously activated -- do the representation vectors interact linearly or non-linearly?
