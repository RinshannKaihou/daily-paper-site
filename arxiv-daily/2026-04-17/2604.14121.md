> **一句话总结：** 本文提出CRAFT框架，通过构建多候选推理轨迹的共识推理知识图谱（RKG），利用TF-IRF加权的共识项提取和拓扑排序生成来同时修复LLM推理链中的步骤内部缺陷（逻辑错误、幻觉）和步骤级缺陷（过度思考、思考不足），在逻辑和数学推理基准上平均提升10%以上的标签预测准确率。

> **TL;DR:** CRAFT builds a Reasoning Knowledge Graph from the consensus parts of multiple candidate CoT traces, then synthesizes a new high-quality trace via topological generation, achieving 10%+ accuracy gains across logical and mathematical reasoning benchmarks while reducing step counts by over 50%.

## 核心贡献

- **反直觉发现**：通过在4个LLM上的系统性实验证明，提供正确答案并不能改善LLM的推理能力（在PRMBench和ROSCOE上均无显著提升），表明推理缺陷源于推理过程本身而非答案不确定性。
- **CRAFT框架**：提出统一的框架同时解决两类推理缺陷——步骤内部缺陷（逻辑错误、幻觉等）和步骤级缺陷（过度思考、思考不足），这是首个同时处理两类缺陷的方法。
- **共识推理知识图谱（RKG）**：将多条候选推理轨迹转化为图结构（步骤为节点、逻辑关系为边），通过跨轨迹共识聚合和结构异常过滤来识别并移除有缺陷的步骤。
- **拓扑引导的轨迹合成**：沿共识RKG的拓扑序重新生成推理步骤，而非从现有候选中选择，从而整合多条轨迹中的正确片段。
- **实证发现**：LLM在大多数推理步骤上是正确的（即使难题也是如此），因此基于共识的聚合能有效放大正确推理、抑制错误。

## Key Contributions

- **Counter-intuitive finding**: Systematic experiments across 4 LLMs show that providing correct answers yields no consistent improvement in reasoning ability on PRMBench (step verification) and ROSCOE (trace quality), demonstrated via paired Wilcoxon signed-rank tests with 95% bootstrap CIs.
- **CRAFT framework**: A unified three-module pipeline that jointly mitigates Step Internal Flaws (logical errors, hallucinations) and Step-wise Flaws (overthinking, underthinking) -- the first approach to address both simultaneously.
- **Reasoning Knowledge Graph (RKG)**: Converts candidate traces into per-trace directed graphs (steps as nodes, logical relations as edges), aggregates them into a consensus RKG via cross-trace edge frequency counting, and filters anomalous nodes/edges.
- **Topology-guided synthesis**: Generates an entirely new trace by traversing the consensus RKG in topological order, combining correct fragments from different traces into a coherent chain.
- **Empirical insight**: LLMs produce correct reasoning steps in the majority of cases regardless of benchmark difficulty, making consensus-based aggregation a viable strategy for trace quality improvement.

## Methodology

CRAFT operates in three modules:

**Module I -- Diverse Trace Generation & Consensus Term Extraction**: For each sample, roll out K=10 candidate traces at temperature T=0.7. Score terms using TF-IRF (Term Frequency-Inverse Reasoning Frequency), a variant of TF-IDF adapted to the multi-trace setting where terms frequent within one sample's K traces but rare across samples are deemed informative. After filtering common logical connectives via a blocklist, extract consensus terms that appear in at least a fraction beta=0.3 of traces.

**Module II -- Consensus RKG Construction & Anomaly Filtering**: Three-pass filtering: (1) Z-score filtering: each step is scored by weighted Jaccard overlap with consensus terms, z-normalized within the trace group; steps below gamma=-1.0 are removed as outliers. (2) Per-trace RKGs are built by prompting the LLM to tag steps as nodes and logical relationships as edges, with edge confidence fused from LLM-reported scores (0.7 weight) and Jaccard overlap (0.3 weight). These are aggregated into a consensus RKG by counting cross-trace edge frequencies and keeping edges above threshold theta=0.3. (3) Structural filtering removes isolated nodes (zero in/out-degree).

**Module III -- Topology-Guided Trace Synthesis**: Topologically sort the consensus RKG and regenerate the trace one step at a time at temperature 0.0, with each step's prompt including the consensus node text as a reference anchor and term hints from pruned traces.

The framework requires approximately 26-33 LLM API calls per sample (comparable to Self-Consistency with K=10).

## Results & Highlights

- **Accuracy**: CRAFT achieves the best or near-best accuracy across all four benchmarks (FLD, FOLIO, GSM8K, OlympiadBench) on both GPT-5.4-nano and o4-mini backbones. On OlympiadBench, it outperforms the next-best baseline by up to +8.5% accuracy while using over 70% fewer steps.
- **Step efficiency**: CRAFT achieves the highest accuracy while using roughly half the steps of most baselines (e.g., 5.4 steps vs. 17.9 on GSM8K, 7.6 vs. 29.0 on OlympiadBench under GPT-5.4-nano).
- **Trace quality**: ROSCOE evaluation shows CRAFT improves grammar (+1.4% to +5.9%), reduces step-level redundancy (-1.5% to -2.7%), and reduces word-level redundancy (-1.2% to -2.8%).
- **Ablation**: Removing the RKG causes -6% to -16% accuracy drop; removing synthesis causes -15% to -21% drop. The full framework provides 20%+ accuracy gains over no-CRAFT baseline on OlympiadBench.
- **Correct answer guidance study**: Providing ground-truth answers shows no significant improvement on PRMBench (p>0.05 for Simplicity and Soundness across all models) and only sporadic, inconsistent effects on ROSCOE.
- **Statistical rigor**: Results validated with 95% Wilson confidence intervals and paired Wilcoxon signed-rank tests.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate-to-high. The key insight -- that cross-trace structural consensus can detect both step-internal and step-wise flaws -- is novel. The RKG construction and topological synthesis approach is distinctive from prior graph-based methods (MGRS selects rather than synthesizes). The finding that providing correct answers does not improve reasoning is an important empirical contribution.
- **Rigor**: Strong. Significance testing with Wilcoxon signed-rank tests and bootstrap CIs; ablation study with clear component decomposition; hyperparameters fixed across all experiments (beta=0.3, theta=0.3, gamma=-1.0) reducing overfitting risk. Manual verification of 50 evaluation cases.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Hyperparameters are fully specified in Appendix I; prompt templates in Appendix N; all thresholds reported. However, code is not mentioned as released. The framework uses ~28 API calls per sample which is feasible but not trivially cheap.
- **Significance**: High practical relevance for LLM training data curation, distillation, and benchmark annotation. The framework addresses a real and underappreciated problem (correct label, flawed reasoning traces).
- **Red flags**: (1) Consensus does not equal correctness -- on hard problems where multiple candidates share the same wrong reasoning pattern, the framework could amplify errors. The authors acknowledge this limitation. (2) Lexical overlap bias from Jaccard similarity may penalize valid paraphrases and symbolic reasoning. (3) The same LLM family is used for both trace generation and RKG extraction, risking confirmation bias. (4) Gains on mathematical benchmarks (GSM8K, OlympiadBench) are smaller than on text-heavy logical benchmarks, likely because TF-IRF and Jaccard are less suited for symbolic content.

## Strengths

- First unified framework addressing both Step Internal Flaws and Step-wise Flaws simultaneously, rather than targeting one flaw type.
- Synthesis-based approach (generating new traces) is fundamentally more powerful than selection-based approaches (picking from existing candidates).
- Strong empirical results: consistent best or near-best across 4 benchmarks, 2 backbone models, and against 9 baselines.
- Step count reduction comes as a free byproduct of quality filtering, not an accuracy trade-off.
- Thorough ablation study and sensitivity analysis demonstrating the contribution of each component.
- Honest and detailed discussion of limitations (6 specific limitations acknowledged).
- The "correct answer guidance study" provides a valuable empirical finding that challenges intuitive approaches.

## Limitations

- **Sequential CoT assumption**: The framework is designed for linear Chain-of-Thought traces and may not extend to parallel or tree-structured reasoning paradigms.
- **Lexical overlap bias**: Jaccard similarity underlies both z-score filtering and edge validation, which penalizes valid paraphrases and is poorly suited for algebraic/symbolic reasoning.
- **Self-extraction bias**: The same LLM used for trace generation also extracts RKG nodes and edges; systematic extraction errors could propagate.
- **Consensus amplification risk**: On problems where many candidates share the same wrong reasoning pattern, consensus can amplify errors rather than suppress them.
- **TF-IRF with small K**: Inverse Reasoning Frequency is computed over only K=10 traces per sample, making term importance estimates noisy.
- **Math domain weakness**: Gains on GSM8K and OlympiadBench are positive but smaller than on text-heavy logical benchmarks, reflecting the limitation of term-overlap methods for symbolic content.

本文与LLM训练稳定性和可靠性直接相关：CRAFT框架可用于改善训练数据中推理轨迹的质量，避免模型在蒸馏过程中继承错误推理模式，同时其共识机制为评估LLM推理的可解释性提供了新的结构化视角。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does CRAFT perform when K is increased to 20 or 50? Does the consensus signal become more reliable, or do diminishing returns set in due to correlated errors?
- Can the framework be extended to handle parallel or tree-structured reasoning (e.g., multi-path problem solving)?
- What happens when combining CRAFT with a process reward model (PRM) for re-ranking the synthesized traces?
- How robust is the consensus assumption when applied to adversarial or intentionally misleading problems where majority reasoning is systematically wrong?
- Could embedding-based similarity (e.g., using text-embedding models) replace Jaccard for better handling of paraphrases and symbolic transformations, and is the ~1% accuracy gain worth the added computational cost?
- How does CRAFT interact with reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) or GRPO-style training -- could consensus RKGs serve as training signals for reasoning models?
