> **一句话总结：** 本文提出使用Earth Mover's Distance（EMD）作为诊断指标，量化脉冲神经网络（SNN）量化后发射分布相对于全精度基线的偏移，发现均匀量化即使在精度保持不变时也会引发显著的发射行为漂移，而LQ-Net式学习量化能同时保持精度和发射动力学行为。

> **TL;DR:** This paper shows that accuracy alone is insufficient for evaluating quantized SNNs. The authors propose Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) to measure firing distribution divergence from full-precision baselines, and find that uniform quantization causes significant firing dynamics drift even when accuracy is preserved, while LQ-Net-style learned quantization maintains both accuracy and behavioral fidelity.

## 核心贡献

- 首次提出将Earth Mover's Distance（EMD）作为SNN量化评估的诊断指标，用于衡量量化模型与全精度基线之间发射率分布的偏移，弥补了传统仅看精度的评估空白。
- 系统性地量化了量化方法、裁剪范围和位宽对SNN发射动力学的影响，揭示在精度相当的情况下不同配置可产生高达3.5倍的EMD差异。
- 首次将LQ-Net风格的学习量化应用于脉冲神经网络，发现学习量化在宽裁剪范围和低位宽下能有效保持发射行为，而均匀量化则严重退化。
- 揭示膜电位量化与权重量化具有本质不同：膜电位量化误差随时间步累积，需要>=4位精度才能可靠保持动力学行为，且无符号范围优于有符号范围。
- 提供了跨架构（SEW-ResNet8/18）和数据集（CIFAR-10/100）的全面实证分析，包括分层发射率分析和死神经元统计。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) as a principled diagnostic metric for measuring firing distribution divergence between quantized SNNs and their full-precision baselines, filling a gap left by accuracy-only evaluation.
- Provides the first systematic characterization of how quantization method, clipping range, and bit-width affect SNN firing dynamics, revealing that configurations with equivalent accuracy can exhibit up to 3.5x differences in EMD.
- Applies LQ-Net-style learned quantization to SNNs for the first time, demonstrating that learned quantization preserves firing behavior where uniform quantization fails, particularly under wide clipping ranges and low bit-widths.
- Identifies distinct challenges in membrane potential quantization versus weight quantization: membrane quantization errors compound across timesteps through recurrent LIF dynamics, requiring >=4 bits and favoring unsigned ranges.
- Delivers comprehensive empirical analysis across SEW-ResNet8/18 architectures on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, including layer-wise firing rate analysis, dead neuron statistics, and distribution-level visualization.

## Methodology

The authors evaluate quantization on SEW-ResNet architectures (ResNet-8 and ResNet-18 variants) with LIF neurons (tau=2.0, v_threshold=1.0, T=4 timesteps) trained directly on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 using SGD with cosine annealing over 200 epochs.

**Quantization methods studied:**
- **Uniform quantization with STE baseline**: Symmetric uniform quantization with Straight-Through Estimator for gradient approximation, evaluated at 2/4/6/8 bits under narrow ([-1,1]) and wide ([-10,10]) clipping ranges.
- **LQ-Net learned quantization**: Replaces fixed uniform levels with learned basis vectors, where quantized weights are expressed as binary linear combinations of these vectors. Basis vectors are jointly optimized with network weights via a closed-form Quantization Error Minimization (QEM) step. Applied to SNNs for the first time.
- **Membrane potential quantization**: Uniform quantization applied to LIF neuron membrane potentials within the recurrence, under signed ([-1.0,1.0]) and unsigned ([0.0,1.0]) ranges. LQ-Net was found to be inapplicable here due to training collapse.

**Dynamics analysis framework:**
- Per-neuron firing rates collected over 20 test batches, averaged across timesteps and spatial dimensions.
- EMD (Wasserstein-1 distance) computed between quantized and FP32 firing rate distributions per layer, reported as network averages.
- Additional metrics: mean firing rate, dead neuron percentage (r_c < 0.05), and per-layer firing rate profiles.

## Results & Highlights

- **Accuracy-dynamics disconnect**: At 6-bit with wide clipping on CIFAR-100 ResNet18, uniform quantization achieves 73.7% accuracy (EMD=0.014) vs. narrow-clip at 74.9% (EMD=0.008) -- a mere 1.2% accuracy gap conceals a 2x divergence in firing dynamics. On CIFAR-10, the EMD gap reaches 3.5x at under 1% accuracy difference.
- **LQ-Net preserves behavior**: At 2-bit with wide clipping on CIFAR-100 ResNet18, where uniform quantization collapses to 51.6% accuracy with EMD=0.121, LQ-Net achieves 74.4% accuracy with EMD=0.012 -- a 10x improvement in dynamics preservation.
- **LQ-Net is clipping-invariant**: Unlike uniform quantization, LQ-Net maintains low EMD across both narrow and wide clipping ranges, since learned quantization levels adapt to the weight distribution rather than being fixed.
- **Membrane quantization needs higher precision**: Weight quantization works well down to 2-bit with LQ-Net, but membrane quantization requires >=4 bits for reliable accuracy and dynamics preservation due to error compounding across timesteps.
- **Unsigned ranges preferred for membranes**: At 2-bit membrane quantization, unsigned ([0,1]) achieves higher accuracy and lower EMD than signed ([-1,1]) consistently across architectures and datasets.
- **Layer-wise systematic degradation**: Wide-clip uniform quantization shows systematically elevated firing rates across all layers compared to the FP32 baseline, potentially increasing computational load at inference independent of accuracy.

## Authors & Credibility

The paper is authored by Evan Gibson Smith, Jacob Whitehill, and Fatemeh Ganji from Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI). The work appears to be a well-scoped academic research contribution. The methodology is systematic -- covering multiple architectures, datasets, bit-widths, clipping ranges, and both weight and membrane quantization -- which lends credibility to the claimed generality of the findings. The paper builds on established prior work (SEW-ResNet, LQ-Net, Q-SNNs) and provides clear comparisons with baselines.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The core novelty is moderate. The application of EMD to SNN firing distributions is a sensible but straightforward methodological contribution. The first application of LQ-Net to SNNs is novel, though LQ-Net itself is an established ANN technique from 2018. The framing of "accuracy is not enough" is conceptually valuable.
- **Rigor**: The experimental setup is thorough, with systematic sweeps across bit-widths, clipping ranges, architectures, and datasets. However, the work is limited to CIFAR-10/100 with relatively small SEW-ResNet models, and the number of timesteps (T=4) is modest. The failure mode of LQ-Net for membrane quantization is reported but not deeply diagnosed.
- **Reproducibility**: The paper provides sufficient detail on architectures, training hyperparameters, and quantization configurations to support reproducibility. The use of standard benchmarks (CIFAR-10/100) and established architectures (SEW-ResNet) aids this.
- **Significance**: The paper makes a valid and important point that accuracy alone misses distributional aspects of SNN behavior under quantization. However, the practical deployment implications (e.g., how much EMD divergence translates to real energy/performance differences on neuromorphic hardware) are discussed but not empirically validated.
- **Red flags**: The LQ-Net training collapse for membrane quantization is a notable limitation that is acknowledged but not resolved. The paper does not evaluate on larger-scale datasets (ImageNet) or with larger models, limiting the generalizability of conclusions. The timesteps T=4 is quite low for SNN evaluation.

## Strengths

- Introduces a principled metric (EMD) that captures distributional differences invisible to scalar summary statistics like mean firing rate.
- The "accuracy is not enough" framing is compelling and well-supported by the data -- multiple configurations show equivalent accuracy with divergent firing dynamics.
- First application of LQ-Net to SNNs demonstrates clear benefits for dynamics preservation.
- Comprehensive experimental design with systematic sweeps across all relevant axes (method, bit-width, clipping, architecture, dataset).
- Layer-wise analysis provides interpretable insights into where and how quantization degrades firing behavior.
- Clear, well-organized writing with good use of figures and tables.

## Limitations

- Limited to CIFAR-10/100 with small SEW-ResNet models; scalability to ImageNet or larger architectures is untested.
- Only T=4 timesteps, which is relatively low; dynamics may differ with longer temporal windows.
- LQ-Net fails for membrane quantization (training collapses), and this failure mode is not diagnosed or resolved.
- No validation on actual neuromorphic hardware (Loihi, TrueNorth) to confirm that EMD-predicted dynamics differences translate to real deployment differences in energy or latency.
- No comparison with other learned quantization methods (LSQ, SpikeFit) in the dynamics-preservation context.
- The EMD threshold for "acceptable" dynamics preservation is left unspecified; no clear practical guidance on when EMD divergence becomes problematic.

## Relevance

本文提出的行为保持（behavior preservation）视角对LLM/ML模型部署可靠性研究具有启发意义：正如SNN量化需要在精度之外评估发射行为漂移，LLM量化（如GPTQ、AWQ）同样应在困惑度之外评估输出分布偏移和涌现行为的退化。EMD作为分布级诊断工具的思路可推广至更广泛的模型压缩可靠性评估。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the EMD-based dynamics findings generalize to larger-scale SNN architectures and datasets like ImageNet with deeper networks and more timesteps?
- Can the EMD framework be extended to capture temporal firing pattern differences (not just per-neuron firing rate distributions) such as inter-spike interval distributions or spike synchrony?
- What is the relationship between EMD divergence and actual energy consumption / latency on neuromorphic hardware platforms like Loihi 2 or TrueNorth?
- Could a hybrid approach combining learned weight quantization (LQ-Net) with specialized membrane quantization schemes achieve dynamics preservation for both weights and membrane potentials simultaneously?
- Is there a critical EMD threshold beyond which deployment behavior (e.g., downstream task performance in a pipeline, safety properties) degrades measurably, and can such a threshold be established empirically?
