> **一句话总结：** RGPO提出用可微分的接受门(sigmoid gate)替代重要性采样比率进行样本选择而非重加权，在保证有界梯度方差和近似单调策略改进的前提下，于MuJoCo连续控制和RLHF偏好对齐任务中实现了比PPO/GRPO更高的奖励和更低的KL散度。

> **TL;DR:** RGPO replaces importance sampling ratios with a smooth, differentiable acceptance gate g(r) in [0,1] that selects trustworthy samples rather than reweighting all of them, yielding bounded gradient variance, near-TRPO KL control at PPO-level cost, and Pareto-dominant reward-KL tradeoffs in RLHF alignment.

## 核心贡献

- 提出RGPO框架：用可微分的接受门 $\alpha_\theta(s,a) = g(r_\theta) \in [0,1]$ 替代无界的重要性采样比率 $r_\theta$，将拒绝采样从数据级启发式方法提升为优化原则
- 统一视角：通过有效梯度权重 $w(r) = g'(r) \cdot r$ 证明REINFORCE是精确特例、TRPO/PPO是梯度级对应、AWR是密切关联方法
- 理论保证：证明了偏差有界（Theorem 1）、梯度方差有限（Theorem 2，即使IS方差发散时也成立）、近似单调策略改进（Theorem 3）
- 双比率门扩展至RLHF：同时锚定前一轮策略和参考模型，实现奖励-KL帕累托最优

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the RGPO framework that replaces unbounded IS ratios with a smooth, differentiable acceptance gate $g(r_\theta) \in [0,1]$, elevating rejection from a data-level heuristic to an optimization principle
- Provides a unifying lens via the effective gradient weight $w(r) = g'(r) \cdot r$: REINFORCE is an exact special case (on-policy limit), TRPO and PPO are gradient-level correspondences, AWR is closely related
- Proves bounded gradient bias (Theorem 1), finite bounded gradient variance even under heavy-tailed IS ratios where IS variance diverges (Theorem 2 + Proposition 1), and approximate monotonic policy improvement analogous to TRPO (Theorem 3)
- Extends naturally to RLHF with a dual-ratio gate anchoring both the previous policy and the reference model, achieving Pareto-dominant reward-KL outcomes

## Methodology

RGPO replaces the standard IS surrogate objective $\mathcal{L}_{TRPO}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{\pi_{old}}[r_\theta \cdot A_{old}]$ with $\mathcal{L}_{RGPO}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{\pi_{old}}[\alpha_\theta(s,a) \cdot A_{old}]$, where $\alpha_\theta = g(r_\theta) \in [0,1]$ is a smooth acceptance gate.

**Core design choices:**
- **Sigmoid gate (recommended):** $g(r) = \sigma(k(r-1))$, centered at $r=1$ with sharpness $k$. The effective weight $w(r) = k\sigma(1-\sigma)r$ is bell-shaped, damping both over- and under-represented samples.
- **Clipped-linear gate:** $g(r) = \text{clip}(r, 0, c)$, equivalent to truncated IS with a hard cap.
- **Temperature-controlled gate:** $g(r) = r^\beta / (1 + r^\beta)$, the most conservative option.

**Practical algorithm:** Adds an adaptive KL penalty $\beta \hat{D}_{KL}(\pi_\theta \| \pi_{old})$ with the same adaptive schedule as PPO's KL-penalty variant. The gate introduces negligible overhead (a single element-wise operation per sample), matching PPO in computational cost with no second-order optimization required.

**RLHF extension (dual gate):** Uses $r = \max(\pi_\theta/\pi_{old}, \pi_\theta/\pi_{ref})$ inside the sigmoid gate, plus separate KL penalties to both anchors, providing a differentiable alignment constraint.

**Gradient derivation:** Through the chain rule, the RGPO gradient is $\nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_{RGPO} = \mathbb{E}_{\pi_{old}}[w_\theta(s,a) \nabla_\theta \log \pi_\theta A_{old}]$ where $w_\theta = g'(r_\theta) \cdot r_\theta$ is the effective gradient weight. This formulation unifies existing methods: each corresponds to a specific choice of $w(r)$.

## Results & Highlights

**MuJoCo Continuous Control (4 environments, 3 seeds, 1M steps):**
- RGPO outperforms PPO on Walker2d-v4 (+81%, p=0.047) and Ant-v4 (+47%, p=0.064), statistically equivalent on HalfCheetah and Hopper
- Cross-seed stability dramatically better: std is 0.36-0.75x that of PPO; coefficient of variation stays below 27% vs. PPO's 48-52%
- KL spike rate: 0% for RGPO vs. 44-84% for PPO across all environments
- At 6M steps on HalfCheetah: RGPO 3095 vs. PPO 1116 (p=0.028); PPO regresses across all budget levels
- TRPO achieves strictest KL but underperforms both PPO and RGPO at 7x computational cost due to single-step updates
- AWR fails catastrophically with multi-epoch training (mean KL 18.85 on Ant, max KL 1000)

**RLHF Preference Alignment (Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct, Anthropic HH-RLHF, 3 seeds):**
- RGPO achieves Pareto-dominant outcome: highest reward (+0.243, +14.8% vs. PPO-RLHF) AND lowest KL to reference (0.364, -16.0% vs. PPO-RLHF, -53.1% vs. GRPO)
- GRPO's KL grows monotonically (textbook reward over-optimization); RGPO stabilizes at lowest KL among online RL methods

**Ablation studies** validate sigmoid gate superiority (best bias-variance tradeoff), optimal sharpness k=5, and that the IS ratio anchoring in the gate is essential for stable multi-epoch optimization.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The conceptual shift from "reweighting" to "selection" via a differentiable acceptance gate is genuinely novel. The unification of TRPO/PPO/REINFORCE/AWR through the effective gradient weight $w(r) = g'(r) \cdot r$ provides clean theoretical insight. The dual-ratio gate for RLHF is a natural and useful extension.

**Rigor (Strong):** Three formal theorems with complete proofs in appendices. Empirical evaluation uses proper statistical tests (Welch t-tests), multiple seeds, and fairness controls (identical architectures, optimizers, hyperparameters across methods). Ablations are thorough (6 categories).

**Reproducibility (Good):** Pseudocode provided, hyperparameters specified, standard benchmarks used. The sigmoid gate has only one additional hyperparameter (k). However, no code repository is linked.

**Significance (High):** Directly addresses training stability in policy optimization and RLHF alignment -- two active areas. The Pareto-dominant RLHF result is practically meaningful. The 0% KL spike rate across all environments is a strong empirical signal.

**Red flags:**
- Only 3 seeds per experiment; some comparisons (e.g., Ant-v4 p=0.064) are marginally significant
- RLHF experiment uses a relatively small model (1.5B); scaling to larger models is untested
- No evaluation on discrete action spaces (Atari) or reasoning tasks
- The theoretical bounds rely on bounded advantages and Lipschitz g, which may not always hold
- Hopper-v4 shows essentially no improvement over PPO in mean return (only variance reduction)
- Authors acknowledge the gate introduces controllable bias, which may be unacceptable for off-policy evaluation

## Strengths

- Clean conceptual contribution: the "selection vs. reweighting" framing is intuitive and powerful
- Strong theoretical grounding with formal bias, variance, and policy improvement guarantees
- The unification of existing methods through $w(r) = g'(r) \cdot r$ is elegant and provides genuine insight
- Excellent KL control (0% spike rate) without second-order optimization, matching PPO cost
- Pareto-dominant RLHF result (highest reward + lowest KL) is practically compelling
- Long-horizon experiments (6M steps) demonstrate sustained advantage over PPO
- Comprehensive ablations covering gating functions, sharpness, bias-variance, ESS, and KL dynamics

## Limitations

- Introduces controlled bias; not suitable for applications requiring strictly unbiased gradient estimates
- Gate sharpness k requires some tuning (though k=5 works well across tested environments)
- RLHF results are on a small model (1.5B parameters); unclear if advantages scale to frontier-size LLMs
- No evaluation on discrete action spaces, reasoning benchmarks, or multi-turn dialogue
- Hopper-v4 shows no mean performance improvement over PPO (only stability gains)
- The theoretical guarantees assume bounded advantages and Lipschitz gating functions
- AWR baseline uses multi-epoch training (n_epochs=10), which is known to be problematic for AWR; single-epoch AWR comparison is acknowledged as future work
- The adaptive beta schedule is borrowed directly from PPO without theoretical justification for its interaction with the gate

## Relevance

本文与LLM训练稳定性和可靠性高度相关。RGPO通过可微分的接受门替代PPO的硬裁剪，从根源上解决了重要性采样方差爆炸问题，在RLHF对齐任务中实现了帕累托最优的奖励-KL权衡。这种将样本选择机制显式建模为可微分组件的思路，为理解和对齐LLM训练过程中的策略漂移提供了新的理论视角。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does RGPO scale to 7B+ parameter language models in production RLHF pipelines? Does the dual-ratio gate remain Pareto-dominant at scale?
- Can the acceptance gate be combined with GRPO-style group-relative advantages for reasoning tasks (e.g., math, code generation)?
- Is there an optimal adaptive schedule for the gate sharpness k during training, analogous to learning rate schedules?
- How does RGPO interact with reward model over-optimization in the "gold reward" vs. "proxy reward" setting studied by Gao et al. (2023)?
- Could the gating mechanism be extended to handle multi-modal action distributions or mixture-of-experts policies?
- What is the theoretical relationship between the RGPO gate and the optimal proposal distribution in adaptive importance sampling?
