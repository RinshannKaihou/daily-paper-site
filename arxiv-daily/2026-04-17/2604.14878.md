> **一句话总结：** GenRec 提出了一种面向工业级推荐系统的生成式检索框架，通过 Page-wise NTP 解决分页请求下的一对多标签歧义问题，利用不对称 Token Merger 将输入序列长度压缩约 2 倍，并引入 GRPO-SR 强化学习方法结合混合奖励机制抑制 reward hacking，在京东 App 线上 A/B 测试中实现点击量提升 9.5%、交易量提升 8.7%。

> **TL;DR:** GenRec is a decoder-only generative retrieval framework for large-scale industrial recommendation that resolves pagination-induced label ambiguity via Page-wise NTP, compresses multi-token Semantic IDs with an asymmetric Token Merger (~2x input reduction), and aligns outputs with user preferences through GRPO-SR (RL with NLL regularization and hybrid rewards), achieving +9.5% clicks and +8.7% transactions in JD.com production A/B tests.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Page-wise NTP** 训练策略：将整个交互页面（订单、点击、曝光）作为联合监督目标，而非逐条预测单个 item，解决了工业分页请求机制下的一对多标签歧义问题，同时提供更密集的梯度信号，将幻觉率降低超过 50%。
- 设计 **不对称线性 Token Merger**：在 prefilling 阶段将每个 item 的 3-token Semantic ID 压缩为单一向量，输入长度减少约 2 倍，解码阶段保留完整 SID 以确保细粒度检索，且精度几乎无损。
- 提出 **GRPO-SR** 强化学习方法：将 Group Relative Policy Optimization 与 NLL 正则化结合以稳定训练，并引入 Hybrid Rewards（稠密偏好模型 + 相关性门控机制）来抑制 reward hacking。
- 在京东 App 主页推荐场景完成大规模线上部署，验证了生成式推荐范式在工业系统中的可扩展性，并观察到模型缩放的收益递减规律（3B 到 7B 提升有限）。

## Key Contributions

- **Page-wise NTP** training objective that supervises over an entire interaction page (ordered, clicked, exposed items) rather than individual items, resolving the one-to-many label ambiguity inherent in pagination-based recommendation systems and reducing hallucination rate by over 50%.
- **Asymmetric linear Token Merger** that compresses 3-token Semantic IDs into single latent vectors during prefilling (~2x input length reduction) while preserving full-resolution decoding, with negligible accuracy loss.
- **GRPO-SR** reinforcement learning method combining Group Relative Policy Optimization with NLL regularization for training stability, plus Hybrid Rewards (dense preference model + relevance gating) to mitigate reward hacking.
- Large-scale production deployment on JD.com's homepage feed, demonstrating +9.5% clicks and +8.7% transactions in month-long A/B tests, with notable long-tail item improvements (+10% exposure, +16% clicks, +13% transactions).
- Empirical investigation of model scaling laws for generative recommendation, revealing diminishing returns beyond 3B parameters.

## Methodology

GenRec formulates recommendation as conditional sequence generation over Semantic IDs (SIDs), where items are discretized via RQ K-means on multimodal embeddings from Qwen2.5-VL.

**Step 1: Semantic ID Construction.** A multimodal encoder (Qwen2.5-VL) encodes visual and textual item information, fine-tuned with domain-specific collaborative pairs. RQ K-means iteratively clusters residual vectors to map each item to a hierarchical 3-token SID tuple.

**Step 2: Page-wise NTP SFT.** Instead of standard next-item prediction, the model is trained to predict the entire page of user interactions (ordered items, clicked items, exposed items, sorted by interaction intensity). This provides denser gradients per forward pass and resolves the one-to-many ambiguity where identical user histories map to multiple valid labels under pagination mechanisms.

**Step 3: Asymmetric Token Merger.** During prefilling, each item's 3-token SID embeddings are concatenated and projected through a linear layer into a single vector, reducing prompt length by ~2x. Special structural tokens (e.g., `<sep>`) remain unmerged. During decoding, full SID tokens are used for fine-grained retrieval.

**Step 4: GRPO-SR Preference Alignment.** Building on GRPO (from DeepSeekMath), the RL stage operates on point-wise beam search rollouts (matching inference protocol). Key innovations include: (1) Hybrid Rewards combining a SIM-based dense preference model with a binary relevance gate that zeros out rewards for semantically invalid SID combinations; (2) Positive item reward calibration anchoring ground-truth interactions to the group maximum reward; (3) NLL regularization over positive trajectories to prevent policy drift, serving as an alternative to KL-divergence penalties.

The backbone is Qwen2.5 decoder-only architecture, tested at 1.5B, 3B, and 7B scales, trained on ~560M user interaction sequences from JD.com.

## Results & Highlights

- **Offline evaluation** (Table 1): GenRec (3B) achieves HR@50 of 0.7192 and N@10 of 0.2635, substantially outperforming LC-Rec (HR@50=0.6226, N@10=0.2146) and TIGER (HR@50=0.3556). Hallucination rate drops to 4.96% vs. LC-Rec's 7.80% and TIGER's 15.46%.
- **Token Merger effectiveness** (Table 1, w/o TM): Removing the Token Merger yields comparable metrics (HR@50=0.7201), confirming the compression preserves critical information.
- **Scaling laws** (Table 2): 1.5B -> 3B yields significant gains (HR@50: 0.6527 -> 0.7192), but 3B -> 7B shows marginal improvement (0.7192 -> 0.7216). The 3B model's greater depth (36 layers) vs. 7B's greater width (28 layers) suggests depth may be more important than width for generative recommendation.
- **RL alignment** (Table 3): GRPO-SR improves Reward@1 by +18.01% relative to SFT baseline. Removing the gating mechanism causes HR@50 to drop below baseline and hallucination rate to nearly halve -- a clear sign of reward hacking being suppressed by the gate.
- **Online A/B test** (Table 4): SFT model alone achieves +8.5% clicks and +7.3% transactions. GRPO-SR alignment pushes this to +9.5% clicks and +8.7% transactions. Long-tail items see +10% exposure, +16% clicks, +13% transactions.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: Moderate-High.** The individual components (GRPO, token merging, page-wise training) draw from existing ideas, but the specific combination and adaptation to industrial recommendation challenges is well-motivated and non-trivial. The Page-wise NTP formulation is the most novel contribution, directly addressing a real systems issue. The GRPO-SR objective with NLL regularization (as alternative to KL penalty) is a meaningful adaptation for recommendation.
- **Rigor: Moderate.** The paper provides ablation studies for key components (Token Merger, gating mechanism, RL variants). However, some details are sparse: the SID vocabulary size is not explicitly stated; the exact architecture configurations for each scale are referenced but not fully detailed. The offline evaluation uses only last-day data from a single month, which may not capture temporal dynamics.
- **Reproducibility: Low-Moderate.** The dataset is proprietary (560M JD.com interactions), making exact reproduction impossible. However, the methodological descriptions are sufficiently detailed for reimplementation on public datasets. Code is not released.
- **Significance: High.** This is one of the few works demonstrating generative retrieval at true industrial scale with production deployment. The +9.5% click improvement in a mature system is substantial. The scaling law analysis provides practical guidance for the field.
- **Red flags:** (1) The 3B vs. 7B comparison is confounded by architectural differences (depth vs. width), making scaling law claims somewhat speculative. (2) The online A/B test reports exposure rate changes (48.7% -> 57.3%) without clearly explaining what this metric means or whether increased exposure itself drives the click/transaction gains. (3) The RL experiments only report reward metrics (R@K), not standard retrieval metrics like HR/NDCG, making it hard to assess whether reward gains translate to recommendation quality improvements.

## Strengths

- Addresses real, practical challenges of deploying generative retrieval in industrial settings (pagination, long sequences, preference alignment).
- Clean decomposition of the problem into three well-motivated sub-problems with corresponding solutions.
- Strong online results with month-long A/B tests on a major e-commerce platform.
- Comprehensive ablation study validating each component's contribution.
- Interesting finding on model scaling (depth vs. width for recommendation) with practical implications.
- The NLL regularization as an alternative to KL-divergence is a practical insight for stabilizing RL training in recommendation.

## Limitations

- Proprietary dataset prevents external validation; no experiments on public benchmarks.
- Scaling analysis is confounded by architectural differences between Qwen2.5-3B and 7B variants.
- The RL section lacks standard retrieval metrics (HR, NDCG), reporting only reward-based metrics.
- The paper does not analyze computational cost / inference latency tradeoffs in detail, which is critical for industrial deployment claims.
- Limited discussion of failure modes or cases where generative retrieval underperforms traditional approaches.
- The Token Merger, while effective, is a simple linear projection; more sophisticated merging strategies are not explored.

## Relevance

这篇论文与 LLM 训练稳定性直接相关：GRPO-SR 中通过 NLL 正则化替代 KL 散度来稳定强化学习训练的思路，以及混合奖励机制抑制 reward hacking 的设计，对于理解大模型对齐训练中的奖励模型鲁棒性和训练稳定性具有参考价值。此外，Page-wise NTP 作为一种多目标联合监督策略，体现了从训练目标设计层面提升模型可靠性的思路。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the Page-wise NTP objective generalize to other sequential generation tasks beyond recommendation (e.g., dialogue, code generation)?
- What is the sensitivity of GRPO-SR to the NLL regularization weight alpha, and is there a principled way to set it?
- Could the Token Merger approach be applied to other multi-token entity representations (e.g., in knowledge-graph-grounded generation)?
- How does the system handle cold-start items that lack sufficient collaborative signal for SID construction?
- What are the failure modes of the relevance gate in Hybrid Rewards -- does it ever suppress genuinely preferred items?
- How does inference latency compare quantitatively between GenRec and the traditional retrieve-and-rank pipeline at production scale?
