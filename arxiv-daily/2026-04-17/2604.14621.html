Differentially Private Conformal Prediction

arXiv: 2604.14621

Date: 2026-04-16

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14621

一句话总结： 本文提出DPCP框架，利用差分隐私机制的稳定性属性设计了一种无需数据分割的保形预测方法，在相同隐私预算下比现有private split CP方法产生更紧凑的预测集，同时提供端到端的(epsilon, delta)-DP保证。
TL;DR: This paper introduces Differentially Private Conformal Prediction (DPCP), a non-splitting conformal inference framework that exploits the stability of DP mechanisms to construct prediction sets from a single model fit, achieving tighter prediction intervals than existing private split conformal methods under the same privacy budget while maintaining end-to-end (epsilon, delta)-DP guarantees.

核心贡献

  • 提出差分保形预测（Differential CP），一种无数据分割的保形预测方法，利用DP机制在相邻数据集上的稳定性属性直接建立与oracle CP的联系，避免传统split CP的统计效率损失。
  • 设计DPCP完整框架（Algorithm 2），将DP模型训练与私有分位数估计机制（Algorithm 1）结合，实现端到端隐私保护，量化校正阶为 2/(n*epsilon)，比现有private split CP方法更紧凑。
  • 给出完整的理论保证：端到端(epsilon, delta)-DP隐私性（Theorem 5）、有限样本覆盖保证（Theorem 6）、以及DPCP预测区间逼近oracle区间的效率界（Theorem 10）。
  • 在合成数据和9个真实数据集上的实验表明，DPCP在强隐私约束（小epsilon）下能显著产生比PSCP更短的预测区间，同时保持接近目标覆盖率。

Key Contributions

  • Differential CP: A novel intermediate conformal procedure that exploits the adjacency stability of (epsilon, delta)-DP mechanisms to build prediction sets without data splitting, establishing a direct bridge between oracle CP and private conformal inference.
  • DPCP Framework (Algorithm 2): A fully private, model-agnostic conformal prediction procedure combining DP model training with a private quantile mechanism (Algorithm 1), achieving a sharper quantile correction of order 2/(n*epsilon) compared to the split-based adjustment in prior work.
  • End-to-End Privacy Guarantee (Theorem 5): Proves that combining an (epsilon_1, delta)-DP training mechanism with the epsilon_2-DP quantile algorithm yields (epsilon_1 + epsilon_2, delta)-DP for the overall prediction set.
  • Coverage Guarantees (Theorem 6): Establishes finite-sample marginal coverage of at least 1 - alpha under two regularity assumptions controlling the randomized private threshold selection behavior.
  • Efficiency Analysis (Theorem 10): Shows that under balanced privacy allocation, the DPCP interval converges in probability to the oracle conformal interval, with the oracle approximation error decomposing into terms from Gaussian perturbation, estimator discrepancy, and private quantile selection.

Methodology

The paper develops DPCP in three stages:

1. Problem Setup (Section 3.1): Given an i.i.d. sample D_n = {(X_i, Y_i)} from an unknown distribution P_XY, the goal is to construct a set-valued predictor C_alpha^dp(X_{n+1}) satisfying Pr(Y_{n+1} in C_alpha^dp(X_{n+1})) >= 1 - alpha while obeying (epsilon, delta)-DP with respect to D_n. The key challenge is that DP mechanisms destroy the exchangeability assumption underlying classical conformal prediction.

2. Differential CP (Section 3.2): An intermediate procedure that avoids data splitting by exploiting DP stability. When a test point is added, the enlarged dataset D_{n+1} is adjacent to D_n. Because a DP mechanism A behaves similarly on adjacent datasets, its stability converts directly into coverage guarantees. The differential CP set is defined as:

C_alpha^d(X_{n+1}) = { y : R((X_{n+1}, y), A(D_n)) <= q(e^{-epsilon_1}(alpha - delta), D_n, D_n) }

This procedure itself is not private (scores are computed from raw data), but it serves as the conceptual bridge to the fully private method.

3. DPCP (Section 3.3, Algorithm 2): The full private procedure consists of two components:

  • Private model training: Compute the fitted model using an (epsilon_1, delta)-DP training mechanism (e.g., DP-SGD, DP-ERM with Gaussian noise).
  • Private quantile estimation (Algorithm 1): A differentially private quantile mechanism that bins candidate thresholds {e_1, ..., e_M} and selects among them using exponential mechanism weights based on empirical counts. The input level is corrected as alpha_0 = alpha_1 - 2/(n*epsilon_2), where alpha_1 = e^{-epsilon_1}(alpha - delta).

The total privacy budget splits as epsilon = epsilon_1 + epsilon_2, and by composition the entire procedure satisfies (epsilon, delta)-DP.

Key design insight: Unlike private split CP (which partitions data into training/calibration, losing efficiency), DPCP uses the full dataset for both model fitting and calibration. The quantile correction 2/(n*epsilon) is sharper than the split-based adjustment, leading to less conservative prediction sets.

Results & Highlights

  • Synthetic experiments: DPCP achieves coverage close to the nominal 1 - alpha level while producing substantially shorter prediction intervals than PSCP (Private Split Conformal Prediction). As sample size n increases, both methods converge, but DPCP stabilizes faster.
  • Privacy budget sensitivity: As epsilon increases, intervals from both methods shrink, but DPCP exhibits markedly faster stabilization in interval length. Under strong privacy (epsilon = 0.1), DPCP's advantage is most pronounced.
  • Real data experiments: Tested on 9 standard datasets (including Communities and Crime, Power Consumption) using least squares, ridge, and lasso regression with Opacus library for private training. DPCP consistently achieves coverage closer to the target 0.9 than the alpha_0'-DPQ method used by PSCP.
  • Small sample regime: When sample sizes are small, the alpha_0'-DPQ method nearly always outputs the maximum nonconformity score (rendering prediction sets meaningless), while DPCP maintains effectiveness.
  • Efficiency convergence (Theorem 10): Under balanced privacy split (epsilon_1 = epsilon_2 = epsilon/2) with epsilon = Theta(n^{-eta}), the DPCP interval converges to the oracle interval in probability at a rate governed by O((1/n^eta + log n / n^{1-eta})^gamma).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The key insight of leveraging DP mechanism stability (adjacency property) to avoid data splitting in conformal prediction is genuinely novel. The differential CP concept as a bridge between oracle CP and private CP is a clean theoretical contribution.
  • Rigor: Strong. The paper provides end-to-end privacy proofs (Theorem 5), finite-sample coverage guarantees (Theorem 6), and asymptotic efficiency analysis (Theorem 10). Assumptions are clearly stated and discussed. The theoretical appendices are thorough.
  • Reproducibility: Moderate to good. Algorithms are clearly specified (Algorithm 1 and 2). The paper uses standard libraries (Opacus) and standard datasets. However, no code repository is mentioned.
  • Significance: High for the intersection of privacy-preserving ML and uncertainty quantification. The efficiency gains over split-based methods are practically meaningful, especially in regimes with limited data and strong privacy constraints.
  • Red flags:
  • Assumptions 1 and 2 for exact coverage (Theorem 6) are non-trivial regularity conditions that may be difficult to verify in practice. The authors acknowledge this and note it as a direction for future work.
  • The approximate i.i.d. assumption (xi_TV-approximately i.i.d.) on score sequences is a strong structural condition whose verifiability is not discussed in depth.
  • The paper is limited to regression settings (Y in R); extension to classification is mentioned only briefly in the appendix.

Strengths

  • Eliminates the statistical efficiency loss inherent in data splitting, which is particularly damaging under DP constraints where privacy noise amplifies the effect of reduced sample size.
  • Model-agnostic framework compatible with general DP learning mechanisms (DP-ERM, DP-SGD, DP-Bayesian methods).
  • The quantile correction of order 2/(n*epsilon) is provably sharper than the split-based adjustment in Angelopoulos et al. (2022).
  • Naturally extends to weighted conformal prediction and distribution shift settings.
  • Comprehensive evaluation across 9 real datasets demonstrating consistent practical improvements.

Limitations

  • Exact coverage guarantees rely on Assumptions 1 and 2, which control the interaction between randomized private threshold selection and the conditional tail behavior of scores. These conditions may be hard to verify for specific models and data distributions.
  • The asymptotic efficiency result (Theorem 10) assumes fixed dimension d and specific decay rates for epsilon. High-dimensional extensions are not discussed.
  • Classification tasks receive limited treatment (deferred to appendix).
  • No code release is mentioned, which may hinder immediate reproduction.
  • The framework currently requires the underlying DP training mechanism to satisfy (epsilon, delta)-DP; compatibility with relaxations such as Renyi DP or concentrated DP is not explored.

Relevance

本文提出的DPCP框架直接关联ML可靠性研究：它通过差分隐私机制提供可证明的预测不确定性量化（uncertainty quantification），将隐私保护与校准的预测区间结合，为高隐私要求场景下可靠机器学习模型的部署提供了理论与方法基础。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can Assumptions 1 and 2 be replaced with more primitive, verifiable conditions, or can finite-sample coverage be guaranteed without them under weaker structural assumptions on the score distribution?
  2. How does DPCP perform in high-dimensional settings where the parameter dimension d grows with n, and does the efficiency advantage over split CP persist?
  3. Can the framework be extended to provide distribution-free conditional coverage guarantees (rather than marginal), particularly for subgroups defined by sensitive attributes?
  4. What is the computational overhead of Algorithm 1 (private quantile estimation) compared to the non-private conformal quantile, and how does it scale with the number of bins M?
  5. How would DPCP interact with adaptive composition when multiple prediction queries are made on the same private model, and can privacy accounting be tightened using Renyi DP or numerical composition?
