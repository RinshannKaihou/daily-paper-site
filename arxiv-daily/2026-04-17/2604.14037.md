> **一句话总结：** 本文利用 ReLU 激活函数在零点处的不可微性，通过代数方法给出了浅层 ReLU 神经网络参数空间对称性的完整分类，证明了在稠密子集上参数纤维微分同胚于 Lie 群 $H_n$（排列与正对角矩阵生成的子群），并提供了可计算的最小形式算法。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides the first complete symmetry classification of shallow (two-layer) ReLU neural networks by exploiting the non-differentiability of ReLU at zero, proving that for a dense (Zariski open) set of parameters the fiber of the realization map is diffeomorphic to the Lie group $H_n$, and giving a computable "minimal form" to fully characterize all parameter symmetries.

## 核心贡献

- 给出了浅层 ReLU 神经网络（架构 $(m,n,k)$）参数对称性的**完整代数分类**，这是以往需要激活函数解析性的方法无法处理的。
- 证明了对稠密（Zariski 开集）参数子集，实现映射的纤维微分同胚于 Lie 群 $H_n$（由排列群 $S_n$ 和正对角矩阵群 $D_n^+$ 生成）。
- 引入了**最小形式（minimal form）**的概念，作为参数等价类的规范表示，可通过第 6 节提供的算法程序计算。
- 分类了参数在 $H_n$ 作用下的**稳定子群**结构：$\text{Stab}_{H_n}(\theta) \simeq S \times H_{n'}$，其中 $S$ 由至多 $\binom{n-n'}{2}$ 个 2-循环生成。
- 揭示了 ReLU 特有的"隐藏对称性"源于恒等式 $\sigma(x) - \sigma(-x) = x$，并给出了一个反例证明已有文献中的充分条件（如 Grigsby-Lindsey-Rolnick 2023 的引理 D15）不足以保证纤维同构于 $H_n$。

## Key Contributions

- Provides the **first complete algebraic classification** of parameter symmetries in shallow ReLU neural networks, a case previously excluded by methods requiring analytic activation functions.
- Proves that for a **dense (Zariski open) subset** of parameters, the fiber of the realization map $\rho_{(m,n,k)}$ is diffeomorphic to the Lie group $H_n$ (generated by permutations $S_n$ and positive diagonal matrices $D_n^+$).
- Introduces the concept of **minimal form** as a canonical representative of parameter equivalence classes, computable via an explicit algorithm provided in Section 6.
- Classifies the **stabilizer structure** of parameters under the $H_n$-action: $\text{Stab}_{H_n}(\theta) \simeq S \times H_{n'}$, where $S$ is generated by at most $\binom{n-n'}{2}$ transpositions.
- Identifies that the only additional symmetry beyond the well-known $H_n$-action in shallow ReLU networks derives from the identity $\sigma(x) - \sigma(-x) = x$, and provides a concrete counterexample showing that existing conditions in the literature (e.g., Lemma D15 of Grigsby, Lindsey, Rolnick 2023) are insufficient to guarantee that the fiber is isomorphic to $H_n$.

## Methodology

The paper takes a purely algebraic (rather than probabilistic or geometric) approach to classifying parameter symmetries of shallow ReLU networks of architecture $(m, n, k)$: $\mathbb{R}^m \to \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^k$.

1. **Stabilizer classification (Section 3):** The author first classifies the possible stabilizers of a parameter under the $H_n$-action. Using the decomposition $H_n = D_n^+ S_n$, the stabilizer is shown to be of the form $\langle \mathcal{S} \rangle \times H_{n'}$, where $\mathcal{S}$ consists of elements of the form $\text{diag}(\lambda_i, \lambda_j)(i,j)$ with $\lambda_i \lambda_j = 1$. This establishes a hierarchy: non-trivial stabilizer implies non-trivial stabilizer on $(A,b)$, which implies the fiber is not $H_n$.

2. **Minimal form construction (Section 4):** The author defines an equivalence relation $\sim$ on the quotient $H_n \backslash \Omega_{(m,n,1)}$ generated by three types of elementary equivalences (zero-weight absorption, proportional-row merging, zero-affine-row constant absorption). A minimal form $\theta_{min}$ is defined as the unique minimum element in each equivalence class with respect to a lexicographic ordering. The key technical tool is the 0-factor reduction, which strips away degenerate rows.

3. **Key proposition on ReLU smoothness (Proposition 14):** The heart of the classification exploits the non-differentiability of ReLU. For a minimal parameter with no 0-factors, the function $v^T \sigma(Ax + b) + C$ equals an affine linear function only if the rows of $A$ and $b$ come in matched pairs $(a_i, b_i)$ and $(-a_i, -b_i)$ with corresponding weights $+1$ and $-1$ -- precisely the structure induced by $\sigma(x) - \sigma(-x) = x$. The proof works by restricting to one-dimensional subspaces and exploiting the kink of ReLU at the origin to produce a differentiability contradiction.

4. **Complete classification (Theorems 3 and 4):** The image of the realization map is shown to be $H_n \backslash \Omega_{(m,n,1)} / \equiv$ where $\equiv$ extends $\sim$ by exactly the $\sigma(x) - \sigma(-x) = x$ symmetry. The multi-output case $(k > 1)$ is reduced to the scalar case via coordinate-wise projections.

5. **Density result (Section 5):** The author constructs an explicit Zariski open set $U = U_4 \cap U_\sim$ in parameter space where the only symmetries are the $H_n$-orbit. This requires (a) all output weights $v_i \neq 0$, (b) all $(a_i, b_i)$ pairwise linearly independent, (c) no zero-affine rows, and (d) all nontrivial signed combinations $\sum \beta(i) v_i a_i \neq 0$. On this set, the fiber equals $H_n \cdot \theta$ and is diffeomorphic to $H_n$ by the Lie group quotient theorem.

## Results & Highlights

- **Theorem 1:** The set of parameters $\theta \in \Omega_{(m,n,k)}$ whose fiber $\rho_{(m,n,k)}^{-1}(\theta)$ is diffeomorphic to $H_n$ is dense (in fact, contains a Zariski open set).
- **Theorem 2:** For any parameter $\theta$, $\text{Stab}_{H_n}(\theta) \simeq S \times H_{n'}$ where $n' \leq n$ and $S$ is a subgroup of $S_{n-n'}$ generated by at most $\binom{n-n'}{2}$ transpositions.
- **Theorem 3:** The image of $\rho_{(m,n,1)}$ is completely described as the quotient $H_n \backslash \Omega_{(m,n,1)} / \equiv$, with the only non-trivial equivalence beyond $\sim$ coming from the identity $\sigma(x) - \sigma(-x) = x$.
- **Theorem 4:** The multi-output case extends naturally via coordinate-wise projection: $\theta_1 \equiv_k \theta_2$ iff $\pi_i(\theta_1) \equiv \pi_i(\theta_2)$ for all $i$.
- **Counterexample:** A concrete parameter $\theta_1 \in \Omega_{(2,3,1)}$ satisfying genericity, supertransversality, LRA, and TPIC conditions of prior work still has a fiber strictly larger than its $H_n$-orbit, demonstrated by exhibiting $\theta_2 \neq h \cdot \theta_1$ with $\rho(\theta_1) = \rho(\theta_2)$.
- An algorithm (Section 6, Python code) is provided for computing the minimal form of any parameter.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. This is the first complete symmetry classification for ReLU networks (previous techniques required analytic activations). The exploitation of ReLU's non-differentiability as a feature rather than a limitation is a genuine methodological insight. The counterexample to prior sufficient conditions is an important corrective.
- **Rigor:** Very high. The paper is written in the style of pure mathematics with complete proofs. Definitions are precise, and the logical dependency chain (stabilizers -> minimal form -> classification -> density) is clearly laid out.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. An explicit Python implementation for computing minimal forms is provided (Section 6). The mathematical constructions are fully specified and could be independently verified.
- **Significance:** The results are foundational and theoretical. While the immediate practical impact on LLM training or applied ML is limited (the paper treats only shallow networks), the work deepens the mathematical understanding of parameter degeneracy in ReLU networks, which underpins issues like loss landscape geometry and optimization dynamics.
- **Red flags:** (1) Single author, no institutional affiliation listed on the paper itself (though IFML support is acknowledged). (2) The restriction to shallow (two-layer) networks is a significant limitation; extending these techniques to deep networks remains an open problem. (3) The algebraic conditions in Proposition 24 (non-vanishing of all signed combinations) grow exponentially in the number of hidden units, raising questions about practical computability for large $n$.

## Strengths

- First complete algebraic treatment of ReLU network symmetries, filling a notable gap in the literature.
- Clean mathematical framework: the minimal form provides a computable canonical representative for each equivalence class.
- The insight that ReLU's non-differentiability (specifically the kink at zero) can be leveraged as a proof technique is elegant and potentially transferable.
- Provides a concrete counterexample demonstrating that previously proposed conditions for parameter identifiability are insufficient even in the shallow case.
- The stabilizer classification (Theorem 2) is a useful standalone result with a clean structural description.
- Open Zariski conditions for the "generic" case are explicit and interpretable.

## Limitations

- **Restricted to shallow (two-layer) networks.** The extension to deep ReLU networks remains entirely open and the techniques here do not trivially generalize.
- **No experimental validation.** The paper is purely theoretical; there are no experiments demonstrating how the symmetry classification affects training dynamics or generalization.
- **Exponential condition growth.** The condition in Proposition 24 requires checking $3^n - 1$ signed combinations $\beta$, which is computationally prohibitive for large hidden layer widths.
- **No direct practical guidance.** While the theory is elegant, the paper does not discuss implications for network initialization, regularization, or optimization.
- **Limited discussion of geometric interpretation.** The author acknowledges that the algebraic equivalence $\equiv$ from Theorem 3 may encapsulate multiple distinct geometric phenomena, but does not pursue this further.

## Relevance

本文对 ReLU 网络参数对称性的完整分类提供了理解神经网络损失景观几何结构的重要理论基础，与训练稳定性和参数可辨识性直接相关。虽然仅限于浅层网络，其最小形式的代数框架和利用 ReLU 不可微性的方法论对深层网络对称性研究具有启发性，与 LLM 训练中参数等价类影响优化动态的问题有间接联系。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the minimal form framework and the exploitation of ReLU's non-differentiability be extended to deep (multi-layer) ReLU networks, or does the combinatorial complexity become intractable?
2. What are the practical implications of the identified "hidden symmetries" (those beyond $H_n$) for gradient-based optimization dynamics -- do they create flat directions or saddle points in the loss landscape?
3. How does the signed-combination condition $\sum \beta(i) v_i a_i \neq 0$ relate to known sufficient conditions for local identifiability, and can it be relaxed or approximated in practice?
4. The identity $\sigma(x) - \sigma(-x) = x$ generates the only additional symmetry class beyond $\sim$. Are there analogous identities for other piecewise-linear activation functions (e.g., leaky ReLU, GELU approximations) that would lead to similar classifications?
5. Can the stabilizer classification (Theorem 2) be used to design symmetry-aware initialization schemes or regularization penalties that improve training convergence?
