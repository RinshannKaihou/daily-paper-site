Truncation breaks the expressive-power equivalence of positional encodings for GNNs

James Flora · Mitchell Black · Weng-Keen Wong · Amir Nayyeri | Oregon State · UC San Diego | 2026-06-11
Key Finding
Truncated spectral, walk-based & k-harmonic PEs are no longer expressively equivalent — each family captures different structure.
Key Metric
0.064MAE
Walks + k-harmonics · ZINC-12k · best single combo
Why This Matters
"Spectral is stronger" intuition fails after truncation. Mixing PE families is the actionable rule for practitioners.
Critical Edge
Separations proven on constructed graph pairs; only 2 benchmarks (BREC, ZINC-12k) validate "mixing helps." No public code.
Core Argument

Full positional encodings (O(n³)) are too expensive, so every practical GNN truncates them. But theory only covers the complete case. This paper proves that truncation fundamentally changes what PEs can distinguish.

  • Spectral PE and walk-based PE are equivalent when complete — not after truncation.
  • Truncated spectral PEs can be weaker than 1-WL (Thm 4.1).
  • Walk-based PEs can be stronger than truncated spectral PEs (Thm 4.2).
Key Theorems
Thm 4.1 — ∃ graph pairs distinguishable by 1-WL but indistinguishable by k-EP-WL for k ∈ Ω(n). Truncated spectral loses to WL.
Thm 4.2 — ∃ graph pairs indistinguishable by Ω(n)-Walk-WL but distinguishable by 1-EP-WL. Neither family dominates.
Thm 4.6 — [2n]-harmonic distances match full EP-WL — the k-harmonic family is complete.

Disproved Conjecture

Zhang et al. (2023) conjectured that effective resistance encodes the full spectrum. Theorem 4.3 + Corollary 4.1 show otherwise:

  • Resistance-WL can be weaker than Adjacency-WL and EP-WL on weighted graphs.
  • Effective resistance does not universally encode the full Laplacian spectrum.

This matters because resistance-based PEs are widely used in practice under the assumption of spectral completeness.

k-Harmonic Distances

A new spectral PE family bridging spectral and polynomial methods:

  • Biharmonic (k=2) distinguishes certain tree pairs in O(1) iterations vs. Ω(n) for resistance.
  • Complete family: [2n]-harmonics match EP-WL.
  • Approximable in O(mk poly log n + n² log n) via fast Laplacian solvers + JL projection.
  • Generic transfer: if one k distinguishes a pair, all but O(n⁵) others do too (Thm 4.8).
BREC Expressivity & Takeaway

BREC benchmark (Table 1):

  • k-EP-WL (k=5): 55% — near the 3-WL ceiling of 54.25%
  • Biharmonic alone: 46.5% — competitive for a single encoding
  • 20 adjacency powers: 53.5%

Rule of thumb: Mix PE families from different truncated groups rather than stacking more dimensions of the same family.

Limitations
  • Theoretical separations use constructed graph pairs — real-world prevalence is unclear.
  • Only two benchmarks (BREC, ZINC-12k) validate the "mixing helps" claim.
  • PE dimensionality fixed to 8 — may favor certain encodings.
  • Some claims on unweighted graphs remain conjectural.
  • No public code or hyperparameter details.
  • Computational overhead of mixing multiple PE families not benchmarked.
