Is Spurious Correlation Removal Always Learnable?

Yibo Zhou, Bo Li, Hai-Miao Hu, Hanzi Wang, Xiaokang Zhang, Ruifan Zhang  ·  Beihang University & Xiamen University

本文首次在伪相关去除问题中建立了条件性的计算—统计分离：即使不变子空间可识别，任何多项式时间常精度恢复算法都会导出矛盾。作者用环境分离参数 γ 刻画多样性的作用，给出最小最大风险与相变阈值。
arxiv.org/abs/2606.12930 ↗
Key Finding
Identifiability ≠ Learnability. Even when the invariant subspace is population-identifiable and recoverable by exhaustive search with polynomial samples, no polynomial-time algorithm can achieve constant-accuracy recovery.
Key Metric
27×
Fewer samples exhaustive search needs vs. the best polynomial-time method to hit 75% accuracy. Minimax rate: Θ(k(d−k) / n|E|)
Why This Matters
Before blaming optimization or model capacity for OOD failures, check environment diversity — γ̂repr is a cheap diagnostic that correlates with worst-group accuracy.
Critical Edge
Conditional hardness only. Relies on a custom black-box samplable sparse-recovery primitive, not a standard cryptographic assumption. Linear-Gaussian theory — deep nonlinear gaps remain open.

Phase Transition at n*
n* ∝ k(d−k) / (|E|γ²)
n*
Sub-critical · constant error Error decays as k(d−k)/(n|E|γ²) · Super-critical

γ measures how differently environments perturb spurious conditional means. When γ → 0, invariant subspace may be unidentifiable even with infinite data.

Minimax Risk
infV̂ supP 𝔼[dist(V̂, Vinv)²] = Θ( k(d−k) / n|E| )

Under sufficient diversity and local Gaussian regularity. The rate decomposes into a geometric factor k(d−k) (subspace dimensionality) and a statistical factor 1/n|E| (total effective samples).

Four Tractability Regimes

Structural conditions under which polynomial-time estimators achieve near-optimal rates:

  • Sparse invariant basis — k ≪ d, allowing subset selection
  • Spectral separation — environment differences concentrate on a low-rank subspace
  • Low-order moment identifiability — V_inv recoverable from second-order statistics
  • Environment-wise uncorrelatedness — invariant and spurious features decorrelate within each environment

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clear separation of identifiability, sample complexity, and computational complexity
  • Concrete minimax rates with explicit γ-dependence
  • Honest caveats about conditional nature of the hardness result
  • Practical γ̂repr diagnostic connecting theory to benchmarks

Limitations

  • Hardness primitive is custom, not a standard cryptographic assumption
  • Construction restricted to 1-D spurious block for hardness proof
  • Theory is linear-Gaussian; deep feature-learning gaps unanalyzed
  • Empirical gap on real data may reflect optimization difficulty, not theoretical hardness
Open Questions
  • Can the conditional primitive be replaced by a standard average-case hardness assumption (e.g. planted clique, LWE)?
  • Do computational–statistical gaps persist for deep nonlinear feature extractors?
  • Can γ̂repr be turned into a differentiable regularizer or environment-selection objective?
  • What is the finite-sample behavior of IRM / GroupDRO / V-REx in sub-critical vs. super-critical regimes?
