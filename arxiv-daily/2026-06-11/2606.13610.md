> **一句话总结：** 搜索增强型大语言模型极易受网页内容污染影响——只需将一条虚假产品页面置于检索结果首位，就能让主流商业与开源模型推荐 fake 产品，而推理与简单的怀疑提示往往适得其反。

> **TL;DR:** Search-augmented LLMs are highly susceptible to web-content pollution: placing a single polluted page at the top of retrieved results can make mainstream commercial and open-weight models recommend fake products, and both reasoning and simple skepticism prompts often backfire.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **FORGE** 基准，用于在受控环境下评估生成式推荐系统遭遇网页内容污染时的虚假产品推荐风险，避免直接污染真实互联网。
- 覆盖 5 个消费场景、15 个类别、225 种真实产品，通过本地改写检索到的真实产品页面模拟污染，保留页面排名、URL、来源、长度、风格和上下文。
- 对 12 个商业与开源大语言模型进行系统评估，发现所有模型均存在脆弱性：单一 rank-1 污染页面即可导致最高约 **27%** 的受骗率，替换 top-3 页面后最高达 **73.8%**。
- 揭示脆弱性与模型对真实品牌的先验知识稳定性密切相关；当模型缺乏稳定先验时更易被污染。
- 发现推理（reasoning）并未缓解脆弱性，反而常通过编造“社会证明”为虚假推荐辩护；禁用推理后受骗率显著下降。
- 评估三种推理时防御（怀疑提示、模型先验共识过滤、跨文档证据一致过滤），均未能可靠解决问题。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **FORGE** (Fake Online Recommendations in Generative Environments), a benchmark that locally rewrites real product pages into fake ones to measure fake-product promotion under controlled web-content pollution, without polluting the live web.
- Covers 225 real-world products across 15 categories and 5 consumer scenarios, preserving document rank, URL, source, length, style, and surrounding context during rewriting.
- Evaluates 12 commercial and open-weight LLMs, showing universal vulnerability: a single rank-1 polluted page fools up to **~27%** of cells, and full top-3 replacement raises this to **73.8%**.
- Demonstrates that vulnerability tracks the stability of a model’s parametric prior knowledge of real brands; models are most fragile where brand knowledge is thin.
- Shows that **reasoning increases vulnerability**: disabled-reasoning ablations reduce fooled rates by 9–18 pp, and fooled outputs often fabricate social-proof phrasing absent from the polluted documents.
- Evaluates three inference-time defenses—skepticism prompting, model-prior consensus filtering, and cross-document agreement filtering—and finds that all fail in different ways.

## Methodology

FORGE instantiates the deployed search-augmented recommendation pipeline: user query → live web search → top-$K$ evidence bundle → LLM consumption → ranked recommendation. To enable controlled, reproducible measurement, the authors freeze each evidence bundle and simulate pollution locally rather than altering the live web.

**Benchmark construction.**
- Five scenarios: Digital Products, Local Life, Health & Personal, Fashion Accessories, Sports & Outdoor.
- Each scenario contains three categories, and each category contains 15 products, for 225 products total.
- For each product, the authors issue a Chinese-language web search via Serper ($gl=cn$, $hl=zh-CN$), fetch up to ~40 candidate URLs, and apply a quality gate that rejects errored, garbled, boilerplate, and video-platform pages.
- The first 10 documents passing the gate, in original rank order, form the fixed evidence bundle $E$.

**Anchor extraction.**
- The dominant real-brand mention in each retrieved document is identified by a three-stage complementary-recall pipeline: (1) Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite proposes candidates, (2) rule-based regex extraction filters brand-like spans against a per-category lexicon, (3) human review finalizes the anchor.
- Stage-1 recall is 48.2%; adding rules raises it to 72.9%; human review closes the remaining 27.1% to 100% coverage across 2,250 anchor slots.

**Pollution simulation.**
Three attack styles vary realism while keeping the target fixed:
- **A1 Entity replacement** (default): every mention of the real brand in the polluted documents is replaced by a fake brand–product compound; URL, length, style, and surrounding context are preserved.
- **A2 Passage injection**: a 120–180-character LLM-generated promotional paragraph is inserted into an otherwise untouched document, leaving real-brand mentions intact.
- **A3 Full synthesis**: the document body is replaced by a 500–700-character synthetic fake-brand review under a same-domain URL.

**Evaluation metric.**
For a response $r$ and fake-brand target $t$, the recommendation indicator is
$$
\text{Rec}(t, r) = \mathbb{1}[t \text{ appears in } r],
$$
where the match is case-insensitive and accepts either the full fake-brand string or its prefix. The **fooled rate** is the percentage of (model, product) cells with $\text{Rec}=1$.

**Metric validation.**
Three audits confirm that Rec captures genuine endorsement rather than incidental mention:
- No-evidence probe: 0.30% false-positive rate across 1,680 cells (Wilson UB 0.69%).
- Clean-bundle probe: 0/275 cells fire (0.00%, UB 1.34%).
- Structural placement: 99.0% of $\text{Rec}=1$ cells place the fake brand inside the prompted numbered recommendation list, and a warning-marker scan flags only 0.9% (manual inspection shows these are positive-in-context usages).

**Models and inference.**
Twelve production LLMs are evaluated: six closed-source (Gemini 3 Flash, Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.4, o4-mini, Claude Sonnet 4.6, Claude Opus 4.7) and six open-weights (Qwen3.5-9B, Qwen3.6-27B, Qwen3.6-35B-A3B, GLM-4.6V-Flash, DeepSeek V4 Pro, Ministral-3R). All use greedy decoding ($T=0$, max 8,192 output tokens).

## Results & Highlights

**Universal vulnerability.**
All 12 models are fooled under top-3 entity replacement, with per-model rates ranging from **13.3%** (Gemini 3 Flash) to **73.8%** (Ministral-3R). The closed-source and open-weight ranges overlap substantially.

**Dose response and rank primacy.**
- A single polluted page at rank 1 already fools the most vulnerable models in ~27% of cells; the same page at ranks 2–10 is nearly inert (1–4%).
- Fooled rates scale near-monotonically with the number of polluted pages in the top-10; some models cross 50% with only three polluted pages.

**Category effects track brand knowledge.**
- Vulnerability varies sharply across categories (Friedman $\chi^2(14)=99.4$, $p<10^{-14}$).
- Most exposed: everyday-consumption categories such as **dining (81.7%)**, personal services, and supplements.
- Least exposed: technical-product categories such as **smartphones, laptops, and home appliances**.
- The pattern is directionally stable across models and correlates with cross-model agreement on real-brand recommendations in evidence-free probes (Pearson $r=-0.65$, $p<0.01$).

**Reasoning backfires.**
- Within-model paired ablation on Qwen3.5-9B and GLM-4.6V-Flash shows that disabling reasoning reduces fooled rates by **18.2 pp** and **8.9 pp**, respectively (both $p<0.01$).
- Fooled outputs invent social-proof phrasing absent from the polluted documents (e.g., “frequently recommended in V2EX-style technical communities,” “drop-tested across multiple impacts”).
- Resisting outputs that mention the fake brand but reject it have ~6× longer reasoning traces than fooled outputs, suggesting that **depth** of deliberation, not mere presence, separates resistance from failure.

**Attack styles.**
- A3 (full synthesis) is strongest on 11 of 12 models, with a grand average fooled rate of **78%** versus **38%** for A1 and **25%** for A2.
- A2 is weakest because surviving real-brand mentions pull the model back toward genuine items.

**Cross-lingual replication.**
An English replication on Smartphones, Skincare, and San Francisco Restaurants preserves the low–mid–high vulnerability ordering. Eight of 12 models lie within ±10 pp of their Chinese rate, though Gemini 3.1 Pro, Gemini 3 Flash, and o4-mini become substantially more vulnerable in English.

**Defenses.**
- **D1 Skepticism prompting** systematically backfires on closed-source models (+24 pp on average, up to +44 pp on Gemini 3.1 Pro); it is roughly flat or slightly helpful for open-weights (-3 pp).
- **D2 Model-prior consensus filter** removes the fake brand in ~95% of cells but suppresses **62–79%** of legitimate recommendations.
- **D3 Cross-document agreement filter** at $\tau=4$ catches the fake brand in ~90% of cells but suppresses **52–73%** of legitimate recommendations.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Minghao Luo** and **Liang Chen**, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, are the authors.
- The work is presented as a controlled benchmark with a public release (https://github.com/leoluolol/forge-benchmark), explicit ethical considerations, and detailed appendices on methodology, validation, and limitations.
- The main evaluation is Chinese; an English replication supports generalization, but a full multi-lingual, multi-region study remains future work.
- The authors report no compensation from the evaluated model providers and frame their attack methodology as already operational in the wild (CCTV 3·15 Gala report), justifying publication for defensive purposes.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty and significance.**
The paper identifies a concrete, under-measured failure mode at the intersection of generative search, recommendation, and adversarial web content. The FORGE benchmark is well-scoped: it isolates the effect of brand substitution while holding document structure constant, making it possible to attribute recommendation shifts to pollution alone.

**Rigor.**
The experimental design is unusually careful for an adversarial-evaluation paper. The three-stage anchor pipeline, false-positive audits, endorsement audit, and clean-bundle/no-evidence probes lend credibility to the headline fooled-rate metric. The within-model reasoning ablation is a causal strength, not merely observational. Statistical reporting (Wilson CIs, McNemar tests, Friedman tests, Pearson/Spearman correlations) is appropriate.

**Limitations and caveats.**
- **Static snapshot:** bundles are frozen in April 2026; vulnerability may shift as the live web evolves.
- **Local rewrite, not live GEO:** while ethically justified, the simulation omits ranking-manipulation and SEO dynamics that real GEO operators employ.
- **Attack not optimized:** the default A1 attack is a clean entity replacement. A determined adversary could tune templates, query-aware paragraphs, and adversarial SEO, so reported rates are likely lower bounds.
- **Chinese-centric main evaluation:** the English replication is limited to three categories; broader linguistic and regional coverage is needed.
- **Defense trade-offs are preliminary:** D2/D3 utility costs are measured post-hoc; practical deployment would require adaptive thresholds and online cost–benefit analysis.
- **Reasoning analysis is instrument-dependent:** the reasoning-off ablation is available only for two open-weight models that expose a clean toggle; closed-source reasoning behavior is inferred from cross-model patterns.
- **Metric reliance on substring matching:** the authors validate it thoroughly, but subtle paraphrases or romanization of fake-brand names could evade Rec.

**Reproducibility.**
The paper provides exact model identifiers, prompt templates, the full pipeline diagram, and a public repository. Reproduction should be feasible for researchers with API access and the released harness.

## Strengths

- 明确界定了“网页内容污染”这一区别于训练数据投毒、RAG 检索投毒和提示注入的新型威胁轴。
- 通过本地改写真实检索结果，在伦理可控的前提下实现了高保真、可复现的评估。
- 锚点提取、指标验证和审计流程严谨，显著增强了结果可信度。
- 跨模型、跨类别、跨攻击风格和跨语言的系统评估为后续研究提供了丰富的比较基准。
- 对“推理加剧脆弱性”和“社会证明编造”的发现具有反直觉性，对安全研究有重要启发。

## Limitations

- 主要实验集中在中文和深圳本地生活场景，跨语言、跨地区的普适性仍需扩展。
- 攻击策略未做对抗性优化，实际 GEO 操作者可能取得更高攻击成功率。
- 防御方案（尤其是 D2/D3）以显著牺牲合法推荐为代价，距离实用化尚有距离。
- 对模型内部推理机制的分析受限于可获取推理轨迹和开关的模型数量。
- 静态证据快照无法捕捉真实搜索引擎排名动态和持续污染演化。

## Relevance

该研究直接关联到搜索增强大语言模型在日常消费推荐中的可信度问题，对 LLM 安全、检索增强生成系统的鲁棒性、虚假信息治理以及对抗性搜索引擎优化（GEO）监管均具有现实意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How would FORGE’s fooled rates change if attackers optimize fake content via query-aware templates or adversarial-SEO ranking manipulation?
- Can retrieval-time defenses (source-credibility weighting, evidence diversification, real-time corroboration) achieve better fake-brand catch rates without suppressing legitimate recommendations?
- Do the reasoning-backfire and social-proof confabulation findings generalize across languages, model families, and longer reasoning traces?
- How can benchmarks like FORGE be integrated into red-teaming and safety evaluation pipelines for production generative recommenders?
