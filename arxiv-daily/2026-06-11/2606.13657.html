Dense Supervision, Sparse Updates

On the Sparsity and Geometry of On-Policy Distillation

Guo Yu · Hao-Xuan Ma · Jun-Peng Jiang · Han-Jia Ye · Wenlin Liu · Yulan Hu

尽管使用密集的教师监督信号，on-policy distillation（OPD）仍产生坐标稀疏、谱集中且偏离源权重主方向的参数更新——OPD 更接近于稀疏的 on-policy 编辑，而非普通稠密重写。

Key Finding

On-policy distillation produces coordinate-sparse, spectrally concentrated parameter updates despite dense teacher supervision — placing OPD closer to sparse on-policy editing than to dense supervised rewriting.

Key Metric
66.72–89.50%
coordinate sparsity at τ = 10⁻⁵

Relative Frobenius norm: just 0.036%–0.142% — two orders of magnitude below offline distillation (11.94%).

Why This Matters

Dense supervision ≠ dense parameter changes. OPD's inherent sparsity opens concrete paths to lighter, specialized post-training methods — sparse masks, PEFT adapters, and optimizer redesign.

Critical Edge

Interventions tested only on 1.5B–4B models. Updates are numerically full-rank — "sparse" describes energy concentration, not literal rank deficiency. OPD sparsity ≠ functional minimality.

Geometric Signature

  • Updates are numerically full-rank (τ = 10⁻⁴), yet spectrally concentrated: top-16 SVD energy captures 19.69%–30.52%; stable rank 7.77–12.58.
  • OPD deltas avoid source principal subspaces: 10% top source coordinates cover only 4.39%–5.46% of visible updates — roughly half the random baseline.
  • Updates are biased toward low-magnitude source coordinates (24.99%–48.57% coverage), suggesting OPD preferentially edits "unimportant" parameter directions.
  • FFN projections dominate update energy (65.06%–85.88%), with attention contributing up to 37.36% in some runs.

Critical Assessment

  • Novel framing: Positions OPD between SFT, offline distillation, and RLVR using parameter geometry — a genuinely new analytical lens for post-training.
  • Causality gap: Claims "on-policy data distribution drives sparsity" but supports it with correlation, not controlled data-distribution swaps. The direct causal mechanism remains unproven.
  • Scale limitation: Intervention experiments (subnetwork training, optimizer ablation) only cover 1.5B–4B models. Generalization to 7B+ is unknown.
  • Threshold sensitivity: Sparsity hinges on τ = 10⁻⁵. Small-magnitude updates excluded by this threshold may still carry functional significance.
  • Optimizer confound: AdamW vs SGD uses very different learning rates (1e-6 vs 1e-2) without systematic grid search, weakening the "AdamW necessary" conclusion.
  • Title risk: "Sparse Updates" may mislead readers — updates are full-rank; sparsity is energetic, not dimensional.

Method at a Glance

  • 10 model pairs: 6 OPD, 3 RLVR, 1 offline distillation (LLM + VLM).
  • Checkpoint diff ∆W computed in float32 from bfloat16 weights.
  • Metrics: relative Frobenius norm, coordinate sparsity, SVD energy, stable rank, source subspace alignment.
  • Functional tests: sparse subnetwork retraining, AdamW vs SGD ablation.

Open Questions

  • Does OPD sparsity hold at 7B+ scale and across code, agent, or embodied tasks?
  • Can training trajectory analysis reveal when sparsity crystallizes?
  • Do sparse coordinates map to interpretable functions (specific reasoning steps, neurons)?
  • Can we design hybrid adapters that jointly exploit low-rank + sparse structure?
  • Can OPD's sparse task vectors be repurposed for model editing, capability composition, or targeted unlearning?
