> **一句话总结：** TWLA 通过后训练量化将 LLM 权重压缩到 1.58-bit 三元值、激活平均量化到 4-bit，并借助 E2M-ATQ、KOTMS 与 ILA-AMP 三个模块，在 LLaMA2/3 与 Qwen3 系列上同时取得了比现有 2-bit/sub-2-bit 方法更高的精度与更快的推理速度。

> **TL;DR:** TWLA is a post-training quantization (PTQ) framework that pushes LLMs to W1.58A4 (1.58-bit ternary weights and 4-bit activations on average). It combines an output-aware ternary quantizer (E2M-ATQ), a Kronecker-structured orthogonal rotation that reshapes weights while dampening activation outliers (KOTMS), and a dynamic-programming-based inter-layer activation mixed-precision allocator (ILA-AMP). Across LLaMA2/3 and Qwen3 families, TWLA outperforms strong 2-bit and prior ternary baselines and delivers up to 3.64× throughput speedup and >80% memory reduction on llama.cpp.

## 核心贡献

- 首次在纯后训练量化（PTQ） setting 下实现 LLM 的 W1.58A4（1.58-bit 三元权重 + 平均 4-bit 激活），弥合了此前方法只能全精度激活或依赖昂贵量化感知训练（QAT）的空白。
- 提出 Euclidean-to-Manifold Asymmetric Ternary Quantizer（E2M-ATQ）：先在欧氏空间做热启动得到稳定三元模式，再在激活二阶矩诱导的流形上重新定位 shift/scale，直接最小化层输出误差。
- 提出 Kronecker Orthogonal Tri-Modal Shaping（KOTMS）：以 Kronecker 结构正交旋转重塑权重为适合三元化的三峰分布，同时通过共享旋转抑制激活异常值；Cayley 变换保证严格正交。
- 提出 Inter-Layer Aware Activation Mixed Precision（ILA-AMP）：把激活比特分配建模为带全局预算的优化问题，显式引入相邻层二阶交互成本，并用动态规划精确求解，避免少数“弱层”引发精度崩塌。
- 在 LLaMA2-7B/13B/70B、LLaMA3-8B 与 Qwen3-8B/14B/32B 上进行了系统评估，并在 Qwen3-32B-Instruct 上验证指令模型的鲁棒性；在 llama.cpp 上展示了显著的吞吐与内存收益。

## Key Contributions

- Achieves W1.58A4 (1.58-bit ternary weights and average 4-bit activations) in a pure post-training quantization (PTQ) setting, closing the gap between weight-only ternarization methods and expensive quantization-aware training (QAT) approaches such as BitNet v2.
- Proposes E2M-ATQ, a two-stage Euclidean-to-Manifold asymmetric ternary quantizer that first obtains a stable ternary pattern via Frobenius warm-start and then relocates the continuous shift/scale parameters on the calibration-induced metric manifold to minimize layer-output error.
- Proposes KOTMS, a Kronecker-structured orthogonal rotation that reshapes weights toward a ternary-friendly tri-modal distribution and, through the shared inverse rotation on activations, reduces heavy-tailed activation outliers; orthogonality is enforced via Cayley transforms.
- Proposes ILA-AMP, an activation mixed-precision allocator that models adjacent-layer second-order interactions and solves the global bit-budget problem exactly with dynamic programming, preventing accuracy cascades caused by a few weak layers.
- Evaluates extensively on LLaMA2/3 and Qwen3 families, including an instruction-tuned Qwen3-32B-Instruct, and demonstrates substantial throughput and memory gains on llama.cpp.

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** TWLA targets full-precision Transformer weights \(W \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times m}\) and aims to compress them to the ternary codebook \(\{-1, 0, +1\}\) while also quantizing activations to low bit-widths. The paper identifies three bottlenecks at W1.58A4: (1) the mismatch between near-unimodal pretrained weight distributions and the tri-modal ternary codebook, (2) the difficulty of calibrating ternary quantizers without retraining, and (3) heavy-tailed activations that dominate low-bit activation distortion.

**E2M-ATQ.** The quantizer uses an asymmetric parameterization
\(\bar{W} = \mu \mathbf{1}^\top + \mathrm{diag}(\alpha) T\), with \(T \in \{-1,0,1\}^{n \times m}\), row-wise shift \(\mu\), and scale \(\alpha\).
- *Euclidean warm-start:* It iteratively updates \(\mu\) (with residual-mean correction), \(\alpha\) via least squares, and \(T\) via thresholding to minimize the Frobenius weight reconstruction error. This yields a stable ternary pattern \(T^{(0)}\) and initialization \((\mu^{(0)}, \alpha^{(0)})\).
- *Manifold relocation:* Freezing \(T=T^{(0)}\), it relocates \((\mu, \alpha)\) by minimizing the layer-output error \(\|WX - \bar{W}X\|_F^2\). Precomputing the activation second moment \(S = \sum_b X_b^\top X_b\) turns the objective into a quadratic form, and each row gives a closed-form \(2 \times 2\) linear system for \((\mu_i, \alpha_i)\).

**KOTMS.** To reduce the geometric mismatch before hard ternary projection, KOTMS learns a Kronecker-structured orthogonal transform \(R = R_1 \otimes R_2\) with \(R_1 \in O(n_1), R_2 \in O(n_2)\). The same rotation is applied to weights during quantization and its inverse to activations during inference, preserving the full-precision mapping. The rotation is optimized with a tri-modal Gaussian-mixture shaping loss on the rotated weights, plus a zero-mode regularizer to avoid degenerate concentration at 0. Orthogonality is maintained by parameterizing each factor with a Cayley transform.

**ILA-AMP.** Because KOTMS suppresses outliers only indirectly and unevenly across layers, a few layers can become activation-quantization bottlenecks. ILA-AMP assigns each layer \(\ell\) an activation bit-width \(b_\ell \in \{2,4,6,8\}\) under a global bit budget. It uses per-token NLL on a calibration set as the value function and builds a DP-friendly second-order surrogate:
- Per-layer cost \(C_\ell(b) = v_{\text{NLL}}(b_\ell=b, b_{\neq \ell}=8) - v_{\text{NLL}}(8)\).
- Adjacent interaction cost \(K_{\ell-1,\ell}(b',b)\) measuring how quantizing two adjacent layers together amplifies error beyond their independent effects.
The objective \(\sum_\ell C_\ell(b_\ell) + \sum_{\ell \geq 2} K_{\ell-1,\ell}(b_{\ell-1},b_\ell)\) is minimized exactly by dynamic programming under the budget constraint.

**Implementation.** Calibration uses 128 WikiText2 samples of length 2048. E2M-ATQ runs 15 iterations; KOTMS optimizes for 100 iterations at learning rate 0.01. Experiments use NVIDIA A6000 GPUs; efficiency measurements use llama.cpp with native ternary kernels.

## Results & Highlights

**Pretrained models (Table 1).** On LLaMA2-7B, TWLA at W1.58A4 achieves WikiText2 perplexity 8.31 and 58.00% average zero-shot accuracy, compared with FP16 (5.47, 69.49%), QuaRot W2A4 (37.79, 30.83%), ResQ (~4.25-bit activations, 11.97, 45.23%), SliM-LLM W2A4 (~4000 PPL, 25.67%), PB-LLM W1.7A4 (458.08, 26.46%), and PT\(^2\)-LLM W1.58A4 (341.08, 27.31%). Gains are consistent across LLaMA2-13B/70B, LLaMA3-8B, and Qwen3-8B/14B/32B. On LLaMA2-70B W1.58A4, TWLA reaches 71.10% zero-shot accuracy and 4.77 PPL, exceeding 92% of FP16 and beating ResQ by 14.7 accuracy points.

**Instruction-tuned model (Table 2).** On Qwen3-32B-Instruct, TWLA W1.58A16 retains ~85–93% of FP16 performance: MMLU 75.59 vs FP16 80.75, HumanEval 42.99 vs 50.41, GSM8K 55.72 vs 66.55. At W1.58A4, it still scores 70.21/37.58/48.67, while competing methods (GPTQ, SliM-LLM, PB-LLM) at low activation bit-widths collapse to near-random on HumanEval and GSM8K.

**Ablations (Table 3).** Without activation quantization, E2M-ATQ and KOTMS each help, with E2M-ATQ contributing the larger gain; their combination performs best. Under 4-bit activations, E2M-ATQ+KOTMS alone degrades, and adding ILA-AMP recovers roughly 12% MMLU on LLaMA2-13B and Qwen3-14B, demonstrating that all three modules are necessary.

**Efficiency (Section 4.4).** On LLaMA2-13B with llama.cpp (batch 4, 128-token prefill, 256-token decode), TWLA improves throughput from 23.70 tokens/s (FP16) to 86.35 tokens/s — a 3.64× speedup over FP16 and 1.3× over QuaRot W2A4. Parameter memory falls from 23.7 GB to 3.34 GB (>80% savings) by packing five ternary values into one 8-bit integer. Quantizing LLaMA2-7B takes 82 minutes, faster than SliM-LLM (158 minutes) and only moderately slower than QuaRot.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The paper is among the first to demonstrate W1.58A4 in a PTQ setting, whereas prior joint ternary-weight/low-bit-activation work (e.g., BitNet v2) relied on expensive QAT. The three proposed components are technically distinct and together address a clear deployment bottleneck.
- **Rigor:** Generally strong. The method is described in enough mathematical detail to reproduce (closed-form updates, Cayley transforms, DP formulation), and the experiments cover multiple scales and task types. However, some comparisons mix weight-only and weight+activation baselines at different bit-widths, which can obscure direct apples-to-apples conclusions; the most fair comparisons are against PT\(^2\)-LLM at the same W1.58 and against ResQ for mixed-precision activations.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. The authors release code and report key hyperparameters (128 calibration samples, 15 E2M iterations, 100 KOTMS iterations, lr 0.01). The llama.cpp-based speed numbers depend on a specific kernel implementation; reproducing them requires that backend.
- **Significance:** Potentially significant for edge and on-device LLM deployment, where memory and throughput dominate. The accuracy retention at W1.58A4 is impressive relative to baselines, but the absolute gaps to FP16 (~5–7 zero-shot points, ~10–15% on reasoning tasks) may still rule out the most demanding applications.
- **Red flags / limitations:**
  - **Hardware-specific speedups:** The throughput gains rely on llama.cpp’s native ternary kernels; general frameworks (PyTorch, TensorRT-LLM, vLLM) may not deliver the same speedups without custom kernels.
  - **Mixed-precision activations:** “A4” is an *average* activation bit-width; some layers use 2, 6, or 8 bits. The headline “4-bit activations” is therefore slightly less aggressive than uniform 4-bit quantization.
  - **KOTMS is weight-domain driven:** Activation outlier suppression is a by-product, and ILA-AMP is needed precisely because this by-product is uneven across layers.
  - **Calibration sensitivity:** 128 samples may be small for some downstream domains; robustness to calibration data distribution is only briefly discussed.
  - **Limited task coverage:** No long-context evaluation, no multilinguality or coding beyond HumanEval/GSM8K, and instruction results are only for Qwen3-32B-Instruct.
  - **Quantization cost:** Although LLaMA2-7B quantizes in 82 minutes, the ILA-AMP surrogate requires evaluating many per-layer and adjacent-layer bit configurations, which could scale poorly without careful implementation for very large models.

## Strengths

- Tackles the end-to-end inference bottleneck by quantizing both weights and activations, not just weights.
- Modular design with well-separated, complementary roles for E2M-ATQ, KOTMS, and ILA-AMP.
- KOTMS uses a compact Kronecker-structured rotation rather than a full dense orthogonal matrix, keeping storage and compute overhead low.
- ILA-AMP explicitly models cross-layer coupling, an important effect often ignored in layer-wise PTQ.
- Strong empirical coverage across LLaMA and Qwen3 families and both pretrained and instruction-tuned settings.
- Code released and efficiency claims validated with a real low-bit inference backend (llama.cpp).

## Limitations

- Absolute accuracy still lags FP16 by a noticeable margin, especially on reasoning and code benchmarks.
- The reported speedups require specialized ternary kernels; portability to other inference engines is not guaranteed.
- “A4” is an average activation bit-width, so uniform 4-bit performance is not directly claimed.
- Activation outlier suppression is an indirect effect of KOTMS; layers that benefit less must be rescued by mixed-precision allocation.
- Calibration is limited to 128 WikiText2 samples; performance on out-of-distribution domains is not explored.
- No evaluation of long-context behavior, generation diversity, or safety-aligned instruction following under quantization.
- ILA-AMP adds a non-trivial calibration cost (many NLL evaluations) that is only partially characterized.

## Relevance

对关注大语言模型端侧部署、推理加速与模型压缩的研究者和工程师而言，TWLA 在 W1.58A4 这一极具挑战性的配置下实现了后训练量化，并在真实推理后端上验证了吞吐与内存收益，是一条值得重点关注的低比特量化路线。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does TWLA behave on long-context tasks and on model families beyond LLaMA/Qwen (e.g., Mistral, Gemma, Phi)?
- What is the accuracy–latency trade-off when TWLA is deployed on hardware without native ternary kernels, and how difficult is kernel portability?
- How sensitive are the results to calibration data size, domain, and sequence length?
- Can the inter-layer activation allocation be approximated more cheaply (e.g., via Hessian proxies) to reduce ILA-AMP calibration cost for trillion-parameter models?
- Does the method preserve safety alignment, instruction following, and multilingual capabilities under aggressive W1.58A4 quantization?
- How would a learned/non-uniform ternary codebook (more than three levels per channel) compare to the fixed \(\{-1,0,+1\}\) codebook used here?
