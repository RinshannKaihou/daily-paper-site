Reward Modeling for Multi-Agent Orchestration

Orch-RM 通过自监督方式从编排器训练日志中构造偏好对，训练 Bradley-Terry 奖励模型为编排方案打分——在测试时缩放中减少 10–60× 验证 token，准确率最高提升 8%。
multi-agent reward-modeling test-time-scaling self-supervised Rutgers · Salesforce AI
Key Finding
Scoring plans, not trajectories, captures most quality signal at a fraction of the cost—10–60× fewer verification tokens.
Key Metric
17× token reduction on BrowseComp+ (142.8M → 8.26M) while beating GPT-5-mini judge (14.0% vs 12.5%)
Why This Matters
Full sub-agent rollouts are the dominant cost in MAS training and inference. Orch-RM makes both tractable.
⚠ Critical Edge
Requires a pretrained orchestrator's training artifacts. Gains collapse on tasks with limited orchestration diversity.

How It Works

Query x
→
Orchestrator samples N plans z₁…zₙ
→
Orch-RM scores each plan r(x, z)
→
Best plan → single rollout

Preference pairs are mined automatically from existing MAS-Orchestra training logs: (1) specialized orchestrator plans beat base-model plans, (2) plans leading to correct answers beat those leading to wrong ones. A Bradley-Terry reward model is trained on these pairs—zero human annotation required.

Methodology

  • Orchestrator policy: Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct
  • Sub-agent backbone: GPT-OSS-120B
  • RM init: Skywork-Reward-LLaMA-3.1-8B
  • Bradley-Terry loss on (x, z_win, z_lose) pairs
  • Two pair sources mixed: domain-level + correctness-based
  • Applied downstream as Best-of-N selector and GRPO reward signal

Experimental Scope

  • Test-time scaling: BoN with N=8 (up to 64 on AIME)
  • Training from scratch: base Qwen → 61.67% on AIME (vs MAS-Orchestra 63.33%)
  • Continued training: only method that consistently improves over the original MAS-Orchestra checkpoint
  • Baselines: LLM-as-Judge (GPT-5-mini), off-the-shelf RMs, DPO, RFT, trajectory-level GRPO
  • Ablation: mixed data beats single-source (correctness-only or domain-only)

Strengths

  • Targets the right bottleneck—full rollouts dominate MAS cost
  • Fully self-supervised; no human preference annotations
  • Strong empirical coverage: 3 training regimes × 4 benchmarks with explicit token-cost accounting
  • Approaches or exceeds trajectory-level RL at ~10× less compute
  • Honest ablations; authors do not oversell
  • Open-source release planned

Limitations

  • Cold-start: needs a pretrained orchestrator and its training artifacts
  • Per-domain RMs; unified cross-domain model is unexplored
  • Gains shrink when orchestration diversity is low (HotpotQA, GPQA)
  • Assumes plan quality predicts answer quality—fragile if execution noise dominates
  • Absolute accuracy on BrowseComp+/HotpotQA remains low
  • No reward-model calibration or failure-mode analysis reported
