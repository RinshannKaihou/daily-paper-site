TWLA pushes LLMs to W1.58A4 — 1.58-bit ternary weights and 4-bit activations on average — through pure post-training quantization.

TWLA: Achieving Ternary Weights and Low-Bit Activations for LLMs via Post-Training Quantization

Zhixiong Zhao, Zukang Xu, Zhixuan Chen, Xing Hu, Zhe Jiang, Dawei Yang
quantization LLM inference post-training quantization ternary quantization low-bit activation

Key metric

3.64×

throughput speedup over FP16 on LLaMA2-13B — 86.35 vs 23.70 tokens/s.

Why this matters

For engineers targeting on-device LLM deployment, TWLA achieves aggressive W1.58A4 post-training quantization with verified throughput and memory gains.

Critical edge

Throughput gains rely on llama.cpp's native ternary kernels; portability to general frameworks is not guaranteed.

Methodology

E2M-ATQ

Two-stage ternary quantizer: Euclidean warm-start for a stable {-1, 0, +1} pattern, then manifold relocation of shift/scale to minimize layer-output error.

KOTMS

Kronecker-structured orthogonal rotation reshapes weights toward a ternary-friendly tri-modal distribution and dampens activation outliers via the shared inverse rotation.

ILA-AMP

Dynamic-programming allocator assigns per-layer activation bit-widths under a global budget, modeling adjacent-layer second-order interactions to stop weak-layer cascades.

Results & Highlights

  • Pretrained models: On LLaMA2-7B W1.58A4, TWLA reaches 8.31 perplexity and 58.00% zero-shot accuracy — far below QuaRot W2A4 (37.79 PPL) and PT²-LLM W1.58A4 (341.08 PPL).
  • Scale holds: LLaMA2-70B W1.58A4 hits 71.10% zero-shot accuracy and 4.77 PPL, exceeding 92% of FP16 and beating ResQ by 14.7 accuracy points.
  • Instruction model: Qwen3-32B-Instruct W1.58A16 retains ~85–93% of FP16 on MMLU, HumanEval, and GSM8K; competing methods collapse to near-random at low activation bit-widths.
  • Ablations: ILA-AMP recovers roughly 12% MMLU under 4-bit activations, showing all three modules are necessary.
  • Efficiency: LLaMA2-13B runs at 86.35 tokens/s (3.64× FP16) with parameter memory falling from 23.7 GB to 3.34 GB (>80% savings).

Strengths

  • Quantizes both weights and activations, not just weights.
  • Modular, complementary design with separated roles for each component.
  • Kronecker rotation keeps storage and compute overhead low.
  • ILA-AMP explicitly models cross-layer coupling.
  • Evaluated across LLaMA and Qwen3, pretrained and instruction-tuned.
  • Code released; efficiency claims validated on real low-bit backend.

Limitations

  • Absolute accuracy still lags FP16, especially on reasoning and code.
  • Speedups require specialized ternary kernels; portability is unclear.
  • "A4" is an average bit-width, not uniform 4-bit.
  • Activation outlier suppression is an indirect effect of KOTMS.
  • Only 128 WikiText2 calibration samples; OOD robustness unexplored.
  • No long-context, multilinguality, or safety-alignment evaluation.
