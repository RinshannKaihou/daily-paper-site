Multi-Label Test-Time Adaptation with Bayesian Conditional Priors

Qiru Li · Ao Zhou · Zhiwei Jiang · Zifeng Cheng · Cong Wang · Yafeng Yin · Qing Gu — Nanjing University

Why This Matters

Frozen VLMs score every label independently—dominant concepts suppress compatible but weaker labels. BCP makes them label-aware with a ~10-line PMI correction: no retraining, no target labels, no backprop. Negligible marginal cost.

⚠ Critical Edge

Gains are anchor-dependent: wrong anchors on small-label-set datasets (VOC) can degrade results (–3.75 mAP). Only pairwise dependencies modeled; higher-order interactions ignored. The conditional independence assumption is unvalidated.

Method — Anchor-Conditioned Bayesian Refinement

BCP reinterprets zero-shot CLIP logits as proxy marginal posteriors under a fixed image-text likelihood. The error is attributed not to the model, but to a mismatched label prior.

/* core correction — closed form */
sk* = sk + PMI(ck, ca)
= sk + log P(ck=1 | ca=1) − log P(ck=1)
1Compute zero-shot logits & Softmax posterior via frozen CLIP
2Select anchor a = argmaxk pk(t) (highest-confidence label)
3If pa > μ and t > 1: apply PMI correction using online co-occurrence stats
4Output corrected prediction; update U and m with original posterior (causal)

Design choices: single anchor only (multiple anchors degrade by mixing heterogeneous contexts); Softmax retained despite mutual-exclusivity bias (aligns with CLIP's InfoNCE pretraining, gives decisive confidence for anchor selection); default threshold μ=0.5 (robust across {0.1…0.95}).

Robustness

  • 5 random orderings: COCO2014 65.45 ± 0.05
  • Non-i.i.d. streams: −0.49 mAP only
  • Last 50% of stream: +8–11 mAP over CLIP
  • Head/medium/tail labels: gains across all groups

Anchor Reliability

  • Anchor-hit (correct): +7.6 avg mAP
  • Anchor-miss (wrong): mixed; −3.75 on VOC
  • Hit rate >89% on COCO/VOC, ~59% on NUSWIDE
  • Even wrong anchors often capture scene semantics
Core Contributions
  • Identifies label independence as the fundamental failure mode of lightweight multi-label TTA for frozen VLMs
  • Reframes the problem as label-prior correction rather than model or prompt adaptation
  • Derives a closed-form PMI correction with a clear information-theoretic interpretation
  • Develops a practical online estimator via second-order co-occurrence statistics
  • Gradient-free, no backbone tuning, no external knowledge—compatible with strict online TTA (batch=1)
  • Large, consistent gains across 5 benchmarks × 4 architectures, outperforming specialized multi-label TTA methods
Strengths
  • ~10 lines of code; runs 2–3× faster than optimization-based TTA
  • No backprop, no target annotations, no external retrieval
  • Causal update protocol prevents self-reinforcing errors
  • Theoretically grounded (Bayes + PMI)
  • Robust to ordering, non-i.i.d., and late-stream evaluation
Limitations
  • Gains concentrated in anchor-hit cases; wrong anchors hurt on small label sets
  • Only pairwise dependencies—higher-order interactions ignored
  • Conditional independence assumption P(x|ck,ca)≈P(x|ck) unvalidated
  • Softmax retention preserves the bias the method seeks to correct
  • No open-vocabulary evaluation; fixed closed-label-set only
Open Questions
How well does P(x|ck,ca)≈P(x|ck) hold? Can joint visual likelihoods relax it?
Can multi-anchor aggregation avoid mixing heterogeneous conditioning contexts?
How to stabilize online priors when the test stream is short or sparse?
Extension to open-vocabulary multi-label detection/segmentation with dynamic label sets?
