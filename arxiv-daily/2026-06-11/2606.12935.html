MARS: Margin-Adversarial Risk-controlled Stopping for Parallel LLM Test-time Scaling

Wenbo Chen (Amazon) · Puheng Li (Stanford) · Mengyang Liu (Amazon) · Weijie Su (UPenn) · Tianpei Xie (Amazon)

By probing intermediate answers inside parallel reasoning traces and bounding the worst-case future vote movement, MARS certifies when it is safe to stop early — saving 25–47% of self-consistency tokens without losing accuracy.

Early-stop via margin adversarial risk beats consensus heuristics
Reframes "when to stop" as "can the leader's margin absorb all future adversarial flips" — a provable safety condition.
25–47% token savings
Accuracy change ≤ 0.6pp across 9 model-dataset settings. Occasionally improves accuracy up to +0.8pp.
Cuts inference cost of parallel test-time scaling
Directly reduces compute, latency, and energy of large reasoning models — applicable on top of any majority-vote pipeline.
Formal guarantees need true switch probabilities
In practice, uses estimated q̂ and γ < 1, making the safety guarantee empirical rather than formal. Evaluated only on math benchmarks.

How It Works

  • Parallel traces — launch N reasoning paths simultaneously; normally you'd run all to completion and majority-vote.
  • Probing — at checkpoints (every 2 048 tokens), truncate a trace, append a "stop-thinking" prompt, read the answer, then resume the original trace undisturbed.
  • Margin check — leader L has vote margin Mk over each challenger k. Compute worst-case "flip cost" cjk per running trace.
  • Stop rule — if Mk ≥ Σ q̂j · cjk(γ) for every challenger (including an unseen-answer sentinel ⊥), declare L the winner and stop.
  • γ calibration — 16 warmup traces fit per-question logistic regression + Platt scaling; γ contraction relaxes conservatism while tracking empirical safety.

Headline Numbers

25–47% token savings (SC) 14–29% additional savings (DCO)
≤ 0.6pp accuracy delta +0.8pp best gain
  • Tested on AIME 2025, HMMT, BRUMO 2025 with DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-8B, Qwen3-32B, Qwen3-next.
  • 4 096 traces per question pre-generated; simulated 512-trace parallel runs, 64 bootstrap iterations.
  • 5-feature logistic model (checkpoint position, probe confidence, flip count, stability streak, confidence trend) is within 1–4pp of oracle.

Parallel-Probe's consensus heuristic either saves tokens but destroys accuracy, or preserves accuracy but barely saves anything. MARS achieves both simultaneously across all 9 settings.

Core Contributions

  • Transforms parallel decoding from opaque to observable via non-destructive intermediate probing.
  • Introduces the margin-adversarial framing — safety = "leader's margin can absorb all future adversarial flips."
  • Decomposes uncertainty into learnable trace-level switch probabilities + adversarial destination uncertainty via γ.
  • Proves a high-probability safety guarantee (Theorem 2.4 / Corollary 2.5) under conservative conditions.

Strengths & Limitations

  • Modular — stacks on any majority-vote or confidence-weighted pipeline.
  • Lightweight — 5-feature logistic model adds <1ms per question.
  • γ calibration elegantly bypasses the hard problem of modeling flip destinations.
  • Requires 16 warmup traces run to full budget per question.
  • Evaluated only on competition math; code, agent tasks, multimodal still open.
  • Constant-weight assumption is only approximate for position-dependent schemes.

Open Questions

Can probing intervals become adaptive — denser when margins are tight, sparser when the lead is safe?
Can warmup traces be reduced via cross-question or cross-model transfer learning?
Do flip dynamics in code generation or long-horizon planning differ fundamentally from math reasoning?
Could a Bayesian treatment of flip destinations replace the γ black-box compression?
