Weight-Space Geometry · Optimization

Different Layers, Different Manifolds

In GPT-2 small pretraining, assigning Stiefel manifold constraints to attention layers and DGram constraints to MLP layers yields the best stable validation loss — while placing DGram on attention provokes singular-value explosion and softmax collapse.

Kirato Yoshihara · Osaka Univ. ICML 2026 Workshop on Weight-Space Symmetries 2026-06-11 arXiv 2606.13276 ↗
Key Finding
Transformer modules demand different weight-space geometries: spectral-bounded (Stiefel) for attention, scale-free (DGram) for MLP. Uniform constraints are suboptimal.

Why DGram Breaks Attention

Stiefel manifolds enforce WTW = I — all singular values clamped to 1, spectral norm bounded. DGram only enforces column orthogonality, leaving singular values free.

Instability chain

  • DGram allows singular values of Q/K projections to grow unchecked
  • Growing spectral norm → amplified attention logits
  • Large logits → softmax saturates into near-one-hot
  • Saturated softmax → vanishing Jacobian → gradient collapse

Why MLP tolerates DGram

GELU's element-wise nonlinearity + LayerNorm + residual connections absorb scale changes. The MLP path is inherently more robust to unconstrained spectral growth.

Experimental Setup

  • Model: GPT-2 small · 124M params · 12L × 12H × 768d · ctx 1024
  • Data: OpenWebText, autoregressive LM
  • Training: 50K iterations · batch 64 · bfloat16 · grad clip 1.0 · seed 1337
  • Optimizer: Manifold Muon (2D weights) + AdamW (embeddings, biases, norms)
  • Schedule: 1K warmup + cosine decay · Muon LR 0.02 · AdamW LR 6×10⁻⁴
  • Manifold Muon: Dual ascent (K=20, α=0.1) on tangent space, retraction via polar decomposition (Stiefel) or SVD (DGram)

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean experimental design isolating geometry's causal effect via shared hyperparameters
  • Mechanistic explanation (singular-value growth → softmax saturation) supported by Proposition 4.2
  • Directly challenges the implicit "uniform constraint" assumption in current Manifold Muon work
  • Code open-sourced for community reproduction

Limitations

  • Single scale (124M), single seed — no statistical error bounds
  • DGram attention instability may be recoverable with per-module tuning; not tested
  • Proposition 4.2 is existential (degradation is possible), not constructive (happens necessarily)
  • No comparison with AdamW, Shampoo, Sophia, or other optimizer baselines
  • Workshop paper — limited peer review depth

Open Questions

Does HETERO's edge persist at 1B+ scale across multiple seeds?
Can meta-learning or automated search discover optimal per-module manifolds?
Would adding an explicit spectral-norm penalty or weight decay stabilize DGram attention?
Do module-level geometry preferences transfer to encoders, MoE, or state-space models?
Can training-time logit gap / softmax entropy / Jacobian norms validate the mechanism directly?
What other manifolds (Grassmann, product manifolds) suit normalization or embedding layers?
