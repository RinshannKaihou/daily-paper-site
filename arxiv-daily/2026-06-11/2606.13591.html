Multiagent Protocols with Aggregated
Confidence Signals

Ali Elahi & Barbara Di Eugenio · University of Illinois Chicago
针对两智能体辩论场景，提出三种置信度驱动的聚合协议（WSV、CGA、HID），将跨模型原始置信度融合为系统级单一置信度，显著提升置信度区分能力并恢复标准辩论在模糊任务上的性能损失。
Key Finding
Three lightweight protocols transform per-agent confidence into a single system-level signal—improving discrimination and recovering the F1 losses that plain multiagent debate causes on ambiguous tasks.
Key Metric
+9.58 AUARC Best gain (CGA, EZStance, self-report). Sequence-probability CGA hits +8.50 on BoolQ. Gains are consistent across 5 benchmarks, 6 model pairs, 14 open-source LLMs.
Why This Matters
Multi-LLM systems lack a reliable "how sure is the system?" signal. Aggregated confidence enables selective answering, smarter routing, and human oversight—knowing when to trust is as important as accuracy itself.
Critical Edge
Only 2 agents, 1 debate round tested. Bayesian fusion assumes conditional independence that is explicitly violated. Evaluated solely on discrete-answer tasks—open-ended generation and math remain unknown territory.

Methodology Pipeline

Zero-shot prediction + confidence → 1-round debate (peer reasoning visible) → Post-debate prediction + confidence → Calibration (Beta / Polynomial / Isotonic) → Protocol aggregation → single answer + single confidence

Confidence estimators: sequence probability (mean token log-likelihood with content-free prior correction) vs. self-report (model verbally rates certainty, averaged over 3 repetitions).

Protocol optimization: TPE with early stopping, jointly minimizing NLL + ECE + L2 regularization.

Bayesian fusion: under conditional independence, agreeing streams fuse as c₁c₂ / [c₁c₂ + (1−c₁)(1−c₂)].

Protocol Comparison

ProtocolStrategyBest forMechanism
WSV Soft weighted vote over all 4 streams F1 recovery · stabilizes accuracy Learned logit weights → soft vote; uses every stream as evidence
CGA Confidence-gated switching + Bayesian fusion AUARC · best discrimination Agent switches to debate answer only if confidence improves past threshold; surviving streams fused
HID Human-inspired deterministic routing Balanced AUARC + F1 Routes by agreement × confidence levels (agree/disagree × high/low); Bayesian fusion for confidence

F1 recovery: Plain MAD degrades F1 on 4 of 5 benchmarks (BoolQ −5.53, Vast −6.02, EZStance −8.37, HatEval −3.25). Confidence protocols turn most losses into gains.

Self-report insight: Sequence probability tends to increase after debate even when the answer becomes incorrect. Self-report changes are smaller and more aligned with actual correctness.

Strengths

  • Identifies a clearly neglected problem: system-level confidence for multiagent LLM systems
  • Lightweight protocols that layer on top of existing MAD implementations
  • Extensive empirical design: 14 models, 5 benchmarks, 4 task types, 6 debating pairs
  • Demonstrates both improved discrimination and F1 recovery simultaneously
  • Interpretable analysis of confidence dynamics during debate

Limitations

  • Only 2 agents and 1 debate round validated—HID would need redesign for larger ensembles
  • Evaluated solely on discrete-answer tasks; open-ended generation and math are untested
  • Correlated errors and adversarial settings not studied
  • Computational cost and latency not analyzed (multiple inference passes required)
  • Conditional-independence assumption in Bayesian fusion is violated—justification is approximate

Open Questions

  • How do WSV, CGA, HID scale to 3+ agents? Does Bayesian-fusion advantage persist?
  • Can these protocols extend to open-ended text, code generation, or multi-step reasoning?
  • What is the inference-time cost vs. single-agent and standard MAD?
  • Robustness when agents share model families or training data (correlated errors)?
  • Would external verifiers or retrieval-augmented evidence further improve aggregated confidence?
  • How does increasing debate rounds interact with confidence dynamics?
