Reasoning as Pattern Matching: Shared Mechanisms in Human and LLM Everyday Reasoning

Zach Studdiford & Gary Lupyan · University of Wisconsin–Madison

Humans and LLMs make the same content-dependent errors in causal reasoning — and the attention heads that drive model outputs also predict human mistakes, suggesting both systems reason via pattern matching rather than abstract world models.

Key Finding
Error profiles of 142 humans and 25 LLMs align at category and item level — the most human-aligned model (gemma-3-27b) correlates at r = 0.84.
Key Metric
76%
Human variance explained by attention-head activations on minimally different prompts (overall ΔR² = 0.054)
Why This Matters
Many "failures" used to argue LLMs can't reason also appear in humans — content-sensitive errors are not unique to AI.
Critical Edge
Circuit analysis limited to 2 models (gemma-2-27b, gemma-3-27b). Human-side link is correlational — shared mechanism is inferred, not proven.
Results at a Glance
Frontier LLMs outperform humans in absolute accuracy DeepSeek-R1 μ=0.90 · GPT-5.2 μ=0.87 · Human μ=0.71 p < 0.001
Most human-aligned model: gemma-3-27b r = 0.84 · R² = 0.28 β=0.067, p<.001
Critical heads more sensitive to non-critical content than structural changes median Cohen's d = 0.437 all p < 0.001
Head activations predict human accuracy above controls ΔR² = 0.054 F(11,407)=3.36, p<.001
Minimal-difference prompts: head activations explain human variance up to 76% R² ΔR² = 0.073 above logits
Human errors are systematic, not random — retest cohort (n=70) r = 0.60 · OR = 3.84× 95% CI [2.92, 5.07]
Methodology
  • 11-category behavioral benchmark: state/position changes, geocentric/egocentric relations, target vs. context framings
  • 142 human participants (Cloud Research prescreened); 8,910 prompt-level observations with reaction times
  • 25 LLMs evaluated — logit difference for open models, binary accuracy for proprietary
  • Causal attention-head ablation on gemma-2-27b / gemma-3-27b → top-k heads (BIC-selected, typically k=10–11)
  • Activation patching: 3 structurally critical + 3 non-critical content substitutions per prompt
  • Linear regressions predict human accuracy from head entropy, controlling for trigram freq, GPT-2 & BERT surprisal
Key Contributions
  • Reverses the usual approach: first isolates the mechanism causally responsible for LLM outputs, then tests whether it predicts human behavior
  • Reveals that frontier models (DeepSeek-R1, GPT-5.2) are more accurate but less human-aligned — scale/post-training may push models away from human heuristics
  • Shows "fail states" are fragile: changing one word drops human accuracy from 0.93→0.77 and model accuracy from 0.78→0.46
  • Controls for associationist explanations via trigram frequency and smaller-LM surprisal — predictive signal survives
Strengths
  • Bridges cognitive psychology and AI interpretability with a mechanism-level hypothesis
  • Multiple replication and control conditions; leave-one-out predictions and permutation tests
  • Clearly distinguishes abstract world-model vs. pattern-matching accounts with an empirical test
  • Reframes LLM reasoning failures by showing identical errors in humans
Limitations
  • Circuit identification restricted to 2 Gemma models; other architectures untested
  • Human data from English-speaking MTurk workers only
  • Short, two-option scenarios — may not extend to open-ended or multi-step reasoning
  • Proprietary models evaluated with coarser binary accuracy, no activation access
研究价值 / Relevance

本文对 LLM 推理评估、认知科学以及人机比较研究都有直接参考价值：它提醒我们不能简单地把"内容敏感性错误"当作 LLM 不懂推理的证据，因为人类在类似的日常因果推理任务中也会犯同样的内容依赖性错误；同时，它展示了用机制可解释性工具来检验人类认知假说的可能性。

Open Questions
  • Do the same content-sensitive circuits exist in LLaMA, Qwen, or GPT-style architectures at similar layers?
  • Can causal interventions on these heads make LLM responses more (or less) human-like — and does that improve robustness?
  • How do results change with chain-of-thought reasoning or explicit world-model training?
  • Do individual differences in human reasoning strategy map onto different attention-head activation patterns?
  • Can the pattern-matching account extend to formal domains (logic, math, scientific reasoning)?
  • Does gemma-3-27b's human alignment reflect genuine cognitive similarity or shared training-data biases?
