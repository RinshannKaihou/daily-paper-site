Key Finding
Replacing hard one-hot targets with a bimodal Gaussian over classifier logits reshapes the aggregate output distribution, implicitly encoding class ambiguity and suppressing overfitting—yielding dramatic gains in data-scarce regimes.
Why This Matters
A plug-in loss that makes medical-image classification robust when annotations are scarce—no architecture changes, no extra data, no augmentation tricks required.
⚠ Critical Edge
No error bars or significance tests reported. Underperforms CE on Caltech256 (256 classes). "Robustness" is untested under adversarial attack, distribution shift, or label noise.

Strengths
  • Intuitive logit-space framing with clear bimodal visualization
  • Two complementary terms: parametric stability + non-parametric flexibility
  • Strong gains precisely where overfitting hurts most—low data
  • Random-noise experiment proves resistance to memorizing spurious mappings
  • Architecture-agnostic, plug-in replacement for CE
  • Public code repository for replication
Limitations
  • No softmax → no calibrated probabilities without extra step
  • Five interacting hyperparameters (m, σ_target, λ, NN-k, KDE-h)
  • Underperforms CE on Caltech256 (256 classes)—scalability unknown
  • No error bars, no significance tests—reliability of small gains unclear
  • "Robustness" untested against adversarial attacks or distribution shift
  • Non-parametric estimator needs large enough batch groups
