> **一句话总结：** 本文提出基于轨迹的量化敏感度分数（TQS），将时间序列模型的推理 rollout 建模为离散时间动力系统，用有限时间内的输出轨迹发散率来先验地衡量各层对量化的敏感程度，并据此构建无需校准数据的混合精度后训练量化框架 TQS-PTQ，在 Aurora-small、TimesFM-2.5 和 Pangu-Weather 上显著扩展了精度–压缩前沿。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes the Trajectory-based Quantization Sensitivity Score (TQS), which models a time-series model's inference rollout as a discrete-time dynamical system and measures each layer's quantization sensitivity as the finite-horizon growth rate of output trajectory divergence. Building on TQS, TQS-PTQ is a calibration-free mixed-precision post-training quantization framework that substantially extends the accuracy–compression frontier on Aurora-small, TimesFM-2.5, and Pangu-Weather.

## 核心贡献

- **动力系统视角：** 将网络的前向 rollout 视为离散时间动力系统 $x_{t+1}=F_\theta(x_t)$，把量化看作对参数的有界扰动，从而用稳定性理论分析误差在多步预测中的传播与放大。
- **TQS 敏感度分数：** 对每一个可量化权重张量，仅替换该张量为量化版本，计算完整预测轨迹与全精度轨迹的平方偏差，并归一化到量化扰动的 Frobenius 范数，得到对数量化敏感度：
  \[
  \gamma^{\text{task}}(\ell)=\frac{1}{T_{\max}}\ln\left(\frac{1}{|S|}\sum_{s}\frac{\|\Delta \hat{Y}_s^{(\ell)}\|_2^2}{\|\delta\theta_\ell^{\text{quant}}\|_F^2+\varepsilon}\right).
  \]
  高 $\gamma$ 层在 rollout 中被放大，需要更高精度；低 $\gamma$ 层可被激进压缩。
- **与量化器解耦：** 通过引入高斯探针 $\gamma^{\text{gauss}}$（用同 Frobenius 范数的各向同性高斯噪声替代量化残差），使敏感度估计不依赖于具体位宽或量化方案，支持黑盒/编译/冻结 ONNX 模型的量化预算规划。
- **TQS-PTQ 框架：** 基于 TQS 排名，通过多选择背包问题（MCKP）或贪心策略，在全局存储预算下为各层分配混合精度，无需校准数据、无需 Hessian、无需二阶近似。
- **实证发现：** 在三个时序/气象基础模型上，TQS-PTQ 在重压缩区域显著优于均匀 RTN、GPTQ、GPTAQ、QEP 等基线；敏感度集中在输入/输出边界，而非 LLM 中常见的 FFN-down 投影。

## Key Contributions

- **Dynamical-systems reframing:** Treats the network's autoregressive rollout as a discrete-time map $x_{t+1}=F_\theta(x_t)$ and quantization as a bounded parameter perturbation, enabling stability-theoretic analysis of error propagation over the forecast horizon.
- **TQS sensitivity score:** For each quantizable weight tensor, replaces only that tensor with its quantized counterpart and measures the finite-time growth rate of output prediction-space divergence, normalized by the perturbation magnitude. Layers with high $\gamma$ are protected; low-$\gamma$ layers are aggressively compressed.
- **Decoupling from quantizer choice:** A Gaussian probe variant $\gamma^{\text{gauss}}$ uses isotropic Gaussian noise matched to the Frobenius norm of a reference quantization residual, allowing a priori sensitivity estimation independent of bit-width or quantization scheme.
- **TQS-PTQ framework:** Uses TQS rankings to assign mixed precision under a global storage budget via a multiple-choice knapsack (MCKP) or greedy allocator, requiring no calibration data, Hessians, or second-order approximations.
- **Empirical results:** Demonstrates large accuracy–compression gains over uniform RTN, GPTQ, GPTAQ, and QEP on Aurora-small, TimesFM-2.5, and Pangu-Weather, and shows that sensitivity concentrates at the input/output boundary rather than in FFN-down projections as in LLMs.

## Methodology

TQS is motivated by the observation that time-series foundation models (weather forecasters, general time-series forecasters) are autoregressive dynamical systems: small weight perturbations can compound across rollout steps and spatial dimensions. Standard layer-wise PTQ methods optimize single-step reconstruction and do not account for this temporal amplification.

**TQS score computation.** For a full-precision model $f_\theta$ and a context window $x_{s:s+C-1}$, the nominal trajectory is $\hat{Y}_s^{(0)}=f_\theta(x_{s:s+C-1})$. For each quantizable tensor $\ell$, the paper applies a perturbation $\delta\theta_\ell$ (either the quantization residual $Q_b(\theta_\ell)-\theta_\ell$ or Gaussian noise of matched norm) to obtain $\hat{Y}_s^{(\ell)}=f_{\theta+\delta\theta_\ell}(x_{s:s+C-1})$. The single-context score is
\[
\gamma_\ell(T)=\frac{1}{T}\ln\left(\frac{\|\hat{Y}_{s,1:T}^{(\ell)}-\hat{Y}_{s,1:T}^{(0)}\|_2^2}{\|\delta\theta_\ell\|_F^2+\varepsilon}\right).
\]
A task-level variant averages squared trajectory divergences over $|S|$ independent context windows before taking the log (Equation 1 in the paper). Probe horizons are model-specific: $T_{\max}=100$ for TimesFM-2.5, $120$ steps (30 days) for Aurora-small, and $4$ steps (24 h) for Pangu-Weather.

**TQS-PTQ allocator.** Given per-layer scores $\gamma_\ell$, parameter counts $n_\ell$, a target compression ratio $C$, a tier set $\{(t_k,b_k)\}$, an FP32 budget fraction $p_{\text{FP32}}$, and a dead-layer threshold $\gamma_{\min}$, the allocator: (1) converts $C$ into a bit budget; (2) assigns sentinel/dead layers (low $\gamma$) to the lowest tier; (3) sorts remaining layers by decreasing $\gamma$; (4) fills the FP32 mass up to $p_{\text{FP32}}$; (5) solves a multiple-choice knapsack problem (or greedy promotion) for the remaining layers under the residual budget. The ranking is computed once and reused across many compression targets.

**Probe variants and ablations.** The paper compares quantization-residual probes ($\gamma^{\text{quant}}$) against Gaussian probes ($\gamma^{\text{gauss}}$), greedy vs. MCKP allocation, bottom tiers INT1/INT2/INT4, and FP32 budgets $0.02$ and $0.10$.

## Results & Highlights

**Models and datasets.** Experiments cover:
- *Aurora-small* (113M parameters) on ERA5 reanalysis, 30-day/120-step autoregressive rollout.
- *TimesFM-2.5* (200M) on ETTh1/2, ETTm1/2, EXCHANGE, and WEATHER, 500-step rollout.
- *Pangu-Weather 6h* (277M, frozen ONNX export) on ERA5, single 6-hour step.

**Accuracy–compression frontier.**
- On Aurora-small, TQS-PTQ reaches $\geq 32\times$ compression at $\leq 1\%$ ERA5-MAE degradation, and TQS-gauss wins all nine evaluated variables at the W2-equivalent grid.
- On TimesFM-2.5, TQS-PTQ reaches $\sim 16\times$ compression and wins 46 of 57 variable–dataset cells at the W2 grid against uniform baselines, with median MAE 1.63 vs. 13.20 for the strongest uniform baseline on the weather dataset.
- On Pangu-Weather, TQS-PTQ achieves on-disk $C=1.67\times$ (block-level $3.57\times$) with only $1.2\%$ mean per-variable MAE degradation, winning all nine variables against the strongest uniform W8 baseline.
- In total, TQS-PTQ wins 64 of 75 variable–model comparisons.

**Computational cost.** Because one sensitivity sweep is reused across compression targets, TQS-PTQ is faster per Pareto point than calibration-based baselines: $\sim$46 min/point on TimesFM-2.5 (vs. 3.5–3.9 h for GPTQ/GPTAQ/QEP), $\sim$32 min/point on Aurora-small (vs. 38–54 min), and $\sim$57.5 min/point on Pangu-Weather (vs. 66.9 min for GPTAQ-W8).

**Structural findings.**
- Sensitivity is moderately heavy-tailed: top 10% of layers carry 17–24% of cumulative $\gamma$-shift, top 25% carry roughly 40%.
- Sensitivity concentrates at the I/O boundary: input tokenizers/embeddings and output prediction heads are the most sensitive, while backbone self-attention and interior FFN blocks are much less sensitive. This contrasts with LLM PTQ literature where FFN-down projections are often the bottleneck.
- Cross-dataset ranking stability on TimesFM-2.5 is strong (mean Spearman $\rho=0.82$), so a single sensitivity sweep transfers across datasets.
- Gaussian and quantization probes disagree at fine tensor granularity but converge at block granularity (Pangu $\rho=0.96$); the resulting mixed-precision frontiers are nearly identical.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The dynamical-systems reframing of PTQ for autoregressive time-series models is conceptually fresh and well-motivated. Decoupling sensitivity estimation from quantizer choice is a genuine methodological advance, especially for black-box or compiled models.
- **Rigor:** Generally strong, with careful ablations (probe type, allocator, bottom tier, FP32 budget, horizon), bootstrap confidence intervals on Aurora, and per-variable breakdowns. However, the headline claim that TQS-PTQ is "robust, high-performing" is strongest at *heavy* compression; at lighter W3-equivalent compression it is not always the best (e.g., TimesFM W3 grid, Aurora W3 grid), which the authors acknowledge.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate. The method is described in detail (Algorithms 1–2, Appendix A), and no calibration data are required. However, the paper does not mention a public code release at the time of writing, and some implementation details (e.g., exact MCKP solver, ONNX wrapper cap, QEP failure diagnosis) are needed for exact reproduction.
- **Significance:** High for operational deployment of large weather and time-series foundation models on resource-constrained hardware. The finding that I/O boundaries dominate sensitivity provides actionable guidance for practitioners.
- **Limitations and red flags:**
  - *Pangu-Weather* deployability is limited by the `onnx2torch` rewrite: only FP32/BF16/INT8 are stable, so the reported 1.67× on-disk compression is modest and partly an artifact of the export pipeline.
  - *QEP* fails on Pangu with NaN activations, which complicates baseline comparisons.
  - *TQS-quant* underperforms on Aurora; the headline results use *TQS-gauss*, so the superiority of the Gaussian probe is model-dependent.
  - The dead-layer rule and bottom-tier choice (INT1 vs. INT2 vs. INT4) are consequential; INT1 catastrophically polarizes TimesFM-2.5, indicating the framework is not entirely hyperparameter-free.
  - The paper evaluates weight quantization; activation quantization is mentioned but not systematically studied.

## Strengths

- Elegant theoretical framing that naturally explains why autoregressive time-series models need rollout-aware sensitivity metrics.
- Calibration-free, gradient-free, Hessian-free: applicable to frozen ONNX exports and compiled graphs.
- Strong empirical gains at aggressive compression ratios, with one sensitivity sweep amortized over many targets.
- Rich diagnostic analysis: I/O-boundary sensitivity, heavy-tailed but non-outlier distribution, Gaussian-vs-quant probe agreement, cross-dataset transfer, and comparison to Hessian-based scores.
- Honest reporting of negative results (W3 grid losses, QEP failure, INT1 collapse), which strengthens credibility.

## Limitations

- Mixed-precision deployment is constrained by the available export toolchain (`onnx2torch`), especially for Pangu-Weather.
- The Gaussian probe, while decoupled from quantizer choice, is model-dependent: it is the operative variant for Aurora but not obviously superior everywhere.
- At moderate compression (W3-equivalent) uniform baselines such as GPTQ-W3 and QEP-W3 can win, so the gains are most pronounced at heavy compression.
- Requires choosing a bottom tier, FP32 fraction, and probe horizon; these are model-specific and need ablation.
- No activation-quantization experiments; memory and latency gains are therefore reported in terms of weight storage rather than full inference acceleration.
- Source code availability is not stated in the paper.

## Relevance

对于从事时间序列基础模型、气象预报模型或边缘设备部署的研究者与工程师，本文提供了一种新的量化思路：与其单独优化每一层的重构误差，不如把模型看作动力系统，衡量权重扰动在多步预测中的放大效应。TQS-PTQ 无需校准数据即可为混合精度位宽分配提供可解释的依据，特别适合黑盒 ONNX 模型、数据隐私受限或计算资源受限的场景。不过，实际部署时仍需结合具体导出工具链和激活量化需求来评估真实加速与压缩收益。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does TQS-PTQ perform with joint weight-and-activation quantization, and does the I/O-boundary sensitivity pattern still hold for activations?
2. Can the TQS ranking be computed efficiently enough for very large models (e.g., billion-parameter weather foundation models) and can it be extended to multi-GPU or distributed settings?
3. How does TQS compare to layer-wise Hessian methods across a broader set of architectures, including Mamba, RNNs, and causal Transformers outside weather/time-series domains?
4. Is the code and full experimental configuration publicly available, and can the reported Pangu-Weather results be reproduced without the `onnx2torch` limitation?
5. Can TQS be used proactively during architecture search or training to design more quantization-friendly time-series models?
