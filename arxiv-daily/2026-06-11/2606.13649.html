Operadic Consistency: a label-free signal for compositional reasoning failures in LLMs

Nathaniel Bottman · Yinhong Liu · Kyle Richardson · 2026-06-11

基于 operad 理论提出 Operadic Consistency，通过比较大模型对组合问题的直接回答与按显式分解逐步组合的回答是否一致，来无标签地检测推理失败。

Three model calls, no labels, no training. Compare an LLM's direct answer to its own step-by-step decomposed answer. If they disagree, the model probably failed at compositional reasoning. This simple check — grounded in operad algebra — correlates with accuracy at r = 0.86–0.94 and beats chain-of-thought self-consistency at equal inference cost.

Key Metric
r = 0.86–0.94 Pearson
OC-rate vs. accuracy across 4 multi-hop QA datasets and 12 instruction-tuned LLMs (4B–671B). Only signal with r ≥ 0.85 on every dataset.
Why This Matters
A mathematically grounded, no-ground-truth confidence signal for knowing when LLMs fail at multi-step reasoning. 3 inference calls, zero labels. Ready for selective prediction, human-review triggers, and capability audits.
Critical Edge
DROP correlation collapses to r = +0.37 (n.s.) under canonical surface-form scoring. Only depth-2 chains tested. Correlation may conflate reasoning quality with surface-level answer stability.
How OC Works — 3 Calls, 1 Score
Call 1 · Direct
m(Q) → adirect
→
Call 2 · Sub-answer
m(Q₁) → a₁
→
Call 3 · Composed
m(Q₂[a₁]) → acomp
⟹
OC Score
agree(adirect, acomp)

The "questions operad" Q treats question templates with blanks as operations; substitution of sub-answers is operadic composition. OC checks whether the LLM's Q-algebra is consistent — disagreement on any tree signals a compositional reasoning failure.

Assessment
Strengths
  • Theoretically principled — operads formalize tree-shaped substitution
  • Broad empirical sweep: 17 models, 4 datasets
  • Fair cost matching — 3-call budget
  • Detailed ablations + honest failure-mode examples
Limitations
  • Depth-2 chains only; deeper trees untested
  • DROP: r = +0.37 under canonical scoring (not significant)
  • Needs annotated decompositions or reliable CoT extractor
  • No GPT-5 / Claude Opus 4.x / Gemini 2.5 Pro in non-thinking cohort
Thinking Models Extension
  • Sub-question chains extracted from the model's own reasoning trace via a fixed Llama-3.3-70B extractor
  • Non-factorable traces discarded (~5–15%)
  • OC remains significant on 3 of 4 datasets (p < 10−18 pooled)
  • Positive selective-prediction lifts on all 16 (dataset × budget × metric) cells
Open Questions
  1. Does the signal strengthen with depth ≥ 3 trees to justify extra inference cost?
  2. Can lightweight automatic decomposition make OC practical for open-domain QA?
  3. How does OC interact with reasoning length on very large frontier systems?
  4. Would combining OC with in-domain PRMs yield a calibrated confidence stack?
  5. Can the operadic framework extend to proof verification, code synthesis, or structured planning?
