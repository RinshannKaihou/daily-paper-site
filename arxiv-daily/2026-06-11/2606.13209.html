Understanding Helpfulness and Harmlessness Tension in Reward Models

54 reward models, causal ablations, and a single number that explains why mixed-objective alignment keeps failing: ~50% of objective-oriented neurons are shared between competing goals.

Eshaan Tanwar · Pepa Atanasova Univ. of Copenhagen 2026-06-11 reward-models rlhf alignment mechanistic-interpretability
Key Finding
Helpfulness & harmlessness rely on heavily overlapping late-layer neurons. The shared circuitry — not external trade-offs — drives alignment tension.
Key Metric
~4.75%
of total neurons are shared, yet ablating them drops harmlessness by 10 pp and helpfulness by 4.4 pp.
Why This Matters
Multi-objective alignment is a circuit-level interference problem. Future methods must decouple internal representations, not just balance rewards.
Critical Edge
Only models ≤ 1.7B on HH-RLHF. Scaling to 7B+ is unproven. Single-dataset; single threshold (τ = 10). No SAE decoding of shared neuron semantics.

⚙ Methodology

DataHH-RLHF (helpfulness + harmlessness subsets)
ModelsSmoLLM2 (1.7B / 360M / 135M), GPT-2 (L / M / S) × 3 seeds
TrainingFull fine-tune, lr 5e-5, no PEFT; single-obj 2 epochs, mixed 1 epoch
ScoringMagnitude-based neuron importance — top τ=10 percentile → ablation
AblationZero-out target neuron activations at inference; measure Δ accuracy
BenchRewardBench, RewardBench2, RM-Bench (safety + helpfulness splits)

⚠ Critical Assessment

  • Model scale ceiling at 1.7B — behavior at 7B+ is speculative
  • Single dataset (HH-RLHF); other preference corpora untested
  • Neuron selection threshold τ=10 not sensitivity-analyzed
  • No sparse autoencoder (SAE) decoding of shared neuron semantics
  • Full fine-tune only — LoRA / PEFT representation structure may differ
  • Does not cover policy optimization stage (PPO / DPO / RLOO)

✓ Strengths

  • Bridges behavioral alignment tension with mechanistic neural evidence
  • Strict experimental control: same arch, same init, only data differs
  • Small fraction of shared neurons → large effect: actionable design insight
  • Lightweight method (activation magnitude) scales to larger models easily
  • Cross-family replication (SmoLLM2 + GPT-2) with 3 random seeds each

◆ Core Insight

  • Mixed-objective RMs consistently underperform single-objective counterparts
  • Helpful RM sometimes beats Harmless RM on safety tasks — training signal cross-pollination
  • Reward scores in mixed RMs cluster near zero: compressed confidence margins
  • Adversarial tasks (LLM Bar, Focus, Factuality) show lowest retention rates
  • Objective-oriented neurons concentrate in late layers → semantic processing
Open Questions
Does shared-neuron overlap remain the primary tension source at 7B–70B+ scale?
Can SAEs disentangle helpfulness vs. harmlessness semantics within shared neurons?
Would orthogonal regularization, modular routing, or multi-task isolation reduce interference?
Do HelpSteer, UltraFeedback, or Skywork datasets reproduce the same overlap pattern?
Does alignment tension amplify or relax when propagated through PPO/DPO policy optimization?
