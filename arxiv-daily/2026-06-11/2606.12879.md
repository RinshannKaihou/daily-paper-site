> **一句话总结：** 本文提出“扩散网络对齐”问题，将独立级联模型生成的有根扩散树与相关参照网络进行顶点匹配，并设计了一种多项式时间的双向传播算法，在稀疏相关Erdős-Rényi图下给出逐顶点正确匹配的显式深度相关概率下界。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces diffusion-network alignment, where a rooted diffusion tree produced by an Independent Cascade process on one correlated network must be aligned to a second observed network. It proposes a polynomial-time two-pass algorithm based on tree correlation tests, and proves explicit, depth-dependent lower bounds on the per-vertex correct-matching probability in the sparse correlated Erdős-Rényi regime.

## 核心贡献

- 定义“扩散网络对齐”问题：在仅能观测到由独立级联（IC）模型产生的扩散树、而另一相关网络完全可观测的不对称设定下，恢复扩散树顶点与参照网络顶点之间的对应关系。
- 提出一种多项式时间算法：通过“向上传播”与“向下传播”两个阶段，在扩散树上聚合局部树相关性测试证据，逐步确认匹配。
- 引入四种匹配准则（Criteria 1–4），综合利用子树相关性测试、子节点匹配信息以及父节点匹配信息，实现根节点与非根节点的对齐。
- 在稀疏相关Erdős-Rényi图模型下，证明算法具有“全局正确性”——以高概率，每个顶点的候选集要么为空，要么仅包含真实对应顶点。
- 对扩散树上每个顶点建立显式正确概率下界 $p_{d'}-o(1)$，其中 $d'$ 与顶点到叶子的距离有关；越靠近根的顶点，下界越大。

## Key Contributions

- Defines the **diffusion-network alignment** problem, motivated by contact tracing, communication traces, and blockchain transaction histories. Unlike classic network alignment, only one side (the diffusion tree) is fully observed, capturing information asymmetry.
- Proposes a **polynomial-time two-pass algorithm** (upward then downward) that propagates local evidence from tree correlation tests along the diffusion tree.
- Introduces **four matching criteria** combining subtree likelihood-ratio tests with matched-child/parent evidence, enabling alignment of both root and non-root vertices.
- Proves a **global correctness guarantee**: with high probability, every candidate set output by the algorithm is either empty or the singleton true match.
- Establishes **explicit, depth-dependent per-vertex probability lower bounds** $p_{d'}-o(1)$ on correct matching, where $d'$ increases as the vertex gets closer to the root.

## Methodology

### Model
- **Correlated Erdős-Rényi pair:** A base graph $G \sim \mathrm{ER}(n, \lambda/(ns))$ is subsampled independently with probability $s$ to obtain two correlated graphs $G_1$ and $G_2$; the vertex labels of $G_2$ are permuted by $\pi^*$ to produce $\tilde G_2$.
- **Independent Cascade (IC) diffusion:** Starting from source vertex 1 in $G_1$, each activated vertex attempts to activate each inactive neighbor once with probability $q$. Successful activations form a rooted diffusion tree $T^{IC}$.
- **Observation:** The algorithm sees $T^{IC}$ and $\tilde G_2$, and knows $\lambda, s, q$. The goal is to recover $\pi^*(u)$ for each $u \in T^{IC}$.

### Algorithmic ingredients
- **Tree correlation tests:** Local neighborhoods in sparse ER graphs are approximated by Poisson Galton-Watson trees. The algorithm tests whether a subtree $T^{IC}_{u,l-1}$ and a BFS tree $\tilde T_{\tilde v,l-1}^{\setminus \tilde w}$ are correlated or independent using the likelihood ratio $L_{l-1}(T,\tilde T)$.
- **Four matching criteria:**
  1. **Criterion 1:** Three children of $u$ each have a dangling neighbor in $\tilde G_2$ whose local BFS tree passes the correlation test.
  2. **Criterion 2:** One child of $u$ is already matched, and a second child's subtree passes the correlation test with a neighbor of $\tilde v$.
  3. **Criterion 3:** Two children of $u$ are already matched to two distinct neighbors of $\tilde v$.
  4. **Criterion 4:** The parent of $u$ is matched to a neighbor of $\tilde v$, and $u$'s own subtree passes the correlation test with $\tilde v$.
- **Two-pass propagation:**
  - **Upward pass:** Starts from depth $d_{\max}$ and moves to the root, applying Criteria 1–3. Matches at lower levels propagate upward as strong evidence.
  - **Downward pass:** Starts from depth 1 and moves toward the leaves, applying Criterion 4. Parental matches help align children that would otherwise be missed.
- **Parameter choice:** $l = \lfloor \log n / \log(\lambda q) \rfloor$ and $d_{\max} = \lfloor (1-\epsilon)\log n / \log(\lambda q) \rfloor$.

### Theoretical sequence
- Define a recursively increasing sequence $(p_d)_{d\ge 0}$ based on Poisson thinning of strong/weak signals in the upward pass. This sequence captures the lower bound on correct-match probability as evidence propagates up the tree.

## Results & Highlights

- **Polynomial-time algorithm:** Algorithm 1 runs in $n^{2+o(1)}$ time under the correlated ER model, because the maximum degree is $O(\log n / \log\log n)$ and each likelihood-ratio test can be computed recursively in $n^{o(1)}$ time.
- **Main theorem (Theorem 1):** Under the conditions $\sqrt{q}s^2 > \alpha$ (where $\alpha \approx 0.338$ is Otter's constant), $\lambda s q > 1$, and $\lambda > \lambda_0$:
  - For a non-root vertex $v$ at depth $d \le d_{\max}$,
    $$\mathbb P\big(M_v = \{\pi^*(v)\} \mid v \in V_d\big) \ge p_{d'} - O(n^{-\epsilon/3}),$$
    where $d' = \min(l_+, d_{\max}-d)$ and $l_+ = \lfloor (\log n)^{3/4} \rfloor$. The bound improves as $d$ decreases (vertex closer to root).
  - For the root vertex 1,
    $$\mathbb P\big(M_1 = \{\pi^*(1)\}\big) \ge p_{l_+} - O(n^{-\epsilon/3}).$$
  - **Global correctness:**
    $$\mathbb P\big(M_u \subseteq \{\pi^*(u)\}, \;\forall u \in V_{\le d_{\max}+1}\big) \ge 1 - O(n^{-\epsilon/3}).$$
- **Interpretation:** The condition $\sqrt{q}s^2 > \alpha$ mirrors the computational threshold for sparse network alignment; here $\sqrt{q}s^2$ is the effective edge-correlation coefficient between the diffusion tree and $G_2$.
- **Comparison to partial recovery:** Because the cascade may be trivial (e.g., only the root is activated) with non-vanishing probability, partial-recovery guarantees are impossible. The paper therefore adopts a vertex-wise guarantee, which is the natural analog for diffusion alignment.
- **Gap to information-theoretic optimum:** When $q=1$ and $\lambda s=2$, the information-theoretic optimal aligned fraction is about 0.7149, while the root success lower bound here is about 0.4125, leaving room for future improvement.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The diffusion-network alignment formulation is a natural and relatively under-explored twist on classic network alignment. The asymmetric observation model is well-motivated by real applications (contact tracing, rumor source inference, deanonymization). The four-criterion two-pass algorithm and the explicit depth-dependent bounds are the strongest technical novelties.
- **Rigor:** The analysis is careful and technically dense. The proof couples local neighborhoods of the ER graphs to correlated Poisson Galton-Watson trees and handles non-tree effects and conditioned paths. Global correctness is reduced to two high-probability events (local tree-likeness and no spurious passing tests), which are then controlled by union bounds and Markov arguments.
- **Reproducibility:** The model, algorithm, and theorem statements are fully specified. However, the paper is purely theoretical; no code, datasets, or empirical validation are provided. Independent replication would require implementing the recursive likelihood-ratio computation and the four matching criteria.
- **Significance:** The work contributes to the theoretical foundations of network deanonymization and diffusion source tracing. The vertex-wise guarantee is well-suited to settings where cascades may be small or sparse. Practical impact depends on whether real-world diffusion trees fit the sparse ER/IC assumptions.
- **Limitations / red flags:**
  - The guarantee is **precision-only plus inclusion**: every returned match is correct with high probability, but the paper does not quantify how many vertices are actually matched (recall). The downward pass is included as a heuristic refinement whose gain is not captured by the bounds.
  - The model assumes knowledge of $\lambda, s, q$ and a clean IC diffusion process; real cascades are often noisy, partially observed, and temporally uncertain.
  - The condition $\lambda s q > 1$ requires the diffusion process to be supercritical, and $\sqrt{q}s^2 > \alpha$ imposes a relatively strong correlation threshold.

## Strengths

- Clean, motivated problem formulation that captures information asymmetry in real-world diffusion data.
- Polynomial-time algorithm with interpretable local matching criteria and a two-pass propagation structure.
- Strong theoretical guarantees: explicit per-vertex probability lower bounds and a global no-false-positive property.
- Careful comparison to related work and honest discussion of the gap to information-theoretic limits.

## Limitations

- No empirical validation on synthetic or real diffusion data; practical performance is unknown.
- Recall guarantees are not provided; the algorithm may leave many vertices unmatched.
- The downward pass improves performance but is not rigorously quantified.
- Strong model assumptions (sparse ER graphs, known IC parameters, supercritical cascade) may not hold in many real applications.
- The recursive likelihood-ratio computation, while polynomial, may still be expensive at scale.

## Relevance

对社交网络去匿名化、传染病接触者追踪、谣言溯源以及区块链交易分析等场景具有理论指导意义；其双向传播与逐顶点概率下界的分析框架也可启发其他非对称图匹配问题的算法设计。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the downward pass be rigorously analyzed to improve the per-vertex probability lower bounds?
2. Is there an efficient way to estimate the IC parameters $\lambda, s, q$ from the observed diffusion tree and network alone?
3. How robust is the algorithm to model misspecification, such as non-IC diffusion, community structure, or missing observations in the diffusion tree?
4. Can the method be extended to dynamic or temporal networks where the diffusion unfolds continuously and edges change over time?
5. What is the exact information-theoretic limit for diffusion-network alignment, and can polynomial-time algorithms achieve it?
