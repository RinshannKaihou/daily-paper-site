Disparate Impact in Synthetic Data Generation

本文提出将合成数据生成的公平性重新定义为"差别影响"，系统论证了近似误差、样本不均衡与差分隐私噪声会以不同方式损害不同敏感群体，并提出按群体分别建模的元算法作为缓解基线。
Paul Andrey · Michaël Perrot · Batiste Le Bars · Marc Tommasi — Univ. Lille, Inria, CNRS, Centrale Lille
fairness synthetic-data-generation disparate-impact probabilistic-graphical-models differential-privacy
Key Finding
Synthetic data generation systematically disadvantages minority groups through three distinct mechanisms — approximation error, estimation error, and DP noise — even when the original data is unbiased.
Key Metric
δTVD = TVD(D̃₁, D₁) − TVD(D̃₀, D₀) — the gap in total-variation distance between groups. All methods show δTVD > 0 disadvantaging Black groups on ACS data.
Why This Matters
Fairness in synthetic data is a blind spot: the generation process itself can introduce inequity that wasn't in the original data, undermining downstream decisions in healthcare, finance, and public policy.
Critical Edge
Group-wise modeling helps in most settings but fails under strong privacy (ε = 1), and the fairness goal of reproducing real distributions may conflict with ethical obligations to correct biased data.

Inequity
  • Approximation error: When a group's conditional distribution exceeds the model's hypothesis class (e.g., requires higher Bayesian-network in-degree), that group is modeled worse.
  • Estimation error: Smaller groups get noisier statistics — minority groups at P=0.1 are consistently disadvantaged even for near-private methods like PrivBayes(ε=100).
  • DP noise: Privacy noise has heterogeneous signal-to-noise ratios across groups; AIM(ε=1) shows disparate impact where its non-DP variant does not, because noise interferes with graph selection.
Cumulative effects: When smaller group size and higher complexity combine (double disadvantage), even methods like non-DP AIM — fair in isolation — show disparate impact due to majority-biased graph selection.

Metric misalignment: Distribution-fidelity fairness and downstream-classifier fairness can diverge. Group-wise modeling may improve one while worsening the other — a critical caution for practitioners.

⟐ Group-wise SDG Meta-Algorithm

The proposed baseline learns separate group-conditional distributions D̃k and noisy group proportions, then samples by first drawing a group and then drawing features from that group's model.

  • Preserves (ε, δ)-DP via parallel composition
  • Prevents majority-biased graph selection
  • Brings double-disadvantage closer to single-disadvantage levels
Trade-off: Under strong DP (ε=1), group-wise modeling suffers from heterogeneous signal-to-noise ratios and lower overall utility. It also loses statistical power for cross-group shared statistics.

⊘ Scope & Limitations

  • Empirical scope limited to PGM-based SDG on tabular categorical data — transfer to deep generative models (VAEs, GANs, diffusion, LLM-based) remains open
  • Fairness goal of matching real distribution may conflict with legal/ethical obligations to correct biased data
  • Group-wise modeling may not scale to many intersecting sensitive attributes or very small groups
  • Downstream utility depends on classifier choice (XGBoost only); other tasks may differ
  • Method-specific behaviors (AIM's annealing, graph-selection nuances) complicate generalization
Reproducibility: Public code, data, and random seeds at GitLab. Controlled experiments with 10 random distributions × 40,000 records. ACS: ~490K records, 10 replicas, 80/10/10 split.

? Open Questions

  1. How do these mechanisms manifest in deep generative models and continuous or high-dimensional data domains?
  2. Can group-wise SDG share statistical strength across groups while preserving privacy and avoiding majority-biased graph selection?
  3. How should practitioners formally trade off faithfully reproducing the real distribution against correcting historical or structural biases?
  4. What are the formal fairness guarantees of group-wise SDG under tight DP, and can adaptive privacy budget allocation across groups help?
  5. How sensitive are downstream utility disparities to the choice of downstream model, task, and evaluation metric?
