> **一句话总结：** 本文首次系统研究图神经网络中截断位置编码（truncated PEs）的理论性质，发现截断后谱 PE、基于游走的 PE 与 k-调和距离在表达能力上出现本质分化，且截断谱 PE 不再保证强于 1-WL，而混合不同截断 PE 族在 ZINC-12k 等真实任务上表现最佳。

> **TL;DR:** This ICML 2026 paper initiates the theoretical study of truncated positional encodings for GNNs, proving that truncation breaks the known expressive-power equivalence between spectral, walk-based, and k-harmonic PE families, makes truncated spectral PEs no longer universally stronger than 1-WL, and shows empirically that mixing truncated PE families outperforms any single family.

## 核心贡献

- 提出并形式化研究了图神经网络中“截断位置编码”的理论问题，填补完整版 PE（O(n³) 复杂度）与实际常用截断变体之间的理论空白。
- 证明在截断情况下，谱 PE（如拉普拉斯特征空间投影、有效电阻）与基于游走的邻接矩阵幂 PE 不再是表达能力等价的；二者在截断后具有不同的区分能力。
- 给出具体分离定理：存在 1-WL 可区分但 k-EP-WL（k ∈ Ω(n)）不可区分的图对；也存在 Ω(n)-Walk-WL 不可区分但 1-EP-WL 可区分的图对。
- 推论：截断后的谱 PE 不再保证强于 1-WL 测试，削弱了完整谱 PE 的理论优势。
- 研究 k-调和距离（k-harmonic distances）这一谱 PE 族：证明 [2n]-调和距离与完整 EP-WL 等价；同时展示 2-调和（双调和）距离与有效电阻在树结构上的迭代复杂度差异。
- 在 BREC 表达性基准和 ZINC-12k 回归任务上验证：混合多种截断 PE（如 Walks + k-harmonics）优于任何单一 PE 族。

## Key Contributions

- Initiates the formal theoretical study of **truncated positional encodings** for GNNs, bridging the gap between expensive complete PEs and the practical truncated variants used in almost all deployments.
- Shows that under truncation, **spectral PEs** (eigenspace projections, effective resistance) and **walk-based PEs** (adjacency matrix powers) become **fundamentally different in expressive power**, unlike their complete forms which are equivalent.
- Proves concrete separation theorems:
  - **Theorem 4.1:** There exist graph pairs distinguishable by 1-WL but indistinguishable by k-EP-WL for k ∈ Ω(n).
  - **Theorem 4.2:** There exist graph pairs indistinguishable by Ω(n)-Walk-WL but distinguishable by 1-EP-WL.
- Proves as a corollary that truncated spectral PEs are **not universally stronger than the 1-WL test**, losing a key theoretical advantage of full spectral PEs.
- Studies **k-harmonic distances** as a spectral PE family: proves [2n]-harmonic distances match EP-WL in power, and shows that biharmonic (2-harmonic) distance can distinguish certain tree pairs in constant iterations while effective resistance needs Ω(n) iterations.
- Empirically validates on **BREC** (expressivity benchmark) and **ZINC-12k** (molecular regression) that **mixing truncated PE families** outperforms any single family.

## Methodology

- **Theoretical framework:** The authors compare truncated PEs using variants of the Weisfeiler-Lehman test adapted to relative positional encodings (ψ-WL / GD-WL), including k-EP-WL, k-Walk-WL, Resistance-WL, and k-Harmonic-WL.
- **Graph constructions:** Explicit graph pairs are constructed to separate the expressive power of different truncated PE families. Key constructions include Cartesian products P₄ × K₁₀ vs. P₄ × K₅,₅ for eigenspace projections, and carefully designed weighted graphs for effective resistance separations.
- **k-harmonic distances:** The authors analyze Hᵏ(u, v) = √((1ᵤ − 1ᵥ)ᵀ(L⁺)ᵏ(1ᵤ − 1ᵥ)) as a bridge between spectral and polynomial PEs, proving completeness (Θ(n) harmonics match EP-WL) and studying truncated behavior.
- **Approximation algorithm:** They show that k-harmonic distances can be approximated in O(mk poly log n + n² log n) time using fast Laplacian solvers and Johnson-Lindenstrauss projections, making them computationally attractive.
- **Experiments:**
  - **BREC:** Contrastive learning benchmark measuring realized expressivity across Basic, Regular, Extension, and CFI graph families.
  - **ZINC-12k:** Molecular constrained solubility regression using Graphormer-GD and GINE architectures with fixed 8-dimensional PE budgets.

## Results & Highlights

- **Truncated PEs are incomparable with the WL hierarchy.** Theorem 4.1 and Theorem 4.2 together show that truncated eigenspace projections can be both stronger and weaker than 1-WL and walk-based PEs, depending on the truncation.
- **Effective resistance loses spectral completeness.** Theorem 4.3 and Corollary 4.1 disprove a conjecture by Zhang et al. (2023) by showing that Resistance-WL can be weaker than Adjacency-WL and EP-WL on weighted graphs, meaning effective resistance does not encode the full spectrum.
- **k-harmonics form a complete family.** Theorem 4.6 shows that concatenating [2n]-harmonic distances matches the power of EP-WL.
- **Different k-harmonics capture different structure.** Theorem 4.7 shows Sparse-Biharmonic-WL can distinguish certain tree pairs in O(1) iterations while Sparse-Resistance-WL requires Ω(n) iterations.
- **Generic transfer:** Theorem 4.8 shows that if one k-harmonic distance distinguishes a graph pair, then all but O(n⁵) other positive k values also distinguish it.
- **BREC results (Table 1):**
  - k-EP-WL with k = 5 achieves 55% total accuracy, approaching the 3-WL ceiling of 54.25% (note: totals are close).
  - Single k-harmonic encodings are competitive: biharmonic (k=2) reaches 46.5%, and adding more harmonics yields diminishing returns.
  - Stacking 20 adjacency powers yields 53.5%, comparable to biharmonic.
- **ZINC-12k results (Table 2, lower MAE is better):**
  - Projections alone: 0.117 ± 0.005
  - k-harmonics alone: 0.076 ± 0.006
  - Walks alone: 0.082 ± 0.003
  - **Walks + k-harmonics: 0.064 ± 0.002** (best)
  - Projections + Walks: 0.075 ± 0.005
  - Projections + k-harmonics: 0.078 ± 0.002
- **Rule of thumb:** The authors recommend mixing PEs from different truncated families rather than relying on a single truncated encoding.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. This is the first systematic theoretical treatment of truncated PEs, which are the de facto standard in practical graph transformers. The paper identifies a real gap between theory (complete PEs) and practice (truncated PEs).
- **Rigor:** Strong. Results are framed within the well-established WL/GD-WL hierarchy, with explicit theorems and graph constructions. The proofs are deferred to appendices, which is standard but prevents full verification from the main text alone.
- **Reproducibility:** Reasonable. The authors use public benchmarks (BREC, ZINC-12k) and standard architectures (Graphormer-GD, GINE). However, exact implementation details, hyperparameters, and code are not provided in the main text.
- **Significance:** Practically important. The results caution against assuming that truncated spectral PEs inherit the power of their complete counterparts, and provide actionable guidance (mix PE families). The k-harmonic distance approximation algorithm is a useful efficiency contribution.
- **Limitations / red flags:**
  - Theoretical separations rely on carefully constructed graph pairs; their prevalence in real-world data is unclear.
  - The ZINC experiments fix PE dimensionality to 8, which may favor certain encodings over others.
  - The “mixing helps” conclusion is supported on two benchmarks, but broader empirical validation across more datasets and tasks would strengthen the claim.
  - Some results (e.g., Resistance-WL vs. 1-WL on unweighted graphs) are stated as conjectures rather than theorems.
  - No code or supplementary material is referenced in the extracted text.

## Strengths

- Identifies and formalizes an important but overlooked theoretical-practical gap in GNN positional encodings.
- Provides concrete, constructive separation theorems rather than purely existential results.
- Disproves an existing conjecture about effective resistance encoding the full spectrum.
- Introduces k-harmonic distances as a computationally efficient and theoretically complete PE family.
- Combines theory with empirical recommendation, giving practitioners a clear rule of thumb: mix truncated PE families.

## Limitations

- Theoretical results are primarily about graph isomorphism distinguishability, which is only a proxy for downstream task performance.
- Experimental scope is limited to two benchmarks (BREC and ZINC-12k).
- PE dimensionality is fixed to 8 in ZINC experiments, which may not reflect all practical settings.
- No public code or hyperparameter details are mentioned in the main text.
- Some claims about unweighted graphs remain conjectural.
- The practical overhead of computing and mixing multiple PE families is discussed but not thoroughly benchmarked.

## Relevance

本文对图神经网络位置编码的理论基础进行了重要补充，尤其提醒实践者：为计算效率而截断 PE 会显著改变其表达能力，简单的“谱方法更强”的直觉在截断后可能失效。研究提出的“混合不同截断 PE 族”策略以及 k-调和距离的高效近似算法，为设计更鲁棒、更可扩展的 GNN 输入特征提供了直接指导。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What is the optimal truncation dimension k for different graph scales, PE families, and downstream tasks?
- Can we derive a principled method for selecting or weighting PEs in a mixture based on graph properties (e.g., diameter, spectral gap, motif structure)?
- How do truncated PEs interact with message-passing depth and transformer layer count in practice?
- Do the theoretical separations correspond to measurable performance gaps on a broader set of downstream tasks beyond BREC and ZINC?
- Can the k-harmonic approximation algorithm be extended to dynamic or large-scale graphs with sub-quadratic memory?
- Is it possible to design a single truncated PE family that provably retains most of the full expressive power while remaining computationally efficient?
