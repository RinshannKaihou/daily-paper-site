Quantizing Time-Series Models As Dynamical Systems

Trajectory-Based Quantization Sensitivity Score (TQS) measures how weight perturbations amplify across autoregressive rollout steps — then uses that signal to assign mixed-precision bit-widths without calibration data, Hessians, or gradients.

ICML 2026 Workshop · Pavlova, Zhu, Semenova, Li · Imperial / Copenhagen · arxiv.org/abs/2606.13300 ↗
Key Finding
TQS reframes quantization as a dynamical-systems stability problem. Layers whose weight perturbations cause exponentially diverging rollout trajectories get high bit-widths; stable layers get aggressively compressed — all without calibration data.
Key Metric
64 / 75
variable–model comparisons won across Aurora-small, TimesFM-2.5 & Pangu-Weather. Aurora: ≥32× compression at ≤1% MAE degradation.
Why This Matters
Deploying large weather & time-series foundation models on edge hardware — without calibration data, Hessian, or gradient access — even on frozen ONNX exports and compiled graphs.
Critical Edge
Gains concentrate at heavy compression; at moderate W3 levels, uniform baselines (GPTQ-W3, QEP-W3) can win. No activation quantization studied. Code availability unconfirmed.
Method — TQS Pipeline
① Perturb Replace one weight tensor with quantized version (or Gaussian probe of matched norm).
→
② Roll Out Run full autoregressive forecast. Measure output trajectory divergence vs. FP32 baseline.
→
③ Score γ Log growth rate of squared divergence normalized by perturbation magnitude. High γ → sensitive layer.
→
④ Allocate Solve multiple-choice knapsack under global bit budget. One ranking, many compression targets.

Probe horizons: TimesFM Tmax=100 · Aurora 120 steps (30 d) · Pangu 4 steps (24 h)

Structural Insight

Sensitivity lives at the I/O boundary

  • Input tokenizers/embeddings and output prediction heads are the most sensitive layers
  • Backbone self-attention & interior FFN blocks are much less sensitive
  • Contrasts with LLM PTQ where FFN-down projections dominate
  • Top 10% of layers carry 17–24% of cumulative γ-shift; top 25% carry ~40%
heavy-tailed I/O boundary not FFN
Quantizer-Free Probing

γgauss decouples from bit-width

  • Replaces quantization residual with isotropic Gaussian noise of matched Frobenius norm
  • Sensitivity estimate becomes independent of quantizer choice
  • Enables planning for black-box / compiled / frozen ONNX models
  • Gaussian and quantization probes converge at block granularity (Pangu ρ=0.96)
calibration-free gradient-free Hessian-free

核心要点（中文）
本文提出基于轨迹的量化敏感度分数（TQS），将时间序列模型的前向推理建模为离散时间动力系统。 通过衡量各层权重扰动在多步预测中的轨迹发散率，为混合精度位宽分配提供无需校准数据的可解释依据。 实验覆盖 Aurora-small、TimesFM-2.5 和 Pangu-Weather 三个基础模型，在重压缩区域显著优于 GPTQ、GPTAQ、QEP 等基线。
Limitations & Caveats
  • At moderate W3 compression, GPTQ-W3 / QEP-W3 can win — TQS-PTQ gains are strongest at heavy compression
  • Pangu deployability limited by onnx2torch rewrite; only FP32/BF16/INT8 stable
  • TQS-quant underperforms on Aurora — headline uses TQS-gauss (model-dependent)
  • INT1 bottom tier catastrophically polarizes TimesFM — framework is not hyperparameter-free
  • No activation quantization experiments; gains are weight-storage only
  • Code release not confirmed
Transferability

One sweep, many datasets

  • Cross-dataset ranking stability on TimesFM-2.5: mean Spearman ρ = 0.82
  • Single sensitivity sweep transfers across evaluation datasets
  • One ranking amortized over multiple compression targets
  • Gaussian vs. quantization probes: fine-grained disagreement but block-level convergence
