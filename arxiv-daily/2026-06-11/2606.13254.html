Evaluating Pluralism in LLMs through Latent Perspectives

Even with persona prompting, LLMs systematically underrepresent rare perspectives— perspective diversity scores are roughly half the human baseline. Surface-level coverage masks a deep distributional gap: much LLM diversity is performative.
pluralistic-alignment perspective-mining LLM-evaluation opinion-diversity unsupervised-learning
Key Finding
Persona prompting improves Overton coverage (97.9%) but leaves distributional pluralism broken—rare perspectives stay suppressed.

Why This Matters
High-temperature sampling and persona tricks inflate coverage metrics without fixing the underlying distribution. Relying on them gives a false sense of pluralism.
Critical Edge
Fixed k=5 perspective count is unmotivated; persona gains may reflect instruction-following, not genuine viewpoint diversity. Validated only on English book reviews.

Methodology Pipeline
Sentences (spaCy) → Embed (F2LLM 0.6B) → UMAP → 5D → HDBSCAN → Aspects
Aspect distribution vector → k-means (k=5) → Perspectives
  • Base set: 1,000 human reviews/book → fit clusters
  • Eval: 500 human vs. 500 LLM-generated reviews
  • 24 topic categories labeled via GPT-4.1-mini
  • Cluster coherence validated by intruder detection (72.4–94.6% accuracy vs. 20% random)

Generation Conditions
  • Baseline — T=0.7, standard prompt
  • High temperature — T=1.5
  • Persona — 300 fixed personas injected
Pre-training exposure: DCLM corpus contains ~150k Goodreads reviews from ~14.9k books, plus ~300k reviews from other book domains. Low diversity is not from data scarcity.
Strengths
  • First unsupervised, domain-agnostic framework for latent perspective mining in free-form text
  • Rigorous train/test split with matched sample sizes for clustering
  • Combines quantitative metrics (coverage, JSD, entropy) with qualitative topic analysis
  • Multiple open + closed models across three prompting strategies
  • Practical finding: persona prompting is a concrete lever for practitioners
Limitations
  • Sentence-level embeddings may miss cross-sentence or implicit perspectives
  • Fixed k=5 not deeply motivated—may under/over-segment true perspective space
  • 300 personas may not match actual reviewer demographics
  • English book reviews only; cross-lingual and cross-domain validity unknown
  • Sentiment classifiers may misclassify irony or nuanced language
Critical Assessment
  • Novelty: Moves pluralistic alignment beyond multiple-choice benchmarks to free-form text with a principled two-level representation.
  • Methodology: Perspective ≈ distribution over aspect clusters is a reasonable but imperfect approximation; authors mitigate with coherence validation.
  • Confounds: Persona prompts inject demographic conditioning—observed gains may reflect instruction following rather than genuine viewpoint diversity shifts.
  • Generalization: Book reviews are opinion-rich but structurally constrained; transfer to political discourse or scientific writing is unclear.
  • Reproducibility: Closed-model results (GPT 4.1, Gemini 2.5) not fully reproducible; open-weight OLMo models partially address this.
Open Questions
  • Does the framework transfer to news comments, social media, or parliamentary debates?
  • Would persona selection conditioned on actual reviewer demographics close the gap further?
  • Can the two-level representation extend to multilingual settings where aspect/perspective boundaries differ culturally?
  • What decoding interventions (diverse beam search, mixture-of-personas, distributional fine-tuning) best increase rare-perspective coverage?
  • How stable are perspective clusters across different embedding models or clustering algorithms?
研究相关性 / Relevance
该研究为评估和提升 LLM 的观点包容性提供了可操作的度量工具，尤其适合需要生成多元意见、减少"主流视角霸权"的应用场景（如评论生成、政策讨论、教育辅导）。同时，它提醒从业者：表面的多样性提示（如高温采样）并不等同于真正的分布多元性，角色提示等干预措施仍需与细粒度评估结合使用。
