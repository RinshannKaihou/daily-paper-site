Drop-by-Drop: Multi-Bitwidth LLM Quantization via Successive Refinement of Additive Codebooks

A single trained checkpoint serves 3-bit through 5-bit inference by progressively dropping codebooks — grounded in an information-theoretic proof that Gaussian weight matrices are successively refinable under weighted MSE.

Liza Babaoglu · Shuangyi Chen · Ashish Khisti University of Toronto, ECE Models tested: 0.5B–32B
Key Finding

Adding Matryoshka-style supervision to AQLM's additive codebooks makes each ordered subset independently usable — one model, multiple bitwidths, zero retraining.

Key Metric
6.19 vs 112.75

Mistral-7B 3×8 WikiText-2 perplexity: DbyD(35W) stays stable while naive AQLM codebook dropping catastrophically fails.

Why This Matters

Replace 3 separate quantized checkpoints with one. Heterogeneous fleet? Same model, different devices, just drop codebooks.

⚠ Critical Edge

Heavily dependent on hand-tuned loss weights (35W). Uniform variant is much weaker. Floors at 3-bit; no 1/2-bit validation. Weights-only — no activation or KV-cache quantization.

Method at a glance

Step 1
Prove Gaussian weights are successively refinable under WMSE via reverse water-filling
Step 2
Add Matryoshka loss: sum WMSE at each codebook prefix k
Step 3
Alternate beam-search assignments + gradient codebook updates
Deploy
Select first k ≥ 3 codebooks → 3/4/5-bit from one file
  • Loss: L = Σ λₖ · DWMSE(W, Ŵ⁽ᵏ⁾) — Weighted (35W) uses λ₃=λ₅=0.5
  • Codebook config: M ∈ {3,4,5} codebooks × 8-bit each, group size g=8
  • Calibration: 1024 samples, block-level finetuning after codebook convergence

Strengths

  • Rigorous theory: first to prove successive refinability for WMSE on Gaussian weights
  • Minimal engineering change: Matryoshka loss on top of AQLM, no architecture changes
  • Broad validation: 6 model families, 0.5B–32B, both perplexity and 5 downstream tasks
  • Direct deployment value: one file → fleet-wide heterogeneous serving
  • Exposes baseline fragility: shows QuIP# and AQLM codebook dropping catastrophically fail

Limitations

  • Hand-tuned weights: 35W is an ablation pick; uniform variant clearly worse
  • Floor at 3-bit: no 1/2-bit exploration; extreme compression untested
  • Weights only: ignores activation & KV-cache, often the real bottleneck
  • Training overhead: M partial reconstructions per step; no wall-clock comparison to single AQLM
  • Anonymous code: reproducibility pending final open-source release

Open Questions

  • Can successive refinement hold at 1–2 bit? Does the Gaussian assumption break?
  • Auto-learn λₖ instead of hand-tuning — task-aware weighting?
  • Joint weight + KV-cache quantization with Matryoshka supervision?
  • Pareto front across group size g, codebook bits B, and codebook count M?
  • System implications: batching & scheduling with variable codebook depth?

Relevance / 实用价值

对于需要在云端、桌面、边缘和移动设备上以轻量可维护方式部署同一 LLM 的团队，Drop-by-Drop 提供"一次训练、多精度推理"的权重量化方案。其信息论动机为未来更低比特、更细粒度的逐次求精量化奠定了理论基础。
