> **一句话总结：** 本文基于 operad 理论提出“Operadic Consistency (OC)”，通过检查大模型对组合问题的直接回答与按显式分解逐步组合得到的回答是否一致，来无标签地检测推理失败；在 12 个指令模型（4B–671B）和 4 个多跳 QA 数据集上，OC 与准确率的相关性高达 0.86–0.94，并在等成本选择性预测上显著优于 chain-of-thought self-consistency。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces Operadic Consistency (OC), a label-free inference-time signal grounded in operad theory that detects compositional reasoning failures by checking whether an LLM's direct answer to a query agrees with the answer obtained by composing a stated decomposition of the same query. Across 12 instruction-tuned LLMs and four multi-hop QA datasets, OC strongly correlates with accuracy (Pearson r ∈ [0.86, 0.94]) and yields consistent selective-prediction gains over chain-of-thought self-consistency at equal inference cost.

## 核心贡献

- 将 operad 理论（迭代替换系统的形式化工具）引入 LLM 推理诊断，定义“问题 operad” Q，其中操作是带空槽的问题模板，复合操作对应子问题替换。
- 提出 operadic consistency：对一个问题分解树，比较模型直接作答与按分解树自底向上逐步作答再复合的结果；不一致即标志潜在推理失败。
- 在 12 个指令微调 LLM（4B 到 671B，开源与闭源）和 4 个多跳 QA 数据集上验证，OC 与准确率的相关性在所有数据集上均 ≥ 0.85，是唯一Uniformly强相关信号。
- 证明 OC 在问题层面提供超出 CoT self-consistency、semantic entropy、P(True) 及构造的分解感知基线的增量预测信息。
- 将 OC 扩展到 5 个前沿 thinking 模型：从其自身思维链中提取显式子问题作为分解，仍能在 16 个（数据集 × 预算 × 指标）单元上全部取得正向提升。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces operad theory as a formal foundation for LLM compositional-reasoning diagnostics. The “questions operad” Q treats question templates with blanks as operations and substitution of sub-answers as operadic composition.
- Defines operadic consistency: for a tree of questions, compare the model's direct answer to its decomposed answer evaluated leaf-to-root; disagreement signals a compositional reasoning failure.
- Empirically validates OC across 12 instruction-tuned LLMs (4B–671B, open-weights and closed-source) on HotpotQA, MuSiQue, StrategyQA, and DROP, obtaining Pearson r ∈ [0.86, 0.94] with accuracy on every dataset.
- Shows OC carries complementary per-question information beyond chain-of-thought self-consistency, semantic entropy, P(True), and decomposition-aware baselines such as decomposed self-consistency and process reward models.
- Extends OC to five frontier thinking models (DeepSeek-R1, GLM-5.1, Cogito v2.1, Kimi-K2.6, MiniMax-M2.7) by extracting depth-2 sub-question chains from the model's own reasoning trace, achieving positive selective-prediction lifts on all 16 tested cells.

## Methodology

- **Operadic framing (§2).** A (non-unital) questions operad Q has Q(k) = question templates with k blanks. Composition ◦i : Q(k) × Q(ℓ) → Q(k+ℓ−1) inserts one template into blank i of another. A language model m induces a candidate Q-algebra by answering instantiated templates; operadic consistency is the predicate that direct and decomposed answers agree on a tree of questions.
- **Depth-2 scalar OC (§2.2, §A.3).** For a two-step chain Q1 → Q2, the protocol issues three temperature-0 calls: (1) direct answer to the full question; (2) answer a1 to Q1; (3) answer to Q2 with [A1] := a1. The continuous OC score ∈ [0, 1] is the per-dataset semantic agreement between the direct and decomposed answers. The binary form thresholds at 0.5.
- **Datasets.** HotpotQA (closed-book, no passage), MuSiQue and DROP (with gold passages), StrategyQA (open-domain). All questions restricted to length-2 chains. Decompositions come from Break QDMR for HotpotQA/DROP and native dataset annotations for MuSiQue/StrategyQA. GSM8K is used only for thinking models.
- **Models.** Non-thinking: Llama-3-8B, Gemma-3n-4B, Qwen2.5-7B, LiquidAI-LFM2-24B, Llama-3.3-70B, Qwen3-235B, DeepSeek-V3.1, Cogito-671B, GPT-4o-mini, Gemini 2.5 Flash, Llama-4 Maverick, Mistral Large 2. Thinking: DeepSeek-R1, GLM-5.1, Cogito v2.1, Kimi-K2.6, MiniMax-M2.7.
- **Baselines (§A.4).** Canonical CoT self-consistency (K=3 and K=10) with mean pairwise per-dataset scorer agreement; semantic entropy over K=10 samples; P(True) self-evaluation; decomposed self-consistency; Skywork-PRM and RLHFlow-PRM process reward models.
- **Scoring (§A.6).** HotpotQA/MuSiQue use normalized token-F1; StrategyQA uses yes/no extraction; DROP/GSM8K use number-equality fallback plus token-F1 with word-to-number conversion, reflecting value-equivalence rather than surface form.
- **CoQ extraction for thinking models (§3.2, §A.5).** A fixed Llama-3.3-70B extractor reads the greedy thinking trace, decides whether it factorizes into a clean depth-2 chain Q1 → Q2, and lifts Q1, a1, and Q2. Non-factorable traces are discarded (typically 5–15%).
- **Statistics.** Per-question logistic regressions with cluster-robust standard errors clustered by question ID. Selective prediction uses leave-one-model-out logistic-regression combiners and AUARC/AUROC with 2000-bootstrap CIs.

## Results & Highlights

- **Cross-model accuracy correlation (§3.1, Fig. 3).** OC-rate vs. per-model accuracy: HotpotQA r = +0.92, MuSiQue r = +0.94, StrategyQA r = +0.94, DROP r = +0.86 (all p ≤ 0.0004). OC is the only evaluated signal with r ≥ 0.85 on all four datasets.
- **CoT-SC is bimodal.** At K=10, CoT-SC matches OC on HotpotQA (+0.93) and DROP (+0.87) but collapses on MuSiQue (+0.43) and StrategyQA (+0.46). Semantic entropy peaks at +0.80 on StrategyQA but drops to +0.44 on MuSiQue; P(True) shows no significant cross-model correlation.
- **Label-free accuracy estimation (§3.1, Fig. 1, §A.9).** Leave-one-model-out linear fit of accuracy ≈ a · OC-rate + b achieves pooled mean absolute error 3.0 pp (2.1–4.7 pp per dataset), versus 4.6 pp for CoT-SC K=10.
- **Per-question complementarity (§3.1, Table 1).** Logistic regression correct ∼ OC + CoT-SC K=10 + (−H K=10 ) yields βOC significant at p ≤ 10⁻³⁸ on HotpotQA and p ≤ 10⁻¹⁶ on the other three datasets, after controlling for the two strongest sample-based signals. Adding P(True) leaves OC significant at p ≤ 10⁻¹⁶ (§A.10).
- **Selective prediction — non-thinking (§3.1, Table 1, §A.12).** At the equal-cost K=3 budget, OC + CoT-SC K=3 improves over CoT-SC K=3 alone: ∆AUARC ∈ [+0.086, +0.096] and ∆AUROC ∈ [+0.092, +0.164]; 95% CIs exclude zero on every dataset. Even against the literature-default K=10 baseline, lifts remain positive with CIs excluding zero.
- **Decomposition-aware baselines ruled out (§3.1, §A.11).** Decomposed self-consistency is either non-significant or significantly negative when added alongside OC, rejecting the hypothesis that OC is merely decomposed-path sample variance. Skywork-PRM contributes on DROP but never displaces OC.
- **Thinking models (§3.2, Table 2).** At K=3, bivariate regression correct ∼ OC + SC K=3 gives βOC = +1.42 (p < 10⁻¹⁸) pooled; OC remains significant on three of four datasets. Selective-prediction lifts are positive on all 16 (dataset, budget, metric) cells, with 95% CIs excluding zero on 12/16 (all eight K=3 cells plus four K=10 cells).
- **Failure-mode examples (§A.8).** OC correctly flags cases where the direct path is lured to a high-prior co-occurrence (e.g., “Clark Gable” for Vivien Leigh's husband) while the decomposed path finds the correct relation. False positives (OC-consistent but wrong) and false negatives (OC-inconsistent but correct) illustrate that OC is necessary but not sufficient for correctness.

## Critical Assessment

- **Conceptual novelty.** Using operads to formalize question decomposition is a genuine theoretical advance over ad-hoc decomposition frameworks. It provides a crisp mathematical justification for why direct-vs-decomposed agreement should matter and suggests natural extensions (deeper trees, branching decompositions, other Q-algebras).
- **Empirical robustness.** The cross-model correlation is unusually strong and consistent, and the leave-one-model-out accuracy-estimation result (≈3 pp MAE) is practically useful. The equal-cost selective-prediction comparison is fair: OC uses exactly three model calls, matching CoT-SC K=3.
- **Limitations.** (1) The scalar instantiation is restricted to depth-2 chains; richer trees might strengthen or complicate the signal. (2) Decompositions are either annotator-supplied or extracted by a fixed auxiliary model, introducing failure modes (annotator-model mismatch, non-factorable thinking traces discarded). (3) The DROP scorer uses value-equality, which is more lenient than the canonical surface-form scorer and crucial for the headline correlation; under the canonical scorer, OC–accuracy correlation on DROP collapses to r = +0.37 (n.s.). (4) The non-thinking cohort does not include the largest commercial systems (GPT-5, Claude Opus 4.x, Gemini 2.5 Pro), and the thinking-model cohort is small (n=5). (5) The off-label PRM comparison uses math-trained Skywork/RLHFlow PRMs; head-to-head against newer in-domain PRMs remains open.
- **Caveats on interpretation.** Strong cross-model correlation does not prove causation: more capable models may simply produce more consistent decompositions in general. Per-question regressions partly address this by clustering by question and holding model cohort fixed, but the signal could still conflate reasoning quality with surface-level answer stability. The false-positive and false-negative examples show OC should be one component of a multi-signal confidence stack, not a standalone gate.

## Strengths

- Theoretically principled: operads give a compact language for tree-shaped substitution and composition, justifying the diagnostic as a structural coherence check rather than an engineered heuristic.
- Empirically broad: 12 non-thinking + 5 thinking models, four multi-hop QA datasets, open-weights and closed-source APIs.
- Strong and uniform cross-model correlation with accuracy, outperforming or complementing standard confidence baselines.
- Fair cost comparison: OC's 3-call budget is matched against CoT-SC K=3, and it still improves over K=10 in absolute terms.
- Detailed ablations and sensitivity analyses (scorer choice, P(True), decomposition-aware baselines, LLM-judge corroboration) strengthen reliability claims.
- Failure-mode examples in the appendix are intellectually honest about false positives/negatives.

## Limitations

- Restricted to depth-2 chains in the main experiments; deeper or branching trees are left to future work.
- Requires either human decompositions or a reliable CoT extractor; applicability to open-domain questions without clean factorization is limited.
- The value-equality DROP scorer is a deliberate but consequential design choice; canonical scoring weakens the DROP result.
- Does not include the very largest frontier models; thinking-model sample size is small.
- Process-reward-model comparisons are off-label and math-trained; newer in-domain PRMs might narrow the gap.
- Correlation with capability means OC may be less informative for very strong models that are consistent by default.

## Relevance

对研究 LLM 推理可靠性、可解释性与不确定性量化的读者而言，本文提供了一个兼具数学严谨性与 empirical 说服力的全新视角：不仅把“问题分解”提升为 operad 代数结构，还把“直接回答与分解回答是否一致”转化为可操作、无标签、可部署的置信信号。OC 与 self-consistency、entropy 等方法正交互补，适合作为多跳问答、科学发现、代码推理等高风险场景中的“自知之明”模块，用于选择性回答、人工复核触发或模型能力评估。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does OC scale to deeper (depth ≥ 3) or branching trees of questions, and does the signal strengthen enough to justify the extra inference cost?
- Can lightweight, automatic decomposition methods make OC practical for open-domain QA where no annotated decomposition exists?
- How does OC interact with reasoning length and model scale on very capable frontier systems (GPT-5, Claude Opus 4.x, Gemini 2.5 Pro)?
- Would combining OC with process reward models trained on general multi-step reasoning (e.g., VersaPRM, DPRM) yield a calibrated, deployable confidence stack?
- Can the operadic framework be extended to other reasoning tasks such as mathematical proof verification, code synthesis, and structured planning, where compositional structure is less explicit?
- What is the mechanistic basis of OC failures — do operadically inconsistent cases correspond to the “hopping too late” or bridge-entity bottlenecks observed in interpretability work?
