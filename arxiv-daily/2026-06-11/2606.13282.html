ERTS

Adversarial Robustness Testing of Ethical AI via Semantic Perturbation in a Bounded Consequence Space

First closed-pipeline framework that encodes ethical dilemmas into a 22-dimensional consequence space, applies 17 semantic perturbations across 7 adversarial categories, and uses a 4-component instability index to produce domain-adaptive CLEARED / CONDITIONAL / FAILED verdicts — only 2 of 6 models passed.
首个将伦理困境编码到 22 维后果空间、通过 17 种语义扰动系统评估 AI 伦理决策对抗稳健性的闭环框架——6 个模型中仅 2 个通过预部署评估。
Pratyush Chaudhari Independent Researcher arxiv.org/abs/2606.13282
Key Metric
33%

Pass rate — only Gemini-2.0-Flash (ERS 0.940) and RuleBased (ERS 0.907) cleared all 7 high-stakes domains. 250 test cases per model.

Why This Matters
RLHF alignment ≠ adversarial ethical robustness

Rule-based baselines outperform RLHF (ERS 0.907 vs 0.852). Current alignment training leaves deployed AI vulnerable to ethical manipulation in healthcare, autonomous driving, and military applications.

Critical Edge
22-D space is engineered, not empirically validated

ECS dimensions chosen iteratively, not via factor analysis. Gemini's zero failures may be an adapter artifact. Only 2 production LLMs tested — no GPT-4, Claude, or Mistral. No confidence intervals reported.

Pipeline
1

Ethical Scene Encoding

Map each decision action to a 22-D ECS vector ∈ [0,1]²². Dimensions grounded in utilitarian, deontological, virtue-ethics, and rights-based frameworks.

2

Semantic Perturbation

17 perturbation functions across 7 adversarial categories under 6 validity constraints (including novel semantic coherence C5).

3

EII & ERS Scoring

4-component Ethical Instability Index → composite Ethical Robustness Score from stability, resistance, fairness, harm, and confidence.

4

Domain-Adaptive Verdicts

Per-domain thresholds (healthcare, military, AV, hiring, finance, education, general) → CLEARED / CONDITIONAL / FAILED.

7 Adversarial Categories · 17 Perturbations
Consequence Reframing Authority Injection Emotional Biasing Information Degradation Fairness Corruption Reward Manipulation Principle Conflict
6 Validity Constraints
C1 Range [0,1]
C2 Budget ‖x'−x‖₁ ≤ 2.0
C3 SingleVar |x'_i − x_i| ≤ 0.5
C4 Dominance cap 0.85
C5 Semantic coherence (novel)
C6 MinImpact ‖x'−x‖₁ ≥ 0.05
Ethical Instability Index
EII = 0.40·Faction + 0.25·Fconf + 0.25·Fscore + 0.10·Frank ERS = ⅕(C + R + F + H + S) C = decision stability R = perturbation resistance F = fairness resistance H = harm resistance S = confidence stability

Weights validated via sensitivity analysis — model rankings remain stable under 3 alternative weight configurations (ERS Δ < 0.01).

Assessment
Strengths
  • Translates formal ethics (utilitarian, Kantian, Rawlsian, Rossian) into a computable vector space — perturbations gain interpretable ethical semantics
  • Semantic coherence constraint C5 prevents logically inconsistent adversarial inputs — solves a known NLP adversarial weakness
  • 4-component EII covers action, confidence, score, and rank failure modes with theoretically justified weights
  • Domain-adaptive verdicts provide auditable pre-deployment checklists aligning with EU AI Act and UL 3115
  • Honest disclosure of limitations (adapter artifacts, numerical insensitivity) strengthens credibility
Limitations
  • 22 ECS dimensions chosen via iterative engineering, not factor analysis or cross-cultural ethics expert validation
  • C5 semantic dependency signs (−0.6, −0.7, −0.8) are heuristic — not calibrated against moral psychology data
  • Only 50 binary-action scenarios; no continuous decisions, multi-agent, or interactive settings
  • Production LLM coverage limited to Gemini and Llama-3.2-1B — missing GPT-4, Claude, Mistral
  • No confidence intervals, multi-seed replication, or human baselines — ERS point estimates' reliability unclear
Open Questions
How were the 22 ECS dimensions validated against moral philosophy and empirical moral psychology?
Can ERTS extend to multi-agent systems, interactive decisions, or continuous action spaces?
How robust are ERS scores to changes in scenario distribution, perturbation strength, and random seed?
Could ablation studies disentangle Gemini's robustness from the adapter's text-based representation?
How would ERTS perform on GPT-4, Claude, or Mistral under identical adapters?
Could ERTS become a certification standard — what governance and independent audits are required?
How should the framework handle cross-cultural ethical variation in ECS dimensions and thresholds?
