ReSET uses online step-level entropy to sharpen confident reasoning steps and warm uncertain ones, restoring NVFP4 accuracy and latency.

ReSET: Accurate Latency-Critical NVFP4 Reasoning via Step-Aware Temperature Scaling

Sihwa Lee · Janghwan Lee · Donghoon Yoo · Jae Gon Kim · Hanyul Ryu · Soojung Ryu · Jungwook Choi

arXiv:2606.13233 NVFP4 quantization Reasoning models Temperature scaling CUDA kernel optimization

Key metric

+2.6

AIME-120 points recovered over the NVFP4 RTN baseline

Why this matters

Pinpoints how NVFP4 quantization corrupts reasoning-chain uncertainty and provides a low-overhead fix for Blackwell deployments.

Critical edge

The global entropy threshold is calibrated from only five problems, and evaluation is restricted to Qwen-family models on Blackwell hardware.

Methodology

Failure analysis. 1.5M tokens sampled from R1-Qwen-14B reveal two NVFP4 distortions: symbolic tokens get probability inflation, while uncertain-step tokens suffer entropy collapse.

Step-aware policy. Steps are delimited by double-newlines; step entropy is the average token entropy. Confident steps use a global threshold τ₀ (80th percentile); uncertain steps use a step-relative threshold. Tokens below the threshold are sharpened with Tlow; others use base/high temperature.

Online estimator. A causal hybrid with window w=32 uses a sliding average early in a step, then switches to the within-step running mean. Overhead is ~100 µs/token (~1.5% of a decode step).

CUDA-core kernel. For M ≤ 8 decode, the kernel fuses multi-token CTAs, uses multi-accumulator threading, and dequantizes FP4 to FP16 in registers, bypassing Tensor Core tile-under-occupancy on Blackwell.

Results & Highlights

  • AIME-120: +2.6 points over NVFP4 RTN (R1-Qwen-7B 39.6 → 43.8; Qwen3-32B 74.4 → 77.5).
  • Kernel speedup: 1.57–2.49× faster than vLLM-CUTLASS at M=1 across QKV/Out/Gate-Up/Down projections.
  • End-to-end latency: up to 1.97× over BF16 on Qwen3-32B (B=1), 1.85× at B=8.
  • Ablation: fixed-entropy and sliding-window rules improve only inconsistently; ReSET wins on every model.
  • Overhead: activation-quantization cost is 1.65–1.81 µs/layer, below rotation-fused PTQ.

Strengths

  • Tackles a real deployment pain point: small-batch NVFP4 serving.
  • Strong algorithm–system co-design.
  • Clear empirical motivation via step-level entropy analysis.
  • Negligible sampler overhead (~1.5% of a decode step).
  • Open-source release with detailed configuration.

Limitations

  • Global threshold calibrated from only five problems.
  • No comparison with richer adaptive-decoding baselines.
  • Limited to Qwen-family models on math/science/code tasks.
  • Kernel benefits specific to Blackwell NVFP4 small-M decode.
  • End-to-end speedups are best-case under small batch/long output.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the step-entropy distribution transfer to non-Qwen reasoning families such as LLaMA-reasoning or Kimi k1.5?
  2. Can ReSET combine additively with QAT, AWQ, or GPTQ beyond the brief GPTQ experiment?
  3. How does it compare with stronger adaptive-decoding baselines like SEAL or confidence-based thresholding?
  4. Can τ₀, Tlow, and Thigh be estimated fully online without a task-specific calibration split?
  5. Does the CUDA-core small-M design generalize to MXFP4, INT4, or other GPU architectures?
