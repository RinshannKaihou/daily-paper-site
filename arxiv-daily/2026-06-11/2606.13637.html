The Stable Recovery Manifold: Geometric Principles Governing Recoverability in Continual Learning

Ayushman Trivedi · Bhavika Melwani 2026-06-11 Split CIFAR-100 · ResNet-18 arxiv.org/abs/2606.13637
Catastrophic forgetting is not information destruction — it is a geometric accessibility failure. Forgotten task knowledge remains preserved in a stable ~8-dimensional recovery subspace; what drifts is the alignment between current and original subspaces.
Key Finding
Recovery subspace dimensionality kt stays stable at mean 8.0 (σ = 0.82) across all 10 sequential tasks — directly falsifying the Recoverability Diffusion hypothesis.
Key Metric
R² = 0.82
geometric predictability of recoverability
Dt dominant predictor · r = −0.862
Why This Matters
Anti-forgetting strategies should target subspace alignment rather than parameter drift — potentially enabling recovery by anchoring just ~8 singular directions.
⚠ Critical Edge
Validated on one dataset (Split CIFAR-100) and one architecture (ResNet-18). Whether kt ≈ 8 generalizes to Transformers, larger tasks, or >10 tasks is unknown. No comparison with EWC/replay baselines.

Methodology

  • Protocol: Naïve sequential training on Split CIFAR-100 (10 tasks × 10 classes), no replay or regularization
  • Architecture: ResNet-18 adapted for 32×32 input; 4 residual stages L1–L4, final dim d = 512
  • Training: SGD + momentum 0.9, cosine annealing, 50 epochs/task
  • Core metrics: ACCt, LPt (linear probe), Rt (recoverability), AGt (accessibility gap), PEt (projection energy), Dt (principal-angle drift), kt (recovery subspace dim)

Relevance & Future Directions

  • Reframes continual learning: from preventing information loss to maintaining subspace alignment
  • Potential for low-dimensional anchor regularization — protect only ~8 directions instead of full parameter space
  • Does kt ≈ 8 persist on ViTs, larger-scale benchmarks (Split ImageNet, CLOC)?
  • How do existing methods (EWC, GPM, replay) affect Dt, PEt, kt?
  • Long-term behavior: does the manifold collapse beyond 10 tasks?

Strengths & Limitations

✓ Strengths

  • Quantifies the "forgetting ≠ destruction" intuition with measurable geometric metrics
  • kt stability provides direct empirical support for subspace methods like GPM
  • Depth-stratification (PR analysis) offers mechanistic explanation for early-layer retention
  • Self-falsification of Recoverability Diffusion hypothesis strengthens scientific credibility
  • Engineering implication: protect ~8 singular directions → efficient recovery

✕ Limitations

  • Single dataset (CIFAR-100) + single architecture (ResNet-18) — existence proof, not universal
  • No comparison with EWC, iCaRL, GEM, replay baselines
  • kt depends on arbitrary 90% probe threshold; threshold sensitivity not analyzed
  • Rt is an upper bound — recovery ≠ real end-to-end task performance
  • Code/data not publicly released; reproducibility of geometric analysis uncertain
