Learning to Reason by Analogy via Retrieval-Augmented Reinforcement Fine-Tuning

RA-RFT replaces semantic-similarity retrieval with a reasoning-utility-aware retriever trained via gold-relevance distillation, then folds the retrieved analogical traces into GRPO-based reinforcement fine-tuning — enabling the policy to learn transferable solution strategies under verifiable outcome rewards.

Zilin Xiao · Qi Ma · C.-C. Jason Chen · X. Chen · A. Atreya · Hanjie Chen · Vicente Ordonez · Meta Superintelligence Labs / Rice University

Why This Matters
Retrieval for reasoning models should optimise for reasoning utility, not surface semantic similarity. RA-RFT demonstrates a paradigm shift: the retriever is trained to recognise transferable problem-solving patterns, yielding gains orthogonal to reward design and curriculum learning. Smaller models benefit most — a boon for efficient deployment.
Critical Edge
Validated only on math (AIME/HMMT/BrUMO); gains shrink with model scale (4B: only +1.6 avg). Uses fixed k = 1 retrieval; multi-trace synergy unexplored. No robustness analysis for misleading or out-of-distribution retrievals. Code and checkpoints not released.

Three-Stage Pipeline

1

Gold-Relevance Distillation

GPT-4o labels query–candidate pairs by "transferable reasoning pattern" similarity (binary), not surface text overlap. Coarse-type filtering cuts labeling cost ~10×.

2

Reasoning-Aware Retriever Training

Reason-ModernColBERT fine-tuned with InfoNCE (τ = 0.05) on gold labels. R@1 surges from 7.2% → 43.5%.

3

Retrieval-Augmented RL Fine-Tuning

Top-1 analogical trace injected as context; GRPO samples G = 16 rollouts per query. Reward = answer correctness. Trained on 64× H100.

Strengths

  • Precisely diagnoses the structural flaw of semantic retrieval in reasoning tasks
  • Closed-loop method: distillation → retriever training → RL policy, each stage grounded in the prior
  • Thorough ablations isolate "retrieval itself" vs "training objective" vs "retriever quality"
  • Consistent gains across four competition-level benchmarks
  • Training dynamics reveal genuine learning to exploit retrieved context (step-0 dip → later recovery)

Limitations

  • Math-only validation; code generation, formal theorem proving, and scientific QA untouched
  • Requires 64× H100 + GPT-4o judge calls + Qwen3-235B trace generation — high cost
  • Fixed k = 1; multi-trace synergy and context-length tradeoffs unexplored
  • No robustness evaluation for misleading or OOD retrieval
  • Gains diminish with model scale (4B: +1.6 avg vs 1.7B: +4.6)
  • Code and checkpoints not released at time of writing

Open Questions

  1. Does RA-RFT transfer to code generation, theorem proving, or scientific QA?
  2. How should a model combine k > 1 analogical traces — attention, routing, or voting?
  3. Can the policy learn to reject misleading retrieved traces?
  4. Does coarse problem-type supervision generalise to unseen problem categories?
  5. Are gains consistent under other RLVR algorithms (PPO, DAPO, Reinforce++)?
  6. Could end-to-end joint training of retriever + policy close the remaining gap?
  7. Is the larger benefit for small models a path toward efficient knowledge distillation?
