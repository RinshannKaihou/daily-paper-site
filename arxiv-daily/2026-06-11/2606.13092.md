> **一句话总结：** 本文针对等变隐世界模型提出了可计算、可证明紧的多步可预测视界证书，证明 T 步 Rollout 误差在每条对称轨道上保持恒定，并按 Lyapunov 谱分层衰减；实证表明结构对称性（而非模型规模）能在零标定数据下买到可认证的长期预测能力。

> **TL;DR:** This paper gives a computable, tight, multi-step certificate of predictable horizon for equivariant latent world models: T-step rollout error is provably orbit-constant and channel-stratified by the Lyapunov spectrum, and empirical audits of public pretrained models show that structure—not scale—can buy a calibrated, calibration-free long-horizon guarantee.

## 核心贡献

1. **紧的可认证视界（§3.2）**：对等变世界模型，T 步展开误差在轨道上恒定（Theorem A）；在近似等变下，通道 j 的可信视界满足 $T_j(\epsilon) \sim \log(1/\epsilon)/\lambda_j$（Theorem B），并给出匹配的下界（Proposition 6），说明近似等变的可预测视界本质受限。
2. **证书为结构所独有（§3.1）**：Lemma 2 证明“轨道恒定误差 ⟺ 等变性”，因此非等变模型无论规模多大都无法拥有该证书。
3. **Noether 铰链（§3.3）**：守恒/不变量通道对应 $\lambda_j \leq 0$，可获得无界视界；Propositions 4–5 给出了表示论强制 placement 与守恒到线性误差的保证。
4. **从理论到可执行程序（§3.4）**：Algorithm 1 将证书转化为可在生成元检查、Jacobian 谱估计后报告的判定流程，并给出有限样本区间（Proposition 10）。
5. **广泛的实验验证**：在合成混沌系统、40-D Lorenz-96、接触物理 PushT、SO(3) 点云、原始像素、公共预训练模型（TD-MPC2、LeWM、V-JEPA 2-AC）及部署监控器上验证了证书。

## Key Contributions

1. **A tight certified horizon.** For equivariant models, T-step rollout error is orbit-constant (Theorem A); under approximate equivariance, channel j is certified to $T_j(\epsilon) \sim \log(1/\epsilon)/\lambda_j$ (Theorem B), with a matching lower bound (Proposition 6) making approximate equivariance provably horizon-limited.
2. **The certificate is exclusive to structure.** Lemma 2 gives the converse: orbit-constant error against every equivariant target characterizes equivariance; no non-equivariant model has the certificate at any scale.
3. **The Noether hinge.** Conserved/invariant channels are certified to all horizons (Propositions 4–5), anchoring the unbounded-horizon edge of the certificate.
4. **An executable procedure.** Algorithm 1 checks generator equivariance, estimates the predictor spectrum, and reports whether a $(w, T, \epsilon)$ query lies in the certified region.
5. **Empirical breadth.** The framework is validated on controlled spectra, Lorenz/Hénon/Rössler, 40-D Lorenz-96, real contact dynamics, SO(3) point clouds, raw pixels, public pretrained world models, and a deployed sensor monitor.

## Methodology

The setup is a latent world model with encoder $E:\mathcal{X}\to\mathcal{Z}$ and predictor $f:\mathcal{Z}\times\mathcal{A}\to\mathcal{Z}$, where $f(E(x),a)\approx E(\Phi(x,a))$ for unknown dynamics $\Phi$. A group $G$ acts on observations, latents (via orthogonal representation $\rho$), and actions (via $\sigma$). The paper assumes:
- (A1) encoder equivariance,
- (A2) predictor equivariance,
- (A3) dynamical symmetry of the true dynamics,
- (A4) orthogonality of $\rho$,
- (A5) equivariant planner and invariant cost (closed-loop case).

**Configuration axis.** Lemma 1 shows that checking (A1)–(A3) on $k$ group generators certifies the exponentially large generated monoid $\langle S\rangle$. Theorem A then proves $Err_T(w\cdot x)=Err_T(x)$ for every word $w$, because the rolled-out predictor remains equivariant and orthogonality preserves the norm.

**Horizon axis.** For approximately equivariant models, let $\epsilon_{\max}$ be the encoder residual and $\delta$ the per-step predictor error. Linearizing the latent Jacobian along a trajectory gives channel-wise multipliers $e^{\lambda_j}$. Theorem B bounds orbit-error variation by
$$\sum_j c_j\,(m\epsilon_{\max}+T\delta)\,e^{\lambda_j T},$$
so channel $j$ is certified only up to $T_j(\epsilon)\sim \log(1/\epsilon)/\lambda_j$ when $\lambda_j>0$, and to $T_j=\infty$ when $\lambda_j\le 0$.

**Tightness.** Proposition 6 constructs a perfect predictor with a single encoder defect of size $\epsilon$ and shows the orbit-error variation is exactly $\epsilon e^{\lambda T}$, matching Theorem B up to constants. Proposition 6$'$ identifies the prefactor as $c_j=1/\sin\theta_j$, equal to 1 on orthogonal isotypic splittings.

**Scope for learned models.** Proposition 7 uses Oseledets’ theorem to lift the log-law to learned chaotic models when $\lambda_1>0$, and flags the near-neutral ($\lambda_1\approx 0$) regime as vacuous. Proposition 8 gives a finite-horizon $C^1$ continuity bound: a one-step-accurate learned model recovers the staircase slope up to an $O(\delta)$ bias plus finite-$T$ truncation.

**Noether hinge.** Proposition 4 forces a conserved charge to live in a specific isotypic block; Proposition 5 shows that a charge conserved to one-step defect $\eta$ has $T$-step charge-value error $\le T\eta$ (linear, not exponential), giving an unbounded horizon at $\eta=0$.

**Decision scope.** Proposition 9 proves that an inflated horizon certificate ($\hat\lambda_1=c\lambda_1$) demands $c\times$ the sensing budget to stay violation-free; Proposition 11 formalizes when the certificate carries decision value (scope-aligned vs. task-mapped decisions).

## Results & Highlights

- **E2 — Lorenz-96 spectrum recovery.** On 40-D Lorenz-96, a $\mathbb{Z}_N$-equivariant cyclic-convolution network recovers the full Lyapunov spectrum ($R^2=0.98$–$0.99$, Liouville identity $\sum_j\lambda_j=-N$ matched to 0.0%), while an identically trained dense MLP fails ($R^2<0$, $\lambda_1$ inflated $\sim$3.4$\times$) and a GRU-BPTT baseline also fails. Sweeping $N\in\{12,20,28,40\}$ shows a phase transition: all architectures tie through $N=28$, but only the equivariant one survives $N=40$.
- **E2 — horizon staircase on real chaos.** Learned one-step models of Lorenz, Hénon, and Rössler produce certified-horizon staircases linear in $\log(1/\epsilon)$ with $R^2=0.975$–$0.995$, recovering textbook Lyapunov exponents to 1–12%.
- **E10 — cone/adapted-metric literal certificate.** On uniformly hyperbolic dynamics the a-priori cone certificate is tight (cat-map exact; learned net 1.17$\times$; nonlinear Anosov 1.06$\times$); on non-uniformly hyperbolic Hénon it is sound but $\sim$3$\times$ conservative and self-abstains via its cone-margin diagnostic.
- **E12 — sensing budget.** Under fixed sensing budget on 40-D Lorenz-96, the equivariant certificate yields 8–16% aggregate violation versus 61–65% for the dense certificate. The inflated dense certificate needs 2.7–3.5$\times$ the observations to catch up; recalibrating the dense model closes the gap but costs rollout calibration data, while the equivariant certificate needs none.
- **E13/E14 — public model audit.** TD-MPC2 official checkpoints are audited training-free: calibrated where strongly expansive (walker ratio 0.94–1.02), optimistic where weakly expansive, and correctly abstaining where contracting. Across the 1M–317M multitask ladder, calibration does not improve monotonically with scale; $\lambda_1$ sign-flips non-monotonically.
- **E15 — deployed monitor.** A sensor-only cheetah-run monitor loaded with pre-published certificate numbers replicates the bench scope map out-of-sample (0.43 vs. 0.43, 0.50 vs. 0.50), detects frozen-actuator faults with recall 1.00 on 3/3 seeds, and confirms the taxonomy: stable-abstain > expansive-priced > bias-abstain.
- **E16 — foundation-scale audit.** V-JEPA 2-AC (1B, real DROID data) reads expansive ($\lambda_1\approx0.178$), but deployment error starts at the representation’s native step-motion scale and grows with log-slope 0.03 $\ll\lambda_1$; the model never enters the linearization neighborhood, so the measured cross-check overrides the raw spectrum.
- **E5–E8 — certificate transfer.** SO(2) PushT contact dynamics are orbit-flat to ratio 1.000; the certificate lifts to non-abelian SO(3) and to raw pixels via frame averaging with no accuracy cost; it becomes orbit-invariant closed-loop control and drives a noise-immune epistemic explorer that certifies 128 compositions in 7 observations.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty & significance:** The paper makes a compelling conceptual move: reframing the “scale vs. structure” debate in world modeling as a question of certified predictability horizons. Linking equivariance, representation theory, Lyapunov spectra, and Noether-type conservation into one computable certificate is novel and significant.

**Rigor:** The central results (Theorem A/B, Lemma 2, Proposition 6) are proved in Appendix B and appear sound under the stated assumptions. The tightness construction is clean. However, the lift to learned models relies on $C^1$-closeness, which the paper verifies only through one-step $L^2$ error—a proxy that can degrade in high dimensions, as the 40-D Lorenz-96 experiment itself shows. The dominated-splitting assumption is assumed, not certified.

**Empirical breadth vs. depth:** The experiments cover many settings, but most are small-scale (1–2 GPUs). The high-dimensional validation is essentially one system (Lorenz-96). The public-model audits are training-free and therefore limited to the policy-prior loop; they do not close the full control loop on the real agent. Honest INCONCLUSIVE results are reported (E11, E16, E5 sub-gates), which is admirable.

**Reproducibility:** Code, seeds, and an experiment-to-claim map are provided in the appendix. Public checkpoints (TD-MPC2, V-JEPA 2-AC, LeWM) make the audit protocol reproducible in principle, although the V-JEPA 2-AC loading required monkey-patching a misconfigured URL, which is disclosed.

**Red flags:** The title claim that “scale buys interpolation, not a calibrated horizon” is supported by E14, but the multitask ladder has only one checkpoint per cell and the dynamics regime flips non-monotonically—suggestive but not a controlled ablation of scale. The V-JEPA 2-AC result shows the certificate can be vacuous at foundation scale, tempering practical deployability claims.

## Strengths

- Provides a formal, computable, two-sided certificate rather than empirical error bars alone.
- Connects deep-learning symmetries to classical dynamical-systems concepts (Lyapunov exponents, Oseledets, Noether conservation).
- Proves exclusivity: the certificate characterizes equivariance.
- Demonstrates training-free auditing of public pretrained world models.
- Honest scope reporting, including inconclusive results and known failure modes.
- Offers concrete actionable applications: sensing-budget scheduling and deployment monitoring.

## Limitations

- Requires genuine dynamical symmetry for exact guarantees; approximate symmetry degrades gracefully but is horizon-limited.
- Prefactor $c_j=1/\sin\theta_j$ can be large for nearly tangential splittings; reported calibration reflects typical, not worst-case, defect alignment.
- The Noether hinge is proved forward but validated mainly on constructed equivariant teachers; emergence in learned unconstrained dynamics remains open.
- The horizon law is vacuous for near-neutral dynamics ($\lambda_1\approx0$), which includes important control regimes.
- Downstream value is primarily budgeted efficiency, not safety; safety-relevant catastrophe avoidance was inconclusive.
- Task-mapped decisions (e.g., MPPI return) dilute certificate value unless the certificate resolution matches the task tolerance.
- Small-scale experiments; absolute multi-step accuracy on raw pixels remains poor for all architectures at the reported scale.
- Single-author preprint; peer review and independent replication are pending.

## Relevance

该论文为可信长期世界模型提供了一套新的理论语言和实用工具：把对称性先验、Lyapunov 谱与守恒量结合起来，给出了“结构买到认证视界”的明确含义。对机器人部署、强化学习 Rollout 安全、主动感知与模型审计等场景具有直接启发，同时提醒研究者：单纯扩大模型规模可能只改善插值误差，却无法保证特定构型在多长时间内可信。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the certificate behave under partial observability and stochastic dynamics, where no exact group action on latents may exist?
- Can the framework be extended to continuous Lie-group equivariance and to approximate symmetries learned from data?
- How sensitive is the certificate to misspecified symmetry groups and to encoder/predictor equivariance residuals in practice?
- Can the Lyapunov-spectrum read-out be used as a regularization objective during world-model training?
- What is the computational cost of the certificate compared with Monte Carlo ensemble rollouts for large latent dimensions?
- How can the certificate be combined with task-level tolerances so that control policies can extract full decision value (Proposition 11)?
