> **一句话总结：** 本文针对两智能体辩论场景提出了三种置信度驱动的聚合协议（WSV、CGA、HID），通过对跨模型的原始置信度进行转换与融合，为整个多智能体系统输出单一答案和单一聚合置信度，在多个基准上显著提升了置信度区分能力（AUARC），并恢复了标准多智能体辩论在模糊任务上的性能损失。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes three confidence-driven aggregation protocols—Weighted Stream Voting (WSV), Confidence Gating with Aggregation (CGA), and Human-Inspired Debate (HID)—for two-agent multiagent debate. By transforming and fusing per-agent confidence signals into a single system-level confidence, the protocols substantially improve confidence discrimination (AUARC) over the best single agent and standard debate baselines, while stabilizing or recovering F1 losses that plain multiagent debate incurs on ambiguous tasks.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统研究了如何为整个多智能体系统生成并评估单一聚合置信度，而不是仅将置信度用于辩论内部的消息加权、触发或单个智能体校准。
- 提出三种基于两智能体辩论的聚合协议：
  - **WSV（Weighted Stream Voting）**：将两个智能体在零样本和辩论后的四个“流”视为独立证据，通过可学习的权重对 logit 置信度进行软投票。
  - **CGA（Confidence Gating with Aggregation）**：每个智能体仅在辩论后置信度提升超过学习阈值时才切换到辩论答案，再对保留下来的两个流做贝叶斯融合。
  - **HID（Human-Inspired Debate）**：模拟人类辩论流程，根据智能体是否一致以及置信度高低进行确定性路由，最终同样用贝叶斯融合得到置信度。
- 比较了两种置信度估计器：**序列概率（sequence probability）** 与 **自报告（self-report）**，以及参数化（Beta、三阶多项式）和非参数化（Isotonic）校准器。
- 在五个基准（MMLU-College、BoolQ、EZStance、Vast、HatEval）、四种任务类型上，对 14 个开源模型组成的六对同质/异质辩论组合进行了系统评估。

## Key Contributions

- Frames and addresses the problem of producing a single, evaluated confidence signal for a multiagent system, moving beyond prior uses of confidence only inside multiagent debate (MAD).
- Introduces three confidence-aware protocols for two-agent debate:
  - **WSV** treats the four (agent × stage) streams as independent evidence and performs a learned weighted soft vote over logit-transformed confidences.
  - **CGA** first applies confidence-gated switching (CGS), then fuses the surviving streams under a conditional-independence assumption using Bayesian fusion.
  - **HID** deterministically routes among the four streams based on agreement and confidence levels, inspired by human debate, and reports confidence via Bayesian fusion.
- Compares **sequence-probability** and **self-report** confidence estimators, and applies **Beta**, **third-order polynomial**, and **isotonic-regression** calibrators.
- Evaluates 14 open-source models across five benchmarks and four task types, with six homogeneous/heterogeneous debating pairs per benchmark.

## Methodology

**Two-agent debate setup.** Each model $M_i$ produces a zero-shot prediction $y_{i,0}$, chain-of-thought $\text{CoT}_{i,0}$, and confidence $c_{i,0}$. After one debate round in which each agent sees the peer’s zero-shot reasoning, it produces a post-debate prediction $y_{i,1}$, reasoning, and confidence $c_{i,1}$. The baselines are standard MAD (always debate) and MAD-D (debate only on initial disagreement).

**Confidence estimation.**
- **Sequence probability**: mean token log-likelihood normalized by length, followed by a content-free prior correction $C(x,\hat{y}) = \log P_\theta(\hat{y}|x) - \log P_\theta(\hat{y}|x_{\text{null}})$ to reduce surface-form bias.
- **Self-report**: the model is prompted to verbally rate its certainty after producing an answer; scores are averaged over three repetitions with temperature 0.3.

**Calibration.** Each stream is calibrated on a held-out validation split using Beta calibration, a third-order polynomial transformation, or isotonic regression. Parametric methods minimize NLL; isotonic regression uses the pool-adjacent-violators algorithm.

**Protocol optimization.** Protocol parameters (stream weights for WSV; per-agent thresholds for CGA/HID) are optimized with Tree-structured Parzen Estimator (TPE) early stopping because routing is non-differentiable. The objective is
$$
\hat{\theta} = \arg\min_\theta \underbrace{\text{NLL}(\theta)}_{\text{performance}} + \lambda_{\text{ECE}} \underbrace{\text{ECE}(\theta)}_{\text{calibration}} + \lambda_{\text{anchor}} \underbrace{\|\theta - \theta_0\|_2^2}_{\text{regularization}},
$$
with $\lambda_{\text{ECE}} = \lambda_{\text{anchor}} = 0.1$.

**Bayesian fusion.** For CGA/HID, if two surviving streams agree, the fused confidence is $c_1 c_2 / [c_1 c_2 + (1-c_1)(1-c_2)]$; if they disagree and $c_1 > c_2$, the selected answer’s confidence is $c_1(1-c_2)/[(1-c_1)c_2 + c_1(1-c_2)]$.

## Results & Highlights

- **Confidence discrimination (AUARC) improves substantially.** The aggregated confidence from WSV, CGA, and HID consistently outperforms the best zero-shot agent and standard MAD/MAD-D baselines. For example, with the sequence-probability estimator, CGA achieves +8.50 AUARC on BoolQ and +7.04 on EZStance; HID reaches +7.81 on BoolQ and +6.00 on EZStance. With self-report, CGA and HID reach +9.58 and +8.35 AUARC on EZStance, respectively, and self-report often yields the highest AUARC across four of five benchmarks.
- **Task accuracy (F1) is stabilized or improved.** Plain MAD degrades F1 on BoolQ (−5.53 pp), Vast (−6.02), EZStance (−8.37), and HatEval (−3.25). The confidence-based protocols turn many of these losses into gains: e.g., WSV reaches +0.77 on BoolQ, +1.32 on Vast, and +2.28 on HatEval (sequence probability); self-report WSV reaches +1.40 on BoolQ. EZStance F1 gains remain marginal for some protocols (CGA −0.24), and MMLU-College F1 stays positive but below plain MAD.
- **Calibration is essential for F1 but less critical for AUARC.** Across Figure 1, moving from uncalibrated (raw) to any calibrated setting generally improves F1, with Beta and polynomial calibration performing strongly. AUARC is more robust to the choice of calibrator; even uncalibrated signals can rank well after protocol-level transformation.
- **WSV favors F1; CGA/HID favor AUARC.** WSV’s soft vote over all four streams yields more consistent F1 improvements, while the more conservative CGA/HID gating mechanisms produce higher discrimination.
- **Self-report confidence behaves more meaningfully during debate.** Sequence probability tends to increase after debate even when the answer becomes incorrect (correct→incorrect transitions). Self-report changes are smaller and more aligned with correctness, partly explaining its stronger AUARC/F1 on several benchmarks.
- **Confident agents are more persuasive when correct.** Figure 2 (left) shows that the more-confident agent is generally more persuasive, and the correct-more-confident agent switches the incorrect agent more often than the reverse, providing the empirical basis for confidence-based routing.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Ali Elahi** and **Barbara Di Eugenio** are at the University of Illinois Chicago. Di Eugenio is an established researcher in NLP, dialogue, and human-computer interaction.
- The study follows a thorough empirical design: 14 models from five families (LLaMA, Mistral, Phi, Gemma, Qwen), six debating pairs per benchmark, multiple confidence estimators and calibrators, and significance testing per pair over five runs.
- The paper reports limitations candidly and provides prompts in the appendix, which supports reproducibility. Code availability is not mentioned in the extracted text.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The paper identifies a clear gap—system-level confidence for multiagent systems—and addresses it with conceptually simple, well-motivated protocols. The move from per-agent confidence to an aggregated confidence signal is practically important and underexplored.
- **Rigor.** The empirical protocol is comprehensive in model and task coverage. However, the results are averaged over only six pairs per benchmark, and the paper does not report confidence intervals or variance estimates in the main table. Significance is reported as counts of pairs (x/6), which is useful but coarse.
- **Reproducibility.** Standard models, public benchmarks, and detailed prompts help reproducibility. The two-stage optimization (calibration + TPE) is clearly described. Without released code, exact replication depends on re-implementing the prompt and optimization details.
- **Significance.** If the gains generalize, aggregated confidence can improve selective answering, routing, and human oversight in multi-LLM systems. The focus on the harder two-agent setting is particularly valuable because prior aggregation-friendly methods often assume larger ensembles.
- **Potential red flags.** Bayesian fusion assumes conditional independence, which the authors note is violated; yet it still works well. The study is limited to two agents and one debate round, and the broader applicability to open-ended generation, math, and code remains to be tested.

## Strengths

- Clearly defines and motivates a neglected problem: system-level confidence for multiagent LLM systems.
- Proposes lightweight protocols that can be layered on top of existing MAD implementations.
- Provides an extensive empirical comparison of confidence sources, calibrators, and routing strategies.
- Demonstrates both improved confidence discrimination (AUARC) and recovery of MAD-induced F1 losses on ambiguous tasks.
- Offers interpretable analyses of how confidence evolves during debate and how persuasiveness relates to confidence and correctness.

## Limitations

- **Limited to two agents and one debate round.** The authors explicitly state this scope; CGA and WSV can in principle scale, but HID would need redesign, and the paper does not validate larger ensembles.
- **Assumes a Q/A or classification framing.** Although the authors argue the routers are not inherently limited to classification, the evaluation covers only discrete-answer tasks; open-ended generation and reasoning/math benchmarks are listed as future work.
- **Correlated errors and adversarial settings are not studied.** If two agents share similar biases, confidence-based aggregation may amplify rather than mitigate errors.
- **Computational cost and latency are not analyzed.** The protocols require multiple inference passes (zero-shot + debate, possibly repeated self-report sampling and validation-based optimization), which may be expensive relative to a single model.
- **Conditional-independence assumption in Bayesian fusion is violated.** While empirically effective, the theoretical justification for the fused confidence is approximate.

## Relevance

对于需要可靠性与可解释性的 NLP 应用（如事实问答、立场检测、仇恨言论识别、医学或法律问答辅助），能够为整个多智能体系统输出一个统一、校准且具区分度的置信度，是提升自动化筛选、人工审核和下游决策路由能力的关键。本文的方法可直接叠加到现有多智能体辩论框架上，工程实现简单，且对高歧义任务中标准辩论造成的性能下滑有明显的修复作用，具有较强的实际参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do WSV, CGA, and HID scale to three or more agents, and does the Bayesian-fusion advantage persist as ensemble size grows?
- Can the protocols be extended to open-ended text generation, code generation, or multi-step mathematical reasoning where answers are not simple labels?
- What is the inference-time and optimization-time cost of the protocols compared with single-agent inference and standard MAD?
- How robust are the protocols when debating agents are drawn from the same model family or fine-tuned on similar data, i.e., when their errors are correlated?
- Would incorporating external verifiers, retrieval-augmented evidence, or human-in-the-loop confidence labels further improve the aggregated confidence signal?
- How does increasing the number of debate rounds interact with confidence dynamics, given prior findings that MAD often behaves like a random walk after the first round?
