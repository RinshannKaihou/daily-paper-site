Key Finding

Validation loss and perplexity in a 4.26M-parameter Llama-style LM reach their minima around 4–5M tokens, then degrade through 20M tokens.

A Quantitative Experimental Repeated Measures Study of Training Dynamics in a Small Llama Style Language Model Under a Compute-Aware Token Budget
Joe Dwyer · ECPI University
training dynamics small LMs repeated-measures ANOVA compute-aware evaluation
Key Metric
2.7996
Minimum mean validation loss near 4M tokens; rises to 3.9010 by the final checkpoint.
Why This Matters
Under a fixed small compute budget, more training tokens can degrade validation performance; interval-level telemetry reveals risks hidden by endpoint metrics.
Critical Edge
Effect sizes (ηg² > .999) are implausibly high, likely inflated by many intervals and only six random seeds.

# Methodology

A decoder-only Llama-style transformer trained in PyTorch on the TinyStories corpus under a strict CPU/fp32 budget. Six independent seeds act as repeated-measures subjects.

Model
  • 4 layers, 4 heads, 256-dim embeddings, 128 context
  • Vocabulary 4,096; rotary positional embeddings
  • 4,262,144 trainable parameters, no dropout
Training
  • Budget: 20,000,768 tokens (CPU fp32)
  • AdamW β₁=0.9, β₂=0.95; LR 4e-4 → 4e-5 with 1M-token warmup
  • Weight decay 0.1, gradient clipping 1.0
  • 21 checkpoints ≈ every 1M tokens × 6 seeds = 126 observations
Derived telemetry

Validation-loss deltas, rolling volatility (5-interval window), backslide indicators, spike detection (z > 2.0, min Δ 0.01), backslide frequency, and a stable-phase candidate indicator.

# Results & Highlights

All six seeds completed the full schedule with no missing data. The trajectory is strongly non-monotonic: loss and perplexity fall to an early minimum, then rise.

  • RQ1 stability: reject null — interval has a significant effect.

# Strengths

  • Pre-registered research questions with explicit variables and hypotheses.
  • Uses repeated-measures ANOVA, effect sizes, and a mixed-effects robustness check rather than curve inspection alone.
  • Detailed hyperparameters and architecture reported; code and data released on Zenodo.
  • Accessible CPU/fp32 setup makes replication feasible for educators and low-resource labs.
  • Introduces interpretable derived metrics: backslide frequency and stable-phase indicator.

# Limitations

  • Only six independent seeds; limited statistical power and robustness.
  • Single dataset (TinyStories), single model size, single architecture, single hyperparameter schedule.
  • No downstream-task evaluation beyond validation loss and perplexity.
  • Does not test whether standard interventions (regularization, LR tuning, early stopping) could remedy the degradation.
  • CPU fp32 differs from modern GPU/mixed-precision pipelines, limiting production relevance.

# Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the same non-monotonic degradation appear in 70M–1B models or on SlimPajama/FineWeb?
  • Would stronger regularization, LR schedule changes, or repetition-aware sampling eliminate the late rise?
  • How do downstream metrics (HellaSwag, ARC, story completion) correlate with the perplexity minimum?
  • Is the early minimum around 4–5M tokens stable across seeds and hyperparameters, and can it be predicted cheaply?
  • Can backslide frequency or the stable-phase indicator become an online stopping criterion?
  • How do results change under GPU mixed-precision, larger batches, or different optimizers?
