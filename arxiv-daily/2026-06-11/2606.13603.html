Large reasoning models hit a sharp commitment boundary inside Chain-of-Thought, after which most later steps no longer change the final answer.

Beyond the Commitment Boundary: Probing Epiphenomenal Chain-of-Thought in Large Reasoning Models

Daniel Scalena, Sara Candussio, Luca Bortolussi, Elisabetta Fersini, Malvina Nissim, Gabriele Sarti

chain-of-thought reasoning interpretability early-exit large-language-models
Key Metric
55%
peak CoT token savings (gemma-4-26B-A4B-it) with near-matched accuracy
Why This Matters
It exposes that much generated chain-of-thought is causal dead weight, making inference cheaper and CoT safety monitoring more fragile.
Critical Edge
The probe is trained on labels from the same truncation procedure it replaces, so it inherits methodological biases.

Results & Highlights

Abrupt commitment Confidence concentrates near 0 and 1; the largest single-step jump is 4.6× the second-largest.
Family-dependent boundary gemma-4-26B-A4B-it commits at 13–23% of CoT; gpt-oss-20b commits much later at 43–68%.
Epiphenomenal tail Corrupting post-boundary tokens preserves the answer far more often than corrupting pre-boundary tokens.
Surface hedging is misleading "Wait", "but", "let's check" appear at similar rates before and after the boundary.
Probes localize reliably Detection exceeds 90% in-distribution and stays high out-of-distribution on AIME, ZebraLogic, and GPQA.
Early exit wins Probe-based exit at k=5 reaches 98% of full-CoT accuracy while saving 26% of tokens.

Strengths

  • Clean framework linking causal attribution, interpretability, and inference efficiency.
  • Strong evidence for abrupt rather than gradual answer formation.
  • Careful verification, collision filtering, and perturbation stress tests.
  • Practical online early-exit with lightweight probes.
  • Coverage across three open-weight model families and four benchmarks.
  • Honest, detailed limitations section.

Limitations

  • Commitment is tied to greedy decoding and an answer-forcing suffix.
  • Sample biased toward traces where no-CoT fails and answers avoid first-token collisions.
  • Sentence-level granularity gives an upper-bound estimate of commitment.
  • Probe inherits biases from the truncation labels it replaces.
  • No closed-weight frontier models, agentic tasks, or long-horizon generation.
  • Early-exit accuracy loss is small but non-zero; OOD early-fire rates up to 21%.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the boundary behave under sampling-based decoding (temperature > 0)?
  • Can token-level attribution shift the boundary earlier and unlock more savings?
  • Do closed-weight frontier models show similar boundaries without activation access?
  • Can models be trained to align verbalized reasoning with their internal commitment point?
  • What functions do post-boundary tokens serve beyond the final answer—calibration, coherence, RL shaping?
  • How robust is the probe on adversarial or semantically unusual prompts?
