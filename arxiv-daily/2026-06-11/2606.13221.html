Soft-Elo calibrates LLM-judge score differences into soft probabilities for Bradley-Terry, cutting Elo error 61% with conformal uncertainty intervals.

From Uncertain Judgments to Calibrated Rankings Bora Kargi & David Salinas · ELLIS Institute Tübingen 2026-06-11
LLM Evaluation Elo Rating Conformal Prediction LLM-as-a-Judge Bradley-Terry
Key Metric
45.9 → 17.9
Mean Elo MAE across 8 judges, 55 held-out models
Conformal interval widths shrink 39–70% while maintaining ≈90% coverage. Calibration ECE drops from 0.14 to 0.03.
Why This Matters
Gives teams using LLM-as-a-judge near-human Elo scores and interpretable uncertainty intervals at low cost — revealing scale distortion that rank-only metrics hide.
Critical Edge
Conformal coverage is marginal, not conditional, and may fail under distribution shifts — new model families, prompt distributions, or temporal drift.

Methodology

1
Score collection. Each of 8 LLM judges scores ~25K battles across 55 models on LMArena. Scores averaged over 6 dimensions (1–10 scale), position-swapped to reduce bias.
2
Soft probability mapping. Score difference s(x) is mapped through a learned temperature β to a calibrated soft preference probability σ(β · s(x)), replacing hard {0, 0.5, 1} labels.
3
Bradley-Terry Elo. Soft targets are fed into the standard BT model. Temperature is fitted per judge via leave-one-model-out CV using human non-tie battles.
4
Conformal intervals. LLM-human Elo residuals are normalized by bootstrap SE, yielding nonconformity scores. Split conformal prediction produces distribution-free prediction intervals for new models.

Strengths

  • Identifies the real failure mode of LLM-judge Elo: scale distortion, not ranking error.
  • Simple and modular — plugs into existing BT + conformal pipelines without changing the ranking model.
  • Strong empirical evidence across 8 diverse judges on a real leaderboard dataset (LMArena).
  • Honest, calibrated uncertainty intervals at model level — exactly what practitioners need to report.

Limitations

  • Requires judges that output continuous score differences; not all LLM-as-a-judge setups provide this.
  • Does not correct judge-specific biases (self-preference, verbosity) beyond position-swap averaging.
  • Conformal intervals assume exchangeability across model families and prompt distributions.
  • Needs a calibration pool of models with known human Elo; intervals degrade under distribution shift.
  • Average MAE 17.9 masks per-judge range of 13.6–24.5 — judge quality still matters.

Open Questions

  1. How does Soft-Elo perform when the judge is explicitly debiased for verbosity, self-preference, or style before score calibration?
  2. Can conformal calibration be made conditionally valid over model families or prompt domains?
  3. What is the minimum human-Elo calibration pool size needed for reliable intervals in deployment?
  4. Does the same score-difference calibration transfer to reward-model training or RLHF preference modeling?
