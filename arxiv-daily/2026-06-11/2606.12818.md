> **一句话总结：** 本研究将锚定效应转化为受控多项选择任务，利用基于归因的电路定位在 7B–8B 的 Qwen 与 Llama 模型中定位锚定敏感通路，发现边级定位比节点级更忠实，且高低锚定电路在模型内共享结构但跨基础/指令微调变体迁移较弱。

> **TL;DR:** The paper converts numerical anchoring into a controlled multiple-choice task and uses attribution-based circuit localization on 7B–8B Qwen and Llama models to identify anchor-sensitive pathways. Edge-level methods are more faithful than node-level methods; low- and high-anchor circuits share substantial structure within a model, but transfer across base and instruction-tuned variants is weaker.

## 核心贡献

- 将 Open Anchoring Question Dataset（OpAQ）中的 100 道数值估计题改编为固定九选项的多项选择任务，使锚定效应可以在共享答案空间内被精确测量，并便于提取答案标签 logits。
- 提出并验证了一个正确–锚定对数几率差指标 m = logit(ycorrect) − logit(yanchor)，通过 ∆m 量化锚定提示使锚定答案相对正确答案变得更有竞争力的程度，并证明其与行为上的期望位移（EV shift）相关。
- 在 Qwen2.5-7B、Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct、Llama-3.1-8B 和 Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct 上比较了节点级与边级归因补丁方法（NAP、EAP、NAP-IG、EAP-IG），发现边级方法（尤其 EAP-IG）的电路忠实度显著更高。
- 发现同一模型内低锚定和高锚定电路在层深度分布上几乎完全重叠（Pearson r > 0.99），且功能迁移性很强；但在基础模型与指令微调模型之间，稀疏电路的功能迁移明显下降。
- 揭示了模型家族特异性：Qwen 的归因集中于中后层，而 Llama 的归因更集中于网络早期。

## Key Contributions

- Adapts 100 numerical-estimation questions from the Open Anchoring Question Dataset (OpAQ) into a fixed nine-option multiple-choice format, enabling precise measurement of anchoring in a shared answer space and well-defined answer-label logits.
- Proposes and validates a correct–anchor logit-difference metric m = logit(ycorrect) − logit(yanchor). The anchored-minus-control change ∆m tracks behavioral expected-value shifts, providing an answer-level target for circuit localization.
- Compares node- and edge-level attribution patching (NAP, EAP, NAP-IG, EAP-IG) on Qwen2.5-7B, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, Llama-3.1-8B, and Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, showing that edge-level methods—especially EAP-IG—are substantially more faithful.
- Shows that low- and high-anchor circuits within the same model localize to nearly identical depth regions (Pearson r > 0.99) and transfer functionally almost as well as matched circuits; however, sparse circuit transfer across base and instruction-tuned variants is weaker.
- Reveals family-specific depth profiles: Qwen models concentrate attribution in mid-to-late layers, while Llama models concentrate it earlier.

## Methodology

The authors construct a controlled multiple-choice anchoring task from 100 OpAQ items. Each item provides a factual question, a ground-truth numerical answer, a low anchor, and a high anchor. A nine-option candidate set is built containing the ground truth, the two anchors, and intermediate values. The model is prompted to answer with the letter only, so each option is scored by a single answer-label token.

For each item, three prompt variants are created:
- **Low-anchor prompt:** an irrelevant slot-machine sentence containing the low anchor is prepended.
- **High-anchor prompt:** the same sentence contains the high anchor.
- **Token-matched control:** the numerical anchor is replaced by a non-numeric X string of matching token length.

Behavioral analyses average over 20 random option permutations per question to mitigate letter- and position-order effects; attribution and circuit localization use four fixed cyclic rotations per question for computational tractability, yielding 400 paired examples per anchor contrast.

The target metric for circuit localization is the correct–anchor logit difference:

m = logit(ycorrect) − logit(yanchor).

Lower m means the anchor option is more competitive. For each matched prompt pair the authors compute ∆m = manchor − mcontrol. They validate that ∆m correlates with the behavioral normalized expected-value (EV) shift.

Circuit localization follows the MIB framework. The tokenizer-matched control prompt is treated as the clean input and the anchored prompt as the corrupted input. The attribution loss is L = −m. The authors compare:
- Node Attribution Patching (NAP)
- Edge Attribution Patching (EAP)
- NAP with integrated gradients (NAP-IG)
- EAP with integrated gradients (EAP-IG)

Circuits are formed by retaining the top-k% ranked nodes or edges. Faithfulness is measured by circuit performance recovery (CPR), the area under the curve of the fraction of the control–anchored gap in m recovered as the retained fraction k varies over {0.001, 0.002, 0.005, 0.01, 0.02, 0.05, 0.1, 0.2, 0.5, 1}. The authors also examine structural overlap (Jaccard similarity and shared fraction) and functional transfer of circuits across anchor contrasts and across base/instruction-tuned variants.

## Results & Highlights

- **Behavioral anchoring is elicited in all four models.** Low anchors shift expected value downward and high anchors shift it upward. The effect is strongest in the Qwen models, especially Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct.
- **The attribution metric tracks behavior.** Mean ∆m is negative for every model and anchor contrast, and all values are significant under a one-sided Wilcoxon signed-rank test (p < 10⁻¹⁵). Spearman correlations ρEV between −∆m and anchor-consistent EV shift reach 0.73 for Qwen-7B and 0.75 for Llama-8B on high-anchor contrasts; the weakest correlation is 0.18 for Llama-Inst on the high-anchor contrast.
- **Edge-level localization is more faithful than node-level.** For example, on Qwen-7B low-anchor contrasts CPR values are: NAP 0.361, EAP 0.939, NAP-IG 0.705, EAP-IG 0.971. On high-anchor contrasts: NAP 0.490, EAP 0.923, NAP-IG 0.657, EAP-IG 0.965. EAP-IG is the most consistently faithful method across models and contrasts.
- **Instruction tuning affects EAP more than EAP-IG.** EAP CPR drops from 0.939 to 0.862 for Qwen low-anchor and from 0.958 to 0.840 for Llama low-anchor, whereas EAP-IG remains above 0.91 across all instruction-tuned settings.
- **Depth localization differs by model family.** Qwen variants show a broad mid-to-late attribution profile; Llama variants concentrate attribution earlier and drop sharply after mid-depth. Within each model, low- and high-anchor curves are nearly identical, with Pearson correlations above 0.99 and centroid differences below 0.01 relative depth.
- **Attention dominates anchoring pathways.** In the full graph, attention-to-attention edges account for about 95–96% of all scored edges. In the top-5% circuits, attention-to-attention edges still make up roughly 68–80%; the selected circuits include many more attention–MLP edges than the full graph, suggesting MLPs participate mainly through attention-mediated pathways.
- **Low- and high-anchor circuits share structure.** At 5% retained edges, Jaccard overlap is 0.49–0.55 and shared fraction is 0.66–0.71. Cross-contrast CPR ranges from 0.933 to 0.981, within about 0.04 of matched recovery.
- **Cross-variant transfer is weaker.** Base and instruction-tuned variants share 0.64–0.66 of their top-5% edges (Jaccard 0.47–0.49). At 5% retained edges, Qwen-7B matched faithfulness is 0.917 but drops to 0.291 when ranked on Qwen-Inst; Llama-Inst drops from 0.408 to 0.166 when ranked on Llama-8B.

## Authors & Credibility

Hillary N. Owusu, Sarah Wiegreffe, and Naomi H. Feldman are researchers at the University of Maryland, College Park, working at the intersection of natural language processing, cognitive science, and mechanistic interpretability. The study builds on prior work by the same authors showing that anchoring depends on confidence and post-training. All experiments use publicly available open-weight models and standard libraries, enhancing reproducibility.

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a valuable contribution by moving anchoring research from pure behavioral measurement to mechanistic explanation. Its strengths include a carefully validated answer-level metric, systematic comparison of localization methods, and explicit transfer analyses across anchor direction and instruction tuning.

There are, however, important caveats:
- **Dataset scale and scope.** The analysis uses only 100 questions from a single anchoring dataset. The results should be interpreted as evidence for this controlled benchmark rather than a comprehensive account of anchoring in open-ended numerical reasoning.
- **Task format.** The fixed-option multiple-choice setup simplifies attribution but differs from free-form generation, where anchoring may manifest through different decoding dynamics, rationales, or intermediate computations.
- **Metric limitation.** The correct–anchor logit difference, though validated against EV shifts, does not capture the full distributional effect of anchoring. Other objectives (e.g., KL divergence, total variation distance, anchor-side probability mass) might localize somewhat different pathways.
- **Approximate interpretability methods.** Attribution patching approximates causal effects; the identified circuits are correlates of the target metric rather than conclusively causal components.
- **Limited model coverage.** Only four models from two families are studied. The family-specific depth patterns are suggestive but not conclusive for other architectures, scales, or post-training procedures.
- **Practical mitigation.** The work identifies pathways but does not propose or evaluate interventions to reduce anchoring bias.

## Strengths

- Clearly links a cognitive bias to internal model computation through a validated metric.
- Uses a controlled task design with token-matched controls, multiple option orderings, and answer-label logits suitable for mechanistic analysis.
- Provides a direct comparison of node- vs. edge-level and standard vs. integrated-gradient attribution patching.
- Examines both structural overlap and functional transfer of circuits, giving a richer picture than overlap alone.
- Highlights the impact of instruction tuning on circuit structure, a practically important finding for post-training mitigation.

## Limitations

- Small and narrow dataset (100 OpAQ items) limits generalizability.
- Multiple-choice format may not reflect open-ended numerical estimation or real-world anchoring scenarios.
- The localization target is a single scalar and may miss other facets of anchoring.
- Attribution-based circuit localization is an approximation, not a definitive causal map.
- Only two model families at 7B–8B are evaluated; broader architectural and scale coverage is needed.
- No intervention experiments are conducted, so the causal role of the localized pathways remains to be tested.

## Relevance

该研究为理解语言模型在数值推理中如何被无关数字“锚定”提供了从行为现象到内部通路的机制性解释，对设计更鲁棒、更可解释的语言模型以及后训练阶段的偏见缓解具有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the observed depth-localization patterns and circuit-transfer results hold for larger models (e.g., 14B, 70B) and other architectures?
- How do the identified circuits change under targeted interventions such as activation patching, pruning, or fine-tuning on debiased data?
- Do the same anchoring pathways appear in open-ended numerical generation, or is the multiple-choice setup driving the observed structure?
- Would alternative localization objectives (KL divergence, TVD, anchor-side probability) identify overlapping or complementary pathways?
- Can the shared low/high-anchor pathway structure be exploited to develop a single, contrast-agnostic debiasing method within a model?
