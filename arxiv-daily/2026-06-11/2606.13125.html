Select and Improve: Understanding the Mechanics of Post-Training for Reasoning

Through controlled finite-field arithmetic experiments on Qwen-2.5-1.5B, this paper decomposes RL post-training gains into two mechanisms — strategy selection and strategy improvement — and shows neither creates entirely novel reasoning from scratch.

Krishnamurthy · Huang · Rajaraman Microsoft Research NYC · UIUC 2026-06-11 arxiv.org ↗
reinforcement-learning reasoning mechanistic-interpretability post-training LLM
Key Finding
RL post-training improves reasoning through two mechanisms — strategy selection (routing problems to pre-existing strategies) and strategy improvement (refining them on harder data) — but cannot create novel strategies from scratch.
Key Metric
96.0% inversion accuracy (B-only, RL on harder 6–9 step data)
vs 38.9% SFT baseline · vs 25.6% in-distribution RL
Why This Matters
SFT data diversity + RL data difficulty are the real levers — the RL algorithm alone is insufficient for reasoning gains.
Critical Edge
Only validated on synthetic finite-field arithmetic with a 1.5B-parameter model. No evidence RL creates genuinely novel strategies — tension with claims from ProRL and DeepSeek-R1.

  • Task: Synthetic finite-field arithmetic (GF(11), robustness on GF(13)). Integer symbols replaced with abstract tokens; each problem prefaced with a random permutation defining the field.
  • Problem types: Evaluation (forward compute) and Inversion (solve for unknown input), with difficulty controlled by step count (2–5 for SFT, 6–9 for main RL, up to 15 for distribution shift).
  • Reasoning strategies: Forward reasoning (execute ops sequentially) vs. Backward reasoning (reverse from output).
  • SFT variants: Forward-only (F), Backward-only (B), and Mixed (FB, random strategy per sample).
  • Compositional tasks: Chained sub-problems requiring intermediate result passing; tests whether RL extends compositional skills.

Two Mechanisms in Detail

Strategy Selection — RL rapidly (~40 steps) learns to route evaluation → forward, inversion → backward. Correct routing precedes accuracy improvement.

Strategy Improvement — Slower timescale; reward continues climbing after routing stabilises. Requires RL data harder than SFT data.

Strategy Amplification — Previously observed phenomenon (e.g. backtracking in DeepSeek-R1) is reinterpreted as a consequence of strategy selection, not a separate mechanism.

Strengths

  • Clean two-mechanism taxonomy that unifies amplification, composition, and selection under one framework.
  • Highly controlled synthetic task allows independent manipulation of strategy diversity and difficulty — stronger causal claims than natural-data studies.
  • Combines behavioural metrics with rule-based strategy classifiers for process-level evidence.
  • Practical, actionable guidance: SFT needs diverse strategies; RL needs harder data.
  • Candid discussion of negative results (no novel strategy emergence).

Limitations

  • Synthetic finite-field arithmetic lacks ambiguity, knowledge dependency, and long-range semantics of real NLP reasoning.
  • Only 1.5B model; transfer to 7B/70B+ is unknown.
  • Single reward signal (outcome only) and single RL algorithm (GRPO).
  • No evidence RL creates strategies absent in pre-training/SFT — tension with ProRL observations.
  • Narrow compositional scope (2-part → 3–5 part linear chains only).
  • Code and checkpoints not confirmed as open-source.

Relevance / 相关性

本文直接回应当前 RL 训练推理模型的核心问题：RL 究竟为什么能提升推理？它为数据构造提供了机制层面的指导——在预训练/SFT 阶段保证推理策略多样性，在 RL 阶段引入难度递进的数据——对构建可扩展的推理训练管线具有重要参考价值。

Open Questions

  • Does the two-mechanism framework hold on natural-language benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH) and code reasoning?
  • How do process rewards, DPO, and PPO shift the selection-vs-improvement balance?
  • Can scaled-up models or longer RL training induce genuinely novel strategies absent from pre-training?
  • Would a learned (not rule-based) strategy classifier change the conclusions?
  • Are the findings transferable when the base model already has a strong bias toward a single reasoning style?
  • Will the authors release code, datasets, and model checkpoints?
