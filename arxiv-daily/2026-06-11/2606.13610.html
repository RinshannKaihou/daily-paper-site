One Polluted Page Is Enough

Search-augmented LLMs are trivially hijacked: a single fake product page at rank-1 is enough to make mainstream commercial and open-weight models recommend nonexistent products — and reasoning makes it worse.

Minghao Luo & Liang Chen · CUHK · FORGE Benchmark · 12 models tested · 2026-06-11
Key Finding
A single rank-1 polluted web page fools every tested LLM into recommending a fake product. All 12 models — commercial and open-weight — are vulnerable without exception.
Key Metric
73.8% Highest fooled rate (Ministral-3R) with top-3 entity replacement. A single rank-1 page already yields ~27%.
Why This Matters
Generative search is deployed to billions of users. This attack requires no model access — just one SEO-optimized web page — and no existing defense works.
Critical Edge
Reasoning backfires. Models that think longer fabricate social-proof phrasing to justify fake picks. Disabling reasoning cuts fooled rates by 9–18 pp.

Rank Primacy Effect

A polluted page at rank 1 alone fools the most vulnerable models in ~27% of cells. The same page at ranks 2–10 is nearly inert (1–4%).

Reasoning Backfires

Within-model ablation shows disabling reasoning reduces fooled rates:

  • Qwen3.5-9B: −18.2 pp (p<0.01)
  • GLM-4.6V-Flash: −8.9 pp (p<0.01)
Fooled outputs invent social-proof phrasing absent from the polluted documents — e.g., "frequently recommended in V2EX-style technical communities" or "drop-tested across multiple impacts". Resisting outputs that mention but reject the fake brand have ~6× longer reasoning traces.

Category Vulnerability

Vulnerability tracks brand-knowledge stability (Friedman χ²(14)=99.4, p<10⁻¹⁴). Everyday categories with thin brand priors are most exposed.

Benchmark Design

  • FORGE — Fake Online Recommendations in Generative Environments
  • 5 scenarios × 3 categories × 15 products = 225 products
  • Each product: 10 real search results → local rewrite → fake brand injection
  • Pollution preserves rank, URL, source, length, style, context
  • Metric: Rec(t,r) = 𝟙[t ∈ r] — validated with 0.30% FP rate

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • First benchmark isolating web-content pollution from training-data poisoning and prompt injection
  • Ethically controlled: local rewrites, no live-web pollution
  • Rigorous validation: anchor pipeline, FP audits, clean-bundle probes
  • Cross-model, cross-category, cross-attack, cross-lingual evaluation

Limitations

  • Chinese-centric main evaluation; English replication limited to 3 categories
  • Attack not adversarially optimized — reported rates are likely lower bounds
  • Static snapshot (April 2026); doesn't capture evolving SEO dynamics
  • Reasoning ablation only on 2 open-weight models
