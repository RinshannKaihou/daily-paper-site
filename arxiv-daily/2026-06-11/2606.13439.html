S-GBT: Smooth Growth Bound Tensor for Certified Robustness Against Word Substitution Attacks in NLP

Mohammed Bouri · Mohammed Erradi · Adnane Saoud — UM6P

Why This Matters

Second-order curvature regularization opens a new angle for certified NLP defense — constraining how gradients change, not just their magnitude.

Critical Edge

Requires smooth activations (tanh) — the Hessian vanishes under ReLU, making this incompatible with modern Transformer architectures.

Methodology

Threat model. Synonym substitution: each word wi is replaced by one of k = 8 nearest GloVe neighbors within Euclidean distance 0.5. The goal is to certify f(x′) = f(x) for every perturbation in Sadv(x).

GBM & S-GBT. The Growth Bound Matrix M element-wise bounds |∂Fi/∂xj| (first order). The Smooth Growth Bound Tensor T bounds |∂²Fi/∂xj∂xk| (second order). Together via Taylor expansion with Lagrange remainder:

|Fi(x′) − Fi(x)| ≤ Σj Mij|δj| + ½ Σj,k Ti,j,k|δj||δk|

Training objective. Both terms join the cross-entropy loss: L = Lce + βLGBM + γLS-GBT, directly penalizing local sensitivity and curvature.

Architecture derivations. Explicit S-GBT bounds are derived for BiLSTM (Prop. 2) via interval arithmetic on gate pre-activations, and for TextCNN (Prop. 3) exploiting the affine structure of convolutions before the activation function.

Results & Highlights

Evaluated on IMDB (binary sentiment, 25k/25k) and Yahoo! Answers (10-class topic, 1.4M/50k) with BiLSTM and CNN models, using 300-d GloVe embeddings. Three strong attacks (PWWS, GA, PSO) implemented via OpenAttack on 1,000 correctly classified test samples each.

Strengths

  • Complete theoretical framework with detailed proofs — GBM → S-GBT upgrade is a natural extension
  • Realizable S-GBT expressions for both BiLSTM and CNN architectures
  • Simple training change: add two regularization terms to the loss
  • Significant robustness gains on complex tasks without sacrificing clean accuracy
  • Authors openly discuss method limitations

Limitations

  • Hessian vanishes under ReLU or linear dynamics — restricted to smooth activations (tanh)
  • Not validated on Transformer / LLM architectures
  • Only two datasets and three attack types; synonym sets must be pre-defined
  • No quantitative training-time or memory-overhead analysis
  • Cannot handle variable-length perturbations (deletion, insertion, paraphrase)

Open Questions

  • Can S-GBT scale to Transformer / BERT / LLM where self-attention creates quadratic Hessian interactions?
  • Does robustness hold on richer tasks (NLI, QA, multi-label) and more diverse attacks (deletion, insertion, paraphrase)?
  • What is the real training-time and memory cost — could Hessian approximation or stochastic sampling help?
  • Could ReLU networks adopt S-GBT via smooth activation replacement or softmax-pooling, and at what cost?
  • How tight is the certified bound versus actual attack success rate — can targeted attacks expose a gap?
