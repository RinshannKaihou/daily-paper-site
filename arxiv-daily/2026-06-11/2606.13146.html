Why This Matters
Enables reliable regime detection in messy real-world time series where outliers are the norm, not the exception — economics, public health, conflict monitoring.
Critical Edge
Does not scale to large T (pairwise dissimilarities recomputed each iteration). No uncertainty quantification for state assignments or weights. Soft weights, not exact zeros.

How It Works

FWJM jointly estimates a state sequence and state-conditional feature weights for multivariate time series. Three forces shape the objective:

f(s, W, m) = /* weighted PAM dispersion */ Σ_t Σ_p ws_t,p dt·m,p
         + ζ Σ_k Σ_p wkp log wkp  /* entropy → uniform weights */
         + λ Σ 𝟙(s_t ≠ s_{t+1})  /* jump penalty → temporal smoothness */

Alternating Optimization

  • Medoids — greedy selection minimizing within-cluster weighted dissimilarity.
  • State sequence — exact via dynamic programming (Viterbi-style).
  • Feature weights — closed-form softmax over per-cluster dispersions.

Robustness

Continuous-feature Gower dissimilarity is wrapped with Tukey's biweight loss (tuning constant c = 4.685 → ~95% asymptotic efficiency). Outliers are down-weighted automatically. Supports mixed-type data (continuous + categorical + ordinal).

Results at a Glance

Simulation (Student-t HMM, ν=3)

  • FWJM achieves ARI and BAC at or near 1.0 across almost all settings.
  • Under 5% contamination: robust FWJM 0.99 vs non-robust nr-FWJM 0.63 (A, K=3); 0.99 vs 0.67 (A, K=4).
  • Competitors (sparse JM, plain JM, k-means) frequently collapse to ARI ≈ 0.
  • COSA and Student-t HMM are the strongest alternatives but remain inferior in long-series scenarios.

Real-World Validation

  • Kosovo conflict homicides (T=1081, P=3): K=2 separates low-violence days from the spring-1999 massacre period; state weights differ markedly across civilian/military death types.
  • Euro-area macro indicators (T=49, P=36): K=2 with median Silhouette 53.5%; 15 variables receive near-zero weight in one cluster, revealing state-specific macro drivers.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Interpretable: each regime tells you which variables matter.
  • Tukey biweight gives large empirical gains under contamination.
  • Handles continuous, categorical, and ordinal features uniformly.
  • DP-based state sequence — no brute-force search.
  • Two very different empirical applications; public code.

Limitations

  • O(T²) per iteration — does not scale to long series.
  • Soft weights, not exact sparsity; domain reasoning still needed.
  • Sensitive to λ and ζ — only 25% of combos yield Silhouette > 50% in the macro example.
  • Non-convex; multi-start heuristic, no global guarantee.
  • No confidence intervals or bootstrap on states/weights.

相关性 / Relevance

FWJM 将稳健统计、特征重要性和时序状态切换整合为一个可解释的框架，适合处理经济学、公共卫生、社会科学等领域中常见的多变量时间序列：数据常伴随异常值、结构断点和不同变量在不同时期作用差异较大的情况。相比需要完整参数设定的隐马尔可夫模型，FWJM 提供了一种半参数、基于相异度的替代方案。

Open Questions

  • Can approximate nearest-neighbor or coreset methods scale FWJM to long series without quality loss?
  • Would L₁ or hard-thresholding yield exact variable selection?
  • How does FWJM compare to Bayesian nonparametric regime-switching or deep state-space models?
  • Can bootstrap or Bayesian extensions provide uncertainty on states and weights?
  • Is Silhouette-based model selection itself robust to outliers?
