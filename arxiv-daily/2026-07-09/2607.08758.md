> **一句话总结：** 作者提出 IdeaGene 框架与 IG-Bench 基准，把论文表示为可审计的"Idea Genome"对象并用 GenomeDiff 对齐以追踪思想继承；在 14 个 LLM 科研系统上测试发现，最强系统在谱系推理 (IG-Exam) 的精确准确率仅 27.3%，谱系结构化上下文主要提升 Heredity 维度但会重排而非均匀提升系统排名。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces the IdeaGene framework and IG-Bench, a benchmark that represents papers as typed, evidence-grounded "Idea Genome" objects aligned by GenomeDiff records to audit scientific lineage (1,961 traces, 1,085 genomes, 920 diffs across 10 domains). On IG-Exam (42 task types, 1,029 instances) the best system (Claude Code on GPT-5.5) reaches only 27.3% exact accuracy vs GPT-5.5's 23.1%, and in IG-Arena the lineage setting improves Heredity from 61.9 (Question|Mutation) to 84.2 (Lineage|Hybrid) while Variation/Selection stay flat — revealing a compositional bottleneck, not a retrieval one.

## 核心贡献

- **IdeaGene 框架**：定义了 Idea Genome（类型化、证据锚定、谱系相关的最小可审计想法对象）、Genome 抽取算子、以及 GenomeDiff 对齐记录；并引入 6 种操作性进化动力学（mutation、adaptive radiation、hybridization、speciation、niche competition、isolation）用于跨论文的思想继承建模。
- **IG-Bench 基准（双部分）**：IG-Exam 提供 42 个任务类型、1,029 个实例的封闭式谱系推理评测；IG-Arena 在三种受控信息设置（Question / Library / Lineage）下评测开放式想法生成，并提出谱系条件化的 Population-Evolution Score (PES = H/V/S 均值) 作为主指标，ELO 作为补充诊断。
- **大规模标注资源**：覆盖 10 个科学领域（NLP、CV、多模态、生物、化学、物理、材料、医学、数学、气候/能源等），含 1,961 条金色谱系、1,085 个 Idea Genome 对象、920 条成对 GenomeDiff；由 50 名研究生标注员参与，动力学标签的标注者间一致性达 84.7%。
- **关键实证发现**：揭示了"组合性瓶颈"——模型能恢复局部信号但难以同时保持父节点选择、驱动机制分配、对象命运与验证标记的一致性；工具脚手架主要帮助 T2 继承追踪（GPT-5.5: 25.7% → 37.9% via Claude Code），但几乎不改善 T4 谱系验证。
- **谱系 vs. 论文级上下文的区分性证据**：结构化谱系上下文在平均上仅带来 +4.4 PES 提升，但其主要作用是诊断性——它能区分"会使用 Idea Genome 结构"与"只会利用更多文本"的系统；PES 的增益几乎全部来自 Heredity 维度。

## Key Contributions

- **IdeaGene framework for scientific lineage**: Defines Idea Genome objects (each `g_i = (t_i, z_i, e_i, c_i)` with role type ∈ {niche, mechanism, observation, limitation, delta, claim}), a genome-extraction operator, and GenomeDiff alignment records that mark source objects as Inherited/Mutated/Lost and target objects as Novel/External, operationalized through six evolutionary dynamics with fixed priority resolution rules (Hybridization before Speciation, etc.).
- **IG-Bench, a two-part benchmark**: IG-Exam (42 task types, 1,029 instances across four capability axes T1–T4) for closed-form lineage reasoning scored by exact match; IG-Arena for open-ended generation under Question/Library/Lineage settings, scored by a lineage-conditioned Population-Evolution Score (PES) decomposed into Heredity/Variation/Selection, with ELO as a preference diagnostic.
- **Large-scale, audited dataset**: 1,961 golden lineage traces, 1,085 curated Idea Genome objects, and 920 pairwise GenomeDiff records across 10 scientific domains; built with 50 graduate annotators, achieving 84.7% inter-annotator agreement on dynamics labels and 80% human–judge concordance in IG-Arena.
- **Compositional-bottleneck finding**: Frontier systems recover local signals but fail to keep parent choice, driver assignment, object fate, and verification flags jointly consistent — the strongest system reaches only 27.3% exact accuracy, and the difficulty gradient rises T1→T4 (T4 best only 17.4%).
- **Differentiated effect of structured lineage context**: Lineage context's median PES gain is +4.4 (Kimi-K2 +6.9, MiniMax +9.9, GPT-5.5 only +2.3), reshuffling rankings rather than uniformly helping; PES gap is Heredity-driven (61.9→84.2) while Variation/Selection stay nearly constant.

## Methodology

**IdeaGene representation layer.** Each paper/proposal `p` is mapped to a set of Idea Genome objects `G(p) = {g_i}`. Every object carries (i) a functional role type `t_i` ∈ {niche, mechanism, observation, limitation, delta, claim}, (ii) a content description `z_i`, (iii) an evidence pointer `e_i` to a section/paragraph/figure/equation, and (iv) optional constraints `c_i`. A valid Idea Genome must be typed, evidence-grounded, minimally self-contained, and lineage-relevant. The extraction operator `E: p → G(p)` is "narrower than summarization": it keeps only objects that will later support diff alignment.

**GenomeDiff alignment.** Given predecessor `p_s` and successor `p_t`, a GenomeDiff `Δ_{s→t}` aligns objects by type and semantic role, marking source objects as Inherited/Mutated/Lost and unaligned target objects as Novel/External. Each record stores the primary transition driver, the relation to the surrounding EcologyContext (task/benchmark/community), and an evidence-backed rationale. Crucially, *lineage ≠ co-location*: shared task ecology without driver inheritance is classified as Niche Competition, not descent.

**Six operational evolutionary dynamics** classify diff patterns: Mutation (YOLO→YOLOv2), Adaptive Radiation (Transformer→ViT), Hybridization (CLIP+LLM→LLaVA), Speciation (Faster R-CNN→DETR), Niche Competition (Faster R-CNN vs. YOLO), Isolation (BERT vs. YOLO). Ambiguous cases follow a fixed priority rule for consistent annotation.

**IG-Bench construction (4 stages):** (1) seed nomination of landmark papers; (2) trace expansion via citation links + semantic search + curation into 3–7-paper lineages; (3) multi-pass LLM-assisted genome extraction + expert audit + pairwise GenomeDiff annotation; (4) benchmark-level audit (schema validity, answer contracts, temporal consistency, anonymization leakage, trace coherence). 50 graduate annotators (CS, biology, physics, materials, etc.) validated labels, item difficulty, and pairwise battles; disagreements adjudicated by a third annotator.

**IG-Exam scoring** uses strict exact match: every required field (parent, driver, fate, etc.) must be correct simultaneously. Four capability axes: T1 Genome Abstraction, T2 Inheritance Tracing, T3 Evolutionary Reasoning, T4 Lineage Verification (the generation-proximate axis).

**IG-Arena PES metric.** Each proposal `x` is judged as a candidate descendant of ordered lineage `L` and neighboring population `P` by a panel of 3 model judges (position-randomized) on three 0–100 dimensions mirroring Darwin's conditions: Heredity H(x|L), Variation V(x|P), Selection S(x|L,P); `PES = (H+V+S)/3`. Reliability: Krippendorff's α = 0.74 inter-judge, 80% human concordance. ELO (Bradley–Terry) is reported as a complementary pairwise preference diagnostic. Three controlled settings — Question-only, Library (unordered summaries), Lineage (ordered Idea Genome objects + GenomeDiff) — form an ablation isolating parametric ideation vs. paper-level context vs. lineage structure.

## Results & Highlights

- **IG-Exam ceiling is low.** The best system overall is the CLI harness Claude Code on GPT-5.5 at **27.3% exact accuracy**; the best direct LLM GPT-5.5 reaches **23.1%**; MiniMax-M2.7 is the weakest at **11.9%**. This is on a 42-task / 1,029-instance exact-match profile where partial-credit answers score zero.
- **Capability-axis gradient (T1→T4).** Best per-axis scores fall as compositional burden rises: T1 Genome Abstraction best **34.4%** (Claude Opus 4.7 + Codex), T2 Inheritance Tracing best **37.9%** (GPT-5.5 + Claude Code), T3 Evolutionary Reasoning best **25.3%** (GPT-5.5 + Claude Code), T4 Lineage Verification best only **17.4%** (Qwen3.6-Max-Preview) — the generation-proximate axis is the hardest.
- **Tool scaffolding helps T2 most.** Adding Claude Code to GPT-5.5 lifts T2 Inheritance Tracing from **25.7% → 37.9%** (+12.2), but gains shrink on T3 (~+2) and nearly vanish / invert on T4 (GPT-5.5 T4: 16.0% → 12.7% with Claude Code), showing scaffolds aid retrieval, not compositional consistency.
- **IG-Arena PES is Heredity-driven.** Decomposing by setting×dynamics: Variation stays at **82.7–84.7** and Selection at **79.7–82.2** across all rows, while Heredity explains the gap — Question|Mutation scores Heredity **61.9** / PES **69.2**, whereas Lineage|Hybrid reaches Heredity **84.2** / PES **83.6** (Heredity +5.4 from Question|Hybrid to Lineage|Hybrid).
- **Lineage context's effect is system-dependent (rank reshuffle).** Median PES gain Question→Lineage is **+4.4**; GPT-5.5 gains only **+2.3** (it already scores PES 85.2 under Question-only), Kimi-K2-Thinking gains **+6.9**, MiniMax **+9.9**. Under Question-only, dynamics labels are a broad mix of Mutation/Adaptive Radiation/Hybridization, but Library and Lineage concentrate ≥97% on Hybridization.
- **ELO diverges from PES.** Spearman ρ = **0.82** between ELO and PES rankings; divergence is clearest when a fluent-but-lineage-incoherent proposal wins pairwise battles over a less-polished but better-grounded one (e.g., CoI-Agent has ELO 1249.9 but PES only 80.5, vs. Codex GPT-5.5 ELO 1476.1 / PES 86.7).
- **Annotation quality.** Inter-annotator agreement on dynamics labels is **84.7%** before adjudication; human judges reach **80%** agreement with the model-judge panel in IG-Arena; PES inter-judge Krippendorff's α = **0.74**.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: high and well-motivated.** The shift from paper-level/citation-graph relevance to a below-paper "Idea Genome + GenomeDiff" object is genuinely new relative to LitSearch/SciRepEval/citation-intent work, and the six-dynamics taxonomy with fixed priority resolution rules is a concrete operationalization rather than a vague biological metaphor. The closest prior work cited (Intern-Atlas) is acknowledged but distinguished as infrastructure rather than an auditable benchmark.
- **Rigor of evaluation design: strong.** Exact-match scoring on IG-Exam correctly exposes the compositional-consistency failures the authors care about (right parent + wrong driver scores zero), and the Question/Library/Lineage three-setting ablation is a clean way to isolate lineage structure from paper text. PES's decomposition into H/V/S and the explicit ELO-vs-PES divergence analysis are thoughtful.
- **Reproducibility: partial concern.** The 1,085 Idea Genome objects, 920 GenomeDiff records, and 1,961 traces are built by LLM-assisted extraction then expert audit; while inter-annotator agreement (84.7%) and human–judge concordance (80%) are reported, the genome-extraction prompts, judge-prompts, and the anonymization leakage checks are not fully detailed in the main text, and the gold labels themselves are LLM-mediated artifacts that other groups may struggle to reproduce exactly.
- **Significance: a plausible new eval axis.** The "plausibility exceeds lineage coherence" finding (Finding 4) and the Heredity-driven PES gap are the kind of results that could redirect auto-research evaluation, but the headline "27.3% ceiling" depends on a strict exact-match regime the authors themselves designed.
- **Red flags / specific weaknesses.**
  - *System-version inflation / unverifiable backbones:* all results hinge on named-but-not-publicly-released model versions (GPT-5.5, Claude Opus 4.7, Qwen3.6-Max-Preview, Gemini-3.1-pro-preview, DeepSeek-V4-Pro, GLM-5.1, MiniMax-M2.7). These cannot be independently re-run by readers as of the paper date, undermining exact reproducibility of the leaderboard.
  - *T4 leaderboard weakness:* on the generation-proximate T4 axis, the best system is Qwen3.6 (17.4%), not the top-ranked GPT-5.5/Claude systems — but the paper's narrative leans heavily on the headline 27.3% (dominated by T2-retrieval gains), which slightly overstates how much "lineage competence" the strongest systems actually show.
  - *Missing ablation:* there is no controlled ablation of the six-dynamics taxonomy itself (e.g., collapsing to a coarser {lineage vs. no-lineage} scheme) to show the full taxonomy buys evaluation signal rather than annotation complexity; only 4% (n=15) of Question-mode items fall under Speciation, so that dynamics category is under-powered.
  - *Cherry-picked canonical examples:* the dynamics table uses high-recall canonical cases (YOLO→YOLOv2, Transformer→ViT) that match the taxonomy by construction; edge-case annotation reliability on the 1,961 long-tail traces is asserted (84.7%) but not shown per-dynamics.
  - *ELO/PES divergence (ρ=0.82) is moderate, not large:* the claim that "pairwise preference captures surface appeal" is directionally right but the 0.82 correlation also means PES and ELO agree most of the time, slightly weakening the case that a new metric was strictly necessary.

## Strengths

- Conceptually original framing (idea inheritance as a genome/diff alignment problem) with a concrete, auditable object layer rather than a metaphor.
- Two-part benchmark design (closed-form IG-Exam + open-ended IG-Arena) lets the same substrate bridge understanding and generation, and the moderate correlation between the two is itself a finding.
- PES is a principled metric: grounded in Darwin's three conditions (heredity/variation/selection), decomposable, with reported inter-judge α (0.74) and human concordance (80%).
- Broad, current system coverage (14 LLM scientists across direct LLMs, research agents, CLI harnesses) and a backbone-controlled comparison (five GPT-5.5 systems) that cleanly separates retrieval help from lineage competence.
- Honest negative findings: agents do not beat the direct LLM on generation (PES), tool scaffolds hurt T4, and structured context reshuffles rather than uniformly lifts systems.

## Limitations

- The six dynamics are *operational categories, not an exhaustive theory*; real transitions often mix patterns, and the framework assigns a single primary driver, which may flatten genuinely multi-mechanism successors.
- All backbone models are pre-release/preview versions whose exact behavior readers cannot replicate.
- LLM-mediated gold labels: genome extraction and (partly) GenomeDiff records are LLM-assisted then audited, so the benchmark's ground truth inherits the LLM's abstraction biases despite the 84.7% annotator agreement.
- IG-Arena's 30 tasks / 10 domains × 3 settings = 1,260 proposals is modest for an open-ended generation arena; per-domain statistical power is limited.
- No released baseline reproduction recipe (prompts, judge prompts, scaffold configs) in the main text; reliability depends on the project page/code release.
- The framework's evaluation value depends on the unverified assumption that Idea Genome granularity is the right level — too fine and annotation becomes noise, too coarse and it collapses back into paper summaries.

## Relevance

本工作直接服务于"自动化科研系统评测"和"LLM 科研想法生成"这两条研究主线，提出的谱系一致性（lineage coherence）视角对评估 AI Scientist、CoI-Agent、AI-Researcher 等自动化研究代理是否能真正继承并扩展前人机制，提供了比单纯新颖性/流畅度更严格的诊断框架。其对"组合性瓶颈"和"plausibility 高于 lineage coherence"的实证发现，对设计下一代具备组合验证模块的 idea-generation 系统具有直接指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the six-dynamics taxonomy buy measurable evaluation signal over a coarser {lineage / no-lineage} binary, or does most discriminative power come from the GenomeDiff object alignment alone?
- How stable are the leaderboard rankings if IG-Exam moves from strict exact-match to partial-credit scoring on joint fields — is the "compositional bottleneck" an artifact of the all-or-nothing metric?
- Since research agents (AI Scientist v2, CoI-Agent) fail to beat the direct LLM on PES, what specific agentic capability would be needed to turn retrieval gains into lineage-coherent generation?
- Can the Heredity component of PES be optimized directly as a training/compositional-verification signal, rather than only used as an eval metric?
- How do the results generalize beyond the 10 domains tested, and does annotation reliability hold for social-science/humanities lineages where mechanism inheritance is less crisply defined?
- Given ELO–PES correlation of 0.82, under what conditions does PES most diverge from pairwise preference, and can a cheap PES proxy be derived from ELO + a small lineage-coherence probe?
