> **一句话总结：** 本文首次系统研究了 boomerang distillation 中"层补丁顺序"(layer patching order) 的设计问题，将最优补丁顺序形式化为 KL 加权布尔格上的最短路径问题，并提出贪心算法 KLPatch，把搜索复杂度从 O(2^N) 降到 O(N²)，在 Qwen3-4B 上 AUIC 达到 0.533（第 100 百分位，并列最优），显著超过 last-to-first 基线的 0.462。

> **TL;DR:** This paper gives the first systematic study of layer patching order in boomerang distillation (zero-shot model size interpolation). It formalizes finding the optimal patching order as a shortest-path problem on a KL-weighted Boolean lattice and proposes KLPatch, a greedy O(N²) algorithm. On Qwen3-4B-Base, KLPatch reaches AUIC 0.533 (100th percentile, tying the best permutation among 200 sampled) vs 0.462 for the last-to-first baseline, while requiring only ~37 minutes of KL evaluations vs ~64,000 minutes for exhaustive search — and it provably recovers the global optimum (AUPIC 0.397) on DistilBERT/Wikitext.

## 核心贡献

- **首次形式化"层补丁"为组合优化问题**：把 boomerang distillation 中学生层补丁顺序的选择定义两个等价问题——基于子集的最优插值（Problem 1）与基于排列的最优顺序（Problem 2），指出二者在贪心构造下算法上坍缩为同一种"嵌套子集链"。
- **最短路径刻画（理论）**：证明在等距模型大小假设下，KL 加权布尔格（interpolation graph G）上的最短路径恰好等价于相对教师的对数 AUPIC（AUPIC_log^T）的最优排列（Proposition 1），把 N! 维组合搜索转化为 DAG 最短路问题。
- **大规模实证刻画**：在 DistilBERT/DistilGPT2（6 层，720 种顺序全枚举）和 Qwen3-4B、Qwen3-8B、Pythia-6.9B（每个采样 200 种顺序）上系统研究补丁顺序，发现 (a) 顺序对插值质量影响巨大；(b) 简单的 last-to-first / first-to-last 往往意外强劲但不总最优；(c) 好的顺序在排列空间中聚类于最优附近（用 Spearman footrule 距离度量）。
- **KLPatch 贪心算法**：每步贪心选择使 KL 散度到教师最小的层进行补丁，把搜索复杂度从 O(2^N)/O(N!) 降到 O(N²) 次 KL 评估，在多数模型上找到接近最优的补丁轨迹。
- **反直觉发现**：指出"任意补丁学生层来增大模型反而可能降低性能"，挑战了"越大越好"的传统认知，凸显补丁顺序是构建插值模型的关键设计选择。

## Key Contributions

- **First formalization of layer patching as combinatorial optimization.** The paper defines two equivalent formulations — optimal interpolation over subsets (Problem 1) and optimal permutation of layers (Problem 2) — and shows they collapse under greedy nested-chain construction, even though they differ in scoring (per-cardinality vs aggregated).
- **A shortest-path characterization with a theorem.** Proposition 1 proves that, under an equidistant-model-size assumption, shortest paths in the KL-weighted interpolation graph G are exactly the optimal permutations w.r.t. teacher-relative log-AUPIC, reducing an N! search to a DAG shortest-path problem. Supporting propositions bound the teacher-vs-data (Prop. 2) and log-vs-raw (Prop. 3) gaps.
- **Large-scale empirical phenomenology.** Exhaustive sweep over all 720 orders on DistilBERT/DistilGPT2 and 200 random orders on Qwen3-4B/8B and Pythia-6.9B shows patching order strongly shapes interpolation, that simple last-to-first/first-to-last orders are surprisingly strong but not always optimal, and that good orders cluster near the optimum in Spearman-footrule permutation space.
- **The KLPatch algorithm.** A greedy O(N²) procedure that picks the cheapest KL edge at each step approximately solves the shortest-path problem, cutting complexity from O(2^N) and yielding near-optimal interpolation trajectories in practice.
- **A counter-intuitive negative result.** Naively increasing an interpolated model's size by arbitrary patching can degrade performance, contradicting the conventional "bigger is better" scaling view and motivating principled patch-order selection.

## Methodology

The paper studies **boomerang distillation** [Kangaslahti et al., 2026]: starting from a teacher T (M layers) and a smaller student S (N<M layers) distilled from it, one builds intermediate-size models by **patching** — replacing contiguous student blocks with their corresponding teacher blocks. The sequence in which student blocks are patched defines a trajectory of intermediate models from S to T, requiring no extra training.

**Formalization (Section 4).** For each cardinality k there are C(N,k) candidate subsets A; the best k-subset maximizes a quality function f_{S,T}(A). Collecting argmaxes across k defines the *optimal interpolation* (Problem 1), which costs O(2^N) evaluations. The alternative *optimal permutation* (Problem 2) searches N! orders but admits a clean greedy interpretation.

**Metrics (Section 4.2).** Interpolation quality is summarized by the area under the curve over model size: **AUIC** for higher-is-better metrics (accuracy) and **AUPIC** for lower-is-better metrics (perplexity), with log and teacher-relative variants.

**Shortest-path reduction (Section 4.3).** Patching states form the Boolean lattice on {1,...,N} (the *interpolation graph* G). Each edge M_A → M_{A∪{i}} is weighted by E_{x~D_cal}[ KL( p_T(·|x) ‖ p_{M_{A∪{i}}}(·|x) ) ]. Proposition 1 shows shortest paths in G equal the optimal permutations under AUPIC_log^T (with Prop. 2/3 bounding the gap to the raw data-AUPIC actually reported).

**KLPatch (Section 6, Algorithm 1).** A greedy procedure: at each step, patch the student layer whose patched version minimizes KL divergence to the teacher on a small calibration set (64 Pile samples), commit it, and repeat for all N layers. Since iteration k evaluates N−k candidates, total cost is O(N²) KL evaluations — a greedy approximation to the shortest path in G.

**Experiments.** (1) Full enumeration of all 720 orders on DistilBERT/DistilGPT2 evaluated on Wikitext pseudo-perplexity/perplexity. (2) 200 random orders per model on Qwen3-4B-Base, Qwen3-8B-Base, Pythia-6.9B, evaluated on a 10-task classification suite (ARC-e/c, BoolQ, HellaSwag, OpenBookQA, PIQA, WinoGrande, RACE, MMLU, RTE), 3 generation tasks (GSM8K, IFEval, MATH), and Wikitext perplexity via lm-evaluation-harness. (3) Spearman-footrule distance analysis showing near-optimal orders cluster around the optimum. (4) KLPatch evaluation on all models plus an ablation suite (calibration set, divergence choice, iterative vs non-iterative) and a Llama-3.2-3B special case.

## Results & Highlights

- **On DistilBERT/Wikitext, last-to-first patching is provably globally optimal**, reaching AUPIC = 0.397 (100th percentile) — identical to the minimum-AUPIC interpolation and to the global shortest KL path; first-to-last patching is much worse at AUPIC = 0.968 (38.75th percentile), against a mean AUPIC of 0.909 (Table 3).
- **KLPatch recovers the optimum on DistilBERT (AUPIC 0.397, 100th percentile)** and is near-optimal on DistilGPT2 (AUPIC 0.831, 95.28th percentile), close to the global shortest KL path (0.824) and to last-to-first (0.815) (Table 3).
- **On Qwen3-4B-Base downstream tasks, KLPatch achieves AUIC = 0.533 at the 100th percentile** (tying the best permutation among 200 sampled), vs last-to-first 0.462 (89.16th %ile) and first-to-last 0.441 (69.46th %ile); the max-AUIC interpolation upper bound is 0.580 (Table 4).
- **On Qwen3-8B-Base, KLPatch reaches AUIC 0.571 (99.51st percentile)**, beating last-to-first (0.495, 85.71%) by +0.076 AUIC and first-to-last (0.464, 63.55%) by +0.107 AUIC; it beats all but one of the 200 random orderings.
- **On Pythia-6.9b, KLPatch AUPIC = 0.567 ties the best permutation (100th percentile)** while last-to-first is markedly worse (0.839, 83.74th %ile); on AUIC, KLPatch scores 0.379 (99.51st %ile) vs last-to-first 0.261 (82.27%) — though first-to-last edges it on this model (0.410) (Tables 4–5).
- **Massive compute savings**: KLPatch yields 548× (Pythia-6.9b) to 1,722× (Qwen3-4B-Base) speedup over exhaustive search — e.g., ~37 minutes vs ~64,302 minutes on Qwen3-4B-Base (Table 6).
- **KL divergence is a strong proxy for perplexity**: Pearson r = 0.981 (DistilBERT) and r = 0.955 (DistilGPT2) between KL-to-teacher and Wikitext perplexity; KL path length vs AUPIC gives r = 0.958 / 0.903 (Appendix G.1), empirically justifying the shortest-path reduction.
- **Negative result**: KLPatch does *not* work out-of-the-box on Llama-3.2-3B and severely underperforms first-to-last patching; a heuristic that forces patching layer 1 first recovers good performance — traced to Llama's student being initialized with the first two teacher layers rather than the last two (Appendix J).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Genuinely new. Prior boomerang-distillation work (Kangaslahti et al., 2026) considered only two patching orders (first-to-last, last-to-first) and left the combinatorial design space explicitly open; this paper opens that space, supplies the optimization formalism, the shortest-path theorem (Prop. 1), and the KLPatch heuristic. The connection to the KL-based iterative layer-pruning of Zhang et al. (2024) is honestly acknowledged (they remove layers; here layers are patched to *build* interpolations).
- **Rigor**: Strong on the small-model regime, where the full 720-order enumeration on DistilBERT/DistilGPT2 grounds every claim empirically and the KL↔perplexity correlations (r up to 0.981) validate the theoretical reduction. The theory is clean (Prop. 1 plus gap bounds Prop. 2/3) but rests on assumptions (equidistant model sizes, teacher-relative log scale) that the authors themselves flag and bound — honest handling.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Models, datasets, the downstream task suite, calibration set (64 Pile samples), and hardware (A100 40GB / H100 80GB) are specified, and everything builds on the public Kangaslahti et al. codebase. However, no code release is mentioned in the main text, and random seed / exact checkpoint versions are not fully pinned.
- **Significance**: Practically useful — KLPatch turns an infeasible O(2^N) search into a 25–38-minute computation while matching or beating strong baselines, and the "naive patching can hurt" finding is a real cautionary tale for anyone building model-size families.
- **Red flags / weak spots**:
  - **Baselines are coarse.** The only fixed-order competitors are the two sequential orders from the prior paper; there is no comparison to layer-pruning-style selection criteria (activation similarity, cosine distance) used as patching policies, so "KLPatch beats baselines" partly means "beats two naive orderings." The authors admit cosine distance correlates poorly with downstream but never benchmark it as a patching policy head-to-head.
  - **Llama failure undercuts generality.** KLPatch needs a hand-crafted "patch layer 1 first" fix for Llama-3.2-3B, traced to student-initialization differences — a clear signal the method is not model-agnostic and that the "principled" recipe has an important uncontrolled variable (initialization convention).
  - **Perplexity ≠ downstream gap.** On the LLMs, last-to-first is the best by Wikitext AUPIC but leaves a large downstream-AUIC gap vs the best sampled order; the authors flag this (citing Liu et al., 2023) but it means the very AUPIC metric the shortest-path theorem optimizes is a leaky proxy for the metric practitioners care about.
  - **Headroom vs optimum is unquantified at scale.** True optimal interpolation/permutation is infeasible for N≈17–19, so "near-optimal" on Qwen/Pythia is only ever relative to 200 random samples plus the two fixed orders — the absolute gap to the true optimum remains unknown.
  - **Magnitude of gains varies and is sometimes marginal** (e.g., on Pythia AUIC, first-to-last already hits 0.410 vs KLPatch 0.379).

## Strengths

- First principled, theory-backed treatment of a design choice (patching order) that prior work left ad hoc.
- Clean shortest-path theorem (Proposition 1) with explicit, bounded assumptions and supporting proofs in the appendix.
- Exhaustive small-model enumeration (720 orders) gives ground-truth optima rather than sampling folklore.
- KLPatch is simple, fast (O(N²)), and consistently near-optimal across the models where it applies.
- Honest reporting of the Llama failure and of where simple sequential baselines remain competitive — no cherry-picking.

## Limitations

- KLPatch does not generalize out-of-the-box; Llama-3.2-3B requires a model-specific initialization-aware heuristic.
- All experiments inherit the limitations and initialization conventions of the distilled students from Kangaslahti et al. (2026), which the authors note may itself influence the optimal patching order.
- The theoretical optimality guarantee (Prop. 1) holds for teacher-relative *log*-AUPIC under equidistant sizes — not the raw data-AUPIC reported, so the bridge to practice is empirical (correlations) rather than exact.
- At LLM scale the true optimum is never computed, so claims of "near-optimal" are relative to 200 sampled orders and two fixed baselines.
- Gains over simple sequential baselines are inconsistent across model families; characterizing when patching-order optimization helps most is left as future work.
- No released code or pinned checkpoints reported in the main text.

## Relevance

这篇论文与"高效 LLM / 模型压缩与知识蒸馏"方向高度相关：它在不重新训练的前提下，通过分析层补丁顺序来构造任意中间尺寸的模型族，直接服务于"按算力预算选模型"的部署需求，其 KLPatch 贪心算法和最短路径视角为模型缩放、蒸馏与层剪枝的研究提供了可操作的新工具与理论框架。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Why does KLPatch fail on Llama-3.2-3B specifically, and can a single initialization-aware variant (rather than a hand-coded "patch layer 1 first" rule) unify behavior across Qwen, Pythia, and Llama?
- How do gains scale to much larger teachers (e.g., 70B+) where N is far larger and KL evaluations become expensive — can the teacher-conditioned KL be amortized or approximated rather than recomputed at every step?
- Could a non-greedy shortest-path solver (e.g., Dijkstra on G with KL edges) be made tractable via pruning, and would it beat greedy KLPatch meaningfully?
- Does the "naive patching degrades performance" finding interact with the student's training-token budget or alignment-loss weight, i.e., is the phenomenon a property of patching or of imperfect distillation alignment?
- Can the shortest-path/AUPIC framework extend to non-uniform (variable-size) teacher blocks or to patching within a single block rather than at block granularity?
- How transferable are the near-optimal patching orders discovered for one student to a sibling student distilled with a different seed or corpus from the same teacher?
