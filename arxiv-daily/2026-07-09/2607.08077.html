Key Finding arXiv 2607.08077 · Pre-training · AI Safety

GRAM trains each dual-use capability into its own module, so ablating one removes that capability while the rest survives — at 5× lower cost than data filtering.

Modular Pretraining Enables Access Control · 2026-07-09 Ethan Roland, Murat Cubuktepe, Erick Martinez, Stijn Servaes, Keenan Pepper, Mike Vaiana, Diogo Schwerz de Lucena, Judd Rosenblatt (AE Studio); Addie Foote; Cem Anil, Alex Cloud (Anthropic)
GRAM Gradient-Routed Auxiliary Modules access-control capability-removal machine-unlearning dual-use-safety pretraining

Key Metric

5×lower cost

Amortized training FLOPS vs data filtering in the 800M realistic dual-use setting with 5 capability profiles.

retain CR 0.95 vs filtering 0.96 · core ~1.00

Why This Matters

A pretraining-time path to differentiated capability deployment — distinct from refusal training or post-hoc unlearning — with rare scaling evidence from 50M to 5B parameters.

Critical Edge

Every result is a cross-entropy compute ratio, not a behavioral test — there is no evidence that higher loss on virology text means an actual loss of hazardous capability.

Operationalized as perplexity, not proficiency

01 Methodology

GRAM augments every Transformer MLP block with one permanent core module plus N−1 small per-capability auxiliary modules. There is no learned router and no per-token MoE routing — selection is driven entirely by data labels.

h_out = Σ m_i · E_i(h_in),   m ∈ {0,1}^N,  m_1 = 1 (core always on)

Each parameter partition (core; one per auxiliary capability) is optimized by a separate AdamW optimizer. Three hyperparameters govern batch composition:

  • Auxiliary Factor p_af — sampling frequency of each auxiliary dataset, with core undersampling to stay compute-equal to baseline.
  • Auxiliary Spread p_as — probability the core params also update on an auxiliary batch (lower ⇒ stronger isolation).
  • Core Robustness p_cr — probability a random auxiliary module co-activates on a core batch, training the core to be robust to module presence/absence.

Capability is measured with a compute ratio CR(M, D_i): the fraction of baseline compute needed to reach the same loss — ideal ≥1 on retain sets, 0 on forget sets. Aggregates: CRC (core), CRR (mean retain), CRF (mean forget). An Elicited Forget protocol finetunes on 512 sequences per forget category to probe whether removal is real or merely suppressed.

Evaluation spans three regimes: Simple Stories (synthetic, 4 of 48 categories auxiliary, 26M model); a realistic 800M model on FineWeb-Edu + arXiv + The Stack (core) with virology, cybersecurity, nuclear physics, and Lisp as auxiliary; and a 50M→5B Chinchilla-optimal scaling sweep retaining only virology.

  • MaxEnt's removal is reversible — elicited-forget rebounds to ~0.89 after finetuning; GRAM/FT-LoRA stay low because the capability was never acquired (structural isolation, not suppression).
  • Scaling widens the removal gap — across 50M/100M/200M/400M/800M/2B/5B Chinchilla-optimal models, the data-filtering forget CR decreases with scale (cleaner removal) while retain stays near baseline; GRAM tracks filtering throughout. (Authors flag one anomalous GRAM 5B retain point — Appendix H.)
  • Composability without training — activating all GRAM modules simultaneously (never seen in training) keeps retain high on every auxiliary category; FT-LoRA degrades when multiple adapters are summed.
  • Scales to many categories — 4→20 auxiliary categories on Simple Stories preserves clear retain/forget separation.

+ Strengths

  • Clean access-control / least-privilege framing makes the practical safety use case vivid and deployable.
  • Rare 50M→5B Chinchilla-optimal scaling study; the favorable data-filtering scaling trend is a valuable standalone finding.
  • Fair compute-matched comparison across Filtering, FT-LoRA, FT-Full, MaxEnt (plus DEMix in appendix).
  • Surprising partial-labeling win with a plausible mechanism (absorption effect) and per-token analysis.
  • Intellectual honesty: 5B anomaly, six explicit limitations, and idea lineage are openly disclosed.

– Limitations

  • No behavioral eval — "virology removed" means higher perplexity, not inability to answer virology questions.
  • Single run at 2B and 5B — the scales that carry the central claim lack seed-based error bars.
  • MaxEnt is a weak unlearning baseline; NPO / representation-engineering / task-arithmetic absent.
  • Capability entanglement (overlapping retain/forget) is acknowledged but never stress-tested.
  • No interaction with instruction tuning / RLHF tested — could erode the separation.
  • Productionization is non-trivial: per-module routing + separate optimizers add complexity.

? Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the favorable scaling trend (widening forget gap) persist to frontier scale (tens of billions+) and under multi-epoch / larger auxiliary fractions?
  2. How does GRAM's separation survive instruction tuning and RLHF — reinforced or re-entangled?
  3. Can removal be validated with downstream behavioral benchmarks (virology QA, exploit generation, Lisp completion) rather than cross-entropy?
  4. How does GRAM behave under capability entanglement, where the target overlaps a retained one (general biology vs. virology)?
  5. Against stronger unlearning baselines (NPO, rep-engineering, task arithmetic), does the robustness-to-finetuning edge hold?
  6. What is the theoretical basis for GRAM's composability over FT-LoRA, and does the absorption-effect explanation survive mechanistic investigation?
  7. For open-weight releases, can module ablation be made tamper-resistant against an adversary who holds the weights?

§ Authors & Credibility

11 authors: eight equal-contribution first authors at AE Studio, an independent researcher (Addie Foote), and Anthropic researchers Cem Anil and Alex Cloud. The Anthropic link is material — Alex Cloud co-authored the Gradient Routing precursor (Cloud et al., 2024, arXiv:2410.04332) that GRAM extends, and Anthropic supplied compute for the scaling runs. The method honestly synthesizes three prior ideas (DEMix, gradient routing, separate-optimizer training) rather than claiming wholesale novelty.
