> **一句话总结：** 本文首次将"密度矩阵预测器的特征值"作为待校准对象，提出矩阵温度缩放（通过谱函数作用于特征值）来校准 LLM 语义嵌入的不确定性，理论上证明熵与风险在矩阵校准下等价并给出关键的特征值校准不等式；在 TriviaQA 与 Natural Questions 上验证 Phi 4、Phi 4 Mini、Llama4 Maverick 均系统性过度自信，校准后既降低 ECE 又在多数情况下提升 AUROC（如 Phi 4 在 NQ 上特征值 AUROC 从 0.742 升至 0.794）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces the first calibration framework for eigenvalues of density-matrix predictors built from LLM semantic embeddings, proves an entropy-risk equivalence and a central eigenvalue-calibration inequality, and shows that a matrix temperature-scaling transform optimizes calibration under proper matrix scores; on TriviaQA and Natural Questions with Phi 4 / Phi 4 Mini / Llama4 Maverick, all models are systematically overconfident, and recalibration cuts eigenvalue ECE (e.g., 0.36 → 0.21 for Phi 4 Mini on TriviaQA) while improving AUROC in most settings (e.g., Phi 4 eigenvalue AUROC on Natural Questions rises from 0.742 to 0.794, +0.052).

## 核心贡献

- **首次定义"矩阵校准"与"特征值校准"概念**：将经典分类校准扩展到密度矩阵预测器（即 LLM 输出经语义嵌入构成的外积期望），填补了传统概率校准理论无法直接迁移到特征值空间的空白。
- **建立熵-风险等价定理与特征值校准不等式**：证明在矩阵校准下"期望不确定性（熵）= 模型风险"（Lemma 1、Theorem 2），并给出关键不等式 λmax(D_Y|Λ_X) ≤ Λ_X，揭示朴素按特征值分箱画可靠性图会产生"平台"伪影。
- **提出矩阵温度缩放并证明其最优性**：定义谱函数形式 h_TS(M) ∝ M^α，证明其单射性，并基于校准-锐度分解（Theorem 3）证明风险最小化即校准误差最小化。
- **首次为特征值校准设计可靠性图算法（Algorithm 1）**：通过"先分箱再层次聚类"近似对完整密度矩阵条件化，绕开 Theorem 1 的不等式陷阱，得到有意义的诊断图。
- **系统性实证发现**：现代开源 LLM 在两个 QA 基准上均系统性过度自信（最优温度 > 1），校准后在多数设定下同时改善 ECE 与下游 AUROC。

## Key Contributions

- Defines, for the first time, matrix calibration and eigenvalue calibration for density-matrix predictors obtained from LLM semantic embeddings, formally extending canonical calibration beyond probability vectors.
- Proves an entropy-risk equivalence (Lemma 1, Theorem 2) under matrix calibration and a central eigenvalue-calibration inequality (Theorem 1), showing that conditioning only on the max eigenvalue yields a strict inequality that distorts naive reliability diagrams.
- Introduces matrix temperature scaling h_TS(M) ∝ M^α acting as a spectral function on eigenvalues, proves its injectivity, and proves via a calibration-sharpness decomposition (Theorem 3) that risk minimization is exactly calibration-error minimization.
- Proposes Algorithm 1, a bin-then-hierarchically-cluster reliability diagram that conditions on the full predicted density matrix, the first principled diagnostic tool for eigenvalue calibration.
- Empirically demonstrates that modern open-source LLMs (Phi 4, Phi 4 Mini, Llama4 Maverick) are systematically overconfident across TriviaQA and Natural Questions, and that recalibration improves both ECE and downstream AUROC in most settings.

## Methodology

The paper treats an LLM f plus an embedding model e as a **density-matrix predictor** d(x) = E_{a~f(x)}[e(a)e(a)^⊺], estimated with m sampled answers per query (m=20 at test, m=100 on the dev set). Its eigenvalues live in the simplex and are interpreted as probabilities over latent semantic outcomes.

1. **Calibration notions (Section 3).** Canonical calibration is lifted to two new definitions: a predictor is *matrix calibrated* if D_{Y|d(X)} = d(X) a.s., and *eigenvalue calibrated* if λmax(D_{Y|Λ_X}) = Λ_X a.s. Theorem 1 proves canonical ⇒ matrix, and matrix ⇒ the equality in Eq. 11, but *only an inequality* λmax(D_{Y|Λ_X}) ≤ Λ_X when conditioning purely on the max eigenvalue — a subtlety that motivates the new diagram algorithm.

2. **Proper matrix scores.** A novel definition (Definition 2) of proper matrix scores S : H_∆^d → R^{d×d} is introduced; Proposition 1 shows such scores generate ordinary proper scores on the hypersphere. The matrix log score yields the von Neumann entropy H_{S_log}(M) = -Σ λ_i log λ_i (the Shannon entropy of eigenvalues), the quantity used by state-of-the-art methods.

3. **Entropy-risk equivalence.** Lemma 1 proves E[H_S(d(X))] = R_S(d) under matrix calibration; Theorem 2 extends this to approximate (limiting) calibration, justifying using average eigenvalue-entropy as a risk predictor in practice.

4. **Matrix temperature scaling (Section 4).** The transform h_TS(M) = (1/Σλ_i^α) Σλ_i^α e_i e_i^⊺ is shown (Proposition 3) to be injective for α > 0. A calibration-sharpness decomposition (Lemma 2) and Theorem 3 establish that minimizing the matrix-log risk over α is *exactly* minimizing the proper calibration error — so the single scalar α = 1/T is fit on a 300-example dev split by matrix-log risk minimization.

5. **Reliability diagrams for eigenvalues (Section 5, Algorithm 1).** Inputs are binned into B=8 equal-mass bins by predicted max eigenvalue; within each bin, predicted density matrices are hierarchically clustered into C=5 groups and the average target eigenvalue per cluster is collapsed back to the bin axis, producing E[λmax(D_{Y|d(X)}) | λmax(d(X))] as required by Eq. 11. A sensitivity sweep (Table 3) shows stability for C ≥ 5.

## Results & Highlights

- **Systematic overconfidence:** In every one of the six model × dataset settings, the risk-optimal matrix scaling temperature T* is greater than 1 (Figure 3), so all three LLMs are systematically overconfident before recalibration.
- **Eigenvalue AUROC gains (Table 1, Natural Questions, fuzzy ROUGE-L correctness):** matrix temperature scaling raises Phi 4 eigenvalue-based AUROC from **0.742 ± 0.001 to 0.794 ± 0.0** (+0.052) and Phi 4 Mini from **0.768 ± 0.001 to 0.778 ± 0.001**; on TriviaQA Phi 4 goes from **0.814 ± 0.001 to 0.820 ± 0.001** and Phi 4 Mini from **0.808 ± 0.0 to 0.814 ± 0.001**.
- **Outperforming semantic-entropy baselines (Table 2, TriviaQA):** the density-matrix eigenvalue/entropy scores beat Semantic Entropy (SE) and Kernel Language Entropy (KLE); e.g., Phi 4 reaches AUROC 0.814 (eigenvalue) vs KLE 0.807 and SE 0.798, and Llama4 Maverick 0.690 vs 0.674 (KLE) / 0.673 (SE).
- **ECE reduction via reliability diagrams:** on the Phi 4 Mini / TriviaQA setup, matrix temperature scaling cuts eigenvalue ECE from **0.36 to 0.21** (Figure 1c → 1d); the motivating example also shows the max eigenvalue dropping from λmax = 0.81 to 0.29 at T = 2.51.
- **Beating the sampling-temperature baseline (Figure 10, B.6):** the Lamb et al. (2025) sampling-temperature calibration reduces eigenvalue ECE only from 0.36 to 0.30, whereas matrix temperature scaling reaches 0.21 on the same setup.
- **Embedder-agnostic (Table 5, Figure 11):** swapping all-mpnet-base-v2 for jinaai/jina-embeddings-v5-text-nano reproduces the same overconfidence and recalibration pattern, with Phi 4 on NQ going from 0.744 → 0.795 eigenvalue AUROC.
- **Co-minimization (Figure 9, B.4):** risk R_S, matrix calibration error Cal_S, and eigenvalue ECE are all minimized at essentially the same scaling temperature across all models/datasets, empirically confirming Theorem 3.
- **Convergence sanity check (Figure 6, B.1):** the eigenvalue estimator has a positive bias but converges to the ground-truth eigenvalue for m ≥ 20, justifying the chosen sampling budget.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and well-scoped. Defining matrix/eigenvalue calibration, a proper matrix score, and an injective spectral temperature transform is genuinely new; it closes a real gap left open by Walha et al. (2026) and Nikitin et al. (2024), which use eigenvalue entropy without a calibration theory. The observation (Theorem 1) that eigenvalue calibration does *not* follow from matrix calibration — producing the "plateau" artifact in naive diagrams — is a non-obvious, paper-specific insight.
- **Rigor:** Theory is clean and fully proved in Appendix C (Theorem 1 uses Gruber 2024 Thm 1 plus convexity of the spectral norm; Theorem 3 mirrors Gruber & Buettner 2022 but re-derives it for the non-injective distribution-to-matrix setting). Empirics include bootstrap error bars (B=20), a sensitivity sweep over (B, C) in Table 3, a clustering-coherence check (Table 4), a second embedding model (Table 5), and a baseline comparison to Lamb et al. 2025 (Figure 10).
- **Reproducibility:** Strong. Public code, named models (Phi 4, Phi 4 Mini, Llama4 Maverick), named datasets (TriviaQA, Natural Questions), specified sample sizes (m=20/100, n=300 dev / 1700 test), and the only hyperparameters (B=8, C=5) are shown to be stable.
- **Significance:** Practically important for any pipeline that consumes spectral uncertainty (semantic entropy, KLE); the calibration diagnostic alone is a useful contribution. However, the absolute AUROC numbers remain modest (0.66–0.82) and the gains from recalibration, while real, are small in several cells.
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - **Scope of evaluation is narrow:** only two QA datasets and three models; generalization to reasoning, code, math, or long-form generation is untested, and the overconfidence claim ("likely a common occurrence in modern LLMs") overreaches relative to this evidence.
  - **The strongest baseline is weak:** the only matrix-calibration baseline is Lamb et al. 2025's *sampling-temperature* calibration, which the authors themselves note was not designed for matrix calibration. There is no comparison to vector-space calibration methods (e.g., beta/dirichlet calibration, isotonic regression) adapted to eigenvalues, nor any ablation against a richer injective transform (e.g., per-eigenvalue scaling, matrix Platt scaling), so the claim that the single-parameter spectral α is *sufficient* rests largely on Figure 3's visual fit rather than a counterfactual.
  - **Missing ablation on the diagram algorithm:** Algorithm 1 introduces a clustering step with a free (B, C) choice; while Table 3 shows stability, there is no comparison to alternative conditioning approximations (k-NN in matrix space, kernel smoothing), so it is unclear how much ECE reduction comes from the diagnostic vs. the recalibration itself.
  - **AUROC results are mixed:** on Natural Questions, Llama4 Maverick and Phi 4 Mini *entropy* AUROC slightly *drop* after temperature scaling (0.668→0.663, 0.776→0.774), and the paper acknowledges there is no theoretical link between calibration and AUROC — yet still headlines AUROC improvements, which is borderline cherry-picking the favorable cells.
  - **Reliance on fuzzy ROUGE-L correctness** for AUROC introduces an evaluator confound that is not stress-tested against exact-match or LLM-judge alternatives.
  - **Small dev set (n=300)** for fitting the single temperature α; no analysis of α variance across resampled dev sets is reported.

## Strengths

- First principled calibration theory for eigenvalue/density-matrix uncertainty, with complete proofs and a clear entropy-risk equivalence that justifies the practical use of average eigenvalue-entropy as a risk proxy.
- The matrix temperature-scaling transform is elegant, single-parameter, post-hoc, and provably injective and calibration-optimal — a clean analog of Guo et al. 2017 for the spectral regime.
- Algorithm 1's bin-then-cluster reliability diagram is a genuinely useful diagnostic that honestly surfaces the inequality of Theorem 1 rather than hiding it.
- Honest about the calibration-vs-AUROC disconnect, and transparent that the method is orthogonal to (and complementary with) semantic-entropy / KLE approaches.
- Solid reproducibility footprint: public code, named models/datasets/embedders, bootstrap error bars, sensitivity analyses, and a second embedding model to check embedder dependence.

## Limitations

- Evaluation is limited to two factual-QA datasets and three models; no reasoning, math, code, multilingual, or long-form tasks, so breadth of the overconfidence claim is unproven.
- The single scalar temperature α may be too coarse: no comparison to richer injective transforms (per-eigenvalue, matrix-wise, or non-parametric recalibration) is provided.
- Downstream AUROC gains are inconsistent and sometimes negative; the paper does not establish a theoretical or empirical regime where recalibration is guaranteed to help downstream tasks.
- AUROC ground truth relies on fuzzy ROUGE-L matching, a known noisy correctness proxy.
- Dev set for temperature fitting is small (n=300); stability of α is not reported.
- The clustering step in Algorithm 1 adds an unstudied approximation; comparison to alternative conditioning estimators is absent.
- All theory assumes finite integrals and, for some results, differentiability/convergence that may not hold for degenerate (rank-deficient) density matrices encountered in practice.

## Relevance

对关注"大模型不确定性量化与校准"的研究方向高度相关：本文为基于语义嵌入谱方法（如 Semantic Entropy、Kernel Language Entropy、谱分解）的不确定性估计提供了首个严格校准理论与可落地的矩阵温度缩放工具，直接服务于"让 LLM 输出可信置信度"这一核心需求，可作为后续可靠性评估与安全部署方法的基础。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the systematic overconfidence finding (T* > 1) extend beyond factual QA to reasoning, code generation, math, and agentic tasks, or is it an artifact of short-answer QA distributions?
- Can a richer injective transform (e.g., per-eigenvalue temperature, matrix-wise Platt scaling, or a normalizing-flow on the simplex of eigenvalues) beat the single-parameter spectral α, and by how much?
- How stable is the fitted temperature α across resampled 300-example dev sets and across different LLM sampling temperatures (here fixed at 0.5)?
- Since calibration and AUROC are theoretically decoupled, under what conditions (task, correctness metric, score) does eigenvalue recalibration reliably improve downstream rejection/deferral?
- Can matrix calibration be evaluated with a tighter proper-score-based error estimator that avoids the bin-then-cluster approximation in Algorithm 1?
- How does the framework behave under distribution shift or adversarial prompts, where the density-matrix predictor may be rank-deficient or degenerate?
