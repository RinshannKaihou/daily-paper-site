01

Method

Boomerang distillation → Boolean lattice of patching states → KL-weighted shortest path → greedy KLPatch. No extra training.

Starting from a teacher T (M layers) and a smaller student S (N<M) distilled from it, intermediate models are built by patching — replacing contiguous student blocks with their teacher counterparts. The order of patching traces a trajectory from S to T with no additional training.

The design space is exponential: best k-subset of patched layers costs O(2ᴺ); the permutation view costs O(N!). The paper's central move is to recast both as one shortest-path problem.

edge  M_A → M_{A∪{i}}  :=  𝔼_{x∼D_cal}[ KL( p_T(·|x) ∥ p_{M_{A∪{i}}}(·|x) ) ]

Proposition 1. Under an equidistant-model-size assumption, shortest paths in the interpolation graph G are exactly the optimal permutations w.r.t. teacher-relative log-AUPIC — turning an N! search into a DAG shortest-path problem (gaps to the raw, data-AUPIC actually reported are bounded by Props. 2–3).

KLPatch — greedy on the lattice

Calibrate
Pick 64 Pile samples as the calibration set D_cal.
Greedy KL step
At each step, patch the unpatched student layer whose patched version minimizes KL divergence to the teacher.
Commit & repeat
Fix that layer, recurse over remaining layers until all N are patched.
Cost: O(N²) KL evals
Iteration k evaluates N−k candidates. A greedy approximation to the shortest path in G.

Metrics: AUIC (higher-better, e.g. accuracy) and AUPIC (lower-better, perplexity), with log & teacher-relative variants.

03

Strengths & Limitations

Theory-backed, exhaustive small-model ground truth, honest Llama failure — but coarse baselines and an initialization-dependent recipe.
+ Strengths
  • First principled treatment of a patching-order design choice that prior work left ad hoc.
  • Clean theorem (Prop. 1) with explicit, bounded assumptions; full proofs in the appendix.
  • Exhaustive 720-order enumeration on DistilBERT/DistilGPT2 gives ground-truth optima, not sampling folklore.
  • KLPatch is simple & fast (O(N²)) and consistently near-optimal where it applies.
  • Honest reporting of the Llama failure and of where simple baselines stay competitive — no cherry-picking.
– Limitations
  • Not model-agnostic. Llama-3.2-3B needs a hand-coded "patch layer 1 first" fix (App. J).
  • Coarse baselines. Only two sequential orders; no head-to-head vs activation-similarity / cosine patching policies.
  • Perplexity ≠ downstream. The AUPIC the theorem optimizes is a leaky proxy for the AUIC practitioners care about.
  • Headroom unquantified at scale. True optimum infeasible for N≈17–19; "near-optimal" is relative to 200 samples + 2 baselines.
  • Theory–practice bridge is empirical. Optimality holds for log-AUPIC under equidistant sizes, not the raw data-AUPIC reported.
  • No released code or pinned checkpoints in the main text.
04

Open questions

Where patching-order optimization goes next.
Can a single initialization-aware variant of KLPatch unify behavior across Qwen, Pythia, and Llama — instead of the hand-coded layer-1 rule?
How do gains scale to much larger teachers (e.g. 70B+) where N is large and per-step KL evals get expensive — can the teacher-conditioned KL be amortized?
Would a non-greedy Dijkstra on G with KL edges, made tractable via pruning, beat greedy KLPatch meaningfully?
Is "naive patching degrades performance" a property of patching or of imperfect distillation alignment — does it interact with training-token budget?
Can the shortest-path / AUPIC framework extend to non-uniform teacher blocks, or to patching within a block rather than at block granularity?
How transferable are near-optimal orders discovered for one student to a sibling student distilled with a different seed or corpus from the same teacher?
