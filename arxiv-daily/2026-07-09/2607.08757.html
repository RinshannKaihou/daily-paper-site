Score Accuracy Along the Forward Diffusion Does Not Certify Numerical Stability in Diffusion Sampling
Yiwei Zhou · UT Austin · 9 Jul 2026 · <yiwei.zhou@utexas.edu>

Why this matters

Precisely exposes the off-path blind spot of standard score-matching — where training loss barely looks — and supplies a simple, actionable stability fix: denoiser projection is clipping, with a contraction guarantee.

Critical edge

The pathology is adversarial and constructed — it is not shown to arise in real large-scale image diffusion training, and the headline amplification factors are conditional on a stress-initialized protocol, not typical operating conditions.

MMethodology

A constructive separation between continuous-time stability and discrete-sampler stability.

Worked in the variance-exploding (VE) parameterization with compactly supported data, Tweedie's identity giving the exact score. The counterexample (Theorem 3.1) adds an inward superlinear perturbation in a remote region where the forward Gaussian marginal has exponentially small mass.

Result: on-path L² error ≤ ε, the learned reverse SDE is non-explosive with finite moments and lies within η in path-space TV of the exact process — yet Euler–Maruyama endpoints converge in probability while every positive moment and every Wp diverges. Rare trajectories entering the tail are amplified by repeated explicit superlinear updates; the amplification order (1+α)K beats the rare-event cost O(K2/α+K).

The same failure is realized inside one fixed adaLN-Zero DiT-S/2 (12 blocks, hidden 384, GELU gates + second-difference multiplier + bounded LayerNorm head) — not an artifact of unbounded function classes.

Stress-test RMS amplification r₈/r₀ rises monotonically from 7.90 (m0) to 14.69 (m3) — the predicted cascade — even as on-path MSE stays below 10⁻² and paired-trajectory path RMSE holds at 0.0119–0.0237.

The fix works: projecting the denoiser onto C = [−1, 1] cuts the final empirical second moment by 1.13×10⁴ – 3.33×10⁴×; stepwise RMS falls from 35–46 → 2.6–3.7 while the raw magnitude keeps growing — an empirical confirmation of Propositions 4.1 / 4.2.

Strengths

  • Crystallizes a previously under-articulated obstruction: forward-marginal score accuracy is blind to the off-path regions where numerical trajectories actually go.
  • Fully proved — including a non-trivial construction inside a fixed DiT-S/2, so the failure is not an artifact of unbounded function classes.
  • Clean equal-budget non-ordering (Corollary 3.4): identical score budget can be both uniformly stable and unstable in every moment.
  • Positive result is elegant — contraction guarantee (Prop 4.2), explicit grid-uniform moment bound (Prop 4.1), Wasserstein convergence (Thm 4.3), and recovers clipping as a special case.
  • Generalizes to arbitrary dimension (radial) and to probability-flow ODE samplers.

Limitations

  • The constructed pathology is adversarial; whether real score networks develop comparable remote-tail behavior is an open empirical question.
  • Experiments are an 8×8 one-channel toy with a single seed and a bespoke four-term loss; amplification/projection factors are stress-init-conditional Monte-Carlo point estimates.
  • Projection requires a known bounded convex C ⊇ data support — does not directly apply to unbounded or unknown-support domains.
  • No comparison against alternative stabilizers (step-size adaptation, score clipping, predictor–corrector, reflected diffusion).
  • Single author; no code release referenced — reproducing the family needs the non-standard training pipeline.

QQuestions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the remote-tail amplification arise spontaneously in standard large-scale image/text diffusion training (DiT-XL on ImageNet, SD3), or is it purely a constructed worst case — and how would one detect it from a trained checkpoint without a stress test?
  2. How does denoiser projection interact with classifier-free guidance, where the guided score pushes trajectories well outside the data support and C is no longer obviously valid?
  3. Can a comparable on-path-vs-off-path obstruction be formalized for Schrödinger bridges and flow-matching / rectified-flow samplers, with projection-like stabilization from bridge geometry?
  4. For data with unbounded or unknown support, is there a data-adaptive convex relaxation of C that retains the contraction and grid-uniform moment properties?
  5. A fair head-to-head: against adaptive step-size, score clipping, predictor–corrector and reflected diffusion — when does projection actually win on quality / stability / compute?
  6. The natural entry probability of the unstable region is not estimated — what is its true order under pT, and does it ever become large at practical step counts?
