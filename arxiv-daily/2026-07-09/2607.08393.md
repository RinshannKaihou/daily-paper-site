> **一句话总结：** 本文形式化了微调 LLM 时"记住但不会用"的 Knowing-Using Gap 现象（准确率差距 + 时间滞后），并提出 self-patching 干预技术，发现新知识被编码在早期/晚期存储层却未被路由到中层推理电路（知识-电路错位），仅用两个固定层对就能恢复 58-75% 的 oracle 上限。

> **TL;DR:** The paper formalizes the Knowing-Using Gap in LLM fine-tuning (accuracy gap + temporal lag between memorization and generalization on multi-hop reasoning over injected facts), and uses a novel self-patching intervention on STaRK-Prime/MAG to show that memorized knowledge is stored in early/late layers but not routed to mid-layer reasoning circuits; a fixed two-pair heuristic recovers 58-75% of the oracle headroom (e.g., chaining 0.121 → 0.357 vs. oracle 0.444 averaged across models), far above CoT prompting.

## 核心贡献

- **形式化"知-用鸿沟"（Knowing-Using Gap）**：在微调注入新知识时，模型在单跳记忆任务上接近满分，但在多跳推理任务上长期滞后且最终准确率显著偏低，该现象由两个维度刻画——准确率差距 ∆A 和时间滞后 ∆T。
- **提出 self-patching 因果干预方法**：相比 causal tracing 和 PatchScope，self-patching 无需"干净正确轨迹"，因此可应用于失败案例；它把源层某锚点位置的残差流表示复制到目标层，扫描所有层对来绘制知识渗透热力图。
- **提出知识-电路错位假设**：微调先把新事实编码到易于拟合的存储层（早期/晚期层，支持直接回忆），但这些表示未被可靠路由到因果上为多步推理所需的中层计算位置；手工把表示重新定位到中层层可立即大幅恢复泛化。
- **从诊断走向实用**：基于有效 patch 集中在"早期→中期"和"晚期→中期"两个簇，提出仅用两个预定义层对（source≈0.1L/0.8L → target≈0.5L）的固定启发式，无需逐例搜索即可恢复 58-75% 的 oracle 上限。
- **发布专用数据集**：基于 STaRK（生物医学 PrimeKG 与学术 Microsoft Academic）构造的 Memorization-to-Generalization 数据集，包含 chaining（链式两跳）和 intersection（带噪声交集）任务，并通过零样本准确率<6% 验证知识新颖性。
- **跨模型/规模/领域的稳健性**：在 Qwen-2.5（1.5B/3B/7B）和 LLaMA-3.x（1B/3B/8B）、LoRA 与 FFT、生物医学与学术两个领域上均复现该现象。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes the Knowing-Using Gap** as a two-dimensional failure mode of fine-tuning-based knowledge injection: an accuracy gap (∆A = memorization − generalization accuracy at convergence) and a temporal lag (∆T between saturation epochs of memorization vs. downstream use).
- **Introduces self-patching**, an activation-patching variant that copies the anchor-token residual-stream representation from a source layer into a target layer and measures the change in answer correctness; unlike causal tracing it does not need a clean correct trajectory, so it applies to *failed* generalization cases.
- **Proposes the knowledge-circuit misalignment hypothesis** with causal-intervention evidence: injected facts get stored in early/late "storage" layers that support direct recall but are not routed into the mid-layer computation required for multi-hop reasoning; manually relocating these representations recovers downstream use.
- **Turns diagnosis into a practical heuristic**: using only two predetermined layer pairs per architecture (early→mid and late→mid, where mid≈0.5L), the fixed heuristic recovers 58–75% of the oracle headroom with no per-instance search.
- **Releases a Memorization-to-Generalization dataset** built on STaRK (STaRK-Prime biomedical, STaRK-MAG academic) with chaining and intersection QA tasks, and validates novelty by showing all base models score ≤6% zero-shot.
- **Demonstrates robustness** across Qwen-2.5 (1.5B/3B/7B), LLaMA-3.2-1B/3B, LLaMA-3.1-8B, LoRA vs. full fine-tuning, and two knowledge domains.

## Methodology

**Problem formalization.** Given model parameters θ_t after t epochs of fine-tuning on a set of injected facts K={f_i}, the authors track two curves: memorization accuracy A_mem(t) on single-hop recall and generalization accuracy A_gen(t;T) on a downstream task T. The Knowing-Using Gap is defined by ∆A(T)=A_mem(T_max)−A_gen(T_max;T) and a saturation-time-based temporal lag ∆T=T_gen−T_mem, where T_mem/T_gen are the earliest epochs at which performance stays stable for ≥w consecutive epochs.

**Dataset construction (§3).** Built on STaRK (semi-structured KGs with millions of entities). Two domains: STaRK-Prime (biomedical, PrimeKG, 18 relation types) and STaRK-MAG (academic, 5 relation types). The atom of knowledge is a fact triplet f=(n1, e12, n2). Memorization tasks are single-hop (head+relation→tail). Two generalization task types are derived from manually designed meta-paths:
- *Chaining* (2-hop): E1→r1→E2 and E2→r2→E3, evaluated on E1→r1→r2→? (must recover the bridge entity E2).
- *Intersection*: identify the shared entity across multiple support facts amid noise confounders.
Novelty is verified by ≤6% zero-shot accuracy across all models; instances the base model already answers correctly are filtered out before patching to rule out leakage.

**Self-patching (§5.1).** For an L-layer transformer, let h_l^t(P) be the residual-stream state at layer l, token position t for prompt P, and T(P,E) the token span of anchor substring E (e.g., the head entity). Self-patching copies h_{l_s}^{T(P_s,E)}(P_s) from source layer l_s into the target run as h̃_{l_t}^{T(P_t,E)}(P_t), then resumes the forward pass and measures ∆I = I(M̃(P_t),y*) − I(M(P_t),y*) with I an Exact-Match/MRR indicator. Scanning all layer pairs (l_s,l_t) yields a "permeation map." Distinguishing features from prior art: (i) unlike causal tracing, no clean correct trajectory is needed → applicable to failed cases; (ii) unlike PatchScope, it scores target-prompt correctness rather than interpreting the source via auxiliary decoding.

**Mechanistic analysis.** Checkpoints are saved every epoch; layer-to-layer self-patching at the head-entity position on the generalization prompt produces heatmaps over (l_src, l_tgt) at each step. The dynamics show: before memorization the map is all-blue (no useful internal representation); at the memorization moment an off-diagonal red region appears (knowledge is stored but unusable end-to-end); as training continues the red region expands toward the diagonal — covering the diagonal ⇒ generalization emerges naturally; halting before the diagonal ⇒ failure, because once M(P) matches y* the loss (and gradient) vanishes.

**Practical heuristic (§5.5).** Motivated by the two-cluster pattern of effective patches (early→mid and late→mid), the fixed strategy uses two predetermined pairs per architecture: source at ~0.1L→target ~0.5L, and source at ~0.8L→target ~0.5L (more precisely ⌊0.10L⌉→⌊0.45L⌉ and ⌊0.82L⌉→⌊0.45L⌉), with no per-instance search.

## Results & Highlights

- **Knowing-Using Gap is large and statistically significant.** Across all six models, the per-instance memorization-minus-chaining gap is significant under McNemar's test with **p < 10⁻⁵⁰ for every model**; the effect size (gap magnitude) is the rightmost column of the confidence-interval table, e.g., Qwen-2.5-1.5B has A_mem=0.998 vs chaining=0.078 (gap 0.920), and LLaMA-3.1-8B has 0.986 vs 0.182 (gap 0.804). Spearman correlation between model size and gap magnitude is r=−0.90 (p=0.015) — bigger models narrow but do not close the gap.
- **Temporal lag persists across fine-tuning regimes.** Under LoRA, chaining saturates memorization at T_mem=10.4±2.8 epochs but generalization only at T_gen=15.0±5.2 (∆T=4.6) with final A_gen=0.303; full fine-tuning memorizes much faster (T_mem=2.4±1.5) yet lags comparably (∆T=5.5) and reaches nearly identical A_gen=0.315 — faster memorization does *not* buy faster/better use.
- **Oracle self-patching recovers most downstream use (Table 4).** On STaRK-Prime chaining, Qwen-2.5-1.5B jumps from 0.078 (no patch) to 0.440 (oracle), a ~5.6× lift; intersection goes 0.793→0.987. LLaMA-3.2-1B chaining lifts 0.102→0.316 (~3.1×). Pattern is uniform across all architectures, scales, and both STaRK-Prime and STaRK-MAG domains — chaining accuracy lifts by 1.5–6× in every cell.
- **Fixed heuristic recovers 58–75% of oracle headroom (Table 7).** Averaged across six models, chaining goes from 0.121 (no patch) → 0.357 (fixed two-pair heuristic) → 0.444 (oracle); intersection 0.808 → 0.926 → 0.966. For Qwen-2.5-1.5B intersection the fixed heuristic (0.942) is already within 4.5 points of the oracle (0.987).
- **Self-patching dominates prompting/perturbation baselines (Table 6).** For Qwen-2.5-1.5B chaining: no-patch 0.078, CoT 0.132, irrelevant-fact patching 0.150, self-patching 0.440 — roughly 3× the best baseline. CoT can even *degrade* intersection performance (Qwen-2.5-1.5B: 0.793 no-patch → 0.243 with CoT), confirming gains come from knowledge-relevant relocation, not decoding tricks.
- **Token-position ablation ties the effect to entity tokens.** Patching at the head-entity position yields mean 0.6408 (p<0.001, ***); <EOS> gives a weaker secondary signal (0.4029, ***); <BOS> is non-significant (0.0485, n.s.) and relation tokens weak (0.1990, *), vs random 0.1359.
- **Cross-domain replication holds.** STaRK-MAG training dynamics mirror the biomedical pattern: Qwen2.5-1.5B on MAG shows memorization >0.95 by epoch 15 while generation stays near zero (gap=0.945, ∆T=0.026 in intersection it is near-parallel).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The framing is genuinely useful. The Knowing-Using Gap is essentially a re-naming and sharpening of the "remembering but not using" / reversal-curse / ripple-effect failures already documented by Zhong et al. (MQuAKE, 2023), Cohen et al. (RippleEdits, 2024), Berglund et al. (reversal curse), and Ovadia et al. The novel contributions are (a) the two-axis (accuracy + temporal-lag) formalization with saturation-time definitions, and (b) the self-patching intervention adapted from Patchscopes/causal tracing. Self-patching is a real but incremental methodological delta — the core trick (swap a residual-stream representation at an anchor across layers) is a known activation-patching variant; the innovation is applying it as a *diagnostic on failed cases* rather than on clean trajectories.
- **Rigor**: Generally strong. 1,000-fact fixed splits, McNemar tests with p<10⁻⁵⁰, Wilson CIs (preferred for near-0/1 proportions), a τ/w sensitivity grid confirming ∆T is stable within ±1.0 epoch, cross-domain (STaRK-Prime biomedical *and* STaRK-MAG academic) and cross-architecture (Qwen + LLaMA) replication. The dataset-novelty check (≤6% zero-shot) and the leakage filtering before patching are responsible.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Anonymous repo, full hyperparameter table (LoRA r=16/α=32, AdamW wd=0.01, lr=2e-4, seed 42, fp32), fixed layer-pair definitions (⌊0.82L⌉→⌊0.45L⌉ etc.), and explicit sample counts. Caveat: code is under an anonymous.4open.science link, so long-term availability is not guaranteed.
- **Significance**: The reframing of fine-tuning generalization failure as a *routing/alignment* problem (knowledge exists but is in the wrong layers) rather than a capacity problem is intellectually valuable and points to "alignment-aware training" as a research direction. However, the practical payoff is modest: the fixed heuristic recovers 58-75% of the *oracle* headroom, but the oracle itself is an upper bound that requires a per-instance layer scan; the heuristic is still an inference-time intervention requiring the memorization prompt's hidden state, not a training-time fix. The paper is honest that it is "mainly diagnostic."
- **Red flags / weak points**:
  - *Intersection is near-ceiling already.* A_gen for intersection is 0.79-0.87 even without patching, and the "knowledge-circuit misalignment" story is really carried by chaining; the headline Knowing-Using Gap is heavily a chaining phenomenon, which somewhat inflates the apparent breadth of the result.
  - *The "novelty" framing slightly overstates.* The underlying failure (models memorize edited/injected facts but fail ripple/multi-hop reasoning) is well-established by MQuAKE/RippleEdits; the gap this paper fills is mechanistic *diagnosis*, not the phenomenon's discovery. The authors do cite this prior work, but the abstract/contributions could be read as claiming more discovery-novelty than is warranted.
  - *Oracle vs. practical gap.* The jump from oracle (per-instance best layer pair) to the fixed two-pair heuristic loses 25-42% of the headroom, and the heuristic still needs the source-prompt forward pass at inference — a deployment cost not discussed.
  - *Scale ceiling.* Largest model is 8B; whether the early/late→mid two-cluster pattern and the 58-75% recovery hold at 70B+ is untested. The r=−0.90 size-vs-gap correlation is over only 6 points and is suggestive rather than conclusive.
  - *CoT baseline is weak/unfair.* CoT here is evaluated under the same exact-match rule and, per the authors' own numbers, sometimes *degrades* intersection — suggesting the CoT implementation may be a naive single-template baseline rather than a strong prompt-engineered one; the comparison "self-patching >> CoT" is therefore somewhat loaded.
  - *Anchor-position fixity.* Self-patching is constrained to a single fixed anchor (head-entity token); as the authors concede, this may understate recoverable headroom, but it also means the estimates of the "two-cluster" pattern are conditioned on that choice.
- **Honest praise**: The permeation-dynamics heatmaps (Figure 4) and the cross-context consistency check (Figure 6, patching P_mem representations into P_gen and recovering the same layer-pair structure) are clean, persuasive causal-intervention evidence that distinguishes "knowledge is absent" from "knowledge is mis-routed." The fixed-heuristic result, even if modest, is a credible move from diagnosis toward remedy.

## Strengths

- Clean, two-axis formalization of a known-but-underdefined failure mode, with saturation-time definitions that are robust to training fluctuation (validated via a τ/w grid).
- Self-patching is a well-motivated tool: it sidesteps causal tracing's need for a clean trajectory and Patchscope's reliance on auxiliary decoding prompts, making it usable on *failed* generalization cases — exactly where the diagnosis matters.
- Strong causal-intervention evidence (permeation dynamics, cross-context patching, token-position ablation, irrelevant-patch control) that the effect is knowledge-specific and not a perturbation/prompting artifact.
- Comprehensive robustness checks: two domains (biomedical + academic), six models spanning two families and 1B-8B scale, LoRA + full fine-tuning, McNemar/Wilson/binomial statistics.
- Honest limitations appendix and a practical (if modest) heuristic that turns a diagnostic into a partial remedy.

## Limitations

- Self-patching intervenes at a single fixed anchor position; multi-position or redundant encodings are not captured, so the reported headroom is a *lower* bound and the two-cluster pattern is anchor-conditioned.
- The oracle is a per-instance best-pair upper bound; the fixed heuristic recovers only 58-75% of it, and the heuristic still requires a source-prompt forward pass at inference (a cost not quantified).
- The mechanism is post-hoc/diagnostic: no early-training signal predicts which facts will fail to generalize, so it cannot drive proactive training-time intervention yet.
- Mechanistic granularity stops at token-layer resolution; no localization to specific attention heads or MLP sublayers.
- Scale ceiling at 8B; the early/late→mid pattern and recovery rates are unverified at 70B+.
- The Knowing-Using Gap is heavily a *chaining* phenomenon — intersection is near-ceiling without intervention — so the headline gap is narrower than the two-task framing suggests.
- The CoT baseline appears to be a single naive template (it degrades intersection), making the "self-patching >> CoT" comparison somewhat loaded.

## Relevance

对研究"LLM 微调知识注入与可解释性"高度相关：本文用因果干预手段揭示了"记住≠会用"的本质是层间路由错位而非容量不足，这与知识编辑（ROME/CAKE）、多跳推理、grokking 等方向的最新进展直接衔接，对设计 alignment-aware 微调策略有启发。若关注 RAG vs. fine-tuning 的知识更新范式对比或机械可解释性方法论（activation patching/circuit analysis），也值得精读。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the early/late→mid two-cluster patching pattern and the 58-75% recovery rate hold at 70B/405B scale, or does the storage/computation layering shift with depth?
- Can the knowledge-circuit misalignment hypothesis be turned into a *training-time* loss or regularizer (e.g., explicitly encouraging representations to permeate into mid-layer reasoning circuits) rather than a post-hoc inference-time intervention?
- How does self-patching interact with model editing methods like ROME/CAKE — does editing at the "storage" layers (early/late) reproduce the same misalignment, and can the fixed heuristic repair ripple-effect failures on MQuAKE/RippleEdits benchmarks?
- Is the strong chaining-vs-intersection asymmetry an artifact of task design (intersection "often aligns with the memorization answer")? Would a harder intersection variant with more confounders reopen a large gap?
- The heuristic needs the source (memorization) prompt's hidden state at inference; what is the real deployment cost, and can a cached/proxy representation remove this dependency?
- Could a per-instance *adaptive* layer selector close the remaining 25-42% gap to the oracle, and is that gap explained by fact-specific storage-layer variation?
- Does the misalignment story extend to in-context-learning knowledge (RAG-provided facts), or is it specific to parametric knowledge injected by gradient updates?
