A single Procrustes rotation aligns two BERTs’ activation spaces so one shared Top-K SAE learns strongly correlated cross-seed “universal” features.

Why this matters

A lightweight, reproducible fix for a hard problem.

Directly tackles cross-seed feature universality in mechanistic interpretability with an alignment scheme (one 768×768 SVD on 500 sequences) reusable as a baseline for larger models and SAE variants.

Critical Edge

Headline is partly a strawman.

Direct comparison with the two most relevant competitors — Feature Aligned SAE and Orthogonal SAE — is explicitly deferred to “future work.”

Critical Assessment · Limitations

01Methodology

Attack the geometric root cause — non-convex SAE training leaves two same-seed-config BERTs with misaligned feature spaces — rather than punishing separate dictionaries with a regularizer.

For dense activations HA, HB ∈ ℝn×768 from the same layer, solve the orthogonal Procrustes rotation in closed form and train one shared SAE on both spaces:

Walign = VUᵀ  from  HAᵀHB = UΣVᵀ  →  Top-K bottleneck fed [hA, W⋆hB]

Unified objective

L = LKL + λDS·LDS + Llocal + λcross·Lcross + λaux·Laux
  • Top-K sparsity (k=32) — structural, no L1 shrinkage bias.
  • End-to-end — LKL on hijacked logits (Braun et al.) + LDS downstream MSE (λDS=1.5).
  • Cross-seed — Lcross = MSE between sparse codes cA, cB (λcross=1.0).
  • AuxK — dead-feature revival (λaux=1/32).
  • Setup — dict 6,144; SAE at layer 10/12 of bert-base-uncased; per-token (not mean-pooled) activations; single RTX 3090.

05Strengths

  • Clean ablation. Six conditions isolate the Procrustes contribution; authors honestly report rotation-only (not Lcross) drives most of the gain.
  • Strong statistics. Permutation null vs 30 random rotations + identity + mismatched-token control; R²=0.704 regression gives a mechanistic explanation.
  • Lightweight & reproducible. One 768×768 SVD on 500 sequences; runs on a single RTX 3090 with full seed/hyperparameter disclosure.
  • Honest about Goodhart risk of Lcross, and surfaces a non-obvious negative result (post-hoc Procrustes fails).

06Limitations

  • Missing direct baselines. No head-to-head with Feature Aligned SAE or Orthogonal SAE — deferred to future work.
  • Narrow scope. One model family (bert-base-uncased), one layer (10/12), three small datasets; scaling unproven.
  • Metric Goodharts via Lcross; the rotation-only defense simultaneously weakens the cross-loss justification.
  • Inflated “Univ.%” over a deflated denominator amid 56–89% dead features; only 78 universal features on SST-2.
  • Single qualitative feature (389); no systematic human/automated interpretability evaluation at scale.

07Open questions for further work

  1. How does it compare head-to-head with Feature Aligned SAE and Orthogonal SAE on these same datasets, controlling for dictionary size and compute?
  2. Does the universality advantage survive at scale — e.g. on Llama-class decoders, or earlier/later layers with different correlation structure?
  3. Can the 56–89% dead-feature rate be cut without sacrificing alignment, and would that raise the absolute universal count on SST-2?
  4. Is the Lcross Goodhart effect quantifiable on a held-out universality metric computed off the SAE training set?
  5. Would a large-scale automated feature-interpretability evaluation (beyond hand-picked feature 389) corroborate the qualitative claim?
