> **一句话总结：** 本文系统审计了 2015–2026 年间 27 篇结肠镜息肉分割论文的评估实践，发现 25/27 篇遗漏 HD95、26/27 篇无统计显著性检验、至少 5 种互不兼容的训练/测试划分协议并存；通过对 5 个代表性模型在 3 种受控协议下用统一打分器重评，证明主流的 Dice 区域重叠指标会掩盖严重的边界与漏检失败，且看似明确的排行榜名次在随机划分与更换指标时会发生逆转，并据此提出五点 Polyp Segmentation Reporting Checklist (PSRC)。

> **TL;DR:** This paper audits evaluation practices across 27 colonoscopy polyp segmentation papers (2015–2026) and re-evaluates 5 representative models (PraNet, Polyp-PVT, SANet, PVT-CASCADE, G-CASCADE) under three controlled protocols on Kvasir-SEG, CVC-ClinicDB, CVC-ColonDB, ETIS, CVC-300, and PolypGen. It finds that 25/27 papers omit HD95, 26/27 use no statistical testing, and ≥5 incompatible split protocols coexist; re-evaluation shows region-overlap Dice silently hides large boundary (PraNet HD95 of 230 px vs Polyp-PVT 127 px on ETIS) and recall failures, the "best" model changes with the metric, and near-tied Dice rankings (PraNet↔Polyp-PVT) reverse across random splits — a gap exceeding 1% that rivals the entire 4-year SOTA progression of 2.94% on Kvasir-SEG.

## 核心贡献

- **系统性文献审计**：构建了 2015–2026 年间 27 篇全监督深度息肉分割论文的元数据集，沿四个维度（指标画像 D1、数据划分 D2、泛化范围 D3、统计严谨性 D4）量化记录每篇的评估实践，并整理为可审查的 Table 1。
- **揭示三类结构性缺陷**：(i) 25/27 篇完全遗漏 HD95 或任何表面距离指标（包括那些以"边界精修"为核心卖点的 SANet、CaraNet）；(ii) 至少 5 种互不兼容的 train/test 划分协议（PraNet 固定划分、随机 80/10/10、Kvasir-only、自定义、视频）在同一排行榜列里被直接比较；(iii) 26/27 篇无任何统计显著性检验。
- **受控多模型重评**：用同一打分器在 P1（固定划分）、P2（3 个随机划分）、P3（PolypGen 六中心 OOD）三种协议下重评 5 个代表性模型，补上被遗漏的 NSD@3px、HD95、Recall 与 lesion-level 检测指标，证明指标选择会改变冠军、近并列排名会跨划分逆转。
- **提出 PSRC 五点报告清单**：C1 报告 HD95；C2 报告 Recall/敏感度；C3 精确说明划分协议并论证可比性；C4 至少一个未见中心的外部/OOD 测试集；C5 报告统计显著性（Wilcoxon/Friedman），小数位差距 <2 位需 p<0.05 才能称 SOTA。
- **披露二级综述表的转录错误**：直接从原始 PDF 核对四篇代表模型的 Dice，发现 G-CASCADE 的 CVC-ClinicDB mDice 在二级表中被误报为 0.936（原始 0.9468，误差 0.011），已超过多篇 SOTA 论文宣称的提升幅度。

## Key Contributions

- **Systematic meta-audit**: Assembles a representative cohort of 27 fully-supervised polyp segmentation papers (2015–2026) and records structural meta-data across four dimensions (D1 Metric Profile, D2 Data Partitioning, D3 Generalization Scope, D4 Statistical Rigor), surfaced as the auditable Table 1.
- **Three structural pathologies**: (i) 25/27 papers completely omit HD95 or any surface-distance metric — including SANet and CaraNet whose primary claimed contribution is boundary refinement; (ii) ≥5 incompatible train/test protocols (PraNet-fixed, 80/10/10 random, Kvasir-only, custom-with-extra-data, video) are compared side-by-side in shared leaderboards; (iii) 26/27 papers report no significance testing at all.
- **Controlled multi-model re-evaluation**: Re-scores five representative models (PraNet, Polyp-PVT, SANet, PVT-CASCADE, G-CASCADE) with one uniform evaluator under three protocols — P1 (PraNet fixed split), P2 (3 random 80/20 splits), P3 (PolypGen six-centre OOD) — adding the omitted NSD@3px, HD95, Recall and lesion-level detection metrics; shows the "best" model changes with the metric and near-tied rankings flip across splits.
- **Post-Metrics-Reloaded regression evidence**: Demonstrates that even the four papers published after Maier-Hein et al.'s Metrics Reloaded framework (Nature Methods 2024) — G-CASCADE, CFFormer, Polyp-DiFoM, HSSAM-Net — perpetuate the same omissions, with Polyp-DiFoM actually regressing to Dice/IoU only.
- **PSRC reporting checklist + transcription-error audit**: Proposes a five-point Polyp Segmentation Reporting Checklist, and verifies secondary survey-table numbers against primary-source PDFs, finding G-CASCADE's CVC-ClinicDB mDice misreported by 0.011 — exceeding the claimed margin of several SOTA comparisons.

## Methodology

The paper combines a literature audit with a controlled empirical re-evaluation.

**Audit meta-dataset (Section 4).** 27 deep fully-supervised still-image polyp segmentation papers were selected from the Mei et al. survey, the Awesome-Polyp-Segmentation repository, PapersWithCode leaderboards, and manual sweeps of CVPR/MICCAI/WACV/MIDL 2024–2026. Inclusion requires (a) a novel fully-supervised architecture for still-image colonoscopic polyp segmentation, (b) quantitative results on ≥1 of the five PraNet-template datasets (Kvasir-SEG, CVC-ClinicDB, CVC-ColonDB, ETIS-LaribPolypDB, CVC-300/EndoScene), and (c) publication 2015–2026. Excluded: video models, weakly/semi/unsupervised methods, SAM-style foundation adaptations, and works validating only on non-standard isolated datasets. Each paper is coded across D1–D4.

**Controlled re-evaluation (Section 6).** Five models (PraNet, Polyp-PVT, SANet from released weights; PVT-CASCADE and G-CASCADE self-trained on the 1450-image PraNet split because no polyp checkpoints are released) are scored by a single evaluator that computes, beyond reported mean Dice/IoU, NSD@3px (validated to ≤10⁻¹¹ against the official MetricsReloaded package), HD95 (medpy convention), pixel recall, per-image scores for significance testing, and a lesion-level connected-component detection metric. Three protocols: **P1** PraNet fixed split tested on unseen CVC-ColonDB, ETIS, CVC-300; **P2** three random 80/20 splits (3 seeds); **P3** out-of-distribution on PolypGen's six unseen centres. A perceptual-hash→SSIM audit confirms train/test disjointness (max cross-set SSIM 0.66), and released-weight models reproduce published Dice within ≈1%.

## Results & Highlights

- **Audit headline numbers**: 25 of 27 papers completely omit HD95 / surface-distance metrics; 25 of 27 fail to report Recall (sensitivity); 26 of 27 report no statistical significance testing — only PolyMamba-Net (2026) provides Wilcoxon signed-rank p-values. On the **Table 1 audit benchmark**, 19 of the 27 papers use the PraNet fixed split (1450 train), while at least four other incompatible split protocols coexist.
- **Region metrics hide boundary/recall failures (P1, ETIS)**: PraNet reaches HD95 of **230.1 px**, nearly double Polyp-PVT's **126.8 px**, and its recall of **0.673** reveals about a third of polyp tissue is missed — all nearly invisible in the mere 0.15 Dice gap between them on the **ETIS** benchmark.
- **Split choice rivals claimed progress**: The four-year Kvasir-SEG SOTA progression under the PraNet fixed split runs PraNet (0.898) → SANet (0.904) → Polyp-PVT (0.917) → G-CASCADE (0.9274), a total gain of **2.94%**, while Fitzgerald et al. showed random partitions of Kvasir-SEG alone shift Dice by **>1%** — i.e. split choice could account for roughly one third of the entire claimed four-year progression.
- **Rankings not robust to splits (P2, Kvasir/ClinicDB pool)**: Over three random 80/20 splits, HSNet 0.930 ± 0.005, Polyp-PVT 0.923 ± 0.005, PraNet 0.919 ± 0.007 Dice — the three models lie within ≈1 std of one another and the **PraNet↔Polyp-PVT ordering reverses between seeds**; training-seed variance is ±0.005–0.007 Dice.
- **OOD degradation on PolypGen (P3, six unseen centres, n=1537)**: every model drops to **Dice 0.713–0.760 with HD95 144.9–162.3 px** (Polyp-PVT best at 0.760/146.5 px, PraNet worst at 0.713/162.3 px), with per-centre Dice ranging from **0.45 (centre 4) to 0.89 (centre 3)** — a uniform absolute collapse invisible to in-distribution leaderboards.
- **Lesion-level ranking differs from pixel-Dice (P1 unseen sets, connected-component matching)**: under any-overlap criterion, PVT-CASCADE tops lesion-F1 (**0.868**), G-CASCADE the best precision (**0.891**), Polyp-PVT the highest sensitivity (**0.898**), while PraNet — competitive on Dice — has the worst lesion-F1 (**0.698**) and lowest precision (**0.615**); ranking is robust across IoU thresholds 0.1–0.75.
- **Transcription errors in secondary survey tables**: G-CASCADE's CVC-ClinicDB mDice is **0.9468** in its primary supplementary but **0.936** in secondary survey tables — an error of **0.011** that exceeds the claimed improvement margin of several published SOTA comparisons; SANet ColonDB shows a smaller 0.001 discrepancy.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are from Lunit (South Korea, a leading medical AI company), RPTU Kaiserslautern (Germany), and Aalborg University & the Pioneer Center (Denmark) — institutions with strong medical-imaging and segmentation credentials. The work directly builds on, and validates its metric implementations against, the Metrics Reloaded framework (Maier-Hein et al., Nature Methods 2024) and the medpy library, and cites the community survey (Mei et al.) and Awesome-Polyp-Segmentation repository as audit seeds. The methodology is unusually transparent about its own threats to validity (single-seed self-training, HD95 convention disagreement, lesion-matching thresholds), which strengthens rather than weakens its credibility.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High within this sub-community. While metric-audit logic exists generally (Metrics Reloaded, "A Metric Learning Reality Check"), applying it systematically to colonoscopy polyp segmentation — quantifying that even post-Metrics-Reloaded papers regress, that HD95 library implementations disagree by up to ~16 px, and that secondary survey tables contain transcription errors exceeding claimed SOTA margins — is a concrete, novel contribution rather than a generic "use better metrics" platitude.
- **Rigor**: Strong. The audit inclusion/exclusion criteria are explicit and defensible; the re-evaluation uses a single uniform scorer with metric implementations verified to ≤10⁻¹¹ against the official MetricsReloaded package; a perceptual-hash→SSIM audit checks for train/test leakage (max cross-set SSIM 0.66); and the paper honestly distinguishes what is and is not protocol-dependent (P1/P3 rankings largely consistent; the genuine instability is in P2 random splits and metric-choice reshuffles). The limitations section is commendably self-critical.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Standard datasets (Kvasir-SEG, CVC-ClinicDB, CVC-ColonDB, ETIS, CVC-300, PolypGen) are all public; the paper commits to an open-source evaluation toolkit; and it reports reproduction deltas for self-trained models (PVT-CASCADE Kvasir 0.917 vs published 0.917; G-CASCADE within 0.018–0.035). The residual gap is that two of five models (PVT-CASCADE, G-CASCADE) lack released polyp weights, so they are self-trained single-seed — the authors correctly flag this and anchor metric-disagreement findings on released-weight models wherever possible.
- **Significance**: Potentially field-resetting for polyp segmentation benchmarking. If adopted, the PSRC would invalidate a large fraction of existing leaderboard comparisons; even without adoption, the paper supplies ammunition for reviewers to demand HD95, recall, significance tests, and external OOD evaluation.
- **Red flags / specific weaknesses**: (i) The headline "25/27 omit HD95" is to some extent a strawman against legacy papers (U-Net 2015, SFA 2019) that predate the HD95 norm — the more telling number is the four post-Metrics-Reloaded papers that still omit it. (ii) The strongest instability claim rests on only **three** random seeds in P2, and the per-image significance tests on CVC-300 are explicitly acknowledged as low-powered (n=60) — the authors hedge this honestly but a sceptical reader might want more seeds. (iii) Self-trained P1 models are single-seed, so absolute HD95/Recall numbers for PVT-CASCADE and G-CASCADE carry residual uncertainty the paper cannot fully remove. (iv) The lesion-level detection metric applies no minimum-size filter, so a single-pixel FP speck counts as a false-alarm lesion — acknowledged but worth noting. (v) The CFFormer-vs-G-CASCADE split-incompatibility comparison is powerful but rests on a single exemplar pair.

## Strengths

- Quantifies, rather than merely asserts, that the field's evaluation is broken — with a per-paper audit table and controlled re-evaluation rather than rhetoric.
- Demonstrates the clinically decisive failure modes (a missed polyp = missed cancer; an inaccurate boundary = wrong resection margin) that Dice under-reports, with a concrete Figure 2 showing Dice 0.75 hiding ~36% missed tissue and a 118 px boundary error.
- The transcription-error audit against primary-source PDFs is a genuinely useful service to the community and rare in this kind of meta-evaluation.
- The PSRC checklist is lightweight, actionable, and domain-adapted rather than a generic Metrics Reloaded re-statement.
- Honest, granular threats-to-validity section that separates the robust findings (uniform OOD degradation, metric-dependent winners) from the narrower ones (P2 split flips between near-tied models).

## Limitations

- Audit is limited to still-image colonoscopic polyp segmentation; extension to video polyp segmentation, skin lesion segmentation, or other parallel medical segmentation fields is left to future work.
- Relies strictly on the main experimental sections of published papers rather than appendices/repositories, so the audit is described as a "conservative lower bound" on reporting vulnerabilities.
- Two of five re-evaluated models (PVT-CASCADE, G-CASCADE) have no released polyp weights and are self-trained single-seed, introducing training-seed variance (±0.005–0.007 Dice) that the paper folds into interpretation but cannot eliminate.
- Absolute HD95 values are implementation-dependent (medpy vs Metrics-Reloaded max-of-directed differ by up to ~16 px on identical masks) and should be compared only within a fixed implementation.
- Lesion-level connected-component matching uses no minimum-size filter; the PolypGen OOD centres were not additionally SSIM-audited against the training pool (noted as a residual low risk).
- P2's three-seed design is modest; the most striking instability result (ranking reversal) would be more convincing with more seeds.

## Relevance

高度相关于"医学图像分割评估方法论与可复现性"这一研究方向：本文与日常关注的 benchmark 审计、指标选择对 SOTA 声称的影响、以及临床指标（HD95/Recall/lesion-level）在医疗 AI 中的作用直接吻合，可作为评估息肉/病灶分割工作时的方法论参照和审稿标准依据。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the audit findings generalize to video polyp segmentation (SUN-SEG, CVC-VideoClinicDB) and to adjacent lesion segmentation tasks (skin, fundus, lung nodule), where the same PraNet-style region-overlap template is also dominant?
- Would a larger P2 seed budget (e.g., 10–20 random splits) turn the near-tied PraNet↔Polyp-PVT ranking flip into a statistically robust instability claim, or does the ranking actually stabilize?
- Can the HD95 library-disagreement (medpy vs Metrics-Reloaded, up to ~16 px) be reconciled into a single community standard, and how much of published boundary-error "progress" dissolves once the convention is fixed?
- Does enforcing PSRC retroactively change the perceived SOTA ordering on Kvasir-SEG/CVC-ClinicDB — i.e., which currently-celebrated models survive once HD95, Recall, significance tests, and ≥1 OOD set are required?
- The lesion-level detection metric currently applies no minimum-size filter; how sensitive are the lesion-F1 rankings to a clinically motivated ≥0.1% area filter, and does PVT-CASCADE's F1 lead persist?
