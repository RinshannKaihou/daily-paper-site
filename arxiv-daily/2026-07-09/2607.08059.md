> **一句话总结：** 本文首次在三个 thinking-mode 视觉语言模型（VLM）家族上实证刻画了"思考模式导致答案熵崩塌"现象——Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking 的答案熵 AUROC 从 Instruct 版的 0.899 跌到 0.492（随机水平），但思考链本身的熵（pooled AUROC 0.786）和链长度（0.776）可作为零额外开销的单次前向幻觉检测信号，并通过 abstention gate 将 POPE 准确率从 71.0% 提升到 93.8%（62.7% 覆盖率）。

> **TL;DR:** This workshop paper provides the first three-family empirical characterization of answer-entropy behavior in thinking-mode VLMs. On 1,000 identical POPE adversarial samples, switching Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct to Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking collapses answer-entropy AUROC from 0.899 to 0.492 (chance); GLM-4.1V-9B-Thinking shows no collapse (0.716) and InternVL3-8B shows selective thinking (50% chain rate). Across all three thinking-mode families, thinking-chain entropy outperforms answer entropy on chain-generating subsets (pooled conditioned AUROC 0.786 vs 0.432, the latter below chance), and a zero-cost chain-based abstention gate lifts POPE accuracy from 71.0% to 93.8% at 62.7% coverage — though it still trails the instruct model's answer-entropy gate (96.7% at 63.0%).

## 核心贡献

- **首次揭示"思考模式导致答案熵崩塌"现象**：通过受控消融（同家族、同视觉编码器，仅思考链生成方式不同）证明，Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking 在 POPE 上的答案熵 AUROC 从 Instruct 版的 0.899 跌到 0.492（随机水平），且这一崩塌源于思考模式本身，而非任务结构。
- **跨家族刻画三种定性不同的熵行为模式**：完整崩塌（Qwen，0.492）、不崩塌（GLM，0.716）、选择性思考（InternVL3，仅 50% 样本生成链）。说明崩塌是训练配方相关，而非思考解码的普适属性。
- **提出思考链信号作为更强的零成本不确定性指标**：在生成链的子集上，思考熵（Hthink）一致优于答案熵——pooled conditioned AUROC 达 0.786（95% CI [0.745, 0.825]），而答案熵 pooled 仅 0.432（CI [0.386, 0.478]，低于随机）；信号可由单次贪心前向免费获得，无需多次采样。
- **揭示答案方向混淆（answer-direction confound）**：无条件 AUROC 分析中，POPE 上答案熵出现倒置（0.392，越自信越易幻觉），因为模型对正预测无论对错都赋予更高置信度；条件化于 yes 预测可消除该混淆。
- **实用的 abstention gate**：refuse unclear + 拒绝 top 20% 长链样本，在 POPE 上将准确率从 71.0% 提升到 93.8%（62.7% 覆盖率），无额外推理开销；并系统记录了 12–22% 的结构化弃答行为及其对缺失物体查询的不对称性。

## Key Contributions

- First controlled demonstration that thinking-mode decoding causes answer-entropy collapse: answer-entropy AUROC drops from 0.899 (Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct) to 0.492 (Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking) on identical POPE samples, with identical questions/images/protocol — the thinking mode itself is responsible, not task structure.
- First three-family empirical characterization revealing that collapse is training-recipe-dependent, not a universal property: complete collapse (Qwen, 0.492), no collapse (GLM-4.1V-9B-Thinking, 0.716), and selective thinking (InternVL3-8B, chains on only ~50% of samples, ans H 0.675 full / 0.602 thinking-only).
- Identifies thinking-chain signals (Hthink and chain length L) as robust zero-cost hallucination predictors extractable from a single greedy forward pass, with pooled conditioned AUROC of 0.786 (Hthink) and 0.776 (chain length), both significant at p < 0.001, while pooled answer entropy (0.432) lies entirely below chance.
- Diagnoses the answer-direction confound that inverts answer entropy on POPE (unconditional AUROC 0.392 < chance) because the model assigns higher confidence to positive predictions regardless of correctness.
- Documents structured abstention (12–22% of queries) with consistent asymmetry toward absent-object queries, and proposes a practical abstention gate raising POPE accuracy from 71.0% to 93.8% at 62.7% coverage with no additional inference cost.

## Methodology

- **Models.** Three thinking-mode VLMs from distinct families plus one non-thinking control, all at 8–9B scale, run in bfloat16 on a single A100-SXM4-40GB: Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking (generates `<think>...</think>` under greedy, `do_sample=False`), Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct (non-thinking control, same family/encoder), GLM-4.1V-9B-Thinking (THUDM, distinct `Glm4vForConditionalGeneration` arch), and InternVL3-8B (OpenGVLab, R1-style system prompt, `</think>` is a 3-token sequence, chains triggered on ~49.8% of samples).
- **Benchmarks.** POPE adversarial (1,000 binary yes/no object-presence questions over COCO val2014, balanced labels); HallusionBench (951 visual questions, 178 text-only excluded, imbalanced 550 no / 401 yes); plus a 300-sample VQAv2 pilot stratified across answer types for open-ended generalization.
- **Signals (all free byproducts of one greedy forward pass).** Per-token Shannon entropy within the `<think>` block: `H_t = -Σ_v p_t(v) log p_t(v)`; `H_think` = mean over thinking tokens; `H_ans` = mean over answer tokens; `L` = number of thinking tokens. Entropy computed over the full softmax vocabulary.
- **Controlled ablation.** The headline collapse result pairs Qwen-Thinking vs Qwen-Instruct on the same 1,000 POPE samples — identical questions, images, and protocol — so any AUROC difference is attributable solely to thinking-mode generation.
- **Evaluation protocol.** A corrected six-group classification (TP/TN/FP/FN/unclear-chain/unclear-nochain). AUROCs computed both unconditionally (error prediction) and conditioned on answer direction (FP vs TP within yes-predictions) to isolate the answer-direction confound. Bootstrap 95% CIs from 10,000 resamples (`numpy.random.default_rng(42)`); effect size |r| from Mann-Whitney U.
- **Abstention gate.** Two-stage: (1) refuse "unclear" samples (chain fails to resolve), then (2) refuse the top X% by chain signal; thresholds computed over committed samples, coverage reported over all 1,000.
- **Open-ended generalization.** 300-sample VQAv2 pilot stratified by answer type (Yes/No, Number, Other/free-form) to test whether chain-signal advantage is an artefact of binary task structure.

## Results & Highlights

- **Answer-entropy collapse (headline ablation, POPE, 1,000 samples):** Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct answer-entropy AUROC = **0.899** vs Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking = **0.492** (chance) — an absolute drop of 0.407 on identical data, attributed entirely to thinking-mode decoding.
- **Three-family characterization (POPE, conditioned AUROC, FP vs TP within yes-predictions):** Qwen-Thinking Hthink = **0.647** vs Hans = **0.492**; GLM-Thinking Hthink = **0.759** vs Hans = **0.716**; InternVL3 Hthink = **0.608** (thinking-only subset) vs Hans = **0.602** (margin not statistically reliable, n_FP = 17).
- **Pooled conditioned AUROCs (n_FP = 163, 1,951 samples):** Hthink = **0.786** (95% CI [0.745, 0.825], |r| = 0.53); chain length = **0.776** (CI lower 0.735, |r| = 0.42); answer entropy = **0.432** (CI [0.386, 0.478], entirely below chance). Chain signals significant at p < 0.001.
- **Answer-direction confound (unconditional, POPE binary-committing):** answer-entropy AUROC for FP prediction = **0.392** (below chance), because higher answer confidence predicts more hallucination on POPE; on HallusionBench Hans = **0.726** (positive) because harder questions produce genuine answer variance.
- **VQAv2 open-ended pilot (n = 300, Qwen-Thinking):** chain entropy Hthink = **0.680** vs answer entropy Hans = **0.595**; gap largest for free-form "Other" answers (**0.733** vs **0.467**, the latter below chance), confirming the advantage is not a binary-task artefact.
- **Abstention gate (POPE, thinking model):** baseline 71.0% accuracy → refuse-unclear 91.1% at 77.9% coverage → +top 20% chain refused **93.8% at 62.7% coverage** (and 94.2% at 54.9% coverage).
- **Instruct-model ceiling (POPE):** the non-thinking Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct using answer-entropy gating reaches **96.7% at 63.0% coverage**, dominating the thinking model's chain gate at all coverage levels; the gap narrows from ~16 pp at 100% coverage to ~3 pp at 40% coverage — the chain gate closes but does not eliminate the practical gap introduced by thinking-mode abstention.
- **Structured abstention:** 22.1% on POPE, 12.6% on HallusionBench, with asymmetry toward absent-object queries (POPE: 31.4% no vs 12.8% yes; HallusionBench: 14.2% no vs 10.5% yes). Mechanisms differ: on POPE 199/221 abstentions involve extended unresolved chains (mean 124.3 tokens); on HallusionBench 118/120 abstentions produce no chain at all, even at 1,024 tokens.
- **Epistemic hierarchy:** both chain length and Hthink increase monotonically from committed-correct (TP: L = 59.9, Hthink = 0.218) through committed-wrong (FP: 74.4, 0.262) to abstaining-with-chain (124.3, 0.338), confirming chain signals track epistemic state.
- **Cross-task transfer:** a POPE-calibrated top-20% entropy threshold applied to HallusionBench without recalibration achieves 91.0% accuracy at 45.5% coverage (vs 88.7% at 23.2% for chain length) — entropy thresholds are more transferable than length thresholds.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel framing. The controlled Instruct-vs-Thinking ablation on identical POPE samples (0.899 → 0.492) is a clean, causal demonstration that collapse is attributable to thinking mode, not task structure — and the three-family comparison showing collapse is training-recipe-dependent (Qwen collapses, GLM does not) is a real empirical contribution that prior work (UMPIRE, VL-Uncertainty, HALP) could not surface.
- **Rigor:** Strong on the Qwen/GLM comparison: bootstrap CIs, pooled conditioned AUROCs with non-overlapping CIs ([0.745, 0.825] vs [0.386, 0.478]), Mann-Whitney effect sizes. The answer-direction confound is correctly diagnosed and the conditioning-on-yes-predictions fix is principled.
- **Weak baselines / missing comparisons:** No empirical comparison against sampling-based uncertainty methods (e.g., self-consistency, semantic entropy). The author acknowledges this but frames the contribution as "the zero-cost single-pass signal" — defensible, but leaves open whether chain signals beat a 5–10 sample self-consistency baseline at matched cost.
- **Statistical fragility of one leg:** The InternVL3 "selective thinking" result rests on n_FP = 17 in the thinking-only subset; the Hthink-vs-Hans margin (0.608 vs 0.602) is explicitly flagged as not statistically reliable, yet InternVL3 is still counted as one of the "three families" supporting the headline claim. The cross-family generalization claim effectively rests on two families (Qwen, GLM), not three.
- **Incomplete InternVL3 pipeline:** InternVL3 is evaluated only on the Table 1 headline comparison; the full analysis (conditioned AUROCs, epistemic hierarchy, abstention gate) covers only Qwen and GLM. The three-family characterization is therefore asymmetric in depth.
- **Scope limits:** only 8–9B models; larger thinking-mode VLMs (32B, 72B) untested; all results under greedy decoding — the author correctly identifies stochastic-sampling rehabilitation of answer entropy as the single most actionable remaining experiment.
- **Overclaim risk:** the abstention gate is presented as a practical win, but on POPE the instruct model's answer-entropy gate (96.7% @ 63.0%) still dominates the thinking model's chain gate (93.8% @ 62.7%); the paper is honest about this, but the headline framing of "chain signals restore the signal" should not be read as "thinking-mode VLMs now beat instruct VLMs for uncertainty-gated deployment."
- **Red flags:** none severe. Single-author workshop paper with transparent limitations and disclosed AI-writing use; claims are appropriately hedged ("we conjecture these findings reflect structural properties ... verification across additional model families is ongoing").

## Strengths

- Clean controlled ablation isolating the effect of thinking-mode decoding from task structure — the Qwen-Instruct vs Qwen-Thinking pairing on identical data is the strongest single result.
- Honest reporting: explicitly flags the unreliable InternVL3 margin (n_FP = 17), the answer-entropy inversion on POPE, and that the instruct baseline still dominates the chain gate.
- Practically actionable: all signals are free byproducts of a single greedy forward pass — no sampling, no probe training, no extra forward pass — lowering deployment barrier.
- Mechanistic depth: the answer-direction confound, the epistemic hierarchy (TP → FP → abstain), and the over-deliberation vs immediate-collapse distinction between POPE and HallusionBench abstention modes add real explanatory content.
- Connects the collapse to a principled uncertainty-theoretic interpretation (conditioning reduces posterior variance; chain entropy proxies pre-commitment epistemic uncertainty).

## Limitations

- Three thinking-mode families but only two (Qwen, GLM) carry the full analysis; InternVL3 is headline-only with low FP count.
- No comparison against sampling-based UQ (self-consistency, semantic entropy) — cost-zero framing is fair but leaves the relative-cost question open.
- Greedy-decoding-only; stochastic sampling (temperature > 0) may rehabilitate answer entropy for Qwen-style models — identified but unresolved.
- Scale: all models at 8–9B; behavior of 32B/72B thinking-mode VLMs unknown.
- Both primary benchmarks (POPE, HallusionBench) are binary yes/no; the VQAv2 generalization is only a 300-sample pilot.
- Threshold selection requires held-out calibration data from the target distribution (entropy is more transferable than length, but not calibration-free).
- Chain signals miss "confident hallucinations": 11/34 FPs on POPE and 14/129 on HallusionBench produce chain entropy below the TP median.

## Relevance

对关注大模型推理、不确定性量化和幻觉检测的研究者高度相关：本文揭示了 thinking-mode（CoT/显式推理链）会破坏传统基于答案分布的不确定性信号这一此前未被刻画的现象，并为 thinking-mode VLM 的可靠部署提供了零额外开销的链级信号方案。其跨家族训练配方差异的发现（Qwen 崩塌 vs GLM 不崩塌）对推理模型的不确定性建模与 RL/训练后处理流程设计有直接启示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the answer-entropy collapse persist — or get rehabilitated — under stochastic sampling (temperature > 0, top-p)? The author names this as the single most actionable experiment; it would determine whether sampling-based UQ methods regain validity for thinking-mode VLMs.
- Do chain-level signals (Hthink, chain length) hold at 32B/72B scale, or is the collapse a small-model phenomenon tied to incomplete pre-commitment?
- Can chain signals beat a matched-cost self-consistency baseline (e.g., Hthink from one greedy pass vs majority-vote over k=5 sampled chains) for hallucination detection?
- Why does GLM-4.1V-9B-Thinking avoid collapse while Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking does not — what training-recipe difference (RL objective, data mixture, thinking-token supervision) controls the degree of pre-commitment?
- Is the answer-direction confound (higher confidence on positive predictions regardless of correctness) a general property of yes/no VLM tasks, or specific to POPE's object-presence framing?
- Can the "confident hallucinations" failure mode (low-entropy wrong chains, 11/34 POPE FPs) be caught by combining chain signals with representation-level probes (e.g., HALP-style frozen-state probing)?
- Does the abstention gate's accuracy-coverage tradeoff generalize to open-ended generation beyond the 300-sample VQAv2 pilot?
