Key Finding

Turning STPA on the LLM safety tool itself derives a code-enforced constitution — and its 21 Tool Principles lift adversarial safety +79% while a generic preamble does nothing.

Paper: "Who Analyses the Analyser? Self-Validating LLM Hazard Analysis with Constitutional Meta-STPA" ↗

A closed loop ("Constitutional Meta-STPA") derives — rather than hand-asserts — a 29-principle governance constitution from the tool's own loss→hazard→UCA→constraint chain, each principle bound to a machine-checked code enforcement point. The same 8/8 governance principles re-emerge from a second, independently authored AI tool while hardware systems stay at a 0/8 floor.

Methodology

A five-step STPA pipeline Π(C, S, M, k) maps a system description S and constitution C to an analysis artifact, a validation report, and a binary export decision. The central design decision is a deterministic skeleton around stochastic steps: the LLM supplies content (free-form losses, hazards, control structure), while a deterministic engine owns structure — it enumerates exactly one UCA slot per (controller × control action × UCA-type) triple across all four STPA types, so four-type coverage is a property of the harness, not model diligence.

The meta-STPA derivation treats the AI-assisted tool itself as the system under analysis; its controllers are the LLM, validator layer, voting harness, export gate, and on-call engineer — surfacing hazards (unlogged calls, version drift, a constitution that changes between runs, unvalidated exports) that classical outward-facing STPA never raises. Each hazard yields one of eight Meta-Safety Principles, each bound to a concrete code enforcement symbol. Governance is enforced by eight deterministic regex/structure validators plus a validator-gated export (Markdown/JSON always written; PDF/email blocked unless errors are zero or an engineer records a verbatim override). A formal coverage operator cov(C,S,M,k) with Lemma 1 certifies that any coverage delta is attested by a fixed lexical witness on artifact text — decoupling measurement from both model and judge opinion. A reproducibility manifest pins tool version, git SHA, model, temperature, seed, and SHA-256 of the system description and constitution.

Strengths

  • Genuinely novel framing. "Who analyses the analyser?" names a real structural blind spot in LLM-for-STPA work; the closed-loop answer is elegant and well-motivated.
  • Exceptional methodological discipline. Pre-registered hypotheses, Bonferroni correction, a constitution ladder isolating the active layer, negative hardware controls, a second-tool generalisation check, three deterministic repeats per cell.
  • Intellectual honesty. Non-monotonicity of meta-coverage reported as a negative construct-validity result; an earlier non-deterministic "jump to full coverage" openly disclosed as a non-replication; κ=0.39 judge agreement stated, not buried.
  • Reusable engineering artefact. Deterministic UCA-slot enumeration, validator-gated export, hash-only audit logging, and run manifest are concrete patterns any LLM-safety-tool builder can adopt.
  • Clean separation of concerns. Content from the model, structural completeness from the engine, discipline from validators/manifest, judgement from the engineer — all auditable.

Limitations

  • No human-expert gold standard. Neither the rubric nor the witness bank is calibrated against practising safety engineers; coverage numbers are internally consistent but not externally validated.
  • Permissive lexical scanner. A regex keyword bank the authors themselves flag as paraphrase-defeatable; κ=0.39 with the LLM-judge is only moderate — "18/21" is not a precise number.
  • Narrow behavioural test. 300 single-turn textual responses measure what the tool writes, not multi-turn interaction, tool use, or downstream deployment outcomes; the MSP zero-effect is partly a probe artifact.
  • Thin class-level sample. The governance claim rests on two AI tools + three hardware controls, one paradigm (STPA), English probes, and models reached via a single router (OpenRouter).
  • Replay not guaranteed. OpenRouter may silently re-route back-ends even with a pinned seed — the manifest makes drift detectable, not impossible.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the derived constitution and coverage framework transfer to other hazard-analysis paradigms (FTA, FMEA, HAZOP), where the structural skeleton differs from STPA's four-UCA-type enumeration?
  • Would pairing each lexical witness with an entailment check raise the scanner's soundness floor — or just inherit LLM-judge failure modes?
  • Under a human-expert gold standard, is 18/21 canonical recovery a real ceiling or an artifact of the authors' witness bank encoding their own judgement of the 21 themes?
  • Can the meta-loop's fixed-point convergence be strengthened from an empirical observation to a contraction argument under a defined metric — and what is the basin of attraction as a function of model capability?
  • Does the Tool-Principles behavioural lift survive multi-turn interaction and tool use, or degrade when the model must chain analyses across sessions and external tools?
  • How sensitive are the cross-vendor depth differences (45 vs 183 UCAs) to prompt phrasing, and is there a depth–precision trade-off the canonical-coverage metric masks?
