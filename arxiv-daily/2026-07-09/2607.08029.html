Critical edge

The headline anomalies are BitsAndBytes-specific — the entire study is locked to one backend, so its deployment guidelines do not generalize to GPTQ / AWQ / FP8 / W8A8.

1Methodology — hypothesis-driven, hardware-grounded

A pre-registered empirical study that formulates five hypotheses (H1–H5) and evaluates them through five research questions, explicitly falsifying or revising several along the way (H1 → H1rev). It isolates, for the first time on sub-3B VLMs, the three components — vision encoder, projector, LLM backbone — under six quantization configs: cfg0 FP16 baseline; cfg1 LLM-INT4; cfg2 Projector-INT8; cfg3 cfg1+cfg2; cfg4 Vision-INT8; cfg5 cfg1+cfg4 (single-component ablations isolate marginal effects; composites test error additivity).

Models (5, architecturally diverse): Qwen3-VL-2B & DeepSeek-VL2-Tiny (MoE), PaliGemma2-3B & LLaVA-OneVision-0.5B (dense), Kosmos-2.5 (non-SigLIP control). Four use SigLIP encoders, making SigLIP a controlled factor. Hardware (2 heterogeneous edge SoCs): Jetson Orin NX (1024-core, 16 GB, 102.4 GB/s, 25 W) and Orin AGX (2048-core, 64 GB, 204.8 GB/s, 50 W), both Jetpack 6.2.1. Quantization via BitsAndBytes INT8/INT4; greedy decoding with fixed max-new-tokens, prompts and exact-match extraction held constant.

Metrics: MME (max 2800), peak VRAM, and per-component latency — vision, projector, and LLM TPOT (prefill excluded). Power via NVIDIA tegrastats at 50 ms sampling; energy = power × end-to-end latency; IPJ = normalized accuracy (Score/2800) per joule. Diagnostics: isolated NVTX/Nsight vision profiling (50 iters) and an AWQ W4A16 sanity check to test whether the TPOT penalty is BitsAndBytes-specific. Baselines are cross-checked against the Open VLM Leaderboard.

Strengths

  • Hypothesis-driven with explicit falsification (H1 → H1rev, H2 narrowed, H4 refined) — intellectually honest and rare in empirical ML.
  • Isolated Nsight profiling + non-SigLIP Kosmos control gives a kernel-level root cause (quant/dequant share, launch inflation), not a hand-wavy “SigLIP is slow.”
  • Two genuinely heterogeneous edge SoCs (NX 25 W vs AGX 50 W); the IPJ metric makes the energy–accuracy trade-off explicit.
  • Transparent validation vs the Open VLM Leaderboard; the AWQ sanity check honestly narrows their own H3.
  • Decision-relevant guidelines: what to quantize first, when INT4 helps or hurts, why accuracy transfers but energy does not.

Limitations

  • BitsAndBytes-only. Both headline anomalies are shown BnB-specific (AWQ), so conclusions do not generalize to GPTQ/FP8/W8A8.
  • Single benchmark (MME, Yes/No). H5 transferability rests on one score distribution; coarse scoring complicates small ΔMME.
  • No variance/CI on ΔMME despite n≥3 — the surprising MoE “improvements” cannot be significance-tested against noise.
  • MoE-robustness mechanism asserted, not isolated — no gate/routing ablation pinpoints sparse activation as the cause.
  • Anonymous, non-durable repo; no pinned seeds/quant revisions; PTQ only (QAT & mixed-precision NAS left to future work).

3Open questions for further exploration

  • Q1Do the MoE-vs-dense findings — and the surprising MoE accuracy gain — replicate on MMMU / MMBench / SEED / TextVQA with confidence intervals, or are they MME-coarseness artifacts?
  • Q2Can an ablation isolate the MoE gate/routing as the true source of INT4 robustness (e.g. frozen gates, or dense-vs-MoE at matched parameter count)?
  • Q3Do the SigLIP INT8 latency and LLM INT4 TPOT penalties vanish under FP8 / W8A8 / TensorRT-LLM, or persist?
  • Q4Can cfg3 additivity / cfg5 non-additivity become a closed-form predictor for composite error, enabling NAS-driven per-component bit-width allocation?
  • Q5Do these edge findings transfer to Apple NE, Qualcomm Hexagon, ARM Ethos, where memory bandwidth and kernel availability differ?
  • Q6Given AWQ keeps accuracy and avoids the TPOT penalty but not peak VRAM, what KV-cache / runtime work yields simultaneous VRAM, latency, and energy wins on Jetson Orin?
