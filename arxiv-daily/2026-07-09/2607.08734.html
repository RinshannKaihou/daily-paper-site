arXiv 2607.08734 / Key Finding / 2026-07-09

Accuracy and perplexity mask quantization's behavioral drift — a new decision-level metric, correctness agreement, exposes it.

一句话总结：提出"正确性一致度"指标，发现即使准确率与困惑度看似稳定，量化模型与基座模型在具体样本上的正确预测重合度已显著下降。

The Illusion of Equivalency: Statistical Characterization of Quantization Effects in LLMs ↗
Baha Rababah · Cuneyt Gurcan Akcora · Carson K. Leung | Univ. of Manitoba · UCF · Red River College Polytechnic
llm-quantization behavioral-evaluation post-training-quantization attention-analysis model-evaluation
Key Metric — the gap
14pt
On Llama-3.2-3B (HellaSwag/Winogrande/ARC macro-avg) at Q8_0: base accuracy 55.5% vs correctness agreement 41.4%.
▲ accuracy hides a 14-point behavioral gap — even at 8-bit.
Why This Matters

Reveals an evaluation blind spot — normal accuracy and perplexity do not imply behavioral consistency — directly actionable for quantization evaluation, attention analysis, and mixed-precision deployment.

⚠ Critical Edge

Correctness agreement's absolute value is bounded by both models' accuracy ceilings, and no ceiling-normalized variant (e.g., P(quant correct | base correct)) is provided — so cross-model comparisons conflate real drift with differing ceilings.

From: Limitations / Rigor

Methodology M / 04

The paper treats post-training quantization (PTQ) as a joint structural-and-behavioral operator on a decoder-only transformer's attention projections (Q, K, V, O).

  • PTQ as an operator. Tc(θ) = Dc(Qc(θ)) over bit-widths b ∈ {2,3,4,5,6,8} via llama.cpp — covering legacy (Q8_0/Q5_0/Q4_0) and K-quant (Q6_K–Q2_K) families.
  • Structural deviation in weight space. Statistical functionals (mean, std, skewness, kurtosis) and divergences (KS, KL, cosine, Euclidean) computed block-by-block and per-projection.
  • Correctness agreement (Def. 1). CA = (1/M)·Σ 1[z_base=1 ∧ z_quant=1], with proven bound 0 ≤ CA ≤ min(Acc_base, Acc_quant) ≤ 1 — ignoring jointly-incorrect cases.
  • Models & hardware. Llama-3.2-3B, Vicuna-7B, Mistral-7B, Llama-3.1-8B; WikiText-2/C4 perplexity; zero-shot HellaSwag/Winogrande/ARC; 8× NVIDIA Tesla V100 (32GB).

Strengths what holds up

  • Clean central insight. Accuracy/perplexity systematically under-report quantization harm, demonstrated with a concrete, bounded metric.
  • Comprehensive scope. 4 models × 9 configs × 2 quant families × 5 datasets, plus layer-wise statistical and divergence analysis.
  • Actionable structural findings. Q/K sensitivity and the Q4_K / Q3_K / Q2_K regimes directly motivate mixed-precision strategies.
  • Honest about perplexity — explicitly shows Q3_K can lower perplexity while degrading behavioral agreement.
  • Table 1 crisply positions against seven prior quantization-evaluation works.

Limitations what's missing

  • No ceiling normalization. CA is bounded by accuracy ceilings, complicating cross-model comparison.
  • Aggregated results. Three benchmarks macro-averaged with no per-task breakdown (ARC vs HellaSwag effect unknown).
  • No alternative-metric comparison. CA is not benchmarked against token agreement, logit KL, or Dutta et al.'s metrics.
  • No remediation validated. Q/K-sensitivity insight motivates structure-aware quantization, but protecting Q/K is not experimentally tested.
  • Scope. Only 3B–8B decoder-only LMs — no instruction-tuned, 70B+, or reasoning-heavy evaluation.

Questions for Further Exploration Q / 06

  1. Would a ceiling-normalized CA (P(quant correct | base correct)) change the model or bit-width ranking, and reveal a cleaner non-linear breakpoint?
  2. How much of the CA gap is recoverable by a structure-aware scheme keeping Q/K high-precision while compressing V/O — and does it recover decisions or merely accuracy?
  3. Does the Q/K-sensitivity pattern persist in instruction-tuned and 70B+ models, or in reasoning tasks where Li et al. (2025) saw distinct degradation?
  4. Which benchmark — HellaSwag, Winogrande, or ARC — drives most of the CA drop, and is drift concentrated in specific reasoning categories?
  5. How does CA compare to token-level agreement and logit-divergence metrics — redundant, and which best predicts downstream reliability?
  6. How sensitive is CA to prompt template and scoring protocol, given scoring is deterministic but template-dependent?
