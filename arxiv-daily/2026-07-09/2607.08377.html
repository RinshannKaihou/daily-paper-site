M Methodology

A spectral recalibration framework.

An LLM f + embedding model e becomes a density-matrix predictor d(x)=E[e(a)e(a)⊺], estimated from m sampled answers (m=20 test, 100 dev). Its eigenvalues live in the simplex as probabilities over latent semantic outcomes.

  1. Two new notions. Matrix calibration D_{Y|d(X)}=d(X) and eigenvalue calibration. Theorem 1: matrix ⇒ equality, but conditioning on the max eigenvalue alone yields a strict inequality — the “plateau” artifact.
  2. Proper matrix scores (Def. 2); matrix log score gives the von Neumann entropy of eigenvalues.
  3. Entropy–risk equivalence (Lem. 1, Thm. 2): average eigenvalue-entropy is a valid risk proxy.
  4. Matrix temperature scaling h_TS(M)∝M^α, injective; Thm. 3 — minimizing matrix-log risk over α exactly minimizes calibration error. α fit on n=300 dev split.
  5. Algorithm 1: bin-then-hierarchically-cluster reliability diagram (B=8, C=5) conditioning on the full density matrix.

+ Strengths

What the paper genuinely adds.

  • First principled calibration theory for eigenvalue / density-matrix uncertainty, with full proofs and an entropy–risk equivalence.
  • Matrix temperature scaling is single-parameter, post-hoc, injective and calibration-optimal — a clean spectral analog of Guo et al. 2017.
  • The bin-then-cluster reliability diagram honestly surfaces Theorem 1's inequality rather than hiding it.
  • Transparent about the calibration-vs-AUROC disconnect; orthogonal to and composable with SE / KLE.
  • Strong reproducibility: public code, named models/datasets/embedders, bootstrap bars, sensitivity sweeps.

! Limitations

Where the claim out-runs the evidence.

  • Only two factual-QA datasets and three models — no reasoning, math, code, multilingual, or long-form tasks.
  • Single scalar temperature α may be too coarse; no richer injective-transform comparison.
  • Downstream AUROC gains are inconsistent, sometimes negative; no regime where recalibration is guaranteed to help.
  • AUROC ground truth relies on fuzzy ROUGE-L, a noisy correctness proxy.
  • Small dev set (n=300) for fitting α; stability of α not reported.
  • The clustering step in Algorithm 1 adds an unstudied approximation; no alternative conditioning estimators.

? Questions for further exploration

Open threads the paper leaves behind.

  • Does the systematic overconfidence (T*>1) finding extend beyond factual QA to reasoning, code, math, and agentic tasks?
  • Can a richer injective transform (per-eigenvalue temperature, matrix Platt scaling, a normalizing flow on the simplex) beat the single α?
  • How stable is α across resampled 300-example dev sets and across different sampling temperatures?
  • Since calibration and AUROC are decoupled, under what conditions does eigenvalue recalibration reliably improve downstream rejection/deferral?
  • Can matrix calibration be evaluated with a tighter proper-score estimator that avoids the bin-then-cluster approximation?
  • How does the framework behave under distribution shift or adversarial prompts, where the density matrix may be rank-deficient?
