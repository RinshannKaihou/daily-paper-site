Open-ended Multi-agent Autocurricula via Visual Inspection of Policies with Multi-modal LLMs

▲ +84 pts vs every baseline
VIP win rate on held-out map 3s vs 4z after fine-tuning (0.84 ± 0.16). Text-only ablation, PLR-PVL and PLR-MaxMC all finish at 0.00.
VLM costs only ~1% of runtime; VIP uses ~100× fewer env interactions than grid-search MAPPO*.
Why this matters

VLMs can act as coaches that watch agent videos to drive unsupervised RL curricula — a flagship of foundation-model-guided multi-agent training.

⚠ Critical edge

Single benchmark (SMAC only), a single lightweight VLM (VideoLLaMa2-7B) with no VLM ablation, and a sanitization step that only works on enumerable task spaces.

01 Methodology

VIP is a deliberately minimal loop (Algorithm 1) for open-ended curriculum generation in multi-agent RL. The task space is SMAC maps × difficulty (1–10) — a finite enumerable subset, split 80% train (12 maps) / 20% held-out test (3 maps). Policies use MAPPO, trained 10⁷ steps per recommended task; all curricula share default hyperparameters and report 95% CIs over 5 seeds.

  1. Train-RL — train policies on the current task θₜ.
  2. Eval — record episode video(s) φₜᵛⁱᵈᵉᵒ plus a tiny text summary (win rate + past tasks).
  3. VLM-Next-Task — feed the video + summary to VideoLLaMa2-7B; it recommends the next most learnable task by judging the agents' capabilities.
  4. Sanitize-Task — sentence-similarity maps the raw answer to the nearest valid task (random fallback if below threshold; never triggered).

Why video beats scalars: a 0%-win-rate policy can be almost winning (e.g. it gains a numbers advantage then panics and loses to one enemy). A VLM watching the rollout recognizes "almost-winning" and correctly keeps training there — something no learning-signal curator (UED / PLR) can do.

03 Strengths

  • Novel framing — open-ended autocurricula driven by direct visual inspection of policy behavior, like a human coach reviewing game tape.
  • Clean ablation isolates the value of video; the Figure-1 "0% win rate but almost winning" example is a compelling motivation.
  • Big gains on hard unseen maps (3s vs 4z: 0.00 → 0.84) where all scalar/text baselines fail.
  • Agent-count-agnostic & hyperparameter-free — desirable when grid search is exactly what one wants to avoid.
  • Honest on compute — VLM is ~1% of runtime; explicit ~100×-fewer-interactions comparison vs grid search.

04 Limitations

  • Single domain (SMAC only); transfer to other MARL or single-agent benchmarks is unproven.
  • Sanitization needs an enumerable task space with language-like names; cryptic or continuous spaces need iterative prompting or a trained embedding model.
  • Inherits the VLM's weaknesses by construction — and only one (lightweight) VLM is tested.
  • 10m vs 11m blind spot — no video advantage over text-only/random, tied to troop-type coverage in the curriculum.
  • Relies on SMAC's renderer, which the authors admit can inaccurately depict game state — so the VLM may not be reasoning from faithful observations.
  • No ablations over video count/length, prompt variants, or alternative VLMs; PLR's degenerate behavior is not deeply diagnosed.

05 Questions for further exploration

  • Does VIP transfer beyond SMAC — to single-agent Procgen/MuJoCo, continuous control, or domains with less informative renderers (partial observability, first-person views)?
  • How does performance scale with VLM capability (VideoLLaMa2-7B vs frontier VLMs), and is there a minimum VLM quality below which VIP underperforms text-only?
  • Can the sanitization step be replaced by a learned / embedding-free method for non-enumerable task spaces (procedurally generated maps coded in natural language or code)?
  • Why exactly do PLR-PVL/MaxMC degenerate onto tiny task subsets on SMAC — would a diversity-regularized PLR close the gap?
  • Would aggregating multiple episode videos per curriculum step improve the VLM's "almost-winning" assessment, and at what compute cost?
  • Is the difficulty-5-harder-than-7 quirk an artifact that inflates VIP's apparent advantage — do results hold at other difficulty levels?
