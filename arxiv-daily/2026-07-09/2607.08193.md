> **一句话总结：** 作者提出 VIP（Visual Inspection of Policies），用轻量级视频语言模型 VideoLLaMa2-7B 直接观看策略的回合录像来推荐下一个训练任务，从而构建开放式课程；在 SMAC 多智能体基准上，VIP 在三张未见过的地图中把胜率从近乎 0% 提升到约 80%，显著优于纯文本消融、PLR（基于标量分数）以及随机课程等基线。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces Visual Inspection of Policies (VIP), an open-ended autocurriculum method that feeds recorded episode videos of the current policy to a Video Language Model (VideoLLaMa2-7B) so it can recommend the next training task. On the StarCraft Multi-Agent Challenge (SMAC) benchmark, VIP fine-tunes to ~84% win rate on the unseen map 3s vs 4z versus 0% for the text-only ablation and both PLR variants, and to 76% on 3s5z where most baselines sit near 0%, while the VLM consumes only ~1% of total training compute.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 VIP（Visual Inspection of Policies）：首个用视频语言模型直接观看策略回合录像来评估学习进度并推荐下一个任务的开源式课程方法，绕过传统标量分数/文本摘要对学习信号的依赖。
- 通过"纯文本消融"严谨证明视频输入的关键作用——视频版与文本版的唯一区别就是是否向 VLM 提供视频，从而干净地分离出视觉信息带来的增益。
- 将开放式无监督课程首次应用到 SMAC（StarCraft 多智能体挑战）这一复杂、策略性、可变智能体数量的协作领域；此前 UED 类方法要么只适用于单智能体，要么（如 MAESTRO）仅适用于两人零和对抗，无法直接迁移。
- 证明方法天然支持任意数量智能体（只要它们被渲染进同一段视频），且除 VLM 与底层 RL 算法外不引入任何新超参数——这对以"替代暴力网格搜索"为目标的开源式课程尤其重要。
- 提供简单可靠的"句子相似度净化"模块处理 VLM 幻觉/格式错误，使方法无需迭代式提示即可在实验中覆盖全部 VLM 失败模式。

## Key Contributions

- VIP, the first open-ended autocurriculum that uses a Video Language Model to directly inspect episode videos of the current policy (rather than scalar scores or textual summaries) in order to assess learning progress and recommend the next task.
- A clean text-only ablation that isolates the contribution of video: the only difference between VIP and its ablation is the presence of videos and phrases referencing them in the prompt, empirically demonstrating that policy videos are crucial to VIP's effectiveness.
- The first application of unsupervised open-ended autocurricula to SMAC, a strategic cooperative multi-agent domain with varying number/type of agents — previously unexplored for unsupervised RL autocurricula (MAESTRO, the closest UED baseline for multi-agent, is restricted to two-player zero-sum adversarial games).
- Agent-count-agnostic design: VIP works for any number of controllable agents as long as they are rendered in a single video, spending the same compute to analyze a whole team as a single policy (N=1 prompt when agents share one model).
- A deliberately minimal method with no new hyperparameters beyond the VLM and the low-level RL algorithm, plus a sentence-similarity sanitization module that recovers all VLM failure modes in their experiments without iterative prompting.

## Methodology

VIP is a simple loop (Algorithm 1 in the paper) for open-ended curriculum generation in (multi-agent) RL:

1. **Train-RL.** Policies are trained on the most recently recommended task θₜ using an RL algorithm — MAPPO (Multi-Agent PPO) in all experiments — for 10⁷ environment steps per task.
2. **Eval.** The resulting policy is evaluated on the current task to collect (a) one or more recorded episode videos φₜᵛⁱᵈᵉᵒ and (b) a small textual summary φₜᵗᵉˣᵗ — in this work just the win rate and the list of previously selected tasks.
3. **VLM-Next-Task.** The video(s) plus the textual summary are fed to the VLM, which is prompted to recommend the next most interesting/learnable task based on its assessment of the agents' current capabilities. The VLM here is **VideoLLaMa2-7B** (the 7B quantized, openly accessible model), used as-is with no video pre-processing; since it does not support video batching, one episode video per prompt is used.
4. **Sanitize-Task.** The raw VLM recommendation is passed through a sentence-similarity module (sinusoidal embeddings) that returns the highest-similarity *valid* task from the enumerable task space, with a threshold that falls back to a random task if no candidate is similar enough (this fallback was never triggered in practice). This recovers VLM hallucinations/format errors without iterative prompting.

Key design properties. The task space is SMAC maps (allied/enemy troop compositions) × difficulty (1–10), a finite enumerable subset of the infinite composition space; maps are split 80% train (12 maps) / 20% held-out test (3 maps: 10m vs 11m, 3s vs 4z, 3s5z, all at difficulty 5). Because zero-shot transfer in SMAC rarely works, generalization is measured by win rate after fine-tuning on each test task for another 10⁷ steps, sharing the same default MAPPO hyperparameters across all curricula (to avoid relying on downstream-task knowledge). All results report 95% confidence intervals over 5 seeds.

Baselines. (i) Text-only ablation (identical prompt minus video); (ii) Robust Prioritized Level Replay (PLR⊥) with two score functions, Positive Value Loss (PVL) and Maximum Monte-Carlo (MaxMC); (iii) control baselines — random curriculum and MAPPO trained from scratch; and a secondary comparison against MAPPO* (MAPPO with best grid-search hyperparameters per task, which is supervised and thus not a fair unsupervised baseline).

Why videos help: scalar/textual summaries compress rich behavior and miss the case where a 0%-win-rate policy is actually close to a winning strategy (e.g., agents gain a numbers advantage then panic and lose to a single enemy — Figure 1). A VLM watching the video can recognize "almost-winning" and correctly keep training on the same task, which any local-learning-signal curator (UED/PLR) fundamentally cannot.

## Results & Highlights

- **Held-out map 3s vs 4z (win rate after fine-tuning, Table 1):** VIP reaches **0.84 ± 0.16**, while the text-only ablation, PLR-PVL, and PLR-MaxMC all hit **0.00 ± 0.00** — a delta of +84 percentage points versus every non-control baseline on the hardest unseen map.
- **Held-out map 3s5z (win rate, Table 1):** VIP reaches **0.76 ± 0.20**, versus **0.03 ± 0.05** for PLR-PVL and **0.00 ± 0.00** for PLR-MaxMC; the text-only ablation only reaches **0.21 ± 0.30** — i.e., adding video lifts win rate from ~21% to ~76% on the same VLM.
- **Seen map 3s vs 3z (win rate, Table 2):** VIP reaches **1.00 ± 0.00**, compared to **0.78 ± 0.38** (text-only), **0.67 ± 0.06** (MAPPO from scratch), and **0.42 ± 0.54** (random) — faster and more stable convergence with a near-perfect final policy.
- **Seen map 3s vs 5z (win rate, Table 2):** VIP fine-tunes to **0.34 ± 0.35**, while *all* baselines (MAPPO, random, text-only, PLR-MaxMC) are stuck at **0.00 ± 0.00** — the only method to find any meaningful solution.
- **Compute efficiency vs grid search (Table 3):** VIP uses **~1.6 × 10⁸** environment interactions across all tasks (16-task curriculum) versus **~1.7 × 10¹⁰ per task** for MAPPO* (≈1700 grid-search combinations × 10⁷ steps), i.e. ~100× fewer interactions, while matching MAPPO* on 3s5z (0.76 vs 0.76 win rate) and showing faster convergence there despite far less compute.
- **VLM is not a bottleneck:** VLM inference consumes only **~1%** of each VIP run's wall-clock time; MAPPO training dominates the other ~99%, so the curriculum cost is negligible (Appendix E).

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are Lorenzo Pantè and Andrea Fanti (Sapienza University of Rome) and Roberto Capobianco (Sony AI, Zurich). Capobianco has a track record in RL and explainable/interactive RL, lending domain credibility. The paper is positioned as a preprint (arXiv:2607.08193v1, 9 Jul 2026) and is methodologically careful: shared default hyperparameters across all curricula to avoid smuggling in downstream-task knowledge, 95% CIs over 5 seeds, a clean text-only ablation that genuinely isolates the video contribution, and an explicit secondary comparison against supervised grid search (MAPPO*) to honestly contextualize that VIP does not yet beat brute-force per-task tuning on every map. The use of a single lightweight open VLM (VideoLLaMa2-7B) rather than a frontier closed model is a credibility plus for reproducibility but also a ceiling on VLM reasoning quality.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The core idea — using a VLM to *watch* policy rollouts and pick the next task — is genuinely fresh relative to OMNI/OMNI-EPIC (which relegate video LMs to mere success detectors and found them unreliable), LLM-POET, and the Zhang et al. (2024b) VLM-guided method that requires a *manually pre-identified skill set*. VIP's contribution is the deliberate simplicity of feeding raw videos as the primary progress signal, which the ablation shows is load-bearing. The honest framing against OMNI-EPIC's negative finding about Video LMs is a strength.
- **Rigor.** Strong on internal validity: shared hyperparameters, 5-seed CIs, a clean text-only ablation, and a frank MAPPO* comparison. Weak on breadth — a single domain (SMAC), only 3 held-out maps, and the task space is a *finite enumerable* subset where the sentence-similarity sanitization is trivially applicable. The paper itself flags that the sanitization approach does not immediately generalize to non-enumerable task spaces (Section 4.1).
- **Reproducibility.** Good in principle (open VLM, no new hyperparameters, standard MAPPO/SMAC) but no mention of code release in the main text, and the curriculum depends on SMAC's built-in rendering, which the authors admit "can sometimes be limited in accurately depicting the state of the game" (Appendix A.3) — a quietly important caveat for whether the VLM is really seeing what matters.
- **Significance.** Meaningful as a proof-of-concept that visual inspection can unlock curricula invisible to learning-signal methods, but the absolute win rates (e.g., 0.53 on 10m vs 11m vs MAPPO*'s 0.84) and the single-benchmark scope keep it from being a definitive state-of-the-art result.
- **Red flags / specific concerns.** (1) On 10m vs 11m VIP only ties the text-only ablation and random curriculum (0.51–0.53) — the authors attribute this to marine troops rarely appearing in VIP curricula, which is a real generalization gap, not just an artifact. (2) PLR is arguably handicapped: both PLR variants end at 0.00 on most held-out maps and PLR-PVL "never chose" 3s vs 3z or 3s vs 5z during its curriculum (Table 2 ⋆ entries), suggesting PLR collapsed onto a tiny subset of tasks — a fair loss, but the paper does not deeply diagnose *why* PLR degenerates here. (3) Difficulty-5 being "harder than difficulty-7" (Appendix B) is a SMAC-specific quirk that makes the benchmark choice look favorable and deserves scrutiny. (4) There is no ablation over VLM choice, video count/length, or prompt variants — so the robustness of the central claim to the specific (and relatively weak) VideoLLaMa2-7B is untested.

## Strengths

- Genuinely novel framing: open-ended autocurricula driven by direct visual inspection of policy behavior, mirroring how a human coach reviews game tape.
- Clean, well-motivated ablation that isolates the value of video input; the Figure-1 "0% win rate but almost winning" example is a compelling, concrete motivation.
- Strong empirical gains on hard unseen SMAC maps (3s vs 4z: 0.00 → 0.84) where essentially all scalar/text baselines fail.
- Agent-count-agnostic and essentially hyperparameter-free — desirable properties for the open-ended-learning setting where grid search is precisely what one wants to avoid.
- Honest about compute: VLM is ~1% of runtime, and an explicit ~100×-fewer-interactions comparison versus supervised grid search.

## Limitations

- Single domain (SMAC only); generalization to other MARL or single-agent benchmarks is unproven.
- The sanitization module relies on an *enumerable* task space and natural-language-like task names; non-enumerable or cryptic task spaces would need iterative prompting or a trained embedding model.
- VIP cannot be robust to the choice of VLM by construction (it inherits the VLM's weaknesses), and only one (lightweight) VLM is tested.
- 10m vs 11m shows no video advantage over text-only/random — a real blind spot tied to troop-type coverage in the generated curriculum.
- Reliance on SMAC's built-in renderer, which the authors admit can inaccurately depict game state, raises a subtle question about whether the VLM is reasoning from faithful observations.
- No ablations over video count/length, prompt variants, or alternative VLMs; PLR's degenerate behavior is not deeply diagnosed.

## Relevance

对研究"开放式课程学习 / 自演化 RL 智能体 / 多智能体协同"方向高度相关：VIP 展示了多模态大模型（VLM）如何作为"教练"直接观察智能体行为视频来驱动无监督课程生成，与 LLM-agent 自演化、reward shaping、curriculum learning 等主题天然衔接，可作为"用基础模型指导 RL 训练"这一趋势在多智能体视觉场景下的代表性工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does VIP transfer beyond SMAC — e.g., to single-agent Procgen/MuJoCo, to continuous-control, or to domains where the renderer is less informative (partial observability, first-person views)?
- How does performance scale with VLM capability (VideoLLaMa2-7B vs larger/frontier VLMs), and is there a minimum VLM quality below which VIP underperforms text-only?
- Can the sanitization step be replaced by a learned/embedding-free method for non-enumerable task spaces (e.g., procedurally generated maps coded in natural language or code)?
- Why exactly do PLR-PVL/MaxMC degenerate onto tiny task subsets on SMAC, and would a diversity-regularized PLR close the gap?
- Would aggregating multiple episode videos (rather than one) per curriculum step improve the VLM's assessment of "almost-winning" policies, and at what compute cost?
- Is the difficulty-5-harder-than-7 quirk an artifact that inflates VIP's apparent advantage, and do results hold at other difficulty levels?
