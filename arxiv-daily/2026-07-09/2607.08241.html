Why this matters

Anyone porting post-training quantization to CFG or Stable Diffusion–class models must heed this null-space failure mode before trusting gap-based diagnostics.

Critical edge

The headline 49% gain beats a synthetic linear interpolation between two uniform endpoints — not real mixed-precision allocators (BRECQ, HAWQ), which are discussed but never run.

Plus: single seed, single 6.1M model on CIFAR-10, no error bars despite ±2–3 FID variance — some ordering claims sit inside the noise band.

Methodology

CIFAR-10 · DASH 6.1M ADM UNet · K=50 DDIM · TAG w=4.0

The paper proves a gap-only PTQ objective admits an exact null space: any shared drift vector δ cancels in the gap (ε_q^y = ε_fp^y + δ, ε_q^∅ = ε_fp^∅ + δ ⇒ L_gap = 0) while the unconditional branch drifts unboundedly — and that drift corrupts the guided prediction 1:1, independent of guidance scale, accumulating as O(K‖δ‖) over the trajectory.

GAMP fixes weights at 4-bit and allocates activation bits by guided-output sensitivity, closing the null space by construction (E(l)=0 ⇒ δ=0):

  1. Dual-branch calibration — record per-layer activation stats through both branches (avoids the conditional branch's wider-range clipping underestimate).
  2. Cache FP32 guided output ε̃_fp for all 1024 calibration images.
  3. Per-layer sensitivity — set each of L=49 layers to A4, measure guided-output error E(l) (49 forward passes, one-time).
  4. Greedy knapsack — rank by sensitivity-per-BOPs, promote A4→A8 until budget b̄ met.
  5. Threshold calibration via L_GAMP over 800 Adam iterations. ~22 min offline, one-time.

Strengths 5
  • Branch-drift trap is genuinely novel — cleanly proven, "obvious in hindsight" insight explaining why naïve gap calibration fails silently.
  • Unusually honest about limits — discloses single seed, single model, the 13.67-vs-8.87 FP32 gap, and the stack-specific INT8 result.
  • System-level diagnosis stands alone — 1.99× CFG tax hidden from BOPs; 147× INT8 slowdown traced to 192 memcpy nodes. Actionable for practitioners.
  • Controlled ablation turns theory into evidence — Gap-only-W4A8 converts Corollary 1 from abstract worry into a measured false positive (ρ=1.004, cos(∆)=0.882 → FID 334).
  • GAMP is principled — calibrates on the exact quantity CFG samples depend on (ε̃); greedy knapsack near-optimality is justified, not asserted.
Limitations 6
  • Scale & scope — only 32×32 CIFAR-10 on a compact 6.1M student UNet. No Stable Diffusion / DiT, no high-res validation.
  • Single seed, no error bars — despite ±2–3 FID variance; GAMP-b̄5 (40.01) vs b̄6 (39.40) is within the noise band.
  • Weak mixed-precision baselines — 49% gain is vs. a synthetic linear interpolation; BRECQ/HAWQ never run.
  • Guidance-scale range — all quality runs at w=4.0; predicted trap intensification at w~7–15 is not empirically demonstrated.
  • Deployment claims are stack-conditional — 147× result is ORT-without-TensorRT on a Kaggle T4; TensorRT/H100 unrealized speedups not measured.
  • No direct instrumentation of δ — trap confirmed indirectly via FID collapse; ‖ε_q^∅ − ε_fp^∅‖ not isolated.

Open questions

Where the contribution needs to be tested next

  1. Does the trap manifest at text-to-image scales (w ~ 7–15) on Stable Diffusion / DiT, and does GAMP's edge over uniform precision grow with w as the analysis predicts?
  2. How does GAMP compare against real per-layer allocators (BRECQ block-reconstruction, HAWQ Hessian) on identical W4/A8 blocks — not a synthetic line?
  3. Would directly instrumenting ‖ε_q^∅ − ε_fp^∅‖ isolate the drift mechanism, and could a cheap unconditional anchor term λ‖ε_q^∅ − ε_fp^∅‖² close the null space without full guided-output calibration?
  4. Does combining GAMP's per-layer allocation with PTQD-style timestep-aware mixed precision (a complementary trajectory error source) yield additive gains?
  5. On TensorRT / H100-class accelerators, does the BOPs-predicted 16× speedup materialize, or do the Slice/downsample and memcpy artifacts persist as architecture-independent bottlenecks?
