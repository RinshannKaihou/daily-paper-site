05

Methodology

Built on Top-K SAEs (expansion R=32, K=256) on the residual stream of Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct at layers 5 / 10 / 15 / 20, treating image patches and text tokens as equivalent units in a shared dictionary. The twist: a structural visual-region prior is injected through two complementary regularizers, and a hierarchical pipeline turns the learned features into human-readable concepts.

  1. Step 1 · Region grouping
    Fuse multi-layer-averaged attention similarity with patch Manhattan distance (d = d_A ⊙ (d_S)^α, α=0.02); agglomerative clustering partitions patches into G coherent regions.
  2. Step 2 · Structured sparsity
    Exclusive Sparsity (ℓ1,2) forces each feature into one region; Group Sparsity (ℓ2,1) makes a region share a sparse feature subset. An STE binarization layer targets co-occurrence, not magnitude.
  3. Step 3 · Hierarchical interpretation
    Qwen3-VL-8B labels masked references (sampled by Ψ = mean-act × max-IoU); Qwen3-30B abstracts and scores cross-modal consistency. Beats direct VLM summary at L5: 98.8% vs 66.4%.

07

Strengths · Limitations

Strengths

  • Well-motivated problem — residual polysemanticity & spatially fragmented activations, shown compellingly in Fig 1.
  • Principled two-regularizer design — ablation cleanly shows ES and GS are complementary, not redundant.
  • STE binarization thoughtfully targets activation co-occurrence rather than magnitude.
  • Hierarchical pipeline empirically justified (98.8% vs 66.4%) with concrete failure modes (Big Ben, sprinkle donut).
  • Rich qualitative analysis — word-cloud concept evolution L5→20; dog/breed case studies.
  • Excellent openness — code, dataset, and interactive explorer all public.

Limitations

  • Circular evaluation — mIoUᶜ uses the model’s own attention clusters as “truth”; no ADE20K/COCO segmentation; absolute MS/consistency stay low.
  • Single backbone — only Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct; InternVL / GLM-V / Qwen3-VL untested.
  • Thin baselines — only vanilla Top-K SAE; no Gated / JumpReLU / BatchTopK / Archetypal, nor the Kaushik 2026 group-sparse multimodal SAE.
  • No downstream utility — no hallucination, steering, editing, or unlearning evaluation.
  • Judge bias — interpretation & consistency scoring depend on specific Qwen3 models, uncontrolled.
  • Abstract anomaly — “60.81 representational efficiency” unreconciled with the 256 → 180–215 body figures.
08

Open questions

Q1

Do gains hold on InternVL, GLM-V, Qwen3-VL and larger backbones — or are region priors specific to Qwen2.5-VL’s attention geometry?

Q2

Do Gated / JumpReLU / Archetypal SAEs benefit equally from ES+GS — is the gain orthogonal to the base SAE?

Q3

Can mIoU be validated against human segmentation (ADE20K/COCO) to rule out circular evaluation?

Q4

Do cleaner features transfer downstream — better concept steering, activation patching for hallucination, targeted unlearning?

Q5

Is the emergent language-side improvement causal (ablatable on the shared dictionary) or a side effect of general regularization?

Q6

Consistency plateaus near ~0.35 — what architectural, data, or training change would push it substantially higher?
