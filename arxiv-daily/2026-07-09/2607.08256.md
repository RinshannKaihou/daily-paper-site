> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示零样本 TTS 中 Best-of-N 推理的验证器排名严重依赖于评估器所属的 ASR 家族（同家族验证器-评估器对可恢复 2-3 倍的 oracle 上限），并提出跨家族排名集成方法在 LibriSpeech-PC 上将平均 WER 降至 1.61%（较 F5-TTS 下降 12%）。

> **TL;DR:** This paper identifies an evaluation confound in Best-of-N (BoN) zero-shot TTS: a verifier's apparent quality depends strongly on which ASR family judges it, with verifier rankings reversing across Whisper, wav2vec 2.0, and HuBERT evaluators on LibriSpeech-PC test-clean. The authors propose cross-family rank ensembles (rank-avg, max-rank) that achieve the lowest mean WER of 1.61% at N=10 (−12% relative vs. F5-TTS baseline at 2.06%), with the best single verifier (distil-v3) reaching 1.72% (−16.5%) under the official F5-TTS evaluator.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示评估混淆现象**：首次系统性地证明 BoN-TTS 中验证器（verifier）的排名高度依赖于评估器（evaluator）所属的 ASR 家族——同一批生成音频在不同家族评估器下被排成相反顺序，这是语音领域类比 LLM-as-a-judge 自我偏见的首次记录。
- **量化混淆的量级**：在 LibriSpeech-PC test-clean 上证明 (验证器, 评估器) 家族配对是比"选择哪个验证器 checkpoint"大得多的杠杆；同家族配对可恢复 2-3 倍的 oracle headroom（如 distil-v3 恢复 26.0% 而 cross-family w2v2-base 仅 7.9%，差距 3.3×）。
- **排除表征相似性解释**：通过线性 CKA 分析证明，验证器-评估器间的音频编码器表征相似度（如同家族 distil-sm↔whisper-med CKA 高达 0.978）并不能预测 WER 排名一致性（该对反而反相关 r=−0.52），混淆更符合身份/谱系级耦合而非表征重叠。
- **提出跨家族排名集成**：设计 rank-avg（平均排名）与 max-rank（最差情况排名）两种跨家族集成方法，在三个独立评估器上同时降低 WER，是最具跨评估器鲁棒性的配置。
- **倡导跨评估器三角验证**：建议将"在至少两个训练谱系不相交的 ASR 家族下报告 WER"作为默认的报告规范。

## Key Contributions

- **Documents an evaluator-dependent verifier-ranking confound.** The same generated BoN candidates are ranked in opposing directions by evaluators from different ASR families, so a verifier preferred under one family can lose under another—the first speech analog of LLM-as-a-judge self-bias.
- **Quantifies the magnitude of the confound.** On LibriSpeech-PC test-clean, the (verifier, evaluator) family pairing is shown to be a much larger lever on reported WER than the choice among common verifier checkpoints, while a large oracle headroom (1.42% at N=3) remains unexploited.
- **Rules out representation similarity as the mechanism.** Linear CKA analysis shows that high audio-encoder similarity (up to 0.978 for same-family pairs) does not predict WER-ranking agreement—the same-family Distil-Whisper pair is in fact anti-correlated (r=−0.52)—pointing instead to identity/lineage-level coupling.
- **Proposes cross-family rank ensembles.** Two aggregation methods (rank-averaging and conjunctive max-rank over the cross-family {w2v2-base, distil-v3} pair) deliver the most cross-evaluator-robust BoN configurations, reaching 1.61% mean WER at N=10.
- **Recommends cross-evaluator triangulation as default reporting practice**, i.e., reporting WER under at least two ASR families with disjoint training lineages.

## Methodology

The study fixes a single shared set of BoN candidates and varies only the verifier and evaluator, enabling clean isolation of the family-alignment confound.

**Synthesis.** The publicly released F5-TTS base model is used with its default inference recipe (32 ODE solver steps, classifier-free guidance scale 2.0, sway sampling). For each utterance, 10 BoN candidates are generated with different random initializations; for each N∈{3,5,10} the leading N candidates are taken and the one with the lowest joint WER+CER score against the reference transcript is selected.

**Verifiers.** Three single-model verifiers plus one three-way ensemble: wav2vec 2.0 base (95M params, "w2v2-base"), small Distil-Whisper (166M, "distil-sm"), large Distil-Whisper (756M, "distil-v3"), and "ens3" (three-way rank-average of these three). For the scaling study, two additional cross-family rank ensembles aggregate only the {w2v2-base, distil-v3} pair: rank-avg (lowest average rank across both families) and max-rank (lowest worst-case rank, a conjunctive form that penalizes single-family inflation).

**Evaluators.** Every candidate is scored by four ASR evaluators spanning three families: faster-whisper Whisper-large-v3 ("fwhisper-lgv3", the official F5-TTS evaluator), wav2vec 2.0 large ("w2v2-lv60"), the same Distil-Whisper used as verifier ("distil-sm"), and large HuBERT ("hubert-lg"). For the CKA–WER analysis, Whisper-medium ("whisper-med") is added as a fourth evaluator.

**Data & normalization.** Evaluation is on the LibriSpeech-PC test-clean cross-sentence subset (1127 samples, 4–10s references). The F5-TTS official evaluation pipeline is run verbatim with its default text normalization applied uniformly across all four ASR outputs; Whisper's English-specific normalizer is deliberately not applied separately to keep the cross-ASR WER comparison on equal footing.

**Quality metrics.** Speaker similarity SIM-o is the cosine of WavLM speaker embeddings (audio resampled 24→16 kHz symmetrically); naturalness is UTMOS from a 22-strong checkpoint ensemble. RTFs are measured isolated on a single RTX 4090. Statistical significance uses two-sided paired permutation tests against F5-TTS (10,000 permutations).

**Mechanism probe.** Linear CKA (Kornblith et al., 2019) is computed between the audio encoders of six ASR checkpoints on 500 F5-TTS-synthesized waveforms, and Pearson r between CKA and WER-ranking agreement is assessed over the 6 evaluator pairs and 13 BoN configurations.

## Results & Highlights

- **Baseline and best single-verifier gain (Table 1, official fwhisper-lgv3 evaluator, LibriSpeech-PC test-clean).** Single-shot F5-TTS reaches 2.06% WER; BoN with distil-v3 reduces it to 1.88% (−8.7% relative, p=0.030) at RTF 0.379—the only statistically significant single-verifier reduction. ens3 is a close runner-up at 1.91% (−7.2%, p=0.057), while w2v2-base (1.99%, p=0.31) and distil-sm (2.04%, p=0.93) are not significant.
- **Verifier ranking reverses across evaluators (Table 2).** Under fwhisper-lgv3 the Distil-Whisper verifier distil-v3 wins (1.88%); under w2v2-lv60 the wav2vec 2.0 verifier w2v2-base wins (1.41%). The distil-v3 BoN reduction is 0.18pp under fwhisper-lgv3 but only 0.07pp under w2v2-lv60—reversing the verifier ranking between Whisper and wav2vec 2.0 evaluators.
- **Same-family pairs recover 2–3× more oracle headroom (Table 3, N=3).** Under fwhisper-lgv3, same-family distil-v3 recovers 26.0% of headroom vs. only 7.9% for cross-family w2v2-base (3.3×); under w2v2-lv60, same-family w2v2-base recovers 26.1% vs. 18.2% for cross-family distil-v3 (1.4×). The N=3 oracle WER is 1.42% (fwhisper-lgv3), 1.09% (w2v2-lv60), 1.18% (hubert-lg), leaving substantial room.
- **Cross-family ensembles scale monotonically and are most robust (Table 4 / Figure 1).** At N=10, rank-avg and max-rank both reach mean WER 1.61% (−12% relative vs. F5-TTS's 1.83% mean); rank-avg at N=5 is the only configuration reaching p<0.05 under all three evaluators simultaneously (.001/.022/.0002). The single cross-family verifier w2v2-base *regresses* under fwhisper-lgv3 from 1.99% (N=3) to 2.20% (N=5), exposing the same-family-evaluator penalty.
- **Best same-family result under the official evaluator.** distil-v3 at N=10 drives WER from 2.06% → 1.72% (−16.5%, p<.001) under fwhisper-lgv3—the largest reduction under that single evaluator.
- **Quality is preserved.** SIM-o stays within ±0.0006 and UTMOS within ±0.005 of F5-TTS (0.9426/3.879) across all configurations, so WER gains come at no measurable naturalness/similarity cost.
- **CKA does not predict the confound (Table 5 / Figure 2).** The same-family Whisper pair distil-sm↔whisper-med has the highest CKA (0.978) yet is anti-correlated in WER agreement (r=−0.52); Pearson(CKA, r)=−0.36 over six pairs reverses to +0.32 once the same-family outlier is removed, confirming representation similarity is not the mechanism.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are Taehyung Yu and Seongjae Kang, both at KAIST (Daejeon, South Korea). The work is accepted at the ICML 2026 Workshop on Machine Learning for Audio (Seoul). Credibility is bolstered by full reproducibility commitments: all models are used at their default public revisions, and code, decoding settings, and evaluation scripts are released at https://github.com/yu1012/BoN-TTS. The experimental design—fixing one shared candidate set and varying only verifier/evaluator—is a clean, well-controlled way to isolate the confound, and the statistical rigor (paired permutation tests with 10,000 permutations) exceeds the norm for TTS evaluation papers.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely useful and underexplored. The LLM-as-a-judge self-bias analog is well-motivated, and translating it to the (ASR-verifier, ASR-evaluator) setting in TTS is a fresh, non-obvious contribution that prior BoN-TTS work (CosyVoice 2, Seed-TTS, NaturalSpeech 3) has overlooked by tuning against a single fixed ASR.
- **Rigor:** Strong on controls and statistics. Using a shared candidate pool, paired permutation tests, and CKA as a mechanistic probe are all appropriate. The honesty about the 0.36pp gap between the single-shot 2.06% and the F5-TTS paper's 2.42% multi-seed mean (and the 2.04 vs 2.06 batching non-determinism footnote) is commendable.
- **Reproducibility:** High. Public checkpoints at default revisions, a single GPU (RTX 4090), released code, and a standard benchmark (LibriSpeech-PC) make replication straightforward.
- **Significance caveats:** The scope is narrow by the authors' own admission—**a single TTS backbone (F5-TTS) on a single benchmark (LibriSpeech-PC test-clean, 1127 samples)**. The confound could plausibly be larger or smaller on CosyVoice 2 / MaskGCT or on noisier test sets, so the headline "2–3× family swing" is a point estimate on one system. The absolute WER differences driving the ranking reversals are small (0.07–0.18pp), so the practical impact on downstream applications is modest even if the methodological lesson is real.
- **Specific weakness — single same-family pair for the oracle analysis.** The CKA "trend reversal" hinges on a single same-family outlier (distil-sm↔whisper-med at CKA 0.978), which the authors themselves acknowledge; the Pearson sign flip from −0.36 to +0.32 is single-point-driven and rests on only 6 evaluator pairs and 13 configurations in a 500-sample pilot. More family pairs would strengthen the identity-vs-lineage disentanglement, which is explicitly deferred to future work.
- **Missing ablation:** The (w2v2-base, distil-v3) cross-family pair is "fixed in advance as the largest publicly available verifier in each family," so the ensemble design is not searched—whether adding the HuBERT-family verifier into the ensemble helps is untested.

## Strengths

- Identifies and cleanly isolates a real, previously undocumented evaluation confound that directly affects how every recent flow-matching TTS paper reports BoN WER.
- The cross-family rank ensembles are simple, training-free, and provably more robust than any single verifier—a practical, immediately adoptable remedy.
- Mechanistic analysis (CKA) goes beyond mere description and actively rules out the most natural alternative explanation.
- Excellent statistical hygiene: paired permutation tests, explicit p-values, and honest disclosure of non-determinism and baseline gaps.
- Concrete, actionable reporting recommendation (cross-evaluator triangulation) that the community can adopt with near-zero cost.

## Limitations

- **Single TTS backbone and single benchmark.** Only F5-TTS on LibriSpeech-PC test-clean (1127 samples); generalization to CosyVoice 2, MaskGCT, and to a fourth ASR family (Parakeet, Canary) is explicitly left to future work.
- **No human-MOS triangulation.** UTMOS saturates around 4.0 and SIM-o is near its resolution limit, so quality-preservation claims rest on automatic metrics that may be at ceiling; human / NISQA / UTMOSv2 triangulation is deferred.
- **Small absolute effects.** The ranking reversals rest on WER deltas of 0.07–0.18pp, which may be within run-to-run or normalization sensitivity for some downstream uses.
- **Identity- vs. family-level coupling not disentangled.** The mechanism is characterized as "consistent with" lineage coupling but the two hypotheses are not separated.
- **Ensemble design is not searched.** Only the pre-fixed {w2v2-base, distil-v3} pair is ensembled; adding HuBERT-family or alternative aggregation rules is unexplored.

## Relevance

本论文直击零样本 TTS 推理与评估方法论的痛点：对于关注 flow-matching 语音合成（F5-TTS、CosyVoice、MaskGCT 等）质量评测与推理时优化（Best-of-N、CFG）的研究者，其揭示的"验证器-评估器家族混淆"现象及跨家族集成方案具有直接的实践指导意义，提示在自研或复现 TTS 系统时应采用多 ASR 家族三角验证以避免报告偏颇。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the confound generalize beyond F5-TTS to CosyVoice 2, MaskGCT, and NaturalSpeech 3, and does its magnitude scale with the TTS backbone's own error rate?
- Would adding a HuBERT-family verifier into the cross-family ensemble (beyond the w2v2-base/distil-v3 pair) further improve cross-evaluator robustness, or does the conjunctive max-rank saturate?
- Can the identity- vs. family-level coupling be disentangled—for instance, by using two independently trained wav2vec 2.0 checkpoints to separate "same architecture" from "same lineage/training data"?
- Is the observed same-family inflation robust to text-normalization choices (the paper deliberately disables Whisper's English normalizer), and could normalization differences alone account for part of the ranking reversals?
- Given that the N=3 oracle WER is 1.42% while the best verifier only reaches ~1.72%, what verifier-design improvements (e.g., adversarial reweighting against in-family inflation) could close the remaining headroom?
- Does the confound appear in human-evaluation correlations, or is it purely an artifact of automatic ASR-based WER scoring?
