Key finding · BoN-TTS evaluation confound

A TTS verifier's apparent quality depends on which ASR family judges it — rankings reverse across Whisper, wav2vec 2.0 & HuBERT.

Why this matters

Every recent flow-matching TTS paper reports BoN WER against a single fixed ASR — this work shows that choice can silently flip which verifier "wins," and gives a training-free fix.

Relevance · TL;DR
▲ Critical edge

Scope is narrow: a single TTS backbone (F5-TTS) on a single benchmark (1127 samples), with ranking reversals resting on WER deltas of just 0.07–0.18pp — a point estimate on one system.

Critical Assessment · Limitations

Methodology

The study fixes one shared set of BoN candidates and varies only the verifier and evaluator, cleanly isolating the family-alignment confound — a well-controlled design paired with 10,000-permutation paired permutation tests.

  • Synthesis. Public F5-TTS base, default recipe (32 ODE steps, CFG 2.0, sway sampling); 10 BoN candidates per utterance via different random inits; for each N∈{3,5,10} the lowest joint WER+CER candidate is kept.
  • Verifiers. w2v2-base (95M), distil-sm (166M), distil-v3 (756M), ens3 (3-way rank-avg); plus cross-family rank-avg & conjunctive max-rank over {w2v2-base, distil-v3}.
  • Evaluators. Four ASR models across three families — fwhisper-lgv3 (official F5-TTS eval), w2v2-lv60, distil-sm, hubert-lg; whisper-med added for the CKA–WER probe.
  • Data & normalization. LibriSpeech-PC test-clean cross-sentence subset (1127 samples). F5-TTS official pipeline run verbatim; Whisper's English normalizer deliberately not applied to keep cross-ASR comparison equal-footed.
  • Mechanism probe. Linear CKA between audio encoders of six ASR checkpoints on 500 synthesized waveforms; Pearson r of CKA vs. WER-ranking agreement assessed over 6 evaluator pairs × 13 BoN configs.
  • Quality metrics. SIM-o via WavLM speaker embeddings; UTMOS (22-checkpoint ensemble); RTFs on a single RTX 4090.

Strengths

  • Identifies and cleanly isolates a previously undocumented confound affecting how every recent flow-matching TTS paper reports BoN WER.
  • Cross-family rank ensembles are simple, training-free, and provably more robust than any single verifier — an immediately adoptable remedy.
  • CKA mechanism analysis goes beyond description, actively ruling out representation similarity as the driver.
  • Excellent statistical hygiene: paired permutation tests, explicit p-values, honest disclosure of non-determinism and baseline gaps.
  • Actionable, near-zero-cost reporting recommendation: cross-evaluator triangulation.

Limitations

  • Single TTS backbone (F5-TTS) and single benchmark (1127 samples); CosyVoice 2, MaskGCT, and a fourth ASR family are left to future work.
  • No human-MOS triangulation — UTMOS saturates ~4.0 and SIM-o is near resolution limit, so quality-preservation rests on possibly-at-ceiling automatic metrics.
  • Small absolute effects: ranking reversals rest on WER deltas of 0.07–0.18pp.
  • Identity- vs. family-level coupling not disentangled; the CKA sign flip is single-outlier-driven (6 pairs, 13 configs, 500-sample pilot).
  • Ensemble design not searched — only the pre-fixed {w2v2-base, distil-v3} pair is tried; HuBERT-family addition untested.

Open questions for further exploration

  1. Does the confound generalize beyond F5-TTS to CosyVoice 2, MaskGCT, NaturalSpeech 3 — and does its magnitude scale with the backbone's own error rate?
  2. Would adding a HuBERT-family verifier to the cross-family ensemble improve robustness, or does conjunctive max-rank saturate?
  3. Can identity- vs. family-level coupling be separated — e.g. using two independently trained wav2vec 2.0 checkpoints to isolate "same architecture" from "same lineage/training data"?
  4. Is the same-family inflation robust to text-normalization choices (the paper disables Whisper's English normalizer), or could normalization alone explain part of the reversals?
  5. Given N=3 oracle WER is 1.42% but the best verifier only reaches ~1.72%, what verifier-design changes (e.g. adversarial reweighting against in-family inflation) could close the gap?
  6. Does the confound appear in human-evaluation correlations, or is it purely an artifact of automatic ASR-based WER scoring?
