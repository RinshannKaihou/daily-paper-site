Why this matters

Separates two questions researchers conflate — which parameters matter vs. which can be trained in isolation — and shows why LoRA's full-layer low-rank coordination, not importance-ranked sparsity, is what actually works.

Critical edge

The load-bearing control anchors the central causal claim on a single seed (seed 42) at one k on one model — far thinner rigor than the 10-seed LoRA ablation the same authors ran.

01 Methodology

The paper asks a clean, falsifiable question: if Super Weights — the individual scalars whose removal inflates perplexity by orders of magnitude (Yu et al., 2025) — are the most important parameters, can they be fine-tuned in isolation? And do full-layer PEFT methods need any special handling of them?

Six experiments are run on OLMo-1B and OLMo-7B: (1) validating that 9 down_proj positions spike in 100% of 1,000 WikiText-2 samples; (2) pruning replication across 10 models; (3) direct Super Weight training (k = 100 → 8,192); (4) 3×3 neighborhood training (~900 → ~36,864 params); (5) LoRA-dproj-SW-freeze, sweeping scale s ∈ {0.0…1.0}; (6) LoRA-ΔW-SW-freeze, freezing the top-1,000 learned ΔW positions.

Two controls on the same down_proj module — an equal-size random-coordinate set and a rank-8 low-rank update — hold the module fixed to disentangle sparsity from coordinate choice. Setup: AdamW, lr 1e-4, 3 epochs, batch 16; LoRA r=8, α=16 on q/k/v/o_proj. Theoretical appendices formalize intrinsic-dimensionality (Prop. 1-4) and optimizer-curvature dynamics (Thm. 1).

03 Strengths

  • The random-position control on the same module cleanly separates "sparsity fails" from "these specific coordinates fail" — the methodological crux.
  • 10-seed ablation with bit-for-bit identical-prediction counts and explicit p-values sets a reporting standard for PEFT comparisons.
  • Front-and-center honesty about the magnitude-based-ID failure on 5/10 models, even though it complicates the universality story.
  • Two independent theoretical lenses (intrinsic dimensionality + optimizer curvature) making different, testable predictions.

04 Limitations

  • Training experiments confined to OLMo-1B/7B — title's "in LLMs" overstates the empirical base.
  • Primary eval is ARC-Easy; on the only secondary task (Winogrande) no method beats the pretrained baseline.
  • Load-bearing random-coordinate control uses a single seed at one k on one model.
  • Hessian-scaling prediction (M²≈100) not borne out — mean measured ratio only 2.48.
  • Only LoRA is tested among PEFT methods; DoRA, prefix tuning, full adapters untested.

05 Questions for further exploration

  1. Does the "importance ≠ trainability-in-isolation" finding hold on harder benchmarks (MMLU, GSM8K, MT-Bench) and on generative tasks?
  2. Would the random-coordinate control still land above baseline when multi-seeded at multiple k values and on OLMo-7B — or is 64.18% partly seed-luck?
  3. Can the gradient collapse at Super Weight coordinates be mitigated by position-specific learning-rate scaling (dividing SW updates by estimated curvature)?
  4. Does the magnitude-ID failure on 5/10 models mean those families lack Super Weights, or that a Hessian-based signal would recover them?
  5. How do conclusions transfer to DoRA, prefix tuning, full adapters — is "structured full-layer decomposition" the real invariant, or LoRA's rank-1 structure specifically?
  6. The 1/M implicit-suppression theorem assumes diagonal second-order updates — how well does it quantitatively predict Adam's actual update ratios at SW positions?
