> **一句话总结：** 本文证明 LLM 中最关键的"超级权重"(Super Weights)虽然单独剪枝会让模型崩溃，但单独训练它们（100 到 36,864 个参数）却会让准确率掉到随机猜测水平；而 LoRA 通过对整层进行低秩分解（仅 0.16% 参数）反而成功，说明"参数重要性"不等于"参数可孤立训练性"，有效的微调依赖整层的结构化分解而非针对个别关键权重。

> **TL;DR:** On OLMo-1B and OLMo-7B over ARC-Easy, training only the most important "Super Weights" (100-36,864 params) collapses accuracy to random-guessing (~25%) while vanilla LoRA (rank-8, 0.16% of params) reaches 66.88%/77.3% (above the 60.65%/73.3% baselines); an equal-size random-coordinate control on the same down_proj layers hits 64.18%, proving the failure is specific to Super Weight coordinates rather than to sparsity, and a 10-seed ablation shows LoRA-dproj-SW-freeze is statistically indistinguishable from vanilla LoRA (p>0.05).

## 核心贡献

- **验证了 Super Weight 的跨输入一致性**：在 OLMo-1B 上跑 1,000 条 WikiText-2 样本，发现有 9 个权重位置在 100% 的样本中都产生最大激活尖峰，将 Yu et al. (2025) 单次前向传播的识别方法扩展为跨样本的结构性验证。
- **证明单独训练 Super Weight 完全失败**：无论选 100 还是 8,192 个 Super Weight（甚至扩展到 36,864 个邻居参数），ARC-Easy 准确率都掉到 ~25% 随机猜测水平。
- **通过两个对照实验隔离了失败原因**：在同一批 down_proj 层上，训练同等数量的随机坐标（排除 Super Weight 坐标）能到 64.18%，对 down_proj 做秩-8 低秩更新能到 68.77%，所以崩溃不是因为稀疏或模块选择，而是因为专门针对 Super Weight 坐标。
- **揭示 LoRA 对 Super Weight 位置不敏感**：LoRA-dproj-SW-freeze（缩放/冻结对应注意力位置）与 vanilla LoRA 在 10 个种子上统计上无差异；LoRA-ΔW-SW-freeze（冻结 LoRA 自身最大的 1,000 个 ΔW 位置）也无影响，说明注意力表征对位置级限制具有鲁棒性。
- **给出两套理论解释**：内禀维度论证（稀疏坐标只覆盖 k/N 比例的微调子空间）和优化器动力学论证（Super Weight 位置的 M² 倍曲率隐式抑制 1/M 的更新）。
- **重要警示**：在 5 个额外模型上用"最大幅值"识别 Super Weight 并剪枝毫无效果（PPL 变化 <0.1%），说明高幅值并不足以识别 Super Weight，该现象依赖模型家族特异的激活异常模式。

## Key Contributions

- Validates that the same 9 weight positions produce the largest activation spikes in 100% of 1,000 WikiText-2 samples on OLMo-1B, generalizing Yu et al. (2025)'s single-forward-pass identification to a structural, input-invariant property and confirming Super Weight criticality via pruning replication on 5 model families.
- Shows that training Super Weights in isolation fails catastrophically: increasing the trainable set from 100 to 8,192 parameters (and neighborhoods to 36,864) yields no improvement, with all variants at random-guessing accuracy (~25% on 4-choice ARC-Easy) on both OLMo-1B and OLMo-7B.
- Isolates the cause of failure to the Super Weight coordinates themselves via two controls on the same down_proj module — an equal-size random-coordinate set (k=4,096, SW coordinates excluded) reaches 64.18% (above the 60.65% baseline) and a rank-8 low-rank update to down_proj reaches 68.77%, so neither sparsity nor module choice explains the collapse.
- Demonstrates that position-aware constraints within LoRA have no measurable effect: LoRA-dproj-SW-freeze and LoRA-ΔW-SW-freeze are statistically indistinguishable from vanilla LoRA across a 10-seed ablation (p>0.05, 80% of seeds bit-for-bit identical on OLMo-1B, 100% on OLMo-7B).
- Provides two complementary theoretical analyses (Appendices H-I): an intrinsic-dimensionality argument showing sparse coordinate-aligned updates capture only a k/N fraction of the fine-tuning subspace, and an optimizer-dynamics theorem showing M²-fold larger curvature at Super Weight positions implicitly suppresses updates by 1/M.
- Reports a generalization caveat: magnitude-based Super Weight identification produces zero measurable pruning effect on 5 of 10 models (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Llama-3.2-3B/1B, Qwen2.5-1.5B, Gemma-2-9B-it), tempering universality claims.

## Methodology

The paper investigates whether the parameters known to be most important — Super Weights (Yu et al., 2025), individual scalars whose removal inflates perplexity by orders of magnitude — can be fine-tuned in isolation, and whether full-layer PEFT methods require any special treatment of them. Six experiments are run:

1. **Super Weight consistency.** Rather than relying on a single forward pass (the methodological limitation of prior work), the authors run 1,000 random WikiText-2 samples through OLMo-1B and record which down_proj positions produce the largest activation spikes. They find 9 positions are 100% consistent and 13 appear in ≥50% of samples.
2. **Pruning replication.** Zeroing Super Weight coordinates (from Yu et al. coordinates where available, magnitude-based otherwise) on 10 models to confirm criticality; this succeeds on 5 models and produces no effect on the other 5, revealing magnitude alone is insufficient.
3. **Direct Super Weight training.** Train only the top-k Super Weights by magnitude (k ∈ {100, 1000, 4096, 8192}) with everything else frozen.
4. **Neighborhood training.** Extend direct training to a 3×3 patch (radius 1) around each Super Weight, yielding ~900 to ~36,864 trainable parameters.
5. **LoRA-dproj-SW-freeze.** Scale attention LoRA updates at indices matching down_proj Super Weight coordinates by a factor s ∈ {0.0, 0.1, 0.2, 0.5, 0.8, 1.0}; s=0.0 fully freezes those positions, s=1.0 recovers vanilla LoRA. A 10-seed ablation (seeds 42-51) compares s=0.0 vs s=1.0.
6. **LoRA-ΔW-SW-freeze.** Load a trained vanilla LoRA, find the top-1,000 highest-magnitude positions in the learned ΔW=BA·α/r, freeze them via gradient hooks, and retrain; compare against vanilla LoRA over 3 seeds on both 1B and 7B.

Two control experiments on down_proj (random sparse coordinates and rank-8 low-rank update) hold the module fixed to disentangle sparsity from coordinate choice. Setup: OLMo-1B (1.28B) and OLMo-7B for all training experiments; AdamW, lr 1e-4, 3 epochs, effective batch 16; LoRA r=8, α=16, targeting q/k/v/o_proj (2.1M trainable = 0.16% of OLMo-1B); primary eval on ARC-Easy with Winogrande for validation. Theoretical Appendices H-I formalize (i) why sparse training captures only a k/N fraction of the fine-tuning subspace (Proposition 1) and why LoRA's rank-r manifold (dim 32,704/layer) provides ~256× more capacity than k=8,192 sparse coordinates (Proposition 2), and (ii) why adaptive optimizers implicitly suppress Super Weight updates by 1/M due to M²-fold larger curvature (Theorem 1, validated empirically on Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct via activation amplification, Hessian scaling, and perturbation-sensitivity measurements).

## Results & Highlights

- **Super Weight consistency (Table 1):** 9 weight positions produce the largest activation spikes in all 1,000/1,000 WikiText-2 samples on OLMo-1B; 13 positions appear in ≥50% of samples; the set stabilizes by n≈100, confirming Super Weights are structural rather than input-specific.
- **Pruning replication (Table 8):** Zeroing Yu et al. Super Weight coordinates collapses WikiText-2 perplexity 3,663× on OLMo-1B (13.09 → 47,951) with ARC-Easy dropping 60.6% → 27.0%, and 4,400× on OLMo-7B (9.59 → 42,024) with ARC 73.3% → 60.5%; Mistral-7B and Meta-Llama-3-8B PPL diverges to ∞ with ARC falling to ~25%.
- **Direct SW training fails (Table 2):** On OLMo-1B ARC-Easy (baseline 60.65%), training top-100 SW gives 25.30%, top-4,096 gives 26.30%, top-8,192 gives ~25% — an 81× increase in parameter budget yields no improvement; OLMo-7B (baseline 73.3%) mirrors this at 26.1-26.3%.
- **Neighborhood training also fails (Table 3):** Even ~36,864 trainable neighborhood parameters stays at 24.96-25.59% on OLMo-1B; the k=4,096 variant's training loss explodes from 2.00 to 201.92.
- **Random-coordinate control succeeds (Table 6):** Training 4,096 random down_proj positions (excluding SW coordinates) reaches 64.18%, above the 60.65% baseline, and a rank-8 low-rank update to down_proj reaches 68.77%, localizing the collapse to SW coordinates specifically.
- **LoRA variants all succeed (Tables 4-6):** Vanilla LoRA reaches 66.88% (1B) / 77.3% (7B); LoRA-dproj-SW-freeze (s=0.0) reaches 66.50% / 76.7% — the full sweep s∈{0.0…1.0} spans less than 1 percentage point, with freezing 6.8%/0.5% of LoRA params having no effect.
- **10-seed ablation (Table 11):** Vanilla LoRA and LoRA-dproj-SW-freeze achieve identical mean accuracy 62.90% ± 0.45% on OLMo-1B (8/10 seeds bit-for-bit identical, p>0.05) and 66.05% ± 1.14% on OLMo-7B (10/10 seeds identical, p=1.0).
- **LoRA's own ΔW positions are non-critical (Table 5):** Freezing the top-1,000 highest-magnitude positions in the trained LoRA ΔW gives 62.77% ± 0.62% vs vanilla 62.72% ± 0.62% on OLMo-1B — statistically identical, in sharp contrast to base-model Super Weights whose removal is catastrophic.
- **Update size does not determine outcome (Tables 9-10):** Direct training changes SW magnitudes 55× more than LoRA (8.19% vs 0.15% mean) yet fails, while LoRA's tiny coordinated updates succeed.
- **Theory matches empirics:** Proposition 1 predicts k=8,192 sparse coordinates capture only 6.4×10⁻⁶ of the OLMo-1B fine-tuning subspace; the LoRA rank-8 manifold has dimension 32,704/layer (total 2,093,056 ≫ d₉₀≈1,600). The measured Hessian ratio at SW positions (mean 2.48, max 74.50) and loss-sensitivity ratio (mean 3.39, max 72.00) on Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct confirm the higher-curvature, sharper-loss-landscape mechanism behind Theorem 1's 1/M suppression.

## Authors & Credibility

All three authors (Shreyas Subramanian, Adewale Akinfaderin, Akarsha Sehwag) are affiliated with Amazon Web Services. The work is published as a conference paper at COLM 2026 (Conference on Language Modeling), a venue whose review process provides credibility. The paper directly engages with and replicates Yu et al. (2025)'s Super Weight work, and is transparent about where its magnitude-based identification fails to generalize — an unusually honest reporting posture. Theoretical claims are backed by both proofs (Propositions 1-4, Theorem 1) and empirical validation experiments on a separate model (Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine and well-scoped. The central question — "if these parameters are so important, can we fine-tune just them?" — is a clean, falsifiable inversion of the pruning literature, and the negative result (importance ≠ trainability-in-isolation) is non-obvious and well-motivated by the Aghajanyan et al. intrinsic-dimensionality line.
- **Rigor:** Strong on controls. The random-coordinate control on the same down_proj layers is the experiment that actually isolates the cause, and the 10-seed ablation (with bit-for-bit identical-prediction counts and p-values) is exemplary for a PEFT paper. The theoretical framework is internally consistent and its predictions are mapped to specific tables.
- **Generalizability gaps (the real red flags):** (1) The magnitude-based Super Weight identification produces *zero* pruning effect on 5 of 10 models tested (Table 8) — Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Llama-3.2-3B/1B, Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct, and Gemma-2-9B-it show <0.1% PPL change — which means the central training experiments rest entirely on OLMo (2 models) using Yu et al.'s activation-spike coordinates. The authors acknowledge this but the title's framing ("in LLMs") is broader than the evidence supports. (2) The random-coordinate control that anchors the central causal claim uses a *single seed (seed 42)* at k=4,096 on OLMo-1B only — the same authors who rightly ran 10 seeds for LoRA did not multi-seed their most load-bearing control, which is an internal-consistency weakness.
- **Task narrowness:** Evaluation is essentially ARC-Easy (a comparatively easy 4-choice benchmark where the 1B baseline is already 60.65%) plus Winogrande, where *no method improves over the pretrained baseline at all* (Table 12) and the paper can only argue the relative pattern holds. Harder benchmarks (MMLU, GSM8K) are explicitly deferred to future work.
- **Theory vs. measurement gap:** Theorem 1 predicts Hessian scaling of M²≈100 (for M≈5-10), but the measured mean Hessian ratio is only 2.48 (max 74.50) — a ~40× gap on the mean. The authors attribute this to finite-difference noise, but this is the weakest link in the theoretical edifice and is acknowledged only in passing.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Models (OLMo-1B/7B) are fully open-source, hyperparameters are specified, seed ranges are disclosed, and the LoRA parameter-count math (2.1M = 0.16%) is checkable. The magnitude-identification failure on 5 models is a reproducibility landmine for anyone extending the work, but it is reported rather than hidden.
- **Significance:** Moderate-to-high for the PEFT community. The practical takeaway — "don't try to fine-tune only the important weights; use full-layer low-rank structure" — is actionable and contradicts the naive extrapolation from intrinsic-dimensionality work that "important parameters should be the best to train."

## Strengths

- The random-position control on the same module is the methodological highlight: it cleanly separates "sparsity fails" from "these specific coordinates fail," which is the crux of the paper.
- The 10-seed ablation with bit-for-bit identical-prediction counts and explicit p-values sets a standard for statistical reporting in PEFT comparisons.
- Intellectual honesty about generalization: the magnitude-based-identification failure on 5/10 models is reported front-and-center rather than buried, even though it complicates the universality narrative.
- Two independent theoretical lenses (intrinsic dimensionality + optimizer curvature dynamics) that make different, testable predictions, each mapped to specific experimental tables.
- Concise writing; the abstract, Table 6, and Section 5's two "Observations" form a self-contained argument.

## Limitations

- Training experiments are confined to OLMo-1B and OLMo-7B; extending to non-OLMo families with activation-spike identification is left to future work, so the title's "in LLMs" overstates the empirical base.
- Primary evaluation is ARC-Easy, a relatively easy benchmark; on the only secondary task (Winogrande) no method beats the pretrained baseline, so absolute-gain claims are weak.
- The load-bearing random-coordinate control uses a single seed (seed 42) at one k on one model, undermining the precision of the central causal claim relative to the rigor applied elsewhere.
- The Hessian-scaling prediction (M²≈100) is not borne out in the mean measured ratio (2.48); the theory's empirical anchor is weaker than presented.
- Only LoRA is tested among PEFT methods; adapters, prefix/prompt tuning, and DoRA are not covered, so "structured decompositions over entire layers" is not fully distinguished from "LoRA specifically."

## Relevance

直接关联高效微调(PEFT)与 LLM 可解释性研究：它厘清了"哪些参数重要"与"哪些参数能被孤立训练"是两个不同问题，并为理解 LoRA 为何有效（整层低秩协调）提供了实验与理论依据。若你关注参数重要性、稀疏训练或 LoRA 机制，这篇论文给出了一个干净的反直觉结论和可复用的对照实验设计。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the "importance ≠ trainability-in-isolation" finding hold on harder benchmarks (MMLU, GSM8K, MT-Bench) and on generative tasks where lowest-perplexity multiple-choice selection is not the eval metric?
- Would the random-coordinate control still land above baseline when multi-seeded at multiple k values and on OLMo-7B, or is the 64.18% result partly seed-luck?
- Can the gradient-amplification collapse at Super Weight coordinates be mitigated by position-specific learning-rate scaling (e.g., dividing SW updates by estimated curvature), turning "isolated SW training" from catastrophic to merely suboptimal?
- Does the magnitude-based Super Weight identification failure on 5/10 models indicate that those families genuinely lack Super Weights, or that a different identification signal (e.g., Hessian-based) would recover them?
- How do the conclusions transfer to other PEFT families (DoRA, prefix tuning, full adapters) — is "structured full-layer decomposition" the real invariant, or is it LoRA's specific rank-1 outer-product structure?
- The 1/M implicit-suppression theorem assumes diagonal second-order updates; how well does it quantitatively predict Adam's actual update ratios at Super Weight positions in practice?
