§ 01

Methodology & Results

A literature audit plus a controlled re-evaluation with one uniform scorer.

Audit meta-dataset (§4). 27 fully-supervised still-image polyp segmentation papers (2015–2026), drawn from the Mei et al. survey, Awesome-Polyp-Segmentation, PapersWithCode, and manual CVPR/MICCAI/WACV/MIDL sweeps. Inclusion requires a novel supervised architecture, results on ≥1 of the five PraNet-template datasets, and 2015–2026 publication. Each paper is coded across four dimensions: D1 metric profile, D2 data partitioning, D3 generalization scope, D4 statistical rigor.

Controlled re-evaluation (§6). Five models (PraNet, Polyp-PVT, SANet from released weights; PVT-CASCADE, G-CASCADE self-trained on the 1450-image PraNet split) scored by a single evaluator adding NSD@3px (verified to ≤10⁻¹¹ vs. official MetricsReloaded), HD95 (medpy), pixel recall, per-image scores, and a lesion-level connected-component metric.

  • P1 PraNet fixed split (1450 train) · tested on unseen CVC-ColonDB, ETIS, CVC-300
  • P2 Three random 80/20 splits (3 seeds) on Kvasir/ClinicDB
  • P3 Out-of-distribution on PolypGen's six unseen centres (n=1537)

§ 02

Strengths & Limitations

▲ Strengths

  • Quantifies the broken evaluation with a per-paper audit table and controlled re-evaluation, not rhetoric.
  • Surfaces clinically decisive failure modes (missed polyp = missed cancer; bad boundary = wrong resection margin) that Dice under-reports.
  • Transcription-error audit against primary-source PDFs — a rare, genuinely useful community service.
  • The PSRC checklist is lightweight, actionable, and domain-adapted.
  • Honest, granular threats-to-validity section separating robust from narrower findings.

▼ Limitations

  • Audit limited to still-image colonoscopic polyp segmentation only.
  • Reads only main experimental sections — described as a conservative lower bound on vulnerabilities.
  • Two of five models (PVT-CASCADE, G-CASCADE) are self-trained single-seed; ±0.005–0.007 Dice seed variance remains.
  • Absolute HD95 values are implementation-dependent (medpy vs Metrics-Reloaded differ up to ~16 px).
  • Lesion matching uses no minimum-size filter; PolypGen centres not SSIM-audited vs. training pool.
  • P2's three-seed design is modest; ranking-reversal needs more seeds to be convincing.
§ 03

Open Questions

  1. Do these findings generalize to video polyp segmentation (SUN-SEG, CVC-VideoClinicDB) and adjacent lesion tasks (skin, fundus, lung nodule) where the PraNet template also dominates?
  2. Would a larger P2 seed budget (10–20 splits) turn the near-tied PraNet↔Polyp-PVT flip into a statistically robust instability claim, or does the ranking actually stabilize?
  3. Can the HD95 library-disagreement (medpy vs Metrics-Reloaded, ~16 px) be reconciled into a single standard — and how much published boundary “progress” dissolves once the convention is fixed?
  4. Does enforcing PSRC retroactively change the perceived SOTA ordering — i.e. which currently-celebrated models survive once HD95, Recall, significance, and ≥1 OOD set are required?
  5. How sensitive are lesion-F1 rankings to a clinically motivated ≥0.1% area filter, and does PVT-CASCADE's lead persist?
