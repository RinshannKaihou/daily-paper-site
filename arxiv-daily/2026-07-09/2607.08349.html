03 Why this matters

Unifies scattered interventional metrics — IIA, activation patching, circuit faithfulness — into causal estimands with anytime-valid uncertainty, fixing point estimates that get quietly contaminated by adaptive peeking.

04 Critical edge

The headline 10–30× speedup is measured only at p=0.1 where fidelities are high — at p=0.5 no non-identity abstraction certifies at all, so the speedup is moot there.

i. Methodology an inference layer around any interventional score

CIF reframes both classes of interventional interpretability claim — (I) abstraction/reduction fidelity (causal abstraction, IIA, structured pruning, causal scrubbing) and (II) component-effect/recovery (activation/path patching, causal tracing, circuit ablation) — as one bounded causal estimand θ = E(X,I)∼D×Π[Y(X,I)], Y∈[0,1].

Three design choices carry the framework: (1) declare the estimand explicitly — input distribution D, intervention distribution Π, bounded score — separating the scientific question from the sampler; (2) report anytime-valid confidence sequences that survive arbitrary, data-dependent stopping (P(∀n: θ∈Cn)≥1−δ); (3) allow adaptive, failure-directed sampling without changing the target, via bounded mixture importance weighting qt=(1−α)Π+αq̃t, giving weights ≤ 1/(1−α) and an unbiased Horvitz–Thompson estimator.

Two interchangeable layers: a transparent Hoeffding-style CS (radius depends only on n and range) and a variance-adaptive betting CS built from an Online-Newton-Step capital process — the source of the 10–30× gains, since it tightens when empirical variance is low. The suite adds fixed-budget CIs, early-stopping sequences, adaptive sampling, and paired comparison with Bonferroni / peeling / e-value FDR control.

  • Estimand: F = 1 − E[d] for fidelity; μ = E[Δ] for recovery — both bounded means.
  • Coverage checked over 500 runs: Hoeffding 1.000, betting 0.930–1.000, always ≥ nominal.
  • Public code & reproducible notebooks: github.com/AsiaeeLab/certified-interventional-fidelity.

iii. Strengths & Limitations side by side

+ Strengths

  • Modular reformulation unifying two large, separately-evaluated claim families under one estimand (Table 4 covers ~a dozen method families).
  • Brutally honest about negatives: reports that no abstraction certifies at p=0.5, and that adaptive sampling helps failure-finding but hurts certification.
  • Practically useful: order-of-magnitude cost cuts, a standardized reporting checklist (Appendix D), public code.
  • Statistically careful — anytime-validity, 500-run coverage validation, explicit handling of selection and multiplicity.

! Limitations

  • Scale capped at a 3-layer MNIST MLP and GPT-2 Small (~124M); no frontier-scale demo despite forward-pass cost being the stated motivation.
  • Simplest guarantees need bounded scores; unbounded metrics (raw logits, KL) need clipping that is discussed, not empirically validated for distortion.
  • Addresses estimation, not identification — whether the chosen abstraction captures true causal structure is out of scope.
  • Adaptive sampling is not a uniform win (doubles certification cost in the high-fidelity regime); direction-dependence lacks a formal explanation.
  • Multiplicity relies on conservative Bonferroni; e-value FDR is left as future work. Single author; breadth constrained.

iv. Questions for further exploration

  1. Does the betting-CS advantage (10–30×) survive at frontier scale (Llama-class IOI / factual-recall circuits), where per-intervention cost is extreme and variance structure may differ from GPT-2 Small?
  2. How do CIF's anytime-valid intervals compare empirically against Shi et al. [2024]'s circuit-hypothesis tests and Gross et al. [2024]'s compact-proof verification — under what regimes is sequential inference preferable to fixed tests or formal verification?
  3. Can the adaptive mixture layer's direction-dependence (helps failure-finding, hurts certification) be given a formal characterization — is there a proposal schedule uniformly no worse than i.i.d.?
  4. Does clipping unbounded metrics (raw logit diff, KL) to [0,1] distort certified conclusions on real LM tasks? How do sub-Gaussian / asymptotic CS extensions compare in practice?
  5. Integrating e-value FDR control (Wang & Ramdas 2022) for hierarchical circuit scans — how much power is recovered over Bonferroni at realistic scan widths?
  6. Can CIF be embedded inside ACDC / AtP* as an active controller that prunes low-fidelity candidates mid-search, turning certification from a post-hoc check into a search component?
