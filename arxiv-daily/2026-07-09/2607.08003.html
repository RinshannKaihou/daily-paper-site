Constraining a frontier LLM to reason over an explicit reaction network yields prospectively validated catalyst levers — and a threefold acetate-selectivity gain.

Reaction-network reasoning with frontier models for experimentally confirmed catalyst-selectivity hypotheses

Sutanay Choudhury et al. · PNNL · IIT Bombay · Oregon State · TU Munich · 09 Jul 2026 · framework: CoThinker (GPT-5.4)

Key metric
≈3×

acetate selectivity (Faradaic efficiency) — 1:1 Cu–Fe oxide vs. matched Cu-rich 4:1 baseline, at otherwise comparable overall conversion.

Hypotheses fixed before synthesis · 1:1 peak of the 4:1→3:1→2:1→1:1 series
Why this matters

A prospectively-validated, graph-structured constraint that tames LLM drift — a transferable design pattern for any domain expressible as a state–transition network.

▲ Critical edge

The headline threefold gain is measured against a Cu-rich baseline that “predominantly produced formate” — intrinsically poor at acetate, which inflates the apparent improvement. Absolute Faradaic efficiencies sit only in the supplement.

Methodology

CoThinker — four sequential stages

01 · FORMULATE A human expert states the catalytic objective (CO₂-to-acetate on Cu–Fe); the system recasts it as a mechanistic optimization problem.

02 · BUILD GRAPH Literature-derived intermediates and elementary steps (PCET, adsorption, surface coupling, hydrolysis) form a directed graph, auto-expanded by inserting missing branches — 32 nodes, 41 edges.

03 · REASON + REFINE GPT-5.4 navigates the network, ranks mechanistic hotspots by influence on product-flux distribution, then runs a reflect–verify–retry loop checking network-wide consistency, pathway completeness, branch compatibility and feasibility — rejecting designs (e.g. subsurface Fe) that ease a local barrier without resolving the upstream CO*–CHO* bottleneck.

04 · VERIFY The surviving architecture is decomposed into independent, falsifiable control levers. Deliberately no ab-initio / NNP energies during reasoning — the authors argue NNPs suffer severe out-of-distribution errors at electrochemical interfaces and are trained on ground states, not the transition-state kinetics that govern selectivity.

Strengths

What lands well

  • Prospective, hypothesis-locked design — predictions fixed before synthesis — the gold standard for “AI-discovered material” claims.
  • Network invariance is a principled, transferable answer to the documented problem of LLM contextual drift in multi-turn reasoning.
  • Decomposition into independent, falsifiable control levers is a genuinely useful experimental-planning artifact.
  • Dual-domain validation: UMA computation (ketene binding) triangulates the electrochemistry (pH / Fe / electrolyte).
  • Honest framing of NNP limits at electrochemical interfaces, grounded in the authors’ own uncertainty-quantification work.

Limitations

Where it is thin

  • Single-system validation (CO₂-to-acetate on Cu–Fe oxide) — the “broadly applicable blueprint” claim is asserted, not demonstrated.
  • Most novel prediction (a CO*–CH₂* route to ketene vs. CO-dimerization) is offered with no experimental differentiation.
  • Absolute Faradaic efficiencies and partial current densities are absent from the main text; “threefold” is a ratio vs. a weak baseline.
  • LLM component is non-reproducible: proprietary GPT-5.4, no released prompts or logs beyond a stylized figure, stochastic outputs.
  • No ablations vs. unconstrained LLM, ChemReasoner, network-size variants, or a human-expert control — marginal value unquantified.
  • Computational chemistry is omitted in-loop, so the framework cannot self-check the energetic feasibility of its own hypotheses.

Open questions for further work

What the paper leaves on the table

  1. Does network invariance generalize beyond Cu–Fe — e.g. N₂ reduction or CO₂-to-ethylene with a different ground-truth mechanism?
  2. What is the quantitative contribution of each component (network constraint, reflect–verify–retry, hotspot ranking) vs. an unconstrained GPT-5.4 and vs. ChemReasoner?
  3. Can the CO*–CH₂* ketene route be distinguished from CO-dimerization by isotope labeling or operando spectroscopy — and does it carry measurable flux?
  4. How sensitive are the top-ranked levers to network completeness (e.g. omitting the buffer-assisted protonation branch)?
  5. What absolute acetate FE / partial current density does the 1:1 catalyst reach vs. dendrimer-functionalized Cu (ref 23) on equal footing?
  6. Would an NNP active-learning loop with calibrated uncertainty improve hypothesis quality, or does error-compounding dominate?
