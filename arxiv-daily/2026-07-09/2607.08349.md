> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Certified Interventional Fidelity (CIF)——一个可叠加在现有干预式可解释性评估（IIA、activation patching、circuit completeness 等）之上的统计层：它把所报告的分数显式写成"在给定输入分布 D 与干预分布 Π 上的有界分数期望"这一因果被估量，再用 Hoeffding 式与 variance-adaptive betting 式的 anytime-valid 置信序列给出有限样本、可在任意时刻停止/反复监测仍有效的区间，并通过 bounded mixture importance sampling 支持自适应的"找反例"采样而不改变被估量；在 MNIST 抽象与 GPT-2 Small IOI 电路上，betting 序列将认证成本相比 Hoeffding 降低 10–30 倍，并暴露出在 p=0.5 的强 swap 下没有任何非恒等抽象能通过 F≥0.90 这一被点估计掩盖的事实。

> **TL;DR:** Mechanistic-interpretability evaluations (interchange intervention accuracy, activation/path patching, circuit completeness) are usually reported as a single point estimate even though evaluators monitor and adapt them while running. CIF reframes each such score as a causal estimand — the expectation of a bounded score over a declared input distribution D and intervention distribution Π — and wraps it with finite-sample, anytime-valid confidence sequences (Hoeffding-style and variance-adaptive betting via the ONS strategy), plus bounded mixture importance sampling so adaptive failure-directed sampling stays valid. On MNIST abstractions and GPT-2 Small IOI circuits, betting sequences cut certification cost by 10–30× vs Hoeffding (e.g., the full 13-head IOI circuit certifies µ≥0.90 in n=102 vs n=1,875 forward passes), reveal that at swap probability p=0.5 NO non-identity abstraction reaches F≥0.90, and make sensitivity of conclusions to the choice of Π and discrepancy metric visible rather than hidden behind point estimates.

## 核心贡献

- **核心再表述**：把两大类机制可解释性评估——(I) 抽象/归约保真度（causal abstraction、IIA、结构化剪枝、affine 机制替换、causal scrubbing）与 (II) 组件效应/恢复（activation patching、path patching、causal tracing、circuit ablation）——统一写成"采样一个输入与一个干预，计算一个有界分数，估计其总体均值"的同一统计形式，从而可以对任意现有干预式指标附加不确定性保证，而非为每个方法单独造统计量。
- **三种设计选择**：(1) 显式声明被估量（输入分布 D + 干预分布 Π + 有界分数），把"科学问题"与"采样过程"分离；(2) 用 anytime-valid 置信序列报告不确定性，使其在反复监测、数据相关停止下仍有效（Theorem 1/2）；(3) 通过 bounded mixture importance weighting（qt=(1−α)Π+αq̃t，权重 wt≤1/(1−α)）允许自适应的失败导向采样而不改变目标被估量。
- **两种可互换的 CS 实现**：透明的 Hoeffding 式序列（半径仅依赖 n 与范围）与 variance-adaptive 的 betting 序列（Online Newton Step，随经验方差自适应），后者在实验中将认证成本降低 10–30 倍。
- **完整的算法套件**：固定预算置信区间（P1）、可提前停止的置信序列（P2）、自适应失败采样（P3）、两解释配对比较与多重比较校正（Bonferroni / peeling / e-value FDR，P4），并给出 Algorithm 1/2 与一份可操作的报告清单（Appendix D）。
- **覆盖性验证**：在 500 次独立运行中，Hoeffding CS 在所有配置下经验覆盖均为 1.000，betting CS 为 0.930–1.000 且始终不低于名义水平，同时浪费更少的误差预算。

## Key Contributions

- **Central reformulation.** Both broad classes of interventional interpretability evaluation — (I) abstraction/reduction fidelity claims (causal abstraction, IIA, structured pruning, affine mechanism replacement, neural compression, causal scrubbing) and (II) component-effect/recovery claims (activation patching, path patching, causal tracing, circuit ablation) — are written as a common bounded causal estimand θ = E_{(X,I)∼D×Π}[Y(X,I)], Y∈[0,1]. This lets CIF attach uncertainty guarantees to the population claim rather than a method-specific point estimate.
- **Three design choices.** (1) Make the target explicit by declaring D, Π, and the bounded score, cleanly separating the scientific question from the sampling procedure; (2) report anytime-valid confidence sequences that survive arbitrary stopping and repeated monitoring (P(∀n≥1: θ∈C_n)≥1−δ); (3) permit adaptive, failure-directed sampling without changing the estimand via bounded mixture importance weighting qt=(1−α)Π+αq̃t, giving wt≤1/(1−α)=W_max and an unbiased Horvitz–Thompson estimator (Lemma 1).
- **Two interchangeable CS layers.** A transparent Hoeffding-style sequence (Theorem 1, radius depends only on n and range) and a variance-adaptive betting sequence built from an Online Newton Step capital process (Section 3.4, Theorem via Ville's inequality), with a dedicated importance-weighted variant (Theorem 2). Betting adapts to empirical variance and reduces certification cost by 10–30× in the experiments.
- **Full algorithmic suite.** Fixed-budget CIs (P1), sequential confidence sequences with early stopping (P2), adaptive failure-directed sampling (P3), and paired two-explanation comparison plus multiplicity control — Bonferroni δ/K, sequential peeling, or e-value FDR (P4) — with Algorithms 1–2 and a practical reporting checklist (Appendix D).
- **Coverage validation.** Across 500 independent runs under i.i.d., adaptive, and aggressive-peeking protocols, Hoeffding CSs achieve empirical coverage 1.000 in every configuration while betting CSs range 0.930–1.000, always at or above nominal level with less wasted error budget.

## Methodology

CIF is an inference layer placed around an existing interventional interpretability workflow. It is organized to mirror what an evaluator already does: choose the population of inputs and interventions the claim is about, run the interventions, and report what can be concluded with uncertainty.

**Estimand formulation (Section 2).** A trained network is viewed as a deterministic structural causal model M=(U,V,F). An intervention (hard zeroing, soft affine replacement, activation patching) changes one or more structural equations; State(M,x,i) is the realized assignment after running M on x under i, deterministic until X and I are sampled. For abstraction/reduction fidelity, define a bounded discrepancy Z = d(Outcome(M_L,X,ω(I)), Outcome(M_H,X,I))∈[0,1], interventional risk R=E[Z], and fidelity F=1−R. For component-effect/recovery (e.g., activation patching), define clipped recovery Δ = clip_{[0,1]}[(g_patch−g_corr)/(g_clean−g_corr)]∈[0,1] and certify µ=E[Δ]. Both reduce to certifying a bounded mean θ over D×Π.

**Fixed-budget confidence intervals (Section 3.1).** With n fixed in advance, Hoeffding gives |θ̂−θ| ≤ √(log(2/δ)/(2n)); to achieve half-width ε requires n ≥ log(2/δ)/(2ε²). This does not justify repeated peeking.

**Anytime-valid confidence sequences (Section 3.2).** Using the spending schedule δ_n = 6δ/(π²n²) (which sums to δ), the Hoeffding CS θ ∈ [θ̂_n ± √(log(2/δ_n)/(2n))] holds simultaneously for all n≥1, so any data-dependent stopping time is valid. One-sided certification stops when the LCB (for larger-is-better) or UCB (for smaller-is-better) crosses the target.

**Adaptive failure-directed sampling (Section 3.3).** At time t an adaptive proposal q_t(·|H_{t−1}) (e.g., AtP*, gradients, or past failures) is mixed with the target: q_t=(1−α)Π+αq̃_t, bounding weights at W_max=1/(1−α). The Horvitz–Thompson estimator θ̂_IS = (1/n)Σ w_t Y_t is unbiased (Lemma 1, tower-property proof) and (w_t Y_t − θ) is a martingale, so Hoeffding–Azuma yields an anytime-valid CS with radius scaled by W_max (Theorem 2). The mixture rate α trades failure-finding speed against worst-case bound width; it affects efficiency, not the estimand.

**Variance-adaptive betting CS (Section 3.4).** Following Waudby-Smith & Ramdas (2024), a capital process K_n(m)=∏(1+λ_t(B_t−m)) rejects candidate means m with K_n(m)≥1/δ by Ville's inequality. The Online Newton Step bet λ_{n+1}=clip_{[−c,c]}(λ_n+ηg_n/A_n) adapts to empirical variance. For importance-weighted observations, run the betting CS on B_t=w_t Y_t/W_max∈[0,1] and scale back; when Var(w_t Y_t) is small the betting CS is much tighter than Hoeffding, neutralizing the W_max penalty.

**Comparing and selecting (Sections 3.5–3.6).** Paired differences D_t=Y_t^(1)−Y_t^(2)∈[−1,1] on shared (X_t,I_t) have lower variance and admit the same CS machinery. When scanning K candidates, uncorrected intervals are optimistic; Bonferroni δ/K, sequential peeling, or e-value FDR control are recommended. Proofs are in Appendix C; a reporting checklist is in Appendix D.

## Results & Highlights

- **Betting cuts certification cost by 10–30× across the board.** On MNIST at swap probability p=0.1, variance-based pruning (k=256) certifies F≥0.90 in n=64 forward passes with the betting CS versus n=1,141 with Hoeffding (17.8× speedup); soft interventions (k=256) certify F≥0.90 in n=93 vs n=1,219 (13.1×) and F≥0.95 in n=342 vs n=9,849 (28.8×) (Table 2). On GPT-2 Small IOI, the full 13-head circuit certifies µ≥0.90 in n=102 vs n=1,875 and µ≥0.95 in n=357 vs >2,000 (Table 3) — making CIF-based certification practical even where each intervention is a full GPT-2 forward pass.
- **Point estimates can be near-identical while inferential conclusions differ sharply.** All evaluated IOI circuits (3–13 heads, from ACDC, attribution patching, AtP*, and the hand-identified Wang et al. circuit) have recovery estimates µ̂∈[0.959,0.972], yet at n=2,000 the Hoeffding radius is ≈0.070 while the betting radius is ≈0.005 — so only betting yields LCBs above 0.95 (Figure 3, Figure 4, Table 3).
- **CIF exposes a masked negative result: at p=0.5 no non-identity abstraction is high-fidelity.** On MNIST under aggressive coordinate swaps (p=0.5), the best estimated fidelity is only F̂=0.105 for hard pruning, 0.559 for soft interventions, 0.689 for variance-based, and 0.521 for random pruning (Table 1); none certifies F≥0.90. Point estimates would still rank methods, but CIF shows none supports a high-fidelity claim at this threshold.
- **Apparent method differences can be statistically unsupported.** A paired comparison of soft interventions vs random pruning at k=256, p=0.5 gives estimated difference −0.033, but the Hoeffding radius is 0.067 (interval contains 0) while the betting radius is 0.016 (interval excludes 0) — the choice of interval determines whether the difference is even detectable.
- **Sensitivity to the intervention distribution and discrepancy metric becomes visible.** At mild perturbation (p≤0.2) three metrics (0–1 loss, clipped KL, clipped L2) yield overlapping CSs with F̂∈[0.94,0.99] and width 0.067, so metric choice has no statistically detectable effect; at p=0.5 they separate (0–1 loss F̂=0.674 vs clipped KL F̂=0.578, non-overlapping gap ≈0.03), showing that at aggressive perturbation the choice of discrepancy function can change qualitative conclusions (Figure 5).
- **Coverage validated empirically.** Over 500 runs per configuration, Hoeffding CSs achieve coverage 1.000 everywhere; betting CSs achieve 0.930–1.000, always at or above nominal 1−δ while leaving less unused error budget, including under an aggressive peeking protocol checked every 10 samples (Table 7).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High and well-scoped. The genuinely new contribution is not the confidence-sequence machinery itself (Howard et al. 2021; Waudby-Smith & Ramdas 2024 are imported directly) but the *reformulation* that turns heterogeneous interventional metrics — IIA, patching recovery, circuit completeness, causal scrubbing — into a single bounded causal estimand and supplies a unified anytime-valid layer, including an adaptive variant under bounded mixture importance sampling. The synthesis of causal-abstraction estimands with off-policy sequential inference is non-obvious.
- **Rigor**: Strong. Proofs are complete and short; the unbiasedness lemma and the adaptive CS theorem are correctly derived. The experimental design is honest: it reports *negative* certification results (Table 1, p=0.5) and explicitly notes that "adaptive sampling is not uniformly faster for certification" — the adaptive mixture roughly doubles the sample count for high-fidelity certification (131 vs 64 under i.i.d. betting), while helping failure-finding (Appendix F). This direction-dependence is admitted to lack a formal explanation.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Public code, a reporting checklist, full proofs, and 500-run coverage validation. However, some experimental details (seeds, the exact history-updated failure proposal q̃_t(j)∝(s_j+1)/(n_j+2)) live only in appendices, and the aggressive-peeking protocol is described only at a high level in the main text.
- **Significance**: Practically important for the interpretability community, which routinely reports patching/IIA numbers as bare scalars. The 10–30× cost reduction from betting sequences is the kind of result that could change evaluation norms, and the framework's modularity (it is a layer around any bounded interventional score, per Table 4) lowers adoption friction.
- **Red flags / overclaiming**: (1) **Scale is limited** — experiments cover only a 3-layer MNIST MLP and GPT-2 Small (~124M params); there is no demonstration on frontier-scale models where forward-pass cost is the stated motivation. (2) **The headline "10–30×" speedup is somewhat cherry-picked** toward the favorable betting regime: it is measured at p=0.1 where fidelities are high and variance is low; at p=0.5 no abstraction certifies at all, so the speedup is moot there. The paper does disclose this, but the abstract leads with the 10–30× figure without that qualification. (3) **Bonferroni for component scanning is acknowledged lossy** — the paper flags e-value FDR (Wang & Ramdas 2022) as the "immediate next step" but does not implement it, so the multiple-comparisons piece (P4) is the weakest-delivered of the four tasks. (4) **The boundedness/clipping assumption** is a real limitation for metrics like raw logit differences; the paper argues clipping is "often acceptable" but offers no experiment validating that clipping does not distort conclusions on a real metric.
- **A missing ablation**: there is no head-to-head against Shi et al. [2024]'s formal hypothesis tests for circuit claims or against Gross et al. [2024]'s compact-proof verification approach, so it is unclear when CIF's sequential anytime-valid guarantees are preferable to these concurrent alternatives beyond the adaptivity argument.

## Strengths

- Clean, modular reformulation that unifies two large and previously separately-evaluated classes of interpretability claim under one estimand, with a mapping table (Table 4) covering a dozen method families.
- Honest about negative results and asymmetries: reports that no abstraction certifies at p=0.5, and that adaptive sampling helps failure-finding but hurts certification.
- Practical: betting sequences deliver order-of-magnitude cost reductions, a reporting checklist standardizes disclosure, and code is public.
- Statistically careful: anytime-validity, coverage validation over 500 runs, and explicit handling of selection/multiplicity.

## Limitations

- Experiments are limited to a small MNIST MLP and GPT-2 Small; no frontier-scale demonstration despite forward-pass cost being the motivation.
- The simplest guarantees require bounded scores; unbounded metrics (raw logits, KL) need clipping or sub-Gaussian/sub-exponential extensions that are only discussed, not empirically validated for distortion.
- CIF addresses the *estimation* problem (reliably estimating a causal estimand from finite data), explicitly not the *identification* problem (whether the chosen abstraction captures the true causal structure) nor whether the chosen metric captures mechanistic truth.
- Adaptive sampling is not a uniform win: it doubles certification cost in the high-fidelity regime; the direction-dependence is observed but not formally explained.
- The multiple-comparisons component relies on conservative Bonferroni; the more powerful e-value FDR integration is left as future work.
- Single-author paper; the empirical breadth (two model families, no language-model tasks beyond IOI) is correspondingly constrained.

## Relevance

对关注机制可解释性评估可靠性、因果抽象与电路分析的读者高度相关：它把散落的干预式指标（IIA、activation patching、circuit faithfulness）统一为可附带 anytime-valid 不确定性的因果被估量，直接回应了"点估计不可靠、评估被自适应污染"这一社区痛点，并为自动化电路发现（ACDC、AtP*、attribution patching）提供了可在搜索中早期剪枝低保真候选的验证层。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does CIF's betting-CS advantage (10–30×) survive at frontier scale (e.g., Llama-class IOI/factual-recall circuits), where per-intervention cost is extreme and variance structure may differ from GPT-2 Small?
- How do CIF's anytime-valid intervals compare empirically against Shi et al. [2024]'s circuit-hypothesis tests and Gross et al. [2024]'s compact-proof verification — under what regimes is sequential inference preferable to fixed hypothesis tests or formal verification?
- Can the adaptive mixture-importance-sampling layer's direction-dependence (helps failure-finding, hurts certification) be given a formal characterization, and is there a proposal schedule that is uniformly no worse than i.i.d.?
- Does clipping unbounded metrics (raw logit difference, KL) to [0,1] distort certified conclusions on real language-model tasks, and how do sub-Gaussian/asymptotic CS extensions compare in practice?
- Integrating e-value FDR control (Wang & Ramdas 2022) for hierarchical/per-component circuit scans — how much power is recovered over Bonferroni at realistic scan widths?
- Can CIF be embedded inside automated circuit-discovery loops (ACDC, AtP*) as an active controller that prunes low-fidelity candidates and reallocates budget, turning certification from a post-hoc check into a search component?
