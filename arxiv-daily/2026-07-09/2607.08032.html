Why this matters

Unifies KV-cache, prompt, and sparse-attention efficiency work with agent-memory consolidation under one rate–distortion lens — directly actionable for efficient long-context and agent design, plus the missing COMPACT-Bench budget axis.

Critical edge

The I★(Q) quantity the whole framework hinges on is conceded unmeasured for real tasks — so the theory's predictive power currently outruns its operationalization.

Methodology

§ 2 · 4–11 · 12 · 13–14

This is a position/survey, not a trained model — the method is a unifying analytical lens. A compaction scheme is a pair (Cθ, U): operator Cθ: H→Z producing a budget-constrained rate(Z) ≤ B, used by U. The design problem is the rate–distortion / information-bottleneck program:

min E[ℓ(U(Cθ(H),Q),Y)] s.t. rate(Z)≤B  ≡  max I(Z;Y|Q) s.t. I(Z;H)≤B

  1. Formalize (§2). Derive a layer-agnostic lower bound Pe ≥ (H(Y|Q)−B−1)/log|Y| from data-processing + Fano — every layer errs identically below the task information I★(Q).
  2. Survey through the lens (§4–11). Re-read KV, prompt, architectural, agent/semantic, sparse-attention, multimodal, and serving layers as competing distortion surrogates.
  3. Collect theory (§12). Haris–Onak Θ(nd) bound, fixed-state recall impossibility, attention-sink necessity, U-shaped recall — as scattered I★(Q) estimators.
  4. Benchmark + reference experiment (§13–14). COMPACT-Bench puts every method on bytes-per-token-of-history; runs Qwen2.5-1.5B on one RTX 4060.

Strengths

  • Truly unifying formalism with a real (if standard) lower bound — KV evictors, prompt pruners, SSM states, and agent summaries become commensurable on one bytes-per-token axis for the first time.
  • Broad, current coverage reaching the serving substrate (PagedAttention, RadixAttention, DistServe, Mooncake, ShadowKV) and trainable sparse frontier (NSA, MoBA, DSA) that KV-only or agent-only surveys omit.
  • The five design principles (P1–P5) and the §10 bridge table are actionable, with predicted failure modes — not just description.
  • Benchmark critique is sharp and correct: the missing axes (multi-budget, cross-layer, repeated-compaction) are precisely what the formalism demands.
  • Intellectual honesty about preprint provenance and the I★(Q) estimation gap.

Limitations

  • It is a survey — no large-scale validation; the reference experiment is one small open model on one commodity GPU, explicitly "not a leaderboard."
  • The lower bound assumes a query-agnostic operator for its cleanest form and treats I★(Q) as given — and estimating I★(Q) for real tasks is itself open and unresolved.
  • The "operator algebra" for stacking quant × low-rank × evict is sketched, not proven; surrogates are non-additive, order matters.
  • Many cited 2025–2026 results (DSA, MEM1, ReSum, IterResearch) are unrefereed preprints, not independently reproduced.
  • The four "falsifiable predictions" are largely retrospective; the super-linear error-growth claim is not cleanly demonstrated by the reference curve.

Open questions for further work

Distilled from the paper's own agenda

Can I★(Q) be estimated cheaply enough at compaction time to make the budget prescription operational — and which surrogate (MI, output-error bound, LLM salience) tracks it best across layers?

Does the reversible-vs-irreversible recall gap (~0.5) widen, narrow, or change shape at frontier-model scale and horizons beyond 25 compaction events?

Is the claimed super-linear error growth under repeated irreversible summarization real, or does it saturate? The reference curve shows only a flat-vs-depressed separation.

How should the objective extend to shared multimodal memory across collaborating agents, where Eq. (2) is stated for a single Z answering a single Q?

Can an honest operator algebra predict which stacking pairs (quant × low-rank × evict; KV × prompt × agent) are synergistic, redundant, or destructive?

Does lossless prefix reuse (RadixAttention) compose safely with lossy budgeting (ShadowKV) — or does one lossy decision silently degrade every descendant request?
