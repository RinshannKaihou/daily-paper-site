> **一句话总结：** 本文将 LLM 拒答问题分解为两个独立的轴——"答案是否正确"与"问题是否可答"，证明单一置信度阈值只能读出前者（执行成功与否），而后者需要隐藏状态探针（0.97–0.99 AUROC vs 输出端 0.54–0.67），并提出双风险预算的"因子化拒答"策略（在 8B 上以 0.75 覆盖率同时认证两个误差预算，是 14B 上唯一能认证的策略）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper shows LLM abstention conflates two distinct axes — answer correctness and question answerability — that separate on the same decisions but require different signals: ordinary answer-confidence orders correct-answerable (C) above wrong-answerable (W)/unanswerable (U) but barely separates W from U, while a hidden-state answerability probe reaches 0.97–0.99 AUROC on SelfAware (vs 0.54–0.67 for trained output readouts) and survives onto the naturally-occurring false-presupposition benchmark CREPE (hidden 0.69–0.78 vs output at/near chance 0.50–0.58). A "Factorized Abstention" policy that gates on both axes certifies both a wrong-answer budget (αW=0.50) and an unanswerable-answer budget (αU=0.15) at 0.75 C-coverage on Llama-3.1-8B (vs 0.31 for confidence-only conformal abstention) and is the only policy that certifies at all on Qwen2.5-14B.

## 核心贡献

- **同一决策上的双轴几何分解**：在 SelfAware 上为每个问题标注三种状态（正确可答 C、错误可答 W、不可答 U），揭示出"L 形"几何结构——答案置信度分离 C 与 W/U（C−W 间距 0.82–1.03），却几乎不分 W 与 U（间距 −0.32 至 0.14，含零）；可答性探针则相反。该模式在 2B–14B、三个模型族（Gemma/Qwen/Llama）上一致复现。
- **诚实的控制实验，界定可答性信号的来源**：SelfAware 上可答性信号大部分可被表面词袋恢复（AUROC 0.87），且 P(IK) 引出在某些模型上能达 0.88；但在 CREPE（真实用户问题、同一分布、消除表面混淆）上，训练后的输出读数退化为随机水平（0.50–0.58），而隐藏读数仍达 0.69–0.78，证明内部信号并非纯表面产物。
- **行为后果与修复**：指令模型"检查前提"会使其无差别地质疑——在 CREPE 上质疑 74% 的假前提却也质疑 57% 的健全问题；而用隐藏探针门控同一提示，在 35% 路由预算下以 14% 误质疑率捕获 42% 假前提，挑战精度约提升 3 倍。
- **三分类选择性接受的形式化与双风险认证**：将拒答形式化为带两个独立误差预算（αU=0.15、αW=0.50、δ=0.10）的三分类问题，用 Clopper–Pearson 精确二项界在独立认证 split 上做保证。因子化策略在 8B 上以 0.75 覆盖率同时认证两预算（置信度单阈值仅 0.31），在 14B 上是唯一能认证的策略；并通过 100 次 seed 重复实验审计证书的稳健性（签发 18–68/100，违反率 0–5.6%，符合 δ 容差）。

## Key Contributions

- **Same-decision two-axis decomposition (C/W/U geometry).** Each question is labeled correct-answerable (C), wrong-answerable (W), or unanswerable (U), revealing an "L-shape": answer-confidence separates C from W/U (C−W gap 0.82–1.03) but barely separates W from U (gap −0.32 to 0.14, intervals crossing zero), while the answerability readout does the opposite. This replicates across Gemma 2 2B-it, Qwen2.5-3B/7B/14B, and Llama-3.1-8B.
- **Honest controls bounding where the answerability signal lives.** The authors do not overclaim internal legibility: on SelfAware a hashed bag-of-words logistic reaches 0.87 AUROC and P(IK) elicitation reaches up to 0.88 (model-dependent). The defensible novelty is restricted to ordinary answer-likelihood (0.54–0.67). On CREPE, where both classes are real user questions from one distribution, every output-side signal collapses to or near chance (0.50–0.58) while hidden readouts hold at 0.69–0.78.
- **Behavioral consequence and a probe-routed repair.** A pre-registered pilot shows instructed premise-checking fails because the model contests indiscriminately (74% false-premise but 57% sound questions contested). Routing the challenge prompt only when the out-of-sample hidden probe says "unanswerable" roughly triples challenge precision (42% false-premise contested at 14% false-challenge, vs 74% at 57% for always-challenge).
- **Formal abstention as three-class selective acceptance with dual-risk certification.** Abstention is formalized with separate budgets UCB(RU)≤αU and UCB(RW)≤αW, certified with exact Clopper–Pearson bounds (Bonferroni 1−δ=0.90) on a split independent of score training and threshold tuning. The "Factorized Abstention" policy (answer iff sA≥τA and sC≥τC) exposes a scale-dependent asymmetry: the unanswerable budget certifies on four of five models, while the wrong-answer budget is bounded by model accuracy and only becomes certifiable at useful coverage as scale grows.

## Methodology

**Models and benchmarks.** Five instruction-tuned models from three families at 2B–14B: Gemma 2 2B-it (full precision), Qwen2.5-3B (full precision), Qwen2.5-7B (8-bit), Llama-3.1-8B (8-bit), Qwen2.5-14B (4-bit). Quantization is validated against full precision on Qwen2.5-3B (identical accuracy 0.300; H→A 0.972 vs 0.973). Two benchmarks: **SelfAware** (Yin et al., 2023) — balanced 150 answerable + 150 unanswerable per model (120 each for 3B); and **CREPE** (Yu et al., 2023) — 500 normal + 500 false-presupposition questions drawn from the official test split, both classes real Reddit ELI5 user questions from the same source/time period (the surface-confound-free test).

**Scoring protocol.** Single user turn ("Answer the question in a few words. Question: {q}"), greedy decoding (≤24 tokens). From the same forward pass the authors record (i) output answer-confidence (mean token log-prob of the greedy answer + first-token predictive entropy) and (ii) final prompt-token hidden states at three fractional depths (0.4/0.6/0.8). Correctness judged by alias matching against gold aliases.

**Symmetric readouts.** To avoid comparing a trained probe against a raw scalar, equal-capacity L2-regularized logistic readouts are trained for all four source-by-axis cells (Output/Hidden × Correctness/Answerability) on shared calibration/test partitions, with the hidden layer chosen on a nested calibration split. Bootstrap 95% CIs over test items.

**Factorized Abstention and certification.** The policy emits iff sA≥τA and sC≥τC (the high-answerability, high-confidence corner). Thresholds are chosen on a tuning split (maximize tune-split C-coverage subject to tune feasibility), frozen, then certified on an independent certification split using exact one-sided Clopper–Pearson upper bounds at δ/2 per risk (simultaneous 1−δ=0.90 by Bonferroni); a held-out test split gives empirical rates. Scores trained on the tuning split only, so certification/test items are out-of-sample for both scores and thresholds. Baselines certified by the identical procedure: answer-confidence-only (≈ conformal abstention), answerability-only, and a learned logistic combiner (Phillips et al. 2026-style fusion).

**Replication audit.** The whole pipeline (split → probe train → tune → certify → test) is re-run over 100 random seeds per model to check whether issued certificates are violated on held-out test at the declared δ rate.

## Results & Highlights

- **Answerability is internally legible; ordinary answer-confidence is not.** On SelfAware, Hidden→answerability AUROC is 0.97–0.99 across all five models (e.g., Llama-3.1-8B: 0.99 [0.97, 1.00]), whereas trained Output→answerability readouts stay at 0.54–0.67 with no scale trend (Qwen2.5-14B: 0.54 [0.46, 0.64]) — a gap of roughly 0.3–0.45 AUROC that does not close at 14B.
- **The C/W/U collapse is not an artifact of the probe's labels.** Training the answerability direction on C-vs-U only (no W items in training) still places held-out W items with C, not U: W-vs-U AUROC 0.97–0.98, with W sitting 0.71–0.85 of the way from the U mean to the C mean (Table 8).
- **On natural false presuppositions (CREPE), the output blind spot sharpens.** Trained output readouts detect false premises at or near chance (AUROC 0.50–0.58; raw confidence 0.49–0.58), while hidden readouts reach 0.69–0.73 (trained logistic) and 0.74–0.78 (difference-of-means). Every elicitation fails to match the internal signal: P(IK) 0.54–0.66, P(True) 0.54–0.58, premise-check 0.43–0.67 (below chance, 0.43, on Qwen2.5-3B). The SelfAware→CREPE transfer is 0.65–0.70, close to the within-CREPE readout.
- **Probe-routed premise checking roughly triples challenge precision.** On CREPE (Llama-3.1-8B), always-challenge contests 74% of false premises but 57% of sound questions; random gating at 35% budget gives 26% / 20%; probe-gated gives 42% false-premise contested at only 14% false-challenge. Replicates on Qwen2.5-7B (41% / 16%).
- **Factorized Abstention is the only dual-risk-certifiable policy at scale.** At αU=0.15, αW=0.50, δ=0.10: on Llama-3.1-8B the factorized policy certifies both budgets at test C-coverage 0.75, vs 0.31 for confidence-only conformal abstention; on Qwen2.5-14B it is the *only* policy that certifies (0.47). The learned joint scalar reaches higher coverage where it certifies (0.88 Gemma, 0.81 at 8B) but forfeits per-axis risk attribution and fails to certify at 14B.
- **The wrong-answer budget is bounded by model accuracy — a scale-dependent asymmetry.** SelfAware answerable accuracy is only 0.28–0.30 at 2B–3B, so holding RW forces near-total abstention and the W budget certifies marginally or not at all; at 8B accuracy reaches 0.42 and the ceiling lifts. The unanswerable budget, by contrast, certifies on four of five models (answerability gate accepts 0–1 U items, UCB(RU) 0.07–0.16).
- **Replication audit supports the protocol.** Over 100 resplits per model, issued certificates are violated on the held-out test split in 0–5.6% of cases (within the δ=0.05 allowance); certificates issue in 18/100 (Gemma 2B) to 68/100 (Llama-8B) replicates, so at 8B certification is repeatable, not just possible.

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author preprint by **Benedikt J. Wagner** (City St George's, University of London), who has prior published work on neuro-symbolic open/closed-world reasoning (Wagner & d'Avila Garcez, 2021/2022) — directly relevant to the paper's framing of the unknown region as a first-class object. The paper is unusually careful about positioning: it explicitly disclaims discovering that answerability is internally legible (crediting Slobodkin et al. 2023, Lavi et al. 2026, Kadavath et al. 2022, Chen et al. 2026) and runs the surface-confound controls (bag-of-words 0.87) that a less careful paper would have omitted. A pre-registered pilot (hypotheses and kill-criteria fixed in the script before running) and a 100-seed replication audit are methodological strengths rare at this scale. Every number is asserted by a continuous-integration verification script against committed score artifacts, with code and data loaders promised.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine but precisely scoped. The same-decision C/W/U geometry, the honest surface/elicitation controls (including the bag-of-words 0.87 confound on SelfAware that most answerability-probing papers ignore), and the dual-risk certificate are the real contributions. The authors are commendably explicit that the internal legibility of answerability is *not* their discovery. The neuro-symbolic "thresholded conjunction of two grounded predicates" framing is conceptually nice but operationally it is just `AND(sA≥τA, sC≥τC)`.
- **Rigor:** High. Four-way leak-free splits (probe-train / tune / certify / test), exact Clopper–Pearson bounds, Bonferroni, bootstrap CIs, a 100-seed replication audit, and a pre-registered behavioral pilot. The paper honestly reports that at small split sizes tuned thresholds are noisy and that zero-count certification bounds can be optimistic — and then audits the consequence.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong on paper. Fixed seeds, single M4 laptop (24GB), validated quantization, CI verification script, committed score artifacts, and promised code/data release. The matched-gate SAE reader construction (Appendix G) is fully documented.
- **Significance:** The decomposition is a useful conceptual lens for anyone building abstention/risk-controlled LLM systems, and the CREPE result (output readouts at chance on natural false premises) is a sharp, deployable finding. However, practical impact is capped by the **weak baselines and small scale**: correctness is judged by alias matching on SelfAware answerable accuracy of just 0.28–0.42, sampling-based semantic entropy (Farquhar et al. 2024) is explicitly *not* run because it doesn't fit the single-pass protocol, and all models are ≤14B. The wrong-answer budget (αW=0.50) is so lenient that it is essentially uninformative — the authors admit budgets tighter than 0.50 "do not certify at any scale we test," partly because certification splits contain only 18–22 W items.
- **Red flags / overclaiming:** Minimal — the paper is notably self-critical. The one overclaim to watch is the 14B headline ("the only policy that certifies at all"): it certifies at a modest 0.47 C-coverage, and the learned combiner's failure there is a certification-split artifact (RW k/n 9/23, UCB 0.58) rather than evidence the combiner is fundamentally worse. The pooled SAE-gate certificate (selective-risk 0.074) is explicitly flagged as conditional on post-hoc selection of capitals+languages because the currencies truth axis was unreliable — a candid caveat that weakens the corroboration section.

## Strengths

- Decomposes a single conflated failure mode into two axes with a clean, replicated geometric characterization (the L-shape) across three model families and 2B–14B.
- Runs the controls the claim actually requires — most notably the bag-of-words surface bound (0.87 on SelfAware) and the CREPE natural-data test that removes the lexical confound — and narrows claims accordingly.
- The probe-routed premise-checking result is a concrete, behaviorally consequential payoff: it turns the abstract "internal signal" into a routing policy that triples challenge precision over always-challenge.
- Dual-risk certification makes the vague "model is too weak" claim measurable and scale-dependent, with a replication audit rather than a single-shot certificate.
- Intellectual honesty throughout: disclaims novelty where due, reports unfavorable intervals, flags optimistic zero-count bounds.

## Limitations

- **Small scale.** All models are ≤14B; the hidden correctness readout already grows sharply from 3B to 8B (0.59→0.88), so the pattern may not persist (or may weaken) at 70B+ where explicit self-assessment improves. The authors explicitly do not claim persistence at frontier scale.
- **Weak correctness signal and lenient wrong-answer budget.** Correctness is alias-matching on SelfAware answerable accuracies of 0.28–0.42; the only wrong-answer budget that certifies is αW=0.50, which is too loose to be operationally meaningful. Tighter budgets fail entirely, partly due to only 18–22 W items per certification split.
- **Missing sampling-based baseline.** Semantic entropy (Farquhar et al. 2024) is excluded because it doesn't fit the single-pass protocol — leaving the strongest known output-side hallucination detector unevaluated against the hidden probe.
- **SelfAware surface confound.** Most of SelfAware's 0.97–0.99 separability is surface-recoverable (bag-of-words 0.87); the transferable core on CREPE is only moderate (0.69–0.78 internal).
- **No causal intervention.** Unlike Slobodkin et al. (2023) and Lavi et al. (2026), this work does not causally steer/erase the answerability direction — evidence is observational plus policy-level.
- **English-only, single-turn, alias correctness only**; CREPE contributes only the admissibility axis (long-form ELI5 answers admit no alias correctness label).

## Relevance

直接相关于 LLM 拒答、不确定性量化与幻觉检测的研究兴趣：本文将"是否拒答"分解为正确性轴与可答性轴的双信号几何，并给出带统计保证的双风险认证策略，对于构建可信、可量化风险边界的大模型系统（尤其是 selective answering / risk-controlled generation 方向）具有方法论参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the C/W/U L-shape persist at 70B/405B, where explicit self-assessment (P(IK), P(True)) and hidden correctness readouts both sharpen — does the answerability axis remain superior to output, or does the gap close?
- How does the Factorized Abstention policy compare against sampling-based semantic entropy when the single-pass constraint is relaxed? Is the hidden answerability probe still competitive, or does multi-sample entropy dominate on the correctness axis?
- Can the wrong-answer budget be tightened below αW=0.50 to operationally useful levels (e.g., 0.10–0.20) by scaling the certification calibration data (18–22 W items is the binding constraint), or is it fundamentally bounded by model accuracy?
- The probe-routed premise checking repairs discrimination but correction quality remains the bottleneck (only 47% NLI-validated correct contests on Llama) — can a second-stage correction model close this, or does confabulation reappear "one level up"?
- Does the dual-risk certificate framework extend to richer logical compositions (OR, negation) over multiple grounded predicates beyond the simple AND conjunction, as the neuro-symbolic framing suggests?
