Methodology § secondary meta-analysis

A secondary meta-analysis of model outputs from 53 graduate-course runners over 5,300 case-result rows on 394 unique cases. Each runner ran both conditions of one controlled axis on the gpt-4.1 family (temperature 1.0, K=50), yielding a mean of 2.5 runners per (condition, case) cell.

  • AXIS A · model tier   nano vs. mini (zero-shot)
  • AXIS B · prompt strategy   mini zero-shot vs. mini chain-of-thought
  • AXIS C · mid vs. frontier   mini vs. gpt-4.1 (zero-shot)

Benchmarks bracket opposite answer spaces: GPQA Diamond (4-option MCQ, where high C is the discriminating regime) and AIME (integer math, where C is floored). Per (case, condition) the paper computes self-consistency C = n_maj/K, sample accuracy A, and majority-correct M. Because deployed majority-vote systems return the majority answer, M is the primary outcome and C is the confidence score under audit.

The primary statistic is Spearman ρ(C,M). All CIs use a hierarchical runner-clustered bootstrap (B=2000), with case-clustered and leave-one-runner robustness checks, Holm correction across twelve per-cell tests, and a controlled logged-snapshot re-run plus an exploratory Claude haiku/sonnet/opus cross-family probe.

GPT & Claude pick the same wrong option on 48–63% of shared-wrong items (haiku p=.003, sonnet p=.005) — cross-model agreement can encode shared bias, not independent confirmation.

Supporting findings: CoT lifts accuracy (ΔA=+0.067 GPQA, +0.069 AIME) but only marginally the agreement signal. An option-shuffle control drops mini accuracy 0.50 → 0.29, and "D" is under-selected (~16% vs 25% uniform) — part of MCQ "confidence" is positional, not semantic. A stop-when-consensus adaptive rule saves ~60% of samples at equal error, and works where abstention does not.

Strengths § what holds up

  • Exceptional statistical hygiene: hierarchical cluster bootstrap, Holm correction, permutation tests, case-clustered robustness, honest borderline CIs.
  • Recurrence-vs-noise separation of confident errors is a genuinely useful move prior consistency work lacked.
  • Option-shuffle control cleanly shows a measurable slice of MCQ "confidence" is positional bias, not semantic certainty.
  • Cross-family shared-error permutation test gives a sobering answer to "if two providers agree, is that safer?"
  • Unusually candid limitations & ethics sections — does not overclaim; explicitly endorses adaptive sampling.

Limitations § where it bends

  • Single-provider primary audit (gpt-4.1); the Claude check is agent-mediated, K=10, no temperature control.
  • No logged model snapshots or timestamps — the most damaging gap; replication is across runners/prompts, not time or backend.
  • Only two benchmarks, both constrained-answer (MCQ, integer math); no open-ended generation or code.
  • K=50 leaves substantial noise; confidence-baseline & shuffle controls use only 50 / 48 cases — wide CIs.
  • Runner-implemented prompts add variance; runners are a non-independent course cohort.
  • Some GPQA items appear ambiguous/mislabeled, inflating apparent confident-wrongness.
  • No reasoning-trained (o1-style) models, whose internal CoT may already supply the calibration lift.

Open questions § for further work

  1. Does the frontier over-confidence paradox hold for reasoning-trained models (o1/R1-style), whose internal chain-of-thought may already calibrate the output distribution — making explicit CoT redundant?
  2. How does the agreement–correctness relationship change on free-form generation and code, where the answer space is unbounded and C cannot be computed as n_maj/K without a semantic-equivalence step?
  3. Can a corrected confidence signal — C deflated by an option-position-prior, or combined with a cross-family disagreement penalty — recover the discriminative power raw C loses on frontier models?
  4. Are the shared cross-family confident errors concentrated in specific knowledge domains (memorized facts, common misconceptions) detectable a priori?
  5. What is the effect of sample budget K on the over-confidence gap — does it shrink, persist, or worsen as K grows?
