Why This Matters

Everyday evaluation moves — swap the judge, enlarge the jury, add a debate round — silently change the score. This paper makes those hidden measurement shifts auditable. 直接关系到「LLM-as-judge 是否可信」：揭示换裁判、陪审团聚合、辩论协议中被忽视的测量偏差与相关性陷阱，并给出可操作的审计清单。

⚠ Critical Edge

The paper's most dramatic numbers — debate shifts of +0.317 — rest on an implementation whose round-1 parse failures silently default to "A", so the headline debate effect cannot be attributed to deliberation.

Methodology

The paper treats LLM-as-judge reliability as a multi-dimensional measurement property, not a single accuracy score, operationalized through four observable constructs — each mapped to a concrete experiment.

Judgment validity
accuracy · Cohen's κ · Spearman
Bias robustness
position flip · verbosity · granularity
Aggregation independence
error correlation ρ · jury gain
Protocol auditability
parse status · fallback · round verdicts

Two evaluator axes. A Qwen3 parameter sweep (1.7B→4B→14B→32B) approximates a controlled ablation; a MiniMax release sequence (M2→M2.1→M2.5→M2.7) is treated as an observed path, not a controlled one. GLM-5.1 and mimo-v2-pro serve as cross-family references.

Datasets. LLMBar (adversarial pairs), PandaLM (broad), Chatbot Arena (human preference), Judge's Verdict (pointwise) — balanced to avoid single-slice overfitting.

Statistics. Paired two-sided McNemar on parse-shared examples, Holm correction over the family of 18 adjacent tests, near-greedy decoding (T=0.1). Homogeneous juries sample one judge at T=0.7 to expose dependence; jury ρ is estimated from the vote matrix and fed into a corrected beta-binomial predictor.

Strengths

  • Intellectually honest throughout — repeatedly narrows its own claims and foregrounds limitations (debate result "suggestive rather than causal").
  • The ρ-corrected beta-binomial jury analysis is the technical highlight: a clean, quantitative demonstration of why majority voting fails for LLM judges.
  • An operational reporting checklist (Table 4) ties each reliability risk to a concrete required report item and the failure mode it avoids.
  • Balanced dataset choice (adversarial / broad / human-preference / pointwise) prevents single-slice overfitting of the conclusions.

Limitations

  • The MiniMax path is an observed release sequence, not a controlled intervention — adjacent non-significance cannot isolate which training stage drove capability.
  • The debate experiment — the largest numerical shifts — lacks raw responses and parse-success logs, so it is non-reproducible for its headline claim.
  • Narrow panel: two primary families plus two cross-family references; generalization to other families, languages, and distributions is unverified.
  • Adjacent McNemar tests are pair-only; there is no formal between-axis test, so "the two axes differ" is descriptive, not inferential.

Open Questions

  1. Does "upgrades not interchangeable" hold under a fully controlled within-family ablation (same data, only parameter count differs), rather than a sweep vs. an opaque release sequence?
  2. How much of the jury correlation ρ=0.944–0.972 is driven by the shared prompt versus the shared model? A prompt-factorial × jury design could disentangle the two.
  3. Can the debate protocol be re-run with full parse-success logging to decide whether the +0.317 cross-capability shifts are genuine deliberation or parser-fallback artifacts?
  4. Do the bias-reduction trends (flip 0.320→0.117, verbosity 0.547→0.13) persist on longer-form, multi-turn, or non-English tasks?
  5. Is a principled single "reliability score" (accuracy + bias + ρ + auditability) possible, or does multidimensional reporting inherently resist compression to one number?
