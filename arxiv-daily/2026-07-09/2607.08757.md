> **一句话总结：** 本文从理论上证明：扩散模型中按前向分布（forward marginal）度量的 score 误差足够小，并不能保证反向 Euler–Maruyama 采样的数值稳定性——构造出 on-path 误差任意小、连续时间动力学接近真实、且其 Euler 离散端点弱收敛却使所有正阶矩（从而所有 W_p）发散的 score 场（甚至在单个固定 DiT-S/2 架构内成立）；并给出正面结果：把 learned denoiser 投影到包含数据支撑的有界闭凸集可同时保住精度、给出网格一致矩界与 Wasserstein 收敛，小规模 DiT 实验中投影把最终二阶矩降低约 1.1×10^4–3.3×10^4 倍。

> **TL;DR:** The paper shows via explicit counterexamples that arbitrarily small forward-marginal (on-path) score-matching error in score-based diffusion models does NOT certify numerical stability of reverse-time Euler–Maruyama sampling: one can build a smooth score field (and even a fixed finite DiT-S/2 network) whose learned reverse SDE is nonexplosive, has all moments, is within any η of the exact process in path-space total variation, yet whose Euler endpoints converge in probability while every positive moment and every Wasserstein distance W_p (p≥1) diverges. On the constructive 8×8 point-mass VE toy model, the family of small DiT-style denoisers keeps score MSE ≤ 7.10×10⁻³ but exhibits RMS amplification r₈/r₀ up to 14.69, and projecting the denoiser onto C=[−1,1] reduces the final second moment by factors of 1.13×10⁴–3.33×10⁴ (RMS falling from 35–46 to 2.6–3.7). There is no standard image benchmark/baseline; the "baseline" being refuted is the prior theoretical assumption that distribution-weighted score error controls discrete sampling stability.

## 核心贡献

- **指出并严格证明了 on-path score 误差的盲区**：构造出单个光滑 score 场，其按前向 marginal 度量的 L² 误差可任意小，连续时间反向 SDE 非爆炸、各阶矩有限、与真实反向过程在路径空间 TV 距离任意接近，但其 Euler–Maruyama 端点在概率收敛的同时所有正阶矩发散，从而对所有 p≥1 都有 W_p(·,ν)→∞（Theorem 3.1）。
- **机制清晰且可迁移**：不稳定源于在前向 marginal 几乎不携带质量的"远端"区域放置一个内向超线性（superlinear）扰动；罕见的数值轨道一旦进入就会被显式更新反复超线性放大，放大阶 (1+α)K 战胜了仅 O(K^{2/α}+K) 的稀有事件代价。该机制推广到任意维（Corollary 3.2，径向扰动）与 probability-flow ODE（Corollary 3.3）。
- **等预算下不可排序（Corollary 3.4）**：存在两个 on-path 预算完全相同的光滑 score 场，一个 Euler 离散有网格一致各阶矩界、另一个丢失所有正阶矩——即该标量指标连"排序采样器稳定性"都做不到。
- **固定有限神经网络内的反例**：在单个固定的 adaLN-Zero DiT 架构（甚至 DiT-S/2：12 层、hidden 384、6 头、patch 2）内，存在一族全局有界且全局 Lipschitz 的 denoiser 参数，使 on-path 误差与路径空间 TV 都趋于 0，但 Euler 端点在每个 W_p 中发散（Theorem 3.5、Corollary 3.6、附录 A/B 的构造）。
- **简洁的正面结果——denoiser 投影**：当数据支撑含于已知有界闭凸集 C 时，将 learned denoiser 投影到 C 不会增加逐点 score 误差（Prop 4.2 反而收缩误差），给出网格一致矩界（Prop 4.1 显式表示），并在温和局部正则性下恢复 Wasserstein 收敛（Theorem 4.3）。对图像像素框情形，这恰好等价于裁剪预测的 clean sample，从而为 Imagen/Reflected Diffusion 等模型中的 clipping 启发式提供了稳定性解释。
- **小规模 DiT 实验证机制**：用同一个 104,388 参数的 DiT-style 网络（8×8 单通道 point-mass VE 模型），实测到 stress 初始化下的逐级 RMS 放大，并验证投影能将其抑制。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifies and proves a blind spot of on-path (forward-marginal) score error.** Theorem 3.1 constructs a single smooth score field whose L²-on-path error is arbitrarily small, whose learned reverse SDE is nonexplosive with finite moments of every order and arbitrarily close to the exact process in path-space TV, yet whose Euler–Maruyama endpoints converge in probability while every positive moment diverges — hence W_p(·,ν)→∞ for every p≥1 and every ν with finite p-th moment.
- **Simple, transportable amplification mechanism.** An inward superlinear perturbation is placed in a remote region that carries negligible mass under the forward marginals; rare numerical trajectories that enter it get amplified by repeated superlinear explicit updates. The amplification order (1+α)K dominates the rare-event cost O(K^{2/α}+K). The mechanism extends to every dimension (Corollary 3.2, radial) and to deterministic probability-flow ODEs (Corollary 3.3).
- **Equal-budget non-ordering (Corollary 3.4).** Two score fields with identical on-path error budget can be, respectively, grid-uniformly stable in every moment and unstable in every positive moment — so the scalar budget cannot even rank samplers by stability.
- **Obstruction inside one fixed finite network.** Theorem 3.5 / Corollary 3.6 (with explicit constructions in Appendices A–B) show the same failure inside a single fixed adaLN-Zero DiT, and even within the concrete DiT-S/2 configuration (12 blocks, hidden 384, 6 heads, patch 2): bounded, globally Lipschitz denoisers with vanishing on-path error and path-space TV whose Euler endpoints diverge in every W_p.
- **A clean positive result — denoiser projection.** Projecting the learned denoiser onto a known bounded closed convex set C containing the data support never increases pointwise score error (Proposition 4.2 — it contracts it), yields grid-uniform moment bounds via an explicit affine representation (Proposition 4.1), and restores Wasserstein convergence under mild local regularity (Theorem 4.3). For image data in a box this coincides with clipping the predicted clean sample, giving a stability-theoretic justification for a heuristic used in Imagen and Reflected Diffusion Models.
- **Small-scale DiT experiments** realize the amplification mechanism and its suppression by projection in a single 104,388-parameter DiT-style network on an 8×8 one-channel point-mass VE model.

## Methodology

The paper is primarily theoretical, supported by a targeted constructive experiment.

**Analytical setup.** Work in the variance-exploding (VE) parameterization, Xt = X0 + √t·g, with data supported in a compact K ⊂ B(0,M) and C0 = conv(K). Tweedie's identity gives the exact score st(x) = (Dt(x)−x)/t where Dt(x)=E[X0|Xt=x] ∈ C0. Reverse-time Euler–Maruyama on a strictly decreasing grid uses the recursion Yk = Yk+1 + hk·s_{t_{k+1}}(Yk+1) + √hk·ξk.

**The counterexample (Section 3).** The corrupted score sbt(x) = st(x) − λ·χR(x)·x|x|^α places a smooth inward superlinear perturbation (cutoff χR active only outside [−R,R]) in a region where the Gaussian forward marginal has exponentially small mass.
- *On-path accuracy*: by dominated convergence, sup_t E_pt|sbt−st|² ≤ ε once R is large.
- *Continuous-time stability*: the dissipative superquadratic term x·sbt(x) ≤ C − c|x|^{2+α} gives a Lyapunov (1+x²)^m bound ruling out explosion and giving uniform finite-horizon moments.
- *Path-space TV*: coupling s_t and sb_t with the same Brownian motion, the two reverse processes agree until first exit from [−R,R]; the TV bound is P(sup|Uu|≥R), which is <η for large R.
- *Discrete moment divergence*: once an Euler iterate reaches |y| ≥ A·K^{1/α}, the active-tail one-step lower bound gives zj ≥ c0·z_{j+1}^{1+α} (normalized recursion). Induction yields super-exponential conditional growth over K steps; the rare shell-and-bounded-noise event has probability ≥ exp(−C3·(K^{2/α}+K)), so the moment lower bound E|Y0|^q grows without bound while the chains still converge in probability.

**Neural realization (Section 3.3, Appendices A–B).** The ideal remote-tail denoiser Fm(x) = −λ·ΓR(x)·x³ is approximated within a fixed adaLN-Zero DiT using GELU "ReLU-positive" gates and a Taylor second-difference multiplication module; a final bounded LayerNorm head keeps the output globally bounded and Lipschitz. Lemma A.2 / Prop B.1 show this fixed circuit fits inside DiT-S/2 with only 3 of 12 blocks active and attention disabled.

**Positive result (Section 4).** Define DP_t(x) = Π_C(Dbt(x)) and sP_t = (DP_t − x)/t. The projected Euler step becomes a convex combination Yk = (1−ρk)Yk+1 + ρk·DP(Yk+1) + √hk·ξk with 0<ρk<1, so the deterministic part has at most linear growth toward C. Iterating yields an explicit representation (Prop 4.1) with telescoping weights and a Gaussian term of uniformly bounded variance, giving grid-uniform Lq bounds. Since projection onto a closed convex set contracts distance to any point of C and the exact Dt lies in C, projection never increases denoiser/score error (Prop 4.2), so stability is gained for free.

**Experiments (Section 5, Appendix C).** Base model: X0=0, exact denoiser zero, t∈[0.1,1]. Network: patch size 2, hidden 64, three adaLN/GELU MLP blocks (104,388 parameters). Four family members differ only in parameters and remote target profile (R, L, stress center y) using a smoothly gated, tanh-saturated cubic target. Training combines on-path score loss, safe-region denoiser loss, cascade-tube regression, and transition consistency, with checkpoint selection by smallest on-path error under fixed cascade/transition error caps. A stress-initialized protocol (8 Euler updates with perturbations uniform on [−0.35,0.35]) isolates post-entry dynamics.

## Results & Highlights

- **On-path accuracy is small and does not predict instability.** On the 8×8 point-mass VE model, the four DiT-style family members achieve forward-marginal score MSE of 1.94×10⁻³, 3.73×10⁻³, 3.89×10⁻³, and 7.10×10⁻³ respectively (max 7.10×10⁻³), with paired-trajectory path RMSE between 0.0119 and 0.0237 (Table 1) — yet they exhibit the predicted amplification.
- **Measured amplification matches the superlinear-cascade prediction.** The stress-test RMS amplification factor r₈/r₀ rises across the family from 7.90 (m0) to 14.69 (m3), reproducing the repeated-superlinear-growth mechanism of Theorem 3.5 even though the neural endpoint still retains ~90%–95% of the target RMS magnitude (Section 5.1).
- **Denoiser projection suppresses the amplification dramatically.** With C=[−1,1] and identical stress initializations, projecting the learned denoiser reduces the final empirical second moment by factors between 1.13×10⁴ and 3.33×10⁴; stepwise projected RMS magnitude falls from an initial 35–46 to 2.6–3.7, while raw RMS magnitude keeps growing (Section 5.2, Figure 2) — a direct empirical confirmation of Proposition 4.1 / 4.2.
- **Theoretical headline numbers.** Theorem 3.1 gives, for every ε,η,λ,α>0: on-path L² error ≤ ε; learned reverse SDE path-law TV ≤ η from exact; yet E|Y0^{(Km)}|^q → ∞ for every q>0 and W_p(L(Y0^{(Km)}),ν)→∞ for every p≥1, ν∈P_p(R). Corollary 3.4 gives equal-budget non-ordering: two fields with identical integrated score budget (b≤ε) where one has grid-uniform moments of every order and the other loses every positive moment.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Single-author paper by Yiwei Zhou** (affiliation: yiwei.zhou@utexas.edu, UT Austin). The argument is rigorous and self-contained: every claim is a stated theorem/corollary/proposition with a full proof, including the non-trivial neural realization (Appendix A) and the DiT-S/2 resource accounting (Appendix B, Prop B.1).
- The theoretical content engages directly with the relevant convergence literature (Song et al. ICLR'21; Chen et al. ICLR'23; Lee–Lu–Tan ALT'23; Benton et al. ICLR'24; Gao–Nguyen–Zhu JMLR'25; Bruno–Sabanis TMLR'25; Conforti–Durmus–Silveri SIDMA'25) and explicitly relates its mechanism to the classical Hutzenthaler–Jentzen–Kloeden (Proc. Roy. Soc. A, 2011) divergence of explicit Euler for superlinear SDEs, correctly noting the difference (here the unstable region has negligible forward-marginal weight and the continuous dynamics remain stable).
- Experimental protocol is honestly described: a single training seed (1729), 1024 trajectories per grid for unconditioned evaluation, 512 paired trajectories for stress/projection experiments, and an explicit statement that the stress-initialized protocol "does not estimate the natural entry probability."

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High and specific. The paper does not merely re-derive Hutzenthaler–Jentzen–Kloeden; the genuinely new observation is the *separation* between forward-marginal score accuracy and discrete-sampler stability — i.e., the unstable region can be placed where the training loss barely sees it while the continuous reverse SDE stays perfectly well-behaved. The equal-budget non-ordering (Corollary 3.4) and the fixed-architecture DiT-S/2 realization (Theorem 3.5 / Corollary 3.6) are strong, non-generic results.
- **Rigor**: Excellent. Proofs are complete, including the delicate Step 5 amplification-vs-rare-event-cost bookkeeping and the constructive neural circuit in Appendices A–B (GELU ReLU⁺ gates + second-difference multiplication + bounded LayerNorm head, embedded tokenwise into a patchified DiT and verified against DiT-S/2's width/depth budget). The author is careful to note that for each fixed parameter m the moment bound is uniform in K, and divergence occurs only along the diagonal (m,Km).
- **Reproducibility**: Moderate-to-good for theory, weak for experiments. The theorems are reproducible from the text. The experiment, however, relies on a hand-crafted training pipeline combining four loss terms (on-path score, safe-region denoiser, cascade-tube regression, transition consistency) and a checkpoint-selection rule; no code is mentioned, and reproducing the family members requires reconstructing this non-standard training setup and the specific remote target profiles (λ=0.02; (R,L,y) = (2.5,700,35),(3,1000,40),(3.5,1450,43),(4,1800,46)).
- **Significance**: Conceptually important for the diffusion-theory community: it sharpens what scalar score-error measures can certify, complements recent "what does score error certify" work (Cao–Chen–Sridharan–Wu arXiv:2603.11319; Khelifa–Turner–Venkataramanan arXiv:2606.06179), and gives a clean theoretical interpretation of clipping. The positive projection result is simple enough to be directly actionable.
- **Red flags / overclaiming**: The biggest caveat is the *gap between the theory and the experiments*. The counterexamples are constructed artifacts (a remote superlinear bump that the network is deliberately trained to reproduce); the paper does not demonstrate that this pathology arises in real, large-scale image diffusion training. The author is mostly honest about this — the experiments are framed as a "constructive realization test" of the mechanism, not as evidence that production samplers fail. A second caveat: the stress-initialized protocol is explicitly acknowledged not to estimate the natural entry probability, so the large amplification factors (up to 14.69) and second-moment reductions (up to 3.33×10⁴) are conditional on stress initialization and should not be read as typical operating-condition numbers. A missing ablation worth noting: there is no comparison against other cheap stabilizers (e.g., clipped/thresholded score, adaptive step-size controllers, predictor–corrector) to show projection is preferred rather than merely sufficient.

## Strengths

- Crystallizes a precise and previously under-articulated obstruction: forward-marginal score accuracy is blind to off-path regions where numerical trajectories actually go.
- Fully proved, including a non-trivial construction inside a fixed DiT-S/2 architecture — the failure is not an artifact of unbounded function classes.
- The positive result (projection) is elegant, comes with a clean contraction guarantee (Prop 4.2), explicit grid-uniform moment representation (Prop 4.1), and a Wasserstein convergence theorem (Thm 4.3), and recovers clipping as a special case — giving a satisfying theory-practice bridge.
- Generalizes cleanly to higher dimensions and to probability-flow ODE samplers.

## Limitations

- The constructed pathology is adversarial: it is not shown to occur in standard large-scale image/text diffusion training. Whether real score networks develop comparable remote-tail behavior is an open empirical question.
- Experiments are on an 8×8 one-channel point-mass toy model with a single training seed and a bespoke four-term loss; reported amplification/projection factors are conditional on a stress-initialized protocol and are Monte-Carlo point estimates.
- The positive projection result requires knowing a bounded convex set C containing the data support; for unbounded or unknown support (e.g., many scientific/continuous-valued domains) it does not directly apply.
- No comparison with alternative stabilizers (step-size adaptation, score clipping, predictor–corrector, reflected diffusion) beyond the conceptual link to clipping.
- Single author; no independent code release referenced.

## Relevance

对关注扩散模型训练动力学、采样稳定性与收敛理论的读者高度相关：它精确刻画了标准 score-matching 目标在"离路径区域"的盲区，并提供了一个简单可落地的 denoiser 投影（即 clipping）的稳定性解释，对理论分析与实际采样器工程（尤其是 VE/概率流采样与 Schrödinger bridge 推广）都有直接启示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the remote-tail amplification pathology arise spontaneously in standard large-scale image/text diffusion training (e.g., DiT-XL on ImageNet, SD3), or is it purely a constructed worst case? How would one detect it from a trained checkpoint without a stress test?
- How does denoiser projection interact with classifier-free guidance, where the guided score can push trajectories well outside the data support and where C is no longer obviously valid?
- Can a comparable on-path-vs-off-path obstruction be formalized for Schrödinger bridges and flow-matching / rectified-flow samplers, as the author suggests in the Discussion — and do bridge geometry or feasible-set constraints give projection-like stabilization there?
- For data with unbounded or unknown support (e.g., scientific continuous-valued generation), is there a data-adaptive convex relaxation of C that retains the contraction and grid-uniform moment properties of Propositions 4.1–4.2?
- A fair head-to-head: against adaptive step-size control, score clipping, predictor–corrector, and reflected diffusion, when does projection dominate in sample quality / stability / compute?
- The natural entry probability of the unstable region is not estimated in the experiments; what is its true order under pT, and does it ever become large enough to matter at practical step counts?
