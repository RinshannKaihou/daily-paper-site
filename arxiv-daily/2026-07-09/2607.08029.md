> **一句话总结：** 本文在 Jetson Orin NX 与 AGX 上对 5 个小于 3B 参数的小型 VLM（Qwen3-VL-2B、DeepSeek-VL2-Tiny、PaliGemma2-3B、LLaVA-OV-0.5B、Kosmos-2.5）做组件级（视觉编码器/投影器/LLM 骨干）量化消融，发现 INT4 量化敏感度由架构范式（MoE 比 Dense 更鲁棒）而非参数量决定（MoE 的 Qwen3-VL 在 MME 上反而 +56.04 分，而 Dense 的 LLaVA-OV 暴跌 -220.02 分），且 SigLIP 视觉编码器在 BitsAndBytes INT8 下出现严重的部署特定延迟反常（最坏变慢 4.66×，但精度不变），同时 LLM INT4 虽能节省约 40-50% 显存却因反量化开销使 TPOT 增加 10-56%——从而给出"按模态与硬件协同分配精度"的边缘部署指南。

> **TL;DR:** This paper runs a controlled component-wise quantization ablation (vision encoder / projector / LLM backbone, six configs from FP16 baseline to all-INT8/INT4) on five sub-3B small VLMs evaluated on the MME benchmark across two heterogeneous edge SoCs (Jetson Orin NX and AGX), and shows that INT4 LLM quantization sensitivity is governed by architecture paradigm rather than scale — MoE backbones (Qwen3-VL-2B +56.04, DeepSeek-VL2-Tiny +39.50 MME) improve while dense ones collapse (LLaVA-OV-0.5B -220.02) — that SigLIP vision INT8 via BitsAndBytes inflates vision latency up to 4.66× with no accuracy loss (a deployment-specific kernel-hardware artifact, confirmed by isolated Nsight profiling showing 25.7% of INT8 kernel time in quant/dequant and 7.4× more kernel launches), and that LLM INT4 saves 40-50% VRAM but raises TPOT by 10-56% and even increases energy, so mixed-precision allocation must be modality- and hardware-aware.

## 核心贡献

- **首个组件级受控量化消融框架**：首次将小型 VLM（≤3B）的视觉编码器、投影器、LLM 骨干三个组件独立分离，定义 6 个量化配置（cfg0 FP16 基线、cfg1 LLM INT4、cfg2 Proj INT8、cfg3 cfg1+cfg2、cfg4 Vision INT8、cfg5 cfg1+cfg4），逐组件测量精度敏感性与资源效率，弥补以往 VLM 量化研究"整体端到端评估、缺乏边际贡献量化"的空白。
- **架构范式主导量化敏感性的发现**：通过假设驱动实验证伪了"参数越小越脆弱"的 H1，并修正为 H1rev——INT4 量化鲁棒性由 MoE vs Dense 决定，MoE（Qwen3-VL、DeepSeek-VL2）在 INT4 后精度反升、Dense（LLaVA-OV、PaliGemma2）显著下降，指出稀疏激活可局部抑制量化噪声传播。
- **揭示 SigLIP-BitsAndBytes-Ampere 延迟反常的根因**：定位到一个具体的硬件-软件栈不兼容问题——SigLIP-So400m 视觉编码器在 BitsAndBytes INT8 下因反量化 kernel 碎片化（Nsight 显示 22.8-25.7% kernel 时间消耗在 quant/dequant，kernel 启动数增加 7.4×），在 Jetson Ampere 上延迟放慢 2.43-4.66×，而非 SigLIP 的 Kosmos-2.5 仅 1.15-1.20×。
- **跨硬件的精度排名稳定性与能效平台特异性**：在 NX 与 AGX 两平台上证明 MME 精度排名与配置无关且平台不变（H5 成立），但延迟与"每焦耳智能"（IPJ）高度平台相关，AGX 上 Qwen3-VL 的 IPJ 高达 NX 的 2.5×，强调边缘 AI 需要 HW-SW 协同设计而非单纯模型压缩。
- **给出实用的部署指南与开源工具**：基于假设检验产出按模态与硬件分配精度的实践策略（如 LLM INT4 仅作显存节省手段而非加速、视觉 INT8 在 SigLIP 上要谨慎、cfg5 复合量化需架构特定的预评估），并发布端到端 profiling 工具与匿名代码库。

## Key Contributions

- **First systematic component-wise quantization ablation for small VLMs (≤3B)** that independently isolates the vision encoder, projector, and LLM backbone under six configurations (single-component cfg1/cfg2/cfg4 and composite cfg3/cfg5), measuring marginal accuracy sensitivity and resource efficiency per component — a gap left by prior VLM quantization work that only reports end-to-end numbers.
- **Architecture-paradigm (MoE vs Dense) dominates quantization sensitivity**, not parameter scale: the authors falsify their own scale-dependent hypothesis H1 and reformulate it as H1rev, showing MoE backbones (Qwen3-VL +56.04, DeepSeek-VL2 +39.50 MME) actually improve under INT4 LLM quantization while dense ones (LLaVA-OV -220.02, PaliGemma2 -62.12) degrade.
- **Root-causes the SigLIP-BitsAndBytes-Ampere INT8 latency anomaly**: an isolated Nsight Systems profiling study (50 iterations) shows SigLIP-So400m vision INT8 inflates vision latency up to 4.66× with no accuracy change, driven by 22.8-25.7% of INT8 kernel time in quant/dequant kernels and 7.4× more kernel launches — a deployment-specific encoder-kernel-hardware interaction, not an inherent SigLIP flaw.
- **Demonstrates that LLM INT4 via BitsAndBytes trades VRAM for latency/energy**: VRAM drops 21.8-47.5% but TPOT rises 10.4-56.3% and energy can rise up to 54.7% (Qwen3-VL on NX), so on Jetson Orin Ampere, INT4 should be treated as a VRAM-saving technique rather than a latency/energy optimization.
- **Cross-platform transferability finding + practical deployment guidelines**: accuracy rankings are platform-invariant (H5) across NX and AGX, while intelligence-per-joule (IPJ) is highly platform-specific (Qwen3-VL AGX IPJ 14.45 vs NX 5.87, ~2.5×), motivating hardware-software co-design. An open profiling tool and anonymous repo are released.

## Methodology

The paper is structured as a hypothesis-driven empirical study. It formulates five hypotheses (H1-H5) and evaluates each through five research questions (RQ1-RQ5).

**Models (5, architecturally diverse sub-3B sVLMs):** Qwen3-VL-2B-Instruct (SigLIP-2 + Qwen3 MoE), DeepSeek-VL2-Tiny (SigLIP-So400m + DeepSeek-MoE), PaliGemma2-3B (SigLIP-So400m + Gemma-2 dense), LLaVA-OneVision-0.5B (SigLIP-So400m + Qwen2-0.5B dense), and Kosmos-2.5-chat (Pix2Struct ViT + decoder-only). Four of five use SigLIP encoders, letting SigLIP serve as a controlled architectural factor; Kosmos-2.5 is the non-SigLIP control.

**Component-wise quantization configurations (6, defined in Table 2):** cfg0 = all FP16 baseline; cfg1 = LLM INT4 only; cfg2 = Projector INT8 only; cfg3 = cfg1+cfg2; cfg4 = Vision INT8 only; cfg5 = cfg1+cfg4. Single-component ablations isolate marginal effects; composite configs test error additivity. Quantization is done with BitsAndBytes INT8/INT4. All models use greedy decoding with fixed max-new-tokens per model wrapper (Table 13); prompts/templates/exact-match answer extraction are held constant across all configs.

**Hardware (2 heterogeneous edge SoCs):** Jetson Orin NX (1024-core Ampere, 16GB LPDDR5, 102.4 GB/s, 25W TDP) and Jetson Orin AGX (2048-core Ampere, 64GB LPDDR5, 204.8 GB/s, 50W TDP), both Jetpack 6.2.1. The Jetson Orin Nano was excluded for consistent OOM failures.

**Benchmark & metrics:** MME (perception+cognition, max 2800). Metrics decomposed into accuracy, peak VRAM (GB), and latency split per-component — vision encoder latency, projector latency, and LLM TPOT (time per output token, prefill excluded). Power is measured via NVIDIA `tegrastats` at 50ms sampling; energy is power×end-to-end latency. Intelligence-per-joule IPJ = normalized MME accuracy (Score/2800) per joule. The profiling pseudocode (Algorithm 1) instruments CUDA sync points around each component.

**Additivity analysis:** For composite configs, the paper compares the actual ΔMME against the expected sum of the constituent single-component ΔMMEs (Tables 9-10) and computes residuals.

**Diagnostic profiling:** Isolated vision-path profiling with NVTX/Nsight Systems (50 iterations) to confirm the SigLIP INT8 anomaly is not a decoding-loop artifact; plus a non-BitsAndBytes AWQ W4A16 sanity check (calibrated on 512 Flickr30k samples) on Qwen3-VL to test whether the TPOT penalty is BitsAndBytes-specific.

**Validation:** Baseline MME scores are cross-checked against the Open VLM Leaderboard (Table 14); three models match closely, the other two differ because the paper uses newer versions (Qwen3-VL vs Qwen2-VL; Kosmos-2.5 vs Kosmos-2).

## Results & Highlights

- **MoE vs Dense under INT4 LLM quantization (cfg1, MME, Table 5):** The MoE-backed Qwen3-VL-2B improves by +56.04 points on NX and +101.88 on AGX; DeepSeek-VL2-Tiny (MoE) improves by +39.50 (NX) / +48.18 (AGX). In contrast, dense backbones collapse: PaliGemma2-3B -62.12 (NX) / -59.35 (AGX), and LLaVA-OV-0.5B (0.49B) drops -220.02 (NX) / -156.08 (AGX). This falsifies the scale-dependent H1 and supports the reformulated H1rev that architecture paradigm (MoE vs dense) dominates scale.
- **SigLIP vision INT8 latency anomaly (cfg4, Table 6):** Vision INT8 inflates vision encoding latency by ×2.43-4.66 for the three SigLIP-So400m models (PaliGemma2 ×4.66 on AGX, DeepSeek-VL2 ×3.02-3.11, LLaVA-OV ×2.43-2.61), only ×1.73-2.51 for the SigLIP-2-based Qwen3-VL, and just ×1.15-1.20 for the non-SigLIP Pix2Struct Kosmos-2.5 — and these latency increases occur with little or no accuracy change (PaliGemma2 +7.3, LLaVA-OV +7.4 ΔMME). Isolated Nsight profiling (Table 16) reproduces the slowdown (PaliGemma2 ×4.93 AGX / ×4.32 NX) and shows quant/dequant kernels account for 22.8-25.7% of INT8 kernel time with a 7.4× rise in kernel launches (Table 17).
- **LLM INT4 VRAM vs TPOT trade-off (cfg1, Table 7):** VRAM is reduced 21.8-47.5% (e.g., Qwen3-VL NX 4.06→2.13 GB, -47.5%), but TPOT increases 10.4-56.3% (e.g., Qwen3-VL NX 111.1→173.1 ms, +55.8%; LLaVA-OV AGX 90.5→141.4 ms, +56.3%). This supports H3: VRAM savings do not translate to latency gains on Jetson Ampere because BitsAndBytes dequantization overhead outweighs INT4's native speedup.
- **Energy paradox (cfg1, Table 8):** Despite lower VRAM, energy consumption rises — Qwen3-VL +54.7% (NX) / +45.3% (AGX), PaliGemma2 +27.7% / +21.2% — because longer dequantization time increases power draw; only the very small LLaVA-OV (0.49B) is nearly flat (+1.4-1.9%).
- **Composite quantization additivity (Tables 9-10):** Projector INT8 + LLM INT4 (cfg3) is near-additive — all residuals within ±4 points (max DeepSeek-VL2 -3.56). But Vision INT8 + LLM INT4 (cfg5) is architecture-dependent and non-additive: PaliGemma2 has an extra -16.33 residual beyond the expected sum, while DeepSeek-VL2 shows a +8.03 super-additive (better-than-expected) residual.
- **Platform-invariant accuracy ranking (H5, Tables 11-12):** MME ranking is identical on NX and AGX across all configs: Qwen3-VL (2021.78/1983.32) > DeepSeek-VL2 (1911.12/1916.71) > PaliGemma2 (1661.01/1658.10) > LLaVA-OV (1361.83/1369.73) > Kosmos-2.5 (662.30/662.05). But IPJ is highly platform-specific: Qwen3-VL cfg0 reaches IPJ 14.45 on AGX vs 5.87 on NX (~2.5×), and cfg1 AGX 10.38 vs NX 3.90.
- **Non-BitsAndBytes AWQ sanity check (Table 18, Qwen3-VL):** AWQ W4A16 nearly preserves MME (2020.93 vs FP16 2021.78) and does NOT reproduce the TPOT penalty (107.5 ms vs BnB INT4 173.1 ms), indicating the TPOT increase is tied to the BitsAndBytes execution path, not to 4-bit quantization inherently — though AWQ does not reduce peak VRAM (4.08 GB), suggesting peak allocation is dominated by runtime activations/KV cache rather than weight storage.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The contribution is a careful measurement/analysis study rather than a new algorithm. The genuinely novel and paper-specific findings are (a) the MoE-vs-dense dominance over scale for INT4 LLM sensitivity (falsifying their own H1 and reformulating it to H1rev), and (b) the isolated Nsight-level root cause of the SigLIP-BitsAndBytes-Ampere anomaly (22.8-25.7% of kernel time in quant/dequant, 7.4× kernel-launch inflation) — a diagnosis that is specific enough to be actionable. The component-wise ablation framework itself is a reasonable but incremental extension of prior VLM quantization surveys (Sharshar et al. 2025; Li et al. MBQ 2025; Xue et al. VLMQ 2025).
- **Rigor:** Strong in places. They pre-register hypotheses, report n≥3 runs, hold decoding/decoding params fixed, exclude the prefill phase to isolate TPOT, and corroborate the vision anomaly with a non-SigLIP Kosmos-2.5 control and a separate isolated Nsight profiling run (Appendix E). The AWQ sanity check (Appendix F) honestly shows the TPOT penalty is BitsAndBytes-specific, not a property of 4-bit quantization in general — which actually narrows their own H3 claim.
- **Weak baselines / scope concerns:** The entire study is locked to BitsAndBytes quantization. This is a major limitation because the headline findings (SigLIP INT8 anomaly, TPOT penalty) are explicitly shown to be BitsAndBytes-specific rather than inherent — meaning the deployment guidelines are really "BitsAndBytes-on-Jetson-Orin" guidelines, not general INT8/INT4-on-edge guidelines. AWQ W4A16 and GPTQ are mentioned in related work but only AWQ gets a single-model sanity check; FP8, W8A8, and hardware-native formats are left to future work. The authors acknowledge this in Limitations but it significantly bounds the generality of the conclusions.
- **Single benchmark:** All accuracy claims rest solely on MME (perception + cognition, Yes/No format). There is no second VLM benchmark (e.g., MMMU, MMBench, SEED, TextVQA), so the accuracy-transferability claim (H5) is demonstrated only on one benchmark's score distribution — an MMCU/MMBench cross-check would have substantially strengthened the "platform-invariant ranking" conclusion.
- **Potentially misleading accuracy "improvements":** The MoE models improving under INT4 (Qwen3-VL +56.04) is a striking but slightly suspicious result — INT4 quantization rarely improves accuracy. The paper attributes it to MoE sparse activation localizing quantization noise, but offers no ablation isolating the MoE routing/gate as the cause, and the improvement may partly reflect BitsAndBytes numerical quirks or MME's coarse Yes/No scoring being within run-to-run noise (no per-config variance/CI is reported for ΔMME, only n≥3 means in bar charts in Appendix C). The critical reader should treat the sign of these deltas cautiously; the magnitude and ranking are the more robust claims.
- **Cherry-picking risk in the latency anomaly story:** The SigLIP anomaly is well-supported, but the framing emphasizes the worst case (×4.66) repeatedly; the SigLIP-2-based Qwen3-VL only slows ×1.73-2.51, which shows the effect is encoder-version-specific within SigLIP, slightly undercutting the clean "SigLIP-BitsAndBytes-Ampere" label.
- **Reproducibility:** Good in principle — fixed decoding, leaderboard validation, released profiling tool, anonymous repo. But the repo is anonymous.4open.science (not a durable open-science repository), no seed or exact quantization revision is pinned, and the Nsight profiling scope differs per model (DeepSeek-VL2-Tiny is measured on a different path and "not strictly comparable," which the authors flag).
- **Significance:** For practitioners deploying small VLMs on Jetson Orin, the practical takeaways (treat LLM INT4 as VRAM-saving not latency-saving; avoid BitsAndBytes INT8 on SigLIP vision encoders; prefer MoE backbones for INT4; re-validate cfg5 per architecture; NX vs AGX accuracy transfers but energy does not) are genuinely useful and underdocumented. For the broader research community, it is a solid empirical study with a notable diagnosis, not a breakthrough method.

## Strengths

- Hypothesis-driven design with explicit falsification (H1 rejected → H1rev, H2 narrowed to a deployment-specific interpretation, H4 refined to "cfg3 additive / cfg5 architecture-dependent") — intellectually honest and rare in empirical ML papers.
- The isolated Nsight Systems profiling (Appendix E, Tables 16-17) with a non-SigLIP control gives a concrete kernel-level root cause (quant/dequant kernel share, kernel-launch inflation) rather than a hand-wavy "SigLIP is slow" claim.
- Cross-hardware evaluation on two genuinely heterogeneous edge SoCs (NX 25W/102.4 GB/s vs AGX 50W/204.8 GB/s), with the IPJ metric making the energy vs accuracy trade-off explicit.
- Transparent validation: baseline scores checked against the Open VLM Leaderboard, and the AWQ sanity check (Appendix F) honestly demonstrates the TPOT penalty is a BitsAndBytes-runtime artifact, not an inherent property of 4-bit weights.
- Practical, decision-relevant deployment guidelines: which component to quantize first, when INT4 helps or hurts, and why accuracy transfers across platforms but energy does not.

## Limitations

- **Quantization backend lock-in:** The entire component-wise study uses BitsAndBytes; the two headline anomalies (SigLIP INT8 latency, TPOT penalty) are explicitly shown to be BitsAndBytes-specific by the AWQ check, so the conclusions do not generalize to GPTQ/AWQ/FP8/W8A8 or hardware-native formats — a scope the authors themselves flag as future work.
- **Single accuracy benchmark (MME only):** no MMMU/MMBench/SEED/TextVQA cross-check, so accuracy-transferability (H5) is established on one benchmark. MME's Yes/No format with short answers also makes the scoring coarse, which complicates interpreting small ΔMME deltas as genuine accuracy changes.
- **No variance/CI on ΔMME:** despite n≥3 runs, only means are reported in the main tables (bar charts in Appendix C), so the surprising INT4 accuracy improvements (Qwen3-VL +56.04) cannot be assessed for statistical significance against run-to-run noise.
- **MoE-improvement mechanism unexplained:** the paper attributes MoE robustness to sparse activation localizing quantization noise but provides no ablation isolating the MoE gate/routing as the cause.
- **Anonymous/non-durable code link** and no pinned seeds/quantization revisions; DeepSeek-VL2-Tiny Nsight profiling is on a different scope than the other models.
- **Limited to PTQ:** only post-training quantization is considered; QAT and mixed-precision NAS for automatic per-component bit-width allocation are left to future work.

## Relevance

对于关注边缘端/设备端多模态大模型部署、模型压缩与量化、以及小型 VLM 的研究者而言，本文提供了一套可直接复用的组件级量化消融框架与硬件感知部署决策依据，尤其契合"如何在 Jetson 等边缘 SoC 上为 VLM 的视觉编码器/投影器/LLM 骨干分配合适比特位精度"这一工程问题，并揭示了 MoE 架构在 INT4 量化下的鲁棒性优势这一有价值的实证结论。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the MoE-vs-dense INT4 sensitivity findings (and the surprising MoE accuracy *improvement*) replicate on additional benchmarks (MMMU, MMBench, SEED-Bench, TextVQA) and with confidence intervals, or are they artifacts of MME's coarse Yes/No scoring?
- Is there an ablation that isolates the MoE gate/routing mechanism as the source of INT4 robustness (e.g., freezing gate weights, or comparing a dense vs MoE variant of the same backbone at matched parameter count)?
- How do the SigLIP INT8 latency anomaly and the LLM INT4 TPOT penalty change under hardware-native quantization formats (FP8 on newer Jetson/Grace, W8A8, or TensorRT-LLM kernels) versus BitsAndBytes — can the anomaly be eliminated entirely?
- Does the near-additivity of cfg3 (Projector INT8 + LLM INT4) and the architecture-dependent non-additivity of cfg5 (Vision INT8 + LLM INT4) extend to a closed-form predictor for composite quantization error, enabling automatic per-component bit-width allocation via NAS?
- How do these edge-deployment findings transfer to non-Jetson edge accelerators (e.g., Apple Neural Engine, Qualcomm Hexagon, ARM Ethos), where memory bandwidth and kernel availability differ substantially?
- Given that AWQ W4A16 preserves accuracy and avoids the TPOT penalty but does not reduce peak VRAM, what runtime/KV-cache optimizations are needed to make 4-bit quantization yield simultaneous VRAM, latency, and energy wins on Jetson Orin?
