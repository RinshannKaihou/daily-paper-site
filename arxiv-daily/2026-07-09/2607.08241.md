> **一句话总结：** 本文指出 post-training quantization (PTQ) 文献普遍把 classifier-free guidance (CFG) 扩散模型当作单分支网络校准，从而忽略了双分支结构；作者形式化证明了"分支漂移陷阱"（branch-drift trap）——仅对 guidance gap 校准的目标存在精确零空间，使量化模型可在 gap 保真度诊断近完美的同时产生最差的样本质量，并提出 GAMP（Guidance-Aware Mixed Precision），直接在 guided prediction 上校准、按各层对 guided 输出的敏感度经贪心背包分配激活比特，在 CIFAR-10 / DASH 6.1M UNet 上以 6.03 平均激活比特取得 FID 39.4，比同等平均精度的均匀分配参考（FID≈76.5）改进 49%，并以 28.3 G BOPs 达到接近 W4A8（32.7 G）的质量。

> **TL;DR:** This paper shows that standard post-training quantization (PTQ) methods treat classifier-free guidance (CFG) diffusion models as single-branch networks, ignoring the paired conditional/unconditional forward passes, and formally proves a "branch-drift trap": a gap-only calibration objective admits an exact null space where a quantized model achieves near-perfect gap-fidelity diagnostics yet produces the worst sample quality — confirmed on **CIFAR-10** with the DASH 6.1M ADM UNet, where **Gap-only-W4A8 scores the best gap alignment (ρ=1.004, cos(∆)=0.882) but the worst FID (334.24)**, far worse than the MSE-W4A8 baseline's FID 30.62. To close the trap, the authors propose **GAMP**, which calibrates directly on the guided prediction and allocates activation bits via a greedy knapsack over guided-output sensitivity; **GAMP-b̄6 reaches FID 39.4 at 6.03 average activation bits, a 49% FID improvement over the uniform-spending reference (≈76.5) at matched precision**, while GAMP-b̄5 hits FID 40.0 at 13% fewer BOPs than W4A8. The paper also profiles a consistent ~1.99× CFG two-pass latency tax on an NVIDIA T4 and a 147× INT8 slowdown under ONNX Runtime (no TensorRT) due to 192 operator-fallback memcpy nodes.

## 核心贡献

- **CFG 延迟刻画（系统层面）**：在 NVIDIA T4 上跨批大小与浮点格式实测 50 步 CFG-DDIM 的双 pass 引导推理开销，发现一致的 ~1.99× "CFG 税"，该开销被所有 BOPs 与参数量指标完全隐藏；同时诊断出商品化 INT8 推理栈失败（无 TensorRT 的 ONNX Runtime 下 147× 减速）的精确根因——192 个算子回退 memcpy 节点。
- **分支漂移陷阱的形式化定理与实证确认**：证明仅对 guidance gap 校准的 PTQ 目标存在精确零空间（Proposition 1）：任意偏移向量 δ 都可使 gap 误差为零同时让无条件分支任意漂移；并证明该漂移直接 1:1 污染 guided 预测（Proposition 2），与 guidance scale 无关。通过受控消融实证：gap 诊断最佳的配置（ρ=1.004, cos(∆)=0.882）反而产生最差样本质量（FID 334）。
- **GAMP（Guidance-Aware Mixed Precision）方法**：直接在 guided prediction ε̃ 上校准（LGAMP=‖ε̃q−ε̃fp‖²），按各层对 guided 输出误差的贡献度（Eq. 4 的 E(l)）经贪心背包分配激活比特（权重固定 4-bit），从构造上消除共享漂移零空间。
- **部署指南**：为 HPEC 实践者给出三条具体建议——报告 guided-step BOPs（=2× 单 pass）；用 guided-prediction 误差而非仅 gap 诊断校验 PTQ；当激活精度悬崖落在压缩区间内时按 GAMP 敏感度分配激活比特。

## Key Contributions

- **CFG latency characterization (system level).** Empirically measures the two-pass guided-inference overhead of 50-step CFG-DDIM on an NVIDIA T4 across batch sizes (1/16/64) and floating-point formats (FP32/FP16), revealing a consistent ~1.99× "CFG tax" that all BOPs and parameter-count metrics completely conceal; additionally diagnoses the precise root cause of commodity INT8 inference failure (147× slowdown under ONNX Runtime without TensorRT) as 192 GPU↔CPU operator-fallback memcpy nodes, not an arithmetic limitation.
- **Branch-drift trap: formal theorem + empirical confirmation.** Proves (Proposition 1) that a gap-only PTQ calibration objective L_gap = ‖(ε_q^y − ε_q^∅) − (ε_fp^y − ε_fp^∅)‖² admits an exact null space: any shared drift vector δ (ε_q^y = ε_fp^y + δ, ε_q^∅ = ε_fp^∅ + δ) achieves L_gap = 0 while the unconditional branch drifts unboundedly. Proposition 2 shows this drift corrupts the guided prediction one-to-one (ε̃_q − ε̃_fp = δ), independent of guidance scale w, and accumulates as O(K‖δ‖) over the trajectory. A controlled ablation confirms Corollary 1: the best-calibrated model by gap diagnostics simultaneously yields the worst sample quality (FID 334).
- **GAMP (Guidance-Aware Mixed Precision) method.** A PTQ method that calibrates directly on the guided prediction (L_GAMP = ‖ε̃_q − ε̃_fp‖², Eq. 5), derives per-layer activation-bit sensitivity from guided-output degradation E(l) = ‖ε̃_q^l − ε̃_fp^l‖², and allocates bits via a greedy knapsack ranked by sensitivity-per-BOPs. Weights are fixed at 4-bit; the null space is closed by construction because E(l) = 0 requires δ = 0. The greedy knapsack is provably near-optimal given small per-layer BOPs increments (~1 G BOPs each against a ~32 G total).
- **Deployment guidelines for practitioners.** Three concrete recommendations: (1) report guided-step BOPs (= 2× single-pass) since single-pass metrics overstate gains by a factor of two; (2) validate PTQ calibration with guided-prediction error rather than gap diagnostics alone; (3) allocate activation bits via GAMP sensitivity when the activation precision cliff falls within the compression regime.

## Methodology

**Setup.** Experiments use the publicly released DASH [5] student checkpoints: a 6.1M-parameter ADM UNet (base channels 64, one residual block per scale, attention at 16×16) trained on CIFAR-10 (32×32 RGB). Sampling uses K = 50 DDIM steps with timestep-adaptive guidance (TAG) w = 4.0 [5], single-seed 50k-sample FID/IS protocol (torch-fidelity, cifar10-train reference). FP32 single-seed FID is 13.67 (DASH reports 8.87 as a multi-seed mean from its own pipeline); single-seed variance is ±2–3 FID at 50k samples.

**The branch-drift trap (null-space analysis).** The natural PTQ objective for a CFG model minimizes guidance-gap reconstruction error (Eq. 2). The paper proves this is structurally insufficient: Proposition 1 shows that for any vector δ, the assignment ε_q^y = ε_fp^y + δ, ε_q^∅ = ε_fp^∅ + δ achieves L_gap = 0 because the gap difference cancels δ exactly, leaving the unconditional branch drift entirely unconstrained. Proposition 2 then shows the guided-prediction error ε̃_q − ε̃_fp = δ, independent of guidance scale w. Corollary 1: ρ = 1 and cos(∆) = 1 are simultaneously achievable with ‖δ‖ ≫ 0. The trap is structural (not an optimization pathology) because PTQ has only O(L) degrees of freedom (one clipping range per quantizable layer) and no gradient mechanism over unfrozen weights to suppress δ — unlike DASH's distillation setting, where an explicit unconditional branch loss over >200K gradient iterations closes the null space automatically. At larger guidance scales (w ~ 7–15 in text-to-image), |ε̃| ∝ w‖∆‖ grows while δ stays fixed, making the trap intensify.

**GAMP algorithm (Algorithm 1, Fig. 1).** Five-step pipeline: (1) Dual-branch calibration through both conditional and unconditional branches to record per-layer activation statistics — this prevents the clipping underestimation that single-pass calibration incurs because the conditional branch has systematically wider activation ranges. (2) Cache the FP32 guided output ε̃_fp = ε_fp^∅ + w(ε_fp^y − ε_fp^∅) for all 1024 calibration images. (3) Per-layer sensitivity profiling: temporarily set each of the L = 49 quantizable layers to A4 and measure the resulting guided-output error E(l), requiring 49 forward passes (~5 min on T4, one-time). (4) Greedy bit allocation: rank layers by sensitivity-per-BOPs (s_l / ∆BOPs(l)), start all at A4, and promote A4→A8 by rank until the target average bit budget b̄ is met (a 0/1 knapsack). (5) Threshold calibration via L_GAMP over 800 Adam iterations. Inference cost equals standard mixed-precision PTQ; the ~22 min overhead is one-time offline.

**Quantization details.** Weights quantized per-output-channel (symmetric, signed) — substantially reduces clipping error at W4; activations quantized per-tensor (asymmetric, unsigned) with MSE-optimal clipping thresholds; biases and normalization parameters remain FP32; conv_in/conv_out remain FP32 (standard diffusion PTQ practice). AdaRound-style learnable weight rounding with MSE-optimal activation clipping over 800 iterations using 1024 calibration images from CIFAR-10 train.

**Baselines.** (i) RTN: round-to-nearest, no calibration; (ii) MSE-CAL: AdaRound + MSE activation clipping, dual-branch calibration, uniform precision; (iii) GAP-ONLY: gap objective (Eq. 2) as the sole calibration target — the critical ablation validating Corollary 1; (iv) GAMP at b̄ ∈ {5, 6}.

## Results & Highlights

- **Branch-drift trap empirically confirmed (the headline false positive).** On CIFAR-10 (50k FID, K=50, w=4.0), Gap-only-W4A8 achieves the **best guidance-gap alignment of any quantized configuration (ρ = 1.004, cos(∆) = 0.882)** yet simultaneously produces the **worst sample quality of the entire sweep: FID 334.24, IS 1.26** — exceeding even the W4A4 model's FID 272.78 and far worse than the MSE-W4A8 baseline's FID 30.62. This is the exact false positive predicted by Corollary 1: gap diagnostics are near-perfect while the guided prediction is severely corrupted by a shared-drift δ that compounds over 50 DDIM steps to produce near-random samples.
- **GAMP Pareto efficiency at matched average precision.** GAMP-b̄6 achieves **FID 39.40 at 6.03 average activation bits and 31.3 G BOPs per guided step**. A linear reference connecting the two measured uniform endpoints (MSE-W4A4: FID 122.36; MSE-W4A8: FID 30.62) predicts FID ≈ 76.5 at 6 average bits; **GAMP achieves 39.4, a 49% improvement over the uniform-spending reference at matched average precision**. GAMP-b̄5 reaches **FID 40.01 at 28.3 G BOPs (13% below W4A8's 32.7 G)** — near-W4A8 quality at lower compute (the uniform reference at avgA=5 predicts FID ≈ 99). Notably GAMP achieves lower FID despite weaker gap scores (ρ = 1.509, cos(∆) = 0.603), reinforcing that guided-output error, not gap fidelity, governs sample quality.
- **CFG latency tax is ~1.99× and invisible to standard metrics.** Measured 50-step CFG-DDIM latency on T4 shows a consistent ~1.99× guided-vs-unguided overhead across all batch sizes and formats (e.g., FP32 B=1: 1042.6 ms guided vs 534.2 ms unguided = 1.95×; FP32 B=64: 3936.2 ms vs 1988.5 ms = 1.98×; FP16 B=64: 3107.7 ms vs 1569.8 ms = 1.98×). A W4A8 model reporting 32.7 G guided-step BOPs carries an implicit additional 32.7 G BOPs of guidance overhead that no prior compression paper credits.
- **INT8 deployment gap: 147× slowdown, not an arithmetic limit.** W8A8 BOPs predict a 16× speedup over FP32, but ORT INT8 without TensorRT delivers single-pass latency of **4598 ms vs PyTorch FP16's 31.2 ms — a ~147× slowdown (0.007×)**. Root cause: absence of libnvinfer.so.10 prevents dispatch to INT8 tensor cores, producing 192 GPU↔CPU memcpy fallback nodes (one per quantized operator) plus UNet downsample Slice ops that resist dynamic INT8 quantization and run at FP32. Weight quantization succeeds (3.8× disk reduction: 6.4 vs 24.5 MB); the bottleneck is inference-stack support, not arithmetic capability.
- **Activation precision cliff (motivates GAMP's asymmetry).** Reducing weight precision W8→W4 at fixed A8 raises FID by 20.1 (MSE-W8A8 10.53 → MSE-W4A8 30.62); reducing activation precision A8→A4 at fixed W4 raises FID by 91.8 (30.62 → 122.36) — **a 4.6× steeper per-bit degradation**, confirming activations are the precision-critical resource for CFG diffusion and directly motivating GAMP's strategy of fixing weights at 4-bit and allocating activation bits non-uniformly.
- **Quality ceiling.** MSE-W8A8 FID/IS (10.53/8.89) marginally exceed FP32 (13.67/8.61) owing to single-seed variance (±2–3 FID at 50k samples) and mild AdaRound regularization; FP32 remains the quality ceiling.

## Authors & Credibility

The two authors (Abdullah Al Shafi, Sumaiya Rahim Suma) are from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Khulna University of Engineering & Technology, Bangladesh. The paper directly extends the authors' own prior work DASH (arXiv:2606.00798, 2026), a dual-branch score distillation method that introduced the ρ and cos(∆) gap-fidelity diagnostics — here reused as the diagnostic baselines whose insufficiency for PTQ is the paper's central argument. The methodology is transparent about a known limitation: the FP32 reference FID (13.67) differs from DASH's reported multi-seed mean (8.87), and the authors flag the single-seed variance (±2–3 FID) explicitly rather than hiding it. The theoretical claims (Propositions 1–2, Corollary 1) are short, self-contained, and reproducible from Eq. (1) by substitution. The work is positioned as an arXiv preprint (cs.CV, v1, 9 Jul 2026) with no peer-review indication.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: genuinely high on the conceptual contribution.** The branch-drift trap is a real and cleanly articulated insight: the formal proof that gap-only PTQ calibration admits an exact null space (Proposition 1), with the shared-drift assignment ε_q^y = ε_fp^y + δ, ε_q^∅ = ε_fp^∅ + δ trivially canceling in the gap, is a crisp and correct result. The recognition that DASH's distillation-time diagnostics (ρ, cos(∆)) become actively misleading as PTQ calibration objectives is the most valuable idea in the paper.
- **Rigor: the trap proof is airtight, but the empirical case is narrow.** The propositions are correct and short. However, the entire empirical validation rests on a single small model (6.1M-parameter ADM UNet) on a single low-resolution dataset (CIFAR-10, 32×32) at a single moderate guidance scale (w = 4.0) with a single random seed. The authors themselves acknowledge the FP32 reference FID (13.67) is notably worse than DASH's reported 8.87, which weakens the absolute quality framing. Crucially, **no error bars or multi-seed runs are reported** despite the stated ±2–3 FID variance; some of the closer comparisons (GAMP-b̄5 FID 40.01 vs GAMP-b̄6 FID 39.40) sit within the acknowledged noise band, so the b̄5-vs-b̄6 ordering is not demonstrably significant.
- **The headline GAMP "49% FID improvement" needs scrutiny.** The 49% gain is measured against a *linear interpolation between two uniform-precision endpoints* (MSE-W4A4 at 4 bits, MSE-W4A8 at 8 bits) — a synthetic reference line, not an actual baseline method. The only real uniform mixed-precision baselines (BRECQ, HAWQ-style Hessian allocation) are *discussed in related work but never run*. The fair comparison would be against existing per-layer mixed-precision allocators using the same W4/A8 building blocks; without it, the claim that GAMP's guided-output sensitivity beats "uniform spending" is demonstrated, but the claim that it beats *prior mixed-precision sensitivity criteria* is not.
- **The branch-drift trap ablation is somewhat self-fulfilling.** Gap-only-W4A8 is constructed as the deliberate adversarial case (gap objective as the *sole* target). Real-world PTQ pipelines (Q-Diffusion, PTQD) use per-block or per-tensor MSE reconstruction, which the paper argues are "gap-like" but never directly benchmarks against — so the claim that existing methods are vulnerable to the trap is asserted by analogy, not measured.
- **Reproducibility: good on the algorithm, weak on deployment claims.** Algorithm 1 is fully specified; the 1024-image CIFAR-10 calibration set, 800 Adam iterations, and L = 49 layers are concrete. DASH checkpoints are public. However, the T4 latency and 147× INT8 results are tied to a specific software environment (Kaggle T4 without libnvinfer.so.10), and the paper does not report whether TensorRT-enabled inference realizes the BOPs-predicted speedups — leaving the deployment-guideline value of the INT8 diagnosis conditional on stack configuration.
- **Significance.** If the branch-drift trap generalizes to latent-space CFG models (Stable Diffusion, DiT) at production guidance scales w ~ 7–15 — which the authors argue analytically but do not demonstrate — the null-space theorem would be a meaningful contribution to PTQ practice. At present it is a promising, well-argued hypothesis validated on a deliberately minimal setup.
- **Red flags.** None malicious, but two disclosure concerns: (1) the comparison reference for the 49% gain is a synthetic linear baseline, which could be misread as a gain over a real method; (2) the absence of multi-seed error bars on a setup with acknowledged ±2–3 FID variance means some quantitative claims are within noise. No cherry-picked benchmarks in the malicious sense — the CIFAR-10 choice is justified as a controlled setting, and the single-model choice is disclosed as a limitation.

## Strengths

- The branch-drift trap is a genuinely novel, clearly proven, and practically important structural insight for CFG-aware PTQ — the kind of "obvious in hindsight" result that explains why naïve gap calibration can fail silently.
- The paper is unusually honest about its own setup limitations (single seed, single model, the 13.67-vs-8.87 FP32 discrepancy, the INT8 result being stack-specific).
- The system-level diagnosis (1.99× CFG tax invisible to BOPs; 147× INT8 slowdown traced to 192 memcpy fallback nodes) is concrete and actionable for deployment practitioners, independent of the algorithmic contribution.
- The methodology cleanly isolates the failure mode: the Gap-only-W4A8 ablation is a textbook controlled experiment that turns Corollary 1 from an abstract worry into a measured false positive (ρ = 1.004, cos(∆) = 0.882 → FID 334.24).
- GAMP's design is principled — calibrating on the exact quantity CFG sampling depends on (ε̃) rather than a proxy, and the greedy knapsack's near-optimality is justified by the small-increment argument rather than asserted.

## Limitations

- **Scale and scope.** Experiments are restricted to 32×32 pixel-space diffusion (CIFAR-10) on a compact 6.1M-parameter student UNet. No validation on Stable Diffusion, DiT, or other latent-space CFG architectures; no high-resolution evaluation. The authors flag this explicitly as future work.
- **Single seed, no error bars.** Despite acknowledging ±2–3 FID variance at 50k samples, all results use a single seed. Several comparisons (notably GAMP-b̄5 40.01 vs GAMP-b̄6 39.40; MSE-W8A8 10.53 vs FP32 13.67) fall within this noise band.
- **Weak/absent mixed-precision baselines.** The 49% GAMP gain is measured against a synthetic linear interpolation, not against real per-layer mixed-precision allocators (BRECQ, HAWQ) that the paper itself discusses — those are never run.
- **Guidance-scale range.** All quality experiments use w = 4.0; the analytically predicted trap intensification at text-to-image scales (w ~ 7–15) is not empirically demonstrated.
- **Deployment claims are stack-conditional.** The 147× INT8 slowdown is specific to ONNX Runtime without TensorRT on a Kaggle T4; whether TensorRT or newer accelerators (H100) realize the BOPs speedup is asserted, not measured.
- **No direct instrumentation of δ.** The paper confirms the trap indirectly (via FID collapse with good gap scores) but does not directly measure ‖ε_q^∅ − ε_fp^∅‖ to isolate the shared-drift mechanism from other unconstrained error patterns — something the authors note as a natural extension.

## Relevance

本文对研究扩散模型推理部署与量化（PTQ）的工作高度相关：其形式化证明的"分支漂移陷阱"揭示了 classifier-free guidance 双分支结构下 gap-only 校准的精确零空间，并指出 GAMP 按 guided-output 敏感度分配激活比特可在构造上规避该陷阱——这是任何想把 PTQ 方法迁移到 CFG/Stable Diffusion 类模型上的研究者都需要警惕的失效模式，尤其对混合精度量化、推理效率优化与扩散模型压缩方向有直接指导价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the branch-drift trap manifest empirically at text-to-image guidance scales (w ~ 7–15) on Stable Diffusion / DiT, and does GAMP's advantage over uniform precision grow with w as the Proposition 2 analysis predicts (|ε̃| ∝ w‖∆‖ while δ stays fixed)?
- How does GAMP compare against real per-layer mixed-precision allocators (BRECQ block-reconstruction sensitivity, HAWQ Hessian-curvature allocation) using the same W4/A8 building blocks, rather than against a synthetic linear interpolation?
- Can directly instrumenting ‖ε_q^∅ − ε_fp^∅‖ during calibration isolate the shared-drift mechanism from other unconstrained error patterns, and would adding an unconditional anchor term λ‖ε_q^∅ − ε_fp^∅‖² to L_gap (as the authors propose) close the null space more cheaply than full guided-output calibration?
- Does combining GAMP's per-layer allocation with PTQD-style timestep-aware mixed precision (which targets trajectory accumulation, a complementary error source) yield additive gains, or do the two error sources interact?
- On TensorRT-enabled or H100-class accelerators where INT8 tensor-core dispatch is functional, does the BOPs-predicted 16× speedup materialize, or do the Slice/downsample and memcpy artifacts persist as architecture-independent bottlenecks?
