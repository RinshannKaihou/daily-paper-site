> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示并修复了开源硬件 PLC（如 Arduino 类设备运行 IEC 61131-3）形式验证中的"部署鸿沟"——现有验证器在抽象模型（无界整数）上证明安全，但设备实际运行 16 位算术且输入受有限分辨率 ADC 约束；在 123 个真实第三方程序上，朴素的 16 位溢出检查产生 44%（54/123）的误报且零真实缺陷，而作者从 PLCopen 地址自动推导的 HAL 输入模型消除了全部 54 个误报（54→0）并保留全部 32 个鲁棒性证明，且复用了 ESBMC 的 SMT 后端与 k-induction 引擎，仅用约 40 行钩子代码实现。

> **TL;DR:** This paper identifies and closes a "deployment gap" in formal verification of open-hardware PLCs: verifiers such as ESBMC-PLC prove safety over an abstract model with unbounded integers, but the deployed binary runs 16-bit arithmetic with finite-resolution ADC inputs. On a **123-program third-party corpus** (water-treatment LD + IEC 61131-3 clone-detection), **naive 16-bit overflow checking is unusable (44% false alarms, 54/123, zero true defects)**, while the authors' automatically-derived **HAL input model eliminates all 54 false alarms (54→0) and preserves all 32 robustness proofs**, reusing ESBMC's SMT backend and k-induction unchanged through a ~40-line hook in the LD frontend. A controlled corpus plus cross-tool replication on CBMC 6.10.0 and Frama-C/EVA 32.1 confirm the gap is real, input-driven (not tool-specific), and that the phantom alarms vanish only when input bounds are supplied — something the method derives automatically from PLCopen `%IW`/`%IX` addresses rather than asking engineers to annotate by hand.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"部署鸿沟"（deployment gap）概念并形式化定义**：现有开源 IEC 61131-3 验证器（ESBMC-PLC、PLCverif、Arcade.PLC）在抽象扫描周期模型上用理想化无界整数与无约束输入证明安全；但真实部署在 8 位 AVR 板（Arduino Uno/Nano/Mega）上的工件执行 16 位机器字算术、输入经 10 位 ADC 读取（范围 [0,1023]），导致"在抽象上证明安全的程序在设备上可能溢出"（漏报）或"验证器报告 ADC 不可能产生的传感器值溢出"（幻影误报）。
- **提出硬件忠实（hardware-faithful）验证方法**：给定程序 P 与声明式 HAL 描述符 H=⟨w, b_adc, b_pwm, φ⟩（机器字宽、ADC/PWM 分辨率、I/O 映射），构造部署模型 M_dev(P,H)，在目标字宽解释算术并将每个输入约束到其硬件可实现范围；给出构造、健全性论证（Theorem 1 + Lemma 1）与间隙诊断过程。
- **关键创新——自动从 PLCopen 地址推导输入范围**：将嵌入式 C（Frama-C、Astrée）中需工程师手写契约/注释提供的输入边界，自动从 IEC 61131-3 直接寻址方案恢复（`%IW` 即模拟输入字，板描述符固定其 ADC 宽度），工程师无需写任何标注，这是可在语料库规模部署的关键。
- **实现 ESBMC-Arduino**：HAL 库参数取自官方 Arduino cores（AVR: int=16-bit, ADC=10, PWM=8；32 位板: int=32-bit, ADC=12–14），输入范围模型实现为 ESBMC LD 前端中约 40 行钩子，SMT 后端与 k-induction 引擎原样复用。
- **实证验证了朴素宽度感知检查的不可用性与 HAL 的修复**：在 123 个真实程序上 44% 误报且零真实缺陷 → HAL 消除全部 54 个误报并保留全部证明；并用受控语料库精确刻画哪些算术模式易受攻击（乘以 ≥33 的常数、累加、平方）。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes the "deployment gap" for open-hardware PLCs.** Existing open-source IEC 61131-3 verifiers (ESBMC-PLC, PLCverif, Arcade.PLC) prove safety over an abstract scan-cycle model with idealized unbounded integers and unconstrained inputs, but the artifact that runs on an 8-bit AVR board (Arduino Uno/Nano/Mega, CONTROLLINO, M-Duino) executes 16-bit machine-word arithmetic and reads sensors through a 10-bit ADC (range [0,1023]). The gap cuts both ways: a program safe in the abstract can overflow on the device (missed defect), and a naive word-width check explores sensor values no ADC can produce (phantom alarm).
- **A hardware-faithful verification method.** Given a program P and a declarative HAL descriptor H=⟨w, b_adc, b_pwm, φ⟩, the method constructs a deployment model M_dev(P,H) that interprets arithmetic at the target word width and constrains every input to its hardware-realizable range. Soundness is proved (Theorem 1) with a supporting non-linear over-approximation lemma; the gap-diagnosis procedure isolates width-attributable defects with physically realizable witnesses.
- **Automatic derivation of input bounds from PLCopen addresses** — the headline novelty. Unlike embedded-C analyzers (Frama-C ACSL contracts, Astrée `known_facts`) which require the engineer to supply input ranges by hand, this method recovers them from the IEC 61131-3 direct-addressing scheme itself: a `%IW` location is by the standard an analog input word, and the board descriptor fixes its ADC width. The engineer writes nothing, which is what makes the method deployable at corpus scale.
- **ESBMC-Arduino instantiation.** A HAL library whose parameters are sourced from the official Arduino cores (AVR: int=16-bit, ADC=10, PWM=8; 32-bit Due/UNO R4/OPTA: int=32-bit, ADC=12–14), with the input-range model realized as a ~40-line hook in the ESBMC LD frontend. The SMT backend and k-induction engine are reused unchanged, so Theorem 1 rests on the backend's existing soundness.
- **Empirically establishes that naive width-aware verification is unsound-in-practice, and that the HAL model repairs it.** On 123 real third-party programs: 44% false-alarm rate (54/123) with zero genuine defects under naive checking; the HAL annotator eliminates all 54 false alarms (54→0) while preserving every robustness proof. A controlled corpus characterizes exactly which arithmetic patterns are vulnerable.

## Methodology

The method's thesis is "what you verify must be what runs on the device," which requires modeling **both** the target word width **and** the hardware-realizable input ranges.

**Execution model.** A PLC program is modeled as a transition system T=(S, s_0, R) over a state partitioned into inputs I, outputs O, and internal state V. One scan re-samples every input, executes the program body once at width w, and checks safety property φ. The abstract model M_abs takes w large and D(i)=ℤ; the deployment model M_dev(P,H) takes both from H.

**HAL descriptor.** H=⟨w, b_adc, b_pwm, φ⟩ where w is the machine word width, b_adc/b_pwm are ADC/PWM resolutions in bits, and φ maps each IEC address class to a role/domain: φ(%IX)=⟨in,{0,1}⟩, φ(%IW)=⟨in,[0, 2^b_adc−1]⟩, φ(%QX),φ(%QW)=out. H is small, declarative, and **independent of P** — per-program bounds come from composing H with addresses already present in P.

**Faithful lowering (Algorithm 1, four transformations):**
1. *Word width* — all machine integers interpreted as w-bit bit-vectors (ESBMC's architecture setting, no rewriting).
2. *Input-range annotation* — immediately after each input is re-sampled, inject `assume(i ∈ D(a))`; this single step restores soundness in practice.
3. *Quantization & saturation* — ADC read modeled as nondeterministic value within representable set (sound over-approximation of any rounding); PWM write clamped to [0, 2^b_pwm−1].
4. *Non-linear abstraction* — function blocks with FP/transcendental ops (PID, thermistor linearization) replaced by fresh nondeterministic output in declared range (sound over-approximation).

**Soundness (Theorem 1).** Under a faithful descriptor, Reach_dev ⊆ Reach_M_dev(P,H); hence M_dev(P,H) ⊨ φ implies the device satisfies φ (modulo non-linear abstraction), and every reported counterexample is physically realizable because it satisfies the injected assumes. The full proof (Lemma 1 + Theorem 1) is in Appendix A.

**Implementation.** The annotator attaches at two sites where inputs are re-sampled (program-level READ_INPUTS and function-block input-sampling), immediately after each nondeterministic assignment, emitting a `code_assumet` node with bound `0 ≤ v ≤ ADCMAX`. Toggled by env var `HAL_ANNOTATE`, parameterized by `HAL_ADC_MAX`, so a single binary reproduces both naive baseline and hardware-faithful configuration — isolating the input model as the only independent variable.

## Results & Highlights

- **Naive width-aware checking is unusable (RQ2, real corpus, 123 programs).** Setting the word width to 16 bits and checking overflow *without* an input model produces a **44% false-alarm rate (54/123)** with **zero genuine defects**. Every one of the 54 reports is a spurious overflow on an unphysical input of the form `IN−c` with counterexamples pinned at the signed-range boundary (e.g. **IN=−32,764** so `IN−5` underflows) — values no 10-bit ADC can ever produce. Two controls confirm it is input-driven, not width-driven: the same 54 fail identically at both 16 and 32 bits (a width defect would differ), and 32 verify safe while 37 are unknown for reasons unrelated to inputs.
- **The HAL annotator restores soundness (RQ3, same 123 programs).** Enabling the input model (same binary, only change is injected bounds) drops the unsafe count from **54 → 0** while preserving all **32 robustness proofs (safe verdicts)**. The eliminated false alarms become *unknown* rather than *safe* (k-induction incompleteness), but no genuine defect is suppressed — because (RQ1) none of the real programs contains the vulnerable high-magnitude arithmetic. The 32 preserved safe verdicts are robustness certificates: proofs of no-overflow for any physically realizable input.
- **The gap is real but rare (RQ1, controlled corpus).** On the 20-program controlled taxonomy, buggy variants flip from **safe under M_abs (32-bit) to unsafe under M_dev on 16-bit boards** (CONTROLLINO Micro/Maxi, M-Duino), each with a realizable witness, and are **safe again on the 32-bit OPTA** — isolating word width as the sole cause; `_ok` variants never flip on any board. Motivating witness: `adc*100` at `adc=898` (≈88%) yields 89,800 > INT16_MAX, wraps, collapses percent to 23, and silences the high-level alarm. Vulnerable pattern: with v≤1,023 (10-bit ADC), 16-bit overflow (>32,767) requires magnifying v — multiply by constant ≥33 (e.g. `v*100`), accumulation, or squaring. Across **341 ST programs in a verification benchmark library, none contained a multiply-by-a-three-or-more-digit constant**.
- **Cross-tool validation — the phantom is not ESBMC-specific (CBMC 6.10.0, Frama-C/EVA 32.1).** On C mirrors, CBMC reproduces the identical spurious overflow with the identical witness **IN1=−32,764**; Frama-C's EVA (-machdep avr_16) raises the identical `assert -32768 <= IN1-5`. In all three engines the phantom fails at both 16 and 32 bits (input-driven), whereas the genuine `adc*100` defect fails only at 16 bits (width-dependent). Crucially, CBMC/Frama-C can *use* a bound but cannot *derive* one — clearing the phantom required inserting `__CPROVER_assume(0<=IN1<=1023)` by hand; only ESBMC-Arduino recovers it automatically from the `%IW` address.
- **Replication on real MATIEC-generated code.** Taking all 54 ESBMC-flagged programs, reconstructing their ST function blocks, compiling to C with MATIEC (the OpenPLC toolchain's generator), and running CBMC at AVR width: CBMC independently reproduces the overflow on **all 54/54** with no input model, and the hand-supplied HAL bound eliminates it on **all 54/54**.
- **Performance (RQ4, ESBMC v8.3.0 + Z3, commodity laptop).** Controlled corpus: **median solve time 168 ms, max 412 ms**; real corpus: **runs complete <1 s per program**. The HAL annotator adds only a pair of assume constraints per input and, by shrinking each input's domain from the full word to a 10-bit range, can make solving *easier*.
- **Completeness limit (stated plainly).** **91/123** programs are *unknown* due to k-induction incompleteness on loop-bearing function blocks; only **32** robustness proofs are obtained. The authors emphasize this is a backend limitation orthogonal to the contribution (37 were already unknown in the naive run) and the soundness result (no false alarms, no suppressed real defects) holds regardless.

The authors sit on the **ESBMC-PLC line** — this paper's three precursor references [7] ESBMC-PLC (TACAS 2027 artefact), [8] ESBMC-GraphPLC, [9] ESBMC-PLC+ are the authors' own prior work (Dantas, Cordeiro, Silva Júnior), so ESBMC-Arduino is a natural, well-motivated extension of their verified toolchain rather than a parachuted critique of a competitor. **Lucas Cordeiro** is the long-standing creator/maintainer of **ESBMC** (SMT-based BMC, widely cited in software verification, TACAS/SV-COMP), lending strong credibility on the backend claims. The work is double-anonymous at submission (author identity withheld in the Zenodo artifact, restored at camera-ready), and partially funded by EPSRC grants (EP/T026995/1, EP/V000497/1, EP/X037290/1) under the UK DSbD Programme, and the Soteria project — credible cyber-security funding aligned with the ICS-testbed motivation. The artifact (Zenodo 10.5281/zenodo.21014209) bundles pinned tools (CBMC 6.10.0, Frama-C 32.1, MATIEC, patched ESBMC), a `setup.sh`/`reproduce.sh` one-command reproduction on Ubuntu 22.04, and an independent CI pipeline that rebuilds the toolchain from source.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — real and well-positioned, but incremental.** The genuinely new idea is *automatic derivation* of hardware input ranges from the IEC 61131-3 direct-addressing scheme (`%IW` → analog word, width from board descriptor), removing the manual-annotation burden that Frama-C/Astrée/CBMC all impose. The word-width discipline itself is explicitly *transferred* from safety-critical embedded C (Frama-C `-machdep`, Astrée) — the authors are candid that this is "not speculative" because the embedded-C community already does exactly this. So novelty is in the PLC-level automation and the ESBMC hook, not in target-faithful modeling per se.
- **Rigor — high.** Soundness is a formal theorem (Theorem 1) with a supporting lemma and full proof (Appendix A); the four-step lowering is precise; the controlled before/after is produced by a *single binary* differing only by the `HAL_ANNOTATE` toggle, cleanly isolating the input model as the only independent variable. The two-controls argument for the 54 false alarms (identical at 16 and 32 bits; 37 already unknown pre-annotation) is a strong design choice.
- **Headline framing vs. actual finding — honestly disclosed, but worth flagging.** The paper's *marketing* number (54→0 false alarms) is a **soundness/false-alarm result, not a bug-finding result**. The paper finds **zero genuine defects in real code** — the gap is "genuine but rare" in the corpora they could ingest, dominated by Boolean/timer logic. The authors state this plainly (§8.2, §9) and reframe the contribution as soundness-restoration, which is the intellectually honest read. A less careful framing could overclaim "finds critical PLC bugs."
- **The strongest baseline is the authors' own prior line.** ESBMC-PLC [7,8,9] is their own work; **PLCverif** (CERN, production since 2019) is positioned in a table but not run head-to-head at scale on the real corpus. Cross-tool validation (CBMC, Frama-C) is on **C mirrors and the 54 flagged programs' MATIEC-generated code**, which is narrower than a full independent-tool evaluation. This is defensible (the contribution is input-model automation, orthogonal to the backend) but a reader should not mistake it for a PLCverif-vs-ESBMC bake-off.
- **Controlled corpus is self-authored.** The 20-program HAL-effect taxonomy that demonstrates the genuine flips was written *by the authors* to isolate the gap — it is a valid experimental isolation, but the only evidence of the method *finding* a real bug is the single motivating `adc*100` alarm example (Listing 1), not a corpus of real-world defects.
- **Completeness gap is material.** 91/123 unknown means the method cannot claim "123 programs proven robust," only "54 false alarms removed and 32 robustness proofs retained." The authors are explicit about this, but it bounds the practical assurance gain: for the majority of real programs the verifier still returns *unknown* even after the HAL fix.
- **Soundness rests on a conservative assumption.** Every integer input is uniformly treated as a 10-bit AVR ADC source; a mis-specified bound (e.g. a 14-bit UNO R4 channel, a digital word, or a network variable) would be unsound "in the same way a wrong `-machdep` would be for Frama-C." Per-address domains are left as future work.
- **No hardware-in-the-loop validation.** All evaluation is at the model level; the verified binaries are never executed on physical boards, so the soundness guarantee is relative to the faithfulness of H and the frontend's semantics, not measured against silicon. The authors flag this as future work.

## Strengths

- **Identifies and crisply formalizes a genuine, previously-under-articulated problem** — the deployment gap sits exactly where computation meets the physical process (ADC + finite-width arithmetic), and the paper makes it concrete, reproducible, and repairable rather than abstract.
- **Elegant, minimal, and adoptable engineering.** The repair is a four-field descriptor and a ~40-line hook that reuses a mature verifier (ESBMC) unchanged — realistic to adopt rather than a clean-slate proposal. A single binary toggles baseline vs. faithful config, making the controlled experiment clean.
- **Automatic input-range derivation is the key practical win.** Removing the manual-annotation burden that Frama-C/Astrée/CBMC impose is what makes corpus-scale deployment feasible for automation engineers unfamiliar with formal specification.
- **Intellectually honest throughout.** The authors repeatedly state the gap is "real but rare," that they find zero genuine defects in real code, that 91/123 are unknown, and that the value is soundness/false-alarm reduction — not defect count. The threats-to-validity section is unusually candid.
- **Strong cross-tool corroboration.** Reproducing the phantom alarm (with the *identical* witness IN1=−32,764) and the width-dependent defect on three independent engines (ESBMC, CBMC, Frama-C/EVA) substantially de-risks the "ESBMC idiosyncrasy" objection, and the all-54/54 replication on real MATIEC-generated code is convincing.
- **Soundness proof provided** (Theorem 1 + Lemma 1 + Appendix A), not merely asserted.

## Limitations

- **Finds essentially zero real bugs in real PLC code.** The vulnerable pattern (multiply-by-≥33 on a 16-bit board with bounded ADC input) is rare in the surveyed public corpora (Boolean/timer-dominated); the paper's value is soundness-restoration, not defect discovery.
- **Completeness is partial: 91/123 unknown.** k-induction fails to close on loop-bearing function blocks (timers, counters, edges), bounding robustness proofs to 32. The headline 54→0 is about false alarms, not about proving programs safe.
- **Scope restricted to the integer/Boolean fragment of IEC 61131-3.** Non-linear FP function blocks (PID, transcendental sensor linearization) are soundly over-approximated (replaced by nondeterministic output) — properties genuinely depending on a PID output cannot be proved under this abstraction, so analog process-control corpora (e.g. ICS testbeds in REAL arithmetic, where the gap may be *more* prevalent) are out of scope.
- **Uniform 10-bit ADC assumption** for every integer input (conservative common case for AVR); per-address domains, 14-bit UNO R4 channels, digital words, and network variables are future work. A mis-specified bound is unsound.
- **Model-level validation only — no hardware-in-the-loop.** The synthesized witnesses (e.g. adc=898) are never confronted with measured silicon behavior; the guarantee is relative to H's faithfulness and the frontend's semantics.
- **The two real datasets are third-party but narrow** (water-treatment LD, 60 programs; clone-detection, 63 programs) and "not the universe of PLC code"; prevalence claims are scoped to integer/Boolean LD programs.
- **Strongest baseline is the authors' own ESBMC-PLC line**; PLCverif is not benchmarked head-to-head at corpus scale.

## Relevance

对于关注形式化方法、嵌入式/工控系统（ICS）安全、以及可信软件验证的研究者，本文直接相关：它揭示了低成本开源硬件 PLC（学术 ICS 安全测试床的默认运行时底座）在抽象验证与设备实际语义之间的"部署鸿沟"，并以可自动推导、低成本（约 40 行钩子）的方式修复，为构建可部署的安全论证提供了可复用的目标模型纪律。其"你验证的必须等于设备上运行的"这一原则，对任何涉及交叉编译、有限字宽与有界物理输入的安全攸关系统验证都有借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How would the method perform on **analog-heavy REAL-arithmetic ICS-testbed corpora** (e.g. process control with PID loops), where the authors concede the gap may be *more* prevalent but which currently fall outside the frontend?
- Can the **91/123 unknown verdicts** be converted to safe robustness proofs via loop-invariant inference or compositional k-induction over function blocks, raising the proof count above 32?
- A **fixed-point abstraction** of non-linear blocks (rather than nondeterministic over-approximation) would be both more faithful and closer to how such computations are actually realized on an MCU — how much precision does it recover, and does it change the prevalence finding?
- **Hardware-in-the-loop validation**: does executing the verified binaries on physical Uno/Due boards and confronting the synthesized witnesses (e.g. adc=898) with measured behavior confirm the soundness theorem against silicon?
- Extending mechanisms **(M1) pin-binding/aliasing faults** and **(M2) ADC quantization** (representable-code sets rather than intervals) within the same framework — would these expose additional defect classes the current width-only focus misses?
- Could the HAL descriptor discipline be **layered onto PLCverif or Arcade.PLC** (as the paper suggests is possible), and would the soundness/automation results transfer to those backends?
