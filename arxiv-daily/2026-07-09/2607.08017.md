> **一句话总结：** 提出 GRAPHEVAL 框架，将 LLM 的多条 CoT 推理路径建模为因果 DAG 并用语义-结构图编辑距离（SS-GED）计算新的不确定性指标 GRCS，配合 medoid 选择式 Graph Self-Consistency 解码与对抗性 medoid 消融，在 GSM8K/BoolQ/StrategyQA/MedQA/GPQA 上证明 GRCS 是唯一在 14/15 配置中与推理忠实度负相关的 UQ 指标，并暴露出小模型 SC 准确率被"侥幸猜对"显著注水。

> **TL;DR:** GRAPHEVAL reframes LLM uncertainty quantification as a holistic reasoning-fidelity problem by decomposing sampled Chain-of-Thought paths into causal DAGs and measuring them with a novel Semantic-Structural Graph Edit Distance (SS-GED). Its UQ metric GRCS is the only one of five evaluated that stays negatively correlated with faithfulness in 14/15 (93.3%) model-dataset configs across Llama 3.1 8B, Phi-4, and DeepSeek R1, and its medoid-based Graph Self-Consistency (GSC) decoding exposes that SC's accuracy in smaller models is inflated by unfaithful "lucky guesses" — on GSM8K the SC-to-GSC gap is -7.6 pp for Llama 3.1 8B (88.9% → 81.3%) but turns positive for DeepSeek R1 (94.3% → 96.9%, +3.1 pp), while GSC achieves 79.5% faithful disagreement wins vs 57.6% for SC (+21.9 pp, p<0.05).

## 核心贡献

- **提出 GRCS 不确定性指标**：基于新的语义-结构图编辑距离 SS-GED，将多条 CoT 的因果 DAG 两两配对求平均距离，得到 GRCS = 1/(1+μ(D(q)))。在 5 个数据集 × 3 个模型的 15 组配置中，GRCS 在 14 组（93.3%）保持与推理忠实度负相关，是唯一在大小模型上都一致有效的指标。
- **提出 Graph Self-Consistency（GSC）解码**：不再用多数投票选最终答案，而是用 structural centrality + 路径共识（α=0.8）选出"medoid"CoT，专门滤除不忠实的侥幸答对；小模型上准确率下降恰好暴露 SC 注水，强模型上不降甚至略升。
- **设计对抗性 medoid 消融实验**：通过 prompt-poisoning 强制禁止模型走 GSC 选中的 medoid 推理路径，证明该路径是"承重"结构——被逼绕路时小模型的准确率上升反而伴随忠实度下降（Llama 斜率 p=0.011，Phi-4 p=0.010），坐实"侥幸猜对"现象。
- **系统刻画了"语义+结构"复合距离**：SS-GED 用公平归一化 + 终节点语义混合（β=0.4 语义、γ=5.0 RBF 结构、cosine≥0.9 阈值）+ Hungarian 算法 O(m³) 匹配，解决了纯拓扑方法（Topo-UQ）无法识别"同拓扑但不同结论"的盲点。
- **严格的统计与标注可靠性**：所有相关性用 1000 次 bootstrap 估计，解码差异用 McNemar 检验，忠实度审计用 Wilcoxon 检验，人工标注与 Gemini 3 Pro 判官一致性 Cohen's κ=0.913。

## Key Contributions

- **A new UQ metric, GRCS**, built on a novel Semantic-Structural Graph Edit Distance (SS-GED) that combines a Hungarian-matched structural GED (RBF kernel, γ=5.0) with terminal-node semantic similarity (β=0.4). GRCS aggregates pairwise distances into a bounded score 1/(1+μ(D(q))) and is the only one of five metrics (vs CoTA, Topo-UQ, dispersion, entropy) that stays negatively correlated with faithfulness in 14/15 (93.3%) model-dataset configurations.
- **Graph Self-Consistency (GSC) decoding**, which replaces majority voting with medoid selection over the SS-GED manifold, scoring each CoT by a blend of structural centrality and cross-graph support (α=0.8). It acts as a faithful-answer filter that exposes and removes SC-inflating "lucky guesses" in smaller models.
- **Adversarial medoid ablation**, a prompt-poisoning protocol that forbids reusing the GSC-selected medoid CoT, empirically demonstrating the medoid is a load-bearing reasoning path whose removal couples accuracy gains with faithfulness drops in smaller GLLMs.
- **A complete pipeline (GRAPHEVAL)** that uses an architecturally independent Decomposer LLM (GPT-4o) and embedding model (Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B) to avoid self-evaluation inductive bias, with 1,000-bootstrap correlation estimates for statistical rigor.
- **A faithful-answer auditing methodology** combining a human annotator and Gemini 3 Pro as LLM judge (κ=0.913) over SC/GSC disagreement cases, with McNemar and Wilcoxon significance tests on every decoding claim.

## Methodology

GRAPHEVAL converts raw text generations into analyzable reasoning topologies through a five-stage pipeline:

1. **Causal Fact Decomposition & DAG Construction.** For each question q, a Generator LLM (GLLM) produces N=20 CoTs at temperature T=0.7, each with [n_l, n_h] = [3, 6] explicitly causally-annotated declarative steps (e.g., `[depends_on: 0, 1]`). To avoid self-evaluation inductive bias, a distinct Decomposer LLM (DLLM = GPT-4o, T=0.0) re-segments each step into Subject–Verb–Object atomic facts while preserving causal dependencies. The result is a DAG G=(V,E) with node embeddings from Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B.

2. **Semantic-Structural Distance (SS-GED).** All C(N,2) DAG pairs are compared with an extended Graph Edit Distance using Hungarian bipartite matching in O(m³) (m ≈ |V_i|+|V_j|), substitution cost = cosine distance. Two improvements over pure topological GED: (a) Fair Normalization scales edit cost by average graph size; (b) Semantic-Structural Blending adds terminal-node semantic similarity so graphs with identical topology but divergent conclusions are still penalized. Final similarity is S(G_i,G_j) = β·S_conc + (1-β)·exp(-γ·nGED²) with β=0.4, γ=5.0.

3. **Graph Reasoning Coherence Score (GRCS).** The pairwise SS-GED distances form D(q); GRCS = 1/(1+μ(D(q))) aggregates them into one distributional UQ score. Mode collapse and confident hallucinations both inflate μ(D(q)) and thus lower GRCS.

4. **Consensus Context Synthesis / GSC.** Each CoT G_i is scored by a weighted blend of structural centrality C(G_i)=Σ S(G_i,G_j) and path consensus P(G_i) (fraction of peer graphs containing a semantically similar node, cosine≥0.9, with terminal nodes weighted 2×). The medoid is z* = argmax[ α·C(G_i)/max_j C(G_j) + (1-α)·P(G_i) ], α=0.8.

5. **Adversarial Medoid Ablation.** A prompt-poisoning attack injects the full medoid CoT z*_q and forbids reusing any of its steps (built on Zou et al. 2023), forcing an alternative path; alternative paths are scored on ablated accuracy, ablated faithfulness, and medoid distance.

Faithfulness ground truth comes from Early Answering: the GLLM is fed expanding medoid facts and a prompt is deemed unfaithful if its answer emerges before the full CoT is supplied. Correlation with faithfulness is measured via Pearson (PCC), Spearman (SRC), and Kendall (KRC), estimated over 1,000 bootstrap resamples (20 questions × 10 responses each). Baselines for UQ are CoTA, Topo-UQ, and SS-GED-adapted dispersion/entropy; the decoding baseline is standard Self-Consistency (SC).

## Results & Highlights

- **GRCS is the most reliable UQ metric.** Across the 15 ⟨GLLM–dataset⟩ configurations (3 models × {GSM8K, BoolQ, StrategyQA, MedQA, GPQA}), GRCS yields the strongest negative PCC against faithfulness in **8/15** and stays negatively correlated in **14/15 (93.3%)**. By contrast, dispersion/entropy flip *positive* on GSM8K/BoolQ for Llama 3.1 8B and on GSM8K/StrategyQA for DeepSeek R1, and Topo-UQ is positive or near-zero for Phi-4 on all tasks.
- **Per-baseline correlation peaks.** Topo-UQ reaches about **-0.25 to -0.30** (Llama/DeepSeek on GSM8K, BoolQ) but degrades on complex domains; CoTA hits **-0.40** on GSM8K for DeepSeek R1 but turns positive on GPQA for both Llama and DeepSeek. GRCS alone is consistent across scales.
- **GSC trades nominal accuracy for faithfulness in small models.** On Llama 3.1 8B, GSC drops accuracy by **-7.6 pp on GSM8K (88.9% → 81.3%)**, **-9.0 pp on MedQA (61.0% → 52.0%)**, and **-5.1 pp on GPQA (24.4% → 19.3%)**, all statistically significant (p<0.05, McNemar) — revealing SC's reliance on lucky guesses.
- **GSC preserves/improves accuracy on capable models.** DeepSeek R1 gains **+3.1 pp on GSM8K (94.3% → 96.9%)**, **+1.0 pp on BoolQ (76.0% → 77.0%)**, **+1.0 pp on MedQA (90.9% → 91.9%)**, and **+1.1 pp on GPQA (52.4% → 53.4%)**, with the differences *not* statistically significant — i.e., GSC filters unfaithful paths without hurting base capability.
- **GSC selects more faithful answers on disagreements.** Averaged across disagreement cells, GSC achieves **79.5% faithful wins vs 57.6% for SC (+21.9 pp, p<0.05, Wilcoxon)**. On Llama 3.1 8B / MedQA only 40% of SC-only wins are faithful vs 100% of GSC-only wins; on GPQA SC-only wins are entirely spurious while GSC recovers 25%.
- **Adversarial ablation confirms the medoid is load-bearing.** After medoid ablation on StrategyQA, **43.2% (Llama), 55.0% (Phi-4), and 15.2% (DeepSeek R1)** of points fall below ΔFaithfulness=0, with significantly negative least-squares slopes for Llama (p=0.011) and Phi-4 (p=0.010); DeepSeek R1 shows no significant linear association (r=-0.01, p=0.856) but still suffers mild faithfulness loss.
- **Annotation reliability is high.** Human reviewer vs Gemini 3 Pro LLM judge agreement reaches **Cohen's κ = 0.913** on the disagreement audit.

## Authors & Credibility

The paper is from the University of Illinois Chicago (ECE and Biochemistry & Molecular Genetics), authored by Riccardo Revalor, Jalees Rehman, and Debjit Pal. It is submitted to COLM 2026 ("Under review as a conference paper"). The framing is methodologically careful: it credits prior work (Topo-UQ, CoTA, Self-Consistency, semantic entropy) and is explicit about its own metric's weakness on large models. The evaluation uses architecturally independent component models (GPT-4o as decomposer, Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B for embeddings) to mitigate self-evaluation bias, and applies standard significance tests (McNemar, Wilcoxon, 1,000-bootstrap correlations) plus an LLM-judge/human agreement check — a credible empirical standard for a UQ/decoding paper.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (genuine).** Combining semantic-terminal-node similarity with structural GED via an RBF kernel and fair normalization is a real, non-trivial extension of Topo-UQ. The core insight — that SC accuracy in small models is inflated by unfaithful lucky guesses that *only* a structural+semantic metric can flag — is well-motivated and the medoid-as-load-bearing-path claim is original.
- **Rigor (mixed).** Statistical hygiene is strong (McNemar, Wilcoxon, 1,000-bootstrap, κ=0.913). However, the paper's headline metric **GRCS weakens precisely on the most capable model (DeepSeek R1)**, which the authors attribute to "greater reasoning heterogeneity." This is honest but means the framework's flagship UQ signal is weakest exactly where frontier deployment happens — a real boundary on significance.
- **Weak/uneven baselines and setup.** The UQ baselines include *statistical adaptations* (dispersion, entropy over SS-GED distances) rather than strong learned UQ methods, so GRCS's win is partly against straw-men; CoTA and Topo-UQ are the only reasoning-aware competitors. MedQA uses only **100 randomly sampled questions (seed 42)** — a small, single-seed slice that makes the dramatic -9.0 pp Llama drop and the 100%-faithful GSC claim fragile. No frontier-scale (≥100B dense) GLLM is tested; the largest is a 70B distill.
- **Hyperparameter surface.** The method exposes β=0.4, γ=5.0, α=0.8, cosine≥0.9, N=20, [3,6] steps — five+ knobs whose sensitivity is relegated to appendices (C.5, C.6). The faithfulness ground truth itself relies on **Early Answering**, a proxy whose validity is debated; an alternative (Counterfactual Editing) is only in an appendix (C.3).
- **Overclaiming check.** Claims are mostly appropriately hedged (e.g., "GRCS correlations weaken on DeepSeek R1" is stated directly). The "load-bearing path" framing for ablation is evocative but the DeepSeek R1 result (r=-0.01, n.s.) tempers it — the load-bearing property is really a *small-model* phenomenon.
- **Reproducibility.** Good on paper: open-source GLLMs, fixed seeds, prompts in Appendix B, full ablations in Appendix C. But reliance on GPT-4o as DLLM and Gemini 3 Pro as judge introduces closed-model dependencies that limit exact reproduction, and per-question compute (O(N²·m³)) is non-trivial.

## Strengths

- Reframes UQ from answer-level to holistic reasoning-fidelity, a genuinely useful conceptual shift for mission-critical deployment (medical/legal).
- SS-GED's semantic-structural blending is a concrete, well-defined fix for Topo-UQ's blind spot to "same topology, different conclusion."
- Decoding analysis (GSC vs SC, with a faithful/spurious audit) directly demonstrates the practical consequence of the UQ metric rather than just reporting correlations.
- Strong statistical reporting: effect sizes, p-values, bootstrap CIs, and inter-annotator agreement (κ=0.913).
- Honest disclosure of where the method breaks (large-capable models, long-horizon reasoning).

## Limitations

- **Time complexity** O(N²·m³) per question (N=20 CoTs, m DAG nodes) is far higher than SC's O(N), though comparable to Topo-UQ.
- **Diminishing signal on capable models.** GRCS's negative correlation and the ablation effect both weaken on DeepSeek R1 — the method's value concentrates on smaller GLLMs.
- **Short-horizon only.** CoTs capped at n_h=6 steps; scalability to long-context, highly compositional (e.g., agentic multi-step) tasks is unaddressed.
- **Small/single-seed slices** for MedQA (100 questions, seed 42) and reliance on closed DLLM/judge models reduce reproducibility and statistical robustness in the most safety-relevant domain.
- **Faithfulness proxy dependency** on Early Answering (with Counterfactual Editing only as an appendix alternative).

## Relevance

与本用户关注的大模型推理、不确定性量化与可信评估方向高度契合：GRAPHEVAL 提供了在 CoT 推理层面（而非仅最终答案）量化不确定性与忠实度的可操作框架，对评估 reasoning model、改进 self-consistency 解码、以及在医疗/法律等安全关键场景中识别"侥幸猜对"具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does GRCS/GSC behave on genuinely long-horizon reasoning (agentic trajectories, multi-thousand-token CoTs from o1-style models) where n_h ≫ 6?
- Can the medoid ablation result be replicated on a frontier dense model (≥100B), or is "load-bearing path" an artifact of distilled/small-model mode collapse?
- Is Early Answering a robust enough faithfulness oracle — how do GRCS correlations change when Counterfactual Editing or human-graded faithfulness is the ground truth instead?
- What is the token/compute cost-benefit frontier of GSC vs SC: at what N does the medoid selection stop paying off relative to simply running more SC samples?
- Could SS-GED be approximated cheaply (e.g., via embedding-only or learned graph kernels) to bring O(N²·m³) down to a regime usable at inference time?
- Does GSC's faithfulness advantage transfer to downstream tasks (e.g., tool use, code generation) where "correct answer via wrong reasoning" is especially costly?
