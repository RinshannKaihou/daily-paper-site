Key Finding

Lightweight probes on a forecasting LLM's internal activations recover a truer confidence — and catch the lies its chain-of-thought hides.

What LLM Forecasters Know but Don't Say: Probing Internal Representations for Calibration and Faithfulness ↗ Raphaël Sarfati · Pratyush Ranjan Tiwari · Siddharth Boppana · Christopher J. Earls · Srikar Varadaraj · Eric Ho

Key Metric · Calibration

0.044

probe ECE  vs  0.093 verbalized

Layer-21 covariance probe on EF-8B (OpenForesight-test, N=3,020 rollouts). More than a 2× reduction in expected calibration error — without touching model weights. rankers statistically tied (AUROC 0.756 vs 0.758)

Why This Matters

Probes turn mechanistic interpretability into deployable forecaster calibration, CoT auditing, and 30–47% inference savings — no retraining required.

Methodology

§ Method

Subjects & Data

EF-8B (RLVR post-trained from Qwen3-8B) is the workhorse; EF-32B for forced-answering; frozen GLM-4.7-Flash & GLM-4.5-Air for probe-only transfer. Trained on nikhilchandak/OpenForesight (52.2k train / 302 test) with OOD splits aljazeera + skysports. A separate math track evaluates pooled AIME24/25 + AMC23/24 (n=580).

Faithfulness Protocols

Following Gur-Arieh et al.: evidence ablation (remove one real article; 354 questions, 1,303 pairs) and diversionary injection (one Claude-Sonnet-written misleading article per question; 489 questions). A layer-20 attention probe reads |∆probe| as an internal "lie detector."

Probe Architectures

Small linear classifiers on frozen residual-stream activations (Alain & Bengio tradition). Three pooling variants over X ∈ ℝ^(N×D):

  • mean-poolarithmetic average along the token axis.
  • attn-pooltrained attention heads aggregate the stream.
  • cov-poolsecond-order covariance via low-rank L,R into a k=64 bottleneck — captures co-fluctuation the others miss.

Readout is a linear inner-product to one logit, BCE-trained on LLM-judge labels. A 36-layer × 3-arch × 7-site sweep selects the deployed probe: covariance probe at layer 21, read late. Metrics: ECE, Brier, AUROC, Pearson r, Spearman ρ — all with 95% question-clustered bootstrap CIs.

Strengths

5 noted
  • One mechanism, three threads. Calibration, faithfulness, and triage all hang off pooling probes on internal activations.
  • Statistical hygiene. Clustered bootstrap CIs, leakage controls, and negative controls (shuffled labels/activations) sitting at chance throughout.
  • The standout finding is well-isolated. The lie-detector result (84% direction-correct, incl. stealth) is shielded from confounds via the matched-|∆prob| contrast.
  • Honest about its losses. Concedes in-distribution math ECE against recalibrated verbal confidence and the non-significant GLM-4.7-Flash ranking delta.
  • Deployable today. Entropy-gated commit-early triage ships with a clear cost/accuracy Pareto curve.

Limitations

6 noted
  • Non-public dataset build. Core forecasting results lean on an "improved-context" OpenForesight variant not in the public release.
  • Unrecalibrated baseline. The verbalized-confidence ECE comparison applies no temperature/Platt/isotonic — narrowing the probe's edge is left untested.
  • 23% vs 2.5% is not apples-to-apples. Different denominators (ablation high-impact pairs vs injection-susceptible questions); "more transparent under injection" reads cautiously.
  • Cross-model triage assumed, not shown. Forced-answer & triage reported on EF-32B; calibration/faithfulness on EF-8B.
  • No code or probe weights released. The agentic "Silico" workflow adds opacity to the experimental pipeline.
  • Math generalization is qualified. DeepScaleR overlap and in-distribution MATH-500 favoring recalibrated verbal confidence support "OOD monitoring," not broad superiority.

Open Questions for Further Exploration

6 posed
  1. Would recalibrating EF-8B's verbalized confidence (temperature / Platt / isotonic) erase most of the probe's headline ECE advantage — or does the probe still win because AUROC is calibration-invariant?
  2. Can the probe signal be distilled back into the model's verbalized probability without degrading the very internal representation the probe reads — and does that training close or widen the self-report gap?
  3. How does entropy-gated triage transfer when the pre-reasoning answer distribution is itself miscalibrated, or when retrieval is actually invoked for high-H questions (the "Retrieve" extension is proposed but untested)?
  4. Does the probe-based lie detector generalize to adversarial CoT — models explicitly post-trained to conceal reasoning ("Reasoning theater") — rather than the incidental stealth observed here?
  5. Are the covariance-probe advantages (better calibration than mean/attention pooling) attributable to the second-order statistics themselves, or to the low-rank bottleneck acting as implicit regularization? An ablation over k would clarify.
  6. The faithfulness framework uses CoT-text similarity (Jaccard + character-sequence) as the "reasoning change" signal; would a semantic/embedding-based similarity change the 23% stealth rate or the ρ = 0.215 coupling?
