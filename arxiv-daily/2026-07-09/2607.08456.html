Two Axes of LLM Abstention: Answer Correctness and Question Answerability

Benedikt J. Wagner · City St George's, University of London · 2026-07-09 · single-author preprint

llm-abstention uncertainty-quantification hidden-state-probing conformal-risk-control hallucination-detection

Key metric

Hidden-state answerability probe on SelfAware, across all five models (2B–14B).

vs trained output readout 0.54–0.67 — a gap that does not close at 14B

Why this matters

Splits a single conflated failure mode into a correctness axis and an answerability axis with statistical, dual-risk guarantees — directly useful for selective answering and risk-controlled LLM systems.

Critical edge

All models are ≤14B, and the only wrong-answer budget that certifies is αW=0.50 — too loose to be operationally meaningful; tighter budgets fail entirely.

01Methodology

Five instruction-tuned models from three families at 2B–14B (Gemma 2 2B-it, Qwen2.5-3B/7B/14B, Llama-3.1-8B; quantization validated against full precision). Two benchmarks: SelfAware (balanced answerable / unanswerable) and CREPE (real Reddit ELI5 user questions — 500 normal + 500 false-presupposition, surface-confound-free). Single user turn, greedy decoding (≤24 tokens); from the same forward pass the authors record (i) output answer-confidence and (ii) final prompt-token hidden states at fractional depths 0.4 / 0.6 / 0.8.

To avoid comparing a trained probe against a raw scalar, equal-capacity L2-regularized logistic readouts are trained for all four source-by-axis cells on shared partitions. The Factorized Abstention policy emits an answer iff sA≥τA AND sC≥τC; thresholds tuned on one split, then certified on an independent split with exact one-sided Clopper–Pearson bounds (Bonferroni 1−δ=0.90). A 100-seed replication audit checks whether issued certificates are violated at the declared δ rate.

03Strengths

  • Clean, replicated geometric characterization — the "L-shape" — across three model families and 2B–14B, decomposing one conflated failure mode into two axes.
  • Runs the controls the claim actually requires: bag-of-words surface bound (0.87 on SelfAware) and the CREPE natural-data test that removes the lexical confound — and narrows claims accordingly.
  • Probe-routed premise checking is a concrete behavioral payoff: turns the abstract "internal signal" into a routing policy that triples challenge precision over always-challenge.
  • Dual-risk certification makes the vague "model is too weak" claim measurable and scale-dependent, with a 100-seed replication audit rather than a single-shot certificate.
  • Intellectual honesty throughout: disclaims novelty where due, reports unfavorable intervals, explicitly flags optimistic zero-count bounds.

04Limitations

  • Small scale. All models ≤14B; the hidden correctness readout grows sharply from 3B→8B (0.59→0.88), so the pattern may not persist — or weaken — at 70B+. Authors do not claim frontier-scale persistence.
  • Weak correctness signal & lenient budget. Correctness is alias-matching on SelfAware accuracies of just 0.28–0.42; the only certifiable wrong-answer budget is αW=0.50, too loose to be operationally useful.
  • Missing sampling-based baseline. Semantic entropy (Farquhar et al. 2024) is excluded because it doesn't fit the single-pass protocol — leaving the strongest known output-side hallucination detector unevaluated.
  • SelfAware surface confound. Most of SelfAware's 0.97–0.99 separability is surface-recoverable (bag-of-words 0.87); the transferable core on CREPE is only moderate (0.69–0.78).
  • No causal intervention (unlike Slobodkin 2023, Lavi 2026); evidence is observational plus policy-level. English-only, single-turn, alias correctness only.

05Open questions for further work

Q1 · Scale

Does the C/W/U L-shape persist at 70B / 405B, where explicit self-assessment and hidden correctness readouts both sharpen — does the answerability axis stay superior, or does the gap close?

Q2 · Entropy

How does Factorized Abstention compare against sampling-based semantic entropy once the single-pass constraint is relaxed — does multi-sample entropy dominate on the correctness axis?

Q3 · Tighter budgets

Can the wrong-answer budget be tightened below αW=0.50 to useful levels (0.10–0.20) by scaling calibration data, or is it fundamentally bounded by model accuracy?

Q4 · Correction

Probe-routed premise checking fixes discrimination, but only 47% of contests are NLI-valid on Llama — can a second-stage correction model close this, or does confabulation reappear "one level up"?

Q5 · Composition

Does the dual-risk framework extend to richer logical compositions (OR, negation) over multiple grounded predicates beyond the simple AND conjunction?
