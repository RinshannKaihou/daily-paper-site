Methodology

Reasoning as a steerable meta-capability

Given an instruct model θM and a reasoning-distilled model θR, the reasoning task vector τreason = θR − θM isolates the deliberative-reasoning update. The overthinking model extrapolates past the trained point:

θOα = θM + α·(θR − θM)   α=0 → M  |  α=1 → R  |  α>1 → overthinking

Layer-wise attenuation. Naïve large-α scaling causes “coherence collapse” (fragmented, repetitive output). The authors formalize collapse onset α* via three convergent signals — perplexity knee, sharp DKL rise, and language drift (Pearson r=−0.87 vs. a GPT-4.1-mini judge, n=18, p<0.001). They then apply per-layer scaling θOα(ℓ) = θM(ℓ) + α·λℓ·τreason(ℓ) across coefficient families:

Uniform; heuristics (Freeze Last 5 / Half, Linear Decay); adaptive Fisher Trace λ∝1/√tr(F); a novel Directional Fisher measuring sensitivity along the task-vector direction; Task Magnitude boosting strong-signal / low-sensitivity layers; plus scale-matched random baselines (Layer-/Global-Norm) to test whether the direction of the task vector is necessary.

Four model organisms on Qwen3-VL (Instruct M vs Thinking R, 2B/4B/8B/32B): In-Context Secret (MMLU), Secret Word (Taboo, LoRA r=16), Secret Belief (Gender, adversarially denied), Secret Behavior (SSC, Base64-encoded). Sweep α∈[0,4] at 0.5 steps, 256 samples/cell; leaks parsed separately in thinking traces vs final answers; significance via paired McNemar + Holm-Bonferroni, on H100 80GB.

Assessment

+Strengths

  • One clean equation (θOα = θM + α(θR−θM)) — trivial to implement, cheap vs. retraining.
  • Scale- and direction-matched random baselines directly test the “any big perturbation suffices” alternative, separating two secret regimes.
  • Coherence collapse validated information-theoretically (perplexity + KL + drift converge on the same α*) — not just a lone LLM judge.
  • Practitioner-oriented: a concrete two-stage protocol (cheap random probe → escalate to Fisher-weighted amplification).
  • Rich qualitative signals — backtracking, identity absorption, Chinese language drift — double as cheap monitors.

Assessment

–Limitations

  • Single model family (Qwen3-VL); cross-family (Llama 3.3) results deferred — generalization unverified.
  • 256 samples/cell and single LLM judge; tighter bars + multi-judge validation left to future work.
  • The three mechanistic disclosure hypotheses are unproven — no activation patching or probing.
  • Headline “up to 10×” leans on best-case comparisons; tabulated OT-alone deltas are typically 3–6×.
  • For noise-fragile secrets (Gender, MMLU), random perturbation is comparably effective — value confined to directionally-protected secrets.
  • Requires white-box weight access; inapplicable to API-only deployments.

Open Questions

For further exploration

  1. Does the directionally-protected vs noise-fragile split hold across Llama 3.3, DeepSeek-R1, and other open-weight families?
  2. Can activation patching or linear probing give mechanistic evidence for the three hypothesized disclosure mechanisms?
  3. How does the auditing window α* shift with reasoning-model quality — is there a scaling-law relationship?
  4. Would per-parameter (not per-layer) Fisher information push the collapse threshold higher than Directional Fisher?
  5. Can overthinking surface organically emergent deception (e.g. alignment-faking from RL), not just LoRA-trained secrets?
  6. Is the judge–perplexity correlation (r=−0.87) stable across families, or could judge artifacts inflate non-Qwen leak counts?
