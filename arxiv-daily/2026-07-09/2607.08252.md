> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 AutoPersonas——一个面向长期 Persona 智能体的多时间尺度"生命-环境"引擎，通过 OSO（Observation/State/Occurrence）因果环、语义状态机与受控发散源来对抗运行时的"自锁"失败模式；在 8 个模型、1600 个事件的压测中直接递归回路的行为类别重复率达 95.2%-97.6%，而启用上下文掩码+逐样本发散目标后宏主题重复率从 61.8% 降至 36.3%（主题数 55→102）。

> **TL;DR:** AutoPersonas frames a new runtime failure mode—"self-locking"—where a long-term persona loop keeps generating text but its life trajectory collapses into stale state, environment, and relationship attractors. Across an eight-model (Claude, DeepSeek, Doubao, Gemini, GLM, GPT, Kimi, Qwen) 40-day, 1,600-event stress test on one persona canon, mean rolling 5-day action-category repetition hit 95.2%-97.6% (all models >80% by day 9, >90% by day 11) and macro-theme repetition 79.0%-88.0%. The proposed fix—context-slice masking plus per-sample divergence targeting inside the OSO causal loop—cuts macro-theme repetition from a 61.8% pre-redesign baseline to 36.3% (masked lane) / 39.4% (blended deploy) on a same-runtime A/B, roughly doubling cumulative themes (55→102).

## 核心贡献

- 明确区分"宏观世界"与"生命-环境层"：指出开放式 Persona 成长由介于顶层世界与自我之间的、个体化且动态变化的环境层所支配，而非共享沙箱世界直接决定。
- 定义并刻画运行时"自锁（self-locking）"失败模式：不是模型崩溃或遗忘，而是 persona 生命-环境二元体在递归运行时陷入旧状态、旧环境、旧关系吸引子的功能性轨迹坍塌；形式化为 `S_t∈A → C_t dominated by A → Y_t∈A → R_t supports A → S_{t+1}∈A`。
- 提出时间权威分离的 OSO 因果环：Observation（证据权威）/ State（连续性权威）/ Occurrence（环境开启权威）三类对象不可合并，Occurrence 必须经"生活化→Observation→State 修订→改变未来可达性"的因果链才算真正推进。
- 引入语义状态机（semantic State machine）：schema 有界但语义开放，用 LLM 语义整合判断"弱信号累积是否升级为状态级事实"，无需手工枚举所有人生转移。
- 给出八模型量化压测与温控探针：证明直接递归回路在 8 个主流模型上迅速收敛到 10-11 个动作类别（重复率 95.2%-97.6%），且升温（Doubao 0.75→1.0）只重分配权重而不扩展动作库。
- 通过受控消融与同运行时 A/B 验证"掩码 + 逐样本发散目标"的反固化效果，并在五岁地精虚构世界这一强张力设定下复现反固化机制（42.8% 重复率、零硬现实入侵）。
- 与 Generative Agents / Agentopia 家族做"互补而非竞争"的定位比较，区分封闭沙箱社会模拟器与开放式 persona 生命-环境引擎。

## Key Contributions

- **The life-environment layer abstraction**: distinguishes the macro-world from an individualized, dynamic "life-environment layer" that mediates between top-level world signals and persona State, arguing this middle layer is the missing abstraction for open-ended persona growth.
- **The self-locking failure mode**: defines recursive runtime collapse of a persona-life-environment dyad (distinct from training-set model collapse and from output-diversity collapse) where locally varied text still returns to the same state, environment shell, route tension, and relationship function.
- **The OSO temporal-authority loop**: separates Observation (evidence authority), State (continuity authority), and Occurrence (environment-opening authority); an event counts as causal growth only if it traverses Occurrence → lived material → Observation → State movement → revised future possibility space.
- **Semantic State machine**: a schema-bounded but semantically open State representation that uses LLM semantic integration to accumulate sparse, high-dimensional weak signals into State-level facts without a handcrafted transition ontology.
- **Quantitative mode-lock stress test**: an eight-model, 1,600-event action-channel and macro-theme convergence baseline establishing that direct recursive persona loops self-lock rapidly across current foundation models.
- **Anti-fixation validation**: controlled ablation (88.0%→46.5% repeat, 24→107 themes) plus a same-runtime A/B (61.8%→36.3-39.4%, 55→102-103 themes) showing masking + per-sample divergence targeting reverses fixation; cross-world generality confirmed on a juvenile-goblin fictional setting.
- **Bounded comparison** with Generative Agents / Agentopia sandbox-society simulators, positioning AutoPersonas as complementary open-environment infrastructure rather than a direct benchmark competitor.

## Methodology

AutoPersonas is presented as a **systems architecture and diagnostic method**, not a foundation-model or training result (it is part of SoulOS, alongside MemorOS and MemeOS). The central design object is a persona-conditioned life-environment that co-evolves with the persona.

**Problem framing.** Open-environment persona evolution must jointly satisfy three conflicting requirements—continuity (identity recognizability), plasticity (evidence revises state), and openness (futures not fully determined by initial persona). The paper formalizes the recursive loop as `C_t = assemble(S_t, O_t, X_t); Y_t = LLM(C_t); R_t = recognize(Y_t); S_{t+1} = revise(S_t, R_t)` and names the failure risk **current-state authority**: because S_t appears on both sides, old state can become the main evidence for its own continuation.

**Three core architectural objects (OSO):**
- **Observation** — accumulated lived evidence (events, summaries, external facts, relationship traces) that may preserve, revise, or leave State unresolved.
- **State** — a semantic State machine: finite auditable dimensions (identity continuity, life phase, life-environment, active routes, constraints, relationships, concerns) but open semantic content; updates are meaning-level, not enumerated label transitions, so both jumps and slow drift are representable.
- **Occurrence** — future-facing environment-side material (invitations, shocks, deadlines, openings) conditioned on persona canon, State, time, relationship, and macro-world signals. Crucially, Occurrence is *not* the persona's action; it is the environment "pushing back."

**Five public mechanisms** (production sampling/ranking/gating details are deliberately withheld):
1. **Conditional variation engine** — conditions the base model's learned social-world prior on persona/State/time/relationship/macro-world constraints to surface plausible non-identical life signals (deliberately narrower than "the model knows reality").
2. **Bounded context governance** — controls relative visibility/authority of State, history, runtime world material, and future-facing signal at each step; the paper reports a ~60% token-budget threshold beyond which divergence degrades.
3. **Information orthogonality** — keeps Observation, State, Occurrence, memory, environment, and reflection as non-interchangeable channels so a local fact cannot masquerade as independent multi-module support.
4. **Progressive causal propagation** — requires an upstream signal to be traceable as lived material → recognized evidence → State movement → revised reachability; isolated signals are "decoration, not evolution."
5. **Trajectory monitoring and reorientation** — audits State, life-environment, route authority, relationship function, Occurrence hardening, and real-world alignment for pattern-lock.

**Multi-timescale revision.** Daily (fast event capture, short continuity), weekly (accumulate weak signals, check State-evidence match), monthly (revise portrait/role/life-environment), long-horizon (life-stage continuity, route authority). The paper argues day-level resolution is a *phase boundary* for self-locking audits: weekly/yearly summaries hide repeated deferrals and decorative opportunities.

**Evaluation method — long-run diagnostic audit.** Because short benchmarks cannot reveal recursive collapse, the paper runs a compressed three-year simulation and classifies each of nine dimensions (current-state progression, narrative progression, life-environment handoff, Occurrence hardening, slow-change accumulation, relationship persistence, recursive indecision, memory boundary, real-world alignment) as PASS / PARTIAL / FAIL / UNVERIFIED.

**Quantitative stress test protocol.** One fixed complex persona canon; eight included models (Claude, DeepSeek, Doubao, Gemini, GLM, GPT, Kimi, Qwen; MiniMax excluded for incomplete generation); 40 days × 5 events/day = 200 events/model, 1,600 total; daily generation temperature 0.75 (Kimi K2.6 code-forced to 1.0); summary compaction 0.2. Primary metric: rolling 5-day action-category repetition vs. all earlier history under a rule-based keyword taxonomy, with stricter variants (exclude Other; require ≥2/≥3 prior events or days). A separate semantic re-keeping grouped events into cumulative macro themes.

**Anti-fixation validation.** Stage 1 isolated-generator ablation (no runtime feedback): baseline vs. masking-only vs. masking+divergence-targeting. Stage 2 full-runtime A/B on a cloned canon: pre-redesign (mechanisms off) vs. redesigned masked-lane / narrative-arc-lane / blended-deploy. A Doubao temperature probe (0.75→1.0) isolates decoding temperature. A juvenile-goblin fictional-world 40-day run tests cross-setting generality with an inverted sentinel scan (checking real-world does *not* leak in).

## Results & Highlights

The paper explicitly frames results as **diagnostic, not benchmark superiority**, but reports substantial quantitative evidence:

- **Eight-model action-channel mode-lock (baseline):** Across 8 models × 200 events (1,600 total), mean rolling 5-day action-category repetition was **95.2%-97.6%** (overall mean 96.5%). All eight models crossed the 80% repetition threshold by **day 9** and 90% by **day 11**. Despite 200 events each, trajectories used only **10-11 primary action categories**; mean top-3 category share was 57.1% and top-5 share 75.4%. (e.g., Claude 97.6%, Gemini 96.2%, Kimi 95.2%.)
- **Macro-theme semantic re-keeping:** Re-grouping the same direct-loop outputs under cumulative macro themes yielded **79.0%-88.0% repetition across all eight models**; the final 10-day block added only 2-8 new themes despite 50 generated events (e.g., Claude 88.0%, Qwen 79.0%).
- **Stricter-variant robustness:** Even requiring a category to appear in ≥3 prior events, all models still crossed 80% repetition by **day 16** (mean range 80.0%-86.6%), ruling out that the effect is just the broad "Other" category.
- **Doubao temperature probe (single-model):** Raising daily generation temperature from 0.75 to 1.0 did *not* broaden the repertoire (11 categories in both) and did *not* delay convergence (mean repetition 96.0% vs 95.9%; first ≥80% window moved *earlier* from day 9 to day 8). Higher sampling redistributed channel weights (Waterline processing 27→45 events; father-family care 63→42) rather than creating durable new channels—directly weakening the "hotter sampling fixes mode-lock" hypothesis.
- **Stage 1 isolated-generator ablation:** No-mask baseline 88.0% repeat / 24 themes; masking-only 85.0% / 30 themes (little effect); **masking + per-sample divergence targeting 46.5% / 107 themes**—masking alone is insufficient; only the combination breaks the attractor.
- **Stage 2 full-runtime A/B (the central systems claim):** Holding runtime, persona, horizon, and generation model fixed, the pre-redesign condition (mechanisms off) showed 61.8% macro-theme repetition and 55 themes. The redesigned masked lane reached **36.3% repetition and 102 themes**; the narrative-arc lane 45.9% / 46 themes; the blended deployed mix **39.4% / 103 themes** with top-5 concentration falling from 33.3% to 18.2%. **Cumulative theme count roughly doubled (55→102).** Internal-validity checks: 160/160 masked events passed queue conversion and per-sample target validation; zero hard-canon leakage sentinel hits.
- **Juvenile-goblin fictional-world generality test:** A five-year-old goblin in an invented underground world ran 40 days through the same redesigned runtime, producing **42.8% blended repetition, 79 themes over 138 events**, with a non-decaying new-theme rate across blocks (24/21/17/17)—the same operating range as the contemporary-urban persona (39.4%). The inverted sentinel scan found **zero hard real-world intrusions and one soft lexical borrowing across 229 events**.
- **Diagnostic audit (three-year compressed simulation):** Multiple dimensions FAILED or PARTIAL—life-environment handoff FAIL (a small set of places became a "watermark shell"), relationship persistence FAIL (people recurred as advice/comfort sources without becoming durable structures), recursive indecision FAIL (information-gathering and unresolved task lists repeated). One post-architecture qualitative validation showed stronger State progression (student route authority shifting from academic safety toward documentary/creative work as paid copywriting and collaboration accumulated).

No single scalar benchmark of "persona-life growth" is provided; the metrics quantify *mode-lock symptoms* and their *mechanism reversal*, which the authors flag as a deliberate boundary rather than an oversight.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Single author: Mengchen Li**, affiliated with **Latrix** (limengchen@latrix.ai), dated July 8, 2026. The work is disclosed as part of **SoulOS**, a commercial persona infrastructure, with an explicit disclosure boundary (architecture and audit method disclosed; production prompts, schemas, ranking, thresholds, raw logs withheld).
- The paper is unusually **intellectually honest about its limits**: it labels itself a "systems architecture and diagnostic audit method, not a foundation-model training result," repeatedly distinguishes its claims from classical recursive self-improvement (Good, Omohundro) and from HyperAgents, and explicitly states it does *not* claim model-weight improvement or intelligence explosion.
- Quantitative artifacts are released as arXiv ancillary files (eight-model CSVs/JSON, the `action_repetition_eval.py` evaluator, the Doubao probe materials, qualitative case-chain index), which is a meaningful reproducibility gesture for the baseline/stress-test portion.
- No peer-review venue is indicated (arXiv preprint). The authorship is single and industry-affiliated, so the positive validation rests largely on the author's own runs and a reviewer-LLM-based semantic re-keeping rather than independent replication.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (genuinely high on framing, moderate on mechanism):** The conceptual reframing of long-term persona failure as runtime "self-locking"—distinct from training-set model collapse and from output-diversity collapse—and the OSO temporal-authority partition is a real and well-argued contribution. However, the *operational* mechanisms (context masking, per-sample divergence targets, conditional occurrence generation) are only described functionally; the production recipe is intentionally withheld, so an outside reader cannot judge how much of the gain comes from the disclosed architecture versus undisclosed sampling/ranking tricks.
- **Rigor (strong on the baseline, weak on the fix):** The eight-model baseline stress test is the most rigorous part: a fixed canon, public protocol, strictness variants, and a temperature probe that explicitly rules out the "just raise temperature" alternative. By contrast, the headline anti-fixation A/B is a **single persona canon, one run per condition, no variance bands**—the paper itself admits "variance bands and multi-persona long-run replication remain future work." The macro-theme metric is computed by a reviewer-LLM ("a reviewer extracted semantic events... grouped them into cumulative macro themes under a reuse rule"), which is a subjective, non-reproducible evaluator at the center of the central claim.
- **Reproducibility (partially good, fundamentally bounded):** The baseline evaluator and aggregate CSVs are released, which is commendable. But the redesigned-runtime gain—the load-bearing result—depends on private "per-sample divergence targeting" interfaces, masked-context assets "generated through the same bootstrap-and-review flow," and withheld gating/seed mechanisms, making the central positive claim effectively non-reproducible from the public package alone. The paper is transparent that this is a deliberate commercial boundary, but it materially weakens the claim.
- **Significance (relevant but narrow):** For practitioners building long-running companion/persona agents, the self-locking diagnosis and the day-level-audit argument are actionable. But the absence of any human evaluation, any comparison against the named Generative Agents/Agentopia baselines on a shared task, and any multi-persona variance means the significance is currently architectural/diagnostic rather than empirically established.
- **Specific red flags:**
  - The macro-theme repeat-ratio metric is generated by an LLM "reviewer" with no inter-annotator agreement, no released grouping logic, and no sensitivity analysis—yet it is the basis for the 61.8%→36.3% headline. This is the single most important point of fragility.
  - The full-runtime A/B confounds event density: the redesigned conditions produced 144 vs. 160 vs. 85 vs. 170 events, and the paper itself notes "theme totals and per-block new-theme counts should be read alongside repeat ratios"—so the theme-count doubling (55→102) is partly an event-volume artifact.
  - The Kimi row uses a *code-forced* temperature of 1.0 (vs. 0.75 for all others) and a *mixed OpenRouter/Moonshot route*; it is still pooled into the eight-model aggregate, which the paper discloses but which nonetheless contaminates the cross-model comparison.
  - No ablation of the OSO sub-components (multi-timescale revision, separate memory lanes, occurrence hardening, daily real-world surfaces) exists yet—the authors list this as future work.
  - The "autonomy-like trajectory selection" positive case is a single hand-described trajectory, not a measured outcome.

The work should be read as a **well-articulated problem definition and a suggestive internal validation**, not as a proven solution. The authors mostly agree with this characterization in their own Limitations section, which is to their credit.

## Strengths

- **Sharp problem definition.** The self-locking concept—functional trajectory collapse beneath varied surface text—and its attribution to coupled model-side diversity collapse and system-side context gravity is a genuinely useful frame that other long-horizon agent builders can adopt immediately.
- **Honest, falsifiable baseline.** The eight-model stress test, with strictness variants and a temperature probe that explicitly attacks a rival hypothesis, is a credible demonstration that the failure is broad and not a single-model artifact.
- **Architectural clarity.** The OSO loop, the explicit distinction between Occurrence (environment-side) and action (agent-side), and the semantic State machine are well-motivated abstractions with a clear comparison table against perception-state-action controllers and against the Generative Agents/Agentopia family.
- **Transparency about the disclosure boundary.** The paper enumerates exactly what is disclosed vs. withheld, releases the baseline evaluator and aggregate artifacts, and does not overclaim universality (e.g., the juvenile-goblin result is labeled "one fictional canon, one run").
- **Practitioner-relevant insights.** The ~60% token-budget threshold for context-dominance, the day-level-as-phase-boundary argument, and the dual-stream recall / CIBE consequence discussion are directly useful design heuristics for companion-agent builders.

## Limitations

- **Single canon, single run, no variance bands** for the central anti-fixation A/B; the positive validation rests on one primary persona plus one fictional stress case.
- **No scalar metric for persona-life growth**—the action/theme metrics measure mode-lock symptoms, not overall evolution quality or fidelity.
- **Subjective, LLM-based macro-theme evaluator** at the heart of the headline result, with no inter-annotator agreement or released grouping rules.
- **Event-density confound** across the A/B conditions (144/160/85/170 events), partially inflating theme-count comparisons.
- **Withheld production mechanisms** (per-sample targeting, masking assets, gating, ranking) make the central positive claim non-reproducible from public materials.
- **No component-level ablation** of OSO subsystems; no human evaluation; no direct head-to-head with Generative Agents/Agentopia on a shared task.
- **Contaminated Kimi row** (forced temp 1.0, mixed route) pooled into the eight-model aggregate.
- **Attractor/gravity/temporal-authority language is diagnostic, not formal**—no fixed-point or entropy formalization of self-locking yet exists.
- **Response-time social orchestration (CIBE)** is sketched but not evaluated; relationship persistence remains an open FAIL dimension even after the architecture.
- **No treatment of training or fine-tuning**—the system is inference-time only and inherits whatever prior the base model provides.

## Relevance

对长期 Persona / 伴侣型智能体与开放世界 Agent 模拟方向高度相关：本文提出的"自锁"失败模式、OSO 因果环与语义状态机，直接对应长期记忆+人格连续性研究中的核心痛点（context gravity 导致轨迹坍塌），其"日级分辨率是审计相界"的论点可为多时间尺度记忆与反思系统设计提供可借鉴的评估范式。对于关注 LLM Agent 自主演化、长时序行为多样性以及沙箱社会模拟的研究者，这是一份值得阅读的问题定义与诊断方法论文（而非已验证的工程方案）。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would the 61.8%→36.3% macro-theme gain survive a multi-persona, multi-seed replication with variance bands, and does the gain decay over horizons longer than 40 days?
- How much of the anti-fixation effect is attributable to the *disclosed* OSO architecture versus the *withheld* per-sample divergence targeting and masking-asset bootstrap? A minimal public reimplementation would isolate this.
- Can the macro-theme repeat-ratio metric be replaced with a deterministic, released grouper (or a human-validated taxonomy) to remove the single-LLM-reviewer fragility?
- Does the self-locking failure appear in task-oriented long-horizon agents (e.g., coding agents with persistent memory), or is it specific to reward-free persona loops?
- How would AutoPersonas perform against Generative Agents / Agentopia on a *shared* sandbox benchmark where believability, social structure formation, and life-reward are measurable?
- The temperature probe shows higher sampling redistributes channel weights rather than expanding the repertoire—does this generalize beyond Doubao, and what does it imply about where diversity must be injected (decoding vs. context vs. prompt structure)?
- Relationship persistence is a persistent FAIL dimension; what minimal mechanism converts a recurring person mention into a durable social structure with mutual obligations?
- Can self-locking be formalized as a fixed-point / attractor-basin problem with an entropy or reachability measure over life-trajectory distributions, turning the diagnostic language into a provable bound?
