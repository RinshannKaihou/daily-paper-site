> **一句话总结：** 本文将 LLM/Agent 中 KV cache 压缩、prompt 压缩、架构状态有界化、Agent 记忆巩固这四个本被视为独立的研究方向，统一为一个率–失真(rate–distortion)优化问题，并给出层无关下界与七轴分类法；其参考实验表明，在相同预算下可逆(retrieval-backed)记忆在多次压缩后召回保持在 ~0.95，而不可逆(summary)方法降至 0.33–0.56。

> **TL;DR:** This survey unifies KV-cache compression, prompt/context compression, bounded architectural state, and agent memory consolidation as one rate–distortion problem, derives a layer-agnostic Fano-style lower bound (Eq. 2) and a seven-axis taxonomy, and runs a small reference experiment (Qwen2.5-1.5B on NIAH): below ~25% of the full KV budget every eviction scorer collapses to near-zero accuracy, and under repeated compaction reversible retrieval holds fact recall at ~0.95 while irreversible summarization falls to 0.33–0.56 — a ~0.5 recall gap that no single-turn benchmark can expose.

## 核心贡献

- **统一的形式化框架**：将四种看似独立的记忆压缩手段（KV cache 驱逐/量化、prompt 剪枝/蒸馏、架构状态有界化、Agent 记忆巩固）统一建模为一个率–失真优化问题（Eq. 1），并以 data-processing + Fano 不等式推出层无关下界（Eq. 2），证明当预算 B 低于任务所需信息量 I★(Q) 时，任何层都必然以相同速率出错。
- **七轴分类法**：提出粒度、生命周期阶段、损失性/保真度、查询自适应性、可学习性、机制、存储介质七个正交轴，对约 70 种方法（H2O、SnapKV、StreamingLLM、Quest、LLMLingua、Mem0、RAPTOR、NSA、MoBA 等）进行统一归类。
- **跨层机制迁移**：明确建立推理层 ↔ Agent 记忆层的桥梁，把 Memory-Bank 的 Ebbinghaus 遗忘曲线当作 KV 驱逐的内容先验，把 Quest 的"永不驱逐、查询时检索"原则当作 Agent 记忆设计，把 Ada-KV 的输出误差界当作 Agent 摘要的停止规则，并提炼出五条压缩感知设计原则（P1–P5）。
- **COMPACT-Bench 提案**：指出当前所有基准（NIAH/RULER/LongBench/LOCOMO/LongMemEval/SCBench/KVPress）都没有在同一预算轴上跨层比较，也没有测量"重复压缩"误差累积；提出以 bytes-per-token-of-history (BPT) 为统一预算轴，加入 loss-attribution、reversibility、calibrated confidence 三类任务族。
- **参考实验**：在单卡 RTX 4060 + Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct 上验证两条预测——(1) 准确率–预算前沿在预算跌破任务信息量时坍塌；(2) 重复压缩下，可逆算子的召回保持平坦，不可逆算子显著下降。
- **可证伪的预测与开放议程**：给出四条可在各层检验的预测（KV 针召回坍塌、prompt 查询感知右移、架构状态硬性精度悬崖、Agent 误差超线性累积），并按"影响×可解性"排出九项研究议程（含预测式压缩标度律、算子代数、多模态×多智能体共享记忆压缩等）。

## Key Contributions

- **A single rate–distortion formalism** spanning all four compaction layers, with a layer-agnostic lower bound (Eq. 2) derived from the data-processing and Fano inequalities, showing that below the task-conditioned information content I★(Q) every layer — KV, prompt, recurrent, or agent — must err at a rate set by the same expression, and that query-agnostic compaction pays a quantifiable H(Q) penalty.
- **A seven-axis taxonomy** (granularity, lifecycle stage, lossiness/fidelity, query adaptivity, learnability, mechanism, storage substrate) that classifies roughly seventy methods uniformly, with a master table per layer.
- **Explicit cross-layer mechanism transfer**: the inference↔agent-memory bridge ports Memory-Bank's Ebbinghaus decay as a KV-eviction prior, Quest's no-eviction retrieval as an agent-memory design, and Ada-KV's output-error bound as a summarization stop rule, yielding five compaction-aware design principles (P1 never discard irreversibly what you cannot re-derive; P2 condition on the query distribution; P3 separate reversible episodic from lossy semantic tiers; P4 make compaction asynchronous and learnable; P5 budget by marginal task-utility).
- **COMPACT-Bench proposal** extending SCBench and KVPress with a shared bytes-per-token-of-history (BPT) budget axis, three capability-probing task families (loss attribution, reversibility, calibrated compaction confidence), and a repeated-compaction protocol — filling gaps no existing long-context or memory benchmark covers.
- **A small but pointed reference experiment** (Section 14) on a single commodity GPU demonstrating both the accuracy–budget frontier collapse and the reversible-vs-irreversible separation under repeated compaction.
- **A prioritized nine-item open research agenda** mapping the unsolved problems (predictive I★(Q) scaling law, operator algebra for stacking quant×low-rank×evict, episodic→semantic consolidation with stop rule, multimodal×multi-agent shared-memory compaction, auditable rollback, etc.).

## Methodology

This is a position/survey paper, so the "method" is a unifying analytical lens rather than a trained model. The authors proceed in four steps:

1. **Formalization (Section 2).** A compaction scheme is a pair (C_θ, U): a compaction operator C_θ: H → Z producing a budget-constrained representation rate(Z) ≤ B, and a usage operator U that consumes Z to produce an output Ŷ. The design problem is the rate–distortion program min E[ℓ(U(C_θ(H), Q), Y)] s.t. rate(Z) ≤ B (Eq. 1), equivalently the information-bottleneck form max I(Z;Y|Q) s.t. I(Z;H) ≤ B. The layer-agnostic lower bound (Eq. 2), P_e ≥ (H(Y|Q) − B − 1)/log|Y| whenever B < I★(Q), follows from the data-processing and Fano inequalities under the Markov chain Y−H−Z given Q. Three consequences fall out: (i) every layer errs identically below I★(Q); (ii) achievable compression ratio scales as 1/I★(Q); (iii) query-agnostic operators pay H(Q) bits. The field's "importance heuristics" (attention mass, perplexity, mutual information, output-error bounds, LLM-judged salience) are reframed as competing surrogate estimators of the same distortion gradient, and three first-class properties — reversibility (P-rev), query-conditioning (P-q), fidelity profile (P-fid) — are promoted from implicit to central.

2. **Survey through the lens (Sections 4–11).** Each compaction layer is read through the formalism: KV cache (eviction, quantization, low-rank, merging, cross-layer); prompt/context (hard prune, soft gist, distillation); architectural (recurrence, SSMs, associative memory); agent/semantic (MemGPT-style paging, RL-learned policies like Context-Folding/MEM1, reflection-based consolidation, structured stores like Mem0/RAPTOR/Zep, parametric editing like ROME/MEMIT); trainable sparse attention frontier (NSA, MoBA, DeepSeek Sparse Attention, DuoAttention, SeerAttention); multimodal/multi-agent (ToMe, FastV, VisionZip, G-Memory); and the systems/serving substrate (PagedAttention, RadixAttention, DistServe, Mooncake, InfiniGen, ShadowKV). A dedicated Section 10 builds the inference↔agent bridge with a Table 4 mapping the five shared design knobs.

3. **Theory (Section 12).** Existing rigorous results — Haris & Onak's Θ(nd) space lower bound for exact attention, Wen et al.'s impossibility of fixed-state in-context recall, Nichani et al.'s linear parametric capacity, attention-sink necessity (Ran-Milo), U-shaped recall (Liu et al.) — are collected as scattered estimators of pieces of I★(Q), with the gap between worst-case bounds and the reported 80–93% KV reductions explicitly named as the unsolved predictive-compression-scaling-law problem.

4. **Benchmark + reference experiment (Sections 13–14).** COMPACT-Bench places every method on bytes-per-token-of-history (BPT) and reports a full accuracy-vs-budget frontier plus three capability probes. The reference experiment runs on Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct on one RTX 4060: Experiment 1 sweeps six KV methods (SnapKV, StreamingLLM, TOVA, Knorm, ExpectedAttn, Random) at five budgets on natural-filler NIAH (2k–8k tokens, depths 10–90%); Experiment 2 compares an irreversible summarize operator against a reversible retrieve operator over 12 planted key–value facts as the number of compaction events grows from 5 to 25.

## Results & Highlights

- **Accuracy–budget frontier collapse (Experiment 1, Qwen2.5-1.5B, natural-filler NIAH, 1,395 generations):** the full cache scores needle accuracy **1.00**, and below roughly **25% of the full KV budget every eviction method (SnapKV, StreamingLLM, TOVA, Knorm, ExpectedAttn) sits at or near 0% accuracy**, exactly as Eq. (2) predicts once the budget drops under the needle's I★(Q). The random-eviction control is competitive at high budget but collapses fastest, isolating the marginal value each scorer adds.
- **Reversible vs irreversible under repeated compaction (Experiment 2, 12 key–value facts, 5–25 compaction events):** the reversible retrieve operator holds fact recall near **0.95 at every compaction frequency**, while the irreversible summarize operator falls to between **0.33 and 0.56** and is weakest at the highest compaction frequency — a separation of roughly **0.5 in recall** between two operators that are indistinguishable under any single-turn benchmark.
- **Reported practice consolidated from the literature:** KV-cache methods routinely achieve **80–93% KV reductions**, prompt compression reaches **~20×**, quantization reportedly sits **within ~2.7× of the information-theoretic limit**, RL-learned agent compaction (Context-Folding, MEM1, ReSum) reports **3–10× active-context reduction at matched accuracy**, and multimodal visual-token compression achieves an **8–18× reduction at almost no quality cost** — all reconciled by consequence (ii) as the gap between worst-case Θ(nd) I★(Q) and the far smaller data-conditional I★(Q) on realistic inputs.
- **Benchmark gap diagnosis (Table 5):** of nine existing suites (NIAH, RULER, BABILong, LongBench, InfiniteBench, HELMET, LOCOMO, LongMemEval, SCBench, KVPress), none simultaneously supports a multi-budget sweep, cross-layer comparison on one axis, and repeated-compaction measurement; LongMemEval alone observes **~30% accuracy drops on sustained interaction**, and NoLiMa shows literal-match NIAH probes systematically flatter models by measuring I★(Q) only in the lexically-marked easy regime.
- **Four falsifiable predictions**, of which predictions (1) and (4) are tested directly: (1) KV attention-score eviction's needle-recall collapse point tracks the entropy of the answer's location and worsens in mid-context; (4) end-task error grows super-linearly in the number of compaction events for irreversible summarization while reversible retrieval stays flat — the Experiment 2 curve confirms the latter.

## Authors & Credibility

Ashwin Gerard Colaco and Nada Lahjouji are at the University of California, Irvine. This is a 24-page arXiv survey (cs.LG, v1, 9 Jul 2026) that cites ~150 references spanning the KV-cache, prompt-compression, recurrent-architecture, agent-memory, sparse-attention, and serving-systems literatures from 2023–2026, including many 2025–2026 preprints (NSA, MoBA, DeepSeek Sparse Attention, MEM1, ReSum, IterResearch, sleep-time compute, Ada-KV). The authors are explicit and intellectually honest about scope: they flag that many cited results are unrefereed preprints, treat reported compression ratios as provisional, and position their own experiment as a methodology demonstration rather than a leaderboard. The formalism rests on standard, citable information-theory machinery (Tishby's IB, Fano, data-processing) rather than novel proofs, which is appropriate for a unifying survey. The coverage claim in Table 1 — that no prior survey spans the serving substrate + RD/IB objective + cross-layer transfer + unified benchmark simultaneously — is plausible and well-argued relative to the named comparators (Memory in the Age of AI Agents [47], KV Cache Management [58], Memory for Autonomous Agents [31]).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (genuine):** The contribution is the unifying lens, not new algorithms. The rate–distortion/IB framing of context compression is not original — Wang et al. [115] already operationalize it for token selection, and prior surveys [47, 31] group memory types — but the combination of (a) a single objective with a provable layer-agnostic lower bound, (b) coverage that reaches the serving stack and quantization/low-rank math alongside agent memory, (c) explicit mechanism transfer, and (d) a benchmark actually run is a real increment over the closest comparators. The honest framing of "importance heuristics as competing distortion surrogates" and the empty-cell diagnosis in Table 4 (no principled stop rule for agent summarization) are the most original parts.
- **Rigor (mixed):** The lower bound (Eq. 2) is a clean but standard application of Fano; it is not a new theorem, and its cleanest form assumes a query-agnostic operator, which the authors concede. The four "predictions" are really post-hoc readings of known phenomena (U-shaped recall, NIAH collapse, SSM multi-key cliffs) rather than novel predictions validated prospectively. The claim that repeated irreversible summarization grows "super-linearly" is asserted in the formalism but the reference experiment only shows a flat-vs-depressed separation — there is no fitted curve demonstrating super-linear growth, and the plotted recall for the irreversible operator does not visibly curve upward in error with more events.
- **Reproducibility (good for the experiment, weak for the survey claims):** The reference experiment uses a single named open model (Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct) on a single commodity GPU (RTX 4060, 8GB), names all six scorers and their budgets, and the authors state code and configurations accompany the survey. However, absolute numbers are tied to this small model, and the authors correctly warn they should be read as methodology illustration, not a leaderboard. The compression-ratio claims aggregated from the literature (80–93% KV reduction, ~20× prompt, ~2.7× quantization) are drawn from heterogeneous setups that the proposed BPT normalization is "meant to reconcile rather than take at face value" — i.e., they are not directly verified here.
- **Significance (high if the benchmark lands):** If COMPACT-Bench is adopted it could correct a real measurement gap — no current suite holds one budget axis across all four layers while measuring repeated compaction, and the BPT axis is a genuinely useful normalization. The cross-layer transfer prescriptions (Ebbinghaus-as-KV-prior, Ada-KV-bound-as-summarization-stop-rule) are concrete and testable but remain unvalidated proposals.
- **Red flags / things to probe:** (1) The reference experiment uses random eviction as the "diagnostic" control, but random eviction being competitive at high budget on a 1.5B model partly reflects that the chosen scorers add modest value at this scale — the authors admit "the gaps are modest at this scale, and the ordering of methods is beside the point," which weakens any claim that the experiment discriminates among real methods. (2) The 0.33–0.56 vs 0.95 recall gap in Experiment 2 is striking but is essentially the expected result of summarizing away 12 facts vs keeping them archived — it confirms the formalism but is not surprising enough to constitute independent evidence for super-linear error growth. (3) The survey leans heavily on very recent (2025–2026) preprints for its frontier coverage (DeepSeek Sparse Attention, MEM1, ReSum, IterResearch), whose numbers are explicitly flagged as unreproduced, so the "direction of travel" claims inherit that provisional status. (4) The I★(Q) quantity that the entire framework hinges on is conceded to be unmeasured and unestimated for real tasks, so the framework's predictive power currently outruns its operationalization.

## Strengths

- Genuinely unifying formalism with a real (if standard) lower bound that makes KV evictors, prompt pruners, SSM states, and agent summaries commensurable on one bytes-per-token axis for the first time.
- Coverage is broad and current, reaching the serving/systems substrate (PagedAttention, RadixAttention, DistServe, Mooncake, InfiniGen, ShadowKV) and the trainable sparse-attention production frontier (NSA, MoBA, DSA) that agent-only or KV-only surveys omit.
- The five design principles (P1–P5) and the Table 4 bridge are actionable and come with predicted failure modes, not just descriptions.
- The benchmark critique (Table 5) is sharp and correct: the missing axes (multi-budget, cross-layer, repeated-compaction) are precisely what the formalism demands and what no existing suite provides.
- Intellectual honesty in the Limitations section about scope, preprint provenance, and the I★(Q) estimation gap.

## Limitations

- It is a survey: no large-scale empirical validation of the formalism or the benchmark is provided; the reference experiment runs on one small open model on one commodity GPU and explicitly should not be read as a leaderboard.
- The layer-agnostic lower bound (Eq. 2) assumes a query-agnostic operator for its cleanest form and treats I★(Q) as given; estimating I★(Q) for real tasks is itself open and unresolved, so the framework's central quantity is not yet operationalized.
- The "operator algebra" for stacking quant × low-rank × evict across layers is called for but only sketched as an empirical composition map, not a set of proven laws; the authors concede distortion surrogates are not additive and composition order matters.
- Many cited 2025–2026 results (DeepSeek Sparse Attention, MEM1, ReSum, IterResearch) are unrefereed preprints whose compression-ratio claims are drawn from heterogeneous setups and not independently reproduced here.
- The four "falsifiable predictions" are largely retrospective readings of already-known phenomena rather than prospectively validated hypotheses, and the super-linear error-growth claim for repeated irreversible summarization is not cleanly demonstrated by the reference experiment's curve.
- The cross-layer mechanism transfers (Ebbinghaus-as-KV-prior, Ada-KV-bound-as-summarization-stop-rule) remain proposals; their empirical payoff is asserted, not shown.

## Relevance

与用户关注的 cs.LG / cs.CL 高效 LLM 推理与长上下文方向高度相关：该综述直接覆盖 KV cache 压缩、prompt 压缩、稀疏注意力（NSA/MoBA/DSA）等推理效率核心技术，并以率–失真视角将这些技术与 Agent 记忆巩固统一，对设计高效长上下文与 Agent 系统有直接的方法论和基准（COMPACT-Bench）参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can I★(Q) be estimated cheaply enough at compaction time to make the framework's budget-selection prescription operational, and what surrogate (mutual-information, output-error bound, LLM-judged salience) tracks it best across layers?
- Does the reversible-vs-irreversible recall gap (~0.5 in the reference experiment) widen, narrow, or change shape when scaled to frontier models and longer horizons than 25 compaction events?
- Is the claimed super-linear growth of error under repeated irreversible summarization real, or does it saturate? The reference experiment shows a flat-vs-depressed separation but no fitted super-linear curve.
- How should the rate–distortion objective be extended to shared multimodal memory across collaborating agents, where Eq. (2) is stated for a single Z answering a single Q and says nothing about per-agent I★(Q) drawn from one compressed store?
- Can an honest "operator algebra" be built that predicts which stacking pairs (quant × low-rank × evict within a layer; KV × prompt × agent across layers) are synergistic, redundant, or destructive, given that distortion surrogates are non-additive?
- Does lossless prefix reuse (RadixAttention) compose safely with lossy budgeting (InfiniGen/ShadowKV), or does a single lossy decision silently degrade every descendant request that reuses the corrupted prefix?
- How would a calibrated "compaction confidence" signal — knowing that B < I★(Q) — be consumed by an agent to defer, fetch, or escalate fidelity, and can it be made reliable enough to act on?
