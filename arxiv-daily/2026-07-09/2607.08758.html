02 Why this matters

Gives auto-research agents a lineage-coherence test stricter than novelty or fluency — can they actually inherit and extend prior mechanisms, not just emit plausible ideas?

03 Critical edge

⚠ Reproducibility

All leaderboard scores hinge on pre-release model versions (GPT-5.5, Claude Opus 4.7, Qwen3.6-Max-Preview…) that readers cannot independently re-run.

04

Methodology

Each paper maps to a set of typed, evidence-grounded Idea Genome objects. A GenomeDiff aligns predecessor→successor objects — source marked Inherited/Mutated/Lost, targets Novel/External — each with a transition driver and ecology context.

role ∈ { niche · mechanism · observation · limitation · delta · claim }
gi = ( ti, zi, ei, ci ) — type · content · evidence ptr · constraints

Six evolutionary dynamics classify diffs under fixed priority rules: Mutation (YOLO→YOLOv2), Adaptive Radiation (Transformer→ViT), Hybridization (CLIP+LLM→LLaVA), Speciation (Faster R-CNN→DETR), Niche Competition, Isolation. Lineage ≠ co-location: shared task ecology without driver inheritance is competition, not descent.

IG-Bench (2 parts): IG-Exam (closed-form, exact-match, axes T1–T4) and IG-Arena (open generation under Question / Library / Lineage settings, scored by a lineage-conditioned PES = (H+V+S)/3 mirroring Darwin's heredity/variation/selection; ELO as pairwise diagnostic).

Strengths ↑

  • Conceptually original: idea inheritance as a genome / diff alignment object with an auditable layer, not a metaphor.
  • Two-part design (IG-Exam + IG-Arena) bridges understanding & generation on one substrate; their moderate correlation is itself a finding.
  • PES is principled — grounded in Darwin's three conditions, decomposable; α 0.74, human concordance 80%.
  • Broad, current coverage: 14 LLM scientists + a backbone-controlled GPT-5.5 comparison isolating retrieval help from lineage competence.
  • Honest negative findings: agents don't beat the direct LLM on PES; scaffolds hurt T4; structured context reshuffles rather than lifts.

Limitations ↓

  • Six dynamics are operational categories, not an exhaustive theory; one primary driver may flatten multi-mechanism successors.
  • All backbones are pre-release / preview versions readers cannot replicate exactly.
  • LLM-mediated gold labels: genome extraction & diffs are LLM-assisted then audited — ground truth inherits abstraction biases.
  • IG-Arena is modest: 30 tasks × 10 domains × 3 settings = 1,260 proposals; per-domain power is limited.
  • No released baseline recipe (prompts, judge prompts, scaffold configs) in the main text.
  • Value hinges on the unverified assumption that Idea Genome granularity is the right level.

Open threads · 06

Questions for further exploration

  1. Does the six-dynamics taxonomy buy measurable signal over a coarser {lineage / no-lineage} binary, or does discriminative power come from GenomeDiff alignment alone?
  2. Are rankings stable if IG-Exam moves from strict exact-match to partial-credit — is the "compositional bottleneck" partly an artifact of the all-or-nothing metric?
  3. Research agents (AI Scientist v2, CoI-Agent) don't beat the direct LLM on PES — what agentic capability turns retrieval gains into lineage-coherent generation?
  4. Can the Heredity component of PES be optimized directly as a training / compositional-verification signal, not just an eval metric?
  5. Do results generalize beyond the 10 domains — does annotation reliability hold for social-science / humanities lineages?
  6. Given ELO–PES ρ = 0.82, when does PES most diverge from pairwise preference, and can a cheap PES proxy come from ELO + a small coherence probe?
