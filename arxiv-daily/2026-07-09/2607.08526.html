Methodology§III

aria is a single-purpose C/CUDA engine for Stable Audio 3 — no DL framework, no linear-algebra library, no third-party dependency beyond the C math library and a threading runtime. Two backends sit behind one dispatch layer: a vectorized multithreaded CPU path (20 threads on the i9-10900KF) with an 8-bit-integer matmul that doubles as the correctness reference, and a half-precision tensor-core GPU path whose per-step denoising loop is captured once and replayed as a single CUDA graph.

  • Memory replacement, not addition. Once the compressed copy is packed, the full-precision source is freed — low precision reduces resident memory rather than adding to it.
  • Three fidelity gates, no assumed budget. Each precision is scored against fp16 on ΔCLAP, FAD, and Δtaste — each scaled by the noise floor an fp16 re-seed alone induces.
  • In-graph activation steering. The runtime owns every intermediate tensor, so steering is compile-time built-in; at α=0 output is bit-identical to the base model.

Edge milestone. 4-bit is what runs the 1.2 B medium model on an 8 GB Raspberry Pi 5 (~200 s, 0.9 GB resident, 3.6 GB peak) — at degraded quality, but previously impossible. CPU-only: a steered 10 s clip decodes in 2.5 s on small-music (~4× real time).

Strengths+

  • Real systems contribution. A 7.7k-line, zero-dependency runtime that runs a full SOTA music latent-diffusion pipeline on a Raspberry Pi 5 — a genuine gap left by both the ggml family and generic graph engines.
  • Memory-replacement design. "Release fp16 once compressed" turns quantization into a net memory reduction — a concrete, well-motivated engineering choice with a measured payoff.
  • Methodological honesty. The multi-oracle panel is designed to expose (and does expose) the failure mode where the optimized metric rises while independent checks collapse; SALTY/SPICY and the LoRA comparator are reported as negative results.
  • Calibration discipline. fp16 re-seed noise floors as the significance bar, pinned baseline commit, both default and tuned baselines measured, Holm correction across five taste axes.

Limitations!

  • Single-hardware results. All efficiency numbers come from one RTX 3070 and one i9-10900KF; generalization to other GPUs/CPUs/mobile SoCs is not measured.
  • Warm win is narrow. 0.13 vs 0.146 s partly rests on a baseline that disables perf options and recompiles per clip length — a fully tuned official fused-attention baseline could narrow the gap.
  • 4-bit ≠ parity. It crosses every re-seed floor (FAD 125.8 vs 44.0); the medium-on-Pi claim is feasibility at degraded quality, not equivalence.
  • Steering scope. One model family, only learned/distributional oracles; genuine control claimed for only 3 of 5 taste axes, and no human listening study yet.

Open Questions for Further Exploration?

  1. Does the 8-bit "indistinguishable from fp16" result hold on larger DiT music models (e.g. ACE-Step 1.5) or non-music audio, where the CLAP/wav2taste oracles and re-seed floors differ?
  2. Is there a mixed-precision schedule (4-bit weights, 8-bit attention) that recovers fidelity while keeping the medium model within the 8 GB Pi envelope — and is ~200 s latency acceptable for any interactive use case?
  3. Would the cold-start and VRAM advantages survive against a maximally tuned official baseline with a FlashAttention kernel and length-cached compilation?
  4. Can the two-stage funnel be made into an automatic safe-operating-point selector that other representation-editing studies adopt without hand-tuning the α-window?
  5. Given wav2taste's held-out Pearson r ≈ 0.67, how much of the "genuine" SWEET/SOUR/BITTER control will be perceptible to humans in a Bradley–Terry study?
