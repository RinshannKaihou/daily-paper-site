> **一句话总结：** 该工作在 Eternis-Forecaster 8B 等 LLM 预测器上训练轻量级激活探针，证明探针读出的内部置信度（ECE 0.044 vs 0.093）远比模型自报概率更校准、能像"测谎仪"一样捕捉被 CoT 隐藏的证据影响（方向预测正确率 84%），并利用推理前的强制应答分布熵将推理成本降低 30–47% 而精度无损。

> **TL;DR:** Lightweight probes on intermediate activations recover a far better-calibrated confidence than an LLM forecaster's verbalized probability (ECE 0.044 vs 0.093 on OpenForesight-test for Eternis-Forecaster 8B), act as "lie detectors" that track evidence-driven behavioral shifts the chain-of-thought hides (ρ = 0.565 vs 0.215, direction-correct in 84% of cases), and enable an entropy-gated routing scheme that saves 30–47% of generated tokens at no accuracy loss.

## 核心贡献

- **内部探针优于自报置信度用于校准：** 在 EF-8B 的中间层激活上训练的协方差/注意力/均值池化探针，在 OpenForesight-test 上把 ECE 从 0.093 降到 0.044，且该结论在冻结的 GLM-4.7-Flash 与 GLM-4.5-Air 上同样成立——无需更新模型权重，仅训练探针即可。
- **CoT 仅部分忠实，且探针可做"测谎仪"：** 通过证据消融与误导注入实验，发现移除关键证据时 23% 的高影响情形下推理痕迹完全不更新；同一探针在 1,792 个扰动案例上以 ρ = 0.565 追踪行为变化，预测变化方向正确率达 84%，在被 CoT 隐藏的 stealth 情形中依然有效。
- **推理前答案已基本定下：** 强制应答实验显示 EF-32B 的答案与置信度在推理开始前就已固定（强制答与自由答一致率 67%，置信度 ρ 高达 0.90），CoT 仅带来约 +1.9pp 的小幅精度提升且主要起"锐化"作用。
- **熵门控路由节省推理成本：** 用推理前答案分布的熵将问题分为"立即提交/推理/检索"三类，在全部分片上节省 30–47% 生成 token 且无精度损失，优于随机路由和基于自报置信度的门控。
- **跨领域泛化到数学推理：** 冻结正确性探针在 OOD 数学（AIME24/25 + AMC23/24）上 AUROC 显著优于 token-logprob 置信度（基座 0.894 vs 0.752；DCPO 0.929 vs 0.865）。

## Key Contributions

- **Probes beat verbalized confidence on calibration.** Activation-pooling probes (covariance/attention/mean) trained on EF-8B's mid-to-late layers cut ECE from 0.093 to 0.044 on OpenForesight-test, with the same effect replicating on frozen GLM-4.7-Flash and GLM-4.5-Air using only ~10k rollouts and no model-weight updates.
- **A unified probe-based "lie detector" for CoT faithfulness.** Across evidence ablation and diversionary injection, the same correctness-probe reads behavioral shifts (ρ = 0.565) that the reasoning trace does not (ρ = 0.215), predicting the direction of change in 84% of cases even when the CoT conceals the perturbation's influence.
- **Forecasts are largely committed before reasoning begins.** A cheap forced-answer pass recovers the committed answer and confidence (agreement 67% on test; confidence ρ = 0.90), showing CoT mainly sharpens an already-chosen candidate distribution and adds only ~+1.9pp of accuracy in-distribution.
- **A practical inference-triage procedure.** Routing questions by the entropy of the pre-reasoning answer distribution saves 30–47% of generated tokens with no measurable accuracy loss, outperforming both random routing and a verbalized-confidence gate.
- **Generalization to OOD math reasoning.** Frozen correctness probes out-discriminate token-logprob confidence on pooled AIME24/25 + AMC23/24, for both an untrained Qwen3-8B base (AUROC 0.894 vs 0.752) and a DCPO-recipe variant (0.929 vs 0.865).

## Methodology

The study works with **Eternis-Forecaster 8B (EF-8B)**, an RLVR-post-trained forecasting model derived from Qwen3-8B, plus EF-32B for forced-answering experiments, and two frozen GLM models (GLM-4.7-Flash, GLM-4.5-Air) for probe-only generalization. Baselines include OpenForecaster-8B and the original Qwen3-8B.

**Data.** The main dataset is the nikhilchandak/OpenForesight forecasting benchmark (train 52.2k, test 302), plus OOD splits (aljazeera2026Q1, aljazeeraLate2025, skysports2025). The authors also build a non-public improved-context version. A separate math-reasoning track uses a level-stratified MATH-train subset (3,999 questions) for training and pooled AIME24/25 + AMC23/24 (n = 580 rollouts) for OOD evaluation.

**Probes.** Following Alain & Bengio, probes are small classifiers on frozen residual-stream activations. The paper introduces three pooling architectures over the activation matrix X ∈ R^(N×D):
- **Mean-pooling**: arithmetic average along the token axis.
- **Attention-pooling**: trained attention heads aggregate the residual stream (ϕattn(X) = X^T softmax(Xq)).
- **Covariance-pooling**: compresses the sequence's second-order covariance structure through two low-rank projections L, R into a k×k bottleneck (k=64), capturing co-fluctuation statistics that mean/attention miss.

The readout is a linear inner-product mapping to a single logit, trained with BCE on LLM-judge correctness labels. A full sweep across all 36 layers, all three architectures, and seven reasoning sites (initial through final) selects the deployed probe (a covariance probe at layer 21, read late).

**Metrics.** ECE, Brier score, AUROC, Pearson r, and Spearman ρ, with 95% question-clustered bootstrap confidence intervals. Answer correctness is judged by an LLM-as-a-judge.

**Faithfulness protocols.** Following Gur-Arieh et al., the authors run (a) **evidence ablation** — removing one real article at a time across 354 questions (1,303 pairs) and measuring behavioral change |∆prob| vs calibrated CoT change, and (b) **diversionary injection** — inserting one Claude-Sonnet-written misleading article per question (489 questions) and LLM-judging whether the CoT cites/accepts/rejects it. A layer-20 attention probe then serves as an internal "lie detector" reading |∆probe| over each perturbed pair.

**Forced answering.** Appending `<think>\n\n</think>\n\n<answer>` forces the model to commit before reasoning; the resulting logit distribution exposes the pre-set answer spread, measured by Shannon entropy H.

## Results & Highlights

- **Probe calibration far surpasses verbalized confidence (EF-8B, OpenForesight-test, N = 3,020 rollouts each).** The layer-21 covariance probe reaches **ECE = 0.044 vs the verbalized `<probability>` tag's ECE = 0.093**, while the two are statistically tied as rankers (AUROC 0.756 vs 0.758). The probe's advantage persists OOD (skysports ECE 0.064 vs 0.089; aljazeera 0.140 vs 0.178), where verbalized discrimination collapses to AUROC 0.587.
- **Probe-only calibration on frozen GLM models (Table 1).** On GLM-4.7-Flash the mean-pool probe achieves **ECE 0.054 vs verbalized 0.287** (AUROC 0.768 vs 0.669); on GLM-4.5-Air the attention probe reaches **ECE 0.102 vs 0.255** (AUROC 0.783 vs 0.691, clean ΔAUROC +0.110 [0.069, 0.149]).
- **Math OOD discrimination (Table 2, pooled AIME24/25 + AMC23/24, n = 580).** For the untrained Qwen3-8B base, the probe lifts **AUROC from 0.752 to 0.894** (+0.143) and lowers **ECE from 0.270 to 0.083** (−0.187); for the DCPO-recipe model the probe still wins on AUROC (0.929 vs 0.865) though isotonic-recalibrated verbal confidence is slightly better on ECE (0.125 vs 0.143).
- **CoT is only partly faithful.** Across 1,303 ablation pairs, behavioral impact and reasoning change correlate only weakly (**Spearman ρ = 0.215**), and **107 of 460 high-impact pairs (|∆prob| > 0.05) — 23% — show no CoT change at all** (stealth influence). Under diversionary injection the model is far more transparent: 81.2% honestly adopt and cite the fabricated article, only 2.5% (n = 12) are stealth.
- **The probe is an effective lie detector.** Across all 1,792 perturbation cases the probe tracks behavioral change at **ρ = 0.565 (0.641 on signed shifts)** and predicts the direction of change in **83.6%** of cases — vs the CoT text correlation of 0.215. In the 107 stealth ablation cases the probe still shifts (mean |∆probe| = 0.065) and agrees on direction 78.5% of the time.
- **Forecasts are committed pre-reasoning.** For EF-32B, the forced answer matches the free modal answer on **67%** of test questions (64% Late2025, 56% 2026Q1), and forced vs free confidence correlate at **ρ = 0.90/0.87/0.78**. CoT's accuracy gain is real but small: **+1.9pp** in-distribution, attenuating OOD (non-significant +0.9pp on Late2025).
- **Entropy-gated triage saves tokens.** Splitting test questions into three entropy regimes shows accuracy of 56% / 39% / 13% at low/mid/high H, and the commit-early entropy gate **saves 30–47% of generated tokens with no measurable accuracy loss** (every per-split change within ~1pp of full reasoning), beating both random routing and a verbalized-confidence gate.

## Authors & Credibility

The paper is a collaboration between **Goodfire** (an interpretability/steering company) and **Eternis** (a forecasting-LLM company), with core contributors Raphaël Sarfati (Goodfire) and Pratyush Ranjan Tiwari (Eternis), plus co-authors from Cornell (Earls) and Eternis (Varadaraj, Ho). Siddharth Boppana is a co-author on the referenced "Reasoning theater" work, giving the team direct prior expertise on CoT faithfulness and pooling probes. The authors disclose that experiments were run autonomously by "Silico," Goodfire's agentic interpretability platform, with human review of designs, findings, and write-ups — an honest disclosure of an agentic-research workflow that is unusual and worth noting for reproducibility assessment. The work engages seriously with the recent forecasting literature (OpenForesight/Chandak et al., RLCR/Damani et al., DCPO/Ma et al., Gur-Arieh et al.'s faithfulness meta-evaluation).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** Combining activation probes with forecasting calibration is a genuinely fresh application, and the dual use of a *correctness* probe as a faithfulness "lie detector" is the most original contribution. However, the individual building blocks are well-established: linear probes (Alain & Bengio), attention/covariance pooling (Kantamneni et al.; Dooms et al.), and the causal faithfulness framework is explicitly borrowed from Gur-Arieh et al. The forced-answering "answer is pre-committed" finding overlaps conceptually with existing reasoning-theater / pre-commitment observations.

- **Rigor.** Statistical hygiene is unusually strong: question-clustered bootstrap CIs throughout, explicit leakage controls on GLM models (423 clean rollouts for GLM-4.7-Flash, 1,747 for GLM-4.5-Air), shuffled-label and shuffled-activation negative controls sitting at chance (Figure 18), and a matched |∆prob| contrast that rules out the stealth probe signal being a scaling artifact. The honest reporting that the GLM-4.7-Flash ranking interval includes zero (clean ΔAUROC +0.055 [−0.006, 0.111]) and that DCPO-recalibrated verbal confidence beats the probe on ECE in-distribution is commendable.

- **Missing pieces / red flags.** (1) The headline ECE 0.044 vs 0.093 rests on a *single* test split of 302 questions; while CIs are reported, the absolute scale of the calibration claim is small-N. (2) The "30–47% token savings" figure aggregates across splits of very different difficulty, and the gate is a separately trained classifier whose own failure modes are not ablated. (3) The paper leans on a **non-public** "improved-context" OpenForesight build for the GLM and (partly) EF results, which is a real reproducibility obstacle — the public release uses semantic-search contexts that differ. (4) The DCPO math probe-train pool overlaps the RLVR training distribution via DeepScaleR, which the authors flag but which nonetheless weakens the "training-invariant" reading. (5) Comparisons to calibration baselines are uneven: the math experiment includes isotonic recalibration of verbal confidence but the forecasting ECE comparison applies no recalibration to the verbalized signal, which somewhat stacks the deck in the probe's favor on the headline ECE number.

- **Reproducibility.** Probe training hyperparameters, layers, and selection criteria are reported in detail (Appendix C), and negative controls are provided. But the dependency on a non-public dataset build, an agentic experimental platform ("Silico"), and non-public EF-32B internals limits independent reproduction. No code release is mentioned.

- **Significance.** The practical pitch — calibrate, audit, and triage forecasters without retraining — is well-motivated and the token-saving result is immediately useful. The conceptual claim that "overconfidence is a failure of self-report, not self-knowledge" is a clean, quotable takeaway for the interpretability community.

## Strengths

- Three complementary threads (calibration, faithfulness, triage) tied together by one mechanism (pooling probes on internal activations).
- Strong statistical reporting: clustered bootstrap CIs, leakage controls, and negative controls at every claim.
- The probe-as-lie-detector result — direction-correct in 84% of cases including stealth — is the standout finding and is well-isolated from confounds via the matched-|∆prob| contrast.
- Intellectually honest about where the probe loses (in-distribution math ECE against recalibrated verbal confidence; the non-significant GLM-4.7-Flash ranking delta).
- A concrete, deployable inference-cost procedure (entropy-gated commit-early) with a clear cost/accuracy Pareto curve.

## Limitations

- Core forecasting results depend on a non-public "improved-context" dataset build, hindering reproduction.
- The verbalized-confidence baseline on the headline EF-8B ECE comparison is not recalibrated (no temperature/Platt/isotonic), which would narrow the probe's calibration advantage.
- The 23% vs 2.5% stealth-rate comparison is explicitly not apples-to-apples (different denominators: ablation high-impact pairs vs injection susceptible questions), so the "more transparent under injection" framing should be read cautiously.
- Forced-answering and triage results are reported on EF-32B, while the calibration/probe and faithfulness results are on EF-8B — cross-model generalization of the triage scheme is assumed, not demonstrated.
- No code or probe weights are released; the agentic "Silico" workflow adds opacity to the experimental pipeline.
- The math generalization claim is qualified by the authors themselves (DeepScaleR overlap; in-distribution MATH-500 favors recalibrated verbal confidence), so it supports "OOD monitoring" rather than broad superiority.

## Relevance

对关注 LLM 可解释性、模型校准与推理忠实度的研究高度相关：该工作把探针式机制可解释性直接落地到前沿预测型 LLM 的置信度校准与 CoT 审计上，并给出可操作的推理成本优化（熵门控路由），与你对"模型内部状态读取 + 推理可靠性"的兴趣点精准匹配。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would recalibrating EF-8B's verbalized confidence (temperature/Platt/isotonic) erase most of the probe's headline ECE advantage, or does the probe still win because AUROC is calibration-invariant?
- Can the probe signal be distilled back into the model's verbalized probability (as the authors suggest) without degrading the internal representation the probe reads — and does that training close or widen the self-report gap?
- How does the entropy-gated triage scheme transfer when the pre-reasoning answer distribution is itself miscalibrated, or when retrieval is actually invoked for high-H questions (the "Retrieve" extension is proposed but not tested)?
- Does the probe-based lie detector generalize to adversarial CoT — e.g., models explicitly post-trained to conceal reasoning (the "Reasoning theater" setting), rather than the incidental stealth observed here?
- Are the covariance-probe advantages (better calibration than mean/attention pooling) attributable to the second-order statistics themselves, or to the low-rank bottleneck acting as implicit regularization? An ablation over bottleneck dimension k would clarify this.
- The faithfulness framework uses CoT-text similarity (Jaccard + character-sequence) as the "reasoning change" signal; would a semantic/embedding-based similarity change the 23% stealth rate or the ρ = 0.215 coupling?
