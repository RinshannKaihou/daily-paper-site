02Why this matters

Thinking-mode breaks the answer-distribution uncertainty signals the field relies on — and this work offers zero-cost, chain-level signals as a practical path to reliable VLM deployment.

03Critical edge

how it was shown

04Methodology

A controlled Instruct-vs-Thinking ablation on identical POPE samples isolates the effect of thinking-mode decoding from task structure.

Three thinking-mode VLM families at 8–9B scale — Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking, GLM-4.1V-9B-Thinking, InternVL3-8B — plus Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct as a non-thinking control, all run in bfloat16 on a single A100-40GB under greedy decoding.

All signals are free byproducts of one greedy forward pass: per-token Shannon entropy within the <think> block (Hthink), over answer tokens (Hans), and thinking-chain length L. A corrected six-group classification (TP/TN/FP/FN/unclear-chain/unclear-nochain) and conditioning on yes-predictions eliminate the answer-direction confound. Bootstrap 95% CIs use 10,000 resamples (seeded RNG).

Benchmarks
POPE adversarial (1,000 yes/no) · HallusionBench (951) · VQAv2 pilot (300)

Two-stage: refuse "unclear", then refuse top X% by chain signal

what holds up

06Strengths

  • Clean controlled ablation isolating thinking-mode decoding from task structure — the Qwen-Instruct vs Thinking pair on identical data is the strongest single result.
  • Honest reporting: flags the unreliable InternVL3 margin (n_FP=17), the POPE entropy inversion, and that instruct still dominates the chain gate.
  • All signals are free byproducts of one greedy pass — no sampling, no probe training, no extra forward pass.
  • Mechanistic depth: answer-direction confound, epistemic hierarchy (TP→FP→abstain), and the POPE vs HallusionBench abstention-mode distinction.

where it frays

07Limitations

  • "Three families" but only two (Qwen, GLM) carry the full analysis; InternVL3 is headline-only with low FP count.
  • No comparison against sampling-based UQ (self-consistency, semantic entropy) — cost-zero framing is fair but leaves relative-cost open.
  • Greedy-decoding-only; stochastic sampling may rehabilitate answer entropy — identified but unresolved.
  • Scale capped at 8–9B; 32B/72B thinking-mode VLM behaviour unknown. Chain signals still miss "confident hallucinations" (11/34 POPE FPs).

open threads

08Questions for further exploration

  1. Q1Does answer-entropy collapse persist — or get rehabilitated — under stochastic sampling (temp > 0, top-p)? Named the single most actionable experiment.
  2. Q2Why does GLM avoid collapse while Qwen does not — which training-recipe choice controls pre-commitment?
  3. Q3Can chain signals beat a matched-cost self-consistency baseline (Hthink vs k=5 majority vote)?
  4. Q4Do Hthink and chain length hold at 32B/72B, or is collapse a small-model, incomplete-pre-commitment phenomenon?
  5. Q5Is the answer-direction confound a general property of yes/no VLM tasks, or specific to POPE's object-presence framing?
  6. Q6Can "confident hallucinations" (low-entropy wrong chains) be caught by combining chain signals with representation-level probes (e.g. HALP)?
