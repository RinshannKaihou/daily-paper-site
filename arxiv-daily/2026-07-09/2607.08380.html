Methodology §04

Rigorous theory · dynamical systems + differential geometry

Builds on the program of prior work [39] for an overparametrised least-squares objective ℓ(w)=½‖τ−f(w)‖², f:ℝp→ℝq. A hierarchy of geometric assumptions (Figure 1) makes the normal form tractable:

  • 3.1τ is a regular value of f, so the minimiser set M = f⁻¹{τ} is a smooth submanifold (NTK full-rank along M).
  • 3.3Top eigenvalue λ₁ is smooth on M\S with a smooth top-eigenvector field ν₁.
  • 3.4F ⊂ M\S is a submanifold of flat minima along which λ₁ is Morse-Bott (geodesically strongly convex normal to F), plus a ν₁-invariance condition.
  • 3.5A regularity condition on a coefficient αη (built from D³ℓ, D⁴ℓ, and a Hessian split Aη) guaranteeing the normal-form PDE is solvable.

Key technical novelty: a centre-manifold dimension-reduction step (q > 1 → q = 1), and a new method for solving a PDE that is singular along the entire manifold F — existing literature [56] only handles PDEs singular at a point. This same machinery repairs a flawed coordinate-transformation proof in [39].

Strengths §06

  • Clean, substantial generalisation of the [39] program — all three regimes carried over, matrix-factorisation case fully worked.
  • Honest intellectual posture: openly flags the flaw in [39]'s proof, the conditional nature of Thm 5.2, and the purely-local scope.
  • The novel singular-PDE technique and the fibre-bundle / Morse-Bott structural results are independently interesting.
  • The normal form itself is conceptually illuminating: GD = RGD on λ₁ ⊕ flip bifurcation ⊕ linear contraction.

Limitations §07

  • Theorems are strictly local — valid only near F; say nothing about arbitrary init or progressive-sharpening / EoS phases.
  • Critical theorem (5.2) is conjectural in general (Conjecture D.7); Assumption D.12 verified only for L = 2.
  • Experiments are minimal — 3-layer 2×2, 5 trials per regime; no larger problems, deep nets, baselines, or code.
  • Geometric assumptions unverified for realistic neural networks; regimes beyond supercritical left to future work.

Questions for further exploration §08

  1. Can the parabolic stable-foliation conjecture (D.7) actually be proved — extending Thm 5.2 to L ≠ 2 matrix factorisation?
  2. Do Assumptions 3.3–3.5 hold for genuine deep networks (wide MLPs, transformers on real data), or are they specific to matrix factorisation?
  3. Can the normal form design step-size schedules or optimisers that target better-generalising submanifolds of F?
  4. How do predictions extend to progressive-sharpening / edge-of-stability regimes the authors exclude — is there a global version?
  5. What happens beyond the supercritical regime — higher-periodicity and chaos [16] — can the fibre-bundle / bifurcation structure predict these transitions?
