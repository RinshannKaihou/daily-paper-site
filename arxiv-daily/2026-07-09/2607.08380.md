> **一句话总结：** 本文将先前关于大步长梯度下降（GD）在"孤立平坦极小值"附近的正则形式与三类收敛定理，推广到"任意余维数"和"平坦极小值流形"的更一般情形，并证明该框架适用于深度矩阵分解——其中平坦极小值流形被证明是球面乘积上的纤维丛，且锐度沿该流形满足 Morse-Bott 性质。

> **TL;DR:** This paper generalises the large-step-size gradient descent (GD) normal form and its three convergence theorems (subcritical, critical, supercritical) of prior work [39] from codimension-1 settings with an *isolated* flat minimum to *arbitrary codimension* least-squares problems with a *manifold* of flat minima. Applied to deep matrix factorisation (verifiable numerically on 3-layer 2×2 problems across 5 trials per regime), the authors prove the flat minima form a fibre bundle over a product of spheres and that the sharpness is Morse-Bott along it; critical-regime iterates converge to F at rate Θ(t^{-1/2}) versus a flip-bifurcation period-2 orbit of amplitude Θ((ηλ₁|_F − 2)^{1/2}) in the supercritical regime.

## 核心贡献

- 将大步长 GD 的正则形式从余维数 1（单标量输出、孤立平坦极小值）推广到任意余维数（向量化输出、含任意多观测的回归问题），覆盖了此前结果无法处理的实际场景。
- 证明并形式化了三类收敛定理在更一般设定下的对应版本：亚临界（指数收敛到次优平坦极小值）、临界（以 Θ(t^{-1/2}) 多项式速率收敛到 F）、超临界（指数收敛到沿 ν₁|_F 的周期 2 轨道，振幅 Θ((ηλ₁|_F − 2)^{1/2})）。
- 通过引入中心流形定理实现降维，并提出一种新的方法求解在平坦极小值流形上奇异的一阶偏微分方程（PDE）——该方法即便在 PDE 理论中也可能是新结果，具有独立价值。
- 证明深度矩阵分解问题契合本框架，并给出若干关于其损失景观的新结构性结论：平坦极小值集合 F 是球面乘积 ∏ S^{d_l−1} 上的纤维丛；锐度 λ₁ 沿 F 满足 Morse-Bott（即沿法方向强凸），并给出 ∇²_M λ₁|_{ν_M F} 特征值的精确刻画（如方向 (i) 上恒为 4σ₁^{2−4/L}，L=2 时为 4）。
- 修正了先前工作 [39] 中正则形式证明的一个缺陷（一个关键坐标变换的证明原本有误），并对其进行了严谨重建。

## Key Contributions

- Extends the normal form for large-step-size GD from codimension-1 problems (single scalar output, isolated flat minimum) to **arbitrary codimension** least-squares problems with vector-valued outputs and arbitrarily many observations — a setting of genuine practical relevance.
- Generalises all three convergence regimes of [39]: **subcritical** (exponential convergence to a suboptimally flat minimum), **critical** (Θ(t^{-1/2}) polynomial convergence to F), and **supercritical** (exponential convergence to a period-2 orbit along ν₁|_F of amplitude Θ((ηλ₁|_F − 2)^{1/2})).
- Introduces a **dimension-reduction technique** via centre manifold theory to reduce effective dimension q > 1 down to q = 1, and develops a **novel method for solving a PDE that is singular along the entire flat-minima manifold F** (whereas [39] only needed to solve a PDE singular at a point using existing literature [56]).
- Proves that **deep matrix factorisation fits the framework**, yielding new structural results about its loss landscape: the global flat minima F form a **fibre bundle over a product of spheres ∏ S^{d_l−1}**, and the sharpness λ₁ is **Morse-Bott** along F, with explicit eigenvalue spectra for ∇²_M λ₁|_{ν_M F} (e.g., 4σ₁^{2−4/L} on conjugation directions, blowing up near S and lower-bounded by 4σ₁/(σ₁ − σ_{d₂}) > 4 at the "balanced solution" for L = 2).
- **Rectifies a flawed proof** in [39]: one of the coordinate transformations underlying the normal form had an incorrect proof, and the paper provides a highly non-trivial correction involving the singular PDE.
- Validates the theory with **numerical experiments** on 3-layer, 2×2 matrix factorisation problems across all three regimes (5 independent trials each).

## Methodology

The paper is a **rigorous theoretical analysis** grounded in dynamical systems and differential geometry, building on the program initiated by [39]. The setting is an overparametrised least-squares objective ℓ(w) = ½‖τ − f(w)‖² with f: ℝ^p → ℝ^q (p > q ≥ 1).

**Geometric assumptions (the hierarchy in Figure 1):**
- **Assumption 3.1** — τ is a regular value of f, so the minimiser set M = f⁻¹{τ} is a smooth (C^∞) submanifold of ℝ^p (equivalent to NTK being full-rank along M).
- **Assumption 3.3** — the set S where λ₁ = λ₂ is not all of M, so the top eigenvalue λ₁ is smooth on an open set M\S with a smooth top eigenvector field ν₁.
- **Assumption 3.4** — there is a submanifold F ⊂ M\S of flat minima along which λ₁ is **Morse-Bott** (i.e., geodesically strongly convex in directions normal to F), plus an invariance condition on span(ν₁|_F).
- **Assumption 3.5** — a regularity condition on a coefficient α_η (built from D³ℓ, D⁴ℓ, and A_η involving the Hessian split along ν₁ vs. ν_{2:q}) that guarantees the normal-form PDE is solvable to zeroth order and C¹.

**Normal form (Theorem 4.1, informal):** Under these assumptions, about any x̄ ∈ F there is an η-dependent C¹ change of coordinates (x, y₁, y_{2:q}) in which GD decouples into three components:
- **GD_x** = Riemannian GD on λ₁ along M, with step size governed by y₁² (so λ₁ descends along M).
- **GD_{y₁}** = a **flip bifurcation** in the top eigendirection ν₁, whose linear part is (1 − ηλ₁(x))y₁ + y₁² + higher order.
- **GD_{y_{2:q}}** = a linear contraction I_{q−1} − ηΛ_{2:q}(x) along the remaining (smaller) eigendirections.

**Three convergence regimes (Section 5):**
- *Subcritical* (η < 2/λ₁|_F): after a finite transient T, iterates enter the stable set and converge at rate O(β^t) to a suboptimally flat minimum.
- *Critical* (η = 2/λ₁|_F): iterates converge at rate Θ(t^{-1/2}) to F. (Hardest case; proved for L = 2 matrix factorisation and otherwise rests on Assumption D.12 and Conjecture D.7 — a parabolic stable-foliation conjecture.)
- *Supercritical* (η slightly > 2/λ₁|_F): iterates converge exponentially to a period-2 cycle along ν₁|_F.

**Case study — matrix factorisation (Appendix E):** For L-layer factorisation with d_l ≥ d_L and simple top singular value σ₁, all assumptions are shown to hold; F is proved to be a fibre bundle over ∏ S^{d_l−1} with fibre an open subset of the lower-dimensional solution manifold, and the spectrum of ∇²_M λ₁|_{ν_M F} is computed explicitly.

## Results & Highlights

This is primarily a theory paper. The "results" are theorems plus small-scale numerical simulations. Concrete quantitative claims:

- **Critical-regime convergence rate:** Theorem 5.2 — GD iterates converge to a point in F at rate **Θ(t^{-1/2})** (verified in Figure 4 on a 3-layer, 2×2 matrix factorisation problem, 5 independent trials, with reference t^{-1/2} dotted lines matching the asymptotic slope).
- **Supercritical period-2 orbit:** Theorem 5.3 — for sufficiently small η > 2/λ₁|_F, iterates converge to a period-two orbit of **amplitude Θ((ηλ₁|_F − 2)^{1/2})** along span(ν₁|_F), at rate **O((1 − C(ηλ₁|_F − 2))^t)** for some C > 0 (Figure 5, 5 trials).
- **Subcritical convergence rate:** Theorem 5.1 — after a transient of **T = O(y₁^{2γ} · (λ₁(x) − λ₁|_F)/(2/η − λ₁|_F))**, iterates converge at rate **O(β^t)** to a suboptimally flat minimum whose sharpness gap satisfies **λ₁(x_∞) − λ₁|_F ≥ exp(−O(y₁,T² (1 − β²)⁻¹)) · (λ₁(x_T) − λ₁|_F)** (Figure 3).
- **Matrix-factorisation Hessian spectrum:** Eigenvalues of ∇²_M λ₁|_{ν_M F} on conjugation-by-scaling directions are **constant at 4σ₁^{2−4/L}** (equal to **4** when L = 2); on conjugation-by-mixing directions they **blow up to infinity** approaching S, achieving a lower bound of **4σ₁/(σ₁ − σ_{d₂}) > 4** at the "balanced solution" for L = 2 — all strictly positive, hence Morse-Bott (Proposition E.5).
- **Fibre-bundle structure:** Up to a linear isometry preserving f, every flat minimum factorises as block-diagonal (σ₁^{1/L}, 0; 0, W_l), exhibiting F as a fibre bundle over ∏_{l=1}^{L−1} S^{d_l−1} (Proposition E.2, Figure 2).

No benchmark comparisons against other optimisers or methods are reported — the experiments are designed to *validate the theoretical predictions*, not to establish SOTA.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely substantial. Extending from codimension 1 / isolated minimum to arbitrary codimension / manifold of minima is a real generalisation, not a cosmetic one — it requires a new dimension-reduction step (centre manifold theorem) and, notably, the authors **had to invent a method to solve a PDE singular along an entire manifold**, because existing literature [56] only handles PDEs singular at a point. The matrix-factorisation structural results (fibre-bundle over product of spheres, explicit Morse-Bott spectrum) are also novel and "not noted in prior work [42, 40]." The authors additionally fix an actual flaw in the proof of [39].

- **Rigor:** High overall. Theorems are precisely stated with explicit assumptions (the hierarchy of Assumptions 3.1/3.3/3.4/3.5 is clean and each is motivated, Figure 1). Proofs are deferred to appendices and use deep invariant-manifold machinery.

- **Reproducibility (the weak point):** The numerical "experiments" are only on **tiny 3-layer 2×2 matrix factorisation** problems with 5 trials per regime — they validate the *asymptotic shape* of the predicted dynamics (Figures 3-5) but provide no scaling evidence, no comparison to other methods, and no code release is mentioned. A reader cannot easily confirm the theorems extend to L > 2 or larger dimensions from the experiments alone.

- **Significance:** Conceptually valuable for the edge-of-stability / large-step-size literature — it rigorously formalises *why* GD behaves as "RGD on sharpness along the solution manifold coupled to a flip bifurcation and stable contractions," generalising [39]'s picture to the regime actually needed for applications like matrix factorisation. However, the authors are admirably explicit that **the theorems are local** and do *not* address the "progressive sharpening" or "edge of stability" regimes proper — only the tail-end after the flat-minima manifold has been reached. So the link to practical deep-network training is still indirect.

- **Red flags / honest caveats:**
  - **Theorem 5.2 (critical regime) is conditional.** In its most general form it relies on **Assumption D.12** (invariance of a submanifold) and **Conjecture D.7** (a stable-foliation theorem for *parabolic* dynamical systems, which the authors state is "outside the scope of this paper" and unproven). The authors can prove D.12 only for **two-layer** matrix factorisation (Proposition E.7); for L ≠ 2 the theorem rests on a conjecture even though "it appears to hold empirically."
  - **No baselines, no benchmarks, no real-world learning experiments** — a pure theory paper, which is fine for its genre, but readers expecting empirical validation of large-step training will not find it here.
  - **The framework's geometric assumptions (esp. 3.4/3.5) are non-trivial to verify**; whether they hold for genuine deep networks (beyond matrix factorisation) is unaddressed.

## Strengths

- A clean, substantial generalisation of a recent theory program, with all three convergence regimes carried over and the matrix-factorisation case fully worked out.
- Honest intellectual posture: the authors openly flag the flaw in [39]'s proof, the conditional nature of Theorem 5.2, and the boundaries of their theory (purely local; does not cover progressive-sharpening/EoS proper).
- The novel PDE-solving technique and the fibre-bundle / Morse-Bott structural results on matrix-factorisation loss landscapes are independently interesting contributions.
- The normal form itself is conceptually illuminating: GD = RGD on λ₁ along M + flip bifurcation in ν₁ + linear contraction in ν_{2:q}.

## Limitations

- **Theorems are strictly local** — valid only near the flat-minima manifold F, and the authors acknowledge they say nothing about convergence from arbitrary initialisations or about the progressive-sharpening / edge-of-stability phases.
- **The critical-regime theorem (5.2) is conjectural in general form** (Conjecture D.7, a parabolic stable-foliation result), and Assumption D.12 is verified only for L = 2 matrix factorisation.
- **Experiments are minimal** — 3-layer 2×2 matrix factorisation, 5 trials per regime; no larger problems, no deep networks, no baselines, no code referenced.
- **Verification of the geometric assumptions for realistic neural networks is not attempted**, leaving the practical reach of the theory open.
- Beyond the supercritical regime (higher-periodicity / chaotic attractors at still larger step sizes) is explicitly left as future work.

## Relevance

这项工作为理解大步长梯度下降的隐式偏置（向平坦极小值收敛）提供了严格的动力学系统理论，与"edge of stability"、"隐式偏置"、"损失景观几何"等优化与深度学习理论方向直接相关。对关注大模型训练优化、锐度感知、平坦极小值泛化的研究者有较高的理论参考价值，但其纯理论与矩阵分解的局限使其距离实际大模型训练仍有一定距离。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the parabolic stable-foliation conjecture (Conjecture D.7) actually be proved, and would a proof extend Theorem 5.2 to L ≠ 2 matrix factorisation rigorously?
- Do Assumptions 3.3–3.5 hold for any genuine deep network architectures (e.g., wide MLPs or transformers on real data), or are they essentially specific to matrix factorisation and quadratic-like problems?
- Can the normal form be used to design **step-size schedules or optimisers** that deliberately target distinguished (better-generalising) submanifolds of the flat-minima manifold, as the authors speculate in the conclusion?
- How do the predictions extend to the **progressive-sharpening and edge-of-stability regimes** that the authors explicitly exclude — is there a global version of this picture?
- What happens for step sizes *beyond* the supercritical regime, where prior work [16] reports higher-periodicity and chaos — can the fibre-bundle / bifurcation structure predict these transitions?
