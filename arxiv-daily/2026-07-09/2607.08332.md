> **一句话总结：** XALPHA 把 Alpha 挖掘重构为一个报告驱动的、记忆驱动的"假说→代码"闭环研究流程：通过 RMA 层把外部研报吸收为结构化 A/B/C 记忆，Macro/Micro/Cross 三脑分别负责主题规划、因子代码进化与三对齐校验、以及经验反馈归因；在 CSI300 10 日预测上，其测试集 IR 达到 1.5853（年化超额收益 14.12%），远超 CogAlpha、R&D-Agent(Q) 等 LLM alpha 挖掘基线。

> **TL;DR:** XALPHA reframes alpha mining as a closed-loop "hypothesis-to-code" research loop run by an LLM-based AI Quant Researcher: a Report-to-Memory Absorption (RMA) layer distills external research reports into structured OHLCV-eligible memory, and three cooperating "brains" (Macro planning, Micro factor-code evolution with ex-ante tri-alignment, Cross feedback consolidation) iteratively discover, validate, and learn from factors. On CSI300 (10-day open-to-open return, test 2022-2025), it reaches IR = 1.5853 and AER = 14.12%, roughly 3.4x the portfolio IR of the best alpha-mining baseline R&D-Agent(Q) (0.4689), while trading a small RankIC deficit to CogAlpha (0.0489 vs 0.0590) for much stronger held-out portfolio performance.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 XALPHA 这一"AI 量化研究员"范式**：把 Alpha 挖掘从孤立的因子生成/搜索，升级为"阅读研报 → 提出假说 → 写成可执行因子代码 → 经验验证 → 反思反馈 → 跨周期演化"的闭环研究流程，而非一次性回测驱动的搜索。
- **Report-to-Memory Absorption（RMA）层**：用 A/B/C 三层分类把外部研报片段转化为与每日 OHLCV 因子契约兼容的结构化可检索记忆——A 层做 OHLCV 可行性 KEEP/DROP 过滤，B 层归入机制大类，C 层构建 48 个 Research Archetype，避免把研报结论硬编码进因子公式。
- **三脑闭环架构**：Macro Brain 做周期级主题规划与 Archetype 路由（含固定/粗引导/记忆驱动三种模式）；Micro Brain 把假说池转为可执行 Python 因子代码并通过 AST gate + TriAlign-Judge（假说/代码/金融合理性三对齐）+ 截断/扰动单元测试做事前校验，再以 mutation/crossover/refinement 做机制级进化；Cross Brain 用 Factor-to-Archetype Attribution（FAA）把经验结果归因回分类体系并生成 GOOD/BAD 反馈，写入 archetype/generation/cycle 三级记忆。
- **在 CSI300 上验证有效性**：相比 LightGBM、CNN、AutoAgent、CogAlpha、R&D-Agent(Q) 等 16 个预测与 alpha 挖掘基线，XALPHA 在 IC、ICIR、RankICIR、AR、AER、IR 上整体领先，且产出可解释、低相关的因子库（跨主题 87.1% 因子对 |corr| < 0.3）。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **XALPHA**, an AI Quant Researcher that formulates alpha discovery as a report-grounded, memory-driven, continuous hypothesis-to-code research loop, explicitly moving beyond one-shot factor generation and isolated backtest-driven search.
- Proposes the **Report-to-Memory Absorption (RMA) layer**, which converts external research reports into structured, retrievable A/B/C memory compatible with the daily OHLCV factor contract (A = OHLCV eligibility KEEP/DROP; B = mechanism family; C = 48 actionable Research Archetypes) rather than stuffing raw text into prompts or hard-coding report claims into formulas.
- Builds a **closed-loop multi-brain architecture**: Macro Brain (cycle-level theme planning and archetype routing with fixed / coarse-guided / memory-driven modes), Micro Brain (hypothesis-to-Python-code generation, AST gating, ex-ante tri-alignment verification of idea/code/financial-plausibility, leakage unit tests, and mechanism-level mutation/crossover/refinement evolution), and Cross Brain (Factor-to-Archetype Attribution plus GOOD/BAD feedback written to archetype-, generation-, and cycle-level memory).
- Designs **multi-gate factor selection** (normal parent pool vs elite archive vs cycle-final library admission) blending winsorized RankIC/IC, rolling out-of-sample robustness, and a complexity discount, with explicit quality floors.
- Empirically demonstrates on **CSI300 (10-day target)** that XALPHA discovers more predictive and robust, yet still interpretable and low-correlation, alpha factors than representative ML, deep-learning, and LLM alpha-mining baselines.

## Methodology

XALPHA is organized as an iterative research loop with three cooperating components on top of a shared multi-source memory.

**1. Report-to-Memory Absorption (RMA).** External reports/papers are ingested, chunked, and filtered through a three-layer A/B/C taxonomy. The **A-layer** is an OHLCV eligibility screen: a fragment is KEEP only if its core mechanism can be directly observed, inferred, or stably proxied from daily open/high/low/close/volume; fundamentals, order-book, tick, intraday, macro, and sentiment content are DROP. The **B-layer** routes retained evidence to broad mechanism families (trend/momentum, reversal/mean-reversion, volatility regime, price-volume interaction, liquidity proxies, directional pressure, drawdown repair, etc.). The **C-layer** specializes each family into fine-grained Research Archetypes (48 in total) that are research cues, not factor formulas. This produces report-grounded knowledge memory that later seeds hypothesis generation.

**2. Macro Brain (research planning).** At the start of each cycle it picks a routing mode — fixed-theme, coarse-guided, or memory-driven (default after 5 coarse-guided warm-up cycles). It selects 1 primary B-layer plus 3-4 supporting B-layers, narrows candidate C-layer archetypes, retrieves report-grounded research paths, and assembles an archetype-guided hypothesis pool handed to the Micro Brain. Memory-driven routing uses accumulated GOOD/BAD summaries, cycle-level outcomes, and archetype coverage to steer away from saturated or repeatedly failed directions.

**3. Micro Brain (hypothesis-to-code factor evolution).** Active research agents turn the hypothesis pool into executable Python factor code operating on OHLCV. Each candidate passes a quality pipeline: an **AST gate** (syntax, unsupported imports/fields, static future leakage such as negative shifts, excessive complexity), a **TriAlign-Judge** that verifies ex-ante consistency among the hypothesis idea, the code logic, and the financial rationale, numerical validation (drop factors whose invalid/extreme ratio exceeds 30%), and **unit tests** (truncation and future-noise perturbation) to catch dynamic leakage. Surviving factors then undergo **mechanism-level evolution** (mutation = mechanism variant; crossover = recompose two parents' mechanisms; refinement = simplify while preserving core mechanism). **Novelty injection** at generations 3 and 7 generates fresh seeds filtered against the current pool at a correlation threshold of 0.95 to refresh the search space.

**4. Factor evaluation and selection.** Cross-sectional IC, ICIR, RankIC, RankICIR are computed on winsorized, direction-aligned signals, with 5-year-train / 6-month-OOS / 3-month-step rolling windows for temporal robustness. **Normal selection** keeps the parent pool: `s_normal = 0.70·α_train + 0.30·r_train,OOS_evo`, with a RankIC floor of 0.005, positive-ratio floor of 0.50, top 45% during warm-up. **Elite selection** preserves strong mechanisms: `s_elite = 0.70·α_train+val + 0.30·r_train+val,OOS_elite`, with a RankIC floor of 0.01, positive-ratio floor of 0.55, top 15% during warm-up. **Library admission** at cycle finalization re-materializes series, rechecks leakage, recomputes train-val metrics, and admits the top portion above a 0.75 score threshold.

**5. Cross Brain (feedback consolidation).** Because repair/mutation/crossover/refinement change a factor's final mechanism, **Factor-to-Archetype Attribution (FAA)** re-attributes elite factors to their final B-layer family and C-layer archetype before feedback is written. Outcomes become compact **GOOD feedback** (validated mechanisms, reusable principles, avoid-copying constraints) and **BAD feedback** (failure types, failed assumptions, avoidance rules, repair conditions). This feeds three memory levels: archetype-level verified hypotheses, generation-level short-term feedback for novelty injection, and cycle-level summaries that guide the next cycle's theme and routing.

**Experimental protocol.** CSI300 via Qlib daily panels; prediction target is the 10-day future adjusted open-to-open return `y = O^adj_{i,t+11}/O^adj_{i,t+1} − 1`. Chronological split: train 2011-01-01–2020-12-31, validation 2021, test 2022-01-01–2025-12-31. LLM backend is a locally deployed **gpt-oss-120b**; downstream alpha model is Ridge regression by default. Portfolio backtest uses Qlib's top-50/drop-5 ranking rebalanced at open, with buy cost 0.05%, sell cost 0.15%, min 5 CNY per order, excess return measured net of costs vs the CSI300 benchmark. Runtime: initial pool 64, parent pool 80, 10 generations/cycle, 6 cycle-final candidates, novelty-injection interval 4.

## Results & Highlights

On CSI300 under the 10-day open-to-open setting (Table 2, test 2022-2025), the quantitative claims are:

- **Portfolio IR**: XALPHA reaches **IR = 1.5853**, versus the strongest alpha-mining baseline **R&D-Agent(Q) at 0.4689** and **CogAlpha at 0.3743** — a roughly 3.4x improvement over the best alpha baseline, the largest single margin in the table.
- **Annualized Excess Return (AER)**: XALPHA achieves **AER = 0.1412 (14.12%)**, compared with **R&D-Agent(Q) 0.0416 (4.16%)** and **CogAlpha 0.0381 (3.81%)** — again about 3.4x the best alpha baseline.
- **IC stability (ICIR / RankICIR)**: XALPHA scores **ICIR = 0.2573** and **RankICIR = 0.3412**, beating **CogAlpha (0.2040 / 0.2937)** and **LightGBM (0.2039 / 0.2674)**, the strongest ML baseline.
- **RankIC trade-off**: XALPHA's **RankIC = 0.0489** is lower than **CogAlpha's 0.0590** (the table's highest rank predictiveness), but XALPHA's portfolio diagnostics (AR 0.1792, AER 0.1412, IR 1.5853) dominate CogAlpha's (0.0639 / 0.0381 / 0.3743), which the authors attribute to a more useful and less redundant factor library rather than raw single-factor rank score.
- **Factor diversity (Figure 2)**: across 60 elite factors sampled from 6 themes, the average absolute pairwise correlation is **0.142** (median 0.099) and **87.1% of factor pairs have |corr| < 0.3**; even within a single theme (50 factors) the average is 0.234 with 68.0% below 0.3, indicating genuine mechanism diversity rather than redundant variants.
- **Interpretable elite factor example**: the `regime_overshoot_pressure_decay_20d_ma20_vol20` factor (regime-dependent reversal after downside overshoot) reaches **train-val IC = 0.0382, RankIC = 0.0564** and **test IC = 0.0440, RankIC = 0.0634**, with raw AST complexity 14.788 — strong test performance consistent with a readable, mechanism-tagged implementation.
- **Computational cost**: ~15 seconds per generated factor, ~16 minutes per generation, ~3 hours per full mining cycle, running on two H100 GPUs with a locally deployed model and zero external API cost.

No quantitative results reported beyond CSI300 — the paper deliberately confines evaluation to this single universe and horizon (see Limitations).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Genuine and well-scoped. The principal new idea is the RMA layer that turns external reports into a structured A/B/C OHLCV-eligible taxonomy (with an explicit eligibility gate that drops fundamentals/order-book/macro/sentiment), combined with a tri-alignment judge that ex-ante checks idea/code/financial-plausibility consistency before any backtest. The framing as a closed-loop Macro/Micro/Cross "brain" architecture is a reasonable synthesis of autonomous-research-agent ideas (AI Scientist, AI Co-Scientist, EvoScientist) ported to quant — incremental conceptually, but the OHLCV-grounded memory and FAA re-attribution are concrete and non-trivial.
- **Rigor — the headline IR is the central concern.** An out-of-sample Information Ratio of **1.5853 net of costs** over a 4-year test (2022-2025) is exceptionally high for liquid large-cap A-shares and is ~3.4x the next-best alpha method. The paper repeatedly (and creditably) hedges this as a "held-out portfolio diagnostic rather than a full portfolio-construction claim," but it never reconciles *why* a Ridge regression over auto-mined OHLCV factors would outperform sophisticated systems by this margin. Plausible explanations (capacity-constrained top-50 universe, lookahead-friendly target alignment, lucky regime, overfitting of the memory loop to CSI300) are not interrogated. The RankIC ordering is actually *inverted* relative to the portfolio story (CogAlpha's RankIC of 0.0590 > XALPHA's 0.0489), which should make any reader cautious about the portfolio magnitude.
- **Missing ablations**. The "component analysis" (Tables 3-4) is purely **qualitative case studies** of RMA and memory routing — there is no quantitative ablation decomposing the contribution of RMA memory, the Macro routing modes (fixed vs coarse-guided vs memory-driven), the TriAlign-Judge, the novelty injection, or the Cross Brain FAA. For a system whose entire claim is that these components compose into a superior loop, this is a notable gap.
- **Generalization is untested.** Everything is run on a single universe (CSI300) and a single horizon (10-day open-to-open). No CSI500/CSI1000, no US/equivalent market, no 1-day/5-day/20-day horizons. The conclusion lists these as future work, but the strong headline numbers are asserted from one setting.
- **Data-contract realism.** RMA's A-layer explicitly discards fundamentals, order book, tick, intraday, macro, news, and sentiment. This is a clean methodological choice (forces OHLCV-implementable factors) but means the "research memory" cannot represent large swaths of real sell-side research, and the resulting factors are necessarily price-volume only — a known alpha-crowding regime.
- **Reproducibility**: reasonably good. Calendar splits, costs, runtime knobs (pool sizes, generation counts, percentile thresholds, RankIC floors, the 0.75 admission gate, the 0.95 novelty correlation filter) and the model (gpt-oss-120b, locally deployed) are all stated, and there is full pseudocode (Appendix B). However the report corpus ingested by RMA and the exact prompts (only "selected excerpts" in Appendix M) are not fully released, so the memory contents cannot be reproduced exactly.
- **Red flags**: the IR magnitude relative to baselines; RankIC-vs-portfolio inversion left unexplained; a single dataset; absence of a quantitative component ablation; and an industry co-authorship that, while credible, raises the bar for independent verification. None of these invalidate the work, but they temper the strength of the empirical claims.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated closed-loop formulation that genuinely maps onto how human quants work (read → hypothesize → implement → validate → reflect), rather than another formula-search variant.
- The RMA A/B/C taxonomy with an explicit OHLCV eligibility gate is an elegant way to keep report-grounded knowledge useful without hard-coding report claims into factors, and the 48-archetype C-layer gives a concrete, auditable research memory.
- Ex-ante tri-alignment (idea / code / financial rationale) plus leakage unit tests are strong correctness safeguards that most alpha-mining systems lack.
- Honest, detailed reporting of selection gates, thresholds, costs, and runtime cost (~3 h/cycle on 2x H100, zero API cost via local gpt-oss-120b).
- Real diversity evidence (87.1% of cross-theme pairs below 0.3 correlation) and a genuinely interpretable, mechanism-tagged example factor with both train-val and test numbers.

## Limitations

- Single dataset (CSI300) and single horizon (10-day); no cross-market, cross-cap, or multi-horizon validation despite the magnitude of the headline results.
- No quantitative ablation isolating RMA memory, Macro routing modes, TriAlign-Judge, novelty injection, or Cross Brain FAA — only qualitative case traces.
- The portfolio IR of 1.5853 is large enough to warrant skepticism; the paper does not analyze regime sensitivity, capacity, turnover, or whether the gain survives a different test window or a less favorable 2022-2025 period.
- RankIC is actually lower than CogAlpha's (0.0489 vs 0.0590); the superior portfolio metrics are attributed to a "more useful factor library" but the mechanism behind the ~3.4x IR jump is not directly diagnosed.
- The OHLCV-only data contract, by construction, excludes fundamentals, order book, tick, macro, and sentiment — a large fraction of real research content and a known crowded factor space.
- The ingested report corpus and full prompt set are not fully released, so exact memory reproduction is not possible.
- Evaluation treats the backtest as a diagnostic, not a deployable strategy; real-world slippage, market impact at scale, and capacity are not modeled.

## Relevance

与"自演化 Agent 与自动化研究"方向高度相关：XALPHA 正是把 AI Scientist / AI Co-Scientist 风格的自主研究 Agent 范式迁移到金融 Alpha 挖掘，其多源记忆、Macro/Micro/Cross 三脑闭环与 GOOD/BAD 反馈归因，直接展示了 LLM 智能体如何通过持久记忆与外部知识吸收实现长程、可自我改进的研究循环。对关注"训练/推理可靠性"也有间接价值——其事前三对齐校验、leakage 单元测试与 AST gate 提供了一种在噪声、非平稳环境下保证生成代码可信的工程范式。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How much of the 1.5853 IR survives on CSI500/CSI1000, on US/equivalent markets, and on 1-day/5-day/20-day horizons — and does the RankIC-vs-portfolio inversion persist or flip?
- What is the quantitative contribution of each component (RMA memory, Macro routing mode, TriAlign-Judge, novelty injection, Cross Brain FAA) in a proper ablation, ideally with and without each piece under an identical compute budget?
- Is the ~3.4x IR gap over R&D-Agent(Q) and CogAlpha attributable to factor quality, lower library redundancy, a capacity-constrained top-50 universe, a favorable 2022-2025 regime, or target-definition alignment — and how does it change with realistic market impact and turnover caps?
- Because CogAlpha has higher RankIC but weaker portfolio metrics, what does XALPHA's factor *combination* (not just single-factor quality) contribute — and does the same hold for a factor-count-matched baseline?
- How sensitive are the results to the RMA A-layer gate: if fundamentals/order-book/sentiment fragments are re-admitted with proxy construction, does coverage and performance improve or does it just introduce noise?
- Does the memory loop exhibit compounding gains (each cycle strictly better than the last) or does it plateau/saturate, and how many cycles before archetype coverage saturates?
- Can the tri-alignment judge and leakage unit tests be evaluated as standalone correctness tools on top of other LLM alpha-mining systems (CogAlpha, RD-Agent(Q)) to test their portability?
