Cross-seed explainability using Procrustes-conditioned Joint End-to-end Top-K Sparse Autoencoders

arXiv: 2607.08499

Date: 2026-07-10

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08499

一句话总结： 本文提出 Procrustes 旋转预对齐 + 联合端到端 Top-K 稀疏自编码器（Joint SAE）的方法，从独立训练的 BERT 模型中提取跨随机种子"通用"特征，在 SST-2、Stanford Politeness 和 TweetEval Emotion 三个数据集上跨种子特征 Pearson r ≥ 0.70，超越独立 SAE + Hungarian 匹配等后对齐基线。
TL;DR: The authors introduce a Procrustes-conditioned Joint End-to-end Top-K Sparse Autoencoder that aligns two BERT seeds' activation spaces via an orthogonal Procrustes rotation before training a single shared SAE, producing more cross-seed "universal" features (Pearson r ≥ 0.70) than post-hoc matched SAE baselines on SST-2, Stanford Politeness, and TweetEval Emotion — e.g., on Politeness top-100 r rises from 0.482 (independent matched) to 0.722 (full pipeline), and on TweetEval from 0.511 to 0.846.

核心贡献

  • 提出 Procrustes 条件化的联合端到端 Top-K 稀疏自编码器架构：先用正交 Procrustes 旋转矩阵将两个独立种子 BERT 的隐空间对齐到同一几何坐标系，再训练单一共享 SAE 提取跨种子通用特征，而非像 Feature Aligned SAE 那样用正则惩罚强行对齐多个独立字典。
  • 将 Top-K 稀疏约束（k=32，结构化稀疏，避免 L1 收缩偏差）与端到端下游损失（KL 散度输出保真 + 下游层 MSE 重建）相结合，并集成 Gao 等人的 AuxK 死特征复活损失，统一为一个联合训练目标 L = LKL + λDS·LDS + Llocal + λcross·Lcross + λaux·Laux。
  • 引入跨种子稀疏激活损失 Lcross，直接最小化两个种子经 Procrustes 旋转后的稀疏码差异；并通过 λcross=0 的消融和置换零假设检验（30 次随机正交旋转）证明 Procrustes 旋转是通用特征增量的主要来源，而非损失项优化目标。
  • 在三个基准数据集、五个种子对（十个 BERT 模型）上系统验证，证明 Procrustes 对齐只有在"条件化联合训练"时才有效，作为独立 SAE 的事后变换并不可靠——这一结论有针对性地纠正了对 Procrustes 对齐用途的常见误解。
  • 提供了最小反事实消融研究（Feature 389 "Hedged Indirect Request"），通过模态替换、语气转换等最小语法编辑定性验证提取特征的可解释性与正交性。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces the Procrustes-conditioned Joint End-to-end Top-K Sparse Autoencoder: an orthogonal Procrustes rotation matrix W_align = VUᵀ (from SVD(H_AᵀH_B)) superimposes the activation spaces of two independently seeded BERT models into a shared geometric coordinate frame before a single shared SAE is trained, avoiding the Mutual Feature Regularization / multi-SAE approach of Marks et al. (2024).
  • Combines three recent SAE advances into one objective: Top-K structural sparsity (k=32, no L1 shrinkage bias per Gao et al. 2024), the end-to-end downstream objective of Braun et al. (2024) with KL output fidelity and downstream-layer MSE, and the AuxK dead-feature revival loss (modified — no ReLU gating on the auxiliary path).
  • Adds a cross-seed sparse-activation loss L_cross that directly penalizes the discrepancy between the two seeds' sparse codes c_A and c_B after rotation, and addresses the Goodhart's-law concern through a λcross=0 ablation plus a permutation null test.
  • Empirically shows that Procrustes alignment helps only when conditioning joint training: applying the rotation post-hoc to independently trained SAEs yields no consistent benefit and sometimes degrades correlations, a finding that refines the community's understanding of when Procrustes alignment is the right tool.
  • Validates the extracted features through a minimal counterfactual causal ablation (Feature 389 on the Politeness model — "sentence-initial modal verbs in interrogative position"), demonstrating that minimal grammatical edits (mood swap, repositioning, directness swap) produce predictable activation drops (up to −100%).

Methodology

The method operates in two stages. (1) Pre-alignment via Orthogonal Procrustes. Given dense activation matrices H_A, H_B ∈ ℝ^(n×d) (d=768, layer 10 of BERT) extracted from two independently initialized BERT models on the same n tokens, the optimal orthogonal alignment matrix is computed by minimizing the Frobenius norm W_align = argmin_{W∈O(d)} ‖H_B W − H_A‖_F, solved in closed form via SVD(H_AᵀH_B) = UΣVᵀ giving W_align = VUᵀ. Only 500 sequences are needed; Appendix B validates this budget by showing the normalized residual error (NRE) has plateaued at this sample size for all three datasets (rotation distance 4.6–13.4% of the theoretical maximum √(2d)≈55.4, cross-split std ≤ 0.013).

(2) Joint End-to-end Top-K SAE training. A single shared TopK SAE (dictionary size 6,144, k=32, ~9.4M parameters, based on ApolloResearch's e2e-sae implementation) is trained on both models simultaneously. Each batch is rotated by W_align before passing through the shared encoder. The unified loss is:

L = L_KL + λ_DS·L_DS + L_local + λ_cross·L_cross + λ_aux·L_aux

  • L_KL: KL divergence between clean and SAE-hijacked output logits (output fidelity, replaces L1 sparsity).
  • L_DS: MSE between SAE-reconstructed and true downstream-layer (L+1) hidden states (downstream consistency), λ_DS=1.5.
  • L_local: per-token local reconstruction MSE on both models (B's hidden state rotated by W_align).
  • L_cross: MSE between the two seeds' sparse codes c_A, c_B (direct cross-seed alignment), λ_cross=1.0 (selected via grid search over {0.1, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0}).
  • L_aux: AuxK dead-feature revival — dead features reconstruct the detached residual error (no ReLU gating, unlike Gao et al.), λ_aux=1/32.

Training uses bert-base-uncased (110M params), 10 seeds paired into 5 pairs (42&137, 99&7, 11&110, 25&225, 35&446), AdamW optimizer, lr=5e-5, cosine schedule, 5 epochs, batch size 16, mixed precision, single RTX 3090. Universality is defined at the token level (per-token activations, more conservative than mean-pooling): a feature is "universal" if it is active in both models (≥10 eval samples) and its per-token activation vectors correlate at Pearson r ≥ 0.70. Baselines include independent SAEs with raw index matching, independent SAEs with Hungarian-algorithm matching, and joint training with/without rotation and with/without cross-loss.

Results & Highlights

  • On Stanford Politeness, the full pipeline (Joint + Procrustes + cross-loss) achieves top-10 r = 0.764 ± 0.019 and top-100 r = 0.722 ± 0.026, versus the strongest fair baseline (independent SAEs + Hungarian matching) at top-10 r = 0.525 ± 0.046 and top-100 r = 0.482 ± 0.042 — a gain of +0.239 in top-10 r. Universal features rise from 236 ± 124 (16.1% of active features) to 889 ± 493 (57.9%).
  • On TweetEval Emotion, the full pipeline reaches top-10 r = 0.865 ± 0.015 and top-100 r = 0.846 ± 0.016, versus 0.646 / 0.511 for the independent matched baseline (+0.219 / +0.335 in top-10 / top-100 r). Universal feature count rises from 81 ± 19 to 398 ± 54.
  • On SST-2, the full pipeline achieves top-10 r = 0.702 ± 0.060, versus independent matched at 0.537 ± 0.091 (+0.165); the absolute number of universal features is lower across all conditions on this short-text dataset (78 ± 54 full vs. 23 ± 12 matched), reflecting SST-2's shorter sequences and higher dead-neuron rates (87.5%).
  • Procrustes rotation is the dominant driver of universality, not the cross-loss. On Politeness, going from Joint-no-rotation (top-10 r=0.459) to Joint-rotation-only (0.738) adds +0.279, while adding cross-loss on top of rotation (0.738→0.764) adds only +0.026. The same pattern holds on TweetEval (+0.122 from rotation vs. +0.006 from cross-loss).
  • The permutation null test confirms rotation is non-trivial. The true Procrustes rotation produced more universal features than all 30 random orthogonal rotations in every experiment (Monte Carlo empirical p ≈ 0.032), and a "mismatched" matrix computed over shuffled token correspondences failed to achieve comparable universality (e.g., on Politeness pair 42–137: True=212 vs. Mismatched=0 vs. Max Random=1).
  • Alignment quality linearly predicts universality. Regressing universal-feature count on (1 − NRE) across all rotation conditions gives R² = 0.704, slope = 0.839, p < 10⁻⁶⁷.
  • Downstream accuracy is essentially preserved. Mean SAE-hijacked classification accuracy change is within ±0.5 percentage points on all datasets and conditions (e.g., Politeness full pipeline −0.08 ± 0.22%; TweetEval +0.01 ± 0.36%), indicating the SAE injection does not distort model function.
  • The counterfactual ablation confirms interpretability. For Feature 389 ("Hedged Indirect Request"), a mood swap ("Should I send you an email tomorrow?" → "I should send you an email tomorrow") drives activation to −100% (zero), and a directness swap ("Would you mind if I had a question?" → "I have a question.") also yields −100%; a modal swap ("would" → "is") yields −11.8%.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The paper is primarily a synthesis rather than a fundamentally new primitive — Top-K SAEs (Gao et al. 2024), end-to-end SAEs (Braun et al. 2024), AuxK dead-feature revival, orthogonal Procrustes alignment (Csiszárik et al. 2021, Puri et al. 2025), and joint SAE training (Anthropic) are all pre-existing. The genuine contribution is the specific recipe of pre-aligning with Procrustes before joint training, and the empirical demonstration that rotation conditions (rather than penalizes) joint training. This is a useful but incremental result.
  • Rigor: The strongest design choice is the permutation null test (Table D4): comparing the true rotation against 30 random rotations, an identity baseline, and a shuffled-correspondence "mismatched" control, with a Monte Carlo p-value, directly addresses whether the universality gains are real or an artifact of the optimization target (the Goodhart's-law concern the authors explicitly raise). The λcross=0 ablation separating rotation from cross-loss is also well done. However, the headline table reports only 5 seed pairs (10 models), all from the same bert-base-uncased family, and the mismatched/random controls in Table D4 are run on only 2 of those pairs — a thin evidence base.
  • Reproducibility: Good on paper — fixed seeds (42), deterministic CUDNN, hyperparameters and appendix tables fully reported, public SAE codebase used. However, no code or trained-model release is mentioned, and the custom joint-training modifications to the ApolloResearch codebase would need to be reimplemented to verify. The 500-sequence Procrustes budget is validated with a nice NRE-plateau analysis (Appendix B).
  • Significance: Cross-seed universality is a real and recognized open problem in mechanistic interpretability (Anthropic, Marks et al.). The result that Procrustes helps only as a conditioning step for joint training — not as a standalone post-hoc transform — is a genuinely actionable finding for practitioners. But the restriction to BERT (110M) and a single late layer (10/12) limits how far the conclusion generalizes; whether this scales to decoder LLMs and larger models is unaddressed.
  • Red flags: (1) The universality metric (Pearson r ≥ 0.70 on per-token activations) and the "universal feature count" are defined by the authors with no external validation that 0.70 is a meaningful threshold. (2) The most striking numbers (top-10 r = 0.865 on TweetEval) come with the caveat that the cross-loss directly optimizes sparse-code similarity — the paper acknowledges this Goodhart risk but the mitigation rests on a single ablation and a small (2-pair) permutation test. (3) "Universal" here means universal across two seeds of the same architecture trained on the same data — a much weaker notion than the cross-architecture universality claimed by Thasarathan et al. (2025, ref [10]); the title's framing risks overstating scope. (4) The qualitative analysis is a single feature (389) on one dataset — purely anecdotal, as the authors concede.

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated synthesis of four recent SAE advances (Top-K, end-to-end, AuxK, joint training) with a coherent unifying loss function and clear ablation structure isolating each component's contribution.
  • The permutation null test with mismatched-correspondence and identity controls is a methodologically sound way to defend against the Goodhart's-law objection, and is unusual to see done this carefully in SAE papers.
  • The finding that Procrustes post-hoc alignment fails on independent SAEs but succeeds as a conditioning step for joint training is a non-obvious, practically useful negative/positive result pair that corrects a plausible misconception.
  • Honest self-assessment: the authors explicitly flag the Goodhart concern, the anecdotal nature of the qualitative analysis, and the layer/architecture limitations in the Limitations section.
  • The counterfactual ablation (Table 2) is a small but concrete demonstration that the extracted features admit causal verification through minimal edits, not just correlational top-token inspection.

Limitations

  • Only a single model family (bert-base-uncased, 110M) and a single SAE injection layer (10 of 12) are evaluated; generalization to decoder LLMs, larger models, or earlier layers is untested. The authors note layer 10 was chosen partly because it is the last layer compatible with the end-to-end loss.
  • Only 5 seed pairs (10 models) are studied; the permutation controls in Table D4 cover only 2 pairs. Standard deviations on universal-feature counts are large (e.g., 889 ± 493 on Politeness), indicating high pair-to-pair variance.
  • No direct comparison with the most relevant competing methods — Feature Aligned SAEs (Marks et al. 2024, ref [8]) and Orthogonal SAEs (Anonymous 2025, ref [9]) — is provided; the authors flag this as future work, but it leaves the central comparative claim under-supported.
  • The qualitative evaluation is a single feature on one dataset; no systematic human or automated evaluation of feature interpretability across the dictionary is performed.
  • The "universality" threshold (r ≥ 0.70) and "active" threshold (≥10 samples) are author-chosen with no sensitivity analysis; the dead-feature rates are very high (65–95% on per-token metrics), meaning the reported Univ.% is a percentage of a small surviving subset, not of the full 6,144-element dictionary.
  • The cross-loss L_cross optimizes the same quantity (sparse-code similarity) that is used to evaluate success; despite the ablation, residual concern about metric gaming remains.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the Procrustes-conditioned joint training result hold for decoder-only LLMs (e.g., Llama, Pythia) and at larger scales (1B+ parameters), where activation geometry may be less rotationally aligned across seeds? Appendix B's low effective rank of H_AᵀH_B for BERT may not transfer.
  • How does the method compare head-to-head with Feature Aligned SAEs (Marks et al. 2024) and Orthogonal SAEs (2025) under matched compute and dictionary budgets? The authors defer this comparison — it is the most important missing baseline.
  • Is the r ≥ 0.70 universality threshold robust, or do the headline universal-feature counts shift dramatically with a threshold of 0.5 or 0.9? A sensitivity analysis would strengthen the quantitative claims.
  • Can the permutation null test be strengthened beyond 2 seed pairs and 30 random rotations (current minimum p ≈ 0.032), to rule out the Goodhart concern more decisively on the full 5-pair set?
  • The counterfactual ablation works for one clearly-interpretable feature (modal verbs). How many of the 6,144 dictionary elements admit such clean causal verification, and how does universality correlate with interpretability across the whole dictionary?
  • Given the very high dead-feature rates (65–95% per-token), is a 6,144-element dictionary over-complete for these datasets, and would a smaller dictionary improve the active/universal fraction without hurting fidelity?
