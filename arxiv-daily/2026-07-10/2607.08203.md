> **一句话总结：** 本文对 2015–2026 年间 27 篇结肠镜息肉分割论文做系统审计，发现该领域 25/27 篇论文省略 HD95 边界指标、25/27 篇不报告 Recall、26/27 篇不做统计显著性检验，且至少存在 5 种互不兼容的训练/测试划分协议；通过对 5 个代表模型在固定划分(P1)、随机划分(P2)和六中心 OOD(P3) 三种协议下的统一重评估，证明区域重叠指标(Dice)掩盖了严重的边界与召回失败(ETIS 上 PraNet HD95 230px、Recall 仅 0.67)、模型排序随指标与随机种子而反转，并提出五点 PSRC 报告清单作为纠偏。

> **TL;DR:** A systematic audit of 27 polyp-segmentation papers (2015–2026) finds that 25/27 omit HD95, 25/27 omit Recall, 26/27 report no significance testing, and ≥5 incompatible train/test split protocols coexist on Kvasir-SEG/CVC-ClinicDB, making leaderboard Dice scores non-comparable; a controlled single-scorer re-evaluation of five models (PraNet, Polyp-PVT, SANet, PVT-CASCADE, G-CASCADE) across fixed-split (P1), random-split (P2), and six-centre OOD (P3) protocols shows that Dice conceals large boundary/recall failures (on ETIS, PraNet HD95 = 230.1 px vs Polyp-PVT 126.8 px with only a 0.15 Dice gap, and PraNet Recall = 0.673), that the "best" model reshuffles with the metric (PVT-CASCADE ranks last on Dice on CVC-300 but 2nd on the harder sets), and that near-tied rankings reverse across random seeds (PraNet↔Polyp-PVT flip within ±0.005–0.007 Dice) — motivating the proposed five-point Polyp Segmentation Reporting Checklist (PSRC).

## 核心贡献

- **对息肉分割领域的首次系统性评估审计：** 系统审计 27 篇 2015–2026 年的深度全监督息肉分割论文，沿四个维度（指标画像 D1、数据划分 D2、泛化范围 D3、统计严谨性 D4）记录其评估实践，揭示 25/27 篇省略 HD95、25/27 篇不报告 Recall、26/27 篇无显著性检验的结构性缺陷。
- **证明划分协议不可兼容使数值比较无效：** 指出至少 5 种并存协议（PraNet 固定划分被 19 篇使用；80/10/10 随机划分如 CFFormer/PolyMamba-Net；Kvasir-only 如 TransNetR；自定义带额外训练数据如 IBoxCLA；视频划分如 PNS-Net），仅划分选择造成的 Dice 差异即可超过 3 个百分点，约为四年声称 SOTA 累计增益(2.94%)的三分之一。
- **三种协议下的统一重评估与指标分歧量化：** 用单一评分器在 P1(固定划分)、P2(三个随机种子)、P3(PolypGen 六中心 OOD) 下重评 5 个模型，证明 Dice 掩盖的边界/召回失败、模型排序随数据集与指标而变、并列排名在随机种子间反转；并报告被遗漏的 NSD@3px、HD95、Recall 与 lesion-level 检测指标。
- **二级综述表转录错误取证：** 通过回溯原始论文 PDF 验证次级综述表数值，发现 G-CASCADE 在 CVC-ClinicDB 上的转录误差达 0.011（次级 0.936 vs 原始 0.9468），超过多篇 SOTA 论文声称的改进幅度。
- **提出 PSRC 五点报告清单：** 作为面向该子领域的轻量级、领域适配纠偏标准——C1 报告 HD95、C2 报告 Recall、C3 精确说明划分协议、C4 包含至少一个外部/OOD 测试集、C5 报告统计显著性（Dice 差距小于两位小数不得在 p<0.05 前标 SOTA）。

## Key Contributions

- **First systematic evaluation audit of the polyp-segmentation sub-field:** Audits 27 fully-supervised deep polyp-segmentation papers (2015–2026) across four dimensions (Metric Profile, Data Partitioning, Generalization Scope, Statistical Rigor), exposing that 25/27 omit HD95, 25/27 omit Recall/Sensitivity, and 26/27 perform no significance testing — the sole exception being PolyMamba-Net (2026) with Wilcoxon signed-rank p-values.
- **Demonstrates split-protocol incompatibility invalidates numeric comparison:** Identifies ≥5 simultaneously active protocols (PraNet fixed split used by 19 papers; 80/10/10 random splits in CFFormer/PolyMamba-Net; Kvasir-only in TransNetR; custom splits with extra training data in IBoxCLA; video splits in PNS-Net), and shows Dice differences attributable to split choice alone can exceed 3 percentage points — roughly one third of the entire four-year claimed SOTA progression (PraNet 0.898 → G-CASCADE 0.9274 = 2.94%).
- **Controlled single-scorer re-evaluation across three protocols:** Re-evaluates five representative models (PraNet, Polyp-PVT, SANet, PVT-CASCADE, G-CASCADE) under P1 (PraNet fixed split), P2 (three random 80/20 seeds), and P3 (PolypGen six-centre OOD) with one uniform evaluator, reporting the omitted boundary (NSD@3px, HD95), Recall, and lesion-level detection metrics with per-image significance, and showing the "best" model changes with the metric and near-tied rankings reverse across seeds.
- **Primary-source verification of transcription errors:** Retrieves Dice scores directly from original paper PDFs and finds secondary survey tables propagate errors — most notably a 0.011 transcription error for G-CASCADE on CVC-ClinicDB (0.936 reported vs 0.9468 in primary source) that exceeds the claimed improvement margin of several published SOTA comparisons.
- **Proposes the five-point Polyp Segmentation Reporting Checklist (PSRC):** A lightweight, domain-adapted corrective (C1 report HD95; C2 report Recall/Sensitivity; C3 specify and justify split protocol; C4 include ≥1 external/OOD test set; C5 report statistical significance) accompanied by an open-source evaluation toolkit validated against the official MetricsReloaded package.

## Methodology

The work combines a literature meta-audit with a controlled empirical re-evaluation. The audit meta-dataset was compiled by seeding from the Mei et al. survey, the Awesome-Polyp-Segmentation repository, and PapersWithCode leaderboards for Kvasir-SEG/CVC-ClinicDB, then expanding via manual sweeps of recent CVPR/MICCAI/WACV/MIDL proceedings, yielding 27 eligible fully-supervised still-image polyp-segmentation papers (2015–2026). For each, the authors extract structural metadata across four dimensions: Metric Profile (region-only vs boundary/clinical), Data Partitioning (PraNet-fixed P, random R, Kvasir-only K, custom C, video V), Generalization Scope (presence of OOD/multi-centre validation), and Statistical Rigor (significance testing).

For the empirical study, five models — PraNet, Polyp-PVT, SANet (released weights), PVT-CASCADE and G-CASCADE (self-trained on the 1450 PraNet split, since no polyp checkpoints are released) — are scored by one uniform evaluator that computes mean Dice/IoU plus the omitted NSD@3px (validated to machine precision against the official MetricsReloaded package), HD95 (medpy convention), pixel Recall, per-image scores for significance testing, and a connected-component lesion-level detection metric. Evaluation runs under three protocols: P1 (PraNet fixed split, tested on held-out CVC-ColonDB, ETIS, CVC-300), P2 (three random 80/20 splits, fixed training budget, no test-set checkpoint selection), and P3 (out-of-distribution on PolypGen's six unseen centres). Implementation faithfulness is confirmed by in-distribution reproduction (PVT-CASCADE within 0.011, G-CASCADE within 0.018–0.035 of published Dice), metric validation against medpy and MetricsReloaded (Dice/IoU/Recall/NSD exact to ≤10⁻¹¹), and a perceptual-hash→SSIM leakage audit (max cross-set SSIM 0.66). Significance is assessed with FDR-corrected Wilcoxon signed-rank tests, bootstrap confidence intervals, and rank-biserial effect sizes. The study adopts the Metrics Reloaded problem-fingerprint recommendation rather than the historical six-metric PraNet template.

## Results & Highlights

- **Boundary failures hidden by Dice (P1, ETIS, HD95 px):** On ETIS, PraNet's HD95 of 230.1 px is nearly double Polyp-PVT's 126.8 px — a boundary gap far larger than the 0.15 Dice difference between them suggests — and PraNet's pixel Recall of 0.673 reveals that roughly a third of polyp tissue is missed, a failure invisible in the reported Dice alone.
- **Ranking reshuffles across datasets and metrics (P1, Table 3):** SANet ranks 2nd on CVC-300 (Dice 0.899) but 3rd on CVC-ColonDB (0.758) and ETIS (0.763), while PVT-CASCADE ranks last on Dice on CVC-300 (0.876) yet 2nd on the harder CVC-ColonDB (0.801) and ETIS (0.771) sets; on the small CVC-300 set (n=60) pairwise differences between top models are not statistically significant under FDR-corrected Wilcoxon tests (p > 0.1), so those apparent SOTA gaps are within noise.
- **Rankings are not robust to the data split (P2, Table 4):** Under three random 80/20 splits, HSNet (0.930 ± 0.005 Dice), Polyp-PVT (0.923 ± 0.005) and PraNet (0.919 ± 0.007) lie within ≈1 std of one another, and the PraNet vs Polyp-PVT ranking reverses between seeds — empirically confirming that a single fixed-split leaderboard does not establish a robust ordering.
- **Lesion-level detection produces a clinically-grounded, different ranking (Table 5):** PVT-CASCADE attains the best lesion-F1 under every criterion (e.g., 0.868 any-overlap F1), G-CASCADE the best precision (0.891, fewest false-alarm lesions) but ranks 4th on Dice (0.869 on CVC-300), Polyp-PVT the highest sensitivity, and PraNet — competitive on Dice — misses up to a third of lesions and raises the most false alarms; the ordering is robust across IoU thresholds 0.1–0.75.
- **OOD testing degrades all models uniformly (P3, PolypGen, n=1537, Table 6):** Every model drops to Dice 0.713–0.760 with HD95 144.9–162.3 px (vs ~0.9 Dice in-distribution), with per-centre Dice ranging from 0.45 (centre 4) to 0.89 (centre 3); the coarse ranking is largely preserved (Polyp-PVT best at 0.760, PraNet worst at 0.713), so OOD contributes a uniform large absolute degradation invisible to in-distribution leaderboards.
- **Transcription errors exceed claimed SOTA margins (Table 2):** G-CASCADE's CVC-ClinicDB mDice is reported as 0.936 in secondary survey tables but as 0.9468 in the primary supplementary — a 0.011 error that exceeds the claimed margin of improvement of several published SOTA comparisons — and its polyp results being confined to supplementary material makes independent verification non-trivial.
- **Audit headline counts:** 25/27 papers omit HD95 and any surface-distance variant (including boundary-focused architectures like SANet and CaraNet); 25/27 omit Recall; 26/27 report no significance testing; and the four papers published after the January-2024 Metrics Reloaded framework (G-CASCADE, CFFormer, Polyp-DiFoM, HSSAM-Net) perpetuate these same omissions, with Polyp-DiFoM actually regressing to Dice/IoU only.
- **HD95 implementation disagreement as a reproducibility finding:** HD95 differs by up to ~16 px on identical masks between the medpy convention and the Metrics-Reloaded max-of-directed convention, meaning absolute HD95 values are comparable only within a fixed implementation.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are Aisha Urooj (Lunit, South Korea — a leading medical-AI company for cancer detection), Zain Ul Abdien (RPTU Kaiserslautern-Landau, Germany), and Neelu Madan (Aalborg University & Pioneer Center, Denmark). The combination of industry (Lunit) and academic affiliations with established medical-imaging groups lends the audit institutional credibility. The work is explicitly grounded in, and its metric implementations validated against, the Metrics Reloaded framework (Maier-Hein et al., Nature Methods 2024) and the broader metric-pitfall literature (Reinke et al., Nature Methods 2024), and cites the controlled re-evaluation precedent of "A Metric Learning Reality Check" (Musgrave et al., ECCV 2020). The methodology is unusually transparent about its own threats to validity: single-seed self-training, library-dependent HD95 conventions, and un-audited PolypGen-vs-training leakage are all disclosed.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Applying meta-evaluation/audit logic (Metrics Reloaded, Musgrave et al.) to the polyp-segmentation sub-field is a useful, domain-specific contribution rather than a fundamentally new methodology. The strongest original elements are the controlled multi-model split-sensitivity study (extending Fitzgerald et al.'s single-model observation to five models) and the primary-source transcription-error forensics (e.g., the G-CASCADE 0.011 error), which are concrete and not generic.
- **Rigor:** Audit claims are checkable and the empirical re-evaluation is methodologically careful — single uniform scorer, metric implementations validated against official packages, FDR-corrected Wilcoxon tests, bootstrap CIs, and an SSIM leakage audit. However, the headline split-instability finding rests on only three random seeds and three models (HSNet, Polyp-PVT, PraNet) in P2, a small sample for variance estimation; and two of the five P1 models (PVT-CASCADE, G-CASCADE) are self-trained single-seed reproductions with 0.018–0.035 Dice gaps to published numbers, so their absolute unseen-set scores carry residual training-protocol confounding that the authors acknowledge but cannot fully eliminate.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong: named models, named datasets (Kvasir-SEG, CVC-ClinicDB, CVC-ColonDB, ETIS, CVC-300, PolypGen), named metric library conventions (medpy vs Metrics-Reloaded max-of-directed), validated in-distribution reproduction, and an open-source evaluation toolkit. The residual weak point is the self-trained G-CASCADE/PVT-CASCADE checkpoints, which are not released weights.
- **Significance:** The PSRC checklist and the open toolkit are immediately actionable and likely to influence the sub-field's reporting norms, analogous to how Metrics Reloaded influenced general medical image analysis. The finding that split choice can account for ~one third of the four-year claimed Kvasir-SEG SOTA progression (2.94%) is a strong, sobering result. Significance is somewhat bounded by the fact that many of the individual deficiencies (Dice alone, no significance tests) are already known problems in the broader community.
- **Red flags:** The claim that "26 of 27 papers make performance claims without any statistical significance test" is a structural observation, not evidence that published rankings are wrong — the paper correctly frames near-tied gaps as "within noise" rather than "proven equivalent," but the framing could be misread as stronger than the evidence. The lesion-level metric applies no minimum-size filter (a single-pixel false-positive speck counts as a false-alarm lesion), which inflates false-alarm counts for PraNet/G-CASCADE; the authors disclose this and note the ranking is stable, but it weakens the precision-based critique. The four-year SOTA-progression argument (PraNet 0.898 → G-CASCADE 0.9274) compares against numbers the authors show contain transcription errors, so the exact "2.94%" figure should be read against the verified primary-source values.

## Strengths

- Clean, four-dimensional audit framework applied rigorously to 27 papers, with a fully reproducible Table 1 of per-paper evaluation practices.
- Controlled single-scorer re-evaluation across three protocols (fixed, random, OOD) that isolates split variance, metric choice, and distribution shift as distinct failure modes — exactly the experiment the field needed.
- Quantifies the metric-implementation disagreement (HD95 differs by up to ~16 px between medpy and Metrics-Reloaded conventions) and the survey-table transcription errors (G-CASCADE CVC-ClinicDB 0.011), turning vague "reproducibility concerns" into concrete, citable numbers.
- PSRC checklist is lightweight, domain-adapted, and directly actionable by authors and reviewers.
- Honest, detailed Threats-to-Validity section (single-seed self-training, library-dependent HD95, un-audited PolypGen leakage, low statistical power on CVC-300 n=60).

## Limitations

- The split-sensitivity conclusion (P2) rests on only three random seeds and three models, a limited basis for variance estimation; the paper frames results as "gaps within noise" rather than proven equivalence.
- Two of the five re-evaluated models (PVT-CASCADE, G-CASCADE) have no released polyp weights and are self-trained single-seed, reproducing published Dice only within 0.018–0.035 — leaving residual training-protocol confounding on their absolute unseen-set scores.
- The audit is restricted to still-image fully-supervised polyp segmentation; video models (SUN-SEG, CVC-VideoClinicDB), weakly/semi/unsupervised methods, foundation-model adaptations (SAM), and non-standard datasets (BKAI-IGH, PICCOLO) are explicitly excluded, so coverage of "the field" is narrower than the framing implies.
- The lesion-level detection metric uses no minimum-size filter, so a single-pixel false-positive can count as a false-alarm lesion; this inflates precision-based criticism of PraNet/G-CASCADE (though the ranking is robust to the matching criterion).
- Absolute HD95 values are only comparable within a fixed library convention (medpy vs Metrics-Reloaded differ by up to ~16 px), which somewhat undercuts cross-paper HD95 benchmarking even after PSRC adoption.
- Extension to parallel fields (e.g., skin-lesion segmentation) is noted as future work, not addressed.

## Relevance

对关注医学图像分割评估方法学、基准可复现性与临床指标落地的读者高度相关：本文以息肉分割为切片，量化揭示了"指标选择 + 划分协议 + 显著性缺失"如何共同制造虚假的 SOTA 进展，并提出可直接采用的 PSRC 报告清单，对所有依赖公开 leaderboard 的医学影像子领域（分割、检测、量化）的评估实践都具有方法学警示与迁移价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the split-sensitivity and metric-reordering findings generalize to parallel medical-segmentation sub-fields (skin lesion, retinal vessel, organ), where similar PraNet-style template inheritance may exist?
- Would adopting a single dataset-official split (or a standardized held-out test release) eliminate the PraNet-fixed vs random split incompatibility, and could a community-maintained benchmark curator enforce it?
- Does the HD95 library-convention disagreement (~16 px between medpy and Metrics-Reloaded) change which model is "best on boundary," and should the community standardize on one convention alongside PSRC C1?
- The lesion-level metric uses no minimum-size filter — how do the precision rankings change once a ≥0.1% area component filter is applied in deployment, and does G-CASCADE's precision advantage persist?
- Can the PSRC checklist be validated by having reviewers apply it prospectively: does enforcing C1–C5 actually reduce the rate of non-reproducible SOTA claims in the next cohort of polyp-segmentation papers?
- Do the post-Metrics-Reloaded papers' continued omission of HD95/Recall reflect author unawareness, or reviewer/publisher incentives tied to leaderboard columns — and what intervention (journal policy, checklist gate) would break the path dependency the paper identifies?
