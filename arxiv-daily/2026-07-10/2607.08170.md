> **一句话总结：** 本文首次系统研究了 boomerang distillation 中"学生层 patching 顺序"对零样本模型尺寸插值的影响，将最优 patching 形式化为一个 KL 加权有向无环图上的最短路径问题（命题1），并提出贪心算法 KLPatch 将搜索复杂度从 O(2^N) 降到 O(N^2)；在 DistilBERT、DistilGPT2、Qwen3-4B/8B、Pythia-6.9B 上 KLPatch 均接近全局最优，且相比穷举搜索带来 548-1722 倍的加速。

> **TL;DR:** The paper gives the first systematic study of *which order* to patch student layers in boomerang distillation for zero-shot model-size interpolation, recasting optimal patching as a shortest-path problem on a KL-weighted Boolean lattice (Proposition 1) and proposing a greedy O(N^2) algorithm, KLPatch. On DistilBERT/DistilGPT2 (full 720-order sweep) and Qwen3-4B/Qwen3-8B/Pythia-6.9B (200 sampled orders), KLPatch reaches near-optimal AUIC/AUPIC — e.g., matching the 100th-percentile AUIC on Qwen3-4B (0.533 vs. 0.441 for first-to-last) while delivering 548-1722x speedup over exhaustive search.

## 核心贡献

- **形式化 patching 为组合优化问题**：将"对每个插值尺寸选哪个学生层子集去 patch"分别建模为 (1) 子集优化（问题1，2^N−2 个候选）和 (2) 排列优化（问题2，N! 个候选），并指出在贪心构造下两种形式算法上坍缩为同一种嵌套链/排列对象。
- **最短路径刻画（命题1）**：证明在层间插值尺寸等距的假设下，KL 加权的 Boolean 格 G 上的最短路径恰好等价于最小化 teacher-relative log-AUPIC 的最优排列，将 N! 维搜索约简为 DAG 最短路径；附录给出 reference-distribution（命题2）和 log-vs-raw（命题3）两类误差界。
- **首批完整的 patching 现象学刻画**：在 DistilBERT/DistilGPT2 上穷举全部 720 种顺序和 64 个部分 patched 模型，发现 last-to-first 在 DistilBERT 上达到全局最优 AUPIC=0.397，在 DistilGPT2 上进入前 5%；并揭示"好的 patching 顺序在排列空间中聚集在最优解附近"（用 Spearman footrule 距离刻画）。
- **KLPatch 算法**：贪心地每步选取使当前模型与 teacher 的 KL 散度最小的学生层进行 patch（即贪心选取 G 上最便宜的出边），把搜索复杂度从 O(2^N) 降到 O(N^2)，并通过 64 个 Pile 校准样本即可工作。
- **强实证结果与可推广性研究**：在 Qwen3-4B/8B、Pythia-6.9B、Llama-3.2-3B 上验证；KLPatch 多次超越全部 200 个随机顺序，并提供 548-1722 倍加速，给出工程上实用的插值构建指南。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalization as combinatorial optimization.** Defines student patching both as a subset-selection problem (Problem 1, 2^N−2 candidates) and as a permutation problem (Problem 2, N! candidates), and notes they collapse under greedy sequential construction into a single nested-chain / permutation object.
- **Shortest-path characterization (Proposition 1).** Proves that, under equidistant interpolation-size spacing, shortest paths in a KL-weighted Boolean lattice G are exactly the optimal permutations minimizing teacher-relative log-AUPIC. Propositions 2-3 bound the gap introduced by using data-reference vs. teacher-reference and raw vs. log scale, justifying the practical use of data-AUPIC.
- **First exhaustive phenomenology of patching.** Sweeps all 720 orderings and all 64 partially-patched models on DistilBERT/DistilGPT2, showing last-to-front is globally optimal on DistilBERT (AUPIC=0.397) and top-5% on DistilGPT2, and that good orderings cluster near the optimum in Spearman-footrule permutation space — a smooth landscape that makes greedy local search viable.
- **KLPatch algorithm.** A greedy O(N^2) procedure that, at each step, patches the student layer minimizing KL divergence to the teacher on a 64-sample Pile calibration set — i.e., greedily taking the cheapest outgoing edge of G. This reduces search complexity from O(2^N) to O(N^2) and approximately solves the shortest-path problem.
- **Strong empirical validation across model families.** On Qwen3-4B/8B and Pythia-6.9B, KLPatch recovers best- or near-best AUIC/AUPIC trajectories (beating all 200 random orders on Qwen3-4B-Base and Pythia-6.9b), and delivers 548-1722x speedup over exhaustive search. An honest failure case (Llama-3.2-3B) and a fix (patch layer 1 first) are documented.

## Methodology

The paper builds on **boomerang distillation** (Kangaslahti et al., 2026), where a smaller student is distilled from a teacher and then expanded by replacing ("patching") student blocks with their aligned teacher blocks. The procedure has three steps: (1) student initialization by partitioning the teacher's M layers into N contiguous blocks; (2) knowledge distillation with a combination of cross-entropy, KL, and cosine alignment losses; (3) student patching to produce intermediate-size models M_A indexed by subsets A ⊆ {1,…,N} of patched blocks.

The central object of study is **the order in which blocks are patched**. The authors formalize this two ways:

- **Problem 1 (Optimal Interpolation):** For each cardinality k, choose the best k-subset A_k = argmax_{|A|=k} f_{S,T}(A); this requires evaluating all 2^N−2 proper subsets, infeasible in practice.
- **Problem 2 (Optimal Permutation):** Choose the permutation π that maximizes Σ_k f_{S,T}(A_{π_k}). The search space is larger (N!) but admits a clean shortest-path interpretation.

Two evaluation metrics summarize an entire interpolation trajectory in one number: **AUIC** (area under the interpolation curve for higher-is-better metrics like accuracy) and **AUPIC** (area under the perplexity interpolation curve, lower-is-better), along with log and teacher-relative variants.

The key theoretical reduction (**Definition 1 + Proposition 1**): vertices of the interpolation graph G are partially-patched models M_A, edges connect M_A → M_{A∪{i}}, and edge weights are KL(p_T ‖ p_{M_{A∪{i}}}) on a calibration set. Patching orders correspond to length-N paths from S to T. Under equidistant interpolation sizes, shortest paths in G are exactly the optimal permutations for teacher-relative log-AUPIC. KL is preferred over perplexity/accuracy as edge weights because it is task-agnostic, dense over the full distribution, requires no sampling from T, and correlates strongly with data-AUPIC.

**KLPatch (Algorithm 1)** greedily approximates the shortest path: at iteration k, it patches the student layer whose patched version yields the lowest KL to the teacher on a 64-sample Pile calibration set, repeating until all N layers are patched. Total cost is Σ_k (N−k) = O(N^2) KL evaluations instead of O(2^N).

Experiments span: (a) exhaustive sweep on DistilBERT/DistilGPT2 (N=6, 720 orders, Wikitext pseudo-perplexity/perplexity); (b) 200 random orderings on Qwen3-4B/8B and Pythia-6.9B over a 10-task classification suite (ARC-easy, ARC-challenge, BoolQ, HellaSwag, OpenBookQA, PIQA, WinoGrande, RACE, MMLU, RTE) plus generation tasks (GSM8K, IFEval) and Wikitext; (c) KLPatch vs. first-to-last/last-to-first/random baselines; and (d) an ablation suite on Llama-3.2-3B.

## Results & Highlights

- **Patching order matters a lot, and last-to-front is a surprisingly strong default.** On DistilBERT, last-to-front patching achieves the global minimum AUPIC of **0.397** (100th percentile), tying the global shortest KL path and KLPatch, whereas first-to-last patching scores **0.968** (only 38.75th percentile). On DistilGPT2 the minimum-AUPIC permutation is **0.713**, and last-to-front (**0.815**, 96.39th percentile) is near-optimal while first-to-last sits at **0.824** (95.56th percentile).
- **KLPatch matches the global shortest KL path on small models.** On DistilBERT, KLPatch recovers AUPIC = **0.397** (100th percentile, identical to the global shortest path and to last-to-front). On DistilGPT2, KLPatch yields **0.831** (95.28th percentile), close to the global shortest KL path's **0.824**.
- **On LLMs, KLPatch produces best- or near-best trajectories, often beating all 200 random orders.** On Qwen3-4B-Base, KLPatch reaches AUIC **0.533** (100th percentile) vs. last-to-first **0.462** (89.16%) and first-to-last **0.441** (69.46%) — a delta of ~0.09 AUIC over the strongest sequential baseline. On Qwen3-8B-Base, KLPatch scores AUIC **0.571** (99.51st percentile) vs. last-to-first **0.495** (85.71%). On Pythia-6.9b, KLPatch ties the minimum-AUPIC permutation at **0.567** (100th percentile).
- **Perplexity (AUPIC) is an imperfect proxy for downstream AUIC in LLMs.** Although last-to-front achieves the lowest Wikitext AUPIC on Qwen, it leaves a substantial downstream-AUIC gap relative to the best-sampled ordering; KLPatch closes this gap on both classification and generation (GSM8K, IFEval) tasks.
- **Massive compute savings.** Estimated exhaustive-search runtime is 64,302 min (Qwen3-4B), 65,327 min (Qwen3-8B), and 14,134 min (Pythia), vs. KLPatch's 37.3, 38.1, and 25.8 minutes respectively — speedups of **1722x, 1715x, and 549x**.
- **Best-order gap is large vs. random.** Mean AUIC over 200 random orders is 0.409 (Qwen3-4B) / 0.447 (Qwen3-8B) / 0.209 (Pythia), while the best-sampled interpolation reaches 0.571 / 0.634 / 0.459 — confirming meaningful headroom for principled patching-order selection.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core technical move — recasting layer patching as a KL-weighted shortest path on a Boolean lattice and proving optimality under an equidistant-size assumption (Proposition 1) — is genuinely novel and cleanly executed. However, the conceptual adjacency to existing layer-pruning work is large: the authors themselves note KLPatch's greedy selection is "closely related" to Zhang et al. (2024)'s FinerCut iterative KL-based layer *dropping*; the delta is "we patch instead of drop." Readers familiar with that line may find the algorithmic mechanism less surprising than the framing suggests.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Proposition 1 is proven, with auxiliary Propositions 2-3 bounding the two assumptions (teacher-reference vs. data-reference; log vs. raw scale) that separate theory from the reported metrics. The exhaustive DistilBERT/DistilGPT2 sweep (720 orders) provides genuine ground truth, not sampling. The Spearman-footrule analysis (Figure 3) gives a clean structural argument for *why* greedy works (good orderings cluster near the optimum).
- **Reproducibility:** Good in principle — uses only public models (DistilBERT, DistilGPT2, Qwen3, Pythia, Llama-3.2) and the public boomerang-distillation codebase, with a 64-sample Pile calibration set fully specified. However, no explicit code release URL is given in the body; downstream AUIC/AUPIC values depend on the exact 200 random seeds, which are not enumerated.
- **Significance:** The practical significance is real but bounded. KLPatch gives ~0.07-0.09 AUIC gains over the strongest sequential baseline and 549-1722x speedup over exhaustive search — useful for practitioners building fine-grained model families, but a narrow audience. The headline finding that "patching order matters" is, in retrospect, almost definitional once one accepts that different subsets of teacher layers have very different perplexity contributions.
- **Red flags / weak points:**
  - **KLPatch fails out-of-the-box on Llama-3.2-3B** (significantly underperforms sequential baselines) and requires a hand-crafted fix ("patch layer 1 first") whose justification is a hypothesis about student initialization (first-two vs. last-two teacher layers kept). This is a meaningful generalization concern, not a footnote.
  - **The proposition's optimality guarantee is for teacher-relative *log*-AUPIC**, which is *not* what the experiments report (data-relative, raw scale). The authors bridge this with Propositions 2-3 and an empirical correlation claim (Appendix G.1), but the gap is real and the bridging arguments rely on assumptions (bounded η; equidistant sizes) whose tightness is not quantified on the actual models.
  - **KLPatch's headline AUIC gains on Qwen3-8B (0.571 vs. 0.585 max-AUIC interpolation) leave a visible gap** to the true subset-based optimum (Problem 1), and the paper does not report variance/seed sensitivity for the main AUIC numbers (a calibration-set-size ablation in Appendix K.3 reports std over 5 seeds for KLPatch only).
  - **The "min-AUPIC interpolation" envelope** (the dotted line over all 64 partially-patched models, including non-nested ones unreachable by any single permutation) shows substantial headroom above even the best permutation on DistilGPT2 — i.e., the permutation constraint itself leaves performance on the table, which somewhat undercuts the "near-optimal" framing.
  - Evaluation is restricted to boomerang-distilled students from one prior paper; whether KLPatch generalizes to other distillation recipes is untested.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated formalization that converts an N! combinatorial explosion into a tractable shortest-path problem with a proof of optimality under stated assumptions.
- Exhaustive ground-truth sweep on DistilBERT/DistilGPT2 (not just sampling) lets the authors validate that KLPatch truly recovers the global shortest KL path.
- Honest, non-rubberstamp reporting: the Llama-3.2-3B failure case and its hand-fix are documented in the main paper and a dedicated appendix, and the gap between permutation-optimum and subset-optimum is shown explicitly.
- Strong compute story: 548-1722x speedup over exhaustive search with minimal calibration data (64 Pile samples).
- Practically actionable: gives a clear default recipe (try KLPatch; fall back to last-to-front if it fails) for an emerging real-world task (constructing fine-grained LLM size families without retraining).

## Limitations

- KLPatch does **not** work out-of-the-box on Llama-3.2-3B; it requires a heuristic modification (patch layer 1 first), and the authors only hypothesize that this stems from the student's initialization convention.
- All experiments inherit the distilled students from Kangaslahti et al. (2026); any quirk of that specific distillation recipe could influence the reported patching-order effects, and generalization to other distillation methods is untested.
- The theoretical optimality guarantee (Proposition 1) is for teacher-relative *log*-AUPIC, while experiments report data-relative raw AUPIC/AUIC; the bridging is empirical (correlation in Appendix G.1) rather than guaranteed.
- The magnitude of KLPatch's gains varies substantially across model families; the conditions under which patching-order optimization helps most are left as future work.
- Only three LLM families (Qwen3, Pythia, Llama) are studied at scale, and within each only one teacher/student pair; the 200-order sample on LLMs cannot reach the true global optimum (N! > 10^12).
- Open directions the authors flag: sub-greedy alternatives exploiting Proposition 1 more globally, amortized KL computation to avoid re-evaluating teacher-conditioned KL at every step, and RL-based adaptive trajectory selection.

## Relevance

本文与"在受限算力下高效构建不同尺寸 LLM"的研究兴趣高度相关：它直接处理零样本模型尺寸插值（无需重训即可得到细粒度尺寸的模型族），同时给出了一种基于最短路径与 KL 散度的通用 patching 顺序选择方法，对知识蒸馏、模型压缩与 LLM 推理效率方向都有方法论与工程借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Why does last-to-front patching systematically outperform first-to-front on DistilBERT (and on Wikitext perplexity for the LLMs)? Is this connected to where BERT/GPT-style models concentrate their representational capacity (early vs. late layers)?
- How tight are Propositions 2 and 3 on the actual Qwen/Pythia models — i.e., what is the empirically measured η between data-PPL and teacher-PPL, and does it stay small enough to justify the bridge from teacher-relative log-AUPIC theory to data-AUPIC practice?
- Can the subset-based optimum (Problem 1, the dotted "interpolation envelope" in Figure 2) be approached by relaxing the single-permutation constraint — e.g., allowing different orderings per cardinality k — and would that close the visible DistilGPT2 gap?
- Does KLPatch generalize beyond boomerang-distilled students — for example, to students produced by structured pruning (Sheared LLaMA, Minitron) or to non-distilled teacher/student pairs initialized differently?
- Could an amortized or learned variant of KLPatch (e.g., predicting the next layer to patch from a small MLP on hidden-state statistics) avoid recomputing teacher-conditioned KL at every step, and would RL-style trajectory selection beat the greedy commitment?
- How do patching-order effects interact with post-training alignment (instruction tuning, RLHF) applied to the interpolated models — does the optimal order change once downstream instruction-following, not perplexity, is the target?
