> **一句话总结：** 在 Eternis-Forecaster 8B（基于 Qwen3-8B 的 RLVR 预测模型）上训练的内部激活探针（pooling probe）把校准误差从 0.093 降到 0.044，并能作为"测谎仪"捕捉到 23% 思维链沉默却改变预测的隐式影响；强制作答实验进一步表明预测在推理开始前就已基本确定，据此分流问题可节省 30–47% 的生成 token 而无损精度。

> **TL;DR:** Lightweight pooling probes on Eternis-Forecaster 8B's internal activations cut expected calibration error from 0.093 (verbalized) to 0.044 on OpenForesight-test (a gain that transfers to GLM-4.7-Flash, 0.054 vs 0.287, and GLM-4.5-Air), and the same probes act as CoT "lie detectors" tracking behavioral shifts at Spearman ρ=0.565 versus 0.215 for the reasoning text; forced answering shows forecasts are largely pre-committed, enabling a triage gate that saves 30–47% of tokens with no accuracy loss.

## 核心贡献

- **内部探针优于言语化置信度**：在 EF-8B 的残差流上训练轻量级 pooling 探针（mean / attention / covariance），把 OpenForesight-test 上的 ECE 从 0.093 降到 0.044，且该结论在仅训练探针权重的 GLM-4.7-Flash（0.054 vs 0.287）和 GLM-4.5-Air（0.102 vs 0.255）上同样成立。
- **思维链仅部分忠实**：通过证据消融（去除真实文章）和诱导注入（插入伪造文章）两类扰动评估 CoT 忠实度；在 460 个高影响消融对中，23% 的预测被改变但思维链毫无变化（隐式影响），而注入场景下模型反而相当透明（仅 2.5% 隐式采纳）。
- **探针即"测谎仪"**：同一组探针在 1,792 个扰动案例上以 Spearman ρ=0.565 追踪行为变化（思维链文本仅 0.215），并在 83.6% 的案例中正确预测变化方向，即便 CoT 沉默也能在隐式案例上检出 |∆probe|≈0.065 的内部位移。
- **预测在推理前已基本确定**：强制作答（空 think 预填充）显示言语化置信与是否推理几乎无关（test 上 ρ=0.90），67% 的问题在推理前就已锁定答案；CoT 主要起"锐化"作用，精度增益仅 +1.9 pp。
- **据此实现的推理分流器**：用强制作答候选分布的熵 H 把问题分成 commit / reason / retrieve 三档，熵门控在所有 split 上节省 30–47% 的生成 token，精度损失在 ~1 pp 内（CI 含零）。
- **跨域泛化到数学推理**：在 OOD 的 AIME24/25 + AMC23/24 上，冻结探针 AUROC 达 0.894（未训练 Qwen3-8B）与 0.929（DCPO 训练版），分别优于 token-logprob 的 0.752 与 0.865。

## Key Contributions

- **Probe-based calibration beats verbalized confidence.** A covariance probe at layer 21 reaches ECE 0.044 vs 0.093 for the model's `<probability>` tag on OpenForesight-test (N=3,020), while being statistically indistinguishable as a ranker (AUROC 0.756 vs 0.758); the calibration advantage persists OOD on skysports (0.064 vs 0.089) and aljazeera (0.140 vs 0.178).
- **Generalizes to frozen GLM models without weight updates.** Probe-only training on ~11.8k improved-context rollouts yields ECE 0.054 vs 0.287 on GLM-4.7-Flash and ECE 0.102 vs 0.255 on GLM-4.5-Air, with GLM-4.5-Air also gaining discrimination (∆AUROC +0.110 [0.069, 0.149]).
- **Causal framework for CoT faithfulness.** Evidence ablation (1,303 pairs over 354 questions) reveals only weak coupling between behavioral and reasoning change (Spearman ρ=0.215) and a 23% "stealth" rate among 460 high-impact pairs; diversionary injection (489 questions) shows the model is largely transparent when actively misled (only 2.5% stealth adoption).
- **Probes double as lie detectors.** Across all 1,792 perturbation cases a layer-20 attention probe tracks behavioral change at ρ=0.565 and predicts change direction in 83.6% of cases; in 107 stealth cases it still shifts (mean |∆probe|=0.065 vs 0.088 in matched faithful pairs), confirming a genuine audit channel rather than a scaling artifact.
- **Forecasts are pre-committed before reasoning.** Forced answering (empty `<think>` prefill) recovers the model's committed answer and confidence in a single forward pass; forced vs free confidence correlates at ρ=0.90 on test, the modal answer matches 67% of the time, and CoT's accuracy gain is only +1.9 pp (CI [+1.0, +2.9]) in distribution.
- **Practical token-saving triage.** A classifier on cheap readouts of the forced pass (entropy, top-probability gap, candidate count) commits confident questions immediately, matching full-reasoning accuracy while saving 30–47% of generated tokens across all splits, beating both random routing and a verbalized-confidence gate.
- **Cross-domain validation on math.** Frozen probes on untrained and DCPO-recipe Qwen3-8B out-discriminate whole-response token-logprob confidence on pooled AIME24/25+AMC23/24 (AUROC 0.894 vs 0.752; 0.929 vs 0.865).

## Methodology

The work targets Eternis-Forecaster 8B (EF-8B), an RLVR post-train of Qwen3-8B for forecasting, with EF-32B used for the forced-answering analysis and OpenForecaster-8B / Qwen3-8B as baselines; two frozen GLM models (GLM-4.7-Flash, GLM-4.5-Air) test probe-only generalization. Data comes from the nikhilchandak/OpenForesight benchmark (train 52.2k, test 302, plus OOD aljazeera/skysports splits), with a custom improved-context build (~half the retrieved-article tokens) for the GLM experiments, and a level-stratified MATH-train subset plus pooled AIME24/25+AMC23/24 for the math stress test.

**Pooling probes.** Rather than reading a single activation, probes compute a pooling function ϕ over the full activation matrix X∈ℝ^{N×D} followed by a linear readout: f(X)=⟨W, ϕ(X)⟩+b. Three variants are swept: mean-pooling (arithmetic average over tokens); attention-pooling (trained query q aggregates via softmax over positions, after Kantamneni et al. and Boppana et al.); and covariance-pooling (low-rank bilinear bottleneck capturing second-order co-fluctuation statistics, after Dooms/Pearce et al.). A full sweep covers all 36 layers, the three architectures, and multiple sites along the reasoning trace from opening `<think>` to closing `</think>`. Discrimination concentrates in mid-to-late layers 19–24; attention probes discriminate but stay overconfident, while covariance probes match discrimination with better calibration — hence the deployed layer-21 covariance probe.

**Faithfulness protocol (after Gur-Arieh et al.).** For each (question, article) pair the authors measure two responses to the same perturbation: the behavioral change |∆prob| (shift in the final `<probability>` tag) and the internal change |∆probe| (shift of a calibrated probe). Evidence ablation removes one real article at a time across 354 questions (1,303 pairs), with same-pipeline controls for sampling noise; diversionary injection inserts one Claude-Sonnet-written misleading article per question (489 questions), with an LLM judge scoring citation and stance.

**Forced answering.** Appending the assistant prefill `<think>\n\n</think>\n\n<answer>` forces EF-32B to commit an answer without free-roaming CoT; the logit readout reproduces the sampled forced-answer distribution at r=0.982. Candidate-answer spread is summarized by Shannon entropy H of the top-8 renormalized continuations, which sorts questions into three regimes and drives the commit-early triage gate.

Metrics include ECE, Brier score, AUROC, and Spearman/Pearson correlations, with 95% question-level (clustered) bootstrap confidence intervals throughout.

## Results & Highlights

- **Probe vs verbalized calibration (EF-8B, OpenForesight-test, N=3,020):** the layer-21 covariance probe reaches **ECE 0.044** vs **ECE 0.093** for the model's verbalized `<probability>` tag, at nearly identical discrimination (AUROC 0.756 vs 0.758); rollouts verbalized at 0.93 resolve correct only 70% of the time, and those at 0.75 only 44%.
- **Probe-only on frozen GLMs (Table 1):** GLM-4.7-Flash mean-pool L28 probe **ECE 0.054 vs 0.287** verbalized (AUROC 0.768 vs 0.669); GLM-4.5-Air attention-1 L18 probe **ECE 0.102 vs 0.255** verbalized (AUROC 0.783 vs 0.691), with a leakage-controlled ∆AUROC of **+0.110 [0.069, 0.149]**.
- **OOD calibration persists:** on skysports ECE **0.064 vs 0.089** and on aljazeera **0.140 vs 0.178**, while verbalized AUROC collapses to 0.587 on aljazeera.
- **CoT faithfulness is weak (evidence ablation, 1,303 pairs, 354 questions):** Spearman ρ between behavioral impact and calibrated CoT change is only **0.215**, and among 460 high-impact pairs (|∆prob|>0.05) **107 (23%) show no CoT change at all** (stealth influence).
- **Diversionary injection (489 questions):** **81.2%** of questions are honest-susceptible (adopt and openly cite the fabricated article), only **2.5% (n=12)** stealth, and 14.7% considered-and-rejected; at the completion level stealth is 46/1,227 = **3.8%** of susceptible completions.
- **Probe-based lie detector (1,792 cases):** the layer-20 attention probe tracks behavioral change at Spearman **ρ=0.565** (vs 0.215 for CoT text; ρ=0.641 on signed shifts) and predicts the direction of change in **83.6%** of cases; in 107 stealth ablation cases it still shifts with mean |∆probe|=**0.065** (vs 0.088 for matched faithful pairs), confirming the signal is not a scaling artifact.
- **Forced answering (EF-32B):** forced vs free confidence correlates at Spearman **ρ=0.90** (test), **0.87** (Late2025), **0.78** (2026Q1); the forced modal answer matches the free answer on **67%** of test questions; logit readout reproduces the forced distribution at **r=0.982**; free CoT beats forced answering by only **+1.9 pp** (95% CI [+1.0, +2.9]) in distribution and a non-significant **+0.9 pp** on Late2025.
- **Token-saving triage:** the entropy gate saves **30–47% of generated tokens** across all splits with every per-split accuracy change within ~1 pp of full reasoning (CI includes zero), beating both random routing and a verbalized-confidence gate.
- **Math reasoning OOD (pooled AIME24/25+AMC23/24, n=580, Table 2):** frozen probe on untrained Qwen3-8B reaches AUROC **0.894 vs 0.752** for token-logprob and ECE **0.083 vs 0.270** (ΔECE −0.187 [−0.227, −0.117]); on DCPO-recipe Qwen3-8B the probe still wins on AUROC **0.929 vs 0.865** though isotonic-recalibrated verbal confidence edges it on ECE (0.125 vs 0.143).

## Authors & Credibility

The core contributors are Raphaël Sarfati and Pratyush Ranjan Tiwari (Goodfire and Eternis), with co-authors from Goodfire (Boppana, Bigelow, Lewis, Merullo lineage via the cited Reasoning Theater work), Brown/Cornell-area affiliation (Earls), and Eternis (Varadaraj, Ho). Goodfire is an established interpretability shop known for sparse-autoencoder and probing tooling, and Eternis provides the EF-8B/EF-32B forecaster models — i.e. the authors control both the probe stack and the model under study, which strengthens internal validity. The paper builds directly on a tight stack of recent, citable methods (RLCR, DCPO, covariance pooling from Dooms/Pearce, Gur-Arieh's faithfulness meta-evaluation, Reasoning Theater) rather than making ungrounded claims. The authors disclose that experiments were run by Silico, Goodfire's agentic interpretability platform, "autonomously with human feedback," and that all designs and write-ups were human-reviewed — a transparency note worth flagging but not, on its own, disqualifying.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The individual ingredients (linear/attention/covariance pooling probes, evidence ablation, forced answering) are not new — covariance pooling is cited to Dooms/Pearce 2026 and the faithfulness framework to Gur-Arieh et al. 2026. The contribution is the synthesis and the specific finding that the *same* probe simultaneously calibrates confidence *and* detects stealth reasoning shifts in forecasting models, plus the practical commit-early triage. That synthesis is genuinely useful but incremental rather than paradigm-shifting.
- **Rigor:** Confidence intervals are question-level clustered bootstraps and the authors are unusually honest about null results — e.g. the GLM-4.7-Flash clean ∆AUROC interval is [−0.006, 0.111], so they explicitly downgrade the claim to "calibration, not ranking" for that model. The probe-site selection (layer 21 covariance for EF-8B, layer 20 attention for the lie detector) is chosen on validation data, which is appropriate, but the paper does not report a held-out probe-selection correction, so some of the headline numbers reflect a best-of-sweep choice.
- **Reproducibility:** Models (EF-8B, EF-32B) are only partially public — EF-8B/EF-32B are Eternis proprietary ("post-trained from Qwen3-8B using an RLVR-style approach"), so independent replication of the central calibration result on the same forecaster is blocked. The GLM and Qwen3-8B experiments are more reproducible, and the OpenForesight dataset is public, but the "improved context" build used for the GLM study is described as non-public. Probe training recipes are documented in appendices but no code release is advertised.
- **Significance:** Practically meaningful: a ~2× ECE reduction without weight updates, a lie detector that works on silent CoT, and a 30–47% token saving are all deployable. The forced-answering + triage result is the most actionable and could plausibly ship in forecasting pipelines.
- **Red flags / data quirks:** (a) The headline EF-8B accuracy is only ~37% with the gold answer absent from the prompt — accuracy climbs to 86–94% when the answer is *contained in the retrieved context*, meaning the forecaster is largely a retriever, not a reasoner, which qualifies how much "calibration" really buys. (b) The "improved context" dataset used for GLM probe-only results is non-public and explicitly built to beat the OpenForecaster paper's contexts, so the GLM numbers are not directly comparable to prior work. (c) Stealth-rate denominators differ between ablation (23% of high-impact pairs) and injection (2.5% of questions), and the authors flag this themselves. (d) The math "OOD" probe-train pool overlaps the RLVR training distribution through DeepScaleR, so cross-model probe similarity is not training-invariant evidence — again, self-disclosed. (e) The paper relies on Claude Sonnet both to generate misleading articles and to judge citation/stance in the injection experiment, creating a single-model dependency for both stimulus and label.

## Strengths

- Three distinct calibration settings (EF-8B forecaster, two frozen GLM models, OOD math) all point the same way, which is more persuasive than a single-model result.
- Honest reporting of null intervals (GLM-4.7-Flash discrimination) and denominator mismatches between faithfulness paradigms.
- The forced-answering analysis is a clean, cheap, and well-motivated diagnostic that directly yields a deployable token-saving gate.
- The lie-detector finding — probe shifts in stealth cases where CoT is silent — is a concrete, falsifiable claim backed by a matched-pair control ruling out a scaling artifact.
- Clear writing with informative figures (reliability diagrams, the four-quadrant injection plot, the entropy-vs-accuracy triage plot).

## Limitations

- Central results depend on proprietary Eternis forecaster models (EF-8B/EF-32B) that independent researchers cannot reproduce; the GLM/Qwen3-8B experiments partially compensate but use a non-public "improved context" build.
- Probe performance is reported at a layer/pooling/site selected on validation data, without an explicit multiple-comparisons or held-out-selection correction, so headline ECE/AUROC may be mildly optimistic.
- Forecaster accuracy (~37% off-context) and the strong dependence on gold-answer containment in the prompt mean the "forecasting" is heavily retrieval-bound; calibration gains are on a model that is often guessing.
- The paper does not test whether the probe signal can be distilled back into verbalized confidence (it raises this as future work), nor whether the commit-early gate degrades on adversarial or distribution-shifted questions.
- Reliance on a single LLM (Claude Sonnet) for both generating injection articles and judging citation/stance introduces a shared-model dependency that is not ablated.
- CoT faithfulness metrics use text-similarity heuristics (Jaccard + character-sequence) rather than semantic entailment, which the cited Gur-Arieh et al. meta-evaluation would flag as imperfect.

## Relevance

对从事 LLM 可解释性、推理模型校准与 CoT 忠实度评估的研究者高度相关：它把"探针读内部状态"从判别正确性扩展到同时校准置信度、审计隐式推理影响并据此节省推理成本，正好覆盖你关注的 reasoning model 评估、置信度校准与机制可解释性三条主线。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the calibrated probe signal be distilled back into the model's own `<probability>` verbalization (e.g. via a probe-loss reward in RLVR) without degrading the internal representation the probe reads?
- Does the commit-early entropy gate remain accuracy-neutral under adversarial or out-of-domain question distributions, or does it selectively skip the hardest, most OOD questions where reasoning would matter most?
- How do covariance-probe calibration gains scale beyond 8B/32B — does the gap between probe and verbalized confidence shrink, grow, or invert at frontier scale?
- Would replacing the Jaccard/character-similarity CoT-change metric with an entailment-based judge (per Gur-Arieh et al.) change the 23% stealth-influence rate or the ρ=0.215 coupling?
- The probe is trained per-layer and per-model; can a single probe transfer across model families (Qwen ↔ GLM ↔ Llama) on the same task, or is the calibrated readout fundamentally model-specific?
- Since accuracy collapses to ~0.26–0.33 when the gold answer is not in the retrieved context, how much of the calibration gain is just the probe detecting "answer in context" rather than genuine epistemic uncertainty?
