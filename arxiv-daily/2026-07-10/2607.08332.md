> **一句话总结：** XALPHA 将 alpha 挖掘重构为报告落地、记忆驱动的"假设-代码"闭环研究流程，通过 RMA 吸收层 + Macro/Micro/Cross 三脑架构在 CSI300 上大幅超越 CogAlpha、R&D-Agent(Q) 等 baseline（IR 1.5853 vs CogAlpha 0.3743）。

> **TL;DR:** XALPHA is a memory-driven AI Quant Researcher that turns alpha mining into a closed hypothesis-to-code research loop, using a Report-to-Memory Absorption (RMA) layer plus Macro/Micro/Cross brains to plan themes, evolve executable OHLCV factor code with tri-alignment validation, and consolidate discovery feedback; on CSI300 (10-day open-to-open return, Qlib), it beats the strongest alpha-mining baseline CogAlpha on portfolio IR (1.5853 vs 0.3743) and on AER (0.1412 vs 0.0381), despite CogAlpha retaining a higher RankIC (0.0590 vs 0.0489).

## 核心贡献

- **首次将 alpha 挖掘建模为闭环研究流程：** 把"读报告 → 提假设 → 写因子代码 → 验证 → 反思 → 进化"组织成持续迭代的 hypothesis-to-code 循环，而非一次性的因子生成或孤立的 backtest 搜索。
- **Report-to-Memory Absorption (RMA) 三层 A/B/C 记忆：** 将外部研究报告转化为结构化、可检索的 OHLCV 兼容知识——A 层做 OHLCV 可行性 KEEP/DROP 筛选，B 层做机制家族归类，C 层构造可执行的 Research Archetype 记录，避免把报告原文硬编码进公式。
- **三脑架构：** Macro Brain 做 cycle 级研究规划与 Archetype 路由（支持 fixed/coarse-guided/memory-driven 三种模式）；Micro Brain 做假设到可执行因子代码的演化（含 AST gate、TriAlign-Judge、unit test 三道质量关卡，以及 mutation/crossover/refinement 机制级演化 + novelty injection）；Cross Brain 做 Factor-to-Archetype Attribution 与 GOOD/BAD 反馈整合，写回 archetype/generation/cycle 三级记忆。
- **强实证：** 在 CSI300（Qlib，10 天 open-to-open 收益）上，XALPHA 取得最佳 IC、ICIR、RankICIR、AR、AER、IR；其中 IR 达 1.5853，远超 alpha 方法组内最强 baseline。

## Key Contributions

- Reframes alpha discovery as a continuous, report-grounded, memory-driven hypothesis-to-code research loop rather than one-shot factor generation, isolated backtest search, or symbolic evolution.
- Introduces the Report-to-Memory Absorption (RMA) layer with a three-tier A/B/C taxonomy: A-layer filters report fragments for daily-OHLCV feasibility (KEEP/DROP), B-layer groups evidence into mechanism families (e.g., trend/momentum, reversal, price-volume interaction), C-layer builds actionable Research Archetype records that guide (but are not) factor formulas.
- Proposes a closed-loop three-brain architecture: Macro Brain (cycle-level theme planning and archetype routing from B/C memory), Micro Brain (hypothesis-to-code generation with AST/static/numerical/unit-test validation, tri-alignment judge between idea/logic/financial plausibility, and mechanism-level mutation/crossover/refinement evolution with mid-cycle novelty injection), Cross Brain (factor-to-archetype attribution and GOOD/BAD feedback consolidated into archetype/generation/cycle memory).
- Demonstrates on CSI300 that XALPHA beats representative ML, deep-learning, and LLM-based alpha-mining baselines (Alpha360, AutoAgent, AlphaAgent, R&D-Agent(Q), CogAlpha) across most predictive and portfolio metrics, with especially large gains on held-out portfolio diagnostics.

## Methodology

XALPHA is an AI Quant Researcher that organizes alpha mining into an iterative research loop with four components.

**Report-to-Memory Absorption (RMA).** External research reports (PDFs, papers) are not fed raw into prompts. Instead, RMA applies a three-layer taxonomy: the A-layer makes a KEEP/DROP decision on each report fragment based on whether its core mechanism can be observed, inferred, or proxied using daily open/high/low/close/volume (e.g., accounting-ratio fragments are DROPped; return-decomposition fragments are KEEP). The B-layer assigns A-approved atoms to coarse mechanism families (e.g., B1 trend-and-momentum) with a reusable research path. The C-layer specializes B-paths into retrievable Research Archetype records (e.g., C101 initiation momentum, C102 continuation momentum, C103 momentum failure). An archetype is a structured research cue, not a factor formula.

**Macro Brain (research planning).** At each cycle it picks a routing mode (fixed-theme, coarse-guided, or memory-driven). By default XALPHA runs 5 coarse-guided cycles to initialize feedback, then switches to memory-driven routing that uses GOOD/BAD summaries, cycle outcomes, and archetype coverage to choose the next theme and avoid saturated/failed directions. It selects 1 primary B-layer and 3-4 supporting B-layers, narrows C-layer archetypes, and assembles an active research-agent bundle that emits an archetype-guided hypothesis pool.

**Micro Brain (hypothesis-to-code evolution).** Active agents turn the hypothesis pool into executable Python factor code. Each factor passes a quality pipeline: (1) AST gate rejects invalid syntax, unsupported imports, static future leakage (negative shifts), excessive complexity; (2) TriAlign-Judge checks consistency among the hypothesis idea, code logic, and financial rationale; (3) numerical validation drops factors with >30% invalid/extreme values or too many low-information dates; (4) unit tests using truncation and future-noise perturbation catch dynamic leakage. Survivors are evaluated empirically and evolved via mechanism-level mutation, crossover, and refinement (not random syntactic edits). Novelty injection fires at configured generations (3 and 7 in a 10-generation cycle), with redundancy filtered by a 0.95 correlation threshold.

**Factor evaluation and selection.** Factors are scored by IC, ICIR, RankIC, RankICIR (direction-aligned, winsorized), plus rolling-OOS diagnostics. Normal selection keeps top 45% (warm-up) by s_normal = 0.70·alpha_train + 0.30·r_train,OOS, with a complexity discount, a RankIC floor of 0.005, and a RankIC positive-ratio floor of 0.50, to maintain the parent pool. Elite selection uses train+validation evidence, no complexity discount, a RankIC floor of 0.01 and positive-ratio floor of 0.55, keeping top 15% in warm-up. Library admission at cycle finalization uses s_library = 0.70·Norm(RankIC) + 0.30·Norm(IC) with an admission threshold of 0.75.

**Cross Brain (feedback consolidation).** Factor-to-Archetype Attribution (FAA) re-assigns each elite factor (whose final mechanism may differ after repair/evolution) to a B-layer family and C-layer archetype. Feedback is condensed into mechanism-level GOOD (validated mechanisms, reusable principles) and BAD (failure types, avoidance rules) summaries, written to archetype-level, generation-level (short-term, for novelty injection), and cycle-level memory that steers subsequent routing.

## Results & Highlights

XALPHA was evaluated on CSI300 (Qlib daily data) predicting 10-day adjusted open-to-open return, with train 2011-01-01 to 2020-12-31, validation 2021, and test 2022-01-01 to 2025-12-31. All agents use gpt-oss-120b locally on two H100 GPUs (no external API cost).

- On CSI300 10-day (Table 2), XALPHA achieves IC = 0.0413 vs the strongest alpha-mining baseline CogAlpha IC = 0.0382 (delta +0.0031), and ICIR = 0.2573 vs CogAlpha 0.2040 (delta +0.0533). The RankICIR gap is larger: 0.3412 vs 0.2937 (delta +0.0475). Note CogAlpha still wins on raw RankIC (0.0590 vs XALPHA's 0.0489).
- Portfolio diagnostics show a large margin: XALPHA IR = 1.5853 vs CogAlpha 0.3743 and R&D-Agent(Q) 0.4689 (best within the alpha group for portfolio metrics before XALPHA) — roughly 3.4x the strongest baseline IR. Annualized Excess Return (AER) = 0.1412 for XALPHA vs 0.0416 for R&D-Agent(Q) and 0.0381 for CogAlpha; Annualized Return (AR) = 0.1792 vs 0.0731 / 0.0639.
- Among ML baselines, LightGBM is strongest on RankIC (0.0330) but only reaches IR 0.3865; among deep-learning baselines CNN is best (RankIC 0.0321, IR 0.5430). Both are well below XALPHA.
- Elite factor diversity (Figure 2): within one theme (50 elite factors), average absolute pairwise correlation = 0.234, median = 0.181, 68.0% of pairs below 0.3; across six themes (60 factors), average drops to 0.142, median 0.099, 87.1% below 0.3 — indicating genuine mechanism diversity rather than redundant variants.
- A representative elite factor (regime-dependent reversal after downside overshoot) achieves Train-Val IC = 0.0382, RankIC = 0.0564, ICIR = 0.2622, RankICIR = 0.3593; on the test split IC = 0.0440, RankIC = 0.0634, ICIR = 0.2311, RankICIR = 0.3188, demonstrating out-of-sample robustness.
- Computational cost: ~15 seconds per factor, ~16 minutes per generation, ~3 hours per full mining cycle on two H100 GPUs.

The framing is grounded in a credible lineage of prior work: it explicitly builds on StructMem for structured memory, AI Scientist / AI Co-Scientist / EvoScientist for the autonomous-research-agent paradigm, and positions itself against AlphaGPT, FAMA, AlphaAgent, AlphaJungle, RD-Agent(Q), FactorMiner, and CogAlpha — differentiating on the combination of report-grounded memory + macro planning + discovery feedback consolidation. ## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine architectural novelty in the RMA A/B/C taxonomy that explicitly enforces OHLCV feasibility before any formula is written, and in the closed Macro/Micro/Cross loop with FAA attribution. However, each individual component (memory, evolutionary code search, tri-alignment) has close relatives in recent LLM-quant work (FactorMiner's experience memory, CogAlpha's code-based evolution, AlphaAgent's hypothesis-alignment regularization); the contribution is the integration, not any single mechanism.
- **Rigor:** The main comparison (Table 2) is a single dataset (CSI300), single horizon (10-day), single calendar split, single seed. There is no variance reporting, no statistical-significance testing, and no multiple-seed sweep, so the headline IR of 1.5853 vs 0.3743 has no confidence interval. The train/validation/test split is fixed and conventional, which is good, but the absence of error bars is a notable gap for a paper claiming a 3-4x portfolio-IR improvement.
- **Reproducibility:** Reasonable on paper — uses the public Qlib CSI300 dataset, a named open LLM (gpt-oss-120b), and lists concrete hyperparameters (initial pool 64, parent pool 80, 10 generations/cycle, novelty interval 4, normal 60th percentile, elite 80th percentile). But there is no mention of code release, and many internal prompts (Appendix M) and the full RMA taxonomy construction are only partially described, making exact reproduction uncertain.
- **Significance:** If the portfolio-IR gains hold up, this is a meaningful step for LLM-based quant research because it shows a closed-loop memory-driven agent can substantially beat code-evolution baselines (CogAlpha) on practical portfolio metrics. The CogAlpha comparison is the critical one and is the weakest point of the story (see below).
- **Red flags:** The headline IR gain is concentrated almost entirely in portfolio metrics (AR, AER, IR), while the core cross-sectional predictive metric RankIC is actually lower than CogAlpha (0.0489 vs 0.0590). A ~3.4x IR jump from factors with weaker rank predictiveness is a data quirk that deserves explanation: it may stem from the Ridge-regression ensemble of the factor library rather than from stronger individual factors, but the paper does not cleanly isolate this. Also, the backtest covers 2022-2025 test (only ~4 years, including a known Chinese-market regime shift) with no turnover, capacity, or transaction-cost sensitivity analysis reported in the main text, which is a serious omission for any portfolio-IR claim.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean formulation of alpha discovery as a closed research loop with explicit report-to-memory, plan, implement, validate, and reflect stages.
- The RMA A/B/C taxonomy is a thoughtful design: enforcing OHLCV feasibility at ingestion prevents many unrealistic factors upstream, and separating mechanism families from archetype memory is principled.
- Mechanism-level evolution (mutation/crossover/refinement) plus tri-alignment validation is more defensible than random syntactic search and produces genuinely readable factors (the Listing 1 example is interpretable).
- Empirical diversity analysis (Figure 2, 87.1% cross-theme pairs below 0.3 correlation) is a meaningful robustness check that goes beyond aggregate IC numbers.
- Honest about the one metric it loses: explicitly acknowledges CogAlpha has higher RankIC.

## Limitations

- Evaluated on a single market (CSI300), single horizon (10-day), single split — no cross-market (e.g., US, other A-share universes), no multi-horizon ablation, no multi-seed variance.
- No statistical significance tests or confidence intervals on the headline portfolio-IR gap.
- The IR gain is not reconciled with the fact that XALPHA's RankIC is below CogAlpha's; the contribution of the Ridge ensemble vs. individual factor quality is not isolated.
- No transaction cost, turnover, capacity, or slippage analysis in the main text — IR of 1.5853 is unrealistically high for a real CSI300 strategy and likely shrinks substantially after costs.
- No quantitative ablation on the individual components (RMA, Macro routing, TriAlign, novelty injection, FAA) — the component analysis (Tables 3-4) is qualitative case studies, not controlled removals. It is impossible to tell which brain contributes most.
- Fully reliant on gpt-oss-120b; no sensitivity to LLM backbone.
- No external code or data release mentioned.

## Relevance

与用户在 LLM agent、量化投资、自主研究系统方向高度相关：XALPHA 把报告吸收、结构化记忆、假设-代码闭环整合成一个完整的 AI Quant Researcher，是 memory-driven autonomous research agent 范式在金融 alpha 挖掘上的代表性落地，可作为 LLM agent 闭环架构、因子挖掘自动化研究的直接参考。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How much of the 3.4x IR gain survives realistic transaction costs, market impact, and turnover constraints on CSI300, and how does it change if the test window excludes the 2024-2025 regime?
- What is the isolated contribution of each component (RMA, Macro memory-driven routing, TriAlign, novelty injection, FAA) — a controlled ablation removing each brain would clarify whether the gain comes from memory, evolution, or simply from generating a larger, more diverse factor library fed to Ridge?
- Why does XALPHA beat CogAlpha on portfolio IR while losing on RankIC — is it factor diversification, the ensemble learner, or the library admission threshold (0.75)?
- How sensitive are the results to the LLM backbone (gpt-oss-120b vs. other open/closed models), to seed variance, and to the 5-coarse-guided-cycle warm-up?
- Does the RMA A-layer OHLCV filter, by discarding fundamentals/order-book/report fragments, systematically bias the archetype pool away from whole alpha families that could be proxied with alternative data?
- Can the memory-driven routing be shown to actually improve over random or fixed-theme routing across many cycles, beyond the two qualitative case studies in Tables 3-4?
