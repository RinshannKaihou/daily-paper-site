Dynamics of Gradient Descent with Large Step Size Near a Manifold of Flat Minima

arXiv: 2607.08380

Date: 2026-07-09

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08380

一句话总结： 本文将大步长梯度下降（GD）在平坦极小值附近的动力学理论从单输出、孤立极小值的余维 1 情形推广到任意余维（向量输出、平坦极小值流形），证明 GD 沿解流形隐式地执行关于锐度（sharpness）的黎曼梯度下降、在法向最高特征方向上呈 flip 分岔、在其余法向上指数收缩，并给出了次临界/临界/超临界三个收敛定理及深度矩阵分解中的应用。
TL;DR: This paper extends the large-step-size gradient-descent (GD) normal-form and convergence theory of MacDonald (the codimension-1, isolated-flat-minimum setting of ref. [39]) to overparametrised least squares of arbitrary codimension with vector-valued outputs and a manifold of flat minima, proving on 3-layer 2×2 matrix factorisation that GD splits into Riemannian GD on the sharpness along the solution manifold, a flip bifurcation along the top normal eigendirection, and contraction along the rest; convergence is Θ(t⁻¹ᐟ²) at the critical threshold η=2/λ₁ and exponential (period-2 orbit of amplitude Θ((ηλ₁−2)¹ᐟ²)) in the supercritical regime, generalising the prior codimension-1 results of [39].

核心贡献

  • 从余维 1 推广到任意余维： 将大步长 GD 的 normal form 与三个收敛定理从单标量输出、孤立平坦极小值的余维 1 设定，推广到向量输出、含任意余维 q 的过参数最小二乘（即可对应任意多观测值的回归问题），大幅提升了理论的实用性。
  • 平坦极小值流形设定： 指出高余维问题（如矩阵分解）中不存在孤立平坦极小值，而存在平坦极小值流形 F；据此建立一整套几何假设层级（Assumptions 3.1, 3.3, 3.4, 3.5），刻画解流形 M、锐度 λ₁ 的光滑性、λ₁ 沿 F 的 Morse-Bott 性、以及奇异 PDE 的正则性。
  • 完整的 normal form（Theorem 4.1）： 证明在 F 附近存在依赖于 η 的 C¹ 坐标变换，使 GD 分解为：(i) 沿 M 对 λ₁ 的 Riemannian GD，步长由 y₁² 与 ζ 控制；(ii) 沿 ν₁ 方向的 flip 分岔；(iii) 沿其余 q−1 个法向的线性收缩。
  • 三个收敛定理： 次临界（η<2/λ₁|F）指数收敛到次优平坦极小值；临界（η=2/λ₁|F）以 Θ(t⁻¹ᐟ²) 收敛到 F；超临界（η>2/λ₁|F）指数收敛到沿 ν₁|F 张成的 period-2 轨道，振幅 Θ((ηλ₁|F−2)¹ᐟ²)。
  • 深度矩阵分解的新结构结果： 证明平坦极小值集合 F 是球面乘积上的纤维丛（fibre bundle over a product of spheres），且锐度 λ₁ 沿 F 是 Morse-Bott 的；并给出 ∇²_M λ₁|ν_F 的谱（在 type-(i) 方向恒为 4σ₁²⁻⁴ᐟᴸ，在 L=2 的均衡解处达到下界 4σ₁²/(σ₁−σ_d²)>4）。
  • 奇异 PDE 的新解法： 提出一种求解沿流形 F 奇异的偏微分方程的新方法（Theorem C.4），并修正了 [39] 中 normal form 一个有缺陷的证明；该方法据称在 PDE 理论中也属新结果，可能具有独立价值。

Key Contributions

  • Beyond codimension 1: Generalises both the normal form and all three convergence theorems of [39] from the restrictive codimension-1 setting (single scalar output, isolated flat minimum) to overparametrised least-squares problems of arbitrary codimension q, covering regression with arbitrarily many observations.
  • Manifold of flat minima (not isolated points): Observes that natural higher-codimension problems such as matrix factorisation do not admit isolated flat minima but a manifold F of flat minima, and builds a hierarchy of geometric assumptions (3.1 manifold structure, 3.3 smooth top eigenvalue, 3.4 Morse-Bott + invariance, 3.5 PDE regularity) sufficient to analyse GD near F.
  • Normal form (Theorem 4.1): Provides a C¹ η-dependent coordinate change near any point of F under which GD decomposes into Riemannian GD on λ₁ along M (step size governed by y₁²·ζ), a flip bifurcation along the top eigendirection ν₁ of the Hessian, and linear contraction I_{q−1}−ηΛ_{2:q} along the remaining q−1 normal eigendirections.
  • Three convergence theorems: Proves subcritical (η<2/λ₁|F: exponential convergence O(βᵗ) to a suboptimally flat minimum after a finite transient), critical (η=2/λ₁|F: Θ(t⁻¹ᐟ²) convergence to F), and supercritical (η>2/λ₁|F: convergence to a period-2 orbit of amplitude Θ((ηλ₁|F−2)¹ᐟ²) at rate O((1−C(ηλ₁|F−2))ᵗ)) regimes.
  • New structural results for deep matrix factorisation: Shows the flat-minima set F is a fibre bundle over a product of unit spheres ∏S^{d_l−1}, that every minimiser has the form (diag(σ₁^{1/L}, W_l))^L up to a linear isometry, and that λ₁ is Morse-Bott along F — structure not noted in prior work [42, 40].
  • A novel solution of a singular PDE: Develops a new method (Theorem C.4) to solve the PDE singular along the manifold F required for the normal form, which cannot be handled by existing point-singularity literature [56]; also rectifies a flawed proof in [39].

Methodology

The paper works in the framework of smooth dynamical systems and differential geometry applied to the GD map GD(η,w) = (η, w − η∇ℓ(w)) for the least-squares objective ℓ(w)=½‖τ−f(w)‖² with a C∞ model f:ℝᵖ→ℝ^{q}, p>q≥1.

The analysis proceeds in four stages:

  1. Geometric setup (Section 3.2). Assumption 3.1 makes the solution set M=f⁻¹{τ} a smooth submanifold of ℝᵖ (equivalent to the NTK being full-rank on M). Proposition 3.2 establishes the correspondence between the kernel/eigenvectors of ∇²ℓ and the tangent/normal directions of M. A hierarchy of assumptions (3.3: λ₁ simple and smooth off a closed set S; 3.4: λ₁ Morse-Bott along a flat-minima submanifold F⊂M\S plus ν₁-invariance; 3.5: a positivity/regularity condition on a coefficient αη of the normal-form PDE) is imposed.
  1. Normal form (Section 4, Theorem 4.1). A centre-manifold theorem [38] performs dimension reduction from q to 1, after which the dynamics along the centre manifold are transformed into the desired normal form by solving a PDE singular along F. This PDE solution (Appendix C) is the main new technical device; in codimension 1 [39] used existing point-singularity results [56], which do not apply here.
  1. Convergence theorems (Section 5). The normal form makes the three regimes transparent: GD_x acts as RGD on λ₁ with step size controlled by y₁² and ζ; GD_{y1} is a flip bifurcation whose linear part differs from −1 by ηλ₁|F−2; GD_{y2:q} is a contraction. Theorems 5.1 (subcritical), 5.2 (critical) and 5.3 (supercritical) formalise the resulting convergence rates, using deep invariant-manifold theory. The critical theorem is the hardest and in its most general form relies on Assumption D.12 and Conjecture D.7 (a parabolic analogue of the strong-stable foliation theorem).
  1. Case study: matrix factorisation (Section 3.3, Appendix E). The authors verify all assumptions for L-layer matrix factorisation f(W₁,…,W_L)=W_L⋯W₁ and prove the fibre-bundle structure of F and the Morse-Bott property of λ₁, with explicit eigenvalue formulae for ∇²_M λ₁|ν_F. Numerical simulations on 3-layer 2×2 matrix factorisation across five independent trials illustrate each of the three regimes (Figures 3–5).

Results & Highlights

This is primarily a theory paper; the "results" are convergence-rate theorems plus small-scale verifying simulations rather than benchmark comparisons against baselines.

  • Critical-regime rate (Theorem 5.2), 3-layer 2×2 matrix factorisation: GD iterates converge to a point in F with rate Θ(t⁻¹ᐟ²); Figure 4 (5 independent trials) shows ‖y_t‖ and d_M(x_t,F) tracking the predicted t⁻¹ᐟ² reference line asymptotically out to ~10⁷ iterations.
  • Subcritical-regime rate (Theorem 5.1), 3-layer 2×2 matrix factorisation: After a finite transient T = O(y₁,0²·((λ₁(x₀)−λ₁|F)/(2/η−λ₁|F))^γ), iterates converge exponentially at rate O(βᵗ) with β = 1−(2−ηλ₁(x_T))<1, to a suboptimally flat minimum whose suboptimality satisfies λ₁(x_∞)−λ₁|F ≥ exp(−O(y₁,T²·(1−β²)⁻¹)·(λ₁(x_T)−λ₁|F)); Figure 3 shows ‖y_t‖ decaying from ~10⁻² to ~10⁻¹⁰ over 80k iterations.
  • Supercritical-regime rate (Theorem 5.3), 3-layer 2×2 matrix factorisation: For η slightly above 2/λ₁|F, iterates converge at rate O((1−C(ηλ₁|F−2))ᵗ) to a period-2 orbit of amplitude Θ((ηλ₁|F−2)¹ᐟ²) along ν₁|F; Figure 5 shows ‖y_t‖ settling to a constant (~10⁻²) while d_M(x_t,F) decays exponentially to ~10⁻⁴, consistent with a stable period-2 cycle centred on F.
  • Spectrum of ∇²_M λ₁|ν_F in deep matrix factorisation (Proposition E.5): eigenvalues in type-(i) directions are all constant, equal to 4σ₁²⁻⁴ᐟᴸ (and in particular =4 when L=2); type-(ii) eigenvalues blow up approaching S and at the "balanced solution" (L=2) achieve the lower bound 4σ₁²/(σ₁−σ_d²) > 4 reported in prior work [21]. This is the quantitative content justifying the Morse-Bott property and shows the Hessian of λ₁ along F is not a scalar multiple of the identity — the very situation that forces Theorem 5.2 to depend on Conjecture D.7.
  • Generalisation over the prior baseline [39]: the new normal form and convergence theorems strictly subsume [39], which is recovered only when q=1 and F is a single point; [39]'s geodesic-strong-convexity assumption is recovered as the special case of Morse-Bott along a point.

No quantitative results on standard ML benchmarks are reported; the empirical evidence is restricted to verifying simulations on 3-layer 2×2 matrix factorisation.

Authors & Credibility

The authors are Lachlan Ewen MacDonald and René Vidal, both at the Innovation in Data Engineering and Science (IDEAS) center, University of Pennsylvania. René Vidal is a highly established researcher (former Hopkins professor, known for foundational work in computer vision, algebraic geometry, and more recently the theoretical foundations of deep learning). The paper is a careful, mathematically dense preprint (cs.LG, math.DS, math.OC) building directly on MacDonald's prior single-author work [39]. The authors explicitly flag and correct a flawed proof in [39] and openly concede that one of their main theorems (Theorem 5.2 in full generality) depends on an unproven dynamical-systems conjecture (Conjecture D.7) — a level of intellectual honesty that lends credibility.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High within its niche. The codimension-1 normal form of [39] was itself recent, and this paper is the natural and substantial generalisation to (a) vector-valued outputs and (b) manifold-of-flat-minima rather than isolated points — both extensions the authors argue are necessary for any practical example such as matrix factorisation. The fibre-bundle / Morse-Bott structural results for the matrix-factorisation loss landscape (Propositions E.2, E.5) appear genuinely new relative to [42, 40, 21].
  • Rigour: Mathematically rigorous, but with a notable caveat. The critical convergence theorem (Theorem 5.2) is, by the authors' own admission, not fully proved in the general case: it is asserted only when ∇²_M λ₁|ν_F is a scalar multiple of the identity, and otherwise is reduced to a conjecture (Conjecture D.7, a parabolic analogue of the strong-stable foliation theorem for normally hyperbolic manifolds). The "honest generalisation" condition does not hold for matrix factorisation, where the type-(i) and type-(ii) eigenvalues (4σ₁²⁻⁴ᐟᴸ and 4σ₁²/(σ₁−σ_d²)>4) are manifestly different. Assumption D.12 is verified only for two-layer matrix factorisation (Proposition E.7), leaving the critical theorem partly conjectural exactly in the paper's flagship application for L≠2.
  • Reproducibility: The theory is fully specified; simulations use a standard 3-layer 2×2 matrix factorisation setup with 5 trials, but no code is referenced, initialisation details, learning-rate schedules, and seeds are not tabulated, and the experiments are confined to an extremely small toy problem. Re-deriving the experiments is feasible but would require effort.
  • Significance: Conceptually important for the edge-of-stability / large-step-size GD literature: it provides a clean geometric picture (RGD on sharpness + flip bifurcation + contraction) that systematises prior empirical observations. However, the practical reach is limited: the theorems are local (only in a neighbourhood of F), the authors explicitly state the results do not address the "progressive sharpening" or "edge of stability" regimes except at their tail-end, and there is no verification on actual deep neural networks despite that being the motivation.
  • Red flags / missing pieces:
  • The flagship critical theorem relies on an unproven conjecture (Conjecture D.7) in the general case most relevant to the application.
  • All experiments are on a 3-layer, 2×2 matrix factorisation toy problem — no scaling, no comparison to other edge-of-stability theories, no DNN experiments.
  • Assumption 3.4 part 2 (ν₁|F-invariance under GD on ℓ) is a non-trivial condition whose necessity is acknowledged but whose breadth of applicability beyond matrix factorisation is not characterised.
  • The normal form is C¹ (not C∞), and the authors do not explore whether higher regularity would yield sharper predictions.

Strengths

  • Clean, general geometric framework that subsumes [39] and unifies the three convergence regimes under a single normal form.
  • Genuine structural results for the matrix-factorisation loss landscape (fibre bundle over a product of spheres; explicit Hessian spectrum) that are independently interesting.
  • The novel PDE-solution technique (Theorem C.4) for equations singular along a submanifold is a methodological contribution that may be reused beyond this paper.
  • Intellectual honesty: the authors clearly delineate which results are fully proved versus conjectural, and explicitly correct a flawed proof in [39].

Limitations

  • Locality: Theorems hold only near the flat-minima manifold F; they say nothing about global convergence, the progressive-sharpening phase, or the bulk of the edge-of-stability regime.
  • Conjectural critical theorem: Theorem 5.2 in the general case depends on the unproven Conjecture D.7 and Assumption D.12 (the latter only verified for L=2 matrix factorisation).
  • Toy experiments only: Verification is restricted to 3-layer 2×2 matrix factorisation; no deep-network experiments despite DL being the motivation.
  • Restrictive assumptions: Morse-Bott of λ₁ along F, ν₁-invariance (Assumption 3.4.2), and Assumption 3.5 on αη are non-trivial and their applicability to general DNNs is unestablished.
  • Beyond supercritical: Higher-periodicity and chaotic dynamics at yet larger step sizes [16] are explicitly left as open problems.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can Conjecture D.7 (the parabolic strong-stable foliation theorem) be proved, fully closing the critical-regime theorem for matrix factorisation with L>2?
  • Do the geometric assumptions (3.4 Morse-Bott + ν₁-invariance, 3.5 αη regularity) hold for any non-trivial deep neural network class, or only for matrix factorisation / linear-ish models?
  • Since the flat-minima manifold F in matrix factorisation is a fibre bundle with non-constant ∇²_M λ₁|ν_F spectrum, do different submanifolds of F generalise differently — giving a sharper, geometry-aware notion of "flat minimum" that refines the flat-minima-generalise-better hypothesis challenged by [22, 50]?
  • Can the local normal form be patched into a global statement that also captures the progressive-sharpening and edge-of-stability regimes the authors explicitly exclude?
  • How do the predictions change when GD is replaced by SGD or momentum-based optimisers, where noise and inertia perturb the flip-bifurcation structure?
