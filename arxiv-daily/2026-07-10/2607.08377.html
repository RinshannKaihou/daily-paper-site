Eigenvalue Calibration for Semantic Embeddings of Large Language Models

arXiv: 2607.08377

Date: 2026-07-09

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08377

一句话总结： 本文首次为 LLM 语义嵌入密度矩阵的特征值提出"矩阵校准/特征值校准"理论框架，证明熵与风险在标定下等价、并通过对特征值做温度缩放来最小化 proper score 风险，实验在 TriviaQA 与 Natural Questions 上证明 Phi 4 / Phi 4 Mini / Llama4 Maverick 普遍过自信（最优温度 >1），校准后 AUROC 多有提升。
TL;DR: The paper introduces a calibration framework for the eigenvalues of density-matrix predictors built from LLM semantic embeddings, proves entropy-risk equivalence under matrix calibration plus a central eigenvalue-calibration inequality, and shows that a matrix temperature-scaling transform (injective on density matrices) minimizes a proper-score calibration error; on TriviaQA and Natural Questions with Phi 4, Phi 4 Mini, and Llama4 Maverick, all models are systematically overconfident (optimal T > 1), and recalibration cuts the eigenvalue ECE (e.g., Phi 4 Mini TriviaQA 0.36 -> 0.21) while improving AUROC in most settings (e.g., Phi 4 on Natural Questions eigenvalue AUROC 0.742 -> 0.794).

核心贡献

  • 首次将 LLM + 语义嵌入形式化为密度矩阵预测器 d(x) = E_{a~f(x)}[e(a)e(a)^T]，把密度矩阵的特征值解读为"潜在语义结果"的概率，并据此给出矩阵校准与特征值校准的严格定义（Definition 1）。
  • 证明关键理论结果：Theorem 1（特征值校准不等式，矩阵校准可推出 λmax(D_{Y|d(X)}) = ΛX，但仅能得到 λmax(D_{Y|ΛX}) ≤ ΛX 的不等式）；Lemma 1 与 Theorem 2（熵-风险等价与收敛，标定下平均熵等于风险）；Theorem 3（对密度矩阵做单射变换时，风险变化恰等于校准误差变化）。
  • 提出矩阵温度缩放 h_TS(M) = M^α / tr(M^α)（Proposition 3，证明 α>0 时单射），并证明在最小化 proper score 风险时该变换恰好优化校准，把经典分类温度缩放严格推广到特征值空间。
  • 提出基于"分箱 + 层次聚类"的特征值可靠性图算法（Algorithm 1），用以近似 Theorem 1 中所需的"以密度矩阵为条件"，从而可视化并量化过自信。
  • 在真实问答任务上验证：当代 LLM 普遍系统性过自信，矩阵温度缩放在多数设定下同时降低 ECE 并提升 AUROC；并对照 KLE/SE 等基线说明谱方法已具竞争力、亟需专属校准手段。

Key Contributions

  • Formalizes the combination of an LLM and a semantic embedding model as a density matrix predictor d(x) = E_{a~f(x)}[e(a)e(a)^T], interprets its eigenvalues as probabilities over latent semantic outcomes, and gives the first formal definitions of matrix calibration and eigenvalue calibration (Definition 1).
  • Proves a chain of theoretical results: the Eigenvalue Calibration Inequality (Theorem 1), which shows matrix calibration descends from canonical calibration but yields only an inequality (≤) for eigenvalues conditioned on the max eigenvalue alone; an entropy-risk equivalence (Lemma 1) and its convergence version (Theorem 2); and a calibration-optimization theorem (Theorem 3) proving that an injective transform's change in proper-score risk equals its change in calibration error.
  • Defines proper matrix scores, links them to Bregman matrix divergences / von Neumann entropy (Propositions 1-2), and introduces matrix temperature scaling h_TS(M) = M^α / tr(M^α) (Proposition 3), proving it is injective for α>0 and therefore optimizes calibration when the matrix log-score risk is minimized — a principled generalization of Guo et al. (2017) to eigenvalues.
  • Proposes Algorithm 1, a reliability-diagram construction for eigenvalues that adds hierarchical clustering on the predicted density matrices within each bin, approximating the conditioning λmax(D_{Y|d(X)}) | λmax(d(X)) required by Theorem 1.
  • Empirically demonstrates that current open-source LLMs are systematically overconfident (optimal temperature > 1 across every model/dataset), that matrix temperature scaling lowers the eigenvalue ECE and improves AUROC in most settings, and that density-matrix spectral methods already outperform Semantic Entropy (SE) and Kernel Language Entropy (KLE).

Methodology

The paper builds a theory-then-practice pipeline spanning Sections 2-5.

Setup (density matrix predictors). Given an LLM f: X_text -> P_text and an embedding model e: X_text -> R^d whose outputs are assumed normalized onto the hypersphere, the density matrix predictor is d(x) := E_{a~f(x)}[e(a)e(a)^T], estimated with m sampled answers. Its eigenvalues lie in the simplex Δ_d and are interpreted as probabilities of latent semantic outcomes (e.g., "It's Paris." and "The answer is Paris." collapse onto the same eigenvalue).

Calibration notions (Definition 1). Matrix calibration requires D_{Y|d(X)} = d(X) a.s.; eigenvalue calibration requires λmax(D_{Y|ΛX}) = ΛX with ΛX = λmax(d(X)). The Eigenvalue Calibration Inequality (Theorem 1) proves that matrix calibration implies eigenvalue calibration when conditioning on the full density matrix, but only an inequality when conditioning on the max eigenvalue alone — a fact that directly motivates the clustering step in Algorithm 1.

Proper matrix scores and entropy-risk equivalence. The authors define proper matrix scores S via tr[S(M_pred) M_target] >= tr[S(M_target) M_target], show they induce Bregman matrix divergences and recover the matrix log score S_log(M) = -log(M) whose entropy is the von Neumann entropy of the eigenvalues. Lemma 1 proves that under matrix calibration the expected entropy equals the proper-score risk R_S(d) = E[Y^T S(d(X)) Y]; Theorem 2 extends this to a convergence statement for sequences approaching calibration; Lemma 2 gives a calibration-sharpness-noise decomposition.

Optimizing calibration via matrix temperature scaling. Theorem 3 shows that for any injective h, R_S(h∘d) - R_S(d) = Cal_S(h∘d) - Cal_S(d), so minimizing the matrix log-score risk provably minimizes calibration error. Proposition 3 proves h_TS(M) = M^α / tr(M^α) = (1/α) Σ λ_i^α e_i e_i^T is injective for α > 0, making it the calibrated choice; 1/α plays the role of the temperature.

Reliability diagrams for eigenvalues (Algorithm 1). Because Theorem 1 demands conditioning on the full matrix, Algorithm 1 bins predictions by max eigenvalue, then runs hierarchical clustering (on the correlation of d(X)) within each bin to approximate conditioning, and collapses per-cluster target eigenvalues onto a single axis for plotting. Sensitivity analysis (Table 3) shows ECE is stable for C >= 5 clusters; defaults are B = 8, C = 5.

Experimental protocol. Datasets: TriviaQA, Natural Questions (random validation subsets). Models: Phi 4, Phi 4 Mini, Llama4 Maverick. Embedder: all-mpnet-base-v2 (reproduced with jina-embeddings-v5-text-nano in Appendix B.7). m = 20 test answers at sampling temperature 0.5; m = 100 on the n=300 dev subset for temperature fitting. Correctness is judged by ROUGE-L fuzzy matching against a temperature-0.1 reference. AUROC error bars via B=20 bootstrap.

Results & Highlights

  • Systematic overconfidence is universal. Across all 6 model×dataset settings the risk-optimal temperature is strictly greater than 1 (Figure 3), meaning every LLM places too much mass on its top latent semantic outcome. The motivating example: Phi 4 Mini on TriviaQA question "What links do Bollywood, Hollywood and Lollywood have?" yields max eigenvalue λmax = 0.81, reduced to λmax = 0.29 after temperature scaling with the optimal T = 2.51.
  • Entropy predicts risk exactly at the optimum (Theorem 2 verified). In every panel of Figure 3 the average-entropy curve touches the empirical matrix log-score risk at the risk-minimizing temperature, empirically confirming the entropy-risk equivalence. Figure 9 further shows risk R_S, matrix calibration error Cal_S, and the eigenvalue ECE are co-minimized at essentially the same temperature.
  • AUROC improves in most settings after calibration (Table 1). Eigenvalue AUROC on Natural Questions: Phi 4 0.742 -> 0.794 (+0.052), Phi 4 Mini 0.768 -> 0.778 (+0.010); on TriviaQA: Phi 4 0.814 -> 0.820, Phi 4 Mini 0.808 -> 0.814. Entropy AUROC for Phi 4 on Natural Questions rises 0.764 -> 0.795. (A few settings, e.g., Llama4 Maverick NQ entropy 0.668 -> 0.663, slightly worsen, since AUROC is not the optimization target.)
  • Spectral methods already beat SE/KLE baselines (Table 2, TriviaQA). Density-matrix eigenvalue/entropy AUROC (Table 1: 0.808-0.814 for Phi 4 / Phi 4 Mini) exceeds KLE (Phi 4 0.807, Phi 4 Mini 0.797) and Semantic Entropy (Phi 4 0.798, Phi 4 Mini 0.789), supporting the claim that these methods warrant a dedicated calibration theory.
  • Matrix temperature scaling outperforms the sampling-temperature baseline. For the Phi 4 Mini / TriviaQA setup, the Lamb et al. (2025) sampling-temperature calibration reduces eigenvalue ECE from 0.36 to 0.30, whereas matrix temperature scaling reaches 0.21 (Appendix B.6, Figure 10).
  • Algorithm 1 is stable and embedder-agnostic. Eigenvalue ECE is stable for C >= 5 clusters (Table 3, e.g., 0.277/0.188/0.220 at B=8 and C=3/5/7), and within-cluster pairwise similarity of density matrices (0.318-0.490) is an order of magnitude higher than within-bin similarity (0.033-0.064, Table 4). Reproducing all main results with jina-embeddings-v5-text-nano (Table 5, Figure 11) yields the same overconfidence-and-recalibration conclusions.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Genuinely novel as the first calibration framework targeted at density-matrix/eigenvalue predictors; the key conceptual move — recognizing that matrix calibration descends from canonical calibration but eigenvalue calibration does not (Theorem 1's strict inequality) — is non-obvious and forces the clustering-based Algorithm 1. That said, the machinery heavily recycles Gruber & Buettner (2022)'s proper-score/injective-transform template, so the engineering novelty beyond that template is the matrix temperature scaling and the reliability-diagram algorithm.
  • Rigor: Theoretical claims are backed by complete proofs (Appendix C) and the Theorem 2 prediction (entropy touches risk at the minimum) is cleanly confirmed in Figure 3. The calibration-error / risk / ECE co-minimization check (Figure 9) is a strong internal consistency test.
  • Reproducibility: Strong on paper — public code, named model/dataset/embedder versions, fixed protocol (m=20 test, m=100 dev, B=8, C=5, bootstrap B=20), plus an embedder-swap reproduction (jina-v5). Caveat: AUROC depends on a ROUGE-L fuzzy-correctness threshold that is only loosely specified, which materially affects the headline numbers.
  • Significance: High for the LLM-UQ subfield because state-of-the-art spectral UQ methods (Nikitin et al. 2024, Walha et al. 2026) report von Neumann entropy as a confidence number without any calibration guarantee; this paper fills that exact gap and shows the reported entropies systematically overstate confidence (T>1 everywhere).
  • Red flags / weak points:
  • Narrow evaluation breadth: only 2 QA datasets (TriviaQA, Natural Questions) and 3 models (Phi 4, Phi 4 Mini, Llama4 Maverick) — all short-form factual QA. No math/reasoning, code, long-form, or safety-critical tasks, so "current LLMs are systematically overconfident" is over-generalized from a thin slice.
  • Missing baselines: the only calibration baseline is the relatively weak Lamb et al. (2025) sampling-temperature method. There is no comparison against distribution-level calibrators (e.g., Dirichlet/calibration-on-the-simplex applied to eigenvalues, or ensemble variance scaling), so "temperature scaling is sufficient" is under-tested.
  • AUROC gains are inconsistent: calibration worsens AUROC in several cells (Llama4 Maverick NQ entropy 0.668 -> 0.663; jina reproductions show further regressions in Table 5), yet the paper asserts calibration "does not deteriorate any downstream tasks" — the evidence is more mixed than the wording.
  • Theorem 1's inequality is both the paper's insight and its practical handicap: it forces an approximate, clustering-based reliability diagram whose only validation is a coherence table (Table 4) and a sensitivity sweep over (B,C); there is no ground-truth check that the clustered target eigenvalues match the true conditional eigenvalues.
  • No human-impact / decision-utility evaluation: if the point of calibration is reliable downstream decision-making, an expected-utility or selective-prediction experiment would be more convincing than AUROC and ECE alone.

Strengths

  • First principled, formally defined notion of calibration for eigenvalue/density-matrix LLM-UQ, with the unexpected (and practically important) result that eigenvalue calibration provably does not follow from matrix calibration without conditioning on the full matrix.
  • Clean, mutually-reinforcing theory and experiments: Lemma 1 / Theorem 2 predict that entropy should equal risk at the calibrated optimum, and Figure 3 shows exactly this across all six settings.
  • Honest about the AUROC-Calibration disconnect: explicitly notes there is no theoretical link between calibration and AUROC, and reports both improving and worsening cells.
  • Practical, drop-in algorithm (Algorithm 1) with sensible defaults (B=8, C=5), a sensitivity analysis, and an embedder-swap reproduction that all hold up.
  • Public code and reproducible protocol; theory generalized beyond classification via proper matrix scores and Bregman matrix divergences.

Limitations

  • Evaluation is restricted to two short-form QA datasets and three models; the central claim of "systematic overconfidence in current LLMs" is not established beyond this slice.
  • Only one calibration baseline (sampling-temperature, Lamb et al. 2025) is compared; richer post-hoc calibrators and non-temperature parametric families are absent, leaving "temperature scaling is sufficient" under-substantiated.
  • The reliability-diagram method rests on a clustering approximation to conditioning on d(X), validated only indirectly via coherence (Table 4); there is no gold-standard conditional-eigenvalue comparison.
  • Fuzzy-correctness depends on a ROUGE-L threshold whose exact value is not pinned down, yet drives every AUROC number.
  • AUROC improvements are inconsistent across settings (and slightly negative for Llama4 Maverick entropy on NQ), tempering the downstream-benefit claims.
  • No selective-prediction, decision-utility, or human-in-the-loop evaluation to demonstrate that the lower ECE translates into better real-world use.

Relevance

直接命中「LLM 推理评估」与「ML 可靠性与数学基础」两个方向：本文为当前 SOTA 谱方法（基于语义嵌入特征值的 UQ）补上了缺失的校准理论基础，并对「LLM 隐藏状态/表征几何 + 数值可靠性」这一主题给出严格统计保证与过自信诊断手段，对评估模型输出的可信度、复现性与失败模式分析高度相关。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the systematic overconfidence (T>1) extend beyond short-form QA to reasoning (e.g., GSM8K, MATH), code generation, long-form summarization, or agentic tool-use settings where the latent semantic outcome space is much larger?
  • Can non-temperature injective transforms (e.g., matrix Dirichlet-style calibrators, or learned spectral maps) beat the single-parameter matrix temperature scaling, especially in the cells where TS slightly hurts AUROC?
  • How does calibration quality interact with the embedding dimension d and the embedding model choice — is the embedder-agnostic finding (Appendix B.7) robust at much higher embedding dimensions?
  • The estimator d_hat has a documented positive bias (Figure 6); does debiasing the density matrix estimator change the recovered optimal temperature or the entropy-risk equivalence empirically?
  • Can Theorem 1's clustering approximation in Algorithm 1 be validated against a synthetic ground-truth distribution where D_{Y|d(X)} is known exactly, to quantify how close the binned-and-clustered target eigenvalues are to the truth?
  • Does eigenvalue calibration translate into downstream utility gains (e.g., better selective prediction / abstention, or improved human-AI decision accuracy) rather than just lower ECE and comparable AUROC?
