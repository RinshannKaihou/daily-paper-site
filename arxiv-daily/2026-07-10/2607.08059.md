> **一句话总结：** 本文首次跨三个模型家族刻画了"思考模式"VLM（Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking / GLM-4.1V-9B-Thinking / InternVL3-8B）中答案熵的行为——发现思考链会把答案熵的幻觉检测 AUROC 从 0.899 压塌到 0.492（随机水平），而思考链自身的熵与链长则成为零额外推理成本的稳健幻觉预测信号（池化 AUROC 0.786），并提出一个基于链信号的弃答门，将 POPE 准确率从 71.0% 提升到 93.8%（62.7% 覆盖率）。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides the first three-family empirical characterization of answer-entropy behavior in thinking-mode VLMs, showing that generating a `<think>` chain collapses answer-entropy AUROC for hallucination detection on POPE adversarial from 0.899 (Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct) to 0.492 (Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking, chance), while per-token entropy inside the thinking chain (`Hthink`) and chain length (L) restore a strong signal (pooled AUROC 0.786 for `Hthink`, 0.776 for L vs. pooled answer-entropy CI [0.386, 0.478] entirely below chance). A zero-cost abstention gate built from these chain signals lifts POPE accuracy from 71.0% to 93.8% at 62.7% coverage, though the non-thinking Qwen control using answer entropy still reaches 96.7% at matched coverage.

## 核心贡献

- **首次三家族刻画答案熵崩溃现象**：在 POPE 对抗集上，Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking 的答案熵 AUROC 仅为 0.492（完全崩溃至随机），GLM-4.1V-9B-Thinking 为 0.716（未崩溃），InternVL3-8B 为 0.675（仅在 50% 样本上生成链，选择性思考）。证明崩溃是训练配方相关的，而非思考解码的固有属性。
- **思考链信号一致优于答案熵**：在链存在时，思考熵 `Hthink` 在 Qwen（0.647 vs 0.492）和 GLM（0.759 vs 0.716）上均优于答案熵；池化后 `Hthink` 达 AUROC 0.786（CI 下界 0.745），链长达 0.776，而池化答案熵 CI 完全低于随机水平。
- **揭示并诊断答案方向混淆**：无条件的答案熵在 POPE 上反向（AUROC 0.392，越自信越易幻觉），因为模型对肯定预测赋予更高置信度；通过限定 yes 预测子集可消除该混淆，作者强调这是诊断手段而非部署手段。
- **跨基准的结构化弃答机制**：弃答率在 POPE 上 22.1%、HallusionBench 上 12.6%，且系统性地偏向缺失物体查询（POPE 31.4% vs 12.8%），但两基准机制不同——POPE 上是过度推理未解决（199/221 涉及长链），HallusionBench 上是即刻链崩溃（118/120 无链）。
- **实用弃答门与跨任务迁移**：基于链信号的弃答门将 POPE 准确率从 71.0% 提升到 93.8%（62.7% 覆盖率），且思考熵比链长更可跨任务迁移（POPE 阈值直接套用到 HallusionBench 得 91.0% @ 45.5% 覆盖率）。
- **推广到开放式 VQA**：300 样本 VQAv2 试点显示链熵（0.680）优于答案熵（0.595），在自由形式答案上优势最大（0.733 vs 0.467），说明链信号优势并非二元任务结构假象。

## Key Contributions

- First three-family empirical characterization of answer-entropy behavior in thinking-mode VLMs, revealing three qualitatively distinct patterns: complete collapse (Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking, 0.492), no collapse (GLM-4.1V-9B-Thinking, 0.716), and selective thinking (InternVL3-8B, ~50% chain rate, 0.675 full / 0.602 thinking-only). These demonstrate collapse is training-recipe-dependent, not a universal property of thinking-mode decoding.
- Controlled ablation isolating the thinking mode as the causal driver: running Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking vs. Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct (same family, same vision encoder, only the thinking chain differs) on identical 1,000 POPE adversarial samples, answer-entropy AUROC drops from 0.899 to 0.492.
- Demonstration that thinking-chain signals (`Hthink` = mean per-token Shannon entropy over `<think>` tokens; L = chain length) consistently outperform answer entropy for the two families with sufficient statistical power, providing two zero-cost hallucination predictors extractable from a single greedy forward pass.
- Documentation of structured abstention replicating across benchmarks with consistent directional asymmetry but mechanistically distinct failure modes (over-deliberation on POPE vs. immediate chain collapse on HallusionBench).
- A practical abstention gate requiring no additional inference: refusing unclear samples and the top-20% longest-chain committed predictions raises POPE accuracy from 71.0% to 93.8% at 62.7% coverage.
- Generalization evidence: a 300-sample VQAv2 pilot confirms chain signals (0.680) beat answer entropy (0.595), with the largest gap on free-form answers (0.733 vs. 0.467), and thinking-entropy thresholds transfer across benchmarks better than chain-length thresholds.

## Methodology

The study compares four models on identical inputs: **Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking** (generates a `<think>...</think>` block under greedy decoding, `do_sample=False`), **Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct** (same family, non-thinking control), **GLM-4.1V-9B-Thinking** (THUDM/Zhipu, distinct `Glm4vForConditionalGeneration` architecture, `</think>` token id 151346), and **InternVL3-8B** (OpenGVLab/Shanghai AI Lab, `</think>` as a 3-token sequence activated via an R1-style system prompt, triggering chains on ~49.8% of samples). All run in bfloat16 on a single A100-SXM4-40 GB GPU.

**Benchmarks**: POPE adversarial (1,000 binary yes/no object-presence questions over COCO val2014, balanced labels); HallusionBench (951 visual questions, 178 text-only excluded as they engage no visual-grounding pathway, imbalanced 550 no / 401 yes); a 300-sample VQAv2 pilot stratified across answer types for open-ended generalization.

**Signals** are all free byproducts of a single greedy forward pass: per-token Shannon entropy `Ht = -Σ_v p_t(v) log p_t(v)` over the model vocabulary, aggregated as `Hthink` (mean of `Ht` over thinking tokens), `Hans` (mean over answer tokens), and `L` (number of thinking tokens). No extra forward passes, no trained probes.

**Evaluation**: conditioned AUROC (false-positive vs. true-positive within yes-predictions) to eliminate the answer-direction confound, plus unconditional AUROC for deployment realism. A six-group classification (TP, TN, FP, FN, unclear-chain, unclear-nochain) is used; HallusionBench samples unclear at 512 tokens are rerun at 1,024 tokens (112/231 resolved). Bootstrap 95% CIs use 10,000 resamples with `numpy.random.default_rng(42)`; effect sizes via Mann-Whitney U.

## Results & Highlights

- **Answer-entropy collapse (controlled ablation)**: On 1,000 identical POPE adversarial samples, answer-entropy AUROC for hallucination detection falls from **0.899** (Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct) to **0.492** (Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking, chance level) — a 0.407 AUROC drop attributable solely to the thinking mode. Same questions, images, and protocol.
- **Three-family comparison (Table 1)**: On POPE, answer-entropy AUROC is 0.492 for Qwen-Thinking, **0.716** for GLM-Thinking, and **0.675** (full) / **0.602** (thinking-only subset) for InternVL3; thinking-chain entropy (`Hthink`) reaches **0.647** (Qwen), **0.759** (GLM), and **0.608** (InternVL3 thinking-only).
- **Pooled conditioned AUROC (Table 3, 163 FPs / 1,951 samples)**: `Hthink` = **0.786** (95% CI [0.745, 0.825], |r| = 0.53), chain length = **0.776** (CI [0.735, 0.815], |r| = 0.42), while pooled answer entropy = **0.432** (CI [0.386, 0.478]) entirely below chance. Both chain signals significant at p < 0.001.
- **VQAv2 open-ended pilot (Table 5, n = 300)**: chain entropy **0.680** vs. answer entropy **0.595** pooled; on free-form "other" answers the gap is largest, **0.733 vs. 0.467** (answer entropy below chance).
- **Abstention gate (Table 4)**: On POPE, the thinking model's baseline accuracy is **71.0%** at 100% coverage; refusing unclear samples reaches **91.1%** at 77.9% coverage; adding refusal of the top-20% longest chains reaches **93.8%** at **62.7%** coverage. The instruct model using answer entropy reaches **96.7%** at 63.0% coverage — the chain gate closes but does not eliminate the gap.
- **Cross-task transfer**: The POPE top-20% thinking-entropy threshold applied to HallusionBench without recalibration achieves **91.0%** accuracy at 45.5% coverage, vs. 88.7% at 23.2% for chain length — practitioners needing cross-task transfer should prefer entropy thresholds.
- **Structured abstention**: Rates **22.1%** on POPE, **12.6%** on HallusionBench, with asymmetry toward absent-object queries (POPE: **31.4%** no vs. **12.8%** yes). On HallusionBench, FP chains are **83% longer** (322.6 vs. 176.2 tokens) and **47% more uncertain** than TP chains.
- **InternVL3 chain-absence signal**: FP rate is **13.1%** when InternVL3 skips thinking vs. **6.0%** when it generates a chain — chain absence itself carries epistemic signal for this model.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The central observation — that a thinking chain collapses answer-token entropy and destroys output-level uncertainty signals — is a genuine, clearly framed new result, and the controlled Qwen-Thinking vs. Qwen-Instruct ablation (same family/vision encoder, only the chain differs) is a clean causal isolation that prior VLM-uncertainty work (VL-Uncertainty, UMPIRE, HALP) could not make. The three-family taxonomy (complete collapse / no collapse / selective thinking) is a useful conceptual contribution. However, the core mechanism (conditioning on extra information reduces posterior variance — MacKay 1992) is theoretically unsurprising; the paper's value is empirical demonstration, not conceptual novelty.
- **Rigor**: Reasonably strong for a workshop paper. Bootstrap CIs with a fixed seed, an explicit six-group classification correction, effect sizes (Mann-Whitney |r|), and honest reporting of the InternVL3 thinking-only subset's low FP count (nFP = 17) being statistically unreliable are all commendable. The paper explicitly declines to over-claim where power is low.
- **Reproducibility**: Hardware, decoding, benchmarks, and seed are all specified, and everything uses publicly available models/data. The main weakness is that code is "released upon acceptance," so an independent reader cannot immediately reproduce the per-token entropy extraction or the six-group pipeline. The controlled ablation is the most reproducible result because it requires only two public checkpoints.
- **Significance**: Practically important for anyone deploying thinking-mode VLMs where uncertainty gating matters, since the paper shows answer entropy is unusable (AUROC 0.492) for exactly the model class gaining adoption. But the practical ceiling is tempered by the instruct-model comparison: the chain gate (93.8% @ 62.7%) still loses to the non-thinking Qwen using answer entropy (96.7% @ 63.0%), so the paper is honest that this is about making thinking-mode deployment tractable, not about beating instruct models.
- **Red flags**: (1) The abstention-rate asymmetry — Qwen-Thinking abstains on **22.1%** of POPE samples — is striking and understated; it means the headline 71.0% thinking-model accuracy is largely an abstention artifact (committed accuracy is 91.1%), which complicates any fair cross-model accuracy comparison. (2) The paper repeatedly frames chain signals as "zero-cost," which is true per-pass but the thinking model itself is far more expensive to run than the instruct model, so the cost framing is one-sided. (3) **Missing baseline**: there is no empirical comparison against any sampling-based uncertainty method (semantic entropy, self-consistency, UMPIRE) — acknowledged in Limitations, but it leaves the central claim ("chain signals are the recommended approach") under-supported relative to the alternatives. (4) Scope is narrow: only 8–9B models, only greedy decoding, and InternVL3 is characterized only on the headline Table 1 — the full analysis pipeline (conditioned AUROCs, epistemic hierarchy, abstention gate) covers only Qwen and GLM. The most actionable untested variable is whether collapse persists under stochastic sampling (temperature > 0), which the paper itself flags.

## Strengths

- Clean controlled ablation isolating the thinking mode as the causal driver of answer-entropy collapse (Qwen-Thinking vs. Qwen-Instruct, same family and vision encoder).
- Honest, well-calibrated statistics: bootstrap CIs, effect sizes, explicit non-claims where statistical power is low (InternVL3 nFP = 17), and a pooled answer-entropy CI shown to lie entirely below chance.
- All proposed signals (`Hthink`, L) are free byproducts of a single greedy forward pass — genuinely zero additional inference cost relative to the thinking model already running.
- The answer-direction confound diagnosis (why unconditional answer entropy inverts on POPE) is a genuinely useful methodological warning for anyone doing thinking-chain uncertainty analysis.
- Cross-family and cross-task generalization (VQAv2 open-ended, HallusionBench transfer) partially de-risks the single-model concern.

## Limitations

- **Model scope**: Only three thinking-mode VLM families, all at 8–9B scale; 32B/72B thinking-mode VLMs untested.
- **Decoding**: All results use greedy decoding; whether collapse persists under stochastic sampling (temperature > 0) is untested and is described as the single most actionable remaining experiment.
- **InternVL3 depth**: Characterized only on the headline Table 1 comparison; the full analysis pipeline (conditioned AUROCs, epistemic hierarchy, abstention gate) covers only Qwen and GLM.
- **Missing baselines**: No empirical comparison against sampling-based methods (semantic entropy / VL-Uncertainty, self-consistency, UMPIRE); the paper positions itself as a zero-cost single-pass signal but does not show it beats the multi-pass alternatives.
- **Primary benchmarks are binary yes/no**: Open-ended evaluation is a 300-sample VQAv2 pilot only; larger-scale open-ended evaluation is future work.
- **Confident-hallucination failure mode**: 11/34 FPs on POPE and 14/129 on HallusionBench produce chain entropy below the TP median (fast, low-entropy chains committing to a wrong answer) — chain signals cannot catch these.
- **Epistemic vs. aleatoric**: Chain entropy may partly reflect inherent image ambiguity rather than model ignorance; disentangling requires controlled image manipulations not performed here.
- **Code release deferred** to post-acceptance.

## Relevance

对研究"思考模式/推理大模型的置信度与不确定性量化、幻觉检测"的工作高度相关：本文直接揭示了一个反直觉且工程上重要的现象——显式推理链会摧毁答案层的不确定性信号，并指出应转向推理链本身的熵与长度作为零成本置信度代理。若你的兴趣在 LLM/VLM 可信推理、自我校准或 test-time scaling 的可靠性，这篇论文提供了可直接复现的信号提取协议和诊断方法（答案方向混淆检查），值得作为 baseline 与机理参考。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does answer-entropy collapse persist under stochastic sampling (temperature > 0), or does sampling prevent full pre-commitment and rehabilitate answer entropy for Qwen-style models — the paper's own most actionable open experiment?
- How do the collapse patterns and chain-signal AUROCs change at larger scale (32B, 72B thinking-mode VLMs), and do they generalize beyond the three families tested?
- Can sampling-based methods (semantic entropy, self-consistency over multiple chains) be combined with the single-pass chain signals to recover the ~11% "confident hallucinations" (fast, low-entropy wrong-committing chains) that chain signals provably miss?
- Is the abstention asymmetry toward absent-object queries a general property of thinking-mode decoding, or specific to the Qwen/GLM training recipes — and does it invert on benchmarks with opposite label structure?
- Does the answer-direction confound generalize to non-binary tasks beyond the VQAv2 pilot, and can a conditioning-free deployment signal match the conditioned AUROCs reported here?
- Could a lightweight trained probe on frozen pre-generation representations (à la HALP) complement chain entropy to cover the regime where greedy chains collapse silently?
