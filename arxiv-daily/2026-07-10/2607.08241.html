Closing the Null Space: Guidance-Aware Quantization for Classifier-Free Diffusion

arXiv: 2607.08241

Date: 2026-07-09

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08241

一句话总结： 本文揭示并形式化证明了 CFG 扩散模型量化中的"分支漂移陷阱"——仅对引导间隙（guidance gap）校准的 PTQ 目标存在精确零空间，会使无条件下支任意漂移、最终样本质量最差（FID 334）；并提出 GAMP，直接对引导预测校准、按引导输出敏感度分配激活位宽，在 CIFAR-10 上以 6 位平均激活精度取得 FID 39.4，比等精度均匀分配基线改善 49%。
TL;DR: The paper proves that gap-only PTQ calibration for classifier-free-guidance diffusion admits an exact "branch-drift" null space, empirically confirmed on CIFAR-10 where the best gap-fidelity config (Gap-only-W4A8, ρ=1.004, cos(Δ)=0.882) yields the worst FID (334.24 vs FP32's 13.67); the proposed GAMP calibrates directly on the guided prediction ε̃=ε∅+wΔ, fixing weights at W4 and allocating activation bits by guided-output sensitivity, reaching FID 39.40 at 6.03 avg bits — a 49% improvement over the uniform-spending linear baseline (predicted FID ≈76.5) — while also measuring a consistent 1.99× CFG latency tax on T4 and diagnosing a 147× INT8 slowdown in ONNX Runtime (no TensorRT).

核心贡献

  • CFG 延迟特征刻画（系统层面发现）： 在 NVIDIA T4 上实测发现，CFG 的双前向传递在每个去噪步带来约 1.99× 的延迟开销，该"CFG 税"被所有参数量与 BOPs 指标完全掩盖；同时诊断出在无 TensorRT 的 ONNX Runtime 中 INT8 推理比 FP16 慢约 147×（4598 ms vs 31.2 ms），根因是 192 个算子回退 memcpy 节点，而非算术能力不足。
  • 分支漂移陷阱的形式化证明与实证确认： 证明了仅以引导间隙 Lgap 为目标的 PTQ 校准存在精确零空间（Proposition 1）：任意平移 δ 同时加到条件/无条件分支上可使 Lgap=0，但无条件下支漂移 δ 不受约束，按 (1) 式传播后使引导预测误差 ε̃q−ε̃fp=δ（Proposition 2）；ρ=1 与 cos(Δ)=1 可与 ∥δ∥≫0 同时成立（Corollary 1）。在 CIFAR-10 上，间隙对齐最优的 Gap-only-W4A8（ρ=1.004, cos(Δ)=0.882）反而取得整个 sweep 中最差的 FID 334.24，构成对该零空间的"假阳性"实证。
  • 提出 GAMP（Guidance-Aware Mixed Precision）： 直接对引导预测 ε̃ 校准（LGAMP=∥ε̃q−ε̃fp∥²），按定义令 δ→0 从而在构造上关闭零空间；权重固定为 4-bit，激活位宽按各层对引导输出退化的敏感度经贪心背包分配（A4→A8）。
  • 部署指南： 为 HPEC 从业者给出三条可操作建议——报告 guided-step BOPs（而非单程 BOPs）、用引导预测误差而非间隙诊断校准 PTQ、在压缩区间落入"激活精度悬崖"时按 GAMP 敏感度分配激活位宽。

Key Contributions

  • CFG latency characterization (systems finding): Empirical measurement on an NVIDIA T4 GPU reveals a consistent ~1.99× guided-inference overhead (two forward passes per DDIM step) that is invisible to all parameter-count and BOPs metrics; a precise software-stack diagnosis attributes the INT8 failure in ONNX Runtime (no TensorRT) to a ~147× slowdown (4598 ms vs 31.2 ms for PyTorch FP16) caused by 192 operator-fallback memcpy nodes, not an arithmetic limitation.
  • Null-space theorem plus empirical confirmation of the branch-drift trap: Formally proves that gap-only PTQ calibration admits an exact null space (Proposition 1: εqy=εfpy+δ, εq∅=εfp∅+δ ⇒ Lgap=0 for any δ), that guided-prediction error equals δ under any such solution (Proposition 2), and that gap-fidelity metrics are therefore insufficient (Corollary 1). A controlled ablation on CIFAR-10 confirms it: the configuration with the best guidance-gap alignment (Gap-only-W4A8: ρ=1.004, cos(Δ)=0.882) produces the worst sample quality of the entire sweep (FID 334.24).
  • Guidance-Aware Mixed Precision (GAMP): A PTQ method that calibrates directly on the guided prediction ε̃=ε∅+wΔ, fixes all weights at W4, and allocates per-layer activation bits by guided-output sensitivity E(l)=∥ε̃ql−ε̃fpl∥² via a greedy knapsack, closing the null space by construction.
  • Deployment guidelines for HPEC practitioners: Report guided-step BOPs (2× single-pass), validate PTQ calibration with guided-prediction error rather than gap diagnostics alone, and allocate activation bits by GAMP sensitivity when the activation cliff falls within the compression regime.

Methodology

The analysis rests on the linear CFG combination ε̃ = ε∅ + w(εy − ε∅), w>1 (Eq. 1), evaluated over K=50 DDIM steps on the publicly released DASH student checkpoint — a 6.1M-parameter ADM UNet (base channels 64, attention at 16×16) trained on CIFAR-10 (32×32 RGB).

Null-space analysis. The most natural PTQ objective for a CFG model is the guidance-gap reconstruction error Lgap = ∥(εqy−εq∅) − (εfpy−εfp∅)∥² (Eq. 2). Proposition 1 shows that for any δ the assignment εqy=εfpy+δ, εq∅=εfp∅+δ achieves Lgap=0, leaving δ unconstrained. Proposition 2 then shows the guided-prediction error is exactly δ, independent of w and regardless of gap calibration quality; trajectory error accumulates as O(K∥δ∥). The trap is structural, not an optimization pathology: PTQ threshold optimization has only O(L) degrees of freedom and no gradient mechanism to suppress δ (unlike DASH's >200K-iteration distillation with an explicit unconditional branch loss).

GAMP. Because activations are far more precision-sensitive than weights in CFG models, GAMP fixes all weights at W4 and allocates activation bits per layer. Per-layer guided-output error E(l)=∥ε̃ql−ε̃fpl∥² (Eq. 4) serves as both the sensitivity signal and the calibration objective; E(l)=0 forces δ=0, closing the null space by construction. Algorithm 1 proceeds in five offline steps: (1) dual-branch calibration over 1024 CIFAR-10 images captures full activation ranges; (2) cache the FP32 guided prediction ε̃fp; (3) per-layer sensitivity s_l = E_l^{A4} − E_base over 49 additional forward passes (~5 min on T4); (4) greedy 0/1 knapsack ranked by s_l/ΔBOPs, promoting layers A4→A8 until the target average-bit budget b̄ is met; (5) calibrate thresholds via LGAMP over 800 Adam iterations. Total one-time overhead ≈22 min on T4; inference cost equals standard mixed-precision PTQ. Quantization uses per-output-channel symmetric signed weights and per-tensor asymmetric unsigned activations, with conv_in/conv_out and biases kept FP32.

Results & Highlights

  • Branch-drift trap confirmed (CIFAR-10, FID, 50k samples, DDIM K=50): Gap-only-W4A8 achieves the best guidance-gap fidelity of any quantized configuration — ρ=1.004 and cos(Δ)=0.882 — yet produces the worst FID of the entire sweep at 334.24 (IS 1.26), worse than even the much-coarser RTN-W4A4 (FID 272.78) and far worse than FP32 (FID 13.67). This is the literal false-positive predicted by Corollary 1: the calibration objective is satisfied near-perfectly while the guided prediction is severely corrupted.
  • GAMP Pareto efficiency (CIFAR-10, FID): GAMP-b̄6 reaches FID 39.40 (IS 7.42) at 6.03 average activation bits and 31.3 G guided-step BOPs. A linear reference interpolating the two measured uniform endpoints (MSE-W4A4: FID 122.36; MSE-W4A8: FID 30.62) predicts FID ≈76.5 at 6 average bits, so GAMP delivers a 49% FID improvement over the uniform-spending reference at matched average precision. GAMP-b̄5 reaches FID 40.01 at 28.3 G BOPs — near-W4A8 quality at 13% fewer BOPs than the 32.7 G uniform W4A8 counterpart (where the same linear reference would predict FID ≈99).
  • Activation precision cliff (CIFAR-10, FID): Reducing weight precision W8→W4 at fixed A8 raises FID by 20.1 (10.53→30.62 for MSE-cal), whereas reducing activation precision A8→A4 at fixed W4 raises FID by 91.8 (30.62→122.36) — a 4.6× steeper per-bit degradation, directly motivating GAMP's strategy of fixing weight bits and allocating activation bits non-uniformly.
  • CFG latency tax (NVIDIA T4, 50-step CFG-DDIM): Measured guided vs unguided latency shows a consistent ~1.99× tax across batch sizes {1, 16, 64} and both FP32 and FP16 (e.g., FP32 B=64: 3936.2 ms guided vs 1988.5 ms unguided = 1.98×; FP16 B=64: 3107.7 ms vs 1569.8 ms = 1.98×). This overhead is invisible to all BOPs and parameter-count metrics, so reporting single-pass metrics overstates realized deployment gain by a factor of two.
  • INT8 deployment gap (T4, ONNX Runtime 1.18, no TensorRT, B=64): W8A8 single-pass BOPs predict a 16× speedup over FP32, but ORT INT8 delivers single-pass latency of 4598 ms vs 31.2 ms for PyTorch FP16 — a ~147× slowdown. Root cause: absence of libnvinfer.so.10 forces 192 quantized operators through GPU↔CPU memcpy fallback nodes plus FP32 execution of UNet downsample Slice ops; the 3.8× on-disk size reduction (6.4 vs 24.5 MB) confirms weight quantization itself succeeds, isolating the failure to inference-stack support rather than arithmetic.

Authors & Credibility

The authors, Abdullah Al Shafi and Sumaiya Rahim Suma, are from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Khulna University of Engineering & Technology (Bangladesh). The first author is also the lead of the closely related DASH work (arXiv:2606.00798, 2026), whose publicly released 6.1M-parameter student checkpoints, ρ/cos(Δ) guidance-fidelity diagnostics, and training-time null-space analysis are the direct foundation this paper extends to the PTQ regime. The argument is presented with formal propositions and proofs, a controlled ablation designed specifically to falsify the theory, and honest acknowledgment of the single-seed ±2–3 FID variance. The systems measurements (T4, ORT 1.18, specific library-absence diagnosis) are concrete and reproducible by anyone with a Kaggle/colab T4 environment.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The branch-drift null-space analysis is a genuinely clean, non-trivial theoretical contribution. Propositions 1–2 plus Corollary 1 are short, correct, and the empirical confirmation is unambiguous (best ρ/cos(Δ) → worst FID 334.24). The CFG-tax and 147× INT8-software-stack findings are systems-level observations that, while not novel as individual facts (two-pass CFG cost is well known), are packaged here as a deliberate, citable measurement gap.
  • Rigor: The theory is sound and the ablation that constructs Gap-only-W4A8 as a deliberate strawman is exactly the right experiment to confirm the null space. However, the GAMP headline "49% FID improvement" is measured against a linear interpolation between two uniform-bit endpoints, not against a uniform mixed-precision baseline at avgA=6 — which the paper never actually runs. A directly comparable uniform-b̄6 MSE-cal config would be the more honest strong baseline and is conspicuously absent from Table III.
  • Absolute quality concern: In absolute terms GAMP-b̄6 (FID 39.40, 6.03 avg bits) is substantially worse than MSE-W4A8 (FID 30.62, 8 avg bits) at the same W4 weight precision. GAMP's pitch is Pareto efficiency at sub-8-bit activation budgets, but the absolute FID gap to the strongest uniform baseline is large, and the paper does not test whether a uniform b̄6 MSE-cal config would close much of it.
  • Reproducibility: Strong on the systems side (named hardware, library versions, specific missing .so). The model side is single-seed with ±2–3 FID variance acknowledged; the FID 10.53 reported for MSE-W8A8 is noted to marginally exceed FP32's 13.67 only due to single-seed noise and AdaRound regularization, a candid caveat.
  • Significance: The diagnostic and theoretical findings (CFG tax, INT8 stack gap, branch-drift trap) are more broadly transferable than GAMP itself. But all evaluation is confined to a single 6.1M-param CIFAR-10 model at 32×32; the paper explicitly flags that GAMP has not been validated on Stable Diffusion or other latent CFG models, and that the trap's predicted intensification at w∼7–15 (text-to-image guidance scales) remains untested.
  • Red flags: The Gap-only-W4A8 "baseline" that produces FID 334 is a deliberately pathological objective nobody would deploy; it functions as a falsification probe rather than a competitor, which the paper is mostly transparent about but which inflates the apparent contrast. The branch-drift argument also depends on the optimizer reaching a shared-drift solution — the paper asserts Lgap≈0 is consistent with a constant δ but does not directly instrument εq∅−εfp∅ to prove the mechanism (it names this as a natural extension).

Strengths

  • Formal, provable statement of the branch-drift trap with crisp propositions and a falsifying empirical confirmation (best gap fidelity coinciding with worst FID).
  • Concrete, reproducible systems findings: the 1.99× CFG tax across formats/batch sizes and the 147× ORT-INT8 slowdown with a root-caused 192-memcpy-node diagnosis.
  • GAMP's calibration objective LGAMP=∥ε̃q−ε̃fp∥² is mathematically the minimal fix — it removes the exact null space by construction rather than adding regularization.
  • Honest caveats throughout (single-seed variance, FP32-as-quality-ceiling, scope limited to CIFAR-10 32×32).
  • Clear, actionable deployment guidelines for practitioners (report guided-step BOPs; validate with guided-prediction error, not gap diagnostics; allocate bits by GAMP sensitivity).

Limitations

  • Evaluation is restricted to a single 6.1M-parameter ADM UNet on CIFAR-10 at 32×32; no validation on Stable Diffusion, DiT, or other latent CFG models, nor on higher-resolution or text-to-image settings.
  • The branch-drift trap's predicted intensification at production guidance scales w∼7–15 is untested (experiments use DASH's TAG w=4.0).
  • The headline 49% gain is relative to a linear interpolation baseline; a uniform mixed-precision b̄6 config is not run, leaving GAMP's advantage over the most natural uniform competitor implicitly argued rather than directly measured.
  • Hardware validation is confined to NVIDIA T4; newer accelerators (H100) with mature INT8 tensor-core support are flagged but not evaluated.
  • Single-seed protocol (acknowledged ±2–3 FID at 50k samples).
  • The shared-drift mechanism is inferred from Lgap≈0 rather than directly instrumented via εq∅−εfp∅.

Relevance

对关注扩散模型高效部署与量化的研究者高度相关：本文首次系统揭示了 CFG 推理的双分支结构如何破坏现有 PTQ 校准目标（分支漂移陷阱），并给出可证伪的理论与实证，对扩散模型压缩、推理加速与 CFG 机制理解都有直接参考价值。其系统侧发现（1.99× CFG 税、ORT-INT8 147× 回退）也对实际部署管线优化具有工程指导意义。
