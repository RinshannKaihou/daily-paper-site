> **一句话总结：** 本文指出 LLM 拒答（abstention）混淆了两种失败模式（答错可答题 vs. 误答不可答题），证明普通输出置信度只能读出"答案正确性"这一轴，而"问题可答性"这一轴需要隐藏态探针（SelfAware 上 0.97–0.99 vs 输出 0.54–0.67 AUROC），并据此提出双风险认证的分解式拒答策略，在 8B/14B 模型上获得唯一可同时满足两个错误预算的策略。

> **TL;DR:** The paper shows LLM abstention conflates two distinct failure axes — answer correctness (did the attempt succeed) and question answerability (should it have been attempted). On SelfAware across five models (2B–14B), trained output-confidence readouts stay at 0.54–0.67 AUROC for answerability while hidden-state probes reach 0.97–0.99; on CREPE's natural false presuppositions, output readouts collapse to chance (0.50–0.58) versus 0.69–0.78 internal. The authors formalize abstention as three-class selective acceptance (C/W/U) with separately certified per-axis risk budgets (αU=0.15, αW=0.50, δ=0.10), and the factorized two-threshold policy is the only one that certifies both budgets at 14B and reaches 0.75 coverage at 8B versus 0.31 for answer-confidence-only.

## 核心贡献

- **识别出拒答的两条独立轴：** 将每个问题标注为正确可答（C）、错误可答（W）、不可答（U）三态，证明普通答案置信度只能分离 C 与 W/U，而可答性轴（分离 W 与 U）需要另一信号；二者在 2B–14B、三个模型族上构成稳定的"交叉几何"（L 形）。
- **诚实的对照实验，限定已有结论的边界：** 量化了 SelfAware 上的表面混淆（词袋 logistic 即达 0.87 AUROC）、P(IK) 启发式（0.61–0.88，因模型而异），并在 CREPE（同分布真实用户问题）上去除该混淆——此时所有输出侧信号均低于内部探针。
- **"探针路由"的行为修复：** 直接指示模型检查前提会无差别质疑真/假前提（74% 假前提 vs 57% 合理问题），而用隐藏探针门控同一指令可使挑战精度提升约三倍（42% 假前提 vs 14% 误挑战）。
- **双风险认证的分解式拒答框架：** 用 Clopper–Pearson 精确二项界 + Bonferroni，在独立的认证/测试划分上分别对不可答率 R_U 与误答率 R_W 预算做认证，揭示了一种随规模变化的不对称性——U 预算几乎处处可认证，W 预算受限于模型精度。
- **可复现性：** 全部数字由提交的 score artifact 经确定性脚本（固定种子）重新生成，并由 CI 脚本断言；100 次重分割复制审计验证证书的发放率与违例率。

## Key Contributions

- **Decomposes abstention into two separable axes.** Labels each SelfAware item as correct-answerable (C), wrong-answerable (W), or unanswerable (U), and shows ordinary answer-confidence separates C from W/U but not W from U, while a separate answerability signal does the opposite — a stable crossed ("L-shaped") geometry replicated across Gemma 2 2B-it, Qwen2.5-3B/7B/14B, and Llama-3.1-8B.
- **Honest controls bounding the claim.** Quantifies the SelfAware surface confound (bag-of-words logistic 0.87 AUROC) and elicitation (P(IK) 0.61–0.88), then removes it on CREPE where both question classes are real user questions from one distribution — there every output-side signal falls below the internal readout.
- **Behavioral repair via probe-routed premise checking.** Shows instructed premise-checking fails (contests 74% false premises but 57% sound questions), while gating the same instruction with the out-of-sample hidden probe triples challenge precision.
- **Formalizes abstention as three-class selective acceptance with separately certified per-axis risk budgets.** Uses exact Clopper–Pearson bounds with Bonferroni over independent certification/test splits; exposes a scale-dependent asymmetry (U-budget certifies almost everywhere; W-budget bounded by model accuracy).
- **Verified reproducibility.** All numbers regenerate from committed artifacts via deterministic scripts asserted in continuous integration; a 100-resplit replication audit confirms issuance and violation rates.

## Methodology

The paper studies a single decision per question under two signals. **Setup:** On SelfAware (Yin et al., 2023), a balanced sample (150 answerable / 150 unanswerable; 120 each for Qwen2.5-3B) is scored once per question on five instruction-tuned models from three families (Gemma 2 2B-it, Qwen2.5-3B/7B/14B, Llama-3.1-8B), recording from one forward pass the output answer-confidence (mean token log-probability and first-token predictive entropy) and final prompt-token hidden states at three depths. Items are labeled C/W/U by alias-matching the greedy answer against gold aliases. 7B/8B models run 8-bit quantized and 14B 4-bit (validated against full precision at 3B).

**Symmetric readouts.** To avoid comparing a trained probe against an untrained scalar, the authors train equal-capacity L2-regularized logistic readouts for all four source-by-axis cells (output/hidden × correctness/answerability) on identical calibration/test partitions, with layer chosen on a nested calibration split. Percentile-bootstrap 95% intervals over test items are reported.

**Controls (Section 4).** (i) Surface confound: a hashed bag-of-words logistic on question text alone; (ii) Elicitation: P(IK) "can you correctly answer" and post-generation P(True); (iii) Beyond surface: matched-template nonce-country questions and transfer probes across surface-disjoint constructions.

**CREPE natural-data test (Section 5).** A balanced 500/500 sample of normal vs false-presupposition questions from the official test split under the same protocol, scored with a leak-free four-way split (probe-train, tune, certify, test) for certified gating.

**Probe-routed premise checking (Section 6).** A pre-registered pilot on Llama-3.1-8B (and replicated on Qwen2.5-7B): 120 false-premise + 120 sound CREPE questions from the non-probe-train half, comparing standard vs challenge prompts, with random-gating and always-challenge controls.

**Factorized abstention (Section 7).** A policy π emits iff sA ≥ τA and sC ≥ τC (the high-answerability, high-confidence corner). Define R_U = P[emit|U], R_W = P[emit|W], Cov_C = P[emit|C]; maximize Cov_C subject to UCB(R_U) ≤ αU and UCB(R_W) ≤ αW, with thresholds frozen on a tuning split and certified via Clopper–Pearson at 1−δ/2 per risk (Bonferroni joint 1−δ=0.90). A held-out test split gives empirical rates; readout scores are trained on the tuning split only, so certification/test items are out-of-sample for both scores and thresholds. Calibration is standard split-conformal risk control (Learn-Then-Test, Angelopoulos et al., 2025).

**Replication audit (Appendix D).** The full pipeline (split, probe training, tuning, certification, held-out test) is re-run over 100 random seeds per model.

**Matched factual-relation gates (Section 8).** Three matched relations (capitals, official languages, currencies) read by two model families, using a Gemma Scope SAE feature battery for Gemma 2 2B-it and a hidden probe for Qwen2.5-3B, with group-level Clopper–Pearson selective-risk certificates and an a-priori prominence proxy.

## Results & Highlights

- **Output answer-confidence cannot read answerability; hidden state can.** On **SelfAware** (Table 1), trained output→answerability readouts stay at **0.54–0.67 AUROC** across all five models (no scale trend), while **hidden→answerability readouts reach 0.97–0.99** — a gap of roughly +0.40 that never closes. The hidden→correctness readout grows with scale (0.64 at 2B up to 0.88 at 8B).
- **On CREPE's naturally occurring false presuppositions, the output blind spot is at or near chance.** Trained output readouts of answer-confidence detect false premises at **AUROC 0.50–0.58** (raw confidence 0.49–0.58), versus internal hidden readouts at **0.69–0.73** and difference-of-means directions at **0.74–0.78**; every elicitation tested (P(IK), P(True), premise-check) falls below every within-CREPE internal readout (Table 2). The asymmetry internal-minus-output is +0.15 to +0.21 on natural data.
- **The C–W collapse is not a label artifact.** Training the answerability direction on C-versus-U items only (no W items in training) still places held-out W items with C, not U, on every model (**W-versus-U AUROC 0.97–0.98**; W sits 0.71–0.85 of the way from U mean to C mean; Table 8).
- **Probe-routed premise checking triples challenge precision.** On **CREPE** (Llama-3.1-8B), always-challenge contests 74% false premises but 57% sound questions; probe-gated policy contests **42% false premises at 14% false-challenge** (Qwen2.5-7B: 41% vs 16%), against random gating at the same 35% budget (26% / 20%) (Table 3).
- **Factorized policy is the only one certifying at 14B and beats single-axis at 8B.** At budgets αU=0.15, αW=0.50, δ=0.10 (Table 4), on Llama-3.1-8B the factorized policy certifies both budgets at **0.75 C-coverage versus 0.31** for answer-confidence-only; at 14B it is the **only policy that certifies at all (0.47)**. The learned two-signal combiner reaches higher coverage where it certifies (0.81 at 8B) but cannot provide per-axis risk attribution and fails at 14B.
- **Replication audit supports the protocol.** Over 100 resplits (Table 12), the unanswerable budget is never violated on held-out test in any replicate; the wrong-answer budget is violated in 0–5.6% of issued certificates; certificates issue in 18/100 (Gemma 2B) to 68/100 (Llama-3.1-8B).
- **Matched factual-relation gates corroborate the surface-controlled result.** Internal answerability readers beat output-only uncertainty on unknown detection (capitals: 0.978 internal vs 0.552 output on Gemma; Table 5), and pooling two relations certifies a full true/false/unknown decision at group-level selective-risk UCB **0.074** (one error group in 51), below a 10% target.

## Authors & Credibility

Single author: **Benedikt J. Wagner**, City St George's, University of London. The author is careful and unusually self-critical about novelty — explicitly crediting Slobodkin et al. (2023) for hidden-state answerability, Lavi et al. (2026) for linear unanswerability directions, Chen et al. (2026) for query-level uncertainty, and Kadavath et al. (2022) for P(True)/P(IK), and stating precisely what is and is not claimed. The methodology demonstrates strong experimental hygiene: pre-registered pilot with hypotheses and kill-criteria fixed before running, leak-free four-way splits, exact binomial certification bounds, a 100-resplit replication audit, continuous-integration verification of every number from committed artifacts, and an explicit honesty note on certification statistics (marginal statements, post-hoc selection would void guarantees, noisy tuned thresholds at small split sizes). The work runs on a single Apple M4 laptop (24 GB), limiting scale but ensuring full reproducibility.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely incremental rather than foundational. The author is explicit that hidden states encoding answerability (Slobodkin et al. 2023), linear answerability directions (Lavi et al. 2026), and query-level uncertainty (Chen et al. 2026) are prior. The real contribution is the *same-decision decomposition* into two axes, the controlled geometry under honest baselines, and the certified dual-risk policy. This is a meaningful conceptual and operational advance but rests on existing primitives.
- **Rigor:** High. Symmetric (equal-capacity trained) readouts, bootstrap CIs, a label-artifact control (training on C-vs-U only, excluding W), a surface confound quantified at 0.87 bag-of-words, a natural-data test (CREPE) that removes the confound by construction, a pre-registered pilot, exact Clopper–Pearson bounds with Bonferroni, and a 100-resplit replication audit. The honesty about certification fragility (zero-count bounds can be optimistic; test coverage is not monotone in budget) is commendable.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong. Fixed seeds, committed score artifacts, deterministic analysis scripts, CI verification, and a stated intent to release all code/data/loaders. Quantization validated against full precision. The main external barrier is the small split sizes for certification.
- **Significance:** Practically relevant for any deployment needing *separate* per-risk guarantees (e.g., "unanswerable-answer rate ≤ x" distinct from "wrong-answer rate ≤ y"), which fused-scalar policies cannot provide. The behavioral repair (probe-routed premise checking) is a concrete, deployable idea.
- **Red flags:**
  - **Scale ceiling is low.** All models are 2B–14B; the hidden correctness readout already grows sharply 3B→8B, so the crossed geometry could weaken or vanish at 70B+ — the author concedes this but it materially limits the generality claim.
  - **SelfAware is heavily surface-confounded** (bag-of-words 0.87), so the headline 0.97–0.99 hidden AUROC overstates clean internal legibility; the defensible claim survives only on CREPE where it drops to ~0.70.
  - **Certification split sizes are tiny** (18–22 W items), so wrong-answer budgets tighter than αW=0.5 cannot certify at any scale — the dual-risk certificate's W side is statistically underpowered and the "best certified coverage" numbers rest on few items (e.g., nC=8 on Gemma 2B).
  - **No causal intervention** on the answerability direction (unlike Slobodkin et al., Lavi et al.), so the evidence is observational-plus-policy, not mechanistic.
  - **Correctness judged by alias matching** on a small number of correct answers (SelfAware accuracy 0.28–0.42); richer correctness readers and human audit would strengthen the C/W split.
  - **Single-pass only** — sampling-based semantic entropy is explicitly excluded from the baseline set under this protocol, leaving open whether a stronger multi-sample baseline narrows the gap.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clarifying: cleanly separates two failure modes that single-threshold abstention conflates, with a memorable geometric ("L-shape") framing.
- Exceptional experimental honesty: surface confound, elicitation recovery, and label-artifact controls are all run and reported rather than hidden.
- Operationally useful: the factorized policy and dual-risk certificate give deployment-ready, separately-attestable risk budgets.
- The behavioral repair (probe-routed premise checking) turns a measurement finding into a concrete fix that roughly triples challenge precision.
- Strong reproducibility posture: CI-asserted numbers, fixed seeds, 100-resplit audit.

## Limitations

- Small models only (2B–14B); pattern unverified at frontier scale where self-assessment may improve.
- SelfAware answerability is largely surface-recoverable (0.87 bag-of-words) and model-dependently elicitation-recoverable.
- CREPE contributes only the admissibility axis (long-form ELI5 answers admit no alias-based correctness label); its presupposition annotations carry inherent ambiguity.
- Certification W-splits have only 18–22 items, capping certifiable αW at 0.5.
- No causal intervention on the answerability direction.
- Single-pass baselines; no semantic-entropy evaluation.
- English data only.
- Correctness via alias matching on few correct answers.

## Relevance

对关注 LLM 可靠性、拒答（abstention）与幻觉控制的研究者高度相关：它把"答错"和"误答不可答题"显式分解为两条独立轴并提供可认证的双风险预算，对需要分别报告"不可答率"与"误答率"的部署场景（如安全敏感问答、检索增强系统）有直接方法学价值。其隐藏态探针路由前提检查的思路也可启发基于内部信号的路由与自我修正机制设计。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the crossed C/W/U geometry persist (or invert) at 70B+ and frontier models where explicit self-assessment is stronger?
- Would sampling-based semantic entropy, run as an additional baseline, narrow the output→answerability gap on CREPE, or is the confident-confabulation regime genuinely opaque to multi-sample signals?
- Can the answerability direction be intervened on causally (à la Slobodkin/Lavi) to *produce* the behavioral repair, rather than just gate a prompt with it?
- How do the dual-risk certificates behave with substantially larger certification splits (e.g., 200+ W items) — do tighter αW budgets become certifiable, and does the factorized policy still dominate the fused combiner?
- Does the same two-axis decomposition apply to retrieval-augmented settings where "unanswerable" means "not supported by retrieved context" rather than "no determinate answer"?
- Can richer logical compositions over named, grounded predicates (the neuro-symbolic direction the paper gestures at) extend the repair payoff from abstention to general behavior routing?
