Can We Trust LLM's Logic? Quantifying Uncertainty, Coherence, and Robustness via a Graph-Based Framework

arXiv: 2607.08017

Date: 2026-07-10

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08017

一句话总结： 本文提出基于图的推理评估框架 GRAPH EVAL，将 LLM 的多条 CoT 解构为因果 DAG 并通过语义-结构图编辑距离（SS-GED）量化推理不确定性；其新指标 GRCS 在 15 个模型-数据集组合中有 14 个与推理忠实度呈负相关，新解码策略 GSC 通过选中位推理路径剔除了 SC 中虚高的"幸运猜测"，并用对抗性中位消融证明该路径是"承重"路径。
TL;DR: GRAPH EVAL reframes LLM uncertainty quantification as a reasoning-fidelity problem by converting sampled Chain-of-Thought (CoT) traces into causal DAGs and measuring pairwise Semantic-Structural Graph Edit Distance (SS-GED). Its metric GRCS is the only one negatively correlated with faithfulness in 14/15 (93.3%) model-dataset configurations across GSM8K, BoolQ, StrategyQA, MedQA, and GPQA, and its Graph Self-Consistency (GSC) decoder raises faithful-win share to 79.5% vs. 57.6% for standard Self-Consistency (+21.9 pp), while exposing that SC's accuracy on small models (e.g., Llama 3.1 8B) is inflated by as much as -9.0 pp on MedQA of "lucky-guess" gains.

核心贡献

  • 新指标 GRCS（Graph Reasoning Coherence Score）： 基于改进的语义-结构图编辑距离（SS-GED）对采样到的 N 条 CoT 推理图进行成对距离聚合，得到一个分布型不确定性度量；GRCS 能同时惩罚"模式坍塌"（反复重复相同错误捷径）与"自信幻觉"（依赖捏造事实），是所评测指标中唯一在 14/15 个配置上与推理忠实度稳定负相关的指标。
  • 新解码策略 GSC（Graph Self-Consistency）： 用 medoid（最居中、最被同伴图支持的 CoT）替代 SC 的多数投票答案，把"结构中心性 + 路径共识"作为选择依据；在小模型上牺牲少量标称精度，换来更高的推理忠实度，而在强模型（DeepSeek R1）上保持或提升精度。
  • 对抗性 Medoid 消融实验： 设计"提示投毒"攻击，显式禁止模型重用 GSC 选中的 medoid 推理步骤，迫使其生成替代路径；结果证明 medoid 是"承重路径"，在小模型上逼离它会让"精度提升"系统性伴随"忠实度下降"，实证揭穿了"幸运猜测"现象。
  • 系统性的图空间不确定性基准： 把 dispersion、entropy、CoTA、Topo-UQ 统一放进 SS-GED 拓扑空间对比，并通过 Early Answering / Counterfactual Editing 两种忠实度定义、1000 次 bootstrap 估计相关性，给出可复现的对比协议。

Key Contributions

  • A new distributional UQ metric, GRCS, built on top of a Semantic-Structural Graph Edit Distance (SS-GED) that augments classical GED with fair normalization, Hungarian bipartite matching, terminal-node semantic blending, and an RBF kernel; GRCS aggregates the mean pairwise distance across the reasoning manifold and is the only evaluated metric that stays negatively correlated with faithfulness in 14 of 15 ⟨GLLM, dataset⟩ configurations (93.3%), winning outright in 8/15.
  • Graph Self-Consistency (GSC) decoding, a medoid-based decoder that selects the most structurally central and peer-supported CoT rather than running majority vote on final answers; it strips out "lucky guesses" and acts as a decoding filter for faithful accuracy.
  • Adversarial medoid ablation, a prompt-poisoning protocol that forbids reuse of the medoid path; empirically the medoid behaves as a "load-bearing path" — for Llama 3.1 8B and Phi-4 the fitted least-squares slope between ∆Accuracy and ∆Faithfulness is significantly negative (p=0.011 and p=0.010), directly catching lucky guesses, while DeepSeek R1's broader high-entropy topology degrades faithfulness without losing accuracy.
  • A unified, topological comparison protocol that places dispersion, entropy, CoTA, and Topo-UQ inside the same SS-GED distance space and grades each metric against two independent faithfulness proxies (Early Answering and Counterfactual Editing) with 1,000 bootstrap resamples, enabling apples-to-apples UQ evaluation across model scales.

Methodology

GRAPH EVAL is a five-stage pipeline that turns raw sampled text into analyzable reasoning topology.

  1. Causal fact decomposition and DAG construction. For each question q, a Generator LLM (GLLM) samples N=20 CoTs at T=0.7, each forced to declare its causal antecedents (e.g., [depends_on: 0, 1]) and to contain 3-6 declarative steps. A separate Decomposer LLM (DLLM = GPT-4o, T=0.0) — chosen to avoid self-evaluation inductive bias — rewrites each step into Subject-Verb-Object atomic facts while preserving causal edges. Each CoT becomes a DAG whose nodes are embedded atomic facts (via Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B) and whose edges encode causal dependencies.
  1. Semantic-Structural Graph Edit Distance (SS-GED). Pairwise distances over all (N choose 2) DAG pairs are computed using the Hungarian algorithm (O(m³) instead of NP-hard A GED). Two innovations over vanilla topological GED: (a) Fair Normalization scales edit cost by average graph size, and (b) Semantic-Structural Blending* combines structural similarity (RBF kernel, γ=5.0) with terminal-node conclusion similarity (cosine), weighted by β=0.4. Final distance d(Gi,Gj) = 1 − S(Gi,Gj).
  1. Graph Reasoning Coherence Score (GRCS). Aggregates the SS-GED distance matrix into a single UQ score: GRCS = 1 / (1 + µ(D(q))), where µ is the mean pairwise distance. Low GRCS signals a fragmented, hallucinated, or mode-collapsed reasoning manifold.
  1. Consensus context synthesis / GSC decoding. For each graph Gi the pipeline computes structural centrality C(Gi) (sum of pairwise similarities) and a graph-level consensus score P(Gi) (cross-graph support, with terminal nodes weighted 2×). The medoid is z* = argmax [ α·(max C(Gj)) + (1−α)·P(Gi) ] with α=0.8, prioritizing centrality while letting path consensus veto weakly supported central nodes.
  1. Faithfulness evaluation via adversarial medoid ablation. The medoid path z*_q is presented back to the GLLM in full while the prompt explicitly forbids reusing any of its steps or conclusions (a prompt-poisoning attack inspired by Zou et al. 2023). Alternative CoTs are scored on ablated accuracy, ablated faithfulness (via Early Answering), and medoid distance.

Faithfulness ground truth comes from Early Answering (iteratively feeding medoid facts to the GLLM; faithful reasoning requires the full CoT, unfaithful produces the answer prematurely). Metric quality is measured by Pearson / Spearman / Kendall correlation against faithfulness across 1,000 bootstrap resamples per dataset (20 questions × 10 responses). Evaluated models span three regimes: Llama 3.1 8B (general-purpose), Phi-4 14B (reasoning-specialized), and DeepSeek R1-Distill-Llama-70B (RL-distilled); benchmarks are GSM8K, BoolQ, StrategyQA, MedQA USMLE (100 sampled items, seed 42), and GPQA Diamond.

Results & Highlights

  • GRCS is the most reliable UQ metric. Across 15 ⟨GLLM, dataset⟩ configurations, GRCS yields the strongest negative Pearson correlation with faithfulness in 8 of them and stays negative in 14/15 (93.3%). By contrast, dispersion and entropy sometimes produce positive correlations (e.g., on GSM8K/BoolQ for Llama 3.1 8B and on GSM8K/StrategyQA for DeepSeek R1), Topo-UQ flips positive on Phi-4 for all tasks and on Llama 3.1 8B for StrategyQA/MedQA/GPQA, and CoTA degrades to positive correlation on GPQA for both Llama 3.1 8B and DeepSeek R1 despite reaching -0.40 on DeepSeek R1/GSM8K.
  • GSC exposes SC's inflated accuracy on small models. On Llama 3.1 8B, GSC vs. SC accuracy deltas are sharply negative: −7.6 pp on GSM8K (88.9% → 81.3%), −9.0 pp on MedQA (61.0% → 52.0%), −5.1 pp on GPQA (24.4% → 19.3%), −3.5 pp on StrategyQA, and −1.0 pp on BoolQ — all statistically significant (p < 0.05, McNemar's test). These drops quantify how much SC accuracy rests on unfaithful "lucky guesses."
  • GSC preserves or improves accuracy on capable models. On DeepSeek R1, GSC improves accuracy on every dataset: +3.1 pp on GSM8K (93.8% → 96.9%), +1.0 pp on BoolQ, +0.4 pp on StrategyQA, +1.0 pp on MedQA (90.9% → 91.9%), +1.1 pp on GPQA (52.4% → 53.4%) — these deltas are not statistically significant, confirming GSC adds fidelity without hurting strong models. Phi-4 sits in between (e.g., +2.5 pp on BoolQ, +2.0 pp on MedQA, but −3.0 pp on GSM8K, −2.0 pp on GPQA).
  • Disagreement audit shows GSC picks the more faithful answer. Averaged across disagreement cells, GSC achieves 79.5% faithful wins vs. 57.6% for SC (+21.9 pp, p < 0.05, Wilcoxon signed-rank test), with strong annotator agreement (Cohen's κ = 0.913 between the human reviewer and Gemini 3 Pro LLM judge). Extreme cases: on Llama 3.1 8B/MedQA only 40% of SC-only wins are faithful vs. 100% of GSC-only wins, and on Llama 3.1 8B/GPQA every SC-only win is spurious while GSC recovers 25% faithful wins.
  • Adversarial medoid ablation confirms the "load-bearing path" hypothesis. On StrategyQA, 43.2% (Llama 3.1 8B), 55.0% (Phi-4), and 15.2% (DeepSeek R1) of ablated points drop in faithfulness. The least-squares slope between ∆Accuracy and ∆Faithfulness is significantly negative for Llama 3.1 8B (r=−0.19, p=0.011) and Phi-4 (r=−0.18, p=0.010), directly capturing the lucky-guess phenomenon; DeepSeek R1 shows no significant linear association (r=−0.01, p=0.856) but still loses faithfulness on 15.2% of points.

Authors & Credibility

The paper is authored by Riccardo Revalor, Jalees Rehman, and Debjit Pal at the University of Illinois Chicago, spanning the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering and the Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Genetics — the cross-disciplinary (clinical/biomedical + ML) authorship plausibly motivates the inclusion of MedQA USMLE as a safety-critical benchmark. It is submitted as a conference paper to COLM 2026 (Under review). The methodology is built on well-cited prior work (Self-Consistency, CoTA, Topo-UQ, Graph of Thoughts, Semantic Entropy) and uses only open-source generator models for transparency, reserving a closed DLLM (GPT-4o) for the decomposition step that the authors explicitly ablate in Appendix C.4. Statistical rigor is above average for the subfield: McNemar's test, Wilcoxon signed-rank test, Cohen's κ, and 1,000-bootstrap correlation estimates are reported rather than bare point numbers.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty is moderate but real: SS-GED is a sensible, well-motivated extension of topological GED (Topo-UQ) — the addition of fair normalization, terminal-node semantic blending, and Hungarian matching is technically clean — but the underlying "map CoTs to a graph and measure dispersion" idea is already established by Topo-UQ and CoTA, so the contribution is more of a careful refinement than a new paradigm.
  • Rigor is a strength: the authors disclose that GRCS's statistical strength diminishes on the largest model (DeepSeek R1), openly admit that dispersion/entropy "mistake repetitive but flawed reasoning for confidence," and run a DLLM ablation (Appendix C.4), an α sensitivity analysis (C.6), and SS-GED hyperparameter sensitivity (C.5). The faithfulness ground truth rests on Early Answering, which itself depends on the same generator model and can be gamed by models that answer eagerly; the Counterfactual-Editing cross-check in C.3 is mentioned but not woven into the main tables.
  • Reproducibility is good in principle (open GLLMs, fixed seed 42 for MedQA, disclosed hyperparameters N=20, T=0.7, α=0.8, β=0.4, γ=5.0, [n_l, n_h]=[3,6]) but depends on GPT-4o as the DLLM, a closed, version-drifting model whose decomposition behavior is not pinned to a snapshot; using Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B is a reasonable architectural-independence choice but the 0.9 cosine threshold for cross-graph support is a hard, under-justified cutoff.
  • Significance is strongest in the small/medium-model regime the authors target: the −9.0 pp MedQA accuracy drop on Llama 3.1 8B and the 100% spurious SC-only wins on GPQA are concrete evidence that SC over-reports trustworthy performance — a genuinely useful finding for safety-critical deployment. The significance claim weakens for frontier models where GRCS correlations flatten and GSC's accuracy advantage is not statistically significant.
  • Red flags / gaps: (1) the headline "8/15 strongest, 14/15 negative" GRCS claim is the count of configurations, not an aggregate test, and the paper does not report a single omnibus significance test across all 15 cells — a per-config win-count is vulnerable to noise; (2) MedQA is sampled at only 100 questions (seed 42), so the −9.0 pp headline number has a wide confidence interval that is not reported; (3) the adversarial ablation's negative slope for Llama (r=−0.19) and Phi-4 (r=−0.18) is statistically significant but the effect size is weak, so the "load-bearing path" framing is stronger than the data strictly supports; (4) GSC is benchmarked only against SC — there is no comparison against other faithfulness-aware decoders such as universal self-consistency, reasoning-path verifiers, or reward-model reranking, leaving the "improves upon naïve majority voting" claim narrower than it sounds.

Strengths

  • Reframes UQ as reasoning fidelity rather than final-answer agreement, which is the right framing for mission-critical use.
  • SS-GED's combination of structural and semantic signals is principled and addresses concrete failure modes (mode collapse, confident hallucination, lucky guesses).
  • Honest reporting of where the method helps (small/mid models) and where it plateaus (DeepSeek R1), instead of cherry-picking the best cells.
  • Strong evaluation protocol: two faithfulness proxies, three correlation coefficients, bootstrap CIs, statistical significance tests for accuracy and disagreement audits, and dual human + LLM-judge annotation with reported κ.
  • The adversarial medoid ablation is a creative, falsifiable test of the medoid's causal importance rather than a purely observational result.

Limitations

  • Compute cost. Time complexity is O(N²·m³) per question vs. O(N) for SC; while it matches Topo-UQ, it is a real overhead at scale.
  • Weakens on large/capable models. GRCS correlations flatten and adversarial-ablation slopes become non-significant for DeepSeek R1, so the framework's value concentrates on smaller GLLMs.
  • Short-horizon only. CoTs are capped at n_h = 6 steps, so the paper explicitly does not address long-context, multi-step agentic, or highly compositional reasoning; scalability to those regimes is left open.
  • DLLM dependence on a closed model. GPT-4o as the decomposer is a reproducibility liability; the authors acknowledge this and provide a DLLM ablation (C.4) but the main numbers use the closed model.
  • Small MedQA sample (n=100). Confidence intervals for the MedQA deltas are not reported and could be wide.
  • Narrow decoder comparison. GSC is compared only to vanilla SC, not to other faithfulness-aware or verifier-based decoding methods.

Relevance

这项工作直接服务于"如何判断 LLM 推理是否可信"这一核心问题，对关注 LLM 推理忠实度评估、不确定性量化与推理路径分析的研究具有较高参考价值，尤其是在需要区分"答对但推理错"的安全关键场景（如医学、法律推理）下；其将 CoT 建模为因果 DAG 并用图编辑距离度量推理流形的思路，可作为下游推理可解释性、过程奖励模型评估与对抗鲁棒性研究的方法基础。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Would GRCS remain the most reliable UQ metric if the faithfulness ground truth were replaced by a human-authored reference reasoning, rather than the self-consistent Early Answering proxy that depends on the same GLLM?
  • How does GSC compare against stronger faithfulness-aware decoders such as universal self-consistency, process-reward-model reranking, or verifier-guided beam search — not only vanilla SC?
  • Does the "load-bearing medoid" finding generalize to long-horizon, multi-step agentic reasoning (n_h >> 6) where alternative paths may be genuinely plentiful rather than artificially suppressed?
  • Can SS-GED and GRCS be made to retain statistical power on frontier/large models, where the authors concede the signal flattens — for example by weighting edges by causal effect rather than topology alone?
  • How sensitive are the headline deltas (e.g., −9.0 pp on MedQA) to the 100-question MedQA sample, and do they survive a full-dataset or cross-seed evaluation with reported confidence intervals?
  • Is the 0.9 cosine-similarity threshold for cross-graph support and the α=0.8 / β=0.4 weights robust across model families other than Llama/Phi/DeepSeek?
