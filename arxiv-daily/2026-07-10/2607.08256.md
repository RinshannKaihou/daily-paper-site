> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示零样本 TTS 中 Best-of-N (BoN) 推理的验证器排序结果严重依赖于评估所用的 ASR 家族——同家族配对可多恢复 2-3 倍的 oracle 空间，作者据此提出跨家族排序集成方法，在三个独立评估器上达到 1.61% 的最低平均 WER（相对 F5-TTS 降低 12%）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper exposes an evaluation confound in Best-of-N (BoN) zero-shot TTS: a verifier's apparent quality flips depending on which ASR family scores it. On LibriSpeech-PC test-clean with F5-TTS, same-family verifier-evaluator pairs recover 2-3× more oracle headroom than cross-family pairs, so cross-family rank ensembles (rank-avg, max-rank) achieve the most robust results, reaching 1.61% mean WER at N=10 (−12% vs. F5-TTS's 2.06%), while the best single verifier drives WER to 1.72% (−16.5%) under the official F5-TTS evaluator.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示了 BoN 评估的根本性混淆因素**：同一批生成音频，不同 ASR 家族的评估器会给出相反的验证器排序——在 Whisper 评估器下最优的 distil-v3，在 wav2vec 2.0 评估器下会输给 w2v2-base，两者互不兼容。
- **量化了混淆的量级**：(验证器, 评估器) 家族配对对 WER 的影响远大于验证器 checkpoint 之间的选择；同家族配对在 N=3 时恢复 26.0% 的 oracle 空间，而跨家族配对仅恢复 7.9%（3.3 倍差距）。
- **排除了表示相似性作为主因**：通过线性 CKA 分析，发现高 CKA（0.978）的同家族对反而反相关（r=−0.52），低 CKA（0.55）的跨家族对却近乎完美一致（r=+0.94），说明混淆源于身份/谱系耦合，是 LLM-as-judge 自偏见的语音版。
- **提出跨家族排序集成**：rank-avg（平均排名）和 max-rank（最坏排名）在三个独立评估器上同时降低 WER，是跨评估器最稳健的配置。
- **提出评估实践建议**：默认在至少两个训练谱系不重叠的 ASR 家族下报告 WER（跨评估器三角验证）。

## Key Contributions

- **Documents an evaluator-dependent ranking confound in BoN TTS**: The same generated outputs are ranked in opposing directions by evaluators from different ASR families, so the verifier preferred under one family can lose under another (a reader using only the F5-TTS official Whisper evaluator would pick distil-v3; one using wav2vec 2.0 would pick w2v2-base).
- **Quantifies the confound's magnitude on LibriSpeech-PC test-clean**: The (verifier, evaluator) family pairing is a much larger lever on reported WER than the choice among common verifier checkpoints, with same-family pairs recovering 2-3× more oracle headroom than cross-family pairs (e.g., 26.0% vs. 7.9% under fwhisper-lgv3).
- **Rules out representation similarity as the explanation**: Linear CKA between audio encoders does not predict WER-rank agreement — the highest-CKA same-family pair (0.978) is anti-correlated (r=−0.52), while a low-CKA cross-family pair (0.55) agrees nearly perfectly (r=+0.94). The pattern is consistent with identity-/lineage-level coupling, a speech analog of LLM-as-judge self-bias.
- **Proposes cross-family rank ensembles** (rank-averaging and conjunctive max-rank) that are the most cross-evaluator-robust configurations tested, matching the best single verifier on mean WER while winning on cross-family evaluator columns.
- **Recommends cross-evaluator triangulation** as a default reporting practice: report WER under at least two ASR families with disjoint training lineages.

## Methodology

**Synthesis backbone.** The publicly released F5-TTS base model with its default recipe: 32 ODE solver steps, classifier-free guidance (CFG) scale 2.0, and sway sampling. For each utterance, 10 BoN candidates are generated with different random initializations; for N ∈ {3, 5, 10} the leading N candidates are taken and the one with the lowest joint WER+CER score against the reference transcript is selected.

**Four-way evaluator ablation.** A shared set of candidates is scored by four ASR evaluators spanning three families: a Whisper-large-v3 evaluator via faster-whisper (`fwhisper-lgv3`, the official F5-TTS evaluator), a large wav2vec 2.0 (`w2v2-lv60`), the same Distil-Whisper used as a verifier (`distil-sm`), and a large HuBERT (`hubert-lg`). For CKA analysis, Whisper-medium (`whisper-med`) is added as a fourth family member.

**Verifiers compared.** Three single-model verifiers — wav2vec 2.0 base (95M, `w2v2-base`), small Distil-Whisper (166M, `distil-sm`), large Distil-Whisper (756M, `distil-v3`) — plus a three-way rank-average ensemble (`ens3`), and two cross-family rank ensembles over the {w2v2-base, distil-v3} pair (`rank-avg` averaging ranks, `max-rank` taking the lowest worst-case rank).

**Data & normalization.** LibriSpeech-PC test-clean cross-sentence subset (Meister et al., 2023; 1127 samples, 4-10 s reference), run through the F5-TTS official evaluation pipeline with uniform default text normalization applied to all ASR outputs (Whisper's English-specific normalizer deliberately not applied, to keep comparisons on equal footing).

**Quality metrics.** Speaker similarity SIM-o = cosine of WavLM speaker embeddings (symmetric 24→16 kHz resampling); naturalness via UTMOS (Saeki et al., 2022) from a 22-strong checkpoint ensemble; real-time factors measured isolated on a single RTX 4090.

**Mechanism probe.** Linear CKA (Kornblith et al., 2019) on mean-pooled last hidden states of six ASR encoders over 500 F5-TTS-synthesized waveforms, correlated against WER-rank agreement (Pearson r) over 13 BoN configurations.

## Results & Highlights

- **BoN gains under the official F5-TTS evaluator (Table 1, fwhisper-lgv3, LibriSpeech-PC test-clean):** F5-TTS baseline = 2.06% WER; the only significant single-verifier reduction is distil-v3 → 1.88% (−8.7%, p=0.030); ens3 is a close runner-up at 1.91% (−7.2%, p=0.057); w2v2-base (−3.5%, p=0.31) and distil-sm (−1.0%, p=0.93) are not significant.
- **Verifier rankings reverse across ASR families (Table 2):** Under fwhisper-lgv3 distil-v3 wins (1.88%); under w2v2-lv60 w2v2-base wins (1.41% vs distil-v3's 1.45%); the distil-v3 BoN reduction is 0.18 pp under fwhisper-lgv3 but only 0.07 pp under w2v2-lv60 — flipping the ranking.
- **Same-family pairs recover 2-3× more oracle headroom (Table 3, N=3):** Under fwhisper-lgv3, distil-v3 recovers 26.0% of headroom vs. 7.9% for cross-family w2v2-base (3.3×); under w2v2-lv60, w2v2-base recovers 26.1% vs. 18.2% for distil-v3 (1.4×). Oracle WER at N=3 reaches 1.42% under fwhisper-lgv3, leaving substantial unexploited headroom.
- **Cross-family rank ensembles are most robust and scale monotonically (Table 4, Figure 1):** At N=10, distil-v3, rank-avg, and max-rank all reach mean WER 1.61% (−12% vs. F5-TTS's 2.06%, p<.001); rank-avg at N=5 is the only configuration reaching p<0.05 under all three evaluators at once (.001/.022/.0002). The single cross-family verifier w2v2-base regresses on fwhisper-lgv3 from 1.99% (N=3) to 2.20% (N=5), exposing the same-family-evaluator penalty.
- **CKA does not predict WER-rank agreement (Table 5, Figure 2):** The same-family Whisper pair (distil-sm ↔ whisper-med, CKA 0.978) is anti-correlated (r=−0.52), while the cross-family wav2vec 2.0/HuBERT pair (CKA 0.55) agrees nearly perfectly (r=+0.94). Pearson(CKA, r)=−0.36 over six pairs but reverses to +0.32 once the same-family outlier is removed — a single-point-driven trend.
- **No measurable quality cost:** SIM-o stays within ±0.001 and UTMOS within ±0.005 of F5-TTS (0.9426 / 3.879) across all configurations.
- **Best single-verifier reduction under the official evaluator:** 2.06% → 1.72% (−16.5%) with distil-v3 at N=10.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and well-scoped. The evaluator-dependence of BoN verifier rankings is documented rigorously for the first time in TTS, with a clear structural precedent (LLM-as-judge self-bias, Zheng et al., 2023) that the authors explicitly connect to rather than overclaim originality. The "speech analog of self-bias" framing is the paper's sharpest conceptual move.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Shared candidate pool across all four evaluators (no confound from regeneration), permutation tests, oracle-headroom bounds, and a mechanistic CKA probe that is correctly interpreted as *not* supporting the naive similarity hypothesis. The honest note that the 2.04 vs. 2.06 single-shot discrepancy is Whisper batching non-determinism is a good sign of careful bookkeeping.
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent. Public F5-TTS checkpoint, public ASR checkpoints, public code, fixed cross-family pair `{w2v2-base, distil-v3}` "fixed in advance as the largest publicly available verifier in each family, prior to any cross-evaluator analysis" — i.e., no cherry-picking after seeing results.
- **Significance:** Practically important for the BoN-TTS subfield: it argues that single-evaluator WER comparisons in prior work (CosyVoice 2, Seed-TTS, NaturalSpeech 3 tune verifiers against a single fixed ASR) are potentially confounded, and offers a concrete fix (cross-family rank ensembles + triangulated reporting). The 2-3× same-family headroom recovery is the headline finding that should change reporting norms.
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - **Single TTS backbone (F5-TTS only) on one benchmark (LibriSpeech-PC test-clean).** Generalization to CosyVoice 2 / MaskGCT / other corpora is explicitly deferred; the confound could be backbone- or corpus-specific. The authors acknowledge this.
  - **Single fixed cross-family pair.** Only the {w2v2-base, distil-v3} pair is ensembled; whether the rank-avg/max-rank advantage holds for other family pairings (e.g., adding HuBERT as a third family) is not tested.
  - **Identity vs. lineage-level coupling left unresolved.** The paper distinguishes these two mechanisms but defers disentangling them; the CKA result is negative evidence against a similarity account rather than positive evidence for a specific lineage mechanism.
  - **Quality triangulation near resolution limit.** SIM-o/UTMOS deltas are within noise and both predictors may be saturating (UTMOS ≈ 4.0); human-MOS / NISQA / UTMOSv2 triangulation is deferred, so the "no quality cost" claim rests on potentially insensitive automatic metrics.
  - **Parakeet/Canary (a fourth ASR family) not included**, so the "disjoint training lineages" recommendation is validated on three families only.

## Strengths

- Crisp, falsifiable central claim backed by a controlled four-evaluator ablation over a shared candidate pool.
- Genuine methodological contribution: cross-family rank-avg / max-rank ensembles are simple, drop-in, and provably the most cross-evaluator-robust configurations, with a practical decision rule (same-family evaluator known → use distil-v3 at N=10; otherwise rank-avg/max-rank at N=5).
- Honest negative-result reporting: the CKA analysis is presented as a refutation of the obvious hypothesis, not buried.
- Open code and pre-registered cross-family pair selection eliminate the most likely reviewer suspicion (post-hoc verifier shopping).
- Statistical testing (permutation tests, regression flags) is more rigorous than typical TTS workshop papers.

## Limitations

- One TTS backbone (F5-TTS), one benchmark (LibriSpeech-PC test-clean), English only — generalization is unproven.
- Only one cross-family verifier pair is ensembled; the robustness of rank-avg/max-rank to other pairings and to three-way ensembles is untested.
- The identity- vs. lineage-level mechanism is identified but not disentangled.
- Automatic quality metrics (SIM-o, UTMOS) may be near saturation, weakening the "no quality degradation" conclusion; human-MOS triangulation is deferred.
- N=10 is the ceiling studied; whether the confound amplifies or attenuates at larger N (where recent BoN-TTS work pushes) is unknown.

## Relevance

这项工作对评估方法论有直接价值：如果研究兴趣覆盖零样本 TTS、推理时优化（BoN/CFG/reverse inference）或语音评估指标，本文揭示的"ASR 家族混淆"是一个必须纳入考量、否则 WER 比较可能系统性失真的陷阱，其跨家族排序集成方案可直接复用于自身的 BoN 实验以提升报告稳健性。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the family-alignment confound persist (or amplify) on stronger backbones like CosyVoice 2 and MaskGCT, and on non-English corpora where ASR families may share less training data?
- Would adding a genuinely independent third family (e.g., Parakeet/Canary, or a non-transformer ASR) to the rank ensemble further improve cross-evaluator robustness, or does it introduce its own lineage bias?
- Can the identity- vs. lineage-level coupling be disentangled — e.g., by using two Whisper variants with different distillation teachers, or by adversarial reweighting against in-family inflation as the paper suggests?
- At much larger N (50-100), does the same-family inflation grow with N (as the N=3→5 regression of w2v2-base under fwhisper-lgv3 hints), and do cross-family ensembles retain their advantage?
- How does the confound interact with verifiers that are themselves fine-tuned on synthetic speech, which is increasingly common in BoN-TTS pipelines?
