CASL-VAE: Learning Structured Latent Variables from Unpaired Data for Semi-supervised Clustering and Paired Sample Generation

arXiv: 2607.08254

Date: 2026-07-09

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08254

一句话总结： CASL-VAE 是一个对比式变分自编码器，将隐空间分解为跨域共享的连续 common latent 与刻画目标域异质性的分层 salient latent（离散子型 + 子型内连续变化），在无需配对数据的条件下，于半合成神经影像和真实阿尔茨海默病数据上实现了更强的子型发现与配对样本生成。
TL;DR: CASL-VAE is a contrastive VAE that decomposes the latent space into a shared continuous common latent and a hierarchical salient latent (discrete subtypes + continuous within-subtype variation), and uses a posterior-decoupling argument to optimize an approximate joint likelihood from unpaired reference/target data; on semi-synthetic iSTAGING neuroimaging it raises joint target+reference ARI to 0.697 (vs. 0.451 for the best SepVAE baseline) at 10–30% atrophy and to 0.587 at 10–20% atrophy, while achieving the highest paired-sample cosine similarity (0.5835/0.5816) and revealing biologically plausible AD subtypes on ADNI.

Key Contributions

  • Introduces CASL-VAE, a contrastive generative framework that learns disentangled shared and target-specific representations from unpaired data using structured latent variables.
  • Proposes a hierarchical salient latent decomposition (discrete subtypes via Gumbel-Softmax + continuous within-subtype factors) that enables unsupervised target-subtype discovery while preserving fine-grained variation.
  • Derives a variational result showing that under a posterior-decoupling constraint, the joint log-likelihood bound decomposes into domain-marginal terms, giving a principled probabilistic justification for standard contrastive-analysis objectives and enabling paired-sample generation without paired training data.
  • Validates the framework against six representative baselines (K-means, GMM, GMVAE, cVAE, SepVAE, Smile-GAN) on semi-synthetic iSTAGING neuroimaging, with consistent gains especially under subtle effect sizes and joint clustering.
  • Demonstrates clinical relevance on ADNI Alzheimer's data, recovering two biologically plausible atrophy subtypes and showing the inferred deviation index correlates strongly with cognition, APOE E4 carrier status, and CSF biomarkers.

Methodology

CASL-VAE targets the unpaired reference–target setting: a target domain X and a reference domain Y with no sample correspondence, plus only weak domain-level labels (reference vs. target). It introduces three latent variables:

  • A continuous common latent z ∈ R^k (k=15) capturing variation shared across both domains (e.g., demographic/scanner variability).
  • A discrete salient latent s_cat ∈ {1,…,M} (Gumbel-Softmax) modeling coarse target subtypes; category 1 is reserved for the reference domain.
  • A continuous salient latent s_cont ∈ R^t (t=2) conditioned on s_cat, modeling fine-grained within-subtype variation. The prior mean μ_ψ(s_cat) is learned per subtype.

The generative model uses an additive decoder: p(x | z, s) = N(d_θz(z) + d_θs(s_cat, s_cont), I). For reference samples, the salient latent is clamped to an uninformative preset s'=(s'_cat=1, s'_cont=0), forcing all reference-specific structure into the common latent and making d_θs(s') a constant baseline offset. This additive form yields directly interpretable prototypical target patterns δ_s_cat = d_θs(s_cat, μ_ψ(s_cat)) − d_θs(s'_cat=1, s'_cont=0).

The key theoretical contribution is a posterior-decoupling argument: the joint ELBO depends on q(z|x,y), which would require pairs, but imposing q(z|x,y) = q(z|x) = q(z|y) makes the common latent domain-invariant and lets the bound decompose into separate target (Lx) and reference (Ly) marginal ELBOs — i.e., optimizable from unpaired data. This recovers standard contrastive-analysis objectives as implicit joint-likelihood maximization.

Four complementary regularizers enforce the assumed structure:

  • MMD (R_MMD) aligns the common-latent distributions q(z|x) and q(z|y) at the distribution level.
  • Latent Consistency (R_LC) enforces sample-level alignment by requiring q(z|ỹ) ≈ q(z|x) for a conditionally generated paired reference ỹ decoded from z with salient set to s'.
  • Binary Cross-Entropy (R_BCE) uses domain labels to push reference samples to s'_cat=1 and discourage target samples from it, reserving categories 2..M for unsupervised subtype discovery.
  • Reference Regularization (R_ref) drives both posterior and prior means of s_cont toward zero for reference samples, suppressing reference information leakage.

The paper provides an information-theoretic interpretation: R_BCE and R_ref jointly minimize an upper bound on I(Y; s_cat, s_cont), making the salient latent provably uninformative about the reference domain. The full objective is J = −L_ELBO + λR_MMD + ηR_LC + ζR_BCE + γR_ref (λ=100, η=10, ζ=1, γ=1), trained with Adam (lr=1e-3, β1=0.5), batch size 128, Gumbel-Softmax temperature annealing, and convergence-based early stopping.

The trained model supports three inference modes: subtype assignment (argmax of s_cat), conditional paired-sample generation (decode z with preset salient for the paired reference), and synthetic paired/unpaired data generation.

Results & Highlights

  • Joint (target+reference) clustering, typical atrophy (10–30%): CASL-VAE achieves ARI = 0.697 (95% CI 0.672–0.722) vs. 0.451 (0.380–0.523) for the best baseline SepVAE — a large gap showing it can simultaneously separate reference from target subtypes.
  • Joint clustering, mild atrophy (10–20%): CASL-VAE ARI = 0.587 (0.521–0.653) vs. 0.159 (0.130–0.188) for SepVAE, a ~3.7× improvement in the harder weak-effect regime.
  • Target-only clustering, mild atrophy (10–20%): CASL-VAE ARI = 0.468 (0.371–0.564), substantially above the best baseline Smile-GAN at 0.183 (0.097–0.293) and cVAE at 0.183; at 10–30% it is statistically on par with the best (0.620 vs. Smile-GAN 0.657, CIs overlap).
  • Paired-sample generation (cosine similarity to ground-truth paired reference): CASL-VAE = 0.5835 (10–20%) / 0.5816 (10–30%), beating cVAE (0.5471 / 0.5208) and SepVAE (0.5287 / 0.5409).
  • Subtype-pattern recovery: Figure 2 shows near-perfect visual correspondence between CASL-VAE's inferred δ_s_cat brain maps and the ground-truth simulated subtype masks.
  • ADNI real-data clinical correlation: The deviation index (DI) correlates with ADAS-Cog-13 at r = 0.473 (p ≈ 5e-37), ADNI-MEM at r = −0.469, MMSE at r = −0.354, APOE E4 carrier status at r = 0.242, and CSF-Abeta at r = −0.254, indicating the salient latent captures clinically meaningful AD pathology.
  • Ablation: Removing MMD causes a noticeable ARI drop (target-specific variation leaks into the common latent); removing BCE degrades reference/subtype separation. Performance is stable across 0–200% hyperparameter scaling once regularizers are active, and degrades to near-random ARI only under extremely mild 0–10% atrophy.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The core idea — combining contrastive analysis (common vs. salient) with a hierarchical salient latent (discrete subtypes + continuous within-subtype factors) inside a single VAE — is a genuine, well-motivated extension over SepVAE (which has a flat salient latent) and Smile-GAN (which clusters but is not generative). The posterior-decoupling derivation that recovers CA objectives as approximate joint-likelihood maximization is a nice theoretical touch, though it largely formalizes what existing CA methods already do heuristically.
  • Rigor: The ELBO derivation, the information-theoretic justification of R_BCE/R_ref as an MI upper bound, the ablation over regularization strengths, and the 10-fold repeated CV with reported 95% CIs are commendable. Confidence intervals are reported and statistical non-inferiority is honestly flagged (e.g., the asterisk noting CASL-VAE's target-only ARI at 10–30% is not significantly different from Smile-GAN).
  • Reproducibility concerns: Model weights/code release is not mentioned in the main text. The architecture, hyperparameters, and algorithm are specified in enough detail to reimplement, but the iSTAGING/ADNI data requires application access, and the data-preprocessing pipeline (MUSE ROIs, ComBat harmonization, residualization) is non-trivial to reproduce exactly. The number of subtypes M is selected by an internal clustering-stability heuristic rather than a held-out criterion, which could be brittle on new datasets.
  • Significance: Clinical utility is demonstrated only via correlation of the deviation index with known AD biomarkers — there is no comparison against established AD subtyping baselines (e.g., the authors' own Smile-GAN or Aglinskas et al.'s contrastive method) on the real ADNI data, and no external validation cohort. The "two subtypes" finding (diffuse vs. medial-temporal atrophy) is plausible but not novel as an AD result.
  • Red flags: (1) On the important weak-effect regime the target-only ARI advantage is the headline claim, yet at typical (10–30%) atrophy CASL-VAE does not beat Smile-GAN on target-only clustering — the strongest claims rest on the mild regime and the joint-clustering metric the authors chose. (2) The "paired-sample generation" evaluation uses cosine similarity, a relatively lenient metric on standardized residualized ROI volumes; no perceptual/clinical plausibility check of generated pairs is provided. (3) All experiments use only 145 ROI volumes as input features — a low-dimensional setting where the gains over simpler baselines are modest in reconstruction MAE (CASL-VAE is statistically tied with SepVAE on reference MAE), raising the question of whether the elaborate hierarchical latent is necessary vs. a well-tuned SepVAE. (4) CSF-Tau and CSF-pTau correlations are weak/non-significant, somewhat undercutting the "biologically meaningful" claim for key AD pathology markers.

Strengths

  • Principled probabilistic formulation that unifies contrastive analysis, disentanglement, and generative modeling under one ELBO with a clear posterior-decoupling argument.
  • The hierarchical salient latent (discrete + continuous) is a natural and well-executed design for heterogeneous target populations, and the additive decoder gives directly interpretable, visualizable subtype patterns.
  • Comprehensive and fair baseline comparison (six methods, matched total latent dimensionality of 18), with honest reporting of overlapping CIs.
  • Strong information-theoretic justification for why R_BCE + R_ref make the salient latent reference-invariant.
  • Clinically grounded: uses established iSTAGING/ADNI pipelines, reports correlations with cognition, genetics, and CSF biomarkers, and recovers biologically plausible atrophy patterns.

Limitations

  • Subtype discovery collapses to near-random ARI under extremely mild (0–10%) atrophy, so the method needs a minimum effect size that is unknown a priori in real data.
  • No code or weights are explicitly released; the iSTAGING/ADNI data is access-restricted, limiting reproducibility.
  • Real-data validation is correlational only — no head-to-head comparison with prior AD-subtyping methods on ADNI, and no external hold-out cohort.
  • Input is limited to 145 scalar ROI volumes; performance on raw images/high-dimensional modalities (the setting where deep generative models matter most) is unaddressed.
  • Paired-generation quality is measured only by cosine similarity on residualized volumes; no clinical or perceptual evaluation.
  • The number of subtypes M is chosen by an internal stability heuristic and the paper reports only M=3 for ADNI; sensitivity to this choice on real disease structure is not deeply explored.

Relevance

This work is directly relevant to disentangled representation learning and unsupervised disease subtyping from unpaired multi-domain medical data — connecting variational inference, contrastive analysis, and hierarchical latent-variable modeling. Its theoretical framing (posterior decoupling enabling joint-likelihood optimization from marginals) and its approach to modeling heterogeneous target variation are transferable to broader reference/target comparison problems beyond neuroimaging.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the hierarchical salient latent still help when the input is raw 3D MRI volumes rather than 145 scalar ROI features, where the generative decoder has real work to do?
  • How does CASL-VAE compare against modern diffusion-based contrastive or normative models (e.g., Bercea et al. 2025) on the same ADNI cohort?
  • Is the deviation index (DI) predictive of conversion (CN→MCI→AD) prospectively, beyond cross-sectional correlation with biomarkers?
  • How sensitive is the inferred number of subtypes M to cohort composition, scanner, and the stability-based selection heuristic?
  • Can the posterior-decoupling argument be extended to paired/longitudinal data when partially available, to tighten the bound?
  • Does the additive-decoder interpretability (δ_s_cat) survive when likelihoods other than Gaussian (e.g., Bernoulli for segmentation masks) are used?
