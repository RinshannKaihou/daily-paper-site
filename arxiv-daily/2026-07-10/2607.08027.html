Structured Pruning of Large Language Models via Power Transformation and Sign-Preserving Score Aggregation with Adaptive Feature Retention

arXiv: 2607.08027

Date: 2026-07-10

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08027

一句话总结： 本文将非结构化剪枝方法 AFR 改造为结构化剪枝，通过幂变换对齐 ReFer/SNIP 异构分数分布、保留符号的聚合评估优化方向一致性、以及百分位离群点去除三招，在 Llama-3-8B/Vicuna-13B/LLaVA-13B 上 50% 剪枝率下取得 1.56–1.57× 推理加速，且精度与无结构 AFR 相当、显著优于 LLM-Pruner/LoRAP/CFSP 等结构化基线。
TL;DR: This paper adapts the unstructured AFR pruner (ReFer + SNIP) to structured FFN pruning by fixing three aggregation pathologies — distribution mismatch, sign loss, and outlier dominance — via power transformation, sign-preserving aggregation, and percentile outlier removal; on Llama-3-8B at 50% FFN pruning it reaches 47.24% average accuracy across WinoGrande/HellaSwag/ARC-e/ARC-c/MMLU, beating naive averaging by +15.13 points and the strongest structured baseline CFSP by +6.47 points, while delivering 1.57× inference speedup.

核心贡献

  • 识别并形式化了将 AFR 迁移到结构化剪枝的三个根本难题： (1) ReFer 与 SNIP 分数的异构分布失配（ReFer 取值范围极宽，SNIP 集中在 [0,1]）；(2) 聚合时取绝对值导致"优化方向一致性"符号信息丢失；(3) 极端离群点（全范围 12,687、剔除 2% 后仅 48.8）会翻转神经元级分数排序。
  • 提出三项配套技术并整合为统一流程： 对 SNIP 分数施加幂变换 ŝ = s^b（b=2.7）实现非线性分布对齐并提升信噪比；将"先取绝对值再平均"改为"先平均再取绝对值"以量化神经元内方向一致性；按第 2/98 百分位逐神经元剔除离群分数，实现鲁棒聚合。
  • 跨三类模型（纯语言 + 视觉语言）验证有效性： 在 Llama-3-8B、Vicuna-v1.5-13B、LLaVA-v1.5-13B 上系统对比无结构 AFR、朴素结构化 AFR 及 LLM-Pruner/LoRAP/CFSP，并报告参数量/显存/延迟的实际收益；对 VLM，语言部分用完整 AFR、视觉部分（CLIP 无任务损失）只用 ReFer 分量剪枝，给出务实的工程处理。
  • 给出可复现的消融与统计依据： 在 Llama-3-8B 上对 b 从 1.0 到 3.0 做完整扫描，证明 b∈[2.5,3.0] 是平台期（变化仅 0.15pp），并用峰度/IQR/范围统计论证 2% 剔除率为最优离群点阈值。

Key Contributions

  • Formalizes three concrete pathologies that emerge when the unstructured AFR score (a standardized sum of feature-based ReFer and gradient-based SNIP) is aggregated to neuron level for structured pruning: heterogeneous score distributions, loss of optimization-direction sign information, and outlier dominance in simple averaging.
  • Proposes an integrated three-component remedy: (1) power transformation s^b on SNIP scores for nonlinear low-importance noise suppression / high-importance signal preservation; (2) sign-preserving aggregation (average signed scores then take absolute value, rather than abs-then-average) that measures intra-neuron optimization-direction coherence; (3) per-neuron percentile (2nd/98th) outlier removal.
  • Demonstrates effectiveness across language-only and vision-language LLMs (Llama-3-8B, Vicuna-v1.5-13B, LLaVA-v1.5-13B), including a pragmatic split for VLMs where the language tower uses full AFR while the CLIP vision tower (which has no task loss to differentiate) is pruned with ReFer only.
  • Provides thorough ablation and statistical justification for both hyperparameters: a full sweep of the power exponent b on Llama-3-8B showing a stable plateau in [2.5, 3.0], and kurtosis/IQR/range analysis justifying the 2% outlier-removal threshold as the point where outlier influence saturates before central-distribution information is lost.

Methodology

The method extends Adaptive Feature Retention (AFR), an unstructured pruner whose weight-level score is the standardized sum of ReFer (sensitivity of pre-trained feature-space geometry, captured via mean singular values of layer outputs) and SNIP (gradient·weight contribution to task loss). To turn AFR into a structured FFN pruner, the authors first show that naive neuron-level averaging |S_ij| collapses (Table 1: e.g. Llama-3-8B WinoGrande drops from 72.61 to 59.35 at 20%, vs 71.59 for unstructured AFR), then attack the three root causes:

  1. Power transformation (Sec 4.1). SNIP scores for converged pre-trained weights pile up in [0,1] near 0; linear standardization amplifies this dense noise and contaminates ReFer. Applying ŝ = s^b (b > 1) crushes near-zero values (x^b ≪ x) while barely affecting near-one values (x^b ≲ x), a soft-threshold effect. An SNR argument shows SNR_transformed/SNR_original = (μ_signal/σ_noise)^(b−1) grows exponentially with b. The exponent b = 2.7 is fixed by the Appendix A sweep.
  1. Sign-preserving aggregation (Sec 4.2). ReFer/SNIP scores are gradient·weight products whose sign encodes whether the weight is decreasing or increasing during optimization. A neuron whose weights all move the same direction is structurally coherent and worth keeping; mixed signs imply internal cancellation. Averaging signed scores then taking the absolute value (instead of abs-then-average) quantifies this coherence — consistent signs survive, canceling signs shrink toward zero.
  1. Percentile outlier removal (Sec 4.3). ReFer score ranges in one layer span −2,884 to 9,803 (width 12,687) but 2% trimming collapses the range to 48.8 and kurtosis from 16,488,324 to 3,505 (a 99.98% reduction), while IQR barely moves (91.72 → 86.71). From 2%→3% the kurtosis drop falls to 54.9%, signaling saturation; from 2%→10% IQR degrades to 68.20, signaling information loss. Hence the 2nd/98th percentiles per neuron are used.

The integrated pipeline (Sec 4.4) runs: per-neuron 2nd/98th-percentile outlier removal on both ReFer and SNIP → signed mean then abs to get neuron-level ReFer and SNIP scores → power transform on the neuron-level SNIP → sum with neuron-level ReFer → prune lowest-scoring neurons. Pruning targets only FFN sub-layers; attention is left intact.

Results & Highlights

  • Llama-3-8B, 50% FFN pruning, average over WinoGrande/HellaSwag/ARC-e/ARC-c/MMLU: the proposed method reaches 47.24%, versus 32.11% for naive-averaging structured AFR (+15.13 points) and 46.92% for unstructured AFR (essentially matching it while being structured). It beats the strongest structured baseline CFSP (40.77% avg) by +6.47 points, LoRAP (38.75%) by +8.49 points, and LLM-Pruner (34.87%) by +12.37 points (Table 3).
  • Llama-3-8B, 20% pruning: proposed 64.46% average vs naive-averaging 43.19% (+21.27 points) and unstructured AFR 65.91% (only −1.45 points gap despite real speedup). On individual benchmarks at 20%, proposed WinoGrande is 74.03, actually above the unpruned model's 72.61 and unstructured AFR's 71.59.
  • Vicuna-v1.5-13B, 50% pruning: proposed 52.91% average vs closest competitor CFSP at 51.20% (+1.71 points), and vs LLM-Pruner 42.61% / LoRAP 44.67% (Table 4).
  • LLaVA-v1.5-13B (GQA/VizWiz/ScienceQA), 50% pruning: proposed 47.08% average; on VizWiz the pruned model reaches 58.28% at 20% pruning, above the unpruned 56.15% (Table 5), indicating genuinely redundant FFN parameters were removed.
  • Real hardware speedup (Llama-3-8B, 50%): parameters 8.03B → 5.21B (−35.1%), VRAM 16.06GB → 10.42GB (−35.1%), latency 22.98 → 14.64 ms/sample (1.57×) using dense kernels with no sparse library (Table 6). LLaVA-13B at 50% reaches 1.56× (756.1 → 481.9 ms/sample) (Table 7).
  • Ablation, power exponent b on Llama-3-8B at 20%: no-transform (b=1.0) 61.29%, peak b=2.7 → 64.46% (+3.17 pp), with the entire b∈[2.5,3.0] band within 0.15 pp of the peak (Table 8), confirming the gain is not an over-tuned artifact.

Authors & Credibility

The work comes from the Chubu University vision/ML group (Kobayashi, Hirakawa, Yamashita, Fujiyoshi) — the same lineage that authored the AFR/ReFer methods (Nitta et al., MIRU 2024/2025) being extended here — in collaboration with Panasonic Holdings Corporation researchers (Ishii, Okuno, Kozuka). The industrial co-authorship and the direct continuation of the group's own prior pruning line give the "extending AFR to structured pruning" framing genuine technical continuity rather than a tangential re-skin. Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Real but incremental. None of the three components (power transform, sign-aware aggregation, percentile clipping) is individually new; the contribution is the diagnosis that these three specific failures occur when AFR scores are neuron-aggregated, plus the integrated pipeline tuned for that setting. The SNR analysis for the power transform and the kurtosis/IQR argument for the 2% threshold are nice, paper-specific justifications rather than hand-waving.
  • Rigor: Solid on the central claim — full comparison grids on three models against three structured baselines plus naive and unstructured AFR, plus an exponent sweep and statistical justification for both hyperparameters. However, the ablation decomposes only the power exponent, not the three components individually; Table 8 varies b with the other two components always on, so the marginal contribution of sign-preserving aggregation vs outlier removal vs power transform is never isolated (the text asserts "synergistic effects" without a component-wise ablation table to back the synergy claim).
  • Comparison fairness is under-specified. LLM-Pruner and LoRAP are conventionally paired with LoRA-based post-pruning recovery/fine-tuning; the paper never states whether baselines were re-run with or without recovery, nor whether the proposed one-shot method is being compared against recovered or unrecovered baselines. If the proposed method (apparently one-shot, no recovery) beats recovered baselines, that is a strong result that deserves to be stated explicitly; if baselines were run without recovery, the margins are overstated. This is the single biggest ambiguity.
  • Reproducibility: Mostly good — models, benchmarks, pruning rates, and b=2.7 are all named; the outlier threshold is fixed at 2nd/98th percentile. Missing: the calibration data used for SNIP gradients and ReFer SVD (amount, source), random seeds, and exact FFN neuron-count/pruning mapping. No code/release is referenced.
  • Significance: Practically meaningful — 1.57× real speedup at 50% FFN pruning with dense kernels is deployment-relevant, and the VLM (CLIP-via-ReFer-only) handling is a useful recipe. Scope is limited, though: only FFNs are pruned (attention untouched, which is why 50% pruning yields ~35% param reduction, not 50%), so the method is not a whole-model compression solution.
  • Red flags / inconsistencies: Minor. The LLaVA section claims 20% pruning "slightly exceeds the original model by 0.06 points," but the reported 20% average (62.89% from Table 5: 60.34/58.28/70.05) is below the unpruned 64.09% (63.29/56.15/72.83) — the "0.06 point" statement is inconsistent with the table (though the single-benchmark VizWiz gain is real). Also, several reported average numbers are computed from per-benchmark tables and are not always printed, inviting transcription drift.

Strengths

  • Identifies and names three concrete, well-illustrated failure modes of naive structured AFR, each backed by a motivating statistic (e.g. ReFer range collapsing from 12,687 to 48.8 after 2% trim) — the diagnosis is the most valuable part of the paper.
  • The three fixes are cheap, training-free, and composable; the method requires no fine-tuning/recovery stage yet still competes with methods that typically need one.
  • Cross-architecture validation (decoder-only LLM + VLM) with a principled, task-loss-aware handling of the CLIP vision tower (ReFer-only, since SNIP gradients are undefined there).
  • Strong practical deployment evidence: real VRAM/latency numbers, not just accuracy, and an explicit explanation of why 50% pruning yields ~1.57× (not 2×) due to leaving attention and kernel overhead intact.

Limitations

  • FFN-only pruning. Attention modules are not pruned, capping achievable compression (50% FFN pruning ≈ 35% total param reduction) and leaving the "whole-model" compression story open — the authors flag extending to attention as future work.
  • No component-level ablation. Table 8 sweeps only b; the individual and pairwise contributions of sign-preserving aggregation and outlier removal are never isolated, so "synergy" is asserted, not measured.
  • Baseline recovery protocol undisclosed. Ambiguous whether LLM-Pruner/LoRAP/CFSP comparisons used post-pruning recovery, which is material to the margin claims.
  • Calibration/seed details missing, hindering exact reproduction.
  • Generalization untested beyond FFN-FFN. No test of whether b=2.7 and 2% trim transfer to non-FFN structures or to other model families (only two Llama-family decoders and one Llama-based VLM).
  • Aggressive-pruning degradation is large on VLMs: GQA collapses from 63.29 → 29.19 at 50% pruning, indicating the method does not gracefully handle high pruning rates on dense vision-language reasoning even if averages look acceptable.

Relevance

与"大模型高效推理/压缩"研究方向高度相关：结构化剪枝是端侧/低成本部署 LLM 与 VLM 的关键路径，本文给出的训练免费、组件可拆分的剪枝分数聚合方案，以及"语言塔用 AFR、视觉塔只用 ReFer"的工程处理，对从事推理加速与模型压缩的研究有直接参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How much of the reported margin over LLM-Pruner/LoRAP/CFSP survives if those baselines are re-run with their standard LoRA recovery protocol — i.e., is the proposed one-shot method genuinely better, or benefiting from a weaker baseline setup?
  • What is the per-component contribution? A 2^3 ablation over {power transform, sign-preserving aggregation, percentile clipping} would quantify whether all three are necessary or whether one dominates.
  • Does b=2.7 and the 2% trim generalize beyond Llama-family FFNs — e.g., to Mistral/Qwen FFNs, to Mixture-of-Experts experts, or to attention head pruning where intra-head direction consistency may behave differently?
  • Can the method be combined with a light recovery stage (LoRA or lightweight distillation), and would that close the remaining ~1.5-point gap to unstructured AFR or push past it?
  • Why does GQA degrade so catastrophically (63.29 → 29.19 at 50%) while VizWiz stays robust — is there a structure-specific (e.g. spatial-reasoning-critical FFN) failure mode the score misses?
