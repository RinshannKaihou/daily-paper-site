> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"正确性一致性"(Correctness Agreement) 这一决策级评估指标，结合统计矩与分布散度，系统刻画了 LLM 从 8-bit 到 2-bit 量化下注意力权重结构的逐层漂移，揭示出即使困惑度/准确率看似保持，量化模型在逐样本决策上仍与基模型大幅背离；并指出 Q/K 投影比 V/O 更敏感、Q4_K 是安全上限、Q2_K 是崩溃区。

> **TL;DR:** The paper argues that standard accuracy/perplexity metrics hide quantization-induced behavioral drift in LLMs. It introduces **correctness agreement (CA)**, a decision-level metric measuring the overlap of correct predictions between a base model and its quantized variants, and pairs it with statistical-moment (kurtosis/skewness/mean/std) and distributional-divergence (KL/KS/cosine/Euclidean) analysis of attention projection weights. Across Llama-3.2-3B, Vicuna-7B, Mistral-7B, and Llama-3.1-8B quantized 8-bit to 2-bit (legacy + K-quant via llama.cpp), CA runs roughly 10-14 points below accuracy even at Q8_0 on HellaSwag/Winogrande/ARC (e.g., Mistral-7B: 61.6% base accuracy vs 48.2% CA at Q8_0), showing that perplexity and accuracy preserve the "illusion of equivalency" while decision behavior diverges. Q/K projections are consistently the most structurally sensitive; V/O stay stable; Q4_K is the safe ceiling, Q3_K the onset of degradation, Q2_K a breakdown regime.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"正确性一致性"(Correctness Agreement, CA) 指标**：衡量基模型与量化变体在相同样本上预测正确性的重叠，独立于绝对准确率，能捕捉准确率/困惑度无法反映的行为漂移。
- **将后训练量化形式化为作用于参数的算子族** T_c(θ)=D_c(Q_c(θ))，并联合分析其对注意力投影权重 (Q/K/V/O) 与决策行为的影响。
- **建立统计矩 + 分布散度的逐层敏感性分析框架**：用 kurtosis/skewness/mean/std 与 KL/KS/cosine/Euclidean 四种散度，定位量化下结构漂移最严重的层。
- **首个从 8-bit 到 2-bit、覆盖 legacy 与 K-quantization 的受控扫描**，逐块追踪注意力投影漂移并经验性地链接到决策行为变化。
- **识别出量化的非线性断点与层敏感性规律**：Q4_K 为安全上限，Q3_K 为退化起点，Q2_K 为崩溃区；Q 和 K 投影始终最敏感，V 和 O 相对鲁棒。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **correctness agreement (CA)**, a decision-level behavioral metric that measures the overlap of correct predictions between a base model and its quantized variants, complementing accuracy and perplexity. It is provably bounded by min(base, quantized) accuracy but captures drift the aggregate metrics miss.
- Formalizes post-training quantization as an operator family T_c(θ) = D_c(Q_c(θ)) and studies its joint effect on attention projection weights and decision behavior.
- Builds a layer-wise sensitivity analysis combining statistical moments (mean, std, skewness, kurtosis) with four distributional divergences (cosine similarity, Euclidean distance, Kolmogorov-Smirnov, KL divergence) computed over dequantized Q/K/V/O weight matrices.
- Presents the first controlled 8-bit-to-2-bit sweep across both legacy (Q8_0/Q5_0/Q4_0) and K-quantization (Q6_K-Q2_K) families via llama.cpp, tracking block-level attention drift and linking it to behavioral changes.
- Identifies non-linear quantization breakpoints and a consistent layer-sensitivity ordering: Q and K projections are the most distortion-prone, V and O the most robust; concludes Q4_K is the safe ceiling, Q3_K the onset of degradation, and Q2_K a breakdown regime.

## Methodology

**PTQ as an operator.** The authors fix a set of quantization configurations C, each encoding a bit-width b(c) ∈ {2,...,8} and a scheme (legacy or K-quant). Post-training quantization is modeled as an operator T_c(θ) := D_c(Q_c(θ)) that maps base parameters θ to dequantized approximate weights, inducing model p_{T_c(θ)}. The object of study is the joint effect of T_c on (a) internal attention weights and (b) decision behavior.

**Structural deviation in weight space.** For each transformer block i they extract the four self-attention projections W_i^L, L ∈ {Q,K,V,O}. They quantify drift with two families of functionals: (1) statistical moments s(·) — mean, std, skewness, kurtosis — aggregated block-wise and model-wise; (2) distributional divergences d(·,·) — cosine similarity, Euclidean distance, KS statistic, KL divergence over empirical weight histograms. Δs(c) = s(T_c(θ)) − s(θ) gives the statistical drift per configuration.

**Correctness agreement.** For a benchmark with deterministic scoring score(ŷ,y) ∈ {0,1}, base labels z_m and quantized labels z_m^(c) are defined; CA(c;θ,D) = (1/M) Σ 1[z_m=1 ∧ z_m^(c)=1]. Crucially CA ignores cases where both models are jointly wrong, so it measures decision overlap rather than agreement-by-accident. It satisfies 0 ≤ CA ≤ min(Acc_base, Acc_quant).

**Models, quantization, datasets.** Four models — Llama-3.2-3B, Vicuna-7B-v1.5, Mistral-7B-v0.1, Llama-3.1-8B — quantized with llama.cpp into legacy (Q8_0, Q5_0, Q4_0) and K-quantization (Q6_K, Q5_K, Q4_K, Q3_K, Q2_K). Perplexity is measured on WikiText-2 and C4; accuracy and CA are measured zero-shot on HellaSwag, Winogrande, and ARC (multiple-choice, scored by summed token log-prob argmax). Hardware: 8× NVIDIA Tesla V100-SXM2 (32 GB). A Python implementation is included with the submission.

## Results & Highlights

- **CA sits ~10-14 points below accuracy even at the lightest quantization, and the gap widens at low bits** (Table 4, macro-averaged over HellaSwag/Winogrande/ARC). On Mistral-7B, base accuracy is 61.6% while Q8_0 gives accuracy 56.9% but CA only 48.2% — i.e., ~9 pts of correct decisions vanish even at 8-bit. On Llama-3.2-3B, base 55.5% → Q8_0 accuracy 53.4% / CA 41.4%, collapsing to accuracy 48.7% / CA 38.5% at Q2_K.
- **The accuracy-CA gap is largest for the smallest model and grows under compression.** Llama-3.2-3B loses ~14 pts of CA between base-equivalent and Q2_K (41.4 → 38.5) while Mistral-7B is the most robust, varying CA by less than ~1 pt across all bit-widths (48.2 at Q8_0 → 47.3 at Q2_K).
- **Perplexity is non-monotonic and sometimes *improves* under quantization, contradicting the behavioral collapse.** On WikiText-2 (Table 2), Llama-3.2-3B base PPL is 2.300 but Q3_K reaches 1.967 and Q2_K 1.857 — *lower* than base — while CA at Q2_K is among the worst. On Llama-3.1-8B, base PPL 2.167 vs Q2_K 1.687. The authors use this explicitly to argue perplexity is an unreliable proxy for behavioral preservation.
- **Statistical/divergence breakpoints are sharp and non-linear at Q3_K/Q2_K.** Kurtosis collapses, skewness spikes, and KL/KS divergence jump disproportionately at low bits, while 8-to-5-bit configs preserve near-base statistics. Cosine similarity stays high until Q2_K; Euclidean distance is comparatively insensitive.
- **Q and K projections are consistently the most sensitive; V and O stay stable.** Per-layer KL divergence (Fig. 5) shows K and Q dominating the distributional shift as bit-width drops, while V and O remain flat except at Q2_K. This pattern recurs across all four models and motivates the authors' call for structure-aware precision allocation.
- **Actionable precision thresholds:** Q4_K is identified as the upper bound of "safe" quantization, Q3_K as the start of degradation, and Q2_K as a breakdown regime — a consistent finding across all evaluated models and metrics.

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are from the University of Manitoba (Dept. of Computer Science), Red River College Polytechnic (Applied Computer Education), and the University of Central Florida (AI Initiative). The work is a self-contained empirical study with a stated Python implementation included in the submission and runs on a documented 8× V100-SXM2 (32 GB) setup. The framing is conservative: the paper explicitly positions CA as a *complement* to (not replacement for) accuracy/perplexity, and the Table 1 comparison positions the contribution against recent quantization-evaluation works (Jin et al. 2024, Kurtic et al. 2025, Li et al. 2025, Husom et al. 2025, Güngör et al. 2025, Zhan et al. 2025, Gong et al. 2024), claiming it is the first to combine statistics, divergence, multi-bit sweep, layer-wise sensitivity, behavioral evaluation, and conventional performance metrics in one analysis.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The correctness-agreement metric is the genuine contribution — decision-overlap metrics are uncommon in quantization eval and the observation that CA ≪ accuracy even at Q8_0 is a sharp, useful finding. The statistical/divergence analysis itself is fairly standard (moments + KL/KS on weight histograms) and overlaps with existing weight-distribution diagnostics.
- **Rigor — weak/absent baselines:** The behavioral evaluation only compares a base model against *its own* llama.cpp quantized variants. Major PTQ methods actually used in practice — GPTQ, AWQ, SmoothQuant, SqueezeLLM, SPQR — are cited in related work but **none are evaluated on CA**. So "quantization breaks behavioral consistency" is really a claim about legacy/K-quant in llama.cpp, not quantization writ large. The title/generalization slightly overclaims relative to what was measured.
- **Rigor — benchmark coverage is thin and conflicts with the abstract:** The abstract and intro emphasize that behavioral preservation "extends beyond task-level accuracy to include factual knowledge, reasoning robustness, and ... safety-related behaviors," and even cite RealToxicityPrompts and HaluEval — but the paper evaluates **no safety/toxicity/factuality benchmark at all**. Only three commonsense benchmarks (HellaSwag, Winogrande, ARC) are used, and Table 4 reports only a *macro average* across them, hiding per-benchmark behavior. No MMLU, no GSM8K/math, no coding, no long-context.
- **Rigor — perplexity anomalies underexplained:** Q3_K and Q2_K *lowering* perplexity below the base FP model for Llama-3.2-3B and Llama-3.1-8B (Table 2) is a red flag. The authors hand-wave this as "model-dependent and dataset-dependent and does not generalize," but a quantized-and-dequantized model beating its own base on PPL warrants investigation (e.g., is llama.cpp applying a calibration/rounding that smooths the distribution? Is the dequantized weight matrix being scored in a way that benefits the KL of next-token probabilities?). The CA argument does not actually need this anomaly, so leaning on it ("perplexity is not reliable") is rhetorically convenient but mechanistically unexplained.
- **Reproducibility:** Good on paper — named models, named quant configs, named datasets, named hardware, code "included in the submission." However, the variance columns in Tables 2-4 are large (e.g., Llama-3.2-3B ARC/HS/WG accuracy 55.5±15.02 at base, 48.7±8.43 at Q2_K), suggesting small sample sizes or high per-benchmark variance that is averaged away; no sample counts or seeds are reported.
- **Significance:** The Q/K-sensitivity and "Q4_K safe / Q2_K broken" thresholds are practically useful rules of thumb for practitioners using llama.cpp, but because no GPTQ/AWQ variants were tested it is unclear whether the layer-sensitivity ranking transfers to other PTQ families.
- **Red flags:** (1) safety/factuality claims in the abstract with zero corresponding experiments; (2) Table ?? cross-references in the appendix text (broken reference labels) suggest the manuscript was not fully proofread; (3) hardware (V100) is two generations old for a mid-2026 paper, though acceptable for a CPU-style quantization tool study.

## Strengths

- The correctness-agreement framing is a genuinely useful diagnostic that surfaces behavioral drift invisible to accuracy/perplexity, and the bound CA ≤ min(Acc) is a clean theoretical anchor.
- The 8-bit-to-2-bit controlled sweep across two quantization families on four models is a broad, well-organized empirical surface.
- The Q/K vs V/O sensitivity finding is consistent across all models and is directly actionable for mixed-precision quantization research.
- Honest about perplexity's failure as a proxy, and conservative in positioning CA as a complement rather than a replacement.

## Limitations

- **No modern PTQ baselines:** only llama.cpp legacy + K-quant; no GPTQ/AWQ/SmoothQuant/SqueezeLLM behavioral comparison, so generalization is limited.
- **Benchmark breadth:** three commonsense tasks only; no math/reasoning, MMLU, coding, long-context, or any safety/factuality eval despite the abstract promising them.
- **Macro-averaged results:** Table 4 hides per-benchmark breakdowns; given the large reported variances, per-task numbers would strengthen the CA claim.
- **Unexplained perplexity anomalies** where quantized models beat the FP base.
- **Older hardware (V100)** and no latency/memory/energy numbers, so the practical efficiency-accuracy-CA trade-off is not characterized.
- **CA is upper-bounded by accuracy**, so part of the accuracy-CA gap is structural rather than purely "lost decisions"; the paper could decompose how much of the gap is the accuracy ceiling vs genuine disagreement on which examples are correct.

## Relevance

对关注 LLM 高效推理与量化部署的研究者，本文提供的"正确性一致性"指标和逐层 (Q/K vs V/O) 敏感性结论，直接可作为量化方案设计与混合精度策略的评估工具，尤其契合量化模型行为一致性、安全对齐保持方面的兴趣。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the Q/K-most-sensitive finding and the Q4_K-safe / Q2_K-broken threshold transfer to GPTQ, AWQ, and SmoothQuant, or is it specific to llama.cpp's block-wise schemes?
- How does correctness agreement behave on harder benchmarks (MMLU-Pro, GPQA, GSM8K, long-context tasks) where accuracy headroom is smaller?
- Can the statistical/divergence layer signatures predict *which* examples will flip between base and quantized model, enabling selective high-precision allocation rather than uniform mixed-precision?
- What drives the anomaly where Q3_K/Q2_K *lowers* perplexity below the FP base — is it a dequantization smoothing effect, and does it correlate with the examples where CA drops?
- Since CA is bounded by min(Acc_base, Acc_quant), how much of the accuracy-CA gap is the ceiling artifact vs genuine disagreement, and is there an agreement metric normalized for accuracy that better isolates behavioral drift?
