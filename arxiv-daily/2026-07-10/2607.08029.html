Rethinking Small VLM Quantization: From Component-Wise Analysis to Hardware-Aware Edge Deployment

arXiv: 2607.08029

Date: 2026-07-09

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08029

一句话总结： 本文在 Jetson Orin NX/AGX 两类边缘 SoC 上，对 5 个 3B 以内的视觉-语言模型（Qwen3-VL-2B、DeepSeek-VL2-Tiny、PaliGemma2-3B、LLaVA-OV-0.5B、Kosmos-2.5）按视觉编码器/投影层/LLM 骨干三组件进行 INT4/INT8 量化消融，发现量化敏感性主要由结构范式（MoE vs Dense）而非参数规模决定，SigLIP 编码器在 BitsAndBytes INT8 下出现部署专属的 2.4–4.9× 延迟异常，且 LLM INT4 省约 47% 显存却让 TPOT 上升最高 56%。
TL;DR: This ICML 2026 workshop paper runs the first controlled, component-wise (vision encoder / projector / LLM) quantization study of five sub-3B VLMs on Jetson Orin NX and AGX, evaluated on the MME benchmark. INT4-quantizing only the LLM backbone (cfg1) yields opposite effects by structure: MoE models Qwen3-VL-2B and DeepSeek-VL2-Tiny actually gain +56.04 / +39.50 MME points on NX, while dense models degrade (PaliGemma2 -62.12, LLaVA-OV-0.5B -220.02 vs FP16 baseline), refuting a scale-only hypothesis. BitsAndBytes LLM INT4 cuts VRAM by up to 47.5% (Qwen3-VL: 4.06→2.13 GB) but raises TPOT by up to +55.8% (111.1→173.1 ms), and SigLIP vision INT8 inflates vision-encoder latency 2.4–4.9× without accuracy loss — a deployment-specific kernel/encoder/Ampere interaction rather than a SigLIP flaw.

核心贡献

  • 首个组件级受控量化消融框架：首次将 VLM 拆为视觉编码器、投影层、LLM 骨干三部分独立量化，定义 6 种配置（cfg0–cfg5，附录补 cfg6），同时评测精度、显存、组件级延迟（LV/LP/TPOT）与每焦耳智能（IPJ）四个维度。
  • 结构范式主导敏感性的实证：通过 MoE（Qwen3-VL、DeepSeek-VL2）与 Dense（PaliGemma2、LLaVA-OV）对照，证伪"参数越小越脆弱"的 H1，修正为"结构范式（MoE vs Dense）决定量化敏感性，规模仅为同族内的加重因子"（H1rev）。
  • 定位 SigLIP-BitsAndBytes-Ampere 三方交互的延迟异常：用 Nsight Systems 内核级分析揭示 SigLIP INT8 路径被拆成"量化→INT8 GEMM→反量化→同步"的碎片化循环，量化/反量化内核占 INT8 时间的 22.8–25.7%，内核启动次数上升 7.4×。
  • 跨硬件可迁移性结论：在 NX/AGX 上证明 MME 精度排序平台无关（Qwen3-VL > DeepSeek-VL2 > PaliGemma2 > LLaVA-OV > Kosmos-2.5），但延迟、功耗与 IPJ 强依赖平台带宽，为模态/硬件感知的精度分配策略提供工程指南。
  • 复现工具开源：发布端到端剖析工具（含模块化量化框架），代码见 https://anonymous.4open.science/r/EdgeQuant-VLMEvalKit/README.md。

Key Contributions

  • First controlled component-wise quantization ablation: Separately quantizes the vision encoder, projector, and LLM backbone across six configurations (cfg0–cfg5, plus cfg6 in the appendix), jointly profiling accuracy, VRAM, per-component latency (L_V / L_P / TPOT), and intelligence-per-joule (IPJ).
  • Architecture-paradigm dominates sensitivity (H1 refuted → H1_rev): Using MoE (Qwen3-VL, DeepSeek-VL2) vs. dense (PaliGemma2, LLaVA-OV) controls, shows the INT4 degradation is governed by structural paradigm, not by parameter scale alone.
  • Isolates a deployment-specific SigLIP-BitsAndBytes-Ampere latency anomaly (H2 refined): Nsight Systems kernel traces show the SigLIP INT8 path is restructured into fragmented quant→INT8 GEMM→dequant→sync cycles, with quant/dequant kernels consuming 22.8–25.7% of INT8 kernel time and a 7.4× rise in kernel launches — not an inherent SigLIP or INT8-vision flaw.
  • Cross-hardware transferability (H5): Demonstrates the MME accuracy ranking is platform-invariant across NX and AGX, while latency, power, and IPJ are bandwidth-bound and platform-specific — guiding hardware-aware precision allocation.
  • Open reproducibility tooling: Releases an end-to-end profiling toolkit with a modular quantization framework (anonymous repo provided).

Methodology

The study targets five architecturally diverse sub-3B sVLMs — Qwen3-VL-2B-Instruct (SigLIP-2 / Qwen3 MoE), DeepSeek-VL2-Tiny (SigLIP-So400m / DeepSeek-MoE), PaliGemma2-3B (SigLIP-So400m / Gemma-2 dense), LLaVA-OV-0.5B (SigLIP-So400m / Qwen-2 dense), and Kosmos-2.5 (Pix2Struct-style ViT / decoder-only transformer) — selected via VLMEvalKit / the Open VLM Leaderboard. SigLIP is held as a controlled factor (four of five models use it); Kosmos-2.5 serves as the non-SigLIP control.

Hardware: Jetson Orin NX (16 GB LPDDR5, 102.4 GB/s, 25 W TDP, 1024-core Ampere) and Jetson Orin AGX (64 GB LPDDR5, 204.8 GB/s, 50 W TDP, 2048-core Ampere). The Jetson Orin Nano was excluded after consistent OOM during BitsAndBytes weight conversion.

Quantization design: INT4 is applied only to the LLM (which is ≥70% of parameters) to save VRAM; vision components stay at INT8 or higher to preserve modality alignment. Six configurations isolate single-component effects (cfg1 LLM INT4, cfg2 Proj INT8, cfg4 Vision INT8) and capture composite interactions (cfg3 = cfg1+cfg2, cfg5 = cfg1+cfg4). All quantization uses BitsAndBytes.

Evaluation: Accuracy on the MME benchmark (max 2,800 = perception + cognition; ACC/ACC+ scoring). Latency is decomposed via CUDA synchronization around each component (Algorithm 1), measuring L_V, L_P, and LLM TPOT (prefill excluded). VRAM is peak GetMaxMemoryAllocated. Energy is from tegrastats at 50 ms sampling; IPJ = normalized MME accuracy (Score/2800) per Joule (Eq. in §4.5). All five models run greedy decoding with fixed max-new-tokens (Table 13); each data point averages n ≥ 3 runs. An AWQ W4A16 sanity check (512 Flickr30k calibration samples) and isolated Nsight vision-path profiling (50 iterations) supplement the main results.

Five pre-registered hypotheses (H1–H5) are each tested against dedicated research questions RQ1–RQ5, with explicit falsification / revision decisions.

Results & Highlights

  • MoE vs dense dominates INT4 sensitivity (H1 refuted): Under LLM-only INT4 (cfg1) on MME/NX, MoE backbones improve — Qwen3-VL-2B +56.04 points (2077.82 vs 2021.78 baseline), DeepSeek-VL2-Tiny +39.50 — while dense backbones collapse: PaliGemma2-3B (Gemma-2, dense) -62.12 points, and LLaVA-OV-0.5B (Qwen2-0.5B, dense) -220.02 points (1361.83 → 1141.81 MME on NX). This scale-contradiction (smaller MoE Qwen3 outperforms larger dense PaliGemma2) rejects the scale-only H1.
  • VRAM savings vs TPOT penalty trade-off (H3 confirmed): BitsAndBytes LLM INT4 (cfg1) cuts peak VRAM by 21.8–47.5% but raises TPOT by 10.4–56.3% across platforms. Qwen3-VL on NX: VRAM 4.06 → 2.13 GB (-47.5%), TPOT 111.1 → 173.1 ms (+55.8%); on AGX TPOT 136.2 → 212.4 ms (+55.9%). Energy paradoxically rises for Qwen3-VL (+54.7% NX, +45.3% AGX) but only +1.4–1.9% for the small LLaVA-OV.
  • SigLIP vision-INT8 latency anomaly (H2 refined): Vision INT8 (cfg4) inflates vision-encoder latency 2.43–4.66× for SigLIP-So400m models but accuracy is unchanged or slightly improved. PaliGemma2: 311.9 → 1,203.5 ms (×3.86 NX), 98.5 → 459.1 ms (×4.66 AGX), with ΔMME +7.3. Isolated Nsight profiling reproduces this: PaliGemma2 4.93× (AGX) / 4.32× (NX), whereas the non-SigLIP Kosmos-2.5 control slows only 1.19× (AGX) / 1.16× (NX). Kernel analysis attributes 22.8–25.7% of INT8 kernel time to quant/dequant with a 7.4× kernel-launch increase.
  • Composite quantization is largely additive, except the modality-alignment path (H4 nuanced): For cfg3 (Proj INT8 + LLM INT4), residuals vs. the linear sum of cfg1+cfg2 fall within ±4 points (near-additive) for all models. For cfg5 (Vision INT8 + LLM INT4), behavior is architecture-dependent: PaliGemma2 residual -16.33 (sub-additive), DeepSeek-VL2 +8.03 (super-additive).
  • Platform-invariant accuracy, platform-specific efficiency (H5): MME ranking is identical on NX and AGX (Qwen3-VL > DeepSeek-VL2 > PaliGemma2 > LLaVA-OV > Kosmos-2.5). But IPJ varies sharply: Qwen3-VL delivers up to 2.5× higher IPJ on AGX than NX, and AGX's 2× memory bandwidth (204.8 vs 102.4 GB/s) can yield lower average power for Qwen3-VL despite its higher 50 W TDP.
  • AWQ sanity check: AWQ W4A16 on Qwen3-VL-2B (median TPOT 107.5 ms, MME 2020.93) does NOT reproduce the BitsAndBytes TPOT penalty, but also does NOT reduce peak VRAM (4.08 GB) — confirming the TPOT inflation is tied to the BitsAndBytes execution path rather than 4-bit quantization per se.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Genuine and well-scoped. Component-wise mixed-precision studies for VLMs are explicitly noted as rare (citing only Sharshar et al. 2025 as a precursor), and the MoE-vs-dense framing of INT4 robustness, plus the Nsight-level attribution of the SigLIP anomaly to BitsAndBytes kernel fragmentation, go beyond standard end-to-end VLM quantization benchmarks.
  • Rigor: Strong on experimental hygiene — identical VLMEvalKit prompts, greedy decoding held fixed across configs, within-model ΔMME design that avoids decoding confounds, leaderboard cross-check, and explicit hypothesis falsification rather than post-hoc narrative. However, all quantitative sensitivity claims rest on a single benchmark (MME); the ordering and additivity conclusions might not transfer to OCR-heavy, chart, or video tasks where component sensitivities differ.
  • Reproducibility: Good but imperfect. Configs, hyperparameters (Table 13), hardware specs (Table 3), and an Algorithm-1 pseudocode are provided, with an open profiling tool at an anonymous repo (no persistent DOI/versioned release). The Jetson Nano OOM exclusion and the note that DeepSeek-VL2 isolated vision numbers are not strictly comparable to PaliGemma/Kosmos are honestly disclosed but limit direct cross-model kernel comparison.
  • Significance: Practically valuable for edge-AI engineers — the take-away that "BitsAndBytes LLM INT4 is a VRAM saver, not a latency saver" and that vision INT8 should be avoided for SigLIP on Ampere is directly actionable. Conceptually, it is a workshop paper: the hypotheses are largely observational/empirical rather than proposing a new quantization algorithm.
  • Red flags / specific weaknesses: (1) The headline "+56 MME from INT4" for Qwen3-VL is surprising and counterintuitive — the paper attributes it to MoE noise resilience but offers no ablation isolating the MoE routing/gate (MoE-Gate is only 0.9M params per Table 4), leaving open whether the gain is signal or evaluation noise within MME's ±~30 point run-to-run band; the n ≥ 3 averaging is reported only as bar charts (Appendix C/D), with no variance/significance figures in the main tables. (2) The strongest "dense degrades" case, LLaVA-OV-0.5B at -220.02, is the only sub-1B dense model, so the dense-vs-MoE contrast is partly confounded with the ultra-small-scale regime the authors themselves flag as fragile. (3) BitsAndBytes is a notably weak/dequantization-heavy backend; an AWQ-only reader could not conclude the same TPOT story, so several "findings" are really "BitsAndBytes-on-Ampere findings," which the paper acknowledges but the framing sometimes generalizes.

Strengths

  • Clean hypothesis-driven structure (H1–H5 → RQ1–RQ5) with explicit accept/refine/reject decisions — uncommon in this subfield.
  • Multi-axis profiling (accuracy + VRAM + decomposed latency + power + energy + IPJ) on two heterogeneous edge SoCs, not just a server GPU.
  • Honest negative/nuanced findings: vision INT8 looks "free" on accuracy but is a latency trap; VRAM savings do not buy speed; cfg5 non-additivity is architecture-specific.
  • Strong diagnostic depth — Nsight kernel breakdown (quant/dequant share, launch inflation) and a non-BitsAndBytes AWQ control strengthen causal claims.
  • Practical, deployable guidance for modality- and hardware-aware precision allocation.

Limitations

  • Single benchmark (MME only): generalization of the architecture-sensitivity and additivity claims to other VLM tasks (MMBench, MMMU, OCR, video) is untested.
  • Backend-bound to BitsAndBytes: the TPOT penalty and SigLIP anomaly are shown (via AWQ) to be backend-specific; conclusions may not hold for GPTQ/AWQ/SmoothQuant or hardware-native FP8/W8A8 — which the authors flag as future work.
  • No statistical significance / variance reporting in main tables despite n ≥ 3 runs; the surprising MoE gains under INT4 are not stress-tested for run-to-run noise.
  • Confounded contrasts: dense-vs-MoE and (for LLaVA-OV) sub-1B-scale factors are entangled, weakening the H1_rev conclusion.
  • Excluded platform (Jetson Nano) due to OOM limits the SoC-coverage claim.
  • Workshop-length scope: no proposed new quantization method or learned precision-allocation policy (NAS) — these are left to future work.

Relevance

该论文与"边缘端轻量化多模态模型部署 + 量化压缩"方向高度相关：其组件级量化敏感性结论（MoE 抗噪、SigLIP-BitsAndBytes 延迟陷阱、省显存≠省延迟）可直接指导我们在 Jetson/移动 SoC 上为视觉编码器、投影层、LLM 分别配置 INT4/INT8/FP16 的混合精度策略，并提示在选择量化后端（BitsAndBytes vs AWQ）时需权衡显存与延迟目标。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do the MoE-INT4 gains (Qwen3-VL +56, DeepSeek-VL2 +39.5 MME) survive on broader benchmarks (MMMU, MMBench, OCRBench, video-QA), and are they statistically significant given MME's run-to-run variance?
  • Which MoE sub-component (expert weights vs. shared router/gate vs. attention) drives INT4 noise resilience — can gate-only FP8 preservation replicate the benefit at lower cost?
  • Would a hardware-native W8A8 or FP8 path on Jetson Orin eliminate both the BitsAndBytes TPOT penalty and the SigLIP vision-INT8 fragmentation, and at what accuracy cost?
  • Is the cfg5 (Vision+LLM) non-additivity, seen most strongly in PaliGemma2 (-16.33) but super-additive in DeepSeek-VL2 (+8.03), predictable from projector type (linear vs. MLP) or from the modality-alignment pathway's Lipschitz properties?
  • Can the IPJ gap between NX and AGX (up to 2.5× for Qwen3-VL) be closed on the cheaper NX via activation sparsification, KV-cache quantization, or dynamic expert offloading rather than wider memory bandwidth?
