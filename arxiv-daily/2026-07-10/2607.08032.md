> **一句话总结：** 本文将 KV-cache 压缩、prompt 压缩、架构化状态压缩与 agent 记忆四条原本割裂的研究线，统一建模为同一个率–失真（rate–distortion）问题，并基于 Fano 不等式给出层无关的下界，证明"查询无关且不可逆的丢弃"在所有层都以同一表达式失败；据此提出 COMPACT-Bench 并在单张 RTX 4060 上跑出参考实验，结果显示预算一旦低于任务信息量精度就崩溃、而可逆记忆在重复压缩下保持 ~0.95 召回、不可逆的摘要式方法掉到 0.33–0.56。

> **TL;DR:** This survey argues that KV-cache eviction, prompt/context compression, architectural state bounding, and agent-memory consolidation are four faces of one rate–distortion decision, and derives a layer-agnostic Fano lower bound showing that query-agnostic, irreversible compaction fails the same way everywhere. It builds a seven-axis taxonomy spanning ~70 methods, ports mechanisms across layers (e.g., Ada-KV's output-error bound becomes a summarization stop rule), proposes COMPACT-Bench to hold one bytes-per-token budget axis across all four layers, and runs a reference experiment on a commodity RTX 4060 with Qwen2.5-1.5B showing accuracy on Needle-in-a-Haystack collapses to near-zero once the budget drops below ~25% of the full KV cache (over 1,395 generations), while under repeated compaction a reversible retrieve operator holds ~0.95 fact recall versus 0.33–0.56 for irreversible summarization — a ~0.5 recall gap that no single-turn benchmark can expose.

## 核心贡献

- **统一的形式化框架**：把内存压缩定义为 compaction operator `C_θ: H → Z` 加 usage operator `U`，设计目标是一个带预算约束 `rate(Z) ≤ B` 的率–失真优化（Eq. 1），并给出其信息瓶颈（IB）形式 `max I(Z;Y|Q) s.t. I(Z;H) ≤ B`，使四种层的压缩方法第一次可以放在同一坐标上比较。
- **层无关的下界**：用数据处理不等式 + Fano 不等式推出 `P_e ≥ (H(Y|Q) − B − 1)/log|Y|`（Eq. 2），表明低于任务信息量 `I*(Q)` 时每一层都必然出错，且压缩比 ∝ 1/I*(Q)；该界把 `Θ(nd)` 精确注意力空间下界、固定状态 RNN 不可检索等已知不可能性结果都纳入同一解释。
- **七维分类法 + ~70 方法的总表**：granularity、lifecycle stage、lossiness/fidelity、query-adaptivity、learnability、mechanism、storage substrate，并指出真正决定方法成败的是 granularity × lifecycle × query-adaptivity 三个轴。
- **推理 ↔ agent 记忆的"桥"**：明确把三个时间尺度（sub-second KV cache、within-task 工作上下文、跨会话长期存储）对齐到同一套 5 个设计旋钮（importance signal、forgetting、query-conditioning、reversibility、stop rule），并把 Memory-Bank 的遗忘曲线、Quest 的"只检索不驱逐"、Ada-KV 的输出误差界分别移植到其它层。
- **COMPACT-Bench 提案**：扩展 SCBench 和 KVPress，新增共享 bytes-per-token-of-history（BPT）预算轴、三个新任务族（loss attribution / reversibility / calibrated confidence），并强制报告 accuracy、latency、memory、dollar cost 在同一张表上；首次使"重复压缩退化"成为被测对象。
- **参考实验**：在单卡 RTX 4060（8GB）+ Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct 上验证两个可证伪预测——精度随预算的崩溃，以及重复压缩下可逆 vs 不可逆算子的发散。
- **5 条 compaction-aware 设计原则 + 7 项优先研究议程**：从 P1（永不不可逆地丢弃无法廉价重构的内容）到 P5（按边际任务效用分配预算，而非均匀比例）。

## Key Contributions

- **A single compaction objective + layer-agnostic lower bound (Eq. 2).** Casts every method as solving `min E[ℓ(U(C_θ(H),Q),Y)] s.t. rate(Z) ≤ B`, and proves via Fano that once the budget `B` drops below the task-conditioned information content `I*(Q)`, no layer (KV, prompt, recurrent state, or agent store) can escape an error rate set by the same expression.
- **A seven-axis taxonomy** that uniformly classifies ~70 methods across the four layers, with a master table isolating granularity, lifecycle, and query-adaptivity as the decisive axes.
- **A layer-by-layer reading through the formalism** (Sections 4–9): KV cache (evict/quantize/low-rank/merge), prompt/context (hard NL vs soft latent), architectural (recurrence/SSM/associative), agent/semantic (curate/consolidate/retrieve), trainable sparse attention (NSA, MoBA, DSA), and multimodal/multi-agent.
- **An explicit inference↔agent-memory bridge (Section 10)** that ports three concrete mechanisms across tiers — Memory-Bank's Ebbinghaus decay curve becomes a KV eviction prior; Quest's no-evict retrieval discipline becomes an agent-memory design; Ada-KV's output-error bound becomes the missing stop rule for summarization — and yields five compaction-aware design principles.
- **COMPACT-Bench proposal** extending SCBench/KVPress with one shared bytes-per-token-of-history axis, three task families that probe loss-attribution, reversibility, and calibrated confidence, and a joint accuracy/latency/memory/cost report — the first protocol designed to measure repeated-compaction degradation.
- **A reproducible reference experiment** (Sections 13–14) on a single 8 GB RTX 4060 with Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct that empirically validates two falsifiable predictions of the bound.
- **A prioritized seven-item research agenda** (Section 15) anchored to the bound and the three properties (P-rev, P-q, P-fid).

## Methodology

This is an analytical/position survey, not an empirical paper. The method is four-step. **(1) Formalization (Section 2):** the authors define a compaction scheme as the pair `(C_θ, U)`, where `C_θ: H ↦ Z` produces a compact representation obeying `rate(Z) ≤ B` (the "currency" of rate differs per layer: GPU bytes for KV, tokens for prompts, state dimensions for recurrent models, store size for agents), and `U` consumes `Z` to produce an output. The design problem becomes the rate–distortion program of Eq. (1), equivalently the information-bottleneck `max I(Z;Y|Q) s.t. I(Z;H) ≤ B`. **(2) Lower bound (Eq. 2):** because for a query-agnostic `C` the chain `Y − H − Z` is Markov given `Q`, the data-processing inequality plus Fano's inequality yields `P_e ≥ (H(Y|Q) − B − 1)/log|Y|` whenever `B < I*(Q)`. Three consequences follow: every layer must err below `I*(Q)`; achievable compression ratio scales as `1/I*(Q)`; and a query-agnostic operator pays an extra `H(Q)` bits. **(3) Taxonomy + survey (Sections 3–9):** the bound's implicit variables are promoted to seven axes, and ~70 methods are classified uniformly, with the heterogeneous "importance" heuristics (attention mass, perplexity, mutual information, output-error bound, LLM-judged salience) re-read as competing distortion surrogates for the same quantity. **(4) Bridge + benchmark + experiment (Sections 10, 13, 14):** three tiers of a memory hierarchy are aligned onto five shared design knobs, mechanisms are ported across tiers, and a small reference experiment validates predictions (1) and (4) of the bound on commodity hardware.

The reference experiment uses Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct on an 8 GB NVIDIA RTX 4060. **Experiment 1** sweeps six KV-compaction methods (SnapKV, StreamingLLM, TOVA, Knorm, expected-attention, and a random-eviction control) over five budgets on Needle-in-a-Haystack with natural Wikitext filler at 2k–8k tokens and depths 10–90%, averaging over 1,395 generations. **Experiment 2** simulates an agent reading a long document in chunks, accumulating 12 key–value facts, and periodically compacting working memory; it contrasts an irreversible LLM-summarize operator against a reversible retrieve-from-archive operator (the Quest/MemGPT-archival discipline), measuring recall of all 12 facts at the end.

## Results & Highlights

- **Accuracy–budget cliff (Experiment 1, prediction 1).** On Needle-in-a-Haystack with Qwen2.5-1.5B, the full KV cache answers perfectly (accuracy = 1.00); every method traces a frontier toward zero, and below roughly **a quarter (~25%) of the full budget all six KV-compaction methods sit at or near 0% accuracy** — exactly the Fano floor where the budget drops under the needle's concentrated `I*(Q)` (1,395 generations averaged).
- **Reversible vs irreversible under repeated compaction (Experiment 2, prediction 4).** As the number of compaction events grows from 5 to 25, the **reversible retrieve operator holds fact recall near 0.95 at every frequency**, while the **irreversible summarization operator drops to 0.33–0.56** and is weakest at the highest compaction frequency; the two operators look identical under single-turn evaluation but **part by roughly 0.5 in recall** once memory is reused.
- **Cross-layer comparability via the BPT axis.** Putting eviction (fraction `f` of tokens costs `f` of full per-token KV bytes), `b`-bit quantization (`b/16`), and `m`-of-`n` prompt compression on one bytes-per-token-of-history axis makes a KV evictor, a quantizer, and an agent summary directly comparable — the random-eviction control isolates exactly how much each scorer's signal buys.
- **Quantization is near the information-theoretic limit.** Quantization reportedly lands **within ~2.7× of the information-theoretic limit**, and practice routinely achieves **80–93% KV reductions** and **~20× prompt compression** on realistic (redundant) data, where `I*(Q)` is far below the worst-case `Θ(nd)`.
- **Reported per-method KV savings** (not directly comparable across setups): H2O ~5×, SnapKV ~8×, PyramidKV ~8×, KIVI ~2.6×, KVQuant ~5×, Palu ~2×, xKV up to 8×, YOCO roughly an `L`-fold factor; MLA matches/beats MHA at a far smaller cache and is the de facto standard for new frontier models.
- **Existing suites over-rate methods.** RULER finds barely half of models advertising ≥32K context survive at 32K; BABILong reports models exploit only 10–20% of their stated window; LongMemEval sees ~30% drops on sustained interaction; NoLiMa shows literal-match NIAH probes flatter quality, so COMPACT-Bench mandates latent-association needles.

## Authors & Credibility

The paper has two authors, both at the University of California, Irvine (Ashwin Gerard Colaco, Nada Lahjouji). It is a position/analytical survey (arXiv:2607.08032v1, cs.LG, dated 9 Jul 2026) with a clearly stated scope (compaction of context-derived memory, work from 2023–2026, excluding weight-level pruning/quantization and unbounded long-context training). The authors explicitly flag that much of the cited literature is recent and unrefereed, and that they "treat preprint numbers as provisional and flag results that have not been reproduced." The reference experiment is honestly framed as a methodology demonstration ("we are not chasing state of the art"), runs on commodity hardware with code and configurations promised alongside the survey, and is positioned as inviting replication at larger scale. The bibliography is large (~148 references) and spans the four communities the survey claims to unify.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The unification thesis itself is partly anticipated: the authors acknowledge two prior surveys (the Forms/Functions/Dynamics scheme of [47] and the write–manage–read POMDP of [31]). The paper is explicit (Table 1) that its distinguishing contributions are the *single optimization objective with a provable bound*, coverage reaching the serving stack and quantization/low-rank math, explicit cross-layer mechanism transfer, and the benchmark it both defines and runs. The Fano bound (Eq. 2), however, is essentially a standard application of data-processing + Fano to a well-known IB setup — the novelty is in *applying* it uniformly across the four layers and naming the heterogeneous heuristics as estimators of one quantity, not in the information-theoretic machinery itself.
- **Rigor:** The bound is correct but conservative and worst-case; the authors are admirably candid that "every result above is worst-case or mechanistic, and none predicts the 80–93% KV reductions and ~20× prompt compression that practice routinely achieves," and that closing that gap (a predictive `I*(Q)` scaling law) "is the central unsolved problem this survey leaves open." The seven-axis taxonomy is reasonable but the axes are not provably orthogonal (the authors hedge with "as close to orthogonal as the design space allows").
- **Reproducibility:** The reference experiment is on a single small model (Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct) on one 8 GB consumer GPU, with only two studies and modest effect sizes the authors themselves describe as "the gaps are modest at this scale." Code/configs are promised but the experiment is explicitly a methodology demo, not a benchmark result — a strong critic could argue the headline curves (Figure 5, Figure 6) are underpowered and not yet shown to carry over to frontier scale.
- **Significance:** If the community adopts the BPT budget axis and the COMPACT-Bench protocol (especially the repeated-compaction test), the survey could reshape how compaction is measured; the inference↔agent-memory bridge (Section 10) and the five design principles are the most actionable artifacts. Significance hinges on adoption, which is not yet demonstrated.
- **Red flags / specific weak points:** (1) The central quantitative claim — that irreversible summarization compounds error super-linearly — is supported only by a 12-fact toy experiment with a single summarizer LLM, no comparison of *different* summarizers, and no measurement of the actual super-linear exponent (only the qualitative curve in Figure 6). (2) COMPACT-Bench is a *proposal*; the paper runs only "a first version of this protocol on the KV and agent layers," so the multi-budget, cross-layer, repeated-compaction claims of Table 5 are largely aspirational. (3) The reduction ratios cited for individual methods (H2O ~5×, SnapKV ~8×, etc.) are copied from original papers under heterogeneous setups and explicitly disclaimed as "not directly comparable" — yet they are still tabulated (Table 3), which invites exactly the comparison the authors warn against. (4) The missing stop-rule for agent summarization (the "open" cell in Table 4) is identified as a research opportunity but the Ada-KV-bound-based proposal is not tested.

## Strengths

- A genuinely unifying lens: the single rate–distortion objective plus the (P-rev)/(P-q)/(P-fid) property triple lets a reader see H2O, LLMLingua, Mamba, and Mem0 as points in one design space, which no prior survey achieves with a provable bound.
- The inference↔agent-memory bridge (Section 10) is the most original section, with three concrete, falsifiable mechanism ports and a candid statement of where the analogy breaks (recall guarantees, query meaning, and cost all differ across tiers).
- The measurement-gap diagnosis is sharp and well-targeted: the observation that "repeated compaction that agents actually perform is almost never measured, and no benchmark holds one budget axis across all the layers at once" is a concrete, fixable problem, and COMPACT-Bench is a credible response.
- Intellectual honesty throughout: preprint numbers flagged as provisional, the worst-case-vs-practice gap openly admitted, the toy scale of the reference experiment disclosed, and the absence of a predictive `I*(Q)` law named as the central open problem rather than glossed over.
- Strong coverage of the 2025–2026 production frontier (NSA, MoBA, DeepSeek DSA, MLA/TransMLA, ShadowKV, SCOPE, sleep-time compute), which keeps the survey current.

## Limitations

- It is a survey/position paper: the formal bound is standard information theory applied uniformly, not a new theorem; the empirical evidence is two small-scale toy studies, not a benchmark evaluation.
- COMPACT-Bench is proposed but not built out — the full protocol (multi-budget frontier, three task families, joint cost reporting) is described; only "a first version" on KV and agent layers is actually run.
- The headline repeated-compaction result rests on a single 12-fact retrieval toy with one summarizer LLM; the predicted super-linear growth is shown qualitatively, not fit to an exponent, and is not tested across summarizer families or frontier models.
- Scope exclusions are significant: weight-level compression (pruning, distillation, weight quantization) and unbounded long-context training are out of scope, so the unification does not extend to the whole efficiency literature.
- Per-method reduction numbers in Table 3 are copied from heterogeneous original setups and disclaimed as non-comparable, yet their tabulation risks being read as a leaderboard.
- Two-author provenance and heavy reliance on unrefereed preprints (2023–2026) mean some load-bearing claims (e.g., 80–93% KV reductions, ~20× prompt compression) are aggregates of provisional results.

## Relevance

与"长上下文 / agent 记忆压缩"主题高度相关：本文正好把 KV-cache 压缩、prompt 压缩、架构化状态压缩与 agent 记忆这四类日常关注的效率技术纳入同一率–失真框架，并指出"可逆 + 查询条件化"是跨层通用的设计准则——对评估和选择长上下文模型、设计 agent 记忆系统、以及规划压缩评测都有直接指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the predicted super-linear growth of error under repeated irreversible summarization hold at frontier scale (e.g., 70B-class models, hundreds of compaction events), and what is the actual exponent — does it match the Fano floor or admit a tighter, summarizer-specific rate?
- Can `I*(Q)` be estimated cheaply *before* compaction runs, yielding the "predictive compression scaling law" the authors name as the central open problem — e.g., from attention-head/layer structure and context length?
- How do the five design principles (especially P3's reversible-episodic + lossy-semantic two-tier, and P5's marginal-utility budgeting) interact when stacked: does quant × low-rank × evict × merge compose synergistically or destructively within and across layers?
- Can the Ada-KV output-error bound be operationalized as a practical, calibrated stop rule for agent summarization, and does it prevent the self-reinforcing error (poisoned reflections / AgentPoison / MINJA) that Section 7 flags?
- Does the Quest-style "never evict, retrieve on demand" discipline actually dominate in realistic agentic workloads once the retrieval latency and store-cost axes (which COMPACT-Bench proposes to report jointly) are included, or does the microsecond-vs-LLM-call cost asymmetry of Section 10.4 reverse the ranking?
