Tubular Neighbourhoods of Pfaffian Sets and Applications to Neural Networks

arXiv: 2607.08370

Date: 2026-07-09

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08370

一句话总结： 本文将代数簇管状邻域体积界的理论推广到光滑 Pfaffian 超曲面，借由 Khovanskii 定理与 Gauss 映射度数界，得到神经网络分类器（使用 sigmoid/tanh 等 Pfaffian 激活）鲁棒性条件数的概率尾界；并对单隐藏层有理权重 sigmoid 网络给出了宽度多项式阶（而非 Khovanskii 指数阶）的管体积界与 O(w^n/t) 尾衰减。
TL;DR: The authors generalise tubular-neighbourhood volume bounds from algebraic varieties to smooth Pfaffian hypersurfaces (via Khovanskii's theorem and Gauss-map degree bounds) and apply them to derive uniform and Gaussian tail bounds on a neural-network classifier's condition number; for single-hidden-layer sigmoid networks with rational weights they replace the exponential-in-width Khovanskii factor 2^{h(h-1)/2} with a polynomial-in-width bound (Theorem 4.15 / Corollary 5.8), yielding an O(w^n/t) tail rate (Corollary 4.18) with a matching Ω(w^n) lower bound on the Gauss-map degree (Proposition 4.11).

核心贡献

  • Pfaffian 超曲面管体积公式（Theorem 3.6）： 对光滑有界 Pfaffian 超曲面 V = Z(f)，格式 (α,β,s)，给出 ε-管状邻域概率界 P{d(X,V) ≤ ε} ≤ C_{α,β,s,n}[(1+(α+β+1)ε/ρ)^n − (1+ε/ρ)^n]，其中常数 C_{α,β,s,n} = 6·2^{s(s−1)/2}·β^n·(n(2α+β−1)+β+1)^s。该公式在 s=0（代数情形）退化为 Lotz [Lot15] 的代数版本。
  • 神经网络条件数尾界（Section 5）： 将管界应用于半 Pfaffian 决策边界 Σ，定义分类器条件数 C(x)=‖x‖/dist(x,Σ)，得到均匀分布（Theorem 5.3）与高斯扰动（Theorem 5.4）下 P{C_p(X)>t} 的 O(1/t) 尾界，直接转化为对随机与半随机扰动的定量鲁棒性保证。
  • 单隐藏层 sigmoid 网络的多项式宽度界（核心结果，Theorem 4.15 / Proposition 4.9 / 4.14）： 对有理权重（公分母 q，格常数 L=q·max|a_{ki}|）的单隐藏层 logistic sigmoid 网络，利用 Bernstein–Kushnirenko–Khovanskii (BKK) 定理与对数多重点换，消去了标准 Pfaffian/Khovanskii 界中的指数因子 2^{h(h−1)/2}，得到 mdeg(V) ≤ C(n,L)·w^n（顶度）与 mdegi(V) ≤ K(n,L)·w^{2n}（所有截面度），从而管界与尾界均为宽度多项式阶。
  • 界的最优性证明（Proposition 4.11）： 通过显式网格构造给出 w=n(m+2) 单位、L=1 的网络，使其零集 Gauss 映射度 mdeg(V) ≥ 2·((m−2)/2)^n = Ω(w^n)，证明 w 的指数 n 是不可改进的（固定 n）。
  • 锐化的主导项（Corollary 4.18）： 利用沿线零点计数（Lemma 4.17，指数多项式至多 (2Lw+1)^n−1 个零点），把管界主导项改进为 2n(2Lw+1)^n·(ε/ρ)，在 ε/ρ = O(w^{−n}) 区域给出 O(w^n·ε/ρ)。
  • 多层情形的形式化猜想（Conjecture 4.13 / 6.1）： 提出块 Khovanskii 界猜想，认为多层 sigmoid 网络（ℓ 层、宽度 n_i）的 mdeg(V_ij) ≤ C(n,L)·(∏n_i)^n，并指出现有 Khovanskii–Rolle 归纳的一维本质与跨层度数复合是主要障碍。

Key Contributions

  • Pfaffian tube formula (Theorem 3.6). For a smooth bounded Pfaffian hypersurface V = Z(f) of format (α,β,s), the probability that a uniform point in B(p,ρ) lies within ε of V is bounded by C_{α,β,s,n}[(1+(α+β+1)ε/ρ)^n − (1+ε/ρ)^n], generalising Lotz's algebraic tube bound [Lot15] (recovered when s=0). The proof combines a Weyl-type tube formula with Gauss-map degree bounds controlled by Khovanskii's theorem.
  • Robustness via condition-number tail bounds (Section 5). Casting classifier robustness as a geometric conditioning problem (C(x)=‖x‖/Δ(x), with Σ the decision boundary), the tube bounds yield P{C_p(X)>t} ≤ (m/2)·C_{α,β,s,n}[(1+(α+β+1)/t)^n − (1+1/t)^n] under both uniform (Theorem 5.3) and Gaussian/smoothed (Theorem 5.4) perturbations, decaying as O(n(2ℓ+2)/t) for large t.
  • Width-polynomial bound for single-hidden-layer sigmoid nets (main novelty; Prop. 4.9, 4.14, Thm 4.15). For rational-weight single-hidden-layer logistic-sigmoid classifiers, a BKK/zonotope-volume argument replaces the exponential Khovanskii prefactor 2^{h(h−1)/2}·(5n+2)^h by a polynomial in w: mdeg(V) ≤ 2·n!(2L)^n·w^n and P{d(X,V)≤ε} ≤ 2K(n,L)w^{2n}[(1+ε/ρ)^n−1].
  • Sharpness of the width exponent (Proposition 4.11). An explicit axis-aligned grid construction produces width-w=n(m+2), lattice-constant L=1 sigmoid networks whose zero-set Gauss-map degree is ≥ 2((m−2)/2)^n = Ω(w^n), showing the exponent n is tight.
  • Refined leading-order tube estimate (Corollary 4.18). A one-dimensional exponential-polynomial zero count (Lemma 4.16/4.17) bounds line-section degree mdeg_0(V) ≤ (2Lw+1)^n−1, giving P{d(X,V)≤ε} ≤ 2n(2Lw+1)^n(ε/ρ) + O(w^{2n}(ε/ρ)^2), i.e. O(w^n·ε/ρ) in the small-perturbation regime.
  • Multi-layer conjectures and obstruction analysis (Conjectures 4.13, 6.1; Section 6). The authors formulate a block-Khovanskii conjecture predicting mdeg(V_ij) ≤ C(n,L)·(∏_i n_i)^n for ℓ-layer sigmoid nets, and diagnose why the standard Khovanskii–Rolle induction (inherently 1-D, with degree compounding across layers) and the sphere obstruction (Remark 4.19) currently block progress.

Methodology

The paper is a self-contained work of real-algebraic / o-minimal geometry with a machine-learning application. The technical pipeline is:

  1. Pfaffian framework (Section 2). Pfaffian functions are defined as functions satisfying a triangular system of first-order PDEs with polynomial coefficients, characterised by a format triple (α, β, s). The section catalogues closure properties (Lemma 2.4: sums/products/derivatives; Lemma 2.6: composition) and lists Pfaffian formats for common activations (sigmoid, tanh, softplus, SiLU, GELU, Mish, Gaussian, arctan) in Table 1, showing networks with smooth activations are themselves Pfaffian with format expressible in terms of depth ℓ, hidden units h, and activation format. Khovanskii's theorem (Theorem 2.10) bounds the number of regular real zeros of a Pfaffian system by 2^{s(s−1)/2}·β_1···β_n·(Σβ_i − n + min{k,s}α + 1)^s.
  1. Gauss-map degree and tube volume (Section 3). The volume of the ε-tubular neighbourhood of a smooth compact Pfaffian complete intersection M = Z(f_1,…,f_c) is bounded by a Weyl-type formula (Theorem 3.2) in terms of the i-th maximal degrees mdeg_i(M) of the generalised Gauss map of generic affine sections. Proposition 3.4 bounds these degrees via Khovanskii applied to the Lagrange-multiplier system f_i=0, Σλ_i∇f_i = v. Combining gives the main tube formula Theorem 3.6, with a codimension-2 boundary correction from the intersection V∩S^{n−1}(p,ρ+ε).
  1. Neural-network specialisation (Section 4). Proposition 4.1 computes the network's Pfaffian format (α_F = ℓ(α+β−1)+α', β_F=β', s_F=sh+s'm). The "off-the-shelf" Pfaffian bound (Corollary 4.7) carries the exponential constant C_{ℓ,h,n,m} ⊃ 2^{h(h−1)/2}. The authors bypass this for single-layer sigmoid nets: under the exponential substitution y_j = e^{−x_j/q}, each sigmoid σ(a_k^⊺x+b_k) becomes a rational function 1/(1+e^{−b_k}y^{p̂_k}) of y, with integer exponent vectors p̂_k = qa_k ∈ Z^n, ∥p̂_k∥_∞ ≤ L. The Gauss-map fibre system then becomes a Laurent-polynomial system whose monomials lie in a zonotope conv(A); Bernstein's theorem bounds its root count by n!·Vol(conv(A)) ≤ n!(2L)^n·w^n (Proposition 4.9). For lower section degrees (i< n−1), where generic affine sections break rationality, the authors pass to a multiplicative chart in which the affine section is encoded by ≤ n logarithms, yielding a Pfaffian chain of length ≤ 2n independent of w, so Khovanskii gives mdeg_i(V) ≤ K(n,L)w^{2n} (Proposition 4.14). Theorem 4.15 combines these into a width-polynomial tube bound.
  1. Robustness tail bounds (Section 5). A union bound over the m(m−1)/2 pairwise decision boundaries V_ij = Z(F_i−F_j) converts tube bounds into condition-number tail bounds. A Fubini reduction (Lemma 5.6) lifts the uniform-ball bound to the Gaussian setting. The single-layer sigmoid case (Corollary 5.8) gives P{C_p(X)>t} ≤ (m/2)·2K(n,L)w^{2n}[(1+1/t)^n−1], with sharp O(w^n/t) leading rate for t ≳ K(n,L)w^n (Remark 5.9). The Gaussian analogue (Proposition 5.10) is a hybrid BKK+Pfaffian bound controlled by a break-even inequality (5.6).
  1. Lower bounds and sharpness. Proposition 4.11 constructs separable networks whose zero sets have Ω(w^n) Gauss-map degree, matching the upper bound's exponent. Lemma 4.10 gives the universal ≥2 lower bound for compact hypersurfaces.

The methodology is purely theoretical (proofs only); there are no experiments, no training, and no empirical benchmarks.

Results & Highlights

This is a theoretical paper; the "results" are proven bounds rather than benchmark scores. The setting replaces a dataset/benchmark with a geometric regime, and the "baseline" is the off-the-shelf Khovanskii/Pfaffian bound. Concrete quantitative claims:

  • Tube-volume probability bound (Theorem 3.6). For a smooth bounded Pfaffian hypersurface of format (α,β,s) and X uniform in B(p,ρ): P{d(X,V) ≤ ε} ≤ C_{α,β,s,n}·[(1+(α+β+1)ε/ρ)^n − (1+ε/ρ)^n], with C_{α,β,s,n} = 6·2^{s(s−1)/2}·β^n·(n(2α+β−1)+β+1)^s. This strictly generalises Lotz's algebraic bound [Lot15] (the s=0 specialisation) to the Pfaffian/transcendental setting. For large ρ/ε the bound decays as C_{α,β,s,n}·n(α+β)·ε/ρ — the O(ε/ρ) rate is essential for deriving the O(1/t) condition-number tails.
  • Gaussian tail bound for general Pfaffian classifiers (Theorem 5.4). For X ~ N(x,σ²I_d) and a classifier with m classes and ℓ hidden layers, h hidden units, (2,1,1)-format activations, softmax output: P{C_x(X)>t} ≤ (m/2)·6·2^{h(h−1)/2}·(n(4ℓ+1)+2)^h·[(1+(2ℓ+3)/t)^n − (1+1/t)^n], decaying for large t as (m/2)·6·2^{h(h−1)/2}·(n(4ℓ+1)+2)^h·n(2ℓ+2)/t. The prefactor is super-exponential in the width h.
  • Width-polynomial replacement for single-hidden-layer sigmoid nets (Proposition 4.9 + Corollary 5.8). For rational first-layer weights (lattice constant L), the top Gauss-map degree is mdeg(V) ≤ 2·n!(2L)^n·w^n. The corresponding uniform tail bound is P{C_p(X)>t} ≤ (m/2)·2K(n,L)·w^{2n}·[(1+1/t)^n − 1], i.e. the Khovanskii prefactor 2^{h(h−1)/2}·(5n+2)^h (exponential in h=w) is replaced by a polynomial of degree 2n in w. In the tail regime t ≳ K(n,L)w^n, Remark 5.9 / Example 5.13 give the sharp leading rate O(w^n/t).
  • Sharp lower bound matching the exponent (Proposition 4.11). For every n≥1 and even m≥4 there exists a single-hidden-layer sigmoid network with w = n(m+2) units and lattice constant L=1 whose smooth compact zero set has Gauss-map degree mdeg(V) ≥ 2·((m−2)/2)^n = Ω(w^n). Hence the exponent n in the upper bound (Proposition 4.9) is tight at fixed lattice constant.
  • Refined leading-order tube estimate (Corollary 4.18). Using a line-section zero count (Lemma 4.17): P{d(X,V)≤ε} ≤ 2n(2Lw+1)^n·(ε/ρ) + O(w^{2n}·(ε/ρ)^2), which is of order O(w^n·ε/ρ) when ε/ρ = O(w^{−n}). This is the sharpest small-perturbation statement and governs the condition-number tail coefficient.
  • Gaussian break-even criterion (Remark 5.11, eq. 5.6–5.7). In the smoothed-analysis setting, the Gaussian tail inherits the polynomial BKK rate only when the Gaussian scale grows with width as σ ≳ R_V·2^{w(w−1)/(2(n+2))}; otherwise the residual Khovanskii term (with constant C_{2,1,w,n} = 6·2^{w(w−1)/2}·(5n+2)^w, super-exponential in w) eventually dominates.

No quantitative empirical results reported — the paper reports no experiments, no training, and no benchmark evaluations; all quantitative claims are proven theoretical inequalities with explicit constants.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty. Genuine and well-scoped. The Pfaffian generalisation of tube bounds (Theorem 3.6) is a clean extension of Lotz [Lot15] via Khovanskii's theorem, and the BKK-based replacement of the exponential Khovanskii factor by a polynomial in w for single-layer sigmoid nets (Proposition 4.9, Theorem 4.15) is the paper's most original contribution. The exponential-to-polynomial improvement is exactly the kind of result the field asks for, and the matching Ω(w^n) lower bound (Proposition 4.11) makes the single-layer story essentially complete.
  • Rigour. High. Proofs are detailed, constants are tracked explicitly (e.g. C_{α,β,s,n} = 6·2^{s(s−1)/2}·β^n·(n(2α+β−1)+β+1)^s), and the authors are careful about genericity/regularity assumptions (Sard's theorem, regular values of the Gauss map) and the limiting arguments that remove them (the ρ+ε genericity removal in Theorem 3.6). Lemma 4.8 is a clean non-degeneracy argument via the implicit function theorem. Sharpness of the Khovanskii bound itself is referenced to [BHH+26].
  • Reproducibility. This is a pure-theory paper with no code, no experiments, and no data — reproducibility means "can a reader reconstruct the proofs," and yes, the arguments are self-contained with full background in Section 2. However, the paper offers no numerical illustration of the bounds (e.g., no plot of the tail bound versus w for concrete n, L), which would have helped a reader gauge practical tightness. The explicit constants are large (e.g. K(n,L) ⊃ n!(2L)^n, and C_{2,1,w,n} = 6·2^{w(w−1)/2}·(5n+2)^w in the residual Gaussian term), so the bounds are asymptotic in flavour and not intended for direct numerical robustness certification.
  • Significance. Solid for the intersection of real-algebraic/o-minimal geometry and learning theory. The robustness application is conceptually clean (decision-boundary geometry ↔ condition-number tails, in the spirit of [BC13]), but the practical ML impact is limited by three restrictions discussed openly in the paper: (i) only single-hidden-layer sigmoid networks get the polynomial bound; multi-layer is an open conjecture (4.13/6.1); (ii) the rational-weights / common-denominator assumption with bounded lattice constant L is strong and not satisfied by trained networks (whose weights are irrational floating-point); (iii) the smoothness/non-vanishing-gradient assumption on V excludes singular decision boundaries, which real networks can exhibit.
  • Red flags / specific concerns.
  • The rational-weight, bounded-L assumption (Proposition 4.9, Theorem 4.15) is the load-bearing hypothesis for the polynomial bound, and the authors are honest that it is a genuine restriction — but they do not discuss how one would verify it for, or approximate it on, a real trained network, nor how the bounds degrade as L grows (K(n,L) ⊃ L^n in Proposition 4.9 and K(n,L) ⊃ (4nL)^{2n} in Proposition 4.14, so large L erodes the advantage over the Khovanskii bound).
  • The sphere obstruction (Remark 4.19) forces the hypothesis V ⊂ B(p,ρ) in Theorem 4.15; dropping it re-introduces an exponential-in-w correction, so the clean polynomial statement depends on a containment assumption whose realism for decision boundaries is not examined.
  • The Gaussian/smoothed bound does not inherit the polynomial rate at fixed Gaussian scale (Remark 5.11): for fixed n and R_V/σ, the Khovanskii term eventually dominates as w→∞, and the break-even requires σ to grow super-exponentially in w (eq. 5.7). This is a substantive caveat to the headline "polynomial-in-width" claim, since the most practically relevant setting (fixed small Gaussian perturbation) is exactly where the improvement fails asymptotically.
  • No comparison with prior neural-robustness bounds (e.g. the Fawzi–Fawzi–Fawzi [FFF18] / Mahloujifar–Diochnos–Mahmoody [MDM19] concentration-of-measure results, or the Zhang–Kileel [ZK25] covering-number generalisation bounds) beyond brief citation: the reader cannot see whether the new bounds are tighter or looser quantitatively in any concrete regime.
  • The two main multi-layer conjectures (4.13, 6.1) are unproven, so the paper's cleanest applied statement is strictly a single-hidden-layer result.

Strengths

  • Clean, self-contained generalisation of tube-volume bounds from algebraic to Pfaffian hypersurfaces, with a full background section making the paper readable by non-specialists in o-minimal geometry.
  • The BKK/zonotope argument that converts the exponential Khovanskii factor into a polynomial in w is elegant, and the matching lower-bound construction (Proposition 4.11) establishes that the width exponent n is sharp — a rare completeness for such bounds.
  • Honest, detailed exposition of obstructions: the sphere obstruction (Remark 4.19), the Gaussian-scale caveat (Remark 5.11, eq. 5.7), and the layered-Rolle obstruction for multi-layer nets (Section 6.1) are all spelled out rather than hidden.
  • Explicit constants throughout, enabling future comparison and potential tightening.
  • A useful table (Table 1) of Pfaffian formats for all common smooth activations, and a clear statement (Proposition 4.1) of how network depth/width map to Pfaffian format.

Limitations

  • The headline polynomial-in-width bound applies only to single-hidden-layer sigmoid networks with rational weights and bounded lattice constant; the multi-layer case (the practically relevant one) is conjectural (Conjectures 4.13, 6.1) and explicitly blocked by the one-dimensional nature of the Rolle lemma and degree compounding across layers.
  • The rational-weights / bounded-L assumption is strong and not met by trained networks; the constants K(n,L) grow with L, so the practical regime where the polynomial bound beats the Khovanskii bound is narrow.
  • The smoothness assumption (non-vanishing ∇f on V) excludes singular decision boundaries; the paper notes (Section 6.2) that extending to singular Pfaffian sets is blocked by the absence of a Pfaffian analogue of polar varieties / complexification.
  • Gaussian tail does not inherit the polynomial rate at fixed perturbation scale (Remark 5.11) — a major caveat for the smoothed-analysis reading of the results.
  • No experiments, no numerical examples, no comparison with concentration-of-measure or covering-number robustness bounds in any concrete regime; the bounds are asymptotic and their constants are large.
  • The decision boundary is analysed as a union of pairwise level sets V_ij ⊇ Σ (eq. 5.1), which is generally a strict superset of Σ, so the bounds control proximity to a larger set than the true decision boundary — a (mild) looseness built into the method.

Relevance

对关注神经网络鲁棒性理论几何刻画（条件数、决策边界几何、对抗/随机扰动概率界）的方向高度相关：它把经典的管体积—条件数框架从代数网络严格推广到 sigmoid/tanh 等 Pfaffian 激活网络，并给出单隐藏层情形下宽度多项式阶的紧界，是理解深度网络鲁棒性的实代数/o-minimal 几何工具的重要补充；若研究兴趣偏经验鲁棒性或多层网络，则需注意其单层、有理权重、光滑性等假设的限制。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can Conjecture 4.13 (mdeg(V_ij) ≤ C(n,L)·(∏_i n_i)^n for ℓ-layer sigmoid nets) be proved for ℓ=2 by extending the BKK/zonotope argument, or does sigmoid composition genuinely introduce transcendentality that defeats Bernstein's theorem beyond one layer?
  • How do the bounds degrade as the lattice constant L grows, and is there a regime of L and w in which the polynomial single-layer bound is actually tighter than the off-the-shelf Khovanskii bound numerically (not just asymptotically)?
  • Can the smoothness/non-vanishing-gradient assumption be relaxed using an o-minimal/real-analytic analogue of the Basu–Lerario [BL23] singular-variety deformation, given the noted absence of Pfaffian polar varieties?
  • Does the Gaussian break-even inequality (5.6) admit a tighter analysis that recovers a polynomial-in-w rate at fixed Gaussian scale, perhaps via a sharper chi-tail bound on γ_n(R_V/σ) or a BKK argument that handles the sphere term?
  • How do these condition-number tail bounds compare quantitatively (in a concrete n, w, t regime) with the concentration-of-measure robustness bounds of [FFF18, MDM19] and the covering-number generalisation bounds of [ZK25]?
  • Can the framework incorporate non-Pfaffian activations (ReLU, ELU) via piecewise-linear / tropical limits [ZNL18], unifying the Pfaffian and ReLU robustness geometries?
