> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Visual Inspection of Policies (VIP)，用轻量级视频语言模型 VideoLLaMa2-7B 直接观看策略的回合视频来推荐课程任务，在 SMAC 多智能体基准上对未见地图取得约 84% / 76% 胜率（最强标量分基线几乎全为 0%），并证明视频信息是关键。

> **TL;DR:** VIP feeds recorded episode videos of the current policy to a lightweight Video Language Model (VideoLLaMa2-7B) that recommends the next curriculum task; on SMAC held-out maps it reaches ~84% and ~76% win rate on `3s vs 4z` and `3s5z` (baselines near 0%), matches grid-search MAPPO* on `3s5z` (0.76 vs 0.76) using ~100x fewer environment interactions, and its text-only ablation collapses — showing the video modality is the key driver of the gains.

## 核心贡献

- 提出新方法 **Visual Inspection of Policies (VIP)**：直接把智能体策略的回合视频喂给视频语言模型 (VLM)，由 VLM 同时分析视频并给出下一个"有趣"任务，用于开放式 (open-ended) RL 自动课程。这是首个用 VLM 直接审视学习进度（而非压缩成文本/标量）来做 autocurriculum 的工作。
- VIP 天然与智能体数量无关：只要多智能体渲染在同一视频中，VLM 处理整个团队的开销与单智能体相同，因而可扩展到任意/可变智能体数。
- 首次将 **无监督开放式 autocurricula** 应用到 StarCraft Multi-Agent Challenge (SMAC) —— 此前 SMAC 仅用于监督式课程。
- 通过设计一个 text-only 消融（去掉视频、保留文字摘要），干净地隔离出"视频"对课程效果的贡献。
- 引入简单的 sentence-similarity "消毒"模块，将 VLM 可能幻觉/格式错误输出映射到合法任务；实验中幻觉率仅 5.6% 且全部被该模块修正，回退机制从未触发。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **Visual Inspection of Policies (VIP)**, a new open-ended autocurriculum that feeds episode videos of the current policy to a Vision/VLM and asks it to pick the next worthwhile task — using the VLM's human-like priors to gauge learning progress that scalar scores and text summaries cannot capture.
- First application of **unsupervised, open-ended autocurricula to SMAC**, a complex cooperative multi-agent benchmark with varying troop types/counts that had previously resisted curriculum learning.
- Shows VIP is **agnostic to the number of agents**: any number of controllable agents rendered in one video costs the same VLM compute, so it scales to variable-team MARL settings where prior VLM/UED methods (MAESTRO, FM self-play) are restricted.
- Provides a clean **text-only ablation** that isolates the contribution of the video modality, plus a sentence-similarity sanitization step that recovers 100% of VLM hallucinations (5.6% rate) with no fallback ever triggered.
- Demonstrates VIP works with a deliberately **lightweight, open VLM (VideoLLaMa2-7B)** where VLM inference is only ~1% of total curriculum time, so the curriculum is not bottlenecked by the LLM.

## Methodology

VIP runs a simple loop (Algorithm 1). At each curriculum step `t`:

1. **Train-RL**: train the team policy on the current task θ_t with a (multi-agent) RL algorithm — MAPPO in all experiments — for 10^7 environment steps.
2. **Eval**: record one or more episode videos φ_video_t plus a short text summary φ_text_t (here: just the win rate and the list of previously selected tasks) by rolling out the current policy on θ_t.
3. **VLM-Next-Task**: feed (φ_video_t, φ_text_t) to the VLM (VideoLLaMa2-7B, temperature 0.4, top-p 0.8) under a "video game expert" system prompt and ask it to recommend the next "interesting" task that is neither too easy nor too hard given the agent's apparent capabilities.
4. **Sanitize-Task**: map the raw VLM output to the nearest valid task in the task space using sentence similarity over sinusoidal embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2); if no candidate exceeds a threshold, pick a random task (never triggered in practice).

Key design points: the VLM is prompted with a system prompt describing SMAC semantics, the 12 selectable maps, and difficulty levels 1–10. The task prompt re-states the current task, win rate, and full history of selected tasks (the full history was necessary because the 7B model lost awareness of past choices without it). Videos are 1024×1024 at 25 FPS, 1–10 s long. The task space is (map × difficulty); maps are split 80% train / 20% test. Observations/action spaces across maps are zero-padded to the size of the largest map (27m vs 30m) to allow transfer. Generalization is measured by fine-tuning the curriculum policy on each unseen map for another 10^7 steps and reporting win rate (5 seeds, 95% CIs), since zero-shot transfer in SMAC is known to fail. All runs share default MAPPO hyper-parameters (no per-task grid search), and the whole pipeline runs on a single Tesla V100-32GB.

## Results & Highlights

- **Held-out maps (Table 1, difficulty 5), win rate after fine-tuning — VIP vs best baseline:**
  - `3s vs 4z`: VIP **0.84±0.16**, while every baseline ≈ 0% (MAPPO 0.04±0.00, Random 0.00, Text-Only 0.00, PLR-PVL 0.00, PLR-MaxMC 0.00).
  - `3s5z`: VIP **0.76±0.20** vs Text-Only 0.21±0.30, Random 0.59±0.09, MAPPO 0.16±0.36, PLR-MaxMC 0.00 — i.e. VIP beats the next-best (Random) by ~17 points and its own text-only ablation by ~55 points.
  - `10m vs 11m`: VIP **0.53±0.22** is the highest but ties Text-Only (0.51) and Random (0.49); the authors note marines appear in ~70% of Random and ~15% of text-only tasks but only once in VIP's curriculum.
- **Seen maps (Table 2), win rate after fine-tuning:**
  - `3s vs 5z` (difficulty 7): VIP **0.34±0.35** vs **0.00±0.00** for MAPPO, Random, Text-Only, PLR-MaxMC.
  - `3s vs 3z` (difficulty 7): VIP **1.00±0.00** vs Text-Only 0.78±0.38, MAPPO 0.67±0.06.
  - `3m` (difficulty 6): VIP **1.00±0.00**; PLR variants plateau lower (0.93 / 0.90) despite high starting points.
- **vs supervised grid search (Table 3):** MAPPO* (best per-task hyper-parameters from ~1700 grid-searched combinations, 1.7×10^10 env interactions per task) gets 0.84 / 0.97 / 0.76 on the three held-out maps; VIP gets **0.53 / 0.84 / 0.76** using only **1.6×10^8 env interactions total** — ~100× fewer — and matches MAPPO* on `3s5z` (0.76 vs 0.76) with faster convergence.
- **Video is the causal driver:** on the two hardest unseen maps the text-only ablation (identical prompt minus video) drops to 0.00 / 0.21 while VIP reaches 0.84 / 0.76, directly confirming the Figure-1 intuition that a 0%-win-rate policy can be visibly "close" to winning.
- **Compute profile:** VLM inference ≈ 4 min/step ≈ **1% of curriculum time**; MAPPO training dominates at ~99%.
- **Robustness:** VLM hallucinated task specs at 5.6% (≈1 per curriculum); the sentence-similarity sanitizer recovered all of them, so no random fallback was ever used.

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are Lorenzo Pantè and Andrea Fanti (Sapienza University of Rome) and Roberto Capobianco (Sony AI, Zurich). Capobianco has a track record in interactive/continual learning and XAI. The work is positioned as a preprint and is internally consistent: the central claim (video > text/scalar for curriculum selection) is tested via a properly matched ablation, all results report 95% CIs over 5 seeds, and the authors disclose the compute (single V100-32GB, ~6 h per 10^7-step MAPPO segment) and VLM failure modes (5.6% hallucination rate) transparently in appendices E and F.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine and well-motivated. Prior LLM-curricula (OMNI, OMNI-EPIC, LLM-POET) compress behavior into text/summaries, and OMNI-EPIC's own experiment with Video LMs relegated them to success detectors and found them unreliable; VIP instead makes the video the primary signal. The claim of being the first to apply *unsupervised* autocurricula to SMAC is defensible (STAR-MARL and PORTAL are supervised toward a known downstream task, MAESTRO is two-player zero-sum only).
- **Rigor:** Solid experimental hygiene — matched MAPPO hyper-parameters across all curricula (Table 5) to avoid leaking downstream-task knowledge, 5 seeds with 95% CIs, and a clean text-only ablation that isolates the video modality. The difficulty-5-vs-7 justification (Appendix B) is a thoughtful detail that most SMAC papers skip.
- **Weak baselines / gaps:** The most glaring gap is the comparison set. The strongest autocurriculum baseline is Robust PLR with two score functions (PVL, MaxMC), which both *collapse to near-zero* on the hard maps — i.e. the bar VIP clears is very low. Newer open-ended methods (OMNI/OMNI-EPIC, MAESTRO, FM self-play) are discussed but **not run**, with the justification that they need code-programmable environments / 2-player zero-sum / pre-defined skill sets. A direct empirical comparison against at least one of these (e.g. an OMNI-style text-curriculum on the same SMAC split) would materially strengthen the contribution.
- **Single benchmark, single VLM:** Everything rides on SMAC and VideoLLaMa2-7B. The authors explicitly acknowledge the method "cannot be truly robust to the choice of LLM/VLM," but they do not test a second VLM or a second domain, so the central empirical claim rests on one model on one benchmark.
- **Reproducibility:** Good — open VLM, default MARLlib hyper-parameters, full prompt templates (Appendix F.3), example VLM outputs, code-ready task encodings, and disclosed compute. The train/test map split (Table 4) and padding scheme are specified.
- **Significance:** Modest but real. VIP is unlikely to move the SMAC SOTA (MAPPO* grid search beats it on 2/3 maps), but the *direction* — using VLMs to watch agents learn — is compelling and the ~100× sample-efficiency story versus grid search is the most defensible contribution.
- **Red flags:** None severe. Minor: the headline "~80% win rate" in Figure 1 is an illustrative qualitative example, not a main-table number, which could mislead a casual reader; and the `10m vs 11m` "win" is statistically a tie, not the clean victory the narrative implies.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean and well-motivated: the "coach watches the game tape" analogy is intuitive and the text-only ablation is the right experiment to prove the video matters.
- Strong, well-isolated result on the two hardest unseen maps, where all scalar/text baselines are stuck at ~0% and VIP reaches ~80%.
- Lightweight by design: a 7B open VLM that costs ~1% of training time is a pragmatic, reproducible choice rather than relying on a frontier closed model.
- Genuinely agnostic to agent count, which is a real architectural advantage over MAESTRO and FM self-play for variable-team MARL.
- Honest about limitations and failure modes (VLM hallucinations, context-window fragility, SMAC rendering ambiguities).

## Limitations

- Evaluated on a **single benchmark (SMAC)** and a **single VLM (VideoLLaMa2-7B)**; no cross-domain or cross-model robustness study.
- Strongest competing autocurricula (OMNI/OMNI-EPIC, MAESTRO, FM self-play) are **discussed but not empirically compared** — the only run baselines are PLR variants that largely fail, plus random and from-scratch controls.
- VIP does not beat supervised grid-search MAPPO* on 2/3 held-out maps, so it is not a new SMAC SOTA; its value is sample efficiency and unsupervised-ness, not peak performance.
- Relies on the environment providing renderable image observations; the SMAC renderer is explicitly described as losing information (e.g. shared shooting-line color for both teams, Figure 5), which could cap what the VLM can see.
- Sanitization via sentence similarity assumes an **enumerable** task space of human-readable names; the authors note non-enumerable or obscure task spaces would need iterative prompting, which is untested.
- Curricula are short (16 tasks); it is unclear how VIP behaves over much longer, truly open-ended horizons where the task space could be exhausted.
- The 7B VLM required manually repeating the full task history in every prompt to avoid repetition — a model--specific quirk that may not generalize.

## Relevance

对研究"用多模态大模型自动设计 RL 课程 / 开放式学习"以及"多智能体协作 MARL 迁移与泛化"方向高度相关：VIP 提供了一个轻量、可复现的 VLM-in-the-loop 课程框架，思路（让模型"看"智能体行为而非读指标）可直接迁移到其它可渲染的 MARL/具身环境，对自动化课程与 lifelong learning 研究有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does VIP behave with a stronger VLM (e.g. a frontier video model) — does performance scale with VLM capability, or is VideoLLaMa2-7B already saturated for this task?
- Can VIP be benchmarked directly against OMNI/OMNI-EPIC-style text curricula and MAESTRO on a common domain to test whether the video advantage holds beyond SMAC?
- Does the method transfer to domains with richer, more informative rendering (e.g. Isaac Gym, Habitat) where the visual signal is less ambiguous than SMAC's minimal circles?
- How does VIP degrade as the task space becomes large or non-enumerable, where the sentence-similarity sanitizer is no longer applicable?
- Is the VLM actually extracting strategy-level cues (kiting, formation, panic) or mostly reacting to unit counts/win rate — could a controlled "video shuffle" or synthetic-video ablation disentangle this?
- Can VIP be combined with multiple LLMs (as in OMNI-EPIC) to handle richer task-generation, not just task-selection, in open-ended settings?
