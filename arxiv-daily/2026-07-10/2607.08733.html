Super Weights in LLMs and the Failure of Selective Training

arXiv: 2607.08733

Date: 2026-07-10

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08733

一句话总结： 本文揭示 LLM 中最重要的"超级权重"(Super Weights) 虽然剪枝会令模型崩溃，但单独训练这些参数（100~36K 个）却让精度跌回随机猜测水平；失败只发生在这些特定坐标上——在相同 down proj 层用等量随机位置训练反而优于基线，而 LoRA 通过全层低秩分解（仅 0.16% 参数）取得成功，证明"参数重要性"并不等价于"参数可孤立训练性"。
TL;DR: The paper shows that training only the most important "Super Weights" in LLMs (OLMo-1B/7B on ARC-Easy) collapses accuracy to random-guessing (~25–26%) regardless of whether 100, 4,096, or 36,864 parameters are trained, while an equal-size random-position control on the same down proj layers improves over the 60.65% baseline (64.18%), and vanilla LoRA with rank-8 (0.16% of params, 2.1M) reaches 66.88% on OLMo-1B / 77.3% on OLMo-7B. Freezing Super-Weight-aligned positions inside LoRA has no measurable effect (p > 0.05 over 10 seeds), establishing that parameter importance does not imply isolated trainability and that effective fine-tuning relies on structured layer-wide decompositions rather than targeting individually important weights.

核心贡献

  • 证伪"重要即可训练"假设：明确区分了"参数重要性"与"参数可孤立训练性"两个属性——超级权重剪枝会让困惑度激增数千倍，但单独训练它们（含 3×3 邻域、最多 36,864 个参数）却让 ARC-Easy 精度跌回随机猜测（~25–26%）。
  • 用对照实验定位失败根因：在相同 down proj 层上，训练等量（k=4,096）随机位置反而达到 64.18%（高于 60.65% 基线），对 down proj 做 rank-8 低秩更新达到 68.77%，说明失败源于"指向超级权重坐标"本身，而非稀疏性或模块选择。
  • 验证 Super Weights 的跨输入一致性：在 1,000 条随机 WikiText-2 样本上确认有 9 个位置在 100% 输入中产生最大激活尖峰，13 个位置在 ≥50% 输入中出现，证实超级权重是预训练模型的结构属性而非单次前向传播的偶然产物。
  • 揭示 LoRA 对位置级约束的鲁棒性：LoRA-dproj-SW-freeze（把对应超级权重坐标的注意力位置完全冻结）与 vanilla LoRA 在 10 个种子上统计上无差异（p > 0.05，80% 种子预测完全一致）；LoRA 自身最高幅值的 ΔW 位置冻结后也无影响（62.77% vs 62.72%）。
  • 跨架构剪枝复现与方法学修正：在 10 个模型上复现 Yu et al. (2025) 的剪枝崩溃现象，但发现纯按幅值排序的识别方法在 5 个新模型（Llama-3.2、Qwen2.5、Gemma-2 等）上完全失效，说明超级权重现象并非普适于所有 LLM。
  • 双视角理论分析：从内禀维度（稀疏坐标更新只能捕获 k/N 的微调子空间，LoRA 的 rank-r 流形提供 256× 容量）与优化器动力学（超级权重处 M² 倍曲率隐式抑制 1/M 的更新）两个角度解释观测。

Key Contributions

  • Falsifies the "important ⇒ trainable" hypothesis: cleanly separates parameter importance from isolated trainability — Super Weights whose pruning multiplies perplexity by thousands still cannot be fine-tuned in isolation (accuracy collapses to ~25–26% random-guessing even with 3×3 neighborhoods up to 36,864 parameters).
  • Isolates the cause via two down proj controls: training an equal number of random positions (k=4,096) in the same down proj layers reaches 64.18% (above baseline), and a rank-8 low-rank update to down proj reaches 68.77% — so neither sparsity nor the module explains the collapse; targeting the Super Weight coordinates themselves does.
  • Validates Super Weight consistency across inputs: extends Yu et al. (2025)'s single-forward-pass identification across 1,000 random WikiText-2 samples, showing 9 positions produce the largest activation spikes in 100% of inputs and 13 positions in ≥50%, confirming they are structural rather than input-specific artifacts.
  • Shows LoRA is robust to position-level restrictions: LoRA-dproj-SW-freeze (fully freezing attention positions matching SW coordinates) is statistically indistinguishable from vanilla LoRA (p > 0.05, 80% of seeds bit-for-bit identical over 10 seeds), and freezing LoRA's own top-1,000 highest-magnitude ΔW positions has no effect.
  • Cross-architecture pruning replication with methodological caveat: replicates the catastrophic pruning collapse on 5 models using Yu et al.'s coordinates, but shows magnitude-based identification completely fails on 5 newer models (Llama-3.2-1B/3B, Qwen2.5-1.5B, Gemma-2-9B-it, Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct), indicating the Super Weight phenomenon is not universal under magnitude ranking.
  • Two complementary theoretical analyses: an intrinsic-dimensionality argument (sparse coordinate-aligned updates capture only a k/N fraction of the fine-tuning subspace; LoRA's rank-r manifold offers 256× more capacity) and an optimizer-dynamics argument (M²-fold larger curvature at SW positions implicitly suppresses their updates by 1/M, making explicit freezing redundant).

Methodology

The authors study whether the most important parameters in an LLM can be fine-tuned in isolation, using OLMo-1B (1.28B params, 16 layers) and OLMo-7B (7B params, 32 layers) as the primary experimental subjects, with ARC-Easy (2,376 test items, 4-way choice ⇒ 25% random-guessing floor) as the main benchmark and Winogrande for validation. Super Weights are identified via magnitude ranking (caching the top 10,000 positions per model); empirically the highest-magnitude weights concentrate in down proj (80% of top-10, 52% of top-100).

Training uses AdamW, lr 1e-4, 3 epochs, effective batch 16. Six experiments are designed:

  1. Super Weight consistency: 1,000 random WikiText-2 samples, single gradient-free forward pass each, measuring how often the same positions produce the largest down-proj activation spikes.
  2. Pruning replication: zeroing Super Weight coordinates across 10 models (5 using Yu et al.'s activation-spike coordinates, 5 using magnitude ranking).
  3. Direct Super Weight training: train only top-k Super Weights with everything else frozen, k ∈ {100, 1000, 4096, 8192}.
  4. Neighborhood training: extend direct training to a 3×3 patch (radius-1) around each Super Weight (≈900 to ≈36,864 parameters).
  5. LoRA-dproj-SW-freeze: scale factor s ∈ {0.0 … 1.0} applied to attention LoRA positions whose (i,j) indices match down-proj Super Weight coordinates (Eq. 1), with a 10-seed ablation (seeds 42–51) at s=0.0 vs s=1.0.
  6. LoRA-ΔW-SW-freeze: identify top-1,000 highest-magnitude positions in a trained LoRA's ΔW=BA·α/r, freeze them via gradient hooks, and retrain from scratch (3 seeds).

Vanilla LoRA uses rank r=8, α=16, targeting q/k/v/o proj (2.1M trainable = 0.16% of OLMo-1B). Two down-proj controls — random-sparse (k=4,096) and rank-8 LoRA on down proj — hold the module fixed to isolate the effect of update structure. Weight-change analysis and theoretical arguments (intrinsic dimensionality, optimizer curvature) support the empirical findings.

Results & Highlights

  • Direct SW training collapses to random-guessing regardless of k: on ARC-Easy, training the top-100 Super Weights yields 25.30% (OLMo-1B) / 26.3% (OLMo-7B); scaling to top-8,192 (81× more parameters) gives ~25% / ~25% — no improvement. Random-guess floor is 25%. (Table 2)
  • Neighborhood training provides zero benefit: 3×3 patches give 24.96% (k=100, ~900 params) up to 25.59% (k=4,096, ~36,864 params) on OLMo-1B, all at chance; OLMo-7B is identical at 26.1–26.3%. Training loss even diverges from 2.00 to 201.92 for the k=4,096 OLMo-1B variant. (Table 3)
  • Random-position control succeeds: training an equal k=4,096 randomly chosen down-proj positions (SW coordinates excluded) reaches 64.18% on OLMo-1B ARC-Easy, above the 60.65% pretrained baseline — proving sparsity is not the cause. A rank-8 LoRA on down proj reaches 68.77%. (Table 6)
  • Vanilla LoRA wins with 0.16% of parameters: 66.88% on OLMo-1B and 77.3% on OLMo-7B (above 73.3% baseline), using just 2.1M trainable params. (Tables 4, 6)
  • LoRA-dproj-SW-freeze is statistically identical to vanilla LoRA: across scale factors s=0.0…1.0, accuracy stays in 66.46–66.88% (OLMo-1B) and 76.4–78.0% (OLMo-7B), a <1pp spread. The 10-seed ablation gives 62.90% ± 0.45% for both s=0.0 and s=1.0 with p > 0.05 and 80% of seeds bit-for-bit identical; on OLMo-7B all 10 seeds identical (p=1.0). (Tables 4, 11)
  • LoRA-ΔW-SW-freeze has no effect: freezing the top-1,000 highest-magnitude positions in the trained ΔW gives 62.77% ± 0.62% vs vanilla 62.72% ± 0.62% on OLMo-1B (3 seeds) — LoRA's own "Super Weights" are not individually critical. (Table 5)
  • Super Weight consistency confirmed: 9 weight positions produce the largest activation spikes in 100% of 1,000 WikiText-2 samples; the set stabilizes by n≈100 with a sharp gap to the next tier at 63.3%. (Table 1)
  • Pruning replication shows catastrophic but non-universal collapse: with Yu et al.'s coordinates, OLMo-1B PPL jumps 13.09 → 47,951 (3,663×) and ARC drops 60.6% → 27.0%; OLMo-7B PPL jumps 4,400× (9.59 → 42,024); Mistral-7B and Llama-3-8B diverge to ∞. However, magnitude-based identification on 5 newer models (Qwen2.5-1.5B, Gemma-2-9B-it, Llama-3.2-1B/3B, Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct) produces zero effect (<0.1% PPL change). (Table 8)
  • Weight-change asymmetry: direct training moves SW positions by 8.19% on average (55× larger than LoRA's 0.15%), yet LoRA succeeds while direct training fails — update magnitude is not the determinant; coordinated full-layer structure is. 35.5% of direct-training SW updates exceed 10% of original value, some reaching 30.89%.

Authors & Credibility

All three authors (Shreyas Subramanian, Adewale Akinfaderin, Akarsha Sehwag) are at Amazon Web Services, giving the work institutional backing and access to compute for the multi-model pruning sweep and 10-seed ablations. The paper is published as a conference paper at COLM 2026 (Conference on Language Modeling), a vetted venue, not merely an arXiv preprint. The experimental design is unusually careful for this type of study: it includes two same-module controls (random-sparse and low-rank on down proj) that properly isolate the cause, a 10-seed ablation with explicit p-values, and an honest negative result (magnitude-based identification failing on 5 architectures) that most groups would have buried. Citations engage directly with the relevant lineage: Dettmers et al. (outlier features), Sun et al. (massive activations), Yu et al. (Super Weights, the work being extended), Aghajanyan et al. (intrinsic dimensionality), and Morris et al. 2026 (TinyLoRA).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High and clearly articulated. The central conceptual contribution — cleanly decoupling parameter importance from isolated trainability — is a genuinely new framing built on top of Yu et al. (2025)'s Super Weights, and the two down-proj controls (random-sparse reaching 64.18%, low-rank reaching 68.77%) are exactly the right experiments to attribute the collapse to the SW coordinates rather than to confounds.
  • Rigor: Strong. Pre-registered-style controls, 10-seed ablation with reported p > 0.05 and bit-for-bit prediction overlap, weight-change quantification, and consistency validation across 1,000 samples. The honest disclosure that magnitude-based identification fails on Qwen2.5-1.5B (whose top weight magnitude 1.20 exceeds OLMo-1B's 0.83) and Gemma-2-9B-it (highest baseline accuracy of any tested model at 85.9%) is a credibility-positive.
  • Reproducibility: Good but imperfect. Models (OLMo-1B/7B) and datasets (ARC-Easy, Winogrande, WikiText-2) are all open; hyperparameters (AdamW, lr 1e-4, 3 epochs, rank 8, α 16) are fully specified. Weaknesses: the random-position control uses a single seed (seed 42) at k=4,096 on OLMo-1B only — the authors themselves flag this as a limitation — and the 64.18%/68.77% control numbers rest on that single seed. Winogrande results are relegated to an appendix and show no method beats the pretrained baseline, slightly narrowing the generality.
  • Significance: The practical takeaway for PEFT practitioners is clear and somewhat counterintuitive (do not try to target "important" weights; full-layer low-rank structure is what matters), and it directly tempers the implications of the Super Weights line of work. The theoretical appendices (intrinsic dimensionality; M²-curvature suppression) are suggestive rather than tight, but they are consistent with the empirical picture.
  • Red flags / concerns:
  • The headline negative result (training SWs fails) is demonstrated only on OLMo-1B/7B; the authors acknowledge that extending training experiments to non-OLMo families using activation-spike identification is "an important direction" left to future work. The failure could in principle be partly OLMo-specific.
  • The Super Weight phenomenon itself is shown not to generalize under magnitude identification to 5 of 10 models, which somewhat undercuts the universality of the core object of study — though the authors are transparent that activation-spike identification (Yu et al.'s method) is the validated route.
  • Evaluation is dominated by the relatively easy ARC-Easy (4-way multiple choice with a 25% floor); the Winogrande appendix shows no method improves over baseline, so the "LoRA succeeds" framing is benchmark-dependent.
  • The theoretical "256× more capacity" and "1/M suppression" claims are presented without derivation depth in the main text (deferred to appendices), so a reader must trust the appendices for the load-bearing math.

Strengths

  • Conceptually clean separation of importance vs. trainability, with precisely the right controls (random-sparse and low-rank on the same down proj layers) to rule out sparsity and module choice as confounds.
  • Honest negative result on magnitude-based Super Weight identification failing across 5 architectures — a finding that constructively narrows and refines Yu et al. (2025).
  • Strong statistical hygiene: 10-seed ablation with p-values and bit-for-bit prediction comparison, not just mean ± std.
  • The "LoRA-ΔW-SW-freeze" experiment is a clever inversion — showing LoRA's own highest-magnitude positions are not critical — that closes a natural follow-up question.
  • Practical, actionable conclusion for PEFT design: derive parameter efficiency from structured layer-wide decompositions, not from targeting individually important weights.

Limitations

  • Primary quantitative training results are restricted to OLMo-1B and OLMo-7B; the cross-architecture sweep covers only pruning, not training, so the central failure claim is not yet demonstrated beyond OLMo.
  • Main benchmark is ARC-Easy, a relatively easy 4-way task; on Winogrande (Appendix E) no method beats the pretrained baseline, so the relative pattern holds but the absolute "LoRA improves" story is benchmark-specific. Harder benchmarks (MMLU, GSM8K) are not evaluated.
  • The critical random-position control (64.18%) and down-proj LoRA control (68.77%) use a single seed (seed 42) on OLMo-1B only — the weakest link in an otherwise rigorous design, and one the authors themselves acknowledge.
  • Consistency validation uses WikiText-2 only; cross-dataset validation would strengthen the "structural property" claim.
  • Only LoRA-family PEFT is studied; adapters, prompt tuning, and other selective methods are not tested.
  • The theoretical arguments (intrinsic dimensionality, optimizer curvature) are sketched in the main text and detailed in appendices; their tightness is not fully established.

Relevance

这篇论文直接关乎参数高效微调（PEFT）与 LLM 可解释性的核心问题——"哪些参数重要、能否针对性训练"，对设计更优的 LoRA 变体、稀疏微调策略以及理解 Transformer 内部关键参数机制都具有参考价值。对关注高效微调、模型可解释性与剪枝的研究者而言，是一项值得跟进的、方法学上相当严谨的工作。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the "training SWs in isolation fails" result replicate beyond OLMo on models where activation-spike Super Weights can be identified (e.g., Llama-3-8B, Phi-3-mini, Mistral-7B), and does the failure mode differ across architectures?
  • Would a random-position control run across multiple seeds and at k=8,192 / k=36,864 reproduce the 64.18% success, or was seed-42 on OLMo-1B a favorable draw?
  • On harder benchmarks (MMLU, GSM8K, BBH) where the pretrained baseline is weaker, does direct SW training still collapse to chance and does LoRA still improve — i.e., is the effect task-difficulty-independent?
  • Can the optimizer-curvature argument (M²-fold larger curvature at SW positions implicitly suppressing updates by 1/M) be verified empirically by measuring the Hessian diagonal / gradient variance at SW vs. random positions during training?
  • Do other PEFT families (bitfit, prefix tuning, adapters, DoRA) also show that position-level targeting of "important" weights is irrelevant, or is the layer-wide coordination property specific to low-rank decomposition?
  • If Super Weights act as "fixed high-gain amplifiers" that LoRA routes representations around, can a principled PEFT method be designed to explicitly preserve SW values while optimizing the input routing — and would it beat vanilla LoRA?
  • The magnitude-based identification failed on 5/10 models: is there a unified, architecture-agnostic Super Weight detection method that recovers Yu et al.'s activation-spike coordinates without a per-model forward-pass sweep?
