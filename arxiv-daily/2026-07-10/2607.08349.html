Certified Interventional Fidelity: Anytime-Valid, Adaptive Evaluation of Causal Claims in Mechanistic Interpretability

arXiv: 2607.08349

Date: 2026-07-10

https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08349

一句话总结： 本文提出 Certified Interventional Fidelity (CIF)，将机制可解释性中的干预式评估（如互换干预精度、激活补丁恢复率、电路完整性）改写为对显式声明输入分布与干预分布的有界因果估计量，并配上 Hoeffding 与 variance-adaptive betting 两类 anytime-valid 置信序列，使得在反复查看、提前停止与自适应采样下仍能给出有效的不确定性保证；在 MNIST 抽象与 GPT-2 Small IOI 电路实验中，betting 序列相比 Hoeffding 将认证开销降低 10-30 倍，并暴露出点估计无法支撑的方法差异。
TL;DR: The paper argues that mechanistic-interpretability evaluations (interchange intervention accuracy, activation patching, circuit completeness) are usually reported as a single point estimate despite being monitored and adapted while running, so reported fidelities may be artifacts of finite sampling rather than stable causal claims. It introduces Certified Interventional Fidelity (CIF), which rewrites every reported score as a bounded causal estimand over a declared input distribution and intervention distribution, then wraps it with anytime-valid confidence sequences (Hoeffding-style and variance-adaptive betting) plus a bounded-mixture importance-weighting layer that permits adaptive failure-directed sampling without changing the estimand. On a 3-layer MNIST MLP abstraction benchmark and GPT-2 Small IOI circuits, CIF certifies which high-fidelity claims hold (e.g., the 13-head IOI circuit certifies μ≥0.90 in 102 vs 1,875 forward passes, a ~18× reduction), shows that apparent method differences are often not statistically supported, and exposes that qualitative conclusions can flip with the intervention-distribution choice (at p=0.5 vs p≤0.2).

核心贡献

  • 把干预式可解释性评估统一改写为有界因果估计量：将抽象/还原保真度（IIA、压缩、剪枝、电路还原）与组件效应恢复（激活补丁、路径补丁、因果追踪）两类评估统一写成 θ = E_{X,I∼D×Π}[Y(X,I)]，Y∈[0,1]，从而把"科学问题"与"采样过程"显式分离。
  • 提供 anytime-valid 置信序列保证：用 Hoeffding-style spending 序列与基于 Online Newton Step 的 variance-adaptive betting 序列（Waudby-Smith & Ramdas 2024），在反复查看与数据相关提前停止下仍保证 P(∀n: θ∈C_n) ≥ 1−δ。
  • 自适应失败导向采样不改变估计量：通过有界混合建议分布 q_t = (1−α)Π + α·q̃_t 与 Horvitz-Thompson 重要权重（w_t ≤ 1/(1−α)）保证自适应采样仍瞄准原目标分布 Π，并给出无偏性与 anytime-valid 自适应定理。
  • 配套算法、配对比较与多重比较修正：给出两/单侧认证算法、配对置信序列（用于比较两种抽象/电路）以及 Bonferroni / peeling / e-value FDR 等多比较控制方案，并附报告清单（Appendix D）。
  • 实证证明 betting 序列将认证开销降低 10-30 倍，并暴露出点估计无法支撑的方法差异与对干预分布 Π 的强敏感性。

Key Contributions

  • Recasts two broad classes of interventional interpretability evaluation — abstraction/reduction fidelity and component-effect recovery — as a single bounded causal estimand θ = E_{(X,I)∼D×Π}[Y(X,I)] with Y∈[0,1], cleanly separating the scientific question (the estimand) from the sampling procedure.
  • Supplies fixed-budget Hoeffding confidence intervals and anytime-valid confidence sequences (both Hoeffding-style spending sequences and variance-adaptive betting sequences) that remain valid under arbitrary stopping and repeated monitoring, addressing the inherently sequential/adaptive nature of interpretability workflows.
  • Introduces adaptive CIF: failure-directed sampling through a bounded mixture proposal q_t = (1−α)Π + α·q̃_t with Horvitz–Thompson importance weights bounded by W_max = 1/(1−α), proven unbiased and anytime-valid (Lemma 1, Theorem 2), so adaptive counterexample hunting does not change the estimand.
  • Provides a complete algorithmic kit: one-/two-sided certification loops (Algorithms 1–2), paired anytime-valid comparison for ranking two explanations, and multiplicity control (Bonferroni, sequential peeling, e-value FDR) for scanning many components, plus a reporting checklist.
  • Empirically demonstrates that betting CSs cut certification cost by 10–30× over Hoeffding and that CIF can certify high-fidelity claims, flag statistically unsupported method differences, and make sensitivity to the intervention distribution explicit.

Methodology

Estimand reformulation. CIF fixes an input distribution D, a target intervention distribution Π, and a bounded interventional score Y∈[0,1], then certifies the population mean θ = E_{(X,I)∼D×Π}[Y(X,I)]. For abstraction fidelity the score is a discrepancy Z = d(Outcome(M_L), Outcome(M_H))∈[0,1], interventional risk R = E[Z], and fidelity F = 1−R. For component-effect/recovery the score is the clipped patching recovery Δ = clip_{[0,1]}[(g_patch − g_corr)/(g_clean − g_corr)]∈[0,1] and the target is μ = E[Δ]. Two correspondence maps — a state map τ and an intervention map ω — specify how a high-level explanation M_H corresponds to the low-level network M_L; CIF does not build M_H, it evaluates it.

Fixed-budget intervals (P1). For n fixed in advance, the Hoeffding interval gives |θ̂−θ| ≤ √(log(2/δ)/(2n)) with probability 1−δ (Proposition 1). Compute planning needs n ≥ log(2/δ)/(2ε²) for half-width ε.

Anytime-valid confidence sequences (P2). Using the spending schedule δ_n = 6δ/(π²n²) and radius b_n = √(log(2/δ_n)/(2n)) yields a CS valid for all n simultaneously via a union bound (Theorem 1). One-sided certification: certify F ≥ F_0 when 1 − U_n(R) ≥ F_0; certify μ ≥ μ_0 when L_n(μ) ≥ μ_0 — valid even with data-dependent stopping.

Adaptive failure-directed sampling (P3). A history-dependent proposal q_t(·|H_{t−1}) lets the evaluator chase likely failures (e.g., via AtP*/gradients or past failures). A mixture q_t = (1−α)Π + α·q̃_t keeps importance weights w_t = Π(I_t)/q_t(I_t) ≤ 1/(1−α) = W_max. The Horvitz–Thompson estimator θ̂_IS is unbiased (Lemma 1) and yields an anytime-valid CS with radius W_max·√(log(2/δ_n)/(2n)) (Theorem 2).

Variance-adaptive betting CSs. The Online Newton Step betting capital process K_n(m) = ∏(1+λ_t(B_t−m)) adapts to empirical variance; by Ville's inequality C_n = {m: K_n(m) < 1/δ} is an anytime-valid CS. Under adaptive sampling, B_t = w_t·Y_t/W_max∈[0,1] is fed to the betting tracker, the interval for E[B_t] = θ/W_max is scaled back, and when Var is small the betting CS is far tighter than Hoeffding.

Comparison & selection (P4). Paired differences D_t = Y_t^(1) − Y_t^(2)∈[−1,1] have low variance and reuse the CS machinery after rescaling. For scanning K candidates, Bonferroni δ/K, sequential peeling, or e-value FDR control avoid optimistic selection.

Results & Highlights

  • Betting CSs reduce certification cost by 10–30× over Hoeffding. On MNIST abstraction (Table 2, p=0.1, i.i.d.), variance-based pruning at k=256 certifies F≥0.90 in 64 forward passes with betting vs 1,141 under Hoeffding (~17.8×); soft interventions at k=256 need 93 vs 1,219 samples (~13.1×) for F≥0.90 and 342 vs 9,849 samples (~28.8×) for F≥0.95. Figure 2 reports per-method ratios ranging from no speedup up to beyond 30× at k=256, p=0.1.
  • A useful negative result invisible to point estimates: at the aggressive swap probability p=0.5, no non-identity abstraction certifies F≥0.90 (Table 1, n=5,000, Hoeffding CS, half-width 0.046), even though point-estimate fidelity ranges 0.105–0.689 and would naively rank methods. CIF shows none supports a high-fidelity claim at this threshold.
  • On GPT-2 Small IOI circuits, the 13-head hand-identified circuit certifies μ≥0.90 in 102 betting samples vs 1,875 Hoeffding samples, and μ≥0.95 in 357 betting samples vs >2,000 (out of budget) (Table 3, i.i.d., 1−δ=0.95). Even the 3-head name-mover circuit certifies μ≥0.90 in 110 betting samples. All evaluated circuits share near-identical point estimates μ̂∈[0.959,0.972] — the distinction is purely inferential: at n=2,000 Hoeffding radii are ~0.070 while betting radii are ~0.005.
  • Paired comparison exposes statistically unsupported method differences. A paired comparison of soft interventions vs random pruning at k=256 yields an estimated difference of −0.033 with Hoeffding radius 0.067 (not significant) but betting radius 0.016 — illustrating that interval choice itself can change whether a method difference is declared real.
  • Sensitivity to the intervention distribution can flip qualitative conclusions (E3). Across swap probabilities p∈{0.05,0.1,0.2,0.5} and three discrepancy metrics, at p≤0.2 the metrics yield overlapping CSs (F̂∈[0.94,0.99], width 0.067, no detectable metric effect), but at p=0.5 they separate: 0–1 loss gives F̂=0.674 while clipped KL gives F̂=0.578 with non-overlapping CSs (gap ≈0.03).
  • Coverage validation (E4) confirms Hoeffding CSs achieve empirical coverage 1.000 across all i.i.d., adaptive, and aggressive-peeking configurations; betting CSs range 0.930–1.000, always at or above the nominal 1−δ while using less excess coverage.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The core idea — treating interventional interpretability scores as causal estimands and attaching anytime-valid, variance-adaptive confidence sequences — is a genuine and well-motivated reformulation. The statistical machinery (Hoeffding CS, ONS betting CS, Horvitz–Thompson importance weighting) is imported, not invented; the contribution is the synthesis and the application. The clearest novelty is adaptive CIF's bounded-mixture importance-weighting layer that preserves the estimand while allowing failure-directed sampling.
  • Rigor — weak/absent baselines on the interpretability side: The GPT-2 IOI evaluation compares only ACDC, attribution patching, AtP*, and the hand-identified Wang et al. circuit, all of which produce near-identical point estimates (μ̂∈[0.959,0.972]) and differ only in inferential sharpness. The paper does not pit CIF against the closest statistical competitor, Shi et al. 2024's formal hypothesis tests for circuit claims, head-to-head — that comparison is left to narrative positioning. Likewise Méloux et al. 2025's variance analysis is cited as motivation but not benchmarked against.
  • Rigor — scale is modest: All transformer evidence is on GPT-2 Small (124M) on a single task (IOI). The paper repeatedly motivates CIF by the cost of "expensive models like GPT-2," but GPT-2 Small is cheap by 2026 standards; the 10–30× betting speedup is demonstrated at sample sizes of tens-to-thousands, and it is unclear how the W_max penalty and betting advantage scale to frontier models where per-forward-pass cost dominates.
  • Rigor — adaptive-sampling benefit is mixed/underclaimed-by-data: Adaptive CIF is not uniformly faster for certification — the paper honestly reports that at k=256 it roughly doubles the sample count versus i.i.d. betting (131 vs 64) in the high-fidelity regime, and relegates the complementary failure-finding case to Appendix F. So the headline "10–30×" reduction is attributable to the betting CS, not to adaptivity; adaptivity's proven value is confined to finding failures, not certifying successes.
  • Reproducibility: Strong on paper — named models (3-layer MLP at >98% MNIST accuracy, GPT-2 Small), named circuits, named sampling protocols, α=0.3, δ=0.95, n up to 10,000, code repo, coverage study over 500 runs (Appendix G). However no seeds, no variance bars across the headline tables, and the adaptive proposal is a single static heuristic (q̃_t(j) ∝ ‖W_{:,j}‖²) with the history-updated variant deferred to Appendix F.
  • Significance: If adopted, CIF would meaningfully discipline an evaluation practice that the paper (and Méloux et al. 2025) document as informal. The "no non-identity abstraction certifies F≥0.90 at p=0.5" and "metric choice flips conclusions at p=0.5" findings are concrete cautionary results. Significance is currently limited by single-task, single-model transformer evidence.
  • Red flags: (1) The "10–30×" abstract/headline claim is driven by Hoeffding-vs-betting, not by adaptivity, yet adaptive sampling is presented as a co-equal headline contribution with weaker supporting evidence; (2) Bonferroni is acknowledged as lossy and the e-value FDR replacement is deferred to "immediate next step," so the multi-comparison story (P4) is incompletely delivered; (3) CIF explicitly disclaims solving identification (whether the chosen abstraction captures the causal structure of interest), so a certified fidelity does not guarantee mechanistic truth — a caveat the paper states but users may overlook.

Strengths

  • The estimand reformulation is clean and unifying: IIA, patching recovery, and circuit completeness all map onto the same bounded-mean form with explicit (D, Π), making the framework broadly applicable.
  • Anytime-validity under arbitrary stopping and peeking directly matches how interpretability experiments are actually run, closing a real gap between theory and practice.
  • The 10–30× betting speedup is a large, practical efficiency gain that makes certification feasible on transformer forward-pass budgets.
  • Intellectual honesty about negative results (no abstraction certifies F≥0.90 at p=0.5; adaptive sampling not uniformly faster) and about CIF's scope (estimation, not identification).
  • Good supporting infrastructure: proofs (Appendix C), coverage validation (E4/Appendix G), a reporting checklist (Appendix D), and a public code repo.

Limitations

  • Single-task, small-model transformer evidence: only GPT-2 Small on IOI; no scaling study to larger models or other tasks (induction heads, factual recall, safety-relevant circuits).
  • Adaptive CIF is not shown to help certification in the high-fidelity regime; its benefit is confined to failure-finding (Appendix F), so the adaptivity contribution is narrower than the framing suggests.
  • Boundedness/clipping assumption: guarantees require Y∈[0,1]; unbounded quantities (raw logit differences) need clipping or sub-Gaussian/asymptotic CS variants, which are only gestured at.
  • Identification not addressed: CIF certifies a metric under a chosen Π; it cannot certify that the metric or abstraction captures the desired causal structure.
  • Multiplicity control incompletely delivered: Bonferroni is known-lossy; the promised e-value FDR integration is left as future work.
  • No comparison against Shi et al. 2024's hypothesis-testing framework or Méloux et al. 2025's variance analysis, the two most directly competing statistical approaches.

Relevance

对关注机制可解释性、电路发现与可解释性评估方法学的研究者，本文提供的 anytime-valid 置信序列与自适应认证层，直接可作为评估电路/抽象保真度时的统计基础设施，尤其契合"可解释性评估可靠性、电路忠实性度量稳健性、量化/压缩模型行为一致性"方面的兴趣——它把若干已知的可靠性问题（点估计不稳、对干预分布敏感、多比较乐观偏差）转化为可形式化认证的工作流。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How do the 10–30× betting speedup and the W_max penalty scale on larger models (Llama-3, frontier LLMs) where per-forward-pass cost dominates and the high-fidelity regime may differ from IOI?
  • Does adaptive CIF (failure-directed sampling) actually reduce total wall-clock cost to certify a target fidelity on harder tasks, or does the mixture-rate/W_max tradeoff erase the benefit outside the MNIST failure-finding regime?
  • How does CIF compare empirically to Shi et al. 2024's formal hypothesis tests for circuit claims and to Méloux et al. 2025's variance analysis — do they agree on which circuit claims survive?
  • Since qualitative conclusions flip with the intervention distribution at p=0.5 (E3), can CIF be extended to certify a family of Π simultaneously (e.g., uniform over p∈[0.1,0.5]) rather than reporting a sensitivity sweep?
  • Can anytime-valid bounds be integrated as active controllers inside automated circuit-discovery (ACDC, AtP*) to prune low-fidelity candidates during search, as the paper proposes, and does this improve the final discovered circuit's certified fidelity?
