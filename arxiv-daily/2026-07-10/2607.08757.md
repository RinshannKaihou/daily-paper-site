> **一句话总结：** 本文从理论上证明，扩散模型中即使学习分数函数沿前向边际的 L2 误差任意小、连续时间反向过程稳定且与精确过程在路径总变差意义下任意接近，其 Euler–Maruyama 离散采样仍可能在每个正阶矩和每个 Wasserstein 距离 Wp(p≥1) 上发散；该失效机理在任意维度、概率流 ODE 以及一个固定的有限 DiT/GELU 神经架构内同样存在，而将学习去噪器投影到包含数据支撑的有界闭凸集上（即图像扩散中常用的"裁剪预测样本"）可在不增加近似误差的前提下恢复网格一致矩界与 Wasserstein 收敛。

> **TL;DR:** This is a single-author theory paper proving that an arbitrarily small *forward-marginal* (on-path) score error does **not** certify numerical stability of Euler–Maruyama (or probability-flow) sampling in score-based diffusion: the author constructs a single smooth score field (and a fixed finite GELU/adaLN-Zero DiT architecture, verified to fit DiT-S/2) whose learned reverse process is nonexplosive, has all moments, and is arbitrarily close to the exact process in path-space total variation, yet whose discretized endpoints converge in probability while **every** positive moment and **every** Wasserstein distance Wp (p≥1) diverges. On a toy 8×8 one-channel point-mass variance-exploding model, four small DiT-style denoisers (104,388 params) keep score MSE ≤ 7.10×10⁻³ while exhibiting post-entry RMS amplification r8/r0 from 7.90 to 14.69; projecting the denoiser onto C=[−1,1] suppresses the final empirical second moment by factors of **1.13×10⁴ to 3.33×10⁴** versus the unprojected (raw) chain. No standard image benchmarks (FID/CIFAR/ImageNet) are reported — the experiments are a constructive realization test, not a generative-quality evaluation.

## 核心贡献

- **识别出"沿前向路径分数精度"的一个盲点（blind spot）**：标准分数学习误差 Escore = ∫ E_{Xt∼pt}‖s̃t−st‖² dt 是在*前向边际*下度量的，但离散反向采样器沿*自身数值轨迹*评估分数；二者存在根本性脱节。
- **构造性反例（Theorem 3.1）**：构造单一光滑分数场 s̃t(x)=st(x)−λχR(x)x|x|^α，其沿前向边际的 L2 误差一致小于任意 ε，连续时间学习反向过程非爆炸且具任意阶矩、与精确反向过程路径总变差 ≤ η，但 Euler–Maruyama 端点依概率收敛的同时**每个正阶矩发散**、每个 Wp(p≥1) 对任意固定律发散。
- **失效机理的普适性**：同一阻碍机制在任意维度（Corollary 3.2）、概率流 ODE（Corollary 3.3，确定性采样器同样适用）、以及一个固定有限 GELU/adaLN-Zero DiT 架构内（Theorem 3.5、Corollary 3.6 证明可嵌入 DiT-S/2）都成立——即使每个去噪器全局有界且全局 Lipschitz。
- **等预算不可排序性（Corollary 3.4）**：两个具有*相同*前向边际误差预算的光滑分数场，一个 Euler 链有网格一致任意阶矩、另一个丢失每个正阶矩。因此该标量误差连"按稳定性给采样器排序"都做不到。
- **正结果——去噪器投影恢复稳定性（Theorem 4.3）**：当数据支撑含于已知有界闭凸集 C 时，将学习去噪器投影到 C 上不增加逐点近似误差（Proposition 4.2），强制每个 Euler 更新的确定性部分退化为向 C 的线性增长凸组合，从而给出网格一致矩界并在温和局部正则性下保证 Wasserstein 收敛；对图像数据该投影恰好等价于"裁剪预测的干净样本"这一启发式，本文为其提供了稳定性解释。
- **机理解释**：在承载前向边际极少质量的远区放置内向的超线性扰动；罕见数值轨迹进入后，显式更新反复触发超线性放大，其放大（指数级）压倒罕见事件概率代价（多项式/次指数级）——这与 Hutzenthaler–Jentzen–Kloeden (2011) 关于超线性 SDE 显式 Euler 经典发散相关，但此处不稳定区在前向误差下几乎不可见而学习连续过程仍稳定。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifies a fundamental blind spot of on-path (forward-marginal) score accuracy.** The standard score-learning budget Escore = ∫ E_{Xt∼pt}‖s̃t−st‖²dt is measured under forward marginals, but a discretized reverse sampler evaluates the learned score along its *own numerical trajectory*. The paper formalizes and exploits this mismatch.
- **Constructive counterexample (Theorem 3.1).** A single smooth score field s̃t(x)=st(x)−λχR(x)x|x|^α has uniformly arbitrarily small on-path L2 error, a nonexplosive learned reverse process with all moments, and path-space TV distance ≤ η to the exact process — yet its Euler–Maruyama endpoints converge in probability while every positive moment diverges, and Wp diverges for every p≥1 against every fixed law.
- **Ubiquity of the obstruction.** The same construction extends to (i) every dimension via a radial perturbation (Corollary 3.2), (ii) the deterministic probability-flow ODE (Corollary 3.3), and (iii) a *fixed finite* GELU/adaLN-Zero DiT architecture where a sequence of globally bounded, globally Lipschitz denoisers has vanishing on-path error and TV distance but unstable Euler endpoints (Theorem 3.5). Corollary 3.6 shows this fixed circuit fits inside the concrete DiT-S/2 configuration (12 blocks, hidden 384, 6 heads, MLP ratio 4, patch size 2).
- **Equal-budget non-ordering (Corollary 3.4).** Two smooth score fields with the *identical* on-path error budget can be one stable (grid-uniform moments of all orders) and one that loses every positive moment — so the scalar score-error budget cannot even rank samplers by numerical stability.
- **Positive result: denoiser projection (Section 4, Theorem 4.3).** For data supported in a known bounded closed convex set C, projecting the learned denoiser onto C before forming the score preserves pointwise/score accuracy (Proposition 4.2 — projection is a contraction toward the exact denoiser), forces the deterministic Euler update to have at most linear growth toward C, gives grid-uniform moment bounds, and yields Wasserstein convergence under mild local regularity. For image data scaled to a box this projection coincides with clipping the predicted clean sample — providing a stability interpretation of a common heuristic (Saharia et al. 2022; Lou & Ermon 2023).
- **Explicit amplification mechanism.** The instability comes from a superlinear inward perturbation placed in a remote region that carries negligible forward-marginal mass; a rare numerical trajectory enters it, after which explicit updates compound superlinearly (log-amplification ~O((1+α)K) vs. rare-event cost ~O(K^{2/α}+K)). This is a diffusion-sampling analogue of the classical explicit-Euler divergence for superlinear SDEs (Hutzenthaler–Jentzen–Kloeden 2011), but with the twist that the unstable region is invisible to the on-path error and the continuous process remains stable.

## Methodology

**Setting.** Variance-exploding (VE) forward process Xt = X0 + √t·g, g∼N(0,Id), with score st(x)=∇log pt(x) and exact denoiser Dt(x)=E[X0|Xt=x] linked by Tweedie's identity st=(Dt−x)/t. A learned score s̃t gives learned denoiser D̃t=x+t·s̃t. The exact reverse-time process uses drift s, the learned uses s̃, and both are then Euler–Maruyama discretized on a decreasing geometric grid δ=t0<t1<…<tK=T with step ρk=hk/tk+1∈(0,1).

**Negative construction (Section 3).** The author places a smooth, even cutoff χR (zero on [−R,R], one outside [−R−1,R+1]) and defines s̃t(x)=st(x)−λχR(x)x|x|^α. Steps of the proof: (1) on-path error → 0 as R→∞ by dominated convergence under Gaussian-tailed marginals; (2) the drift x·s̃t(x)≤C−c|x|^{2+α} gives a Lyapunov (1+x²)^m supermartingale → nonexplosion + all moments; (3) coupling with the exact process shows path TV ≤ η; (4) localization to a truncated Lipschitz drift gives Euler convergence in probability; (5) a "rare terminal shell" event EK at radius ~AK^{1/α} plus bounded-noise conditions gives an iterated one-step lower bound |Yj| ≥ (λa/4K)|Yj+1|^{1+α}, hence (1+α)^K-power growth that dominates the exp(−CK^{2/α}) shell probability, forcing E|Y0|^{(Km)}|^q→∞. Wasserstein divergence follows by Minkowski's lower bound.

**Neural realization (Appendices A–B).** A fixed-size GELU arithmetic circuit (gate module, square module, two multiplication modules) realizes the remote cubic profile Fm(x)=−λΓRm(x)x³ to arbitrary tolerance on [−Q,Q]; a LayerNorm head makes the output globally bounded. This is embedded tokenwise into a fixed adaLN-Zero DiT and shown to fit DiT-S/2 with only three active MLP blocks (nine blocks as identities, attention disabled).

**Positive construction (Section 4).** Define the projected denoiser D^P_t(x)=Π_C(D̃t(x)) and projected score s^P_t=(D^P_t−x)/t. Iterating the Euler recursion (22) gives an explicit telescoping representation (Proposition 4.1) with nonnegative denoiser weights summing to 1−tk/T; because D^P_t∈C⊂B(0,rC), the deterministic part has at most linear growth, yielding grid-uniform Lq bounds independent of the raw score's off-path behavior. Continuity + local Lipschitz of D^P_t gives localized Euler convergence; uniform integrability upgrades it to Wp convergence; Girsanov + Pinsker + a TV–moment interpolation gives the endpoint bound (28).

**Experiments (Section 5, Appendix C).** An 8×8 one-channel point-mass VE model (X0=0, exact denoiser identically zero) on t∈[0.1,1]. All four family members share one fixed DiT-style architecture: patch size 2, hidden dim 64, three adaLN-Zero/GELU residual MLP blocks, 104,388 trainable parameters; they differ only in parameter values and remote target profile (R,L,y^stress) ∈ {(2.5,700,35),(3,1000,40),(3.5,1450,43),(4,1800,46)}. Training combines on-path score loss, safe-region denoiser loss, cascade-tube regression, and transition consistency. A *stress-initialized protocol* (8 Euler updates with perturbations uniform on [−0.35,0.35]) isolates post-entry dynamics and explicitly does *not* estimate the natural entry probability. Evaluations use 1024 (unconditioned) / 512 (stress, projection) paired trajectories, single training seed 1729.

## Results & Highlights

- **Forward-marginal accuracy is tiny yet amplification is large (Table 1).** On the 8×8 point-mass VE model, all four fixed-architecture DiT denoisers (104,388 params) achieve on-path score MSE of **1.94×10⁻³, 3.73×10⁻³, 3.89×10⁻³, 7.10×10⁻³** (max 7.10×10⁻³) and paired fine-grid (512-step) trajectory RMSE of **0.0119, 0.0167, 0.0171, 0.0237**, yet the stress-test RMS amplification r8/r0 climbs from **7.90 → 9.78 → 13.02 → 14.69** — small on-path error coexists with large along-trajectory growth.
- **The neural cascade reproduces the theoretical superlinear recursion.** Under the stress-initialized protocol, the neural chain retains about **90%–95% of the target RMS magnitude** at the endpoint (r8^neural/r8^target), confirming the fixed architecture realizes the predicted cubic amplification.
- **Projection suppresses the second moment by four orders of magnitude.** Projecting the denoiser onto C=[−1,1] with identical stress initializations and perturbations reduces the final empirical second moment by factors between **1.13×10⁴ and 3.33×10⁴** versus the unprojected (raw) chain; raw RMS magnitude grows while projected RMS magnitude falls from an initial 35–46 down to **2.6–3.7** (Figure 2), removing the amplification mechanism entirely.
- **Headline theoretical separation (Theorem 3.1).** For every ε,η,λ,α>0 there is a single smooth score field with on-path error sup_{t∈[δ,T]} E_pt|s̃t−st|² ≤ ε, a nonexplosive learned reverse process with sup E|Zu|^q<∞ for all q≥2, and path TV ≤ η — but Euler–Maruyama endpoints satisfy E|Y0^{(Km)}|^q → ∞ for every q>0 and W_p(L(Y0^{(Km)}),ν)→∞ for every ν∈P_p(ℝ), p≥1, even though Y0^{(Km)}→Z_{T−δ} in probability.
- **Equal-budget non-ordering (Corollary 3.4).** Two score fields with the *identical* integrated on-path budget b∈(0,ε] can satisfy (19) grid-uniform moments of every order vs. (20) E|Y0^{(Km),bad}|^q→∞ for every q>0 — the scalar budget cannot rank samplers.
- **Fixed finite architecture suffices (Theorem 3.5, Corollary 3.6).** The obstruction persists within a *single fixed* adaLN-Zero DiT (and the concrete DiT-S/2 of Peebles & Xie 2023): a sequence of globally bounded, globally Lipschitz denoisers with on-path error → 0 and path-TV → 0 still has Euler endpoints diverging in every Wp.

The classical superlinear-Euler reference (Hutzenthaler, Jentzen & Kloeden 2011, Proc. Roy. Soc. A) is correctly invoked.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: high and well-targeted.** Prior diffusion-convergence theory (Chen et al. 2023; Lee–Lu–Tan 2023; Benton et al. 2024; Gao–Nguyen–Zhu 2025; Bruno–Sabanis 2025; Conforti–Durmus–Silveri 2025) controls sampling error under distribution-weighted score error *plus* additional regularity assumptions; recent work (Cao et al. 2026; Khelifa–Turner–Venkataramanan 2026) has begun questioning what scalar score-error measures certify. This paper sharpens that critique by pinpointing a *distinct* obstruction: the mismatch between where the error is measured (forward marginals) and where it is amplified (the numerical trajectory), and shows it persists even when the *continuous* dynamics are stable and arbitrarily close to exact. The connection to clipping-as-projection (Saharia 2022; Lou–Ermon 2023) is a genuinely nice bonus that gives a stability rationale for an existing heuristic.
- **Rigor: very strong on the theory.** The proofs are constructive and self-contained; the fixed-architecture result (Theorem 3.5) is the most valuable contribution because it defeats the natural rebuttal "real networks are bounded/Lipschitz." The DiT-S/2 resource accounting (Appendix B) is unusually concrete for this kind of result.
- **Reproducibility: mixed.** The experimental protocol is described in genuine detail (Appendix C: seeds, grid sizes, the four (R,L,y^stress) configurations, the 104,388-param architecture, the four-term training loss, the checkpoint selection rule). However, the result is fundamentally a *theory* paper and the experiments are an existence proof on a deliberately engineered toy (a point-mass model whose exact denoiser is identically zero), not an evaluation of real generative behavior; there is no code release mentioned and reproducing the cascade-tube regression / transition-consistency training without code would be nontrivial.
- **Significance: theoretical; limited immediate practical bite.** The counterexample is conservative: it requires the network to be trained *with* a remote superlinear target profile, and the paper's own stress protocol admits it does not estimate the natural entry probability. So the paper does *not* show that instability occurs in normally trained image diffusion; it shows that on-path error is *not by itself* a certificate. That is an important conceptual point but its downstream practical impact depends on whether the failure mode is observed in real samplers, which this paper does not establish.
- **Red flags / overclaiming: minimal.** The author is careful: the abstract and discussion do not claim real-sampler instability, only the failure of a particular certification route. One mild caveat: the headline "weak convergence holds while every Wp diverges" is a worst-case artifact of a hand-built score field with a deliberately engineered remote tail; readers should not mistake it for a typical-training phenomenon.
- **Missing analyses a reviewer would want.** (a) No estimate of how *likely* the rare trajectory is under realistic training/sampling — only a stress-initiated demonstration that it can be reached when forced. (b) No experiment on a non-trivial data distribution (everything uses X0=0); the positive projection result is also only validated on the same point-mass toy. (c) No comparison to alternative stabilizers (e.g., implicit/Heun solvers, the contractive-diffusion principle of Tang & Zhao 2024) beyond projection/clipping. (d) The equal-budget non-ordering (Corollary 3.4) is elegant but the "safe" comparator is a constant score shift, which is a somewhat artificial baseline.

## Strengths

- Identifies and cleanly formalizes a previously under-stated gap (forward-marginal vs. on-trajectory score accuracy) with a single, simple, reusable mechanism (a remote superlinear perturbation).
- Full proofs for all claims, including the genuinely hard "fixed finite neural architecture" and the concrete DiT-S/2 fit — closes the obvious escape route that bounded/Lipschitz networks are immune.
- The positive projection result is short, sharp, and gives a principled stability justification for the widespread clip-the-prediction heuristic; the bound (27)/(28) is grid-uniform and independent of off-path score growth.
- Equal-budget non-ordering is a particularly clean negative statement: the scalar score-error budget cannot even *rank* samplers by stability.
- Honest, non-overclaiming writing: the author repeatedly flags that the stress test is not a natural-probability estimate and that projection is "one" structural safeguard, not a universal remedy.

## Limitations

- **All experiments are on a point-mass toy model** (8×8 one-channel, exact denoiser zero). No results on CIFAR/ImageNet or any non-degenerate data distribution; no FID/IS or any generative-quality metric. The empirical evidence is an existence demonstration, not an evaluation of practical diffusion samplers.
- **The instability is engineered.** The four family members are *trained with* a remote superlinear target profile and a cascade-tube regression loss; nothing is shown about whether normally trained diffusion models exhibit this rare-trajectory amplification or how often it occurs.
- **The stress-initialized protocol deliberately bypasses natural entry probability.** The author states this explicitly, but it means the headline 4-orders-of-magnitude suppression by projection is conditional on being placed in the stress region, not on naturally reaching it.
- **Projection requires a known bounded convex set C containing the data support.** For bounded images this is fine, but for unbounded or unknown-support data the positive result does not directly apply, and the paper does not discuss how to choose/estimate C in practice or the cost of a wrong C.
- **Single author, no external validation/replication; no mentioned code release.** The construction is detailed enough to be reproducible in principle, but the custom four-term training loss and checkpoint calibration rule would benefit from reference code.
- **No comparison to other stabilization strategies** (implicit solvers, Heun, contractive-diffusion drift design, adaptive step-size control) — projection is the only remedy studied.
- **Theory is one-dimensional in the main construction** (with radial extensions to d dimensions stated as corollaries without full re-derivation), and the data are assumed compactly supported for the positive result.

## Relevance

对关注扩散模型采样理论与数值稳定性的研究者高度相关：它指出"沿前向边际的分数误差"作为收敛性证书存在根本盲点，并为图像扩散中广泛使用的"裁剪预测样本"启发式提供了首个稳定性解释，对后续设计可证明稳定的扩散采样器、以及将此类分析推广到 Schrödinger 桥等学习型传输动力学具有直接的启发意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the rare-trajectory amplification occur in *normally trained* image diffusion samplers (e.g., EDM, DiT on ImageNet), and if so at what empirical frequency and magnitude under natural (non-stress) initialization?
- How does denoiser projection interact with state-of-the-art high-order solvers (DPM-Solver, Heun, exponential integrators) — does it compose cleanly, or does the linear-growth guarantee only hold for Euler–Maruyama?
- Can the positive result be extended to unbounded or merely sub-Gaussian data supports, e.g., via soft projection / a Lipschitz barrier rather than hard projection onto a known compact C?
- Does the same forward-marginal-vs-trajectory gap and projection-style fix extend to Schrödinger bridges and other learned-transport samplers, where bridge-marginal accuracy is the usual target?
- Are there alternative, weaker *scalar* certificates (e.g., a transition-kernel-weighted score error, or a contractivity statistic as in Tang & Zhao 2024) that actually rank samplers by stability where the on-path L2 budget provably cannot?
- What is the natural (non-stress) entry probability into the unstable region for realistic network families, and can it be bounded or estimated from training diagnostics?
