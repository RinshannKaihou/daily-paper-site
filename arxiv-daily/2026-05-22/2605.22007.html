Methodology
- Commitment step identification: In short-form QA with instruction-tuned models, the answer emission step $t_c$ is fixed at $t = 1$. Token-level entropy analysis shows that predictive information about correctness is localized to this single step. - Semantic probability mass ($P_{\text{mass}}$): Defined as $P_{\text{mass}}(t; c) = \sum_{v \in S_c} p_\theta(v \mid Q, y_{<t})$, where $S_c$ collects the first-token IDs of all surface forms (aliases) for the correct concept $c^*$. Results & Highlights
- Commitment failure rate rises monotonically with scale: Qwen Instruct 16% (0.8B) -> 47% (72B); Llama Instruct shows similar trend. Base models show no such trend. - Within-population gap is dramatic: At matched $P_{\text{mass}}$, the max single-token probability for the correct concept is 0.26 (failures) vs. 0.78 (correct), Cohen's $d = 2.98$ (Qwen3-9B Instruct). - Wrong-token probability sharpens with instruction tuning: Instruct models rise from 0.31 to 0.57 (Qwen) across scale; Base models remain flat at ~0.30. - Multi-token sharpening: Type A divergences (correct bigram prefix, wrong continuation) have $H_{t=2} \approx 0.05$—near-deterministic commitment to wrong multi-token phrases. - Pre-generation hidden states carry predictive signal: Instruct models show

Critical Assessment

**Clean experimental design**
The $P_{\text{mass}}$ probe elegantly separates knowledge availability from selection quality. The within-population matched comparison (controlling for $P_{\text{mass}}$) is methodologically strong.
**Scale and family consistency**
Results hold across Qwen and Llama families, Instruct and Base, from 0.8B to 72B, lending confidence to the generality of findings.
**Actionable implication**
The paper directly suggests concept-aware decoding (argmax over alias-clustered top-k tokens) as a concrete intervention.
**Multi-level evidence**
The sharpening story is supported at first-token, multi-token, and representation levels, creating a coherent narrative.
**Connects to alignment tax**
The framing of confident hallucination as one face of the alignment tax (alongside calibration loss, accuracy loss, mode collapse) is insightful.
**$P_{\text{mass}}$ requires ground-truth aliases**
As the authors acknowledge, this is an analytical probe, not a deployable detector. Practical use requires inferring $S_c$ from context.
