# Manifold-Guided Attention Steering (MAGS)

## 一句话总结

MAGS 通过监测 LLM 注意力头激活在低维"正确性流形"上的偏离，仅在检测到误差信号时施加定向投影修正，实现了轨迹感知的推理时干预，在数学推理、代码生成和分子生成任务上持续超越无干预基线和静态激活引导方法。

## TL;DR

Existing activation steering applies fixed correction vectors regardless of where the model is in its generation, harming correct steps as much as incorrect ones. MAGS learns a low-dimensional correctness manifold from contrastive correct/incorrect traces for each attention head via contrastive PCA on difference vectors. At inference time it monitors each head's proximity score to this manifold and applies targeted projection corrections only when deviation exceeds a learned threshold. Evaluated on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Gemma-4-E4b-it, and GPT-OSS-20B across MATH-500, GSM8K, HumanEval, MBPP, and SMILES benchmarks, MAGS consistently outperforms unsteered baselines and static steering methods (ITI, Angular Steering, Contrastive Decoding) by up to 10.8%, while maintaining perplexity close to the unsteered model.

## 核心贡献

1. **几何洞察与实证验证**：假设并验证了推理错误在特定注意力头输出空间中表现为向低维"错误子空间"的结构性漂移，且正确与错误轨迹在该子空间中高度可分离。漂移信号稀疏且集中在特定头部，而非所有头部的弥散性质。
2. **轨迹感知的条件干预**：提出基于邻近度评分（proximity score）的动态触发机制，仅在注意力头输出投影到错误子空间的幅度超过校准阈值时才施加修正，避免对已正确步骤的破坏。修正方式为正交投影——仅移除错误子空间分量，完全保留其余 d_h - k 个维度的信息（Proposition 1: Information Preservation）。
3. **对比式流形构造**：通过逐问题计算正确/错误轨迹均值差异向量（而非直接池化），再用 PCA 提取错误子空间，确保提取的方向反映的是"错误行为"而非问题内容差异。
4. **多目标组合引导**：提出 MAGS^U 方案，为不同目标学习独立流形，各目标对应不相交的头部集合，避免多目标引导中的修正冲突。
5. **跨模型跨领域通用性**：在三个模型家族（Llama, Gemma, GPT-OSS）、五个基准上均取得一致改进，表明正确性流形是 LLM 注意力几何的普遍特征。

## Key Contributions

1. **Geometric hypothesis and empirical validation**: Hypothesizes and confirms that reasoning errors manifest as structured drift toward a low-dimensional error subspace in specific attention heads. Diagnostic experiments show correct and incorrect trajectories are highly separable (per-head AUROC > 0.65 for selected heads), with the signal sparse and concentrated in specific heads rather than diffuse.
2. **Trajectory-aware conditional intervention**: Introduces a proximity-score-based dynamic trigger that applies orthogonal projection corrections only when a head's projection onto the error subspace exceeds a calibrated threshold, preserving the remaining d_h - k dimensions untouched (Proposition 1: Information Preservation).
3. **Contrastive manifold construction**: Computes per-problem difference vectors between correct and incorrect trace means, then applies PCA to extract the error subspace. This contrastive construction ensures extracted directions capture error-specific signals rather than problem-content variation.
4. **Compositional multi-objective steering (MAGS^U)**: Learns independent manifolds for different objectives (e.g., molecular validity vs. binding affinity), each with disjoint head sets, avoiding correction conflicts in multi-objective settings.
5. **Cross-model, cross-domain generality**: Consistent improvements across three model families (Llama-3.1-8B, Gemma-4-E4b, GPT-OSS-20B) and five benchmarks, suggesting correctness manifolds are a general feature of LLM attention geometry.

## Methodology

The method has three stages:

**Stage 1: Contrastive Error Manifold Construction (offline)**
- For each problem p_i, generate S independent traces, label as correct (T^+) or incorrect (T^-).
- Compute per-problem per-head class means and difference vectors (Eq. 2-3).
- Stack difference vectors into a matrix D and perform SVD to extract top-k right singular vectors as error subspace basis B (Eq. 4-5).
- Compute a global correct-state centroid from all correct traces (Eq. 6).
- Key design: PCA on difference vectors (not raw error activations) isolates error-specific directions, not problem-content features (Remark 1).

**Stage 2: Proximity-Based Error Detection**
- At each decode step t, compute proximity score d_t = ||B(a_t - mu_c)||^2, measuring how much the current head output projects onto the error subspace (Eq. 7).
- A correction triggers only when d_t > tau, where tau is set to the q-th percentile of proximity scores over correct training traces (Eq. 8).

**Stage 3: Manifold-Guided Steering (inference)**
- When triggered, apply: a_tilde = a_t - alpha * B^T B (a_t - mu_e), which decomposes the deviation into error-subspace and complement components, discarding only the former (Eq. 9-10).
- Head selection: pre-select top-K heads by held-out AUROC, reducing per-step overhead from O(L * H * k * d_h) to O(K * k * d_h).
- Full inference procedure detailed in Algorithm 1.

| ITI | 0.498 | 0.855 | 0.573 | 0.548 |
| Angular Steering | 0.506 | 0.857 | 0.591 | 0.546 |
| Contrastive Decoding | 0.492 | 0.858 | 0.591 | 0.555 |
| **MAGS** | **0.530** | **0.867** | **0.604** | **0.574** |

- MATH-500: +5.2 pts over unsteered baseline, the largest gain (multi-step derivations benefit most from trajectory-aware correction).

**Reasoning benchmarks (Table 2: Gemma-4-E4b-it)**

|--------|----------|-------|-----------|------|
| Unsteered | 0.614 | 0.899 | 0.560 | 0.587 |

| **MAGS** | **0.648** | **0.913** | **0.604** | **0.604** |

- Code generation: MAGS improves HumanEval by ~4 pts on both models. All three baselines degrade Gemma's HumanEval performance, confirming unconditional interventions disrupt syntactic coherence.

**Molecular generation (Table 3: GPT-OSS-20B)**

| Method | Validity (up) | Binding Affinity (down) |
|--------|---------------|------------------------|
| Unsteered | 50.4% | -7.36 |
| ITI | 57.8% | -7.20 |
| **MAGS** | **54.8%** | **-7.54** |
| **MAGS^U** | **54.8%** | **-7.56** |

- ITI improves validity but degrades binding affinity (overcorrection to generic valid scaffolds).
- MAGS^U improves both objectives simultaneously via independent manifolds for validity and affinity.

**Perplexity preservation**: MAGS consistently matches or approaches unsteered perplexity across benchmarks, while ITI and Angular Steering generally raise perplexity, indicating distribution distortion.

**Hyperparameter robustness**: On MATH-500 with Llama, MAGS varies narrowly (0.492-0.530) across all configurations, while ITI spans 0.214-0.498 and Angular Steering 0.206-0.506, confirming the proximity threshold reduces sensitivity to other hyperparameters.

## Critical Assessment

### Strengths

- **Principled geometric framing**: Moving from fixed correction vectors to a manifold-based, trajectory-aware approach is conceptually cleaner and better motivated. The contrastive PCA construction is well-designed to isolate error-specific signals.
- **Selective intervention with information preservation guarantee**: Proposition 1 formally proves the correction preserves all information in the (d_h - k)-dimensional complement of the error subspace. The proximity trigger avoids correcting already-correct steps.
- **Comprehensive evaluation**: Three model families (Llama, Gemma, GPT-OSS), five benchmarks, comparison with ITI, Angular Steering, Contrastive Decoding. Perplexity analysis confirms distribution preservation. Ablation on hyperparameter sensitivity demonstrates robustness.
- **Multi-objective compositional steering**: The MAGS^U variant elegantly handles conflicting objectives by learning independent manifolds with disjoint head sets.
- **Latent trajectory analysis**: Visualization of steered vs. unsteered activations provides mechanistic insight into how corrections reshape internal representations.

### Limitations

- **Model scale**: Evaluated on 8B and 4B models (plus GPT-OSS-20B). Unclear whether correctness manifolds maintain the same low-dimensional structure at frontier-model scale (70B+, 405B+).
- **Manifold construction cost**: Requires generating multiple contrastive traces per training problem and computing per-head statistics. The paper does not quantify the one-time offline cost of manifold construction.
- **Threshold calibration**: The q-th percentile threshold is set on correct training traces; sensitivity to distribution shift (e.g., OOD tasks) is not evaluated.
- **Dependence on self-generated traces**: Manifold quality depends on the model's own correct/incorrect traces; for tasks where the model rarely produces correct traces, the construction may fail (the paper discards problems without both correct and incorrect traces within 8 samples).
- **Limited error analysis**: The paper does not analyze which types of reasoning errors are most amenable to correction, or whether MAGS introduces new error modes.

### Relevance

Highly relevant to research on:
- Representation engineering and activation steering
- Inference-time interventions for LLM reliability
- Geometric and mechanistic interpretability of LLM reasoning
- Error correction and self-repair in autoregressive generation
- Multi-objective controllable generation (e.g., molecular design)

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How well do correctness manifolds transfer across model scales -- can manifolds learned from a smaller model guide a larger one?
2. What is the failure mode when a problem has very few correct traces (e.g., at the frontier of model capability)?
3. Can MAGS be combined with process reward models (PRMs) to provide finer-grained step-level supervision for manifold construction?
4. Does the approach extend to non-reasoning tasks where errors are less localized (e.g., hallucination in open-ended generation)?
5. What is the relationship between the selected attention heads and known functional circuits (e.g., induction heads, copy-suppression heads)?
