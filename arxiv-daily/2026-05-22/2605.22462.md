> **一句话总结：** 提出一个五阶段因果特征分析方法论（探针设计、特征提取、因果验证、鲁棒性测试、部署集成），在GPT-2 small的IOI任务上端到端验证，发现SAE特征的检测鲁棒性与因果鲁棒性之间存在关键差距——特征在分布偏移下仍然可靠触发，但其因果效应却大幅退化。

> **TL;DR:** This single-author paper introduces a disciplined five-stage pipeline for causal feature analysis in transformer LMs: probe design, feature extraction (SAE), causal validation (ablation), robustness testing (distribution shifts), and deployment integration (cost-based monitoring). Demonstrated end-to-end on GPT-2 small doing Indirect Object Identification (IOI), it recovers the canonical IOI circuit (L9H9 recovery +1.02) and extracts per-name SAE features with large selectivity (30-50 activation units). The central empirical contribution is the discovery that real LMs operate in an "intermediate causal regime": features are selective and individually causal, but collectively redundant (ablating all 15 top features leaves 98% accuracy). Selectivity ratio anticorrelates with causal force (r = -0.56), and detection robustness does not imply causal robustness — under prompt reformulation, feature firing patterns survive but ablation effects drop by ~50%. The paper also adapts the NLA evaluation framework (Fraser-Taliente et al., 2026) to show that interpreted SAE features explain only 31% of activation variance vs. the SAE's 99.7%.

## 核心贡献

- 提出系统化的五阶段因果特征分析方法论，将探针设计、SAE特征提取、因果验证、鲁棒性测试和部署集成形成闭环流水线
- 提出"中间因果机制"(intermediate causal regime)概念：特征具有选择性和因果效力，但集体冗余，消融15个最选择性特征后模型仍保持98%准确率
- 发现选择性比率与因果效力呈负相关(r = -0.56)：最显著的选择性特征往往因果效力最小
- 揭示检测鲁棒性与因果鲁棒性的根本差距：特征在提示重构后仍可靠触发，但消融效果下降约50%
- 引入NLA启发的保真度分层评估：15个名字选择性特征仅解释31%的激活方差，而完整SAE解释99.7%
- 展示基于成本的部署评估：最优SAE-only监控配置达到每1000查询$8.96的成本（节省99.1%）
- 所有实验在CPU上约20分钟完成，使用TransformerLens和公开GPT-2权重

## Key Contributions

- **Five-stage methodology** formalizing the complementarity of existing interpretability techniques (activation patching, SAE decomposition, causal ablation, robustness testing, deployment evaluation) into a disciplined pipeline
- **Intermediate causal regime** concept: features that look causally important by correlational standards are only partially causal. This is argued to be the typical case for real language models with redundant solutions
- **Selectivity-causality anticorrelation** (r = -0.56): the most strikingly selective SAE features (e.g., Sid at 321:1 selectivity) have the smallest causal effects, while moderately selective features (Jane, Tom, Mary at 9:1 to 19:1) have the largest causal effects
- **Detection vs. causal robustness gap**: under prompt frame reformulation ("When...went to" -> "After...arrived at...it was...who handed"), feature firing patterns survive (activation drops only 10-20%) but ablation effects drop by ~50% (mean 0.89 -> 0.42 logits)
- **Fidelity stratification** (NLA-inspired): the 15 name-selective features explain only 30.7% of activation variance at the critical layer/position, vs. the SAE's full reconstruction ceiling of 99.7%
- **Three causal regimes** hypothesized: (1) clean causal (features selective and ablation produces large effects, e.g., refusal directions), (2) selective-but-inert (correlational only), (3) intermediate (the IOI case)
- **Cost-based deployment evaluation**: under stated assumptions ($50/FN, $0.42/FP, 2% error rate), SAE-only at threshold 15 is optimal, with composition strategy sensitive to cost ratio and error base rate

## Methodology

The paper proposes a five-stage pipeline applied to GPT-2 small (124M params, 12 layers, 12 heads) on the IOI task (Wang et al., 2022):

**Stage 1 - Probe Design:** Pre-registers seven hypotheses (H1-H7) covering circuit recovery, per-name SAE features, causal vs. correlational status, fidelity stratification, reliability stratification, robustness, and deployment characteristics.

**Stage 2 - Feature Extraction:** Trains a sparse autoencoder (d_sae=1024, 4x expansion, L1 coeff 0.5, 3000 steps) on residual stream activations at layer 9 END position (identified by patching). Achieves L0=87.3 and 99.8% variance explained. Finds per-name selective features for all 16 names with selectivity gaps of 28-48 activation units.

**Stage 3 - Causal Validation:** Single-feature ablation produces specific but small effects (~0.67 logit drop per feature on preferred-name prompts). Cumulative ablation of all 15 top features: logit difference drops from +3.71 to +3.33, accuracy from 100% to 98.4%. Two NLA-inspired evaluations: (a) fidelity stratification — interpreted features explain 30.7% of variance vs. SAE's 99.7%; (b) reliability stratification — selectivity ratio anticorrelates with causal force (r = -0.56).

**Stage 4 - Robustness Testing:** Three distribution shifts: (4a) OOD content words (synonym replacement), (4b) held-out names (8 new single-token + 5 multi-token), (4c) prompt frame reformulation. Circuit transfers cleanly across all shifts (L9H9 recovery +0.96 under reformulation vs. +1.02 in-distribution). SAE features fire reliably but ablation effects degrade: mean drop 0.89 -> 0.42 under reformulation. Held-out names reveal generic name-position features (features 103, 179, 181) not surfaced by in-distribution analysis.

**Stage 5 - Deployment Integration:** (5a) Monitor calibration — all features achieve ROC-AUC 1.00 in-distribution. (5b) Monitor robustness — AUC stays 1.00 under distribution shifts. (5c) Defense-in-depth composition with heuristic parser — AND composition achieves zero FPR across all conditions. (5d) Cost-based evaluation with stated assumptions — optimal is SAE-only at threshold 15 ($8.96/1000 queries, 99.1% savings).

## Results & Highlights

- **Finding 1 (Circuit Recovery):** Activation patching recovers the canonical IOI circuit. L9H9 alone: +1.02 recovery. L9H6: +0.32. L10H0: +0.11. L10H7 (backup): -0.54. H1 confirmed.
- **Finding 2 (Per-Name Features):** SAE recovers per-name IO-selective features for all 16 names, with selectivity gaps of 28-48 activation units. H2 confirmed.
- **Finding 3 (Specific but Partial Causality):** Features are individually causal (0.67 logit drop each) but collectively not necessary (98.4% accuracy after ablating all 15). H3 resolves to "causal contribution" branch.
- **Finding 4 (Fidelity Gap):** 15 name-selective features explain 30.7% of activation variance vs. SAE's 99.7%. Single top feature explains 25% by magnitude but only 3.4% for selective features. H4 confirmed.
- **Finding 5 (Selectivity-Causality Anticorrelation):** r = -0.56 between selectivity ratio and causal drop. Top 3 by selectivity (Sid 321:1, Eve 165:1, Adam 80:1) have smallest effects (0.34, 0.30, 0.52). H5 confirmed.
- **Finding 6 (Detection vs. Causal Robustness):** Circuit robust to all shifts. Feature firing robust to paraphrase and reformulation. But feature ablation effects drop ~50% under reformulation while firing drops only 10-20%. H6 confirmed in nuanced form.
- **Finding 7 (Deployment Composition):** SAE-only at threshold 15 is optimal ($8.96/1000 queries). Composition strategy varies: AND for high FN/FP cost ratios, OR for high error rates, SAE-only for low error rates. H7 confirmed.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: Moderate.** Each individual technique (activation patching, SAE training, ablation, robustness testing) is well-established. The primary novelty is the systematic five-stage pipeline and the empirical findings about the intermediate causal regime. The selectivity-causality anticorrelation and the detection-vs-causal robustness gap are genuine contributions, though they may not surprise researchers who have worked extensively with SAEs.

- **Rigor: Strong.** The pre-registration of seven hypotheses before experimentation is commendable and rare in interpretability work. The NLA-inspired fidelity and reliability stratifications provide quantitative grounding. The robustness testing across three distinct distribution shifts is thorough. The honest discussion of what the deployment evaluation is and is not (Section 12.4 "What this is and is not") is exemplary.

- **Reproducibility: Excellent.** Public model (GPT-2 small), standard library (TransformerLens), CPU-only computation (~20 minutes total). A motivated researcher could replicate the entire paper in an afternoon. No code repository is mentioned in the abstract, but the implementation details are sufficiently specified.

- **Significance: Moderate to High.** The detection-vs-causal robustness gap is an important cautionary finding for the field. The intermediate causal regime framing provides a useful conceptual tool. However, the generalizability question looms large — GPT-2 small on IOI is a well-studied toy setting, and whether these findings transfer to frontier models on complex tasks remains entirely open.

- **Red Flags:**
  - Single-author paper claiming a broad methodological framework based on one model and one task. The five stages are reasonable but not validated beyond this setting.
  - The SAE is deliberately small (1024 features, 3000 steps) and the author acknowledges this is below state-of-the-art. Some findings (e.g., partial causality) may be artifacts of an undersized SAE rather than fundamental properties of the model.
  - The deployment evaluation (Section 12) uses entirely synthetic cost parameters ($50/FN, $0.42/FP). While the methodology is claimed to transfer, the 99.1% savings figure is attention-grabbing but meaningless in absolute terms.
  - The connection to NLA work (Fraser-Taliente et al. 2026) is through adaptation of evaluation frameworks, not through actual NLA training or comparison. The "NLA-inspired" framing may overstate the depth of engagement with that line of work.
  - The hypothesis that "the intermediate regime is typical" for real language models is speculative — it is demonstrated only on GPT-2 small doing IOI.

## Strengths

- Pre-registered hypotheses (H1-H7) provide a strong methodological template that increases trust in the empirical findings
- The selectivity-causality anticorrelation (r = -0.56) is a concrete, surprising finding with clear implications for SAE-based interpretability practice
- The detection-vs-causal robustness gap is empirically clean and operationally important: a feature that reliably fires may not reliably cause
- The fidelity stratification (31% vs 99.7% variance explained) quantifies a gap that is often discussed qualitatively but rarely measured
- Exceptional honesty about limitations — the author explicitly states what the deployment numbers are and are not, and identifies the small model/task/SAE as limitations
- The three-regime framework (clean causal, selective-but-inert, intermediate) provides a useful conceptual vocabulary
- All experiments reproducible on CPU in 20 minutes

## Limitations

- Demonstrated only on GPT-2 small (124M params) performing IOI — whether the intermediate causal regime generalizes to larger models (billions of parameters) and more complex tasks (open-ended generation, multi-hop reasoning) is entirely unknown
- The SAE is deliberately small (1024 features, 3000 steps) and below the scale where monosemanticity peaks — some findings may reflect SAE limitations rather than model properties
- The NLA-inspired evaluations are reframings of existing SAE data, not actual NLA experiments — no NLA was trained or compared
- The deployment evaluation uses entirely synthetic cost parameters — the 99.1% savings figure should not be quoted out of context
- The held-out name experiment reveals that multi-token names fundamentally break the single-position SAE methodology, a limitation that applies to most real-world text
- The paper does not compare against other systematic interpretability pipelines (e.g., Automated Circuit Discovery, Sparse Feature Circuits) to validate that the five-stage approach produces strictly better findings

## Relevance

本文对机械可解释性领域有重要贡献，特别是关于SAE特征的检测鲁棒性与因果鲁棒性之间差距的发现，以及对选择性不等于因果性的定量验证。对于使用SAE进行模型内部机制分析的研究者，这些发现具有重要的方法论警示意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the selectivity-causality anticorrelation (r = -0.56) replicate with larger SAEs (e.g., 64k+ features as in Templeton et al. 2024) or with gated SAEs (Rajamanoharan et al. 2024)?
- At what model scale does the "intermediate causal regime" give way to either the clean causal or selective-but-inert regime, if at all?
- Can the detection-vs-causal robustness gap be bridged through better feature extraction, or is it a fundamental property of distributed representations?
- How do the findings change for tasks without a known circuit ground truth — i.e., where Stage 1 (probe design) cannot be validated against prior literature?
- Does the optimal deployment monitoring configuration change qualitatively when the monitored behavior is safety-relevant (e.g., deception, harmful content) rather than a simple name-identification error?
- What is the relationship between feature redundancy (many features each contributing small causal effects) and the model's generalization ability?
