> **一句话总结：** CEDAR通过学习正交变换U=exp(A-A^T)结合top-k稀疏瓶颈，在不扩展维度的前提下将CLIP/BLIP嵌入解耦为可被文本概念解释的轴对齐坐标，用户研究证实其解释质量优于MSAE等稀疏自编码器。

> **TL;DR:** CEDAR learns an invertible orthogonal rotation with a top-k sparsity bottleneck to disentangle pretrained VLM embeddings into axis-aligned interpretable coordinates, achieving competitive reconstruction quality vs. SAEs (which require 8x dimensionality expansion) while producing more human-aligned explanations validated through three user studies.

## 核心贡献

- 提出CEDAR（Conceptual Embedding Disentanglement via Adaptive Rotation），通过正交变换U=exp(A-A^T)在原始嵌入空间内实现解耦，无需像SAE那样扩展维度（CEDAR用768维，SAE基线用6144维）
- 设计top-k稀疏算子+课程学习策略（从密集逐渐过渡到稀疏），训练后每个坐标对应一个可解释的语义轴
- 在CLIP类判别模型中，坐标可通过CLIP文本编码器与文本概念对齐；在BLIP类生成模型中，稀疏嵌入可直接解码为自然语言描述
- 三项用户研究（共66名参与者）系统验证了解释质量，CEDAR在偏好度、概念选择准确性和描述质量上均优于MSAE

## Key Contributions

- Introduces CEDAR, which learns an orthogonal transformation U = exp(A - A^T) paired with a top-k sparsity bottleneck to reorganize pretrained embeddings into sparse, axis-aligned, disentangled coordinates -- all within the original 768-dimensional space (vs. 6144 for SAE baselines)
- Proposes a homotopy-style training curriculum that smoothly transitions from dense (k=D) to sparse (k_target=10) reconstruction, followed by random k sampling for robustness
- Provides a unified interpretability framework: concept-based explanations via CLIP text encoder alignment for discriminative models, and free-form text generation via the decoder for generative models (BLIP)
- Conducts three rigorous user studies (23, 22, 21 participants respectively) demonstrating that CEDAR produces more preferred, relevant, and semantically faithful explanations compared to MSAE
- Achieves competitive reconstruction-sparsity trade-off and ranks among top-2 methods in information capacity at higher sparsity regimes, supporting the hypothesis that VLM entanglement is primarily a basis misalignment problem

## Methodology

CEDAR operates as a post-hoc method applied to frozen pretrained vision-language embeddings. The core pipeline is:

1. **Invertible transformation:** Given a pretrained embedding z in R^D, CEDAR applies z_tilde = U(z - b), where b is the mean embedding and U = exp(A - A^T) is an orthogonal matrix (guaranteeing U^T U = I and ||Uz||_2 = ||z||_2). The matrix exponential parameterization ensures invertibility and prevents trivial scaling/collapse.

2. **Top-k sparsification:** In the transformed space, the top-k operator Pi_k retains only the k coordinates with largest absolute magnitude, setting all others to zero. The sparse representation is mapped back via z_hat = U^{-1}(Pi_k(U(z - b))) + b.

3. **Training objective:** Minimize E[||z - z_hat||_1] over U, using a curriculum that starts at k=D (identity reconstruction) and gradually anneals to k_target=10. After reaching target sparsity, k is uniformly sampled from {1, ..., 2*k_target - 1} for robustness.

4. **Interpretation:** Each coordinate in the transformed space corresponds to a direction c_d = U^{-1}_{[d,:]} in the original space. For CLIP-like models, each axis is matched to its nearest text concept via cosine similarity. For BLIP-like models, the sparse reconstruction is passed through the text decoder to generate natural language captions.

**Experimental setup:** CLIP ViT-L/14 backbone, trained on ImageNet-1K training set, evaluated on validation split. Compared against MSAE, TopK-SAE (k=64), ReLU-SAE (lambda=0.01), and BatchTopK-SAE (k=32). All SAE baselines use 8x expansion (768 -> 6144 dimensions). Metrics include FVU, information capacity (IC = log C(n,k)), cosine similarity (CS), linear probing cross-entropy (LP), and CKNNA.

## Results & Highlights

- **Reconstruction-sparsity trade-off (Table 1):** At matched FVU levels (0.25, 0.3, 0.35), CEDAR remains competitive with all SAE variants despite operating in a much lower-dimensional space. CEDAR ranks among the top-2 methods in information capacity (IC), meaning it achieves comparable fidelity with less encoded information.
- **Semantic preservation (Figure 8):** CKNNA and linear probing cross-entropy show CEDAR maintains competitive semantic alignment. TopK and BatchTopK achieve slightly better CKNNA, but CEDAR follows similar degradation trends.
- **User Study 1 -- Method preference:** Participants consistently preferred CEDAR over MSAE across all preference levels, with a significant share expressing strong preference for CEDAR.
- **User Study 2 -- Concept selection:** CEDAR concepts were significantly more frequently identified as relevant (majority of cases: 2 or 3 out of 3 selected) while MSAE concepts were frequently rejected (0 or 1 selected).
- **User Study 3 -- Description quality:** CEDAR captions closely matched the dense (non-sparse) baseline in quality ratings, while a sparse variant without the learned transformation U was dominated by low ("does not describe") ratings -- proving the transformation is essential.
- **Qualitative results:** CEDAR identifies more accurate high-level concepts (e.g., "dog", "husky", "wolf" vs. MSAE's "marley", "petrol") and generates coherent sparse BLIP captions. Individual neurons show semantic consistency (e.g., neuron 347 consistently activates for fishing-related imagery).
- **Key insight:** The dense-to-sparse ablation proves that sparsity alone is insufficient -- the learned rotation U is critical for preserving semantic information under compression.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: Moderate-to-high.** The core idea -- using an orthogonal rotation instead of overcomplete expansion for disentanglement -- is clean and well-motivated. The matrix exponential parameterization for orthogonality is standard but effective. The key conceptual contribution is reframing entanglement as a basis misalignment problem rather than an insufficient-dimensionality problem.
- **Rigor: Good.** The evaluation is thorough: quantitative benchmarks following the MSAE protocol (Table 1 with 5 metrics at 3 FVU levels), three separate user studies with proper randomization, and qualitative analysis of neuron activations. The dense-to-sparse ablation (Study 3) is a particularly strong control showing the transformation is necessary.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate.** The method is architecturally simple (a single orthogonal matrix + top-k operator). However, the training curriculum details (tau, k_target, k_max) and the predefined text vocabulary for concept matching are only partially specified. The MSAE codebase is used for baselines, which helps.
- **Significance: Moderate.** The paper provides a convincing argument that SAE overcompleteness may be unnecessary for VLM interpretability. However, the comparison is limited to CLIP ViT-L/14 -- whether the basis-misalignment hypothesis holds for larger models or LLMs remains open. The user studies, while well-designed, have relatively small sample sizes (21-23 participants).
- **Red flags:** (1) The orthogonal transformation is inherently linear, which limits expressiveness -- the authors acknowledge this. (2) CEDAR is inherently lossless without sparsification, but with sparsification the comparison against lossy SAEs may not be entirely apples-to-apple. (3) The concept vocabulary for CLIP alignment is predefined but not detailed in the main paper. (4) User study participants were likely not domain experts, which may bias preference toward "obvious" concepts.

## Strengths

- Elegant theoretical motivation: demonstrates that entanglement is primarily a basis alignment issue, not a dimensionality issue
- Preserves original embedding geometry through invertible orthogonal transformation -- no information loss in the dense regime
- Model-agnostic framework supporting both discriminative (CLIP) and generative (BLIP) VLM architectures
- Three well-designed user studies with proper controls and randomization provide strong evidence for interpretability gains
- Competitive quantitative performance despite using 768 dimensions vs. SAEs' 6144 dimensions
- The dense-to-sparse ablation in Study 3 cleanly isolates the contribution of the learned transformation

## Limitations

- Orthogonal (linear) transformations limit expressiveness; nonlinear alternatives (normalizing flows) are acknowledged but not explored
- Text-based supervision is not incorporated into training -- concepts are mapped post-hoc via cosine similarity to a fixed vocabulary
- Evaluation is limited to CLIP ViT-L/14 (768-dim); scalability to larger models (e.g., SigLIP, EVA-CLIP with higher-dimensional embeddings) is not tested
- User studies have modest sample sizes (21-23 participants) and participant demographics are not reported
- The predefined text vocabulary for concept matching is not detailed -- its coverage and quality could significantly affect interpretability results
- Does not modify or improve the original model; purely a post-hoc analysis tool

## Relevance

本文与视觉-语言模型的可解释性和mechanistic interpretability研究高度相关。CEDAR提出的"基失配导致纠缠"假说为理解多模态嵌入的内部结构提供了新视角，其无需维度扩展的解耦方法对VLM分析和可控生成有实用价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the basis-misalignment hypothesis hold for larger-scale models (e.g., CLIP ViT-bigG, SigLIP) and for language model embeddings (e.g., in LLMs)?
- How would incorporating text-based supervision directly into the training objective (rather than post-hoc alignment) affect the quality of disentangled concepts?
- Can the learned disentangled coordinates be leveraged for downstream tasks such as controllable image generation, zero-shot classification, or adversarial robustness?
- What is the sensitivity of CEDAR to the choice of k_target, and is there an optimal sparsity level that balances interpretability with reconstruction fidelity?
- How does the method compare against nonlinear invertible transformations (e.g., normalizing flows) in terms of both reconstruction quality and interpretability?
- Can CEDAR be applied to intermediate layer representations (not just the final embedding) for finer-grained mechanistic analysis?
