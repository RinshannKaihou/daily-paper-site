> **一句话总结：** 本文通过FFN表示的Renyi有效秩分析发现，不同优化器（AdamW vs. Muon/NorMuon）在同一Transformer架构上产生截然不同的频谱缩放规律——Muon在TAIL token上实现线性hard-rank缩放（beta=1.02），而AdamW仅为0.44；匹配的困惑度不意味着匹配的表示结构，且优化器引起的频谱偏移常超过注意力秩或位置编码等架构干预的效果。

> **TL;DR:** This paper demonstrates that optimizer choice (AdamW, Muon, NorMuon, Dion) fundamentally changes how FFN width converts into spectral capacity in Transformers, even under fixed architecture and data. Muon achieves linear hard-rank scaling on TAIL tokens (beta=1.02) vs. AdamW's weak 0.44 -- a 2.3x gap. Crucially, matched validation perplexity does NOT imply matched spectral geometry: extended AdamW training that matches Dion(1/16) in loss still shows near-zero hard-rank scaling. Optimizer-induced spectral shifts exceed architectural interventions (attention rank, RoPE removal) in 28 of 30 comparisons. Experiments span GPT-2 160M and 350M with 5 optimizers across 8 FFN widths, using FineWeb-Edu. The work motivates treating optimization as a first-class axis in scaling law research and optimizer-architecture co-design.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示优化器是表示缩放的一等公民：同一GPT-2架构在AdamW、Muon、NorMuon和Dion下展现出截然不同的FFN频谱缩放规律，hard-soft rank asymmetry本身也是优化器依赖的。
- Muon/NorMuon在TAIL token上实现线性hard-rank缩放（beta_hard=1.02/1.04），而AdamW仅为0.44；MID regime的AdamW-to-Muon增益最大（Delta beta=+0.69）。
- 证明"匹配的损失不等于匹配的表示结构"：AdamW 12K步匹配Dion(1/16)的困惑度，但hard-rank缩放指数降至0.03（vs Dion的0.50）。
- Dion rank sweep揭示update rank是hard-capacity的控制旋钮：orthonormalization本身不够，低rank更新使缩放退化为AdamW水平。
- 优化器引起的频谱偏移在28/30个对比中超过注意力秩架构干预的效果；RoPE移除产生优化器依赖的频谱重分配。
- 附加发现：Muon族优化器能训练AdamW无法稳定的partial PostLN架构，扩展了可训练的架构空间。

## Key Contributions

- **Optimizer-induced spectral scaling laws:** The same Transformer architecture produces markedly different FFN spectral-capacity scaling laws depending on optimizer. Hard-soft rank asymmetry is optimizer-dependent, not an architectural invariant.
- **Matched loss != matched geometry:** AdamW trained for 12K steps matches Dion(1/16) perplexity but exhibits near-zero hard-rank scaling (beta_hard=0.03) vs. Dion's 0.50, demonstrating that optimizer-induced representation geometry is not a transient convergence artifact.
- **Frequency-dependent capacity allocation:** MID tokens show the largest AdamW-to-Muon hard-rank gain (+0.69), followed by TAIL (+0.58) and HEAD (+0.33). Optimizer effects are most diagnostic in MID and TAIL regimes.
- **Update rank as a capacity control knob:** Dion rank sweep shows orthonormalization alone is insufficient -- low update rank (r=1/16) produces AdamW-like scaling, while high rank (r=1/2) approaches Muon/NorMuon.
- **Optimizer effects exceed architectural interventions:** In TAIL hard-rank, AdamW-to-Muon shift (|Delta beta|=0.74) is 2.0x the attention-rank effect and 2.5-5.2x RoPE/NoPE effects. Attention-rank intervention changes which optimizer is best matched to the architecture.
- **Expanded trainable architecture space:** Muon-family optimizers train partial PostLN configurations where AdamW diverges, showing optimizer geometry affects both capacity utilization and the feasible architecture space.

## Methodology

**Architecture & Training:** GPT-style decoder-only Transformers with Pre-RMSNorm, RoPE, squared-ReLU FFNs, QK-normalization (modded-nanoGPT configuration). Primary experiments at 160M (12 layers, 12 heads, d_model=768) with FFN width sweep D=m*d_model for m in {1,...,8}. Replication at 350M (24 layers, 32 heads, d_model=1024) with m in {1,2,3,4}. Trained on FineWeb-Edu (3.15B tokens at 160M, 4.19B at 350M), sequence length 512, batch size 1024.

**Optimizers compared:** AdamW (lr=3e-3), Muon (lr=2e-2, Newton-Schulz orthogonalization), NorMuon (Muon + per-neuron normalization), Dion at rank fractions r in {1/2, 1/4, 1/8, 1/16}. All matrix-aware optimizers use Lion for scalar parameters with spectral-condition LR scaling.

**Spectral analysis framework:** For each FFN layer, the authors probe pre-activation (z = W_in * x) and post-activation (a = phi(z)) states. They compute empirical covariance matrices and trace-normalized eigenspectra, then measure effective rank using the **Renyi entropy family** R_alpha = exp(H_alpha(p)):

- **Soft rank (alpha=1):** Shannon-entropy weighted spectral spread (sensitive to diffuse eigenvalue distribution).
- **Hard rank (alpha=2):** Participation ratio (sensitive to concentration in dominant eigendirections).
- **Hard-soft asymmetry:** Delta_{1,2} = beta_soft - beta_hard, where higher values indicate concentrated spectra.

**Scaling law fitting:** R(D) proportional to D^beta, fitted across FFN widths. Reported separately for HEAD/MID/TAIL token frequency regimes (defined by cumulative occurrence mass tertiles). Full Renyi sweep at alpha in {0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 3, 5} in appendix.

**Architectural interventions:** (1) Increasing per-head attention rank by reducing heads from 12 to 6 at fixed parameter count. (2) Removing RoPE (NoPE) at 350M. (3) Partial PostLN normalization placement as a trainability stress test.

## Results & Highlights

- **TAIL hard-rank scaling:** Muon beta_hard=1.02 (R^2=0.81), AdamW beta_hard=0.44 (R^2=0.66). NorMuon reaches 1.04. The gap is not closable by learning rate tuning -- Muon's worst LR gives beta=0.80, exceeding AdamW's best at 0.44.
- **Extended AdamW training breaks hard-rank scaling:** At 12K steps, AdamW's aggregate beta_hard drops to 0.03 (R^2=0.01) while perplexity improves. Wider FFN configs lose dominant-mode capacity faster than narrower ones, breaking the width-capacity ordering needed for power-law fits.
- **Dion rank bottleneck:** At r=1/2, Dion approaches Muon (TAIL beta_hard=0.88); at r=1/16, it degrades to AdamW levels (0.40). Soft-rank degrades more gradually (0.95 to 0.72), showing low update rank primarily limits dominant-mode hard capacity.
- **Scale consistency (160M to 350M):** Optimizer ordering is preserved. At 350M, Muon TAIL beta_hard=1.13 (R^2=0.94), AdamW=0.39 (R^2=0.82). Hard-soft asymmetry patterns also generalize.
- **Optimizer > architecture (attention rank):** Optimizer-induced gain exceeds attention-rank shift in 28/30 comparisons. The attention-rank intervention also changes which optimizer achieves the best scaling exponent -- under 12 heads, NorMuon leads in 5/6 regimes; under 6 heads, Muon leads in all 6.
- **RoPE removal is optimizer-dependent:** NoPE increases Muon HEAD beta_hard from 1.083 to 1.286 but decreases NorMuon from 1.023 to 0.656. Under NoPE, Muon shifts peak positional processing to earliest layers; NorMuon is more distributed. Muon/NorMuon flip from HEAD-favored to TAIL-favored positional processing under NoPE.
- **PostLN trainability:** Muon/NorMuon train PostLN-75 at PPL=40.9/32.8, while AdamW only reaches PPL=106.7 with lower LR. Full PostLN remains unstable for all optimizers.
- **Renyi-family analysis:** Pre-activation spectra show stable optimizer hierarchy (NorMuon > Muon > Dion(1/2) > Dion(1/16) > AdamW) across all alpha. Post-activation, the hierarchy reorganizes -- Dion(1/2) achieves highest post-activation hard rank due to nonlinear reinjection. AdamW's reinjection ratio (rho_2=51.9) is ~10.8x larger than Muon's (4.8), but compensatory rather than beneficial.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** This is the first work to systematically show that optimizer choice changes spectral scaling *exponents* (not just efficiency factors) in LLM representations. The "matched loss != matched geometry" finding is a genuinely new insight with practical implications.
- **Rigor: Strong.** The experimental design is careful: fixed architecture/data, controlled optimizer sweeps, FFN width sweeps (8 points at 160M, 4 at 350M), learning rate ablations, extended training controls, layer-wise robustness checks, and architectural intervention comparisons. The Dion rank sweep is a clever controlled experiment isolating update rank from orthonormalization.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Full hyperparameters provided in appendix (Tables 9-11). Modded-nanoGPT is public. Compute requirements are modest. Single-seed limitation is acknowledged.
- **Significance: Potentially high, with caveats.** The finding that optimizer geometry reshapes representation scaling more than architectural interventions is impactful. However, the link to downstream task performance is not established -- spectral capacity may not directly translate to better benchmarks. The work raises an important hypothesis but does not close the loop on practical impact.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - **Single-seed experiments:** All scaling fits use single seeds per (optimizer, width) configuration. The authors acknowledge this but do not quantify seed variance. Some HEAD/MID hard-rank fits have low R^2 (e.g., AdamW MID hard-rank R^2=0.36), which is acknowledged but somewhat undermines precision of those exponents.
  - **Limited model scale:** 160M and 350M are small by modern standards. The 350M sweep is coarser (4 width points). Whether these patterns hold at 1B+ scale remains unknown.
  - **FFN-only analysis:** The entire analysis targets FFN representations. Attention layers, embedding layers, and the LM head are not analyzed spectrally. The claim that "optimization is a first-class axis" is thus limited to FFN capacity.
  - **No downstream evaluation:** The paper measures spectral properties and perplexity but does not evaluate on any downstream benchmarks. Whether Muon's superior spectral scaling leads to better factual recall on rare knowledge, better few-shot performance, or other practical gains is untested.
  - **Token frequency vs. knowledge:** The HEAD/MID/TAIL stratification is by token type frequency, not by knowledge concept frequency. The connection to "long-tail knowledge" in the abstract is somewhat loose.

## Strengths

- Elegant framing: the optimizer as a representation scaling axis, not just a convergence tool.
- Clean controlled experiments: Dion rank sweep is particularly clever for isolating causal mechanisms.
- "Matched loss != matched geometry" is a powerful conceptual contribution with practical implications for model evaluation.
- Comprehensive appendix (30 tables, layer-wise analyses, Renyi-family sweeps, learning rate ablations, PostLN trainability, positional processing analysis).
- Honest about limitations (scale, single-seed, FFN-only, no downstream tasks).
- The finding that architectural interventions have optimizer-dependent effects is important for the architecture search community.

## Limitations

- **Scale:** 160M/350M models are far from frontier scale. The compute required for scaling-law measurements (training 8 width variants per optimizer) makes 1B+ experiments expensive, but the gap remains.
- **FFN-only scope:** The analysis does not extend to attention layers, which also contain significant parameters and may exhibit different optimizer-dependent behavior.
- **No downstream task evaluation:** Perplexity and spectral metrics are proxies; the practical impact of better spectral scaling on downstream performance is unverified.
- **Single-seed uncertainty:** No error bars from multiple seeds; some fits have low R^2 values that warrant caution in interpreting exact exponent values.
- **Correlation vs. causation:** While Dion rank sweeps provide some causal evidence, the broader claim that optimizer geometry *causes* better representations (rather than correlating with factors that cause better performance) is not fully established. The extended training experiment is the strongest causal evidence, but it only shows that AdamW's hard-rank scaling degrades, not that Muon's better scaling is the mechanism for its lower perplexity.

## Relevance

本文通过系统的频谱分析揭示了优化器几何对Transformer FFN表示缩放的根本影响，与理解大模型缩放规律、设计新型优化器（如Muon族）、以及优化器-架构协同设计的研究方向高度相关。发现"匹配损失不等于匹配表示"对模型评估方法学也有重要启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does better TAIL hard-rank spectral scaling translate to improved performance on rare-knowledge benchmarks (e.g., long-tail factual QA, low-resource language tasks)?
- Do these optimizer-dependent spectral scaling laws persist at 1B+ parameter scale, or does the gap narrow as models get larger?
- Can spectral diagnostics (hard-soft asymmetry, Renyi-family profiles) be used as real-time training monitors to guide optimizer switching or hyperparameter scheduling?
- How does the optimizer interact with data mixture, multilinguality, and data quality -- are spectral effects amplified or dampened under heterogeneous training data?
- Is there a causal pathway from Muon's superior spectral scaling to its lower perplexity, or are these parallel phenomena driven by a common underlying mechanism?
- Could hybrid optimizers (e.g., AdamW for attention layers, Muon for FFN layers) achieve the best of both worlds, as the paper's co-design framing suggests?
- What happens to spectral scaling under mixed-precision training, distributed training, or different normalization schemes (LayerNorm vs. RMSNorm)?
