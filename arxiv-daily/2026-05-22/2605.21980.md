# Interpreting and Enhancing Emotional Circuits in Large Vision-Language Models via Cross-Modal Information Flow

## 一句话总结

本文提出了一种基于 steering vector 的因果归因框架来解析大型视觉语言模型 (LVLMs) 中处理情感的内部机制，发现视觉情感线索在中层通过情感特定的注意力头聚合，而在深层通过情感通用路径转化为叙事生成，并据此设计了无需训练的推理时干预方法 VEEENA，在 MER-UniBench 上将平均性能从 58.1% 提升至 64.8%。

## TL;DR

The authors investigate how Large Vision-Language Models internally process emotional information from visual inputs. They introduce a steering-vector-based causal attribution framework for descriptive emotional reasoning and construct semantically controlled visual counterfactuals. Applying this framework reveals a three-stage "Adapt-Aggregate-Execute" mechanism with a key functional decoupling: in middle layers (around layer 19), sentiment-specific attention heads aggregate visual emotional cues into the query token, while in deep layers (layer 35), emotion-general heads drive narrative generation. They further trace MLP neurons, finding "Contextual Trigger Neurons" in aggregation encoding situational cues and "Explicit State Neurons" in execution encoding emotional states. Based on these findings, they propose VEEENA, a training-free inference-time intervention comprising Visual Emotion Enhancement (VEE) that strengthens attention flow and Emotional Neuron Augmentation (ENA) that amplifies semantic activation. On MER-UniBench across 9 datasets, VEEENA raises average performance from 58.1% to 64.8% (+6.7%) with only ~3% parameter overhead, outperforming baselines VISTA and PAI, while preserving general vision-language capabilities.

## 核心贡献

1. 提出了基于 steering vector 的因果归因框架，通过构建语义控制的视觉反事实数据和 Latent Restoration Metric，克服了离散指标无法捕捉情感表达整体性的问题，首次精确定位了 LVLMs 中的情感电路
2. 揭示了"适应-聚合-执行"三阶段情感机制及其功能解耦：中层使用情感特定的注意力头聚合情感意图，深层通过情感通用头执行叙事生成，实现了从"特定到通用"的处理层次
3. 提出了 VEEENA，一种无需训练的推理时干预框架，包含 VEE（增强注意力流）和 ENA（放大语义激活），仅干预约 3% 参数即可显著提升情感理解性能
4. 在 MER-UniBench 的 9 个数据集上验证了方法有效性，平均提升 6.7%，同时保持通用视觉语言能力不受影响

## Key Contributions

1. **Steering-vector-based causal attribution framework**: Constructs semantically controlled visual counterfactuals (emotional vs. neutral image pairs with neutral text) and designs a Latent Restoration Metric that shifts evaluation from discrete token probabilities to hidden-space similarity, enabling precise localization of emotional circuits in LVLMs
2. **"Adapt-Aggregate-Execute" mechanism discovery**: Reveals functional decoupling across three stages -- Shallow Layers adapt visual features for modality alignment, Middle Layers (~layer 19) aggregate emotional signals via sentiment-specific attention heads (with distinct clusters for negative emotions), and Deep Layers (~layer 35) execute narrative generation via emotion-general heads
3. **VEEENA intervention framework**: A training-free, surgical inference-time method comprising Visual Emotion Enhancement (VEE, flow-aware attention scaling with phase-specific enhancement during prefill/decoding) and Emotional Neuron Augmentation (ENA, sparse scaling of critical MLP neurons), operating on only ~3% of parameters
4. **Comprehensive validation**: On MER-UniBench spanning Basic Emotion Recognition, Sentiment Analysis, and Fine-grained Emotion Recognition across 9 datasets, VEEENA improves average performance from 58.1% to 64.8% (+6.7%), achieving +9.2% on the challenging OV-MERD+ benchmark; outperforms prior methods VISTA and PAI; and preserves general capabilities on POPE, CHAIR, and MME

## Methodology

**Stage I -- Emotional Direction Extraction**:
- Constructs paired emotional (+) and neutral (-) image datasets using Google Nano Banana for neutralization, paired with neutral event descriptions from Gemini-3.0-Pro
- Computes emotional directions as the difference in hidden states: `s_{i,l} = h_{i,l}^+ - h_{i,l}^-`
- Filters pairs to retain only those where the model successfully exhibits the target emotion (threshold-based quality control)

**Stage II -- Hierarchical Circuit Discovery**:
1. **Critical Layer Identification**: Injects emotional directions into each layer during inference and measures behavioral impact via hit rate change ratio C
2. **Attention Head Localization**: Uses backward activation patching with the Latent Restoration Metric R to identify upstream heads whose outputs align with the global emotional direction
3. **MLP Neuron Tracing**: Employs Attribution Patching (first-order Taylor approximation) via gradient backpropagation from Key projections of critical heads to upstream MLP neurons

**Stage III -- VEEENA Intervention**:
- **VEE (Visual Emotion Enhancement)**: Flow-aware attention scaling with phase-specific logic -- during prefill (t=0), amplifies V->Q attention at upstream layers (l <= l_emo); during decoding (t>0), strengthens V->L attention at downstream layers (l > l_emo), with enhancement coefficient beta=2.0
- **ENA (Emotional Neuron Augmentation)**: Sparse scaling mask on critical MLP neurons with excitation coefficient gamma=1.5

**Primary model**: Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct; extended evaluation on Qwen3-VL-8B and LLaVA-OneVision-1.5-4B-Instruct

## Results & Highlights

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Average improvement on MER-UniBench (9 datasets) | 58.1% -> 64.8% (+6.7%) |
| Best single-dataset gain (OV-MERD+, Fine-grained) | +9.2% |
| Parameter overhead | ~3% of model parameters |
| MER2023 Hit Rate (vs. Baseline/VISTA/PAI) | 66.03 vs. 53.92/52.94/59.61 |
| Latency overhead | Minimal (1-10 ms/token range) |
| General capability preservation | Slight trade-offs on some metrics (POPE, CHAIR, MME), demonstrating circuit specificity |

**Key mechanistic findings**:
- Layer 19 identified as the pivotal aggregation layer where emotional semantics crystallize
- Upstream heads show sentiment-specific clustering (negative emotions share circuits); downstream heads are emotion-agnostic
- Recovery/knockout experiments confirm causal role of identified heads (random heads produce negligible effects)
- Neurons in aggregation encode contextual intents (e.g., "killing"); neurons in execution encode explicit emotional states (e.g., "angry")
- Universal visual grounding heads exhibit a coarse-to-fine attention evolution from aggregation to execution

## Critical Assessment

### Strengths

- Rigorous mechanistic analysis pipeline: from counterfactual construction through hierarchical circuit discovery to targeted intervention, with each step grounded in causal reasoning
- The functional decoupling finding (sentiment-specific aggregation -> emotion-general execution) is a genuine structural insight with implications beyond this specific task
- The Latent Restoration Metric is a thoughtful methodological contribution that addresses the fundamental mismatch between discrete metrics and diffuse emotional expression
- VEEENA's design is directly motivated by the discovered mechanism rather than being a generic intervention, and achieves strong results with minimal parameter overhead (~3%)
- Comprehensive evaluation: 9 emotion datasets + 3 general ability benchmarks + ablation studies + comparison with prior methods
- The paper validates circuit specificity by showing that random intervention fails to reproduce improvements and that general capabilities are largely preserved

### Limitations

- **Model scope**: Primary analysis is on Qwen3-VL-4B; while extended to 8B and LLaVA-OV-1.5-4B, the mechanism may be architecture-dependent. The LLaVA results show slightly different patterns (e.g., MME score drops by 53.1 with VEEENA)
- **Emotion granularity**: Analysis focuses on 4 basic emotions (happy, sad, angry, fear). Whether the mechanism scales to nuanced, mixed, or culturally-dependent emotions is unexplored
- **Counterfactual quality**: Using Google Nano Banana for image neutralization may introduce artifacts; the claim of "semantic content preservation" is not rigorously validated
- **Latency claim**: The paper claims "without additional latency" but Table 1 shows latency ranges from 1.25% to 10.56% overhead depending on intervention scale. This is modest but nonzero
- **Cultural bias**: All emotion datasets are from specific cultural contexts (movies, TV series); cross-cultural generalizability is not addressed
- **Safety concerns**: While the impact statement discusses alignment, the ability to manipulate emotional circuits could be misused for affective manipulation

## Relevance

This work sits at the intersection of mechanistic interpretability and multimodal emotion understanding. It is relevant to researchers working on:
- **Mechanistic interpretability** of vision-language models: extends circuit-level analysis from object perception to abstract emotional processing
- **Cross-modal information flow**: provides a concrete framework for tracing how visual signals propagate through LVLM layers to influence language generation
- **Inference-time model steering**: VEEENA demonstrates a principled approach to activation engineering guided by mechanistic discoveries
- **Emotional AI / empathetic agents**: offers a training-free method for improving emotion understanding reliability
- **Hallucination mitigation**: introduces the concept of "emotional hallucinations" and provides a targeted intervention distinct from object hallucination approaches

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the "Specific-to-Universal" processing hierarchy (sentiment-specific aggregation -> emotion-general execution) generalize to other LVLM architectures with different visual encoders (e.g., SigLIP, InternViT)?
2. The paper focuses on 4 basic emotions -- how does the mechanism change for compound emotions (e.g., bittersweet, nostalgic) or culturally-specific emotional expressions?
3. Can the circuit discovery framework be applied to other subjective visual judgments (aesthetic evaluation, humor detection, social norm assessment)?
4. What happens when emotional cues are ambiguous or conflicting (e.g., a smiling face in a somber setting)? Does VEEENA amplify the dominant signal or create instability?
5. The LLaVA-OV-1.5-4B results show a notable MME score drop (-53.1) with VEEENA -- does this indicate architecture-specific risks where emotional circuits overlap more with general visual capabilities?
