# Beyond Euclidean Proximity: Repairing Latent World Models with Horizon-Matched Trajectory Reachability Metrics

## 一句话总结

本文提出轨迹可达性度量（TRM），通过在潜空间世界模型的后处理阶段用经过时域匹配监督训练的成对排序头来替代欧氏距离，从而大幅提升基于潜变量的模型预测控制（MPC）规划成功率。

## TL;DR

Latent MPC planners typically rank candidate action sequences by Euclidean distance between predicted terminal latent states and the goal, assuming raw latent distance correlates with reachability. This assumption is often wrong. The authors propose Trajectory Reachability Metrics (TRM): a lightweight, post-hoc pairwise head trained on logged trajectory data with horizon-aware supervision. TRM replaces or augments the raw latent distance as the terminal cost, leaving the encoder, dynamics model, sampler, and optimizer untouched. On a hard TwoRoom benchmark, TRM boosts success from 7.0% to 97.0%; on PushT manipulation tasks, it similarly improves ranking quality. Mechanistic audits reveal that XY position is linearly decodable from latents (R^2=0.998) yet raw latent MSE systematically misranks candidates, because the XY-relevant subspace accounts for less than 1% of total latent MSE but carries most decision-relevant signal.

## 核心贡献

1. **提出了轨迹可达性度量（TRM）**：一种后处理式的终端排序方法，不修改已有的潜空间世界模型（编码器、动力学模型、采样器、优化器均保持不变），仅替换终端代价函数中的欧氏距离。TRM 训练一个小型成对比较头（pairwise head），从已记录的轨迹数据中学习可达性排序。
2. **时域匹配监督（Horizon-Aware Supervision）**：关键设计选择——在广泛的、平衡的时间间隔上训练成对排序头，使其匹配长时域终端候选排序问题，而非仅关注短时域邻近性。消融实验表明短时域 TRM 仅达 35.0% 成功率，而全时域 TRM 达 97.0%。
3. **机制性分析框架**：论文引入了三重诊断工具——（a）线性可解码性探测（XY position R^2=0.998），（b）子空间占比分析（XY-probe rowspace < 1% of latent MSE but carries most signal），（c）SCSA（Subspace Coverage and Selection Audit）审计，系统性解释原始潜空间距离为何失败以及 TRM 为何成功。
4. **通用性验证**：TRM 在 LeWorldModel (LeWM) 和 PLDM 两种潜空间世界模型架构上均有效（PLDM: 32.7% → 84.0%），表明其作为"面向规划器的修复"方法具有广泛适用性。在 PushT 连续操作任务上，TRM-style 指标同样改善了 SCSA 排序质量。

## Key Contributions

1. **Trajectory Reachability Metrics (TRM)**: A post-hoc terminal-ranking method for fixed latent world models. It trains a small pairwise head from logged trajectory structure and uses it as a replacement or hybrid cost, without modifying any other component of the planning pipeline (encoder, dynamics, sampler, optimizer all remain frozen).
2. **Horizon-matched supervision**: The metric is trained on broad, balanced temporal separations specifically to match the long-horizon terminal candidate ranking problem. This is the key design choice — short-horizon TRM only reaches 35.0% vs. 97.0% for full-horizon, and shuffled-label controls stay at 0.0%.
3. **Mechanistic explanation framework**: Three diagnostic tools — linear decodability probes, subspace fraction analysis, and SCSA (Subspace Coverage and Selection Audit) — rigorously explain why raw latent distance fails and why TRM succeeds.
4. **Generality across architectures and tasks**: Demonstrated improvements across two latent world model architectures (LeWM and PLDM) and two task domains (TwoRoom navigation and PushT manipulation).

## Methodology

### Problem Setup

The paper considers latent model-predictive control (MPC): a pre-trained encoder maps observations to latent states, a learned dynamics model rolls out candidate action sequences in latent space, and a terminal cost ranks candidates by comparing predicted terminal states to the goal latent state. The standard terminal cost is Euclidean (L2) distance in latent space.

### Core Problem Identification

The authors identify that Euclidean distance in latent space does not properly weight reachability-relevant variables. Specifically, the dimensions encoding task-relevant state (e.g., XY position) may constitute a tiny fraction (<1%) of total latent variance, so they are drowned out in MSE by task-irrelevant dimensions. This means the planner is effectively "blind" to the features that matter for decision-making.

### TRM Design

- **Pairwise comparison head**: A small neural network trained to predict whether one latent state is reachable from another, using logged trajectory data.
- **Training data construction**: State pairs are sampled from logged trajectories with broad, balanced temporal separations (not just nearby states). This horizon-aware sampling is critical.
- **Supervision signal**: Derived from trajectory structure — a pair (s_i, s_j) from the same trajectory with j > i indicates reachability.
- **Integration as cost**: TRM serves as a drop-in replacement for raw L2 distance in the terminal cost. It can also be used as a hybrid cost (combining raw distance and TRM).

### Analysis Tools

The paper introduces a mechanistic audit framework:

1. **Linear decodability probes**: Fit a linear model from latent states to ground-truth XY position. Even when R^2=0.998 (near-perfect decoding), raw latent MSE still misranks candidates.
2. **Subspace fraction analysis**: Project the terminal-goal MSE onto the XY-probe rowspace. The XY-relevant subspace accounts for less than 1% of total MSE but carries most candidate-quality signal.
3. **SCSA (Subspace Coverage and Selection Audit)**: Audits whether the metric improves the ordering and selected endpoints seen by the planner, both at the subspace level and at the level of final selected states.

### Baselines and Controls

- **LeWorldModel (LeWM)**: A latent world model trained with standard reconstruction + dynamics losses.
- **PLDM**: Another latent world model architecture.
- **Shuffled-label control**: Training the pairwise head with randomly shuffled temporal labels. Result: 0.0% success, confirming the signal is genuinely from trajectory structure.
- **Short-horizon TRM**: Training with only short temporal separations. Result: 35.0% success, confirming horizon-aware supervision is critical.

## Results & Highlights

### Main Results

| Benchmark | Method | Success Rate |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| TwoRoom | LeWM (raw latent L2) | 7.0% |
| TwoRoom | LeWM + full-horizon TRM | **97.0%** |
| TwoRoom | LeWM + shuffled-label control | 0.0% |
| TwoRoom | LeWM + short-horizon TRM (100K pairs) | 35.0% |
| Three seeds avg. | PLDM baseline | 32.7% |
| Three seeds avg. | PLDM + TRM | **84.0%** |

### PushT Manipulation Results

On PushT go50 and go75 tasks, TRM-style task-state metrics improve SCSA ranking quality and selected final distance. The paper notes that in continuous manipulation, closed-loop success is a noisy training signal, motivating auxiliary hybrid costs (combining TRM with task-specific state metrics).

### Mechanistic Findings

1. **XY position is linearly decodable** from latent space (R^2=0.998) — the encoder does contain the task-relevant information.
2. **Raw latent MSE misranks candidates** despite this decodability, because the XY-probe rowspace accounts for less than 1% of terminal-goal latent MSE.
3. **TRM re-weights the ranking** to focus on the reachability-relevant subspace, which SCSA audits confirm improves both the ordering and the selected endpoints.

### Ablation Insights

- Horizon-aware supervision is the single most important design choice (97% vs. 35% with short-horizon).
- The pairwise head does not need to be large — it is described as "small."
- The training budget is modest (100K pairs for the short-horizon ablation).

## Authors & Credibility

- **Liangyu Li, Shengzhi Wang, Qingwen Liu**: The authors provide unusually thorough mechanistic analysis (linear decodability, subspace fraction analysis, SCSA audits) that goes well beyond typical empirical evaluation in model-based RL papers. The paper is structured as a careful scientific investigation rather than a benchmark-driven engineering effort.
- The inclusion of proper negative controls (shuffled labels → 0.0%, short-horizon → 35.0%) and the cross-architecture validation (LeWM + PLDM) strengthen the claims significantly.
- The paper is 26 pages including appendices, indicating substantial experimental depth.

## Critical Assessment

### Strengths

- **Exceptionally clear problem framing**: The paper precisely identifies *why* Euclidean latent distance fails — not just that it fails — through careful subspace analysis. The <1% subspace fraction finding is elegant and memorable.
- **Minimal intervention, maximal impact**: TRM is a post-hoc repair that leaves the entire world model fixed. This is both practical and intellectually satisfying — it cleanly separates the representation problem from the planning problem.
- **Strong mechanistic evidence**: The three-part diagnostic (linear probe → subspace fraction → SCSA audit) provides a layered explanation that is rare in the model-based RL literature.
- **Proper controls**: Shuffled-label (0.0%) and short-horizon (35.0%) ablations cleanly isolate the contribution of horizon-aware supervision.
- **Dramatic improvements**: 7% → 97% on TwoRoom is a striking result that clearly demonstrates both the severity of the problem and the effectiveness of the solution.
- **SCSA as a general diagnostic**: The Subspace Coverage and Selection Audit framework could become a standard tool for diagnosing latent planning failures beyond this specific paper.

### Limitations

- **Scale of evaluation**: TwoRoom and PushT are relatively simple environments compared to real-world robotics, high-dimensional visual control, or complex manipulation tasks. The latent spaces involved are likely modest in dimensionality. It remains unclear how TRM scales to representations learned from raw pixels in complex environments.
- **Trajectory data requirement**: TRM requires logged trajectory data for training the pairwise head. The paper does not thoroughly explore sensitivity to data quantity and quality, nor whether off-policy or suboptimal trajectory data degrades performance.
- **Computational overhead**: The pairwise head inference cost during planning is not discussed. For real-time MPC with thousands of candidate sequences, even a small overhead per candidate could accumulate.
- **Pairwise nature**: The pairwise comparison formulation means TRM must evaluate all (or many) candidate-goal pairs during planning, which scales quadratically with the number of candidates if not carefully implemented.
- **Theoretical grounding**: The paper is empirically strong but lacks formal analysis. For instance, under what conditions does horizon-matched supervision provably improve the Lipschitz properties of the cost landscape? When is raw latent distance sufficient (and TRM unnecessary)?
- **Limited task diversity**: Both tasks (2D navigation and planar pushing) have low-dimensional ground-truth state. Whether TRM helps when the relevant task factors are not easily linearly decodable remains an open question.

## Relevance

- **For latent world model researchers**: Directly and immediately relevant — highlights a fundamental pitfall of using raw latent distance for planning and provides a practical, well-validated fix.
- **For model-based RL practitioners**: TRM is a lightweight add-on that could improve existing latent MPC pipelines with minimal engineering effort. The insight that "the encoder contains the right information but the cost function cannot access it" is broadly applicable.
- **For planning in robotics**: The subspace analysis methodology (relevant dimensions <1% of total variance) is a cautionary tale for anyone using learned representations for planning, reward shaping, or distance-based objectives.
- **Broader impact**: The SCSA audit framework and the mechanistic analysis methodology could become standard diagnostic tools for latent planning systems, extending beyond this specific contribution.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does TRM perform on high-dimensional visual control tasks (e.g., DM Control from pixels, MetaWorld, or real robot manipulation) where the latent space has hundreds of dimensions and the task-relevant subspace is less clearly defined?
2. What is the sensitivity of TRM to the amount and quality of logged trajectory data? Can it work effectively with offline datasets that were not generated by the world model itself, or with suboptimal demonstrations?
3. Can the horizon-aware supervision signal be derived analytically from the world model dynamics (e.g., via reachability analysis or controllability Gramians) rather than requiring logged trajectories?
4. How does TRM interact with non-Euclidean latent spaces (e.g., VQ-VAE discrete latents, normalizing flow latent spaces) or with hierarchical latent world models?
5. Could TRM be integrated into the world model training loop as an auxiliary loss to encourage the encoder to produce representations that are inherently better-suited for distance-based planning, eliminating the need for a post-hoc repair?
6. Does TRM help in settings where multiple task-relevant factors must be jointly considered (e.g., object position + orientation + grasp quality), or does the single pairwise score become insufficient?
