> **一句话总结：** 本文通过训练 10 层 Transformer 执行底数提取任务（从 N 的 B 进制展开中提取第 D 位系数），发现线性探针能以高 R^2 解码闭式解的中间计算步骤（B^D、N/B^D、floor 等），但因果干预（消融、修补、稀疏电路搜索）表明输出流仅从 D 流读取 D 选择性信息而非商类中间量，且 N/B/D 三个变量走的是几乎分离的路径直到最后才在输出侧合并，直接证明"表示不等于计算"。

> **TL;DR:** A 10-layer Transformer trained on base-digit extraction ($\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor \bmod B$) achieves 99.83% held-out accuracy. Linear probes decode all closed-form intermediates (B^D, N/B^D, floor) with R^2 up to 1.00, making staged computation look plausible. But causal interventions tell a different story: the D-to-output route carries only D-selective information (not N or B), and sparse circuit search reveals mostly separate N, B, D routes that combine only at the output. The model *represents* the algorithmic intermediates but does not *use* them as causal intermediates -- a clean demonstration that probe-based conclusions can diverge sharply from causal reality even when explicit algorithmic hypotheses are available.

## 核心贡献

- 设计了底数提取（base-digit extraction）任务作为机械可解释性的干净测试平台：输入 N/B/D，输出 B 进制下第 D 位系数，闭式解 $\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor \bmod B$ 提供了明确的候选中间计算步骤
- 三个随机种子的 10 层 Transformer 在未见过的 (N,B) 组合上达到 99.83% 精确准确率，建立可靠的行为基线
- 线性探针从 D_ones 残差流解码 B^D (R^2=1.00)、N/B^D 和 floor(N/B^D) (R^2=0.96)、最终答案 (R^2=0.94)，使分阶段计算假说看起来非常合理
- 通过累积消融发现输出流对早期 D_ones 通信最敏感（第 0-1 层消融导致准确率从 99.83% 降至 73.08%），而非探针最强的第 2 层
- Key/value 修补（patching）实验证明 D_ones-to-output 路径仅传递 D 信息：修补 N 或 B 无影响（100% 源匹配），仅修补 D 时行为完全翻转（99.84% 供体匹配）
- 稀疏电路搜索发现 N、B、D 走几乎独立的路径（94.44% 跨种子 IoU），在输出侧才合并，而非探针暗示的分阶段路径
- 初始化探针显示部分中间量在训练前就已高度可解码，进一步削弱"可解码=被使用"的推理

## Key Contributions

- Introduces base-digit extraction as a clean mechanistic interpretability testbed with explicit closed-form intermediates: $\lfloor N/B^D \rfloor \bmod B$
- Trains 10-layer decoder-only Transformers (3 seeds) to 99.83% exact-answer accuracy on held-out number-base intersections (N in {0,...,999}, B in {2,...,30})
- Shows linear probes decode closed-form intermediates with high R^2: B^D from D_ones at layer 0 (R^2=1.00), quotient-like quantities at layer 2 (R^2=0.96), final answer from output stream at layer 9 (R^2=0.94)
- Demonstrates via cumulative attention ablation that output behavior is most sensitive to early D_ones communication (layers 0-1), not the later layers where quotient-like quantities peak
- Uses key/value patching to show the D_ones-to-output route carries D-selective information exclusively: N or B donor changes have zero behavioral effect, while D donor changes flip behavior almost perfectly
- Sparse circuit search (greedy right-to-left) finds mostly factorized N, B, D routes (94.44% IoU across seeds) that converge only at output streams, retaining 28.33% of relations and 20.25% of layer-edges while preserving 93.40% accuracy
- Initialization probes reveal substantial pre-training decodability of intermediates, showing high R^2 need not mark a learned causal intermediate

## Methodology

**Task.** Given N (0-999), B (2-30), and D (digit position), output the coefficient of B^D in the base-B expansion of N as a two-digit decimal string. Training shuffles N/B/D field order across all 6 permutations, forcing reliance on field tags rather than position.

**Architecture.** GPT-style decoder-only Transformer: 10 layers, width 384, 12 attention heads, FF width 1536, dropout 0.01, max sequence length 13. Trained for 1000 epochs with AdamW (lr=5e-4), batch size 2048. A 5-layer companion shows the same qualitative pattern.

**Evaluation.** Held-out by (N,B) intersection (100/1000 N values and 5/29 B values held out). Autoregressive greedy decoding; exact-answer accuracy requires both answer digits correct. 95% CIs via percentile bootstrap over 3 seeds.

**Analysis pipeline (three stages):**
1. **Linear probes** -- OLS regression on frozen activations (5-fold CV R^2) for B^D, N/B^D, floor(N/B^D), and the final answer. Also run on initialized (untrained) networks as baseline.
2. **Causal interventions** -- (a) Cumulative attention ablations (shallow-to-deep and deep-to-shallow) masking D_ones-to-output attention at selected layers. (b) Key/value patching: substituting donor D_ones key/value vectors into output-query rows at layer 1 or all layers, testing whether behavior follows source or donor answer.
3. **Sparse circuit search** -- Greedy right-to-left search from output streams, retaining attention routes whose removal damages validation accuracy. Kept-only circuit evaluated on test split. 3x3 threshold sweep for robustness.

## Results & Highlights

- **Task mastery:** 99.83% exact-answer accuracy (seeds 0/42/1337: 99.50%/100%/100%) on held-out number-base intersections
- **Probe plausibility:** All four closed-form intermediates strongly decodable; staged algorithm looks representationally plausible. Even initialization probes show substantial decodability (training-added gain: 98% for B^D at layer 0, 89% for final answer at O[1] layer 9)
- **Causal divergence -- ablation:** Forward sweep: masking D_ones-to-output layers 0-1 drops accuracy to 73.08%; masking all layers drops to 32.06%. Reverse sweep: masking layers 4-9 barely affects performance (99.75%); masking layers 1-9 drops to 35.06%. Most sensitive readout is tied to earliest D_ones communication, not where quotient-like quantities peak
- **Causal divergence -- patching:** Layer-1 patching: source-exact drops to 25.11%, donor-exact rises to 67.63%. Full-route patching shows complete D-selectivity: N-change 100% source-exact, B-change 100% source-exact, D-change 99.84% donor-exact
- **Sparse circuit:** 93.40% accuracy retained with 28.33% of relations and 20.25% of layer-edges. 94.44% IoU across seeds. Separate N, B, D scaffolds route into output streams with late integration
- **5-layer model confirms pattern:** Same qualitative results -- 99.89% accuracy, D-selective routing, late integration

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The paper makes a clean, memorable contribution: "represented is not computed." While the general warning about probes vs. causality exists in the literature (Belinkov 2022, Elazar et al. 2021), this paper demonstrates it with unusual clarity in a setting where the algorithmic ground truth is known exactly. The dissociation is quantitative and striking -- probes show R^2=0.96 for quotient-like quantities that the causal route demonstrably ignores.

- **Rigor: Very strong.** Three independent seeds, held-out intersections (not just examples), all six input permutations during training, cumulative ablation sweeps in both directions, patching with matched source sets across conditions, initialization baselines, threshold sensitivity analysis, and a smaller companion model to test scale dependence. The 95% bootstrap CIs (over 3 seeds) are reported throughout. The task is precisely defined and the closed-form solution eliminates ambiguity about what the "right" algorithm is.

- **Reproducibility: Excellent.** Task defined by closed-form formula, small model (10-layer, width 384), code publicly available. The arithmetic setting eliminates the ambiguity that plagues interpretability work on language.

- **Significance: High for mechanistic interpretability.** This is a cautionary methodological paper that directly challenges a common interpretability workflow (probe first, conclude algorithm). The clean dissociation makes the argument hard to dismiss.

- **Potential concerns:**
  - **Scale limitation:** 10-layer, width-384 model. Whether the same probe-causality divergence occurs in larger models (e.g., 7B-parameter LLMs) remains unknown, though the 5-layer companion showing the same pattern is encouraging.
  - **Task specificity:** Base-digit extraction is a particularly clean arithmetic task. Whether analogous divergences occur in language tasks where the "algorithm" is less well-defined is unclear (though the authors acknowledge this and note that the clarity of the setting is precisely what makes the dissociation visible).
  - **Output-side computation not decomposed:** The paper localizes what reaches the output streams but does not decompose how O[0] and O[1] combine N, B, D inputs to produce the answer. The final computation "may still be structured and arithmetic; it is just not organized as the staged prompt-side transmission suggested by the probes." This is an honest limitation but leaves the full picture incomplete.
  - **Seed count:** Three seeds with bootstrap CIs are descriptive summaries rather than population estimates. The authors are transparent about this.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally clean experimental design: closed-form ground truth, held-out intersections, permutation-invariant training
- Multi-method triangulation: probes, ablations, patching, and circuit search all converge on the same conclusion
- Initialization probes are a clever control that strengthens the argument substantially -- showing some intermediates are decodable before training undermines the "training created this representation" narrative
- The D-selectivity patching result is particularly convincing: 0% source-exact / 99.84% donor-exact for D-change vs. 100% source-exact for N or B changes
- Honest about what is and is not claimed: "the claim is deliberately narrower than saying that the model never implements the closed-form computation"
- Draws a clear analogy to cognitive neuroscience (decoding vs. perturbation)

## Limitations

- Small model scale (10 layers, 384 width) -- generalization to practical-scale models is uncertain
- Single task type (arithmetic) -- the divergence pattern may not hold for tasks where the algorithmic structure is less clean
- Output-side computation remains a black box; the paper shows what arrives at O[0] and O[1] but not how they compute the answer
- Only 3 seeds -- the bootstrap CIs are descriptive, not inferential
- The "represented is not computed" framing, while memorable, applies specifically to the D_ones-to-output route and the closed-form intermediates tested; it does not rule out that other routes or nonlinear intermediates play a causal role

## Relevance

本文对机械可解释性的方法论有直接启示，特别是对使用线性探针推断 Transformer 内部算法的研究范式提出了严格的实证警告。对于研究模型如何实现算术推理、以及在什么条件下探针结论可以信任的方向尤为关键。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the probe-causality divergence persist in LLMs (e.g., 7B+ models) performing arithmetic, or is it specific to small toy models?
- What is the output-side computation? How do O[0] and O[1] combine the separate N, B, D inputs to produce the correct two-digit answer?
- Can the initialization-probe finding (high decodability before training) be predicted theoretically from the architecture, tokenization scheme, and data distribution?
- Would training with explicit intermediate supervision (e.g., forcing the model to output B^D as an auxiliary target) make the staged route causally effective?
- How does this relate to Nikankin et al. (2024), which found LLMs use "a bag of heuristics" rather than algorithms for arithmetic? Are these two faces of the same phenomenon?
- Is the factorized N/B/D routing pattern a general feature of multi-input integration in Transformers, or specific to this task structure?
