Methodology
Core approach: Given lm_head weight matrix $W \in \mathbb{R}^{V \times d}$, compute the economy SVD $W = U S V^\top$, where $U \in \mathbb{R}^{V \times d}$ contains left singular vectors, $S \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d}$ holds singular values, and $V^\top \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d}$ contains right singular vectors. Key insight: Because RMSNorm normalizes the hidden state onto the unit sphere before the lm_head projection, only directional structure matters. The singular directions are thus the fundamental axes of vocabulary selection. Each left singular vector $U[:, i]$ identifies tokens most readily selected when the hidden state aligns with the $i$-th singular direction. Critical Assessment

**Elegant simplicity**
The method genuinely requires only 5 lines of PyTorch and no inference -- an unusually low barrier to adoption for a safety auditing tool.
**Directly actionable**
Unlike many interpretability papers that are interesting but hard to operationalize, this provides a concrete auditing procedure with quantifiable metrics (VCS, WPS).
**Compelling empirical evidence**
The base-instruct comparison showing RLHF's failure to remove problematic subspaces is the first direct token-level evidence of this phenomenon.
**Practical glitch token detection**
Recovering a known glitch token statically is a convincing proof-of-concept.
**Well-connected to theory**
The Davis-Kahan bound provides theoretical grounding for why RLHF doesn't alter singular vector structure.
**Responsible disclosure**
The authors chose not to directly publish ethically inappropriate vocabulary found in Qwen, demonstrating ethical awareness.
