Methodology
### Problem Setup The paper considers latent model-predictive control (MPC): a pre-trained encoder maps observations to latent states, a learned dynamics model rolls out candidate action sequences in latent space, and a terminal cost ranks candidates by comparing predicted terminal states to the goal latent state. The standard terminal cost is Euclidean (L2) distance in latent space. ### Core Problem Identification The authors identify that Euclidean distance in latent space does not properly weight reachability-relevant variables. Specifically, the dimensions encoding task-relevant state (e.g., XY position) may constitute a tiny fraction (<1%) of total latent variance, so they are drowned out in MSE by task-irrelevant dimensions. Critical Assessment

**Exceptionally clear problem framing**
The paper precisely identifies why Euclidean latent distance fails — not just that it fails — through careful subspace analysis. The <1% subspace fraction finding is elegant and memorable.
**Minimal intervention, maximal impact**
TRM is a post-hoc repair that leaves the entire world model fixed. This is both practical and intellectually satisfying — it cleanly separates the representation problem from the planning problem.
**Strong mechanistic evidence**
The three-part diagnostic (linear probe → subspace fraction → SCSA audit) provides a layered explanation that is rare in the model-based RL literature.
**Proper controls**
Shuffled-label (0.0%) and short-horizon (35.0%) ablations cleanly isolate the contribution of horizon-aware supervision.
**Dramatic improvements**
7% → 97% on TwoRoom is a striking result that clearly demonstrates both the severity of the problem and the effectiveness of the solution.
**SCSA as a general diagnostic**
The Subspace Coverage and Selection Audit framework could become a standard tool for diagnosing latent planning failures beyond this specific paper.
