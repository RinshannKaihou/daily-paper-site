Critical Assessment

**Grand unification attempt**
Successfully shows that seemingly disparate robustness methods share a common geometric structure. The reinterpretation of CORAL, IRM, adversarial training, augmentation, and metric learning as different estimators of the same nuisance covariance is compelling and potentially field-shaping.
**Falsifiable theory**
Unlike many ML theory papers that prove vacuous bounds, this work provides specific, testable predictions (the matched > isotropic > wrong-W ordering) with pre-registered experiments. The Office-31 failure being predicted in advance is a strong signal of theoretical maturity.
**TDI is practical**
A label-free diagnostic for representation robustness fills a genuine gap — most robustness metrics require label access or are computationally expensive.
**Scale of validation**
Going from linear models to Qwen2.5-7B in a single paper is rare and demonstrates the theory is not purely academic.
**Honest scope claims**
The paper explicitly states its contribution is "naming the deployment nuisance covariance, stating what the regulariser must do, and supplying a closed-form falsifiable theory once that object is identified, not universality on every leaderboard." This intellectual honesty is commendable.
**Single-author risk**
A 54-page paper with 13 experimental blocks and multiple formal theorems by a single author places heavy burden on peer review. Independent verification of proofs and experiments is essential.
