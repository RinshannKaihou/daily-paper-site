Critical Assessment

**Complete theoretical picture**
Matching upper and lower bounds (minimax optimality) provide a definitive characterization of what is achievable in RDP auditing. The $O(d/\varepsilon^2)$ rate is tight.
**Practical black-box setting**
Realistic scenario where auditors cannot inspect internal algorithm details (gradients, noise levels). Only requires observing model outputs.
**Clean separation of errors**
Explicitly distinguishes statistical estimation error from true privacy leakage via non-asymptotic confidence intervals, which is conceptually important and practically useful.
**Focus on the hardest regime**
Low-to-moderate Rényi orders are where prior methods fail, and this is precisely where the paper shows the strongest improvements.
**Honest about limitations**
Limits experimental evaluation to $\alpha \in \{1.25, 2.0\}$ because the DV estimator has high MSE at larger $\alpha$ values, noting this as a known constraint.
**Restricted to small $\alpha$**
The DV Rényi estimator becomes unreliable at larger $\alpha$ due to high mean squared error, so experiments are limited to $\alpha \in \{1.25, 2.0\}$. This is a significant practical constraint since RDP accounting often uses a range of $\alpha$ values.
