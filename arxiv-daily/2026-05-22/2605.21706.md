# Latent-space Attacks for Refusal Evasion in Language Models

## 一句话总结

本文将语言模型的拒绝行为抑制重新定义为针对线性探针的潜在空间规避攻击，揭示先前差异均值（DiM）消融方法仅将表征投影到决策边界（最小置信度规避），并提出两种受控潜在空间规避攻击变体（CLE-P和CLE-A），通过优化置信度将表征推过决策边界进入合规区域，在15个模型上达到SOTA攻击成功率。

## TL;DR

Safety-aligned language models can be "jailbroken" by manipulating their internal representations. This paper reframes refusal suppression as a latent-space evasion attack against linear probes, revealing that prior difference-in-means (DiM) ablation methods merely project representations onto the decision boundary -- equivalent to the minimum-confidence evasion attack from DeepFool. The authors propose Controlled Latent-space Evasion (CLE) in two variants: CLE-P (projective, reprojecting every token activation) and CLE-A (additive, computing perturbation once on the post-instruction token and reusing it). CLE-A outperforms CLE-P, showing that continuous reprojection is unnecessary, and achieves state-of-the-art attack success rates across 15 instruction-tuned, multimodal, and reasoning models, surpassing both refusal-ablation baselines and per-prompt jailbreak attacks like GCG.

## 核心贡献

1. **理论重新框架化**：将拒绝抑制正式定义为针对线性探针的潜在空间规避攻击，为先前DiM方向消融方法提供了原则性的理论解释——它恰好对应于DeepFool的最小置信度二分类规避攻击。
2. **揭示根本局限性**：指出先前方法仅将表征投影到线性探针的决策边界上，由于最小置信度规避按构造是最弱的翻转线性决策的扰动，攻击效果受限。
3. **提出CLE攻击**：设计了受控潜在空间规避攻击，通过引入逐层置信度边际（margin），将表征推过决策边界进入合规区域，并使用贝叶斯优化（BO）搜索最优层选择和边际参数。
4. **发现加法变体更优**：CLE-A仅需在post-instruction token上计算一次扰动并统一施加，无需逐token重新投影，且效果优于CLE-P。

## Key Contributions

1. **Theoretical reframing**: Formally recasts refusal suppression as a latent-space evasion attack against linear probes, showing that DiM ablation is exactly the DeepFool minimum-confidence evasion attack against a binary classifier.
2. **Fundamental limitation identified**: Prior methods only project activations onto the decision boundary (minimum-confidence evasion), which is by construction the weakest perturbation that flips a linear decision.
3. **CLE attack**: Proposes Controlled Latent-space Evasion with per-layer confidence margins optimized via Bayesian Optimization, pushing representations well past the boundary into the compliant half-space.
4. **Additive variant superiority**: Shows CLE-A (single fixed perturbation computed once, applied universally) outperforms CLE-P (per-token reprojection), demonstrating that continuous reprojection is suboptimal.

## Methodology

### Problem Formulation
- Refusal suppression is modeled as a constrained optimization problem: find layer-wise perturbations that minimize the expected refusal rate of a safety-aligned model.
- An abstract oracle decides whether the model refuses a prompt; this oracle is surrogated by linear probes trained at each layer to separate harmful from harmless representations (using linear SVMs with normalized weights).
- The optimization reduces from tensor-valued perturbations to 2L scalar parameters: a binary layer mask {lambda_l} selecting where evasion matters, and a non-negative margin vector {m_l} controlling evasion confidence.

### Recasting Prior Work
- **Difference-in-Means (DiM) ablation [Arditi et al., 2024]**: The DiM direction defines a linear probe weight vector; ablating it corresponds to orthogonal projection onto the probe's decision boundary -- a minimum-confidence evasion.
- **Concept Cones [Wollschlager et al., 2025]**: Uses orthonormal basis directions; still applies the projection step, leaving steered activations short of the boundary.
- **Multiple Directions [Wei et al., 2025]**: Applies per-direction ablation through multiple SOM-derived probes; still boundary-level.

### CLE-P (Projective Variant)
- Reprojects every token activation past the probe's boundary by enforcing target confidence on each projected activation.
- Intervention: Psi_l(h) = h - lambda_l * (phi_l(h) + m_l) * w_l, applied to both input and generated tokens.
- Geometrically, when lambda_l=1, the update is the Euclidean projection onto a shifted hyperplane inside the compliant half-space.

### CLE-A (Additive Variant)
- Computes the evasion perturbation once on the post-instruction token h_[EOS] and reuses it across all subsequent activations.
- The perturbation delta_l(t) = -lambda_l * (phi_l(h_[EOS]) + m_l) * w_l is added uniformly to every token without further projection.
- A single fixed perturbation suffices to sustain evasion throughout generation.

### Optimization
- The 2L parameters {lambda_l, m_l} are optimized via Bayesian Optimization (BO) on a held-out validation set.
- Each BO query instantiates the steered model, generates completions, and scores them with a validation oracle.

## Results & Highlights

- **State-of-the-art attack success** across 15 models, including instruction-tuned (LLaMA-2, Qwen, Mistral, Gemma), multimodal (LLaVA), and reasoning models.
- **Outperforms all baselines**: Surpasses DiM ablation, Concept Cones, Multiple Directions, and specialized per-prompt jailbreak attacks (GCG, SAA).
- **CLE-A > CLE-P**: The additive variant consistently outperforms the projective variant, demonstrating that continuous per-token reprojection is suboptimal -- a single prompt-dependent perturbation is sufficient.
- **Ablation insights**: Performance is driven by the combination of accurate probes, layer selection, and margin optimization. Layer selection alone without positive margin control is insufficient; the all-layer minimum-confidence intervention achieves the lowest performance.
- **Model coherence preserved**: Optimized interventions do not jeopardize general model behavior while suppressing refusal (verified in Appendix H).
- **Linear separability holds consistently**: The CLE approach relies on linear separability of refused vs. answered requests in latent space, which held across all tested models.

## Critical Assessment

### Strengths

- **Principled theoretical contribution**: The reframing from "refusal direction ablation" to "latent-space evasion attack" is elegant and provides genuine explanatory power. Showing the exact correspondence between DiM ablation and DeepFool's minimum-confidence attack is a clean, impactful insight.
- **Strong empirical coverage**: Evaluation across 15 models spanning instruction-tuned, multimodal, and reasoning architectures demonstrates broad applicability.
- **Actionable insight for safety**: By clearly characterizing the vulnerability (linear separability of refusal), the work points toward concrete defenses -- alignment procedures that discourage linear separability.
- **Surprising finding that CLE-A outperforms CLE-P**: The discovery that a single fixed perturbation suffices over per-token reprojection is counterintuitive and practically significant (lower computational cost, stronger attack).
- **Well-connected to prior art**: The recasting of multiple prior methods (DiM, Concept Cones, MD) within a unified evasion framework is thorough and illuminating.

### Limitations

- **Linear probe assumption**: The theoretical framework relies on the linear separability of refused vs. answered representations. While this held across all tested models, it may not hold for future models with different alignment procedures. The authors acknowledge this but do not test it empirically.
- **Requires model internals**: Latent-space attacks require white-box access to model weights and activations, which limits real-world threat models compared to black-box jailbreaks. The practical risk is therefore narrower than prompt-level attacks.
- **Bayesian Optimization overhead**: The BO procedure for optimizing layer masks and margins adds non-trivial computational cost, though the authors note future work could make these differentiable.
- **Limited discussion of defenses**: While the paper mentions that discouraging linear separability could mitigate attacks, concrete defense strategies are left entirely to future work.
- **Potential dual-use concerns**: As with all jailbreak research, there is a risk of misuse, though the authors' framing is clearly oriented toward exposing vulnerabilities.

## Relevance

This work is highly relevant to AI safety and alignment research. It demonstrates that current safety training (RLHF, constitutional AI) produces a linearly separable refusal direction that is trivially exploitable by anyone with model access. The key insight -- that refusal ablation is just the weakest possible evasion -- suggests the alignment community needs to think beyond linear directions. The connection between classical adversarial ML theory (evasion attacks, DeepFool) and modern representation engineering is a productive bridge that could accelerate progress on both sides. For practitioners, this work highlights that latent-space safety mechanisms are fundamentally brittle as currently implemented.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can alignment procedures be designed to actively discourage linear separability of refusal (e.g., distributing refusal representations across non-trivial manifolds)?
2. How would adversarial training against CLE-style attacks affect model robustness and helpfulness?
3. Does the linear separability of refusal hold for models aligned with newer methods (e.g., DPO, RLAIF, constitutional AI with more iterations)?
4. Could the CLE framework be extended to nonlinear probes, and would this reveal additional vulnerabilities?
5. How does the attack transfer across models of different scales, and can the optimized margins from one model initialize attacks on another?
