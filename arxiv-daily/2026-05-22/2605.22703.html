Critical Assessment

**Diagnosis-first approach**
The decomposition of clipping into decision vs. execution roles and the controlled ablation (coupled vs. decoupled noise) is methodologically elegant. It provides genuine causal evidence rather than just correlation.
**Minimal and practical**
NSR is a genuinely simple modification -- sampling uniform noise and a three-case conditional -- with negligible overhead. It can be applied to any clipping-based RLVR objective without architecture changes.
**Strong theoretical grounding**
The closed-form derivation of the $O(1/r^2)$ implicit gradient decay provides mechanistic interpretability. The layered ablation comparing stochastic rescue vs. deterministic decay is a meaningful contribution.
**Comprehensive evaluation**
Tested across 3 model sizes, 2 architectures, 5 benchmarks, and with both token-level (DAPO) and sequence-level (GSPO) clipping.
**Narrow model family**
All experiments use Qwen-based models (Qwen2.5-Math, Qwen3). Generalization to other model families (Llama, DeepSeek, etc.) is not demonstrated.
**Task scope**
Evaluation focuses on math reasoning (AIME, AMC) and general science (GPQA, MMLU-Pro). Performance on coding, open-ended generation, or multi-turn tasks is not explored.
