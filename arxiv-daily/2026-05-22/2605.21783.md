> **一句话总结：** 本文将源分布周围的 MMD 球解释为 Walley 不精确概率理论中的 credal sets，在 RKHS-Lipschitz 损失假设和协变量偏移条件下推导出显式依赖 MMD 的 PAC-Bayesian 泛化界，并通过上下风险分解形式化地分离了认知不确定性与偶然不确定性，被 ICML 2026 EIML Workshop 接收。

> **TL;DR:** Interprets MMD-balls around the source distribution as credal sets in Walley's imprecise probability theory, deriving PAC-Bayesian generalization bounds with explicit MMD-dependent shift penalties under covariate shift, and formally separating epistemic from aleatoric uncertainty via a lower-upper risk decomposition. Accepted at EIML@ICML 2026.

## 核心贡献

- 在协变量偏移和 RKHS-Lipschitz 损失假设下，推导出显式由 MMD(Ps, Pt) 参数化的 PAC-Bayesian 泛化界（Theorem 1），并给出基于无偏 MMD 估计量的有限样本版本（Theorem 3），浓度率为极小极大最优的 O(1/sqrt(min(m,n)))
- 将 MMD 球定义为 Walley 不精确概率理论中的 credal sets（Definition 5），证明其凸性和弱闭性（Lemma 6），为 TTA 中的认知不确定性提供公理化基础
- 在整个 credal set 上推导一致最坏情况风险界（Proposition 7）及上下风险分解（Corollary 9），形式化地分离了来自分布偏移的认知不确定性与来自有限数据的偶然不确定性
- 证明测地线保持界（Proposition 10）：在 RKHS 中邻域间的测地距离失真以 sqrt(2*gamma) * C_W * MMD(Ps, Pt) 为上界，为核引导适应保护罕见类别结构提供几何论证（Corollary 11）
- 首次统一 PAC-Bayesian 泛化理论、核均值嵌入和 Walley 不精确概率理论三个数学框架，并提出基于 credal set 宽度的适应决策准则

## Key Contributions

- Derives a PAC-Bayesian bound (Theorem 1) with MMD-dependent shift penalty under RKHS-Lipschitz loss and covariate shift, plus a finite-sample version (Theorem 3) with minimax-optimal concentration rate O(1/sqrt(min(m,n)))
- Defines MMD-balls as credal sets in Walley's imprecise probability theory (Definition 5), proves convexity and weak closure (Lemma 6), providing an axiomatic foundation for epistemic uncertainty in TTA
- Establishes a uniform worst-case risk bound over all distributions in the credal set (Proposition 7) with a lower-upper risk decomposition (Corollary 9) that formally separates epistemic uncertainty (shift penalty) from aleatoric uncertainty (empirical risk)
- Proves geodesic distortion bounds (Proposition 10): geodesic distance distortion in RKHS neighborhoods is bounded by sqrt(2*gamma) * C_W * MMD(Ps, Pt), yielding a formal argument for rare-class robustness of kernel-guided adaptation (Corollary 11)
- First unified framework connecting PAC-Bayesian generalization, kernel mean embeddings, and Walley's imprecise probability theory, with a principled decision criterion for when adaptation is warranted

## Methodology

The paper develops a purely theoretical framework with four main technical components:

1. **PAC-Bayesian Bound with MMD Shift Penalty (Theorem 1):** Under covariate shift (Pt(y|x) = Ps(y|x)) and Assumption 1 (the conditional expected loss L(w, x) belongs to the RKHS with bounded norm), the target risk is bounded by: R_Pt(rho) <= R_hat_Ps(rho) + sqrt(KL(rho||pi) + log(2*sqrt(n)/delta)) / sqrt(2n) + L_H * MMD(Ps, Pt). The proof proceeds in three steps: (a) apply standard PAC-Bayesian bound on source risk, (b) bound the risk difference via the reproducing property and Cauchy-Schwarz, (c) combine via union bound. When MMD = 0, the bound recovers the standard PAC-Bayesian guarantee.

2. **Finite-Sample Analysis (Theorem 3):** Replaces population MMD with the unbiased estimator, using sub-Gaussian concentration (Sutherland et al., 2017) with kernel bounded in [0,1]. The concentration penalty epsilon_{m,n} = sqrt(2*log(4/delta)/min(m,n)) is minimax-optimal. The bound tightens as more target samples arrive during deployment.

3. **Credal Set Framework (Section 6):** Defines MMD-balls as credal sets C_epsilon(Ps) = {Q : MMD(Ps, Q) <= epsilon}. Proves convexity (via linearity of kernel mean embedding) and weak closure (via continuity of bounded kernels). The uniform worst-case risk bound (Proposition 7) holds for all distributions in the credal set simultaneously. The lower-upper risk decomposition (Corollary 9) yields an imprecision interval [R_underline, R_overbar] with width bounded by 2*sqrt(KL/2n) + 2*L_H*epsilon. This provides three concrete implications: (a) epistemic vs. aleatoric separation, (b) decision criterion for adaptation (adapt when R_overbar > r_max > R_underline), (c) hypothesis testing connection via MMD two-sample test calibration.

4. **Geodesic Preservation (Section 7):** Under Assumption 2 (encoder decomposes as f_theta(x) = W * phi_theta(x) with ||W||_op <= C_W), the expected geodesic distance distortion between source and target neighborhoods is bounded by sqrt(2*gamma) * C_W * MMD(Ps, Pt) + O(epsilon_bar^2). This shows kernel-guided adaptation preserves local feature geometry independent of class frequency, providing formal justification for rare-class robustness.

The paper uses a learned RBF kernel k_theta(x,y) = exp(-gamma * ||f_theta(x) - f_theta(y)||^2) on the encoder feature space, which is bounded in [0,1], universal, and task-adaptive. Appendices discuss relaxing Assumption 1 to average boundedness over the posterior, spectral normalization for Assumption 2, and a proposed combination with conformal prediction where the credal width adapts the coverage level.

## Results & Highlights

- **Theorem 1:** Target risk bounded by source empirical risk + PAC-Bayesian complexity + L_H * MMD(Ps, Pt), recovering standard bound when shift is zero
- **Theorem 3:** Finite-sample version with computable unbiased MMD estimator and minimax-optimal O(1/sqrt(min(m,n))) concentration rate
- **Proposition 7:** Uniform worst-case risk over all distributions in the MMD-ball, certifying performance cannot degrade beyond the stated bound
- **Corollary 9:** Lower-upper risk decomposition with imprecision width <= 2*sqrt(KL/2n) + 2*L_H*epsilon, formally separating epistemic from aleatoric uncertainty
- **Proposition 10:** Geodesic distortion <= sqrt(2*gamma) * C_W * MMD(Ps, Pt) + O(epsilon^2), showing MMD-bounded adaptation preserves local geometry
- **Corollary 11:** Formal argument for rare-class robustness of kernel-guided TTA over entropy-based methods
- No empirical experiments -- purely theoretical contribution with complete proofs in appendices

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The credal set interpretation of MMD-balls is genuinely novel and provides a fresh perspective on TTA uncertainty. However, the individual mathematical tools (PAC-Bayes + MMD risk transfer, MMD concentration) are well-established. The main novelty is the synthesis: connecting three distinct communities (PAC-Bayes, kernel methods, imprecise probability) in a way that produces actionable uncertainty quantification for TTA. The geodesic preservation result adds geometric depth.
- **Rigor:** Proofs are complete and detailed across 8 appendices. The progression from population-level (Theorem 1) to finite-sample (Theorem 3) to credal set (Proposition 7, Corollary 9) to geometric (Proposition 10) is well-structured. However, Assumption 1 (RKHS membership of the loss) is non-trivial: the paper acknowledges this is hard to verify rigorously for deep networks with softmax, and the informal justification via RBF universality is a gap. Assumption 2 (bounded encoder decomposition) is also idealized for practical architectures.
- **Reproducibility:** As a theoretical paper, the main claims are mathematical proofs that are verifiable. No code is provided. No empirical experiments are conducted, which means the practical tightness of the bounds remains unknown.
- **Significance:** The framework addresses a real and important gap -- existing TTA methods have no formal uncertainty quantification. The decision criterion (adapt when the imprecision interval straddles the risk threshold) is conceptually useful. However, without empirical validation, it is unclear whether the bounds are tight enough to guide real-world decisions or whether they are vacuously loose (as PAC-Bayesian bounds often are in practice).
- **Red flags:** (1) No experiments at all -- the paper is entirely theoretical, which limits its immediate impact. (2) Single author, which while not disqualifying, means the proofs have not been co-validated. (3) The Assumption 1 relaxation discussion (Appendix E) is informal and does not provide a path to rigorous verification. (4) The proposed combination with conformal prediction (Appendix F) is speculative and lacks formal analysis of coverage properties. (5) The MMD convergence rate O(1/sqrt(n)) may be loose in practice, and no data-dependent tightening is explored.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-organized theoretical exposition with complete proofs in appendices
- Genuinely novel synthesis of PAC-Bayesian theory, kernel methods, and imprecise probability
- Formally separates epistemic from aleatoric uncertainty -- directly addressing a well-known challenge in uncertainty quantification (Hullermeier & Waegeman, 2021)
- The credal set framework is interpretable and provides a principled decision criterion for TTA
- The finite-sample result is fully computable, unlike many PAC-Bayesian bounds that remain at the population level
- The geodesic preservation result provides geometric intuition beyond purely statistical bounds
- The connection to hypothesis testing (calibrating epsilon via MMD two-sample test) is practically useful
- Comprehensive discussion of assumption relaxations and connections to conformal prediction

## Limitations

- **No empirical validation:** The entire paper is theoretical with zero experiments. The practical tightness of the bounds, computational feasibility of credal set operations, and actual usefulness of the decision criterion are all untested.
- **Restrictive assumptions:** Assumption 1 (loss in RKHS with bounded norm) is hard to verify for practical neural networks. The paper acknowledges this but only offers informal support via RBF universality. Assumption 2 (bounded encoder decomposition) holds only approximately for standard architectures.
- **Computational concerns:** Computing sup_{Q in C_epsilon} R_Q(rho) over all distributions in the MMD-ball may be intractable in high dimensions. The paper does not discuss computational algorithms.
- **Covariate shift assumption:** The framework requires Pt(y|x) = Ps(y|x), which may be violated in many practical TTA scenarios where the label distribution also shifts.
- **Bound looseness:** PAC-Bayesian bounds are notoriously loose in practice. The additional MMD penalty term may make the bounds too conservative to be actionable.
- **Learned kernel dependency:** The framework depends on a learned RBF kernel from the pretrained encoder. If the encoder itself degrades under shift, the MMD estimates may be unreliable.

## Relevance

该论文为测试时适应中的认知不确定性提供了理论框架，将 PAC-Bayesian 界、MMD 与不精确概率相结合，对分布偏移下可靠模型部署的理论基础有参考价值，但作为纯理论工作缺乏实验验证，实际适用性有待验证。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How tight are the PAC-Bayesian bounds in practice on standard TTA benchmarks (e.g., ImageNet-C, CIFAR-10-C)? Are they actionable or vacuously loose?
- Can the RKHS-Lipschitz assumption (Assumption 1) be empirically verified via kernel ridge regression diagnostics, and what does the estimated L_H look like for standard architectures?
- Is there an efficient computational procedure for the credal set operations (sup/inf risk over MMD-balls), or do they require intractable optimization?
- How does the framework extend to joint covariate-label shift, and what additional assumptions are needed?
- Can the geodesic preservation insight (Proposition 10) be operationalized into a practical regularizer for TTA algorithms that outperforms existing entropy-based methods?
- Does the proposed conformal prediction combination (Appendix F) yield valid coverage in finite samples, and can g(epsilon) be calibrated efficiently?
