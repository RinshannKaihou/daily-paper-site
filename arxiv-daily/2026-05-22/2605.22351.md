# QuantSR+: Pushing the Limit of Quantized Image Super-Resolution Networks

## 一句话总结 / One-Sentence Summary

QuantSR+ 是一个统一的低比特量化图像超分辨率框架，通过重分布驱动的比特确定（RBD）、量化可瘦身架构（QSA）和瘦身引导的函数定位蒸馏（SFD）三大技术，在 2-bit 量化下实现了精度与效率的最优权衡，且对 CNN 和 Transformer 架构均有效。

## TL;DR

Low-bit quantization (2-4 bits) causes severe accuracy drops in super-resolution models due to diminished representational capacity and the detail-sensitive nature of SR. QuantSR+ addresses this at three levels simultaneously: (1) RBD reshapes quantization distributions in both forward and backward passes at the individual quant level, with packed weights that match standard integers (zero inference overhead); (2) QSA starts from a 2x over-parameterized model and progressively prunes blocks during training to meet efficiency budgets; (3) SFD introduces block-aware feature alignment via a direct distillation loss and progressive function-local training schedule. At 2-bit, QuantSR+ improves PSNR by 0.29 dB over prior SOTA on Urban100 x4 (SwinIR-S backbone) while cutting FLOPs by 87.9% and storage by 88.2%. On SRResNet, the 4-bit QuantSR+* variant even matches or exceeds the full-precision baseline (32.17 dB vs 32.16 dB on Set5 x4). The framework is validated on CNN-based (SRResNet), Transformer-based (SwinIR-S), and diffusion-based SR backbones. This is an extended journal version of the prior conference paper QuantSR.

## 核心贡献

1. **Redistribution-driven Bit Determination (RBD)**：在前后向传播中，通过基于传播的重分布机制，在单个量化级别（individual quant level）上精细化权重分布重塑，保持表征保真度。产生的打包量化权重与标准整数完全一致，推理时无额外开销。
2. **Quantized Slimmable Architecture (QSA)**：从 2 倍过参数化的初始结构出发，在训练过程中按块和阶段渐进式瘦身剪枝，而非会议版本中的突然架构切换，使架构演化与训练过程更好耦合。
3. **Slimming-guided Function-localized Distillation (SFD)**：引入基于局部功能重建的蒸馏损失，逐块优化瘦身后的量化架构，确保模型在瘦身演化过程中保持稳定并加速收敛。
4. 系统性地识别了量化 SR 模型在超低比特下性能退化的三大根源：算子退化（operator degradation）、结构约束（structure constraints）和优化扰动（optimization perturbations）。
5. 在 CNN（SRResNet）、Transformer（SwinIR-S）和扩散模型三种 SR 架构上均验证了有效性。

## Key Contributions

1. **Redistribution-driven Bit Determination (RBD)**: Propagation-based redistribution that reshapes weight distributions at the individual quant level during both forward and backward passes. Packed quantized weights are exactly consistent with standard integers, incurring zero additional inference overhead.
2. **Quantized Slimmable Architecture (QSA)**: Starts from a 2x over-parameterized model and distributes the slimming process across blocks and training stages (rather than an abrupt architecture change), better coupling structural evolution with training.
3. **Slimming-guided Function-localized Distillation (SFD)**: A novel distillation loss based on localized functional reconstruction for targeted optimization of the slimmed quantized architecture, ensuring stability during slimming evolution and faster convergence.
4. Systematic diagnosis of three root causes for ultra-low-bit SR degradation: operator degradation, structure constraints, and optimization perturbations.
5. Validated on three diverse SR architectures: CNN (SRResNet), Transformer (SwinIR-S), and diffusion-based SR.

## Methodology

QuantSR+ is a unified framework with three tightly integrated pillars:

**RBD (Redistribution-driven Bit Determination)** -- Operator Level:
- Operates on the quantization operator level, reshaping weight/activation distributions during both forward and backward passes.
- Refines redistribution down to the individual quant level (finer than channel-wise or tensor-wise methods), improving flexibility and accuracy of the quantized representation.
- The symmetric quantization baseline uses: Q_b^s(x) = round(clip(x/s, -1, 1)) * s * v_b^s
- RBD modifies this process to redistribute the quantization decision boundaries via propagation-based optimization.
- Key property: the packed quantized weights are exactly consistent with standard integers, meaning zero inference overhead at deployment.

**QSA (Quantized Slimmable Architecture)** -- Structure Level:
- Starts with an over-parameterized initial structure that is 2x larger than the targeted compressed model.
- Unlike the conference version which abruptly changes the entire architecture at a certain training point, QSA distributes the slimming process across blocks and stages of training.
- Progressive structural evolution during training allows the quantized model to be continuously optimized, pushing the upper accuracy limits of compressed models under specific inference efficiency constraints.
- This better couples architectural evolution with the training procedure.

**SFD (Slimming-guided Function-localized Distillation)** -- Optimization Level:
- Introduces a direct distillation loss based on localized functional reconstruction.
- Implements a progressive strategy for optimizing network blocks based on their local features.
- By adjusting the granularity of internal feature alignment, SFD effectively supervises the quantized SR model during slimming.
- Provides targeted optimization of the slimmed model quantized architecture, ensuring stability and faster convergence compared to standard training pipelines.

**Training Pipeline**: The full pipeline integrates QAT (quantization-aware training) with the above three components. SR models accept low-resolution input I_LR and output I_SR = R * E_H * E_L(I_LR), where weights and activations are quantized to b-bit representations.

## Results & Highlights

### CNN-based (SRResNet backbone):
- **2-bit**: QuantSR+ consistently outperforms all prior fixed-precision baselines on Set5, Set14, B100, Urban100, and Manga109 benchmarks at both x2 and x4 scales. On Set5 x4, improves from 31.81/0.8880 (ODM) to 31.89/0.8908 (+0.08 dB / +0.0028 SSIM).
- **4-bit**: QuantSR+* (with activation shifting from I2NQ) reaches 32.17 dB / 0.8951 on Set5 x4, surpassing the full-precision SRResNet (32.16 dB / 0.8951).
- **Mixed-precision comparison**: QuantSR+* exceeds Granular-DQ by 0.03 dB on Urban100 x4.

### Transformer-based (SwinIR-S backbone):
- **2-bit**: Outperforms the original QuantSR by 0.17 dB PSNR and 0.0015 SSIM on Set5 x4.
- **vs. recent methods**: Improves by 1.81 dB PSNR over 2DQuant and 0.29 dB over ODM on Urban100 x4 (under same 300K iterations).
- 2-bit QuantSR+ performs comparably to existing SOTA methods using 4 bits.

### Diffusion-based SR backbone:
- **4-bit QuantSR+**: Achieves best PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS, DISTS, and FID among all quantized baselines.
- vs. standard Quant: improves PSNR by +0.06 dB, SSIM by +0.0148, reduces LPIPS by 0.0238, DISTS by 0.0118, and FID by 9.62.

### Efficiency:
- **2-bit**: 87.9% FLOPs reduction, 88.2% storage reduction vs. full-precision counterpart.
- Significant accuracy improvements achieved without additional efficiency cost.

## Critical Assessment

### Strengths

- **Truly unified framework**: Addresses quantization at three orthogonal levels (operators, architecture, training) simultaneously, rather than optimizing only one aspect. This holistic approach is more principled than prior single-aspect solutions.
- **Zero-overhead quantizer**: RBD produces packed weights consistent with standard integers, meaning the accuracy gains come at no additional inference cost -- a practically important property.
- **Strong and consistent empirical gains**: 0.29 dB over 2-bit SOTA on Urban100 is substantial in SR where gains are hard-won. Results are consistent across 5 benchmarks, 3 architectures, and multiple bit-widths.
- **4-bit matches full precision**: QuantSR+* at 4-bit matching or exceeding the full-precision SRResNet is a remarkable result.
- **Architecture generality**: Validation on CNN, Transformer, AND diffusion SR backbones demonstrates genuine generality rather than method-overfitting to one architecture.
- **Progressive design philosophy**: QSA's distributed slimming across blocks and stages is a clear improvement over abrupt architecture changes, showing thoughtful design.

### Limitations

- **Scope limited to SR**: The techniques are specialized for image super-resolution. Generalization to other dense prediction tasks (denoising, deblurring, segmentation) is not explored.
- **No real-hardware latency reported**: FLOPs and storage reductions are reported, but actual inference latency measurements on edge hardware (mobile NPUs, dedicated accelerators) are missing -- this is the gap between theoretical and practical efficiency.
- **Training complexity**: Starting from a 2x over-parameterized model plus progressive slimming plus distillation likely increases training time and resource requirements substantially compared to simple QAT. The training cost analysis is not prominently discussed.
- **Dependency on teacher model**: SFD requires a full-precision teacher, adding to the overall training pipeline complexity.
- **Ablation granularity**: While three components are proposed, the interaction effects between them could be more thoroughly analyzed (e.g., does RBD help more with or without QSA?).

## Relevance

This work is highly relevant to researchers and practitioners working on:
- **Efficient deep learning** and model deployment on resource-constrained devices
- **Low-bit quantization** techniques for vision models, especially below 4-bit
- **Image super-resolution** with practical deployment requirements
- **Combined quantization + pruning** strategies as a co-design paradigm
- **Knowledge distillation** in the context of quantized models
- The systematic diagnosis of quantization failure modes (operator degradation, structure constraints, optimization perturbations) provides a useful analytical framework for the community.

The result that 4-bit quantized SR can match full-precision performance is particularly significant for practical deployment scenarios.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does the total training time and GPU memory of QuantSR+ (with 2x over-parameterized initialization + progressive slimming + distillation) compare to simple QAT baselines?
2. Can RBD be applied as a plug-in quantizer improvement to other quantization-aware training frameworks beyond SR?
3. What is the pruning criterion in QSA -- is it magnitude-based, gradient-based, or does it leverage quantization-specific signals?
4. How does the progressive slimming schedule interact with the learning rate schedule? Is the schedule sensitive to hyperparameters?
5. What are the actual on-device inference latency numbers on common edge platforms (e.g., Qualcomm Hexagon DSP, Apple Neural Engine)?
6. Can the QuantSR+ pipeline be extended to other image restoration tasks (denoising, deblurring, JPEG artifact removal) that share the detail-sensitive nature of SR?
