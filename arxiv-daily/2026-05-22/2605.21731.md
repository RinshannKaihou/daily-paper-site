> **一句话总结：** 提出I-SAFE框架，基于Wasserstein距离定义三种分布一致性指标（QBM、WCM、TI-WCM），通过对药物-靶点交互预测模型的蛋白残基进行结构引导的扰动审计，揭示在可比预测精度下仅TAPB模型对KLIFS结合口袋区域扰动表现出先验选择性的分布响应。

> **TL;DR:** I-SAFE is a post-hoc distributional auditing framework that uses three Wasserstein-based coherence metrics to assess whether scientific AI models' output distributions reorganize coherently under domain-informed perturbations. Applied to three DTI models with comparable AUROC, it reveals that only TAPB exhibits prior-selective distributional responses to binding-pocket perturbations.

## 核心贡献

- 提出I-SAFE框架，一个模型无关的事后分布审计方法，通过结构引导的扰动对比评估科学AI模型是否学习了领域相关的结构而非捷径特征。
- 定义三种互补的度量指标：QBM用于位置级别一致性（基于分位数位移）、WCM用于序数一致性（基于最优传输的排序保持）、TI-WCM用于形状一致性（平移不变，分离均值位移后的残差形状）。
- 引入"机制性扰动 vs. 虚假扰动"的先验相对对比设计，确保扰动规则和干预大小完全匹配，仅先验对齐不同。
- 在Davis激酶基准上验证框架，使用KLIFS结合口袋注释作为外部结构先验，审计三个DTI模型（DeepConvDTI、DeepDTA、TAPB），发现仅TAPB在QBM和WCM上表现出正向先验对比。
- 提供完整的理论分析：证明所有指标在[0,1]区间内归一化，WCM具有尺度不变性，TI-WCM是比WCM更严格的度量。

## Key Contributions

- Propose I-SAFE, a model-agnostic post-hoc distributional auditing framework that evaluates how raw model output distributions reorganize under structurally guided perturbations, rather than just measuring scalar sensitivity.
- Define three complementary metrics grounded in optimal transport theory: QBM (quantile-level location coherence), WCM (ordinal coherence via optimal pairing), and TI-WCM (translation-invariant shape coherence removing mean shifts).
- Introduce matched perturbation pair design (mechanistic vs. spurious) where both interventions share the same operator and cardinality, differing only in alignment with the structural prior.
- Demonstrate that among three DTI models with comparable AUROC (~0.876-0.907), only TAPB shows positive prior-relative contrasts on both QBM (+0.093) and WCM (+0.063), with the signal localized to location and ranking rather than shape preservation.
- Provide formal proofs that all metrics are normalized to [0,1], WCM is scale-invariant, and TI-WCM upper-bounds WCM.

## Methodology

The I-SAFE framework operates through the following pipeline:

1. **Input decomposition and structural prior.** Inputs are decomposed into identifiable components (e.g., protein sequence residues). An external structural prior P(t) (KLIFS binding-pocket annotations) identifies domain-relevant components independently of the model.

2. **Matched perturbation pairs.** For each input, two interventions are constructed:
   - **Mechanistic perturbation:** applies the perturbation operator to prior-selected components (KLIFS pocket residues).
   - **Spurious perturbation:** applies the same operator with identical cardinality to components outside the prior (non-pocket residues).
   - Operators include masking (replacing with mask token) and physicochemically constrained substitution (replacing with same-class amino acid).

3. **Interventional response profiles.** The raw model outputs before and after perturbation form paired response profiles, preserving both empirical output distributions and the input-wise pairing.

4. **Three coherence metrics:**
   - **QBM** (Eq. 5): Compares displacement of representative quantile locations (q25, q50, q75) against total MSE. Normalized to [0,1]; higher values mean quantile structure is preserved despite pointwise changes.
   - **WCM** (Eq. 6): Measures the gap between the natural input-wise output pairing and the optimal pairing after reordering via the Wasserstein-1 distance between sorted output vectors. Equals 0 when the natural coupling maximizes covariance; equals 1 when a non-identity permutation perfectly matches the outputs.
   - **TI-WCM** (Eq. 8): Removes the mean shift contribution from the Wasserstein distance before normalization, making it invariant to additive bias in the model output.

5. **Prior-relative contrasts.** For each metric M, the contrast Delta-M = M_spurious - M_mechanistic. Positive contrasts indicate greater coherence under mechanistic perturbations (prior-selective response).

6. **Experimental setup.** Auditing set: 208 kinase targets, 3,044 drug-target pairs from Davis benchmark. Models audited from frozen checkpoints across 5 training seeds. AUROC verified comparable predictive regime (DeepConvDTI: 0.876, TAPB: 0.882, DeepDTA: 0.907). Median KLIFS pocket size: 85 residues.

## Results & Highlights

- **Absolute metrics (Table 1):** Under mechanistic perturbations, TAPB shows the strongest coherence with lowest QBM (0.515) and WCM (0.443). DeepDTA shows the least coherent response with highest QBM (0.842) and WCM (0.768). DeepConvDTI lies in between (QBM: 0.737, WCM: 0.512). This already separates the models beyond what AUROC reveals.

- **Prior-relative contrasts:** TAPB achieves positive Delta-QBM = +0.093 [0.039, 0.147] and Delta-WCM = +0.063 [0.028, 0.099], indicating its coherent response is selectively associated with pocket perturbations. DeepConvDTI shows negative contrasts (Delta-WCM = -0.046), and DeepDTA shows no significant prior-aligned advantage (Delta-WCM = -0.013).

- **Translation-invariant signal:** Delta-TI-WCM is negative across all models, meaning TAPB's positive signal is localized to location and ranking, not residual shape preservation after removing the mean shift.

- **Key insight:** Only TAPB (which incorporates target-aware attention and interventional debiasing) exhibits prior-selective distributional response to the binding-pocket prior, despite all three models having comparable predictive performance.

- **Computational efficiency:** The full audit pipeline (excluding model training) completes in approximately 17 minutes on a standard laptop without GPU acceleration.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The shift from scalar sensitivity auditing to full distributional auditing via optimal transport is a genuine methodological advance. The three-metric decomposition (location, ordinal, shape) is well-motivated and provides richer diagnostic information than prior work. The connection to Wasserstein distance theory is natural and well-exploited.

- **Rigor:** The formal definitions, propositions, and proofs are clean and rigorous. All metrics are normalized to [0,1] with clear interpretive meaning. The matched perturbation design controls for operator and cardinality. Confidence intervals are computed across 5 seeds with non-parametric bootstrap. However, the paper does not establish formal statistical tests (e.g., hypothesis testing framework) for the contrasts.

- **Reproducibility:** Strong. The supplemental archive includes per-seed audit outputs, and the reproduction pipeline runs in ~17 minutes without GPU. All models are audited from frozen checkpoints with publicly available training protocols. KLIFS annotations are a public database.

- **Significance:** The core finding -- that comparable AUROC masks fundamentally different structural behaviors -- is important for the scientific ML community. However, the current evidence is limited to one task domain and one structural prior. Whether the framework generalizes broadly remains to be demonstrated.

- **Red flags:** (1) The framework requires a structural prior, which creates a dependency on domain-specific knowledge that may be unavailable or incomplete. (2) The paper audits only target-side interventions with drug held fixed; joint or drug-side interventions are left to future work. (3) The auditing set has significant class imbalance (5.1% positive pairs), which may affect metric behavior but is not discussed. (4) The prior-relative interpretation does not establish causal validity, which the authors acknowledge but could be more prominently flagged.

## Strengths

- Principled theoretical grounding in optimal transport with clean formalization and proofs.
- Three complementary metrics provide multi-axis diagnostic information unavailable from scalar summaries.
- The matched perturbation design rigorously isolates the role of prior alignment.
- Model-agnostic and requires only black-box access -- no internal representations needed.
- Computationally efficient post-hoc audit (17 minutes on laptop, no GPU required).
- Clear separation between predictive performance and distributional coherence as distinct evaluation dimensions.
- The finding that TAPB's signal is localized to location/ranking (not shape) demonstrates the diagnostic value of the multi-metric decomposition.

## Limitations

- Requires external structural priors, limiting applicability in domains without curated domain knowledge.
- Demonstrated on only one task domain (DTI on Davis benchmark) with one structural prior (KLIFS).
- Only target-side perturbations are audited; drug-side and joint interventions are not explored.
- The auditing set has severe class imbalance (5.1% positive, 94.9% negative), which may bias the output distributions being compared.
- No formal hypothesis testing framework for the prior-relative contrasts; interpretation relies on confidence interval overlap.
- The perturbation operators (masking, constrained substitution) are relatively simple; more sophisticated domain-specific perturbations could yield different results.
- QBM sensitivity to quantile grid choice is acknowledged but only briefly explored in appendix.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does I-SAFE behave under different structural priors of varying granularity or completeness (e.g., partial or noisy pocket annotations)?
2. Can the framework be extended to domains like molecular property prediction (where SMILES/graph substructures provide natural decompositions) or protein structure prediction?
3. What is the relationship between I-SAFE coherence metrics and out-of-distribution generalization performance across multiple benchmarks?
4. How robust are the prior-relative contrasts to the choice of perturbation operator (masking vs. substitution vs. more sophisticated perturbations)?
5. Does the severe class imbalance in the auditing set (5.1% positive) affect the metric behavior, and how should this be accounted for?
6. Can a formal hypothesis testing framework be developed for the contrasts, providing p-values or Bayes factors rather than reliance on CI overlap?
