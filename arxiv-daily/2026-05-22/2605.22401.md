> **一句话总结：** 通过RSA方法比较五种学习规则（BP、FA、PC、STDP及随机基线）在猕猴电生理数据与人类fMRI中的脑对齐表现，发现早期视觉区域的对齐模式跨物种保守（STDP和PC在两物种V1区均表现最优），但高级视觉区域（IT）的学习规则排序在物种间无相关性，且预训练ResNet-50在IT区的对齐（rho=0.25）远超所有自定义CNN（rho=0.07-0.14），表明模型容量和训练数据在高级区域占主导地位。

> **TL;DR:** This study extends prior human fMRI work (Leutenegger, 2026, arXiv:2604.16875) to macaque electrophysiology, testing five learning rules (BP, FA, PC, STDP, random baseline) on the identical trained 3-layer CNN weights. All models achieve higher RSA alignment with macaque early visual cortex (rho=0.15-0.30 at V1/V2) than with human fMRI (rho=0.01-0.08), reflecting electrophysiology's superior SNR. STDP (rho~0.30) and PC (rho~0.28) lead at macaque V1/V2, consistent with their top ranking in human V1 -- confirming that local learning rules outperform BP at early visual stages across species. At IT, cross-species rank correlation is zero (Kendall's tau=0.00, p=1.00), though n=5 rules yields power only at tau=+/-1.0. A pretrained ResNet-50 achieves rho=0.25 at IT vs. 0.07-0.14 for custom CNNs, demonstrating that model capacity and training data dominate learning rule choice at higher visual areas. The paper includes noise ceilings (0.52-0.79), 5-seed variability, and a stimulus-control analysis revealing that IT ranking differences are partially driven by stimulus domain (THINGS vs. HVM objects).

## 核心贡献

- 首次使用完全相同的模型权重进行跨物种RSA比较，将五种学习规则（BP、FA、PC、STDP、随机基线）的人类fMRI脑对齐结果扩展到猕猴电生理数据
- 发现早期视觉区域（V1/V2）的模型-脑对齐模式跨物种定性保守：STDP和PC在人类和猕猴的早期视觉区域均优于BP，且在猕猴V1达到最高对齐值（rho~0.30）
- 揭示高级视觉区域（IT）的学习规则排序在物种间无可检测相关性（Kendall's tau=0.00），且预训练ResNet-50（rho=0.25）远超所有自定义CNN（rho=0.07-0.14），表明模型容量和训练数据在高级区域比学习规则更重要
- 系统报告了噪声上限（0.52-0.79）、5种子变异性、物种x学习规则交互效应和刺激控制分析，提供了严谨的统计框架
- 刺激控制分析揭示IT区的跨物种排序差异部分源于刺激域差异（THINGS vs. HVM），而非纯粹的物种差异

## Key Contributions

- First systematic cross-species RSA comparison of four biologically plausible learning rules plus an untrained baseline, using identical model weights (3-layer CNN trained on CIFAR-10) across both human fMRI and macaque electrophysiology
- Demonstrates conserved early visual alignment: local learning rules (STDP, PC) consistently outperform BP at V1/V2 in both species, with STDP achieving the highest macaque V1 alignment overall (rho~0.30)
- Shows that higher-area (IT) learning rule rankings have zero cross-species correlation (Kendall's tau=0.00, p=1.00), with a pretrained ResNet-50 (rho=0.25) dramatically outperforming all custom CNNs (rho=0.07-0.14), indicating model capacity and training data richness dominate over learning rule at IT
- Provides rigorous statistical framework: noise ceilings via Spearman-Brown corrected split-half (range 0.52-0.79), bootstrap 95% CIs (N=10,000 resamples), 5-seed variability, species x learning rule interaction effects, and a stimulus-control analysis showing IT ranking instability across stimulus domains (tau=-0.40)
- Identifies that the V2-to-V4 alignment drop in macaques confounds cortical hierarchy changes with a dataset switch (FreemanZiemba2013 textures vs. MajajHong2015 objects), a key interpretive caveat

## Methodology

**Models:** All five conditions use the identical architecture and weights from the companion study (Leutenegger, 2026, arXiv:2604.16875): a custom 3-layer CNN (Conv1: 32 filters, Conv2: 64, Conv3: 128, FC1: 512, FC2: 10) trained on CIFAR-10 for 40 epochs. The five learning rules are: (1) Backpropagation (BP), (2) Feedback Alignment (FA; Lillicrap et al. 2016), (3) Predictive Coding (PC; Whittington and Bogacz 2017), (4) STDP (Bi and Poo, 1998), and (5) Random Weights (untrained). Results averaged across 5 random seeds. A pretrained ResNet-50 (ImageNet-1K-V2 weights) serves as a capacity control with layer mapping: layer1->V1/V2, layer2->V4, layer4->IT.

**Macaque Neural Datasets (via Brain-Score API):**
- MajajHong2015: Multi-electrode array recordings from V4 (88 neurons) and IT (168 neurons), 3,200 HVM object stimulus presentations
- FreemanZiemba2013: Single-unit recordings from V1 (102 neurons) and V2 (103 neurons), 135 texture stimuli

**Human Data:** THINGS-fMRI dataset (720 stimuli, 3 subjects) from the companion study, with an additional Conv2->V4 alignment computed for cross-species comparison.

**RSA Pipeline:** Feature extraction, RDM construction (correlation distance), and RSA scoring (Spearman rho between upper triangles). Bootstrap 95% CIs from N=10,000 stimulus resamples. Noise ceilings estimated via split-half reliability (N=100 splits), Spearman-Brown corrected.

**Layer-to-Region Mapping:** Custom CNN: Conv1->V1/V2 (FreemanZiemba), Conv2->V4 (MajajHong and THINGS-fMRI), FC1->IT (MajajHong). All stimuli resized to 224x224.

**Cross-Species Analysis:** Kendall's tau between human and macaque rho-vectors per region. Exhaustive permutation test (5!=120 permutations) for exact p-values. Species x learning rule interaction quantified as (Delta_rho_human - Delta_rho_macaque) where Delta_rho = rho_rule - rho_random.

**Stimulus Control:** Computes model RSA on both THINGS and macaque stimuli for each learning rule, then correlates rankings across stimulus sets to assess whether ranking differences reflect stimulus domain rather than species.

**Notable Caveat:** Seed-0 STDP checkpoint saved only convolutional weights (FC1 was randomly initialized), so STDP results at IT (FC1-based) use seeds 1-4 only; V1/V2 results (Conv1-based) are unaffected.

## Results & Highlights

1. **Higher Absolute Alignment for Macaque Electrophysiology:** Macaque rho values (0.15-0.30 at V1/V2) substantially exceed human fMRI values (0.01-0.08 at V1). All macaque values fall well below noise ceilings (0.52-0.79), indicating substantial room for improvement.

2. **STDP and PC Lead at Macaque V1/V2:** STDP (rho~0.30) and PC (rho~0.28) achieve the highest macaque V1 alignment, followed by Random (rho~0.18), FA (rho~0.16), and BP (rho~0.16). This partially mirrors the human pattern (Random >> STDP >> PC >> BP >> FA at V1), with the notable difference that Random does not dominate at macaque V1 -- local learning rules do.

3. **Sharp V2-to-V4 Drop, Partial IT Recovery:** All conditions show a sharp decrease from V2 to V4 (rho~0.03-0.06), followed by partial recovery at IT (rho~0.07-0.14). This is confounded by the dataset switch: FreemanZiemba2013 (135 texture stimuli, single-unit) at V1/V2 vs. MajajHong2015 (3,200 HVM objects, multi-electrode arrays) at V4/IT. Within MajajHong data, the V4-to-IT recovery has a plausible explanation: FC1's lower-dimensional bottleneck preserves coarse categorical structure matching IT's primary organizational dimension.

4. **V1 Learning-Rule Sensitivity Differs Across Species:** Human V1 Delta_rho = 0.064; macaque V1 Delta_rho = 0.147. Electrophysiology resolves larger differences between learning rules at V1 than fMRI. The interaction analysis shows STDP has the strongest negative interaction at V1 (-0.138) and V2 (-0.124): STDP's advantage over Random is much larger in macaque than in human.

5. **Cross-Species Ranking Comparison:** At V1 (tau=0.40, p=0.48) and V4 (tau=0.20, p=0.82), rankings show weak positive correlation. At V2 (tau=-0.20, p=0.82) and IT (tau=0.00, p=1.00), no correlation. No comparison reaches significance given n=5 provides power only at tau=+/-1.0. All macaque points lie above the identity line, reflecting electrophysiology's SNR advantage.

6. **ResNet-50 Capacity Control:** At V1/V2, ResNet-50 (rho~0.14-0.21) performs comparably to BP/FA but below STDP/PC. At IT, ResNet-50 (rho~0.25) dramatically outperforms all custom CNNs (0.07-0.14), suggesting the IT convergence in the companion study may reflect a capacity floor rather than a genuine property of learning rules.

7. **Stimulus Control:** Rankings moderately stable at V1 (tau=0.40) but weakly inverted at IT (tau=-0.40), suggesting cross-species IT differences are partially driven by stimulus domain (THINGS objects vs. HVM objects) rather than genuine species differences.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Moderate):** The cross-species extension is a natural but well-executed next step. The identical-weights design is the key novelty -- most cross-species work does not control for this. The finding that early visual alignment is conserved is moderately novel and valuable for the field.

**Rigor (Good):** The paper is commendably transparent about its limitations. Noise ceilings, multi-seed variability, stimulus-control analysis, explicit power analysis for Kendall's tau, and the STDP seed-0 caveat are all reported. The exhaustive permutation test (120 orderings) for exact p-values is correct. However, the noise ceiling estimation splits neurons rather than trials, which may overestimate electrophysiology reliability. Bootstrap CIs resample stimuli (not measurement noise), capturing stimulus variability only.

**Reproducibility (Good):** Uses publicly available datasets (MajajHong2015, FreemanZiemba2013) via Brain-Score API. The companion study's weights are referenced. Code availability statement is included.

**Significance (Moderate-High):** The finding that STDP and PC outperform BP at early visual stages in both species provides genuine cross-species validation of the "local learning rules are more brain-like at V1" hypothesis. The ResNet-50 capacity control is an important negative result -- suggesting IT convergence may be an artifact of small models rather than a fundamental property. This has implications for how the field interprets brain-score benchmarks.

**Red Flags / Concerns:**
- The n=5 learning rules makes rank-order analysis essentially descriptive (only tau=+/-1.0 detectable). The paper is honest about this, but it means the core cross-species ranking comparison is underpowered by design.
- The V2-to-V4 alignment drop is completely confounded by the dataset switch (different stimuli, recording methods, number of presentations). The paper acknowledges this but the cortical hierarchy profile in Figure 1 (right panel) could be misleading to casual readers.
- The custom CNN is very small (3 conv layers, CIFAR-10, 10 classes). The ResNet-50 comparison changes architecture, training data size, AND pretraining regime simultaneously, making it impossible to isolate which factor drives the IT improvement.
- The stimulus-control analysis shows IT rankings are weakly inverted across stimulus sets (tau=-0.40), undermining confidence in the IT cross-species comparison.
- Noise ceiling splitting neurons (not trials) may overestimate the ceiling, making the "substantial room for improvement" conclusion somewhat unreliable.

## Strengths

- Rigorous statistical framework: noise ceilings, bootstrap CIs (N=10,000), 5-seed variability, explicit power analysis, exhaustive permutation tests
- Honest and transparent handling of null results and limitations -- sets a good standard for the field
- Direct reuse of identical model weights from the human study eliminates a major confound (training variability)
- The stimulus-control analysis is a valuable methodological contribution, explicitly addressing a confound that many cross-species studies ignore
- Clear, focused research question with well-defined methodology and layer-to-region mapping
- The ResNet-50 capacity control provides important context for interpreting the main results

## Limitations

- Only 5 learning rules compared, making all rank-order analyses descriptive rather than inferential -- the core cross-species question cannot be answered with adequate statistical power
- Stimulus sets fundamentally differ between species (THINGS objects, FreemanZiemba textures, HVM objects), confounding species differences with stimulus domain effects
- The custom CNN (3-layer, CIFAR-10, 10 classes) is far too small to capture IT-level representations; the ResNet-50 comparison confounds architecture depth, training data scale, and pretraining regime
- Human fMRI (millions of neurons, hemodynamic signal) vs. macaque electrophysiology (hundreds of neurons, spiking activity) are fundamentally different measurement modalities, making direct rho comparisons across species difficult to interpret
- The V2-to-V4 cortical profile conflates a genuine hierarchy change with a dataset switch -- this is acknowledged but cannot be resolved with existing data
- Noise ceiling estimation splits neurons rather than trials, which may overestimate reliability for electrophysiology data
- Human data from only 3 subjects (with sub-03 showing weak signal); macaque data from a small number of animals

## Relevance

这项研究通过跨物种比较验证了"局部学习规则（STDP、PC）在早期视觉皮层比反向传播更符合大脑"这一假说，对计算神经科学中生物plausible学习规则的研究提供了重要的跨物种实证支持。如果你关注生物 plausible 学习规则与深度学习的关系、Brain-Score 评估框架、或 RSA 方法论在跨物种比较中的应用，这篇论文值得深入阅读。其核心启示——模型容量和训练数据在高级视觉区域比学习规则更重要——对理解DNN与大脑的对齐机制具有指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would using matched stimulus sets across species (e.g., the same images presented to both humans and macaques) reveal genuine cross-species rank correlations at IT?
- How much of the pretrained ResNet-50 advantage at IT comes from architecture depth vs. training data scale vs. ImageNet's object-centric bias? A factorial design (same architecture, different training data) would be informative.
- Could a deeper custom CNN trained with STDP or PC close the gap with the pretrained ResNet-50 at IT, or is the training data the binding constraint?
- What happens to cross-species alignment when using calcium imaging (intermediate spatial/temporal resolution between fMRI and electrophysiology)?
- Are there intermediate visual areas (V4) where cross-species rank correlations are detectable with more learning rule conditions (n>5)?
- Does the noise ceiling estimation method (splitting neurons vs. splitting trials) meaningfully affect the "room for improvement" interpretation?
- Would the early-visual conservation result hold with more diverse architectures (transformers, state-space models) rather than only CNNs?
