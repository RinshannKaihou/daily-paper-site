> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示分布偏移导致字典解释器（SAE/Transcoder）解码器子空间与OOD激活子空间几何错位，形式化定义"忠实性差距"，并提出GAE——一种仅需无标签OOD激活、无需梯度更新的闭式旋转+约束重拟合方法，在GPT-2和Pythia-1.4B上以不到3秒的计算时间超越所有需要百万token训练的基线方法。

> **TL;DR:** Dictionary-based explainers (SAEs, transcoders) lose faithfulness under distribution shift because the OOD data activates a rotated subspace, misaligning the ID-trained decoder. The authors formalize this as the "faithfulness gap" (Frobenius distance between decoder subspace and OOD-active subspace) and prove it controls faithfulness degradation. They propose GAE, a two-step closed-form method: (1) Procrustes rotation to align the decoder column space with the OOD-active subspace, (2) constrained ridge regression to refit decoder columns while preserving alignment. GAE requires only unlabeled OOD activations, no gradient updates, and completes in under 3 seconds. On GPT-2 Small and Pythia-1.4B across three shift types (temporal, domain, adversarial), GAE matches or surpasses all training-based baselines (Finetune, Retrain, SAEBoost, FaithfulSAE, TERM) on 5 of 6 settings, with the largest gains on adversarial shift (93% reduction in |delta-CE| vs. best baseline).

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了分布偏移下字典解释器失效的几何机制：OOD输入使模型激活子空间发生旋转，导致ID训练的解码器子空间与OOD子空间错位，通过二阶矩矩阵 M_OOD vs M_ID 的特征向量结构变化进行刻画。
- 形式化定义"忠实性差距" (faithfulness gap)，即解码器子空间与OOD激活子空间的Frobenius距离，并证明（命题2）其控制OOD忠实性退化的可约减部分。
- 提出GAE两步法：步骤1通过Procrustes问题找到最优正交旋转，使解码器列空间对齐OOD活跃子空间；步骤2通过闭式约束岭回归重拟合解码器，保持几何对齐的同时优化样本级重建。无需梯度更新，仅需无标签OOD激活。
- 定理1保证GAE相对ID解释器的改进量随错位程度二次方增长（即shift越严重，GAE收益越大），改进量被 gamma_OOD * Delta(Pi_ID)^2 / 2 下界约束。
- 实验在GPT-2 Small和Pythia-1.4B上，覆盖时序、领域和对抗性三种分布偏移，GAE在5/6设置中超越所有训练基线，计算时间不到3秒。

## Key Contributions

- Identify the geometric mechanism behind OOD faithfulness degradation: distribution shift rotates the model's active subspace (top-r eigenspace of second-moment matrix), misaligning the ID-trained decoder subspace. The shift is quantified via the second-moment matrices M_ID and M_OOD.
- Formalize the "faithfulness gap" (Definition 1): Delta(Pi_dec) = ||Pi_OOD - Pi_dec||_F, the Frobenius distance between the explainer's decoder subspace and the OOD-active subspace. Proposition 2 proves this gap controls the reducible part of OOD faithfulness loss.
- Propose GAE with two closed-form steps: (1) Solve a Procrustes problem to find optimal orthogonal rotation T* aligning the decoder's top-r left singular vectors with the estimated OOD active subspace, yielding W_dec* = W_dec T*; (2) Constrained ridge regression that penalizes decoder mass outside Pi_OOD while regularizing toward W_dec* for feature preservation.
- Theorem 1 provides a theoretical guarantee: L_OOD(Pi_GAE) <= L_OOD(Pi_ID) - (gamma_OOD / 2) * Delta(Pi_ID)^2, where gamma_OOD is the eigengap. The improvement grows quadratically with the ID explainer's misalignment.
- Empirically demonstrate GAE outperforms all training-based baselines (Fixed, TERM, Finetune, Retrain, SAEBoost, FaithfulSAE) on 5 of 6 settings across GPT-2 Small and Pythia-1.4B, completing in under 3 seconds with no gradient computation.

## Methodology

**Problem formulation.** The target model produces hidden activations h(X) at a designated layer. Dictionary-based explainers (SAEs, transcoders) learn a decoder W_dec and encoder W_enc, reconstructing h(X) = W_dec * sigma(W_enc * h(X) + b_enc) + b_dec. The decoder columns serve as learned feature directions. The explainer is trained on in-distribution (ID) activations from P_ID.

**Key geometric insight.** The second-moment matrix M_e = E[h(X)h(X)^T] captures the representational geometry. The top-r eigenspace Pi_e (the "active subspace") concentrates most representational energy. Under distribution shift, M_OOD differs from M_ID, rotating the active subspace. Since dictionary-based explainers' optimal reconstruction directions depend on eigenvectors of M_e (following prior work [21]), a shift in M_e creates misalignment.

**Faithfulness gap (Definition 1).** Delta(Pi_dec) = ||Pi_OOD - Pi_dec||_F, where Pi_dec is the explainer's top-r decoder subspace. Proposition 1 shows this gap is bounded by ||M_OOD - M_ID||_F for ID-trained explainers. Proposition 2 shows the gap controls the reducible OOD faithfulness loss.

**GAE Step 1: Subspace alignment via Procrustes rotation.** Compute the top-r eigenvectors U_OOD of M_OOD from unlabeled OOD activations. Then solve min_T ||U_OOD - U_dec * T||_F subject to T^T T = I (orthogonal Procrustes problem). The solution is T* = VU^T where U_dec^T * U_OOD = USV^T (SVD). Apply: W_dec* = W_dec * T*.

**Theorem 1 guarantee.** Under gamma_OOD > 0 (eigengap), Delta(Pi_ID) > 0, and sufficient OOD samples, the projection loss improvement satisfies L_OOD(Pi_GAE) <= L_OOD(Pi_ID) - (gamma_OOD / 2) * Delta(Pi_ID)^2.

**GAE Step 2: Constrained decoder refinement.** Since the encoder is frozen, feature activations z_i are constants. Reconstruction reduces to linear least-squares in W_dec. The convex objective penalizes: (1) decoder mass outside Pi_OOD via lambda_geom * ||(I - Pi_OOD) W_dec||_F^2, and (2) deviation from Step 1's rotation via lambda_pres * ||W_dec - W_dec*||_F^2. This yields a closed-form solution (Eq. 8-11) involving covariance matrices of z_i and h_i.

**OOD settings.** Three types of distribution shift: (1) FineWeb-Edu (temporal shift -- web text from a different time period), (2) Edgar corpus (domain shift -- financial filings), (3) HaluEval (adversarial shift -- hallucination detection data).

**Baselines.** Fixed (ID-trained, no adaptation), TERM (tilted empirical risk minimization), Finetune (continue training on OOD data), Retrain (train from scratch on OOD data), SAEBoost (residual capacity via boosting), FaithfulSAE (train on model's own generations).

**Faithfulness metrics.** nAOPC (normalized area over the perturbation curve -- higher is better), nComp (normalized comprehensiveness -- higher is better), |delta-CE| (absolute change in cross-entropy after feature ablation -- lower is better). These measure causal faithfulness: does ablating identified features actually change model behavior?

## Results & Highlights

**Controlled experiment (toy MLP).** On a 2-layer ReLU MLP (d=256), as OOD severity increases from 0 to 1, the Fixed explainer's faithfulness gap grows monotonically while GAE maintains near-zero gap and flat reconstruction error throughout. This validates the geometric mechanism in isolation.

**GPT-2 Small results (Transcoder).** GAE achieves best performance on all 9 columns (3 metrics x 3 shift types). Highlights:
- HaluEval (adversarial): nComp 1.303 (vs Retrain 1.276), |delta-CE| 0.0014 (vs Finetune 0.0218, 93% reduction)
- Edgar (domain): nComp 0.809 (vs Retrain 0.797), |delta-CE| 0.0037
- FineWeb (temporal): |delta-CE| 0.0011 (vs SAEBoost 0.0120)

**GPT-2 Small results (SAE).** GAE again leads all 9 columns with similar margins.

**Pythia-1.4B results.** GAE leads on nComp and |delta-CE| in 5 of 6 columns. Retrain is a stronger competitor here, taking best nComp on FineWeb for transcoders (1.681 vs 1.677) and best nAOPC on Edgar for SAEs (0.970 vs 0.968).

**Circuit attribution case study.** On a prompt predicting "American," GAE's decoder rotation alone corrects feature-level attribution: Fixed's class-specific DLA score is -0.55 (circuit points away from target), GAE's is +1.39 (circuit points toward target). The same top-3 features are selected; only the decoder columns differ.

**Mechanism analysis.** Principal angle measurements confirm GAE drives all angles to ~10^-3 degrees vs. 40-90 degrees for Fixed and Finetune. Step ablation shows Step 1 alone closes the faithfulness gap (7.63 to 0) but hurts nComp (0.74 to 0.44); Step 2 restores nComp to 0.96 at the cost of a small gap (1.59). The two steps are complementary.

**Efficiency.** GAE completes in under 3 seconds with no gradient computation, vs. baselines consuming up to 100M tokens.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The geometric framing of OOD faithfulness degradation is genuinely novel. The faithfulness gap formalization (linking second-moment shift to explainer misalignment) and the two-step closed-form GAE solution represent a clean conceptual contribution. Prior work addressed SAE OOD issues heuristically without diagnosing the underlying geometric cause.
- **Rigor: Strong.** The paper provides formal definitions (Definition 1), propositions with proofs (Propositions 1-2), a main theorem (Theorem 1 with proof in Appendix A.7), and controlled experiments that isolate the geometric mechanism. The step ablation analysis clearly decomposes the contribution of each component.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** The method is closed-form (no gradient tuning), uses standard models (GPT-2 Small, Pythia-1.4B), and the experimental setup is well-described. No code repository is mentioned, but the mathematical description is complete enough for reimplementation.
- **Significance: High for mechanistic interpretability.** The finding that gradient-based finetuning does not by itself drive subspace alignment (Figure 4a shows Finetune leaves 40-90 degree gaps) is important and somewhat counterintuitive. This challenges the assumption that standard training on OOD data is sufficient.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  1. **Model scale limitation.** The authors acknowledge they have not evaluated on larger-scale models. GPT-2 Small (124M) and Pythia-1.4B are small by modern standards. The top-r SVD truncation of W_dec may lose important feature information at larger scales where d is much larger.
  2. **Retrain competitiveness on Pythia.** On Pythia-1.4B, Retrain matches or slightly outperforms GAE on some metrics (FineWeb nComp 1.681 vs 1.677), suggesting the advantage narrows on more capable models.
  3. **OOD estimation quality.** GAE requires estimating the OOD active subspace from samples. With limited OOD data, the estimated Pi_OOD may be noisy. The paper does not systematically study sensitivity to OOD sample size.
  4. **Rank selection.** The choice of r (top-r eigenspace dimension) is critical. The paper does not provide guidance on how to select r in practice. Appendix F covers hyperparameter sensitivity but this is a key practical concern.
  5. **Encoder remains fixed.** Only the decoder is adapted. If the OOD shift also changes which features should be active (not just their geometric direction), the fixed encoder may miss important features.

## Strengths

- Principled geometric formulation that precisely diagnoses the root cause of OOD faithfulness degradation.
- Elegant closed-form solution requiring no gradient updates -- completes in under 3 seconds.
- Strong theoretical guarantees: improvement grows quadratically with the severity of misalignment (Theorem 1).
- Comprehensive experimental design: controlled MLP experiment, two LLM architectures, two explainer types (SAE + transcoder), three shift types, six baselines, three faithfulness metrics.
- The finding that gradient-based finetuning does not fix subspace alignment is a valuable negative result.
- Step ablation cleanly decomposes the contribution of each component.
- Circuit attribution case study provides qualitative evidence that decoder rotation alone corrects feature-level attribution.

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on relatively small models (GPT-2 Small 124M, Pythia-1.4B). Scalability to 7B+ models is unknown.
- Top-r SVD truncation discards feature information in residual (d-r) singular directions, which may be more important at larger scales.
- Encoder is kept fixed; only decoder is adapted. This limits GAE's ability to handle shifts that change which features should be active.
- Rank r selection is not systematically studied; poor choice of r could degrade performance.
- The OOD settings (FineWeb, Edgar, HaluEval) may not cover all practically relevant shift types (e.g., prompt distribution shift in instruction-tuned models, multilingual shift).
- On Pythia-1.4B, Retrain is competitive on some metrics, suggesting the advantage may diminish on more capable models with different eigenvalue decay profiles.

## Relevance

本文从几何视角揭示了SAE/Transcoder在分布偏移下忠实性退化的机制并提出闭式解法，对于从事机械可解释性研究、尤其关注LLM安全分析中字典学习方法的鲁棒性的研究者具有直接参考价值。其几何分析框架也可启发其他基于子空间的解释方法在OOD场景下的理论分析。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does GAE perform on larger models (7B, 70B) where the eigenvalue decay profile may differ and the rank-r approximation may capture less variance?
- Can the encoder also be adapted in a similar closed-form manner, or would this break the feature structure that GAE aims to preserve?
- How sensitive is GAE to the OOD sample size? What is the minimum number of unlabeled OOD activations needed for reliable subspace estimation?
- Does GAE extend naturally to instruction-tuned or RLHF models where the "distribution" includes prompt format shifts?
- Could the geometric alignment framework be combined with online/continual dictionary learning for deployment in settings with ongoing distribution drift?
- Is there a connection between GAE's Procrustes rotation and optimal transport on the Grassmannian (as mentioned in the conclusion), and could this yield adaptive rank selection?
- How does GAE interact with feature steering interventions -- does better subspace alignment translate to more reliable steering under OOD?
