## 一句话总结

OWPO 通过将优化方向（由验证器决定）与更新幅度（由参考策略调节）解耦，解决了 RLVR 中对称 KL 约束导致的"力反转"问题，使 LLM 能够持续自我进化。

## TL;DR

Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) trains LLMs using sparse binary rewards from verifiers, but standard KL-regularized methods penalize all deviations symmetrically, flipping the update direction when the policy outperforms the reference ("Force Reversal"). OWPO introduces asymmetric reweighting: Accelerated Alignment for inferior deviations and Gain Locking for superior ones. With iterative reference updates creating a "Ratchet Effect," OWPO outperforms DAPO, OPD, and MOPD on math benchmarks, enabling continuous self-evolution beyond fixed priors.

## 核心贡献

1. **识别"力反转"问题**: 揭示了现有 RLVR 方法中对称 KL 约束在策略超越参考策略时会翻转验证器决定的优化方向，从而抑制性能提升。
2. **提出 OWPO 方法**: 基于方向与幅度解耦的原则，设计非对称信任区域——对劣偏差执行加速对齐（Accelerated Alignment），对优偏差执行增益锁定（Gain Locking）。
3. **理论分析**: 通过 Lemma 4.1 证明 OWPO 等价于方向感知的正则化策略梯度；Proposition 4.2 证明有效进度系数始终为正（不反转）；Corollary 4.3 给出方差上界。
4. **棘轮效应（Ratchet Effect）**: 通过迭代更新参考策略，OWPO 持续巩固已获得的增益，实现自进化。
5. **超越次优先验**: 实验证明 OWPO 能突破初始弱参考策略的性能上限，4 轮迭代即达到约 40% Pass@1（AIME24），而 MOPD 需 6 轮才达到约 37%。

## Key Contributions

1. **Identifies the "Force Reversal" problem**: Standard symmetric KL regularization in RLVR flips the verifier-determined update direction when the policy outperforms the reference, suppressing gains rather than enabling them.
2. **Proposes OWPO**: Decouples optimization direction (set by the verifier) from update magnitude (modulated by the reference policy) via asymmetric reweighting with Accelerated Alignment for inferior deviations and Gain Locking for superior deviations.
3. **Rigorous theoretical grounding**: Proves OWPO is equivalent to a direction-aware regularized policy gradient (Lemma 4.1), the effective progress coefficient is always positive (Proposition 4.2), and gradient variance is bounded (Corollary 4.3).
4. **Ratchet Effect for self-evolution**: Iterative reference refresh consolidates gains across training stages, enabling the model to continuously improve without external teacher models.
5. **Breaks suboptimal prior ceilings**: Demonstrates that OWPO can transcend weak initial reference policies through iterative bootstrapping, converging to ~40% on AIME24 in 4 iterations versus MOPD's ~37% in 6 iterations.

## Methodology

### Problem Setup
OWPO operates in the RLVR framework where an LLM policy is trained with sparse, binary verifier rewards at the sequence level. A reference policy provides token-level stabilization but introduces conflicts when symmetric constraints are applied.

### Core Mechanism
OWPO introduces a **directional deviation** measure:
$$\delta_t(\theta) = \text{sgn}(A_t) \cdot \log \frac{\pi_\theta(y_t | s_t)}{\pi_{\text{ref}}(y_t | s_t)}$$

and a **one-way weight**:
$$w_t = \text{clip}(\exp(-\delta_t), \epsilon_{\text{low}}, \epsilon_{\text{high}})$$

where $0 < \epsilon_{\text{low}} < 1 < \epsilon_{\text{high}}$.

- **Inferior deviations** ($\delta < 0$, policy lags behind reference): $w > 1$, amplifying the gradient for faster correction (Accelerated Alignment).
- **Superior deviations** ($\delta > 0$, policy surpasses reference): $w \in [\epsilon_{\text{low}}, 1)$, reducing step size to lock in gains without reversing direction (Gain Locking).

The key insight is that $w$ is always positive, so the verifier-determined direction is never flipped. In contrast, symmetric KL penalties can produce negative effective coefficients when deviations are large.

### Self-Evolving Protocol
1. Train with OWPO using $\pi_{\text{ref}}$.
2. Periodically refresh $\pi_{\text{ref}} \leftarrow \pi_\theta$ (every 80 steps).
3. Active sample count linearly decays from group size $G$ to 1, shifting from exploration to exploitation.

## Results & Highlights

### Main Results (Pass@1, Best Checkpoint)
| Model | Method | AIME24 | AIME25 | AMC | Average |
|-------|--------|--------|--------|-----|---------|
| Qwen-2.5-Math-7B | DAPO | 37.19 | 15.62 | 68.86 | 40.56 |
| Qwen-2.5-Math-7B | MOPD | 38.85 | 15.93 | 68.56 | 41.11 |
| Qwen-2.5-Math-7B | **OWPO** | **41.67** | **17.92** | **72.14** | **43.91** |
| Qwen-3-8B | DAPO | 36.67 | 27.39 | 71.88 | 45.31 |
| Qwen-3-8B | MOPD | 37.80 | 29.38 | 72.03 | 46.40 |
| Qwen-3-8B | **OWPO** | **44.38** | **33.54** | **77.79** | **51.90** |

### Key Highlights
- On Qwen-3-8B, OWPO achieves **51.90% average Pass@1**, outperforming the next best (MOPD: 46.40%) by **+5.5 points**.
- OWPO maintains competitive Pass@16 scores, confirming that gains are not narrow top-1 improvements but reflect broader coverage of correct reasoning.
- At convergence, OWPO still leads (Qwen-3-8B: 50.74% vs MOPD's 45.35%), demonstrating resistance to late-stage regression.
- OWPO generalizes to GPQA and MMLU-Pro, outperforming both DAPO and MOPD on general science benchmarks.
- In iterative self-evolution experiments with a suboptimal reference (30.0%), OWPO reaches ~40% in 4 iterations while MOPD needs 6 iterations to reach ~37%.

### Ablation Studies
Removing the asymmetric design (w/o Asym), Gain Locking (w/o Locking), or Accelerated Alignment (w/o Accel) all degrade performance, confirming each component's necessity.

## Critical Assessment

### Strengths
- **Clear problem identification**: The "Force Reversal" phenomenon is a real and well-articulated issue in RLVR. The intuition that symmetric KL constraints suppress exploration gains is compelling.
- **Principled design**: OWPO is not ad-hoc; the asymmetric reweighting has clean theoretical justification, and the proofs are rigorous.
- **Strong empirical results**: Consistent improvements across two base models (Qwen-2.5-Math-7B, Qwen-3-8B) and four math benchmarks, plus generalization to GPQA/MMLU-Pro.
- **Self-evolution capability**: The iterative bootstrapping protocol with suboptimal priors is a convincing demonstration of practical utility.
- **Comprehensive ablations**: Each component is validated, and the hyperparameter sensitivity analysis adds transparency.

### Limitations
- **Evaluation scope**: Focused primarily on math and general reasoning with reliable verifiers. Extension to coding, tool-use, and interactive RLVR is acknowledged as future work.
- **Theory is local**: The regularization equivalence (Lemma 4.1) is a local first-order result, not a global guarantee for the full clipped objective. The authors are transparent about this.
- **Hyperparameter sensitivity**: OWPO introduces new hyperparameters ($\epsilon_{\text{low}}$, $\epsilon_{\text{high}}$, reference refresh interval). While the chosen values (0.8, 1.2, 80 steps) work well, the sensitivity to these choices is not fully explored.
- **Incremental reference update**: The "hard swap" for reference refresh is simple but may discard useful information. Softer update schemes could be more robust.
- **No comparison with very recent concurrent work**: The paper does not compare with some concurrent RLVR methods published around the same time.

## Relevance

- Directly relevant to RLVR and post-training of reasoning LLMs, a rapidly growing area.
- The direction-aware regularization principle is broadly applicable beyond math reasoning to any RLVR setting with sparse rewards.
- The self-evolution capability addresses a key bottleneck: current RLVR methods plateau at the reference policy's performance ceiling.
- Related to ongoing work on iterative self-improvement (STaR, ReST, self-play) and policy optimization for LLMs (GRPO, DAPO, PPO variants).

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does OWPO interact with process-based (dense) reward models rather than outcome-based (sparse) verifiers? Would the Force Reversal issue persist?
2. Can the asymmetric reweighting principle be applied to other RL settings beyond LLMs (e.g., robotic control with sparse rewards)?
3. What is the computational overhead of the iterative reference refresh protocol compared to single-stage training?
4. How sensitive is OWPO to the quality of the initial reference policy in more extreme cases (e.g., randomly initialized reference)?
5. Could the directional deviation $\delta_t$ be extended to incorporate multi-token or segment-level information rather than token-level weights?
