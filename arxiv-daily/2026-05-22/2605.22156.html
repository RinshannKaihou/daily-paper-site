Methodology
### Problem Setup OWPO operates in the RLVR framework where an LLM policy is trained with sparse, binary verifier rewards at the sequence level. A reference policy provides token-level stabilization but introduces conflicts when symmetric constraints are applied. ### Core Mechanism OWPO introduces a directional deviation measure: $$\delta_t(\theta) = \text{sgn}(A_t) \cdot \log \frac{\pi_\theta(y_t | s_t)}{\pi_{\text{ref}}(y_t | s_t)}$$ and a one-way weight: $$w_t = \text{clip}(\exp(-\delta_t), \epsilon_{\text{low}}, \epsilon_{\text{high}})$$ where $0 < \epsilon_{\text{low}} < 1 < \epsilon_{\text{high}}$. - Inferior deviations ($\delta < 0$, policy lags behind reference): $w > 1$, amplifying the gradient for faster correction (Accelerated Alignment). - *Superior deviations

Critical Assessment

**Clear problem identification**
The "Force Reversal" phenomenon is a real and well-articulated issue in RLVR. The intuition that symmetric KL constraints suppress exploration gains is compelling.
**Principled design**
OWPO is not ad-hoc; the asymmetric reweighting has clean theoretical justification, and the proofs are rigorous.
**Strong empirical results**
Consistent improvements across two base models (Qwen-2.5-Math-7B, Qwen-3-8B) and four math benchmarks, plus generalization to GPQA/MMLU-Pro.
**Self-evolution capability**
The iterative bootstrapping protocol with suboptimal priors is a convincing demonstration of practical utility.
**Comprehensive ablations**
Each component is validated, and the hyperparameter sensitivity analysis adds transparency.
**Evaluation scope**
Focused primarily on math and general reasoning with reliable verifiers. Extension to coding, tool-use, and interactive RLVR is acknowledged as future work.
