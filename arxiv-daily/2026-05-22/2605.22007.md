## 一句话总结

LLM幻觉往往不是因为模型"不知道"正确答案,而是因为模型在commitment step(承诺步)将概率质量分散在正确答案的多个surface form(表面形式)上,被错误概念的单一尖锐token击败——且这一现象随模型规模和指令微调的增强而恶化。

## TL;DR

The paper introduces a semantic probability mass metric ($P_{\text{mass}}$) that aggregates token-level probabilities across all surface forms of a correct answer concept. Across 18 Qwen and Llama models (0.8B-72B, Instruct and Base), 16-47% of hallucinations in Instruct models have substantial mass ($\geq 0.2$) on the correct concept at the commitment step yet still emit a wrong answer. The rate rises monotonically with scale. The key finding: the problem is not knowledge absence but probability fragmentation. Instruction tuning sharpens answer commitment, which helps when right but makes hallucinations more confident when wrong.

## 核心贡献

1. **提出语义概率质量 $P_{\text{mass}}$ 探针**: 将同一答案概念的所有surface form（如"Saint"、"St."、"C"都指代圣瓦西里大教堂）的概率质量聚合,超越单token置信度,在概念层面衡量模型的"知识可用性"。

2. **发现commitment failure现象并量化其规模依赖性**: 在Instruct模型中,16%-47%的幻觉属于"承诺失败"——模型在承诺步已将$\geq 0.2$的概率质量放在正确概念上,却仍然输出了错误答案。该比例随模型规模单调递增（Qwen: 16%@0.8B -> 47%@72B）。

3. **揭示指令微调驱动的"锐化"机制**: 指令微调使模型在承诺步的决策更加尖锐（错误token概率从~0.31升至~0.57），Base模型则保持平坦（~0.30）。同一锐化机制在正确时产生高置信正确输出,在错误时产生高置信幻觉——两者是同一分布状态的两个面。

## Key Contributions

1. **Semantic probability mass probe ($P_{\text{mass}}$)**: Aggregates token probabilities across all surface forms of a correct answer concept (e.g., "Saint", "St.", "C" for Saint Basil's Cathedral), going beyond single-token confidence to measure concept-level knowledge availability.

2. **Commitment failure phenomenon with scale dependence**: In Instruct models, 16-47% of hallucinations are "commitment failures" where the model assigns $\geq 0.2$ probability mass to the correct concept at the commitment step yet emits a wrong answer. This rate rises monotonically with model scale (Qwen: 16%@0.8B -> 47%@72B).

3. **Instruction-tuning-driven sharpening mechanism**: Instruction tuning sharpens commitment decisions (wrong-token probability rises from ~0.31 to ~0.57 with scale), while Base models stay flat at ~0.30. The same sharpening produces confident correctness when right and confident hallucination when wrong—two outcomes of one distributional state.

## Methodology

- **Commitment step identification**: In short-form QA with instruction-tuned models, the answer emission step $t_c$ is fixed at $t = 1$. Token-level entropy analysis shows that predictive information about correctness is localized to this single step.

- **Semantic probability mass ($P_{\text{mass}}$)**: Defined as $P_{\text{mass}}(t; c) = \sum_{v \in S_c} p_\theta(v \mid Q, y_{<t})$, where $S_c$ collects the first-token IDs of all surface forms (aliases) for the correct concept $c^*$. This is an analytical probe (requires ground-truth aliases), not a deployable detector.

- **Commitment failure classification**:
  - *First-token selection failures* (~20% of CFs): greedy $y_1$ does not match any surface form of $c^*$, despite $P_{\text{mass}} \geq 0.2$.
  - *Multi-token divergences* (~80% of CFs): $y_1$ matches a surface form but continuation diverges from any valid alias.

- **Within-population matched comparison**: Compares selection failures against correct outputs with matched $P_{\text{mass}}$ to isolate the structural difference (mass concentration vs. fragmentation).

- **Representation-level analysis**: Logistic regression on last-layer hidden states at $t = 1$ (before generation), attention analysis, and multi-token entropy tracking across 18 models (Qwen and Llama families, 0.8B-72B, Instruct and Base variants).

## Results & Highlights

- **Commitment failure rate rises monotonically with scale**: Qwen Instruct 16% (0.8B) -> 47% (72B); Llama Instruct shows similar trend. Base models show no such trend.

- **Within-population gap is dramatic**: At matched $P_{\text{mass}}$, the max single-token probability for the correct concept is 0.26 (failures) vs. 0.78 (correct), Cohen's $d = 2.98$ (Qwen3-9B Instruct).

- **Wrong-token probability sharpens with instruction tuning**: Instruct models rise from 0.31 to 0.57 (Qwen) across scale; Base models remain flat at ~0.30.

- **Multi-token sharpening**: Type A divergences (correct bigram prefix, wrong continuation) have $H_{t=2} \approx 0.05$—near-deterministic commitment to wrong multi-token phrases.

- **Pre-generation hidden states carry predictive signal**: Instruct models show higher correctness-predictive AUROC from hidden states (+0.08 over Base), attention to question tokens (+0.09), and output-level detection (+0.29).

- **$P_{\text{mass}}$ is well-calibrated**: ECE 0.023-0.096 across Instruct models, with accuracy increasing monotonically in $P_{\text{mass}}$ bins.

## Critical Assessment

This paper provides a genuinely new structural account of why LLMs hallucinate. Rather than treating hallucination as a knowledge deficit, it reframes a substantial portion as a *selection* deficit—the model "knows" the answer but fails to commit to it. The monotonic scale trend is particularly important: it suggests that simply scaling up models will not fix this failure mode, and may worsen it.

### Strengths

- **Clean experimental design**: The $P_{\text{mass}}$ probe elegantly separates knowledge availability from selection quality. The within-population matched comparison (controlling for $P_{\text{mass}}$) is methodologically strong.
- **Scale and family consistency**: Results hold across Qwen and Llama families, Instruct and Base, from 0.8B to 72B, lending confidence to the generality of findings.
- **Actionable implication**: The paper directly suggests concept-aware decoding (argmax over alias-clustered top-k tokens) as a concrete intervention.
- **Multi-level evidence**: The sharpening story is supported at first-token, multi-token, and representation levels, creating a coherent narrative.
- **Connects to alignment tax**: The framing of confident hallucination as one face of the alignment tax (alongside calibration loss, accuracy loss, mode collapse) is insightful.

### Limitations

- **$P_{\text{mass}}$ requires ground-truth aliases**: As the authors acknowledge, this is an analytical probe, not a deployable detector. Practical use requires inferring $S_c$ from context.
- **Greedy decoding only**: The analysis is restricted to greedy decoding. Behavior under temperature sampling or top-p truncation remains unexplored.
- **Short-form QA focus**: The commitment step is fixed at $t=1$ via prompt format in short-form QA. Long-form generation with multiple commitment events is left to future work.
- **Limited model families**: Only Qwen and Llama are tested. The authors expect the pattern to generalize but cannot verify for closed models without first-token distribution access.
- **Correlational, not causal**: The sharpening mechanism is inferred from Instruct-Base comparisons rather than demonstrated through controlled intervention (e.g., varying instruction tuning intensity).

## Relevance

- **对LLM可靠性和安全性的研究具有直接相关性**: 揭示了幻觉的根本机制不只是知识缺失,而是选择失败,这对幻觉检测和缓解策略的设计有重要指导意义。
- **对模型扩展和指令微调的实践有警示意义**: 更大的模型和更强的指令微调可能加剧承诺失败,而非缓解它。
- **与alignment tax文献直接对话**: 将幻觉定位为alignment tax的一个面向,有助于理解指令微调的多面性代价。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **概念感知解码（concept-aware decoding）的实际效果如何？** 论文建议在top-k tokens上按alias聚类后argmax,这种方法在现实任务中能挽回多少承诺失败?计算开销如何?
2. **温度采样是否能缓解承诺失败？** 在高温或低top-p下,模型的概率分布是否更可能选中正确概念的某个surface form?
3. **承诺失败是否出现在长文本生成（如摘要、翻译）中？** 多个commitment step如何交互?domain commitment和answer commitment是否独立?
4. **指令微调的具体哪个阶段（SFT vs. RLHF/DPO）驱动了锐化效应？** 如果是RLHF阶段,调整奖励函数是否可以在保持有用性的同时减少过度锐化?
5. **闭源模型（GPT-4、Claude）的承诺失败率如何？** 没有first-token分布访问权限,是否可以通过API的logprobs做近似分析?
