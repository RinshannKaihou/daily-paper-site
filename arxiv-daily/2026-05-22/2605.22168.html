Methodology
### Problem Formulation The paper formalizes VLM inference as a cooperative game where players are input features (visual patches and text tokens). Critical Assessment

**Fundamental problem identification**
The evaluation collapse phenomenon is rigorously proven both theoretically (boundary conditions under redundancy) and empirically (Kendall's tau = -0.06).
**Principled metric design**
F_syn is grounded in well-established cooperative game theory, not ad-hoc heuristics.
**Strong statistical rigor**
The LMM framework with pairwise significance testing provides far more trustworthy conclusions than simple leaderboard averaging.
**Counterintuitive and impactful finding**
Challenging the assumption that VLM-native explainers are superior is valuable for the community -- these methods produce visually plausible but mathematically unfaithful explanations.
**Practical efficiency**
24x speedup makes large-scale benchmarking feasible.
**Restricted to VQA format and open-weight models**
The empirical validation covers only Visual Question Answering tasks and mid-scale, open-weight VLMs. Proprietary frontier models (GPT-4V, Gemini) could not be tested as their black-box APIs do not expose logits required by explainers.
