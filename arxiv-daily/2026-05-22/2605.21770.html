Methodology
The method has three stages: Stage 1: Contrastive Error Manifold Construction (offline) - For each problem p_i, generate S independent traces, label as correct (T^+) or incorrect (T^-). - Compute per-problem per-head class means and difference vectors (Eq. 2-3). - Stack difference vectors into a matrix D and perform SVD to extract top-k right singular vectors as error subspace basis B (Eq. 4-5). - Compute a global correct-state centroid from all correct traces (Eq. 6). - Key design: PCA on difference vectors (not raw error activations) isolates error-specific directions, not problem-content features (Remark 1). Critical Assessment

**Principled geometric framing**
Moving from fixed correction vectors to a manifold-based, trajectory-aware approach is conceptually cleaner and better motivated. The contrastive PCA construction is well-designed to isolate error-specific signals.
**Selective intervention with information preservation guarantee**
Proposition 1 formally proves the correction preserves all information in the (d_h - k)-dimensional complement of the error subspace. The proximity trigger avoids correcting already-correct steps.
**Comprehensive evaluation**
Three model families (Llama, Gemma, GPT-OSS), five benchmarks, comparison with ITI, Angular Steering, Contrastive Decoding. Perplexity analysis confirms distribution preservation. Ablation on hyperparameter sensitivity demonstrates robustness.
**Multi-objective compositional steering**
The MAGS^U variant elegantly handles conflicting objectives by learning independent manifolds with disjoint head sets.
**Latent trajectory analysis**
Visualization of steered vs. unsteered activations provides mechanistic insight into how corrections reshape internal representations.
**Model scale**
Evaluated on 8B and 4B models (plus GPT-OSS-20B). Unclear whether correctness manifolds maintain the same low-dimensional structure at frontier-model scale (70B+, 405B+).
