Methodology
QuantSR+ is a unified framework with three tightly integrated pillars: RBD (Redistribution-driven Bit Determination) -- Operator Level: - Operates on the quantization operator level, reshaping weight/activation distributions during both forward and backward passes. - Refines redistribution down to the individual quant level (finer than channel-wise or tensor-wise methods), improving flexibility and accuracy of the quantized representation. - The symmetric quantization baseline uses: Q_b^s(x) = round(clip(x/s, -1, 1)) s v_b^s - RBD modifies this process to redistribute the quantization decision boundaries via propagation-based optimization. - Key property: the packed quantized weights are exactly consistent with standard integers, meaning zero inference overhead at deployment. **Q
Results & Highlights
### CNN-based (SRResNet backbone): - 2-bit: QuantSR+ consistently outperforms all prior fixed-precision baselines on Set5, Set14, B100, Urban100, and Manga109 benchmarks at both x2 and x4 scales. On Set5 x4, improves from 31.81/0.8880 (ODM) to 31.89/0.8908 (+0.08 dB / +0.0028 SSIM). - 4-bit: QuantSR+* (with activation shifting from I2NQ) reaches 32.17 dB / 0.8951 on Set5 x4, surpassing the full-precision SRResNet (32.16 dB / 0.8951). - Mixed-precision comparison: QuantSR+* exceeds Granular-DQ by 0.03 dB on Urban100 x4. ### Transformer-based (SwinIR-S backbone): - 2-bit: Outperforms the original QuantSR by 0.17 dB PSNR and 0.0015 SSIM on Set5 x4. - vs. recent methods: Improves by 1.81 dB PSNR over 2DQuant and 0.29 dB over ODM on Urban100 x4 (under same 300K iterations).

Critical Assessment

**Truly unified framework**
Addresses quantization at three orthogonal levels (operators, architecture, training) simultaneously, rather than optimizing only one aspect. This holistic approach is more principled than prior single-aspect solutions.
**Zero-overhead quantizer**
RBD produces packed weights consistent with standard integers, meaning the accuracy gains come at no additional inference cost -- a practically important property.
**Strong and consistent empirical gains**
0.29 dB over 2-bit SOTA on Urban100 is substantial in SR where gains are hard-won. Results are consistent across 5 benchmarks, 3 architectures, and multiple bit-widths.
**4-bit matches full precision**
QuantSR+* at 4-bit matching or exceeding the full-precision SRResNet is a remarkable result.
**Architecture generality**
Validation on CNN, Transformer, AND diffusion SR backbones demonstrates genuine generality rather than method-overfitting to one architecture.
**Progressive design philosophy**
QSA's distributed slimming across blocks and stages is a clear improvement over abrupt architecture changes, showing thoughtful design.
