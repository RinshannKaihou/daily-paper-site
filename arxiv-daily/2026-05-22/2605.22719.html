Reading Task Failure Off the Activations: A Sparse-Feature Audit of GPT-2 Small on Indirect Object Identification

2605.22719 2026-05-22 Mahdi Nasermoghadasi
mechanistic-interpretabilitysparse-autoencoderGPT-2indirect-object-identificationfeature-auditing
TL;DR
本文用稀疏自编码器(SAE)特征审计GPT-2 Small在间接宾语识别(IOI)任务上的失败模式，发现最强失败相关特征（"cryptographic keys"，d=+2.93）只是词汇同义词混淆的相关变量而非因果机制，消融实验无法恢复准确率；核心贡献在于提出了一套三层控制验证框架（消融、原始表示基线、种子鲁棒性），为SAE特征审计设定了方法论标准。

Relevance
本文对使用SAE进行机制可解释性研究的方法论具有直接参考价值，特别是其三层控制框架（消融、原始表示基线、种子鲁棒性）为SAE特征审计设定了必要的方法论标准。核心负面发现——SAE增加可解释性但不增加预测力——对评估SAE在LLM内部机制分析中的实际作用具有重要警示意义。
Methodology
Setup: - Model: GPT-2 small (124M params, 12 layers, 768-dim residual stream), loaded via TransformerLens. - SAE: Bloom's layer-8 residual-stream SAE (gpt2-small-res-jb), d_sae=24,576 features, accessed via SAELens. Layer 8 chosen because IOI name-mover heads are in later layers, so candidate-answer representations should be present by then, and this layer has thorough Neuronpedia coverage. - Task corpus: 300 IOI prompts sampled from a procedural generator following Wang et al.'s IOI templates: 29 first names, 8 transferred objects (a drink, the keys, a book, the ball, the gift, a card, the bag, the flowers), 8 locations, 4 surface templates. Seed fixed at 42. Pipeline (Figure 1): 1. Forward pass each prompt through GPT-2 small with greedy decoding; score as success/fa
Results & Highlights
- Overall accuracy: GPT-2 small reached 239/300 = 79.7% on IOI (within Wang et al.'s reported range). 61 failed trials, 239 successful. - Feature statistics: 146 of 24,576 features cleared Holm-Bonferroni significance at alpha=0.05; 209 had |Cohen's d| > 0.5; 105 had |d| > 0.8. ~96% of features had |d| within +/-0.2 (no discrimination). - Top feature -- Feature 17,491: d=+2.93, Welch t=11.4, Holm-corrected p < 10^-10. Mean activation 10.28 on failed trials vs. 0.18 on successful (56x higher). 42 of 61 failed trials activated this feature; only 3 of 239 successful trials did. Neuronpedia label: "terms related to encryption and security keys" (GPT-3.5) / "references to cryptographic keys and key management concepts" (GPT-4o-mini, score 96/100). Top positive logits: stroke, */, ch

Key Contributions

  1. SAE feature audit pipeline: Takes a task corpus, an LM, and a residual-stream SAE as inputs; produces per-feature failure-vs-success discrimination reports (Welch's t, Cohen's d, Holm-corrected p-values, Neuronpedia URLs). Runs in ~20 minutes on a laptop.
  2. Three-control validation framework: (i) Causal ablation -- zeroing the top-|d| feature's decoded contribution from the residual stream; (ii) Raw representation baseline -- logistic regression on 768-dim residual stream to measure SAE's marginal predictive value; (iii) Seed robustness -- re-running across 5 random seeds to distinguish stable behavioral effects from unstable feature attributions.
  3. Discovery of the "keys" homonym failure mode: 93.3% failure rate when transferred object is "the keys" vs. 7.5% for other objects; behavioral effect is robust across seeds (75.0-93.3%), but the identity of the top-|d| feature is seed-dependent (feature 17,491 is top in only 1 of 5 seeds).
  4. Key negative result -- SAE adds interpretability, not predictive power: Top-100 SAE features (AUC 0.927) match raw residual-stream logistic regression (AUC 0.929); single-feature AUC of 0.839 is better read as "this feature is a near-perfect proxy for the lexical keys signal" than as a mechanistic factor.
  5. Full reproducibility: Code, data, 300x24,576 activation matrix, ablation scripts released at github.com/mahdinaser/agent-failure-circuits under MIT License. No GPU required.

Critical Assessment

**Novelty**
Moderate-to-high. The per-feature comparison across success/failure conditions is technically straightforward, but the three-control framework (ablation, raw baseline, seed robustness) as a mandatory package for SAE-based failure audits is a genuine methodological contribution. The finding that SAE adds interpretability but NOT predictive power is an important and underappreciated negative result.
**Rigor**
Very high. The three controls are exactly what a mechanistic claim should have to pass. The honesty is unusual for the field: the paper's central narrative is that its own initial mechanistic reading was wrong. Holm-Bonferroni (more conservative than BH FDR) was chosen deliberately for per-feature error control. The conditional analysis on the keys-free subset is an additional rigor layer not required by the main argument.
**Reproducibility**
Excellent. Runs on a laptop without GPU. All artifacts publicly released. The paper even specifies the exact macOS build number (14.4, build 23E214). Five-seed robustness check provides a minimum standard.
**Significance**
The specific finding (the "keys" homonym confound) is narrow and somewhat idiosyncratic. But the broader methodological lesson -- that striking single-feature d-values are where controls should START, not where the story should be filed -- is significant and should become standard practice in SAE-based interpretability. The result that IOI's 8 transferred objects are NOT exchangeable also has implications for anyone using IOI as a benchmark.
**Red flags**
(1) Single-author, single-model, single-layer, single-SAE study -- generalization is untested. (2) The seed-dependence of the top feature (1/5 runs) is a genuine concern for anyone hoping to use SAE features for stable mechanistic attribution. (3) The ablation is single-feature, single-layer -- a multi-feature or earlier-layer intervention could potentially show a causal effect, but the paper does not pursue this. (4) Only 5 seeds for the robustness check; a tighter effect-size interval would require more. (5) The Neuronpedia labels used for interpretation are auto-generated (GPT-3.5/GPT-4o-mini) and could be wrong; interpretation was done by a single person.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths
  1. Exceptional methodological honesty: the paper's central contribution is showing that its own headline finding does not hold up to scrutiny, and treating that gap as the result worth reporting.
  2. Three-control framework sets a standard that should be adopted by any SAE-based failure audit.
  3. Conditional analysis (keys-free subset) demonstrates the audit pipeline's ability to surface both real and spurious findings, and to separate them.
  4. Fully reproducible on consumer hardware (~20 minutes on M3 Max laptop).
  5. Clear, precise writing with appropriate statistical rigor (Holm correction, Welch's t, Fisher exact test, 5-fold CV AUC).
  6. Important negative result for the field: SAE basis adds interpretability, not predictive power over raw representations.
Limitations
  1. Single model, single SAE, single layer, single task: The pipeline is general by construction, but the empirical content has not been validated on Gemma Scope, Llama 3 SAEs, other layers of GPT-2, or non-IOI tasks.
  2. Single-feature, single-layer ablation: The negative ablation result does not rule out that the keys effect has a mechanistic cause distributed across multiple features, an earlier layer, or a position-specific intervention.
  3. Seed-dependence of feature attribution: The top-|d| feature changes across seeds (17,491 is top in only 1/5 runs), raising questions about the stability of any single SAE feature as a mechanistic explanation.
  4. Small scale: 300 prompts on a 124M-parameter base model that cannot follow instructions; relevance to instruction-tuned models or agent tasks is discussed but not demonstrated.
  5. Single human auditor: Neuronpedia labels are auto-generated and may be incorrect; interpretation was done by one person without independent verification.
  6. Position aggregation: Feature signal is averaged over the last 3 tokens; a finer per-position analysis is supported by the pipeline but not reported.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Would the same three-control audit pipeline applied to larger models with Gemma Scope SAEs yield more stable feature correlates, or is seed-dependence an inherent property of SAE feature attribution?
  2. Can the pipeline be combined with multi-feature ablation or path patching to identify genuinely causal feature sets (rather than single correlates)?
  3. Is the "keys" homonym failure mode a token-frequency effect specific to GPT-2's training data, or does it generalize to other models -- and does stratifying IOI results by transferred-object become a standard reporting practice?
  4. What does a per-position analysis (rather than last-3-token average) reveal about where in the prompt the discriminating SAE signal is concentrated?
  5. How does the pipeline extend to multi-step agent tasks where failure modes are more complex and the activation matrix grows substantially (e.g., 50K prompts x 16K features)?
