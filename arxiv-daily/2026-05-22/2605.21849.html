Methodology
Problem formulation. The target model produces hidden activations h(X) at a designated layer. Dictionary-based explainers (SAEs, transcoders) learn a decoder W_dec and encoder W_enc, reconstructing h(X) = W_dec sigma(W_enc h(X) + b_enc) + b_dec. The decoder columns serve as learned feature directions. The explainer is trained on in-distribution (ID) activations from P_ID. Key geometric insight. The second-moment matrix M_e = E[h(X)h(X)^T] captures the representational geometry. The top-r eigenspace Pi_e (the "active subspace") concentrates most representational energy. Under distribution shift, M_OOD differs from M_ID, rotating the active subspace. Key Contributions

  1. Identify the geometric mechanism behind OOD faithfulness degradation: distribution shift rotates the model's active subspace (top-r eigenspace of second-moment matrix), misaligning the ID-trained decoder subspace. The shift is quantified via the second-moment matrices M_ID and M_OOD.
  2. Formalize the "faithfulness gap" (Definition 1): Delta(Pi_dec) = ||Pi_OOD - Pi_dec||_F, the Frobenius distance between the explainer's decoder subspace and the OOD-active subspace. Proposition 2 proves this gap controls the reducible part of OOD faithfulness loss.
  3. Propose GAE with two closed-form steps: (1) Solve a Procrustes problem to find optimal orthogonal rotation T aligning the decoder's top-r left singular vectors with the estimated OOD active subspace, yielding W_dec = W_dec T; (2) Constrained ridge regression that penalizes decoder mass outside Pi_OOD while regularizing toward W_dec for feature preservation.
  4. Theorem 1 provides a theoretical guarantee: L_OOD(Pi_GAE) <= L_OOD(Pi_ID) - (gamma_OOD / 2) * Delta(Pi_ID)^2, where gamma_OOD is the eigengap. The improvement grows quadratically with the ID explainer's misalignment.
  5. Empirically demonstrate GAE outperforms all training-based baselines (Fixed, TERM, Finetune, Retrain, SAEBoost, FaithfulSAE) on 5 of 6 settings across GPT-2 Small and Pythia-1.4B, completing in under 3 seconds with no gradient computation.

Critical Assessment

**Novelty
High.** The geometric framing of OOD faithfulness degradation is genuinely novel. The faithfulness gap formalization (linking second-moment shift to explainer misalignment) and the two-step closed-form GAE solution represent a clean conceptual contribution. Prior work addressed SAE OOD issues heuristically without diagnosing the underlying geometric cause.
**Rigor
Strong.** The paper provides formal definitions (Definition 1), propositions with proofs (Propositions 1-2), a main theorem (Theorem 1 with proof in Appendix A.7), and controlled experiments that isolate the geometric mechanism. The step ablation analysis clearly decomposes the contribution of each component.
**Reproducibility
Good.** The method is closed-form (no gradient tuning), uses standard models (GPT-2 Small, Pythia-1.4B), and the experimental setup is well-described. No code repository is mentioned, but the mathematical description is complete enough for reimplementation.
**Significance
High for mechanistic interpretability.** The finding that gradient-based finetuning does not by itself drive subspace alignment (Figure 4a shows Finetune leaves 40-90 degree gaps) is important and somewhat counterintuitive. This challenges the assumption that standard training on OOD data is sufficient.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths
  1. Principled geometric formulation that precisely diagnoses the root cause of OOD faithfulness degradation.
  2. Elegant closed-form solution requiring no gradient updates -- completes in under 3 seconds.
  3. Strong theoretical guarantees: improvement grows quadratically with the severity of misalignment (Theorem 1).
  4. Comprehensive experimental design: controlled MLP experiment, two LLM architectures, two explainer types (SAE + transcoder), three shift types, six baselines, three faithfulness metrics.
  5. The finding that gradient-based finetuning does not fix subspace alignment is a valuable negative result.
  6. Step ablation cleanly decomposes the contribution of each component.
  7. Circuit attribution case study provides qualitative evidence that decoder rotation alone corrects feature-level attribution.
Limitations
  1. Only evaluated on relatively small models (GPT-2 Small 124M, Pythia-1.4B). Scalability to 7B+ models is unknown.
  2. Top-r SVD truncation discards feature information in residual (d-r) singular directions, which may be more important at larger scales.
  3. Encoder is kept fixed; only decoder is adapted. This limits GAE's ability to handle shifts that change which features should be active.
  4. Rank r selection is not systematically studied; poor choice of r could degrade performance.
  5. The OOD settings (FineWeb, Edgar, HaluEval) may not cover all practically relevant shift types (e.g., prompt distribution shift in instruction-tuned models, multilingual shift).
  6. On Pythia-1.4B, Retrain is competitive on some metrics, suggesting the advantage may diminish on more capable models with different eigenvalue decay profiles.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does GAE perform on larger models (7B, 70B) where the eigenvalue decay profile may differ and the rank-r approximation may capture less variance?
  2. Can the encoder also be adapted in a similar closed-form manner, or would this break the feature structure that GAE aims to preserve?
  3. How sensitive is GAE to the OOD sample size? What is the minimum number of unlabeled OOD activations needed for reliable subspace estimation?
  4. Does GAE extend naturally to instruction-tuned or RLHF models where the "distribution" includes prompt format shifts?
  5. Could the geometric alignment framework be combined with online/continual dictionary learning for deployment in settings with ongoing distribution drift?
  6. Is there a connection between GAE's Procrustes rotation and optimal transport on the Grassmannian (as mentioned in the conclusion), and could this yield adaptive rank selection?
  7. How does GAE interact with feature steering interventions -- does better subspace alignment translate to more reliable steering under OOD?
