> **一句话总结：** 本文系统性综述了从室温到低温（4K-77K）环境下各类主流与新兴存储技术（6T/8T SRAM、1T1C/2T1C/3T1C DRAM、eDRAM、NAND/NOR Flash、RRAM、STT-MRAM、FeFET）的工作原理、读写机制与设计约束，并讨论了UTBB-SOI伪静态存储和约瑟夫森结场效应晶体管（JJFET）等面向超导/量子计算平台的低温存储方案。

> **TL;DR:** A comprehensive survey chapter covering volatile memories (6T/8T SRAM, 1T1C/2T1C/3T1C DRAM, eDRAM) and non-volatile memories (NAND/NOR Flash, RRAM, STT-MRAM, FeFET) at both room temperature and cryogenic conditions (4K-77K), with detailed analysis of read/write mechanisms, design constraints, retention behavior, and emerging cryogenic technologies (UTBB-SOI, JJFET) for superconducting and quantum computing systems.

## 核心贡献

- 详细剖析了6T和8T SRAM的读写操作原理：6T SRAM共享读写端口，需3周期完成读后写；8T SRAM通过解耦读写端口将读后写优化为2周期，并支持全摆幅放电实现简化感测
- 系统比较了DRAM变体：1T1C（破坏性读出、读即刷新）、2T1C增益单元（非破坏性读出但感测裕度受限）、3T1C（更高感测裕度），以及eDRAM利用后端金属层电容实现单片集成的优劣
- 深入分析了NAND Flash的编程/擦除机制（Fowler-Nordheim隧道效应、约20V高压编程、块级擦除）和多级存储能力（可达1024级），以及NOR Flash在IoT/微控制器中的应用现状
- 阐述了RRAM的双极/单极切换机制（灯丝形成与断裂）、1T1R拓扑结构、set/reset电压约束和读写裕度，指出了高开关电压和低耐久性（~10^6次）的局限性
- 分析了STT-MRAM基于磁隧道结（MTJ）的自旋转移扭矩写入机制，强调其高耐久性（~10^15次）但低OFF/ON电阻比的权衡
- 讨论了FeFET/FeMFET利用铁电HZO层的非易失性存储，以及recessed FeFET和FeMFET降低写入电压的设计策略
- 概述了低温存储（4K-77K）的机遇与挑战：SRAM漏电降低但阈值电压升高、写入裕度下降；RRAM在低温下保持特性改善但灯丝动力学变化；UTBB-SOI伪静态电路和JJFET器件面向超导计算的创新方向

## Key Contributions

- Provides transistor-level detailed analysis of 6T and 8T SRAM operations, including initiation/completion phases of write, precharge-read sequence, sense amplifier requirements (100mV sensitivity), and Vmin constraints from retention
- Systematically compares DRAM variants: 1T1C (destructive read with inherent refresh), 2T1C gain cell (non-destructive read but limited sense margin from unselected row leakage), and 3T1C (improved sense margin through dedicated read transistors, similar to 8T SRAM)
- Analyzes eDRAM advantages (monolithic logic integration, 3D stacking for bandwidth) and limitations (backend metal-layer capacitor limits capacitance scaling, reducing retention time), with IGZO-based eDRAM as a novel solution for ultra-low leakage
- Details NAND Flash programming (Fowler-Nordheim tunneling at ~20V, row-level granularity) and erasing (block-level), multi-level cell capability (up to 1024 levels), and GIDL avoidance in unselected columns
- Explains RRAM bipolar switching (set: filament formation at positive voltage; reset: filament rupture at negative voltage), 1T1R topology, read margin dependence on OFF/ON resistance ratio, and limitations (high set/reset voltages, ~10^6 cycle endurance)
- Describes STT-MRAM's magnetic tunnel junction (pinned/spacer/free layers), spin-transfer torque switching mechanism, high endurance (~10^15 cycles), but lower OFF/ON resistance ratio compared to RRAM
- Covers FeFET evolution: standard HZO-based FeFET (high write voltage due to ILD voltage division), recessed FeFET (concentrated electric field), and FeMFET (independent ferroelectric capacitor at gate, but floating node susceptibility)
- Surveys cryogenic memory challenges and opportunities: improved SRAM leakage but degraded write margins; RRAM retention improvements but variable filament dynamics; UTBB-SOI pseudo-static storage for near-threshold cryogenic operation; JJFET devices for ultra-low-temperature logic with steep switching

## Methodology

This is an extended book chapter (originally published in 2023) that synthesizes the author's own prior publications with the broader literature. The author is from UT Austin and has published extensively on memory design (SRAM, DRAM, RRAM, FeFET, cryogenic memories, compute-in-memory architectures). The chapter follows a structured bottom-up approach:

1. **Volatile memories**: SRAM (6T, 8T bitcells with detailed circuit operation), DRAM (1T1C, 2T1C, 3T1C variants), and eDRAM (including IGZO-based novel approaches)
2. **Non-volatile memories**: NAND/NOR Flash (floating gate physics), RRAM (filament-based resistive switching), STT-MRAM (spin-transfer torque and MTJ physics), FeFET/FeMFET (ferroelectric polarization)
3. **Cryogenic memories**: Temperature-dependent behavior of all above technologies at 4K-77K, plus UTBB-SOI and JJFET devices specific to ultra-low-temperature computing

Each technology is analyzed through the lens of write/read/retention operations with explicit design constraints. The references are heavily self-cited (approximately 20 of 50 references are the author's own work), providing continuity with the author's research trajectory in near-memory computing and cryogenic systems.

## Results & Highlights

- **6T vs 8T SRAM tradeoff**: 6T offers density advantages (shared read/write port) but requires complex design constraints for read stability; 8T enables back-to-back read-after-write in 2 cycles via decoupled ports and full-swing RBL discharge enabling simple AND-gate sense amplifiers
- **DRAM variant comparison**: 1T1C offers natural refresh-on-read but destructive reads; 2T1C enables non-destructive reads at the cost of limited sense margin (constrained to threshold voltage); 3T1C improves sense margin through additional read transistor
- **eDRAM opportunity**: Monolithic integration enables higher bandwidth than commodity DRAM for last-level caches, but backend capacitor limits retention time; IGZO transistors offer extreme low leakage as a promising solution
- **RRAM characteristics**: Set voltages ~3-3.2V (reducible to ~1V in some devices), multi-level storage capability, but endurance limited to ~10^6 cycles
- **STT-MRAM advantage**: Orders of magnitude higher endurance (~10^15 cycles) than RRAM, ready for mass production, but lower read margin due to smaller OFF/ON resistance ratio
- **FeFET innovation pathway**: Standard FeFET to recessed FeFET (geometric field concentration) to FeMFET (independent capacitor optimization), progressively reducing write voltage but introducing floating node noise susceptibility
- **Cryogenic insights**: SRAM shows improved leakage and reduced standby power at cryogenic temperatures but suffers from increased threshold voltage, degraded write margins, and larger process variation sensitivity; RRAM exhibits improved retention but variable switching dynamics; JJFET devices enable steep switching and high noise margins at ultra-low temperatures

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Low -- this is a survey/review chapter with no new experimental results. The value lies in organizing and presenting the author's accumulated knowledge across memory technologies.
- **Rigor:** Moderate to good. The transistor-level circuit analysis is technically detailed (write initiation/completion phases, sense margin derivations, voltage constraints). However, the cryogenic section (Section 3) is notably less detailed than the room-temperature sections, reading more as a literature summary than a deep technical treatment. Quantitative comparisons (latency numbers, energy per access, area per bit) are largely absent -- the paper focuses on qualitative design constraint analysis rather than benchmarking.
- **Reproducibility:** N/A for a survey. The circuit-level descriptions are sufficiently detailed to serve as educational material for someone learning about these memory technologies.
- **Significance:** Moderate. As a pedagogical reference for memory design fundamentals, it has value. The bridging of room-temperature and cryogenic domains is a useful contribution. However, the field already has extensive textbooks and review papers on each individual topic. The chapter's primary differentiator is the unification of both temperature regimes in a single treatment.
- **Red flags:** (1) Heavy self-citation (~40% of references), which limits the breadth of literature surveyed. (2) The cryogenic section is substantially shorter and less rigorous than the room-temperature sections, despite being highlighted in the title. (3) No quantitative comparison tables or figures benchmarking technologies across metrics (latency, energy, density, endurance). (4) The paper is categorized under cs.AR but much of the content is circuit/device physics, potentially limiting its audience reach. (5) Originally published as a 2023 book chapter -- the arXiv version appears to be a lightly updated repost rather than a substantially new contribution.

## Strengths

- Provides unusually detailed transistor-level explanations of memory operations (e.g., write initiation vs. completion in SRAM, sense margin analysis in 2T1C DRAM, GIDL avoidance in NAND Flash) that go beyond typical survey papers
- Effectively bridges room-temperature and cryogenic memory domains, which is increasingly relevant as quantum computing and superconducting processors gain traction
- The author's direct research experience across multiple memory technologies (SRAM, DRAM, RRAM, FeFET, cryogenic) lends practical credibility to the analysis
- Educational value is high for graduate students and researchers entering the memory design field

## Limitations

- Lacks quantitative comparison tables (e.g., a table comparing latency, energy/bit, density, endurance across technologies would significantly enhance utility)
- The cryogenic section (Section 3) is underdeveloped relative to the room-temperature sections, despite being featured prominently in the title
- Heavy self-citation limits the breadth of the literature review; many important works from other groups are not referenced
- No discussion of emerging architectures like compute-in-memory, processing-in-memory, or near-memory computing in the main technical sections (despite the author's extensive publications in these areas)
- NAND NOR Flash coverage is dense but could benefit from clearer comparison tables
- The paper does not address 3D stacking approaches (3D NAND, 3D DRAM) in significant detail despite their industrial importance

## Relevance

该论文为硬件层面的存储技术综述，与机器学习算法研究无直接关联。但对于关注AI硬件加速器、存算一体架构（compute-in-memory）或量子计算控制系统的研究者，提供了重要的存储技术背景知识。作者本人的研究轨迹（近存计算加速器、Ising机、GNN加速）也暗示了存储技术与AI系统设计的交叉点。
