> **一句话总结：** 本文用稀疏自编码器(SAE)特征审计GPT-2 Small在间接宾语识别(IOI)任务上的失败模式，发现最强失败相关特征（"cryptographic keys"，d=+2.93）只是词汇同义词混淆的相关变量而非因果机制，消融实验无法恢复准确率；核心贡献在于提出了一套三层控制验证框架（消融、原始表示基线、种子鲁棒性），为SAE特征审计设定了方法论标准。

> **TL;DR:** A sparse-autoencoder feature audit of GPT-2 small on the IOI task identifies 146 significant features differing between failed and successful trials, with the strongest correlate (feature 17,491, "cryptographic keys," d=+2.93) being a lexical confound rather than a causal mechanism -- ablation does not restore accuracy, raw 768-dim residual stream matches SAE predictive power (AUC 0.929 vs 0.927), and the top feature identity changes across seeds. The main contribution is the three-control audit pipeline itself.

## 核心贡献

- **提出了一套SAE特征审计流水线**：输入任务语料库、语言模型和残差流SAE，输出逐特征的失败vs成功判别报告（Welch's t统计量、Cohen's d、Holm校正p值、Neuronpedia标签链接）。
- **三层控制验证框架**：(i) 因果消融：将top-|d|特征在残差流中置零，观察准确率是否恢复；(ii) 原始表示基线：在原始768维残差流上训练logistic回归，验证SAE是否增加预测力；(iii) 种子鲁棒性：多随机种子运行，区分为稳定的行为效应还是不稳定的特征归因。
- **发现GPT-2 Small在IOI任务上"keys"同义词混淆失败模式**：当转移宾语为"the keys"时，失败率93.3%（vs 其他七个宾语的7.5%），Fisher精确检验p=8.79x10^-33，该行为效应跨5个种子稳定（75.0-93.3%），但top特征身份在5次运行中仅出现1次。
- **关键负面发现：SAE提供可解释性而非预测力**：top-100 SAE特征（5-fold ROC AUC=0.927）与原始768维残差流logistic回归（AUC=0.929）性能持平，SAE是"有用的透镜"而非"有用的压缩"。
- **完整可复现性**：代码、300提示语料、300x24,576激活矩阵、消融和基线脚本均以MIT协议发布于GitHub，全部流水线在Apple M3 Max笔记本上运行（约20分钟，无需独立GPU）。

## Key Contributions

- **SAE feature audit pipeline**: Takes a task corpus, an LM, and a residual-stream SAE as inputs; produces per-feature failure-vs-success discrimination reports (Welch's t, Cohen's d, Holm-corrected p-values, Neuronpedia URLs). Runs in ~20 minutes on a laptop.
- **Three-control validation framework**: (i) Causal ablation -- zeroing the top-|d| feature's decoded contribution from the residual stream; (ii) Raw representation baseline -- logistic regression on 768-dim residual stream to measure SAE's marginal predictive value; (iii) Seed robustness -- re-running across 5 random seeds to distinguish stable behavioral effects from unstable feature attributions.
- **Discovery of the "keys" homonym failure mode**: 93.3% failure rate when transferred object is "the keys" vs. 7.5% for other objects; behavioral effect is robust across seeds (75.0-93.3%), but the identity of the top-|d| feature is seed-dependent (feature 17,491 is top in only 1 of 5 seeds).
- **Key negative result -- SAE adds interpretability, not predictive power**: Top-100 SAE features (AUC 0.927) match raw residual-stream logistic regression (AUC 0.929); single-feature AUC of 0.839 is better read as "this feature is a near-perfect proxy for the lexical keys signal" than as a mechanistic factor.
- **Full reproducibility**: Code, data, 300x24,576 activation matrix, ablation scripts released at github.com/mahdinaser/agent-failure-circuits under MIT License. No GPU required.

## Methodology

**Setup:**
- **Model**: GPT-2 small (124M params, 12 layers, 768-dim residual stream), loaded via TransformerLens.
- **SAE**: Bloom's layer-8 residual-stream SAE (gpt2-small-res-jb), d_sae=24,576 features, accessed via SAELens. Layer 8 chosen because IOI name-mover heads are in later layers, so candidate-answer representations should be present by then, and this layer has thorough Neuronpedia coverage.
- **Task corpus**: 300 IOI prompts sampled from a procedural generator following Wang et al.'s IOI templates: 29 first names, 8 transferred objects (a drink, the keys, a book, the ball, the gift, a card, the bag, the flowers), 8 locations, 4 surface templates. Seed fixed at 42.

**Pipeline (Figure 1):**
1. Forward pass each prompt through GPT-2 small with greedy decoding; score as success/failure by exact match of first non-whitespace alphanumeric token against the known indirect-object name.
2. Capture residual stream at blocks.8.hook_resid_pre on every forward pass; encode through SAE to recover per-token feature activations. Take mean SAE encoding over the last 3 tokens (so the position immediately before the answer dominates). This yields one R^24,576 vector per prompt (300x24,576 activation matrix, ~28 MB as float32 .npy).
3. For each of 24,576 features: compute per-class means, Welch's t-statistic, two-sided p-value (conservative df = min(n_fail, n_succ) - 1), and Cohen's d with pooled SD. Apply Holm-Bonferroni correction across all 24,576 tests (chosen over BH FDR because each significant feature gets manually interpreted against its Neuronpedia description).

**Baselines and Controls:**
- **Feature-space logistic regression**: Predict failure from top-K SAE features (K in {1,5,10,20,50,100}), 5-fold stratified CV, class-balanced weights, liblinear solver.
- **Raw-activation control**: Same logistic regression on the raw 768-dim residual stream (input to the SAE encoder).
- **Causal ablation**: Subtract feature 17,491's decoded contribution f_i(x) * W_dec[i,:] from the residual stream at every token position, re-decode greedily.
- **Seed robustness**: Re-run with seeds {0, 42, 100, 200, 300}; record overall accuracy, keys-subset failure rate, and identity/|d| of top feature.

## Results & Highlights

- **Overall accuracy**: GPT-2 small reached 239/300 = 79.7% on IOI (within Wang et al.'s reported range). 61 failed trials, 239 successful.
- **Feature statistics**: 146 of 24,576 features cleared Holm-Bonferroni significance at alpha=0.05; 209 had |Cohen's d| > 0.5; 105 had |d| > 0.8. ~96% of features had |d| within +/-0.2 (no discrimination).
- **Top feature -- Feature 17,491**: d=+2.93, Welch t=11.4, Holm-corrected p < 10^-10. Mean activation 10.28 on failed trials vs. 0.18 on successful (56x higher). 42 of 61 failed trials activated this feature; only 3 of 239 successful trials did. Neuronpedia label: "terms related to encryption and security keys" (GPT-3.5) / "references to cryptographic keys and key management concepts" (GPT-4o-mini, score 96/100). Top positive logits: stroke, */, chain, caps, binding -- cryptographic primitives.
- **The "keys" effect (Figure 4)**: 42/45 prompts using "the keys" as transferred object fail; only 19/255 of the rest fail. Fisher exact p = 8.79x10^-33, odds ratio ~174. The 8 transferred-object choices (treated as exchangeable in the IOI literature) are NOT exchangeable on GPT-2 small -- they span a ~0% to ~93% accuracy range.
- **Control 1 -- Ablation fails (Figure 5)**: Zeroing feature 17,491 on the 45 keys prompts moves accuracy from 6.7% to 4.4% (wrong direction, -2.2 pp). The feature is a correlate/downstream effect, not a sufficient cause at layer 8.
- **Control 2 -- Raw baseline matches SAE (Figure 6, Table II)**: Raw 768-dim residual stream: AUC 0.929. Top-100 SAE features: AUC 0.927. Full 24,576 SAE features: AUC 0.933. Single feature 17,491: AUC 0.839. SAE basis is interpretable but adds no predictive power.
- **Control 3 -- Seed dependence (Figure 7)**: Across 5 seeds, keys-subset failure rate: 75.0-93.3% (stable, mean 85.2%, sd 6.6). But feature 17,491 is top-|d| in only 1 of 5 seeds (seed 42). Other top features: {7536, 7536, 10960, 19149}. The behavioral effect is robust; the feature attribution is not.
- **Conditional analysis (keys-free subset)**: On 255 non-keys prompts (236 successes, 19 failures), only 5 features clear Holm significance (vs. 146 on full corpus); largest |d| ~1.40 (vs. 2.93). After removing the keys effect, the residual discriminating signal is small.
- **Success-correlated feature**: Feature 10,036 (d=-1.01, Neuronpedia label "concepts related to providing opportunities, granting, or offering something to others") fires more on successful trials (mean 2.75) than failed (mean 1.19). Its top tokens are prepositions "to", "unto", "onto" -- exactly the surface forms closing IOI transfer sentences, corroborating the existing name-mover circuit story at the feature level.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate-to-high. The per-feature comparison across success/failure conditions is technically straightforward, but the three-control framework (ablation, raw baseline, seed robustness) as a mandatory package for SAE-based failure audits is a genuine methodological contribution. The finding that SAE adds interpretability but NOT predictive power is an important and underappreciated negative result.
- **Rigor**: Very high. The three controls are exactly what a mechanistic claim should have to pass. The honesty is unusual for the field: the paper's central narrative is that its own initial mechanistic reading was wrong. Holm-Bonferroni (more conservative than BH FDR) was chosen deliberately for per-feature error control. The conditional analysis on the keys-free subset is an additional rigor layer not required by the main argument.
- **Reproducibility**: Excellent. Runs on a laptop without GPU. All artifacts publicly released. The paper even specifies the exact macOS build number (14.4, build 23E214). Five-seed robustness check provides a minimum standard.
- **Significance**: The specific finding (the "keys" homonym confound) is narrow and somewhat idiosyncratic. But the broader methodological lesson -- that striking single-feature d-values are where controls should START, not where the story should be filed -- is significant and should become standard practice in SAE-based interpretability. The result that IOI's 8 transferred objects are NOT exchangeable also has implications for anyone using IOI as a benchmark.
- **Red flags**: (1) Single-author, single-model, single-layer, single-SAE study -- generalization is untested. (2) The seed-dependence of the top feature (1/5 runs) is a genuine concern for anyone hoping to use SAE features for stable mechanistic attribution. (3) The ablation is single-feature, single-layer -- a multi-feature or earlier-layer intervention could potentially show a causal effect, but the paper does not pursue this. (4) Only 5 seeds for the robustness check; a tighter effect-size interval would require more. (5) The Neuronpedia labels used for interpretation are auto-generated (GPT-3.5/GPT-4o-mini) and could be wrong; interpretation was done by a single person.

## Strengths

- Exceptional methodological honesty: the paper's central contribution is showing that its own headline finding does not hold up to scrutiny, and treating that gap as the result worth reporting.
- Three-control framework sets a standard that should be adopted by any SAE-based failure audit.
- Conditional analysis (keys-free subset) demonstrates the audit pipeline's ability to surface both real and spurious findings, and to separate them.
- Fully reproducible on consumer hardware (~20 minutes on M3 Max laptop).
- Clear, precise writing with appropriate statistical rigor (Holm correction, Welch's t, Fisher exact test, 5-fold CV AUC).
- Important negative result for the field: SAE basis adds interpretability, not predictive power over raw representations.

## Limitations

- **Single model, single SAE, single layer, single task**: The pipeline is general by construction, but the empirical content has not been validated on Gemma Scope, Llama 3 SAEs, other layers of GPT-2, or non-IOI tasks.
- **Single-feature, single-layer ablation**: The negative ablation result does not rule out that the keys effect has a mechanistic cause distributed across multiple features, an earlier layer, or a position-specific intervention.
- **Seed-dependence of feature attribution**: The top-|d| feature changes across seeds (17,491 is top in only 1/5 runs), raising questions about the stability of any single SAE feature as a mechanistic explanation.
- **Small scale**: 300 prompts on a 124M-parameter base model that cannot follow instructions; relevance to instruction-tuned models or agent tasks is discussed but not demonstrated.
- **Single human auditor**: Neuronpedia labels are auto-generated and may be incorrect; interpretation was done by one person without independent verification.
- **Position aggregation**: Feature signal is averaged over the last 3 tokens; a finer per-position analysis is supported by the pipeline but not reported.

## Relevance

本文对使用SAE进行机制可解释性研究的方法论具有直接参考价值，特别是其三层控制框架（消融、原始表示基线、种子鲁棒性）为SAE特征审计设定了必要的方法论标准。核心负面发现——SAE增加可解释性但不增加预测力——对评估SAE在LLM内部机制分析中的实际作用具有重要警示意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would the same three-control audit pipeline applied to larger models with Gemma Scope SAEs yield more stable feature correlates, or is seed-dependence an inherent property of SAE feature attribution?
- Can the pipeline be combined with multi-feature ablation or path patching to identify genuinely causal feature sets (rather than single correlates)?
- Is the "keys" homonym failure mode a token-frequency effect specific to GPT-2's training data, or does it generalize to other models -- and does stratifying IOI results by transferred-object become a standard reporting practice?
- What does a per-position analysis (rather than last-3-token average) reveal about where in the prompt the discriminating SAE signal is concentrated?
- How does the pipeline extend to multi-step agent tasks where failure modes are more complex and the activation matrix grows substantially (e.g., 50K prompts x 16K features)?
