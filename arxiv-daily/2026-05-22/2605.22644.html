Critical Assessment

**Fundamentally correct observation**
The paper identifies a genuine mathematical gap -- the Langevin sqrt(eta) noise scaling is an imposed choice for the continuous-time limit, not a property of the discrete SGD update. The eta-organized expansion is more faithful to actual SGD.
**Qualitative prediction, not just quantitative correction**
The toy model shows that the mismatch can lead to fundamentally different predictions (stationary vs. divergent variance), not merely a small numerical error.
**Unified theoretical picture**
The confined/diffusive mode decomposition provides a clean conceptual framework that connects to prior work (inverse variance-flatness relation) while making new predictions (non-stationarity in flat directions).
**Honest about limitations**
Section 6 explicitly lists all assumptions (quadratic approximation, sampling with replacement, diagonal Hessian fluctuations) and discusses what is not covered.
**Reproducibility**
Small-scale experiments are designed to be exactly reproducible. The paper provides complete implementation details (Appendix C) and computational resource estimates (Appendix E).
**Local quadratic approximation**
The entire analysis near critical points assumes a quadratic loss. Real neural network loss landscapes are highly non-quadratic, especially during the transient training phase. The theory says nothing about dynamics far from critical points or during the bulk of training.
