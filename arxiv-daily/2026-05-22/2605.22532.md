> **一句话总结：** 本文提出基于KL散度的线性探测方法（KL-RP），系统验证了语言模型中关系线性性质的存在及其层级变化规律，发现表面句法特征（语言、时态）在浅层即呈线性编码，而抽象语义特征（真实性、主观性）在中间层达到峰值。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces a KL-divergence-based probing method (KL-RP) to empirically validate relational linearity in LLMs. Across four datasets (language, tense, subjectivity, truthfulness), the authors find that relational linearity is model-dependent, exhibits layer-wise patterns (surface features in early layers, abstract features in middle layers), and is moderately affected by query paraphrasing.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了基于KL散度的线性关系探测方法（KL-RP），用于评估语言模型表示中的关系线性性质，避免了先前工作（LRE）中对Jacobian的粗糙近似
- 在四个数据集（语言、时态、主观性、真实性）上系统验证了Llama-3.1-8B和Gemma-2-2B中的关系线性性
- 发现了清晰的层级动态规律：表面句法特征（如语言识别、时态判断）在浅层即可线性探测，抽象语义特征（真实性、主观性）在中间层达到峰值
- 分析了查询改写（paraphrase）对线性探测结果的影响，发现Llama-3.1对查询形式变化更鲁棒

## Key Contributions

- Introduces KL-RP (KL-based Relational Probe), a novel probing method that directly minimizes KL divergence between the probe's output distribution and the LLM's conditional distribution, avoiding crude Jacobian approximations used in prior LRE work (Hernandez et al., 2024)
- Provides the first comprehensive empirical validation of the relational linearity formulation proposed by Marconato et al. (2025), testing across four diverse datasets: LANG (20 languages), TENSE (past/present/future), SUBJ (opinion vs. fact), and TRUTH (true vs. false)
- Demonstrates clear layer-wise dynamics: surface syntactic features (language ID, tense) are linearly decodable from early layers, while abstract semantic features (truthfulness, subjectivity) peak in middle layers -- consistent with prior interpretability literature
- Shows that KL-RP outperforms LRE consistently, often discovering linear relational structure that LRE misses entirely (e.g., LANG F1(GT): 0.98 vs. 0.06 for Llama-3.1)
- Investigates the effect of query paraphrasing on relational probing, finding that Llama-3.1 is robust to surface form changes while Gemma-2 shows more sensitivity

## Methodology

The paper builds on the relational linearity hypothesis from Marconato et al. (2025): for a fixed relation/query q, the unembedding of an answer y can be predicted from the embedding of a context s alone via a linear map, without concatenating q to the input.

**KL-based Relational Probe (KL-RP):** A linear layer parameterized by weights W and biases b is trained to map context-only representations f(s) to a probability distribution over target tokens. The training objective minimizes the KL divergence between the probe's distribution and the LLM's distribution when given the full context-query input:

$$p_{W,b}(y | s) = \frac{\exp(w_y^\top f(s) + b_y)}{\sum_{y'} \exp(w_{y'}^\top f(s) + b_{y'})}$$

**Evaluation Metrics:**
- F1(GT): F1-score against ground-truth labels
- F1(LLM): F1-score against the model's most likely tokens
- d_KL: KL divergence between LLM and probe predictions
- Random baseline: KL-RP trained on randomly permuted embeddings

**Models:** Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (32 layers, 4096-dim) and Gemma-2-2B-IT (26 layers, 2304-dim), both with 8-bit quantization.

**Datasets:** LANG (9542 samples, 20 languages), TENSE (10K samples, 3 classes), SUBJ (8093 samples, multilingual), TRUTH (9123 samples, binary balanced).

## Results & Highlights

- **Q1 (Does relational linearity exist?):** Yes, but it varies significantly by model and relation. Llama-3.1 shows strong relational linearity for LANG (F1=0.98, d_KL=0.06), TENSE (F1=0.89, d_KL=0.02), and TRUTH (F1=0.86, d_KL=0.04). Gemma-2 shows weaker effects, especially for LANG (F1=0.51, d_KL=2.19).
- **KL-RP vs LRE:** KL-RP substantially outperforms LRE across most settings. For example, on LANG with Llama-3.1: KL-RP achieves F1(GT)=0.98 vs LRE's 0.06. LRE also shows sensitivity to hyperparameter choices (alpha, beta), leading to inconsistent results.
- **Q2 (Layer-wise dynamics):** Surface features (LANG, TENSE) are decodable from early layers. Abstract features (TRUTH) peak in middle layers (around layer 16 for Llama-3.1). Last layers often show slight degradation in KL divergence.
- **Q3 (Paraphrase effects):** Changing query language for LANG slightly impacts results. For TRUTH paraphrases (True/False vs Valid/Invalid vs Yes/No), Llama-3.1 is robust while Gemma-2 shows substantial variation, especially in earlier layers. This suggests Gemma-2 encodes relational linearity more abstractly in middle layers.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate):** The KL-RP probe is a reasonable but incremental methodological contribution -- it is essentially a linear probe trained with KL loss rather than cross-entropy. The primary value is in the systematic empirical evaluation rather than a new technique. The finding that LRE is unreliable is practically useful.
- **Rigor (Good):** Multiple evaluation metrics (F1-GT, F1-LLM, d_KL), random baselines, sanity checks (collapse-on-simplex score), and cross-model comparison. However, only two models are tested (both relatively small by current standards), and both use 8-bit quantization which could affect representations.
- **Reproducibility (Good):** Models are publicly available, datasets are specified, and the method is clearly described. Training details (Adam, lr=1e-4) are provided. Prompts are in appendices.
- **Significance (Moderate):** The layer-wise findings largely confirm what was already known from probing literature (e.g., Tenney et al., 2019; Cheng et al., 2025). The main new insight is the model-dependent variation in relational linearity and the unreliability of LRE.
- **Red flags:** (1) Only two relatively small models tested -- unclear how findings transfer to larger models. (2) The variation from Marconato et al.'s original formulation (using system prompts with q~s~t instead of s~q) is acknowledged as a limitation. (3) The scope is limited to relations with well-defined discrete answers, not open-ended relational knowledge.

## Strengths

- Comprehensive experimental setup with four diverse datasets spanning syntactic and semantic properties
- KL-RP is a clean, principled probing method that avoids the Jacobian approximation issues of LRE
- Clear evidence that LRE is unreliable and hyperparameter-sensitive, which is valuable for the community
- Layer-wise analysis connects well to prior interpretability literature
- Honest discussion of limitations, including the variation from the original formulation and dataset restrictions

## Limitations

- Only two models tested (Llama-3.1-8B and Gemma-2-2B); findings may not generalize to larger or differently-trained models
- 8-bit quantization may alter internal representations compared to full-precision models
- Restricted to relations with well-defined, discrete answer sets -- does not address open-ended relational knowledge (e.g., "plays" -> "guitar" vs "trumpet")
- The original formulation from Marconato et al. (2025) is tested with a modified prompt structure, which the authors acknowledge may not hold for the exact original formulation
- No investigation of how model scale, distillation, or training data affects relational linearity
- The SUBJ dataset has a high triviality score (0.91), making it a weak test case

## Relevance

本文对语言模型内部表示的线性结构进行了系统实验验证，对理解LLM如何编码关系知识有直接参考价值。其基于KL散度的探测方法和对层级动态规律的分析，可为mechanistic interpretability研究和activation steering应用提供方法论基础。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does relational linearity scale with model size (e.g., Llama-3.1-70B vs 8B)? Does distillation alter these patterns?
- Can the linear maps discovered by KL-RP be used for effective activation steering or knowledge editing in downstream applications?
- Does relational linearity hold for open-ended relations where multiple valid answers exist (e.g., "Miles Davis plays ___")?
- How do these findings transfer to decoder-only models with significantly different architectures (e.g., mixture-of-experts models)?
- What happens to relational linearity when models are trained with RLHF or other alignment procedures -- does alignment preserve or disrupt linear structure?
