Cross-Species RSA Reveals Conserved Early Visual Alignment but Divergent Higher-Area Rankings Across Human fMRI and Macaque Electrophysiology

TL;DR
通过RSA方法比较五种学习规则（BP、FA、PC、STDP及随机基线）在猕猴电生理数据与人类fMRI中的脑对齐表现，发现早期视觉区域的对齐模式跨物种保守（STDP和PC在两物种V1区均表现最优），但高级视觉区域（IT）的学习规则排序在物种间无相关性，且预训练ResNet-50在IT区的对齐（rho=0.25）远超所有自定义CNN（rho=0.07-0.14），表明模型容量和训练数据在高级区域占主导地位。

Relevance
这项研究通过跨物种比较验证了"局部学习规则（STDP、PC）在早期视觉皮层比反向传播更符合大脑"这一假说，对计算神经科学中生物plausible学习规则的研究提供了重要的跨物种实证支持。如果你关注生物 plausible 学习规则与深度学习的关系、Brain-Score 评估框架、或 RSA 方法论在跨物种比较中的应用，这篇论文值得深入阅读。其核心启示——模型容量和训练数据在高级视觉区域比学习规则更重要——对理解DNN与大脑的对齐机制具有指导意义。
Methodology
Models: All five conditions use the identical architecture and weights from the companion study (Leutenegger, 2026, arXiv:2604.16875): a custom 3-layer CNN (Conv1: 32 filters, Conv2: 64, Conv3: 128, FC1: 512, FC2: 10) trained on CIFAR-10 for 40 epochs. The five learning rules are: (1) Backpropagation (BP), (2) Feedback Alignment (FA; Lillicrap et al. 2016), (3) Predictive Coding (PC; Whittington and Bogacz 2017), (4) STDP (Bi and Poo, 1998), and (5) Random Weights (untrained). Results averaged across 5 random seeds. A pretrained ResNet-50 (ImageNet-1K-V2 weights) serves as a capacity control with layer mapping: layer1->V1/V2, layer2->V4, layer4->IT. Key Contributions

  1. First systematic cross-species RSA comparison of four biologically plausible learning rules plus an untrained baseline, using identical model weights (3-layer CNN trained on CIFAR-10) across both human fMRI and macaque electrophysiology
  2. Demonstrates conserved early visual alignment: local learning rules (STDP, PC) consistently outperform BP at V1/V2 in both species, with STDP achieving the highest macaque V1 alignment overall (rho~0.30)
  3. Shows that higher-area (IT) learning rule rankings have zero cross-species correlation (Kendall's tau=0.00, p=1.00), with a pretrained ResNet-50 (rho=0.25) dramatically outperforming all custom CNNs (rho=0.07-0.14), indicating model capacity and training data richness dominate over learning rule at IT
  4. Provides rigorous statistical framework: noise ceilings via Spearman-Brown corrected split-half (range 0.52-0.79), bootstrap 95% CIs (N=10,000 resamples), 5-seed variability, species x learning rule interaction effects, and a stimulus-control analysis showing IT ranking instability across stimulus domains (tau=-0.40)
  5. Identifies that the V2-to-V4 alignment drop in macaques confounds cortical hierarchy changes with a dataset switch (FreemanZiemba2013 textures vs. MajajHong2015 objects), a key interpretive caveat

Critical Assessment

Point 1
The n=5 learning rules makes rank-order analysis essentially descriptive (only tau=+/-1.0 detectable). The paper is honest about this, but it means the core cross-species ranking comparison is underpowered by design.
Point 2
The V2-to-V4 alignment drop is completely confounded by the dataset switch (different stimuli, recording methods, number of presentations). The paper acknowledges this but the cortical hierarchy profile in Figure 1 (right panel) could be misleading to casual readers.
Point 3
The custom CNN is very small (3 conv layers, CIFAR-10, 10 classes). The ResNet-50 comparison changes architecture, training data size, AND pretraining regime simultaneously, making it impossible to isolate which factor drives the IT improvement.
Point 4
The stimulus-control analysis shows IT rankings are weakly inverted across stimulus sets (tau=-0.40), undermining confidence in the IT cross-species comparison.
Point 5
Noise ceiling splitting neurons (not trials) may overestimate the ceiling, making the "substantial room for improvement" conclusion somewhat unreliable.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths
  1. Rigorous statistical framework: noise ceilings, bootstrap CIs (N=10,000), 5-seed variability, explicit power analysis, exhaustive permutation tests
  2. Honest and transparent handling of null results and limitations -- sets a good standard for the field
  3. Direct reuse of identical model weights from the human study eliminates a major confound (training variability)
  4. The stimulus-control analysis is a valuable methodological contribution, explicitly addressing a confound that many cross-species studies ignore
  5. Clear, focused research question with well-defined methodology and layer-to-region mapping
  6. The ResNet-50 capacity control provides important context for interpreting the main results
Limitations
  1. Only 5 learning rules compared, making all rank-order analyses descriptive rather than inferential -- the core cross-species question cannot be answered with adequate statistical power
  2. Stimulus sets fundamentally differ between species (THINGS objects, FreemanZiemba textures, HVM objects), confounding species differences with stimulus domain effects
  3. The custom CNN (3-layer, CIFAR-10, 10 classes) is far too small to capture IT-level representations; the ResNet-50 comparison confounds architecture depth, training data scale, and pretraining regime
  4. Human fMRI (millions of neurons, hemodynamic signal) vs. macaque electrophysiology (hundreds of neurons, spiking activity) are fundamentally different measurement modalities, making direct rho comparisons across species difficult to interpret
  5. The V2-to-V4 cortical profile conflates a genuine hierarchy change with a dataset switch -- this is acknowledged but cannot be resolved with existing data
  6. Noise ceiling estimation splits neurons rather than trials, which may overestimate reliability for electrophysiology data
  7. Human data from only 3 subjects (with sub-03 showing weak signal); macaque data from a small number of animals

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Would using matched stimulus sets across species (e.g., the same images presented to both humans and macaques) reveal genuine cross-species rank correlations at IT?
  2. How much of the pretrained ResNet-50 advantage at IT comes from architecture depth vs. training data scale vs. ImageNet's object-centric bias? A factorial design (same architecture, different training data) would be informative.
  3. Could a deeper custom CNN trained with STDP or PC close the gap with the pretrained ResNet-50 at IT, or is the training data the binding constraint?
  4. What happens to cross-species alignment when using calcium imaging (intermediate spatial/temporal resolution between fMRI and electrophysiology)?
  5. Are there intermediate visual areas (V4) where cross-species rank correlations are detectable with more learning rule conditions (n>5)?
  6. Does the noise ceiling estimation method (splitting neurons vs. splitting trials) meaningfully affect the "room for improvement" interpretation?
  7. Would the early-visual conservation result hold with more diverse architectures (transformers, state-space models) rather than only CNNs?
