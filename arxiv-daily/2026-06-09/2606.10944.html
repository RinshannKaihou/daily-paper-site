arXiv 2606.10944 Efficient Attention cs.LG 2026-06-09 Preprint

Convert any non-causal attention approximation into a streaming causal one — with matching guarantees.

Express Language Modeling

Albert Gong · Annabelle M. Carrell · Raaz Dwivedi · Lester Mackey  —  Cornell Tech · Cambridge · Microsoft Research

Key metric
82×
speedup over FlashAttention 2 at 512K tokens on ChatGLM2-6B-32K prefill.
Why this matters

Provides formal theoretical guarantees for causal attention approximation and frontier-level efficiency across the full long-context language modeling pipeline.

Critical edge

Validated on 6B–8B models only; scalability to 70B+ frontier models is untested, and no code release is mentioned.

Methodology

Three building blocks, one streaming cache. Express as an updatable weighted cache, run for the key–value attention kernel κatt.

  • HALVE — the offline sub-Gaussian halving algorithm (e.g. kernel halving KH(δ)) that turns nin points into a weighted coreset of ⌊nin/2⌋.
  • COMPRESS₂ — generalizes COMPRESS: applies HALVE to small groups of geometrically increasing size, cutting thinning time while preserving quality.
  • SUBSAMPLE — stratified uniform subsampling from 2mnout down to 2m̂nout (m̂ = min(m, m̄)) as the preprocessing step.
Phase 01
Exact
First nout points stored verbatim into the cache.
Phase 02
Thin
Batch incoming points into groups of 2m̄nout, SUBSAMPLE + COMPRESS₂ down to nout, merge into cache.
Phase 03
Halve
When cache hits 4 nout, apply HALVE twice back to nout and increment m̄.

Theoretical guarantee (Thinformer Express, bounded inputs): error ≤ log3/2(n)/s with O(s) memory and O(s² log(s) log(n/s)) compression overhead.

Strengths

Principled, broad, consistent.

  • Theoretical backbone. Explicit approximation guarantees with sub-Gaussian thinning theory.
  • One meta-procedure, four scenarios. Same Express cache covers prefill, KV compression, memory- and compute-bounded decoding.
  • Dominates strong baselines. Beats HyperAttention, SnapKV, StreamingLLM, ExpectedAttention, KeyDiff, Knorm on accuracy-efficiency curves.
  • Real engineering. I/O-aware Triton kernel inspired by FlashAttention 2.
  • Compelling headline. 82× at 512K tokens with improved perplexity.
Limitations

Open scope, untested scale.

  • Model scale. Validated on 6B–8B; 70B+ frontier models remain to be demonstrated.
  • Perplexity "improvement". Compression may act as a beneficial bias, not just approximation — needs more careful analysis.
  • System complexity. Express interacts with KV cache methods (SnapKV, StreamingLLM, PyramidKV) — composition adds tuning burden.
  • Hyperparameter sensitivity. Inflation factor m tuned ad hoc (m = h−2 for prefill, m = h for decoding) with no systematic study.
  • No code release. Reproducibility hinges on detailed appendix, not source.
  • Bounds assume bounded inputs (vmax); may be loose in practice.
Questions for further exploration

Where the compression story might bend. Open directions worth chasing next.

  • How does Thinformer Express interact with KV-cache quantization (e.g. 4-bit)? Does thinning complement or conflict with numerical precision reduction?
  • What happens to approximation quality under strong-locality attention patterns (e.g. code) vs. global patterns (e.g. RAG)?
  • Can Express be combined with Mixture-of-Experts where only a subset of experts fire per token?
  • How does approximation error accumulate across layers of a deep transformer — is there depth-dependent degradation?
  • Would kernel-thinning benefit from task-specific kernels beyond κatt?
