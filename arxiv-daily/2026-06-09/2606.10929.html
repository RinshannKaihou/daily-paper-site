Linear structures in trained networks are evolving local geometries, not global task directions.

Recoverable but Not Stationary: Local Linear Structures in Weights and Activations

Irina Piontkovskaia·Sergey Nikolenko

LoRA Task vectors Activation steering Linear structures Interpretability Random search
Key Metric

77%

of LoRA recovery displacement captured by trajectory-prefix bases from early recovery updates — not by a single static task direction.

Why This Matters

It challenges the core assumption behind task-vector arithmetic, LoRA merging, and activation steering — these methods all act as if linear structures are static global directions.

Critical Edge

Experiments cap at GPT-2 / Qwen-0.5B and the activation-shift cosine similarity is only 0.58 — generalization to modern LLMs and the strength of the parameter↔activation bridge remain unproven.

⚠ ABSTRACT-ONLY SUMMARY — full PDF not yet processed

Methodology

01 / Approach
  • Synthetic multitask Transformer experiments probing local low-rank task-gradient structure.
  • LoRA adapter experiments on DistilGPT-2 and GPT-2 to study recovery directions and basis drift.
  • Theoretical analysis: a Gaussian local-linear theorem for random search in high dimensions.
  • Activation steering: compare single gradient-step activation shifts against labeled-contrast CAA vectors.
  • Validation: steering effect checked on Qwen-0.5B with BoolQ statements.

Strengths

03a
  • Challenges an implicit assumption in the field — task directions as fixed global entities.
  • Combines synthetic + real LLM + theory in one coherent investigation.
  • Quantitative, testable claims: 77% / 0.58 / 100 steps.
  • Bridges parameter-space (LoRA, task vectors) and activation-space (steering) methods.
  • New conceptual frame: evolving local geometries.

Limitations

03b
  • Model scale limited to GPT-2 family + Qwen-0.5B — 7B+ behavior unverified.
  • Theory assumes Gaussian structure; real weight distributions are not.
  • 0.58 cosine is moderate — parameter↔activation link is suggestive, not decisive.
  • Abstract does not mention code release.
  • Single-task vs. multitask dynamics not fully disentangled.

Questions for Further Exploration

04 / Open
  1. Do the local linear structures and basis drift hold at 7B / 70B scale, or does geometry consolidate with size?
  2. How does the 77% trajectory-prefix capture rate scale with model size and task complexity?
  3. Can the Gaussian local-linear theorem be extended to non-Gaussian weight distributions found in trained networks?
  4. What are the implications for task-vector arithmetic and LoRA merging that assume composable global directions?
  5. Does the 0.58 cosine between parameter perturbation and activation steering imply two distinct control mechanisms, or a stronger relationship under specific conditions?
  6. How does basis drift speed vary across layers, attention heads, and architectures?
