> **一句话总结：** 基于韩国高考数学十年数据（664题，339题附带全国数十万考生逐题官方错误率）构建KCSAT-ML基准，提出与准确率正交的DRG指标，揭示test-time scaling在极难题上反缩放、简单题上过度思考，且同精度模型的难度对齐可截然相反。

> **TL;DR:** Introduces KCSAT-ML, a decade of Korean CSAT math problems (664 items, 339 with nationwide per-item error rates from hundreds of thousands of examinees), paired with the DRG metric (r=+0.34 with accuracy), revealing that test-time scaling is difficulty-asymmetric — anti-scaling on the hardest items, overthinking on easier ones — and models with identical accuracy can have opposite difficulty-alignment profiles.

## 核心贡献

- **KCSAT-ML基准**：覆盖2014-2025年韩国高考(Suneung)数学共664道题，其中339题附带全国数十万考生的官方逐题错误率，提供行为层面的ground-truth难度信号
- **DRG指标(Difficulty-aligned Reasoning Gain)**：与准确率近乎正交(Pearson r=+0.34)，衡量TTS带来的改善是集中在人类难题(Hard bin)还是简单题(Easy bin)，在首次开启TTS时即锁定(r=+0.94跨预算档位)
- **三大实验发现**：(1) 低预算解码在高人类错误率尾部的准确率全面崩塌；(2) TTS的token消耗与题目难度近似线性(r=0.59)，但准确率增益呈非单调曲线，在中等难度处达到峰值；(3) 同一模型家族内TTS在Hard题上反缩放、Easy题上过度思考，是同一对齐失败的两面
- **三阶段能力分区**：TTS将模型分为no-reasoning(错误与难度无关)→human-aligned(斜率≈1)→below-cohort(错误率低于人类)三个阶段

## Key Contributions

- **KCSAT-ML benchmark**: 664 math problems from 2014–2025 Korean CSAT exams; 339-item core set with official per-item error rates from nationwide cohorts of hundreds of thousands of examinees
- **Difficulty-aligned Reasoning Gain (DRG)**: Score-orthogonal metric (r=+0.34 with accuracy) that decomposes TTS gains by difficulty tier; locks in at first TTS turn-on (r=+0.94 between lowest and highest budget)
- **Three core findings**: (i) low-budget accuracy collapses on high-human-error tail at every model size; (ii) TTS token use grows linearly with cohort error rate (r=0.59) but accuracy gain is non-monotonic, peaking at moderate difficulty; (iii) within one family, TTS anti-scales on Hard items and overthinks on Easy items
- **Three capability regimes**: TTS partitions models into no-reasoning → human-aligned (slope ≈ 1) → below-cohort regimes; the same TTS turn-on drives opposite transitions for weak vs. frontier models

## Methodology

- **Dataset construction**: Curated all Korean CSAT mathematics problems from 2014–2025 (664 total); the 339-item core set with official nationwide cohort error rates was reformulated from multiple-choice to short-answer format to reduce guessing
- **Evaluation pipeline**: VLMs evaluated directly on original Korean exam images; LLMs evaluated via OCR-extracted text. Answer equivalence judged by LLM (GPT-5) to handle symbolic expressions and formatting variation; judge is blind to model identity and TTS configuration
- **Difficulty tiers**: Easy (0–50% cohort error, 148 items), Medium (51–75%, 113 items), Hard (76–100%, 78 items)
- **Test-time scaling (TTS)**: Compared wo. TTS (force-non-think, answer-only prompt) vs. w. TTS (open-ended reasoning prompt) across 22 model families spanning open-weight VLMs (Qwen3-VL series, HyperCLOVA-X-Seed, Kanana, GPT-OSS), open-weight LLMs, and closed-source APIs (GPT-5/GPT-5.1, Claude Sonnet/Opus 4.5, Gemini 2.5/3)
- **DRG metric**: For a model with both wo. TTS and w. TTS runs, DRG = (SCF_Hard_off − SCF_Hard_on) − (SCF_Easy_on − SCF_Easy_off), where SCF (sub-cohort fraction) measures how often the model underperforms the human cohort within a bin. Positive DRG means TTS preferentially closes the Hard-bin gap
- **Regression analysis**: Regressed per-item model error rate on cohort error rate separately for wo. TTS and w. TTS, extracting slope (failure–difficulty alignment) and intercept (offset from human parity)

## Results & Highlights

- **Collapse–recovery pattern**: Most models collapse to single-digit accuracy under low decoding budgets and recover to 60–99% once TTS is enabled (e.g., HyperCLOVA-X-Seed-5 recovers from ~1% to 87%)
- **Cohort error rate >> examiner points**: Cohort error rate predicts per-item model accuracy with nearly 2× the explanatory power of examiner-assigned point values (R² = 0.27 vs. 0.15 under wo. TTS)
- **TTS cost–accuracy disconnect**: TTS raises token use linearly with difficulty (r=0.59, ~102 tokens per pp of cohort error), but accuracy gain is non-monotonic — peaking at moderate difficulty and declining on the hardest items
- **Anti-scaling**: On the 23 items with cohort error ≥ 93%, a 4× smaller model with TTS (Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking) solves 9/23 while the larger sibling without TTS (Qwen3-VL-32B-Instruct) solves 0/23 — inference-time compute, not parameter count, drives the improvement
- **Overthinking**: On easier items (<60% cohort error), TTS degrades accuracy in 2–5% of items across three distinct model families; e.g., GPT-OSS-120B answers correctly in 576 tokens wo. TTS but fails in all 5 trials at 3,000–4,000 tokens w. TTS
- **Frontier models approach/surpass human performance**: Gemini-3-Pro-Preview reaches 99.4% accuracy (slope 0.01, intercept −3.8), firmly below-cohort; GPT-5.1 reaches 93.2%; Claude-Opus-4.5 reaches 87.6%
- **DRG reveals hidden differences**: Claude Sonnet-4 (DRG = −4.0) vs. Sonnet-4.5 (DRG = +24.3) — comparable accuracy, opposite reasoning targets; the DRG–accuracy correlation is only r = +0.34
- **Robustness**: 5-run repeated decoding shows median Std = 0.014 across 76 configurations; Hard-tier model rankings are stable even at k=10 folds (r = 0.975)

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The key insight is not just another math benchmark but pairing per-item human difficulty signals with a score-orthogonal metric. The DRG formulation is elegant and the three-regime framing is a genuine conceptual contribution.
- **Rigor**: Very strong. Nationwide cohort error rates, 5-run repeated decoding stability checks, k-fold subset stability on Hard tier, joint OLS regression to show cohort signal carries independent information — unusually thorough for a benchmark paper.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Open-source code/dataset builder promised. Exam problems are public; structured metadata with bounding boxes enables image reconstruction. The LLM-based equivalence judge introduces minor but bounded noise (mitigated by blinding).
- **Significance**: High impact for the reasoning evaluation community. The finding that accuracy-identical models can have opposite DRG values fundamentally challenges how we compare reasoning models. The anti-scaling/overthinking duality has practical implications for test-time compute allocation.
- **Red flags**: (1) The 78-item Hard tier is small — though robustness checks partially address this. (2) TTS is not an identical intervention across model families (some change checkpoint, others only budget), so strictly causal same-checkpoint conclusions are limited to families where ablations are available. (3) Data contamination cannot be fully ruled out since KCSAT items are public, though the paper provides reasonable evidence against simple surface-form memorization. (4) The paper evaluates on a single exam style (Korean CSAT); generalization to other math cultures/formats is untested.

## Strengths

- Unique per-item human difficulty signal grounded in actual nationwide exam performance (not heuristic ratings)
- DRG metric is principled (score-orthogonal, locks in at first TTS turn-on) and reveals what accuracy cannot
- Three-regime framework provides a clean conceptual lens for understanding TTS behavior across capability levels
- Extensive robustness analyses (repeated decoding, k-fold stability, positional/temporal contamination checks)
- Evaluates both VLMs (on original Korean images) and LLMs (via OCR), covering both modalities
- Practical cost–accuracy analysis with dollar-per-correct-answer estimates

## Limitations

- Restricted to Korean CSAT math format; problem style (short-answer, Korean notation, specific topic distribution) may not generalize to other mathematical domains or languages
- 339-item core set (78 Hard items) is a finite benchmark; susceptibility to overfitting increases as models are iterated on this specific test
- TTS is heterogeneous across model families — some change checkpoints, others change only inference budget — limiting strictly causal claims
- LLM-judged answer equivalence introduces bounded but nonzero noise
- Does not deeply analyze *why* anti-scaling occurs (e.g., is it reward hacking, distribution shift, or fundamental reasoning limitations?)
- The paper does not explore remediation: how to train or decode to improve DRG

## Relevance

该工作提供了首个带有人类全国队列难度校准的数学推理基准，揭示了当前推理模型test-time scaling的对齐缺陷——反缩放和过度思考是同一问题的两面。DRG指标为评估推理质量提供了超越准确率的新维度，对理解和改进reasoning model的推理行为有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can adaptive TTS strategies (e.g., budget proportional to estimated item difficulty) mitigate both anti-scaling and overthinking simultaneously?
- Does the DRG framework extend to non-math domains (code generation, logical reasoning) where human difficulty signals are available?
- Is the anti-scaling phenomenon on hard items a failure of exploration (not searching enough) or exploitation (getting trapped in wrong solution paths)?
- How does DRG relate to item response theory (IRT) difficulty parameters — are the insights redundant or complementary?
- Could curriculum training that progressively increases difficulty improve DRG without sacrificing accuracy?
- What happens to DRG when models are specifically fine-tuned on the KCSAT-ML distribution — does alignment improve or do new pathologies emerge?
