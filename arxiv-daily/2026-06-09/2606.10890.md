> **一句话总结：** 本文提出PiSO（Piecewise Scale Optimization）算法，利用校准数据在round-to-nearest量化下精确且高效地计算逐通道权重量化的最优缩放因子——通过将scale搜索空间划分为有限区间，每段目标有闭式最优解；在Llama-3和Qwen-2.5上2/3/4-bit量化中持续改善WikiText-2困惑度与零样本准确率，且优势随位宽变窄而扩大，配合误差校正（GPTQ/Qronos）效果更佳。

> **TL;DR:** PiSO exactly computes optimal channel-wise weight scales under round-to-nearest (RTN) quantization by partitioning the scale search space into finitely many intervals, each admitting a closed-form minimizer of the data-aware layer-reconstruction objective. On Llama-3 (1B/3B/8B) and Qwen-2.5 Instruct across 2/3/4-bit weight-only quantization, PiSO consistently beats absmax, data-free grid-search, and Beacon on WikiText-2 perplexity and zero-shot accuracy (ARC-Easy/Challenge, HellaSwag), with gains widening at lower bit-widths. At 3-bit Llama-3 8B, Qronos⊙PiSO reaches WikiText-2 7.01 perplexity and 61.4% accuracy vs. Qronos+absmax's 8.27 / 57.9%. RTN+PiSO also reaches near-asymptotic perplexity from a single 2048-token calibration sequence.

## 核心贡献

- **精确逐通道scale求解器PiSO**：证明在RTN量化下，数据感知的逐通道层输出重构目标的非凸误差可被精确最小化——关键观察是q(w;s)在s上分段常数，故实数轴可划分为有限区间，每段误差有闭式最小化，扫掠即得全局最优；复杂度O(D²|G|)每通道。
- **群组量化的两种原则性近似**：将PiSO扩展到group-wise量化，独立启发式（忽略组间交互）和顺序启发式（捕捉跨组交互通过激活）；2-bit下顺序启发式比独立的好一个数量级。
- **与误差校正的三种交错策略**：将PiSO与GPTQ/Qronos集成，解耦（+）和交错（⊙）两种方式，以及层级（*）/组级（†）集成；最紧密耦合GPTQ⊙PiSO†在2-bit群组量化上取得最佳。
- **位宽越窄收益越大**：从4-bit到2-bit，PiSO相对absmax的优势随量化难度上升而扩大。
- **校准数据效率提升**：RTN+PiSO仅需单个2048-token校准样本即恢复近渐近困惑度与准确率，而GPTQ+absmax需要数十个样本。

## Key Contributions

- **Exact channel-wise scale solver PiSO**: Proves that the non-convex data-aware layer-reconstruction error under RTN quantization can be minimized exactly per channel. The key insight is that the unscaled grid assignment q(w;s) is piecewise constant in s with closed-form breakpoints (at most |G|−1 transition scales per weight), so the real line partitions into finitely many intervals each with a closed-form quadratic minimizer; sweeping yields the global optimum in O(D²|G|) per channel.
- **Two principled group-wise approximations**: Extends PiSO beyond channel-wise — an independent heuristic (ignores cross-group interactions) and a sequential heuristic (captures cross-group interactions through activations); the sequential heuristic outperforms the independent one by an order of magnitude at 2-bit.
- **Three integration strategies with error correction**: Decoupled (+) and interleaved (⊙) compositions of PiSO with GPTQ/Qronos, plus layer-wise (*) and group-wise (†) integration variants; the tightest coupling GPTQ⊙PiSO† achieves the best 2-bit group-wise perplexities.
- **Bit-width-narrowing gains**: The absmax-vs-PiSO gap widens as the target bit-width narrows and quantization becomes harder.
- **Calibration-data efficiency**: RTN+PiSO recovers near-asymptotic perplexity/accuracy from a single 2048-token calibration sequence, while GPTQ+absmax requires tens of samples; GPTQ⊙PiSO is also more data-efficient than GPTQ+absmax.

## Methodology

**Problem.** Weight-only PTQ maps each weight onto a quantization grid G={g₁<…<g_L} via a symmetric quantizer Q(x;s)=s·⌊x/s⌉_G. The scale s is shared across multiple weights: channel-wise (one scale per output channel) or group-wise (each channel partitioned into groups of contiguous weights, each with an independent scale). The objective is the data-aware layer-output reconstruction error E(s)=‖XW − X̃Q(W;s)‖²_F, where X is the layer input activation and X̃ is the (partially) quantized model's activation.

**Channel-wise separability.** E(s) separates into M independent channel-wise problems E(s)=Σ_j E_j(s_j), so each s_j is optimized independently for weight vector w∈ℝ^D.

**Closed-form optimum for fixed grid assignment.** For fixed q, E(s)=‖Xw − sX̃q‖² is convex in s with minimizer s*(q)=α(q)/β(q), where α(q)=q^⊤G̃w, β(q)=q^⊤H̃q, H̃=X̃^⊤X̃, G̃=X̃^⊤X.

**Piecewise-constant structure (key insight).** q(w;s) is piecewise constant in s with closed-form breakpoints: for a positive scalar weight w_i, as s decreases from +∞, the assignment q_i walks through G, jumping from g_ℓ to g_{ℓ+1} exactly when w_i/s crosses their midpoint. This yields at most |G|−1 transition scales per weight. Sweeping these intervals with efficient incremental updates yields the global optimal scale for each channel. The objective accommodates cross-activation reconstruction (X≠X̃, as in Qronos/GPTAQ), self-activation reconstruction (X=X̃, as in GPTQ), or data-free reconstruction (X=X̃=I).

**Group-wise extension.** Direct extension is intractable; two approximations: (1) independent — optimize each group's scale independently; (2) sequential — account for cross-group interactions through activations by processing groups in order.

**Integration with error correction.** Three strategies interleaving PiSO with GPTQ/Qronos: decoupled (GPTQ+PiSO, optimize scales once then run error correction) vs. interleaved (GPTQ⊙PiSO, re-optimize scales during error correction), at layer-wise (*) or group-wise (†) granularity.

**Experimental setup.** Llama-3 (1B/3B/8B) and Qwen-2.5 Instruct, HuggingFace instruction-tuned checkpoints. Calibration: 128 sequences of 2048 tokens from WikiText-2 training split. Weight-only quantization to 2/3/4-bit; scales stored in BF16. Metrics: WikiText-2 validation perplexity; zero-shot accuracy on ARC-Easy, ARC-Challenge, HellaSwag (LightEval). Baselines: absmax, data-free (activation-independent variant of PiSO = exact version of grid-search heuristic, not restricted to [0, s_absmax]), Beacon (joint scale+assignment coordinate descent).

## Results & Highlights

- **3-bit Llama-3 8B (channel-wise)**: Qronos⊙PiSO reaches WikiText-2 perplexity 7.01 and 61.4% avg zero-shot accuracy vs. Qronos+absmax 8.27 / 57.9%; GPTQ⊙PiSO 7.41 / 62.6% vs. GPTQ+absmax 9.23 / 55.1%. BF16 baseline is 5.89 / 68.6%.
- **2-bit is catastrophic for RTN regardless of scale, but PiSO+error-correction rescues it**: e.g., on Qwen-2.5 8B at 2-bit, Qronos⊙PiSO improves over Qronos+absmax (paper reports consistent improvements; full 2-bit table in Table 1).
- **RTN+PiSO competitive with GPTQ+absmax at 3-bit**: At 3-bit, RTN+PiSO already produces competitive perplexities, even surpassing GPTQ+absmax for multiple model sizes — RTN+PiSO Llama-3 8B 3-bit WikiText-2 8.99 vs. GPTQ+absmax 9.23.
- **Group-wise 2-bit (Llama-3 3B, G32)**: GPTQ⊙PiSO† achieves 14.3 perplexity vs. GPTQ+absmax 30.4 (≈2× improvement); the sequential heuristic RTN+PiSO_S (19.4) outperforms independent RTN+PiSO_I (2e4) by an order of magnitude, confirming cross-group interaction matters.
- **Bit-width-narrowing gains widen**: At 4-bit Llama-3 8B channel-wise, RTN+PiSO 6.64 vs. RTN+absmax 7.83 (gap 1.19); the absmax-vs-PiSO gap is larger at 3-bit and largest at 2-bit.
- **Calibration-data efficiency**: RTN+PiSO on Llama-3.2-1B 3-bit reaches near-asymptotic perplexity/accuracy from a single 2048-token calibration sample; GPTQ+absmax requires tens of samples to stabilize; GPTQ⊙PiSO is also more data-efficient than GPTQ+absmax despite optimizing the same number of weight assignments plus scales.
- **Minimal runtime overhead**: Wall-clock runtimes and overhead with respect to GPTQ reported in Table 4 (Appendix D) show minimal overhead.

## Critical Assessment

- **The exact-optimality guarantee is, by construction, tied to the channel-wise RTN regime.** Both group-wise variants are approximations, and the interleaved integration with GPTQ/Qronos breaks the RTN assumption by design — so the headline theoretical guarantee does not carry over to the configurations that produce the best end-to-end numbers (GPTQ⊙PiSO†, Qronos⊙PiSO). The authors are explicit about this but it means the strongest empirical results rest on unprincipled approximations.
- **The data-free baseline is a strengthened strawman.** The authors' "data-free" baseline is a simplified activation-independent variant of PiSO that is "a stronger version of the grid-search heuristic ... not restricted to [0, s_absmax] and exact rather than discrete" — yet it yields substantially degraded results at 3/4-bit (deferred to Appendix D Table 10). This asymmetry (data-free PiSO weak, data-aware PiSO strong) is partly tautological since the whole contribution is using activations, so the baseline's weakness is the point being made.
- **Beacon comparison has reproducibility caveats.** The authors note none of CDQuant/COMQ/Beacon has publicly available code; Beacon is selected as "the most recent representative, noting that it is methodologically very close to COMQ." So the head-to-head Beacon numbers depend on the authors' own reimplementation, and COMQ (also channel-wise joint, very close to Beacon) is not separately benchmarked.
- **Group-wise error-correction comparison is restricted to GPTQ.** To keep results compact (two heuristics × three integration variants × two group sizes), the group-wise error-correction comparison drops Qronos — so the strongest channel-wise method (Qronos⊙PiSO) has no group-wise counterpart reported.
- **Hardware-format constraints out of scope.** The work uses unconstrained BF16 scales; real hardware formats (OCP MX, NVFP4) impose low-bit or hierarchical scale constraints that fall outside the framework, and asymmetric quantization is unhandled — a practical deployment gap.

## Strengths

- Genuine algorithmic contribution: an exact polynomial-time solver for a problem the field had treated only with grid-search heuristics, via the elegant piecewise-constant partition insight.
- Cleanly decomposes the contribution: channel-wise exact solver → group-wise approximations → error-correction integration strategies, each evaluated.
- Comprehensive evaluation: two model families (Llama-3, Qwen-2.5), three sizes (1B/3B/8B), three bit-widths (2/3/4), channel-wise and group-wise (G16/G32), standalone and combined with two error-correctors (GPTQ, Qronos).
- The calibration-data-efficiency finding (single 2048-token sample suffices for RTN+PiSO) is practically valuable and underexplored in the PTQ literature.
- Honest about the optimality boundary: explicitly states the guarantee is RTN-and-channel-wise only.

## Limitations

- Exact optimality does not extend to the group-wise variants or to interleaved error-correction integration (the RTN assumption is broken by design).
- Group-wise error-correction comparison restricted to GPTQ; Qronos group-wise not reported.
- Beacon/COMQ comparison depends on authors' reimplementation (no public code for either).
- Unconstrained BF16 scales; hardware formats (MX, NVFP4) with low-bit/hierarchical scales and asymmetric quantization out of scope.
- The deeper analysis of the data-efficiency phenomenon (why GPTQ⊙PiSO is more data-efficient than GPTQ+absmax) is deferred to future work.

## Relevance

本文直接映射到「Computation Reliability & Precision Errors」研究兴趣——精确求解权重量化的最优缩放因子，降低低比特量化引入的精度误差。同时也与「LLM Inference Evaluation」相关：通过改善量化scale选择，提升低比特LLM在WikiText-2困惑度与ARC/HellaSwag零样本准确率上的推理质量。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The exact-optimality guarantee breaks for the interleaved GPTQ⊙PiSO variants that produce the best end-to-end numbers — how sensitive is the empirical gain to the interleaving schedule, and is there a tighter theoretical bound on the approximation gap?
- The data-efficiency phenomenon (GPTQ⊙PiSO more data-efficient than GPTQ+absmax despite optimizing more parameters) is deferred to future work — what is the mechanism by which a well-calibrated grid reduces GPTQ's calibration dependence?
- Does PiSO's advantage hold under hardware-constrained scale formats (OCP MX, NVFP4) where scales are low-bit or hierarchical, which the authors flag as out of scope?
- The group-wise comparison drops Qronos for compactness — would Qronos⊙PiSO† beat GPTQ⊙PiSO† in the group-wise regime, as it does channel-wise?
- The data-free baseline (activation-independent PiSO) is a strengthened version of grid-search yet performs poorly at 3/4-bit — does this imply grid-search scale selection is dominated by activation-awareness rather than by the [0, s_absmax] cap or discrete granularity?
