How Does Reasoning Flow? Tracing Attention-Induced Information Flow for Targeted RL in LLMs

Zhichen Dong*, Yang Li*, Yuhan Sun, Weixun Wang, Yijia Luo, Zinian Peng, Taiheng Ye, Chao Yang, Wenbo Su, Yu Cheng, Bo Zheng, Junchi Yan  ·  SJTU · Alibaba Group · Shanghai AI Laboratory

reinforcement learning credit assignment attention analysis LLM reasoning token-level reward Doob-h reweighting

FlowTracer reweights attention flows to isolate answer-targeting tokens, improving RL-trained reasoning accuracy by 2–4% over GRPO.

Key Metric
+4.0%
Qwen3-8B · 1K context average accuracy: 43.4% vs GRPO's 39.4% across AIME24/25, AMC23, MATH500, and OlympiadBench.
Why This Matters

It gives RL-trained LLMs a structured way to credit individual tokens for final answers — solving credit dilution that hurts long-context reasoning.

Critical Edge

Structural delimiters (punctuation, newlines) dominate the high-flow signal — raising the question of whether the method captures reasoning or merely formatting scaffolding.

Methodology

  1. Raw Influence Graph. Build a time-ordered DAG over the generated sequence. Edge weight W_ik is the mean attention from token i to k, aggregated over middle layers (L/3 to 2L/3) and averaged across heads.
  2. Doob-h Reweighting. Define an answer region A (e.g. \boxed{} content) and a virtual sink. Compute reachability potential h(i) recursively, then reweight edges as W' = W · h(k)/h(i) — guaranteeing local flow conservation.
  3. Forward Propagation. Inject unit flow from a super-source on question tokens, propagate forward over W', and rank tokens by throughput τ(k). The top 40% become the reasoning backbone.
  4. RL Reward Shaping. Multiply GRPO's token-level loss by γ = 1.5 on high-flow tokens, γ = 1 elsewhere. One extra forward pass per step; no new parameters.

Results

Causal test — perturbing top-20% high-flow tokens causes 45.9% answer changes vs 29.5% for random perturbation. Training overhead: 2.2–4.5%.

Strengths

  • Principled graph-theoretic formulation with a formal flow-conservation proof (Theorem 3.1)
  • Low overhead — single extra forward pass per step (2.2–4.5%)
  • Causal validation that high-flow tokens truly drive reasoning outcomes
  • Scales to 8K context where credit dilution is most acute
  • Cross-architecture generalization across Qwen3 and Llama families

Limitations

  • Assumes a localized answer region; open-ended or tool-use trajectories unaddressed
  • Structural delimiters dominate the high-flow signal; semantic tokens add only marginal gain
  • Hard top-40% + γ=1.5 recipe is brittle; continuous reweighting underperforms
  • Outcome-only rewards cannot credit intermediate reasoning that leads to a wrong final answer
  • Noisy attention graphs at 16K+ contexts may degrade flow extraction quality

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does FlowTracer compose with process reward models (PRMs)? Could combining flow-based credit with dense process rewards yield further gains?
  2. Can the Doob-h reweighting run online during training instead of as a post-hoc analysis — and would online flow improve credit stability?
  3. What happens when the answer region is diffuse or multi-span — multi-step tool calls, agentic trajectories with intermediate verification?
  4. Is the dominance of structural delimiters in the high-flow set a feature (reliable routing hubs) or a limitation (missing semantic reasoning)?
  5. How does flow quality degrade in very long contexts (16K–128K), and could adaptive or hierarchical flow extraction mitigate this?
