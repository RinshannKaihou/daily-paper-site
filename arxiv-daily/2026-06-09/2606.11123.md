> **一句话总结：** 本文发现反馈对齐（FA）失败的根本原因是梯度有效秩的坍缩，通过引入Muon优化器（正交化权重更新）和隐藏层激活归一化来提升梯度维度，在CIFAR100/ResNet-18上取得了9个百分点的精度提升。

> **TL;DR:** Identifies low effective rank of FA gradients as the root cause of its failure in deep networks, and shows that orthogonalising weight updates (Muon optimizer) and normalising hidden activities consistently improve FA across architectures and benchmarks, yielding up to 9pp accuracy gains on CIFAR100 with ResNet-18.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了反馈对齐（FA）在深层网络中失败的核心机制：梯度的有效秩（effective rank）显著低于反向传播（BP），导致优化被困在低维子空间中
- 提出了两种缓解秩坍缩的方法：Muon优化器（通过正交化步骤提升更新维度）和隐藏层激活归一化（促进激活正交性）
- 在多个架构（CNN、ResNet-18、AlexNet）和数据集（CIFAR10、CIFAR100、STL-10、Tiny ImageNet）上验证了方法的有效性
- 两种方法联合使用可进一步改善性能，表明高维更新几何是扩展FA的关键方向

## Key Contributions

- Diagnoses FA failure through systematic analysis of gradient effective rank, gradient trajectory dimensionality, and feature dimensionality — all substantially lower in FA vs. BP
- Proposes Muon optimizer (momentum + orthogonalisation via Newton-Schulz) to increase the effective dimensionality of FA weight updates
- Proposes hidden activity normalization (BatchNorm-based) to promote activation orthogonality, indirectly increasing gradient rank
- Demonstrates consistent improvements: e.g., +9pp on CIFAR100/ResNet-18 over FA baseline, across multiple architectures and datasets
- Shows that both methods together yield additive benefits, supporting the hypothesis that high-dimensional update geometry is critical for FA scaling

## Methodology

The paper follows a diagnose-then-fix methodology:

**Diagnosis (Section 3):** The authors train CNNs with 1–4 convolutional layers (64 channels, 3×3 kernels) on CIFAR10 using both BP and FA, measuring weight alignment (cosine similarity of forward and feedback weights), gradient alignment (cosine similarity of FA gradient vs. BP gradient), gradient effective rank (exponentiated entropy of normalized singular values), gradient trajectory dimensionality (effective rank of Gram matrix of stacked gradients), and feature dimensionality (effective rank of layer activations).

Key finding: FA gradients have markedly lower effective rank than BP, and this rank collapses as training progresses. The gradient trajectory also becomes highly correlated across steps (high cosine similarity between consecutive gradients), explaining why FA cannot escape the low-dimensional subspace.

**Interventions (Section 4):**
1. **Muon optimizer:** Replaces standard SGD/AdamW update with M_t = βM_{t-1} + (1-β)∂E/∂W, then W_{t+1} = W_t − η·Orth(M_t), where Orth(X) = UV^⊤ via SVD (approximated by Newton-Schulz). This forces all singular values of the update to 1, preventing rank collapse.
2. **Hidden activity normalization:** Standard BatchNorm applied to hidden activations, which prior work showed increases activation dimensionality. Higher-dimensional activations lead to richer gradient signals.

Both methods are evaluated on CIFAR10 (4-layer CNN), STL-10 (AlexNet), CIFAR100 (ResNet-18), and Tiny ImageNet (ResNet-18), with SGD+momentum and AdamW baselines.

## Results & Highlights

- On CIFAR10 (4-layer CNN), Muon+FA achieves comparable accuracy to BP, closing most of the FA-BP gap
- On CIFAR100 with ResNet-18: FA baseline ~25% accuracy; Muon+FA ~34% (+9pp); combining with activity normalization yields further gains
- On STL-10 (AlexNet) and Tiny ImageNet (ResNet-18): consistent improvements over FA baselines with both methods
- The effective rank of gradients remains elevated throughout training with Muon/activity normalization, confirming the mechanism
- Weight alignment and gradient alignment both improve, validating the hypothesis that low-dimensional gradients blocked alignment
- Both methods together provide additive benefits, supporting the complementary nature of the interventions

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The rank-collapse diagnosis is new and well-supported, though the idea that FA struggles with depth is well-known. Applying Muon to FA is a natural but effective idea.
- **Rigor:** Good. The experimental methodology is thorough (multiple architectures, datasets, optimizers, and diagnostic metrics). However, the analysis is primarily empirical rather than theoretical.
- **Reproducibility:** High. Standard architectures (ResNet-18, AlexNet), common datasets, and well-described experimental settings. Muon is publicly available.
- **Significance:** Moderate. The paper improves FA performance but does not close the gap with BP on harder benchmarks. The maximum accuracy achieved on CIFAR100/ResNet-18 (~34%) is still far below BP (~70%+). The contribution is more diagnostic than practical.
- **Red flags:** Absolute performance on harder benchmarks remains low. The Muon optimizer introduces non-trivial computational overhead from the orthogonalisation step.

## Strengths

- Clear, systematic diagnostic approach: rank collapse is a compelling and well-supported explanation
- Two complementary interventions that validate the hypothesis from different angles
- Multiple architectures and datasets strengthen the generality of findings
- Good ablations: both methods separately and combined, with appropriate baselines
- The connection to Muon's orthogonalisation mechanism is insightful

## Limitations

- The FA-BP gap is not closed on harder benchmarks; absolute performance remains far from BP
- No theoretical analysis of why rank collapse occurs in FA specifically — purely empirical
- The Muon optimizer adds computational overhead (SVD or Newton-Schulz per update)
- Experiments limited to image classification; no evaluation on NLP or other domains
- Limited scalability evidence: no experiments on modern large-scale architectures

## Relevance

与生物启发的学习规则和优化方向相关：本文揭示了FA梯度低维性这一根本瓶颈，提出的Muon+FA方案为探索反向传播替代方案提供了新思路，对理解神经网络训练中的梯度几何具有启发意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the rank-collapse diagnosis apply to other biologically plausible learning rules (target propagation, equilibrium propagation)?
- Can Muon + activity normalization close the FA-BP gap on larger-scale tasks (ImageNet, language modeling)?
- What is the theoretical explanation for why FA gradients have lower effective rank than BP?
- How does the computational overhead of Muon compare to the benefits in wall-clock training time?
- Are there other interventions that could further increase gradient dimensionality in FA?
