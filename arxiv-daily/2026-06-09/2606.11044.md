> **一句话总结：** 本文将广义共形预测系统（GCPS）扩展到非可交换场景，通过观测特定的置换权重编码分布偏移，并引入"权重不确定性框"在权重需估计时构建有限样本或渐近置信保证的鲁棒CDF包络；在5维协变量偏移回归和AAV衣壳包装的反馈协变量偏移实验中，加权方法恢复了接近名义水平的90%预测区间覆盖率，而无权重方法仅为0.83–0.88。

> **TL;DR:** The authors extend generalized conformal predictive systems (GCPS) beyond exchangeability by encoding distributional shifts via observation-specific permutation weights, yielding shift-aware predictive envelopes that remain valid whenever the test point is a weighted draw from observed atoms. Because weights are usually estimated, they introduce weight-uncertainty boxes (coordinatewise multiplicative bounds) and construct robust CPS envelopes with finite-sample/asymptotic confidence guarantees (Theorem 2). On a synthetic 5D covariate-shift regression, unweighted CMCPS/CBIN/CIDR under-cover at 0.833/0.878/0.860 vs. nominal 0.90, while CI-based weighting reaches 0.953/0.963/0.971. On the AAV capsid packaging feedback-covariate-shift benchmark (7.55M training sequences, 10⁶ held-out), importance weighting restores near-nominal coverage across design inverse temperatures τ∈{1,…,7}.

## 核心贡献

- **置换权重扩展GCPS**：用观测特定的置换权重w_i(z_{1:n+1})编码非可交换性——条件于无序样本，测试点是观测原子的加权抽取；由此构建在任意分布偏移下保持校准的预测系统（定理1）。
- **权重不确定性框的鲁棒包络**：因权重通常需估计，引入逐坐标乘性边界L_i w_i ≤ w_i ≤ U_i w_i构成权重不确定性框W_WU，作为w的(1−ε)置信域；在W_WU上优化得鲁棒CDF带，提供有限样本或渐近置信保证（定理2）。
- **高效计算规则**：为conformity-measure CPS（CMCPS）、共形分箱（CBIN）和共形保序分布回归（CIDR）导出闭式包络与高效求值规则（命题1、命题2）。
- **反馈协变量偏移的实际应用**：在AAV衣壳包装的ML驱动生物分子设计场景（Fannjiang等2022的协议）验证，FCS感知加权可与共形分布校准器组合，获得校准的预测分布。

## Key Contributions

- **Permutation-weight extension of GCPS**: Encodes non-exchangeability via observation-specific permutation weights w_i(z_{1:n+1}) — conditional on the unordered multiset of atoms, the test point is a weighted draw from the n+1 observed atoms with probabilities w_i(Y_{n+1}). Building the predictive system with the weighted empirical law and inserting the test atom at its realized weight yields a calibrated predictive distribution under the true (possibly non-exchangeable) law (Theorem 1, with probabilistic/isotonic/auto-calibration variants).
- **Weight-uncertainty boxes for robust envelopes**: Since weights are usually estimated, introduces coordinatewise multiplicative bounds L_i w_i ≤ w_i ≤ U_i w_i defining W_WU as a (1−ε) confidence region for the true w. Optimizing the predictive system over W_WU yields robust CDF bands with finite-sample (Theorem 2a) or asymptotic (Theorem 2b) confidence that the envelope contains a calibrated predictive CDF.
- **Efficient closed-form evaluation**: Derives efficient computation and closed forms for conformity-measure CPS (CMCPS), conformal binning (CBIN), and conformal isotonic distributional regression (CIDR), under both known and uncertain weights (Propositions 1–2).
- **Feedback covariate shift in biomolecular design**: Validates on the AAV capsid packaging use case (Fannjiang et al. 2022 protocol), showing FCS-aware weighting combines with conformal distributional calibrators to produce well-calibrated predictive distributions under model-induced (feedback) shifts.

## Methodology

**Predictive system (Definition 1).** For a distributional regression procedure G mapping (μ, x, y)→G[μ,x](y) (a CDF in y), the predictive-system envelopes are Ψ[μ,x;c](y)=inf_{y′} G_{P_{μ+c·δ_{(x,y′)}}}(y) and Ψ^u[μ,x;c](y)=sup_{y′} G_{...}(y), where P=μ/μ(Z). Ψ[μ,x;c] is the set of CDFs whose graphs lie between the envelopes.

**Non-exchangeability via permutation weights (Eq. 2).** w_i(z_{1:n+1})∈[0,1] is the probability that Z_{n+1}=z_i conditional on the unordered multiset {Z_1,…,Z_{n+1}}={z_1,…,z_{n+1}}. Under exchangeability, w_i=1/(n+1). The weighted observed measure μ^obs_y′=Σ_{i=1}^n w_i(y′)δ_{Z_i} and full weighted measure μ̃_e=Σ_{i=1}^{n+1} w_i(Y_{n+1})δ_{Z_i}.

**Shift-aware construction (Theorem 1).** Set insertion weight c=w_{n+1}(Y_{n+1}) so μ^obs+w_{n+1}(Y_{n+1})δ_{(X_{n+1},Y_{n+1})}=μ̃_e. The bounds Ψ_l[X_{n+1}](y)=inf_{y′} G_{P_{μ^obs+w_{n+1}(y′)δ_{(X_{n+1},y′)}}}(y) and Ψ^u[X_{n+1}](y)=sup_{y′}… contain a calibrated predictive distribution under the true law. The instance for probabilistic calibration is a classical CPS, for isotonic calibration conformal IDR, for auto-calibration conformal binning.

**Weight-uncertainty box (Eq. 4).** W_WU={w∈ℝ^{n+1}: L_i w_i ≤ w_i ≤ U_i w_i ∀i}, a (1−ε) confidence region (finite-sample or asymptotic). The robust envelope optimizes the predictive system over W_WU.

**Synthetic experiment.** Covariate shift in regression with p=5 covariates, shift acts only through marginal P_X. Split setup: linear regression fit on independent training set, held fixed on calibration set. CMCPS uses linear regression residual as conformity score; CIDR uses fitted value ŷ(x) as single-index summary with IDR; CBIN constructs 10 clusters via k-NN then weighted empirical-CDF binning. Three weighting regimes: oracle weights (true w_i), plug-in odds weights (estimated w_i), CI-odds weights (weight bands). Metrics: CRPS (lower better), PIT dispersion (=1/12≈0.0833 under probabilistic calibration), marginal coverage of 90% prediction intervals.

**AAV biomolecular design.** Follows Fannjiang et al. 2022 protocol: AAV5 7-mer peptide-insertion library from NNK distribution, packaging enrichment scores. Hold out 10⁶ labeled sequences; train neural network predictor ψ on remaining 7,552,729. Designed test distributions P_{test,τ} indexed by inverse temperature τ∈{1,…,7} (exponential tilts of baseline NNK). Per τ: split 10⁶ into 990,000 proposal + n_cal=10,000 calibration; rejection-sample up to 100 test sequences over T=500 trials. Likelihood-ratio weights w_τ(x)=dP_{test,τ}/dP_train. CMCPS uses residual; CBIN uses k=5 quantile bins from calibration predicted values.

## Results & Highlights

- **Synthetic 5D covariate-shift — coverage recovery**: Unweighted CMCPS/CBIN/CIDR under-cover at 0.833/0.878/0.860 (90% nominal). Oracle weighting: 0.907/0.934/0.939. Estimated weighting: 0.913/0.933/0.937. CI-based weighting (most conservative): 0.953/0.963/0.971. (Means over Monte-Carlo replicates; [q₀.₁, q₀.₉] intervals reported.)
- **CRPS sharpness tradeoff**: For CMCPS, no-weighting CRPS 1.149 vs. oracle 1.142 vs. estimated 1.147 vs. CI-based 1.169 — CI-based is slightly worse (more conservative bands), as expected. CBIN follows the same pattern (1.113/1.093/1.096/1.150).
- **PIT dispersion calibration**: For CMCPS, no-weighting dispersion 0.0961 (miscalibrated vs. 0.0833 ideal) vs. CI-based 0.0616; CIDR no-weighting 0.0900 vs. CI-based 0.0555 — weighting brings dispersion closer to and below the calibrated level.
- **AAV capsid packaging (Figure 2)**: Importance weighting restores near-nominal (often slightly conservative) coverage under p_{test,τ} across τ∈{1,…,7} compared to under-covering unweighted variants, for CMCPS, CBIN, and CIDR. At the most extreme shift (τ=7), weighted CBIN and CIDR incur slightly worse CRPS due to severe effective-sample-size collapse and markedly wider bands (increased epistemic uncertainty). T=500 trials, 10⁶ held-out sequences, 7.55M training sequences.
- **Band-width informativeness (Figure 1)**: For a fixed test point, band thickness decreases as (n_train, n_cal) grow from (500, 500) to (5000, 5000) — reduced epistemic uncertainty from both model fitting and calibration; CI-weight bands also contract with sample size. Fixing sample size and moving the test point to a weakly-supported region widens the band (Figure 3).
- **Threshold-wise reliability (Figures 4–10)**: Weighted procedures retain good calibration for rare high-packaging outcomes {Y≥c} (high empirical quantiles), confirming extreme-event calibration.

## Critical Assessment

- **The closed-form envelopes (Propositions 1–2) assume the active set / IDR order is fixed across W_WU.** The authors flag this in Limitations: it restricts the binning, score, and ordering choices to those defined prior to weighting — so reordering-sensitive procedures cannot use the closed form and would need the general (slower) optimization over W_WU.
- **Theorem 2's confidence guarantee depends on W_WU being a valid joint confidence region for w.** In the experiments, W_WU is constructed from a fitted propensity model and the resulting coverage is asymptotic only — so the finite-sample guarantee (Theorem 2a) is not actually delivered in the empirical validation; only the asymptotic variant (2b) applies.
- **CRPS evaluation via the CRPS-optimal crisp CDF discards epistemic uncertainty.** The authors are candid that collapsing the conformal band to a single distribution for CRPS summary is "only a partial view of predictive performance," especially for CI-based envelopes — and explicitly call for future scoring procedures that account for epistemic uncertainty (e.g., selective evaluation curves). So the CRPS numbers, especially the slightly-worse CI-based ones, should not be read as a strict quality ranking.
- **At extreme shift (τ=7 in AAV), weighted CBIN/CIDR incur slightly worse CRPS** due to effective-sample-size collapse — the method recovers coverage but at a sharpness cost that the headline "restores near-nominal coverage" can obscure.
- **The synthetic and AAV settings are both covariate-shift / feedback-covariate-shift regressions.** Label shift and temporal drift are mentioned as motivations but not empirically validated; the permutation-weight framework is general, but the empirical breadth is narrow.

## Strengths

- Principled extension of GCPS to non-exchangeable settings via a clean permutation-weight interface, with three calibration notions (probabilistic, isotonic, auto) handled uniformly.
- The weight-uncertainty box is a principled way to propagate weight-estimation error into the predictive envelope, with both finite-sample and asymptotic confidence guarantees.
- Efficient closed-form evaluation rules for three concrete instances (CMCPS, CBIN, CIDR) make the method practical, not just theoretical.
- Honest treatment of the epistemic-uncertainty/CRPS tradeoff — explicitly advises reporting crisp forecasts from estimated (non-CI) weights while using CI-based bands to quantify epistemic uncertainty.
- Real-world biomolecular-design validation on a published benchmark (Fannjiang et al. 2022) with large-scale data (7.55M sequences), not just a toy regression.

## Limitations

- Closed-form envelopes assume the active set / IDR order is fixed across W_WU, restricting binning/score/ordering choices.
- Theorem 2's finite-sample guarantee requires a valid joint confidence region; experiments use a fitted propensity model giving only asymptotic coverage.
- CRPS-optimal crisp CDF evaluation discards the epistemic uncertainty encoded by band width; a partial view.
- At extreme shifts (τ=7), weighted variants incur worse CRPS due to effective-sample-size collapse.
- Empirical breadth is narrow: covariate-shift and feedback-covariate-shift regressions only; label shift and temporal drift unvalidated.

## Relevance

本文直接映射到「ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」研究兴趣——为分布偏移下的不确定性量化提供有限样本/渐近置信保证的数学框架（共形预测+置换权重+权重不确定性框）。同时也与「Computation Reliability & Precision Errors」相关：在权重需估计时显式建模权重不确定性，避免错误权重导致的覆盖率失真，属于预测系统的可靠性保证。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The finite-sample guarantee (Theorem 2a) requires W_WU to be a valid joint confidence region, but the experiments use a fitted propensity model giving only asymptotic coverage — what would a finite-sample-valid W_WU construction look like for the covariate-shift likelihood-ratio weights, and is it tractable?
- The closed-form envelopes assume the active set / IDR order is fixed across W_WU — how much accuracy is lost when this assumption is violated and one must fall back to the general optimization over W_WU?
- The authors call for scoring procedures that account for epistemic uncertainty (selective evaluation curves) — has the crisp-CDF-vs-band-width tradeoff been formalized into a proper scoring rule that rewards honest width?
- At extreme shift (τ=7 in AAV), effective-sample-size collapse hurts CRPS — is there a principled cutoff on the minimum effective sample size below which the method should refuse to predict?
- The framework is motivated by label shift and temporal drift but only covariate/feedback-covariate shift are validated — do the permutation weights extend cleanly to label shift where the weights depend on Y rather than X?
