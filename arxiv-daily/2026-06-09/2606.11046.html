Converting LLMs to reason better systematically degrades alignment — across toxicity, stereotyping, refusal calibration, and privacy.

Does Reasoning Preserve Alignment? On the Trustworthiness of Large Reasoning Models

Prajakta Kini · Avinash Reddy · Souradip Chakraborty · Satya Sai Srinath Namburi GNVV · Furong Huang · Amrit Singh Bedi · Alvaro Velasquez · 2026-06-09

Audit surface

6×3

trustworthiness dimensions × post-training methods (SFT · RL · distillation)

Why it matters

Reasoning models (o1, DeepSeek-R1) are being rapidly deployed — trading safety for reasoning capability could be a hidden cost we have not yet measured.

Critical edge

Findings may not generalize to proprietary reasoning models such as OpenAI o1/o3 or Claude thinking mode — the very systems people are actually shipping.

Methodology

The authors compare reasoning models against matched instruction-tuned baselines across three post-training regimes, then probe six trustworthiness dimensions:

  • SFT — supervised fine-tuning on reasoning traces
  • RL-based — RLHF / GRPO-style post-training
  • Distillation — student trained from a reasoning teacher
  • Drift metric — KL divergence between reasoning model and its instruction-tuned counterpart turns "alignment regression" into a measurable shift.

Results & highlights from abstract

Regressions observed across the six dimensions, consistent with measurable distributional shift from the aligned baseline.

DimensionRegression
Toxicity↑ increased
Stereotyping & bias↑ amplified
Refusal calibration↯ miscalibrated
Machine ethics↑ ethical drift
Privacy↯ contextual leakage
OOD robustness↓ degraded

Strengths04

  • First systematic trustworthiness audit spanning three reasoning post-training methods.
  • Matched instruction-tuned baselines — not just cross-model comparisons.
  • KL-divergence quantification turns "alignment regression" into a measurable phenomenon.
  • Timely: o1, DeepSeek-R1, and similar systems are already in production.

Limitations04

  • Generalization to proprietary reasoning models (o1/o3, Claude thinking) is uncertain.
  • Reproducibility depends on whether benchmarks and base models are fully specified.
  • Mechanistic causes — reward hacking, distributional shift, or otherwise — remain unexplained.
  • Whether findings hold across all model scales and families is an open question.

Questions for further explorationopen problems

  1. What specific mechanism during post-training causes alignment regression — reward hacking, distributional shift, or something else?
  2. Can alignment-preserving constraints be folded into reasoning training without sacrificing reasoning gains?
  3. Do the regressions track reasoning chain length, and at what point does alignment start to bend?
  4. Which trustworthiness dimensions are most susceptible to regression — and which are the most recoverable?
