> **一句话总结：** 本文将CIC-IDS2017重构为基于5元组会话的真实时序流序列（T=20滑动窗口），在9种模型×4种评估协议下发现：决定Transformer入侵检测性能的不是架构而是填充方式——在无填充真实序列上Transformer取得全实验最高macro-F1（0.89），但换成zero-pad+mask后骤降0.24，而无泄漏group-by-5-tuple划分使其误报率从0.04%暴涨至2.7%（67倍，常规随机划分完全掩盖此缺陷）；class-balanced Random Forest反而是最鲁棒的部署模型（macro-F1 0.769→0.778）。

> **TL;DR:** The authors reformulate CIC-IDS2017 (2.83M flows, 15 classes) into ordered T=20 sliding-window flow sequences grouped by 5-tuple conversation, and benchmark 9 architectures under a random split plus two leakage-free splits and a padding ablation. On genuinely sequential (non-padded) windows the CNN+Transformer achieves the highest macro-F1 of any model (0.89); under zero-pad+mask it drops −0.24 (vs only −0.02 for LSTM/GRU). Under the leakage-free group-by-5-tuple split, the Random Forest is most robust (macro-F1 0.769→0.778, +0.009) while the Transformer's false-alarm rate grows 0.04%→2.7% (67×) — an effect invisible under the conventional random+repeat-last split. The LSTM matches the Transformer's random-split macro-F1 (0.776 vs 0.775) at 13× lower latency (0.13 ms vs 1.73 ms/sample on A100).

## 核心贡献

- 将CIC-IDS2017重构为真正的时序任务：按5元组会话分组、按时间戳排序、取T=20滑动窗口，窗口标签为其最后一跳流的类别（模拟流式IDS实时分类），而非像先前CNN-LSTM-Transformer工作那样把时序模块喂入长度为1的序列。
- 系统性证明**填充方式（padding convention）而非架构**决定Transformer在IDS上的表现：在无填充真实序列子集上Transformer取得全实验最高macro-F1(0.89)，但用zero-pad+mask时骤降0.243，而LSTM/GRU/1D-CNN仅降~0.02–0.05。
- 揭示**评估协议掩盖的关键缺陷**：无泄漏group-by-5-tuple划分下Transformer误报率从0.04%升至2.7%（67倍），而该效应在常规随机+repeat-last划分下完全不可见；Random Forest的误报率几乎不变(0.16%→0.14%)且macro-F1略升(+0.009)，成为最鲁棒的部署模型。
- 通过对照消融隔离变量：time-based划分在本数据集退化为多数类基线（仅2类存活），group划分+zero-pad下Transformer的下降几乎全部来自填充协议(0.53 random vs 0.55 group)而非会话记忆。
- 提供可复现代码与显式协议披露：https://github.com/zachmocz/temporal-ids-bench。

## Key Contributions

- Reformulates CIC-IDS2017 as a genuine temporal IDS task: 5-tuple conversation grouping, timestamp sorting, T=20 sliding windows labeled by the final flow's class (streaming-IDS style), producing ~630k windows (~73% padded) across 13 classes after dropping 2 un-stratifiable classes.
- Benchmarks 9 architectures — static (Linear SVM, Random Forest, MLP, Image-CNN) and temporal (LSTM, GRU, 1D-CNN, CNN+Transformer, Transformer-small) — across 3 split protocols (random, time-based, group-by-5-tuple) plus a padding ablation.
- Demonstrates padding convention dominates Transformer performance: highest macro-F1 of the whole experiment (0.89) on non-padded sequential windows, but −0.243 under zero-pad+mask, while LSTM/GRU/1D-CNN drop only ~0.02–0.05.
- Exposes that leakage-free group-by-5-tuple evaluation reveals a 67× Transformer false-alarm-rate increase (0.04%→2.7%) hidden by random+repeat-last splits; Random Forest is the most robust model (macro-F1 +0.009, FPR 0.16%→0.14%).
- Shows evaluation methodology (≤0.24 macro-F1 swing) has a larger effect than architectural choice, and that prior CNN-LSTM-Transformer papers (Liu et al. 2025, Yao et al. 2023) feed sequence-length-1 inputs to their temporal modules, so their reported gains do not test temporal modeling.

## Methodology

- **Dataset & preprocessing.** Full CIC-IDS2017 capture: 2,827,677 labelled flows over 5 days, 1 benign + 14 attack classes. Drop identifiers/5-tuple/timestamp/label columns (Table I), retaining 76 numeric CIC-FlowMeter features. Pipeline: replace ±∞ with NaN and drop; encode 15-class label; sort by Timestamp; group by 5-tuple; build T=20 sliding windows; drop classes with <2 windowed examples (Heartbleed, DoS Slowhttptest) → 13-class subset covering ≥99.6% of flows; stratified 80/20 split; z-score (train-only) + min-max for image encoders; inverse-frequency class weights w_c=N/(C·N_c) for deep models. Cap any single 5-tuple at K=50 windows to prevent one DDoS conversation dominating. Result: ~1.46M 5-tuple groups → ~630k windows (~73% padded), ~504k train / ~126k test (random split).
- **Five-tuple temporal windowing (Eq. 1).** Partition flows by 5-tuple q, sort each bucket by timestamp, take X_{q,s}=(x_{i_s},…,x_{i_s+T−1}); label = class of final flow y_{q,s}=y_{i_{s+T−1}} (streaming-IDS mimicry). Conversations shorter than T use **repeat-last padding** (primary protocol, matches prior IDS work); the ablation re-runs with **zero-pad + explicit attention mask**.
- **Splits.** (1) Random 80/20 (primary, matches prior work). (2) Time-based: chronological first-80%/last-20% — *degenerate* on CIC-IDS2017 (only 2 of 13 classes survive in test because attacks were scheduled day-by-day; all models collapse to ~0.50 macro-F1). (3) Group-by-5-tuple: partition 5-tuples 80/20 so every window from a conversation stays on one side; uses zero-pad+mask. Windows are constructed after the split so none straddles the boundary.
- **Models (Table II).** Static (last flow only): Linear SVM (C=1.0, balanced), Random Forest (100 trees, depth 20, balanced), MLP (Dense 128–64–C), Image-CNN (9×9 grayscale encoding, Conv2D 32→64). Temporal (full T×F window): LSTM (64→32), GRU (64→32), 1D-CNN (Conv1D 64→128 + GAP), CNN+Transformer (TimeDistributed CNN-128 encoder → sinusoidal PE → learnable [CLS] → 2× encoder blocks d=128, h=4, d_ff=256 → classify from [CLS]), and Transformer-small (1 block, 2 heads, d=64, ~70k vs ~318k params). All deep models: Adam, grad-clip 1.0, sparse CE, early-stop patience 6, ReduceLROnPlateau, ≤30 epochs, val-split 0.15, seeds {2,42,123}.
- **Metrics.** Macro-F1 is the headline (BENIGN dominates: 80.3% of flows / 88.5% of windows, so weighted-F1 saturates). Also macro precision/recall, ROC-AUC, PR-AUC, operational false-alarm rate (FPR: fraction of BENIGN windows flagged as attack) and missed-attack rate (FNR). Latency: 20 reps of a 128-window batch on an NVIDIA A100 (40GB); TensorFlow 2.20, 80GB RAM.

## Results & Highlights

- **Random split (Table III).** LSTM leads macro-F1 **0.776±.01**, statistically tied with CNN+Transformer **0.775±.02**; Random Forest **0.769±.01**, GRU **0.762±.04**, 1D-CNN **0.754±.03**, Transformer-small **0.685±.06**. The top-5 cluster within 0.022 macro-F1; LSTM's lead over RF is 0.007 (within seed std). On accuracy the Transformer is 0.9989, in the same range as prior work (Liu 0.9920; Yao 0.9106), but macro-F1 exposes the rare-class story accuracy hides.
- **Padding ablation (Table VII).** Removing the repeat-last cue costs the full Transformer **−0.243 macro-F1** (0.78→0.53) and Transformer-small **−0.289** (0.69→0.40, with ±0.08 variance), while LSTM/GRU drop only ~0.02 and 1D-CNN 0.046 — recurrent/convolutional inductive biases are largely padding-invariant.
- **Non-padded subset (Table VIII).** On the ~27% of windows with no padding (9 classes), every temporal model improves; the Transformer posts the **highest macro-F1 of the entire experiment, 0.89±.02** (+0.118 over its padded-set score), confirming it is a capable sequence modeller on clean data.
- **Leakage-free group split (Table IX).** Random Forest is the most robust model: macro-F1 **0.769→0.778 (+0.009)**. LSTM/GRU/1D-CNN cluster at 0.73–0.75 (deltas −0.021 to −0.027, within seed std). CNN+Transformer drops **−0.226 to 0.55** — but the matched zero-pad baseline (Table VII) shows the split itself costs almost nothing (0.53 random-zero-pad vs 0.55 group-zero-pad); the collapse is attributable to padding, not conversation memorization.
- **Operational false-alarm explosion.** Random Forest FPR is essentially unchanged (0.16%→0.14%), while the Transformer's FPR grows from **0.04%→2.7% — a 67× increase** invisible under the conventional random+repeat-last protocol.
- **Per-class recall (Figs. 8–9).** High-volume classes (DDoS, FTP-/SSH-Patator) recalled ≥0.99 by nearly every model under both splits. Bot recall drops ~0.5–0.6 for all temporal models (LSTM/GRU/1D-CNN 0.80→0.22); the Transformer additionally collapses on Web-XSS (0.77→0.00), Web-SQLi (0.67→0.00), SSH-Patator (1.00→0.66).
- **Sequence-length sweep (Table V).** Macro-F1 rises monotonically with T for 3 of 4 temporal models (1D-CNN peaks at T=10); gain from T=5 to best horizon is +0.069 (1D-CNN) to **+0.173 (Transformer)**. T=20 is best/near-best for all.
- **Latency / cost-accuracy (Figs. 5–6).** At T=20, Transformer latency 1.73 ms/sample vs LSTM 0.13, GRU 0.12, 1D-CNN 0.09 (all ~13–20× cheaper); RF 0.27 ms on CPU. The LSTM **Pareto-dominates** the CNN+Transformer (equal macro-F1 0.776 vs 0.775 at 13× lower latency).
- **Full 15-class static re-run (Table VI).** Random Forest-15 reaches macro-F1 **0.801±.030** in ~5 s training, detecting all 3 classes the windowed pipeline drops — competitive with the best temporal model.

## Critical Assessment

- **Time-based split is reported as "degenerate" and then largely discarded.** Because CIC-IDS2017 schedules attacks day-by-day, the chronological 80/20 split leaves only 2 of 13 classes in the test set and every model collapses to ~0.50 macro-F1. The authors treat this as a dataset property rather than a benchmark, so one of the two advertised "leakage-free splits" contributes almost nothing — the paper's evidence rests almost entirely on the group-by-5-tuple split.
- **The headline 67× false-alarm claim conflates two variables.** The leakage-free group split simultaneously changes *both* the split protocol *and* the padding convention (group-split uses zero-pad+mask, random uses repeat-last). The authors' own Table VII shows the split itself costs the Transformer almost nothing (0.53 random-zero-pad vs 0.55 group-zero-pad), so the 67× FPR explosion is primarily a padding effect mis-attributed in the abstract framing to "leakage-free evaluation" — a conflation the body of the paper partially corrects but the abstract/TL;DR do not.
- **Only 3 seeds, treated informally.** Differences within one per-seed standard deviation are declared "ties"; with n=3 there is essentially no statistical power, yet several claims (e.g., RF "+0.009 most robust") hinge on sub-std deviations. Transformer-small shows ±0.08 variance under masking, suggesting the Transformer results are noisier than the headline means imply.
- **Confounding input representation.** The CNN+Transformer pre-encodes each flow with a CNN and operates on min-max-scaled 9×9 images, whereas LSTM/GRU consume raw z-scored features. The authors flag this ("Architectural asymmetry") but do not run the obvious control (Transformer on raw features), so the Transformer's padding fragility may partly stem from the CNN image encoding rather than self-attention per se.
- **Single-dataset scope.** All conclusions are specific to CIC-IDS2017, which the paper itself notes (citing Engelen et al. 2021) contains label noise and flow-construction artefacts; generalization to UNSW-NB15, CIC-IoT-2023, etc., is asserted as future work, not tested.

## Strengths

- Genuinely rigorous multi-protocol design that isolates architecture, split, and padding as separate variables via matched ablations (Tables VII–IX) — a model of methodological care for the IDS benchmarking community.
- Operationally meaningful metric (false-alarm rate) reported alongside macro-F1, directly exposing deployment risk that accuracy/weighted-F1 hide.
- Falsifiable, well-supported central claim; the non-padded-subset result (Transformer 0.89) is a fair positive finding that prevents the paper from being purely anti-Transformer.
- Concrete, citable critique of two specific prior papers (Liu et al. 2025 sequence-length-1 via pseudocode; Yao et al. 2023 collapsed taxonomy with Patator precision 0.28) rather than vague hand-waving.
- Open-source code, explicit hyperparameter table (Table II), and honest reporting of seed std and degenerate splits.

## Limitations

- Single dataset (CIC-IDS2017); known to have labelling/capture artefacts; generalization untested.
- The 67× FPR headline conflates split and padding changes (acknowledged in the body but not the abstract).
- Only 3 seeds with informal statistical treatment; Transformer variance under masking is high.
- Input-representation confound (CNN image encoding for Transformer vs raw features for RNNs) is uncontrolled.
- Time-based split is degenerate and contributes little; all weight rests on group-by-5-tuple.
- No streaming/online IDS evaluation; latency measured only on A100 GPU, not edge CPU deployment.

## Relevance

映射到用户兴趣区 **(2) LLM Inference Evaluation** 与更广义的评估方法论：本文虽以入侵检测为载体，但核心贡献是评估方法论批判——揭示填充约定、数据泄漏、序列构造方式对模型性能报告的决定性影响，与"如何可信地评估序列模型"直接相关。其"标准评估协议掩盖部署缺陷（67×误报）"的发现，也与 **(6) Silent Data Corruption (SDC)** 的精神相通——评估流程中的静默缺陷导致报告性能与真实部署性能严重背离。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The 67× false-alarm headline conflates the split change with the padding change (the body's Table VII shows the split itself costs ~nothing); does the Transformer's FPR still explode when the split is varied *with padding held fixed* at repeat-last?
- The CNN+Transformer pre-encodes flows into 9×9 images while LSTM/GRU use raw z-scored features — does the Transformer's padding fragility persist when it operates directly on raw features without the CNN pre-encoder (the control the authors flag as future work)?
- Do the padding-convention and split-protocol effects replicate on datasets with different attack scheduling (UNSW-NB15, CIC-IoT-2023), where the time-based split would not be degenerate?
- Only 3 seeds are used and differences within one std are declared ties; would a higher-powered seed study change the "Random Forest is most robust (+0.009)" conclusion, given Transformer-small shows ±0.08 variance under masking?
- The paper cites Engelen et al. 2021 on CIC-IDS2017 label noise — how much of the Infiltration undetectability (best F1 0.04, 4 training windows) is genuine difficulty versus label/flow-construction artefact, and does cleaning those artefacts change the architecture ranking?
