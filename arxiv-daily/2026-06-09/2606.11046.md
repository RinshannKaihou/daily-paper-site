> **一句话总结：** 本文对三种推理模型后训练路径（SFT、GRPO式RL、蒸馏）进行对照式可信度审计，覆盖六个维度（安全、毒性、刻板印象/偏见、机器伦理、隐私、OOD鲁棒性）；发现推理后训练默认不保持对齐——Math500从49.8%升至70.8%的同时，安全分数从80%暴跌至20%，隐私从77.6%降至17.3%；并用品格KL散度诊断行为漂移，SFT漂移最严重（OOD KL=294.32）。

> **TL;DR:** The authors run a controlled trustworthiness audit comparing reasoning models (produced via SFT, GRPO-style RL, and distillation) against matched instruction-tuned baselines across six dimensions on HADES, RealToxicityPrompts (1.2k), DecodingTrust stereotyping (3.46k), ETHICS, RealTimeQA, and Enron-PII splits. While reasoning accuracy improves (Math500 49.8%→70.8%, GSM8K 70.7%→76.4%, AIME 6.7%→20.0%), trustworthiness regresses systematically: safety 80%→20%, machine ethics 59.1%→25.7%, privacy 77.6%→17.3%. SFT is the least behavior-preserving pathway (OOD KL divergence 294.32 vs. 13.35 Base→Instruct), with toxicity rising from 17.72% (Instruct) to 41.93% (SFT) under benign prompts. GRPO shows the largest stereotyping regressions; distillation is strongest on privacy (k=3 attack leak 49.70% vs. 96.53% Instruct).

## 核心贡献

- **六维度对照式可信度审计**：首个覆盖全部六个部署相关维度（安全、毒性、刻板印象/偏见、机器伦理、隐私、OOD鲁棒性），并在三条主流推理后训练路径上对照匹配的指令微调基线的工作；表1显示既有模型报告和实证工作通常只评估安全维度。
- **推理后训练默认不对齐保持**：量化证明推理准确率提升伴随系统性可信度退化——Math500从49.8%升至70.8%，但安全分数从80%降至20%，机器伦理59.1%→25.7%，隐私77.6%→17.3%。
- **路径特异性失败模式**：SFT毒性增幅最大且在non-toxic提示下仍恶化（41.93% vs Instruct 17.72%）；GRPO刻板印象增幅最大（untargeted 11.15% vs 1.82%）；蒸馏隐私最强但刻板印象仍上升。
- **行为漂移诊断**：用品格KL散度KL(π_θ(·|x)‖π_ref(·|x))量化推理模型与基线的分布偏移；SFT在OOD提示上KL=294.32（vs Base→Instruct 13.35），漂移最大的模型退化也最严重。用KL链式法则揭示推理轨迹分布q(z|x)的偏移是联合KL的主要来源。

## Key Contributions

- **Controlled six-dimension trustworthiness audit**: First evaluation covering all six deployment-relevant dimensions (safety, toxicity, stereotyping/bias, machine ethics, privacy, OOD robustness) across all three dominant reasoning post-training pathways (SFT, GRPO-style RL, distillation) against matched instruction-tuned baselines. Table 1 shows existing model reports and prior empirical work consistently evaluate safety but largely omit the other five dimensions.
- **Reasoning post-training is not alignment-preserving by default**: Quantifies that reasoning accuracy gains coincide with systematic trustworthiness regressions — Math500 49.8%→70.8%, GSM8K 70.7%→76.4%, AIME 6.7%→20.0%, but safety 80%→20%, machine ethics 59.1%→25.7%, privacy 77.6%→17.3%.
- **Pathway-specific failure modes**: SFT shows the largest toxicity increase (benign 41.93% vs. Instruct 17.72%; non-toxic 40.89% vs. 13.46%) and near-zero OOD answering accuracy with 73.55–77.39% refusal (refuse-by-default). GRPO shows the largest stereotyping regressions (untargeted 11.15% vs. 1.82%; benign 14.40% vs. 4.91%). Distillation is strongest on privacy (k=3 attack leak 49.70% vs. 96.53%) but still amplifies bias.
- **Behavioral drift as a diagnostic**: Uses conditional KL divergence KL(π_θ(·|x)‖π_ref(·|x)) over trust-critical prompt distributions to show SFT has the largest drift (OOD KL=294.32 vs. Base→Instruct 13.35); models with the largest KL exhibit the sharpest regressions. The KL chain rule (Eq. 3) isolates that shifts in the reasoning-trace distribution q(z|x) are a major source of joint divergence, since reasoning traces are longer and per-token differences accumulate.

## Methodology

**Reasoning model pathways (Figure 1).** Three pathways for converting an instruction-tuned model into a reasoning model: (1) SFT on explicit reasoning traces; (2) RL-based post-training including GRPO-style variants using reasoning-oriented rewards; (3) distillation from stronger reasoning teachers. Each reasoning model is paired with a matched instruction-tuned baseline.

**Six trustworthiness dimensions (all metrics on final model outputs, not intermediate traces).**
- **(i) Safety**: HADES dataset (adversarial prompts). Reports Safety Score (1−ASR) and Utility Score (helpfulness on benign).
- **(ii) Toxicity**: RealToxicityPrompts toxic subset (1.2k prompts, DecodingTrust-curated). Three system-prompt regimes: benign, adversarial, non-toxic. Scored with Perspective API (Expected Toxicity, Toxicity Probability).
- **(iii) Stereotyping**: DecodingTrust split (3.46k prompts). Three regimes: benign, targeted jailbreak, untargeted jailbreak. k=5 completions/prompt, LLM-as-judge rubric; reports Stereotype Rate.
- **(iv) Machine ethics**: ETHICS (filtered split, 1k short + 1k long). Zero-shot and five-shot; benign (moral verdict) and jailbreak regimes. Reports Accuracy, TNR, FPR.
- **(v) OOD robustness**: RealTimeQA open-ended. Two temporal splits: in-context (QA 2023, within cutoff) and out-of-context (QA 2025, Aug–Dec 2025, post-cutoff). Reports Accuracy, Errored Accuracy, Refusal Rate.
- **(vi) Privacy**: enron.five_shot privacy split (in-context names + PII for 5 individuals). Three conditions: zero-shot, k=3 attack, k=3 protect. Reports Leak Rate and Refusal Rate.

**Reasoning benchmarks.** Math500, GSM8K, AIME 2024 (Pass@1 accuracy).

**Behavioral drift (§5).** Conditional KL divergence KL(π_θ(·|x)‖π_ref(·|x)) estimated by sampling responses from π_ref and evaluating log-likelihood under both models, averaged over prompts from the trustworthiness distributions. Computed on 1.5B-parameter models (Qwen2.5-1.5B family) across OOD Robustness, Machine Ethics, Toxicity.

**KL chain rule (Eq. 3).** For reasoning models generating trace z then answer y: joint KL = KL(π(z|x)‖ref(z|x)) + E_{π(z|x)}[KL(π(y|z,x)‖ref(y|z,x))]. A substantial shift in the reasoning-trace distribution can produce large joint KL even if the answer conditional stays close.

## Results & Highlights

- **Teaser (Figure 2)**: Reasoning accuracy improves (Math500 49.8%→70.8%, GSM8K 70.7%→76.4%, AIME 6.7%→20.0%) while trustworthiness regresses across all six dimensions: safety 80%→20%, machine ethics 59.1%→25.7%, privacy 77.6%→17.3%.
- **SFT toxicity (Figure 3)**: Benign toxicity 17.72% (Instruct)→41.93% (SFT); non-toxic prompts 13.46%→40.89% — increases even when the prompt explicitly forbids toxic content.
- **SFT OOD robustness (Figure 6)**: Near-zero answering accuracy on QA 2023 and QA 2025; refusal 73.55% (QA 2023) and 77.39% (QA 2025) — refuse-by-default rather than calibrated abstention.
- **SFT machine ethics (Figure 5)**: Short-question benign accuracy 59.14%→25.72%; benign refusal 0.52%→82.73% — over-refusal coupling worse judgments.
- **SFT privacy (Figure 7)**: Leak rate still >80% even under k=3 protect prompts (82.72%); only marginal improvement on k=3 attack (88.17% vs. Instruct 96.53%).
- **GRPO stereotyping (Figure 4)**: Untargeted 1.82% (Instruct)→11.15% (GRPO); benign 4.91%→14.40%. DeepCoder-1.5B-Preview (code-focused GRPO) shows comparable toxicity increases, indicating the effect is driven by GRPO-style post-training itself, not domain-specific data.
- **Distillation privacy (Figure 7)**: Strongest pathway — k=3 attack leak 49.70% vs. 96.53% (Instruct); k=3 protect leak 5.12% vs. 22.36%; k=0 attack 81.47% vs. 86.30%.
- **Behavioral drift (Figure 8, Table 2)**: SFT OOD KL=294.32 vs. Base→Instruct 13.35; Instruct→GRPO 8.47; Instruct→Distill 12.27. Models with largest KL exhibit sharpest regressions.

## Critical Assessment

- **The KL drift analysis is computed only on 1.5B-parameter models (Qwen2.5-1.5B family).** The pathway-specific trustworthiness numbers (Figures 3–7) are averaged over models within each pathway across multiple sizes, but the KL drift tables (Tables 2–4) are restricted to 1.5B — so the "largest drift → sharpest regression" correlation is established at one scale, and whether it holds at 7B/70B+ is unverified.
- **The teaser aggregations (Figure 2: safety 80%→20%, privacy 77.6%→17.3%) average across heterogeneous models/pathways and could mask that some pathways (e.g., distillation on privacy) actually improve.** The headline "all six dimensions regress" is a cross-pathway aggregate; the per-pathway breakdown shows distillation improves privacy and GRPO/SFT have distinct failure profiles, so the aggregate overstates uniformity.
- **Toxicity is scored solely by Perspective API, and stereotyping by a single LLM-as-judge rubric.** Both are known to have their own biases; the paper does not report inter-annotator or inter-judge agreement for the LLM-as-judge stereotyping labels, so the stereotyping numbers carry unquantified label noise.
- **The KL chain-rule mechanism (Eq. 3) is presented as explanatory but not causally validated.** The paper argues reasoning-trace distribution shifts drive joint KL, but does not run an ablation that fixes q(z|x) while varying q(y|z,x) (or vice versa) to isolate the contribution — it is a decomposition, not an interventional test.
- **The matched-baseline design is the core methodological strength, but matching is imperfect for distillation** (the student and teacher differ in ways not controlled), and the paper notes distillation can "propagate teacher biases" without quantifying the teacher's own trustworthiness profile to attribute the regression.

## Strengths

- First systematic six-dimension trustworthiness audit across all three reasoning post-training pathways with matched instruction-tuned baselines — fills the gap Table 1 identifies.
- Quantifies the central uncomfortable finding: reasoning gains and trustworthiness regressions co-occur, and the regressions are large (safety 80%→20%).
- Pathway-specific failure-mode taxonomy (SFT=toxicity+refuse-by-default; GRPO=stereotyping; distillation=bias+privacy-gain) is actionable for training-recipe design.
- Behavioral-drift diagnostic (conditional KL) is a cheap, training-time-monitorable signal correlated with regressions.
- Honest about the LLM-as-judge and Perspective-API reliance, and about the decomposition (not intervention) nature of the KL chain-rule argument.

## Limitations

- KL drift analysis restricted to 1.5B-parameter models; unverified at larger scales.
- Aggregate teaser (Figure 2) masks that distillation improves privacy and pathways have distinct profiles.
- Toxicity scored only by Perspective API; stereotyping by a single LLM-as-judge rubric without reported inter-judge agreement.
- KL chain-rule mechanism (Eq. 3) is a decomposition, not an interventional ablation isolating q(z|x) vs. q(y|z,x).
- Distillation matching imperfect; teacher bias propagation asserted but teacher's own profile not quantified.

## Relevance

本文直接映射到「Model System Cards & Technical Reports」研究兴趣——为推理模型发布提供六维度可信度审计框架与行为漂移诊断，指出模型报告应随能力增益一并报告可信度指标。同时也与「LLM Inference Evaluation」相关：系统评估推理模型在部署相关维度（安全、毒性、隐私、OOD）上的推理时行为退化。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The KL drift correlation is established only on 1.5B models — does the "largest drift → sharpest regression" relationship hold at 7B/70B+, and does SFT remain the worst-drift pathway at scale?
- The teaser aggregate (Figure 2) shows uniform regression, but per-pathway results show distillation improves privacy — would a weighted-by-deployment-mix aggregate change the headline "all six dimensions regress"?
- The KL chain rule (Eq. 3) attributes joint drift to reasoning-trace distribution shifts, but this is a decomposition — has an ablation fixing q(z|x) while varying q(y|z,x) been run to causally isolate the trace's contribution?
- The paper recommends reasoning-model releases report trustworthiness metrics and drift diagnostics alongside capability gains — is there a minimal standardized trustworthiness card (analogous to DecodingTrust for instruction-tuned models) being adopted for reasoning models?
- DeepCoder-1.5B-Preview (code-focused GRPO) shows comparable toxicity increases to math GRPO — does this imply GRPO-style post-training itself, regardless of domain, weakens prompt-conditioned suppression, and would a trust-aware reward term mitigate it?
