> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一个三信号诊断框架，联合分析多智能体辩论中的 token 级 log-probability、LLM-as-Judge 评分和任务准确率，发现 Constructor（支持方）的置信度与推理质量的相关性约为 Auditor（审计方）的两倍，且置信度能以 AUROC 0.804 检测 Constructor 的关键推理失败。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes a tri-signal diagnostic framework for multi-agent debate that jointly analyzes token-level log-probabilities, LLM-as-judge rubric scores, and task accuracy. Experiments in rubric-based scoring reveal a consistent four-phase confidence trajectory and a strong role asymmetry: confidence–quality correlation is ~2x stronger for the Constructor (supportive) role than the Auditor (adversarial) role, and critical-failure detection AUROC reaches 0.804 for the Constructor vs. 0.634 for the Auditor.

## 核心贡献

- **三信号联合诊断框架**：首次系统性地将 token 级 log-probability 轨迹、LLM-as-Judge 评分和最终任务准确率三个信号关联起来，用于评估多智能体辩论中的中间推理质量，而非仅看最终答案是否正确。
- **四阶段置信度轨迹发现**：揭示辩论中智能体生成过程存在一致的四阶段模式——高置信度开头 → 急剧下降 → 稳定中间平台 → 尾部高波动，且这一模式在不同角色间保持一致。
- **角色不对称性量化**：定量证明 Constructor（支持方）的置信度与推理质量相关性约为 Auditor（审计方）的两倍（ρ̄ = 0.335 vs. 0.177，不对称比率 1.89×），关键推理失败检测 AUROC 分别为 0.804 和 0.634。
- **跨领域研究议程**：提出将分析框架扩展到数学推理（GSM8K）和事实问答（Natural Questions）的计划，并设计了发散检测机制来识别"高置信-低质量"等异常模式。

## Key Contributions

- **Tri-signal diagnostic framework**: First systematic approach to jointly correlate token-level log-probability trajectories, LLM-as-judge rubric scores, and downstream task accuracy for evaluating intermediate reasoning quality in multi-agent debate.
- **Four-phase confidence trajectory**: Identifies a consistent structural pattern across debate roles — high-confidence opening, sharp early decline, stable mid-response plateau, and volatile tail — suggesting this is a general property of debating agents rather than task-specific.
- **Quantified role asymmetry**: Demonstrates that confidence–quality alignment is ~2x stronger for the Constructor (supportive reasoning) than the Auditor (adversarial reasoning), with critical-failure detection AUROC of 0.804 vs. 0.634 respectively.
- **Divergence detection taxonomy**: Defines three diagnostic divergence conditions (high-confidence/low-quality, high-quality/incorrect-answer, low-confidence/correct-answer) that can expose failure modes invisible to accuracy-only metrics.
- **Cross-domain research agenda**: Proposes extending the framework to mathematical reasoning (GSM8K) and factual QA (Natural Questions), with multi-judge consistency checks across GPT-5.5, Claude Opus 4.7, and Gemini 3 Pro.

## Methodology

The framework operates in three stages applied to a two-agent debate architecture:

1. **Debate System**: A Constructor agent produces a primary argument (advocate/solver/proponent depending on domain), and an Auditor agent produces a critical rebuttal (critic/verifier/challenger). A Synthesizer agent reads the full transcript and produces the final task output. Neither debater produces a final answer directly (except in math), ensuring log-probability trajectories reflect reasoning tokens only.

2. **Confidence Extraction**: Token-level log-probabilities are captured during decoding. Window-based segmentation (fixed-length and percentage-based) computes summary statistics — mean, variance, slope, entropy — over contiguous sub-sequences to analyze how confidence evolves across response phases.

3. **LLM-as-Judge Meta-Evaluation**: A separate judge model scores each agent's reasoning along three rubric dimensions (Instruction Following, Justification Quality, Evidence Grounding) on a 3-point scale, plus a binary critical-failure flag that zeroes out the composite score (range 0–9). Judge consistency is assessed via Krippendorff's α and inter-model agreement across multiple frontier models.

Experiments instantiate the framework in the rubric-based scoring domain using the ASAP essay dataset, with GPT-4o-mini for generation and GPT-5-mini for judging.

## Results & Highlights

- **Four-phase confidence trajectory** replicated across both Constructor and Auditor roles, with the Constructor maintaining ~0.05 higher token probability than the Auditor throughout the plateau region (tokens ~100–400).
- **Constructor mean correlation** between confidence and judged quality: ρ̄ = 0.335 (Instruction Following: 0.384, Justification Quality: 0.319, Evidence Grounding: 0.289, Aggregate: 0.350).
- **Auditor mean correlation**: ρ̄ = 0.177 (Instruction Following: 0.170, Justification Quality: 0.231, Evidence Grounding: 0.103, Aggregate: 0.202).
- **Role asymmetry ratio**: Constructor/Auditor = 1.89× across all reasoning dimensions.
- **Critical-failure detection**: AUROC 0.804 (Constructor) vs. 0.634 (Auditor), suggesting confidence-based diagnostics are most effective for detecting hallucination-style failures in supportive reasoning.
- **Late-response volatility**: The tail region (tokens 550+) shows substantially higher confidence volatility in both roles, potentially carrying underutilized diagnostic information.

## Authors & Credibility

All four authors are from the University of California, Irvine. The work is supported by the National Science Foundation under Grant No. 2315294. The paper is well-positioned at the intersection of multi-agent systems, uncertainty quantification, and automated evaluation — all active areas of LLM research. The methodology section is unusually detailed and pre-registers cross-domain experiments, though the current results cover only one of three planned domains.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The idea of correlating log-probabilities with LLM-as-judge scores in debate is genuinely novel. The role asymmetry finding (Constructor vs. Auditor) is interesting and non-obvious. However, the paper is essentially a position/framework paper with only preliminary results from a single domain.
- **Rigor**: The methodology is carefully designed with clear formalization, well-defined rubrics, and proper statistical aggregation. However, empirical results are limited to one domain (rubric scoring), one model family (GPT-4o-mini/GPT-5-mini), one dataset (ASAP), and one judge instance — making it impossible to assess generalizability. The paper acknowledges this limitation candidly.
- **Reproducibility**: High. The framework is clearly specified, datasets are public (ASAP, GSM8K, Natural Questions), and the prompt templates are in the appendix. The window-based segmentation and statistical aggregation procedures are fully described.
- **Significance**: Potentially significant if cross-domain results confirm the findings. The "confident liar" phenomenon (high confidence + low quality reasoning) is an important practical concern for multi-agent system reliability. However, the current paper reads more like a research proposal than a completed study.
- **Red flags**: The paper reports only rubric-scoring results but frames the contribution as spanning three domains. The math and QA experiments are described in methodology but have no results. The title ("The Confident Liar") is somewhat misleading — it implies a dramatic finding about deception, but the actual result is more measured (moderate correlations, one-domain evidence). The model names (GPT-5.5, Claude Opus 4.7, Gemini 3 Pro) in the methodology section appear to be forward-looking/hypothetical as of the paper date.

## Strengths

- Rigorous formalization of the debate setting with clear notation and problem definition.
- The window-based log-probability segmentation approach is well-designed and the statistical features are comprehensive (mean, variance, slope, entropy).
- Honest and thorough limitation section that explicitly flags the narrow scope of current results.
- The divergence detection taxonomy (three failure modes) is practically useful for system designers.
- Clean separation between methodological contribution and empirical findings.

## Limitations

- Empirical results cover only 1 of 3 planned domains (rubric scoring), making all findings preliminary and domain-specific.
- Single model family (GPT-4o-mini/GPT-5-mini) for both generation and judging — no evidence that findings transfer across models.
- Simplified debate architecture (2 agents, 1 round, fixed order) that may not reflect real-world multi-agent systems with iterative refinement or retrieval augmentation.
- The relationship between token-level confidence and actual internal computation is unclear — log-probabilities may capture post-hoc rationalization rather than genuine reasoning.
- No human evaluation to validate the LLM-as-judge rubric scores against ground-truth reasoning quality assessments.
- No ablation of the judge prompt design or scoring protocol sensitivity.

## Relevance

本文对多智能体辩论系统的中间推理质量评估提供了创新性诊断框架，特别是 log-probability 与 LLM-as-Judge 评分的联合分析方法，对理解 LLM 辩论架构的可靠性有直接参考价值。角色不对称性发现提示在构建多智能体系统时需对不同角色采用不同的置信度校准策略。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the four-phase confidence trajectory generalize to settings with more than 2 debate rounds, or to multi-agent topologies beyond pairwise debate?
- How stable are the Constructor/Auditor asymmetry ratios across different model families (e.g., open-source vs. proprietary, different parameter scales)?
- Can the divergence detection conditions be operationalized as real-time stopping criteria to abort debates that are likely to produce unreliable outputs?
- What is the causal direction: does high confidence cause lower reasoning effort, or do inherently easier reasoning paths simply produce both higher confidence and better quality?
- How do retrieval-augmented debate settings (where agents have access to external evidence) change the confidence–quality relationship?
