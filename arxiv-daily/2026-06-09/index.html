Must Read Today

Three papers the curator flagged above the fold — a calibrated math benchmark, an optimizer that treats forgetting as a general phenomenon, and a mechanistic discovery of an encoding subspace inside LLM agents.

Mathematical Reasoning
Test-time scaling is difficulty-asymmetric — it anti-scales on the hardest items and overthinks the easy ones
Sanghee Park, Geewook Kim, Kee-Eung Kim
基于韩国高考数学十年数据（664题，339题附带全国数十万考生逐题错误率）构建 KCSAT-ML 基准，提出与准确率近乎正交的 DRG 指标，揭示 TTS 在极难题上反缩放、在简单题上过度思考——同精度模型难度对齐可截然相反。 KCSAT-ML calibrates a decade of Korean CSAT math (664 items, 339 with nationwide per-problem error rates from hundreds of thousands of examinees) and pairs it with the DRG metric (r=+0.34 with accuracy) — revealing that TTS is difficulty-asymmetric: anti-scaling on the hardest items, overthinking on easier ones, and models with identical accuracy can have opposite difficulty-alignment profiles.

Optimization
FOGO reframes forgetting as a general optimization phenomenon — and beats Adam & Muon everywhere it's tested
Toan Nguyen, Yang Liu, Trung Le, Celso de Melo, Flora D. Salim
谱正交化动量 + 随机投影码本记忆 + 近端提升三步机制，在类别不平衡 CIFAR-10、持续视觉学习、LLaVA-7B 微调、GPT-2 预训练中一致优于 Adam 和 Muon；理论保证 Lint 损失既保准确性又保可干预性。 Spectrally orthogonalized momentum + random-projection codebook memory + proximal lifting — consistently beats Adam and Muon across class-imbalanced, continual, VLM fine-tuning, and GPT-2 pretraining settings.

LLM Safety
MIRAGE finds a shared low-dimensional encoding subspace in LLM agents — and exploits it for real-time covert-exfiltration detection (AUC 0.918)
Pratibha Revankar, Kargi Chauhan, Jihye Kim, Sadiba Nusrat Nur, Vincent Siu, Chenguang Wang
首次发现跨 9 种编码家族、5 种架构共享的编码子空间；探针在未见过的编码上 AUC 0.975–1.000；规划 token 处极性翻转；MIRAGE 双通道监控器在 126 个泄露场景中 AUC 0.918，远超仅检测输出的 0.518。 A shared low-dimensional subspace lights up across 9 encoding families and 5 architectures; polarity flips at the planning token; the MIRAGE dual-channel monitor hits AUC 0.918 on 126 exfiltration scenarios, vs. 0.518 for output-only detection. Suppress the subspace and you destroy the encoding.
