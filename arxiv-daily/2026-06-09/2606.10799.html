§ 01

Methodology

方法 · tripartite elaboration over a state-driven agent

The tripartite frame  (Γᵢ, Σᵢ, Tᵢ)

A proof S = (s₁, …, sₙ) is decomposed into steps. For each deduction step sᵢ with assertion aᵢ, the agent must produce an elaboration s′ᵢ that:

  • concludes with the identical assertion as sᵢ
  • strictly preserves the original reasoning trajectory
  • explicitly extracts Γᵢ (internal context: theorem/definition in the proof text), Σᵢ (external knowledge: literature citations), Tᵢ (background theory: trivial rewrites)

State-driven agent & halting

An agent maintains a Theorem Ledger tracking external claims Σᵢ. A step transitions to VERIFIED only if every external item is supported by a sketch or citation. FLAWED triggers an adversarial confirmation phase to guard against false positives.

Dynamic decomposition splits coarse steps into finer sub-steps. Four transitions drive the loop: OPEN→VERIFIED, OPEN→FLAWED, FLAWED→OPEN (after confirmation), and dynamic split.

Diagnostic suite & baselines

21 carefully curated research-level proofs: 11 valid (9 from Aletheia, 2 from OpenAI) and 10 invalid (8 from FirstProof baselines, 1 from Aletheia, 1 from OpenAI). Topics span algebraic topology, Lie theory, and geometric analysis.

Baselines compared: (1) Global GPT-5.4-xhigh (zero-shot LLM-as-judge), (2) Global Codex 5.4-xhigh (sentence-by-sentence reading), (3) Codex SxS ablation (mechanical splitting, no tripartite constraints).

§ 03

Strengths & Limitations

优点与局限

Strengths

  • Principled theoretical framework with clean formalization of step-level elaboration
  • Constructive approach makes verification transparent and auditable
  • Zero false acceptances — a strong practical guarantee on adversarial cases
  • "Pedantic hyper-rigor" is a genuine insight about the nature of mathematical communication
  • Well-designed ablation isolates the contribution of the tripartite structure
  • Detailed worked traces show the agent's verification process end-to-end

Limitations

  • Evaluation set is very small (21 proofs) — generalization is unclear
  • Depends on the base model's domain knowledge for external knowledge verification
  • Internet-search retrieval for Σᵢ is uncontrolled and variable
  • Tripartite structure adds significant generation overhead
  • Does not compare against formal verifiers (Lean, Coq, Isabelle)
  • Single-author work; broader validation would strengthen the claims
