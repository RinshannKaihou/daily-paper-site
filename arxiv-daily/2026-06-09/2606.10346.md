> **一句话总结：** 提出DiRL框架，通过从模型残差流中提取推理-记忆方向来指导GRPO的探索，选择性放大推理对齐的多样性、抑制记忆对齐的变化，在数学和通用推理基准上显著优于现有探索方法。

> **TL;DR:** DiRL extracts a reasoning-memorization direction from LLM residual streams, uses direction-weighted gradient features to partition rollouts, and shapes GRPO rewards to amplify reasoning-aligned exploration while suppressing memorization-aligned variation, achieving consistent gains on MATH500, AMC, AIME, GPQA, and MMLU-Pro.

## 核心贡献

- 提出DiRL（Direction-Aware Reinforcement Learning）框架，首次将推理-记忆区分融入RL训练过程
- 从模型残差流中提取推理-记忆方向向量k，作为探索的几何锚点
- 设计方向加权梯度特征Φ和推理锚定的探索分数ν，选择性奖励推理对齐的多样性
- 在GRPO中通过奖励塑形实现方向感知的探索，仅增加13-18%训练开销

## Key Contributions

- A reasoning-memorization direction k extracted from contrastive prompt sets (D+: reasoning-intensive, D-: memory-intensive) via mean activation difference in the residual stream
- Direction-weighted gradient features Φ(i) = Σ α_t ϕ_t, where α_t weights tokens by alignment with k, emphasizing reasoning-relevant policy updates
- Reasoning-anchored exploration score ν(i) that measures novelty relative to the reasoning subgroup GR (not the full group), suppressing memorization-driven diversity
- Direction-aware reward shaping: r̃ = r + λ+ν̄ for reasoning-aligned responses, r̃ = r - λ-ν̄ for memorization-aligned responses
- Demonstrated consistent improvements across 6 benchmarks on Qwen3-1.7B and 4B backbones

## Methodology

**Direction extraction:** Construct D+ (multi-step math, reasoning MMLU-Pro, MATH-R) and D- (factual recall, humanities C-Eval, memory MMLU-Pro, MATH-M). Compute k = mean(h*(x∈D+)) - mean(h*(x∈D-)) from the final-layer hidden state at the last prompt position. k is computed once before RL training and kept fixed.

**Gradient features:** For each token, compute ϕ_t = W[e(y_t) - p_t] (per-token gradient feature from the forward pass). Weight by α_t = σ(⟨h_t, k⟩) to emphasize reasoning-aligned tokens. Aggregate: Φ(i) = Σ α_t ϕ_t.

**Partitioning:** Project each response's mean hidden state onto k to classify as reasoning-aligned (s(i) > 0 → G_R) or memorization-aligned (s(i) ≤ 0 → G_M).

**Exploration score:** For reasoning responses, measure cosine distance from reasoning peers (novel update direction); for memorization responses, measure distance from reasoning group (penalize drifting away).

**Reward shaping:** λ+ = λ- = 1.5 (default), clipped to [-c, c] before GRPO advantage standardization. Four trajectory types receive differentiated rewards based on reasoning/memorization alignment × correct/wrong.

## Results & Highlights

- **MATH500:** 68.5 (1.7B, +3.9 over GRPO), 83.0 (4B, +5.6)
- **AMC:** 43.6 (1.7B, +6.0), 61.0 (4B, +8.0)
- **AIME24:** 10.9 pass@1 (1.7B, +4.4), 26.4 (4B, +6.6); AIME25: 7.3 (1.7B, +2.9), 19.6 (4B, +7.3)
- **GPQA:** 25.8 (1.7B, +2.6 over G2RL), 39.9 (4B, +4.0)
- **MMLU-Pro:** 41.4 (1.7B, +1.2), 58.3 (4B, +4.2)
- **Reasoning ratio:** Increases from 35.8% → 40.0% (1.7B) and 45.8% → 60.0% (4B) on AIME25
- **GSM-Symbolic robustness:** DiRL maintains advantages under symbolic perturbations (P1, P2)
- **Direction stability:** Angular drift < 6° throughout training; classification accuracy stays above 0.84
- **Overhead:** Only 13% (1.7B) and 18% (4B) slower than GRPO per step

## Authors & Credibility

Authors from University of Georgia, Tencent AI Lab, Education University of Hong Kong, and Hong Kong Polytechnic University. The work is well-motivated by recent mechanistic interpretability findings (Hong et al., 2025) on the reasoning-memorization direction in LLMs. The experimental setup is thorough with multiple baselines (GRPO, Entropy Bonus, EVOL-RL, G2RL), two model scales, and extensive ablations. Code available.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. Using the reasoning-memorization direction from mechanistic interpretability to guide RL exploration is a genuinely new idea. The direction-weighted gradient features and subgroup-partitioned exploration score are well-designed.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Comprehensive ablations (No-α, No-Sub, No-MR, No-Shape) isolate each component's contribution. Direction stability analysis (geometric + semantic) supports the fixed-direction design. GSM-Symbolic tests reasoning robustness beyond benchmark accuracy.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code available, clear hyperparameters (Table 5), standard benchmarks. However, the MATH-R/MATH-M split requires GPT-4o labeling, adding a dependency.
- **Significance:** High. The core insight — not all diversity is equal in RL for reasoning — is important and likely to influence future RL methods for LLMs. Consistent gains across benchmarks and model scales strengthen the claim.
- **Red flags:** The reasoning-memorization direction is a single global vector; the authors acknowledge this may miss multi-dimensional reasoning behaviors. Gains on the hardest benchmarks (AIME) are still modest in absolute terms. The MATH-R/MATH-M labeling uses GPT-4o and a threshold of 0.5, which introduces some subjectivity.

## Strengths

- Clever integration of mechanistic interpretability into RL training, bridging two typically separate research areas
- Consistent improvements across 6 benchmarks and 2 model scales with modest computational overhead
- Thorough ablation study showing all components contribute
- The reasoning ratio analysis provides direct evidence that DiRL shifts rollout composition
- GSM-Symbolic results demonstrate genuine reasoning improvement, not benchmark overfitting
- Direction stability analysis is a thoughtful addition that justifies the fixed-direction design choice

## Limitations

- Single global direction may not capture the full spectrum of reasoning behaviors (planning, symbolic manipulation, etc.)
- Effectiveness may depend on the quality of contrastive prompt sets D+/D- for direction extraction
- Evaluated only on Qwen3 backbones (1.7B and 4B); generalization to other architectures unknown
- Gains on AIME are meaningful but absolute numbers remain low (10.9–26.4 pass@1)
- The MATH reasoning-memory labeling adds an external dependency (GPT-4o)

## Relevance

DiRL将机制可解释性的发现（推理-记忆方向）与RL训练相结合，对如何让LLM通过强化学习获得真正的推理能力（而非记忆模式匹配）提供了新思路，与当前LLM推理能力提升的研究方向高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would a multi-direction approach (extracting several reasoning-related directions) capture more nuanced reasoning behaviors?
- Can DiRL be applied to code generation or scientific reasoning tasks beyond math?
- How does the direction quality depend on the choice of contrastive sets D+/D-?
- Does the reasoning-memorization direction transfer across model families (e.g., Llama, Gemma)?
- Could DiRL be combined with process reward models for finer-grained reward shaping?
