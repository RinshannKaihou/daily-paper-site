Reasoning or Memorization? Direction-Aware Diversity Exploration in LLM Reinforcement Learning →

Key metric 45.8% → 60.0% AIME25 reasoning ratio · Qwen3-4B
Why this matters Bridges mechanistic interpretability with RL — selectively amplifies reasoning-aligned diversity while suppressing memorization-driven variation, yielding consistent gains across 6 benchmarks at modest compute cost.
Critical edge A single global direction may miss multi-dimensional reasoning behaviors, and AIME pass@1 stays low in absolute terms (10.9 – 26.4).

Methodology

  1. Direction extraction. Build contrastive prompt sets D+ (multi-step math, MATH-R) and D− (factual recall, MATH-M). Extract k = mean(h*) − mean(h*) from the final-layer hidden state at the last prompt position; keep k fixed during RL training.
  2. Direction-weighted gradient features. Per-token ϕ_t = W[e(y_t) − p_t]; weight by α_t = σ(⟨h_t, k⟩) to emphasize reasoning-aligned tokens. Aggregate: Φ(i) = Σ α_t ϕ_t.
  3. Subgroup partitioning. Project each rollout's mean hidden state onto k: s(i) > 0 → reasoning group G_R; s(i) ≤ 0 → memorization group G_M.
  4. Reasoning-anchored exploration score ν. For reasoning rollouts, measure cosine distance from G_R peers (novelty). For memorization rollouts, penalize drift away from G_R.
  5. Direction-aware reward shaping. r̃ = r + λ₊ν̄ for reasoning-aligned, r̃ = r − λ₋ν̄ for memorization-aligned, with λ₊ = λ₋ = 1.5 and clipping to [−c, c] before GRPO advantage standardization.

Strengths

  • Bridges mechanistic interpretability with RL training — an uncommon integration.
  • Consistent gains across 6 benchmarks × 2 model scales at modest compute cost.
  • Full ablation suite (No-α, No-Sub, No-MR, No-Shape) isolates each component's contribution.
  • Reasoning-ratio analysis gives direct evidence of rollout-composition shift.
  • GSM-Symbolic tests argue the gain is reasoning, not benchmark overfitting.

Limitations

  • A single global direction may miss planning, symbolic manipulation, and other modes.
  • Effectiveness depends on the quality of D+/D− contrastive sets.
  • Only Qwen3 (1.7B / 4B) evaluated; cross-family transfer is unknown.
  • AIME pass@1 stays low in absolute terms (10.9 – 26.4).
  • MATH-R / MATH-M labeling requires GPT-4o — an external dependency.

Questions for further exploration

  1. Would a multi-direction representation capture more nuanced reasoning behaviors?
  2. Can DiRL extend to code generation or scientific reasoning beyond math?
  3. How sensitive is direction quality to the choice of D+/D− contrastive sets?
  4. Does the reasoning-memorization direction transfer across model families (Llama, Gemma)?
  5. Could DiRL be combined with process reward models for finer-grained shaping?
