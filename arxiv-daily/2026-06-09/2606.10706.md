> **一句话总结：** 本综述以资源约束为中心视角，统一梳理了LLM训练中的数据效率（选择什么数据训练）、内存效率（如何把训练装进硬件）和计算预算感知（何时停止或重新分配FLOPs）三个耦合瓶颈，提出"边际效用最大化"的统一原则，并指出从静态数据选择到动态影响力感知方法的关键研究空白。

> **TL;DR:** A constraint-centric survey unifying data efficiency, memory efficiency, and compute budget awareness in LLM training under a common principle of resource-conditioned decision-making, identifying the critical gap between static pre-filtering and dynamic influence-aware data selection.

## 核心贡献

- 提出资源约束生命周期框架，将LLM训练效率组织为三个耦合阶段：数据效率（基础）、内存效率（约束）、计算预算（调节器）
- 系统综述数据选择方法：从基于学习动态的代理信号到梯度/影响力评分，再到难度感知和课程式策略
- 指出当前最优数据选择方法（如LESS）主要是静态预过滤的，存在从静态到动态的关键研究空白——需要训练期间动态重新评估数据影响力的方法
- 提出统一原则：所有技术的共同目标是在单位受限资源（GB VRAM、FLOPs或Wall-time）下最大化性能增益

## Key Contributions

- Structures the literature via a **resource-constrained lifecycle**: data efficiency (the foundation), memory efficiency (the constraint), and compute budget awareness (the governor), with coupled feedback loops between them
- Identifies the **"Static-to-Dynamic" gap**: current state-of-the-art data selection methods (e.g., LESS) are predominantly static pre-filtering due to computational costs; bridging this gap requires hybridizing data selection with memory-efficient approximations
- Unifies diverse techniques under the principle of **marginal utility per resource**: whether filtering data samples, adding parameters, or extending training, the goal is maximizing performance gain per unit of constrained resource
- Shows that GPU memory, not raw compute, is often the dominant bottleneck in fine-tuning, requiring joint reduction of weight storage, optimizer states, and activation memory

## Methodology

This is a comprehensive survey paper that adopts a constraint-centric perspective rather than organizing methods by architecture or task. The authors review and synthesize recent progress across three axes:

1. **Data efficiency**: Selection and pruning methods including scalable proxy signals (loss-based, gradient-norm), gradient- and influence-based scoring (LESS, datamodels), difficulty-aware strategies (curriculum learning, Rho-1 token-level selection), and redundancy removal
2. **Memory efficiency**: Parameter-efficient fine-tuning (LoRA, adapters), quantization (INT8, FP8), memory-efficient optimizers (block-wise updates, HiFT), zeroth-order methods, and activation memory reduction techniques
3. **Compute budget awareness**: Compute-optimal scaling laws (Chinchilla), mixture-of-experts architectures, adaptive inference (speculative decoding, early exit), and stopping rules based on marginal performance gains

The survey highlights interdependencies: sophisticated data selection can incur prohibitive memory overhead, while memory-saving strategies may restrict data adaptivity.

## Results & Highlights

- Different notions of "good data" dominate in different regimes—optimal subsets depend on task objective and resource budget rather than being universal
- Pretraining dominates overall computational cost, but fine-tuning is increasingly the primary mechanism for rapid adaptation—making fine-tuning efficiency a central research focus
- The optimization trilemma (data, memory, compute) is not just theoretical but a physical necessity for edge intelligence
- Static data selection methods (pre-filtering before training) dominate practice, but dynamic methods that re-evaluate data influence during training are needed for truly compute-optimal training
- Isolated optimizations often shift bottlenecks rather than resolve them—e.g., aggressive data pruning reduces iterations but may require more memory for influence computation

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate for a survey—the main contribution is the unifying framework (resource-constrained lifecycle) and the static-to-dynamic gap identification, not individual techniques. The marginal utility principle is a useful synthesis lens but not fundamentally new.
- **Rigor:** The survey covers a broad landscape with clear categorization and cross-referencing. However, as with any survey, depth is traded for breadth—individual method comparisons are necessarily brief.
- **Reproducibility:** N/A for a survey paper; the contribution is conceptual organization rather than experimental results.
- **Significance:** High practical value for practitioners navigating the fragmented efficiency literature. The identification of the static-to-dynamic gap is a meaningful research direction.
- **Red flags:** The paper's own scope is ambitious—covering data, memory, and compute efficiency means some important topics receive only cursory treatment. The unified framework, while useful, may oversimplify the coupling between dimensions in practice.

## Strengths

- First survey to explicitly unify data, memory, and compute efficiency under a single constraint-centric framework
- Clear identification of the static-to-dynamic gap as a key research direction
- The marginal utility principle provides a useful decision-making lens for practitioners
- Highlights practical interdependencies that isolated method papers often ignore

## Limitations

- Breadth comes at the cost of depth—some methods are described without sufficient detail for implementation
- The framework is descriptive rather than prescriptive—doesn't provide concrete algorithms for joint optimization
- Heavy focus on fine-tuning efficiency; pretraining efficiency receives less detailed treatment
- The "Dynamic LESS" vision is aspirational without concrete proposals for achieving it

## Relevance

这篇综述对LLM训练效率研究有重要参考价值。统一的资源约束视角和对数据/内存/计算三者耦合关系的分析，对理解LLM训练瓶颈和设计高效训练策略高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How can we design practical dynamic data selection systems that re-evaluate influence during training without prohibitive memory or compute overhead?
2. What are the quantitative trade-offs between data quality, memory usage, and compute allocation across different model scales and tasks?
3. Can the marginal utility principle be operationalized into an automated training budget allocation system?
4. How do the findings generalize to multimodal or multi-task training settings where the resource trilemma may have different optimal operating points?
5. What hardware co-design opportunities exist for jointly optimizing data pipelines, memory hierarchies, and compute scheduling?
