Robust Regression of General ReLUs with Queries

Ilias Diakonikolas (UW–Madison)  ·  Daniel M. Kane (UCSD)  ·  Mingchen Ma

The first computationally efficient learner for biased ReLUs under Gaussians — achieving exponential label savings over passive learning.

learning-theory agnostic-learning active-learning relu-regression query-complexity
Key Metric · Query Complexity
d · polylog(1/ε) + Õ(min{1/p, 1/ε})
black-box label queries to reach O(opt) + ε error under Gaussian marginals. Versus poly(d, 1/ε) labels for passive learning — an exponential (logarithmic) gap in 1/ε.
Why This Matters
Nails the label complexity for robust regression of biased ReLUs: logarithmic savings in 1/ε, with matching lower bounds — completing the theory of the problem.
⚠ Critical Edge
Gaussian marginals & squared loss only · single ReLU neuron (no multi-neuron / deep extension) · asymptotic algorithm efficiency, no empirical validation.

Methodology & Results

Methodology is derived from the abstract only — full-text methods section pending.

Methodology

  • Setting. Agnostic learning under standard Gaussian marginals; squared-loss regression of σ(w·x + b).
  • Model. Interactive black-box label queries — learner adaptively asks for labels, rather than receiving a fixed labeled sample.
  • Cost decomposition. Query complexity splits into a dimension-dependent localization term d · polylog(1/ε) and a bias-dependent residual Õ(min{1/p, 1/ε}).
  • Lower bounds. Information-theoretic — proven against adversarial query models and pool-based active learners.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Complete theory. Algorithmic upper bound paired with matching lower bounds — the gold-standard structure for learning theory.
  • Clean message. Interactive query access — not generic active learning — is what unlocks the savings.
  • Practical generalization. Lifts prior homogeneous-ReLU results to the biased case that arises in practice.
  • Fine-grained complexity. Bias-aware term Õ(min{1/p, 1/ε}) is interpretable, not buried inside an opaque poly().

Limitations

  • Distributional. Holds under the Gaussian; behavior on general or adversarial distributions not addressed.
  • Loss model. Squared loss only.
  • Concept class. Single general ReLU — no multi-neuron or deep-network extension.
  • Theoretical only. No empirical validation; asymptotic efficiency may still hide large constants or exponents.
03

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Is the bias-dependent term Õ(min{1/p, 1/ε}) fundamental to general (biased) ReLUs, or can it be removed entirely?
  2. Does the Ω̃(d/ε) lower bound for pool-based active learning extend to weaker, non-pool active models?
  3. Can the results be lifted to multi-neuron or shallow ReLU networks under Gaussian marginals?
  4. How do the guarantees degrade under misspecification — approximately Gaussian or log-concave marginals?
  5. Does the same localization-plus-query machinery transport to other GLM-like activations (sigmoid, leaky-ReLU)?
