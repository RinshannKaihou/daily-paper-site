> **一句话总结：** 本文构建了540张图像×4种问题变体的短语控制基准，揭示VLM严重依赖文本先验而非真正"看"图像，并通过GRPO后训练部分缓解了这一依赖。

> **TL;DR:** A phrasing-controlled 540-image benchmark with four question variants shows VLMs rely heavily on textual priors rather than visual content; no-image ablation collapses open-model accuracy to 1–9%, and GRPO post-training on a 4B model partially reduces this reliance with OOD transfer.

## 核心贡献

- 构建了540张图像、6个推理类别、4种问题变体的短语控制基准，通过控制问题措辞（而非图像内容）来隔离VLM对文本先验的依赖
- 评估了11个VLM（含闭源和开源），发现所有模型在最难的Vision-Grounded变体上显著退化，开源模型从10–16%到闭源27–38%
- 设计了no-image消融实验，证明移除图像后开源模型准确率崩塌至1–9%，证实问题不可仅靠文本回答
- 展示GRPO后训练Qwen3.5-4B可以在所有4个变体上持续提升准确率，且迁移到分布外数据

## Key Contributions

- A 540-image × 4-variant phrasing-controlled benchmark across six reasoning categories (OCR, Chart, Physics, Spatial, Visual Reasoning, Visual Math)
- Comprehensive evaluation of 11 VLMs showing that Vision-Grounded variants (written from image only, no source text) are uniformly the hardest for every model
- No-image ablation demonstrating that open-weight models collapse to 1–9% accuracy without images, establishing genuine image-dependence
- Three triangulation analyses: LLM-rated difficulty, base-to-final textual similarity, and human re-annotation corroborating image-dependence
- In-context exemplar matching shows the deficit is missing grounding, not ill-posedness
- GRPO post-training of Qwen3.5-4B with per-variant LoRA adapters improves all variants in-distribution (+17–27 pp) and transfers to OOD set

## Methodology

The authors curate 540 images from 21 public benchmarks across six categories. For each image, Gemini-2.5-Pro generates four question variants with decreasing reliance on source text: (1) Question-Guided (rewrite source question harder), (2) Subquestion-Guided (synthesize perceptual sub-questions in one pass), (3) Multi-Signal (fuse chat-elicited sub-questions with source), (4) Vision-Grounded (generate directly from image alone, no source text). The key design: images are fixed, only phrasing varies.

Evaluation uses Qwen3-14B as LLM-as-judge with binary yes/no equivalence. The no-image ablation runs the same questions without images on six open-weight models. Difficulty and similarity are rated by Claude. Human re-annotation uses three annotators per item.

For mitigation, GRPO trains per-variant LoRA adapters on Qwen3.5-4B (frozen vision tower, language layers only) with format + correctness reward, evaluated on 100-sample held-out split and 200-question OOD set from six provenance-clean sources.

## Results & Highlights

- **Open vs. proprietary gap on Vision-Grounded:** Open models score 10–16% vs. proprietary 27–38%
- **No-image collapse:** Removing images drops open models to 1–9% accuracy across all variant types, including the base question (e.g., Qwen3.5-9B: 69.3%→~5%)
- **Difficulty ratings:** Vision-Grounded rates 4.07/5 vs. base 2.50/5; textual similarity to base: 1.31/5 (most distinct)
- **Human re-annotation:** 42.8% of Vision-Grounded answers were corrected (hardest variant)
- **Matching in-context exemplars:** Subquestion exemplars → +18.0 pp for Subquestion-Guided; chat exemplars → +18.5 pp for Multi-Signal
- **GRPO gains (in-distribution):** +17.1 to +26.7 pp across variants
- **OOD transfer:** Best adapter (Question-Guided) reaches 58.4% vs. 40.9% base (+17.5 pp)

## Authors & Credibility

Authors from IIT Roorkee, Raeth AI, and Lossfunk. The work is a systematic benchmark study with strong experimental design (controlled variables, multiple corroboration methods, human annotation). The scale of evaluation (11 models, 540 images × 5 conditions) is substantial. Code and dataset promised upon acceptance.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The 4-variant phrasing-controlled design is genuinely novel for isolating textual-prior reliance. Prior work (ViLP) used hand-built text-answerable items, but this approach is more systematic and scalable.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The no-image ablation is a clean experimental design. Three independent corroboration analyses (difficulty, similarity, human re-annotation) triangulate well. The GRPO study has proper train/test splits and OOD evaluation.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate. Code/data promised but not yet released. Uses proprietary Gemini-2.5-Pro for generation and Claude for rating, which introduces some reproducibility concerns.
- **Significance:** High. Identifies a fundamental limitation of current VLMs — they don't truly "see" as much as we think — and shows it's partially trainable away. Has practical implications for VLM deployment.
- **Red flags:** GRPO evaluated only on a single 4B backbone; 100-sample test split is small. The lenient answer extraction (fallback to full completion when `</answer>` is missing) may inflate numbers. Difficulty ratings come from a single LLM (Claude), not independent human ratings.

## Strengths

- Elegant experimental design: fixing images while varying phrasing is the right control
- The no-image ablation is the gold-standard diagnostic for textual-prior reliance
- Comprehensive model coverage (11 VLMs, open + proprietary)
- The finding that in-context exemplars matching variant construction recover accuracy is insightful — it proves the deficit is grounding, not unanswerability
- GRPO transfer to OOD is de-confounded evidence (reward doesn't target those sources)

## Limitations

- GRPO tested on only one 4B model with LoRA — unclear if effects scale to larger models
- 100-sample test split too small for per-category claims
- Lenient answer extraction favors recall over precision, compressing reported gains
- Difficulty and similarity ratings from single LLM judge (Claude), not fully independent ground truth
- Generated questions depend on Gemini-2.5-Pro quality; potential generator-model bias
- The benchmark covers 6 categories but some (Visual Math: 20 samples) are thin

## Relevance

这篇论文直接触及VLM评估的核心问题——模型到底是在"看"还是在"猜"，对理解多模态模型的实际能力和部署可靠性有重要参考价值。GRPO后训练减少文本先验依赖的方法论对VLM训练也有实践意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the textual-prior reliance finding generalize to video-language models or audio-visual models?
- How does the GRPO mitigation scale to 7B+ or 70B+ models with full fine-tuning?
- Can textual-prior reliance be measured during pretraining, and does it decrease naturally with scale?
- Would adversarial training against textual priors (e.g., contrastive loss between with-image and no-image outputs) be more effective than GRPO?
- Is the open–proprietary gap on Vision-Grounded driven by vision encoder quality or language model capacity?
