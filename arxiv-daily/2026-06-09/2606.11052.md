> **一句话总结：** 本文发现对蒸馏得到的混合线性注意力模型（HypeNet、Jet-Nemotron）做CoT监督微调会系统性地破坏其长上下文召回——例如HypeNet-9B在NIAH-S2@256K上从67.2%暴跌至9.40%；其根因是CoT的局部梯度使保留的softmax层中查询/键投影（WQ、WK）发生"局部化漂移"，作者据此提出零训练成本的QK-Restore（仅把softmax层的WQ、WK换回SFT前权重）来恢复长程路由，同时保留推理增益。

> **TL;DR:** The authors show that CoT-SFT on distilled hybrid linear-attention models (HypeNet-2B/5B/9B and Jet-Nemotron-2B) catastrophically degrades long-range recall: on the Miromind math CoT dataset, HypeNet-9B drops from 67.2% to 9.40% on NIAH-Single-2@256K and from 72.4% to 17.4% on NIAH-Single-3@128K. They trace this to locality-biased drift in the WQ/WK projections of the retained softmax-attention layers (formalized by a Gradient-Locality theorem under a CoT-Markov assumption) and propose QK-Restore, a training-free transplant of pre-SFT WQ/WK that recovers, e.g., HypeNet-5B NIAH-S3@256K from 65.4% to 76.4% (+11.0) while keeping MATH500/GSM8K essentially unchanged.

## 核心贡献

- 识别出一种**结构化的后训练失效模式**："Attention Amnesia"——CoT-SFT专门削弱混合模型中由保留softmax层负责的长程召回，而非通用型灾难性遗忘；难度更高的NIAH设置（Single-2、Single-3）和更长的上下文窗口下退化最严重。
- 给出**理论与实证**：在CoT-Markov假设下推导出"梯度局部性定理"（attention-logit梯度随token距离以 ρ^τ 指数衰减），并用token自相关与梯度衰减曲线（W_corr≈172 tok vs W_grad更短）验证CoT数据把梯度集中在短距离交互上。
- 提出**QK-Restore**：零训练成本——仅把保留softmax层的 WQ、WK 换回SFT前权重，保留所有其它SFT后参数（含WV、WO），从而恢复长程路由同时保留推理提升；并给出去掉有害"局部漂移"但保留有益"数学适应"的 **Procrustes变体（QK-Pro）**。
- 通过**对照实验**证明该现象是CoT特异的：非CoT的指令微调（Tulu-3）反而改善或保持长上下文召回；现象在数学（Miromind）和代码（OpenThoughts-3）领域都成立。

## Key Contributions

- Identify CoT-SFT-induced recall degradation as a *structured* post-training failure mode in distilled hybrid models — distinct from generic catastrophic forgetting because it selectively hits long-range routing in retained softmax layers while leaving the intended reasoning gains intact.
- Provide a theoretical and empirical account (Gradient-Locality Theorem 4.6 + Routing-Extraction Gradient Decoupling Theorem 5.1) showing CoT-SFT concentrates training signal on short-range token interactions and induces locality-biased drift in WQ/WK, while WV/WO still receive uniformly-bounded gradient.
- Introduce QK-Restore, a training-free method that restores only WQ/WK of the retained softmax-attention layers from the pre-SFT checkpoint, plus a Procrustes variant (QK-Pro) that separates harmful routing drift from beneficial reasoning adaptation.
- Show the failure is specific to CoT-style traces (non-CoT instruction SFT on Tulu-3 preserves or improves retrieval) and generalizes across math and code CoT data and across HypeNet (2B/5B/9B) and Jet-Nemotron-2B architectures.

## Methodology

- **Setting — hybrid models.** A hybrid interleaves a small set L_attn of standard softmax-attention layers (governing long-range recall via the pre-softmax logit e_ts = q_tᵀk_s/√d_h, set entirely by WQ, WK) with efficient linear/recurrent layers (e.g., Lightning Attention with state S_t = diag(λ_t)S_{t−1} + k_t v_tᵀ). Long-context recall in distilled hybrids depends disproportionately on L_attn.
- **Theory — why CoT-SFT disrupts routing.** CoT traces are modeled as a latent stationary reversible ergodic Markov chain over reasoning states (Assumption 4.1, spectral radius ρ∈(0,1)). Under norm bounds (Assumption 4.3) and the cross-entropy score-function identity (Assumption 4.4), the authors prove the **Gradient-Locality Theorem (4.6)**: the distance-conditioned gradient g(τ)=E[∂L/∂e_{t,t−τ}] ≤ C′·e^{−τ/W} with W=−1/log ρ. They then prove **Routing-Extraction Gradient Decoupling (Thm 5.1)**: ∇WQ,∇WK decay as ρ^τ (shaped only by short-range pairs) whereas ∂L/∂v_s is bounded below uniformly (independent of distance) — so CoT-SFT simultaneously corrupts WQ/WK and improves WV/WO.
- **Empirical validation.** They fit log-linear models y(τ)=C·ρ^τ to (i) token autocorrelation ρ̄(τ) as a proxy for the latent chain and (ii) measured E[|∂L/∂e_ts|] on HypeNet-2B, confirming both are exponential and that CoT math text has a *longer* correlation horizon (W_corr≈172 tok) than general prose (≈115 tok) while the gradient horizon W_grad is shorter — leaving a band where data needs long-range attention but the gradient no longer reinforces it.
- **QK-Restore (Algorithm 1).** Initialize θ_rep from the post-SFT checkpoint; for each ℓ∈L_attn copy WQ^{ℓ,rep}←WQ^{ℓ,pre} and WK^{ℓ,rep}←WK^{ℓ,pre}; leave all other post-SFT parameters. Zero training cost.
- **QK-Pro variant.** Because post-SFT WQ = WQ^pre + δWQ^route + δWQ^math entangles harmful routing drift with beneficial reasoning adaptation, QK-Pro solves a Procrustes problem: min over WQ^new,WK^new of ‖WQ^new−WQ^post‖²_F + ‖WK^new−WK^post‖²_F s.t. WQ^new WK^newᵀ = R^pre — preserving the pre-SFT routing geometry R=WQ WKᵀ while staying close to the SFT-adapted weights.
- **Models / training.** HypeNet (2B/5B/9B, trained in-house for both pre-training and SFT) and Jet-Nemotron-2B (SFT applied to the public pre-trained checkpoint). Main CoT-SFT data: Miromind (math); additional runs on OpenThoughts-3 (code reasoning) and Tulu-3 (non-CoT instruction following) as controls.
- **Evaluation.** Long-context recall: NIAH (Needle-in-a-Haystack) at 32K/64K/128K/256K and three difficulty levels Single-1/2/3. Reasoning: MATH500 and GSM8K (Avg@16 and Maj@16), AIME24, and LiveCodeBench-V5 (coding); IFEval for the Tulu-3 control.

## Results & Highlights

- **CoT-SFT drives the degradation.** On Miromind CoT-SFT, HypeNet-9B NIAH-Single-2@256K drops from **67.2% (pre-train) → 9.40% (post-SFT)**, and NIAH-Single-3@128K from **72.4% → 17.4%**; HypeNet-2B NIAH-Single-3@128K drops from **30.4% → 7.60%**. Degradation grows with context length and is negligible on the easy NIAH-Single-1 (stays ~100%).
- **QK-Restore recovers recall.** On HypeNet-5B, NIAH-Single-3@256K improves from the post-SFT **65.4% → 76.4% (+11.0)** (and even exceeds the 75.2% pre-train baseline). On HypeNet-9B, NIAH-Single-2@256K recovers **9.40% → 19.6%** and NIAH-Single-3@256K **22.8% → 42.6% (+19.8)**; HypeNet-2B NIAH-Single-3@256K improves **10.6% → 30.2% (+19.6)**. Under OpenThoughts-3 CoT-SFT, HypeNet-9B NIAH-Single-3@256K recovers **40.6% → 61.2%**.
- **QK-Restore preserves reasoning.** On HypeNet-5B, MATH500 Avg@16 changes only **96.6 → 97.0** and GSM8K Avg@16 **90.6 → 90.2** (−0.4) after QK-Restore, i.e., the recovered recall comes at ~0.4-point math cost. On HypeNet-2B, MATH500 Avg@16 is essentially unchanged (47.4 → 56.2, an *increase*), and AIME24 Maj@16 even rises **0.0 → 3.3**.
- **Failure is CoT-specific.** Non-CoT instruction SFT (Tulu-3) does *not* degrade retrieval: HypeNet-5B NIAH-Single-3@256K improves 75.2 → 83.8 and HypeNet-9B 52.0 → 86.8, while IFEval jumps (e.g., HypeNet-5B 12.0 → 26.6). This isolates the structured locality of CoT traces as the cause, not post-training per se.
- **Generalizes to code CoT.** OpenThoughts-3 SFT lifts LiveCodeBench-V5 (HypeNet-5B 0.27 → 6.37; HypeNet-9B 1.66 → 13.11) but still degrades long NIAH — QK-Restore recovers most retrieval while *further* improving HypeNet-5B coding to 6.92.
- **Ablations confirm the WQ/WK specificity.** V-Restore (restoring WV) fails to recover NIAH and degrades MATH500; Q-Restore and K-Restore alone give only partial recovery (e.g., K-Restore collapses NIAH-Single-3 to 13.2 vs 30.8 for joint QK-Restore); QK-Frozen (freezing pre-SFT WQ/WK during SFT) underperforms post-hoc QK-Restore on both retrieval and math — unconstrained SFT lets WV adapt better. QK-Pro scores highest on MATH500 (34.2) while trading some routing recovery.
- **Attention-map evidence.** On HypeNet-5B Layer-33 (the most affected layer, 25/32 heads shift shorter), QK-Restore restores off-diagonal long-range attention that post-SFT suppressed — matching the locality prediction.

## Critical Assessment

- **Recall task is narrow (NIAH only).** All long-context claims rest on the synthetic Needle-In-A-Haystack family; the paper does not evaluate on natural long-context tasks (multi-document QA, long-code understanding, RAG). NIAH-Single is a single-fact retrieval probe, so "long-context capability restored" is demonstrated only for the simplest recall pattern.
- **QK-Restore sometimes under-recovers severely degraded cells.** On HypeNet-9B NIAH-Single-2@256K, recovery is only 9.40 → 19.6 — still far below the 67.2 pre-train value — and on NIAH-Single-3@128K only 17.4 → 44.0 vs 72.4 pre-train, so the fix is partial at the largest scale rather than a full restoration.
- **Theory relies on strong assumptions.** The Gradient-Locality theorem assumes a 1-step reversible ergodic Markov chain; the authors concede CoT is not generally reversible and fall back to singular-value bounds, but the spectral-gap value ρ is never measured on the latent chain (only the token-autocorrelation proxy is fit), so the predicted decay constant W is not directly validated against the measured gradient decay constant.
- **Procrustes variant is under-explored.** QK-Pro is introduced and shown to maximize MATH500, but no sweep over the routing/ reasoning trade-off parameter or sensitivity analysis is provided, so practitioners cannot tune it confidently.
- **Scope limited to hybrid (distilled) models.** Whether the same WQ/WK-locality mechanism affects standard full-softmax Transformers (or RLHF/DPO rather than SFT) is not tested; the phenomenon may be specific to architectures where a *small* set of softmax layers carries all long-range recall.

## Strengths

- Identifies a genuine, previously-unknown failure mode of a ubiquitous procedure (CoT-SFT) on a timely architecture class (hybrid linear attention), with a clean controlled contrast (Tulu-3 non-CoT SFT) that pinpoints CoT locality as the cause.
- The fix is trivial to implement (a parameter copy) and zero-training-cost, lowering the barrier to adoption.
- Combines theory (Gradient-Locality + Routing-Extraction Decoupling), empirical gradient/autocorrelation fits, attention-map visualization, and component ablations (Q-/K-/V-Restore, QK-Frozen) — a multi-angle mechanistic case rather than a single empirical patch.
- Cross-architecture (HypeNet, Jet-Nemotron), cross-scale (2B–9B), and cross-domain (math + code) evidence makes the core claim robust.

## Limitations

- Long-context evaluation is restricted to synthetic NIAH; no real-world long-context benchmarks.
- Partial recovery at 9B on the hardest/longest cells leaves a large residual gap to pre-train performance.
- The Markov/reversibility assumptions are idealizations; the key spectral parameter is only indirectly validated.
- No investigation of interaction with RLHF/DPO/RL post-training, only SFT.
- QK-Pro trade-off parameter is not swept or sensitivity-analyzed.

## Relevance

映射到用户兴趣区 **(1) LLM Training Stability & Observability**：本文诊断的是后训练（CoT-SFT）阶段一种"静默退化"——长程召回能力被悄悄破坏，属于训练稳定性的可观测失效模式。其 WQ/WK 局部化漂移的机制也触及 **(3) LLM Hidden States & Interpretability**（对注意力路由几何的机理分析），而"标准训练流程悄悄破坏关键能力"这一点与 **(6) Silent Data Corruption (SDC)** 的精神相通——一种训练过程中的静默能力退化。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the same WQ/WK locality-biased drift occur during RLHF/DPO/RL post-training (the paper only studies SFT), and would QK-Restore still be applicable after preference optimization?
- The authors measure only the token-autocorrelation proxy for the spectral radius ρ; can the latent-chain spectral gap be estimated directly to quantitatively validate the predicted decay horizon W = −1/log ρ against the measured W_grad?
- QK-Restore leaves a large residual gap on the hardest 9B cells (e.g., NIAH-S2@256K 19.6 vs 67.2 pre-train) — would combining QK-Restore with a small amount of long-context retrieval replay close that gap, and at what reasoning cost?
- The phenomenon is demonstrated only on NIAH; does CoT-SFT-induced routing drift also degrade natural long-context tasks (multi-document QA, long-code comprehension) where QK-Restore would be needed?
- The paper's contrast shows non-CoT (Tulu-3) SFT preserves retrieval — can a locality-regularized CoT-SFT objective (penalizing short-range-only gradient concentration) prevent the drift in the first place, making post-hoc QK-Restore unnecessary?
