In Defense of Information Leakage in Concept-based Models
Mateo Espinosa Zarlenga · University of Oxford (Trinity College) · arXiv:2606.10669 · 2026-06-09

Some information leakage in concept-based models is benign—and becomes necessary when real-world concept sets are incomplete.

Key Metric
L_int = E[−log P(y | g(x | C:=c))] Sufficiency: I(Y;R|C) ≈ I(Y;X|C)  ·  Localization: I(Y;C̄ᵢ|Rᵢ,Ĉ₋ᵢ) ≈ I(Y;Cᵢ|C₋ᵢ)
Why This Matters
Reframes concept-model design from eliminate leakage to control and exploit leakage—the shift required to deploy CMs in real-world settings where concept annotations are inherently incomplete.
Critical Edge
Author has a vested interest (designed CEMs). Empirical scope is narrow—mostly CUB. The benign-leakage decomposition (C̄ᵢ, Rᵢ) may not always be identifiable in practice. POSITION / ANALYSIS PAPER — no new method, no optimal "how much".

Methodology

5-step analysis

A general CBM framework is established with formal definitions of concept encoders, label predictors, scoring functions, and interventions. Two leakage forms—inter-concept and task—are formalized via mutual information.

Three categories of anti-leakage arguments are then systematically rebutted before the positive case is made. Empirical work on the CUB dataset compares six CBM families as the concept set becomes progressively incomplete.

Theoretical Framework

Definition 5.1

Anti-leakage arguments (intervenability, interpretability, safety) implicitly assume concept completeness—a premise that rarely holds outside curated benchmarks.

When concepts are incomplete, the bottleneck discards information the task actually needs. Controlled leakage preserves it, and the intervention loss L_int is the architecture-agnostic objective that achieves it.

Strengths

contribution side
  • Courageous contrarian position backed by rigorous formalization
  • Benign-leakage definition (sufficiency + localization) gives actionable design criteria
  • L_int is an elegant, architecture-agnostic training objective
  • Clear exposition of why concept incompleteness undermines the anti-leakage narrative

Limitations

scope side
  • Empirical evaluation is limited primarily to CUB; medical/clinical CMs would strengthen the case
  • Decomposition (C̄ᵢ, Rᵢ) assumes it exists—verifying it in practice may be difficult
  • Safety concerns (shortcut learning, distribution shift) are somewhat dismissed as "general DNN problems"
  • Position / analysis paper—no practical guidance on "how much" leakage is optimal

Questions for Further Exploration

5 open threads
  1. How can we verify in practice that a concept model's leakage is "benign" (satisfies both sufficiency and localization) rather than harmful?
  2. What is the optimal trade-off between leakage amount and intervenability across domains (medical imaging, autonomous driving, ...)?
  3. Can benign leakage be combined with recent label-free concept discovery pipelines to build more practical CMs?
  4. How does benign leakage interact with distribution shift—are the safety concerns dismissed too quickly?
  5. Is there a quantitative characterization of "how much" leakage is needed as a function of concept incompleteness?
