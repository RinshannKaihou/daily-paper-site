Anchor fact — error rate
η + Õ(Δ1/3/γ)
Information-theoretic optimum is Δ1/2. The polynomial-time algorithm provably cannot reach it — the Δ1/3 rate is both achieved (Algorithm 1) and unavoidable (Theorem 4.1).
Why it matters — in one breath
A clean information-computation gap for noisy drift learning.
Efficient algorithms are forced to pay the “agnostic price” (Δ1/3) even in realizable settings — a crisp separation between statistical and computational optimality that anchors the design of real-world drift-adaptive systems.
Critical edge — caveat to read first
Hardness is conditional on the low-degree conjecture, and the Ω(Δ−2/3) epoch length means the learner cannot react to sudden large shifts.

Methodology — DriftedMassart

§ 03

Online rounds t = 1…T over a drifting distribution D(t) on Sd−1 with dTV(D(t), D(t+1)) ≤ Δ, target is a γ-margin halfspace, labels corrupted by η-Massart noise.

i
Epoch schedule. Length W = 2⌊Δ−2/3 log(Δ−1)⌋; hypothesis updated only at epoch boundaries.
ii
DriftPerceptron (Alg. 2). Split the epoch buffer into S₁ (gradients) and S₂ (selection). Leaky-ReLU-style gradient g(w;x,y) = ((1−2η)sign(w·x)−y)x / max{|w·x|,γ}.
iii
Hypothesis selection. Pick the iterate minimizing empirical error on S₂; Hoeffding concentration transfers the choice across the epoch.
iv
Regret-to-error (Lemma 3.1). The geometric change ‖w*(i)−w*(i+m)‖ can be large even when TV drift is small — control prediction error via expected regret plus a drift term O(TΔ/γ), bypassing direct weight tracking.
v
RCN variant. Binary-search the noise level η* inside each epoch to reach optT + Õ(Δ1/3/γ).

Key insight — under Massart/RCN noise, efficient algorithms pay the agnostic Δ1/3 price rather than the realizable Δ1/2 rate. One unified framework produces three results.

Strengths

+
A complete story — information-theoretic baseline, efficient algorithm, and matching computational lower bound, all in one paper.
  • Regret-to-error technique (Lemma 3.1) is elegant and likely portable to other drift settings.
  • Three algorithmic results (Massart, RCN, realizable) from a single epoch framework.
  • Lower-bound construction is clever: drift signal is embedded only in the last m = γ−1/6Δ−2/3 rounds, then RCN-masked.
  • No strong distributional assumptions beyond the standard margin — broadly applicable inside the theory.

Limitations

−
A purely theoretical result whose computational story leans on a conjecture and whose warm-up horizon is unforgiving.
  • Margin γ is required. Without it, even non-drifting Massart halfspace learning is computationally hard.
  • Epoch length Ω(Δ−2/3) → no fast reaction to sudden large distribution shifts.
  • No empirical evaluation on synthetic or real data — bounds only.
  • Bounded-TV drift is a simplification; real concept drift is often structured.
  • RCN variant pays a multiplicative overhead for binary-searching η*.
  • Hardness is low-degree-conditional; SQ analogue is open.

Questions for further exploration

§ 06
Can the regret-to-error lemma extend to broader function classes — intersections of halfspaces, or shallow neural networks?
Under structured covariates (e.g. log-concave) where agnostic halfspace learning is tractable without drift, does the Δ1/3 wall survive?
Is there an SQ analogue of the low-degree lower bound — a step toward cryptography-based hardness?
Can the epoch schedule be combined with adaptive drift estimation to remove the need to know Δ in advance?
How does Algorithm 1 behave empirically on synthetic drifting streams vs. adaptive online methods (Ader, dynamic-regret minimizers)?
Can the information–computation gap be closed under stronger cryptographic assumptions?
