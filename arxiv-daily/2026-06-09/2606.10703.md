> **一句话总结：** 本文用token级路由感知消融审计三种高冗余MoE模型（OLMoE-1B-7B、Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B、DeepSeek-V2-Lite）的四种观测性剪枝指标（利用率、激活范数、激活std、激活时平均路由权重），发现没有任何观测指标在多重比较校正后能预测专家的因果重要性（全部60个metric-layer组合的效应量Cohen's d<0.17），现有剪枝方法的成功源于早期层冗余而非指标识别出可丢弃专家。

> **TL;DR:** The authors audit whether four observational pruning metrics (utilization rate, activation norm, activation std, mean routing weight when active) predict token-level causal expert importance across three high-redundancy MoE models (OLMoE-1B-7B-0924, Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B, DeepSeek-V2-Lite) using router-aware per-token ablation on WikiText-2. Across all 60 metric-layer cells, no observational metric reaches Bonferroni-corrected significance (αadj=0.0025), with effect sizes below Cohen's d=0.17. A per-token routing-weight control recovers only one signal (OLMoE Layer 15, d=+0.231, p=0.0013), ruling out insufficient power. Progressive ablation shows removing 7 of 8 active experts at OLMoE layers 0–9 leaves mean loss change below +0.083 nats — explaining why metric-guided pruning "succeeds" via early-layer redundancy rather than expert selection.

## 核心贡献

- **观测到干预的因果审计框架**：在Pearl因果层级中，将MoE剪枝文献用观测性路由统计预测干预结果的隐含假设，形式化为"rung-1到rung-2的推断"，并提供一个直接的可证伪测试（指标有效性定义2.1）。
- **三模型一致零结果**：在跨主要设计维度的三种高冗余MoE架构上，60个metric-layer单元无一在校正后达到显著，效应量均低于Cohen's d=0.17，符号跨层不一致——典型的零分布模式而非弱真效应。
- **排除统计功效不足**：per-token路由权重对照实验用相同机制和相同n，恢复出OLMoE第15层的唯一显著信号（d=+0.231, p=0.0013），证明零结果不是功效问题。
- **用冗余机制解释剪枝成功**：渐进消融显示OLMoE第0-9层移除8个活跃专家中的7个仍使平均损失变化<+0.083 nats，说明任意选择准则在这些层都看起来无害——现有剪枝方法的成功不可归因于指标识别出可丢弃专家。
- **OLMoE末层效应的局部存在性证明**：唯一存活信号是OLMoE最后一层，伴随gap norm从第0层的0.0041增至第15层的0.1697（41倍），但Qwen有可比较的gap-norm增长却无功能集中，排除了残差流增长作为充分解释。

## Key Contributions

- **Observational-to-interventional causal audit framework**: Formalizes the MoE pruning literature's implicit assumption — that population-averaged routing statistics (utilization, activation norm, etc.) predict token-level interventional expert importance — as a Pearl hierarchy rung-1→rung-2 inference, and gives it a directly testable Definition 2.1 (metric validity).
- **Three-model uniform observational null**: Across 60 metric-layer cells (4 metrics × 5 layers × 3 models), no observational metric reaches Bonferroni-corrected significance (αadj=0.0025); effect sizes stay below Cohen's d=0.17 with sign inconsistencies across layers.
- **Power ruled out via per-token routing-weight control**: Identical machinery and identical n applied to a token-conditioned quantity recovers one Bonferroni-significant signal (OLMoE Layer 15, d=+0.231, p=0.0013), proving the null is not a power artifact.
- **Redundancy mechanism explains pruning success**: Progressive ablation on OLMoE shows removing 7 of 8 active experts at layers 0–9 leaves mean loss change below +0.083 nats; layer 15 breaks at k=2 (mean ∆L=+0.155 nats) and reaches +0.431 at k=7. Early-layer redundancy renders most selection criteria interchangeable.
- **Localized existence proof of a per-token predictor**: The sole surviving signal is OLMoE's final MoE layer, where gap norm grows 41× (0.0041→0.1697); Qwen shows comparable gap-norm growth without functional concentration, ruling out residual-stream scaling alone as sufficient.

## Methodology

**Audit protocol (Definition 2.1).** An observational metric m is causally valid at the token level if, for token position t with active expert set At, higher m(e) among e∈At predicts larger functional importance ∆L_e^(t). For each metric-layer-model cell, n=200 token positions are sampled from WikiText-2 test split. At each position the active routed-expert set is identified, ranked by the target metric, the highest-ranked expert ablated (∆L_high), then the lowest-ranked ablated (∆L_low). Validity implies the paired difference ∆L_high − ∆L_low is reliably positive. Tested with paired t-test (Cohen's d effect size) and Wilcoxon signed-rank as distribution-free check; cells where t and Wilcoxon disagree are treated as noise.

**Functional importance.** For expert i active at token t, causal contribution measured via ablation: y_t^(−i) = layer output with E_i replaced by zero; functional importance ∆L_i^(t) = L_t − L_t^(−i), where L_t = −log p_θ(x_{t+1}|x_{≤t}). Also reports gap norm δ_i^(t) = ‖y_t − y_t^(−i)‖₂ (residual-stream displacement regardless of output change).

**Models.** Three high-redundancy MoE LMs: OLMoE-1B-7B-0924 (16 layers, 64 experts, top-8, no shared expert, α=0.01), Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B (24 layers, 60 routed + 1 shared, top-4, α=0.001), DeepSeek-V2-Lite (27 layers with layer 0 dense, 64 routed + 2 shared, top-6, multi-objective load balancing). Top-k activation ratios span 6.7%–12.5%. Shared experts in Qwen/DeepSeek are never modified.

**Audited layers (5 per model, proportional to depth).** OLMoE: L0, L4, L7, L11, L15; Qwen: L0, L6, L12, L18, L23; DeepSeek: L1, L7, L13, L20, L26. Yields 20 cells/model, 60 total. Bonferroni correction per model: αadj=0.05/20=0.0025 (audit) and 0.05/5=0.01 (routing-weight control over 5 layers).

**Routing-weight control.** Same protocol but with token-conditioned g_i(x_t) (the routing weight at the specific token under evaluation) in place of the population-averaged observational metric.

**Progressive ablation.** On OLMoE, removes k highest-weight active experts at a target layer, records cumulative loss change (n=500 per layer-k cell, layers {0,1,7,8,9,15}, k∈{1,…,7}).

## Results & Highlights

- **Observational null (all 3 models)**: No observational metric reaches Bonferroni-corrected p<0.0025 at any tested layer in any model. Effect sizes stay below Cohen's d=0.17 across all 60 metric-layer combinations, with sign inconsistencies across layers.
- **OLMoE per-cell detail**: 19/20 cells non-significant at uncorrected p<0.05; the one cell at p_t=0.048 (activation std, Layer 11, d=+0.141) reverses sign at Layer 15 (d=−0.020).
- **Activation norm (Jaiswal et al.'s strongest of 16 criteria)**: |d|≤0.157 at every tested layer in every model; never reaches Bonferroni significance.
- **Routing-weight control — the sole survivor**: OLMoE Layer 15, d=+0.231, p=0.0013 (survives αadj=0.01). Qwen stays within |d|≤0.124 across all five layers; DeepSeek within |d|≤0.098.
- **Progressive ablation (OLMoE)**: At layers 0–9, mean loss change stays below +0.083 nats at k=7 (removing 7 of 8 active experts). Layer 15 breaks at k=2 (mean ∆L=+0.155 nats) and reaches +0.431 at k=7.
- **Gap-norm scaling (OLMoE)**: Mean residual-stream displacement under single-expert ablation grows from 0.0041 (Layer 0) to 0.1697 (Layer 15), a 41× increase; Qwen shows comparable growth without functional concentration.

## Critical Assessment

- **The audit tests only single-expert ablation at one token position, whereas deployed pruning makes one-shot global decisions and recovers through fine-tuning.** The authors are explicit that the token-level null does not directly falsify the deployed pipeline, but it removes the per-token mechanism that pipeline assumes — a scope boundary that limits the practical blow to pruning.
- **All three audited models are high-redundancy (top-k 6.7%–12.5%).** Whether observational metrics regain validity in low-redundancy architectures (Switch-style, Mixtral-8x7B) remains open; the authors note the literature's reported pruning successes on those architectures may be partly attributable to the regime in which metrics were originally developed. So the null is regime-scoped.
- **n=200 per cell is modest.** While the routing-weight control rules out power as the explanation, the cell-level estimates carry uncertainty; the Wilcoxon/t agreement rule treats borderline cells as noise, which is conservative but means near-threshold effects could be real.
- **The OLMoE Layer 15 signal is an existence proof, not a deployable predictor.** g_i(x_t) requires the forward pass to that layer at that token, so it cannot be computed before inference — it is not a counterexample to the broader null for pruning purposes. Three architectures are also too few to attribute the OLMoE-specific pattern to any single training/design factor.
- **The paper uses null-hypothesis testing, which measures evidence against a metric's validity, not evidence in favor of any competing account.** The authors themselves note a Bayesian comparison (Bayes factors / IC model selection) would be the natural next step to quantify relative support — a limitation they acknowledge rather than paper over.

## Strengths

- Clean causal framing via Pearl's hierarchy: makes the implicit observational→interventional inference in the pruning literature explicit and falsifiable.
- Pre-registered-style rigor: Bonferroni correction per model, dual test (t + Wilcoxon) agreement rule, and a power-ruling control experiment with identical machinery.
- Cross-architecture breadth spanning the major MoE design dimensions (single vs multi-objective load balancing, top-k ratios, scratch vs upcycled, shared experts).
- Honest redundancy-mechanism explanation that reconciles the null with the literature's reported pruning gains rather than dismissing them.
- Released code and per-cell appendix tables enable direct replication.

## Limitations

- Token-level, single-expert, single-position audit does not directly test the deployed pruning pipeline (global one-shot decisions + fine-tuning recovery).
- All three models are high-redundancy; low-redundancy architectures untested.
- n=200 per cell; near-threshold effects treated as noise under the dual-test agreement rule.
- The sole surviving OLMoE Layer 15 signal requires a per-token forward pass, so it is not a deployable pre-inference predictor.
- Three architectures insufficient to attribute the OLMoE-specific late-layer pattern to any training/design cause.

## Relevance

本文直接映射到「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」研究兴趣——审计观测性可解释性指标（路由统计）能否支持token级干预性结论，属于机制可解释性的因果有效性问题。同时也与「ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」相关：用Pearl因果层级框架检验可解释性方法的推断有效性，揭示观测-干预推断失效的结构性模式。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The authors explicitly note (§5) that a Bayesian comparison of metric models (Bayes factors / information-criterion model selection) would quantify relative support each criterion receives — has this been run, and does it corroborate the null-hypothesis-testing conclusion?
- Does the observational null hold in low-redundancy architectures (Switch-style, Mixtral-8x7B), where top-k activation ratios are higher and early-layer redundancy may be smaller — the open question the authors flag in §5?
- The OLMoE Layer 15 signal coincides with a 41× gap-norm scaling, yet Qwen shows comparable scaling without functional concentration — what architectural/training factor (the paper cites checkpoint variation as the follow-up) dissociates the two?
- The audit ablates one expert at one position; does the null persist under multi-expert or multi-position ablation, which is closer to what deployed pruning actually does?
- The progressive-ablation redundancy buffer (removing 7/8 experts at layers 0–9 leaves ∆L<0.083 nats) suggests metric-vs-random interchangeability — would a random-expert baseline match metric-guided pruning end-to-end after fine-tuning recovery, as the redundancy argument predicts?
