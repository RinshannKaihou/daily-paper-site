WorldKernel: A World Model is the Coupling Kernel of Admissible Possible Worlds
Fabio Rovai · 9 Jun 2026
Key Metric
28%
of 300 structural causal models receive infeasible counterfactual outputs from a strong language-model predictor — predictors succeed on identified quantities, fail on couplings.
Why This Matters

Predictors structurally fail on counterfactuals; the PSD kernel gives polynomial-time partial-identification bounds with an explicit computational barrier (the Sly–Sun threshold).

Critical Edge

The "strong language model" baseline is unspecified — a significant omission behind the headline 28% infeasibility claim about predictor failure.

Methodology · Five Questions on One Object

§ Method
Q1 · IS IT REAL
Predictive insufficiency
Two states with identical posteriors can differ on cross-world queries (Prop. 2). In a "permissive-lava" arena, an unshielded planner enters forbidden regions 36.4% of the time; the diagonal projector commits zero violations across 250 episodes.
Q2 · CAN WE BOUND IT
PSD relaxation
For PN, marginals P(Y₀), P(Y₁) are identifiable from rung-1/2 data, but the coupling c = P(Y₀=1, Y₁=1) is not. The 2×2 PSD constraint M ⪰ 0 restricts c to [max(0, r₀+r₁−1), min(r₀, r₁)] — tighter than marginals alone, in polynomial time.
Q3 · DOES STRUCTURE HELP
Ontology axioms
Domain axioms (Open Ontologies) enter as linear constraints on kernel entries. They propagate through PSD to tighten bounds on couplings the axioms never directly mention — leverage that flat causal-bounds tools cannot exploit.
Q4 · CAN WE ACQUIRE IT
Targeted scarring
When a trajectory violates a constraint, the violation localises the responsible constraint (a "blood-localised" scar). Targeted scars close the identification gap 4× faster than random ones, with a Cheeger spectral-gap-restoration guarantee (Thm. 5).
Q5 · WHEN IS IT REACHABLE
Sly–Sun threshold
Admissible worlds of a disjointness ontology ↔ independent sets of a graph. Kernel normaliser Z_G is a partition function. Below degree 5: tractable via Weitz correlation decay. Above degree 6: inapproximable unless NP = RP. Order parameter (d−1)η crosses 1 at the transition.

Strengths & Limitations

§ Verdict

Strengths

  • Elegant unification — one PSD kernel separates prediction (diagonal) from counterfactual reasoning (off-diagonal).
  • The Predictive Insufficiency Theorem (Thm. 2) is clean and persuasive.
  • PSD bounding is practically useful: polynomial-time bounds matching exact programs at tractable scales.
  • The Sly–Sun threshold gives an honest, sharp computational boundary.
  • Targeted scarring with spectral-gap restoration (Thm. 5) is a clever online-learning move.
  • The five-question structure organises a multi-domain argument into one navigable spine.

Limitations

  • Extremely dense, theory-heavy — the path from kernel theory to deployed systems is unclear.
  • Limited empirical scale: 300-SCM "battery" and lava arena are illustrative, not comprehensive.
  • Language-model baseline unspecified — a significant omission for a 28%-infeasibility claim.
  • Single author across causal inference, complexity, ontology, spectral graph theory, and approximate counting — breadth/depth tradeoff.
  • Generative use of the kernel (sampling admissible worlds) is explicitly left to future work.
  • No comparison with existing partial-identification tools (DoWhy, Ananke) or causal-bounds software.
  • Literary style ("worst-case bound bleeds on," "blood-localised cutting planes") can obscure technical content.
