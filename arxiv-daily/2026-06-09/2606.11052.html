Critical Edge

Caveat

Methodology

The authors study hybrid linear-attention models (linear attention for efficiency, softmax attention for accuracy) and find that CoT-SFT causes attention amnesia — a dramatic collapse of long-context retrieval.

The diagnosis: CoT-SFT shifts attention gradients toward short-range patterns, corrupting the WQ and WK projections that handle long-range routing in linear-attention layers.

The fix — QK-Restore — is post-hoc parameter surgery: after CoT-SFT, swap just WQ and WK back to their pre-SFT values, leaving every other trained parameter intact. A Procrustes-alignment variant interpolates pre/post WQ/WK via optimal rotation, trading routing preservation against reasoning adaptation.

Evaluated on Needle-in-a-Haystack (NIAH) at up to 256K tokens across difficulty S1–S3, plus standard reasoning benchmarks.

Strengths

  • Identifies a previously unknown failure mode of a widely-used training procedure
  • Embarrassingly simple fix — a two-matrix swap with zero added training
  • Mechanistic insight, not just empirical patching: gradient analysis pinpoints WQ/WK
  • Validated across multiple architectures and scales (5B–9B)
  • Procrustes variant gives principled interpolation instead of a hard swap

Limitations

  • Scope: hybrid linear-attention only — standard transformers unverified
  • Gradient analysis is suggestive but doesn't rule out other contributing factors
  • No investigation of RLHF / DPO / other post-training interactions
  • Procrustes interpolation hyperparameter sensitivity not deeply analyzed
  • Long-context eval is NIAH-only — no real document QA or code-understanding tasks

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does CoT-SFT cause similar attention degradation in standard full-softmax transformers — just masked by softmax's stronger inductive bias?
  2. Can QK-Restore be applied after RLHF / DPO, or do those methods modify WQ/WK in beneficial ways?
  3. Is there a regularization term that could prevent this degradation during SFT itself, rather than requiring post-hoc repair?
  4. How does the Procrustes interpolation parameter affect downstream task performance beyond NIAH?
  5. Does the severity of attention amnesia correlate with the length or complexity of CoT chains used during SFT?
