Why this matters
IDS papers routinely overstate Transformer gains through leaky evaluation; deployment-grade benchmarks need leakage-free splits to be trusted.

Critical edge
The Transformer's strong non-padded performance could reflect residual within-conversation leakage rather than genuine temporal reasoning.

01 Methodology

CIC-IDS2017 is reformulated as a genuine ordered flow-sequence task by grouping records into bidirectional network conversations — so sequence-modeling claims can be tested rather than assumed.

  • 9 models spanning classical ML (Random Forest, etc.) and deep learning (LSTM, GRU, 1D-CNN, Transformer, others).
  • 3 split protocols — conventional random plus two leakage-free group splits that prevent the same conversation from crossing train/test.
  • Padding ablation — repeat-last vs. zero-pad + mask, isolating the effect of padding convention from architecture.
  • Metrics — macro-F1 and false-alarm rate reported side by side to surface operational risk alongside headline accuracy.

03 Strengths

  • Multi-protocol design — isolates architecture, split, and padding as separate effects rather than confounding them.
  • Operationally meaningful metric — false-alarm rate reported alongside macro-F1, exposing deployment risk.
  • Falsifiable central claim — a testable thesis supported by controlled ablations.
  • Open-source protocol — code released for independent replication.

04 Limitations

  • Single dataset (CIC-IDS2017) — well-documented label and capture artifacts; findings may be partly dataset-specific.
  • No modern datasets — CIC-IDS2018/2019, UNSW-NB15, TON_IoT remain unaddressed.
  • No online / streaming setting — generalization to time-split, real-time IDS not discussed.
  • Residual leakage risk — non-padded Transformer gains could reflect within-conversation leakage, not temporal reasoning.

05 Questions for further exploration

Do the padding and split-protocol effects replicate on CIC-IDS2018/2019, UNSW-NB15, TON_IoT, CSE-CIC-2018?
Why is the Transformer uniquely sensitive to zero-pad+mask while recurrent and CNN models stay stable — attention dilution over padded positions?
How do the leakage-free findings transfer to true online / streaming IDS where conversations split across train and test in time?
Can attention attribution identify which sequential cues the Transformer exploits on non-padded windows, confirming genuine temporal reasoning?
Does the Random Forest's robustness survive adversarial perturbation and concept drift, not just leakage-free group splits?
