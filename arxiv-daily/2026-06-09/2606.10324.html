Methodology

方法

Setup

The simplest tractable architecture for the RG analogy: a pure MLP residual stack trained on masked token prediction over synthetic Markov-chain sequences with known spectral properties.

  • Architecture. Pure MLP residual stack (no attention, no convolutions).
  • Data. Synthetic Markov chains, correlation length ℓ ≈ 1 vs ℓ ≈ 7 — a ground truth for "relevant" vs "irrelevant" degrees of freedom.
  • Task. Masked token prediction.
  • Order parameter. Effective rank of the residual stream, measured position-level (controls for mean-pooling artifacts).
  • Flow probe. Inter-layer kernel drift to locate fixed-point plateaus.

Strengths & Limitations

优势 / 局限

Strengths

  • Clean experimental design with controlled spectral properties on synthetic data.
  • First quantitative (not merely qualitative) evidence for the RG–NN analogy.
  • Position-level analysis properly controls for mean-pooling artifacts.
  • Clear, testable predictions grounded in physics theory.

Limitations

  • Restricted to synthetic Markov-chain inputs with a simple MLP residual architecture.
  • No validation on real-world data or modern architectures (transformers, CNNs).
  • Masked token prediction, while controlled, is far from practical NLP or vision.
  • Trained networks are studied post-hoc; the training dynamics that produce the RG structure are not analyzed.

Questions for Further Exploration

延伸问题
  1. Does the selective rank collapse phenomenon generalize to transformer architectures with attention mechanisms — or is it specific to MLP residual stacks?
  2. Can the RG order parameter (effective rank) be used as a practical diagnostic for network design — e.g., to determine optimal depth?
  3. What happens to the RG structure when the network is trained on data with mixed correlation lengths or hierarchical structure?
  4. Is the discrete fixed-point plateau structure related to grokking or phase transitions observed during training?
