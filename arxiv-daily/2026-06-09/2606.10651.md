> **一句话总结：** 快手的 Keye-VL-2.0-30B-A3B 是一个仅激活3B参数的 MoE 多模态模型，首次把 DeepSeek 稀疏注意力（DSA）适配到 GQA 多模态骨干以实现256K无损长视频上下文，并用 Cross-Modal 多教师在线策略蒸馏（MOPD）+ Context-RL/Video-RL 在 TimeLens 时间定位和 LongVideoBench/Video-MME-v2 长视频理解上达到同规模 SOTA。

> **TL;DR:** Kwai's Keye-VL-2.0-30B-A3B (30B MoE, 3B active) is the first to port DeepSeek Sparse Attention to a GQA-based multimodal backbone for lossless 256K context. On LongVideoBench it scores **74.1** vs Qwen3.5-35B-A3B 61.6 and Qwen3-VL-235B-A22B 70.5; on Video-MME-v2 accuracy (512 frames) **42.4** vs Qwen3-VL-235B 36.8; and it takes best mIoU on all three TimeLens subsets (ActivityNet **58.5**, QVHighlights **70.1**, Charades **58.4**) vs Qwen3-VL-235B-A22B (52.1/64.6/47.8). DSA inference cuts prefill cost >3× and decode cost >5× vs full attention at 128K. MOPD consolidates 13 RL-trained domain teachers (math, OCR, grounding, counting, video, tool-use, etc.) via top-k overlap on-policy distillation.

## 核心贡献

- **首次将 DeepSeek 稀疏注意力（DSA）适配到 GQA 多模态骨干**：用 MQA 风格的 Lightning Indexer 做全局打分 + GQA 分组稀疏聚合，把核心注意力复杂度从 O(L²) 降到 O(L·k)（k=2048），支撑256K token无损上下文
- **跨模态多教师在线策略蒸馏（MOPD）**：维护13个RL训练的领域专家教师（数学/OCR/grounding/counting/video/tool-use等），通过动态路由 + Segmented Prompt-Response Re-tokenization + Top-k 重叠集估计，把异构能力无冲突地蒸馏回单一 MoE 学生，解决多任务对齐中的灾难性遗忘
- **四阶段预训练课程 + 多阶段后训练**：Stage 0 Projector初始化 → Stage 1 32K/1T token通用对齐 → Stage 2 64K/2T token多任务注入 → Stage 3 256K/500B token长上下文扩展；后训练含 SFT、合成数据RL、General RL（GSPO算法）、Specialized RL专家、Video-RL、Agentic-RL
- **DSA 训练/推理基础设施**：ViT-LM异构并行 + ExtraIO异步视频I/O + Chunk ViT + 相邻query Top-k去重（128K下16个相邻query仅需~8K有效KV）等优化，端到端吞吐提升约20%，DSA内核相对开源基线>2×加速
- **同规模模型SOTA**：在TimeLens三个子集mIoU、LongVideoBench、Video-MME-v2准确率上领先开源同规模及更大模型

## Key Contributions

- **First DSA adaptation to a GQA-based multimodal backbone**: An MQA-style Lightning Indexer computes global index scores (ReLU-based, FP8) to select a Top-k=2048 sparse index set per query; the same index set is then applied across all GQA groups for sparse aggregation, reducing core attention from O(L²) to O(L·k) and enabling lossless 256K context
- **Cross-Modal Multi-Teacher On-Policy Distillation (MOPD)**: Maintains 13 RL-trained domain teachers; each student on-policy rollout is routed to the best-matching teacher, which gives token-level feedback via a Top-k overlap advantage estimator (Eq. 13–16) plus token-category-aware scaling and localized repetition penalty — consolidating heterogeneous capabilities without forcing a single response style
- **Four-stage pre-training curriculum** (Stage 0 projector init → Stage 1 32K/1T tokens → Stage 2 64K/2T tokens multi-task → Stage 3 256K/500B tokens long-context) plus a multi-track post-training stack (SFT with Synthetic CoT, Synthetic-Data RL, General RL via GSPO, Specialized domain experts, Video-RL, Agentic-RL for code/tool/search)
- **DSA training and inference infrastructure**: ViT–LM heterogeneous parallelism with a recompute-or-offload strategy, ExtraIO asynchronous video I/O, a dual-queue async pipeline that boosts production throughput 3–5×, and FlashInfer/TileLang DSA kernels giving >2× speedup over the open-source baseline; inference uses Chunk ViT, adjacent-query Top-k deduplication (16 adjacent queries need only ~8K effective KV at 128K), and DSA decode optimizations
- **State-of-the-art at its scale**: Best mIoU on all three TimeLens subsets, best accuracy on LongVideoBench and Video-MME-v2, competitive on MLVU/Video-MME, and strong on code (LiveCodeBench v6 64.2, SWE-bench Verified 62.0) and tool-use (τ²-Bench 82.6, VitaBench 33.1) benchmarks

## Methodology

**Architecture.** Four components: (1) a native-resolution ViT inherited from Keye-VL-1.5 (SigLIP-400M-384-14 backbone, with interpolated adaptive position embeddings + 2D RoPE + NaViT Patch-n'-Pack sequence packing), pre-trained on 500B tokens with the SigLIP loss aligned to the SigLIP text tower on DataComp/LAION/CC12M/PD12M/COCO plus internal data; (2) a Qwen3-30B-A3B-Thinking-2507 LLM decoder (MoE, 3B active); (3) a randomly-initialized MLP projector trained in Stage 0; (4) a GQA-compatible DSA module. Visual encoding is unified for images and video: each video frame is treated as an independent high-resolution image with a prepended natural-language timestamp, and an adaptive pixel budget scales per-video resolution by duration (256s/512s/1024s/2048s thresholds → 0.125/0.25/0.5/1.0 scaling factors).

**DSA design.** The Lightning Indexer follows MQA key-sharing, computing global scores I_{t,s}=Σ_j w^I_{t,j}·ReLU(q^I_{t,j}·k^I_s) over H^I indexer heads, then selects Ω_t=Top-k(I_{t,:}). The GQA backbone (G groups) applies the same sparse index set: u_{t,g}=Attn(h_{t,g},{c_{s,g}|s∈Ω_t}), with k=2048. Training uses a two-stage **dense warm-up → sparse adaptation**: warm-up (≈2B multimodal tokens) aligns the indexer's distribution to the dense GQA attention via KL loss L_warmup=Σ_tΣ_g DKL(p_{t,:,g}‖Softmax(I_{t,:})); then sparse adaptation trains all parameters with next-token loss plus a KL computed only over the selected Top-k set (the indexer input is detached to reduce gradient interference), giving L_total=L_NTP+λ·L^I_sparse.

**Pre-training curriculum (Table 1 video setup).** Stage 1: 15s clips, 24K video tokens, ~1T total tokens; Stage 2: 15min, 64K video tokens, ~2T tokens, injects OCR/STEM/GUI/grounding/counting/coding/tool-use; Stage 3: 2h max video, 180K video tokens, 256K context, 500B tokens, long video/document/code/agent trajectories mixed 1:1 long:short. Data cleaning uses Hash+CLIP joint deduplication; a dual-queue async pipeline improves production throughput 3–5×.

**Post-training.** SFT on ~500B tokens (~40% text-only) with Synthetic CoT (teacher-generated reasoning traces filtered at query/response/process level, plus a Doubt2Clean second-pass over 27 math datasets). RL uses **Group Sequence Policy Optimization (GSPO)** with sequence-level importance ratios and group-normalized advantages. Tracks include Synthetic-Data RL (procedurally-generated image-difference tasks with rule-based rewards), General RL (verifiable-answer tasks, ContextRL reward based on Qwen2.5-VL-72B-Instruct judge), Specialized RL (grounding/spatial/math/counting/OCR experts with deterministic rewards), Video-RL (~31K samples, tIoU reward for TVG, LLM-as-judge for dense captioning, FrameForge synthetic videos), and Agentic-RL (code via Online Judge + SWE-bench containerized repos; tool-use over 150 simulated API domains; multi-turn search). **MOPD** then consolidates 13 RL teachers via the top-k overlap advantage A_{i,t}=Σ_{v∈Ω} π̄_θ(v|s)·[log π^r_T(v|s)−log π_θ(v|s)] with token-category scaling and localized repetition penalty after collapse point τ_i.

## Results & Highlights

- **LongVideoBench (Table 2)**: Keye-VL-2.0 **74.1** vs Qwen3.5-35B-A3B **61.6**, Qwen3-VL-235B-A22B Thinking **70.5**, InternVL3.5-241B-A28B **67.1** — best among all compared open- and closed-source models.
- **Video-MME-v2 accuracy (64 / 512 frames)**: Keye-VL-2.0 **35.3 / 42.4** vs Qwen3-VL-235B-A22B Thinking **33.3 / 36.8** and Qwen3.5-35B-A3B **32.6 / 28.5**; Non-Linear score **18.5 / 24.2** vs Qwen3-VL-235B **26.3 / 28.1** (Qwen3-VL higher on the non-linear metric).
- **TimeLens temporal grounding mIoU (Table 2)**: Keye-VL-2.0 best on all three — ActivityNet **58.5** (vs Qwen3-VL-235B 52.1, Qwen3.5 53.2), QVHighlights **70.1** (vs 64.6 / 65.7), Charades **58.4** (vs 47.8 / 49.1).
- **Video-MMMU knowledge acquisition**: **80.0**, matching Qwen3-VL-235B-A22B Thinking (80.0) and Qwen3.5-35B-A3B (80.4), approaching GPT-5-mini (82.5); InternVL3.5-241B only 75.0.
- **Code agents (Table 3)**: LiveCodeBench v6 **64.2** (vs Qwen3.5 62.8, GPT-5-mini 51.5), OJBench **71.5** (vs Qwen3.5 70.2, GPT-5-mini 58.7), SWE-bench Verified **62.0** (vs Qwen3.5 63.5, GPT-5-mini 55.5).
- **Tool use (Table 4)**: τ²-Bench **82.6** (best; vs Qwen3.5 81.2, GPT-5-mini 69.8), VitaBench **33.1** (best; vs Qwen3.5 31.9, GPT-5-mini 13.9), BFCL-V4 **65.7** (second to Qwen3.5 67.3).
- **MLVU / Video-MME (w/o sub)**: **82.8 / 78.3** — competitive but not leading (Qwen3.5 85.6/82.5, Qwen3-VL-235B 83.8/79.0 lead these mature benchmarks).
- **DSA inference efficiency (Figure 4 + §5.3)**: At 128K context on H800 ($2/GPU-hour), DSA-specific optimizations cut **prefill cost >3× and decode cost >5× vs full attention**; with topk=2048, 16 adjacent queries need only ~8K effective KV tokens via Top-k deduplication; ViT–LM heterogeneous parallelism + load balancing improves end-to-end training throughput ~20%; FlashInfer/TileLang DSA kernels give >2× speedup over the open-source baseline.
- **Video-RL ablation**: Video RL improves general video benchmark performance by approximately 1 percentage point.

## Critical Assessment

- **"Lossless 256K" claim is asserted, not ablated**: The abstract and intro repeatedly claim "lossless 256K context processing," but the paper provides no direct head-to-head ablation comparing dense-attention vs DSA accuracy at 256K on the same checkpoint. The only DSA-vs-dense evidence is the dense-warm-up/sparse-adaptation training recipe (which shows the indexer can match dense attention distributionally during training), not an end-task quality comparison at 256K. The "lossless" framing is therefore stronger than the evidence supports.
- **Many baseline columns are empty**: In Tables 2–4, Qwen3-VL-235B-A22B/32B/30B-A3B Thinking and InternVL3.5-241B have "–" for most video, code, and tool-use benchmarks, so several "best result" claims (e.g., TimeLens, OJBench, SWE-bench) are made against only 1–2 actual competitors (mostly Qwen3.5-35B-A3B and GPT-5-mini). The strongest competitors at this scale are under-represented.
- **Non-Linear score on Video-MME-v2 is actually lost**: Keye-VL-2.0 scores **18.5/24.2** on the Non-Lin metric vs Qwen3-VL-235B-A22B's **26.3/28.1** — a notable deficit the paper does not discuss, despite highlighting Video-MME-v2 as a headline win. The win is only on raw accuracy, not the more demanding group-correlation metric.
- **Mature-benchmark regression**: On MLVU (82.8 vs Qwen3.5 85.6) and Video-MME w/o sub (78.3 vs 82.5), Keye-VL-2.0 trails smaller open-source models. The paper frames these as "competitive" but does not analyze the regression — relevant because MOPD's stated goal is to *preserve* foundational capabilities.
- **No per-component ablation of MOPD**: The paper describes 13 teachers, dynamic routing, SPRR, top-k overlap, token-category scaling, and repetition penalty, but reports no ablation isolating which of these mechanisms drive the capability-consolidation benefit vs simpler on-policy distillation. The Video-RL ablation (~1pp gain) is the only quantified component contribution.
- **Video-RL gain is small relative to its complexity**: Video RL "improves general video benchmark performance by approximately 1 percentage point" despite covering TVG, dense captioning, FrameForge, etc. — a modest return on a substantial pipeline stage, not discussed.
- **Inference-cost figures are pricing-illustrative, not measured throughput**: Figure 4 reports cost-per-million-tokens under an assumed $2/H800-hour pricing rather than measured wall-clock tokens/sec; absolute throughput numbers (tokens/s, latency) are not reported, only the >3×/>5× relative ratios.
- **Reward-model dependence**: General RL's outcome/process/ContextRL rewards all rely on Qwen2.5-VL-72B-Instruct as judge — a model from a competitor family — introducing an unquantified reward-model bias and circularity risk that the paper does not address.

## Strengths

- Genuinely novel engineering: first credible port of DSA to a GQA (not MLA) multimodal backbone, with concrete Top-k=2048 indexing + GQA sparse aggregation and a clean two-stage dense-warm-up/sparse-adaptation training recipe
- Strong long-video results: best on LongVideoBench (74.1) and Video-MME-v2 accuracy, and best mIoU on all three TimeLens subsets — the headline capabilities are empirically supported
- Practical efficiency story: 3B active parameters, >3×/>5× prefill/decode cost reduction, ~20% training throughput gain, 3–5× data-production throughput from the async pipeline
- Open-weight release (HuggingFace + GitHub) at 30B/3B-active scale, useful for the community
- Comprehensive post-training design: GSPO + Synthetic-Data RL + specialized experts + Agentic-RL + MOPD consolidation is a thorough, modern stack

## Limitations

- "Lossless 256K" is asserted without a direct DSA-vs-dense accuracy ablation at 256K
- Most baseline columns in Tables 2–4 are empty ("–"), weakening several "best" claims to comparisons against 1–2 models
- Actually loses on Video-MME-v2 Non-Linear score and trails on MLVU/Video-MME — partial regression on foundational benchmarks that MOPD was designed to prevent
- No ablation isolating MOPD's sub-components (routing, SPRR, top-k overlap, token scaling)
- Video-RL yields only ~1pp despite its complexity
- Inference cost reported as pricing-illustrative ($/Mtok under $2/H800-hr) rather than measured tokens/sec
- Reward system depends on a competitor's model (Qwen2.5-VL-72B-Instruct) as judge
- 53 co-authors; individual contributions not separately verifiable
- Heavy reliance on internal Kuaishou data (e-commerce, business-oriented) whose composition is not detailed, limiting reproducibility of the data flywheel

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The paper claims "lossless 256K" but never runs a direct DSA-vs-dense-attention accuracy comparison at 256K on the same checkpoint — what is the actual end-task quality delta (e.g., on LongVideoBench/Video-MME-v2) when dense attention is swapped in at 256K, and at what compute cost?
- Why does Keye-VL-2.0 *lose* on Video-MME-v2 Non-Linear score (18.5/24.2 vs Qwen3-VL-235B's 26.3/28.1) while winning on raw accuracy — does DSA's Top-k=2048 selection hurt the correlated-group reasoning that the Non-Lin metric rewards?
- MOPD combines 13 teachers with dynamic routing, SPRR, top-k overlap, token-category scaling, and localized repetition penalty (Eqs. 12–17) — which of these mechanisms actually drive the consolidation benefit? No ablation is reported.
- Video-RL yields only ~1 percentage point on general video benchmarks despite TVG + dense captioning + FrameForge; is this stage worth its complexity, or could a lighter Video-RL schedule match it?
- The General RL reward system uses Qwen2.5-VL-72B-Instruct (a competitor model) as judge for outcome/process/ContextRL rewards (cited Bai et al. 2025) — how sensitive are the final scores to this judge, and does self-rewarding with Keye-VL-2.0 itself change rankings?
- DSA inference cost (Figure 4) is reported under an assumed $2/H800-hour pricing rather than measured tokens/sec — what are the absolute prefill/decode latencies and throughputs at 64K/128K/256K?
- Many baseline columns in Tables 2–4 are "–"; how do the TimeLens/OJBench/SWE-bench "best" claims hold once Qwen3-VL-235B-A22B and InternVL3.5-241B are actually evaluated on those benchmarks?
- The dense-warm-up stage uses ~2B tokens to align the indexer to dense GQA attention (Eq. 4) — how stable is the final sparse model to the warm-up token budget and to the λ weight on L^I_sparse in Eq. 7?
