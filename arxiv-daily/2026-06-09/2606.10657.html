Are We Evaluating Knowledge or Phrasing? Mitigating MCQA Sensitivity with ParaEval

João Maria Janeiro, Mathurin Videau, Andrea Caciolai, Benjamin Piwowarski, Patrick Gallinari, Loic Barrault / 2026‑06‑09 / arXiv:2606.10657

02 Why this matters

If standard MCQA benchmarks measure phrasing rather than knowledge, many claimed model comparisons across MMLU‑style leaderboards may need revisiting.

Applies to any choice‑based LLM evaluation pipeline.
03 Critical edge

Best‑phrasing scoring may overestimate capability by rewarding lucky wording matches.

— Limitations, ParaEval paper

Methodology

The authors build a controlled testbed that isolates phrasing from knowledge, then propose a paraphrase‑based scoring fix.

  1. Train 1B–8B models on identical underlying knowledge, varying only the surface form of answers.
  2. Score with standard log‑likelihood MCQA — measure the gap attributable to phrasing alone.
  3. Generate multiple paraphrases per answer option with an LLM.
  4. Re‑score each model using its most favorable phrasing — the ParaEval signal.
  5. Validate that artifacts and improvements persist in frontier 70B and 120B open‑source models.

Strengths  /  Limitations

Strengths

  • Controlled design cleanly isolates the phrasing confound.
  • Simple, practical fix — multi‑paraphrase scoring.
  • Wide scale coverage: 1B all the way to 120B.
  • Targets a fundamental issue in how the field evaluates LLMs.

Limitations

  • Compute cost of generating paraphrases for every option.
  • Best‑phrasing scoring may overestimate capability.
  • Unclear interaction with option‑ordering and prompt sensitivity.
  • Coverage bias from the paraphrase generator itself.
