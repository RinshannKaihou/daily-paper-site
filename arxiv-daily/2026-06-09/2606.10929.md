> **一句话总结：** 本文在合成多任务Transformer和LoRA适配器（DistilGPT-2/GPT-2）上发现任务恢复方向是低维且有用的，但并不位于任何固定任务平面——它沿优化轨迹漂移（100步内主子空间重叠从1.0降到0.12–0.17），而恢复前若干步的"轨迹前缀"基能捕获约77%的LoRA恢复位移；并用高斯局部线性定理解释了高维随机搜索为何有效，还发现单次梯度步在Qwen2.5-0.5B上产生的激活偏移与对比式CAA steering向量余弦达0.58。

> **TL;DR:** The authors study which linear structures exist in trained networks via a "recovery after forgetting" loop (multitask θ_mt → single-task θ_forg → recovery θ_rec, giving ground-truth displacement ∆_GD). On a synthetic 4-layer Transformer and LoRA adapters (DistilGPT-2 rank-16, GPT-2 rank-8), they find task gradients are low-rank (effective rank 2–8) but the static task plane misses recovery — projected recovery reaches only ~0.10 EM vs. 0.30 for full descent on the synthetic model — while the first 10 recovery steps (trajectory prefix) capture ~77% of the LoRA ∆_GD. A Gaussian best-of-N theorem explains why random search works in 10^9-dim space, validated at σ≈10⁻⁴ on Qwen2.5-0.5B where ensembled BoolQ accuracy rises ~+10 points. A single gradient step yields an activation shift with cosine 0.58 to a CAA steering vector, recovering up to +10 held-out points.

## 核心贡献

- **拒绝静态任务平面假设**：合成模型上，将梯度每步重投影到遗忘检查点的局部任务子空间，恢复EM仅0.10，而完整下降达0.30；遗忘前的任务平面更差（0.07）。局部任务平面在100步内主角度重叠从1.0漂移到0.12–0.17——漂移的是朝向而非维度。
- **轨迹前缀作为恢复对象**：LoRA上，前10步恢复向量构成的基捕获约77%的∆_GD，前20步约90%；静态局部任务平面仅捕获约15%，遗忘前平面<2%。
- **高斯best-of-N随机搜索定理**：等向高斯扰动捕获固定有用方向a的信号方差为σ²‖a‖²/D，与参数数无关；best-of-N再放大√(2 log N)倍。解释了10^9维空间中随机搜索为何有效。
- **密度而非质量命题**：匹配范数搜索下，每维信号密度为‖P_U a‖/√r；增加与a正交的方向会稀释信号——预测rank-10前缀胜过rank-32任务平面+轨迹。
- **Krylov恢复引理**：二次损失下恢复位移位于Krylov子空间K_T(H,g₀)=span{g₀,Hg₀,…,H^{T-1}g₀}，仅当梯度是Hessian特征向量时才退化成固定方向。
- **权重到激活的pushforward桥接**：单次梯度步的激活影子在Qwen2.5-0.5B第19–20层与CAA steering向量余弦0.58，注入干净模型提升留出集准确率最高+10点。

## Key Contributions

- **Rejects the static task-plane hypothesis**: On the synthetic Transformer, projecting gradient descent at every step onto the local task subspace at θ_forg recovers only 0.10 EM vs. 0.30 for full descent; the pre-forgetting plane is worse (0.07). The top-r subspace drifts in orientation from principal-angle overlap 1.0 to 0.12–0.17 over 100 recovery steps.
- **Trajectory prefix as the recovery object**: On LoRA adapters, the first 10 recovery steps capture ~77% of ∆_GD; the first 20 capture ~90%; static local task plane ~15%; pre-forgetting plane <2%. This holds on both DistilGPT-2 and GPT-2 backbones.
- **Gaussian best-of-N theorem (Theorem 1)**: For an isotropic Gaussian perturbation, the variance of the projection onto a fixed useful direction a is σ²‖a‖²/D — independent of dimension D. Best-of-N amplifies this by E max_{n≤N} X_n ≈ √(2 log N), so the targeted-task signal grows with search budget and is dimension-independent while orthogonal-direction damage stays flat.
- **Density-over-mass proposition**: Under matched-norm search in subspace U of rank r, per-dimension signal scales as ‖P_U a‖/√r; padding U with directions orthogonal to a raises r without raising ‖P_U a‖, lowering the signal — predicting the rank-10 prefix beats the rank-32 task-plane+trajectory.
- **Krylov recovery lemma**: Under a quadratic loss with constant Hessian, the recovery displacement lies in the Krylov subspace K_T(H,g₀); a fixed direction captures it only in the degenerate case where the gradient is a Hessian eigenvector.
- **Weight-to-activation pushforward bridge**: A single gradient step on Qwen2.5-0.5B yields an activation shift with cosine ~0.58 to a contrastive CAA steering vector at layers 19–20, recovering up to +10 held-out points when reinjected into the clean model — matching CAA built from 80 labelled contrast pairs.

## Methodology

**Recovery-after-forgetting loop.** (1) multitask training to θ_mt on an equal mixture of T tasks; (2) single-task continuation (forgetting) to θ_forg where another task degrades; (3) recovery: a small number of gradient steps on the original mixture (100 for synthetic, 50 for LoRA) reaching θ_rec. The recovery displacement ∆_GD = θ_rec − θ_forg is the ground-truth gradient-based adaptation candidate subspaces are compared against.

**Candidate subspaces (3 families).** Local task subspace U_τ^(r)(θ) = span of top-r eigenvectors of per-task gradient covariance (1/m)Σ g_τ(θ;b_i)g_τ(θ;b_i)^⊤; trajectory prefix U_traj^(k) = span of first k recovery step vectors; explicit Krylov span{g,Hg,H²g,H³g} via finite-difference Hessian-vector products (curvature diagnostic only).

**Random search scoring (3 metrics).** Draw N perturbations, evaluate on a held-out split, keep top k: best-of-N (single best candidate's gain — the Theorem 1 quantity); ensemble-k-of-N (majority vote of top k); pass@N (fraction solved by ≥1 kept candidate). Native sampler: isotropic Gaussian δ~N(0,σ²I); diagnostic sampler restricts to U either Gaussian in U or norm-matched on U's sphere.

**Synthetic Transformer.** 4-layer, 128-dim, 2-head causal Transformer (~5·10⁵ params) on four procedurally generated digit-sequence tasks (copy, reverse, sort, mod-add). Multitask pretraining 20,000 steps; single-task continuation 10,000 steps; recovery ≤100 steps. Three seeds.

**LoRA validation.** DistilGPT-2 rank-16 adapters on sort→mod-add; GPT-2 rank-8 adapters on reverse→sort; three seeds each, six runs total.

**LLM scale (§5.3).** Qwen2.5-0.5B/3B/7B and OLMo-7B. Per-task gradient covariances estimated via random sketch; F1/F2 finite-difference probe separates linear vs curvature parts of a perturbation's effect across scales σ∈{10⁻⁶…10⁻²}. Perturbation-to-steering pipeline (Figure 9): search split S_sel (keep best-of-N weight perturbations) → hidden-state split S_vec (average activation shadow into steering vector) → operating-point selection (layer ℓ∈{15…20}, scale α) on S_sel → report on held-out S_rep.

## Results & Highlights

- **Synthetic Transformer geometry**: Per-task gradient effective rank r₉₀ = 2–8; cross-task overlap (top-r, 6 pairs) 0.11 vs. random-subspace baseline ~6·10⁻⁵. Worst other-task EM drop on forgetting 0.56–0.95. Recovery EM: full descent 0.30, projected-local-plane 0.10, projected-pre-int-plane 0.07. Local-plane drift 1.0 → 0.12–0.17 over 100 steps.
- **Synthetic random search ordering (forgotten sort, n=3)**: Trajectory prefix EM 0.152 (gain 0.090) > Task-plane-local 0.120 (0.059) > Task-plane-pre-int 0.106 (0.032) > Isotropic 0.055 (0.003) > Random rank-matched 0.045 (0.002).
- **LoRA trajectory-prefix capture**: First 10 steps capture ~77% of ∆_GD; first 20 ~90%; static local task plane ~15%; pre-forgetting plane <2% (Figure 2a).
- **LoRA random search (n=6 runs, matched step size)**: Trajectory prefix (r=10) best-of-N gain 0.165 (√normalized 0.052) > full trajectory (r=16) 0.134 (0.034) > task-plane+trajectory (r=32) 0.055 (0.010) > task-plane-local (r=16) 0.023 (0.006). Pass@N: prefix 0.552 vs. task-plane-local 0.270.
- **Per-candidate alignment**: A candidate's cosine with ∆_GD correlates with gain (r≈0.52); overlap with static local task plane anti-correlates (r≈−0.40), ~1.2k candidates pooled over 6 runs.
- **Sequential specialization is brittle**: Simultaneous schedules (raw grad-sum, alternating, PCGrad-lite) keep worst-task EM ~0.58–0.69 on DistilGPT-2 vs. sequential A→B at 0.000 and B→A at 0.208 (Table 5).
- **LLM random search at σ≈10⁻⁴ (Qwen2.5-0.5B)**: Ensembled BoolQ accuracy rises ~+10 points with small side-task damage; ARC-Easy is curvature-dominated at every scale (no useful random search).
- **Weight→activation steering (Qwen2.5-0.5B)**: Cosine between gradient-step activation shift and CAA vector peaks ~0.58 at layers 19–20; reinjection improves held-out accuracy up to +10 points, closely tracking the CAA vector curve. Structured transport beats matched-norm random steering on 9 of 14 (model, task) variants across Qwen2.5-0.5B/3B/7B and OLMo-7B (Table 6); the OLMo BoolQ lane buys +1.2 target points at −17 side-task points.

## Critical Assessment

- **The LLM-scale low-rank claim is only partial, by the authors' own admission.** On Qwen-7B the measured gradient effective rank "essentially coincides" with the random-sketch control, so the apparent low rank is "indistinguishable from sampling noise at this sample size" — the clean low-rank result holds on the small/LoRA models but only partly survives at billion-parameter scale. The headline trajectory/Krylov story is thus grounded on a ~5·10⁵-parameter synthetic model and two LoRA task pairs.
- **The trajectory prefix needs a trajectory.** The authors are explicit (Limitation 4) that the prefix cannot be turned into a practical algorithm because building it requires already running the recovery — so the strongest empirical object is a diagnostic, not a deployable method.
- **Few task pairs underpin the central geometric claim.** The trajectory-vs-plane ordering rests on one synthetic family (copy/reverse/sort/mod-add) and two LoRA task pairs; the authors are "cautious about generalizing the exact magnitudes."
- **The CAA-steering match is one model and one task.** The cosine-0.58 / +10-point result is Qwen2.5-0.5B on BoolQ only; the multimodel table (Table 6) shows the bridge is "task- and architecture-dependent," with OLMo BoolQ buying +1.2 target points at −17 side-task points — a poor trade.
- **Random search is explicitly not an optimizer replacement.** The authors flag that it can occasionally beat gradient descent on the target task but is unstable and hard to guarantee on other tasks; the theorem only holds in the locally-linear regime (σ≈10⁻⁴ on Qwen-0.5B), which is task-dependent (ARC-Easy is curvature-dominated at every scale).

## Strengths

- Genuine theoretical contribution: a dimension-independent best-of-N theorem that explains the counterintuitive effectiveness of random search in 10⁹-dim space, plus the density-over-mass proposition that predicts subspace rankings.
- Clean Krylov-subspace lemma rigorously connects recovery displacement to optimization trajectory structure.
- Multi-scale experimental ladder (synthetic → LoRA → billion-param) with appropriate controls (random-sketch rank control, F1/F2 linearity probe, matched-norm steering control).
- Honest about limitations: explicitly flags the partial LLM low-rank result, the few-task-pairs caveat, and that the trajectory prefix is diagnostic not algorithmic.
- The weight→activation pushforward bridge is a novel unifying idea, empirically validated (cosine 0.58 to CAA) even if only on one model/task.

## Limitations

- Cleanest results are on a ~5·10⁵-parameter synthetic Transformer and small LoRA adapters; LLM low-rank claim only partial (Qwen-7B indistinguishable from sampling noise).
- The trajectory prefix is not actionable as an algorithm (requires running recovery to build).
- Central geometric ordering rests on one synthetic family + two LoRA task pairs.
- The gradient-step-to-CAA match is one model (Qwen-0.5B) and one task (BoolQ); multimodel steering is task/architecture-dependent with poor trades on some lanes.
- Random-search theorem only valid in the locally-linear regime; ARC-Easy is curvature-dominated and yields nothing useful.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The LLM low-rank claim is indistinguishable from sampling noise on Qwen-7B — would a larger, better-controlled gradient sketch (more minibatches) separate measured rank from the random-sketch control at billion-parameter scale, as the authors flag as natural future work?
- The trajectory prefix is diagnostic because building it requires running recovery — can the Krylov-subspace structure (Lemma 1, finite-difference Hessian-vector products {g,Hg,H²g,H³g}) substitute as a pre-computed proxy that does not require the trajectory?
- The best-of-N theorem predicts dimension-independent signal; does the σ≈10⁻⁴ linear-regime window shift predictably with model size, or is it task/architecture-specific (as the ARC-Easy curvature-domination suggests)?
- The weight→activation pushforward gives cosine 0.58 to CAA on Qwen-0.5B BoolQ — does this match survive on tasks beyond true/false statement formats, and what causes the OLMo −17 side-task degradation?
- The authors propose (Conclusion) a Lie-theoretic description of adaptation dynamics as a unifying next step — has any follow-up connected the local tangent geometry to a transformation-group constraint?
