Critical Edge

No adversarial robustness. An attacker who knows the probe's direction in residual space could evade it using the same activation-steering mechanism the paper itself proposes.

§ 01

How it works — a frozen LLM, one mid-layer, three head shapes

Method

Train a tiny head on a single mid-layer of a frozen generator; read that head every π tokens during decoding; trigger on threshold. Three head shapes are compared:

  • aLINEAR-TOKEN — single linear projection zt = w⊤hℓt + b.
  • bMLP-TOKEN — 2-layer MLP, hidden 1024. The workhorse used in the online streaming run.
  • cDUAL-HEAD — convex combo α·zmlp + (1−α)·zlin, α=0.8. Best offline; linear branch doubles as a steering direction.
Backbone: DeepSeek-R1-0528-Qwen3-8B · layer 21 · loss = per-token weighted BCE with TOP-K=16 loss-time pooling (only the 16 most informative tokens per sequence contribute). Architecture discovered by an LLM-agent AutoResearch loop across 177 trials.

§ 02

Strengths & Limitations — what the recipe gives you, and what it doesn't

Strengths
  • Honest framing. The probe is a "cheap inlined surrogate" of a guard model — not a claim of superior accuracy.
  • Concrete deployment recipe. Layer 21 · π=2 · MAX aggregation · INSTANT trigger. Drops into a vLLM serving stack.
  • AutoResearch loop yields a real architecture win (+0.009 F1) and surfaces negative results cleanly.
  • Detection → steering bridge. The linear branch is also a direction in residual space — same hidden-state read can become a hidden-state write.
  • Strong ablation discipline. Layer selection, head shape, aggregation strategy, and probe interval all swept, not asserted.
Limitations
  • Single backbone (DeepSeek-R1-Qwen3-8B) — limited evidence of transfer to Llama, Mistral, or base (non-RLHF) models.
  • No adversarial evaluation. Red-team prompts that try to evade the hidden-state signature are untested.
  • Proprietary ZHUYI corpus (Chinese). English results (BeaverTails F1 0.750) trail Chinese in-domain by ~15 F1 points.
  • Online pipeline uses MLP-TOKEN, not the stronger DUAL-HEAD checkpoint — full-data DUAL-HEAD is not yet integrated into vLLM.
  • Per-dataset calibration. Thresholds and aggregation need re-tuning per task; no off-the-shelf defaults for production.
§ 03

Questions for further exploration — what this paper makes you want to try next

  1. Does the "mid-network compliance direction" appear at the same relative depth in Llama, Mistral, or other Qwen sizes? Family-transfer evidence is the open question.
  2. Can adversarial training of the probe against red-team prompts that explicitly try to evade the hidden-state signature improve robustness without hurting clean accuracy?
  3. What is the quantitative effect of activation steering along the linear branch? Safer continuations, or just refusal-like outputs?
  4. Could a multi-layer ensemble (weighted layers 19–24) close the gap on obfuscation categories where single-layer probing struggles (Pinyin, leetspeak)?
  5. On base (non-RLHF) models, does the probe still find a compliance direction? Is the safety signature a product of alignment training, or of pre-training?
