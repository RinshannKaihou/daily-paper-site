> **一句话总结：** 本文用一个在合成马尔可夫链上训练的6层MLP残差堆栈，定量验证了残差流有效秩随深度的坍缩受输入分布谱结构控制——短相关链(ξ≈1.2)发生8.4倍秩坍缩而长相关链(ξ≈6.7)完全不坍缩(1.0倍)，且层间核漂移集中在1–2个特定层转换处形成固定点平台，首次从位置层面给出了神经网络实现重整化群选择性粗粒化的定量证据。

> **TL;DR:** On a 6-layer MLP residual stack (d=64) trained with BERT-style masked-token prediction over synthetic Markov chains, the authors measure the residual stream's effective rank and inter-layer CKA kernel drift as RG order parameters. Short-correlation chains (ξ≈1.2) show an 8.4× rank-collapse compression ratio while long-correlation chains (ξ≈6.7) show no collapse (1.0×), and kernel drift concentrates at isolated transitions (long-ξ spikes of 0.56 at L1→L2 and 0.38 at L5→L6) with near-zero drift elsewhere — the first position-level quantitative evidence that an MLP residual stack implements a selective coarse-graining governed by the input's spectral structure.

## 核心贡献

- **首次定量验证RG类比**：在已知谱特性（可解析计算相关长度 ξ=−1/log|λ₂|）的合成马尔可夫链上，定义并测量了可量化的RG序参量（残差流有效秩 ρ_eff 与层间核漂移 Δ_CKA），将神经网络与重整化群的类比从定性隐喻推进到受控定量实验
- **选择性秩坍缩**：短相关链(ξ≈1.2)有效秩坍缩8.4倍、坍缩点在L3；长相关链(ξ≈6.7)位置层面完全不坍缩(1.0×)，秩保持在8–11——网络精确保留了与预测任务相关的自由度，仅丢弃无关的
- **离散固定点平台**：长相关链的核漂移集中在两个孤立转换(L1→L2=0.56, L5→L6=0.38)，中间四层近零漂移；短链同样呈现"入口转换—近恒等中层—出口读出"的托架式平台，与连续RG流预测的均匀级联不符但符合固定点解释
- **识别并排除三个混淆**：mean-pool测量对慢混合长链产生虚假坍缩(6.5×)；无注意力架构使上下文利用不可能（最优预测恒为平稳分布π）；长链较大的损失间隙(Δ=0.009 nats)是初始化伪影而非学习差异

## Key Contributions

- **First quantitative, position-level RG evidence**: Defines a measurable RG order parameter (effective rank ρ_eff of the residual stream, plus CKA kernel drift) and tests it under controlled variation of the input distribution's spectral properties on synthetic Markov chains whose correlation length ξ=−1/log|λ₂| is analytically known
- **Selective rank collapse**: Rank collapses 8.4× for short-ξ chains (ξ≈1.2) but is fully preserved (1.0×) for long-ξ chains (ξ≈6.7) at the position level — the network discards only task-irrelevant degrees of freedom, exactly the Wilsonian RG relevance criterion
- **Discrete fixed-point plateau structure**: Kernel drift is concentrated at one or two specific layer transitions with the rest of the network near a fixed point; both regimes converge to a bracketed plateau (entry transition → near-identity middle → exit readout), inconsistent with a naive uniform depth-as-scale cascade
- **Artifact dismissal**: Explicitly identifies and rules out three confounds — a mean-pooling artifact that fabricates collapse for slow-mixing chains, the attention-free architecture making context exploitation impossible, and an initialization artifact explaining the loss-gap difference

## Methodology

The authors study the simplest tractable architecture for the RG analogy: a **pre-norm MLP residual stack with no attention**, deliberately removing cross-token mixing so that any inter-token structure must arise from the training signal alone. The model is: a linear input projection ϕ_in: ℝᵛ→ℝᵈ (no bias); L residual blocks each computing x←x+MLP(LayerNorm(x)) where the MLP is two-layer with hidden dimension 4d and GELU; a final LayerNorm; and a linear classification head ϕ_head: ℝᵈ→ℝᵛ. **All experiments use d=64, L=6, V=16.**

The **synthetic corpus** is sequences of length T=64 drawn from a row-stochastic Markov chain transition matrix P over a 16-token vocabulary. Two regimes are tuned via the Dirichlet concentration α used to draw P's rows:
- **Short-ξ**: α=10 → near-uniform rows, λ₂≈0.44, ξ≈1.2 (decorrelates within ~5 steps)
- **Long-ξ**: α=0.05 → sparse rows, λ₂≈0.86, ξ≈6.7 (mixing time ~31 steps for ε=0.01)

Training uses a **BERT-style masked-token prediction** objective (15% of positions replaced with the zero vector), fresh sequences sampled every step, **10,000 steps, batch size 32, Adam at lr 3×10⁻⁴**. Two order parameters are measured: (1) **effective rank** ρ_eff(H)=exp(−Σpᵢ log pᵢ) over the normalized singular-value spectrum (entropy of the spectrum, ρ_eff∈[1,d]); (2) **kernel drift** Δ_CKA(l,l+1)=1−CKA(H⁽ˡ⁾,H⁽ˡ⁺¹⁾). Crucially, ρ_eff is computed in **flatten (position-level) mode** — each of the B×T positions is a separate row — which the authors show is necessary because mean-pooling confounds the chain's spectral structure with sequence-clustering.

## Results & Highlights

- **Short-ξ rank collapse (Table 1, flatten mode)**: Effective rank falls monotonically L0→L6 from **14.4 → 13.6 → 7.6 → 3.4 → 2.6 → 2.2 → 1.7**, an **8.4× compression ratio** with collapse centered at L3; at initialization the profile is flat (1.05×). The L3 rank stays near 8 through step 8,000 then drops sharply to 3.4 between steps 9,000–10,000 (a phase-transition-like late event).
- **Long-ξ no collapse (Table 2, flatten mode)**: Effective rank profile is **11.2 → 10.8 → 8.1 → 8.1 → 8.1 → 8.0 → 10.8**, a **1.0× compression ratio** (no collapse). By contrast, mean-pool mode fabricates a 6.5× collapse for the same long-ξ chain — an artifact of state-clustering in slow-mixing sequences.
- **Kernel drift — long-ξ (Table 3 / Eq. 8, flatten mode)**: Drift profile **0.074 → 0.560 → 0.004 → 0.003 → 0.001 → 0.383** — two isolated spikes at L1→L2 (0.56, entry) and L5→L6 (0.38, exit readout), with four near-identity layers in between forming a fixed-point plateau.
- **Kernel drift — short-ξ (Eq. 9, flatten mode)**: Drift profile **0.253 → 0.266 → 0.292 → 0.034 → 0.011 → 0.077** — high drift in the entry region L0→L3, near-zero plateau L3→L5, small exit drift at L5→L6.
- **Loss gaps vs. H(π) floor (Figure 5)**: Short-ξ converges to within **Δ=0.001 nats** of H(π)=2.722; long-ξ ends **Δ=0.009 nats** above H(π)=2.212 — the larger long-ξ gap is an initialization artifact (peaked target far from near-zero bias init), not a learning difference.
- **Stability of plateau (Figure 9)**: Drift heatmaps show the entry/exit transitions lock in early and do not migrate across all 10,000 training steps — the fixed-point plateau is a stable attractor of training dynamics, not a transient.

## Critical Assessment

- **Controlled but narrow architecture**: The study is restricted to a single 6-layer, d=64 attention-free MLP residual stack on a 16-token synthetic Markov chain — far from modern transformer LLMs. The authors concede this explicitly (Limitation (i)): 6 layers are "too small to exhibit the full range of RG behavior," and the planned sweep over L∈{6,12,24} and ξ∈{1,3,10,30} to test whether collapse depth scales linearly with ξ is **not performed**. The headline "RG structure of MLP residual networks" is therefore supported only at one depth and one width.
- **Unresolved input-rank confound**: There is a **29% input-rank gap between the two chains under flatten mode (L0 rank 11.2 vs 14.4)**, arising from lower token diversity of slow-mixing sequences. The authors flag this as "a genuine confound" (Limitation (ii)) but do not fix it; the selective-collapse claim rests on comparing two regimes whose inputs already differ in rank, and the perturbation-decay experiment that would cleanly isolate it is left as future work.
- **Context exploitation is impossible by construction**: Because there is no attention and each token is processed independently, a masked token (zero vector) has no access to context, so the theoretically optimal prediction is always the stationary distribution π. The authors acknowledge this (Section 5.2) — meaning the rank profiles cannot measure context exploitation, only how the stack organizes representations to output a fixed target from a zero input. This substantially narrows what the "coarse-graining" finding actually demonstrates.
- **Single-seed, no error bars**: Tables report single effective-rank and drift numbers with no confidence intervals or repetition across seeds, so the sharp collapse window (L3 dropping between steps 9,000–10,000) and the exact drift-spike magnitudes could be seed-dependent.
- **Evaluation/train distribution mismatch**: The model is trained with 15% masking but evaluated with no masking (Limitation (iii)), which the authors admit is "slightly out of distribution" and may influence the measurements.

## Strengths

- Clean, principled experimental design with analytically known input spectral properties (ξ tunable via Dirichlet α)
- First position-level (not just qualitative) quantitative test of the RG–neural-network analogy, with a measurable order parameter
- The flatten-vs-pool comparison is a genuinely useful methodological contribution that exposes a mean-pooling artifact that would have produced a diametrically wrong conclusion (apparent 6.5× collapse for long-ξ chains)
- Drift heatmaps across training (Figure 9) demonstrate the plateau is a stable attractor, not a transient
- Honest about limitations — three confounds are explicitly named and (partially) dismissed

## Limitations

- Restricted to one small synthetic setting (6 layers, d=64, V=16); no depth/width sweep, no scaling study of collapse-depth vs ξ
- No attention, so results may not transfer to transformer LLMs; the RG relevance to modern architectures is untested
- 29% input-rank gap between short- and long-ξ chains is an acknowledged but uncorrected confound
- Single-seed results without error bars or statistical significance
- Does not analyze the training dynamics that *produce* the RG fixed-point structure, only its final/stable form
- The perturbation-decay experiment needed to extract quantitative RG scaling exponents is identified as "the natural next step" but not executed

## Relevance

映射到 **ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations（数学基础）** 与 **LLM Hidden States & Interpretability（隐藏状态与可解释性）** 两个领域：它用重整化群理论为残差流表示如何随深度演化、何时坍缩提供了首个定量的几何刻画，对理解深层网络表示动力学与有效维度变化具有直接的数学与可解释性价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the selective rank-collapse phenomenon persist when depth and correlation length are swept together (L∈{6,12,24} × ξ∈{1,3,10,30}) — i.e., does collapse depth scale linearly with ξ as the RG picture predicts? (This is the experiment the authors themselves flag as needed.)
- Does the fixed-point plateau structure survive once attention (cross-token mixing) is added, or does attention redistribute the kernel drift into a smoother cascade — the paper cites [Martin, 2023] and [Alpay and Kilictas, 2026] on attention-as-partition-function but tests only the attention-free case.
- The paper proposes a perturbation-decay experiment (injecting perturbations along individual eigenvectors of P and measuring layer-wise decay) as the route to RG scaling exponents — would those exponents differ between the short-ξ and long-ξ regimes in a way consistent with Wilsonian universality?
- Can the effective-rank compression ratio serve as a practical depth-selection diagnostic, given the plateau observation that most layers are near-identity?
- The 29% input-rank confound could be removed by fixing the evaluation distribution; would the 8.4× vs 1.0× selectivity gap shrink or hold under that control?
