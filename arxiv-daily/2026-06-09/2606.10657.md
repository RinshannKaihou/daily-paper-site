> **一句话总结：** 本文用同知识库训练的英/西1B–8B对照模型证明，标准MCQA的log-likelihood打分把"对答案措辞的熟悉度"误当成知识，制造出最多约4分的虚假差距；提出的ParaEval对每个选项生成k=5个释义并取最高概率打分，把同知识模型间的虚假差距压到1分以内，并在70B/120B前沿模型上同样有效。

> **TL;DR:** Using controlled LLaMA-architecture models (1B/3B/8B) trained on the exact same DCLM-EDU corpus (English vs. GPT-OSS-translated Spanish, plus an English back-translation control) with matched tokenizers, the authors show standard raw-LL MCQA scoring fabricates gaps up to +4.0 on ARC-Easy/ARC-Challenge/MMLU/HellaSwag between models with identical knowledge. On MMLU the gap is +3.80 at 3B; ParaEval (5 Claude-generated paraphrases per option, scored by max log-likelihood) cuts it to +0.52. On ARC-Easy 1B the gap falls from +2.30 to +0.28; on the English-vs-backtranslated-English wording control, HellaSwag's gap drops from +4.4 to −1.3. Frontier models (LLaMA-3.1-70B, Qwen2.5-72B, Nemotron-3-120B) show EN–ES gaps of 3.0–7.3 on ARC-Challenge, reduced 14–32% by ParaEval.

## 核心贡献

- **建立对照实验台**：训练1B/3B/8B LLaMA架构的英、西单语模型，二者在完全相同的120B token DCLM-EDU语料上训练（西班牙语版由GPT-OSS翻译），并配独立训练的匹配压缩比tokenizer（英≈5.0、西≈5.1 chars/tok），从而把"知识差异"完全排除，剩余性能差距只能是评测伪影
- **措辞控制（EN↔回译英文）**：再训一个1B英文模型，使用同一语料的60B回译token（EN→ES→EN）训练——同一语言、同一文档级知识、仅风格分布不同，任何性能差距都纯粹源于措辞熟悉度
- **诊断打分敏感性**：在ARC-Easy上把raw LL、acc_norm(chars)、acc_norm(tokens)、PMI四种打分全部测试，没有任何常见归一化能消除EN–ES差距（token归一化反而把差距从+5.3夸大到+8.0）；仅改写正确答案可提升准确率5–14分，证明"错误"很多是措辞不匹配而非知识缺失
- **提出ParaEval**：对每个选项用Claude-4.6-Opus生成k=5个释义（含短/中/长形式），按最高对数似然打分 ŝ_i=max_j logP(a_i^(j)|q)，并应用到正确选项和干扰项，使每个含义都以自己最佳措辞参与比较
- **跨规模验证**：在LLaMA-3.1-70B、Qwen2.5-72B、Nemotron-3-120B、GPT-OSS-120B前沿模型上同样观测到3.0–7.3的EN–ES差距，ParaEval降低14–32%，证明这不是小模型病理

## Key Contributions

- A **controlled testbed**: 1B/3B/8B monolingual English and Spanish LLaMA-architecture models trained from scratch on the exact same 120B-token DCLM-EDU corpus (Spanish via GPT-OSS translation), with language-specific 128k-vocab tokenizers engineered to matched compression ratios (≈5.0 vs ≈5.1 chars/token), so any residual benchmark gap must be an evaluation artifact rather than a knowledge difference
- A **wording-only control**: a 1B English model trained on 60B backtranslated (EN→ES→EN) tokens of the same corpus, isolating phrasing from language entirely — standard scoring still fabricates a +4.4 HellaSwag gap that ParaEval collapses to −1.3
- A **diagnostic** that no common normalization removes the artifact: on ARC-Easy (1B), raw-LL gap is +5.3, char-norm +5.9, token-norm +8.0, PMI +4.4; paraphrasing only the correct answer lifts accuracy by 5–14 points, confirming a large fraction of "errors" are phrasing mismatches
- **ParaEval**: paraphrase all options (correct and distractors) with k=5 Claude-4.6-Opus-generated variants, score each option by its max log-likelihood, with deduplication and cross-choice overlap removal — bringing models with identical knowledge to within ~1 point and reducing gaps by 14–96%
- **Scale generalization**: the artifact and ParaEval's mitigation persist in frontier 70B/120B open-weight models, with ParaEval cutting ARC-Challenge EN–ES gaps by 14–32%

## Methodology

**Controlled training.** Decoder-only LLaMA-architecture models at 1B/3B/8B. English trained on original DCLM-EDU; Spanish trained on the GPT-OSS translation of the *same* 120B tokens; an additional 1B English model trained on 60B backtranslated (EN→ES→EN) tokens. Training follows DCLM-recommended hyperparameters (LR, batch size) consistently across languages at each scale; 1B trained for 60B tokens, 3B for 80B, 8B on 120B, sequence length 4096 with packing, using the Lingua framework. **Matched tokenizers** (128k vocab each) are trained per-language to equalize compression (~5.0 EN / ~5.1 ES chars/token), eliminating the fertility confound where the default LLaMA tokenizer fragments non-English text.

**Evaluation.** All benchmarks translated by Claude-4.6-Opus; evaluated via lm-evaluation-harness, 5-shot with native-language prompt templates (0-shot caused small-model miscalibration). Benchmarks: ARC-Easy, ARC-Challenge, HellaSwag, MMLU (MCQA); Natural Questions, TriviaQA (generative exact-match).

**ParaEval.** Standard MCQA picks ỹ=argmax_i logP(a_i|q). ParaEval instead samples k=5 paraphrases per option via Claude-4.6-Opus (temperature 0.3) in a two-stage prompt (the model first gives its own free-form answer as a variant, then batch-generates paraphrases for all four choices varying length/articles/word order/vocabulary), applies deduplication (normalized form: lowercased, no punctuation/articles) and cross-choice overlap removal, then scores ŝ_i=max_{j∈{0..k}} logP(a_i^(j)|q) and predicts ỹ=argmax_i ŝ_i. The **max** (not average) is deliberate: the question is whether the model knows the fact in *any* form, not in every form. Appendix G explores average vs max; Appendix J.1 shows GPT/Gemini as paraphraser give the same trend, so the result is paraphraser-invariant.

**Generative extension.** The same paraphrase principle expands the exact-match reference set; for NQ and TQA the corrected protocol uses 5-shot native prompts plus augmented references.

## Results & Highlights

- **Standard scoring fabricates gaps (Table 2, raw LL)**: On matched-knowledge EN vs ES models, MMLU gap is **+2.68 (1B) / +3.80 (3B) / +3.40 (8B)**; ARC-Challenge **+1.14 / +1.88 / +0.80**; ARC-Easy **+2.30 / +0.34 / +0.08**; HellaSwag **+0.82 / +1.30 / +1.60**. (1B reported as mean±std over 3 seeds.)
- **ParaEval collapses the gaps (Table 2)**: MMLU gap drops to **+0.49 / +0.52 / +0.40**; ARC-Challenge to **+0.03 / +0.51 / +0.50**; ARC-Easy to **+0.28 / +0.04 / +0.04**; HellaSwag to **−0.16 / +0.30 / +0.50** — all reduced to ≤1 point.
- **Wording-only control (Table 3, 1B EN vs backtranslated-EN)**: Standard scoring finds ARC-Easy gap **+2.3** and HellaSwag gap **+4.4** *within the same language*; ParaEval reduces them to **+0.1** and **−1.3** respectively — proving the artifact is phrasing, not language.
- **Normalization cannot fix it (Table 1, ARC-Easy 1B)**: raw-LL gap **+5.3**, char-norm **+5.9**, token-norm **+8.0** (worse), PMI **+4.4** — no common normalization eliminates the gap; generative 5-shot EM gives **−0.1**, confirming the models have equivalent knowledge.
- **Paraphrase ablation (Section 4.3)**: Paraphrasing *only* the correct answer boosts ARC-Easy accuracy by **≈8/14/14 points** at 1B/3B/8B; paraphrasing *only* distractors drops it by **≈12/13/13 points** — direct evidence that many "errors" are wording mismatches, not knowledge gaps.
- **Coverage scaling (Figure 4)**: On ARC-Easy 1B the EN–ES gap falls monotonically from **2.3 pp at k=1** to **0.5 pp at k=13** as paraphrase coverage increases — the artifact vanishes as more of the linguistic space is covered.
- **Generative evaluation (Table 4)**: On Natural Questions the EN–ES gap drops from **+2.22 to +0.03** at 1B and *reverses* at 3B/8B (+1.22→−0.59, −0.58→−0.80); on TriviaQA it narrows by 29% at 1B (+6.12→+4.36) and 64% at 8B (+6.12→+2.20); a Claude/Gemini/GPT judge ensemble brings all TriviaQA gaps below 2%.
- **Frontier models (Table 26, Appendix J.2)**: LLaMA-3.1-70B, Qwen2.5-72B, Nemotron-3-120B show standard EN–ES gaps of **3.0–7.3 on ARC-Challenge** and **4.7–6.9 on HellaSwag**; ParaEval reduces these by **14–32% (0.9–1.4 absolute)**. Table 27 shows paraphrasing only the correct answer (the optimistic upper bound) inflates scores by 5–9 points — justifying why all options must be paraphrased.
- **Cost (Appendix H, Table 16)**: ParaEval makes the corpus ~3× larger in total tokens; in lm-evaluation-harness (no few-shot caching) mean wall-clock per evaluation rises from **25s→36s (1B)**, **21s→41s (3B)**, **23s→55s (8B)** over 8×H100 GPUs — still small in absolute terms.

## Critical Assessment

- **ParaEval's "best phrasing" can *lower* scores, contradicting the optimistic-bias worry**: The authors themselves note (Table 2 discussion) that on HellaSwag absolute performance *degrades* for both models under ParaEval, and on ARC-Easy the English model's score drops because its wrong options were originally poorly phrased (making them easy to dismiss). This is honest, but it means ParaEval is not a monotone "fairer upper bound" — it reshuffles which option wins, and the net effect on *absolute* accuracy is benchmark-dependent and under-analyzed.
- **No comparison to existing surface-form debiasing on the same data**: The paper cites PMI reweighting and calibration (Wiegreffe et al. 2023) and notes they "do not reliably fix this and can harm instruction-tuned models," but does not run PMI/length-norm on the controlled models as a head-to-head baseline beyond the four-method Table 1 (ARC-Easy only). The claim that ParaEval *dominates* these alternatives rests on a single benchmark row.
- **Paraphrase generation depends on a frontier LLM (Claude-4.6-Opus) and translation on GPT-OSS**: The controlled testbed's Spanish corpus is GPT-OSS-translated and paraphrases are Claude-generated — if the paraphraser systematically prefers certain phrasings or injects translation artifacts, ParaEval's gap reduction could partly reflect paraphraser bias rather than pure knowledge recovery. Appendix J.1 shows GPT/Gemini give the "same trend," but only trend invariance is reported, not whether absolute gap magnitudes are stable across paraphrasers.
- **Frontier-model reduction is modest (14–32%)**: Unlike the controlled models where gaps collapse to <1 point (up to 96% reduction), on 70B/120B models ParaEval only removes 14–32% of the gap — leaving 2–6 point residual disparities. The headline "reduces the false performance gap to below 1 point" holds for the *controlled* testbed but **not** for frontier models, a scope limitation the abstract blurs.
- **k=5 is chosen without a coverage/cost Pareto study in the main text**: Figure 4 shows the gap keeps shrinking up to k=13, yet the method defaults to k=5; the trade-off between extra paraphrases and gap reduction (and the 3× token cost) is shown only as a curve, with no recommended operating point justified against cost.
- **Generative TriviaQA gap remains large (+4.36 at 1B even after correction)**: The corrected protocol only cuts TQA by 29% at 1B, leaving a substantial gap that the authors attribute to "incomplete reference coverage" — an unverified explanation, since they do not measure reference coverage directly.
- **Single-seed for 3B/8B**: Only the 1B results are reported with 3-seed mean±std; the 3B and 8B numbers in Table 2 are single-run, so the residual ≤1-point gaps are within plausible run-to-run variance for some cells (e.g., ARC-Challenge 8B +0.50).

## Strengths

- Exceptionally clean controlled design: matched corpus (same 120B tokens), matched-compression tokenizers, matched hyperparameters, plus an English-only backtranslation control that isolates phrasing from language — a genuinely strong identification strategy
- The paraphrase ablation (correct-only vs distractor-only) is a convincing decomposition showing the artifact is symmetric in the expected direction
- Honest about ParaEval not being a pure upper bound (it can lower scores) and about frontier-model residuals being smaller
- Practical and drop-in: works inside lm-evaluation-harness, requires no retraining, paraphraser-invariant across Claude/GPT/Gemini
- Addresses both MCQA and generative exact-match, with cross-lingual and monolingual (EN↔BT-EN) evidence

## Limitations

- Frontier-model gap reduction is only 14–32%, far less dramatic than the controlled-model <1-point collapse; the headline does not generalize fully to the models practitioners actually compare
- Relies on Claude-4.6-Opus for paraphrase generation and GPT-OSS for the Spanish corpus — paraphraser/translation bias is only partially controlled (trend invariance, not magnitude stability)
- No head-to-head against PMI/calibration debiasing beyond a single ARC-Easy row
- k=5 default is not justified against the k=13 coverage curve or the 3× token cost
- 3B/8B numbers are single-seed; some residual ≤1-point gaps are within seed variance
- TriviaQA residual gap (+4.36 at 1B) is attributed to reference coverage without direct measurement
- Wall-clock comparisons are in lm-evaluation-harness without few-shot KV-caching, which the authors note *inflates* ParaEval's relative cost vs an optimized pipeline — so the 25s→55s numbers are pessimistic but the optimized-case speedup is not measured

## Relevance

直接映射到 **LLM Inference Evaluation（推理评测）** 领域——本文正是对MCQA评测方法学可靠性的核心审视，揭示log-likelihood打分把措辞熟悉度误判为知识，并提供即插即用的ParaEval修正方案。它同时也关联到 **ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations（数学基础与可靠性）**：用受控对照实验定量分离"评测伪影"与"真实能力差异"，为基准分数的统计可解释性提供了严格的实验范式。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Why does ParaEval reduce gaps by 14–32% on frontier 70B/120B models but by up to 96% on the controlled 1B/3B/8B models — does the residual frontier gap reflect genuine capability differences the controlled testbed cannot exhibit, or paraphrase-coverage limits at larger scale?
- The paper relies on Claude-4.6-Opus for paraphrases and GPT-OSS for the Spanish corpus; Appendix J.1 shows trend invariance across GPT/Gemini paraphrasers, but are the *absolute* gap magnitudes stable, or does paraphraser choice systematically shift them?
- PMI reweighting and calibration (Wiegreffe et al. 2023, cited in the paper) are dismissed as unreliable, but only ARC-Easy 1B is compared across raw-LL/char-norm/token-norm/PMI (Table 1) — how does ParaEval compare to PMI on MMLU/HellaSwag/ARC-Challenge at 3B/8B?
- Figure 4 shows the gap keeps shrinking to k=13, yet the method defaults to k=5 — is there a principled cost/coverage operating point, and does the optimal k scale with model size?
- ParaEval can *lower* absolute accuracy (HellaSwag for both models, ARC-Easy for English) because it reshuffles which option wins — how should leaderboards report ParaEval scores given that absolute numbers move non-monotonically?
- The TriviaQA residual gap (+4.36 at 1B) is attributed to "incomplete reference coverage" without direct measurement — can reference-set completeness be quantified to confirm this explanation?
- The controlled Spanish corpus is a *translation* (GPT-OSS) of DCLM-EDU; do translation artifacts (vs. natively-Spanish text) inflate the EN–ES gap independently of phrasing, and would a matched native-Spanish corpus change the baseline gap?
- Wall-clock costs (25s→55s) are measured in lm-evaluation-harness *without* few-shot KV-caching, which the authors note inflates ParaEval's relative cost — what is the actual speedup when KV-caching is enabled, given the theoretical 3× bound?
