2Why this matters

Forgetting is not just a continual-learning problem — it lives inside every standard training run. FOGO gives a single optimizer that protects rare directions, with direct implications for LLM data efficiency and catastrophic forgetting.

3Critical edge

Tuning complexity. More moving parts than Adam/Muon (dual streams, codebook, angular bands, proximal lifting). LLM experiments stop at GPT-2 & LLaVA-7B; per-step overhead vs. Adam is not benchmarked.

Methodology 方法

  1. Dual-stream orthogonalization. Slow (μs > μf) and fast momentum streams, both Newton–Schulz orthogonalized, then projected to ℝd via a fixed Rademacher matrix Π.
  2. SLERP fusion + codebook. Spherical interpolation of projected directions, normalized, snapped to nearest centroid under cosine similarity; EMA sufficient statistics.
  3. Memory-guided correction. Short-term angular-band filter (deflate over-aligned components) + long-term importance-weighted orthogonality on frozen past-task centroids.
  4. Proximal lifting. Closed-form least-squares lift back to parameter space, applied as update with weight decay.

Strengths

  • Unified theoretical framework bridging standard and continual training forgetting.
  • Elegant scalable codebook via random projection — no raw gradient storage, grows only as log|θ|.
  • Strong empirical wins across vision, VLM, and LM pretraining in one paper.
  • Theory-backed distance preservation (JL lemma) and memory-protection guarantee (Theorem 4).

Limitations

  • Multiple new hyperparameters: codebook size C, projection dim d, angular bands εst/εlt, interpolation strength ξ.
  • LLM scale ceiling tested only at GPT-2 and LLaVA-7B — no billion-parameter evidence.
  • Per-step wall-clock overhead vs. Adam/Muon is not clearly benchmarked.
  • Proximal lifting is approximate (Π non-invertible); distortion impact not deeply analyzed.
  • Codebook collapse prevention adds another tuning knob.

Questions for Further Exploration 待探索

  1. Does the codebook overhead stay manageable when FOGO is dropped into billion-parameter LLM training?
  2. What is FOGO's per-step wall-clock cost vs. Adam and Muon on modern GPUs at realistic batch sizes?
  3. Can the codebook memory be transferred or shared across related tasks to bootstrap continual adaptation?
  4. How does FOGO interact with mixed precision, gradient accumulation, and data-parallel sharding?
  5. Is short-term forgetting specific to SGD-style methods, or does it survive into second-order optimizers?
