Accuracy on the hardest Vision-Grounded variant — questions written from the image alone, with no source text.

02 WHY THIS MATTERS

It isolates whether VLMs truly see or merely guess — central to evaluating multimodal reliability and deployment safety.

03 CRITICAL EDGE

GRPO tested on one 4B model with LoRA — unclear if the mitigation scales to larger VLMs.

§04

Methodology

方法

Phrasing-controlled design. 540 images are held fixed; only the question wording varies across four variants with decreasing reliance on source text:

① Question-Guided — rewrite the source question harder · ② Subquestion-Guided — synthesise perceptual sub-questions · ③ Multi-Signal — fuse chat-elicited sub-questions · ④ Vision-Grounded — written from the image alone, no source text.

Judging. Qwen3-14B acts as an LLM-judge with binary yes/no equivalence. Difficulty and similarity are scored by Claude; three human annotators re-check a subset. The no-image ablation runs the same questions image-free on six open-weight models.

Mitigation. GRPO trains per-variant LoRA adapters on Qwen3.5-4B (vision tower frozen) with a format + correctness reward, evaluated on a 100-sample held-out split and a 200-question OOD set from six provenance-clean sources.

Strengths

  • Elegant control: fix the image, vary only the phrasing — the right way to isolate textual-prior reliance.
  • The no-image ablation is the gold-standard diagnostic for genuinely image-dependent answering.
  • Comprehensive coverage: 11 VLMs across open and proprietary families.
  • In-context exemplars matched to the variant construction recover accuracy — proves the deficit is missing grounding, not unanswerability.
  • OOD transfer is a de-confounded test: the GRPO reward never sees those source datasets.

Limitations

  • GRPO tested on a single 4B backbone with LoRA — scale-up behaviour is unknown.
  • 100-sample test split is too small to support per-category claims.
  • Lenient answer extraction (fallback to full completion when </answer> is missing) may inflate reported gains.
  • Difficulty and similarity ratings come from a single LLM (Claude) — not fully independent ground truth.
  • Question generation depends on Gemini-2.5-Pro — possible generator-model bias.
  • Some categories are thin: Visual Math has only 20 samples.

Questions for Further Exploration

开放问题
  1. Does the textual-prior reliance finding generalise to video-language or audio-visual models?
  2. How does the GRPO mitigation scale to 7B+ or 70B+ models with full fine-tuning?
  3. Can textual-prior reliance be measured during pretraining, and does it decrease naturally with scale?
  4. Would adversarial training against textual priors (e.g. a contrastive loss between with-image and no-image outputs) outperform GRPO?
  5. Is the open–proprietary gap on Vision-Grounded driven by vision-encoder quality or by language-model capacity?
