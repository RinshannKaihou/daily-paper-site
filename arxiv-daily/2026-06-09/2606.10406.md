> **一句话总结：** 提出FOGO优化器，将遗忘重新定义为通用优化现象（非仅限于持续学习），通过谱正交化动量更新+随机投影码本记忆+近端提升三步机制，在类别不平衡分类、持续视觉学习、LLaVA-7B微调和GPT-2预训练中一致优于Adam和Muon。

> **TL;DR:** Introduces FOGO, a scalable optimizer that treats forgetting as a general optimization phenomenon and mitigates it through spectrally orthogonalized momentum updates combined with a compact codebook memory built on random projection, consistently outperforming Adam and Muon across class-imbalanced, continual, and LLM training settings.

## 核心贡献

- 将遗忘重新定义为通用优化现象：标准训练中主导梯度方向会抑制稀有但有用的更新方向，造成短期遗忘；当这些知识不被重新访问时，短期遗忘累积为长期遗忘
- 提出FOGO优化器：双时间尺度（慢/快）正交化动量流 → 随机投影到低维空间 → 球面码本记忆存储代表性方向 → 短期角带过滤和长期近正交保护 → 近端提升回原始空间
- 证明在随机投影空间中码本记忆的距离保持特性（基于Johnson-Lindenstrauss引理），且存储开销仅随参数维度对数增长
- 理论证明记忆保护在活跃学习方向上减少更新能量，同时保持不受约束的方向不变（Theorem 4）

## Key Contributions

- **Unified forgetting view**: Shows that forgetting is not confined to continual learning but occurs in standard training—dominant mini-batch gradients suppress rare update directions (Theorem 1 + Corollaries 2, 3), validated on class-imbalanced CIFAR-10
- **FOGO optimizer architecture**: Dual-stream (slow/fast) orthogonalized momentum → spherical interpolation fusion → random projection to d-dimensional space → online codebook memory with EMA centroids → short-term angular-band filtering + long-term near-orthogonal protection → proximal least-squares lifting back to parameter space
- **Scalable directional memory**: Codebook in projected space (d ≪ |θ|) with Johnson-Lindenstrauss distance preservation, growing only logarithmically in parameter dimension—no raw data storage required
- **Theoretical guarantees**: Memory protection preserves learned directions (Theorem 4); codebook collapse prevention via decorrelation and re-seeding

## Methodology

FOGO operates in four stages per step:

1. **Dual-stream orthogonalization**: Slow (μs > μf) and fast momentum streams capture persistent trends and recent changes respectively, both orthogonalized via Newton-Schulz iterations and projected to d-dimensional space via fixed Rademacher random matrix Π
2. **Fusion + codebook**: Projected directions are fused via spherical interpolation (SLERP); fused vectors are normalized and assigned to nearest codebook centroids under cosine similarity, with EMA sufficient statistics
3. **Memory-guided correction**: Short-term correction removes conflicting components and deflates over-aligned ones; long-term correction imposes importance-weighted orthogonality constraints on frozen past-task centroids
4. **Proximal lifting**: Corrected directions are lifted back to original parameter space via proximal least-squares (closed-form solution), then applied as parameter updates with weight decay

The theoretical analysis (Theorem 1) shows that off-subspace gradient mass grows only through subspace drift and newly injected energy—when both are small, non-dominant directions contract progressively, causing forgetting.

## Results & Highlights

- **Class-imbalanced CIFAR-10** (5 rare classes at 10% data): FOGO achieves 0.92 rare-class accuracy vs. 0.88 for Adam, with significantly lower forgetting
- **Continual visual learning** (domain and class shifts): Consistently improves retention and convergence over Adam and Muon
- **LLaVA-7B fine-tuning** (visual question answering): Improves knowledge retention over Adam baseline
- **GPT-2 pretraining**: Faster convergence and better final performance than Adam and Muon
- Controlled experiments confirm the causal chain: persistent gradient conflict → directional bias → per-step loss degradation on suppressed groups
- Random batching mitigates short-term forgetting in balanced settings (explaining why it was previously overlooked) but fails under imbalance or distribution shift

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The unification of standard training forgetting with continual learning forgetting is novel, and the combination of spectral orthogonalization + random projection codebook + proximal lifting is technically sophisticated and original.
- **Rigor:** Strong theoretical foundations (Theorems 1, 4 with proofs) and comprehensive empirical evaluation across four diverse settings. The controlled CIFAR-10 experiments clearly demonstrate the causal mechanism.
- **Reproducibility:** The algorithm is well-specified with all hyperparameters described. Newton-Schulz iterations and random projections are standard components. Appendices provide additional details.
- **Significance:** Potentially high impact if the approach scales well to larger models. The insight that forgetting is a general optimization phenomenon (not just continual learning) reframes an important problem.
- **Red flags:** The optimizer has significantly more moving parts than Adam/Muon (dual streams, codebook, angular filtering, proximal lifting)—tuning complexity could be a practical barrier. The LLM experiments (GPT-2, LLaVA-7B) are relatively small-scale compared to current frontier models. Per-step overhead compared to Adam is not clearly quantified.

## Strengths

- Unified theoretical framework for understanding forgetting across standard and continual training
- The random projection codebook is an elegant, scalable alternative to storing raw gradients
- Strong empirical results across diverse settings (vision, VLM, language model pretraining)
- Theoretical guarantees on memory protection and distance preservation

## Limitations

- Significant algorithmic complexity compared to Adam/Muon—multiple new hyperparameters (codebook size C, projection dimension d, angular bands ϵst/ϵlt, interpolation strength ξ)
- LLM experiments limited to GPT-2 and LLaVA-7B; unclear how FOGO scales to billion-parameter models
- Per-step computational overhead not clearly benchmarked against Adam/Muon
- The proximal lifting step introduces approximation error (Π is not invertible)—the impact of this distortion is not thoroughly analyzed
- Codebook collapse prevention adds further tuning complexity

## Relevance

本文对优化器设计和持续学习研究有重要贡献。FOGO的"遗忘是通用优化现象"视角对理解LLM训练中的数据效率、灾难性遗忘和训练动态有直接启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does FOGO scale to training LLMs with billions of parameters—does the codebook overhead remain manageable?
2. What is the per-step wall-clock overhead of FOGO compared to Adam and Muon on modern GPUs?
3. Can the codebook memory be shared or transferred across related tasks to accelerate continual adaptation?
4. How does FOGO interact with other training techniques (mixed precision, gradient accumulation, data parallelism)?
5. Is the short-term forgetting observed in standard training specific to SGD-style optimizers, or does it also affect second-order methods?
