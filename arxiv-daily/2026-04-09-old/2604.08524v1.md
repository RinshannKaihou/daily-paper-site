## 一句话总结

本文通过提出多 token 激活修补框架，对拒绝引导向量进行机制分析，发现不同引导方法共享功能可互换的电路，且引导主要通过注意力 OV 电路而非 QK 电路传播效果，同时引导向量可稀疏化 90-99% 而几乎不损失性能。

## 核心贡献

1. **多 token 激活修补框架**: 将传统仅适用于单 token 的电路发现方法扩展至多 token 引导生成场景，适配 EAP-IG 方法用于分析引导向量的因果机制。
2. **跨方法电路一致性**: 发现 DIM、NTP、PO 三种不同引导方法在相同层应用时，利用高度可互换的电路（>90% 重叠），尽管向量间余弦相似度仅为 0.10-0.42。
3. **OV 主导、QK 可忽略**: 通过冻结 QK 电路实验，证明仅造成 ~8.75% 性能下降，而冻结 OV 电路则导致 71.75% 性能下降。
4. **引导值向量（SVV）分解**: 提出数学分解方法，将引导向量在注意力头中的直接效应分解为语义可解释的成分，即使原始引导向量本身不可解释。
5. **稀疏化发现**: 基于激活修补的梯度稀疏化可将引导向量稀疏化 90-99%，且不同方法收敛于共享的重要维度子空间。

## Methodology

The authors study refusal steering on two open LLMs: **Gemma 2 2B Instruct** and **Llama 3.2 3B Instruct**. Their methodology proceeds in four stages:

**Circuit Discovery via Multi-Token Activation Patching.** The authors adapt Edge Attribution Patching with Integrated Gradients (EAP-IG) to steered multi-token generation. The key adaptation treats the steered representation as "clean" and the base (unsteered) representation as "corrupt," computing the indirect effect of each edge via gradients taken at linearly increasing steering coefficients. They process each decoded token position sequentially via teacher forcing and aggregate importance scores.

**Cross-Method Circuit Comparison.** Three steering vector methodologies are compared: (1) Difference-in-Means (DIM), a non-learning baseline; (2) Next Token Prediction (NTP), which learns vectors via the language modeling objective; and (3) Preference Optimization (PO), which uses contrastive preference pairs. Circuits are extracted using a greedy graph construction algorithm, and faithfulness is measured as the fraction of the full steering effect recovered by the circuit.

**Attention Mechanism Analysis.** The authors perform a mathematical decomposition showing that the steering vector's direct effect on attention outputs factorizes into an input-dependent coefficient times an input-invariant **steering value vector (SVV)**, defined as `svv(s) = (s . gamma) W_OV_h`. They ablate QK vs. OV circuits by caching unsteered attention weights and patching them during steered forward passes.

**Sparsification.** Using the dimension-level indirect effect from activation patching, the authors propose gradient-based sparsification: zeroing dimensions whose normalized contribution falls below a threshold. They compare against IE-based, bottom-k, and random dropout baselines.

## Key Results

- **Circuit faithfulness**: ~10% of model edges suffice to recover 85% of the steering effect on multi-token generation for both models. The circuit complement achieves near-zero faithfulness.
- **Circuit interchangeability**: The minimum-faithful circuit from one steering method (e.g., DIM) recovers high faithfulness when applied to another method (e.g., NTP or PO). Random circuits at 2x the minimum-faithful size achieve <10% faithfulness.
- **OV dominance**: Freezing the QK circuit at every layer during steering drops performance by only 8.75% on average across both models and datasets. Freezing OV drops performance by 71.75%. The SVV ablation accounts for 74.9% of the OV performance drop.
- **Semantic interpretability via SVV**: Logit lens on individual SVVs reveals interpretable tokens related to refusal/harmfulness (e.g., "forbid," "prohibition," "dangerous") even when the raw steering vector is uninterpretable (e.g., NTP on Gemma 2, DIM on Llama 3.2).
- **Sparsity**: Gradient-based sparsification retains steering performance up to ~90% sparsity on Gemma 2 and ~95%+ on Llama 3.2. Different methods converge to a statistically significant shared subspace (IoU above random at all thresholds, p < 0.05 via hypergeometric test).

## Critical Assessment

**Strengths.** The paper tackles a genuinely important question -- how do steering vectors actually work inside the model? -- with rigorous mechanistic tools. The multi-token activation patching extension is a legitimate methodological contribution that addresses a real limitation of prior circuit discovery work. The finding that QK is largely irrelevant is both surprising and practically useful, as it suggests that steering operates primarily by modifying "what information is written" rather than "where information is read from." The SVV decomposition is elegant and offers a new lens for interpreting steering vectors. The cross-method circuit interchangeability result is strong and well-supported by both overlap metrics and functional faithfulness tests.

**Weaknesses.** (1) The analysis is limited to only two models (Gemma 2 2B, Llama 3.2 3B), both relatively small, and only a single concept (refusal). Whether the OV-dominance and circuit interchangeability findings generalize to larger models or other concepts (e.g., persona, reasoning style) remains an open question the authors acknowledge but do not address. (2) The MLP contribution is substantial (~47-53% of incoming top edges go to MLPs) but is largely unanalyzed. The paper notes this as future work, but it leaves a significant gap in the mechanistic story. (3) The sparsity results, while impressive, raise questions about the original steering vectors' efficiency -- if 90-99% of dimensions can be zeroed out, it suggests the learning procedures may not be producing optimally targeted vectors, which somewhat undermines claims about these methods' effectiveness. (4) The EAP-IG approximation with only T=10 integration steps may introduce approximation errors that are not quantified. (5) The evaluation relies heavily on ASR (Attack Success Rate) as the primary metric, which does not capture generation quality degradation from steering, a known practical concern.

**Significance.** This work represents a solid step toward demystifying steering vectors. The OV-dominance finding, if it generalizes, has clear implications for designing more efficient and interpretable steering interventions. The SVV decomposition is a reusable analytical tool that could benefit the broader interpretability community. However, the limited scope (one concept, two small models) tempers the immediate impact.
