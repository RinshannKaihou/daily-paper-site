LINE: LLM-based Iterative Neuron Explanations for Vision Models

arXiv: 2604.08039v1

Date: 2026-04-09

Tags: [neuron-labeling, interpretability, vision-models, LLM, text-to-image, mechanistic-interpretability, black-box]

Summary

LINE (LLM-based Iterative Neuron Explanations) is a training-free, black-box, iterative framework for automatically labeling individual neurons in vision models with human-interpretable textual concepts. The method closes the loop between an LLM (which proposes candidate concepts), a text-to-image generator (which synthesizes images for those concepts), and the target vision model (which provides neuron activations). Through iterative refinement over multiple rounds, LINE progressively discovers better concept descriptions than any single-pass or vocabulary-constrained approach.

Core Pipeline

  1. Initialization -- The concept scoreboard is seeded with the top-5 and 5 random labels from the ImageNet-1K class vocabulary, scored against 50 images per class.
  2. Concept Proposal -- An LLM (Llama 3.1 8B) proposes a new concept description based on the current scoreboard and a generation history that prevents repeated proposals.
  3. Image Synthesis -- A text-to-image model (SDXL) generates diverse synthetic images for the proposed concept using varied angles and lighting conditions.
  4. Activation Extraction -- The synthetic images are passed through the target vision model to collect the neuron's activation values.
  5. Concept Scoring -- A simple mean-activation scorer replaces AUC (which saturates near 1.0) and MAD (which is control-set dependent).
  6. Scoreboard Update -- A stochastic replacement strategy balances exploitation (keeping high-scoring concepts) with exploration.
  7. Final Summary Step -- After N iterations, the LLM generalizes the top-3 concepts into a single higher-order description, which is scored and appended.

This loop repeats for a configurable number of iterations (default 10 + 1 summary step), and the highest-scoring concept from the final scoreboard is returned as the neuron label.

Key Results

  • CoSy Benchmark: LINE achieves state-of-the-art AUC improvements of up to 0.18 on ImageNet and 0.05 on Places365 across ResNet50, ResNet18, and ViT-B/16, with statistically significant margins (p < 0.05) in nearly all configurations.
  • Beyond Predefined Vocabularies: The LLM-generated label wins in 22-39% of neurons depending on the dataset, demonstrating that fixed vocabularies systematically miss relevant concepts.
  • Visual Explanations: LINE produces interpretable, natural-looking synthetic images competitive with gradient-dependent activation maximization methods (DiffExplainer, DEXTER), without requiring differentiability. It consistently achieves higher activations than DEXTER while producing cleaner visuals than DiffExplainer.
  • Causal Validation: Image ablation experiments confirm that removing the labeled concept from real images causes significant activation drops (often near zero), supporting the fidelity of assigned labels.

Ablation Studies

  • Iteration count: Extending from 10 to 20 iterations yields monotonically increasing activation scores. The most substantial gains occur around iteration 10, but marginal improvements persist even at step 20. The final summary step is shown to be strictly necessary, as it most frequently yields the overall best concept.
  • T2I model choice: Comparing SD1.5, SDXL, and FLUX.1[dev] shows no statistically significant difference in activation scores, though FLUX.1[dev] is most frequently selected as the winner (43% vs. 27% for SDXL). Switching T2I models changes the top concept in 80% of cases, but the resulting descriptions remain semantically consistent (e.g., "bookshop" vs. "bookcase").

Strengths

  1. Fully black-box: No access to gradients or internal model structure is needed beyond forward-pass activations. This enables compatibility with any architecture and modern T2I models that lack differentiability.
  2. Open-vocabulary: By leveraging LLM generative capabilities, LINE escapes the constraint of predefined concept lists, capturing abstract and higher-order concepts (e.g., "farming" from "strawberry, harvester, tractor").
  3. Transparent process: The full iteration history, LLM reasoning traces, and generated images are retained, enabling polysemanticity analysis and audit trails.
  4. Scalable and modular: Each component (LLM, T2I model, scorer) can be independently upgraded.

Weaknesses and Limitations

  1. Initialization dependency: When target neurons are unrelated to the ImageNet class vocabulary (e.g., Salient ImageNet bias features), the initial scoreboard can misdirect the search, causing LINE to miss relevant features entirely.
  2. Polysemantic convergence: LINE tends to converge on the dominant semantic direction of a neuron, potentially ignoring secondary concepts. The authors suggest iterative restarts with exclusion prompts as a remedy but do not implement this.
  3. Domain mismatch for specialized imaging: In domains like medical or satellite imaging where high-fidelity T2I generators are unavailable, LINE's reliance on synthetic images becomes a fundamental bottleneck.
  4. LLM stalling behavior: The iterative traces reveal that the LLM occasionally proposes duplicate or semantically redundant concepts (e.g., "separation" appearing at steps 7 and 10), wasting iterations.
  5. Computational cost: Each iteration requires an LLM inference call and T2I generation for multiple images, making the full pipeline substantially more expensive than single-pass methods like CLIP-Dissect or INVERT.
  6. Evaluation scope: The causal validation via image ablation reveals that the generative editing model itself introduces artifacts (resolution changes, incomplete concept removal), making it difficult to disambiguate labeling failures from generation failures.

摘要 (Chinese Summary)

LINE（基于LLM的迭代神经元解释）是一种无需训练、黑盒、迭代式的视觉模型神经元自动标注框架。该方法在LLM（提出候选概念）、文生图模型（合成概念图像）和目标视觉模型（提取神经元激活）之间建立闭环迭代优化流程，逐步发现比单次前向传播或固定词表方法更优的概念描述。

核心流程：初始化阶段利用ImageNet-1K类别词表为概念记分板填充种子标签；每轮迭代中，LLM基于当前记分板提出新概念，T2I模型生成对应的多样化合成图像，目标模型提取神经元激活值，简单的均值评分函数量化概念质量，记分板通过随机替换策略在利用与探索之间取得平衡；最后一步汇总迭代中LLM将top-3概念归纳为更高阶的全局描述。

主要结果：在CoSy基准上，LINE在ImageNet上AUC提升最高达0.18，Places365上提升0.05，涵盖ResNet50、ResNet18和ViT-B/16三种架构，统计显著性检验几乎全部通过。LLM生成的新标签在22-39%的神经元中成为最终获胜者，证明固定词表的固有局限。LINE生成的视觉解释在可解释性上优于梯度依赖的激活最大化方法（DiffExplainer、DEXTER）。图像消融因果实验验证了标签的保真度——移除标注概念后神经元激活值显著下降。

优势：纯黑盒设定、开放词表、透明可审计、模块化可扩展。局限：初始化依赖词表质量、对多义神经元可能仅捕获主语义方向、在医学/卫星等专业图像领域受限于T2I模型能力、LLM偶尔重复提出相似概念浪费迭代、计算开销显著高于单次前向方法。
