# Robust Length Prediction: A Perspective from Heavy-Tailed Prompt-Conditioned Distributions

**Authors**: Jing Wang, Yu-Yang Qian, Ke Xue, Chao Qian, Peng Zhao, Zhi-Hua Zhou

**Affiliations**: Nanjing University (LAMDA Group, National Key Laboratory for Novel Software Technology)

## Summary

This paper addresses a fundamental but previously overlooked issue in LLM output-length prediction: the fact that a fixed prompt under a fixed model and decoding configuration does not produce a deterministic output length, but rather induces a **prompt-conditioned output length distribution** with heavy-tailed characteristics. The authors argue that the standard practice of using a single sampled output length as the training label is statistically misaligned, and propose the **ProD (Prompt-conditioned length Distribution)** framework to construct robust supervision targets from repeated independent generations of the same prompt.

## Key Observations

1. **Noise radius**: Even under identical decoding settings, the same prompt produces output lengths with substantial stochastic spread. Across 8 model-scenario settings (Qwen-2.5-7B and Llama-3-8B on Math, Coding, LongSequence, and Chat), the median noise radius ranges from 16.1 to 42.9 tokens, with normalized noise ratios of 11.5%--18.2%.

2. **Heavy-tailed behavior**: The prompt-conditioned length distributions exhibit heavy tails, with max-to-median ratios reaching up to 4.03x. This means a few unusually long generations can shift the sample mean far from the stable central tendency, making single-sample labels unreliable.

## Method: ProD

The core idea is to draw r independent generations per training prompt and construct robust supervision targets. Both variants reuse the served LLM's last-layer hidden states (last token) as input representation with a shared two-layer MLP predictor head that outputs logits over K length bins.

- **ProD-M (Median supervision)**: Computes the sample median of r output lengths, discretizes it onto the K-bin grid as a one-hot target, and trains with standard cross-entropy. This compresses the heavy-tailed distribution into a single robust point target aligned with the Bayes-optimal prediction under MAE.

- **ProD-D (Distributional supervision)**: Projects all r sampled lengths onto the K bins to form an empirical histogram (soft target), and trains with soft cross-entropy. This preserves the full prompt-conditioned uncertainty rather than collapsing to a scalar.

At inference, both variants extract the **median** of the predicted bin distribution (not argmax or expectation), which is more robust to skewed predictions.

## Theoretical Contributions

Under a linear surrogate model with heavy-tailed noise (bounded (1+epsilon)-th moment), the authors prove (Theorem 1) that using repeated-sampling median labels for ridge regression yields an estimation error bound where the failure probability term decays as 4N*exp(-r/8), i.e., **exponentially with the repeated-sampling budget r**. When r >= 8*log(4N/delta), the bound holds with probability at least 1-2*delta. This formally justifies the statistical value of repeated sampling for constructing robust labels.

## Experiments

**Setup**: Qwen-2.5-7B and Llama-3-8B on four benchmarks (GSM8K/Math, MBPP/Coding, LongBench/LongSequence, LMSYS-Chat-1M/Chat), with 16 repeated generations per prompt, temperature 0.8. Ground truth is the 16-sample median.

**Main results** (Table 1):
- ProD-D achieves the lowest average MAE: 56.78 on Qwen and 44.31 on Llama, outperforming the strongest baseline TRAIL-Last (64.23/49.08) by 11.6% and 9.7% respectively.
- ProD-D even falls below the noise radius on several scenarios (e.g., 30.35 vs. 32.94 on Qwen-Math), indicating it estimates the population median more accurately than the finite-sample average deviation.
- On some scenarios, ProD-D achieves up to **25% reduction** in prediction error over previous SOTA.

**Ablation -- single-sample supervision** (Tables 2, 3): When all methods are trained with only one sampled length per prompt, performance degrades significantly. ProD-M's average MAE rises from 60.74 to 80.93 on Qwen, confirming the gain comes from robust target construction, not architecture.

**Budget fairness** (Figure 2): Under a fixed total inference budget, concentrating repeated observations on fewer prompts consistently matches or outperforms single-sample methods trained on the full prompt set. For example, on LongSequence/Qwen, ProD-D with only ~1263 unique prompts (3 repeated samples) achieves 57.83 MAE vs. TRAIL-Last's 67.91 with full coverage.

## Critical Analysis

**Strengths**:
- Identifies and rigorously characterizes a real, previously ignored statistical phenomenon (heavy-tailed prompt-conditioned length distributions) that fundamentally undermines existing single-label approaches.
- The theoretical analysis cleanly isolates the statistical benefit of repeated sampling, with the exponential decay in failure probability being a particularly clean result.
- The ProD-D variant is elegant: by training on the empirical distribution rather than a point estimate, it naturally captures uncertainty without requiring parametric assumptions.
- Budget-fairness experiments convincingly demonstrate practical viability -- the approach works even when total inference cost is held constant.
- The insight that the conditional median is Bayes-optimal under MAE for heavy-tailed distributions is simple but important.

**Weaknesses**:
- The heavy-tailed characterization relies on max-to-median ratios from only 10 representative prompts per setting (100 repeats each). While illustrative, a more systematic statistical test (e.g., formal heavy-tail tests on the full prompt pool) would strengthen the empirical claim.
- The paper focuses exclusively on the **prompt-only** setting. While the formulation in Section 2.2 accommodates iterative remaining-length prediction, no experiments validate the approach during decoding, which is the more practical serving scenario.
- The theoretical analysis uses a linear surrogate model that is acknowledged as not matching the deployed nonlinear predictor. The gap between theory and practice is honest but leaves open how well the guarantees transfer.
- The cost of collecting 16 repeated generations per training prompt is non-trivial. While budget-fairness experiments partially address this, the paper does not quantify the wall-clock time or dollar cost of data collection.
- Chat remains the hardest scenario (MAE roughly 2x the noise radius), suggesting the approach has limited headroom for open-ended generation where output length is inherently more uncertain.
- The concurrent work by Zheng et al. (2026) takes a parametric distribution fitting approach to the same observation; a direct empirical comparison would have been informative.

## Impact

This work reframes output-length prediction from deterministic regression to robust estimation from heavy-tailed conditional distributions, a conceptual shift with implications beyond LLM serving. The principle of using repeated sampling to construct robust supervision targets is generalizable to other stochastic prediction problems in ML systems. The practical gains (up to 25% MAE reduction) are significant for deployment scenarios where accurate length prediction directly translates to better GPU utilization and lower latency.

## 中文摘要

本文揭示了LLM输出长度预测中一个被忽视的基本问题：即使模型和解码配置固定，同一prompt产生的输出长度也不是确定值，而是服从一个具有**重尾特性**的prompt条件分布。作者指出，现有方法使用单次采样长度作为训练标签在统计上是错误的。

基于此，本文提出**ProD（Prompt-conditioned length Distribution）框架**，通过对同一prompt进行多次独立采样来构建鲁棒的监督目标。两个变体分别为：（1）**ProD-M**，使用多次采样的中位数作为点目标（在MAE准则下与贝叶斯最优预测对齐）；（2）**ProD-D**，将采样长度投影到分箱直方图上作为分布目标，保留完整的prompt条件不确定性。

理论上，在线性代理模型和重尾噪声假设下，作者证明了增加重复采样预算r可以使估计误差的失效概率以exp(-r/8)的速率指数衰减。在Qwen-2.5-7B和Llama-3-8B上的四个基准测试中，ProD-D在两个模型上均取得了最低的平均MAE（56.78和44.31），相比先前最优方法最多降低25%的预测误差。预算公平性实验进一步表明，即使在固定总推理预算下，将重复观测集中在较少prompt上仍能匹配甚至超越使用全量prompt的单样本方法。

本文的核心贡献在于将输出长度预测重新定义为从重尾条件分布中进行鲁棒估计的问题，这一概念转变对LLM服务系统及其他涉及随机预测的ML系统具有广泛意义。
