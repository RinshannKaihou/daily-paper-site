Faithful GRPO: Improving Visual Spatial Reasoning in Multimodal Language Models via Constrained Policy Optimization

Paper Summary

This paper identifies and addresses a critical but underexplored problem in multimodal reasoning models trained with RLVR (Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards): accuracy gains often come at the cost of reasoning quality. The authors observe that Chain-of-Thought (CoT) traces generated by such models frequently contradict the final answer and are poorly grounded in visual evidence. They propose Faithful GRPO (FGRPO), a constrained variant of GRPO that enforces logical consistency and visual grounding as hard constraints via Lagrangian dual ascent, rather than treating them as soft reward terms.

Key Contributions

  1. Systematic characterization of reasoning quality degradation in RLVR-trained MRMs along two axes: logical consistency (does the CoT entail the final answer?) and visual grounding (do reasoning steps accurately describe image content?). This degradation is observed not only in their own GRPO-trained models but also in contemporary MRMs like ViGoRL-Spatial and TreeVGR.
  1. Verifiable reward signals for trustworthy visual reasoning: a consistency reward via an LLM judge, a semantic grounding reward via per-sentence VLM scoring, and a spatial grounding reward based on CIoU matching of predicted bounding boxes.
  1. Faithful GRPO (FGRPO), which formulates a constrained optimization problem maximizing task accuracy subject to minimum thresholds on consistency and grounding rewards, enforced via Lagrangian dual ascent with decoupled advantage normalization.

Methodology

Problem Identification

The authors train a standard GRPO model with task reward (GRPO-T) on Qwen2.5-VL-7B using a two-stage pipeline (SFT followed by RL). Despite achieving 65.2% average accuracy, GRPO-T exhibits:

  • 26.1% inconsistency rate: over one in four samples have reasoning that contradicts the final prediction.
  • 72.7% semantic grounding score: over a quarter of visual claims are inaccurate.

Two failure modes are identified:

  • Logical Inconsistency: reasoning argues toward one conclusion but the model flips to a different final answer.
  • Visual Ungroundedness: reasoning steps describe objects, attributes, or spatial relationships that are inaccurate with respect to the image.

Reward Design

Three complementary reward signals are defined:

  • Consistency reward R_C: binary signal from a text-only LLM judge checking whether the CoT entails the final answer.
  • Semantic grounding reward R_S: per-sentence VLM judge scoring whether each reasoning step accurately describes image content (objects, attributes, spatial relationships).
  • Spatial grounding reward R_G: CIoU-based matching of predicted bounding boxes against ground-truth regions, using Hungarian matching.

The authors show that naively adding these as reward terms (additive or multiplicative) fails: additive shaping reduces inconsistency but sacrifices accuracy, while multiplicative gating degrades both.

FGRPO: Constrained Policy Optimization

The key insight is to treat consistency and grounding as hard constraints rather than soft reward terms. The optimization problem is:

$$\max_\theta \mathbb{E}[R_{task}(o)] \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \mathbb{E}[R_C(o)] \geq \tau_C, \quad \mathbb{E}[R_S(o)] \geq \tau_S, \quad \mathbb{E}[R_G(o)] \geq \tau_G$$

This is converted to an unconstrained Lagrangian with multipliers updated via dual ascent. The multipliers adaptively increase pressure on violated constraints and decrease on satisfied ones, creating an emergent curriculum.

Decoupled advantage normalization: Each signal (task, consistency, grounding) is independently group-normalized before combination, preventing high-variance signals from dominating or canceling out. The final advantage is:

$$\hat{A}_{FGRPO}(o_i^j) = \hat{A}_{task}(o_i^j) + \sum_{k \in \{C,S,G\}} \lambda_k \cdot \hat{A}_k(o_i^j)$$

Training Pipeline

  • Backbone: Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct and 3B-Instruct
  • SFT Stage: ~45K CoT traces distilled from Qwen2.5-VL-72B via MCTS with synthetic backtracking
  • RL Stage: ~49K samples with difficulty-based filtering, 8xH100 GPUs, G=5 rollouts per prompt
  • Constraint thresholds: tau_C=0.95, tau_S=0.95, tau_G=0.65

Experimental Results

Accuracy (Pass@1 on 7 spatial benchmarks)

| GRPO-T (7B) | 79.97 | 85.92 | 41.71 | 74.00 | 40.90 | 66.67 | 67.00 | 65.17 |

FGRPO achieves the best average accuracy among open-weight 7B models, outperforming all MRM baselines (TreeVGR: 62.36%, ViGoRL-Spatial: 62.34%, VL-Rethinker: 63.41%) and the GRPO-T baseline (+2.0%).

Reasoning Quality

  • Inconsistency rate: FGRPO reduces from 26.1% (GRPO-T) to 1.7%, compared to 26.0% (TreeVGR) and 16.2% (ViGoRL).
  • Semantic grounding: FGRPO achieves 86.0% vs. 72.7% (GRPO-T), a +13.3pp improvement. Largest gains on MindCube (+22.8pp) and OmniSpatial (+21.1pp).
  • FGRPO virtually eliminates inconsistency on six of seven benchmarks.

Ablation Studies

  • Coupled vs. decoupled advantage: Naively combining signals before normalization fails (additive: -0.2 accuracy; multiplicative: -1.7 accuracy). Decoupled normalization is essential.
  • Consistency-only constraint: Already improves both accuracy (+1.0) and inconsistency (0.54%), confirming signal cancellation is resolved.
  • Adaptive vs. fixed multipliers: Fixed multipliers achieve 66.32% accuracy; adaptive dual ascent further improves to 67.16% by reallocating optimization pressure.
  • Accuracy vs. inconsistency trajectory: GRPO-T's inconsistency more than triples during training (7.4% to 26.1%) as accuracy climbs, while FGRPO keeps inconsistency near or below the starting rate throughout.

Critical Analysis

Strengths

  • The paper identifies a genuinely important problem: reasoning quality degradation in RLVR-trained models is pervasive yet underappreciated. The empirical evidence across seven benchmarks and five MRM baselines is compelling.
  • The constrained optimization formulation is principled and well-motivated. Treating consistency and grounding as hard constraints rather than soft rewards is a clean conceptual contribution.
  • The decoupled advantage normalization addresses a real technical issue (signal cancellation in GRPO's group normalization) with a practical solution.
  • Strong empirical results: FGRPO simultaneously improves accuracy AND reasoning quality, challenging the perceived tradeoff between the two.
  • The adaptive Lagrangian dual ascent removes the need for manual reward weight tuning, which is practically valuable.

Weaknesses and Limitations

  • Computational cost: FGRPO requires running online VLM judges (Qwen3-VL-30B) for consistency and semantic grounding rewards during training, adding significant overhead. The paper does not report wall-clock training time comparisons.
  • Judge dependence: The consistency and grounding rewards rely on LLM/VLM judges, which themselves may be imperfect. While the authors validate judge agreement (kappa=0.997), this is against GPT-5 rather than human annotators. The quality of training is only as good as the judge.
  • Reward masking to correct predictions only: Masking R_C and R_S to samples where R_acc > 0 means the model receives no consistency/grounding feedback on incorrect answers. This design choice avoids reward hacking but may limit the model's ability to improve reasoning on hard samples.
  • Limited benchmark diversity: All seven benchmarks focus on spatial reasoning. It remains unclear whether the approach generalizes to other multimodal reasoning domains (e.g., mathematical, temporal, causal reasoning).
  • Marginal accuracy gains over GRPO-T: The +2.0% average accuracy improvement, while consistent, is modest. On some benchmarks (MMVP, RWQA, SAT-Real), FGRPO actually underperforms GRPO-T. The primary benefit is in reasoning quality rather than task accuracy.
  • Comparison fairness: FGRPO uses curated training data and MCTS-based SFT that may partly explain the strong baseline (GRPO-T already outperforms most MRMs). The contribution of FGRPO over this strong baseline is relatively incremental in accuracy terms.
  • 3B results less conclusive: The 3B results show smaller gains (61.33% to 62.39%) with some benchmark regressions, suggesting scaling behavior deserves further study.

Conclusion

This paper makes a valuable contribution by highlighting and addressing the reasoning quality problem in RLVR-trained multimodal models. The core message -- that accuracy alone is an incomplete measure of reasoning ability, and that faithful reasoning actually enables better answers -- is important for the field. FGRPO provides a principled and effective framework for enforcing reasoning quality through constrained optimization. The approach is well-grounded in constrained MDP theory and the empirical evaluation is thorough. The main limitation is the computational overhead of online judges and the modest accuracy gains over the already-strong GRPO-T baseline, though the dramatic improvements in reasoning quality (inconsistency from 26.1% to 1.7%) are arguably the more significant result.

中文摘要

本文研究并解决了一个关键但被忽视的问题：使用可验证奖励的强化学习（RLVR）训练的多模态推理模型，其准确性提升往往以推理质量下降为代价。作者观察到，模型生成的思维链（CoT）经常与最终答案矛盾，且缺乏视觉依据。

作者沿两个互补维度系统刻画了推理质量退化现象：逻辑一致性（推理过程是否蕴含最终答案）和视觉扎根性（推理步骤是否准确描述图像中的物体、属性和空间关系）。在七个空间推理基准测试中，GRPO-T 模型尽管达到 65.2% 的准确率，却存在 26.1% 的不一致率和仅 72.7% 的语义扎根得分。该问题同样存在于 ViGoRL-Spatial、TreeVGR 等当代多模态推理模型中。

为此，作者提出了 Faithful GRPO (FGRPO)，将一致性和视觉扎根性作为硬约束（而非软奖励项），通过拉格朗日对偶上升法求解约束优化问题。关键技术贡献包括：(1) 为一致性和扎根性定义了可验证的奖励信号；(2) 解耦优势归一化，避免 GRPO 组内归一化导致的信号抵消；(3) 自适应拉格朗日乘子更新，形成自动优先级的涌现课程。

在 Qwen2.5-VL-7B 和 3B 骨干网络上的实验表明，FGRPO 将不一致率从 24.5% 降至 1.7%，语义扎根得分提升 +13 个百分点，同时将答案准确率提高 +2%（从 65.17% 到 67.16%），证明忠实推理和准确答案是互补目标。

核心贡献在于揭示了 RLVR 训练中的推理质量退化问题，并提供了基于约束优化的原则性解决方案。主要局限在于在线 VLM 判断器的计算开销、对判断器质量的依赖，以及相较于强基线 GRPO-T 的准确性提升幅度有限（+2%），但推理质量的大幅改善（不一致率降低至 1.7%）是更具意义的成果。
