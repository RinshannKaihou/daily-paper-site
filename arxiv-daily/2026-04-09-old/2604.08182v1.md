## 一句话总结

Wattlytics 是一个基于浏览器的交互式决策支持平台，首次将基准驱动的 GPU 性能缩放建模、DVFS 感知的功耗建模与多年期 TCO 分析统一集成，帮助 HPC 利益相关者在预算、功耗、性能等多重约束下做出最优 GPU 集群设计与采购决策。

## 核心贡献

1. **统一决策支持系统**：将基准性能模型、DVFS 功耗模型和可配置的多年期 TCO 核算整合到单一交互平台，支持 Pareto 最优基础设施设计。
2. **不确定性下的决策支持**：提供 elasticity（弹性）、Sobol 全局敏感性指数和 Monte Carlo 不确定性传播三种互补方法，量化 GPU 数量、频率、能源价格、系统寿命等参数对 work-per-TCO 等指标的影响。
3. **系统级案例研究**：在 GROMACS 和 AMBER 分子动力学负载上，揭示非直觉设计权衡——在预算或能源约束下，能效更高的中低端 GPU（如 L4、L40S）通过规模扩展可在单位成本产出上超越高端 GPU（如 GH200、H100）。

## Methodology

Wattlytics adopts a modular two-tier architecture: (i) an interactive web frontend with input, analysis, visualization, and collaboration layers, and (ii) a modeling backend integrating four empirically validated model families.

**Performance modeling**: Single-GPU throughput is modeled as a linear function of GPU graphics frequency, capped by a maximum throughput ceiling. Aggregate cluster performance accounts for multi-GPU efficiency losses via a compounding factor combining inter-node and intra-node scalability terms.

**Power modeling**: A piecewise linear-quadratic function captures DVFS behavior across two regimes: a low-frequency leakage-dominated (linear) regime and a high-frequency voltage-dominated (quadratic) regime, with coefficients fitted via Levenberg-Marquardt nonlinear least squares. The TDP serves as an upper bound.

**TCO modeling**: Total cost decomposes into capital expenditure (GPU, server, infrastructure, facility, software costs) and operational expenditure (electricity, PUE, maintenance, depreciation, software subscriptions, utilization inefficiency, heat recovery credits) over the system lifetime. Both fixed and variable annual cost components are explicitly modeled.

**Sensitivity analysis**: Three complementary measures are provided: (1) analytical elasticity (local signed sensitivity), (2) Sobol total-order indices via Jansen estimator with 2000 Monte Carlo samples capturing global variance contributions including nonlinear interactions, and (3) one-at-a-time Monte Carlo for isolating individual parameter uncertainty propagation.

**Supported GPUs**: L4, A40, L40, L40S, A100, H100, GH200 (Ampere, Ada Lovelace, Hopper, Grace Hopper architectures).

**Workloads evaluated**: Six GROMACS benchmarks and eleven AMBER benchmarks spanning small to large atom counts.

## Key Results

- Under a fixed EUR 10M budget with ideal multi-GPU efficiency, L4 and L40S achieve **3.2--3.8x higher work-per-TCO** than GH200/H100 for GROMACS, and **2.5--4.5x higher** for AMBER, by deploying many more GPUs at lower per-unit cost and power.
- However, even modest multi-GPU efficiency losses (eta = 0.995) can **reverse** this conclusion, favoring fewer high-end GPUs.
- GPU hardware cost is the dominant driver of work-per-TCO (elasticity up to -76%), while operational uncertainties (node maintenance, electricity, system usage) are the primary risk drivers.
- Moderate GPU frequency reduction (DVFS tuning) can improve work-per-TCO at zero capital expense by finding "knee points" where power savings exceed performance losses.
- Optimal GPU selection is **workload-dependent**: L4 dominates only under low-intensity AMBER benchmarks, while L40S is consistently the best choice for GROMACS under budget constraints.
- Rankings are largely stable across typical HPC system lifetimes (3--7 years), with longer lifetimes slightly favoring energy-efficient GPUs.

## Critical Assessment

**Strengths**:
- The first platform to genuinely integrate benchmark-driven performance, DVFS-aware power, and multi-year TCO in a single interactive tool -- a real gap in the HPC procurement landscape.
- Strong sensitivity analysis framework (elasticity + Sobol + Monte Carlo) gives users principled ways to reason about uncertainty, beyond simple best-case/worst-case analysis.
- Open-source and browser-based with zero installation, lowering the barrier to adoption. Reproducibility links (sitography) for all case studies is commendable.
- The case studies are well-structured as decision-maker questions (Q1--Q9), making the paper accessible to HPC operators and procurement officers.

**Limitations**:
- The performance model is a simple linear frequency-scaling function with a hard cap; it does not capture memory-bound versus compute-bound regime transitions within a single workload, which are well-documented in the DVFS literature.
- Power model coefficients are fitted per GPU per workload but no cross-GPU or cross-workload transferability analysis is provided. Users must supply their own benchmark data for workloads beyond GROMACS/AMBER.
- Multi-GPU scalability is treated as a single scalar parameter rather than modeled from measured strong/weak scaling curves, which limits realism for communication-intensive workloads.
- Only NVIDIA GPUs are supported (Ampere through Grace Hopper); AMD and Intel accelerators and upcoming architectures (Blackwell) are acknowledged as future work.
- CO2 / embodied carbon is explicitly excluded from the current TCO model, which weakens its sustainability claims.
- The evaluation uses qualitative cost tiers (Low/Medium/Very High) rather than actual procurement prices, limiting the precision of absolute TCO estimates.
- No comparison with actual procurement decisions or retrospective validation against real-world HPC deployments is provided.

**Overall**: Wattlytics addresses a genuine and timely need -- HPC procurement is increasingly constrained by energy and budget, yet no prior tool integrates all three dimensions interactively. The engineering contribution is substantial. However, the analytical models underlying the platform are deliberately simple (linear performance scaling, piecewise power), and the paper does not rigorously validate whether these simplifications distort decision outcomes compared to more sophisticated models. The claim of "research-grade analytical platform" would be stronger with cross-validation against real procurement case studies and support for more diverse hardware and workload types.
