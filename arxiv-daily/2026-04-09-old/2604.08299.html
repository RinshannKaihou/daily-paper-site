SeLaR: Selective Latent Reasoning in Large Language Models

arXiv: 2604.08299v1

Date: 2026-04-09

Tags: [latent-reasoning, chain-of-thought, entropy-gating, contrastive-regularization, training-free, soft-embeddings, LLM-reasoning]

SeLaR: Selective Latent Reasoning in Large Language Models

Authors: Renyu Fu, Guibo Luo

Venue: ACL 2026 (Main Conference, Camera-ready)

English Summary

Problem and Motivation

Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning forces language models to commit to a single discrete token at each decoding step, discarding information about alternative reasoning paths. Recent latent reasoning methods attempt to address this by replacing discrete tokens with soft embeddings (probability-weighted mixtures of token embeddings), but suffer from two key issues: (1) global activation -- applying soft embeddings indiscriminately at every step, including high-confidence ones where it introduces destabilizing perturbations; and (2) premature collapse -- soft embeddings quickly converge toward the highest-probability token, effectively degenerating back to greedy decoding.

The authors make an important empirical observation: during CoT decoding, the normalized entropy of the model's output distribution follows a clear long-tail pattern. Most steps are low-entropy (deterministic), where the model is confident, while a small fraction of steps are high-entropy (exploratory), where multiple candidates compete. This motivates a selective approach to latent reasoning.

Method: SeLaR

SeLaR (Selective Latent Reasoning) is a lightweight, training-free framework with two components:

  1. Entropy-gated selective mechanism: At each decoding step, the normalized entropy over the top-k tokens is computed. If the entropy is below a threshold $\tau$, standard discrete decoding is used (deterministic step). If above $\tau$, a soft embedding is constructed from the top-k candidates (exploratory step). This ensures latent reasoning is activated only at genuinely uncertain steps.
  1. Entropy-aware contrastive regularization: At exploratory steps, a repulsion term pushes the soft embedding away from the dominant (highest-probability) token's direction. The repulsion strength is scaled by the normalized entropy: stronger when the model is more uncertain, weaker when it is more confident. This counters the premature collapse phenomenon by sustaining exploration of alternative reasoning paths.

The method requires no training, no parameter updates, and introduces minimal computational overhead. Importantly, the entropy threshold $\tau$ is shown to be robust across a wide range ($\tau \in [0.3, 0.7]$), functioning as a coarse regime separator rather than a finely tuned hyperparameter.

Experiments

Evaluated on five reasoning benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH500, AIME 2024, AIME 2025, GPQA-Diamond) across three Qwen3 model scales (1.7B, 8B, 32B) and DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B:

  • SeLaR consistently outperforms standard CoT (sampling), Soft Thinking, and SwiReasoning across all model scales.
  • Average improvements over CoT (Sampling): +1.41% (Qwen3-1.7B), +4.63% (Qwen3-8B), +2.45% (Qwen3-32B).
  • Gains are most pronounced on the hardest benchmarks (AIME 2024/2025), e.g., +13.33% on AIME 2025 with Qwen3-8B.
  • SeLaR is also more efficient: 28.6% speedup and 12.4% token reduction over CoT (Sampling) on GSM8K, because selective activation skips soft embedding computation at ~90% of steps.
  • Ablation studies confirm both components are essential: removing selective activation drops average accuracy by 5.19%; removing contrastive regularization drops it by 7.82%.
  • On DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B, improvements are smaller (+2.77%), attributed to the model's lower baseline confidence triggering more frequent (and sometimes excessive) exploratory steps.

Strengths

  • Clean motivation: The entropy long-tail observation is well-grounded and intuitive -- not all reasoning steps benefit from latent exploration.
  • Simple and effective: No training required; the method plugs into standard autoregressive decoding with minimal modification.
  • Strong empirical results: Consistent improvements across multiple benchmarks and model scales, especially on challenging math competition problems.
  • Efficiency: Selective activation is not only more accurate but also faster than global latent reasoning methods.
  • Thorough analysis: Includes ablation studies, sensitivity analysis, case studies with logit lens visualization, and cross-model-family evaluation.

Weaknesses and Limitations

  • Token embedding space constraint: Like other training-free latent reasoning methods, SeLaR operates at the input embedding level, which is inherently less expressive than manipulating hidden states directly. The authors acknowledge this limitation.
  • Diminished gains on less confident models: The improvements on DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B are noticeably smaller, as the model's higher baseline entropy triggers excessive exploratory steps, introducing more perturbation than benefit.
  • Dataset-specific threshold selection: Although $\tau$ is claimed to be robust across a wide range, the reported results use dataset-specific thresholds ($\tau = 0.4$ to $0.7$), raising questions about automatic threshold selection for new tasks.
  • Limited model diversity: Experiments are primarily on Qwen3 models with one additional DeepSeek variant. Generalization to other architectures (e.g., Llama, Mistral) remains underexplored.
  • Top-k heuristic: The choice of $k=3$ is justified empirically but not theoretically. The sensitivity analysis shows performance degrades significantly with larger $k$, which may limit applicability to tasks with broader token diversity.

中文总结

问题与动机

链式思维 (CoT) 推理要求语言模型在每个解码步骤提交一个离散 token，从而丢弃了关于替代推理路径的信息。近期的潜在推理方法尝试用软嵌入（概率加权的 token 嵌入混合）替代离散 token，但存在两个关键问题：(1) 全局激活——在所有步骤（包括高置信度步骤）统一使用软嵌入，引入不必要的扰动；(2) 过早坍缩——软嵌入迅速向最高概率 token 收敛，实质上退化为贪心解码。

作者发现了一个重要的经验规律：CoT 解码过程中，模型输出分布的归一化熵呈现明显的长尾模式。大多数步骤为低熵（确定性步骤），模型确信其选择；少数步骤为高熵（探索性步骤），多个候选 token 竞争。这启发了选择性潜在推理的思路。

方法：SeLaR

SeLaR（选择性潜在推理）是一个轻量级、无需训练的框架，包含两个组件：

  1. 熵门控选择性机制：在每个解码步骤计算 top-k token 的归一化熵。若熵低于阈值 $\tau$，使用标准离散解码；若高于 $\tau$，构建软嵌入。这确保潜在推理仅在模型不确定的步骤激活。
  1. 熵感知对比正则化：在探索性步骤中，添加一个排斥项将软嵌入推离主导 token 方向，排斥强度按归一化熵缩放——熵越高排斥越强。这防止软嵌入过早坍缩到单一 token。

实验结果

在五个推理基准（GSM8K、MATH500、AIME 2024、AIME 2025、GPQA-Diamond）和多个模型规模上评估：

  • SeLaR 在所有模型规模上一致优于标准 CoT、Soft Thinking 和 SwiReasoning。
  • 相比 CoT (采样) 的平均提升：Qwen3-1.7B +1.41%，Qwen3-8B +4.63%，Qwen3-32B +2.45%。
  • 在最难的基准 (AIME 2024/2025) 上收益最大，例如 Qwen3-8B 在 AIME 2025 上提升 +13.33%。
  • 效率更高：在 GSM8K 上比 CoT (采样) 快 28.6%，token 数减少 12.4%，因为选择性激活跳过了约 90% 步骤的软嵌入计算。
  • 消融实验证实两个组件均不可或缺：移除选择性激活平均准确率下降 5.19%；移除对比正则化下降 7.82%。

评价

该论文基于扎实的经验观察提出了简洁有效的方法。熵门控的思路自然且直观，实验设计全面。主要局限在于：(1) 仅在 token 嵌入空间操作，表达能力受限；(2) 对低置信度模型的提升有限；(3) 虽声称 $\tau$ 具鲁棒性，但实际结果使用了数据集特定的阈值。总体而言，这是一项对无需训练的潜在推理方法的有价值贡献。
