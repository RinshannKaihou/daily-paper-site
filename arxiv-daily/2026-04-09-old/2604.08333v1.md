# Lost in the Hype: Revealing and Dissecting the Performance Degradation of Medical Multimodal Large Language Models in Image Classification

## English Summary

### Overview

This paper presents the first systematic, layer-wise and module-level dissection of why medical Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) consistently underperform traditional deep learning methods on image classification tasks. Through extensive feature probing experiments on 14 open-source medical MLLMs across three medical imaging datasets (breast ultrasound, CT COVID-19, and chest X-ray), the authors trace how classification-relevant visual information degrades as it flows through the MLLM pipeline: from the vision tower, through the connector, into the LLM, and finally to the generative output. The work distills its findings into four failure modes, introduces quantitative health scores for principled cross-model comparison, and identifies three critical barriers that prevent current medical MLLMs from fulfilling their clinical promise.

### Key Contributions

1. **Systematic Diagnosis via Feature Probing**: The authors train lightweight MLP probes on frozen intermediate activations at every layer of every module across 14 medical MLLMs, enabling explicit visualization of where and how classification signals are distorted, diluted, or overridden throughout the pipeline.

2. **Four Failure Modes Identified**:
   - **Quality Limitation in Visual Representation**: The vision tower fails to encode task-specific discriminative features for fine-grained medical patterns, producing generic representations that lack diagnostic specificity. This deficit is irrecoverable -- downstream modules cannot reconstruct critical visual cues from impoverished inputs.
   - **Fidelity Loss in Connector Projection**: The connector often operates as a lossy compression channel, diluting fine-grained spatial and textural details essential for medical diagnosis in favor of coarse semantic alignment.
   - **Comprehension Deficit in LLM Reasoning**: Due to the causal attention mechanism of autoregressive LLMs, image tokens evolve only through self-attention within the visual prefix without being contextualized by task-relevant language that follows, yielding little discriminative gain or even degradation.
   - **Misalignment of Semantic Mapping**: A fundamental incompatibility exists between the generative text decoding objective and the discriminative nature of classification -- the LLM is trained to produce fluent text, not to map latent representations to a fixed set of categorical outputs with high fidelity.

3. **Feature Health Score (FHS)**: Inspired by the Sharpe Ratio from financial economics, the authors propose a novel quantitative metric that holistically assesses how well a module preserves and refines task-relevant information. The FHS combines final probing performance, a growth factor measuring net improvement from early/worst layers, and a volatility penalty quantifying trajectory stability. This enables apples-to-apples cross-model comparisons.

4. **Three Critical Barriers**:
   - **Limited Impact of Medical Adaptation**: Medical MLLMs fine-tuned from general-domain counterparts (e.g., Qwen2.5-VL-7B, Gemma-3-4B) achieve only negligible accuracy gains (0.59%--1.37%) over their base models, and MedGemma-4B actually underperforms Gemma-3-4B by 1.66%. Extensive domain-specific pre-training and fine-tuning yield almost no classification benefit.
   - **Suboptimal State of Vision Tower**: Vision towers trained under autoregressive objectives produce features far less discriminative than those obtained through simple cross-entropy supervision. Unfreezing the vision tower during task-specific fine-tuning does not reliably improve classification and may even harm it.
   - **Comprehension-Utilization Entanglement in LLM Reasoning**: The LLM's comprehension of visual representations (averaged image token embeddings) fundamentally limits its utilization ability (final input token). These two capabilities exhibit a tightly coupled hierarchical dependency where the ceiling of utilization is constrained by the ceiling of comprehension.

### Experimental Setup

- **Datasets**: BUSI (breast ultrasound, 780 images, 3 classes), COVID19-CT (754 CT images, binary), Chest-Xray (5,856 chest X-ray images, binary).
- **Models**: 14 open-source medical MLLMs spanning 6 representative series (MedVLM-R1, HuatuoGPT-Vision-7B/34B, Lingshu-7B/32B, ShizhenGPT-7B/32B-VL, Hulu-Med-4B/7B/14B/32B, MedGemma-4B/27B, MedGemma-1.5-4B), plus 23 traditional deep learning baselines (8 CNN-based, 4 MLP-based, 4 ViT-based, 7 hybrid-based).
- **Probing Method**: Two-layer MLP probes with ReLU and dropout (0.1) attached at each layer of each module, trained while backbone is frozen. LoRA (rank 8, alpha 32) used for task-specific SFT.

### Key Results

- All 14 medical MLLMs consistently underperform traditional deep learning methods across all three datasets (Figure 1). The gap persists across medical imaging modalities, suggesting systematic architectural limitations.
- The FHS four-score profiles (FHS_V, FHS_C, FHS_L, P_final) reveal that the vision tower and LLM are the dominant bottleneck modules, while the connector shows the least variation across models.
- Parameter scaling does not consistently improve all FHS metrics within model series -- larger models do not inherently resolve the identified failure modes.
- BUSI consistently achieves the highest FHS scores, followed by COVID19-CT, with Chest-Xray lagging substantially, mirroring the performance gap observed between medical MLLMs and traditional models.

### Critical Assessment

**Strengths**: The paper provides a genuinely systematic and methodologically rigorous analysis. The layer-wise, module-level probing approach goes well beyond prior phenomenological observations to pinpoint exactly where information loss occurs. The FHS metric is a creative and useful contribution for standardizing cross-model evaluation. The three barrier discussions are thoughtfully designed and the vision tower SFT comparison (Table 3) starkly demonstrates the suboptimality gap.

**Limitations**: The study focuses exclusively on classification, so the conclusions may not generalize to other medical tasks like detection or segmentation. The three datasets are relatively small and span only 2D imaging modalities -- whether the findings hold for 3D volumetric data or pathology whole-slide images remains untested. The probing methodology, while informative, trains classifiers on frozen features and thus captures only what information is potentially recoverable, not necessarily what the model actively uses. The paper is primarily diagnostic rather than prescriptive: it catalogs failure modes but does not propose concrete architectural fixes.

## Chinese Summary

### 概述

本文首次对医疗多模态大语言模型（MLLM）在图像分类任务上持续逊色于传统深度学习方法的根本原因进行了系统性、逐层逐模块的剖析。通过对14个开源医疗MLLM在三个医疗影像数据集（乳腺超声、CT新冠诊断、胸部X光）上进行大规模特征探测实验，作者追踪了分类相关视觉信息在MLLM流水线中的退化轨迹：从视觉编码器、到连接器、到LLM、再到生成输出。研究提炼出四种失效模式，提出了用于跨模型定量比较的特征健康分数，并揭示了阻碍当前医疗MLLM实现临床潜力的三个关键障碍。

### 核心贡献

1. **基于特征探测的系统诊断**：在14个医疗MLLM的每个模块的每一层冻结激活上训练轻量级MLP探针，明确可视化了分类信号在整个流水线中被扭曲、稀释或覆盖的位置和方式。

2. **四种失效模式**：
   - **视觉表征质量受限**：视觉编码器未能编码细粒度医疗模式的任务特定判别特征，产生缺乏诊断特异性的通用表征，且下游模块无法弥补这一缺陷。
   - **连接器投影保真度损失**：连接器作为有损压缩通道，稀释了对医疗诊断至关重要的细粒度空间和纹理细节。
   - **LLM推理中的理解缺陷**：自回归LLM的因果注意力机制导致图像token仅在视觉前缀内通过自注意力演化，未被后续任务相关语言上下文化。
   - **语义映射不对齐**：生成式文本解码目标与分类的判别性本质之间存在根本性不兼容。

3. **特征健康分数（FHS）**：受金融经济学中夏普比率启发，提出了一种综合评估模块保留和优化任务相关信息能力的定量指标，实现了公平的跨模型比较。

4. **三个关键障碍**：
   - **医疗适配的有限影响**：医疗MLLM相比通用域基座模型仅获得0.59%--1.37%的微弱提升，MedGemma-4B甚至低于Gemma-3-4B 1.66%。
   - **视觉编码器的次优状态**：自回归目标下训练的视觉编码器产生的特征远不如简单交叉熵监督训练所得的判别性强。
   - **LLM推理中的理解-利用纠缠**：LLM对视觉信息的理解能力从根本上限制了其利用能力，两者呈现紧密耦合的层级依赖关系。

### 评价

本文方法论严谨，逐层逐模块的特征探测超越了以往现象层面的观察，精确定位了信息损失发生的位置。FHS指标具有创新性和实用价值。但研究仅聚焦分类任务，数据集较小且限于2D影像，探测方法仅反映信息可恢复性而非模型主动使用方式。论文以诊断为主，未提出具体的架构改进方案。
