# Rethinking Residual Errors in Compensation-based LLM Quantization

## English Summary

### Problem and Motivation

Post-training quantization (PTQ) methods based on weight compensation -- notably GPTQ and its successor GPTAQ -- have become the dominant paradigm for compressing large language models (LLMs). These methods work by iteratively quantizing weight columns and compensating for the resulting error by adjusting remaining unquantized weights, guided by Hessian-based second-order information. GPTAQ further improved upon GPTQ by introducing *asymmetric calibration*, which propagates residual errors from previous layers to correct inter-layer error accumulation.

This paper identifies a previously overlooked **sub-optimal calibration objective** in both GPTQ and GPTAQ. During the intra-layer iterative quantization process, the column-level objective aligns the quantized output with the output from *compensated* weights, rather than with the *original* full-precision model output. As compensation progressively modifies weights across iterations, the calibration target drifts away from the true full-precision reference, introducing a systematic error that accumulates throughout the quantization process.

### Key Insight: Compensation-Aware Error

The central contribution is the decomposition of the residual error into two components:

1. **Inter-layer propagated error (r1)**: The output difference arising from input discrepancies between the quantized inference path (Quant-flow) and the full-precision path (FP-flow). This term is already captured by GPTAQ.
2. **Compensation-aware error (r2)**: A *newly identified* term that captures the discrepancy between the *original* weights and the *compensated* weights within the current layer. Specifically, r2 = (W^(0) - W^(q)) * X_tilde, where W^(0) is the original weight and W^(q) is the weight state after q steps of compensation. This term was neglected by all prior methods.

By reformulating the column-level objective to always align with the original full-precision output (rather than the drifting compensated-weight output), the authors derive a corrected weight update equation that incorporates both error terms.

### Technical Approach

The method leverages GPTAQ's *neuron decomposition* technique to efficiently compute the new compensation-aware error term without expensive full recomputation. The key steps are:

- **Redefining the objective**: Replace `min ||(w^(q) + delta_w) X - w^(q) X_tilde||^2` (GPTAQ's objective, which uses compensated weights as reference) with `min ||(w^(q) + delta_w) X - w^(0) X_tilde||^2` (strictly aligning with original FP output).
- **Neuron decomposition of r2**: Decompose R2 = (W^(0) - W^(q)) * X_tilde column-wise, enabling per-column updates analogous to how GPTAQ handles R1.
- **Precomputation via Cholesky factors**: A new precomputed matrix P2 is derived using the upper-triangular mask trick from GPTAQ, allowing batch processing with minimal overhead (~5% increase in quantization time over GPTAQ).
- **Lazy batch updates**: Following GPTQ's design philosophy, the compensation-aware error term is integrated into the lazy batch update scheme for GPU efficiency.

The method introduces only minimal modifications to existing GPTQ/GPTAQ codebases: an additional precomputed matrix P2 and a copy of the original weights W^(0).

### Experimental Results

Experiments span a wide range of settings:

**Weight-only quantization (3-bit)**: Across Llama 2 (7B, 13B), Llama 3 (8B, 70B), and Llama 3.1-8B-Instruct models:
- GPTQ+Ours reduces C4 perplexity of Llama2-7B from 13.60 to 8.34 (a dramatic improvement).
- GPTAQ+Ours on Llama3.1-8B-Instruct improves average zero-shot accuracy from 70.3% to 72.3%.
- Consistent gains observed on WikiText-2 perplexity and six downstream tasks (PiQA, ARC, HellaSwag, Winogrande, BoolQ).

**Extreme 2-bit quantization with rotation (QuaRot)**: On Llama2-13B, GPTAQ+Ours reduces WikiText-2 perplexity from 7.50 to 7.32 and improves average accuracy from 55.8% to 58.2%.

**Weight-activation quantization (W2A4KV4)**: Combined with SpinQuant and QuaRot rotations, the method yields consistent improvements. On Llama2-13B with SpinQuant, WikiText-2 perplexity drops from 9.55 to 8.60 while average accuracy rises from 50.2% to 52.2%.

**Vision Transformers**: On DeiT models under W2A4, the method improves DeiT-Base accuracy from 61.3% (GPTAQ) to 62.1%, demonstrating generalizability beyond language models.

**Qwen3 models**: Significant improvements on Qwen3-4B (C4 perplexity from 57.1 to 39.9, accuracy from 45.6% to 52.2%).

**Ablation studies** confirm that the compensation-aware error term provides complementary benefits to GPTAQ's existing residual error, and the method is robust to varying calibration dataset sizes (64-192 samples) and different calibration data sources.

### Efficiency

The method incurs only ~5% additional quantization time over GPTAQ and requires storing two additional matrices (W^(0) and P2). Peak GPU memory increases modestly (e.g., from 63.7GB to 69.5GB for Llama3-70B quantization). Importantly, there is **zero inference-time overhead** -- the improvements are purely in the offline quantization process.

### Critical Assessment

**Strengths**:
- The identification of the compensation-aware error is a clean, principled insight that arises from careful mathematical analysis of the optimization objective. The fix is natural and well-motivated.
- The method is remarkably practical: minimal code changes, negligible computational overhead, and seamless integration with existing frameworks.
- Comprehensive evaluation across diverse model families (Llama 2/3/3.1, Qwen3, DeiT), quantization settings (W2A16, W3A16, W2A4KV4), and calibration configurations.
- The paper is well-organized, with clear derivations and thorough ablation studies.

**Limitations**:
- The gains are sometimes modest, particularly on larger models (e.g., Llama3-70B shows only marginal improvements in some settings), suggesting the compensation-aware error may be less critical when the model has sufficient capacity to absorb quantization noise.
- The method inherits the limitations of the underlying frameworks (GPTQ/GPTAQ): it remains a layer-wise, Hessian-based approach that does not capture cross-layer dependencies beyond the asymmetric calibration mechanism.
- The memory overhead for storing W^(0) and P2, while manageable, doubles the calibration memory compared to GPTAQ for the largest layers, which could be a concern for extremely large models on memory-constrained hardware.
- The catastrophic failure of SpinQuant-based methods at 2-bit quantization on Llama3-70B is noted but not fully analyzed or resolved.
- The paper does not compare against more recent quantization methods beyond AWQ, GPTQ, and GPTAQ (e.g., methods like QuIP# or AQLM that use different algorithmic paradigms).

## 中文总结

### 问题与动机

基于权重补偿的后训练量化方法（如 GPTQ 和 GPTAQ）是当前大语言模型压缩的主流范式。这些方法通过迭代地量化权重列并利用 Hessian 二阶信息补偿量化误差。GPTAQ 在 GPTQ 基础上引入了非对称校准机制，通过传播前一层残差误差来缓解层间误差累积。

本文发现现有方法存在一个被忽视的**次优校准目标**：在层内迭代量化过程中，列级目标将量化输出与*补偿后权重*的输出对齐，而非与*原始*全精度模型输出对齐。随着补偿逐步修改权重，校准基准逐渐偏离真实的全精度参考值，引入系统性误差。

### 核心洞察：补偿感知误差

本文将残差误差分解为两个部分：

1. **层间传播误差 (r1)**：由量化推理路径与全精度路径输入差异引起的输出差异。此部分已由 GPTAQ 捕获。
2. **补偿感知误差 (r2)**：本文**新发现**的误差项，捕获当前层内原始权重与补偿后权重之间的差异。具体地，r2 = (W^(0) - W^(q)) * X_tilde。此前所有方法均忽略了这一项。

通过将列级优化目标重新定义为始终与原始全精度输出对齐，作者推导出了包含两个误差项的修正权重更新方程。

### 技术方法

该方法利用 GPTAQ 的*神经元分解*技术高效计算新的补偿感知误差项，无需昂贵的完整重计算。关键步骤包括：重新定义优化目标、对 r2 进行逐列神经元分解、利用 Cholesky 因子预计算矩阵 P2、以及将新误差项融入懒批量更新机制。该方法仅对 GPTQ/GPTAQ 代码库做最小修改。

### 实验结果

- **3 位仅权重量化**：在 Llama2-7B 上，GPTQ+Ours 将 C4 困惑度从 13.60 大幅降至 8.34；在 Llama3.1-8B-Instruct 上，GPTAQ+Ours 将零样本平均准确率从 70.3% 提升至 72.3%。
- **极端 2 位量化（含旋转）**：在 Llama2-13B 上，WikiText-2 困惑度从 7.50 降至 7.32，平均准确率从 55.8% 提升至 58.2%。
- **权重-激活联合量化 (W2A4KV4)**：在 Llama2-13B 上结合 SpinQuant，困惑度从 9.55 降至 8.60，准确率从 50.2% 提升至 52.2%。
- **视觉 Transformer**：在 DeiT-Base W2A4 设置下，准确率从 61.3% 提升至 62.1%。
- **Qwen3 模型**：在 Qwen3-4B 上，C4 困惑度从 57.1 大幅降至 39.9，准确率从 45.6% 提升至 52.2%。

### 效率

该方法仅增加约 5% 的量化时间，需要额外存储原始权重和预计算矩阵 P2。推理时零额外开销。

### 评价

**优点**：补偿感知误差的识别是一个简洁、有原则的数学洞察；实际可行性极强，代码改动极小；实验覆盖面广。

**局限**：在最大模型上增益有时较为有限；继承了 GPTQ/GPTAQ 的层间独立优化局限；校准内存翻倍可能对超大规模模型构成压力；未与 QuIP#、AQLM 等不同范式的量化方法进行比较。
