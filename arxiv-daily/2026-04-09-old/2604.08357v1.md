# Bias-Constrained Diffusion Schedules for PDE Emulations: Reconstruction Error Minimization and Efficient Unrolled Training

## Summary (English)

This paper addresses two critical weaknesses of conditional diffusion models when used as autoregressive PDE solvers: (1) suboptimal single-step reconstruction accuracy compared to deterministic baselines, and (2) the prohibitive cost of applying unrolled training to stabilize long-term rollouts. The authors identify that both problems are linked through **Reconstruction Exposure-Bias (REB)** --- the discrepancy between the model's error when given ground-truth noisy inputs during training versus its own noisy predictions during inference.

### Key Contributions

1. **Reconstruction Exposure-Bias analysis.** The authors decompose REB into an "own-prediction bias" (error amplification from feeding the model its own predictions) and a "propagation residual" (inherited error from prior steps). They prove that the own-prediction bias is governed by the clean-input error at each noise level, and establish a **stability threshold**: at each noise level, there exists a critical clean-input error below which the model remains stable. This connects schedule design directly to reconstruction error.

2. **Adaptive Noise Schedule.** Based on this theory, they propose a two-phase algorithm. Phase 1 (Exploration) trains on a dense log-uniform schedule, progressively removing "solved" noise levels where the own-prediction bias falls below a threshold. Phase 2 (Construction) greedily selects the largest feasible noise-level jumps that keep the two-steps bias bounded, yielding the minimal number of diffusion steps with no wasted model capacity. This implements a "slow error decrease principle" --- the optimal schedule is naturally coarse at high noise and fine near zero.

3. **Proxy Unrolled Training.** Because the adaptive schedule minimizes exposure bias, the model's output after just a few denoising steps serves as a high-fidelity proxy for the full sampling chain. This proxy enables efficient unrolled training: instead of backpropagating through the entire diffusion Markov chain (intractable), one backpropagates through only the final few denoising steps, dramatically reducing cost while still sensitizing the model to its own distributional shifts.

### Experimental Results

The methods are evaluated on three benchmarks: 1D Kuramoto-Sivashinsky (KS), 2D Transonic Flow (Tra), and 2D Kolmogorov Flow (Kolmo). Key findings:

- The adaptive schedule alone substantially reduces 1-step MSE compared to standard schedules (linear, cosine, sigmoid) and PDE-Refiner. On Kolmogorov Flow, combining adaptive scheduling with proxy unrolled training improves the Frechet Spectral Distance by multiple orders of magnitude (from 3.7e5 down to 3.8e-1).
- Proxy unrolled training significantly enhances long-term stability, preventing artifact formation and maintaining bounded physical consistency.
- PDE-Refiner is competitive on KS (its low-noise-focused design aligns with the slow error decrease principle), but the adaptive approach generalizes better across datasets.
- The obtained schedules are exposure-bias-free: inference-input error matches clean-input error across noise levels.

### Critical Assessment

**Strengths:**
- The theoretical analysis cleanly connects noise schedules to exposure bias through the stability threshold concept. The decomposition of REB into own-prediction bias and propagation residual is intuitive and empirically validated.
- The two-phase algorithm is principled and requires no more total training epochs than a single baseline run (2x the epochs, but the exploration phase becomes progressively cheaper).
- Strong empirical improvements, particularly the dramatic FSD improvement on Kolmogorov Flow.

**Weaknesses:**
- The stability threshold proof relies on two assumptions (Wiener denoiser structure and spectral bias) that do not hold exactly in practice. The authors acknowledge this but the gap between theory and practice remains.
- The method requires a two-stage training pipeline (exploration then construction), which adds complexity compared to simply choosing a schedule. The claim of "smart hyperparameter exploration" is reasonable but the method is still more involved than standard approaches.
- The paper does not address how the reconstruction error landscape might change with different denoiser parameterizations (x0-prediction, v-prediction), which could fundamentally alter the dynamics.
- The connection between the obtained minimal noise levels and physical properties of the data (e.g., energy spectrum) is observed but not fully explained.

## 摘要 (Chinese)

本文解决了条件扩散模型作为自回归PDE求解器时的两个关键缺陷：(1) 单步重建精度不如确定性基线模型；(2) 对扩散模型应用展开训练（unrolled training）以稳定长期预测的计算成本过高。作者指出这两个问题通过**重建曝光偏差（Reconstruction Exposure-Bias, REB）**相互关联——即模型在训练时接收真实噪声输入与推理时接收自身预测输入之间的误差差异。

### 核心贡献

1. **重建曝光偏差分析。** 作者将REB分解为"自预测偏差"（模型使用自身预测作为输入时的误差放大）和"传播残差"（从前一步继承的误差）。证明了自预测偏差由每个噪声水平的干净输入误差决定，并建立了**稳定性阈值**：在每个噪声水平，存在一个临界的干净输入误差，低于该阈值模型保持稳定。

2. **自适应噪声调度。** 基于上述理论，提出了两阶段算法。第一阶段（探索）在密集的对数均匀调度上训练，逐步移除已"解决"的噪声水平。第二阶段（构建）贪婪地选择保持两步偏差有界的最大可行噪声级别跳跃，得到最少扩散步数且不浪费模型容量的调度。这实现了"慢误差下降原则"——最优调度在高噪声处自然稀疏，在低噪声处精细。

3. **代理展开训练。** 由于自适应调度最小化了曝光偏差，模型仅经过最后几个去噪步骤的输出即可作为完整采样链的高保真代理。这使展开训练的计算成本大幅降低，同时仍能让模型学会应对自身生成的分布偏移。

### 实验结果

在三个基准数据集（1D Kuramoto-Sivashinsky、2D Transonic Flow、2D Kolmogorov Flow）上评估：

- 自适应调度显著降低单步MSE，优于线性、余弦、Sigmoid调度和PDE-Refiner。在Kolmogorov Flow上，结合代理展开训练将Frechet谱距离改善了多个数量级（从3.7e5降至3.8e-1）。
- 代理展开训练显著提升长期稳定性，防止伪影形成并保持物理一致性。
- PDE-Refiner在KS上表现竞争性（其低噪声聚焦设计符合慢误差下降原则），但自适应方法在不同数据集上泛化更好。

### 评价

**优点：** 理论分析将噪声调度与曝光偏差通过稳定性阈值概念清晰关联；两阶段算法原理明确且计算开销可控；在Kolmogorov Flow上的FSD改善尤为显著。

**不足：** 稳定性阈值证明依赖实际中并不精确成立的假设；两阶段训练管线增加了方法复杂性；未探讨不同去噪器参数化方式的影响；最小噪声水平与数据物理特性之间的关系仍需进一步研究。
