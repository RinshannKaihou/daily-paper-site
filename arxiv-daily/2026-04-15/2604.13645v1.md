# A Mechanistic Analysis of Sim-and-Real Co-Training in Generative Robot Policies

> **一句话总结：** 通过理论分析和大量机器人实验，揭示了仿真与真实数据协同训练（Co-Training）中"结构化表示对齐"（兼顾跨域对齐与域可辨别性）是决定性能的主导因素，并提出简单组合方法CFG-ADDA将成功率提升约20%。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides the first systematic mechanistic analysis of sim-and-real co-training for diffusion-based robot policies, identifying "structured representation alignment" (balancing cross-domain alignment with domain discernibility) as the primary driver of success, and proposing a simple CFG-ADDA method that consistently improves success rates by ~20% over prior approaches.

## 核心贡献

- 首次通过理论分析揭示了协同训练中两个内在效应：**结构化表示对齐**（主导因素，解释约50%性能方差）和**重要性重加权效应**（辅助调制因素，解释约20%性能方差），为理解Co-Training提供了统一框架。
- 发现表示对齐可以在端到端训练中**隐式涌现**：浅层呈现局部几何对齐，深层呈现全局对齐；对齐强度与任务成功率呈中强正相关（Pearson/Spearman系数0.6~0.8），但**域可辨别性**不可缺失，否则会导致负迁移。
- 通过该分析框架重新审视了三种代表性方法（OT对齐、ADDA对抗域适应、CFG无分类器引导），发现它们各自仅强调对齐或可辨别性的一方面，解释了其改进不稳定的原因，并由此提出简单的**CFG-ADDA组合策略**。
- 在三个sim-to-sim和sim-to-real操作任务上验证了分析结论，CFG-ADDA在真实世界实验中达到约74%的平均成功率，相比基线Co-Training提升约20%。

## Key Contributions

- Provides the first theoretical decomposition of co-training into two intrinsic effects: **structured representation alignment** (primary, ~50% variance explained) and **importance reweighting** (secondary, ~20% variance explained), offering a unified explanatory framework.
- Demonstrates that representation alignment **emerges implicitly** in end-to-end co-training within balanced mixing ratios, showing local geometric alignment in shallow layers and global alignment in deep layers, with moderate-to-strong correlation (r = 0.6--0.8) to task success rates.
- Shows that **domain discernibility** is indispensable -- a simple 2-layer MLP achieves ~100% binary domain classification accuracy on aligned features, and suppressing alignment via adversarial discrimination degrades performance.
- Proposes **CFG-ADDA**, a simple combination of classifier-free guidance and adversarial domain adaptation that jointly promotes alignment while preserving domain discernibility, achieving ~20% improvement over vanilla co-training in real-world robot experiments.

## Methodology

The paper follows a theory-to-practice pipeline:

1. **Theoretical Analysis (Sec. 2):** Starting from the diffusion policy training objective with mixing ratio $w$, the authors derive the analytical optimal score function as a domain-weighted combination: $s_w^*(a_t, t, o) = \hat{w}_t \cdot s_t^* + \hat{w}_s \cdot s_s^*$, where the dynamic weights depend on the learned representations $z = f_\phi(o)$. They identify three regimes based on representation alignment: (i) disjoint (no transfer), (ii) structured aligned (effective transfer with adaptation), and (iii) overlapping (negative transfer due to bimodal action distributions). A secondary importance reweighting effect arises from the mixing ratio modulating per-sample contributions via a Softmax mechanism.

2. **Controlled Toy Experiments (Sec. 3):** A 4-layer MLP diffusion model is co-trained on two manifolds with controlled representation distances ($\Delta z$). ANOVA variance decomposition confirms that representation alignment explains ~50% of loss variance vs. ~20% for mixing ratio, validating structured alignment as the dominant factor.

3. **Robot Manipulation Experiments (Sec. 4):** Three tasks from robosuite (NutAssembly, MugCleanup, MugHang) are used with controlled domain gaps (visual-only, physics-only, visual-physics). UMAP visualizations and quantitative metrics (Wasserstein distance, Gromov-Wasserstein distance) confirm that alignment emerges at balanced mixing ratios ($w \in [0.016, 0.3]$) and correlates with success rates.

4. **Unified View and CFG-ADDA (Sec. 5):** Existing methods are categorized by their alignment-vs-discernibility emphasis. The proposed CFG-ADDA attaches one-hot domain embeddings for classifier-free guidance while encouraging alignment in remaining representation dimensions via an adversarial discriminator. A key insight: the guidance scale $\lambda$ acts as an inference-time transfer control, and setting $\lambda = -0.5$ actively transfers averaged knowledge across domains.

## Results & Highlights

- **Toy experiments:** Structured alignment is the "sweet spot" -- overlapping representations cause bimodal predictions, while disjoint representations yield no positive transfer. Changing mixing ratio alone cannot compensate for poorly aligned representations.
- **Sim-and-sim:** Best performance consistently achieved with balanced mixing ratios ($w \in [0.016, 0.3]$). Alignment-based methods (OT, ADDA) help under balanced mixing but hurt under unbalanced mixing. CFG is robust to unbalanced mixing but has limited peak performance. CFG-ADDA achieves strong results across both regimes.
- **Sim-and-real:** On three real-world manipulation tasks, CFG-ADDA achieves an average of ~74% success rate (21/30 trials), compared to ~50% for vanilla co-training and ~29% for real-only training.
- **Guidance scale insight:** Negative $\lambda$ values (e.g., $-0.5$) outperform the traditional positive guidance, actively transferring surrogate domain knowledge at inference time rather than amplifying domain-specific action gaps.
- **Domain discernibility:** Even when representations appear well-aligned in 2D UMAP, a shallow MLP achieves ~100% domain classification accuracy, confirming that co-training naturally preserves domain-specific information.

## Critical Assessment

This paper makes a valuable contribution by opening the "black box" of co-training through principled theoretical analysis grounded in diffusion model score functions. The decomposition into structured representation alignment and importance reweighting is clean and well-motivated. The toy-to-real experimental pipeline is systematic, with controlled ablations that support causal claims rather than mere correlations.

A notable strength is the unified perspective on prior methods: the insight that OT/ADDA emphasize alignment while CFG emphasizes discernibility elegantly explains why these methods work in some settings but fail in others. The proposed CFG-ADDA is refreshingly simple rather than over-engineered.

However, the analysis is limited to diffusion/flow matching policies and does not extend to autoregressive or VLA architectures. The theoretical framework also assumes access to the analytical optimal score function, which is an idealization. The real-world experiments, while convincing, use only 30 trials per checkpoint per task, and the tasks (while more challenging than basic pick-and-place) are still relatively structured tabletop manipulations.

## Strengths

- First systematic theoretical framework decomposing co-training mechanisms with quantitative variance attribution.
- Clean experimental design: toy model for controlled validation, then scaling to real robot manipulation.
- The unified view of prior co-training methods (OT, ADDA, CFG) through the alignment-discernibility lens is elegant and predictive.
- Practical impact: a simple method (CFG-ADDA) with consistent real-world improvements (~20% over baseline).
- Insight that negative guidance scale actively transfers cross-domain knowledge at inference time.

## Limitations

- Focused exclusively on diffusion/flow matching policies; applicability to autoregressive transformers or large VLA models is not validated.
- Does not characterize the dynamic learning process -- how alignment emerges over training iterations remains unexplored.
- Real-world experiments are limited to three tabletop manipulation tasks with 30 evaluation trials each.
- Does not investigate the impact of practical factors such as limited batch sizes or varying numbers of demonstration trajectories.
- The theoretical analysis treats the optimal score function as analytically available, which is an idealization of practical training.

## Relevance

本文对理解机器人策略学习中仿真到真实的迁移机制具有重要意义。其核心发现——**结构化表示对齐**（跨域不变特征对齐 + 域特定信息保留）是有效知识迁移的关键——不仅适用于sim-to-real场景，也为跨具身（cross-embodiment）、人类到机器人等协同训练提供了统一理解框架。对于关注表示学习、域适应和机器人模仿学习的研究者来说，这项工作提供了重要的理论洞见和实用方法。CFG-ADDA方法的简洁性使其易于集成到现有训练流程中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do these findings transfer to large-scale VLA models (e.g., pi0, Gr00t N1) where the representation space is much higher-dimensional and pre-trained on internet-scale data?
- Can the mixing ratio be dynamically adjusted during training to maintain structured alignment as representations evolve?
- What is the role of the visual encoder architecture (ResNet-18 vs. ViT vs. pre-trained vision-language models) in determining whether and how structured alignment emerges?
- Does the framework extend to reinforcement learning settings where reward signals, not just demonstrations, are available from simulation?
- How many real-world demonstrations are minimally needed for structured alignment to emerge implicitly versus requiring explicit alignment regularization?
