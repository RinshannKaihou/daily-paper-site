# Gradient Descent's Last Iterate is Often (slightly) Suboptimal

> **一句话总结：** 本文证明了在无预先知晓停止时间的情况下，任何步长序列都无法使梯度下降（GD）或随机梯度下降（SGD）的最后迭代达到信息论最优的 O(1/sqrt(T)) 收敛率，至少会多出一个 poly-log(T) 因子，从而证实了 Jain et al. (2019) 的猜想。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proves that no stepsize sequence can ensure the information-theoretically optimal O(1/sqrt(T)) convergence rate for the last iterate of GD or SGD in an anytime fashion -- an excess poly-logarithmic factor in T is unavoidable -- confirming the conjecture of Jain et al. (2019) and showing that suboptimal stopping times are unavoidably common.

## 核心贡献

- 证实了 Jain et al. (2019) 关于 SGD 最后迭代的猜想：在不知道停止时间 T 的情况下，没有任何步长序列能使最后迭代达到最优 O(1/sqrt(T)) 收敛率。
- 给出了比原猜想更强的结论：即使在无噪声的确定性 GD 情况下，anytime 最后迭代保证也不可避免地需要承受至少 Omega(log^{1/8}(T) / sqrt(T)) 的额外 poly-log 因子。
- 揭示了次优停止时间的普遍性：对于任何步长序列，当 T 趋于无穷时，随机选取的停止时间 t in [T] 都会承受上述 poly-log 开销，即次优停止时间具有正自然密度。
- 证明方法结合了低维构造（V 形函数和二次函数给出两个基本下界）与高维构造（基于 Nemirovski-Yudin 的 max-of-linear 下界技术），并通过精细的求和不等式完成论证。

## Key Contributions

- Proves the conjecture of Jain et al. (2019) that without a priori knowledge of the stopping time T, no stepsize sequence can achieve the optimal O(1/sqrt(T)) last-iterate convergence rate for SGD.
- Strengthens the result beyond SGD to the noiseless deterministic GD setting, establishing that even GD's last iterate suffers an unavoidable Omega(log^{1/8}(T) / sqrt(T)) anytime lower bound.
- Shows that suboptimal stopping times are not rare exceptions but are in fact common -- they have positive natural density, meaning a uniformly random stopping time inevitably incurs the poly-log overhead.
- Develops a multi-pronged proof technique combining low-dimensional constructions (V-shaped and quadratic functions) with a high-dimensional lower bound based on Nemirovski-Yudin's max-of-linear technique.

## Methodology

The paper establishes its main lower bound (Theorem 3) through a careful analysis of three complementary lower bounds on the anytime convergence guarantee E_eta(t) for any stepsize sequence eta:

1. **First lower bound (Lemma 6.1):** E_eta(t) >= eta_{t-1}. This comes from a simple one-dimensional V-shaped function argument: if the iterate happens to be near the minimum at time t-1, the last step of size eta_{t-1} necessarily moves it away by about eta_{t-1}. This captures the fundamental issue that the algorithm does not know when to stop.

2. **Second lower bound (Lemma 6.2):** If the cumulative step sum exceeds 1/2, then E_eta(t) >= 1/(4e^2 * sum of steps). This comes from a quadratic function construction and implies that the step sum must grow at rate roughly sqrt(t)/phi(t).

3. **Third lower bound (Lemma 7):** A high-dimensional lower bound using a modified technique from Harvey et al. (2019), building on Nemirovski and Yudin's max-of-linear construction. This yields a sum involving eta_j^2 * j / (t+1-j) terms.

The proof then combines these three bounds via averaging over a large time scale T, using harmonic number estimates and careful algebraic manipulation to derive phi(T) >= Omega(log^{1/8}(T)), where phi(t) captures the excess factor beyond the optimal 1/sqrt(t) rate.

## Results & Highlights

- **Main theorem (Theorem 3):** No stepsize sequence eta has an anytime convergence guarantee satisfying E_eta(t) = o(DG * log^{1/8}(t) / sqrt(t)). This means the poly-log overhead is fundamental and unavoidable.
- The result applies even to deterministic GD (no stochastic noise), making it strictly stronger than the original SGD conjecture.
- The gap between the lower bound Omega(log^{1/8}(T)/sqrt(T)) and the known upper bound O(log(T)/sqrt(T)) remains open -- the exact poly-log exponent is not yet pinned down.
- The "doubling trick" (concatenating optimal fixed-T stepsize schedules at powers of 2) yields optimal convergence only at exponentially sparse stopping times, forming a zero-density subset of natural numbers.

## Critical Assessment

This is a clean, well-structured theoretical contribution that resolves a natural open problem posed by a prominent COLT 2019 paper. The result is notable for being stronger than what was originally conjectured -- the original conjecture was about SGD (with noise), while this paper proves the lower bound even for noiseless GD. The proof technique is elegant in combining simple one-dimensional constructions with a more sophisticated high-dimensional argument. The paper is concise (13 pages including appendix) and clearly written.

The quantitative gap between log^{1/8}(T) and log(T) leaves room for future work, but the qualitative message is clear and impactful: anytime last-iterate optimality is fundamentally impossible for GD/SGD in the non-smooth convex setting.

## Strengths

- Resolves a well-known open conjecture from Jain et al. (COLT 2019) that had remained unproven for several years.
- The result is qualitatively stronger than the original conjecture, as it applies to deterministic GD rather than only noisy SGD.
- Elegant proof structure that decomposes the problem into three complementary lower bounds and combines them through averaging.
- The observation that suboptimal stopping times have positive natural density adds a valuable qualitative insight beyond the asymptotic rate.
- The paper clearly frames the open problems for future work (strongly-convex case, tightening the log exponent, density of optimal stopping times).

## Limitations

- The quantitative gap between the lower bound Omega(log^{1/8}(T)/sqrt(T)) and the known upper bound O(log(T)/sqrt(T)) is significant -- the exact poly-log factor remains undetermined.
- The result applies only to the non-smooth convex setting; the smooth case has different behavior and remains an open problem (with polynomial rather than logarithmic gaps).
- The analysis does not cover the strongly-convex case, where similar gaps between anytime and fixed-T rates exist.
- As a purely theoretical result, it does not provide practical algorithmic guidance for practitioners choosing stepsize schedules.
