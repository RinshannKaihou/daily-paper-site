# C-Voting: Confidence-Based Test-Time Voting without Explicit Energy Functions

> **一句话总结：** 提出基于置信度的测试时投票策略（C-voting），通过从多个随机初始化的潜在状态中选择平均top-1概率最高的轨迹来提升循环推理模型性能，无需显式能量函数，在Sudoku-extreme上达到95.2%准确率（远超HRM的55.0%），发表于ICLR 2026。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes C-voting, a model-agnostic test-time voting strategy for recurrent reasoning models that selects the trajectory with highest average top-1 probability from multiple random initializations, outperforming energy-based voting by 4.9% on Sudoku-hard and achieving 95.2% on Sudoku-extreme with the lightweight ItrSA++ model (~3M params), published at ICLR 2026.

## 核心贡献

- **提出C-voting策略**：一种无需显式能量函数的测试时投票方法，通过从多个随机初始化的潜在状态轨迹中选择平均top-1概率最高的候选，实现推理性能提升，适用于任意具有随机初始化的循环模型。
- **引入ItrSA++架构**：设计了一个仅约3M参数的轻量级注意力循环模型（约为HRM参数量的1/9），结合C-voting在Sudoku-extreme（95.2% vs 55.0%）和Maze-hard（78.6% vs 74.5%）任务上大幅超越HRM。
- **验证广泛适用性**：将C-voting成功应用于AKOrN（超越E-voting 4.9%）和HRM（在非原生随机初始化设置下仍有增益），证明其模型无关性。
- **深入分析置信度机制**：通过可视化与校准分析揭示C-voting在Sudoku任务中表现优异的原因，以及在Maze任务中增益有限的根源（模型对错误预测持有不当高置信度）。

## Key Contributions

- **C-voting strategy**: A model-agnostic test-time voting method that selects the trajectory with the highest average top-1 probability across prediction positions from multiple randomly initialized latent states, requiring no explicit energy function.
- **ItrSA++ architecture**: A lightweight recurrent model (~3M parameters, ~1/9 of HRM) combining cross-attention input mixing, recurrent self-attention layers, and SwiGLU, achieving SOTA on Sudoku-extreme (95.2%) and Maze-hard (78.6%) when paired with C-voting.
- **Broad applicability demonstration**: C-voting outperforms energy-based voting on AKOrN by 4.9% on Sudoku-hard, and shows non-trivial gains even on HRM (which was not originally designed for random initialization).
- **Confidence mechanism analysis**: Detailed visualization and calibration studies explain why C-voting scales well on Sudoku (diverse confidence distributions for correct vs. incorrect predictions) and less so on Maze (incorrectly high confidence regardless of initialization).

## Methodology

The paper introduces C-voting as a test-time scaling strategy for recurrent models of the form z_{i,t+1} = f(z_{i,t}, x_i; theta). The procedure works as follows:

1. **Multiple random initialization**: Sample K initial latent states {z^(k)_{i,0}} from a standard Gaussian distribution.
2. **Recurrent inference**: Run each candidate through T iterations of the recurrent model to obtain final latent states z^(k)_{i,T}.
3. **Confidence computation**: For each candidate, compute the average top-1 probability across all prediction positions: C^(k)_i = (1/|L|) * sum_{l in L} max_j P_{j,l}(z^(k)_{i,T}), where the probabilities come from a softmax readout.
4. **Selection**: Choose the candidate k* = argmax_k C^(k)_i with the highest confidence.

The theoretical justification rests on the observation that, under good calibration, maximizing average top-1 probability approximates maximizing average accuracy across all positions.

The paper also introduces **ItrSA++**, designed with three principles: (1) cross-attention for mixing random initialization with input (outperforming linear mixing), (2) recurrent attention layers with periodic SwiGLU, and (3) RMSNorm normalization. The architecture applies cross-attention followed by S self-attention iterations and SwiGLU, repeated T times. It uses Geometry-Aware Attention for positional encoding, EMA for training stability, and gradient truncation for memory efficiency.

## Results & Highlights

- **C-voting vs E-voting on AKOrN**: On Sudoku-hard, C-voting achieves 94.4 +/- 0.1% (4096 samples) vs E-voting's 89.5 +/- 2.5%, a 4.9% improvement, demonstrating that confidence is a better selection criterion than energy even when energy functions are available.
- **ItrSA++ SOTA results**: Sudoku-hard: 94.4% (vs AKOrN 89.5%, HRM ~80%); Sudoku-extreme: 95.2% (vs HRM 55.0%, AKOrN ~40%); Maze-hard: 78.6% (vs HRM 74.5%).
- **Scaling behavior**: Performance improves monotonically with the number of random samples for ItrSA++ and AKOrN, showing strong test-time scaling. Scaling on Maze-hard is weaker due to the model's misplaced high confidence on incorrect predictions.
- **HRM with C-voting**: Even though HRM was designed for fixed (not random) initial states, C-voting still provides non-trivial gains on Sudoku-extreme, improving from 55.0% to above 50% with just 16 samples.
- **Alternative metrics**: Negative entropy and log-probability produce nearly identical results to top-1 probability, confirming the robustness of the approach.
- **Ablation**: Cross-attention (vs linear), SwiGLU (vs FFN), EMA, and gradient truncation are all critical for ItrSA++ performance; removing EMA or gradient truncation drops accuracy to 0%.

## Critical Assessment

C-voting is an elegantly simple idea -- select the most confident prediction among multiple random trajectories -- that yields surprisingly strong results. The key insight is that average top-1 probability serves as a good proxy for board-level accuracy under reasonable calibration assumptions. The paper makes a convincing case that this approach is more broadly applicable than energy-based voting, which requires specialized model architectures. The ItrSA++ model, while not revolutionary in architecture, demonstrates that a well-designed lightweight recurrent model paired with C-voting can substantially outperform larger, more complex models like HRM. However, the approach is fundamentally limited by the quality of model calibration: when the model is confidently wrong (as observed in Maze-hard), voting provides little benefit. The computational cost also scales linearly with the number of candidates, which the paper acknowledges but does not deeply address.

## Strengths

- Extremely simple and intuitive method that requires no model architecture changes -- only random initialization at test time.
- Model-agnostic: applicable to any recurrent model with randomized initial states (AKOrN, HRM, ItrSA++, and even standard transformers).
- Strong empirical results: 4.9% improvement over E-voting on AKOrN, and ItrSA++ with C-voting achieves SOTA on multiple benchmarks with ~3M parameters.
- Comprehensive analysis: confidence visualizations, calibration studies, ablation experiments, alternative metric comparisons, and seed sensitivity analysis.
- Amenable to parallel computation, unlike increasing recurrence depth.

## Limitations

- **Confidence misalignment**: When models are miscalibrated (confidently wrong), as observed in Maze-hard, C-voting provides minimal gains. The method's effectiveness is fundamentally bounded by calibration quality.
- **Computational cost**: Requires running the full recurrent model K times (linear scaling), though parallelizable. The paper does not analyze the compute-accuracy tradeoff in depth.
- **HRM compatibility**: C-voting shows limited gains on HRM because HRM was not designed for random initialization, suggesting the method works best when models are trained with randomness in mind.
- **Limited task diversity**: Evaluation is restricted to Sudoku variants and Maze-hard; generalization to other reasoning tasks (ARC-AGI, language tasks) remains unexplored.
- **Theoretical justification is thin**: The connection between average top-1 probability and board accuracy relies on a calibration assumption (Equation 13) without formal guarantees.
