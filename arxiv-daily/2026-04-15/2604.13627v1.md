> **一句话总结：** 本文从优化动力学视角揭示了学习率通过隐式正则化控制灾难性过训练的机制——微调时低学习率能更好保留预训练特征，而预训练阶段的学习率衰减会导致模型锐度增加，进而加剧SFT阶段的灾难性遗忘。

> **TL;DR:** This paper shows that finetuning learning rates implicitly regularize feature preservation (lower LR = less forgetting), and that LR decay during pretraining increases model sharpness, which in turn amplifies catastrophic forgetting during SFT — a phenomenon known as catastrophic overtraining.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示微调学习率的隐式正则化效应**：在达到相同SFT训练损失的前提下，使用低学习率微调的模型其表征与基座模型的偏离（以主成分夹角MPA衡量）显著更小，能更好地保留预训练能力。
- **建立锐度与特征漂移的因果联系**：通过损失景观分析和二层对角网络模型，证明模型锐度与学习率对特征漂移的影响具有等价性——更锐利的基座模型在固定微调学习率下表现类似于更高的学习率。
- **阐明灾难性过训练的根本机制**：预训练阶段的学习率衰减导致模型锐度上升（符合Edge of Stability理论），锐度更高的基座模型在SFT时更容易进入"丰富"优化区域，从而加剧特征覆写和灾难性遗忘。
- **提出实用建议**：微调时应使用能接受的最小学习率；预训练时可考虑不在预训练阶段衰减学习率，而将退火推迟到后训练阶段。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifies implicit regularization of finetuning LRs**: For models reaching the same SFT loss, lower finetuning LRs lead to qualitatively different solutions that preserve base model features better, as measured by Mean Principal Angles (MPA) between representations.
- **Establishes the role of sharpness in feature drift**: Through loss landscape interpolation analysis and a diagonal-network toy model, demonstrates that model sharpness and learning rate have analogous effects on feature drift — sharper initializations behave like higher LRs.
- **Unifies catastrophic overtraining under an optimization lens**: Links pretraining LR decay to progressive sharpening (Edge of Stability), showing that sharper base models exacerbate forgetting during SFT. This explains why longer-pretrained models are harder to finetune.
- **Provides actionable training recommendations**: Use the smallest acceptable finetuning LR; consider avoiding LR decay during pretraining in favor of annealing only during post-training.

## Methodology

The paper employs a multi-pronged experimental and theoretical approach:

1. **SFT experiments across models and LRs**: The authors finetune four 1B-parameter models (OLMo 1, OLMo 2, Hubble, Gemma 3) on the Anthropic-HH dataset using constant LR schedules with warmup, sweeping LRs from 1e-6 to 1e-4. They track training loss and OOD performance (6 benchmark tasks: ARC-easy, ARC-challenge, PIQA, HellaSwag, WinoGrande, SciQ).

2. **Feature drift measurement via Mean Principal Angles (MPA)**: To quantify how much representations change during finetuning, the authors compute the mean principal angle between the top-k right singular vectors of the residual stream activations of the base and finetuned models at each layer. This is validated against Sparse Autoencoder (SAE) L2 distance metrics on Gemma 3.

3. **Loss landscape interpolation analysis**: For each gradient step during the first 100 steps of finetuning, the authors interpolate along the update direction and measure both loss and MPA, revealing that MPA increases approximately linearly with step size while loss decreases convexly — causing higher LRs to accumulate more feature drift per unit of loss reduction.

4. **Diagonal network toy model**: A two-layer diagonal linear network captures the pretrain-finetune feature overlap setting, demonstrating that large LRs forget pretrained-only features while small LRs remain in a lazy regime.

5. **Sharpness estimation**: For large models where computing the Hessian is infeasible, the authors use a perturbation-based proxy: average KL-divergence between the original and Gaussian-noised model predictions. This is validated against true Hessian eigenvalues on Pythia-14m.

6. **Overtraining analysis**: Using pretraining checkpoints from 5 LLMs (including SmolLM3-3B and Apertus-8B with WSD schedules), the authors track sharpness, forgetting, and feature drift across pretraining progress, demonstrating that sharpness surges coincide with LR cooldown phases.

## Results & Highlights

- **Lower finetuning LRs consistently preserve OOD performance better**: Across all four 1B models, at matched SFT loss levels, models finetuned with lower LRs achieve higher OOD scores (e.g., OLMo-1B: ~60% OOD at LR=1e-6 vs ~45% at LR=1e-4).
- **MPA scales approximately linearly with LR while loss scales convexly**: This asymmetry means that per unit of loss decrease, higher LRs incur disproportionately more feature drift.
- **Sharpness increases during pretraining LR decay**: For models using WSD schedules (SmolLM3, Apertus), sharpness is flat during the stable phase and spikes dramatically during cooldown. Models with cosine decay show gradual sharpness increase.
- **SmolLM3 shows a smoking gun**: Forgetting and MPA surge exactly at the point where pretraining LR is decayed, providing strong correlational evidence for the sharpness-driven overtraining mechanism.
- **Diagonal networks reproduce the phenomenon**: Large LR leads to forgetting of pretrained-only features; small LR stays in the lazy regime. Higher initial sharpness produces greater forgetting at the same LR.

## Critical Assessment

The paper provides a compelling and mechanistically grounded explanation for catastrophic overtraining. The core insight — that LR and sharpness are coupled knobs controlling feature drift — is elegant and well-supported by evidence from real LLMs, toy models, and loss landscape analysis. The dual narrative (lower finetuning LR helps; lower pretraining LR via decay hurts) is nuanced and practically relevant.

However, the sharpness-forgetting link is correlational rather than causal. The authors acknowledge this limitation openly: they cannot fully claim causality between LR decay and sharpness without retraining models with modified schedules. The perturbation-based sharpness proxy, while validated on Pythia-14m, is an approximation for billion-parameter models. The experiments are limited to 1-3B parameter models, and it remains to be seen whether the same dynamics hold at the 70B+ scale where most production LLMs operate.

## Strengths

- Clear, logical progression from empirical observation (LR affects forgetting) to mechanistic explanation (sharpness mediates feature drift) to the overtraining connection (LR decay increases sharpness).
- Thorough experimental coverage across multiple models, datasets (Anthropic-HH, Tulu 3), and analysis methods (MPA, SAE features, loss landscape interpolation).
- The diagonal-network analysis provides theoretical grounding and demonstrates the generality of the phenomenon beyond transformers.
- The SmolLM3 WSD-schedule experiment provides a particularly clean demonstration of the sharpness-forgetting connection.
- Practical recommendations are clearly stated and immediately actionable.

## Limitations

- All experiments are on 1-3B models; scalability to frontier-scale models is unverified.
- The sharpness measurement is a proxy (perturbation-based KL divergence), not a direct Hessian computation.
- The causal link between pretraining LR decay and sharpness relies on correlational evidence (particularly SmolLM3 timing); a true causal test would require retraining with modified LR schedules.
- The paper does not explore interactions with other SFT techniques (e.g., LoRA, data mixing, multi-stage training) that are standard in practice.
- Constant LR with warmup is used for SFT, while most practical SFT uses cosine schedules; the authors argue they are comparable (citing Pareja et al., 2025), but this may not hold universally.

## Relevance

本文对LLM训练实践具有直接指导意义。它为"预训练越久的模型越难微调"这一反直觉现象提供了优化动力学的解释，并将问题归因于预训练学习率衰减导致的模型锐度增加。这对预训练调度设计（是否应该推迟LR退火到后训练阶段）和SFT超参数选择（在满足ID性能的前提下尽量用低学习率）都有重要参考价值。研究也启示我们，在设计预训练-后训练流水线时，需要将两个阶段的优化动力学作为整体来考虑，而非孤立优化每个阶段。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would the observed sharpness-forgetting dynamics hold at 70B+ scale, where pretraining schedules and optimization dynamics may differ substantially?
- Can the sharpness proxy be used as a real-time diagnostic during pretraining to decide when to stop LR decay?
- How do the findings interact with parameter-efficient finetuning methods like LoRA, which constrain updates to low-rank subspaces?
- Could sharpness-aware pretraining (as in Watts et al., 2026) be combined with the LR-based recommendations here for compounded benefits?
- Is the relationship between sharpness and forgetting monotonic, or does there exist a "sweet spot" of base model sharpness that balances feature preservation and plasticity?
- What role does the SFT dataset composition play — could curriculum design or data mixing mitigate sharpness-induced forgetting?
