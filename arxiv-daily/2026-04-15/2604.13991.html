Adaptive Conformal Prediction for Improving Factuality of Generations by Large Language Models

一句话总结： 提出基于条件分位数回归的自适应共形预测方法，通过prompt嵌入对不确定性分数进行输入依赖的归一化，在保留边际覆盖保证的同时显著提升LLM长文本生成和多选题问答中跨异构类别的条件覆盖一致性。
TL;DR: Proposes an adaptive conformal prediction framework for LLMs that uses prompt-embedding-conditioned quantile regression to normalize nonconformity scores, preserving marginal coverage guarantees while significantly improving conditional (per-category) coverage for both long-form generation and multi-choice QA tasks.

核心贡献

  • 提出了一种基于条件分位数回归的非一致性分数变换方法，通过对prompt嵌入学习条件分位数来对分数进行自适应归一化，使共形预测阈值能够根据输入难度动态调整。
  • 在长文本问答和多选题问答两个任务上验证了该方法的有效性，实验覆盖Mistral-7B、Llama-3.1-8B、Gemma-3-12B三个模型及多个领域类别，在条件覆盖方面显著优于现有基线。
  • 从理论上证明了该方法保留了split conformal prediction的有限样本边际覆盖保证（coverage介于1-alpha和1-alpha+1/(N+1)之间），同时通过分数变换改善了跨异构prompt的条件覆盖。
  • 系统比较了多种claim-level不确定性量化方法（Maximum Probability、Perplexity、CCP、TokenSAR等），发现Claim Conditioned Probability（CCP）在所有模型上表现最优。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces a conformal score transformation method based on conditional quantile regression over prompt embeddings, enabling prompt-dependent calibration of uncertainty thresholds for LLM factuality assessment.
  • Demonstrates consistent improvements in conditional coverage across heterogeneous categories on both long-form QA (8 categories, 3 LLMs) and multi-choice QA (16 MMLU categories, 2 LLMs), while preserving finite-sample marginal coverage guarantees.
  • Provides theoretical proof that the adaptive method maintains the standard split conformal prediction coverage bound (between 1-alpha and 1-alpha+1/(N+1)) under exchangeability.
  • Conducts a systematic comparison of six claim-level uncertainty scoring functions, identifying Claim Conditioned Probability (CCP) as the most effective for claim filtering across all evaluated models.

Methodology

The method builds on the split conformal prediction framework with two key innovations:

Score Transformation Framework. Given a nonconformity score V(x,y), the method transforms it by dividing by an estimated conditional quantile tau(x): V_tilde(x,y) = V(x,y) / tau(x), where tau(x) is the estimated (1-alpha)-quantile of the score distribution conditioned on input x. This multiplicative normalization rescales scores so that their conditional quantiles become approximately input-invariant.

Three-way Data Split. The calibration data is split into three disjoint subsets: (1) D_cal1 for training the conditional quantile estimator (a two-layer MLP with ReLU, trained via pinball loss on prompt embeddings), (2) D_cal2 for computing the global conformal threshold on transformed scores, and (3) D_test for evaluation. Prompt embeddings are extracted using multi-qa-mpnet-base-dot-v1 and reduced from 768 to 32 dimensions via PCA.

Long-form QA Pipeline. LLM outputs are decomposed into atomic claims. A claim-level uncertainty score s(c) ranks claims, and the nonconformity score V(x,y) is defined as the maximum uncertainty threshold such that all retained claims are factually correct. Both s(c) and V(x,y) are normalized by tau(x), enabling comparison under a single global threshold.

Multi-choice QA Extension. The same pipeline applies, with the Least Ambiguous Classifier (LAC) score V(x,y) = 1 - p(x)[y] as the nonconformity measure, producing prediction sets of candidate answer classes.

Results & Highlights

  • Two-category calibration (Long-form QA): When jointly calibrating on "Inventions" and "Persons," standard Conformal Factuality achieves marginal coverage but shows severe category-wise miscalibration (over-coverage for harder Inventions, under-coverage for easier Persons). The adaptive method closely tracks the diagonal for each category individually.
  • All-category calibration (Long-form QA): Across 8 categories with Mistral 7B, the adaptive method reduces coverage variability (e.g., Landmarks improves from 73.49% to 79.54% coverage at target 80%) and typically reduces the fraction of removed claims.
  • Multi-choice QA (Dolan-More profiles): The adaptive method consistently outperforms the baseline across both Mistral 7B and Llama3 8B, achieving the best calibration error on a larger fraction of problem instances and maintaining closer performance to the best method as delta increases.
  • Claim scoring comparison: CCP achieves the highest PR-AUC across all models (0.360 for Mistral 7B, 0.367 for Llama3 8B, 0.238 for Gemma3 12B), substantially outperforming random baselines and other scoring methods.

Critical Assessment

The paper addresses a genuine and important limitation of existing conformal prediction methods for LLMs -- the lack of input-dependent adaptivity leading to poor conditional coverage. The core idea of normalizing nonconformity scores via learned conditional quantiles is elegant and well-motivated. The theoretical contribution is modest (the coverage guarantee follows from standard conformal prediction theory applied to transformed scores), but the practical impact is significant. The three-way data split is necessary for maintaining coverage guarantees but requires sufficient calibration data, which the authors address by generating 300 samples per category. The reliance on pre-trained sentence embeddings for prompt representation is reasonable but may limit adaptivity to domain-specific or multilingual settings where embedding quality degrades.

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical framework that preserves formal coverage guarantees while improving practical calibration.
  • Comprehensive evaluation across multiple models (Mistral-7B, Llama-3.1-8B, Gemma-3-12B), tasks (long-form QA, MCQA), and domains (8+16 categories).
  • Systematic comparison of uncertainty scoring functions providing practical guidance for claim-level filtering.
  • The method is modular and can be combined with any underlying uncertainty estimator and embedding model.
  • The approach naturally supports selective prediction, enabling downstream filtering of unreliable outputs.

Limitations

  • Requires a three-way data split (30/40/30), reducing effective calibration data for each stage -- the authors note that "several hundred samples per category are required" for robust estimates.
  • The conditional quantile estimator (2-layer MLP) is relatively simple; more expressive models might capture finer-grained input dependencies but risk overfitting with limited data.
  • Evaluation is limited to white-box models with access to token-level probabilities; applicability to black-box API-only models is not explored.
  • The method relies on the quality of prompt embeddings and the assumption that embedding space captures task-relevant difficulty variation.
  • Long-form QA factuality labels are generated using GPT-4o, introducing potential annotation noise rather than human-verified ground truth.

Relevance

本研究与"LLM可靠性"和"不确定性量化"高度相关。共形预测作为一种分布无关的不确定性量化方法，为LLM输出的可信度提供了形式化保证，对构建安全可靠的AI系统具有重要意义。该工作拓展了共形预测在NLP中的应用边界，特别是从全局阈值到自适应阈值的改进思路，对后续研究有启发价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the method perform with black-box LLMs where only output text (no token probabilities) is available? Can alternative uncertainty signals such as self-consistency or verbalized confidence serve as effective nonconformity scores?
  • What is the sensitivity of the conditional quantile estimator to the choice of embedding model and dimensionality reduction method? Would task-specific fine-tuned embeddings yield further improvements?
  • Can the three-way data split be relaxed or made more data-efficient, e.g., through cross-validation or data recycling schemes, to reduce the calibration data requirements?
  • How does the method scale to open-ended generation tasks beyond QA, such as summarization, translation, or code generation, where factuality assessment is more nuanced?
  • Could the adaptive framework be combined with group-aware conformal prediction methods (e.g., Cherian et al., 2024) to achieve both prompt-level and group-level calibration simultaneously?
