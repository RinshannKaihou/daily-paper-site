# DiPO: Disentangled Perplexity Policy Optimization for Fine-grained Exploration-Exploitation Trade-Off

> **一句话总结：** 提出DiPO方法，通过困惑度空间解耦（PSD）将样本划分为四个细粒度象限，并设计双向奖励重分配（BRR）机制，在不扰动验证奖励分布的前提下实现探索-利用的精细化平衡，在数学推理和函数调用任务上全面超越DAPO、GRPO等基线。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes DiPO, which disentangles the perplexity space into exploration/exploitation subspaces via PSD, applies bidirectional reward reallocation (BRR) to hard groups in the exploitation space and easy groups in the exploration space with minimal perturbation to verification rewards, achieving state-of-the-art results on mathematical reasoning (6 benchmarks) and function calling (BFCLv3).

## 核心贡献

- 提出困惑度空间解耦策略（PSD），利用在线统计估计和优势判断机制，基于困惑度与正确性的条件概率分布将样本空间划分为探索空间（ErS）和利用空间（EiS），从而精确识别需要鼓励探索的难样本和需要鼓励利用的易样本。
- 设计双向奖励重分配机制（BRR），对利用空间中的难组将最大困惑度样本奖励设为1以鼓励探索，对探索空间中的易组将最大困惑度样本奖励设为0以惩罚探索，且仅在零梯度的极端组上操作，保证重分配奖励与验证奖励正交，实现稳定训练。
- 在数学推理（AIME24/25、MATH500、AMC、OlympiadBench、Minerva）和函数调用（BFCLv3）两大任务上进行全面评估，在Qwen3-4B/8B、Qwen2.5-7B、Llama3.1-8B等多个基座模型上均取得最优平均性能，证明了方法的通用性和有效性。
- 提供了最大困惑度奖励/惩罚对策略熵增减影响的理论证明（Theorem 1 & 2），并通过实验验证了困惑度奖励导致熵增、困惑度惩罚导致熵减的趋势。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces Perplexity Space Disentangling (PSD), an online statistical estimation method that computes conditional probability distributions Pr(R|P) from a PPL Queue, uses advantage judgment with 95% confidence intervals to ensure meaningful PPL-correctness correlation, and finds the optimal threshold by minimizing classification errors.
- Designs Bidirectional Reward Reallocation (BRR), a novel mechanism that reallocates rewards only on zero-gradient extreme groups (hard groups with all-0 rewards, easy groups with all-1 rewards) by setting the maximum-PPL sample reward to 1 or 0 respectively, ensuring orthogonality between reallocated and verification rewards for stable policy optimization.
- Achieves consistent state-of-the-art results across mathematical reasoning (AVG 50.55% on Qwen3-4B, 54.79% on Qwen3-8B, 43.56% on Qwen2.5-7B) and function calling (Overall 55.03% on 3B, 62.51% on 7B), outperforming GRPO, DAPO, DAPO+EL, and CDE baselines.
- Provides theoretical analysis (Theorems 1-2) proving that maximum-PPL reward increases average policy entropy while maximum-PPL penalty decreases it, with experimental verification on Qwen3-0.6B confirming the entropy trends.

## Methodology

DiPO builds upon the DAPO framework and introduces two core modules:

**Perplexity Space Disentangling (PSD):** Maintains a PPL Queue caching recent (pi, ri) pairs from the last two batches. For a candidate threshold tau, computes empirical conditional probabilities Pr(R=1|P<tau), Pr(R=0|P<tau), Pr(R=1|P>tau), Pr(R=0|P>tau) with 95% confidence intervals. An advantage judgment mechanism checks whether the PPL space is meaningfully correlated with correctness (both Delta_EiS > 0 and Delta_ErS > 0 must hold using conservative confidence interval boundaries). Among valid thresholds, the optimal tau* is selected by minimizing classification errors. This partitions the sample space into four quadrants: CH (correct-high PPL), CL (correct-low PPL), EH (error-high PPL), EL (error-low PPL). The key samples for EETO are hard groups in EiS (mean PPL < tau*) and easy groups in ErS (mean PPL > tau*).

**Bidirectional Reward Reallocation (BRR):** For hard groups located in EiS, the reward of the maximum-PPL sample is set to 1 (encouraging exploration). For easy groups located in ErS, the reward of the maximum-PPL sample is set to 0 (penalizing exploration). Normal groups with mixed rewards are excluded (all rewards set to 0). This ensures reallocated rewards Rr are orthogonal to verification rewards R. The total objective is J_DiPO(theta) = J_DAPO(theta, R) + alpha * J_DAPO(theta, Rr), where alpha controls the weight of the reallocation loss.

## Results & Highlights

- **Mathematical Reasoning:** DiPO achieves the highest average across all three base models on 6 benchmarks. On Qwen3-8B-Base, DiPO reaches 35.00% on AIME24 and 27.50% on AIME25, outperforming the second-best DAPO+EL by 1.25 and 2.08 points respectively. The overall AVG improvement ranges from +1.12 to +1.56 points over the best baseline.
- **Function Calling:** On BFCLv3, DiPO achieves the highest Overall accuracy (55.03% on 3B, 62.51% on 7B). Notably, Multi-Turn Acc improves from 19.75% to 24.50% on the 7B model, a +4.75 percentage point gain over ToolRL+DAPO.
- **Cross-family Generalization:** On Llama3.1-8B-Instruct, DiPO achieves the best MATH score (56.75%) and highest AVG (73.39%), while entropy loss and CDE both degrade below the DAPO baseline.
- **Majority Vote:** DiPO ranks first in 10 out of 18 benchmark-model pairs on ACC/maj@8, confirming reliable reasoning consistency.
- **Ablation:** PSD alone with BRR yields +2.99 AVG on 4B and +3.88 on 8B over baselines without PSD. BRR outperforms direct PPL reward shaping by 0.93 and 2.23 points respectively, validating that minimal perturbation to verification rewards is critical.
- **Coefficient Robustness:** DiPO maintains stable performance across alpha from 0.1 to 1.0 (only 1.43 AVG variation), whereas entropy loss collapses catastrophically when coefficient increases from 0.001 to 0.01 (7.90+ AVG drop).

## Critical Assessment

The paper provides a well-motivated and technically sound approach to the exploration-exploitation trade-off in RLVR. The two dilemmas identified (degradation of extreme sample advantages and ineffective exploration/exploitation) are clearly formulated and supported by empirical observations. The PSD module is particularly elegant in its use of conservative confidence interval bounds for advantage judgment, preventing premature activation when PPL-correctness correlation has not yet emerged. BRR's orthogonal design ensures that exploration-exploitation signals do not interfere with the primary verification reward landscape. However, the method introduces several hyperparameters (alpha, queue size, confidence interval width) though the paper demonstrates robust sensitivity. The theoretical proofs rely on idealized assumptions (ignoring cross-context updates, assuming negligible F-term differences) and are acknowledged as approximate by the authors.

## Strengths

- Clear identification and formalization of two EETO dilemmas in GRPO-based methods with supporting data analysis.
- The PSD strategy is principled, using statistical estimation with confidence intervals and advantage judgment to ensure reliable threshold selection.
- BRR achieves minimal perturbation to verification rewards by operating only on zero-gradient extreme groups, a clever design choice.
- Comprehensive evaluation across 6 math benchmarks, BFCLv3, 3+ model families, and multiple metrics (mean@8, maj@8).
- Strong ablation study isolating the contributions of PSD and BRR, and comparing against direct PPL reward shaping.
- Theoretical grounding with entropy change proofs and experimental validation.

## Limitations

- The optimal alpha=0.1 still requires tuning; while the range is forgiving, the default alpha=0 is essentially DAPO, so some tuning is unavoidable.
- The PPL Queue uses only the last two batches, which may be insufficient for stable estimation in very early training stages or with small batch sizes.
- The method adds computational overhead from PPL computation and threshold optimization at each step, though the paper does not quantify this cost.
- Evaluation is limited to binary verification rewards for mathematical reasoning; the function calling adaptation requires heuristic mapping of multi-level rewards to binary correctness.
- The theoretical proofs make strong idealized assumptions and only provide approximate entropy trend estimates, as acknowledged by the authors.
- The risk prediction experiment (Appendix E.4) seems tangential and uses a proprietary dataset with limited reproducibility.
