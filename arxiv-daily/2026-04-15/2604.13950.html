Causal Drawbridges: Characterizing Gradient Blocking of Syntactic Islands in Transformer LMs

Sasha Boguraev, Kyle Mahowald

arXiv: 2604.13950v1

Date: 2026-04-15

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13950v1

Causal Drawbridges: Characterizing Gradient Blocking of Syntactic Islands in Transformer LMs

一句话总结： 利用因果干预（DAS）方法发现Transformer语言模型复现了人类对VP并列句法岛的梯度可接受性判断，揭示了经典的filler-gap机制被选择性梯度阻断的"吊桥"机制，并通过语料投影提出"and"在可提取与不可提取结构中具有不同词汇语义表征的新语言学假设。
TL;DR: Shows that Transformer LMs replicate human gradient acceptability judgments of coordination island violations; uses Distributed Alignment Search (DAS) to reveal that the same filler-gap mechanism underlies both extractable and unextractable conjuncts but is selectively blocked to varying degrees ("causal drawbridges"); projects identified subspaces onto a corpus to generate a novel linguistic hypothesis about distinct lexical representations of the conjunction "and."

核心贡献

  • 在25个不同规模（70M-9B参数）的Transformer LM上验证了模型对VP并列句法岛的梯度可接受性判断与人类评分高度相关（Pearson r = 0.54-0.80），为LM与人类句法处理的一致性提供了细粒度实证。
  • 利用DAS因果干预发现：从并列结构中的提取依赖与经典嵌入式wh-疑问句共享同一filler-gap机制，但在句法岛情况下该机制被梯度性阻断，定位了负责"阻断/放行"的因果子空间（即"因果吊桥"）。
  • 将因果识别出的子空间投影到Gutenberg语料库上，发现"可提取"例子中的"and"趋向于编码关系性依赖（如事件序列连接词），而"不可提取"例子中的"and"更接近逻辑合取（如名词并列），提出了关于连词语义的多义性梯度假设。
  • 分析了Transformer不同子组件（注意力头输出 vs. MLP激活 vs. Transformer块输出）在filler-gap处理中的分工：注意力模块负责信息搬运，MLP负责语言加工。

Key Contributions

  • Demonstrates that 25 Transformer LMs across 5 model families (70M to 9B parameters) replicate human gradient acceptability judgments of VP coordination island violations with Pearson correlations of 0.54-0.80, providing fine-grained behavioral alignment evidence.
  • Uses DAS causal interventions to show that extraction from coordination islands engages the same filler-gap mechanism as canonical embedded wh-questions, but this mechanism is selectively and gradiently blocked -- identifying the causally relevant subspaces ("causal drawbridges") in Transformer block outputs, attention modules, and MLP activations.
  • Projects causally identified subspaces onto a large corpus (100K chunks from Project Gutenberg), generating a novel linguistic hypothesis: the conjunction "and" is represented differently in extractable versus non-extractable constructions -- as relational/sequential markers versus logical conjunctions.
  • Provides architectural insights into Transformer sub-components: attention modules move filler-gap information while MLPs perform linguistic processing of that moved information, with blocking mechanisms detectable as early as the first verb position.

Methodology

The paper conducts four experiments using stimuli adapted from Fergus et al. (2025), comprising 46 VP conjuncts with human Likert-scale acceptability ratings (1-7), converted into embedded wh-question templates yielding 33 minimal-pair templates.

Experiment 1 (Behavioral): Computes wh-licensing interaction scores (surprisal-based gap vs. gap-less interactions) for 25 LMs across 5 families and correlates these with human judgments.

Experiment 2 (Mechanism Generalization): Trains DAS (Distributed Alignment Search, Geiger et al. 2024) on non-conjunct embedded wh-questions to identify 1D causal subspaces responsible for filler-gap processing at Transformer block outputs, attention head outputs (OV-circuit), and MLP activations. Evaluates generalization of these interventions to conjunct stimuli. Uses ODDS metric for causal efficacy evaluation with control conditions (flipped labels).

Experiment 3 (Blocking Mechanism): Trains DAS on minimal pairs contrasting highly extractable vs. unextractable conjuncts (8+8, with leave-out validation) to identify the "drawbridge" subspaces. Evaluates faithfulness by correlating subspace positions of held-out gradient conjuncts with their wh-licensing scores.

Experiment 4 (Linguistic Feature Discovery): Projects 100K corpus chunks from Project Gutenberg onto the drawbridge subspaces learned in Experiment 3. Analyzes top-activating chunks at the "AND" position to discover what linguistic features the subspaces encode.

Results & Highlights

  • LM-Human Correlation: All tested LMs show significant correlation between wh-licensing interaction and human acceptability, with larger models generally showing stronger alignment. LMs correctly prefer extraction in extractable cases and suppress it in unextractable cases.
  • Shared Filler-Gap Mechanism: Classic filler-gap interventions trained on non-conjunct embedded wh-questions generalize strongly to conjunct stimuli, with high ODDS across all three intervention sites. The correlation between ODDS on conjunct stimuli and wh-licensing is high, confirming shared processing pathways.
  • Causal Drawbridges: Interventions trained on extractable vs. unextractable conjunct pairs show strong causal efficacy on held-out conjuncts, with absolute correlations between subspace position and wh-licensing reaching up to 0.6-0.8. The blocking mechanism is detectable from the first verb position onward, suggesting incremental processing akin to psycholinguistic accounts.
  • Linguistic Hypothesis: Top "extractable" chunks feature "and" as an intra-event connective (e.g., "crept up and stolen," "look up and see"), while top "unextractable" chunks feature "and" as logical conjunction of nominal entities (e.g., "meat and bread," "books and papers"). Non-"and" tokens also split: purpose/causal markers ("to," "for," "just") activate on the extractable side; disjunctions ("or," "nor") activate on the unextractable side.
  • Subspace-Human Correlation: The identified subspaces correlate even more strongly with human acceptability judgments than with LM wh-licensing scores, suggesting the subspaces capture linguistically meaningful structure.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The paper makes a genuine contribution by being the first to use mechanistic interpretability to study gradient island effects at this level of granularity. The "causal drawbridge" framing is both intuitive and technically grounded. The corpus projection method (Experiment 4) for generating linguistic hypotheses from causally identified subspaces is methodologically innovative.
  • Significance: The work bridges two communities -- formal linguistics and mechanistic interpretability -- in a way that generates testable predictions for human experiments. The discovery that LMs incrementally process extraction from the first verb onward mirrors and extends psycholinguistic theories.
  • Rigor: The use of control interventions, multiple seeds, held-out evaluation, and cross-model validation (gpt2, pythia-410m-deduped, Qwen2-0.5b) strengthens claims. However, Experiment 4 is explicitly qualitative and the authors acknowledge they cannot rule out surface-level correlates (e.g., transitivity) as confounds.
  • Scope: Focused specifically on VP coordination islands (CSC). Does not address other island types (complex NP constraint, subject island, adjunct island) or non-English languages, which limits generalizability of the specific findings.

Strengths

  • Comprehensive behavioral evaluation across 25 LMs and 5 model families provides strong evidence for LM-human alignment on gradient island effects.
  • Multi-layered mechanistic analysis that systematically decomposes contributions of attention modules, MLPs, and full Transformer blocks.
  • The corpus projection methodology is creative and yields a novel, linguistically grounded hypothesis about conjunction semantics that connects to prior theoretical work (Ross 1967, Kehler 2002).
  • Strong correlational evidence that identified subspaces capture meaningful linguistic structure, with subspaces correlating more strongly with human judgments than with LM behavior.
  • Clear presentation with illustrative examples and thorough supplementary materials including all top-activating chunks.

Limitations

  • Experiment 4 is explicitly qualitative and noisy; the authors cannot directly observe what linguistic features drive the subspace discrimination, nor rule out confounds like verb transitivity.
  • Only tested on English VP coordination islands; generalizability to other island types and languages is unexplored.
  • Models tested are small (up to 9B parameters); results may not transfer to frontier models with different architectures or training regimes.
  • The stimuli transformation from matrix to embedded wh-questions could potentially alter argument structure and affect licensing judgments.
  • While the paper generates linguistic hypotheses, no human experiments are conducted to validate them, leaving the key claims about conjunction semantics untested in human subjects.
  • The DAS intervention framework learns 1D subspaces, which may oversimplify what are likely multidimensional phenomena.

Relevance

本文对"机制可解释性"（mechanistic interpretability）研究具有重要参考价值。它展示了如何将因果干预方法（DAS）应用于复杂、梯度的语言学现象分析，提供了从模型行为到内部机制再到语言学假设的完整分析链路。对于关注LLM内部语法表征、语言模型与人类认知对齐、以及如何利用可解释性方法生成语言学新假设的研究者而言，本文提供了方法论上的示范。语料投影方法（将因果识别的子空间应用于大规模语料分析）也拓展了机制可解释性工具的应用场景。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do the causal drawbridge mechanisms generalize to other island types (complex NP constraint, subject island, adjunct island) in English, or are they specific to VP coordination?
  • Can the same methodology reveal cross-linguistic differences in island processing, particularly for languages where the Coordinate Structure Constraint behaves differently?
  • Would the conjunction semantics hypothesis (relational "and" vs. logical "and") be confirmed by human experiments using syntactic priming or eye-tracking paradigms?
  • How do the identified subspaces relate to features discovered by unsupervised methods like sparse autoencoders or transcoders?
  • Does the incremental processing observed from the first verb position onward have direct parallels in human real-time sentence processing, and could this be tested with EEG/eye-tracking?
  • As model scale increases beyond 9B parameters, do the drawbridge mechanisms become more or less localized, and do they shift across architectural components?
