> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"认知断路器"框架，通过提取LLM中间层隐藏状态并计算语义置信度与内部确定性之间的"认知失调差值"(Delta)，实现对幻觉和"伪装真实性"的实时低延迟检测，在Qwen、DeepSeek上跨数据集AUROC达0.73-0.75，且推理开销仅增加微秒级。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes the Cognitive Circuit Breaker, a systems engineering framework that extracts hidden states during an LLM's forward pass and computes a "Cognitive Dissonance Delta" (gap between softmax confidence and latent probe certainty) to flag hallucinations in real time. Achieves AUROC of 0.73-0.75 on OOD datasets for Qwen and DeepSeek models with microsecond-level overhead, running 1.4-1.5x faster than extrinsic LLM-as-a-judge baselines.

## 核心贡献

- **提出认知失调差值 (Cognitive Dissonance Delta, Delta)：** 新指标量化了模型外显语义置信度 (Psemantic，经温度缩放T=1.5的softmax) 与线性探针从最优中间层(Lopt)提取的内部确定性 (Platent) 之间的差距，Delta = Psemantic - Platent，高Delta值即指示"伪装真实性"。
- **设计认知断路器运行时架构：** 在LLM前向传播过程中提取最优中间层隐藏状态，由预训练的Logistic Regression探针实时评估，动态阈值（F1最大化ROC校准）触发告警，无需额外API调用或二次推理。
- **揭示架构依赖的跨域泛化差异：** 线性探针在ARC上训练后在OBQA上做OOD测试，Qwen2.5-3B和DeepSeek-7B保持AUROC 0.736/0.753，而Gemma-7b-it退化至0.596（统计不显著），表明不同架构的内部真值表示泛化能力存在根本差异。
- **验证"表示坍缩"现象：** 所有测试模型在最终输出层均出现AUROC下降，说明模型在最终层从事实回忆转向语言流畅性生成，支持在中间层提取信号的必要性。

## Key Contributions

- **Cognitive Dissonance Delta metric:** Novel metric operationalizing the gap between outward semantic confidence (temperature-scaled softmax, T=1.5) and internal latent certainty (logistic regression probe at optimal layer), with Delta = Psemantic - Platent as a hallucination signal.
- **Cognitive Circuit Breaker runtime architecture:** Intrinsic middleware that extracts hidden states during the forward pass at an optimal intermediate layer (Lopt), runs a lightweight probe, and triggers dynamic F1-maximized thresholds -- no secondary API calls or full re-inference required.
- **Architecture-dependent OOD generalization finding:** Linear probes trained on ARC generalize to OBQA with AUROC 0.736 (Qwen2.5-3B) and 0.753 (DeepSeek-7B), but Gemma-7b-it collapses to 0.596 (p > 0.05), demonstrating that latent truth representation generalization is fundamentally architecture-dependent.
- **Representation collapse at final layers:** Across all tested architectures, the final output layers consistently show AUROC drops, indicating a structural shift from factual recall to linguistic fluency.

## Methodology

The framework operates as middleware during LLM inference: at an optimized intermediate layer (Lopt), hidden state tensors are extracted and fed to a pre-trained logistic regression probe that outputs Platent, while the final layer's softmax (temperature-scaled at T=1.5) yields Psemantic. The Cognitive Dissonance Delta is computed as their difference, and a dynamic threshold tau (calibrated via F1-maximized ROC on validation data) determines whether to trip the circuit breaker. The experimental pipeline uses four stages -- inference/extraction across all layers, binary ground-truth labeling, per-layer probe training via logistic regression, and Lopt optimization by AUROC. Experiments are conducted on Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, DeepSeek-LLM-7B-Chat, and Gemma-7b-it, using ARC-Challenge for training and OpenBookQA for strict OOD evaluation, with balanced classes (50/50), 1000-iteration bootstrapped confidence intervals, and paired bootstrap significance testing. The novelty lies in operationalizing mechanistic interpretability (representation engineering + linear probes) into a production-viable, latency-minimal systems architecture.

## Results & Highlights

- **Qwen2.5-3B OOD performance:** Peak AUROC 0.736 [0.71, 0.76] on OBQA (probe) vs. 0.750 (Delta); Delta Max F1 = 0.755 at tau=0.52; statistically significant (p < 0.05).
- **DeepSeek-7B OOD performance:** Peak AUROC 0.753 [0.72, 0.78] on OBQA (probe) vs. 0.772 (Delta); Delta Max F1 = 0.775 at tau=0.48; statistically significant (p < 0.05).
- **Gemma-7b-it OOD failure:** Peak AUROC collapsed to 0.596 [0.51, 0.69] on OBQA, not statistically significant (p > 0.05), indicating truth representations are coupled to dataset-specific syntax.
- **Lopt is architecture-dependent:** DeepSeek peaks at center layers (0.50-0.57 depth), Gemma peaks late (0.86 on ARC), Qwen ranges early-to-mid (0.11-0.75).
- **Latency:** Circuit Breaker runs 1.4-1.5x faster than LLM-as-a-judge baseline on NVIDIA Tesla T4, with probe execution at O(microseconds).
- **Live demo output:** Successfully flagged a hallucinated fact with outward confidence 0.529 vs. internal certainty 0.003 (Delta = 0.526).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate. The individual components -- linear probes for truth detection (Azaria & Mitchell, 2023), representation engineering (Zou et al., 2023), temperature scaling -- are all established. The main contribution is the systems-level integration and the Delta metric framing, which is a useful but conceptually straightforward combination.
- **Rigor:** Reasonable but with gaps. Class balancing, bootstrapped CIs, and paired significance testing are commendable. However, the paper only tests on multiple-choice QA datasets (ARC, OBQA), which limits claims about general hallucination detection. The "1.4-1.5x faster than LLM-as-a-judge" claim lacks detailed methodology -- which judge model? What prompt? How many tokens?
- **Reproducibility:** Medium-high. Models are open-weight (Qwen, DeepSeek, Gemma), datasets are public (ARC, OBQA), and the methodology (logistic regression probes) is straightforward. However, the code is not referenced as publicly available.
- **Significance:** The framing as a "systems engineering" contribution is appropriate -- the value proposition is the operationalization rather than new science. The finding that Gemma's truth representations are dataset-specific while DeepSeek/Qwen generalize is practically useful for model selection in production systems.
- **Red flags:** (1) Single-author paper from a government agency, with no code release mentioned. (2) Experiments limited to 3B-7B models and multiple-choice formats -- unclear if findings scale to larger models or free-form generation. (3) The "resilient extraction architecture" description reads more like engineering blog content than a research contribution. (4) The paper is relatively short (5 pages) and could benefit from more thorough baselines beyond LLM-as-a-judge (e.g., comparison to uncertainty quantification methods like semantic entropy or ensemble-based approaches).

## Strengths

- Clean, well-defined metric (Delta) with clear mathematical formulation and intuitive interpretation.
- Rigorous OOD evaluation protocol -- training on ARC, testing on OBQA without retraining probes.
- The finding of architecture-dependent generalization of truth representations is practically valuable for production model selection.
- Negligible latency overhead (microsecond-level probe execution) is a genuine engineering advantage over post-hoc methods.
- The "representation collapse" observation at final layers across all architectures is a useful empirical finding.

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on multiple-choice QA formats; free-form, long-form, and RAG generation scenarios are untested (acknowledged by the author).
- Requires white-box access to hidden states, incompatible with closed-source API-only deployments (acknowledged).
- Scale tested is modest (3B-7B parameters); behavior at 70B+ scale is unknown.
- The LLM-as-a-judge latency baseline is underspecified, making the 1.4-1.5x speedup claim difficult to contextualize.
- No comparison to other uncertainty quantification baselines (e.g., semantic entropy, Monte Carlo dropout, ensemble disagreement).
- Gemma's poor OOD performance raises questions about whether the framework is universally applicable or only works on favorable architectures.
