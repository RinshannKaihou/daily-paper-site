# Linear Probe Accuracy Scales with Model Size and Benefits from Multi-Layer Ensembling

> **一句话总结：** 在12个模型（0.5B-176B）上系统验证了线性探针检测LLM欺骗的准确率随模型规模对数线性增长（每10x参数量提升约5% AUROC, R=0.81），并提出基于双重错误分析（double-fault）选择互补层的多层集成方法，在Llama-70B上使单层探针几乎失效的Harm-Pressure Knowledge任务AUROC从0.51提升至0.91（+78%）。

> **TL;DR:** Across 12 models (0.5B-176B), linear probe accuracy for deception detection scales log-linearly with model size (~5% AUROC per 10x parameters, R=0.81). A 5-layer ensemble using double-fault-based layer selection and logistic regression stacking on Llama-70B improves mean AUROC by +12.7%, with the largest gains on the hardest tasks: Insider Trading +29% and Harm-Pressure Knowledge +78%. Geometric analysis shows deception directions rotate gradually across layers rather than localizing to one layer, explaining both single-layer brittleness and ensemble success.

## 核心贡献

- 证明线性探针检测LLM欺骗的准确率随模型规模对数线性增长：在12个模型（Llama 1B-70B、Qwen 0.5B-72B、Mistral 7B-8x22B）和5类欺骗任务上，约5% AUROC per 10x参数量（R=0.81, p<0.001）
- 提出基于双重错误分析（double-fault analysis）的多层集成方法：选择跨深度互补层（early layers 20-30 + late layers 55-65），通过logistic regression stacking组合5层探针输出，在Llama-70B上实现均AUROC 0.932（单层最佳0.827）
- 几何解释：欺骗方向在层间逐渐旋转（相邻层cosine similarity高，远距离层低，R=-0.435），不存在单一"欺骗层"，这解释了单层探针的脆弱性和跨深度集成的有效性
- 揭示任务难度层级：显式欺骗（Convincing Game, Instructed Deception）AUROC>0.95几乎完美检测，隐式欺骗（Harm-Pressure Choice/Knowledge）仍具挑战性

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates log-linear scaling of linear probe accuracy for deception detection: ~5% AUROC improvement per 10x parameter increase across 12 models (0.5B-176B) from three families (Llama, Qwen, Mistral), with R=0.81 and p<0.001
- Introduces multi-layer ensemble method using double-fault analysis for complementary layer selection and logistic regression stacking for score combination: 5-layer ensemble on Llama-70B achieves mean AUROC 0.932 vs. 0.827 for best single layer (+12.7%)
- Provides geometric explanation: deception directions rotate gradually across layers (cosine similarity between probe weight vectors negatively correlates with AUROC difference at R=-0.435), meaning no single layer captures the full signal
- Reveals clear task difficulty hierarchy: explicit deception (role-playing, instructed lying) achieves AUROC >0.95 and scales fastest; implicit deception (harm-refusal pressure) remains challenging even at 70B+ parameters

## Methodology

The authors train L2-regularized logistic regression probes (C=0.1, no intercept) on residual stream activations extracted at each layer of 12 open-weights models. Training uses the REPE honesty dataset (612 contrastive prompt pairs with "honest AI" vs. "dishonest AI" system prompts), while evaluation uses Liars' Bench spanning five deception types from explicit instruction-following to implicit pressure. For ensembling, they use double-fault analysis to greedily select layers with complementary failure modes (minimizing maximum pairwise double-fault), then combine k layer-wise probe scores via logistic regression stacking (for k=3,5) or grid-searched weighted averaging (for k=2), evaluated on a held-out 80/20 split.

## Results & Highlights

- **Scaling law:** ~5% AUROC per 10x parameters across 60 model-task pairs (R=0.81). Explicit deception tasks scale fastest; Harm Refusal Choice scales weakest
- **5-layer ensemble on Llama-70B:** Insider Trading AUROC 0.737 -> 0.953 (+29.3%); Harm-Pressure Knowledge 0.510 -> 0.910 (+78.4%); Mean across 5 tasks: 0.827 -> 0.932 (+12.7%)
- **Ensembling helps most where needed most:** Tasks where single-layer probes already excel (Convincing Game 1.0, Instructed Deception 0.939) show 0% to -5.3% change; tasks where single-layer probes struggle see large gains
- **Fixed-depth heuristics fail:** The 65% depth heuristic fails catastrophically on Insider Trading (-0.467 AUROC vs. baseline), highlighting that no fixed layer works universally
- **Mixtral 8x22B (MoE):** Performs comparably to dense models, suggesting residual stream aggregation provides sufficient signal regardless of expert routing
- **Error analysis:** Misclassified samples cluster near the decision boundary (correctly classified samples are 2.08x farther), indicating errors occur on genuinely ambiguous cases

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The scaling law result is a useful empirical contribution, though not deeply surprising given prior findings that larger models exhibit more linearly separable representations (Burger et al., 2024). The multi-layer ensembling idea is intuitively sound but not conceptually novel -- ensembling classifiers is standard ML practice. The real contribution is the geometric analysis linking gradual rotation of deception directions to practical ensembling success.
- **Methodological confound:** A significant issue is that 2-layer ensembles use grid-searched mixing weights while 3/5-layer ensembles use logistic regression stacking. The authors themselves acknowledge they "cannot isolate whether gains come from additional layers, the switch to logistic regression, or both." This confound weakens claims about the value of adding more layers specifically.
- **Ensemble evaluation scope:** Ensembling is only evaluated on a single model (Llama-70B), limiting generalizability claims. The scaling analysis covers 12 models, but the ensemble results do not.
- **Baseline comparison:** The paper compares against Liars' Bench baselines (Unrelated Question Classifier), which is appropriate, but does not compare against SAE-based detection or non-linear probe methods, despite citing work showing linear probes outperform SAEs.
- **Cherry-picking concern:** The headline numbers (+29%, +78%) come from the two tasks where single-layer probes perform worst. The authors are transparent about this and note that already-strong tasks see no benefit or slight degradation, which is intellectually honest.
- **Domain validity:** Training on simple contrastive pairs ("honest AI" vs. "dishonest AI" system prompts) and evaluating on complex strategic scenarios raises valid concerns about whether probes detect genuine deceptive reasoning vs. elicitation artifacts. The authors acknowledge this limitation thoroughly.
- **Reproducibility:** Code builds on public Apollo Research repository. Hyperparameters (C=0.1, 80/20 split) are clearly stated. Models are all open-weights. Reproducibility should be high.

## Strengths

- Systematic evaluation across 12 models from 3 families with consistent methodology, providing the broadest scaling analysis for deception probes to date
- Transparent reporting of negative results (2-layer ensembles failing to universally improve; fixed-depth heuristics failing on specific tasks)
- Strong geometric analysis that provides mechanistic insight: the gradual rotation of deception directions across layers is a clean, testable explanation
- Honest discussion of the confound between ensemble size and weight-learning method
- Clear task hierarchy finding (explicit > implicit deception detection) has practical implications for deployment

## Limitations

- Ensembling evaluated on only one model (Llama-70B); unclear if results transfer to other architectures or model families
- The 2-layer vs. 5-layer comparison is confounded by different weight-learning methods (grid search vs. logistic regression stacking), making it impossible to attribute gains to layer count alone
- Training data (REPE contrastive pairs) is simplistic and may produce probes that detect prompt artifacts rather than genuine deceptive intent
- No evaluation on long-context, adversarial, or multi-turn scenarios where prior work shows probes fail (Kramar et al., 2026; Cunningham et al., 2026)
- No cross-family transfer analysis; family-specific patterns suggest approaches may not generalize to new model releases
- Already-strong tasks (CG, ID) see slight degradation from ensembling, indicating the method is not universally beneficial

## Relevance

直接关联LLM安全监控与可解释性研究：揭示了模型隐藏状态中欺骗信号的表示结构与规模效应，为在生产环境中部署欺骗检测探针提供了实用指导（多层集成优于单层），同时也为理解LLM内部表示的几何结构提供了新视角。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the scaling law hold for emergent (non-instructed) deception in agentic settings, or only for prompted deception scenarios?
- How much of the 5-layer ensemble improvement is attributable to logistic regression stacking vs. genuinely complementary layer information, given the methodological confound?
- Can probe configurations (selected layers, stacking weights) trained on one model family transfer to another, or must they be retrained per model?
