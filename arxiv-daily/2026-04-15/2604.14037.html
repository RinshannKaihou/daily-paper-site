A Complete Symmetry Classification of Shallow ReLU Networks

一句话总结： 利用ReLU非可微性，首次给出浅层ReLU前馈网络参数空间的完整对称性分类：证明Hn-轨道微分同胚于纤维的参数构成稠密集，并揭示除正缩放与排列之外唯一源于恒等式 sigma(x)-sigma(-x)=x 的额外对称类。
TL;DR: This paper provides the first complete symmetry classification for shallow (two-layer) ReLU neural networks by exploiting the non-differentiability of ReLU rather than requiring analyticity. It proves that the set of parameters whose fibre under the realization map is diffeomorphic to the Lie group Hn is dense (Theorem 1), fully classifies all parameter symmetries (Theorems 3 and 4), and gives a complete description of stabilizer subgroups (Theorem 2).

核心贡献

  • 首次实现浅层ReLU网络的完整对称性分类。 此前的完整分类技术均要求激活函数的解析性（如多项式或sigmoid），本文反其道而行，利用ReLU在0处不可微的性质构造证明，突破了长期存在的技术瓶颈。
  • 证明"典型"参数的纤维微分同胚于Hn。 定理1表明，满足纤维 rho^{-1}(theta) 微分同胚于 Lie 群 Hn（正对角矩阵与排列群的半直积）的参数在参数空间中构成稠密子集，且包含一个Zariski开集。
  • 完整刻画所有隐藏对称性。 定理3和4揭示：除已知的Hn对称（正缩放+排列）外，浅层ReLU网络仅存在一类额外的对称，它源于恒等式 sigma(x) - sigma(-x) = x，并通过"最小形式"(minimal form)和"0-因子约化"(0-factor reduction)的代数工具进行了完全刻画。
  • 分类了参数在Hn作用下的稳定子群结构。 定理2给出任意参数的稳定子必为有限置换群与自由Hn-作用的乘积，并据此建立参数的层级包含关系。

Key Contributions

  • First complete symmetry classification for shallow ReLU networks. Prior approaches to complete classifications required analytic activation functions, excluding ReLU. This work exploits the non-differentiability of ReLU at zero to achieve a full classification, overcoming a longstanding technical barrier.
  • Dense genericity of "simple" fibres. Theorem 1 proves that the set of parameters whose fibre under the realization map is diffeomorphic to Hn (the group of positive rescalings and permutations) is dense in parameter space and contains a Zariski open set, meaning "almost all" parameters have no hidden symmetries.
  • Complete description of all hidden symmetries. Theorems 3 and 4 show that beyond the well-known Hn symmetries, the only additional class of symmetries in shallow ReLU networks arises from the identity sigma(x) - sigma(-x) = x. The classification is achieved via algebraic tools: a "minimal form" canonical representation and a "0-factor reduction" procedure.
  • Stabilizer subgroup classification. Theorem 2 fully classifies the possible stabilizers of any parameter under the Hn action, showing they decompose as a product of a finite permutation group (generated by at most C(n-n',2) two-cycles) and a free H_{n'} action.

Methodology

The paper takes a primarily algebraic approach, in contrast to the probabilistic or geometric methods used in prior work on deep or polynomial-activation networks.

  1. Minimal Form Construction (Definition 3): For any parameter theta = (v, A, b, C), the author defines a canonical "minimal form" theta_min within the equivalence class H_n \ Omega / ~, where ~ captures three types of trivial equivalences: (i) zeroing the row of a zero weight, (ii) merging rows with linearly semi-dependent (a_i, b_i) pairs, and (iii) absorbing constant neurons into the bias. This minimal form is computable via the Python algorithm provided in Section 6.
  1. 0-Factor Reduction (Definition-Lemma 5): Parameters are reduced by removing zero-weight rows, yielding a unique "reduced" parameter with no 0-factors. Two parameters share the same minimal form if and only if their 0-factor reductions coincide.
  1. Key Technical Proposition (Proposition 14): The heart of the proof determines when a minimal parameter with no 0-factors realizes a smooth (affine linear) function. By checking differentiability everywhere on the domain, the author exploits the non-differentiability of ReLU at zero to show this only occurs when the parameter has the special paired structure (a_i, b_i) and (-a_i, -b_i) with coefficients +1/-1, corresponding to the identity sigma(x) - sigma(-x) = x.
  1. Lifting from k=1 to general k: The scalar-output case (k=1) is solved first, then extended to general k via projection maps pi_i and a fibre product argument (Proposition 20, using a category-theoretic lemma about fibre products of isomorphisms).
  1. Zariski openness (Theorem 5): An explicit Zariski open set U is constructed where all parameters have fibres diffeomorphic to Hn, by requiring: (a) all weights nonzero, (b) rows of (A,b) pairwise linearly independent, and (c) all nontrivial sign-weighted combinations of rows nonzero.

Results & Highlights

  • Theorem 1 (Dense genericity): Parameters whose fibre is diffeomorphic to Hn form a dense set in Omega(m,n,k), containing a Zariski open set. This means hidden symmetries are "measure-zero" phenomena in an algebraic-geometric sense.
  • Theorem 2 (Stabilizer structure): Stab_{Hn}(theta) is isomorphic to S x H_{n'}, where S is generated by at most C(n-n',2) elements of the form diag(lambda_i, lambda_j)(i,j).
  • Theorem 3 (Scalar output classification): im rho(m,n,1) = H_n \ Omega(m,n,1) / ≡, where ≡ extends ~ by one additional equivalence class arising from the sigma(x) - sigma(-x) = x identity.
  • Theorem 4 (General output classification): im rho(m,n,k) = Omega(m,n,k) / ≡_k, where ≡_k requires coordinate-wise agreement via projections.
  • Counterexample to prior work: The paper provides an explicit example (theta_1, theta_2 in Omega(2,3,1)) satisfying the genericity, supertransversality, LRA, and TPIC conditions of Grigsby-Lindsey-Rolnick (ICML 2023), yet whose fibres are NOT determined by Hn alone, showing those conditions are insufficient for global identifiability.
  • Practical algorithm: A Python implementation for computing the minimal form is provided (Section 6), enabling algorithmic symmetry detection.

Critical Assessment

This is a mathematically rigorous contribution to the theory of neural network parameter spaces. The key insight --- exploiting rather than avoiding the non-differentiability of ReLU --- is elegant and inverts the standard approach in the field. The complete classification is a significant milestone for the shallow case, providing exact algebraic conditions for when hidden symmetries exist.

The counterexample to the Grigsby-Lindsey-Rolnick conditions is particularly valuable, as it clarifies the gap between local identifiability (which those conditions ensure) and the global classification achieved here. The hierarchy of parameter sets (Stab nonzero, subset of (A,b)-stabilizer nonzero, subset of non-Hn fibres) is a useful organizing framework.

However, the restriction to shallow (two-layer) networks is a major limitation. The algebraic approach here relies heavily on the structure of the single hidden layer, and extending these techniques to deep networks appears non-trivial. The paper acknowledges this and notes that the geometric interpretation of the algebraic equivalence in Theorem 3 is not fully understood.

Strengths

  • Complete solution for the shallow ReLU case. This is the first work to provide a full symmetry classification without requiring analyticity, filling a notable gap in the literature.
  • Elegant core idea. Exploiting the non-differentiability of ReLU at zero (via Proposition 14) to constrain possible hidden symmetries is conceptually clean and powerful.
  • Constructive and computable. The minimal form algorithm makes the classification practical, not just existential.
  • Explicit Zariski open set. The proof is not merely existential --- it constructs explicit polynomial conditions characterizing the "generic" parameters.
  • Clarifies limitations of prior work. The explicit counterexample to ICML 2023 conditions advances the community's understanding of what local identifiability results actually guarantee.

Limitations

  • Shallow networks only. The techniques are tailored to two-layer architectures. Extension to deep ReLU networks would require fundamentally new ideas, as the layered composition breaks the algebraic structure exploited here.
  • No optimization implications yet. While the neuromanifold's geometric properties are known to affect optimization, this paper does not develop those consequences. The connection from symmetry classification to training dynamics or generalization remains unexplored.
  • Geometric interpretation incomplete. The author acknowledges that the algebraic equivalence in Theorem 3 (the ≡ relation beyond ~) may encapsulate multiple geometric phenomena that are not yet disentangled.
  • No experimental validation. The paper is purely theoretical with no experiments demonstrating the practical impact of the symmetry classification on network behavior or training.
  • Single-author work without extensive peer review context. While mathematically rigorous, the paper appears as a preprint and would benefit from community validation of the intricate proofs (particularly Proposition 14 and the fibre product argument).
