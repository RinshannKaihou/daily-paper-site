> **一句话总结：** FIDeL 是一个策略无关的机器人模仿学习运行时故障检测框架，通过基于最优传输的表征异常检测、时空共形预测阈值和 VLM 语义过滤三阶段流水线，将良性偏差与真正的任务故障区分开来，在异常检测 AUROC 上提升 5.30%，在故障检测准确率上提升 17.38%。

> **TL;DR:** FIDeL is a policy-independent runtime failure detection module for imitation learning in robotics that combines representation-based anomaly detection via optimal transport, spatio-temporal conformal prediction thresholds, and VLM semantic filtering to discriminate benign anomalies from genuine task failures, achieving +5.30% AUROC and +17.38% failure-detection accuracy over state-of-the-art baselines.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了一种基于单类 (One-Class) 范式的表征异常检测方法，利用最优传输 (Optimal Transport) 对专家演示的视觉特征进行全局匹配，生成异常分数和定位热力图，无需故障数据即可训练。
- 将共形预测 (Conformal Prediction) 扩展至时空联合维度，提出了一种同时考虑时间对齐和空间 patch 级差异的自适应阈值机制，使异常检测具有分布无关的统计保证。
- 引入 VLM (Qwen 2.5-7b) 语义过滤模块，通过结合任务描述、专家参考帧和异常热力图，自动区分良性偏差（如背景变化）与真正的任务故障，显著降低误报率。
- 发布了 BotFails 多模态数据集，涵盖 10 个家用与工业场景的真实机器人任务，包含视觉、本体感知和自然语言指令，填补了机器人故障检测领域数据集的空白。

## Key Contributions

- A novel representation-based vision anomaly detection algorithm following the One-Class paradigm, using optimal transport to align incoming observations with a compact memory of expert demonstration features for anomaly scoring and heatmap generation.
- An extension of Conformal Prediction to handle both temporal and spatial (patch-level) variations simultaneously, providing distribution-free, adaptive thresholds with memory-based temporal alignment for variable execution speeds.
- A semantic filtering mechanism leveraging a Vision-Language Model (Qwen 2.5-7b) that combines task descriptions, expert reference frames, and anomaly heatmaps to discriminate benign anomalies from task-critical failures, reducing false positives.
- BotFails, a new multimodal benchmark dataset of 10 real-world robotic tasks (6 domestic, 4 industrial) collected via teleoperation on LeRobot, featuring both anomaly and failure-level labels to address the scarcity of failure-oriented robotics datasets.

## Methodology

FIDeL operates in two phases -- offline and online -- and is policy-independent, requiring only nominal (expert) demonstration data.

**Offline Phase:**
- Expert demonstrations are encoded using a frozen vision backbone (ResNet-18 or DINOv2), producing patch-level feature tensors.
- Features are aggregated across episodes into a statistical memory buffer M storing per-timestep, per-patch Gaussian statistics (mean and standard deviation), providing a compact representation of the nominal distribution.
- A calibration subset of demonstrations is used to derive spatio-temporal adaptive thresholds via an extended Conformal Prediction framework, which computes per-patch upper bounds controlling the false positive rate at a user-specified level.

**Online Phase (three-stage pipeline):**
1. **Anomaly Detection:** At each timestep, the current observation is encoded and aligned to the memory via entropic-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn). The minimum transport cost across all nominal timesteps yields the anomaly score, while the cost matrix produces localized anomaly heatmaps.
2. **Thresholding:** The anomaly score is compared against conformal prediction bounds. An observation is flagged as anomalous when the fraction of patches exceeding their respective bounds surpasses a configurable threshold, allowing sensitivity tuning.
3. **Semantic Filtering:** When an anomaly is flagged, a VLM (Qwen 2.5-7b) receives the task description, an expert reference frame at the most similar timestep, the current observation, and the anomaly heatmap. The VLM classifies the anomaly as either benign (robot may continue) or a genuine failure requiring intervention.

## Results & Highlights

- **Anomaly Detection:** The representation-based AD method achieves the best AUROC across both datasets: 70.50% on BotFails (+5.30% over the second-best logpZ0) and 86.72% on Real-PI soldering (+4.10% over logpZ0).
- **Thresholding:** Conformal prediction with spatial and temporal extensions (CP t&s) consistently outperforms Gaussian-based thresholding, providing robust and distribution-free anomaly decisions.
- **End-to-End Failure Detection:** FIDeL (Representation + CP t&s + Semantic Filtering) achieves +17.38% accuracy improvement on BotFails compared to existing methods. On Real-PI soldering, FIDeL reaches the highest weighted accuracy among all baselines.
- **Semantic Filtering Impact:** The VLM filter substantially reduces false positives by filtering benign anomalies (e.g., background changes, unknown objects), though it introduces a small number of false negatives.
- **STAC Baseline Limitation:** STAC, which operates on proprioceptive signals and action consistency, underperforms visual methods, achieving only 53.55% AUROC on the soldering task.

## Critical Assessment

FIDeL addresses a genuinely important problem -- runtime failure detection in deployed IL policies -- with a well-designed modular pipeline. The combination of representation-based AD with conformal prediction provides statistically grounded anomaly detection without distributional assumptions. The VLM semantic filtering is a pragmatic innovation that bridges the gap between anomaly detection and failure identification. However, the approach inherits limitations from each component: the memory-based representation cannot capture order-dependent (non-Markovian) anomalies, the VLM adds computational overhead at inference time, and the conformal prediction exchangeability assumption may be violated when demonstration and deployment distributions drift. The BotFails dataset, while valuable, is relatively small (10 tasks) and collected in controlled settings. The improvements over baselines, while consistent, are moderate rather than transformative.

## Strengths

- Policy-independent design: works alongside any IL policy without modification.
- Modular and interpretable: each stage (AD, thresholding, semantic filtering) can be independently analyzed, with heatmaps and VLM explanations providing human-readable justifications.
- Statistically principled: conformal prediction provides distribution-free coverage guarantees.
- Comprehensive evaluation: compares against multiple AD families, thresholding strategies, and end-to-end failure detection pipelines on both collected and real-policy datasets.
- Practical usability: only requires nominal demonstrations, no failure data needed for training the anomaly detector.
- New benchmark dataset (BotFails) fills a gap in the field.

## Limitations

- Heavy dependence on demonstration diversity: insufficient variation in expert data leads to over-flagging benign deviations as anomalies.
- VLM computational cost: the semantic filtering step introduces significant inference latency, and excessive false alarms would compound this issue.
- Spatial and temporal invariance can mask fine-grained or order-dependent anomalies, particularly problematic for non-Markovian tasks (e.g., sequential button pressing).
- No explicit temporal modeling for detecting anomalies that depend on action/state history rather than current observation alone.
- Task-dependent balance between invariance and sensitivity requires manual tuning of the patch-exceedance threshold.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does FIDeL scale with the number of tasks and demonstration episodes? Does the memory buffer become a bottleneck in multi-task settings?
- Can the VLM semantic filtering be replaced or augmented with a lighter-weight classifier to reduce inference latency while preserving accuracy?
- How robust is the conformal prediction threshold to domain shifts between the demonstration environment and the deployment environment (e.g., different lighting, camera angles)?
- Could explicit temporal modeling (e.g., recurrent or transformer-based memory) be integrated to detect non-Markovian anomalies without sacrificing the One-Class formulation?
- What is the failure mode breakdown by anomaly type -- does FIDeL perform equally well on hardware faults vs. environmental disturbances vs. policy mistakes?
