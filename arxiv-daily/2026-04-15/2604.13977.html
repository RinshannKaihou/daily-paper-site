How Can We Synthesize High-Quality Pretraining Data?

一句话总结： Hugging Face团队通过生成超万亿token的受控实验，系统对比了重写策略、生成器模型和源数据三个维度对合成预训练数据质量的影响，发现结构化教学格式（数学题、FAQ、表格、教程）显著优于简单改写，且1B参数模型即可达到最优性价比，据此构建了4860亿token的FinePhrase开放数据集，生成成本降低最高30倍。
TL;DR: A systematic study generating over one trillion tokens to evaluate rephrasing strategies, generator models, and source data for synthetic pretraining data. Structured pedagogical formats (math, FAQ, table, tutorial) consistently outperform paraphrasing baselines; generator models beyond 1B parameters yield negligible gains; the mix-in dataset matters more than source quality. The resulting FinePhrase dataset (486B tokens) outperforms all synthetic baselines at up to 30x lower generation cost.

核心贡献

  • 系统性三维度对比实验：在重写策略（8种已有方法 + 4种新的结构化教学格式）、生成器模型（6个模型家族，135M到27B参数规模）和源数据选择（4种数据集）三个轴上进行受控消融实验，生成超万亿token，首次提供跨设计维度的统一比较。
  • 关键发现：结构化教学格式最优：新提出的四种结构化教学格式（math、FAQ、table、tutorial）全面超越DCLM基线和所有已有合成方法，其中math格式提升最大（+1.54），table格式紧随其后（+1.06）。
  • 颠覆性结论：小模型即够用：生成器模型在1B参数后即出现性能饱和，1B模型甚至优于27B变体；SmolLM2 1.7B配合教学格式达到最佳性价比，输出多样性比格式一致性更重要。
  • FinePhrase数据集与成本效率：发布4860亿token的开放数据集FinePhrase，使用SmolLM2 1.7B生成，相比REWIRE（Llama 3.3 70B）成本降低30倍，相比Cosmopedia（Mixtral 8x7B）降低13倍，在宏平均得分上超越所有已有合成数据基线。

Key Contributions

  • Systematic three-axis ablation generating over one trillion tokens across rephrasing strategy (8 established + 4 new pedagogical formats), generator model (6 families, 135M-27B parameters), and source/mix-in data composition -- the first unified cross-dimensional comparison for synthetic pretraining data.
  • Identification of structured pedagogical formats as dominant factor: Four new formats (math word problems, FAQ, tables, tutorials) consistently outperform both curated web baselines (DCLM) and all prior synthetic methods; the math format achieves +1.54 over DCLM, table achieves +1.06.
  • Generator scale saturation at 1B parameters: Increasing generator model size beyond 1B provides no additional benefit for most rephrasing prompts; the 1B Gemma 3 model (15.31) outperforms the 27B variant (14.76). Output diversity matters more than formatting consistency -- a phenomenon termed "template collapse."
  • FinePhrase dataset (486B tokens) released openly with all prompts and generation framework, achieving up to 30x cost reduction over prior work while outperforming all existing synthetic data baselines (peak macro-average 17.18 vs. DCLM's 13.77).

Methodology

The study constructs a controlled experimental framework evaluating three axes of synthetic data generation:

Rephrasing Strategy: Eight established prompts are compared against four new pedagogical structured formats. Established methods include Nemotron-CC's five strategies (Diverse QA Pairs, Extract Knowledge, Distill, Wikipedia, Knowledge List), REWIRE's Guided Rewrite, and BeyondWeb's Continue/Summarize. The four new formats (math, FAQ, table, tutorial) restructure web text into pedagogically rich structures with discrete, logical, queryable units.

Generator Model: Evaluated along two dimensions -- parameter scale (Gemma 3 at 270M/1B/4B/12B/27B and SmolLM2 at 135M/360M/1.7B) and architectural family (Gemma 3, Llama 3.2, Qwen 3, Granite 3.1, Falcon 3, SmolLM2, all at ~1B scale).

Mix-in and Source Data: Experiments vary the mix-in dataset (DCLM, Cosmopedia, FineWeb-HQ, FineWeb-LQ) and source dataset independently, testing pure synthetic vs. mixed training, and evaluating up-cycling of low-quality data.

Evaluation Protocol: All models use a 1.2B-parameter Qwen 2 architecture trained for 21B tokens (10K steps on 64 H100 GPUs). Performance is assessed across 12 benchmarks in 6 categories using 3-shot cloze prompting with macro-averaged scores.

FinePhrase Construction: Uses SmolLM2 1.7B with suffix-32 speculative decoding on 100 H100 GPUs to rephrase FineWeb documents into four structured formats, yielding 1.35 billion samples and 486B completion tokens.

Results & Highlights

  • Prompt design is the dominant factor: Among established prompts, only Diverse QA Pairs (14.58), Continue (13.73), and Guided Rewrite (13.72) rival DCLM (13.77). All four pedagogical formats surpass DCLM: math (15.31), table (14.83), FAQ (14.45), tutorial (14.30).
  • Generator scale saturation: Performance plateaus at 1B parameters. Gemma 3 1B (15.31) outperforms 27B (14.76). SmolLM2 shows consistent gains up to 1.7B but the trend is clear -- larger is not better for rephrasing.
  • SmolLM2 1.7B is the best generator: Achieves 16.55 macro-average across four prompts, outperforming all other families by 1.01-2.06 points, attributed to its instruction-tuning data containing explicit rewrite tasks (Smol-Rewrite).
  • Mix-in data is more critical than source data: When source quality varies but mix-in is fixed at high quality, the performance delta narrows to 1.78 points maximum. Low-quality source data can be successfully "up-cycled" when paired with a robust mix-in corpus.
  • Pure synthetic training is suboptimal: Mixed training consistently outperforms synthetic-only across all prompts. Original web data is essential for restoring NLU capabilities (WinoGrande, PIQA, HellaSwag).
  • Template collapse phenomenon: Qwen 3 achieves 100% instruction adherence but produces repetitive outputs (115 identical opening patterns), while SmolLM2 achieves only 68% complete solutions but yields more diverse outputs -- and better downstream performance.
  • Cost efficiency: The Pareto frontier is dominated by 1B-class models with structured prompts. FinePhrase generation costs ~14,700 GPU-hours vs. ~352,000 for REWIRE (24x less total compute while generating more tokens).
  • FinePhrase results: FinePhrase-Table achieves peak macro-average of 17.18 (+3.41 over DCLM, +3.63 over Nemotron-HQ-Synth). FinePhrase dominates in General Knowledge and Reading Comprehension while DCLM retains a nominal NLU advantage.

Critical Assessment

This is a well-designed and rigorously executed study that addresses a significant gap in the synthetic data literature. The three-axis experimental framework is methodologically sound, with controlled ablations that isolate individual factors. The sample size (over one trillion tokens generated) is impressive. The findings are counterintuitive in important ways -- particularly the saturation of generator scale at 1B parameters and the discovery that output diversity trumps formatting consistency -- which adds genuine value beyond confirming existing assumptions.

However, the evaluation is limited to 1.2B-parameter models trained on only 21B tokens, following the FineWeb protocol. Whether these findings transfer to frontier-scale training (100B+ parameters, trillions of tokens) remains an open question. The study also focuses exclusively on English benchmarks, limiting generalizability. The "template collapse" analysis, while insightful, is based on only two model families and deserves deeper investigation. Cost comparisons with prior work rely on reported figures that may vary across hardware configurations.

Strengths

  • First truly systematic cross-dimensional comparison of synthetic pretraining data generation, filling a critical gap in the literature.
  • Counterintuitive findings with practical impact: 1B generators suffice, prompt design matters more than model scale, diversity beats consistency.
  • Excellent cost-benefit analysis with Pareto frontier visualization, providing actionable guidance for practitioners.
  • Open release of 486B-token dataset, all prompts, and the complete generation framework -- a major resource for the community.
  • Thorough qualitative analysis of the "template collapse" phenomenon explaining why higher instruction adherence can hurt downstream performance.

Limitations

  • All experiments use a 1.2B model trained on 21B tokens; findings may not scale to larger models or longer training runs.
  • Evaluation is restricted to English benchmarks; applicability to multilingual settings is unknown.
  • The four pedagogical formats, while effective, may not cover the full space of useful structured transformations.
  • Template collapse analysis is limited to two model families and the math prompt; broader investigation is needed.
  • The binary 50/50 synthetic-to-web mixing ratio is not extensively ablated; optimal mixing proportions remain unexplored.
  • Cost comparisons depend on reported figures from prior work that may not be directly comparable across hardware setups.

Relevance

本研究对LLM预训练数据工程具有直接且重要的指导意义。核心发现——结构化教学格式显著优于简单改写、小模型（1B）足以生成高质量合成数据——为预训练数据集构建提供了可立即落地的实践方案。FinePhrase数据集（4860亿token）及其生成框架的开放发布，为社区提供了可直接使用的大规模合成预训练资源。研究揭示的"模板坍塌"现象（输出多样性比格式一致性更重要）对合成数据质量评估提供了新视角。对于关注LLM训练数据质量、数据效率和训练成本优化的研究者与工程师而言，本研究是必读文献。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How do these findings transfer when training models at the 10B-100B+ parameter scale with trillions of tokens?
  • What is the optimal mixing ratio between synthetic and original web data, and does it vary by model scale or training stage?
  • Can the structured pedagogical formats be extended to multilingual settings and code generation domains?
  • How does the "template collapse" phenomenon manifest across a wider range of generator models and prompts?
  • Would curriculum learning -- starting with simpler formats (tutorial) and progressing to more complex ones (math) -- yield additional gains?
  • Can the up-cycling of low-quality data be further improved by adaptive prompt selection based on source document characteristics?
  • What is the long-term impact of synthetic data on model hallucination rates and factual reliability?
