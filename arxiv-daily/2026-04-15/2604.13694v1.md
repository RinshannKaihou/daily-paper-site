# Weight Patching: Toward Source-Level Mechanistic Localization in LLMs

> **一句话总结：** 提出Weight Patching——一种参数空间干预方法，通过将行为特化模型的模块权重移植到基础模型中来定位LLM中能力的真正参数级"源头"，揭示指令跟随能力由浅层MLP神经元作为源端载体、中层注意力头负责聚合路由、下游神经元负责执行的层次化分工结构，并将定位评分应用于机制感知的模型合并，在多个专家组合上取得最优平均性能。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes Weight Patching, a parameter-space intervention that transplants module weights from a specialized model into a base model under fixed input to identify which components actually encode a target capability in their parameters — revealing that activation-space importance and parameter-space source encoding diverge systematically, and that instruction-following is organized as a hierarchy of shallow source carriers, mid-layer aggregation heads, and downstream execution neurons.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Weight Patching (WP)** 方法：在配对的同架构模型（base vs. instruction-tuned）之间，于固定输入下替换特定模块（注意力头或MLP神经元）的权重参数，通过测量对向量锚点控制状态的恢复程度来定位能力在参数中的真正编码位置，而非仅观测激活空间的信号流动
- 引入 **vector-anchor 行为接口**：通过提取任务向量并选择锚点层构建共享内部判据，使参数空间与激活空间的分析在同一标准下可比较，解决了开放生成任务中token级评估不稳定的问题
- 揭示指令跟随的 **层次化机制分工**：浅层MLP神经元作为候选源端载体（阈值式集体效应）、中层注意力头负责聚合/路由（对指令token选择性关注）、下游神经元负责执行（加性模式，直接投射到输出行为），这一分工在六个IFEval任务及3B/8B/13B三种规模上稳定复现
- 提出 **梯度近似加速筛选**：基于一阶Taylor展开的权重归因分数作为精确WP的高效代理，头部级排序重叠率约66-68%，在保持定位保真度的同时将神经元级分析时间从约176万秒（13B精确WP）降至28秒
- 将WP组件评分复用于 **机制感知模型合并 (Precise Fusion)**：以组件级粒度而非全局均匀方式融合多专家参数，在4个专家组合中的3个取得最佳平均性能，IFEval提升最高达+14.77分

## Key Contributions

- Proposes **Weight Patching (WP)**, a parameter-space intervention for source-level mechanistic localization: under fixed input, replaces selected module weights from a specialized model into the base model and measures whether the target control state is recovered, directly testing whether a capability is written in a module's own parameters rather than merely amplified through it
- Introduces a **vector-anchor behavioral interface** that provides a shared internal criterion for evaluating whether a task-relevant control state has been formed or recovered in open-ended generation, enabling consistent comparison between parameter-space and activation-space analyses
- Reveals a **hierarchical division of labor** in instruction following: shallow MLP neurons serve as candidate source-side carriers (exhibiting threshold-like collective effects), mid-layer attention heads aggregate and route instruction-conditioned signals (showing selective attention to instruction tokens), and downstream neurons execute control into output behavior (additive pattern with interpretable output projections) — this hierarchy is stable across six IFEval tasks and three model scales (3B, 8B, 13B)
- Develops a **gradient-based first-order approximation** for efficient full-model screening: achieves ~66-68% top-20 head overlap with exact WP at the head level, reducing neuron-level analysis time from ~1.77M seconds (13B exact) to 28 seconds
- Demonstrates **Precise Fusion** — mechanism-aware model merging reusing WP component scores for component-wise expert fusion: achieves best average performance in 3 of 4 expert combinations, with IFEval gains up to +14.77 points over baselines including DARE, TIES-Merging, Task Arithmetic, and WIDEN

## Methodology

The framework operates on **paired same-architecture models** (a pretrained base model M_base and a behavior-specialized counterpart M_sft) that differ in how strongly they express a target capability. The methodology proceeds in three stages:

1. **Vector-Anchor Behavioral Interface Construction**: A paired dataset of instructed vs. instruction-removed inputs is used to extract a task direction per layer by averaging residual-stream differences at a designated anchor position. The anchor layer is selected by maximizing behavioral recovery under steering injection. The resulting anchor utility F_a measures the cosine similarity between a model's anchor representation and the task vector, providing a shared internal criterion for whether the control state has been formed.

2. **Weight Patching — Exact and Approximate**: For each candidate component c (attention head or MLP neuron), the method replaces the base model's parameter slice with the specialized model's corresponding slice. For attention heads, only query and output projections are replaced (avoiding ambiguity from grouped-query attention shared key/value projections). For SwiGLU neurons, the gate column, up-projection column, and down-projection row are jointly replaced. The source-level effect E_w(c) measures the normalized anchor gap restoration. For scalable screening, a first-order Taylor expansion approximates the restoration as a gradient-difference inner product over the parameter slice.

3. **Hierarchy Recovery**: Activation Patching (AP) provides complementary evidence for aggregation/routing sites. The framework traces upstream suppliers of AP-identified aggregation heads by computing need directions and weight-side support scores. The joint analysis yields a staged hierarchy: source carriers (identified by WP), aggregation/routing modules (identified by AP), and downstream execution units (identified by KL-divergence readouts after the anchor layer).

The method is evaluated on IFEval instruction-following tasks across Llama-3.2-3B, Llama-3.1-8B, and Llama-2-13B, with supplementary results on Qwen2.5, Mistral-7B-v0.3, and Gemma 2. For model merging, WP scores guide component-wise fusion of WizardLM-13B, WizardMath-13B, and Code-Alpaca-13B experts.

## Results & Highlights

- **Source-Aggregation Separation**: WP and AP systematically identify different components. AP concentrates on middle-layer heads (aggregation/routing), while WP shifts toward shallow neurons (source carriers). Layer distributions are misaligned with limited overlap — the two interventions capture different functional stages rather than redundant views.
- **Causal Validation of Hierarchy**: Ablating AP-ranked upstream heads sharply degrades performance but restoring them alone yields limited recovery (consistent with routing role). WP-ranked upstream neurons show both strong degradation under ablation and substantially stronger recovery under restoration (consistent with source encoding).
- **Cross-Task Stability**: The hierarchical organization (neurons as sources/executors, heads as routers) generalizes across all six IFEval tasks, though specific component instances are largely task-specific with partial sharing.
- **Fine-Grained Signatures**: Upstream neurons exhibit threshold-like collective effects (small subsets can trigger abrupt collapse); downstream neurons behave more additively with interpretable output projections. Critical heads in the instruction-tuned model allocate substantially more attention to instruction tokens than matched heads in the base model.
- **MLP Submodule Analysis**: Neuron localization depends primarily on gate+up branches of SwiGLU (activation-forming), not the down branch alone (write-back).
- **Gradient Approximation Fidelity**: First-order scores retain ~66-68% top-20 head overlap with exact WP; neuron-level overlap is 80% on 3B but decreases on larger models. Speedup ranges from ~24x (heads) to ~63,000x (neurons on 13B).
- **Mechanism-Aware Model Merging**: Precise Fusion achieves the best average result in 3 of 4 expert combinations (Code+Instruction, Instruction+Math, Code+Instruction+Math). On IFEval, gains over the next-best method reach +13.67 (Code+Instruction), +8.76 (Instruction+Math), and +14.77 (Code+Instruction+Math). Gains are largest when fused experts are behaviorally distinct.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The core insight — that activation-space importance does not establish parameter-space encoding — is genuinely important and fills a real gap in mechanistic interpretability. The Weight Patching methodology is a natural but non-obvious extension of activation patching into parameter space, and the formal treatment of component-level parameter slices (especially handling grouped-query attention) is technically careful.
- **Experimental rigor**: The paper features a well-structured experimental progression: interface validation, AP-vs-WP comparison, causal hierarchy validation, fine-grained analysis, cross-task generalization, approximation fidelity, and model merging. The inclusion of three model scales and supplementary experiments on three additional architectures strengthens external validity.
- **Scope limitations**: The analysis is limited to instruction-following behaviors on IFEval, where task-vector extraction yields reliable anchor directions. The authors acknowledge that not all IFEval tasks produce stable anchor vectors, restricting analysis to six tasks. Whether the hierarchy generalizes to reasoning, safety, or factual knowledge capabilities remains untested.
- **Conceptual circularity risk**: The vector-anchor interface is itself extracted from model representations, meaning the localization results may depend on the quality and stability of the extracted direction. The paper validates steering recovery but does not provide a formal guarantee that the anchor captures the full capability.
- **Practical impact**: The model merging application demonstrates tangible downstream utility beyond explanation. However, the component-level fusion requires computing WP scores per expert per component, which adds computational overhead compared to global merging methods.

## Strengths

- Clean and important conceptual distinction between activation-space importance and parameter-space source encoding, challenging existing circuit analysis assumptions
- Formally rigorous methodology with precise component-level parameter slice definitions and careful handling of architecture-specific details (grouped-query attention, SwiGLU)
- Comprehensive experimental validation across three model scales, six tasks, and six architectures (supplementary)
- Practical downstream application to model merging with strong empirical results, demonstrating that interpretability insights can directly improve engineering outcomes
- Detailed supplementary material (36 pages total) with full derivations, additional experiments, and code availability

## Limitations

- Restricted to instruction-following tasks where task-vector extraction yields reliable anchors; generalization to reasoning, safety, or factual capabilities is not demonstrated
- Requires paired same-architecture models (base + specialized), which limits applicability to settings where both checkpoints are available
- Anchor-vector stability is not uniform across tasks — the method is only reliable when the extracted steering direction produces sufficient behavioral recovery
- Neuron-level first-order approximation fidelity degrades on larger models (53% overlap on 8B, 49% on 13B vs. 80% on 3B), suggesting scalability challenges
- The source-localization interpretation is relative to the paired-model difference and intervention granularity used; it does not establish absolute mechanistic uniqueness

## Relevance

直接关联LLM机制可解释性(mechanistic interpretability)研究前沿。论文提出从激活空间干预转向参数空间干预的分析范式，对于理解后训练(post-training)如何将能力写入模型参数具有重要参考价值。其层次化分工发现（源端载体-聚合路由-执行电路）为分析其他能力（如推理、安全性）的参数级编码提供了可迁移的分析框架。模型合并应用也连接了可解释性与实际工程需求的桥梁。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can Weight Patching identify where safety-relevant capabilities (e.g., refusal behaviors, harmful content generation) are encoded in parameters, and could this inform targeted safety editing?
- How do source-level localization results differ across post-training methods (SFT vs. RLHF vs. DPO vs. constitutional AI)?
- Does the source-aggregation-execution hierarchy hold for multi-step reasoning capabilities, or is it specific to the relatively localized control required by instruction following?
- Could WP-localized source carriers serve as targets for efficient fine-grained fine-tuning or surgical capability removal?
- How sensitive are the hierarchy results to the choice of anchor extraction procedure — would alternative steering or probing methods yield the same organization?
