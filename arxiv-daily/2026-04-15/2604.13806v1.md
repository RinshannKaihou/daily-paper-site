# Robust Ultra Low-Bit Post-Training Quantization via Stable Diagonal Curvature Estimate

> **一句话总结：** 本文提出DASH-Q框架，通过丢弃噪声敏感的非对角Hessian元素、仅保留稳定的对角曲率估计，结合迭代加权最小二乘闭式求解，在2-bit超低精度量化下零样本推理准确率平均提升7.01%、最高提升14.01%，量化速度比其他优化方法快最多74.5倍。

> **TL;DR:** DASH-Q discards noise-prone off-diagonal Hessian entries and uses diagonal curvature with iterative weighted least squares for ultra low-bit (2-4 bit) post-training quantization, improving zero-shot reasoning accuracy by 7.01% on average (up to 14.01%) over the strongest baselines across five LLMs while being up to 74.5x faster and stable with as few as 2 calibration samples.

## 核心贡献

- 系统分析了GPTQ等基于全Hessian的PTQ方法在超低位宽下退化的根本原因：非对角Hessian元素的信噪比（SNR）远低于对角元素，导致跨通道误差补偿在有限校准数据下引入大量采样噪声
- 提出DASH-Q框架：仅使用对角Hessian近似作为特征重要性权重，将量化问题解耦为独立的加权最小二乘子问题，每个都有闭式最优解，通过坐标下降迭代求解
- 在2-bit量化下，于Llama-3.1-8B、Qwen3-14B、DeepSeek-MoE-16B、Phi-3.5-MoE、Mixtral-8x7B五个模型上零样本准确率平均提升7.01%，最高提升14.01%，且量化速度比其他优化方法快最多74.5倍
- 仅需极少校准数据（2个样本）即可保持稳定性能，而GPTQ在同等条件下困惑度发散至100以上

## Key Contributions

- Provides rigorous empirical and analytical evidence that off-diagonal Hessian entries have substantially lower SNR than diagonal entries, causing GPTQ's cross-channel error compensation to amplify noise at ultra low bit-widths
- Proposes DASH-Q: diagonal Hessian approximation combined with iterative weighted least squares yielding closed-form solutions for scale and zero-point, solved via coordinate descent with ridge regularization
- Achieves state-of-the-art zero-shot reasoning accuracy at 2-bit across five diverse LLMs (dense and MoE), improving over strongest baselines by 7.01% on average and up to 14.01%
- Demonstrates extreme calibration-data efficiency (stable with n=2 samples) and quantization speedup of up to 74.5x over optimization-based PTQ methods (e.g., AWQ, QuaRot)

## Methodology

DASH-Q addresses the layer-wise weight reconstruction objective min ||WX - W_hat X||^2 by reformulating it as a weighted least squares problem where the diagonal of the empirical Hessian H_diag = diag(sum x_j^2) serves as per-channel importance weights. The key insight is that while GPTQ uses the full Hessian inverse for coordinate selection and cross-channel error propagation, the off-diagonal entries are unreliable when estimated from small calibration sets. DASH-Q instead solves for optimal affine quantization parameters (scale s, zero-point z) in closed form:

- s* = Cov_h(W, Q) / (Var_h(Q) + lambda), where h denotes diagonal Hessian weights and lambda is a ridge regularization term
- z* = s* * q_bar_h - w_bar_h

These are iteratively refined via coordinate descent: alternating between updating quantized integers Q (fixing s, z) and re-computing s, z (fixing Q). The algorithm converges within ~9 iterations. A smoothing hyperparameter alpha blends diagonal estimates across layers for stability. The method is evaluated at 4-bit (group size 128), 3-bit (group size 64), and 2-bit (group size 32) across Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen3-14B, DeepSeek-MoE-16B, Phi-3.5-MoE, and Mixtral-8x7B-Instruct-v0.1, comparing against RTN, AWQ, GPTQ, QuIP, QuaRot, and OWQ.

## Results & Highlights

- **2-bit (ultra low-bit):** DASH-Q achieves the highest average zero-shot accuracy on all five models, outperforming the strongest baseline by 7.01% on average and up to 14.01%. On Llama-3.1-8B at 2-bit, DASH-Q achieves 56.52% average accuracy vs. the next best OWQ at 42.51% (a 14.01% gap).
- **Perplexity vs. reasoning disconnect:** On Qwen3-14B at 2-bit, DASH-Q achieves only marginally better perplexity (11.38 vs. QuaRot's 11.79) yet maintains a 10.48% point lead in reasoning accuracy, demonstrating that lower perplexity does not always mean better downstream performance.
- **Calibration robustness:** With just n=2 calibration samples, DASH-Q maintains stable perplexity (8.22-8.36) on Llama-2-7B, while GPTQ's perplexity diverges to 100+ and remains unstable even with n>=28.
- **Quantization speed:** DASH-Q is up to 74.5x faster than optimization-based baselines (e.g., 69s vs. 7719s for AWQ on Qwen3-14B at 2-bit).
- **MT-Bench:** At 2-bit, DASH-Q preserves functional instruction-following capability (e.g., 2.91 avg on Llama-3.1-8B, 6.12 on Qwen3-14B, 6.56 on Mixtral-8x7B) while all other baselines collapse to ~1.0 (near-random).
- **Deployment compatibility:** DASH-Q uses standard affine quantization without architectural modifications, making it directly compatible with TensorRT-LLM and vLLM inference engines.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core insight -- that off-diagonal Hessian entries are noise-prone and that discarding them improves ultra low-bit quantization -- is conceptually simple but well-supported by SNR analysis and batch-to-batch variance experiments. The diagonal approximation trades bias for reduced variance, and this tradeoff is favorable specifically at low bit-widths.
- **Experimental rigor:** Comprehensive comparison against six baselines (RTN, AWQ, GPTQ, QuIP, QuaRot, OWQ) across five models at three precision levels, plus MT-Bench evaluation, calibration sensitivity analysis, and convergence studies. The finding that perplexity does not correlate with reasoning quality at low bits is notable and well-documented.
- **Reproducibility:** Algorithm is clearly specified with closed-form solutions and pseudocode. Hyperparameters are stated (T=9, alpha=0.5, lambda=0.01). However, no code repository is mentioned.
- **Significance:** Practically important for deploying LLMs in resource-constrained environments at extreme compression. The speed advantage (74.5x) and calibration efficiency are strong practical selling points.
- **Limitations of venue:** As a 12-page workshop paper, the theoretical analysis is limited. The bias-variance tradeoff argument is made empirically rather than formally. The method is not compared against training-aware quantization or mixed-precision schemes.

## Strengths

- Clear, well-motivated diagnosis of why GPTQ degrades at ultra low bit-widths (off-diagonal Hessian noise)
- Extremely calibration-data efficient -- stable quantization with as few as 2 samples
- Closed-form solutions enable fast quantization (up to 74.5x speedup over AWQ/QuaRot)
- Consistent improvements across dense and MoE architectures at 2-bit
- Standard affine quantization format ensures deployment compatibility with existing inference engines
- The perplexity-vs-reasoning disconnect analysis is a valuable empirical finding for the community

## Limitations

- Workshop paper (EuroMLSys '26) -- limited theoretical depth; the bias-variance argument remains informal
- The diagonal approximation advantage is specific to ultra low-bit regimes; at 4-bit, DASH-Q is competitive but not dominant
- No comparison with training-aware quantization methods or mixed-precision approaches
- No code repository mentioned, potentially limiting reproducibility
- Evaluation limited to weight-only quantization; activation quantization is not addressed
- The advantage may diminish with very large calibration sets where full Hessian estimates become reliable
