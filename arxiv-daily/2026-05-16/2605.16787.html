Headline Finding

16-30% of hard examples in RLVR training are fundamentally unlearnable — correct rollouts and reward signals are not enough to learn them.

Across three model families, a substantial fraction of difficult math problems resist learning even when the model receives correct solutions throughout training. The root cause is deficient representations that produce gradient outliers and "fake reasoning," not insufficient RL optimization.

H

Hypothesis Testing: What Does NOT Explain It

H1: Rollout Scarcity
Unlearnable examples lack enough correct rollouts during training.
REJECTED — Controlling kpos=1, SFT with correct responses, and k=64 groups all fail.
H2: Gradient Regularization
Clipping or KL penalty suppresses learning signal on hard examples.
REJECTED — Ablating both has no effect on unlearnable examples.
H3: Gradient Interference
Incorrect rollouts push gradients in opposing directions for hard examples.
REJECTED — Cosine similarity shows correct/incorrect gradients remain aligned.
Root Cause Identified
Unlearnable examples are gradient outliers — their gradients are misaligned with the rest of the training set.
CONFIRMED — Low gradient similarity persists throughout training.

R

Fake Reasoning on Unlearnable Examples

Even when the model produces correct final answers on unlearnable examples, the intermediate reasoning is incoherent.

  • GPT-5-mini scores reasoning quality on a 0–5 scale across reasoning traces
  • Unlearnable examples score lowest, and the quality gap widens during training rather than closing
  • Traces contain contradictions and logical errors despite correct final answers
  • The model exploits ungeneralizable shortcuts, not genuine reasoning pathways

Mid-Training as a Remedy

The only intervention that works: improving representations before RL via mid-training.

  • OctoThinker models mid-trained from Llama-3B on 20B tokens show consistently higher gradient similarity for difficult examples
  • Representation quality must be addressed before the RL stage, not during it
  • Data augmentation with similar problems is learnable in isolation but does not transfer to original unlearnable examples
  • Curriculum learning (training easy data first) also fails to improve reasoning quality on unlearnable examples

Critical Edge — Caveats and Open Questions

  • Only tested on math reasoning with small models (0.5B–3B); generalization to code, logic, or agentic tasks is unknown
  • Whether unlearnability persists at 7B, 14B, 70B+ scales remains an open question
  • Unlearnability threshold (pass@1 < 0.1 with N=32) is somewhat arbitrary, though mitigated by 3-run intersection
  • Reasoning quality assessment depends on GPT-5-mini judgment (0–5 scale), introducing subjectivity
  • Mid-training comparison is observational (OctoThinker vs. Llama), not a controlled ablation
  • No concrete algorithmic solution proposed beyond "improve pre-training representations"
  • Process reward models (PRMs) and step-level supervision not explored as potential fixes
  • No analysis of what makes specific examples unlearnable (structure, skills, topic)
