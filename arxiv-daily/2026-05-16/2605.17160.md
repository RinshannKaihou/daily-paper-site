> **一句话总结：** 本文首次系统揭示模型量化会"悄悄"破坏算法反事实（algorithmic recourse）——量化保持预测精度不变，但使用户获得的"最小可行操作建议"失效、成本增加或方向反转；提出CFQ方法，通过反事实一致性正则化和混合精度分配，在保持精度的同时大幅提升recourse稳定性。

> **TL;DR:** Standard quantization preserves predictive accuracy but silently breaks algorithmic recourse: a counterfactual action that flips a decision pre-quantization can fail post-quantization or become substantially more costly. This paper formalizes counterfactual sensitivity under quantization through validity, cost, and direction stability, introduces VD (Validity Drop) and CRG (Counterfactual Recourse Gap) metrics, and proposes CFQ — which jointly trains quantizer parameters and mixed-precision bit allocation via a counterfactual-consistency regularizer at teacher recourse points. On Adult, German Credit, and COMPAS, CFQ reduces VD by roughly 2x vs. the best accuracy-matched baseline while maintaining accuracy, e.g., VD drops from 0.121 to 0.061 on Adult and from 0.110 to 0.052 on German. The paper also provides margin-based theoretical sufficient conditions for recourse transfer under bounded quantization perturbations.

## 核心贡献

- 首次揭示并系统性地形式化了"量化保持精度但破坏算法反事实"这一关键问题，从有效性（validity）、成本（cost）和方向稳定性（direction stability）三个维度定义了量化下的反事实敏感性
- 提出两个新指标：Validity Drop (VD) 衡量预量化 recourse 在量化后失效的概率，Counterfactual Recourse Gap (CRG) 衡量量化后最小 recourse 成本的相对增幅
- 提出 CFQ 方法：通过 teacher recourse point 上的反事实一致性正则化 + Gumbel-Softmax 混合精度比特分配，联合优化量化器参数与精度策略
- 提供基于边际（margin）的理论分析，给出量化扰动下 recourse 可迁移的充分条件
- 在三个标准表格型 recourse 基准上，CFQ 在保持精度的同时将 VD 相对最优非 CF 基线降低约 2 倍

## Key Contributions

- Identify and formalize a novel deployment mismatch: quantization preserves average-case accuracy while altering local decision boundary geometry that determines counterfactuals, causing recourse to break silently
- Introduce Validity Drop (VD) — the fraction of test points whose full-precision recourse fails post-quantization — and Counterfactual Recourse Gap (CRG) — the relative increase in minimal recourse cost after quantization
- Propose CFQ (Counterfactual-Faithful Quantization), which augments QAT with a validity preservation loss at teacher recourse points (computed via K projected gradient steps, detached via stopgrad) plus optional cost/direction matching and hinge margin regularization
- Design a differentiable mixed-precision allocation via Gumbel-Softmax over candidate bitwidths {2, 3, 4, 8}, with layer-specific LSQ step sizes and PACT clipping thresholds per candidate precision
- Provide margin-based sufficient conditions: if the quantization-induced perturbation at a recourse point is bounded by the decision margin gamma, the counterfactual outcome is preserved

## Methodology

CFQ addresses the problem in three main components:

**1. Problem formalization (Section 3):** The paper defines counterfactual sensitivity under quantization along three axes:
- **Validity:** Does the counterfactual point x + delta_tp still flip the quantized model's prediction?
- **Cost:** Does the minimal recourse cost increase after quantization?
- **Direction stability:** Do the feature-level change directions remain consistent between teacher and quantized model?

**2. CFQ objective (Section 4.2):** The core training objective combines three terms:
- Standard task loss L_task (cross-entropy) on the quantized model
- Validity preservation loss L_valid: cross-entropy at teacher recourse points x + delta_tp, forcing the quantized model to predict y_tgt there
- BitCost penalty (parameter-count-weighted sum of layer bitwidths) under a global bit budget

Optional add-ons include:
- **Direction/cost matching (L_match):** Computes a "stitch" recourse delta_st with the quantized model (K projected steps, gradients NOT detached) and minimizes L1 distance and cost gap between delta_st and delta_tp
- **Hinge margin (L_hinge):** Enforces a margin gamma at recourse points so that the target logit exceeds the strongest competing class by at least gamma, providing a buffer against quantization perturbations

**3. Mixed-precision allocation (Section 4.3):** Per-layer bitwidths are selected from {2, 3, 4, 8} via Gumbel-Softmax relaxation. Straight-through estimator is used: hard argmax selection in forward pass, soft relaxed gradients in backward pass. Each layer maintains separate quantizer parameters (LSQ step sizes, PACT thresholds) per candidate bitwidth.

**4. Quantizer parameterization (Section 4.4):** Weights use LSQ-style learnable step sizes; activations use PACT learnable clipping thresholds. Symmetric signed integer quantization with standard ranges.

**5. Teacher recourse computation:** delta_tp is approximated via K projected gradient steps on a differentiable counterfactual loss (cross-entropy to target class), with stopgrad to prevent bilevel optimization overhead. The projection enforces immutability constraints, feasibility bounds, and sparsity constraints.

## Results & Highlights

**Main results (Table 1):** At a representative bit budget on three tabular benchmarks:

| Method | ADULT (Acc/VD/CRG) | GERMAN (Acc/VD/CRG) | COMPAS (Acc/VD/CRG) |
|---|---|---|---|
| FP32 | 0.858 / 0.000 / 0.000 | 0.772 / 0.000 / 0.000 | 0.688 / 0.000 / 0.000 |
| LSQ QAT 8-bit | 0.858 / 0.010 / 0.149 | 0.772 / 0.141 / 0.128 | 0.686 / 0.176 / 0.216 |
| PACT QAT 8-bit | 0.857 / 0.021 / 0.621 | 0.771 / 0.110 / 0.531 | 0.686 / 0.136 / 0.178 |
| CFQ (ours) | 0.857 / 0.001 / 0.071 | 0.771 / 0.002 / 0.064 | 0.687 / 0.074 / 0.083 |

Key observations:
- All quantized models match FP32 accuracy, confirming accuracy-centric quantization works as intended
- LSQ-QAT yields VD of 0.14-0.18 and CRG of 0.13-0.22, meaning 14-18% of recourse actions fail and costs increase 13-22%
- PACT partially mitigates counterfactual fragility via activation range control, but still shows substantial instability (VD up to 0.14)
- CFQ reduces VD by approximately 2x vs. best non-CF baseline (MixedPrec: from 0.121 to 0.061 on Adult, from 0.110 to 0.052 on German)
- Mixed-precision allocation alone (without CF) reduces VD by 15-30% relative to uniform-bit QAT

**Ablation results (Table 2 on ADULT):**
- Removing counterfactual validity term (eta=0) approximately doubles VD (0.061 -> 0.120) and doubles CRG (0.071 -> 0.162) while accuracy stays identical
- Replacing learned mixed precision with uniform bits at matched average cost increases both VD (0.061 -> 0.079) and CRG (0.071 -> 0.094)
- Increasing teacher recourse approximation steps (K=1 to K=3) improves stability

**Budget sensitivity (Figure 3):** CFQ consistently dominates baselines across compression regimes; the gap is largest at low bit budgets where quantization noise is strongest.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The intersection of quantization and algorithmic recourse is essentially unexplored. The insight that accuracy-preserving quantization silently breaks recourse is non-obvious and practically important. The formalization through validity/cost/direction stability is clean and well-motivated.
- **Rigor:** Strong theoretical grounding with margin-based sufficient conditions. The metrics (VD, CRG) are clearly defined and properly evaluated. Ablations systematically isolate component contributions. However, the recourse solver uses only K projected gradient steps as a surrogate for the true constrained optimum — the sensitivity of CFQ to teacher recourse quality deserves more scrutiny (the paper mentions Appendix G addresses this but the analysis is in supplementary material).
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Standard benchmarks (Adult, German Credit, COMPAS) are publicly available via UCI and ProPublica. The optimization framework is described in detail including hyperparameter grids (eta in {0.25, 0.5, 1, 2}, lambda in {1e-4, ..., 1e-1}). Code availability is not stated.
- **Significance:** High for the fairness and trustworthy deployment community. The paper makes a compelling case that accuracy alone is insufficient for evaluating quantized models in regulated decision-making contexts. The VD and CRG metrics are useful contributions that could become standard evaluation tools.
- **Red flags:**
  - Evaluation is restricted to three small tabular datasets with simple neural network architectures. While these are standard recourse benchmarks, the practical impact of quantization is largest in vision and language models.
  - The paper mentions an appendix (Appendix K) evaluating on non-tabular image datasets with structured latent/semantic action spaces, but this extension is not in the main paper.
  - The MixedPrec baseline uses "standard accuracy- or sensitivity-driven criteria" rather than a specific published method, making it somewhat of a straw-man comparison for the mixed-precision aspect.
  - CFQ requires computing teacher recourse actions during training, adding K forward/backward passes per training step — the computational overhead is mentioned but not rigorously benchmarked.
  - The COMPAS results show CFQ still has VD=0.074, meaning ~7.4% of recourse actions still fail — not negligible in a criminal justice context.

## Strengths

- Identifies a genuinely novel and practically important problem at the intersection of model compression and algorithmic fairness
- Clean formal framework: validity/cost/direction stability decomposition is intuitive and comprehensive
- The VD and CRG metrics are simple, interpretable, and directly actionable for practitioners
- Strong ablation study that cleanly isolates the contribution of each component (counterfactual regularizer, mixed precision, teacher approximation quality)
- The margin-based theoretical analysis provides formal grounding and explains why CFQ reduces validity failures
- Results are compelling: accuracy stays identical while VD drops by ~2x, demonstrating the practical value of the approach
- The paper includes a thoughtful Impact Statement discussing societal implications

## Limitations

- Evaluation is limited to three small tabular datasets (Adult: ~48K samples, German Credit: 1K samples, COMPAS: ~7K samples) with simple MLP architectures; scalability to large-scale models is untested
- The computational overhead of CFQ's recourse-aware training is acknowledged but not rigorously quantified or benchmarked against standard QAT wall-clock time
- Does not address interaction with other common compression techniques (pruning, knowledge distillation, structural pruning)
- The non-tabular extension (Appendix K) is deferred to supplementary material rather than integrated into the main results
- The teacher recourse quality depends on the projected gradient approximation (K steps); while Appendix G studies sensitivity, the theoretical analysis assumes exact recourse
- The MixedPrec baseline is described generically rather than compared against a specific published mixed-precision method
- No discussion of how CFQ interacts with fairness-aware training objectives or whether it differentially affects recourse stability across demographic groups

## 相关性

本文将模型压缩（量化）与可信AI（algorithmic recourse、公平性）深度结合，揭示了部署管线中一个被忽视的风险——量化可能"悄悄"使推荐给用户的操作建议失效。对于关注模型压缩对下游决策质量影响、以及在金融/司法等受监管场景部署量化模型的研究者和工程师，本文提供了重要的理论框架和实践工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does CFQ scale to large-scale vision transformers or LLMs where quantization (e.g., 4-bit GPTQ, AWQ) is most impactful? Does the teacher recourse computation remain tractable?
- Does CFQ differentially affect recourse stability across demographic subgroups? Could it inadvertently improve recourse for some groups while degrading it for others?
- What is the wall-clock training overhead of CFQ (including teacher recourse computation) relative to standard QAT, and how does it scale with model size and dataset size?
- How does CFQ interact with fairness-aware training objectives (e.g., demographic parity constraints, equalized odds)? Can they be jointly optimized?
- Can the margin-based theoretical guarantees be extended to non-linear recourse methods (e.g., diffusion-based counterfactuals, CCHVAE) or models with non-smooth activation functions?
- How does the approach extend to post-training quantization (PTQ) regimes where full retraining is infeasible?
