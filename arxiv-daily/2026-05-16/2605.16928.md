> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 RTPurbo，基于全注意力 LLM 的内在稀疏性（仅 15% 的查询头为检索头、16 维低秩投影索引器、动态 top-p token 选择），通过两阶段训练（仅约 600 步）将全注意力模型转换为稀疏推理模型，在 Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B 上实现 1M 上下文下 9.36 倍 prefill 加速和 2.01 倍 decode 加速，且在 LongBench、RULER 和推理基准上精度几乎无损。

> **TL;DR:** RTPurbo exploits three properties of full-attention LLMs — only ~15% of query heads are "retrieval heads" that need long-range context; a 16-dimensional low-rank projection (applied pre-RoPE) suffices as a token indexer; and dynamic top-p selection adaptively allocates per-query token budgets, outperforming any fixed top-k. With a two-stage training pipeline (~600 steps, ~840K trainable parameters), it converts Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B into a sparse-inference model achieving 9.36x prefill and 2.01x decode speedup at 1M context, with near-lossless accuracy on LongBench, RULER (up to 512K), AIME, and MMLU-PRO.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示全注意力 LLM 的内在稀疏性：通过离线校准发现仅约 15% 的查询头为"检索头"（retrieval heads），其余 85% 为"局部头"（local heads），仅需滑动窗口注意力
- 设计 16 维低秩投影索引器（pre-RoPE），避免高频 RoPE 分量对长距离亲和力的干扰，仅约 840K 可训练参数即可学习检索子空间
- 提出 query-dependent 的动态 top-p 选择机制，自适应调整每个头的活跃 token 预算（如 NIAH 任务仅需 ~469 token，multi-K 任务需 ~2462 token）
- 两阶段训练流程：第一阶段训练低维投影（~600 步，~30M token 预算），第二阶段端到端微调
- 在 Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B 上实现 1M 上下文 9.36 倍 prefill 加速和 2.01 倍 decode 加速，RULER 上平均 90.06%（vs 全注意力 89.65%）

## Key Contributions

- Identifies intrinsic sparsity in full-attention LLMs: only ~15% of query heads are "retrieval heads" requiring long-range context, while 85% are "local heads" that only need sliding-window attention + attention sinks
- Designs a 16-dimensional low-rank projection indexer applied pre-RoPE, circumventing high-frequency RoPE degradation of long-range affinity, with only ~840K trainable parameters
- Proposes query-dependent dynamic top-p selection that adaptively adjusts per-head active token budgets (e.g., ~469 tokens for NIAH vs. ~2462 for multi-K at 32K context)
- Two-stage training pipeline: Stage 1 trains low-dimensional projections (~600 steps, ~30M tokens from FineWeb), Stage 2 performs end-to-end fine-tuning
- Achieves 9.36x prefill and 2.01x decode speedup at 1M context on Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B, with RULER average of 90.06% (vs. full attention 89.65%)

## Methodology

RTPurbo is a head-wise sparse attention framework with four components:

1. **Offline Head-wise Calibration (Section 3.1):** A calibration sequence is constructed by inserting an identical "needle" span at both the beginning and end of a long FineWeb document. Each head's retrieval score is measured as the attention mass from the later needle to the earlier one. This calibration requires only a single long text sequence and partitions heads into a retrieval set (top ~15%) and a local set. The retrieval behavior is highly stable and input-agnostic.

2. **Adaptive Sparse Attention Mechanism (Section 3.2):** Local heads use sliding window (8192 tokens) + attention sinks (4 tokens) during both prefill and decode. Retrieval heads perform dense attention during prefill but switch to query-aware dynamic sparse selection during decode. Low-rank projections (W_Q, W_K in R^{16 x 128}) are applied to pre-RoPE features to estimate query-key relevance, then a top-p threshold selects the active token set S_h(m). The full feature space and exact positional geometry are preserved for actual attention computation.

3. **Two-Stage Training (Section 3.3):** Stage 1 trains the low-dimensional projection parameters for retrieval heads (~840K total parameters for 210 retrieval heads), aligning compressed attention distributions with original distributions using 8000 sequences from FineWeb (32K-80K tokens). Stage 2 performs end-to-end fine-tuning. Total training is ~600 steps with ~30M tokens.

4. **Hardware-Aware Decoding Kernel (Section 3.4):** A bandwidth-optimized single-warp CTA design with no shared memory, using register-only state and 2-token unrolled inner loop with vectorized half2 instructions. KV ranges are partitioned into multiple splits for parallel CTA processing when needed.

For MQA/GQA models, compute sparsity is measured at query-head level, while memory sparsity is measured at KV-head level (union of selected token sets across mapped query heads).

## Results & Highlights

**Accuracy on LongBench (Table 3):** RTPurbo with top-p achieves average 54.24% (best among sparse methods), vs. full attention 53.80%, RazorAttn 52.98%, MInference 48.39%, Quest 50.69%. The top-p variant outperforms the top-k variant (53.30%) by 0.94 points.

**Accuracy on RULER 64K (Table 4):** RTPurbo top-p achieves 90.06% average, matching or exceeding full attention (89.65%). Baselines degrade: MInference drops to 65.61% at 128K, FlexPrefill to 77.77%, Quest to 70.60%. RTPurbo top-p: 85.49% at 128K.

**Ultra-long Context (128K-512K):** RTPurbo sustains robust accuracy at extreme lengths with sparsity >97.1% at 512K, while all baselines experience catastrophic degradation.

**Reasoning Benchmarks (Table 5):** On AIME24/AIME25, RTPurbo top-p perfectly matches full attention (86.67/86.67). On MMLU-PRO subcategories, remains within ~1 point of full attention across most subjects.

**Efficiency:** Prefill speedup ranges from 2.83x at 32K to 9.36x at 1M over FlashAttention-2. Decode speedup ranges from 1.47x at 32K to 2.01x at 1M.

**Ablations:**
- Retrieval head ratio: 15% vs. 30% shows negligible accuracy difference; 10% causes substantial drops (e.g., MMLU-PRO Math: 88.2 -> 79.3).
- Low-dim size: dim=16 and dim=32 yield identical accuracy; dim=4 has weaker fitting ability and requires recalling many more tokens.
- Top-p vs. top-k: top-16k computes ~8K extra tokens but recovers only 3.8% more attention mass than top-p (0.9).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The combination of three observations is well-motivated and cleanly articulated. The retrieval head identification via needle-based calibration is simple but effective. The pre-RoPE low-rank projection is a technically sound contribution — recognizing that RoPE's high-frequency components degrade long-range affinity is insightful. Dynamic top-p is a natural extension of nucleus sampling to sparse attention. However, individual components have precedents: DuoAttention/RazorAttention also partition heads; DSA also uses learned indexers; top-p selection is analogous to nucleus sampling.

**Rigor:** The experimental evaluation is thorough — LongBench, RULER (64K and 128K), ultra-long (up to 512K), AIME24/25, MMLU-PRO subcategories. The ablation studies are solid (retrieval head ratio, low-dim size, top-p vs. top-k). The claim of "a few hundred training steps" is verified: 600 steps with ~30M tokens. However, the paper only tests on Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B and Qwen3-30B-A3B-Think — both MoE models from the same family. Generalization to dense models (Llama, Mistral) and smaller/larger scales is not demonstrated. The reasoning benchmark results (Table 5) show some concerning drops: MMLU-Chem drops from 88.00 to 51.40 with top-k and is not reported for top-p in the extracted tables, suggesting potential weaknesses in certain domains.

**Reproducibility:** Good overall. Training details are provided in Appendix C (8000 sequences from FineWeb, learning rate 1e-3 with cosine annealing). The calibration procedure is simple (single sequence with inserted needles). The kernel design is described in detail. However, the custom CUDA kernel implementation may be difficult to reproduce exactly.

**Significance:** High practical impact. The finding that full-attention models are already intrinsically sparse and can be converted with minimal training is valuable for the deployment community. The 9.36x prefill speedup at 1M context is impressive and could enable new applications.

**Red flags:**
- Only tested on two closely related Qwen3 MoE models — generalization is a major open question
- The reasoning benchmark table (Table 5) shows RTPurbo top-k has severe drops on MMLU-Chem (51.40 vs 88.00), MMLU-CS (50.49 vs 86.10), MMLU-Math (71.60 vs 93.60), and MMLU-Philosophy (51.70 vs 69.94). While top-p recovers most of this, it suggests the sparse approximation is fragile for certain reasoning domains.
- Speedup numbers are for a single attention layer, not end-to-end model inference. Actual application-level speedups will be lower.
- The 15% retrieval head ratio was tuned for this specific model; it is unclear how to determine this for other models.

## Strengths

- Clean decomposition of the problem into three well-supported observations about attention sparsity
- Minimal training cost (~600 steps, ~840K parameters) makes adoption very practical
- Dynamic top-p mechanism elegantly handles the variable-density nature of retrieval
- Strong performance on RULER benchmarks, especially at extreme context lengths (128K-512K) where baselines collapse
- Comprehensive ablation studies validating each design choice
- Hardware-aware kernel design that accounts for memory-bound nature of sparse decoding
- Per-head adaptivity: concentrated heads use minimal tokens, diffuse heads expand as needed

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on Qwen3 MoE models (30B total / 3B active parameters); generalization to dense models and other architectures is unproven
- Speedup measured at single-layer level, not end-to-end model inference — actual wall-clock improvements may be significantly lower
- The reasoning results with top-k show severe drops in certain MMLU subcategories, suggesting the sparse approximation quality is domain-sensitive
- The indexer overhead during prefill (computing low-rank projections for all retrieval heads) is not thoroughly analyzed
- Only 15% retrieval head ratio is thoroughly evaluated; the paper does not provide a principled method for determining this ratio for new models
- The training data (FineWeb, Dolma 3 Longmix) may not cover all domains equally, potentially biasing the learned projections
- The approach assumes retrieval head behavior is stable across inputs; while supported empirically, edge cases may exist

## Relevance

本文与高效长上下文推理直接相关，提出的稀疏注意力转换方法对大规模语言模型部署具有重要实用价值。低训练成本（~600 步）使其可快速应用于现有模型。动态 top-p 和低维检索子空间的发现对理解注意力机制本质也有启发意义。对 Qwen3 系列模型的使用者尤为实用。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does RTPurbo perform on dense models (Llama 3, Mistral) and at different scales (7B, 70B)?
- What is the end-to-end inference speedup (not just single-layer), including indexer computation and sparse attention kernel overhead?
- Can the retrieval head ratio be automatically determined without manual tuning, perhaps via a threshold on calibration scores?
- How does RTPurbo interact with KV cache compression methods like group quantization — are they complementary?
- What causes the severe accuracy drops on MMLU-Chem and MMLU-CS with the top-k variant, and are there similar (smaller) drops hidden in the top-p variant?
- Does the pre-RoPE projection capture the same information as RoPE-free attention, or does it learn a fundamentally different retrieval pattern?
- How stable are the retrieval head assignments during fine-tuning or continued pretraining of the base model?
