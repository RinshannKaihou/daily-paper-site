Structural Representation Alignment for Accelerating Diffusion Transformers

sREPA augments REPA's point-wise feature alignment with pairwise token relational geometry matching via Gram matrix regression. On SiT-XL/2, it reaches FID 7.17 in 400K steps — beating vanilla SiT trained 18x longer (7M steps, FID 8.3) — at just 1.77% throughput cost.

Method — Three-Loss Pipeline

1
Flow Matching Loss (baseline)
Standard SiT training objective via Scalable Interpolant Transformer on SD-VAE latents.
2
Point-wise Alignment (from REPA)
Minimize negative cosine similarity between projected student features and DINOv2 teacher at each spatial token: L_proj = mean(1 - cos_sim(g(h_s), h_t))
3
Structural Alignment (new)
Compute Gram matrices of pairwise cosine similarities for student & teacher. Mask diagonals. Two variants: sREPA-MSE (regress off-diagonal entries directly, parameter-free) or sREPA-KL (softmax-normalize rows, match via KL divergence with τ=0.2). MSE wins slightly.

Strengths

  • Clean motivation: follows logically from iREPA's finding that spatial structure drives REPA's effectiveness
  • Parameter-free MSE variant — just Gram matrix computation, minimal implementation cost
  • Well-designed control experiment (Table 10) proving gains are not from stronger point-wise supervision alone
  • Consistent improvements across all model scales (B, L, XL) and training durations (100K to 1M)
  • Plug-and-play compatibility with iREPA and REG demonstrated

Limitations & Caveats

  • O(N²) pairwise complexity — bottleneck for high-resolution or video generation
  • Only evaluated on ImageNet class-conditional (256×256, 512×512); no text-to-image or video
  • 512×512 improvements are marginal (FID 2.29 vs 2.32) — diminishing returns at higher resolution
  • Structural loss alone fails without point-wise alignment — it is a complement, not a replacement
  • No theoretical explanation for why MSE outperforms KL

Critical Assessment

Novelty is moderate — Gram matrix structural losses have a long history (Gatys 2015, relational KD), and the contribution is identifying the right formulation for DiT alignment, not inventing the loss. Rigor is strong with extensive ablations. The key weakness: CFG comparisons are not at equal compute (sREPA at 200 epochs vs vanilla at 80), and the 512px gains are marginal. The O(N²) scaling is a real limitation for higher-resolution work.
