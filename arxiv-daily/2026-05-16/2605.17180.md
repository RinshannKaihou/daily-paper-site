> **一句话总结：** 本文将自监督学习中的投影头建模为骨干网络表示流形上的可训练黎曼度量，从理论上证明光滑非线性投影头通过诱导Hessian负特征值使维度坍缩成为不稳定鞍点，而线性/ReLU投影头在连续梯度流下无法逃逸坍缩只能依赖离散时间动力学和BatchNorm；同时从度量退化角度解释了投影头训练后必须被丢弃的"断头台效应"。

> **TL;DR:** This paper develops a Riemannian geometry theory for projection heads in SSL, modeling the head as a trainable metric on the representation manifold. Key results: (1) smooth nonlinear heads (Swish/GELU) induce negative Hessian eigenvalues at collapsed equilibria, making collapse an unstable saddle point, while linear and ReLU heads cannot escape collapse under continuous-time gradient flow; (2) the head performs implicit subspace whitening, with depth controlling geometric capacity; (3) enforcing invariance forces the head to produce a metric singularity that annihilates information along augmentation tangent directions (21.85x orbit compression on CIFAR-10), explaining why the head must be discarded post-training. Published at ICML 2026.

## 核心贡献

- 将投影头建模为骨干网络表示流形上的可训练黎曼度量，建立了完整的几何理论框架，证明了线性投影头等价于学习Mahalanobis度量
- 证明非线性MLP投影头可以沿弯曲轨迹进行局部度量适应（通过拉回度量实现隐式白化），而线性投影头因曲率屏障（Proposition 3.3）无法全局展平弯曲的损失流形
- 提出了投影头深度与几何容量的定量关系（Proposition 3.4）：近似误差 epsilon 必须小于 lambda_min/(2LM) 才能保证各向同性条件数，过浅的网络会导致条件数失效
- 核心定理（Theorem 4.1）：在坍缩平衡点处，光滑非线性头的曲率交互项诱导负Hessian特征值，使坍缩状态成为不稳定鞍点；而线性头的二阶导为零，坍缩状态是不可逃逸的
- 从度量奇异性角度（Proposition 5.2, Theorem 5.3）解释了"断头台效应"：投影头在增强切空间方向产生度量退化，Fisher信息矩阵的秩在投影后降低 dim(V_aug)，因此必须丢弃投影头以恢复骨干网络保留的语义信息

## Key Contributions

- Models the projection head as a trainable Riemannian metric (pullback metric) on the backbone representation manifold, with linear heads equivalent to learning a Mahalanobis metric M = W^T W
- Proves the Curvature Barrier (Proposition 3.3): no constant linear map can globally flatten a curved loss manifold because the pullback metric inherits nonzero Riemann curvature from the ambient space
- Establishes the Conditioning Failure Threshold (Corollary 3.5): head approximation error must satisfy epsilon < lambda_min(H_eff*) / (2LM), providing a quantitative capacity-depth relationship
- Proves Generic Instability of Collapse (Theorem 4.1): smooth nonlinear heads induce negative eigenvalues in the effective Hessian at collapsed equilibria, converting stable minima into strict saddle points; linear/ReLU heads have zero second derivatives and cannot provide escape directions under continuous-time gradient flow
- Proves the Linear Rank Propagation theorem (Theorem 5.1): linear heads force the backbone covariance to align with the row space of W, limiting rank to at most k
- Characterizes the Metric Singularity (Proposition 5.2) and Information Hierarchy (Theorem 5.3): enforcing invariance produces a degenerate pullback metric that annihilates information along augmentation tangent directions, with Fisher information rank decreasing by dim(V_aug)
- Empirically validates via continuous Hessian tracking: Swish heads inject explicit negative curvature (lambda_min < 0) while ReLU/linear heads remain PSD; orbit compression of 21.85x on CIFAR-10 (ResNet-18) and 4015x on ViT-Tiny

## Methodology

The paper develops its theory across three interconnected pillars:

**1. Riemannian Framework (Sections 2-3).** The representation space Z is treated as an open subset with coordinates from ambient Euclidean space. The projection head h_phi induces a pullback metric G(z) = J_h(z)^T nabla^2 L(h(z)) J_h(z) on the tangent space T_z Z. The effective Hessian decomposes into a PSD Gauss-Newton term and an interaction/curvature term driven by the head's second derivatives. Linear heads produce a constant Jacobian (Mahalanobis metric), while nonlinear heads produce state-dependent Jacobians that can locally adapt. The Intrinsic Hessian Rank r (Definition 2.1) captures the effective dimensionality of the loss landscape. Theorem 3.2 proves that MLPs with smooth activations can epsilon-approximate the ideal whitening map along training trajectories via C^1-universal approximation, while Proposition 3.3 shows linear heads provably cannot achieve this on curved manifolds.

**2. Collapse Instability Analysis (Section 4).** At a collapsed configuration z* where f_theta(x) ~ c for all inputs, the effective Hessian H_eff = G(z*) + M(z*) is analyzed. G(z*) is PSD but rank-deficient (rank r < d). For nonlinear heads, the interaction term M = sum rho_i nabla^2 h_i is a random symmetric matrix with full support (Assumption 5), which almost surely has negative eigenvalues in the null space of G. This makes z* a strict saddle point. For linear/ReLU heads, nabla^2 h = 0 identically, so no negative curvature is available. The paper motivates this via Wigner's semicircle law: as d increases, the spectral radius of M grows as O(sqrt(d)), overpowering the fixed positive eigenvalues of G.

**3. Information-Invariance Trade-off (Section 5).** The augmentation tangent space V_aug(z) captures local nuisance directions. Proposition 5.2 shows that achieving invariance forces G(z) to become singular on V_aug. Theorem 5.3 quantifies the Fisher information loss: rank(I_{h(z)}) <= rank(I_z) - dim(V_aug), formalizing why the head must be discarded (the "guillotine effect").

**Experimental Setup.** CIFAR-10/100 with ResNet-18 and ViT-Tiny backbones; SimCLR/SimSiam objectives; Hessian eigenvalue tracking via shifted power iteration with exact Hessian-vector products (double backpropagation, 20 iterations per estimate); augmentation orbit analysis using rotation sweeps (0-360 degrees, 12 steps); linear and MLP probes to quantify information loss. Foundation model analysis on DINO, VICReg, and Barlow Twins pretrained checkpoints.

## Results & Highlights

- **Collapse escape dynamics (Figure 3):** Swish heads exhibit a clear negative eigenvalue spike (lambda_min < 0) precisely when representation variance begins to increase from pseudo-collapsed initialization. The condition number kappa shows a violent spike (Spearman rho_s = 0.609) as the local basin stretches into a steep saddle point before stabilizing. ReLU heads show no such transitions.
- **ReLU gap (Figure 4):** Under continuous-time gradient flow (no BatchNorm), ReLU and linear heads remain trapped at collapse (lambda_min > 0 always). Only discrete-time noise and BatchNorm allow escape. Swish/GELU heads escape natively.
- **Orbit compression (Table 1, CIFAR-10/ResNet-18):** The projection head achieves 21.85x mean orbit spread reduction (from 2.25e-2 to 1.0e-3), with 1.93x higher local curvature. Critically, this is selective: intra-orbit distance shrinks 4.78x while inter-class distance only shrinks 3.89x, yielding 1.22x class/orbit separation improvement.
- **Information hierarchy:** Backbone linear probing accuracy is 52.27% vs 37.55% in the head (-14.72%), confirming information destruction. The nonlinearity gap (MLP-Lin) increases from 3.19% (backbone) to 6.01% (head), indicating the head entangles rather than merely erases information.
- **ViT results (Table 5):** Effects are amplified in ViT-Tiny with 4015x orbit compression (vs 21.85x in ResNet) and 2.59x curvature ratio (vs 1.93x), but ViTs exhibit optimization stiffness where all heads remain trapped near collapse in 20 epochs due to sharper loss landscapes.
- **Depth ablation:** Linear heads (L=1) force rank bottleneck that propagates upstream; deeper heads (L=2,3) successfully isolate the metric singularity.
- **Foundation models:** DINO heads act as geometric compressors (implicit whitening), while VICReg/Barlow Twins heads act as dimensional expanders (explicit whitening demands full-rank covariance). In all cases, the head serves as a geometric buffer.
- **Timescale separation (Table 3):** Without BatchNorm, head updates are 56-2544x larger relative to weights than backbone updates. BatchNorm suppresses this to near-parity (<3x), showing it disrupts natural timescale separation.
- **Curvature stability across datasets:** CIFAR-10 curvature ratio is 1.93x; CIFAR-100 is nearly identical at 1.84x, suggesting the compression mechanism is a property of the MLP architecture itself rather than dataset-dependent.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** This is the first work to model projection heads as Riemannian metrics and provide rigorous proofs connecting head curvature to collapse instability. The ReLU gap result -- that piecewise-linear activations lack the theoretical mechanism for collapse escape under continuous-time dynamics -- is particularly novel and has practical implications.
- **Rigor: Very strong.** The paper provides complete proofs (29 pages with appendices) for all theoretical claims. Seven assumptions are explicitly stated and individually justified (Appendix A.2). The proof of Theorem 4.1 uses a clean decomposition of the effective Hessian and applies results from random matrix theory (Wigner's semicircle law) and transversality theory. The C^1 universal approximation argument for Theorem 3.2 is well-constructed with explicit error bounds.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Detailed experimental setup in Appendix C, publicly available code, and specific hyperparameter choices reported. However, some Hessian tracking experiments appear computationally expensive (double backpropagation per batch).
- **Significance: High.** Provides actionable design principles: use smooth activations (Swish/GELU) over ReLU, maintain sufficient head depth, and the theory explains the community's empirical shift toward GELU in modern SSL frameworks (DINO, MAE). The metric singularity framework also suggests paths toward designing "head-less" objectives.
- **Red flags:**
  - **Assumption load:** The theory requires 7 assumptions (smooth loss, full-rank Jacobian, regular training trajectories, backbone nondegeneracy, generic initialization, nonzero residual gradients, timescale separation). While individually justified, their joint satisficing is not fully verified.
  - **Limited scale of experiments:** CIFAR-10/100 with ResNet-18 and ViT-Tiny are relatively small-scale. No ImageNet-scale validation of the Hessian tracking claims. The foundation model analysis is observational rather than controlled.
  - **ViT results partially contradict the theory:** The paper honestly acknowledges that ViTs exhibit "optimization stiffness" where all heads remain trapped near collapse in 20 epochs, despite the theoretical prediction of instability. This suggests the theory may be necessary but not sufficient -- a significant caveat.
  - **Timescale separation assumption under BatchNorm:** The empirical data (Table 3) shows BatchNorm suppresses timescale separation to near-parity, which would violate Assumption 7. Yet BatchNorm is standard in practice. This creates a tension between the theory's assumptions and practical architectures.
  - **No downstream task performance comparison:** The paper evaluates geometric properties but does not show whether smooth activations actually improve downstream accuracy or convergence speed in large-scale settings.

## Strengths

- Remarkably comprehensive single-author work with rigorous mathematical proofs and complementary empirical validation
- Provides the first mechanistic explanation connecting projection head curvature to collapse avoidance, resolving a standing theoretical puzzle in non-contrastive SSL
- The ReLU gap result has immediate practical implications and explains the industry shift to GELU/Swish
- Honest about limitations: explicitly discusses the ViT stiffness issue and BatchNorm's disruption of timescale separation
- The orbit compression and information hierarchy framework elegantly formalizes the informal "guillotine effect" explanation
- Universal across contrastive (SimCLR) and non-contrastive (SimSiam) methods, and across CNN/ViT architectures
- Suggests concrete future directions: metric-preserving "head-less" objectives, extending to discrete augmentations

## Limitations

- Experimental scale is limited (CIFAR-10/100 only); no ImageNet-scale validation of the Hessian tracking or collapse escape dynamics
- ViT results partially contradict the theoretical predictions (all heads trapped in 20 epochs), suggesting the theory is necessary but not sufficient
- The timescale separation assumption (Assumption 7) appears violated when BatchNorm is used, which is the standard practice
- The theory does not provide explicit convergence rate bounds or predict optimal hyperparameters (depth, width, learning rate) for projection heads
- No comparison of downstream task performance between smooth and piecewise-linear activations at scale
- The framework assumes continuous augmentation parameters; discrete augmentations (e.g., horizontal flip) are not covered
- Single-author limitation: no co-authors to provide independent verification of proofs or additional experimental resources

## Relevance

本文从黎曼几何角度系统解释了自监督学习中投影头的设计原理，对于理解表示学习中的维度坍缩机制、设计更优的投影头结构（如使用光滑激活函数、控制深度与几何容量的关系）具有直接的理论指导意义，也为设计无需投影头的新SSL目标函数提供了理论线索。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the theory predict optimal projection head depth as a function of backbone dimensionality d, loss intrinsic rank r, and augmentation tangent space dimension m?
- How does the framework extend to multi-modal SSL (e.g., CLIP), where the projection head maps between different modality spaces?
- Is it possible to design invertible or variance-preserving losses that enforce invariance without inducing the metric singularity, thus eliminating the need for a projection head?
- What is the quantitative relationship between head capacity (depth x width) and the approximation error epsilon, and can this inform architecture search?
- How do the geometric predictions change when using normalization layers other than BatchNorm (e.g., LayerNorm, GroupNorm) in the projection head?
- Can the Hessian tracking methodology be scaled to ImageNet efficiently, perhaps via sketching or randomized methods?
