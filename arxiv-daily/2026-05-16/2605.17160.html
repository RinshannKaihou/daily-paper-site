When Bits Break Recourse: Counterfactual-Faithful Quantization

Chaymae Yahyati, Ismail Lamaakal, Khalid El Makkaoui, Ibrahim Ouahbi — Mohammed First University

Standard quantization preserves predictive accuracy but silently breaks algorithmic recourse: a counterfactual action that flips a decision pre-quantization can fail post-quantization or become substantially more costly — and no one monitors for this.
Key Metric
~2×
VD reduction vs. best accuracy-matched baseline. VD drops 0.121 → 0.061 on Adult, 0.110 → 0.052 on German — with identical accuracy.
Why This Matters
Accuracy alone is insufficient for evaluating quantized models in regulated decision-making.
Recourse actions given to users can silently break after quantization — a deployment hazard no one currently monitors.
Critical Edge
7.4% of COMPAS recourse still fails under CFQ.
Evaluation limited to three small tabular datasets. Computational overhead not rigorously benchmarked. No interaction with fairness-aware training studied.
Novelty
Unexplored
The intersection of quantization and algorithmic recourse is essentially unexplored. First paper to formalize and address this mismatch.

Assessment
  • Genuinely novel problem at the intersection of compression and fairness
  • Clean formal framework: validity / cost / direction stability decomposition
  • VD and CRG metrics are simple, interpretable, and directly actionable
  • Strong ablation isolating each component's contribution
  • Margin-based theoretical grounding for recourse transfer conditions
  • Thoughtful Impact Statement on societal implications
  • Only three small tabular datasets; no vision/LLM evaluation in main paper
  • Computational overhead of recourse-aware training not rigorously quantified
  • No interaction studied with fairness-aware training or demographic subgroups
  • MixedPrec baseline described generically, not a specific published method
  • 7.4% COMPAS VD remains non-negligible in criminal justice context
  • Non-tabular extension deferred to supplementary Appendix K
CFQ Method
Full-Precision Model → Teacher Recourse δtp → CFQ Training → Quantized Model
1
Formalize Sensitivity
Define counterfactual sensitivity along validity, cost, and direction stability. Introduce VD and CRG metrics.
2
Compute Teacher Recourse
Approximate δtp via K projected gradient steps on the full-precision model, detached via stopgrad.
3
Counterfactual Consistency Loss
Lvalid forces the quantized model to predict ytgt at teacher recourse points. Optional Lmatch aligns cost/direction; Lhinge enforces a margin buffer.
4
Mixed-Precision via Gumbel-Softmax
Per-layer bitwidth selection from {2, 3, 4, 8} with layer-specific LSQ step sizes and PACT clipping thresholds. Straight-through estimator for gradients.
New Metrics Introduced
VD — Validity Drop
Fraction of test points whose full-precision recourse fails post-quantization. Measures reliability: does the recommended action still flip the decision?
CRG — Counterfactual Recourse Gap
Relative increase in minimal recourse cost after quantization. Measures burden: how much more expensive does the cheapest action become?
Open Questions
