COAST -- Contrastive Conceptor Activation Steering for Vision-Language-Action Models

A training-free, inference-time method that steers frozen robot policies by applying Boolean conceptor algebra to residual-stream activations. Extracts a contrastive success/failure subspace from as few as 15 rollouts and gates the action expert at inference -- no weight updates, no gradient computation.

Miao, Kim, Yang, Ungar UPenn 2026-05-16
Headline Claim

Zero-weight-update policy repair at inference time -- deploy a frozen VLA model, then patch its failure subspace from a handful of rollouts for +20% simulation and +40% real-robot success rate improvement.

How It Works

Two-Phase Pipeline
Phase 1 -- Offline Fitting

Extract Contrastive Subspace

Collect N rollout episodes from the frozen policy. Extract residual-stream activations from the action expert at a selected layer. Fit separate conceptors for success (C+) and failure (C-) activations, then compose via Boolean algebra.

C_steer = C+ AND NOT C- = (C+-1 + (I - C-)-1 - I)-1
Phase 2 -- Inference Steering

Multiplicative Gate on Residual Stream

At each denoising step, the residual activation h at layer l is replaced by h' = h MT. Identity interpolation ensures soft projection, with beta controlling steering strength.

M = (1-beta) * I + beta * C_steer   |   h' = h MT

Mechanistic Insights

Why It Works

⬢ Low-Rank but Multi-Dimensional

Only ~1% of the hidden dimension is discriminative between success and failure, but that subspace is strictly multi-dimensional. Rank-one CAA captures only ~half the gain that the full conceptor achieves.

⇄ Shared Failure Geometry

Failure representations share substantial cross-task geometry (r=0.30--0.49, p<.005) while success representations are task-specific. This enables zero-shot conceptor transfer between tasks with similar failure modes.

◈ Overlap Predicts Efficacy

The success-failure subspace overlap strongly predicts per-task steering efficacy (rho=0.59). Tasks with nearly disjoint subspaces show small gains, as expected.

⚙ Efficient Hyperparameter Selection

Geometric diagnostics (conceptor quota for layer selection, overlap for aperture selection) recover 93% of oracle performance while evaluating only 3--8% of the full configuration grid.

Cross-Task Conceptor Transfer

Failure subspace containment between source/target pairs correlates with transfer gain, while success containment shows no such relationship. Transferred conceptors sometimes exceed self-steered gains.

Assessment

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Comprehensive evaluation: 3 architectures, 3 sim benchmarks, real hardware (DROID)
  • Strong mechanistic analysis connecting subspace geometry to steering efficacy
  • Training-free, minimal data (15 rollouts), negligible per-step overhead
  • Clean ablation study isolating multiplicative gating vs. contrastive subspace vs. directions
  • Cross-task transfer finding is both surprising and practically valuable
  • Efficient hyperparameter selection via geometric diagnostics (93% oracle recovery)

Limitations

  • Requires both success and failure rollouts; uniformly successful tasks cannot use full contrastive steering
  • Real-robot evaluation limited to 3 tasks with 15 trials each
  • Diffusion Policy benefits less than VLA models (VLM pretraining may be key)
  • ~24% inference latency overhead from the forward hook (non-negligible for real-time control)
  • Early checkpoints chosen specifically for mixed success/failure rates; gains smaller on fully-trained policies
  • Hyperparameter selection heuristic not yet validated beyond tested architectures
