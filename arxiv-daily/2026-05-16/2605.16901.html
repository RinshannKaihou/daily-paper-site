CAR-SAM: Cross-Attention Reconstruction for Post-Training Quantization of the Segment Anything Model

Identifies two SAM-specific failure modes in 4-bit quantization — attention dissipation and reconstruction oscillation — and solves them via MatMul-Aware Compensation and Joint Cross-Attention Reconstruction.

Houji Wen · Jiangyong Yu · Jun Li · Dawei Yang
Nanjing Univ. Sci. & Tech. · Houmo AI

+14.6 mAP over PTQ4SAM
CAR-SAM reaches 39.3 mAP at W4A4 vs. PTQ4SAM's 24.7 and QDrop's 26.4. Full precision is 53.3. Covers SAM, SAM2 families across segmentation, detection, and VOS.

Why This Matters

Enables practical edge deployment of SAM models at 4-bit precision with no retraining. The cross-attention quantization framework generalizes beyond SAM to any vision foundation model with bidirectional attention (LLaVA, BLIP, etc.).

Critical Edge

Absolute 4-bit mAP (39.3 on SAM-B) still lags full precision (53.3) by 14 points. Underperforms PTQ4SAM on SAM2-L (38.2 vs. 38.8). No code release mentioned.

⚠
Two Failure Modes Identified

Attention Dissipation

Under 4-bit quantization, cross-attention maps lose semantic focus and collapse. The query-key and attention-value MatMul operations amplify quantization noise, causing the decoder to "forget" which image regions matter.

Reconstruction Oscillation

SAM's bidirectional cross-attention (token-to-image + image-to-token) creates a coupled error system. Independent per-block reconstruction causes errors to bounce between branches, preventing convergence.

⚙
Method: MAC + JCAR
M

MatMul-Aware Compensation

Formulates reconstruction for QKT and AV MatMul ops explicitly. Transfers activation quantization error back to projection weights via closed-form solutions (Sylvester equation + SVD regularization).

J

Joint Cross-Attention Reconstruction

Theorem 3.1 proves coupled error propagation between bidirectional branches. Joint optimization of both cross-attention modules as a single unit eliminates oscillation. Requires only small unlabeled calibration data.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean problem identification with insightful analysis of SAM decoder's unique cross-attention challenges
  • Strong theoretical grounding: Theorem 3.1 with formal proofs, Sylvester equation derivations
  • Comprehensive evaluation across SAM + SAM2 families, three tasks, multiple bit-widths
  • Practical efficiency gains with zero retraining required
  • Qualitative attention heatmaps effectively illustrate the dissipation problem

Limitations

  • No code release mentioned — hinders reproducibility
  • Does not consistently beat PTQ4SAM on all variants (SAM2-L segmentation, SAM-L detection shows "-")
  • Absolute 4-bit performance remains far from full precision, limiting real-world use
  • Calibration set size and PTQ computational cost not discussed
  • No evaluation on other cross-attention vision models to test generalization claims
