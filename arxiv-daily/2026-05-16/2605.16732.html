Post-training Quantization for Diffusion Transformers

DiRotQ: Rotation-Aware Quantization
for 4-bit Diffusion Transformers

A W4A4 quantization framework that decomposes DiT activations via PCA into a high-variance FP16 tail and a low-variance 4-bit body, then fuses the inverse rotation offline into weights at zero cost. On PixArt-Sigma, it achieves FID 15.9 — lower than the FP16 baseline of 16.6.

Why This Matters

4-bit DiT quantization that matches or exceeds FP16 quality, enabling 12B-parameter models to run on a single 24GB consumer GPU with 2.3x speedup.

Critical Edge

FID lower than FP16 is suspicious — may indicate overfitting to the evaluation protocol. Forward rotation XV must remain online, adding unquantified compute overhead per inference step.

Key Finding

PCA + random orthogonal rotation reduces channel variance ratio from 83x to 2x, making 4-bit activation quantization viable. The offline weight fusion of VT eliminates inverse rotation cost entirely.

Method Pipeline
01
PCA Rotation

02
Mixed Precision
Top ~10% channels (XUh) stay FP16. Residual ~90% (XUl) quantized to 4-bit.
→
03
Residual Rotation
Random orthogonal R equalizes residual variance: max/median drops 83x → 2x.
→
04
Offline Fusion
Inverse VT fused into weights offline. Forward XV runs online (AdaLN blocks fusion).
→
05
GPTQ Weights
Both weight branches quantized via GPTQ (damping 0.01, block 128).

Strengths
  • Substantial, consistent improvements over SVDQuant across 3 architectures, 2 datasets, and 7+ metrics
  • Clean offline weight fusion: inverse rotation adds zero inference overhead
  • 9.2x speedup at batch=2 eliminates CPU offloading on consumer hardware
  • Thorough ablation demonstrating each component's contribution and GPTQ synergy
  • Robust tail fraction: performance saturates at r=10%, no per-layer tuning needed
Limitations
  • Forward rotation X·V must remain online; compute cost not explicitly quantified
  • Time-averaged PCA basis shared across all denoising steps; per-timestep-group rotations could improve quality
  • Not evaluated on video DiTs with temporal coherence constraints
  • PTQ4DiT comparison missing on FLUX.1-dev (prohibitively expensive calibration)
  • Memory overhead of FP16 rotation matrices and high-precision branches not explicitly quantified in absolute terms
  • VLM-as-a-Judge correlation with human judgment not validated for these specific judges in the quantization setting
