> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Subspace PGA 指标，量化语言模型中间层表征几何是否为预测而组织，发现小模型（d ≤ 1024）在训练后期出现"绕行"现象——少数主导方差方向偏离读出子空间、掩盖预测结构——而大模型（d ≥ 2048）全程保持直接对齐，揭示了规模如何决定表征几何的组织方式。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces Subspace PGA, a metric that quantifies whether a language model layer's distance geometry aligns with the unembedding readout subspace versus random subspaces of equal dimension. Across seven Pythia models (70M--6.9B) and three cross-family models (OLMo-1B, Phi-1.5, Gemma-2B), intermediate layers show significant predictive alignment (z = 9--24). However, small models (d <= 1024) progressively lose this alignment at late layers during training -- with z dropping as low as -32 in Pythia-410M -- while large models (d >= 2048) preserve it throughout. The mechanism is a capacity trade-off: a few dominant directions (PC1) migrate away from the readout subspace, masking rather than destroying predictive structure. Removing top-k dominant directions (CCR-5/10 correction) restores positive z for all models with d >= 768. Neither spectral metrics (RankMe, stable rank, participation ratio, etc.) nor loss curves capture this distinction. The readout subspace also concentrates cross-architecture convergent structure, and z-scores correlate with downstream probing accuracy (rho = 0.69, p = 0.002 on Pythia-1B for AG News).

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Subspace PGA** 指标，量化某层表征距离结构与 unembedding 矩阵 W_U 的读出子空间的对齐程度，通过与等维随机子空间的 z-score 比较，检验几何是否为预测而组织
- 发现规模依赖的**双模态**行为：大模型（d >= 2048）"直接"保持预测对齐；小模型（d <= 1024）在训练后期出现"绕行"——PC1 偏离读出子空间、z 降至负值——最终层再恢复对齐
- 揭示了**掩蔽机制**而非破坏：移除 5 个主导方向后，所有 d >= 768 的模型在各层均恢复正向 z-score，说明预测结构被掩盖而非消除
- 训练动态显示：所有模型在前 1000 步即建立预测组织，小模型约在 96k 步开始出现渐进掩蔽，而此现象在验证损失上无任何痕迹
- 读出子空间**集中了跨架构的收敛结构**：100 维读子空间的 RSA（表征相似性分析）在多个模型对中超过全 2048 维空间的 RSA
- Subspace PGA 可作为**无任务层选择信号**：在 Pythia-1B 上 z-score 与 AG News 探针准确率层间相关性达 0.69（p = 0.002）

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **Subspace PGA**, a principled metric that projects hidden states onto the top-k right singular vectors of W_U and compares how well the resulting distance structure preserves full-space distances, versus 100 random k-dimensional subspaces sampled uniformly on the Grassmannian, yielding a z-score
- Identifies two geometric regimes: **Direct** (large models, d >= 2048) where geometry stays prediction-aligned across all layers, and **Detour** (small models, d <= 1024) where late-layer PC1 migrates away from readout, z drops to negative values, and the final layer projects geometry back into readout alignment
- Demonstrates that collapse is **masking, not destruction**: CCR-5/10 correction (removing top-5 dominant directions) restores positive z at every layer for all models with d >= 768
- Training dynamics reveal the detour is **learned, not architectural**: predictive organization is established within the first 1,000 steps for all models; progressive masking emerges around step ~96k only in small models, coinciding with the "compression-seeking" phase where effective rank declines
- Shows the readout subspace **concentrates cross-architecture convergent structure**: 100-dimensional readout RSA exceeds full 2,048-dimensional RSA in several cross-model pairs (Pythia-1B, OLMo-1B, Phi-1.5)
- Subspace PGA serves as a **task-free layer selection criterion**: z-scores correlate with AG News probing accuracy on Pythia-1B (rho = 0.69, p = 0.002)

## Methodology

- **Subspace PGA**: Performs SVD on the unembedding matrix W_U, takes top-k = 100 right singular vectors to define the readout subspace V_k. Projects hidden states onto V_k and onto 100 random k-dimensional subspaces, computes how well each projection preserves full-space pairwise distance structure (Mantel test with 1,000 permutations), and reports a z-score against the random null distribution.
- **Anisotropy correction**: Mean-centering followed by CCR-1 (componentwise centering and removal of the first principal component). CCR-5/10 used as diagnostic to test masking hypothesis.
- **Models**: Seven Pythia models (70M, 160M, 410M, 1B, 1.4B, 2.8B, 6.9B) plus cross-family models OLMo-1B, Phi-1.5, and Gemma-2B. The Pythia 1B/1.4B pair isolates depth contribution (same width, different depth).
- **Data**: 1,000 contexts from OpenWebText (>= 64 tokens, truncated to 512). Same texts for all models to isolate model effects.
- **Training dynamics**: Tracked across 143,000 training steps using Pythia checkpointed models (160M, 410M, 1B).
- **Cross-model RSA**: Representational Similarability Analysis comparing pairwise distance correlations in readout subspace vs. full space across model pairs.
- **Downstream probing**: Linear probes for AG News topic classification on per-layer hidden states.
- **Spectral metrics compared**: RankMe, stable rank, participation ratio, alpha-ReQ, isotropy, condition number, TwoNN intrinsic dimensionality -- none reliably distinguish the two regimes.

## Results & Highlights

- Peak z-scores of 9--24 at mid-layers across all models with >= 12 layers, confirming that next-token prediction shapes representation geometry toward the readout
- **Sharp transition at d ~ 2048**: minimum z-score across layers shows a clear phase transition from negative (small models) to positive (large models)
- Pythia-410M reaches z = -32 at late layers; Pythia-160M shows similar collapse in the 65--95% relative depth range
- **Collapse zone timing**: Small models develop late-layer masking around step ~96k, coinciding with the "compression-seeking" phase where effective rank declines; Pythia-1B undergoes comparable PC migration but never crosses into negative z
- **Loss is blind**: Validation loss descends smoothly and similarly across all three checkpointed models; the collapse leaves no fingerprint on loss
- **Cross-architecture convergence**: Readout RSA exceeds random null in all three cross-model pairs and sometimes exceeds full-space RSA, suggesting the readout filters out model-specific variance
- **Probing correlation**: In Pythia-1B, z and probing accuracy track layer-by-layer (rho = 0.69, p = 0.002). In Pythia-410M, probe accuracy stays ~0.85 even in the collapse zone, confirming masking rather than erasure
- **Spectral metrics fail**: None of the seven spectral metrics tested reliably separate direct from detour regimes

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The reframing from "what shape does geometry have?" to "what is geometry organized for?" is conceptually clean and productive. Subspace PGA is a principled, well-defined metric with a proper statistical test (Mantel permutation).
- **Rigor**: Strong. The masking-vs-destruction diagnostic (CCR-5/10) is elegant. The training dynamics analysis (143k steps across three scales) provides temporal evidence. Cross-family validation with both tied and untied embeddings strengthens generalization claims. The readout coverage argument (fixing k = 100 is conservative for small models) is well-reasoned.
- **Reproducibility**: High. All models publicly available on HuggingFace. Data (OpenWebText) is public. Hyperparameters specified (N = 1000, k = 100, 100 random draws). Code promised upon acceptance.
- **Significance**: The finding that scale changes *how* (not just *how well*) models organize representations has clear implications for mechanistic interpretability, layer selection, and understanding emergence. The observation that logit lens fails in collapse zones while probing succeeds is practically useful.
- **Concerns**: (1) Single authorship limits the breadth of perspectives and peer feedback. (2) The AG News probing experiment uses only one task and one dataset; stronger evidence would use multiple diverse downstream tasks. (3) The causal claim is explicitly weakened -- the paper acknowledges it observes geometric patterns, not causal mechanisms; a stronger test (rotating PC1 back into V_k) is left to future work. (4) The scale threshold (d ~ 2048) is observed primarily in the Pythia family; cross-family models tested (Phi-1.5 at d = 2048, OLMo-1B at d = 2048, Gemma-2B) are fewer and at similar scales, so the generality of the exact threshold remains uncertain. (5) SSMs, MoEs, and encoder-decoder models are not analyzed. (6) The paper assumes linear readout; nonlinear routing (tuned lens) is acknowledged as a limitation.

## Strengths

- Clean metric design with principled null hypothesis (Grassmannian random subspaces, Mantel permutation test)
- Systematic evaluation across seven model scales plus three cross-family models, spanning both tied and untied embeddings
- Elegant masking-vs-destruction diagnostic via CCR correction
- Training dynamics analysis provides temporal causality evidence
- Practical utility: task-free layer selection via z-scores
- The detour/direct regime framing is intuitive and memorable
- Honest about limitations (metric scope, CCR sensitivity, generalization, causality)

## Limitations

- Probing validation limited to a single classification task (AG News); does not establish general utility of z as a layer selection signal
- Scale threshold (d ~ 2048) primarily observed in Pythia; cross-family validation uses models at similar widths, so the universality of the threshold is uncertain
- No causal mechanistic evidence -- the paper explicitly acknowledges this; the geometric pattern is observed, not causally intervened upon
- Assumes linear readout; nonlinear routing before W_U (tuned lens) is not captured
- SSMs, MoEs, and encoder-decoder models are outside scope
- Final-layer states taken post-LayerNorm; the effect of normalization on the metric is not analyzed
- Single-author work; broad peer review not yet conducted

## 相关性

本文研究了语言模型表征几何的组织方式与模型规模的关系，发现大模型在所有中间层保持"为预测而组织"的几何结构而小模型出现"绕行"现象。这对于理解 scaling 对模型内部机制的影响、mechanistic interpretability 工具（如 logit lens）的适用条件，以及如何选择最佳中间层用于下游任务具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the detour/direct regime distinction predict differences in few-shot learning, in-context reasoning, or other emergent capabilities at scale?
2. Can the scale threshold (d ~ 2048) be shifted through architectural choices (e.g., wider but shallower models, different normalization schemes, mixture-of-experts)?
3. What computational function does the dominant direction that migrates away from the readout serve? Can it be linked to specific circuits or features?
4. Would a causal intervention (rotating PC1 back into V_k at collapse-zone layers) restore logit lens interpretability without degrading loss?
5. How does Subspace PGA behave during fine-tuning, RLHF, or other post-training alignment -- does the direct regime persist, or can alignment training induce detours?
6. Does the cross-architecture convergence in readout directions extend to multilingual or multimodal models?
