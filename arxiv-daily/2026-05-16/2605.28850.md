> **一句话总结：** 本文构建了 TradeArena 审计式交易代理测试平台，系统研究了 LLM 在金融决策中的行为对齐与表征动态，发现在 80 个滚动失败锚点下有效秩收缩率高达 97%，且 BGE-M3 Transformer 和 Qwen2.5-0.5B 白盒探针均验证了该签名，同时揭示 GPT-5.5 和 Gemini 3.1 Pro 在 51 股票日内实验中对高相关资产对（如 GOOGL/GOOG 相关系数 0.994）的集中风险敞口被风险层反复削减。

> **TL;DR:** This single-author paper introduces TradeArena, an auditable LLM trading-agent testbed, and discovers that LLM planning representations exhibit measurable pre-failure signatures — effective-rank contraction occurs in 97% of 80 rolling failure anchors across hash, LSA, BGE-M3 Transformer, and Qwen2.5-0.5B white-box probes. In a 51-stock intraday experiment, GPT-5.5 assigns combined intended weight 1.6 to GOOGL/GOOG (correlation 0.994) before the risk layer clips approved weight below 0.11. Structured risk feedback improves calibration for Gemini but placebo feedback sometimes yields higher short-horizon returns, revealing an alignment-vs-performance trade-off. The paper explicitly frames findings as research diagnostics, not investment evidence.

## 核心贡献

- 构建了 TradeArena 审计式交易代理测试平台，具备六大设计目标：模块化、可复现性、执行真实性（含佣金、滑点、延迟、部分成交）、风险感知、可审计性和代理原生评估
- 发现了 LLM 交易失败前的可测量表征签名：规划嵌入偏离正常状态质心、融合计划-风险表征分离、有效秩收缩（80 个锚点收缩率 97%）
- 跨四种嵌入探针验证鲁棒性：Hash64（收缩率 97.5%）、LSA32（收缩率 85-86.3%）、BGE-M3 Transformer（GPT-5.5 和 Gemini 3.1 Pro 收缩率 100%）和 Qwen2.5-0.5B 白盒隐藏状态
- 揭示了结构化风险反馈的微妙效果：Gemini 的真实反馈校准分数显著高于安慰剂/隐藏反馈，但安慰剂反馈有时产生更高短期回报，反映了"对齐税"现象
- 在 51 只股票日内实验中发现了"相关性盲点"：GPT-5.5 对 GOOGL/GOOG（相关系数 0.994）分配组合目标权重 1.6，风险层将审批权重削减至 0.11 以下；Gemini 对 HD/LOW（0.924）、JPM/BAC 等高相关对犯同样错误

## Key Contributions

- Introduces TradeArena with six design goals (modularity, reproducibility, execution realism, risk awareness, auditability, agent-native evaluation), featuring commission, slippage, latency, participation limits, rejected/partial fills, and replayable trajectories
- Discovers pre-failure representation signatures: planning embeddings drift from normal-state centroids, effective-rank contracts on the representation manifold (97% contraction rate across 80 rolling failure anchors in hash space)
- Validates robustness across four probe types: Hash64 (97.5% contraction), LSA32 (85-86.3%), BGE-M3 Transformer (100% on GPT-5.5 and Gemini 3.1 Pro), and Qwen2.5-0.5B white-box hidden-state probe
- Shows structured risk feedback acts as an alignment signal without fine-tuning: Gemini's true-feedback runs show much higher calibration scores than placebo/hidden runs, but placebo feedback sometimes yields higher short-horizon returns, revealing an alignment tax
- Identifies a correlation blind spot in 51-stock intraday experiment: GPT-5.5 assigns combined intended weight 1.6 to GOOGL/GOOG (correlation 0.994) before risk layer clips to below 0.11; both GPT-5.5 (2,924 clipped decisions) and Gemini (1,200) far exceed the Markowitz baseline (357)

## Methodology

The paper is organized around five research questions rather than a single tool description, using TradeArena as the experimental substrate.

**TradeArena Architecture.** Four design choices separate observations from decisions, make risk a first-class stage (pre-trade, in-trade, post-trade reports), model execution as environment response (delays, partial fills, rejections, slippage), and write every stage to shared replayable trajectories. Uses deterministic synthetic market data as default; real data adapters can be added without changing the logging contract.

**Experimental Matrix (Table 2).** The study spans 23+ experiment families including:
- Core baselines: risk-aware realistic agent vs. buy-and-hold, ideal execution, no-risk gate (seeds 3, 7, 11)
- 30-seed statistical robustness sweep with confidence intervals and paired differences
- 120 heterogeneous synthetic market stress tests (Gaussian, Student-t, jump, mixed tails)
- Historical rolling-window validation (4 two-year windows, GSPC/BTC-USD/ETH-USD, 2021-2026)
- Frontier model matrix: GPT-5.5, Gemini 3.1 Pro, Kimi K2.5, GLM-5, Claude Opus 4.7 (cached Poe-mediated)
- CoT-free ablation, contrarian audit probe, noise-injection (0/5/10/20% OHLCV perturbation)
- 51-stock 1-hour intraday portfolio with Markowitz MVO baseline

**Representation Diagnostics.** The key analysis pipeline:
1. Embed plan texts and fused plan-risk texts using hash, LSA, BGE-M3, or hidden-state encoders
2. Define rolling failure anchors (80 total across 8 LLM trajectories)
3. Measure effective rank (normal vs. pre-failure windows), centroid distance, and step velocity
4. Report contraction rate, balanced accuracy, and rank delta

**Risk Feedback Protocol.** Three conditions tested: true feedback (actual clipped/blocked/violation counts), hidden (risk reports removed), and placebo (same structure but counterfactual values). A contrarian audit probe injects false severe risk reports during benign trajectories.

## Results & Highlights

**Core Performance (Table 3).** Risk-aware realistic agent: Return 0.423, Sharpe 7.696, Max DD -0.025, Fill rate 0.876. Buy-and-hold: Return 0.257, Sharpe 2.913, Max DD -0.261. Both use same realistic execution, so the difference is not from easier fills. No-risk ablation has higher return (0.468) but only 0.673 fill rate and 62.3 rejected orders vs. 22.0 for risk-aware.

**30-Seed Robustness (Table 4).** Risk-aware vs. buy-and-hold paired return difference: 0.137 [0.117, 0.156] 95% CI, does not cross zero. Drawdown improvement: 0.234 [0.228, 0.240].

**120-Market Stress Test (Table 5).** Risk-aware outperforms buy-and-hold by 0.153 mean return, win rate 0.842, p < 10^-12. In crisis-volatility markets, return advantage is not significant (p = 0.288), but drawdown improvement remains large and significant.

**Representation Signatures (Tables 25-27).** Hash64 plan-view: 80 anchors, mean shift 0.122, rank delta 8.703, contraction rate 0.975. LSA32: contraction rate 0.850-0.863. BGE-M3 on GPT-5.5: rank delta 279.860, contraction 1.000, balanced accuracy 0.921. White-box Qwen2.5-0.5B confirms the same directional pattern.

**Lexical Controls (Table 29).** Across 200 anchors, rank delta 9.025 with 97% contraction, but TTR change only -0.002 and token entropy increases 0.045. The model is not simply repeating fewer words — the diagnostic captures semantic/financial-reasoning narrowing, not trivial lexical collapse.

**Noise Injection (Table 30).** Market-fused view stays above 0.75 balanced accuracy at 5%, 10%, and 20% OHLCV perturbation. Plan view is unaffected by construction (language-only). Rank contraction persists at 93.3-100% across all noise levels.

**51-Stock Intraday (Table 12-13).** GPT-5.5: Return -0.022, 2,924 clipped decisions, Herfindahl 0.045. Gemini: Return -0.005, 1,200 clipped, Herfindahl 0.035. Markowitz baseline: Return -0.005, 357 clipped, Herfindahl 0.023. GPT-5.5 assigns combined intended weight 1.6 to GOOGL/GOOG (correlation 0.994), clipped to 0.095. Rationales are "single-name momentum" and "name-level rationale" — explicit covariance language is rare.

**Risk Feedback.** Gemini's true-feedback runs have much higher calibration scores in both crisis scenes (2022 Tech/Rates, 2023 SVB). DeepSeek V4 Pro direct-provider comparison shows true feedback improves Tech/Rates return and SVB calibration relative to placebo/hidden, but risk gate still intervenes at every step.

**Manifold Diagnostics (Table 31).** Pre-failure step distance exceeds normal step distance for Model A (ratio 1.176 plan, 1.167 fused) and Model B no-risk (ratio 1.127 plan, 1.113 fused). Normal/pre effective rank shows dimensionality contraction: e.g., Model A plan 27.68/20.60, Model B no-risk plan 26.79/17.90.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Weicheng Xue** (single author). The paper is explicitly positioned as a research study of LLM financial decision dynamics rather than evidence of deployable trading performance. The author is transparent about limitations: synthetic data, short horizons, Poe-mediated routing rather than direct vendor APIs, and the white-box probe using Qwen2.5-0.5B as a surrogate rather than exposing proprietary frontier model internals. The 32-page paper covers an unusually broad experimental matrix for a single investigator, which is both a strength (thoroughness) and a concern (breadth vs. depth trade-off).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The application of representation-manifold diagnostics (effective-rank contraction, embedding drift velocity) to LLM trading agents is novel. The correlation-blindness failure mode — showing that LLMs generate plausible single-name narratives while failing to internalize pairwise covariance — is a concrete, well-demonstrated finding with clear practical implications. TradeArena fills a genuine gap: existing systems (FinRL, Qlib, FinGPT, TradingAgents) lack full agent-trace, risk-lifecycle, execution-realism, and raw-trajectory support simultaneously.
- **Rigor**: Generally good but with caveats. The 80-anchor rolling analysis with four probe types and lexical controls is thorough. The 30-seed statistical robustness with CIs and p-values is appropriate. However, many frontier model results are cached through Poe rather than direct vendor APIs, which introduces routing, wrapper prompt, and truncation confounds the author acknowledges. The white-box probe uses Qwen2.5-0.5B on recorded decision texts, not actual frontier model activations — it is a surrogate, not direct evidence.
- **Reproducibility**: Partial. The paper mentions "standard-library only" implementation for p-value computation and cached JSONL responses, but does not explicitly state whether TradeArena code is open-sourced. The reliance on Poe-mediated frontier model access with caching partially addresses reproducibility but introduces platform-specific confounds.
- **Significance**: The correlation-blindness finding is the most impactful result — it directly demonstrates a systematic failure mode in LLM financial reasoning that is not fixable by better prompting alone. The pre-failure representation signatures are scientifically interesting but their operational value (can they be used for real-time intervention?) remains undemonstrated. The broader alignment lesson — that constraint-following language is not the same as constraint-grounded reasoning — connects to a wider LLM safety concern.
- **Red flags**: (1) The paper is extremely broad for a single-author work — 23+ experiment families across 32 pages. Some analyses (crisis-scene probes with only 12 decisions per trajectory) are too shallow to draw firm conclusions. (2) The Markowitz baseline is justified as isolating the covariance-reasoning gap, but the absence of any RL baseline (PPO, DQN) weakens the practical significance — the paper acknowledges this as future work. (3) The LLM probes in the 51-stock experiment cover only two models (GPT-5.5, Gemini 3.1 Pro) over 40 hourly steps — this is a narrow window. (4) The unsupported-claim proxy for hallucination detection is audit-grounded but unvalidated against human annotation (the template is provided but not completed).

## Strengths

- Exceptionally honest framing — explicitly states results are "benchmark diagnostics, not investment evidence"
- The correlation-blindness analysis is concrete and well-illustrated with specific asset pairs (GOOGL/GOOG at 0.994 correlation, HD/LOW at 0.924)
- Thorough robustness validation: four embedding types, lexical controls, noise injection, CoT-free ablation, contrarian audit probe
- TradeArena's architecture cleanly separates data, analysis, strategy, risk, execution, and evaluation — a genuine methodological contribution
- The "alignment tax" insight (benign placebo feedback can make agents sound more risk-aware while adversarial false audits push toward unjustified conservatism) connects financial agents to broader LLM safety concerns
- Multiple direct-provider sanity checks alongside Poe-mediated frontier matrix

## Limitations

- The paper's breadth comes at the cost of depth in individual analyses — crisis-scene probes have only 12 decisions per trajectory
- No deep RL baseline (PPO, DQN, FinRL-style) — the Markowitz baseline answers a different question than "can another learned policy do better"
- White-box probe uses Qwen2.5-0.5B surrogate rather than actual frontier model activations — it validates directional consistency but not the exact mechanism
- Historical experiments use daily Yahoo Finance data with weekly decisions, not intraday limit-order-book data
- 51-stock intraday LLM probes cover only two models over a single 40-hour week
- The unsupported-claim hallucination proxy is unvalidated against human annotation
- The pre-failure signatures' operational value (real-time detection latency, minimum trajectory length) is not quantified
- Poe-mediated access introduces routing confounds the author acknowledges but cannot fully control

## Relevance

本文系统性揭示了 LLM 交易代理在组合构建中无法内化资产间协方差约束的"相关性盲点"，并提出了基于表征流形诊断的失败预警方法，对关注 LLM 在高风险决策场景中的可靠性、可解释性和安全性研究具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What is the minimum number of decision steps needed before the effective-rank contraction signature becomes reliably detectable for real-time intervention?
- Would incorporating explicit covariance information in the LLM prompt (e.g., a rolling correlation matrix) mitigate the correlation blind spot, or is the failure rooted deeper in the model's reasoning architecture?
- How do the representation signatures change when LLM agents interact in a multi-agent market where each agent's trades affect prices observed by others?
- Can the pre-failure diagnostics be adapted into a lightweight monitor that runs alongside production LLM agents without requiring full embedding recomputation at each step?
- Do the same pre-failure representation signatures appear in non-financial sequential decision-making domains (e.g., autonomous navigation, clinical treatment planning)?
- How sensitive are the findings to the choice of risk-gate thresholds — does the correlation blind spot worsen or improve with looser constraints?
