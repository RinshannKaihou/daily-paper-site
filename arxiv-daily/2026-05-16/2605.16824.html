Confidence Geometry Reveals Trace-Level Correctness in LLM Reasoning

Token-level confidence trajectories alone encode measurable geometric structure that separates correct from incorrect reasoning paths — no questions, reasoning text, hidden states, or verifiers required.

Why This Matters

A lightweight, content-agnostic signal for smarter inference-time scaling — sample and weight by confidence geometry, not just count votes.

◆

Key Finding

The 1D confidence trajectory — a sequence of token-level distributional peakedness scores — carries correctness-related geometric structure. A lightweight 1D CNN (NeuralConf) trained only on binary correctness labels learns to discriminate correct from incorrect traces using nothing but this sequence.

The signal is concentrated at the tail of reasoning and grows with longer observed segments, meaning the model's late-stage confidence dynamics are the most informative.

⚠

Critical Edge

  • Single model family: All main results use DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B. Generalizability to larger or different-architecture models (Llama, GPT-4, Claude) is untested.
  • Modest practical gains: +0.7-0.8pp over majority voting may not justify training overhead when simply sampling more traces could close the gap.
  • No comparison to learned verifiers: The content-agnostic design precludes comparison with ORM/PRM methods — the most competitive alternatives for trace scoring.
  • Restricted benchmarks: MMLU limited to STEM; all benchmarks are structured (math, MCQ). Open-ended generation and code are untested.
  • Training data requirement: NeuralConf needs labeled correct/incorrect traces, limiting applicability where ground truth is unavailable.

✓ Strengths

  • Elegant content-agnostic experimental isolation — clean test of whether confidence alone carries correctness information.
  • Thorough positional analysis via sliding-window, matched head-vs-tail, and length sweeps.
  • Honest limitations framing — authors acknowledge modest gains and scale-dependence.
  • Question-level splits, five random seeds, multiple complementary metrics (DBI, AUC, aggregation accuracy).
  • ReClor boundary-case analysis adds nuance; not overclaiming universality.

✗ Limitations

  • Single backbone for main results — distilled 1.5B model only.
  • Confidence metric (k=20) inherited from DeepConf without ablation.
  • No comparison with process/outcome reward models.
  • 1,200-question subsets are small; scaling behavior untested.
  • ReClor shows the phenomenon is not universally strong — critical model scale threshold unknown.
?

Open Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does confidence geometry hold across different LLM families (Llama, GPT, Claude, Gemini) and at larger scales?
  2. Can NeuralConf scores be combined with process reward models or self-consistency for compound gains?
  3. What is the minimum training set size needed for reliable discrimination?
  4. Does tail-concentration generalize to agentic/multi-step reasoning where answers are not at the end?
  5. Would improving model calibration (temperature scaling, conformal prediction) strengthen or weaken the signal?
  6. Is there a critical model scale threshold at which confidence geometry becomes reliably informative?
