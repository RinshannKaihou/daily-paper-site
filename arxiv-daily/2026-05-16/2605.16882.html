E-PMQ: Expert-Guided Post-Merge Quantization with Merged-Weight Anchoring

W. Wang, Y. Gu, S. Cai, Y. Wang, P. Wang, J. Wu, H. Yang PolyU & InfiX.ai 2605.16882 2026-05-16

Naively quantizing a merged model causes catastrophic failure because quantization deviation and merging deviation are coupled in low-bit reconstruction. E-PMQ decouples them via expert-guided Hessian calibration plus a merged-weight anchor, recovering accuracy from 34.8% to 76.7% on 20-task 4-bit merging.

Key Metric
+41.9%
accuracy recovery on 20-task CLIP-ViT-L/14 at 4-bit (34.8% → 76.7%)

Why This Matters
Merged models are increasingly deployed for multi-task edge scenarios — but they catastrophically fail under quantization. E-PMQ makes low-bit multi-task deployment practical by treating merged quantization as a distinct problem, not an afterthought.
Critical Edge
Requires retaining all K source expert models during calibration and computing per-expert Hessians — storage and compute scale linearly with K. LLM gains are modest (1–2% on Llama-3.1), raising questions about overhead justification at scale.

How It Works

01 · Expert Hessians
Compute per-expert Hessian matrices Hil from calibration data passed through each source expert independently. These capture each expert’s output sensitivity.
02 · Anchoring
Add Frobenius-norm regularizer λl ||Ql−Wml||F2 to prevent calibration drift and make the effective Hessian positive definite.
03 · Closed-form + Rounding
Solve the combined quadratic in closed form, then feed into GPTQ-style sequential rounding (Algorithm 1). Plug-and-play with GPTQ, AWQ, RTN.
Qcontl = (Σi Wil Hil + λl Wml) (Hql + λl I)−1

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical formulation with closed-form solution that naturally extends GPTQ
  • Evaluated across three architecture families (ViT, encoder-decoder LM, decoder-only LLM) and multiple merging strategies
  • Anchor ablation (collapse to ~5%) powerfully demonstrates necessity of both components
  • Plug-and-play compatibility with existing PTQ (GPTQ, AWQ, RTN) and merging methods
  • Code available for reproducibility
✗

Limitations & Open Questions

  • Requires retaining all K source expert models during calibration — storage and compute scale linearly with K
  • LLM improvements are modest (1–2%), suggesting diminishing returns at larger scales
  • The λ hyperparameter needs tuning, though ablation shows stability across a reasonable range
  • No exploration of compressed expert representations to reduce overhead
  • Not tested on 70B+ models or merging more than 20 tasks
  • No comparison with QAT-based approaches or other quantization-aware merging strategies
