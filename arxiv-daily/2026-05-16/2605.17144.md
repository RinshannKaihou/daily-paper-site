> **一句话总结：** 本文提出COAST方法，利用conceptor（一种线性子空间算子）从少量成功/失败rollout中提取对比子空间，在推理时对VLA模型的残差流进行乘法门控，在无需训练的情况下将仿真和真实机器人的任务成功率分别提升20%和40%以上。

> **TL;DR:** COAST is a training-free, inference-time steering method for frozen VLA robot policies. It uses Boolean conceptor algebra to identify success-critical subspaces from as few as 15 rollout episodes (success and failure), then applies a multiplicative gate on the action expert's residual stream. Validated across three architecturally distinct policies (flow-matching VLA, autoregressive VLA, Diffusion Policy) on three simulation benchmarks and real robots, it achieves +20% simulation and +40% real-world success rate improvements, consistently outperforming CAA, SAE steering, and supervised fine-tuning baselines. Key mechanistic insight: failure representations share substantial cross-task geometry while success representations are task-specific, enabling zero-shot conceptor transfer between tasks with similar failure modes.

## 核心贡献

- **无需训练的策略引导：** 首次将conceptor布尔代数应用于VLA action expert的残差流，通过闭式解（无需梯度计算）构建乘法门控矩阵，仅用15条rollout即可提取对比子空间
- **跨架构通用性：** 在flow-matching VLA（pi0.5）、自回归VLA（pi0-FAST）、Diffusion Policy三种架构，以及MetaWorld/LIBERO/RoboCasa三个仿真基准和DROID真实机器人上均验证有效
- **机理洞察：** 发现VLA的action expert中，任务相关的表征占据低秩但非一秩的子空间（解释了为什么conceptor优于单方向加法方法）；成功-失败子空间重叠度可预测引导效果（rho=0.59）
- **跨任务迁移：** 失败表征在不同任务间共享大量几何结构，而成功表征则因任务而异；利用共享的失败几何结构，无需重新拟合即可将conceptor迁移到新任务上

## Key Contributions

- **Lightweight training-free policy steering via conceptor Boolean algebra:** Constructs a contrastive conceptor C_steer = C_success AND NOT C_failure from rollout activations. The resulting multiplicative gate M = (1-beta)*I + beta*C_steer is applied to the action expert's residual stream at a single layer during inference, requiring no weight updates or gradient computation.
- **Cross-architecture validation:** Demonstrated on pi0.5 (flow-matching VLA with PaLI-Gemma 2 backbone), pi0-FAST (autoregressive VLA), GR00T N1.5 (NVIDIA DiT-based VLA), and Diffusion Policy -- establishing that the steerable latent geometry is architecture-independent.
- **Mechanistic analysis of subspace structure:** The eigenvalue spectra of success/failure conceptors decay rapidly (~1% of hidden dimension is discriminative), but the discriminative subspace is strictly multi-dimensional (rank-one CAA captures only ~half the gain). Success-failure overlap strongly predicts per-task steering efficacy.
- **Cross-task conceptor transfer via shared failure geometry:** Failure subspace containment between source/target pairs correlates with transfer gain (r=0.30--0.49, p<0.005), while success containment shows no such relationship.

## Methodology

COAST operates in two phases:

**Offline fitting:** Collect N rollout episodes (as few as 15) from the frozen policy on a target task. Extract residual-stream activations from the action expert at a selected layer. Fit separate conceptors for success (C+) and failure (C-) activations. A conceptor C = R(R + alpha^{-2} I)^{-1} captures the principal subspace of activation covariance R. Compose them via Boolean algebra: C_steer = C+ AND NOT C- = (C+^{-1} + (I - C-)^{-1} - I)^{-1}. This retains directions present in success but absent from failure.

**Inference-time steering:** Apply the contrastive conceptor as a multiplicative gate: M = (1-beta)*I + beta*C_steer. At each denoising step, the residual activation h at layer ell is replaced by h' = h M^T. The identity interpolation ensures soft projection, with beta controlling steering strength.

Three strategies are evaluated: (1) Global -- single conceptor across all denoising steps; (2) Per-step -- separate conceptor per denoising step; (3) Positive-only -- C_steer = C_success without failure subtraction.

An efficient hyperparameter selection heuristic uses geometric diagnostics (conceptor quota for layer selection, success-failure overlap for aperture selection) to recover 93% of oracle performance while evaluating only 3-8% of the full configuration grid.

## Results & Highlights

**Simulation results (Table 1):**
- pi0.5 MetaWorld ML45: 0.57 -> 0.82 mean SR (+0.25, p<.001), with pick-place-wall recovering from 0.20 to 1.00
- pi0.5 LIBERO-10: 0.43 -> 0.80 mean SR (+0.37, p<.001), largest gains on long-horizon tasks
- pi0-FAST LIBERO-10: 0.65 -> 0.84 (+0.23, p<.001)
- GR00T N1.5 RoboCasa: 0.59 -> 0.75 (+0.16, p=.006)
- Diffusion Policy RoboCasa: 0.32 -> 0.46 (+0.14)

**Real-world results (DROID platform):** pi0.5+COAST achieves ~40% average improvement across Open Drawer, Close Microwave, and Put Duck in Cabinet tasks, with Close Microwave improved using only a single successful rollout.

**Baseline comparisons:** COAST consistently outperforms CAA (+0.02 to +0.04 gains), SAE steering (+0.04 to +0.17), and SFT with LoRA (-0.16 to +0.05, often negative due to overfitting with 15 rollouts). Linear ablation recovers only ~half the contrastive gain; random-direction conceptor matches the unsteered baseline, ruling out generic low-rank perturbation as the cause.

**Cross-task transfer:** Transferred conceptors retain most self-steered gains, and in several cases exceed them (e.g., TwoMokas conceptor achieves +0.67 on SoupCheese vs. +0.20 self-steered).

**Robustness:** Steering improvements are consistent across training epochs (300-1500), network layers (0-11), and hyperparameter choices. Positive-only steering never regresses below baseline on high-SR tasks. Failure-only steering (without any success examples) lifts 3/4 zero-baseline tasks to nonzero success.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The core idea -- adapting conceptor algebra (Jaeger 2014) from recurrent networks / LLMs to VLA action experts -- is a meaningful but incremental extension. The Boolean AND-NOT composition and multiplicative gating follow directly from prior conceptor literature (Postmus & Abreu 2024, 2025). The genuinely novel contributions are: (1) the insight that VLA failure modes are the primary bottleneck (not the VLM backbone), (2) the discovery that failure geometry transfers across tasks, and (3) the geometric hyperparameter selection heuristic.

**Rigor:** The experimental evaluation is unusually thorough for a systems paper. Three architectures, three benchmarks, real-robot validation, ablations (linear, random, failure-only, positive-only), cross-task transfer, robustness across checkpoints/layers/hyperparameters, statistical significance tests (paired t-tests and z-tests), and latency benchmarks. The 15-rollout / 30-test-rollout split with strict seed separation is well-designed. However, some concerns remain:
- The early checkpoints used for MetaWorld/LIBERO are chosen specifically to produce mixed success/failure rates, which is where COAST is most effective. The method helps less on well-trained policies (the paper acknowledges this).
- The real-robot evaluation covers only 3 tasks with 15 trials each -- a limited but reasonable initial validation.
- The Diffusion Policy results are weaker than VLA results, suggesting VLM pretraining contributes to steerable geometry.

**Reproducibility:** Excellent. Complete pseudocode, hyperparameter grids, checkpoint paths, and activation extraction details are in the appendices. Pre-collected datasets are on HuggingFace. Code is promised at submission time.

**Significance:** If the findings generalize, this is a practically important result: a closed-form, training-free method that can meaningfully improve robot policy performance at near-zero cost. The mechanistic insights about failure geometry sharing could inspire new approaches to multi-task robot learning.

**Red flags:**
- The ~24% inference latency overhead from the forward hook (Table 24) applies to all hook-based methods equally, but is not negligible for real-time control.
- COAST requires at least 3 success and 3 failure rollouts per task. For entirely novel tasks with zero baseline, only failure-only steering (which is much weaker) or positive-only steering (which requires demonstrations) applies.
- The hyperparameter selection heuristic was validated only on the architectures tested; whether it generalizes remains open (the authors acknowledge this).

## Strengths

- Comprehensive evaluation across diverse architectures, benchmarks, and real hardware
- Strong mechanistic analysis connecting subspace geometry to steering efficacy
- Practically useful: training-free, requires minimal data (15 rollouts), negligible per-step overhead
- Clean ablation study isolating the contributions of multiplicative gating vs. contrastive subspace vs. specific directions
- The cross-task transfer finding (shared failure geometry) is both surprising and practically valuable
- Efficient hyperparameter selection via geometric diagnostics (93% oracle recovery at 3-8% of grid)

## Limitations

- Requires both success and failure rollouts; uniformly successful tasks fall back to positive-only (weaker) or cannot use contrastive steering at all
- Tasks with nearly disjoint success/failure subspaces show small gains (as predicted by overlap-efficacy relationship)
- Per-architecture hyperparameter selection may not generalize beyond tested architectures
- Real-robot evaluation is limited (3 tasks, 15 trials each)
- Diffusion Policy benefits less than VLA models, suggesting VLM pretraining contributes to steerable geometry
- The steering hook adds ~24% inference latency (same as baselines, but non-negligible for real-time control)
- Evaluation uses early checkpoints for some benchmarks (MetaWorld, LIBERO) specifically chosen for mixed success/failure rates; benefits are smaller on fully-trained policies

## Relevance

该论文与机器人学习和策略优化方向高度相关。COAST提出的推理时激活引导方法为VLA模型在部署时的快速适配提供了一种无需训练的新范式，其发现的不同任务间共享失败几何结构的洞察可能对多任务机器人学习和迁移学习研究有启发意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does COAST interact with online learning / continual learning? Could conceptors be updated incrementally as new rollout data arrives?
- Can the shared failure geometry insight be exploited at training time (e.g., via regularization) rather than only at inference?
- How does COAST scale to higher-dimensional action spaces and longer-horizon tasks beyond those tested?
- What is the theoretical relationship between conceptor-based steering and optimal control / policy distillation?
- Could conceptors be composed hierarchically for multi-stage tasks (e.g., sub-task conceptors + a meta-conceptor)?
- Does the geometric hyperparameter selection heuristic generalize to other model families (e.g., large Diffusion Transformers for image generation)?
