> **一句话总结：** 本文发现LLM推理时生成的token级别置信度轨迹蕴含与最终答案正确性相关的"置信度几何"结构；基于此提出的NeuralConf（1D CNN轻量估计器）仅从置信度序列即可预测trace正确性，在DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B上的GSM8K/MATH/MMLU基准中，以128条trace预算实现答案聚合准确率0.9407/0.9493/0.8240，均优于多数投票和TailConf等基线。

> **TL;DR:** The authors show that token-level confidence trajectories from LLM reasoning encode a measurable "confidence geometry" that separates correct from incorrect traces -- using only the 1D confidence sequence, with no access to questions, reasoning text, hidden states, or verifiers. They propose NeuralConf, a lightweight 1D CNN readout trained on binary correctness labels, which learns this geometry and produces trace-level correctness scores. On GSM8K, MATH, and MMLU (using DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B, 128 traces/question), NeuralConf improves aggregation accuracy to 94.1%, 94.9%, and 82.4% respectively, consistently outperforming majority voting, TailConf, and Bottom-10Conf. The signal is concentrated in the tail of reasoning and strengthens with longer observed trajectory segments. ReClor experiments reveal scale-dependence: the 1.5B model shows only chance-level signal, while 7B recovers meaningful structure.

## 核心贡献

- 发现"置信度几何"（confidence geometry）现象：仅用token级别置信度轨迹的一维序列，不依赖问题文本、推理内容、隐藏状态或外部验证器，即可在低维表示空间中分离正确与错误推理路径。
- 揭示正确性信号在推理轨迹中不均匀分布，尾部（late-stage）的置信度动态携带最关键的正确性信息；通过滑动窗口和头-尾对齐对照实验进行了严格验证。
- 提出NeuralConf，一个基于1D CNN + 残差块的轻量序列读出模型，仅从置信度轨迹学习trace级别正确性评分，在固定128条trace预算下答案聚合准确率优于所有基线。
- 在ReClor上发现信号恢复具有尺度依赖性：1.5B模型信号微弱，7B模型可恢复有意义的结构，表明置信度几何现象的适用范围与模型能力和任务难度相关。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies "confidence geometry" -- a content-agnostic, measurable geometric structure in token-level confidence trajectories that correlates with trace-level final-answer correctness, requiring no access to questions, reasoning text, hidden states, or external verifiers.
- Demonstrates that correctness-related information is unevenly distributed along the trajectory: late-stage confidence dynamics carry the strongest signals, confirmed via sliding-window analysis and matched head-vs-tail comparisons.
- Proposes NeuralConf, a lightweight 1D CNN encoder with residual blocks that maps confidence trajectories to trace-level correctness scores, trained with class-weighted binary cross-entropy on question-level splits.
- Shows scale-dependent recoverability on ReClor: the signal is near chance at 1.5B but becomes substantially recoverable at 7B, indicating the phenomenon depends on model capability and task difficulty.

## Methodology

**Confidence trajectory construction:** At each decoding position, a token confidence score is computed following the DeepConf formulation: c_i = -(1/k) * sum_{j=1}^{k} log P_i(j), where k=20 and P_i(j) is the probability of the j-th highest-probability token among top-k candidates. This measures distributional peakedness (not the NLL of the sampled token). Larger c_i indicates higher confidence.

**Tail-aligned input:** Variable-length confidence trajectories are converted to fixed-size tail-aligned segments of length L_max. Trajectories longer than L_max retain only the final L_max values; shorter ones are left-padded with a binary mask. L_max is swept over {4, 8, 16, 32, 64, 128, 256, 512, 1024, 2048}.

**NeuralConf architecture:** A compact 1D convolutional encoder with residual blocks produces a trace embedding h(tau), followed by a small prediction head that maps to a scalar score p_theta(tau) in (0,1). Training uses class-weighted binary cross-entropy. All splits are at the question level (not trace level) to prevent leakage.

**Baselines:** TailConf (mean confidence of final tokens), Bottom-10Conf (mean of bottom-10% confidence values per group), and majority voting. All use the same token-level confidence definition.

**Analysis tools:** Davies-Bouldin Index (DBI) for representation-level cluster separation (lower = better), AUC for trace-level discrimination, and aggregation accuracy for downstream functional testing.

**Backbone model:** DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B for main experiments. DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B and 7B for ReClor scale analysis. K=128 traces per question sampled.

## Results & Highlights

- **Table I (Aggregation accuracy, K=128):** NeuralConf achieves 0.9407 (GSM8K), 0.9493 (MATH), 0.8240 (MMLU), outperforming majority voting (0.9333, 0.9467, 0.8167), TailConf-90% (0.9367, 0.9467, 0.8200), and Bottom-10Conf-90% (0.9367, 0.9467, 0.8133). Gains are modest but consistent across all benchmarks.
- **Learned representation separates correct/incorrect traces:** UMAP visualizations show NeuralConf embeddings produce clearer separation than raw confidence trajectories. DBI consistently lower in learned space.
- **Longer tail segments reveal more structure:** DBI decreases and AUC increases as L_max grows from 4 to 2048, indicating correctness-related information is cumulative and sequence-dependent, not captured by short summaries.
- **Signal concentrated at reasoning tail:** Sliding-window analysis (window=64, stride=32) shows windows closer to the end of the trajectory yield lower DBI and higher AUC. Matched head-vs-tail comparison at equal lengths confirms tail alignment is superior.
- **NeuralConf consistently outperforms TailConf across all lengths:** Not just at short lengths -- the advantage persists even at L_max=2048, suggesting the relevant signal is distributed across the trajectory in a way that fixed summaries cannot fully capture.
- **ReClor scale-dependence:** At 1.5B, both NeuralConf and TailConf are near chance (AUC ~0.52-0.54). At 7B, NeuralConf maintains consistently higher AUC across input lengths while TailConf is strongly length-sensitive. This reveals the phenomenon is not universal but depends on model capability and task difficulty.
- **Bottom-10Conf grouping-length sensitivity:** Non-monotonic AUC as grouping length varies; even at optimal settings, remains below NeuralConf at L_max=2048.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The core insight -- that token-level confidence trajectories alone encode correctness-related geometric structure without any semantic input -- is genuinely novel. Prior work (DeepConf, self-consistency) collapses confidence into fixed summaries or requires content access. The paper shows the signal is richer than fixed summaries suggest.

- **Rigor: Moderate-to-High.** The experimental design is careful: question-level splits prevent leakage, sliding-window and matched head-vs-tail analyses isolate positional effects from length effects, and multiple complementary metrics (DBI, AUC, aggregation accuracy) are used. However, significant concerns remain: (a) Only one backbone model family (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen) is used for the main experiments. The 1.5B backbone is a relatively small model, and it is unclear whether the findings generalize to larger or different-architecture models. (b) The aggregation gains are modest (0.7-0.8 percentage points over majority voting), and the authors acknowledge this honestly but attribute it to the coarseness of aggregation as an endpoint. (c) The paper does not compare against process reward models, outcome reward models, or other learned verifiers -- only against confidence-based baselines. This is by design (content-agnostic constraint) but limits practical significance claims. (d) The 1,200-question subsets are relatively small; scaling behavior is untested.

- **Reproducibility: High.** Code is available. The method uses only standard API-accessible confidence scores (top-k token probabilities). The 1D CNN architecture is lightweight. Training requires only binary correctness labels.

- **Significance: Moderate.** The conceptual contribution (confidence geometry as a phenomenon) is significant and opens a new angle on LLM reasoning reliability. However, the practical impact on aggregation accuracy is limited. The ReClor results showing scale-dependence are important for calibrating expectations: the signal is not universally strong. The paper's honest framing of these limitations is commendable.

- **Red flags:** (1) Single model family for main results limits generalizability claims. (2) The aggregation improvements, while consistent, are small enough that in practice the added complexity of training NeuralConf may not justify the gains over simple majority voting with more traces. (3) The token confidence definition (top-k entropy-like measure) is inherited from DeepConf without ablation on the choice of k=20 or alternative confidence metrics. (4) The MMLU subset is restricted to STEM subjects only, not the full benchmark.

## Strengths

- Elegant experimental isolation: the content-agnostic constraint forces a clean test of whether confidence alone carries correctness information.
- Thorough positional analysis: sliding-window, matched head-vs-tail, and length sweep provide multiple complementary perspectives on where the signal lives.
- Honest assessment of limitations: the authors clearly acknowledge the modest aggregation gains and the scale-dependence on ReClor.
- Clean methodological design: question-level splits, multiple metrics, five random seeds for NeuralConf, and comparison against the most relevant baselines (DeepConf-style).
- The ReClor boundary-case analysis adds nuance and shows the authors are not overclaiming universality.

## Limitations

- **Single backbone model for main results:** DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B is a distilled, relatively small model. Whether confidence geometry holds for larger models, non-distilled models, or closed-source models (GPT-4, Claude) is unknown.
- **Modest practical gains:** 0.7-0.8 percentage point improvement over majority voting may not justify the training overhead in practice, especially when simply sampling more traces could achieve similar gains.
- **No comparison to learned verifiers or reward models:** The content-agnostic design precludes comparison with ORM/PRM methods, making it hard to assess the practical value proposition relative to the most competitive alternatives.
- **Restricted benchmarks:** MMLU is limited to STEM subjects; all benchmarks are structured (math, multiple-choice). Behavior on open-ended generation, creative tasks, or code generation is untested.
- **Confidence definition dependence:** The top-k distributional peakedness measure (k=20) is not ablated. Different confidence metrics or values of k could yield different results.
- **Training data requirement:** NeuralConf requires labeled correct/incorrect traces for training, which limits applicability to settings where ground truth is unavailable.

## Relevance

本研究与LLM推理可靠性评估和推理时扩展（inference-time scaling）方向高度相关。利用置信度几何进行答案聚合提供了一种低成本、无需外部工具的自适应推理策略，对于提升多采样推理系统的效率具有直接参考价值。ReClor上的尺度依赖性发现也为理解不同规模模型的推理可靠性提供了新视角。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does confidence geometry hold across different LLM families (Llama, GPT, Claude, Gemini) and model sizes beyond 1.5B/7B? Is it stronger or weaker for non-distilled models?
- Can NeuralConf scores be combined with process reward models or self-consistency for compound gains in answer aggregation?
- What is the minimum training set size (number of labeled traces) needed for NeuralConf to achieve reliable discrimination?
- Does the tail-concentration finding generalize to agentic/multi-step reasoning scenarios where the "answer" is not at the end of generation?
- Would improving model calibration (e.g., via temperature scaling or conformal prediction) strengthen or weaken the confidence geometry signal?
- How does the choice of k=20 in the confidence metric affect results? Is there an optimal k, or does it interact with model architecture?
- The ReClor results suggest the phenomenon is scale-dependent -- is there a critical model scale threshold at which confidence geometry becomes reliably informative?
