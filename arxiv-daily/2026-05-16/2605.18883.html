2605.18883 Prediction Is Not Physics
Index Source .md arXiv Code
Headline Finding

A diffusion model achieves rollout MSE near 10-3 but its energy standard deviation is 36,000x larger than ground truth — prediction accuracy and physical conservation are orthogonal axes of model quality.

Bukowski, Kothari, Shi & Rao · Yale University · 2026 · Course project, CPSC 4520
Anchor Metric
36,000×
energy std ratio vs. ground truth
DDPM with soft energy-guided projection on spring-mass system. Even post-hoc correction cannot close the gap. The range across all three systems: 7,500–36,000x.
Why This Matters
Neural simulators can look numerically accurate while silently violating fundamental conservation laws — a critical failure mode for scientific computing, robotics, and physics-informed design.
Low MSE does not certify physics fidelity. Anyone deploying generative models for physical simulation must audit conservation separately.
Critical Edge
The CDN alignment loss requires ground-truth energy at t=0 — you need to know the answer to find the answer.
This undermines the "discovery" framing. Without alignment (λalign=0), CDN R² collapses below 10-3 on pendulum/spring-mass. Temporal consistency alone is underdetermined.

Four Models Benchmarked
1. SE Network
Structured T(v)+V(q) with separate kinetic/potential sub-networks. Architectural prior matches test systems.
2. Black-box CDN
Unconstrained NN with temporal consistency loss + variance hinge + optional alignment at t=0.
3. Polynomial CDN
Same loss but conserved quantity parametrized as polynomial+trig function. Short vs. long schedule.
4. Conditional DDPM
3-layer MLP (dim 256), sinusoidal embeddings. Learns Δs conditioned on state. Optional soft energy projection.
Hamiltonian Test Systems
Projectile H = ½(u² + v²) + 9.81y
Pendulum H = ½ω² − 9.81 cosθ
Spring-Mass H = ½v² + ½kx²
All three have known analytical energy expressions, enabling direct R² evaluation of learned conserved quantities.
Key Findings
F1 — The Fidelity Gap
Diffusion models achieve low prediction error but catastrophically violate energy conservation, even with soft energy-guided projection during sampling.
Local denoising objectives and post-hoc correction are fundamentally insufficient for conservation. The gap is not a tuning problem.
F2 — Underdetermined Discovery
Pure temporal consistency (λalign=0) converges to spurious invariants, not the physical Hamiltonian.
Many scalars are constant along a trajectory. Without at least a weak anchor to ground truth at t=0, the CDN has no reason to find the "right" one.
F3 — Optimization over Expressivity
Polynomial CDN on short schedule learns only the kinetic energy term (ω²), missing cosθ entirely.
Long schedule (2x epochs, 50x data) recovers R²=0.9998. Same result with/without noise — training regime is the operative variable, not model capacity.
F4 — Noise Robustness Reversal
Under 1% Gaussian noise, black-box CDN outperforms the structured SE network on 2 of 3 systems.
SE's separate T(v)+V(q) fitting is brittle with noisy inputs. CDN's unconstrained architecture may better absorb perturbations. Single seed, preliminary.

Strengths
  • Clean ablation design — Tables tell a coherent, interpretable story
  • Honest self-assessment — Transparent about single-seed limitation, oracle nature of STLSQ, preliminary noise results
  • Polynomial CDN insight — Kinetic-only partial solution on short schedule reveals the conservation loss landscape structure
  • Clear thesis — Prediction accuracy and physical conservation are distinct axes of model quality
Limitations
  • Single seed — Statistical significance of all results is uncertain
  • SE R² is partly circular — T(v)+V(q) prior exactly matches all three test systems
  • No HNN/symplectic baselines — Missing the most direct comparison class
  • CDN needs ground truth at t=0 — Undermines "discovery" framing
  • Only 3 low-dimensional systems — No chaotic or high-dimensional test cases
