> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 E-PMQ 框架，通过利用源专家模型权重的 Fisher 信息矩阵构建专家引导校准目标，并结合合并权重锚定正则项防止校准漂移，系统解决了模型合并后直接量化导致性能严重下降的问题。锚定消融实验显示去除锚定会导致准确率从约 68% 崩溃至约 5%，验证了两个组件的必要性。

> **TL;DR:** Directly applying post-training quantization (PTQ) to a merged model is unreliable because quantization deviation and expert-relative merging deviation are coupled. E-PMQ formulates a per-layer quadratic objective that combines expert-guided reconstruction targets (using per-expert Hessian matrices from source models) with a merged-weight anchoring regularizer (Frobenius-norm penalty on deviation from merged weights). The closed-form solution feeds into a GPTQ-style sequential rounding procedure. On CLIP-ViT-B/32 8-task 4-bit merging, E-PMQ boosts GPTQ from 65.0% to 73.6% (Task Arithmetic) and from 69.1% to 74.8% (TIES-Merging). On 20-task CLIP-ViT-L/14, it recovers from a catastrophic 34.8% to 76.7%. Ablation shows that removing the anchor causes accuracy to collapse from ~68% to ~5%, proving both components are essential.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统性地提出 Post-Merge Quantization (PMQ) 问题设定，通过理论分析揭示模型合并后直接量化失败的根因：量化偏差与合并偏差在低比特重建中被耦合放大。
- 提出 E-PMQ 框架，构建逐层二次优化目标，利用源专家模型的 Hessian 矩阵提供专家引导的输出重建目标，有效解耦两类偏差。
- 引入合并权重锚定 (Merged-Weight Anchoring) 正则项，通过 Frobenius 范数约束量化权重偏离合并权重的程度，同时使 Hessian 矩阵正定可逆。消融实验证实去除锚定导致准确率从约 68% 崩溃至约 5%。
- 方法与现有 PTQ 方法 (GPTQ, AWQ, RTN) 和合并方法 (Task Arithmetic, TIES-Merging) 即插即用兼容，并扩展验证到 Llama-3.1-3B/8B 大语言模型。

## Key Contributions

- Formulates Post-Merge Quantization (PMQ) as a distinct problem setting, providing theoretical analysis showing that quantization deviation and expert-relative merging deviation are coupled when PTQ is naively applied to a merged model, leading to amplified error accumulation.
- Proposes E-PMQ, which constructs a per-layer quadratic optimization objective: the expert-guided term uses per-expert Hessian matrices (H_i^l) from source models as curvature-weighted reconstruction targets, while the anchor term adds a Frobenius-norm regularizer on deviation from merged weights with strength lambda^l.
- Derives a closed-form continuous relaxation of the quantized weights: Q_cont^l = (sum W_i^l H_i^l + lambda^l W_m^l)(H_q^l + lambda^l I)^{-1}, where the anchor term also ensures positive definiteness of the Hessian. This feeds into a GPTQ-style sequential rounding solver (Algorithm 1).
- Demonstrates that merged-weight anchoring is not merely a tuning detail but a critical stabilizer: removing the anchor (alpha=0) causes accuracy to collapse from ~68% to ~5% on CLIP-ViT-B/32 8-task.
- Validates across CLIP-ViT-B/32, CLIP-ViT-L/14 (up to 20 tasks), FLAN-T5-base GLUE, and Llama-3.1-3B/8B, showing consistent improvements under both Task Arithmetic and TIES-Merging.

## Methodology

E-PMQ addresses the PMQ problem through a unified per-layer quadratic objective with two complementary components:

**1. Expert-Guided Output Targets:** For each layer l, E-PMQ uses the source expert weights {W_i^l} and their corresponding per-expert Hessian matrices {H_i^l} (computed from calibration data passed through each expert independently). The expert-guided term minimizes sum_i Tr[(Q^l - W_i^l) H_i^l (Q^l - W_i^l)^T], where the per-expert Hessians weight the reconstruction error according to each expert's output sensitivity. This provides richer calibration targets than reconstructing the merged model alone.

**2. Merged-Weight Anchoring:** A Frobenius-norm regularizer lambda^l ||Q^l - W_m^l||_F^2 is added, where W_m^l is the merged weight at layer l. This serves two purposes: (a) it prevents the calibration from drifting too far from the merged model's integrated behavior, and (b) it adds diagonal loading (lambda^l I) to the effective Hessian, making it positive definite and the inverse well-defined.

**3. Optimization Procedure:** The combined quadratic form is minimized in closed form for the continuous relaxation, then fed into a GPTQ-style sequential rounding procedure (Algorithm 1). The effective curvature is H_E-PMQ^l = H_q^l + lambda^l I, and the right-hand side is R_E-PMQ^l = sum_i W_i^l H_i^l + lambda^l W_m^l.

**4. Compatibility:** The framework is plug-and-play with existing PTQ backbones (GPTQ, AWQ, RTN) and model merging methods (Task Arithmetic, TIES-Merging, Simple Averaging, WUDI).

## Results & Highlights

**CLIP-ViT-B/32 (8 tasks, 4-bit):**
- Task Arithmetic: GPTQ 65.0% -> E-PMQ 73.6% (+8.6%)
- TIES-Merging: GPTQ 69.1% -> E-PMQ 74.8% (+5.7%)

**CLIP-ViT-L/14 (20 tasks, 4-bit):**
- GPTQ 34.8% -> E-PMQ 76.7% (+41.9%), demonstrating recovery from catastrophic failure of naive PTQ

**FLAN-T5-base GLUE (4-bit):**
- Task Arithmetic: GPTQ 78.26% -> E-PMQ 83.34% (+5.08%)
- TIES-Merging: 79.98% -> E-PMQ 83.48% (+3.50%)
- Notable per-task gains on MNLI: 55.99% -> 82.50% (Task Arithmetic), 65.87% -> 82.48% (TIES-Merging)

**Llama-3.1 (4-bit, Task Arithmetic):**
- 3B: GPTQ 58.71% -> E-PMQ 60.27% (+1.56%)
- 8B: GPTQ 61.66% -> E-PMQ 62.91% (+1.25%)

**Ablation - Anchor is critical:**
- Removing anchor (alpha=0): accuracy collapses from 68.00% to 5.37% (Task Arithmetic) and 72.20% to 4.57% (TIES-Merging)
- Performance remains stable across a reasonable range of positive alpha values

**Bit-width analysis:** E-PMQ consistently outperforms GPTQ from 3-bit to 8-bit, with largest gains at the most aggressive low-bit regimes (3-4 bit).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The PMQ problem formulation is a genuine and timely contribution -- the insight that merged models are unreliable quantization targets is well-motivated. The per-layer quadratic formulation combining expert Hessians with anchor regularization is elegant and principled. The approach naturally extends GPTQ rather than reinventing quantization.
- **Rigor:** The paper includes comprehensive ablation studies (anchor ablation showing catastrophic collapse without anchoring, bit-width analysis from 3-8 bit, per-task breakdowns). The anchor ablation is particularly convincing -- the 5% accuracy without anchoring proves both components are essential. However, there is no ablation isolating expert-guided targets alone (without per-expert Hessians, using just activations).
- **Reproducibility:** Code is publicly available. The method is well-specified with clear mathematical derivations. The reliance on per-expert Hessian computation is the main practical barrier.
- **Significance:** The 41.9% gain on 20-task CLIP-ViT-L/14 is dramatic, though the extremely low baseline (34.8%) is partly an artifact of naive PTQ catastrophically failing. The Llama-3.1 gains (1-2%) are modest but consistent, suggesting diminishing returns at larger scale. The FLAN-T5 MNLI gains (27% improvement) are remarkable.
- **Red flags:** (1) The requirement to retain all K source expert models during calibration and compute per-expert Hessians introduces significant storage and compute overhead when K is large -- this is acknowledged but not quantified. (2) The LLM gains are modest (1-2%), raising questions about whether the overhead is justified at scale. (3) The catastrophic 34.8% baseline on 20-task 4-bit is a genuine failure mode but also makes the improvement look more dramatic than it may be in practice. (4) No comparison with QAT-based approaches or other quantization-aware merging strategies.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical formulation with closed-form solution that naturally extends GPTQ.
- Comprehensive evaluation across three architecture families (ViT, encoder-decoder LM, decoder-only LLM) and multiple merging strategies.
- Ablation studies are thorough and convincing -- the anchor ablation (collapse to ~5%) powerfully demonstrates the necessity of both components.
- Bit-width analysis shows consistent gains from 3-bit to 8-bit.
- Plug-and-play compatibility with existing PTQ and merging methods.
- Code available for reproducibility.

## Limitations

- Requires retaining all K source expert models and computing per-expert Hessian matrices during calibration. Storage and compute overhead scale linearly with K, which may be impractical for very large expert sets or large models.
- LLM improvements are modest (1-2% on Llama-3.1), suggesting the method may have diminishing returns at larger scales where merged models are already better quantization targets.
- The lambda (anchor strength) hyperparameter needs tuning, though the ablation shows stability across a reasonable range.
- No exploration of approximating expert guidance with fewer than K experts or with compressed expert representations.
- The method has not been tested on very large models (70B+ parameters) or on merging more than 20 tasks.

## Relevance

本文研究模型合并后的量化问题，与高效模型部署和多任务学习直接相关。E-PMQ 的逐层二次优化框架为实际场景中合并多个专家模型并进行低比特部署提供了理论清晰且即插即用的解决方案，尤其适合需要同时整合多个任务能力并部署在资源受限设备上的应用场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can expert guidance be approximated using a compressed representation of source experts (e.g., low-rank factors) rather than retaining full expert models?
- How does calibration cost scale with K (number of experts) and model size? Is there a practical upper limit?
- Why are LLM gains (1-2%) so much smaller than CLIP gains (5-42%)? Is this due to architecture, task diversity, or merging method characteristics?
- Can E-PMQ be combined with quantization-aware training (QAT) for further gains after the initial PTQ calibration?
- How does the optimal anchor strength lambda relate to the number of merged tasks, model size, and bit-width?
- Would the approach work for merging heterogeneous models (different architectures or initialization seeds)?
