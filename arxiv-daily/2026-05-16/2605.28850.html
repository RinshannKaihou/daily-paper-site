Research Diagnostics — Not Investment Evidence

Representation Signatures & Risk-Feedback Alignment in LLM Trading Agents

LLM planning representations exhibit measurable pre-failure signatures — effective-rank contraction in 97% of rolling failure anchors — and LLMs systematically fail to internalize pairwise covariance when constructing portfolios, assigning combined weight 1.6 to GOOGL/GOOG (corr. 0.994) before the risk layer clips it below 0.11.

Correlation Blind Spot — 51-Stock Intraday

Both frontier models load correlated assets as if independent. The risk layer intervenes massively. GPT-5.5: 2,924 clipped decisions. Gemini 3.1 Pro: 1,200. Markowitz baseline: 357.

TradeArena Architecture
1Separate observation from decision — market data and agent reasoning flow through distinct stages.
2Risk as first-class stage — pre-trade, in-trade, and post-trade reports with structured feedback.
3Execution as environment response — delays, partial fills, rejections, slippage, commission, participation limits.
4Replayable trajectories — every stage logged to shared audit trail for deterministic replay.
Six design goals: modularity, reproducibility, execution realism, risk awareness, auditability, agent-native evaluation. Deterministic synthetic data by default; real-data adapters pluggable without changing the logging contract.
Risk Feedback — Alignment Tax

Three feedback conditions tested: true (actual counts), hidden (removed), placebo (counterfactual values). Plus a contrarian audit probe injecting false severe reports.

True feedback Higher calibration (Gemini)
Placebo Sometimes higher short-term returns
Contrarian Pushes unjustified conservatism
Alignment tax: Benign placebo feedback can make agents sound more risk-aware while adversarial false audits push toward unjustified conservatism. Structured risk feedback acts as an alignment signal without fine-tuning.
+ Strengths
  • +Exceptionally honest framing — "benchmark diagnostics, not investment evidence"

  • +Four embedding probes + lexical controls + noise injection + CoT-free ablation

− Limitations
  • −No deep RL baseline (PPO, DQN, FinRL) — Markowitz answers a different question
  • −White-box probe is a Qwen2.5-0.5B surrogate, not frontier model activations
  • −51-stock LLM probes: only 2 models over a single 40-hour week
  • −Poe-mediated access introduces routing confounds acknowledged but uncontrolled
  • −Pre-failure signatures' operational value (detection latency, min trajectory length) unquantified
Open Questions for Further Exploration
  1. What is the minimum trajectory length before effective-rank contraction becomes reliably detectable for real-time intervention?
  2. Would explicit covariance information in the prompt (rolling correlation matrix) mitigate the correlation blind spot, or is the failure deeper?
  3. How do signatures change in multi-agent markets where each agent's trades affect prices others observe?
  4. Can pre-failure diagnostics be adapted into a lightweight production monitor without full embedding recomputation?
  5. Do the same signatures appear in non-financial sequential decision-making (navigation, clinical planning)?
  6. How sensitive are findings to risk-gate thresholds — does the blind spot worsen or improve with looser constraints?
