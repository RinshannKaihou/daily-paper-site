> **一句话总结：** sREPA 在 REPA 的逐点特征对齐基础上引入基于 Gram 矩阵的结构化约束（MSE 或 KL 散度变体），使扩散 Transformer 的特征图在 token 间关系几何上与 DINOv2 教师对齐，在 SiT-XL/2 上以 400K 迭代达到 FID 7.17（超越 REPA 的 7.9 和 iREPA 的 7.52），且吞吐量仅下降 1.77%。

> **TL;DR:** sREPA augments point-wise representation alignment (REPA) with explicit pairwise token relational geometry matching via Gram matrix regression (MSE) or distribution alignment (KL divergence), using off-diagonal cosine similarities from L2-normalized features. On ImageNet 256x256 with SiT-XL/2, sREPA achieves FID 7.17 at 400K iterations vs. REPA's 7.9 and iREPA's 7.52, matches vanilla SiT trained for 7M steps (FID 8.3), and with CFG achieves FID 1.91 at 200 epochs -- all with only 1.77% throughput overhead.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 sREPA 框架，在 REPA 逐点对齐的基础上增加显式结构化对齐目标，基于 Gram 矩阵（token 间成对余弦相似度）的 off-diagonal 元素进行回归
- 提供两种变体：sREPA-MSE（直接回归相似度矩阵）和 sREPA-KL（将关系分布视为概率分布，用 KL 散度匹配），MSE 变体略优
- 通过 similarity map 可视化证明：纯逐点对齐产生的特征图对比度低、注意力分散，加入结构化约束后产生更集中、更有意义的相似度模式
- 在 SiT-B/2, SiT-L/2, SiT-XL/2 三个规模上均验证了有效性，吞吐量仅下降 1.77%，GPU 显存增加仅 0.16%

## Key Contributions

- Proposes sREPA, a structural representation alignment framework that enforces consistency in pairwise token relational geometry (off-diagonal Gram matrix elements) rather than point-wise feature matching alone
- Introduces two complementary variants: sREPA-MSE (direct Gram matrix regression, parameter-free) and sREPA-KL (distribution matching via softmax-normalized similarities with temperature control)
- Provides motivating evidence via similarity map visualization showing point-wise alignment produces diffuse, low-contrast attention patterns while structural supervision yields concentrated, meaningful similarity maps
- Demonstrates consistent improvements across SiT-B/2, SiT-L/2, SiT-XL/2 model scales with only 1.77% throughput reduction and 0.16% memory overhead at 256x256

## Methodology

sREPA operates on the SiT (Scalable interpolant Transformer) framework with flow matching. The method has three components:

1. **Point-wise alignment (from REPA):** Minimizes negative cosine similarity between projected student hidden states and teacher (DINOv2) features at corresponding spatial locations: L_proj = (1/N) * sum(1 - cos_sim(g_theta(h_i^S), h_i^T)).

2. **Structural alignment (new):** After L2-normalizing both teacher and mapped student features (Z^T, Z^S), computes pairwise cosine similarity matrices S^T and S^S (Gram matrices). Diagonal elements are masked to prevent trivial self-matching solutions. Two variants:
   - **sREPA-MSE:** Directly minimizes MSE between off-diagonal similarity values: L_struc = (1/(N(N-1))) * sum_{i!=j} ||S_ij^T - S_ij^S||^2. Parameter-free.
   - **sREPA-KL:** Applies softmax to off-diagonal similarities to form probability distributions per token, then minimizes KL divergence: L_struc = (1/N) * sum_i sum_{j!=i} p_ij^T * log(p_ij^T / p_ij^S), with temperature parameters tau_s = tau_t = 0.2.

3. **Training objective:** L_total = L_flow + lambda_proj * L_proj + lambda_struc * L_struc. Optimal weights found via grid search: for MSE variant (lambda_proj=1.0, lambda_struc=2.0); for KL variant (lambda_proj=1.0, lambda_struc=0.5).

The total training is conducted on ImageNet with Stable Diffusion VAE encoding, AdamW optimizer, batch size 256, on 8 NVIDIA H200 GPUs.

## Results & Highlights

**Without CFG (ImageNet 256x256):**
- SiT-B/2 at 400K: sREPA FID 20.5 vs. REPA 24.4 (baseline 33.0)
- SiT-L/2 at 400K: sREPA FID 8.73 vs. REPA 9.7 (baseline 18.8)
- SiT-XL/2 at 400K: sREPA FID 7.17 vs. REPA 7.9 vs. iREPA 7.52; surpasses vanilla SiT-XL/2 trained for 7M steps (FID 8.3)
- SiT-XL/2 at 1M: sREPA FID 6.15 vs. REPA 6.4

**With CFG (ImageNet 256x256):**
- SiT-XL/2 + sREPA at 200 epochs: FID 1.91, sFID 4.50, IS 271.8
- SiT-XL/2 + REPA at 80 epochs: FID 2.39; at 200 epochs: FID 2.06
- SiT-XL/2 vanilla at 80 epochs: FID 2.06

**ImageNet 512x512:**
- SiT-XL/2 + sREPA at 100 epochs: FID 2.29 vs. REPA 2.32; IS 260.3 vs. 255.7

**Overhead:** Throughput drops only 1.77% (2044 -> 2008 img/s at 256x256), memory increases 0.16% (27.3 -> 27.3 GB). At 512x512: 2.05% throughput drop, 2.30% memory increase.

**Ablation insights:**
- Structural loss alone without point-wise alignment fails to improve (point-wise alignment is a necessary "semantic anchor")
- MSE variant slightly outperforms KL overall
- Increasing point-wise alignment strength in REPA alone yields only marginal gains (FID 17.4 -> 17.2 at 1M); sREPA with structural supervision achieves FID 16.5
- sREPA generalizes across alignment depths (layers 4, 6, 8) and diverse vision encoders

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate. The core idea -- replacing point-wise matching with Gram matrix (pairwise similarity) alignment -- is a clean and well-motivated extension of REPA. However, Gram matrix / structural similarity losses have a long history in neural style transfer (Gatys et al., 2015) and knowledge distillation (e.g., CKA-based alignment, relational knowledge distillation). The contribution is primarily in identifying and validating this specific formulation for DiT alignment, not in inventing the structural loss itself. The paper acknowledges this lineage implicitly but could be more explicit about the relationship to prior structural losses.

- **Rigor:** Generally strong. The paper includes extensive ablations: loss variants (MSE vs. KL), temperature analysis, loss weight sweeps, alignment depth studies, different teacher encoders, compatibility experiments with iREPA and REG, point-wise alignment strength controls, and overhead analysis. The point-wise strength ablation (Table 10) is a particularly convincing control showing the gains are not from stronger point-wise supervision alone.

- **Reproducibility:** Good. Algorithm 1 provides PyTorch-style pseudocode with explicit formulas. Hyperparameters are tabulated. However, code is not yet released at time of writing.

- **Significance:** Solid. The FID improvements are consistent and meaningful: ~0.7-4.0 FID points improvement over REPA depending on model scale and training length. The training acceleration claim is well-supported (400K sREPA surpasses 7M vanilla SiT). The negligible overhead makes adoption practical.

- **Red flags:** (1) The CFG results in Table 2 show sREPA at 200 epochs (FID 1.91) vs. vanilla SiT at 80 epochs (FID 2.06) -- the comparison is not at equal compute, which somewhat weakens the "acceleration" narrative for CFG settings. (2) The 512x512 improvements are quite marginal (FID 2.29 vs 2.32). (3) The quadratic complexity of pairwise matching is acknowledged as a limitation for higher resolutions and video, but the paper does not explore efficient alternatives. (4) All experiments are on ImageNet class-conditional generation only; no text-to-image or larger-scale evaluation.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean motivation: follows logically from iREPA's finding that spatial structure drives REPA's effectiveness
- Simple, parameter-free MSE variant with minimal implementation overhead (just Gram matrix computation)
- Comprehensive ablation studies with well-designed control experiments (especially Table 10 on point-wise alignment strength)
- Negligible computational overhead (1.77% throughput drop) makes it practically adoptable
- Consistent improvements across all model scales (B, L, XL) and training durations (100K to 1M iterations)
- Plug-and-play compatibility with other methods (iREPA, REG) demonstrated

## Limitations

- Quadratic complexity in token count for pairwise similarity computation; will become a bottleneck for high-resolution or video generation
- Only evaluated on ImageNet class-conditional generation (256x256 and 512x512); no text-to-image or video experiments
- Marginal improvements at 512x512 resolution suggest diminishing returns at higher resolutions
- MSE variant outperforms KL, but no theoretical justification for why
- The "structural alignment" without point-wise alignment fails, indicating the structural loss alone is insufficient -- the contribution is complementary rather than replacement
- Due to computational constraints, the largest model experiments are limited to SiT-XL/2 (675M parameters)

## Relevance

本文对扩散 Transformer 训练加速中的表征对齐提出了结构化补充，直接继承 REPA 和 iREPA 的研究路线，对于从事高效扩散模型训练、表征蒸馏或视觉生成模型加速的研究者具有直接参考价值。方法的低开销和即插即用特性也使其易于集成到现有训练流程中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the quadratic complexity of Gram matrix computation be reduced via local/windowed structural matching without losing effectiveness?
- How does sREPA interact with other training acceleration techniques (e.g., progressive training, mixed-resolution training, distillation)?
- Would sREPA be effective for text-to-image or text-to-video DiTs where spatial structure is more complex and variable?
- Is there a theoretical explanation for why MSE outperforms KL for structural alignment in this context?
- Can the structural alignment signal be used as an unsupervised metric for training progress or representation quality during DiT training?
