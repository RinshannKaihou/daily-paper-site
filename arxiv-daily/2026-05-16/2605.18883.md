> **一句话总结：** 本文系统评估了神经网络模拟器在三个哈密顿系统中学习守恒量（如能量）的能力，发现扩散模型虽预测精度高（MSE ~10^{-3}）但能量标准差比真实值高3–4个数量级，结构化T(v)+V(q)能量模型能精确拟合解析能量（$R^2 \geq 0.9999$），而纯时间一致性损失不足以发现真实守恒量——需要至少少量对齐损失（$\lambda_{\text{align}}=0.2$）作为锚点，否则CDN的$R^2$在钟摆和弹簧系统上降至$<10^{-3}$。

> **TL;DR:** A DDPM diffusion model trained on Hamiltonian trajectories achieves rollout MSE near $10^{-3}$ but has energy standard deviation 7,500–36,000x larger than ground truth—even when conditioned on a learned invariant and softly projected toward a learned energy level set. This paper benchmarks four approaches (structured $T(v)+V(q)$ energy network, black-box CDN, polynomial CDN, conditional diffusion) on projectile, pendulum, and spring-mass systems. The structured model reaches $R^2 \geq 0.9999$ on clean data; the black-box CDN needs $\lambda_{\text{align}}=0.2$ to reach $R^2 \geq 0.996$. Without alignment ($\lambda_{\text{align}}=0$), CDN $R^2$ collapses to $<10^{-3}$ on pendulum/spring-mass, proving temporal consistency alone is underdetermined. Under 1% noise, CDN outperforms structured model on two of three systems. Polynomial CDN is optimization-limited: $R^2=0.78$ on short schedule vs. 0.9998 on long schedule, with identical results with/without noise—training regime dominates model capacity.

## 核心贡献

- 量化了扩散模型预测精度与能量守恒之间的鸿沟：即使使用学习的不变量进行条件生成和软投影校正，能量标准差仍比真实值高7,500–36,000倍
- 系统对比了四种守恒量学习方法在三个哈密顿系统上的表现，提供了清晰的基准数据（Table 1–5）
- 揭示了守恒目标的欠定性问题：纯时间一致性损失无法唯一确定物理哈密顿量，需要$t=0$处的弱对齐信号
- 发现多项式CDN的瓶颈在于优化而非表达能力：短训练(256 ep/20K轨迹)下$R^2=0.78$，长训练(512 ep/1M轨迹)下$R^2=0.9998$，且有无噪声结果一致
- 在1%高斯噪声下，黑盒CDN在抛体和弹簧系统上超越结构化模型，提示无约束架构在噪声环境中可能更具鲁棒性

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies the fundamental gap between prediction accuracy and physics fidelity: DDPM achieves MSE ~$10^{-3}$ but energy std is 7,500–36,000x ground truth, even with soft energy-guided projection during sampling
- Provides a systematic benchmark (Tables 1–5) of four conservation-learning approaches across three Hamiltonian systems with clear ablations
- Demonstrates the underdetermination of pure temporal consistency: with $\lambda_{\text{align}}=0$, the CDN converges to spurious conserved scalars ($R^2 < 10^{-3}$) rather than the physical Hamiltonian
- Identifies optimization—not expressivity—as the binding constraint for polynomial CDNs: short schedule captures only kinetic energy ($\omega^2$), missing the potential ($\cos\theta$) term; long schedule recovers full Hamiltonian
- Shows CDN outperforms structured $T(v)+V(q)$ model under 1% noise on projectile and spring-mass, suggesting architecture flexibility aids noise robustness

## Methodology

**Systems:** Three classical Hamiltonian systems with known analytical energy:
- Projectile motion: $H = \frac{1}{2}(u^2 + v^2) + 9.81y$
- Pendulum: $H = \frac{1}{2}\omega^2 - 9.81\cos\theta$
- Spring-mass: $H = \frac{1}{2}v^2 + \frac{1}{2}kx^2$

**Models:**
1. **Structured Energy (SE) Network:** Parametrizes $H = T(v) + V(q)$ with separate sub-networks for kinetic and potential energy. Architectural prior exactly matches the test systems.
2. **Black-box CDN:** Unconstrained neural network trained with temporal consistency loss ($\text{Var}[f(s,\tau)]$ across trajectory) plus variance hinge (prevents constant output) and optional standardized alignment loss at $t=0$.
3. **Polynomial CDN:** Same training objective as CDN but with conserved quantity parametrized as a polynomial-plus-trigonometric function. Uses short schedule (256 epochs, 20K trajectories) and long schedule (512 epochs, 1M trajectories).
4. **Conditional Diffusion (DDPM):** Three-hidden-layer MLP (dim 256) with sinusoidal timestep embeddings. Learns $\Delta s = s_{t+1} - s_t$ conditioned on current state. Autoregressive rollout with optional soft energy projection: $s \leftarrow s - (H_{\text{SE}}(s) - H_0)\frac{\nabla H_{\text{SE}}(s)}{\|\nabla H_{\text{SE}}(s)\|^2 + \epsilon}$.

**Evaluation:** $R^2$ (squared Pearson correlation, affine-invariant), Spearman rank correlation, rollout MSE, energy standard deviation ratio. All computed on held-out trajectories.

**Additional tool:** STLSQ sparse regression over polynomial-plus-trigonometric feature library, using analytical energy as oracle target (not unsupervised discovery—validates expressivity of basis).

**Table 1 — Noise-free trajectories ($R^2$ / Spearman):**

| CDN-Aligned ($\lambda_{\text{align}}=0.2$) | 0.997 / 0.998 | 0.997 / 0.9999 | 0.996 / 0.9997 |
| Polynomial (short) | 0.996 / 0.996 | 0.781 / 0.930 | 0.956 / 0.986 |
| SE Network | 0.9999 / 0.9999 | 0.9999 / 0.9999 | 0.9999 / 1.0000 |

**Table 2 — No-alignment ablation ($\lambda_{\text{align}}=0$, Pearson $R^2$):**

| Model | Projectile | Pendulum | Spring-Mass |
|-------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| CDN-Conservation | 0.711 | $5.6 \times 10^{-4}$ | $2.0 \times 10^{-4}$ |

**Table 3 — Polynomial schedule sensitivity on pendulum:**

| Schedule | Epochs / Data | $\sigma=0$ | $\sigma=0.01$ |
|----------|--------------|-----------|--------------|
| Short | 256 ep / 20K trajs | 0.781 | 0.781 |
| Long | 512 ep / 1M trajs | 0.9998 | 0.9998 |

**Key findings:**
- Diffusion model with soft energy projection still has energy std 7,500–36,000x ground truth (Table 5)—local denoising objectives and soft guidance are insufficient for conservation
- SE network's $R^2 \geq 0.9999$ is partly tautological: the $T(v)+V(q)$ prior exactly matches all three test systems
- CDN $\lambda_{\text{align}}=0$ collapse reveals many temporally-conserved scalars exist; only one is the physical Hamiltonian
- Short-schedule polynomial learns only kinetic energy ($\omega^2$), missing $\cos\theta$ potential term—a partial Hamiltonian proxy
- Long schedule eliminates the gap entirely, with identical results with/without noise—training regime is the operative variable
- Under 1% noise, CDN outperforms SE on projectile and spring-mass; SE's separate $T(v)+V(q)$ fitting is more brittle with noisy inputs

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate. The prediction-vs-conservation gap in neural simulators is well-known (Greydanus et al. 2019 HNN already showed this). The systematic quantification of the diffusion model's energy std ratio (7,500–36,000x) is a clean empirical contribution. The CDN alignment ablation is the most novel finding—demonstrating that temporal consistency is underdetermined for conservation discovery.
- **Rigor:** Mixed. The ablations are well-designed and the tables tell a clear story. However, all experiments use a single seed per condition—the authors acknowledge this limitation. The $\lambda_{\text{align}}=0$ collapse is convincing but would benefit from multiple seeds. The noise robustness finding (CDN > SE under 1% noise) is described as "preliminary" by the authors themselves.
- **Reproducibility:** High. Simple systems, standard architectures, code publicly available. The polynomial CDN schedule sensitivity is straightforward to verify.
- **Significance:** The core message is important but not surprising to the physics-informed ML community. The paper's main value is as a clear empirical benchmark rather than a methodological breakthrough. As a course project, it is impressively thorough.
- **Red flags:** (1) SE network's $R^2 \geq 0.9999$ is somewhat circular—the architectural prior matches the test systems exactly. (2) No comparison to HNNs (Greydanus et al. 2019) or symplectic integrators, which directly address the conservation problem. (3) STLSQ symbolic regression uses oracle targets—not a genuine discovery experiment, though the authors are transparent about this. (4) The CDN's requirement for alignment at $t=0$ with analytical energy fundamentally limits it as a "discovery" tool—you need to know the answer to find the answer.

## Strengths

- Clean experimental design with informative ablation tables that tell a coherent story
- Honest self-assessment: authors transparently note the single-seed limitation, the oracle nature of STLSQ, and the preliminary status of noise results
- The polynomial CDN optimization analysis (kinetic-only partial solution on short schedule) provides genuine insight into the conservation loss landscape
- Well-written discussion connecting findings to HNNs, symplectic integrators, and the broader physics-informed ML landscape
- Clear articulation of the central thesis: prediction accuracy and physical conservation are distinct axes of model quality

## Limitations

- Single seed per condition—statistical significance of all results is uncertain
- Only three simple, low-dimensional Hamiltonian systems; no complex, chaotic, or high-dimensional test cases
- No comparison to established physics-informed baselines (HNNs, Lagrangian NNs, Symplectic ODE Nets)
- CDN alignment loss requires ground-truth energy at $t=0$, undermining the "discovery" framing
- 1% noise is a single noise level; no systematic noise sweep
- The DDPM baseline uses only soft post-hoc energy projection; stronger energy-guided sampling (e.g., energy-based DDIM) is left as future work
- As a course project, the scope is inherently limited—both in resources and depth of investigation

## Relevance

与神经物理模拟器和物理信息机器学习直接相关。核心发现——高预测精度不等于物理一致性，以及守恒量发现的欠定性问题——对评估和改进基于学习的物理模拟方法（包括扩散模型用于科学计算）具有重要参考价值。扩散模型虽能生成低MSE轨迹但严重违反守恒律这一发现，对将生成模型应用于物理系统的研究者尤为重要。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the CDN framework discover conservation laws in non-energy-conserving systems (momentum, angular momentum, or unknown invariants in complex systems)?
- How does the alignment threshold scale—what is the minimal signal ($\lambda_{\text{align}}$) needed, and can symmetry-based priors replace point-wise ground-truth alignment?
- Would HNNs or symplectic integrators achieve both low MSE and low energy drift on the same systems, providing a stronger baseline?
- Does the CDN noise robustness finding hold across multiple seeds, noise levels, and higher-dimensional systems?
- Can the CDN-learned invariant be fed into a symbolic regression pipeline (CDN-SINDy) to recover closed-form conservation laws, as the authors suggest as future work?
- How do results change for chaotic Hamiltonian systems (e.g., double pendulum, Henon-Heiles) where trajectory-level prediction itself is fundamentally limited?
