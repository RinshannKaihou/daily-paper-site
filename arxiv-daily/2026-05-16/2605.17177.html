High-dimensional Limit of SGD for Diagonal Linear Networks

Begona Garcia Malaxechebarria Courtney Paquette Maryam Fazel Dmitriy Drusvyatskiy

In high dimensions, SGD on diagonal linear networks loses its randomness — risk, curvature, and estimation error converge to trajectories governed by a deterministic PDE, with errors vanishing as O(d−ε).

Convergence

Each SGD step maps to continuous time via k = floor(t·d). The resolvent-based concentration argument shows both SGD and the SDE solve the same PDE system. Stability of that PDE yields the deterministic limit.

Theorem 3.7 — Main Concentration
For any admissible statistic φ, sup0≤t≤T |φ(xfloor(td)) − φ(t)| ≤ C · d−ε with overwhelming probability. C is independent of dimension.

Core Contributions

  • SDE approximation (rigorous). Streaming SGD uniformly tracks a continuous-time "homogenized SDE" for any admissible statistic, even at fixed (large) stepsizes.
  • Deterministic PDE limit. A partial integro-differential equation propagates all observable statistics. SGD randomness becomes negligible in high dimensions.
  • Exponential convergence. Under squared parametrization (u²−v²), risk decays as Ce−μt with probability ≥ 1−δ.
  • Composite polynomial-resolvent statistics. Framework covers risk, scaled Hessian trace (sharpness), squared norms, and estimation error — not just the loss.

Curvature Dynamics

The framework captures the "flat progress" phenomenon: risk increases sharply while curvature (scaled Hessian trace) drops. This connects directly to progressive sharpening and edge-of-stability dynamics observed in deep learning. Numerical validation confirms that the homogenized SDE tracks SGD curvature more accurately than classical stochastic gradient flow, especially at larger stepsizes.

Limitations

  • Diagonal covariance required. Resolvent commutativity depends on K being diagonal. Real data rarely satisfies this; extending beyond it is a fundamental barrier.
  • Gaussian data only. Conditional moment calculations rely on Gaussianity. Extension to light-tailed distributions is mentioned but not pursued.
  • Streaming SGD only. Fresh samples at each iteration — not the multi-pass or finite-dataset regimes used in practice.
  • Narrow model class. Diagonal linear networks are the simplest nonlinear departure from linearity. Far from practical architectures.
  • Implicit stepsize constant. The threshold for exponential convergence is not given an explicit value, reducing practical guidance.

Critical Edge

Fundamental Scope Limit

The diagonal covariance assumption is not a convenience — it is load-bearing. The resolvent commutativity that enables PDE closure collapses without it. This excludes essentially all real-world data distributions and represents the boundary of what this technique can currently reach.

Open Questions

  1. Can resolvent-based PDE closure extend to non-diagonal K via free probability?
  2. What does φ(t) predict near the edge-of-stability stepsize threshold?
  3. Does the deterministic limit recover known implicit bias results for gradient flow?
  4. Does the deterministic description survive in the multi-pass regime?
  5. What happens with non-quadratic inner functions ψ (deeper networks)?
