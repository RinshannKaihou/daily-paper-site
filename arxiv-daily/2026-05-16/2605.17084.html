Scale determines whether language models organize their internal geometry for prediction — small models mask it, large models preserve it.
Scale Determines Whether Language Models Organize Representation Geometry for Prediction Weilun Xu 2026-05-16
representation-geometry scaling-laws mechanistic-interpretability

Key Metric

z = −32
Late-layer alignment collapse in Pythia-410M, versus peak z = 9–24 at mid-layers across all models

Why This Matters

Scale changes how models organize representations, not just how well — with direct implications for when mechanistic-interpretability tools like the logit lens will succeed or fail, and how to select optimal intermediate layers for downstream tasks.

Critical Edge

No causal mechanistic evidence — the paper explicitly acknowledges this; the geometric pattern is observed, not causally intervened upon, and the proposed causal test (rotating PC1 back into Vk) is left to future work.

Methodology

SVD on WU → Top-k=100 singular vectors (readout subspace Vk) → Project hidden states onto Vk → Mantel test vs. 100 random Grassmannian subspaces → Report z-score
  • Models: 7 Pythia scales (70M–6.9B) + OLMo-1B, Phi-1.5, Gemma-2B for cross-family validation
  • Data: 1,000 OpenWebText contexts (≥64 tokens, truncated to 512), identical across models
  • Anisotropy correction: Mean-centering + CCR-1; CCR-5/10 used diagnostically to test masking hypothesis
  • Training dynamics: 143k steps across Pythia-160M, 410M, 1B checkpoints
  • Cross-model RSA: Compares readout-subspace vs. full-space representational similarity across architectures
  • Downstream probing: Linear probes for AG News on per-layer hidden states
  • Compute: ~60–80 A100 GPU-hours

Results

Finding Detail
Mid-layer peak z = 9–24 at intermediate layers for all models with ≥12 layers
Scale phase transition Sharp regime shift at d ≈ 2048: negative z (small) → positive z (large)
Small-model collapse Pythia-410M reaches z = −32; Pythia-160M collapses at 65–95% relative depth
Masking, not destruction CCR-5 restores +z for all d ≥ 768
Collapse timing Emerges ~step 96k (compression-seeking phase); invisible in validation loss
Probing correlation z vs. AG News accuracy: rho = 0.69, p = 0.002 (Pythia-1B)
Cross-architecture RSA 100-dim readout RSA exceeds full 2048-dim RSA in multiple model pairs
Spectral metrics 7 metrics fail to separate regimes

Strengths

  • Principled metric design — Grassmannian random subspaces with Mantel permutation test provide a rigorous null hypothesis
  • Systematic scale coverage — 7 Pythia scales plus 3 cross-family models, spanning tied and untied embeddings
  • Elegant masking diagnostic — CCR correction cleanly separates collapse from erasure
  • Temporal evidence — 143k-step training dynamics show the detour is learned, not architectural
  • Practical utility — z-scores enable task-free layer selection for downstream probing
  • Memorable framing — the Direct / Detour regime distinction is intuitive and communicable
  • Honest about scope — limitations clearly stated (metric scope, CCR sensitivity, causality)

Limitations

  • Single-task probing — only AG News; does not establish z as a general layer-selection signal
  • Scale threshold generalization — d ≈ 2048 observed primarily in Pythia; cross-family models are at similar widths
  • No causal evidence — geometric patterns observed, not causally intervened upon; the definitive test is deferred
  • Linear readout assumed — nonlinear routing (tuned lens) not captured
  • Architecture scope — SSMs, MoEs, and encoder-decoder models excluded
  • LayerNorm confound — final-layer states post-LayerNorm; normalization effect on metric not analyzed
  • Single author — limited breadth of peer feedback prior to this preprint

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the Direct / Detour regime distinction predict differences in few-shot learning, in-context reasoning, or other emergent capabilities at scale?
  2. Can the scale threshold (d ≈ 2048) be shifted through architectural choices — wider-shallower models, different normalization, mixture-of-experts?
  3. What computational function does the dominant direction migrating away from readout serve? Can it be linked to specific circuits or features?
  4. Would a causal intervention (rotating PC1 back into Vk at collapse-zone layers) restore logit lens interpretability without degrading loss?
  5. How does Subspace PGA behave during fine-tuning, RLHF, or other post-training alignment — does the Direct regime persist?
  6. Does the cross-architecture convergence in readout directions extend to multilingual or multimodal models?
