> **一句话总结：** CAR-SAM针对SAM解码器中交叉注意力结构的量化问题，提出MatMul感知补偿（MAC）缓解注意力消散，以及联合交叉注意力重建（JCAR）抑制双向耦合引起的振荡，在W4A4量化下比现有方法在SAM-B/SAM-L上分别提升14.6%/6.6% mAP。

> **TL;DR:** CAR-SAM identifies two key failure modes in low-bit post-training quantization of SAM's decoder -- attention dissipation (cross-attention maps collapse under 4-bit quantization) and reconstruction oscillation (bidirectional coupling between image-token and token-image cross-attention branches causes unstable convergence). The proposed solution combines MatMul-Aware Compensation (MAC), which transfers activation quantization errors from attention MatMul operations back to preceding linear projection weights via closed-form solutions, and Joint Cross-Attention Reconstruction (JCAR), which jointly optimizes both cross-attention branches with theoretical grounding (Theorem 3.1 on coupled error propagation). Experiments across SAM and SAM2 families show CAR-SAM achieves stable 4-bit quantization, surpassing PTQ4SAM by 14.6% mAP on SAM-B and 6.6% on SAM-L at W4A4, while delivering ~64% storage reduction and ~4.1x compute speedup.

## 核心贡献

- 识别了SAM解码器在低位量化中的两大问题：**注意力消散**（attention dissipation）和**重建振荡**（reconstruction oscillation），这是之前PTQ方法未考虑的交叉注意力结构特有问题。
- 提出**MatMul感知补偿（MAC）**机制，通过推导QK^T和AV矩阵乘法的重建目标函数，将激活量化误差转移到前级线性权重上，使用闭式解（涉及Sylvester方程）进行求解。
- 提出**联合交叉注意力重建（JCAR）**策略，基于Theorem 3.1理论分析双向交叉注意力的耦合误差传播机制，证明联合重建比独立重建更稳定。
- 在SAM和SAM2全系列模型上验证了W4A4量化的有效性，覆盖实例分割、目标检测和视频物体分割三个下游任务。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies two novel failure modes specific to SAM's decoder cross-attention architecture under low-bit PTQ: (1) attention dissipation where cross-attention maps lose semantic focus, and (2) reconstruction oscillation from bidirectional coupling between token-to-image and image-to-token attention branches.
- Proposes MatMul-Aware Compensation (MAC) that formulates reconstruction objectives for QK^T and AV MatMul operations explicitly, deriving closed-form compensation for Q, K, and V projection weights via ridge-regression-style solutions.
- Proposes Joint Cross-Attention Reconstruction (JCAR) with theoretical justification (Theorem 3.1) showing that coupled error propagation between bidirectional cross-attention modules causes oscillation, and that joint reconstruction yields more stable optimization.
- Comprehensive evaluation across SAM (B/L/H) and SAM2 (T/S/B+/L) on COCO instance segmentation, COCO object detection, and DAVIS video object segmentation.

## Methodology

CAR-SAM operates as a post-training quantization framework with two core components:

**1. MatMul-Aware Compensation (MAC):** Standard PTQ methods (GPTQ, BRECQ, QDrop) only compensate for quantization errors in linear projection layers, ignoring the MatMul operations in attention computation. MAC explicitly formulates reconstruction objectives for the QK^T and AV MatMul operations. For the QK pathway, it parameterizes quantized Q and K with learnable weight perturbations on their projection weights, minimizing ||Q_hat * K_hat^T - Q * K^T||^2. The optimization reduces to a Sylvester equation, solved via SVD-based regularization. The same approach extends to the V projection pathway, completing compensation for all [Q, K, V] branches.

**2. Joint Cross-Attention Reconstruction (JCAR):** The SAM decoder contains bidirectional cross-attention: token-to-image (f) and image-to-token (g). The authors prove via Theorem 3.1 that quantization errors propagate through a coupled system: Delta_I' = J_g^{(T')}(J_f^{(T)} * Delta_T + J_f^{(I)} * Delta_I) + J_g^{(I)} * Delta_I, showing hierarchical and cross-branch feedback terms. This theoretical analysis demonstrates that independent per-block reconstruction causes oscillation, motivating joint reconstruction of both cross-attention modules as a single optimization unit.

The framework combines MAC for weight compensation with JCAR for stable block-level reconstruction, requiring only a small unlabeled calibration dataset.

## Results & Highlights

- **COCO Instance Segmentation (SAM):** At W4A4, CAR-SAM achieves 39.3 mAP on SAM-B (+14.6 over QDrop's 26.4, +14.6 over PTQ4SAM's 24.7), 48.5 mAP on SAM-L (+10.5 over QDrop, +6.6 over PTQ4SAM), and 51.6 mAP on SAM-H (+2.8 over QDrop, +0.9 over PTQ4SAM).
- **COCO Instance Segmentation (SAM2):** CAR-SAM achieves 40.4 mAP on SAM2-T, 43.2 on SAM2-S, 46.5 on SAM2-B+, and 38.2 on SAM2-L at W4A4, consistently competitive or superior to QDrop and PTQ4SAM.
- **Object Detection (SAM):** At W4A4, CAR-SAM reaches 60.6 mAP on SAM-B (+9.8 over PTQ4SAM), and 66.3 on SAM-H (+2.3 over PTQ4SAM).
- **Video Object Segmentation (DAVIS):** J&F scores on SAM2 variants show graceful degradation at W4A4 compared to baselines.
- **Efficiency:** W4A4 quantization yields ~64% storage reduction and ~4.1x median compute speedup across all SAM/SAM2 variants.
- **Ablation:** Both MAC and JCAR contribute positively; JCAR (joint cross-attention reconstruction granularity) yields the highest mAP compared to finer-grained alternatives.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: Moderate-high.** The identification of attention dissipation and reconstruction oscillation as SAM-specific failure modes is genuinely novel. The theoretical analysis (Theorem 3.1) of coupled error propagation in bidirectional cross-attention is a meaningful contribution. However, the MAC mechanism is conceptually similar to prior weight compensation methods (GPTAQ, ERQ), with the main extension being application to MatMul operations rather than just linear projections.

**Rigor: Strong.** The paper provides formal theorem statements with complete proofs in the appendix. The ablation study isolates the contributions of MAC and JCAR. Experiments cover multiple SAM/SAM2 variants, three downstream tasks, and multiple bit-width settings.

**Reproducibility: Good.** The method uses standard PTQ calibration setups. Pseudocode is provided in Appendix D. The closed-form solutions (Sylvester equation solving, SVD-based regularization) are well-specified. However, no code repository link is mentioned.

**Significance: Moderate.** The practical impact is clear for edge deployment of SAM models. The 4-bit quantization results are substantial improvements. However, the absolute mAP numbers at W4A4 (39.3 on SAM-B) still represent significant degradation from full precision (53.3), limiting real-world usability at 4-bit.

**Red flags:**
- On SAM2-L at W4A4, CAR-SAM achieves 38.2 mAP, which is actually lower than PTQ4SAM's 38.8, suggesting the method may not consistently outperform on the largest SAM2 variant.
- Some table entries show CAR-SAM matches PTQ4SAM exactly at W6A6 (e.g., SAM-B W6A6 = 53.3 for both), raising the question of whether W6A6 improvements are meaningful.
- The lambda regularization parameter uses an empirical energy threshold (t=0.1) without sensitivity analysis.
- No comparison with mixed-precision methods like Mix-QSAM under the same conditions (Mix-QSAM is discussed but not directly compared in tables).

## Strengths

- Clean problem identification with insightful analysis of SAM decoder's unique cross-attention challenges.
- Strong theoretical grounding with formal proofs for both the error propagation analysis and the compensation formulations.
- Comprehensive evaluation across SAM and SAM2 families, multiple tasks, and multiple bit-widths.
- Practical efficiency gains (~64% storage reduction, ~4.1x speedup) with no retraining required.
- Qualitative visualizations (attention heatmaps) effectively illustrate the attention dissipation problem.

## Limitations

- No code release mentioned, which may hinder reproducibility.
- The method does not consistently outperform PTQ4SAM on all model variants (e.g., SAM2-L W4A4 for segmentation, SAM-L W4A4 for detection where CAR-SAM shows "-").
- Absolute performance at 4-bit remains far from full precision, limiting practical deployment scenarios.
- The calibration set size and computational cost of the PTQ process itself are not discussed in detail.
- No evaluation on more recent SAM variants or other foundation models with similar cross-attention decoders.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the calibration dataset size and composition affect MAC and JCAR performance? Is there a minimum calibration set requirement?
- Can the MAC compensation formulation be extended to grouped-query attention or multi-head attention with different head dimensions?
- Does the coupled error propagation analysis (Theorem 3.1) generalize to other architectures with bidirectional attention, such as multimodal models (e.g., LLaVA, BLIP)?
- What is the computational overhead of the JCAR joint reconstruction compared to independent block-wise reconstruction during the PTQ calibration phase?
- Could combining CAR-SAM with mixed-precision strategies (assigning higher bits to the decoder) yield further improvements?
