PARALLAX: Separating Genuine Hallucination Detection from Benchmark Construction Artifacts

4 of 6 widely-used hallucination detection benchmarks embed ground-truth answers in the input prompt via teacher-forcing. A naive text-similarity baseline hits AUROC 0.98 without any model internals. Most published methods collapse to near-chance under controlled conditions.

Key Findings
  • Teacher-forcing inflates AUROC by up to 0.43 points; DRIFT drops from 0.95 (TF) to 0.62 (live-gen) on HaluEval
  • Methods B and F reach AUROC 1.00 on TF corpora — pure artifact exploitation
  • Approach A (self-supervised, no labels) is the only label-free method exceeding chance on RAGTruth at 0.571
  • Cross-corpus transfer is poor: probes transfer within regime (HaluEval: 0.725) but collapse across regimes
  • Legal corpus: all methods at ~0.50, unexplained outlier
Critical Assessment
Strengths
TxTEMB is a brilliantly simple control; 22×12×6 evaluation is unprecedented; statistical rigor (bootstrap CIs, permutation tests); honest about DRIFT ≈ SAPLMA; sets new evaluation standard
Limitations
Open-weight instruction-tuned only (no GPT-4, Claude); DRIFT requires labels; indistinguishable from simpler SAPLMA; RAGTruth difficulty may reflect benchmark not ceiling; cross-corpus transfer failure unanalyzed; 4-bit quantization may degrade signal
Verdict
Field-resetting. If accepted, years of published hallucination detection results using TF benchmarks need reinterpretation. The TxTEMB control is elegant and devastating. The methodological contribution (DRIFT) is incremental, but the evaluation contribution is the real value.
Open Questions
Why do SAPLMA (single-layer) and DRIFT (multi-layer) achieve statistically identical performance? Is the hallucination signal already captured at one layer?
What makes RAGTruth so much harder than HaluBench? Is it the RAG context, question types, or annotation methodology?
Can poor cross-corpus transfer be addressed through multi-task training, or is the signal fundamentally corpus-specific?
How do findings change with proprietary models where hidden states are inaccessible? What are implications for real-time production monitoring?
