In-context learning is constrained by pretraining representational geometry — high-variance directions are learnable, low-variance directions are not.
Large Language Models Reorganize Representational Geometry During In-Context Learning
Hua-Dong Xiong, Li Ji-An, R. C. Wilson, K. Lee, X.-X. Wei  | 2026-05-16
in-context learning representational geometry mechanistic interpretability prototype learning neural manifolds

Key Metric

r = −0.87
Pearson correlation between intrinsic dimensionality of final-layer representations and ICL accuracy (p < 0.001), across 8 LLMs and 5 datasets.

Why This Matters

Provides a principled geometric framework to predict which tasks are learnable in-context and why ICL fails on others — with actionable implications for prompt design and pretraining objectives.

Critical Edge

The evidence is largely correlational: geometry co-varies with ICL success, but causal proof that untangling drives learning requires intervention experiments the paper does not include.

Methodology

The authors design representation-defined classification tasks: for each sentence, they extract a hidden-state vector from a target layer, project it onto a chosen axis, and binarize the projection into a label. This avoids semantic confounds from natural language labels.

Task-defining axes span a controlled difficulty gradient:

  • Logistic Regression (LR) axis — aligned with pretrained semantic partition
  • Principal components PC1 through PC512 — from highest to lowest variance

Geometric analysis uses manifold capacity theory to track how final-layer representations reorganize during ICL. Four metrics are computed: capacity (separability proxy), radius, intrinsic dimensionality, and signal-to-noise ratio.

Algorithmic comparison fits six online learners (prototype, exemplar, 1-NN, online gradient descent, Bayesian class-mean, Kalman filter) to the same representations and compares their output distributions against the LLM.

Tested on 8 pretrained LLMs, 5 datasets, 5 target layers per model, with 200 runs per condition.

Results

ICL accuracy improves with more examples for all axes, but the rate and ceiling vary dramatically. Leading axes (LR, PC1, PC4, PC8) succeed; trailing axes (PC128, PC512) barely exceed chance.

Geometric Metric Correlation with ICL Acc. Significance
Manifold capacity r = 0.83 p < 0.001
Intrinsic dimensionality r = −0.87 p < 0.001
Manifold radius r = −0.78 p < 0.001
Signal-to-noise ratio r = 0.71 p < 0.001

Among six online learning algorithms, prototype learning (class centroids) best matches the LLM's output distribution. Notably, more flexible algorithms (online gradient descent, Kalman filter) achieve higher accuracy from the same representations, indicating the LLM follows a prototype-like compression strategy rather than being accuracy-optimal.

For "easy" axes, manifold capacity rises while radius and dimensionality shrink as examples increase. For "hard" axes, these geometric shifts are attenuated — representations fail to reorganize toward separability.

Strengths

  • Elegant paradigm that avoids semantic confounds by defining labels from the model's own representation space
  • Systematic sweep: 8 models, 5 datasets, 5 layers each
  • Principled geometric framework grounded in established neuroscience theory (manifold capacity)
  • Strong quantitative correlations (r up to 0.87) between geometry metrics and ICL accuracy
  • Thorough algorithmic comparison (6 models) revealing prototype-like behavior
  • Honest limitations discussion and clear articulation of future directions
  • Unifying language connecting neuroscience, representation learning, and ICL

Limitations

  • Binary linear classification is a narrow slice of what ICL does; generalization to reasoning or generation is unknown
  • Only linear partitions explored; real-world tasks may depend on nonlinear structure invisible to PC/LR axes
  • Evidence is correlational — no causal interventions (e.g., perturbing representations) to confirm untangling drives ICL
  • Prototype-learning match may be task-specific: binary centroid comparison suffices here but may not capture ICL in complex settings
  • Axis choice (PCs, LR) is reasonable but not exhaustive; random or attention-derived directions might reveal different constraints

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can geometric analysis predict ICL difficulty for real-world NLP tasks (sentiment, NLI) by measuring concept-level representational geometry?
  2. Are there identifiable "untangling circuits" in attention heads, analogous to induction heads?
  3. Would causal perturbation of representations (boosting low-variance PCs, projecting out high-variance directions) change ICL success rates?
  4. Can modified pretraining objectives produce representations with better untangling properties, expanding the range of ICL-amenable tasks?
  5. How does the prototype-like update rule emerge mechanistically from transformer attention?
  6. Does the framework extend to chain-of-thought reasoning, where generated tokens provide extra computation for further reorganization?
  7. How do instruction tuning and RLHF reshape the representational geometry that constrains ICL?
