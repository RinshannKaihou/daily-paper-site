Self-Supervised Micro-Gesture Foundation Models

iMiGUE-3K: The Largest In-the-Wild Micro-Gesture Benchmark with Dual-Modality SSL

A 10x-scaled dataset (3,484 videos, 332 subjects) paired with skeleton and RGB foundation model branches closes the gap between unsupervised and supervised micro-gesture recognition — demonstrating that self-supervised pretraining can match or beat fully supervised baselines on a domain long limited by annotation scarcity.

Collected from Grand Slam tennis post-match press conferences over 7 years. Athletes have minimal preparation time, producing involuntary micro-gestures. Subject-independent train/test split (37 train / 38 test subjects) ensures zero overlap. Video-level emotion labels derived from match outcome (win/loss).

M Dual-Modality Architecture

MG-FM-Skele (Skeleton)
  • USDRL dual-stream Dense Spatio-Temporal Encoder
  • Multi-grained feature decorrelation (temporal + spatial + instance)
  • Augmentations: speed jitter, frame dropping, joint corruption, 2D rigid transforms
  • Stronger linear separability; excels in linear probing
MG-FM-RGB (Appearance)
  • SMILE-style teacher-student masked video modeling
  • ViCLIP frozen teacher provides semantic targets
  • Progressive pretraining: Stage I (synthetic motion aug + trajectory masking), Stage II (standard tube masking)
  • Excels after fine-tuning; best single-modality supervised
Modality trade-off: Skeleton branch produces more linearly separable representations (better for linear probing). RGB branch benefits more from fine-tuning (better supervised). This suggests complementary strategies depending on label availability.

? Open Questions

  1. How does the ~60% noise in SSL clips affect learned representation quality? Would stricter filtering help or hurt diversity?
  2. Can MG-FMs transfer to other domains — clinical interviews, job screening, casual conversation?
  3. Would text/speech modalities (e.g., interviewer questions) complement visual-only MG analysis?
  4. Is binary win/loss sufficient for emotion, or should finer valence-arousal annotations be collected?
  5. Does SSL help or hurt rare MG categories (e.g., C14 at 0.04% frequency)?
  6. What is the scaling curve — does 120K clips saturate, or would more data keep helping?

Quick Verdict

Significant dataset contribution that could become the standard MG benchmark. The SSL framework is well-executed but architecturally incremental (adaptations of USDRL + SMILE). The finding that unsupervised skeleton representations match supervised performance is the standout insight.

Novelty: moderate · Rigor: good · Impact: high potential

Strengths

  • 10x dataset scale fills a critical gap for foundation model pretraining in MG
  • Principled clip sampling with empirical validation (IoD analysis, ablations)
  • Subject-independent splits ensure fair, uncontaminated evaluation
  • Five-task benchmark spanning multiple supervision regimes
  • Clear modality-dependent behavior insight for future work

Limitations

  • Single-domain data (tennis press conferences) — limited gestural diversity
  • Severe long-tailed distribution; rare classes (0.04%) may be unevaluable
  • Binary emotion proxy loses granular emotional information
  • SSL corpus ~40% MG occupancy — high noise, not analyzed for downstream impact
  • No cross-dataset transfer experiments reported
  • Architectures are adaptations, not novel designs
