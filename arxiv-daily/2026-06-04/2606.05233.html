Browser ASR 0/140—same weights, coding injection 100%

HEADLINE: CUA-HandCrafted (793 episodes) shows frontier browser agents resist hand-crafted prompt injection in weights—not prompts—while SkillBench on identical checkpoints reaches 100% (Sonnet 4.6) and 79% (GPT-5.4) coding-skill ASR; literature 42–98% ASR largely irreproducible.

Headline claim

Frontier CUA safety is domain-conditioned: browser hardening does not transfer to coding-agent skill-file injection on fixed weights.

Anchor fact
0/140 multi-step ASR · CP 95% UB 2.60% · vs SkillBench 40/40
So what

Do not extrapolate published 42–98% CUA ASR or vendor browser cards to coding agents—report ASR vectors per surface.

Critical edge

Phase 9 approximates unreleased RL strings; Phase 10 ($10) ≠ full GRPO; two frontier models only; legacy DoS 6.5% remains.

0/140 multi-step ASR on Sonnet 4.6 + GPT-5.4 (valid targets). Clopper–Pearson 95% upper bound 2.60% (p < 10⁻¹⁷ vs 25% null). Raw 2/158 on bank_check_balance excluded (ground-truth drift).

8 attack categories × 3 tasks → 0/16 per model. TRAP buttons, WASP P.S., RL-Hammer polite tone, MUZZLE compliance—all fail on capable frontier models.

Advanced DOM: label rewrite, pre-fill, role="alert", input listeners → 0/40. Rules out a11y truncation as sole explanation.

10 literature-informed templates approximating unreleased optimized strings → 0/40. Approximation ≠ original RL strings (limitation).

AutoInject adaptive-random-suffix: Qwen2-1.5B mutations, 5 queries/pair, ~$10 budget → 0/50 per model. Tightens but does not close RL-is-the-gap hypothesis vs full GRPO.

VPI-Bench on same models: popup overlay 1/30 (3.3%); in-content malicious text 5/20 (25%)—image channel partially open.

Practitioner takeaway

  • Headline ASRs cluster on GPT-4o / Sonnet 3.5 + unreleased RL strings
  • 0% on approximations ≠ 0% on original optimized attacks
  • Demand release of optimized strings or attacker models for full fidelity

Critical assessment

Strengths

  • 793-episode public benchmark + Zenodo + full traces
  • Compelling same-weight browser vs coding contrast
  • Rigorous BU/ASR joint reporting + CP bounds
  • Honest reproducibility audit + Phase 10 RL probe

Novelty & significance

  • Fixed-weight cross-surface ASR at frontier checkpoints
  • Separates hand-crafted floor from RL-optimized ceiling
  • High impact for CUA deployment risk & benchmark design

Read before citing

  • Only Sonnet 4.6 + GPT-5.4 in main body—no Gemini / open-weights panel
  • Phase 9 templates approximate unreleased RL-Hammer/WASP/TRAP strings
  • Phase 10 ($10, 5 queries/pair) does not bound full GRPO AutoInject or GCG
  • Legacy single-step DoS 6.5% on Sonnet 4 / GPT-4o—not zero attack surface
  • Two surfaces only (browser + coding)—not OS-BLIND / MCP / voice
  • Single author—in-paper independent replication not reported

Relevance 4/5 — LLM agent safety, CUA red-teaming, prompt-injection evaluation, reproducibility. Challenges extrapolating literature ASR to current deployments.

Open questions
