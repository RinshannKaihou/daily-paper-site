Strengths

  • Compelling cost story: ~21× fewer LLM calls than MemGPT-Faithful while higher recovery
  • Practical latency/storage profile (<25ms archive, local SQLite, model-agnostic)
  • Transparent failure decomposition (ranking vs coverage; config miss rate)
  • Coverage–coherence trade-off gives actionable deployment guidance (tutoring vs debugging)

Limitations

  • **Evaluation chain:** Facts extracted and recovery judged by LLMs; mitigated but not eliminated by κ=0.81 human audit
  • **Dataset:** ShareGPT only; ≥20-turn filter; not LoCoMo/LongMemEval yet
  • **Baselines:** MemGPT-Faithful excludes full paging loop; no controlled comparison to Mem0/Letta/Zep
  • **Embedding:** Fixed MiniLM; semantic categories (decisions, goals) remain competitive for LLM-extraction approaches

TL;DR

LANTERN is compaction-aware middleware that proactively archives every turn (zero LLM calls, <25ms/turn) and restores lost details after compaction via RRF-fused hybrid retrieval (semantic + FTS5 + keyword + importance, optional single-call rerank). On 94 ShareGPT conversations with 1,894 human-validated facts (κ=0.81), LANTERN-Rerank recovers 78.3% of facts lost to compaction vs. MemGPT-Faithful 72.4% (Wilcoxon p<0.0001, 95% CI [+3.1,+8.6] pp, d=0.43) at ~21× fewer LLM calls; base LANTERN (76.3%) still beats MemGPT at zero LLM cost (p=0.005). Four production LLMs gain +8.4pp average accuracy with restored context (p<0.05 each). Failure analysis shows archival coverage—not ranking—is the binding constraint (~361/420 misses never surface in the candidate set).
