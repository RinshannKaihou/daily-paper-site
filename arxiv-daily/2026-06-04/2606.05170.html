Key Finding

Matched error rate ε cannot tell you how catastrophic failures are — severity tail index b does.

ERRORQUAKE-10K scores 21 open-weight LLMs on a continuous 0–4 severity scale and fits a Gutenberg–Richter b index to the upper tail. At nearly identical ε, models diverge sharply in rare high-severity events.

Key Metric
85/210

Model pairs with disjoint 95% bootstrap CIs on b while |Δε|<0.05 under human-consensus scoring — 2.7× more than LLM-judge-only (31 pairs). Example: DeepSeek-V3.2 vs Ministral-14B both ε≈0.586, Δb≈0.47.

Why This Matters
~20×

Spread in expected M≥3.0 events per million queries at matched accuracy (e.g. Gemma-3-4B ~63K vs Qwen3-Next-80B ~3.3K). Binary error counts hide deployment risk in law, medicine, finance.

Critical Edge

Pre-registered catastrophe prediction fails magnitude calibration (ρₛ=0.443 vs required ≥0.75). All 10K queries are LLM-generated; judges overcall 33.5% at severity 2.0 — rankings defended, absolute counts inflated.

Methodology

ERRORQUAKE-10K

10,000 stratified factual queries: 8 domains (BIO, LAW, HIST, GEO, SCI, TECH, FIN, CULT) × 5 tiers (T1–T5). T4–T5 trap questions elicit fabrication. 21 open-weight instruction-tuned models (~3B–37B).

Dual-judge scoring

8-model round-robin pool; mean if agreement ≤1.0 else median-of-three. ICC(2,1)=0.374 pooled; human validation n=519, ICC(2,k=3)=0.85, rank ρ=0.89 with judges.

b estimation

log₁₀ N(M≥m) = a − b(m − m_min). Aki (1965) MLE, δ=0.5 bins, m_min via KS to exponential tail (≥30 events). 2,000 bootstrap resamples for 95% CIs.

Non-reducibility

Theorem 1: models can share arbitrary ε while differing in b. Empirical I(b; model | ε)≈1.56 bits; only 35.6% of cross-model b variance explained by ε (R²=0.356).

Robustness

Cross-domain jackknife: 25–41 disjoint-CI pairs per drop (all ≥3). Threshold sweep T=30→200: ≥10 pairs remain. mmin choice flips scaling sign (bulk +0.837 vs upper-tail −0.562) — interpret tail regime carefully.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Compelling matched-ε example (Δb≈0.47 at ε=0.586)
  • 10K×21 scale with domain/tier stratification
  • Human validation (ICC=0.85) beats judge-only discrimination
  • Formal non-reducibility theorem + mechanism taxonomy
  • Transparent PASS/FAIL pre-registration; full open release

Limitations

  • 33.5% judge overcall at score 2.0; fair ICC(2,1)=0.374
  • 21 open-weight models only; no proprietary frontier
  • All queries LLM-generated — ecological validity risk
  • b cannot extrapolate catastrophe counts across tiers
  • Scaling sign sensitive to mmin; ~35 human items per model
