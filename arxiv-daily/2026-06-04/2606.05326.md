> **一句话总结：** 本文在 Edge of Stability（EoS）大学习率振荡训练下，提出耦合慢变量 \(\theta\) 与快振荡协方差 \(\Sigma\) 的连续时间有效模型，并定义有效自由能 \(F(\theta,\Sigma)=E(\theta)+\frac{\eta}{2}\nabla^2 E:\Sigma\) 作为比损失更合适的宏观监测量；在宽两层网络上导出含波动方向的新动能方程，可解释为加权 Wasserstein-2 梯度流，并在矩阵分解与 CIFAR-10（CNN/MLP）上验证模型能追踪振荡包络与 sharpness 动力学。

> **TL;DR:** For gradient descent in the **Edge of Stability** regime (\(\lambda_{\max}\approx 2/\eta\), persistent loss/sharpness oscillations), this paper derives a **slow–fast continuous-time model** coupling the averaged weights \(\theta\) with the time-averaged fluctuation covariance \(\Sigma\). The key observable is an **effective free energy** \(F(\theta,\Sigma)=E(\theta)+\frac{\eta}{2}\nabla^2 E:\Sigma\), which tracks training better than the raw loss. Unlike Central Flow, \(\Sigma\) evolves dynamically (no instantaneous relaxation to \(\mathrm{Im}\,\Sigma\subset\ker(\nabla^2 E-2/\eta)\)), enabling explanation of **sharpness re-increase** below the stability threshold. For wide two-layer nets, a **mean-field kinetic equation** (MMD teacher–student case made explicit) is shown to be a **\(W_{2,\eta}\) gradient flow** of a macroscopic free energy, with well-posedness results. Numerics on **depth-3 matrix factorization** and **CIFAR-10** (500-image subset, \(\eta\) above stability) match oscillation envelopes and support free energy over Central Flow on longer horizons.

## 核心贡献

- **EoS 慢–快有效模型（式 3.4）：** 将离散 GD 轨迹分解为 \(\tilde\theta_k=\theta_k+\sqrt{\eta}\,\delta\theta_k\)，对快振荡时间平均得到 \(\Sigma_k=\mathbb{E}[\delta\theta_k\delta\theta_k^\top]\)，导出耦合 ODE：
  \[
  \dot\theta=-\eta\nabla E(\theta)-\tfrac{\eta^2}{2}\nabla\nabla^2 E(\theta):\Sigma,\quad
  \dot\Sigma=-\eta(\Sigma\nabla^2 E+\nabla^2 E\Sigma)+\eta^2\nabla^2 E\,\Sigma\,\nabla^2 E.
  \]
  可追踪 **sharpness 边界附近的振荡包络**，且 \(\Sigma\) 与 \(\theta\) 可在相近时间尺度上共同演化（非 Central Flow 的瞬时平衡假设）。
- **有效自由能 \(F(\theta,\Sigma)\)：** \(F=E+\frac{\eta}{2}\nabla^2 E:\Sigma\) 由能量在 \(\sqrt{\eta}\) 尺度下的 Taylor 展开自然出现；实验表明应将其与真实 GD 的 \(E(\tilde\theta_k)\) 对照，而非仅用慢变量损失 \(E(\theta_k)\)。在 \(\|\nabla^2 E\|_\infty\le 2/\eta\) 时，沿流 \( \frac{d}{dt}F=-\eta\|\nabla_\theta F\|^2\le 0\)（命题 3.3）。
- **相对 Central Flow 的概念推进：** 保留自稳定曲率漂移项 \(-\frac{\eta^2}{2}\nabla\nabla^2 E:\Sigma\)，但允许 \(\Sigma\) 独立演化，从而解释 **稳定阈值以下 eigenvalue 的再次上升**（\(\eta^2\nabla^2 E\,\Sigma\,\nabla^2 E\) 项），并覆盖 \(1/\eta\) 量级的短时间尺度。
- **宽两层网络的平均场动能方程：** 在 \(N\to\infty\) 极限下，将权重与低秩波动 \(v_i\) 编码为相空间密度 \(f(t,\theta,v)\)，对 MMD/核教师–学生情形得到闭式输运方程（3.13），宏观自由能（3.14）的 **加权 \(W_2\) 梯度流**；定理 3.6 给出适定性与 \(F(f)\) 沿流递减。
- **数值验证：** 2×2、深度 3 矩阵分解（\(\eta=0.077\)）与 CIFAR-10 子集上的 2 层 CNN/MLP（\(\eta=0.02\gg 2/\lambda_{\max}\)）显示模型追踪 **有效 sharpness \(\eta\lambda_{\max}\)** 与损失/自由能包络；高维情形仅跟踪 Hessian 前 \(r\) 个特征向量，特征值演化 \(\dot\lambda_i=-2\eta\lambda_i+\eta^2\lambda_i^2\) 在短时间尺度上近似良好。

## Key Contributions

- **Continuous-time EoS effective dynamics:** Decomposes GD as \(\tilde\theta_k=\theta_k+\sqrt{\eta}\,\delta\theta_k\) with \(\Sigma_k=\mathbb{E}[\delta\theta_k\delta\theta_k^\top]\), yielding coupled evolution for slow \(\theta\) and fluctuation envelope \(\Sigma\) (Eq. 3.4)—designed to track oscillation envelopes when \(\Sigma\) and \(\theta\) evolve on comparable timescales.
- **Effective free energy as the right macroscopic quantity:** \(F(\theta,\Sigma)=E(\theta)+\frac{\eta}{2}\nabla^2 E:\Sigma\) emerges from averaging a second-order Taylor expansion; numerics argue it should be compared to \(E(\tilde\theta_k)\), not \(E(\theta_k)\). Proposition 3.3: \(dF/dt=-\eta\|\nabla_\theta F\|^2\le 0\) when \(\|\nabla^2 E\|_\infty\le 2/\eta\).
- **Beyond Central Flow [CDT+24]:** Shares the curvature-driven self-stabilization term but **does not** impose \(\mathrm{Im}\,\Sigma\subset\ker(\nabla^2 E-2/\eta)\) at all times; dynamic \(\Sigma\) explains **post-stabilization sharpness increase** via \(\eta^2\nabla^2 E\,\Sigma\,\nabla^2 E\) and captures \(O(1/\eta)\) short-time envelopes.
- **Mean-field kinetic limit for two-layer networks:** Empirical measure over \((\theta,v)\) with rank-preserving \(\Sigma=v\otimes v\); closed transport equation (3.13) for translation-invariant kernel / **MMD** teacher–student setup; interpreted as gradient flow of macroscopic \(F(f)\) in **\(W_{2,\eta}\)** (metric \(d\theta^2+\eta\,dv^2\)); Theorem 3.6 establishes existence, compact \(v\)-support, and energy dissipation (3.17).
- **Experiments:** Matrix factorization (\(d=2,L=3,\eta=0.077\)) and CIFAR-10 (500 images, 32-channel 2-layer CNN or MLP, MSE, \(\eta=0.02\)) validate envelope tracking; low-rank Hessian eigenvector approximation (recompute every 100 steps) works on short scales; free energy outperforms Central Flow on longer CNN/MLP runs.

## Methodology

**Setting.** Minimize risk \(E(\theta)\) by gradient descent \(\tilde\theta_{k+1}=\tilde\theta_k-\eta\nabla E(\tilde\theta_k)\). Classical descent lemma: stable decrease when \(\eta\lambda_{\max}<2\) (\(\lambda_{\max}\) = top Hessian eigenvalue / sharpness). **EoS:** empirically \(\lambda_{\max}\approx 2/\eta\) with oscillating but **on-average** decreasing loss [CKL+21].

**Derivation sketch (Appendix A).** Ansatz \(\tilde\theta_k=\theta_k+\sqrt{\eta}\,\delta\theta_k\) with \(\mathbb{E}[\delta\theta_k]=0\), justified when \(\eta\lambda_1(\nabla^2 E)\approx 2\) and fluctuation “entropy” \(\mathbb{E}\|\tilde\theta-\theta\|^2\sim \eta E(\theta)\). Taylor-expand \(\nabla E(\tilde\theta_k)\) around \(\theta_k\), average for \(\theta_{k+1}\), subtract for \(\delta\theta_{k+1}\), and average outer products for \(\Sigma_{k+1}\). Continuous limit with \(\Delta t=1\) gives (3.4). \(\eta\to 0\) (time rescale \(\tau=t\eta\)) recovers gradient flow.

**Free energy & stationary points.** Beyond saddle points of \(E\), equilibria satisfy \(\nabla_\theta F=0\) and \(\nabla^2 E\,\Sigma+\Sigma\nabla^2 E-\eta\nabla^2 E\,\Sigma\,\nabla^2 E=0\), including persistent EoS oscillations. Flat-landscape limit (\(\lambda\to 0\), coarse time) reduces \(\Sigma\) dynamics to **Bures-metric** gradient flow and **rank-preserving** factorization \(\Sigma=v\otimes v\) (Remarks 3.1–3.2).

**Two-layer mean field.** Network output \(f(\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_N,x)=\frac{1}{N}\sum_i \phi(\theta_i,x)\); risk \(E(\rho)=\mathbb{E}_{(x,y)\sim\mathcal{D}}\ell\big(y,\int \phi(\theta,x)\,d\rho(\theta)\big)\). Per-neuron EoS dynamics (3.10) at timescale \(N/\eta\); quadratic \(\Sigma\) term is \(O(1/N)\) and dropped in \(N\to\infty\). Empirical \(f_N=\frac{1}{N}\sum_i \delta_{(\theta_i,v_i)}\) → kinetic equation (3.13) via flow map (3.16). **MMD case:** squared loss, teacher \(\mu\), kernel \(g(\theta-\theta')=\mathbb{E}_x\phi(\theta,x)\phi(\theta',x)\), \(E(\rho)=\frac{1}{2}\int g*(\rho-\mu)\,d(\rho-\mu)\).

**Experiments (high-dim trick).** When full \(\Sigma\) is intractable: Hessian \(\nabla^2 E=UXU^\top\), track top-\(r\) eigenpairs \((v_i,\lambda_i)\) with \(\dot\lambda_i=-2\eta\lambda_i+\eta^2\lambda_i^2\) and frozen eigenbasis on short horizons.

## Results & Highlights

**Theory.**

| Result | Statement |
|--------|-----------|
| Prop. 3.3 | Local solution to (3.4); if \(\|\nabla^2 E\|_\infty\le 2/\eta\), then \(dF/dt=-\eta\|\nabla_\theta F\|^2\le 0\) |
| PL-type remark | If \(\|\nabla_\theta F\|^2\ge \frac{\lambda}{\eta}F\) along flow, then \(F(t)\le F(0)e^{-\lambda t}\) |
| Thm. 3.6 | For \(g\in C_b^{3,1}\), compactly supported \(f_0\) in \(v\): unique \(W_{2,\eta}\) gradient-flow solution to MMD kinetic eq.; dissipation inequality (3.17); regularity propagation if \(g\) smooth enough |

**Comparison to Central Flow (Eq. 2.1).**

| Aspect | Central Flow [CDT+24] | This work |
|--------|----------------------|-----------|
| Monitored quantity | Loss \(E(\bar\theta)\) | **Free energy** \(F(\theta,\Sigma)\) |
| \(\Sigma\) dynamics | Instantaneous constraint on \(\mathrm{Im}\,\Sigma\) | **Coupled ODE**, slow envelope |
| Timescales | Coarse, post-relaxation | **\(O(1/\eta)\)** oscillation envelope |
| Sharpness below \(2/\eta\) | Not explained | **\(\eta^2\nabla^2 E\,\Sigma\,\nabla^2 E\)** term |

**Numerics (Section 4).**

- **Matrix factorization:** \(E=\frac{1}{2}\|W_L\cdots W_1-Y\|_F^2\), \(d=2,L=3\), \(\eta=0.077\); model tracks loss/free-energy envelopes and **effective sharpness** \(\eta\lambda_{\max}\) (EoS threshold = 2.0).
- **CIFAR-10:** 500 images, 32-ch 2-layer CNN, GELU, 10-class MSE, \(\eta=0.02\); short-time envelope match; **free energy** tracks downward trend when raw loss oscillates.
- **MLP variant:** Recomputing top Hessian eigenvectors every 100 steps ≈ every step on short scales → **local Hessian eigenbasis stability** at low energy; longer runs: free energy beats Central Flow as loss predictor.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High for linking EoS oscillations to a **thermodynamic-style free energy** and a **kinetic mean-field** picture with fluctuation directions \(v\). Extends Central Flow in a principled slow–fast direction and offers a quantitative mechanism for **sharpness cycling** below \(2/\eta\).
- **Rigor:** Mixed. Strong structure (energy dissipation for \(F\), \(W_{2,\eta}\) gradient-flow interpretation, well-posedness for MMD equation) but **no full finite-\(\eta\) GD convergence theorem**; scaling of \(\delta\theta\) and ergodic averaging of fast modes remain preliminary (Discussion §5.3).
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate. Matrix factorization is fully specified; CIFAR setup is described but **no code release** mentioned; high-dim experiments rely on partial Hessian tracking heuristics.
- **Significance:** High for optimization theory and **why large learning rates generalize** (flat-minima / sharpness narratives [HS97, KMN+17, FKMN21]). Connects ML training spikes to kinetic-theory analogies (Boltzmann/Landau-style degenerate diffusion in \(v\)).
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - Derivation **formal**; neglected higher-order terms may dominate **long** training (author acknowledges worse long-time fit vs matrix case).
  - Mean-field EoS regime may **not** match practical **maximal update** scaling [YH21].
  - Relation to concurrent **Rod Flow** [RC26a,b] left open (possible interpolation with Central Flow).
  - CIFAR experiments are **small subset + MSE**, not standard cross-entropy full-data training.

## Strengths

- **Unified macroscopic picture:** Slow \(\theta\), fast \(\delta\theta\), envelope \(\Sigma\), and **free energy** give a coherent language for EoS “spikes” and envelopes.
- **Clear positioning vs Central Flow:** Tabulates conceptual differences (free energy, dynamic \(\Sigma\), sharpness re-growth).
- **Mean-field extension:** First-class kinetic equation with **\(W_{2,\eta}\)** structure and MMD closed form—not only finite-dimensional ODEs.
- **Numerical sanity checks:** From \(d=2\) matrix factorization to real CNN/MLP loss landscapes; effective sharpness normalization (\(\eta\lambda_{\max}\), threshold 2) aids interpretation.
- **Honest limitations:** Formal derivation, long-time gaps, open kinetic long-time behavior, and comparison to Rod Flow.

## Limitations

- **No end-to-end guarantee** linking discrete GD to (3.4) with explicit error bounds or timescale separation conditions.
- **Long-horizon drift:** Model less accurate over full training; higher-order GD terms not systematically included.
- **Practical scaling:** Derived regime (\(N/\eta\) rescaling, dropped \(O(1/N)\) terms) may not align with standard **feature-learning** wide-net training.
- **CIFAR scope:** 500 images, MSE on 10 classes, moderate architectures—generalization to ImageNet-scale SGD/Adam EoS untested.
- **Computational cost:** Full \(\Sigma\) infeasible; eigenvector-freezing heuristic is approximate.
- **No public code** in the PDF.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can one prove **quantitative approximation** of discrete GD by (3.4) under explicit EoS assumptions (spectral gap, almost-periodicity à la Rod Flow)?
- Does the effective model **converge to Central Flow** on longer timescales when \(\Sigma\) relaxes—can both be derived from a single multi-scale expansion?
- How does the framework extend to **Adam / adaptive methods** and **stochastic mini-batch noise** (orthogonal to discretization oscillations)?
- In standard classification training, does \(F(\theta,\Sigma)\) (with tractable low-rank \(\Sigma\)) predict **generalization** better than \(E(\theta)\) or sharpness alone?
- What are **long-time stationary measures** of kinetic equation (3.13)—connection to global MMD minimizers vs EoS limit cycles?
- Can the **frozen Hessian eigenbasis** observation be turned into a landscape geometry theorem (low-energy valley structure)?
- Numerically, how does the model compare to **Rod Flow** [RC26a] on the same matrix-factorization and CIFAR setups?
