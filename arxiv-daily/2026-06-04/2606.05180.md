> **一句话总结：** 在 NCTE 课堂转录的 CLASS「反馈质量」维度（6,005 段、1–7 分制）上，作者提出句级可解释框架，对比微调 PLM 的 SHAP 归因与多种开源 LLM 的句子排序式「理由」；微调 PLM 评分更准但标签向中段压缩，删除实验表明 SHAP 更能忠实驱动预测且可跨架构迁移，而 LLM 理由与 PLM 决策对齐弱、跨模型影响小且不稳定。

> **TL;DR:** On 6k NCTE transcript segments scored for CLASS Quality of Feedback (1–7), a sentence-level framework compares hierarchical fine-tuned PLMs (SHAP on sentence embeddings) against few-shot LLM scorers plus zero-shot top-10 sentence rankings. Fine-tuned PLMs win on MAE (~0.96–1.00 vs best LLM 1.02) but compress labels to mid-scale; deletion tests show SHAP induces larger, more coherent score shifts and transfers across architectures, whereas LLM rationales overlap weakly (Jaccard≈0.085, Spearman≈0.062) and perturb PLMs poorly—arguing Shapley attributions over persuasive LLM prose for high-stakes rubric scoring.

## 核心贡献

- **通用句级可解释框架**：将基于评分的文本评估形式化为「标量 rubric 分 + 句级解释」，在课堂教学质量评估上实例化，并可通过删除测试与跨模型扰动系统评估解释忠实度（faithfulness）。
- **PLM vs LLM 评分对比（RQ1）**：在 QoF 维度上，8 组微调 PLM（BERT/ALBERT/RoBERTa/DeBERTaV3）MAE 0.96–1.00，优于 9 个提示式开源 LLM（最佳 Mistral Small Instruct MAE 1.02）；但 PLM 预测几乎从不触及 1/7 极端分，LLM 覆盖全量程。
- **忠实度评估（RQ2）**：逐步删除 top-10「重要句」并重打分；SHAP 排序对 PLM 产生更大、更单调的预测变化（如 BERT large 平均连续 ∆=0.0329），LLM 自产排序变化更小且更不稳定（多数 ∆<0.02）。
- **跨模型迁移（RQ3）**：用 LLM 选句删除后重评 PLM，扰动远弱于 SHAP 选句；反向用 PLM 的 SHAP 选句扰动 LLM 常出现「首步大跳」后趋稳，说明 SHAP 句级特征跨架构相关，而 LLM 理由难以刻画 PLM 依赖的证据。
- **随机删除对照**：ranked 删除显著大于 random 删除（random 平均 ∆ 近零），排除仅由删句长度或通用扰动造成的假象。
- **政策与实践含义**：面向 EU AI Act 等高后果教育场景，主张用因果一致的 SHAP 类归因支撑教师反思，而非将 LLM 自由文本理由当作决策依据。

## Key Contributions

- **General sentence-level interpretability framework** for rubric-based scoring with deletion-based faithfulness and cross-model perturbation protocols, instantiated on CLASS Quality of Feedback.
- **RQ1 scoring benchmark**: Eight fine-tuned hierarchical PLMs (MAE 0.96–1.00, MSE 1.31–1.56) outperform nine prompted open LLMs (best Mistral Small Instruct MAE 1.02) on 1,230 test segments, at the cost of mid-scale label compression vs full-range LLM dispersion.
- **RQ2 faithfulness via deletion**: Progressive removal of top-10 SHAP- or LLM-ranked sentences; SHAP yields larger, more coherent prediction shifts for PLM scorers; LLM self-rankings produce smaller, often non-monotonic changes.
- **RQ3 cross-architecture transfer**: SHAP-selected sentences perturb LLM scores substantially (especially first removal); LLM-selected sentences barely move PLM predictions—SHAP generalizes, LLM rationales do not.
- **Random-deletion ablation**: Ranked removals ≫ random removals, isolating influential content identification from generic discourse disruption.
- **Stakeholder-oriented takeaway**: For high-stakes educational rubrics, Shapley sentence attributions are more faithful and transferable than LLM-generated sentence rankings presented as explanations.

## Methodology

**Task & rubric.** Classroom teaching quality as rubric-based text scoring: predict a scalar Quality of Feedback (QoF) score (CLASS Instructional Support, 1–7) from elementary math lesson transcripts. QoF captures meaningful feedback, scaffolding, metacognitive prompts, elaboration, and clarification.

**Dataset (NCTE).** >1,600 lessons → **6,005** fifteen-minute segments; expert QoF labels (mean **4.21**, σ=**1.13**, **81%** in 3–5). **80/20** split by class (4,775 train / 1,230 test); test utterances ~70% teacher / 29% student. Single expert label per dimension (no inter-rater reliability; no human sentence-level evidence annotations).

**PLM scorers.** Fine-tuned BERT, ALBERT, RoBERTa, DeBERTaV3 (base & large). Hierarchical encoder: per-sentence [CLS] → attention-weighted document embedding → linear regression head (MSE). Max 128 tokens/sentence, ≤263 sentences/doc. **SHAP** (KernelExplainer) on sentence embeddings for document-level QoF prediction.

**LLM scorers.** Open-weight instruction models for local/privacy deployment: Llama 3.1 (8B, 70B), Mixtral (8×7B, 8×22B), Qwen3 (4B, 30B, 235B), Mistral (Small, Small 24B). Tasks: (1) few-shot QoF scoring; (2) zero-shot **top-10 sentence index** ranking (not free-form CoT) aligned to PLM segmentation. Deterministic decoding, 4-bit NF4; up to 10 retries on malformed index lists.

**Faithfulness protocol.** For each segment, rank top-10 sentences (SHAP or LLM), delete one at a time in rank order, re-score with the **same** model (RQ2) or **alternate** model family (RQ3). Metric: cumulative mean absolute prediction change ∆ after k removals; also Jaccard overlap and Spearman correlation between SHAP vs LLM top-10 sets. Random same-length deletion baseline (Table 3).

## Results & Highlights

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Best PLM accuracy** | DeBERTaV3 large: **MAE 0.96**, MSE 1.31 (~1 rubric point error on 1–7 scale) |
| **Fine-tuned PLM range** | MAE **0.96–1.00**; architectures differ by only ∆0.04 MAE |
| **Best LLM accuracy** | Mistral Small Instruct: **MAE 1.02**, MSE 1.78 (still above PLM & constant baseline MAE 0.96) |
| **LLM variability** | Cross-model spread ∆**0.96** MAE, ∆**3.54** MSE |
| **Label compression (PLM)** | DeBERTaV3 large predictions ∈ **[2.03, 5.89]**; mean 4.14 (σ=0.16) vs dataset 4.22—never predicts 1 or 7 |
| **Label coverage (LLM)** | Mistral Small mean **4.37** (σ=0.77), spans full 1–7 |
| **Explanation overlap** | Mean Jaccard **0.085** (~1–2 shared sentences / 10); Spearman **0.062** |
| **Speaker bias (both)** | ~75–80% selected sentences are **teacher** utterances (QoF-aligned) |
| **Faithfulness ∆ (PLM, SHAP)** | BERT large **0.0329** highest among PLMs; DeBERTaV3 large lowest **0.0049** |
| **Faithfulness ∆ (LLM, self-rank)** | Qwen3 235B **0.0388** max; most LLMs **<0.02** |
| **Random baseline** | Ranked removal ≫ random (random ∆ ≈ 0, e.g., DeBERTaV3 large random **−0.0064**) |
| **Cross-model** | LLM ranks → small PLM shifts; SHAP ranks → large LLM first-step jumps then stabilize |

**Trade-off (RQ1).** Accuracy vs flexibility: supervised PLMs optimize rubric error but inherit training distribution (mid-scale compression); LLMs are deployable without fine-tuning and preserve score variance at higher error and compute cost.

**Faithfulness asymmetry (RQ2–3).** Even when PLMs and LLMs surface similar *types* of teacher-centric evidence, they disagree on *which* sentences matter. SHAP removals causally move scores; LLM index lists are often plausible yet weakly coupled to PLM computation—consistent with unfaithful CoT literature (Turpin et al., 2023; Ye & Durrett, 2022).

**Practical LLM issues.** Prompted top-10 lists are inconsistently sized despite retries (Mixtral 8×7B, Qwen3 235B most noncompliant); only Llama 3.1 8B averages closest to ten sentences—deployment risk for structured educational tooling.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (moderate-to-high).** Sentence-level SHAP for hierarchical rubric regression plus matched LLM index-ranking is a clean, reusable evaluation template beyond prior essay-level SHAP work. The cross-model faithfulness protocol is the distinctive empirical contribution.

**Rigor (strong).** Systematic comparison across PLM families and open LLMs; ablation against random deletion; quantitative overlap metrics between explanation methods. Weaknesses: no human construct-relevance judgments on highlighted sentences; ordinal 1–7 treated via regression/MAE; LLM ranking reliability required heavy engineering.

**Reproducibility (moderate).** Detailed hyperparameters in appendices; deterministic decoding and fixed splits help, but full replication needs NCTE access, multi-GPU local LLM inference, and SHAP compute on long transcripts.

**Significance (high for XAI + EdTech).** Directly challenges deploying LLM-generated rationales as trustworthy feedback in teacher professional development; SHAP offers audit-friendly, transferable evidence for contesting automated scores under emerging AI regulation.

**Red flags:**
- Deletion faithfulness ≠ pedagogical causality (acknowledged)
- PLM mid-scale compression may overstate LLM "flexibility" advantage without calibration
- LLM sentence-count noncompliance complicates fair comparison (mitigated by retries/min caps)

## Strengths

- Clear three-RQ structure with matched deletion protocol across explanation types
- Honest trade-off analysis: accuracy (PLM) vs score range (LLM) vs explanation faithfulness (SHAP)
- Cross-model experiments go beyond single-model eraser benchmarks
- QoF dimension choice justified (less skewed CLASS axis, text-manifest indicators, pedagogical importance)
- Ethics section aligns with decision-support (not replacement) and warns against authoritative LLM rationales

## Limitations

- **Scope:** QoF only; unclear generalization to less discourse-heavy CLASS dimensions (e.g., Productivity)
- **Modality:** Text transcripts ignore prosody, timing, video—standard CLASS practice uses richer cues
- **Labels:** Single expert per segment; no inter-rater reliability; extreme scores rare → PLM compression
- **Evaluation gap:** No human audit whether top sentences instantiate rubric constructs
- **Perturbation artifact:** Sentence deletion distorts classroom pragmatics; may differentially penalize LLM scorers
- **LLM engineering:** Structured index prompting constrains natural explanatory capacity; reliability issues persist

## Relevance

该工作与 **可解释 NLP、LLM 理由忠实度、高后果 rubric 自动评分** 直接相关：在 daily_paper 过滤中 relevance_score=3，强调「SHAP 归因 + LLM 理由」用于可解释评分评估。对部署 **课堂/教育类 LLM 评分助手**、设计 **EU AI Act 合规的可审计解释**、以及区分 **「听起来合理」vs「因果上驱动预测」** 的工程师与研究者具有参考价值；与当日关注的 LLM 可靠性、评测方法论形成互补（非 Agent/推理加速主线，但是 faithfulness 评测的扎实范例）。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Would human rubric experts rate SHAP top-10 sentences as higher construct-relevance than LLM picks**, closing the gap between faithfulness and pedagogical validity?
2. **Can calibration or ordinal/regression heads on PLMs recover extreme QoF labels** without sacrificing MAE, and does SHAP faithfulness hold after rebalancing?
3. **Do multimodal CLASS scorers (audio/video)** preserve the SHAP≫LLM-rationale faithfulness gap when explanations must cite non-text evidence?
4. **Can LLM explanations be made faithful** via constrained attribution (e.g., Shapley approximations for LLMs) rather than free ranking prompts?
5. **How do deletion-based metrics correlate with teacher trust and behavior change** in field trials beyond offline ∆ scores?
