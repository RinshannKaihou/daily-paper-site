> **一句话总结：** 针对 GRPO 每条 rollout 仅用一次即丢弃的样本浪费，作者提出 rollout 级经验回放：以训练步龄 τ_max 驱逐过期样本、以 fresh-anchored 批次保留 on-policy 锚点、以 |A_i| 优先回收组内高梯度信号少数派；在 Qwen3-Base 0.6B/1.7B/4B 上五套数学基准平均准确率随规模增至 +4.35 pp，4B 上 pass@k 与 AES 效率增益最大。

> **TL;DR:** GRPO discards each rollout after one gradient step, wasting high-signal completions. This paper introduces a rollout-level replay buffer with (1) age eviction (τ_max=10) to bound policy lag, (2) fresh-anchored batch composition that always retains fresh on-policy rollouts before concatenating replay samples, and (3) |A_i|-prioritized sampling (PER-style, α=0.5) to recycle within-group minority rollouts. On DeepScaleR-Preview with Qwen3-Base at 0.6B/1.7B/4B, the method beats GRPO and naive query-level pool-replay baselines at every scale, with five-benchmark avg gains of +0.38/+0.83/+4.35 pp and the largest pass@k (+10.06 pp) and AES (+0.579) margins at 4B.

## 核心贡献

- **Rollout 级 GRPO 回放缓冲**：以单个 rollout（而非整组 G 条）为存储与采样单元，使优先级信号可区分组内少数高 |A_i| 样本。
- **步龄驱逐（age eviction）**：每条 rollout 记录 birth step；超过 τ_max=10 训练步即删除，以步数而非容量约束最坏 policy lag，且与模型规模无关。
- **Fresh-anchored 批次构成**：每步保留全部 fresh on-policy rollout，再按回放比例 r=0.5 从缓冲单独采样并拼接；相对 pool composition（DQN/PER 式混合池）在 4B 上避免性能跌破 GRPO。
- **|A_i| 优先采样**：采用 PER 概率 P(i)∝|A_i|^α（α=0.5），回收 skewed group 中绝对优势最大的少数 rollout；相对 query-level σ_g 在 1.7B 上 pass 高 +3.19 pp。
- **跨尺度实证**：DeepScaleR-Preview 上训练 Qwen3-Base 0.6B/1.7B/4B 至 step 200，在五项数学基准（MATH-500、AIME25/26、HMMT-F25/26）上全面优于 GRPO 与 uniform/σ_g 朴素回放。
- **效率与采样多样性**：AES（准确率+长度联合指标）相对 GRPO 在 4B 达 +0.579；正确 rollout 平均更长（4B +34.9%）、错误更短（−10.2%），pass@k 显示采样多样性增益。

## Key Contributions

- **Rollout-level GRPO replay buffer**: Stores and samples individual rollouts rather than binding all G completions of a prompt as one query-level unit, enabling within-group priority resolution.
- **Age eviction with τ_max**: Removes rollouts older than τ_max training steps (default 10), bounding worst-case policy lag independently of buffer capacity and model scale.
- **Fresh-anchored composition**: Retains all fresh on-policy rollouts in every gradient step and concatenates a separate replay draw (r=0.5); avoids the 4B failure mode of pool composition where stale samples displace fresh anchors.
- **|A_i|-prioritized replay**: PER-style sampling with p_i=|A_i| and α=0.5 targets high-gradient minority rollouts that single-pass GRPO discards; beats rollout-level σ_g by +3.19 pp on pass at 1.7B.
- **Cross-scale evaluation**: Three Qwen3-Base scales on DeepScaleR-Preview (40,306 prompts) with DAPO-style clipped GRPO; consistent gains over GRPO and naive replay grow with size (+4.35 pp five-bench avg at 4B).
- **Efficiency and diversity analysis**: Largest AES margin (+0.579) and pass@k gain (+10.06 pp vs. GRPO) at 4B; asymmetric length shift (longer correct, shorter incorrect rollouts) linked to recycling rare-action completions.

## Methodology

**Problem setting.** Post-training reasoning LLMs with Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) under binary verifiable rewards r_i∈{−1,+1}. Group-relative advantage A_i=(r_i−μ_g)/(σ_g+ε_σ). Zero-variance groups are dropped. Standard GRPO uses each rollout once in a PPO-style clipped surrogate with importance ratio ρ_i=π_θ(o_i|q)/π_t(o_i|q), cached at generation time for replay.

**Buffer design (Algorithm 1).** Four phases per step: (1) generate B_fresh fresh rollouts and filter zero-variance groups; (2) draw r·B′_fresh replay rollouts via Equation 3 with |A_i| priority; (3) GRPO update on fresh survivors ∥ replay draw; (4) insert fresh survivors with birth step t and evict age>t−τ_max. Twenty-step warmup without replay precedes activation. Capacity cap 30,000 acts as backstop; at τ_max=10, age eviction binds (steady-state ≤1,250 rollouts at 4B).

**Three design axes vs. prior work.** Prior GRPO replay (ExGRPO, RePO, RLEP, Buffer Matters, Fatemi) shares fresh+replay mixing but operates at **query level** with group-constant priorities (correctness×entropy, solve rate, σ_g, etc.). Naive baselines in this paper use pool composition + query-level storage + uniform or σ_g priority, matching replay budget to isolate composition and granularity effects.

**Training setup.** DeepScaleR-Preview (≤1024 tokens), G=8 rollouts/prompt, global step 200 checkpoint, DAPO clip-higher (ε_low=0.2, ε_high=0.28), verl implementation, bfloat16 on H100 GPUs (4 GPUs for ≤1.7B, 8 for 4B). Hyperparameters held constant across GRPO, naive replay, and proposed method.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (moderate-to-high within GRPO replay niche).** The individual ingredients—PER priority, DQN FIFO, fresh+replay mixing (ExGRPO/RePO)—are known. The contribution is their **joint instantiation at rollout granularity** with step-based staleness control, motivated by fast LLM policy drift and within-group advantage skew. The argument that query-level σ_g cannot target minority high-|A_i| rollouts is well-supported analytically and empirically.

**Rigor (strong for scope).** Controlled comparisons hold replay budget, τ_max, α, and warmup fixed; naive baselines isolate design axes. Clipping-rate decomposition (fresh vs. replay) links τ_max to off-policy stress. Limitations are explicit (math-only, binary rewards, single snapshot, one seed). Weakness: no multi-seed training variance; indirect stability via five benchmarks only.

**Reproducibility (good).** Detailed Appendix A (40,306 prompts, Table 7 hyperparameters, decoding T=0.6/top-p=0.8, k conventions, seed policy). Dependencies on verl/DAPO stack may complicate exact replication but methodology is fully specified.

**Significance (moderate for RLVR practitioners).** Practical recipe: rollout-level |A_i| + τ_max age eviction + fresh-anchored batches. Largest impact at 4B suggests replay pays off when mixed-reward groups are common—important for scaling post-training budgets. Not validated on frontier models, non-math domains, learned reward models, or PPO/REINFORCE.

**Red flags:**
- Evaluation frozen at step 200—unknown late-training failure modes or ceiling effects
- 0.6B gains smaller and non-monotonic across some metrics (scarce mixed-reward groups)
- Automatic answer checking only—length/efficiency claims not human-validated
- AES gain partly from longer correct chains (+34.9% tokens at 4B), which may or may not be desirable at deployment
- Ethical dual-use noted (exam cheating, synthetic data contamination) but no artifact release limits immediate risk

## Strengths

- Clear problem: single-use rollouts waste high-|A_i| minority signal in skewed GRPO groups
- Principled staleness bound via τ_max steps rather than opaque buffer capacity
- Fresh-anchored composition shown necessary at 4B—actionable design rule
- Comprehensive ablations with naive baselines matching replay budget
- Scaling trend (+4.35 pp at 4B) aligned with mixed-group prevalence analysis
- pass@k and AES analyses beyond avg accuracy—addresses diversity and token cost
- Extensive related-work positioning (classical PER → modern LLM RL replay design space)

## Limitations

- **Domain & reward:** Math reasoning with binary verifiers only; DAPO-style GRPO; no learned RM or other RL algorithms
- **Training horizon:** Single checkpoint at global step 200; no long-run or continued-training study
- **Scale & transfer:** Qwen3-Base up to 4B on one prompt set; no larger models, agents, or multi-turn tasks
- **Small-model regime:** 0.6B shows smaller margins; only 34% mixed-reward groups limits |A_i| replay pool
- **Seeds & variance:** One training seed; per-seed training variance not reported
- **Evaluation:** Programmatic answer checker only; no human quality assessment of reasoning chains
- **No public artifacts:** No released checkpoints or datasets with this submission

## Relevance

该工作直接面向 **RLVR/GRPO 后训练样本效率** 这一当前热点：在 rollout 生成占主导成本的前提下，如何安全复用高价值轨迹而不因策略漂移导致训练崩溃。若你正在优化数学推理模型的 GRPO 管线、对比 ExGRPO/RePO 等回放方案，或需要在准确率与 pass@k/生成长度之间权衡，本文的三轴设计（步龄驱逐 + fresh 锚定 + rollout 级 |A_i| 优先）提供了可操作的实现细节与跨尺度实证依据。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Does the τ_max=10 sweet spot hold on larger models (8B+) and longer training** where per-step drift and mixed-group rates may differ?
2. **Can difficulty-adaptive prompt selection or buffer filtering** address the 0.6B scarcity of mixed-reward groups and non-monotonic small-scale gains?
3. **How does rollout-level |A_i| replay interact with fresh-side pruning methods** (CPPO, DPPO, PODS)—composable pipeline or redundant?
4. **Will fresh-anchored composition remain necessary under importance-sampling corrections** (e.g., Arnal et al., 2026) that target off-policy replay without an explicit fresh batch?
5. **Does the asymmetric length shift (longer correct, shorter incorrect) improve human-judged reasoning quality**, or mainly optimize verifiable-reward metrics and AES?
