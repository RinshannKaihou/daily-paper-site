Satisfying the physics ≠ sampling the posterior

Headline: Hard PDE projection & guidance on generative priors omit the co-area Jacobian [det(JJ⊤)]−½ — systematically wrong posteriors when constraint sensitivity varies on the manifold.

Anchor fact
96% PCFM posterior-std error · 21× W₁ no Fixman · 9× projection · det G: 12–33× (d=8/16) to >10⁵ (2D d=64)
So what

PCFM, D-Flow, FlowDPS-style hard constraints can report calibrated UQ while error bars are severely miscalibrated — use CoCoS or measure-correct CoCo-Flow.

Critical edge

Scope: post-hoc hard constraint + UQ claims on fixed priors; exact CoCoS is MCMC-costly; no public code; CoCo-Flow less validated than CoCoS.

Recovery

CoCoS within 2.3× i.i.d. arbiter floor; std error 1% vs PCFM 96%; CoCo-Flow ~56 ms/query.

§1Measure-theoretic bias

p★(x) ∝ π(x) · ℓ(y|x) · [det(JJ⊤)]−½   on M = {x : c(x)=0},   J = ∇c

Residual limit (correct)

{x : ‖c(x)‖ ≤ ε}, ε→0 under c ~ N(0,γI). Yields co-area posterior via Federer. Thm. 1: soft pγ → p★ weakly.

Euclidean limit (biased)

{x : dist(x,M) ≤ ε} — Hausdorff restriction of π without Jacobian. PCFM projection, guidance, soft penalty at wrong limit when det G varies.

When limits coincide

  • Cor. 1: det G constant a.e. on M
  • Rare for PDEs; holds mildly in 1D Burgers
  • All samplers ~2× floor there

Sensitivity heterogeneity

  • G = JJ⊤ varies 12–33× (d=8/16 Darcy)
  • >5 orders magnitude (2D d=64)
  • Bias grows with spatial variation of det G

Evaluation arbiter

  • i.i.d. rejection: x ~ π, keep ‖c‖ < ε
  • Converges to p★; avoids HMC mixing failure
  • Scalar post-hoc reweight cannot fix projection

§2CoCoS & CoCo-Flow

CoCoS — Constrained Co-Area Sampler (Alg. 1)

Exact MCMC on M; invariant law p★ (Thm. 2). Potential V = −log π − log ℓ − ½ log det G.

  1. Tangent projector P = I − J⊤(JJ⊤)−1J at x ∈ M
  2. Propose v = PTξ, ξ ~ N(0, Is)
  3. Newton re-projection along J⊤ onto M
  4. Reverse-projection reversibility check
  5. Metropolis accept with log r = V(x) − V(x′) + (‖v‖² − ‖v′‖²)/2s

CoCo-Flow (amortized)

  • Joint (θ,y) forward sim → correct FM (Prop. 4)
  • Or distill CoCoS teachers offline
  • ~56 ms/query · training ~90 s (2D Darcy)

Paradigms tested

  • Projection (PCFM)
  • Inference-time guidance (D-Flow)
  • Soft penalty (γ=0.02) — up to ~40× floor

§5Critical assessment

Strengths

  • Borel–Kolmogorov / co-area insight for SciML
  • CoCoS proven invariant law, not heuristic
  • i.i.d. arbiter avoids MCMC confounds
  • Covers projection, guidance, soft penalty

Novelty & significance

  • Fixman known in MD; absent in PCFM/D-Flow
  • 96% std error is actionable for practitioners
  • CoCo-Flow bridges theory → fast inference

Reproducibility

  • FD adjoints verified to 10−10
  • No public code in extracted text
  • Hyperparams in tables; prior training abbreviated

Critical edge — read before citing

  • Targets post-hoc hard constraint + calibrated UQ claims — not all physics-informed generators
  • Forward-simulation FM is already measure-correct (Prop. 4)
  • 2D d=64 needs informed-subspace analysis; full-field averages dilute signal
  • Exact CoCoS: per-query MCMC + log det G at codimension m
  • CoCo-Flow promising but less validated than exact CoCoS

Authors: Jian Xu (RIKEN iTHEMS & AIP), Delu Zeng (SCUT), John Paisley (Columbia), Qibin Zhao (RIKEN AIP).
