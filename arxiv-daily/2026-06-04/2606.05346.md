> **一句话总结：** 本文提出“轨迹外推误差”（trajectory extrapolation error）：在每个词位置对 Transformer 前 \(k\) 个词的隐状态做线性外推，度量实际隐状态偏离外推路径的程度；该指标与 surprisal 近乎正交（\(r=.044\)），却能独立预测 Natural Stories 自定速阅读时间，并在花园路径句、多尺度 GPT-2 与 Pythia/RoPE 架构上复现，揭示人类语言加工成本可分解为词级预测误差与局部表征连续性两个维度。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces **trajectory extrapolation error**: at each word, fit a linear trajectory to the preceding \(k\) hidden states of a transformer LM and measure Euclidean deviation from the extrapolated position. The measure is nearly orthogonal to surprisal (\(r=.044\)) yet independently predicts self-paced reading times on Natural Stories, replicates in garden-path sentences and across GPT-2 scales and Pythia/RoPE architectures, and dissociates from one-step displacement magnitude—supporting two dissociable components of human processing cost: word-level prediction error (surprisal) and sensitivity to short-horizon representational continuity (trajectory extrapolation error).

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **轨迹外推误差（trajectory extrapolation error）**：在词 \(t\)，对位置 \(t-k,\ldots,t-1\) 的隐状态向量 \(h\) 做最小二乘线性拟合，外推一步得到 \(\hat{h}_t\)，以 \(\|h_t - \hat{h}_t\|\) 量化当前词使表征偏离“既定漂移方向”的程度；高误差表示表征被强行扭转，低误差表示延续局部动量。
- 在 **Natural Stories**（约 84.5 万词观测、181 名被试）上证明该指标与 surprisal **近乎正交**（\(r=.044\)），且在控制词长、词位、前一 log RT、log 词频后，对 log 阅读时间有 **独立、可加** 的预测贡献；三分位解离分析显示“高 surprisal / 低外推误差”与“低 surprisal / 高外推误差”均显著拉长阅读时间。
- 在 **SAP Benchmark 经典花园路径子集**（24 项、2000+ 被试）上，在消歧词及两个 spillover 位置，轨迹外推误差在控制 surprisal 与词频后显著改善拟合（GPT-2 Small L6, \(w=3\)：\(\Delta\)AIC \(=+10.7\), \(p=3.7\times10^{-4}\)），而 surprisal 本身在该设定下未带来改进；歧义句在消歧点的外推误差显著高于无歧义对照。
- **位移对照（displacement control）** 表明效应不能归结为单步表征变化幅度：一步位移 \(\|h_t-h_{t-1}\|\) 与 surprisal、外推误差联合进入模型时，**符号相反**——位移预测更快阅读（\(\beta=-0.008\)），外推误差预测更慢（\(\beta=+0.001\)），说明代价来自“偏离外推轨迹”而非“表征移动本身”。
- **方向保持（direction preservation）** 分析揭示中间层（L6）轨迹结构 **严格局部**：当前步方向保持 0.44，一步后即降至 0.10（随机基线约 0.029）；最优窗口为 **3 词线性拟合**，与语言产出的短程规划假说一致。
- **跨模型与跨架构鲁棒性**：花园路径效应在 GPT-2 Small/Medium/Large 上复现；Natural Stories 上 **Pythia-160M/410M（RoPE）** 同样在外推误差加入 surprisal 后显著改善拟合（\(\Delta\)AIC \(=+67.0/+13.3\)），且与 surprisal 的相关仍约 0.046；GPT-2 与 Pythia 在位移对照上的符号模式不同，但“超越 surprisal 的轨迹信息”这一核心结论为架构无关。

## Key Contributions

- **New behavioral predictor from hidden-state dynamics:** Introduces **trajectory extrapolation error**—at word \(t\), OLS-linear fit to hidden states at \(t-k,\ldots,t-1\), extrapolate one step to \(\hat{h}_t\), and define error as \(\|h_t - \hat{h}_t\|\). High error means the representation was heading one way and the current word forced a turn; low error means local drift continued.
- **Dissociation from surprisal:** On Natural Stories (~845k word observations, 181 participants), surprisal and extrapolation error correlate at \(r=.044\). Mixed-effects models show **independent, additive** contributions to log reading time after lexical controls. A 3×3 tercile dissociation matrix confirms both off-diagonal cells elevate RTs: surprise without trajectory disruption (+0.039 log RT) and trajectory disruption without surprise (+0.008 log RT).
- **Garden-path validation:** On the SAP Benchmark Classic Garden Path subset (24 items, 2000+ participants), extrapolation error at the disambiguator and two spillover positions improves fit beyond surprisal and log frequency (GPT-2 Small, layer 6, window 3: \(\Delta\)AIC \(=+10.7\), \(p=3.7\times10^{-4}\)), while surprisal alone does not help in that analysis. Ambiguous items show substantially higher extrapolation error at the disambiguation point.
- **Displacement control rules out magnitude confound:** When both one-step displacement \(\|h_t-h_{t-1}\|\) and extrapolation error enter jointly with surprisal, coefficients have **opposite signs** in GPT-2: displacement facilitates reading (\(\beta=-0.008\), \(p=.004\)); extrapolation error impedes it (\(\beta=+0.001\), \(p=.012\)). Large representational moves help when they continue the trajectory; deviations from the extrapolated path cost extra time.
- **Strictly local trajectory structure:** Direction-preservation analysis at layer 6 shows preservation 0.44 at the current step but ~0.10 one step ahead (random baseline ~0.029 for 768-d vectors). A **3-word linear window** at an intermediate layer (layer 6 for GPT-2 Small) consistently wins over longer windows and quadratic fits.
- **Cross-scale and cross-architecture replication:** Garden-path effects replicate across GPT-2 Small (117M), Medium (345M), and Large (774M). Pythia models with RoPE positional encoding replicate the Natural Stories finding (near-identical orthogonality, large \(\Delta\)AIC gains). The GPT-2-specific opposite-sign displacement pattern does not replicate in Pythia, but extrapolation error predicting RTs beyond surprisal does.

## Methodology

**Core measure.** Let \(h_t\) be the hidden-state vector at word \(t\) in a given transformer layer. For window size \(k\), fit a linear trajectory to \(\{h_{t-k},\ldots,h_{t-1}\}\) by ordinary least squares, extrapolate to time \(t\) to obtain \(\hat{h}_t\), and define:

\[
\text{Extrapolation Error}_t = \|h_t - \hat{h}_t\|_2
\]

For subword-tokenized models, use the hidden state at the **last subword** of each word. Primary configuration: **GPT-2 (117M)**, **layer 6** (intermediate; syntactic representations), **\(k=3\)**, **linear** fit—chosen after sweeping \(k \in \{3,5,7\}\) and linear vs. quadratic fits at layers 6 and 12.

**Datasets.**

1. **Natural Stories** (Futrell et al., 2018): 10 narratives, ~10,000 words; self-paced reading from 181 participants → 845,479 observations after filtering RTs to 100–3000 ms. Stories processed through GPT-2 in overlapping chunks (1,024-token limit).
2. **Classic Garden Path subset, SAP Benchmark** (Huang et al., 2024): 24 items (MV/RR, NP/S, NP/Z types), ambiguous vs. unambiguous conditions; 2000+ participants, RTs filtered 100–5000 ms. Analysis ROI: disambiguating word + two spillover positions.

**Additional analyses.**

- **Direction preservation:** Absolute cosine similarity between fitted trajectory direction and actual displacement at current and +1/+2/+3 steps.
- **Displacement control:** One-step Euclidean displacement \(\|h_t - h_{t-1}\|\) entered jointly with extrapolation error.
- **Robustness:** GPT-2 Medium (L12 mid-layer) and Large (L18 mid-layer); Pythia-160M and Pythia-410M (RoPE) on Natural Stories subsample (100 participants).

**Statistical models.** Linear mixed-effects models predicting **log reading time**, random intercepts for participants, all continuous predictors **z-scored**. Baseline M0: word length, word position, previous log RT (Natural Stories also includes log Zipf frequency). Nested models add surprisal (M1) and/or extrapolation error (M2+). Model comparison via AIC, BIC, and likelihood-ratio tests.

## Results & Highlights

**Garden-path sentences (controlled disruption).**

| Model | Key result |
|-------|------------|
| M0 + surprisal | \(\Delta\)AIC \(=-1.9\) vs. M0 (worse; surprisal not justified after frequency control) |
| M1 + extrap. error (L6, \(w=3\)) | \(\Delta\)AIC \(=+10.7\), \(\chi^2(1)=12.7\), \(p=3.7\times10^{-4}\), \(\beta=+0.005\) |
| M1 + extrap. error (L12, \(w=5\)) | \(\Delta\)AIC \(=+56.4\) (strongest configuration in table) |
| Validation | Ambiguous > unambiguous extrapolation error at disambiguation across all layer/window configs |

**Natural Stories (naturalistic text).**

- **Orthogonality:** \(r(\text{surprisal}, \text{extrap. error}) = .044\).
- **Dissociation matrix (mean log RT, low/low baseline):**
  - High surprisal / low extrap. error: **+0.039** (\(t=21.24\), \(p<10^{-100}\)) — surprising but trajectory-continuing words (e.g., thematic content words, discourse pivots like "however").
  - Low surprisal / high extrap. error: **+0.008** (\(t=4.67\), \(p=3.0\times10^{-6}\)) — routine connectives that open new structure (e.g., "and", "that").
- **Regression:** Joint model prefers **additive** over interaction model (\(\Delta\)AIC \(=-2.0\) for simpler model).
- **Displacement control:** \(r(\text{displacement}, \text{extrap. error})=.16\); both significant with opposite signs when controlling surprisal and lexical covariates.

**Direction preservation (layer 6 vs. layer 12, \(w=3\)).**

| Layer | Current | +1 step | +2 | +3 | Random baseline |
|-------|---------|---------|-----|-----|-----------------|
| L6 | 0.44 | 0.10 | 0.08 | 0.08 | 0.029 |
| L12 | 0.61 | 0.54 | 0.54 | 0.54 | 0.029 |

At the predictive intermediate layer, direction "dies" after one word—extrapolation error captures **local positional continuity**, not long-range momentum. Surprising words show lower direction preservation (\(r=-0.10\) with surprisal at L6).

**Cross-model scale (garden path, mid-layer, \(w=3\)).**

| Model | \(\Delta\)AIC extrap / surp | \(p\) |
|-------|---------------------------|-------|
| GPT-2 Small (L6) | +10.7 | \(3.7\times10^{-4}\) |
| GPT-2 Medium (L12) | +1.6 | .058 (marginal) |
| GPT-2 Large (L18) | +13.1 | \(1.0\times10^{-4}\) |

**Cross-architecture (Natural Stories).**

| Model | Pos. encoding | \(r\) | \(\Delta\)AIC extrap/surp |
|-------|---------------|-------|---------------------------|
| GPT-2 Small | Absolute | .044 | +2.5 (full sample; note table also reports +67 for Pythia with subsample) |
| Pythia-160M | RoPE | .046 | +67.0 |
| Pythia-410M | RoPE | .047 | +13.3 |

Pythia direction preservation at mid-layer mirrors GPT-2: 0.42 → ~0.11 at +1 step.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High for the intersection of psycholinguistics and LM interpretability. Surprisal-from-LMs is well established; framing **hidden-state trajectory deviation** as a dissociable, nearly orthogonal predictor of human RTs is a fresh and conceptually clear contribution. The asymmetric logic (surprisal expected under both frameworks; trajectory adds discriminative evidence for dynamical accounts) is well argued.
- **Rigor:** Strong on Natural Stories (hundreds of thousands of observations, crossed random effects, tercile dissociation, displacement control, direction-preservation characterization). Garden-path analysis is well designed but limited to **24 items**; the author acknowledges that by-item random intercepts absorb much between-item variance at the disambiguation point.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate. Corpora and models (GPT-2, Pythia via HuggingFace ecosystem) are public, and the measure is algorithmically simple (OLS + Euclidean distance). However, **no code release** is mentioned; exact surprisal computation, chunking for 1,024 context, and layer extraction details would need replication from the paper text.
- **Significance:** High for researchers linking **LM internal geometry** to human cognition. Demonstrates that hidden states encode processing-relevant structure that **surprisal discards**, with implications for surprisal scaling paradox (Oh & Schuler, 2023b), lossy-context surprisal (Futrell et al., 2020), and evaluation of whether larger LMs better model *human* incremental processing.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - **Self-paced reading only** — no eye-tracking; cannot separate first-pass from reanalysis.
  - **Effect size modest in naturalistic text** — coefficient small; authors frame it as theoretically diagnostic rather than dominant variance explained.
  - **Abstract vs. PDF wording:** arXiv abstract mentions effect "strengthens with model scale"; PDF emphasizes replication **across** scales (Medium marginal on garden path) rather than monotonic scaling—interpret cautiously.
  - **Pythia subsample** (100 vs. 180 participants) and large \(\Delta\)AIC for Pythia-160M warrant checking sample-specific volatility.
  - **Causal direction** between prediction and trajectory sensitivity in humans remains open; authors propose matched-stimulus experiments as the cleanest next test.

## Strengths

- **Clear construct with intuitive geometry:** Trajectory extrapolation error is easy to define, visualize (Figure 1 PCA schematic), and interpret as "representational U-turn cost."
- **Strong dissociation design:** Near-zero correlation, tercile matrix, opposite-sign displacement control, and garden-path validation jointly support two-factor theory better than any single test alone.
- **Theoretically grounded model/layer choices:** GPT-2 Small, intermediate layer 6, 3-word window tied to production-planning horizon and direction-preservation decay.
- **Multi-dataset strategy:** Controlled garden paths + large naturalistic corpus addresses both mechanism illustration and generality.
- **Honest limitation discussion:** Item-count constraints, self-paced modality, surprisal scaling paradox connection, and open question of human vs. LM processing strategy.

## Limitations

- **Modality:** Self-paced reading aggregates many processes; eye-tracking would sharpen claims about first-pass trajectory sensitivity.
- **Small garden-path item set:** 24 items; crossed item random effects weaken inferential power for between-sentence trajectory differences.
- **LM as proxy for human state:** Hidden states are model representations, not measured neural states; EEG/MEG suggested as needed for mechanistic closure.
- **Local-only trajectory:** Direction dies within one step at L6; the paper explicitly does **not** claim long-range directional momentum in human comprehension.
- **No released implementation** and no pre-registered analysis plan.
- **Pythia displacement dissociation fails to replicate** — core RT prediction replicates, but the "large moves facilitate if on-trajectory" story may be geometry-dependent.

## Relevance

该论文直接落在「LLM 隐状态与可解释性」与计算心理语言学交叉区：用 Transformer 隐状态的几何轨迹（而非仅输出 surprisal）解释人类增量加工代价，为“隐状态是否编码人类可感知的加工结构”提供了强行为证据，并与 surprisal 缩放悖论、lossy-context 等议题自然衔接。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can researchers **construct minimal pairs** matched on surprisal but differing in trajectory extrapolation error (and vice versa) to obtain causal evidence independent of corpus correlates?
- Does trajectory extrapolation error predict **neural signals** (EEG/MEG N400/P600) after controlling surprisal, and at which cortical time windows?
- How does the measure behave under **instruction-tuned or larger LMs** (Llama, Mistral) where surprisal–RT alignment may differ from GPT-2 Small?
- Can **decoding or generation objectives** that penalize high extrapolation error produce text with measurably easier human reading profiles?
- Is there a principled link between **direction-preservation decay at L6** and known syntactic-probing layers—i.e., which subspaces carry trajectory-continuity vs. surprisal-relevant information?
- For deployment monitoring: could trajectory statistics on model activations serve as a **readability or coherence diagnostic** complementary to perplexity/surprisal in long-form generation?
