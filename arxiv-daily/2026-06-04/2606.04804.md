> **一句话总结：** 本文指出，在生成式先验上对硬 PDE 约束做投影/引导后验采样，因忽略 Borel–Kolmogorov 悖论下正确的 co-area（Fixman）Jacobian 因子 \([det(JJ^\top)]^{-1/2}\)，会系统性采样错误分布；作者提出测度感知的 CoCoS 约束采样器与可摊销的 CoCo-Flow，在受控与 Darcy/Burgers 逆问题中恢复与 i.i.d. 金标准后验一致的不确定性。

> **TL;DR:** Hard-constraint physics-constrained diffusion/flow-matching methods (projection, guidance) condition on a measure-zero PDE manifold but target the wrong zero-measure limit—omitting the co-area (Fixman) factor \([det(JJ^\top)]^{-1/2}\) that arises from the physically correct small-residual-noise limit. The authors formalize this bias (growing with constraint-sensitivity heterogeneity), validate it against an i.i.d. rejection arbiter (20× error without Fixman; 9× for PCFM projection), and introduce **CoCoS** (exact constrained MCMC) and **CoCo-Flow** (amortized conditional flow matching) that recover the gold-standard co-area posterior and calibrated UQ.

## 核心贡献

- **理论刻画测度偏差**：硬约束 \(c(x)=0\) 定义测度为零的可行流形 \(M\)；在残余噪声 \(\gamma\to 0\) 极限下，正确后验为 \(p_\star(x)\propto \pi(x)\,\ell(y|x)\,[det(JJ^\top)]^{-1/2}\)（\(J=\nabla c\)），而欧氏最小位移投影与无测度修正的引导法对应不同的零测度极限（命题 1–2，定理 1）。
- **量化 Fixman 缺口**：当约束 Gram 矩阵 \(G=JJ^\top\) 在流形上非常数时（PDE 逆问题中普遍），投影/引导后验与 \(p_\star\) 不可互换；敏感性 \(\det G\) 在后验上可变化 **12–33×**（d=8/16 Darcy）乃至 **五个数量级**（2D d=64）。
- **CoCoS 约束采样器**：在 \(M\) 上做切空间高斯提议、法向 Newton 回投影、带 co-area 势 \(V\) 的 Metropolis 接受与反向投影可逆性检验，使不变分布为 \(p_\star\)（算法 1，定理 2）。
- **CoCo-Flow 摊销**：离线用 CoCoS 或 co-area 重加权投影锚点蒸馏教师样本，训练条件流匹配 \(q_\phi(\theta|y)\)，测试时单次 ODE 求解；前向模拟联合训练 \((\theta,y)\) 自动测度正确（命题 4）。
- **实验验证**：i.i.d. 残余带拒绝 arbiter 为金标准；CoCoS 在受控 d=4 问题上 W1 为地板 **2.5×**；去 Fixman **21×**；PCFM 投影 **9×**；标量后验重加权更差。Darcy 逆问题 UQ：CoCoS 后验标准差误差 **1%**，PCFM **96%**，协方差 Frobenius 误差 **122%**。

## Key Contributions

- **Measure-theoretic bias formalization**: Conditioning a generative prior on hard PDE constraint \(c(x)=0\) (measure-zero manifold \(M\)) is ambiguous (Borel–Kolmogorov paradox). The physically correct small-residual-noise limit \(\gamma\to 0\) yields co-area posterior \(p_\star \propto \pi\,\ell(y|\cdot)\,[det(JJ^\top)]^{-1/2\), which projection- and guidance-based methods omit by targeting the Euclidean/perpendicular-tube limit (Thm. 1, Prop. 1–2, Cor. 1).
- **Sensitivity-heterogeneity scaling**: Bias grows with spatial variation of constraint sensitivity \(\det(JJ^\top)\); for PDE inverse problems this is generic (12–33× variation at d=8/16; >5 orders of magnitude in 2D d=64 Darcy), and vanishes only when \(\det G\) is nearly constant (mild 1D Burgers).
- **CoCoS (Constrained Co-Area Sampler)**: Exact MCMC on \(M\) with tangent Gaussian proposals, normal-direction Newton re-projection, Metropolis acceptance using Boltzmann potential \(V = -\log\pi - \log\ell - \tfrac{1}{2}\log\det G\), and reverse-projection reversibility—guaranteeing \(p_\star\) as invariant law (Alg. 1, Thm. 2).
- **CoCo-Flow amortization**: Distill measure-correct teacher samples into conditional flow matching for fast test-time inference; forward-simulation amortization \((\theta,y)\sim\) joint is automatically correct (Prop. 4), unlike post-hoc projection/guidance on a fixed prior.
- **Empirical validation**: Against i.i.d. residual-band rejection arbiter: CoCoS within **2.3×** floor (d=8 Darcy, 3 seeds); no-Fixman **8.3×**; PCFM **~36×**; guidance/soft-penalty up to **~40×**. UQ: CoCoS posterior-std error **1%** vs. PCFM **96%**; empirical density ratios follow predicted \(\propto \det(JJ^\top)\) law (Fig. 6).

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** PDE inverse problems: parameters \(\theta\) (e.g. log-permeability basis coefficients) map through differentiable solver \(G\) to field \(u\); sparse observations \(y = H(G(\theta^\star))+\eta\). Inference variables \(x\) (typically \(\theta\), optionally augmented with \(u\)) satisfy hard constraint \(c(x)=0\) defining codimension-\(m\) manifold \(M=\{x: c(x)=0\}\). A conditional diffusion/flow-matching model provides learned prior \(\pi(x)\).

**Two conditioning limits (Def. 1).**
- *Residual limit* (correct): \(\{x : \|c(x)\|\le\varepsilon\}\) with \(\varepsilon\to 0\) under Gaussian residual model \(c(x)\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\gamma I)\)—yields co-area posterior (1).
- *Euclidean limit* (biased): \(\{x : \mathrm{dist}(x,M)\le\varepsilon\}\)—Hausdorff restriction of \(\pi\) on \(M\) without Jacobian factor (Prop. 1).

**Theorem 1 (residual-noise limit).** Soft posterior \(p_\gamma \propto \pi\,\ell(y|\cdot)\exp(-\|c\|^2/2\gamma^2)\) converges weakly as \(\gamma\to 0\) to \(p_\star\) supported on \(M\) with density w.r.t. \(\mathcal{H}^{n-m}\) given by co-area formula (Federer). **Cor. 1:** Limits coincide iff \(\det G\) is \(\mathcal{H}^{n-m}\)-a.e. constant on \(M\) (e.g. affine constraints)—rare for PDEs.

**Projection & guidance bias (Prop. 2).** Minimal-displacement projection \(\Pi(x_0)=\arg\min_{x\in M}\|x-x_0\|\) (PCFM-style Gauss–Newton) transports \(\pi\) along Euclidean normals; pushforward \(\Pi_\#\pi\) differs from \(p_\star\) by factor \([\det G]^{-1/2}\). Scalar post-hoc reweighting of projected samples cannot recover the target in general (curvature-dependent distortion).

**Variational characterization (Prop. 3).** \(p_\star\) is \(\gamma\to 0\) limit of minimizers of residual free energy \(F_\gamma(q)=\mathrm{KL}(q\|\pi_y)+\frac{1}{2\gamma^2}\mathbb{E}_q\|c\|^2\)—not an ad hoc reweighting.

**CoCoS algorithm.** At \(x\in M\): (1) build tangent projector \(P=I-J^\top(JJ^\top)^{-1}J\); (2) propose \(v=PT\xi\), \(\xi\sim\mathcal{N}(0,Is)\); (3) project \(x+v\) onto \(M\) along \(J^\top\); (4) reverse-projection reversibility check; (5) Metropolis accept with \(\log r = V(x)-V(y')+(\|v\|^2-\|v'\|^2)/2s\). Only \(\log\det G\) at current/proposed points needed (no gradients through det).

**CoCo-Flow.** Option A: joint forward simulation \((\theta_i,y_i)\) with \(y_i=H(G(\theta_i))+\eta\) → conditional FM is measure-correct (Prop. 4(i)). Option B: offline CoCoS teachers + conditional FM; or co-area-reweighted flow matching on projection anchors \(w_i\propto dp_\star/d(\Pi_\#\pi)\).

**Evaluation arbiter.** i.i.d. rejection: draw \(x\sim\pi\), keep if \(\|c(x)\|<\varepsilon\); converges to \(p_\star\) as \(\varepsilon\to 0\), free of MCMC mixing pathologies (unlike small-\(\gamma\) HMC gold standard, which fails to mix in stiff regime).

**Additional findings.**
- **Paradigm coverage (Table 3):** Projection (PCFM), inference-time guidance (guided Langevin / D-Flow mechanism), and soft-penalty (\(\gamma=0.02\)) all deviate up to **~40×** floor; bias is UQ-relevant, not cosmetic W1 shift.
- **Covariance fidelity:** CoCoS Frobenius error on \(\Sigma\) **5%**; no-Fixman **10%**; PCFM **122%** (Fig. 2).
- **Sensitivity law (Fig. 6):** Density ratio of Hausdorff/PCFM samplers vs. true posterior vs. \(\det(JJ^\top)\) follows predicted \(\propto \det G\) (dashed); CoCoS flat at ratio \(\approx 1\) (0.92–1.27).
- **Mild heterogeneity:** 1D viscous Burgers—all samplers within **~2×** floor (Cor. 1: \(\det G\) nearly constant).
- **Amortization:** CoCo-Flow on d=4: **1.2–3.6×** arbiter at **~56 ms/query** vs. minutes for arbiter; 2D Darcy: training ~90 s, inference ~56 ms, **5.7×** floor on held-out \(y\).
- **Rank deficiency:** Near-singular \(G\) along weakly identified directions is correct Bayesian behavior (co-area up-weights); CoCoS regularization in proposal only, not invariant law; Tikhonov in target samples controlled relaxation \(p_\star^\epsilon\to p_\star\).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (high).** Connecting physics-constrained generative inference to the classical Borel–Kolmogorov / co-area / Fixman measure on constraint manifolds is a sharp, under-appreciated insight. While Fixman corrections are standard in constrained molecular dynamics, their absence in recent PCFM, D-Flow, FlowDPS, and related PDE generative work is well documented and now quantified. Distinguishing residual vs. Euclidean conditioning limits gives a principled vocabulary for a crowded literature.

**Rigor (strong).** The theory chain (Lemma 1 co-area → Thm. 1 residual limit → Cor. 1 Fixman gap → Prop. 2 projection bias → Thm. 2 CoCoS reversibility → Prop. 3 variational optimality → Prop. 4 amortization) is internally consistent. Experiments deliberately isolate the measure question via i.i.d. arbiter and report failures of scalar fixes and HMC baselines honestly.

**Reproducibility (moderate).** Controlled benchmarks use analytic gradients; PDE cases use differentiable finite-difference / matrix-free CG solvers with FD-verified adjoints (\(10^{-10}\) relative error). No public code release mentioned in the extracted text; hyperparameters (arbiter \(\varepsilon\), \(\gamma\), step size \(s\)) are specified in tables but full training details for learned priors \(\pi\) are abbreviated.

**Significance (high for scientific ML / UQ).** The paper directly challenges the widespread practice of reporting projected or guided samples as Bayesian posteriors. Demonstrated **96%** posterior-spread error and **122%** covariance distortion from PCFM are consequential for trust in error bars in PDE inverse problems. CoCo-Flow shows the correction can amortize to production-like latency.

**Red flags (minor):**
- Scope limited to methods claiming calibrated posterior / UQ—not all physics-informed generators
- 2D d=64 field case: per-coordinate averages diluted by prior-dominated directions; informed-subspace analysis is necessary for fair comparison
- CoCo-Flow amortization results are promising but not as extensively validated as exact CoCoS
- Computational cost of exact CoCoS may limit adoption without amortization

## Strengths

- Clear central thesis: **"satisfying the physics" ≠ "sampling the posterior"**
- Exact characterization of omitted Jacobian and when it matters (sensitivity heterogeneity)
- CoCoS with proven invariant law \(p_\star\), not another heuristic reweighting
- i.i.d. rejection arbiter methodology avoids MCMC mixing confounds
- Headline quantitative gaps (20×, 9×, 96% std error) are memorable and actionable
- Covers projection, guidance, and soft-penalty paradigms—not only PCFM
- CoCo-Flow bridges theory to fast inference with forward-simulation vs. distillation paths
- Excellent figures linking theory (Fig. 1, 6) to UQ failure modes (Figs. 2–5)

## Limitations

- **Scope:** Critique applies to post-hoc hard-constraint + posterior/UQ claims on fixed priors; soft-penalty at finite \(\gamma\) is a different target; forward-simulation FM is already correct
- **Compute:** Exact CoCoS requires MCMC per query and \(m\times m\) \(\log\det G\) (manageable for codimension 3, costly at large \(m\))
- **Assumption 1:** Full-rank Jacobian on \(M\) may fail; near-singular directions need careful interpretation (though argued as correct Bayesian up-weighting)
- **Amortization:** CoCo-Flow validated on selected benchmarks; scaling to diverse observation operators and very high-dimensional fields left as future work
- **Artifacts:** Code/data availability not stated in main text reviewed
- **Learned priors:** Experiments assume a pretrained \(\pi\); quality of \(\pi\) itself is orthogonal to the measure correction
