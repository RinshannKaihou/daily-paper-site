> **一句话总结：** 系统比较三种 QKV 投影共享变体（Q-K=V、Q=K-V、Q=K=V）及与 GQA/MQA 的组合；在 12 项任务与 300M/1.2B（10B token）语言建模上，**Q-K=V** 以 **50% KV cache 削减** 换取仅 **3.1%（300M）/ 2.48%（1.2B）** 困惑度退化，与 GQA/MQA 正交叠加可达 **87.5%–96.9%** cache 压缩；机制上 K/V 表征冗余而 Q=K 破坏因果方向性。

> **TL;DR:** This ICML 2026 study benchmarks three projection-sharing constraints—Q-K=V (shared K&V), Q=K-V (shared Q&K), and Q=K=V (single projection)—plus (X)+ 2D positional encodings for symmetric variants, across 12 synthetic/vision/NLP tasks and 300M/1.2B GPT-style LMs on 10B SlimPajama tokens. **Q-K=V** halves the KV cache (K reused as V) with only **+3.1%** (300M) / **+2.48%** (1.2B) perplexity degradation and **−0.41%** average downstream accuracy; it stacks orthogonally with GQA/MQA (**87.5%–96.9%** cache reduction). **Q=K-V** trains competitively (+4.9% PPL) but offers **zero** cache savings; **Q=K=V** collapses quality (+25.4% PPL). Analysis ties success to K–V representational redundancy (cosine 0.73) and preserved Q–K asymmetry.

## 核心贡献

- **三轴投影共享框架**：形式化并系统评估 (a) **Q-K=V**（K 与 V 合一）、(b) **Q=K-V**（Q 与 K 合一）、(c) **Q=K=V**（单投影）；对产生对称注意力图的变体引入 **(X)+** 二维正弦位置编码以恢复方向性（主要用于非因果视觉/合成任务）。
- **Q-K=V 为语言建模最优折中**：300M 模型验证困惑度 5.27 vs 基线 5.11（**+3.1%**）；1.2B 为 5.128 vs 5.004（**+2.48%**）；下游 5-shot 平均准确率仅降 **0.41%**（35.99% vs 36.40%），困惑度与任务能力部分解耦。
- **Cache 收益与参数收益解耦**：仅 **K=V** 约束在自回归推理中只需缓存 K（V 由 K 复用），实现 **50% KV cache**；**Q=K-V** 虽减少 33% 投影参数，仍须分别缓存 K 与 V，**无部署级 cache 优势**。
- **与 GQA/MQA 严格正交**：Q-K=V + GQA-4 → **87.5%** cache 削减（300M，+3.9% PPL）；Q-K=V + MQA → **96.9%**（+4.8% PPL）；1.2B 上 Q-MQA 达 **98.5%** cache 削减（+4.16% PPL），逼近理论极限。
- **跨域实证图谱**：5 项合成序列任务、6 项视觉任务（含 ViT 分类、集合异常检测、MRI 分割扩展）、2 个尺度 LLM 预训练；Q=K-V/(X)+ 在视觉常匹敌 QKV，Q=K=V 在 LM 上灾难性退化（+25.4%）。
- **机制与可复现**：训练后 QKV 模型中 K–V 余弦相似度 **0.73**、有效秩相近（687 vs 702/1024），Q–K 仅 **0.42**；A100 前向/生成基准证实理论 cache 节省转化为 **6.5–13.6%** 峰值显存与 **4.4–13.2%** 吞吐提升；代码开源于 [Brainchip-Inc/Do-Transformers-Need-3-Projections](https://github.com/Brainchip-Inc/Do-Transformers-Need-3-Projections)。

## Key Contributions

- **Unified projection-sharing taxonomy** with three hard constraints (Q-K=V, Q=K-V, Q=K=V) and optional **(X)+** 2D sinusoidal PE to break symmetry when Q=K; complements prior Borji (2023) / Kowsher et al. (2025) lines of work and contrasts with MLA-style latent compression (DeepSeek-V2).
- **Q-K=V as the Pareto-optimal projection variant for causal LMs**: 50% KV cache by storing K only; +3.1% PPL at 300M, +2.48% at 1.2B; downstream avg −0.41% on ARC/PIQA/HellaSwag/WinoGrande—practical for serving without proportional capability loss.
- **Deployment-critical insight**: parameter/FLOP savings (25–50% on attention projections, ~5–11% total MACs at 2048 ctx) are modest; **inference memory** dominates—Q=K-V is a trap (good training PPL, **0%** cache win). Example: 32k ctx × 100 users → 7 vs 4 A100 GPUs ($72k/yr savings for Q-K=V).
- **Orthogonal stacking with head sharing**: Q-GQA-4 (87.5% cache, +3.9% PPL), Q-MQA (96.9% / 98.5% at 1.2B, <5% PPL); fills gap between full QKV and GQA/MQA on efficiency–quality frontier (Appendix Figure 10).
- **12-task controlled comparison** (not SOTA chasing): synthetic (Reverse/Sort/Sub/Swap/Copy), vision (MNIST→TinyImageNet, set anomaly), LM (SlimPajama 10B, DDP on 8×A100); stable ranking 300M→1.2B with larger models slightly more robust to constraints.
- **Empirical mechanism**: K and V occupy similar subspaces; Q enforces directional QK⊤; Q=K forces symmetric KKT, breaking causality; appendix links Q=K=V collapse to linear-attention/SSM recurrence (outer-product state updates).

## Methodology

**Variants (Figure 1).** Standard attention A = Softmax(αQKᵀ)V with independent W_q, W_k, W_v. Shared forms: (1) **Q=K-V**: A = Softmax(αKKᵀ)V; symmetric; **(Q=K-V)+** adds broadcast 2D PE + 1×1 conv. (2) **Q-K=V**: A = Softmax(αQKᵀ)K with V=K. (3) **Q=K=V**: A = Softmax(αKKᵀ)K. Combined: **Q-GQA-g** (K=V within g groups), **Q-MQA** (K=V on single KV head).

**Complexity & cache (Table 1, 7).** Projection FLOPs/params: QKV 3nd²/3d² → Q=K-V & Q-K=V 2nd²/2d² → Q=K=V nd²/d². Autoregressive cache: QKV & Q=K-V store **K+V**; Q-K=V & Q=K=V store **K only** (50% KV bytes). Head-sharing baselines: GQA-4 (75%), MQA (93.8%).

**Training setup.** *Synthetic*: 5 digit-list tasks, encoder-only, varied embed dim/layers/heads/seq len, 3×2 epochs averaged. *Vision*: ViT-style on MNIST/Fashion-MNIST/CIFAR-10/100/TinyImageNet; set anomaly with ResNet34 features; segmentation extension (SETR/CvT hybrids, Appendix A.3.3). *NLP*: GPT-style decoder-only—300M (20L, d=1024, 16 heads) and 1.2B (22L, d=2048, 32 heads), vocab 50,304, SlimPajama ~10B tokens, AdamW, cosine LR, bf16 DDP on 8×A100 40GB; matched configs except attention projections.

**Evaluation.** Validation loss/PPL; KV cache MB @32k/128k; MAC breakdown; 1.2B A100 forward (batch {1,4,16}, seq {1024,2048}) and AR gen (128+128 tokens); lm-eval-harness 5-shot on five benchmarks; K–V similarity analysis on trained QKV weights.

## Results & Highlights

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **300M LM (Table 4)** | QKV val PPL **5.11**; Q-K=V **5.27** (+3.1%); Q=K-V **5.36** (+4.9%); Q=K=V **6.41** (+25.4%); GQA-4 **5.15** (+0.7%); MQA **5.19** (+1.5%) |
| **KV cache @32k** | QKV/Q=K-V **2.62 GB**; Q-K=V/Q=K=V **1.31 GB** (−50%); GQA-4 **0.66 GB**; MQA **0.16 GB**; Q-MQA **0.08 GB** |
| **1.2B LM (Table 9)** | QKV **5.004**; Q-K=V **5.128** (+2.48%); GQA-8 **5.030** (+0.52%); MQA **5.057** (+1.06%); Q-MQA **5.212** (+4.16%) |
| **Combined cache** | Q-GQA-4 **87.5%** (300M); Q-MQA **96.9%** (300M) / **98.5%** (1.2B, 88 MB cache) |
| **Downstream (1.2B, Table 11)** | Q-K=V avg **35.99%** vs QKV **36.40%** (−0.41%); Q-GQA-8 **36.72%** (beats baseline); Q-MQA **34.38%** (aggressive) |
| **Vision (Table 3)** | (Q=K-V)+ ≈ QKV on MNIST/CIFAR; Q=K=V competitive on TinyImageNet (best acc in one config); Q-K=V slightly behind |
| **Synthetic (Table 2)** | Q=K-V strong on Sort/Swap/Copy; Q=K=V weak without (X)+; (X)+ recovers Q=K=V on several tasks |
| **A100 inference (Tables 14–15)** | Q-K=V: **6.5–6.9%** mem ↓, **4.4–5.3%** tok/s ↑; Q-MQA: up to **13.6%** mem ↓, **13.2%** tok/s ↑ |
| **Long-context PPL (Table 16)** | Q-K=V degradation **5.4%→2.2%** (512→2048 tokens); rankings stable across lengths |
| **Attention MAC share @4096** | Attention **>50%** of total compute—projection savings matter more as context grows |

**Representative insights.** Q-K=V validation curves track QKV throughout 10B-token training (Appendix Figures 11–12). Perplexity gaps do not map 1:1 to downstream rank (GQA-8 better PPL than Q-K=V but similar task scores). Deployment table (Table 10): cloud quality → GQA-4; edge balanced → Q-K=V; IoT → Q-MQA.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (moderate).** Projection sharing was foreshadowed in Borji (2023) and related MLP-mixer / linear-attention literature; contribution is **systematic** cross-task characterization, **cache-centric** deployment analysis, and **orthogonality proof** vs GQA/MQA at 1.2B with wall-clock validation. Distinct from MLA (latent compression, K≠V after expand).

**Rigor (strong for an architectural study).** Matched training, multi-seed synthetic averaging, FLOP/cache accounting, downstream transfer, and mechanism probes (cosine similarity, effective rank). Gaps: no 7B+ scale; Q=V ablation omitted by design; long-context PPL table uses fixed truncation (methodology caveat); vision/NLP not jointly tuned for SOTA.

**Reproducibility (good).** Public code repo; SlimPajama + standard benchmarks; API-independent architecture change. Model checkpoints not emphasized in abstract—verify repo completeness.

**Significance (high for inference engineering).** Directly actionable for edge/on-device LLM serving and memory-bound cloud batching; challenges assumption that three independent projections are necessary; Q-K=V is a drop-in attention-block modification composable with existing GQA stacks.

**Red flags:**
- BrainChip commercial context—efficiency narrative aligns with product domain
- 25.4% Q=K=V failure mode shows constraint sensitivity; practitioners must not confuse Q=K-V (no cache win) with Q-K=V
- Relative PPL improvements at larger scale (2.48% < 3.1%) extrapolated to 7B+ without evidence

## Strengths

- Clear decision tree: when to use Q-K=V vs Q=K-V vs Q=K=V vs combined GQA/MQA (Section 3.4, Table 10)
- Separates **parameter reduction** from **KV cache reduction**—corrects common conflation
- Multi-scale LM + downstream + A100 serving benchmarks form a complete deployment story
- Mechanistic K–V redundancy analysis explains *why* Q-K=V works and *why* Q=K fails
- Comprehensive appendix (26 pages): Pareto plots, segmentation extension, linear-attention↔SSM unification
- Stable rankings 300M→1.2B increase confidence for architectural decisions before expensive runs

## Limitations

- **Scale ceiling:** 1.2B parameters only; trend toward lower degradation at larger size is hypothesized, not proven at 7B+
- **Theory:** Explanations empirical (similarity, rank, symmetry)—no formal expressivity bounds
- **Context length:** Main training/eval focused ≤2048; ultra-long (32k–128k) cache math extrapolated; Table 16 truncation methodology limits precise long-ctx PPL claims
- **Scope:** Decoder-only primary; cross-attention in encoder–decoder mentioned but not exhaustively benchmarked
- **Missing ablation:** Q=V not studied (authors argue Q not cached, different functional role)
- **Vision/LM asymmetry:** Best variant task-dependent—no single universal replacement for QKV
- **Industry dataset:** SlimPajama only for LM; no multi-domain or multilingual validation

## Relevance

若你关注 **KV cache 优化、端侧/高并发推理、或与 GQA/MQA 正交的架构改动**，本文给出可量化的投影共享方案：**Q-K=V** 是语言建模场景下最值得试的默认变体；与现有 Llama2/Mistral 式 GQA 栈叠加可进一步压榨显存。对 Transformer 结构与注意力冗余的分析也适用于模型压缩与架构搜索方向。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Does Q-K=V perplexity gap continue to shrink at 7B–70B**, making 50% cache reduction effectively free at production scale?
2. **Can learned soft tying (low-rank K–V coupling) outperform hard K=V** without full weight sharing?
3. **How does Q-K=V interact with MLA, quantization (INT4 KV), and speculative decoding**—are savings multiplicative in vLLM-style serving?
4. **Will instruction-tuning or RLHF amplify or mask** the +3% pretraining PPL gap on chat benchmarks?
5. **Does Q-K=V preserve quality on multimodal or encoder–decoder cross-attention layers** where Q/K/V roles diverge more sharply?
