> **一句话总结：** 针对分块并行 Gated DeltaNet 中严格下三角矩阵求逆这一长上下文瓶颈，本文提出**仅需矩阵乘法**的近似逆算法：低阶 Neumann 截断 + 对角掩码 + 并行残差校正，在 Snapdragon NPU 上实现最高 **5×** 单核加速、解码层开销约降 **20%**，并在 Qwen3.5 全系列上保持与 FLA 基线相当的 PPL 与下游精度（含 FP16/INT16/W4A16）。

> **TL;DR:** Chunk-wise Gated DeltaNet needs **T = (I − A)⁻¹** for strictly lower-triangular **A**; forward substitution is sequential and NPU-unfriendly, consuming **22–31%** of layer cycles. The authors replace exact inversion with **truncated Neumann series** (energy concentrates on/near the diagonal), **diagonal masking** to suppress outlier tails (critical for INT), and **parallel residual correction** **T ≈ T⁽⁰⁾ Σ Eˢ** mapped to MatMuls. Default **N = 3**, **S = 8** (k = 64); under W4A16, **N ≤ 3** avoids dynamic-range explosion from higher powers. On **Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5**: **5.2× / 4.2× / 4.6×** inverse-kernel speedup at chunk 32/64/128; layer cycles **−18.1% / −19.4% / −24.6%**. End-to-end **Qwen3.5** (0.8B–9B): matched PPL/MMLU/CSR/RWQA vs. FLA; better **RWQA** retention under aggressive quantization.

## 核心贡献

- **近似即充分（Good-Enough）的 MatMul-only 求逆**：在分块 Gated DeltaNet 中，将 **T = (I − A)⁻¹**（**A** 严格下三角）从顺序前代替换为**低阶 Neumann 展开**、**结构对角掩码**与**并行残差校正**，在浮点与整数量化下保持精度，并将主导计算映射到 NPU 友好的稠密矩阵乘。
- **理论 + 经验支撑的低阶截断**：引理表明 **Aⁿ** 仅贡献第 **n** 条次对角及以下；逆矩阵能量集中于主对角附近。全阶展开对大块不必要且高阶项在 FP16 下易溢出（图 2）；实践中 **N ≈ 30** 可达 >0.99 能量比，但低 **N** 更利于量化——故用掩码 + 残差弥补截断误差。
- **对角掩码抑制离群值**：掩码 **M⁽ᴷ⁾** 保留 **i − j ≤ K**（**K = N**）条目，剔除截断产生的大幅度伪影，将最坏情况 SNR 从 **−51.11 dB** 提升至 **47.98 dB**（FP16，表 4），对 INT8/INT16 的 min–max 标定至关重要。
- **并行残差校正消除顺序依赖**：定义 **E = I − (I − A)T⁽⁰⁾**，用 **T ≈ T⁽⁰⁾ + T⁽⁰⁾E + … + T⁽⁰⁾Eˢ** 将迭代改写为矩阵幂累加；**S = 4–8** 通常饱和，避免提高 **N** 带来的动态范围膨胀（Neumann 阶 3→4 时最大值 **978 → 16216**，图 3）。
- **低比特 INT 扩展与分块自适应**：量化下限制 **N ≤ 3** 并以残差补偿；按 chunk size 调节 **N** 与 **S**（如 k=32 用 **N=3, S=4**；k=64 用 **S=8**）。在 Qwen3.5 上验证端到端无损，W4A16 下优于 FLA 量化（尤其 RealWorldQA）。

## Key Contributions

- **Approximate-but-sufficient MatMul-only inversion** for strictly lower-triangular matrices in chunk-wise Gated DeltaNet, replacing forward substitution with truncated Neumann series, diagonal masking, and parallel residual correction.
- **Structure-aware numerical design**: leverages nilpotency (**Aᵏ = 0**), diagonal localization of Neumann terms, and masking to control worst-case error under FP16/INT quantization.
- **Hardware-efficient NPU execution**: removes loop-carried triangular-solve dependencies; exposes dense MatMuls with tunable **N** and **S**; up to **5×** single-kernel speedup and **~20%** decode-layer overhead reduction on target silicon.
- **End-to-end validation on Qwen3.5** (0.8B–9B): no PPL/MMLU/CSR regression vs. Flash Linear Attention (FLA); improved robustness under **W4A16** vs. quantized FLA.

## Methodology

**Problem setting (§2).** Gated DeltaNet (Yang et al., 2025a) uses chunk-wise parallel linear attention (Yang et al., 2024). Within each chunk of size **k**, inversion **T = (I − A)⁻¹** with **A** strictly lower triangular (**A = KKᵀ** after ℓ₂-normalized keys, **ρ(A) < 1**). FLA-style forward substitution (Eq. 2–3) needs **k − 1** sequential steps—poor NPU utilization; block-wise decomposition limits parallelism.

**Neumann exact form (§2.3).** For nilpotent **A**, **(I − A)⁻¹ = Σₙ₌₀^{k−1} Aⁿ** (finite series). Full expansion is costly and numerically risky at high **n**.

**Low-order truncation (§3.1).** Initial **T⁽⁰⁾ = Σₙ₌₀^N Aⁿ**, **N ≪ k**. Lemma 3.1: **(Aⁿ)ᵢⱼ = 0** if **i − j < n**. Lemma 3.2 bounds truncation error **‖E_N‖**. Empirically, **N ≈ 30** captures most inverse energy but FP16 overflow on high-order terms motivates lower **N** in production.

**Diagonal masking (§3.3).** **T⁽⁰⁾ ← M⁽ᴺ⁾ ⊙ T⁽⁰⁾** with **Mᵢⱼ = 1** iff **i − j ≤ N**. Separates well-resolved near-diagonal mass from heavy-tailed truncation artifacts.

**Parallel residual correction (§3.4, Algorithm 1).** **E = I − (I − A)T⁽⁰⁾**; accumulate **P ← PE**, **T ← T + P** for **s = 0 … S−1**; return **T · T⁽⁰⁾**. Reformulates sequential Richardson-style updates into MatMul-only power accumulation.

**Low-bit “good enough” (§3.5).** Under INT8/INT16, repeated quantized powers amplify dynamic range; **N ≤ 3** + residual correction balances stability, accuracy, and FLOPs. Higher **N** needs fewer **S** steps in FP but fails INT16 range (Table 5: **N = 4** unstable without enough **S**).

**Defaults.** Experiments: chunk **k = 64**, **N = 3**, **S = 8**; profiling also **k ∈ {32, 64, 128}**.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Strong **systems + numerics** contribution for linear-attention deployment—not a new model, but a principled replacement of a known hotspot (triangular inverse) with **MatMul-native** approximate algebra tuned for NPUs and quantization.
- **Rigor:** Formal lemmas on sub-diagonal support, truncation bounds, and entrywise growth; extensive ablations (mask, **N**, **S**, precision); single-kernel SNR + multi-scale E2E benchmarks.
- **Reproducibility:** Clear Algorithm 1; default hyperparameters stated; appendices for FLA comparison, quantization configs (Appendix G), and chunk/CL ablations (Appendix I). Models are public Hugging Face checkpoints.
- **Significance:** Directly relevant to **edge/mobile long-context LLMs** using Gated DeltaNet (Qwen3.5-class). `filtered_papers.json` relevance_score: **3** — low-precision matrix inversion for quantized Gated DeltaNet.
- **Caveats:**
  - **Approximate** inverse—correctness relies on structured **A** and gating/normalization assumptions.
  - **NPU-specific** gains may not transfer 1:1 to GPUs without similar MatMul dominance.
  - **20% decode overhead** claim less detailed in main text than cycle-breakdown figures.
  - Higher **N** helps FP residual budget but hurts INT—trade-off is hardware-regime dependent.

## Strengths

- **Clear bottleneck diagnosis** with measured cycle share (22–31%) motivating the problem.
- **Coherent algorithm stack**: truncation → mask → parallel residual, each ablated with SNR evidence.
- **Quantization-aware design** explicitly addresses heavy-tailed Neumann activations (Fig. 3).
- **Production-scale E2E** on full Qwen3.5 family plus aggressive **W4A16**.
- **Hardware-aligned**: eliminates sequential substitution, maximizes MatMul utilization on NPUs.

## Limitations

- Specialized to **strictly lower-triangular** chunk matrices in **Gated DeltaNet / chunk-wise linear attention**—not a general-purpose inverter.
- Approximation parameters (**N**, **S**) depend on chunk size and precision regime; optimal settings require profiling.
- Long-sequence / prefill–decode trade-offs mostly in appendices; main text emphasizes k = 64 and fixed L = 128 profiling.
- Low-precision approximation may affect reliability in edge cases—authors note responsible deployment (Impact Statement).
- Comparison centered on **FLA** forward-substitution path; fewer cross-baselines vs. other approximate solvers.

## Relevance

与 **线性注意力 / 长上下文 LLM / 端侧 NPU 推理 / 低比特量化** 高度相关：Gated DeltaNet 等架构在 Qwen3.5、KiMi 等模型中普及，分块并行求逆是实测显著的 cycle 瓶颈；本文给出可部署的 **“足够好即最优”** 方案，在几乎不损失语言建模与下游任务精度的前提下显著降低求逆与解码层开销，对关注 **移动端长上下文、W4A16 量化、Qualcomm NPU** 的工程师与研究者具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the method behave on **GPU** backends where triangular solve libraries are highly optimized—does MatMul-only still win at large batch / long context?
- Can **adaptive (N, S)** be chosen per-layer or per-token based on online SNR monitors without E2E drift?
- Does the approximation interact with **speculative decoding** or **KV compression** in Qwen3.5-style hybrid stacks?
- What is the **worst-case failure mode** when gating or normalization is miscalibrated so **ρ(A) ≥ 1**?
- Can diagonal masking + low-order Neumann generalize to other **structured triangular** solvers (e.g., Mamba/SSM chunk operators)?
- Open-source kernel integration timeline for **FLA / vLLM / llama.cpp**-class runtimes?
