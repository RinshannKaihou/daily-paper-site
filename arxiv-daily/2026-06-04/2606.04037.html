Strengths

  • Coherent three-layer framework (specify → generate → attest) with clear governance mapping (OWASP, NIST, Vietnam AI Law)
  • Rigorous comparison ladder G1–G4 isolating persona vs RAG vs structured ontology
  • Cross-model replication undermines "Claude knows regulations" confound
  • Honest hypothesis reporting (H2 fail, H1 partial, VN banking G3>RAG cautionary tale)

Limitations

  • **Ontology completeness:** Absent regulations → systematic blind spots; ongoing curation burden
  • **Judge/generator bias:** Claude Sonnet 4 fixed judge; same-author ontology/checklist; inter-rater triangulation future work
  • **Statistical vs formal assurance:** Pilot is V1 simulation; no agent satisfied θhigh; PBMC/runtime V2–V3 proposed not demonstrated at scale
  • **FDR null:** Ontology does not improve fault detection vs baselines; Claude execution FDR lowest for G4

TL;DR

Tuan & Sanyal propose a pre-deployment assurance stack for regulated enterprise LLM agents: (1) **Agent Operational Envelope** EA=⟨Π,Σ,Φ,Γ,Λ⟩ derived from industry ontologies; (2) **ontology-to-scenario generation** (Algorithm 1: regulatory SR, KPI SK, adversarial SA suites); (3) **Trust Certificate** CA=⟨EA,S,R,V,t,sig⟩ with graduated verdicts (θhigh=0.95, θlow=0.80) and infrastructure **simulation gate**. A controlled pilot (4 conditions × 5 US/VN industry-regime cells × 3 reps × 30 scenarios = 1,800; 125 primary-source regulations; 25 injected faults) plus 3-generator replication (Claude Sonnet 4, Qwen 2.5 72B, Gemma 4 26B → 5,400 total) shows G4 beats persona G2 on regulatory coverage (48.3% vs 33.1%, pc=.0006) and industry specificity (4.77/5.0, p=2×10⁻⁶), while G4 vs G1/G3 RC gains are not Bonferroni-robust, FDR is null (p=.891), and adversarial coverage is equivalent (~88–91%).
