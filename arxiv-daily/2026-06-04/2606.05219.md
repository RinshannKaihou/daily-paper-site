> **TL;DR:** This ICML 2026 paper shows that discrete Gradient Descent with large step sizes qualitatively overturns Gradient Flow predictions for multi-pathway Deep Linear Networks. While GF drives "winner-takes-all" symmetry breaking (Shi et al., 2022), large-step GD at the Edge of Stability induces pathway re-balancing: single-path minima are sharp (S_i = LПғ_{вӢҶi}^{2вҲ’2/L}), whereas equal splitting across H pathways minimizes sharpness by factor H^{2/LвҲ’1}. Training proceeds in two phasesвҖ”early symmetry breaking, then oscillation-driven signal redistribution until sharpness falls below 2/О·. A Worst-Case Return threshold О·_WCR = Оі_WCR(L)В·(2/S_1) with Оі_WCR(L) = Оҳ(log L) bounds safe learning rates; experiments extend to Tanh MLPs and heterogeneous-depth pathways.

## Key Contributions

- **Sharpness reduction via parallelism (Theorem 4.2):** On the depth-balanced SVS manifold, among all global minima with вҲ‘_h Пғ_{hi} = Пғ_{вӢҶi}, mode-wise Hessian sharpness О»_i is minimized when Пғ_{1i} = В·В·В· = Пғ_{Hi} = Пғ_{вӢҶi}/H, yielding О»_i^{min} = L H^{2/LвҲ’1} Пғ_{вӢҶi}^{2вҲ’2/L} вҖ” a factor H^{2/LвҲ’1} smaller than the single-pathway case
- **Two-phase large-step GD dynamics (Section 5):** Small О· (< 2/S_1) reproduces GF's winner-takes-all via depth-L amplification ПғМҮ_{hi}/ПғМҮ_{ki} = (Пғ_{hi}/Пғ_{ki})^{LвҲ’1}; large О· (> 2/S_1) triggers re-balancing once Пғ_{11} вүҲ Пғ_{вӢҶ1} drives О»_max past 2/О·, with EoS oscillations acting as directed drift toward flatter minima (not noise)
- **Worst-Case Return threshold (Section 6):** Reduces transient dynamics to scalar deep-chain map w_{t+1} = w_t вҲ’ О· w_t^{LвҲ’1}(w_t^L вҲ’ Пғ_{вӢҶ1}); О·_WCR(L, Пғ_{вӢҶ1}) = Оі_WCR(L)В·(2/S_1) with Оі_WCR(L) > 2 computable by bisection; grows as Оҳ(log L), widening the overshoot-but-return window relative to classical stability О·_stable = 2/S_1
- **Rank-p re-balancing window (Eq. 24):** For О· in (2/S_p, min{2/S_{p+1}, О·_max}), the top p modes re-balance while tail modes remain GF-like; О·_max = min{2/О»_1^{min}, О·_WCR} with both ceilings increasing with depth
- **Contrast with depth-wise balancing:** Pathway symmetry breaking is a spontaneous GF attractor (Shi et al., 2022; Saxe et al., 2022), unlike layer-wise imbalance that requires unbalanced initialization; discrete instability is the mechanism that counteracts this intrinsic bias
- **Heterogeneous depth (Section 5.3):** Optimal sharpness allocation favors deeper pathways beyond equal splitting; large О· overrides shallow-path kinetic dominance observed under GF
- **Nonlinear validation:** Two-pathway Tanh MLPs (L=3, H=2) show winner-takes-all at О· вүҲ 1/S_1 and balanced K_{11} вүҲ K_{21} at О· вүҲ 2/О»_1^{min}

## Methodology

**Architecture.** H parallel pathways, each with depth L_h and weights W_{hв„“} вҲҲ в„қ^{dГ—d}. End-to-end map О©_h = W_{hL_h}В·В·В·W_{h1}; total M = вҲ‘_{h=1}^H О©_h. Loss L(Оҳ) = ВҪвҖ–M вҲ’ M_вӢҶвҖ–_FВІ minimized by discrete GD W_{hв„“}^{(t+1)} = W_{hв„“}^{(t)} вҲ’ О·вҲҮ_{W_{hв„“}} L.

**SVS parametrization.** Following Ghosh et al. (2025), restrict to Singular Vector Stationary set where W_{hв„“} = Q_{h,в„“+1} ОЈ_{hв„“} Q_{hв„“}^вҠӨ with Q_{h1} = V_вӢҶ, Q_{h,L_h+1} = U_вӢҶ. GD preserves SVS; dynamics decouple across target modes via Пғ_{hi}(t) = u_{вӢҶi}^вҠӨ О©_h(t) v_{вӢҶi}, Пғ_i(t) = вҲ‘_h Пғ_{hi}(t).

**Depth-balanced manifold.** Initialization W_{hв„“}(0) = Оұ_h^{1/L_h} I_d places Оҳ(0) on manifold where Пғ_{hв„“,i} = Пғ_{hi}^{1/L_h} вҲҖв„“ (Proposition B.4). Tiny pathway scale asymmetries Оұ_1 > Оұ_2 > В·В·В· > Оұ_H trigger symmetry breaking.

**Sharpness analysis (Section 4).** At global minima on depth-balanced manifold, Hessian is block-diagonal; mode i has single eigenvalue О»_i = вҲ‘_h L_h Пғ_{hi}^{2вҲ’2/L_h}. Lagrange optimization yields equal-split minimizer (Theorem 4.2). Homogeneous depth L_h = L gives reduction factor H^{2/LвҲ’1}.

**Dynamics beyond stability (Section 5).** Single-path sharpness S_i = LПғ_{вӢҶi}^{2вҲ’2/L}; classical bound О·_stable = 2/S_1. Re-balancing mechanism: on SVS manifold, GF conserves leaf imbalance coordinate z with sharpness вҲқ вҖ–zвҖ–ВІ; large-step GD at EoS breaks conservationвҖ”residual alternates sign and вҖ–zвҖ– decays over step pairs (Appendix E), driving drift until 2/S_1 < О· < 2/О»_1^{min}.

**WCR threshold (Section 6).** When mode concentrates in one pathway, dynamics collapse to deep-chain scalar w_t with update (19). Definition 6.1: О· is two-step return-safe if all overshoots from (0, w_вӢҶ) return to (0, w_вӢҶ) after one further step. Theorem 6.2: unique Оі_WCR(L) > 2 exists; О·_WCR = Оі_WCR(L)В·(2/S_1).

## Results & Highlights

**Theorem 4.2 (sharpness):** Equal allocation Пғ_{hi} = Пғ_{вӢҶi}/H minimizes О»_i; balanced sharpness О»_1^{min} = H^{2/LвҲ’1} В· LПғ_{вӢҶ1}^{2вҲ’2/L} vs. single-path S_1 = LПғ_{вӢҶ1}^{2вҲ’2/L}.

**Figure 1 (L=20, H=2, Пғ_{вӢҶ1}=1):**
| Regime | О· condition | Outcome |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Small LR | О· < 2/S_1 | Пғ_{11} вҶ’ Пғ_{вӢҶ1}, Пғ_{21} вҶ’ 0; О»_max вүҲ S_1 |
| Large LR | О· > 2/S_1 | Two phases: symmetry breaking then re-balancing; О»_max вҶ’ 2/О· |
| At threshold | О· = 2/О»_1^{min} | Fully balanced minimum |

**Figure 2:** Beyond stability threshold, (Пғ_{11}, Пғ_{21}) bifurcate to balanced minimum with О»_max < S_1.

**Heterogeneous depth (Figure 4):** At О· = 2/О»_1^{min}, trajectories for (L_1, L_2) вҲҲ {3,5,7}ВІ converge to numerically predicted equilibrium equalizing marginal sharpness вҲӮО»_i/вҲӮПғ_{hi}, overriding shallow-path GF dominance.

**WCR scaling (Theorem 6.2, Prop 6.3):** Оі_WCR(L) = Оҳ(log L); deeper chains tolerate larger О·_WCR/О·_1 ratio (Figure 5 knife-edge at вүҲ0.97 vs. 1.03 О·_WCR).

**Rank-p window:** О· вҲҲ (2/S_p, min{2/S_{p+1}, О·_max}) re-balances top p modes; О·_max = min{2/О»_1^{min}, О·_WCR}.

**Nonlinear (Figure 3):** Tanh MLP (L=3, H=2): small О· вҶ’ K_{11} dominates; О· вүҲ 2/О»_1^{min} вҶ’ K_{11} вүҲ K_{21}.

**Warm-up insight (Section 7):** Early sharpness peak during single-path transient may exceed О·_WCR; warm-up carries trajectory safely through fragile phase before large-step re-balancing.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High for bridging multi-pathway GF symmetry breaking with discrete EoS re-balancing. The sharpnessвҖ“parallelism theorem (H^{2/LвҲ’1}) is clean and actionable. The WCR threshold provides a principled upper bound absent in prior EoS work. The pathway-vs-depth-wise balancing distinction is conceptually sharp.
- **Rigor:** Strong within stated assumptions: square matrices, SVS restriction, symmetric targets M_вӢҶ = V_вӢҶ ОЈ_вӢҶ V_вӢҶ^вҠӨ, depth-balanced initialization. Proofs are complete in appendix. Limitations are acknowledged: linearity, SVS confinement, mode-wise decoupling.
- **Reproducibility:** Good for theory (explicit equations, bisection for Оі_WCR). Numerical experiments are described with parameters (L, H, Пғ_{вӢҶ1}, Оұ_h) but no code release mentioned in the paper. Nonlinear MLP experiments follow Shi et al. (2022) setup.
- **Significance:** High for optimization theory and implicit bias research. Directly challenges GF-based architectural predictions (MoE, multi-branch networks, lottery ticket sparsity). Practical implication: large learning rates + depth + parallelism favor shared representations over specialization.
- **Red flags:** (1) Restricted to deep *linear* networks with SVS manifoldвҖ”real networks may not remain on this manifold. (2) Nonlinear validation is limited to small Tanh MLPs. (3) ICML proceedings version may differ from arXiv preprint. (4) Connection to MoE/attention stated as future work only.

## Strengths

- Clear theoretical narrative unifying sharpness geometry, GF symmetry breaking, and EoS re-balancing in one framework
- Exact sharpness reduction factor H^{2/LвҲ’1} with constructive equal-split characterization
- Mechanistic explanation of re-balancing via broken conservation law and directed вҖ–zвҖ– decay (not heuristic)
- WCR threshold with depth scaling Оҳ(log L) provides actionable learning-rate guidance
- Thoughtful contrast between pathway re-balancing (spontaneous, GF-attracted) and depth-wise balancing (initialization-dependent)
- Heterogeneous depth analysis resolves kinetic-vs-stability bias conflict with numerical verification
- Preliminary nonlinear extension suggests broader applicability
- Connects to warm-up scheduling as practical consequence of transient fragility

## Limitations

- **Linear networks only:** Core theory assumes deep linear maps; nonlinear generalization (MoE, attention) is conjectured but not proven
- **SVS manifold restriction:** Analysis assumes trajectories remain in SVS and depth-balanced sets; sign-flip failure mode explicitly breaks this
- **Symmetric target assumption:** M_вӢҶ = V_вӢҶ ОЈ_вӢҶ V_вӢҶ^вҠӨ required for depth-balanced invariance (Proposition B.4)
- **Square weight matrices:** W_{hв„“} вҲҲ в„қ^{dГ—d} for tractability; may not reflect practical architectures
- **Mode-wise independence:** Decoupled scalar dynamics per singular mode; cross-mode interactions not studied
- **Nonlinear experiments are small-scale:** Two-pathway Tanh MLP with canonical basis inputs; no large-scale or real-data validation
- **No code release** mentioned in the paper text
