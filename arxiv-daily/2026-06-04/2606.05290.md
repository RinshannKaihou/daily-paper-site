## 一句话总结

本文提出首个跨模型安全引导框架：在源 LLM 中从成对 safe/unsafe 提示学习安全方向向量，仅用良性数据对齐后迁移至异构文生图/文生视频模型，在目标侧无需接触不安全数据即可显著降低攻击成功率，且生成质量与在目标模型上直接用不安全数据学习的方向相当。

## TL;DR

Generative safety interventions are usually model-specific. This paper asks whether safety can be encoded as a **portable latent direction** shared across heterogeneous models. The authors introduce **cross-model safety steering**: (1) estimate a safety vector in a source LLM from paired safe–unsafe prompts; (2) learn a lightweight mapping between source and target representation spaces using **benign anchors only**; (3) apply the transferred direction at inference via activation steering. Evaluated on text-to-image (Flux, Qwen-Image, Z-Image-Turbo) and text-to-video (Wan2.2) with Llama3.1, Mistral, and Qwen3.5 as sources, transferred directions reduce ASR to levels comparable to—or sometimes better than—a **native target oracle** trained with unsafe target data, while preserving CLIP/FID trade-offs. A multi-vector extension enables category-selective control. Results support a modular, geometry-based view of safety grounded in shared representations rather than model-local artifacts.

## 核心贡献

1. **首个跨模型安全引导框架**：将安全表示建模为可在异构生成器间迁移的潜空间方向，而非为每个新架构单独重训或定制干预。流程为：源模型估计安全方向 → 仅用良性锚点对齐 → 推理时激活引导。

2. **严格隔离目标侧不安全数据**：对齐映射完全在良性文本锚点（WikiText、COCO、Flickr，共 27,000 条）上拟合，目标生成器从不接触 unsafe 样本，从而直接检验“安全是否可通过共享表示几何迁移”，而非依赖目标侧监督。

3. **多向量类别化扩展**：除全局单一安全方向外，按 SafeSteerDataset 的六类安全类别分别估计并迁移方向，支持更精细、可选择性的不安全内容抑制（含 oracle 类别加权）。

4. **跨模态实证验证**：在文生图（Flux1-Schnell/Dev、Qwen-Image、Z-Image-Turbo）与文生视频（Wan2.2）上，多种源 LLM（Llama3.1-8B、Mistral-7B、Qwen3.5-9B）与三种对齐方式（SVD 正交 Procrustes、Ridge、小 MLP）均显示稳定 ASR 下降；SVD 对齐在部分设置下甚至优于使用目标侧 unsafe 数据的 native oracle。

## Key Contributions

1. **First cross-model safety steering framework** that treats safety as a latent direction learned once in a source LLM and reused across heterogeneous text-to-image and text-to-video generators via lightweight representation alignment.

2. **Target-side unsafe-data-free transfer**, isolating whether safety-relevant structure is portable through shared geometry rather than requiring model-specific unsafe supervision on every new backbone.

3. **Multi-vector, category-specific steering** extending the global direction to per-category safety vectors with optional oracle weighting for finer safety–utility control.

4. **Broad empirical evidence** across diverse source–target pairs and alignment mechanisms, showing transferred directions achieve ASR/CLIP/FID trade-offs comparable to native target-side directions learned with unsafe data.

## Methodology

**Problem formulation.** Given source model \(M_s\) (LLM) and target model \(M_t\) (diffusion image/video generator with text encoder), the goal is to transfer a safety displacement from \(\mathcal{Z}_s\) to \(\mathcal{Z}_t\) without unsafe target data.

**Step 1 — Source safety direction.** From \(N=1000\) controlled safe–unsafe prompt pairs (SafeSteerDataset, six categories), compute mean-pooled final-layer activations and pairwise differences \(\Delta_{s,i} = \mu(h_s(x_i^+)) - \mu(h_s(x_i^-))\). The global direction is \(v_s = \frac{1}{N}\sum_i \Delta_{s,i}\). Category-specific directions \(v_{s,c}\) are computed analogously per class.

**Step 2 — Cross-model alignment (benign only).** A mapping \(T_{s \to t}: \mathcal{Z}_s \to \mathcal{Z}_t\) is fit on 27,000 benign anchor prompts (centered representations from both models). Three instantiations: **SVD/orthogonal Procrustes** (rigid, preserves inner products), **ridge regression** (flexible linear), **small MLP** (1024-dim, GELU, lightweight nonlinear). Train/val/test split: 80/10/10 on anchors.

**Step 3 — Magnitude calibration.** Raw transfer \(\tilde{v}_t = T_{s \to t}(v_s)\) is scaled so \(\|v_t\|_2 = \beta \|v_s\|_2\), where \(\beta\) is the median ratio of centered anchor \(\ell_2\) norms in target vs. source space—decoupling direction from arbitrary scale distortion by the alignment map.

**Step 4 — Inference steering.** Target hidden states are modified as \(\hat{h}_t(x) = h_t(x) + \alpha v_t\) (or weighted sum of category vectors), with strength \(\alpha\) swept per model (e.g., \(\alpha \in \{-1,0,1,3,5,7\}\) for most T2I; denser grid for Z-Image-Turbo). Steering is applied on the **text-conditioning encoder** (e.g., T5-XXL for Flux, Qwen2.5-VL for Qwen-Image, UMT5-XXL for Wan2.2).

**Baselines.** Unmodified generator (\(\alpha=0\)), **native target direction** (oracle, uses unsafe pairs in target encoder), random direction, string-concatenation concept steering.

**Evaluation.** Safety: Attack Success Rate (ASR) on I2P (4,703 prompts) and MMA-Diffusion (1,000 prompts) for images; T2VSafetyBench-tiny for video—using NudeNet + Q16 detectors. Utility: CLIP similarity (LAION captions) and FID vs. safe reference images.

## Results & Highlights

| Setting | Representative result |
|--------|-------------------------|
| **Flux1-Schnell** (Llama3.1 + SVD, \(\alpha=5\)) | ASR **0.307 → 0.038**; native oracle: 0.085 |
| **Flux1-Dev** (Mistral + SVD) | ASR **0.286 → 0.033**; native: 0.096 |
| **Z-Image-Turbo** (Llama + SVD) | ASR **0.304 → 0.002** (near-complete suppression; FID rises to 73.5) |
| **MMA-Diffusion** (Flux1-Dev, Llama SVD) | ASR **0.211 → 0.020**—below native oracle (0.027) |
| **Wan2.2 T2V** (Llama + SVD) | ASR **~0.75 → ~0.07** at \(\alpha=5\); CLIP-Sim relatively stable |

- **Transferred ≈ native oracle**: Cross-model directions often match or exceed target-side directions learned with unsafe data, despite zero unsafe target supervision.
- **Alignment trade-off**: **SVD** yields strongest ASR reduction but largest CLIP/FID degradation; **Ridge/MLP** offer gentler safety gains with better semantic preservation.
- **Multi-category steering**: Oracle-weighted category vectors on Flux1-Schnell achieve lower ASR at comparable CLIP-Sim vs. a single global vector.
- **Ablations**: Removing magnitude calibration consistently weakens steering; larger source LLMs (70B/27B) show similar trends to 8B sources; MLP alignment rarely beats linear maps—suggesting transferable safety structure is **largely linear**.

## Critical Assessment

**Honest evaluation**: This is a timely and well-motivated paper at the intersection of representation engineering, Platonic-representation-style alignment, and generative safety. The experimental design cleanly isolates the scientific claim—*can safety geometry transfer without target unsafe data?*—and the answer appears to be **yes, often surprisingly well**. The magnitude-calibration ablation and native-oracle baseline strengthen causal interpretation.

**Caveats worth noting:**

- **Intervention locus is narrow**: Steering is applied only to **text encoders**, not full diffusion backbones; effectiveness for UNet/DiT internal representations is untested.
- **Detector-dependent safety metrics**: ASR relies on NudeNet + Q16, which may miss nuanced harms (hate symbols, contextual violence, emerging jailbreaks) and can produce false positives/negatives.
- **Quality–safety tension is real**: Aggressive SVD steering on Z-Image-Turbo drives ASR near zero but **FID explodes** (73.5 vs. 31.7 baseline)—practical deployment would need per-model \(\alpha\) tuning and alignment choice.
- **Source supervision still required**: While the *target* avoids unsafe data, the pipeline still needs curated safe–unsafe pairs in the **source LLM** (SafeSteerDataset); this is not fully “unsafe-data-free.”
- **Linear alignment sufficiency** limits claims about deeply nonlinear cross-modal structure; adversarial or adaptive attacks against steering are not evaluated.
- **No explicit limitations section** in the main paper; generalization to closed commercial models, multi-lingual prompts, or compositional/multi-concept unsafe prompts remains open.

## Strengths

- Novel, principled framing connecting representation alignment and modular generative safety.
- Strong experimental breadth (4 T2I targets, 1 T2V target, 3 source LLMs, 3 alignments, multiple benchmarks).
- Rigorous baseline suite including native oracle, random direction, and concept-string steering.
- Benign-only target alignment is practically important (privacy, compliance, inability to collect target unsafe data).
- Multi-vector extension demonstrates finer-grained, category-aware control with clear oracle vs. uniform trade-offs.
- Transparent reporting of safety–utility Pareto curves and qualitative examples.

## Limitations

- Text-encoder-only steering; no end-to-end diffusion backbone intervention.
- Safety evaluation tied to specific NSFW detectors; no human study or red-team adaptive attacks.
- Requires source-side safe–unsafe pairs and manual \(\alpha\) per target model.
- SVD alignment can severely degrade image fidelity on sensitive models.
- Cross-modal transfer assumes sufficient shared geometry; failure modes for highly divergent encoders are not characterized.
- Paper includes explicit harmful imagery in figures (content warning in abstract).

## 相关性 (Relevance)

Highly relevant for researchers and practitioners working on:

- **Generative AI safety** (T2I, T2V): lightweight inference-time guardrails without per-model retraining.
- **Representation engineering / activation steering**: extending refusal-direction and CAA-style methods across modality boundaries.
- **Model alignment & Platonic representations**: empirical support that safety-relevant structure may be shared across LLMs and visual generators.
- **Responsible deployment**: organizations that cannot fine-tune on unsafe data for every new diffusion release may reuse a single source safety direction library.
- **Content moderation pipelines** at scale (e.g., media platforms—consistent with Amazon co-authorship).

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Backbone-level steering**: Does transferring directions into DiT/UNet intermediate layers improve safety without the FID penalties seen with aggressive text-encoder SVD steering?
2. **Adaptive attacks**: How robust are transferred directions to paraphrased jailbreaks, multilingual prompts, or optimization-based adversarial perturbations?
3. **Fully unsafe-data-free pipeline**: Can source directions be estimated from refusal logits, RLHF signals, or synthetic contrasts without explicit unsafe prompt pairs?
4. **Compositional harms**: Can multi-vector steering scale to combinatorial unsafe concepts (e.g., hate + violence) without oracle category labels at inference?
5. **Closed-model transfer**: Does the framework generalize when source/target weights are inaccessible and only API-level embeddings are available?
