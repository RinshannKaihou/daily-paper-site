> **TL;DR:** This paper reframes model collapse as a bilayer epidemic process: data corpora and AI models form coupled S/I/R compartments linked by cross-layer transmission. The authors derive $R_0$ via the Next Generation Matrix, calibrate three illustrative scenarios (all supercritical), identify detection ($\gamma_D$) as the top Sobol leverage parameter, validate mean-field ODEs with a bipartite ABM ($R^2>0.96$ on dense networks), and bridge theory with 1,280 GPT-2 fine-tuning runs showing dose-response collapse, modest source-diversity buffering only at $\alpha=1$, and negligible diversity benefit at $\alpha=0.5$.

## Key Contributions

- **Bilayer SIR/SIRS framework**: Phenomenological mean-field ODE coupling data corpora ($S_D, I_D, R_D$) and AI models ($S_M, I_M, R_M$) via cross-layer rates $\beta_D$ (modelвҶ’data) and $\beta_M$ (dataвҶ’model). The **SIRS extension** (immunity waning $\delta$) is the primary recommendation, capturing re-contamination of filtered corpora and retrained models.
- **Threshold theory**: Derives $R_0 = \sqrt{\beta_D \beta_M / [(\gamma_D+\mu_D)(\gamma_M+\mu_M)]}$ via the Next Generation Matrix; proves DFE stability iff $R_0<1$, endemic equilibrium existence when $R_0>1$, and transcritical bifurcation at $R_0=1$. SIRS shares the same $R_0$ but permits damped oscillatory endemic convergence.
- **Illustrative calibration**: Three scenarios from public AI-text prevalence and detection data yield $R_0 \in \{1.10, 2.62, 6.63\}$ (optimistic/baseline/pessimistic). Global Sobol analysis ($N=512$, 7,168 evaluations) gives $P(R_0>1)=98.2\%$ over assumed ranges; $\gamma_D$ (detection/removal) has highest total-order index $S_T=0.324$.
- **Stochastic consistency check**: Bipartite-network ABM (100 data, 50 model nodes) agrees with ODE under dense mixing ($R^2=0.976$ data, $0.968$ model at $p_{\text{edge}}=0.8$); degrades under sparsity and superspreader heterogeneity.
- **GPT-2 empirical bridge**: 192 single-chain + 1,088 matched-budget runs on WikiText-103 and Tiny Shakespeare. Dose-response perplexity growth, diversity collapse (Distinct-2: 0.68вҶ’0.38 at $\alpha=1$), borderline source-diversity attenuation at $\alpha=1$ only ($\sim$2 PPL, Cohen's $d\approx0.8$, one-sided $p=0.047$), null effect at $\alpha=0.5$.
- **Intervention analysis**: Six strategies across 135 pairwise evaluations; detection-based filtering and herd immunity dominate; practical message prioritizes reducing contamination fraction over ecosystem diversity.

## Methodology

**Conceptual mapping.** Table 1 maps epidemic compartments to the AI ecosystem: clean/contaminated/filtered corpora ($S_D, I_D, R_D$) with rates $\Lambda_D, \beta_D, \gamma_D, \mu_D$; clean-trained/contaminated/retrained models ($S_M, I_M, R_M$) with $\Lambda_M, \beta_M, \gamma_M, \mu_M$. Infection is operationalized via a synthetic-fraction threshold $\tau$; experiments use mixture fraction $\alpha$ directly.

**ODE system (Eqs. 1вҖ“6).** Six coupled equations with mass-action cross-layer terms $I_M/N_M$ and $I_D/N_D$. Standard epidemic machinery (NGM, Jacobian block analysis, Sotomayor transcritical proof) applied with numerical verification on 200 random configurations.

**SIRS extension.** Waning rates $\delta_D, \delta_M$ move $R\to S$ without altering the infected-subsystem linearization, hence **identical $R_0$ and threshold theorems**. Endemic dynamics can oscillate (verified on 50 random SIRS configs).

**Source-diversity hypothesis (auxiliary).** Effective $\beta_M^{\text{eff}}(K) = \beta_M/f(K)$ with $f(1)=1$, $f$ increasing, predicting $R_0(K) = R_0(1)/\sqrt{f(K)}$вҖ”tested empirically, not derived from base ODE.

**Calibration (Section 4).** $\beta_D \approx 0.217$ from 2023вҖ“2025 AI text prevalence regression [11, 22]; $\gamma_D=0.099$ from detection recall $\times$ deployment coverage; $\beta_M=0.340$, $\gamma_M=0.060$ from training/retraining heuristics; turnover $\mu_D=0.02$, $\mu_M=0.03$.

**ABM (Section 5).** Bipartite random graph, Bernoulli per-node infection/recovery/turnover/waning; 20 realizations per config; threshold sweep 18/20 correct.

**GPT-2 experiments (Section 6).** GPT-2 124M, 8-generation chains, $\alpha \in \{0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1\}$, 3 seeds (single-chain) / 8 seeds (diversity); fine-tune 3 epochs, lr $5\times10^{-5}$, seq len 128. Matched-budget design fixes pool $n=2000$, varies $K\in\{1,3,5\}$ at $\alpha=1$ and $K\in\{1,5\}$ at $\alpha=0.5$.

**Qualitative SIR analogy (not quantitative fit).** Contamination fraction $\alpha$ вҶ” transmission intensity; $(1-\alpha)$ real-data dilution вҶ” recovery; $\alpha=0$ control вҶ” disease-free; sustained growth at $\alpha=1$ вҶ” supercritical. ## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (moderate-to-high for framing).** Prior model-collapse work (Shumailov et al., Dohmatob et al., Gerstgrasser et al.) analyzes single-chain recursion. The bilayer cross-contamination metaphor and geometric-mean $R_0$ are a fresh systems-level vocabulary. The mathematical machinery itself is standard epidemic theory applied to a new domainвҖ”the contribution is conceptual organization and intervention structure, not new mathematics.

**Rigor (strong on theory, cautious on empirics).** ODE analysis is thorough with formal proofs and numerical checks. Calibration is explicitly illustrative with documented simplifying assumptions. GPT-2 experiments are well-designed (size-matched $\alpha=0$ control, bootstrap CIs, AIC growth-vs-plateau tests) but remain a qualitative bridge at small scale. Source-diversity effect is borderline ($p=0.047$ one-sided, $p=0.068$ two-sided) and non-monotonic ($K=3 \approx K=5$).

**Reproducibility (unclear).** Paper describes protocols in detail (Section 6, appendices) but does not mention public code/data release in the extracted text. Compute requirements are modest (GPT-2 124M) but 1,280 runs still need disclosure of infrastructure.

**Significance (moderate for AI ecosystem discourse).** Provides actionable framing: prioritize detection/filtering ($\gamma_D$, reducing $\alpha$) over relying on multi-model diversity. Timely given estimates of 74% AI-modified newly indexed web pages and 4Г— AI-text prevalence growth in top search results (2023вҖ“2025). Limited direct policy quantification until larger-scale validation and data-driven calibration.

**Red flags:**
- Phenomenological S/I/R discretization may not capture continuous contamination gradients
- Mean-field breaks under realistic web-scale heterogeneity (superspreaders, sparse graphs)
- Optimistic scenario $R_0=1.10$ is only marginally supercriticalвҖ”small parameter errors could flip subcritical
- Source-diversity "confirmation" rests on one-sided $p=0.047$ with 8 seeds
- No experiments on modern LLMs (7B+) or continual pretraining regimes

## Strengths

- First stylized mathematical framework for **ecosystem-level** cross-model synthetic data contamination (vs. single-chain collapse)
- Clean, interpretable $R_0$ with cross-layer ratio cancellation and clear intervention leverage story
- SIRS extension appropriately models re-contamination; damped oscillations predict periodic resurgence after cleanup
- Comprehensive sensitivity analysis pinpoints detection as highest-leverage parameter
- GPT-2 experiments include size-matched controls, cross-domain replication (WikiText + Shakespeare), and matched-budget source-diversity ablation
- Exceptionally honest about limitations, illustrative nature of calibration, and borderline statistical claims

## Limitations

- **Phenomenological mapping**: S/I/R compartments are coarse; $\tau$ threshold and rate parameters are not directly measured from ecosystem data
- **Mean-field validity**: ODE degrades under network sparsity, superspreaders, and active detectors ($R^2$ as low as 0.29)
- **Calibration uncertainty**: All $R_0$ values are scenario outputs conditional on assumed uniform parameter ranges, not frequentist ecosystem estimates
- **Scale gap**: GPT-2 124M only; whether dose-response and diversity patterns hold at frontier scale is open
- **No ODE fitting**: Experiments test qualitative predictions only; compartment fractions not measured
- **Source diversity**: Modest, borderline effect at $\alpha=1$ only; vanishes at realistic $\alpha=0.5$; non-monotonic in $K$
- **Low replication seeds**: 3 seeds (single-chain), 8 seeds (diversity)вҖ”underpowered for definitive claims

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Can compartment fractions ($I_D, I_M$) be directly measured** from web crawl metadata, model cards, and training-data disclosures to replace illustrative calibration?
2. **Does the bilayer $R_0$ structure hold for dense vs. proprietary data pipelines**, where corpora are no longer shared (pushing toward sparse-network ABM regimes)?
3. **Do dose-response and source-diversity patterns replicate on 7B+ models** with realistic continual-pretraining and mixed real-synthetic regimes?
4. **Can watermarking + detection be jointly modeled** as a time-varying $\gamma_D(t)$ to capture improving detector arms races?
5. **How does the SIRS oscillation prediction manifest empirically**вҖ”do periodic "contamination waves" appear in public benchmark degradation over model generations?
