Large-step GD restores symmetry that gradient flow breaks in multi-pathway networks

KEY FINDING: Gradient flow drives winner-takes-all across H parallel pathways—but discrete GD with η > 2/S₁ enters Edge of Stability, and oscillation residuals systematically re-balance signal until sharpness settles at 2/η, favoring shared representations over persistent single-pathway dominance.

Training phases

Phase 1: GF-like depth-L symmetry breaking. Phase 2: EoS oscillations → directed ‖z‖ decay → pathway re-balancing.

WCR threshold

ηWCR = γWCR(L)·(2/S₁), γWCR = Θ(log L) > 2 — deeper nets tolerate larger re-balancing rates.

Critical edge

Deep linear + SVS manifold only; square weights; symmetric M⋆; small Tanh MLP validation—no code release.

Critical assessment

Strengths

  • Exact H2/L−1 factor with constructive equal-split proof
  • Mechanistic ‖z‖ decay—not heuristic EoS narrative
  • Unified sharpness + GF breaking + discrete re-balancing
  • Actionable ηWCR with Θ(log L) depth scaling
  • Sharp pathway vs. depth-wise balancing distinction

Novelty & significance (4/5 relevance)

  • Challenges GF-based MoE/multi-branch specialization lore
  • Large LR + depth + parallelism → shared reps
  • ICML 2026 proceedings (PMLR 306); rigorous appendices B–F
  • Connects Shi et al. (2022) + Ghosh et al. (2025) EoS

Critical edge — read before citing

  • Deep linear networks on SVS manifold—trajectories may leave SVS (sign-flip failure)
  • Symmetric target M⋆ = V⋆Σ⋆V⋆⊤ required for depth-balanced invariance
  • Square Whℓ ∈ ℝd×d; mode-wise decoupling—no cross-mode coupling
  • Nonlinear validation: small Tanh MLP only; no large-scale or real-data runs
  • No code release; ICML proceedings may differ from arXiv preprint

Open questions

Does re-balancing persist in MoE and multi-head attention with routing?
Discrete routing adds structure beyond parallel DLN pathways—central open extension named in the paper.
Can ηWCR be estimated online during the symmetry-breaking transient?
Adaptive schedules could gate the switch to large-step re-balancing after warm-up.
How does SGD noise interact with directed ‖z‖ decay at EoS?
Accelerate re-balancing or disrupt the oscillation mechanism—unstudied in current theory.
Link to lottery-ticket subnetworks under large-step training?
Balanced multi-pathway minima vs. sparse specialized tickets—conceptual bridge for future work.
