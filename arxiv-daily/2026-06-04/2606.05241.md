> **一句话总结：** 本文系统定义并检测深度研究智能体在推理时通过网页检索造成的「搜索时污染」（STC），将其细分为基准元数据泄露、问题上下文泄露与显式答案泄露三级；在六个医学 QA 基准上评估 Tongyi Deep Research 等系统，发现 STC 普遍且可使 HLE 等子集性能虚高约 4%，并倡导隔离沙箱、透明搜索轨迹与受控基准访问。

> **TL;DR:** Deep research agents that search the web during inference can retrieve public benchmark metadata, question context, or ground-truth answers—Search-Time Contamination (STC)—inflating measured reasoning performance. The authors define a three-level taxonomy (BML → QCL → EAL), deploy regex URL matching, lexical overlap, and LLM-as-Judge detectors, and evaluate six medical benchmarks with Tongyi Deep Research (plus Gemini, Step, Valyu). STC is widespread (e.g., ~25% answer-retrievable on MedMCQA; up to 4% score inflation on HLE subsets); EAL subsets reach 95–100% accuracy with hazard ratios 2.2–8.9× for correct prediction. They advocate isolated knowledge sandboxes, transparent search trajectories, and controlled benchmark indexing.

## 核心贡献

- **形式化 STC 三级分类体系**：按严重程度递增定义 Benchmark Metadata Leakage（BML）、Question-Context Leakage（QCL）、Explicit Answer Leakage（EAL），并在单条搜索轨迹中允许多类型共存
- **可操作的检测流水线**：BML 用正则 URL 匹配（HuggingFace/GitHub/Quizlet/Chegg 等 + 数据集关键词）；QCL 用最长公共子串归一化重叠度；EAL 用 DeepSeek V4 Pro 作 LLM-as-Judge（Medbullets5op：83.3% recall / 100% precision；MedQA：94.85% precision）
- **双层评估框架**：问题级按 STC 子集对比准确率；轮次级分析预测偏移、时变 Cox 生存模型（STC 作为时变协变量）及 Kaplan–Meier 级联升级曲线
- **大规模实证**：在 MedQA、MMLU、MedMCQA、MedXpertQA、HLE-149、Medbullets5op 六个医学基准上，以 Tongyi Deep Research 为主实验（可观测完整 search/visit 轨迹），并验证 Gemini / Step / Valyu 的泛化性
- **量化性能虚高**：STC 可导致最高约 **4%** 的性能膨胀（HLE 生物/化学子集）；MedMCQA 近 **四分之一** 题目可从网上检索到答案；EAL 触发后准确率常 **>95%** 且快速收敛
- **揭示 BML 单独不足以解释增益**：BML 子集在四基准上更高、两基准上更低，与 Han et al. (2025) 仅做仓库 URL 匹配的结论不同——需细粒度到 QCL/EAL 才能判断真实污染影响
- **跨系统 STC 风险**：Gemini Deep Research 在 100 道 MedQA 上 **60%** 答案泄露率；Valyu 在 MedQA 为 0% 但在 PubMedQA 达 **78%**（65/100），说明风险取决于检索语料与基准来源的重叠而非仅「开放网页 vs. 受限源」
- **污染感知评估倡议**：隔离知识沙箱（如 ToolUniverse）、公开完整搜索查询/URL/中间证据、对基准实施门禁与反爬虫以防止被搜索引擎索引

## Key Contributions

- **Three-level STC taxonomy:** Benchmark Metadata Leakage (search-step URL exposure), Question-Context Leakage (visit-step question overlap without answer), and Explicit Answer Leakage (both query and label retrieved)—with increasing severity and possible co-occurrence in one trajectory (Figure 1, Chegg example)
- **Detection algorithms:** Regex URL templates for BML (Appendix G: data hosts, exam-prep sites, dataset keywords); normalized longest-common-substring for QCL; DeepSeek V4 Pro LLM-as-Judge for EAL with validated precision/recall on Medbullets5op and MedQA
- **Dual evaluation protocol:** Question-level splits by BML/QCL/EAL co-occurrence; turn-level prediction-shift analysis and time-varying Cox proportional hazards modeling STC events as covariates affecting time-to-correct prediction
- **Empirical scope:** Six medical QA benchmarks; primary agent Tongyi Deep Research with full trace observability; base Qwen3-30B-A3B and commercial agents (Gemini, Step, Valyu) for generalizability (~$858 experiment cost)
- **Performance inflation quantified:** Up to 4% inflation on HLE biological/chemical subsets; MedMCQA ~25% questions answer-retrievable from web; EAL-triggered subsets often 95–100% accuracy vs. dataset averages
- **Fine-grained insight beyond URL matching:** BML alone does not consistently inflate scores (worse on 2/6 benchmarks); EAL subsets drive the largest gains (e.g., 100% on HLE-149 BML+EAL, 73.91% on MedXpertQA BML+EAL)
- **Cross-agent leakage audit:** Gemini 60% EAL rate on 100 MedQA samples; Step 9%; Valyu 0% on MedQA but 78% on PubMedQA—corpus–benchmark overlap determines risk
- **Recommendations:** Isolated sandboxes, transparent search trajectories for auditors, and controlled benchmark access (gated repos, no search-engine indexing)

## Methodology

**Agent formalization.** Following ReAct (Yao et al., 2023), trajectory H_T = {q, (τ_0, a_0, o_0), …, (τ_T, a_T)} with reasoning τ_t, action a_t (e.g., web search query), observation o_t; policy (τ_t, a_t) ~ π(·|H_{t-1}). Web search comprises Search (ranked URLs + snippets) and Visit (page read/summarize).

**STC definition (extending Han et al., 2025).** STC occurs when retrieval returns benchmark-derived artifacts, letting agents bypass intended reasoning. Prior URL–HuggingFace matching misses exam sites, Q&A platforms, and visit-stage answer exposure; this work operates at step-level granularity.

**Detection.**
- **BML:** Regex over (1) common artifact hosts (HuggingFace, GitHub, Quizlet, Chegg, Scribd, CourseHero, etc.) and (2) benchmark-specific patterns (medqa, medmcqa, HLE, MedBullets, arxiv dataset papers).
- **QCL:** Longest common substring between question and retrieved content, normalized by question length; cases with EAL excluded.
- **EAL:** LLM-as-Judge (DeepSeek V4 Pro) determines whether retrieved content provides direct answer evidence (prompt in Appendix Figure 3).

**Evaluation design.**
- **Question-level:** Compare accuracy on BML vs. non-BML splits; within BML, further split by QCL/EAL presence (four subgroups + all-types).
- **Turn-level:** Prediction shift = accuracy at turn t vs. t+1 after STC at t; missing intermediate predictions counted incorrect. Time-varying Cox model estimates hazard ratios for reaching correct prediction after each STC type. Kaplan–Meier curves reset at first BML to measure turns until QCL or EAL; Cox models QCL → EAL escalation.

**Experimental setup.** Six medical benchmarks (reasoning-intensive, widely mirrored online). MMLU/MedMCQA: exclude shortest half of questions (factual-recall bias, Thapa et al., 2025). HLE open-ended items judged by LLM-as-Judge per HLE protocol. Agents: Tongyi Deep Research (main), Qwen3-30B-A3B base, Tongyi without web search, Gemini/Step/Valyu on 100 MedQA samples. Results released on anonymous GitHub.

## Results & Highlights

**Table 1 — STC prevalence (Tongyi, turn/question level):** All six datasets show non-trivial BML/QCL/EAL counts. MedMCQA highest average turns (15.87); MedMCQA has 569 QCL and 934 EAL turn-level events; MedQA 834 questions without STC vs. 44 with BML at question level. Older benchmarks (MedQA, MedMCQA, MMLU) show stronger signals; recent MedXpertQA and HLE-149 still leak via publications/forums.

**Table 2 — Tongyi Deep Research question-level accuracy (selected):**

| Setting | MedQA | MedMCQA | MMLU | MedXpertQA | HLE-149 | Medbullets5op |
|--------|-------|---------|------|------------|---------|---------------|
| Qwen3-30B-A3B (base) | 83.58% | 70.27% | 89.72% | 21.80% | 12.75% | 67.45% |
| Tongyi (no search) | 89.00% | 72.47% | 91.96% | 28.45% | 15.44% | 73.15% |
| Tongyi (with search) | 91.28% | 87.34% | 94.86% | 40.61% | 24.83% | 76.17% |
| BML + EAL subset | 95.65% | 97.53% | 96.88% | 72.73% | 100% | 100% |
| BML + QCL + EAL | 100% | 90.16% | 100% | 100% | — | — |

Web search improves over no-search Tongyi on all benchmarks, but fine-grained splits show gains concentrate in EAL-co-occurring subsets.

**Table 3 — Turn-level prediction shift (Before → After STC):**
- **EAL:** MedQA 7.69%→89.74%; MedMCQA 19.25%→79.45%; Medbullets5op 0%→80%; HLE-149 20%→100%
- **BML:** Generally decreases accuracy (e.g., MedQA 48.44%→30.94%)—metadata alone often misleads
- **QCL:** Mixed; MedQA 4.55%→22.73%

**Table 4 — Cox hazard ratios (STC → correct prediction):**
- **EAL:** HR 2.21 (MMLU) to 8.92 (HLE-149), all >1, significant on most datasets
- **BML:** HR <1 on five benchmarks (e.g., MedMCQA 0.45, MedQA 0.62)—metadata leakage alone insufficient
- **QCL:** Mixed, often HR <1 (negative association on several sets)

**Table 5 — QCL → EAL escalation:** HR 2.50–6.74 (significant except HLE-149 wide CI). Figure 2: after first BML, escalation to EAL more likely than to QCL (especially MMLU, MedMCQA).

**Table 6 — Commercial agents (100 MedQA, manual EAL verification):**

| Agent | Accuracy (search on) | Leakage rate |
|-------|----------------------|--------------|
| Gemini Deep Research | 99% | **60%** |
| Step Deep Research | 97% | 9% |
| Valyu Deep Research | 93% | 0% (MedQA); **78%** on PubMedQA |

**Qualitative finding (Figure 1):** Tongyi retrieves Chegg page with explicit answer; agent abandons medical reasoning, adopts leaked label (option ordering shuffle can cause 1–2 turn correction delay).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High for operationalizing a fine-grained STC taxonomy with automated detectors and dual-level statistical evaluation (Cox + KM) beyond prior HuggingFace URL matching. The Chegg/EAL case study is compelling and reproducible in released traces.
- **Rigor:** Solid detection validation (precision/recall on subsets); survival analysis appropriately models time-to-correct after contamination events. Assumption of comparable difficulty across STC splits is stated but not independently verified. BML regex recall acknowledged as incomplete.
- **Reproducibility:** Good—released GitHub with trajectories, explicit URL templates (Appendix G), agent configurations in Appendix C, cost table. Anonymous repo may limit long-term citation. Commercial agents evaluated on only 100 samples with opaque internals.
- **Significance:** High for anyone evaluating web-enabled LLM agents, medical QA leaderboards, or "deep research" product claims. Directly challenges interpreting search-augmented benchmark gains as pure reasoning improvements. Relevance score 4/5 in daily pipeline (reproducible agent/benchmark evaluation).
- **Red flags:** (1) Scope limited to medical/clinical benchmarks—general QA STC claimed but not fully evaluated here. (2) Only three commercial agents at small sample size. (3) EAL detector depends on proprietary DeepSeek V4 Pro judge. (4) Dual-use ethics noted—methodology could guide deliberate leakage (authors argue transparency outweighs risk).

## Strengths

- Clear, escalating three-type taxonomy aligned with search vs. visit pipeline stages
- Practically deployable detectors with reported precision/recall, not only conceptual definitions
- Question-level and turn-level analyses complement each other (static splits + dynamic prediction shift + survival modeling)
- Full-trace study on Tongyi Deep Research enables causal narrative (Figure 1) impossible with summary-only APIs
- Important counter-result: BML alone often hurts or is neutral—refines over-simplified "found benchmark URL = inflated score" narrative
- Cross-agent study surfaces Gemini 60% leakage despite high accuracy—policy-relevant for benchmark integrity
- Valyu PubMedQA result elegantly shows corpus–benchmark overlap as root cause, not open-web dichotomy
- Actionable recommendations (sandbox, trajectory transparency, benchmark access control) with ToolUniverse citation

## Limitations

- **BML detection recall:** Predefined URL patterns miss unknown redistribution sites → underestimated BML
- **Domain scope:** Experiments only on clinical/medical benchmarks; broader QA, math, coding benchmarks need extension
- **Commercial agent coverage:** Three systems, 100 MedQA samples each, no internal trajectories for proprietary agents
- **Difficulty-matched splits:** Performance gaps attributed to STC assume comparable question difficulty across subsets without formal matching test
- **Judge dependency:** EAL detection relies on DeepSeek V4 Pro; errors propagate to contamination labels
- **Cost barrier:** ~$858 total limits scale; MedQA EAL human post-check reports precision only (no full recall)
- **Ethics / dual-use:** Published URL patterns and detection logic could inform adversarial benchmark seeding (authors discuss mitigations)

## Relevance

该论文与 **可搜索/深度研究型 LLM 智能体评估、基准可复现性、Agent-as-a-Judge 与医学 QA 排行榜** 高度相关（相关性 4/5）。若团队使用带网页检索的 agent 在公开基准上报告 SOTA，本文表明需审计搜索轨迹中的 STC，否则性能提升可能部分来自答案泄露而非推理能力。对构建 MCP/工具调用 agent、设计私有测试集或沙箱化检索环境的工程师具有直接方法论参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How much does STC inflate scores on non-medical benchmarks (GPQA, BrowseComp, Humanity's Last Exam full set) with the same detectors?
- Can difficulty-matched or propensity-score stratification tighten causal claims about STC-induced inflation?
- Do isolated sandboxes (ToolUniverse) eliminate STC while preserving the reasoning benefits of retrieval on legitimately private evaluation sets?
- How should leaderboard protocols require search-trajectory logging and automated STC audits before accepting submissions?
- Can lightweight on-device detectors replace expensive LLM-as-Judge for EAL at scale without sacrificing 94%+ precision?
- What governance mechanisms (robots.txt, gated HuggingFace, DMCA) most effectively prevent benchmark indexing without harming legitimate medical education resources?
- Does fine-tuning for deep research (Tongyi vs. Qwen3 base) increase propensity to exploit leaked answers once EAL occurs, beyond base model behavior?
