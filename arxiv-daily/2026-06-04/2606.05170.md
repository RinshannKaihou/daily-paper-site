> **一句话总结：** 作者提出 ERRORQUAKE-10K 基准，用 0–4 连续严重度刻度与 Gutenberg–Richter 尾指数 *b* 刻画 21 个开源模型的错误分布；在准确率几乎相同时，85/210 对模型的 *b* 95% 置信区间仍不相交，说明“错多少”无法替代“错多狠”。

> **TL;DR:** ERRORQUAKE introduces a 10K-query, 8-domain benchmark that scores LLM errors on a continuous 0–4 severity scale and summarizes tail risk with a Gutenberg–Richter *b* index. At matched error rates, 85/210 model pairs have statistically distinct severity tails (e.g., DeepSeek-V3.2 vs. Ministral-14B at ε≈0.586 but Δ*b*≈0.47), backed by human validation, a non-reducibility theorem, and a mechanism taxonomy showing larger models shift failures from retrieval slips toward fabrications.

## 核心贡献

- **ERRORQUAKE-10K 基准**：10,000 条查询，覆盖 8 个领域（BIO、LAW、HIST、GEO、SCI、TECH、FIN、CULT）与 5 个难度层级（T1–T5），对 21 个开源指令微调模型逐条评分。
- **连续严重度刻度**：0.0（正确）至 4.0（重大捏造），以 0.5 为步长的 9 级刻度；区分“日期差一年”与“虚构法院判决”等量级差异。
- **Gutenberg–Richter 尾指数 *b***：借鉴地震学，用 *b* 描述错误严重度的上尾斜率；*b* 越小表示罕见错误越“灾难性”。
- **匹配准确率下的判别力**：在人工共识评分下，210 对模型中有 **85 对**在 |Δε|<0.05 时仍具有不相交的 *b* 95% 置信区间（LLM 评判基线仅 31 对）。
- **不可约性定理**：证明严重度剖面与错误率 ε 在信息论上非冗余；I(*b*; model | ε)≈1.56 bits，仅 35.6% 的跨模型 *b* 方差可由 ε 解释。
- **机制分类学**：低严重度错误以检索失败为主（71%），高严重度以捏造为主（39%）；大模型显著更多捏造（p<0.0001）。
- **开源资源**：基准、评分工具链、Croissant 元数据与 HuggingFace 发布，支持复现。

## Key Contributions

- **ERRORQUAKE-10K benchmark**: 10,000 stratified factual queries across 8 domains and 5 difficulty tiers, evaluated on 21 open-weight instruction-tuned models (~3B–37B active parameters).
- **Continuous severity scale (0–4)**: Nine-level, 0.5-spaced rubric from correct responses to major fabrication, designed to capture orders-of-magnitude differences invisible to binary error counts.
- **Gutenberg–Richter severity index *b***: Upper-tail slope parameter analogous to seismic magnitude-frequency laws; lower *b* implies heavier catastrophic tails at matched accuracy.
- **Matched-accuracy discrimination**: 85/210 model pairs have disjoint 95% bootstrap CIs on *b* when |Δε|<0.05 under human-consensus scoring—2.7× more pairs than LLM-judge-only scoring (31 pairs).
- **Non-Reducibility Theorem**: Formal proof that severity profile and error rate carry non-redundant information; empirical mutual information I(*b*; model | ε)≈1.56 bits with R²=0.356 for ε explaining *b* variance.
- **Severity mechanism taxonomy**: Six categories (retrieval, fabrication, amplification, reasoning, format, metacognitive); severity shifts categorically from retrieval to fabrication as magnitude increases.
- **Open release**: Benchmark, dual-judge scoring pipeline, human-validation subset, analysis code, and Croissant metadata for replication.

## Methodology

**Benchmark construction.** ERRORQUAKE-10K comprises 1,250 queries per domain, each stratified into five 100-query difficulty tiers. T1–T2 are straightforward factual lookups; T4–T5 include trap questions designed to elicit confident fabrication. Queries were LLM-generated and tier-calibrated (~6% mis-tiered cells regenerated).

**Severity scoring.** Each model response receives a continuous score on {0.0, 0.5, …, 4.0}: 0.0 = correct; 0.5–1.0 = minor imprecision; 1.5–2.0 = moderate errors; 2.5–3.0 = substantial misleading errors; 3.5–4.0 = fabrication. The scale is intentionally dense near the “harmless slip vs. consequential failure” boundary.

**Dual-judge pipeline.** Two judges from an 8-model round-robin pool (excluding self-judging) score each response. Final score = mean when judges agree within 1.0; otherwise median-of-three with tiebreaker. Pooled ICC(2,1)=0.374 on the averaged scores; ICC(2,k=2)=0.545.

**Distribution fitting.** Five candidate families (power law, truncated power law, exponential, stretched exponential, lognormal) are fit via MLE with discreteness correction; BIC-best family selected, Vuong test at p<0.05 for decisiveness. 17/21 models are non-exponential by BIC; 17/21 Vuong-decisive.

***b* estimation.** Gutenberg–Richter relation log₁₀ N(M≥m) = a − b(m − m_min). *b* estimated via Aki (1965) MLE with δ=0.5 bin width; m_min chosen by minimizing KS distance to exponential tail (≥30 events above threshold). 95% CIs from 2,000 bootstrap resamples.

**Human validation.** 519 items, 3 expert raters, blind to model/judge scores, stratified across 15 models and 5 severity bands. ICC(2,k=3)=0.85; human–judge rank correlation ρ=0.89; human overcall at score 2.0 = 13.7% vs. 33.5% for LLM judges.

**Theoretical framework.** Theorem 1 (Non-Reducibility): models can share arbitrary ε while differing arbitrarily in *b*. Proposition 2 (Resolution Bound): minimum detectable Δ*b*≈0.253 given median SE(*b̂*)=0.064.

## Results & Highlights

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Headline discriminator** | 85/210 pairs with disjoint 95% *b* CIs at \|Δε\|<0.05 (human-consensus); e.g., DeepSeek-V3.2 (ε=0.586, *b*=0.655) vs. Ministral-14B (ε=0.586, *b*=1.122), Δ*b*=0.47 |
| **Distribution characterisation** | 17/21 non-exponential best-fit; 13 stretched exponential, 4 truncated power law, 4 exponential; zero pure power-law fits |
| **Dense scaling** | ρₛ(log₁₀ params, *b*) = −0.562 (p=0.006, n=14) on judge data; −0.86 on human-validated data (n=11)—larger models have heavier upper tails |
| **Catastrophe prediction (Exp. 3)** | Pre-registered ρₛ≥0.75 **fails** (ρₛ=0.443, p=0.044); rank-significant but magnitude calibration fails (only 4/21 within 1.5× at M≥3.0) |
| **Domain variation** | Friedman test significant (χ²=15.94, p=0.026) but Kendall W=0.108—domain rankings are model-idiosyncratic |
| **Mechanism taxonomy** | Low severity: 71% retrieval; high severity: 39% fabrication; small models (3–9B) 44.7% retrieval vs. large (≥24B) 29.2% fabrication |
| **Deployment risk** | Models with matched ε diverge ~20× in expected M≥3.0 events per million queries (e.g., Gemma-3-4B ~63K vs. Qwen3-Next-80B ~3.3K) |

**Robustness checks.** Cross-domain jackknife: 25–41 disjoint-CI pairs per domain drop (all ≥3). Judge-aggregation alternatives: 7–58 pairs depending on rule. Exceedance threshold sweep T=30→200: discriminator stays ≥10 pairs. Sign flip on scaling when m_min fixed at bulk (ρₛ=+0.837) vs. upper-tail selector (ρₛ=−0.562)—both regimes are real but measure different tail properties.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (high).** The core insight—that matched error rates can mask order-of-magnitude differences in catastrophic failure rates—is well-motivated and empirically demonstrated at scale. Borrowing Gutenberg–Richter *b* from seismology for LLM error tails is a fresh framing, though related work (Asgari et al., 2025) has argued for severity-aware evaluation with coarser bins. The Non-Reducibility Theorem adds formal weight beyond anecdotal examples.

**Rigor (mixed-to-strong).** Strengths include pre-registration, bootstrap CIs, Vuong tests, cross-domain jackknife, leave-one-out scaling checks, and honest reporting of failed criteria (Exp. 3, S1). Weaknesses: LLM judges overcall 33.5% at severity 2.0; dual-judge ICC(2,1)=0.374 is only fair–moderate; secondary judge failures on small models introduce non-random missingness; all 10K queries are LLM-generated (ecological validity concern); scaling sign flips depending on m_min choice require careful interpretation.

**Reproducibility (good).** Benchmark, scoring pipeline, rubric anchors, analysis scripts, and saved outputs are released. However, inference ran on third-party GPU infrastructure without disclosed hardware/runtime, and three large models were excluded after rate-limit exhaustion.

**Significance (moderate-to-high for evaluation community).** Practical recommendation—report *b* alongside ε—is actionable and supported by deployment risk tables showing ~20× spread in catastrophic events at matched accuracy. Scope is limited to 21 open-weight instruction-tuned models (3–37B active); no proprietary frontier or reasoning models. The paper explicitly positions itself as a diagnostic tool, not a leaderboard.

**Red flags:**
- LLM-generated benchmark queries may not reflect real user failure modes
- Judge overcalling inflates absolute severity counts (~5× at M≥2.5) though ranking claims are defended
- Exp. 3 catastrophe prediction fails magnitude calibration—*b* is not a reliable extrapolator across difficulty tiers
- DeepSeek v3.1→v3.2 version bump shows no significant *b* change despite being a natural test case
- Dense scaling correlation is sensitive to m_min and covariate residualisation (partial ρ drops to −0.20, p=0.31)
- Single-author independent work warrants extra scrutiny on statistical claims, though pre-registration and open data mitigate this

## Strengths

- Compelling motivating example: identical ε=0.586 but Δ*b*=0.47 between DeepSeek-V3.2 and Ministral-14B
- Large-scale evaluation (10K queries × 21 models) with systematic domain/tier stratification
- Human validation study (519 items, ICC=0.85) substantially outperforms LLM-judge-only discrimination (85 vs. 31 pairs)
- Formal Non-Reducibility Theorem with empirical mutual-information confirmation
- Mechanism taxonomy connects tail heaviness to fabrication vs. retrieval composition
- Exceptionally transparent about limitations, failed pre-registered criteria, and misuse risks
- Full open release with reproduction guide and Croissant metadata

## Limitations

- **Judge reliability:** 33.5% overcall at score 2.0; verbose/hedged correct answers systematically penalised; 9-point scale is load-bearing (coarsening destroys ranking stability)
- **Model coverage:** 21 open-weight models only; LLaMA-3.1-405B, GPT-OSS-120B, MiniMax-M2.5 excluded; no proprietary frontier or reasoning models; MoE scaling underpowered (n=7, p=0.16)
- **Query provenance:** All 10K queries LLM-generated, not human-authored—may bias difficulty and phrasing
- **Prediction failure:** Cannot reliably predict catastrophic counts from easy-tier *b* (only rank signal, not magnitude)
- **Scaling ambiguity:** Negative correlation between size and *b* is upper-tail-specific; bulk decay moves opposite direction
- **Human validation scope:** ~35 items per model—adequate for ICC/ranking but limited per-model *b* precision
- **Compute opacity:** Third-party API hides GPU types and wall-clock runtime

## Relevance

该论文直接回应“仅靠错误率/幻觉率选模型”的评估盲区，与关注 LLM 事实性、风险感知部署及重尾失败模式的读者高度相关；若你关心开源模型在医疗/法律等高后果场景下的尾部风险，其 *b* 指标与机制分类学提供了比二元准确率更细粒度的选型依据。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **Does the matched-accuracy *b* discriminator hold on proprietary frontier models** (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini) where error rates are lower and tail events rarer?
2. **Can human-authored or domain-expert queries change the severity distribution shape**, or is the heavy-tail finding an artifact of LLM-generated trap questions?
3. **Would calibration-aware decoding or RLHF on severity-weighted rewards shift *b* independently of ε**, and could models be optimised to hide catastrophic tails while maintaining low error rates?
4. **How does the fabrication-vs-retrieval mechanism shift interact with retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)**—does grounding reduce high-severity fabrication without changing ε?
5. **Can the Gutenberg–Richter extrapolation be salvaged with difficulty-conditioned *b* tiers**, given that rank prediction works but magnitude calibration fails due to level shifts rather than slope mismatches?
