Trajectory extrapolation error predicts reading time beyond surprisal

KEY FINDING: Linear extrapolation of hidden-state trajectories yields a nearly orthogonal predictor of human self-paced RTs—dissociating word-level surprise from short-horizon representational continuity.

Key metric
r = .044 surprisal ⊥ extrap. error · ΔAIC +10.7 garden path · 845k obs.
Why this matters

LM hidden geometry encodes human incremental cost that surprisal discards—two additive dimensions of comprehension.

Critical edge

24 garden-path items; self-paced only; no code release; modest naturalistic effect sizes.

Headline

High surprisal / low trajectory error +0.039 log RT; high error / low surprisal +0.008—both off-diagonal cells slow readers.

Critical assessment

Strengths

  • Clear geometric construct + PCA schematic
  • Near-zero r + tercile + displacement control
  • Controlled + naturalistic dual validation

Novelty & significance

  • Fresh LM→human bridge beyond surprisal
  • Implications for surprisal scaling paradox
  • Architecture-agnostic core finding

Critical edge — read before citing

  • Self-paced RT only — no eye-tracking / first-pass separation
  • 24 garden-path items; item RE absorbs between-item variance
  • No public code; replication from paper text required
  • Abstract vs PDF: scaling claim is replication across scales, not monotonic
  • Pythia subsample (n=100) may inflate ΔAIC volatility

Open questions
