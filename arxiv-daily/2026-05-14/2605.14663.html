Optimal Asymptotic Rates for (Stochastic) Gradient Descent under the Local PL-Condition: A Geometric Approach

arXiv: 2605.14663

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14663

一句话总结： 本文利用微分几何方法证明，在局部满足PL不等式的非凸C²函数上，（随机）梯度下降的渐近收敛速率与强凸二次函数上的最优速率一致，填补了优化理论中的一个重要空白。
TL;DR: This paper proves that the asymptotic convergence rate of (stochastic) gradient descent near a local minimum satisfying the Polyak-Lojasiewicz (PL) inequality matches the optimal rate for strongly convex quadratics, using a geometric approach based on manifold structure of the set of minima. The result holds despite possible non-convexity and non-uniqueness of critical points.

核心贡献

  • 证明了一个令人惊讶的结论： 在局部满足PL不等式的（可能非凸的）C²函数上，(S)GD的渐近收敛速率与强凸二次函数上的最优速率完全一致。
  • 利用微分几何方法： 借助Rebjock和Boumal的近期工作，利用PL条件下极小值点集形成C¹流形的结构，将法向和切向分解处理。
  • 推导出精确的渐近收敛速率公式 m(γ)： 该速率严格优于此前基于C¹¹假设的有限时间收敛速率，且在条件数较大时改善幅度可达 4(1 + σ/(2μ)) 倍。
  • 给出了最优步长和收敛速率的显式表达式： 分别针对高噪声和低噪声两种情况给出了最优步长选择。

Key Contributions

  • Main theorem (Theorem 1.3): Proves that near a (μ, L, σ)-regular point, SGD with step-size 0 < γ < 2/(L + σ²) converges almost surely to a local minimum with asymptotic rate m(γ) = max{(1 − γμ)² + γ²σμ/2, (1 − γL)² + γ²σL/2}, matching the quadratic case.
  • Geometric insight: The PL-inequality implies the set of local minima forms a C¹-manifold (building on Rebjock & Boumal, 2024). The Hessian's normal eigenvalues are bounded below by μ, allowing a decomposition into strongly contracting normal directions and asymptotically negligible tangent directions.
  • Strict improvement over prior PL-based rates: The rate m(γ) is strictly better than the previously known rate φ(γ) = 1 − 2μγ + γ²L(2μ + σ)/2 for all step-sizes when L > μ.
  • Optimal step-size characterization: Derives the optimal step-size γ* as a function of μ, L, σ, with two regimes depending on whether σ ≥ 2(L − μ) or not.
  • Corollary for deterministic GD (Corollary 1.4): In the noiseless case, GD achieves rate max{|1 − γμ|², |1 − γL|²}, recovering Polyak's classical result but now under local (non-convex) PL conditions.

Methodology

The proof proceeds in three stages:

  1. Quadratic analysis (Section 2): The authors first analyze the special case of strongly convex quadratic objectives f(θ) = ⟨Aθ, θ⟩/2 with spec(A) ⊂ [μ, L] under multiplicative noise. The key insight is that the convergence rate factorizes across eigenspaces of A — the flat (μ) and steep (L) directions separate, so the worst-case rate is determined by the maximum over individual eigenspaces rather than a combined bound.
  1. Descent lemma in transformed coordinates (Section 3): Using a chart Φ that flattens the manifold of minima, the authors derive a descent lemma (Lemma 3.1) controlling ∥proj_N(Φ(θ_k))∥² — the squared distance of the iterate to the manifold in chart coordinates. This involves careful estimation of Taylor approximation errors and the interplay between the chart geometry, gradient field, and noise.
  1. Manifold chart construction (Section 4) and main proof (Section 5): The chart Φ is explicitly constructed using the manifold structure from the Rebjock-Boumal result. The normal component behaves like a collection of one-dimensional strongly convex quadratics, while the tangent component is asymptotically negligible. The convergence rate then reduces to the quadratic case.

The approach requires C²-regularity (twice continuously differentiable) of the objective function, which is a stronger assumption than the C¹¹ (Lipschitz gradient) assumption used in prior finite-time PL convergence results.

Results & Highlights

  • The asymptotic rate m(γ) is provably optimal — it cannot be improved without additional assumptions, as shown by constructing matching lower bounds using specific noise models on quadratic objectives.
  • Speed-up factor analysis: In the ill-conditioned limit (κ → ∞), the ratio of contraction improvements (1 − m)/(1 − φ) approaches 4(1 + σ/(2μ)), demonstrating that the asymptotic speed-up grows with both condition number and noise level.
  • Step-size condition for convergence: m(γ) < 1 if and only if 0 < γ < 2/(L + σ²), providing a clean convergence guarantee.
  • Deterministic GD result: Recovers the classical Polyak rate max{|1 − γμ|², |1 − γL|²} for gradient descent on strongly convex functions, but now valid for the much broader class of locally PL-satisfying C² functions.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The result closes a genuine gap — it was previously unknown whether the local PL condition could yield rates matching the strongly convex quadratic case. The geometric approach using manifold charts is elegant and the separation of tangent/normal directions is a powerful idea.
  • Rigor: Very high. Complete proofs with explicit error bounds, careful handling of the stochastic terms, and a proper Borel-Cantelli argument for almost sure convergence.
  • Reproducibility: Good. The mathematical arguments are self-contained with explicit constants. The paper provides clear theorem statements that can be independently verified.
  • Significance: High for optimization theory. The result is conceptually important — it shows that (S)GD on locally PL functions is "as good as" (S)GD on strongly convex quadratics, asymptotically. This justifies the practical success of SGD on overparameterized neural networks from a theoretical standpoint.
  • Red flags / concerns:
  • The result is asymptotic — it only describes behavior as k → ∞ after the iterates reach a small neighborhood of the minimum. It says nothing about how quickly iterates reach that neighborhood (the "global convergence" phase).
  • The C² assumption is stronger than what some prior results require (C¹¹). For neural networks with ReLU-like activations, C² may not hold, potentially limiting applicability.
  • The multiplicative noise model (noise variance proportional to optimality gap) is motivated by overparameterized networks but may not capture all practical scenarios.
  • No experimental validation is provided — this is a pure theory paper.

Strengths

  • Elegant geometric interpretation connecting the PL condition to manifold structure of minima.
  • Sharp, provably optimal convergence rates with clean closed-form expressions.
  • Comprehensive analysis including optimal step-size derivation and detailed comparison with prior rates.
  • The result unifies and extends several prior works on GD/SGD under PL conditions.
  • Clear exposition with good intuition provided before technical proofs.

Limitations

  • Asymptotic only — no finite-time / non-asymptotic guarantees. The constants involved in the neighborhood size V may depend on the problem in opaque ways.
  • C² requirement excludes objectives with non-smooth activations (e.g., ReLU) or piecewise-linear losses common in modern deep learning.
  • Local result — assumes the iterates eventually reach a neighborhood of a PL-satisfying minimum; does not address how to escape saddle points or reach such neighborhoods.
  • No empirical validation — while the theory is self-contained, experiments on neural network training would strengthen the practical impact narrative.
  • The multiplicative noise assumption, while motivated by overparameterized models, is restrictive compared to additive noise models.
