Understanding Imbalanced Forgetting in Rehearsal-Based Class-Incremental Learning

arXiv: 2605.14785

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14785

一句话总结： 本文系统揭示了基于回放的类增量学习中，尽管每个旧类分配了等量的回放样本，不同类别的遗忘程度差异显著且普遍，并通过推导三个最后一层梯度级干扰系数（SIC、CIC、NIC）可靠地预测了各类别的遗忘排序，其中自诱导干扰系数SIC是最强预测因子且受新类干扰系数NIC影响。
TL;DR: This paper systematically demonstrates that imbalanced forgetting—where some classes are forgotten substantially more than others despite equal per-class rehearsal allocation—is a severe and systematic phenomenon in rehearsal-based CIL. The authors derive three last-layer gradient-level interference coefficients (SIC, CIC, NIC) from a principled Class-Wise R-SGD lemma, showing they reliably predict the forgetting-based ranking of past classes, with SIC emerging as the strongest predictor.

核心贡献

  • 系统性地证明了在基于回放的类增量学习（CIL）中，即使在每类等量回放样本分配下，不平衡遗忘现象依然严重且普遍存在
  • 提出了"类别级R-SGD引理"（Class-Wise R-SGD Lemma），从理论上识别了导致不同旧类优化差异的梯度级因素
  • 构建了三个最后一层不平衡遗忘系数：自诱导偏置干扰系数（SIC）、跨类偏置干扰系数（CIC）和新数据集干扰系数（NIC），分别量化不同来源的梯度干扰
  • 发现SIC是最强的遗忘预测因子，SIC与NIC之间存在密切关联，控制实验表明NIC可能对SIC产生方向性影响
  • 基于两个大规模全因子基准（CIFAR-100和Tiny-ImageNet），涵盖1200个增量步骤样本，进行了全面验证

Key Contributions

  • Comprehensively benchmarks imbalanced forgetting across two full-factorial CIL benchmarks (1,200 sampled incremental steps from CIFAR-100 and Tiny-ImageNet), showing it is systematic and severe (mean Forgetting Range consistently exceeds 0.55)
  • Derives the Class-Wise R-SGD lemma that upper-bounds the expected change in per-class loss during a single rehearsal SGD step, identifying three interference pathways
  • Constructs three Last-Layer Imbalanced Forgetting Coefficients (SIC, CIC, NIC) that aggregate gradient-level interference across the full training trajectory of an incremental step
  • Shows SIC achieves strong mean Spearman correlations with forgetting rankings (ρ ≈ 0.86–0.88 on CIFAR-100, ρ ≈ 0.56–0.68 on Tiny-ImageNet)
  • Demonstrates that a joint linear model combining all three coefficients outperforms SIC alone (ρ ≈ 0.88–0.91 on CIFAR-100, ρ ≈ 0.72–0.77 on Tiny-ImageNet)
  • Provides controlled experiments suggesting NIC influences SIC, with mean class-wise NIC-SIC correlation of 0.40–0.76 across re-runs

Methodology

The paper follows a principled theory-driven empirical approach:

  1. Problem Formulation: Defines CIL with rehearsal formally, including the R-SGD optimization procedure with class-balanced uniform sampling.
  1. Class-Wise R-SGD Lemma: Derives an upper bound on the expected one-step change in per-class loss under Lc-smoothness. The bound decomposes into three terms, with the second term (Ic) capturing interference from (a) self-class bias (rehearsal vs. original gradient gap), (b) cross-class bias, and (c) new-class gradients.
  1. Last-Layer Approximation: For computational tractability, restricts gradients to last-layer parameters only, motivated by prior evidence that last layers disproportionately contribute to forgetting. Constructs SIC, CIC, and NIC by aggregating the interference operator (negative scalar projection) across the training trajectory using evenly spaced checkpoints.
  1. Benchmarks: Constructs ΩC100 and ΩTIN as full-factorial benchmarks varying class-introduction percentage (10%/20%/50%), rehearsal retention rate (8%/20%/40%), and random seeds. Uses stratified sampling (40 samples per partition, 1,200 total).
  1. Evaluation: Measures forgetting via FG-R (range between most/least forgotten) and FG-HG (half-gap). Evaluates coefficient predictive strength via Spearman's ρ (marginal) and partial Spearman's ρp (conditional). Uses step-wise leave-one-out protocol with mixed-effects models for joint evaluation.
  1. Controlled Experiments: Re-runs training with different new-class sets while holding rehearsal set, seed, and initial parameters fixed, to investigate the NIC→SIC directional influence.

Results & Highlights

  • Imbalanced forgetting is severe: Mean FG-R exceeds 0.55 across all settings, reaching 0.71 on third-step Tiny-ImageNet. Even FG-HG (conservative metric) averages 0.25–0.28.
  • Imbalanced forgetting scales with complexity: More pronounced on Tiny-ImageNet than CIFAR-100, and increases from second to third incremental steps.
  • SIC is the dominant predictor: Achieves ρ ≈ 0.86–0.88 on CIFAR-100 and ρ ≈ 0.56–0.68 on Tiny-ImageNet for marginal associations, with conditional associations (ρp) only slightly lower, indicating largely unique predictive information.
  • Joint model outperforms SIC alone: All three coefficients together achieve ρ ≈ 0.88–0.91 on CIFAR-100 and ρ ≈ 0.72–0.77 on Tiny-ImageNet, with smaller standard deviations.
  • NIC influences SIC: Controlled experiments show that when new classes interfere more with a past class (higher NIC), that class's rehearsal set also produces higher SIC. Mean NIC-SIC Spearman correlation across re-runs: 0.40–0.76.
  • Early training is informative: Computing coefficients using only the early portion of the R-SGD trajectory yields comparable predictive performance to using the full trajectory.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. While imbalanced forgetting was observed in Xu et al. (AAAI 2024), this is the first systematic investigation with quantitative metrics, principled theoretical derivation, and mechanistic coefficients. The Class-Wise R-SGD lemma and the three coefficients (SIC, CIC, NIC) are novel contributions.
  • Rigor: Very high. The full-factorial benchmark design with 1,200 sampled steps, stratified sampling, 95% CIs, mixed-effects models, and controlled experiments demonstrates exceptional methodological rigor. The paper is careful to distinguish prediction from causation.
  • Reproducibility: Strong. Code is available, all hyperparameters are specified, datasets are standard (CIFAR-100, Tiny-ImageNet), and the experimental protocol is described in sufficient detail.
  • Significance: High for the CIL community. The finding that uniform rehearsal allocation is fundamentally suboptimal—because classes experience different interference levels—has direct practical implications for future method design.
  • Red flags: The analysis is limited to standard rehearsal without auxiliary losses, distillation, or dynamic architectures. Whether the coefficients transfer to these more complex rehearsal-based methods remains unknown. The last-layer approximation, while empirically supported, is still an approximation of the full-network dynamics. Predictive strength degrades noticeably on Tiny-ImageNet (ρ ≈ 0.56 for SIC at third step), suggesting the coefficients may be less informative in more complex scenarios.

Strengths

  • Exceptional thoroughness: comprehensive benchmarks, rigorous statistical analysis, extensive appendices covering fine-grained analyses
  • Principled theoretical foundation: the Class-Wise R-SGD lemma provides a clean derivation from optimization theory
  • Clear practical implications: identifies adaptive rehearsal allocation as a promising direction
  • Honest about limitations: explicitly notes that prediction ≠ causation and that the last-layer restriction is an approximation
  • The NIC→SIC causal hypothesis with controlled experiments is a particularly insightful finding

Limitations

  • Restricted to standard rehearsal without knowledge distillation, dynamic architectures, or auxiliary losses—coverage of the broader rehearsal-based CIL landscape is limited
  • Only evaluated on image classification with ResNet backbones (CIFAR-100 and Tiny-ImageNet); generalization to other modalities or architectures is untested
  • Predictive strength degrades on Tiny-ImageNet and at later incremental steps, suggesting the coefficients may not fully capture interference in complex settings
  • The paper does not propose or validate an actual mitigation method for imbalanced forgetting—it stops at understanding and prediction
  • The joint predictive model (linear combination of SIC, CIC, NIC) requires training data from the same benchmark partition, limiting its applicability as a training-time intervention

Relevance

本文深入分析了类增量学习中回放机制的固有缺陷——不平衡遗忘现象，提出的基于梯度干扰的系数分析方法对理解持续学习中的灾难性遗忘机制有重要启发，其发现的自诱导干扰（SIC）为核心驱动因素为设计更公平的回放策略提供了理论依据。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can SIC/NIC values estimated at the end of one incremental step predict forgetting in subsequent steps, enabling proactive rehearsal reallocation?
  • How do the three coefficients behave when auxiliary losses (e.g., knowledge distillation) or dynamic network expansion are introduced?
  • Would adaptive rehearsal allocation based on SIC predictions (more samples for high-SIC classes) effectively reduce imbalanced forgetting without harming overall performance?
  • Is the NIC→SIC causal pathway an artifact of the last-layer approximation, or does it hold at the full-network level?
  • How does imbalanced forgetting manifest in continual learning settings beyond image classification (e.g., NLP, reinforcement learning)?
