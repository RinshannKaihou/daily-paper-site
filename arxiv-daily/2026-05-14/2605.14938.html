Key Contributions

  • Proposes HiFGO (History-Free Gradient Orthogonalization), enforcing gradient-level orthogonality constraints without needing historical task data.
  • Introduces a two-stage finetuning strategy that decouples task adaptation from regularization, achieving principled plasticity-stability trade-off.
  • Achieves new SOTA on UCIT benchmark, surpassing prior best by 2.14% (Avg) and 6.82% (Last).
  • Eliminates privacy and storage concerns associated with rehearsal-based methods and computational overhead of architecture-based approaches.

Methodology

Octopus is a two-stage continual learning framework for MLLMs:

  1. Stage 1 — Task Adaptation: The model is finetuned on the new task data to acquire new knowledge, focusing on plasticity.
  2. Stage 2 — Regularization via Gradient Orthogonalization: Without using any historical task data, HiFGO enforces gradient-level orthogonality constraints to project updates into directions orthogonal to previously learned task subspaces, thereby preserving stability.

The key insight is decoupling these two objectives: first learn freely, then regularize in a principled way. The gradient orthogonalization operates at the parameter level without requiring replay buffers or architectural modifications.

The novelty lies in the specific decoupling of adaptation and regularization stages.

  • Reproducibility: The method appears conceptually straightforward (gradient orthogonalization + two-stage finetuning). Reproducibility depends on implementation details not assessable from the abstract alone.
  • Significance: Addressing catastrophic forgetting in MLLMs without historical data is practically important. The reported gains are meaningful but modest (+2–7%).
