A Novel Schur-Decomposition-Based Weight Projection Method for Stable State-Space Neural-Network Architectures

arXiv: 2605.14489

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14489

⚠️ Abstract-only summary

一句话总结： 本文提出基于 Schur 分解的状态矩阵投影方法，将线性离散时间状态空间层的准上三角因子投影到最近稳定等价形式，以最小过参数化代价保证渐近稳定性，并在合成系统和真实数据集上达到与 SOTA 稳定系统辨识方法相当的精度。
TL;DR: A Schur-decomposition-based projection scheme enforces asymptotic stability in linear discrete-time state-space neural network layers by projecting the quasi-triangular Schur factor to its nearest stable counterpart, achieving SOTA-comparable accuracy with fewer weights.

核心贡献

  • 提出了一种基于实 Schur 分解的新型稳定性投影方案，可在反向传播训练过程中保证线性离散时间状态空间层的渐近稳定性。
  • 设计了预因子化（pre-factorized）替代公式，进一步简化投影计算流程。
  • 投影作用于准上三角 Schur 因子而非整个状态矩阵，实现了最小化过参数化。
  • 实验表明该方法在合成线性系统上精度和收敛速度与 SOTA 稳定系统辨识方法相当，且在堆叠非线性神经网络架构中因参数量更少而加速收敛。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces a novel Schur-decomposition-based projection that is both stability-ensuring and backpropagation-compatible for state-space neural network layers.
  • Provides an alternative pre-factorized formulation to streamline the projection pipeline.
  • Projects the quasi-triangular Schur factor (not the full state matrix) onto its nearest stable peer, minimizing overparameterization.
  • Demonstrates SOTA-comparable accuracy on synthetic linear systems and faster convergence on stacked architectures with static nonlinearities on real-world datasets.

Methodology

The authors leverage the real Schur decomposition of the state matrix $A$ in a linear discrete-time state-space model ($x_{k+1} = Ax_k + Bu_k$). Since $A$ is factored into $UTU^T$ where $T$ is quasi-triangular (block upper triangular with 1×1 or 2×2 diagonal blocks corresponding to real and complex eigenvalues), the projection operates directly on $T$ to enforce that all eigenvalues lie within the unit circle (asymptotic stability). This avoids parameterizing the full orthogonal matrix $U$ and instead projects only $T$, yielding a lower parameter count compared to methods that constrain the full matrix. A pre-factorized alternative formulation is also introduced. The scheme is compatible with standard backpropagation, allowing end-to-end training of neural networks containing these state-space layers.

Results & Highlights

  • On synthetic linear systems, the method achieves accuracy and convergence rates comparable to state-of-the-art stable-system identification techniques.
  • There is a marginal increase in computational complexity due to the Schur decomposition, but this does not harm practical convergence.
  • In stacked neural-network architectures with static nonlinearities on real-world datasets, the lower weight count facilitates faster convergence without sacrificing accuracy.
  • The framework is numerically robust and satisfies strict asymptotic-stability requirements throughout training.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-to-high. Applying Schur decomposition to constrain stability in learnable state-space layers is a natural but previously underexplored idea. The quasi-triangular projection is elegant and distinct from existing eigenvalue-clipping or Lyapunov-based approaches.
  • Rigor: The paper claims backpropagation compatibility and asymptotic stability guarantees; the mathematical argument for the nearest-stable-peer projection on the quasi-triangular factor needs careful reading (abstract-level information limits full verification).
  • Reproducibility: Without access to code or detailed algorithmic pseudocode in the abstract, reproducibility cannot be fully assessed. The Schur decomposition is a standard linear-algebra primitive, which aids implementation.
  • Significance: Relevant to the growing field of structured state-space models (S4, Mamba, etc.) where stability guarantees matter for safety-critical control and long-horizon forecasting.
  • Red flags: The "marginal increase in computational complexity" claim may become significant at very large state dimensions. The paper focuses on linear state-space layers — it is unclear how the method extends to nonlinear state transitions common in modern SSM architectures.

Strengths

  • Mathematically principled approach grounded in well-understood matrix decompositions.
  • Minimal overparameterization compared to methods that constrain the full state matrix.
  • Compatible with standard backpropagation — no custom optimizer needed.
  • Demonstrated on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

Limitations

  • Applicable only to linear discrete-time state-space layers; extension to continuous-time or nonlinear state transitions is not addressed.
  • Computational overhead of Schur decomposition at every training step may be non-trivial for high-dimensional states.
  • Abstract-only assessment limits evaluation of experimental depth and ablation studies.
