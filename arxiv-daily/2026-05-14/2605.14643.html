Key Contributions

  • Provides a principled analysis of the bias induced by the commonly used Euler-Maruyama discretization in BSDE training losses.
  • Develops an unbiased, second-order-free training framework that eliminates EM bias without requiring explicit Hessian evaluations.
  • Avoids the computational overhead and re-introduction of second-order spatial derivatives that high-order schemes (e.g., Heun) entail.
  • Releases open-source code for reproducibility.

Methodology

The paper addresses a fundamental limitation in BSDE-based deep learning for PDEs. The standard approach uses Euler-Maruyama discretization of the backward SDE, which introduces a systematic bias in the training loss. While higher-order schemes like Heun's method can eliminate this bias, they require computing second-order spatial derivatives (Hessians), negating one of the key advantages of BSDE methods.

The authors analyze the EM-induced bias analytically and propose a corrected training objective that removes the bias while remaining second-order-free. The approach leverages the probabilistic structure of BSDE representations to achieve unbiased gradient estimates without Hessian computations.

Results & Highlights

  • Demonstrates that EM discretization introduces measurable bias in BSDE training losses across multiple PDE benchmarks.
  • The proposed unbiased framework achieves accuracy comparable to Heun-based training while avoiding second-order derivative computation.
  • Maintains the computational efficiency that makes BSDE methods attractive for high-dimensional problems.
  • Code is publicly available for verification and extension.
