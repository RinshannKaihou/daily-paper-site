Dynamics of the Transformer Residual Stream: Coupling Spectral Geometry to Network Topology

arXiv: 2605.14258

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14258

一句话总结： 本文通过对 Llama 3.1 8B、OLMo 3 7B 和 Gemma 4 E4B 三个生产级 LLM 进行完整雅可比矩阵特征分解，发现训练会在模型深度方向安装一个从旋转主导（早期层）到近对称（晚期层）的单调非正规梯度，同时造成累积低秩瓶颈（将扰动压缩到仅约 4–40 个有效维度），并揭示激活图社区结构的边界节点与雅可比放大/抑制之间存在由算子类型决定的耦合关系。
TL;DR: Full Jacobian eigendecomposition of three production-scale LLMs reveals a learned monotonic non-normality gradient through depth (rotation-dominated early → near-symmetric late), a cumulative low-rank bottleneck funneling perturbations into ~4–40 effective dimensions, and a learned per-unit coupling between activation-graph community topology and Jacobian amplification whose sign is set by local operator type.

核心贡献

  • 发现训练产生的单调非正规梯度： 训练后的 LLM 各层雅可比矩阵从早期层的旋转主导（高度非正规）平滑过渡到晚期层的近对称算子，约 98% 的特征值为复共轭对，表明信息通过耦合旋转模式传播——这一结构在随机初始化时不存在。
  • 揭示累积低秩瓶颈： 将各层雅可比矩阵组合计算端到端传播时，有效秩从单层的 ~436 骤降至全组合的 ~7（Llama 3.1 8B），意味着仅极少数独立特征能从嵌入层完整传播到输出层，且该瓶颈是训练习得的而非架构固有的。
  • 通过 Schur 分解除非正规结构证明瓶颈来源： 将雅可比矩阵分解为谱分量和非正规分量，仅缩放非正规分量即可使有效秩恢复 6.4 倍，证明瓶颈源于训练产生的非正规前馈结构而非特征值谱。
  • 建立拓扑-动力学耦合： 激活相关性图的社区结构中，跨社区边界节点被雅可比优先放大或抑制，放大/抑制的符号由局部算子类型（近对称层放大、非正规层抑制）决定，该耦合在训练过程中单调涌现。

Key Contributions

  • Monotonic non-normality gradient installed by training: Full eigendecomposition across Llama 3.1 8B, OLMo 3 7B, and Gemma 4 E4B shows per-layer Jacobians transition from rotation-dominated early layers to near-symmetric late layers. ~98% of eigenvalues are complex conjugate pairs, revealing rotational dynamics invisible to SVD-based analyses. OLMo step-0 null model confirms this gradient is learned, not architectural.
  • Cumulative low-rank bottleneck: End-to-end Jacobian composition reveals effective rank collapses from ~436 (single layer) to ~7 (full composition) in Llama 3.1 8B. Gemma funnels to ~5.9 across 42 layers. OLMo step 0 retains rank ~326, confirming the bottleneck is training-induced.
  • Non-normal feedforward as bottleneck mechanism: Schur decomposition surgery scaling only the upper-triangular (non-normal) component shows erank recovers 6.4× when non-normality is removed (c=0), while the untrained baseline is nearly flat. The bottleneck is a property of trained non-normal structure, not the eigenspectrum.
  • Per-unit topology-dynamics coupling: Boundary nodes (high participation coefficient in activation-graph communities) are preferentially amplified or suppressed by the Jacobian. The sign is determined by operator type: boundary amplification at near-symmetric layers, de-amplification at non-normal layers. This coupling is absent at initialization and emerges monotonically over training (0 → 14 → 24 FDR-significant layers across OLMo checkpoints).
  • Architecture-vs-training decomposition: Using OLMo's step-0 checkpoint as a null model, depth regimes and adjacent-layer subspace decoupling are shown to be architectural, while the non-normality gradient, dimensional funneling, and boundary-unit coupling are installed by training.

Methodology

The authors compute exact per-sample Jacobians $J_\ell = \partial h_{\ell+1}/\partial h_\ell$ at every transformer block boundary for three production-scale LLMs:

  • Models: Llama 3.1 8B (32 layers, d=4096), OLMo 3 7B at three checkpoints (step 0, 471k, 1.41M; 32 layers, d=4096), Gemma 4 E4B (42 layers, d=2560).
  • Data: 1,000 WikiText-2 samples, last-token activations, recording residual stream at both pre-attention and pre-MLP sub-layer boundaries.
  • Jacobian computation: Exact Jacobians via torch.autograd.functional.jacobian in float32, mean Jacobians accumulated in float64.
  • Spectral analysis: Full eigendecomposition of mean Jacobians, SVD for condition numbers and singular values, self-alignment and Henrici departure for non-normality quantification, cumulative Jacobian products for end-to-end propagation analysis.
  • Schur surgery: Decompose $J_\ell = Q_\ell(\Lambda_\ell + N_\ell)Q^*_\ell$, scale only $N_\ell$ by dose $c \in \{0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1, 1.5, 2\}$ to isolate the contribution of non-normal structure.
  • Activation-graph community detection: Sparse signed correlation graphs (top-k=20 edges per unit), community detection via signed Leiden CPM at $\gamma=0.001$, participation coefficient for boundary-node identification.
  • Statistical testing: Cohen's d between top/bottom 10% participation tails for column norms, FDR-corrected (Benjamini-Hochberg, $\alpha=0.05$) across layers. Spearman correlations for topology-dynamics coupling.

The OLMo step-0 checkpoint serves as a critical architectural null model: it shares the same architecture, depth, and wiring but differs only in weights, allowing clean separation of architectural vs. learned properties.

Results & Highlights

  • Three-regime structure: Jacobians partition into early (anisotropic, rotation-dominated, $\kappa \sim 10^6$), mid (contracted, $\kappa \sim 10^2$–$10^3$), and late (re-expanded, near-symmetric, self-alignment ~0.55) regimes. Similar patterns across all three architectures.
  • ~98% complex eigenvalue pairs: The predominance of complex conjugate pairs means each layer implements coupled rotational modes, not simple radial scaling—structure invisible to SVD-only analyses.
  • Extreme dimensional collapse: Full cumulative Jacobian has effective rank of only ~6.7 (Llama) and ~5.9 (Gemma) out of thousands of residual-stream dimensions, bounding how many independent features propagate end-to-end.
  • Non-normality drives the bottleneck: Removing trained non-normal structure (Schur surgery, c=0) recovers 5.7–6.6× effective rank across all trained models; the untrained baseline is nearly flat across the same dose range.
  • Weak inter-layer coordination: Forward alignment between adjacent layers sits at the random baseline (~k/d ≈ 0.016), consistent with the residual stream as a shared workspace with minimal directed inter-layer communication.
  • Community topology is partly architectural: At OLMo step 0, community participation already explains significantly more Jacobian variance than random partitions (32/32 layers, median z=19.2).
  • Learned boundary-node coupling: The per-unit coupling between community boundary status and Jacobian amplification emerges monotonically over training (0 → 14 → 24 FDR-significant layers in OLMo), with sign governed by self-alignment (operator type).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. This is the first work to perform full Jacobian eigendecomposition on production-scale LLMs and connect spectral geometry to activation-graph topology. The finding that ~98% of eigenvalues are complex conjugate pairs and the rotational dynamics characterization are genuinely new. The Schur surgery experiment cleanly isolating non-normality as the bottleneck mechanism is methodologically innovative.
  • Rigor: Strong. The OLMo step-0 null model is a particularly rigorous design choice that cleanly separates architectural from learned contributions. Multiple architectures (Llama, OLMo, Gemma) and three training checkpoints provide robustness. Statistical testing with FDR correction across layers is appropriate. The convergence with independent findings from Golden (2025), Fu et al. (2025), and Jacobs et al. (2026) using different methods strengthens the dimensional collapse result.
  • Reproducibility: Moderate. The paper uses publicly available models (Llama 3.1 8B, OLMo 3 7B, Gemma 4 E4B) and WikiText-2 data. Methods are described in sufficient detail in the appendix. However, computing exact Jacobians for production-scale models is computationally expensive (1000 samples × 32+ layers × full d×d Jacobians), which may limit independent reproduction.
  • Significance: High. The findings have direct implications for mechanistic interpretability (information propagates through a tiny subspace), signal propagation theory (initialization-time theory misses the key structures), and architecture design (the non-normality gradient is learned and could potentially be engineered). The recommendation that activation-graph studies report random-init baselines is a valuable methodological contribution.
  • Red flags: Effect sizes for the per-unit boundary-node coupling are moderate (Cohen's d ~0.1–0.25), and the OLMo three-checkpoint trajectory cannot distinguish whether prolonged training increases effect sizes or merely propagates existing signal across more layers. The analysis is limited to last-token activations on WikiText-2; distributional shift to other inputs could affect results. Gemma's instruction-tuned checkpoint (rather than base) introduces a potential confound for the cross-architecture comparison.

Strengths

  • First full eigenvalue-level characterization of production-scale transformer Jacobians after training, closing the gap between initialization-time theory and approximate linearizations.
  • Clean architecture-vs-training decomposition via OLMo step-0 null model—a model experimental design.
  • Schur surgery experiment is an elegant causal intervention that isolates the mechanism behind the dimensional bottleneck.
  • Cross-architecture replication (three model families with different depths, dimensions, and attention configurations) strengthens generality claims.
  • Convergence with independent findings from multiple research groups using different mathematical tools.

Limitations

  • Computationally expensive methodology: exact Jacobians for d=4096 layers require substantial GPU memory and time, limiting scalability to even larger models.
  • Per-unit effect sizes (Cohen's d) are moderate; practical implications for interpretability or editing remain to be demonstrated.
  • Single dataset (WikiText-2) and single token position (last token) limit claims about generality across input distributions.
  • The Gemma model used is instruction-tuned rather than base, introducing a potential confound.
  • Three OLMo checkpoints are insufficient to characterize the full training dynamics trajectory (e.g., whether the coupling saturates or continues growing).
  • The signed Leiden CPM with γ=0.001 is a specific community-detection choice; while alternatives yield qualitatively similar patterns, the absolute magnitudes are detector-dependent.

Relevance

本文与 mechanistic interpretability 和 LLM 内部动力学研究高度相关。通过揭示训练如何在残差流中构建谱几何结构并产生低秩瓶颈，为理解信息在 LLM 各层间的压缩与传播提供了精确的数学刻画，对信号传播理论和可解释性研究都有重要参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the cumulative low-rank bottleneck (~4–40 effective dimensions) imply a fundamental limit on how many independent features any single forward pass can represent, and can this bottleneck be relaxed through architectural modifications?
  2. How does the non-normality gradient interact with task-specific circuits identified by mechanistic interpretability (e.g., induction heads, refusal directions)?
  3. Would the boundary-node coupling persist or change under distributional shift (e.g., code, multilingual text, adversarial inputs)?
  4. Can the Schur surgery intervention be used as a tool for controlled editing of model behavior—e.g., selectively reducing non-normality in specific layers to suppress undesired computations?
  5. How does the spectral geometry evolve during fine-tuning or RLHF, and does the non-normality gradient sharpen or flatten when models are aligned?
