Your CLIP has 164 dimensions of noise: Exploring the embeddings covariance eigenspectrum of contrastively pretrained vision-language transformers

arXiv: 2605.14893

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14893

一句话总结： 通过对CLIP/SigLIP嵌入的协方差矩阵进行谱分解，发现对比学习VLM的潜在空间中存在大量共享噪声维度（如CLIP ViT-L/14中有164维），剪除这些噪声维度不仅无害，反而能提升下游任务的文本-图像对齐性能。
TL;DR: Spectral decomposition of covariance matrices from CLIP/SigLIP embeddings reveals a consistent shared noise subspace (e.g., 164 of 768 dimensions in CLIP ViT-L/14) that is invariant across data subsets. Pruning these noise dimensions preserves zero-shot classification accuracy while improving text-image alignment cosine similarity.

核心贡献

  • 通过协方差矩阵的谱分解，将对比学习VLM（CLIP、SigLIP）的潜在空间形式化地分解为语义信号子空间和共享噪声子空间两部分。
  • 发现噪声子空间在不同数据子集（甚至不相关的ImageNet类别）之间具有高度不变性，mSCSA重叠度在最大模型上超过90%。
  • 证明剪除这些共享噪声维度对下游任务基本无害：ImageNet零样本分类准确率几乎不变，而LAION-2B上的文本-图像余弦相似度系统性提升。
  • 对噪声维度进行定性分析，发现其编码的内容多为无效占位符图像或分布外内容（如漫威电影画面），进一步印证了其非语义性质。

Key Contributions

  • Formally decomposes VLM latent spaces into a semantic signal component and a shared noise subspace via spectral decomposition of empirical covariance matrices.
  • Demonstrates strong subgroup invariance of the noise subspace: eigenspectra computed over unrelated ImageNet classes are highly similar, with noise eigenvectors aligning across classes (mSCSA > 90% for the largest models).
  • Shows that pruning shared noise dimensions via projection ($P = I - VV^T$) is mostly harmless: negligible impact on ImageNet zero-shot Top-5 accuracy, while systematically improving average cosine similarity on LAION-2B alignment.
  • Provides qualitative evidence that noise-activated images contain placeholders, broken links, or out-of-distribution content (e.g., Marvel movie stills when covariance is estimated from ImageNet alone).

Methodology

The authors analyze the latent spaces of six contrastively pretrained VLMs: CLIP ViT-B/16, ViT-B/32, ViT-L/14, SigLIP ViT-B/16, SigLIP2 ViT-B/16, and SigLIP2 ViT-L/16.

  1. Covariance estimation: Compute empirical covariance matrices $\Sigma$ over large-scale datasets — ImageNet-1K (1.2M images) for image modality, and ~1M text descriptions from LAION-2B for text modality. Define a cross-modal average covariance $\Sigma_{avg} = \frac{1}{2}(\Sigma_{img} + \Sigma_{text})$.
  1. Spectral decomposition: Eigen-decompose $\Sigma_{avg}$ and identify a sharp drop in eigenvalues at a noise threshold (~$10^{-3.6}$). Dimensions below this threshold constitute the shared noise subspace.
  1. Subspace overlap metric (mSCSA): Quantify alignment between two subspaces via the mean squared cosine of principal subspace angles, computed from the SVD of the overlap matrix $M = V_1^T V_2$.
  1. Noise removal projection: Define $P = I - VV^T$ where $V$ spans the noise eigenvectors. Evaluate the projected embeddings on ImageNet zero-shot classification (Top-5 accuracy) and LAION-2B text-image alignment (cosine similarity).
  1. Qualitative inspection: For CLIP ViT-L/14, surface images with the strongest activations in the noise subspace to visually inspect what these dimensions encode.

Results & Highlights

  • Noise dimensions are substantial: CLIP ViT-L/14 has 164/768 (21%) noise dimensions; SigLIP2 ViT-L/16 has 405/1024 (40%). Smaller models have proportionally fewer (3-4% for CLIP ViT-B variants).
  • Classification is preserved: Removing noise dimensions causes ≤0.1% Top-5 accuracy drop on ImageNet zero-shot across all models. Random direction removal of the same cardinality causes significantly more degradation.
  • Alignment improves: Average cosine similarity on LAION-2B pairs systematically increases after noise removal, with gains up to +1.60×10⁻² for SigLIP ViT-B/16.
  • Noise is universal, not class-specific: mSCSA between global noise directions and per-class low-variance eigenvectors exceeds 50% for most models and 90% for the largest ones, despite no shared samples between classes.
  • Noise-activated images contain artifacts: Placeholder images for unavailable URLs dominate; when using ImageNet-only covariance, Marvel movie images appear as noise-activated (likely OOD relative to the pre-ImageNet-era collection).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (Moderate): The idea of analyzing latent spaces via covariance eigenspectra builds directly on Jing et al. (2022) dimensional collapse work and Betser et al. (2025) whitened CLIP. The extension to cross-modal shared noise is new, but the analytical toolkit is largely borrowed.
  • Rigor (Good): The paper includes proper ablations (random direction removal), uses a principled overlap metric (mSCSA), and tests across six model variants. The noise threshold selection via the elbow method is reasonable but somewhat ad hoc — the authors acknowledge this limitation.
  • Reproducibility (Good): Uses publicly available models and datasets (CLIP, SigLIP, ImageNet, LAION-2B). The methodology (compute covariance, decompose, threshold, project) is straightforward to replicate. No code repository is mentioned, but the procedures are described clearly enough.
  • Significance (Moderate-High): The finding that 21-40% of dimensions in popular VLMs encode shared noise has practical implications for feature extraction, model compression, and understanding contrastive learning dynamics. The connection to dimensional collapse and modality gap provides useful mechanistic insight.
  • Red flags: The noise threshold (~$10^{-3.6}$) is set as the minimum across all models' elbow points, which may be overly aggressive or conservative for specific models. The qualitative analysis is limited to CLIP ViT-L/14 only. The paper does not establish a causal mechanism — it remains correlational/observational. The claim that noise dimensions encode "architecture-level" noise is asserted but not rigorously proven (alternative: data-level artifacts).

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated methodology that is easy to understand and reproduce.
  • Surprising and practically useful finding: removing ~20-40% of dimensions without performance loss.
  • Strong empirical evidence across multiple model families (CLIP, SigLIP, SigLIP2) and multiple scales.
  • Honest about limitations and alternative explanations in the Discussion section.
  • The mSCSA metric is a sensible choice for quantifying high-dimensional subspace overlap.

Limitations

  • No theoretical framework explaining why shared noise dimensions emerge — remains observational.
  • Noise threshold selection is heuristic (elbow method on average covariance); per-model tuning could yield different results.
  • Qualitative analysis is limited to a single model and a small set of examples.
  • Does not explore whether noise removal benefits other downstream tasks (e.g., retrieval, segmentation, VQA) beyond the two tested.
  • Does not investigate whether these noise dimensions could be exploited adversarially (e.g., via adversarial perturbations targeting the noise subspace).

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Is the emergence of shared noise dimensions causally linked to the contrastive loss objective, or is it a more general artifact of high-dimensional representation learning?
  • Can the noise subspace be exploited for adversarial attacks, e.g., by perturbing inputs to maximally activate noise dimensions?
  • Would iterative re-training with noise-projected embeddings eliminate the noise subspace entirely, or does it re-emerge?
  • How does the noise subspace interact with the modality gap — is it a subset, orthogonal, or independent?
