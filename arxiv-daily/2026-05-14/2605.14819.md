## Key Contributions

- **Identifies the Velocity Deficit phenomenon**: In high-dimensional spaces, the MSE objective causes Flow Matching models to systematically underestimate velocity field magnitude, with predicted velocity decaying monotonically from the noise end to the data end, deviating from the constant Optimal Transport target velocity.
- **Reveals dynamic asymmetry of velocity contraction**: Velocity contraction at trajectory start is harmful (signal starvation), causing Integration Lag; at the trajectory end, it is beneficial (implicit denoising), helping preserve high-frequency details.
- **Proposes the training-free Scale Schedule Corrector (SSC)**: A one-line code modification that applies a time-dependent scaling factor to the velocity field during inference, injecting energy at the trajectory start while annealing to identity at the end, at zero retraining cost.
- **Proposes the training-based Magnitude-Aware Flow Matching (MAFM)**: Adds an explicit magnitude supervision term to the training objective, forcing the model to overcome magnitude collapse; achieves better local texture quality (sFID).
- **Extensive validation**: SSC shows consistent improvements on ImageNet-1k (256├Ś256 and 512├Ś512), MS-COCO text-to-image generation, and SANA text rendering, and is stable across architectures (Transformer and U-Net).

## Methodology

The paper provides a rigorous theoretical and empirical analysis of the Velocity Deficit in Flow Matching:

1. **Theoretical Framework**: Under independent data coupling (Assumption 3.1), the MSE objective's global minimizer is the conditional expectation v*(x_t, t) = E[v_target | x_t]. Due to the non-zero variance of the target velocity distribution p(v_target | x_t) at intermediate states, Jensen's inequality guarantees that the squared norm of the learned velocity is strictly less than the expected squared norm of the target velocity (Eq. 3). Boundary analysis at tÔćĺ0 and tÔćĺ1 confirms strict energy deficits at both ends (Eqs. 6, 8).

2. **Asymmetry Discovery**: The velocity deficit has fundamentally different physical implications at the two trajectory boundaries. At tÔćĺ0, contraction loses the data signal (harmful signal starvation); at tÔćĺ1, contraction averages out noise (beneficial implicit denoising). This motivates asymmetric energy injection.

3. **Scale Schedule Corrector (SSC)**: A training-free inference intervention with ╬│(t) = s_start┬Ě(1-t) + s_end┬Ět, where s_start > 1.0 (typically 1.1) injects energy early and s_end = 1.0 respects natural convergence. Implementation requires a single line of code change in standard Euler/Heun solvers.

4. **Magnitude-Aware Flow Matching (MAFM)**: A training-based objective L_MAFM = L_FM + ╬╗(t)┬Ě(||v_╬Ş|| - ||x1-x0||)┬▓ with ╬╗(t) = ╬╗ÔéÇ┬Ě(1-t), enforcing magnitude constraints at early timesteps while relaxing them near t=1.

5. **Fr├ęchet Latent Distance (FLD)**: A novel metric tracking distance-to-manifold in the VAE latent space across sampling timesteps, directly quantifying Integration Lag.

## Results & Highlights

- **ImageNet-1k 256├Ś256 (SiT-XL/2, 7M steps)**: SSC reduces FID from 13.68 to 7.58 (44.6% improvement) at NFE=50, outperforming the 250-step baseline (FID 8.65) ÔÇö a 5├Ś speedup.
- **Consistent gains across model scales**: FID improvements range from 10.8% (SiT-XL/2 with MAFM) to 23.7% (SiT-XL/2 with SSC), with sFID improvements exceeding 30% across all scales.
- **Training-free SSC outperforms training-based MAFM**: On SiT-XL/2, SSC achieves FID 14.26 vs. MAFM's 16.68, suggesting the Integration Lag is easier to correct at inference than unlearn during training.
- **High-resolution generalization**: On ImageNet-1k 512├Ś512, SSC reduces FID by 20.7ÔÇô28.4% (e.g., 11.72Ôćĺ8.39 on REPA SiT-XL/2).
- **Text-to-Image (MS-COCO)**: SSC improves FID from 6.03 to 4.71 (~22% improvement) on REPA-MMDiT.
- **Cross-architecture stability**: SSC improves FID by 18.2% on a 76M parameter U-Net, confirming the velocity deficit is architecture-agnostic.
- **FLD tracking confirms Integration Lag**: SSC-corrected trajectories are up to 28.2% closer to the data manifold at t=0.8 compared to baseline.
- **Text rendering improvement on SANA**: SSC corrects character misspellings (e.g., 'Homor'Ôćĺ'Honor'), demonstrating precise convergence for high-frequency details.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High)**: The identification of the Velocity Deficit as a structural bias of MSE minimization in high-dimensional Flow Matching is novel and insightful. The asymmetry observation (harmful at start, beneficial at end) is a non-obvious contribution.
- **Rigor (High)**: Theoretical analysis is grounded in measure concentration theory and Jensen's inequality, with boundary proofs and empirical verification of the cross-term approximation (relative error ÔëĄ 1.56% in D=4096).
- **Reproducibility (Very High)**: SSC is a one-line code change with a single hyperparameter (s_start=1.1) that was searched once on SiT-S/2 and transferred to all other settings. No retraining required.
- **Significance (High)**: 44.6% FID improvement with 5├Ś speedup is a substantial practical contribution. The training-free nature makes it immediately applicable to existing models.
- **Red flags**:
  - The linear SSC schedule is empirically driven, not analytically optimal. The paper acknowledges this but could benefit from more theoretical guidance on the optimal schedule shape.
  - SSC's gains diminish or reverse under very high CFG (Ôëą4.0), suggesting the method is not universally applicable to all guidance regimes.
  - The authors are from a relatively unknown industry lab (Jiiov Technology) rather than a major research institution, though the ICML 2026 acceptance provides vetting.
  - The MAFM+SSC combination leads to kinetic energy oversaturation on larger models, indicating the two methods are not fully complementary and require careful tuning.

## Strengths

- Extremely simple and practical: one line of code, zero retraining, negligible overhead.
- Strong theoretical grounding with formal proofs and empirical validation.
- Comprehensive evaluation across architectures, scales, resolutions, and tasks.
- The FLD metric for directly measuring Integration Lag is a useful diagnostic tool.
- The asymmetry insight (harmful at start, beneficial at end) provides a principled motivation for the injection schedule.
- Compatible with and complementary to existing techniques like REPA and CFG.

## Limitations

- Relies on high-dimensional orthogonality assumptions; may cause overshooting on ultra-low-dimensional toy datasets.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity in high-CFG regimes (CFG Ôëą 4.0): the default s_start can cause over-saturation, requiring grid search.
- The exact temporal profile of ╬│(t) is heuristic rather than analytically derived; Quad-In slightly outperforms Linear but the gap is small.
- MAFM and SSC are not fully complementary: combining them leads to oversaturation on larger models.
- The analysis is limited to independent coupling; the velocity deficit under structured couplings (e.g., mini-batch OT) is not studied.
