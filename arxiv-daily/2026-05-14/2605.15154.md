> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 RoSHAP 框架，通过 Bootstrap 重采样估计 SHAP 特征归因值的分布，并基于活跃度、强度和稳定性三个维度构建稳健的特征排序指标，解决了传统 SHAP 在不同随机种子下特征排名不稳定的问题。

> **TL;DR:** RoSHAP models the full distribution of SHAP feature attribution scores via bootstrap resampling and KDE, derives a Gaussian approximation under mild conditions to cut computational cost, and defines a robust ranking metric that rewards features which are active, strong, and stable across resampling runs.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了一个分布式的特征归因框架，通过 Bootstrap 重采样和核密度估计（KDE）建模 SHAP 值的完整经验分布，而不仅仅依赖单次运行的平均值。
- 证明了在温和正则条件下，聚合后的特征归因分数渐近服从高斯分布（基于 Lyapunov CLT），从而大幅减少了所需的 Bootstrap 重复次数（从 1000 次降至 10 次仍可保持良好效果）。
- 定义了稳健特征归因指标 RoSHAP = (1 - P₀) × m²/s，同时奖励活跃（非零频率高）、强度大（中位数值大）且稳定（方差小）的特征。
- 采用零膨胀混合模型（zero-inflated mixture）建模 SHAP 值中的精确零值问题，将稀疏性与量级解耦。
- 在仿真数据和四个真实数据集（Golub 基因表达、Musk 分子分类、UJIIndoorLoc 室内定位、CIFAR-10 图像分类）上验证了方法的有效性。

## Key Contributions

- A distributional framework for feature attribution that estimates the full empirical distribution of SHAP scores via bootstrap resampling and kernel density estimation (KDE), moving beyond single-run point estimates.
- A theoretical result proving the aggregated feature-level attribution score is asymptotically Gaussian under a Lyapunov CLT condition, enabling substantial reduction in bootstrap repetitions (1000 → 10 runs with competitive performance).
- The RoSHAP metric defined as (1 - P₀) × m²/s, jointly rewarding activity (low zero-probability), strength (high median nonzero attribution), and stability (low standard deviation).
- A zero-inflated mixture model that decouples sparsity from magnitude in SHAP values, particularly relevant for tree-based models where features absent from a prediction path receive exact zero attribution.
- Empirical validation on 4 real-world datasets spanning tabular classification, regression, and image classification (CIFAR-10 with ViT), plus controlled simulations.

## Methodology

The framework consists of three main components:

1. **Bootstrap-based distribution estimation.** Given a dataset D with n observations and p features, the procedure draws B bootstrap samples, fits a model on each, and computes out-of-bag (OOB) SHAP values. This yields B realizations of the feature-level attribution score Uⱼ = Σᵢ |Tᵢⱼ| for each feature j.

2. **Zero-inflated modeling and Gaussian approximation.** Sample-feature SHAP values Tᵢⱼ are modeled as a zero-inflated mixture: Tᵢⱼ ~ wᵢⱼδ₀ + (1-wᵢⱼ)Gᵢⱼ, where δ₀ is a point mass at zero and Gᵢⱼ is the nonzero component. The aggregated score Uⱼ is shown to be asymptotically Gaussian via Lyapunov CLT under independence and moment conditions, meaning the distribution can be summarized by just its mean and variance.

3. **RoSHAP metric.** For each feature j, three quantities are extracted from the estimated distribution: P₀ⱼ = Pr(Uⱼ = 0) (inactivity), mⱼ = Median(Uⱼ | Uⱼ > 0) (strength), and sⱼ = SD(Uⱼ) (noise). The final robust score is RoSHAPⱼ = (1 - P₀ⱼ) × mⱼ² / sⱼ, which rewards features that contribute frequently, with large magnitude, and consistently across resampling runs.

When the Lyapunov condition fails (e.g., small-sample settings like Golub with n=72), the distribution is estimated nonparametrically using KDE rather than the Gaussian shortcut.

## Results & Highlights

- **Simulation (n=600, d=1000, s=10 signals):** RoSHAP with 1000 bootstrap runs recovered 8 of 10 signal features in the top 12; with only 10 bootstrap runs, it still recovered 8 signals in the top 12. Single-run SHAP recovered only 4 signals in the top 12, missing the strongest signal x₁. Information gain, XGBoost gain, and LIME also showed unreliable rankings.
- **Golub (72 samples, 7129 genes):** RoSHAP provided clearer and more consistent gene rankings than seed-specific SHAP. Feature-selected models achieved strong accuracy and F1 with only 1-15 genes.
- **Musk V2 (6598 instances, 166 features):** Performance across metrics was comparable due to distributed predictive signal; RoSHAP remained competitive.
- **UJIIndoorLoc (21048 obs, 520 WAPs):** RoSHAP achieved competitive and stable regression performance (RMSE, MAE, MAPE) with LightGBM.
- **CIFAR-10 (ViT-base, 50 bootstrap runs):** The framework extended to patch-level image attribution, revealing that single-run explanations are sensitive to training randomness. Correct predictions showed attribution focused on the target object; incorrect predictions showed attribution on irrelevant regions, aiding error diagnosis.
- **Computational efficiency:** Reducing B from 1000 to 10 cuts SHAP computation by ~100x while maintaining broad data coverage (probability of observing each sample at least once ≈ 1 - 0.368¹⁰ ≈ 1).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The distributional view of feature attribution is a meaningful contribution. While bootstrap-based stability analysis for SHAP has been explored before, the formal zero-inflated modeling, the Gaussian approximation theorem, and the specific RoSHAP metric combining activity/strength/stability are novel.
- **Rigor:** The theoretical contribution is solid — the Lyapunov CLT derivation is standard but correctly applied. The zero-inflated decomposition is sensible for tree-based models. However, the independence assumption between (Bᵢⱼ, Hᵢⱼ) pairs across observations i is strong and may not hold when features are correlated.
- **Reproducibility:** Code is publicly available. Experiments use standard datasets (Golub, Musk, UJIIndoorLoc, CIFAR-10) and common models (XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost, ViT).
- **Significance:** The problem of unstable SHAP rankings is practically important, especially in high-stakes domains like genomics. The solution is practical and well-motivated.
- **Red flags:** The RoSHAP metric formula m²/s has no principled derivation — it is a heuristic combination. The paper does not compare against recent stability-focused methods like stochastic SHAP or LOCO with repeated subsampling baselines in depth. The Gaussian approximation may not hold in small-sample settings (acknowledged by the authors, who fall back to KDE). The CIFAR-10 experiment is qualitative (single image examples) rather than quantitative.

## Strengths

- Addresses a practically important and underappreciated problem (SHAP instability across random splits).
- Strong theoretical grounding with the asymptotic Gaussian result enabling computational savings.
- Comprehensive evaluation across diverse data types (genomics, molecular, localization, images) and model families.
- The three-component metric (activity, strength, stability) is interpretable and well-motivated.
- Public code repository for reproducibility.

## Limitations

- Bootstrap-based approach is still significantly more expensive than single-run SHAP, especially for large models or datasets.
- The RoSHAP formula is heuristic; no optimality guarantees are provided for this particular combination of components.
- The independence assumption in the CLT derivation may be violated in practice with correlated features.
- The CIFAR-10 / ViT experiment is limited to qualitative illustration with cherry-picked examples rather than systematic evaluation.
- No comparison with stability-focused feature selection methods from the statistics literature (e.g., stability selection by Meinshausen and Bühlmann).
- The method inherits all limitations of SHAP itself (e.g., computational cost for non-tree models, potential inconsistencies with causal interpretations).

## Relevance

该论文与可解释机器学习和特征选择直接相关。RoSHAP 提供了一种更稳健的特征归因方法，对于需要可靠特征排名的应用场景（如基因发现、模型诊断）具有实用价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does RoSHAP compare with stability selection (Meinshausen & Bühlmann, 2010) in terms of both feature recovery and computational cost?
- Can the Gaussian approximation be extended to capture higher-order dependencies between features, addressing the independence limitation?
- What is the sensitivity of RoSHAP rankings to the choice of the underlying model (e.g., neural network vs. tree ensemble) when the model class is misspecified?
- How does the framework scale to datasets with millions of features (e.g., genomics with full-genome data)?
- Could the three-component RoSHAP metric be replaced by a learned combination optimized for downstream task performance?
