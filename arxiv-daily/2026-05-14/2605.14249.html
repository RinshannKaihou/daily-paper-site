EnergyLens: Predictive Energy-Aware Exploration for Multi-GPU LLM Inference Optimization

arXiv: 2605.14249

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14249

一句话总结： EnergyLens 提出了一个基于 einsum 接口的端到端框架，无需实际 GPU 运行即可预测多 GPU 场景下 LLM 推理的能耗与延迟，在 Llama3 和 Qwen3-MoE 上实现了 9.25%–13.19% 的能耗 MAPE，并揭示了分布式推理中显著的能耗优化空间。
TL;DR: EnergyLens is an end-to-end framework that predicts LLM inference energy and latency from high-level einsum-based specifications without requiring implementations, execution traces, or GPU access. Validated on Llama3-70B and Qwen3-MoE across tensor-parallel and expert-parallel configurations, it achieves 9.25%–13.19% energy MAPE and reveals up to 52.9x energy variation across decode configurations.

核心贡献

  • 提出了 EnergyLens 框架，用户仅需几行 einsum 表达式即可描述稠密模型和 MoE 模型的 LLM 推理行为（含算子融合、张量并行、专家并行和计算-通信重叠），无需实际代码或 GPU 访问即可预测能耗与延迟
  • 构建了分布式能耗建模栈：包含经验驱动的通信能耗模型、重叠感知聚合机制和负载不均衡感知的 MoE 建模，支持 Megatron 风格的计算-通信重叠分析
  • 在 Llama3-70B 和 Qwen3-30B-A3B 上验证，多 GPU prefill 和 decode 能耗 MAPE 在 9.25%–13.19% 之间，重叠配置下 MAPE 为 12.97%
  • 揭示了关键能效洞察：分布式推理中 prefill 和 decode 的优化目标冲突强烈支持分离式服务（disaggregated serving）；计算-通信重叠难以凭直觉优化，但 EnergyLens 能正确识别 Pareto 最优配置

Key Contributions

  • Introduces EnergyLens, a framework where practitioners specify dense and MoE LLM inference architectures in a few lines of einsum-based code with parallelism and overlap annotations, producing energy and latency predictions without execution traces or GPU access
  • Develops a distributed energy modeling stack combining empirically driven communication energy modeling, overlap-aware aggregation, and load-imbalance-aware MoE modeling for tensor parallelism, expert parallelism, and Megatron-style compute-communication overlap
  • Achieves energy MAPEs of 9.25%–13.19% for multi-GPU prefill and decode on Llama3 and Qwen3-MoE, and 12.97% across SM allocations for Megatron-style overlap
  • Demonstrates energy-driven design-space exploration: reveals up to 1.47x prefill and 52.9x decode energy variation across configurations, motivates disaggregated serving, and identifies Pareto-optimal overlap configurations that naive maximum-overlap heuristics miss

Methodology

EnergyLens operates through a pipeline of four components:

  1. EnergyLens Interface: An einsum-based specification language where developers describe LLM operators (e.g., QKV projections, attention, FFN) with lightweight annotations for parallelism (parallel="K") and overlap. The same specification covers both prefill (variable sequence length) and decode (s=1) phases. Model dimensions are supplied at runtime, so one specification covers different scales.
  1. Lightweight Interpreter: Maps each einsum specification to GEMM dimensions (M, K, N), arithmetic intensity, and required communication kernels. For MoE models, it derives effective expert counts (E) and tokens-per-expert (T) accounting for routing load imbalance and grouped-GEMM tile quantization. Supports both uniform-routing assumption (early-stage) and measured routing statistics from single-GPU execution.
  1. Communication Energy Model: Empirically driven model for multi-GPU communication (AllReduce, All-to-All) that captures overhead-dominated behavior at small transfer sizes missed by bandwidth-based models. Validated against NCCL microbenchmarks on A100 GPUs.
  1. Overlap-Aware Aggregation: Combines compute kernel predictions (using EnergAIzer as primary backend) with communication predictions under Megatron-style compute-communication overlap. Accounts for SM partitioning between compute and communication kernels.

The framework is modular: alternative compute latency backends (NeuSight, Li et al.) can be plugged in. Validation uses TensorRT-LLM for Llama3 and Qwen3-MoE on A100-80GB GPUs with NVML power measurement at 10ms sampling.

Results & Highlights

  • Energy accuracy: Multi-GPU prefill energy MAPE of 12.18% (Llama3-70B), 9.25% (Qwen3-30B-A3B); decode energy MAPE of 12.86% (Llama3-70B), 13.19% (Qwen3). Overlap energy MAPE of 12.97% across SM allocations.
  • Prefill insights: TP2 dominates the Pareto frontier for 70B prefill due to reduced communication overhead; B4 TP2 is both faster and consumes 24% less energy than B16 TP8 at ISL=4096 (confirmed by measurements: 30% energy savings).
  • Decode insights: Larger batch sizes yield dramatic EPOT reduction (92.63% at B16 vs B1 under TP8) with minimal latency increase, but are memory-constrained. This motivates disaggregated serving where prefill uses lower batch sizes while decode uses higher batch sizes.
  • Overlap insights: Maximum-overlap heuristic recovers only 20% of the exact measured Pareto frontier; EnergyLens predictions recover 69% without any measurement, identifying configurations where disabling overlap or using moderate SM allocation is preferable.
  • Fusion evaluation: QKV fusion yields 4.37% average energy savings in prefill; savings increase to 8.18% in decode where operations are more memory-bound.
  • TDP inadequacy: TDP-based energy estimation can overestimate decode energy by up to 60% due to strong power variations between prefill and decode phases.

The paper uses real production-grade frameworks (TensorRT-LLM) and real hardware measurements (NVML power sampling).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-to-high. The einsum-based specification is a clean abstraction that differentiates from prior work requiring execution traces. The combination of communication energy modeling with overlap awareness and MoE load-imbalance modeling is novel. However, the compute kernel backend (EnergAIzer) is from the authors' own prior work.
  • Rigor: Strong. Systematic evaluation across two model families (dense and MoE), multiple parallelism strategies, full sweeps of batch size / sequence length / TP degree / SM allocations. Reproducibility check in Appendix L is a commendable practice.
  • Reproducibility: Good. Detailed experimental setup in Appendix M with GPU-hour costs, specific software versions (TensorRT-LLM v0.14/v1.0, Megatron-LM v0.15.3), and dataset information. No code release mentioned, which is a limitation.
  • Significance: High for practitioners. The framework addresses a real gap — the cost of energy profiling multi-GPU configurations is prohibitive (12 hours of 8-GPU profiling replaced by 15 minutes of simulation). The insights on disaggregated serving and overlap tuning are practically actionable.
  • Red flags: (1) Decode latency prediction has high error (MAPE 25.45%), which the authors honestly acknowledge but could limit utility for latency-sensitive optimization. (2) No code or artifact release mentioned. (3) The framework depends on EnergAIzer (the authors' prior tool) as the primary backend — independence is unclear. (4) Limited to A100 GPUs; generalization to H100/B200 is not discussed. (5) Communication model is validated only on NCCL; other collectives libraries are not tested.

Strengths

  • Elegant einsum-based interface that dramatically reduces specification burden
  • First framework to jointly model energy for compute, communication, overlap, and MoE load imbalance
  • Strong empirical validation with 1,054 GPU-hours and reproducibility checks
  • Actionable insights for deployment (disaggregated serving motivation, overlap tuning)
  • Modular design supporting alternative compute backends
  • Honest reporting of limitations (decode latency errors, backend-specific issues)

Limitations

  • Decode latency prediction error is high (25.45% MAPE) due to challenges with low-arithmetic-intensity kernels — this limitation persists across all tested backends
  • Evaluated only on NVIDIA A100 GPUs; generalization to newer hardware (H100, B200) is untested
  • No public code or artifact release at the time of publication
  • MoE evaluation limited to one model (Qwen3-30B-A3B) with at most 4 GPUs (constrained by KV head count)
  • Does not model custom kernel libraries (e.g., FlashAttention, custom CUDA kernels) that may differ from cuBLAS-dispatched kernels
  • Framework does not capture pipeline parallelism or context parallelism energy effects

Relevance

这篇论文与 LLM 推理评估和分布式系统可靠性直接相关——它提供了无需实际部署即可量化推理能耗和延迟的工具，填补了系统级推理质量评估中能效维度的空白。其关于配置空间探索的方法论也与 ML 系统可靠性研究有交叉。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does EnergyLens transfer to next-generation hardware (H100, B200) with different SM architectures and NVLink bandwidth?
  • Can the communication energy model be extended to non-NCCL collectives or heterogeneous GPU clusters?
  • What is the energy impact of pipeline parallelism and context parallelism, which are increasingly used for long-context models?
  • Could the framework incorporate dynamic batching and request scheduling to model real serving scenarios with variable arrival rates?
  • How sensitive are the overlap optimization insights to network topology (e.g., NVLink vs. PCIe interconnects)?
