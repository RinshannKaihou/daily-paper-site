> **一句话总结：** 本文构建零失配的 rollout 引擎 VeXact 作为诊断基线，证明训练-推理不匹配（TIM，由 kernel/实现差异导致的 token 级概率漂移）本身就能独立引发 LLM RL 训练崩溃，并指出 recomputation 与 bypass 两种实现各自如何改变有效优化目标、以及 rcorr + TIS 组合可逼近零失配基线。

> **TL;DR:** The authors build VeXact — a rollout engine achieving bit-wise-aligned log-probabilities with FSDP via batch-invariant kernels — and use it as a TIM-free diagnostic baseline. With TIM as the only difference (vLLM vs VeXact), a Qwen3-30B-A3B MoE REINFORCE run on DAPO-Math-17k collapses from training reward 0.574 / AIME24 val 0.293 to 0.255 / 0.067, while VeXact reaches 0.753 / 0.534; in Qwen3-1.7B GRPO on Sanity-Test-R1D-1.5B, vLLM recomputation collapses to near-zero reward after step ~1665 whereas the rcorr-based TIS+SRS patch closely tracks VeXact.

## 核心贡献

- **VeXact 零失配基线**：通过统一 HuggingFace 模型实现 + batch-invariant（确定性、batch 无关）kernel（RMSNorm、batched GEMM、Fused MoE，禁用 KV-split 的 FlashAttention），让 rollout 与 FSDP 训练引擎在相同权重下产出位级一致的 token log-prob；代码开源于 verl-project/vexact。
- **TIM 因果分离**：在 REINFORCE 下以 TIM 为唯一变量，证明 TIM 单独即可导致 Qwen3-1.7B（dense）与 Qwen3-30B-A3B（MoE）的 RL 训练崩溃，而非二阶副作用。
- **失配路径机制**：区分两种 π_old 获取实现——trainer-side recomputation 与 rollout-side bypass，证明二者会改变有效优化问题：recomputation 在 advantage-weighted 损失上产生符号失衡的偏斜（C(r)=-(r-1)A 在正/负 advantage 上非对称放大），bypass 则因训练侧 ∇log π 的失配空间导致策略更新无法落地。
- **算法补偿诊断**：在 VeXact 参考下系统比较 token-level 截断重要性采样（TIS, τ_tok=2）与 sequence-level rejection sampling（SRS, τ_seq=0.001），证明以 r_corr（=π_old^train/π_old^rollout）为过滤信号的 r_corr+SRS+TIS 组合可最接近地逼近零失配基线。

## Key Contributions

- Release **VeXact**, a lightweight (~nano-vLLM LOC) zero-mismatch rollout engine that achieves bit-wise alignment with the FSDP trainer by reusing HuggingFace model implementations and registering deterministic, batch-invariant kernels (RMSNorm, batched matmul, batch-invariant Fused MoE, KV-split-disabled attention), integrated with chunked prefill, CUDAGraph, pipeline parallelism, and optimistic KV allocation.
- **Causally isolate TIM** as a destabilizer: by using REINFORCE (single policy-gradient step per rollout) to remove PPO-clipping confounds, they show that with TIM as the only difference (vLLM vs VeXact) RL training collapses on both dense (Qwen3-1.7B) and MoE (Qwen3-30B-A3B) models.
- **Explain failure modes** of recomputation and bypass at the objective level via a zero-centered loss contribution C(r)=-(r-1)A, showing recomputation induces a sign-imbalanced skew (asymmetric amplification of harmful contributions under PPO clipping) that aggregate KL estimators K1/K3 cannot detect during the first ~700 steps.
- **Diagnose algorithmic TIM compensations**: compare truncated importance sampling (TIS, τ_tok=2), sequence-level rejection sampling (SRS, τ_seq=0.001), masking signals (r_corr vs r^rollout_ppo), and KL instantiations (K1 vs K3), showing that r_corr-based SRS with TIS most closely tracks the VeXact reference.

## Methodology

**TIM definition.** For context x, response y=(a1,…,aT), state s_t=(x,y_<t), the token-level mismatch is δ_t = log π_old^train(a_t|s_t) − log π_old^rollout(a_t|s_t). It exists before any choice of update rule (REINFORCE/PPO/GRPO) and is infrastructure-level, hence not addressable by naive PPO clipping.

**VeXact construction.** TIM has two sources: (1) model/kernel implementation differences between training (FSDP/Megatron) and inference (vLLM/SGLang) engines (e.g., FlashInfer only on inference side); (2) variation in kernel reduction order and tiling (atomic-add non-determinism, batch-dependent auto-tuning triggering different launch grids). VeXact removes both by reusing HF model code in the FSDP engine and fixing tiling/reduction via batch-invariant kernels.

**Diagnostic objective.** REINFORCE with batch-whitened advantages is used as the cleanest probe (one policy-gradient step per rollout, no clipping).

**Recomputation vs bypass (PPO/GRPO).** Both use the clipped surrogate L^ppo(r,A) = -min(rA, clip(r,1-ε,1+ε)A). Recomputation uses r^train_ppo = π_θ/π_old^train in the denominator; bypass uses r^rollout_ppo = π_θ/π_old^rollout. The numerator π_θ^train is always trainer-side.

**Zero-centered loss contribution.** C(r^ppo) = -(r^ppo − 1)·A_t, which shares the gradient of -r·A but is zero when r=1, isolating the ratio-dependent surrogate contribution.

**Algorithmic patches.** Correction ratio r_corr = π_old^train / π_old^rollout. Token-level TIS truncates r_corr at τ_tok=2; sequence-level rejection masks trajectories where K1 or K3 aggregated over tokens exceeds τ_seq=0.001. Four configurations tested: srs-k3-corr-ratio, srs-k3-ppo-ratio, tis-srs-k1-corr-ratio, tis-srs-k3-corr-ratio.

**Experimental settings (Appendix Table 2).** Dense GRPO: Qwen3-1.7B, GRPO, Sanity-Test-R1D-1.5B, AIME2024 every 50 steps, global batch 64 / minibatch 16 / rollout group 8, prompt 1024 / response 8192, 1 node 8×H100, FSDP2 + vLLM/VeXact. Dense REINFORCE: same model/data, global batch 64. MoE REINFORCE: Qwen3-30B-A3B, REINFORCE, DAPO-Math-17k, AIME2024 every 20 steps, global batch 512, prompt 2048 / response 20480, 8 nodes 64×H100.

## Results & Highlights

- **TIM alone collapses MoE REINFORCE (Fig. 2)**: on Qwen3-30B-A3B with DAPO-Math-17k, the vLLM run improves until step ~280 then degrades from training reward 0.574 and AIME24 val 0.293 down to 0.255 and 0.067, while VeXact reaches training reward 0.753 and val reward 0.534.
- **TIM collapses dense GRPO too (Fig. 3)**: on Qwen3-1.7B with Sanity-Test-R1D-1.5B, VeXact keeps training reward ~0.93; vLLM recomputation degrades from ~0.87 to ~0.40 in the first 650 steps, partially recovers, then collapses to near-zero after step ~1665; vLLM bypass degrades to ~0.40 but does not collapse to zero and shows no comparably synchronized loss spike.
- **Aggregate KL is blind to early failure (Fig. 4)**: under recomputation, K1 and K3 KL estimators stay nearly flat and close to VeXact over the first 700 steps even though reward is already degrading; under bypass, K1/K3 rise visibly.
- **Sign-imbalanced contribution (Fig. 5)**: recomputation skews C(r)=-(r-1)A asymmetrically across positive vs negative advantage samples — harmful contributions are amplified while offsetting ones are not, before any KL spike appears.
- **Algorithmic patch ranking (Fig. 6, §4.2)**: r_corr-based SRS beats r^rollout_ppo-based SRS; combining token-level TIS (τ_tok=2) with r_corr-based sequence rejection (τ_seq=0.001) most closely tracks VeXact; K1 vs K3 choice has comparatively minor effect. Configurations: srs-k3-corr-ratio, srs-k3-ppo-ratio, tis-srs-k1-corr-ratio, tis-srs-k3-corr-ratio.
- **Token-level drift magnitude (Fig. 1, Table 1)**: on Qwen3-8B (bf16) greedy-sampled on an AIME2024 problem, mean |δ_t| is small per batch but per-token max |δ_t| reaches ~1.0 over a 2000-step run; one observed argmax flip changes "that" → ":\n\n".

## Critical Assessment

- **Sample of models is narrow.** All main results use the Qwen3 family (Qwen3-1.7B dense, Qwen3-30B-A3B MoE, plus a Qwen3-8B example for δ_t illustration); generalisation to Llama, Mistral, or proprietary stacks (Megatron + SGLang) is asserted but not demonstrated. The Discussion section itself flags this as a limitation.
- **Algorithmic-patch tuning is ad hoc without VeXact.** The authors admit that τ_tok=2 and τ_seq=0.001 are diagnostic instantiations, and that without VeXact as a ground-truth reference "patch configuration remains largely ad hoc" — so the practical recipe for someone without a zero-mismatch baseline is underspecified.
- **Throughput cost of VeXact is acknowledged but not quantified.** Batch-invariant kernels trade performance for numerical stability (fixed tiling/reduction); the paper says VeXact "retains reasonable throughput" via chunked prefill / CUDAGraph / pipeline parallelism, but provides no tokens/sec or wall-clock comparison vs vLLM, so the production-readiness case is incomplete.
- **The REINFORCE diagnostic is cleaner than GRPO but less representative.** The headline causal-isolation result uses REINFORCE (no clipping); the GRPO results (Fig. 3) show that bypass fails differently and less synchronously, so the precise TIM-to-collapse causal chain in production PPO/GRPO is partly indirect (relies on the zero-centered contribution argument rather than direct observation).

## Strengths

- Cleanly isolates an infrastructure-level confounder that the RLHF/RL community routinely conflates with off-policy drift, by building a true zero-mismatch reference rather than another stabilisation heuristic.
- The zero-centered loss contribution C(r)=-(r-1)A is an elegant diagnostic that explains *why* recomputation silently distorts the optimisation surface before KL estimators notice.
- Concrete, falsifiable algorithmic-patch ablations with explicit thresholds (τ_tok=2, τ_seq=0.001) and a clear ranking (r_corr > r^rollout_ppo; TIS+SRS most faithful).
- Open-sources VeXact under the verl-project umbrella, making the diagnostic baseline reproducible.

## Limitations

- Evaluation scale is finite — limited model families, datasets (Sanity-Test-R1D-1.5B, DAPO-Math-17k), and system configurations; the authors explicitly state generalisation across broader RL settings is unproven.
- No quantitative throughput / overhead numbers for VeXact, despite the batch-invariant-kernel cost being the central practical concern.
- The paper does not study interaction with other known stability levers (e.g., DAPO non-symmetric clipping, GSPO sequence-level ratios, R3 MoE routing replay) beyond a brief related-work mention.
- Asynchronous RL is discussed only at the implication level; no async experiments are run despite async being where TIM is arguably most damaging.

## Relevance

高度契合"LLM Training Stability & Observability"——把训练-推理数值失配定位为可观测、可诊断的一阶系统级因子，并提供 VeXact 作为校准工具。同时也直接服务于"Computation Reliability & Precision Errors"（bf16 浮点非结合性、kernel tiling/reduction 顺序、batch 无关 kernel）与"Silent Data Corruption (SDC)"（rollout 侧 δ_t 重尾极值、argmax 翻转即为静默数值腐蚀）。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does TIM severity scale with precision format — does FP16 (cf. Qi et al. 2025, arXiv:2510.26788) or FP8 narrow/widen δ_t tails relative to BF16?
2. Can the zero-centered contribution diagnostic C(r)=-(r-1)A be turned into an online training-time alarm that fires before KL estimators detect failure?
3. How do DAPO's asymmetric clipping and GSPO's sequence-level ratios interact with TIM-induced sign-imbalanced skew — do they amplify or suppress it?
4. What is the VeXact vs vLLM throughput gap on production-scale (e.g., 70B+ MoE) training, and can TBIK (arXiv:2511.17826) close it?
5. In asynchronous RL, does TIM compound with policy staleness multiplicatively, or are they orthogonal failure axes?
