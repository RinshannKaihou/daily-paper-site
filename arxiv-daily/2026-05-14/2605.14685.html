Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking and Goldstone Modes for Deep Information Propagation

arXiv: 2605.14685

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14685

一句话总结： 本文借鉴物理学中自发对称性破缺产生的Goldstone模式理论，在U(1)和O(k)等变的深层神经网络中严格证明了一类受保护的自由度可以在无需残差连接或归一化的条件下跨层和跨时间步稳定传播信息，并在100层前馈网络和长序列RNN/GRU任务上验证了该机制的有效性。
TL;DR: By constructing deep networks whose internal layers are equivariant under continuous symmetries (U(1), O(k)), the authors show that spontaneous symmetry breaking yields Goldstone-like modes that keep a component of the input-output Jacobian at O(1) regardless of depth, enabling training of 100-layer MLPs without skip connections or normalization and improving RNN/GRU performance on long-sequence tasks.

核心贡献

  • 将物理学中的Goldstone定理引入深度学习信号传播理论：当等变网络的内部层在连续对称性（U(1)或O(k)）下发生自发对称性破缺时，网络涌现出类Goldstone自由度，使得输入-输出Jacobians的一个分量始终保持在O(1)，无论网络多深。
  • 提出了一种无需残差连接和归一化的深层网络训练机制：在SSB相中，100层MLP无需LayerNorm、BatchNorm或skip connections即可训练，且可训练范围覆盖宽泛的超参数区间（不限于edge of chaos的临界点）。
  • 通过等变性保证表征多样性的下界：O(k)等变网络中表征的有效秩最低为k，从根本上防止了深层网络中的秩坍缩问题。
  • 将Goldstone机制扩展到循环网络：U(1)等变RNN和GRU在变延迟复制任务（Tmax=200）和psMNIST上显著优于非等变基线，且参数更少。

Key Contributions

  • Derives a mean-field theory for equivariant networks at initialization using stochastic path integrals, obtaining a closed-form order parameter evolution equation and demonstrating a sharp phase transition at σ_W = 1 separating unbroken and spontaneously broken symmetry phases.
  • Proves that in the SSB phase, a protected component of the input-output Jacobian (Eq. 7) is O(1) regardless of depth, providing a guaranteed information channel — unlike vanilla networks where Jacobians collapse or explode.
  • Shows empirically that trainability of 100-layer MLPs on Fashion-MNIST begins precisely at the SSB phase transition, with no other architectural mitigations needed.
  • Demonstrates that larger symmetry groups O(k) store more information in Goldstone modes: O(4) equivariant networks show no degradation up to 100 layers, and an O(24) RNN matches GRU performance on psMNIST despite having no gating mechanism.
  • Achieves strong results on long-sequence benchmarks: U(1) GRU solves copy task at Tmax=200 (only model to do so), and equivariant models consistently outperform non-equivariant counterparts on psMNIST across all parameter ranges.

Methodology

The paper develops a complete theoretical and empirical framework:

Theoretical framework (Section 2):

  • Considers deep networks with L+2 layers where internal layers f^l are equivariant under a symmetry group G (U(1) or O(k)). Input/output layers are general and break equivariance.
  • For U(1): representations are complex z_i ∈ C with phase-rotational equivariance; nonlinearity ϕ(z) = tanh(|z|) · z/|z| preserves the phase while compressing the magnitude.
  • For O(k): representations are x ∈ R^{N×k} split into N blocks of k-dimensions; weight matrices take the form W_{αa;βb} = w_{αβ} δ_{ab} (identity within blocks, unconstrained mixing between blocks).
  • Uses stochastic path integrals (Martin-Siggia-Rose formalism) in the large-N limit to derive the order parameter evolution equation c_{l+1} = 2∫ du u e^{-u²} ϕ(u σ_W √c_l)². For tanh nonlinearity, the fixed point c*=0 becomes unstable at σ_W > 1, signaling the SSB phase transition.
  • Analyzes two-input correlations to show the relative phase φ_l between inputs is exactly conserved across layers (φ_{l+1} = φ_l), identifying this as the Goldstone mode from breaking U(1)_a × U(1)_b → diagonal subgroup.
  • Derives that the protected Jacobian component (Eq. 7) is proportional to the order parameter and thus O(1) throughout the SSB phase.

Recurrent extensions (Section 3):

  • Equivariant RNN: h_t = ϕ_G(u_t + W_G h_{t-1}) with U(1)-equivariant (complex) or O(k)-equivariant (block-structured) recurrent weights.
  • Equivariant GRU: gates are phase-invariant functions of |h_{t-1}|, while candidate hidden update remains U(1)-equivariant.

Results & Highlights

  • Fashion-MNIST (100-layer MLP): Test accuracy jumps from ~10% (random) to ~80% precisely at the SSB phase transition σ_W = 1, matching the theoretical prediction. Non-equivariant models with the same nonlinearity remain untrainable at all σ_W values.
  • Effective rank preservation: O(k) equivariant networks maintain minimum effective rank of k across all layers; generic networks can collapse to rank 0. The O(4) model shows no accuracy degradation from 20 to 100 layers.
  • Jacobian health: The protected Jacobian component remains stable during training for equivariant models, while the full Jacobian of generic models collapses — connecting to the rank-collapse phenomenon.
  • Copy task (Tmax=25, RNN): U(1) equivariant RNN (32 complex capsules, 64 real coords) dramatically outperforms non-equivariant RNNs with 32 or 64 real hidden units across 10 seeds.
  • Copy task (Tmax=100, GRU): U(1) GRU (6K real params) solves more often and with lower loss than non-equivariant GRU (15K real params).
  • Copy task (Tmax=200): Only the U(1) GRU achieves a seed that solves the task (test CE loss 3.9×10⁻⁷).
  • psMNIST: Equivariant RNNs and GRUs consistently outperform non-equivariant counterparts at all parameter counts. O(24) RNN (~96-97% accuracy) matches comparably-sized GRU performance and competes with specialized long-sequence models (IndRNN, Lipschitz RNN, coRNN, LEM).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The application of Goldstone's theorem to neural network signal propagation is genuinely novel and goes beyond superficial analogy — the authors derive actual mean-field equations, identify a sharp phase transition, and connect the order parameter to Jacobian properties. The key insight that equivariance can be used purely as an architectural tool (independent of task symmetry) is conceptually important.
  • Rigor: High. The theoretical framework is self-contained and derived from first principles using stochastic path integrals. Predictions (phase transition at σ_W = 1, Jacobian protection) are verified empirically. The mean-field theory matches numerical experiments well (Figure 2).
  • Reproducibility: Good. Architectures are clearly described (complex weights for U(1), block-structured weights for O(k), radial nonlinearities). Hyperparameter sweeps (learning rates, activations, initialization) are reported. Implementation uses standard PyTorch. Appendix provides full experimental details.
  • Significance: Moderate-to-High. The conceptual contribution (symmetry breaking as a design principle for information flow) is significant. However, experiments are limited to simple benchmarks (Fashion-MNIST, copy tasks, psMNIST). The approach has not been tested on modern architectures (Transformers, state-space models) or large-scale datasets.
  • Red flags: (1) The two-input correlation magnitude Δ decays exponentially (no non-trivial fixed point), meaning information in the magnitude channel still degrades with depth — only the phase is perfectly conserved. (2) The parameter savings from equivariance (N² vs (Nk)² parameters) means the models have fewer effective degrees of freedom, which could limit expressiveness. (3) No comparison against well-tuned baselines with skip connections on the same task — the 80% Fashion-MNIST accuracy for 100 layers is not compared to a 100-layer ResNet. (4) The restriction to simple symmetries (U(1), O(k)) may limit the richness of information that Goldstone modes can carry.

Strengths

  • Deep theoretical foundation rooted in well-established physics (Goldstone theorem, stochastic path integrals, order parameters).
  • Clean separation: equivariance is used purely for information propagation, not tied to task symmetry.
  • Sharp, falsifiable theoretical predictions (phase transition at σ_W = 1) confirmed by experiments.
  • Jacobian protection provides a principled alternative to heuristic solutions (skip connections, normalization).
  • Effective rank floor from representation theory elegantly prevents rank collapse.
  • Preliminary exploration of topological defects (vortices in 2D ConvRNNs) suggests further physics-inspired extensions.

Limitations

  • Experiments are limited to simple benchmarks; no evaluation on ImageNet, language modeling, or modern architectures (Transformers, Mamba/S4).
  • The magnitude channel of two-input correlations still decays exponentially (Δ_l ~ exp(-l/ξ_Δ)); only the phase is exactly preserved, potentially limiting the bandwidth of the Goldstone channel.
  • No head-to-head comparison with standard architectural solutions (ResNets, LayerNorm) on identical tasks and depths.
  • Equivariant weight structure reduces parameter count (N² vs (Nk)²), which may limit model expressiveness relative to parameter-matched baselines.
  • The approach requires choosing a symmetry group a priori; it is unclear how to automatically select the optimal group for a given task.
  • The authors themselves note: "our current experiments are limited to simple benchmarks."

Relevance

本文将凝聚态物理中的自发对称性破缺理论严格应用于深度网络的信息传播问题，与等变网络设计、深层网络可训练性理论以及序列建模中的长程依赖问题均密切相关。其通过对称性保护Jacobians的思路为理解深层网络为何可训练提供了新的理论视角。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the Goldstone mode mechanism be combined with existing architectural innovations (residual connections, attention) for additive gains, or does it subsume their role?
  • How does the mechanism interact with attention-based architectures — could equivariant attention layers preserve Goldstone modes in Transformers?
  • Is there a principled way to automatically discover the optimal symmetry group G for a given task or dataset?
  • Could the exponentially decaying magnitude channel (Δ) be stabilized by architectural modifications, achieving a true non-trivial fixed point as the authors speculate?
  • What is the relationship between the Goldstone modes here and the "edge of chaos" criticality in dynamical isometry approaches — are they complementary or competing mechanisms?
  • Do topological defects (vortices, domain walls) provide a complementary information channel to Goldstone modes in practical settings?
