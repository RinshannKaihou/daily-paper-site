> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Exemplar Partitioning（EP），一种无需训练的激活空间聚类方法，用流式 leader-clustering 构建 Voronoi 分区字典，以约1000倍低于 SAE 的计算成本在 AxBench 上达到 0.881 AUROC，并支持跨层/跨模型/跨 checkpoint 的字典对比、因果干预和 OOD 检测。

> **TL;DR:** Exemplar Partitioning (EP) constructs interpretable feature dictionaries from LLM activations via streaming leader-clustering into Voronoi partitions, achieving mean AUROC 0.881 on AxBench (within 0.030 of SAE-A) at ~1000x less build compute, with native support for cross-layer/model/checkpoint comparison, causal ablation (refusal collapse), and out-of-distribution detection.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了 Exemplar Partitioning（EP）方法：通过在线 leader-clustering 对 LLM 激活空间进行 Voronoi 分区，无需训练，单次流式扫描即可构建特征字典，计算成本比 SAE 低约 1000 倍。
- EP 字典以真实观测激活（exemplar）为锚点，字典大小不由预设参数决定，而由激活空间几何结构自动确定，使得跨层、跨模型、跨训练 checkpoint 的字典可以直接对比。
- 在 Gemma-2-2B-it L20 上，EP p1 在 AxBench 500 个概念检测任务中达到 0.881 AUROC，比 GemmaScope SAE canonical 入口高 0.126，仅比 SAE-A 低 0.030。
- 发现拒绝行为（refusal）在指令微调模型中集中在一个 EP 区域，对该区域的 exemplar 进行消融可以将拒绝率降低最多 Δ = −0.96。
- EP p10 one-hot 编码在 ℓ₀ = 1 条件下保留了原始激活探测准确率的 ~97%，揭示密度结构本身承载了大部分线性可解码信息。
- 最近 exemplar 距离可免费提供 OOD 检测信号，能区分 Pile 文本与随机 token 激活。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces Exemplar Partitioning (EP), an unsupervised, training-free method that builds interpretable feature dictionaries from LLM activations via streaming leader-clustering into Voronoi partitions, at ~10³x less compute than comparable SAEs.
- EP dictionaries are anchored on observed activations (exemplars) rather than learned directions, with dictionary size determined by activation geometry rather than a preset hyperparameter, enabling direct cross-layer, cross-model, and cross-checkpoint comparison via shared/subsumed exemplars.
- Achieves mean AUROC 0.881 on AxBench (500 concepts, Gemma-2-2B-it L20) at p1 resolution — +0.126 over the canonical GemmaScope SAE and within 0.030 of SAE-A, the strongest dictionary-based baseline.
- Demonstrates that refusal in instruction-tuned Gemma concentrates in a single EP region whose exemplar ablation collapses held-out refusal by up to Δ = −0.96, with size-and-coherence-matched non-refusal nulls giving Δ = 0.
- Shows that EP p10 one-hot codes at ℓ₀ = 1 retain ~97% of raw-activation probe accuracy, indicating that density structure alone preserves most linearly-decodable identity.
- Provides free out-of-distribution detection via nearest-exemplar distance at inference, separating Pile from random-token activations.

## Methodology

EP builds feature dictionaries in four steps:

1. **Calibration**: Sample a fixed budget of activations from the build corpus, compute pairwise cosine distances, and set the distance threshold θ_p to the p-th percentile of these distances. Subtract the corpus mean μ to centre activations on the unit sphere.

2. **Streaming construction**: Each incoming activation is centred, L2-normalised, and compared against the current exemplar matrix. If the nearest exemplar is within θ_p, the activation joins that region; otherwise it becomes a new exemplar. Construction stops when a saturation window (default: one full extraction batch of 16,384 activations) passes with no new exemplars.

3. **Region representation**: Each region stores three geometric quantities — the immutable exemplar direction e_i, the mean member direction m_i, and the member coherence c_i — plus member count and example activations. The statistic D_i = log₁₀(N_i · c_i²) predicts cross-seed reproducibility (Spearman ρ ≈ 0.68).

4. **Inference**: New activations are centred, normalised, and assigned to the nearest exemplar. The dictionary can be 1-sparse (closest region only), n-sparse (top-n regions), or dense (full distance vector).

The key design choice is using fixed observed exemplars rather than averaged centroids: this prevents centroid drift, ensures coherent region representatives, and enables direct exemplar matching across dictionaries built from the same data stream.

Experiments are conducted on Gemma-2-2B (base and instruction-tuned) across layers L4, L12, and L20. Dictionaries are built from the Pile corpus. Evaluation includes AxBench latent concept detection (500 concepts), SAEBench probing, refusal localisation and causal ablation, cross-checkpoint Hungarian matching, domain saturation analysis (math/code/chat), and OOD detection.

## Results & Highlights

- **AxBench concept detection**: EP p1 exemplar reaches mean AUROC 0.881 across all 500 concepts. Finer EP dictionaries (p1, p2, p4) all beat the canonical GemmaScope SAE (0.755). Text concepts score 0.896, math 0.851, code 0.841 at p1 exemplar.
- **Probing preservation**: EP p10 one-hot at ℓ₀ = 1 retains ~97% of raw-activation test accuracy (0.930 vs IdentitySAE's 0.959) on SAEBench at Gemma-2-2B L12.
- **Refusal ablation**: Ablating the refusal-loaded region's exemplar direction at L20 collapses held-out refusal by Δ = −0.96 at p = 12, while size-and-coherence-matched non-refusal nulls give Δ = 0. The exemplar basis outperforms the mean-member basis by 0.4–0.6 across the working percentile range, despite the two directions being ~0.94 cosine-similar.
- **Cross-checkpoint matching**: Hungarian matching between base and instruction-tuned Gemma-2-2B p10 dictionaries shows low median matched cosines (0.24 at L12, 0.20 at L20), with only 15 and 5 matched pairs above cosine 0.7 respectively, indicating that instruction tuning largely re-anchors the base model's directional structure.
- **Domain saturation**: At p = 8, chat saturates at larger dictionary sizes than code across all layers (58–89 vs 42–44 regions), with math non-monotonic (peaking at L12 with 62 regions), suggesting saturation size measures representation complexity per domain per layer.
- **OOD detection**: Random-token activations sit 0.04–0.08 further from their nearest exemplar than Pile activations, with the gap widening at finer resolution. Bulgarian Wikipedia (under-represented in the Pile) falls between Pile and random.
- **EP vs SAE comparison**: ~20% of EP regions match an SAE feature at F1 > 0.5 at p10. EP and SAEs agree where directions are both density-concentrated and linearly separable; outside this intersection, EP captures broad content-anchored regions that SAEs splinter.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Jessica Rumbelow** is affiliated with Leap Laboratories. This is a single-author paper, which is noteworthy for the scope of the contribution.
- The paper provides open-source code at `github.com/jessicarumbelow/exemplar-partitioning`.
- The experimental methodology is rigorous: comparisons use established benchmarks (AxBench, SAEBench, GemmaScope), include null controls (size-and-coherence-matched non-refusal partitions), and report results across multiple seeds and percentile resolutions.
- The paper is transparent about limitations, including seed dependence, the exploratory nature of several experiments, and the fact that centred-cosine geometry cannot represent features whose identity depends on activation magnitude.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. EP is a genuinely different approach to unsupervised feature discovery that avoids learned reconstruction objectives entirely. The insight that density structure alone carries most linearly-decodable signal is a meaningful contribution, though leader-clustering itself is not new (Hartigan 1975).
- **Rigor**: Generally strong. The refusal ablation includes proper null controls. The AxBench comparison is protocol-matched (labels at selection, not construction). However, several experiments (cross-checkpoint matching, domain saturation, OOD detection) are described as "small and exploratory" by the author herself, and lack the statistical depth of the benchmarked sections.
- **Reproducibility**: Good. Code is released. The method uses only standard library operations (cosine distance, leader-clustering). The calibration procedure is well-specified. Seed dependence is acknowledged and partially characterised via the D_i statistic.
- **Significance**: Potentially high for the interpretability community. The ~1000x compute reduction over SAEs could democratise feature dictionary construction. The cross-dictionary commensurability property (via shared exemplars) is unique and enables analyses that SAEs cannot easily support. However, the claim of broad utility beyond concept identification remains to be validated at scale.
- **Red flags**: (1) Single-author work with no peer review yet (preprint as of May 2026). (2) The refusal ablation is evaluated on a within-distribution held-out set (same source as build), not on OOD adversarial prompts. (3) The "working range" of percentile thresholds for refusal ablation (p ∈ {10, 12, 16}) is somewhat fragile, with failure at p = 8 due to fragmentation. (4) The paper does not evaluate EP on larger models (only Gemma-2-2B). (5) EP's one-hot representation is inherently lossier than SAEs for reconstruction tasks, which may limit downstream applications.

## Strengths

- Dramatically lower compute cost (~10³x) than SAEs for dictionary construction, making interpretability more accessible.
- Training-free: no learned parameters, no reconstruction objective, no hyperparameter tuning of dictionary size.
- Cross-dictionary commensurability via shared exemplars is a genuinely novel capability not available with SAEs.
- The finding that density structure preserves ~97% of probe accuracy at ℓ₀ = 1 is an important insight about activation geometry.
- Causal ablation of refusal works with proper null controls, and the exemplar-vs-mean analysis reveals precise directional localisation.
- The paper is unusually honest about limitations and the exploratory nature of several experiments.

## Limitations

- Seed dependence: leader-clustering is order-dependent, and while D_i predicts stability, the method is not seed-invariant. This is more transparent than SAE seed variance but still a practical concern.
- Reconstruction-unfriendly: EP's one-hot code is extremely lossy in raw bits (~7.7 bits per activation at p10), making it unsuitable for tasks requiring faithful reconstruction.
- Centred-cosine geometry assumption: the method cannot capture features whose identity depends on activation magnitude rather than direction.
- Scale: evaluated only on Gemma-2-2B (2B parameters). Behaviour on larger models is unknown.
- Percentile sensitivity: some applications (e.g., refusal ablation) work only within a specific percentile range, and the optimal range is not known a priori.
- Several key experiments (cross-checkpoint, domain saturation, OOD) are explicitly described as small and exploratory.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does EP scale to larger models (7B, 70B, frontier-scale)? Does the ~1000x compute advantage hold, and do the qualitative findings (density-preserves-probe-accuracy, refusal localisation, OOD signal) transfer?
2. Can the percentile selection problem be automated — e.g., by sweep-and-select based on a downstream criterion, or by adaptive thresholding during construction?
3. What is the relationship between EP region coherence and circuit-level functionality? Can EP regions be used as primitives for automated circuit discovery?
4. How does EP interact with non-residual-stream activations (MLP outputs, attention heads)? Could EP reveal complementary structure in these components?
5. Is the saturation-size-as-complexity-measure finding robust across more domains and models? Could it serve as a diagnostic for representation quality during training?
