Dimension-Level Intent Fidelity Evaluation for Large Language Models: Evidence from Structured Prompt Ablation

arXiv: 2605.14517

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14517

一句话总结： 本文通过5W3H结构化提示消融实验（2,880个输出、6个LLM、3种语言）提出维度级意图保真度评估框架，发现25.7%（中文）至58.6%（英文）的整体满分输出存在可测量的维度级意图缺失，揭示了一种区别于事实幻觉的"结构-保真分裂"对齐失败模式。
TL;DR: This paper proposes a dimension-level intent fidelity evaluation framework using 5W3H structured prompt ablation across 2,880 outputs (6 LLMs, 3 languages, 3 domains). It reveals a "structural-fidelity split" where 25.7% (Chinese) to 58.6% (English) of outputs with perfect holistic scores (GA=5) exhibit measurable dimensional intent deficits — a failure mode distinct from factual hallucination and invisible to current holistic benchmarks.

核心贡献

  • 提出维度级意图保真度评估框架：将评估拆分为结构恢复（s-ICMw）和意图保真（f-ICMw）两个独立层，分别测量每个语义维度，突破了整体评分的粒度瓶颈。
  • 发现系统性的"结构-保真分裂"现象：模型广泛恢复缺失维度的结构骨架（填充槽位），但不恢复用户特定的意图内容——作者称之为"浅层补偿"（shallow compensation）。
  • 大规模消融实验验证：覆盖6个LLM（Claude Sonnet 4、GPT-4o、DeepSeek-V3、Qwen-Max、Gemini 2.5 Pro、Kimi）、3种语言（中英日）、3个领域（旅行、商业、技术），共2,880条输出记录。
  • 公开-私有分解：对2,520个消融单元分类，揭示旅行领域公开率最高（0.514），"How-to-do"维度最公开（0.550），"Who"维度最私有（0.156）。
  • 权重容忍度高原效应：中等权重扰动几乎无影响，但严重维度反转（高低权重互换）一致性地造成WAS下降1.78–1.86分，零例外。

Key Contributions

  • Dimension-level intent fidelity framework decoupling structural recovery (s-ICMw) from intent fidelity (f-ICMw), revealing that holistic metrics miss dimensional deficits in 25.7–58.6% of seemingly perfect outputs.
  • Identification of the structural-fidelity split and shallow compensation: models fill structural slots with training-prior content without matching user-specific intent — a failure mode orthogonal to factual hallucination.
  • Public-private decomposition of 2,520 ablation cells: 31.5% public (recoverable from priors) vs. 68.5% private (user-specific, unrecoverable). Travel domain most public (0.514); Who and How-much dimensions most private.
  • Proxy annotation distinguishing prior inferability from default recoverability: these are separable mechanisms (e.g., travel|where: 85% recovery-public rate but only 10% proxy-inferability).
  • Weight-tolerance plateau: matched, uniform, and perturbed conditions cluster in a robustness plateau (f-ICMw 0.959–0.994); severe dimensional inversion falls to a cliff zone (f-ICMw 0.750–0.861), with the Perturbed–Mismatched gap 15–25× larger than the Uniform–Perturbed gap.

Methodology

The study uses the 5W3H structured prompting protocol (What, Why, Who, When, Where, How-to-do, How-much, How-feel) as a testbed. Thirty task scenarios span three domains (travel planning TR01–TR10, business analysis BZ01–BZ10, technical documentation TC01–TC10). For each task, one FULL condition and 7 ablation conditions (removing exactly one dimension each; What anchored as task identifier) are constructed, yielding 8 conditions × 30 tasks per model-language pair.

Models: Claude Sonnet 4, GPT-4o (frontier); DeepSeek-V3, Qwen-Max (strong); Gemini 2.5 Pro, Kimi (mid-tier). All six models contribute to Chinese (ZH) outputs (1,440 records); three international models cover English (EN, 720 records) and Japanese (JA, 720 records).

Evaluation metrics:

  • GA (Goal Alignment): holistic 1–5 score, judge blind to ablation condition.
  • s-ICMw (Structural Intent Coverage): per-dimension {0, 0.5, 1} for structural slot presence.
  • f-ICMw (Fidelity Intent Coverage): per-dimension {0, 0.5, 1} against the FULL prompt as gold reference; f-ICMw = Σ wᵢ·fᵢ.
  • DS (Deficiency Signature): whether removing dimension-k caused the expected failure pattern.

A three-pass scoring protocol (GA → ICMw → DS) with progressive information disclosure prevents judge contamination. Primary judge: DeepSeek-V3 across all 2,880 records.

Public-private classification: an ablation cell is "public" if f-ICMw ≥ 0.7 when that dimension is ablated; otherwise "private". External proxy annotation by GPT-4o and Claude Sonnet 4 (blind to outputs) distinguishes prior inferability from default recoverability.

Weight-perturbation experiment: four conditions (matched, uniform, ±30% perturbed, high/low-swapped mismatched) across 20 tasks, 3 models, 2 domains in two rounds (v2: 240 records; v3_clean: 120 leakage-audited records).

Human evaluation: 60 stratified samples (25 split-zone, 15 agree-high, 10 FULL-baseline, 10 agree-low) scored by two independent raters blind to condition and LLM scores.

Results & Highlights

  • Structural-fidelity split prevalence: 25.7% of Chinese outputs (370/1,440) and 58.6% of English outputs (372/634) with GA=5 had f-ICMw < 0.8 — holistic evaluation awarded full marks to outputs with detectable dimensional deficits.
  • Holistic ceiling effect: 84.7% of Chinese-language records received GA=5, demonstrating severe compression of quality variation.
  • Structural vs. fidelity divergence: s-ICMw ablation hypothesis supported at 83% (ZH), 89% (EN), 78% (JA); f-ICMw supported at only 17% (ZH), 0% (EN), 22% (JA). Models restore structural skeletons using training-prior content without matching user intent.
  • Human validation: split-zone outputs (LLM GA=5) received human mean GA=3.12 (95% CI: ±0.306). Dimensional fidelity f-ICMw correlated strongly with human judgment (Spearman ρ=0.695, p<0.001) while holistic GA did not (ρ=0.251, p=0.053, n.s.).
  • Public-private landscape: 795/2,520 cells (31.5%) public. Super-recovery effect in 54.1% of public cells (mean f-ICMw improvement of 0.118 upon ablation). Travel domain: 0.514 public rate; business 0.227; technical 0.205.
  • Inferability vs. recoverability dissociation: travel|where has 85% recovery-public rate but only 10% proxy-inferability — default-recoverability ≠ prior inferability.
  • Weight-tolerance plateau: matched/uniform/perturbed cluster at f-ICMw 0.959–0.994; mismatched drops to 0.750–0.861. WAS drops 1.78–1.86 points under mismatch with 100% consistency across all cells.
  • Cross-language robustness: domain ordering (travel > business ≈ technical) and dimension ordering (How-to-do most public, Who most private) preserved across ZH, EN, JA.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The dimension-level decomposition and the structural-fidelity split concept are genuinely novel. The distinction between this failure mode and factual hallucination is well-articulated and practically important. However, the framework is tightly coupled to the author's own 5W3H/PPS prompting protocol, raising questions about generalizability to arbitrary prompts.
  • Rigor: The experimental design is thorough — 6 models, 3 languages, 3 domains, structured ablation protocol, three-pass scoring, human evaluation, and proxy annotation. The public-private decomposition and weight-perturbation experiment add analytical depth. However, the primary judge being DeepSeek-V3 (a model also used as a subject) creates a circularity risk, though the human evaluation partially mitigates this.
  • Reproducibility: Strong — data, code, and prompts are publicly available on GitHub. PRS audit confirmed textual weight fidelity for all prompts. The ablation protocol and scoring rubrics are clearly specified.
  • Significance: High practical relevance for LLM evaluation methodology. The finding that 25.7–58.6% of "perfect" outputs have hidden dimensional deficits challenges current evaluation practices (MT-Bench, Chatbot Arena, HELM). The actionable recommendations (structured encoding, dimensional metrics, private-dimension prioritization) are concrete.
  • Red flags: (1) Single authorship for a study of this scale is unusual. (2) The author has a commercial interest in the 5W3H/PPS platform (Lateni AI), creating incentive for favorable framing. (3) The 5W3H dimensions are treated as natural/universal rather than one possible decomposition, which may overstate the framework's generality. (4) Human evaluation sample is small (N=60, 2 raters) — the inter-rater GA agreement (Cohen's κ=0.006) is alarmingly low, undermining confidence in holistic human evaluation as a baseline. (5) Using DeepSeek-V3 as both subject and primary judge is a methodological concern despite the blinding protocol.

Strengths

  • Identifies and rigorously characterizes a concrete, previously under-appreciated failure mode in LLM evaluation.
  • Multilingual, multi-domain, multi-model experimental design provides strong generalizability evidence.
  • Human evaluation validates the core claim that dimensional scores track human judgment better than holistic scores.
  • The public-private decomposition and inferability vs. recoverability distinction provide actionable diagnostic tools.
  • Open data and code ensure reproducibility.
  • The weight-tolerance plateau finding has clear practical implications: completeness beats precision in prompt design.

Limitations

  • The framework is tied to the 5W3H protocol; generalizability to unstructured or differently-structured prompts is not demonstrated.
  • The author's commercial interest in the 5W3H/PPS platform (Lateni AI) may introduce bias in framing and interpretation.
  • Human evaluation is limited (N=60, 2 raters) with near-zero inter-rater agreement on holistic GA (κ=0.006).
  • The 30-task sample limits statistical power for cross-domain rank comparisons.
  • The weight experiment covers only 3 Chinese-language models.
  • The study is correlational/black-box; mechanistic analysis of why specific dimensions are more public or private would strengthen causal claims.
  • The public-private classification threshold (f-ICMw ≥ 0.7) is somewhat arbitrary, though sensitivity analysis at 0.6 and 0.8 partially addresses this.

Relevance

该论文提出的维度级意图保真度评估方法对LLM评估方法论具有重要参考价值，特别是"结构-保真分裂"现象的发现揭示了当前整体评估指标的盲区。公开-私有分解为提示工程提供了实用指导：Who和How-much等私有维度的显式指定收益最大。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the framework generalize beyond 5W3H to other structured or unstructured prompt formats?
  • Can dimensional intent fidelity be predicted from prompt characteristics alone, enabling proactive prompt optimization?
  • What mechanistic explanations underlie why "Who" is the most private dimension — is this a training data bias or architectural constraint?
  • How does the structural-fidelity split interact with reasoning-heavy tasks (e.g., math, code generation)?
  • Would using an external, non-subject model as the primary judge change the quantitative findings?
  • Can the public-private classification be used dynamically during inference to trigger intent clarification dialogs?
