Enjoy Your Layer Normalization with the Computational Efficiency of RMSNorm

arXiv: 2605.14521

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14521

一句话总结： 本文通过列中心约束（CCC）和基于列的权重中心化（CBWC）将Layer Normalization的中心化操作折叠进上游线性层，定义了"可折叠LN"并开发了基于图的检测算法，在GPT-2、BERT、ViT等主流模型上实现2%–12%的精确等价推理加速，且在训练场景中也能接近LN的性能。
TL;DR: This paper proposes a general framework to exactly replace Layer Normalization (LN) with computationally cheaper RMSNorm in arbitrary DNNs by folding LN's centering operation into upstream linear layers via column-centered constraint (CCC) and column-based weight centering (CBWC). A graph-based detection algorithm identifies "foldable LNs" automatically. Evaluated on GPT-2, BERT, ViT, BLOOM, OPT, and Phi, the method achieves 2%–12% end-to-end inference speedup with mathematically identical outputs, and remains competitive with LN even during training when dropout breaks exact equivalence.

核心贡献

  • 提出了列中心约束（CCC）和基于列的权重中心化（CBWC）两种技术，将LN的中心化操作折叠进上游线性层，使LN可被RMSNorm等价替换
  • 定义了"可折叠LN"（Foldable LN）的形式化概念，并通过"零均值图"（Zero-Mean Graph）从图论角度刻画了LN可折叠的结构条件
  • 开发了基于正向遍历的自动检测算法（Algorithm 1），可在任意DNN中识别所有可折叠LN及其对应的需要施加CBWC的上游线性层
  • 证明GPT-2、BERT、ViT、BLOOM、OPT、Phi等主流模型中的所有LN均为可折叠LN，VMamba中的大部分LN也可折叠
  • 在推理时实现2%–12%的端到端加速且不改变模型输出，训练时即使dropout破坏了精确等价性，CBWC+RMSNorm仍接近LN性能

Key Contributions

  • Introduce column-centered constraint (CCC) — a constraint on linear layer weights ensuring zero-mean output across neurons — and column-based weight centering (CBWC) — a reparameterization V = (I − (1/m)𝟙ₘ𝟙ₘᵀ)W that enforces CCC while preserving model capacity
  • Formally define foldable LN (Def. 4.1): an LN is foldable w.r.t. a parameter subspace Θ* if RMSNorm and LN produce identical outputs for all inputs under that subspace
  • Propose the zero-mean graph (Def. 4.2): a recursive graph construction that backtracks from an LN through the computation graph, characterizing the structural conditions for foldability based on layer types (general linear V_l, scalar V_s, residual V_r, intrinsically zero-mean V_c, and other V_−)
  • Develop an automatic detection algorithm (Algorithm 1) that performs forward traversal tracking tensor "centered" state and corresponding upstream layers, identifying all foldable LNs in O(|V|+|E|) time
  • Show that all LNs in GPT-2, BERT, ViT, BLOOM, OPT, and Phi are foldable; most LNs in VMamba are also foldable; LLaMA and Mamba2 already use RMSNorm by design
  • Demonstrate that auxiliary centering can make all LNs in both pre-norm and post-norm Transformers foldable (Appendix D.3)

Methodology

The paper tackles the computational overhead of Layer Normalization (LN), whose per-sample centering and scaling introduces non-negligible inference cost. RMSNorm removes centering for efficiency but may lose its conditioning benefits (the authors empirically show centering stabilizes activation norms across layers, especially with residual connections).

Core insight: If the input to an LN already has zero mean, centering is redundant and LN ≡ RMSNorm. The key is enforcing zero-mean output from upstream linear layers.

  1. CCC (Def. 3.1): A linear layer W satisfies CCC if each column sums to zero (wᵢᵀ𝟙ₘ = 0 for all i). Under CCC, the output h = Wx has zero mean for any input x (Prop. 3.1).
  1. CBWC (Def. 3.2): A reparameterization that enforces CCC during training. The effective weight V = φ(W) = (I − (1/m)𝟙ₘ𝟙ₘᵀ)W. Gradients flow through φ, so the optimizer, LR scheduler, weight decay, and gradient clipping are all preserved.
  1. Equivalence (Prop. 3.2): CBWC + RMSNorm yields identical outputs and gradients to the original linear + LN model, ensuring mathematical equivalence in both inference and training.
  1. Extension to arbitrary DNNs: The zero-mean graph backtracks from an LN through the computation graph. Leaf nodes that are general linear layers (V_l) or intrinsically zero-mean layers (V_c) enable folding; leaf nodes that are nonlinear operations (V_−) prevent it. Scalar operations (V_s) and residual additions (V_r) are transparent to the analysis.
  1. Algorithm 1: A forward-pass algorithm tracking whether each tensor is "centered" and which upstream layers are responsible, identifying all foldable LNs and their CBWC targets in a single pass.
  1. Extensions: The framework applies to Group Normalization (grouped-CCC/CBWC in Appendix C.3), convolution layers, and recurrent layers by unifying them as general linear transformations.

Results & Highlights

Inference acceleration (exact equivalence):

  • End-to-end speedup of 2%–12% on GPT-2, BERT, BLOOM, OPT, and Phi at batch size 2, sequence length 1024 on A100 GPU
  • Layer-level latency reduction of 50%–80% for the normalization step itself
  • Consistent 2.5%–5.9% latency reduction + 1,119–3,385 tokens/s throughput gain across V100 and A100 GPUs
  • Acceleration increases with longer sequence lengths

Training performance (with dropout breaking exact equivalence):

  • Multi30K translation (Transformer): CBWC+RMSNorm achieves BLEU scores between LN and vanilla RMSNorm, consistently outperforming RMSNorm after epoch 100
  • AG News text classification (Transformer): CBWC+RMSNorm matches LN's convergence and test accuracy while achieving training/inference speed close to vanilla RMSNorm (5 random seeds)
  • ImageNet-100 (Swin-Tiny): CBWC+RMSNorm achieves 57.77% test accuracy vs. 57.27% (LN) and 57.08% (RMSNorm) — the best among all three variants, with lower test loss (3.232 vs. 3.352 and 3.350)

Fine-tuning (GPT-3 Medium 350M):

  • Pre-trained on WikiText-103 with LN, then fine-tuned on Alpaca
  • CBWC+RMSNorm achieves the best test loss (0.2430) and perplexity (1.28) among all variants (LN: 0.2553/1.29, RMSNorm: 0.2526/1.29)
  • Validates that converting a pre-trained LN model to CBWC+RMSNorm before fine-tuning is a viable strategy

Centering benefits (empirical):

  • MLP ablations on CIFAR-10/MNIST show LN keeps the final layer's input norm in a narrower range throughout training, while RMSNorm exhibits larger variations
  • Effect is amplified in deeper networks and with residual connections

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The generalization from the pre-norm Transformer-specific analysis of Jiang et al. (2023) to arbitrary DNNs via graph theory is a genuine contribution. The formal definitions (foldable LN, zero-mean graph) and the automatic detection algorithm are novel.
  • Rigor: Very strong. Formal definitions, propositions with proofs (in appendices), clear separation of exact equivalence vs. practical approximation cases, and honest discussion of when equivalence breaks (dropout). The claim of "exact equivalence" is precisely scoped.
  • Reproducibility: High. Code is available. Experiments use standard benchmarks (CIFAR-10, MNIST, Multi30K, AG News, ImageNet-100, WikiText-103, Alpaca) and standard architectures. Multiple random seeds are used.
  • Significance: Practically significant for deployment. The ability to convert any LN-based model (GPT-2, BERT, ViT, etc.) to RMSNorm with exact output equivalence is valuable for production inference. The 2%–12% speedup is modest per-request but meaningful at scale. The training-time benefits are more nuanced.
  • Red flags: (1) The speedup lower bound of 2% is borderline negligible — the paper acknowledges this but argues absolute savings matter at scale. (2) Dropout in Transformers breaks exact equivalence during training, so the "exact" claim only fully applies to inference. (3) The VMamba result is notably weaker ("most" not "all" LNs foldable), suggesting the method may struggle with certain architectures. (4) No experiments on very large-scale LLMs (only up to 350M parameters); the scaling behavior is unclear.

Strengths

  • Mathematically rigorous framework with formal definitions, propositions, and proofs
  • Generalizes beyond specific architectures to arbitrary DNNs via graph-based analysis
  • Exact output equivalence at inference time — no accuracy regression whatsoever
  • Comprehensive experiments spanning NLP (translation, classification, fine-tuning) and vision (ImageNet-100)
  • Publicly available code
  • Honest discussion of limitations (dropout, practical trade-offs between fidelity and efficiency)
  • Even outperforms LN in some settings (Swin-Tiny on ImageNet-100, GPT-3 fine-tuning)

Limitations

  • The 2% lower bound on inference speedup is modest; benefits are architecture-dependent
  • Dropout layers break exact equivalence during training — the paper's strongest guarantee only holds at inference or when dropout is disabled
  • No experiments on billion-scale LLMs; scalability to models like GPT-4-class architectures is untested
  • Some architectures (e.g., VMamba) have non-foldable LNs due to non-linear operations between linear layers and normalization
  • Auxiliary centering (to fold more LNs) introduces additional computation that partially offsets the savings
  • The paper does not evaluate interaction with quantization (FP8, INT8) or other inference optimizations (FlashAttention, speculative decoding)

Relevance

本文对Normalization层的理论分析和推理优化框架与模型效率和深度学习系统优化直接相关。其基于图的LN折叠检测方法可应用于各种Transformer架构（包括LLM和VLM）的部署优化，在实际大规模推理场景中具有直接的工程价值。
