Mitigating Mask Prior Drift and Positional Attention Collapse in Large Diffusion Vision-Language Models

arXiv: 2605.14530

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14530

一句话总结： 本文揭示了大型扩散视觉语言模型（LDVLM）在长文本生成中存在掩码先验漂移和位置注意力坍塌两个根本性问题，并提出两种无需训练的推理时方法（Mask Prior Suppression 和 Monotonic RoPE Scaling）来有效缓解这些问题，显著提升了视觉定位和长文本生成质量。
TL;DR: This paper identifies two failure modes in large diffusion vision-language models (LDVLMs) — mask prior drift causing repetitive generation and positional attention collapse degrading visual grounding — and proposes two training-free inference-time techniques (Mask Prior Suppression and Monotonic RoPE Scaling) that consistently improve visual grounding and long-form generation across diverse LDVLM architectures.

核心贡献

  • 系统性地揭示了LDVLM在长文本生成中的两大根本性问题：掩码先验漂移（所有生成token从共享掩码token初始化，导致隐藏表示收敛到共同的先验方向，产生重复生成）和位置注意力坍塌（RoPE的局部性偏置与迭代去掩码过程的失配，抑制了对远距离视觉token的注意力）
  • 提出了两种无需训练的推理时方法：Mask Prior Suppression（在最终隐藏状态中抑制掩码先验子空间分量）和 Monotonic RoPE Scaling（单调放大低频RoPE分量以保持对远距离视觉token的注意力）
  • 在LLaDA-V和LaViDa两个主流LDVLM架构上，于9个基准测试中取得一致改进，无需任何额外训练或参数更新
  • 所提出的方法是即插即用的轻量级方案，仅修改约0.07%的隐藏维度，推理开销极小

Key Contributions

  • Identifies mask prior drift as a root cause of repetitive generation in LDVLMs: all generation tokens initialize from the same mask token, and their hidden states progressively converge toward the vocabulary mean embedding direction across transformer layers
  • Identifies positional attention collapse: RoPE's locality bias causes generation tokens to attend disproportionately to nearby semantically empty mask tokens, suppressing attention to distant informative visual tokens
  • Proposes Mask Prior Suppression (MPS): projects final-layer hidden states onto a low-dimensional prior subspace (constructed via PCA on vocabulary-mean representations) and adaptively suppresses aligned components based on cosine similarity
  • Proposes Monotonic RoPE Scaling (MRS): applies a sigmoid-gated, frequency-aware scaling to RoPE rotation angles that progressively amplifies low-frequency components (governing long-range interactions) while preserving high-frequency local information
  • Both techniques are training-free, plug-and-play, and generalize across LLaDA-V and LaViDa architectures with consistent gains on visual grounding and long-form generation benchmarks

Methodology

Problem Analysis

The authors conduct a systematic analysis of LDVLM generation failures:

  1. Mask Prior Drift: Through PCA visualization, they show that the hidden state trajectory of the uncontextualized mask token converges with the vocabulary embedding mean at the final transformer layer (L31). During inference, contextualized mask token embeddings exhibit high cosine similarity to the vocabulary mean, which increases as generation steps decrease. This leads to structured repetition of a small set of function tokens (e.g., \n, the, ,) accounting for >95% of repeated outputs.
  1. Positional Attention Collapse: Attention analysis reveals that generation tokens allocate substantial attention weights to mask tokens with incomplete semantic representations. Frequency decomposition shows that high-frequency RoPE components dominate attention to nearby mask tokens, while low-frequency components supporting long-range attention to visual tokens decay with distance.

Mask Prior Suppression (MPS)

  • Constructs a prior subspace by forwarding the vocabulary mean embedding through all transformer layers, computing the mean representation, and performing PCA to obtain an orthonormal basis U ∈ R^{d×k} (k=3 principal components)
  • For each masked position, computes the deviation from the mean, projects onto the prior subspace, and measures cosine similarity with the prior direction
  • Adaptively suppresses aligned components: z̃ = z - α·⟨z, û⟩·û, where α = λ·max(0, cosine_similarity)
  • Only modifies ~0.07% of dimensions (3 out of 4096)

Monotonic RoPE Scaling (MRS)

  • Defines a sigmoid gate γ_i = σ(η(τ_i - τ_0)) over normalized frequency indices, creating a smooth monotonic scaling schedule
  • Scaling factor: s_i = 1 + β·γ_i, applied to RoPE rotation angles as mθ_i ← s_i·mθ_i
  • High-frequency components (short-range) receive scaling ≈1; low-frequency components (long-range) are progressively amplified up to 1+β
  • Hyperparameters: τ_0=0.6 (frequency boundary), η=8.0 (sharpness), β=0.01 (max scaling)

Results & Highlights

  • Visual grounding: RefCOCOg improved from 64.8→65.0 (LLaDA-V) and 36.9→44.0 (LaViDa); Ferret from 60.4→62.9 (LLaDA-V) and 25.9→35.7 (LaViDa)
  • Long-form generation: LLaVA-Bench from 61.3→64.1 (LLaDA-V) and 39.5→46.5 (LaViDa); DetailCaps from 59.8→63.6 (LLaDA-V) and 8.3→56.1 (LaViDa, dramatic improvement)
  • Hallucination reduction: CHAIR_s reduced from 29.4→27.0, CHAIR_i from 9.5→8.3 on LLaDA-V, with coverage preserved — indicating genuine grounding improvement, not conservative generation
  • Robustness across generation steps: Method maintains higher distinct-n and lower repetition ratio consistently across T ∈ {4, 8, 16, 32} steps
  • Ablation: MPS and MRS play complementary roles — MPS has larger impact on long-form generation, MRS more on visual grounding
  • Superior to existing RoPE scaling: NTK-aware and YaRN scaling often degrade visual grounding, while MRS consistently improves it

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (High): The identification of mask prior drift and positional attention collapse as specific, diagnosable failure modes in LDVLMs is novel and well-motivated. The training-free solutions are elegant and principled. The connection between vocabulary mean embedding and mask token collapse via the Linear Representation Hypothesis is insightful.
  • Rigor (High): The paper provides extensive empirical analysis (PCA trajectories, cosine similarity measurements, frequency decomposition of attention) to support each claim. Ablations are comprehensive across both architectures. The comparison against alternative RoPE scaling methods (NTK, YaRN) strengthens the case for the proposed monotonic approach.
  • Reproducibility (High): All hyperparameters are specified and fixed across experiments. Code is available. The methods are deterministic inference-time modifications.
  • Significance (High): As diffusion-based language models gain traction, understanding and fixing their generation pathologies is critical. The training-free nature makes adoption immediate.
  • Red flags: The absolute improvements on LLaDA-V are sometimes modest (e.g., RefCOCOg 64.8→65.0). The LaViDa baseline shows surprisingly poor performance on DetailCaps (8.3), making the relative improvement (8.3→56.1) impressive but raising questions about the baseline's quality. The method only modifies 3 PCA dimensions — while this suggests efficiency, it also suggests the improvements may be fragile under distribution shift or with different model architectures. The analysis is primarily empirical; a stronger theoretical grounding for why low-frequency RoPE amplification specifically helps would strengthen the work.

Strengths

  • Identifies and rigorously analyzes two previously unrecognized failure modes in an emerging model family
  • Training-free approach with negligible inference overhead
  • Consistent improvements across two distinct LDVLM architectures and nine benchmarks
  • Elegant decomposition of the problem into representation-level (MPS) and positional-level (MRS) interventions
  • Strong ablation study showing complementary roles of the two components
  • Reduces hallucination as measured by CHAIR and AMBER-G, confirming improved visual grounding

Limitations

  • Absolute gains on some benchmarks are modest (e.g., +0.2 on RefCOCOg for LLaDA-V)
  • The method's effectiveness depends on the quality of underlying pretrained representations
  • Only validated on the standard full-suffix iterative unmasking setup; compatibility with semi-autoregressive or block-wise diffusion variants is untested
  • The vocabulary mean as a proxy for the mask prior direction is motivated empirically rather than theoretically
  • LaViDa baseline performance is notably weak, which inflates relative improvements on some metrics
  • No analysis of scaling behavior — unclear how methods perform with larger models or different training data scales

Relevance

本文提出的掩码先验漂移分析和位置注意力修复方法对理解扩散语言模型的生成机制具有重要参考价值，特别是RoPE频率分解视角和无需训练的推理优化策略，与扩散模型推理加速和视觉语言模型研究高度相关。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How do MPS and MRS interact with different unmasking schedules (e.g., confidence-based, block-wise) beyond the standard iterative setup?
  • Can the mask prior drift analysis be extended to discrete diffusion models in other modalities (e.g., image generation with masked tokens)?
  • What is the theoretical justification for why amplifying low-frequency RoPE components specifically alleviates attention collapse, beyond the empirical frequency decomposition?
  • How do the methods scale with model size (e.g., 13B, 70B parameters)?
  • Could the prior subspace direction be learned or adapted per-task rather than fixed as the vocabulary mean?
  • Do the identified failure modes persist in models trained with different masking strategies (e.g., complementary masking in LaViDa)?
