Minimal-Intervention KV Retention: A Design-Space Study and a Diversity-Penalty Survivor

arXiv: 2605.14292

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14292

一句话总结： 本文以"匹配均值缓存 + sympy 评分 + n≥200 + Qwen+Llama 双模型 + 预注册 dev/confirm 划分 + Bonferroni 联合检验"为证据标准，对 KV-cache 压缩的五家族七机制做设计空间研究并全部拒绝；唯一的存活者 α 是对 TriAttention 1d 评分器的一处单函数修改——把 argmax-top-k 换成带 V 空间冗余惩罚的贪心设施选址选择器，在 λ=0.5 时四单元格中两格通过 Bonferroni。
TL;DR: Under a pre-registered matched-memory protocol on MATH-500 (sympy-graded, n≥200) with DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B and DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B at budgets b ∈ {64,128}, the authors test seven KV-cache compression mechanisms across five families (state, routing, cadence, decoding, scoring) and reject all seven. The only survivor is α, a one-function modification to the TriAttention 1d scorer that replaces top-k argmax with a greedy facility-location selector penalising V-space redundancy (controlled by a single λ). At λ=0.5, α clears Bonferroni on Qwen b=128 (+4.79 pp, p=0.0022) and Llama b=64 (+2.23 pp, p=0.0054); no cell is significantly negative.

核心贡献

  • 设计空间分类：把 KV-cache 压缩的设计空间分解为五个干预面——State（缓存里存什么）、Routing（哪些 head/layer 看哪些子集）、Cadence（何时压缩、用什么预算）、Decoding（压缩时模型生成什么）、Scoring（在固定预算下保留哪些 token），并据此给出七机制的负面裁决表。
  • 六类共享失败模式：iso-cache underperformance、n=50→200 magnitude evaporation、b=64 extraction collapse、proxy-runtime drift、cross-model transfer failure、family-level bundling——为严格的 KV-cache 压缩评估协议提供可复用的方法论骨架。
  • α 评分器：对 TriAttention 1d（"1ddiv"）的单函数修改——把 argmax-top-k 替换为带 V 空间冗余惩罚的贪心 facility-location 选择器（边际增益 Δ(i|S_t)=s_i − λ·max(0, max_{j∈S_t} cos(v_i, v_j))）；λ=0 时退化为 bitwise 等价的 top-k（显式快速路径）。
  • 预注册证据协议：MD5 桶化的 201/299 dev/confirm 划分、λ-probe、3 seed × 4 cell × n≥200、cluster bootstrap (n_boot=10,000)、Bonferroni 联合检验（α=0.0125）、预先提交的 Branch A/B/C 决策表——这是使"最小干预胜出重度结构重设计"这一非对称结论可见的证据标准。

Key Contributions

  • A five-axis taxonomy of the KV-retention design space (state, routing, cadence, decoding, scoring) and a retrospective seven-gate verdict table in which every structural mechanism (per-head PCA V basis, span-mean synthetic memory, static per-head routing, round-indexed budget schedule, compact "State:" anchor prompting, V-direction prior, reasoning-statistics recalibration) is killed under matched-memory evaluation.
  • A six-mode shared-failure catalogue (iso-cache underperformance, n=50→200 magnitude evaporation, b=64 extraction collapse, proxy-runtime drift, cross-model transfer failure, family-level bundling) that operationalises what an honest KV-compression evaluation must defend against.
  • α, a one-function modification to the TriAttention 1d scorer: replace top-k argmax with a greedy facility-location selector Δ(i|S_t) = s_i − λ·max(0, max_{j∈S_t} cos(v_i, v_j)), with V-signatures computed by mean-pooling V across layers and heads then unit-normalising. At λ=0 the selector is bitwise top-k via an explicit fast path.
  • A pre-registered evaluation protocol (frozen MD5-bucketed dev/confirm split, three seeds per cell, cluster bootstrap n_boot=10,000, Bonferroni-corrected joint test α=0.0125 over four (model, budget) cells, pre-committed Branch A/B/C decision table) that is the evidence standard under which the asymmetry between minimal-scoring and structural redesign became visible.

Methodology

Matched-memory instrumentation. Mean and peak per-problem cache length are instrumented across the full decode horizon; methods that do not recompress during decode can silently use ~5× their reported end-of-prefill budget, so end-of-prefill matching is rejected as a gate-passing condition.

Workload & grading. MATH-500 (Hendrycks et al. 2021), long-form chain-of-thought generation, sympy-based grading that normalises \frac vs \dfrac, simplification, and sign convention — removing the 5–10 pp formatting-noise swings of exact-string graders. At b=64, 44–62 % of incorrect runs produce no parseable \boxed{} answer (extraction collapse), so an exact-string grader would conflate extraction failure with reasoning failure.

Models & budgets. DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B and DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B at b ∈ {64, 128}; n ≥ 200 per cell.

α selector (Algorithm 1). Compute V-signatures v_i = u_i/(‖u_i‖₂+ε), u_i = (1/LH)Σ_{ℓ,h} V_i^{(ℓ,h)}. Build n×n cosine matrix Sims = VV⊤ once per compression event. First pick i_1 = argmax_i s_i (matches baseline). For t=1,…,k−1: a = s − λ·m with m the per-candidate max cosine to the selected set (floored at 0), a[S]=−∞, pick argmax a, update m ← max(m, Sims[i,:]). Complexity O(n²d + nk); at b ∈ {64,128} overhead is negligible versus decode.

Dev/confirm split. MATH-500 problem ids hashed with MD5, bucketed by (md5 mod 5): buckets {0,1} → 201-problem dev split (Phase 1 λ-probe); buckets {2,3,4} → 299-problem held-out confirm split (Phase 2). Split committed in code before Phase 1; not recomputed.

Phase 1. λ ∈ {0.5, 1.0, 1.5}, single seed, dev split only. Decision rule: pick λ maximising mean Δ(1ddiv − 1d) across four cells; ties within ±0.5 pp broken toward smaller λ (Occam).

Phase 2. λ=0.5 evaluated on confirm split, 3 seeds per cell, 4 cells = 12 cells. Per-cell paired deltas with cluster bootstrap (n_boot=10,000) on per-problem accuracy differences. Bonferroni family α = 0.05/4 = 0.0125.

Pre-committed branches. Branch A (full design-lesson paper): ≥1 Qwen cell with Δ>0 at per-cell p<0.05, positive Llama mean Δ across cells, no significantly negative Llama cell. Branch B: Qwen condition satisfied but one Llama condition fails. Branch C: no qualifying Qwen cell.

Head-to-head (post-confirmation, outside branch rule). α (λ=0.5) vs SnapKV-style windowed-attention baseline, same confirm split, 3 seeds, 4 cells.

Two of four cells clear Bonferroni; no cell significantly negative; Branch A triggers.
  • n=50 → n=200 evaporation. The α direction showed +5.6 pp lift at n=50 on Qwen-7B but shrank to +0.2–+0.5 pp at n=200 on two models — direct empirical evidence for the n-evaporation failure mode.
  • Head-to-head vs SnapKV-style (Fig. 2b, same confirm split). Qwen b=64: +4.46 pp (p=0.0034, PASS Bonferroni); Qwen b=128: +4.79 pp (p=0.0024, PASS); Llama b=64: +8.25 pp (p<10⁻⁴, PASS); Llama b=128: +2.45 pp (p=0.0934, not significant). Mean Δ across cells: +3.93 pp; 3 of 4 cells clear Bonferroni against the SnapKV-style baseline.
  • Auxiliary contrast (1d vs SnapKV-style, uncorrected). At Qwen b=128 the internal 1d baseline is +3.46 pp above SnapKV-style (uncorrected p=0.048); at Qwen b=64 +2.90 pp (p=0.058); on Llama the two methods are within ±0.78 pp (p>0.6) — i.e., α's lift does not sit on top of an artificially weak floor.
  • Seven negative gates (Table 1). subspace (per-head V PCA, killed by proxy-runtime drift), syn mem (span-mean synthetic states, 4 sub-attempts all failed iso-cache), static route (calibrated per-head policy, proxy-runtime drift), schedule (round-indexed budget, phase-independent kill), state emit (compact "State:" anchor prompting, anchors crowd out reasoning), variant b (V-direction prior, proxy-runtime drift), Q stats (reasoning-stat recalibration, sign-inconsistent n-evaporation).
  • b=64 extraction collapse. At b=64, 44–62 % of incorrect runs produce no parseable \boxed{} answer, isolating structured-output fluency as a separate failure mode from retention quality.

Critical Assessment

  • The headline result is two of four cells. Only Qwen b=128 (+4.79 pp) and Llama b=64 (+2.23 pp) clear the Bonferroni threshold; Qwen b=64 has a CI containing zero (p=0.20) and Llama b=128 is a robust null (p=0.86). The authors are unusually honest about this ("positive point estimates at three of four cells, of which two clear the joint Bonferroni threshold"), but the design-lesson claim that "minimal scoring beat heavier structural redesigns" rests on a partial confirmation, not a clean sweep.
  • The seven negative gates are retrospective, not pre-registered. Section 3 is explicitly a retrospective design-space study; only the α evaluation in Sections 4–5 is pre-registered. The strength of the "structural mechanisms fail" claim therefore rests on chronology and family-level bundling rather than on a pre-committed factorial sweep, which the authors flag ("Sequential, not factorial") but readers may still overweight.
  • Single workload, two models, one parameter grid. Evaluation is MATH-500 only with two 7–8B distilled-reasoning models; λ grid is {0.5, 1.0, 1.5} with three λ=1.5 cells incomplete. The authors explicitly disclaim transfer to LongBench, code generation, larger models, or non-reasoning models without re-evaluation, so the design lesson is narrow in workload even if broad in mechanism class.
  • The SnapKV comparator is not the official implementation. The head-to-head uses a "SnapKV-style" baseline implemented inside the authors' matched-memory harness under tag snapkv with window; they explicitly note "we do not claim exact parity with the official SnapKV implementation," so the +3.93 pp mean Δ vs SnapKV-style is suggestive, not definitive.
  • Branch A was designed to be triggerable. The pre-committed Branch A requires only one Qwen cell to pass and the Llama mean to be positive — a low bar relative to "all four cells pass." The authors disclose this but it means Branch A triggering is a weaker statement than the headline suggests.

Strengths

  • Exceptional methodological rigour: matched mean-cache instrumentation over the full decode horizon, sympy grading, n≥200, two-model requirement, frozen MD5 dev/confirm split, pre-committed branch table, Bonferroni correction — a genuine raise of the evidence bar for KV-compression claims.
  • Honest reporting of negatives: the seven-gate verdict table, the six failure modes, the n=50→200 evaporation of α's own headline, and the two non-significant cells are all disclosed rather than buried.
  • The α modification itself is genuinely minimal: one function, one hyperparameter, bitwise top-k at λ=0, sub-second overhead at b ∈ {64,128}. The "smallest intervention surface" framing is well-earned.
  • The six-mode failure taxonomy is reusable beyond α — it is a checklist any future KV-compression paper can be evaluated against.

Limitations

  • Two of four cells pass Bonferroni; one cell is a robust null.
  • Negative gates are retrospective; the structural-vs-scoring asymmetry rests on chronology, not pre-registration.
  • Single workload (MATH-500), two models, incomplete λ=1.5 arm, no transfer experiments.
  • SnapKV comparator is an in-house re-implementation, not the official code.
  • The matched-memory runtime harness and TriAttention integration are not yet public, so the central evaluation infrastructure is not independently reproducible at submission time.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does α compose with PyramidKV (per-layer budget) or Ada-KV (per-head budget) — does the within-budget scorer's gain stack on top of an allocation lever?
  2. Does the n=50 → n=200 evaporation pattern generalise across other KV-compression papers — i.e., how many published small-n headlines would survive this protocol?
  3. Can the V-signature aggregation be improved (per-head, per-layer, or learned projection) without breaking the minimal-intervention property that made α win?
  4. Does the Qwen-vs-Llama cell heterogeneity (Qwen wins at b=128, Llama wins at b=64) reflect a model-specific saturation property that predicts which (model, budget) cell α will help in?
  5. Could a λ schedule (heavier early decode, lighter late) recover additional lift beyond constant λ=0.5?
