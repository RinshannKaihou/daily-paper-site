Minimal-Intervention KV Retention: A Design-Space Study and a Diversity-Penalty Survivor

arXiv: 2605.14292

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14292

⚠️ Abstract-only summary

一句话总结： 本文系统研究了KV-cache压缩的七种机制在低预算下均失败后，提出了一种基于多样性惩罚的设施选址式保留评分器α，在部分设置下通过了严格的预注册Bonferroni检验，证明了最小干预的设计可以胜过重度结构重设计。
TL;DR: A rigorous design-space study of seven KV-cache compression mechanisms for reasoning models finds all rejected at small budgets; a simple diversity-penalized retention scorer (α) based on facility-location selection clears Bonferroni-corrected tests on two of four cells, demonstrating that minimal scoring modifications can outperform heavier structural redesigns.

核心贡献

  • 系统化的KV-cache压缩设计空间研究：在缓存表示、注意力头路由、压缩节奏、解码行为和预算内评分五个维度上研究了七种机制，在匹配平均缓存大小的条件下进行公平比较。
  • 提出α评分器：对TriAttention保留评分器进行单函数修改，用基于设施选址（facility-location）的贪心选择替换argmax-top-k选择，并引入V空间冗余惩罚项。
  • 严格的预注册实验协议：在冻结的开发集上调参，在不相交的保留集上确认结果，使用Bonferroni校正进行多重比较检验，建立了高标准证据规范。
  • 发现不对称性：在低预算（b=64, 128）的长数学推理任务中，最小的评分修改比重度结构重设计更有效，这一发现只有在严格协议下才能显现。

Key Contributions

  • Conducted a systematic design-space exploration across five families of KV-cache compression mechanisms (cache representation, head-wise routing, compression cadence, decoding behavior, within-budget scoring) under matched mean cache conditions.
  • Proposed α, a single-function modification to TriAttention's retention scorer that replaces argmax-top-k with greedy facility-location-inspired selection penalized by V-space redundancy (controlled by a single hyperparameter λ).
  • Established a rigorous pre-registered evaluation protocol: tune on a frozen dev split, confirm on a disjoint held-out split, with Bonferroni correction across four (model, budget) cells.
  • Demonstrated that all seven existing mechanisms were rejected at small budgets (b ∈ {64, 128}) on MATH-500 with distilled reasoning models, while α cleared significance on two cells with no negative results anywhere.

Methodology

The study evaluates KV-cache compression for long-form mathematical reasoning on MATH-500 using two distilled reasoning models: Qwen-7B and Llama-8B variants of DeepSeek-R1-Distill. Seven mechanisms spanning cache representation, head-wise routing, compression cadence, decoding behavior, and within-budget scoring are tested under matched mean cache budgets of b=64 and b=128.

All seven mechanisms are rejected under this regime. The authors then propose α, which modifies TriAttention's retention scorer by replacing argmax-top-k selection with a greedy facility-location-inspired algorithm that incorporates a V-space redundancy penalty controlled by a single weight λ. The key insight is that diversity in retained key-value pairs matters more than simply keeping the highest-scoring ones.

A pre-registered protocol is used: λ is tuned on a frozen development split, then confirmed on a disjoint held-out split. With λ=0.5, α clears Bonferroni correction on two of four (model, budget) cells (Qwen b=128 and Llama b=64), with no cell showing significantly negative results. The pre-registered Branch A is triggered.

Results & Highlights

  • Seven mechanisms rejected: All tested KV-cache compression approaches failed at small budgets under the matched-cache protocol.
  • α passes on 2/4 cells: With λ=0.5, α clears Bonferroni-corrected significance tests on Qwen b=128 and Llama b=64, demonstrating genuine improvement.
  • No negative cells: Crucially, α never significantly hurts performance in any cell, suggesting it is a safe modification.
  • Asymmetry finding: A minimal single-function modification outperforms heavier structural redesigns — an important negative result for the field.
  • Rigorous evidence standard: The matched-memory, sympy-graded, held-out confirmation protocol is itself a contribution that makes the asymmetry visible.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. The α scorer itself is a simple modification (one function change with one hyperparameter), but the systematic negative result for seven existing mechanisms and the discovery of the asymmetry are novel and valuable.
  • Rigor: High. Pre-registered protocol, Bonferroni correction, matched-cache conditions, frozen dev/held-out splits — this is unusually rigorous for an ML systems paper.
  • Reproducibility: Likely high given the single hyperparameter λ and use of standard models (DeepSeek-R1-Distill variants on MATH-500), though full code release is not confirmed from the abstract.
  • Significance: The negative results for seven mechanisms are arguably more valuable than the positive result for α, as they caution against heavy structural redesigns at small cache budgets.
  • Red flags: The positive result only holds for 2 of 4 cells — the improvement is not universal across all settings. The study is limited to mathematical reasoning and small budgets; generalization to other tasks and larger budgets is unknown.

Strengths

  • Exceptionally rigorous evaluation methodology with pre-registration and proper statistical correction.
  • Honest reporting of negative results for seven mechanisms, which is rare and valuable for the community.
  • Minimal-intervention design philosophy (simple modification > heavy redesign) is well-motivated.
  • Single hyperparameter λ makes the approach practical and easy to adopt.
  • Cross-architecture testing on both Qwen and Llama.

Limitations

  • Only tested on mathematical reasoning (MATH-500); generalization to other domains (code, dialogue, long-context QA) is unproven.
  • Only tested at very small budgets (b=64, 128); behavior at moderate or large budgets is unknown.
  • Positive result only on 2/4 cells — the method does not universally improve over baselines.
  • Abstract-only analysis limits assessment of implementation details, compute costs, and latency impact.
  • Facility-location selection adds computational overhead compared to simple argmax-top-k.
