> **一句话总结：** 提出DDC框架，通过置信度加权贝叶斯终止协议和趋势感知分层剪枝策略联合优化采样宽度与推理路径深度，在5个推理基准上以超过10倍的token效率提升达到或超越强基线准确率。

> **TL;DR:** DDC unifies inter-path early stopping (Confidence-Weighted Bayesian Termination) with intra-path hallucination filtering (Trend-Aware Stratified Pruning) into a single adaptive inference-time scaling framework, achieving >10x token reduction while matching or exceeding strong baselines across 5 reasoning benchmarks on models from 1.7B to 32B.

## 核心贡献

- **统一优化框架：** 首次将推理时扩展中的采样宽度（inter-path）和路径深度（intra-path）作为联合优化目标而非正交目标，通过共享的质量信号同时校准终止和剪枝决策。
- **置信度加权贝叶斯终止：** 将早停建模为贝叶斯序列决策问题，用路径级置信度作为权重更新Beta后验分布，仅在统计证据充分且高质量时才终止采样，防止在虚假共识上不安全退出。
- **趋势感知分层剪枝：** 将推理过程映射到动态相空间，通过特征分解区分推理趋势与随机噪声，使用Tukey Fences自适应阈值识别并剪除幻觉路径，同时保留有临时置信度下降但最终正确的复杂推理链。
- **显著的效率-准确率权衡：** 在AIME25上使用Qwen3-4B实现比Self-Consistency高15.6%的准确率，同时减少约27倍token消耗；平均token效率提升超过10倍。
- **理论上限分析：** 提供了置信度加权终止比频率终止收敛更快的充分条件证明，并形式化分析了剪枝策略对三类幻觉（随机型、衰减型、自我强化型）的处理能力。

## Key Contributions

- **Unified dual-dimensional framework** that jointly optimizes sampling width and path depth for adaptive inference-time scaling, departing from prior work that treats them as orthogonal objectives.
- **Confidence-Weighted Bayesian Termination protocol** that models early stopping as a Bayesian sequential decision problem with path-quality-weighted Beta posterior updates, guaranteeing statistical significance before termination.
- **Trend-Aware Stratified Pruning** that decomposes reasoning trajectories into position-velocity state vectors, uses eigendecomposition to distinguish genuine reasoning trends from stochastic noise, and applies query-adaptive Tukey Fences thresholds.
- **Multi-granular confidence metrics** spanning token-level (local/global), group-level (sliding window), and path-level (minimum group confidence) to quantify reasoning quality at every granularity.
- **>10x average token reduction** across five benchmarks on models ranging from Qwen3-1.7B to Qwen3-32B and DeepSeek-R1-0528-Qwen3-8B, while maintaining or exceeding baseline accuracy.

## Methodology

DDC operates in four phases:

**1. Init Phase Confidence Calculation.** Generate $B_{init}=16$ initial paths. For each path, compute multi-granular confidence metrics: local confidence (max token probability), global confidence (negative average log-prob of top-k tokens), group confidence (mean token confidence over sliding windows of 2048 tokens), and path-level confidence (minimum group confidence across all windows). These establish query-specific quality baselines.

**2. Inter-Path: Confidence-Weighted Bayesian Termination.** Frame the decision to stop sampling as a Beta-Bernoulli model. The leading answer $u^*$ competes against aggregated alternatives. Parameters $\alpha_u$ and $\beta_u$ accumulate confidence-weighted evidence using a Power Prior framework. Each path contributes $w_i = C_{path}(y^{(i)})$ to the side matching its answer. Generation terminates when $P(p_{u^*} > 0.5 | D) > 0.95$, computed via the regularized incomplete beta function. The Laplace prior $\text{Beta}(1,1)$ prevents overconfidence in early stages.

**3. Intra-Path: Trend-Aware Stratified Pruning.** Paths are classified into three tiers. Top-10% by local confidence bypass analysis. Bottom-20% by global confidence are pruned immediately. The middle tier undergoes dynamical analysis: each step's normalized position and velocity form a state vector $z_t \in \mathbb{R}^2$; eigendecomposition of the lag-covariance matrix yields a Structural Instability Score $R$ that penalizes both isotropic noise ($\lambda_1 \approx \lambda_2$) and negative momentum. Paths with $R > \tau_{risk}$ (Tukey Fences: $Q_3 + 1.5 \cdot \text{IQR}$) are pruned.

**4. Final Consensus.** Confidence-weighted majority voting over all verified paths: $\hat{y} = \arg\max_u \sum_{y^{(i)} \in S} \mathbb{1}\{ans_i = u\} \cdot C_{path}(y^{(i)})$.

The theoretical analysis proves that confidence-weighted termination converges strictly faster than frequency-based termination when correct paths receive higher average confidence weights (Assumption: $\mu_c > \mu_i$), and provides a safety brake mechanism when weights are uniformly low.

## Results & Highlights

- **Token efficiency:** Average >10x token reduction vs. Self-Consistency; up to 27x on AIME25 with Qwen3-4B.
- **Accuracy gains:** +8.6% average accuracy over SC on Qwen3-4B; +15.6% on AIME25 specifically.
- **Beats Adaptive-Consistency by 3x** on token consumption while achieving higher accuracy.
- **Outperforms compute-intensive methods:** Exceeds $\phi$-Decoding by 15.0% on AIME25 (Qwen3-4B) with 29x fewer tokens; surpasses Predictive Decoding across most settings.
- **Latency reduction:** Up to 12.4x total generation time reduction on Qwen3-32B AIME25 compared to DeepConf-Low.
- **Ablation confirms synergy:** Removing weighted Bayesian increases tokens ~2.5x; removing trend-aware analysis drops accuracy 6.8% on AIME25; removing intra-path pruning causes the largest degradation (92.4% → 85.5% on MATH500).
- **Unified hyperparameters** across all model sizes (1.7B–32B) demonstrate strong generalizability.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The key insight of jointly optimizing width and depth through shared quality signals is genuinely novel. The trend-aware pruning using phase-space eigendecomposition is a creative application of signal processing to LLM reasoning. The Power Prior formulation for confidence-weighted Bayesian updates is principled.

**Rigor (High):** Theoretical proofs for convergence acceleration (Theorem B.1) and pruning efficacy (Proposition B.2) are provided. Monte Carlo simulations validate theoretical predictions. Ablation studies are comprehensive. The paper honestly discusses the systematic miscalibration limitation.

**Reproducibility (Moderate-High):** Hyperparameters are clearly specified ($B_{init}=16$, $L=2048$, $\gamma=0.5$, $\tau_{stop}=0.95$, $\eta=0.5$). Algorithms 1–4 provide complete pseudocode. However, some implementation details (e.g., exact top-k for global confidence) are not fully specified in the main text.

**Significance (High):** The >10x efficiency gain with accuracy improvements addresses a critical practical bottleneck for deploying LLM reasoning. The framework is model-agnostic and requires no training.

**Red flags:**
- The method inherently cannot handle systematic miscalibration where the model is confidently wrong across all paths — the authors acknowledge this but it remains a fundamental limitation of all consensus-based approaches.
- On some benchmarks (e.g., Qwen3-8B AIME24: 80.0% vs D-L 80.4%), DDC slightly underperforms in accuracy, suggesting the aggressive pruning may occasionally discard valid paths even for middle-tier cases.
- The init phase adds fixed overhead that could negate benefits on very simple queries where Self-Consistency with small budgets would suffice.

## Strengths

- Principled Bayesian formulation with theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Elegant unification of two previously orthogonal optimization dimensions.
- Strong empirical results across diverse model scales and benchmarks.
- Negligible computational overhead for analytical components (~7 orders of magnitude smaller than LLM inference).
- Honest discussion of failure modes and theoretical assumptions.
- Query-adaptive thresholds via Tukey Fences eliminate per-dataset hyperparameter tuning.

## Limitations

- **Systematic miscalibration:** Cannot recover when the model is confidently wrong across all sampled paths; the Bayesian termination will eagerly stop on incorrect consensus.
- **Init phase overhead:** Fixed cost of 16 initial samples may be wasteful for easy queries; the authors show $B_{init}=8$ can be better for simple tasks.
- **Assumption dependency:** Theoretical acceleration guarantee requires correct paths to have higher average confidence ($\mu_c > \mu_i$), which may not hold for all model/dataset combinations.
- **Token-level confidence noise:** Raw token probabilities can be noisy; while the sliding window and trend analysis mitigate this, they introduce lag in detecting hallucinations.
- **Limited to sampling-based methods:** Does not address training-based inference-time scaling (e.g., RLVR) or structured search methods.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does DDC interact with process-level reward models (PRMs) — could external verification signals replace or complement the internal confidence metrics?
2. Can the trend-aware pruning framework be extended to multi-turn reasoning or agentic workflows where reasoning chains span multiple model calls?
3. What is the interaction between DDC and temperature scaling — does the optimal temperature change when using confidence-weighted termination?
4. How would DDC perform on non-mathematical reasoning tasks (e.g., code generation, scientific QA) where answer correctness is less discrete?
5. Could post-hoc confidence calibration (e.g., temperature scaling, Platt scaling) be integrated to mitigate the systematic miscalibration limitation the authors identify?
