SliceGraph: Mapping Process Isomers in Multi-Run Chain-of-Thought Reasoning

arXiv: 2605.14619

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14619

一句话总结： 本文提出 SliceGraph，通过构建基于稀疏激活键 Jaccard 相似度的 mutual-kNN 图来分析多次 CoT 采样的推理过程结构，发现 85.5% 的问题中正确推理路径会分裂为多个不同的"过程同分异构体"（process isomers），即相同答案但走不同推理路线的轨迹族，揭示了仅聚合最终答案会忽略的丰富多路径过程几何。
TL;DR: SliceGraph builds post-hoc graphs from sparse activation-key overlap across multi-run CoT trajectories, revealing that 85.5% of correctly-solved problems contain multiple "process isomers"—distinct route families reaching the same answer through different intermediate computation, a structured multi-route geometry lost by final-answer aggregation.

核心贡献

  • 提出 SliceGraph，一种基于稀疏 MLP 激活键 mutual-kNN 的后验图结构，用于刻画同一问题多次 CoT 采样中的共享、分叉和汇合的推理过程几何。
  • 发现并定义了"过程同分异构体"（process isomers）：在 85.5% 的问题-模型单元中，达到相同正确答案的 CoT 轨迹分裂为多个不同的正确推理路线族，平均 76.6% 的正确轨迹对属于不同路线族。
  • 通过双盲人工标注验证了图的基本语义单元：双连通分量对应共享推理状态（跨标注者 κ=0.97），过程族对应同策略路线（族内一致率 94.4%）。
  • 引入标签种子奖励场（reward field）发现成功区域常分裂为多个不连通的高价值核心，且不同路线族会在不同核心上特化，而非简单复制。
  • 通过有类型状态转移分析证明不同路线族在同一 atlas 上具有显著不同的转移核（80.9% 的单元超过 null p95），且结果在跨架构（Llama-8B）和跨尺度（32B, 72B）上可复现。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces SliceGraph, a post-hoc per-problem-model-cell graph built from mutual-kNN over sparse activation-key Jaccard similarity between CoT slices, treated as a measurement object for process geometry rather than a decoding program.
  • Discovers and formally defines "process isomers": correct CoT runs reaching the same normalized answer but belonging to different correct-only process families — observed in 85.5% of 954 problem-model cells, with 76.6% of correct run pairs being cross-family on average.
  • Validates graph structural units through two blinded annotation studies: BCCs correspond to shared reasoning states (cross-annotator κ=0.97) and process families to within-family strategy-coherent route units (94.4% decisive agreement).
  • Introduces a label-seeded reward field revealing that success-associated regions often split into disconnected high-value cores, with route families specializing over distinct core footprints (specialization score 0.59).
  • Shows family-specific typed-state transition kernels differ significantly under matched null controls (80.9% of cells exceed family-label shuffle null p95, median z=3.14), confirmed across architecture (Llama-8B) and scale (Qwen3-32B, Q2.5-72B) replications.

Methodology

SliceGraph operates in five layers, the first three building a label-free route scaffold and the last two adding value-landscape and temporal information:

  1. Graph construction (§3.1): For each problem-model cell, N=64 CoT runs are sampled. Sparse activation keys (top-k post-gating MLP intermediates, keyed by layer+unit) are extracted per token, aggregated into 256-token slices as binary key sets. Edges connect slices via mutual-kNN on Jaccard distance (k=6, RBF-weighted), yielding a per-problem graph G.
  1. Biconnected-component decomposition (§3.2): G is decomposed into BCCs. Trivial K₂ bridges are retained as connectors but excluded from semantic analysis. Non-trivial blocks (|b|≥3) are assigned one of five roles: shared_trunk, answer_basin, weak_basin, decision_point, or intermediate, based on run coverage, size, answer purity, and median position.
  1. Process families and isomers (§3.3): Each run is reduced to the set of non-trivial blocks it visits. A weighted Jaccard co-visitation graph on normalized-correct runs (with rare-block upweighting) is partitioned via Louvain community detection into correct-only process families. Two correct runs reaching the same answer in different families are process isomers (Definition 1).
  1. Label-seeded reward field (§3.4): A support-shrunk excess correctness seed per block is diffused over a block adjacency matrix (combining block-cut structure and temporal transitions) via PageRank-style propagation (α=0.65, 24 steps). The high-value core is the top quartile of positive field values; disconnected core components reveal multi-basin solution landscapes.
  1. Typed-state transition dynamics (§3.5): Each run's block trajectory is lifted to a typed-state chain (role × position-bin × core indicator). Per-family Laplace-smoothed transition kernels are estimated, and family-specific total variation (TV) divergence is compared against size-preserving label-shuffle nulls.

The experimental setup uses three primary models (DeepSeek-R1-0528-Qwen3-8B, Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507, Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507) on five benchmarks (AIME24, AIME25, BRUMO25, HMMT25, GPQA Diamond), yielding 60,622 analysed trajectories across 954 problem-model cells. Replication models include Llama-8B (cross-architecture), Qwen3-32B (within-family cross-scale), and Q2.5-72B (cross-generation cross-scale).

Results & Highlights

  • Process isomers are pervasive: 85.5% of cells contain multiple correct-only families; 76.6% of correct run pairs are cross-family (bootstrap 95% CI [75.6, 77.5]%).
  • Multi-core value landscapes: 67.6% of reward-evaluable cells have disconnected high-value cores (mean 2.61 components); 2nd-to-1st core size ratio is 0.54.
  • Family–core non-redundancy: Family and core counts correlate weakly (ρ=0.08), yet families spatially specialize over core footprints (mean specialization=0.59).
  • Coverage criticality: Removing the most coverage-critical family loses >10% of block coverage in 94.5% of multi-family cells.
  • Family-specific dynamics: Family TV exceeds null p95 in 80.9% of cells (median z=3.14); common-support TV preserves the signal at 77.4% above null p95.
  • Representation matters: PCA-50 compresses family counts by ~1/cell; MiniLM nearly collapses the atlas (only 21/954 valid cells). Activation-key Jaccard uniquely preserves multi-route structure.
  • Cross-model robustness: Llama-8B matches all patterns (78.3% multi-family, family-TV z=3.14); Qwen3-32B closely matches or exceeds primary headlines; Q2.5-72B attenuates on saturated math but retains structure on GPQA (86.4% multi-family).
  • Sampling robustness: At m=4 correct runs, 82.1% of cells remain multi-family with 58.2% pairwise isomer rate; split-half stability yields 97.2% both-halves multi-family.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The concept of "process isomers" and the post-hoc graph measurement paradigm are genuinely new. Prior graph-based CoT work (Tree-of-Thoughts, Graph-of-Thoughts) uses graphs as decoding programs; SliceGraph uniquely treats the graph as a measurement/audit tool for process geometry after sampling.
  • Rigor: Very high. The validity ladder (semantic validation → representation preservation → geometry → value landscape → dynamics) is methodical. Each claim is paired with a targeted null control. The five null families address distinct confounds (degree, type, family label, temporal order, correctness label). Split-half and held-out validation go beyond typical ML papers.
  • Reproducibility: Good. All models are open-weight HuggingFace checkpoints; full algorithmic details and hyperparameters are specified; code is released. The pipeline runs on CPU in 2–5s per problem. However, reproducing the full 200 GPU-hour activation cache requires significant compute (8×H20 GPUs).
  • Significance: High for interpretability and test-time compute research. The finding that same-answer correct CoTs are routinely process-isomeric has direct implications for self-consistency, best-of-N selection, and process reward models. If correct routes are genuinely diverse, selecting by answer frequency may discard valid alternative strategies.
  • Red flags / concerns:
  • The across-family human differentiation rate is only 47.6% (decisive) — families capture finer-grained structure than human "strategy" categories, which is acknowledged but somewhat weakens the "different strategy" narrative.
  • The 72B replication shows attenuation (multi-core 42.9%, multi-family 67.3%), suggesting the effect diminishes with stronger/more deterministic models. The authors attribute this to saturation, but the trend raises questions about whether process isomerism persists at frontier model scale.
  • The method requires access to internal activations (sparse MLP cache), limiting applicability to open-weight models with hook access.
  • SliceGraph is purely observational/diagnostic — it does not improve decoding. The gap between "measuring route diversity" and "exploiting it for better inference" remains open.
  • The label-seeded reward field's multi-core counts cannot be one-sided null-tested against label permutation (real multi-core rate 67.6% is below permuted 85.4%), which is honestly disclosed but limits the value-landscape claims.

Strengths

  • Exceptionally thorough experimental design with a clear validity ladder and matched null controls for every claim.
  • The process isomer concept provides a precise, graph-grounded formalization of reasoning diversity that goes beyond vague "diversity" rhetoric.
  • Representation ablation convincingly shows that unprojected activation keys uniquely preserve route structure where PCA and sentence embeddings fail.
  • Strong cross-architecture and cross-scale replication evidence (4 additional models beyond the 3 primary).
  • Blinded human annotation with two independent non-author annotators and cross-annotator agreement metrics (κ=0.97 for BCC, κ=0.74 for families).
  • The paper clearly separates what the graph measures (route geometry) from what it does not do (decode, predict correctness).

Limitations

  • Requires internal activation access (MLP hooks), restricting use to open-weight models with instrumentation support.
  • Across-family human strategy differentiation is partial (47.6%), indicating families are finer-grained than human strategy categories — the "different strategy" interpretation requires careful qualification.
  • Effect attenuates on the 72B model where near-deterministic solving compresses multi-route structure; applicability to frontier-scale models is uncertain.
  • Purely diagnostic with no decoding application; how to exploit process isomerism for improved inference remains future work.
  • Scoped to non-code math/science benchmarks; code generation tasks may exhibit different process geometry.
  • The label-seeded reward field multi-core claim is not a clean one-sided null result (label permutations produce more multi-cores than real labels).

Relevance

本文提出的 SliceGraph 框架与 LLM 推理多样性分析、链式思维机制可解释性研究高度相关。过程同分异构体的发现对理解 test-time compute 中的采样策略和 self-consistency 方法有直接启发，为未来设计更好的推理路径选择和过程奖励模型提供了新的结构化视角。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can process isomer detection be used online during sampling to guide diverse trajectory generation, rather than only post-hoc analysis?
  • How does process isomerism behave on frontier-scale models (e.g., >100B parameters) where correctness rates are much higher — does the multi-route geometry collapse or persist?
  • Can the reward field be inverted to serve as a process reward model (PRM) that scores partial trajectories by their alignment with high-value cores?
  • What is the relationship between process families discovered by SliceGraph and the latent reasoning strategies identified by probing-based methods?
  • Does actively maintaining route diversity (e.g., by rewarding cross-family sampling) improve best-of-N selection accuracy compared to standard self-consistency?
