Forgetting That Sticks: Quantization-Permanent Unlearning via Circuit Attribution

arXiv: 2605.15138

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15138

一句话总结： 本文揭示了现有机器遗忘方法在4-bit量化后遗忘知识会恢复的根本原因（参数更新幅度远低于NF4量化bin宽度），并提出了MANSU方法，通过因果电路归因定位知识子图、电路受限零空间投影和参数幅度下限机制，首次同时实现有效遗忘、保留能力、量化永久性和结构性擦除四个目标。
TL;DR: Existing LLM unlearning methods fail under 4-bit quantization because per-parameter updates are 47–828× below the NF4 bin width. MANSU resolves this by combining causal circuit attribution, circuit-restricted null-space projection, and a magnitude floor, achieving the first method that jointly satisfies meaningful forgetting, retain preservation, quantization permanence, and structural erasure across multiple model families and hazard benchmarks.

核心贡献

  • 揭示了机器遗忘的双重失败模式：基于梯度的方法在量化后遗忘知识恢复（PTQ gap +0.05至+0.07）；偏好优化方法虽然量化稳定但几乎没有真正改变模型（遗忘集准确率平均仅降低1.6个百分点）
  • 提出了稀疏性-永久性权衡（Sparsity-Permanence Tradeoff）：当梯度更新分散在数十亿参数上时，单参数更新幅度远低于NF4量化bin宽度（约8.4×10⁻⁴），因此量化时被抹零
  • 设计了MANSU三阶段管线：EAP-IG因果电路定位 → 电路受限零空间投影（对角Fisher保留约束）→ 参数幅度下限保证量化存活
  • 引入了电路归因散度（CAD）指标，首次区分了结构性知识擦除与行为抑制，这是现有行为指标无法做到的
  • 在94个非MANSU实验中系统性验证了双重失败模式，覆盖3个模型族、3个危害领域和2个基准测试

Key Contributions

  • Dual failure mode documentation: First systematic evidence across 94 experiments that no existing method achieves both meaningful forgetting and quantization permanence — gradient methods recover under NF4 while preference-optimization methods barely change the model
  • Sparsity-permanence tradeoff formalization: Per-parameter updates lie 47–828× below the NF4 bin width because gradient-based objectives distribute updates over billions of parameters (Proposition 1)
  • MANSU method: Three-component approach with formal guarantees — circuit-restricted null-space projection gives tighter retain bound than global projection (Theorem 1, via Cauchy interlace), and magnitude floor guarantees quantization survival by construction (Lemma 1)
  • Circuit Attribution Divergence (CAD): First mechanistic verification metric distinguishing structural knowledge erasure from behavioral suppression — CAD ≈ 0 for inference-time redirection (LUNAR), CAD ≫ 0 for weight-level erasure (MANSU)
  • Cross-architecture validation: Results replicate on Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen-3-8B, and six additional model variants across WMDP-bio/chem/cyber and MUSE benchmarks

Methodology

MANSU operates in three phases:

  1. Localize: Uses EAP-IG (Edge Attribution Patching with Integrated Gradients) to identify the minimal MLP circuit causally responsible for forget-set answers. Aggregating attribution over 50 forget examples yields the top-K circuit covering ~3.2% of parameters.
  1. Project: Gradient updates restricted to circuit C are masked along high-Fisher coordinates (99th percentile threshold), approximating projection into the null space of the circuit-restricted retain Fisher. All parameters outside C are frozen. This yields a provably tighter retain bound than global projection (Theorem 1).
  1. Floor: After training converges, a post-hoc magnitude floor rescales every cumulative update below the NF4 bin width δᵢ to clear the nearest bin boundary, guaranteeing Q₄(θᵢ + Δθᵢ) ≠ Q₄(θᵢ) by construction (Lemma 1). The training objective jointly encodes forget loss maximization, frozen-reference KL divergence on retain set, and the magnitude floor constraint.

Additionally, CAD is defined as the relative collapse of EAP-IG attribution mass on the original forget circuit when re-run on the unlearned model. Companion metrics AS-C/AS-NC measure activation shifts inside/outside circuit C to diagnose whether changes are localized.

Results & Highlights

  • On Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct / WMDP-bio: MANSU achieves forget accuracy 0.430 (BF16) → 0.390 (NF4), PTQ gap −0.040 (quantization amplifies erasure), MMLU 0.573 (within 0.030 of zero-shot 0.603)
  • Qwen-3-8B / WMDP-bio: Forget 0.617 → 0.581, PTQ gap −0.036, MMLU 0.729 (vs zero-shot 0.741)
  • MANSU's ∆PTQ is non-positive on all 8/8 (model, dataset) cells — NF4 amplifies rather than reverses erasure
  • By contrast, Global GA's PTQ gap is +0.050 (knowledge returns under quantization); SimNPO/GU+SimNPO have PTQ gap 0.000 but destroy MMLU (0.295/0.200)
  • CAD for MANSU: 1.143 (WMDP-bio), 1.097 (chem), 1.118 (cyber), 1.671 (MUSE) — indicating genuine structural erasure
  • LUNAR's CAD is 0.029–0.045 across all cells, confirming it only redirects outputs without structural changes
  • Ablations confirm each component is necessary: removing the floor weakens ∆PTQ to −0.008; removing null-space projection drops MMLU to 0.449; random circuit reduces CAD by 35% (1.143 → 0.743); global projection + floor still fails (∆PTQ = +0.013)

Authors & Credibility

The authors (Saisab Sadhu, Pratinav Seth, Vinay Kumar Sankarapu) are affiliated with Lexsi Labs. The paper provides rigorous formal analysis with full proofs in appendices (Theorem 1, Lemma 1, Propositions 1–2), extensive ablation studies (Table 4), and 94 baseline experiments across multiple model families and benchmarks. The work builds on established methods (EAP-IG, GU, NPO) and directly engages with concurrent findings (ICLR 2025 paper on quantization reversing unlearning). The authors commit to releasing implementation and evaluation scripts upon acceptance.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The sparsity-permanence tradeoff formalization is a genuinely new insight — the connection between update sparsity and quantization bin widths is elegant and well-argued. CAD as a mechanistic verification metric addresses a real gap in evaluation practice.
  • Rigor: Strong formal treatment with proofs. Theorem 1's use of Cauchy interlace for tighter bounds is clean. However, the diagonal-Fisher approximation in Phase 2 introduces unquantified error (acknowledged in Appendix C). The magnitude floor is applied post-hoc rather than during training, which could interact with convergence in subtle ways.
  • Reproducibility: All datasets are public (WMDP, MMLU, MUSE). EAP-IG is open-source. Fixed evaluation indices will be released. The method requires EAP-IG attribution which is computationally expensive but well-documented.
  • Significance: The finding that standard unlearning evaluations are fundamentally inadequate (behavioral metrics in full precision do not predict post-quantization behavior) has major practical implications for AI safety certification. If widely adopted, the PTQ gap and CAD metrics could reshape evaluation standards.
  • Red flags: (1) Results are limited to 8B-parameter models; scaling behavior of the magnitude floor to 70B+ models is not verified, though the theoretical argument (√(1/d) scaling) is consistent. (2) MANSU's forget accuracy (0.430 on WMDP-bio) is not as aggressive as Global GA (0.260) — there is a meaningful gap in absolute forgetting depth. (3) The paper evaluates on MCQ benchmarks and MUSE open-ended memorization; generalization to more complex knowledge formats remains open. (4) The 3.2% circuit coverage is method-specific and may not generalize to all types of hazardous knowledge.

Strengths

  • Identifies a fundamental structural problem (quantization bin width vs. update magnitude) rather than proposing incremental fixes
  • Clean three-phase pipeline where each component has a formal justification and is independently validated via ablations
  • CAD metric is a genuine contribution that could outlast the specific method
  • Consistent results across two flagship 8B model families, three hazard domains, and two benchmark types
  • Honest about limitations (8B regime, factual-recall benchmarks)

Limitations

  • Forget depth (0.430 on WMDP-bio) is moderate compared to more aggressive methods — 57% of hazardous knowledge remains accessible
  • Only evaluated on 8B models; the 1/d scaling argument suggests larger models face even wider bin gaps, but circuit identification may also become harder
  • EAP-IG circuit attribution is computationally expensive and its reliability for unlearning targeting has been questioned by concurrent work ([18], [19])
  • The magnitude floor introduces parameter perturbation that is provably safe for retain performance in theory but could interact unexpectedly with downstream tasks not measured by MMLU/IFEval
  • The paper does not address adversarial attacks (e.g., fine-tuning attacks) on MANSU-unlearned models

Relevance

本文与AI安全和模型编辑的研究方向高度相关。揭示了现有遗忘方法在部署条件（量化）下系统性失效的问题，对实际部署中的知识擦除具有重要意义。因果电路定位和量化感知遗忘的结合为可信赖AI提供了新思路。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does MANSU scale to 70B+ models where the NF4 bin width / update magnitude ratio is even more unfavorable?
  • Can the magnitude floor be integrated into training (rather than applied post-hoc) to improve convergence properties?
  • How robust is MANSU to fine-tuning attacks that attempt to recover unlearned knowledge?
  • Does CAD correlate with other notions of robust unlearning (e.g., resistance to jailbreaks, adversarial prompts)?
  • What happens when the forget set is broader (e.g., entire domains rather than specific MCQ hazards) — does the 3.2% circuit assumption break down?
