> **一句话总结：** 本文发现从同一基座微调得到的PDE端点专家在权重空间中形成一条可校准的物理方向，据此提出CCM方法，通过物理元数据或短轨迹前缀选择合成坐标，实现免训练的跨PDE参数区间迁移，在OOD区间分别降低54.2%、42.8%和13.8%的推演误差。

> **TL;DR:** This paper shows that endpoint experts fine-tuned from a shared PDE surrogate define a calibratable physical direction in weight space, and proposes Calibration-Conditioned Merge (CCM) to compose a single checkpoint at the correct coordinate for the target regime — achieving training-free OOD rollout error reductions of 54.2%, 42.8%, and 13.8% across three PDE families.

## 核心贡献

- 将同一基座微调得到的PDE端点专家重新解释为权重空间中物理方向的有限差分探针，而非孤立的检查点。
- 将端点残差分解为"族共享适应"和"有符号物理方向"，从几何角度解释了为什么静态平均在区间内有效但在外推时衰减物理信息。
- 提出CCM方法，利用物理元数据、标定映射或短观测前缀推断目标合成坐标，部署单一合并检查点，无需目标区间微调、路由或集成。
- 在反应–扩散、Navier–Stokes和径向溃坝三个基准上验证，外推区间误差显著下降。

## Key Contributions

- Reinterpret same-anchor PDE endpoint experts as finite-difference probes of a local physical direction in weight space rather than isolated regime checkpoints.
- Decompose endpoint–anchor residuals into a family-shared adaptation component and a signed physical direction, explaining why static averaging interpolates but attenuates endpoint-specific physics.
- Propose Calibration-Conditioned Merge (CCM), a post-hoc coordinate readout method that composes a single merged checkpoint using physical metadata (CCM-Coord), a calibrated scale (CCM-Scale), or a short rollout prefix (CCM-Prefix) — no target-regime fine-tuning, routing, or ensembling required.
- Demonstrate OOD rollout error reductions of 54.2% (DiffReact), 42.8% (NS2D), and 13.8% (RDB) relative to the family anchor baseline.

## Methodology

The method operates on three fixed objects: a family anchor θ₀ trained on support regimes, and two endpoint experts θ_low and θ_high fine-tuned from θ₀ on the low and high endpoint regimes respectively. The key insight is the decomposition of endpoint residuals:

- **Family-shared adaptation**: Δ₊ = (Δ_low + Δ_high) / 2, capturing solver improvements common to both endpoints.
- **Signed physical direction**: Δ₋ = (Δ_high − Δ_low) / 2, capturing the low-to-high physical variation.
- **Composed checkpoint**: θ(α) = θ₀ + Δ₊ + αΔ₋, where α is the coordinate along the physical direction.

The physical coordinate is normalized so that endpoints sit at α = ±1, interpolation at |α| ≤ 1, and extrapolation at |α| > 1. CCM selects α through three variants:
1. **CCM-Coord**: Uses known normalized physical coordinate s(λ) directly (α = s).
2. **CCM-Scale**: Calibrates a single global scaling factor γ when the coordinate axis is ordered but mis-scaled (α = γs).
3. **CCM-Prefix**: Searches over a finite bank of candidate α values using a short K-step observed rollout prefix, selecting the one with minimal prefix L2 error, then evaluates future-only rollout.

The method is entirely post-hoc: no gradient updates, no architecture changes, no ensembling at inference. A single composed checkpoint is deployed for the entire rollout.

## Results & Highlights

- **DiffReact f2 dense (CCM-Coord)**: OOD mean L2 drops from 0.188 to 0.086 (54.2% reduction), matching the per-task oracle. Static averaging achieves only 2.4% improvement.
- **NS2D viscosity (CCM-Scale)**: OOD mean L2 drops from 0.591 to 0.338 (42.8% reduction). Endpoint averaging yields negligible 0.9% gain; the coordinate must be rescaled for viscosity-parameterized flows.
- **RDB high-center (CCM-Prefix)**: Future-rollout OOD mean L2 drops from 0.471 to 0.406 (13.8% reduction) using only a 4-step prefix for calibration.
- **Wrong-sign control**: Flipping α = −s instead of α = s increases errors 1.69×–3.91× across all task groups, confirming that gains arise from physical orientation rather than arbitrary interpolation.
- **Architecture robustness**: Gains hold across small/base/large FNO scales (endpoint averaging improves 9.6%→24.8% with scale), a DPOT-style backbone (CCM-Coord matches oracle at 0.0183 vs. base 0.0447), and matched-seed independent reruns.
- **Generic merge baselines**: Task arithmetic, TIES, and DARE all fail catastrophically on DiffReact f3 large FNO (errors 27100–298.3 vs. 0.113 for endpoint average), demonstrating that CCM's physical decomposition is critical.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The key insight — decomposing same-anchor PDE expert residuals into shared adaptation + signed physical direction — is elegant and previously unexplored. The connection between weight-space geometry and physical coordinates is a genuine conceptual advance over generic model merging.
- **Rigor**: Strong. The paper includes wrong-sign controls, matched-seed reruns, FNO scale sweeps, DPOT backbone validation, and oracle comparisons. The distinction between coordinate-selection and endpoint-selection is cleanly separated. However, the "local chart" assumption means the method's validity region is not precisely bounded.
- **Reproducibility**: Moderate-to-good. Training settings, family constructions, and merge protocols are detailed in appendices. The code does not appear to be publicly available at time of writing. The method itself (weighted combination of three checkpoints) is trivially implementable.
- **Significance**: Significant for the neural operator community. CCM provides a principled, training-free way to compose PDE experts that outperforms naive averaging and generic merging by a large margin in extrapolative regimes. The diagnostic value of the decomposition (revealing whether expert fine-tuning is physically structured) is also notable.
- **Red flags**: The method presupposes a shared anchor and same-architecture experts fine-tuned along a single scalar physical axis. Real-world PDE families with coupled multi-parameter variation would require extension. The locality assumption (|s| near endpoint interval) limits extrapolation range. The RDB gains are modest (13.8%), and CCM-Prefix depends on having access to a short observed rollout, which is not truly zero-shot.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical framing: weight-space decomposition into shared + directional components is intuitive and well-motivated.
- Comprehensive evaluation across three distinct PDE families with multiple CCM variants adapted to different information conditions.
- Strong OOD improvements, especially on DiffReact and NS2D, far exceeding static averaging baselines.
- Training-free: no gradient updates, no routing, no ensembling — a single checkpoint is deployed.
- Wrong-sign controls are a particularly convincing ablation that rules out arbitrary interpolation as the explanation.

## Limitations

- Restricted to single-scalar family axes; multi-parameter PDE families with coupled interactions are not addressed.
- The local chart assumption breaks down for large endpoint gaps or far extrapolation — no theoretical bound on the valid coordinate range.
- CCM-Prefix requires an observed rollout prefix, precluding true zero-shot deployment.
- RDB improvements are modest relative to the other benchmarks, suggesting the approach may be less effective when physical metadata is unreliable and rollout dynamics are complex.
- The endpoint branch selection uses a "locality criterion" (conservative gaps, low learning rate, support–anchor mixing) that introduces some hand-tuning.

## Relevance

这篇工作对神经算子基础模型的权重空间几何提供了深刻见解，提出的CCM方法为PDE代理模型在不同物理参数区间间的免训练迁移提供了一条简洁有效的路径，与参数化PDE求解和模型合成的核心研究方向高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can CCM be extended to multi-parameter PDE families using cross-axis experts or higher-order weight-space charts?
2. How does the local chart assumption degrade as a function of endpoint gap size — is there a predictable boundary beyond which the linear decomposition fails?
3. Could CCM be combined with parameter-efficient fine-tuning (e.g., LoRA) to reduce the storage cost of maintaining multiple endpoint experts?
4. Does the physical direction persist across fundamentally different architectures (e.g., U-Nets, graph neural operators) or is it specific to spectral methods like FNO?
5. What is the theoretical relationship between the CCM coordinate and the underlying PDE parameter — is linearity exact in some limit or always an approximation?
