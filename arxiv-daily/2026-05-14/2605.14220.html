Diagnosing Training Inference Mismatch in LLM Reinforcement Learning

arXiv: 2605.14220

Date: 2026-05-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14220

⚠️ Abstract-only summary

一句话总结： 本文揭示了LLM强化学习中 rollout 生成与策略优化阶段之间的 token 概率数值不一致（Training-Inference Mismatch），证明了这种微小的数值差异能独立导致训练崩溃，并提出了一套诊断框架和缓解方案。
TL;DR: This paper identifies Training-Inference Mismatch (TIM) — numerical disagreements in token probabilities between rollout generation and policy optimization in LLM RL — and shows that even small TIM can independently cause training collapse, advocating for treating it as a first-order stability factor.

核心贡献

  • 在零不匹配诊断设定（VeXact）下隔离并量化了 LLM 强化学习中的 Training-Inference Mismatch（TIM）现象。
  • 证明了即使是很小的 token 级数值不一致也能独立导致训练崩溃，而非仅仅是可忽略的数值噪声。
  • 揭示了 TIM 会改变实际面对的优化问题本质。
  • 提出了一系列缓解 TIM 的具体措施。

Key Contributions

  • Introduce VeXact, a zero-mismatch diagnostic setting that isolates TIM from off-policy drift and stabilization mechanisms.
  • Demonstrate that small token-level numerical disagreements between rollout and training pipelines can independently cause training collapse.
  • Show that TIM alters the effective optimization problem being solved.
  • Identify a set of practical remedies to mitigate TIM in LLM RL systems.

Methodology

The authors isolate TIM by constructing a controlled diagnostic environment (VeXact) that eliminates confounding factors such as off-policy drift and common stabilization tricks. In this zero-mismatch setting, they measure token-level probability disagreements between the rollout generation stage and the policy optimization stage under identical model weights. They then analyze how these numerical discrepancies propagate through training and affect RL stability.

Results & Highlights

  • Small token-level numerical mismatches between rollout and training are not benign — they can independently trigger training collapse.
  • TIM effectively changes the optimization landscape, meaning the algorithm is not optimizing the intended objective.
  • The proposed remedies can mitigate TIM, suggesting it is a tractable systems-level issue.

Authors & Credibility

The work comes from a multi-institution team including Geoffrey Fox (a highly cited computer scientist known for work in distributed and high-performance computing) and researchers with expertise in ML systems and reinforcement learning. The topic is timely given the widespread adoption of RLHF and similar RL-based LLM training pipelines.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate to high. While numerical precision issues in deep learning are well-known, systematically diagnosing and quantifying their impact specifically in the LLM RL pipeline (rollout vs. optimization) is a valuable and relatively understudied angle.
  • Rigor: The use of a controlled diagnostic setting (VeXact) to isolate TIM from confounders is methodologically sound. However, without access to the full paper, it is unclear how extensive the empirical evaluation is across different model scales and RL algorithms.
  • Reproducibility: Likely high if VeXact is clearly specified; the core insight relies on comparing token probabilities between two implementations, which is straightforward to verify.
  • Significance: Potentially high. If TIM is indeed a first-order factor in LLM RL instability, this could influence how all major LLM RL training infrastructure is built and validated.
  • Red flags: Abstract-only assessment limits depth. The claim that TIM "changes the effective optimization problem" is strong and needs careful empirical substantiation across diverse settings.

Strengths

  • Addresses a practical and underappreciated systems-level issue in modern LLM RL training.
  • Clean experimental design isolating TIM from other sources of instability.
  • Actionable recommendations rather than just diagnosis.

Limitations

  • Without the full paper, the generality of the findings across different RL algorithms (PPO, GRPO, DPO, etc.), model sizes, and infrastructure implementations is unclear.
  • The abstract does not quantify how common TIM is across popular training frameworks or how expensive the proposed remedies are.
