> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"最小核心"框架——通过贪心反向消除从过度完整的CoT推理链中提取保持预测行为的最小步骤子集，发现平均46%步骤可被移除而86%情况下保留原答案，且最小核心嵌入比全轨迹更紧凑（探针精度+11点、内在维度降34%、跨模型家族85%答案保留率）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper formalizes "minimal cores" — the smallest subsets of chain-of-thought steps that preserve a model's final answer (answer-sufficient) or predictive distribution (distribution-sufficient) — and extracts them via greedy backward elimination. Across six benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH500, AIME24, AMC23, GPQA-Diamond, StrategyQA) and four reasoning models (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B/32B, Qwen3-32B, GPT-5), greedy minimal cores retain only ~54% of steps while preserving 86% of full-trace answers; versus random deletion at matched 40–50% budgets on GSM8K, greedy deletion raises answer retention from ~0.47 (random) to ~0.79.

## 核心贡献

- **轨迹级最小核心（minimal core）**：把生成式推理轨迹形式化为"过度完整对象"，定义保持模型最终答案或预测分布不变的最小步骤子集，将推理链分析转为子集充分性问题。
- **必要性与过度完整度量**：提出压缩比 CR、冗余质量 RM、留一消除步骤必要性 Δt、以及 top-k 必要性质量 NMass_k 四个互补指标，刻画可移除推理质量与预测支持的稀疏性。
- **提取保证**：证明最小核心存在性（Theorem 3.1）、贪心反向消除返回局部不可约充分子集（Theorem 3.2），并给出过度完整性证书（充分删除集上界 CR ≤ (T−k)/T）与稀疏边际必要性证书。
- **几何与迁移分析**：在六个基准（GSM8K、MATH500、AIME24、AMC23、GPQA-Diamond、StrategyQA）与四个模型（DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B/32B、Qwen3-32B、GPT-5）上证明最小核心嵌入比全轨迹更紧凑、更可分、更可迁移。

## Key Contributions

- Define the **minimal core** as a smallest subset of generated reasoning steps preserving either the top-1 answer (answer-sufficient) or the full predictive distribution within divergence ε (distribution-sufficient).
- Introduce four complementary metrics — compression ratio (CR), redundancy mass (RM), leave-one-out step necessity (Δt), and top-k necessity mass (NMass_k) — together with greedy backward elimination (Algorithm 1), necessity-guided pruning, and a matched-budget random-deletion baseline.
- Provide formal guarantees: existence of minimal sufficient subsets (Theorem 3.1), local irreducibility of greedy deletion (Theorem 3.2), overcompleteness certificates that bound CR and RM from any sufficient deletion set, and a sparse marginal-necessity certificate.
- Empirically demonstrate, on GSM8K/MATH500/AIME24/AMC23/GPQA-Diamond/StrategyQA across DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B, Qwen3-32B, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B and GPT-5, that minimal cores are shorter, transferable across model families, and occupy a cleaner representation geometry than full traces or removed-step embeddings.

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** Given input x and model M generating trace z = (r1,…,rT) followed by answer, a retained subset S induces prediction ŷ_M(x, zS). A subset S is answer-sufficient if ŷ_M(x,zS) = ŷ_M(x,z[T]); distribution-sufficient if D(p_M(·|x,zS), p_M(·|x,z[T])) ≤ ε. The **minimal core** S* = argmin|S| s.t. Suff(S;x,z,M)=1.

**Extraction (Algorithm 1).** Greedy backward elimination: start from full trace, compute admissible deletions A(S) = {t ∈ S : Suff(S\{t}) = 1}, delete the admissible step with smallest harm score h_t(S) (verifier score / answer confidence / margin / loss-increase depending on access), recompute admissibility, repeat until none admissible. Worst-case O(T²) sufficiency checks (versus O(2^T) exhaustive). Necessity-guided pruning (O(T) evaluations, no recompute) and matched-budget random deletion serve as scalable / baseline alternatives.

**Necessity scores.** Leave-one-out Δt = ℓ(x, z[T]\{t}) − ℓ(x, z[T]); normalized weight w_t = max(Δt,0) / (Σⱼ max(Δⱼ,0) + η), η=10⁻⁸; top-k mass NMass_k = Σ_{t∈TopK} w_t.

**Representation geometry.** Step embedding ϕ(r_t) = mean-pooled token hidden states from a fixed decoder layer (final transformer layer for open-weight generators; sentence encoder for API-only models). Full-trace h(z), minimal-core h(z*), removed-step h(z⁻) embeddings, plus necessity-weighted h_Δ(z). Compared along compactness (Var), correctness separation (linear probes, nearest-neighbour, silhouette, Davies–Bouldin), and intrinsic dimensionality (NN/PCA participation ratio).

**Compute & models.** Open-weight runs on 8× NVIDIA A100 80GB, inference-only; GPT-5 via API. Traces generated with low-temperature CoT prompting, segmented into numbered or sentence-level steps. Distributional sufficiency uses KL divergence over candidate-answer distribution when token likelihoods are available.

## Results & Highlights

- **Overcompleteness (Table 1).** Averaged across six benchmarks and four models: full length 9.9±1.9 steps, core length 5.3±1.3, CR = 0.54±0.03, RM = 0.46±0.07, Top-3 Mass = 0.65±0.09, Retention = 0.86±0.01. Strongest redundancy on GSM8K (RM up to 0.61 for GPT-5) and StrategyQA; weakest on AIME24 / GPQA-Diamond.
- **Pruning baselines (Table 2, GSM8K @ 40% removal).** Random 0.47 → Nec.-guided 0.70 → Greedy 0.79 answer retention; @50%: Random 0.57 → Nec. 0.77 → Greedy 0.84. Greedy beats one-shot necessity-guided by +0.07 to +0.09 over random's own Δ.
- **Necessity concentration (Table 3a).** Across datasets, top-1 step T1 ≈ 0.29, top-3 T3 = 0.65, top-5 T5 = 0.82, Gini 0.54 of normalized necessity mass — sharply above uniform.
- **Cross-model transfer (Table 3b).** Minimal-core length 5.2 vs full 9.9; off-diagonal answer retention 0.85 (minimal core) vs 0.88 (full trace) vs 0.64 (matched-length random subset) — i.e., ~97% of full-trace transfer signal retained at 53% relative length.
- **Geometry (Table 4a).** On GSM8K, correctness-probe accuracy: Full 0.71 → Core 0.82 → Removed 0.56; silhouette 0.18 → 0.31; Davies–Bouldin 1.42 → 0.96; intrinsic dim 18.7 → 11.3 (~−40%). Across the table, cores improve probe accuracy by ~+11 points and cut intrinsic dimensionality by ~34% on average.
- **Ablations (Table 4b).** Distribution-sufficiency (ε=0.05) inflates cores to CR 0.66 / RM 0.34 but retention rises to 0.93. Plan-then-solve (CR 0.57) and concise-CoT (CR 0.68, shorter full length 6.4) remain overcomplete. Hard-split retention 0.69 vs easy 0.91. Segmentation (sentence/numbered/paragraph) changes absolute length but preserves the trend.

## Critical Assessment

- **Greedy is only locally irreducible, not globally minimal** (explicitly stated in §3.1 and the Conclusion). Theorem 3.2 guarantees termination and sufficiency but not optimality; the empirical gap to the true optimum is never measured, so the reported 46% removable mass is a *lower-bound* on true overcompleteness, not an unbiased estimate.
- **Behavioural, model-relative sufficiency.** The authors are candid that minimal cores are *not* causal or human-rationale explanations (§2.1, §6). Sufficiency holds only under the chosen sufficiency oracle; the same trace may yield different cores under answer- vs distribution-preserving criteria (Table 4b: CR jumps from 0.54 to 0.66 under ε=0.05 KL).
- **Leave-one-out necessity undercounts substitutable steps.** The paper flags that duplicate or substitutable steps each receive low Δt even if the group collectively supports the answer (§2.3); necessity-mass numbers therefore characterise *unique marginal* support, not total contribution — a caveat readers can easily miss given the headline "65% of necessity" framing.
- **Closed-model asymmetry.** GPT-5 supports only behavioural metrics; likelihood/hidden-state geometry claims (the −34% intrinsic-dimensionality and +11 probe-accuracy results) rest entirely on the three open-weight Qwen/DeepSeek models, so geometry conclusions do not transfer verbatim to GPT-5.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated formalisation (minimal core, CR, RM, NMass_k) that turns a fuzzy "CoT is verbose" intuition into a measurable subset-sufficiency problem.
- Honest baselines: matched-budget random *and* one-shot necessity-guided pruning isolate the value of adaptive greedy deletion from raw trace-length slack.
- Theory is non-trivial but load-bearing: existence, local irreducibility, overcompleteness certificates, and a sparse-necessity certificate all follow from the same sufficiency predicate.
- Strong robustness story across sufficiency criterion, prompting style, difficulty split, and segmentation rule.

## Limitations

- Greedy extraction is O(T²) sufficiency checks per trace; the paper notes worst-case cost but does not give wall-clock numbers or scaling beyond short CoT traces.
- The 86% retention figure means ~14% of answers flip post-extraction; failure-mode characterisation (which tasks, which step types) is thin.
- Post-hoc analysis only — the paper does not train or prompt models to emit minimal cores directly, so downstream efficiency gains are implied rather than demonstrated.
- Geometry analysis depends on the choice of decoder layer and embedding pooling rule; layer sensitivity is not reported in the main text.

## Relevance

直接对应"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"（最小核心嵌入的几何分析、必要性权重探针、内在维度估计）与"LLM Inference Evaluation"（提出基于子集充分性的可压缩性/必要性度量，揭示CoT评估中正确性与冗余的混淆）。其留一消除框架也可服务于"LLM Training Stability & Observability"下的轨迹级诊断。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can models be RL-finetuned to emit minimal-core traces directly (cf. TRIMS arXiv:2603.17449, Surgical Trimming), and does that close the 14% answer-flip gap?
2. How does the gap between greedy and exhaustive minimal cores grow with trace length T, and is it bounded empirically?
3. Are the high-necessity steps stable across sampling temperatures / prompts, or do they shuffle — i.e., is "necessity" a property of the step or of the sampling?
4. Does minimal-core geometry transfer to *non-reasoning* tasks (classification, code generation), or is the cleaner-geometry effect specific to deliberative traces?
5. Could overcompleteness certificates (any sufficient deletion ⇒ overcomplete) be used as an online training-time regulariser against verbose CoT collapse?
