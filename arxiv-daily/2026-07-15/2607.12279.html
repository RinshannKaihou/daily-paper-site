A Shared Subcircuit Lets LLMs Count Down Across Tasks

Jacob Dunefsky (Yale) · Wes Gurnee (Anthropic) · Emmanuel Ameisen (Anthropic)

arXiv:2607.12279v1 · 14 Jul 2026 · arxiv.org/abs/2607.12279 ↗

Key Finding

Three attention heads at Llama-3.1-70B's layer 18 form a "countdown subcircuit" that reads the target length and continuously tracks the remaining distance — and the same geometric motif recurs across tasks and models.

Countdown heads · L18
earlyposition →late
Key Metric 0.93–0.97 r²

Cross-task length representations fit near-linear lines across base64, DNA, and explicit-length prompt types — evidence of one shared length code.

Why This Matters

A reusable paradigm: reverse-engineer one circuit, then probe its outputs unsupervised to discover how widely it's reused — with cross-task and cross-model geometric backing.

Critical Edge

Natural-task claims rest on probe activation alone: the three countdown heads are never causally ablated to prove they're necessary on tweets, hashes, or haikus.

01

Methodology

1

Controlled sentence-planning task · Sec. 2

Prompt: Write a 10-word sentence ending in "telescope". Near-perfect for targets < 11 words; error grows slowly beyond. Each prompt carries a target-length token and a length-unit token (-word).

2

Residual-stream activation patching · Sec. 2.2

For prompt pairs sharing a target word but differing in length, source activations are patched at the length tokens per layer; the target-word logit jumps from ~layer 4 and collapses after layer 18 — localizing length info there.

3

Countdown-head identification · Sec. 2.3

Output norms of all layer-18 heads: only L18H24 / H28 / H30 rise above zero, with overlapping receptive fields (H28 early, H24 mid, H30 late) peaking smoothly right before the target length.

4

Implicit-length repeated-string setting · Sec. 3

Delimited base64 / DNA strings of equal length. Patching ending-delimiter activations shifts the model's predicted length toward the source — delimiters store causally-sufficient length info, bracketed by roofline & baseline.

5

Observational geometry · Sec. 3.3–3.4

Pairwise cosine similarities of length/delimiter keys are unimodal & linear (r² 0.93–0.97). PCA shows aligned curved 1-D manifolds with a diagonal peak plus off-diagonal "ringing" — the same motif as Claude 3.5 Haiku line-wrapping.

6

Unsupervised probing for universality · Sec. 4

Distance probes from countdown-head outputs run on a 10k-sample Pile subset and 5k-conversation LMSYS-1M subset; high-activation chunks are labeled & curated into task themes.

02

Results & Highlights

Controlled-task accuracy

Median error < 4 words at length 24 — "(almost) all exactly the same length" below 11 words, degrading slowly thereafter.

Localized to layer 18

Patched target-word logit rises sharply from ~layer 4, then "drastically drops" after layer 18 to baseline.

Exactly three countdown heads

Only L18H24 / H28 / H30 have norms above zero; overlapping early/mid/late fields peak right before the target — consistent across lengths.

Implicit length causally stored at delimiters

Delimiter patching makes the model predict source length l_src, near the roofline & well below baseline for patching lengths 10–24.

Near-linear cross-task representation

Best-match target lengths fit lines with 0.93 ≤ r² ≤ 0.97 across explicit / base64 / DNA prompt types.

Wide task coverage from probing

Over 10k Pile + 5k LMSYS samples, ≥7 themes fire: tweets (280-char), DNA, coin tosses, SHA-256 (64 hex), 5-7-5 haikus, rounding, ASCII tables.

03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Multi-pronged causal localization: residual patching → head-norm analysis → delimiter patching with roofline/baseline bracketing.
  • Implicit-length-at-delimiters is a non-obvious, concrete mechanistic insight.
  • Strong cross-task linearity evidence (r² ≥ 0.93) across three very different prompt types.
  • A genuinely useful methodological template — and the loop paid off (probing-discovered DNA inspired the Sec. 3 study).
  • Honest framing relative to concurrent work (Merzouk et al., 2026).

Limitations

  • Natural-task evidence is qualitative & selection-biased (LLM-labeled + manually cherry-picked); no measure of real-world engagement rate.
  • No causal ablation of the three heads on natural tasks — necessity unproven outside controlled settings.
  • Controlled task degrades beyond ~11 words, capping the faithfully-characterized regime.
  • Probe threshold of 1.0 is "chosen heuristically" with no robustness sweep.
  • Cross-model "universality" is geometric/observational, not a new causal experiment in Claude.
  • Single model family (Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct) studied in depth.
04

Open Questions

?

Are L18H24/H28/H30 necessary, not just active, on natural tasks? What happens to tweets, hashes, and haikus when ablated or patched?

?

How robust is the discovered task set to the heuristic 1.0 probe threshold — what fraction of high-activation chunks are clean "countdown" themes vs. noise?

?

Does the same motif/geometry appear in Qwen, Mistral, Gemma — and can the unsupervised pipeline rediscover countdown heads there without supervision?

?

Can the delimiter mechanism be exploited to steer the model — injecting delimiter activations to force premature or extended string termination?

?

Is this the same physical circuit behind Merzouk et al. (2026)'s linear remaining-output-length probe, or a distinct overlapping one?

?

In the degraded regime (> 11 words), does the countdown become noisy, or does a different mechanism take over?
