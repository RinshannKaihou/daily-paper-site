Agent-Safety Evaluations as Load-Bearing Evidence: A Vendor-Neutral, Cross-Harness Reconstructability Metric

Key Finding

Identical nominal safety outcomes can rest on materially different evidence regimes — and now that gap is measurable.

A vendor-neutral, cross-harness “reconstructability” metric scores eight decision-property classes in [0,1], binds every evidence element to its decision, and shows two agent runs that read identically on the surface can hide a 0.375 spread in actual evidence sufficiency.

Why This Matters

Turns “does this safety number have evidence?” into a measurable, byte-reproducible engineering metric — directly usable as a CI release gate for agent evaluations.

Critical Edge

The required-evidence sets are author-declared, not empirically validated — so every score’s denominator is asserted, not measured.

Methodology

The unit of analysis is the evaluation claim — an assertion a safety eval makes about an agent decision on the strength of a recorded run. The instrument only measures evidence sufficiency: it does not adjudicate whether behaviour was safe, nor rank harnesses or vendors.

01

Eight property classes

Cross-walk Nian et al.’s five auditability dims to the claim unit; add claim sufficiency and eval-awareness confounding.

02

Per-property scoring

r_p = |present(p)| / |R_p| — proportion of a property’s required evidence the trace contains and binds to the decision.

03

Non-compensatory aggregation

Bottleneck scalar R_min = min over claim-required props. One zero drives the headline to zero.

04

Overclaim gap

G(A,B) = |R(A) − R(B)| between regimes sharing a nominal outcome, plus a property-resolved vector.

05

Replay protocol

Intervention spec separated from a deterministic precondition probe that re-runs nothing — possible / degraded / impossible per decision.

Key null observation: the two real public traces report identical sufficiency (0.458) and identical status counts (4 / 5 / 3), yet different blocking composition (F07 vs. F06). The discriminating signal lives in the blocking-gap lists, not the headline scalar. Counterfactual-replay preconditions were unmet in 100% of the six reported cards.

Strengths

  • Conceptually clean wedge. Shifts the question from “which step failed?” to “could the evidence the harness kept even answer that?” — distinct from AgenTracer (attribution) and EviBound (agent-self gating).
  • Deterministic, model-free, no judge in the loop. Well-motivated given the best LLM scores only 11% on TRAIL trace debugging.
  • Non-compensatory bottleneck scalar is the right design — prevents a high coverage mean from masking a zero-scored required property.
  • Standout reproducibility. Byte-identical regeneration via pinned SOURCE_DATE_EPOCH, sha256-verified inputs, Apache-2.0, archived DOI.
  • Unusual intellectual honesty. §7 lists five named threats T1–T5 with closure items; repeatedly flags what the demo does not establish.

Limitations

  • Schema validity is asserted, not measured (T1). Required-evidence sets R_p are author-declared with no inter-annotator agreement or ground truth — the central calibration target deferred to FW2.
  • Tiny empirical substrate. Four scored inputs; three of six Table 2 entries are synthetic fixtures. No real matched-outcome pair exists, so the overclaim gap G is exercised only on a synthetic pair.
  • Cross-harness ingestion demo'd on Inspect only. ControlArena reader is implemented but never run on a real export. Only one real harness is actually exercised.
  • No executed counterfactual replay. RQ3 answered only in precondition form; the causal question (does replay change a verdict?) is deferred to v2.
  • Released v0 is coarser than the design — 3-valued not unit-interval, 12 fields not 8 classes, per-trace not per-decision — and the field-level analogue of G is not the defined G.

Open Questions

  1. How would the eight required-evidence sets R_p change under a two-annotator ground-truth labelling exercise, and how sensitive are the bottleneck scores to membership perturbations of a single set?
  2. On a real ControlArena export (not synthetic), does the adapter still produce covered monitor-coverage verdicts, and does per-decision scoring surface gaps the v0 aggregation hides?
  3. Does executing the counterfactual-replay protocol ever change a reconstructability verdict on traces whose preconditions are met — i.e., is RQ3’s causal form ever non-null in practice?
  4. Can the field-level sufficiency_score analogue be formally related to Definition 4’s bottleneck R_min, or is the gap between “mean over twelve fields” and “min over claim-required properties” material enough to need a different v0?
  5. How does this instrument behave on traces from frontier / closed models and non-Inspect harnesses (e.g., MALT, LinuxArena), where logging conventions differ substantially?
