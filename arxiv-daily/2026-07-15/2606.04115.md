> **一句话总结：** dMX 提出一种面向 OCP MXFP 浮点格式的可微混合精度量化框架，将每层比特宽度折叠为一个连续可学习偏移量 β，配合温度退火逐步离散化到硬件支持的 MXFP8/MXFP4 或 MXFP6/MXFP4 配置；在 Llama 3.2 1B、Qwen3 1.7B、SmolLM2 1.7B（并扩展到 8B）上，dMX 在 WikiText-2 困惑度与四项 zero-shot 推理基准上 Pareto 支配均匀 MXFP4 基线，并在中间比特宽度区间优于基于 KL 散度的层选择启发式。

> **TL;DR:** dMX is the first gradient-based mixed-precision framework tailored to OCP MXFP floating-point formats (MXFP8/MXFP4, MXFP6/MXFP4). Each layer's format is folded into one continuous learnable offset β that is progressively discretized via temperature annealing, with a target-aware regularizer hitting a user-specified average bit-width budget. On Llama 3.2 1B, Qwen3 1.7B, and SmolLM2 1.7B (scaling to Llama 3.1 8B / Qwen3 8B), dMX Pareto-dominates uniform MXFP4 baselines and KL-divergence-based layer-selection heuristics on WikiText-2 perplexity and four zero-shot reasoning benchmarks; e.g., on Llama 3.2 1B at ~6.04 avg bits it reaches 9.83 PPL vs the 11.68 of MXFP4 (Δ ≈ −1.85) and 49.61 vs 47.82 zero-shot accuracy.

## 核心贡献

- **面向浮点的可微比特宽度参数化**：将每层浮点格式表达为统一形式 `MXFP(β) = E(2+β)M(1+β)`，把 (e, m) 的多维设计空间折叠成单个连续偏移 β ∈ [0, 2]，β=0 对应 MXFP4、β=2 对应 MXFP8、中间值对应 MXFP6；权重与激活可绑定，使每层只需学习一个标量。
- **连续前向 + 温度退火**：训练期间 β 取连续值，避免逐 forward 用 round+STE 在 MXFP4/MXFP8 间反复震荡；通过温度调节的 sigmoid `β = 2 / (1 + e^(−T·(β̂/2−0.5)))` 从近线性逐步锐化为阶跃函数，保证最终映射到硬件兼容格式，缩小训练/推理行为间隙。
- **目标感知正则化**：提出两种代价（简单缩放 `R_s = λ·b̄` 与目标感知 `R_t = max(0, λ·(b̄_current − b̄_target))`），后者可把平均比特宽度直接拉向用户预算；并比较了简单平均与按张量大小加权两种模型级代表比特宽度。
- **PTQ 兼容性**：可与基于梯度的权重优化、旋转预处理（如 SpinQuant 风格的 learned rotations）联合优化，校准仅用 FineWeb 的 3200 样本 / 400 步，远比完整 QAT 便宜。
- **实证证据**：在三类 LLM 家族（Llama、Qwen3、SmolLM2）及 3B–8B 规模上系统验证，并给出每层被保留为高精度的频率表，揭示 down proj 与 v proj 是量化敏感层。

## Key Contributions

- A differentiable bit-width parameterization for floating-point quantization that enables end-to-end gradient-based optimization of per-layer precision assignments, the first such framework explicitly targeting the OCP MX format family.
- A continuous forward-pass representation of the floating-point format during calibration that yields smoother optimization landscapes than discrete round+STE alternatives (which the paper shows oscillate between MXFP4 and MXFP8).
- A temperature-based annealing mechanism (exponential ramp with a warm-up ratio `T_ratio`) that progressively discretizes learned offsets to hardware-compatible MXFP endpoints, minimizing the train/inference behavior gap.
- A target-aware regularization term that steers the model's average bit-width toward a user-specified budget, plus a tensor-size-weighted average option that aligns the penalty with actual memory cost.
- Compatibility with existing PTQ pipelines (gradient-based weight optimization, rotation-based preprocessing such as SpinQuant), and a demonstration that MXFP6/MXFP4 is generally more bit-width efficient than MXFP8/MXFP4.

## Methodology

dMX rests on three observations: (1) floating-point quantization equations remain well-defined when exponent/mantissa bit-widths are treated as real-valued parameters (range bounds, exponent bias, and the ULP scale all vary continuously with e and m); (2) deployment still requires the final configuration to collapse onto the discrete, hardware-supported MXFP set; (3) gradients can be propagated w.r.t. the bit-width as with any learnable parameter, as long as the bit-width is stored as a float — an idea previously used for integer quantization (FracBits, SDQ, BSQ) but not for floating-point/MX.

**Continuous FP parameterization.** The standard FP value `x = (−1)^S · 2^(u−b) · (1 + Σ M_i · 2^(−i))` is quantized via a coarse scale `s = t/q_max^FP` and a per-element power-of-two fine scale `s_s(x̃) = 2^η(x̃)` with `η(x̃) = max(⌊log2|x̃|⌋ − m, e_min)` and `e_min = 1 − b − m`. With e and m treated as reals, `x_q` is differentiable in (e, m) except at rounding, which is handled by a straight-through estimator (Appendix A derives the m- and e-gradients across normal, subnormal, and clipped regimes). To restrict the search to admissible hardware formats, the family is collapsed to `MXFP(β) = E(2+β)M(1+β)`: β=2 → E4M3 (MXFP8), β=0 → E2M1 (MXFP4); intermediate values give MXFP6-like formats. For MXFP8/MXFP4 both e and m are optimized; for MXFP6/MXFP4 only m is optimized. Tying weight and activation bit-widths reduces the problem to one scalar per layer.

**Temperature annealing.** A sigmoid `β = F(β̂, T) = 2 / (1 + e^(−T·(β̂/2 − 0.5)))` is near-linear at low T (free exploration) and sharpens to a step at β=1 as T grows, pushing offsets below 1 to MXFP4 and above 1 to MXFP8/MXFP6.

**Regularization.** Either a simple scaling penalty `R_s = λ·b̄_current` or a target-aware penalty `R_t = max(0, λ·(b̄_current − b̄_target))`, with `b̄` computed as either a simple or tensor-size-weighted average over layers. The target-aware form produces denser, more predictable operating-point spreads.

**Training setup.** Calibration uses 3200 FineWeb samples (batch 8, 400 steps; 800 steps for 3B–8B models), jointly optimizing rotations and bit-widths in a SpinQuant-like infrastructure, quantized via Brevitas. Baselines are homogeneous MXFP4/MXFP6/MXFP8 with identical calibration; only the bit-widths are frozen.

## Results & Highlights

- **MXFP8/MXFP4 on Llama 3.2 1B (Table 1):** uniform MXFP4 gives WikiText-2 PPL 11.68 and zero-shot 47.82; dMX at an average bit-width of 6.04 achieves PPL **9.83** and zero-shot **49.61** (Δ ≈ −1.85 PPL, +1.79 acc over MXFP4), while MXFP8 (8.0 bits) sits at PPL 9.15 / 51.39 — i.e., dMX recovers most of the MXFP8 quality at substantially lower average bits.
- **MXFP8/MXFP4 on SmolLM2 1.7B:** dMX at 6.06 avg bits reaches PPL **8.26** and zero-shot **57.28** vs MXFP4 baseline PPL 10.28 / 53.42 and MXFP8 PPL 7.86 / 58.69.
- **MXFP6/MXFP4 is more bit-width efficient (Table 2):** on Llama 3.2 1B, dMX with MXFP6/MXFP4 at 5.02 avg bits reaches PPL **10.02** / zero-shot **50.26**, beating the MXFP8/MXFP4 point at a comparable 5.11 bits (PPL 10.60 / 49.27); at 5.98 avg bits dMX matches MXFP6-uniform quality (PPL 9.48 vs 9.16).
- **Learned vs KL-divergence pre-selected layer precision (Fig. 5):** at intermediate bit-widths (~5.0–7.0) the learned assignment achieves noticeably lower perplexity than the KL-based heuristic, whose sensitivity approximation flattens out in this region; both converge toward MXFP8 at the high-bit-width end.
- **Continuous vs ROUND+STE (Fig. 8, Appendix C):** the continuous forward representation consistently achieves better perplexity and zero-shot than ROUND+STE across all three models and is more reliable at hitting the target bit-width — evidence that avoiding abrupt format switches stabilizes optimization.
- **Scaling to 8B (Fig. 9, Appendix D):** trends carry over to Llama 3.2 3B, Llama 3.1 8B, Qwen3 4B, Qwen3 8B (with training steps raised to 800 and β-learning rate to 2); MXFP6/MXFP4 stays the more efficient configuration.
- **Sensitivity ranking (Tables 3–7, Appendix E):** across all models and configurations the `down proj` and `v proj` layers are the most frequently assigned the high-precision format (e.g., for Llama-3.2-1B under MXFP8/MXFP4, `down proj 1` and `v proj 1/2` hit frequency 1.00), confirming them as the most quantization-sensitive tensors.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel in scope. Prior differentiable bit-width work (FracBits, SDQ, BSQ, Q-ViT, HMQ) targets integer formats; the floating-point/MX setting is harder because (e, m) and the exponent bias are coupled and only a handful of configurations are hardware-legal. The single-offset `MXFP(β) = E(2+β)M(1+β)` parameterization and the continuous-forward + temperature-anneal design are a clean, well-motivated contribution, and the authors credibly claim first-mover status for gradient-based MX bit-width allocation.
- **Rigor:** Reasonable. Closed-form gradient derivations (Appendix A) are given across normal/subnormal/clipped regimes; ablations cover regularization form (target-aware vs simple scaling), cost averaging (simple vs weighted), discretization strategy (continuous vs ROUND+STE), and format pair (MXFP8/MXFP4 vs MXFP6/MXFP4). Three model families plus a 3B–8B scaling appendix give breadth.
- **Weak baselines / gaps:** The strongest external baseline is a **KL-divergence layer-selection heuristic** evaluated only on Llama 3.2 1B (Fig. 5) — not on SmolLM2 or Qwen3, and not against HAWQ/Hessian-trace, APTQ, MicroMix, or any integer-domain mixed-precision competitor. MicroMix (channel-wise MX assignment, 2025) is discussed as related work but never benchmarked head-to-head. This is the most conspicuous gap for a method whose selling point is "Pareto-dominating."
- **Missing ablations:** No sensitivity analysis to `T_ratio` values, temperature-schedule shape (only exponential ramp is used), or λ magnitude is reported in the main text; the warm-up robustness claim ("fairly robust") is asserted without numbers. The highest-target-bit-width failure mode (dMX undershooting the 8.0 target, visible in Table 1 where Llama hits 6.04/8.0 but never cleanly reaches intermediate 7.x) is acknowledged but attributed loosely to "more extensive hyper-parameter search."
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate-to-good. Calibration recipe (3200 FineWeb samples, batch 8, 400/800 steps), frameworks (Brevitas, LightEval, SpinQuant-like infra), and the exact MXFP(β) parameterization are specified; hyper-parameter tables are deferred to Appendix B but referenced. Code release is not clearly committed in the body.
- **Overclaim risk:** "Consistently yields Pareto-dominating models" is supported within the studied range, but the authors themselves note that for Llama models MXFP6/MXFP4 can underperform the uniform MXFP6 baseline on perplexity — a candid caveat that slightly tensions the headline claim and signals the method is not uniformly dominant across (model, format) pairs.
- **Red flags:** None severe. The Δ vs MXFP4 is partly mechanical (adding average bits lowers PPL), so the more meaningful comparisons are vs MXFP6-uniform and vs KL heuristic, where the wins are real but narrower and less uniformly reported.

## Strengths

- First principled, gradient-based framework for MXFP-format mixed precision, with a clean one-parameter-per-layer design that maps exactly onto legal hardware formats.
- Continuous forward representation plus temperature annealing is a well-engineered solution to the FP4↔FP8 oscillation problem, and the ROUND+STE ablation provides direct evidence it matters.
- Target-aware regularization gives practitioners a usable knob (desired average bit-width) rather than an opaque λ.
- Honest reporting: per-layer sensitivity tables, candid discussion of the high-target-bit-width failure and of model-specific underperformance, and a real scaling appendix up to 8B.
- Practical training cost (400 calibration steps) keeps it firmly in the PTQ regime.

## Limitations

- Baselines are thin: only uniform MXFP and a KL heuristic on one model; no comparison to HAWQ-V3, APTQ, MicroMix, or integer mixed-precision methods ported to the same setting.
- Hyper-parameter sensitivity (T_ratio, schedule shape, λ) is largely asserted rather than systematically swept.
- Only weights+activations are considered; no latency/throughput/memory measurements on real MX-capable hardware to validate the "inference cost proxy" framing — average bit-width is a coarse proxy.
- Evaluation is PTQ-only on ≤8B dense models; mixture-of-experts models (acknowledged as future work) and instruct-tuned chat evaluations are absent.
- Highest target bit-widths are not reliably hit, and per-model hyper-parameter retuning is sometimes needed (explicitly shown necessary for the 3B–8B scaling).
- No public code-release commitment stated in the paper body.

## Relevance

对关注 LLM 低比特量化部署（尤其是 MXFP/OCP 浮点格式与混合精度分配）的研究者，dMX 提供了一个可直接借鉴的可微框架，其「连续 β + 温度退火」思路和 down/v proj 敏感性结论对量化算法设计与硬件协同优化有较高参考价值；若你的兴趣在 PTQ、量化感知训练或旋转+量化联合优化，本文是 2026 年该方向值得精读的工作之一。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does dMX compare against integer-domain differentiable mixed-precision methods (BSQ, FracBits, HMQ) when all are ported to the same MX hardware and the same average-bit-width budget?
- Can the single-offset parameterization be extended to a richer format set (e.g., including MXFP8 E5M2, INT, or non-uniform MX variants) without losing the smooth-annealing property?
- What is the real end-to-end latency/throughput/memory delta on production MX-capable accelerators, beyond the average-bit-width proxy?
- How does the learned layer ranking (down proj / v proj most sensitive) align with Hessian-spectrum or outlier-structure rankings from HAWQ/SpinQuant, and is it stable across calibration seeds?
- Does the framework transfer cleanly to mixture-of-experts models, where per-expert sensitivity varies dramatically, and to instruction-tuned chat models evaluated on MT-Bench/AlpacaEval?
- Would a learned per-layer (rather than uniform) temperature schedule, or a budget-aware constrained optimization (Lagrangian) replacing the max-penalty, improve convergence to the target bit-width at the high end?
