> **一句话总结：** 作者将混合精度量化损失视为布尔立方上的集合函数并经 Walsh/Möbius 两种基变换分析，证明在 4-bit 权重+激活量化下 85–93% 的损失方差由一阶（逐层）效应解释，并提出一个由"饱和"机制驱动的、每层一个参数的 coverage 模型 f(S)=c(1−∏(1−a_i))；该模型作为内存受限的分配器在 Qwen3-30B 上比 NVIDIA 生产级 ModelOpt 基线的 KL 散度低 17–21%，并在 sub-4-bit 下让推理任务仍能正常终止。

> **TL;DR:** This paper treats 4-bit weight-and-activation quantization damage f(S) as a set function on the Boolean cube and analyzes it via the Walsh and Möbius transforms, finding that 85–93% of the damage variance is first-order (per-layer) and an additive index misranks at most 2% of configuration pairs. It explains both facts with a one-parameter-per-layer saturating "coverage model" f(S)=c(1−∏(1−a_i)) whose additive coefficient is recoverable by a single Aumann–Shapley path integral (proven under the model). As a memory-matched allocator on Qwen3-30B-A3B it reaches calibration KL of 0.0264 at eff-bits 5.0 versus ModelOpt's 0.0331 (a 17–21% reduction), holds the lowest KL on MoE models from 30B to 355B, and keeps reasoning tasks producing terminating generations at sub-4-bit budgets where gradient-sensitivity allocations collapse.

## 核心贡献

- **封闭形式的理论**：给出 coverage 模型的精确 Möbius 谱（定理3）与 Walsh 谱（定理4），并确认其符号展开的发散行为；建立"加性预测误差 = 阶数≥2 的 Walsh 能量"的恒等式（定理1），以及"单调变换下可加性是序性质"的刻画（定理2）。
- **带证明的负面结果**：在隔离状态下测得的逐层敏感度（包括 Hessian-trace 版本的 HAWQ-v2）会把配置损失高估 71–376%，论文用闭式因子解释了这一膨胀。
- **无需采样配置的估计器**：一次 Aumann–Shapley 路径积分即可恢复加性模型的最优系数（在某层特定的有效部署密度处）以及层排序，并在 coverage 模型假设下给出证明。
- **可解释、省测量的预测器**：coverage 与 additive 两个 L+1 参数模型在小测量预算下与 RF/GP/RBF/二阶交互等黑盒代理持平或更优，在配置外推时退化不超过 2×，而高斯过程代理退化 2–5×。
- **内存受限的最优分配器**：在 0.6B–355B 的稠密与 MoE 大模型上取得最低 KL 散度（Qwen3-30B 比 ModelOpt 低 17–21%，Qwen3-235B 低 13–27%，GLM-4.6 低 20–37%），并在 sub-4-bit 下决定服务任务能否产生终止的生成。

## Key Contributions

- **A closed-form theory of quantization damage as a set function.** The coverage model's exact Möbius spectrum (Theorem 3) and Walsh spectrum (Theorem 4) are derived, and the divergent signed expansion is empirically confirmed; the identity "additive predictor MSE = order-≥2 Walsh energy" (Theorem 1) and an ordinal characterization of additivity-up-to-a-monotone-transform (Theorem 2) are proven.
- **A negative result with a closed-form account.** Sensitivities measured in isolation — including a Hessian-trace HAWQ-v2 — mis-price a configuration's loss by 71–376%, and the responsible inflation factor is derived in closed form.
- **A fit-free estimator.** A single Aumann–Shapley path integral recovers the additive model's optimal coefficient at a layer-specific effective deployment density and its exact layer ranking, with no sampled configurations, proven under the coverage model.
- **An interpretable, measurement-efficient predictor.** The L+1-parameter coverage and additive models are best or tied against random-forest/Gaussian-process/RBF/pairwise baselines at small measurement budgets, degrade at most 2× under configuration extrapolation (GP degrades 2–5×), and are validated on LLMs from 0.6B to 355B plus a diffusion DiT.
- **A memory-matched allocator with the lowest KL among compared methods**, beating the production ModelOpt baseline by 17–21% on Qwen3-30B, 13–27% on Qwen3-235B, and 20–37% on GLM-4.6, and deciding served-task survival below four bits.

## Methodology

The paper reframes mixed-precision quantization allocation as a problem about a set function. For a contiguous block of L layers, the damage f(S) = ΔLCE is measured by actually quantizing exactly the layer set S and evaluating the partially quantized model (stochastic rounding averaged over 16 draws). Configurations are analyzed under the *deployment distribution* µ_p, where each layer is independently quantized with probability p=0.6.

Two classical changes of basis are applied to the measured f:

1. **Walsh / functional-ANOVA decomposition** splits Var(f) by order: W_k is the variance attributable to order-k (k-way) interactions. By Parseval, the best degree-≤1 predictor's squared error exactly equals the order-≥2 energy (Theorem 1). Empirically, order-1 carries 85–93% of the variance (98–99% after noise correction), and on exact 2^8 lattices over eight consecutive linear units at W4A4 the order-1 share is 96.9–99.7% on dense models from 0.6B to 72B and 90.7% on the sparse-MoE gpt-oss-120B.

2. **Möbius expansion** reconstructs f from signed terms; coverage predicts these coefficients are nonzero at every order and alternate in sign — exactly the divergent overshoot observed (up to 32× the true loss on the 10-layer Qwen3-8B lattice).

The unifying explanation is **saturation**: because the loss is bounded, each quantized layer consumes a fixed fraction a_i (its *break-rate*) of the remaining headroom toward a ceiling c. This yields the **coverage model**

  f(S) = c · (1 − ∏_{i∈S}(1 − a_i))

fitted by nonlinear least squares. A **certificate** — the order-≥2 share, forecast in closed form from the L+1 fitted parameters via the "saturation-depth law" (Proposition 5) — is reported with every result, telling the user in advance how well any additive model can do. The measured share tracks the fitted saturation depth τ at Spearman ρ=0.958, and the exact heterogeneous prediction matches the measured share at Pearson r=0.984 across 36 cells.

Two predictors are then deployed: the **additive model** (optimal first-order projection, MSE = order-≥2 energy by Theorem 1) and the **coverage model** itself, each with L+1 parameters fitted on N≈L sampled configurations. A single **Aumann–Shapley path integral** (the loss gradient averaged over increasingly quantized contexts) recovers the optimal additive coefficient at a layer-specific effective density without sampling configurations, proven to preserve the exact layer ranking under coverage. Used as a **memory-matched allocator**, minimizing predicted damage becomes a knapsack over per-demotion log-headroom costs.

## Results & Highlights

- **Damage variance is almost entirely first-order.** Across {Qwen3-0.6B, Qwen2.5-3B, Qwen3-4B, Qwen3-8B, Llama-3.2-3B, Qwen3-30B-A3B} × {wiki, code} at whole-layer FP4, the sampled order-1 share is 85–93%; on a 10-layer Qwen3-8B block it is 87.3% measured and 98.1% after noise correction; on exact 2^8 W4A4 lattices the share is 96.9–99.7% on dense models up to Qwen2.5-72B and 90.7% on gpt-oss-120B.
- **Isolated sensitivities mis-price loss by 71–376%.** Isolate-and-sum overstates the fully quantized corner by 71–346%; a Hessian-trace HAWQ-v2 overstates it by 89% (Qwen3-8B) and 376% (Qwen3-0.6B). Median isolate-and-sum relative error on held-out protection sets is 51% (Qwen3-8B) and 203% (Qwen3-30B-A3B), versus 2–3% for the fitted additive/coverage models.
- **Lowest KL divergence as a memory-matched allocator on MoE models.** On Qwen3-30B-A3B at eff-bits 5.0 the additive allocator reaches calibration KL 0.0264 versus ModelOpt's 0.0331 (≈20% lower); the in-context family leads at every measured budget, 17–21% below the production ModelOpt baseline overall, and the margin holds at 8× scale on Qwen3-235B (13–27%) and on GLM-4.6-355B (20–37%: AS-coverage KL 0.059/0.045/0.036 at eff 5/6/7 versus ModelOpt 0.088/0.071/0.045).
- **Sub-4-bit survival is decided by allocation.** On a Qwen3-30B GPTQ ladder the additive allocator's KL sits 3.0–6.6× below ModelOpt's at servable budgets (eff ≥ 2.75). Served HumanEval at eff 2.9 orders exactly by calibration KL (0.215, 0.228, 0.236, 1.042 → 73.2, 44.5, 38.4, 0.0); ModelOpt and MC-MoE collapse to 0.0 while additive stays at 73.2. On Qwen3-235B, the additive allocation preserves AIME-2025 reasoning at eff 3.5 (73.3) and stays functional at eff 2.9 (42.9) where uniform/ModelOpt collapse.
- **Out-of-distribution robustness.** Fit at p=0.6 and tested at p=0.85/0.35, coverage's error grows 1.3–2.0× and the additive model's 1.1–1.9×, while the Gaussian-process surrogate degrades 2.1–4.9× and the random forest 2.0–3.9× — stationary kernels do not encode saturation.
- **Matched-memory serving speedups.** On dense Qwen3.6-27B the additive configuration at 5.41 effective bits matches vendor NVFP4 checkpoints on AIME25 (90.0) / MMLU-Pro (86.5) while being fastest at every prompt/generation shape — 1.5–2.0× bf16 throughput and 8–24% above Unsloth's 6.73-bit checkpoint. On GLM-5.2 (753B) it reads 28.3 GB/token versus the checkpoint's 45.9, decoding 1.19× faster at matched accuracy.

The paper combines real systems-level serving results (vLLM/SGLang on B200, vendor NVFP4 checkpoints from NVIDIA and Unsloth) with rigorous Boolean-function-analysis theory, suggesting a strong theory-systems bridge.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: high and genuinely original.** Applying the Walsh/functional-ANOVA decomposition and additive-conjoint-measurement (Luce–Tukey, Scott) to the *measured* quantization-loss set function, then closing the loop with a saturating coverage model whose Möbius divergence is observed empirically, is a fresh and non-obvious framing. The Aumann–Shapley recovery of the in-context coefficient is a clean theoretical contribution.
- **Rigor: strong, with honest certificates.** The paper's signature feature is that every result is paired with a certificate (the order-≥2 share) stating how well any additive model could do, measured exactly on lattices and forecast in closed form at full-network scale. The split-half + matched-noise-null certification of the Möbius oscillation (Fig. 2) is a credible defense against the obvious "amplified noise" objection.
- **Reproducibility: moderate-to-good.** Calibration corpora, seeds (3-seed headline cells, std ≤ 0.03), quantizer settings, and serving stacks (vLLM/SGLang, B200, concurrency) are specified, and the predictor has only L+1 parameters fit on N≈L configurations. However, no code/release is mentioned, and the full measurement pipeline (lattice enumeration, nonlinear least-squares fit, Aumann–Shapley path integral) is non-trivial to re-implement.
- **Significance: practically meaningful but scoped.** The KL wins over ModelOpt (17–21% on 30B) are real, but the paper itself concedes that in the served ≥4-bit regime the margins "vanish, which is near-lossless by construction" — i.e., the headline allocation advantage matters mainly at contended budgets and below four bits. The serving results (Qwen3.6-27B at 5.41 bits matching AIME25 90.0) are compelling where activations are quantized at runtime.
- **Red flags / things to probe:**
  - **Weak baseline concern for allocation.** MC-MoE's per-expert form "cannot be loaded by the fused serving kernels, so the fusion column is its servable form," and CoopQ "scores whole layers rather than the 240 per-linear units of the other methods, the only granularity its measurement design affords" — so two named competitors are compared at a coarser/different granularity than the proposed method. This is disclosed but favors the additive allocator.
  - **AIME-2025 signal is weak.** Table 12 reports every method between 66 and 76 against a bf16 anchor of 73.3 with "no significant ordering at any budget" on Qwen3-30B — so the calibration-KL story is the load-bearing evidence, not end-task accuracy, in the ≥4-bit regime.
  - **The coverage model's scope is explicitly limited.** The certificate forecasts breakdown under per-tensor activation scales (order-≥2 share reaches 0.67–0.81 on the 0.6B lattice, where nonparametric surrogates win) and on the diffusion DiT (share 0.36, above every LLM cell). The method targets NVFP4 per-block formats; it is not a universal allocation recipe.
  - **Termination threshold rather than accuracy.** The sub-4-bit "survival" results hinge on whether a configuration still emits its end-of-sequence token (a termination boundary near 0.45 nats), not on accuracy gaps — and at eff 2.75 the KL–accuracy ordering breaks (uniform has lowest KL but scores 19.5 vs additive's 54.9), undercutting the clean "lower KL = better" narrative exactly at the budgets being advertised.

## Strengths

- Unifies two empirical facts (concentrated first-order variance + near-perfect additive ranking) under a single, intuitive saturation mechanism with closed-form Möbius/Walsh predictions that are then confirmed.
- Provides a *certificate* accompanying every result — a principled bound on how much any additive model can improve, which is rare and intellectually honest.
- The Aumann–Shapley fit-free estimator avoids the ~100L progressive-quantization evaluations required by CoopQ's SPQE, a genuine measurement-cost win.
- Real deployment validation: matched-memory serving against vendor NVFP4 checkpoints on B200 hardware, with throughput and end-task accuracy (AIME25, GPQA, MMLU-Pro, HumanEval), not just calibration KL.
- Strong scale coverage: 0.6B to 753B parameters, dense and MoE, LLMs and a diffusion transformer.

## Limitations

- **Applicability is format-dependent.** Saturation lives in the activation channel under per-block NVFP4 scaling; under coarse per-tensor activation scales the order-≥2 share explodes (up to 0.81) and the method breaks down, with nonparametric surrogates then winning.
- **High-order interactions exist where claimed scope ends.** The diffusion DiT (Qwen-Image-2512) has order-≥2 share 0.36, above every LLM cell, so the "90% first-order" story is an LLM-per-block-NVFP4 phenomenon, not a universal law.
- **Sub-4-bit results depend on an error-compensating base (GPTQ ladder).** Under round-to-nearest every allocator collapses regardless of scale, so the survival advantage is conditional on a specific base quantizer.
- **End-task signal is noisy in the ≥4-bit regime.** AIME-2025 shows no significant method ordering at any budget on Qwen3-30B; the load-bearing metric there is calibration KL.
- **Single-author work, no public code mentioned**, making the non-trivial measurement pipeline (lattice enumeration, nonlinear fits, path integral) hard to reproduce independently.
- **The "data randomness in the theory" is unmodeled** — f(S) is treated as deterministic but is an expectation over the calibration distribution; a two-source variance decomposition is left as future work.

## Relevance

对关注大模型量化、混合精度分配与高效推理服务的研究者高度相关：它从布尔函数分析的角度给出了量化损失"近似可加"的理论根据与可证明的误差证书，并提供了在生产级 ModelOpt 之上、在内存受限且预算紧张时切实降低 KL 并守住 sub-4-bit 推理终止能力的分配器，直接服务于 4-bit/低比特 LLM 部署这一核心场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the coverage model's saturation law hold for activation quantization formats beyond NVFP4 (e.g., INT4 block-wise, FP6, MX formats), or is the 85–93% first-order share specific to per-block-16 floating point?
- How does the certificate (order-≥2 share) behave under rotation-based pre-processing (QuaRot, SpinQuant, QuIP#)? The paper states the framework composes with such bases — does composing lower the share enough to make surrogates unnecessary even in the diffusion-DiT regime (share 0.36)?
- Can the Aumann–Shapley fit-free estimator be made calibration-data-aware, incorporating the data-sampling variance that the paper explicitly leaves unmodeled, to produce a data-aware certificate tied to calibration-set size?
- The termination-threshold failure mode (EOS-token emission stopping near 0.45 nats) is discovered but not predicted — can the coverage model forecast the threshold from break-rates, turning "survival" into a priced quantity?
- At sub-4-bit budgets the derivation corpus (not the scoring rule) sets the usable budget on Qwen3-235B (58.3 vs 39.6 AIME at eff 3.0). How sensitive is this to corpus choice, and is there a principled corpus-selection rule under the coverage framework?
