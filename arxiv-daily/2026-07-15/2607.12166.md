> **一句话总结：** 在已知 ground truth 的玩具模型中复现超叠加/SAE 经典实验，发现即使 cosine 相似度高达 0.9998 的"恢复"特征中也有最高 77%（退化 SAE）和 9%（良好 SAE）在因果上是惰性的——并据此提炼出结构性 vs 竞争性惰性、读惰性 vs 写惰性的双轴分类法，封装为可复现的 `sae-causal-audit` 工具。

> **TL;DR:** On a controlled toy superposition model where ground truth is known, the author reproduces the Elhage et al. (2022) superposition phase diagram and the Gao et al. (2024) TopK-vs-L1 SAE comparison from scratch (confirming L1 shrinkage: magnitude refinement closes 91.2% of L1's reconstruction gap with a +22.5% magnitude shift vs -0.05% for TopK), then subjects every correlationally recovered feature to ablation and sign-correct steering. The result: up to 77% of features passing the standard cosine≥0.90 recovery bar in a degraded TopK k=13 SAE (and 9% in a well-trained TopK k=4 SAE with precision 1.00) are causally inert, including matches at cosine≈1.000. The methodology is packaged as `sae-causal-audit` with a hash-verified deterministic pipeline, and a first census on GPT-2-small (`gpt2-small-res-jb`, 83 concepts, one hook layer) finds 14% inert among the 7 recovered features plus a replicated atom-collision signal (atom 14149 nearest-matches 8 unrelated concepts).

## 核心贡献

- **从复现到因果审计**：在已知 ground truth 的玩具模型上从零复现 Elhage 2022 超叠加相图与 Gao 2024 的 TopK-vs-L1 比较，并把所有"相关性恢复"的特征都跑一遍 ablation + 符号校正的 steering，把"恢复"从几何对齐提升为因果验证。
- **几何恢复 ≠ 因果有效**：即使 cosine 高达 0.9998，退化 SAE 中 77%（17/22）、良好 SAE 中 9%（2/22）的恢复特征实际上"从不触发"（fired_frac=0）——这是因果惰性，不是噪声。
- **惰性的双轴分类**：按成因分为结构性惰性（antipodal pair 超叠加 + positive-pass encoder 系统性产生，良好 SAE 也有）与竞争性惰性（TopK 选择病态，退化 SAE 独有，同时也是数值边界不稳定源头）；按方向分为读惰性（ablation 不可见）与写惰性（steering 无效），5 对 antipodal 完全 dissociate——读惰性同时写特异性高达 143–310。
- **可复现工具 `sae-causal-audit`**：通过运行时协议接受任何 SAE（含 sae_lens.SAE），37 个测试 + Hypothesis property-based tests + 哈希校验 CI gate；并把"可复现性"重新定义为分层 claim（SHA-256 单环境字节精确 + rtol=1e-4 跨平台语义等价）。
- **真实模型首次普查**：对 GPT-2-small（blocks.8.hook_resid_pre, 83 个手工撰写概念）跑出 7 个恢复特征中 1 个（14%）因果惰性；并发现 atom collision（atom 14149 为 8 个无关概念的最近匹配，跨 3 个独立概念批次复现）。
- **方法学诊断**：抓到并报告 4 个 bug（含一个 sign-convention bug 会把反方向 steering 静默读成 0），把"cosine 是个只适合好天气的仪器"这一结论量化——退化 SAE 上 r=0.471 vs 良好 SAE 上 r=0.859。

## Key Contributions

- **From reproduction to causal audit.** Multi-seed from-scratch reproductions of the Elhage et al. (2022) superposition phase diagram and the Gao et al. (2024) TopK-vs-L1 Pareto comparison, plus a direct activation-magnitude refinement test that quantitatively confirms L1 (Lasso) shrinkage rather than asserting it.
- **Two new toy-regime findings.** A documented convergence artifact at high sparsity (under-training masquerading as superposition, fixed by sparsity-aware step budgets) and a previously under-described "diffuse sharing" regime at extreme overcompleteness (ratio 8, s=0.99: Di≈0.08 but ‖Wi‖₂≈1.40, recon loss 0.136±0.009) that the dedicated/antipodal/dropped trichotomy misses when ‖Wi‖₂ is not checked.
- **Causal gap in correlational recovery.** Recovery-precision 1.00 does not imply causal efficacy: 17/22 (77%) of cosine≥0.90 matches in the degraded TopK k=13 SAE and 2/22 (9%) in the well-trained TopK k=4 SAE are causally inert (fired_frac=0), with inert matches at cosine up to 0.9998 in the good SAE.
- **Two-axis taxonomy of inertness.** By cause: structural (antipodal-pair geometry + positive-pass encoder, present even in good SAEs) vs competitive (TopK-selection pathology of degraded SAEs, coincident with their numerical boundary instability). By direction: read-inert (ablation blind) vs write-inert (steering impotent), which all 5 antipodal pairs dissociate completely — ablation effect exactly 0 with steering specificity 143–310.
- **`sae-causal-audit` instrument.** Model-agnostic, protocol-typed (any object exposing `W_dec`, `encode`, `decode` — including `sae_lens.SAE`), with a sign-handling bug class made unrepresentable at the interface level, 37 tests, and a two-tier reproducibility guarantee (SHA-256 byte-exact in the pinned CI env; rtol=10⁻⁴ + ±1 boundary-sensitive counts everywhere).
- **First real-model census and atom-collision signal.** Auditing `gpt2-small-res-jb/blocks.8.hook_resid_pre` over 83 hand-authored concepts: 7/83 cleared cosine≥0.5; 1/7 (14%) causally inert. Atom 14149 nearest-matches 8 semantically disjoint concepts, replicated across three independently constructed concept batches of growing size.

## Methodology

**Toy model (Section 3).** A from-scratch ReLU autoencoder `h=Wx, x̂=ReLU(W⊤h+b)` with `W∈ℝ^(n_hidden×n_features)`, `n_hidden<n_features`, sparse inputs (each feature zero w.p. s, else Uniform(0,1)), trained with importance-weighted MSE. Two importance schedules are kept strictly separate: *decayed* (`I_i=0.9^i`, used for all SAE experiments, "32→8 at s=0.95") and *uniform* (`I_i=1`, used only for the phase diagram).

**Phase diagram (Section 4).** Fixed `n_hidden=20`, ratio ∈ {1,2,4,8}, sparsity s ∈ {0,...,0.99}, 3 seeds/cell. Each feature classified by dimensionality `D_i = ‖W_i‖² / Σ_j (W_i·W_j)²`: dedicated (>0.9), superposed (0.1–0.9), or "candidate not represented" which is then split via ‖W_i‖₂ into truly dropped (<0.1) vs diffusely shared (≥0.1). This ‖W_i‖₂ check is the move that surfaces diffuse sharing.

**SAE comparison (Section 5).** L1 (`λ‖f‖₁`) and TopK (keep k largest ReLU'd pre-activations, k IS the L0) trained on the toy model's hidden activations, 16×-overcomplete dictionaries, 3 seeds. Recovery precision counts ground-truth features whose best decoder match has unsigned cosine≥0.90. The shrinkage test freezes each SAE's support on a held-out batch and re-optimizes only the magnitudes (projected GD, non-negativity) to see how much reconstruction gap closes and in which direction magnitudes move.

**Causal battery (Sections 6, 7, 10).** For every matched feature–atom pair, propagated through the model's *actual* output stage `x̂=ReLU(ĥW+b)`:
1. **fired_frac** — fraction of feature-present samples where the atom's code is nonzero (the cheap screen that alone explains all 17 originally-inert cases).
2. **Ablation specificity** (read direction) — targeted drop in the feature's reconstructed output divided by mean off-target movement; ∞ for a perfectly surgical ablation.
3. **Sign-correct steering specificity** (write direction) — with the matched *sign* as a required argument, force the atom on feature-off inputs and measure the targeted rise.

The matcher is deliberately greedy per-feature argmax (not bipartite) so atom collisions surface as findings.

**Real-model harness (Section 11).** Same pipeline pointed at published SAEs via SAELens + TransformerLens, with two explicit substitutions: ground truth → difference-in-means probe direction over 8-pos/8-neg prompt sets, and toy readout → logits spliced back into the residual stream at the hook point.

**Reproducibility stack (Section 8).** Single-threaded, `foreach=False` Adam, deterministic algorithms, fixed seeds; result files are SHA-256 hashed and verified in CI. Two-tier guarantee: byte-exact within the pinned `ubuntu-24.04 / torch==2.13.0+cpu` env, semantic equality (rtol=10⁻⁴, counts within ±1) elsewhere.

## Results & Highlights

- **TopK dominates the Pareto front and shrinkage is directly measured (Table 3).** Support-frozen magnitude refinement at matched L0≈4 closes **91.2%** of the L1 SAE's reconstruction gap while shifting mean active magnitudes **+22.5%**; the same refinement on TopK closes 64.8% but moves magnitudes only **−0.05%** (indistinguishable from zero) — direct quantitative evidence that the L1 penalty systematically biases reported activation strengths low.
- **Recovery precision at matched L0≈4 (Table 2).** TopK k=4 reaches **1.00±0.00** recovery precision on the 22 well-represented features versus **0.77±0.11** for L1 (λ=0.1) — every represented feature recovered in every seed.
- **Causal inertness rate (Tables 4, 5).** Of features passing cosine≥0.90 in the degraded TopK k=13 SAE, **17/22 (77%)** are causally inert (originally, multi-threaded run); in the well-trained TopK k=4 SAE (precision 1.00), **2/22 (9%)** are inert. The deterministic re-audit gives 3/18 (17%) for k=13 and 2/22 (9%) for k=4.
- **Inert matches are not marginal (Section 9).** Bad SAE inert cosines: 0.986, 0.994, 0.9995. Good SAE inert cosines: 0.9997, 0.9998 — geometrically perfect yet fired_frac=0 across 500 samples.
- **Cosine is a fair-weather instrument.** Correlation between cosine and log ablation specificity over *firing* pairs: **r=0.859 (n=21)** for the good SAE vs **r=0.471 (n=18)** for the bad SAE — the proxy degrades precisely where an audit is most needed.
- **Diffuse-sharing regime (Section 4.4).** At ratio 8, s=0.99 (160 features into 20 dims): every feature reports D_i≈0.08 yet **‖W_i‖₂≈1.40** (larger than a clean orthogonal feature's norm of 1.0), reconstruction loss 0.136±0.009 — a third geometric strategy the dedicated/antipodal/dropped trichotomy does not name.
- **Read/write dissociation (Section 10.2).** Five antipodal pairs across both SAEs have ablation specificity exactly 0 (read-inert) yet steering specificities of **143, 203, 261, 310** (and 143–261 in the bad SAE's three cases) — the same atom is unmonitorable yet highly steerable.
- **GPT-2-small census (Section 11.1).** On `gpt2-small-res-jb/blocks.8.hook_resid_pre`, 83 concepts, cosine bar lowered to ≥0.5: 7/83 recovered, **1/7 (14%)** causally inert; median ablation specificity 1.63 (95% CI [0.05, 5.34]), median steering specificity 2.23 (95% CI [0.69, 3.54]).
- **Atom collisions replicate in production (Section 11.2).** Atom 14149 is the nearest match for 8 of 83 concepts (astronomy, cryptography, law, …), replicated across three independently constructed concept batches (n=33, 48, 83); atoms 4504 and 17413 each match 5, atom 17241 matches 4.
- **Reproducibility is not one number.** Byte-exact SHA-256 reproduction held across three independent CI runs of one commit; cross-platform (Windows vs Linux) reproduction is shown to be unavailable *by construction* (different BLAS/MSVC-vs-GCC binaries), motivating the two-tier guarantee.

## Authors & Credibility

Single author: **Mohamed Abdessalem Bal**, independent researcher in Algiers, Algeria (no institutional affiliation). Credibility rests on (a) full open-sourcing of both code repositories (`superposition-to-monosemanticity` for toy reproduction, `sae-causal-audit` for the instrument), (b) a 37-test suite including hand-derivable unit cases and Hypothesis property-based tests, (c) a hash-verified deterministic CI gate, and (d) an unusually frank bug log: four bugs are caught and reported in the paper itself (sign-convention steering bug, serialization leak of `runtime_seconds` into hashed JSON, Adam `foreach=True` fused path, and cross-platform BLAS divergence). The author's stated epistemic standard — "looked wrong, checked why, fixed it" — is applied to his own numbers, including an impossible 82% superposition result at capacity-sufficient ratio=1 that he traced to under-training. The work is calibration-grade on the toy setting and explicitly does not claim production transferability.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The correlational-vs-causal gap in SAE feature recovery is *not* a new idea — Chanin et al.'s "feature absorption" (arXiv:2409.14507) documents latents failing to fire on present concepts on production LLM SAEs, and SAEBench (arXiv:2503.09532) shows proxy-metric gains do not transfer across 200+ SAEs. The paper's genuine novelty is (i) doing the audit *interventionally end-to-end with exact ground truth* in the toy regime, exposing the read/write dissociation that firing statistics alone cannot see; (ii) the structural-vs-competitive / read-vs-write taxonomy tied to specific mechanisms; and (iii) the packaging of the battery as a typed, tested, hash-verified instrument rather than experiment scripts.
- **Rigor.** Strong on the toy side: multi-seed, bootstrap intervals, unit tests asserting exact values (∞, 0.0, 0.5, 1.0), property-based tests, an honest ±1 tolerance band on the boundary-sensitive k=13 census. The reproducibility stack discussion (three-layer debugging of cross-platform nondeterminism) is more rigorous than typical ML papers.
- **Reproducibility.** Excellent in principle: `make reproduce` in ~3 minutes on CPU, hash-verified CI, open code. The author is admirably explicit that byte-exact cross-platform reproducibility is *unavailable by construction* and proposes the two-tier stack as the honest fix. The one weakness is that the deterministic pipeline converges to *different weights* than the original multi-threaded runs, so headline counts moved (77%→17% for the bad SAE) even though the qualitative claim held — the author frames this as a robustness check, which is fair, but it does mean the 77% figure in the abstract is from the *original* non-deterministic run while the reference deterministic environment gives 17%.
- **Significance.** Moderate-to-high for interpretability methodology. The read/write dissociation (atoms that are ablation-dead but steering-specificity-310) is a genuinely useful conceptual contribution for anyone building monitoring or steering on top of SAEs. The practical guidance (Section 12) — never gate an automated action on raw SAE activation; validate per direction of use; re-validate on every retrain; version-pin checkpoints — is concrete and defensible.
- **Red flags / overclaiming.** (1) The headline 77% number in the abstract comes from a configuration the author later calls a "noise amplifier" with explicitly unstable integer counts (±1 band, flips between Linux and Windows); the deterministic reference run reports 17%, and the author does not foreground this enough. (2) The GPT-2-small "first census" is genuinely small: 7 recovered features from 83 concepts at a *lowered* cosine bar of ≥0.5 (vs 0.90 in the toy regime), with 1/7 = 14% — a single inert feature; the wide bootstrap intervals ([0.05, 5.34] for ablation specificity) and the author's own caveat that the probe-direction proxy makes the two regimes non-comparable limit how much weight this carries. (3) Sample sizes in the toy regime are small (22 well-represented features, 2 SAE configurations, 1 seed under the deterministic pipeline); the read/write decomposition rests on 5 antipodal pairs within one run. (4) The "diffuse sharing" regime is described but left mechanistically unresolved ("whether this many-way diffuse packing is a documented phase or an under-described corner is left open").

## Strengths

- Causal validation performed end-to-end through the model's actual output stage rather than proxy activations — the strongest form of interventional test available in this setting.
- The two-axis taxonomy (structural vs competitive; read vs write) is mechanistically grounded and gives actionable diagnoses (structural inertness is screenable from the dictionary alone via greedy-argmax collisions; competitive inertness needs the fired_frac screen).
- Interface-level bug prevention: the sign-convention failure is made *unrepresentable* (`MatchResult.sign` as a separate field, `steering_effect` requires sign argument) rather than merely patched — a permanent regression test encodes the original failure.
- Exceptional honesty about limitations and failures: four bugs are reported, the cross-platform reproducibility ceiling is documented rather than hidden, and the abstract's 77% number is explicitly tied to a configuration the author flags as numerically unstable.
- Open, tested, deterministic tooling that other researchers can actually run on `sae_lens.SAE` objects with no adapter code.

## Limitations

- All headline causal numbers come from small synthetic toy models (≤32 features, ≤8 hidden dims); the author repeatedly and correctly stresses that every absolute number is "a calibration point for the easy case, not a benchmark for a production model."
- The real-model census is a single SAE (`gpt2-small-res-jb`), a single hook layer (blocks.8.hook_resid_pre), 83 concepts with only 8 pos/8 neg prompts each, and a lowered cosine bar of 0.5; only 7 features cleared recovery. Atom-collision disambiguation (antipodal geometry vs proxy-direction artifact vs genuine polysemantic hub) is explicitly unresolved.
- The causal battery tests features in isolation on the ON side; multi-feature interaction effects are untested.
- The read/write dissociation rests on 5 antipodal pairs within one toy run and one seed (under the deterministic pipeline); cross-seed rates are not reported for the decomposition.
- The original 77% headline comes from non-deterministic multi-threaded runs; the deterministic reference environment reports 17% (3/18) — the qualitative claim holds but the magnitude is environment-dependent.
- The "diffuse sharing" regime is observed but not theoretically explained or replicated at other ratios.

## Relevance

对研究机制可解释性与 SAE 评测方法直接相关：本文为"SAE feature 是否真的对应模型使用的特征"提供了可复现的因果审计工具与定量证据，对任何用 SAE 做监控、steering 或对齐审计的研究都是关键参考。读/写惰性 dissociation 与 atom collision 现象对生产级 SAE 的可信度评估尤其有指导价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the read/write dissociation (ablation-dead but steering-specificity-310) generalize beyond antipodal pairs — e.g., to polysemantic hub features in production LLM SAEs where multiple unrelated concepts collide on one atom?
- What is the inertness rate on larger production SAEs (Gemma Scope, Claude-scale dictionaries) at *multiple* hook layers and at the field-standard cosine≥0.90 bar, rather than the lowered 0.5 used here?
- Can the "diffuse sharing" regime at ratio 8, s=0.99 be theoretically characterized — is it a known phase of the Thomson-problem-like packing, or genuinely new geometry?
- How do Gated SAEs and BatchTopK SAEs fare under the same causal battery — do they avoid competitive inertness, or merely relocate it?
- The author notes that the deterministic pipeline converges to *different weights* than the multi-threaded one and that absolute counts moved (77%→17%). Is there a principled way to report a distribution of inertness rates across admissible deterministic configurations rather than a single environment's number?
- Under actual optimization pressure (e.g., a model fine-tuned to resist interpretability probing), how much larger does the "geometrically plausible, causally misleading" solution space become — and can the fired_frac screen detect it?
