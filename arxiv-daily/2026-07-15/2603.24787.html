Methodology & Diagnosis

The Failure Mode

AUC collapse: text-only → multimodal
Even on caption-converted twins, adding the image degrades routing — so it's the representation, not the probe.

Confirmed via a classifier-free HSIC/CKA intervention: caption inputs expose the strongest dependence between the last-token state and the correctness label; raw multimodal is weaker; blank images weakest. The signal is dispersed, not absent.

Two Complementary Fixes

  • Attention Probe. Replaces the implicit next-token aggregation with a learned token-level attention pool — one shared query q over the previous layer's states. Backbone frozen; only (q, φ) trained. Recovers distributed cues the final token dilutes.
  • ReLope (LoRA + VIB). Inserts LoRA (rank 128, α 256) at layer l to reshape states; maps the adapted last-token feature through two heads to (μ, log σ²), samples a bottleneck z_B, and trains the probe on it with BCE + KL-to-N(0,I).
  • Why it works. LoRA restores separability; VIB suppresses task-irrelevant visual variation. Ablation ties each component to its specific failure mode.
  • Setup. Layer l=24 for 32-layer models, l=40 for Gemma3-12B; rule-based correctness labels (no judge model); escalation backend GPT-4.1.

Efficiency, Robustness & Stability

  • Latency. 97.6 → 99.0 ms/sample (+1.4 ms = +1.4%); memory 16.89 → 17.00 GB (+0.11 GB). Backbone forward dominates.
  • Ablation (A-OKVQA). Probe 82.03 → LoRA-only 85.12 → VIB-only 84.45 → ReLope 86.17. Both components contribute; combo best.
  • Perturbation robustness. Mean ΔAUC under {noise, JPEG, blur}: Probe 5.30, Attention Probe 2.69, ReLope 1.44.
  • Stability. MMMU across 3 seeds: ReLope 80.79 ± 0.40 vs Probe 76.61 ± 0.20.
  • OOD. Leave-one-dataset-out: best probe method, top on 4/5 targets — except MathVision (see Critical Edge).
03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Classifier-free HSIC/CKA diagnosis isolates representation quality from probe-fitting artifacts.
  • Two methods, each tied to a specific failure mode via clean component ablation.
  • Wins every cell of a 3-backbone × 5-benchmark table; gains up to +9.48 pp avg.
  • Negligible latency overhead and the strongest robustness to image perturbations.
  • Honest reporting: MathVision OOD weakness, parameter overhead, and label dependence disclosed.

Limitations

  • Learned routers (incl. ReLope) lose to non-learned MoT under MathVision domain shift — generalization to math-vision reasoning is open.
  • Only 7–12B backbones tested; the "on-device" framing is not validated on genuinely small (<4B) models.
  • Requires supervised correctness labels and 29.54M extra trainable params (≈30× the Probe).
  • Hybrid-system cost is proxied by routing ratio, not measured in dollars or end-to-end latency.
  • No deep mechanistic explanation of why visual tokens dilute the correctness signal.
04

Open Questions

1

Does ReLope transfer to genuinely small (<4B) on-device MLLMs, and does the 29.54M / 0.11 GB overhead remain acceptable there?

2

Can the MathVision OOD gap be closed — e.g. by routing-specific pretraining, or by combining ReLope with the consistency-based MoT signal it currently loses to?

3

Which layers/tokens carry the diluted signal — and is the dilution an artifact of vision-token positional encoding or attention dilution across long visual prefixes?

4

Can ReLope be trained without supervised correctness labels (self-consistency pseudo-labels, unsupervised VIB on answer entropy)?
