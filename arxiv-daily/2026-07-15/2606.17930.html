How Inference Compute Shapes Frontier LLM Evaluation

Jessica McFadyen · Ole Jorgensen · Harry Coppock · Kevin Wei · Cozmin Ududec
UK AI Security Institute · Univ. of Oxford · Harvard

7 benchmarks · 6–12 frontier models · 3 generations · May 2025–Mar 2026 arxiv.org/abs/2606.17930 ↗
Key finding

Benchmark scores are protocol-dependent, not intrinsic — capability must be reported as a curve over inference compute.

Key metric
+11.7pp

FrontierMath mean uplift, pooled across models, scaling tokens 1M → 10M. HLE rises +11.9 pp (64k→5M); HealthBench near-flat at +0.32 pts.

Why this matters

Single-number scores hide compute sensitivity — for safety & policy comparisons, capability should be plotted as a curve at matched budgets.

Critical edge

FrontierMath's headline +11.7 pp rests on only 12 tasks, with flagged possible train/test overlap — suggestive, not definitive.

01

Methodology

  • Models Six frontier models, two families, three generations: Anthropic Opus 4 → 4.5 → 4.6, OpenAI GPT-5 → 5.2 → 5.4. Cyber suites add up to 12 (incl. o3, Sonnet 4.5, Codex variants, Mythos Preview). Run at high reasoning effort with 16,000 reasoning-token budget — HLE's reported peak.
  • Benchmarks 5 main + 2 cyber: TerminalBench 2.0 (86), SWE-Bench Pro (100), FrontierMath (12), HealthBench-Hard (100), HLE (100), plus Cyber CTFs (71) & The Last Ones (1 range). Per-trajectory caps 5M–100M tokens.
  • Scaffold Unified ReAct via Inspect AI; context compaction (summary-based) triggers above 130k tokens (~65% of the smallest 200k window).
  • Feedback Two conditions: no feedback (ambiguous acknowledgement) vs oracle score (correct/incorrect told; HealthBench gives partial credit). Trajectory ends on first correct submission.
  • Replicates 5 trajectories × 2 conditions per cell; scoring programmatic for stateful tasks, GPT-4o-mini @ t=0 LLM-judge for HLE & HealthBench. 90-min per-call timeout; affected runs re-run.
  • Analysis Cumulative scaling curves S_agg(t); plateau slope g_cap (weak growth < 1 pp / 10×); task-level regressions for reach / efficiency / reliability; parallel = pass@k, k∈{1,2,5,10}.

Three simple inference-scaling levers

  1. Larger budgets — 5M–30M tokens per trajectory, 1–3 orders of magnitude above typical published defaults.
  2. Context compaction — Inspect AI's default summary strategy, keeping the agent inside its context window on long runs.
  3. Repeated submission — up to 999 attempts, with a lightweight LLM-judge guard halting after 3+ semantically-equivalent submissions.

Deliberately a lower bound on what simple, general, reproducible methods elicit — not maximal elicitation.

03

Strengths & Limitations

● Strengths

  • Genuinely cross-domain and cross-generational under one controlled protocol — rare and valuable.
  • Honest framing as a lower bound; weak scaling is a property of the protocol, not proof of a capability ceiling.
  • The reach / efficiency / reliability decomposition is a clean, interpretable lens future eval papers should adopt.
  • Per-benchmark Table 3 shows exactly where typical budgets understate capability (FM, HLE) vs where they do not (HB, SBP).
  • Policy-facing recommendations are well-grounded in the data and appropriately cautious.

▲ Limitations

  • One ReAct scaffold — benchmark-level differences can't be separated from scaffold mismatch; cross-benchmark comparisons are weakened.
  • FrontierMath = 12 tasks — low power; possible train/test overlap for newer models inflates the headline +11.7 pp.
  • Deliberately simple — no adaptive branching, verifier-guided search, or hybrid serial/parallel: a conservative lower bound, not an upper bound.
  • LLM-judge dependence (HLE/HealthBench grading, repetition guard) adds non-uniform strictness across domains.
  • Six main models make generational-trend correlations (Kendall τ) descriptive only.
  • No public raw trajectories/prompts; proprietary frontier models block independent reproduction.
04

Open questions

  • 1Does HealthBench's saturation survive a non-ReAct scaffold (verifier-guided search, tree-of-thought, adaptive branching) — a property of the task, or of ReAct?
  • 2Would a larger FrontierMath set (100+ held-out with overlap controls) preserve the +11.7 pp gain and the strong reach coefficient β=+0.631?
  • 3Can reach/efficiency/reliability become a predictive diagnostic — forecasting inference-scaling headroom before running expensive evals?
  • 4What is the minimum-feedback signal (cheaper than full oracle) that captures most of the HLE / SWE-Bench Pro gain?
  • 5As newer models parallelize worse, are fixed-budget single-trajectory evals becoming more representative — or is headroom shifting entirely to serial depth?
  • 6How transferable are these protocol recommendations to open-ended agentic tasks with no oracle verifier (real SWE, autonomous research)?
