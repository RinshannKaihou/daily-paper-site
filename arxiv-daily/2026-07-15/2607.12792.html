◆ Key Finding

JADR reads a model's internal danger recognition in a single forward pass — before any reply token is generated — catching safety erosion that behavior metrics miss.

Why This Matters

A judge-free, single-pass internal probe that catches safety erosion in quantized and fine-tuned checkpoints before harm rates ever rise.

Use case: cheap pre-deployment screen after quant / RLHF.
Critical Edge

No adaptive-attack evaluation. The protocol's central advantage over ASR — catching fragile defenses — is never directly demonstrated, only argued on static StrongREJECT / XSTest sets.

Also: 6 models, 2 families; SafeRL harm@0.5 partly a grader artifact.
01 / METHOD

Reading what the model holds ready to say.

STEP 01

Jacobian lens

Per layer compute J_ℓ from N=100 calibration prompts (Gurnee 2026 recipe). Read lens_ℓ(h) = softmax(W_U · norm(J_ℓ h)) — content held ready, not a next-token prediction.

STEP 02

Decision point

All metrics read at rel_pos = −1: the last token before the first response token. Causally prompt-only — no decoding, no judge.

STEP 03

Frozen 6-axis lexicon

Multilingual (EN/ZH/RU) stems split into safety / compliance / evasion / softening / hedging / harm. Count + rank-weighted DCG per axis.

STEP 04

Rank AUC

AUC = Pr(c_danger > c_safe) via normalized Mann–Whitney. SafetyAUC at fp is the primary criterion. 0.5 = chance.

STEP 05

Cross-modification diagnostics

ΔAUC, Dlog ratio, Safety Headroom ΔSH (folds safety + compliance), and a Jaccard index for layer relocation.

STEP 06

Behavioral validation

Independent of internal metrics: StrongREJECT Gemma-2B grader @0.5 → harm@0.5, plus regex refusal + over-refusal rate.

Danger set: 313 StrongREJECT Safe control: 320 = XSTest-safe 250 + OKTest 70 Models: Gemma2-9B, Qwen3-1.7B/4B/8B, Ablit-4B, SafeRL-4B Weights: BF16 · INT8 · INT4 (NF4+double-quant)

03 / ASSESSMENT

Strengths against limitations.

Strengths

  • Conceptually clean. "Measure what the model holds ready to say before it says anything" — entirely local, zero judge call.
  • Dimensionless rank AUC makes cross-model, cross-tokenizer comparison meaningful — a real pain point in jailbreak-eval literature.
  • Recognition-vs-behavior distinction + the ΔSH metric that operationalizes it is a substantive, deployable diagnostic.
  • Honest about limits: §8 explicitly states what AUC / Jaccard cannot prove, and flags the SafeRL / grader confound.
  • Ablit-4B calibration pole gives a striking sanity check — 0.593 ≈ chance exactly where expected.
  • Reproducible: frozen lexicon, deterministic decoding, NF4 via bitsandbytes, released workspace-token artifacts.

Limitations

  • Single-token frozen lexicon systematically underestimates non-literal or multi-token expressions of danger.
  • Causal layer claims need intervention; current results are correlational, yet the paper still leans on layer-level narrative.
  • Narrow coverage: 2 families, 6 checkpoints, 3 weight regimes; transfer requires re-tuning k and important layers.
  • Wide CIs on auxiliary axes (evasion, hedging) — e.g. Ablit-4B evasion 0.304 [0.052, 0.700] — weaken per-axis quantization stories.
  • No adaptive / held-out attack eval — the central advantage over ASR is asserted, not demonstrated.
  • SafeRL-4B numbers entangled with StrongREJECT grader's bias against templated soft refusals.
04 / OPEN

Questions worth chasing next.

Q · ADAPTIVE

Does SafetyAUC predict robustness under adaptive attacks (GCG, PAIR) held out from StrongREJECT?

Q · SENSITIVITY

How stable is SafetyAUC to k=100, to the N=100 calibration size, and to lexicon extensions?

Q · CAUSAL

Does activation patching at AUC-important layers match the causally-validated layer set? (Paper hints it does not.)

Q · TRANSFER

Does the protocol hold beyond Qwen3 / Gemma2 — to Llama, Mistral, MoE — and does compliance stabilize?

Q · GRADER

Would a refusal-agnostic harm grader corroborate SafeRL-4B's 0.345, or is it largely a StrongREJECT artifact?

Q · FINE-TUNE

Can JADR detect safety regressions from fine-tuning, not just quantization — the stated but untested motivation?
