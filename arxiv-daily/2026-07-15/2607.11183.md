> **一句话总结：** 作者提出 Amplitude Gating (AG)，一种在推理时只调制 SwiGLU FFN 激活幅度而保持预训练权重方向不变的非破坏性干预方法，配合一个区分 oracle 上界与可部署 gate 的严格评测协议，在 Qwen3.5-9B 工具/函数调用任务上获得 +4.27 pp 提升（38.66% → 42.92%），在 Qwen3-8B 的 Hermes JSON 任务上获得 +11.36 pp 提升，并把 Qwen2.5-7B 作为"有 oracle 余量但无可部署 gate"的边界案例。

> **TL;DR:** The paper proposes Amplitude Gating (AG), a non-destructive inference-time intervention that masks/shrinks SwiGLU FFN activation magnitudes while leaving pretrained FFN weight directions untouched, paired with a strict four-level evaluation protocol (combination oracle / best fixed config / learned gate / diagnostic-only) that resists overclaiming. On Qwen3.5-9B, a category-level learned gate lifts tool/structured/agentic tasks from 38.66% to 42.92% (+4.27 pp), with Hermes function-call at about +7.6 pp; on Qwen3-8B, Hermes JSON mode jumps from 41.83% to 53.19% (+11.36 pp); Qwen2.5-7B is a deliberate boundary case where +4.03 pp oracle headroom exists but no category-level learned gate captures it. The honest message: AG is a model- and category-routed candidate-generation mechanism, not a universal accuracy booster, and it is strongest on multi-token tool/JSON trajectories rather than one-token multiple-choice.

## 核心贡献

- **从破坏性方法转向非破坏性方法。** 早期尝试 ORP（Orthogonal Residual Projection，基于 SVD 改变 FFN 残差方向）被证伪为不可部署：在 GSM8K 上 SVD-ORP 修复了 34 个错误却损害了 56 个正确样本（净 -22），且 1012/1032 条生成偏离基线 token 路径。论文将这一负面结果提炼为"预训练 FFN 方向是语义结构、不应被旋转覆盖"的设计原则，进而提出 Amplitude Gating（AG）——只调整激活幅度、不动权重矩阵。
- **为 SwiGLU FFN 定义六点干预分类法。** 沿用 ORP 的 P1/P2/P3（FFN 输入、内部融合、输出），并依据 SwiGLU 的 gate/value 分支结构新引入 P1s（gate-branch 输入）、P2a（SiLU 后融合前）、P2b（up_proj 后融合前），区分了机制发现优先级与低风险部署优先级。
- **提出避免过度声明的四层评测协议。** 明确区分 combination-oracle（标签泄露上界）、best fixed single config、learned gate（仅用在线可观测特征）、diagnostic-only，并系统点名了 pooled multi-config bias、best-selected bias、row-level bias 三种夸大形式。
- **跨模型、跨任务刻画 AG 的边界。** 在 Qwen3.5-9B（混合线性/全注意力）、Qwen3-8B、Qwen2.5-7B（均密集全注意力，FFN 扩展比不同）上证明：工具/结构化任务是最可信的首个部署目标，但不同 checkpoint 最强子任务不同（Qwen3.5 偏函数调用、Qwen3 偏 JSON、Qwen2.5 无可部署 gate），需要 model/category-specific routing 而非通用开关。
- **区分 non-NS 与 Newton-Schulz-windowed 两族 AG。** 固定池对比显示两者各有胜场：non-NS 是更锐利的当前 token 幅度门控，NS 通过最近 token 窗口加入局部轨迹多样性；NS 在 Long QA/factuality 更强，non-NS 在工具格式行更强。

## Key Contributions

- **Conceptual pivot from a destructive method to a conservative one.** Orthogonal Residual Projection (ORP), an SVD-based direction-rotating intervention, is shown to be undeployable: on a 1,032-question GSM8K audit it fixed 34 but broke 56 samples (net -22) and made 1,012/1,032 generations diverge token-level. The paper distills this into a design principle — pretrained FFN directions encode semantic priors that must not be overwritten — and proposes Amplitude Gating (AG), which masks/shrinks activation magnitudes while leaving all FFN weight matrices unchanged.
- **A six-point intervention taxonomy for SwiGLU FFNs.** Inherits P1 (FFN input), P2 (post-fusion internal), P3 (FFN output) from ORP and adds three SwiGLU-specific refined points: P1s (gate-branch-only input), P2a (post-SiLU pre-fusion), P2b (post-up-projection pre-fusion), each annotated with a research-priority vs deployment-priority split.
- **A four-level evaluation protocol engineered against overclaiming.** Explicitly separates combination-oracle (label-leaking upper bound), best fixed single-configuration, learned gate (restricted to online-observable features), and diagnostic-only; names and forbids pooled multi-config bias, best-selected bias, and row-level bias. Introduces task-aware strict vs partial10 fix/harm metrics so binary and partial-credit datasets are not conflated.
- **Cross-model boundary characterization.** On Qwen3.5-9B (hybrid linear/full attention), Qwen3-8B, and Qwen2.5-7B (dense full attention with different FFN expansion ratios), the paper shows tool-structured generation is the most credible first target, but the strongest subtask differs per checkpoint (function-call for Qwen3.5, JSON for Qwen3, none-deployable for Qwen2.5), arguing for model- and category-specific routing rather than a universal gate.
- **Two complementary AG families.** Non-NS entropy AG and Newton-Schulz-windowed NS AG are shown to be non-hierarchically complementary: non-NS wins tool-format rows as a sharper current-token amplitude gate; NS wins Long QA / factuality by adding short-window local-trajectory scoring.

## Methodology

AG starts from the FFN intervention hypothesis: tool-structured failures (wrong argument, function name, JSON bracket, schema continuation) are last-mile transformation errors that arise inside the FFN, which is the architecture-general, attention-agnostic substrate common to Transformer-like LLMs.

**From ORP to AG.** ORP used per-token SVD to project residual-stream FFN representations at P1/P2/P3, parameterized by an energy value e. Archived sweeps revealed non-monotonic e-sensitivity (e=0.93 net +9, e=0.97 net -5, e=0.80 net -2 on a 1,319-sample GSM8K P1 sweep) and a sample-specific fix/harm imbalance (49 benefit-only vs 79 harm-only among 128 flipping samples). The decisive negative evidence is the full-GSM8K matrix: the SVD-ORP checkpoint `qwen35|svd_orp|p1|e0.95|w3` produced 34 fixes / 56 harms, strict accuracy 85.27% vs 87.72% baseline, with 1,012/1,032 token-path divergences.

**Amplitude Gating.** AG takes an FFN activation x at a selected point and returns a masked/shrunk x' that preserves FFN weight directions. The main family is entropy-based: hard AG (L2-energy prefix mask), soft AG (shrinkage), top-k AG (fixed budget), local-norm AG (std threshold), NMF-style AG (separate positive/negative prefixes), and NS AG (Newton-Schulz-windowed entropy ranking using a w4 window of recent decode activations). The parameter e denotes retained L2-energy or retained entropy mass.

**SwiGLU six-point taxonomy.** On the gate_proj → SiLU → up_proj → fusion → down_proj path, P1/P2/P3 are inherited; P1s/P2a/P2b exploit SwiGLU's gate/value split. Deployment priority ordering is P3 (clean output-side hidden-dim signal) > P1s > task-specific variants; P2/P2a/P2b are research/oracle points due to 4x-hidden cost and noise.

**Gate and fallback.** AG is evaluated as a gated candidate, not an unconditional replacement. A learned policy uses only online-observable gate-policy features (prefix/format/schema, early logprob trajectory, baseline uncertainty, divergence/energy); label-derived and post-fork diagnostic features are forbidden for deployable claims. The baseline is always available as fallback and is excluded only from oracle candidate choice.

**Evaluation levels and metrics.** Four levels are reported: combination oracle (per-sample best non-baseline candidate, upper bound only), best fixed single config, learned gate (deployable offline evidence), diagnostic-only. Each dataset's native score is used; strict fix/harm is derived from the full-credit indicator, and a partial10 fix/harm (10 coarse score buckets) catches aggregate benefit on partial-credit datasets where strict ==1 understates baseline quality.

## Results & Highlights

- **Qwen3.5-9B tool/structured category (main result).** A category-level learned mixed gate improves the category from **38.66% to 42.92% (+4.27 pp)** against the baseline; the combination-oracle upper bound is +11.99 pp (50.65%), so the gate captures 35.6% of oracle headroom. Strict balance is positive (388 fixes / 317 harms) and partial10 balance strongly positive (2,425 / 1,401).
- **Qwen3.5-9B Hermes function-call datasets.** Hermes function call: baseline 29.02% → best learned 36.66% (**+7.64 pp**, 69.4% oracle capture). Hermes function call single-turn: 28.76% → 36.38% (**+7.62 pp**, 71.7% capture). ToolBench out-domain: 40.93% → 47.49% (+6.56 pp). ToolBench in-domain: 33.99% → 37.95% (+3.96 pp).
- **Qwen3-8B Hermes JSON mode (strongest single dataset).** Baseline 41.83% → best learned 53.19% (**+11.36 pp**, 77.6% oracle capture), driven by prefix/format online features. Category tool/structured lift is +2.64 pp (42.78% → 45.42%).
- **Qwen2.5-7B boundary case.** Tool/structured category: baseline 44.17% → learned 44.13% (**-0.04 pp**) despite a combination-oracle headroom of **+4.03 pp** (oracle capture -0.94%). This is the key counter-example showing oracle space does not imply a deployable gate; Long QA (-0.19 pp vs +2.98 pp oracle) and even Factuality (+0.76 pp vs +4.78 pp oracle) repeat the pattern.
- **Aggregate strict-score sanity check (9,886 examples).** Baseline 9.92% full-score; best single P3/e0.93 11.03% (+1.10 pp); three-position union oracle 11.72% (+1.80 pp), with 178 fixes / 141 harms — nonzero signal but a large harm rate, which motivates the move to route-specific claims.
- **Feature-family ablation with 95% bootstrap CIs (n=2,556 per model).** Qwen3.5-9B P/e + nonconfidence features: **+4.555 pp, CI [3.746, 5.470]**, oracle +11.994 pp CI [11.123, 13.002]. Qwen3-8B: **+2.553 pp, CI [1.916, 3.235]**. Qwen2.5-7B: **+0.039 pp, CI [-0.275, 0.327]** — CI straddles zero, confirming the boundary case. Qwen3-8B Hermes JSON mode under P/e + nonconfidence: **+11.449 pp, CI [9.374, 13.648]**.
- **NS vs non-NS fixed-pool comparison.** Non-uniform winner: Qwen3.5-9B tool/structured favors non-NS (40.76% vs 40.11%) while Long QA and Factuality favor NS (5.00% vs 4.64%; 23.75% vs 22.15%); Qwen3-8B tool/structured favors NS (50.20% vs 49.40%) but the deployable gain there is carried by JSON/prefix features.
- **MC/choice diagnostics (negative claim boundary).** C-Eval: +0.000 pp CI [-0.808, 0.808]; MMLU-Pro: +0.399 pp CI [0.133, 0.664] for static P/e but choice/trace feature gate -0.085 pp CI [-0.229, 0.060]. Consistent with the decode-only design: one-token choice tasks give AG little trajectory to reshape.
- **Gate trade-off curve (Qwen3.5-9B ToolBench, p1/e0.93).** No gate: 33.3% fix capture / 33.0% harm exposure; crossppl <= 1.25833: 22.2% / 13.0%; std3&crossppl: 11.5% / 7.4% — a Pareto trade-off rather than a binary enable/disable.

## Authors & Credibility

The six authors (Sheng Xu, Junhua Wang, Boyuan Huang, Ke Jia, Jiadun Zhu, Zhen Chen) are all affiliated with Alibaba Cloud (alibaba-inc.com / taobao.com email addresses), giving them direct access to production-scale LLM inference infrastructure and the Qwen model family, which is the natural testbed for a Qwen-centric FFN-intervention study. The paper is unusually self-critical: it foregrounds a failed internal method (ORP) as motivation, openly reports a boundary case (Qwen2.5-7B) where the method does not work, and adds 95% bootstrap confidence intervals in a feature-family ablation rather than relying on point estimates. No external peer-review status is indicated (arXiv preprint). The reference list is compact (14 entries) and mixes canonical sources (Vaswani 2017, Shazeer GLU 2020, ToolLLM, MMLU-Pro, C-Eval) with one project-archive note (DeepSeek-V4 [5]) that the authors explicitly flag as "not public or peer-reviewed evidence" — a notable honesty marker.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate but genuine. The core idea (modulate FFN activation magnitudes, never weight directions, under an online gate) is a sensible narrowing of representation-engineering/steering work into a non-destructive, deployment-flavored candidate mechanism. The six-point SwiGLU taxonomy (P1s/P2a/P2b exploiting the gate/value split) is a real methodological artifact. However, the paper is more an empirical characterization than a new algorithm — the "AG" family is largely magnitude/entropy masking with a Newton-Schulz ranking variant, and the Newton-Schulz component's mechanistic justification is thin (it borrows terminology from Muon-style optimizer normalization but only uses it to re-rank mask dimensions at inference).
- **Rigor:** High on evaluation hygiene, the paper's clear strength. The four-level protocol (oracle / best-fixed / learned / diagnostic) and the explicit forbidden-feature policy (label-derived, post-fork cross-PPL excluded from deployable gates) are a genuine methodological contribution that other inference-intervention papers should adopt. Bootstrap CIs on the feature-family ablation are the right statistical move. That said, **parameter overfitting is explicitly acknowledged as not fully excluded**: learned gates are validated only within the existing aligned CSV pools with a stable hash split, with no independent time-split or new held-out dataset, so the Qwen3.5 +4.27 pp and Qwen3-8B +11.36 pp numbers should be read as offline upper-bound-ish evidence rather than prospective online gains.
- **Reproducibility:** Mixed. The protocol is described in enough detail to reimplement (candidate matrix = 12 old-P + 12 new-P, e-grid fixed at 0.93/0.95, w4 NS window, feature taxonomy table), and 1,026 gate-policy features plus 31 diagnostic features are enumerated by class. But there is no mention of code release, and several artifacts are "project-archive" internal runs (the ORP sweep, the Qwen3.5 MC trace) that an external party cannot access. Reference [5] (DeepSeek-V4) is an internal technical note, not citable evidence.
- **Significance:** Targeted rather than broad. The honest framing — AG helps tool/JSON/agentic trajectories most, fails or is neutral on MC and weak on long QA — narrows the impact but makes the surviving claims more credible. The position-by-e and model-by-task interactions (P1s best for Qwen3.5 function-call, P2a for Qwen3, no positive cell for Qwen2.5) are exactly the kind of fine-grained map that future deployment work needs.
- **Red flags / things to probe:** (1) The **Qwen2.5-7B negative result is presented as a feature, not a failure** — rhetorically convenient, though the CI-negative feature gate does make it a real boundary rather than a spin. (2) **Qwen3-8B tool/structured has a negative strict fix/harm balance (22/27)** even though partial10 is positive (218/40); the headline +2.64 pp category gain rests on partial-credit scoring, which the paper itself warns can overstate baseline quality — readers should weight the strict numbers more heavily for binary-deployment decisions. (3) The **e-grid is fixed at only two values (0.93/0.95)** inherited from early sweeps; the authors admit a denser e-sweep "remains useful future work," so reported best cells may be local optima. (4) **All three models are from one family (Qwen)**; cross-family transfer (Llama, Mistral, Gemma) is untested despite the architecture-generality rhetoric. (5) The combination-oracle numbers (e.g., +20.08 pp on Qwen3.5 ToolBench out-domain) are large and repeatedly cited, which risks readers misremembering oracle headroom as deployable gain despite the paper's warnings.

## Strengths

- Intellectual honesty is the standout virtue: a failed method (ORP) is the conceptual centerpiece, the strongest negative result (Qwen2.5-7B) is reported in full, and bootstrap CIs are added rather than hidden.
- The four-level evaluation taxonomy and the named overclaiming traps (pooled multi-config, best-selected, row-level bias) are a reusable protocol contribution for the inference-intervention subfield.
- The SwiGLU-aware six-point intervention taxonomy (P1s/P2a/P2b) is concrete and ties intervention sites to the gate/value branch structure, giving future work a precise coordinate system.
- Decode-only design rationale (AG starts at the first generated token, leaving prefill untouched) cleanly explains why multi-token tool/JSON trajectories benefit while one-token MC tasks do not.
- The NS-vs-non-NS analysis resists declaring a winner and instead offers a mechanistic two-family routing interpretation tied to model architecture (hybrid vs dense, narrow vs wide FFN).

## Limitations

- **Single model family.** Only Qwen3.5-9B, Qwen3-8B, Qwen2.5-7B are evaluated; no Llama/Mistral/Gemma evidence, so the architecture-generality claim is unproven beyond one family.
- **Offline-only deployment evidence.** No prospective online A/B, no time-split, no new held-out dataset; parameter overfitting not fully excluded.
- **Mechanism under-explained.** P1s/P2a/P2b are shown to help empirically, but the hypotheses that P1s isolates gate-branch correction and that P2a/P2b are orthogonal mechanism probes remain untested; layer-wise sensitivity is an incomplete ablation.
- **Sparse e-grid.** Only e=0.93/0.95 are probed; deployment calibration and a denser sweep are deferred to future work, so best cells may be local optima.
- **Cost not measured in a serving stack.** Latency/throughput/memory estimates come from offline runs; multi-candidate mining is acknowledged as too expensive for production, and the online cost of augmented candidate paths is not directly benchmarked.
- **Strongest Qwen3-8B category gain leans on partial credit.** The tool/structured strict balance is negative (22/27), so the +2.64 pp rests on partial10 scoring the paper itself cautions about.

## Relevance

This paper is directly relevant to inference-time LLM optimization and tool-using/agent reliability — the precise intersection of efficient serving and structured-output quality that matters for production agent stacks. Its strict oracle-vs-deployable-gate evaluation protocol and the explicit catalog of overclaiming traps are reusable methodological lessons for anyone evaluating activation-level or decoding-level interventions on LLMs.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the AG effect and the P1s/P2a/P2b ranking transfer to non-Qwen families (Llama-3, Mistral, Gemma) with different FFN gating (GeGLU, ReGLU) or non-gated GELU FFNs?
- Can an online-computable proxy for post-fork cross-PPL recover the harm-suppression seen in the offline gate trade-off curve (7.4% harm at 11.5% fix capture) without a second forward pass?
- How do AG gains interact with constrained/grammar-guided decoding (e.g., JSON-schema enforcement)? Do they stack, substitute, or interfere at the FFN-vs-decoder levels?
- Would a denser e-sweep (e.g., 0.80–0.99) change the best fixed cell, and is there a single per-model calibrated e that subsumes the 0.93/0.95 split?
- Can the FFN trajectory signals (first-divergence, layer-trace) be used on the training side to detect noisy tool-supervision examples, as the paper speculates in future work, and does that improve a freshly fine-tuned tool model more than inference-time AG?
