arXiv 2607.12640 ·2026-07-14 ·RL / GRPO Web Agent Mechanistic Interpretability RLVR

GRPO cannot credibly beat supervision on already-mastered web-agent tasks — the null is a headroom condition, not a broken pipeline.

A tightly controlled 18-run study on Qwen3-VL (4B/8B) over MiniWoB. No GRPO configuration credibly exceeds the supervised baseline on mastered tasks; learning rate alone moves success, and only downward. Weight grafting causally localizes the failure.

Why this matters

Tells practitioners when GRPO pays off on a web agent — and when to spend that compute on supervision or distillation instead, using a free pre-check.

01Methodology

One agent, one environment, one reward, one task set — so any success-rate change traces to the RL choices.

The agent is Qwen3-VL at 4B and 8B on MiniWoB, emitting one action per step (clicks, typing, selection, navigation, finish). Two observation tracks share all other logic: a text track (serialized page) for the grid and mechanism, and a Set-of-Marks (SoM) screenshot track as a modality check. Reward is a sparse binary terminal signal r(τ)=1[success(τ)]∈{0,1}.

The GRPO recipe follows Liu et al. (2025) with group-mean-centered advantages (no std division) and a DAPO-style clip-higher bound (ϵlo=0.20 < ϵhi=0.28), plus an optional k3 KL anchor. The controlled null is an 18-run grid over LR, KL weight β, seed, init (SFT vs base), and clip bound, with ablations on warmup+cosine and group size G∈{8,16,32}.

Evaluation & causality apparatus

Clean greedy decoding on the 11-task grid at 5 seeds (55 matched episodes), scored with Wilson intervals and an exact McNemar test on discordant pairs plus a task-clustered bootstrap 95% CI on ∆SR=SR_arm−SR_SFT; an arm is "credible" only if the CI excludes zero; equivalence via TOST within ±δ. Mechanism uses a fixed cache of N=1999 hidden states (layer-35 effective rank, participation ratio, argmax agreement, entropy, degeneracy). Causality via weight grafting — restore one component group (attention / MLP / embedding) from init and re-measure — benchmarked against a magnitude-matched random-group null over 24 draws.

02Results & Highlights

Learning rate is the only dial that moves success — and it only moves it downward.

Positive control: 20.0%→42.0% on headroom tasks, p=0.007.
  • On mastered tasks the best arm (SFT init, LR 3e-6) reaches only 52.7%, a +3.6pp nominal gain with task-clustered CI [+0.0, +10.9], McNemar p=0.50 — indistinguishable from noise.
  • The null is robust across axes: 25 eval seeds lower baseline to 44.7% with no credible winner; a 6-seed replication of the nominal-best averages 49.7% (CI [47.7, 51.7]) — on baseline — so the +5.1pp single-seed edge was training-seed noise.
  • Positive control: on ten headroom tasks (50 episodes) the identical recipe lifts 20.0%→42.0% (+22.0pp, CI [+8,+40], p=0.007); five of six configs rise (four credibly), and the no-op LR gains +12 to +18pp here.
  • Mechanism via grafting (degrade regime, frontier subset): restoring attention alone lifts SR 11.4%→37.1%, MLP alone →40.0% — both above the random null (p95=22.9%) — while restoring the embedding →14.0%, clearing it despite its dominant drift.
  • Late-layer (L35) effective rank: base 7.0, SFT 9.2, no-op 10.1, degrade 1.2, collapse 13.9 — a rank inversion stable over the full cache. On headroom tasks the climbing checkpoint holds rank 7.14 (42% SR) while the degrading one collapses to 3.21 (12% SR).
  • A distillation pilot (5 tasks, 6 seeds): expert supervision lifts the base 50%→80% while GRPO on top of any init adds nothing.

03Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • A genuine controlled null paired with a matched positive control that isolates the cause (headroom) from a broken pipeline — a design most RL papers skip.
  • Causal, not correlational mechanism: weight grafting with a magnitude-matched random-component null overturns the naive "embedding drifted most = culprit" reading.
  • Statistically literate: episode-matched paired tests, task clustering, TOST equivalence, and a seed replication that exposes a +5.1pp lucky-seed artifact.
  • Honest scoping: explicit text-track-vs-SoM and 4B-vs-8B boundaries; mechanism point estimates flagged as noisy and reported qualitatively.
  • Actionable output: the ∆head screen and "supervision/distillation over RL for competent agents" guidance, backed by a 50%→80% distillation pilot.

Limitations

  • Single benchmark (MiniWoB) and single model family (Qwen3-VL 4B/8B) — chosen for mechanistic control; null and mechanism may differ on WebArena or other backbones.
  • Credible harm is 4B text-track-only: on Set-of-Marks the high-rate degradation is nominal (p=0.070, CI touches zero), so "GRPO subtracts skill" is not modality-universal.
  • Mechanism rests on few checkpoints per regime with noisy point estimates (~35 episodes for grafting), reported qualitatively rather than with tight CIs.
  • Rank-capability coupling breaks at 8B — the central mechanistic narrative is 4B-specific; 8B behavior is acknowledged but not explained.
  • Null concerns only mastered-task regime — the positive control shows the same recipe climbs when headroom exists.
  • No large-scale / longer-training probe; a different reward shape or far longer RL could, in principle, shift the verdict.

04Questions for Further Exploration

Does the headroom condition (∆head>0) generalize beyond MiniWoB — e.g. WebArena or tool-use / OS-world agents where greedy-vs-sampled gaps and reward noise differ?
Why does the rank-capability coupling break at 8B? Is 8B absorbing rank collapse via redundancy, or does capability live at a different layer/probe than L35?
Would a denser or shaped reward (vs sparse binary terminal success) change the verdict on mastered tasks, or does the climbs-toward-sampled bound hold regardless?
Can the grafting-localized attention/MLP lesion become a preventive intervention — per-block LR scaling or selective freezing — that preserves the no-op regime?
How does the result interact with online curriculum RL (e.g. WebRL) that continuously shifts the task distribution toward headroom tasks — does it sidestep the null by construction?
Is the "embedding drift is causally inert" finding stable under different KL anchors or reference choices, or does it depend on the k3 estimator and frozen-init anchor used here?
