> **一句话总结：** 本文将 Pesme & Flammarion (2023) 关于两层对角线性网络鞍点到鞍点动力学的分析，推广到一般的两层及深层对角线性网络（任意初始化方向）情形，证明其在无穷小初始化下的隐式偏差为修正 ℓ1 范数 R(k)=Σᵢ(tᵢ⁺kᵢ⁺+tᵢ⁻kᵢ⁻)，并以"结构不变流形 (SIM)"作为统一机制解释了距离-速度论证与特征选择两阶段过程。

> **TL;DR:** Extending Theorem 1 of Pesme & Flammarion (2023), this ICML 2026 theory paper proves that the saddle-to-saddle gradient-flow dynamics of both deep diagonal linear networks (under generic initialization directions) and a general class of two-layer diagonal linear networks (Definition 4.1) are exactly captured by a recursive Algorithm 1, which converges to a modified ℓ1-norm minimizer R(k)=Σᵢ(tᵢ⁺kᵢ⁺+tᵢ⁻kᵢ⁻) on the synthetic 4-neuron / M=3,N=8 testbeds; verified at initialization scale 10⁻⁶⁰ with LR 0.1 where Algorithm 1's iterates k⁽¹⁾=(0.8,0,0,0), k⁽²⁾=(0,0,1.4,0), k⁽³⁾=(0,0,10/7,−10/49) match the empirical GD trajectory (e.g. final k=(−1.71e−6,−2.48e−57,1.429,−0.204)). The unified mechanism is the Structural Invariant Manifold (SIM), which the authors identify as the geometric object inducing the sign-locking and two-phase (feature-selection + learning) behavior.

## 核心贡献

- **训练轨迹刻画（Theorem 4.5）：** 将 Pesme & Flammarion (2023) 关于两层对角线性网络的鞍点到鞍点动力学结果，推广到（i）满足 Definition 4.1 的一般两层对角线性网络，以及（ii）在**通用初始化方向**（不再局限于 Woodworth et al. 2020 的特定方向）下的深层（L≥3）对角线性网络；证明其梯度流轨迹可被 Algorithm 1 逐次等价刻画。
- **隐式偏差刻画（Corollary 4.6）：** 证明上述两类网络在无穷小初始化下的隐式偏差是**修正 ℓ1 范数** R(k)=Σᵢ(tᵢ⁺kᵢ⁺+tᵢ⁻kᵢ⁻)，而非标准 ℓ1 范数——其权重 tᵢ⁺、tᵢ⁻ 由初始化方向（深层网络）或 Hessian 极特征值（一般两层网络）决定。这是对 Woodworth et al. (2020) / Gunasekar et al. (2017) 标准 ℓ1 结论的严格推广。
- **算法收敛性（Theorem 4.2）：** 证明 Algorithm 1 在合理非退化条件下有限步终止，且其输出恰为修正 ℓ1 最小化问题 min R(k) s.t. Xk=y 的解（通过 KKT 条件证明）。
- **统一机制揭示（Section 6）：** 引入 Structural Invariant Manifold (SIM, Zhao et al. 2026) 作为解释动力学底层机制的统一几何框架——SIM 由轨道（orbit）构成，参数沿简单曲线演化，从而自然诱导符号锁定与"特征选择 + 学习"的两相动力学。
- **距离-速度直觉（Section 5）：** 以三层对角网络为例，用"距离 D⁺（由初始化方向决定）÷ 速度 |v₀|（由特征-目标相关性决定）"的最小激活时间原则，直观解释了为何只有最短时间的神经元被增量激活。

## Key Contributions

- **Training-trajectory generalization (Theorem 4.5).** Extends the saddle-to-saddle analysis of Pesme & Flammarion (2023) from (a) standard two-layer networks and (b) deep networks restricted to specific initialization directions, to (a) the broad family of two-layer diagonal networks of Definition 4.1 and (b) deep (L≥3) diagonal networks under **generic** initialization directions. The gradient-flow trajectory is shown to be exactly tracked by Algorithm 1.
- **Modified-ℓ1 implicit bias (Corollary 4.6).** Under infinitesimal initialization the implicit bias of both architectures is the weighted norm R(k)=Σᵢ(tᵢ⁺kᵢ⁺+tᵢ⁻kᵢ⁻), not the plain ℓ1 norm. The weights tᵢ± come from initialization-direction integrals (deep nets) or Hessian eigenvalues (general two-layer nets). This generalizes the classic ℓ1 results of Woodworth et al. (2020) and Gunasekar et al. (2017).
- **Algorithmic convergence (Theorem 4.2).** Algorithm 1 is proved to terminate in finitely many iterations and its output is exactly a KKT solution of the convex program min R(k) s.t. Xk=y — giving an algorithmic handle on the implicit-bias characterization.
- **SIM as the unifying mechanism (Section 6).** The Structural Invariant Manifold (Zhao et al., 2026) is identified as the geometric structure inducing sign-locking and the two-phase (feature-selection + learning) behavior. Theorem 6.5 shows the SIM factorizes as a product of per-coordinate orbits O_F(θ)=∏ᵢ O_Fᵢ(θᵢ), each a simple analytic curve (Hermann–Nagano theorem).
- **Distance-Speed intuition (Section 5).** Provides an accessible three-layer derivation: the time for a neuron to reach O(1) magnitude equals "distance" D⁺ (set by initialization) divided by "speed" |v₀| (set by feature–target correlation), explaining the greedy incremental feature activation.

## Methodology

The paper is purely theoretical (with small synthetic confirmatory experiments), structured as a generalization of Pesme & Flammarion (2023). The proof strategy has three layers:

1. **Algorithm 1 + Theorem 4.2 (algorithmic layer).** The authors propose a recursive algorithm parameterized by positive weights {tᵢ⁺, tᵢ⁻}: at each step it picks the inactive neuron j with minimal activation time δⱼ = min(tᵢ⁺/uᵢ if uᵢ>0 else tᵢ⁻/|uᵢ|), adds it to the active set with a sign constraint, and re-solves a sign-constrained least-squares subproblem. Theorem 4.2 proves finite termination and that the output satisfies the KKT conditions of the convex program min Σᵢ(tᵢ⁺kᵢ⁺+tᵢ⁻kᵢ⁻) s.t. Xk=y.

2. **Theorem 4.5 (dynamics-to-algorithm equivalence).** For deep networks, the conserved quantities aᵢ,ⱼ²(t)−aᵢ,L²(t) = s²μᵢ,ⱼ² and a variable substitution reduce the per-coordinate ODE to dã/dτ = s^(L−1)·√(ã²+μ̃₁²)·√(ã²+μ̃₂²), giving "blow-up times" tᵢ⁺=Fᵢ(+∞), tᵢ⁻=−Fᵢ(−∞) via explicit integrals. For general two-layer networks the times reduce to tᵢ⁺=1/λᵢ⁺, tᵢ⁻=−1/λᵢ⁻ from the Hessian eigenvalues (using Lemma A.2 of Li et al. 2021). A timescale-separation argument (feature selection lasts O(1/s), learning is O(1)) shows the trajectory's limit points coincide with the algorithm's iterates.

3. **Section 6 (mechanism via SIM).** The orbit structure O_F(θ)=∏ᵢ O_Fᵢ(θᵢ) (Theorem 6.5) is shown to imply: (i) parameters initialized at 0 stay at 0 (inducing the feature-selection phase); (ii) parameters initialized away from 0 travel along a simple curve, so sign changes require returning to initialization scale (inducing sign-locking during learning); (iii) under generic data conditions only one neuron has minimal δᵢ, giving incremental (one-neuron-at-a-time) learning.

The technique deliberately bypasses the explicit mirror-flow representation used by Pesme & Flammarion (2023), replacing it with the more general SIM framework (which applies to any analytic parametric model, including matrix sensing).

## Results & Highlights

The paper is theoretical; quantitative claims come from small synthetic confirmatory experiments rather than benchmark leaderboards. All headline numbers are from the authors' own constructions, not standard datasets:

- On a 4-coordinate general two-layer diagonal linear network (Definition 4.1 with k₁,k₂=2aᵢbᵢ+(aᵢ²+bᵢ²+cᵢ²)cᵢ and k₃,k₄=aᵢtanh(aᵢ)−(e^{bᵢ}−1)²) trained by GD with **initialization scale 10⁻⁶⁰ and learning rate 0.1** (200-decimal mpmath precision), Algorithm 1's predicted iterates **k⁽¹⁾=(0.8,0,0,0), k⁽²⁾=(0,0,1.4,0), k⁽³⁾=(0,0,10/7,−10/49)** match the empirical GD trajectory's plateau values, e.g. the third plateau is **(−1.71e−6,−2.48e−57,1.429,−0.204)** — within rounding of the theoretical k⁽³⁾=(0,0,1.429,−0.204). This validates Theorem 4.5 on a non-standard (non aᵢbᵢ) two-layer model where Pesme & Flammarion (2023) does not apply.
- On the data matrix X=[[1,0.5,0.7,0],[0.5,1,0.1,0.7]], y=[1,0]ᵀ, the paper shows standard ℓ1 minimization selects support {3,4} (k₃=1.43, k₄=−0.20), whereas **modified ℓ1 with a 0.5 penalty on |k₁| shifts the recovered support to {1,4}** (k₁=1.0, k₄=−0.71). Both stay sparse but recover different features — directly demonstrating that the modified-ℓ1 bias is not cosmetic.
- On a deep diagonal linear network (w=a⊙b⊙c) with **M=3 samples, N=8 features, initialization scale ≈10⁻⁴ and strict layer hierarchy a>b>c** (Figure 2), the theoretically predicted per-stage growth times (computed as integrated virtual distances) "align perfectly" with the empirical emergence order and relative timing of neurons (qualitative match, no numerical RMSE reported).
- **Controllability result (Figure 3):** amplifying the initialization magnitude of neuron 5 by a factor of 5 forces it to emerge first, overriding the natural data-correlation-driven order — and Algorithm 1 correctly predicts this altered emergence sequence.
- **ℓ1-vs-ℓ0 verification (Figure 4):** under "Zero-Balanced" initialization (a=b, c=0, scale ≈10⁻⁴) the parameters converge to the **minimum ℓ1-norm solution rather than the sparse ℓ0 ground truth**, confirming the c=0 initialization induces a strong ℓ1 inductive bias.

No quantitative results reported on standard ML benchmarks (ImageNet, CIFAR, GLUE, etc.) — this is a learning-theory paper, and the experiments are toy-scale confirmations of the theorems.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (genuine):** The result is a real generalization, not a re-derivation. Pesme & Flammarion (2023) require symmetric growth times tᵢ⁺=tᵢ⁻=1 (Remark 4.3 calls this out) and a specific mirror-flow form; this paper handles arbitrary tᵢ⁺≠tᵢ⁻ and a class of two-layer networks (Definition 4.1) far broader than the canonical kᵢ(θᵢ)=aᵢbᵢ. The observation that the modified-ℓ1 bias can shift the recovered support (e.g. {3,4}→{1,4} on the 4×2 example) is a substantive, under-appreciated point — it means "ℓ1 implicit bias" is not a single universal statement.
- **Rigor (strong):** Proofs are complete (Theorem 4.2 via KKT, Theorem 4.5 via conserved-quantity + timescale-separation, Theorem 6.5 via Hermann–Nagano). Assumptions are explicit and mostly mild (unique minimizer of (a*ᵢ,k)², non-degeneracy of selected δᵢ). The extension to large-initialization regimes (Theorem A.11 in the appendix) is a nice bonus.
- **Reproducibility (mixed):** Code is not mentioned. The synthetic experiments are simple enough to re-implement (mpmath, 200 decimal digits, LR 0.1, scale 10⁻⁶⁰), but the use of 10⁻⁶⁰ initialization is wildly impractical — it is a faithful proxy for the infinitesimal limit, not a deployable recipe, and the paper honestly labels it as such ("impractical to implement", Section 7).
- **Significance (moderate, narrow):** The contribution is concentrated within the diagonal-linear-network / implicit-bias subfield. The authors themselves flag (Section 8.2) that extending to matrix sensing is "an interesting direction for future research" — i.e., not done here. There is no path proposed toward ReLU networks or real architectures.
- **Red flags / overclaiming:**
  - The **gradient-flow vs gradient-descent gap is a serious caveat the authors handle well but do not resolve.** Section 8.2 concedes that curved SIMs (the generic case here) are *not* exactly invariant under GD/SGD, and Figure 1 indeed shows k₁ changing sign between epochs 3000–4000 without returning to its initialization scale (10⁻¹²⁰) — a direct violation of Theorem 6.5's GF prediction. So the theorems describe a limit (s→0, continuous time) that real training only approximates.
  - The modified-ℓ1 support-shift example (Section 8.2) is on a hand-crafted 4×2 matrix with a manually chosen 0.5 penalty; it illustrates the phenomenon but does not characterize *when* support shift happens in general.
  - **Missing ablations:** no sensitivity analysis to the initialization scale s in the intermediate regime (between infinitesimal and large), no comparison to the Berthier (2023) incremental-learning characterization, and no empirical check of whether the modified-ℓ1 weights tᵢ± are recoverable from finite-s training.
  - The "feature-selection controllability" claim (Figure 3, 5× amplification) is a single-neuron, single-dataset demonstration.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated generalization of a recent NeurIPS result, with the gap from prior work (symmetric growth times, restricted init directions) explicitly stated in Remark 4.3 and Section 1.1.
- The SIM-based mechanism (Section 6) is a genuinely unifying viewpoint that explains *why* the algorithmic description works, and it is reusable for other analytic models (matrix sensing is explicitly named).
- Intellectual honesty about limitations: the GF/GD invariance gap is not hidden — it is illustrated with a concrete failure mode in Figure 1 (k₁ sign-flip anomaly) and tied to the curvature of the SIM.
- The modified-ℓ1 result is more than cosmetic: the support-shift example shows it changes *which* features are learned, not just the norm.
- Distance-Speed intuition (Section 5) is pedagogically valuable and makes the otherwise opaque blow-up-time integrals readable.

## Limitations

- **Infinitesimal initialization only.** The analysis holds in the s→0 limit; the authors note Woodworth et al. (2020) achieved characterization across all initialization scales, which this paper does not match.
- **Gradient-flow assumption.** Results do not directly extend to GD/SGD/Adam; curved SIMs lose exact invariance under discretization, a gap the authors acknowledge but leave open (referencing Ziyin et al. 2024).
- **Narrow model class.** Restricted to diagonal linear networks. Extension to matrix sensing, ReLU networks, or any realistic architecture is future work.
- **Toy-scale experiments only.** No standard benchmarks; the 4-coordinate and M=3,N=8 setups confirm the theorems but do not establish practical relevance.
- **No released code** mentioned in the paper.
- The non-degeneracy assumptions (unique argmin of δᵢ, non-orthogonality to Hessian eigenvectors) exclude measure-zero but practically encountered degenerate cases without discussion of how to perturb out of them.

## Relevance

高度相关于深度学习隐式偏差与训练动力学的理论研究方向。论文将对角线性网络的鞍点到鞍点动力学与修正 ℓ1 隐式偏差刻画推广到更一般的初始化方向与网络结构，并以结构不变流形（SIM）作为统一几何机制，对理解过参数化模型为何泛化、以及初始化如何塑造特征选择有直接借鉴价值，契合 implicit bias / optimization geometry / deep learning theory 等主题的跟踪需求。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the modified-ℓ1 weights tᵢ± be estimated or recovered from a *finite-scale* trained network, allowing the theory to predict the recovered support in practice?
- How robust is Theorem 4.5 to the non-degeneracy assumptions (unique argmin of δᵢ, non-orthogonality to Hessian eigenvectors)? Is there a stable perturbation argument for near-degenerate data?
- Does the SIM framework extend to matrix sensing / low-rank factorization, where the "connectivity" assumption of Bai et al. (2024) plays an analogous role? The authors flag this as future work but do not sketch a path.
- Can the GF/GD invariance gap (Section 8.2, Figure 1 anomaly) be quantified — e.g., a bound on how far GD trajectories drift from the curved SIM as a function of learning rate and curvature?
- Is there a connection between the modified-ℓ1 bias here and the Lasso regularization path characterization of Berthier (2025), which also studies diagonal linear networks?
- The modified-ℓ1 support-shift example uses a hand-picked 0.5 penalty; can one characterize the full set of supports achievable by varying {tᵢ±}, i.e., a modified-ℓ1 analogue of the Lasso solution path?
