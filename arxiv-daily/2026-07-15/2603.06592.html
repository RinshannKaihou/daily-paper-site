01 / CAUSAL CHAIN

Four theorems, one data-side cause

hierarchy ⇒ aggregation ⇒ specialization ⇒ redundancy
THM 1
Multi-branch aggregation
A Bayes-optimal finite-capacity model must pull the global latent Z⁽⁰⁾ from disjoint contexts via intermediate latents. Mechanistic correlate of induction heads + function vectors.
THM 2
Additive log-evidence
Conditionally independent branches contribute their log-evidence additively to the shared global latent's posterior.
THM 3
Block-diagonal Fisher
Additivity + gradient decorrelation ⇒ F = Σ Fᵢ + O(ε). Drives emergent specialization (Corollary 3.1).
THM 4
The Hydra effect
Under directional concavity, E[Δ̃ₖ] ≥ E[Δ]: ablating a specialized unit cannot reduce expected predictive influence.

Closing the chain requires two model-side assumptions — Assumption 5 (gradient decorrelation, |cos(uᵢ,uⱼ)| ≤ ε) and Assumption 6 (directional concavity of readout) — both probed empirically on trained models.

02 / METHOD

A latent hierarchy, two controlled regimes

Shared semantics across positions are modelled as a latent hierarchy Z⁽⁰⁾ → Z⁽¹⁾ → X of depth D ≥ 2 — a global latent, per-segment local latents, and observables. Four assumptions (regularity, hierarchical dependence, non-degeneracy, disjoint evidence streams) make the latents both necessary and recoverable.

  • Toy regime. 64-token vocab, length 128, 16 segments; hierarchical (K₀=4 global, K₁=16 local) vs flat DGP with identical surface statistics. 6-layer / 8-head / d=64 Transformers, AdamW lr 3e-4, 5,000 steps, 10 seeds.
  • Large-scale regime. 400M sentences each of PCSG (hierarchical, context-sensitive grammar) vs N-gram (flat, Zipf Markov). Identical downscaled LLaMA2-style model; OLMo-1B used as reference trajectory. 4×H100, ~1 day.
  • Probes. headwise/layerwise Hydra, induction score, function-vector logit-increase; plus direct probes for Assumption 5 (min cross-head gradient cosine) and Assumption 6 (mean directional Hessian curvature).
  • Bayes-gap floor 0.08 nats enforced so the flat baseline is a genuinely hard predictor, not a strawman.
Latent hierarchy (depth D ≥ 2)
Two disjoint token spans share the same global latent Z⁽⁰⁾ through distinct, conditionally-independent local latents (Assumption 4) — the structural seed of induction, function vectors, and redundancy.

04 / ASSESSMENT

Strengths against limitations

Strengths

  • Genuine unification of three separately-studied phenomena under one data-generative cause — a rare conceptual advance in mech-interp.
  • Rigorous toy hygiene: matched surface statistics, Bayes-gap floor, 10 seeds, paired parametric + non-parametric tests, Bonferroni, Cohen's d.
  • Probes the theory's assumptions (gradient decorrelation, directional curvature), not just its predictions.
  • Clean, reusable operational definitions for all three mechanistic scores.
  • Crisp two-stage internalization finding, mirrored in the loss trajectory.

Limitations

  • Hydra theorem is conditional on Assumption 6, verified post-hoc — the "guaranteed emergence" framing overstates unconditional force.
  • PCSG vs N-gram confound: PCSG also adds document-level context-sensitivity the flat baseline lacks; hierarchy not cleanly isolated.
  • Scale results are a trajectory match against a reference OLMo-1B, not a controlled same-compute comparison; custom model is downscaled.
  • 10-seed effect sizes are very large and timing-sensitive (AUC window); no power analysis or seed ablation.
  • Layerwise Hydra — the canonical granularity — fails Bonferroni, undercutting the headline where readers expect it.
  • All synthetic data is token-level abstract; transfer to natural language (semantics, discourse, coreference) is asserted, not shown.
05 / OPEN

Questions worth chasing

Q1

Can Assumption 6 (directional concavity) be derived from LayerNorm + softmax unembedding, rather than verified post-hoc — making Theorem 4 unconditional?

Q2

Does a PCFG without document-level PermuteOrder context-sensitivity still produce all three phenomena — isolating hierarchy from context-sensitivity?

Q3

If redundant specialization defeats single-point ablation, can the theory predict how many redundant realizations must be neutralized to suppress a harmful capability?

Q4

Does the hierarchy → block-diagonal Fisher link survive under alternative optimizers (SGD without momentum, Shampoo) where gradient decorrelation differs?

Q5

Does the result extend beyond toy tokens to natural semantic hierarchy (coreference, discourse, world knowledge) where ground-truth parse trees are messy?

Q6

Hydra peaks in middle layers (5–10) — is this depth-dependence predicted by the depth-D hierarchy, or an architectural artifact?
