> **一句话总结：** 提出 Critic Experience Bank (CEB)，一个无需训练、基于 hindsight 标注的自演化 critic 框架，在 Mind2Web / AMEX / InterCode-Bash 三个 agent 基准和 GPT-4.1 / GPT-5.4 / Qwen3-Next 三个 backbone 上对每个 dataset–critic 组合都取得了最优的 ECE / Brier / AUC，ECE 相对最强 training-free baseline 最多降低 54%。

> **TL;DR:** CEB is a training-free critic that augments a frozen LLM critic with a self-growing memory bank of hindsight-labeled (productive / unproductive) execution records; after each trajectory a hindsight LLM votes on per-step productivity, and the bank is retrieved contrastively (top-k positives + top-k negatives) into the critic prompt at scoring time. Across Mind2Web (web), AMEX (mobile GUI), and InterCode-Bash (shell), and three backbones (GPT-4.1, GPT-5.4, Qwen3-Next), CEB wins every ECE/Brier/AUC cell, reducing ECE by up to 54% versus the strongest training-free baseline (CoT / P(True) / Verbalization / PRO / PPL / ME), closing up to 95% of the gap to a ground-truth oracle bank.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统化定义 **LLM agent 的 step-level confidence estimation** 问题：在执行动作之前，给出一个经过校准的、表示"该动作对任务是否有推进作用"的概率，用于在线干预（重规划 / 人工复核 / 选择性执行）。
- 提出 **Critic Experience Bank (CEB)**：一个无需训练、critic 权重冻结、完全靠 hindsight 标注 + 检索增强来"自演化"的 critic 框架。证据来自 agent 自己历史轨迹的执行反馈，不依赖任何 ground truth step label。
- 设计 **contrastive 检索机制**：记忆库按 productive / unproductive 分桶，使用 `task || state || action` 的复合键 + TF-IDF cosine 相似度做 top-k 检索；每条记录还保存 agreement flag（critic 原始判断是否与 hindsight 一致）以提示自身系统性偏差。
- 在 3 个 agent 基准 × 3 个 critic backbone 共 **9 个 dataset–critic 单元** 上全面最优（ECE / Brier / AUC），并验证了下游可选择性执行（selective execution）和固定复核预算下更高的最终任务成功率。
- 充分的机制分析：组件消融、self-evolving 随任务流增长的曲线、与 ground-truth oracle 的 headroom 对比，定位出"对比负例 + 状态条件检索"是主要驱动力。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes **step-level confidence estimation for LLM agents** as a distinct problem from response-level LLM confidence: the score must be produced *before* execution and must predict whether the action will productively change the environment state, not just whether the response fits the prompt.
- Introduces **Critic Experience Bank (CEB)**, a training-free, weight-frozen, self-evolving critic. After each trajectory, a hindsight LLM (conditioned on the full action–observation sequence) votes V=5 times to produce a majority-vote binary pseudo-label per step; records (task, state summary, action, observation, pseudo-label, prior critic score, agreement flag) are appended to a class-split bank B+/B-.
- Builds a **contrastive, state-conditioned retrieval** mechanism: composite key `task:q || state:s_t || action:a_t`, TF-IDF cosine similarity, top-k per non-empty class bucket; includes a repetition flag for short loops that semantic similarity misses.
- Empirically dominates **9 / 9 dataset–critic cells** (Mind2Web, AMEX, InterCode-Bash × GPT-4.1, GPT-5.4, Qwen3-Next) on ECE, Brier, and AUC, with ECE reductions of up to 54% over the strongest training-free baseline.
- Provides mechanism-level evidence: per-component ablation isolating the contrastive split and state-conditioned retrieval as primary drivers; a self-evolving curve showing the gap to a bank-free Static critic widens as the bank fills; and an oracle headroom analysis showing CEB closes up to 95% of the Static→GT-oracle calibration gap on Mind2Web.

## Methodology

CEB reframes step-level confidence as **retrieval-augmented critic calibration** over the agent's own past state–action outcomes. The estimator is a fixed LLM critic f_θ conditioned on (q, s_t, h_<t, a_t) plus contrastive experiences retrieved from an evolving bank B_t:

**Trajectory & scoring target.** A trajectory is τ = (s_1,a_1,o_1),...,(s_T,a_T,o_T). At step t the critic must emit ẑ_t ∈ [0,1] for proposed action a_t *before* it is executed, to support online intervention (defer / replan / human review).

**Hindsight pseudo-labeling.** After the full trajectory completes, a hindsight LLM g_ϕ — seeing the entire (s_1:T, a_1:T, o_1:T, q) — emits a binary productivity label per step. To reduce noise on ambiguous steps, g_ϕ is sampled V=5 times at temperature τ>0 and majority vote gives ỹ_t = 1[mean(ŷ) ≥ 1/2].

**Bank record structure.** Each record stores task q', compact state summary s' (truncated DOM for web, visible clickables for mobile, recent terminal output for shell), action a', observation summary ǒ', pseudo-label ŷ', prior critic score ẑ', and an agreement flag χ' = 1[ẑ'≥1/2 == ŷ'] that exposes where the critic was previously over/under-confident. The bank is class-split: B+ = {ŷ=1}, B- = {ŷ=0}, making retrieval explicitly contrastive.

**Retrieval.** Composite key κ_t = task:q || state:s_t || action:a_t (state and task fields prevent retrieval from collapsing onto short reusable action strings). Cosine similarity under TF-IDF embeddings fit to the bank's keys; top-k (deployed k=2) from each non-empty class bucket form R+_t, R-_t. A repetition flag ρ_t flags actions matching a prefix of a_t-3:t-1.

**Critic prompt & scoring.** The prompt contains q, s_t, h_<t, a_t, retrieved R±_t, and ρ_t; retrieved examples expose their execution feedback and past confidence. The critic returns a JSON numeric score r_t clipped to [0,1]. No weights change; adaptation is purely contextual as the bank grows.

**Crucially online.** Trajectories are processed in stream order, and CEB appends a trajectory to the bank only after all of its own actions have been scored, so no future feedback leaks into an online estimate.

## Results & Highlights

- **Sweep over 9 dataset–critic cells (Table 1):** CEB wins ECE, Brier, and AUC in *every* cell. On Mind2Web with GPT-5.4, CEB reaches **ECE 0.176 / AUC 0.704** vs the strongest training-free baseline CoT at **ECE 0.459 / AUC 0.596** (PRO had ECE 0.205 but AUC 0.589). On InterCode-Bash with GPT-4.1, CEB reaches **ECE 0.181 / AUC 0.648** vs CoT **ECE 0.440** and P(True) **AUC 0.534** — a 54% relative ECE reduction (paper's headline number).
- **Permissive LLM-as-judge labels (Table 2, Mind2Web & AMEX):** CEB remains best in every cell even when the judge credits alternative valid paths. On Mind2Web with GPT-5.4, CEB attains **ECE 0.076 / AUC 0.835** vs CoT 0.335/0.690 and P(True) 0.314/0.699; on AMEX with GPT-5.4, CEB reaches **ECE 0.057 / AUC 0.800** vs Verbalization 0.091/0.758 — confirming the advantage is not an artifact of strict reference matching.
- **Component ablation (Table 3, Mind2Web/GPT-5.4):** Removing the bank entirely (Static) gives the largest hit, ΔECE +0.142. Within the bank, dropping the contrastive split (positives only) costs +0.065 ECE; dropping TF-IDF retrieval for random sampling costs +0.042; majority vote (V=1) costs +0.028; repetition flag costs +0.017.
- **Self-evolving curve (Figure 3):** Cumulative ECE gap over the Static critic grows from indistinguishable at the start to **+0.073 after 30 tasks, +0.104 after 50, +0.136 after 150, +0.142 on the full stream** — calibration improves purely from bank growth even though critic weights never change.
- **Selective execution (Table 4, Mind2Web/GPT-5.4):** With 50% abstention, retained GT-positive rate is **0.288 for CEB** vs base 0.198 (+0.090), while CoT only reaches 0.234 (+0.036) and Mean Entropy *drops* to 0.114 (actively misaligned, correct actions ranked below wrong ones).
- **Final task success under review budget (Table 5, GPT-5.4):** With m=2 reviewed steps, CEB reaches **12.5% on Mind2Web** (CoT 11.6%, Verbalization 11.5%) and **44.7% on AMEX** (CoT 44.7%, Verbalization 43.2%); Oracle ceiling is 21.2% / 60.7%.
- **Oracle headroom (Table 6, GPT-5.4):** CEB-vs-GT-oracle residual gap is only **0.007 ECE on Mind2Web** (Static→Oracle headroom 0.149, so **95% closed**) and 0.029 on AMEX (61% closed) — pseudo-label noise is *not* the dominant residual error source.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine. The reframing — agent confidence targets an *execution event* rather than a *response*, so the signal must come from prior execution consequences, not introspection on the current step — is well-motivated and the diagnostic in Figure 1 (off-the-shelf confidence signals are unstable across within-trajectory depth on Mind2Web/GPT-5.4) makes the gap concrete. Placing memory on the *critic* side (vs Reflexion / ExpeL / ReasoningBank which improve the *actor*) is a clean distinction.
- **Rigor:** Strong. 3 datasets × 3 backbones = 9 cells, all metrics reported, two labeling policies (reference-match + LLM-as-judge), per-component ablation, self-evolving curve averaged over 5 random stream orders with ±std, oracle headroom analysis, and a selective-execution + task-success downstream study. Online-leak control (bank updated only after a trajectory's own actions are scored) is explicit.
- **Reproducibility concerns:** (1) TF-IDF retrieval depth and V=5 vote count are given, but the full critic prompt template and JSON parsing/repair logic are not in the main text — only Appendix B references serving/decoding details. (2) ReAct agent framework with action generation is standard, but exact state-summary truncation rules (DOM snippet size, terminal output window) are underspecified. (3) Stream order matters (Figure 3 shows large early-prefix variance), yet the main Table 1 numbers do not report variance or which stream order was used — only the self-evolving figure is averaged over 5 orders. (4) No code release is mentioned in the body.
- **Baseline caveats:** Verbalization and PRO generate their *own* actions jointly with the confidence signal and are scored on those actions, whereas CEB and the post-hoc scorers (CoT, P(True), PPL, ME) score the *same* frozen agent actions. The paper flags this, but it does mean CEB's favorable comparison against Verbalization/PRO is partially apples-to-oranges on the action side; the cleaner comparison is CEB vs CoT/P(True)/PPL/ME on identical actions, where CEB's margin remains large.
- **Data quirk:** InterCode-Bash labels are reward-derived ("the action that increases the reward is the productive action"), which the authors themselves note "may have some noise" — and InterCode-Bash is exactly the substrate where the headline 54% ECE reduction is reported. Mind2Web and AMEX use reference-matching labels that the authors admit "can be conservative toward alternative valid paths," motivating the LLM-as-judge re-evaluation.
- **Overclaiming check:** Mostly restrained. The selective-execution study is explicitly a replay proxy, not a closed-loop replanning policy ("This does not claim a closed-loop replanning policy"). The 95%-of-oracle figure is headline-grabbing but only on Mind2Web; AMEX closes only 61%.
- **Red flags:** Same backbone used as both critic and hindsight labeler (the paper flags this in Limitations and does not isolate an independent labeler) — pseudo-label quality could be inflated by shared model biases. No bank eviction/compression, so scaling claims to long deployment streams are unvalidated.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated problem formulation that distinguishes step-level agent confidence from response-level LLM confidence.
- Training-free and ground-truth-free — directly deployable in the fixed-API regime where process reward models and step verifiers are unusable.
- Contrastive, state-conditioned retrieval is a principled design, and the ablation actually supports each component (not just generic longer-prompt gains).
- Comprehensive evaluation: 9 cells, two label types, ablations, self-evolving curve, oracle headroom, and two downstream utility studies (selective execution + task success under review budget).
- Honest about limitations (same-model labeler, label noise on InterCode-Bash, no bank compression) and about what the selective-execution proxy does and does not claim.

## Limitations

- Same backbone serves as critic and hindsight labeler; the effect of an independent labeler is not isolated.
- Step labels ultimately still depend on reference annotations or LLM-as-judge relabeling; InterCode-Bash reward-derived labels are noisy (and drive the headline 54% number).
- No bank eviction or compression — untested on streams much larger than the ~200–1000 trajectories evaluated.
- Main Table 1 does not report variance or stream-order sensitivity (only Figure 3 is averaged over orders).
- Critic prompt template, JSON repair, and exact state-summary truncation rules are relegated to appendix / underspecified, hurting reproducibility.
- Closed-loop intervention (live replanning / human-in-the-loop) is simulated via replay, not actually run end-to-end.

## Relevance

对研究 LLM agent 可靠性与可校准 confidence / abstention 的方向高度相关：CEB 提供了一个无需训练、可在固定 API 模型上即插即用的 step-level 置信度估计框架，与 selective prediction、human-in-the-loop review、过程奖励建模等课题形成对照，可作为 agent 部署阶段可靠性评估的新基线。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does CEB behave with an *independent* (stronger or weaker) hindsight labeler than the critic backbone — does pseudo-label quality scale with labeler capability, and at what capability gap does the bank become net harmful?
- Does the bank saturate, and what eviction / compression / clustering policy preserves calibration on streams 10–100× larger than evaluated?
- Can the retrieval key move beyond TF-IDF (e.g., embeddings of rendered DOM / screenshots for mobile and web) without losing the training-free property?
- How does CEB interact with a *closed-loop* replanner or human reviewer that actually consumes the low-confidence steps, rather than the replay proxy used here?
- Since the same-model critic+labeler setup may share systematic biases, would ensembling multiple hindsight labelers (or self-consistency over labeler prompts) materially reduce the residual gap to the GT oracle?
- Can the agreement-flag signal be used not just as context but as an explicit reweighting of retrieved precedents?
