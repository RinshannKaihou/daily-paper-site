> **一句话总结：** 本文以 Qwen3-VL (4B/8B) 在 MiniWoB 上跑出 18 组受控 GRPO 配置，发现在已掌握的任务上没有任何配置可信地超过有监督基线，且中等学习率会损毁、高学习率会击穿智能体；同一 recipe 在有"采样>贪心"余量的任务上却可信地涨 22 个百分点，并揭示了 4B 模型中晚期层 effective rank 与能力耦合、在 8B 失效的机制。

> **TL;DR:** In a tightly controlled study, GRPO on a supervised Qwen3-VL 4B/8B web agent (MiniWoB, 11-task grid, 55 matched episodes per run, 18-run LR/KL/seed/init/clip grid) fails to credibly beat the supervised baseline (49.1%) on already-mastered tasks — no arm clears a task-clustered paired interval, and moderate LRs (1e-5) degrade success by −15.0pp while high LRs (2e-5) collapse it to 0.0% (McNemar p<0.001). The identical harness/reward/recipe instead climbs +22.0pp (20.0%→42.0%, CI [+8,+40], p=0.007) on tasks whose sampled policy already beats greedy, framing the null as a headroom condition rather than a broken pipeline; weight grafting causally localizes the degrade lesion to attention/MLP blocks while the dominant embedding drift is causally inert.

## 核心贡献

- **受控零结果：** 在 4B 文本轨道上以 18 组网格（学习率、KL 权重、种子、初始化、clip 边界）+ 配对 McNemar 检验 + 任务聚类自助法证明，没有任何 GRPO 配置能可信地超过有监督基线；五组最强配置中四组在 ±5pp 内与基线统计等价。
- **正向对照锁定病因为"任务余量"而非流水线损坏：** 用完全相同的 harness/reward/recipe 换成"采样成功率>贪心"的任务集，成功率从 20.0% 可信提升至 42.0%（+22pp，区间 [+8,+40]），证明零结果源于任务本身缺乏 GRPO 可攀爬的方向。
- **学习率门控的双分离机制：** 中等学习率（1e-5）破坏晚期层 effective rank 而高学习率（2e-5）破坏 readout；grafting 把"degrade"病变因果定位到 attention/MLP 块（恢复 frontier 11.4%→37.1%/40.0%，超过随机零假设 p95=22.9%），而漂移最大的 embedding 因果无效。
- **4B 与 8B 的尺度边界：** 4B 时晚期层 effective rank 与能力双向耦合（climb 保持 rank 7.1、degrade rank 崩到 3.2），8B 时 rank 与 argmax 一致性从不同时下降，因此 rank 账户是 4B 特性而非普适规律。
- **实践筛选器与统计严谨性：** 提出 ∆head=SR_sample−SR_greedy 作为部署前零成本筛选器；配套完整配对统计、TOST 等价检验、25 评估种子、6 训练种子、checkpoint 选择、失败模式分类与可解释性测量全部开源。

## Key Contributions

- A **controlled null**: across an 18-run grid (learning rate, KL weight, seed, initialization, clip bound) on the 4B text track with episode-matched paired testing, no GRPO configuration credibly beats the supervised baseline (49.1% on the 11-task MiniWoB grid); four of the five strongest arms are statistically equivalent within ±5pp via two one-sided tests.
- A **positive control** attributing the null to a **headroom condition**: the same harness, reward, and recipe, rerun on ten tasks where sampled success already exceeds greedy success, gains a credible +22.0pp (20.0%→42.0%, task-clustered CI [+8,+40], McNemar p=0.007), refuting the "broken pipeline" reading.
- A **mechanistic, learning-rate-gated double dissociation**, causally localized by weight grafting: the degrade regime (LR 1e-5) destroys late-layer effective rank and is repaired by restoring attention or MLP from init (11.4%→37.1%/40.0%, above a magnitude-matched random null p95 of 22.9%), while the collapse regime (LR 2e-5) spares rank, destroys the readout, and cannot be repaired by any single group — and the embedding, whose drift dominates raw weight movement, is causally inert.
- A **scale boundary**: at 4B late-layer effective rank tracks capability in both directions (climb keeps rank 7.1, degrade collapses it to 3.2); at 8B rank and argmax agreement never fall together across the LR sweep, so the rank-capability coupling is reported as 4B-specific.
- A **practitioner-facing screen** (∆head = SR_sample − SR_greedy) that predicts both the null and the climb from the supervised policy alone, plus a released battery of paired statistics, equivalence tests, and interpretability measurements for reproducibility.

## Methodology

The study fixes a single agent, environment, reward, and task set so that any success-rate change traces to the RL choices. The agent is **Qwen3-VL at 4B and 8B** running on **MiniWoB**, emitting one action per step from a compact vocabulary (clicks, typing, selection, navigation, finish). Two observation tracks share all other logic: a **text track** (serialized page) used for the grid and mechanism, and a **Set-of-Marks (SoM) screenshot track** used as a modality check. The reward is a sparse binary terminal signal r(τ)=1[success(τ)]∈{0,1}.

The GRPO recipe follows Liu et al. (2025) with group-mean-centered advantages (no std division) and a DAPO-style clip-higher bound (ϵlo=0.20 < ϵhi=0.28), plus an optional k3 KL anchor to the frozen reference. The controlled null is an **18-run grid** over learning rate (3e-6 to 2e-5), KL weight β, seed, initialization (SFT vs base), and clip bound, with a recipe-ablation battery adding warmup+cosine schedule and group size G∈{8,16,32}.

Evaluation is **clean greedy decoding** on the 11-task grid at 5 seeds (55 matched episodes), scored with Wilson score intervals and compared against the supervised baseline via an **exact McNemar test** on discordant pairs plus a **task-clustered bootstrap** 95% interval on ∆SR=SR_arm−SR_SFT; an arm is "credible" only if the interval excludes zero, and equivalence is asserted via a **two one-sided tests (TOST)** within ±δ. Interpretability uses a fixed cache of N=1999 hidden states, reading layer-35 effective rank (Roy & Vetterli 2007), participation ratio, argmax agreement with initialization, entropy, and degeneracy; causality is established by **weight grafting** (restore one component group — attention, MLP, or embedding — from initialization and re-measure recovery), benchmarked against a magnitude-matched random-group null over 24 draws.

## Results & Highlights

- On the mastered 11-task grid (4B text), the supervised baseline scores **49.1%**; the best arm (SFT init, LR 3e-6) reaches only **52.7%, a +3.6pp nominal gain whose task-clustered CI is [+0.0, +10.9] (McNemar p=0.50)** — i.e., indistinguishable from noise; the base-init best gives the same +3.6pp.
- Learning rate is the only dial that moves success and it only moves it downward: **LR 1e-5 degrades SR to 33.3% (−15.0pp, CI [−20.9,−9.5], p≤0.04)** and **LR 2e-5 collapses SR to 0.0% (−49.1pp, CI [−62.7,−36.4], p<0.001)**; the KL rescue arm at 2e-5 stays episode-identical to supervision (0/0 discordant pairs).
- The null is robust across axes: 25 evaluation seeds lower the baseline to 44.7% with no credible winner; a 6-seed replication of the nominal-best arm averages **49.7% (95% CI [47.7, 51.7])**, on baseline, so the +5.1pp single-seed edge was training-seed noise; SoM track raises baseline to 63.6% with no credible gain; 8B repeats the pattern.
- **Positive control**: on ten headroom tasks (50 episodes), the same recipe climbs the supervised baseline from **20.0% to 42.0% (+22.0pp, task-clustered CI [+8.0,+40.0], McNemar p=0.007)**; five of six configurations rise (four credibly), and the LR that was a no-op on the mastered grid gains +12 to +18pp here.
- **Mechanism via grafting** (degrade regime, frontier subset): restoring **attention alone lifts frontier SR 11.4%→37.1%** and **MLP alone →40.0%**, both above the magnitude-matched random null (mean 19.3%, p95 22.9%), while restoring the **embedding →14.0%** (near as-is), localizing the lesion to compute pathways and clearing the embedding despite its dominant drift.
- Late-layer (L35) effective rank reads: base 7.0, SFT 9.2, no-op 10.1, degrade **1.2**, collapse **13.9** — a rank inversion stable across the full 1999-state cache; on the positive-control family, the climbing checkpoint holds rank at **7.14** (42% SR) while the degrading checkpoint collapses to **3.21** (12% SR), so rank tracks capability both ways at 4B.
- A distillation pilot (5 tasks, 6 seeds, n=30): expert supervision lifts the base model **50%→80%** while GRPO on top of any initialization adds nothing.

The authorship spans independent research (Chengguang Gan, corresponding via chengguangg1024@gmail.com), Monash University (Zhixi Cai), University of Chinese Academy of Sciences (Yunhao Liang, Hanjun Wei), Shenzhen University of Advanced Technology (Shiwen Ni), and Pusan National University (Qinghao Zhang). The work is methodologically careful in a way unusual for RL-for-agents papers: it pairs a null with a matched positive control, uses episode-matched exact McNemar tests and task-clustered bootstrap CIs (not bare means), pre-registers equivalence via TOST, runs seed replications to expose lucky-seed effects, and stress-tests the verdict against checkpoint selection and a reward-hacking taxonomy (premature finish near 0% in every regime). The statistical apparatus and the honest scoping of claims (text-track vs SoM, 4B vs 8B) read as a genuine attempt to falsify the null rather than to sell a result. No external funding or affiliation with a frontier lab is declared, which is consistent with the "independent" framing but also means there is no institutional compute disclosure.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (genuine):** This is, to the authors' knowledge and plausibly correctly, the first study to pair a *controlled* RL null for a web agent (not a reasoning benchmark pass@k debate) with a causal, weight-level mechanism. The framing of GRPO's limit as a *headroom* condition (∆head>0) and the grafting-based causal localization are fresh contributions, as is the explicit 4B-vs-8B scale boundary on the rank-capability coupling.
- **Rigor (strong, with caveats):** Paired McNemar + task-clustered bootstrap + TOST equivalence + 6-seed replication + reward-hacking taxonomy + best-checkpoint re-test is well above the field norm. However, several quantitative mechanism claims rest on **single checkpoints per regime with noisy point estimates** (the paper itself flags this: "rest on a small number of checkpoints per regime and their point estimates are noisy, so we report them qualitatively"), and the grafting recoveries are over only ~35 episodes. The frontier-subset gain that is used to argue "headroom exists but isn't captured" (+5.7pp, CI lower bound 28.0 < baseline point 37.1) is itself not credible, which slightly undercuts the "there *is* headroom" sub-argument.
- **Reproducibility (good):** Frozen result tables, released paired-statistics/equivalence/interpretability battery, exact recipes, and publicly available backbones (Qwen3-VL) and benchmark (MiniWoB) make reproduction feasible. Missing: training-seed variance is reported for only one configuration (Table 10), and grafting is not seeded across many draws for every regime.
- **Significance:** Practitioner-relevant — the ∆head screen is a cheap, deployable pre-check, and the "spend on supervision/distillation, not RL, for a competent agent" recommendation is actionable. The mechanism (attention/MLP-localized degrade, distributed readout collapse, inert embedding drift) is a concrete, falsifiable contribution to RLVR interpretability.
- **Red flags / overclaiming to watch:** The title's "Failure of GRPO" is fairly earned *only* on the 4B text track on mastered tasks; the credible-harm claim is explicitly **text-track only** (SoM harm is nominal, p=0.070), and the rank mechanism **does not transfer to 8B**. A careless reader could over-generalize the null to "GRPO doesn't help web agents," which the authors are careful to disclaim ("we make no claim that reinforcement learning is useless for web agents in general"). Single-benchmark (MiniWoB) scope is a deliberate control tradeoff but limits external validity vs noisier-but-deployment-realistic WebArena.

## Strengths

- A genuine controlled null paired with a matched positive control that isolates the cause (headroom) from a broken pipeline — a design most RL papers skip.
- Causal, not correlational, mechanism: weight grafting with a magnitude-matched random-component null is the right tool and overturns the naive "embedding drifted most, so it's the culprit" reading.
- Statistically literate: episode-matched paired tests, task clustering, TOST equivalence, and a training-seed replication that exposes a +5.1pp lucky-seed artifact.
- Honest scoping: explicit text-track-vs-SoM and 4B-vs-8B boundaries, and an admission that mechanism point estimates are noisy and reported qualitatively.
- Actionable practitioner output: the ∆head screen and the "supervision/distillation over RL for competent agents" guidance, backed by a 50%→80% distillation pilot.

## Limitations

- **Single benchmark (MiniWoB) and single model family (Qwen3-VL 4B/8B):** chosen for mechanistic control, but the null and mechanism may differ on WebArena or other backbones; external validity is bounded.
- **Credible harm is a 4B text-track finding only:** on Set-of-Marks the high-rate degradation is nominal (p=0.070, CI touches zero), so the "GRPO subtracts skill" claim is not universal across observation modalities.
- **Mechanism rests on few checkpoints** per regime with noisy point estimates (over ~35 episodes for grafting, single checkpoints for the rank-capability coupling), reported qualitatively rather than with tight CIs.
- **Rank-capability coupling breaks at 8B:** the central mechanistic narrative is 4B-specific; the 8B behavior (rank collapse without behavior loss, or agreement collapse with rank preserved) is acknowledged but not explained.
- **Null concerns only the mastered-task regime:** the positive control shows the same recipe climbs when headroom exists, so the result should not be read as "RL is useless for web agents."
- **No large-scale / longer-training probe:** it is possible that far longer RL or different reward shaping would change the verdict on mastered tasks, though the headroom argument suggests the direction (toward sampled, not past greedy) is fundamentally bounded.

## Relevance

对关注"RLVR/GRPO 是否真正为智能体增加能力还是仅锐化已有行为"这一核心问题的研究者高度相关：它把推理任务上 pass@k 的 sharpening 之争带到交互式 web 智能体，并给出受控零结果 + 因果机制 + 可部署的 ∆head 预筛器，直接回答了"小模型已掌握任务上要不要继续投入 RL"的工程决策。对做 LLM/VLM 智能体后训练、机制可解释性（effective rank、weight grafting）以及 RL 与监督/蒸馏边界的人来说都是值得精读的负结果研究。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the headroom condition (∆head>0) generalize beyond MiniWoB — e.g., on WebArena or tool-use/OS-world agents where greedy-vs-sampled gaps and reward noise differ?
- Why does the rank-capability coupling break at 8B? Is the 8B model genuinely absorbing rank collapses via redundancy, or is a different layer/probe (not L35) where capability lives at that scale?
- Would a denser or shaped reward (instead of sparse binary terminal success) change the verdict on mastered tasks, or does the GRPO-only-climbs-toward-sampled argument hold regardless of reward shape?
- Can the grafting-localized attention/MLP lesion be turned into a *preventive* intervention (e.g., per-block LR scaling or selective freezing) that preserves the no-op regime while allowing gains elsewhere?
- How does the result interact with online curriculum RL (e.g., WebRL), which continuously shifts the task distribution toward headroom tasks — does that sidestep the null by construction?
- Is the "embedding drift is causally inert" finding stable under different KL anchors or reference choices, or does it depend on the k3 estimator and the frozen-init anchor used here?
