> **一句话总结：** 本文发现多模态 LLM 中隐藏状态探针（probe）路由因视觉输入稀释了正确性信号而显著退化，并提出两种互补方法——基于注意力的 Attention Probe 聚合跨 token 的分布式信号，以及 ReLope 通过 LoRA 适配 + KL/VIB 正则学习"路由感知"表示；在 5 个多模态基准 + 3 个 MLLM 上，ReLope 平均 AUC 达 88.21%（Qwen2.5-VL-7B），较最强 probe 基线提升约 5.15 个百分点，且延迟开销仅 +1.4%。

> **TL;DR:** Probe-based routing, effective for text-only LLMs, degrades sharply under multimodal inputs because visual tokens dilute correctness cues in the last-token hidden state (confirmed via HSIC/CKA representation analysis). The authors propose the **Attention Probe** (learns a token-level attention aggregation over frozen hidden states) and **ReLope** (inserts a LoRA adapter + variational information bottleneck to learn routing-aware features). On 5 benchmarks (MMMU, A-OKVQA, ScienceQA, ChartQA, MathVision) × 3 backbones (Qwen2.5-VL-7B, Gemma3-12B, Phi-4-MM), ReLope raises average routing AUC to 88.21% on Qwen (+5.15 over the Probe baseline at 83.06, and beats BEST-Route 82.81 / RouteLLM 77.02), with only +1.4 ms (+1.4%) latency and +0.11 GB over a standard Probe.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示多模态探针路由的根本失效模式**：通过 AUC 对比（ScienceQA 文本 95.36 vs 多模态 83.95）与 HSIC/CKA 表示分析，证明视觉输入会稀释/分散最后 token 隐藏状态中的正确性信号——而非仅仅是探针优化失败。这是该问题首次被系统性地从"表示层面"而非"准确率层面"刻画。
- **提出 Attention Probe**：用单个可学习路由查询向量 q 对上一层 token 隐藏状态做注意力加权聚合（backbone 冻结），恢复被最后 token 状态稀释掉的分布式正确性信号。
- **提出 ReLope**：在选定 transformer 层插入 LoRA 适配器（rank=128, α=256）重塑隐藏表示，再经变分信息瓶颈（对角高斯后验 + KL 正则到 N(0,I)）压缩，迫使特征只保留与正确性相关的信息。这是首个"主动改造隐藏状态"而非"在固定状态上训探针"的路由范式。
- **完整的效率与鲁棒性刻画**：端到端延迟仅 +1.4 ms (+1.4%)、+0.11 GB；在图像扰动（高斯噪声/JPEG/模糊）下 ∆AUC 仅 1.44（Probe 为 5.30）；在 leave-one-dataset-out OOD 上 4/5 数据集最强。
- **诚实消融**：LoRA-only 与 VIB-only 单独均有效，组合最优，直接把每个设计选择与观察到的失效模式关联起来。

## Key Contributions

- **Diagnoses the representation-level failure of probe routing in MLLMs.** Beyond showing AUC drops (ScienceQA text-only 95.36 vs multimodal 83.95), the authors run a *classifier-free* matched intervention measuring HSIC and CKA between the last-token state and the correctness label under caption / raw-image / blank-image inputs. Caption-based inputs consistently expose the strongest dependence, raw multimodal weakens it, and blank images are worse still — confirming the signal is dispersed, not absent.
- **Attention Probe.** Replaces the implicit next-token-optimized aggregation with a learned token-level attention pooling over the previous layer's hidden states, with a single shared query vector `q` trained jointly with the probe while the MLLM backbone stays frozen. Recovers distributed routing cues that the final token dilutes.
- **ReLope (KL-Regularized LoRA Probe).** Inserts a LoRA adapter at layer `l` to reshape hidden states, maps the adapted last-token feature `z_L` through two heads to `(μ, log σ²)` defining a diagonal-Gaussian bottleneck, samples `z_B` (reparameterized at train time, `μ` at inference), and trains the probe on `z_B` with BCE + KL-to-`N(0,I)`. The VIB objective explicitly suppresses task-irrelevant visual variation.
- **Consistent, efficient, and robust gains.** Across 5 benchmarks × 3 MLLMs, ReLope wins every cell of Table 2, adds only +1.4 ms / +0.11 GB end-to-end (Table 3), is the most robust to image perturbations (Table 5: ∆AUC 1.44 vs Probe 5.30), and best on 4/5 leave-one-dataset-out OOD targets (Table 6).
- **Mechanism-aligned ablations.** Table 4 isolates each component (LoRA-only, VIB-only, both), tying LoRA to improved separability and VIB to suppression of unhelpful variation.

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** A hybrid system pairs a small MLLM `f_s` (on-device) with a larger, more capable MLLM `f_l` (cloud). The router `r(x) ∈ {0,1}` must predict whether `f_s`'s answer will be correct (`Y=1`); if not, escalate to `f_l`. Routing = binary correctness prediction on the small model.

**Standard probe (baseline).** A 5-layer MLP `g_φ` is trained on the last-token hidden state `z_n^(l)` at a chosen layer `l` to output `P(Y=1)` via sigmoid. Works well for text-only LLMs but degrades with images (Table 1).

**Diagnosis (Sec. 3.3).** Two pieces of evidence:
1. AUC drops on MMMU (81.51→76.51), A-OKVQA (87.50→82.03), ScienceQA (95.36→83.95) when images are present, even on caption-converted text-only twins.
2. A matched, *classifier-free* intervention measuring HSIC and normalized HSIC (CKA) between `z_n^(l)` and `Y` under three visual conditions: original image, GPT-generated caption replacing the image, and blank image. Caption inputs always show the strongest dependence; raw multimodal is weaker; blank image is weakest. Conclusion: visual tokens dilute routing-relevant signal in the last-token state.

**Attention Probe (Sec. 4.1).** Uses `Z^(l-1)` (previous layer's token states). Computes attention weights `ω_i = softmax_i(qᵀ z_i^(l-1) / √d)` with one learnable query `q`, pools to `z_A = Σ ω_i z_i^(l-1)`, and feeds `z_A` to the probe. Backbone frozen; only `(q, φ)` trained.

**ReLope (Sec. 4.2).** Inserts LoRA into layer `l` to produce adapted `Ẑ^(l)` and adapted last-token `z_L`. Two linear heads map `z_L → (μ, log σ²)`. Bottleneck feature `z_B ~ N(μ, diag(σ²))` (reparameterized in training, `z_B = μ` at inference). Probe predicts from `z_B`. Loss: `L = E[BCE(y, ŷ(z_B))] + β·D_KL(q_ψ(z_B|z_L) ‖ N(0,I))`, with `β = 1.0`. Following VIB, the KL term forces compression that retains correctness-relevant information and discards irrelevant multimodal noise.

**Training details.** PyTorch + transformers + peft; AdamW lr 1e-4; probe is a 5-layer MLP; layer `l=24` for Qwen2.5-VL-7B and Phi-4-MM (32 layers), `l=40` for Gemma3-12B (48 layers); LoRA rank 128, α 256; A100-80GB. Correctness labels are *rule-based* (option match for MCQ; relaxed exact match for ChartQA; symbolic equivalence for MathVision) — **no judge model** is used. Hybrid-system large model is GPT-4.1 (GPT-5.1 for MathVision).

## Results & Highlights

- **Probe routing collapses under multimodal inputs (Table 1, Qwen2.5-VL-7B).** Routing AUC drops from text-only → multimodal: MMMU 81.51 → 76.51 (−5.0), A-OKVQA 87.50 → 82.03 (−5.47), ScienceQA 95.36 → 83.95 (−11.41).
- **Main result (Table 2, Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct, Avg over 5 benchmarks).** ReLope = **88.21** vs Probe 83.06 (+5.15), vs Attention Probe 84.44 (+3.77), vs BEST-Route 82.81 (+5.40), vs RouteLLM 77.02 (+11.19), vs MoT 73.98 (+14.23). Per-dataset on Qwen: MMMU ReLope 80.89 vs Probe 76.51; A-OKVQA 86.17 vs 82.03; ScienceQA 91.79 vs 83.95; ChartQA 86.51 vs 82.51; MathVision 95.68 vs 90.28.
- **Backbone generalization (Table 2).** ReLope improves over Probe by +9.48 pp on Gemma3-12B (74.60 → 84.08) and +9.32 pp on Phi-4-Multimodal-Instruct (76.61 → 85.93), winning every cell of the 3×5 grid.
- **Latency / memory overhead (Table 3, MMMU, Qwen2.5-VL-7B, A100-80GB, fp16, batch 4).** Probe 97.6 ms/sample → ReLope 99.0 ms (+1.4 ms = +1.4%) and 16.89 → 17.00 GB (+0.11 GB). Trainable params: Probe 0.96M → ReLope 29.54M (~30× more, but dominated by the backbone forward pass).
- **Component ablation (Table 4, Qwen2.5-VL-7B, layer 24).** A-OKVQA AUC: Probe 82.03 → LoRA-only 85.12 → VIB-only 84.45 → ReLope (both) 86.17. MMMU: 76.51 → 78.00 → 78.97 → 80.89. Both components contribute; combination is best.
- **Robustness to image perturbations (Table 5, A-OKVQA).** Mean AUC drop from clean to {noise, JPEG, blur}: Probe ∆AUC 5.30, Attention Probe 2.69, **ReLope 1.44** — the bottleneck suppresses task-irrelevant visual variation.
- **Leave-one-dataset-out OOD (Table 6).** ReLope is best probe-based method and top on 4/5 targets (MMMU 76.66, A-OKVQA 83.68, ScienceQA 81.58, ChartQA 75.57); on MathVision the non-learned MoT baseline (82.59) is substantially stronger than all learned routers (ReLope 66.83), exposing a math-vision domain-shift weakness.
- **Stability (3 seeds, MMMU).** ReLope 80.79 ± 0.40 vs Probe 76.61 ± 0.20.
- **Parameter sensitivity (Figure 4).** AUC improves with LoRA rank up to 128 then plateaus; intermediate/later layers give stronger routing signals; `β ∈ [0.5, 1.0]` is optimal.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: high.** The framing — "visual inputs dilute correctness cues in the last-token state, so we must *reshape* hidden states for routing rather than just probe them" — is a genuinely fresh angle on MLLM routing. The Attention Probe is a modest, well-motivated change; ReLope's combination of LoRA + VIB for routing-specific representation learning is novel relative to the Probe/Post-Hoc Embed line.
- **Rigor: strong.** The matched HSIC/CKA intervention is the right control: it sidesteps the "is it the probe or the representation?" confound that the AUC-only comparison leaves open. The component ablation (Table 4) cleanly attributes gains to LoRA (separability) and VIB (noise suppression).
- **Reproducibility: good.** Public code, named layer indices (l=24 for 32-layer models, l=40 for Gemma3-12B), LoRA rank 128 / α 256 / β 1.0, rule-based correctness labels (no proprietary judge model). The main risk is compute access (A100-80GB) and the paid GPT-4.1 / GPT-5.1 escalation backend.
- **Significance: moderate-to-high.** Routing is practically important, and the MLLM setting (where inference is genuinely expensive due to visual tokens) is the right place to attack it. If the result holds up on smaller on-device MLLMs and on production traffic, the impact is real.
- **Red flags / weak points (paper-specific):**
  - **MathVision OOD failure (Table 6).** On the leave-one-out MathVision target, the *non-learned* MoT baseline (82.59) crushes every learned router including ReLope (66.83). The authors flag this honestly, but it materially bounds the generalization claim — learned routing-aware features struggle precisely on the hard math-vision reasoning that is most in need of escalation.
  - **Parameter overhead vs. stated motivation.** The whole premise is an "on-device small model" routing to the cloud, yet ReLope adds 29.54M trainable params (≈30× the 0.96M Probe). The authors correctly note the backbone forward pass dominates wall-clock latency (+1.4%), but for true on-device memory budgets the 0.11 GB / 29.5M delta is not free and is only measured on the 7B model, not on the smaller models the framing implicitly targets.
  - **Narrow backbone and benchmark coverage (self-acknowledged Limitation §7).** Only 3 MLLM families (Qwen2.5-VL-7B, Gemma3-12B, Phi-4-MM), all in the 7–12B range; no truly small (<4B) or open-weights-frontier model tested. Sensitivity to probe layer (Figure 4b) is reported only for Qwen2.5-VL-7B.
  - **Dependence on supervised correctness labels.** ReLope needs labeled correctness, which the authors concede limits new-domain scalability; no semi/weakly-supervised variant is explored.
  - **Cost proxy, not measurement.** Routing ratio is a stand-in for "deployment cost" with a paid API backend; actual dollar cost and the end-to-end system cost-quality frontier (incl. API latency) are not reported.
  - **Mechanism still partly black-box.** The paper shows *that* visual tokens dilute the signal (via HSIC/CKA), but explicitly notes (§7) that *why* probe methods fail in MLLMs "remains necessary" — the causal story (which layers/tokens carry the signal, how visual position-encoding interacts) is open.

## Strengths

- Principled, classifier-free diagnosis (HSIC/CKA) that isolates representation quality from probe-fitting artifacts.
- Two complementary methods whose roles are validated by a clean ablation, each tied to a specific failure mode.
- Wins every cell of a 3-backbone × 5-benchmark AUC table; gains are large (up to +9.48 pp avg).
- Negligible latency overhead and the strongest robustness to image perturbations.
- Honest, non-cherry-picked reporting: MathVision OOD weakness, parameter overhead, and label dependence are all disclosed.

## Limitations

- Learned routers (incl. ReLope) underperform the non-learned MoT baseline under leave-one-out MathVision domain shift — generalization to math-vision reasoning is an open problem.
- Only 7–12B backbones tested; the "on-device" framing is not validated on genuinely small models.
- ReLope requires supervised correctness labels and 29.54M extra trainable parameters, limiting scalability to new domains.
- Hybrid-system cost is proxied by routing ratio, not measured in dollars or end-to-end latency.
- No deep mechanistic explanation of *why* visual tokens dilute the correctness signal.

## Relevance

对关注 LLM/MLLM 高效推理、推理路由（routing / cascade）与表示学习的方向高度相关：ReLope 把"路由"从在固定隐藏状态上做预测，推进到"主动重塑表示以服务路由"，这条思路对多模态与推理任务的可信度估计、自适应计算分配都有直接借鉴价值，可作为后续 on-device 部署与 test-time scaling 研究的基础工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does ReLope transfer to genuinely small (<4B) on-device MLLMs, and does the 29.54M parameter / 0.11 GB overhead remain acceptable there?
- Can the MathVision OOD gap be closed — e.g., by routing-specific pretraining, or by combining ReLope with the consistency-based MoT signal it currently loses to?
- Which layers/tokens actually carry the diluted correctness signal, and is the dilution an artifact of vision-token positional encoding or of attention dilution across long visual prefixes?
- Can ReLope be trained without supervised correctness labels (e.g., self-consistency pseudo-labels, or unsupervised VIB on answer entropy)?
- How does ReLope interact with stronger escalation backends (open frontier models) and with actual dollar-cost / latency frontiers rather than routing-ratio proxies?
