> **一句话总结：** 作者构建了一个由多个虚拟实验室组成的 LLM 驱动"AI 科学共同体"（每室含规划/数值/评审三类 agent），用粒子群+引用经济的机制在五类 PDE（Advection、Burgers、Navier-Stokes、Darcy、分段回归）上自动发现神经算子架构；所有 9623 次 LLM 调用均被审计零回退，发现全部为多家族混合架构（如 Darcy 混合达 0.0631 相对 L2 误差，与 DeepONet 持平；NS 混合达 0.0005，与 FNO 相当但参数更少），并通过规则化对照消融证明 LLM agency 的价值在于保持架构多样性而非提升精度。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes an "AI scientific community" — a swarm of virtual labs (each with an LLM planner, a numerical worker, and an LLM reviewer) coordinated by a citation-based economy — to autonomously discover neural operator architectures over a shared vocabulary of DeepONet, Fourier, Transformer, wavelet, and residual-conv blocks. Evaluated on five PDE/regression problems (piecewise regression, linear advection, Burgers, 2D Navier-Stokes, 2D Darcy), every LLM-discovered winner was a multi-family hybrid: the Darcy hybrid reaches 0.0631 relative L2 error (statistically tied with DeepONet at 0.0649) and the Navier-Stokes hybrid reaches 0.0005 (on par with FNO at 0.0002, beating Transformer at 0.2624 and DeepONet at 0.1613) using ~1.1-1.3M parameters. All 9,623 LLM calls are audited with zero rule-based fallbacks. A controlled ablation replacing LLM planner/reviewer with rule-based agents (identical training budget, same seeds) shows LLM agency does NOT improve screening accuracy, but DOES preserve architectural diversity — rule-based collapses to canonical single-family stacks (pure Fourier on NS, wavelet-dominated on Darcy), and on NS the LLM hybrids match accuracy at 2.5x fewer parameters than the rule-based (2.2M vs 5.6M).

## 核心贡献

- **面向神经算子的智能体架构搜索框架**：首次将"AI 科学共同体"（群体智能 + 虚拟实验室集群）用于神经算子架构发现，每个虚拟实验室包含 LLM 规划者、数值 worker（梯度训练）、LLM 评审者三类 agent，共享 DeepONet/Fourier/Transformer/Wavelet/ResidualConv 五种算子积木。
- **基于引用经济+粒子群的生命周期**：高被引实验室获得繁殖权、未被引用者被替换；复合适应度由精度(0.70)+泛化(0.20)+参数效率(0.05)+新颖性(0.05)加权（精度不达标直接清零）。
- **完整 LLM agency 审计**：公开全部 9623 次 LLM 调用记录，零规则回退认证（gemma3:12b via Ollama，无付费 API，完全本地可复现）。
- **LLM agency 的对照消融**：用规则化 agent 替换 LLM 规划/评审（相同训练预算与随机种子），证明 LLM 价值在保持多样性（多家族混合）而非提升精度——规则化会坍缩到单家族解（NS 上纯 Fourier、Darcy 上小波主导）。
- **神经算子"没有免费午餐"实证**：跨 5 问题 30 次仿真，证实无单一架构普适最优，FNO 主宰周期/谱友好的 NS 问题，DeepONet 系在椭圆型 Darcy 问题胜出。

## Key Contributions

- **Agentic framework for neural operator discovery**: An "AI scientific community" combining swarm intelligence with virtual labs (planner + numerical worker + reviewer agents per lab) over a shared block vocabulary (BRANCH_TRUNK, FOURIER, ATTENTION, WAVELET, RESIDUAL_CONV), solving the bilevel architecture-search program via a citation-economy-driven particle swarm.
- **Citation-based lab lifecycle**: Well-cited labs breed and persist; uncited labs are replaced; composite fitness fuses accuracy (w=0.70), noise-robustness (0.20), parameter parsimony (0.05), and novelty (0.05) with an accuracy floor that zeros degenerate small/novel-only models.
- **Full LLM-call auditing**: All 9,623 LLM calls (system prompt, user prompt, raw response, role, parse status) are logged; Table 3 certifies zero rule-based fallbacks across 15 LLM runs (after switching from gemma4:e4b, which silently degraded, to gemma3:12b).
- **Controlled LLM-vs-rule-based ablation**: Identical swarm size (N=16), iteration count (T=20), training budget, fitness, and random seeds (42, 137, 2024) — only planner/reviewer policy differs. This cleanly isolates what LLM agency adds: architectural diversity, not accuracy.
- **Empirical no-free-lunch for neural operators**: Across 5 problems × 3 seeds, no architecture dominates; FNO wins the periodic spectral-friendly Navier-Stokes problem (0.0002), DeepONet wins the elliptic Darcy problem (0.0649), while discovered hybrids are competitive at lower parameter counts.

## Methodology

The paper frames operator architecture discovery as a bilevel program. The **inner problem** is standard gradient-based training of a fixed genome; the **outer problem** is solved by a swarm of N=16 virtual labs running T=20 iterations. A genome g = (block_sequence, hidden_channels, fourier_modes, activation, gating, skip_connections, dropout, learning_rate, weight_decay) is drawn from a fixed vocabulary of 5 operator block families. Each iteration has four phases: (1) Planning — each lab queries the LLM (gemma3:12b via Ollama, local, 8192-token context) for the next genome given its history, global best, and a swarm summary; (2) Training — the worker compiles and trains the genome at deliberately reduced "screening fidelity" (small data, few epochs) so each candidate trains in seconds and 320 trainings/swarm fit on one GPU; (3) Peer review — labs cast soft votes over a sample of peers, accumulating citation-like influence and trust scores; (4) Lifecycle — composite fitness updates the global best, influential labs breed (founding new labs that follow their research direction), uncited labs are deactivated.

The composite fitness is F = 0.70·accuracy + 0.20·generalization(noise robustness, Gaussian ε~N(0,0.1²)) + 0.05·parameter-efficiency + 0.05·novelty, zeroed when accuracy is below a floor. Weights are fixed a priori, identical across conditions, and not tuned. The numerical worker is deliberately NOT an LLM so fitness signals are comparable across labs. The LLM ablation swaps in rule-based planner (explore/exploit PSO updates) and reviewer (accuracy-proportional voting) sharing everything else.

## Results & Highlights

- **Darcy 2D (hardest)**: Discovered Darcy hybrid attains **0.0631 ± 0.0083 relative L2 error**, statistically tied with the best baseline DeepONet (0.0649 ± 0.0037), far better than FNO (0.3070 ± 0.0404) and Transformer (0.5207 ± 0.0102) — Darcy is elliptic, spectral methods fail here.
- **Navier-Stokes 2D (spectral-friendly)**: Discovered NS hybrid reaches **0.0005 ± 0.0000 relative L2 error** at 1.1M params, on par with FNO (0.0002 at 1.2M params), and well below every non-FNO baseline (POD-DeepONet 0.0033, DeepONet 0.1613, Transformer 0.2624).
- **LLM planner behavior**: Across 4,817 planner calls, the LLM chose HYBRIDIZE in **99.8%** of decisions (never EXPLOIT), producing multi-family hybrids in 100% of winners; rationales condition on swarm state ("several labs are converging on FNO... I'll introduce a hybrid approach by incorporating attention blocks").
- **Reviewer groundedness**: Across 4,804 review calls, Spearman rank correlation between reviewer vote and candidate accuracy is ρ̄ = **0.64** (positive in 88% of calls, perfect ordering in 50%); rationales cite accuracy 97%, efficiency 83%, robustness 71%, novelty 26% of the time.
- **Ablation — accuracy parity but architectural collapse**: Screening accuracies are statistically indistinguishable between LLM and rule-based on all five problems (within 1 s.d.); however on Navier-Stokes the LLM hybrids match accuracy at **2.5× fewer parameters** on average (2.2M vs 5.6M rule-based), where rule-based collapses to pure Fourier stacks (F-F-F-F, 5.1M–10.9M params) while LLM produces F-A-T-W-F type hybrids.
- **Computational overhead**: LLM coordination costs ~14 sec/lab-evaluation in 1D problems (19.4 vs 4.9 s) and 25–33s in 2D (51.3 vs 18.7s on NS), a 2–4× wall-clock increase over rule-based; full NS run = 270.9 ± 104.3 min vs 103.1 ± 6.7 min on a single RTX 4080.
- **Agent audit**: All 15 LLM runs show **0 rule-based fallbacks** with 100% parse success (e.g., 640/640 per run), certifying genuine LLM-driven planning/review.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Genuine. The combination of swarm intelligence + virtual labs + LLM-as-planner/reviewer over an operator-block genome is distinct from prior NAS (which uses RL/evolution/DARTS) and from EvoPrompting (which mutates code). The ablation design — pairing every LLM run with a rule-based arm under identical budget — is a real methodological strength that most agentic-AI papers omit.
- **Rigor**: Strong on ablation hygiene (shared swarm size, iteration count, training budget, fitness, and seeds), auditing (9,623 logged LLM calls, zero-fallback certification), and reproducibility (resume-safe runner, released transcripts, local model). The switch from gemma4:e4b to gemma3:12b after detecting silent fallbacks is candidly disclosed.
- **Significance of accuracy claims — must be read carefully**: The headline Table 1 (Darcy 0.0631 vs DeepONet 0.0649; NS 0.0005 vs FNO 0.0002) retrains only the two winning architectures and four baselines to convergence. The swarm itself screens at reduced fidelity, so absolute errors during search (Table 2, e.g. Darcy LLM 0.0095 ± 0.0003) are not comparable to the final retrained numbers (Darcy 0.0631). This fidelity mismatch is disclosed but easy to misread.
- **Honest negative result (red flag defuser)**: The authors explicitly state "we do not claim an accuracy win for LLM agency" — the screening accuracies in Table 2 are statistically indistinguishable, so the value-add is architectural diversity and parameter efficiency, not raw accuracy. This is intellectually honest but also somewhat deflates the headline framing.
- **Weak/cherry-picked points**: Only 5 problems (3 of them 1D toy problems — piecewise regression, advection, Burgers); the two 2D problems are the canonical FNO/DeepONet benchmarks where the expected winners are well known. No results on irregular geometries, time-dependent multi-step rollout, or 3D PDEs, so the "no-free-lunch" claim is narrow.
- **Missing ablations**: The planner and reviewer are ablated jointly, not factorially (acknowledged as a limitation); the contribution of LLM-as-reviewer alone is unknown. The fitness weights (0.70/0.20/0.05/0.05) are fixed and not swept. No comparison to other NAS or EvoPrompting baselines — only to four operator baselines and the internal rule-based arm.
- **Confounding factor (self-acknowledged)**: A mid-size LLM has read the operator-learning literature and may already "know" FNO suits periodic spectral-friendly problems — so this is not prior-free discovery. The rule-based arm provides the prior-free reference but cannot remove the LLM's literature priors.
- **Model scale**: Single local model (gemma3:12b). Whether GPT-4/Claude/frontier models change discovery quality is unknown.
- **Data quirk**: Darcy winner evaluated on NS gives 0.7127 (terrible); NS winner on Darcy is ~3.7× worse than the Darcy winner — the discovered architectures are problem-specific, not transferable, which slightly undercuts the "community discovers" framing.

## Strengths

- Methodologically clean ablation: identical budget, seeds, fitness, and swarm size isolate the LLM contribution.
- Full audit of 9,623 LLM calls with zero-fallback certification addresses a real and under-reported failure mode in agentic pipelines.
- 100% local (gemma3:12b via Ollama, RTX 4080), no paid API — strong reproducibility posture.
- Intellectually honest about the negative accuracy result; reframes LLM value as diversity preservation.
- Conceptual elegance: citation economy + breeding + lifecycle produces a self-organizing research community analog.

## Limitations

- Small benchmark scope: 5 problems, 3 of them 1D toys; no irregular domains, 3D, or long-horizon rollout.
- LLM is used as a policy, not as autonomous tool-using agents (no code execution, no external tool calls).
- Single LLM backend (gemma3:12b); frontier-model behavior untested.
- Joint planner+reviewer ablation, not factorial — individual role contributions unknown.
- Fixed, untuned fitness weights; adaptive weighting left to future work.
- LLM stochasticity: three seeds reported, no bit-for-bit reproducibility claimed.
- Screening fidelity means swarm-time errors are not directly comparable to final converged numbers.
- Literature-prior confound: the LLM is not prior-free.
- Discovered architectures are not transferable across problems (NS winner fails on Darcy and vice versa).

## Relevance

对神经网络算子（FNO/DeepONet 等）自动化架构搜索与 agentic AI for science 方向有直接参考价值；其"LLM 作为规划/评审策略 + 数值 worker 提供真实 fitness + 完整调用审计"的设计范式，以及"LLM agency 带来多样性而非精度提升"的诚实结论，对构建多智能体科研系统、自动化 PDE 求解器选型均有借鉴意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would a factorial ablation (LLM-planner-only, LLM-reviewer-only, both, neither) reveal that one role dominates the diversity-preservation effect?
- How do frontier LLMs (GPT-4-class) change the discovered architectures and the planner's HYBRIDIZE bias — is the 99.8% hybridization rate an artifact of gemma3:12b's training distribution?
- Can the prompt-induced "preserve diversity" instruction be replaced by an explicit action-space debiasing to separate prompt effects from intrinsic LLM behavior?
- Does the citation-economy breeding dynamic converge to a single research direction over much longer horizons (T >> 20)?
- How does the framework perform on harder regimes — irregular geometries, 3D PDEs, long-horizon time rollout, or stiff/chaotic systems where no single operator family is known to dominate?
- Would adaptive fitness weights (rather than the fixed 0.70/0.20/0.05/0.05) shift the accuracy-diversity trade-off materially?
- Can the discovered problem-specific hybrids be combined into a single transferable meta-architecture via a meta-learning outer loop?
