Critic Experience Bank: Self-Evolving Step-Level Confidence Estimation for LLM Agents

Yaopei Zeng · Congchao Wang · JianHang Chen · Nan Wang · Yurui Chang · Lu Lin (corresponding)
Pennsylvania State University · Virginia Tech · Purdue University · Amazon AGI
training-free ground-truth-free llm-agents confidence-estimation calibration retrieval-augmented self-evolving
◆ Key Finding

A training-free, self-evolving critic that augments a frozen LLM with hindsight-labeled memory wins every calibration cell across nine dataset–critic combinations.

Key Metric
54%
Relative ECE reduction vs. the strongest training-free baseline — reported on InterCode-Bash / GPT-4.1.
9/9 cells won 95% oracle gap closed (Mind2Web) k=2 top-k retrieval
Why This Matters
Training-free, plug-and-play step-level confidence for fixed-API LLM agents — unlocks selective execution and human-in-the-loop review without ground-truth labels or retraining.
Critical Edge
The headline 54% reduction is reported on InterCode-Bash, whose reward-derived step labels the authors themselves admit “may have some noise.”
01

Methodology — retrieval-augmented critic calibration

weights frozen · adaptation is purely contextual
1

Trajectory & scoring target

For a trajectory τ the critic emits ẑt∈[0,1] for proposed action a_t before execution — to support online defer / replan / human review. Confidence targets an execution event, not a response.

2

Hindsight pseudo-labeling

After the full run, a hindsight LLM g_ϕ sees the entire (s,a,o,q) and votes V=5 times at τ>0; majority vote yields a binary per-step productivity label — no ground-truth step labels needed.

3

Bank record structure

Records store task, compact state summary, action, observation, pseudo-label, prior critic score, and an agreement flag exposing prior over/under-confidence. Class-split into B+ / B− for explicit contrast.

4

Contrastive, state-conditioned retrieval

Composite key task ‖ state ‖ action, TF-IDF cosine similarity, top-k=2 from each non-empty bucket → R⁺t, R⁻t. A repetition flag catches short loops semantic similarity misses.

5

Critic prompt & scoring

Prompt = q, st, history, at, retrieved R±t, repetition flag; retrieved examples expose their execution feedback and past confidence. Critic returns a JSON score clipped to [0,1]. No weights change.

6

Crucially online

Trajectories processed in stream order; a trajectory enters the bank only after all its own actions are scored — no future feedback leaks into an online estimate.

Advantage holds under permissive labels that credit alternative valid paths — not an artifact of strict reference matching.
  • Component ablation (Mind2Web/GPT-5.4): removing the bank entirely (Static) costs ΔECE +0.142; dropping the contrastive split +0.065; TF-IDF→random +0.042; V=1 majority vote +0.028.
  • Selective execution @50% abstention: retained GT-positive rate 0.288 for CEB (+0.090) vs CoT +0.036; Mean-Entropy drops to 0.114 — actively misaligned.
  • Task success under review budget (m=2): CEB reaches 12.5% Mind2Web / 44.7% AMEX; oracle ceiling 21.2% / 60.7%.
  • Contrastive negatives + state-conditioned retrieval isolated as the primary drivers — not generic longer-prompt gains.

Strengths & limitations

side-by-side

Strengths

  • Clean problem formulation — distinguishes step-level agent confidence from response-level LLM confidence.
  • Training-free & ground-truth-free — directly deployable where process reward models and step verifiers are unusable.
  • Principled, ablation-backed design — contrastive state-conditioned retrieval actually earns its place.
  • Comprehensive evaluation — 9 cells, two label types, ablations, self-evolving curve, oracle headroom, two downstream studies.
  • Honest about same-model labeler, label noise, and what the selective-execution proxy does not claim.

Limitations

  • Same backbone as critic & hindsight labeler — effect of an independent labeler not isolated; pseudo-labels may share model bias.
  • Noisy InterCode-Bash labels are reward-derived — and drive the headline 54% number.
  • No bank eviction / compression — untested on streams ≫ the ~200–1000 trajectories evaluated.
  • Main Table 1 reports no variance or stream-order sensitivity (only Figure 3 is averaged over orders).
  • Reproducibility gaps — critic prompt template, JSON repair, state-summary truncation relegated to appendix.
  • Closed-loop intervention simulated via replay, not run end-to-end.
04

Questions for further exploration

open edges
Q1

How does CEB behave with an independent hindsight labeler — at what capability gap does the bank turn net harmful?

Q2

Does the bank saturate? What eviction / clustering policy preserves calibration on streams 10–100× larger?

Q3

Can retrieval move beyond TF-IDF (rendered-DOM / screenshot embeddings) without losing the training-free property?

Q4

How does it interact with a closed-loop replanner or human reviewer that actually consumes low-confidence steps?

Q5

Would ensembling multiple hindsight labelers reduce the residual gap to the GT oracle?

Q6

Can the agreement flag drive explicit reweighting of retrieved precedents, not just act as context?
