Attractor Geometry of Transformer Memory

Qiyao Liang (MIT) · Risto Miikkulainen (UT Austin / Cognizant) · Ila Fiete (MIT, McGovern)
Key finding

Conflict and hallucination are the same geometry — basin competition vs basin absence — and the frozen LM head is blind to both.

Key metric · margin AUROC
1.000
vs entropy 0.622 — pretrained factual queries, no adapter
A single scalar — hidden-state distance to the nearest memorized basin — separates correct recall from hallucination at zero false refusals on the 100%-catch threshold.
Why this matters

Geometric margin detects hallucinations entropy misses — and it sharpens with scale while entropy degrades.

Critical edge

Margin AUROC is near-ceiling only on a 5-digit-code task with 1,600 entities; the headline numbers depend on a robust multi-template training regime, not on brittle single-template PM.

01

Methodology — a causal probe, not a metaphor

Synthetic ground truth

Entity → 5-digit code

1,600 entities (E000000–E001599) each map to a unique code drawn from 90,000 strings — chance overlap only 1.76%, so "hallucination" is unambiguous. Installed on Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct (d=2048, 36 layers).

Causal probe

Targeted rank-8 LoRA placement

Identical hyperparameters, only the target module differs: QK, VO, MLP, or Full. Placement dissociates routing from readout from basin-carving — an intervention, not a correlation.

Geometry signal

Margin δ(x) = mini‖h(x) − mi‖₂

Distance from the hidden state to the nearest basin center. PM = persistent MLP-carved basins; WM = transient attention steering; the frozen head reads only logit gaps and erases basin occupancy.

Verification

Jacobian S/A decomposition

J = S + A on exact 2048×2048 Jacobians of the pretrained model. MLP dominates ‖S‖²F by 25× (basin shaping); VO symmetry φ≈0.59 peaks at layer 15 (Hopfield readout); QK is unstructured (routing). Roles are intrinsic, not fine-tuning artifacts.

02

The scaling law — why bigger models hallucinate more confidently

C = exp(−c / Δ̄)

The confident-hallucination fraction rises with scale even as the raw hallucination rate H ∝ N−0.27 falls. Parameter-free fit across 21 (model, benchmark) points, 4 families, 0.36B–14B — r² = 0.88. Derived from softmax saturation + logit gap ∝ N1/3. Undetectable errors U = H·C grow from <0.5% (sub-1B) to ~18% (14B).

03

Results — margin vs entropy across regimes

LoRA task · margin AUROC 0.993 vs entropy 0.968

Per-condition margins separate cleanly: PM-seen δ≈22, format-generalization δ≈34.9, conflict δ≈102.2, hallucination δ≈133.1.

100%-catch threshold · margin keeps 100% of correct recalls

Entropy must refuse 99.5% of correct recalls. ~13% of hallucinations emit near-zero entropy, indistinguishable to entropy but separated by margin.

Pretrained knowledge · 196 queries · margin 1.000 vs entropy 0.622

No LoRA adapter. Correct-recall margin 44.7 vs hallucination 87.5; margin preserves 149/149 correct vs entropy's 16/149.

TruthfulQA · 817 Q · margin 0.858 vs entropy 0.656

Near-1.000 on factual subcategories (Misconceptions 0.999; Law/Health/Society 1.000). Cross-domain transfer from capitals fails (0.476) — bounds the framework to basin-absence errors.

Circuit dissociation · MLP & Full adapters → 100% PM, 0% WM

"Context deafness." QK-only keeps WM at 95% but fails robust PM at 12%. VO sits in the middle (53%/53%). Roles are cleanly separable.

Negative result · end-to-end head collapses margin 0.994 → 0.728

Training a correctness head disrupts the basin geometry it reads. Geometric distillation only partly recovers (0.932 vs oracle 0.993). Basin geometry matures only at layer ~24 — a temporal paradox for single-pass self-monitoring.

04

Strengths vs Limitations — side by side

PROWhat holds up

  • Unified framework. Conflict and hallucination explained as two configurations of one attractor landscape — not two separate phenomena.
  • Causal, interventionist. LoRA placement + Jacobian decomposition isolate which circuit does what, beyond correlational probing.
  • Derived, parameter-free law. C = exp(−c/Δ̄) fits 21 points across 4 families, 0.36B–14B — a non-trivial universality claim.
  • Intellectual honesty. Explicit TruthfulQA stress test, cross-domain failure, negative result on metacognitive heads, layer-wise temporal paradox.
  • Practical payoff. One scalar, zero false refusals at the 100%-catch threshold, transferring synthetic → pretrained knowledge.

CONWhere it strains

  • Basin centers don't scale. Requires averaging hidden states over canonical templates for every fact; distillation only partly closes the gap (0.932 vs 0.993).
  • Headline depends on robust training. Under brittle single-template PM, margin AUROC collapses to chance at N≥400 (Table 27) — not unconditional.
  • Narrow NL validation. 196 curated capital/language queries on one family; TruthfulQA drops to 0.858 and cross-domain transfer fails (0.476).
  • Conflict unsolved. Static margin AUROC < 0.75 on conflict; trajectory methods left as future work.
  • Missing ablations. No robust-regime rank sweep; no Semantic Entropy Probe comparison at matched compute; no RLHF-drift test.
05

Open questions for further work

Q1Can margin be estimated without exhaustive basin-center computation — e.g., a lightweight head trained once on a generic domain and transferred? Cross-relation transfer (capitals → AUROC 0.887) hints yes, but is it robust to RLHF drift?
Q2Does C = exp(−c/Δ̄) survive temperature / nucleus sampling / RLHF-tuned distributions, where softmax saturation may behave differently?
Q3Can trajectory margin over autoregressive steps recover diagnostic power on conflict (static AUROC < 0.75) — and at what compute cost?
Q4Is there a continuous predictor (basin depth / volume) interpolating brittle-collapse and robust-recovery regimes without re-training?
Q5Does basin geometry still predict retrieval-ignore in RAG, where WM is a variable-length passage rather than a 5-digit code?
