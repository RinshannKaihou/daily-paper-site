> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一个厂商无关、跨测试框架的"可重构性"评测有效性度量，对智能体安全评测中八类决策属性逐项打分，并通过 Evidence Sufficiency Card 与 counterfactual-replay 前置条件探针，证明相同的"标称结果"（任务成功/攻击成功/监控分数）可能建立在证据充分性差异巨大的记录之上（充分性分 0.458–0.833）。

> **TL;DR:** This single-author instrument paper argues that identical nominal outcomes in agent-safety evaluations (task success, attack success, monitor AUROC) can rest on materially different evidence regimes, and delivers a vendor-neutral, cross-harness "reconstructability" metric scoring eight decision-property classes in [0,1], an Evidence Sufficiency Card, a replayability-precondition probe, and a claim-evidence (overclaim) gap. On four public/bundled traces (Inspect eval logs, generic JSON/JSONL agent traces), twelve-field evidence sufficiency spans 0.458–0.833 across inputs sharing only a surface reading (a 0.375 spread), a synthetic release-gate pair blocks its raw variant at 0.542 and passes the instrumented one at 0.667 (a 0.125 field-level gap vs. EviBound/Nian-style disclosure which assert rather than measure sufficiency), and counterfactual-replay preconditions are unmet in 100% of the six reported cards.

## 核心贡献

- **属性级可重构性度量（property-level reconstructability metric）**：在单位区间 [0,1] 上对八类决策属性逐项打分——动作可恢复性、权限可恢复性、监控可见性、策略状态可见性、责任归因、反事实可重放性、断言充分性、评测感知混淆，并用"瓶颈标量"（最弱链）防止单一汇总数掩盖完全不可重构的属性。
- **厂商无关的跨框架归一化适配器**：将至少两种评测框架（UK AISI 的 Inspect 与 ControlArena）的原生轨迹映射到统一的 Evidence Sufficiency Card，新增框架只需新增一个 ingest 模块，无需修改 Card schema 或度量本身。
- **反事实重放干预协议 + 可重放性前置条件探针**：规定了重放需改动的条件（监控可见性、权限、策略状态、评测感知线索），并实现了一个"只打分、不执行"的前置条件探针，可对每条决策给出 possible / degraded / impossible 判定。
- **断言-证据（过度声称）间隙 G**：对共享同一标称结果的两个证据制度，量化其可重构性差距；并提出按运行（per-run）、可机器检查的监控覆盖发布门（monitor-coverage release gate）——任何被识别监控未实际打分的风险工具跨度都会阻断 eval 的 PASS。
- **可复现工程产物**：`inspect-evidence-sufficiency` v0.3.0 评分器、Card schema、回放探针、发布门以 Apache-2.0 开源，固定时间戳下可逐字节复现每个报告数字。

## Key Contributions

- **A property-level reconstructability metric** scoring, in the closed unit interval, how much of each of eight decision-property classes a captured trace recovers, aggregated via a non-compensatory bottleneck scalar (the minimum over claim-required properties) so that one zero-scored required property drives the headline to zero — directly operationalising the auditability literature's "a single omitted field can render a class of policies uncheckable."
- **A vendor-neutral cross-harness normalization adapter** that maps native trace records from Inspect and ControlArena (plus generic JSON/JSONL) onto one per-decision Evidence Sufficiency Card, where each evidence element is bound to the decision by a (decision_id, agent_step_id, tool_call_id) triple so an unbindable element counts as missing.
- **A counterfactual-replay intervention protocol with an implemented replayability-precondition probe**: rather than executing replays, it scores six preconditions (model binding, decision context, determinism controls, monitor-view spec, permission/policy state, environment re-creation) and emits possible / degraded / impossible per decision.
- **The claim-evidence (overclaim) gap G = |R(A) − R(B)|** for two regimes sharing a nominal outcome, plus a per-run, machine-checkable monitor-coverage release gate that fails an evaluation's PASS whenever a risky tool-execution span was not actually scored by an identified monitor.
- **A deterministic, model-free engineering artifact** (`inspect-evidence-sufficiency`, v0.3.0, archived 2026-07-01): the v0 scorer operationalises the design as twelve three-valued field checks F01–F12, byte-identical given fixed inputs and a pinned `SOURCE_DATE_EPOCH`, shipped as an open reproducibility package.

## Methodology

The unit of analysis is the **evaluation claim** — an assertion a safety evaluation makes about an agent decision on the strength of a recorded run — and a claim is reconstructable to the degree the decision properties it depends on can be recovered from captured evidence. The instrument is purely a measurement of evidence sufficiency: it does not adjudicate whether behaviour was safe, nor rank harnesses or vendors.

The construction proceeds in five parts. **(1) Eight property classes (Definition 1)** cross-walk the five auditability dimensions of Nian et al. (2026) to the evaluation-claim unit and add two (claim sufficiency, evaluation-awareness confounding). Each class pairs a decision property with the evidence that reconstructs it and the claim it underwrites. **(2) Per-property scoring (Definition 2):** r_p = |present(p)| / |R_p|, the proportion of a property's declared required-evidence set that the trace both contains and binds to the decision; learned-parameter-free and model-in-the-loop-free, generalising the author's prior discrete 1.0/0.5/0.0 pilot scale to a unit-interval grid. **(3) Aggregation (Definition 3):** the primary output is the full reconstructability vector (r1…r8); the headline bottleneck scalar R_min is the minimum over claim-required properties (non-compensatory), with a coverage mean R_mean reported only alongside the vector. **(4) The overclaim gap (Definition 4):** G(A,B) = |R(A) − R(B)| between two regimes sharing a nominal outcome, plus a property-resolved vector g_p. **(5) The replay protocol (§3.5):** an intervention specification (altered monitor visibility, permissions, policy state, evaluation-awareness cues) separated from a deterministic precondition probe that re-runs nothing but checks six preconditions per decision.

The released v0 scorer (§4.4) is explicitly **coarser than the design**: twelve three-valued field checks F01–F12 rather than eight unit-interval class scores; per-trace rather than per-decision; responsibility attribution and evaluation-awareness confounding carried only indirectly; and a field-level analogue of G (a mean-like twelve-field sufficiency_score difference) rather than the bottleneck R_min — the paper states the equivalence between the two aggregations is *not* established, and measuring Definition 4's G as defined is the "v2 protocol." The cross-harness adapter targets Inspect (demonstrated) and ControlArena (reader implemented, fixture-covered only). The empirical component (§5) is explicitly a **demonstration on existing traces, not a controlled experiment**: no new model runs, no statistical hypothesis tests, not pre-registered, scoring is a deterministic pass over public CC-BY-4.0 traces and synthetic fixtures.

## Results & Highlights

The empirical demonstration is deliberately bounded but reports concrete, reproducible numbers:

- **Evidence sufficiency spans 0.458–0.833** across the four scored inputs (Trace Commons coding-agent JSONL trace = 0.458 insufficient; Assayo judged web-research JSON trace = 0.458 insufficient; Inspect eval log mockllm = 0.667 conditional; synthetic Inspect-style fixture = 0.833 conditional), a cross-substrate spread of |0.833 − 0.458| = **0.375** beneath inputs that share only a surface reading of "an agent run completed and left a usable trace." None reaches sufficient-for-named-decision; two are insufficient and two conditional.
- **The synthetic release-gate pair** (same billing exchange, same recorded success status, raw vs. eval-instrumented) cards the raw variant at sufficiency **0.542** (present 4 / partial 5 / missing 3; blocking F03, F07, F09, F10) and the instrumented variant at **0.667** (present 6 / partial 4 / missing 2; blocking F09, F10) — a field-level analogue of G of **|0.667 − 0.542| = 0.125**, resolved to F03 and F07 (the two fields on which the blocking lists differ). Under `--gate` with `--require-present F07`, the raw variant exits 1 and the instrumented exits 0.
- **Counterfactual-replay preconditions are unmet in 100% of the six reported Evidence Sufficiency Cards** (the four of Table 3 plus both release-gate cards): F10 (policy/counterfactual replay notes) is missing on all three real inputs and partial on the synthetic fixture (the ceiling for a face-value declared claim); F09 never rises past partial on any real input; no real input resolves its model binding (F03 partial on all three). Executed replay is therefore "not merely unperformed but undefined."
- **Monitor-coverage gate** on the walkthrough pair: the covered fixture (monitor `agentic_payments_suspicion_monitor` scored all three risky tool turns) yields coverage_ratio **1.0**, verdict covered, gate exit 0; the uncovered fixture (monitor scored one of three risky turns) yields coverage_ratio **0.333**, uncovered_spans naming the transfer and delete_record turns, verdict partial-coverage, gate exit 1. Narrowing the risky set to the two mutating tools drives coverage to **0.0** and verdict uncovered.
- **A key null observation:** the two real public traces report identical sufficiency 0.458, identical status counts (4/5/3), yet *different* blocking composition (F07 vs. F06) — so the discriminating signal sits in the blocking-gap lists, not the headline scalar.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (genuine):** The wedge is real and well-articulated. Where AgenTracer (Zhang et al., 2025) executes counterfactual replay to *attribute* a failure and EviBound (Chen, 2025) gates the *agent's own* completion claim against artifact stores, this instrument audits the *monitor* and can return "insufficient evidence" as a verdict — a third outcome the attribution/governance lines are structured to avoid. The shift from "which step failed?" to "could the evidence the harness kept even answer that?" is a legitimate conceptual contribution.
- **Rigor (weak — the paper's central vulnerability):** The metric is "deterministic given the schema," but the schema's correctness is **author-declared, not empirically validated** (threat T1). The eight required-evidence sets R_p and the claim-required subset R are the denominator of every score, and their membership is "asserted, not empirically validated." No inter-annotator agreement, no ground-truth labelling — the author flags this as "the central calibration target" deferred to "FW2." Combined with **no statistical hypothesis tests** and a non-pre-registered design, the numbers in §6 are descriptive points, not generalisable estimates.
- **Reproducibility (strong):** Genuinely good. Fixed `SOURCE_DATE_EPOCH` pins cards byte-for-byte; inputs are sha256-verified at pinned dataset commits; two offline commands regenerate every §6 number; Apache-2.0 package. This is the paper's standout engineering virtue.
- **Significance (premature to claim broad impact):** The demonstration substrate is tiny — four scored inputs, three of the six "inputs" in Table 2 are hand-built synthetic fixtures, and cross-harness ingestion is demonstrated on **Inspect only**; the ControlArena reader is "implemented but exercised only against a synthetic fixture," not a real export (threat T3). No frontier or closed model is scored. The genuinely cross-harness, eight-property, executed-replay form of all three RQs is explicitly labelled the "v2 protocol" — i.e., not done.
- **Specific red flags:** (a) The released v0 field-level analogue is **not** Definition 4's G — it is a mean-like twelve-field sufficiency_score difference, and "no equivalence between the two aggregations is established," so the headline overclaim-gap concept is asserted by design but not actually measured. (b) Two of the twelve scored fields (F01 scoring-time context, F12 the card's own residual-uncertainty statement) are *not* trace-earned, so sufficiency_score "modestly overstates trace-earned sufficiency" — the paper concedes a stricter recount is deferred. (c) Unconditional membership in Definition 6 cannot distinguish a structurally absent element from an inapplicable one, "conservatively depressing r_p." (d) The "matched-outcome pair" that exercises the overclaim gap is synthetic; the substrate holds **no real matched-outcome pair**, so the central motivating claim ("identical nominal outcomes sit atop different evidence regimes") is demonstrated only on hand-built fixtures.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean and well-positioned: the contrast with AgenTracer (attribution), EviBound (agent-self gating), Nian et al.'s Auditability Card (per-system disclosure), and ADAS (deployment licensing) is precise and non-overclaiming.
- Deterministic, model-free, no-judge-in-the-loop scoring — a deliberate and well-motivated choice given that the best LLM scores only 11% on TRAIL trace debugging (Deshpande et al., 2025).
- The non-compensatory bottleneck scalar is the right design: it prevents a high coverage mean from masking a zero-scored required property.
- Excellent reproducibility engineering: byte-identical regeneration, pinned inputs, open license, archived software DOI.
- Intellectual honesty about limits: §7 is unusually thorough, and the paper repeatedly flags what the demonstration does *not* establish rather than overselling.

## Limitations

- The required-evidence sets R_p are author-declared and uncalibrated — the entire metric is deterministic relative to a schema whose own validity is asserted, not measured (T1).
- The empirical substrate is tiny (four scored inputs) and three of six entries are synthetic fixtures; no real matched-outcome pair exists, so the overclaim gap G is exercised only on a synthetic pair.
- Cross-harness ingestion is demonstrated on Inspect only; ControlArena is implemented but not run on a real export. Only one real harness is actually exercised.
- No executed counterfactual replay: RQ3 is answered only in its precondition form; the causal question (does replay change a verdict?) is deferred to v2.
- The released v0 scorer is coarser than the design (3-valued not unit-interval; 12 fields not 8 classes; per-trace not per-decision), and the field-level analogue of G is not the defined G.
- No statistical testing, not pre-registered; the two-annotator ground-truth benchmark is future work.
- Single author, independent (no institutional affiliation or co-authors for cross-checking); most citations are arXiv preprints.

## Relevance

直接契合"智能体安全评测"与"评测有效性"研究兴趣：它把"一个安全评测数字到底有没有证据支撑"变成了可度量、可复现的工程指标，对构建可信 Agent 评测流水线、设计安全案例（safety case）和 CI 发布门有直接参考价值，尤其适合关注 Inspect/ControlArena 等控制评测框架的工作者。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How would the eight required-evidence sets R_p change under a two-annotator ground-truth labelling exercise, and how sensitive are the headline bottleneck scores to membership perturbations of a single set?
- On a real ControlArena export (not a synthetic fixture), does the adapter still produce covered monitor-coverage verdicts, and does the per-decision (rather than per-trace) scoring surface gaps the v0 aggregation hides?
- Does executing the §3.5 counterfactual-replay protocol ever *change* a reconstructability verdict on traces whose preconditions are met — i.e., is RQ3's causal form (H3) ever non-null in practice?
- Can the field-level sufficiency_score analogue be formally related to Definition 4's bottleneck R_min, or is the gap between "mean over twelve fields" and "minimum over claim-required properties" material enough to require a different v0 operationalisation?
- How does this instrument behave on traces from frontier/closed models and from non-Inspect harnesses (e.g., MALT, LinuxArena), where logging conventions differ substantially?
