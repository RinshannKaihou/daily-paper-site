01Methodology

A report should depend only on legitimate evidence and be invariant to forbidden incentives. Because “resist pressure” and “track evidence” pull apart, the authors score both on a constructible benchmark — then causally isolate the coordinates that commit a report.

STEP 01 · FRAMING

Internal incentive-compatibility (IC)

Define report-stage sycophancy as a two-sided contract failure. Introduce the (resist, update) dual-control axis — a scalar sycophancy rate is uninformative, since a never-updating model minimizes it by construction.

STEP 02 · BENCHMARK

Bayesian-witness setting

Binary world state, exact posteriors by log-odds. A stated source-reliability variable turns the same user disagreement into licensed evidence or forbidden pressure across ≥9 counterfactual variants — breaking the evidence/pressure confound by construction.

STEP 03 · CAUSAL ID

Low-rank report coordinates

Interchange patching at the post-reasoning decision position localizes an answer coordinate to late layers (L24/28), rank-16, with near-orthogonal confidence (L25) and caveat (L23) coordinates — independently clampable.

STEP 04 · CRC CLAMP

Counterfactual report-coordinate clamp

Read the model's own report coordinate from an incentive-neutralized counterfactual prompt and clamp the pressured run's L24–27 window toward it. Path-specificity comes from the reference — not a global strength α.

03Strengths & Limitations

A measurement instrument as much as a method — strong on causal identification and honest negatives, weaker on ecological validity and deployable scale.

Strengths

  • The (resist, update) dual-control axis is a better primitive than a scalar sycophancy rate — it diagnoses why a method works. The resist-only SFT → update 0.01 result is the crisp illustration.
  • Bayesian-witness benchmark elegantly renders identical disagreement as evidence-or-pressure via a stated reliability variable, with fully known posteriors.
  • Mechanistic rigor: sufficiency (0.95), rank saturation (ρ₁₆=0.93), block necessity (L24–27 → 0.53), tri-coordinate orthogonality (|cos|≤0.10) — a fuller battery than typical steering papers.
  • Honest negative controls on natural data: mismatched-reference at 0.31–0.39, paired McNemar on the same items, borderline Mistral result (p=0.07) reported rather than spun.

Limitations

  • Ecological validity is partial. The witness benchmark is constructed; on SycophancyEval the licensed-evidence turn is also constructed (worked-solution on ~⅓ of update items).
  • Two-pass clamp needs a separable counterfactual at inference — degrades to reference quality when forbidden and licensed signals are entangled in one span.
  • Lossy single-pass deployment reaches only 0.73/0.97 — a 27-point resist gap flagged as motivation for future RLAIF/DPO internalization.
  • Scale & architecture: only dense 3–8B models; MoE and frontier-scale untested, so the “report-commit regularity” rests on three small dense families.
  • Caveat behavior unsolved: the caveat coordinate composes, but the behavioral caveat policy is over-cautious — caveat results support composability only.

04Open Questions

Where the certificate stops and the hard problems begin.

  1. How does the clamp behave when forbidden pressure and licensed evidence entangle in one span? Can a learned reference-constructor recover dual control, or does the method fundamentally require separability?
  2. Does the low-rank report coordinate (relative depth 0.86–0.91) survive at frontier scale / MoE, or does the report-commit stage restructure with scale and routing?
  3. Can the counterfactual-invariance contract be internalized via RLAIF/DPO to close 0.73/0.97 → ~1.0/1.0 without collapsing into the resist-only-SFT stubbornness failure?
  4. Mistral's worked-solution update only rises 0.18 → 0.37 (borderline p=0.07) — is this a clamp limit or a weak baseline on long evidence?
  5. How does the method interact with chain-of-thought, where the report-commit stage may be distributed across many tokens rather than a late residual window?
  6. Does the striking |cos|≤0.10 orthogonality hold under distribution shift, or is it an artifact of the structured-report parsing the benchmark enforces?
